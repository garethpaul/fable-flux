# Della the Dolphin’s Cozy Guest Room Adventure

Della was a playful dolphin with bright, curious eyes. One rainy day, Della visited her friend’s house and stayed in the cozy guest room. The guest room smelled like clean soap and lemon. The soft rug felt warm on Della’s fins. The window showed tiny raindrops tap-tap-tapping. Plip, plop, plip!

“Hmm,” Della said, wiggling her tail. “It’s a stay-inside day. What can I do?”

She looked around. There was a small table, a comfy chair, and a basket of safe craft things: paper, crayons, stickers, and a little mirror. A sign on the basket said, “Please ask before using.” Della’s tummy fluttered. She loved to make art. She also liked to be honest.

Della swam—swoosh, very gently—across the room and called, “Excuse me!” Her friend’s parent peeked in. “Yes, Della?”

“May I use the craft basket?” Della asked. “I want to make something cheerful.”

“Yes,” they smiled. “Be creative. Be careful. And clean up after, please.”

“I will,” Della said. “Thank you.”

Della opened the basket. “Ooo!” she squeaked. “Crayons that smell like blueberry. Stickers that sparkle like tiny stars.” She spread a sheet of paper on the table. “I will make a ‘Rainy-Day Joy Poster!’”

She drew a big, happy sun with a crayon that smelled sweet. She added a smiling cloud with rosy cheeks. She stuck star stickers that twinkled when the lamp glowed. “This is fun,” Della giggled. “I can make my own sunshine!”

The rain made soft music outside—hush, hush, hush. Della hummed along. Her belly made a little rumble. “I feel hungry,” she said. She thought for a moment. “Self-care time!”

She checked a note by the lamp. It said, “Snacks in the little basket. Water on the tray.” Della poured herself some water, sip-sip-sip. She peeled a banana from the snack basket. “Mmm,” she smiled. “This gives me energy to create.”

After her snack, Della looked in the mirror. “My eyes look sparkly. My fins feel strong. I am ready to try something new!” She wanted to make a friendly puppet to match her poster.

Della found a paper bag. She drew a dolphin face on it, just like her own, with a big smile. She added sticker eyes and crayon freckles. “Hello, I’m Little Della!” she said in a playful voice.

“Hello, I’m Big Della,” she answered in her normal voice. She laughed. “We can make a show for the guest room.”

Della set the chair like a small stage and propped the poster behind it. She whispered, “I can do this by myself.” She felt proud to try. She was being self-reliant—using her own ideas and her own fins.

“Welcome to the Rainy-Day Joy Show!” Little Della squeaked. “Today we make our own sunshine.”

Big Della said, “How do we do that?”

Little Della answered, “We use creativity! We draw. We sing. We tell the truth. We take good care of ourselves!”

Della sang a soft song: “Plip, plop, rain, you can’t stop play. I make my joy in a cozy way.” Her voice was gentle like the rain. The room felt warm and safe and happy.

Then Della noticed a tiny crayon mark on the table. “Oh no,” she gasped. “I made a mark by mistake.” Her cheeks felt hot. “I must be honest.”

She called to her friend’s parent. “I’m sorry,” Della said when they came in. “I made a small crayon mark. I did not mean to. May I clean it?”

“Thank you for telling me the truth,” they said kindly. “Let’s clean it together.” They showed Della a soft cloth and a little soap. Della rubbed gently—wipe, wipe, wipe—until the mark was gone.

“You did great,” they said. “You were honest and careful.”

Della smiled. “I feel better. Telling the truth helps my heart feel light.”

She put the crayons back in their box, closed the glue, and placed the stickers neatly away. She folded the scrap paper and tossed tiny bits in the bin. The guest room looked tidy. The lemony smell came back, fresh and clean.

Her friend’s parent looked at the poster and the puppet. “You made all this by yourself?”

“Yes,” Della said proudly. “I used my ideas. I took snack and water breaks. I told the truth when I made a mistake. And I made my own sunshine!”

“That is creativity, self-care, and self-reliance,” they said. “You turned a rainy day into a happy day.”

Della hugged the paper puppet. The rain slowed to a gentle drip. The window showed a soft glow. Della whispered, “I can make joy wherever I go.”

She taped the poster to the wall with permission. The stars on it sparkled. The smiling sun seemed to wink. Della waved her fin. “Thank you, cozy guest room. Thank you, rainy day.”

Then she said to Little Della, “What did we learn today?”

Little Della answered, “We learned to use our ideas to create. We learned to care for our bodies with rest, water, and snacks. We learned to try by ourselves and to ask for help when needed. And we learned to be honest, even about little mistakes.”

Della nodded. “Yes. That is how we make our own sunshine.”

The End.
