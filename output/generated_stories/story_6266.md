# Polly’s Rainbow Snack Plan

In a warm kitchen, the sun made yellow squares on the floor. Pots hummed softly on the stove. A bowl of apples shone red and shiny. A bunch of spinach smelled fresh and green. In the middle of it all stood Polly, a creative parrot with bright blue feathers and a curious eye.

“Squawk! Snack time!” Polly chirped, hopping from the chair to the counter. She looked at the cookie jar. The cookies smelled sweet like sugar and vanilla. Her tummy rumbled.

“Cookies?” Polly asked.

Mama, humming a gentle tune, wiped her hands on a soft towel. “Hmm,” she said, smiling. “Cookies are a sometimes treat. Your body needs energy. Your body needs fuel. Let’s make a rainbow snack!”

Polly’s wings fluffed. “Rainbow snack? Craft a snack? Squawk! Yes, yes!”

Polly loved to craft. She made paper hats, noodle necklaces, and leaf crowns. Crafting a snack sounded fun.

Mama opened the fridge. Cool air tickled Polly’s beak. “We have red strawberries, orange carrots, yellow bananas, green spinach, blue blueberries, and purple grapes,” Mama said. “Colors help your body grow strong. Each color helps in a special way.”

Polly looked at the counter. “So many colors,” she whispered. She could hear carrots crunch. She could smell strawberries sweet like sunshine. She could feel the smooth skin of the grapes.

But there was a problem.

The fruit and veggies were in messy piles. A sticky jar of jam had leaked. A few wilted spinach leaves drooped. A plastic bag crinkled. The cutting board was not clean.

Polly tilted her head. “Uh-oh. Messy mess. Is this safe?” she asked.

Mama nodded kindly. “Good question. Clean kitchen, clean hands, healthy snack. Let’s solve this together.”

“Problem-solving! Squawk!” Polly flapped her wings.

Step one: wash hands. Polly sang, “Scrub, scrub, scrub-a-dub,” as warm water splashed. The soap smelled like lemons. She rubbed her claws and counted slowly, “One, two, three… to twenty!” Her feathers felt fluffy and clean.

Step two: tidy and care for the Earth. Mama said, “We reuse and recycle when we can.” Polly sorted. Plastic bag in the recycling bin. Food scraps in the compost bowl. Wilted leaves for compost too. The compost smelled like damp forest, not yucky, just earthy.

“Environmental care!” Polly chirped. “We care for our home and our Earth.”

Step three: wipe and wash. Mama washed the cutting board. Polly wiped the table with a damp cloth. “Swish, swish,” she whispered, making little circles.

Now the kitchen looked neat and bright. The warm air smelled like cinnamon and oranges. The sink gurgled happily.

“Now, let’s craft!” said Mama.

Polly put on a tiny apron. “Chef Polly!” she giggled.

Mama showed her how to slice safely. “I’ll cut. You place the pieces.”

Polly arranged the fruit and veggies into a rainbow on a clean plate:
- Red strawberries like little hearts
- Orange carrots cut into stars
- Yellow banana moons
- Green spinach leaves like tiny boats
- Blue blueberries like shiny beads
- Purple grapes like round balloons

“Color, color, color!” Polly sang. “Each color helps me grow.”

Mama nodded. “Red helps your heart. Orange helps your eyes. Yellow gives you energy. Green helps you stay strong. Blue and purple help your brain.”

Polly touched her head. “Brain smart! Squawk!”

They added fun faces to make snack art. A strawberry smile. Blueberry eyes. Carrot eyebrows. Spinach hair. Banana ears that wiggled when the plate moved.

“It’s a snack craft!” Polly laughed. “I made a rainbow friend.”

But there was one more problem. Polly felt thirsty. “Water?” she asked.

Mama pointed to a pitcher. “Yes, water first. Water helps your body work well.” The water was cool and clear. Polly sipped and felt fresh.

“Now taste,” Mama said.

Polly tried a strawberry. “Sweet and soft.” She munched a carrot star. “Crunchy and orange!” She nibbled a spinach leaf. “Leafy and green.” Blueberries popped like tiny bubbles. Bananas were creamy. Grapes were juicy.

Her tummy felt happy and light. “My body says thank you,” Polly whispered.

They saved the peels and stems for the compost. “Nothing wasted,” Mama said. “Good for the garden.”

Polly nodded. “Good for Earth. Good for me.”

When the plate was almost empty, Polly saw the cookie jar again. She lifted the lid and sniffed. “Cookies later?” she asked.

Mama smiled. “Yes, a small cookie after a healthy snack is okay sometimes.”

Polly perched on the chair, proud and calm. “I solved two problems,” she said. “Messy kitchen and hungry tummy. I used clean hands, kind Earth care, and a rainbow snack craft.”

Mama hugged her. “You made a smart plan. That’s good problem-solving, Polly.”

Polly fluffed her feathers and chirped, “Rainbow first, cookies later. Water always. Clean hands, happy tummy. Squawk!”

The sun moved across the warm kitchen floor. The plate was bright with a few blueberries left. Polly popped them into her beak and giggled.

Today, she learned a clear lesson: Eating many colors helps her body grow strong and smart. Making food can be a fun craft. Cleaning and caring for Earth keeps the kitchen safe. With a plan, problems get solved.

Polly spread her wings. “Tomorrow,” she said, “I will make a veggie rainbow sailboat!”

Mama winked. “I can’t wait, Chef Polly.”

The End.
