# Ollie the Otter’s Rooftop Rainbow

Ollie the otter was playful and curious. One sunny morning, Ollie climbed up a sturdy ladder to a flat, cozy rooftop garden. The roof felt warm under his paws. A soft breeze tickled his whiskers. Flowers in little pots smelled sweet, like honey and lemons. Birds chirped, peep peep, from the edge of the roof.

“Today, I have a big goal,” Ollie said, patting his round tummy. “I will make a rainbow banner for the whole neighborhood!”

Ollie brought crayons, soft fabric, and a paintbrush. He laid the fabric flat and wiggled his tail. “Red, orange, yellow,” he sang. “Green, blue, purple.” He dipped his brush. Swish, swish! The paint made tiny splashes and smelled like berries.

Just then, Ollie heard a gentle coo. Pippa the pigeon landed nearby, flapping her soft gray wings. “Hello, Ollie,” Pippa cooed. “This rooftop is my quiet nap place. The sun feels warm here. Could I nap soon?”

Ollie smiled. “Hi, Pippa! I am making a rainbow banner. I want to hang it on the fence so everyone can see.”

Pippa peered at the bright colors. “It is lovely,” she said. “But the tapping and swishing are noisy to me. My head feels busy.”

Ollie paused. His ears perked. He listened: tap, tap, swish, swish, peep, peep. “I did not know it was too loud for you,” he said softly. “Thank you for telling me.”

A small squirrel named Suri popped up with a flick of her fluffy tail. “Hi friends!” she chirped. “I am here to plant tiny sunflower seeds.” She held a little scoop that went scoop, scoop in the soil. “But I am scared of loud noises too.”

Ollie looked at Pippa and Suri. His heart felt warm and careful. He wanted his rainbow, but he also wanted his friends to feel safe and calm.

“I think we can use peaceful words,” Ollie said. “Let’s talk and make a plan together.”

Pippa nodded. “I like peaceful talking.”

Suri nodded too. “Me too!”

Ollie took a deep breath. “My goal is to finish the rainbow banner today,” he said. “What are your goals?”

Pippa said, “My goal is to take a quiet nap in the warm sun.”

Suri said, “My goal is to plant seeds without feeling jumpy.”

Ollie smiled. “We all have goals. Maybe we can share the roof and help each other.”

He asked a kind question. “Pippa, what sounds feel okay to you?”

Pippa cooed, “Soft sounds are fine. Loud tapping makes me fluttery.”

Ollie turned to Suri. “Suri, what would help you feel brave?”

Suri twitched her nose. “Slow and gentle sounds help me. And I like to know what will happen next.”

“Thank you,” Ollie said. “I hear you. I care about you.”

He looked around the garden. The leaves whispered. The clouds drifted fluffy and white. “What if we take turns?” Ollie said. “I can paint quietly for ten minutes. Then I will stop, and Pippa can nap for ten minutes. While Pippa naps, Suri can scoop very softly. Then we switch.”

Pippa tilted her head. “That sounds fair.”

Suri smiled. “We can use a timer! When the little bell rings, we switch.”

Ollie clapped softly. “Great idea. This is our teamwork plan.”

They set a tiny bell on a flower pot. Ding! The first turn began. Ollie used slow, gentle brush strokes. Swish… pause… swish. He hummed a quiet tune, la-la-la, like a soft breeze. The paint glowed bright on the fabric. Red turned to orange, then sunny yellow.

The bell went ding! Pippa curled her wings and closed her eyes. “Mmm,” she sighed. “Warm sun.” Her feathers puffed. Suri planted seeds with careful scoops, scoop… pat… scoop. Ollie cleaned his brush and whispered, “You are doing great.”

Ding! Time to switch. Pippa stretched and cooed, “Thank you, friends. I feel rested.” Ollie painted green, then blue, with tiny quiet dabs. Suri watered the little seeds. Drip, drip, drip. The sound was soft like rain.

As they worked and rested, they used peaceful words.

“Can I have a little more space?” Suri asked.

“Of course,” Ollie said, moving over.

“I feel sleepy,” Pippa said.

“We will whisper,” Ollie and Suri replied.

They kept their plan. They listened. They took turns. They helped. Soon the rainbow banner was almost done. Purple shimmered in the sun.

A small cloud passed, and a cool shade brushed their fur and feathers. The air smelled like wet leaves. Ollie tied the banner to the fence with gentle knots. The fabric fluttered, whoosh, whoosh, like soft wings.

“Ta-da!” Ollie cheered in a quiet voice. “We did it together.”

Pippa smiled. “The colors make my heart feel peaceful.”

Suri giggled. “Our seeds are tucked in and happy.”

Neighbors below looked up. “Ooo,” said a child. “A rooftop rainbow!” The banner waved hello to everyone.

Ollie felt proud, not just of the banner, but of the kindness on the roof. He looked at his friends. “Thank you for using peaceful words. Thank you for the compromise and teamwork,” he said.

Pippa cooed, “Thank you for listening.”

Suri added, “Thank you for making a plan.”

Ollie wrapped his tail around his paws. “Today I learned that when we listen, speak kindly, and take turns, everyone’s goals can shine. Peaceful-communication helps us share and care.”

They sat together in the warm light, quiet and happy, as the rainbow banner danced gently in the breeze.

The End.
