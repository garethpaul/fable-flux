# Joey Helps in the Shed

Joey the joyful kangaroo joey loved to bounce. Boing, boing, boing! One sunny morning, he bounced past the garden and stopped at the garden shed. The door was a little stuck, and the air smelled like fresh dirt, wood, and flowers. Joey’s ears twitched. He heard a tiny sneeze.

“Achoo!” squeaked a voice.

Joey peeked inside. “Hello? Is someone in here?” he asked.

From behind a watering can, a little mouse named Mimi popped out. She wore a tiny leaf as a hat. “Hi, Joey,” she said. “I’m Mimi. I came to find string, but the shed is messy. I can’t reach anything.”

Joey looked around. The shed was full of things. There were buckets, rakes, gloves, and pots. Some things were on the floor. Some things were piled high. Dust danced in the sunbeams. The tools looked sleepy and mixed up.

Joey smiled. “I can help,” he said. “Helping is kind. Helping makes hearts feel warm.”

Mimi’s eyes sparkled. “Really?”

“Really!” Joey said. His tail thumped with happy thumps. Thump, thump.

Just then, Old Turtle Tom poked his head in. His shell was green and smooth. “Good morning,” he said slowly. “I was coming to tidy the shed, but my legs are slow today.”

Joey bounced once. “We can help you, Tom!”

Tom smiled. “That would be very kind.”

Joey took a big breath. He smelled the sweet straw in a basket. He heard birds chirping outside. He felt brave and ready. “Let’s make a plan,” he said. “We will put things in their homes.”

Mimi clapped her tiny paws. “Yes!”

First, Joey picked up the gloves. “Gloves go together,” he said. He matched glove to glove. “Hello, friend glove,” he giggled. “Here is your buddy.” He set them in a small box. The box made a soft shuff sound.

Mimi tugged a roll of string. “String goes on a hook,” she said. She reached, but it was too high. Joey kneeled. “Climb on my tail,” he said. Mimi climbed up and looped the string on the hook. “Thank you!” she said.

“Helping is like a team hug,” Joey said. He felt warm inside.

Old Turtle Tom moved a broom. Swish, swish. “I can sweep,” he said. Dust swirled like tiny clouds, but the floor began to shine. Joey watched where Tom swept and moved the pots to the side. “Pots here,” Joey said. He counted. “One, two, three, four.” He liked the click-clack when he stacked them. Click, clack.

A little ladybug flew in and landed on Joey’s nose. “Hello, little friend,” Joey said. The ladybug tickled. Joey giggled. Mimi giggled too. “We are all in the shed,” Mimi said. “It feels busy and happy.”

Joey found a small rake under a blanket. “Oh!” he said. “Rake was hiding.” He felt the smooth wood and the bumpy teeth. He hung it on a peg. It made a tiny tap. Tap.

“Thank you for helping,” Old Turtle Tom said. “You are very kind to give your time.”

Joey nodded. “This is called volunteering,” he said carefully. “We choose to help. We use our time and our hearts.”

They kept going. Joey filled a basket with seed packets. The paper felt crisp. The packets rustled like leaves. Mimi dusted the shelves with a soft cloth. Wipe, wipe. She hummed a tiny song. Tom swept the last corner. Swish, swish, swish.

Soon the shed looked cozy and neat. Sunlight made bright squares on the clean floor. The tools stood tall on their hooks. The air smelled like lemon soap and fresh hay. Joey’s chest felt big with pride.

Mimi squeaked, “I can find the string now! Thank you, Joey!”

Joey beamed. “We did it together.”

Old Turtle Tom nodded. “This shed is safe and ready. You were a great helper, Joey.”

Joey’s ears perked up. He heard a soft meow. A small kitten peeked in with curious eyes. “I lost my ball of yarn,” the kitten said. “Have you seen it?”

Joey looked around and spotted a round, blue ball under a bench. He bent down, felt the soft fuzz, and rolled it to the kitten. “Here you go.”

The kitten purred. “Thank you! You are very kind.”

Joey’s heart felt like a warm muffin. “Helping feels good,” he said. “When we help, we share smiles.”

Mimi nodded. “And we make places better.”

Old Turtle Tom said, “And we do it together.”

They stood in the doorway and took a deep breath. The garden smelled sweet, and the breeze felt cool. Joey bounced once more. Boing! “I will come again,” he said. “I can volunteer to help next time, too.”

Tom smiled. “You are welcome any day.”

Joey waved to Mimi, patted the kitten gently, and looked back at the tidy shed. He felt calm, kind, and strong. “Today,” he said, “I learned that being helpful is a big kindness. Little paws, slow feet, and bouncy tails can all help.”

He put a small sign on the door. It said, “Helpers welcome.” He traced the letters with his finger and smiled.

“Let’s share our time. Let’s share our care,” Joey said softly.

Mimi squeaked, “Yes!”

Old Turtle Tom whispered, “Yes.”

And the garden seemed to whisper, too, with rustles and chirps, “Yes.”

The End.
