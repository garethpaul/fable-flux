# Benny's Big Building Plan

In a cozy closet hideaway behind winter coats and old shoes, lived a busy little beaver named Benny. His hideaway smelled like cedar wood and felt warm and safe. Benny loved to build things with twigs, leaves, and anything he could find.

"Today I'll build the BIGGEST dam ever!" Benny said excitedly, his brown fur bristling with energy. He grabbed armfuls of twigs and started piling them up high, higher, HIGHER!

But then... CRASH! All the twigs tumbled down like pickup sticks. Benny's ears drooped. "Oh no! My dam fell down again!"

He tried once more, building even faster. His little paws moved quickly, stacking and piling. But CRASH! Down it came again, scattering twigs everywhere.

"Building is too hard!" Benny huffed, sitting down with a thump. "I'm not good at this!"

Just then, he heard a gentle tapping sound. *Tap, tap, tap.* 

"Hello?" called a soft voice. "I'm Willow the woodpecker. I live in the coat pocket. I heard some crashes. Are you okay?"

Benny peeked out from behind a fallen boot. "I keep trying to build a big dam, but it always falls down. Maybe I should just give up."

Willow flew down and perched on a shoelace. Her red feathers gleamed in the dim closet light. "Hmm, can I see what you're doing?"

Benny showed her his pile of twigs. "I just stack them up really high and really fast!"

"I see," Willow nodded wisely. "When I build my nest, I have a special plan. Would you like to hear it?"

"Yes, please!" Benny's tail wiggled with interest.

"First, I listen to the wind to find the safest spot. Then I start small with the strongest twigs at the bottom. I weave them together carefully, one by one. And I always use what nature gives me - no waste!"

Benny's eyes grew wide. "You have a strategy! A plan!"

"Exactly!" chirped Willow. "And when something doesn't work, I try a different way. That's how I grow and learn."

Benny felt excited again. "Can you help me make a strategy?"

Together, they looked around the closet. "Listen," whispered Willow. "What do you hear?"

Benny closed his eyes and listened carefully. "I hear the air moving near the floor. It's quieter by the wall!"

"Perfect! That's the best spot," said Willow. "Now, let's start with your strongest, thickest twigs."

Benny carefully chose his biggest twigs and placed them side by side against the wall. Then he wove smaller twigs between them, just like Willow showed him. He worked slowly and thoughtfully.

"This twig is broken," Benny noticed. "I'll save it for kindling instead of throwing it away!"

"Wonderful!" said Willow. "You're thinking about sustainability - using everything wisely!"

Step by step, twig by twig, Benny built his dam. When one part wobbled, he tried a different way to weave the twigs. His dam grew stronger and more beautiful.

"Look, Willow! It's not falling down!" Benny clapped his paws together.

"That's because you used strategy, listened carefully, and didn't give up when things got tricky," Willow smiled. "Your brain grew stronger too!"

Benny admired his sturdy little dam. It wasn't the biggest dam ever, but it was perfect for his closet hideaway. "Thank you for teaching me that having a plan makes building so much better!"

From that day on, Benny always remembered: listen first, start small, make a plan, and keep trying new ways. With strategy and patience, he could build anything!

The End.
