# Colors of Sorry

In a cozy bedroom with soft pillows and a warm yellow lamp, a creative parrot named Pico fluffed his rainbow feathers. Pico loved to make art. He had a small table by the window, a basket of paper, and a set of gentle watercolor paints that smelled a little like sweet flowers. Drip, drip, drip went the brush into a cup of water. Swish, swish went the bristles on the paper.

“I will paint the morning,” Pico chirped. “Soft sun. Quiet clouds. Happy colors!”

Pico dipped his brush. Tiny feet pattered on the rug. His best friend, Mia the mouse, peeked from under the bed. She wore a tiny blue scarf. “Hi, Pico!” squeaked Mia. “Can I watch?”

“Yes! Sit on the pillow,” said Pico. He painted a round sun and a fluffy cloud. The room felt cozy and safe. The blanket smelled like clean cotton. The lamp hummed. Pico’s heart felt light.

Mia’s whiskers twitched. “I brought my crayons,” she said, hugging a small box. “I want to draw next to you.”

Pico smiled. “We can make art together!”

They worked side by side. Mia colored soft trees. Pico painted golden rays. The table was full of color. Then, Pico stretched his wing and—oh no!—his brush tipped. The water cup wobbled. Splash!

Cold water spilled over the paper. The sun and the cloud blurred. The colors ran like tiny rivers. Pico’s picture melted into swirls.

Mia jumped back. “Eek!” Her scarf got wet. A little drip landed on her drawing. “My tree!” she squeaked.

Pico’s tummy felt tight. “I didn’t mean to,” he whispered. His feathers drooped. His eyes felt prickly.

Mia pressed her lips together. She looked at her damp paper. She looked at Pico. The room was quiet except for the lamp’s low hum and the slow drip… drip… drip from the table.

Pico’s voice was small. “I’m sorry, Mia. I’m really, really sorry.”

Mia took a slow breath. She touched her wet scarf. She looked at Pico’s sad eyes. “I feel upset,” she said softly. “My tree got a drip.”

Pico nodded. “I understand. My sun got all swirly. I made a mistake.”

Mia’s whiskers relaxed. “You said sorry,” she said. “Thank you for saying sorry.”

Pico blinked. “Can you forgive me?”

Mia looked around the cozy room. The pillow was still soft. The lamp was still warm. Her friend was still her friend. “Yes,” she said. “I forgive you.”

Pico felt warmer, like a small sun in his chest. “Thank you,” he chirped gently. “How can I help?”

Mia thought. “Maybe we can fix it together.”

Pico tilted his head. “Together?”

Mia nodded. “Let’s turn the spills into something new. We are different. You like paint. I like crayons. We can mix our ideas.”

Pico’s eyes sparkled. “A swirly sky!”

“And a rainy tree,” said Mia with a tiny smile.

They smiled at each other. Pico dabbed the edges with a soft cloth. Mia patted her paper dry. They made a plan.

“Peaceful plan,” said Pico. “We share the table. We move the water cup far away.”

Mia moved the cup to the corner. “Compromise,” she giggled. “We both make space.”

Pico tapped the paper with his brush. “Drip, drip,” he sang. The swirls turned into a big, bright rainbow sky. Mia used her brown crayon to make a sturdy tree trunk and her green crayon to draw leaves that looked like little hands. Where the drip had been, she drew shiny raindrops.

“Plip-plop leaves,” Mia said.

Pico added tiny birds with pink bellies. “Tweet, tweet,” he chirped. He painted a small umbrella for the tree. Mia laughed. “Trees don’t use umbrellas!”

“In our picture, they can,” said Pico. “We are creative.”

They leaned close. They could smell the soft paint and the warm blanket. The paper felt a little bumpy where it had dried. Their hearts felt light again.

Soon, the picture was new and special. The rainbow sky curved over the rainy tree. Little birds danced. The spilled water had become sparkles.

Mia patted Pico’s wing. “I’m glad we chose peace,” she said.

Pico nodded. “I’m glad you forgave me.”

Mia twirled her scarf. “And I’m glad we are different. Your colors make my drawings brighter. My crayons make your paintings bold.”

Pico hopped. “Different is good. Different makes our art happy.”

They cleaned up together. Pico sang a soft song while he washed the brush. Swish, swish. Mia folded a dry cloth. Pat, pat. The cozy bedroom felt safe and calm again.

They placed their picture on the wall above the bed. The rainbow seemed to smile at them.

Pico stretched his wings. “What did we learn today?”

Mia raised her tiny paw. “We learned to say sorry and to forgive. Forgiveness makes our hearts soft.”

Pico nodded. “We learned peaceful solutions. We made a plan and shared.”

Mia squeaked, “We celebrated differences. We used paint and crayons.”

“And we compromised,” said Pico. “We moved the water cup and made space.”

They hugged. The lamp hummed. The soft pillows hugged them back. The room smelled like clean cotton and sweet paint. Everything felt warm.

Pico whispered, “When we make mistakes, we can say sorry, forgive, and fix things together.”

Mia smiled. “That is our bright rainbow lesson.”

The End.
