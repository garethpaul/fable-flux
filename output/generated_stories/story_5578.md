# Splash's Special Story Time

Splash the dolphin loved to jump and play in the sparkling blue ocean. But today was different. Today, Splash had been invited to visit Grandma Rose's peaceful front porch by the shore. The old wooden porch had white rocking chairs and colorful flowers in pretty pots.

"Welcome, dear Splash!" called Grandma Rose, waving from her favorite rocking chair. She had silver hair that sparkled in the sunshine and kind, twinkling eyes.

Splash swam close to the porch and poked his shiny gray head above the water. "Hello, Grandma Rose! Why did you invite me here today?"

Grandma Rose smiled warmly. "I want to share something very special with you, little one. Would you like to hear about the old traditions of the sea?"

Splash's eyes grew wide with curiosity. "Yes, please! But... what's a tradition?"

"A tradition is something special that families and friends share together, year after year," explained Grandma Rose gently. "Like a favorite song, or a special way of celebrating, or important stories."

Just then, Splash heard arguing voices from the water nearby. Two young fish, Finn and Coral, were swimming in circles, looking upset.

"That's MY favorite seaweed patch!" squeaked Finn, his little fins flapping angrily.

"No, it's MINE!" replied Coral, puffing up her cheeks. "I saw it first!"

Splash felt worried. His friends were fighting, and it made his heart feel heavy. "Grandma Rose, my friends are arguing. I want to help, but I don't know how."

Grandma Rose rocked slowly in her chair, listening to the gentle waves. "This reminds me of an old sea tradition, dear Splash. Would you like to learn about it?"

"Yes!" said Splash, swimming closer to listen.

"Long, long ago," began Grandma Rose in her soft, storytelling voice, "when sea creatures had disagreements, they would gather in a circle and share the 'Kindness Current.' Each friend would say one nice thing about the other, and soon their angry feelings would wash away like the tide."

Splash's eyes sparkled. "That sounds wonderful! But will my friends want to try it?"

"Sometimes being brave means trying something new to help others," said Grandma Rose encouragingly. "Why don't you share this tradition with them?"

Splash took a deep breath, feeling the cool ocean water fill his lungs. He swam over to his arguing friends. "Finn! Coral! I learned about a special sea tradition. Want to try it together?"

The two little fish stopped arguing and looked curious. "What kind of tradition?" asked Coral.

Splash explained about the Kindness Current, just like Grandma Rose had taught him. Soon, all three friends were swimming in a gentle circle.

"Finn, you're really good at finding the prettiest shells," said Coral sweetly.

"And Coral, you always share your favorite sea berries with everyone," replied Finn, smiling.

As they spoke kind words, their angry feelings melted away like sea foam. They decided to share the seaweed patch and play together instead.

"Thank you for teaching me that tradition, Grandma Rose!" called Splash happily. "It helped my friends feel better!"

Grandma Rose clapped her hands with joy. "Traditions are gifts we share with others, dear Splash. When we pass them on, they help make the world a kinder, more peaceful place."

As the sun began to set, painting the sky in soft pinks and oranges, Splash felt proud and happy. He had learned that sharing traditions brings friends together and helps solve problems with kindness.

The End.
