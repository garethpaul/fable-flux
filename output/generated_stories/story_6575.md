# Poppy Penguin’s Helpful Music Day

Poppy was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a wiggly walk. Every morning, Poppy followed a happy routine. First, a big stretch. Then, a little wiggle. Then, she splashed her flippers in a bowl of warm water, dab-dab-dab. “Good morning, day!” Poppy chirped.

Today, Poppy waddled to the musical practice room. The room smelled like fresh wood and lemon polish. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. Drums sat like round muffins. A shiny triangle hung on a hook. A piano smiled with white and black teeth. The room was quiet, like it was waiting.

Poppy clapped. Clap, clap! “Hello, friends!” she said to the instruments.

“Ping!” went the triangle, as if to say hello back.

“Boom-boom,” whispered a small drum when Poppy tapped it gently.

Poppy loved to move to music. She hopped. She twirled. She slid on her little penguin feet. “Slide to the side, glide-glide-glide!” she sang in a soft voice.

Poppy looked around. Uh-oh. There were crinkly cups on the floor by the drum. There were paper bits by the piano. A cardboard box sat open, sleepy and empty. The trash can was full. The recycling bin was half-open.

Poppy blinked. “This room needs help,” she said. Her heart felt warm. Helping made Poppy happy.

“Can I help?” Poppy asked the room.

The triangle gave a hopeful “Ping!”

Poppy smiled. “Yes! I can help. I can tidy. I can recycle. I can follow my routine. Clean, then play!”

Poppy started her helpful routine song. “Pick up, sort out, tidy-tidy-tidy,” she sang, swaying her body. She picked up a plastic cup. It was smooth and cool. “You go in recycling,” she said kindly. She waddled to the blue bin. Plop!

She found a crinkly paper flyer. It felt dry and light. “You are paper. You go in recycling too.” Flutter! In it went.

She found a banana peel. It was squishy and a little smelly. “You are trash,” Poppy said, nose wiggling. She dropped it in the trash can. Thunk!

“Good job, Poppy,” she told herself. “Helping is kind. Helping makes things safe.”

Poppy made it a game. “Tap-tap, sort and clap!” She tapped her flippers on her tummy after each piece. Tap-tap! Clap-clap! She moved side to side as she worked, like a tiny dance. Her feet made whisper sounds on the floor. Swish, swish.

Soon, she saw her friend, Mr. Metronome, on the shelf. He was a small wooden metronome with a little swinging arm. “Tick-tock,” he said when Poppy touched him.

“Hello, Mr. Metronome,” said Poppy. “Can you help me keep a clean-up beat?”

“Tick-tock, tick-tock,” went Mr. Metronome, steady and calm.

Poppy bobbed to the sound. “Pick up on the tick. Sort on the tock,” she said. She picked up a shiny bottle cap. Tink! It made a tiny sound. “Metal,” she said. She put it in the right bin. Tock!

She picked up a small cardboard tube. It was light and hollow. “Paper,” she said, peeking through it like a telescope. “Hello!” Her voice echoed. She giggled and placed it in recycling. Tick!

Poppy checked the floor. She found a ribbon. “You can be used again,” she said. She wrapped it around the empty box like a bow. “We can use this box to carry music sheets.” She wrote a label with a fat blue marker. The marker smelled a little like berries. She wrote slowly, “Music Papers.”

Just then, Ms. Melody the music teacher popped her head in. She wore bright green shoes that squeaked softly. “Good morning, Poppy!” she said. “Oh! The room looks wonderful. Did you help?”

Poppy stood tall. “Yes! I sorted recycling. I put trash in the trash. I made a box for music papers. I used my helpful routine: pick up, sort out, tidy-tidy-tidy!”

Ms. Melody smiled. “Thank you, Poppy. Helping makes our room safe and happy. You are a star helper.”

Poppy’s cheeks felt warm and proud. “Helping feels good,” she said.

Ms. Melody clapped a gentle rhythm. Clap, clap, clap. “Let’s make music to celebrate. Can you lead a movement song?”

Poppy nodded. “Yes!” She tapped the small drum. Boom-boom. She rang the triangle. Ping! She pointed to Mr. Metronome. Tick-tock, tick-tock.

“Follow me,” Poppy sang. “Slide to the side.” The children who came in slid with soft shuffles. “Glide and stop.” Everyone froze. Giggle-giggle! “Pick up, sort out.” The children pretended to pick up cups and drop them into bins. Plop! “Clap and sway.” Clap-clap, sway-sway.

The room filled with happy sounds. Drums thumped like tiny heartbeats. The triangle chimed like a star. Little shoes squeaked. Little hands clapped. Smiles grew wide.

After the song, Poppy said, “Helping is our habit. We can do it every day.”

Ms. Melody nodded. “Yes. Our routine is: play, clean, recycle, and say thank you.”

Everyone said together, “Thank you!”

Poppy looked around the bright, tidy room. The floor was clear. The bins were neat. The instruments seemed to shine. Poppy felt calm and ready.

She gave the triangle one last kiss of sound. “Ping!”

“Today we learned,” Poppy said, “that helping is kind, routines make things easy, and recycling takes care of our world.”

Ms. Melody added, “When we all help, we all smile.”

Poppy wiggled with joy. “I can help every day!”

And that is just what she did.

The End.
