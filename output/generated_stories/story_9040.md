# Polly Plans a World of Care

Polly the parrot loved colors and making things. She had bright green feathers, a sunshine-yellow chest, and a beak the color of warm cocoa. Today, Polly fluffed her wings and looked around the cozy guest room. It smelled like clean sheets and lemons. A small window let in soft light. A big bed with a rainbow blanket sat in the middle. On a low table, there were crayons, paper, stickers, and a little globe that could spin.

“Squawk! Today is a planning day,” Polly chirped. “I want to help our big world.”

She hopped from the back of a chair to the table, her claws clicking softly. Click, click, click. She peered at the spinning globe. It showed blue water and green land. She tapped it gently with her beak. Spin, spin, spin. The globe slowed. Polly saw tiny pictures: a dolphin, a tree, a snowflake, and a house.

“Friends all over,” Polly said. “We share one big home.”

She spread a sheet of paper and drew a big circle with a crayon. The crayon smelled waxy and sweet. “This is Earth,” she said. “We can plan to care.”

Just then, the door creaked. Creak! The guest room always felt safe and quiet, but today it felt busy and bright. A small breeze brushed the white curtains. Polly flapped her wings. “Movement time helps thinking!” she chirped, hopping in a little circle. Tap, hop, flap!

A tiny voice squeaked from the window. It was Miko the mouse, peeking in. “Hello, Polly! What are you doing?”

Polly smiled. “Hi, Miko! I’m planning. We can be global citizens. That means we care for people and places all over the world.”

Miko’s whiskers wiggled. “Even far away?”

“Yes,” said Polly. “We are all neighbors. We share water, air, and kindness.”

Miko climbed onto the windowsill. “How do we start?”

Polly made a list with big, simple pictures. “Step one: Learn. Step two: Plan. Step three: Move and do!”

She drew a sun for learning. “We listen and look. We learn about others.” She drew a heart for planning. “We make a kind plan.” She drew two footprints for doing. “We move our bodies and help.”

Miko clapped his tiny paws. “Let’s learn!”

Polly spun the globe again. It stopped near a blue area. “The ocean,” Polly said softly. She closed her eyes and imagined waves. Shhh… whoosh… She could almost smell salty water. “Some friends throw trash away. Some trash goes to the water. That is not nice for fish.”

Miko nodded. “We can keep water clean.”

Polly drew a little fish with a smile. Then she moved her wing and spun the globe again. It stopped at a green area. “A forest,” she said. She imagined trees whispering. Hush, hush. She smelled pine and dirt. “Trees are homes. We can save paper and plant more trees.”

Miko sniffed the crayon. “Paper comes from trees?”

“Yes,” said Polly. “We use what we need. We share. We do not waste.”

Polly’s eyes shone. “Now we plan.” She made a big map on the paper with simple shapes: a blue circle for water, a green rectangle for trees, and a little house for home. She added tiny stickers: a bottle with a red X, a tree with a smiling face, and hands holding a heart.

“Today,” Polly said, “we will do three things. One: pick up tiny bits of paper in the guest room. Two: make a toy from a snack box. Three: call friends and share our plan.”

Miko squeaked, “We can do it!”

They moved together across the room. Polly flapped slowly to the floor. Swish, swish. Miko scurried. Scurry, scurry. They found a crinkly wrapper under the chair. “Aha!” said Polly. “Trash belongs in the bin.” She dropped it in. Plunk! The room felt cleaner and bright.

Next, Polly took an empty snack box from the table. It smelled faintly of crackers. She snipped the edges with tiny, safe scissors. Snip, snip. She colored it with blue and green and added wing-shaped stickers. “Look, Miko! It’s a mini-recycling truck!” she laughed.

Miko pushed the little box truck. “Vroom, vroom! We can use it again and again.”

They danced a tiny celebration dance. Polly flapped. Miko wiggled. “Movement helps our hearts feel strong,” Polly said. “And it is fun!”

Then Polly pressed a button on a small toy phone. Beep! “Hello, Poppy Panda,” she said. “We are global citizens. Today we cleaned, reused, and shared. You can try too!”

Through the speaker came a gentle rumble. “Thank you, Polly. I will tell my forest friends.”

Polly smiled. She called Tiko Turtle next. “We will keep our beach tidy,” said Tiko’s slow voice.

Polly’s feathers felt warm with pride. “We are all part of one big team,” she told Miko. “We learn, plan, and move. We help near and far.”

The guest room felt extra cozy now. The air smelled like crayons and lemon. The rainbow blanket looked like a tiny map of colors. Polly pinned her plan on the wall with a soft tack. Tap. It showed the sun, the heart, and the footprints.

Miko yawned. “We did a lot today.”

Polly nodded. “Small steps help the big world,” she said. “We can do more tomorrow.”

They stood by the window. Outside, the sky turned peach and gold. Polly held the globe gently and whispered, “We care for you.”

Then she turned to Miko. “What did we learn?”

Miko squeaked proudly, “We are global citizens. We learn about others. We plan with care. We move and help. We do not waste. We share kindness.”

Polly’s eyes sparkled. “Yes,” she said. “When we care for our world, we care for each other.”

The End.
