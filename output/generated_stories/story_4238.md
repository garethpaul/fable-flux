# Pebble's Truth in the Guest Room

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a tiny orange beak. Pebble loved to tell stories and waddle fast. One sunny afternoon, Pebble visited a cozy guest room at Auntie Seal’s house. The guest room smelled like fresh soap and sweet lemon. Soft blankets lay on the bed. A little lamp made warm, golden light. A bowl of shiny shells sat by the window.

Pebble’s best friend, Lulu the little puffin, hopped in too. “Hi, Pebble!” Lulu chirped. “Let’s play and make stories!”

Pebble clapped flippers. “Yes! Let’s tell a story about brave friends.”

Auntie Seal peeked in, smiling. “You two can play in the guest room,” she said. “But please be careful with my special things.”

Pebble nodded. “We will, Auntie! We will be gentle.”

They started a story game. Pebble made a deep voice. “Once upon a time, a penguin knight found a magic shell!”

Lulu squeaked and flapped. “And the puffin princess sang a happy song!”

They laughed and twirled. Pebble picked up a shiny shell from the bowl. It felt cool and smooth. Pebble held it like a treasure. “This shell says, ‘Be brave!’”

As Pebble waddled, the shell slipped. Clink! It bumped another shell. Crack. A tiny chip fell to the rug. Pebble froze. Lulu’s eyes grew round.

“Oh no,” Pebble whispered. “I chipped Auntie’s shell.”

Lulu leaned close. “Are you okay?”

Pebble’s tummy felt tight. Pebble’s face felt warm. “I’m scared,” Pebble said softly. “What if Auntie is mad?”

Lulu tilted her head. “We can tell the truth,” she said. “Honesty is brave.”

Pebble looked at the little chip. It was white and chalky. The shiny shell had a small bite missing. Pebble wanted to hide it. Pebble wanted to say, “It was not me.”

Pebble took a deep breath. In, out. Pebble remembered: Honesty keeps hearts safe. Pebble touched the shell gently. “I did it,” Pebble said. “I need to tell Auntie.”

“But first,” Lulu said, “let’s be kind to ourselves.” She brought a soft pillow and set it near Pebble. “Sit and take one more breath.”

Pebble sat on the pillow. It felt squishy and warm. Pebble rubbed flippers together and took another breath. In. Out. “Okay,” Pebble said. “I’m ready.”

Auntie Seal came back with a plate of orange slices. The room smelled bright and sweet. “Snack time,” she said. “Mmm, citrus!”

Pebble waddled forward. Pebble’s voice was small but steady. “Auntie, I have to tell you something.” Pebble held up the shell. “I was playing. The shell slipped. It chipped. I’m sorry. I was not careful.”

The room was quiet. Pebble’s heart thumped like a drum. Lub-dub. Lub-dub.

Auntie knelt and looked at the shell. She looked at Pebble’s eyes. “Thank you for telling me the truth,” she said gently. “You were honest. That is very brave.”

Pebble blinked. “You’re… not mad?”

“I feel a little sad about the shell,” Auntie said. “But I feel proud of you. Honesty helps us trust. And we can fix small things together.”

Lulu chirped, “We can help!”

Auntie smiled. “Let’s make the chip smooth.” She brought a tiny cloth. Together they rubbed the shell. Shhh, shhh. It felt silky again. The chip spot still showed, like a little moon. Auntie put the shell back in the bowl. “It has a story now,” she said. “A story of truth and care.”

Pebble’s tummy felt calm. “Auntie,” Pebble said, “can I make it up to you?”

“That is kind,” Auntie said. “How would you like to help?”

Pebble thought. “I can be gentle with the guest room. I can share my favorite pebbles for the bowl. And I can help clean.”

Lulu clapped. “And we can tell a thank-you story!”

They washed their flippers with warm, soapy water. The bubbles smelled like lavender. Pebble hummed a soft song. Self-care made Pebble feel safe and strong. Then they tidied the blankets. They dusted the lamp. Pebble carefully placed three smooth pebbles—pink, gray, and blue—into the shell bowl. “These are for you,” Pebble said.

“Thank you,” Auntie beamed. “That is generous.”

Pebble and Lulu made a circle on the rug. Pebble told a new story. “Once, a brave penguin told the truth. The penguin felt scared, but honesty made the penguin strong.”

Lulu added, “And friends helped. And Auntie forgave.”

Auntie Seal hugged them both. Her fur felt soft, like a warm blanket. “Honesty, self-care, and generosity,” she said. “These are good friends in our hearts.”

Pebble smiled big. “I will always try to tell the truth. I will take care of myself and others. I will share what I can.”

They ate the juicy orange slices. Drip, drip, sweet and sticky on their beaks. They laughed, and the guest room glowed with happy light.

The End.
