# Millie Mouse and the Cozy Guest Room

Millie was a kind little mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, she tip-tapped into the cozy guest room. The rug was fluffy like a cloud. The curtains were yellow and smelled like lemon soap. A small window let in warm light, and dust sparkles danced in the air.

“Good morning, guest room,” Millie squeaked. “I will be gentle and kind today.”

On the bedside table sat a shiny red button toy. It made happy “boop!” sounds when pressed. Next to it was a blue picture book with smooth pages. There was also a tiny basket of wooden blocks that made a friendly clack-clack when moved.

Millie’s whiskers wiggled. “I want to try the red button,” she said. She reached out a tiny paw.

Just then, her friends peeked in. Benny Beetle clicked his little feet. “Hi, Millie!” he buzzed. Lila Ladybug fluttered her red wings with black polka dots. “We want to play too!”

Millie paused and took a slow, mindful breath in and out. She listened to the soft hum of the window fan and felt the warm sun on her back. “We can take turns,” she said. “First, we choose. Then we wait with calm bodies.”

Benny pointed to the blue book. “I want to read!” Lila smiled at the blocks. “I want to build!” Millie nodded. “I want the red button. Let’s line up our turns.”

They agreed: first Millie with the red button, then Lila with the blocks, and Benny would read the book while he waited. After that, they would switch.

Millie pressed the button once. Boop! It sounded cheerful. She pressed it twice. Boop! Boop! Her heart felt happy. “This is fun,” she said.

Benny began to read the book softly. “Look, a moon and stars,” he said, tracing a picture. Lila stacked two blocks. Clack. Clack. “I am making a tiny tower,” she whispered.

Soon, Millie noticed a small note beside the button toy. The letters were big and neat. Millie sounded out the words. “Please ask before you play. —From Nana Mouse.”

Millie’s ears drooped. “Oh,” she said. “I did not ask. I pressed the button without asking.”

Lila looked up. “It’s okay, Millie. You didn’t see the note before.” Benny nodded. “Now you know.”

Millie touched her heart. It felt a little heavy. “I want to do the right thing,” she said. “Integrity means telling the truth and fixing my mistake.”

She took another slow breath. In, and out. She felt the rug under her paws. She heard the fan spin. She smelled the lemon curtains. Then she called, “Nana Mouse?”

Nana Mouse poked her head in the door. Her eyes were gentle. “Yes, Millie?”

“I used the red button without asking,” Millie said, looking up. “I am sorry. I saw your note now. Next time I will ask first. May we please play with the toys in the guest room?”

Nana smiled warmly. “Thank you for your honesty, Millie. That is integrity. You told the truth right away and tried to make it right.” She patted Millie’s head. “Yes, you may play, as long as you are careful and take turns.”

Millie’s heart felt light again. “We are taking turns,” she said proudly. “I had one turn. Now I will pass it.”

She handed the red button toy to Lila. “Your turn.” Lila’s wings fluttered. “Thank you, Millie.” Boop! The toy chirped.

Benny finished a page and closed the book gently. “My turn to build?” he asked. Lila nodded and passed the blocks. Clack, clack. Benny stacked them into a tiny bridge.

Millie sat on the fluffy rug and waited, breathing slow and calm. She looked at the sun squares on the floor. She listened to the happy boops and clacks. She whispered, “I can wait with a quiet body. I can wait with kind thoughts.” Her tail curled like a little question mark.

When the red button came back to Millie, she pressed it once, then stopped. “I had a turn,” she said. “Now I pass again.” She smiled at her friends.

Nana Mouse watched from the doorway. “I see honesty. I see mindful waiting. I see sharing and taking turns,” she said. “You are making the guest room feel safe and happy.”

Millie beamed. “Integrity feels good inside,” she said. “It is like warm sunshine in my tummy.”

As the morning ended, they put the toys back. The red button sat on the table. The blue book lay smooth and neat. The blocks rested in the basket. Millie read the note one more time. “Please ask before you play.” She nodded. “I will remember.”

“Thank you, guest room,” Millie whispered. “Thank you, friends.” The room smelled like lemon and felt calm and cozy.

Nana Mouse gave them tiny oatmeal cookies. They were warm and sweet. “One at a time,” said Nana. Millie took one, then passed the plate.

Benny crunched. Lila nibbled. Millie savored hers slowly. “Mmm,” she said. “Taking turns tastes nice, too.”

They all giggled.

The End.
