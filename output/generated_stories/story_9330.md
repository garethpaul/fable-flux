# Fawn Builds a Brave Bridge

In the busy basement, lights buzzed softly. Fans whirred. The washing machine hummed and swished. Boxes were stacked like little mountains. It smelled a bit like soap and warm socks.

A gentle deer fawn named Fawn tiptoed down the steps. Her hooves made tiny clack-clack sounds on the wooden stairs. She wore a small tool belt made from ribbon and buttons. It jiggled when she moved.

“I’m going to build something big,” Fawn said to herself. “I want to build a bridge!”

Her heart beat a fast, excited thump-thump. She looked around. There were old cardboard tubes, smooth wooden blocks, soft towels, and colorful tape. There were also two friends. Squeak the mouse peeked from a paint can. Clonk the turtle stood near a toolbox, slow and steady.

“Hi, Fawn!” squeaked Squeak. “What are you building?”

“A bridge,” said Fawn, lifting her chin. “A strong, safe bridge for rolling marbles. It will go from this box to that chair.”

Clonk blinked. “That is big,” he said. “Do you feel ready?”

Fawn took a deep breath in. She felt the cool basement air tickle her nose. She let the breath out slowly. “I think I am,” she said. “I will try.”

They got to work. Fawn set the wooden blocks in a line. Clack. Clack. Clack. She placed a cardboard tube across two blocks. It wobbled.

“Oh!” Fawn said, stepping back. “It’s shaky.”

Squeak tugged a towel to make a soft mat. “If it falls, it won’t crash,” he said.

“Thank you,” said Fawn. Her shoulders relaxed. She tried again. She pressed one block down. She tapped another block gently with her hoof. She added a strip of colorful tape. Rip! Stick!

Then came a loud whir from the dryer. Fawn’s ears twitched. Her tummy did a tiny flip-flop. The bridge wiggled and toppled over. Plunk! Plonk! Pieces rolled everywhere.

“I messed up,” Fawn whispered. Her eyes felt watery. “Maybe I can’t build a bridge.”

Clonk stepped closer. “Building takes time,” he said in his slow, warm voice. “We can try again.”

Squeak nodded fast. “We can make a plan! We can build step by step.”

Fawn sniffled and wiped her eyes with the edge of her ribbon belt. “Okay,” she said softly. “A plan sounds good.”

She took another deep breath. In. Out. She put her hoof on her heart. Thump. Thump. “I am calm,” she said. “I can try.”

They made a plan:

- Step 1: Make a strong base.
- Step 2: Add supports.
- Step 3: Test and fix.
- Step 4: Roll marbles and cheer!

“Step 1!” Fawn said. She found the biggest blocks and set them wide apart. “A wide base,” she explained. “Like a strong tree trunk.”

“Step 2!” Squeak squeaked. He held small blocks like tiny pillars. Clonk gently pressed them in place. Fawn taped the cardboard tube on top. Rip! Press! Smooth!

The washer beeped. Beep-beep! The bridge trembled—but did not fall.

Fawn smiled a little. “Step 3,” she said. “We test.” She nudged the tube. It wiggled. The left side dipped low.

Squeak pointed. “That side needs help.”

Clonk offered a washer as a weight. “We can balance it,” he said. Fawn added the washer with a bit of tape. She slid a folded towel under the low spot.

“Try again,” Fawn said.

She rolled one shiny marble. Click… click… swish! The marble rolled across and dropped into a soft bowl with a gentle plop.

Fawn’s eyes lit up. “It worked!” she cried.

“Let’s roll two!” Squeak cheered.

They rolled two marbles. Then three. Then four! The bridge held steady. The marbles sang a tiny song as they rolled: clickety-swish, clickety-swish.

Just then, a small conflict bubbled up. Squeak wanted to add glitter tape. Clonk wanted to add more blocks. They both talked at the same time.

“Glitter!” squeaked Squeak.

“More blocks,” said Clonk, firm but kind.

Fawn raised a hoof. “Let’s take turns,” she said. “We all have ideas. We can listen.”

She looked at Squeak. “Tell us why glitter is good.”

Squeak’s whiskers twitched. “Glitter tape makes the bridge easy to see. It’s bright!”

Fawn nodded. She turned to Clonk. “Tell us why more blocks are good.”

Clonk tapped the bridge. “More blocks make it safer and strong.”

Fawn thought. “We can do both,” she said. “First, we add two blocks. Then we add glitter tape.”

Squeak and Clonk smiled. “Good plan,” they said together.

They added the blocks. They added a shiny strip of glitter tape that sparkled like tiny stars under the basement lights. The bridge looked bright and felt strong.

Fawn stood tall. Her heart felt warm. “We built it,” she said. “We made a plan, we took turns, and we stayed calm.”

She rolled the last marble. It zipped across the bridge and chimed against the bowl. Ting!

“I did it,” Fawn whispered. “I can build.”

Squeak clapped his tiny paws. Clonk nodded. “You built with care,” he said. “You built with friends.”

Fawn smiled big. “When something wobbles,” she said, “I can breathe, plan, and try again.”

The basement hummed softly. The bridge shone. The friends rested on the towel mat, sipping warm apple water from tiny cups.

Fawn felt brave, strong, and well. Her body felt calm. Her mind felt clear. Her heart felt happy.

“We can build again tomorrow,” she said.

“Yes,” said Squeak.

“Yes,” said Clonk.

The End.
