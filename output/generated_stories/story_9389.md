# Rosie Rabbit's Art Show Surprise

Rosie Rabbit hopped excitedly into Grandpa's garage workshop. Tomorrow was the big art show at school, and she wanted to make something special. The garage smelled like wood and paint. Tools hung neatly on the walls.

"I'll make the most beautiful painting ever!" Rosie said, thumping her foot happily.

She grabbed a big brush and dipped it in bright red paint. Splash! She painted a wobbly circle. Then she reached for blue paint without cleaning her brush first.

"Oops!" The colors mixed into muddy brown. Her sun looked like a dirty puddle.

Rosie's ears drooped. She tried again quickly. This time she grabbed yellow paint and made quick strokes across the paper. But the paint dripped everywhere because she used too much.

"This isn't working!" Rosie cried. Paint covered her paws and the workbench. Even her whiskers had purple spots.

Just then, Grandpa Bunny hopped into the garage. His eyes grew wide when he saw the colorful mess.

"Oh my carrots!" he exclaimed. "What happened here, Rosie?"

Rosie's ears hung low. "I'm sorry, Grandpa. I wanted to make something beautiful for the art show. But everything went wrong!"

Grandpa's voice was gentle but firm. "Rosie, I can see you're excited about your art. But good artwork takes preparation. You can't rush creativity."

"Preparation?" Rosie asked, tilting her head.

"Yes," Grandpa smiled. "First, we clean up this mess together. Then I'll show you how real artists prepare."

They wiped paint from the workbench and washed Rosie's paws in warm, soapy water. The garage began to smell fresh again.

"Now," said Grandpa, "let's gather our supplies properly."

He showed Rosie how to lay out newspaper to protect the table. They arranged small containers of water for cleaning brushes. Grandpa gave her different sized brushes for different jobs.

"Always clean your brush before changing colors," he explained. "And test your paint on scrap paper first."

Rosie nodded eagerly. This time, she dipped her brush in water, then gently touched it to yellow paint. She made a small test mark. Perfect!

Slowly and carefully, she painted a bright yellow sun. She cleaned her brush thoroughly, then added blue for the sky. The colors stayed clean and bright.

"This is much better!" Rosie said proudly.

She painted fluffy white clouds and green grass. With a tiny brush, she added red flowers that looked like little hearts. Each step was careful and planned.

"Grandpa, look!" Rosie called out. "I made something beautiful!"

Grandpa hopped over and gasped with delight. "Rosie, this is wonderful! You took your time and prepared properly. That made all the difference."

Rosie beamed with pride. "I was so excited that I forgot to prepare. But when I slowed down and got ready first, everything worked perfectly!"

The next day at the art show, Rosie's painting won a special ribbon for "Most Improved Artist." She stood tall and proud next to her artwork.

When her friend Benny asked how she made such a beautiful painting, Rosie spoke up clearly: "I learned that preparation is the secret to good art. You have to get ready first, then take your time."

That evening, Rosie carefully cleaned all the brushes and put the art supplies away neatly in Grandpa's workshop.

"Thank you for teaching me about preparation, Grandpa," she said, giving him a big hug.

"You're welcome, little artist," Grandpa replied. "Remember, whether it's art or anything else, taking time to prepare helps you do your very best."

Rosie nodded wisely. She had learned that rushing leads to mistakes, but preparation leads to success.

The End.
