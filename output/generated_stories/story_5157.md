# Ella's Quiet Attic Calm-Down

The old stairs creaked as Ella, a gentle elephant calf, climbed up to the quiet attic. The air smelled like warm wood and lavender. Dust sparkles floated in the sunbeams. “Oooh,” Ella whispered. “It’s cozy up here.”

Mama Elephant smiled and set down a soft basket. Inside were big puzzle pieces, wooden blocks, and a small timer that tick-tick-ticked. “This is our quiet attic time,” Mama said. “We take turns, we try new skills, and we practice calm bodies.”

Ella’s big ears wiggled. “I like quiet,” she said. Her voice sounded soft in the fluffy attic. Outside, a light rain pattered on the roof. Tap, tap, tap. It felt safe.

Soon, friends climbed the stairs too. Tavi the tiny turtle carried a paintbrush. Mina the mouse rolled a yarn ball. Jax the jaybird fluttered to the window ledge. “We are all here,” said Mama. “We will build a block bridge together. We will take turns. We will use gentle words.”

Ella’s heart felt warm. “I can do that,” she said.

Mama turned the small timer. Tick tick tick. “First turn: Ella places one block.”

Ella picked up a smooth block. It felt cool in her trunk. She set it down carefully. Clack. “Your turn, Tavi,” she said, smiling.

Tavi nudged a block into place. Mina added a tiny block like a button. Jax placed a long block like a plank. Clack, clack, clack. The bridge grew taller.

But then, the tall block Ella wanted was in the middle of the pile. She reached for it. Mina reached for it too. Their paws and trunk touched. The block wobbled. The bridge leaned. “Uh-oh!” said Jax.

Ella’s cheeks felt hot. Her tummy felt twisty. Her ears got stiff. “I wanted that block!” she blurted. Her foot stomped. Thump. The bridge trembled and a few blocks fell. Clatter clatter.

Mina’s whiskers drooped. Tavi pulled into his shell. Jax fluttered back. The attic felt not-quiet now. Ella’s eyes got wet. “I don’t like this feeling,” she sniffed. “I feel mad.”

Mama Elephant walked slowly closer. Her steps were soft. “Thank you for telling me,” Mama said. “Feelings are okay. We can help your body feel safe.”

Ella nodded, sniffling. Mama pointed to a small rug with a picture of a cloud. “This is our calm cloud. Let’s try our calm plan.”

“What is the calm plan?” Ella asked, voice wobbly.

Mama held up her trunk. “First: Stop and spot. Say, ‘I feel mad.’ Second: Breathe like an elephant. In slow. Out slow. Third: Squeeze and release. Hands, toes, whole body gentle. Fourth: Use words to ask or take turns.”

Ella took a step onto the cloud rug. The rug felt soft and squishy on her feet. She put her trunk to her chest. “I feel mad,” she said. A tear fell, warm on her cheek.

“Good spotting,” Mama said. “Now breathe.”

Ella breathed in through her trunk. Whooo. She breathed out slow. Hooo. The rain on the roof helped her count. Tap, tap, tap. She did it again. Whooo. Hooo. Her ears relaxed.

“Now squeeze and release.” Mama showed her. Squeeze hands. Let go. Squeeze shoulders. Let go. Squeeze toes. Let go.

Ella copied. Squeeze. Release. Squeeze. Release. Her tummy unknot. The hot feeling cooled, like shade under a big tree.

“How do you feel now?” Mama asked.

“Calmer,” Ella said. Her voice was small but steady.

Mama nodded. “Now use your words.”

Ella turned to Mina. “I am sorry for stomping,” she said. “I felt mad. I wanted the tall block. Can we take turns? Can I have it after you?”

Mina’s whiskers perked. “Thank you for asking,” Mina said. “Yes. I can go first, and then you. We can share.”

Tavi peeked out of his shell. “I can help hold the bridge,” he said softly. Jax fluttered closer. “I can count turns!”

Mama set the timer again. “Mina’s turn,” she said. Tick tick tick. Mina placed the tall block. Clack. The timer dinged. “Ella’s turn,” said Jax. Ding!

Ella felt ready. She lifted another tall block. It felt heavy and smooth. “I breathe in,” she whispered to herself. Whooo. “I breathe out.” Hooo. She set the block down gently. Clack. The bridge stood strong.

Together they kept building. They took turns. They used words. Tavi steadied the blocks with slow, steady hands. Jax counted in a sing-song voice. “One turn, two turns, three turns!” The attic filled with happy sounds: clack of wood, tick of the timer, patter of rain, and soft giggles.

When the bridge was done, it reached from one sunny window to a pile of storybooks. “We did it!” Ella cheered. Her heart felt big and light.

Mama smiled. “How did you do it?”

Ella tapped her chest. “I used my calm plan. I said my feeling. I breathed like an elephant. I squeezed and let go. I used my words. And we took turns.”

Mina nodded. “And we included everyone!”

Tavi lifted a tiny flag. “Team Bridge!”

Jax fluffed his feathers. “Skill-building success!”

Ella looked at her friends. “When I feel mad again,” she said, “I can stop, breathe, squeeze, and use words. I can wait for my turn.”

Mama patted Ella’s head. “That is a strong skill, little one.”

Ella listened. The rain had slowed to soft drips. The attic felt quiet and warm again. She smiled and touched the bridge. It felt smooth and safe, like a promise.

The End.
