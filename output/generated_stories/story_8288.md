# Squeaky Squirrel’s Calm-Down Plan

In a cozy bedroom with soft pillows and a night-light that glowed like a tiny moon, a resourceful squirrel named Squeaky lived in a big toy basket. Squeaky had bright eyes, a fluffy tail, and tiny paws that could fix almost anything. The room smelled like clean blankets and cinnamon soap. The window hummed with a gentle breeze, and a clock tick-tocked like a friendly heartbeat.

Every afternoon, Squeaky played with Luna, the child who lived there. Today, Luna whispered, “Let’s build a pillow fort.” Squeaky’s tail swished with joy. “Yes! Let’s make a fort with a snack shelf!” he chirped.

They stacked pillows high and pulled a blanket over the top. The blanket felt warm and smooth. Squeaky arranged a little row of toy acorns. “Snack shelf ready!” he said proudly.

Then Luna rolled a red toy car toward Squeaky. “Your turn,” she said.

Squeaky loved the red car. He held it, feeling the cool, shiny plastic in his paws. “Vroom,” he squeaked, making soft engine sounds. He zoomed it along the blanket road.

After a minute, Luna reached for the car. “My turn now,” she said.

Squeaky’s heart thumped. He wanted more time. His tail bristled up. “No! Mine!” he squealed, hugging the car. His face felt hot. His tummy felt tight. He felt a big, stormy feeling. Anger.

Squeaky stomped his little feet. The fort wobbled. “I’m not done!” he shouted.

Luna paused. She looked surprised. The room seemed too quiet, except for the tick-tock of the clock and Squeaky’s fast breaths.

Squeaky’s paws shook. He remembered something he had taped to the wall of the fort: a tiny Calm-Down Plan he had drawn with crayon. It had three pictures. Picture 1: a leaf. Picture 2: a glass of water. Picture 3: two paws talking.

Squeaky peeked at the plan. “I can be resourceful,” he told himself. “I can use my plan.”

He looked at the leaf picture. “Step one: Leaf Breaths.”

Squeaky sat down, put the car beside him, and breathed in slowly like he was smelling a fresh leaf. He breathed out slowly like he was blowing it across a pond. In… and out. In… and out. His tail smoothed down. His tummy untied its knot a little.

He looked at the water picture. “Step two: Sip-sip.” He reached for his little water cup on the shelf. He took tiny sips. The water felt cool, like a gentle rain. Swish, gulp. His face cooled, too.

He looked at the two paws picture. “Step three: Use my words.” Squeaky turned to Luna. His voice was small but steady. “I feel mad,” he said. “I’m not ready to give the car yet.”

Luna nodded. “Thanks for telling me,” she said softly. “I want a turn, too.”

Squeaky thought. He was good at fixing things. He could fix this, too. “Let’s make a plan,” he said. “We can take turns.”

“How?” asked Luna.

Squeaky listened to the clock. Tick-tock. “We can use ten tick-tocks,” he said. “I drive for ten ticks. Then you drive for ten ticks. Deal?”

Luna smiled. “Deal!” They bumped fists. It felt friendly and firm.

Squeaky picked up the car. He counted with the clock. “One tick… two tick…” His voice was calm now. The counting felt like a gentle song. When he reached “ten tick,” he handed the car to Luna. “Your turn.”

Luna grinned and made the car go “Beep-beep!” She counted, too. The wheels made a quiet whirr on the blanket. The fort felt steady again.

Soon, they were both laughing. They added a second toy car, a blue one, and made a tiny garage from two books. “We can park here,” Squeaky said. “We can also share the road.”

When Luna’s ten ticks ended, she handed the red car back. “Your turn,” she said, smiling.

Squeaky’s chest felt warm and proud. He had been angry, but he didn’t let the anger be the boss. He used his Calm-Down Plan. He used his words. He made a fair plan for taking turns.

They played until the night-light glowed brighter. Then it was time to tidy up. Squeaky stretched. “Healthy habit time,” he said. “We put toys away before bed.” The soft blanket felt cozy as they folded it. The pillows went back with a gentle whoosh. The cars clicked into their box.

Luna yawned. “Thanks for the fun,” she said.

Squeaky patted the little Calm-Down Plan on the fort wall. “Thanks, plan,” he whispered. He looked at Luna. “If we get mad again, we can breathe, sip, and talk. We can make a plan and take turns.”

Luna nodded. “We can be good problem solvers.”

Squeaky’s tail curled like a comma. He felt peaceful. The clock went tick-tock, tick-tock, like a lullaby. The room smelled like soap and a little like cinnamon. The night-light made everything soft and golden.

“Good night, Luna,” Squeaky said.

“Good night, Squeaky,” she replied.

And in the cozy bedroom, two friends rested, knowing they could handle big feelings with calm breaths, kind words, and fair turns.

The End.
