# Pippin's Tower of Truth

Pippin the penguin waddled into the dining room, his orange flippers flapping with excitement. The morning sun streamed through the big window, making everything look golden and warm. Today felt like a perfect day for something special!

"Good morning, Mama!" Pippin chirped, sliding across the smooth wooden floor on his belly. His black and white feathers gleamed in the sunlight.

"Good morning, my little explorer," Mama Penguin smiled, setting down a stack of colorful blocks on the dining table. "I have a special job for you today. Can you help me organize these building blocks by color?"

Pippin's eyes grew wide and sparkly. "Ooh! Building blocks!" He loved the way they felt smooth and cool in his flippers. The red ones smelled like strawberries, the blue ones like blueberries, and the yellow ones like sunshine.

"First, let's sort them," Mama explained. "Red blocks in this pile, blue blocks here, and yellow blocks over there. It's an important chore that helps keep our home tidy."

Pippin nodded eagerly and began picking up blocks. *Click, clack, click* went the blocks as he sorted them. But then something caught his curious eye – the blocks could stack! He quietly began building a tall, wobbly tower while Mama wasn't looking.

"How's the sorting going, Pippin?" Mama called from the kitchen.

Pippin looked at his tower, then at the messy, unsorted blocks scattered around. His tummy felt funny and twisty. "Um... good, Mama!" he called back, but his voice sounded small and squeaky.

The tower grew taller and taller. *Stack, stack, stack!* Red on blue on yellow. It was the most magnificent tower Pippin had ever built! But the blocks weren't sorted at all – they were all mixed up in his creation.

*CRASH!* The tower tumbled down, blocks rolling everywhere across the shiny dining room floor. They bounced and scattered with loud *thunk, thunk, thunks*.

Mama hurried in. "Oh my! What happened here?"

Pippin's flippers trembled. The dining room looked like a colorful block storm had blown through. His heart beat fast like a little drum. He wanted to say the tower fell by itself, but looking at Mama's kind eyes, he knew what he had to do.

"Mama," Pippin said in a tiny voice, "I wasn't sorting the blocks. I was building a tower instead. I'm sorry I wasn't honest when you asked how it was going."

Mama's face softened into a warm smile. She knelt down and gave Pippin a gentle hug that smelled like vanilla and love. "Thank you for telling me the truth, sweetheart. Honesty is very important. I'm proud of you for being brave enough to tell me what really happened."

"But I didn't do my chore," Pippin sniffled.

"Well," Mama said with a twinkle in her eye, "how about we sort the blocks together? And maybe... we can build a tower afterward!"

Pippin's face lit up brighter than the morning sun. "Really?"

"Really! Chores are more fun when we do them together. And building towers is wonderful – after our work is done."

So Pippin and Mama sorted all the blocks – red, blue, and yellow. Then they built the most amazing tower together, even taller than Pippin's first one. It stood proud and strong in the sunny dining room.

"Being honest feels good in my heart," Pippin said, patting his fluffy white chest.

"It always does," Mama agreed. "Truth makes everything better."

The End.
