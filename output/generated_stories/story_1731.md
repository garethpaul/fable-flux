# Pebble Shares the Blocks

Waddle-waddle! Pebble the cheerful penguin slid across the soft rug in the playful living room. The room smelled like warm cocoa and clean soap. Sunlight made bright rectangles on the floor. Toys were in colorful baskets. Blocks, cars, and stuffed animals waited for fun.

“I’m curious today!” Pebble chirped, tapping a shiny blue block with a flipper. “I want to build the tallest tower ever!”

Pebble stacked one red block, then one yellow block, then a green one. Clack, clack, clack. The tower wobbled and then stood tall. Pebble giggled. “Hello, tower!”

Just then, Pebble’s friends waddled in: Mia the mouse with tiny pink ears, and Benny the bunny with long, floppy ears. Their noses twitched with excitement.

“Ooo,” said Mia, eyes wide. “Can we play too?”

“I want to build a garage for my car,” Benny said, holding a little blue car.

Pebble hugged the tower with both flippers. “I want the tower to be the tallest. I need all the blocks.”

Mia’s whiskers drooped. Benny’s ears sagged. The room felt heavy and quiet.

Pebble tilted their head. Pebble was curious. “Why do your faces look so sad?”

Mia whispered, “It doesn’t feel fair.”

Benny nodded. “We want to build too.”

Pebble looked at the baskets. There were many blocks, but Pebble’s tower used most of the big ones. Pebble’s tummy felt tight. “But I started first,” Pebble said in a small voice.

Mia took a deep breath. “Fairness means everyone gets a turn. Fairness means we share, so all of us can have fun.”

Benny added, “We can work it out. We can make a plan!”

Pebble sat down on the soft rug. It felt warm under the feathers. Pebble thought about the word fair. Fair felt kind. Fair felt friendly. Fair felt like sunshine.

Pebble nodded slowly. “Let’s try a plan.”

Mia smiled. “We can sort the blocks by color. We can each pick blocks in turns.”

Benny wiggled his ears. “And we can build together! A tower, a garage, and a bridge can all match.”

Pebble’s eyes sparkled. “Yes! Let’s be fair and take turns.”

They poured the blocks out with a soft clatter. The room filled with colors—red, yellow, green, blue. Pebble loved the sound: clink, clack, slide. The blocks felt smooth and cool.

Mia said, “Let’s make a fairness rule. Pebble picks one block, then me, then Benny, and we keep going.”

Pebble took a deep breath and smiled. “Okay. I’ll go first, but only one block.”

Pebble picked a long blue block and put it in a pile. Mia chose a yellow block. Benny picked a red block. Round and round they went. No one grabbed. No one rushed. They used gentle voices.

Pebble felt the tight feeling float away. Pebble’s chest felt light like a balloon. “This is fun,” Pebble said. “Fair is fun!”

After picking, Pebble said, “Now we build.”

Mia clapped. “I will help with patterns. Yellow, red, yellow, red.”

Benny rolled his car on the rug. Vroom! “I will test the garage.”

Pebble pressed blocks together. “I will make the tall part.”

They worked as a team. They asked questions. They were curious. “What happens if we use two small blocks instead of one big block?” Pebble asked.

Mia tried it. “Click-click. It works!”

Benny tried the car. “Vroom under the bridge! It fits!”

The living room was full of sounds—happy giggles, soft clacks, little “vrooms.” The sunlight felt warm on their faces. The tower stood tall but not too wobbly. The bridge made a neat arc. The garage had a safe roof.

Suddenly, the tower leaned. Uh-oh. It wobbled. Pebble reached out, but the tower tumbled—clatter-crash!

Pebble’s eyes got wet. “Oh no. My tower!”

Mia put a tiny paw on Pebble’s flipper. “It’s okay.”

Benny rubbed Pebble’s shoulder. “We can fix it. We’ll fix it together.”

Pebble sniffled and nodded. “Yes. We can make a new plan.”

They took turns again. Pebble added a wide base. Mia found strong square blocks. Benny held the tower steady with gentle paws and ears. They counted together, “One, two, three, four.” The tower stood tall and steady.

Pebble smiled so big the tips of their flippers tingled. “We did it! Fair and together!”

Mia twirled. “Fairness helped us all play.”

Benny parked the car in the garage. “Fairness makes room for everyone.”

Pebble looked at the tower, the bridge, and the garage. Pebble listened to the gentle clack of the last block. Pebble felt proud and calm.

Pebble said, “Today I learned something. Fair means sharing and taking turns. Fair means listening and making a plan. Fair feels good.”

Mia and Benny cheered, “Fair feels good!”

They cleaned up together, singing a tidy-up song. The baskets were full again. The rug looked smooth. The room smelled like cocoa again. Pebble’s heart felt warm.

Pebble whispered, “Next time, we’ll be fair from the start.”

Mia nodded. “And we’ll be curious and ask questions.”

Benny grinned. “And we’ll solve little problems with kind words.”

Pebble flapped happily. “We are a fair team!”

The End.
