# Rocky Raccoon’s Backyard Band

Rocky was a musical raccoon with soft, striped fur and bright, curious eyes. He loved to play music in the sunny backyard. The grass felt cool under his feet, and the warm sun tickled his ears. Birds chirped, bees hummed, and the leaves made a gentle shh-shh sound in the breeze.

Rocky had a tiny drum made from a bucket, a shiny spoon he used like a bell, and a string he plucked like a little guitar. “Dum-dum! Ting-ting! Plink-plonk!” Rocky giggled. “I love music!”

One bright morning, Rocky had an idea. “I want to make a backyard band,” he said. “Playing together will be fun!” He looked around the sunny yard. Flowers nodded. A blue ball rolled. The smell of fresh grass was sweet.

Rocky took a deep breath. “Friends, who wants to make music with me?” he called.

Squeak the mouse popped out from under a leaf. “I do! I can shake seeds in a cup,” squeaked Squeak.

Sunny the sparrow swooped down. “Tweet! I can sing,” chirped Sunny.

Bella the bunny hopped close. “I can thump my feet,” she said, her nose wiggling.

Rocky smiled. “Great! Let’s start.”

Rocky tapped his bucket drum. “Dum-dum!” Squeak shook the seeds. “Shaka-shaka!” Sunny sang, “Tweet-tweet!” Bella thumped, “Thump-thump!”

But soon the sounds got mixed up. Everyone played at the same time. The music sounded loud and messy. “DUM-SHAKA-TWEET-THUMP!” It was too much. Sunny’s feathers ruffled. Bella’s ears drooped. Squeak covered his tiny ears.

Rocky stopped tapping. He felt a little twist in his tummy. “Oh no. This is not fun,” he said quietly. He looked at his friends’ faces. They looked worried and confused.

Rocky took a slow breath. He remembered what Mama Raccoon always said: “Talk it out. Listen. Make a plan.”

Rocky lifted his paw. “Friends, let’s talk,” he said in a calm voice. “Our music is loud and jumbled. I think we need a plan.”

Squeak peeped, “What plan?”

Sunny asked, “How do we make it sound nice?”

Bella said, “I want to help!”

Rocky nodded. “We can take turns. We can count together. We can listen to each other. That is communication. We use our words and our ears.”

He drew shapes in the dirt with his paw. “First, I play my drum two beats. Dum-dum. Then Squeak shakes two beats. Shaka-shaka. Then Sunny sings two tweets. Tweet-tweet. Then Bella thumps two beats. Thump-thump. We will go in a circle.”

He looked up. “What do you think?”

Squeak smiled. “I like it.”

Sunny chirped, “Me too!”

Bella nodded. “Yes. I can wait for my turn.”

Rocky said, “Let’s try. I will count us in. One, two, ready, play!”

He said the numbers slowly and clearly. “One, two…”

Rocky tapped, “Dum-dum.” Squeak followed, “Shaka-shaka.” Sunny sang, “Tweet-tweet.” Bella thumped, “Thump-thump.”

They did it again. “Dum-dum. Shaka-shaka. Tweet-tweet. Thump-thump.”

The music sounded smooth and happy. It felt like the warm sun on their fur and feathers. A little breeze carried the beat across the grass. Rocky’s tummy twist was gone. He felt proud.

“Let’s add a soft part and a loud part,” Rocky said. “Soft like a whisper. Loud like a cheer. We will say ‘soft’ and ‘loud’ so we all know.”

“Soft,” Sunny said gently. The next round was quiet and sweet. “Dum-dum. Shaka-shaka. Tweet-tweet. Thump-thump.”

“Loud!” Bella said with a grin. The next round was bouncy and bright. “DUM-DUM! SHAKA-SHAKA! TWEET-TWEET! THUMP-THUMP!”

They laughed. It was fun! The music sounded like sunshine.

Then Rocky saw the blue ball rolling toward the flower bed. “Oh!” he gasped. “The ball might bump the flowers.” He felt responsible for the yard. “Friends, we must take care of the backyard while we play.”

He raised his paw. “Pause!” he called.

Everyone stopped. “What happened?” asked Squeak.

“The ball is rolling,” Rocky said. “We need a plan. Who is closest?”

Bella looked. “I am closest,” she said.

“Okay,” said Rocky. “Bella, please hop and stop the ball. Squeak, please hold the seed cup. Sunny, please watch the flowers. I will count us back in after.”

Bella hopped quickly. “Hop-hop-hop.” She gently stopped the ball with her soft paws. Sunny watched the flowers. Squeak held the cup steady. The flowers stayed safe. The yard felt happy.

“Thank you,” said Rocky. “We talked. We listened. We made a plan. That is responsibility.”

They got back in a circle. Rocky smiled. “Ready?”

“Ready!” his friends said.

“One, two, ready, play!” Rocky called.

“Dum-dum. Shaka-shaka. Tweet-tweet. Thump-thump.” The rhythm went round and round. They added claps. They added a wiggle dance. They even added a quiet “la-la” at the end.

When the song was done, Rocky put down his spoon bell. He looked at his friends. “I had so much fun,” he said. “Thank you for talking with me and listening. Communication helped us make music. Thinking step by step helped us make a plan. Taking care of the yard showed responsibility.”

Sunny chirped, “We are a great band!”

Squeak squeaked, “We can do it again tomorrow!”

Bella thumped happily, “Yes!”

Rocky felt warm and proud. The sun hugged the backyard with golden light. The breeze whispered, “Well done.”

Rocky waved his paw. “Friends, remember: Use your words. Use your ears. Make a plan. Take care. That way, we can play, solve problems, and have fun—together.”

They all smiled, and the backyard felt like a happy song.

The End.
