# Tilly Turtle’s Quiet Song

Tilly was a shy turtle who loved soft things and quiet sounds. Her cozy bedroom was her favorite place. The walls were a warm, leafy green. A tiny lamp made a gentle glow, like a candle inside a seashell. Her bed was a little nest of blankets that felt like warm moss. A small window showed a sprinkle of stars, and the night air smelled like clean sheets and sweet soap.

On her shelf sat a shiny, blue ukulele. Tilly loved to strum it with her small, careful claws. The strings made a soft “plink-plunk” sound that tickled her ears. She had a little song she called her Quiet Song. She hummed, “La-la-la,” and the gentle notes floated like bubbles.

Tilly’s big sister, Tessa, knocked on the door. “Tilly, can I come in?”

“Yes,” Tilly whispered.

Tessa peeped inside with a smile. “I love your Quiet Song. Mom says Grandma is visiting tomorrow. We’re going to have a tiny concert in your cozy bedroom! Would you like to play your song?”

Tilly’s tummy felt fluttery, like a butterfly was bouncing inside. “I don’t know,” she said. “I’m shy. What if I make a mistake?”

Tessa sat on the soft rug. “It’s okay to feel shy,” she said. “Feelings are like visitors. They come and then they go. We can practice together.”

Tilly looked at her ukulele. It shone in the lamp light. She touched the smooth wood and took a slow breath in and out. The breath smelled like her clean blanket. “Okay,” she said. “I can try.”

They practiced. Tilly’s claws went “plink-plunk, plink-plunk,” and her voice went “la-la-la.” Sometimes the strings squeaked. Sometimes Tilly forgot a note. When that happened, her shoulders felt tight, and she tucked her head into her shell.

Tessa gently tapped the shell and said, “Let’s take a turtle breath.” They both breathed in for three counts—one, two, three—and out for three counts—one, two, three. Tilly felt her shoulders soften. The squeaky note didn’t seem so big anymore.

After a while, Mom peeked in with a warm smile. “I heard music,” she said. “I also heard quiet. That is a nice mix.”

Tilly looked down. “I’m scared to play for Grandma. What if I mess up? What if people laugh?”

Mom sat on the bed and patted the blanket. “Thank you for telling me your feelings,” she said. “That is brave. We can make a plan.”

“What kind of plan?” Tilly asked.

Mom held up her fingers. “First, we practice little by little. Second, we use turtle breaths when we feel tight. Third, if a problem comes, we use kind words to solve it.”

Tilly nodded. A plan felt safe. Her heart slowed down.

That night, Tilly practiced her Quiet Song again. The ukulele sounded like rain tapping a window. Her voice was soft as a pillow. She whispered to herself, “Little by little.” She hummed and strummed until her eyes felt sleepy. The warm lamp light made the room feel like a hug.

In the morning, the sun made golden stripes on the floor. Grandma came with a gentle hug and a basket of warm blueberry muffins. The cozy bedroom smelled sweet and buttery. Tessa brought in two small chairs. Mom sat on the bed, and Grandma sat on a chair. “We are ready when you are,” Grandma said, her voice like a soft blanket.

Tilly felt the butterflies flutter again. Her claws wiggled. Her shell felt extra heavy. She remembered the plan. She took a turtle breath—one, two, three in, and one, two, three out. “I am shy,” she said. “But I want to try.”

“You’ve got this,” Tessa whispered.

Tilly began to play. “Plink-plunk, plink-plunk,” went the ukulele. “La-la-la,” went Tilly’s voice. The notes floated like soft feathers. Then, oops! A string squeaked. Tilly’s eyes went wide. Her face felt warm. She stopped.

Grandma smiled and said, “Mistakes are how music learns to walk.” Mom nodded. “Let’s use kind words,” she said gently. “What do you need, Tilly?”

Tilly took another turtle breath. “I need a tiny pause,” she said. “Then I want to try again.”

“Good plan,” said Tessa.

Tilly closed her eyes for a moment. She felt the cozy bed in the room, smelled the muffins, and heard the quiet hush. Her heart found a steady beat: ba-bump, ba-bump. She opened her eyes and strummed again. “Plink-plunk, plink-plunk.” This time the notes were smooth and round. Tilly sang her Quiet Song from start to end. The last note hovered in the air like a glowing bubble, then popped into silence.

Grandma clapped softly. “Beautiful,” she said. “I heard practice. I heard courage. I heard your heart.”

Tilly’s cheeks lifted into a smile. “I felt shy,” she said. “But I used turtle breaths. And when there was a problem, I used kind words and a plan.”

Tessa hugged her. “You solved the conflict with yourself,” she said. “Your feelings wanted to hide, but your brave part wanted to sing. You helped them be friends.”

Mom added, “That is conflict-resolution. You listened, you practiced, and you chose kind action.”

Tilly held her ukulele close. It felt warm and friendly. “I can practice again tomorrow,” she said. “Little by little.”

Grandma passed around muffins. They were soft and sweet, with tiny bursts of blueberry. Tilly took a bite and giggled. “Plink-plunk,” she said to the muffin. Everyone laughed, and the cozy bedroom filled with happy, gentle sounds.

The End.
