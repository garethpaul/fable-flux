# Miri Mouse and the Colorful Stories

Miri was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. She loved the creative art room at the library. It smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Sunlight made puddles of gold on the floor. Paints glowed like rainbows on a long table.

Every Saturday, Miri volunteered to help in the art room. She swept tiny paper bits with her tail. She passed out brushes with a happy, “Here you go!” She liked helping everyone feel welcome.

One Saturday, friends came to make story pictures. There was Bongo the bunny with big, bouncy ears. There was Tiko the turtle with a smooth, shiny shell. There was Suri the sparrow, small and quick. And there was Pippa the puppy who wagged so much that her tail made a soft thump-thump-thump.

“Today we make story art!” said Miri. She smiled and wiggled her whiskers. “We will paint our favorite stories and tell them to each other.”

Bongo grabbed a wide brush. “I like big colors!” he said. Splish! Splash! He painted huge orange circles. The circles smelled like sweet oranges and looked like suns.

Tiko moved slowly and carefully. “I like tiny dots,” he said. Tap. Tap. Tap. His picture had tiny blue stars, neat and tidy.

Suri pecked a feather into the paint. “I like soft lines,” she said. Swoosh. Swoosh. Her lines looked like gentle breezes.

Pippa sniffed the clay and wagged hard. “I use clay,” she said. Smoosh. Squish. She made a lumpy, bumpy mountain that felt cool and mushy.

Miri clapped her paws. “Wonderful! Now we share our stories.”

Bongo stood tall. “This is my story of a jumping sun,” he said. “It bounces across the sky and makes everyone laugh.”

Next, Tiko spoke softly. “This is my story of tiny stars that help me find my way home.”

Suri chirped, “This is a wind that carries songs to friends far away.”

Pippa barked, “This is a mountain where puppies climb and slide and help each other.”

Miri’s heart felt warm. “These are beautiful,” she said.

But then a small hedgehog peeked into the room. Her name was Nini. She hugged a blank paper to her chest. “I am new,” she whispered. “I am shy. My prickles poke holes in paper. My lines are wobbly. My story sounds funny. Maybe I should go.”

The room grew quiet. Miri could hear the tick-tick of the clock and the soft swish of Suri’s feathers.

Miri walked to Nini and smiled. “Hi, Nini. I am Miri. We are glad you are here. Your prickles, your wobbles, your funny story voice—they are part of you. We celebrate differences here.”

Nini blinked. “But what if my picture looks wrong?”

Bongo hopped closer. “My circles are too big for some friends,” he said. “But big is my way.”

Tiko nodded slowly. “My dots are very small. Small is my way.”

Suri fluffed her feathers. “My lines are soft. Soft is my way.”

Pippa wagged. “My art is lumpy. Lumpy is my way!”

Miri put a tiny paw on Nini’s paper. “Let’s make a plan,” she said. “We can solve this together.”

Problem: Nini feels different and worried. Solution: We use our different ways to help.

Miri volunteered to guide. “I will tell a story as we work,” she said. “We will build it together.”

Miri whispered a gentle beginning. “Once there was a small hedgehog who carried stars on her prickles.”

Tiko smiled. “I can dot tiny stars,” he said. Tap. Tap. Tap. He dotted around Nini’s paper.

Suri sang a soft tune. “The wind will help the stars glow,” she said. Swoosh. She brushed light lines that made the dots shimmer.

Bongo laughed. “The sun wants to join!” Splish! He painted a big, happy sun in the corner.

Pippa nosed a bowl of soft clay. “A cozy hill for the hedgehog,” she said. Smoosh. She pressed a clay hill at the bottom of Nini’s page.

Nini took a breath. She touched the paint with one small prickle. Prick. Prick. Little heart shapes popped on the paper. “I can make hearts,” she said. “They are pokey but sweet.”

Miri clapped. “Yes! Your hearts are perfect.”

As they worked, the room smelled like oranges and wet paint. Brushes made gentle scratch-scratch sounds. Clay squished. Friends laughed. Nini’s eyes grew bright.

“Would you like to tell the story, Nini?” Miri asked.

Nini nodded. Her voice was small but brave. “The hedgehog carried stars to friends who were lost. She made heart-lights on the path. The sun bounced, the wind sang, the hill waited, and everyone found home.”

Everyone cheered. “Again! Again!” Pippa barked with joy.

Nini smiled. “I thought my prickles were a problem,” she said. “But they made hearts. My wobbly lines made light.”

Miri looked around the colorful room. “Our differences made this story bright,” she said. “Big, small, soft, lumpy, pokey—we all matter.”

Bongo twirled his brush. “Different is not wrong.”

Tiko tapped a dot. “Different helps.”

Suri sang, “Different sings.”

Pippa wagged, “Different feels fun!”

Nini held her picture. It was full of stars, hearts, wind lines, a sunny corner, and a clay hill. It felt bumpy and smooth at the same time. It looked like friends.

Miri hung the picture on the wall. “This is our friendship story,” she said. “Thank you for sharing and helping.”

Nini stood tall. “Thank you for welcoming me,” she said. “I will come back and help, too.”

Miri nodded. “We volunteer, we tell stories, we learn, and we celebrate differences—together.”

The End.
