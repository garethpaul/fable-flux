# Roo’s Balcony Games

Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey. He lived in a cozy home with a sunny balcony garden. The balcony had pots of mint, little tomato plants, and a tiny lemon tree. Bees hummed. Leaves smelled fresh and green. The floor felt warm under Roo’s feet.

One bright morning, Roo bounced out and said, “Today, I will play sports!” His tail swished happily. “I want to jump far and run fast!”

Mama called from the kitchen, “Sports are fun, Roo. Sports help our bodies and our brains. But we must prepare.”

Roo blinked. “Prepare? What is prepare?”

Mama smiled. “Prepare means get ready. We plan. We warm up. We drink water. We make space. Then we play safe and learn.”

Roo clapped. “Okay! I will prepare!”

First, Roo looked around the balcony garden. The breeze was soft. Sunlight made the leaves sparkle. He could smell the mint, cool and sweet. He saw a small ball, a jump rope, and two little cones made from cups.

Roo said, “Step one, make space.” He gently moved the pots to the sides. He made a clear path. “Step two, water.” He sipped cool water. “Ahh!” It felt good.

Mama brought a soft towel. “Step three,” she said, “warm up.”

Roo lifted his arms high. He wiggled his paws. “Stretch, stretch.” He rolled his shoulders. He bent his knees. “Bounce, bounce.” He took deep breaths. In and out. The air smelled like lemons.

“Great job,” Mama said. “Warm-ups wake up your muscles.”

Roo’s eyes shone. “Now I play sports!”

He set the two cups on the floor. “These are goal posts,” he said. He rolled the small ball with his paws. “Kick, tap, kick!” The ball brushed his toes. It felt smooth and a little bouncy.

“Go, Roo!” cheered Mama.

Roo aimed between the cups. Tap! The ball rolled right through. Roo laughed. “Goal! I did it!”

He tried again. This time the ball bumped a pot. Roo froze. “Oh no!”

Mama touched his shoulder. “We learn from mistakes. How can we fix this?”

Roo looked and thought. “I can move the pot a little more.” He slid it safely aside. “I can also kick softer.” He took a breath. Tap. The ball glided straight and smooth. “Goal!”

Next, Roo wanted to jump. He laid the jump rope in a long line. “This is a river,” he said. “I will jump over the river.”

He bent his knees. He felt his heart flutter with joy. “One, two, three!” he whispered.

Boing! He hopped over the rope. His feet landed softly. “I did it! I did it!”

He made the “river” wider. Boing! He jumped again. His tail helped him balance. He spread his paws. He felt strong and brave.

“Remember,” Mama said, “sports are about trying, learning, and caring for your body.”

Roo nodded. “Try, learn, care.”

He tried running next. He set the two cups far apart. “This is my track,” he said. “I will run to the lemons and back.”

Roo took a deep breath. The lemon tree rustled. He heard a tiny bird tweet nearby.

“Ready, set, go!” he called.

Pitter-patter, pitter-patter went his feet on the warm floor. Wind brushed his ears. He turned at the lemon tree and ran back, smiling big.

When he reached the start, Roo stopped. He put a paw on his chest. Ba-dum, ba-dum went his heart. “My heart beats fast,” he said.

Mama nodded. “That means your body is working. After we move, we rest and drink water.”

Roo sipped water. Cold drops tickled his tongue. He wiped his forehead with the soft towel. He felt calm again.

“Sports are fun,” Roo said. “But I also want to learn new skills.”

Mama pointed to the jump rope. “Let’s learn small hops in place. Ready?”

Roo held the rope handles. He placed the rope behind him. “Swing, hop,” Mama said.

Roo swung the rope. Whoosh! He hopped. The rope touched his toes and stopped. Roo frowned a little.

“It’s okay,” said Mama. “We try again. Learning takes time.”

Roo nodded. He took a breath and smiled. “Swing, hop.” Whoosh! Boing! The rope slid under his feet. He did one hop. Then two! “I did it! I learned!”

“You prepared, you practiced, and you succeeded,” Mama said. “That is how we grow.”

Roo looked around his balcony garden. The mint plants danced in the breeze. The lemon tree leaves made tiny claps. The sunlight felt warm like a hug.

Roo said, “Today I learned that sports are fun. I learned to prepare. I learned to try, learn, and care. I learned to drink water, make space, and warm up.”

Mama smiled. “That is a wonderful lesson.”

Roo hugged Mama. “Tomorrow, I will prepare and play again. I will keep learning.”

“Every day,” Mama said, “your body gets stronger. Your brain gets smarter. Sports and preparation help you do your best.”

Roo bounced once, softly. “I am ready for more adventures,” he whispered.

The End.
