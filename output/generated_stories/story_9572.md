# Nutkin's Special Helper Day

Nutkin the squirrel lived in a cozy oak tree right outside Mrs. Chen's house. Every morning, he loved to peek through the guest room window and watch the sunshine dance across the pretty blue walls.

One sunny Tuesday, Nutkin heard something new. "¡Hola! Hello!" came a cheerful voice from the guest room. Through the window, he saw a little girl with curly hair unpacking a colorful suitcase.

"That sounds different from the words I usually hear," thought Nutkin, twitching his fluffy tail with curiosity.

The little girl was Sofia, Mrs. Chen's granddaughter from Mexico. She was visiting for a whole week! But as Nutkin watched, he noticed Sofia looked a bit sad.

"Abuela, I miss speaking Spanish with my friends," Sofia said softly to her grandmother.

Mrs. Chen hugged her tight. "I know, sweetie. But remember, knowing two languages makes you extra special!"

Nutkin's bright black eyes sparkled with an idea. He loved helping others, and he had noticed something wonderful in the guest room - Mrs. Chen had put out books in both English and Spanish for Sofia!

"I want to help!" chittered Nutkin excitedly. He scampered down the tree and through the pet door that Mrs. Chen always left open for the neighborhood animals.

In the guest room, Nutkin found Sofia sitting on the soft yellow bedspread, looking at a picture book about animals.

"Oh!" gasped Sofia. "A squirrel!"

"¡Hola!" squeaked Nutkin, remembering the word he'd heard Sofia use. His tiny voice made Sofia giggle.

"You said hello in Spanish!" Sofia clapped her hands. "Do you want to learn more words?"

Nutkin nodded his little head up and down enthusiastically.

Sofia picked up her Spanish-English animal book. "This is a 'ardilla' - that's 'squirrel' in Spanish!" she said, pointing to a picture that looked just like Nutkin.

"Ar-dee-ya!" Nutkin tried to repeat, making Sofia laugh with delight.

For the next hour, Sofia taught Nutkin Spanish words while he showed her how to make different animal sounds. When Sofia said "gato" for cat, Nutkin made a tiny "meow!" When she said "perro" for dog, he barked like a puppy.

Mrs. Chen peeked into the room and smiled warmly. "Sofia, you're such a wonderful teacher! And Nutkin, you're being so helpful."

"Abuela, teaching Spanish is fun!" Sofia beamed. "And learning English animal sounds from Nutkin is even better!"

"That's the magic of languages, dear," said Mrs. Chen. "When we share words with others, we share pieces of our hearts too."

Nutkin spent the whole week visiting Sofia. Each day, they learned new words together. Sofia taught him "sol" for sun and "árbol" for tree. Nutkin showed her that "chirp" and "chitter" were squirrel words for happy sounds.

By the end of the week, Sofia felt proud of both her languages. She had volunteered to teach Spanish words to Nutkin, and in return, learned that sharing languages brings friends together.

"Gracias, Nutkin!" Sofia said on her last day, giving him a gentle pat. "Thank you for helping me love my words again!"

Nutkin chittered happily. He had learned that every language is like a special gift - and the best part about gifts is sharing them with friends.

From that day on, whenever Nutkin heard different languages in the neighborhood, he remembered Sofia's lesson: words are bridges that connect hearts, no matter what language they're spoken in.

The End.
