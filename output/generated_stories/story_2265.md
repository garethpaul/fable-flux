# Leo the Little Helper

In a cozy nursery with soft yellow walls and fluffy white clouds painted on the ceiling, lived a brave little lion cub named Leo. His golden fur was as warm as sunshine, and his tiny mane was just starting to grow around his fuzzy ears. Leo loved his comfortable nursery with its rainbow-colored toys scattered across the soft carpet.

One sunny morning, Leo woke up from his nap feeling refreshed and happy. He stretched his little paws and yawned, showing his tiny white teeth. "What a beautiful day!" he said to himself, looking around his nursery.

But as Leo looked around, he noticed something that made his whiskers twitch with concern. His toys were everywhere! Building blocks were scattered near the window, his favorite teddy bear was upside down by the door, and crayons were rolling around the floor.

"Oh my," Leo said, his amber eyes growing wide. "My nursery is very messy. This doesn't look nice at all."

Just then, Leo heard his mama calling from downstairs. "Leo, sweetie! Your friend Bunny is coming to play today!"

Leo's ears perked up with excitement, but then he looked around his messy room again. His little heart felt heavy. "I can't let Bunny see my nursery like this," he thought. "It wouldn't be fair to her, and it's not safe either. Someone might trip on these toys!"

Leo remembered what his papa had taught him: "Taking care of our spaces is important, little one. When we keep things clean and organized, everyone feels happy and safe."

"I know what I need to do!" Leo said bravely, puffing out his small chest. "I need to clean up my nursery. It's my responsibility!"

Leo started with his building blocks. He picked them up one by one with his careful paws and placed them gently in their special blue box. "One, two, three," he counted happily as each block found its home.

Next, he rescued his teddy bear and gave it a warm hug before placing it on his bed. The soft brown fur felt comforting against his cheek. "There you go, Teddy. Much better!"

Then Leo gathered all his crayons, smelling their waxy sweetness as he put each colorful stick back in its box. Red, blue, yellow, green – each one had its special place.

As Leo worked, he felt proud and strong. His little muscles were getting exercise, and his mind felt clear and happy. "Cleaning up makes me feel good inside," he realized.

When Bunny arrived, she gasped with delight. "Leo! Your nursery looks wonderful! Everything is so neat and pretty!"

Leo's chest swelled with pride. "Thank you, Bunny! I cleaned it all by myself. I wanted to make sure it was safe and nice for your visit."

"That was very thoughtful of you," Bunny said, her pink nose twitching with approval. "You're such a good friend!"

As they played together in the clean, organized space, Leo felt a warm glow in his heart. He had learned that taking care of his environment wasn't just about having a pretty room – it was about being responsible and caring for others too.

"Taking care of our spaces helps everyone feel happy and safe," Leo said to Bunny as they built a tall tower with his blocks.

From that day forward, Leo always kept his nursery tidy, knowing that being responsible for his space was one way to show love and care for his family and friends.

The End.
