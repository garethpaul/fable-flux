# Gigi's Big Art Day

Gigi the giraffe loved visiting the bright, colorful art room at her school. The walls were covered with sparkly paintings, and shelves held jars full of crayons that smelled like berries and flowers. Today felt extra special because it was "Make Your Own Art" day.

"Good morning, everyone!" called Ms. Robin, their teacher. "Today you can choose any art supplies you want and create something all by yourself!"

Gigi's long neck swayed with excitement as she looked around the room. There were paintbrushes as soft as feathers, clay that felt squishy like mud after rain, and colored paper that crinkled when touched.

Her friend Benny the bear was already at the painting station. "Gigi, come paint with me!" he called. "We can make the same rainbow picture!"

Gigi started walking toward Benny, but then she stopped. Something colorful caught her eye - a basket of beautiful fabric scraps in the corner. They felt smooth and bumpy and fuzzy all at the same time.

"Actually, Benny," Gigi said in her gentle voice, "I think I want to try something different today."

Benny looked surprised. "But we always do art together! Don't you want to paint rainbows?"

Gigi's heart felt a little flutter. She did like doing things with Benny, but the fabric pieces were calling to her. "I want to make something with these pretty fabrics," she said, standing a little taller. "But you can paint your rainbow. It will be beautiful!"

Ms. Robin smiled at Gigi. "What a wonderful choice! What would you like to make?"

Gigi picked up a piece of soft yellow fabric that reminded her of sunshine. "I want to make a blanket for my toy elephant at home. He gets cold at night."

"That sounds perfect," said Ms. Robin. "The glue and scissors are right here when you need them."

Gigi spread the fabrics on the table. There was blue like the sky, green like grass, and orange like carrots. She carefully chose each piece, thinking about how they would look together.

"This is harder than I thought," Gigi whispered to herself as she tried to cut the fabric. The scissors felt big in her hooves.

"Take your time," said Ms. Robin, watching nearby. "You're doing great!"

Slowly and carefully, Gigi cut each piece. She arranged them like a puzzle, making a pattern of colors. When she squeezed the glue bottle, some came out too fast and made a big blob.

"Oops!" Gigi giggled. She used her finger to spread it around. "That's okay. I can fix it."

As Gigi worked, other friends came to see what she was making.

"Wow, Gigi!" said Luna the lamb. "I've never seen anyone make a fabric blanket before. It's so pretty!"

"You thought of that all by yourself?" asked Benny, looking amazed.

Gigi felt proud and happy. "Yes! I wanted to try something new, and I figured it out step by step."

When art time was over, Gigi held up her finished blanket. It had wobbly edges and one corner where the glue had made a funny bump, but it was perfect to her.

"I made this all by myself!" Gigi announced with a big smile. "And tonight, my elephant won't be cold anymore."

Ms. Robin clapped her wings. "Gigi, you showed wonderful independence today. You chose your own project, spoke up for what you wanted, and learned something completely new!"

That night, Gigi tucked her toy elephant under the special blanket she had made. It felt even more cozy because she had created it with her own hooves, her own ideas, and her own brave heart.

The End.
