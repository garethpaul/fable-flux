# Nutkin's Big Guest Room Adventure

Nutkin the squirrel had fluffy gray fur and a big bushy tail that twitched when he was excited. Today, he was VERY excited because his family was having visitors! His cousin Acorn was coming to stay in the guest room upstairs.

"I can't wait to show Acorn all my favorite hiding spots!" Nutkin chattered, bouncing up and down on his tiny paws.

But when Nutkin scampered up to the guest room, he found something surprising. The room smelled like fresh pine needles and sunshine, but there was already someone there! A little field mouse named Pip was curled up in the corner, looking very scared.

"Oh no!" squeaked Pip, covering her eyes with her tiny paws. "Please don't hurt me! I was just looking for somewhere warm to sleep."

Nutkin's first thought was to tell Pip to leave right away. After all, this was HIS family's guest room! But then he noticed that Pip was shivering, and her whiskers were trembling. She looked cold and hungry.

Nutkin took a deep breath and remembered what his mama always said: "When we meet someone new, we should use our gentle words first."

"Hello there," Nutkin said in his softest, kindest voice. "I'm Nutkin. What's your name?"

Pip peeked through her paws. "I'm... I'm Pip," she whispered. "Are you going to chase me away?"

"Well," said Nutkin, thinking carefully, "this room is for my cousin Acorn. But you look cold and scared. Can you tell me what happened?"

Pip's eyes filled with tiny tears. "The big storm last night blew down my nest. I've been looking everywhere for a safe place to stay, but everyone keeps telling me to go away."

Nutkin felt his heart grow warm with kindness. He sat down next to Pip, making sure to move slowly so he wouldn't frighten her.

"That sounds really scary," Nutkin said gently. "Being without a home must feel awful."

"It does," sniffled Pip. "I just need somewhere safe until I can build a new nest."

Nutkin's tail twitched as he thought of a wonderful idea. "You know what? I think I can help! The guest room is big enough for both you and my cousin. And I know the perfect spot in our oak tree where you could build a new nest!"

Pip's eyes grew wide with hope. "Really? You would help me?"

"Of course!" said Nutkin cheerfully. "But first, let's go ask my mama and papa if it's okay for you to stay. When we talk to them together and explain nicely, I think they'll understand."

Hand in paw, Nutkin and Pip went downstairs. Nutkin used his best polite voice to explain the situation to his parents. Just as he hoped, they were happy to help Pip.

"What a kind and brave thing to do," said Mama Squirrel, giving Nutkin a proud hug. "You used your words to solve a problem instead of being mean."

That evening, the guest room was filled with happy chatter. Cousin Acorn had arrived and was delighted to meet Pip. They all shared acorns and told funny stories until bedtime.

As Nutkin curled up in his own cozy bed, he felt proud and happy. He had learned that when you meet someone who seems different or scary, the best thing to do is talk kindly and listen carefully. Sometimes what looks like a problem can become the start of a wonderful friendship!

The End.
