# Momo’s Music Room Helpers

Momo was a courageous monkey with bright, curious eyes. He loved to swing, to dance, and to tap his toes. Today, he scampered into the musical practice room. The room was full of sounds: soft drum thumps, gentle piano notes, and the shhh-shhh of shakers. Sunlight slid across the floor, making warm, golden puddles.

“Oh! It’s messy,” Momo said. Sheet music was crinkled on the floor. Sticks were scattered. A tambourine lay under a chair. The room smelled like polished wood and orange soap, but it looked untidy and sleepy.

Momo took a brave breath. “I can help. I will do my chores,” he said. He liked to help. Helping made his chest feel big and warm.

Just then, his friends tumbled in. Pip the porcupine wore tiny dance shoes. Lula the lamb had a soft blue bow. Tiko the tiny tiger carried a ribbon stick.

“Dance time!” cheered Pip.

Momo held up a broom. “Chore time first,” he said kindly. “Then dance.”

“Aww,” Tiko sighed. “Chores are boring.”

Momo shook his head. “Chores make our room safe and happy. We can make it a game!”

He hummed a beat. Tap-tap. Tap-tap-tap. He swished the broom to the beat. Swish-swish. Swish-swish. The floor sang a soft hush. Momo smiled. “Let’s do the Clean-Up Dance!”

Pip wiggled. “A dance? I can try!”

Lula nodded. “I can pick up sheets. I like the crinkle sound.”

Momo clapped a rhythm. Clap, clap, clap. “Step, pick, and place,” he sang. “Step, pick, and place!”

They stepped. They picked up sticks. They placed them in a shiny bin. Tiko stomped, thump-thump, and shook a shaker, shhh-shhh. Pip twirled, click-click, tambourine jingling. Lula gathered music pages, crinkle-crinkle, and made neat stacks.

The room began to glow. Sunlight danced on the tidy floor. Momo felt proud. His tail swished with joy.

Then—oops! Tiko swung his ribbon too wide. A drum tumbled. Thump! A cup of water wobbled, splashed, and spilled. The drum made a sad plop.

Tiko’s eyes grew big. “I’m sorry,” he whispered. His whiskers drooped. “I didn’t mean it.”

Pip looked startled. Lula stepped back. Momo felt a quick hot spark in his tummy. He had worked so hard. He wanted to say, “Why?” But he took a deep breath. In… and out. He remembered courage means being brave in heart. Courage can be gentle.

Momo knelt by Tiko. “I hear you,” he said softly. “You didn’t mean it. Accidents happen. I forgive you.”

Tiko blinked. “You do?”

“Yes,” said Momo. “And we can fix it together.”

Momo handed Tiko a towel. “We will pat the water. Pat, pat, pat.” They patted the puddle until the floor was dry. Pip picked up the drum. Lula wiped the drum with a soft cloth.

“Thank you,” Tiko said. His voice was small but hopeful.

Momo smiled. “We help each other. That is compassion.”

“Com-pash-on,” Lula said slowly. “It feels warm.”

“It does,” Momo giggled. He pointed to a broom. “Tiko, want to try sweeping?”

Tiko’s ears perked. “I never swept before.”

“That’s okay,” said Momo. “Let’s be open-minded. We can try new jobs.”

Momo showed him. “Hold here. Gentle hands. Sweep like a soft wave.” Swish. Swish.

Tiko tried. Swish-swish. He grinned. “It sounds like the ocean.”

Pip tapped a tiny beat for them. Tap-tap-tap. Lula hummed a sweet tune. Momo added a step: slide and sweep, slide and sweep. They made a new Clean-Up Dance. Their feet felt light. Their hearts felt light too.

Soon, the room was neat and shiny. The bins were full of sticks and shakers. The drum sat tall and dry. The piano keys winked in the sun.

Momo stood tall. “Chores done,” he said. “Now we dance!”

They danced with happy feet. Tiko’s ribbon made rainbows. Pip clicked the tambourine, jingle-jingle. Lula swayed like a cloud. Momo spun and clapped, clap-clap, tail twirling in circles. Their laughter tickled the air.

After the dance, they sat on the warm floor. Momo passed around orange slices. The sweet, bright smell made them smile.

Pip licked sticky paws. “Chores were fun today,” he said. “I liked the music sweep.”

Lula nodded. “I liked helping Tiko. My heart feels soft.”

Tiko hugged the broom. “Thank you for forgiving me,” he said to Momo.

Momo patted his shoulder. “We all make mistakes. We can help. We can forgive. We can try new things. And we can do our chores with a song.”

They listened to the quiet room. It felt safe. It felt ready. It felt loved.

Momo whispered, “Clean rooms help us dance our best.”

“Yes,” his friends said together. “Chores help us shine.”

The End.
