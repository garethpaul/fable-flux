# Bubbles, Songs, and Little Goals

In a bright blue bathroom, with tiles like tiny sky squares, lived a singing bird named Pip. Pip was small and shiny, with a golden chest that shimmered like morning sun. Every day, Pip perched on the towel rack above the sink and sang. The bathroom smelled like minty soap and warm water. Drip, drip, drip went the tap. Chirp, chirp, chirp went Pip.

“Good morning, toothbrush,” Pip sang. “Good morning, bubbles!”

Today, Pip had an idea. Pip puffed up with excitement. “I want to learn something new,” Pip chirped. “I will teach and learn, all in one day!”

Pip hopped to the edge of the sink. The smooth counter felt cool on tiny feet. Pip looked into the mirror. A bright bird looked back. “Hello, me!” Pip giggled.

Flutter, flutter! Pip saw a little basket by the bathtub. Inside were soft washcloths rolled like tiny burritos, a rubber duck, and a colorful cup with stars. On the floor by the tub was a round, blue sponge ball.

“A ball!” Pip sang. “Let’s play bathroom sports!”

Pip nudged the sponge ball with a wing. It boinged softly. “Boing!” Pip laughed. “Sports can be gentle and safe.”

Pip set up a goal. Two shampoo bottles stood like tall trees at the end of the bath mat. “This is my goal,” Pip said. “I will try to roll the ball between them.” Pip took a breath. “I can do this. I will take initiative. That means I start and try.”

Pip pushed the ball with a tiny foot. The ball rolled, swish-swish across the fuzzy mat. It bumped the shampoo. Clunk. It did not go through.

Pip felt a little droop in the wings. Then Pip stood tall. “It’s okay,” Pip said out loud. “Learning takes practice.”

Just then, the rubber duck squeaked. “Squeak! What are you doing, Pip?”

“I’m learning,” said Pip. “I’m making a little sports game. I roll the ball. I aim. I try again.”

“Squeak-squeak! That sounds fun,” said the duck.

Pip tried again. Push. Roll. Swish. The ball went right between the bottles. “Goal!” Pip sang. “I did it!”

From the window, a soft wind came in, smelling like rain. Pip closed eyes and listened. “I will share a song from my bird family,” Pip whispered. Pip sang a sweet, old tune. It was a tradition, a song Mama Bird sang in the morning nest. It sounded like honey and sunshine.

“La-la-li, la-la-lo,” Pip sang. The tune filled the bathroom like warm steam. The duck swayed. The toothbrush shivered with joy. The mirror hummed back.

Pip opened eyes and smiled. “Tradition means we share special things from our family,” Pip told the duck. “I can teach you the words. Then you can sing, too.”

“Squeak?” asked the duck. “How do I learn?”

“We listen. We repeat. We practice,” said Pip. “That is education. Education is learning new things so our minds grow.”

Pip sang one line. The duck tried. “Squeee—ak!” It was funny and a little squeaky, but it was brave. Pip clapped with wings. “Great trying!”

Pip made a plan to teach. “Step one: listen. Step two: repeat. Step three: practice with a smile.” Pip drew the steps on the foggy mirror with a tiny wet feather: 1, 2, 3. Little numbers glowed in the mist.

“Now,” Pip said, “let’s make a learning game. First, a sports warm-up!” Pip hopped and stretched. “We move our bodies. We wake up our brains.”

They did three gentle hops on the soft bath mat. Hop, hop, hop. Their feet felt tickly, and their hearts felt brave. They giggled.

“Next, we sing the line together.” Pip sang. The duck tried again. This time it was clearer. The sound bounced off the tile, bright and happy.

“Wonderful!” Pip cheered. “You learned because you listened, tried, and didn’t give up.”

Tap, tap, tap went the drops from the tap, like a tiny drum. Pip got another idea. “We can count the drops and make a beat. Counting is part of education.”

They counted together. “One, two, three, four.” Pip tapped the sponge ball gently: tap-tap, tap-tap. The duck kept time with squeaks: squeak-squeak! It was a bathroom band.

Pip mixed the lessons. “Roll the ball to the beat, then sing the line.” Roll, tap-tap. Sing, la-la. Roll, tap-tap. Sing, la-la. They learned to listen, to move, and to share a tradition song. They felt proud and warm, like a cozy towel.

Soon, the room smelled like orange soap, and the mirror fog cleared. Sunlight printed gold squares on the floor. Pip took a deep breath. “Duck, what did we learn today?”

“We learned that education is listening, trying, and practicing,” said the duck. “We shared your family song. That is tradition-sharing. We played a safe, gentle sports game. And we started by ourselves. That is initiative!”

Pip’s heart glowed. “Yes,” Pip said. “And we learned with kindness.”

They finished with one more goal. Pip rolled the ball. It went between the bottles, smooth and sure. “Goal!” they cheered, echoing off shiny tiles.

Pip fluffed feathers and sang the old tune once more, slow and sweet. The duck joined in, squeaky and proud. Their voices blended like bubbles popping in a warm bath. The bathroom felt like a school, a gym, and a little concert hall, all at once.

Pip looked into the mirror and smiled. “Today, I took initiative to learn and teach. I shared my tradition. I moved my body with sports. I used my mind with education. I can do all these things, one small step at a time.”

The duck nodded. “And we can help each other.”

Pip chirped, “That is the best lesson of all: We learn better when we try, share, and care.”

The End.
