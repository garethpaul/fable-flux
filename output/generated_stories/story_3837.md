# Tully the Tortoise and the Garden Shed Surprise

The garden shed smelled like warm wood and fresh soil. Sunlight slid through a tiny window and made golden dust dots dance. In the quiet corner, a wise tortoise named Tully blinked his gentle eyes. His shell was smooth and green like a leaf after rain.

Tully loved the garden shed. He loved the soft brush bristles that tickled his nose. He loved the sound of the tiny drip drop from a leaky watering can. Most of all, Tully loved helping friends.

One breezy morning, Tully heard a soft sniffle. He tucked his head out of his shell and listened. Sniff. Sniff. It came from behind a stack of flower pots.

“Hello?” Tully called in a calm, warm voice. “Is someone there?”

A little field mouse peeked out. Her whiskers trembled. “It’s me,” she squeaked. “I am Mimi. I am trying to build a cozy nest. But I can’t reach the soft straw on that high shelf.”

Tully looked up. On the top shelf sat a neat bundle of straw. It smelled sweet, like summer. But it was high, very high, even for a tall garden rake.

Tully smiled kindly. “We can solve this together,” he said. “Friends help friends.”

Mimi’s ears perked. “Really? Thank you!”

Just then, the shed door creaked again. A bumblebee buzzed in, round and fuzzy. “Bzz-hello,” hummed the bee. “I am Bibi. My wing is a little sore. I need a quiet place to rest. But the straw is too high for me too.”

Tully nodded. “Welcome, Bibi. We will find a way. Let’s think with calm hearts.”

They sat in a small circle on the cool floor. Tully could feel the smooth boards under his feet. He took a slow breath. “First,” he said softly, “we will talk kindly and make a plan.”

Mimi squeaked, “Maybe I can climb the rake?”

Bibi buzzed, “Bzz-maybe I can push the straw down?”

Tully shook his head gently. “The rake may wobble. And pushing might hurt your wing. Let’s try something safer.”

He looked around the shed. There was a short stool with round legs. There was a sturdy broom. There was a big clay pot that felt cool to the touch.

Tully smiled. “We can make a steady tower. Not too tall. Not too wobbly. We will stack the stool and the pot, and I will stand beside them. If anything wiggles, my shell will keep it safe.”

Mimi’s whiskers twitched with hope. “I can tie the broom to the stool with this twine,” she said, pulling a soft string from the floor. “It will hold the broom still.”

Bibi buzzed softly. “I can watch and say ‘Left’ or ‘Right’ if the tower leans.”

“Teamwork,” said Tully. “That is friendship.”

They worked slowly. The stool made a gentle tap-tap on the floor. Mimi’s tiny paws wrapped the twine around and around. It felt scratchy but strong. Tully pressed his shell against the stool to steady it. Bibi hovered and hummed, “Right a little… now left a little… perfect!”

“Ready,” said Tully.

Mimi climbed the stool. Her little feet pat-patted each step. She held the broom like a long arm and reached up, up, up. The straw rustled. It smelled like sunshine. “I can almost touch it!” she squeaked.

The tower wobbled just a tiny bit.

“Pause,” said Tully in a calm voice. He pushed his shell closer to the stool. The wobble stopped. “Slow and steady.”

Mimi tried again. Rustle, rustle. The straw slid, then floomp! It landed softly on Tully’s shell, then slid down to the floor like a golden blanket.

“We did it!” Bibi buzzed a happy song. “Bzz-hooray!”

Mimi hugged the straw with her tiny paws. Her eyes shone. “Thank you, Tully. Thank you, Bibi. I can make a cozy nest now.”

Tully smiled. “I am glad. But we have one more friend to help.”

He looked at Bibi’s wing. It was a little bent. The air in the shed felt cool and gentle. “Let’s make a comfy corner for Bibi,” Tully said. “Mimi, may we share some straw?”

Mimi nodded. “Yes! There is plenty.”

Together, they shaped a soft bed in a quiet corner. Tully added a leaf for shade. It felt smooth and cool. Bibi curled into the straw with a sweet hum. “Bzz-thank you,” she whispered. “My wing feels better already.”

Mimi pat-patted her nest. “I have a cozy home now,” she said. “Tonight, I will share crumbs if you visit.”

Tully’s heart felt warm, like sunshine on his shell. He looked at his friends. “Today we used kind words. We listened. We made a safe plan. We shared. That is friendship.”

Mimi smiled. “Friendship means helping, even when it is hard.”

Bibi added, “Friendship means gentle voices and brave hearts.”

Tully nodded. “Yes. When we talk calmly and care for each other, problems can become plans. And plans can become happy endings.”

The shed grew quiet and peaceful. The dust dots danced in the light. Mimi snuggled into her nest. Bibi hummed a sleepy tune. Tully settled by the door, his shell warm, his smile soft.

He whispered, “Friends together can solve anything, one kind step at a time.”

The End.
