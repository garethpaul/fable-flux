# Olli Otter’s Rooftop Word Parade

Olli was a playful otter with bright eyes and a quick tail. He lived in a cozy house with a flat rooftop. The rooftop felt warm in the sun and cool in the breeze. Olli loved to climb up, peek at the sky, and listen to city sounds—honk, hum, whoosh!

Today, Olli had a big plan. “I will host a Rooftop Word Parade!” he said, clapping his little paws. “We will celebrate words. Words are sounds. Words are feelings. Words help us share.”

Olli wanted to be ready. He spread a soft blanket. He set out small paper flags. Each flag had a sweet word written on it: hello, please, thank you, sorry, yes, no, maybe, friend. He taped them along the low wall. The flags flapped gently—flip, flap, flip!

“Preparation first,” Olli said proudly. He made a list. “Water bottle—check. Sun hat—check. Word cards—check.” He put the cards in a tiny backpack and zipped it up. Zzzip!

Neighbors came to the rooftop. Mia the mouse brought cheese crackers. Ben the pigeon fluttered down with a ribbon. Lala the ladybug landed on Olli’s nose. “Tickle!” Olli giggled.

“Welcome to the Word Parade!” Olli said. “We will try new words. We will listen. We will be brave. We will be kind.”

Mia wiggled her whiskers. “I want to learn a polite word,” she squeaked.

Olli pulled a card. “This word is please,” he said. “Please is soft and sweet. Please is a smile word.”

Mia tried it. “Please,” she said. The word felt gentle. Her ears relaxed. “Please, may I have a cracker?” she asked.

“Of course,” Olli said. “Thank you for asking with please.”

Ben puffed his chest. “I want a sky word,” he cooed.

Olli pulled another card. “This word is hello,” he said. “Hello opens doors. Hello makes friends.” He shaded his eyes and waved to the tall buildings. “Hello, sky! Hello, sun!”

Ben spread his wings. “Hello!” he called to the clouds. The clouds looked like big pillows floating by.

Lala buzzed softly. “I like tiny words,” she said. “Short and shiny.”

Olli smiled. “This word is yes,” he said. “Yes feels bright, like a lantern.” He held up a yellow flag. “Yes can say, let’s try!”

“Yes!” Lala chirped, and did a tiny loop in the air.

A gust of wind rose—whooosh! The flags danced faster. One flag tugged free and flew—flip, flip—up and over the edge.

“Oh no!” Olli cried. “My thank you flag!”

Olli’s tummy felt wobbly. He wanted to stop the parade. But he took a slow breath in… and a slow breath out. “We can be resilient,” he said softly. “When something goes wrong, we try again.”

He checked his backpack. “I prepared an extra marker,” he said. He found a blank card. He wrote t-h-a-n-k y-o-u in big round letters. His paw shook, but he kept going. “There,” he said. “We fixed it.”

Mia clapped. “You did it!”

Olli held up the new flag. “Thank you is a warm word,” he said. “It feels like a hug.” He gave the flag a gentle hug, then taped it tight.

Just then, a small cat named Nori peeked from the stair door. “I’m shy,” Nori whispered.

Olli’s voice was calm and kind. “Welcome, Nori. You can listen first,” he said. “Listening is part of language too.”

Nori sat by the blanket. She smelled the cheese crackers. She hears the city hum. She watched the flags sway—flip, flap, flip.

Olli pulled another card. “This word is sorry,” he said. “Sorry helps fix little hurts.”

Ben’s wing brushed Mia’s cracker plate. A cracker fell. “Oops!” Ben said.

Olli smiled. “You can try the sorry word.”

Ben took a breath. “Sorry, Mia,” he cooed.

Mia’s whiskers twitched. “Thank you for the sorry,” she said. “Would you like to share a cracker?”

Ben bobbed. “Yes, please!”

Nori’s eyes brightened. She whispered, “Hello.”

Olli beamed. “Hello, Nori!” he answered. “Thank you for your brave word.”

The wind came back—whooosh!—and tugged at Olli’s sun hat. The hat tumbled and bumped—a-thump!—into a flower pot. Olli blinked. His heart went bump-bump-bump. He felt a little scared.

“We can try again,” he told himself. He picked up the hat, brushed off the dust, and tied the strap. “There. Prepared and safe.”

Now it was time for the parade. Olli tapped a tiny drum. “Step, step,” he chanted. They marched in a small circle. With each step, they shared a word.

Mia: “Please.”

Ben: “Hello!”

Lala: “Yes!”

Olli: “Thank you.”

Nori took a big breath. “Friend,” she said.

The word friend felt soft and bright. It sounded like sunshine. Everyone smiled. The flags waved. The rooftop smelled like warm stone and tiny flowers. The sky looked wide and kind.

Olli raised his paws. “Words help us share,” he said. “Preparation helps us feel ready. Resilience helps us try again.”

Nori nodded. “Words are like little bridges,” she said.

Olli grinned. “Yes. We can cross together.”

They sat on the blanket and munched crackers. Olli poured water and passed cups. “Please,” “thank you,” and “hello” floated in the air like pretty bubbles.

As the sun slid lower, Olli packed up carefully. He checked his list—hat, marker, cards. He tied the flags so they would not fly away. He felt proud, calm, and glad.

“Today I learned,” Olli said, “that words are helpful tools. When we prepare, we feel safe. When trouble comes, we breathe and try again.”

Everyone nodded. The rooftop hummed softly. The city lights began to twinkle.

“Good-bye, rooftop,” Olli said gently. “Good-bye, sky. Thank you for a good day.”

The End.
