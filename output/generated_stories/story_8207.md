# Ollie Otter’s Helpful Ride

Ollie the playful otter buckled into the family car seat with a soft click. The car smelled like warm crackers and pine tree air freshener. The seats were cozy and the windows were bright with sun. Ollie wiggled his whiskers. He loved car rides.

“Ready, Ollie?” asked Mama Otter, turning the key. The engine hummed like a happy bee.

“Ready!” said Ollie. He kicked his little webbed feet and giggled. “Where are we going?”

“We’re taking boxes of books to the community center,” said Mama. “Families from around the world meet there to share stories.”

“Stories from everywhere?” Ollie’s eyes grew wide. “Like ocean stories and mountain stories?”

“Yes,” said Mama. “We are part of a big world. We help each other. That’s called being a good neighbor and a global friend.”

Ollie nodded. “I want to help!”

The car rolled down the street. Ollie watched trees wave hello. He smelled fresh bread from a bakery. He heard birds tweet-tweet in the sky.

At a red light, Ollie saw a squirrel hurrying with a big nut. “That nut looks heavy,” said Ollie. “I feel… a little tug in my heart.”

“That feeling is empathy,” said Mama. “It means you notice how others feel, and you care.”

“I care,” whispered Ollie. He held the box on his lap gently, like it was a sleepy kitten.

Soon, fat, gray clouds gathered. Raindrops pattered on the windows, tap-tap-tap. The world looked shiny and wet. The wipers swished. Swish, swish.

They turned a corner and—oh!—there was a small car stopped by the curb. A goat family stood nearby. A tiny goat kid held a droopy paper bag. The bag had torn open. Apples rolled on the wet sidewalk, plip, plop, patter.

Ollie gasped. “The apples are escaping!”

Mama pulled over safely. “Let’s ask if they need help.”

Ollie’s tummy fluttered. He remembered the tug in his heart. He took a deep breath. “I can help.”

Mama cracked the window. “Hello! Do you need a hand?”

Papa Goat smiled, a little worried. “Thank you. Our bag ripped. We must bring food to the center for tonight’s world dinner.”

“We can help,” said Mama. She parked and put on her bright yellow raincoat. Ollie zipped into his tiny blue raincoat and stepped out. The rain was cool and tickly on his fur. He could smell wet grass and apples. He could hear the soft glug-glug of a puddle by his feet.

“Hi,” Ollie said to the goat kid. “I’m Ollie.”

“I’m Gigi,” said the kid, sniffing. “Our apples fell. I feel sad.”

Ollie looked into Gigi’s eyes. They were shiny with little tears. He felt the tug in his heart again. “I hear you,” he said softly. “When my blocks fall, I feel sad too.” He held out his paw. “We can pick them up together.”

Gigi sniffled, then nodded. “Together.”

Ollie and Gigi bent down. Plop—an apple into the box. Plop—another apple. The apples were cool and smooth, and they smelled sweet. Mama and Papa Goat found a stronger bag. Papa Goat said, “Teamwork makes the load lighter.”

Ollie smiled. “Teamwork means we help like a team!”

A car splashed by, and water sprinkled their paws. Gigi giggled. “Splashy!”

Soon all the apples were safe. The goat family smiled big smiles. “Thank you,” said Mama Goat. “Tonight families from many places will share food and stories. Because of you, we will have apple slices.”

“That’s global friendship,” Mama Otter said. “We care for people near and far.”

Gigi looked shy. “Would you like to ride with us to the center? We can go together.”

Ollie looked at Mama. “Can we?”

“Yes,” said Mama. “We can follow each other. We’ll bring the books, and you bring the apples.”

Back in the family car, the seats felt warm and happy. Ollie buckled in. His heart felt full, like a basket with no rips. “Mama,” he said, “my tummy feels like hot cocoa.”

“That feeling is pride,” said Mama. “You used empathy. You listened, you understood, and you helped. That is community service.”

Ollie beamed. “Empathy means I look, I listen, and I care.”

At the community center, people from many places smiled and waved. A fox family brought rice. A panda family brought tea. A robin flew in with napkins. The room smelled like cinnamon, apples, and flowers. There were stories in many voices, soft and loud, fast and slow.

Ollie and Gigi set apples on a table. “Thank you, helpers,” said the center leader. “When we work together, everyone belongs.”

Ollie held up a book from his box. “Story time?” he asked.

Gigi nodded. “Yes!”

They sat on a soft rug. Ollie read with Mama’s help. He showed the pictures, bright like rainbows. Children leaned close. They listened. They laughed. They clapped.

After the story, Gigi whispered, “When my bag ripped, I felt scared. You helped me feel safe.”

Ollie whispered back, “When I saw your eyes, I felt your feelings. That told me what to do.”

On the ride home, the night was calm. The car hummed a sleepy song. Raindrops ticked lightly on the roof. Ollie yawned a big, round yawn.

“What did we learn today?” Mama asked.

Ollie smiled, eyes drooping. “Empathy is my heart’s tug. Teamwork is helping hands. Being a global friend means we care for everyone, everywhere.”

Mama squeezed his paw. “Yes.”

Ollie sighed, cozy and proud. “I can do that every day.”

The End.
