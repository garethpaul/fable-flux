# Toby's Wobbly Workshop Day

The garage workshop smelled like sawdust and paint. Toby the tortoise looked at his workbench with worried eyes. Yesterday, he had tried to build a birdhouse for his friend Robin. But everything went wrong!

"Oh no," Toby sighed, looking at the crooked pieces of wood scattered everywhere. The hammer lay on the floor. Paint was spilled on his shell. "I made such a mess. My hands were too shaky. My movements were too fast."

Toby's friend Robin flew into the workshop. "Good morning, Toby! How's my birdhouse coming along?"

Toby's head drooped into his shell. "I'm sorry, Robin. I tried to build it yesterday, but I was rushing. My coordination was all mixed up. The wood pieces don't fit together. I even got paint on everything!"

Robin perched gently on the workbench. "What happened, friend?"

"I was so excited to surprise you," Toby explained. "I grabbed the hammer too quickly. I didn't measure carefully. My flippers moved too fast, and everything got jumbled up. Now your birdhouse is ruined!"

Robin's eyes sparkled kindly. "Toby, it's okay to make mistakes. But maybe we can try again together?"

Toby peeked out of his shell. "Really? You're not upset?"

"Of course not!" chirped Robin. "But first, let's clean up this mess and take care of you."

Together, they swept up the wood shavings. Robin helped Toby wash the paint off his shell with warm, soapy water. It felt good to be clean again.

"Now," said Robin, "let's try building again, but slowly this time."

Toby nodded. "You're right. I need to move more carefully."

They started fresh. Toby took three deep breaths. He picked up one piece of wood with both flippers, moving slowly and steadily. "One... two... three," he counted as he placed it down.

"Perfect!" cheered Robin. "See how much better that is?"

Next, Toby measured twice before cutting. His movements were smooth and controlled. When he hammered, he went tap-tap-tap instead of BANG-BANG-BANG.

"My coordination feels so much better when I slow down," Toby smiled. "My flippers know exactly where to go!"

Robin helped by holding pieces steady while Toby worked. They painted together, using careful brush strokes. No drips this time!

"Look at those steady hands!" Robin sang. "You're doing wonderfully!"

When they finished, the birdhouse was beautiful. The walls were straight. The roof fit perfectly. The paint was smooth and bright blue.

"Thank you for being patient with me," Toby said. "I learned that good coordination means taking my time and moving with purpose."

Robin hugged his friend's neck. "And I learned that helping friends feels even better than getting presents!"

Toby cleaned his brushes carefully and put away his tools. "Taking care of my workspace is important too," he said. "When everything is organized, I can work better."

They hung the birdhouse on a strong tree branch. Robin flew inside and peeked out the little round door.

"It's perfect!" Robin chirped happily. "And you know what? Building it together made it even more special."

Toby smiled proudly. "Next time I'll remember: slow and steady movements, deep breaths, and asking for help when I need it. That's how to have good coordination!"

From that day on, Toby's workshop was always organized, and his projects turned out beautifully because he learned to move with care and purpose.

The End.
