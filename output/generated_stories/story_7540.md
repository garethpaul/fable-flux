# Ollie the Otter Learns to Share

Ollie the otter loved bath time more than anything in the whole wide world. He would splash and dive and blow bubbles until the warm water turned cold. His sleek brown fur would shimmer as he twisted and turned, making the most wonderful splashing sounds.

But today was different. When Ollie arrived at the big community bathroom by the pond, he found three little ducklings already there.

"Quack, quack! We were here first!" said Penny, the smallest duckling. Her yellow feathers were all fluffy and dry.

"But I always take my bath at this time!" Ollie said, his whiskers twitching with disappointment. The bathroom had the most perfect warm water and the shiniest mirrors, and Ollie had been looking forward to his bath all morning.

"We need to get clean for the pond party tonight," chirped Danny, shaking his tiny wings. "Mama Duck said we must be sparkling clean!"

Ollie's dark eyes grew sad. He really wanted his bath, but he could see the ducklings needed to get ready too. The warm steam from the bathtub smelled like lavender soap, and he could hear the gentle bubble sounds calling to him.

"Maybe we can work something out," Ollie said thoughtfully, sitting on his fluffy tail. "What if we take turns? That way everyone gets clean!"

"How would that work?" asked the third duckling, Daisy, tilting her head.

Ollie's eyes lit up with an idea. "Well, you ducklings are much smaller than me. What if you all bathe together for ten minutes, then I take my turn for ten minutes? We could even help each other!"

The ducklings looked at each other and whispered softly. "Quack, quack, quack."

"That sounds fair," Penny said with a smile. "But what if we help each other get extra clean? You could reach the high spots we can't reach!"

"And we could help you wash behind your ears!" added Danny excitedly.

Ollie clapped his paws together. "What a wonderful idea! We could be a cleaning team!"

So they all climbed into the warm, bubbly water together. Ollie gently helped the ducklings wash their backs with his soft paws, while the ducklings used their little beaks to clean the spots behind Ollie's ears that he always missed.

"This is so much more fun than bathing alone!" giggled Daisy as she splashed gently.

"And we're all getting super clean!" Ollie laughed, his whiskers dripping with soapy bubbles.

When they were all squeaky clean, they helped each other dry off with the fluffy towels. The ducklings' feathers were bright and shiny, and Ollie's fur was soft and smooth.

"Thank you for sharing the bathroom, Ollie," said Penny. "And thank you for helping us get ready for the party!"

"Thank you for letting me join your cleaning team," Ollie replied warmly. "This was much better than bathing by myself. We all got clean AND had fun together!"

As they walked out of the bathroom together, Ollie felt proud and happy. He had learned that when friends need the same thing, talking about it and taking turns makes everyone happy. Sometimes sharing makes everything more fun!

From that day on, Ollie and the ducklings always bathed together, taking turns and helping each other stay clean and happy.

The End.
