# Sunny the Squirrel and the Closet Clean-Up

Sunny the Squirrel loved clever ideas. She was small, quick, and very resourceful. One rainy day, Sunny found a secret place in the house: a cozy closet hideaway. It was dark but snug, with soft sweaters like puffy clouds and a basket of buttons that clicked like tiny raindrops.

“Ooh! What a perfect hideaway,” Sunny whispered, her tail twitching. She tiptoed inside. It smelled dusty, like old leaves after a storm.

Sunny wrinkled her nose. “Achoo!” she sneezed. “Hmm… this closet needs care.”

Inside the closet, Sunny found her friends: Pip the Mouse, Dot the Ladybug, and Gus the Goldfinch. They had made a tiny tea table from a shoe box and buttons.

“Welcome, Sunny!” chirped Gus. “We have apple-scent tea!”

Sunny smiled. “Thank you! But first, we need to make this place clean and fair for everyone.”

“Why?” asked Pip, nibbling a crumb.

Sunny pointed with her paw. “Look! Crumbs on the floor. Sticky cups. A muddy sock. And a little puddle by the boots. If we don’t clean, germs can grow. Germs can make us sick.”

Dot buzzed. “I don’t like feeling sick. My wings feel heavy when I am ill.”

Sunny nodded. “We can fix it. We will keep our hideaway clean. That is healthy, and it is fair—justice means we take care of our shared space so everyone feels safe.”

Pip’s ears drooped. “But I am small. What can I do?”

“You can do a lot,” Sunny said kindly. “We will make a plan.”

Sunny made a tiny chart from a napkin. She drew simple pictures:
- A paw washing
- A broom sweeping
- A cup rinsing
- A nose blowing into a tissue

“First,” Sunny said, “we wash our paws before tea.” She led the way to a little water dish with gentle soap that smelled like lemon. The bubbles felt soft and tickly.

“Rub, rub, rub,” Sunny sang. “Front, back, between, and thumbs. Rinse and dry. Good job, everyone!”

Gus flapped happily. “My feathers feel fresh!”

“Second,” said Sunny, “we clean our cozy spot.” She handed Pip a tiny broom made from a twig and string. Pip swept the crumbs, swish-swish, into a neat pile. Dot wiped the cups with a clean cloth, squeak-squeak, until they shone. Sunny found the muddy sock’s owner. “This sock lives in the laundry basket,” she said, and put it there.

“Third,” Sunny said, “we set fair rules.” She spoke slowly, so everyone understood. “We share the space. We put trash in the bin. We sneeze and cough into our elbows or a tissue. We wash paws after we play, after we sneeze, and before we eat. That is healthy. That is just.”

Pip raised a tiny hand. “What about spills?”

“Good question,” said Sunny. “We clean spills right away.” She showed them how to dab and wipe. “Dab-dab, wipe-wipe!”

They all practiced. It felt good. The closet began to smell like lemon and apple tea, not dust. Light peeked in through the doorway. The sweaters looked fluffy and clean. The buttons clicked happily in their basket.

Soon, someone peeked in. It was Rocco the Raccoon. He loved snacks and sometimes forgot the rules.

“Hi, friends!” Rocco grinned. He stepped inside with sticky paws. Splat! A blob of jam fell onto the floor.

Pip gasped. “Oh no!”

Sunny took a calm breath. She did not yell. She stood up tall and gentle. “Rocco,” she said, “we have new rules to keep everyone healthy and safe. We clean our paws before we come in.”

Rocco looked at his paws. “I… forgot,” he said softly. “I’m sorry.”

Sunny smiled. “That’s okay. We can fix it. Justice means when a rule is broken, we make it right. Will you help us clean the jam and then wash your paws?”

Rocco nodded. “Yes!” He got a cloth and wiped the jam with Sunny. Dab-dab, wipe-wipe. Then he washed his paws—rub, rub, rub—front, back, between, and thumbs. He dried them and sniffed. “Mmm, lemony!”

“Great job,” Dot buzzed. “Now we can have apple tea.”

They sat at the shoe-box table. The tea was warm and smelled sweet. The cups were shiny. The floor was clean. The closet felt bright and safe.

Sunny raised her cup. “To healthy habits! To clean paws! To fairness for everyone!”

“To healthy habits!” they all cheered.

As they sipped, they sang a little song:
“Wash, wash, wash our paws,
Keep our friends okay.
Clean and kind and fair we are,
Every single day!”

After tea, they put the cups in the wash bin, swept the floor, and checked the chart. Pip put a gold sticker by “Paw Washing.” Dot put a sticker by “Cup Rinsing.” Rocco put a sticker by “Spill Cleaning.” Gus put a sticker by “Sneeze into Elbow.”

Sunny looked around, happy. The closet hideaway was tidy, fresh, and fair. Everyone felt safe. Everyone helped.

Sunny said, “When we keep clean, we keep kind. Healthy habits help us play more and worry less. And when we follow fair rules, that is justice.”

Rocco smiled. “I like our clean, fair hideaway.”

“Me too!” said everyone.

They snuggled into the soft sweaters, listening to the gentle patter of rain outside. The lemon scent mixed with apple tea, warm and cozy. Sunny’s tail curled around her like a soft blanket.

Sunny whispered, “We did it together.”

The End.
