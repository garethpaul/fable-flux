# Polly's Big Heart Cookies

In a cozy, warm kitchen filled with the sweet smell of vanilla and cinnamon, lived a beautiful green parrot named Polly. Her feathers sparkled like emeralds in the morning sunlight streaming through the window. Polly loved to perch on the wooden spoon rack and watch Grandma Rose bake delicious treats.

"Good morning, Polly!" chirped Grandma Rose as she tied her flowery apron. "What shall we bake today?"

Polly fluffed her bright feathers and squawked excitedly, "Cookies! Pretty cookies!"

The kitchen was Polly's favorite place. The warm oven made everything feel snug, and the counters were always dusted with flour that tickled her tiny feet. Today, something special was happening. Grandma Rose was making heart-shaped sugar cookies for the neighborhood bake sale.

As Grandma Rose mixed the buttery dough, Polly watched with her bright, curious eyes. "Can Polly help?" she asked, tilting her colorful head.

"Of course, my dear! You can be my special helper," Grandma Rose smiled.

Polly felt proud and important. She carefully carried small measuring spoons in her beak and even helped sprinkle rainbow sprinkles on the cookies. The kitchen smelled like happiness itself - warm, sweet, and full of love.

When the first batch came out of the oven, golden and perfect, Polly's tummy rumbled. "Mmm! Cookies smell yummy!" she squawked.

But then Polly heard something that made her pause. Through the open window came the sound of crying. It was little Tommy from next door.

"What's wrong, Tommy?" called Grandma Rose from the window.

"My mom is sick, and we don't have anything sweet for dessert," Tommy sniffled. "And I really wanted to help with the bake sale, but we don't have money for ingredients."

Polly's heart felt heavy like a stone. She looked at the beautiful cookies cooling on the rack. They were supposed to be sold to raise money for the new playground. But Tommy looked so sad.

Polly took a deep breath, puffing out her chest feathers. Even though she was just a small parrot, she wanted to help. "Grandma Rose," she whispered, "can we give Tommy some cookies?"

Grandma Rose's eyes twinkled with pride. "What a wonderful idea, Polly! But that means we'll have fewer cookies to sell."

Polly thought for a moment, then had an even bigger idea. "We make MORE cookies! Polly help extra hard!"

And that's exactly what they did. Polly worked harder than ever before, even though her little wings got tired. She helped measure, mix, and decorate. The kitchen grew even warmer and more wonderful as they baked batch after batch.

When they finished, they had made twice as many cookies as planned! Polly carefully carried a plate of the prettiest heart cookies to Tommy. His face lit up like sunshine.

"Thank you, Polly! You're the kindest parrot ever!" Tommy hugged the plate gently.

At the bake sale, people loved the cookies so much that they bought every single one. The playground fund had more money than ever before!

That evening, as Polly snuggled in her cozy cage, Grandma Rose stroked her soft feathers. "Polly, you showed such generosity today. When we share what we have, wonderful things happen."

Polly felt warm and happy inside, warmer than even the cozy kitchen. She had learned that being generous made her heart feel as big as the whole world.

"Sharing is caring!" Polly chirped sleepily, and dreamed of baking more cookies to share tomorrow.

The End.
