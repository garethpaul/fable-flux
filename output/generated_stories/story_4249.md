# Pippa the Penguin’s Cozy Guest Room Day

Pippa was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a bouncy waddle. One sunny morning, Pippa stayed in the cozy guest room at her friend Tia’s house. The guest room was soft and warm, with a fluffy rug, a little plant in the window, and a big quilt that smelled like clean soap and sunshine.

“Good morning, guest room!” Pippa chirped. She stretched her wings wide. “I will take good care of myself today.”

Pippa touched her beak and smiled. “First, self-care!” she said. She washed her face with a gentle cloth. Splash, splash! The water felt cool and tickly. She brushed her beak with a tiny, soft brush. Swish, swish! The minty smell made her giggle. She took deep belly breaths. In… and out… slow and calm. Her tummy felt happy.

Knock, knock! Tia, a kind turtle, peeked in. “Good morning, Pippa! Do you feel okay?”

“I feel great!” Pippa said. “I’m doing my morning care. When I care for me, I have energy for you!”

Tia smiled. “That is wise. I have a small problem. My toy shelf is messy. I want to clean it for our friends who are visiting. But my shell feels tired.”

Pippa waddled over and patted Tia’s shell gently. “Let’s help each other. We can use care and kindness.”

They looked around the guest room. Books were in a little pile. A box of crayons lay open like a rainbow. A tiny trash bin stood by the door.

Pippa put a hand on her tummy and took another deep breath. “Step one: rest a bit,” she said. “Self-care helps us do hard things.”

They sat on the soft quilt for a minute. The quilt felt warm and smooth. Outside, a bird sang, tweet-tweet. Tia’s eyes brightened. “I feel a little better,” Tia said.

“Step two: drink water,” Pippa said. They sipped cool water from a cup. The water sounded like glug-glug and felt fresh.

“Step three: make a plan,” Pippa said. “We will sort, clean, and be gentle with the Earth.”

“How do we do that?” Tia asked.

Pippa tapped her flipper to her beak. “We can learn a new skill! Let’s sort. Keep. Share. Recycle.”

They started with toys on the rug. Pippa held up a red car. “Keep or share?”

Tia smiled. “Keep. I play with that.”

Pippa held up a puzzle with missing pieces. “Maybe recycle?” she asked.

“We can reuse the picture on the back for art,” Tia said. “That is Earth-care!”

They made three neat piles: Keep, Share, and Reuse/Recycle. The crayons clicked as they went back into their box—click, click. The plant leaves rustled when Pippa dusted—shhh, shhh. The room began to shine.

“Thank you, Pippa,” Tia said. “You are generous.”

Pippa nodded. “Sharing is caring. But I must care for me, too.” She checked her feelings. “I feel a little tired now.”

Tia looked worried. “Do we stop?”

Pippa smiled and shook her head. “We take a self-care break. Then we finish.” She lay on the fluffy rug and stretched her toes. “Let’s do calm breaths. Smell the flower.” She sniffed the clean quilt. “Blow the candle.” She blew softly—whoo.

Tia copied her—slow in, slow out. Their bodies felt calm, like floating on a gentle wave.

Just then, their friends Mina the mouse and Benji the bear peeked into the guest room. “Can we help?” asked Mina.

“Yes!” Pippa said. “We are practicing a skill. Sort, clean, and care.”

Benji picked up tiny blocks. “I can stack,” he said. Clack, clack. Mina tied a little ribbon around the Share box. “This will go to friends who need toys,” she squeaked.

Pippa wiped the low shelf with a soft cloth. “Round and round,” she sang. “Small circles so the shelf shines.” The lemon smell of the cleaner filled the air. Fresh and bright!

When the last toy found a home, the guest room looked happy. The rug was smooth. The books stood like little soldiers. The plant smiled in the sun. The air felt light and clean.

“Let’s wash our hands,” Pippa said. “Self-care after cleaning.” They rubbed soap—squish, squish—then rinsed—splash! Their hands smelled like oranges.

Tia opened the window a crack. A gentle breeze danced in. “Thank you, friends,” Tia said. “You were so generous and careful.”

Pippa hugged Tia. “We did it together. We took care of ourselves, each other, and the Earth.”

Mina nodded. “I learned to sort.”

Benji grinned. “I learned to stack.”

Pippa clapped. “I learned to listen to my body. When I rest, drink water, breathe, and wash, I feel strong. Then I can help more.”

They carried the Share box to the door. “We will give these toys to the kids at the library,” Tia said.

Pippa felt warm and proud. “Self-care first. Then we share our best selves.”

Everyone smiled. The guest room was cozy and calm, ready for new guests and new stories.

And the clear lesson? Taking care of your body and feelings helps you be kind, learn new skills, and care for the world, too.

The End.
