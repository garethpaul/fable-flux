# Pippa the Rooftop Helper

Pippa was a cheerful penguin who loved to waddle and whistle. She didn’t live on ice. She lived in a busy city, on a sunny rooftop garden. The rooftop had planters full of leafy lettuce, bright marigolds, and tiny trees in big pots. The air smelled like wet soil and sweet flowers, and the wind made the wind chimes sing, ting-a-ling!

Each morning, Pippa popped out of her cozy nest by the skylight. “Good morning, green friends!” she chirped, flapping her small wings. She watered the plants with a tiny blue watering can. Drip, drip, drip. The water sparkled in the sun. Pippa loved to help the earth.

One warm day, Pippa heard footsteps on the stairs. Clomp, clomp. The rooftop door squeaked open. Her neighbors arrived: Mia the mouse, Tiko the turtle, and Buzzy the bee from the hive box. They all shared the garden. They all cared for the plants.

“Hi, Pippa!” said Mia, with twitchy whiskers. “Let’s have a picnic!”

“Let’s pick lettuce,” said Tiko slowly.

“Bzz-zz,” sang Buzzy. “Flowers need room. Don’t crush!”

Pippa nodded. “We will take turns. We will be gentle. We will keep our rooftop clean,” she said proudly.

First, they set a blanket near the planter boxes. Pippa smelled the warm bread Mia had brought. It smelled like sunshine and toast. Tiko brought carrot sticks, crunchy and sweet. Buzzy buzzed happily over the marigolds.

Pippa saw a crinkly bag near the drain. It was shiny and light. The wind was pushing it. Rustle, rustle. “Oh no,” she said. “Trash does not belong here. It can fly away and hurt birds. It can block the water. The plants need clean water.” She picked up the bag with two careful flippers.

Mia nodded. “We can use the blue bin,” she said. “That bin is for recycling.”

“Yes,” said Pippa. “We sort our trash. That is caring for our world.” She dropped the bag into the blue bin. Clink!

“Good job,” said Tiko. “Small helps are big helps.”

Then they began to pick lettuce for the picnic. Pippa waited. Mia picked one leaf. Tiko picked one leaf. Pippa picked one leaf. Buzzy buzzed, “Taking turns keeps plants safe.”

Pippa saw a big, crunchy leaf and reached out fast. “I want that one!” she chirped. But Tiko’s slow hand was already there.

Tiko looked up. “I was next,” he said softly.

Pippa stopped. She felt her cheeks warm. She pulled her flipper back. “I’m sorry, Tiko,” she said. “I will wait. I will use gentle hands.”

“Thank you,” said Tiko, smiling. He picked the leaf and passed the basket to Pippa. “Your turn.”

Pippa’s heart felt light again. Waiting was hard, but it felt good to share.

As they ate, the wind grew stronger. Whoosh! The napkins fluttered. One napkin flew toward the planter. Pippa reached and grabbed it. “Got it!” She pressed it under a little stone.

“We should keep our things in the basket,” said Mia. “That way, nothing blows away.”

“That is being responsible,” said Pippa. “We take care of our things and our rooftop.”

After lunch, Pippa noticed muddy footprints near the tiny trees. Splish, splash. The dirt had spilled out of a pot. “Oops,” said Mia. “I stepped too close.”

Buzzy buzzed in a worried voice. “Roots need space. This is the trees’ place.”

Pippa nodded. “This planter is the trees’ home. We respect personal boundaries. That means we stay outside their space unless we are helping.”

“I will fix it,” said Mia. She scooped the dirt back with a little trowel. Pippa gently patted the soil. Tiko brought water. Drip, drip, drip. The tiny trees looked happy again, their leaves shiny and green.

Next, they did a rooftop clean-up. Pippa gave each friend a job. “Mia, please check the corners for tiny trash. Tiko, please sweep the path. Buzzy, please look for plastic near the flowers.”

“Bzz-yes!” buzzed Buzzy.

They sang while they worked. “Pick it up, sort it right, keep our rooftop clean and bright!” The broom swished. The bin lids clicked. The sun warmed their backs. The air smelled fresh and leafy.

When the cleaning was done, Pippa brought out the watering can. “Who would like to water?”

Mia raised her paw, then paused. “Let’s take turns,” she said. “My paws are small. I can water the marigolds.”

“I can water the lettuce,” said Tiko.

“I can water the tiny trees,” said Pippa.

“Bzz-zz,” buzzed Buzzy. “I will make sure the flowers are safe.”

They each took a turn, moving slowly and carefully. Drip, drip. Plip, plop. The plants drank happily.

Just then, a big gust of wind whooshed again. Whoosh! The rooftop door swung open, then banged shut. Bang! A loose paper flew up. Pippa caught it. She looked at her friends. “We can hang a sign,” she said, “to remind everyone.”

Together they drew a bright sign with big simple words:

“Be Kind to Our Rooftop.
Put Trash in the Bin.
Take Turns.
Use Gentle Hands.
Ask Before You Step in a Plant’s Home.”

They taped the sign inside the door, safe from the wind. Pippa stepped back. “We did it,” she said, chest puffed with pride.

The sun started to soften, and the sky turned orange and pink. The city sounds grew quiet. The wind chimes whispered, ting-ting.

Pippa snuggled by the skylight. Her heart felt cozy and calm. “We cared for our rooftop,” she said. “We were responsible. We respected space. We took turns.”

Mia yawned. “And we had fun,” she added.

“Bzz-zz,” hummed Buzzy. “The plants say thank you.”

Tiko nodded. “Small helps make a big, green world.”

Pippa smiled. “We can help the earth every day,” she said. “When we care for our place, we care for everyone.”

The End.
