# Nutmeg’s Tinker Time

In a cozy basement lab under the stairs, a resourceful squirrel named Nutmeg wore tiny blue goggles and a bright yellow apron. The room smelled like warm wood and orange soap. Little tools lined up on a shelf—soft brushes, safe scissors, and a small, friendly timer that went tick-tick-tick.

“Good morning, Lab!” Nutmeg squeaked. She patted her fluffy tail and wiggled her paws. “Today I will make a better nut-catcher. I want to gather nuts faster, then have time to play outside.”

Nutmeg spread out soft felt, paper tubes, yarn, and a big bouncy rubber band. She drew a picture. “First, I plan,” she said. “Plan, then do. Step by step.” She set the timer. “Ten minutes for planning. Tick-tick-tick!”

Nutmeg’s friend, a gentle ladybug named Dot, flew in and landed on the warm lamp. “Hi, Nutmeg! It smells nice down here,” Dot buzzed. “What are you making?”

“A nut-catcher that is better than before,” said Nutmeg. “I want improvement.”

“Woo! Let me watch,” said Dot.

Nutmeg started crafting. Snip went the scissors. Swish went the brush with glue. “I will make a scoop and a slide,” she said. “Scoop the nut, slide to the basket.” She tied yarn with careful little knots. Her paws felt sticky, and the yarn felt fuzzy.

Tick-tick-tick went the timer. It dinged! “Time to test,” Nutmeg said. She rolled a pretend nut—a soft brown pom-pom—down the slide. The nut stopped halfway and fell with a plop.

Nutmeg’s whiskers drooped. “Oh no. It did not work.”

Dot patted her tail. “It’s okay, friend. You can try again.”

Nutmeg took a deep breath. “I can improve.” She smiled. “I will fix the slide.” She tilted the paper tube. “Lower here, higher there. Step by step.” She set the timer again. “Five minutes for fixing.”

Rustle, tape, press. The tape felt smooth and cool. Nutmeg tried again. She rolled the pretend nut. The nut slid—swish!—but bounced out of the basket.

“Hmm,” said Nutmeg. “I need a soft stop.” She added a cushion of felt at the end. She pressed it down and counted, “One, two, three.” The glue smelled sweet, like oranges.

“Test again!” Dot cheered.

This time the nut slid—swish—and landed softly in the basket. Thump! “Yes!” Nutmeg clapped. “It works better!”

Nutmeg looked at her list. “Next, a handle for carrying.” She braided yarn—over, under, over. The braid felt bumpy. She tied it tight. “Now I can carry it outside after I finish my lab work.”

Then Nutmeg peeked at the little timer. “Oh! I used lots of minutes,” she said. “I want time to play in the park.”

Dot nodded. “You can use time blocks,” she said. “One block to build, one block to test, one block to clean.”

Nutmeg grinned. “Yes! Time blocks help me.” She set the timer again. “Ten minutes to clean.” She hummed a happy tune while she put tools away—snip, brush, tape, all in rows. She wiped a tiny glue dot off the table. The cloth felt warm and damp. The lab looked shiny and neat.

“Thank you for helping me watch the time, Dot,” Nutmeg said.

“You’re welcome,” said Dot. “Thank you for letting me be here. Your lab is cozy.”

Nutmeg’s heart felt big and warm. “I am grateful for you,” she said. “I am grateful for my lab. I am grateful I can improve.”

With her new nut-catcher in her paws, Nutmeg climbed up the stairs. Fresh air brushed her face. Sunlight tickled her whiskers. Outside, the grass was green and cool, and the sky was soft blue.

At the big oak tree, Nutmeg tried her tool. “Ready, set, scoop!” She scooped a nut. Swish! Thump! The nut slid and landed in the basket. “It works!” she laughed. She scooped again. Swish! Thump! Soon she had a small pile. Birds chirped. Leaves whispered. Nutmeg’s tail swished with joy.

A young rabbit hopped by. “Wow,” said the rabbit. “What is that?”

“It is my nut-catcher,” said Nutmeg. “I made it in my basement lab. It did not work at first. I felt sad. Then I tried again. I changed it a little. I used my plan and my timer. I improved it!”

“That is smart,” said the rabbit. “Can I try?”

“Yes,” said Nutmeg. “Hold the handle. Scoop gently.” The rabbit scooped—swish! Thump! “I did it!” the rabbit squealed.

“Great job,” said Nutmeg. “We can share. When we help, we learn.”

Dot flew out and landed on a sun-warm rock. “Time for a break,” she buzzed.

Nutmeg looked at the sky. “Yes. Work time, then play time,” she said. “I used my time well. Now I can play.” She bounced a little. “Let’s run and jump!”

They played tag around the tree, then sat in the grass. Nutmeg nibbled a nut. It tasted toasty and sweet. She smiled at her friends and the bright day.

Nutmeg said, “Today I learned a big lesson. When I try, fix, and try again, I improve. When I use my time well, I have time to play. And I am grateful for friends who cheer me on.”

Dot and the rabbit nodded. The leaves made a soft clapping sound, as if the tree agreed.

The End.
