# Bear Cub’s Big Share and Stretch

The friendly bear cub woke up with a wiggle. “Good morning!” he said to his toes. He stretched his arms wide, then touched his furry nose. The living room was bright and playful. Colorful cushions made a soft mountain. A rainbow rug felt warm under his paws. The air smelled like cinnamon toast.

Mama Bear called, “Friends are coming to play!”

“Yay!” the cub cheered. He clapped his paws. He loved friends. He loved games. He loved learning new things.

Soon, the door went knock-knock. The cub skipped to open it. There was Mei the panda, with a red scarf. There was Ravi the sun bear, with shiny bracelets that jingled. There was Amina the polar cub, with a blue hat. Each friend smiled big.

“Welcome!” said the bear cub. “Let’s play in my living room. It’s soft and safe.”

Mei sniffed. “Mmm, something smells sweet.”

“Cinnamon toast,” said Mama Bear. “We will share later.”

The cub bounced. “First, exercise time! Let’s get our wiggles out.” He pointed to a basket. “I have music shakers, scarves, and balls.”

Ravi shook a shaker. Chika-chika! Mei tied her red scarf like a cape. Amina rolled a ball gently. Thump, thump.

The cub said, “We can take turns. Fair turns. Everyone gets to try.”

“Fair is good,” said Amina, nodding.

They started with stretches. The cub reached up. “Tall trees!” he said.

“Tall trees!” the friends copied, giggling.

They bent down. “Low rocks!”

They twisted. “Windy trunks!” Their scarves swished. Their bracelets jingled. Their hats bobbed. Their paws pat-patted the rug. It felt fun and safe to move.

“Now let’s dance,” said the cub. He put on music. Tappy drums hopped with clappy hands. The friends moved in a circle.

Mei said, “At my grandma’s house, we dance in a circle and clap. We say, ‘Ni hao!’ That means hello.” She clapped a gentle rhythm. Clap, clap… pause… clap!

Ravi smiled. “In my family, we stomp our feet and shake our hands.” He stomped: stomp-stomp—pause—stomp! His bracelets sang ting-ting!

Amina spun. “We glide like snowflakes,” she said softly. She slid her paws. Swish, swish.

The bear cub watched. His eyes felt bright. His heart felt warm. “Wow! We all have special dances. Let’s try them all!”

They took turns being the leader. Mei led the clap dance. Everyone said, “Ni hao!” and felt the happy pattern. Ravi led the stomp dance. Everyone stomped and laughed at the ting-ting. Amina led the snow-glide. Everyone whispered, “Woosh,” and moved smooth and slow.

The cub said, “My family does a bounce dance.” He bounced, boing-boing-boing. The floor gently squeaked. They tried that, too. The room felt full of different music and different moves. It was like a big hug of dances.

Then the cub saw the baskets. “Uh-oh,” he said. “We have two shakers and four friends.”

Mei looked at the shakers. “I would like one,” she said softly.

Ravi said, “I want to try a shaker too.”

Amina touched the scarf. “I can use the scarf now,” she said.

The bear cub took a deep breath. He wanted a shaker, too. His paws felt tingly. He remembered his plan. “We can make fair turns,” he said. “Two friends use shakers. Two friends use scarves. Then we switch!”

“That is fair,” said Mama Bear from the doorway. “You are caring for each other. That is our living room rule.”

“Caring is a good rule,” said Amina.

The cub made pairs. “First, Ravi and Mei shake. Amina and I will dance with scarves. Then we switch.”

They played. Chika-chika, swish-swish, clap-clap, stomp-stomp. The rug tickled their paws. The music made their bodies happy. After a little while, the cub said, “Switch!” They traded gently. “Thank you,” said Ravi. “Your turn,” said Mei. It felt fair. It felt friendly.

After exercise time, they sat in a circle on the rainbow rug. Mama Bear brought a tray. Cinnamon toast, crunchy and warm. Slices of mango, sweet and sunny. Cool water that went glug-glug into cups.

Mei said, “In my home, we share fruit.”

Ravi said, “We pass food to the left.”

Amina said, “We say, ‘Please’ and ‘Thank you.’”

The bear cub nodded. “Let’s do all of that.” He passed the plate to the left. “Please,” he said. Mei smiled. “Thank you,” she said. They tasted the toast. Crunch! They tasted the mango. Mmm! Their tummies felt cozy.

When they finished, the cub looked around. Cushions were moved. Scarves were on the floor. A shaker had rolled under the chair.

The cub stood up tall. “Friends, this is our living room. We all played here. Let’s all help clean up.”

“That is civic responsibility,” Mama Bear said gently. “A big word for caring for our shared space.”

The cub repeated slowly, “Civ-ic re-spon-si-bil-i-ty.” He giggled. “It means we help.”

They picked up scarves. They stacked cushions. The cub reached under the chair. “I found the shaker!” He put it in the basket. Their paws made soft pat sounds. The room became neat and bright again.

Mei said, “We danced many dances today.”

Ravi said, “We learned fair turns.”

Amina said, “We helped our shared space.”

The bear cub smiled. His heart felt big. “We learned about each other, too. We are different. We are the same. We can share, move, and care together.”

Mama Bear gave them a proud nod. “That is cultural diversity,” she said. “Different ways, together play.”

The friends hugged. The room smelled like cinnamon and mango. The rug felt warm. Their smiles felt like sunshine.

The bear cub waved at the door. “Come back soon. Bring a new dance!”

“We will!” the friends said.

The bear cub wiggled his toes one more time and whispered, “I love our big, different, together world.”

The End.
