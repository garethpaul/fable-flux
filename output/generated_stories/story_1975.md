# Pippa Parrot Builds a Playground

Pippa the parrot perched on the back of a dining room chair. Sunlight slid across the shiny table. The table smelled like lemon polish. Her bright green feathers shimmered, and her orange beak clicked with ideas.

“Squawk! Idea time!” Pippa chirped. “Let’s make something fun.”

On the table sat paper, crayons, and a big bowl of apples. Pippa loved to draw in the dining room. It was quiet and cozy. She could hear the soft tick-tock of the wall clock. Tick. Tock.

Pippa drew a picture with a purple crayon. “A playground!” she sang. “Slides! Steps! A balance path!” She added swirls and stars. “We can build it outside. We can help our friends play.”

Her best friends, Ben and Mia, walked in. They were twins with big smiles. “Hi, Pippa,” Ben said. “What are you drawing?”

“A play-place!” Pippa said proudly. “We can build it in the yard. We can use boxes and sticks. We can move our bodies outside. We can help everyone play and share.”

Mia clapped. “Let’s do it!”

Pippa hopped down. Her claws tapped on the wood floor. “First we plan,” she said. “Plan in the dining room. Build in the fresh air.”

They spread the drawing on the table. Pippa pointed with her wing. “We need soft grass. We need strong sticks. We need a safe path.”

Mia sniffed the apples. “Snack first?” she asked.

Pippa laughed. “Yes! Builders need energy.” They crunched apples. Crunch. Crunch. The sweet smell made Pippa’s tummy feel warm and brave.

Outside, the morning air felt cool and bright. The grass tickled their toes. The sky looked like a big blue blanket. Birds sang, “Tweet tweet,” and a breeze whispered, “Whoosh.”

“Outdoor time helps our bodies grow,” Pippa said. “We move. We stretch. We feel the sun.”

Ben carried a cardboard box. “Heavy,” he grunted.

“Slow and steady,” Pippa coached. “We can share the job.” She flapped to one side and held the box with her wing while Mia held the other side. “Teamwork!” Pippa cheered.

They set the box on the grass. “This can be the clubhouse,” Mia said.

Pippa nodded. “Let’s build a balance path, too.” She spotted a line of flat stones near the flowers. “We can move those.”

Ben tried to lift a stone. “It’s stuck,” he said, eyes wide.

Pippa tilted her head. “Ask for help,” she reminded him. “We can do it together.”

They pushed gently, one, two, three. The stone slid and landed with a soft thud. Thud. Pippa’s feathers ruffled in the breeze. “Great job!” she said. “Helping hands make big jobs small.”

They made a path with the stones. “Step, step, step,” Mia said as she tested it. She held her arms out like wings. “I can balance!”

Pippa gathered sticks. She found long ones and short ones. She smelled the wood, earthy and fresh. “Let’s make a low bridge,” she said. “A safe one.”

They tied the sticks with string from the dining room drawer. Pippa tugged gently. “Strong,” she said. “Safe.”

Ben looked at the big box. “How will we make a window?” he asked.

Pippa blinked. “We ask a grown-up for the cutting part,” she said. “That keeps us safe.” They called to Dad. He came with kind eyes and careful hands. Snip, snip. A square window appeared.

“Thank you,” Pippa said. “We can do the rest.”

They painted a big blue sun on the box. They drew smiling stars. Mia sprinkled soft leaves inside for a cozy floor. It smelled like the forest. “Mmm,” Pippa hummed. “Fresh and green.”

Soon, neighbors came to see. A shy bunny named Dot peeked from under a bush. “Is it for everyone?” Dot asked.

Pippa bobbed her head. “Yes. We built it to help friends play outside. Come try!”

Dot hopped onto the balance stones. “I’m scared,” Dot whispered.

Pippa flew to her side. “I feel scared sometimes, too,” she said gently. “Let’s go slow. I will count with you.”

“One… two… three…” Pippa counted softly. Dot’s feet went tap, tap, tap. Dot reached the clubhouse and smiled. “I did it!”

“You did it!” Mia cheered. Ben clapped. Pippa fluffed her feathers with pride.

A small cloud passed over the sun. The light turned soft and silver. A chill brushed Pippa’s wings. “Let’s take a warm break,” she said. “Water and rest help builders.”

They sipped water on the porch. The cups felt cool in their hands. Pippa listened to the breeze and felt her heart slow. “Our bodies need care,” she said. “Then we can play more.”

When the sun peeked out again, more friends tried the bridge and the path. They took turns. They waited and watched. They helped each other when someone wobbled. Laughter bubbled like a happy stream.

At the end of the day, Pippa led everyone back to the dining room to tidy up. They washed paint brushes in a bowl. Swish, swish. They put away the crayons. Click, click.

Dad smiled. “You built a playground. You helped others. You played outside. How do you feel?”

Pippa puffed with joy. “I feel strong in my body and kind in my heart,” she said. “Outdoor play helps us grow. Building together makes us brave. Helping friends makes us happy.”

Mia hugged Pippa carefully. Ben gave a thumbs-up. Dot wiggled with glee.

Pippa perched back on the chair and looked at the door to the yard. She could still smell the grass and hear the birds. “Tomorrow,” she whispered, “we build again.”

The End.
