# Pebbles, Pencils, and Porch Plans

Pip was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a happy waddle. One sunny day, Pip sat on a peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like warm wood and lemon tea. The wind made the chimes sing ting-ting-ting. Pip tapped a pencil on a big paper pad. Tap, tap. Pip wanted to write a porch list, a plan for a big picture story.

“I will write a story about our day,” Pip said. “I will draw, and I will write. It will be fun!”

Soon Pip’s friends came to visit. There was Bree the bunny, with soft ears and a giggle. There was Tiko the turtle, slow and steady. And there was Dot the duck, who loved to quack new ideas.

“Let’s write a story together on the front porch,” Pip said, flapping with joy.

“Yes!” said Bree. “I want to draw a big blue sky.”

“I want to write about snacks,” said Dot. “Crunch, crunch.”

“I want to write slowly and carefully,” said Tiko. “One word at a time.”

Pip smiled, but then Pip frowned a tiny frown. They had one pencil. They had one big paper pad. They had many ideas. Who would write first? Who would draw? Everyone started talking at once.

“I want the pencil first!” said Bree.

“Quack! Snacks first!” said Dot.

“Slow and steady,” said Tiko. “We must think.”

The porch felt less peaceful. The chimes went clink-clonk. Pip’s tummy felt twisty. This was a problem.

Pip took a deep breath. In, out. The air smelled like fresh porch paint and sweet flowers. Pip put the pencil down and held up a flipper.

“Friends,” Pip said gently, “let’s use good words and make a plan. We can take turns. We can listen. We can use logical thinking. We can negotiate.”

“Ne-go-shi-ate?” Bree said, ears up.

“It means we talk and make a fair plan,” Pip said. “We think of what each friend needs. We agree on steps.”

Tiko nodded. “A plan is good.”

Dot tilted her head. “Does the plan have snacks?”

Pip laughed. “We can write snacks into the plan!”

Pip drew a big circle on the paper and wrote: PLAN. The pencil made a soft scritch-scratch sound. The porch felt calm again.

“First,” Pip said, “let’s share what is important to each of us.”

Bree said, “I want to draw a big blue sky.” She touched the crayons. They smelled a little waxy.

Dot said, “I want to write about crunchy snacks.”

Tiko said, “I want time to write slowly.”

Pip said, “I want us to work together and finish. I want to write about our friendship.”

Pip wrote down each need. Scritch-scratch. “Now let’s think. What is the problem?”

“We all want the pencil,” said Bree.

“And we have one paper pad,” said Tiko.

“Quack. And I am hungry,” said Dot.

Pip nodded. “Let’s make a fair plan. We can take turns with the pencil. We can use a timer.” Pip set a little sand timer on the porch railing. The sand was pink and glowed in the sun.

“Bree, you can draw the sky first with crayons,” Pip said. “You do not need the pencil yet. The crayons can swoosh-swoosh.” Bree grinned and began to color. Swoosh, swoosh. The paper smelled like paper and crayons and sunshine.

“Dot, while Bree draws, you and I can write a snack list,” Pip said. “We will plan what to eat after we write. Carrots for Bree. Crunchy crackers for Dot. Lettuce leaves for Tiko. Ice chips for me!”

Dot clapped. “Quack, yes!”

“Tiko,” Pip said, “after the sky is done, you will get the pencil next. You can write one careful sentence. We will listen and wait.”

Tiko smiled. “I like this plan.”

Pip wrote the plan at the top of the page: 1. Bree draws. 2. Tiko writes. 3. Pip writes. 4. Dot writes. 5. Snacks! Everyone cheered.

The sand timer whispered down. Bree’s sky spread across the page, big and blue. Whoosh, swoosh. Little clouds looked like cotton. Bree passed the pencil to Tiko, just as the last grain of sand fell.

“Thank you,” said Tiko. He wrote slowly: “We are friends on a porch.” The letters looked neat and careful. Pip and Bree and Dot waited quietly. They watched the pink sand fall again. The porch felt peaceful. A bird sang tweet-tweet.

When Tiko finished, he passed the pencil to Pip.

Pip wrote: “We talk. We plan. We help.” Then Pip passed the pencil to Dot.

Dot wrote: “We like crunchy snacks.” Dot made a little crumb drawing. Crumb, crumb. Everyone giggled.

Soon the paper was full. It had Bree’s blue sky at the top, and careful words in the middle, and a funny crumb picture at the bottom. Pip clapped soft flipper claps. “We did it!”

“Snack time!” Dot quacked.

They spread a small picnic on the porch. Crunch, crunch went the crackers. Munch, munch went the carrots. The lemonade smelled lemony and sweet. Tiko sipped slowly. Pip nibbled ice chips and felt cool and happy.

Bree leaned back and looked at their page. “We made this together,” she said. Her ears twitched in the breeze.

Pip smiled. “We used negotiation. We listened. We took turns. We made a plan. That is teamwork.”

Tiko added, “Writing can be a group job.”

Dot licked a crumb from her beak. “Quack. Talking nicely helps. And snacks help too.”

The wind chimes sang a soft song. The porch felt warm and kind and very peaceful again. Pip hugged each friend.

Pip said, “Today we learned: when we have a problem, we can talk. We can share our needs. We can make a fair plan. We can write it down. We can work as a team.”

Everyone nodded. “Yes!”

They looked at their page one more time. Pip wrote one last line at the bottom: “We can solve problems together.”

They all signed their names, one by one, with the pencil making happy little scritch-scratch sounds.

The End.
