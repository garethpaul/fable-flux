# Nutkin's Honest Choice

Nutkin the squirrel loved exploring new places. Today, he scampered through an open window into a cozy house. His tiny paws pattered across the floor as he discovered a bright, clean bathroom.

"Wow!" Nutkin squeaked, his bushy tail twitching with excitement. The white tiles sparkled, and everything smelled fresh like flowers. He had never seen such a wonderful room!

On the counter sat a pretty glass jar filled with colorful soap bubbles. They looked just like the rainbow nuts Nutkin loved to collect! His tummy rumbled loudly.

"Maybe just one little taste," Nutkin whispered to himself. He climbed up the smooth counter, his tiny claws clicking softly on the surface.

Just as Nutkin reached for the jar, he heard footsteps. A little girl named Emma walked into the bathroom with her mama.

"Oh my!" said Mama, pointing at Nutkin. "What a cute little visitor!"

Nutkin froze. His heart went thump-thump-thump. He could run away, but something inside told him to stay.

"Hello, little squirrel," Emma said gently. "What are you doing here?"

Nutkin's whiskers twitched nervously. He could pretend he was just looking around, but that wouldn't be honest. He took a deep breath that smelled like vanilla soap.

"I'm sorry," Nutkin said in his squeaky voice. "I was hungry, and your pretty bubbles looked like nuts. I almost took some without asking."

Emma's eyes grew wide with surprise. "You can talk!"

"You were going to take my bubble soap?" Emma asked, but she didn't sound angry. She sounded curious.

Nutkin nodded, his fluffy tail drooping. "Yes, but I know that was wrong. I should have asked first. I'm really, really sorry."

Mama smiled warmly. "Nutkin, it takes courage to tell the truth. We're proud of you for being honest."

"But soap bubbles aren't food for squirrels," Emma giggled. "They would make your tummy hurt!"

Nutkin's eyes grew round. "Oh no! Thank you for telling me!"

Emma ran to her room and came back with a small bag of real nuts. "These are much better for you," she said, shaking the bag. The nuts rattled like tiny drums.

"For me?" Nutkin asked, hardly believing it. "But I almost took your things!"

"Because you were honest and said sorry, we want to share with you," Emma explained. "Honest friends deserve kindness."

Nutkin's heart felt warm and fuzzy, like being wrapped in a soft blanket. He carefully took one nut and crunched it happily. It tasted nutty and sweet - much better than soap would have!

"From now on, I'll always ask before taking anything," Nutkin promised. "And I'll always tell the truth, even when it's scary."

"That's what good friends do," Mama said, gently patting Nutkin's tiny head.

Emma filled a small bottle cap with fresh water for Nutkin. The cool water felt wonderful on his tongue after his adventure.

"Can you visit us again?" Emma asked hopefully.

"Yes, but I'll knock on the door next time!" Nutkin laughed, his tail swishing happily.

As Nutkin scampered home with his new friends waving goodbye, he felt proud and happy. Being honest had felt scary at first, but it led to something wonderful - new friends and delicious nuts!

From that day on, Nutkin always remembered that telling the truth, even when it's hard, is always the right choice.

The End.
