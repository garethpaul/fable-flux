# Tully the Tidy Tortoise

In a cozy bedroom with a soft blue rug, a tiny nightlight glowed like a friendly star. The curtains whispered shhh as the evening breeze drifted in. On the bed, a wise tortoise named Tully tucked his shell into a striped blanket. He wore little round glasses and smiled kindly.

Tully’s bedroom was full of things he loved: a shelf of picture books, a basket of wooden blocks, and a small potted plant named Sprout. Sprout’s leaves were shiny and green.

“Good night, Sprout,” Tully said. He sniffed the air. “Hmm. Something smells a bit… stinky.”

Tully peeked around. There were crumpled papers under the bed, a sticky cup by the window, and a pile of toy cars on the rug. A sock was hiding on his lamp! The trash bin was full—so full that a banana peel hung over the edge like a tired tongue.

“Oh dear,” Tully whispered. “My cozy room isn’t cozy right now. It’s messy, and it doesn’t feel healthy for me… or for the Earth.”

Sprout’s leaves quivered in the breeze. A tiny droplet slid down the pot. “Drip.”

Tully listened to the gentle “whoosh” of the curtains and the quiet “tick-tock” of his clock. He felt a little worried. He loved the Earth. He loved clean air and fresh water. He wanted his bedroom to help the Earth, not hurt it.

“I have a problem,” Tully said softly. “My room is messy. The air smells yucky. I don’t know where to start.”

He took a deep breath. In and out. Slow and calm.

“I am a wise tortoise,” he told himself. “I can make a plan.”

Tully climbed out of bed. His shell made a soft rub-rub sound on the blanket. He put on his glasses and smiled.

“Step one,” he said. “Open the window a little for fresh air.” He nudged it open. Cool air kissed his cheeks. “Ahh.”

“Step two: sort, tidy, and care for the Earth.”

He looked around and talked to his things. “Toy cars, you go in your garage.” He rolled the cars into their basket. Click, click, vroom.

“Books, you rest on your shelf.” He patted the covers. Thump, thump.

“Dirty sock, you do not live on a lamp.” He giggled and dropped the sock into the laundry basket. Plop.

Tully found the sticky cup. “You need a wash,” he said. He carried it carefully. The cup smelled like old juice. “Pee-yew!” Tully wrinkled his nose and felt proud for noticing. “Good hygiene is good for me and good for the Earth.”

He came back to the trash bin and the papers under the bed. He made two piles on the rug. “Trash and recycle,” he said. “Paper goes to recycle. Banana peels go to compost.”

Tully had a small green compost pail near the door. He lifted the lid. “Hello, compost friends.” He placed the banana peel inside. Plop. “You will turn into rich soil. Sprout will like that.” He smiled at Sprout. Sprout’s leaf wiggled. “Rustle.”

He sorted the crumpled papers. “Paper for recycle.” He sorted a plastic bottle. “Bottle for recycle.” He found a broken crayon. “Too small to color. Trash.” He moved carefully, shell swaying, feet going tap-tap on the floor.

Soon the rug looked smooth and blue again. The nightlight seemed to glow brighter. The air smelled cleaner, like fresh rain.

But Tully noticed something else. His hands felt sticky from the cup. He touched his mouth and thought, “Hygiene time.”

He went to the sink. Swish, swish, water went over his hands. “Scrub, scrub,” Tully sang. “Top and bottom, between and around.” He dried with a soft towel. “Now I will brush my teeth.” He squeezed a pea of toothpaste. “Minty!” Brush, brush, little circles. He looked in the mirror and smiled at his shiny clean smile.

Back in the bedroom, Tully picked up a cloth and gently wiped his windowsill. “Dust can make me sneeze,” he said. “Wipe, wipe.” He dusted the lamp and the shelf. He watered Sprout with a tiny cup. “Sip, sip,” said the soil. “Thank you, Sprout, for cleaning my air. I will care for you, too.”

Tully stood in the middle of his room and listened. The curtains whispered shhh. The clock went tick-tock. His heartbeat felt slow and happy. The room looked tidy and safe.

“Problem solved,” Tully said with a nod. “I cleaned up, I sorted trash and recycle, I used the compost, and I washed up. My chores help me and help the Earth.”

He climbed back into bed. The blanket felt warm. The pillow smelled like soap and sunshine. The nightlight glowed like a sleepy moon.

Sprout rustled a tiny thank-you. “Rustle, rustle.”

Tully whispered, “When I care for my bedroom, I care for the Earth. I can do small things every day.”

He yawned a slow tortoise yawn. “Tomorrow, I will turn off lights when I leave the room. I will use just a little water. I will keep sorting and wiping. Little steps make a big difference.”

Tully smiled. His shell felt proud and peaceful. “I am a wise tortoise,” he said softly. “I help the Earth, I stay clean, and I do my chores.”

The night air was cool. The room was cozy. Tully closed his eyes and felt the whole Earth smile back.

The End.
