# The Brave Brush Bunny

In a sunny school, there was a bright creative art room. It smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Paints sparkled in little cups. Music hummed softly from a tiny speaker. In the middle of the room stood a small, determined rabbit named Rumi.

Rumi wore a blue smock with tiny carrots on it. His whiskers twitched. His ears stood tall. Today was Dance-Paint Day! The class would paint a big poster and then dance to show their art. Rumi’s paws tingled with excitement and worry.

“I want to help,” Rumi whispered, “but I feel a little scared.”

Ms. Lark, the kind teacher, clapped her hands. “Friends, we’re making a poster about courage! Then we’ll do a courage dance. Who will start?”

Rumi’s heart thump-thump-thumped. He looked at the big blank paper. It was so white and wide.

His friend, Mia Mouse, squeaked, “You can do it, Rumi!”

Rumi took a deep breath. He sniffed the sweet, squishy smell of orange paint. He dipped a brush. His paw shook. A tiny drip fell. Plop.

“Oh no,” Rumi said. “I made a drip.”

Ms. Lark smiled. “A drip can become art. What could you do?”

Rumi thought. He remembered the three steps Ms. Lark had taught them:
- Stop and breathe.
- Pick a plan.
- Try with courage.

Rumi stopped. He breathed in… and out. He felt brave bubbles in his tummy. Then he made a decision.

“I will turn the drip into a sun,” he said.

He used a little strategy. He added yellow rays around the orange drip. Swish, swish. The white paper warmed with a happy sun.

“Wow!” said Mia. “The sun is smiling!”

Rumi smiled too. “I did it.”

Soon, the class gathered more paints and tools. There were soft brushes, squishy sponges, and a roller that went rrr-rrr-rrr. Ms. Lark said, “Let’s share the tools and space.”

Rumi reached for the blue brush. At the same time, Leo Lion cub reached too. Their paws touched.

Rumi looked at Leo. He made another decision. “Let’s share,” Rumi said. “You paint a blue river. Then I’ll paint a blue sky.”

Leo grinned. “Deal!”

They took turns. The brush felt smooth in Rumi’s paw. He painted a soft, swirly sky. He heard the roller rrr-rrr-rrr. He smelled the fresh paint. The poster grew with color—green grass, red apples, a purple kite, and Rumi’s bright sun.

Then Ms. Lark said, “Now, the courage dance! We will dance in front of the poster. Who wants to go first?”

Rumi’s paws felt tingly again. His ears drooped a little. “What if I trip?” he asked.

Mia held his paw. “It’s okay to feel scared. Courage is trying, even when you feel scared.”

Rumi nodded. He remembered the steps:
- Stop and breathe.
- Pick a plan.
- Try with courage.

He stopped. He breathed in… and out. He made a plan. “I will do small steps first,” he said. “Then I will hop and spin.”

Ms. Lark turned up the music. Tap-tap-tap went the beat. Rumi began with tiny toe taps. Tap, tap. He swayed. Sway, sway. He wiggled his whiskers. Wiggle, wiggle. The class giggled with joy.

Rumi hopped once. Hop! He hopped twice. Hop, hop! He spun in a little bunny twirl. Whee!

For one moment, his foot slid on a paint drop. Slip! Rumi wobbled. His heart thumped again. He wanted to hide. But he remembered his plan.

“Try with courage,” he whispered to himself.

He bent his knees. He held his balance. He made the slip into a fancy slide. Sliiiide… then a gentle hop. The class clapped and cheered.

“You turned a slip into a dance move!” Leo roared softly.

Rumi’s ears stood tall again. “I did it! I was brave.”

Soon, it was time for everyone to dance. Mia added a squeaky spin. Leo did a soft roar pose. They all took turns and shared the space. When friends bumped, they said, “Oops, excuse me,” and tried again. They made a big circle and danced together. The room was full of swishes, taps, and happy laughs.

At the end, the poster shone on the wall. The smiling sun glowed over the blue sky and river. Ms. Lark read the big letters Rumi had painted at the top: “Courage Means Try!”

Ms. Lark said, “Class, what did we learn today?”

Rumi lifted his paw. “We learned that courage is trying, even when you feel scared. We can stop and breathe, pick a plan, and try. We can share and take turns.”

Mia added, “And if we slip, we can make a new move!”

Leo nodded. “Strategy helps. Plans help. Friends help.”

Ms. Lark smiled. “Yes. You all made brave choices.”

Rumi looked at the poster, then at his friends. He felt warm and proud. His tummy felt like sunshine. He whispered, “I can be brave again tomorrow.”

The End.
