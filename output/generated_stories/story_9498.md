# Ruby's Workshop Song

Ruby was a little red robin who loved to sing. Every morning, she would perch on the windowsill of Papa Joe's garage workshop and fill the air with her beautiful melodies. "Tweet-tweet-trill!" she would sing, watching Papa Joe work on his wooden toys.

Papa Joe always smiled when he heard Ruby's songs. "Good morning, little friend!" he would call out. "Your singing makes my work so much happier!"

One sunny Tuesday morning, Ruby flew into the workshop as usual. But something was different. Papa Joe wasn't there! Instead, she saw his tools scattered all over the workbench. Hammers, screwdrivers, and paintbrushes were everywhere. Wood shavings covered the floor like tiny golden snowflakes.

"Oh my!" chirped Ruby. "What a mess! Papa Joe must have forgotten to clean up yesterday."

Ruby knew Papa Joe would be back soon. She had seen him walk to the store with his shopping bag. As she looked around the messy workshop, she remembered something Papa Joe always said: "A clean workspace is a safe workspace!"

Ruby felt worried. What if Papa Joe tripped on the wood shavings? What if he couldn't find his favorite hammer? She wanted to help, but she was just a small bird.

"I may be little," Ruby said to herself, "but I can still do something!"

First, Ruby used her tiny beak to pick up the wood shavings, one by one. It was hard work! Her beak got tired, and her feet felt sore from hopping around. But she kept going.

"Tweet-tweet!" she sang softly to keep herself cheerful. "Cleaning up, cleaning up, making things bright!"

Next, she pushed the small screws that had rolled off the workbench back into their proper container. Push, push, push with her little orange feet! The metal felt cold and smooth under her toes.

When she came to Papa Joe's paintbrushes, Ruby stopped. They were covered in dried blue paint and looked very messy. Ruby knew these brushes were special to Papa Joe. She had watched him paint many beautiful toy trains with them.

Ruby flew to the water bucket and dipped her beak in the cool water. Then she carefully cleaned each brush, one by one. The water turned blue and cloudy, but the brushes became clean and soft again.

Just as Ruby finished organizing the last tool, she heard Papa Joe's footsteps. "Hello, Ruby!" he called as he entered the workshop.

Papa Joe stopped and looked around with wide, surprised eyes. "My goodness! Everything is so clean and organized! Did you do this, little Ruby?"

Ruby puffed out her red chest proudly. "Tweet-tweet-yes!" she sang.

Papa Joe's eyes grew misty with happiness. "Ruby, you are such a responsible little bird! You saw something that needed to be done, and you did it, even though it was hard work. Thank you for taking such good care of my workshop."

Ruby felt warm and happy inside. She had learned that being responsible meant doing the right thing, even when no one was watching. It also meant taking care of herself by resting when she got tired.

"You know what, Ruby?" Papa Joe said with a big smile. "From now on, you're my official workshop helper!"

Ruby sang her most beautiful song yet, filling the clean, bright workshop with joy.

The End.
