# The Little Mouse Dance Fix

On a sunny morning, a kind mouse named Mimi lived in a cozy treehouse. The treehouse smelled like warm pine and sweet apples. Wind whispered through the leaves, making a soft shhh sound. Mimi’s tiny feet pat-patted on the wooden floor as she hummed a happy tune.

“Today is the Friendship Dance!” Mimi squeaked with joy. “Friends from everywhere will come. We will dance together!”

Mimi looked around. Bright ribbons were ready. Soft leaf mats were on the floor. A basket of apples and berries sat on the table. A sign said, “Welcome, Everyone!” in colorful letters.

Mimi heard a knock. Tap-tap! She opened the door and saw friends from all around.

Babu the bunny hopped in with long ears. “Hello, Mimi!” he said. “I brought carrots to share.”

Zuzu the zebra clip-clopped in with bold stripes. “Hi, Mimi! I brought drums,” she said, tapping the drum. Boom-boom!

Pico the parrot fluttered in with shiny green feathers. “Squawk! I brought songs!” he chirped.

Tara the turtle slipped in slowly. “Good morning,” she smiled. “I brought a bell.” Ding-ding!

Soon, the treehouse was full of friends. The floor creaked with happy feet. The air filled with music, the sweet smell of apples, and tiny giggles.

Mimi clapped. “Let’s dance!”

But when Zuzu hit the drum—BOOM!—one of the leaf mats slid. Babu hopped and slipped—whoa! The ribbon banner drooped and fell. Plop! Pico’s feather got stuck in sticky berry juice. “Squawk! Oh no!” he cried. Tara’s bell rolled under a chair. Ding—clink!

Everyone froze.

“The floor is too slippery,” Babu said, rubbing his paw.

“The banner won’t stay up,” Zuzu sighed.

“My feather is sticky,” Pico squawked.

“My bell is way under there,” Tara whispered.

Mimi took a deep breath. She put a paw on her chest. In, out. “It’s okay,” she said kindly. “We can fix this. We will use what we have.”

She looked around the treehouse. She sniffed the air. She touched the floor. Her whiskers twitched.

“First,” said Mimi, “we need non-slip mats.” She opened a drawer and found a basket of walnut shells and old socks. “Hmm,” she said softly. “I can make grippy feet for the mats!”

She cut socks into little strips. Snip-snip! She glued the strips under the leaf mats. Press, press. She sprinkled a tiny pinch of dry cornmeal under the edges. “This will help,” she said. “Feel the rough bump-bump?”

Babu wiggled his toes on the mat. “Oh! It feels safe now,” he said, smiling.

“Next,” Mimi said, pointing up. “The banner needs strong ties.” She looked at the fallen ribbons. She found some vine strings in a jar. She braided them—over, under, over, under—while humming. She tied the banner to sturdy branches. Knot, knot. “Tug it,” she said.

Zuzu tugged. The banner stayed. “It’s strong and pretty!” Zuzu cheered.

“Now, sticky feather,” Mimi said to Pico. “We can clean it.” She warmed a tiny bowl of water. Steam rose, soft and swirly. She added a drop of lemon. It smelled fresh. She dipped a cloth and dabbed the feather. Dab-dab. “Gentle,” she said. “We do it slow.”

Pico shook his clean feather. “Shiny again! Thank you!”

“Last, Tara’s bell,” Mimi said. She lay on her tummy and peered under the chair. It was dark and dusty. “I need a long reach.” She saw a paintbrush, a magnet, and a ribbon. “Aha!”

She tied the magnet to the ribbon. She taped the ribbon to the brush. Swish-swish. “Long arm,” she giggled. She slid the brush under the chair. Clink! The magnet kissed the bell. Mimi pulled it out. “Got it!”

Tara smiled wide. “You are so clever, Mimi.”

Mimi’s ears warmed with happy pride. “We are all clever,” she said. “We helped together.”

They tried the dance again. Zuzu tapped the drum—boom-boom! The mats stayed put. Babu hopped—hop, hop, twirl! Pico sang, “La-la-la!” Tara rang the bell—ding-ding! Mimi danced with tiny tiptoe steps. Pitter-pat, swish, spin!

Mimi looked around. Friends of stripes, spots, shells, feathers, and soft fur moved together. Each one danced in their own way. Some hopped high. Some swayed slow. Some clapped. Some tapped. All smiles.

Babu said, “I like to hop-hop.”

Zuzu said, “I like big stomps.”

Pico said, “I like wing wiggles!”

Tara said, “I like slow sways.”

Mimi said, “I like little twirls.”

Mimi clapped. “Let’s make a Friendship Dance,” she said. “We will use everyone’s moves.”

They made a pattern. Hop-hop, stomp-stomp, wiggle-wiggle, sway-sway, twirl! They practiced. They laughed. The treehouse filled with music, warm light, apple smells, and happy hearts.

When the sun turned orange, the dance was perfect. Mimi looked at her friends. “Today we had a problem,” she said gently. “We fixed it by looking, thinking, and using what we had. We used socks, vines, water, and a magnet. That is called being resourceful.”

“And we danced together,” Zuzu added, smiling.

“And we were kind to our different moves,” Tara said softly.

Mimi nodded. “Different is wonderful. Together, we make a great dance.”

Everyone cheered. “Hooray for the Friendship Dance!”

They ate apples and carrots. Crunch, munch! They sang and swayed as the leaves whispered good-night.

The End.
