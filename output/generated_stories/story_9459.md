# Penny's Colorful Voice

Penny the parrot sat on her perch in Papa's home office. Her bright green feathers looked dull today. Her tummy made rumbly sounds. She felt very tired.

"Papa types click-click-click all day," Penny said softly. "But he forgets about me."

The office smelled like coffee and paper. Sunlight came through the big window. It made warm spots on Penny's colorful tail feathers. Usually she loved watching Papa work. But today felt different.

Papa sat at his desk. His fingers moved fast on the keyboard. Click-click-click! He had been working since morning. Penny's food bowl was empty. Her water dish was almost dry.

"I'm hungry," Penny whispered. But Papa didn't hear her small voice.

Penny looked around the office. She saw Papa's art supplies on the shelf. Bright markers, colorful paper, and shiny stickers. Penny had an idea!

She flew to the shelf. Her wings felt wobbly because she was so hungry. Penny picked up a red marker with her beak. She flew to Papa's desk.

"What are you doing, Penny?" Papa asked, still looking at his computer.

Penny drew a big circle on white paper. Then she drew smaller circles inside. It looked like her empty food bowl! She picked up a blue marker and drew wavy lines. Just like water!

"Squawk! Look, Papa! Look!" Penny called loudly.

Papa turned around. His eyes grew wide. "Oh my! Penny, did you draw this?"

"Yes! My tummy is empty like this bowl," Penny said, pointing with her wing. "And my water is almost gone like these little lines!"

Papa's face looked sad. "Oh, Penny! I'm so sorry. I was working so hard, I forgot to take care of you. That wasn't fair."

Papa stood up quickly. He walked to the kitchen. Penny heard the sound of seeds pouring into her bowl. Splash! Fresh water filled her dish.

"Here you go, sweet girl," Papa said, placing the full bowls near her perch. "Thank you for telling me what you needed."

Penny ate the crunchy seeds. They tasted nutty and delicious! The cool water felt wonderful on her throat. Her tummy felt happy again.

"Papa, I was scared to ask," Penny said between bites. "I thought you were too busy."

Papa gently stroked Penny's soft head feathers. "Penny, you should always tell me when you need something. Your needs are very important. I want to take care of you."

"Really?" Penny asked.

"Really! And your drawing was so clever. You used your voice AND your art to help me understand. That was very smart!"

Penny felt proud. Her feathers looked bright and shiny again. "Can we make a schedule together? So you remember my meal times?"

"What a wonderful idea!" Papa said. "We can use your colorful markers to make a beautiful chart."

They worked together. Papa wrote the times. Penny drew pictures of food bowls and water drops. She used every color - red, blue, yellow, green, and purple!

"There!" Papa said, hanging the chart on the wall. "Now we'll both remember."

Penny felt warm and happy inside. "Thank you for listening to me, Papa. And thank you for the yummy food!"

"Thank you for being brave and telling me what you needed," Papa said. "You're my special helper."

From that day on, Penny never felt afraid to speak up. She learned that using her voice was important. And Papa learned to listen better too.

The End.
