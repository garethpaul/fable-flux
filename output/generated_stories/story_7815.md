# Bubbles Makes a Plan

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to splash, spin, and squeak with joy. One rainy day, Bubbles visited a bright, warm laundry room. The room hummed like a soft song. The washing machine whirred. The dryer tumbled with a low whoosh. The air smelled like soap and sunshine.

Bubbles slid across a clean tile floor, his smooth skin cool and shiny. “Squeee!” he chirped. “What a fun place!” Colorful socks hung on a line like tiny flags. Shirts were folded in neat stacks. A basket of clothespins sat open, and a small chart with pictures hung on the wall.

Bubbles peeked at the chart. It showed steps with little drawings:
1. Sort clothes.
2. Wash clothes.
3. Dry clothes.
4. Fold clothes.
5. Put clothes away.

Just then, a little mouse named Mina rushed in. Her whiskers twitched, and her eyes looked worried. She carried a baby mouse sock with stars on it.

“Bubbles! Help!” Mina squeaked. “My baby brother’s star-sock is missing. He needs it. His feet are cold.”

Bubbles blinked. He loved to play, but he wanted to help. “We will find the sock,” he said. “But first, we need a plan.”

Mina sniffled. “A plan?”

“Yes,” said Bubbles. “Planning helps us think, step by step. Like this chart.” He pointed with his fin. “We can make our own plan for finding the sock.”

They made a simple plan:
- Step 1: Look safely and slowly.
- Step 2: Listen and sniff.
- Step 3: Check each place.
- Step 4: Keep things fair.

Mina nodded. “Okay. I can do that.”

They started with Step 1. Bubbles and Mina moved carefully. They did not run. They did not push. They checked under the laundry basket. “Nothing,” Mina said.

Step 2: They listened and sniffed. The dryer hummed. The soap smelled sweet. Bubbles cupped his fin to his ear. “Shhh,” he said. “I hear a tiny flap.”

“Flap, flap,” went a sound from the clothesline.

They looked up. A striped sock waved in a gentle breeze. “Squeee! Is that it?” Bubbles asked.

Mina shook her head. “No stars.”

Step 3: Check each place. They opened a drawer. They peeked behind the big bottle of soap. They looked in the warm, empty dryer. “It smells like warm mittens,” Mina said, smiling a little.

Suddenly, a swoosh! A gray cat named Coco padded in, tail high. In her mouth, she held a small, starry sock.

“My brother’s sock!” Mina squeaked. “Coco, that is not yours.”

Coco’s eyes went wide. She dropped the sock on the floor. “I just wanted a soft toy,” she purred. “I did not mean harm.”

Bubbles took a deep breath. “Step 4: Keep things fair,” he said. “Justice means giving back what belongs to someone. Mina’s brother needs his sock.”

Coco looked sorry. “You’re right,” she said. “I will give it back.”

Mina picked up the sock and hugged it. “Thank you, Coco.”

Bubbles smiled. “We solved the problem because we made a plan.”

Mina’s whiskers twitched. “Yes! But… can we help my brother right now? His feet are still cold.”

“Let’s use resourcefulness,” said Bubbles. “We can finish the laundry steps for Mina’s family.” He pointed to the picture chart. “We will move, sort, wash, dry, fold, and put away.”

They sorted a tiny pile: socks with socks, shirts with shirts. They giggled as they matched colors. Bubbles used his fin to push the light basket. Mina hopped inside and rode like a little boat. “Wheee!” she laughed. Bubbles made a splashy squeak and then said, “Careful and slow,” and they both nodded.

They washed the star socks with gentle soap. Swish, swish. The washer hummed. They waited, and Bubbles told a short, silly rhyme:
“Soap makes bubbles, bubbles shine,
Clean and warm, and feeling fine!”

The dryer made a soft whoosh. Soon, the socks were warm and cozy. Mina held the star sock. “It’s ready,” she whispered.

They folded the tiny socks together. Bubbles pressed them flat with his fin. “Smooth and neat,” he said. They put them in a small basket with a soft cloth on top.

Coco came closer. “I am sorry,” she said again. “Can I help?” She picked up clothespins and clicked them together like little claps. Click, click! “I can carry the basket.”

They all moved with care. Mina led the way. Bubbles balanced the folded shirts. Coco carried the small basket, tail swishing.

At the door, Mina paused. “Thank you for making a plan,” she said. “We found the sock. We were fair. We worked together.”

Bubbles grinned. “Planning helps us know what to do,” he said. “Resourcefulness helps us use what we have. Justice helps us be fair to others.”

Mina hugged the sock again. “My brother’s feet will be warm.”

Bubbles twirled once, softly. The laundry room still hummed, smelled sweet, and felt bright. Everyone felt calm and proud.

The lesson was clear: When there is a problem, make a simple plan, follow the steps, use what you have, and be fair. Then you can solve it together.

The End.
