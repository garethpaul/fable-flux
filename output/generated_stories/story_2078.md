# Ollie's Big Guest Room Adventure

Ollie the otter wiggled his whiskers as he peeked into Grandma's guest room. The fluffy white bed looked so bouncy! The soft carpet felt tickly under his little paws.

"Wow!" Ollie squeaked. "This room is perfect for playing!"

But then Ollie heard a tiny sound. *Sniff, sniff.* Someone was crying! He tilted his furry head and listened carefully with his round ears.

"Hello?" Ollie called softly. "Is someone sad?"

From under the big wooden dresser came a small voice. "I'm stuck! I can't get out!"

Ollie's brown eyes grew wide. He got down on his belly and looked under the dresser. There was a little gray mouse with tears in her eyes.

"My name is Ollie," he said gently. "What's your name?"

"I'm Pip," squeaked the mouse. "I was looking for crumbs, but I crawled too far under here. Now I'm stuck!"

Ollie sat up and scratched his head with his paw. He had to make a decision. Should he run and get help? Should he try to move the heavy dresser? Or was there another way?

"Hmm," Ollie thought out loud. "Let me look around the room. There must be something to help you!"

Ollie's dark nose twitched as he sniffed around. He saw a long wooden ruler on the desk. Too short. He spotted a jump rope in the corner. Too thick to fit under the dresser.

Then Ollie's eyes sparkled! On the nightstand was a long, thin flashlight.

"Pip, I have an idea!" Ollie said excitedly. "But I need to be very patient and careful."

Ollie picked up the flashlight with his teeth. It was heavier than he thought! He had to stop and rest twice before reaching the dresser.

"I'm going to slide this flashlight under to you," Ollie explained. "Can you grab onto it?"

"I think so," Pip said, wiping her tiny tears.

Slowly, slowly, Ollie pushed the flashlight under the dresser. His paws were getting tired, but he didn't give up.

"I can see the light!" Pip squeaked happily. "I'm grabbing on now!"

Very gently, Ollie pulled the flashlight back out. Inch by inch, Pip slid across the smooth floor, holding tight to the flashlight.

"You did it!" Ollie cheered as Pip popped out from under the dresser. "You're free!"

Pip hugged Ollie's furry leg. "Thank you so much! You were so kind and smart!"

"I'm just happy you're safe," Ollie said, his whiskers twitching with joy. "When I saw you needed help, I knew I had to think of the best way to help you."

Pip smiled. "You made a really good decision. You could have just run away, but you stayed and figured out how to help me."

Ollie felt warm and proud inside. "My mama always says that when someone needs help, we should think carefully about the best way to help them."

"Would you like to be friends?" Pip asked.

"Yes!" Ollie clapped his paws together. "We can play in the guest room together!"

From that day on, Ollie and Pip were the best of friends. And whenever they faced a problem, they remembered to stop, think carefully, and make good decisions together.

The End.
