# Poppy Panda’s Gentle Paw

The nursery was soft and sunny. The rug felt fluffy like clouds. The shelves were tidy, and the plants by the window smelled fresh and green. In a cozy corner sat Poppy Panda, a patient panda with warm eyes and a calm smile. Poppy wore a blue apron with a tiny leaf on it.

Every morning, Poppy greeted the toys. “Good morning, blocks. Good morning, books. Good morning, paint,” Poppy said in a gentle voice. The children came in laughing and humming. Shoes squeaked. The fish tank bubbled. The clock went tick-tock, tick-tock.

Poppy loved to help. Poppy loved to listen. Poppy also loved to say, “Please ask,” and “Let’s take turns,” because personal space and gentle words keep everyone safe and happy.

One day, the class was extra busy. Giggles bounced around the room. Little hands reached for crayons. Tiny feet pattered on the rug. Poppy set out a green mat for painting, a yellow mat for building, and a blue mat for reading. “Each mat is a space,” Poppy said. “We say, ‘May I join?’ before we step in.”

Milo the monkey liked to build. He stacked the blocks high. “Block, block, block!” he sang. His tail wiggled. Luna the lamb wanted to paint. She dipped her brush and whispered, “Swish, swish.” The air smelled like water and clean paper. Tiko the tiger cub curled on the blue mat, humming at the picture books.

Then, Zoey the zebra trotted in fast. She was excited. Her hooves clicked on the floor. “I want the red block!” she said, and she grabbed it from Milo’s tower. The tower wobbled. Milo’s eyes went wide. “Hey!” he said. “That was part of my tower.”

Zoey blinked. “But I want it,” she said.

Poppy took a slow breath. In, out. Calm and patient. “Zoey,” Poppy said softly, “Milo is using that block. We ask first. We respect his space.”

Zoey frowned. “I forgot,” she whispered.

“It’s okay to forget,” Poppy said. “Let’s practice. You can say, ‘May I have a turn when you are done?’”

Zoey turned to Milo. “May I have a turn when you are done?”

Milo hugged the red block close. “Yes,” he said, “when my tower is tall.”

Poppy smiled. “Thank you, Milo. Thank you, Zoey. Asking is kind.”

Later, Poppy got out the soft, round brushes to sweep crumbs from snack time. Crumbs felt sandy under paws. The broom made a hush-hush sound. Poppy said, “We care for our space. We put things away where they belong. That helps everyone.”

Luna finished her painting. “I made a tree!” she said. The paint was cool and wet. Green and brown and a little yellow sun. “I will put my brush in the water,” Luna said. Swish, drip, tap. “And I will hang my picture to dry.” The room smelled like oranges as Poppy opened the window a little. Fresh air tickled noses.

Then Tiko yawned and lay back on the blue mat. He hugged a pillow. Zoey tiptoed over and poked his ear. “Wake up! Play with me!” she said. Tiko pulled the pillow closer. “No, thank you. I need quiet.”

Zoey reached for the pillow. “Mine!” she said.

Poppy padded over and sat nearby. “Zoey,” Poppy said, “Tiko said ‘No, thank you.’ That is a boundary. A boundary is a gentle line that says, ‘This is my body or my space.’ We respect it.”

Zoey looked at Tiko’s worried eyes. “But I want to play now,” she said, her voice small.

Poppy nodded. “I hear you. You want to play. And Tiko wants quiet. Both are important. Let’s find a way.”

Poppy pointed to the play schedule chart with simple pictures. “It’s reading time on the blue mat. It’s building time on the yellow mat. If you want to play, you can come with me to the block mat. You can also ask Tiko, ‘When will you be ready to play?’”

Zoey took a deep breath. In, out. “Tiko, when will you be ready to play?” she asked.

Tiko peeked out. “In five ticks,” he said, pointing to the clock.

Zoey nodded. “Okay. I will build while I wait.”

Poppy clapped softly. “That was respectful,” Poppy said. “You used kind words. You gave Tiko space.”

Milo and Zoey built side by side. “May I use the red block now?” Zoey asked when Milo’s tower was tall.

“Yes,” Milo said, smiling. “Thank you for waiting.”

Zoey said, “Thank you for sharing.”

After playtime, Poppy sang the tidy-up song. “Put it back, put it right, make our space clean and bright!” Everyone joined in. Blocks clicked into bins. Books went to shelves with a gentle thump. Crayons clinked in their cups. The room looked calm and cozy again.

Poppy held up a little sign with a heart and a circle on it. “The heart is for kindness,” Poppy said. “The circle is for boundaries. Boundaries keep our hearts safe.”

Zoey raised her hoof. “A boundary is when I say, ‘No, thank you,’ or ‘Please wait,’” she said.

“And when we hear a boundary, we listen,” Poppy added. “We can ask. We can wait. We can find another way. That is respect. That is caring for each other and our nursery.”

The children smiled. The plants by the window seemed to smile too, their leaves shining in the warm light.

Zoey walked to Tiko. “Are you ready to play now?” she asked.

Tiko stretched. “Yes,” he said. “Thank you for waiting.”

Zoey beamed. “I am proud of me,” she said.

Poppy’s warm eyes sparkled. “I am proud of you too,” Poppy said. “Today we learned to ask, to wait, and to care for our space. Personal boundaries help everyone feel safe, happy, and loved.”

The clock went tick-tock, tick-tock. The nursery was peaceful and bright. The children played with gentle paws, gentle hands, and gentle hearts.

The End.
