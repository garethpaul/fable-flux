# Leo’s Cozy Closet Plan

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and bright, curious eyes. He had a special place he loved—a closet hideaway lined with a cozy blanket, a tiny lamp that glowed like a firefly, and a box of crayons that smelled like new rainbows. The closet was quiet and warm. It was Leo’s thinking spot.

One sunny morning, Leo bounced into the closet hideaway. “Roar! I will draw a giant sun!” he cheered. He opened the crayon box and wiggled with excitement. But his paw knocked over a cup of buttons that Mama Lion kept on the shelf. The buttons tumbled. Clip-clap! Plip-plop! They rolled under shoes and into corners.

“Oh no,” whispered Leo. His ears drooped. “Mama will be mad.”

He pushed the closet door a little. Mama Lion was sorting laundry. “Mama,” Leo called, “I… I dropped the buttons.”

Mama’s eyes were kind, but her eyebrows made a small mountain. “Thank you for telling me, Leo. Buttons are tiny. They can be lost. We will fix it after lunch.”

Leo felt a twist in his tummy. He didn’t want to wait. He wanted to make it right now. But his tummy also rumbled. Lunch smelled like warm bread and sweet apples. His whiskers twitched from the smell.

At the table, Leo ate slowly. Crunch, munch. He looked at the clock on the wall. Tick-tock, tick-tock. “I want to fix the buttons,” he said softly.

Mama smiled. “We will. Let’s make a plan. Plans help us use our time. After lunch, we clean. Then play. Then drawing.”

Leo nodded. A plan felt good, like a soft blanket. “Clean, play, draw,” he said. He wiped his face and hopped down.

Back in the closet hideaway, the buttons winked from every dark corner. “I will make amends,” said Leo. He stood brave and tall. He brought a little cup and a tiny broom. He took a deep breath. The closet smelled like pine wood and crayons. The floor felt cool under his paws.

“Hello, buttons,” Leo said. “I am sorry I knocked you down. I will help you find your home.” He began to sweep. Swish, swish. Tap. He listened for tiny clicks. He moved shoes gently. He picked up each button and put it in the cup. Plink. Plink.

Mama Lion peeked in. “How’s it going, my brave cub?”

“I am cleaning,” Leo said. “I am making amends.” He showed the cup with a proud smile.

“That is kind and responsible,” Mama said. “Say what happened. Fix what you can. That is making amends.”

“Say it. Fix it,” Leo repeated. The words felt strong in his mouth.

Leo found a shiny blue button under a fuzzy slipper. “Found you!” He giggled. He found a tiny red button near the lamp. “Don’t be shy,” he whispered. He crawled on his belly to reach a green button that smelled a little like soap. He stretched his paw all the way. “Almost!” His whiskers tickled the floor, and he sneezed. “Achoo!” He laughed and tried again. Pluck! Into the cup it went.

Soon, the cup was full. Leo looked around. “All home now,” he said. He carried the cup to Mama. “I am sorry, Mama. I knocked them down. I fixed it.”

Mama knelt and hugged him. Her fur felt warm and safe. “Thank you, Leo. That is making amends. You said sorry, and you worked to fix it.”

Leo’s heart felt light, like a kite. “Now play?” he asked, peeking at the clock.

“Yes,” Mama said. “You used your time well. Clean, then play, then draw.”

Leo’s tail swished. He pulled out his toy blocks. Click, stack, click! He built a tiny button shop in the closet hideaway. “Welcome, buttons!” he said. “Please line up.” He practiced putting them in color rows. Red, blue, green, yellow. He sang, “Clean up, line up, we make things right. Clean up, line up, hearts feel light.”

After play, Leo looked at his crayon box. He wanted to draw the giant sun. He set a timer that went beep-beep softly. “When it beeps, drawing time,” he said. He was learning to follow a routine.

The lamp glowed warm and golden. Leo chose a yellow crayon. It felt smooth in his paw. He drew a big circle sun. He added little rays that looked like tiny lion whiskers. “Hello, Sun,” he said. “You shine bright like good choices.”

Mama came to see. “What a creative sun,” she said.

“It is my making-amends sun,” Leo said. “It shines when I fix things.”

Mama smiled wide. “What did you learn today?”

Leo thought. He smelled crayons, heard the gentle tick-tock, and felt his happy heart thump-thump. “I learned to say sorry,” he said. “I learned to fix what I did. I learned to make a plan with time. Clean, play, draw.”

“And how did that feel?” Mama asked softly.

“Good,” Leo said. “Brave. Cozy. Like my closet hideaway.”

Mama kissed his head. “That is a wonderful routine. We can practice it every day.”

Leo held up his drawing. “I will hang my sun in the closet,” he said. He taped it carefully. The paper rustled. The sun smiled back at him.

He curled up on the blanket and yawned. “Roar,” he whispered with a sleepy grin. “I can make amends. I can use my time. I can follow my routine.”

The lamp hummed. The closet felt safe and sweet. Leo closed his eyes, proud and peaceful. He dreamed of buttons, bright suns, and brave, kind choices.

The End.
