# Oliver Owl's Rainbow Garden Art

Oliver the wise old owl lived in a cozy nest on Mrs. Chen's balcony garden. Every morning, he would stretch his fluffy brown wings and look around at all the pretty flowers and green plants.

"Hoot, hoot! What a beautiful day!" Oliver said, ruffling his soft feathers in the warm sunshine.

But today, something looked different. There were empty yogurt cups, old newspapers, and cardboard boxes scattered around the balcony. Mrs. Chen had been cleaning out her kitchen.

"Oh my," Oliver thought, tilting his big round head. "This mess doesn't look very pretty next to all these lovely flowers."

Oliver was about to fly away when he had a wonderful idea. His golden eyes sparkled with excitement.

"I could make something beautiful with all these things!" he hooted softly. "But first, I need a good plan."

Oliver perched on his favorite sunflower and thought carefully. He loved making art, and his mama had always taught him that the best art projects started with good planning.

"Let me see," he said, counting on his wing feathers. "First, I'll gather everything I need. Then, I'll clean the containers. After that, I can start creating!"

Oliver flew down and began collecting the empty containers. The yogurt cups were smooth and round. The newspapers felt crinkly under his talons. The cardboard was sturdy and perfect for cutting.

"These aren't trash at all," Oliver realized. "They're art supplies waiting to be discovered!"

He carefully washed the yogurt cups with water from the garden sprinkler. The cool water felt refreshing on his feathers as tiny droplets splashed around him.

Next, Oliver used his sharp beak to carefully tear the newspaper into long, colorful strips. The paper made a satisfying "rip" sound that made him giggle.

"Now for the fun part!" Oliver hooted with joy.

He arranged the clean yogurt cups in a row along the balcony railing. Then he filled each cup with fresh soil from the garden. The earth smelled rich and sweet, like chocolate cake.

Oliver planted different colored flower seeds in each cup - red poppies, yellow marigolds, purple pansies, and orange nasturtiums. He decorated the outside of each cup with the newspaper strips, creating beautiful patterns and designs.

"This is going to be a rainbow garden!" Oliver said proudly, stepping back to admire his work.

Just then, Mrs. Chen came outside with her watering can.

"Oh my!" she exclaimed, looking surprised. "Who made this beautiful garden art?"

Oliver felt a little nervous. Should he tell the truth? His mama had always taught him that honesty was very important.

"Hoot, hoot! It was me, Mrs. Chen," Oliver said politely. "I hope you don't mind. I used the containers you were throwing away."

Mrs. Chen's face broke into a huge smile. "Oliver, this is absolutely wonderful! You turned my old containers into something beautiful. And you were so honest to tell me the truth."

She gently patted Oliver's soft head. "You know what? I think this balcony looks more beautiful than ever. Thank you for recycling and creating such lovely art!"

Oliver's chest puffed up with pride. "Planning made it work perfectly," he said. "And telling the truth felt really good too."

From that day on, Oliver and Mrs. Chen worked together on many art projects, always planning carefully and using recycled materials to create beautiful things for their garden.

The End.
