# Polly's Paintbrush Problem

In a bright, colorful art room filled with paints, brushes, and paper, lived a beautiful green parrot named Polly. Her feathers sparkled like emeralds in the sunshine that streamed through the big windows. Polly loved to paint with her beak and claws, creating wonderful pictures of rainbows, flowers, and trees.

One sunny morning, Polly heard soft crying coming from the corner of the art room. "Sniff, sniff," went the sound. She flapped her wings and flew over to see what was wrong.

There sat three little mice - Pip, Pop, and Pep - looking very sad. Their tiny whiskers drooped, and their eyes were filled with tears.

"What's wrong, little friends?" asked Polly kindly, tilting her colorful head.

"We want to make pretty pictures for our mommies," squeaked Pip. "But we're too small to reach the paintbrushes!"

"And the paint bottles are too heavy for us to carry," added Pop, pointing his tiny paw at the tall shelves.

"We tried and tried, but we can't do it alone," whispered Pep sadly.

Polly's bright eyes sparkled with an idea. "Don't worry! I can help you," she said cheerfully. "But first, let me think about the best way to solve this problem."

Polly walked around the art room, looking at everything carefully. She saw the tall shelves, the heavy paint bottles, and the long brushes. Then she had a wonderful idea!

"I know what to do!" Polly announced. "Pip, you're the smallest, so you can mix the colors in the little cups. Pop, you're good at climbing, so you can help me organize the supplies. And Pep, you have the steadiest paws, so you can hold the paper still."

The three mice looked up hopefully. "But how will we reach everything?" asked Pip.

"Watch this!" said Polly proudly. She flew up high and carefully picked up a small paintbrush in her beak. Then she gently placed it where the mice could reach it. Next, she used her strong claws to carefully pour just a little bit of red paint into a tiny cup.

"Now we can work together!" chirped Polly. "I'll get the supplies from up high, and you three can create your beautiful pictures down here."

The mice's faces lit up with joy. "Hooray!" they squeaked together.

For the next hour, the art room was filled with happy sounds. Polly flew back and forth, bringing down brushes, paints, and fresh paper. She showed the mice how to dip their brushes gently and make smooth strokes.

"Remember to practice making circles first," Polly encouraged as she watched them paint. "Practice makes everything easier!"

Pip painted a bright yellow sun. Pop created a blue sky with fluffy white clouds. Pep made a garden full of colorful flowers. Each mouse worked carefully, and their pictures became more and more beautiful.

"These are wonderful!" exclaimed Polly, admiring their artwork. "Your mommies will love them so much!"

The three mice danced with happiness. "Thank you, Polly!" they said together. "You're the best leader ever! You helped us solve our problem and taught us how to paint!"

Polly felt warm and proud inside. "When we work together and help each other, we can solve any problem," she said with a big smile.

From that day on, Polly became the art room leader, always ready to help others reach their dreams - no matter how high up they seemed!

The End.
