# Benny's Dancing Laundry Day

Benny the rabbit loved to hop and dance. His fluffy white tail wiggled when he moved, and his long ears bounced up and down. Today, Mama Rabbit was doing laundry in the big, warm laundry room.

"Benny, please sit still while I fold these clothes," Mama said gently. The washing machine was making a loud WHOOSH-WHOOSH sound. The dryer was tumbling with a soft THUMP-THUMP-THUMP.

But Benny couldn't sit still! His paws wanted to tap. His body wanted to wiggle. "Mama, the machines are making music!" he said excitedly. "Listen! WHOOSH-WHOOSH, THUMP-THUMP-THUMP!"

Benny started hopping around the laundry room. Hop, hop, hop! He bumped into a basket of clean towels. CRASH! The soft, fluffy towels scattered everywhere.

"Oh no!" Mama sighed. "Benny, you need to calm your body down."

Benny's ears drooped. He felt sad that he made a mess. But his paws still wanted to move! The machine music was so catchy. WHOOSH-WHOOSH, THUMP-THUMP-THUMP!

"I'm sorry, Mama," Benny said. "But I can't stop moving! The washing machine sounds like drums, and the dryer sounds like a marching band!"

Mama Rabbit looked at her little bunny. She could see his nose twitching with excitement. She could see his feet tapping to the machine rhythm. Then Mama had a wonderful idea.

"Benny," she said with a smile, "what if we make the laundry room our dance studio? But we need to dance in a special way."

Benny's eyes grew big and bright. "Really? We can dance?"

"Yes!" Mama said. "But first, we need to listen carefully to the machine music. Can you hear the beat?"

Benny tilted his head and listened. WHOOSH-WHOOSH, THUMP-THUMP-THUMP. "I hear it, Mama!"

"Good! Now, let's dance in our own space so we don't bump into anything. You dance on this side by the dryer, and I'll dance on this side by the washer."

Benny hopped to his special dancing spot. The floor felt smooth under his paws. He started moving his arms like the washing machine - round and round. Then he stomped his feet like the dryer - THUMP-THUMP-THUMP!

Mama danced too! She swayed back and forth while folding clothes. She made folding look like a beautiful dance.

"This is the best laundry day ever!" Benny giggled. He spun in circles, but stayed in his special space. He wiggled his ears to the beat, but didn't bump into anything.

When the washing machine stopped, Benny stopped too. When the dryer finished, they both took a bow.

"You did such a good job listening to the music and controlling your body," Mama said proudly. "You found a way to move and dance without making a mess."

Benny felt so happy. His body felt calm and relaxed now. "Mama, can we dance while we fold the towels?"

"Of course!" Mama laughed. "We can make everything into a dance if we listen carefully and move thoughtfully."

Together, they picked up the scattered towels, dancing and swaying to their own quiet humming. Benny learned that moving his body felt even better when he listened first and found the right space to dance.

From that day on, laundry day became dance day in Benny's house!

The End.
