# Fennel’s Turn in the Shed

On a soft spring morning, a curious fox kit named Fennel peeked into the garden shed. The shed smelled like fresh wood and warm earth. Sunlight made bright stripes across the floor. Dust sparkles danced in the air.

“Wow,” Fennel whispered, nose twitching. “So many things!”

On a low shelf sat neat jars of seeds: big pumpkin seeds, tiny carrot seeds, and round pea seeds. Beside them were small tools, a little watering can, and a shiny scoop. Fennel’s paws tingled with excitement.

“I want to plant!” Fennel said. “I want to scoop! I want to water!”

Just then, Fennel’s friends arrived. Pip the mouse, with soft whiskers, peeked from a hole in the wall. Bella the bluebird perched on a peg, ruffling her feathers. Tilly the turtle slowly shuffled to the doorway, smiling with kind eyes.

“Let’s make a garden plan,” Bella chirped. “We can plant a row of peas.”

“A plan helps,” Tilly agreed, her voice gentle. “First we plan, then we take turns.”

Fennel bounced. “I can do the scoop! I can do the water! Me first!”

Pip wiggled his whiskers and said, “We can all have a turn. We can share.”

Bella fluttered down and drew a simple map in a little patch of dust. “Step one: scoop soil into the tray. Step two: make tiny holes. Step three: drop in seeds. Step four: water.”

“That’s a good plan,” Tilly said. “We will practice taking turns. It makes the work fair.”

Fennel’s tail swished. Waiting felt hard. “But I really, really want to scoop!”

“We will take turns,” Tilly repeated kindly. “It will be your turn soon.”

Pip went first with the shiny scoop. Scritch, scritch! He filled the tray with soft soil. Fennel sniffed the soil. It smelled like rain and warm leaves. Fennel bounced again.

“Now me?” Fennel asked, eyes wide.

“Next is making holes,” Bella said. She used her beak to tap, tap, tap gentle holes in a row. “Tap, tap, tap,” she sang.

Fennel’s ears drooped. “I want a turn.”

Tilly noticed. “Fennel, would you like to be the Seed Helper? That is a very special job.”

Fennel’s ears popped up. “Yes! Seed Helper!”

Tilly opened the pea jar. The peas clicked softly. “Hold out your paw,” she said.

Fennel held out a small paw. Plop, plop, plop—three peas landed in it. They felt cool and round.

“Drop one seed in each hole,” Bella said. “Gently.”

Fennel took a deep breath. “I can do gentle.” Plunk. Plunk. Plunk. He smiled. “I did it!”

“Great job,” Pip squeaked. “My turn to smooth the soil.”

Pip’s tiny paws patted the soil. Pat, pat, pat. The tray looked neat and cozy. It felt soft under Fennel’s paw when he touched it.

“Now watering,” Tilly said.

Fennel’s tail swished fast. “Please! Is it my turn now?”

Tilly nodded. “Yes, Fennel. Your turn.”

Fennel carefully picked up the small watering can. It felt cool and heavy with water. He tipped it just a little. Drip… drip… drip… “Slow and steady,” he said. He watched the soil turn dark and damp. It made a quiet earthy smell rise up. Fennel giggled. “I like this job!”

“We did it!” Bella chirped. “Our plan worked.”

“Because we took turns,” Pip added.

Fennel felt warm and proud inside. “Taking turns helped me wait. I got a turn, and you did too.”

“Let’s label the tray,” Tilly said. She set a small stick with a paper tag. “Peas,” she wrote. “We will practice patience every day.”

“Practice, practice,” Bella sang, flapping her wings.

They all cleaned the tools together. Scrub, wipe, clink. The shed was tidy again. The friends sat on a cozy mat by the door. Sunlight touched their faces. A soft breeze moved the hanging gloves like tiny flags.

“What will we do tomorrow?” Pip asked.

“We can plan,” Fennel said thoughtfully. “Maybe carrots next. First we plan, then we take turns, then we practice again.”

“That is smart,” Tilly said. “Planning helps us know what to do. Practice makes us better.”

Fennel’s tummy made a happy flutter. “Thank you for waiting for me,” he said to his friends.

“Thank you for waiting for us,” Bella chirped.

“Thank you for sharing the watering can,” Pip squeaked.

Fennel looked at the tray of peas, snug in their soil bed. He felt grateful. “I’m glad we are a team,” he said. “I’m proud of me for waiting, and I’m proud of you for helping.”

Just then, a tiny green sprout was not there yet, but Fennel imagined it. He pictured a tall pea plant with sweet pods. He smiled big. “Taking turns helps little seeds and little foxes grow,” he said.

The friends nodded.

“Tomorrow we will check the tray,” Tilly said. “We will take turns peeking, and we will say thank you to each helper.”

Fennel stood, tail high. “I can wait my turn to peek,” he said. “I can practice waiting. I can plan with my friends.” He felt brave and calm.

They closed the shed door with a soft click. The garden hummed. Bees buzzed. Leaves rustled a gentle shhh.

As they walked home, Fennel squeezed the air with a happy sigh. “Today I learned to take turns,” he said. “It feels good inside.”

“It feels good for all of us,” Bella sang.

“Thank you, Fennel,” Pip added.

“Thank you, friends,” Fennel replied.

The End.
