# Joey's Big Helper Practice

Joey the kangaroo joey bounced excitedly in Grandma's guest room. The soft purple carpet felt springy under his tiny paws. Colorful quilts covered the guest bed, and sunshine streamed through lace curtains.

"Today I'm going to help everyone!" Joey announced, hopping up and down. "I'll be the best helper ever!"

But when Joey tried to help, things didn't go as planned.

First, he rushed to help Grandma make breakfast. "I can crack eggs!" he said eagerly. CRACK! SPLAT! Egg shells and gooey egg white splashed everywhere. Joey's ears drooped as he watched the mess drip down the counter.

"Oh no," Joey whispered, feeling his cheeks grow hot with anger at himself.

Next, he hurried to help Grandpa water the garden. "I can hold the hose!" Joey grabbed the heavy green hose with both paws. WHOOSH! Water sprayed in all directions - on the flowers, on the fence, and all over Grandpa's glasses!

"I'm terrible at helping!" Joey cried, his bottom lip trembling. He stomped his foot and felt tears prick his eyes.

Grandma found Joey sitting sadly on the guest room bed, hugging a fluffy pillow tight.

"What's wrong, little one?" she asked gently, sitting beside him. The bed creaked softly as she settled down.

"I wanted to help everyone, but I keep messing up," Joey sniffled. "I'm too little and clumsy."

Grandma's warm hand patted his soft fur. "Oh, Joey. Helping is a skill that needs practice, just like hopping or counting."

"Practice?" Joey looked up with curious brown eyes.

"Yes! Let me show you." Grandma took Joey's small paws in her wrinkled hands. "First, we'll practice cracking eggs slowly and carefully."

In the kitchen, Grandma demonstrated tapping the egg gently on the bowl's edge. "Tap, tap, crack," she said in a sing-song voice. "Now you try."

Joey took a deep breath and tapped softly. TAP, TAP, CRACK! The egg split perfectly into the bowl.

"I did it!" Joey bounced with joy.

"Now let's practice with the hose," Grandpa said, smiling. He showed Joey how to hold it with both paws and aim carefully. "Steady hands, gentle squeeze."

Joey practiced pointing the water stream. First at a bucket, then at the thirsty tomato plants. The cool water made a pleasant trickling sound as it soaked into the dark soil.

"Practice makes everything easier," Joey realized, feeling proud and calm.

The next day, Mrs. Robin visited with her three baby birds. "I need help feeding my little ones," she chirped worriedly.

"I can help!" Joey said. But this time, he remembered to practice first. He carefully practiced holding the tiny seeds, moving slowly and gently.

When he fed the baby birds, his patient movements kept them safe and happy. The little birds chirped sweetly as they ate from his steady paws.

"Thank you, Joey!" Mrs. Robin sang. "You're such a wonderful helper!"

That evening, Joey snuggled into the guest room bed, feeling warm and happy. The soft quilt smelled like lavender and love.

"Grandma," he said sleepily, "practicing made me a better helper and a better friend."

"Yes, dear one," Grandma whispered, tucking him in. "When we practice with patience, we can help our whole community."

Joey smiled as he drifted off to sleep, dreaming of all the ways he could help tomorrow.

The End.
