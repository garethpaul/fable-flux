# Sunny Bird’s Study Corner Song

Sunny was a small singing bird with bright yellow feathers. Sunny lived by a cozy window. Right under the window was a neat study corner. There was a little desk, soft crayons, smooth paper, and a tiny chair just right for Sunny’s feathered tail.

Every morning, Sunny practiced songs at the desk. The desk smelled like sharpened pencils and warm wood. Sunlight made a golden square on the paper. Sunny sang softly, “Tweet, tweet, traa-la-la,” and the notes felt like warm honey in the air.

One day, Sunny heard small footsteps. Tap, tap, tap. A friend peeked around the chair. It was Miko, a shy mouse with round ears and a tiny backpack.

“Hi, Sunny,” said Miko. “I brought my counting beads. I want to learn. Can I sit with you?”

Sunny’s feathers fluffed with joy. “Yes! Friends learn together,” Sunny chirped.

Miko climbed onto a cushion by the desk. The cushion was squishy and smelled like clean cotton. Miko spread the colorful beads. Click, click, click. Sunny spread paper and crayons. Scritch, scritch, scritch.

Sunny began a new song. “One, two, three, sing with me.”

Miko tried to sing, but the notes wobbled. “I can’t sing like you,” Miko whispered. His whiskers drooped.

Sunny tilted a bright eye. “It’s okay. We can learn. My grandma bird taught me a special song from our family. It is a tradition. Would you like to hear it?”

Miko’s eyes shone. “Yes, please.”

Sunny stood tall on the chair and sang softly, “Good morning, little world. Breathe in, breathe out. Be kind. Be brave. Let your heart sing out.” The song was simple and sweet. The notes fluttered like butterflies. The words felt like a hug.

Miko listened. He placed a paw on his chest. “That makes my heart feel warm.”

Sunny smiled. “My grandma bird said we share our songs to make friends and to help us grow. Do you have a song from your family?”

Miko nodded slowly. “My grandma mouse taps a rhythm when she cooks. Tap-tap, tap-tap-tap.” Miko tapped the desk gently. Tap-tap, tap-tap-tap. The sound was like little raindrops.

Sunny’s eyes sparkled. “Let’s share! We can mix our traditions. We can make a new study song.”

Together, they tried. Sunny sang, “Be kind. Be brave,” and Miko tapped, “Tap-tap, tap-tap-tap.” But the song got fast. Sunny’s voice squeaked. Miko dropped a bead. Clatter!

Miko covered his face. “I messed up.”

Sunny shook his head and sang softly, “It’s okay to try. It’s okay to learn. Friends help friends.”

Miko took a deep breath. “Can we slow down?”

Sunny nodded. “Yes. Improvement is step by step.”

They tried again. Sunny started with a long, gentle note. “Aaaaah.” Miko tapped slowly. Tap… tap… tap. Sunny added words, “Be kind.” Tap. “Be brave.” Tap. “We learn.” Tap. “We share.” Tap.

The study corner felt calm and bright. The sunlight warmed their backs. The paper smelled like fresh trees. Their hearts beat together, soft and steady.

Miko smiled with his whole face. “I can do it! Listen!” He tapped the pattern and counted beads at the same time. “One… two… three… four.” Click. Click. Click.

Sunny flapped with pride. “You are improving!”

Just then, a gentle breeze came through the window. It moved the paper. It tickled Sunny’s feathers. It carried their song out into the room.

“Again?” asked Miko.

“Again,” said Sunny.

They sang and tapped. They counted beads and drew shapes. Triangle. Circle. Square. They took turns leading. Sometimes Sunny sang a little louder. Sometimes Miko tapped a little stronger. Each time, they listened to each other. Each time, they smiled.

Soon, a new friend peeked in. It was Pippa, a tiny puppy with curious eyes. “That sounds happy,” Pippa wagged. “Can I learn too?”

Sunny chirped, “Yes! Friends are welcome.”

Miko patted the cushion. “We share our traditions. What do you bring?”

Pippa’s tail thumped. “My family hums before we start a new task. Mmm-mmm-mmm.”

Sunny nodded. “Let’s add it.”

They made a plan:
- First, Pippa hummed, “Mmm-mmm-mmm.” It felt like warm soup.
- Then, Miko tapped, “Tap… tap… tap.” It sounded like tiny rain.
- Then, Sunny sang, “Be kind. Be brave. We learn. We share.” It felt like sunshine.

Together, they counted beads, colored pictures, and practiced sounds. They cheered for small steps. “Good try!” “Nice tap!” “Lovely hum!” Every try was a little better.

When the sun moved and the golden square slid off the desk, Sunny folded the paper. Miko packed the beads. Pippa yawned a happy yawn.

Sunny sang a soft goodbye, “Good friends help each other grow.”

Miko added, “We share our family ways.”

Pippa hummed, “Mmm-mmm-mmm,” and wagged.

They all felt proud, cozy, and calm. Their study corner smelled like crayons and hope. Their hearts were full.

Lesson: Friendship means listening, sharing our traditions, and practicing together. When we try, we improve, and learning feels bright and kind.

The End.
