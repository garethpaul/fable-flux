# Milo the Brave Bathroom Helper

Milo was a small, courageous monkey with bright eyes and quick feet. Every morning, he did his bathroom routine. He liked the shiny mirror, the soft, fluffy towels, and the minty smell of toothpaste. The tiles were cool like smooth pebbles under his toes, and the water from the tap sang, drip, drip, drip.

One sunny morning, Milo swung into the bathroom and landed gently on the bath mat. “Good morning, sink! Good morning, soap!” he said with a smile.

But the bathroom was busy today. His sister, Mina the monkey, was brushing her teeth. His baby cousin, Pip, clapped at the bubbles in the tub. The family parrot, Pippa, perched on the towel rack, chirping, “Brush, brush, swish-swish!”

Milo felt a wiggle in his tummy. He wanted to wash his hands and brush his teeth too. He bounced on his toes. He wiggled his tail. He even did a little march to get his wiggles out. Step, step, step! But the sink was not free.

“I need the sink now,” Milo blurted. His voice came out louder than he meant. Mina paused and looked at him in the mirror. “I’m almost done,” she said softly.

Milo took a breath. He remembered the family rule: We use peaceful solutions. He put his hand on his chest and felt his heart going thump-thump. “I can be brave and kind,” he whispered.

He tried a peaceful plan. “Mina,” he said, using his calm voice, “when you are done, may I have a turn, please?”

Mina smiled. “Yes, Milo. One more song, then your turn.”

Pippa the parrot chirped, “One more song! One more song!” Pip splashed the water, splash-splash, and giggled.

Milo still felt wiggly. He wanted to move! So he made a plan to use his body in a helpful way. “I will set up,” he said. He hopped to the drawer, slid it open, and took out his tiny toothbrush. He lined up his cup and toothpaste. He put a towel close by for drying hands. He even made a little step with a stool. Tap, tap, tap—he tested it with his toes. Steady!

While he waited, Milo helped Pip. “Pip, can you give the duck a gentle float?” he asked. They moved the toy duck slowly across the water. “Quack, quack,” said Milo in a silly voice. Pip laughed, “Quaaa!”

Mina finished and rinsed her mouth. “All done,” she said. She stepped away from the sink. “Your turn!”

Milo climbed the stool. He turned on the water a tiny bit. The water made a soft hiss like a snake. He rubbed the soap. Bubbles tickled his hands. “Oooh, slippery!” he giggled. He scrubbed—tops and bottoms, around each finger. “Happy hands,” he sang. “Scrub-scrub, rub-rub!” The bubbles smelled like lemons.

Then he brushed his teeth. “Two minutes,” he said, looking at the tiny sand timer. The bristles felt fuzzy. The toothpaste was cool and minty. “Brush the front, brush the back,” he sang. Swish-swish. Pippa copied him, “Swish-swish!”

Suddenly, Pip reached for the toothbrush cup. “Mine!” Pip said, grabbing. Water wobbled. The cup tipped. Oh no!

Milo froze for a second. He felt a big feeling bubbling up. He could yell. He could grab. But he remembered the peaceful plan. He took one deep breath in and blew out slow. “Like blowing a bubble,” he told himself.

In a gentle voice, Milo said, “Pip, I am using the cup. I feel worried when it might spill. Can we share a different cup?” He looked around and spotted a spare cup on the shelf. It had blue stars. He pointed. “This one is for you, Pip. It has stars!”

Pip looked at the stars. “Stars!” Pip cheered, letting go of Milo’s cup. The wobble stopped. No spill!

Mina clapped. “Nice solution, Milo,” she said. Pippa chirped, “Peaceful plan! Peaceful plan!”

Milo felt proud and warm in his chest. He finished brushing and rinsed with a small sip. Swish, spit. He wiped his mouth with the towel. Soft and cozy.

Then he saw a splash on the floor. A little puddle. He did not step over it. He took initiative. “I will fix this,” he said. He moved with careful feet—step, step—and got a dry towel. He pressed it on the puddle. Squish. He squeezed the towel in the sink and hung it neatly.

Milo looked at Mina and Pip. “Thank you for waiting and sharing,” he said. “We all used the bathroom together.”

Mina nodded. “We took turns. We used kind words.”

Pip hugged the star cup. “Star!”

Pippa fluffed her feathers. “Respect, respect! Brush and rinse, then next!”

Milo smiled at his family. His body felt calm. His mind felt clear. The bathroom smelled like mint and lemons and warm towels. He loved the sound of peaceful voices more than any splash.

At breakfast, Mama Monkey asked, “How did it go in the bathroom today?”

Milo sat tall. “We used peaceful solutions,” he said. “We asked, we waited, we shared, and we cleaned up. We moved our bodies to help, not to push.”

Mama Monkey hugged him. “That is brave and kind.”

Milo nodded. “When we are calm and respectful, everyone gets a turn,” he said. “That is our routine.”

He took a bite of banana and grinned. “Peace feels good.”

The End.
