# Benny Beaver Speaks Up

Benny Beaver was a very busy little beaver who loved to build things with his wooden blocks. Every morning, he would wake up, stretch his fuzzy brown fur, and get ready for the day in his cozy bathroom.

But today was different. When Benny turned on the faucet to brush his teeth, only a tiny drip came out. "Drip... drip... drip," went the water, so slowly that Benny couldn't fill his cup.

"Oh no!" said Benny, looking at his reflection in the mirror. "How can I brush my teeth with just drips?"

Benny tried turning the handle harder. He stood on his tippy-toes and wiggled it left and right. But still, only tiny drops fell into the sink with soft "plink, plink" sounds.

For a moment, Benny felt frustrated. His little beaver paws felt warm, and his tummy felt tight. He wanted to give up and just skip brushing his teeth. But then he remembered what Mama Beaver always said: "When something isn't working right, speak up and ask for help!"

Benny took a deep breath that smelled like the fresh mint toothpaste on his counter. He marched out of the bathroom, his flat tail making a gentle "thump, thump" on the wooden floor.

"Mama! Papa!" called Benny in his clearest, loudest voice. "I need help! The water in my bathroom is only dripping!"

Mama Beaver looked up from making breakfast. The kitchen smelled like warm pancakes and sweet maple syrup. "Thank you for telling us, Benny! That's very important information."

Papa Beaver put down his newspaper with a crinkly sound. "You did exactly the right thing by speaking up, son. Let's go look together."

In the bathroom, Papa Beaver examined the faucet while Benny watched carefully. "I see the problem," Papa said. "This handle is loose. Good thing you told us instead of trying to fix it yourself!"

Papa Beaver tightened the handle with his special tool. "Try it now, Benny!"

Benny turned the handle, and whoosh! Clear, cool water flowed out perfectly. "It works!" Benny cheered, clapping his small paws together.

"Benny," said Mama Beaver, giving him a warm, fuzzy hug, "you showed great leadership today. Instead of getting upset or giving up, you communicated clearly about the problem."

"What's leadership?" asked Benny, tilting his furry head.

"Leadership means speaking up when something needs to be fixed, and helping your family by sharing important information," explained Papa Beaver. "You were brave to ask for help."

Benny felt proud and warm inside, like sunshine was glowing in his chest. He brushed his teeth with the flowing water, making happy humming sounds. The water felt cool and refreshing on his teeth, and his toothbrush made satisfying "scrub, scrub" sounds.

After breakfast, when Benny's friend Rosie Rabbit came to play, she noticed that her favorite toy was stuck under the couch.

"I can't reach it," Rosie said sadly.

"Remember what I learned today!" said Benny excitedly. "When something isn't working, we should speak up and ask for help!" He called to Mama Beaver, who helped them retrieve the toy easily.

"Wow, Benny," said Rosie. "You're really good at knowing when to ask for help!"

That night, as Benny brushed his teeth again with perfectly flowing water, he smiled at himself in the mirror. He had learned that speaking up wasn't scary – it was helpful and brave.

The End.
