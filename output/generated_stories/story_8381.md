# Benny Bear's Special Morning

Benny Bear stretched his fuzzy arms in the quiet attic where he lived with Grandma Bear. Sunlight peeked through the dusty window, making golden stripes on the wooden floor. His tummy rumbled like a tiny thunderstorm.

"Oh my," Benny said to himself, rubbing his round belly. "I'm very hungry, but Grandma is still sleeping." He could hear her gentle snores coming from downstairs.

Benny looked at his messy fur in the old mirror. His brown coat was all tangled from sleeping. His paws felt sticky from yesterday's honey treat. His teeth felt fuzzy too.

"I should wait for Grandma to help me," Benny thought. But then he remembered what Grandma always said: "Little bears who take care of themselves grow up to be strong, happy bears."

Benny took a deep breath. "I can do this myself!" he whispered bravely.

First, he found his special little brush. It had soft bristles that felt nice on his fur. Benny started brushing very carefully. At first, his paws felt clumsy. The brush went this way and that way. But soon, he found a good rhythm. Brush, brush, brush. His fur became soft and fluffy.

"Look at that!" Benny said, admiring himself in the mirror. "I look much better!"

Next, Benny climbed onto his step-stool by the tiny attic sink. He turned the water on slowly, just like Grandma taught him. The water felt cool and fresh on his paws. He squeezed a tiny bit of soap and washed his face gently. The soap smelled like pine trees and made little bubbles.

"This feels so good," Benny giggled as the cool water dripped from his whiskers.

Then came the tricky part - brushing his teeth. Benny squeezed just a little bit of toothpaste on his small toothbrush. He moved it in gentle circles, just like Grandma showed him. His coordination was getting better every day!

"Much fresher!" Benny said, showing his clean teeth to the mirror.

For breakfast, Benny carefully made himself a simple meal. He spread honey on bread, being very careful not to drip. He poured milk into his favorite blue cup without spilling. He even put a small apple on his plate for healthy eating.

Just as Benny finished preparing everything, he heard Grandma's footsteps on the attic stairs.

"Good morning, my sweet cub," Grandma Bear said, appearing at the top of the stairs. Then she stopped and stared. "Oh my goodness! Look at you!"

Benny beamed proudly. "I took care of myself this morning, Grandma! I brushed my fur, washed my face, brushed my teeth, and made breakfast!"

Grandma Bear's eyes sparkled with joy. "Benny, I am so proud of you! You showed such wonderful independence and self-care. Your fur looks beautiful, and this breakfast looks delicious."

"It felt really good to do it myself," Benny said, giving Grandma a big, fluffy hug. "And I was very careful with everything."

"That's what growing up is all about," Grandma said, hugging him back. "Taking good care of ourselves shows that we love ourselves and others love us too."

Benny and Grandma shared the breakfast together in the sunny attic, both feeling happy and proud. Benny learned that taking care of himself wasn't just about being clean - it was about feeling good inside and out.

The End.
