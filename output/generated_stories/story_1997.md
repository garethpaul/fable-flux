# Pebble the Penguin’s Cozy Night Plan

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a soft, round belly. Pebble loved to sing little songs and wiggle happy feet. Pebble’s cozy bedroom was snug and warm, with a fuzzy blue rug, a tiny lamp that glowed like a firefly, and a blanket that smelled like clean soap and sunshine.

One evening, Pebble was busy playing with blocks. “Clack! Clack!” went the blocks as Pebble stacked them high. The clock on the wall hummed a gentle tick-tock. The lamp made a soft golden circle on the floor. Pebble’s tummy grumbled a small “grr,” and Pebble yawned a long, sleepy “aaaah.”

Mama Penguin peeked in. “Hi, Pebble,” she said kindly. “It’s getting late. It’s time for your cozy night plan.”

Pebble wiggled. “But I want to keep playing!” Pebble chirped. “Just one more tower!”

Mama nodded. “I see you love your blocks. Playing is fun. But your body needs care. We can respect your body by helping it rest. Let’s use time like a friend. First we tidy, then we wash, then we read, then we sleep.” She tapped the clock. “We can do it step by step.”

Pebble looked at the tall, wobbly tower. Pebble felt a little worried. “What if I miss the fun?” Pebble whispered.

Mama smiled and held Pebble’s flipper. “Fun will wait. When we take care of ourselves, tomorrow feels even better.”

Pebble listened. Tick-tock. Tick-tock. The gentle sound felt like a hug. “Okay,” said Pebble. “Let’s try the cozy night plan.”

Step 1: Tidy Time.
Pebble and Mama sang, “Tidy, tidy, put away!” Pebble picked up a red block. Click! Into the basket. The blocks made happy clinks. The rug felt soft under Pebble’s feet. Pebble took a deep breath. The room looked neat. Pebble felt proud.

Step 2: Wash Time.
In the little bathroom, warm water whispered, “Shhhh.” Pebble dipped flippers in the suds. Bubbles popped like tiny stars. Pop! Pop! Pebble giggled. “Hello, bubble beard!” Pebble said, making a silly face in the mirror.
Mama laughed softly. “We wash to care for our bodies. We show respect to ourselves.”
Pebble nodded. “I am caring for me.” The towel felt fluffy and warm. Pebble smelled like soap and minty toothpaste. The mint felt cool and tingly. Ting-gle!

Step 3: Pajama Time.
Pebble slid into soft pajamas with little fish on them. “So snuggly!” Pebble chirped, rubbing the cozy fabric with happy flippers. The lamp glowed gentle gold. The blanket waited like a cloud.

Step 4: Story Time.
Pebble hopped into bed. Rustle, rustle. Mama opened a book. Her voice was calm and slow, like waves. Pebble listened to the words. Pebble’s eyes grew heavy. Pebble felt safe. Pebble whispered, “I like our plan.”

Just then, Pebble remembered the blocks. “Mama, did I respect our plan?” Pebble asked.
Mama kissed Pebble’s head. “Yes. You respected our plan, you respected your body, and you used time kindly. That is good self-care.”

Pebble smiled. But there was one more little problem. Pebble had a big feeling inside. “Sometimes I don’t want to stop,” Pebble said softly. “I feel mad at the clock.”

Mama nodded with understanding. “Big feelings are okay. We can talk to them. We can say, ‘Hello, feeling. I see you.’ Then we can choose a gentle step. Would you like to choose a small step for tomorrow?”

Pebble thought. The room smelled like clean sheets. The night was quiet. “Tomorrow after play, I will tidy for five minutes when the clock says tick-tock to seven,” Pebble said. Pebble held up five little flippers—well, pretended to. Pebble giggled.

Mama smiled. “That is a good plan. You are using time wisely. You are caring for you. And you are respecting our home.”

Pebble felt warm all over. Pebble whispered, “Thank you, body, for today. Thank you, room, for being cozy. Thank you, time, for helping me.”

The lamp made a tiny hum. The blanket hugged Pebble close. Pebble took a slow breath in… and out. “I can do small steps,” Pebble murmured. “Small steps help me.”

Mama turned the lamp low. “Good night, Pebble.”

“Good night, Mama,” Pebble said.

In the gentle glow, Pebble pictured tomorrow: play, tidy, wash, read, sleep. Pebble felt calm. The clock whispered tick-tock like a lullaby. Pebble’s eyes closed, and Pebble drifted into sweet dreams, knowing that caring for self, showing respect, and using time kindly made the day feel just right.

The clear lesson whispered in Pebble’s heart: When I care for me with small steps, I respect myself and others, and I make time my friend.

The End.
