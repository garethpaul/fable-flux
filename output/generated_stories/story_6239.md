# Nutkin's Clean Words Adventure

Nutkin the squirrel loved living in the cozy home office. The big wooden desk was perfect for storing his acorn collection, and the soft carpet felt wonderful under his tiny paws. But Nutkin had a problem that made him feel very sad.

"Achoo! Achoo!" Nutkin sneezed loudly. His nose was runny, and his whiskers felt sticky. Worst of all, his teeth were yellow and his breath smelled yucky. When he tried to talk to his friend Birdie through the window, she flew away quickly.

"Why won't anyone listen to my stories?" Nutkin wondered aloud. He loved sharing tales about his acorn adventures, but lately, nobody wanted to hear them.

Mrs. Chen, who worked at the big computer, noticed Nutkin looking upset. "Oh my," she said gently. "Little squirrel, I think you need to learn about staying clean and healthy."

Nutkin's ears perked up. He was curious but also a little scared. He had never learned about hygiene before.

"First," Mrs. Chen explained, "let's wash those paws." She showed Nutkin a small bowl of warm, soapy water. The bubbles sparkled like tiny rainbows, and the water felt nice and warm.

"Scrub, scrub, scrub!" Nutkin sang as he washed his paws. The soap smelled like fresh flowers, and his paws felt so much cleaner. "This feels wonderful!"

Next, Mrs. Chen brought out a tiny, soft toothbrush. "Now we brush those teeth," she said kindly. "Clean teeth help us speak clearly and keep our breath fresh."

Nutkin was nervous at first. "Will it hurt?" he asked in his squeaky voice.

"Not at all," Mrs. Chen smiled. "Be brave, little one. Good hygiene helps us communicate better with others."

Nutkin took a deep breath and opened his mouth. The minty toothpaste tasted cool and fresh. As he brushed, his teeth became white and shiny again. "Look how sparkly they are!" he chirped happily.

Mrs. Chen helped Nutkin clean his whiskers with a damp cloth that smelled like lavender. Then she showed him how to use tissues when he sneezed. "Always cover your nose and mouth," she explained. "This keeps germs from spreading to others."

"And remember," Mrs. Chen added, "we can reuse these cloths after washing them. Taking care of ourselves and our planet go together!"

Nutkin practiced everything he learned. He washed his paws before eating, brushed his teeth twice a day, and always used tissues when he sneezed. Soon, he felt much better and more confident.

The next morning, Birdie landed on the windowsill. "Good morning, Nutkin!" she chirped. "You look wonderful today!"

"Thank you!" Nutkin replied with his bright, clean smile. "Would you like to hear about my adventure finding the golden acorn?"

"Oh yes!" Birdie said excitedly. "I love your stories!"

Nutkin told his tale with clear words and fresh breath. His clean whiskers twitched with joy as Birdie listened to every word. Other animals gathered around the window to hear his wonderful story too.

"Being clean helps me share my words better," Nutkin realized happily. "When I take care of myself, others want to listen to what I have to say!"

From that day on, Nutkin kept up his healthy habits. He learned that good hygiene wasn't just about looking nice – it helped him communicate clearly and feel confident sharing his thoughts and stories with friends.

And whenever young animals visited the office, Nutkin would teach them about staying clean and healthy too, just like Mrs. Chen had taught him.

The End.
