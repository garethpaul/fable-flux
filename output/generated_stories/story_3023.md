# Ollie the Otter Tries Again

Ollie the otter was a playful little swimmer, but today he was not in the water. He was in the family car with Mama, Papa, and Baby Mia. The car smelled like oranges from Mama’s snack. The seat felt soft and warm from the sun. The engine hummed like a friendly bee: brrrrrmmm.

Ollie wiggled his whiskers. “We go to the lake?” he asked.

“Soon,” said Mama, smiling. “First we drive. Seat belts on.”

“Click!” went the belts. Ollie loved that sound. He loved the windows too. Outside, trees whooshed by in green blurs. The breeze from the vent felt cool on Ollie’s nose.

Ollie had a small puzzle toy from the back seat pocket. It was a round, blue toy with little shapes: a star, a triangle, a circle, and a square. The shapes needed to go into the right holes. There was also a little button that made a soft chime: ting!

“I can do it,” Ollie said. His paws wiggled. He liked to try new things.

He picked up the star. It was bumpy and bright. He pushed it into the circle hole. Thunk. It did not fit.

“Hm,” said Ollie. He tried again. Thunk. It still did not fit.

Baby Mia giggled. “Star goes there?” she asked.

Ollie’s ears felt warm. He wanted to be fast. He wanted to be right. “I can do it,” he whispered. He took a breath. The car smelled like oranges and a tiny bit like crayons. The hum of the engine made his tummy feel calm.

He turned the star, and it slid into the star hole. Click!

“I did it!” Ollie cheered.

Papa smiled in the rearview mirror. “You kept trying, Ollie. That is persistence.”

“Per… sis… tence,” Ollie said slowly. The word felt big and brave. He liked it.

Ollie picked up the square. It had sharp corners. He pushed it toward the triangle hole. Thunk. Not right.

He tried the circle hole. Thunk. Not right.

His whiskers drooped. “This is hard,” he said. A tiny tear sat in his eye.

Mama’s voice was soft. “It is okay to feel frustrated. Take a little breath. Try one small change.”

Ollie puffed in and out. In… out… The vent blew cool air on his face. He looked closely. He discovered that the square had four corners, and the square hole had four corners too.

He lined them up. Click!

“Yay!” said Baby Mia, clapping. Her little hands made happy pats.

Ollie’s heart felt light. He liked this feeling of figuring things out. He picked up the triangle. He lined it up. Click!

“Three!” Ollie said. “One more!”

He grabbed the circle. Smooth and round. He pushed it toward the circle hole. It almost fit—but the toy wiggled. It slipped and fell under his seat.

“Oops!” said Ollie. He leaned down. He saw the circle hiding in the dark. It smelled dusty like old crackers.

“I want it,” Ollie said. He reached his paw down.

Papa shook his head gently. “Ollie, please keep your belt on and your paws up. We are driving. That keeps everyone safe.”

Ollie sat back. His paws twitched. He wanted the circle now. He felt a grumpy bubble in his belly.

Mama said, “We wait until we stop. That is respect for safety and for others in the car.”

Respect. Another big word. Ollie looked at Baby Mia. She was watching him with wide eyes. He didn’t want to scare her. He wanted to be a kind big brother.

“I will wait,” said Ollie. He held the blue toy close and squeezed the button. Ting. Ting. Ting. The sound was soft and happy. It helped the grumpy bubble get smaller.

They drove past a field. The sun made the grass look golden. Ollie listened to the tires sing on the road: shhhh. He watched birds glide in the sky. Waiting felt long, but he kept going. He was practicing persistence with patience too.

At last, the car slowed down. Papa parked. The engine stopped humming. The world became quiet and still.

“Now we can look,” said Papa.

Ollie unbuckled. “Click.” He slid a paw under the seat. It felt cool and a little dusty. He wiggled his paw left, then right. He could not reach.

“I will try again,” Ollie said. He lay on his tummy on the seat. He stretched a little more. His whiskers brushed the seat edge. He felt something smooth. “I feel it!”

Mama handed him a paper tube from the map. “Use this to help.”

Ollie smiled. He pushed the tube under the seat. Roll, roll, roll. The circle rolled out and bumped his paw. Tap!

“I got it!” Ollie cheered.

He lined the circle up with the circle hole. Click!

“All done,” he said, holding the toy high. The sun made the blue plastic shine. Baby Mia clapped again. Papa and Mama smiled.

“How did you do it?” Papa asked.

Ollie’s eyes sparkled. “I kept trying. I looked closely. I waited my turn. I used a helper. I respected the rules. That is persistence!”

Mama hugged him. “Yes, Ollie. You discovered new ways and kept going. That is how we learn.”

Ollie felt proud and warm. The car smelled like oranges and victory. He pressed the button one more time. Ting! He laughed.

Then he peeked at Baby Mia. “Do you want to try the puzzle?” he asked.

Baby Mia nodded. Ollie handed it to her gently. “I can help if you want. I will wait and watch.”

They took turns. They used kind words. They listened to each other. The family car felt cozy and happy as they shared and learned together.

Ollie wiggled his whiskers and whispered, “Try, try, try again.”

The End.
