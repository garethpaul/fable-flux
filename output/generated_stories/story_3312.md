# Benny Beaver's Big Dinner Problem

Benny Beaver was the busiest beaver in the whole forest. He loved to help everyone with everything! His big front teeth clicked together when he smiled, and his fluffy brown tail made a happy *thump-thump* sound when he walked.

Today was very special. Benny had invited all his forest friends for dinner in his cozy dining room. The wooden table gleamed in the warm light, and colorful flowers sat in a pretty vase in the center.

"This will be perfect!" Benny said, clapping his little paws together. "Everyone will love my famous acorn soup!"

But when Benny opened his cupboard, his heart sank. *Crash!* An empty soup pot fell out. There were no acorns anywhere!

"Oh no!" Benny cried, his whiskers twitching with worry. "My friends will be here soon, and I have nothing to serve them!"

Benny sat down in his favorite dining room chair and felt very sad. He put his head in his paws and sighed a big, deep sigh. "What am I going to do?"

Then Benny remembered what his mama always told him: "When things get tough, take a deep breath and think carefully."

Benny closed his eyes and breathed in slowly through his nose. *Sniff.* He could smell the fresh pine trees outside. He breathed out slowly through his mouth. *Whoosh.* He felt calmer already.

"I can figure this out," Benny said to himself. "I'm good at solving problems!"

Benny looked around his dining room with fresh eyes. Sunlight streamed through the window, making everything sparkle. On the windowsill sat a basket of fresh berries he had picked yesterday.

"Berries!" Benny exclaimed, his tail thumping excitedly.

In the corner, he spotted some honey his bee friend Buzzy had given him last week. On the shelf were nuts that Chippy the chipmunk had shared.

"I have everything I need!" Benny realized. "I can make a wonderful fruit salad instead!"

Benny got to work right away. He washed the sweet, juicy berries until they glistened like little jewels. *Splash, splash* went the water. He carefully cracked the nuts with his strong teeth. *Crack, crack!* Then he drizzled the golden honey on top. It smelled like sunshine and flowers.

Just as Benny finished arranging everything in his prettiest bowl, he heard *knock, knock, knock* at the door.

"Come in!" Benny called cheerfully.

In came Rabbit, Squirrel, Owl, and all his forest friends. Their eyes grew wide when they saw the beautiful fruit salad sparkling on the dining room table.

"Wow, Benny!" said Rabbit, her nose twitching. "This looks amazing!"

"It smells so sweet!" hooted Owl.

"But I thought you were making acorn soup," said Squirrel, tilting her head.

Benny's cheeks turned a little pink. "Well, I ran out of acorns," he admitted. "At first I felt really worried. But then I took some deep breaths and looked around. I realized I could use what I already had to make something even better!"

All his friends gathered around the table. The fruit salad was the most delicious thing they had ever tasted!

"You're so smart, Benny!" said Rabbit. "You turned a problem into something wonderful!"

Benny beamed with pride. He learned that when things don't go as planned, taking a moment to breathe and think can help you find new solutions. Sometimes the best ideas come when you use what you already have in creative ways.

The End.
