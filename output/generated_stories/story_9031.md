# Miko's Big Helper Day

The guest room was quiet and cozy. Sunlight streamed through the soft yellow curtains, making dancing shadows on the wooden floor. Miko the monkey sat on the big bed, swinging his furry tail back and forth.

"Today feels special," Miko said to himself, scratching his chin thoughtfully.

Just then, he heard voices downstairs. His family was preparing for their guests from far away - the Chen family from China, who were visiting for the week.

Miko's ears perked up. He scampered down the stairs, his little hands gripping the banister. In the kitchen, he found Mama unpacking boxes and Papa arranging flowers.

"Oh my," sighed Mama, looking at all the work. "There's so much to do before our guests arrive. The guest room needs fresh sheets, and I haven't finished cooking yet."

Miko's chest puffed up with determination. "I can help!" he announced bravely.

Papa smiled. "That's very kind, Miko, but you're still little."

"But I'm a big helper!" Miko insisted, jumping up and down. "Please let me try!"

Mama and Papa looked at each other. "Alright," said Mama gently. "But you'll need to be very careful."

Miko clapped his hands together. "I'll be the best helper ever!"

First, Miko climbed up to the guest room. The sheets smelled like fresh lavender. He tugged and pulled, making the bed as neat as he could. His small hands smoothed every wrinkle.

"This looks perfect!" he said proudly.

Next, Miko remembered something important. He ran to his toy box and carefully selected his favorite stuffed elephant - the one that always made him feel safe at night.

"Every guest should feel welcome," Miko whispered, placing the soft elephant on the pillow.

When the doorbell rang, Miko raced downstairs. The Chen family stood at the door with their little daughter, Li Wei, who looked shy and tired from traveling.

"Welcome to our home!" Miko said loudly, spreading his arms wide like he'd seen Papa do.

Li Wei peeked out from behind her mother's leg. She didn't speak much English yet, but Miko had an idea.

"Come see!" Miko said gently, taking Li Wei's hand. He led her upstairs to the guest room.

Li Wei's eyes grew wide when she saw the cozy bed and the friendly elephant waiting for her.

"For me?" she asked in her quiet voice.

Miko nodded enthusiastically. "Yes! His name is Peanut. He keeps away bad dreams."

Li Wei hugged the elephant tight and smiled her first real smile since arriving.

At dinner, Miko made sure Li Wei had the best seat. He showed her how to use chopsticks and shared his favorite crackers. Even though they spoke different languages, they laughed together when Miko made funny faces.

"You're a wonderful leader, Miko," Papa whispered during dessert. "You helped our guests feel at home."

Miko's heart felt warm and fuzzy. "Leading means helping everyone feel happy and safe," he realized.

That night, as Li Wei cuddled with Peanut in the guest room, Miko felt proud. He had practiced being brave, shared his special toy, and learned that being a good leader meant caring for others - no matter where they came from.

"Tomorrow, I'll teach Li Wei my favorite games," Miko planned sleepily. "Good leaders always find new ways to help."

The End.
