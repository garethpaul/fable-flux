# Dori the Dolphin’s Splashy Art Day

Dori was a playful dolphin who loved to wiggle, giggle, and splash. Today, Dori swam through the bright blue doors of the creative art room. The room smelled like fresh paper and lemon soap. Sunlight danced on shiny tables. Soft music went, “la-la-la,” from a corner radio.

“Hello, art room!” Dori sang, flipping her tail. “I’m ready to make a big, happy picture!”

On the wall, a big clock ticked. Tick-tock, tick-tock. A friendly turtle teacher named Tula waved her paintbrush. “Welcome, Dori,” said Tula. “We have one hour to make art, clean up, and do our wiggle exercise.”

“One hour?” Dori’s eyes grew wide. “I want to paint a rainbow reef, glue seashells, stamp starfish, and sprinkle glitter! Can I do it all?”

“You can try,” Tula smiled. “Use a plan. That’s a strategy. It helps us make smart choices and use our time.”

Dori felt a little flutter in her tummy. So many ideas! “Okay,” Dori whispered. “I’ll make a plan.” She tapped her fin to the beat of the clock.

“First,” Dori said to herself, “sketch the reef. Next, paint colors. Then, stamp starfish. Last, glue shells. Save glitter for the end.”

Dori drew soft, wavy lines. The pencil made a whispery sound, skritch-scratch. “This looks nice,” Dori said, smiling. She dipped her brush into blue paint. The paint felt cool and smooth, like ocean water on a quiet day. Swish, swish. The reef grew on the paper—curvy coral, swaying seaweed, cozy little caves.

“Great start,” said Tula. “How much time is left?”

Dori looked at the clock. “Forty minutes!” she chirped. “I can do this!”

Curiosity bubbled in Dori’s mind. “What does yellow look like on blue?” She dabbed yellow on a blue wave. “Ooh, it makes green!” Dori giggled. “I learned something new!”

She moved to the stamp tray. The starfish stamps were squishy and smelled like clean rubber. “Stamp, stamp, stamp!” Dori pressed carefully. A starfish landed crooked. “Oh no,” Dori frowned. “It’s messy.”

Tula swam over slowly. “What now?” she asked gently.

Dori took a deep breath. “I can fix it.” She stamped a second starfish right next to the first. “Ta-da! They’re friends,” she said. “My mistake made something cute.”

“That’s resilience,” Tula said. “You didn’t give up.”

Dori’s heart felt warm. “I can be resilient!”

She checked the clock. “Twenty-five minutes left,” Dori said. “Time to glue shells.” She opened the glue. A sweet, sticky smell floated up. Dori squeezed too hard—squish! A big blob plopped onto the paper. It slid like a little snowball.

Dori’s cheeks felt hot. “Oh, fish fins,” she whispered. “I messed up again.”

She looked at the blob. She looked at the shells. Then she looked at her plan. “I can change my strategy,” Dori said softly. “I’ll use a tiny squeeze and a tiny shell. Little by little.”

Squeeze. Tap. Press. Dori’s fin moved slow and steady. The shells stuck down like shiny moon rocks. “Yes,” Dori smiled, “this is working.”

“Ten minutes!” called Tula, pointing to the clock.

Dori’s eyes popped. “Glitter time!” She shook the glitter. Nothing came out. Tap-tap. Still nothing. She tilted the bottle. A tiny sneeze of glitter puffed into the air and sprinkled her nose. “It tickles!” Dori laughed.

“Try a different angle,” said Tula. “New strategy!”

Dori held the bottle sideways and shook gently over the glue. The glitter fell like soft, silver rain. Her reef sparkled. “So pretty,” Dori sighed.

“Five minutes left,” Tula said. “Time for our exercise and clean up.”

“But my picture is almost done,” Dori said, looking worried. “I want to keep going.”

Tula nodded kindly. “We can move our bodies first. Exercise helps our brains think and our hearts feel calm. Then we’ll use the last minutes to finish and tidy.”

Dori blinked. “Okay. I can try.” She set down her brush.

They stretched fins and flippers. Reach up, reach down. Wiggle-wiggle. Twist-twist. The music went faster—da-da-da! Dori’s tail swooshed, swish-swish, as she hopped in place. Her heart beat a happy, thump-thump. “I feel strong,” Dori said. “And calm!”

“Two minutes,” Tula said.

Dori swam back to her table. She checked her plan again. “Just one more shell. Just one more star,” she said. She placed them carefully. Then she wiped the table, washed her fin in warm, soapy water, and dried it with a soft towel. She put the lids on the glue and glitter. Click. Twist.

“All clean,” Dori said proudly.

Tula looked at the picture. The coral glowed. The starfish friends smiled. The glitter shimmered like morning waves. “Dori,” said Tula, “you did it.”

Dori’s eyes shined. “I made a plan. I changed my plan when I needed. I stayed calm. I fixed my mistakes. I used my time.”

“That is resilience,” Tula said, patting Dori’s fin. “You kept trying, even when it was hard. You used strategy and curiosity. You moved your body and managed your time. You grew stronger today.”

Dori hugged her picture. “When things go wrong, I can try again,” she said. “I can learn new ways. I can keep going.”

“Yes,” said Tula. “That’s the magic.”

Dori smiled at the sparkly reef. “Tomorrow, I’ll make the sea sky! I’ll plan, I’ll try, and I’ll shine.”

The End.
