# Leo’s Tick-Tock Closet Adventure

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and bright, curious eyes. He had a special place he loved—a closet hideaway with a cozy blanket, a flashlight, and a tiny clock that went tick-tock, tick-tock. The closet smelled like clean laundry and a little bit like crayons. It felt safe and snug, like a warm hug.

One sunny morning, Mama Lion said, “Leo, we have three things to do today. First, pick up your toys. Second, read a book with me. Third, get ready for a walk before lunchtime.”

Leo’s ears perked up. “Three things! I’m brave. I can do it!”

Mama Lion smiled. “Yes, you can. We will listen to the tick-tock to help us. Ten minutes for toys, ten minutes for reading, and ten minutes to get ready. We can do it if we listen, focus, and breathe.”

Leo nodded. “Listen, focus, breathe.” He liked how the words felt in his mouth, soft like the blanket in his closet hideaway.

Leo ran to his room. Toys were everywhere—blocks, a red ball, a tiny drum that went boom-boom. He felt a little wiggly inside. “There’s so much!” he said. His tail swished.

Mama Lion knelt beside him. “Let’s make it fun. When the clock says start, we pick up. When it goes ding, we stop. Ready?”

“Ready!” Leo said.

They tapped the little clock. Tick-tock, tick-tock. Leo took a slow lion breath. In through the nose. Out through the mouth. He felt calm, like a quiet puddle.

“Listen to the clock,” Mama Lion said softly. “Pick up one thing at a time.”

“One at a time,” Leo repeated. He picked up the blocks—clack, clack into the bin. He rolled the red ball into the basket—thump. He put the tiny drum on the shelf—boom, but a gentle boom.

The tick-tock sounded like a tiny marching song. Leo moved with the beat. Tick, he picked up a car. Tock, he put it away. He smiled. “I’m doing it!”

Ding! The clock rang. Leo looked around. “We did it!” The room looked neat and friendly. He felt proud, his chest warm like sunshine.

Mama Lion hugged him. “Good listening. Good time-keeping. Now, let’s read in your closet hideaway.”

They snuggled under the blanket. The flashlight made a small circle of light. It felt cozy and smelled like crayons and soap. Mama opened a book about a little cloud. Her voice was soft. “The little cloud took a breath and floated slow.”

Leo took another lion breath with the cloud. In… and out… He listened to the words and the quiet tick-tock of the closet clock. His ears relaxed. His paws felt still.

Ding! The clock rang again. “Reading time is done,” Mama said kindly. “Now it’s getting-ready time, before our walk.”

Leo wanted one more page. His whiskers drooped. “But I like the cloud.”

Mama nodded. “Me too. We can read it after our walk. Remember our plan. Plans help the day feel smooth.”

Leo thought about the plan. He remembered the proud sunshine feeling when he finished his toys. He took a lion breath. “Okay. I will listen to the plan.”

They went to the hallway. Leo looked at his shoes and his little green hat. He felt a little swirl of hurry. “What first?” he asked.

“Let’s use mindful steps,” Mama said. “We’ll do three things. One: put on socks. Two: put on shoes. Three: put on hat. We can count the tick-tocks.”

Leo smiled. Counting sounded fun. He sat down. The floor was cool on his paws. He slid on his soft socks. “One, two, three tick-tocks,” he said. The socks felt snug and warm.

He pulled on his shoes. “Four, five, six tick-tocks.” The laces made a whispery sh-sh sound as Mama tied them.

He put on his green hat. “Seven, eight, nine tick-tocks.” The hat felt light and silly. Leo giggled.

Ding! The clock rang. Leo stood tall. “Ready!” he roared, but just a tiny roar, like a purr with a smile.

Outside the closet, the house smelled like toast. Outside the door, the air smelled like grass. Leo took Mama’s paw, and they went for their walk. Birds chirped. Leaves swished. Leo felt calm and brave.

On the way back, Mama said, “Leo, how did you do all your tasks?”

Leo thought. “I listened. I used the clock. I did one thing at a time. I took lion breaths.”

Mama nodded. “That is time-management. You made a plan. You followed it. You were mindful. You listened. And you finished with time for fun.”

Back in the closet hideaway, Leo snuggled again with his book and blanket. The tick-tock was gentle. He smiled a big lion-cub smile.

“I like plans,” Leo said. “Plans help me play and rest.”

Mama kissed his head. “Yes, little lion. When we listen, breathe, and use our time well, the day feels just right.”

Leo closed his eyes for a moment and took one more lion breath. In… and out… He felt proud, calm, and ready for his next adventure.

The End.
