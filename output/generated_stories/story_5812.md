# Ollie Otter and the Tidy Time Timer

Ollie the playful otter loved to wiggle and splash. Today, the sun was warm, and the garden shed smelled like pine wood and fresh soil. The shed was full of tools, pots, and shiny buttons on a little red timer. Ollie’s whiskers twitched with excitement.

“Let’s play!” Ollie chirped, bouncing inside. His paws felt the cool floor. He saw a stack of tiny pots and colorful seed packets. He also saw a broom, a watering can, and a friendly picture chart on the wall. The chart showed steps with little drawings: Sweep, Sort, Plant, Water, Clean Up.

Grandma Bee, a kind neighbor with gentle eyes, waved from the door. “Hello, Ollie. Today we plant flowers with friends. We follow the steps, one at a time. We use the timer to help us.”

Ollie nodded fast. “I can do it!”

His friends came in: Mia the mouse with soft gray fur, Ben the bluebird with bright feathers, and Zara the zebra snail with a shiny stripey shell. They all smiled.

Grandma Bee pointed to the chart. “Step one: Sweep. We have ten minutes. When the timer rings, we move to the next step. Ready?”

“Ready!” everyone said.

Ollie grabbed the broom. Swish, swish! Dust danced in the sunlight. The bristles made a soft scratchy sound. Ollie giggled and twirled. He made broom wiggles on the floor like drawing lines in the sand.

Mia squeaked, “Ollie, can I try too?”

Ollie remembered inclusion means everyone helps. He smiled. “Yes, Mia! Your turn now.” He passed the broom. Mia swept under the bench. Ben pecked at a leaf and dropped it in the bin. Zara hummed and pushed tiny pebbles with her shell.

Ding! The little timer rang. It sounded like a bell on a tiny bike.

Grandma Bee clapped softly. “Well done. Time to switch. Step two: Sort. Seeds here, pots here.”

Ollie looked at the seed packets. There were pictures of sunflowers, daisies, and bluebells. He wanted to open them all at once. His paws wiggled. “I want to see them all!”

Grandma Bee smiled kindly. “We follow directions. One packet at a time. Let’s set the timer: ten minutes.”

Ollie took a slow breath. He felt the cool air tickle his nose. “One at a time,” he repeated. He sorted daisies with daisies, sunflowers with sunflowers. The packets made a soft papery whisper. Mia read the pictures out loud. Ben sang a tiny sorting song. Zara slid labels onto small sticks.

Ding! The timer rang again.

“Step three: Plant,” said Grandma Bee, pointing at the chart. “Use your scoops. Three scoops of soil in each pot.”

Ollie wanted to splash the water can. He loved the swishy sound. But he looked at the chart and the timer. “Plant first,” he told himself. He counted, “One scoop… two scoop… three!” The soil felt cool and crumbly. It smelled like rain. Ollie made a little hole with a careful claw. Plop! One seed. Pat, pat. A gentle cover. He felt proud.

Ben chirped, “You’re doing it, Ollie!”

Zara added, “You are following the steps.”

Mia whispered, “Thank you for letting us help.”

Ding! The timer chimed.

“Step four: Water,” Grandma Bee said. “Just a little sip for each pot.”

Ollie held the watering can. The water made a happy glug-glug sound. Tiny drops sparkled in the light. He wanted to make a big splash, but he remembered time-management. “Little sips,” he said. “We have ten minutes.” He shared the can with Mia, Ben, and Zara. Each friend watered some pots, slow and gentle.

Ding! The timer sang its cheerful ring.

“Step five: Clean Up,” said Grandma Bee. “Tools on hooks. Floor clear.”

Ollie felt a wiggle in his tail. He wanted to run outside now. The sky looked so blue! But he was brave with his choices. Courage can be quiet. It can be finishing what you start. He set the timer again. “We can do it before it rings,” he said.

They worked together. The broom went back on its hook. The scoops went into a box. The seed packets went into a tin with a soft click. The watering can stood by the door, drip, drip, drip.

Ding! The last ring was bright and happy.

Grandma Bee beamed. “You followed directions. You took turns. You watched the time. Look at your neat pots!”

Ollie’s heart felt warm. He saw all the little pots lined up like a parade. Soil smelled sweet. The air felt still and safe. He looked at his friends. “We did it together,” he said.

Mia squeaked, “We made a routine!”

Ben chirped, “We used the timer to help!”

Zara said softly, “We included everyone.”

Grandma Bee nodded. “When we plan our time and follow steps, work feels easy and fun. You were patient, Ollie, even when you wanted to rush. That is smart and brave.”

Ollie grinned. “Time helps me choose. I can play after I finish my steps.”

He pressed the red button one more time. “Next time,” he said, “we can paint labels. We’ll set the timer, take turns, and clean up at the end.”

Everyone cheered. The shed felt bright and calm. Outside, a breeze rustled the leaves. Inside, the little pots waited, quiet and cozy, ready to grow.

Ollie squeezed his friends’ paws and wings and shell. “Thank you,” he said. “We can do big things when we use our time well.”

The End.
