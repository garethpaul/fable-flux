# Penny Penguin Fixes Feelings

Penny was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a shiny black-and-white coat. She wore a tiny yellow tool belt around her belly. Today, she waddled into Grandpa Gus’s garage workshop.

The workshop smelled like fresh wood and orange soap. Sunlight made little dust sparkles dance in the air. Tools hung on the wall—hammers, brushes, and a big red toolbox. The floor felt cool and smooth under Penny’s feet.

“Good morning, Grandpa Gus!” Penny chirped. “I’m ready to help.”

Grandpa Gus, an old gray walrus with kind eyes, smiled. “Good morning, Penny. We will build a small birdhouse today. Our friends the sparrows need a cozy home.”

Penny clapped her flippers. “Yay! I love helping.”

They measured a piece of wood. Tap, tap, tap went the pencil. Penny held the wood while Grandpa sawed. Zip-zip-zip went the saw, and sweet wood smell floated up.

Just then, their friend Max the mouse ran in, holding a broken toy car. The shiny blue paint was chipped, and one wheel hung off.

“My car is broken,” Max sniffled. His whiskers drooped. “Can you fix it?”

Penny’s heart did a little flip. She wanted to help Max. But the birdhouse was not done. She felt a swirl inside—a mix of happy, busy, and worried.

Grandpa Gus put down the saw. “We can help, but let’s make a plan.”

Penny stared at the car and then at the birdhouse pieces. Her tummy felt twisty. Her cheeks felt warm. She didn’t like the twisty feeling.

Max looked at her with big eyes. “Please?”

Penny took a deep breath. She put a flipper on her chest. “Hello, feelings,” she whispered softly. “I feel… busy and worried. I want to help! But I also promised to finish the birdhouse.”

Grandpa Gus nodded. “That is good noticing, Penny. You are being aware of your feelings.”

Penny nodded slowly. “I can solve this. First, I will calm my body.”

She breathed in for a count of one-two. She breathed out for a count of one-two. Her tummy felt softer. Her cheeks cooled.

“Okay,” she said. “Max, I want to help you. Grandpa, I want to keep my promise too. Can we make a small team?”

Grandpa Gus smiled. “That sounds like leadership.”

Penny tapped her beak, thinking. “I have an idea! We can finish one small step of the birdhouse first. Then we can do one small fix on the car. We will take turns, step by step.”

Max wiped his eyes. “Step by step?”

“Yes,” Penny said. “Step one: glue the birdhouse roof.” She squeezed a line of glue. Squish! “We press it down and let it dry.”

They pressed. The glue smelled a little like lemons. Penny checked the clock. “Now we wait for the glue to set. Step two: fix one wheel on your car.”

She lifted the car and peered closely. “The screw is loose. We will tighten it.” She picked up a tiny screwdriver. Click-click. The wheel turned smooth again.

Max giggled. “It spins!”

Penny smiled. “Step three: back to the birdhouse. We can sand the edges.” She rubbed the wood with sandpaper. Shh-shh-shh. The wood felt soft and safe.

Max watched. “Can I help?”

“Yes, thank you,” said Penny. “Helping is kind.” She handed Max a small brush. “Please brush off the dust.” Brush, brush, brush went the tiny strokes.

Grandpa Gus nodded. “You all are doing great. I am proud of your teamwork and honesty.”

Penny blinked. “Honesty?”

“You said how you felt,” Grandpa Gus said. “You told the truth about your worry. You kept your promise to the birds. And you helped Max. That is integrity.”

Penny’s chest felt warm and light. “Thank you, Grandpa.”

They painted the birdhouse a happy yellow. Swish, swish. Penny added a little blue heart on the front. Max added a tiny welcome dot with his paw.

“Step four,” Penny said, “final check.” She touched each part. “Roof? Check. Door? Check. Smooth sides? Check.”

Max held up his car. “Wheel? Check. Paint? I can fix the chip later.”

Penny nodded. “We can do it together after the birdhouse is dry.”

When everything was done, they hung the birdhouse by the workshop window. The air smelled like paint and pine. Outside, a little breeze danced across the leaves. Two sparrows hopped closer. Chirp-chirp! They peeked in, then fluffed their feathers with joy.

Max hugged his car and looked up at Penny. “Thank you for helping me.”

Penny hugged back. “Thank you for waiting and helping too. I felt worried before. Now I feel proud and calm.”

Grandpa Gus clapped his flippers. “Today, you listened to your feelings, made a plan, and led with kindness. That is the way of a strong heart.”

Penny smiled big. “When feelings get twisty, I can breathe, name them, and choose helpful steps. I can keep my promises and help friends too.”

The sparrows chirped again. The sun made the birdhouse glow. The workshop felt warm and happy.

Penny patted her tool belt. “Feelings fixed. Birdhouse built. Friend helped.”

She waddled to the door and waved. “We did it, together.”

The End.
