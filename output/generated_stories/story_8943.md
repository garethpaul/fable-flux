# Tweet’s Calm Desk Concert

Tweet was a small singing bird with bright blue feathers and a tiny silver microphone. She lived in a cozy home office with a big desk, a soft chair, and a window full of morning light. The keyboard clicked, the printer hummed, and the air smelled like warm paper and lemon tea.

Today, Tweet wanted to sing a song for her friends on the computer. The screen showed their happy faces in little squares. “Hello, friends!” Tweet chirped. “I made a new song!”

Tweet took a deep breath. “La—” But the printer beeped. Beep-beep! The sound startled her. Her feathers fluffed up. “Oh no,” Tweet said. “That was not my song.”

She tried again. “La la—” The chair squeaked. Eeeek! Tweet’s beak drooped. “I can’t sing with all these sounds,” she sighed.

Her friends spoke kindly through the speaker.
“Hi, Tweet!” said Pip the mouse. “We can’t wait to hear you.”
“You’ve got this,” said Bella the bunny. “Take your time.”

Tweet nodded, but inside she felt hot and tight, like a string pulled too hard. She stomped her tiny feet on the desk. Tap tap! “Why won’t it be quiet?” she cried. A small tear sparkled on her cheek.

Grandma Wren, who sat in a photo frame on the desk, seemed to smile. Tweet remembered Grandma’s gentle words: “When the world feels too loud, make a calm cloud.”

“What is a calm cloud?” Pip asked.

Tweet sniffled. “It’s a way to help my feelings.” She put her wing on her chest. “First, I stop.” She stood still. “Then I breathe.” In… 1, 2, 3. Out… 1, 2, 3. Again, in… out… The lemon tea smelled sweet. The warm light felt soft on her feathers. The hum of the room sounded like a sleepy cat.

“I can do one more thing,” Tweet said. “I can name what I feel.” She touched her chest. “I feel frustrated. My body is tight. My face is frowny. I want to shout.” She looked at her friends. “But I will be kind to myself. I can take care of me.”

Pip squeaked, “How can we help?”

Tweet wiped her tear. “We can prepare. We can make the room ready for singing.”

She looked around. “Printer, please rest,” she said. She pressed the little button. The printer light turned from green to sleepy orange. “Chair, I will move you slow,” she whispered. She slid the chair so it would not squeak. She found a soft cloth and put it under one wobbly leg. No more eeeek.

Tweet checked her list in a tiny notebook with a sunflower on the cover.
- Take three calm breaths
- Turn off noisy things
- Sip water
- Ask friends for help
- Try again

“Water!” Tweet chirped. She took a small sip. The cool water felt smooth, and her mouth didn’t feel sticky anymore.

“Lights are a little bright,” said Bella softly. “Would lower lights help you feel calm?”

“Good idea,” Tweet said. She tilted the shade. The light turned gentle and golden. The desk felt cozy, like a nest.

“Do you want a count-in?” asked Pip. “I can tap 1, 2, 3.”

“Yes, please,” Tweet said. “I like to be ready.”

Pip tapped the desk. Tap… tap… tap…

Tweet closed her eyes. She pictured a fluffy white cloud in her mind. She sat on the cloud and let her feelings float. “I can try again,” she told herself. “I am safe. I am ready. I am calm.”

She opened her eyes and smiled. “Thank you for waiting,” she told her friends. “Thank you for helping.”

Then she sang. Her voice was clear and bright, like sunlight on a little bell. “When I feel tight and want to shout, I pause and breathe and let it out. I make a plan, I take good care, and try again with love to share.”

The friends swayed. Pip clapped his tiny paws. Bella’s ears wiggled like happy ribbons. Even the keyboard clicked softly, click-click, like it was clapping too.

Tweet finished with a sweet “doo-dee-doo.” She felt warm and proud. The tight string feeling was gone. In its place was a soft, strong feeling, like a hug.

Pip said, “Your calm cloud helped you sing!”

Bella said, “Your plan helped too. You were prepared!”

Tweet nodded. “When I felt frustrated, I did three things. I stopped and breathed. I took care of me. I made a plan to try again. And I asked for help. We can all do that.”

She looked at the camera. “Friends everywhere, your feelings matter. If you feel frustrated, it’s okay. Pause. Breathe. Prepare. Ask for help. Then try again.”

The printer rested. The chair stayed quiet. The home office glowed with gentle light. Tweet’s microphone sparkled like a tiny star.

Tweet fluffed her feathers and giggled. “Now,” she chirped, “who wants to sing along?”

“Me! Me!” said everyone, every voice welcome. Together, they sang the calm cloud song, soft and bright.

The End.
