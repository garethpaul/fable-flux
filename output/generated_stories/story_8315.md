# Toby Turtle Stands Up Fairly

In a quiet study corner, with a small desk, soft lamp, and a basket of crayons, lived a shy turtle named Toby. Toby liked the smell of pencil shavings and the gentle tick-tick of the clock. He liked the feel of smooth paper under his little turtle toes. The study corner was his safe, cozy place.

Each afternoon, friends came to the study corner to read picture books and draw. Buzz the bee buzzed softly, “Bzz-bzz, hello!” Pip the mouse squeaked, “Hi!” Lila the ladybug tapped her tiny feet on the desk. Toby would whisper, “Hi,” and tuck his head a little into his shell. He was shy, but he loved his friends.

On the desk sat a jar of tasty veggie sticks—crisp carrots and crunchy cucumbers. Next to it was a tin for crayons. A sign on the wall said, “We share. We listen. We care.”

One sunny day, the friends planned a little learning time. “Let’s draw our planet,” said Lila. “Green and blue!” “And eat a crunchy snack,” buzzed Buzz. “Carrots help us grow strong,” squeaked Pip.

Toby nodded. He loved the sweet, earthy smell of carrots. He loved the cool, watery bite of cucumbers.

But just as Toby reached for a carrot, Zip the magpie swooped in through the window. Zip liked shiny things and liked to be first. He flapped and landed on the pencil cup with a clack.

“Mine first!” said Zip. He grabbed a whole claw full of carrots. He pulled the green crayon, the blue crayon, and even the silver one, stuffing them under his wing.

Pip’s whiskers drooped. “Hey, we were going to share.”

Buzz’s hum went low. “Bzz… that doesn’t feel fair.”

Toby’s heart pattered, thump-thump, thump-thump. He tucked deeper into his shell. He didn’t like loud moments. He didn’t like to speak up. But he knew the sign on the wall. We share. We listen. We care. That was their study corner rule. It was about justice—about what is fair for everyone.

Lila spoke gently. “Zip, we all need crayons to draw our planet.”

Zip looked around with shiny eyes. “But I’m fast! I got them first.”

Toby peeped out of his shell. His voice was soft. “Being fast doesn’t make it fair,” he said. “Fair means everyone gets a turn.”

Zip tilted his head. “But I’m hungry, and I love carrots.”

Toby took a little breath. “We can listen to you, Zip,” he said kindly. “You can say you’re hungry. And we can help. But taking everything isn’t fair.”

The clock ticked. The room felt still and warm. Toby felt his shell loosen as he stood a tiny bit taller.

“Let’s make a plan,” Toby said, a little braver now. “We take turns with crayons. We count to five, then pass. And we share the veggie sticks so all bellies feel good.”

Buzz buzzed bright again. “Bzz-yes! That is fair.”

Pip nodded. “We can even save some peels for the compost. That helps the earth.”

Lila smiled. “That’s caring for our planet.”

Zip looked at the carrots under his wing. He looked at Toby’s calm eyes. “I… I didn’t think. I just grabbed. I’m sorry.”

Toby smiled a small, shy smile. “Thank you for saying sorry. We accept you. You can join if we all follow the plan.”

Zip slowly set down the carrots. Tap-tap, went the orange sticks on the desk. He placed the green and blue crayons back in the tin. “I will listen. I like your plan.”

They made a little turn chart. Pip drew tiny circles with numbers: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5. Buzz hummed while counting, “One… two… three… four… five,” and then passed the green crayon. Lila dotted blue oceans while Toby colored gentle green land. Zip took his turn with silver to draw the moon. The crayons felt smooth and warm in their hands. The paper made soft scratch-scratch sounds.

After drawing, they shared the snack. Crunch! went the carrots. Munch! went the cucumbers. The cool cucumber juice dripped on Toby’s chin. He giggled, “It’s so fresh!” Pip wiped it with a napkin. They put carrot tops and cucumber peels in a small compost cup. “This goes back to the garden,” said Lila. “It helps plants grow.”

Zip chewed slowly. “Thank you for sharing. It feels nice when things are fair.”

Toby felt a glow in his shell. He had spoken up. He had used his quiet voice to make justice happen in their study corner. Justice meant rules that help everyone, and kind listening to make sure all are treated right.

“Today we learned,” Toby said softly. “We share. We listen. We care. Fair is for everyone.”

Buzz buzzed happily, “Bzz-yes!”

Pip squeaked, “We accept friends, even when they make a mistake.”

Lila added, “And we eat good foods that help our bodies grow and care for the earth too.”

Zip fluffed his feathers. “I will remember. Next time, I’ll ask and wait.”

The lamp hummed. The clock ticked. The planet picture shone with green, blue, and a silver moon. The study corner felt safe again—warm, bright, and fair.

Toby tucked into his shell, cozy and proud, and whispered, “Justice feels like a hug for everyone.”

The End.
