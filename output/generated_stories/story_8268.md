# Penny Penguin Writes on the Rooftop

Penny was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a shiny black-and-white coat. One sunny morning, Penny waddled up the safe, wide stairs to the rooftop of the community center. The rooftop garden smelled like fresh mint and warm soil. The wind tickled her feathers, and the sun felt cozy on her back.

Penny loved to write. She carried a little blue notebook and a stubby yellow pencil. “Tap, tap, tap,” went the pencil on the paper. “I’m going to write my story,” Penny chirped. “I will write about helping!”

On the rooftop, Penny saw raised flower boxes, soft green grass mats, and a painted path with arrows for walking. A sign by the door said, “Rooftop Rules: Walk, stay behind the rail, and share.” Penny read the words slowly. “Walk… stay… share,” she whispered. “These are safety rules. I will write them in my story.”

She found a shady spot under a fluttering kite flag. Penny opened her notebook. “Dear Story,” she said, “today I will write about being safe and helpful.” She pressed her pencil and began to write big letters.

Just then, Penny heard clattering steps. Two little chicks, Pip and Dot, rushed out onto the roof. Their feathers were fluffy like dandelions. “We want to fly our paper planes!” Pip peeped. “We want to run fast!” Dot chirped.

The wind whooshed. The paper planes rustled. Penny looked at the painted path and the rooftop rail. Her belly felt wiggly. “Running near the rail is not safe,” she thought. “I must be careful and kind.”

Pip and Dot started to run. Their planes swirled. A pot of mint leaves wobbled. The smell of mint puffed into the air. “Oh!” Penny called. “Please walk on the arrows. The sign says walk.”

Pip slowed down. Dot did not hear. Dot’s plane zipped close to the rail. Penny’s heart went thump-thump. She remembered the rules and her story. She wanted to help. She wanted to keep everyone safe.

Penny took a calm breath. She waddled to Dot with slow, steady steps. “Dot,” she said in a soft, friendly voice, “let’s walk on the arrows. It keeps us safe.” She pointed to the sign. “See the words? Walk. Stay behind the rail. Share.”

Dot blinked. “I didn’t see the sign,” Dot said. “I was excited.”

“It is okay to be excited,” Penny said. “We can write a plan so it is safe and fun.” Penny smiled. “I love to write. Writing helps us remember.”

Pip flapped his little wings. “A plan?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Penny. “Let’s write rooftop rules together in big, simple words. We can draw pictures too!”

They sat on the grass mats. The mats felt springy and soft. The wind made the pages flutter. “Hold the page,” Penny giggled. “Wind, please be gentle.” She wrote: “1. Walk. 2. Stay behind the rail. 3. Share. 4. Ask for help.”

Pip and Dot drew a little penguin walking on the arrows. They drew a rail with a smiley face to remind them to stay back. They drew two chicks sharing a plane. “This is fun!” Pip said.

Penny added, “5. Take turns with planes.” She read the list out loud in a sing-song voice. “Walk. Stay. Share. Ask. Take turns.” The list felt like a friendly song.

Dot nodded. “I will walk,” Dot said. Pip nodded too. “I will take turns,” he said.

Penny taped the paper to the big sign with bright purple tape. The tape made a sticky “shhrrrp” sound. The paper fluttered but stayed put. “Now everyone can see,” Penny said. “We are being helpful to our community.”

Soon, other friends came up to the rooftop: a turtle named Tessa, a bunny named Bo, and a kind janitor named Mr. Lee. Mr. Lee smelled like fresh soap and orange peel. He smiled. “What a great idea,” he said. “You wrote a clear plan. That keeps us safe. That is civic responsibility.”

Penny’s cheeks felt warm with pride. “We wrote it together,” she said. “Writing helps us share ideas.”

They all played with paper planes. They took turns. They walked on the arrows. They stayed behind the rail. The mint leaves stayed still. The sun shone bright, and the wind whooshed friendly, soft whooshes.

Later, Penny sat again with her notebook. She wrote her story ending: “Today, we used writing to help friends. We followed rules to stay safe. We were kind and shared. Writing makes our community strong.”

Pip peeped, “What does your story teach?”

Penny read aloud. Her voice was clear and happy. “It teaches: Use writing to make a plan. Follow safety rules. Be helpful. Care for everyone.”

Dot clapped. Mr. Lee gave a thumbs-up. Tessa the turtle whispered, “Slow and safe is great.” Bo the bunny added, “Sharing is caring!”

Penny closed her notebook with a soft “thump.” She looked at the rooftop sign with their new paper. The purple tape glowed in the sun. She felt calm, smart, and kind.

“We did it,” Penny said. “We solved the problem with our pencils and our hearts.”

The wind sang a gentle song. The garden smelled sweet. The rooftop felt safe and friendly. Penny smiled her big cheerful smile.

The lesson: Writing can help us make plans, keep us safe, and help our whole community.

The End.
