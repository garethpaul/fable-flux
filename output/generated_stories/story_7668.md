# Pippa the Parrot’s Plan

Pippa was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a splash of sunny yellow on her chest. She loved the cozy study corner by the window. The rug was soft, the little desk smelled like fresh wood, and crayons waited in a colorful cup. Sunlight made warm patches on the floor. Pippa ruffled her feathers and chirped, “Squawk! It is chores time. Let’s make a plan!”

Her friend Sam, a small boy with curly hair, peeked in. He wore socks with tiny stars. “Hi, Pippa,” Sam said. “I need to clean the study corner, but it feels big. There are books, buttons, and crayons everywhere.”

Pippa tilted her head. “When a job feels big, we use a strategy,” she said, tapping her beak softly on the desk. “A strategy is a smart plan. We do one step at a time.”

Sam smiled. “One step at a time. That sounds good.”

Pippa flapped once and pointed with a bright feather. “Step one: Look. Step two: Sort. Step three: Put away. Step four: Celebrate!”

Sam giggled. “I like step four!”

They looked around. The study corner smelled like paper and lemon cleaner. The window made a little breeze that felt cool on their cheeks. Pippa listened. She could hear a soft tick-tock from the wall clock. Tick. Tock. It felt like the clock was helping them count.

“Let’s start,” Pippa said. “First, we look.” They walked their eyes around the room. “Books on the floor. Buttons in a bowl, but some spilled. Crayons here. Paper there.”

Sam nodded. “I see them.”

“Now we sort,” Pippa said. “Books with books. Buttons with buttons. Crayons with crayons. We can make little homes.” She drew small squares on sticky notes: a book picture, a button picture, and a crayon picture. She stuck them on three small baskets.

Sam picked up a blue book. “Books go here,” he said, placing it in the book basket.

Pippa picked up a red crayon. It felt waxy and warm from the sun. “Crayons go here,” she chirped, placing it in the crayon basket. “Good sorting!”

They worked together. Pippa hummed a happy tune. Sam tapped his socks on the rug. “Tap, tap, tap,” he sang.

Soon, they found a small, shiny button with a flower. “This is from our family sweater,” Sam said softly. “Grandma taught me a tradition. On cleaning days, we sing a button song.”

Pippa’s eyes sparkled. “I love traditions! Please share it.”

Sam cleared his throat and sang in a sweet voice, “Round little button, where do you go? Back to your bowl, neat in a row.” He smiled. “Grandma says we sing, and we think of gentle hands and careful work.”

Pippa bowed her head. “Thank you for sharing your tradition. I will learn it too.” She sang along, her voice light and bright. “Round little button, where do you go?” The song made the work feel calm and happy.

As they sorted, Sam found a tiny puzzle piece under the desk. “Where does this go?” he asked.

Pippa blinked. “Hmm. I am curious. Let’s use strategy again. We can test. We can try.”

They checked the puzzle box picture. It showed a sunny farm. “Look for colors,” Pippa said. “What colors are on the piece?”

Sam held it close. “Green and yellow.”

Pippa pointed to the puzzle on the shelf. “The green field is here. The yellow sun is there. Let’s try the corner.” Sam slid the piece in. Click! It fit.

“We did it!” Sam cheered. “Our plan worked.”

“Strategy helps,” Pippa said softly. “We look, we think, we try.”

Next, Pippa saw paper scraps under the chair. She puffed her feathers. “I have an idea. Let’s make a paper train to carry scraps to the bin.”

Sam’s eyes went wide. “How?”

Pippa folded a long paper strip. “Choo-choo!” She made train sounds. They placed scraps on the paper “train” and pulled it to the trash bin. The paper rustled. The bin made a hollow thump. This felt fun.

Soon, the study corner looked neat. The rug showed its pretty pattern again. The little desk shone in the sunlight. The crayon cup was full, like a rainbow bouquet.

“Last step,” Pippa whispered. “Celebrate!” She flapped twice and did a tiny dance. Sam clapped. They shared a high-five. Smack!

Sam took a deep breath. “It smells clean. It feels calm.”

Pippa nodded. “We used strategy. We were open-minded. We learned your family song. We tried new ideas. We finished our chores.”

Sam hugged Pippa gently. “Thank you, Pippa. When a job feels big, I can make a plan.”

Pippa smiled. “Yes. Look, sort, put away, celebrate. Try, test, and sing if you like. Strategy helps our hearts feel steady.”

They placed the shiny flower button on top of the bowl. Sam whispered, “Thank you, Grandma.” Pippa added, “And thank you, study corner, for being our bright place.”

Together they sat in the warm sun patch, sipping cool water and sharing a crunchy apple. The apple was sweet and juicy. The clock said tick-tock, soft as a lullaby. Their smiles felt light as feathers.

The study corner was ready for new drawings, new books, and new songs. Pippa fluffed her feathers and chirped, “With a good plan, we can do big things, one small step at a time.”

The End.
