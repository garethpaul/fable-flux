# Polly's Safety Helper Day

Polly was a bright green parrot with beautiful red feathers on her head. She lived in a cozy home office with her human friend, Miss Sarah. The office had a big wooden desk, colorful books on tall shelves, and a sunny window where Polly loved to perch.

"Good morning, Polly!" said Miss Sarah as she sat down at her computer. "I have important work to do today. I'm making safety posters for our neighborhood."

Polly fluffed her feathers excitedly. "Safety posters! Safety posters!" she squawked. Polly loved helping with special projects.

Miss Sarah smiled. "Would you like to help me, Polly? But remember, we must follow the safety rules in our office too."

"Yes! Yes! Polly helps!" chirped the creative parrot, bobbing her colorful head up and down.

Miss Sarah pointed to the electrical cords near her desk. "First rule, Polly. Never touch the black cords with your beak or claws. They have electricity that could hurt you."

Polly nodded seriously. "No touch cords. Polly understands!"

"Second rule," continued Miss Sarah, "stay away from the paper shredder when it's running. It makes loud noises and has sharp parts."

"Loud machine scary," agreed Polly, remembering the buzzing sound it made.

Miss Sarah began working on a bright yellow poster. She drew pictures of children wearing helmets while riding bikes. "This poster will help kids in our neighborhood stay safe," she explained.

Polly watched carefully, then had a wonderful idea. "Polly make poster too!"

"What a great idea!" said Miss Sarah. "You can help our community by making your own safety poster."

Miss Sarah gave Polly some colorful, non-toxic crayons and a big piece of white paper. Polly held a red crayon carefully in her claw and began to draw. She made squiggly lines and colorful dots.

"What are you drawing, Polly?" asked Miss Sarah.

"Stop sign!" announced Polly proudly. "Red means stop! Look both ways!"

Miss Sarah clapped her hands. "That's wonderful! Your poster will help remind everyone to stop and look before crossing the street."

As they worked together, Polly followed all the safety rules. When her crayon rolled toward the electrical cords, she remembered Miss Sarah's words and flew to get it instead of walking near the dangerous wires.

"Good job following directions, Polly!" praised Miss Sarah. "You're being very safe."

The afternoon sun streamed through the window, making Polly's green feathers shimmer like emeralds. She felt proud and happy helping her community.

When they finished, Miss Sarah held up both posters. "Look what we made together! Your colorful drawing is different from mine, but both posters will help keep people safe."

"Different is good!" squawked Polly. "Polly's poster special!"

"Yes," agreed Miss Sarah, giving Polly a gentle head scratch. "Everyone has different talents and ways to help others. Your creative ideas make our community safer and more beautiful."

That evening, Miss Sarah hung both posters in the neighborhood community center. Many families stopped to look at the bright, cheerful safety reminders.

"Thank you for being such a good helper today, Polly," said Miss Sarah as they returned home. "You followed all the safety rules and helped make our neighborhood safer."

Polly preened her feathers proudly. She had learned that following safety rules kept everyone protected, and that even a small parrot could make a big difference in helping her community.

The End.
