# Ellie’s Bubbly Bath Plan

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and a tickly trunk. One sunny morning, she tromp-tromp-tromped to the bathroom. The floor felt cool on her feet. The room smelled like sweet soap and fresh towels. Drip… drip… went the faucet. Plink! A drop landed on Ellie’s trunk and made her giggle.

Mama Elephant called, “Bath time, Ellie!”

Ellie looked at the big bathtub. It seemed like a small lake to her. “It’s so big,” she whispered. “How do I do this right?”

She wanted to wash well. She wanted to be clean. But the soap was slippery, and the water was splashy. Ellie took a deep breath. “I can learn,” she said softly. “I can try.”

Mama set a bright blue stool by the sink. “We will have a plan,” Mama said. “A plan is a smart way to do a big job.”

Ellie’s ears perked up. “A plan?” she asked.

“A plan is steps,” said Mama. “Step by step, you will do it.”

Ellie smiled. “I like steps!”

Mama clapped gently. “We will use our bath plan. We will also use kind words for our brains. That is called a growth mindset. We say, ‘I can learn. I can try. I can grow.’”

Ellie wiggled her toes. “I can learn. I can try. I can grow,” she echoed.

Step 1: Ellie tested the water. Swish, swish. Warm and cozy! “It feels like a hug,” she said.

Step 2: Ellie climbed in slowly. Splash, splash! Water hugged her legs. “I’m okay,” she told herself. “I’m learning.”

Step 3: Time for soap. The soap smelled like oranges. Mmm! But the bar slipped—whoosh!—right out of her trunk. Plop! It dropped into the tub.

Ellie’s eyes grew wide. “Oh no! I can’t do it,” she cried.

Mama knelt by the tub and touched Ellie’s shoulder. “You can’t do it yet,” Mama said kindly. “Let’s try a new strategy.”

“Strat…egy?” Ellie tried the big word.

“It means a smart way to try again,” Mama said. “Would you like a washcloth? It will help you hold the soap.”

Ellie nodded. “Yes, please.”

Step 4: Ellie wrapped the soap in a soft washcloth. Squish, squish—little bubbles puffed up. “Bubbles!” she giggled. The bubbles tickled her trunk. Pop, pop, pop!

Ellie rubbed her arms in small circles. “Round and round,” she sang. “I can learn. I can try. I can grow.”

She did her legs. She did her tummy. She did behind her ears. “Don’t forget your trunk,” Mama reminded.

Ellie reached for her trunk. The washcloth slipped a little. “Oops!” she said.

“What can we do?” asked Mama.

Ellie thought. She tapped her chin with her trunk. “New strategy!” she said. “I can make gentle squeezes.” She held the washcloth with two little trunk curls. Squeeze, wipe, squeeze, wipe. It worked!

Step 5: Rinse time. Ellie tipped a small cup. Whoosh! Warm water ran down her back. It felt like rain on leaves. She shivered and laughed. “It tickles!”

She tried to rinse her trunk, but the cup wobbled. Water splashed her eyes. “Eep!” Ellie squeezed them shut.

“It’s okay,” Mama soothed. “Blink, blink. Try slow. You can also tilt your head back. That is another strategy.”

Ellie took a deep breath. “I can try slow.” She tilted her head, poured gently, and blinked. Drip, drop, drip. “I did it!”

Step 6: Tooth time! Ellie climbed onto the blue stool. The mirror showed her bright eyes and a bubble on her head. She giggled. Mama handed her a tiny brush with minty paste.

“Small circles,” Mama said.

Ellie brushed. The paste tasted cool and fresh. “Minty!” she said. She brushed the tops. She brushed the bottoms. She missed a back tooth and frowned. “I can’t reach.”

“Not yet,” Mama said. “What is our plan?”

Ellie looked at the mirror. “Open big like a lion,” she decided. “Roar!” She opened wide. Now she could reach. Swish, swish, swish. “I can learn. I can try. I can grow.”

Step 7: Dry and done! Mama wrapped Ellie in a fluffy towel. The towel was warm like sunshine. Pat, pat, pat went the towel. Ellie’s skin felt clean and soft.

Mama smiled. “You used many strategies. You kept trying.”

Ellie stood tall on her little feet. She felt proud and calm. “At first, the bath was hard,” she said. “But I made a plan. I used new ways. I didn’t give up.”

Mama kissed her forehead. “That is a growth mindset. Your brain grows when you try.”

Ellie nodded. “My brain can grow. My body can be clean. I can do big things step by step.”

She picked up the washcloth and the tiny brush. She put them back in their places. “Part of my plan is to finish and tidy,” she said.

Mama winked. “Smart strategy.”

Ellie flapped her ears and did a happy little stomp. Tromp, tromp, tromp! The bathroom smelled like oranges and mint. The mirror showed a shiny, clean elephant calf with a bright smile.

“I did it!” Ellie said. “Tomorrow, I can try again and learn even more.”

“Yes,” said Mama. “Every day, we learn.”

Ellie hugged Mama with her trunk. “I can learn. I can try. I can grow,” she whispered.

The End.
