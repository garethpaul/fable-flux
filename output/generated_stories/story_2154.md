# Oliver Owl's Rooftop Sharing Adventure

High up on a tall red rooftop, Oliver the wise old owl stretched his soft brown wings in the warm morning sunshine. "Ahh," he hooted happily, "what a beautiful day to take care of myself!"

Oliver loved his morning routine. First, he did his wing exercises - flap, flap, flap! Then he preened his feathers until they were smooth and shiny. Finally, he enjoyed his favorite breakfast of juicy berries from his special bowl.

Just as Oliver was about to eat his berries, he heard tiny chirping sounds. "Peep! Peep! Peep!" Three little baby birds had landed on his rooftop, looking very hungry and tired.

"Oh my," hooted Oliver gently. "You little ones look like you need some help. Are you hungry?"

The smallest baby bird nodded sadly. "We've been flying all morning looking for food, but we can't find any berries anywhere!"

Oliver looked at his bowl full of plump, purple berries. His tummy rumbled loudly. He was quite hungry too! But seeing the tired little birds made his heart feel warm and caring.

"Hmm," thought Oliver out loud. "I have some berries, but there might not be enough for all of us. What should we do?"

The middle baby bird chirped up, "Maybe we could share? Our mama always says sharing makes everyone happy!"

"That's a wonderful idea!" hooted Oliver. "But first, let's all do some stretches together. Moving our bodies helps us think better!"

Oliver showed the baby birds how to stretch their tiny wings up high, then down low. They marched in place, lifting their little feet up and down. "This feels good!" peeped the biggest baby bird.

After their exercises, Oliver counted his berries carefully. "Let me see... one, two, three, four, five, six berries. And there are four of us. How can we share fairly?"

The baby birds huddled together, whispering and thinking hard.

"I know!" chirped the smallest one. "You could have two berries because you're bigger, and we could each have one berry!"

"But wait," said the middle bird. "Oliver found these berries and this is his home. Maybe he should have three?"

Oliver's wise eyes twinkled. "You're both thinking so nicely about fairness! Here's what I think - let's each have one berry first, and then we'll share the last two berries by breaking them into pieces!"

The baby birds clapped their tiny wings with joy. "That sounds perfect!"

They all sat in a circle on the sunny rooftop. Oliver gave each baby bird a whole berry, then carefully broke the remaining berries into small pieces so everyone could have more.

"Mmm!" they all said together, tasting the sweet, juicy berries. "These are delicious!"

As they ate, Oliver taught the baby birds about taking care of themselves. "Remember to drink water, get plenty of sleep, and exercise every day," he hooted wisely.

When they finished eating, the baby birds felt strong and happy. "Thank you for sharing with us, Oliver!" they chirped. "You're the kindest owl ever!"

"Sharing made me feel happy too," hooted Oliver. "When we share what we have, everyone feels good inside!"

The baby birds gave Oliver gentle wing-hugs before flying away to find their family. Oliver smiled as he watched them go, feeling proud that he had helped others and taken care of himself too.

"What a wonderful way to start the day," he hooted contentedly, settling down for a cozy nap in the warm sunshine.

The End.
