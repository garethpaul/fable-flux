# Ollie's Rooftop Routine

High up on the tallest building in the city, there lived a playful otter named Ollie. He had made his cozy home in a big water tank on the rooftop, where he could splash and play all day long.

Every morning, Ollie would wake up when the sun painted the sky orange and pink. "Good morning, sunshine!" he would chirp, stretching his furry paws. But there was one big problem - Ollie never did things the same way twice.

One day he would eat breakfast first, then brush his whiskers. The next day he would swim first, then maybe forget to eat at all! Sometimes he would collect shiny bottle caps before cleaning his den, and other times he would take a nap right in the middle of organizing his treasures.

"Oh dear," sighed Mrs. Pigeon, who lived in a nest nearby. "Ollie, you seem so scattered and worried all the time."

It was true. Ollie often felt mixed up and forgot important things. He would lose his favorite shell, forget to drink enough water, or miss the beautiful sunset because he was rushing around.

One breezy morning, Ollie met Rosie, a wise old raccoon who visited the rooftop to collect recyclable cans and bottles. Her gray fur sparkled in the sunlight, and she moved with calm, steady steps.

"Hello there, little otter," Rosie said kindly. "You look a bit frazzled. What's troubling you?"

"I feel so messy and forgetful," Ollie explained, his brown eyes drooping. "I'm different from other animals. They all seem to know what to do and when to do it."

Rosie smiled warmly. "Being different is wonderful, Ollie! But let me share a secret that helps me every day - having a routine."

"What's a routine?" asked Ollie, tilting his head curiously.

"A routine is doing the same helpful things in the same order each day," Rosie explained. "It's like a gentle rhythm that makes life easier and happier."

Rosie showed Ollie her daily routine. First, she stretched and said good morning to the sky. Then she ate a healthy breakfast of berries and nuts. Next, she collected recyclables to help keep the earth clean. After that, she groomed her fur and organized her den. Finally, she watched the sunset and told herself a story before bed.

"The beautiful thing about routines," Rosie said, "is that everyone can make their own special one!"

Ollie's eyes sparkled with excitement. "Can I create my own otter routine?"

"Of course!" Rosie encouraged.

Together, they designed Ollie's special routine. Every morning, he would greet the sun, eat fish and kelp, swim twenty laps, organize his shell collection, help Rosie collect recyclables for the earth, practice his storytelling, and end each day watching the stars.

"This feels so good!" Ollie exclaimed after following his routine for a week. "I remember everything now, and I feel calm and happy!"

Mrs. Pigeon noticed the change too. "Ollie, you seem so peaceful and joyful now!"

From that day forward, Ollie loved his routine. It helped him feel organized and confident. He still kept his playful, unique personality, but now he had a steady rhythm that made each day special.

"Routines help us shine our brightest," Ollie would tell other young animals who visited the rooftop. "They're like a gentle hug for our busy days!"

The End.
