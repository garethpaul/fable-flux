# Gigi the Brave and Gentle Giraffe

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with long, soft lashes and warm brown spots. Today she stood in the creative art room. The room smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Sunlight made tiny sparkles on jars of paint. Brushes whispered in water cups, swish-swish. Colored paper felt smooth under Gigi’s hoof.

“Good morning, art room!” Gigi said in her gentle voice.

“Good morning!” sang the brushes and crayons in Gigi’s imagination. Gigi giggled. She loved to imagine.

Ms. Dot, the art teacher, clapped her hands. “Today we make food collages,” she said. “Cut and glue pictures of yummy, healthy foods.”

Gigi’s friend, Max the monkey, bounced. “Bananas! Bananas!” he chattered.

Luna the lion cub wiggled her tail. “I like carrots,” she purred.

Gigi touched a shiny picture of broccoli. “I like trees—broccoli looks like tiny trees,” she said. She felt calm and happy.

The friends began to cut and glue. Snip, snip went the scissors. Squish, squish went the glue. Gigi placed apples, carrots, broccoli, and a bowl of noodles on her paper. She added a cup of water. “These foods help me grow tall and strong,” Gigi whispered. “They give me energy to paint and play.”

Max reached across the table. “I need that apple picture,” he said. He pulled Gigi’s apple off her paper without asking.

Gigi’s tummy did a tiny flip. She felt a squeeze in her chest. Her spots felt hot. She looked down at her paper. There was a sticky apple-shaped space.

Luna frowned. “Max, you didn’t ask,” she said softly.

Max shrugged. “I was fast!”

Gigi took a slow breath in and out, like Ms. Dot had taught. In… out. She remembered: Be brave and gentle. Use your words. Say what you need.

Gigi looked at Max. She kept her voice calm and clear. “Max,” she said, “I feel upset when you take my picture without asking. Please give it back.”

Max blinked. He stopped bouncing. The room felt quiet, except for the drip of a paintbrush. “Oh,” he said. “I didn’t know you felt upset.”

Gigi nodded. “I worked hard. I need the apple for my healthy plate.”

Ms. Dot came closer. She smiled. “Thank you, Gigi, for using strong and kind words. That is called being assertive.”

Max’s ears drooped. He looked at the apple in his hand, sticky with glue. “I’m sorry, Gigi,” he said. “I didn’t listen. I took it too fast.” He held out the apple picture. “Here. Can I find another apple for me?”

Gigi’s chest felt soft again. “Thank you for the apology,” she said. “Yes, let’s look together.”

They flipped gently through pages. Rustle, rustle. They found another red apple and a green pear. Max smiled. “I can add a pear too. Pears are sweet!”

Luna tapped her collage. “I have carrots and hummus. Crunchy!”

Gigi added a little bowl of blueberries. “Blueberries are tiny and juicy,” she said. “They make my tongue purple.” She giggled.

Ms. Dot held up a big poster of a rainbow plate. “Fruits and veggies come in many colors,” she said. “Your bodies like different colors. Water helps too.”

Gigi raised her hoof. “I have water on my collage,” she said proudly. “Water helps me think and paint.”

Max lifted his picture of a banana and milk. “These help me swing higher,” he said. “But I will ask before I take things now.”

A few minutes later, Luna reached toward Gigi’s broccoli tree picture. Her paw hovered. “Gigi, may I please use this broccoli picture?” she asked.

Gigi smiled. “Thank you for asking. I still need that one. But here is another broccoli picture you can use.” She slid one over.

Luna purred. “Thanks, Gigi. You used your words, and I used mine too.”

The room was full of happy sounds again. Snip, glue, swish, and soft chatters. The air smelled like orange peels from snack time and fresh paper.

When the collages were done, Ms. Dot clapped. “Time to share!”

Gigi held up her art. Apples, broccoli, carrots, noodles, blueberries, and water made a bright rainbow. “Today I was brave and gentle,” she said. “I said how I felt. I asked for what I needed. That helped me and my friends.”

Max held up his collage and looked at Gigi. “I’m sorry I took your apple. Next time I will listen first and ask.”

Gigi nodded. “I forgive you.”

Luna added, “We can help each other choose healthy foods, and help each other feel safe.”

Ms. Dot smiled wide. “What wonderful artists and friends you are. Being assertive means using strong, kind words. Listening means giving space and ears. Empathy means caring how others feel. Together, they make our room warm and friendly.”

Gigi felt tall inside, not just on the outside. The sunlight on the paint jars seemed to shimmer brighter. The brushes whispered, swish-swish, as if they were clapping too.

Gigi said, “My voice matters. Your voice matters. We can be brave and gentle.”

Everyone nodded. The creative art room felt cozy, colorful, and kind.

The End.
