# Shelly's Big Truth

In a cozy, dusty attic filled with old boxes and forgotten treasures, lived a very shy turtle named Shelly. The attic was quiet and peaceful, just the way Shelly liked it. Sunbeams danced through the small window, making golden spots on the wooden floor.

Shelly had made a special home behind a big, soft cushion. She loved her quiet space where she could read books and draw pictures with her crayons. The attic felt safe and warm.

One sunny morning, Shelly heard footsteps on the stairs. *Thump, thump, thump.* Her heart beat fast like a little drum. She quickly pulled her head into her shell.

"Hello?" called a gentle voice. "Is someone up here?"

It was Mrs. Robin, the kind lady who owned the house. Shelly peeked out just a tiny bit. Mrs. Robin was looking around the attic with curious eyes.

"I keep hearing little sounds up here," Mrs. Robin said softly. "Like someone moving around. But I can't find anyone."

Shelly's tummy felt funny. She wanted to hide, but she also felt bad. Mrs. Robin seemed worried, and Shelly knew she was the one making the sounds.

For three whole days, Mrs. Robin came to the attic. Each time, she looked more confused. "Maybe it's mice," she said to herself. "I might need to call someone to help."

Shelly felt worse and worse. She didn't want Mrs. Robin to worry, but she was too scared to come out. What if Mrs. Robin didn't want a turtle in her attic?

On the fourth day, Shelly heard Mrs. Robin talking on the phone. "Yes, I think there might be animals in my attic. Can you come check tomorrow?"

Shelly's eyes filled with tears. She knew that telling the truth was important, just like her mama had taught her. But being honest felt so scary!

That night, Shelly couldn't sleep. She looked at her cozy corner and thought about how kind Mrs. Robin's voice sounded. Maybe, just maybe, Mrs. Robin would understand.

The next morning, Shelly took three deep breaths. *In... out... in... out... in... out.* She slowly walked to the middle of the attic floor.

When Mrs. Robin came upstairs, Shelly was waiting there, shaking like a leaf.

"Oh my!" Mrs. Robin gasped, but her voice was gentle. "A little turtle! So you're the one I've been hearing."

Shelly nodded, her voice very quiet. "I'm sorry I hid from you. I was scared, but I know hiding wasn't honest. I live behind the cushion."

Mrs. Robin smiled the warmest smile. "Thank you for telling me the truth, little one. That was very brave. What's your name?"

"Shelly," she whispered.

"Well, Shelly, I'm not upset at all. I was just worried about you! This attic gets cold in winter. Would you like to live in my sunny garden instead? You could have your own special space."

Shelly's eyes grew wide with joy. "Really? You're not mad?"

"Not at all! Honest little turtles are always welcome here," Mrs. Robin said with a laugh.

From that day on, Shelly lived happily in the beautiful garden. She learned that being honest, even when it's scary, always leads to good things. And whenever she felt shy, she remembered that taking her time and being truthful was always the right choice.

The End.
