# Rhythm Rocco Gets Ready

In a bright creative art room, paints sparkled like tiny rainbows, scissors made snip-snip sounds, and paper crinkled softly. A small drum sat by the window. Next to it danced Rhythm Rocco, a musical raccoon with a striped tail and a little jingling vest. His whiskers twitched with every beat.

“Today is Art and Music Day!” Rocco sang, tapping his drum. “Boom-boom, tappy-tap!” He spun in a happy circle. The room smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Sunlight warmed the long tables.

Mina the mouse peeked from a paint cup. “Are we ready?” she squeaked. “The children will come soon.” Her tiny nose wiggled.

Rocco smiled. “I’m ready! I’m ready!” He banged his drum louder. Paintbrushes rattled in their jar. A glue stick rolled off the table. Thunk.

Mina shook her head. “We need to prepare. We must do our chores. Then we can play and make art.”

“Chores?” Rocco groaned. He looked at the splashy table. There were crayons without boxes, paper scraps on the floor, and lids off the paint pots. His tail swished. “But I want to play music now.”

Finn the frog hopped onto a stool. “Ribbit. If we get ready first, we won’t lose things,” he said. “Last time I sat in paint. It was sticky.”

Rocco giggled. “Sticky frog!” He tapped the drum again. “Boom-boom—”

Mina held up a tiny paw. “Listen, Rocco. Preparation helps us. We feel calm. We find our tools. We share. We include everyone.”

Rocco stopped. His ears perked up. He did want everyone to feel welcome. He wanted all friends to play and make art together. “Okay,” he said slowly. “What are the chores?”

Mina pointed. “Let’s make a plan. Step one: put crayons in the crayon box. Step two: put lids on paints. Step three: wipe the tables. Step four: set out clean paper and aprons. Step five: make a music basket for sharing.”

“A music basket?” Rocco lit up. “I like that chore!”

Finn smiled. “We can sing while we work. Ribbit.”

Rocco took a deep breath. He made a soft beat. “Tap… tap… tap.” He hummed a tidy tune. “Put-away, hooray. Put-away, hooray.”

Mina giggled and began to sort crayons by color. “Red with red, blue with blue,” she sang. “Snick, snick, snick.” The crayons clicked as they lined up.

Finn put lids on paint pots. “Twist, twist, twist,” he said. “No spills today.” The lids made a gentle pop as they closed. Pop! Pop! Pop!

Rocco wiped the tables with a damp cloth. Swish, swish. The cloth smelled like lemons. He watched the smudges disappear. “Oh! It feels nice to see the tables shine,” he said. His chest felt warm and proud.

They set out clean paper in neat stacks. They laid small aprons across chairs. They added the drum, shakers, and bells to a little basket with a friendly sign: “Please share.”

Rocco stroked the sign. “This helps everyone know they can join,” he said. “No one will be left out.”

Just then, two birds fluttered in, Peep and Pip, with soft gray feathers. They looked shy. “We heard music,” Peep whispered. “Can we play too?”

Rocco nodded. “Yes! We prepared a music basket for sharing.” He handed them tiny bell bracelets. The bells chimed like stars. Ting-a-ling!

Tara the turtle slowly pushed open the door. “I like to paint, but I move slow,” she said. “Will there be a seat for me?”

Rocco guided her to a chair with an apron. “We saved a place. We got ready for friends of all speeds.”

The room was quiet and tidy. It smelled sweet and clean. Everything had a spot. Rocco’s heart felt steady. “I think I like preparation,” he said. “It makes my music even better.”

Then a small problem popped up. Finn looked around. “Ribbit… Where is the glue?” He checked a shelf. He checked a bin. No glue.

Peep and Pip looked worried. “We wanted to make a glitter star.”

Rocco felt a flutter in his tummy. “Oh no,” he said. “We prepared, but we missed one thing.”

Mina took a breath. “Peaceful problem-solving,” she whispered. “Let’s ask and look together.”

Rocco nodded. “Team search!” he sang softly. “Look high, look low. Ask before you go.”

He asked Tara, “Have you seen the glue?”

Tara pointed with a gentle smile. “Check the tidy tray,” she said. “We have labels now.”

They read the labels. Brushes. Paper. Tape. Glue! Rocco lifted the glue stick. “Here it is!” he cheered. The cap clicked. Click!

Everyone laughed. “Preparation helped us find it,” Finn said, clapping softly.

Rocco tapped his drum. “One, two, three—music and art for you and me!” The friends made art together. Peep and Pip shook bells. Tara painted a slow, careful rainbow. Finn glued glitter stars. Mina drew tiny flowers.

Rocco played a gentle beat so everyone could join. Boom… shhh… ting-a-ling. He watched all his friends smile. No one was left out. No one felt rushed. The room stayed clean and calm.

When they finished, Rocco called, “Chore time again!” He handed out small jobs. “We clean as we go.” They wiped, sorted, and put things back. Swish, click, snick. The art room stayed bright and ready.

Rocco’s tail curled with joy. “Now I know,” he said, “preparation is like a song at the start. It helps the whole day sound sweet.”

Mina nodded. “We prepare, we include, we care.”

Finn added, “And we solve problems peacefully.”

Rocco tapped a final happy beat. “Boom-boom, tappy-tap! Ready friends make ready fun.”

The End.
