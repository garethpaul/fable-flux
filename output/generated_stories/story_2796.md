# Oliver Owl's Clean Discovery

High up in the quiet attic of an old farmhouse lived Oliver Owl, the wisest bird in the whole neighborhood. His feathers were usually soft and fluffy, and his big golden eyes sparkled like sunshine. But lately, something was very wrong.

"Achoo! Achoo!" Oliver sneezed loudly, his voice echoing through the dusty attic. His beautiful feathers looked messy and dull. His beak felt stuffy, and his eyes were watery. Worst of all, he had started to smell funny - not like the fresh pine scent he usually had.

Down below, Oliver could hear the little mice whispering. "What's that smell?" squeaked one mouse. "It's coming from the attic," said another. "Maybe we should stay away."

Oliver felt very sad. His friends didn't want to visit him anymore. Even Mrs. Robin, who used to bring him the latest forest news, hadn't stopped by in days.

"What's wrong with me?" Oliver hooted sadly to himself. He looked around his cozy attic home and noticed something he had never seen before. Dust covered everything! His favorite perch was grimy, his water bowl was green and slimy, and there were crumbs everywhere from his midnight snacks.

"Oh my!" Oliver gasped. "When did everything get so dirty?"

Just then, he heard tiny footsteps. Little Pip the mouse peeked around a dusty box, holding his nose with his tiny paws.

"Oliver?" Pip squeaked carefully. "Are you okay? You look... different."

Oliver's face turned red under his feathers. "I'm sorry, Pip. I know I smell bad. I don't know what happened."

Pip looked around the messy attic with his bright black eyes. "Oliver, when did you last clean yourself? Or wash your water bowl? Or tidy up your home?"

Oliver thought hard, his head tilting to one side. "I... I can't remember. I've been so busy reading my books and watching the stars that I forgot!"

"That's the problem!" Pip said kindly. "My mama always says that staying clean keeps us healthy and happy. Would you like me to help you?"

Oliver's eyes lit up with hope. "Oh yes, please!"

First, Pip showed Oliver how to clean his water bowl with fresh rainwater. "Clean water tastes so much better," Pip explained. "And it helps wash away germs that make us sick."

Next, they worked together to sweep away all the dust and crumbs. Oliver used his strong wings while Pip gathered the tiny pieces.

"Now for the most important part," Pip said. "You need to clean your feathers!"

Oliver learned how to preen each feather carefully, removing dirt and old oil. He splashed in his fresh, clean water bowl, feeling the cool water wash away all the grime.

"This feels wonderful!" Oliver hooted with joy. "I can breathe better already!"

When they finished, Oliver's feathers were fluffy and bright again. His attic sparkled in the moonlight, and he smelled fresh and clean.

"Thank you, Pip," Oliver said gratefully. "I'm sorry I let myself get so messy. Will you teach me how to stay clean every day?"

"Of course!" Pip smiled. "Clean feathers, fresh water, and a tidy home every day!"

Soon, all of Oliver's friends returned to visit. Mrs. Robin brought news, the squirrels shared acorns, and everyone enjoyed the sweet, clean smell of Oliver's attic home.

From that day on, Oliver made sure to clean himself and his home every single day. He learned that taking care of himself made him feel better and kept his friends happy too.

The End.
