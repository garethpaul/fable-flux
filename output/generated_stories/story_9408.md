# Mimi Mouse’s Colorful Plan

Mimi was a kind little mouse who lived in a playful living room. The room had soft pillows like marshmallows, a bright blue rug that tickled her toes, and a big window that let sunshine pour in like warm honey. When the afternoon light came, the dust sparkles danced, and the air smelled like clean soap and orange peels.

One sunny day, Mimi clapped her tiny paws. “Today, I will make art!” she squeaked. “I have a goal. I want to make a picture that makes hearts feel happy.”

She set out her things on the blue rug. She had chunky crayons, soft paintbrushes, and scrap paper that crinkled like crunchy leaves. Mimi took a deep breath. She sniffed the waxy crayon smell. She wiggled her whiskers. “I can do it,” she whispered. “I will try, step by step.”

Her friend, Bop the bouncy ball, rolled over and boinged softly. “What are you making, Mimi?”

“I’m making a big, bright picture,” said Mimi. “I want to use red like strawberries, yellow like sunshine, and blue like the sky.”

Bop gave a happy bounce. “Goal set! Boing! How will you start?”

Mimi tapped her chin. “First, I will plan. Then, I will draw. Last, I will add color.” She smiled. “That is my plan.”

She drew a small circle. “This is the sun.” She drew a big shape. “This is a house.” She drew tiny shapes. “These are friends holding hands.”

Crunch, crinkle went the paper as she slid it to the side. She picked a big sheet. “This will be my special art.”

Just then, Tippy the timid toy turtle peeked out from under a pillow. “I am shy,” said Tippy softly. “I’m not good at art.”

Mimi’s kind eyes sparkled. “Everyone can make art. Art is your feelings on paper. Let’s try together. We will do it one step at a time.”

Tippy took a tiny step. “Okay,” he whispered.

Mimi held up a yellow crayon. “Listen.” She pressed it on the paper. Scritch, scritch. The sound was like a little song. “Do you hear that? The crayon is singing.”

Tippy giggled. “I hear it!”

Mimi dipped a brush in blue paint. Plip, plop. “The paint is raindrops,” she said.

Bop did a soft boing. “What about me?”

“You can cheer us on,” said Mimi. “Say, ‘You can do it!’”

Bop puffed up proudly. “You can do it! Boing!”

Mimi made a bright yellow circle. “Sun,” she said. “Goal one: draw the sun. Done!”

She made a small check mark on a sticky note. “Check!” The sticky note smelled like sweet paper and glue.

“Goal two,” said Mimi. “Draw a house.” She drew a square and a triangle roof. “Check!”

“Goal three: draw friends holding hands.” She drew a mouse, a turtle, and a round ball with a smile. She laughed. “Check!”

Tippy’s eyes were wide. “You did it, Mimi!”

Mimi nodded. “Now it’s your turn. What would you like to draw?”

Tippy thought. He looked at the window and the dancing dust sparkles. “I want to draw… brave Tippy, standing in the sun.”

Mimi clapped. “That is a wonderful idea. That is creative. That is you.”

Tippy held a crayon. His hand shook a little. “I am scared.”

Mimi took a slow breath and showed him. “In and out,” she said. “You are safe. You can try.”

Tippy breathed with her. In and out. He put yellow on the paper. Scritch, scritch. He drew a little turtle with a big smile. He added a tiny heart on the shell. He looked at Mimi. “I made a heart because my heart can be brave.”

Bop bounced softly. “You can do it! You did it!”

Tippy stood taller. “I did it,” he whispered. “I made art.”

Mimi smiled so wide her whiskers wiggled. “You did! Your art shows your feelings. That is creative expression.”

Mimi added red strawberries in the corner. She dabbed green leaves with plip, plop sounds. The room felt cozy, warm, and happy, like a hug. She hummed a little tune. Tippy hummed too. Bop kept a gentle beat with tiny boings.

When their pictures were done, Mimi taped them on the wall with careful paws. The tape made a soft rip sound, rrrip, and stuck with a tiny pop. They stepped back together.

The wall was colorful. The sun glowed. The house looked friendly. The friends held hands. Tippy’s brave turtle smiled in the warm yellow light. The whole room seemed to sparkle brighter.

“How do you feel?” Mimi asked.

“I feel proud,” said Tippy. “I tried. I set a little goal, and I did it.”

Mimi nodded. “That is self-esteem. That is your brave heart saying, ‘I can try.’”

Bop bounced once, slowly. “I feel happy. The room looks like a rainbow.”

Mimi hugged Tippy gently. “Today we learned: make a plan, try your best, and show your feelings with colors and shapes. Your art is your voice. When you create, you grow.”

Tippy smiled. “Tomorrow, I will make a picture of rain. Plip, plop!”

Mimi giggled. “And I will make a picture of giggles. Hee-hee!”

They turned on a small lamp. Click. The pictures glowed in the soft light. The orange-peel smell floated by again, and the blue rug tickled their toes. Mimi whispered, “We did it, step by step. We shared our hearts.”

Bop gave one last happy boing. “Goal met!”

Mimi nodded. “Yes. And new goals can start tomorrow.”

The End.
