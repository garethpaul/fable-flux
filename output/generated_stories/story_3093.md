# Penny Parrot's Colorful Kindness

In a bright, sunny art room filled with paintbrushes, crayons, and colorful paper, lived a beautiful parrot named Penny. Her feathers were as green as fresh grass, with splashes of red and yellow that sparkled in the morning light.

Penny loved to paint! She would dip her brush in the paint pots and create wonderful pictures on her easel. The art room smelled like fresh paint and had big windows that let in warm sunshine.

One day, three new friends came to visit the art room. There was Benny the brown bear, who walked slowly and carefully. Luna the white rabbit, who hopped quickly from place to place. And Sam the orange fox, who moved his hands in special ways to talk.

"Hello!" squawked Penny excitedly. "Welcome to my art room! Let's paint together!"

But as they started painting, Penny noticed something. Benny painted very slowly with big, careful strokes. Luna's paws made her paintings look different from Penny's. And Sam couldn't hear Penny's instructions, so he painted something completely different.

Penny felt frustrated. Her bright feathers ruffled up. "This isn't how I paint!" she squawked. "You're all doing it wrong!"

The three friends looked sad. Benny's ears drooped. Luna stopped hopping. Sam put down his brush.

Penny saw their sad faces and felt a warm feeling in her heart. She remembered what her mama had taught her: "Being different makes the world more beautiful, just like how different colors make the prettiest paintings."

"Wait!" called Penny. "I'm sorry I was upset. Can you show me how you like to paint?"

Benny smiled and showed Penny his slow, careful painting of a big, strong tree. "I like to take my time," he said in his deep, gentle voice.

Luna giggled and showed her quick, bouncy painting of flowers dancing in the wind. "I like to paint fast and fun!" she chirped.

Sam pointed to his beautiful painting of the sunset, painted in smooth, flowing strokes. He used his hands to show Penny that he painted with his heart.

"Wow!" said Penny, her eyes growing wide. "Your paintings are all so beautiful and special! They're different from mine, but that makes them wonderful!"

Penny had an idea. "Let's make a painting together! We can each paint one part."

So they did! Benny painted a strong tree trunk. Luna added bouncing flowers. Sam painted a gorgeous sky. And Penny painted colorful birds flying through it all.

When they stepped back to look, their painting was the most beautiful thing any of them had ever seen!

"Look what we made when we worked together!" squawked Penny happily. "It's so much prettier than anything I could make alone!"

The four friends hugged (very carefully, so they didn't get paint on each other). The art room filled with laughter and joy.

From that day on, Penny learned that being kind meant celebrating how different and special each friend was. When she felt frustrated, she would take a deep breath and remember that differences make the world more colorful and beautiful.

And whenever new friends came to the art room, Penny would say, "Welcome! Show me your special way of making art. Together, we can create something amazing!"

The End.
