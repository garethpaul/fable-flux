# Joey's Workshop Words

Joey the kangaroo was a bouncy little fellow who loved hopping around Grandpa Koala's garage workshop. The workshop smelled like wood shavings and had shiny tools hanging on the walls. Joey's favorite sound was the gentle "tap-tap-tap" of Grandpa's hammer.

One sunny morning, Joey bounced into the workshop. "Good morning, Grandpa!" he chirped happily.

"Good morning, little Joey," said Grandpa Koala, adjusting his tiny glasses. "I'm trying to fix Mrs. Rabbit's broken chair today, but I have a big problem."

Joey's ears perked up. "What's wrong, Grandpa?"

Grandpa pointed to a piece of paper covered in squiggly marks. "Mrs. Rabbit wrote me instructions in her language, but I can't understand what they say. Without knowing what she wants, I can't fix her chair properly."

Joey looked at the mysterious marks and felt puzzled. "Those look like wiggly worms!" he giggled.

"That's because they're written in a different language," Grandpa explained gently. "Just like how you speak Kangaroo and I speak Koala, Mrs. Rabbit speaks Rabbit. All languages are special and important."

Joey scratched his head with his tiny paw. "But how can we help Mrs. Rabbit if we can't read her words?"

"That's where good listening and thinking come in," said Grandpa. "Would you like to volunteer to help me solve this puzzle?"

"Yes, yes!" Joey bounced excitedly. "I love puzzles!"

Grandpa smiled. "First, let's stay calm and think carefully. What do we know about Mrs. Rabbit?"

Joey sat still for a moment, thinking hard. "She has a big family! And she loves reading bedtime stories to her little bunnies."

"Excellent listening and remembering!" praised Grandpa. "Now, what kind of chair would be perfect for reading stories?"

Joey's eyes sparkled. "A rocking chair! One that's soft and cozy!"

Just then, they heard a gentle knock. Mrs. Rabbit peeked into the workshop, her whiskers twitching nervously.

"Hello, Mrs. Rabbit!" Joey called out warmly. "We want to help you, but we can't read your special Rabbit language."

Mrs. Rabbit's eyes lit up. "Oh my! I forgot you speak different languages. Let me try to show you instead of just writing."

She hopped over to her broken chair and pointed to the wobbly leg. "Loose," she said clearly. Then she pretended to rock back and forth. "Smooth rocking, please."

"Ah!" said Grandpa. "Now I understand perfectly!"

Joey clapped his paws together. "Mrs. Rabbit, your language sounds beautiful! Could you teach me some Rabbit words while Grandpa fixes your chair?"

Mrs. Rabbit's face beamed with joy. "I would love to! In Rabbit, we say 'thump-thump' for thank you."

"Thump-thump!" Joey repeated, giggling as he tried the new sounds.

As Grandpa worked on the chair, Mrs. Rabbit taught Joey how to say "hello," "friend," and "happy" in Rabbit language. Joey listened carefully to each new word, his ears wiggling with excitement.

When the chair was fixed, Mrs. Rabbit tested it with a gentle rock. "Perfect! Thump-thump, both of you!"

"Thump-thump!" Joey replied proudly, using his new Rabbit words.

Grandpa patted Joey's head. "You solved our problem beautifully, little one. By staying calm, listening carefully, and appreciating Mrs. Rabbit's language, we all worked together."

Joey hugged them both. "Every language is like a special song! I want to learn more words from all our friends."

From that day on, Joey became the workshop's official language helper, always eager to listen and learn new words from every visitor.

The End.
