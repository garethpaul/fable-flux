# Benny Beaver’s Rooftop Rainbow

Benny Beaver was a very busy beaver. His paws were quick, his tail was flat, and his ideas were big. Today, he climbed up, up, up to the rooftop garden. The sun felt warm on his fur. A soft breeze tickled his whiskers. He could smell mint and tomatoes, and hear bees buzzing like tiny motors. Bzzzz.

“I want to make art,” Benny said. “Big, bright art!” His eyes sparkled. “I will make a rooftop rainbow.”

Benny opened his little backpack. Inside were chalks, paper, cloth scraps, string, and a roll of tape. He set them out in a neat row. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple. He tapped his tail—thump, thump—thinking hard.

“I can draw a rainbow on the floor,” he said. He picked up red chalk. Scritch, scritch! He drew a big curve. Then orange. Scritch, scritch! Then yellow, shiny as the sun. Soon, his rainbow was growing, but he frowned a little.

“Hm. It looks nice,” Benny said, “but I want it to move. I want it to dance in the wind.”

Benny took a deep breath. In, out. He put his paw on his chest. Pat, pat. “I can think. I can try. I can make a plan.” This helped him feel calm.

Just then, a soft voice called, “Hello?” Mia Mouse popped up from behind a pot of basil. Her whiskers twitched. She wore a tiny blue scarf.

“Hi, Mia!” Benny smiled. “I’m making a rooftop rainbow. Do you want to help?”

Mia’s eyes shone. “Yes! I love colors.”

They looked around. The rooftop had a short fence, a shed, and a clothesline that went from one side to the other. The line wiggled in the breeze. Wiggle, wiggle.

“The line!” Benny said. “We can hang colors on the line. They will flap and dance.”

Mia clapped her tiny paws. “We can make flags!”

They cut colored cloth into simple flag shapes. Snip, snip. They taped them to the string. Tap, tap, press. The wind puffed. Whoosh! The flags fluttered and whispered—flaff, flaff.

“It’s a rainbow parade!” Mia cheered.

Benny grinned, but then he looked at the chalk rainbow. Some chalk dust blew into his nose. Ah-choo! The chalk smudged. The red line smeared into the orange. Benny’s smile faded. “Oh no. My drawing is messy now.”

Mia leaned close. “It’s okay. Smudges can be pretty. See? The colors blend like a sunset.”

Benny blinked. He knelt down and gently rubbed the colors with his paw. Rub, rub. The lines turned soft and swirly. “You’re right. It’s like the sky,” he said. He felt warm and proud inside. He had changed his plan, and it worked.

A tiny voice squeaked from a flower pot. “Can I help?” It was Pip, a little pigeon chick with fluffy gray feathers. His head tilted left and right. “I can peep and flap!”

Benny nodded. “Yes, Pip! We need sound for our art show.”

Pip puffed out his chest. “Peep-peep, coo-coo!” He cooed a gentle tune. The sound floated across the rooftop like a soft blanket.

“Let’s add smells,” Mia said, sniffing the basil. “Art can smell yummy.”

Benny and Mia tied mint and lavender sprigs to the clothesline, between the colored flags. Sniff, sniff. The wind carried cool mint and sweet lavender. The flags swished through the scent. Swish, swish.

Benny looked at the shed wall. It was plain and gray. “That wall needs art too,” he said. He taped paper to the wall and set out crayons. “We can draw together. Everyone draw what the rainbow feels like.”

Mia drew a big circle with tiny dots. “It feels like bubbles,” she said. Pip drew wavy lines. “It feels like flying.” Benny drew a smiling sun wearing a hat. “It feels like happy.”

They worked and worked. Their paws and feathers got colorful. Red smudge on Benny’s cheek. Yellow on Mia’s scarf. A blue dot on Pip’s beak. They giggled. Hee-hee!

Suddenly, the wind blew stronger. WHOOSH! The flags flapped hard. One purple flag popped free and flew up, up like a bird.

“My purple!” Benny gasped. His tail thumped fast. Thump-thump-thump.

Benny closed his eyes and took a slow breath. In, out. He put his paw on his chest again. “I can stay calm. I can fix it.” He looked around and saw extra tape.

“Team plan,” he said. “Mia, can you hold the line? Pip, can you watch the flag?”

“I’ve got the line,” Mia said, gripping with tiny paws.

“I see the purple! It’s on the tomato stake,” Pip peeped.

Benny waddled over and gently lifted the flag. “Got you,” he said softly. He added more tape. Press, press. The flag held tight. The wind pushed, but the flag stayed. Flap, flap.

When they finished, they all stepped back. The rooftop rainbow was alive. Colors danced. Chalk swirls glowed. Music cooed. Mint and lavender floated on the air. The sun warmed their backs. It was beautiful.

Benny’s heart felt big. “We did it,” he said. “We made art together.”

A few neighbors peeked over from their own rooftops. “Ooooh! Ahhh!” they said. One waved. Another clapped.

Pip puffed with pride. “We’re a team!”

Mia smiled. “We used our ideas. We stayed calm. We helped each other.”

Benny nodded. “Art can be many things. It can move. It can smell. It can sing. It can be made by friends.”

He looked at his friends and the bright flags. “I learned that my ideas grow when I share. I learned I can breathe and try again. And I learned that making art with friends feels so good.”

They sat together, tails and paws and little wings touching. The rainbow fluttered over them like a soft hug.

The End.
