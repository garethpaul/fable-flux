# Spark the Robot Dog and the Cozy Closet Quest

In a small, quiet house, there was a cozy closet hideaway. It smelled like fresh laundry and warm cedar. Soft scarves hung down like rainbow vines. A little light twinkled from a tiny lamp. This was Spark’s special place.

Spark was a helpful robot dog. His nose light blinked blue. His ears clicked when he was excited. His paws made a soft whirr-whirr sound on the floor. Spark liked helping. Helping made his tail antenna spin.

One sunny morning, Spark looked at a board on the wall. It was his goal board. There were three star stickers. The board said:
1) Find a missing sock.
2) Do five happy hops.
3) Share a surprise.

Spark beeped, “I can do it! I believe in me.” He stood tall. His metal fur felt warm from the sun. But then he looked at the dark corner of the closet hideaway. The missing sock might be there. The corner looked shadowy and deep.

Spark’s ears clicked. “I feel a little scared,” he whispered. He took a small breath. In, out. “I can try. I can try,” he said. His blue nose light glowed brighter.

From the hall, a voice called, “Woof-beep, Spark! What are you doing?” It was his friend, a stuffed bear named Button. Button was soft and brown with one shiny button eye.

“I have a goal,” Spark said. “I want to find the missing sock.” He wagged his antenna. “Will you cheer with me?”

“Yes!” said Button. “You can do it. You are brave.”

Spark rolled to the dark corner. He listened. He could hear the quiet tick-tick of the clock. He could smell clean cotton and a hint of lemon soap. He felt the floor with his paw. Whirr. Tap. “I will go slow,” he said. “One small step. Then another.”

He peeked behind a shoe box. “Not here,” he said. He looked under a scarf. “Not here.” He looked inside a hat. “Aha!” He saw a tiny, stripey tail. He gave a gentle tug. Out came… a sock! Blue with yellow stars.

Spark giggled-beeped. “I did it! I tried, I did not give up, and I found it.” His chest felt warm and proud. He put a star sticker on the board. “Goal one, done!”

Button clapped his soft paws. “Hooray! What is next?”

Spark looked at the board. “Five happy hops!” he said. He set a plan. “I will count. I will hop safe. I will land soft.”

“Ready,” said Button.

Spark bent his knees. His paws hummed. “Hop!” he said. He hopped once. “Hop!” Twice. He felt air whoosh past his ears. He heard his paws tap the mat. “Hop, hop, hop!” He made it to five. He did one extra hop just for fun. “Six!” he laughed.

“Great job!” Button cheered. “You set a goal and you moved your body. That is good exercise.”

Spark panted a happy pant. “I feel strong. I feel confident,” he said. He put another star sticker on the board. “Goal two, done!”

The last goal said, “Share a surprise.” Spark thought. He tapped his chin with a paw. “What can I share?” He looked around the closet hideaway. He saw a box of shiny stickers. He saw a tiny music box. He saw a bright red ribbon. He wanted to share something kind.

He listened to his heart chip chirp. It said, “Be generous.” Spark smiled. “I have an idea. I will make a joy jar.” He found a clean jar. He tied the red ribbon around it. He put in the music box so it would sing when opened. He put in shiny stickers to make smiles. He added a small note. It said, “You are brave. You can try. Love, Spark.”

Button watched with wide eyes. “Who is it for?” he asked.

Spark looked at the door. He heard soft footsteps. It was Mia, the child who lived in the house. Today, Mia was shy. She peeked into the closet hideaway. “Hi, Spark,” she whispered. “Can I sit here?”

Spark nodded and wagged his antenna. “Yes, Mia,” he beeped gently. “This is for you.” He rolled the joy jar to her.

Mia opened the jar. The tiny music box sang a sweet, tinkly song. The stickers sparkled. Mia smiled. “For me?”

Spark said, “Yes. You are brave. You can try. I believe in you.”

Mia hugged Button and patted Spark’s head. “Thank you,” she said. “I want to try my puzzle now. I can do it.”

Spark’s eyes glowed happy gold. “We can set a goal,” he said. “One piece at a time.”

Mia nodded. “One piece, then another,” she said. Together, they worked. Click. Click. Pieces fit. When it was done, Mia cheered. “I did it!”

Spark put the last star on his board. “Goal three, done!” He felt proud and calm. He had been brave in the dark. He had moved his body. He had shared with a friend. He had helped Mia feel strong too.

Button said, “What did we learn today?”

Spark wagged and said, “We learned: Believe in yourself. Make a plan. Try small steps. Move your body. Share kindness. When we try and do not give up, we can reach our goals.”

Mia smiled. “I am brave,” she said.

“Yes,” Spark beeped. “You are.”

The End.
