# Ollie Otter’s Calm Cozy Bedtime

Ollie the playful otter wiggled on his soft, squishy bed. His cozy bedroom smelled like clean blankets and warm cocoa. Moonlight made silver shapes on the walls. A tiny fan hummed, “whirr, whirr,” like a sleepy bee.

“I want to jump!” Ollie giggled, bouncing a little on his pillows.

Mama Otter peeked in with a smile. “Hello, wiggle worm. It’s almost sleep time.”

“But my tummy feels busy,” said Ollie. He patted his belly. “It feels like popcorn. Pop! Pop! Pop!”

Mama sat on the edge of the bed and stroked his smooth fur. “When our tummies feel pop-pop, we can practice calmness.”

“Calmness?” Ollie asked, eyes wide.

“Calmness means we help our body be peaceful. Let’s try our bedtime plan. It helps us be ready, steady, and sleepy.”

Ollie nodded. He liked plans.

Step one was the “Quiet Basket.” Ollie reached for his basket beside the bed. It had a tiny book, a soft star pillow, and a sparkly bottle with floating glitter. The bottle felt cool in his paws.

“Shake, shake,” said Ollie.

“Now watch,” Mama whispered.

The glitter swirled fast, like a snowstorm. Ollie’s eyes followed the sparkles. The room felt softer. The fan hummed. The moonlight glowed. The glitter began to sink slowly, like sleepy fish.

“My tummy feels slower,” Ollie said.

“Good,” said Mama. “That is mindfulness. We watch and breathe.”

Ollie took a breath in. He smelled cocoa and clean soap. He breathed out. “Fffff.”

Step two was “Star Breaths.” Mama gave Ollie the soft star pillow. “Trace a point and breathe in,” she said, guiding his paw. “Trace the next side and breathe out.”

“In… out… in… out…” Ollie whispered. His shoulders dropped. His paws felt warm and cozy under the blanket.

Then there was a tiny tap at the door.

“Hi,” said Nia the mouse, peeking in. She wore stripy pajamas. “Can I join? My room felt too noisy.”

Ollie sat up, smiling. “Yes! We are doing calmness.”

Mama Otter nodded. “Everyone is welcome. We can all be calm together.”

Nia climbed onto a beanbag beside the bed. “My whiskers feel tingly,” she whispered.

“Let’s do Star Breaths together,” Ollie said. He showed Nia how to trace the star. “In… out…”

Nia’s whiskers stopped twitching so much. “I like this,” she said softly.

“Now step three,” said Mama. “The Gentle Checklist. Being ready helps our minds feel safe.”

Ollie looked at the picture chart by his bed. It had simple drawings. He touched each one with a paw.

“Toothbrush picture—done!” he said. “Pajamas—done! Water cup—here.” He took a small sip. “Favorite stuffie—Mr. Pebble.” He hugged his pebble-shaped plush. It felt cool and smooth.

Nia checked her tiny chart in her pajama pocket. “I have my blanket. I have my night light. I did my stretches.”

“Stretches?” Ollie asked.

Nia stood and reached up. “Grrrrrow tall like a tree… sigh.” She smiled. “It helps me feel brave.”

Ollie copied. He stretched up, then curled small, like a pebble in a shell. The blanket brushed his fur. It felt soft and safe.

Just then, a sudden thump sounded outside the window. Ollie’s eyes went round. Nia’s ears perked up.

“What was that?” Ollie whispered.

“It might be the wind bumping a branch,” Mama said. “Let’s check in with our bodies.”

Ollie put a paw on his chest. His heart felt fast, like little feet.

“Remember the plan,” Mama said. “We can be calm, even when we hear a new sound.”

Ollie nodded. “Glitter watch,” he said.

“Star Breaths,” Nia added.

Together, they watched the glitter sink again. In… out… in… out… The thump did not come back. The room went quiet. The fan hummed its sleepy song. The moonlight felt gentle, like a hand on your back.

“How do you feel now?” Mama asked.

“My heart is slower,” said Ollie. “My tummy is not popcorn. It is oatmeal. Warm and slow.”

Nia giggled. “My whiskers are quiet.”

Mama gave them both a proud smile. “That is resilience. You had a problem. You used your plan. You stayed with your feelings. You found calm.”

Ollie yawned. “I like calm.”

“Calm helps us sleep,” Mama said. “Calm helps us think. Calm helps us be kind.”

Ollie tucked Mr. Pebble under his chin. He looked at Nia. “You can stay for the bedtime story.”

“Thank you,” Nia said. “I feel included. That makes my heart cozy.”

Mama opened the tiny book from the Quiet Basket. Her voice was soft like the moon.

“Once upon a time,” she began, “a little cloud learned to float slow…”

Ollie listened. He smelled cocoa and clean blankets. He heard the fan and Mama’s gentle voice. He felt safe, ready, and loved.

Before the last page, Ollie whispered, “Mama?”

“Yes, sweet Otter?”

“When my tummy pops, I can breathe, check my list, and watch the glitter. I can be calm.”

“Yes,” Mama said, kissing his head. “You can always find your calm.”

Ollie smiled. Nia smiled. The glitter bottle rested, quiet and still. The cozy bedroom held them like a hug.

And the night felt soft.

The End.
