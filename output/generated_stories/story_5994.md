# Panda Paws and the Sparkly Spoon

In a sunny dining room, a patient panda named Poppy sat at a round table. The table smelled like warm rice and sweet peaches. The light was soft and golden. Poppy’s tummy gave a happy rumble. “Mmm,” Poppy said, patting her round belly. “Snack time!”

On the table sat a small blue bowl, a shiny spoon, and a cup of cool water. Poppy reached for the spoon. It looked a little smudgy. A tiny sticky spot glowed in the light.

Poppy blinked. “Oh,” she said softly. “My spoon is not clean.”

Her ears wiggled. Her cheeks felt warm. Poppy wanted her snack. She felt a little squeeze in her chest. That squeeze was frustration.

Poppy took a slow breath in. She let a slow breath out. “I can be calm,” she said. “I can be patient.”

Mama Panda peeked in from the kitchen. “Hello, Poppy,” Mama said with a smile. “I hear a hungry tummy.”

“I want my peaches,” Poppy said. “But my spoon is sticky.”

Mama nodded. “Good noticing, Poppy. Clean tools help healthy tummies. What can we do?”

Poppy thought. She tapped her furry chin. “We can wash it,” she said.

“Yes,” said Mama. “Let’s use warm water, gentle soap, and careful paws.”

They walked to the sink. The water hummed and turned warm. Steam floated up like tiny clouds. Poppy held the spoon. “It feels icky,” she said, wrinkling her nose.

“That’s okay,” said Mama. “We can make it sparkly.” Mama put a drop of soap on a soft sponge. It smelled like lemons. “Rub, rub,” Mama sang.

Poppy smiled. “Rub, rub,” she sang too. She rubbed the spoon. Bubbles popped and tickled her paws. “Pop, pop!” Poppy giggled. “The bubbles sound fun.”

“Rinse, rinse,” Mama said, turning the water. The water whooshed and washed the bubbles away. The spoon shone like a little moon.

Poppy held it up. “It’s sparkly!” she cheered.

Back at the table, Poppy sat straight and proud. “Clean spoon. Clean paws.” She looked at her paws. “Oh! I should wash my paws too.”

Mama nodded again. “Great idea. Clean paws keep germs away.” They went back, and Poppy scrubbed her paws. “Tops and bottoms,” she sang. “Between my fingers.” The water felt warm. The soap smelled sunny. Poppy shook her paws gently. “Drop, drop,” she giggled as little drops fell.

They dried their paws on a soft towel. It felt fluffy like a cloud. “Now I am ready,” Poppy said.

Poppy picked up her spoon and took a small bite of peaches. “Mmm.” The peaches were sweet and cool. The rice was warm and soft. Her tummy felt happy and calm.

But then, Poppy saw a tiny crumb on the table. “Oh no,” she said, frowning. “Another mess. I want it to be perfect now.” The squeeze came back.

Poppy put her spoon down. She placed a paw on her chest. She took a slow breath in. She let a slow breath out. “I can be calm,” she whispered. “I can clean a little bit at a time.”

Mama smiled. “You are taking good care of yourself, Poppy. That’s wellness.”

Poppy got a small cloth. It was damp and cool. “Wipe, wipe,” she sang. “Little by little.” The crumb went away. The table felt smooth.

“Good job,” said Mama. “You are keeping things clean and shining.”

Poppy grinned. “Clean tools, clean paws, clean table,” she said. “My body feels good. My mind feels calm.”

“Sometimes we feel rushed,” Mama said. “Sometimes we feel grumpy. It helps to breathe, wait, and try again.”

Poppy nodded. “I can wait. I can try again.” She took another bite. “Delicious!”

A tiny drop of peach juice rolled on Poppy’s chin. It felt sticky. Poppy giggled and dabbed it with a napkin. “Wipe, wipe,” she sang softly. “Sticky be gone.”

When her bowl was empty, Poppy carried it to the sink. She turned the water on low. “Warm water, gentle soap, careful paws,” she said. She washed her bowl and spoon. The bubbles popped like little stars.

Mama clapped softly. “You did it. You kept your things clean. You kept your feelings calm. That keeps your body strong.”

Poppy stood tall and felt proud. “Clean helps me feel good,” she said. “Clean helps me stay well.”

Mama gave Poppy a cuddle. The hug felt cozy, like a warm blanket. The dining room was quiet and bright. Poppy’s tummy was full. Her heart felt peaceful.

Poppy whispered, “When I see a mess, I can breathe. I can clean. I can be patient.”

Mama whispered back, “Yes, little panda. You can.”

Poppy waved to her sparkly spoon. “Thank you, spoon,” she said. “Let’s be clean friends every day.”

The End.
