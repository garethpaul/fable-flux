# Fawn's Special Building Project

Little Fawn had soft brown fur with white spots that looked like tiny stars. She stood in Grandma Rabbit's cozy guest room, looking around with her big, gentle eyes. The room smelled like lavender and sunshine.

"Oh no!" Fawn said sadly. She had knocked over a tall tower of colorful blocks that Grandma Rabbit's grandchildren had built. The blocks were scattered all over the soft blue carpet like a rainbow explosion.

Fawn's ears drooped down. "I was just trying to look out the window," she whispered to herself. "Now their beautiful tower is all broken."

She heard little footsteps in the hallway. Three young rabbits hopped into the room - Benny, Bella, and Baby Bo.

"Our tower!" cried Benny, his whiskers twitching.

"It's all fallen down," said Bella, her nose wiggling sadly.

Baby Bo's bottom lip trembled. "We worked so hard on it."

Fawn felt terrible. Her heart felt heavy like a stone. "I'm so sorry, little friends," she said in her gentle voice. "I accidentally bumped into your tower. It was an accident, but I want to make it right."

The three rabbits looked at the kind fawn. Her eyes were shiny with tears.

"It's okay," said Benny softly. "Accidents happen."

"But how will we build it again?" asked Bella. "It was so tall and pretty."

Fawn's ears perked up. She had an idea! "What if we build it together? Four friends can build something even better than three!"

"Really?" asked Baby Bo, his little tail wiggling with excitement.

"Yes!" said Fawn. "But first, let's make a plan. What should our new tower look like?"

The friends sat in a circle on the soft carpet. Fawn felt the fuzzy rug under her hooves - it was warm and comfortable.

"I want it to be as tall as the window!" said Benny.

"With lots of colors!" added Bella.

"And maybe... maybe it could look like a castle?" whispered Baby Bo.

"Those are wonderful ideas," said Fawn. "Let's set a goal: we'll build the most beautiful block castle that reaches the window!"

They started sorting the blocks by colors. Fawn used her gentle hooves to carefully stack the red blocks. Benny arranged the blue ones. Bella organized the yellow blocks, and Baby Bo lined up all the green ones.

"Building together is more fun!" said Bella as they worked.

"And easier too," agreed Benny.

Slowly, their castle grew taller and taller. Fawn was very careful with her hooves, making sure each block was perfectly placed. When Baby Bo couldn't reach high enough, Fawn gently lifted him up.

"Thank you, Fawn!" giggled Baby Bo.

"We help each other," said Fawn with a warm smile. "That's what friends do."

Finally, their castle reached the window! It had towers and walls, with blocks of every color creating beautiful patterns. Sunlight streamed through the window, making their creation sparkle.

"We did it!" cheered all four friends together.

"It's even better than our first tower," said Bella.

"Because we built it with kindness and teamwork," said Fawn.

Grandma Rabbit peeked into the room. "What a magnificent castle! I can see you all worked together beautifully."

Fawn felt warm and happy inside. She had turned her mistake into something wonderful by being kind, setting a goal, and working with her new friends.

"Building together makes everything better," Fawn said, nuzzling her new rabbit friends gently.

The End.
