# Polly's Musical Morning

Polly the parrot loved bright colors and beautiful sounds. Every morning, she flew to the sunny music room in her house. The room had shiny instruments everywhere - a big black piano, silver bells, and drums that went "boom boom boom!"

"Good morning, music room!" Polly chirped happily. Her green feathers sparkled in the sunlight streaming through the windows.

But today felt different. Polly wanted to learn a new song, but it seemed so hard. She looked at the sheet music on the piano stand. The little black dots looked like tiny ants marching across the white paper.

"This looks too tricky," Polly sighed, ruffling her bright red tail feathers. "Maybe I should just play my old songs instead."

Polly's friend Oliver the owl peeked through the window. "What's wrong, Polly?" he hooted softly.

"I want to learn this new song, but it's so difficult!" Polly squawked. "The notes are too fast, and my wings feel clumsy on the piano keys."

Oliver smiled wisely. "Remember what you do every morning, Polly? You practice your scales, then your old songs, then try something new. That's your special routine!"

"Oh yes!" Polly's eyes lit up like tiny stars. "I almost forgot my morning routine!"

First, Polly warmed up her voice. "La la la, me me me, do re mi!" she sang sweetly. Her voice echoed around the room like a gentle bell.

Next, she practiced her scales on the piano. Her colorful claws tapped the white and black keys carefully. "Do, re, mi, fa, sol, la, ti, do!" Each note sounded clearer than before.

Then Polly played her favorite easy song - "Twinkle, Twinkle, Little Star." Her wings moved smoothly across the keys. The familiar melody filled the room with joy.

"Now I feel ready!" Polly chirped confidently. She looked at the new song again. This time, the notes didn't seem so scary.

Polly started slowly. One note at a time. "Plink... plink... plink." Some notes sounded wrong, but she kept trying.

"That's it!" Oliver encouraged from the window. "Take your time!"

Polly tried again. And again. Her feathers got a little messy from concentrating so hard, but she didn't give up. Each time, the song sounded a little better.

After many tries, something magical happened. The notes began to flow together like a gentle stream. The melody was beautiful!

"I did it! I did it!" Polly danced around the room, her colorful feathers swirling like a rainbow. "My routine helped me learn the new song!"

Oliver clapped his wings. "You were so persistent, Polly! You didn't give up, even when it was hard."

Polly felt proud and happy. Her heart felt as warm as sunshine. "My morning routine makes me feel confident," she realized. "First warm-up, then scales, then old songs, then new challenges. When I follow my routine, I can do anything!"

From that day on, Polly never forgot her special routine. Every morning, she followed the same steps, and every day she grew stronger and more confident. She learned that routines help us feel safe and ready for new adventures.

"Routine is like a good friend," Polly sang happily. "It helps me grow and learn each day!"

The End.
