# Ellie’s Rooftop Fair Share

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft, gray ears and big, careful feet. One sunny afternoon, Ellie climbed the wide, safe stairs to the rooftop garden. The warm wind tickled her trunk. She could smell mint and basil in the planters. She could hear birds chirping and the soft hum of city sounds below.

On the rooftop, a small box of colorful chalk sat by the wall. The wall was smooth and perfect for drawing. Ellie’s eyes sparkled. “I will make a rainbow!” she whispered. The chalk felt powdery in her trunk. Scritch, scratch, scritch. Red, orange, yellow. Ellie hummed a happy tune.

Just then, two friends popped up from the stairs—Mina the kitten and Pip the parrot. Mina’s whiskers trembled with excitement. Pip flapped his green wings. “Wow!” said Mina. “A rainbow!” “Pretty! Pretty!” squawked Pip.

“Would you like to draw too?” Ellie asked gently.

“Yes!” said Mina. “I want the blue chalk for a big blue moon!”

“Blue! Blue!” Pip echoed. “I need blue for the sky!”

Mina and Pip both grabbed for the same blue chalk. Their paws and claws touched at once. “Mine!” said Mina. “I was first!”

“No, mine!” squawked Pip. “I saw it first!”

Ellie felt her tummy wobble. She did not like when friends argued. Their voices got louder. Mina’s tail puffed up. Pip’s feathers ruffled. The happy rooftop felt tight and prickly.

Ellie took a slow, deep breath. She lifted her trunk and said, “Pause.” Her voice was calm and warm. She stepped back so everyone had space. “Let’s solve this,” Ellie said. “We can be fair. We can be kind.”

Mina blinked. Pip tilted his head. The wind brushed their faces. Somewhere far below, a car beeped softly.

Ellie said, “We have one blue chalk. You both want it. That is a problem. Let’s share and take turns.”

Mina tapped the roof with her paw. “But I want a big blue moon now.”

Pip squawked, “And I want a big blue sky now.”

Ellie nodded. “I hear you. You both want the same thing at the same time. That feels hard.”

Mina’s whiskers drooped. Pip tucked a wing. Ellie smiled kindly. “Let’s use our words. Tell me what you need.”

Mina said, “I need blue to make my moon round and bright.”

Pip said, “I need blue to make my sky wide and clear.”

Ellie looked at the box. There was green, red, yellow, orange, purple, white, and black. She thought and thought. The breeze smelled like basil again. The chalk dust tickled her trunk.

“I have an idea,” Ellie said. “Justice means being fair so everyone gets a chance. We can take turns with a timer. I will count to ten. First Mina uses blue for ten counts, then Pip uses blue for ten counts. While you wait, you can draw with other colors.”

Mina’s ears perked. “I can use white and yellow to make stars while I wait.”

Pip bobbed. “I can use green to draw trees under the sky.”

Ellie clapped her big, soft feet gently. “Good plan. Are we all okay with this?”

Mina nodded. “Okay.”

Pip nodded. “Okay!”

Ellie placed the blue chalk in Mina’s paw. “Your turn for ten.” Ellie counted in a sing-song voice, “One, two, three…” Mina drew a round moon. Scritch, scritch. The chalk made a soft whisper sound. Stars twinkled in white and yellow. Mina smiled. The moon looked cozy and calm.

“Nine, ten! Time to switch,” Ellie said. She handed the blue chalk to Pip. “Your turn for ten.”

Pip drew the sky with careful strokes. Scritch, scratch, sweep. The blue spread like soft water. He added green trees with fluffy tops. He smiled so wide his eyes shone.

Ellie clapped again. “You both used blue! That was fair.”

Mina looked at Pip’s sky. “It is big and beautiful.” She purred.

Pip looked at Mina’s moon. “It is round and bright.” He fluttered.

Ellie said, “Now let’s connect our pictures. The moon can glow in the sky above the trees. I can add a rainbow between!”

They worked together. Ellie drew the rainbow with red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple. Scritchy-scratch, swish-swish. The colors glowed in the sun. Mina added tiny stars. Pip added small birds like little letter V’s.

When they were done, they stepped back. The rooftop wall showed a peaceful night sky with a bold moon, tall trees, and a bright rainbow. The air smelled like chalk and mint. The city sounds felt far away and soft.

Mina rubbed her cheek on Ellie’s leg. “Thank you for helping,” she said.

Pip chirped, “Thank you for fairness!”

Ellie’s heart felt warm. “We used our words. We listened. We took turns. That is how we solve conflicts. That is justice.”

Mina nodded. “Sharing helps everyone.”

Pip added, “Understanding feels good.”

Ellie smiled. “When we are kind and fair, we all shine.”

They packed up the chalk together. Tap, tap, tap. The box clicked closed. The sunset painted the clouds pink and gold. The rooftop garden rustled softly.

“Good-bye, moon. Good-bye, sky. Good-bye, rainbow,” Ellie said. The friends headed to the stairs, happy and calm.

The lesson was clear: when we listen, share, and take turns, we solve problems fairly and keep our friends’ hearts bright.

The End.
