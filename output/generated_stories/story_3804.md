# Rocco Raccoon’s Quiet Song

Rocco the raccoon loved music. He wore a tiny red scarf and a shiny bell on his wrist. The bell made a soft ting when he moved. Rocco lived with Nana in a playful living room full of cozy pillows, bright blocks, and a basket of instruments.

Morning sunshine tickled the curtains. Dust sparkles danced in the warm light. The carpet felt fluffy under Rocco’s paws. He wiggled his whiskers. “Music time!” he cheered.

He shook a rainbow maraca. Chik-chik! Chik-chik! He tapped a little drum. Tap-tap! Tap-tap! He strummed a toy guitar. Twang-twang! Twang!

Nana smiled from her rocking chair. She knitted a blue scarf. “Good morning, Rocco,” she said. “Let’s start with a calm song.”

Rocco’s tail swished. “A calm song? But I want a loud song!” he said. He tapped faster. Tap-tap-tap-tap! The bell on his wrist rang, ting-ting-ting!

Just then, Rocco heard a tiny squeak. A small mouse peeked from a little house by the bookshelf. Her name was Mimi. She held a crumb of toast.

“Good morning,” Mimi squeaked. “Is it safe to eat my breakfast?”

Rocco hopped closer. “Yes! I’m making music!”

Mimi rubbed her ears. “My ears feel sleepy,” she said softly. “Could we have a calm song first?”

Rocco puffed his cheeks. “But loud songs are fun.” His paws felt wiggly. His tummy felt fizzy like soda bubbles. He wanted to play fast, fast, fast!

Nana’s rocking chair creaked, creak, creak. She hummed a slow tune. “Let’s take a calm breath,” she said. “In through your nose like smelling a cookie. Out through your mouth like blowing a feather.”

Rocco sniffed the air. He smelled warm cinnamon and clean soap. He breathed in slowly. He breathed out gently. The bubbles in his tummy got smaller. His paws felt soft and warm.

“Again,” Nana said kindly.

Rocco breathed in. He breathed out. His shoulders dropped. His tail swished slow-slow. “I feel better,” he whispered.

Mimi smiled. “Thank you for being calm,” she said. “You are being kind to my ears.”

Rocco looked at his instruments. He touched the drum with just one gentle paw. Tap… tap. The sound was soft like raindrops. He shook the maraca as light as a butterfly. chik… chik. He plucked the guitar string like a whisper. twiiing.

“Wow,” he said. “Calm music feels cozy.”

Nana nodded. “Calm helps us listen. Calm helps us care. Calm helps us be responsible.”

Rocco tilted his head. “What is responsible?”

“Responsible means we take care of people, pets, and things,” Nana said. “We use music that fits the moment.”

Mimi nibbled her crumb. “This moment is breakfast,” she said. “Calm is perfect.”

Rocco smiled. “I can do calm.” He sang a little song:

“I breathe in, I breathe out,
Soft and slow, no need to shout.
Tap-tap soft, and chik-chik light,
Calm songs make our hearts feel bright.”

Nana hummed along. The living room felt warm and peaceful. The clock ticked, tick-tock, tick-tock. The sunlight made stripes on the rug.

After breakfast, Rocco’s friend Pippa the puppy padded in. Her ears were floppy, and her nose was wet. “Play with me!” Pippa woofed. “Let’s jump! Let’s spin! Let’s drum fast!”

Rocco felt the soda bubbles try to come back. He looked at Mimi, who was washing her tiny dish. He looked at Pippa, whose tail thumped-thumped-thumped. He put a paw on his bell and took one calm breath.

“In this room,” Rocco said kindly, “Mimi needs quiet. We can be loud later in the yard. Right now, we play calm.”

Pippa blinked. “I like loud,” she said. “But I can try.” She sat and wagged slowly. “Teach me.”

Rocco’s eyes shone. “Let’s do a calm band!” He gave Pippa a soft shaker and Mimi a tiny triangle. Ting! Rocco took the drum and tapped like raindrops. Tap… tap… tap.

They listened to each other. If Pippa shook too hard, she paused and tried again. If Mimi’s triangle tinged too bright, she waited for the beat. They practiced being patient. They nodded and smiled. It felt good to work together.

Nana whispered, “What good listeners. That is tolerance. You are making space for each other.”

Rocco felt proud. He kept his voice gentle. “Curiosity time,” he said. “What sounds can we find that are calm?”

Pippa put her ear by the window. “I hear wind,” she said. “Hoooo.” She blew softly. Hoooo.

Mimi tapped the couch with one tiny paw. “I hear a soft thump,” she squeaked. Thup… thup.

Rocco brushed his tail on the rug. “I hear a whoosh,” he said. Whooooosh.

They made a calm song with wind, thumps, and whooshes. The room felt like a big, cozy hug.

When the song ended, Rocco helped put the instruments back in the basket. He lined them up carefully. “This is responsibility,” he said. “We clean up our things.”

Pippa licked his cheek. “And we help our friends,” she said.

Mimi clapped her tiny paws. “Thank you for being calm. My ears are happy.”

Rocco’s bell chimed, ting. “I learned something,” he said. “When I am calm, I can listen. When I listen, I can help. Calm music makes room for everyone.”

Nana kissed Rocco’s head. “That is a beautiful lesson.”

Rocco cuddled into the fluffy pillows. He took one more slow breath. He felt peaceful. He felt proud. He felt ready for a new song, at the right time.

The End.
