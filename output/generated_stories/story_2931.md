# Neli’s Balcony Garden Plan

Neli was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and a tiny, curly tail. She lived high up in a sunny home with a balcony garden. The garden smelled like mint and sweet tomatoes. Bees buzzed softly. Leaves tickled Neli’s trunk when the breeze came to visit.

One morning, Neli stepped onto the balcony. Her feet went pat-pat on the warm floor. “Good morning, plants,” she sang. “It is water time!”

Neli looked at the watering can. It was big, and the water inside sloshed and splashed. She tried to lift it with her small trunk. “Oof!” The can wobbled. A little spill went splish on her toes.

Neli felt a tiny frown. “This is hard,” she said. “But I can think. I can make a plan.”

She sat on her soft mat. She closed her eyes. She breathed in minty air. She breathed out slow. Her ears relaxed. “I will use a strategy,” Neli whispered. “A smart way to do this.”

First, she looked around carefully. Sunlight made bright patches on the floor. The pots were lined up: tomatoes, basil, and a brave little lemon tree. Near the door, a small shelf held cups, a funnel, and a tiny spray bottle.

Neli smiled. “Step one: small sips,” she said. “I don’t need to carry all the water at once.”

She picked up a little yellow cup. It felt light in her trunk. She dipped it into the watering can. Swish. Then she poured slowly. Drip, drip, drip. The basil leaves shivered with happy drops.

“Good job, me,” Neli said. “Step two: make a path.”

She moved the pots a little closer, one by one. Scrape, shuffle. She made a neat line from the watering can to the farthest pot. “Now I can go cup by cup,” she planned. “I will not run. I will not spill.”

Just then, a tiny sparrow landed on the railing. “Chirp-chirp! May I have a sip too?” the bird asked, tipping his head.

Neli nodded. “We can share,” she said kindly. “But first I will water the thirsty plants. They cannot fly to find water.”

The sparrow hopped and watched. “What’s your plan?” he asked.

Neli lifted her cup again. “I am using a strategy,” she explained. “Small sips, slow steps, and a path. Also, I will use the spray bottle for the soft leaves so they do not get hurt.”

The sparrow chirped. “Smart!”

Neli walked slow. Pat… pat… pat. Small cup, steady trunk. The lemon tree got a gentle sprinkle. The soil made a cozy, earthy smell. The tomato vines sighed, like “ahhh,” when cool water kissed their roots.

Halfway through, the yellow cup slipped. Plop! Water splashed her legs. Neli blinked. Her heart did a quick thump. She wanted to stomp in the puddle. She wanted to say “This is too hard!”

She paused. She breathed in. She breathed out. “I will try again,” she told herself. “I can be strong and calm.”

The sparrow peeped softly. “You can do it.”

“Thank you,” Neli said. “Step three: fix problems.”

She got the small towel from the shelf and patted the puddle. Pat, pat, pat. She set the cup down on the mat so it would not slip. Then she tried with the tiny funnel. She held the funnel over the pot and poured through it. The water went right where it needed to go. No splash!

Neli’s eyes shone. “The funnel helps me aim,” she said. “That is part of my plan now.”

Soon, every pot looked happy and dark with water. The garden smelled fresh and cool. Bees buzzed like tiny songs. Neli smiled and filled the birdbath saucer for her new friend.

“Chirp-chirp!” The sparrow sipped and fluffed his feathers. “Thank you for sharing.”

“You are welcome,” Neli said. “Sharing is part of caring for our garden friends.”

Just then, a neighbor kitten peeked over the balcony divider. “Meow… may I see your plants?” she asked shyly.

“Yes,” Neli said. “But please step gently. The leaves are tender.”

The kitten nodded and tiptoed. “How did you water all by yourself?” she asked.

Neli lifted the spray bottle and the funnel. “I made a plan,” she said. “Small sips, slow steps, a neat path, fix problems, and share tools.”

The kitten’s eyes grew wide. “Can I help next time?”

“Yes,” Neli said. “We can take turns. You can spray the basil. I can pour for the tomatoes. We can talk if something is hard. We can be patient.”

The sun slid a little lower. The lemon tree swayed. The air felt cool on Neli’s cheeks. She put the tools back on the shelf. She tapped the watering can gently with her trunk. “Thank you, water,” she whispered.

The sparrow fluttered up. “Today you used strategy, you stayed calm, and you shared.”

Neli nodded. “When a job is big, I make a plan. When I spill, I try again. When friends come, we use kind words.”

Her heart felt light. The garden looked proud.

The End.
