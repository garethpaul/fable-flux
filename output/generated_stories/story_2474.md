# Miko the Brave Little Painter

Miko was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived in a creative art room where the air smelled like fresh paper and sweet glue. Paintbrushes stood like tiny trees in shiny cups. Colorful papers fluttered like butterflies. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor.

One morning, the art teacher placed a big sign on the wall. “Art Show Today!” it said in bright blue letters. Children clapped. Crayons rolled. The room buzzed like a happy bee.

Miko peeked from behind a paint jar. “An art show!” he whispered. His whiskers tickled with excitement. “I want to share my painting.” He held a small canvas he had made at night. It showed his mouse family dancing under moonlight. He used gentle blues, bright yellows, and tiny silver dots for stars. It was a special picture from an old mouse tradition—every spring, Miko’s family painted a moon dance to share their stories.

But then Miko’s tummy felt wiggly. “What if no one likes it?” he wondered. “What if I make a mess?” His ears drooped. He tucked the canvas behind him.

Just then, his friend Pippa the paper crane fluttered down. “Hi, Miko!” she chirped. “What are you hiding?”

Miko squeaked, “It’s my moon dance painting. It’s from my family’s tradition. But I am scared. I am only a little mouse.”

Pippa tilted her paper head. “Sharing makes understanding,” she said kindly. “And the art show needs art! Your picture can help others learn. That is responsibility.”

Miko took a tiny breath. He could smell lemony soap and chalk dust. He could hear children laughing and water cups clinking. “I want to be brave,” he said softly. “But how?”

Pippa fluttered her wings. “Courage is doing the good thing even when your tummy feels wiggly. Start with one small step.”

Miko nodded. One small step. He carried his canvas to a low table. His paws padded softly on the wood. He set the painting down. His heart went thump-thump, like a small drum.

A child with red shoes knelt to look. “Ooo,” the child said. “Who painted the moon dance?”

Miko’s whiskers trembled. He stood tall—well, as tall as a little mouse could. “I did,” he said. “It is my family’s spring tradition. We paint our stories to share. It helps us remember and understand.”

The child smiled. “I like the tiny stars,” they said. “They sparkle!”

Miko felt a warm glow inside. “Thank you,” he whispered.

Soon, the teacher came by. “What a thoughtful painting,” she said. “Would you like to hang it up for the show?”

Miko gulped. “I will try.”

Pippa cheered. “Small step!”

Miko looked up at the wall. It seemed very high. He thought about giving up. But then he thought about his family and their moon dance. He thought about being responsible—keeping his promise to himself to share. He thought about courage. He took another breath. “I can do it,” he said.

The teacher gently lifted Miko in her soft hands. Miko could see the whole room now: bright splashes of red, blue, and green; glitter like tiny snow; the shiny floor where light danced. He pressed the little hook onto the wall. Click! The painting hung straight.

“Ta-da!” Pippa sang.

People gathered. “What is it?” asked a boy with a paint-splotched apron.

Miko spoke clearly. “This is a moon dance. In my family, we dance under the moon each spring. We paint the story and share it. It is our tradition. Sharing helps others understand who we are.”

The boy nodded. “I have a tradition too,” he said. “We make paper lanterns with my grandma.” He smiled. “Maybe we can make lanterns here!”

The teacher clapped softly. “Traditions teach us and bring us together. Thank you, Miko, for sharing with courage.”

Then, a little spill happened. A cup of blue water tipped over. Splash! It puddled by the wall near Miko’s painting.

Miko blinked. His tummy did a small flip. He could run away. But he remembered responsibility. “I can help,” he said. He grabbed a tiny cloth and patted the floor. “Tap, tap, tap.” The spill dried. The painting was safe.

“Good teamwork,” said the teacher. “That was brave and responsible.”

As the art show went on, children painted stars and moons, lanterns and dances. They asked Miko questions. Miko listened and answered. He felt calm. He felt proud. He felt understood.

At the end, the teacher handed Miko a little ribbon that said, “Courage to Share.” It was soft and yellow like warm light.

Miko smiled. “I was scared,” he said to Pippa. “But I took small steps. I shared my art. I helped clean the spill. I told my story.”

Pippa flapped happily. “That is courage,” she said. “And your art helped us all understand.”

Miko looked at his painting on the wall. The moon seemed to glow. He could almost hear his family’s tiny feet dancing: tip-tap, tip-tap. He hoped other children would share their stories too.

Miko placed his paw over his heart. “When my tummy feels wiggly,” he said, “I will take one small brave step.”

The End.
