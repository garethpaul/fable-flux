# Nutkin's Big Mistake

High up in the tallest oak tree lived a clever little squirrel named Nutkin. His cozy treehouse had round windows, a rope ladder, and shelves full of acorns. Nutkin loved his treehouse, but he loved playing even more.

One sunny morning, Nutkin's friend Rabbit called up from below. "Nutkin! Can you help me gather berries for the forest picnic? Everyone is counting on us!"

"Sure!" Nutkin called back. "Just let me finish organizing my acorns first!"

But when Nutkin saw his collection of shiny marbles on the shelf, he forgot all about the berries. "Just one quick game," he said to himself. He rolled the marbles across his wooden floor, watching them sparkle in the sunlight.

*Click, clack, roll!* The marbles made such pretty sounds.

Hours passed. Nutkin played and played, completely forgetting his promise to Rabbit.

As the sun began to set, Nutkin heard sad voices below. He peeked out his window and saw all his forest friends sitting around empty picnic baskets.

"Where are the berries?" asked Bear, his tummy rumbling.

"I don't know," said Rabbit quietly. "Nutkin was supposed to help me, but he never came."

Nutkin's fluffy tail drooped. His heart felt heavy like a stone. He had broken his promise! His friends were hungry because of him.

"Oh no," Nutkin whispered. "I made a big mistake."

He scurried down his rope ladder as fast as his little paws could carry him. His friends looked up with surprised faces.

"I'm so sorry!" Nutkin said, his voice shaky. "I promised to help gather berries, but I got distracted playing with my marbles. I should have listened and followed through like I said I would."

Rabbit's ears drooped. "We're all hungry, Nutkin. The berry bushes are far away, and it's getting dark."

Nutkin's whiskers twitched as he thought hard. Then his eyes lit up! "Wait here! I have an idea!"

He raced back up to his treehouse and gathered every single acorn from his shelves. His arms were so full, acorns tumbled down like brown raindrops.

"Here!" Nutkin panted, spreading the acorns on the picnic blanket. "I know these aren't sweet berries, but they're crunchy and filling. Please share my acorn collection!"

Bear's eyes grew wide. "Nutkin, these are your special acorns that you've been saving all season!"

"I want to make things right," Nutkin said firmly. "You're my friends, and I let you down. This is how I can show I'm truly sorry."

Owl hooted softly. "That's very thoughtful, Nutkin."

As everyone munched on the tasty acorns, Nutkin felt the heavy feeling in his chest grow lighter. 

"Thank you for sharing your acorns," said Rabbit, giving Nutkin a warm hug. "And thank you for saying sorry. That took courage."

"From now on," Nutkin promised, "when I say I'll help, I'll follow through. No more getting distracted!"

The forest friends smiled and patted Nutkin's back. They spent the evening sharing stories and crunching acorns under the twinkling stars.

Nutkin learned that when you make a mistake, the best thing to do is say sorry and try to make it better. True friends will always forgive you when you show you care.

The End.
