# Rooftop Rainbow Picnic

Sunshine the gentle deer fawn tiptoed up the stairs, clip-clop, clip-clop. Her soft hooves made a tiny tap on each step. At the top, she reached the rooftop garden. The air felt warm. A breeze tickled her ears. She smelled fresh mint and sweet flowers. She could hear city birds calling, “Cheep-cheep!” and pots clinking in a neighbor’s kitchen.

Sunshine blinked her big brown eyes. “Today is a special day,” she whispered. “A rooftop rainbow picnic! Friends will come and share foods from their homes.” Her little heart made a happy thump.

Sunshine wanted to be ready. Preparation is a big word, but it means getting things set. “I can prepare,” she said. She spread a bright blanket. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple—just like a rainbow. She set soft pillows in a circle. She filled a pitcher with cool water and lemon slices. She checked the tiny umbrella bucket in case clouds came. She made a welcome sign: “Hello, friends! Please share and tell us about your food.”

Soon, her friends came. Paco the pigeon flapped down with a basket. “Coo-coo! I brought empanadas. My Tía makes them. They smell buttery and warm.”

Mai the cat climbed up the ladder. “Meow! I have rice with sesame and seaweed. It is from my family’s meals.”

Amir the squirrel bounced on the railing. “Chirp! I brought dates. My Nana says they are sweet like stories.”

Luna the rabbit hopped in with a drum. “Thump-thump! I brought a song and flatbread. We dance and eat together.”

Sunshine’s ears wiggled with joy. “Thank you! Your foods and music are special. I can’t wait to learn.” She put on her helper apron. It felt snug and safe.

They all looked at the table. It was small. The plates were different sizes. There were not many cups. Sunshine felt a tiny pinch of worry. “Oh,” she said softly. “We have more friends than space.”

Paco cooed, “We must share.”

Mai purred, “We must plan.”

Amir chittered, “We can take turns.”

Luna thumped her drum, “We can talk and make a good plan together.”

Sunshine took a slow breath. She remembered: preparation and negotiation help friends. Negotiation is a big word, but it means talking to find a way that feels fair.

“Let’s make a plan,” Sunshine said. “We can make a food train. One plate goes round and round.”

Paco puffed his feathers. “Coo! My empanadas are hot. They go first?”

Mai swished her tail. “My rice is sticky. It is easy for little paws. Maybe it goes in the middle.”

Amir held up a date. “Sweet dessert can be last!”

Luna tapped a gentle beat. “And I can sing while we pass the plates.”

Sunshine nodded. “That sounds fair. Paco first, then Mai, then Luna’s bread, then Amir’s dates.” She looked at the cups. “We have three cups and five friends.”

They all thought. The breeze made the mint leaves whisper.

Amir said, “We can share cups with careful sips.”

Sunshine shook her head kindly. “We need to be safe and clean.”

Mai’s whiskers twitched. “We can use the little jars from the herb shelf.”

Sunshine smiled. “Yes! Little jars can be cups.” She washed the jars with soap and warm water. Splash-swish-swish. The jars sparkled in the sun.

Luna placed her drum in the center. “When I tap one time, we pass left. When I tap two times, we pause and say thank you in our way.”

Sunshine clapped her hooves. “What a lovely idea! Let’s practice.”

Luna tapped, tap! The plates moved left. Sunshine smelled buttery empanadas. Her nose twitched. “Mmm.” She took a tiny bite. “So flaky! Paco, what is special about them?”

Paco’s eyes shone. “My Tía sings when she cooks. She tells stories of our home. Empanadas remind me of hugs.”

Tap-tap! They all paused.

“Gracias,” Sunshine said carefully. “That means thank you in Spanish, right?”

Paco cooed, “Sí! Yes!”

Tap! The rice came to Sunshine. It was warm and gentle. “Mai, tell us more.”

Mai smiled. “My family sprinkles sesame like little stars. We use chopsticks. We listen to sea songs.”

Tap-tap! Pause.

“Arigatō,” Sunshine said, bowing her head.

Mai purred, “You said it so sweetly.”

Tap! Luna offered her flatbread. “We stretch the dough and dance.” She hummed a tune, la-la-la. The bread smelled toasty.

“Shukran,” Sunshine said to Amir as the dates arrived.

Amir grinned. “You are welcome! On holidays, we share dates at sunset. We say prayers and stories.”

As they ate, the city sounds made a soft music: birds, breezes, a faraway bell. The sun warmed their backs. The mint tasted bright. Everyone smiled.

A small cloud scooted across the sky. Plip! A tiny drop landed on Sunshine’s nose. “Rain!” she giggled. “Good thing we prepared.” She popped open the tiny umbrella and set it over the food. Paco stretched his wings to shield the bread. Mai pulled the blanket edges in. Amir tucked the jars under the umbrella rim. Luna changed her song to a rainy rhythm, pat-a-pat, pat-a-pat.

They kept sharing and talking. They learned words from each other. They learned songs and ways to show care. They took turns. They listened. They felt cozy and proud.

When the rain stopped, a rainbow curved over the rooftops. The colors glowed. Sunshine’s heart felt big and warm.

“Friends,” she said softly, “today we prepared together. We talked and made fair choices. We tasted foods and words from different homes. We learned new ways. That is cultural appreciation. It means we love and respect each other’s traditions.”

Everyone nodded. Paco cooed, “We are a rainbow of friends.”

Mai purred, “Different and beautiful.”

Amir chirped, “Sharing makes us strong.”

Luna thumped her drum, “Negotiation helps us agree.”

Sunshine nuzzled each friend. “I learned that planning helps. Talking kindly helps. And listening helps my heart grow.”

They packed up the jars and folded the blanket. The rooftop smelled like rain and mint. The city sang a soft goodnight.

Sunshine looked at the rainbow one more time. “Thank you,” she whispered, to her friends, to the food, and to the big, wide world of cultures.

The End.
