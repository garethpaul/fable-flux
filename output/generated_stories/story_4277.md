# Bubbles in the Busy Garage

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to splash, flip, and laugh. One sunny morning, Bubbles visited a big, bright garage workshop. The garage smelled like lemon soap and fresh wood. Sunlight made little sparkles on the floor. Tools hung on the walls like shiny toys—hammers, brushes, and colorful jump ropes.

“Hello!” Bubbles chirped, clicking happily. “I am ready to move and play!”

In the garage, friends from all around the neighborhood were busy. There was Mei with purple sneakers, Jamal with a green cap, Sita with bright bangles that jingle-jangled, and Nico with a blue scarf. They came from different places and spoke in different ways, but they shared one big smile.

Coach Tia, a kind grown-up with gentle eyes, clapped her hands. “Welcome, team! Today we will move our bodies and help others. We will exercise, we will build, and we will share.”

Bubbles flicked his tail in a bucket of warm water. Splash! The water felt cozy on his smooth skin. He listened to the whirr of a fan and the soft tap-tap of little hands. “What do we share today?” he asked.

Coach Tia pointed to a wooden balance beam and a stack of jump ropes. “We will share our tools and our time. Everyone gets a turn.”

Mei giggled. “I want the red jump rope!”

“I want it too!” said Jamal.

Bubbles looked at the red rope. It was bright and shiny, like a strawberry. His heart said, I want it first! His cheeks felt hot. A bubble of anger rose in his tummy.

He puffed out a small snort. “I need it now,” he said.

Coach Tia knelt beside him. “Bubbles, take a slow breath. In through your blowhole… out nice and slow.”

Bubbles tried. In… and out. The bubble of anger felt smaller. He wiggled his fins. “I can calm down,” he whispered.

Sita held up a yellow rope. “We can share. We can take turns. I will count to five for each turn,” she said, bangles jingling. “One, two, three, four, five! Next friend!”

“That is fair,” Nico said. “That is justice—fair and kind for everyone.”

Bubbles looked at Mei and Jamal. “We can share,” he said. “Let’s make a plan.”

Mei smiled. “I use the red rope first. Jamal can go next. Bubbles, you can go after Jamal.”

Jamal nodded. “And while we wait, we can do stretches together!”

They made a little circle. Their shoes squeaked on the floor. The fan hummed. “Arms up like tall trees,” Coach Tia said. “Bend and sway.” Bubbles swayed his fins. The friends giggled. It felt good to move while waiting. It was exercise and it was fun.

Sita counted for Mei. “One, two, three, four, five!” Mei jumped and spun, her braid swinging. Then it was Jamal’s turn. “One, two, three, four, five!” Jamal hopped and laughed, his cap bouncing.

Now it was Bubbles’ turn. He felt calm, not hot and angry. He took the red rope in his fins. “My turn! Thank you for sharing.”

He jumped gently, up and down. “Swoosh, swoosh,” went the rope. “Thump, thump,” went the rope on the floor. Bubbles’ fins moved fast. He counted. “One, two, three, four, five!” His friends cheered. “Go, Bubbles, go!”

After jumping, Bubbles shared the rope with Nico. “Here you go,” Bubbles said. “My turn is done.”

Nico smiled. “Gracias,” he said. “Thank you.”

They all clapped. Coach Tia said, “You did it! You shared and you waited. That is fair. That is just.”

Next, they built a tiny balance beam from smooth wood. The wood smelled sweet, like a pencil. They took turns sanding, painting, and drying. “We will share the tools,” Mei said. “One brush for you, one brush for me.” Sita counted again, “One, two, three, four, five!” Everyone got time to paint.

When they finished, a little bird peeked in the door. Chirp, chirp! Jamal laughed. “Even the bird wants a turn.”

Bubbles waved. “We can share the space. But the bird can watch.”

They lined up at the beam. “Step, step, step,” they said together. Bubbles balanced, tail swishing, eyes bright. He wobbled but did not fall. The friends cheered each other in many voices—“Great job!” “Bien hecho!” “Shabash!” The words sounded different, but they all meant the same kind thing.

Then a little bump happened. Nico stepped on Bubbles’ fin by mistake. “Ow,” Bubbles said, surprised. The hot bubble tried to come back. But Bubbles remembered. In… and out. Calm breath. “I feel upset,” he said softly, “but it was an accident.”

Nico looked sorry. “Lo siento. I’m sorry.”

Bubbles nodded. “Thank you for saying sorry. We can be fair. We can try again.”

Coach Tia smiled. “That is justice with kindness.”

At the end of the day, the garage was full of happy sounds—claps, giggles, and the soft buzz of the fan. The jump ropes hung in a neat row. The beam was smooth and dry. The friends washed their hands and fins. Lemon soap made tiny bubbles that popped like kisses.

Bubbles looked at his friends. “Today we shared. We took turns. We moved our bodies. We were fair and calm.”

Mei hugged him. “Sharing makes the day shiny.”

Jamal nodded. “Exercise makes us strong.”

Sita twirled her bangles. “Justice is being fair to everyone.”

Nico waved. “And calming down helps us share.”

Bubbles clicked and splashed one happy splash. “I learned that sharing keeps the fun going for all. When we wait, breathe, and take turns, everyone gets a smile.”

They all smiled back, and the garage felt warm and bright.

The End.
