# Leo’s Little Balcony Garden

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and bright curious eyes. He lived in a cozy home with a sunny balcony garden. The balcony had pots of green mint, tiny tomatoes, and tall sunflowers that waved in the breeze. The air smelled like wet soil and sweet leaves. Birds chirped from a nearby tree, and a gentle hum of city sounds floated below.

One morning, Mama Lion said, “Good morning, Leo! Today is garden day. We will water, clip old leaves, and pick the little tomatoes. We will do it safely and slowly.”

Leo puffed out his chest. “I can help! I am brave,” he said.

Mama smiled. “Yes, you are brave. And brave means careful too. We use our eyes, ears, and paws safely. We stay away from the edge, and we move one step at a time.”

Leo nodded. He put on his tiny blue watering can backpack and his soft paw gloves. The gloves felt snug and warm. The watering can sloshed with cool water, making a happy swish-swish sound.

“First job,” Mama said, pointing to a pot of mint. “We water the mint until the soil is damp, not soggy.”

Leo bent close. He sniffed. “Mmm! It smells fresh, like toothpaste!” He carefully tilted the can. Drip, drip, drip. The soil turned dark and shiny.

“You did it!” Mama cheered. “You used careful paws.”

Leo felt a little spark inside. His whiskers twitched with pride. “I can do the next job too,” he said.

Mama nodded and held up safe, round-tipped scissors. “Second job: clip the old brown leaves. I will hold the pot. You will clip. Remember, slow and steady.”

Leo took a deep breath. Snip. Snip. The scissors made quiet, crunchy sounds. A tiny ladybug crawled by. “Hello, little dot friend,” Leo whispered. The ladybug tickled his nose, and he giggled.

“Great work,” Mama said. “Now the plant can grow.”

Leo’s spark of pride grew warmer. He looked at the tomato plant. Small red tomatoes peeked out like shiny buttons. “Third job! I can pick the tomatoes,” he said.

“Before we pick,” Mama said, “we check safety. Where are we standing?”

Leo looked down. The balcony had a wide, safe mat. A chalk line showed the “no-go zone” near the edge. Leo put his paws behind the chalk line. “I stay inside the safe line,” he said.

“Good choice,” Mama smiled. “Now, we pick the soft, red ones.”

Leo reached out. He touched a tomato. It felt smooth and cool. Pop! It came off in his paw. “Tomato!” he laughed.

Pop-pop-pop. Soon, he had a small bowl of red tomatoes. His tail swished with joy.

Then Leo saw a very big tomato, high on the plant. It looked extra red and juicy. He stretched his paw up, up, up. The bowl wobbled. The plant wiggled. The balcony breeze whispered, “Careful, Leo.”

Leo’s tummy felt fizzy. “I want to get it,” he said. “But I feel wobbly.”

Mama’s voice was calm and warm. “Brave cubs listen to their bodies. Wobbly means pause. What can we do to be safe?”

Leo looked around. He saw a small step stool with grippy feet. He remembered the safe line. He remembered slow and steady. “We can move the stool far from the edge. You can hold it. I can use two paws,” he said.

Mama grinned. “That is a smart plan.”

They set the stool inside the safe line. Mama held it strong. Leo climbed one step. He planted his paws and took a deep breath. His heart beat, thump-thump, but he felt steady now.

“Ready,” he whispered.

He reached up and gently twisted. Pop! The big tomato came free. Leo smiled so wide his cheeks felt sunny. He climbed down and held the tomato close. It smelled sweet and warm from the sunshine.

“You did it!” Mama cheered. “You made a safe plan. You finished your jobs. How do you feel?”

Leo pressed a paw to his chest. “I feel proud,” he said. “My brave is inside me. It helps me do things, one step at a time.”

They washed the tomatoes and tasted one. It burst with juicy flavor—sweet and tangy. Leo licked his lips. “Yum!”

Mama hugged him. “Today you showed three big skills: you believed in yourself, you finished your tasks, and you stayed safe.”

Leo beamed. He looked at the garden. The mint leaves shone. The tomato plant stood tall. The sunflowers nodded like happy friends. The city breeze felt soft on his fur.

He took off his gloves and put away his tools. “I can help again tomorrow,” he said. “I am brave, careful, and kind. I can do hard things.”

Mama patted his head. “Yes, Leo. You can.”

Leo looked at the big tomato in the bowl. “This tomato is my brave tomato,” he said with a giggle. “It reminds me to try, to plan, and to finish.”

He cuddled close to Mama as a bird sang a sweet evening song. The balcony felt safe and calm. Leo’s heart felt big and bright.

And the little lion cub knew: I am strong. I am safe. I can do my best.

The End.
