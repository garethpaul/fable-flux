# Ruby Rabbit’s Study Corner Choice

Ruby was a small, determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. She had a cozy study corner under a sunny window. The rug was fluffy on her paws. A little table held crayons, paper, and a timer shaped like a carrot. The air smelled like fresh paper and a hint of lavender from a tiny plant. Ruby loved her study corner. It was quiet, warm, and full of ideas.

Today, Ruby wanted to make a picture chart for her burrow neighbors. “I want everyone to remember to share the path and hop safely,” she said. “I want to help.” She wiggled her whiskers. Civic responsibility, her teacher had said, means caring for your community.

Ruby sat down and looked at her paper. “What should I draw first?” she asked herself. Her ears twitched. She had so many ideas! A big sign? A small sign? A picture of a rabbit looking both ways?

She picked up a red crayon. Then she paused. “Stop and think,” she whispered. “Good choices start with thinking.” Ruby closed her eyes and took a deep breath. In, out. The room was still. She heard the soft tick-tick of her carrot timer.

Ruby opened her eyes and spoke aloud. “First, I will make a plan.” She drew three boxes on her paper. In the first box, she wrote, “Listen.” In the second, “Choose.” In the third, “Try.”

“Listen,” Ruby said. She put a paw on her heart. “I will listen to my feelings. I feel excited. I also feel a little unsure.” She nodded. “That’s okay.”

“Listen to others too,” she added. She hopped to the burrow door and peered down the hall. Her neighbor, Tilly Tortoise, was reading a book. “Tilly?” Ruby called softly. “Do you have a minute?”

Tilly looked up with a slow smile. “Yes, Ruby. What is it?”

“I want to make a safety chart to help everyone share the path. What do you think is important?” Ruby asked.

Tilly tapped her chin. “I think… slow is safe around corners. And using kind words if we bump.”

Ruby’s ears stood tall. “Thank you! That helps.” She wrote slow is safe and use kind words on her list. “I respect your ideas,” she said.

Back in her study corner, Ruby moved to the second box. “Choose,” she whispered. She had two ideas: a long list of rules, or a simple picture chart with three clear steps. She imagined little bunnies stopping to look. “Simple is better for quick eyes,” she said. “I choose the picture chart.”

She began to draw. In the first picture, a rabbit looked left and right with big, bright eyes. In the second, two friends shared the path, one saying, “After you,” and the other saying, “Thank you.” In the third, a bunny slowed at a corner, tiny motion lines showing a gentle hop. She added small stars and arrows that felt friendly and fun. The crayons made soft scritch-scratch sounds. The paper was smooth under her paw.

“Try,” Ruby said, pointing to the third box. “I will test my chart.” She set the carrot timer for one minute, then pretended to be a neighbor walking by. Tick-tick-tick. She looked at the chart. “Look left and right… share the path… slow at corners,” she read. “I can understand it fast!” She smiled.

Just then, Buzz the Bee hovered at the window, wings humming like a tiny drum. “Buzzz-hello, Ruby!”

“Hi, Buzz!” said Ruby. “Can you look at my chart? Does it feel kind? Does it feel safe?”

Buzz zipped closer. “Bzzz, I like the arrows. They show where to go. And the words are friendly. But maybe add a little sun for bright corners?”

Ruby nodded. “Good idea! Innovation means trying new ideas.” She drew a small sun in the corner picture. The yellow crayon felt warm in her paw.

Ruby hopped down the burrow hall to the community board. She paused. “I should ask,” she said. “Respect means I check first.” She found Grandma Hare by the board, pinning up a music note poster.

“Grandma Hare,” Ruby said politely, “may I put my safety chart here?”

Grandma Hare’s eyes twinkled. “What a thoughtful idea, dear. Yes, you may. Thank you for asking.”

Ruby used two tiny clips. Clip, clip. The chart hung straight.

Later, neighbors came by. Pip Pup stopped. “I can read the pictures!” he cheered. Tilly Tortoise nodded slowly. “Clear and kind,” she said. Buzz buzzed a happy tune.

That afternoon, Ruby walked the path with her friends. At a corner, she slowed. She looked left and right. She met a friend and said, “After you.” Her friend smiled. “Thank you.” The path felt calm and safe. Ruby’s heart felt big and warm.

At bedtime, Ruby sat in her study corner again. The lavender plant smelled sweet. The carrot timer was quiet. She looked at her three boxes: Listen, Choose, Try. She added a fourth word under them: Share.

Ruby whispered, “Today I listened to feelings and friends. I chose a simple plan. I tried it. I shared it kindly.” She sighed a happy sigh. “Good decisions help everyone.”

She turned off the lamp. The study corner glowed soft and cozy. Ruby’s whiskers twitched with new ideas for tomorrow.

The clear lesson: When we stop, think, and listen, we can make safe, kind choices that help our whole community.

The End.
