# Melody Bird Learns New Words

Melody was a small singing bird with bright blue feathers and a tiny golden beak. She loved to sing, “La, la, la!” every morning. One sunny day, Melody fluttered into the creative art room. The room smelled like fresh paper and clean soap. Sunlight made soft squares on the floor. Paints, crayons, and glue sticks sat on a low table, ready for fun.

“Good morning!” chirped Melody. “What can we make today?”

Lina and Sam, two cheerful children with paint aprons, clapped their hands. “Let’s make word art!” said Lina. “We can learn new words and sing them.”

Melody’s feathers twitched with excitement. “New words? I love words! Words help my songs.”

Sam held up a big, clean brush. “First, we wash our hands,” he said. He showed Melody the sink. The water was warm and made a splash-splash sound. The soap smelled like lemons.

Melody watched carefully. “I want to try,” she chirped. She dipped her tiny feet in a little dish of water beside the sink. Splash! She rubbed them on a soft cloth. “Clean feet for a clean room,” she sang. “Clean means we care.”

“Great initiative, Melody!” Lina smiled. “You saw what to do and did it.”

They sat at the table. Paper felt smooth under their fingers. Markers clicked open and smelled a little sweet. Lina drew a big sun. “This word is ‘sun,’” she said.

Melody repeated, “Sun.” Her voice was soft and round. “Sun warms our wings.”

Sam drew a blue cup. “Cup,” he said.

“Cup,” Melody sang. “Cup holds water. Glug, glug!”

They laughed. The room felt bright and happy. Melody hopped closer. “Can we learn words from your families too?” she asked. “I want to hear your traditions.”

Lina’s eyes sparkled. “My grandma says ‘abuela’ for grandmother. It is from our family’s language.”

“Abuela,” Melody tried. The word felt warm in her beak, like a hug. “Abuela loves and tells stories.”

Sam nodded. “We eat dumplings on special days. My grandpa says ‘jiaozi’ for dumplings.”

“Jiaozi,” Melody chirped carefully. The word was new, like a soft drum. “Jiaozi taste yummy and bring us together.”

Lina drew a big heart and wrote, “Love.” Then she added, “Amor,” the word her abuela used. Sam drew a round dumpling and wrote, “Jiaozi.” Melody sang both words. “Amor, jiaozi!” The sounds made the room glow with feeling.

Melody looked around. “Let’s make a Language Tree!” she said. “We can write words from many traditions on paper leaves.” She took initiative and fluttered to the green paper. Snip-snip went the child-safe scissors as Lina cut leaf shapes. Sam dabbed glue with gentle, sticky taps. Melody placed each leaf on a big brown paper trunk. The leaves rustled softly.

“What other words do we know?” Melody asked.

“Water,” Sam said, pointing to the sink. He added, “Shui.” The word was cool and smooth, like a sip.

“Song,” Lina said. “Canto,” she added with a smile.

Melody learned each word and sang it, changing her tune to fit the feeling. “Sun, cup, abuela, jiaozi, water, shui, song, canto.” Her voice rose like a kite and fell like a feather. The sounds tickled her tongue. The children swayed to the rhythm.

A small sneeze came from the door. “Achoo!” It was Max, who had been painting a star. “Oops,” he said. “I forgot to cover my sneeze.”

Melody fluttered over kindly. “It’s okay, Max. We can learn a hygiene word too. ‘Tissue.’” She pointed with her wing to the tissue box. “We use a tissue and wash our hands.”

Max took a tissue. “Thank you,” he said. He washed his hands with soap. Bubbles popped like tiny balloons. “Clean and healthy,” he said. He smiled.

“Add ‘tissue’ to the tree,” Lina said. Sam wrote it and added the word “pañuelo,” which his friend used at home. The tree grew fuller and brighter. It looked like a garden of sounds.

Melody felt proud. “Every word is like a color,” she sang softly. “Words paint our world.”

They all sat on the rug. The rug felt soft under knees and tiny bird feet. Melody began a gentle song:

“Words from near,
Words from far,
Words shine bright,
Like a star.
Sun and shui,
Canto too,
Amor, jiaozi,
Me and you.”

The children hummed. Their voices were warm and cozy. The art room was calm and happy.

Lina patted the paper trunk. “We shared our traditions,” she said.

Sam nodded. “We learned to care for our health,” he added.

Max grinned. “We took initiative and made a tree,” he said proudly.

Melody’s heart felt big and light. “Today I learned that every language has a special song,” she chirped. “When we listen and share, we understand and feel close.”

The sun moved across the floor. It made the leaves on the Language Tree glow. Melody bowed her little head.

“Let’s keep our words safe,” she said. “We will wash hands, care for our room, and care for each other.”

“Yes!” the children cheered.

Melody sang one last line, soft and sweet. “Different words, one happy room.”

The End.
