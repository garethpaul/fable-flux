# Poppy Panda’s Rooftop Craft Day

Poppy Panda lived in a tall, sunny building with a big rooftop garden. The roof felt warm under her fuzzy paws. The air smelled like mint and fresh rain from the little herb pots. Bees hummed. The city made soft whooshing sounds far below.

“Today is craft day!” Poppy said with a happy clap. She was a patient panda who liked to take her time. She carried a basket full of clean bottles, bright buttons, soft fabric scraps, and shiny boxes. She loved recycling. She loved turning old things into new treasures.

Her friends met her on the roof. There was Tiko Turtle, slow and curious. There was Mina Magpie, who loved sparkle. And there was Lila Lamb, gentle and kind.

“Let’s make a big kite!” Mina chirped. Her eyes sparkled like the buttons. “I want the kite to be all shiny!”

Tiko shook his head. “I want a bird house,” he said in a soft, slow voice. “A quiet place for birds to rest.”

Lila smiled. “I want flower crowns,” she said. “Soft and pretty.”

Poppy looked at the basket. So many ideas! So many friends! Her tummy felt fluttery, like a little kite in the wind. “We can’t do everything at once,” she said softly. “What should we do?”

Mina puffed her feathers. “Sparkle kite!” she chirped louder.

Tiko pulled his head in a little. “Bird house,” he whispered.

Lila lowered her eyes. “Flower crowns,” she said, almost a whisper.

The bright rooftop felt a bit tight, like a band that was too snug. The wind tugged Poppy’s ears. She took a slow breath. In… and out. The minty leaves tickled her nose. “We can find a way,” Poppy said. “Let’s listen to each other. Let’s make a plan.”

She set down the basket. The buttons rattled. The bottles clinked. “We can compromise,” Poppy said. “That means we each give a little and get a little. We can make one special thing together that has pieces we all like.”

Mina tilted her head. “Sparkle?”

Tiko peeked out. “Quiet for birds?”

Lila’s tail wiggled. “Flowers too?”

Poppy nodded. “Yes. Let’s make a cozy bird rest with a tiny flag that flutters like a kite, and we will decorate it with soft flowers and a little sparkle. We will use our recycling. We will share.”

The wind swirled. The sun felt warm again. The friends smiled.

They began to craft. Poppy gently cut a shiny box to make a little house. Snip, snip. Tiko held the box steady with his careful turtle feet. “Thank you, Tiko,” Poppy said. “You are steady.”

Mina sorted buttons by color. “Blue, gold, silver!” she sang. She glued one tiny silver button on the roof. “A tiny star,” she said. It sparkled when the sun kissed it.

Lila braided green fabric strips. Swish, swish. She made soft loops like leaves and soft circles like flowers. She sniffed the mint pot. “Mmm, fresh,” she said, tucking a safe, fallen leaf near her braid for a nice smell.

Poppy tied a light ribbon to a short stick. The ribbon fluttered in the breeze. “This is our kite flag,” she said. The ribbon made a whispery sound, like shhh-shhh.

As they worked, they talked.

Mina chirped, “I like shine.”

Tiko nodded. “I like quiet.”

Lila smiled. “I like soft.”

Poppy’s ears wiggled. “I like when we share,” she said.

Soon, the little bird rest was ready. It had a strong shiny box body, a soft green wreath around the door, one twinkly button on the roof, and a fluttering ribbon flag that danced in the wind. It looked happy.

But then the wind blew harder. Whoooooosh! The ribbon flag tugged. The bird rest wobbled.

“Oh no!” cried Lila.

Poppy put her paw on the house. “Hold on,” she said. “We can fix this together.”

Tiko found a smooth, heavy pebble near the herb pots. “We can make it steady,” he said.

Mina brought a bit of string from the basket. “Tie it, tie it!” she chirped.

Lila added another braid for balance. “Soft and strong,” she said.

Poppy tied the string to the pebble and the base of the house. The house sat firm. The ribbon fluttered, but it did not pull the house over.

They all stepped back. The sun warmed their faces. A tiny finch landed on the roof rail and peeped. Peep-peep! It hopped near the little house and peeked inside.

“It works,” whispered Tiko.

“It’s pretty,” said Lila.

“It sparkles!” Mina sang.

Poppy’s heart felt big and calm. “We did it,” she said.

They sat on the warm rooftop tiles and shared cool water from a clean bottle. They listened to the wind’s shhh-shhh. They smelled mint and sunshine. The city hummed below, but up here felt peaceful.

Mina looked at Poppy. “I like compromise,” she said. “We all got something we wanted.”

Tiko nodded. “I gave a little. I got a little. We made one good thing.”

Lila patted Poppy’s paw. “We respected each other.”

Poppy smiled. “When we listen, share, and wait our turn, we make room for everyone. Compromise helps us make kind choices.”

The tiny finch peeped again, and the ribbon flag danced. The friends laughed together.

The End.
