# Timmy Turtle Packs a Plan

In a quiet house on a sunny street, there was a cool, cozy basement. The basement smelled a little like soap and warm dust, and it hummed with soft sounds. Beep. Whirr. Tap. This was Daddy’s lab, with neat shelves, bright labels, and a long table full of safe, simple tools. In the corner, there was a small green turtle named Timmy.

Timmy was shy. He liked to tuck his head into his shell when loud things happened. Today, Timmy’s head peeked out just a little. He looked at a flyer on the table. It said, “Park Music Parade! Bring your songs! Bring your smiles!”

Timmy whispered, “I want to play music. I want to go outside.” His tummy fluttered like a tiny drum.

Daddy walked down the stairs. Step, step, step. “Good morning, Timmy,” Daddy said. “The lab is open. Beep!” He pressed a little button, and a friendly light blinked on.

Timmy pointed to the flyer. “I want to play my pot drum at the parade,” he said in a small voice, “but I am shy. And what if I forget things?”

Daddy smiled. “We can prepare,” he said. “Preparation is a plan we make before we go.”

“Prepare?” Timmy asked. The word felt round and new.

Daddy nodded. “Prepare means we get ready. We think. We pack. We practice. When we prepare, we feel calm.”

Timmy took a deep breath. He smelled the lemony wipes and the warm plastic bins. “Okay,” he said. “Let’s prepare.”

They made a list on a big paper. Daddy said each word slowly, and Timmy drew a little picture next to it.

- Drum: a shiny pot with a soft blue strap.
- Sticks: two smooth wooden sticks.
- Water: a cold, clear bottle.
- Hat: a sun hat with a tiny turtle on it.
- Snacks: crunchy carrot coins.
- Blanket: for sitting.
- Song plan: three simple beats.

Timmy tapped the table. Tap tap tap. “That is beat one,” he said.

Daddy tapped too. Tap…tap…tap. “That is beat two.”

Timmy giggled and made a fast beat. Tap-tap-tap-tap! “Beat three!”

They put each thing into a bright red backpack. Zzzzip! The zipper sounded happy. Timmy tried the blue drum strap around his shell. It fit snug and soft. He swayed side to side and listened. The strap made a gentle swish.

“Let’s practice,” Daddy said. “Practice is part of preparation.”

Timmy stood in the lab. He closed his eyes and felt the cool floor on his feet. He tapped his pot drum softly. Tum… tum… tum. The sound was round and low, like a friendly hello.

“I can do a quiet song,” Timmy said. “Quiet feels safe.”

Daddy nodded. “And outside, there will be other sounds. You can still be you.”

They practiced the steps. Timmy said, “We put on the hat.” Plop. “We drink water.” Sip. “We check our list.” Check, check, check. “We breathe.” In… out… in… out.

Timmy smiled. His tummy felt calmer now. He was still shy, but he felt ready. Ready felt warm, like a hug.

Soon it was time to go. The basement light clicked off. The stairs creaked. Outside, the sun felt soft and kind. At the park, children laughed. A puppy barked. Someone shook a tambourine. Jing-a-ling!

Timmy tucked his head for a moment. It was loud. He held Daddy’s hand. “I have my plan,” he whispered.

Daddy knelt. “You prepared. Let’s find a cozy spot.”

They found a tree with speckled shade. The blanket went down. Floof. Timmy put on his hat. He took a sip of water. Sip. He checked his list. He breathed. In… out… in… out.

A little bunny named Bella hopped over. “Hi,” she said. “I like your drum.”

Timmy peeked out. “Thank you,” he said softly. “I made beats. Do you want to listen?”

Bella’s ears wiggled. “Yes!”

Timmy tapped Beat One. Tap tap tap. It sounded steady, like tiny footsteps. Bella smiled. A squirrel skittered close. Two ducklings waddled near. Quack. Quack. Soon, three children came and sat criss-cross on the blanket edge.

“I am shy,” Timmy told them, “but I prepared. I have quiet beats and water and snack time.”

“That is smart,” said a child with a red cap. “Can we tap along?”

Timmy nodded. “We can do Beat Two.” Tap…tap…tap. The group tapped with hands, sticks, and gentle feet. They moved like clouds in a slow dance. Then Timmy tried Beat Three. Tap-tap-tap-tap! The ducklings flapped. Quack-quack-quack-quack!

Everyone laughed. It was a soft, kind laugh. Timmy felt the giggle bubble in his chest. He was playing music! He was outside! He was still shy, but he felt accepted. The friends liked his quiet songs. They liked his careful steps.

A small fox looked worried. “I forgot my maracas,” she said.

Timmy thought of his list. He looked in the red backpack. “I have extra wooden spoons,” he said. “You can shake and tap them.” He held them out with a smile.

“Thank you,” said the fox. She shook them. Clack-clack. “You are helpful.”

The parade began with a gentle march. Timmy led a tiny line by the shade. Step, tap. Step, tap. They moved around the tree, then around a flower bed. They were an outdoor music team.

When the songs were done, Timmy ate crunchy carrot coins. Crunch. He sipped water. Sip. He felt proud and calm. Daddy gave him a thumbs-up.

“You prepared,” Daddy said. “Your plan helped you feel ready. You shared, and you were kind.”

Timmy nodded. “Preparation helps me. Music helps me. Friends accept me.”

He patted his drum. Tum. “Next time, I will make a new list,” he said, “and we can help more friends.”

The End.
