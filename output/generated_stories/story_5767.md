# Leo the Lion Cub's Big Goal

Leo was a small, fluffy lion cub with golden fur and bright amber eyes. He was staying in Grandma Gazelle's cozy guest room, which smelled like lavender and had soft blue curtains that danced in the breeze.

"I want to be the strongest lion in the whole savanna!" Leo announced one morning, puffing out his tiny chest. But when he tried to roar, only a squeaky "mew" came out.

Leo felt sad. His little shoulders drooped as he looked at himself in the guest room mirror. "I'm too small," he whispered. "I'll never be strong like the big lions."

Grandma Gazelle knocked gently on the door. "May I come in, little one?" Her voice was warm and kind.

"Yes, Grandma," Leo said softly.

She sat on the edge of the bed, and the mattress made a gentle creaking sound. "I heard you talking about wanting to be strong. That's a wonderful goal, Leo!"

"But I'm too little," Leo said, his voice shaky. "And my roar sounds like a mouse!"

Grandma Gazelle smiled. "Every big lion started as a small cub, just like you. But to reach your goal, you need a plan. Would you like to make one together?"

Leo's ears perked up. "A plan? How?"

"First," Grandma said, "strong lions need healthy food. What do you usually eat for breakfast?"

Leo's tummy rumbled. "Um... sometimes I skip breakfast because I'm not hungry."

"Ah!" Grandma nodded. "Strong bodies need good fuel. How about we start with eating three healthy meals every day? Protein for muscles, fruits for energy, and vegetables for vitamins."

Leo thought about this. "Okay! I can do that!"

"Second," Grandma continued, "you need to practice being brave and speaking up for yourself. That's called being assertive."

"What does that mean?" Leo asked, tilting his head.

"It means saying what you need and standing up for what's right, even when you feel scared."

Leo nodded seriously. "I want to learn that!"

The next morning, Leo woke up early. His tummy was growling loudly - it sounded like a tiny thunderstorm! He marched to the kitchen with determined steps.

"Grandma, I'm ready for my healthy breakfast!" he said in his clearest voice. "I want to eat good food to help me grow strong!"

Grandma beamed. "That's being assertive, Leo! I'm proud of you."

She made him scrambled eggs that smelled delicious, sweet orange slices that were juicy and bright, and green spinach that crunched between his teeth.

Every day, Leo practiced his plan. He ate his colorful meals, did little exercises like jumping and stretching, and practiced speaking up. When other cubs were mean, he said, "Please don't do that. It's not kind." When he needed help, he asked clearly.

One week later, Leo stood in front of the guest room mirror again. He took a deep breath and... "ROAR!"

It wasn't huge, but it was definitely a real roar! Leo's eyes sparkled with joy.

"I did it!" he shouted. "My plan worked!"

Grandma Gazelle hugged him tight. "You learned something very important, Leo. Big goals happen one small step at a time. You set a goal, made a plan, and worked hard every day."

Leo felt proud and strong - not just in his body, but in his heart too.

"Now I know," Leo said with a big smile, "I can reach any goal if I make a good plan and keep trying!"

The End.
