# Kora the Calm Koala in the Pantry

Kora was a calm koala with soft, gray fur and a gentle smile. One sunny afternoon, she tiptoed into the pantry. The pantry smelled like sweet apples, warm bread, and a tiny sprinkle of cinnamon. Shelves stood tall like quiet trees, holding jars and boxes and baskets.

“Hello, pantry,” Kora whispered. “I am here to help.”

Kora’s friend, Benny Bunny, bounced in behind her. His ears were tall, and his nose wiggled. “I’m hungry!” Benny said. “Let’s eat everything!”

Kora took a slow breath in and a slow breath out. “We will find a snack,” she said softly. “We will ask, we will listen, and we will be calm.”

Just then, Mia Mouse peeked from behind a jar. Her whiskers twitched. “Oh! You’re here,” Mia squeaked. “I’m sorting snacks by color. Red, yellow, and green!”

Benny reached for a shiny red apple. “Mine!” he shouted.

Kora gently put her paw on her own chest, not on Benny. “I need a calm space,” she said. “Benny, please use a quiet voice. The pantry is small. Loud hurts my ears.”

Benny froze, ears drooping. “Oh. I forgot,” he said. “I’m sorry.”

Kora nodded. “Thank you for listening.” She glanced at Mia. “May we have a snack?”

Mia smiled. “Yes. We have enough. But first, we sort. Red apples here. Yellow bananas here. Green cucumbers here.”

Benny’s nose wiggled fast. “But I want it now!”

Kora felt the want, too. Her tummy made a tiny rumble. She placed her paw on her belly. “Hello, tummy,” she whispered. “We can wait.” She took another slow breath in and a slow breath out.

She turned to Benny. “Let’s learn and then eat. Learning helps us make good choices.”

Mia handed them little labels. “These are for names,” she said. “When we finish sorting, we put our names on our snack, so everyone knows what is ours. That is a boundary. Boundaries keep snacks safe and friends happy.”

Benny blinked. “Bound-a-rees?”

Kora smiled. “Boundaries are gentle rules. They tell us what is okay and what is not okay. We can have our snack. We can keep our bodies calm. We can keep our voices soft. We can ask before we touch.”

Benny looked at the apple again. “May I take this red apple?” he asked Mia.

“Yes,” Mia said. “After we sort the rest. Thank you for asking.”

They worked together. Kora held a soft, fuzzy peach. It smelled like sunshine. She set it in the yellow basket. Benny carried a green cucumber, cool and bumpy. Mia stacked crackers that went crunch, crunch, crunch in the box.

Suddenly, a tower of cereal boxes wobbled. Wibble-wobble, wibble-wobble. “Oh no!” Benny cried. The tower leaned like a sleepy tree.

Kora did not shout. She did not leap. She placed her feet still and took a calm breath. “I see the wobble,” she said. “We can fix it. Mia, can you hold the bottom? Benny, can you step back to make space?”

Benny hopped back. “Okay!”

Mia held the bottom box with tiny paws. “Got it!”

Kora slid one box to the side. She gently moved another. Tap, tap, tap. The wobble stopped. The tower stood tall again.

“We did it,” Kora said. “We stayed calm. We used our bodies safely. We used our knowledge.”

Benny clapped softly. “Calm is strong.”

Mia nodded. “Calm is smart.”

At last, it was snack time. Benny hugged his apple to his chest. “I will write my name,” he said. He pressed a label onto the apple: BENNY. “This is my snack.”

Mia wrote her name on a cracker pack. “This is my snack.”

Kora wrote KORA on a ripe, yellow banana. “This is my snack.”

Benny looked at Kora’s banana. “It looks yummy,” he said. He reached a paw toward it, then stopped. “May I touch?”

Kora smiled. “Thank you for asking. Not now. I am not ready to share. That is my boundary.”

Benny nodded. “Okay.” He took a bite of his apple. Crunch! “I can wait and I can ask.”

They sat on the pantry floor. It was cool and smooth. The light made gentle stripes on the shelves. They chewed slowly. Crunch. Munch. Yum. Kora listened to the quiet sounds and felt safe.

After snacks, Mia asked, “Will you help me clean up?”

“Yes,” Kora said. “We can put things back.”

Benny wiped a tiny drip of juice. “I like helping,” he said.

Kora placed jars in a neat row. She hummed a soft tune. “When we are calm, we can think. When we think, we can make good choices. Calm helps our bodies and our friends.”

Benny took another slow breath, just like Kora. “In… out…”

Mia smiled. “The pantry feels happy.”

Kora patted her belly again. “Thank you, tummy, for waiting. Thank you, friends, for listening.”

They all giggled. The pantry smelled sweet and safe. The shelves stood tall and tidy.

Kora said, “Today we learned: Stay calm, ask first, and use what we know. Boundaries are kind. Calm is powerful.”

Benny and Mia nodded. “We will remember.”

The End.
