# Koala Kimi Listens in the Sunny Backyard

Koala Kimi woke up with a gentle stretch. She was a calm koala with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. The sun was warm and friendly, and the backyard smelled like sweet flowers and fresh grass. Birds chirped, “Tweet, tweet!” and a soft breeze whispered, “Whoosh,” through the leaves.

Kimi climbed down from her shady eucalyptus tree and blinked at the sunny backyard. “Good morning,” she said softly. “Today feels like a good day.”

From across the yard, her friends were gathering. Busy Bee buzzed close to the yellow marigolds. “Bzzz! We need to plant more flowers,” Bee said.

Tidy Tortoise was brushing leaves into a neat pile. “We should clean the path,” Tortoise said, moving slowly and carefully.

Squeaky Squirrel bounced along the fence. “Let’s build a bird feeder! Right now!” Squirrel squeaked, tail twitching.

Kimi felt excited. So many ideas! So many things to do! But her heart stayed calm. She took a deep breath and listened to the sounds of the backyard. The breeze, the birds, the soft crunch of leaves under Tortoise’s feet.

“Friends,” Kimi said in her gentle voice, “let’s listen to each other. If we listen, we can decide what to do first.”

Busy Bee buzzed closer. “Bzzz, I think flowers first.”

Tidy Tortoise shook his head slowly. “The path is messy. Cleaning first.”

Squeaky Squirrel hopped in place. “Bird feeder! Bird feeder!”

Kimi folded her paws and nodded. She listened to each friend’s words and watched their faces. Busy Bee’s wings moved fast. Tidy Tortoise’s eyes looked worried about the mess. Squeaky Squirrel’s tail swished with big energy.

Kimi looked around the sunny backyard. The flowers did need more friends. The path did have crunchy leaves. And the birds chirped, “Cheep, cheep,” as if they were hungry for seeds.

Kimi smiled. “I hear all of you,” she said. “Thank you for sharing. Let’s make a plan. We can help the birds, clean the path, and plant flowers. We can do all three, one by one.”

Squeaky Squirrel bounced. “Which one first?”

Kimi took another deep breath. She listened again. “Cheep, cheep,” sang the birds from the fence. Their little bellies looked round but empty.

Kimi made a decision. “First, we will build the bird feeder. The birds are asking with their songs. After that, we will clean the path so no one trips. Then we will plant flowers to make nectar for Bee.”

Busy Bee buzzed in a happy circle. “Bzzz, that is a good plan.”

Tidy Tortoise nodded. “Slow and steady. I agree.”

Squeaky Squirrel grinned. “Let’s go!”

Kimi picked up a small wooden cup from the garden table. It felt smooth. She found string that felt soft but strong. “I will tie the string,” she said. “Squirrel, can you hold the cup?”

“I can!” Squirrel said, standing very still.

Kimi tied a neat knot. She listened to the string go “swish” as it slid through the cup handle. “Bee, can you bring seeds?”

“Bzzz, yes!” Busy Bee zipped to the seed bag and sprinkled tiny seeds that smelled nutty.

Tidy Tortoise used careful steps to carry the feeder to a low branch. “Nice and easy,” he said, placing it gently. The feeder swayed just a little. “Sway, sway.”

The birds chirped louder. “Cheep, cheep!” They flapped down to try it. Tap, tap went their little beaks. “Yum!”

Kimi felt warm and proud. “We listened to the birds,” she said. “Now, the path.”

They worked together. Tidy Tortoise pushed the leaves. Shh, shh. Kimi held a small rake and pulled it gently. Scratch, scratch. Squirrel scooped leaves into a bin—plop, plop. Busy Bee brushed dust away with her tiny wings—fwip, fwip. Soon the path was clear and smooth.

“Last, flowers,” Kimi said. She patted the soil. It felt cool and crumbly. “We will plant in a line so Bee can visit each one.”

Squirrel brought little cups of water—sloshing carefully. Tortoise dug small holes—scoop, scoop. Bee showed where the sun was warmest. “Bzzz, here is good.”

Kimi tucked each seed in bed. “Good night, little seeds. Grow strong.”

They watered the ground until it smelled like rain. The sun kissed the earth, and the breeze hummed a happy tune.

Squirrel clapped. “We did it all!”

Bee buzzed a soft thank you. “Bzzz, I love these flowers.”

Tortoise smiled. “This backyard feels peaceful.”

Kimi sat under her eucalyptus tree. She listened to the birds eating seeds, to the leaves whispering, and to her friends laughing. Her heart felt calm and full.

Squirrel curled beside her. “Kimi, how did you make such a good plan?”

Kimi smiled. “I listened—first to each of you, and also to the backyard. When we listen, we understand. Then we can decide and help. That is good volunteering.”

Busy Bee nodded. “Bzzz, listening helps us all.”

Tidy Tortoise added, “And taking turns makes the work easy.”

Kimi wrapped her arms around her knees. “Next time, we can listen again and choose our next adventure.”

The sun glowed soft and golden. The birds sang sweet lullabies. The fresh flowers stretched their tiny green leaves. Kimi closed her eyes for a moment and breathed in the happy garden smell.

Today, Kimi learned: when we listen carefully, we make kind choices, we help others, and we do good things together.

The End.
