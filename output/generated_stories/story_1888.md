# Daisy's Big Mistake

Daisy the deer fawn loved visiting Papa's home office. The room smelled like fresh coffee and had tall windows with warm sunshine streaming through. Papa's big wooden desk held his important computer and neat stacks of papers.

"Good morning, Daisy!" Papa said, typing on his keyboard. "I have an important meeting today. Can you play quietly while I work?"

Daisy nodded her small spotted head. "Yes, Papa! I'll be very good."

She tiptoed around the office on her tiny hooves. Click, click, click went her steps on the smooth floor. Papa had given her special crayons and paper to draw pictures.

But then Daisy saw something sparkly on Papa's desk. A beautiful glass paperweight caught the sunlight like a rainbow. It looked so pretty and smooth.

"I just want to touch it," Daisy whispered to herself.

She stretched her neck up high. Her nose bumped the paperweight. CRASH! It tumbled to the floor and broke into pieces.

Papa's typing stopped. He turned around and saw the broken glass.

"Oh no," Daisy said, her brown eyes filling with tears. "I'm sorry, Papa. I didn't mean to break it."

Papa's face looked sad. "Daisy, that paperweight was very special. Grandma gave it to me before she went to heaven."

Daisy's heart felt heavy like a rock. She had broken something that couldn't be fixed. Something precious and important to Papa.

"I should have asked first," Daisy said softly. "I should have kept my hooves on the ground."

Papa knelt down and hugged her gently. "Thank you for telling the truth, little one. That takes courage."

"But how can I make it better?" Daisy asked. "I want to fix my mistake."

Papa thought carefully. "Well, you could help me clean up the pieces safely. And maybe we could find another way to remember Grandma together."

Daisy nodded eagerly. She held the dustpan while Papa swept up every tiny piece of glass. Then she had an idea.

"Papa, could I draw you a picture of Grandma? I remember her smile and her silver hair."

Papa's eyes brightened. "That would be wonderful, Daisy."

Daisy worked very hard on her drawing. She used her prettiest colors - silver for Grandma's hair, blue for her kind eyes, and pink for her warm smile. At the bottom, she wrote "I'M SORRY" in her best letters.

When she finished, Papa hung the picture right where the paperweight used to sit.

"This is even more special than the paperweight," Papa said, hugging Daisy tight. "Because you made it with love to say you're sorry."

Daisy learned that making mistakes happens to everyone. But the important thing is to tell the truth, say sorry, and try to make things better. Sometimes making amends means more than just saying "I'm sorry" - it means showing you care with your actions too.

From that day on, Daisy always asked before touching anything in Papa's office. And every morning, Papa smiled when he saw Grandma's picture watching over his desk.

The End.
