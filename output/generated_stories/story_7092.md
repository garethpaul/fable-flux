# Lila the Singing Bird Learns to Listen

Lila was a small singing bird with bright yellow feathers. She loved to sing. Her songs sounded like warm sunshine. Today, she was visiting a cozy guest room in a friendly house. The guest room smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap. The curtains were soft and blue. A quiet fan hummed, whir, whir, whir.

Lila perched on the window sill. “La-la-la!” she sang. Her voice filled the room like a happy breeze.

Down on the bed sat a little suitcase. It was open and neat. Inside were a colorful scarf, a tiny drum, a picture book, and a pair of small shoes. Visitors from different places came to this guest room. They brought new smells, new sounds, and new stories.

Today’s visitor was a child named Noor. Noor had warm brown eyes and hair in two fuzzy puffs. Noor hugged a book close. The book had pictures of lakes, trees, fish, and happy people. The title said, “We Love Our Water.”

“Hello,” chirped Lila, tilting her head. “I’m Lila. I sing songs.”

“Hi, Lila,” Noor said with a smile. “I’m Noor. I like to learn. My family travels. We meet new friends. We learn their stories.”

Lila puffed her chest. “I can sing a big song now!” She took a deep breath.

But Noor gently raised a hand. “Wait, please. I need quiet to read. I have school work. It’s about water and how to take care of it.”

Lila’s wings drooped a little. “Oh,” she said softly. “I love to sing. I don’t want to stop.”

Noor nodded kindly. “I understand. Your songs are pretty. But I must learn. When we learn, we help the world. Can we make a plan?”

Lila fluttered in a small circle. “A plan?” she echoed.

“Yes,” Noor said. “I read first. Then we sing together later. We can share. That is fair.”

Lila took a tiny breath. The fan hummed. The curtain brushed her feathers with a whisper. She wanted to sing right now. But she remembered a wise old robin who once said, “Listening is a brave song too.”

Lila felt a tiny knot in her belly. It was hard to wait. But she wanted to be brave. She wanted to help. “Okay,” she chirped. “I will listen. I will wait.”

“Thank you,” Noor said. Noor climbed onto the bed and opened the book. The pages were smooth. The pictures were bright. Noor read in a gentle voice. “Clean water helps fish, frogs, and people. We can save water by turning off taps. We can pick up trash. We can plant trees.”

Lila leaned close. She could see a clear blue lake. She could almost smell the cool water and the green leaves around it. “Ooh,” she whispered. “Water is special.”

Noor smiled. “Yes. In my grandma’s village, people sing to thank the river. In my friend’s city, people dance when it rains. Different places, same love for water.”

Lila’s eyes sparkled. “Songs for water? I love that.”

Noor read more. “Some children walk far to bring water home. When we learn, we can help. We can teach others to share and care.”

Lila’s heart beat fast like tiny drum taps. She felt brave again. “I want to learn to help. I want to teach too.”

Noor nodded. “Learning is for everyone. Big people, small people, and singing birds!”

They both giggled. The guest room felt warm and kind.

Soon, Noor closed the book. “I finished,” Noor said. “Thank you for waiting.”

Lila’s chest lifted. “Can we sing now?” she asked.

“Yes,” Noor said. “Let’s make a water song. It can be a learning song.”

Lila hopped to the open suitcase. She gently tapped the little drum with her beak. Tap-tap. Noor picked up the colorful scarf and waved it like a flowing river. The fan blew the scarf so it swished and whispered.

Lila sang softly first, like raindrops. “Plip-plop, plip-plop.” Noor added words. “We save our water, every day. Turn off the tap, hooray, hooray!”

Lila’s song grew bright like sunlight on a pond. “Trees, trees, drink and grow,” she sang. Noor twirled the scarf and chanted, “Pick up trash, keep streams aglow!”

They laughed and danced around the guest room. Their song was happy and gentle and brave. It sounded like learning. It sounded like caring. It sounded like friends from different places sharing one big idea.

Then Lila paused. She remembered the plan. “Is the music too loud?” she asked. “Do you need quiet again?”

Noor listened. Outside, a soft rain began. Pit-pat, pit-pat on the window. “The rain is singing with us,” Noor said. “But yes, let’s keep it soft. Soft songs can teach too.”

Lila nodded. Courage felt calm now. She sang quietly, like a warm hug. Noor whispered the words. Together, they made a kind, gentle lesson-song.

When the song ended, Noor clapped. “We did it! We learned first. We sang next. We helped each other.”

Lila felt proud. “I was brave to wait. I learned about water and different ways people care for it. I can teach others with my song.”

Noor hugged the book. “And I learned that listening is part of learning. Sharing makes us strong.”

They wrote their little song on a paper page and drew waves and trees. They put the paper in the suitcase, ready to travel and teach more friends.

The guest room was quiet again, except for the soft rain and the happy hum of the fan. Lila perched by the window, her feathers warm and bright.

“Learning helps us care for our world,” she sang softly. “We listen, we share, we act.”

“Together,” Noor added.

The End.
