# Milo the Brave Monkey Makes Things Right

Milo was a little brown monkey who lived in a cozy house with his family. He had bright, curious eyes and a long, wiggly tail that helped him swing from place to place. Milo loved exploring, but sometimes his curiosity got him into trouble.

One sunny morning, Milo's tummy rumbled loudly. "Grumble, grumble!" it said. He smelled something sweet and yummy coming from the kitchen pantry. The door was slightly open, and wonderful smells drifted out like invisible ribbons.

"Mmm, what's that delicious smell?" Milo wondered aloud, his nose twitching.

Without asking, Milo quietly swung into the pantry. Inside, he saw jars of colorful jam, boxes of crackers, and bags of his favorite banana chips. His eyes grew wide with excitement.

"Just one little taste," he whispered to himself.

But one taste became two, then three, then many more! Soon, Milo had made a big mess. Crumbs were scattered everywhere, jam was dripping from the shelf, and banana chips were all over the floor.

Suddenly, he heard footsteps. "Oh no!" Milo gasped, his heart beating fast like a little drum. He quickly hid behind a big bag of rice, feeling scared and worried.

Mama came into the pantry and saw the mess. "Oh my! What happened here?" she said, looking confused and sad.

Milo's tummy felt funny, but not from being hungry. It felt twisty and uncomfortable because he knew he had done something wrong. He watched Mama start cleaning up the mess, and his heart felt heavy.

"I made Mama sad," Milo thought. "I should have asked first. I need to be brave and tell the truth."

Taking a deep breath, Milo slowly came out from his hiding spot. His hands were shaking a little, but he knew what he had to do.

"Mama," he said in a small voice, "I... I made this mess."

Mama turned around and saw Milo standing there with crumbs on his face and jam on his paws. Instead of being angry, she knelt down to his level.

"Thank you for telling me the truth, Milo," she said gently. "That was very brave of you. But why didn't you ask first?"

Milo's eyes filled with tears. "I was hungry, and it smelled so good. I'm really, really sorry, Mama. I feel bad in my tummy for making you sad."

Mama gave him a warm hug. "I appreciate your honesty, little one. When we make mistakes, the brave thing to do is tell the truth and make things right."

"How can I make things right?" Milo asked, wiping his eyes.

"Well," Mama smiled, "you can help me clean up this mess, and next time, you can ask before taking food from the pantry."

Milo nodded eagerly. "Yes! I want to help!"

Together, they swept up the crumbs, wiped the sticky jam, and organized the shelves. Milo worked hard, and soon the pantry looked clean and tidy again.

"There!" said Mama. "Good as new!"

Milo felt much better. The twisty feeling in his tummy was gone, replaced by a warm, happy feeling.

"Being brave and telling the truth feels good," Milo said, smiling big.

"Yes, it does," Mama agreed. "And now, how about we make a healthy snack together?"

From that day on, Milo always asked before taking food. He learned that being brave means telling the truth, even when it's scary, and that making amends helps everyone feel better.

The End.
