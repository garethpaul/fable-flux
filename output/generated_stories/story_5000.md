# Pip the Penguin on the Rooftop

Pip was a cheerful penguin with a bright orange beak and wobbly little feet. One sunny morning, Pip slid on a shiny ramp and popped up onto a rooftop. The rooftop was warm and flat. It smelled like fresh rain and felt a little bumpy under Pip’s webbed feet.

“Hello, rooftop!” Pip chirped. “What can I learn today?”

Pip loved to learn. Pip loved to try. Pip loved to say, “I can do it!”

On the rooftop, Pip saw many things. There was a soft green garden in a square box. There were silver pipes that hummed. There was a little weather vane that spun and clicked, click, click, in the breeze.

Pip waddled closer. “What makes you spin?” Pip asked the weather vane.

“I spin when the wind blows,” the weather vane seemed to say, creakily turning. The wind tickled Pip’s feathers. Whoosh! Pip giggled. “The wind helps you move. I learned that!”

Pip wanted to look far, far out. Pip waddled to the edge but stopped. The edge was high. The street below looked small. Beep-beep went the tiny cars. “I am brave,” Pip whispered, “but I must be safe.”

Pip found a short wall around the edge. It was rough and gray. Pip carefully climbed onto a safe spot and peeped over. The sky was a bright blue bowl. Fluffy clouds drifted like slow boats. “Hello, clouds,” Pip said. “You float!”

On the rooftop, Pip met a small gray pigeon named Dot. Dot had shiny eyes and soft coos. Coo, coo.

“Hi, I’m Pip,” said Pip. “I am learning about the rooftop.”

“I’m Dot,” said Dot. “I live here. I can show you what I know.”

Pip flapped happily. “Yes, please! I want to learn.”

Dot showed Pip the garden box. Tiny leaves poked up from dark, wet soil. It smelled earthy and sweet. “These are baby plants,” Dot cooed. “They need sun and water.”

Pip touched the soil with a gentle flipper. “It is cool and squishy,” Pip said. “Plants need care. I learned that!”

Just then, a problem came. A big, bendy hose lay across the rooftop like a long snake. It made a tricky path between the garden and the water tap. Dot looked worried. “We need water for the baby plants, but the hose is tangled,” she cooed.

Pip wanted to help. Pip wanted to be independent and figure it out. “I can try,” Pip said. “I will learn how.”

Pip waddled to the tap. The metal was cold. The hose was twisty. It squeaked when Pip pulled it. Squeak. Pip tugged. Pip pushed. The hose still made a knot.

Pip took a deep breath. “When I do not know, I can think. I can look. I can try again.”

Pip looked closely. The hose looped over itself. Pip remembered how the weather vane moved with the wind, not against it. “Maybe I should move with the hose,” Pip said. Slowly, Pip slid a flipper under the loop and lifted. The loop loosened. Pip rolled the hose gently, round and round, like turning a wheel. The knot began to open.

Dot watched. “Coo! You are patient,” she said.

Pip smiled. “I am learning. It takes time.”

At last, the hose lay straight. Pip turned the tap a tiny bit. Drip, drip. The water was cold on Pip’s flipper. Pip turned it a bit more. Swish! A lovely stream! Pip quickly turned it back to a gentle flow. “I learned to control the water,” Pip said. “Not too much. Not too little.”

Pip and Dot watered the plants. The leaves sparkled. The sun felt warm. The wind smelled like clean water and sweet soil.

A loud caw came from the other side. A big crow hopped near. “This is my place,” the crow cawed. “Go away!”

Pip felt a twist in the tummy. Dot fluttered her wings. Pip stood tall, but friendly. “Hello,” Pip said softly. “My name is Pip. We are helping the plants. There is room for you, too.”

The crow tilted his head. “Room for me?”

“Yes,” Pip said. “We can share. We can learn from each other. You can teach us something.”

The crow blinked. “I know where the shade is coolest,” he said. He hopped to a chimney and pointed with his beak. “Here. When the sun is hot, you can rest here.”

“Thank you,” said Pip and Dot together.

They all sat in the cool shade. The roof hummed. The wind whispered. The plants drank. Pip felt proud and calm.

“I learned many things today,” Pip said. “The wind moves the vane. Plants need care. Knots can open if I am patient. Water should be just right. And sharing makes friends.”

Dot cooed happily. The crow gave a soft caw. Pip wiggled little feet and laughed.

Pip looked at the wide sky. “I can learn every day,” Pip said. “I can try on my own, and I can listen to others. That is how I grow.”

The End.
