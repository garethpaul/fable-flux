# Benny Beaver's Moving Morning

Benny Beaver sat at the big wooden dining table, his fluffy brown tail twitching with excitement. The morning sun streamed through the window, making golden patches dance on the floor. His mama had just finished setting out his favorite breakfast - sweet maple pancakes with blueberries!

"Yum, yum!" Benny said, rubbing his round belly. "These smell so good, Mama!"

But as Benny reached for his fork, he noticed something. His arms felt heavy and slow. When he tried to cut his pancakes, his paws moved like thick honey. "Oh my," he said, looking puzzled.

Mama Beaver smiled warmly. "Benny, when did you last go outside to play and move your body?"

Benny thought hard, tapping his chin with his paw. "Um... yesterday I watched cartoons all morning. Then I built with blocks sitting down. Then I read books in my chair..." His eyes grew wide. "I don't remember moving much at all!"

"Ah-ha!" said Mama. "Your body is telling you something important. Just like our teeth need brushing and our bellies need food, our muscles need exercise to stay strong and happy."

Benny's ears perked up. "Really? What happens if I don't exercise?"

"Well," Mama explained, sitting beside him, "our muscles get sleepy and weak. We might feel tired or grumpy. But when we move and play, our hearts pump happy blood, our muscles grow strong, and we feel wonderful!"

Benny nodded seriously. "I want to feel wonderful! But Mama, I already promised myself I'd finish this yummy breakfast first."

"That's very honest of you to keep your promise," Mama said proudly. "Keeping our word shows integrity - that means being truthful and doing what we say we'll do."

"Can I exercise after breakfast?" Benny asked hopefully.

"Of course! But first, let's think of ways to move right here in the dining room while we eat!"

Benny's eyes sparkled. "We can?"

Mama stood up and marched in place. "We can march our feet like this! One, two, one, two!" Her paws swung back and forth.

Benny giggled and copied her, his little legs pumping up and down. "This is fun! What else?"

"We can do shoulder rolls!" Mama rolled her shoulders in big circles. "Round and round, like rolling logs!"

Benny rolled his shoulders too, making silly faces. "I feel tingly and warm!"

They sat back down, and Benny noticed something amazing. "Mama! My arms feel strong now! Cutting my pancakes is easy!"

As they ate, they continued their dining room exercises. They stretched their arms up high like tall trees, twisted their bodies left and right like swaying branches, and wiggled their toes under the table.

"I feel so good!" Benny exclaimed, finishing his last bite. "My whole body feels awake and happy!"

"That's the magic of exercise," Mama said, giving him a warm hug. "And I'm grateful we discovered this together."

"Me too!" Benny said. "Thank you for teaching me, Mama. Can I tell Papa this story when he comes home?"

"That's a wonderful idea. Sharing what we learn helps others too."

Benny jumped up from his chair, full of energy. "Now can we go outside and exercise more?"

"Absolutely!" Mama laughed. "Let's go build a dam and swim in the pond!"

And off they went, Benny's body feeling strong and grateful for the gift of movement.

The End.
