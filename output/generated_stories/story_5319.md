# Joey's Helpful Day

Joey was a little kangaroo who loved to bounce and play. He lived with Mama Kangaroo in a cozy house where Mama worked in her home office. The office had a big wooden desk, a soft rolling chair, and colorful pictures on the walls.

Every morning, Joey would hop into the office to say good morning. "Bounce, bounce, bounce!" went his little feet on the carpet.

"Good morning, my sweet Joey!" Mama would say, giving him a warm hug that smelled like lavender soap.

One Tuesday morning, Joey noticed something different. Mama was sitting at her desk with papers scattered everywhere. Her computer screen showed lots of numbers, and she had a worried look on her face.

"Oh my," Mama sighed, rubbing her forehead. "I have so much work to finish today, and I can't find my special red pen anywhere!"

Joey's ears perked up. He wanted to help! "Mama, what does your red pen look like?" he asked in his sweetest voice.

"It's bright red with a silver clip, just like a tiny rocket," Mama explained. "I use it to mark important papers."

Joey hopped around the office, looking high and low. He checked under the desk where dust bunnies tickled his nose. "Achoo!" He looked behind the plant with the big green leaves that felt smooth and cool. No red pen anywhere!

Then Joey had an idea. "Mama, may I help you look in other rooms too?"

"That would be wonderful, Joey! Thank you for being such a good helper," Mama said with a smile that made Joey's heart feel warm and fuzzy.

Joey bounced to the kitchen. He looked on the counter next to the fruit bowl that smelled like sweet apples. No pen there. He hopped to the living room and checked the soft blue couch cushions. Still no pen!

In the bathroom, Joey stood on his tippy-toes to peek at the sink counter. There, next to Mama's toothbrush, was something red and shiny!

"Found it! Found it!" Joey called out, carefully picking up the pen with both paws.

He bounced back to the office as fast as his little legs could carry him. "Mama! Mama! I found your rocket pen!"

Mama's face lit up like sunshine. "Oh, Joey! You're such a wonderful helper! I must have left it there when I was brushing my teeth this morning."

Joey felt proud and happy inside, like he had butterflies dancing in his tummy. "I like helping you, Mama!"

"And I love having your help," Mama said, giving Joey another big hug. "You know what? You've been such a good helper that I finished my work early. Would you like to have a special snack together?"

They shared crackers and juice while sitting in the sunny office. The crackers were crunchy and tasted like cheese, and the juice was cold and sweet.

"Joey," Mama said gently, "when we help others, it makes everyone feel good. You helped me, and now we both feel happy!"

Joey nodded, his little tail wiggling with joy. "Helping is fun! Can I help you again tomorrow?"

"Of course! Every day we can find ways to help each other," Mama said.

From that day on, Joey made helping others part of his daily routine. He learned that even little kangaroos can make big differences when they use kind words and helpful actions.

The End.
