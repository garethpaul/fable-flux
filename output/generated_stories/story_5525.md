# Rocco Raccoon's Bubble Beat

The bathroom was warm and steamy. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet. The mirror was foggy, and the air smelled like minty soap. Rocco Raccoon pattered in on soft feet, his striped tail swishing. He loved music more than anything. He tapped rhythms on pots, pans, and even the floor. Today, he wanted to make a special Bubble Beat song.

“Bomp-bomp, tap-tap!” Rocco said, tapping his toothbrush on the sink. He hummed a happy tune. “I will make music and get ready fast!”

Mama Raccoon peeked in with a smile. She wore a bright scarf with tiny stars. “Good morning, Rocco. We leave for the park in thirty minutes,” she said. “You need to use the potty, brush your teeth, and wash your hands. Then you can practice your song.”

Rocco’s nose twitched. Thirty minutes sounded big and small at the same time. “I can do it!” he said. He pointed to the big sand timer on the shelf. The sand was pink and sparkly. “Timer help?”

“Yes,” Mama said, turning the timer over. “Ten minutes for potty, ten for teeth and hands, ten for practice. That’s our plan.”

“Plan!” Rocco clapped. He liked plans. They felt like gentle hugs.

Rocco hopped onto his step stool. “First, potty.” The toilet seat felt cool. He swung his feet and listened. Drip, drip. He started to sing, “Pee-pee potty song, do-do-dee!” But then he saw the bath toys by the tub. A yellow duck. A purple boat. A little drum with suction feet.

“Ooooh, instruments!” Rocco reached for the drum. He gave a quick tap. Boom. The sound bounced off the tiles. It felt exciting, like bubbles popping.

“Rocco,” Mama reminded gently from the doorway. “Potty first. You can drum later.”

Rocco’s ears lowered. He felt a tiny squeeze in his chest. He wanted to play now. He took a deep breath in and blew it out slow, like blowing a bubble. “Potty first,” he repeated to himself. He did his potty job. “I did it!” he cheered.

Mama turned the timer to the next pink ten. “Teeth and hands.”

Rocco grabbed his toothbrush. He loved the shiny rainbow handle. He started to brush, up and down, round and round. The minty taste made his tongue tingle. “Mmm! Spicy mint!” he giggled.

He began to tap the toothbrush on the sink again. Tap, tap, ting! The sound was so bright! “I can make a brushing beat,” he said. “Tap and brush and tap and—”

Oops! A blob of toothpaste plopped onto his nose. It felt cold and sticky. Rocco’s eyes got watery. “This is hard!” he said. He felt frustrated, like when a song has a knot.

Mama handed him a soft tissue. “It’s okay to feel upset,” she said in a calm voice. “Let’s try our calm steps. One: pause. Two: breathe. Three: try again.”

Rocco paused. He breathed in through his nose and out through his mouth. “Pffff.” He wiped his nose. His chest felt less tight. “Try again,” he whispered.

He brushed slowly this time. One-two, one-two, like a gentle drum. He counted, “One, two, three, four,” and the counting helped. When he finished, he rinsed, then washed his hands with warm, soapy water. The bubbles were soft like clouds. They smelled like oranges. He rubbed palms, backs, fingers, and thumbs, singing, “Rub-a-dub, one-two-three!”

The timer’s sand ran out with a soft hush. Mama smiled. “Great time use, Rocco. Ten minutes left to practice.”

Rocco’s tail flicked happily. He stuck the little drum to the side of the tub. Thwop! He put the rubber duck nearby and a comb on the counter. He turned the water to a tiny drip for a metronome beat. Drip. Drip. Drip.

He tried to play fast. Boom-boom-boom! Tap-tap-tap! But the sounds bumped into each other. “It doesn’t sound right!” he said. His cheeks felt hot.

Mama held up her hand. “Remember your plan. Use the drip as your steady beat. Start slow. Practice makes strong music.”

Rocco nodded. He breathed in. He whispered, “Slow is okay.” He listened to the drip. Drip… drip… drip… He tapped the drum on each drip. Boom… boom… boom… Then he clicked the comb lightly in between. Click… click… click…

His ears perked. It sounded smooth now. Rocco added the duck squeak for a happy accent. Squeak! He laughed. “It works!”

From the hallway, neighbors passed by and waved. Mr. Badger, who used a shiny cane, called, “Nice rhythm, Rocco!” Ms. Sparrow, with colorful feathers, chirped, “Great focus!” Baby Panda clapped tiny paws. Rocco loved that his friends were all different and all kind. Music was for everyone.

The timer finished. Mama hugged Rocco. “You followed your plan. You paused when frustrated. You practiced slowly. Now we have time for the park.”

Rocco looked in the mirror. His whiskers were neat. His teeth were bright. His paws were clean. He felt proud. “I used my time like a song,” he said. “First the quiet parts, then the music.”

As they left, Rocco sang his Bubble Beat: “Plan it, do it, breathe and try. Use your time and reach the sky!”

The lesson floated in his heart like a warm bubble: When he made a plan, took calm breaths, and practiced slowly, he could do hard things and still have time for fun.

The End.
