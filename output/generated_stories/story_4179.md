# Beary Calm in the Basement Lab

In a cozy house on a quiet street, there was a basement lab. It was not a scary lab. It was a bright, friendly place with soft lights, colorful buttons, and shelves of jars. Some jars held sparkly sand. Some jars held crunchy leaves. Some jars held round, bouncy balls. The air smelled like lemon soap and warm cocoa.

A friendly bear cub named Beary lived there with friends who loved to learn. Beary had soft brown fur, round ears, and a tiny blue backpack. Beary’s friends were all different and special. Tiko the turtle wore shiny glasses. Nala the fox had a fluffy tail and painted spots of many colors. Pip the penguin waddled and laughed with a snort.

“Today,” said Nala, wagging her tail, “we test our music machine!”

The music machine sat on a table. It had big red buttons and rainbow lights. It made sounds like “ding,” “bop,” and “twinkle-twinkle.” Beary’s eyes grew wide. “I love music!” Beary said.

Tiko put on safety goggles. “We try one button at a time,” he said calmly. “Slow and steady.”

Beary felt excited. Beary’s paws wiggled. “Can I press the big red button?” Beary asked.

“Sure,” said Pip. “We take turns.”

Beary pressed the big red button. BOOM-BOOM went the bass. Lights flashed blue, green, and purple. The room buzzed. It felt like bubbles in soda. Beary bounced. “Fun!”

“Now my turn,” said Nala. She pressed a yellow button. Tinkle-tinkle like tiny bells floated in the air. It smelled sweet, like oranges. Pip clapped.

Then Pip pressed a green button. The machine went “whoosh!” A gentle breeze tickled their fur and feathers. Tiko smiled. “Nice.”

Beary felt more and more excited. Beary wanted to press all the buttons. Beary’s heart thumped fast. Beary’s paws squeezed into fists. Beary forgot to wait.

“I want again!” Beary shouted, and pressed three buttons at once.

The music machine blinked. Beep! Beep! BEEP! Lights flashed too fast. The sound got very loud. RATTLE-CLANG-WAAAH! The jars shook. The sparkly sand swirled into the air like glittery snow. The breeze turned into a whirl. Pip fell on his belly. Nala’s ears flattened. Tiko covered his ears and said, “Too much!”

Beary felt a hot, heavy feeling in the chest. “Oh no,” Beary whispered. “I made a mess.”

The machine kept blaring. Beary wanted to run away. Beary’s eyes felt watery. The room smelled like dust and lemon. The lights made Beary’s head feel spinny.

Tiko touched Beary’s paw gently. “We can fix this,” he said. “Let’s help our bodies feel calm first.”

Nala nodded. “We can try our calm steps.”

Pip waddled close. “We do it together.”

Beary blinked. “Calm steps?”

Tiko pointed to a friendly poster on the wall. It had pictures and big, simple words.

Step 1: Stop.  
Step 2: Breathe.  
Step 3: Squeeze and release.  
Step 4: Ask for help.  
Step 5: Try again, slow.

Beary took a small step back. “Stop,” Beary said softly.

Beary closed eyes. “Breathe in… smell the cocoa.” Beary breathed in through the nose. The warm cocoa smell made Beary’s shoulders loosen. “Breathe out… blow the bubbles.” Beary blew out slowly, like blowing bubbles. The loudness felt a tiny bit less scary.

“Good,” said Nala. She breathed with Beary. In… out… in… out. Pip breathed too, making a little “hoo” sound. Tiko breathed slow and steady.

“Now squeeze,” said Pip. Beary hugged the blue backpack tight. Squeeze… one, two, three. Release… one, two, three. Beary felt the tight hot feeling melt like ice in sunshine.

Beary opened eyes. The lights still flashed, but Beary felt ready. “Help, please,” Beary said.

“We are here,” said Tiko. “I will turn the volume knob to low.”

“I will press the soft bell button,” said Nala. “It helps the big sounds rest.”

“I will catch the sparkly sand with this towel,” said Pip.

Beary nodded. “I will switch off the whirl,” Beary said, remembering the small blue switch.

They moved slow and careful. Click. Click. Click. The sound softened: clink… tinkle… hush. The lights slowed to a gentle glow. The breeze faded. The sparkly sand settled like quiet snow on the towel.

Beary took one more deep breath. “Thank you,” Beary said.

Tiko smiled. “You used self-regulation.”

“What is that?” asked Beary.

“It means you helped your body and feelings,” said Nala. “You noticed big feelings, you paused, you breathed, and you asked for help.”

“It means you can choose,” said Pip. “You can choose calm.”

Beary’s ears perked up. “I can choose calm,” Beary repeated.

They cleaned together. Pip swept. Nala wiped jars. Tiko checked buttons. Beary picked up the bouncy balls, one by one. The basement lab looked shiny and neat again.

“Let’s try again,” said Tiko. “This time, slow and steady. We take turns. We listen to each other. We can all like different sounds.”

“I like deep boom,” said Pip.

“I like tiny bells,” said Nala.

“I like whoosh,” said Tiko.

Beary smiled. “I like them all. I like that we are different.” Beary paused and patted the chest. “I also like to be calm.”

They made a plan. One button at a time. Count to three between turns. Use a calm breath if the sounds feel big. Beary pressed one red button. BOOM… pause… breathe. Nala pressed yellow. Tinkle… pause… smile. Tiko pressed green. Whoosh… pause… nod. Pip pressed a purple star and sang, “La-la!”

The music was gentle and happy. The lights glowed like fireflies. The air smelled like cocoa and oranges. Beary’s heart beat soft and steady.

Beary looked at friends. “We did it together,” Beary said.

“Yes,” said Tiko. “We were open-minded. We listened to different ideas and sounds.”

“Yes,” said Nala. “We showed diversity. We are different, and we belong.”

“Yes,” said Pip. “We used self-regulation. We chose calm.”

Beary hugged each friend. “When I feel too big inside,” Beary said, “I will stop, breathe, squeeze, ask for help, and try again.”

All the friends nodded. The basement lab felt safe and warm.

The End.
