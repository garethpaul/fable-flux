# Ollie's Rainbow Garden Adventure

Ollie the otter loved to splash and play, but today something special caught his eye. From his favorite pond, he could see Mrs. Chen's colorful balcony garden high above. The bright flowers seemed to dance in the morning sun, and Ollie's whiskers twitched with curiosity.

"I wonder what makes that garden so beautiful," Ollie said to himself, his sleek brown fur glistening as he climbed out of the water.

With a playful bounce, Ollie scampered up the fire escape to the balcony. When he peeked over the railing, his dark eyes grew wide with wonder. The garden was like a rainbow! Red tomatoes hung heavy on green vines, purple eggplants sat proudly in clay pots, and yellow sunflowers stretched toward the sky.

"Oh my!" gasped Mrs. Chen, nearly dropping her watering can. "A little visitor!"

Ollie's nose twitched nervously. "I'm sorry! I just wanted to see your amazing garden. It smells so wonderful – like sunshine and earth and growing things!"

Mrs. Chen smiled warmly. "Don't be sorry, little otter. I'm happy to share! Would you like to learn about my plants?"

Ollie clapped his small paws together. "Yes, please!"

Mrs. Chen pointed to different sections of her garden. "These red peppers came from seeds my grandmother brought from Mexico. The purple cabbage grows from seeds my neighbor shared from Korea. And these orange marigolds? My friend from India taught me how to plant them!"

"Wow!" Ollie's eyes sparkled. "So many different places!"

"That's right," Mrs. Chen said, gently touching a bright yellow squash. "Just like people, plants come from all over the world. When we grow them together, they make the garden more beautiful and stronger."

Ollie watched a busy bee buzz from a white jasmine flower to a pink rose. "Look! The bee doesn't care what color the flowers are – it visits them all!"

"Very smart, little otter!" Mrs. Chen laughed. "The bee knows that different flowers give different kinds of sweet nectar. Diversity makes everything better."

Mrs. Chen showed Ollie how the tall sunflowers protected the smaller herbs from strong wind, and how the deep roots of some plants helped water reach others. "When different plants grow together, they help each other," she explained.

Ollie touched the soil with his paw – it felt cool and rich. "It's like they're all friends!"

"Exactly!" Mrs. Chen said. "Would you like to help me plant something new?"

Together, they planted tiny basil seeds from Italy in a small pot. Ollie carefully patted the dark soil with his paws while Mrs. Chen sprinkled water that caught the sunlight like tiny diamonds.

"These seeds will grow into something delicious," Mrs. Chen said. "And now this garden has a little bit of Italy too!"

As Ollie prepared to leave, Mrs. Chen handed him a small tomato, warm from the sun. "Thank you for visiting my rainbow garden, Ollie. Remember – just like my plants, when different friends come together, they make the world more beautiful."

Ollie took a sweet, juicy bite of the tomato. "I'll remember! And I'll tell all my friends that being different makes us special!"

He waved goodbye and slid down the fire escape with a happy splash into his pond. From now on, whenever Ollie looked up at the colorful balcony garden, he would remember that diversity – like a rainbow – makes everything more wonderful.

The End.
