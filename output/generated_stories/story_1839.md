# Milo Mouse's Cozy Home Problem

In a warm, dark closet behind hanging coats, little Milo Mouse had built the perfect hideaway. Soft fabric scraps made his bed. A bottle cap held his water. Everything was just right in his tiny home.

But one rainy morning, Milo woke up to a big problem. Drip, drip, drip! Water leaked through a crack in the closet wall. His cozy bed was getting wet!

"Oh no!" squeaked Milo, his whiskers twitching with worry. "My home is flooding! What can I do?"

The water puddle grew bigger. His fabric scraps floated like tiny boats. Milo's cheese crumbs were getting soggy too.

First, Milo tried to catch the water with his tiny paws. Splash! The drops were too fast and too many. His little paws couldn't hold enough water.

"This isn't working," Milo said, shaking water from his fur. "I need a better plan."

Then Milo remembered something important. His grandmother always said, "When you have a problem, look around. The answer might be hiding nearby!"

Milo looked up, down, and all around his closet home. He saw old shoes, winter scarves, and... wait! A small plastic cup had fallen from someone's coat pocket.

"Perfect!" squeaked Milo excitedly. He pushed and pulled the cup under the dripping water. Plink, plink, plink! The drops landed safely in the cup instead of on his bed.

But Milo had another problem. The cup was filling up fast. Soon it would overflow too!

Milo scratched his head with his tiny paw. Then he spotted a long, thin scarf hanging nearby. He had an idea!

Carefully, Milo climbed up the scarf like a rope. He tied one end around the cup handle. Then he made a pulley system using a coat hanger above.

"If I can lift the cup when it's full," Milo said to himself, "I can pour the water into that old boot over there!"

Working together with his tools, Milo pulled the scarf rope. Up went the heavy cup! He swung it over to the boot and tipped it carefully. Splash! The water poured out safely.

"Hooray!" cheered Milo. "It's working!"

But just then, he heard a voice. "Hello? Is someone in there?"

Milo froze. It was Sarah, the little girl who lived in the house. She had heard his squeaking and splashing.

"Please don't be scared," said Sarah gently, peeking into the closet. "I just want to help."

Milo's heart raced. But Sarah's voice was kind and soft. Maybe she really did want to help.

"I'm Milo," he squeaked bravely. "I live here, and my home is leaking!"

Sarah smiled. "Hi Milo! You're so clever with your cup and rope! But I think I know how to fix that leak for good."

Working together, Sarah helped Milo seal the crack with some tape. Milo showed her his resourceful water-catching system. Sarah was amazed at how smart the little mouse was.

"You solved that problem all by yourself!" Sarah said proudly. "You used what you had around you and didn't give up."

From that day on, Milo's closet home stayed dry and cozy. He learned that being resourceful meant looking around for solutions and using his creativity. And sometimes, talking peacefully to others could bring even better help!

Milo's whiskers twitched happily as he settled into his warm, dry bed. "Every problem has a solution," he whispered. "I just need to be clever and brave enough to find it."

The End.
