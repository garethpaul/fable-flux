# Ollie Otter and the Balcony Garden Helpers

Ollie the playful otter lived with Nana on the tenth floor. Outside their door was a sunny balcony garden. It had pots of mint, tomatoes, tiny strawberries, and a big blue watering can. Bees hummed. Leaves rustled. The air smelled like fresh soil and sweet mint.

“Good morning, garden!” Ollie sang, wiggling his whiskers. He loved to splash, but today he wanted to help. He patted the soft soil with his paw. “I can be gentle,” he said. “I can say what I need.”

Nana brought a small apron. “Ollie, this is for you,” she said, tying it carefully. “We will water the plants. We will share. We will take turns.”

Ollie smiled. “Nana, I want to help. Can I have my own little job?” he asked in a clear voice.

“That is good self-advocacy, Ollie,” Nana said. “You used your words. Yes. You may be the Mint Monitor.”

Ollie puffed with pride. “I am the Mint Monitor!” He sniffed the mint leaves. “Mmm! They smell cool, like a tiny breeze.”

The neighbor children, Mei and Leo, came out to their own balcony. “Hi, Ollie!” they called across the rail. “Our watering can is stuck. The top is tight. We can’t water our beans.”

Ollie looked at Nana. He put his paw on his chest. “Nana, I want to help Mei and Leo,” he said. “But I also want to keep my Mint Monitor job.”

Nana nodded. “You can say what you need and make a plan. Tell them clearly.”

Ollie waved. “Mei, Leo! I want to help you. I also need to water the mint. Can we do both?”

“Yes, please!” Mei said. “How?”

Ollie thought. He looked at the blue watering can. He looked at a soft rubber jar grip on the shelf. “I have an idea,” he said. “We can use the grip. We can twist together. That is innovation!”

Nana passed the grip with a smile. Ollie spoke up again. “I need one person to hold. I need one person to twist with me. Nana, can you steady the can? Mei, can you hold the handle? Leo, can you twist with me?”

“Okay!” said Leo. “I will twist with you, Ollie.”

“Ready,” said Nana.

Ollie and Leo wrapped the grip around the lid. “Twist, twist,” Ollie said. The lid squeaked. Squeeeak! Then—pop! Off it came.

“You did it!” Mei cheered. The children clapped. The bees hummed louder, as if they clapped too.

Ollie giggled. “My paws feel tingly!” He shook them out. “Now I need to water the mint,” he said. “That’s my job.”

Mei nodded. “We can wait. Thank you for telling us what you need.”

Ollie’s heart felt warm. He watered his mint with gentle drips. Drip, drip, drip. The leaves shivered and sparkled. “How do you feel, mint?” he asked.

The mint did not talk, but it smelled even sweeter. Ollie smiled. “I think you say thank you with your smell,” he whispered.

Then Ollie turned back to Mei and Leo. “Now we help your beans,” he said. “We can share our water.”

They took turns with the can. The water made soft patter sounds on the soil. Patter, patter. The bean leaves looked shiny and happy.

Suddenly, a little ladybug landed on Ollie’s nose. “Oh!” Ollie blinked and giggled. “Hello, tiny friend.” The ladybug tickled his fur and flew to a tomato leaf.

Mei pointed. “Our tomato plant leans. It looks tired.”

Ollie watched the plant sway. He felt a small worry in his tummy. “I want to help,” he said. “But I need something. I need a stick and a string.”

Nana brought a wooden stick and soft plant tie. “Tell us your plan, Ollie,” she said.

Ollie spoke slowly. “I will hold the tomato up. I need someone to hold the stick. I need someone to tie the soft tie. We will be gentle.”

“I can hold the stick,” said Mei.

“I can tie,” said Leo.

Ollie lifted the tomato stem with careful paws. “Soft and slow,” he said. Mei held the stick next to the stem. Leo tied the bow. The bow was loose and kind, like a hug. The tomato stood tall.

“Thank you, team,” Ollie said. “Thank you for listening when I asked.”

“Thank you for asking in a kind way,” Nana said. “That is self-advocacy. You used your words to share your needs. You helped others. You found new ideas.”

Mei smiled. “We are grateful, Ollie.”

Leo nodded. “I am grateful too.”

Ollie looked around the balcony garden. He heard bees hum. He felt the warm sun on his fur. He smelled mint and wet soil. He tasted a tiny strawberry Nana picked just for him. Sweet! His heart felt big.

Ollie turned to Nana. “Nana, today I learned something,” he said.

“What did you learn?” Nana asked.

Ollie stood tall. “When I say what I need, people can help me. When I listen to others, I can help them too. We can make gentle plans. We can try new ideas. We can say thank you.”

Nana hugged him. “Yes, Ollie. That is the lesson.”

Ollie waved to Mei and Leo. “Thank you, friends!”

“Thank you, Ollie!” they called back.

The balcony garden glowed in the soft afternoon light. The mint was happy. The beans were watered. The tomato stood proud. Ollie’s paws felt calm. His smile felt bright.

“We did it together,” Ollie said.

The End.
