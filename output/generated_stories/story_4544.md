# Mimi Builds a Table Town

Mimi was a kind little mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. She lived behind a warm oven in a sunny dining room. Every morning, the dining room smelled like toast and jam. The wooden table felt smooth, and the chairs made gentle scraping sounds on the floor. Mimi loved that sound. It meant a new day and a new idea.

One day, Mimi peeked out and squeaked, “Today, I will build!” She wiggled her whiskers. She wanted to build a tiny Mouse Town on the dining room table. She wanted streets and bridges and a tall, safe tower.

Mimi gathered her building things. She rolled a clean napkin like a soft road. She stacked sugar cubes into a little tower—one, two, three. She lined up crayons from the pencil cup like colorful logs—red, blue, green, yellow. She pressed her paw on the table and felt it cool and smooth. It made her feel calm and ready.

“First, I plan,” Mimi said to herself. She took a tiny notebook and a stubby pencil from her pouch. She loved writing her ideas. Scratch, scratch went the pencil. Mimi drew a picture of her Mouse Town. She wrote, “Road. Bridge. Tower. Park.” Her letters were wiggly but clear. The writing helped her brain feel steady.

Mimi started with the road. “Slow and gentle,” she whispered. She laid the napkin road flat and straight. Next, she made a little park with a green leaf. It smelled fresh, like morning rain. Her whiskers tingled. “I’m building with care,” she said.

Just then, Thump! Thump! A friendly puppy named Bingo trotted in. His tail wagged like a happy broom. “Hi, Mimi!” barked Bingo, nose wet and shiny. “What are you doing?”

“I’m building Mouse Town,” Mimi said. “Please walk slow. My tower is small and new.”

Bingo sniffed the sugar cubes. “Ooh, sweet!” He wagged harder. The table wobbled. The sugar-cube tower shook—wobble, wobble—then tumble! Cubes scattered like tiny snow.

Mimi’s heart went fast. Her ears drooped. “Oh no,” she said. She felt hot tears trying to come.

Bingo’s ears fell flat. “I’m sorry,” he whispered. “I didn’t mean to. I got too excited.”

Mimi took a big, slow breath in. She held it. Then she let it out, slow like a soft wind. “I can calm my body,” she told herself. She placed her paw on her chest. Thump, thump. “I can try again.”

She looked at Bingo’s sad eyes. Mimi felt a warm, kind feeling. “I know you didn’t mean it,” she said. “Thank you for saying sorry. Do you want to help me build? We can build with slow paws and slow paws,” she giggled.

Bingo’s tail wagged softly now. “Yes, please,” he said. “I can be gentle.”

“Let’s write a plan,” Mimi said. She opened her notebook. “Rule one: Slow steps. Rule two: Gentle paws. Rule three: We ask before we move something.” Scratch, scratch. She drew a smile next to the rules.

“Rules help me remember,” Bingo said. He sat and panted quietly. His breath was warm and soft.

Mimi showed him how to stack the cubes. “One at a time,” she said. “Feel your paws. Breathe.” Together, they placed cube on cube. The tower stood tall again, four cubes high. “We did it!” Mimi cheered.

They built a bridge from two crayons and a spoon. The spoon made a shiny arch. Mimi touched the spoon. It felt cool and heavy. “Strong bridge,” she said. Bingo placed a small paper card to make a sign. Mimi wrote on it in tiny letters: “Bridge. Go slow.” She smiled. Writing helped her feel safe and clear.

A small breeze came from the open window. The napkin road fluttered. “Oh!” Mimi said. “The wind is strong.” She held the road. Bingo gently closed the window, click. The breeze stopped. “Thank you,” Mimi said. “You noticed the wind. That helps our building.”

They kept working. They made a tiny park bench from two crackers and a stick. Crunchy crumbs sprinkled the table. “Crumbs look like sand!” Bingo laughed. Mimi giggled and used a small brush to sweep a neat path. “I like clean paths,” she said. “Clean helps me think.”

When the town was almost done, Mimi felt a little tired. Her paws shook. She paused and placed both paws flat on the table. “Pause and breathe,” she reminded herself. In, hold, out. Calm spread in her tummy. “Now I’m ready.”

At last, Mouse Town was complete. The tower stood tall. The bridge was strong. The road was smooth. The park was green and tidy. Mimi and Bingo sat quietly and looked. The room smelled like warm wood and a little sweet sugar. The table felt cool and steady under their paws.

“It’s beautiful,” Bingo whispered.

“It is,” Mimi said. “We built it together.”

Mimi opened her notebook one more time. She wrote, “We planned. We were gentle. We asked. We breathed. We fixed mistakes. We built.” She showed Bingo. “These words tell our story.”

Bingo smiled. “Today I learned to go slow. I learned to say sorry. I learned to help.”

Mimi nodded. “I learned that building needs a plan. Building needs calm. Building needs kind friends.”

They placed a tiny paper flag at the top of the tower. Mimi wrote one word on it: “Care.” The flag rustled softly.

Mimi and Bingo bumped noses. “Teamwork,” they said together.

The End.
