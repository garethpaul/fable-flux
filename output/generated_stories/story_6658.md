# Miko’s Smart Morning Plan

Miko was a kind little mouse who lived in a cozy home office. The home office smelled like warm paper and fresh pencils. The desk was a mountain to Miko, with soft sticky notes like colorful leaves. The computer hummed a gentle song, “Bzzz-zz.”

“Good morning,” Miko squeaked. “Today I will do many things!” His whiskers twitched with excitement.

He looked at his list. It was written in big, simple pictures. A sun for morning tidy. A book for story time. A clock for practice time. A star for snack and rest. Miko smiled. “I need a plan,” he said. “A plan helps me use my time.”

Just then, Click the keyboard made a tiny clack, clack. Paper the notepad rustled softly. “Play with us now!” they seemed to say.

Miko took a deep breath. He pressed his paws together. “I will use a strategy,” he said. “First, I tidy. Next, I tell a story. Then I practice my new words. After that, I have a crunchy carrot snack.”

He set a small timer made from a bottle cap and a bead. “Ding in five minutes,” he said. “That will help me manage time.”

Miko began to tidy. He stacked paper squares. He lined up three pencils: red, blue, green. Tap. Tap. Tap. He put the paper clips together like tiny silver fish. Click. Click.

“Almost done,” Miko said. Just then, his friend, Dottie the dust bunny, bounced from under the printer. “Poof!” Dottie said with a smile. “Let’s play chase! Now, now, now!”

Miko looked at his plan. He looked at Dottie. He wanted to be kind. He also wanted to finish his tidy task.

“I have an idea,” Miko said. “Let’s make a compromise.”

“What’s a com-pro-mise?” Dottie asked, her soft fluff shining in the light.

“A compromise is when we find a way that works for both of us,” Miko explained. “I tidy for two more minutes. Then we play a quick game that also helps my plan.”

Dottie blinked. “Okay! But what game?”

“Let’s play ‘Find the Red, Blue, Green,’” Miko said. “You help me find the colored pencils and put them in a row. That is play and tidy together!”

Dottie clapped her fuzzy paws. “Yes!”

They searched. “Red!” Dottie called, rolling the red pencil with her poofy belly. “Blue!” Miko squeaked, lifting the blue pencil with both paws. “Green!” they said together, giggling. The pencils clicked into place. The timer went, “Ding!”

“Tidy is done,” Miko said proudly. “Strategy worked!”

“Now play more?” Dottie asked.

“Next is story time,” Miko said, pointing to the book picture. “But you can be in the story!”

Dottie’s eyes sparkled. “Ooooh.”

They hopped onto the soft mouse-sized chair. The chair smelled like warm sunshine. Miko opened a tiny notebook. He used a big, friendly voice.

“Once there was a brave dust bunny,” Miko began, “who helped a kind mouse find colors in a busy office.”

“I am brave!” Dottie giggled.

Miko added fun sounds. “The computer went hummm. The pencils went tap-tap. The clock went tick-tock.” He wiggled his whiskers and smiled at Dottie. “Words are wonderful,” he said. “I love how they sound.”

“I love words too,” Dottie said. “Can we add a new word?”

“Yes,” Miko replied. “Our new word is strategy. It means a smart plan to help us do our tasks.”

They said it together, slow and proud. “Stra-te-gy.”

Miko drew a big heart next to the word. “Story time helps us love language,” he said.

The bottle-cap timer sat quietly. Miko checked his plan. “Now it is practice time,” he said. He pointed to the clock picture. “I will practice pressing keys on the keyboard. One-two-three.”

Dottie looked worried. “Will I be bored?”

Miko thought. He wanted Dottie to feel happy. He also wanted to practice. He used his strategy again. “Compromise time,” he said gently. “I press three keys. Then you press three keys. We take turns. That helps us share time.”

Dottie smiled. “Deal!”

Miko pressed, “Tap, tap, tap.” Dottie pressed, “Pff-pff” with her soft fluff. They counted together. “One, two, three!” They waited between turns. Waiting felt hard. Miko’s tail wiggled. But he breathed slowly. “I can wait,” he whispered. “Waiting is part of my plan.”

After three rounds, Miko checked his list. “Last is snack and rest,” he said. He took a tiny carrot stick from a thimble cup. Crunch! It was bright and sweet. He shared a crumb with Dottie. “Thank you for helping,” he said.

Dottie snuggled close. “Your plan was smart,” she whispered. “I like strategy. I like compromise. And I like stories with sounds.”

Miko looked around the neat home office. The pencils were lined up. The paper was stacked. The keyboard smiled with little squares. The room felt calm and happy.

“I used strategy to plan my time,” Miko said. “I used compromise to be kind to a friend. I used storytelling to enjoy words and sounds. That made our day work.”

Dottie yawned a tiny poof. “Can we do it again tomorrow?”

“Yes,” Miko said. “We can make a plan each day.”

He added a star next to his list. “Star means: We did it!”

They listened to the quiet hum. They felt proud and peaceful.

The lesson: A smart plan, kind compromise, and loving words help us do our tasks and have fun together.

The End.
