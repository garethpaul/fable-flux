# Melody's Messy Art Room

Melody the little blue bird loved to sing in the bright art room at Sunny Day School. Every morning, she would perch on the windowsill and chirp happy songs while the children painted and drew.

But today, when Melody flew into the art room, she gasped. "Oh my!" she tweeted. Paint brushes were scattered everywhere. Colorful papers covered the floor like a rainbow carpet. Glue bottles lay on their sides, and crayons rolled around the tables.

"What a mess!" Melody sang sadly. She had promised Mrs. Chen, the art teacher, that she would help keep the room tidy. But yesterday, Melody had been so excited about learning a new song that she forgot all about her promise.

Melody's friend Tommy the turtle poked his green head through the doorway. "Wow, Melody! This room looks like a tornado came through!"

Melody's little heart felt heavy. "I was supposed to help clean up yesterday," she chirped quietly. "But I flew away to practice my singing instead. Now look what happened!"

Tommy slowly walked into the room, his shell clicking softly on the floor. "Hmm," he said thoughtfully. "Let's think about this step by step. What do you think we should do first?"

Melody tilted her feathery head. She looked around the messy room with her bright black eyes. "Well," she sang softly, "I think I should say sorry to Mrs. Chen first. Then maybe... maybe we could clean up together?"

"That sounds like a smart plan!" Tommy smiled. "But this is a really big mess. How do you think we should tackle it?"

Melody hopped from table to table, thinking hard. The room smelled like sweet paint and fruity markers. She could hear children laughing in the hallway. Soon they would come for art class!

"I have an idea!" Melody chirped excitedly. "We could sort things into groups. All the brushes in one pile, all the crayons in another pile, and all the papers in a stack!"

"Brilliant thinking!" Tommy nodded. "That will make it much easier."

Just then, Mrs. Chen walked in carrying her morning coffee. The warm, cozy smell filled the air. When she saw the mess, her eyes grew wide.

"Oh dear," Mrs. Chen said softly.

Melody flew down and landed gently on Mrs. Chen's shoulder. "Mrs. Chen, I'm very sorry," she sang in her sweetest voice. "I forgot to help clean yesterday, and now the room is messy. But Tommy and I have a plan to fix it!"

Mrs. Chen smiled and gently stroked Melody's soft feathers. "Thank you for apologizing, little one. I appreciate your honesty. What's your plan?"

Melody explained their sorting idea. Mrs. Chen nodded approvingly. "That's excellent critical thinking! You broke down a big problem into smaller, manageable pieces."

Together, the three friends worked as a team. Melody used her beak to pick up crayons. Tommy gathered papers with his strong flippers. Mrs. Chen organized the paint supplies. They sang cleanup songs and told funny stories while they worked.

Soon, the art room sparkled like new. The brushes stood proudly in their holders. Crayons lay neatly in their boxes. Papers were stacked in perfect piles.

"We did it!" Melody sang joyfully, doing a little loop in the air.

"And just in time," Mrs. Chen said as the first children arrived for art class.

Melody perched on her favorite windowsill, her heart feeling light and happy. She had learned something important today: when you make a mistake, the best thing to do is apologize, think carefully about how to fix it, and work together with friends.

From that day on, Melody never forgot her cleaning promise again. And whenever there was a big problem to solve, she remembered to think step by step, just like she did in the messy art room.

The End.
