# Hop, Paint, and Listen

Bunny Roo was a determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. Today, Bunny Roo hopped into the creative art room. The room smelled like fresh paper and orange glue. Sunlight danced on the floor. Paints, brushes, big paper rolls, and tiny bells were on the tables.

“I am ready to make art!” Bunny Roo said. “I will paint a rainbow that moves!”

Ms. Dot, the friendly art teacher, smiled. She wore a big blue apron with dots like blueberries. “Today we will paint with our bodies,” Ms. Dot said. “We will move, and we will listen. Our movements will make our marks.”

Bunny Roo’s ears stood tall. “Move and paint? I can do that!” Bunny Roo felt excited. His feet wiggled.

Ms. Dot clapped softly. “First, we warm up. Wiggle fingers, wiggle toes. Stretch tall like a tree. Now curl small like a pebble.”

Bunny Roo stretched tall. He felt his ears brush the air. He curled small. He felt the cool floor on his paws.

“Now,” Ms. Dot said, “when you hear the tiny bell—ding—we hop. When you hear my drum—boom—we freeze like a statue. We listen to know what to do.”

“I can listen,” Bunny Roo said. He wanted to try his best.

Ding! The bell sounded like a raindrop. Bunny Roo hopped. His paws thumped softly. He felt happy.

Boom! The drum sounded like thunder. Bunny Roo froze. He held still. His ears trembled a little. He felt nervous but proud for listening.

“Great listening,” Ms. Dot said. “Now, we will paint with movement! Here is a long paper path.” She rolled out a long white sheet. It whispered across the floor. “We will put paint on our feet. We will hop, slide, and tiptoe.”

Bunny Roo looked at the paint trays. Red, blue, yellow, and green swirled like a rainbow soup. He dipped one paw in blue. It felt cold and squishy. “Brr!” he giggled.

“Remember,” Ms. Dot said gently, “move slowly first. Listen to your body. If you feel wiggly or worried, take a slow breath.”

Bunny Roo nodded. He wanted his rainbow to move. He put one paw on the paper. Splat! A blue paw print bloomed. “Wow!”

Ding! Bunny Roo hopped-hopped-hopped, making dots of blue. The room filled with soft thumps and happy squeaks.

Boom! Bunny Roo froze. His chest puffed. He held still. He listened. His heart went ba-dump, ba-dump. He felt calm.

Then it happened. Bunny Roo saw the yellow tray. “I want yellow hops!” he said, and he hopped too fast. Slide—whoops! His paw slipped. He fell on his fluffy tail and smudged the paper. A big green smear spread where blue and yellow mixed.

Bunny Roo’s eyes got wet. “Oh no. I ruined it,” he whispered. His tummy felt twisty.

Ms. Dot knelt beside him. “Take a slow breath with me,” she said. They breathed in. They breathed out. The room smelled like lemons and paint.

“How do you feel?” Ms. Dot asked.

“I feel sad,” Bunny Roo said. “And a little mad at my slip.”

“It’s okay to feel sad and mad,” Ms. Dot said softly. “Let’s name the feeling and listen to it. Your body is telling you to slow down. What can we try next?”

Bunny Roo wiped a tear with a painty paw. He looked at the green smear. It glowed like grass after rain. “Maybe the smear can be the start of a grassy hill,” he said. “I can hop up the hill slowly.”

“That is a helpful idea,” Ms. Dot said. “You are persistent. You keep trying.”

Bunny Roo dipped a paw in yellow again, but only a little. He listened for the bell.

Ding. Hop. Splat. Hop. Splat.

Boom. Freeze. Breathe. Smile.

He moved with care. He felt the cool paper. He heard brushes swish and bells ding. He smelled sweet glue and soapy water. He tasted a tiny puff of dust and giggled.

A friend, Mia Mouse, tugged a tiny brush. “Can I help?” she squeaked.

“Yes!” Bunny Roo said. “Please paint sunshine at the top of my hill.”

Mia made small yellow circles. Bunny Roo made little green hops up the hill. Then he tiptoed in red around the sun. Tip-tip-tip. Red footprints became a dancing path.

Ms. Dot beat the drum softly. Boom. Boom. “Freeze,” she said.

Everyone froze. Bunny Roo listened to his breath. In-out. In-out. His body felt steady.

“Now make waves with your arms,” Ms. Dot called. “Your arms can guide your feet.”

Bunny Roo waved his arms slow like seaweed. His feet slid gently. Shhh… His footprints made a curvy blue river. He felt proud. He felt calm. He was moving and listening at the same time.

A little friend, Theo Turtle, frowned at his own paper. “I can’t do it,” Theo said.

Bunny Roo hopped over carefully. “I slipped before,” he said kindly. “I felt sad and mad. I took a breath. I moved slow. Want to try together?”

Theo nodded. Bunny Roo counted. “One, two, breathe. Hop.” They made tiny dots together. Dot-dot, dot-dot. “Great listening, Theo!” Bunny Roo cheered.

At last, the big paper showed a rainbow that moved—green hills, a blue river, a red dancing path, and yellow sunshine. It looked alive. It looked joyful.

Ms. Dot clapped. “You used movement to make art. You listened to sounds, to your bodies, and to your feelings. You were helpful and you kept trying.”

Bunny Roo’s heart felt warm. “I learned that moving can help me make art,” he said. “When I listen and breathe, my body knows what to do. If I slip, I can try again. I can help friends too.”

Everyone cheered. Ding! Boom! Giggle!

The End.
