# Daisy's Dancing Day

In a cozy nursery filled with soft toys and warm sunlight, a gentle deer fawn named Daisy sat very still on her favorite blue blanket. Her spotted coat gleamed in the morning light streaming through the window.

Daisy loved to watch the mobile above her spin around and around. The colorful butterflies danced in circles, their wings catching the light. But Daisy herself never moved much. She preferred to stay put and watch.

"Time for morning exercises!" called Mama Deer from the doorway. "Come stretch with me, little one."

Daisy shook her head. "I like sitting here, Mama. Moving is too hard."

Her legs felt wobbly when she tried to stand. Her knees knocked together like wooden blocks. It was much easier to stay on her soft blanket.

Mama Deer's eyes sparkled with understanding. "Would you like to help me prepare for Baby Bear's visit today? He's coming for a playdate."

Daisy's ears perked up. She loved Baby Bear! "What do we need to do?"

"Well," Mama said, tapping her hoof thoughtfully, "we need to gather all the toy blocks from around the room. And we should move the cushions to make a cozy reading corner."

Daisy looked around the nursery. Colorful blocks were scattered everywhere - under the rocking chair, behind the toy chest, even near the window. That was a lot of moving around!

"I want to help," Daisy said quietly. "But my legs feel so shaky."

"That's okay, sweetheart," Mama smiled. "We can take tiny steps. Every little movement makes us stronger."

Daisy took a deep breath. She slowly pushed herself up from the blanket. Her legs trembled like jelly, but she didn't fall down.

"Look at you!" Mama cheered. "You're standing!"

Encouraged, Daisy took one small step toward a red block near the toy chest. Her hooves made a soft *clip-clop* sound on the wooden floor. She reached down and picked up the block with her mouth.

"I did it!" she mumbled around the block.

Step by step, Daisy moved around the nursery. Each movement felt a little easier than the last. Her legs grew steadier with every *clip-clop*. Soon she was trotting from corner to corner, gathering blocks in a wicker basket.

"Now for the cushions," Mama said.

Daisy grabbed a fluffy yellow cushion with her teeth. It was bigger than the blocks, but she had grown stronger. She dragged it across the room, her tail swishing with pride.

When Baby Bear arrived, his eyes grew wide. "Wow! This looks perfect for playing!"

"Daisy prepared everything," Mama said proudly. "She moved all around the nursery to help."

Baby Bear hugged Daisy tight. "Thank you for getting ready for me! Want to play tag?"

Daisy's eyes sparkled. "Yes! I love moving now!"

The two friends ran and jumped and danced around the cozy nursery. Daisy's legs felt strong and sure. Moving wasn't scary anymore - it was fun!

As the sun set, Daisy curled up on her blue blanket, tired but happy. Her muscles felt warm and good from all the movement.

"I'm proud of you," Mama whispered, nuzzling Daisy's spotted head. "You took initiative to help, and look how strong you've become."

Daisy smiled sleepily. Tomorrow, she would move even more. Every step made her braver and stronger, ready to help others and explore her world.

The End.
