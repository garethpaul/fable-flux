# Shelly's Sunny Garden Friends

In a bright, sunny backyard filled with colorful flowers and buzzing bees, lived a wise old tortoise named Shelly. Every morning, Shelly would slowly walk around her garden home, saying hello to all her friends.

"Good morning, Robin!" Shelly called to the red bird perched on the apple tree. The warm sun felt wonderful on her green shell.

"Good morning, Shelly!" chirped Robin happily.

Shelly continued her daily walk, greeting the busy ants marching in lines and the fluffy white clouds floating overhead. She loved her peaceful routine.

But today was different. As Shelly munched on some fresh lettuce leaves, she heard crying coming from behind the big sunflower. 

"Sniff, sniff... nobody wants to play with me," sobbed a small voice.

Shelly slowly made her way over and found a little gray mouse sitting all alone. His whiskers were wet with tears.

"Hello there," said Shelly gently. "I'm Shelly. What's your name?"

"I'm Max," whispered the mouse. "I just moved to this backyard yesterday, but all the other animals run away when they see me. They say mice are trouble."

Shelly's kind eyes grew sad. "That doesn't sound fair at all, Max. Everyone deserves friends."

Just then, Robin flew down and landed nearby. When she saw Max, she fluffed up her feathers nervously.

"Shelly, be careful! Mice eat bird eggs!" Robin warned.

Max's little shoulders shook. "I would never hurt anyone! I only eat seeds and berries. I just want friends to play with."

Shelly looked at Robin, then at Max. "Robin, did you know that Max likes the same foods you do? And look how small he is - he's probably scared too, being new here."

Robin tilted her head, thinking. She had never really talked to a mouse before.

"Max," said Shelly wisely, "why don't you tell Robin about yourself? Sometimes when we don't know someone, we feel worried. But talking helps us understand each other."

Max wiped his eyes with his tiny paws. "I love to collect pretty pebbles and sing songs. At my old home, I helped the garden by eating the bugs that hurt the plants."

"You sing?" asked Robin, suddenly interested. "I love singing too!"

Soon, more animals gathered around - Benny the rabbit, Ana the ant, and Buzz the friendly bee. At first, they stayed far away from Max, but Shelly encouraged everyone to listen.

"In my garden," said Shelly slowly and clearly, "everyone is welcome. We are all different, but that makes us special. Max is our new neighbor, and neighbors help each other."

Max showed them his collection of smooth, colorful pebbles. He sang a sweet little song about friendship. The other animals began to smile.

"I was wrong about you, Max," said Robin. "I'm sorry I judged you before getting to know you."

"That's okay," said Max, his eyes bright with hope. "Can we be friends now?"

"Yes!" chirped Robin. "Want to sing together?"

From that day on, Max became part of Shelly's garden family. Every morning during their routine walk, Shelly would smile as she watched all her friends playing together - Robin and Max singing duets, Benny sharing his carrots, and Ana showing Max the best spots to find seeds.

"When we include everyone and give them a chance," Shelly said to herself happily, "our garden becomes even more beautiful."

The End.
