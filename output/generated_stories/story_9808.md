# Ellie’s Warm-Kitchen Morning

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and bright, kind eyes. One sunny morning, Ellie padded into the warm kitchen. The room smelled like cinnamon toast and sweet oranges. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor.

“Good morning,” Ellie rumbled softly.

“Good morning, Ellie,” said Mama, stirring oatmeal. “We are making a healthy breakfast. Would you like to help?”

“Yes, please,” said Ellie. She loved helping. Helping felt warm inside, like a hug.

Ellie washed her little trunk and her big feet at the sink. The water felt cool and splashy. “I am responsible,” Ellie whispered, proud of her clean trunk.

Mama smiled. “Clean trunk, clean hands, healthy habits!”

Ellie carefully carried a bowl of blueberries to the table. Plop, plop, plop went the berries. They looked like shiny little moons. “I will share,” Ellie said, arranging them in a circle.

Just then, tap-tap-tap came a sound at the back door. It was Pip, the tiny field mouse, with a crumbly hat made from a dry leaf. “Hello!” squeaked Pip. “May I come in? My whiskers are cold.”

Ellie blinked. Pip was so small. Ellie was worried. “What if Pip eats all the blueberries?” she thought. “What if Pip is messy?” Her tummy made a wiggly feeling.

Mama opened the door with a gentle smile. Warm air hugged Pip. “Everyone is welcome in our warm kitchen,” Mama said. “We use kind words. We share. We are careful and clean.”

Pip’s eyes were bright. “I will try my best,” he said, wiping his little paws on the mat.

Ellie took a breath. The warm kitchen smelled like cinnamon and kindness. “Hello, Pip,” she said. “I am Ellie. You can sit on this napkin. It is your tiny table.”

“Thank you,” squeaked Pip. He sat very straight on the napkin. “I like blueberries, but I can wait.”

Ellie watched. Pip did not grab. Pip did not spill. Pip waited. Her wiggly worry got smaller.

Mama scooped oatmeal into bowls. “A little honey, a little fruit,” she sang. “Healthy habits help us grow.”

Ellie used her trunk to place three blueberries in Pip’s tiny bowl. “One, two, three,” she counted. “We share.”

Pip’s whiskers twitched. “Delicious! Thank you, Ellie.”

Ellie smiled. Her chest felt warm. “I can be tolerant,” she thought. “I can give Pip a chance.”

Then—oops! Ellie’s big trunk bumped the spoon. Splat! A drop of oatmeal landed on the floor. “Oh no,” Ellie said. Her ears drooped. “I made a mess.”

“That’s okay,” Mama said calmly. “We take responsibility. We clean up together.”

Ellie found a cloth. Pip held a corner with his tiny paws. They wiped the splat—swish, swish—until the floor was clean. “Teamwork,” Pip squeaked. “We can do hard things gently.”

Ellie felt proud again. “Thank you for helping, Pip.”

After breakfast, Ellie brushed her teeth—the minty bubbles felt tingly and fresh. Pip polished his whiskers with a single drop of water. Mama washed the bowls. The warm kitchen sang with little clinks and soft hums.

Suddenly, Pip sniffed the air. “I smell peanut butter,” he said.

Ellie’s eyes widened. “I don’t like peanut butter,” she said. “The smell is sticky.” Her nose wrinkled.

Pip nodded. “I love peanut butter,” he said. “It gives me energy.”

Ellie paused. She thought about kindness. She thought about sharing. “We like different things,” she said slowly. “That is okay.”

Mama set out apple slices. “We can make two snacks,” she said. “Apple with peanut butter for Pip. Apple with cinnamon for Ellie.”

Ellie looked at Pip. “I will sit on this side,” she said, pointing to a sunny spot. “The smell is softer here. You can sit near the window.”

Pip’s tail curled with joy. “Thank you for understanding, Ellie.”

Ellie took a crunchy bite. Pip took a tiny nibble. Crunch, nibble, crunch. Both smiled. They were different, and they were fine.

After snack, Pip wanted to play “Roll the Blueberry.” He rolled a blueberry across the table. It went sloooow to Ellie. Ellie rolled it back very gently. It went sloooow to Pip. They laughed. “Gentle is fun,” Ellie said.

When it was time for Pip to go, he put on his crumbly leaf hat. “Today I learned something,” Pip said. “Being clean and careful keeps everyone safe. And being patient feels nice.”

Ellie nodded. “I learned something too,” she said. “Tolerance means giving others a chance, even when they are different. We can share. We can listen. We can find a way.”

Mama hugged them both, one big trunk hug and one tiny whisker hug. “Kind hearts make a warm kitchen warmer,” she said.

The sun made golden squares again. Ellie felt proud, strong, and kind. “Tomorrow,” she said, “we can make carrot sticks!”

Pip waved. “I will bring my best manners.”

Ellie waved back. “I will bring my gentle trunk.”

They both giggled. The warm kitchen hummed with happy, healthy, helpful sounds.

The End.
