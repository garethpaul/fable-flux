# Pebble the Penguin and the Cozy Guest Room

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with a bright orange beak and tiny, happy flippers. One rainy afternoon, Pebble waddled into the cozy guest room at Grandma’s house. The guest room was warm and soft. The bed had a puffy blue blanket. The rug felt squishy under Pebble’s feet. The lamp made a gentle golden glow. Pebble loved the guest room. It smelled like clean sheets and a little like cookies.

“Hello, comfy room!” Pebble chirped.

Pebble was excited. Today, Pebble’s friend, Tiny Tern, was coming to visit. Tiny Tern was small, shy, and a little nervous. Pebble wanted Tiny Tern to feel welcome. Pebble fluffed the pillows. Pebble lined up three smooth pebbles on the windowsill. Pebble brought a bowl of sweet, juicy berries. Plop, plop, plop.

Knock, knock.

“Come in!” Pebble said.

Tiny Tern peeked in. “Hi, Pebble,” Tiny Tern whispered. Tiny Tern’s feathers were damp, and Tiny Tern shivered a little.

“You can dry off here,” said Pebble. “This guest room is for sharing. It is cozy and kind!”

Tiny Tern smiled. “Thank you. It smells nice.”

Pebble handed Tiny Tern a soft towel. “You are safe here,” Pebble said gently. “You are welcome here.”

They sat together on the rug and listened to the rain tap-tap-tap on the window. But then they heard a rustle under the bed. Rustle, rustle. Two bright eyes looked out. It was Wally Weasel!

“Eep!” Tiny Tern squeaked, scooting closer to Pebble.

Wally popped out and grabbed the bowl of berries. “Mine!” he said, clutching the bowl. “I was here first. I hid under the bed.”

Pebble’s beak opened in surprise. “Wally, these berries are for sharing,” Pebble said. “You may have some, but not all. Tiny Tern is our guest.”

Wally hugged the bowl tight. “I am hungry! I want them all!” His whiskers twitched. His voice sounded snappy.

Pebble took a slow breath. Pebble’s heart felt a little thumpy. Pebble wanted to be fair. Pebble also wanted to be kind.

“Wally,” Pebble said softly, “it is not fair to take everything. Justice means we share and take turns. Everyone gets what they need.”

Tiny Tern nodded. “I only want a few berries,” Tiny Tern said in a small voice.

Wally frowned. He looked at the shiny berries. He looked at Tiny Tern’s little shiver. He looked at the cozy room. His ears drooped. “I am very, very hungry,” Wally said. “I did not eat breakfast. I felt scared to ask. So I hid.”

Pebble’s eyes grew gentle. Pebble felt compassion. Compassion means caring when someone is hurting. Pebble imagined the rumble in Wally’s tummy. Pebble put a flipper on Wally’s paw.

“I hear you,” Pebble said. “Your tummy hurts. That is hard. You can ask. We can help. You do not need to hide.”

Wally blinked. “Really?”

“Really,” Pebble said. “We can be generous. Generous means we share even when we don’t have to. But we also keep it fair, so everyone feels okay.”

Pebble pointed to the table. “Let’s make a plan. That is justice. It is fair and kind.”

They sat at the little table. Pebble split the berries into three small bowls. Plop, plop, plop. Then Pebble brought out crunchy crackers and a little pitcher of warm mint tea. The tea smelled fresh. Tiny Tern smiled at the steam. Wally’s tummy growled—grrrr.

“Here is the plan,” Pebble said. “We each get a bowl of berries. We each get crackers. We each get a cup of tea.” Pebble paused. “If you are still hungry, Wally, you can have the last cracker from the plate. You asked, and you told us the truth.”

Tiny Tern clapped tiny wings. “That is fair!”

Wally’s eyes filled with tears. “Thank you,” he sniffled. “I am sorry I grabbed. I was scared. I forgot to be kind.”

Pebble nodded. “Saying sorry is brave.”

They ate together. The berries were sweet and cool. The crackers went crunch, crunch. The tea was warm and cozy in their tummies. The rain went patter, patter on the window. The guest room felt even warmer now.

After the snack, Pebble gave Wally a small napkin to keep. “This is your Welcome Napkin,” Pebble said. “When you use it, remember: you can ask for help.”

Wally smiled. “I will remember.”

Tiny Tern looked at the three smooth pebbles on the windowsill. “Can we share those too?” Tiny Tern asked.

“Yes,” said Pebble. “We can each have one. When you hold your pebble, think of compassion. Think of caring.”

They each picked a pebble. Smooth. Cool. Safe.

Then they cleaned up together. Pebble washed the cups. Tiny Tern dried them. Wally stacked the plates. Working together felt good. It sounded like clink, swish, and giggle.

Before Wally left, he paused at the door. “Pebble,” he said, “thank you for being kind and fair.”

Pebble smiled big. “Thank you for trying again.”

Tiny Tern added, “Thank you for sharing.”

They hugged. Wally waved and ran out into the rain with his Welcome Napkin tucked in his pocket.

Pebble looked around the guest room. It was still cozy. It was still warm. But now, it also felt full of love.

Pebble said, “Today we learned: Compassion means we care. Generosity means we share. Justice means we make a fair plan. When we do these things, everyone feels welcome.”

Tiny Tern nodded. “I feel welcome,” Tiny Tern said.

Pebble fluffed the pillows again and giggled. “Me too.”

The End.
