# Dottie the Dolphin Visits the Guest Room

Dottie was a playful dolphin who loved to splash and spin. One sunny day, Dottie visited her friend Mia’s house. Mia had a big, cozy guest room where Dottie could stay. The guest room smelled like fresh soap and warm blankets. The curtains were soft and blue, like the ocean. A small clock tick-tick-ticked on the nightstand.

“Welcome, Dottie!” Mia said, hugging her. “This is your room.”

Dottie smiled. “Thank you! It feels calm, like quiet waves.”

On the bed, there was a checklist with a glitter star on top. It said:
- Brush teeth.
- Put toys in basket.
- Read two books.
- Lights out at 8:00.

Dottie read the list slowly. “I can do this,” she said. She liked to be helpful, and she wanted to do things the right way. She wanted to use her voice when she needed help. Dottie had a big heart and a brave voice.

After dinner, Mia and Dottie swished into the guest room. Dottie had a small travel bag with a toothbrush, a tiny towel, and her favorite shell toy that made a soft shhh sound, like sea foam.

“Let’s get ready for bedtime,” Mia said. “We have one hour.”

Dottie looked at the clock. Tick-tick-tick. The little hand was near seven.

Dottie opened her bag. “Oh no,” she whispered. “My toothpaste is missing.” She felt a tiny twist in her tummy. She wanted to do her list. She wanted to brush her teeth. She wanted to do it on time.

Mia was in the hallway, folding towels. Dottie took a deep breath, like taking a big gulp of ocean air. She remembered: Use your words. Ask for what you need. That is self-advocacy.

Dottie peeked out. “Mia?” she called in a clear voice. “I need help. My toothpaste is missing.”

Mia turned and smiled. “Thank you for telling me,” she said. “That was brave and smart.” She brought a small minty toothpaste from the bathroom. It smelled fresh and cool.

Dottie grinned. “Thank you! Now I can do my list.”

She brushed her teeth. Swish, swish. The mint felt tingly and clean. She checked the box on the list with a little green crayon. Check!

Next, Dottie played with her shell toy. She made it sing. Shhh, shhh. She tossed it into the air and caught it. But the shell slipped and rolled under the bed with a soft clink.

Dottie knelt down and peeked into the shadow. “It’s dark under there,” she said. Her tummy fluttered again. She could say nothing and pretend. But Dottie wanted to be honest. That is integrity. She also wanted to fix it by herself if she could. That is self-reliance.

She took another brave breath. “I can try first,” she told herself. She found a long, smooth ruler on the desk. She lay on the soft rug that smelled like lavender and gently slid the ruler under the bed. Scritch, scritch. Tap. The shell rolled out into the light.

“I did it!” Dottie cheered. She put the shell in the toy basket and checked the next box. Check!

Mia peeked in. “Nice work, Dottie. You solved the problem.”

Dottie nodded. “I tried by myself, and I told the truth.”

Mia looked at the clock. “We have twenty minutes left. Time for books.”

Dottie picked two books from the little shelf. One had bright, splashy fish. The other had a brave star. They curled up on the bed. The blanket felt warm and soft. Dottie could hear the clock, tick-tick-tick, and the night breeze, whooosh, through the window.

Mia read in a gentle voice. “Swim, swim, little fish.” Dottie smiled and swayed, like she was floating. Then Mia read about the brave star who spoke up when it needed space and rest. Dottie listened closely.

“That is like me,” Dottie said. “I asked for help. I used my voice.”

“Yes,” Mia said. “That is self-advocacy.”

When the books were done, Dottie looked at the clock. It was almost eight. She felt a tiny wiggle of worry. The lights were still on. The list was almost done, but not yet. Dottie wanted to manage her time. She wanted to be ready.

She sat up tall. “Mia,” she said. “It is almost eight. May we turn off the lights now, please?”

Mia smiled proudly. “Great time-check, Dottie. Yes, let’s do it.”

Click. The lights went off. The room was cozy and dim. Moonlight made soft shapes on the wall, like little waves. Dottie checked the last box. Check!

Dottie felt calm and strong. She had asked for what she needed. She had tried to solve her own problems. She had told the truth when the shell rolled away. She had watched the time and made a good plan.

Mia tucked the blanket around her. “How do you feel?” she asked.

“I feel brave,” Dottie said, cuddling her shell. “I can use my voice. I can try first. I can be honest. And I can watch the clock.”

Mia nodded. “Those are wonderful skills.”

Dottie smiled and whispered, “Tomorrow, I will do it again.”

Outside, the night sang softly. Whooosh. Tick-tick-tick. Shhh.

And Dottie slept, cozy and proud.

The clear lesson: When you need help, use your voice. Try to solve problems on your own, tell the truth, and use time wisely. That is being brave and kind to yourself.

The End.
