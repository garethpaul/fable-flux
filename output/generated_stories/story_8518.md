# Momo's Rooftop Morning

High above the busy street, a small brown monkey named Momo lived on a sunny rooftop. Every morning, Momo loved to stretch his arms wide and feel the warm sunshine on his fuzzy fur.

"What a beautiful day!" Momo said, jumping up and down. "Time for my morning exercise!"

Momo began doing jumping jacks on the flat rooftop. One, two, three! His little heart pumped faster, and he felt happy and strong. The cool morning breeze tickled his whiskers as he moved.

"Exercise makes me feel so good!" Momo giggled, touching his toes. "Stretch up high, bend down low!"

Suddenly, Momo heard a sad chirping sound. He looked around and saw a tiny sparrow sitting on the edge of the roof. The little bird looked very tired.

"Hello there," Momo said gently, walking slowly toward the sparrow. "My name is Momo. Are you okay?"

The sparrow looked up with droopy eyes. "Hi, I'm Sunny. I flew so far looking for food, but I'm too tired to fly anymore. My wings feel so heavy."

Momo's kind heart felt sad for his new friend. "Oh no! You look exhausted. Would you like to exercise with me? Moving our bodies can give us energy!"

Sunny shook her small head. "I don't think I can move anymore."

"That's okay," Momo said peacefully. "Let's start very small. Can you just flap your wings a tiny bit?"

Sunny tried. Flap, flap. Her wings moved just a little.

"Great job!" Momo cheered. "Now watch me!" Momo did gentle stretches, reaching his arms up to the sky. "Stretching feels so nice!"

Sunny watched and tried copying Momo. She stretched one wing, then the other. "Oh! That does feel better!"

"Exercise helps our bodies feel strong and happy," Momo explained. "Let's do some more together!"

Momo and Sunny did simple exercises side by side. They marched in place, making their feet go tap-tap-tap on the rooftop. They rolled their shoulders in big circles. They even did silly wiggle dances that made them both laugh.

"I'm feeling much better!" Sunny chirped, her eyes now bright and shiny. "Thank you for being so kind and patient with me!"

Momo's tummy rumbled loudly. "Oh! All this exercise made me hungry!" He reached into his little pouch and pulled out some delicious banana pieces he had been saving for later.

"Would you like to share my breakfast?" Momo asked, holding out the sweet fruit.

"Really? You would share with me?" Sunny's voice was full of surprise and joy.

"Of course!" Momo smiled. "Friends share with each other. Plus, eating good food after exercise helps our bodies grow strong!"

They sat together on the warm rooftop, eating the tasty banana and watching the clouds float by. Sunny's energy had returned completely.

"Thank you, Momo," Sunny said. "You taught me that exercise can make me feel better, and you shared your food so generously. You're the kindest friend ever!"

"You're welcome!" Momo replied. "Remember, when we move our bodies every day, we feel happy and strong. And when we're kind to others, we make the world a better place!"

Sunny flapped her wings, now feeling powerful and ready to fly. "I'll remember! Goodbye, dear friend!"

As Sunny flew away into the bright blue sky, Momo continued his morning exercises, feeling proud that he had helped someone and shared what he had.

The End.
