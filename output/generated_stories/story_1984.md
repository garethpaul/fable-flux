# Tinka Builds a Song

Tinka was a helpful robot dog with shiny silver ears that went beep-beep when she wagged her tail. She lived in a musical practice room with soft rugs, bright posters of instruments from around the world, and shelves full of rattles, drums, and tiny pianos. The room smelled like fresh wood and lemon polish. Sunlight made little sparkles on the floor.

“Good morning,” Tinka chirped in a friendly robo-ruff. “Time to build a song!”

Her friends, Mei and Leo, came in carrying small instruments. Mei had a drum from Japan that went tap-tap. Leo had a shaker from Kenya that went shaka-shaka. Tinka had a plan to build a music stand out of colorful blocks, so they could hold the music sheets high and not step on them.

“We will build, then we will play,” Tinka said. Her eyes blinked bright blue. “Building first. Playing next. That is our plan.”

Mei clapped. “Let’s build together!”

Leo nodded. “I want it tall!”

Tinka looked at the blocks. They were red, blue, green, and yellow. Some were long and flat. Some were short and thick. Tinka made a gentle whirr as she thought. “We need a strong base,” she said. “Strong helps safe. Safe helps fun.”

They began to stack. Tap, tap. Click, click. The blocks made happy sounds. Tinka placed long blocks at the bottom. “Big blocks first,” she said. “We are builders!”

But Leo wanted it taller. He put a tiny block on top of a tiny block. Wobble, wobble. Mei wanted it wider. She added long blocks that stuck out like wings. Tinka’s ears beeped a soft warning. “Wobble detected,” she said.

The tower trembled. The music sheets slid off the top and floated down like white leaves.

“Oh no,” Mei whispered.

“My tall is falling,” Leo frowned.

Tinka took a slow robo-breath. She pressed a button and made a calm hum, like a lullaby. “It is okay. We can fix it. Builders try, learn, and try again.”

Mei and Leo nodded. Tinka pointed to a poster on the wall. It showed children from many countries playing music—flutes from Peru, sitars from India, and djembes from West Africa. “We all share music,” Tinka said softly. “We can share ideas, too.”

Leo hugged his shaker. “I like tall.”

Mei patted her drum. “I like wide.”

Tinka wagged. “We can do both. Compromise means we all get a little of what we want. Tall and wide can be strong.”

They made a new plan. Tinka drew a simple picture on a big paper: a wide base, a sturdy middle, and a tall top with a safe shelf. “Step by step,” she said. “First base, then middle, then top.”

They worked together. Tap, tap. Click, click. Scritch-scratch went the marker on the paper. When the tower got wobbly, Tinka added a cross piece. “Crisscross makes it strong,” she explained. “Like bridges around the world.”

Soon the music stand looked stable and proud. “Test time,” Tinka said. She placed a music sheet on top. It stayed still. No wobble!

“Yay!” Mei cheered, beating a gentle drum rhythm—tap… tap-tap… tap.

“Shaka-shaka hooray!” Leo sang, shaking his beads.

Tinka’s tummy light blinked. “Hydration alert,” she chirped. “Builders take care of their bodies.” She passed out little water cups and sliced apples. Crunch, crunch. Sip, sip. They washed their hands with foamy soap that smelled like oranges.

“Self-care keeps us strong,” Tinka said. “Healthy bodies help healthy building.”

They put the cups in the recycling bin. “We help the Earth,” Mei said, pointing to a picture of the globe on the wall.

“We share one big home,” Leo added. “We keep it clean.”

Tinka nodded. “Global awareness: we think about everyone and our planet.”

Now it was time to play. Mei tapped her drum. Leo shook his beads. Tinka pressed her nose button and made a gentle beat from her speaker—boop-boop, boom-boom. The room filled with happy sounds—tap, shaka, boom, boop. The new stand held the music high like a little rainbow bridge.

But then, a tiny problem. The stand was a bit too high for Mei. She had to reach up, and her arms got tired.

“I can’t see well,” Mei said quietly.

Tinka’s ears perked. “We can adjust.” She looked at Leo. “What do you think?”

Leo looked at the blocks and at Mei. “We can make the top lower. Not too low. Just right.”

“Compromise,” Tinka smiled. They removed one layer and added a slant piece so Mei could see. Tinka tightened the cross blocks with a soft click.

Mei tried again. “Perfect!” she said, eyes bright.

They played their song. It sounded warm and cozy, like a hug. Tinka wagged and kept the beat. When they finished, all three bowed to the stand, to each other, and to the Earth poster.

Tinka said, “Today we learned to build step by step, to listen, to care for our bodies, and to care for our world.”

Mei added, “We shared and changed the plan.”

Leo grinned. “We built it strong, together.”

Tinka’s tail went beep-beep. “Builders try, learn, and help. That is our song.”

The End.
