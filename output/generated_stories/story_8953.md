# Sunny’s Porch Song

On a peaceful front porch, a small blue bird named Sunny liked to sing. The porch smelled like warm wood and sweet lemonade. The sun felt gentle, and the breeze was soft like a whisper. Sunny’s feathers were bright, and his voice was clear and kind.

Every morning, Sunny sat on the porch rail and sang, “Tweet-a-lee, tweet-a-loo! Good morning to me and you!” Neighbors would smile. A puppy wagged its tail. A baby giggled and clapped. Sunny felt happy.

But one day, Sunny noticed something new. His tummy felt tight and twisty. His heart beat fast. Sunny wanted to sing, but no sound came out. The porch was quiet. Even the wind seemed to wait.

“Oh no,” Sunny whispered. “Where did my song go?”

He tried again. “Twee—” Nothing. Sunny looked at the rocking chair. It creaked slowly. He looked at the potted flowers. They were bright red and yellow, but he did not feel bright. He felt worried.

A friendly squirrel named Nutty peeped up over the step. “Hi, Sunny! Where is your song today?”

“I don’t know,” Sunny said. “My song is hiding.”

Nutty wiggled his tail. “Maybe you are tired. Maybe you need a plan.”

“A plan?” Sunny tilted his head. He liked plans. Plans are strategies to help when things are hard.

Nutty nodded. “Tell me a story about your morning. Stories help me when I feel stuck.”

“A story?” Sunny took a small breath. He looked at the porch. He listened to the breeze. He smelled the flowers. “Okay,” he said softly. “I will try.”

Sunny hopped closer to the rail. He began his story in a tiny voice. “Once there was a little blue bird who loved to sing. He woke up, stretched his wings, and flew to a peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like wood and sunshine. The rocking chair said, ‘Creak, creak.’ The flowers said, ‘Hello!’ The bird was ready to sing…”

Sunny paused. “And then his tummy felt tight.”

Nutty looked kind. “What did the little bird do next in the story?”

Sunny thought. “He remembered to take care of himself,” he said. “He took a slow breath in… 1… 2… 3… and a slow breath out… 1… 2… 3…”

Sunny closed his eyes. He breathed in. He breathed out. The air felt cool in his beak. His shoulders relaxed. The tight feeling felt smaller.

“And then?” asked Nutty.

“And then the bird had a sip of cool water.” Sunny took a sip from the little porch saucer. The water felt fresh. He patted his chest with a wing. “He also stretched his wings—up, out, and around!”

Sunny stretched his wings—up, out, and around. The sun warmed his feathers. The breeze tickled. He smiled.

Nutty clapped his tiny paws. “Your story is a good plan. What else?”

Sunny looked around. He listened. He noticed three sounds, like a game. “I hear the leaves shhh… I hear the chair creak… I hear a far-away car hum.” He noticed three things he could see. “I see the red flower. I see the blue sky. I see the striped mat.” He noticed three things he could feel. “I feel the warm sun. I feel the smooth rail. I feel the cool breeze on my face.”

“That’s mindfulness,” Nutty said proudly. “It helps you be here now.”

Sunny nodded. “And I will give my song a cozy place in my story.”

He began again, a little stronger. “The little blue bird took care of his body and his mind. He breathed slowly. He drank water. He stretched. He listened and looked and felt. Then he told his story to his friend.”

Sunny smiled at Nutty. “And the friend listened with big, kind ears.”

Nutty wiggled. “I do have very fine ears!”

Sunny’s voice grew warm. “The little bird did not rush. He used a strategy: first breathe, then sip, then stretch, then notice, then tell the story. He was gentle with himself.”

Sunny felt his song peeking out like the sun from behind a cloud. He waited. He took one more soft breath.

“Now,” he whispered, “I think my song is ready.”

He sang, very softly at first. “Tweet… tweet… tweet-a-lee…” The notes were sweet and round, like little blue bubbles floating up. The porch felt bright again. The puppy wagged. The baby giggled. Even the rocking chair seemed to creak in rhythm: creak, creak, creak.

Sunny’s song grew and grew. “Tweet-a-lee, tweet-a-loo! I found my voice. So can you!”

Nutty danced. “Hooray! Your story helped you!”

Sunny nodded. “Yes. I told my story. I took care of myself. I used a plan. The story was the map to my song.”

He looked at the children on the sidewalk. “When you feel stuck,” Sunny said kindly, “you can tell a story, too. Tell what you see, hear, and feel. Breathe slow. Sip water. Stretch. Use your helpful plan. Be gentle with yourself. Your song will come back.”

The breeze carried his words like soft feathers. The sun felt warm like a hug. The porch smelled like lemons and love.

Sunny finished his song and bowed. “Thank you for listening,” he chirped.

Nutty bowed too. “Thank you for telling your story.”

Sunny added one last line, like a ribbon on a gift. “Stories can help our hearts. Self-care is a strategy. We can be mindful and brave.”

And the porch, the friends, and the day all felt peaceful again.

The End.
