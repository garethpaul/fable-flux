# Sunny Bird Writes a Song

Sunny was a small, yellow singing bird who loved to chirp and hop. Sunny lived in a playful living room with soft pillows, a blue rug with stars, a tiny table, and a big window that let warm sunshine in. Toys made gentle clacks when they moved, and a clock went tick-tock, tick-tock, like a steady drum.

Every morning, Sunny followed a cozy routine. “Good morning, window! Good morning, clock!” Sunny chirped. Sunny stretched wings, sipped a little water from a shiny dish—sip, sip—and shook tiny feathers—rustle, rustle. Then Sunny sang a cheerful hello song. “La-la-la! Tweet-tweet!”

One day, Sunny had a bright idea. “Today, I will write my very own song,” Sunny said. The idea made Sunny’s feathers tingle with joy. Writing a song felt like catching a sunbeam. But writing takes preparation. Sunny remembered: First we get ready, then we write, then we share.

Sunny hopped to the tiny table. On the table was a small notebook with a cloud on the cover, and a purple crayon that smelled a little like blueberries. Sunny touched the smooth paper. It felt cool. The crayon felt warm in Sunny’s little claws. “I am ready,” Sunny whispered.

But the living room was busy. A soft ball rolled by—thump, thump. A toy car hummed—vrrr, vrrr. The clock said tick-tock, tick-tock. Sunny took a deep breath. “To write, I need a calm space,” Sunny said. Sunny pushed the ball into a basket—roll, plop. Sunny paused the toy car—click. Sunny asked the clock, “Clock, can you help me keep time?”

The clock ticked kindly. “Tick-tock. I can help. Let’s write for a short time, and then take a little wiggle break.”

Sunny smiled. “Yes, that is a good plan.” Sunny drew a tiny sun in the notebook to show the start of writing time.

At first, the page looked big and blank like a quiet sky. Sunny felt a little flutter of worry. “What if my song is not good?” Sunny asked.

The window let in a warm breeze that smelled like fresh soap from the nearby couch cushions. The breeze said, “Whoosh. Try one word.” Sunny chirped, “Hello.” Sunny wrote HELLO in big, wiggly letters. The letters looked friendly.

Sunny listened. The blue rug felt soft under tiny feet. The clock went tick-tock. Sunny heard the gentle squeak of the couch. “I hear sounds!” Sunny said. “I can write the sounds.” Sunny wrote: TICK-TOCK. SQUEAK. WHOOSH. The crayon made a scratchy song on the page—scratch, scratch.

Sunny began to sing the words. “Hello, tick-tock, hello whoosh, hello squeak!” Sunny giggled. The words made a happy rhythm. Sunny clapped little wings. Clap, clap!

Sunny felt proud, but also a little wiggly. The clock said, “Tick-tock. It is time for a short wiggle break.”

Sunny stood up, stretched, and did three gentle hops. “Hop, hop, hop!” Sunny drank a sip of water—sip. Then Sunny sat again. “I am ready to write more.”

Sunny looked around for more ideas. A red pillow looked like a strawberry. The air smelled a little like cinnamon from the wooden shelf. Sunny wrote: RED PILLOW. CIN-NA-MON. “These words taste good,” Sunny chirped.

Just then, a little dust bunny rolled from under the couch—fluff, fluff. Sunny laughed. “Hello, dust bunny. You can be in my song too.” Sunny wrote: FLUFF, FLUFF.

Sunny sang again, soft and sweet. “Hello tick-tock, hello whoosh. Hello squeak and cinnamon swoosh. Red pillow, fluff, fluff, too. Hello world, I sing with you.” The melody felt warm, like sunshine on feathers.

But Sunny’s letters started to wiggle off the lines. Sunny paused. “When I rush, my writing gets messy.” Sunny remembered the plan: prepare, write, and check. Sunny straightened the notebook and held the crayon gently. “Slow and steady,” Sunny said. “I can do this.”

Sunny carefully traced each letter. H…E…L…L…O. The letters stood tall and clear. Sunny felt calm and strong. “Writing is like flying,” Sunny whispered. “First I flap, then I glide.”

The clock ticked a happy rhythm. “Tick-tock. One more minute,” it said.

Sunny nodded and added the last lines: THANK YOU, ROOM. THANK YOU, DAY. I WRITE MY SONG. I FIND MY WAY.

Sunny put down the crayon and took a deep breath. The notebook smelled sweet like blueberries again. Sunny read the song out loud to the living room. “Hello tick-tock, hello whoosh. Hello squeak and cinnamon swoosh…” The room seemed to listen. The window shone. The blue rug felt extra soft. Sunny’s heart felt full.

“Good job,” said the clock, tick-tocking proudly. “You prepared your space. You followed a routine. You took breaks. You wrote with care.”

Sunny bowed. “Thank you, helpers. Preparation made it easy to start. Routine helped me keep going. Care helped me finish.” Sunny smiled. “Writing is fun! Writing is how I share my song.”

Sunny put the notebook and crayon back on the tiny table, ready for next time. Sunny hummed the new song as the sun moved across the floor, making gold squares on the rug. It was time for a snack, then play, then rest—just like the routine said.

Sunny looked at the notebook and felt brave and happy. “Tomorrow, I will write again,” Sunny chirped.

The End.
