# Binky’s Brave Bath Art

Binky was a small, determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. One morning, Binky hopped into the bathroom. The bathroom smelled like fresh soap and minty toothpaste. The tiles were cool and shiny. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet. Plink, plink went the drops into the tub.

Binky wiggled his nose. “Today, I will make bathroom art!” he said. “I have a new idea.”

On the counter sat a toothbrush, a cup of water, a clean washcloth, and safe, washable bath paints that Binky’s grown-up had placed there. There was also a note that said, “Use the paints in the tub. Keep water low. Ask for help if you need it. Love, Nana.”

Binky read the note out loud, slowly. “Use the paints in the tub. Keep water low. Ask for help.” He nodded. “I will follow directions. I can be brave and careful.”

Binky turned the faucet a tiny bit. The water made a soft whoosh and warm steam puffed up. “Mmm, warm,” he said. He kept the water low, just like the note said.

He hopped into the tub. The floor felt smooth and a little slippery. “I will be safe,” Binky whispered. He sat down and held the sides.

Binky opened the paints. Plop! A dot of blue landed on the washcloth. “Blue like the sky,” he said. He dabbed it on the wall of the tub. Smear, smear. “This is my sky!”

Binky put a dot of yellow on his toothbrush. “Yellow like the sun.” He brushed the paint gently in circles. Swish, swish. “A sun with tickly rays,” he giggled.

Soon, a small rainbow grew. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple. Binky tapped with his paw. Tap, tap, tap. The colors made soft, swishy sounds. The bathroom smelled like soap and a little like fruit from the paints.

Binky had a thought. “I want to make bubbles that sing!” he said. “I will make music art.”

He dipped the end of the toothbrush into water and tapped it on the side of the tub. Ting, ting. He dipped the washcloth and squeezed it. Squish, squish. He listened to the sounds. He made a rhythm: ting, squish, plink, plink, ting!

Binky smiled. “I am making art with sounds and colors. This is my new idea. This is innovation!” He tried new ways to paint—dots with his paw, lines with the brush, and big gentle pats with the washcloth. He was careful to keep the paints in the tub. He checked the water. “Still low,” he said.

Just then, the soap slipped and slid, thump, onto the floor. Binky’s ears twitched. “Uh-oh,” he said. He reached but could not grab it. The floor looked very slippery. “I need help.”

Binky remembered the note. “Ask for help.” He took a brave breath. “Nana!” he called. “Can you help me, please?”

Nana came in with a smile. “I’m here, little bunny. I like your brave voice,” she said. She picked up the soap and placed a bath mat by the tub. “You followed directions. Good job.”

Binky’s cheeks felt warm with pride. “I am making bathroom art,” he said. “I am also making music. Listen!” He tapped: ting, squish, plink, plink, ting!

Nana clapped softly. “What a clever idea. You used the things around you in a new way. That is innovation.”

Binky beamed. “I want to paint a bunny friend,” he said. He had an idea for a big, soft bunny in blue and purple. But he paused. “Can I, Nana?”

“Yes,” said Nana. “Keep the paints in the tub. Keep the water low. And clean up when you finish.”

“I can do that,” said Binky. He held the brush with two paws and painted long ears. He made eyes with tiny dots and a nose like his own. He used the washcloth to make fuzzy fur. Swish, swish, pat, pat. The bunny looked friendly.

Binky looked at his art. He felt a little shy. “Do you like it?” he asked softly.

Nana knelt by the tub. “I love it. I accept your art just as it is. It is yours. You were brave to share it.”

Binky’s heart felt big. “Thank you,” he said. “Now I will clean up.”

He turned the water off. He rinsed the paints from the tub walls. Whoosh! The colors swirled like a rainbow river and ran down the drain. He washed the brush and the washcloth until they were clean. He placed them back on the counter. He wiped up splashes with a small towel. Rub, rub, rub.

“All clean,” Binky said. The bathroom smelled fresh again. The tub was shiny. The floor was dry.

Nana gave Binky a gentle hug. “You had courage to try something new. You followed directions to stay safe. You made art in a new way. Today you learned that when you have a new idea, you can try it, ask for help, and clean up after. That is how innovation grows.”

Binky wiggled his nose. “I can be brave, kind, and clever,” he said. “I can make art, make music, and follow directions.”

“Yes,” said Nana. “You can.”

Binky felt proud. He had turned a simple bath into a bright, musical art time. He had kept himself safe. He had tried a new idea. His heart felt warm, like the sun he painted.

“I can’t wait for my next idea,” Binky said with a happy hop.

The End.
