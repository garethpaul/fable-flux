# Oliver Owl's Self-Care Discovery

Oliver Owl sat hunched over his desk in the cozy basement lab. His big round eyes felt tired and scratchy. His soft brown feathers looked messy and ruffled.

"Just one more experiment," Oliver hooted quietly to himself. He had been working all day and all night. His tummy rumbled loudly. GRUMBLE! But he ignored it.

The basement lab smelled like old books and dusty bottles. Colorful beakers bubbled on wooden shelves. Papers with drawings covered every table. Oliver loved his work, but he felt very tired.

His friend Ruby Robin hopped down the creaky stairs. "Hoo-hoo! Oliver, are you still working?" she chirped sweetly.

Oliver rubbed his tired eyes. "Yes, Ruby. I must finish this painting of the stars. And mix this purple potion. And read these big books."

Ruby tilted her red head. "When did you last eat? Or sleep? Or stretch your wings?"

"I... I don't remember," Oliver admitted. His voice sounded weak and scratchy.

"Oh my!" Ruby fluttered her wings. "Oliver, you need to take care of yourself! Your body needs food and rest to work well."

Oliver looked confused. "But I have so much important work to do!"

"Your work will be better when YOU feel better," Ruby explained gently. "Let me show you!"

Ruby brought Oliver a bowl of warm seeds and berries. The food smelled delicious! Oliver's tummy rumbled again. He ate slowly, savoring each bite.

"Mmm, this tastes wonderful!" Oliver hooted. "I feel stronger already!"

Next, Ruby helped Oliver stretch his big wings. They flapped them up and down, up and down. The movement felt amazing! Oliver's body tingled with new energy.

"Now let's clean your feathers," Ruby suggested. She brought warm, soapy water that smelled like lavender. Oliver preened his feathers carefully. They became soft and shiny again.

"I look much better!" Oliver admired himself in the mirror.

"Now try painting," Ruby encouraged.

Oliver picked up his paintbrush with a steady wing. The colors flowed smoothly across the paper. His star painting looked more beautiful than ever! The yellow stars seemed to twinkle. The blue sky looked peaceful and deep.

"Wow!" Oliver gasped. "My painting is so much better now!"

"That's because you took care of yourself first," Ruby explained. "When we eat good food, rest our bodies, and keep clean, we can do our best work!"

Oliver felt proud and happy. His eyes sparkled brightly. His feathers gleamed in the basement's warm light.

"Thank you for teaching me about self-care, Ruby," Oliver said gratefully. "I didn't know how important it was!"

"Taking care of yourself isn't selfish," Ruby chirped. "It helps you take better care of others too!"

From that day on, Oliver made a special schedule. Every morning, he ate breakfast. Every afternoon, he stretched his wings. Every evening, he cleaned his feathers and rested.

His experiments became more successful. His paintings grew more colorful. His inventions worked better than ever!

Most importantly, Oliver felt happy and healthy. He had energy to help his friends and share his discoveries.

"Self-care makes everything better!" Oliver hooted joyfully as he showed Ruby his newest star painting. The basement lab felt warm and cheerful, filled with the sounds of friendship and learning.

The End.
