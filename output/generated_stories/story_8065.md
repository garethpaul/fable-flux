# Rooftop Riddle Race

Sunny the bear cub loved to climb the stairs to the rooftop. The roof was flat and safe, with a soft green mat and a little fence around it. The city hummed below. Breezes brushed Sunny’s fur. Pigeons cooed, “Coo, coo.” The sky was bright and blue.

“Today is sports day!” Sunny giggled, bouncing a red rubber ball. “I will run and kick and roll!” The ball felt smooth and warm in Sunny’s paws.

Mama Bear came up with a small backpack. “Sports are fun,” Mama said with a smile. “And being responsible is important too. What is your plan?”

Sunny blinked. “Plan?”

“A plan helps you think,” said Mama. “Use your smart bear brain. Look, listen, think, then act.”

Sunny’s ears wiggled. “Look, listen, think, then act,” Sunny repeated. “Okay!”

Mama pointed to three colorful cones, a soft rope, and a whistle. “Let’s set up a Rooftop Riddle Race. You will play a sport, use your brain, and practice being responsible.”

Sunny clapped. “Yes, please!”

They placed the cones in a triangle. The soft rope became a jumping line. Mama put the whistle around her neck. “We will have three rounds,” she said. “Each round has a riddle. Solve the riddle, then choose what to do. That is logical thinking.”

Sunny nodded. “Look, listen, think, then act.”

Round One began. Mama blew the whistle. “Toot!” She read the first riddle card. “I roll but do not run. I bounce under the sun. What am I?”

Sunny held the red ball and grinned. “The ball! The ball!”

“That’s right,” said Mama. “Now think: How can we keep the ball from rolling off the roof?”

Sunny looked at the fence, the cones, and the rope. Sunny listened to the wind and the faraway cars. Sunny thought hard. “If I kick too hard, the ball might fly. If I kick soft, it stays by me. I can use the cones to make a safe path.”

Sunny set the cones close together and kicked the ball gently between them. Tap, tap, tap. The ball rolled softly and stayed on the mat.

Mama cheered. “You looked, listened, thought, and then acted. That is responsible.”

Sunny felt proud and warm, like sunshine on fur.

Round Two began. “Toot!” Mama read the next card. “I show fast and slow. I help you know when to go. What am I?”

Sunny looked up. “The whistle! The whistle says stop and go.”

“Good thinking,” said Mama. “Now listen for the whistle. When you hear one toot, jump over the rope. When you hear two toots, stop and breathe. Ready?”

Sunny nodded, knees bendy, toes wiggly.

“Toot!” Sunny hopped over the rope. The rope was soft, and Sunny’s paws made a gentle thump on the mat. “Toot, toot!” Sunny stopped, put paws on belly, and took a slow breath. In… and out. The breeze smelled like fresh laundry from a nearby line. Sunny smiled.

“You followed the rules,” Mama said. “That is being responsible in sports.”

Sunny’s tail nub wiggled. “I like following rules. It keeps me safe.”

Round Three began. The wind blew a little stronger, and a gray cloud moved over the sun. “Toot!” Mama read the last card. “I am big and high. I block the sky. I might bring rain. What am I?”

Sunny gazed up. “A cloud!” Sunny said.

Mama nodded. “Now use logic. If the wind gets stronger and the cloud gets darker, what should we do?”

Sunny looked, listened, and thought. The breeze tickled harder. The cloud grew darker like gray paint. The pigeons flapped away. “If it rains, the roof gets slippery,” Sunny said. “So we should pack up and go inside.”

“That is a smart plan,” Mama said.

Sunny gathered the cones, the rope, and the ball. The ball went into the backpack. Sunny checked the mat to be sure nothing was left. “I am responsible,” Sunny said softly. Sunny took Mama’s paw, and they walked downstairs.

They reached the cozy kitchen. The window tapped with tiny drops. Pitter-patter. Rain! Sunny giggled. “We made a good choice!”

Mama poured warm cocoa. The sweet smell filled the room. They sat by the window and watched raindrops race. Sunny imagined a new game. “Mama, can we make an indoor course? We can still play sports, but safe.”

Mama smiled. “Yes. Let’s use cushions for gentle jumps and tape lines for running paths. First, what is your plan?”

Sunny looked around the room. Sunny listened for space and quiet. Sunny thought carefully. “We move the plant so it won’t tip. We put the tape on the floor. We jump on cushions only. We keep our paws to ourselves. Then we run slow inside.”

Mama nodded. “You used logic again. You showed responsibility. And you still love sports!”

Sunny set up the course. Jump. Clap. Step. Pause. “Toot!” Mama laughed and made whistle sounds with her mouth.

When the rain stopped, the sky turned blue again. Sunny sipped the last warm cocoa and hugged Mama. “I learned a big lesson today.”

“What did you learn?” Mama asked.

Sunny smiled. “Look, listen, think, then act. That helps me make good choices. That keeps me safe and happy. And that makes sports even more fun!”

Mama hugged back. “Exactly.”

The End.
