# Timo the Tortoise and the Bathtub Builders

Timo the tortoise lived in a sunny house with a big, bright bathroom. The bathroom smelled like sweet bubbles and warm towels. The floor felt cool and smooth under Timo’s tiny feet. Every morning, Timo visited the bathroom to brush, wash, and think.

Timo was very wise. He liked to solve little puzzles. He also loved to share stories from his family. “Sharing makes small things feel big,” he would say with a smile.

One morning, Timo found a surprise in the bathroom. A basket of bath toys sat by the tub—soft sponges, colorful blocks, a little blue boat, and a yellow rubber duck. Sunlight made the toys glow. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet, singing a tiny water song.

Timo blinked. “Oh my,” he said. “Today looks like a building day.”

Just then, two friends peeked around the door. Pip the mouse had whiskers that twitched with excitement. Lila the ladybug wore a tiny red scarf.

“Hi, Timo!” squeaked Pip. “Can we play in the bathroom?”

“Yes,” said Timo, “but let’s play kindly. We can share the toys and build together.”

Pip ran to the basket and grabbed the blue boat. Lila reached for the yellow duck. Timo rolled over and picked up the soft sponges.

Pip puffed out his cheeks. “I want the duck too!” he squeaked.

Lila hugged the duck gently. “I found it first,” she said with a tiny frown.

Timo took a slow breath. He felt the cool air and listened to the drip, drip, drip. He touched the smooth sponge and thought.

“Friends,” Timo said softly, “my grandma tortoise taught me a bath-time tradition. We share. We build. We take turns. That is how we make the best boats. Would you like to try our tradition?”

“What’s a tradition?” asked Lila.

“It is a special way of doing things,” Timo said, “passed from old to young. It helps our hearts feel close.”

Pip’s whiskers twitched. “How do we do it?”

Timo smiled. “Step one: We make a plan together. Step two: We share the tools. Step three: We take turns and help.”

Pip and Lila nodded.

“Let’s plan,” said Timo. He tapped his shell, thinking. “We will build a bubble bridge and a sponge dock. The blue boat can park at the dock. The yellow duck can cross the bridge.”

“That sounds fun!” chirped Lila.

“I like docks,” squeaked Pip. “They are strong and neat.”

“Good,” said Timo. “Now we share the tools. Pip, you hold the boat. Lila, you hold the duck. I will hold the sponges. We will swap when it is time.”

They turned on the warm water. Swish, swish went the bubbles, soft and foamy. The bathroom smelled like oranges. Timo placed the sponges in the tub. They felt squishy and bouncy.

“Turn time,” said Timo. “First we need blocks for the dock.”

Pip picked up the colorful blocks and set them by the tub. “One block here,” he said, “and one block here.”

Lila watched carefully. “We need a middle block,” she added. “Or the dock will fall.”

Timo nodded. “Good thinking. That’s logical thinking—using our brains to make good choices.”

They built the sponge dock: squish, press, pat. They built the bubble bridge: scoop, scoop, sweep. Lila made a soft, bubbly arch. Pip made a straight path with the blocks. Timo added a tiny towel ramp.

“Time to share,” said Timo. He looked at Pip and Lila with kind eyes. “Pip, please pass the boat to Lila, so she can test the bridge.”

Pip hugged the boat for one more second. His cheeks puffed. Then he took a deep breath and handed it to Lila. “Here you go.”

“Thank you,” said Lila. She placed the boat in the tub. The boat bobbed gently. “Boop, boop,” she whispered, smiling.

“Now Lila,” said Timo, “please pass the duck to Pip. It is his turn.”

Lila patted the duck. She gave it to Pip. Pip giggled. “Quack, quack!”

Pip set the duck on the bubble bridge. The duck slid a little but did not fall. The bridge held strong. Pip clapped. Lila clapped. Timo nodded with pride.

“Sharing works,” said Pip.

“Sharing makes room for everyone,” said Lila.

Timo’s eyes sparkled. “Yes. And our tradition says we tell a Thank-You Tale at the end.”

“What is a Thank-You Tale?” asked Lila.

“It is a short story about what each friend did to help,” said Timo. “I will start. Today, Pip shared the boat. He built a strong dock. Lila shared the duck. She made a lovely arch. You both used your minds and your hearts.”

Pip smiled. “Today, Timo showed us a plan. He taught us to take turns.”

Lila added, “Timo told us a tradition we can keep.”

The bathroom felt warm and bright. The bubbles popped like tiny kisses. The boat bobbed. The duck glided. The friends took turns, passing the toys back and forth, giggling and cheering.

At last, they dried off with fluffy towels. Timo wrapped a small towel around his shell. It felt cozy and safe.

“I learned something,” said Pip. “Sharing makes play better.”

“I learned something too,” said Lila. “When we plan and take turns, our building stays strong.”

Timo smiled, wise and kind. “That is our bath-time tradition: share, build, and think. We can do it every day.”

They placed the toys back in the basket, neat and tidy. The bathroom was quiet again, except for the soft drip, drip, drip.

“See you tomorrow,” said Pip.

“See you,” said Lila.

“See you, friends,” said Timo. “And thank you for sharing.”

The End.
