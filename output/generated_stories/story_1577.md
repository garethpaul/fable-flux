# Milo Mouse Builds Each Day

Milo was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived in a sunny backyard beside a big yellow sunflower. Every morning, he stretched, wiggled his whiskers, and listened to the birds sing, “Tweet-tweet-tweet!” The grass smelled fresh, and the warm sun felt like a cozy hug.

Milo had a plan. He wanted to build a safe little tool bench for his tiny backyard projects. “I can fix things! I can build things!” he squeaked happily. But building was a big job, and sometimes big jobs felt tricky.

Milo’s friend, Bella the bluebird, hopped down from a branch. “Good morning, Milo! What’s your plan today?”

Milo smiled. “I’m going to build my tool bench. I will do a little bit every day. I will follow my routine.”

“What’s a routine?” asked Bella.

“A routine is what we do, step by step, each day,” said Milo. “It helps me do my best and stay safe.”

Milo’s routine began with safety. He put on tiny acorn-shell goggles and snug leaf gloves. “Safety first,” he said. “Then fun!”

He looked at his list, written in berry juice on a flat stone:
- Stretch.
- Snack.
- Safety gear.
- Plan.
- Build a little.
- Clean up.

Milo nibbled a crunchy oat for energy. Crunch, crunch! He looked at his plan and tapped it with his paw. “Today, I will gather wood.”

He picked up small, smooth sticks and stacked them neatly. “One. Two. Three,” he counted. The pile smelled like pine and sunshine.

Soon, his friend Tilda the turtle peeked from the clover. “Hello, Milo. Need help?”

“Yes, please,” said Milo. “But let’s go slow and safe.”

They carried sticks together. Sometimes a stick slipped. Thump! Milo took a deep breath. “It’s okay. Try again,” he said. “We are persistent. That means we keep going.”

By lunchtime, Milo’s stick pile was ready. “Routine says clean up!” Milo sang. He put extra sticks in a basket and swept the path with a leaf broom. Swish, swish. He washed his paws at the dew puddle. Splish-splash!

The next sunny morning, Milo followed his routine again: stretch, snack, safety, plan. “Today, I will sand the wood,” he decided. He rubbed the sticks with soft moss. Shhh, shhh. The sticks felt smooth, like river stones.

Bella fluttered near. “It looks great!”

“Thank you,” said Milo. “My routine helps me do a little every day.”

On the third day, the sun was extra bright. Milo put on his goggles and leaf gloves. “Safety first,” he said again. He began to tie the sticks together with long, strong grass. Tilda held the other end steady.

“Oops!” said Tilda as a knot slipped loose.

Milo’s whiskers drooped. “This is hard.”

Bella chirped, “You can do it, Milo. Remember your routine.”

Milo nodded. “I will try again.” He took a calm breath in and out. He checked his plan. “Step by step.” He tied one knot. Pull. He tied another knot. Pull. The grass smelled sweet, and the knots felt firm under his paws.

But suddenly, a little wind blew. Whoosh! The light sticks wobbled.

Milo stepped back. “Pause for safety,” he said. “Let’s use the rock to hold this corner.” He set a smooth rock on the edge. The wobble stopped. “There! Safer now.”

On the fourth day, clouds covered the sun, but the backyard was still bright and warm. Milo was ready to add the tiny shelf for his acorn hammer and his thimble nails. He checked his list. “Plan first,” he said. He measured with a piece of string. “One mouse-tail long,” he giggled.

As Milo lifted the shelf, he heard a “Coo?” from the fence. It was Poppy the pigeon. “Careful,” Poppy cooed, “lift with slow wings.”

Milo smiled. “I lift with slow paws.” He moved slowly and steady. He listened to his heartbeat. Thump-thump. He placed the shelf just right.

Then, oh no! A thimble nail rolled away. Tink, tink, tink. It fell near a prickly thorn bush.

Milo looked at the thorns. “That is not safe.” He called, “Friends, I need help.”

Tilda nodded. “I will move the thorn branch with a stick.”

Milo put on an extra leaf glove. “We will go slow.” Together, they pushed the thorny branch away and used a long spoon leaf to scoop up the nail. “We did it safely!” Milo cheered.

At last, on the fifth day, the sun shined bright, and the birds sang a happy song. “Tweet-tweet-tweet!” Milo followed his routine one more time: stretch, snack, safety, plan. He checked each knot and each stick. He pressed gently on the bench. It was strong and steady.

Bella fluttered down. “Is it ready?”

Milo smiled wide. “Yes! We built it with routine, persistence, and safety.”

Everyone gathered around. Milo placed his acorn hammer on the shelf. He patted the smooth wood. “Thank you, friends. We did a little each day. We kept trying. We stayed safe.”

Bella chirped, “What did we learn?”

Milo said, “A routine helps us finish big jobs. We keep going, even when it’s hard. We slow down and stay safe.”

Tilda added, “And we clean up!” They all laughed.

Milo swept the yard. Swish-swish. He folded his list and tucked it into a tiny pocket by the bench. The sunny backyard smelled like fresh grass and sweet clover. Milo’s heart felt warm.

“Tomorrow,” said Milo, “we will use the bench to fix my wagon. We’ll follow our routine again!”

Everyone cheered. “Hooray for routine! Hooray for safe building!”

The End.
