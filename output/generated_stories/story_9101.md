# Bubbles Finds the Right Beat

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved sounds. One bright morning, Bubbles swam through a round blue door and splashed into a musical practice room. The room was cozy and cool. Soft lights glowed like seashells. Drums sat in a row. Little bells hung like shiny raindrops. A big piano waited with black and white keys that looked like smiling teeth.

“Click-clack, ting-ting!” Bubbles sang, flipping his tail. He made tiny bubbles that went pop-pop-pop. He loved to sing, and he loved to learn.

Inside the room was Ms. Coral, the music teacher. She had kind eyes and gentle hands. “Good morning, Bubbles,” she said. “We will make music. We will also care for our bodies and our words.”

Bubbles nodded. “I want to sing! I want to tap! I want to do it all right now!”

“First,” said Ms. Coral, “we warm up. Healthy habits help our bodies. Take a deep breath. In through the blowhole, slow and sweet.”

Bubbles tried. “Hooooo.” He felt the cool air go in. He let it out. “Pfffff.” It felt soft, like a wave on sand.

“Good,” said Ms. Coral. “Now sip some water.”

Bubbles slurped from a tiny shell cup. The water tasted fresh and clean. He smiled. His voice felt smooth.

Around them were friends: a shy crab named Pinch, a little turtle named Dot, and a singing seahorse named Lila. Lila hummed, “La-la-la,” in many ways. Sometimes high, sometimes low. She loved words in different languages.

“Hola,” Lila said with a bounce. “That means hello.”

“Namaste,” said Ms. Coral, bowing a little. “That means I see you.”

Bubbles clapped his fins. “I like hello words! Hello, hola, namaste!”

They began to practice. Ms. Coral tapped a drum. Tap-tap. Tap-tap. The beat was steady, like a heart.

Bubbles wanted to show how fast he could go. He smacked the drum with his fin. BOOM-BOOM-BOOM! He sang very loud. “LA-LA-LA-LA!” His voice splashed like a wave in a storm.

Pinch jumped. Dot pulled into her shell. Lila’s tail shook. The room felt too bright and too big.

Ms. Coral raised her hand. “Pause, please.”

Bubbles blinked. His face grew warm. “I was excited,” he said. “I did not see my friends were scared.”

Lila took a breath. “Bubbles, I like your song,” she said softly. “But the sound was too loud for my ears.”

Dot peeked out. “And the drum hits made my shell buzz.”

Bubbles’ tail drooped. “I’m sorry,” he whispered. “I forgot to check if it was okay.”

Ms. Coral smiled kindly. “This is a good time to practice understanding. We listen. We care. We ask. That is brave.”

Bubbles took another deep breath. In. Out. He felt calm, like a slow wave. “Friends,” he said, “how can I make it better?”

Lila smiled. “Maybe ask, ‘Is this too loud?’”

Pinch raised a claw. “Maybe use soft hands.”

Dot nodded. “Maybe we can have signals! If I tap my shell, it means, ‘Too much, please slow down.’”

Bubbles brightened. “Yes! Let’s make signals in many languages.” He tried a new word. “Please is por favor.” He looked at Ms. Coral. “Is that right?”

“Yes,” she said. “And thank you is gracias. In sign language, please is a hand moving in a circle on your chest.”

Bubbles placed a fin on his chest and circled slowly. “Please,” he said. He looked at Lila. “Is this okay volume?”

Lila listened as Bubbles sang, “Laaa,” very soft, like a feather. “Yes,” she said. “Much better.”

They tried again. The room filled with gentle sounds. Tap-tap. Ting-ting. La-la. Bubbles watched his friends and watched their signals. When Dot tapped her shell, Bubbles lowered his voice. When Pinch waved, Bubbles slowed his beat. When Lila sang “bravo,” Bubbles smiled wide.

Soon they came to a tricky part. The notes were high and long. Bubbles felt a tickle in his throat. He wanted to push. He wanted to be loud. He stopped. He remembered healthy habits.

“Water break,” he said. He sipped. He stretched his neck. He hummed low, mmm-mmm-mmm. His voice felt safe and strong.

Ms. Coral nodded. “That is wise.”

At the end, Ms. Coral said, “Let us share our music.” The friends stood in a circle. Bubbles took a brave step forward. “Friends,” he said, “I will start soft. If it is too much, please use our signals. I will watch. I will listen.”

They began. The song rose like sunrise. It smelled like clean sea salt and sounded like gentle rain. Bubbles kept the beat steady. Tap. Tap. His notes were warm. Lila added hello words from near and far. “Hello. Hola. Namaste. Jambo.” Dot chimed a bell. Pinch clicked a light rhythm.

When the final note faded, the room was quiet and happy. Ms. Coral clapped. “Well done. You made music with care.”

Bubbles beamed. “I learned to understand. I ask. I listen. I watch. I keep my body safe with water and warm-ups. I use kind words. I respect space.”

Lila swished her tail. “Gracias, Bubbles.”

Pinch bowed. “Thank you.”

Dot smiled. “Namaste.”

Bubbles placed a fin on his chest and signed, “Please,” then “Thank you.” He felt brave, not because he was loud, but because he was kind.

Ms. Coral said, “The lesson today: Understanding helps us make music and keeps our friends and our bodies safe.”

Bubbles giggled and made tiny bubbles. Pop-pop-pop. “I like that beat,” he said.

The End.
