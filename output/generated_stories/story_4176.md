# Tiny Pots, Big World

In a warm kitchen with sunny windows, a wise tortoise named Tavi shuffled along the tiled floor. The tiles felt smooth and cool under Tavi’s sturdy feet. The air smelled like cinnamon toast and orange peels. A kettle hummed softly, and tiny raindrops tapped the window like gentle drums.

On the table, there were little pots of paint. Red, blue, yellow, green, and a sparkly gold. Paintbrushes sat in a jar, their bristles fluffy and ready. Paper lay in a neat stack, white as clouds.

Tavi looked up and smiled. “Good morning, warm kitchen,” Tavi said in a slow, kind voice. “Today, we make art.”

A little mouse named Mino peeked from a teapot. “Art? Can I paint too?” asked Mino, whiskers twitching.

“Of course,” said Tavi. “We will make a picture of the world. The world is big. The world is many. We will show care and respect.”

Mino climbed onto the table, tiny paws pat-patting on the wood. “What does the world look like?” Mino asked.

Tavi dipped a brush in blue. “The world is round like this pan lid,” Tavi said, tapping a shiny lid with a gentle clink. “There is water, big and blue.” He swished the brush. The paper whispered as the paint spread, like a soft wave.

Mino dipped a brush in green. “And land,” Mino said proudly, splotching little shapes. “Where friends live!”

Tavi nodded. “Yes. Friends live in many lands. They speak in many ways. They eat many foods. They sing many songs. That is global awareness. It means we notice and care about the big, shared world.”

Mino’s eyes shone. “Let’s paint friends!”

They painted a smiling sun with gold sparkles. It looked warm and happy. They added tiny homes. One had a red roof like a berry. One had a flat roof with a little garden, where carrots stood like orange pencils. One was a tall house with many windows, glowing yellow.

“How do friends from far-away places say hello?” Mino asked, dipping into yellow.

Tavi smiled and dabbed dots of color. “Some say ‘Hello.’ Some say ‘Hola.’ Some say ‘Bonjour.’ Some say ‘Jambo.’ Some say ‘Ni hao.’ We can greet with respect. We can smile. We can listen.”

Mino tried the words. “Hola! Bonjour!” He giggled. The sound was light, like spoons clinking.

From the open window, a breeze brought the smell of fresh bread. The kettle clicked. The toaster popped, and toast hopped up. Tavi slid the toast onto a plate. “Art and snacks,” Tavi said. “We take care of our bodies and our hearts.”

They munched crunchy toast with a tiny smear of honey. Honey dripped like sunshine. Tavi dabbed at a sticky spot with a soft cloth. “We also take care of our space,” he said kindly.

Back at the paper, Mino painted people. One wore a bright scarf. One wore a tiny cap. One had curly hair like little springs. One had straight hair like silky threads. “Are they friends?” Mino asked.

“Yes,” said Tavi. “Friends can be different and still be kind. We respect each other. We listen. We learn.”

Mino made a little drum. “Boom-boom,” he hummed. Tavi painted a flute. “Peep-peep,” he whistled. They painted bowls of food—steamy rice, spicy beans, long noodles, and sweet fruit. The kitchen smelled like cinnamon, but they imagined other smells too: warm soup, roasted corn, and mango.

Mino looked thoughtful. “What if I do not know someone’s words? Can we still be friends?”

Tavi nodded. “Yes. We can use our hands. We can show with art. We can say please and thank you in any language—with our eyes, our faces, our voices. We can ask, ‘May I learn with you?’”

Mino painted a big heart in the middle of the world. He added tiny hearts near each home. “Hearts everywhere,” he said.

Tavi dipped into sparkly gold and traced a gentle path around the paper. “This is our caring path,” he said. “It goes from the warm kitchen to the big world, and back again.”

The rain outside slowed to a soft drip. The room felt cozy. Tavi and Mino added final touches—little stars, a moon, and many hands waving hello.

“Let’s give our picture a title,” Tavi said.

Mino tapped his chin. “Tiny Pots, Big World,” he said. “Because our paint pots are tiny, but our world is big.”

Tavi clapped slowly, his shell making a quiet knock-knock. “A good title,” he said. “Our picture shows global awareness. It shows we see others. It shows respect. It shows creativity. Art helps us share feelings with friends near and far.”

They cleaned the brushes with warm, soapy water. Swish, swish. They wiped the table with gentle circles. The kitchen smelled fresh again. Tavi pinned the painting to the wall. The gold sparkles caught the light and twinkled.

Mino stood back and sighed a happy sigh. “I feel close to friends I have not met yet,” he said.

Tavi nodded. “That is the magic of caring. When we look, listen, and learn, we make the world kinder.”

Mino took Tavi’s paw. “Tomorrow, can we learn hello in one more way?”

“Yes,” said Tavi. “Every day, a little more.”

They watched the painting glow in the warm kitchen. The toast crumbs were gone. The brushes were clean. Their hearts felt full.

The End.
