# Pippa Penguin’s Guest Room Dance

Pippa was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a happy wiggle. One sunny morning, Pippa waddled into the guest room. The guest room smelled like fresh soap and lemon. The curtains were soft and blue. The rug felt fuzzy under Pippa’s little feet.

“Tap, tap, tap!” Pippa said, tapping her feet on the rug. “Today, I will dance!”

Auntie May was visiting and staying in the guest room. Her suitcase sat by the bed. A tall lamp stood quietly. A small radio blinked with a tiny blue light.

Auntie May peeked in and smiled. “Good morning, Pippa! What are you up to?”

“I’m practicing a dance,” Pippa chirped. “I want to learn new steps. I want to be helpful too!”

Auntie May nodded. “That’s wonderful. Dancing is fun and good for your body. It helps your brain learn patterns. Would you like some music?”

“Yes, please!” said Pippa.

Click. The radio played a gentle beat: thump-thump, tippy-tap. Pippa took a deep breath. She felt the music tickle her tummy. Her wings fluttered. Her feet began to move.

“Step-step, slide,” she said softly. “Step-step, slide.”

Auntie May clapped. “Great counting! Let’s count the beats together. One, two, three, four.”

Pippa listened. “One, two, three, four,” she echoed. The numbers helped her feet know when to move. She could feel the warm sun on her feathers and the cool air on her cheeks. She smiled and wiggled.

Suddenly, Pippa saw a little problem. A pile of clean towels sat on the chair. They were about to topple. “Oh!” she gasped. “The towels need help.”

Pippa paused her dance and waddled over. She carefully folded each towel. Fold, pat, smooth. “Helping first, then dancing,” she said.

Auntie May beamed. “Thank you, Pippa. That is very helpful. Dancers use their bodies to move, and they use their hearts to care.”

Pippa’s cheeks felt warm with pride. “Now I will try a twirl!” She stood tall, lifted her wings, and spun slowly. Whoosh! The air whispered in her ears. She giggled, but her foot slid on the smooth rug. “Whoops!”

Auntie May reached out a hand. “Balance, little penguin. Try a small twirl first. Bend your knees just a bit.”

Pippa nodded. “Small twirl. Bend knees.” She tried again. Spin… stop. “I did it!”

“Wonderful!” said Auntie May. “Here is a tiny dance lesson. First, we warm up. Then, we practice steps. We listen and count. We try again if it’s hard.”

“Warm up, practice, listen, count, try again,” Pippa repeated. She tapped her head. “That helps my brain.”

They warmed up by wiggling fingers, shaking shoulders, and bending knees. “I feel ready!” said Pippa. The radio music lifted and sang. The beat felt like a friendly drum in the room.

“Let’s make a helping dance,” Auntie May said. “A dance that cleans and cares.”

Pippa’s eyes sparkled. “Yes! A helpful dance!”

They made dance moves for jobs in the guest room:
- A folding move: fold, pat, smooth.
- A dusting move: swish, swish, swish.
- A making-the-bed move: tuck, tuck, fluff.

They put the moves together. “Fold, pat, smooth. Swish, swish, swish. Tuck, tuck, fluff!” Pippa sang as she danced. The guest room began to look neat and cozy. The lamp shone softly. The pillows looked puffy and proud.

Auntie May laughed. “You are cleaning and dancing. You are learning and helping at the same time!”

Pippa felt strong and smart. “Dancing teaches my body and my mind,” she said. “And it can help others.”

They took a tiny water break. The water felt cool and sweet. Pippa licked a little dribble from her beak and giggled.

“Let’s finish with a show,” Auntie May said. “I’ll clap the beat.”

Clap, clap, clap, clap. Pippa stepped to the numbers. “One, two, three, four.” She added a slide. She added a little twirl. She added the helpful moves. Fold, pat, smooth. Swish, swish, swish. Tuck, tuck, fluff.

At the end, Pippa bowed. The room smelled clean. The rug felt cozy. The radio hummed softly like a friend.

Auntie May gave Pippa a gentle hug. “Bravo! You learned to count beats. You practiced safely. You helped make the guest room lovely.”

Pippa hugged back. Her heart felt light and warm. “Today I learned: Dance can teach me to listen and count. Dance can help me move with care. Dance can help me help others.”

Auntie May nodded. “Yes. When we dance, we learn with our bodies, our brains, and our hearts. We can be kind and helpful while we have fun.”

Pippa looked around the guest room. It was ready for any guest, with fluffy pillows and neat towels. She smiled her biggest penguin smile.

“Tomorrow,” Pippa said, “I will dance and help again.”

The End.
