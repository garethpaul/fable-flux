# Ollie Owl and the Tidy Time Map

In a comfortable nursery with soft rugs and sunshine stripes on the floor, a wise old owl named Ollie blinked his big golden eyes. The room smelled like clean cotton and crayons. Toys made gentle clinks as a breeze tickled a mobile of tiny paper stars.

“Good morning,” hooted Ollie in a warm, low voice. “Today is a good day to get ready for big ideas.”

Ollie fluffed his speckled feathers and looked around. Blocks were scattered like little mountains. Books were open like sleepy mouths. Crayons rested under a chair, quiet and shy.

Ollie smiled. “I see a puzzle,” he said. “A fun one! It is called Tidy Time.”

A small teddy bear with a blue bow, a soft giraffe with a friendly grin, and a round red ball listened closely. The teddy bear whispered, “What is Tidy Time?” The ball rolled and squeaked, “Is it a game?” The giraffe nodded, “Is there a map?”

Ollie blinked and chuckled. “Yes, friends. It is a game. And we do have a map.” He spread his wings and pointed to a big poster on the wall. It had colors and pictures: a square for blocks, a basket for balls, a shelf for books, a cup for crayons. At the top it said, “Our Tidy Time Map.”

“We can do this,” Ollie said kindly. “We will put things where they belong. When we are organized, we find things fast. We feel calm. We help our world.”

“Our world?” asked the teddy bear.

Ollie nodded. “Our nursery is a tiny part of a big, big world. People around the globe care for their places, too. They sort, they share, they clean. When we use what we have and keep it safe, we help everyone, near and far.”

The room felt bright and brave. Ollie tapped his wing to set a rhythm. Tap, tap. “Let’s begin.”

First, the blocks. Ollie hooted a little song. “Blocks go click, blocks go stack, blocks go home and never lose track.” He picked up a blue block. “Blue to the blue bin,” he said. The teddy bear lifted a red block and giggled. “Red to the red bin!” Together they made a happy clack, clack, clack as blocks found their boxes.

Next, the books. The giraffe used its long neck to nudge pages gently. “Books rest on the shelf,” Ollie said softly. “Spines out, covers in, like friends in a line.” The books made a cozy thump as they lined up. “Now we can read faster,” the giraffe said. “And we won’t step on stories,” Ollie added with a wink.

The ball rolled to its basket. “I can do it myself!” it squeaked. It hopped up with a soft thud. “Good job,” said Ollie. “That is assertiveness. When you speak up and help, you are strong and kind.”

The crayons peeped from under the chair. “We feel lost,” they whispered. Ollie crouched and hooted gently. “I hear you. You belong in the cup.” He showed them the cup on the table. “In you go, color friends.” The crayons clicked like tiny raindrops as they settled. “We did it!” they chimed.

Ollie pointed back to the map. “We followed the plan. We worked as a team. This is organization.” He fluttered to the window and looked at the bright sky. “All over the world, children sort toys, put away books, and care for their homes. When we tidy, we save time, we save things, and we save energy. That helps our whole planet.”

The teddy bear looked proud. “We can help every day,” it said.

“Yes,” Ollie agreed. “Daily steps make big change. Small wings, big wisdom.”

Just then, a tiny rattle rolled out from under the rug and bumped Ollie’s foot. “Oh!” said the rattle. “I am late!”

Ollie smiled kindly but firmly. “Rattle, your home is the music box,” he said in a clear voice. “We keep you there so you are safe and ready.”

The rattle wiggled. “I like to dance.”

“You can dance,” Ollie said, “and when the song is done, you go home.” His voice was calm and sure. “That is our rule.”

The rattle jingled happily and rolled to the music box with a soft ring-a-ling. “Thank you for telling me,” it said.

The room felt light. The rugs were smooth. The shelves stood tall and neat. The air smelled like lemon wood and warm naps. Ollie stretched his wings and gave a content hoot.

“Look,” he said. “Now we have space to play, space to read, and space to dream.”

The friends gathered in a circle. “What did we learn?” asked Ollie.

The giraffe raised a hoof. “We learned to follow the Tidy Time Map.”

The ball bounced once. “We learned to speak up and help.”

The teddy bear hugged itself. “We learned that being organized helps our whole world.”

Ollie’s eyes shone. “Yes. Organization means finding a home for every thing and putting every thing in its home. It helps us be calm, be kind, and be ready. When we care for our small place, we care for our big planet.”

He blinked slowly. “Tomorrow, we will tidy again. A little each day.”

The mobile of paper stars twirled softly. The room hummed with happy quiet.

“Owl hoot hooray!” the friends cheered.

Ollie bowed. “Owl hoot hooray,” he echoed, warm and proud.

The End.
