# Hoot and the Calm-Down Dance

High in a cozy treehouse, a wise old owl named Hoot lived among soft pillows, leafy curtains, and strings of shiny pinecones. The air smelled like warm wood and sweet apples. Sunlight peeped through round windows, painting gold circles on the floor.

One breezy morning, Hoot set out a basket of bright scarves. “Today we learn a calm-down dance,” Hoot hooted gently. “When our feelings get big, our bodies can help our hearts.”

Little animals climbed into the treehouse. Squeak the mouse twitched his whiskers. Rolo the raccoon rubbed sleepy eyes. Pip the squirrel bounced in fast, chattering. Thump, thump, thump went Pip’s feet on the wooden floor.

“Welcome, friends,” said Hoot. “We will use our words and our bodies in kind, peaceful ways.”

“I like to twirl!” Pip squeaked, grabbing a blue scarf.

“I like quiet,” Squeak whispered, hugging a green one.

Hoot nodded. “We can like different things. We can talk and listen. We can share.”

Hoot clapped soft wings. “First, a warm-up. Wiggle toes. Wiggle nose.” Everyone giggled and wiggled.

Then Hoot placed a small wooden drum in the center. “We take turns,” Hoot explained. “One at a time, we dance while the drum taps.”

Pip jumped up. “Me first!” He pounced on the drum and banged it fast—BOOM-BOOM-BOOM! The room shook with sound. Squeak covered his ears. “Too loud!” he squealed.

“Hey! My ears!” Rolo frowned, hands over his head.

Hoot lifted a calm wing. “Pause,” Hoot said. The room went still. The sunlight felt warm again. “Pip, your body is full of energy. That can feel big and stormy. We will learn to calm the storm.”

Pip’s tail flicked. “I’m not a storm,” he muttered, cheeks hot. “I just want to go fast.” His heart thumped hard. He felt a squeeze in his chest and heat in his face. Angry bubbles fizzed inside.

Hoot nodded kindly. “It is okay to feel mad or fast. Feelings come and go. We can help them. Would you like help?”

Pip looked at Squeak and Rolo. They looked a little scared. Pip felt a twist in his tummy. “Yes. Help.”

“Thank you for telling me,” said Hoot. “Friends, when we feel mad, we can stop, notice, and choose. Stop. Notice your body. Choose a calm action.”

Hoot tapped the drum gently. tap… tap… tap… “Let’s try the Calm-Down Dance.”

Hoot spread a soft scarf on the floor. “Step one: Hoot Breath. Breathe in like you smell a flower.” Everyone sniffed. “Breathe out like you blow a feather.” They blew. A tiny feather floated and danced in the sunbeams. Pip watched it with wide eyes. His shoulders dropped a little.

“Step two: Owl Wings.” Hoot lifted wings slow, up and down. “Up… and down… slow and smooth.” The animals moved their arms like wings. Whoosh… whoosh… The room sounded like gentle wind in leaves.

“Step three: Peaceful Paws.” Hoot pressed feathered tips together. “Hands together, press, then soften.” Press… soften… Pip felt the strength in his little paws, then a soft melt. His tummy felt less twisty.

“Step four: Hoot and Listen.” Hoot cupped an ear. “Say how you feel. Then listen to others.”

Pip took a Hoot Breath. “I feel mad when I wait. I want to go fast,” he said, voice small but clear.

Squeak lowered his paws from his ears. “I feel scared when it’s loud,” he said.

Rolo nodded. “I feel grumpy when I cannot hear myself think.”

Hoot smiled. “Thank you for using peaceful words. Now we can solve the problem.”

Pip looked at the drum. “Can I try again? I will go slower.”

Hoot tapped a steady beat. tap… tap… tap… Pip matched it. Boom… boom… boom… soft and steady. He swayed his tail. He lifted his arms like owl wings. He breathed in and out. Squeak peeked out, then smiled. “That’s nice,” Squeak said.

“Your turn, Squeak,” Hoot invited.

Squeak tiptoed to the center. He danced tiny steps like raindrops. Pip clapped quietly with Peaceful Paws. Rolo took a turn next, rolling and spinning slowly, making everyone laugh with happy, gentle chuckles.

When it was Pip’s turn again, his feet wanted to go fast. He felt a spark in his chest. “Uh-oh,” he said. “Big feeling.” He remembered: Stop. Notice. Choose. He placed his hands together. Press… soften… He took a Hoot Breath. In… and out… Then he whispered, “I want to go fast. Can I have a fast turn after Squeak has a quiet turn?”

“Yes,” said Squeak. “And I will cover my ears a little for fast time.”

“And I will count to ten,” said Hoot, “so fast time has a start and an end.”

Pip nodded. Hoot counted, “One… two…” Pip danced fast, feet pattering like rain on the roof. The drum tapped quick but not too loud. Squeak covered his ears a tiny bit and still smiled. “Nine… ten,” said Hoot. “And now slow.” The room softened back to calm.

Everyone cheered. The pinecones clicked like tiny bells. The air felt bright and sweet.

Hoot gathered the friends close. “What did we learn?”

Pip raised his paw. “When I feel mad or fast, I can stop, notice my body, and choose a calm action. I can breathe, make owl wings, and use peaceful words.”

Squeak squeaked, “I can say how I feel and ask for quiet.”

Rolo grinned. “We can take turns and make a plan.”

Hoot’s eyes twinkled. “Yes. Big feelings are okay. We can dance them in kind ways. We can speak with peace. We can listen with care.”

They all did one more Calm-Down Dance together—breathing, winging, pressing Peaceful Paws, and sharing words. The treehouse felt safe and warm. The sunlight made little stars on the floor, and the tiny feather floated once more, soft as a whisper.

Pip hugged the blue scarf. “I’m not a storm,” he said with a smile. “I’m a dancer who knows how to calm down.”

“And a good friend,” Hoot added.

The End.
