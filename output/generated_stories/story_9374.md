# Penny's Porch Dance

Penny the penguin loved her cozy front porch. Every morning, she would waddle outside and do her happy dance on the smooth wooden boards. The porch creaked softly under her orange feet as she spun and twirled.

"Good morning, sunshine!" Penny called to the bright yellow sun. She flapped her little wings and did three spins in a row. The cool morning breeze felt wonderful on her black and white feathers.

But today was different. As Penny began her favorite wiggle-waggle dance move, she bumped into Mrs. Robin's beautiful flower pot. CRASH! The pretty red pot tumbled off the porch railing and broke into pieces on the ground below.

"Oh no!" gasped Penny, covering her beak with her wing. The colorful flowers lay scattered in the dirt, and pieces of the broken pot sparkled in the morning light.

Mrs. Robin flew down from her nest in the oak tree. Her bright red breast looked worried. "My flower pot!" she chirped sadly. "I planted those flowers for my babies to enjoy."

Penny's tummy felt heavy and sad. She had made a big mistake. Her orange feet shuffled nervously on the porch boards. "I... I was just dancing," she whispered, looking down at her feet.

Mrs. Robin's small brown eyes looked hurt. "Dancing is wonderful, Penny, but you need to be more careful."

Penny knew what she had to do. She took a deep breath and waddled closer to Mrs. Robin. "I'm very sorry," she said in her clearest voice. "I didn't mean to break your flower pot. I was dancing and not watching where I was going."

Mrs. Robin's feathers ruffled softly in the breeze. "Thank you for saying sorry, Penny. That makes me feel better."

"But how can I fix this?" Penny asked, looking at the broken pieces. She wanted to make things right again.

Penny thought and thought. Then her eyes lit up like tiny stars. "I have an idea! Wait right here!"

She waddled quickly into her house and came back with her favorite blue watering can and a small shovel. "We can plant the flowers in my garden! And I'll help you find a new pot at the garden store."

Mrs. Robin's beak curved into a smile. "That's a wonderful idea, Penny!"

Together, they carefully picked up each flower and planted them in Penny's sunny garden patch. Penny used her little shovel to dig holes, and Mrs. Robin arranged the flowers just right. The purple petunias and yellow marigolds looked beautiful in their new home.

"Now," said Penny, "I want to show you my careful dancing. I'll make sure to look around first and dance in the middle of the porch where it's safe."

Penny did a gentle, careful dance. She spun slowly, checked around her, and did her wiggle-waggle move right in the center of the porch. Mrs. Robin clapped her wings happily.

"That's beautiful dancing, Penny! And very thoughtful too."

From that day on, Penny always looked around carefully before dancing on her porch. She learned that saying sorry when you make a mistake helps everyone feel better, and thinking of ways to fix problems makes everything even better.

And every morning, Mrs. Robin would watch Penny's careful, joyful dances from her new favorite spot in the garden.

The End.
