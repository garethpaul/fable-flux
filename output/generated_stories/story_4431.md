# Polly Paints with Courage

In a cozy bedroom, soft and warm, a little night-light glowed like a tiny moon. The bed had a blanket with stars. A gentle hum came from a small fan, and the room smelled like clean soap and lavender.

On the pillow sat Polly, a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a splash of blue on her tail. Her beak was shiny, and her eyes were big and kind.

“I have an idea!” Polly chirped. “I will make a big, brave painting!”

She looked at the art box on the desk. Paints in red, blue, yellow, and green. Soft brushes. A cup of water. Paper. The paper made a soft crinkle sound when Polly touched it.

But Polly remembered a rule. “Paint only on paper,” she whispered. “Not on walls. Not on the bed.”

Polly took a deep breath. “I can do it,” she said. “I can be brave and careful.”

She dipped her brush in blue. Swish, swish. The color made a pretty ribbon on the paper. She added yellow dots. Tap, tap. She laughed. “It looks like sunshine!”

Polly heard the door. “Polly, are you painting?” called Mia, the child who loved Polly very much.

“Yes, Mia!” Polly said. “Come see!”

Mia stepped in, wearing soft pajamas. “Hi, Polly,” she said, smiling. “I love your colors.”

Polly felt proud. She wanted to make the painting even bigger. She wanted to show her brave heart.

“I will paint a big ocean,” Polly said. “Waves like whoosh!” She moved the brush wide and fast.

Oops! The brush slipped. A blue drip fell. Plop! It fell on the blanket.

Polly froze. Her feathers felt hot. Her tummy felt twisty. “Oh no,” she whispered. “I made a mess.”

Mia looked. The blue spot was small, but it was still on the star blanket.

Polly’s heart went boom-boom. She wanted to hide. She wanted to pretend it was not her. But she remembered something. Courage means telling the truth, even when you feel scared. Courage means fixing mistakes.

Polly took a slow breath in and out. “Mia,” she said softly, “I made a drip on the blanket. I am sorry.” She looked at the floor. “I did not want to. I was excited.”

Mia knelt by Polly. “Thank you for telling me the truth,” Mia said. Her voice was calm and gentle. “That was brave.”

Polly blinked. “Brave?” she asked.

“Yes,” Mia said. “You told me, and you said sorry. That is courage.”

Polly felt a tiny warm glow inside. “What can we do?” she asked.

“We can clean it together,” Mia said. She went to the little basket by the bed. It held tissues, a small towel, and a bottle with a clean, soapy smell.

“First, we dab,” Mia said. Dab, dab, dab went the towel. The blue spot started to fade.

Polly dabbed too with a small tissue. “Dab, dab,” she chirped. “We are helpers!”

They had to wait a little bit. “We need patience,” Mia said. “Let the soap do its job.”

Polly nodded. Waiting was hard. She wanted the spot to go away fast. She wanted to paint again. But she remembered: patience is part of courage too. She took little breaths and looked at her paper.

While they waited, Mia said, “Let’s put a sheet of paper under your painting next time, to keep the bed clean.”

“That is smart hygiene,” Polly said proudly. “Clean and careful!”

When the spot was light and almost gone, Mia smiled. “All clean enough,” she said. The blanket smelled fresh, like bubbles.

Polly flapped her wings once. “Thank you for helping me,” she said. “I feel better.”

“You showed courage,” Mia said. “Now, do you want to try again?”

“Yes,” Polly said. “But I will set up my art space.” She placed a big sheet of paper on the desk. She put the cup of water on a little tray. She put a towel nearby. Tap-tap went her claws on the desk as she worked.

Polly looked at her brushes. “I am ready,” she said. “I am brave, and I am careful.”

She painted the ocean again. Swish, whoosh, wave! She painted a little boat with a tiny flag. She painted a bright sun with smiley rays. She even painted a parrot on the boat. The parrot looked brave and happy.

Mia clapped softly. “Polly, it is beautiful,” she said.

Polly puffed her feathers. “Thank you,” she chirped. “I used courage to say sorry. I used courage to try again. I used patience to wait. And I used clean, smart steps to take care of our room.”

She signed the paper with a little blue dot and a tiny feather print. “Art is my brave voice,” she said.

They hung the picture above the desk. The cozy bedroom felt even warmer and brighter. The fan hummed. The night-light glowed. The blanket was clean, and the air smelled sweet.

Polly cuddled into the pillow. “Today I learned,” she whispered, “that courage is telling the truth, fixing mistakes, and trying again.”

Mia tucked in the blanket. “Yes,” she said. “And your creative heart makes our room shine.”

Polly closed her eyes with a soft peep. “Tomorrow,” she said, “I will make more brave art.”

The End.
