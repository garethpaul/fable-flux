# Momo’s Bubble-Time Plan

Momo was a courageous monkey with bright eyes and warm, gentle paws. One sunny morning, Momo hopped into the laundry room. The room smelled like fresh soap and warm towels. The washer hummed, “Whirr, whirr,” and the dryer sang, “Tum-tee-tum.” Socks sat like sleepy little clouds.

Momo loved helping. “Today, I will make the laundry perfect,” Momo said, patting a basket. “I can be brave and smart.”

Momo started fast. He tossed shirts high in the air. “Whee!” he giggled. But a red sock slipped into the white pile. A cap rolled under the washer. A bottle of soap tilted… “Uh-oh,” said Momo. A drip… then another… then splish! Blue soap slid onto the floor like a shiny river.

Momo’s tail drooped. “I made a mess,” he whispered. His tummy felt tight. His cheeks grew warm. He felt like hiding in a laundry basket.

Just then, Mama came by. She wore a bright scarf with tiny flowers from Grandma’s country. It smelled like lavender and home.

“Momo,” Mama said softly, “what happened?”

Momo took a big, slow breath, in and out, like a balloon. “I went too fast,” he said. “The soap spilled. I wanted to help.”

Mama knelt down. “Thank you for being honest. We can fix a mess. Let’s use our calm monkey minds. First, we breathe.”

Momo nodded. Together they breathed: in… out… in… out. The tight feeling softened. Momo’s shoulders relaxed.

“Next,” Mama said, “let’s make a plan. Healthy habits help us feel safe. We do one step at a time.”

Momo’s eyes sparkled. “A plan! Can I write it?”

Mama smiled. “Yes. Writing helps our minds remember.”

Momo carried a small notebook from the shelf. The cover had stickers from places Grandma told him about—colorful markets, music nights, and big family dinners. Momo liked to hear stories about different places and how people cared for clothes with love and respect.

He took a fat crayon. Scratch, scratch. “Step 1: Breathe.” He drew a puffy cloud for breathing.

“Step 2,” Mama said, “wipe up the soap.”

Scratch, scratch. Momo drew a big blue puddle with a smile.

“Step 3: Sort clothes by colors,” Momo added. He drew red, white, and dark dots.

“Step 4,” Mama said, “use the right scoop. Not too much.”

Momo drew a tiny spoon and a happy washer. He looked up. “This is our Bubble-Time Plan!”

They followed the plan. Momo held a towel. Squish, squish. The soap felt cool and slippery. “It smells like lemons,” he giggled.

“Lemons from the garden,” Mama said, wringing the towel. “Some families use soap nuts from faraway trees. People around the world have many ways to clean and care.”

Momo’s ears perked up. “I like learning what others do. We can respect all the ways.”

They sorted together. “Reds here,” Momo said, placing the red sock in the right pile. “Whites there. Darks over here.” He moved slowly. His tail swished, steady and calm.

A little cap peeked from under the washer. Momo took a mindful breath and reached carefully. “Got you,” he said. He dusted it gently. “All clothes are special. We treat them with care.”

Mama nodded. “That is respect.”

Momo stood tall. “I will write more steps.” He added, “Step 5: Check pockets.” He patted each pocket. He found a shiny button. Click, click. “Treasure!”

“Step 6: Set a timer,” Mama said. “Then we rest while we wait.”

Momo pressed the washer button. “Whirr, whirr,” it began. He set a sand timer on the shelf. Tiny grains danced down.

Now they waited. Momo felt wiggly inside. He wanted to open the door. He wanted to jump. He wanted to do ten things at once.

Mama sat on a folded towel. “Let’s practice mindful sitting,” she said. “Hands on belly. Feel your breath.”

Momo sat. His belly rose. His belly fell. He listened. “Whirr, whirr.” He smelled warm soap. He felt soft towels under his paws. He whispered, “I am calm. I am kind. I can wait.”

When the sand ran out, the washer stopped. Click.

Momo opened the door slowly. Warm steam puffed out like a tiny cloud. “Ahh,” he said. “It’s cozy.”

They moved wet clothes to the dryer. Plop, plop. Momo measured a small scoop, just like his drawing. Then he wrote, “Step 7: Fold with love.”

When the clothes were dry, Momo folded with care. He lined up the scarf with little flowers. He smoothed a shirt with smiling suns. He placed socks together like best friends.

“All done,” Momo said. He read from his notebook. “We breathed. We cleaned. We sorted. We waited. We respected our things. We wrote our plan.”

Mama hugged him. “You were brave and mindful. You used healthy habits. I am proud.”

Momo grinned. His tummy felt light. “I learned that going fast is not always best. When I slow down, breathe, write a plan, and show respect, I can fix problems.”

He taped the Bubble-Time Plan on the wall. “Now we can use it every time,” he said. “And I can share it with friends. Everyone has different ways, and we can learn from each other.”

The laundry room hummed happily. “Whirr, whirr, tum-tee-tum.” Momo wiggled his tail and gave a little bow to the room. “Thank you, clothes. Thank you, soap. Thank you, plan.”

He took one more calm breath and smiled.

The End.
