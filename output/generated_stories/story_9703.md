# Benny Beaver Finds the Beat

Benny the busy beaver rushed into the musical practice room with a clack-clack of his little wooden tail. The room smelled like fresh pine and lemon polish. Bright drums glowed red. Shiny bells winked. A piano sat like a big, friendly whale, its keys black and white like little teeth.

“I will play all the sounds!” Benny said. His whiskers twitched with excitement. “Loud and fast!”

He grabbed two drumsticks. Tap! Tap! BOOM! The big drum rattled. He rang the tiny triangle too—ting, ting—and pressed the piano keys with his paws—plinka-plonk-plink! All the sounds tangled together like a pile of sticks.

Miss Melody, the music teacher, smiled kindly. She wore a scarf with tiny notes on it. “Hello, Benny. I can hear you are very excited.”

“I want to be the best player today,” Benny said. His eyes were bright. “I will do it now, now, now!”

Miss Melody nodded. “Being the best takes time. We grow by trying, listening, and trying again. Would you like to learn a steady beat?”

Benny puffed his cheeks. “But I want to play fast!” He tapped faster—tap-tap-tap—until his paws got tangled. The sticks slipped. Clatter! One rolled under the piano.

“Oh no,” Benny said softly. His ears drooped. “I can’t do it.”

Miss Melody crouched beside him. “When sticks fall, we can pick them up and learn. That’s a growth mindset. It means we say, ‘I can learn this if I practice and listen.’”

Benny took a deep breath. He listened. The room hummed gently. Far away, a clock tick-tocked. Tick. Tock. Tick. Tock.

“I hear it,” Benny whispered. “Like a tiny footstep.”

“That’s our beat,” Ms. Melody said. “Let’s try a strategy. Step one: listen. Step two: go slow. Step three: try again.”

Benny’s whiskers perked up. “Listen. Go slow. Try again.” He liked steps. Steps felt like a tidy dam.

He found his runaway stick and sat on a little round stool. Miss Melody tapped her foot softly. “We clap on tick,” she said. “One and two and—clap.” Her clap was gentle, like a leaf touching water.

Benny held the sticks over the drum. He listened to the tick-tock. “Now?” he asked.

“Now,” Miss Melody said.

Benny tapped once. Boom. The sound was warm and round, like a hug. He smiled. “Again?”

“Again,” said Miss Melody.

Boom. Boom. Boom. Benny kept the beat. His tail swished happily. The drum felt friendly now, not bossy.

A little bunny named Dot peeked in, her ears tall. “That sounds nice,” she said. “So steady!”

Benny beamed. “I am learning the beat. I used a plan!”

Miss Melody nodded. “Would you like to add the triangle on every fourth boom?”

Benny blinked. “That is a lot.” His tummy made a flutter. He looked at the triangle. It looked like a tiny slice of moon.

“Let’s use our strategy again,” Miss Melody said softly. “Step one?”

“Listen,” Benny said. He closed his eyes. Tick. Tock. Boom, boom, boom, boom—ting.

“Step two?” she asked.

“Go slow,” Benny said. He moved his paws like quiet fish.

“Step three?” Miss Melody whispered.

“Try again,” Benny said, and he did: boom, boom, boom, boom—ting. The triangle sang like a raindrop on a leaf.

Dot clapped her paws. “Pretty!”

Benny’s chest felt big and proud. He remembered when his sticks fell. Now his sounds were friends. He wasn’t the fastest. He was steady and kind with the beat.

Miss Melody walked to the piano and played just three notes: plink, plonk, plinka. “Would you like to listen and add your beat under my notes?”

Benny listened first. He could feel the notes like small waves. He tapped slow: boom… boom… boom… Then he counted in a whisper, “One… two… three… four… ting.”

The room was full of soft sounds—drum heartbeats, a sweet triangle drip, and the piano’s gentle swimming notes. Benny’s ears tingled. He felt calm. He felt ready.

Dot picked up two maracas and shook them softly, swish-swish on the second and third beats. “I will listen too,” she said.

They made music together. Sometimes Benny slipped and tinged the triangle too soon. He paused, listened for the tick-tock, and tried again. Each time he fixed it, his smile grew.

Miss Melody played a little ending and lifted her hands. The music floated down like a feather. Silence felt like a friendly hush.

Benny put down his sticks. “I did it. Not all at once. Step by step.”

“What did you learn?” Miss Melody asked.

Benny tapped his chest. “I learned that I can grow. When it’s hard, I listen, go slow, and try again. That is my plan.”

Dot nodded. “That is a smart strategy.”

Miss Melody smiled. “Your ears were your best helpers.”

Benny giggled. “And my heart,” he said, patting the drum. “Boom-boom.”

The clock tick-tocked. Benny listened and hummed along. He felt brave for next time. He knew he could learn new songs, one steady beat at a time, with his listening ears and his try-again plan.

The End.
