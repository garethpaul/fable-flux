# Ollie Otter's Musical Gift

Ollie the otter loved to splash and play, but today was special. He wiggled his whiskers as he walked into the bright musical practice room at the community center. The room smelled like wood polish and had shiny instruments everywhere.

"Wow!" said Ollie, his dark eyes sparkling. "Look at all these beautiful instruments!"

Mrs. Robin, the music teacher, smiled warmly. "Hello, Ollie! Are you here for the children's music class?"

Ollie nodded eagerly, his sleek brown fur still damp from his morning swim. "Yes! But I brought something special to share."

From his waterproof pouch, Ollie carefully pulled out a set of smooth river stones. Each stone was a different size and made a different musical sound when tapped.

"These are river drums," Ollie explained proudly. "My grandmother taught me how to play them. Her grandmother taught her, and her grandmother's grandmother taught her too!"

Mrs. Robin's eyes lit up. "What a wonderful tradition! Would you like to share it with the other children?"

Soon, little bear cubs, bunny kits, and fox pups filled the practice room. They sat in a circle on the soft carpet, their eyes wide with curiosity.

"This is Ollie," said Mrs. Robin. "He's going to share a very special family tradition with us."

Ollie felt a little nervous, but he remembered his grandmother's gentle words: "Sharing our traditions helps keep them alive and brings friends together."

He placed the river stones in front of him. "These stones come from the river where my family has lived for many, many years," Ollie began. "Each stone sings a different song."

*Tap, tap, ping!* The smallest stone made a high, sweet sound.

*Thunk, thunk, thunk!* The biggest stone made a deep, warm sound.

"Oh!" gasped little Benny Bunny. "They really do sing!"

Ollie's confidence grew. He showed the children how to hold their paws just right and how to tap gently. "My grandmother says the stones like to be treated kindly, just like friends."

Soon, all the children were tapping their own rhythm stones that Mrs. Robin had brought out. The practice room filled with a beautiful, bouncing beat.

*Tap-ping-thunk, tap-ping-thunk!*

"This is so much fun!" squeaked Penny Fox. "Can you teach us the song your grandmother taught you?"

Ollie beamed. He taught them a simple river song about fish swimming and water flowing. The children's voices mixed with the stone music, creating something magical.

When the class ended, Benny Bunny raised his paw. "Ollie, thank you for sharing your family's music. Now we can share it with our families too!"

"That's exactly right," said Mrs. Robin. "When we share our traditions, they grow stronger and bring more joy to everyone."

Ollie felt warm and happy inside. He had been worried that his river drums might seem different or strange, but instead, everyone loved learning about them.

As the children hugged goodbye, Ollie realized something important. Sharing his family's tradition didn't make it less special – it made it more special! Now his grandmother's music would live in more hearts and homes.

Walking home by the river, Ollie couldn't wait to tell his grandmother about his day. He had learned that traditions are like seeds – when you plant them with others, they grow into beautiful gardens of friendship and understanding.

The End.
