# Daisy Fawn's Important Day

Daisy Fawn loved spending quiet time in Mama's cozy home office. The soft carpet felt warm under her tiny hooves, and sunlight streamed through the big window, making golden patches on the floor.

Daisy had spotted fur with white dots, and the sweetest brown eyes. She was learning to be a big girl, just like Mama Deer who worked at her desk with the glowing computer screen.

This morning, Daisy felt very tired. Her little legs were wobbly, and her tummy rumbled loudly. "Grumble-grumble!" it said.

"Mama," said Daisy softly, "I'm hungry and sleepy."

But Mama Deer was typing *click-click-click* on her keyboard. She had an important meeting with other grown-up deer on the computer screen.

"Just a few more minutes, sweetie," Mama said without looking up.

Daisy's tummy rumbled again. Her eyes felt heavy and droopy. She wanted to ask Mama for breakfast and maybe a cozy nap, but Mama looked so busy.

"I don't want to bother Mama," thought Daisy. "I should be a good little fawn and wait quietly."

So Daisy curled up on the soft rug and tried to ignore her hungry tummy and sleepy eyes. But waiting made her feel worse and worse. Her head felt fuzzy, and her legs felt shaky.

Soon, Daisy started to feel grumpy. When a pencil rolled near her, she kicked it away with her hoof. *Thunk!* When her favorite stuffed bunny was nearby, she turned away from it.

"Why do I feel so cranky?" wondered Daisy.

Just then, Grandpa Deer peeked into the office. His wise eyes noticed Daisy right away.

"Hello, little one," said Grandpa gently. "You look like you need some care."

Daisy's ears drooped. "I'm hungry and tired, but Mama is working. I don't want to interrupt."

Grandpa Deer smiled kindly. "Oh, sweet Daisy. Taking care of yourself isn't bothering anyone. It's very important! When our bodies need food and rest, we must listen."

He helped Daisy stand on her wobbly legs. "Let's tell Mama what you need. She wants to help you feel better."

Together, they walked to Mama's desk. "Mama," said Daisy in her small voice, "I need breakfast and maybe a nap. My body feels tired and hungry."

Mama Deer immediately stopped typing and turned around. "Oh my! Thank you for telling me, sweetheart. Your needs are always important to me."

Mama scooped Daisy into a warm hug that smelled like flowers. "Taking care of yourself helps you feel happy and strong. And when you feel good, you can be kind to others too."

They went to the kitchen where Mama made Daisy's favorite breakfast: sweet clover pancakes with honey. The warm food filled Daisy's tummy and made her feel much better.

After breakfast, Mama tucked Daisy into her cozy bed for a nap. The soft blankets felt like a warm cloud.

"Remember," whispered Mama, "always tell someone when you need food, water, rest, or help. Taking care of yourself is not selfish - it's smart and important."

Daisy smiled sleepily. "I will remember, Mama. Self-care helps me be the best little fawn I can be."

When Daisy woke up from her nap, she felt wonderful! Her energy was back, her tummy was happy, and she felt ready to play and learn.

From that day on, Daisy always spoke up when her body needed something. She learned that taking care of herself helped her be kind, happy, and strong.

The End.
