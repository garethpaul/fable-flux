# Tessa Tortoise Makes a Plan

In a sunny little home office, with a soft rug and a big leafy plant, lived Tessa the wise tortoise. The room smelled like fresh paper and warm tea. The window was open, and a tiny breeze tickled Tessa’s nose. Tap, tap, tap went the keyboard. Click, click, click went the mouse. Tessa wore small round glasses, and her shell felt cozy against the rug.

“Good morning, desk!” Tessa said. “Good morning, plant! Good morning, work!”

On the wall, there was a bright chart with stickers. It showed Tessa’s healthy habits: Stretch, Sip Water, Snack Smart, and Rest Eyes. She loved to do good things for her body and mind.

Just then, there was a knock. Knock-knock!

Tessa peeked over her glasses. “Come in, friends!”

In came Pip the playful puppy and Mina the gentle mouse. Pip’s tail wagged so fast it made a soft whoosh-whoosh sound. Mina carried a tiny basket that smelled like cinnamon and apples.

“Tessa!” Pip barked. “Let’s play jump-and-spin in the hallway. Right now!”

Mina squeaked, “And I brought apple slices and tiny crackers. It is Sharing Snack Day in my family. We tell a little story with each bite. It is our tradition!”

Tessa smiled. Her heart felt warm. She loved to play. She loved to learn about family traditions. But she also had work. Her screen showed a long list with little boxes to check.

Tessa took a deep breath. She remembered her lesson about negotiation. Negotiation means talking, listening, and finding a plan that works for everyone.

“Friends,” Tessa said softly, “I want to play. I want to share your special snack and your stories, Mina. I also need to finish my work. Let’s make a plan.”

Pip’s ears perked. “A plan? Like a game plan?”

Mina nodded. “A plan can be fair.”

Tessa touched her chart. The stickers shone like tiny stars. “We can make a plan that helps our healthy habits too. Listen.”

She raised one finger. “First, I work for ten quiet minutes. You can color at the little table.” She pointed to a basket filled with crayons that smelled like new wax and paper that felt smooth. “I’ll set a timer that goes beep-beep.”

Beep-beep, Tessa showed them the timer.

She raised a second finger. “Next, we all do Stretch Time. We stand up, reach high like trees, and roll our shoulders like slow wheels.”

Pip tried a tiny stretch and wiggled. “I can be a tree!”

She raised a third finger. “Then, Snack Story Time with Mina. We sit, sip water, and listen to the tradition story.”

Mina’s eyes shone. “Yes! In my family, we say, ‘Crunch and share, we always care.’”

Tessa raised a fourth finger. “Last, we have Play Time in the hallway. Jump-and-spin for five minutes. Then I rest my eyes, and you two tidy the crayons.”

Pip tilted his head. “But I want to play first. My paws are ready now.”

Mina bit her lip. “And I wanted to share the snack while it is still warm.”

Tessa nodded. She listened with her whole shell. “Thank you for telling me how you feel. Let’s see. What can we change to help everyone?”

She thought. Tap, tap, tap went her claw on the floor. “How about this: We make Work Time shorter. Only eight minutes. Then we do a tiny Play Time first—just two jumps and one spin. After that, Snack Story Time right away, so the apples are still warm. Then we stretch together. Then I rest my eyes for one minute, and you tidy crayons. After that, I finish my work while you color more. Deal?”

Pip’s tail thumped. “Two jumps and one spin? I can do that!”

Mina sniffed the basket and smiled. “Warm apples now? That is kind to my tradition.”

Tessa smiled back. “This is negotiation. We talked. We listened. We made a new plan together.”

They all put a hand, a paw, and a tiny paw in the middle. “Deal!”

Tessa set the timer. Beep! Work Time started. Pip and Mina colored quietly. The crayons shhh-shhh-shhed across the paper. Tessa typed, “Click-clack,” and checked two little boxes. The room hummed like a happy bee.

Beep! The timer sang. “Tiny Play Time!” Pip yipped. They stepped to the hallway. “One jump!” Pip bounced. “Two jumps!” Mina hopped. “One spin!” Tessa twirled slowly, her shell swish-swish-swish. They laughed.

Back at the desk, Mina opened the basket. The apples smelled sweet and warm. “Crunch and share, we always care,” she said. She told a story about her grandma who made apple slices for everyone and told jokes. Tessa and Pip listened, eyes bright, mouths munching, water cups cool in their hands.

“Stretch Time!” Tessa said gently. They reached up, up, up. They rolled their shoulders. They took three deep breaths. In. Out. In. Out. In. Out.

Then Tessa closed her eyes for a Rest Eyes minute. The room felt calm and cozy. Pip and Mina put crayons back in the basket. Plink, plink.

When Tessa opened her eyes, she finished her work with a happy heart. She checked the last box. “All done!”

Pip wagged. “We played!”

Mina beamed. “We shared my family tradition!”

Tessa nodded. “And we kept healthy habits.”

She hugged her friends. Her shell felt warm. The plant rustled like it was clapping. “Today we learned: When we negotiate, we listen and make a plan that is fair. We can share traditions, play, and stay healthy too.”

They all said together, “We talk. We listen. We care.”

The End.
