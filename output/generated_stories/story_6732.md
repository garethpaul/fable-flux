# Rocco’s Quiet Belly Beat

In a cozy house with blue curtains, a musical raccoon named Rocco tip-tapped into the dining room. His striped tail swished like a soft metronome. The table smelled like warm toast and sweet jam. Sunlight made tiny squares on the floor.

Rocco loved sounds. He loved clink, clink spoons. He loved tap, tap plates. He loved hum, hum songs. Today, he held his little drum. It was round and red. His drum made a friendly thump.

“Good morning, drum,” Rocco whispered. “Let’s play a happy hello.” He tapped, thump-thump, thump. It sounded like a slow heartbeat.

Mama Raccoon set down bowls. “Breakfast time, Rocco,” she said with a smile. “Oatmeal with berries.” The berries were shiny and blue. Steam curled up like tiny clouds.

Rocco’s tummy went “goooorrrgle.” He giggled. “My tummy is singing too!”

Mama chuckled. “I hear it. After breakfast, we can learn a new calm song.”

Rocco’s eyes got bright. “A calm song?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “A song for big feelings. Calm songs help our bodies feel safe.”

Rocco ate a warm spoonful. “Mmm, cozy,” he said. The oatmeal tasted soft and sweet. The spoon felt cool in his paw. He heard the clock say tick-tock, tick-tock.

Then—ding! The doorbell rang. It was loud and pointy. Rocco jumped. His drum bumped the chair. A fork fell with a clatter. His heart raced, fast-fast-fast.

“I feel tight,” Rocco said, pressing his paws to his chest. “My tummy feels wiggly.”

“That is a feeling,” Mama said gently. “Maybe surprise. Maybe “whoa!” feelings. Let’s notice them.”

Rocco nodded. “My face feels hot. My shoulders feel high.”

Mama’s voice was soft like a blanket. “Good noticing. Let’s try a calm plan.”

“What is the plan?” Rocco asked, his eyes big.

“First, we pause,” Mama said. “Feet on the floor.” Rocco placed his paws down. The floor felt cool and smooth.

“Second,” Mama said, “we breathe like a belly drum. In…and out.” She put a paw on her tummy. Rocco did too. He breathed in slowly. His tummy rose like a round balloon. He breathed out slowly. His tummy fell, soft and low.

“In for four,” Mama said. “One, two, three, four.” They breathed in. “Out for four,” she said. “One, two, three, four.” They breathed out.

Rocco listened. He heard the clock again: tick-tock. He heard the oatmeal bubble: blip-blip. He felt his shoulders drop. His heart slowed. Thump…thump.

“I feel softer,” Rocco said. “Like a pillow.”

Mama smiled. “That is calm. You used your knowledge. You noticed your body. You breathed. You paused.”

Rocco hugged his drum. “Can my drum help me remember?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Let’s make a calm song for your drum.”

Rocco tapped gently. Thump…thump. He sang in a whisper. “In, two, three, four. Out, two, three, four.” The drum answered with a slow beat. It felt like warm tea inside.

Ding-dong! The bell rang again. Rocco’s ears perked. He felt a tiny jump inside. He put a paw on his belly. “Hello, feeling,” he said. “I see you.” He breathed in…two, three, four. He breathed out…two, three, four. He tapped the calm beat: thump…thump.

The tight feeling faded, like fog in the sun. Rocco grinned. “I did it!”

Mama opened the door. It was Auntie Roo with a basket. The basket smelled like fresh bread. The crust crackled softly. “Good morning!” Auntie Roo said. “I brought a loaf.”

Rocco waved. “Hi, Auntie. I used my belly drum.”

Auntie Roo tilted her head. “Your belly drum?”

Rocco stood tall. “When the bell rang, I felt a big feeling. I put my paw here.” He patted his tummy. “I breathed slow. In for four. Out for four. I tapped a calm beat.” He showed her. Thump…thump. His drum made a cozy sound in the sunny room.

Auntie Roo clapped lightly. “What a smart raccoon! You know your feelings. You know what to do. That is good knowledge.”

They sat at the table. The bread was warm and buttery. The knife made a soft shhh as it sliced. Rocco spread jam. It sparkled like rubies.

As they ate, a glass wobbled. It almost tipped. Rocco felt another tiny jump. He noticed it. “Hello again, feeling,” he said. He breathed in, two, three, four. He breathed out, two, three, four. Calm came back, gentle as a hug.

Mama touched his paw. “I’m proud of you,” she said. “You found calm in the dining room.”

Rocco nodded. “Calm helps me hear, see, and think.” He looked around. “I hear the clock. I see the sun squares. I feel the chair under me. I taste sweet jam. I smell warm bread.”

“That is mindful,” Mama said. “Your senses are friends that guide you.”

Rocco tapped his drum softly. “My belly beat is my friend too.”

After breakfast, Rocco made a little rhyme:

“When big bells ring,
or small things fall,
I breathe in slow,
and I stand tall.
In, two, three, four,
out, two, three, four,
I find my calm,
from head to floor.”

He giggled. “That rhyme feels snug.”

Auntie Roo laughed kindly. “Rocco, you made music and calm at the same time.”

Rocco swished his tail. “Today, I learned a calm song. I can use it anytime.”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Calm helps our hearts, our minds, and our bodies.”

Rocco hugged his drum. The dining room felt safe and bright. He took one more belly breath. In…two, three, four. Out…two, three, four.

“I am calm,” he said.

The End.
