# Fox Kit’s Calm Porch Plan

On a sunny morning, a curious fox kit named Fenn sat on the peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like warm wood and pine needles. Birds chirped. A soft breeze tickled Fenn’s whiskers.

Fenn had a big plan. He wanted to make a special porch basket for treasures. He had shiny rocks, soft leaves, a tiny bell, and a blue ribbon. He lined them up in a row.

“I will make my porch look nice,” Fenn said. “I will put each thing in the basket.”

Fenn picked up the tiny bell. Ding-a-ling! The sound made him giggle. He placed it in the basket. Next, he reached for the blue ribbon.

Just then, a squirrel friend named Pip jumped onto the porch. Thump! Pip’s tail swished, and his paws skittered. “Hi, Fenn! Can I see?” Pip asked, eyes wide.

“Yes,” Fenn said. “But please be gentle.”

Pip nodded, but he was very excited. He touched the rocks. Clack, clack. He whisked the ribbon. Whoosh! The neat row wiggled, then tumbled. The shiny rocks rolled. The leaves fluttered to the porch boards.

Fenn’s face felt hot. His ears bent down. His tummy felt twisty. “Oh no,” he said in a small voice. “My row! My plan!”

Pip blinked. “Oops,” he said. “I didn’t mean to.”

Fenn wanted to shout. He wanted to stomp. He squeezed his paws. His heart thumped fast. The porch did not feel peaceful now. It felt messy and loud.

Fenn remembered Mama Fox’s calm words: “When big feelings come, stop, breathe, and choose.”

Fenn put a paw on his chest. He took a slow breath in like smelling a flower. Sniff. He blew out like blowing a feather. Whooo. He did it again. Sniff. Whooo.

His heart slowed. His ears lifted a little. He looked at Pip. Pip’s eyes were shiny. Pip looked sorry.

Fenn spoke softly. “I feel frustrated,” he said. “I worked hard. I wanted my row to stay.”

Pip nodded. “I understand,” he said. “I got too bouncy. I can help fix it.”

Fenn thought. He liked to help others, and he liked to be kind to himself too. “We can fix it together,” he said. “But let’s make a porch plan. That will help.”

“What is a porch plan?” Pip asked.

Fenn tapped the basket. Tap, tap. “First, we make calm. Then, we make rules. Then, we make rows again.”

Pip tilted his head. “Okay!”

Fenn showed the calm again. “Smell the flower,” he said. They sniffed. “Blow the feather.” They whoooed. They both giggled. The porch felt softer now.

“Now, the rules,” Fenn said. “This is my basket. Please ask before touching.” He pointed to the ribbon. “Gentle hands. One thing at a time.”

Pip said, “I will ask. I will be gentle.”

Fenn smiled. “Thank you. That is my boundary. It keeps my plan safe.” He liked the way the word boundary sounded, like a gentle fence around his things.

They began to organize. Fenn said, “Let’s sort by feel.” He whispered, “Soft leaves here.” Swish. “Hard rocks here.” Clack. “Jingly things here.” Ding-a-ling!

Pip watched with bright eyes. “May I hand you a leaf?” he asked.

“Yes,” Fenn said.

Pip picked up a leaf with slow, careful paws. He placed it in Fenn’s paw. Swish. “I did it gently,” Pip said.

“You did,” said Fenn. “Thank you for helping.”

A butterfly floated down and rested on the blue ribbon. Its wings looked like tiny fans. Fenn smiled at the soft flutter. “May I lift the ribbon now?” Pip asked.

“Yes,” Fenn said. “Lift it like you lift a cloud.”

Pip lifted. Whoosh, but very soft. They set the ribbon in the basket on top of the leaves. The porch looked neat and calm again.

Mama Fox peeked out the door. The warm smell of muffins floated out. “I see careful paws,” she said. “I see calm breaths. I see a team.”

Fenn’s chest felt warm and proud. “I felt frustrated,” Fenn told Mama. “I stopped. I breathed. I used words. We made a plan. Pip helped.”

Mama nodded. “That is smart and kind,” she said. “You kept your boundary and you let a friend help. That is good organization.”

Pip’s tail flicked with joy. “I learned to ask,” he said. “I learned to be gentle.”

Fenn looked at the porch basket. It was tidy. It was beautiful. He touched the tiny bell. Ding-a-ling! The sound was happy again.

He turned to Pip. “Do you want to make your own little pile?” he asked. “I can help you find a spot on the porch.”

Pip smiled big. “Yes! My own pile!”

Fenn said, “This is your space. You can choose what goes there.” He pointed to the other side of the porch. “We will keep our things cozy and safe.”

They worked side by side. Pip found a smooth acorn. Fenn found a feather. The breeze felt cool. The sun felt warm. The porch felt peaceful again.

Fenn whispered, “When feelings get big, I can stop, breathe, and choose.” Pip whispered it too.

They rang the tiny bell together. Ding-a-ling! Calm and clear.

The End.
