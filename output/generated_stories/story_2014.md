# Kody's Big Sorry

Kody the koala loved his cozy study corner. It had soft blue cushions, colorful books on low shelves, and a big window that let in warm sunshine. Every morning, Kody would climb down from his favorite eucalyptus tree and settle into his special reading spot.

"Good morning, books!" Kody would say cheerfully, patting the covers with his fuzzy paws.

One sunny Tuesday, Kody was reading his favorite book about butterflies when he heard tiny voices nearby.

"Excuse me," squeaked a small voice. "Could we please share your study corner? We want to learn about flowers."

Kody looked down and saw three little field mice standing politely by his cushions. Their whiskers twitched nervously, and they carried tiny notebooks made from leaves.

But Kody was feeling grumpy that morning. His tummy rumbled because he'd forgotten to eat breakfast eucalyptus leaves.

"No way!" Kody said loudly, hugging his book tight. "This is MY study corner. Go find somewhere else!"

The little mice's ears drooped sadly. "Oh," whispered the smallest mouse. "We're sorry to bother you." They shuffled away with their heads down, their leaf notebooks dragging on the ground.

Kody felt proud of himself for protecting his space. He opened his butterfly book again, but something felt different. The colorful pictures didn't seem as bright. The soft cushions didn't feel as cozy. Even the warm sunshine felt a little cold.

"Why don't I feel happy?" Kody wondered aloud, touching his fuzzy chest where his heart was beating.

He tried reading about beautiful blue butterflies, but he kept thinking about the mice's sad faces. He remembered how their little whiskers had drooped when he said no.

Suddenly, Kody discovered something important in his heart - a heavy, uncomfortable feeling.

"Oh no," he said softly. "I think I hurt their feelings. That wasn't very kind."

Kody closed his book and looked around his study corner. There was plenty of room for everyone! The cushions were big enough for him and three tiny mice. The bookshelf had lots of books about flowers that the mice would love.

"I made a mistake," Kody said to himself. "I need to say sorry."

Kody climbed down from his cushions and went looking for the three mice. He found them sitting under a mushroom, looking very sad.

"Hello," Kody said gently, sitting down so he was closer to their size. "I came to say I'm sorry. I was not kind to you, and that was wrong."

The mice looked up with surprise in their bright black eyes.

"I was grumpy because I was hungry," Kody explained. "But that's no excuse for being mean. Would you like to share my study corner? We could learn together!"

The smallest mouse's whiskers perked up. "Really? You mean it?"

"Yes!" Kody smiled his biggest smile. "I have books about flowers, and you can teach me what you discover!"

Soon, all four friends were snuggled together on the blue cushions. Kody read aloud from a book about sunflowers while the mice took notes in their leaf notebooks. They discovered that sunflowers always turn to face the sun!

"This is much more fun than studying alone," Kody said happily.

"Thank you for saying sorry," said the smallest mouse. "And thank you for sharing!"

From that day on, Kody's study corner became everyone's favorite learning place. And Kody learned that saying sorry when you make a mistake helps everyone feel better and makes friendships grow stronger than the tallest eucalyptus tree.

The End.
