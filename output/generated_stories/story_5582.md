# Splash's Special Story Time

Splash the dolphin loved visiting his friend Emma's house by the sea. Every morning, he would swim up to the shallow water near her peaceful front porch and say hello.

"Good morning, Splash!" Emma called out, sitting in her cozy rocking chair. The wooden porch creaked gently as she rocked back and forth. "Would you like to hear a special story today?"

Splash's gray skin sparkled in the warm sunlight as he bobbed up and down in the clear blue water. "Oh yes, please! I love your stories, Emma!"

Emma smiled and pulled out a beautiful, old book with golden pages. "This book belonged to my grandmother, and her grandmother before that. It's full of wonderful tales about the ocean and all the creatures who live there."

"Wow!" Splash clicked excitedly. "It looks very special!"

"It is," Emma said softly, running her fingers over the smooth cover. "Every day, my grandmother would sit on this same porch and read these stories to me. Now I get to share them with you!"

As Emma began to read, Splash listened carefully to the tale of a brave little seahorse who helped lost fish find their way home. The salty ocean breeze carried the sweet scent of jasmine flowers from the porch garden, and seagulls called softly in the distance.

"That seahorse was so kind!" Splash said when the story ended. "I want to help other sea creatures too!"

Emma nodded. "That's exactly what my grandmother taught me. She said these stories help us learn how to be good friends and neighbors."

Splash thought for a moment, then had a wonderful idea. "Emma, could I share a story tradition from my dolphin family too?"

"I would love that!" Emma said, closing the precious book carefully.

Splash took a deep breath and began to sing a beautiful dolphin song. His voice rose and fell like ocean waves, telling the story of how dolphins always take care of each other and share their food with those who are hungry.

"That was magical, Splash!" Emma clapped her hands. "What a beautiful tradition!"

"My mother taught me that song, and her mother taught her," Splash explained proudly. "We dolphins believe that sharing stories and songs makes everyone feel loved and connected."

Emma smiled warmly. "You know what? Let's make this our special tradition. Every morning, I'll share a story from my grandmother's book, and you can share a dolphin song or story from your family."

"That sounds perfect!" Splash splashed happily, sending tiny water droplets sparkling in the sunshine.

From that day forward, Emma and Splash met every morning on the peaceful porch. They would take turns sharing their family traditions - sometimes Emma would read ancient tales, and sometimes Splash would sing dolphin songs that had been passed down for generations.

Other sea creatures began to gather in the shallow water to listen. Soon, crabs, starfish, and colorful fish were sharing their own family stories too. The porch became a special place where everyone felt welcome to share the traditions that made them unique and proud.

"Sharing our family traditions makes us all closer friends," Emma said one sunny morning.

"And it helps us remember that even though we're different, we all have special stories to tell," Splash added with a joyful click.

The End.
