# Rikki Raccoon’s Kind Music Treehouse

Rikki the raccoon loved music. He had soft gray fur, bright curious eyes, and tiny paws that tapped like little drumsticks. Rikki lived in a sunny treehouse high in a friendly maple tree. The treehouse smelled like pine and fresh leaves. Colorful ribbons fluttered in the breeze. Rikki’s shiny tin can drums, bottle-cap bells, and stringy guitar made from yarn and a cereal box rested on a shelf.

“Good morning, music!” Rikki sang. His voice was warm and bouncy.

He picked up his bottle-cap bells. “Ting-a-ling!” The sound was crisp like tiny stars. He hit his tin can drums. “Boom-bum-bum!” The drums sounded like happy thunder. He plucked his yarn guitar. “Plink-plink!” It sounded sweet like a little bird.

Rikki smiled. “I love making music from old things. Recycling makes new music!” He felt proud. He had cleaned and reused the cans, caps, and boxes. He treated them with care and respect.

Today, Rikki planned a small music show. He set out a soft leaf mat and tiny wooden seats. He hung a sign: “Peaceful Music Show.” He whispered to his tree, “Thank you for holding my home.” The bark felt cool under his paw. Rikki respected his tree, too.

Soon, friends arrived. Bella Bluebird fluttered in. “Cheep-cheep! I brought a straw that I don’t need. Can you use it?”

“Thank you, Bella,” Rikki said. “I can make a whistle.” He smiled.

Squeek the squirrel bounced along the branches. “I found a shiny lid! It’s clean. Can it be a cymbal?”

“Yes!” said Rikki. “We can recycle it. We will make gentle music together.”

They all giggled and hummed. The treehouse filled with warm sunlight and the smell of maple leaves and apple slices waiting in a small bowl.

Then, Tully Turtle climbed the ladder, slow and steady. He carried a tiny bag. He looked shy. “Hello,” he said softly. “Um… I… I brought my favorite wooden spoon. I want to play, too.”

Rikki clapped his paws. “Welcome, Tully! We respect each other here. We share and we listen.”

Tully smiled with relief. “Thank you.”

Rikki began the show. “Today we play peaceful music. We use soft paws. We take turns. We respect each other and our things.”

He counted, “One, two, three!”

“Plink.” “Ting.” “Tap.” The sounds layered like a gentle rainbow. Bella sang “Cheep-cheep,” and Squeek swished his tail to the beat. Tully tapped his spoon softly on a small box, “Tok-tok.”

But then, clatter! A gust of wind rolled a big plastic bottle into the treehouse door. It bumped the tin can drums. “Clonk!” The music stopped.

Squeek frowned. “Who left this bottle? It made a mess!”

Bella fluffed her feathers. “Maybe someone dropped it.”

Tully looked down. “I saw some kids at the park sharing snacks. Maybe they forgot to recycle.”

Rikki took a deep breath. He put a paw on the bottle. It felt smooth and cool. “Let’s stay calm. We can solve this peacefully. We can respect the bottle, too. It can be useful.”

Squeek crossed his paws. “But it scared me.”

Rikki nodded. “I hear you, Squeek. Your feelings are important. Thank you for telling me.” He looked at Bella and Tully. “We respect feelings, and we fix problems with gentle choices.”

Rikki peeked out the treehouse window. He saw two little raccoon kits on the ground. They looked worried. One called up, “We lost our bottle. We’re sorry!”

Rikki waved. “It’s okay. Thank you for telling the truth. Would you like to learn how to recycle it?”

The kits nodded eagerly.

Rikki turned to his friends. “Let’s make a bottle shaker. Then we will show the kits our recycling music.”

They gathered clean, colorful beads from Rikki’s craft box. Tully held the bottle steady. Bella dropped in the beads. Plink, plink, plink. Squeek found a cap and twisted it on tight. “Twist-twist.” Rikki shook it. “Sha-ka-shaa!” It sounded like soft rain on leaves.

“Wow!” said the kits from below.

Rikki smiled down. “We respect the Earth when we recycle. We respect each other when we listen and share. And we respect ourselves when we choose peaceful solutions.”

He invited the kits up carefully. “One at a time, please.” They climbed slowly, holding the rail. Rikki praised them. “Good taking turns.”

Now the music show began again. Rikki counted, “One, two, three!”

“Plink-plink,” went the yarn guitar.
“Ting-a-ling,” sang the bottle-cap bells.
“Tok-tok,” tapped Tully’s spoon.
“Sha-ka-shaa,” whispered the new bottle shaker.
“Cheep-cheep,” trilled Bella.
“Swish-swish,” danced Squeek’s tail.

The sounds blended like a cozy hug. Everyone smiled. The treehouse felt warm and safe again. The apple slices tasted sweet and juicy during the break. The breeze kissed their cheeks.

When the music ended, Rikki said, “Thank you, everyone. Today we learned to be respectful.”

He held up his paw and counted gently:
- We respect feelings. Squeek felt scared, and we listened.
- We respect people. The kits said sorry, and we forgave them.
- We respect things. We recycled the bottle into music.
- We respect peace. We solved a problem with calm voices.

The little raccoon kits said, “We will recycle and speak kindly.”

Rikki nodded. “That is music for the heart.”

They cleaned up the treehouse together, sorting scraps into bins: “Paper,” “Plastic,” and “Metal.” They put the instruments back on the shelf carefully. The treehouse looked neat. The maple leaves rustled happily, like applause.

As the sun dipped, Rikki strummed one more “Plink.” He whispered, “Thank you, tree. Thank you, friends. Thank you, Earth.” Everyone felt proud and calm.

They climbed down slowly, paws and feet steady, hearts full of music and respect.

The End.
