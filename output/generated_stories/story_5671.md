# Benny Beaver’s Bright Idea

Benny was a busy beaver with quick paws and a warm heart. He lived near a small garden with a cozy shed. The shed smelled like pine wood and fresh soil. Sunlight slid through a dusty window and made golden stripes on the floor. Inside, there were jars of buttons, boxes of nails, neatly stacked wood scraps, and rainbow paints on a high shelf.

“Tap, tap, tap!” went Benny’s little tail on the floor. “I want to make something today,” he said. “I want to make art that shows how I feel.”

He peeked into the jars. “Clink, clink,” sang the buttons. He touched the wood scraps. They felt smooth and warm. He sniffed the paint. “It smells like lemons and rain,” he giggled.

Benny had a problem. The paints were on the high shelf. “Hmm,” he hummed. “How can I reach?” He looked around. He found a tiny ladder, but one leg wobbled. “Wobble-wobble,” it said. “That’s not safe.”

Benny sat on a soft burlap sack to think. He tapped his tail again. “Think, Benny, think,” he whispered. “Let’s solve the problem.”

First, he found a strong box. He tested it with a paw. “Solid,” he said. He placed the box against the shelf and pushed the wobbly ladder aside. Then he stacked two sturdy planks on top of the box like a wide step. “Safe and steady,” he checked, pressing down. No wobble!

Benny climbed up carefully. “One step, two step.” He reached the paints and brought them down. “Problem solved,” he cheered.

Just then, a soft squeak came from the corner. It was Mimi Mouse with a tiny blue scarf. She looked worried. “Benny,” she said, “someone took my seed bag. It was by the door. It is gone.”

Benny blinked. “That’s not fair,” he said. “Things should be safe here. We need to find the truth.”

They listened. They heard a faint “rustle-rustle” under a workbench. Benny peeked and saw Mr. Crow. He had a shiny seed bag and three buttons. Mr. Crow loved shiny things.

Benny took a deep breath. He used a calm voice. “Mr. Crow, those belong to Mimi,” he said. “It’s not right to take things. Everyone deserves fairness.”

Mr. Crow ruffled his feathers. “Caw… I am sorry,” he said. “The seeds and buttons were so shiny. I forgot to ask.”

Mimi held her scarf and nodded. “Please give them back,” she said.

Mr. Crow pushed the bag toward Mimi. “Caw, here. I will make it right,” he added. “How can I help?”

Benny smiled. “Let’s fix it together,” he said. “We can make a Sharing Sign for the shed. It will remind us to ask before we take. It will remind us to be fair.”

Mimi clapped. “Yes! A sign!”

Benny got to work. He picked a smooth wooden board. He painted bright colors—sunny yellow, garden green, and sky blue. He dipped his little brush in the paint. “Swish-swish,” went the brush. He painted big friendly letters: “Ask First. Share Fair.”

Mimi added a tiny mouse drawing in the corner. “Dot-dot, dot-dot,” she hummed as she painted little daisies that smelled like honey. Mr. Crow brought three feathers from the garden and glued them at the top like a crown. “Caw! Fancy,” he said.

Benny felt happy. “This is creative expression,” he said softly. “We are telling a kind rule with colors, shapes, and care.”

They hung the sign on the shed door with two shiny screws. Benny used a gentle twist. “Turn-turn.” The sign sat straight and proud.

A breeze came in and tickled their noses. Paint smells mixed with the sweet scent of soil. The shed felt bright and friendly.

“Thank you,” Mimi said to Mr. Crow. “You gave my seeds back.”

Mr. Crow bowed. “Caw, I will ask next time,” he promised. “And I can help you plant.”

Benny nodded. “That is justice,” he said. “We found the truth. We were fair. We fixed the hurt. Now we can grow.”

Mimi smiled. “Let’s celebrate with art,” she said.

So they made more. Benny painted a long river with dancing fish. The blue paint felt cool under his brush. Mimi pressed tiny leaf prints along the edges. “Crunch-press, crunch-press,” went the leaves. Mr. Crow tapped little dots with his beak. “Tap-tap-tap.”

Soon, the garden shed walls were full of color. Their art showed kindness, asking, sharing, and joy. It also showed their feelings—bright, big, and brave.

Benny leaned back and looked around. His busy paws were a little messy with paint. “I feel proud,” he said. “We solved a problem. We were fair. And we made something beautiful.”

Mimi hugged her seed bag. “I feel safe,” she said.

Mr. Crow fluffed his feathers. “I feel helpful,” he added.

Benny pointed to the sign. “Ask First. Share Fair,” he read aloud. “Art can teach. Art can heal. Art can tell the truth.”

They all smiled. Together, they opened the garden shed door. Sunlight touched the new paintings and made them glow.

The End.
