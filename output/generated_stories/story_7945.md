# Sunny the Squirrel’s Attic Choices

In a quiet attic, where dust sparkled like tiny stars, lived a resourceful squirrel named Sunny. The attic smelled like old books and pine wood. Soft light came through a small window and made warm squares on the floor. Sunny’s tail twitched with ideas.

Today, Sunny had a big plan. “I will make something beautiful!” Sunny chirped. On the floor was a little pile of treasures: buttons, ribbon, leaves, paper, and a shiny bell. Sunny loved to create. Sunny also loved to help.

Just then, a soft hoot came from a corner. “Hoo-hoo, hello,” said Olive the Owl. Olive’s voice was gentle. “I am feeling sleepy. The light is bright. Could you hang a curtain on the window?”

Sunny peeked at the window. The light was bright, tickling Sunny’s whiskers. Sunny wanted to make art. But Olive needed rest. And Sunny’s tummy made a tiny rumble. “Grumble,” it said.

Sunny took a deep breath. “I have choices,” Sunny whispered. “What should I do first?”

Sunny remembered a simple plan:
- Pause,
- Pick,
- Proceed,
- And check feelings.

Sunny sat on a soft scarf. “Pause,” Sunny said. “How do I feel?” Sunny touched a paw to the chest. “I feel hungry. I feel excited to create. I feel kind. Olive needs shade.”

“Pick,” Sunny said. “I will take care of me, then help Olive, then make art.”

Sunny looked at Olive. “I will help you after I eat,” Sunny said kindly.

Olive nodded. “That is wise. Hoo-hoo, thank you.”

Sunny opened a tiny snack box and nibbled crunchy acorns. “Crunch, crunch.” Sunny sipped water from a lid. “Sip, sip.” The snack made Sunny’s body feel strong and calm.

“Proceed,” Sunny said with a smile. Sunny tied a soft cloth to a stick and made a gentle curtain. The light turned from bright white to quiet gold. The attic felt cozy. Olive’s eyes blinked slowly.

“Thank you,” Olive hooted. “I can rest now.”

Sunny felt warm inside. “You’re welcome,” Sunny said. “Now I will make art!”

Sunny spread the treasures like a rainbow: blue button, red ribbon, green leaf, shiny bell, and crinkly paper. The attic air smelled like cinnamon from an old box. Sunny’s paws tingled with energy.

Just then, tiny feet pattered. Pip and Dot, two little mice, peeked out. “Hi, Sunny!” squeaked Pip. “We want a sign for our door. It says ‘Welcome!’ But we don’t know how.”

Dot held a tiny crayon. “We tried to draw, but we got nervous,” she said.

Sunny’s tail waved. Another choice! Make art alone, or help the mice first? Sunny put a paw to the chest again. “Check feelings,” Sunny said softly. “I still feel excited. I also feel caring. My tummy is happy now.”

Sunny smiled. “I can share my art time. We can make the sign together. That is creative and kind!”

Pip and Dot clapped. “Yay!”

Sunny taped paper to a small box to make a little table. “We will decide together,” Sunny said. “What colors do you like?”

“I like blue,” said Pip.

“I like yellow,” said Dot.

Sunny nodded. “Blue and yellow make green when they mix. Would you like a green leaf border?” Sunny showed a leaf.

“Yes!” the mice squeaked.

Sunny drew big bubble letters: WELCOME. Pip colored the W and E in blue. Dot colored the L and C in yellow. Sunny added a border of green leaves and glued the shiny bell at the bottom. “Ting!” it sang when they wiggled it.

Pip sniffed. “It smells like leaves and glue,” he giggled.

Dot touched the ribbon. “It feels smooth,” she said.

They were almost done when Sunny paused. “I want to add a red ribbon bow,” Sunny said. “But is it too much?”

Sunny thought again. “Pause, pick, proceed.” Sunny asked, “What do you think? Ribbon or no ribbon?”

Pip tilted his head. “I think yes.”

Dot wrinkled her nose. “I think maybe no.”

Sunny smiled. “We can solve this. We can use a small ribbon so it is not too much. How about a tiny bow in the corner?”

Both mice nodded. “That’s a good choice!”

Sunny tied a tiny bow and glued it in the corner. “Perfect,” Sunny said.

Olive peeked from the shade and whispered, “It looks lovely.”

Sunny’s heart felt big. “We made this together,” Sunny said.

Pip and Dot carried the sign carefully. “Thank you, Sunny,” they squeaked. “You shared, you helped, and you made art.”

Sunny stretched and felt calm and proud. The attic glowed soft and golden. The bell on the sign gave a happy “ting!” as the mice wobbled down their little ladder.

Sunny looked around. There was one blue button left. Sunny tucked it into a tiny box labeled “For later.” “Saving some is self-care too,” Sunny said. “It helps me feel ready next time.”

Sunny sat on the soft scarf and breathed in and out. “Today I paused, picked, and proceeded. I took care of me. I helped friends. I used my ideas. I shared.”

Sunny smiled. “That is good decision-making,” Sunny said. “I can do it again tomorrow.”

The End.
