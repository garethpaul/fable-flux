# Benny Beaver’s Big Choice Day

Tap-tap-tap went Benny the busy beaver’s tail on the floor. He sat in his cozy home office. His desk was made of smooth wood. His chair was squishy and squeaked, eek! The room smelled like warm pencils and fresh apples. Sunlight made golden stripes on the wall.

Benny said, “I have many jobs today!” He looked at his picture list. There were three big choices.

- Make a healthy snack.
- Help neighbors sign up for Clean River Day.
- Finish his block tower plan.

Benny wiggled his whiskers. “Which first?” he asked. His tummy gave a little rumbly rumble. Grumble-rumble!

“Hmm,” said Benny. “My tummy is talking.” He smiled. “I can listen to my body.”

He washed his paws. Splash-splash. He cut crunchy carrots. Snip-snap. He spread peanut butter. Smear-smooth. He added sweet apple slices. Plop-plop. He poured a cup of cold water. Glug-glug.

“Yum!” said Benny. Crunch! Munch! He felt strong and calm. “Good food helps me think,” he said.

Ring-ring! His tablet blinked. It was Dot Duck from down the river. “Hi, Benny!” quacked Dot. “Can you help me right now? I want to sign up for Clean River Day, but I’m stuck.”

Benny took a breath. He looked at his list. He felt a little tug in his chest. He wanted to help Dot. He also wanted to finish his block tower plan on time. His tail went tap-tap-tap.

Benny said softly, “I can make a plan. I can choose.”

He used his calm voice. “Dot, I want to help. I am eating my snack. Can I call you back in ten minutes?”

Dot smiled. “Okay! Thank you for telling me. I’ll get my hat while I wait!”

Benny finished his snack. He drank all his water. “Ahh.” He wiped his paws. He felt ready.

He called Dot. “I am here now!” he said.

Dot quacked, “Yay!” She sent a picture of the sign-up page. “Where do I click?”

Benny said, “Look for the big green button. It says, ‘I Help the River!’”

Dot found it. “Click!” She cheered, “I did it!”

“Great job,” said Benny. “Clean water keeps fish happy. Helping can be fun.”

Dot laughed. “Thank you, Benny. See you on Clean River Day!”

After the call, Benny looked at his list. “Snack, done. Help Dot, done.” He wiggled. “Now my block tower plan!”

Benny loved to build. He used paper and crayons. Scratch-scratch. He measured with a tiny ruler. Tick-tick-tick. He placed blocks. Thunk, thunk, thunk.

Knock-knock! The door went tap-tap. It was Gus Goose. “Hi, Benny! Can you build my kite right now?”

Benny’s paws paused. He felt happy to see Gus. He also felt a bit squished in his day. His tail twitched. Tap-tap-tap.

Benny took a slow breath. In… and out. He put a paw on his chest. “I can say yes. I can say no. I can say later.”

He smiled kindly. “Gus, I like helping. I am working on my plan right now. I can help you after lunch. Will that work?”

Gus thought. “After lunch is okay! I will draw my kite first.” He flapped. “Thanks for telling me.”

“Thank you for asking,” said Benny. They waved. Swish-swish went the door.

Benny felt proud. He set a little timer. Beep! “Work time,” he said. He built his tower plan with focus. He hummed a little tune. La-la-la. The blocks clicked together: click, clack, click. He tried a tall part. It wobbled. Wibble-wobble!

“Oh!” said Benny. He felt a tiny worry bubble. “What should I do?”

He stopped. He thought. He touched the wobbly part. He listened. The blocks made a tiny creak. Benny smiled. “I choose to make a wide base.” He added strong blocks at the bottom. Thunk. He tested again. Steady! He clapped. “Good choice!”

Beep-beep! The timer chirped. It was lunchtime. Benny made a rainbow plate: red tomatoes, orange carrots, green cucumbers, and brown bread with cheese. Crunch-crunch. Chew-chew. He drank water. Glug-glug. He felt bright and full.

After lunch, Gus came back with a colorful kite picture. “Look!” he honked.

Benny nodded. “We can do it. First, we read the steps.” They read slowly. They took turns. Snip-snip with safe scissors. Tie-tie with soft string. Whoosh! The kite looked ready.

“Let’s try it outside,” said Gus.

Benny looked at his list. “I can come for ten minutes,” he said. “Then I clean my office.”

They ran out. The wind whispered, whooosh. Gus held the kite. Benny held the tail. “Ready?” asked Benny.

“Ready!” honked Gus. Up, up, up the kite went, flutter-flutter! They laughed. Their hearts felt light.

Back inside, Benny cleaned his home office. He put away crayons. Snap. He stacked blocks in a bin. Plunk. He wiped his desk. Swish-swish. It smelled fresh and lemony.

Benny sat and checked his list. All done! He felt calm, kind, and strong. He said, “Today I made choices. I listened to my body. I helped my friends. I cared for our river. I kept my plan. I used my words.”

He smiled at the sunlight stripes. “Choosing well helps me and helps others.”

The End.
