# Kora Koala and the Gentle Garden Shed

Kora was a calm koala with soft gray fur and bright, kind eyes. She loved quiet places. She loved gentle sounds. One sunny morning, Kora padded to the garden shed behind her home. The shed smelled like fresh soil and mint. A tiny window let in warm, golden light. Dust sparkled in the sunbeam like tiny stars.

“I like it here,” Kora whispered. She pressed her fuzzy cheek to the cool wooden door. “It is calm. It is cozy.” She stepped inside to tidy up. Kora liked to keep things neat. Neat spaces helped her feel well and peaceful.

She opened a box of seeds. “Hello, little seeds,” she said. “We will plant you soon.” She stacked watering cans and swept the floor. Swish, swish, swish went the little broom. The swish sound made Kora smile.

Suddenly, there was a clatter. Clang, rattle, bump! Kora jumped. “Oh!” she said. Her heart did a tiny tap-tap-tap.

A bright-eyed sparrow fluttered in through the window. “Chirp! Chirp! Is this the snack room?” the little bird asked.

Kora took a deep breath in and out. In… and out. She placed her paw on her chest. “Hello, Sparrow,” she said softly. “This is the garden shed. It is a quiet work place. We can talk and share. Please use a soft voice.”

“Chirp, I can try!” said Sparrow. She perched on the rake handle and tucked her wings.

Then came a shy rustle near the door. A small green beetle peeked in. “Hello,” Beetle said. His voice was tiny, like a leaf whisper. “I found a shiny lid. Is it safe here?”

Kora looked at the shiny metal lid on the floor. “This is trash,” she said gently. “It does not belong on the ground. It can hurt little feet. Let’s care for the garden.” Kora pointed to a blue bin. “Trash goes here.”

Beetle nodded and rolled the lid. Clink! Into the bin it went. “I helped!” he said, standing tall.

Kora smiled. “Thank you, Beetle. That was kind to the Earth.”

Just then, the door swung wide. Whomp! Wind rushed in. Leaves swirled. A young possum named Pip hurried inside. “Hi! Hi! Hi! Let’s build! Let’s bang! Let’s make a big birdhouse right now!” Pip grabbed a hammer. Tap! Tap! TAP-TAP-TAP!

Sparrow fluffed her wings. “Oh my! That is loud.”

Beetle covered his tiny ears. “Eep!”

Kora felt the loud taps in her tummy. She closed her eyes. In… and out. She touched the handle of the broom and felt its smooth wood. Then she spoke in a clear, steady voice.

“Pip,” Kora said, “I like your big idea. A birdhouse is great. Right now, the sound is too loud for this quiet shed. I feel worried when it is loud. I need soft sounds in here.”

Pip froze. He blinked. “I made you worried?” His tail curled. “I didn’t want to. I was excited.”

Kora nodded. “I hear that you are excited. That is okay. Thank you for telling me. Let’s find a way to build that is kind to ears and kind to hearts.”

Pip tilted his head. “How?”

Kora thought. She tapped her paw. Tap… tap… tap. “We can make a calm plan.”

- “First,” she said, “we use quiet voices.”
- “Second, we put soft cloth under tools to make less noise.”
- “Third, we take turns. One gentle tap at a time.”

Sparrow chirped softly. “I can count the taps. One… two… three.”

Beetle lifted a tiny square of soft cloth. “I can place this under the hammer.”

Pip took a deep breath. “I can tap slow and soft.”

Kora smiled. “This is peaceful communication. We listen. We share feelings. We ask for what we need. We make a plan together.”

They began. Pip set the wood on the cloth. Tap… pause… tap. The sound was muffled, like raindrops on a leaf. Sparrow counted. “One… two… three.” Beetle steadied a tiny nail with care. Kora held the wood and hummed a gentle tune. Mmm, mmm, mmm. The shed felt warm and safe again.

Soon the birdhouse was done. It smelled like fresh pine. It felt smooth under Kora’s paw. Sparrow peeked inside. “Oh! It is cozy!”

Kora opened the window wide. Fresh air drifted in. She looked around the shed. The broom was in its place. The seeds were stacked. The trash bin was closed, and the garden was tidy and green.

“We worked as a team,” Kora said. “We were calm and kind. We were also strong and clear.”

Pip grinned. “I learned to say, ‘I am excited, and I can go slow.’”

Beetle nodded. “I learned to help the Earth by cleaning up.”

Sparrow flapped softly. “I learned to listen and count in a gentle voice.”

Kora took one more deep breath. In… and out. “I learned that when we use peaceful words and gentle actions, everyone feels well.”

They carried the birdhouse outside and hung it on a low branch near the shed. A sweet breeze rustled the leaves. The garden smelled like lavender and rain. Sparrow hopped inside and sang a little song. “Chirp… chirp… thank you.”

Kora hugged her friends with a careful, cozy squeeze. “Thank you for listening. Thank you for trying. We did it together.”

The sun began to set, painting the sky pink and gold. The garden shed looked calm and happy. Kora felt calm and happy, too.

Today’s lesson: Use peaceful communication. Say how you feel. Say what you need. Make a kind plan together. That is wellness. That is being brave and gentle at the same time.

The End.
