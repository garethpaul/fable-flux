# Robo's Big Problem

In the cozy study corner of the library, a shiny blue robot dog named Robo wagged his metal tail. His LED eyes glowed bright green, and his speakers made happy beeping sounds. Robo loved helping children find books and learn new things.

"Beep beep! Good morning, everyone!" Robo chirped as children arrived for story time.

But today, something was wrong. Two little boys, Sam and Jake, were arguing loudly near the picture books.

"That's MY book!" shouted Sam, pulling on one end of a colorful dinosaur book.

"No, I saw it first!" yelled Jake, tugging the other end.

The book made a scary ripping sound. Other children started to cry because the shouting was so loud.

Robo's sensors detected the problem. His tail stopped wagging, and his eyes turned yellow with worry. "Oh no," he thought. "The children are upset, and the book might get hurt. I need to help!"

First, Robo tried using his loudest speaker. "ATTENTION! PLEASE STOP FIGHTING!" he announced.

But the boys kept pulling and shouting. The other children covered their ears.

Robo's circuits felt all mixed up. His first idea didn't work. What should he do now?

Then Robo remembered his special programming about helping others. He rolled over slowly and gently placed his soft, cushioned paw between the two boys.

"Sam and Jake," Robo said in his kindest, quietest voice. "I can see you both really want to read about dinosaurs. That sounds wonderful!"

The boys stopped pulling and looked at Robo's gentle, glowing eyes.

"But when we pull and shout, books get hurt, and friends get scared," Robo continued. "Look around. Do you see how the other children feel?"

Sam and Jake looked around the study corner. Little Emma was hiding behind a chair. Baby Max was crying. Their friend Lily looked very sad.

"We made them scared," whispered Sam, letting go of the book.

"I don't like when people are sad," Jake said softly, also releasing his grip.

Robo's eyes turned back to happy green. "You both have kind hearts! Now, let's find a solution that makes everyone feel better."

Robo's screen lit up with a picture of the library's dinosaur section. "Did you know we have FIVE different dinosaur books? And there's a big, cozy reading cushion where two friends can share!"

"Really?" both boys asked, their eyes growing wide.

"Beep beep! Follow me!" Robo rolled toward the dinosaur section, his tail wagging again.

Sam and Jake walked together behind Robo. They found the soft, green reading cushion shaped like a giant leaf.

"Wow!" said Sam. "This is perfect for two people!"

"We can read together!" agreed Jake.

Robo helped them choose two different dinosaur books. The boys sat side by side on the cushion, sharing stories and pointing at colorful pictures.

"Look, Sam! This T-Rex has tiny arms just like in your book!" Jake giggled.

"And your book shows baby dinosaurs! They're so cute!" Sam smiled.

Soon, all the other children gathered around to listen. The study corner filled with happy voices and laughter instead of angry shouts.

Robo's circuits hummed with joy. His eyes sparkled bright blue. "When we use kind words and think about others' feelings, everyone wins," he beeped softly.

From that day on, whenever children had disagreements in the study corner, they remembered Robo's lesson: stop, listen, and find solutions that help everyone feel happy and safe.

The End.
