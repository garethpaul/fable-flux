# Rhythm in the Dining Room

Rory the musical raccoon loved to hum little tunes. His mask was soft gray, his tail was ringed like a drum, and his paws tapped rhythms wherever he went. One sunny morning, Rory padded into the dining room. The table was big and smooth. The chairs were tall and warm from the sun. A window let in a breeze that smelled like toast and jam.

Rory set down a basket. Inside were wooden blocks, soft scarves, and a tiny bell. “Today,” Rory sang, “we will build!”

From under the table, a small mouse named Mimi peeked out. “Build what?” she squeaked.

Rory twinkled his bell. Ding! “We will build a story,” he said. “First, we build with blocks. Then we tell what we made. We will use slow breaths and kind words.”

Mimi climbed onto a chair. “I want to help,” she said.

Rory nodded. “Everyone can help. That is inclusion. We use everyone’s ideas.”

He tipped the blocks onto the table. Clack, clack, clack! The sound was like tiny tap shoes. Rory placed a red block. “Tap, tap,” he said. Mimi placed a blue block. “Tip, tip,” she echoed.

Soon, a squirrel named Sori skittered in. “Can I build too?” she asked, her tail flicking.

“Of course,” said Rory. “We make space for friends.” He moved the basket so there was room for Sori’s paws. That was integrity—doing what felt right and fair.

Rory took a deep breath. In through the nose. Out through the mouth. “Let’s be mindful,” he said softly. “We move slow. We listen. We feel.”

They all breathed together. The dining room felt calm and cozy. The table smelled like clean wood. The bell sat quiet, waiting.

Rory tapped three blocks in a line. “This is a bridge,” he said. “It goes from chair to cup.”

Mimi added a yellow block. “This is the sun,” she said. “It warms the bridge.”

Sori balanced a tall green block tower. “This is a tree,” she said. “Birds can sit here.”

Rory smiled. “We are building with care,” he said. “We are telling a story with shapes.”

Just then, a small cup rolled and bumped the tower. Clatter! The green blocks fell down. Sori’s eyes grew wide. “Oh no,” she whispered.

Rory put a gentle paw on the table. “Pause,” he said calmly. “Let’s take a breath.”

They all breathed in and out. The room felt soft again. “It’s okay,” said Rory. “Things fall. We can build again. That is part of building.”

Sori nodded. “I will try again,” she said. Mimi patted Sori’s paw. “I can help.”

Rory picked up the bell and rang it lightly. Ding-ding. “New plan,” he said. “We build a strong base. Strong bases help towers stand.”

He showed them how two blocks could lie flat, side by side. He put one block across the top. “Like this,” he said. “Wide, then tall.”

Mimi placed blocks carefully. “Slow and steady,” she sang. Sori placed her blocks on the base. Tap, tap. The tower stood taller this time.

Rory hummed. “Let’s add a door,” he said. He slid a block to make a little arch. “Now our story can walk in.”

Mimi giggled. “Our story needs a friend.” She tied a soft scarf around two blocks. “This is a blanket,” she said. “For rest.”

Sori’s tail swished. “The tree can shade the bridge,” she added, placing a flat block like a leaf.

Rory clapped his paws. “Listen,” he said. “What does our story sound like?” He tapped the table softly. Tump-tump. Mimi shook the bell. Ding. Sori brushed the scarf. Swish.

Rory spoke in a warm voice. “Once upon a dining room, friends built a safe place. The sun warmed it. The tree shaded it. The bridge helped others cross. The blanket gave rest.”

Mimi smiled. “And when the tower fell, they took a breath,” she said.

Sori nodded. “They tried again. They built stronger.”

Rory’s eyes shone. “That is our story,” he said. “We build with our hands. We build with our words. We build with our hearts.”

Just then, a little ant marched by the edge of the table. It stopped at the bridge. Rory whispered, “Look.” The ant scurried across the blocks, under the arch, and rested by the scarf blanket.

Mimi giggled. “We helped the ant!” Sori beamed. “Our building helps others.”

Rory felt proud. “That is integrity,” he said. “We make things that are kind and safe. We include everyone, big or small.”

The sun shifted, painting the table gold. The dining room was quiet and happy. Rory tapped a soft rhythm. Tap, tap, tap. “Time to clean up,” he said. “We put blocks back with care.” They placed each piece in the basket. Clack. Clack. Mimi folded the scarf. Sori set the bell on top.

Rory took one last breath with his friends. In and out. He smiled. “Today we learned to build slow and strong. We listened. We told a story. We helped a tiny friend. We kept our hearts gentle.”

Mimi squeezed his paw. “I can build like that every day.”

Sori nodded. “Me too.”

Rory strummed his tail like a guitar. “Then our story keeps growing,” he sang.

The End.
