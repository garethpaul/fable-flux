# Panda Pingo Leads the Calm Dance

Pingo was a small, fluffy panda with soft black ears and bright, caring eyes. Pingo loved music. Pingo loved dance. Pingo was patient, like warm tea that cools slowly. Every afternoon, Pingo went to the musical practice room at the community center. The room smelled like clean wood and orange polish. Shiny drums, a piano, and little bells sat on shelves. A big mirror covered one wall, and a bouncy blue mat covered the floor.

Today, Pingo had a plan. “I will teach a calm dance,” Pingo whispered, patting the blue mat. “A dance that helps us feel quiet and strong.”

Friends arrived with a clatter and a chatter. Tiko the tiger cub bounced in with tap shoes that clicked, click-click! Mimi the mouse squeaked, carrying tiny jingle bells. Lolo the llama hummed and swished a scarf. Bibi the bird flapped and chirped, “Let’s go fast! Let’s spin! Let’s zoom!”

Pingo smiled. “Hello, friends. Today we learn a calm dance.”

“Calm?” Bibi tilted her head. “But fast is fun!”

“Fast is fun,” Pingo agreed, “and calm is kind. Calm helps us learn. Calm helps us lead.”

Tiko’s shoes clicked louder. Mimi shook her bells. Lolo’s scarf whooshed like wind. The room grew noisy. The mirror shook just a tiny bit. Pingo felt the noise in their tummy, like bubbles popping. Friends were excited, but it was too much.

Pingo took a slow breath in, like smelling a flower. Then a slow breath out, like blowing a bubble. “Friends,” Pingo said softly, “let’s try my calm start. Sit with me.”

“Sit?” Tiko asked.

“Sit,” Pingo said. “We will listen.”

They sat on the blue mat. It was cool and springy. Pingo tapped the floor gently. Tip. Tap. Tip. Tap. The sound was soft, like raindrops.

“Close your eyes, if you like,” Pingo said. “Listen to the quiet.” The room felt cozy, like a hug. They heard the tiny hum of the air vent. They heard the tick of the clock. Tick… tick… tick.

Mimi whispered, “I hear the clock.”

Lolo said, “I hear my breath.”

Bibi chirped softly, “I hear my heart. Thump-thump.”

Pingo smiled. “Good listening. Now we try the calm dance.”

Pingo stood and showed the first move. “Step slow,” Pingo said, “heel, toe, pause.” The mat squeaked softly. Squeak. Pause. Squeak. Pause.

“Now float your hands like clouds.” Pingo’s paws floated up and down. “One, two, three, soft.” The scarf on Lolo’s neck drifted like a feather. Mimi’s bells gave a gentle ring, not a jangle. Tiko’s tap shoes clicked, but quietly, like tiny raindrops on leaves.

Bibi flapped just a bit. “Small flaps,” Bibi said to herself. “Little wings.”

They moved together. Step. Pause. Float. Breathe. The room felt peaceful. The mirror showed four friends moving like slow water. Pingo led with a calm voice. “In… and out. Step… and pause. We can be calm leaders.”

Suddenly, the door creaked open. Creak! Mr. Otter, the music teacher, peeked in. “Hello, dancers! The big dance show is tonight. The stage is ready. Can you show me your dance?”

Bibi gasped. “Tonight? Oh no! I feel wiggly!”

Tiko’s tail twitched. “I feel bouncy!”

Mimi’s bells shook. “Jingle-jingle!”

Lolo’s scarf spun. “Whoosh!”

The room grew loud again. The calm floated away, like a balloon.

Pingo felt the wiggly feeling too. Their belly fluttered. But Pingo remembered the flower breath. “Friends,” Pingo said in a warm, low voice, “watch me.”

Pingo stood still. Paws on belly. “Smell a flower.” Pingo breathed in. “Blow a bubble.” Pingo breathed out. The sound was soft and long.

Tiko copied. Mimi copied. Lolo copied. Bibi copied. In… out… in… out…

The room grew quiet again.

Pingo nodded. “We can feel wiggly and still choose calm. Calm helps us think. Calm helps us dance together.”

Mr. Otter clapped softly. “What good leadership, Pingo.”

They went to the stage. The lights were warm and bright. The red curtains smelled dusty and sweet. The floor was smooth. Lots of eyes were watching. The sound of whispers buzzed like bees.

Bibi’s wings trembled. “I’m scared.”

Pingo took Bibi’s wing. “Hold my paw. We breathe.” In… out…

The music began. It was a gentle piano, plink-plonk, like stars. Pingo lifted their paws. “Step… pause,” Pingo whispered. Tiko stepped slow. Mimi rang soft. Lolo floated the scarf. Bibi made little wings.

They moved together. Calm hearts. Calm feet. Calm hands. The dance looked like clouds and rivers and slow moonlight. The crowd grew quiet, too. They watched and breathed. Some smiles shone like tiny suns.

At the end, Pingo placed a paw on their heart and bowed. The friends bowed, too. The crowd clapped softly, then loudly. Clap, clap, clap!

Back in the practice room, everyone giggled and hugged. Bibi chirped, “Calm helped me be brave!”

Tiko grinned. “Calm helped me listen.”

Mimi squeaked, “Calm helped me learn the steps.”

Lolo nodded. “Calm helped us lead each other.”

Pingo smiled, warm and wide. “When we feel wiggly, we can stop, breathe, and choose calm. Calm helps us learn. Calm helps us be kind. Calm helps us lead.”

They all took one more flower breath together. The room smelled like orange polish and happy hearts.

The End.
