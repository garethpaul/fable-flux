# Gigi's Thinking Spot

Gigi the giraffe had a very special place. Deep inside the big hall closet, behind the winter coats, she had made a cozy hideaway. Soft blankets covered the floor. A small flashlight made everything glow warm and yellow. This was where Gigi went to think.

Every morning after breakfast, Gigi would duck her long neck and squeeze into her thinking spot. The closet smelled like lavender soap and old books. It felt safe and quiet.

"Time to think about my day," Gigi whispered to herself. This was her routine.

But today felt different. Through the closet door, Gigi could hear her little brother Benny crying.

"Mama! Mama! I can't find my red truck anywhere!" Benny sobbed.

Gigi's ears perked up. She wanted to help, but she also wanted her quiet thinking time. What should she do?

"Think, Gigi, think," she said to herself. "What would be the right thing to do?"

Gigi closed her eyes and listened carefully. She heard Mama's footsteps in the kitchen. She heard Benny sniffling in the living room. She heard the tick-tock of the grandfather clock.

Then Gigi remembered something important. Yesterday, she had seen Benny's red truck. But where?

"I need to think step by step," Gigi said, tapping her hoof on the soft blanket. "Where did I see Benny playing yesterday?"

First, she remembered the sandbox outside. "No, that's not right," she thought.

Then she remembered the kitchen table during lunch. "No, not there either."

Gigi took a deep breath. The closet air felt warm and stuffy now. She needed to think harder.

"Wait!" Gigi's eyes opened wide. "I remember now! Benny was playing under the big oak tree. He was making roads in the dirt with his truck!"

Gigi felt excited, but then she felt worried too. What if she was wrong? What if Benny got upset with her for interrupting his search?

"I need to be brave," Gigi told herself. "Even if I'm scared, I should try to help."

Gigi squeezed out of her closet hideaway. Her long legs felt wobbly as she walked to the living room.

"Benny?" she said softly. "I think I might know where your truck is."

Benny looked up with big, teary eyes. "Really, Gigi? Where?"

"Well, I was thinking really hard," Gigi explained. "I remembered seeing you play under the oak tree yesterday. Maybe we should look there?"

Benny's face lit up like sunshine. "Yes! Let's go look!"

Together, they walked outside. The grass felt cool and damp under their hooves. Birds chirped in the morning air.

Under the oak tree, half-buried in the soft dirt, was Benny's bright red truck.

"You found it! You found it!" Benny cheered, hugging his sister tight.

"I'm sorry I didn't help you right away," Gigi said. "I was in my thinking spot, but I should have listened when you needed help."

"That's okay," Benny smiled. "Your thinking spot helped you remember!"

Mama came outside and saw them playing together. "What good problem-solvers you both are," she said proudly.

That evening, Gigi returned to her closet hideaway. But now she had a new routine. First, she would listen for anyone who might need help. Then, she would think carefully about how to solve problems. And finally, she would be brave enough to try.

"Thinking is important," Gigi whispered in her cozy spot. "But helping others is important too."

The End.
