# Ellie’s Brave Closet Dance

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and big, careful feet. One rainy afternoon, the house was quiet. Drip, drop, drip went the rain on the window. Ellie’s favorite place was the closet hideaway in the hallway. It was warm and cozy, with a soft blanket, a tiny lamp, and a basket of shiny scarves. When Ellie opened the door, she could smell clean laundry and a little whiff of dusty shoes.

“I’m ready to practice my dance,” Ellie whispered, stepping inside. Her feet felt the fuzzy rug. Her trunk wiggled. Her heart went thump-thump, thump-thump.

Tomorrow was Family Dance Night. Ellie loved to watch, but she had never danced in front of everyone. Thinking about it made her tummy feel fluttery like little butterflies.

“I’m a little scared,” she told the closet. “But I want to be brave.”

From the top shelf came a tiny rustle. A pair of friendly sock puppets peeped out—Milo with blue stripes, and Lila with pink dots.

“Hello, Ellie!” chirped Milo. “We heard your heart going thump-thump.”

“We love dance,” sang Lila. “But we also understand being scared.”

Ellie’s ears wiggled. “I want to dance, but I’m shy. What if I make a mistake?”

Lila swished her dotty sock tail. “Everyone makes mistakes. We can help you practice.”

Milo nodded. “And we can make a plan. Plans help courage grow.”

Ellie took a deep breath. She smelled the sweet soap in the blanket. “Okay. What is the plan?”

“First,” said Milo, “we listen to the sounds.” They all got quiet. Drip, drop. Hummm went the tiny lamp. Swish went the scarves. “Music is everywhere,” Milo smiled.

“Second,” said Lila, “we make a dance with steps that feel safe.” She hopped from shelf to hanger. “Let’s make three brave steps.”

Ellie lifted her foot and tapped it gently. Tap, tap. “Step one is Tap-Tap.”

She twirled a scarf with her trunk. Whoosh. “Step two is Scarf-Swish.”

She wiggled her hips and smiled. Wiggle-wiggle. “Step three is Happy-Wiggle.”

Milo clapped his sock hands. “Tap-Tap, Scarf-Swish, Happy-Wiggle! That is a brave dance.”

Ellie practiced, slow and soft. Tap-tap. Whoosh. Wiggle-wiggle. The rug tickled her toes. Her ears swayed. Her fear felt smaller, like a tiny pebble instead of a big rock.

But then, a little shadow moved behind a shoe box. A tiny mouse peeked out, with bright eyes and a quivery nose.

“Eep! This is my quiet corner,” squeaked the mouse. “Your dancing is too loud!”

Ellie froze. Her ears drooped. “Oh! I didn’t know. I don’t want to bother you.”

Lila whispered, “This is a good time to talk and make a fair plan.”

Milo nodded. “Negotiation means we listen and we share.”

Ellie took another deep breath. “Hello, Mouse. I’m Ellie. I am practicing my brave dance. I can dance softly. Can we make a plan so we both feel okay?”

Mouse sniffed. “I like quiet. But I also like music sometimes.” He tapped the shoe box. “Maybe you dance soft for a while, and I rest. Then I can clap with my tiny paws.”

Ellie smiled. “That sounds fair. How about I dance soft for one song. Then we take a break, and you clap.”

Mouse’s whiskers twitched. “Deal!”

Ellie felt courage warm her chest. She turned the tiny lamp lower. The light was cozy and calm. She tried Tap-Tap very softly. Tap…tap. She moved her scarf like a little cloud. Whoosh… The Happy-Wiggle turned into a gentle sway. Wiggle…wiggle.

Mouse closed his eyes and relaxed. After a little while, he opened them and squeaked, “Time to clap!” He pat-pat-patted with tiny paws. “Again!”

Ellie giggled. “Thank you, Mouse. Your claps help my courage grow.”

Soon, Ellie's family walked down the hall. “Ellie?” called Mama. “Where are you?”

“I’m in my closet hideaway,” Ellie said, peeking out. “I was practicing.”

Mama knelt and smiled. “Would you like to show us?”

Ellie looked at Milo, Lila, and Mouse. Her heart went thump-thump again, but it felt like a happy drum now.

“I will try,” she said. “I can be brave.”

She stepped onto the fuzzy rug. “This is my brave dance. It has three steps.”

She began. “Tap-Tap.” Her feet went tap, tap like raindrops. “Scarf-Swish.” Her scarf floated like a soft breeze. “Happy-Wiggle.” Her hips wiggled and her ears flapped.

Her family clapped and cheered. Mouse squeaked, “Bravo!” Milo and Lila waved from the shelf.

Ellie bowed. Her cheeks felt warm and proud. “I was scared,” she said. “But I made a plan. I asked for help. I talked kindly to Mouse. And I danced!”

Mama hugged her gently. “That is courage,” Mama said. “Courage is trying, even when you feel scared. You listened, you shared, and you found a way.”

Ellie smiled big. “Tap-Tap, Scarf-Swish, Happy-Wiggle,” she sang. “I can be brave.”

The rain outside slowed to a soft pitter-patter. The closet hideaway felt safe and bright. Ellie looked at her friends. “Tomorrow at Family Dance Night, I will dance again. Maybe we can all dance together.”

“Deal!” squeaked Mouse.

“Plan!” cheered Milo.

“Twirl!” sang Lila.

Ellie laughed. Her heart beat steady and strong. She knew the steps. She knew the plan. And she knew she was brave.

The End.
