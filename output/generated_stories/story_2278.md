# Mango the Brave Monkey Helps in the Shed

Mango was a small, courageous monkey with bright eyes and soft, fuzzy fur. He wore a tiny red scarf that made him feel brave. One sunny morning, Mango tiptoed to the garden shed. The shed smelled like fresh wood and warm soil. The door creaked, “Creeeak,” as Mango pushed it open.

Inside the shed, it was cool and a little dusty. Sunlight made golden stripes on the floor. Mango heard tiny squeaks. “Hello?” Mango whispered. “Is someone there?”

“Down here!” chirped a little cricket. “I’m Crinkle. My leg is stuck under a flower pot.”

Mango blinked. He looked around and saw a small clay pot on its side. “I can help,” said Mango. “I am brave, and I like to help others.”

Mango knelt down. He placed both hands on the pot. “One, two, three, lift!” he said. The pot was heavy. Mango puffed. “Hoo, hoo!” His hands felt the cool clay. He tried again. “One, two, three, lift!” This time, the pot wiggled. Crinkle squeaked with hope.

“You can do it, Mango,” Crinkle chirped. “Thank you for helping me.”

Mango smiled. “We can do it together. Can you push with your other legs?”

Crinkle nodded. “Yes! I’ll push.”

“Ready,” Mango said. “One, two, three, go!” Mango lifted. Crinkle pushed. The pot rolled away with a soft thump. Crinkle jumped free!

“You did it!” Crinkle sang. “My leg feels better now.”

Mango clapped softly. “Teamwork helps. Are you okay?”

“Yes,” said Crinkle. “But… do you hear that?”

They both listened. From the back of the shed came a gentle “meow.” It sounded sad.

Mango followed the sound. He stepped past a broom, a watering can, and a bag of seeds that smelled like toast. In the corner sat a small gray kitten. The kitten’s fur was dusty, and its whiskers quivered.

“Hi, kitten,” Mango said softly. “I’m Mango. I can help.”

“Meow,” said the kitten. “My name is Miso. I climbed a high shelf. Now I’m scared to get down.”

Mango looked up. The shelf made a long shadow. The boards were rough. Miso’s eyes were big and shiny. Mango’s heart felt warm and brave. “We will help you,” Mango said. “Crinkle, can you be our helper?”

“Yes!” chirped Crinkle. “What do we do?”

Mango took a deep breath. “First, we talk. We use kind words. We make a plan.”

Mango called up, “Miso, can you scoot closer to the ladder?”

“There is a ladder?” Miso peered down.

Crinkle tapped a small ladder on the wall. “Here it is! But it’s dusty.”

Mango dusted the ladder with his tail. Dust danced in the sun like tiny stars. “There,” he said. “Miso, I will hold the ladder. Crinkle will cheer. You climb slowly. We will not rush.”

Miso nodded. “Okay. I will try.”

Mango pressed the ladder steady. His paws felt the wood. “You are safe,” he said in a calm voice. “One step. Then another.”

“Step, step,” sang Crinkle. “You can do it, you can do it!”

Miso put one paw down. Then the next. The ladder creaked, but it held. Miso trembled. Mango spoke gently, “Breathe in… and out. We are with you.”

Miso took another step and another. Finally, Miso reached the ground. “Meow!” Miso rubbed against Mango’s scarf. “Thank you. You helped me feel brave.”

Mango hugged Miso. “You did the brave climbing. We just helped.”

Crinkle chirped, “Helping feels good.”

Just then, a watering can tipped over. Water splashed with a cool, silver sound. Mango jumped back and giggled. “Oops! We should clean up.”

“Yes,” said Miso. “Let’s help the shed too.”

Mango, Miso, and Crinkle worked together. They put the broom upright. They stacked the flower pots carefully. They wiped the small puddle with a cloth. The shed smelled fresh and clean. Sunlight made everything look bright.

When they finished, Mango stretched. “We did it! We helped a friend. We helped our space. We used kind words and a plan.”

Crinkle chirped, “And we did not give up.”

Miso purred, “I learned to ask for help. I learned to listen and be brave.”

Mango smiled. “Helping others is kind. Talking and listening help us work together. When we try and try, we grow.”

Outside, birds sang sweet songs. The garden flowers waved in the breeze. Mango tied his red scarf snug and felt proud, not just for being brave, but for being helpful.

“Friends?” Mango asked.

“Friends,” said Miso and Crinkle.

They sat by the shed door, warm in the sun. Mango shared a tiny banana snack. Miso licked some milk from a saucer. Crinkle sipped a drop of water from a leaf.

Mango whispered, “Today was a good day. We helped.”

“Yes,” said his friends. “Helping makes the day bright.”

The End.
