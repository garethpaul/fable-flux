# Kora Koala Says, "I Forgive You"

The morning was soft and warm on the peaceful front porch. The wooden boards felt smooth under Kora the calm koala’s paws. She sat on a little woven mat and sipped cool eucalyptus tea from a tiny cup. The breeze smelled like fresh leaves. Birds sang tweet-tweet in the gum tree. A wind chime tinkled, ding-ding.

Kora loved quiet mornings. She hummed a gentle tune and tapped her fuzzy feet. On the porch lay her favorite puzzle, with big, sunny pieces. “I will finish my bright sun puzzle,” Kora said in her calm voice. “Piece by piece.”

Tap, tap. Clip, click. She fit one piece, then another. The picture grew. Yellow rays spread like warm hugs.

“Hi, Kora!” called a quick, squeaky voice. It was Benny Bandicoot, zippy and bouncy. He hopped onto the porch, tail wiggling with happy energy. “Can I play too?”

Kora smiled. “Yes, Benny. We can play. Please use gentle paws. This puzzle is special to me.”

“Gentle paws! Got it!” Benny chirped. He scooted close and reached for a piece. His nose twitched with excitement. His whiskers wiggled. The porch smelled like lemon muffins cooling on the table. Kora’s tummy made a tiny grr-glrr—she was a little hungry.

Benny grabbed a sunny piece. “This one goes here!” he said too fast. His paw bumped the puzzle. The pieces slid. Swish—scatter! A few pieces tumbled off the porch and into the soft grass.

Kora’s eyes went wide. Her chest felt tight. “Oh,” she whispered. The warm feeling in her tummy turned twisty. She wanted to say, “Benny!” in a loud voice. But she pressed her paws together and took a slow breath. In… and out. The wind chime sang with her breath.

Benny froze. His ears drooped. “I… I’m sorry,” he said in a small voice. “I got too excited. I didn’t listen.” His eyes were shiny, like little ponds that might spill.

Kora looked at Benny. She could see he felt sad. She put a paw on her chest. “I feel upset,” she said calmly. “My puzzle is important to me.”

“I made a mistake,” Benny whispered. “I didn’t use gentle paws.”

Kora took another slow breath. She remembered the warm sun, the birds, the lemon smell. She thought, Mistakes happen. We can talk. We can fix. Her shoulders softened. “Thank you for saying sorry,” she said. “I forgive you, Benny.”

“You do?” Benny blinked. He sniffed. “Even though I messed up?”

“Yes,” Kora said. “Forgiving means I choose to let the mad feeling go. We can try again.”

Benny’s ears lifted. “I want to help. I can pick up the pieces!” He hopped to the grass and gently lifted each sunny piece. “Here is one. And two. And three.” He moved slowly, careful as a turtle.

Kora smiled. “Thank you, Benny. Let’s use gentle paws together.” She showed him how to hold a piece with two fingers. “Soft and slow,” she said. “We look, we think, then we place.”

They worked side by side. The porch felt peaceful again. The puzzle made soft click sounds. Kora’s calm voice guided them. “Where does this one go?”

Benny tilted his head. He looked carefully. “Hmm,” he hummed. “I think… it goes here. The edges match.” He placed it softly. Click. His eyes shone. “I did it gently!”

“You did,” Kora said. “Good listening.”

After a while, the sun puzzle was almost complete. Only one piece was left. It was the center of the sun, bright and round.

Kora held the last piece. Her paw paused. She looked at Benny’s hopeful face. “Would you like to finish it?” she asked.

“Me?” Benny’s whiskers quivered. “Are you sure?”

“Yes,” Kora said kindly. “I am open to sharing. We can both enjoy the finish.”

Benny took a careful breath, just like Kora. In… and out. He set the piece in place. Click! The sun picture glowed with happy yellow. The birds sang a louder tweet-tweet, as if they were cheering.

“We did it!” Benny laughed. He clapped, soft-soft.

Kora nodded. “We did it together. We talked. We listened. We forgave.”

Just then, Kora’s caregiver, Nanna Koala, opened the door. She carried a plate of lemon muffins. The warm, sweet smell drifted around them like a hug. “I heard good teamwork,” Nanna said, smiling. “Would you like a snack?”

“Yes, please,” Kora and Benny said.

They ate on the porch steps. The muffins were soft and crumbly. Crumbs tickled their paws. Benny licked a bit of lemon sugar from his nose and giggled. “Kora,” he said, “thank you for forgiving me. I will remember gentle paws and slow breaths.”

Kora patted his shoulder. “Thank you for saying sorry and helping. When we listen and forgive, our hearts feel light.”

Benny looked at the finished sun. “Our porch feels extra bright now,” he said.

Kora smiled at the glowing puzzle and the glowing day. “Yes,” she said. “Forgiveness is like sunshine for friends.”

The End.
