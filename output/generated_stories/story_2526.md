# Ruby's Fair Cookie Day

Ruby the fox kit loved helping Mama in the warm kitchen. The sweet smell of cookies filled the air. Ruby's fluffy orange tail wagged as she watched Mama pull golden cookies from the oven.

"These look perfect!" Mama said, placing the tray on the wooden counter. "Let's count them together, Ruby."

Ruby stood on her tippy-toes. "One, two, three... twelve cookies!" she said proudly. Her nose twitched at the yummy cinnamon smell.

Just then, Ruby's big brother Max bounded into the kitchen. His paws were muddy from playing outside. "Cookies!" he shouted, grabbing three big ones before Ruby could blink.

"Hey!" Ruby said, her ears drooping. "That's not fair! We didn't wash our paws yet, and you took too many!"

Max shrugged and took a big bite. Crumbs scattered on the clean counter. "I was hungry," he mumbled with his mouth full.

Ruby felt upset. The cookies were for everyone to share equally. And Mama always said to wash paws before eating. Ruby's whiskers twitched as she thought hard.

"Mama," Ruby said carefully, "shouldn't we all follow the same rules?"

Mama nodded wisely. "You're absolutely right, Ruby. What do you think we should do?"

Ruby took a deep breath. She had an idea! "Max, what if we make this fair for everyone? We could wash our paws together, then divide the cookies equally."

Max looked at his dirty paws, then at Ruby's sad face. "But I already ate three," he said quietly.

"That's okay," Ruby said kindly. "You can help me make more cookies tomorrow. And we can each have three clean ones now if we wash up first."

Ruby pulled over the little step stool. "Look! I'll show you how to make washing fun!" She turned on the warm water and squeezed soap that smelled like lemons. "We can make bubbles and sing the washing song!"

Max watched as Ruby scrubbed her small paws, making fluffy white bubbles. "♪ Scrub, scrub, scrub your paws, make them squeaky clean! ♪" Ruby sang cheerfully.

"That does look fun," Max admitted. He stepped up beside Ruby. Soon both fox kits were giggling as they washed their paws together, making bubble mountains in the sink.

"All clean!" Ruby announced, shaking water droplets from her fur.

Mama smiled warmly. "Now let's divide the remaining cookies fairly. Nine cookies left, and three of us. How many does each person get?"

"Three each!" Ruby said happily. She carefully placed three cookies on each plate, making sure they were the same size.

Max looked at his clean paws and his fair share of cookies. "Thanks for helping me understand, Ruby. You were right about being fair."

Ruby's tail wagged proudly. "And thanks for listening, Max! These cookies taste even better when we share them the right way."

As they munched their warm, sweet cookies together, Ruby felt happy and proud. She had used her words to solve the problem instead of getting angry. Everyone got a fair share, everyone was clean, and everyone was smiling.

"Good job using your voice for fairness, Ruby," Mama said, giving her a gentle hug. "That's what makes our family special."

Ruby snuggled close, her tummy full of cookies and her heart full of joy. Being fair felt even sweeter than the yummiest cookie!

The End.
