# Tully Builds a Balcony Home

Morning light warmed the little balcony garden. Leaves rustled softly. Bees hummed by the bright flowers. In a shady corner, a shy turtle named Tully peeked out from his cozy shell.

Tully loved the balcony garden. He loved the mint’s cool smell and the sunflowers’ bright faces. He loved the sound of the wind chimes going ting-ting. But Tully felt nervous when others looked at him. He pulled his head in when he heard new voices.

“Good morning, Tully!” chirped Pip the sparrow, landing on the railing. Pip’s feathers were soft gray and brown. He hopped and tilted his head. “What are you doing today?”

Tully blinked slowly. “I… I want to build a little home,” he whispered. “A tiny place, right here, by the pots. A home that feels safe.”

Pip fluttered with excitement. “Building is fun!”

From behind the tomato vines, Bella the ladybug crawled out. Her red shell shone in the sun. “I like building too,” she said with a friendly smile. “What do you need, Tully?”

Tully looked at the small balcony world. There were smooth pebbles by the mint. There were short twigs under the basil. There were soft leaves on the soil. He took a deep breath. “I think… I need pebbles, twigs, and leaves. But I am slow,” he said softly. “And I am shy.”

Pip nodded. “It is okay to be shy,” he chirped. “You are you. We can help, if you like.”

Tully’s cheeks felt warm. “Yes, please,” he said.

They began. Pip picked up a tiny twig in his beak. “Twit!” he sang, dropping it near Tully. Bella rolled a small pebble with her little legs. “Push, push,” she said, smiling. Tully pushed a leaf with his nose. It smelled sweet and green.

Tully placed a pebble. Tap. He placed a twig on top. Click. He laid a leaf for a soft roof. Whisper. The garden sounds made Tully feel calm.

But building was not easy. A breeze came whoosh and lifted the leaf up, up, up! It flew over the lavender pot.

“Oh no,” Tully said, pulling into his shell a little. “Maybe my home won’t stay.”

Pip flapped to the leaf and brought it back. “Try again,” he said kindly. “We can make it stronger.”

Bella nodded. “We can be patient. We can keep going, little by little.”

Tully peeked out. He took another breath. “I will try again,” he said. “I will keep trying.”

They made a plan. Tully would choose two heavier pebbles for the corners. Pip would find a tiny bendy stem to tie the leaf. Bella would look for a bigger leaf that wouldn’t fly away so fast.

Soon, Tully found two smooth, cool pebbles. They felt heavy and safe in his hands. Pip brought the bendy stem. Bella came back with a broad green leaf that smelled like lemon. “This one is strong,” she said.

Tully set the pebbles. Tap, tap. Pip helped loop the bendy stem around the twig. Bella held the leaf in place. Tully pressed gently. The roof stayed. The little home grew, piece by piece.

Then came a bump. Donny the puppy next door poked his nose through the railing slats. “Woof! What are you doing?” His nose wiggled. His tail thumped-thump-thumped.

Tully felt very small. He wanted to hide. But he remembered Pip’s words: It is okay to be shy. He looked at Donny and spoke softly. “We are building my tiny home. I am shy. Please be gentle.”

Donny’s ears folded. He stepped back a little. “Okay,” he said in a quiet puppy voice. “I will watch softly.” He lay down and wagged his tail slowly.

Tully’s heart felt warm. Donny accepted him. Pip and Bella accepted him. Tully smiled. “Thank you,” he said.

Together, they kept building. Tully placed another twig. Pip sang a sweet tune. Bella dotted around, checking each corner. The sun felt like a warm hug on Tully’s shell. The soil smelled rich and earthy. A butterfly floated by, painting the air with orange wings.

At last, the tiny home was ready. It had a pebble floor, a twig wall, and a leafy roof. It was small and neat, just right for a shy turtle.

Pip chirped, “You did it!”

Bella clapped her tiny feet. “You kept trying. That is persistence!”

Donny wagged. “It looks cozy.”

Tully stepped inside and peeked out. He felt safe. He felt proud. He looked at his friends. “Thank you for helping,” he said. “Thank you for accepting me.”

Pip hopped near. “We like you just as you are.”

Bella nodded. “Being different is okay. Being shy is okay.”

Tully’s smile grew. “I learned something,” he said. “I can ask for help. I can keep trying. I can build, even when I am slow. And I can be me.”

The wind chimes sang ting-ting again. The leaves whispered shhh. Tully rested in his tiny home while his friends sat close by. The balcony garden felt peaceful and bright.

And whenever a breeze whooshed, the little home stayed strong, because it was built with patience, care, and friends who accepted one another.

The End.
