# Cinnamon Stars for Sunny

Sunny was a gentle deer fawn with soft, speckled fur and big curious eyes. One bright morning, Sunny stepped into a warm kitchen. The room smelled like cinnamon and sweet vanilla. The windows let in golden light, and the oven hummed softly. Pots and pans made tiny clinks, like little bells. Sunny’s hooves tapped on the wooden floor. Tap, tap, tap.

“I want to bake cookies,” Sunny said in a small but brave voice.

Mama Rabbit hopped in with a smile. “Cookies sound lovely,” she said. “Let’s do it together.”

Sunny’s ears perked up. “Together?”

“Yes,” Mama Rabbit said. “We can cooperate. That means we help each other. When we help, we do our best work.”

Sunny took a deep breath. “Okay! Let’s cooperate.”

The warm kitchen felt even warmer. Sunny saw a big bowl, a wooden spoon, flour, sugar, butter, and a little jar of stars. The stars were tiny cookie cutters, shiny and bright.

Mama Rabbit read a simple recipe. “Step one: wash our hooves and paws.”

Sunny turned on the tap. The water was cool. Splash, splash. “I like the water,” Sunny giggled.

“Step two,” Mama Rabbit said. “Measure the flour.”

Sunny wanted to try. The flour felt soft, like a cloud. “Oops!” A little puff of white dust flew up. It tickled Sunny’s nose. “Ah-choo!”

Mama Rabbit laughed kindly. “That’s okay. We can try again. We’ll do it together.”

Sunny held the cup. Mama Rabbit held the bag. They counted, “One cup, two cups.” They poured slowly. No spills this time.

“Step three: butter and sugar,” Mama Rabbit said.

Sunny pressed the butter with the spoon. Squish. “It’s slippery!”

“We can use strategy,” Mama Rabbit said. “A strategy is a smart plan. Let’s warm the butter a tiny bit.”

They warmed the butter near the oven, just a little. Now it mixed better. Stir, stir, stir. The sugar made soft crunch sounds.

Sunny smiled. “Our plan worked!”

Mama Rabbit nodded. “Yes. Strategies help us when a job is hard.”

They cracked an egg. Plop! “Egg!” Sunny said, eyes wide. They added a little vanilla. Sniff, sniff. “It smells like sweet clouds.”

“Now, creative expression time,” Mama Rabbit said. “We can choose shapes and decorations together.”

Sunny looked at the shiny star cutters. “I like stars! They look brave.”

“Stars it is,” Mama Rabbit said. She rolled the dough. Sunny pressed the star cutter down. “Press, wiggle, lift.” Stars formed in a neat row. Sunny’s heart felt big. “I did it!”

“You did it with care,” Mama Rabbit said. “And we did it together.”

They put the stars on a tray. Mama Rabbit opened the oven. Warm air whooshed out. “It feels like a hug,” Sunny said, closing eyes for a moment.

While the cookies baked, Sunny wanted to make them special. “Can we paint them?” Sunny asked.

“We can make icing,” Mama Rabbit said. “We’ll mix powdered sugar and a little milk.”

Sunny stirred slowly. Swirl, swirl. The icing was glossy and white. “It looks like snow!”

The timer pinged. The cookies were golden. Sunny waited, wiggling tail. “I want to do it now!”

“Let’s wait one minute,” Mama Rabbit said. “Waiting is part of our plan.”

They counted together. “One, two, three…” The waiting felt long, but Sunny breathed in and out. In the warm kitchen, the cookies smelled like cinnamon stars in the sky.

“Ready,” Mama Rabbit said.

Sunny dipped a tiny brush in icing. “I will make smiling faces,” Sunny said. The first star got two dots and a curve. “Hello, Star!”

Mama Rabbit added little sugar sprinkles. Tinkle, tinkle. “I will give them twinkles,” she said.

Sunny made a star with a happy wink. “This one is brave.” Sunny made one with rosy cheeks. “This one is shy.” Sunny’s voice grew steady. “I can make many feelings.”

“You are expressing yourself,” Mama Rabbit said.

Sunny paused. “What does that mean?”

“It means you show your ideas and feelings with colors, shapes, and care,” Mama Rabbit said.

Sunny looked at the tray. Every star was special. Some had tiny hats. Some had little bows. One had a cape. “We made a star team,” Sunny said proudly. Sunny’s chest felt warm. “I feel good about my work.”

“That is healthy self-esteem,” Mama Rabbit said. “You feel proud because you tried, you planned, and you cooperated.”

They packed a small plate of stars. Knock, knock. Two little friends peeked in—Milo Mouse and Bella Bird.

“Cookies?” Milo asked, whiskers twitching.

“Stars!” Bella chirped.

Sunny held the plate. “We baked them together. Would you like to help share?”

They all cooperated. Milo carried napkins. Bella sang a soft song. Sunny passed the cookies. Everyone said, “Thank you.” Crunch, crunch. The stars were sweet and warm.

Milo smiled. “These are yummy.”

Bella flapped. “And so pretty!”

Sunny’s tail swished. “We used a plan. We helped each other. We made art, too.”

Mama Rabbit nodded. “When we cooperate, work feels light. When we use strategy, hard things get easier. When we express ourselves, we shine.”

Sunny looked at the last cookie, the one with a tiny cape. “This one is for us,” Sunny said. They broke it in four, one piece for each friend. They chewed and giggled. The warm kitchen felt like a bright, safe nest.

Sunny whispered, “Together is best.”

The End.
