# Polly’s Painted Perch

High in a leafy green tree, there was a cozy wooden treehouse. The treehouse smelled like fresh wood and sweet flowers. Sunlight sprinkled in through the windows. This was Polly’s home. Polly was a creative parrot with bright blue and green feathers and a beak that loved to tap, tap, tap tunes.

Polly loved to paint. She had little jars of safe, bright paint. She had soft brushes and tiny stamps. She hummed a happy tune, “Tra-la-la,” as she painted a rainbow on her special perch. The perch was smooth and warm. “This is my art spot,” Polly chirped. “This is my safe space.”

Her friend, Squeak the squirrel, scampered up the ladder. Thump-thump-thump! His little paws made the floor wiggle. “Hi, Polly!” Squeak said. He sniffed the air. “Mmm, it smells like oranges and rainbows!”

Polly giggled. “It’s my orange paint! And my rainbow, too.”

Squeak clapped. “Can I paint on your perch? Can I sit there? Can I try your brush?”

Polly’s feathers fluffed. She liked sharing, but the perch was special. It was where she felt calm and safe. She took a deep breath. “Squeak,” she said kindly, “this is my art spot. I need it to be just mine.”

Squeak blinked. “Just yours? But I want to try it now.”

Polly’s tummy fluttered. She didn’t want to hurt Squeak’s feelings. She also wanted to keep her perch safe. “I like you, Squeak,” Polly said gently. “I like playing with you. But I don’t like sharing my perch. It is my boundary. I need space.”

Squeak’s tail drooped. “Oh,” he said softly. “I didn’t know. I thought we could share everything.”

Polly nodded. “We can share many things. We can share songs. We can share snacks. We can share the table. But this perch is just for me. I feel calm here. I keep my feelings cozy here.”

Squeak looked at the bright paints and the soft brush. He wiggled his whiskers. “I want to respect you,” he said. “Respect means I listen to your words.”

“That’s right,” Polly said. “Thank you for listening.”

Squeak took a step back. The floor creaked. “Can I have a space too?” he asked. “A spot where I can feel cozy?”

Polly’s eyes sparkled. “Yes! Let’s make you a spot.” She pointed to a sunny corner. “That corner is perfect. We can set up a little mat and a little box for your nuts.”

Squeak’s tail twitched with joy. “I will call it my Squeak Spot!”

They got to work. Polly brought a soft leaf mat. Squeak brought a tiny pillow made of moss. They found a little sign and painted it together. Polly painted a green heart. Squeak painted a brown acorn. The sign said, “Squeak Spot.”

As they worked, Windy the wind blew by, whispering, “Whooo, whooo.” The curtains fluttered like butterfly wings. Sunshine warmed their backs. They smiled and hummed.

Soon, Red the robin hopped in. “Tweet! What’s all this?” Red chirped.

Polly stood tall on her painted perch. “This is my art spot,” she said. “I keep my body and my perch safe. It is my boundary.”

Squeak nodded and pointed. “And this is my Squeak Spot! It is my safe space. I ask before I touch Polly’s perch. She asks before she touches my spot.”

Red blinked and bobbed. “Oh! Good idea. May I sit on the rug?”

Polly smiled. “Yes, the rug is for everyone.”

Red hopped to the rug and sang. “Tweet-tweet! We can ask. We can listen. We can respect.”

Polly dipped her brush in blue paint. “Squeak,” she asked, “may I stamp a tiny acorn on your sign?”

Squeak grinned. “Yes, you may. Thank you for asking.”

Red fluffed his feathers. “Polly, may I look at your painting from here?”

Polly nodded. “Yes, thank you for asking and for giving me space.”

They spent the afternoon painting and singing. Sometimes Polly needed quiet. She said, “I need quiet now.” Everyone whispered. Sometimes Squeak needed a rest. He said, “I need a break in my Squeak Spot.” Everyone waited. Red wanted to drum on the table. He asked, “Is it okay if I drum?” Polly said, “Yes, the table is okay.” Tap-tap-tap went Red’s beak.

At snack time, they crunched crunchy nuts and sweet apple slices. Squeak licked a bit of honey from his paw. “Sticky!” he giggled. Polly wiped her beak. “Silly!” she chirped. The treehouse smelled like apples and warm sun.

When the sun began to set, the treehouse turned golden. Polly looked at her rainbow perch. Her heart felt calm and proud. “I spoke up,” she said softly. “I used my words. I kept my space safe.”

Squeak smiled and patted his Squeak Spot. “I listened,” he said. “I respected your boundary. I have my own, too.”

Red flapped his wings. “We asked. We listened. We respected.”

Polly spread her bright wings. “That is how friends take care of each other,” she said. “We can be kind and still say, ‘No.’ We can love and still need space.”

They all took a deep breath. The tree whispered, “Shhhh,” and the stars began to twinkle.

Polly tucked her brush away. “Today we learned,” she said, “that my body, my feelings, and my special things are mine. I can say what I need. You can say what you need. We can listen and care.”

Squeak and Red nodded. “We can ask. We can wait. We can respect.”

Polly snuggled on her perch. Squeak curled in his spot. Red settled on the rug. The treehouse felt safe and warm. Everyone had space. Everyone felt loved.

The End.
