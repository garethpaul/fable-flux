# Benny Beaver's Creative Porch Day

Benny Beaver was always busy, busy, busy! He loved to build dams and chop wood with his big orange teeth. *Chomp, chomp, chomp!* went his teeth all day long.

One sunny morning, Benny sat on his peaceful front porch. The wooden boards felt warm under his furry bottom. Birds chirped sweetly in the tall pine trees. A gentle breeze made the wind chimes sing *ting-a-ling, ting-a-ling.*

But Benny felt sad. His paws drooped and his big flat tail dragged on the porch floor.

"I'm so tired," Benny sighed. "All I do is work, work, work. I chop wood and build dams every single day. I never have time for fun."

Just then, his friend Penny Rabbit hopped up the porch steps. Her soft gray ears bounced with each hop.

"Hello, Benny!" said Penny cheerfully. "Why do you look so sad on this beautiful day?"

"I only know how to work," Benny said with a frown. "I wish I could do something creative and fun, like you do with your flower gardens."

Penny's pink nose twitched thoughtfully. "Everyone can be creative, Benny! You just need to try new things."

"But I'm only good at chopping wood," Benny said, tapping his strong teeth together. *Tap, tap, tap.*

"Let's see..." Penny looked around the porch. She spotted Benny's pile of wood chips from yesterday's work. "What if we used these wood chips for something special?"

Benny's brown eyes grew wide. "Like what?"

"We could make art!" Penny exclaimed. "Wood chip pictures!"

Together, they gathered the sweet-smelling wood chips. Some were big and some were tiny. Some were light brown and others were dark.

"Let's make a picture of the sun," suggested Penny.

Benny carefully arranged the chips in a big circle. The light chips made bright rays spreading out like sunshine. "This is fun!" he said, his tail wiggling with excitement.

Soon, their friend Oliver Owl flew down to the porch. "Hoot, hoot! What are you making?"

"Wood chip art!" said Benny proudly. "Would you like to help us?"

"Yes, please!" hooted Oliver.

The three friends worked together, creating beautiful pictures. Oliver made a wise old tree. Penny made a field of flowers. Benny made a happy beaver family.

"Look what we created together!" said Benny, clapping his paws. The porch was covered with colorful wood chip art that sparkled in the sunshine.

"Being creative feels wonderful," Benny said. "And it's even better when we share it with friends."

"You were creative all along," said Penny gently. "You just needed to see your wood chips in a new way."

Oliver nodded wisely. "And taking time to play and create helps us feel happy and rested."

From that day on, Benny still worked hard building dams. But every afternoon, he sat on his peaceful porch and made beautiful art with his wood chips. Sometimes his friends joined him, and sometimes he created quietly by himself.

"Work is important," Benny would say, "but so is taking time to be creative and have fun. It makes my heart feel light and happy."

And whenever a friend felt sad or tired, Benny would invite them to his porch to create something beautiful together.

The End.
