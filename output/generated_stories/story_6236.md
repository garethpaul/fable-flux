# Rosie Rabbit's Clean Art Corner

Rosie Rabbit loved her cozy study corner. It had a soft blue rug, colorful books on low shelves, and a special art table just her size. Every morning, she would hop over to draw pictures and practice writing her letters.

But today was different. Rosie woke up feeling excited about painting a rainbow, but when she looked in the mirror, she made a funny face. "Yuck!" she said, covering her nose. "My teeth feel fuzzy and my breath smells funny."

Rosie's mama called from the kitchen, "Time for breakfast, little bunny!"

Rosie thought about going straight to her art table. She really, really wanted to paint that rainbow. "Maybe I'll just skip brushing my teeth today," she whispered to herself. "I want to start painting right now!"

She hopped to her study corner and pulled out her bright paints - red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple. But as she leaned over her paper, she caught a whiff of her own breath. "Pee-yew!" she said, wrinkling her pink nose.

Just then, her best friend Benny Bear knocked on the door. "Hi Rosie! Can I paint with you?"

"Of course!" Rosie said, but when Benny came close, he stepped back a little.

"Rosie," Benny said gently, "your breath smells like old carrots. Did you forget to brush your teeth?"

Rosie's ears drooped. She felt embarrassed, but she knew Benny was being honest and kind. "Yes," she admitted. "I was so excited to paint that I skipped brushing my teeth. I thought it wouldn't matter."

Benny smiled. "My mama says clean teeth help us feel fresh and healthy. Plus, when we feel clean, we can focus better on our art!"

Rosie thought about this. She used her logical thinking skills, just like when she solved puzzles. "You know what? You're right! If my mouth feels yucky, I can't enjoy painting as much."

Even though she really wanted to start her rainbow right away, Rosie made a brave choice. She hopped to the bathroom, squeezed minty toothpaste onto her soft toothbrush, and brushed her teeth carefully. She brushed the front, the back, and even her tongue!

"Much better!" she said, looking at her clean, white teeth in the mirror. Her mouth felt fresh and tingly.

When she returned to her study corner, Benny was waiting with a big smile. "Now you smell like fresh mint!" he said.

Rosie felt proud of herself for being honest about her mistake and for making the right choice, even when it was hard. She picked up her paintbrush with clean paws and dipped it in red paint.

"Let's make the most beautiful rainbow ever!" she said happily.

As they painted together, Rosie realized that taking care of her hygiene made everything better. She could focus on her art, enjoy time with her friend, and feel confident and fresh.

"From now on," Rosie declared, adding a bright yellow stripe to her rainbow, "I'll always brush my teeth before I start my day. Clean teeth make everything more fun!"

Benny nodded, painting a fluffy white cloud. "And being honest about our mistakes helps us learn and grow!"

Their rainbow turned out absolutely perfect, and Rosie learned that taking care of herself was just as important as taking care of her art.

The End.
