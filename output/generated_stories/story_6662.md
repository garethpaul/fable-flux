# Leo's Big Art Room Discovery

Leo was a brave little lion cub with a golden mane that sparkled in the sunlight. His bright green eyes always looked for new adventures. Today, he bounded into the creative art room at his school.

"Wow!" Leo gasped. The room smelled like fresh paint and new crayons. Colorful paintings hung on every wall. Tables were covered with brushes, markers, and glittery supplies.

In the center sat the most amazing thing Leo had ever seen - a giant easel with rainbow paints!

"I want to paint!" Leo roared excitedly. He rushed toward the easel.

But wait. Three other animal friends were already there. Bella the bunny held a paintbrush. Max the monkey had purple paint on his paws. Zoe the zebra was mixing colors in a cup.

"Can I paint now?" Leo asked, his tail swishing with excitement.

"We're using it right now," said Bella gently. "But you can have a turn soon!"

Leo's ears drooped. His whiskers twitched with disappointment. He wanted to paint RIGHT NOW. The colors looked so bright and fun!

"But I'm brave and strong!" Leo said. "I should go first!"

Max shook his head. "Being brave doesn't mean going first. It means being patient too."

Leo sat down with a big sigh. He watched his friends paint. Bella made tiny purple flowers. Max painted funny orange circles. Zoe created beautiful black and white stripes.

"Their paintings look amazing," Leo thought. "Maybe watching can teach me something new."

As he watched, Leo got a wonderful idea. He noticed how Bella dipped her brush gently. He saw how Max mixed colors to make new ones. He watched Zoe paint careful, straight lines.

"I have an idea!" Leo announced. "When it's my turn, I'll paint something no one has ever painted before!"

"What will you paint?" asked Zoe, her stripes shimmering.

"A rainbow lion!" Leo said proudly. "With purple mane, orange paws, and green whiskers!"

His friends giggled with delight. "That sounds wonderful!" said Bella.

Soon, it was Leo's turn. He picked up the brush carefully, just like Bella. He mixed colors thoughtfully, just like Max. He painted with steady paws, just like Zoe.

Leo created the most magnificent rainbow lion anyone had ever seen. His painting had swirls of blue, dots of yellow, and stripes of red.

"Leo, your painting is incredible!" said his teacher, Ms. Owl. "You waited so patiently and learned by watching your friends."

Leo's chest puffed up with pride. "Taking turns helped me make something even better! I learned new painting tricks while I waited."

"That's what makes you truly brave," said Max. "You made a good choice to wait and learn."

All the friends hung their paintings together on the wall. Leo's rainbow lion sat right in the middle, surrounded by purple flowers, orange circles, and beautiful stripes.

"Tomorrow, let's all paint together!" suggested Leo. "We can take turns and share ideas!"

Everyone cheered. Leo learned that taking turns wasn't just about waiting - it was about learning, sharing, and making everything more fun for everyone.

The End.
