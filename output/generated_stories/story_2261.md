# Leo Helps in the Shed

One sunny morning, a brave little lion cub named Leo padded across the soft grass. His paws felt tickly from the dew. He sniffed the warm air and heard birds chirp, “Tweet, tweet!” Leo’s whiskers wiggled. “I want to help today,” he said. “I am small, but I am brave and kind.”

At the edge of the yard stood a garden shed. The wooden door creaked. “Creeeak!” Inside, it was cool and cozy. The shed smelled like fresh dirt and flowers. Tools hung on hooks, and little pots sat in neat rows. Sunlight made gentle stripes on the floor.

Leo peeked in. “Hello? Is anyone here?” he called.

From a corner came a soft rustle. “Squeak!” Little Miko the mouse popped out from behind a bag of seeds. His tiny nose twitched. “Oh! Hello, Leo,” Miko said. “I have a problem. I dropped a seed packet. It slipped under the shelf.”

Leo’s ears perked up. “I can help,” he said. “Let’s talk about it. Where did it go?”

Miko pointed with his tiny paw. “Under there. I can’t reach it.”

Leo lay on his tummy and peered under the shelf. It was dark and dusty. He smelled the earthy seeds. He saw the shiny packet, just out of reach. Leo tried to slide his paw under. “Hmm,” he said. “My paw is too big.”

Miko looked worried. “What can we do?”

Leo took a deep breath. “We can try a tool! Let’s find a long stick.” He looked around slowly. He saw a wooden rake. He saw a broom with soft straw. He saw a little trowel that looked like a tiny shovel. Leo remembered to speak kindly and clearly. “Miko, can you hold the broom steady? I will nudge the packet.”

“Yes!” Miko said. He held the broom handle with both paws, his whiskers trembling with effort.

Leo used the trowel to tap, tap, tap. “Tap, tap.” The packet slid a bit. “Almost there,” Leo said. He tried again, gently. “Tap.” The packet slid out into the light.

“You did it!” Miko squeaked. He hugged the packet. “Thank you, Leo!”

Leo smiled. His tail swished happily. “We worked together. We talked and we listened. That helps.”

Just then, the shed heard another sound. “Clink, clink, clatter!” A small watering can wobbled on a high shelf. It looked scared, like it might fall.

“Oh no,” said Miko. “What if it spills? The floor will be slippery.”

Leo stood tall. “We can fix this,” he said. “I will not rush. I will use careful paws.” He looked up at the shelf and said, “Watering can, I see you. We will help you get safe.”

Miko nodded. “How can I help?”

Leo thought. He used calm words. “Miko, please hold the step stool so it does not wiggle. I will climb up and hold the can.”

Miko pressed the stool with all his might. “I am ready,” he said.

Leo climbed carefully. “Step. Step.” He felt the wood under his paws. He reached the shelf and gently touched the watering can. It was cool and smooth. “I’ve got you,” Leo whispered. He lifted the can down, slow and steady.

“Clink.” The can sat on the floor. Safe and sound.

Miko cheered. “Hooray for Leo! You are so helpful.”

Leo’s heart felt warm. Helping felt good. He looked around the shed again. He noticed a packet of flower bulbs with a tiny tear. A few bulbs had rolled out. He could smell their sweet, earthy scent. He could hear a bee outside, humming softly.

“Let’s clean up,” Leo said. “We can be helpful again. I will hold the packet open. You can pop the bulbs back in.”

Miko giggled. “Pop, pop!” He put the bulbs inside. Leo folded the top and said, “All done.” They placed it on a low shelf, safe and tidy.

Then they heard a gentle knock. “Knock, knock.” It was Pippa the puppy at the door. She wagged her tail. “Hi friends! I wanted to water the flowers, but I don’t know which can to use.”

Leo smiled. “Let’s talk and plan,” he said. “We will choose the small can. It is not too heavy.”

Pippa listened. “Thank you!” she said. She picked up the small can. Leo showed her the faucet outside. He spoke slowly. “Turn it a little. Not too much.” Water splashed like tiny rain. “Splish, splash.” Pippa laughed. “This is fun!”

Together, they watered the flowers. The petals looked bright and happy. The air smelled fresh. Leo felt proud. He had used kind words, careful paws, and a brave heart.

Back in the shed, Leo said, “Today we solved problems by being helpful. We asked. We listened. We took turns. We stayed calm.” Miko and Pippa nodded. Their faces glowed with big smiles.

Miko squeaked, “When we help others, we all feel good.”

Pippa wagged, “And talking kindly makes helping easy.”

Leo purred softly. “Yes. Helping and good talking make strong friends.”

They high-fived—paw to paw to paw. The shed was neat. The tools were safe. The flowers were watered. The day felt sunny inside and out.

Leo looked at his friends. “I may be small,” he said, “but I can help in big ways.”

And the shed seemed to whisper back, “Creeeak,” like a happy thank-you.

The End.
