# Gigi's Garden of Sharing

Gigi the giraffe loved spending time in her special balcony garden high up in the clouds. Every morning, she would stretch her long, spotted neck and water her colorful plants with her bright yellow watering can.

"Good morning, little tomatoes!" Gigi said cheerfully, sprinkling cool water on the red fruits. "Good morning, sweet carrots!" she called to the orange vegetables peeking out of the soil.

The warm sun tickled her face, and the gentle breeze made her feel happy. But today, something was different. Gigi heard tiny voices coming from below.

"I'm so hungry," squeaked a small voice.

"Me too," chirped another. "We haven't found any food all day."

Gigi carefully looked down from her tall balcony and saw a family of rabbits searching through empty trash cans. Their fur looked messy, and their little bellies were making rumbling sounds.

"Oh my!" Gigi gasped softly. "Those poor little rabbits need help!"

Gigi thought very carefully. She looked at her beautiful garden full of fresh vegetables and fruits. Her tummy was full from breakfast, and she had more food than she could eat.

"That's not fair," Gigi said to herself. "I have so much yummy food, but they have none. Everyone should have enough to eat!"

Without hesitation, Gigi picked the biggest, juiciest tomatoes from her plants. She gathered sweet carrots, crunchy lettuce, and plump strawberries. The vegetables felt smooth and cool in her hooves, and they smelled fresh and delicious.

"Hello down there!" Gigi called gently to the rabbit family.

The rabbits looked up with their big, round eyes. "Oh! Hello, tall friend!" said Mama Rabbit.

"I have some extra vegetables from my garden," Gigi said kindly. "Would you like to share them with me?"

The little rabbits' eyes grew wide with excitement. "Really?" asked the smallest bunny. "You would share with us?"

"Of course!" Gigi smiled. She carefully lowered a basket full of fresh vegetables down to the rabbits using a long rope. "There's plenty for everyone!"

The rabbit family gathered around the basket, their noses twitching with joy. "Thank you so much!" said Papa Rabbit. "You are so kind!"

As the rabbits munched happily on the crisp vegetables, Gigi felt warm and fuzzy inside. She realized that sharing her food made her feel even better than eating it all by herself.

"You know what?" Gigi called down. "Tomorrow I'll plant even more vegetables so we can all share together every day!"

The little bunnies clapped their paws with delight. "Hooray for Gigi!" they cheered.

From that day on, Gigi made her balcony garden bigger and bigger. She grew enough food to share with all the animals in the neighborhood. Birds, squirrels, and even busy ants came to visit Gigi's sharing garden.

Gigi learned that when we help others and share what we have, everyone feels happier. She discovered that fairness means making sure everyone has what they need, and that even small acts of kindness can make the whole world a better place.

Every evening, as the golden sun set behind the mountains, Gigi would look at her thriving garden and smile. She knew that tomorrow would bring another wonderful day of growing, sharing, and helping her friends.

The End.
