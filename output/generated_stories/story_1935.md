# Benny Bear's Big Game Day

Benny Bear loved his sunny backyard. The grass felt soft under his fuzzy paws. The warm sunshine tickled his brown fur. Today was special - Benny wanted to play his first game of catch!

Benny bounced outside with his bright red ball. The ball was round and smooth. It smelled like new rubber. Birds chirped happy songs in the tall oak tree.

"I'm going to throw this ball really far!" Benny said with a big smile.

Benny held the ball tight. He threw it as hard as he could. The ball flew up, up, up... then BONK! It hit the fence and rolled back to his paws.

"Hmm," Benny scratched his fuzzy head. "That didn't work very well."

Benny picked up the ball again. This time he threw it gentler. The ball went a little farther, but it still didn't go where he wanted.

"Maybe I need to think about this," Benny said to himself.

Benny sat down on the warm grass. He watched a butterfly flutter by. The butterfly moved its wings slowly and smoothly. It flew exactly where it wanted to go.

"The butterfly doesn't rush," Benny noticed. "Maybe I shouldn't rush either."

Benny had an idea! He ran inside and got his big yellow crayon and some paper. He sat at his little table by the window.

"I'll draw how to throw the ball!" Benny said.

Benny drew a picture of himself. He drew his arm back like this. Then he drew his arm forward like that. He drew the ball flying in a nice, smooth line.

"First, I hold the ball like this," Benny said, looking at his drawing. "Then I step forward with one paw. Then I throw!"

Benny ran back outside with his drawing. The sun felt warm on his back. He looked at his picture, then at the ball.

Benny held the ball just like in his drawing. He stepped forward with his left paw. He threw the ball in a smooth, gentle motion.

WHOOSH! The ball sailed through the air in a perfect arc. It landed softly in the flower bed across the yard.

"I did it!" Benny cheered, dancing on his tippy-toes.

Just then, Mama Bear came outside. She had been watching from the kitchen window.

"Benny, that was wonderful!" Mama Bear said, giving him a big, warm hug. "You used your thinking brain and your drawing to solve the problem."

"I drew a picture to help me remember," Benny explained proudly. "And I watched the butterfly to learn about going slow and smooth."

"That's very smart," Mama Bear said. "You discovered that sports need practice and thinking, not just throwing hard."

Benny and Mama Bear played catch together. The ball went back and forth, back and forth. Each throw got better and better. The grass smelled sweet, and the sunshine made everything golden.

"Sports are fun when you think about them," Benny said, catching the ball with both paws.

"And writing down your ideas helps you remember them," Mama Bear added.

That evening, Benny drew more pictures in his sports notebook. He drew himself catching the ball. He drew himself kicking the ball. He drew himself running with the ball.

"Tomorrow I'll try kicking," Benny said sleepily. "But first, I'll draw how to do it!"

Benny learned that sports are more fun when you think carefully and practice slowly. He learned that drawing his ideas helped him remember the right way to play. Most importantly, he learned that the best part of sports was playing and learning together.

The End.
