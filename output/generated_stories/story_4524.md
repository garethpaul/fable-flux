# Tully Turtle Finds Her Song

Tully was a shy turtle with a smooth green shell and kind, quiet eyes. She liked soft sounds. She liked the gentle drip of rain. She liked the tiny hum of bees in the garden. Today, Tully was in the musical practice room at school. The room was full of sounds. A piano sat by the window, its keys like little teeth in a big smile. A drum rested on a soft rug. Shiny triangles hung on hooks. A row of small bells sparkled like stars.

Tully came to practice, but her tummy felt wiggly. She pulled her head into her shell a little. “What if I’m too slow? What if I’m too quiet?” she whispered.

Coach Melody, the music teacher, smiled with warm eyes. “Hello, Tully. I’m glad you’re here,” she said softly. “Today we will find your sound.”

Tully peeked out. “My sound?” Her voice was tiny, like a whispering leaf.

“Yes,” said Coach Melody. “Music comes from the heart. Let’s listen first.”

They stood very still. The room was cozy. A sunbeam touched the piano. Tully heard the clock tick…tick…tick. She heard her own breath, in and out, slow like waves. She heard a bird outside chirp, cheerfully.

Coach Melody nodded. “Good listening. Now, try a small sound. You can choose an instrument.”

Tully looked around. The drum seemed loud. The piano looked big. The triangle looked small and shiny. Tully reached for the triangle. It felt cool in her hand. She held the little metal beater. “Ting,” she tapped, very softly.

“Beautiful,” said Coach Melody. “Can you make the sound like a raindrop?”

Tully closed her eyes and thought of rain. “Ting… ting… ting.” The notes fell like tiny drops.

She felt her shoulders relax. But then, the door opened. Two classmates came in. Max the mouse and Bella the bunny hopped and squeaked, “Hi!”

Tully’s head slipped back into her shell. “Oh,” she said, cheeks warm.

Max and Bella smiled kindly. “We’re practicing too,” said Bella. “Can we listen to your raindrops?”

Tully peeked out. “I’m shy,” she said, “and I’m not sure what to play.”

Coach Melody nodded. “When we feel shy, we can use music to help. Let’s make a plan.”

They sat on the rug. Coach Melody drew a little plan on a paper with pictures:
1. Breathe in slow. Breathe out slow.
2. Find a gentle sound.
3. Add a friend.
4. Try a little louder.

Tully touched the paper. “I can try,” she said.

Step 1: Tully breathed in like smelling a sweet flower. She breathed out like blowing a soft bubble. In… out… In… out…

Step 2: Tully made her raindrop sounds again. “Ting… ting… ting.” She felt calm, like a small pond.

Step 3: Max picked up two tiny bells. “I can be the wind,” he said. He shook them very softly. “Shhh, shhh.” Bella took a little drum and used her paw to tap gently. “Pup… pup,” like distant thunder.

The room was full of small, kind sounds: ting… shhh… pup. The sounds fit together like puzzle pieces.

Tully smiled a little. Her shell felt lighter. She opened her eyes wide. “It sounds like a storm far away,” she said.

Coach Melody clapped quietly. “You made music with feelings,” she said. “Shy can be soft. Soft can be beautiful.”

But there was a new problem. The piano needed a part in the song, and no one wanted to play it. “It’s too big,” said Max. “It’s too loud,” said Bella.

Tully looked at the piano. She walked over slowly. She pressed one key. “Plink,” it went, like a drop in a deep well. Not scary at all.

“I have an idea,” Tully said, feeling brave. “We can make the piano sound like big raindrops. We can play only the low, quiet keys.”

Coach Melody’s eyes shone. “That is very resourceful, Tully. You used the piano in a new way.”

Tully pressed another low key. “Plonk.” She smiled. “I will play two big drops after every three triangle drops. Like this: ting… ting… ting… plonk, plonk.”

Max nodded and shook his bells like swishing leaves. Bella tapped the drum like a soft heartbeat. Together, they made a song: the Raindrop Band.

Tully counted softly, “One, two, three… now.” Her voice was steady. Her tummy felt calm. Her shell felt warm. She was leading!

Coach Melody whispered, “You did it. You turned shy feelings into gentle music.”

At the end, Tully finished with a tiny “ting,” like the last raindrop. The room was quiet. Then everyone smiled.

“I liked our storm,” said Bella.

“I liked your plan,” said Max.

“I liked your courage,” said Coach Melody.

Tully tucked her beater away. “I learned that music can help me share my feelings,” she said. “I can start soft. I can make a plan. I can find a way.”

Coach Melody nodded. “Music is listening, feeling, and trying. You were brave and clever. You found your sound, and you helped your friends.”

Tully stood tall in her shell. She was still a little shy, and that was okay. She had a song in her heart. She had friends. She had a plan. And she knew how to make gentle sounds grow strong.

That day, the Raindrop Band put their plan on the wall:
- Breathe slow.
- Choose a gentle sound.
- Add a friend.
- Try a little louder.
- Share your song.

Tully traced the words with her finger. “Next time,” she said with a smile, “maybe we can make sunshine sounds.”

“Yes,” said Coach Melody. “With music, we can make any weather.”

The End.
