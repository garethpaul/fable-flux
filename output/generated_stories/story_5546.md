# Cubby Cleans the Workshop

In a cozy garage workshop, a friendly bear cub named Cubby stood on a little wooden stool. The air smelled like pine wood and warm rubber. Sunlight peeked through the small window and made bright squares on the floor. Tools hung on the wall like shiny fish: hammer, wrench, and tiny screws in clear jars.

Cubby’s Mama Bear handed him a small list. “Today is Chore Day,” she said gently. “Please tidy the floor, sort the nuts and bolts, and sweep the sawdust. We do our chores with care.”

Cubby’s ears perked up. He liked helping. He liked the squeak of the broom and the clink-clink of metal parts. But today, he also wanted to play with his toy race car. It was blue and fast and made a soft whirring sound when he pushed it.

Cubby looked at the list. He looked at the race car. His paws wiggled. “I will do my chores,” he said. “I can do it!” He puffed his cheeks and smiled.

He started with the floor. There were wood scraps, tiny screws, and a sticky puddle of orange glue. “Oh no,” said Cubby. “This is a big mess.” He held the broom. The broom bristles felt rough in his paw.

He swept once. Swish. A pile of sawdust grew like a little sand hill. “That’s good,” he said. But then he saw the sticky glue spot. When he touched it, his paw went “squish!” Cubby wrinkled his nose. “Yuck. How do I clean this?”

He looked around. He sniffed. He listened. In the quiet workshop, he could hear a drop… drip… drop from a leaky faucet at the sink. Cubby thought hard. “I need a plan,” he said. “I will use critical-thinking.”

He found a rag in a drawer. It was soft and smelled like soap. He found warm water at the sink. He dabbed the glue spot. Dab, dab, rub! The glue came off with a little squeak. “Yes!” Cubby cheered.

Next, Cubby went to the jars of nuts and bolts. Some were mixed up. Big bolts were with tiny nuts. Little washers were hiding in the corner like shiny moons. Cubby’s eyes felt big. “This will take forever,” he whispered.

He paused. He looked at his toy race car. An idea rolled in like a bright-blue wave. “Innovation time!” he said. He clicked on a small magnet from the tool shelf—it made a soft clack. He taped the magnet to the front of his toy car. “Vroom-vroom Magnet Car,” he giggled.

He set the car on the table. “Go, Car, go!” The car rolled slowly, and the magnet picked up a trail of small screws. Click, click, click! Cubby clapped. He steered the car toward a jar and gently let the screws slide off. Plink, plink! “Sorted!” he said. He used the Magnet Car to gather washers and tiny nails too, keeping each kind in its own jar. He had to think. “Round ones here. Pointy ones there. Big ones in the big jar.” He checked the labels twice.

Then Cubby saw something he did not like. Under the workbench was a dark corner. Dust bunnies hid there, and a shiny bolt rolled just out of reach. He could pretend he did not see it. He could say, “All done!” and run to play. Cubby’s heart thumped. He remembered Mama’s words: “We do our chores with care.”

Cubby took a deep breath. “Integrity means doing the right thing, even when no one is watching,” he said softly. He lay down on his belly. The floor felt cool on his fur. He reached his paw under the bench. Stretch… stretch… tap! He felt the bolt. He scooped it out and smiled. “Got you!”

Now the last chore: sweeping. “Swish-swish,” sang Cubby as the broom moved. Sawdust danced in the sun squares. He listened to the soft whisper of the bristles. He smelled clean wood and a tiny whiff of lemon oil from the rag. He made neat piles and used a dustpan. “All tidy,” he said, standing tall.

Mama Bear came back and looked around. Her eyes shone. “Cubby, the workshop looks wonderful,” she said. “The floor is clean. The jars are sorted. Even the corner is tidy!”

Cubby’s cheeks felt warm with pride. “I used my Magnet Car to help,” he said. “And I cleaned the sticky glue. I also picked up the bolt under the bench, even though it was hard.”

Mama Bear hugged him. Her hug was soft and snug. “You worked hard. You used smart thinking. And you were honest and careful. That is integrity,” she said. “Chores help our home. Chores help our hearts grow strong.”

Cubby smiled and held up the Magnet Car. It hummed a tiny whirr. “Chores can be fun,” he said. “When I make a plan, and I do my best, I feel good inside.”

Mama Bear nodded. “Yes. We solve problems step by step. We try new ideas. We finish what we start.”

Cubby put the last jar on the shelf. Clink. He looked around the bright, clean workshop. “Now can we race the car outside?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Mama Bear. “You earned it.”

Cubby opened the garage door. Warm air rushed in, smelling like sunshine and grass. “Vroom!” he laughed, and the little blue car zoomed across the driveway.

He looked back at the tidy workshop and waved. “Thank you, chores,” he giggled. “You taught me to think, to be honest, and to try.”

The End.
