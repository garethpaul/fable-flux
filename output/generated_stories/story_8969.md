# Bounce, Thump, Talk!

Sunny the joyful kangaroo joey loved to bounce. Boing, boing, boing! One bright morning, Sunny hopped into Grandpa Roo’s garage workshop. The workshop smelled like fresh wood and warm oil. Sunlight made little dust sparkles dance in the air. Tools hung on the wall: shiny wrenches, happy hammers, and paintbrushes with colorful tips.

Grandpa Roo smiled. “Hello, Sunny! Want to make music today?”

Sunny’s ears perked up. “Yes, please! I want to make a band!”

Grandpa Roo pointed to a big wooden box, a tin can, and a set of little bells. “We can build a tiny drum, a shaker, and a jingle stick,” he said. “But we must talk and listen. That helps us work together.”

Sunny nodded fast. “I can do that!”

They started with the little drum. Tap-tap-tap went Grandpa’s hammer. Thump-thump-thump went Sunny’s paws on the box. The drum made a soft, deep sound. “It sounds like a heartbeat,” Sunny giggled. Thump-thump, thump-thump.

Then they made a shaker by putting dry beans in the tin can. Ticka-ticka-ticka! The sound made Sunny wiggle. They tied bells to a stick with bright, blue string. Jingle-jingle-jingle! The garage filled with happy sounds.

Sunny bounced with joy. “Let’s play a song!”

Grandpa Roo nodded. “Yes, but we need a plan. First we talk, then we play. I can keep the beat on the drum. You can shake and jingle. We count to four and start together. Ready?”

Sunny’s tail whooshed. “Ready!”

“One, two, three—” said Grandpa.

Sunny could not wait. He started early. Jingle-jingle-jingle! Ticka-ticka-ticka! Thump—oof! The beat got messy. The sounds bumped into each other. Sunny bumped into a stool. Clatter! A box of soft rags fell to the floor.

Sunny’s smile drooped. “Oh no. I wanted it to be perfect.”

Grandpa Roo picked up a rag and wiped a tiny spot of dust from Sunny’s nose. “It’s okay, little hopper. We can fix it. Let’s try again, but this time we use our words and ears. Talking helps music make sense.”

Sunny took a deep breath. He felt the cool air and smelled lemon wood polish. “Okay,” he said softly. “What should I do?”

Grandpa said, “You can ask when you are not sure. And I can tell you what I need. That is communication. Let’s practice.”

Sunny nodded. “Grandpa, when do I start?”

Grandpa smiled. “After I count to four. I will say, ‘Start!’ Then you begin. If I say, ‘Slow,’ we slow down. If you need help, say, ‘Help, please.’”

Sunny’s ears wiggled. “I can do that. Let’s try.”

They stood ready. The garage was quiet. Sunny felt excited little bubbles in his tummy. He held the shaker still. Grandpa lifted the drumsticks.

“One, two, three, four,” Grandpa said. “Start!”

Thump-thump. Ticka-ticka. Jingle-jingle. The sounds took turns. Sunny watched Grandpa’s gentle smile and listened to the steady beat. The music felt smooth, like sliding on soft grass.

“Slow,” Grandpa said.

Sunny whispered back, “Slow.” He shook the can more gently. Tick…tick…tick. The bells made a tiny, twinkly sound. Jingle…jingle.

“Louder,” Sunny said. “Please!”

Grandpa nodded and thumped a little stronger. Thump-THUMP, thump-THUMP. Sunny grinned. He felt proud. He was using his words. He was listening, too.

They played to the end of the song. Then they stopped together. The last note hung in the sunny air like a smiling bubble.

“We did it!” Sunny cheered. He bounced once, softly, so nothing would fall.

Grandpa clapped. “That was beautiful. How did it feel?”

Sunny thought. He felt warm and light. “It felt like we were talking with sounds,” he said. “When we used our words, the music got better.”

Grandpa winked. “That is the secret. Communication helps teams. It helps music. It helps problems get solved.”

Sunny looked at the jingle stick. “Can we make a new song? A faster song?”

“We can,” said Grandpa. “But we might make mistakes. If that happens, what will we do?”

Sunny puffed his chest. “We will talk. We will listen. We will keep trying.”

“Perfect,” said Grandpa.

They tried a faster song. “One, two, three, four—Start!” Thump-THUMP-THUMP! Ticka-ticka-ticka-tick! Jingle-jingle-jingle-jing! The beat slipped for a moment. Sunny frowned, then raised his paw.

“Help, please,” he said.

Grandpa slowed and nodded. “Let’s count together.”

They counted, they listened, and they played again. This time the music danced around the garage workshop, bright and shiny. The tools seemed to smile. The dust sparkles twirled. Sunny felt brave, calm, and happy.

When they finished, Sunny hugged Grandpa. “Thank you for making music with me.”

“Thank you for communicating,” Grandpa said. “You used kind words. You listened. You were patient. That is how we solve problems.”

Sunny bounced toward the door, the bells making a soft goodbye jingle. He turned back. “I learned something today,” he said. “When I talk and listen, I can make music with others. When I keep trying, I get better.”

Grandpa nodded. “That is a wonderful lesson.”

Sunny hopped into the sunshine, humming the beat. Thump-thump. Ticka-ticka. Jingle-jingle. His heart felt like a happy drum.

The End.
