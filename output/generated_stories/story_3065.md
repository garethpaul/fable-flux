# Gigi's Guest Room Adventure

Gigi the giraffe was very tall and very thoughtful. She lived in a cozy house with her family, and today they were having special visitors - her little cousin Leo and his mama.

"Gigi," called her mama from downstairs, "can you help me get the guest room ready? Leo and Aunt Lucy will be here soon!"

Gigi stretched her long neck and looked into the guest room. The bed was made with fluffy blue blankets, and there were soft pillows everywhere. But something felt wrong to Gigi. She closed her big brown eyes and thought carefully.

"Hmm," she said to herself, tapping her hoof on the wooden floor. "Leo is much smaller than me. Will he feel comfortable in this big room?"

Gigi remembered when she was little and visited her grandparents. Their guest room had felt so enormous and scary at night. The ceiling seemed so far away, and the shadows looked like monsters.

"I need to think about how Leo might feel," Gigi whispered. She took three deep breaths - in through her nose, out through her mouth - just like mama taught her when she needed to calm down and think clearly.

Looking around the room again, Gigi noticed how high everything was. The light switch was too tall for little Leo. The bed was so big he might feel lost in it. Even the window was too high for him to see outside.

"I know what to do!" Gigi said excitedly.

First, she carefully moved a small, soft chair next to the light switch so Leo could reach it. The chair made a gentle scraping sound on the floor as she pushed it with her nose.

Next, she gathered some of her favorite stuffed animals - a cuddly bear, a soft bunny, and a squishy elephant. She placed them on the bed so Leo wouldn't feel alone.

"Now for something special," Gigi murmured. She found her collection of glow-in-the-dark stars and carefully stuck them on the wall next to the bed, right at Leo's eye level. They felt smooth and cool under her hooves.

When she heard a car door slam outside, Gigi's heart beat faster. "They're here!" she called out.

Soon, little Leo walked into the guest room holding his mama's hand. His eyes were wide as he looked around.

"Wow!" Leo gasped, pointing at the glowing stars. "Look, Mama! Stars just for me!"

"And look, Leo," Gigi said gently, "here's a special chair so you can turn the lights on and off all by yourself."

Leo climbed onto the chair and flicked the switch. "I did it!" he giggled, his face lighting up with joy.

"Gigi," said Aunt Lucy with a warm smile, "you thought of everything to make Leo feel welcome. How did you know just what he needed?"

Gigi felt proud and happy inside. "I remembered how I felt when I was little," she explained. "I tried to think about what would make Leo feel safe and happy."

That night, Leo slept peacefully surrounded by the soft stuffed animals, with his special stars glowing beside him. Gigi felt wonderful knowing she had helped her little cousin feel at home.

"When we think about how others feel and what they need," Gigi's mama said as she tucked Gigi into her own bed, "we show them love and make them feel special."

Gigi smiled sleepily. Tomorrow, she would show Leo all her favorite games and make sure he felt even more welcome.

The End.
