# Dilly the Dolphin's Musical Mix-Up

In a cozy nursery with soft yellow walls and fluffy white clouds painted on the ceiling, lived a very special friend named Dilly the Dolphin. Dilly wasn't like other dolphins who lived in the ocean. She was a magical toy dolphin who could swim through the air and talk to all the children who visited the nursery.

Dilly loved music more than anything else. The nursery had a beautiful music corner with colorful instruments: shiny tambourines, wooden xylophones, and tiny drums that went "tap-tap-tap!" Every day, Dilly would practice playing songs for the children.

One sunny morning, Dilly was very excited. "Today is the big music show!" she chirped, spinning in the air. "All the children are coming to hear me play 'Twinkle, Twinkle, Little Star' on the xylophone!"

But when Dilly swam over to the music corner, she gasped. "Oh no! Where are all the xylophone pieces?" The colorful bars that made the pretty sounds were scattered all around the room. Some were under the rocking chair, others were behind the toy chest, and one was even hiding under a soft teddy bear.

Dilly's fins drooped sadly. "I can't play music without all the pieces! The show is ruined!" She started to cry little sparkly tears.

Just then, her friend Benny the Bear waddled over. "What's wrong, Dilly?" he asked gently.

"I was practicing so fast yesterday that I accidentally knocked all the xylophone bars everywhere," Dilly sniffled. "I was in such a hurry that I didn't clean up. Now I can't find them all!"

Benny patted Dilly's fin with his soft paw. "Don't worry! Let's think about this problem step by step. Where did you see the pieces last?"

Dilly stopped crying and thought carefully. "Well, I remember one rolling under the rocking chair when it fell. And I heard another one 'clink' behind the toy chest."

"Great thinking!" said Benny. "What else do you remember?"

Dilly closed her eyes and really concentrated. "I remember hearing different sounds! The high notes made 'ting-ting' sounds, and the low notes made 'dong-dong' sounds!"

"That's very smart, Dilly! So if we listen carefully while we search, we can find them all," Benny suggested.

Together, they began their search. "Ting-ting!" went a high note when they moved the teddy bear. "Dong-dong!" went a low note from behind the bookshelf. Dilly used her thinking skills to remember where each sound came from.

"I found the red one!" Dilly cheered, swimming under the rocking chair.

"And here's the blue one!" Benny called from behind the toy chest.

Piece by piece, they found all the xylophone bars. Dilly carefully put them back in the right order: red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple, and pink.

When they finished, Dilly swam over to Benny. "I'm sorry I didn't clean up yesterday. If I had put everything away properly, we wouldn't have had this problem."

"Thank you for apologizing, Dilly. What will you do differently next time?" Benny asked kindly.

"I'll clean up right after I practice, and I'll think before I act!" Dilly said proudly.

Just then, the children arrived for the music show. Dilly played "Twinkle, Twinkle, Little Star" beautifully, and all the children clapped and laughed.

"I learned something important today," Dilly told the children. "When we have a problem, we can use our thinking skills to solve it! And it's always good to say sorry when we make mistakes."

The nursery filled with happy music and laughter, and Dilly felt proud that she had used her brain to solve her problem.

The End.
