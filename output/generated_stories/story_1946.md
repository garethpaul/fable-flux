# Benny's Big Mistake

Benny the beaver loved building things. His thick brown fur felt warm in the morning sun. He carried his favorite wooden blocks up to the rooftop garden where all the animals played.

"Look what I'm making!" Benny called to his friends. His flat tail slapped excitedly on the roof tiles. "It's going to be the biggest tower ever!"

Ruby the rabbit hopped over. Her pink nose twitched with curiosity. "Wow, Benny! That looks amazing!"

Benny stacked block after block. Higher and higher his tower grew. He felt so proud watching it reach toward the fluffy white clouds.

"Be careful," chirped Pip the sparrow from nearby. "The wind is getting stronger up here."

But Benny was too excited to listen. He reached for one more block. His paw stretched too far. CRASH! The whole tower tumbled down.

The falling blocks scattered everywhere. One block bonked Ruby right on her head. Another knocked over Pip's nest of twigs.

"Ow!" Ruby cried, rubbing her head with her paw.

"My nest!" Pip squeaked sadly. "All my hard work is ruined!"

Benny's heart felt heavy like a stone. His friends looked upset. His big brown eyes filled with tears.

"I'm sorry," Benny whispered. His voice was so quiet, like a leaf falling. "I didn't mean to hurt anyone."

Ruby's ears drooped down. Pip's feathers looked ruffled and messy. They both turned away from Benny.

Benny's bottom lip trembled. "I'm just a clumsy beaver," he said to himself. "I always mess things up."

He sat alone on the rooftop. The warm sun didn't feel nice anymore. His fur felt cold and prickly.

Then Benny took a deep breath. The air smelled like flowers from the garden below. He remembered what his mama always said: "When you make a mistake, you can make it right."

Benny stood up tall. His heart beat fast like a drum, but he felt brave. He walked over to Ruby first.

"Ruby," Benny said clearly. "I'm really, really sorry I hurt you. I should have been more careful."

Ruby's ears perked up a little. "It did hurt," she said softly.

"I know," Benny nodded. "Can I help make you feel better? I could find you some cool water for your head."

Ruby's eyes grew warmer. "That would be nice, Benny."

Next, Benny hopped over to Pip. The little bird's nest was all scattered.

"Pip, I'm so sorry about your nest," Benny said. "I want to help you build a new one. I'm good at building things when I'm careful."

Pip tilted his tiny head. "You really want to help me?"

"Yes!" Benny's tail wiggled with hope. "I'll be super gentle this time."

Together, they worked all morning. Benny carefully placed each twig exactly where Pip wanted it. Ruby helped too, bringing soft moss for the nest.

When they finished, Pip's new nest looked even better than before.

"Thank you, Benny," Pip chirped happily. "You're a really good friend."

Ruby nuzzled Benny's furry arm. "We forgive you, Benny. Everyone makes mistakes sometimes."

Benny's heart felt light and warm again. The sun seemed brighter. The rooftop smelled like friendship and fresh starts.

"I learned something important today," Benny said with a big smile. "When I make a mistake, I can say sorry and help fix things. That makes me feel brave and good inside."

The three friends played together until sunset, building careful towers and cozy nests, sharing giggles and warm hugs.

The End.
