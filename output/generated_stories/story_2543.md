# Polly's Perfect Day

Polly the parrot stretched her bright green wings in the sunny living room. Colorful toys were scattered everywhere on the soft carpet. A cozy reading corner had pillows piled high. The morning light streamed through big windows.

"Good morning, world!" Polly squawked cheerfully. She loved starting each day the same special way. First, she would organize her art supplies. Then she would practice flying around the room. After that, she would help tidy up.

But today felt different. Polly looked around at all the mess. Crayons rolled under the couch. Building blocks were mixed with puzzle pieces. Books lay open everywhere.

"Oh my!" Polly chirped. "Everything is so messy. I don't know where to start!"

She tried to pick up a crayon with her beak, but it slipped away. She flapped her wings, feeling frustrated. The room seemed too big and the mess too much.

Polly's friend Sam the teddy bear watched from his spot on the shelf. "Maybe start with just one thing," Sam suggested gently. "Remember your morning routine?"

Polly paused. She had forgotten about her special routine! "You're right, Sam. First, I organize my art supplies."

She flew to her art corner and began sorting. Red crayons went in one cup. Blue ones in another. "This feels better already," she said, her voice getting happier.

Next came flying practice. Polly soared around the room three times. The wind under her wings felt wonderful. She swooped and glided, feeling strong and free.

"Now for helping time!" Polly announced. She picked up one book at a time, carrying each to the bookshelf. It was slow work, but she didn't give up.

When a puzzle piece seemed too heavy, Polly pushed it with her foot instead of her beak. When blocks were too many to carry, she moved them one by one.

"I can do this myself," Polly said proudly. "I just need to take my time."

As she worked, something magical happened. The room began to look neat and tidy. The reading corner became cozy again. Her art supplies sparkled in their organized cups.

Sam clapped his fuzzy paws. "You did it, Polly! You followed your routine and solved the problem all by yourself."

Polly felt warm and happy inside. "I was worried at first," she admitted. "But my routine helped me know what to do. Even when things seem hard, I can handle them step by step."

She perched on her favorite swing and looked around the beautiful, clean living room. Everything was in its place. The afternoon sun made the colors look extra bright.

"Tomorrow I'll remember to follow my routine from the very beginning," Polly promised herself. "And if I make mistakes, that's okay too. I can always try again."

Sam smiled. "That's what makes you so special, Polly. You never give up, and you always help make things better."

Polly preened her feathers proudly. She had learned that routines weren't just rules to follow. They were helpful friends that made big jobs feel smaller. They helped her feel calm and confident.

As the day ended, Polly felt grateful. She had forgiven herself for the messy morning. She had relied on her own skills to fix things. And she had helped make their living room beautiful again.

"Every day is a chance to do my best," Polly chirped softly as she settled in for the evening.

The End.
