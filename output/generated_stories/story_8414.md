# Miko’s Treehouse Treasure Map

High in a gentle oak, a cozy treehouse smelled like warm wood and pine. A kind mouse named Miko lived there. He wore a tiny blue scarf and kept his home tidy. Acorns sat in neat jars. Ropes and ribbons hung in a row. Miko loved to help friends and loved to learn new things.

One bright morning, a paper slid under his little door. The paper crackled when he picked it up. “A treasure map!” Miko squeaked. His whiskers quivered with joy.

On the map, there were little pictures and arrows. At the top, big letters said: “Follow the steps. Do them in order. Be fair. Share.”

Miko’s tail flicked. “I can do that!” he said. He grabbed his backpack and a tiny lantern.

Knock-knock! His friend, Tula the turtle, tapped on the ladder. “Hi, Miko,” Tula said softly. “What’s that?”

“A treasure map!” Miko beamed. “Let’s go!”

They looked at the first step. It said: “Step 1: Count to three. Clap two times. Then open the window.”

Miko giggled. “Easy!” But his paws were excited. He wanted to open the window right away. His fingers reached for the latch.

Tula cleared her throat. “Miko, the map says to count first,” she reminded him.

Miko paused. His ears drooped a little. “You’re right,” he said. “I almost forgot to follow the steps.”

They stood together.
“One, two, three,” they counted.
Clap! Clap!
Creak! Miko opened the window. A cool breeze whooshed in. It smelled like leaves and sunshine.

“Step 2: Tie a red ribbon on the bell,” the map said. A tiny bell hung by the window with three ribbons nearby—red, blue, and green.

Miko grabbed the blue ribbon. “Blue is my favorite,” he said.

Tula blinked slowly. “The map says red.”

Miko took a breath. He set the blue ribbon down. “You’re right,” he said again, smiling. “I am learning to follow directions.” He tied the red ribbon carefully. Ting! The bell sang a bright sound.

“Step 3: Sweep the floor in little circles,” the map read.

Miko picked up his small broom. Swish, swish, swish! The floor whispered under the broom. Dust danced in the sunbeams like tiny stars. It felt good to be neat.

Then they read, “Step 4: Share a snack with a friend.”

Miko opened a jar. Crunchy acorn cookies smelled sweet. He counted, “One for me, one for Tula,” and placed them on a napkin. They munched happily.

“This step says be fair,” Tula said kindly. “We both get the same.”

Miko nodded. “Fair and friendly,” he said with a grin.

“Step 5: Tap the floor three times near the round rug.”

Tap. Tap. Tap.

The rug quivered. A little square door popped open in the floor! Inside was a small wooden box tied with twine.

Miko’s heart thumped. “We found it!” he squeaked. He reached for the lid, but the twine was in a knotted mess. His paws tugged and tugged. The knot only got tighter. His eyes felt hot, and his tail drooped.

“I can’t do it,” he sighed.

Tula took a slow breath. “Let’s read the map again,” she said gently. “Maybe we missed something.”

They checked the corner of the map. There was a tiny note they hadn’t seen before. It said: “If the knot is hard, stop. Breathe. Ask for help. Try again slowly.”

Miko closed his eyes. In and out. Calm and slow. “Help me, please,” he asked.

“Of course,” Tula said. They worked together. Tula held the twine steady. Miko lifted one loop at a time. Pull, pause, wiggle. The knot softened. The twine slipped free.

Click. The box opened.

Inside was not gold. It was better. There was a little book called “How to Build a Fair Sharing Shelf,” some colorful stickers that said “I Follow Directions!”, and a tiny wooden sign. The sign read: “Good job! You learned to follow steps. Keep learning. Keep helping.”

Miko’s eyes sparkled. “This is wonderful!”

Tula smiled. “You did the steps. You asked for help. You were fair.”

Miko put up the tiny sign over his door. Then he read the little book. It showed how to make a shelf so everyone could reach. He measured the boards the way the book said. He lined up the nails just like the picture. Tap, tap, tap—careful and slow. Tula held the board. Miko checked each step.

When it was done, the shelf stood straight and strong. Miko placed snacks at the bottom, water cups in the middle, and napkins at the top. “This way, big friends and small friends can all use it,” he said.

Neighbors climbed the ladder—Suri the squirrel, Pip the pigeon, and Nib the hedgehog. “What a fair shelf!” they cheered. Miko handed out stickers: “We can all follow directions together,” he said. “We can learn and improve, one step at a time.”

The treehouse felt bright and happy. The bell chimed in the breeze. Cookies smelled sweet. The wood was warm under their feet.

Miko looked at the map and smiled. “When we follow directions, we can solve problems. We can be fair. We can learn and get better,” he said.

Everyone nodded. “Let’s keep practicing,” Tula added.

Miko’s heart felt big and light. He tucked the map into a safe place, right next to his tiny blue scarf.

The End.
