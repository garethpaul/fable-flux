# The Helpful Song in the Workshop

Chirpy was a small singing bird with a bright yellow chest and a tiny, shiny beak. Every morning, Chirpy flew to a cozy garage workshop at the end of Maple Street. The workshop smelled like fresh wood and a little bit like lemon soap. Sunlight made warm stripes on the floor. Tools hung on the wall—hammers, brushes, and friendly-looking wrenches. The door squeaked, “Eeeek!” when it opened.

“Good morning, workshop!” sang Chirpy. “Tweet-a-dee! Tweet-a-doo!”

Mr. Ben, the kind man who fixed things, waved. “Hello, Chirpy! I have lots to do today. I’m making toy cars for the kids at the park.”

Chirpy’s heart felt warm. “I can help! I love to help,” he sang. Chirpy liked to volunteer, which means helping without being asked, just to be kind.

On the big worktable, there were little wooden wheels, tiny screws, and red paint that smelled sweet and tangy. Mr. Ben smiled. “Thank you, Chirpy. Helping hands—and wings—are welcome. But first, let’s make a plan.”

Chirpy tilted his head. “A plan is a good idea,” he chirped. Strategy is a big word, but it means thinking about the best way to help.

They made three simple steps:
1) Sort the wheels.
2) Carry the cars to the shelf.
3) Sing to keep the work happy.

Chirpy clapped his wings. “Tweet! I can do that!”

He fluttered to the box of wheels. The box felt cool and smooth. Chirpy picked up a small wheel with his beak. “Light and round,” he chirped. He made two piles—little wheels and big wheels. “Little here. Big there.” He hummed a sorting song, soft and sweet. “Little, big, little, big, we help by sorting, not by dig!”

A little mouse poked out from a corner. “Squeak… hello,” said the mouse. The mouse had soft gray fur and tiny, nervous eyes. “I’m Miko. I want to help, but I’m shy.”

Chirpy smiled with his eyes. “It’s okay to be shy, Miko. You can help with me. We can be a team.”

Miko took a tiny breath. “I can roll wheels,” he said. His whiskers twitched.

“Great plan!” sang Chirpy. “We volunteer together.” He felt glad. Empathy means noticing how someone feels. Chirpy could tell Miko needed a gentle start.

Mr. Ben brushed wood dust away. The brush made a soft “whoosh.” “You two are super helpers,” he said.

Chirpy and Miko worked side by side. The wheels made a clicky sound as they touched. Click-click, click-click. The workshop felt busy and happy.

Soon, they had neat piles. “Step one, done!” chirped Chirpy.

“Now step two,” said Mr. Ben. “Please carry the finished toy cars to the top shelf.”

Chirpy fluttered up, but the shelf was high. His wings were small. “Oh,” he peeped. “This is hard.”

Miko looked up too. His tail curled. “I can’t reach,” he squeaked.

Chirpy took a calm breath. “Let’s think. Another strategy!” He looked around and saw a small wooden ramp Mr. Ben had made for rolling toys.

“Mr. Ben,” chirped Chirpy, “can we use the ramp to slide the cars up to you?”

Mr. Ben grinned. “Smart thinking! Yes.”

They made a little line. Miko gently rolled a car onto the ramp. Chirpy sang, “Slow and steady, roll and ready!” Mr. Ben caught the car at the top and placed it on the shelf. Thunk—safe!

Roll. Sing. Catch. Thunk. The sound made a happy rhythm. Chirpy’s song filled the room, bright and cheerful. The paint smelled like berries. The wood felt smooth under their paws and claws. The light felt warm on their feathers and fur.

After many cars, Chirpy saw a squirrel at the window. The squirrel’s ears drooped. “Are the cars for us?” she asked softly.

Chirpy fluttered closer. “Yes, for the park! Would you like to help too?”

The squirrel nodded, eyes shiny. “I can test the wheels,” she said.

“Welcome!” Chirpy chirped. “We all can help.” He felt his chest glow with kindness. Volunteering brings friends together.

The squirrel spun each wheel. Whirr… whirr… “This one is wobbly,” she said.

Chirpy listened. “Thank you for telling us. We can fix it.” He thought again. “Let’s make a ‘Fix-It’ box for wobbly wheels.” That was another strategy to keep things clear.

They wrote with a blue crayon: FIX-IT. The box smelled like paper and glue. Into the box went the wobbly wheels. Problem solved.

At last, the shelf was full. The toy cars were shiny red, like apples in the sun. Mr. Ben wiped his hands and smiled wide. “We did it! Thanks to our volunteers—Chirpy, Miko, and Squirrel.”

Chirpy sang a gentle thank-you song. “We helped with care, we helped with heart, together we made a happy start!”

Miko’s whiskers wiggled with pride. “I wasn’t too shy,” he squeaked.

“You were brave and kind,” said Chirpy. He felt proud of his friends. He felt proud of himself too.

Mr. Ben packed the cars in a box for the park. “These toys will make many children smile,” he said. The thought made the workshop feel even warmer.

Chirpy looked around at the clean table, the FIX-IT box, and the happy faces. “Volunteering means we give our time and our care,” he chirped. “We listen, we plan, and we help.”

The friends nodded. The sun slid across the floor. The door gave one last friendly squeak.

“Same time tomorrow?” asked Mr. Ben.

“Tweet-a-dee! Yes!” Chirpy sang. “We can help again.”

And he added, in a soft, sweet voice, “When we volunteer, we use empathy to notice feelings, and we use smart strategies to make helping easier. Helping together feels good.”

The End.
