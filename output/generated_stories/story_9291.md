# Gigi the Giraffe Learns to Speak Up

Gigi the giraffe had a very special job. She worked in a cozy home office with big windows and soft sunlight streaming in. Her long neck helped her reach the tall shelves where important papers lived. Her job was to help organize files and make sure everything was safe and tidy.

One sunny morning, Gigi stretched her long neck up high to get some papers from the top shelf. "Oh my!" she gasped. She could see something that made her worried. Through the window, she spotted her friend Benny the bunny playing near the busy street without looking both ways.

Gigi's heart beat fast like a drum. "Thump-thump, thump-thump!" She knew Benny could get hurt if a car came by. But Gigi felt shy about calling out. She was usually very quiet and thoughtful.

"What should I do?" Gigi whispered to herself, twisting her spotted neck nervously. She looked around the office. Mrs. Chen, who worked at the big desk, was talking on the phone. "I don't want to interrupt," Gigi thought.

But then she remembered what her mama always told her: "When someone might get hurt, it's always right to speak up, even if you feel scared."

Gigi took a deep breath that filled her long chest. She walked over to Mrs. Chen's desk, her hooves making soft "clip-clop" sounds on the wooden floor.

"Excuse me, Mrs. Chen," Gigi said in her gentlest voice. "I'm sorry to bother you, but I can see my friend Benny playing near the street. He might not be safe."

Mrs. Chen looked up with kind eyes. "Thank you for telling me, Gigi! Let's call out to him together."

They both went to the window. "Benny!" they called together. "Come play in the yard instead!"

Benny heard them and waved. "Oh! You're right!" he called back. "Thank you for caring about me!" He hopped safely away from the street to play in the grassy yard.

Mrs. Chen smiled at Gigi. "You did exactly the right thing. Speaking up when someone needs help is very brave and kind."

Gigi felt warm and happy inside, like sunshine was glowing in her chest. "I was scared to interrupt you," she admitted.

"You know what?" Mrs. Chen said, patting Gigi's spotted neck gently. "The best teams always communicate. When we share important information, we can work together to keep everyone safe. You're an excellent team member."

Later that day, Benny came to visit the office. He brought Gigi a beautiful daisy chain he had made. "Thank you for speaking up and keeping me safe," he said. "You're a wonderful friend."

From that day on, Gigi learned that her voice was important. When she had something helpful to say, she would speak up clearly and kindly. She discovered that good communication helped everyone work together better.

The office became an even happier place because Gigi shared her thoughts and observations. Mrs. Chen often asked for Gigi's help because she knew the thoughtful giraffe always looked out for others.

"Speaking up isn't scary when you're trying to help," Gigi would tell her friends. "Our words can keep people safe and make things better for everyone."

And whenever Gigi saw someone who needed help, she remembered to use her voice with courage and kindness.

The End.
