# Squeaky Builds a Cozy Bridge

In a warm, comfortable nursery, soft sunlight made the curtains glow. The room smelled like clean blankets and crayons. Toys sat on shelves, blocks were stacked in neat rows, and a tiny breeze whispered through the window.

Squeaky the resourceful squirrel lived in a little basket bed near the bookshelf. His tail was fluffy, his eyes were bright, and he loved to build. He also loved to move, hopping from pillow to pillow like a brave little gymnast.

One morning, Ms. Maple, the kind teacher, smiled and clapped her hands. “Good morning, friends! Today, we will build a Cozy Bridge with blocks. We’ll follow the picture directions, step by step. When we follow directions, our building is safe and strong.”

Squeaky’s ears perked up. “I can build! I am brave!” he squeaked, twitching his whiskers.

Ms. Maple showed a big picture card. It had four steps. Step 1: Lay a blue mat. Step 2: Make two block towers. Step 3: Place a long plank on top. Step 4: Test with a gentle walk.

“These are our directions,” Ms. Maple said. “We will go slow and steady.”

Squeaky’s tail swished. He loved to go fast. He loved to zoom. He bounced to the blocks, paws tingling with excitement. “I can do it all at once!” he chirped.

“Remember,” Ms. Maple said softly, “We follow the directions. Step by step.”

Squeaky nodded, but his paws danced. The blocks were smooth and cool. The mat felt soft under his feet. He could smell the wood of the blocks, warm and light.

“I want to help!” said Mia, a small child with a big smile.

“Me too!” said Jay, holding the long plank.

Ms. Maple handed Squeaky the picture card. “Squeaky, you can be our Leader Helper. Read each step aloud, and we will do it together.”

Squeaky’s chest puffed up. “I am the Leader Helper,” he said bravely. He held the card carefully. The edges tickled his paws. “Step 1,” he read. “Lay a blue mat.”

Together, Mia and Jay spread the soft blue mat. It made a gentle whoosh sound. Squeaky patted it smooth. “Blue and flat,” he said.

“Step 2,” Squeaky read. “Make two block towers.”

Squeaky picked up a red block. It felt heavy in his little paws. “One block, two block,” he counted. “We stack them straight.”

Jay tried to rush and put a big block on a tiny one. The tower wobbled. “Uh-oh,” Jay said.

Squeaky took a brave breath. “Let’s follow the directions,” he reminded. “Big blocks at the bottom. Small blocks on top.” He gently moved the blocks. The tower stood tall and steady. They built a second tower, just the same.

“Step 3,” Squeaky read, his whiskers bouncing. “Place a long plank on top.”

Mia and Jay held the plank. It was smooth and long. “Slow and steady,” Squeaky said. “We can do this together.”

They lifted the plank. It made a soft tap when it met the towers. Squeaky checked the picture. “Straight and centered,” he said. He lined it up with his tiny paws. It was perfect.

“Step 4,” Squeaky read. “Test with a gentle walk.”

“I want to try!” Mia said.

“Let’s check safety first,” Squeaky said bravely. He pressed his paws on the plank. It did not wiggle. “Gentle steps,” he reminded.

Mia stepped. Tap, tap. Jay stepped. Tip, tap.

Squeaky took a step too. Pit, pat. The Cozy Bridge was strong!

Just then, they heard a rustle. The window rattled a little. A small plush bunny had fallen between the crib and the shelf. It was stuck in a narrow gap.

“Bunny!” Mia cried. “Bunny needs help!”

Squeaky’s heart thumped. The gap was dark and tight. It smelled like dust and lavender. He felt a tiny flutter of worry. But he stood tall. “I can be brave,” he said softly. “We will help. We will follow directions.”

Ms. Maple nodded. “Squeaky, what is your plan?”

Squeaky looked at the Cozy Bridge picture. He thought, Step by step. He made a new plan with simple steps.

Step 1: Move the blue mat under the gap for soft landing.

Step 2: Use two blocks to make a little step-stool.

Step 3: Lie the plank from the stool to the gap to make a safe slide.

Step 4: Slide in gently and pull Bunny out slowly.

“Let’s do it together,” Squeaky said.

They moved the mat. Whoosh. They built the little stool. Tap, tap. They set the plank. Soft tap.

Squeaky put one paw on the plank. “Gentle steps,” he reminded. He scooted forward. The space smelled dusty, but he kept going. His fur brushed the wall. He reached the bunny’s soft ear.

“I’ve got you,” Squeaky whispered. He pulled slowly, following his own steps. Mia held the mat. Jay held the stool. Ms. Maple watched with kind eyes.

With a soft tug, Squeaky freed the bunny. They slid back. Squeak, slide, soft thump on the mat. Safe!

“You did it!” Mia cheered.

“You were brave and careful,” Ms. Maple said. “You followed directions and helped a friend.”

Squeaky hugged the bunny. His heart felt warm. The room smelled sweet again.

Squeaky looked at the picture card. “Following directions helped us build,” he said. “Following directions helped us help.”

Ms. Maple smiled. “Yes. When we listen, look, and go step by step, we can do big, brave things.”

Squeaky’s tail swished happily. “Step by step,” he repeated. “Slow and steady, safe and ready.”

They all crossed the Cozy Bridge one more time, gentle steps in a happy line.

The End.
