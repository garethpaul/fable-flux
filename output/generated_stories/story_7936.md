# Nutmeg Leads the Attic Team

In a quiet attic, where dust danced in the sun and wooden beams smelled like warm pine, a resourceful squirrel named Nutmeg made a cozy home. The attic was still and soft. The floor felt cool on tiny feet. Old boxes whispered when the wind moved the roof. Nutmeg liked the peaceful sound.

Nutmeg collected gentle things. She saved ribbon, a shiny button, a little bell, and soft bits of felt. “These will help,” she said, patting her small tool pouch. She liked to craft useful items from simple things.

One morning, Nutmeg heard a faint drip… drip… drip. A small hole in the roof let rain water fall, right over the nest where Nutmeg and her friends rested. The drops splashed on their blankets.

“Oh no,” said Pip, a little bat who liked to nap. “My blanket is wet.”

“My scarf is soggy,” squeaked Dot, a shy dormouse.

“We need to fix the drip,” Nutmeg said. She stood tall, tail high. “We will work together. I will lead, and we will each have a job.”

Pip blinked. “Can I help, even if I am sleepy?”

“Yes,” said Nutmeg kindly. “You can help in a way that feels good for you. We will take care of ourselves while we work.”

Nutmeg took a deep breath. She touched her chest and felt her heart slow. “First, we calm down. Then, we make a plan.”

They listened to the attic sounds: tap of the rain, creak of the beam, soft wind shhh. Everyone felt a little better.

Nutmeg pointed with a small paw. “Problem: rain is dripping here. Goal: keep beds dry. Plan: craft a patch, hang a drip-catcher, and clean up.”

Dot’s whiskers trembled. “I am small, but I can help,” she whispered.

Nutmeg smiled. “Dot, you are careful and quick. You can cut felt squares. Pip, you can hang high things. I will make the patch and show each step.”

They gathered supplies: felt, ribbon, button, bell, a tiny jar from an old box, and a long strip of cloth. The attic smelled like wool and apples from a crate.

Nutmeg washed her paws. “Self-care first,” she said. “Clean paws help good work.” She handed Dot a sip of water and a crumb of oat cookie. “Energy helps, too.”

Dot nibbled. Pip stretched his wings. “I will rest for one minute,” he said, closing his eyes. After a short rest, he whispered, “I am ready.”

Nutmeg showed them. “We practice before we do the big job. Practice makes strong paws and brave hearts.”

They practiced tying knots with ribbon. The knot was loose. It slipped. The jar almost fell.

“Oh!” Pip gasped.

“It is okay,” Nutmeg said gently. “We try again. We learn.”

They tried once more. This time the knot held tight. Pip smiled. “We did it.”

Nutmeg crafted a felt patch by layering squares, then sewing around the edges with thread she found in a tin. “Prick, pull. Prick, pull,” she said softly, keeping a steady beat. Dot watched closely. “Can I try?” Dot asked.

“Yes,” Nutmeg said. “I will show, then you practice.” Dot took tiny stitches. Prick, pull. Prick, pull. “I can do it!” she beamed.

Next, Nutmeg led them to the drip. “Pip, please hang the jar under the drip with the ribbon. Dot, please hand me the button.” Pip tied the ribbon to a beam and set the jar just right. Dot passed the button, her paws steady.

Nutmeg tested the jar. Drip… plink! The water fell into the jar with a happy sound. “Good job, team,” she said. “Now the felt patch.”

They could not fix the roof from outside, but they could soften the drop. Nutmeg placed the layered felt above the jar to guide the water. She stitched the button to secure the cloth strip. “This will help the drip slide into the jar.”

Drip… whoosh… plink! The water slid along the strip and into the jar. The floor stayed dry. Pip clapped his wings. Dot twirled.

They cleaned the wet blankets and laid them near a warm vent to dry. Nutmeg led a stretch. “Arms up, breathe in. Arms down, breathe out.” They smiled. “Taking care of our bodies keeps our minds clear,” Nutmeg said.

Just then, the ribbon slipped again. The jar tilted. “Uh-oh,” Pip said.

Nutmeg nodded. “We check, we fix, we try again.” She retied the ribbon with a double knot and showed Pip how. “Over, under, pull. Over, under, pull.” They practiced three times. The knot stayed strong.

Soon the attic felt cozy again. The air smelled like dry wood. The blanket felt warm and soft. The drip sang a little plink song in the jar, and the floor was safe.

Dot hugged Nutmeg. “You are a good leader.”

Nutmeg shook her head kindly. “We are a good team. A leader helps friends feel calm, gives clear jobs, listens to needs, and takes care of the team and self. We rested. We practiced. We fixed the problem together.”

Pip smiled. “I learned to tie a strong knot.”

Dot giggled. “I learned prick, pull.”

Nutmeg wrapped them both in a dry blanket. “And I learned that gentle words and practice make brave helpers.”

They all listened to the soft plink, plink, plink. It sounded like a tiny song of “thank you.”

The End.
