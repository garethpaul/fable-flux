# Whiskers and the Big Storm

Whiskers was a very clever orange cat who loved spending time in the sunny backyard. The warm grass tickled her paws, and she could smell the sweet flowers blooming everywhere. Birds chirped happily in the tall oak tree, and butterflies danced around the colorful garden.

One bright morning, Whiskers stretched her fluffy body and yawned. "What a beautiful day!" she purred, feeling the sunshine warm her fur. But as she looked up at the sky, she noticed something different. Dark clouds were gathering far away on the horizon.

"Hmm," thought Whiskers, twitching her whiskers. "Those clouds look like they might bring rain later."

Most cats would just ignore the distant clouds and keep playing. But Whiskers was clever and always liked to be prepared. She remembered what her mama cat had taught her: "A smart cat always plans ahead."

Whiskers decided to get ready, just in case. First, she found her favorite sunny spot under the garden shed where the roof would keep her dry. She tested it by lying down. "Perfect!" she meowed.

Next, she gathered her toys. She carefully carried her little red ball, her feather wand, and her catnip mouse to her safe, dry spot. "If it rains, I'll still have fun things to do," she said to herself.

Then Whiskers noticed her food bowl was almost empty. She trotted over to her human's door and meowed loudly. "Meow! Meow!" When her human came out, Whiskers rubbed against her legs and led her to the empty bowl.

"Oh, you're hungry, Whiskers!" said her human, filling the bowl with delicious kibble. Whiskers ate some right away and made sure there was plenty left for later.

As the morning went on, the dark clouds moved closer and closer. The wind began to blow, making the tree branches sway and dance. Other cats in the neighborhood started looking worried.

"Whiskers!" called her friend Mittens from next door. "The sky looks scary! What should we do?"

"Don't worry," Whiskers called back confidently. "I've been getting ready all morning. I have a dry place to stay, toys to play with, and food to eat. Come over if you need help!"

Just then, the first raindrops began to fall. Pitter-patter, pitter-patter on the leaves. The rain started gently, then became heavier and heavier. Thunder rumbled in the distance like a giant purring.

While other cats ran around looking for shelter, Whiskers was cozy and comfortable under the garden shed. She played with her toys, took a nice nap, and enjoyed her food. The sound of rain on the roof was actually quite soothing.

"This isn't so bad," she purred contentedly. "I'm warm, dry, and happy."

When the storm finally passed and the sun came out again, Whiskers emerged from her shelter. The backyard sparkled with water droplets that looked like tiny diamonds. The air smelled fresh and clean.

Mittens came over, looking amazed. "Whiskers, you were so smart to prepare! While I was running around getting wet and scared, you were comfortable the whole time."

Whiskers smiled and stretched in the returning sunshine. "My mama always said that being prepared makes everything easier. When we plan ahead, we don't have to worry!"

From that day on, Whiskers became known as the wisest cat in the neighborhood. And whenever storm clouds appeared, she always remembered to prepare, plan, and stay calm.

The End.
