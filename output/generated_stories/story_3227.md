# Sprig the Squirrel Helps in the Laundry Room

Sprig the squirrel had a fluffy tail, bright eyes, and quick paws. Sprig loved to help. Helping made Sprig’s heart feel warm, like a cozy blanket right out of the dryer.

One sunny morning, Sprig scampered into the laundry room. The room smelled like clean soap and warm cotton. The washing machine hummed, “Whirr-whirr,” and the dryer clicked, “Tick-tick.” The baskets were full of soft shirts, tiny socks, and big towels that felt like clouds.

“Good morning, Sprig,” said Mama, folding a blue shirt. “Would you like to help with the laundry?”

“Yes, please!” Sprig chirped. “I am a helper!”

Mama smiled. “Helping is wonderful. Let’s follow directions and be responsible. That means we listen, we do it safely, and we finish what we start.”

Sprig nodded. “Listen. Safe. Finish.”

Mama pointed to a little list on the wall. “These are our laundry words and steps. We say the words, then we do the steps.”

Sprig leaned close. The paper had simple words and tiny pictures.

“Sort,” Mama read. “Wash. Dry. Fold. Put away.”

Sprig repeated the words slowly, tasting each one like a new nut. “Sort. Wash. Dry. Fold. Put away.” The words felt fun on Sprig’s tongue. “Sort means make groups,” Sprig said proudly.

“That’s right,” Mama said. “Let’s sort.”

They made a pile of light clothes and a pile of dark clothes. Sprig found a small red sock hiding in the light pile.

“Oops! Red sock goes with dark,” Sprig said. “I am paying attention.”

“You are being responsible,” Mama said. “Thank you for your careful eyes.”

The washing machine door opened with a soft click. Sprig heard the rubber seal go “squeak” and smelled a sweet soap smell.

“Now we wash,” Mama said. “Please put the light clothes in. Only up to this line.” Mama pointed to a little sticker. “We follow this direction.”

Sprig filled the drum slowly. “Stop at the line,” Sprig said, stopping right on time. “I followed the direction!”

“Excellent helper,” Mama said. “Now, one little scoop of soap.”

Sprig held the scoop and felt the tiny soap grains like soft sand. “One scoop,” Sprig whispered, and poured carefully. “Not too much.”

“Perfect,” Mama said, pushing the button. The machine began to swish and sing, “Swish-swish, glug-glug.”

Sprig clapped. “Laundry has songs! Swish! Glug!”

“We can say our laundry words again,” Mama said.

“Sort. Wash. Dry. Fold. Put away,” Sprig sang, tapping the rhythm on the basket.

While they waited, Sprig noticed a little towel on the floor. It looked lonely.

“I can help,” Sprig said, picking it up. “Towel belongs in the basket.” Sprig patted the towel gently. “Everything has a place.”

The washer beeped. Beep! Beep!

“That sound means it’s done,” Mama said. “Now we dry.”

Sprig opened the washer. Warm, wet air puffed out, smelling like clean rain. The clothes were heavy with water.

“These go in the dryer,” Mama said. “But first, check the lint screen. It keeps the air moving.”

Sprig pulled the lint screen out. It was fuzzy and gray, like a tiny blanket. “So soft,” Sprig giggled. “We clean it, right?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Then we put it back.”

Sprig wiped the lint into the bin and slid the screen in. “Click,” it went.

“Now we put the clothes in,” Mama said. “No climbing inside. That is not safe.”

Sprig stood back. “I do not climb. I listen.” Sprig handed clothes to Mama one by one. “Here is a sock. Here is a shirt. Here is a towel.”

“Thank you, helper,” Mama said, closing the door. The dryer rumbled, “Rrrr-rrr,” like a friendly purr.

While it turned, Sprig practiced the words. “Sort. Wash. Dry. Fold. Put away.” The words felt like a story. Sprig liked how each word meant a job. Sprig felt strong and smart.

When the dryer finished, warm air puffed out again. The clothes were soft and toasty. Sprig snuggled a warm towel for a second. “Mmm, cozy,” Sprig murmured, then placed it on the table. “Time to fold!”

Mama showed Sprig how to fold a washcloth. “Fold in half,” Mama said. “Then fold again.”

Sprig tried. “Fold in half… then fold again.” The corners met like two friends holding hands. “I did it!”

“You followed directions,” Mama said. “You are responsible and helpful.”

They folded socks by making pairs. Sprig matched patterns. “Stripe with stripe. Star with star. Friends together.”

A little sock had no partner. Sprig frowned. “Where is your friend?”

“Maybe it’s in the basket,” Mama said.

Sprig looked under a shirt and found it. “Here!” Sprig cheered, and put the sock pair together. “Everybody has a friend.”

“Last step,” Mama said. “Put away.”

Sprig carried folded stacks carefully. The towels felt warm, the shirts smelled fresh, and the socks were bouncy. Sprig walked slowly, holding the pile like a treasure. “I will not drop you,” Sprig whispered.

They placed everything in drawers and on shelves. When they finished, the laundry room was tidy. The baskets were empty. The list on the wall smiled with its neat words.

Sprig stood with paws on hips, tail high. “We did all the steps,” Sprig said. “Sort. Wash. Dry. Fold. Put away.”

Mama knelt and hugged Sprig. “You were so helpful. You followed directions and were responsible. How do you feel?”

Sprig’s eyes sparkled. “I feel proud. Helping makes me happy.”

Mama nodded. “Helping helps everyone. When we listen and finish our steps, we take care of our home and each other.”

Sprig looked at the little list again. “Words help, too,” Sprig said. “Words show the way.”

“That’s right,” Mama said. “Words can guide us.”

Sprig patted the washer and the dryer. “Thank you for your songs,” Sprig said. “I like our laundry adventure.”

“Every day can be an adventure when we help,” Mama said.

Sprig smiled and waved a tiny paw. “I am Sprig. I am helpful. I follow directions. I am responsible!”

The laundry room hummed softly, and Sprig’s heart hummed, too.

The End.
