# Momo's Messy Masterpiece

Momo the monkey swung into the bright, colorful art room at Little Sprouts School. The walls were covered with cheerful paintings, and shelves held jars of paint in every color of the rainbow. The sweet smell of crayons and the fresh scent of paper filled the air.

"Today we're making nature collages!" announced Ms. Lily, their teacher. "First, we'll go outside to collect leaves and flowers. Then we'll come back to glue them on paper."

Momo's eyes sparkled with excitement. He loved adventures, especially ones that involved exploring outdoors! But as he looked at the clock, he noticed they only had one hour for the whole project.

"Don't worry," whispered his friend Bella the bunny. "We have plenty of time if we work step by step."

Outside in the garden, Momo's curiosity took over. "Ooh, look at this shiny red leaf!" he chattered, picking it up. Then he spotted a yellow flower. "And this pretty daisy!" Soon his arms were full of treasures - smooth pebbles, soft moss, and colorful petals.

But when Momo looked around, he realized he had wandered far from his friends. The other children were already heading back inside, and his collection was scattered all over the ground.

"Oh no!" Momo's voice trembled. "I dropped everything, and now I'm behind!"

Momo felt his tummy flutter with worry. He wanted to give up and hide behind a tree. But then he remembered what his mama always said: "When things get tough, take a deep breath and try again."

Momo closed his eyes and breathed in the fresh outdoor air. The gentle breeze rustled through the trees, and he could hear birds chirping encouragement. "I can do this," he said to himself. "I just need to focus."

Quickly but carefully, Momo gathered his favorite pieces - three colorful leaves, two smooth stones, and one perfect flower. Instead of trying to collect everything, he chose just what he needed.

Back in the art room, Momo's friends were already gluing their collections. His hands felt a bit shaky as he squeezed the cool, sticky glue onto his paper.

"I'm so far behind," Momo sighed, watching Bella finish her beautiful collage.

"It's okay to work at your own pace," said Ms. Lily gently, sitting beside him. "Sometimes the best art takes a little extra time."

Momo nodded and focused on his work. He carefully placed each leaf, feeling their bumpy textures under his fingers. The stones made satisfying little *tap* sounds as he positioned them. The flower's soft petals felt like velvet.

When time was up, Momo looked at his collage. It wasn't finished like the others, but it was uniquely his. The colors were bright and cheerful, and each piece was placed with love.

"Wow, Momo!" exclaimed Bella. "Your collage tells such an interesting story!"

Ms. Lily smiled warmly. "Momo, you showed real resilience today. When things got difficult, you didn't give up. You took your time and made something wonderful."

Momo's chest puffed up with pride. He had learned that it was okay to make mistakes and that trying again was what made him brave. Sometimes the best adventures happen when you keep going, even when things don't go as planned.

"Tomorrow I'll remember to manage my time better," Momo said thoughtfully. "But I'm proud I didn't give up!"

The End.
