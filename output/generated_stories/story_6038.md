# Rocky Raccoon Listens and Builds

Rocky was a musical raccoon who loved to hum and tap. He wore tiny earmuffs with stars on them. Tap-tap-tap went his paws. Hum-hum-hummm went his song.

Rocky’s friend, Dr. Dot, had a bright basement lab. The lab smelled like oranges and lemon cleaner. Lights twinkled. Buttons blinked. Tools clinked softly. There were bins for paper, plastic, and metal. There was a big sign that said, “Listen First.”

“Rocky,” said Dr. Dot with a smile, “today we will build a Clean-Stream Machine. It will help keep the river happy and clear.”

Rocky’s striped tail swished. “I can make music while we build!” he cheered, tapping a drum beat on a metal can.

Dr. Dot nodded. “Music is fun. But listening is very important. We must listen to the plan. We must listen to each other. We must listen to the sounds of the tools.”

Rocky nodded fast. “I will listen,” he said. His ears perked up.

Dr. Dot pointed to a big paper plan. It showed shapes and arrows. “Step one,” she said. “We sort clean, safe parts from the recycle bins. Step two, we bolt them together. Step three, we test the machine. Ready?”

“Ready!” Rocky said. He hummed softly.

They opened the bins. Clink, clink! Rocky heard a glass jar. Clatter! He heard a loose screw. He sniffed the clean metal. It felt cool. “This pipe is perfect,” he whispered.

“Good listening,” said Dr. Dot. “Only clean parts go in the machine. Dirty parts make trouble.”

Rocky found a plastic tube. He wanted to drum on it. Tap-tap—then he stopped. He took a deep breath. “Listen first,” he reminded himself. He listened to Dr. Dot.

“Put pipes in the blue box,” she said. “Put bolts in the red cup. Put the pump on the table.”

Rocky did just that. Clink in the red cup. Clonk in the blue box. Thump on the table. It felt good to do it right.

Soon it was time to build. Dr. Dot handed Rocky a tiny wrench. “Hold the pipe. I will turn,” she said.

Rocky held the pipe. He listened for the click-click of the bolt. Click. Click. Click. The sound was small and neat. He felt proud.

Then Rocky heard a new sound. Drip. Drip. Drip.

“What is that?” he asked, ears up.

“Let’s listen,” said Dr. Dot. They followed the sound. Drip. Drip. It came from a hose.

Rocky touched the hose. It was wet. “Oops,” he said. “A leak!”

“Good listening,” said Dr. Dot. “We can fix it.” She gave Rocky some tape. “Hold steady.”

Rocky took a slow breath. He listened to the sticky sound. Rippp. He wrapped the tape tight. The drip stopped.

They worked more. Rocky felt excited. He started to sing loud. “La-la-la! Boom-boom-boom!”

Dr. Dot raised her hand. “Rocky,” she said gently. “Let’s use quiet voices now. Loud sounds make it hard to hear the tools.”

Rocky’s eyes grew big. He put a paw on his mouth. “Oh! I forgot. I will listen.”

He tried again. He hummed softly like buzzing bees. Bzz-bzz. He listened for the gentle whirr of the pump. Whirr. He listened for the soft whoosh of air. Whoosh. The sounds helped him know the machine was working.

Suddenly, the lights blinked twice. Blink. Blink.

“What does that mean?” asked Rocky.

Dr. Dot pointed to the plan. “Blinking means we must check the filter. Listen for scratchy sounds.”

They stood still. Scratch-scratch-scrrr. “I hear it!” said Rocky. He felt the filter with his paw. It felt rough.

“This filter is full,” said Dr. Dot. “We need to clean it.”

Rocky carried the filter to the sink. He turned on the water. Swish-swash. He watched dirt swirl away. He liked how fresh it smelled. Like rain!

Back at the table, they put the clean filter in. Click. The light glowed green. “We did it!” Rocky cheered, tail swishing.

“Almost done,” said Dr. Dot. “Let’s test by listening to the river water.”

They poured water into the Clean-Stream Machine. The pump went whirr. The pipes went whoosh. The filter went shhh. Clear water came out the other side. It sparkled like tiny stars.

Rocky clapped. “The river will be happy!” he said. He imagined ducks quacking, frogs croaking, and fish swishing in clean water.

Dr. Dot smiled. “You listened to the plan. You listened to the tools. You listened to the sounds of a problem. Because you listened, we could care for the Earth. We built something helpful.”

Rocky stood tall. “Listening helps me build. Listening helps me help the river.” He pressed his paw to his chest. “I can do that.”

He hummed a soft thank-you song. Hum-hum-hummm. It sounded like a hug.

Dr. Dot put a gold sticker on Rocky’s earmuffs. It was shaped like a leaf. “Team Listener,” she said.

Rocky giggled. “I like being on Team Listener.” He looked around the tidy lab. The bins were sorted. The tools were clean. The air smelled bright. He felt calm and proud.

That night, Rocky walked to the river with Dr. Dot. They listened. Splish. Splash. Crickets chirped. The moon glowed. The water was clear and smooth.

Rocky whispered, “Thank you, river. I will listen to you.”

He took a deep breath. He listened to the quiet night. He listened to his happy heart.

Lesson: When we stop, look, and listen, we can build good things and take care of our world.

The End.
