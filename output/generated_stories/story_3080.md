# Polly's Paint Problem

In a bright, colorful art room, there lived a creative parrot named Polly. She had beautiful green feathers and a curved orange beak. Polly loved making art with paintbrushes in her claws. The art room smelled like fresh crayons and had rainbow pictures on every wall.

Every morning, Polly flew to her favorite easel. She painted pretty flowers and sunny skies. The soft brushes felt nice in her feathers. But today, something was wrong.

"Oh no!" squawked Polly. She looked at her paint palette. All her favorite colors were almost gone! The red paint was just a tiny drop. The blue paint was nearly empty too.

Polly felt worried. "How can I make my beautiful pictures without enough paint?" she wondered. She flapped her wings and paced around the art room.

Just then, her friend Benny the bunny hopped into the room. Benny had fluffy white fur and long ears. He carried a small paintbrush in his paw.

"Hi Polly!" said Benny cheerfully. "Want to paint together today?"

Polly looked down at her feet. "I can't paint today, Benny. I don't have enough paint left."

Benny tilted his head. "What do you mean?"

"Look!" Polly showed him her palette. "I used too much paint yesterday. Now there's barely any left for today."

Benny's nose twitched as he thought. "Hmm, I have an idea! What if we share our paint? I have lots of yellow and purple."

Polly's eyes lit up. "Really? You would share with me?"

"Of course!" Benny smiled. "Friends help each other. Plus, we can make new colors by mixing them together!"

Polly felt much better. "That's so kind of you, Benny. Next time, I'll be more careful with my paint. I'll only use what I need."

Together, they squeezed small amounts of paint onto their palettes. Benny showed Polly how to mix yellow and red to make orange. Polly discovered that blue and yellow made a beautiful green.

"Look!" chirped Polly excitedly. "We're making rainbow colors!"

They painted side by side. Polly made a picture of two friends under a bright sun. Benny painted colorful butterflies flying together. The art room filled with happy giggles and the gentle sound of brushes on paper.

"This is even more fun than painting alone," said Polly. "And we're not wasting any paint!"

Benny nodded. "When we share and use just what we need, there's enough for everyone."

As they cleaned their brushes in the water jar, Polly learned something important. She realized that being careful with art supplies meant she could create more pictures. And sharing with friends made everything more special.

"Thank you for helping me today," Polly said to Benny. "Tomorrow, let's paint together again!"

"I'd love that," replied Benny. "We make a great team!"

From that day on, Polly always used just the right amount of paint. She and Benny became the best of painting partners. They shared their supplies and created the most wonderful artwork together.

The art room was filled with their colorful creations, and more importantly, it was filled with friendship.

The End.
