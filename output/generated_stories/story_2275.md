# Ellie Helps in the Nursery

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and a tiny, wiggly tail. She lived in a comfortable nursery full of cozy blankets, bright blocks, and the sweet smell of warm milk. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor, and the room sounded like happy hums and quiet giggles.

One morning, Ellie woke up to the soft chime of the nursery clock. “Good morning,” she whispered, stretching her little trunk. Her blanket felt warm and fuzzy. She loved the nursery. It was safe, soft, and full of friends.

Caregiver Coco, with kind eyes and a sweater the color of blueberries, clapped gently. “Good morning, little ones. Today we tidy the play corner, then share snack time.”

Ellie’s ears flapped with excitement. “I can help!” she said. Helping made Ellie’s heart feel big and warm, like a hug.

Across the room, two friends, Bibi the bunny and Max the mouse, were building a tall block tower. Blocks clicked and clacked. “Our tower is the biggest,” Bibi said proudly.

“It’s as tall as the lamp!” squeaked Max.

Ellie watched, smiling. She wanted to help them too. But her feet were big. Her trunk was curious. She took a careful step. The floor felt cool under her toes. She took another step, extra slow.

“Can I help?” Ellie asked in a small, gentle voice.

Bibi’s whiskers twitched. “Um… maybe just a little,” Bibi said.

Max looked up at the tall tower. “Please be careful,” he squeaked.

Ellie wanted to be helpful. She wanted her friends to feel happy. She reached out with her trunk to add one more block. But her tail swished. Her foot nudged. The tower wobbled, wibble-wobbled—then CRASH! Blocks tumbled in a clattery rain.

Bibi’s eyes grew wide. “Oh no!”

Max squeaked, “Our tower!”

Ellie’s ears drooped. Her tummy felt twisty, like a knot. “I didn’t want to break it,” she said softly. “I wanted to help.”

Bibi crossed her little paws. “I feel sad.”

Max hugged a block. “Me too.”

Caregiver Coco knelt, her sweater soft against Ellie’s cheek. “I see a problem,” Coco said calmly. “We have big feelings. Let’s use gentle words and find a way to fix it.”

Ellie took a slow breath. She remembered: helpful hands, helpful words. “I’m sorry,” she said to Bibi and Max. “I made a mistake. I want to help fix it.”

Bibi’s nose twitched. “Thank you for saying sorry,” she said, a little less sad.

Max nodded. “We can try again.”

“How can I help in a better way?” Ellie asked.

Bibi thought. “Maybe you can hand us blocks.”

Max added, “And we will do the stacking.”

Coco smiled. “That is a kind plan.”

Ellie’s ears perked up. “Okay!” she said. She sat on the soft rug. It felt fluffy under her legs. She lined up blocks by color: sunny yellow, sky blue, leafy green, and cherry red. “Here you go,” she said, passing a blue block to Max.

“Thank you,” Max squeaked. His thank-you made Ellie’s cheeks feel warm and sparkly.

“Here’s a green one for you,” Ellie said to Bibi.

“Thanks, Ellie,” Bibi said. “That helps a lot.”

The tower grew, steady and strong. Click. Clack. Click. Clack. Ellie kept the floor tidy, placing extra blocks in a neat basket. She even used her trunk like a gentle crane, sliding blocks close but not touching the tower.

When the tower was as tall as Coco’s shoulder, everyone stepped back. “Wow,” Ellie breathed. The tower looked bright and brave. The nursery lights twinkled on the smooth block tops.

Bibi hopped in a happy circle. “We did it!”

Max squeaked, “Teamwork!”

Ellie’s heart felt light. She had helped in a way that worked. “I like helping,” she said. “Helping feels good.”

Coco clapped softly. “You solved a problem together,” she said. “You used calm voices. You made a plan. You tried again. That is helpfulness.”

Ellie nodded. “And I said sorry,” she added. “Thank you for letting me try again.”

“Thank you for helping,” said Bibi.

“Thank you,” squeaked Max. “You were careful and kind.”

Snack time came. The nursery smelled like sweet bananas and warm toast. Ellie passed napkins with her trunk. “One for you, Bibi. One for you, Max. One for Coco.” She liked the rustle of the napkins and the soft thump as she set them down.

“Thank you,” everyone said.

Ellie smiled. Her ears made small, happy flaps. The room felt peaceful, like a cozy blanket. She took a bite of banana. It was soft and sweet.

Coco sat beside them. “What did we learn today?”

Ellie thought. She tapped her trunk gently. “Helping means asking, listening, and doing my part,” she said. “When we make a mistake, we say sorry and try again.”

Bibi added, “We can make a plan together.”

Max squeaked, “And say thank you!”

Ellie’s eyes shone. “Helpfulness makes friends feel happy and safe.”

Coco hugged them all in a warm, gentle squeeze. “Yes,” she said. “Helpfulness, gratitude, and kind problem-solving make our nursery a wonderful place.”

Ellie looked at the tall tower, the tidy floor, and her smiling friends. Her heart felt as soft as her favorite blanket. “I can help,” she whispered to herself, “every single day.”

The End.
