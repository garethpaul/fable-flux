# Leo's Colorful Mistake

Leo was a brave little lion cub with a golden mane that sparkled in the sunlight. Today was his first day in the creative art room at his new school. The room smelled like fresh paint and colorful crayons.

"Welcome, Leo!" said Ms. Rosa, his art teacher. She had kind brown eyes and wore a paint-splattered apron. "Today we're building friendship towers with blocks from around the world."

Leo's eyes grew wide. The art room was amazing! There were red blocks from China, blue blocks from Greece, and yellow blocks from Mexico. Children sat in groups, carefully stacking their towers.

Leo joined a table with three other cubs. Maya had soft gray fur, Chen had stripes like a tiger, and Zara had spots like a leopard. They were building the most beautiful tower Leo had ever seen.

"Can I help?" Leo asked excitedly. His tail swished with happiness.

"Of course!" said Maya. "We're making it super tall!"

Leo reached for a bright purple block from India. But his paw was too eager. CRASH! The whole tower tumbled down. Blocks scattered everywhere with loud clattering sounds.

"Oh no!" gasped Chen. "Our tower!"

"We worked so hard on that," whispered Zara, her whiskers drooping.

Leo's brave heart felt very small. "I'm sorry! I'm really, really sorry!" His golden mane seemed to lose its sparkle.

The other cubs looked sad. Their beautiful tower was just a pile of blocks now. Leo wanted to run away and hide.

"I didn't mean to knock it down," Leo said quietly. "I just wanted to help make it pretty."

Maya looked at Leo's sad face. She remembered when she had accidentally spilled paint on her friend's picture last week. "It's okay, Leo," she said gently. "Accidents happen."

"But you worked so hard," Leo sniffled.

Chen picked up a green block from Brazil. "You know what? Maybe we can build an even better tower together. Four friends can build something amazing!"

"Really?" Leo's ears perked up hopefully.

"Yes!" said Zara, her spots seeming to dance. "We forgive you, Leo. Friends forgive each other."

Ms. Rosa smiled as she watched. "What a wonderful lesson about forgiveness," she said. "Now, let's build together!"

The four friends sat in a circle. This time, they planned their tower carefully. Leo suggested they make it look like a rainbow castle. Maya wanted windows from different countries. Chen added bridges between the sections. Zara made it strong at the bottom.

"Your turn, Leo," said Maya, handing him a red block from Japan. "Build with us!"

Leo carefully placed the block. His paws were gentle this time. The tower grew taller and more beautiful than before. It had colors from all around the world - just like their friendship.

"Look how amazing it is!" Leo said proudly. "It's even better than the first one!"

"That's because we built it together," said Chen, grinning.

"And because we forgave each other," added Zara.

When their parents came to pick them up, all four friends were still playing together. Their rainbow tower stood tall and proud in the center of the table.

"I learned something important today," Leo told his mama. "When we forgive our friends and work together, we can build something even more beautiful."

Mama Lion nuzzled Leo's golden mane. "That's a very wise lesson, my brave little cub."

From that day on, Leo, Maya, Chen, and Zara were the best of friends. And whenever someone made a mistake, they always remembered the magic of forgiveness and building together.

The End.
