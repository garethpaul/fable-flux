# Sparky Plans a Peaceful Sleepover

Click-whirr! Sparky, the helpful robot dog, padded into the guest room on soft rubber paws. The room smelled like fresh soap and lemon polish. The blankets were fluffy like clouds, and the lamp made a warm, golden glow.

“Beep-boop! This is the cozy room,” Sparky said in his cheerful, tinny voice. “Our guests will sleep here tonight.”

Sparky looked around with his bright blue eyes. His nose light blinked. He had a plan chart on his tummy screen. It said: Plan for Guests:
1. Clean
2. Share
3. Quiet fun
4. Good sleep

“Planning helps things go smoothly,” Sparky hummed. “Let’s get ready.”

Then Sparky heard tiny footsteps. Tap-tap-tap. Two cousins peeked in the door. Mia held a teddy bear. Ben held a little drum.

“Hi, Sparky!” Mia whispered. “It smells nice in here.”

Ben grinned. “Can I play my drum?”

Sparky wagged his metal tail. “Welcome, friends! Let’s talk and make a peaceful plan.”

Ben’s smile faded. “I want to drum now.”

Mia hugged her bear. “I want to brush my teeth and get cozy.”

Sparky beeped softly. The room felt tense, like a string pulled tight. He remembered his main rule: peaceful solutions. He sat down and spoke calmly.

“I hear two good ideas,” Sparky said. “Ben wants drum time. Mia wants clean teeth and quiet. We can make a plan so everyone feels good.”

“How?” Ben asked, shifting his drum.

“First, we take care of hygiene,” Sparky said kindly. “Clean helps us stay healthy. Then, we share time for noise and quiet. We use a strategy—take turns!”

Mia nodded. “I like turns.”

Ben tilted his head. “Okay… but I want to hear the drum.”

Sparky’s ear antenna twitched. “We can use soft sticks.” He opened his side pocket with a shhh-click and pulled out two fuzzy drum sticks. “These make a gentle sound.”

Ben tapped them on his hand. Thup-thup. “They’re soft!”

“Great,” Sparky said. “Step one: brushing teeth. Step two: soft drum time. Step three: quiet story. Does that plan work?”

Mia smiled. “Yes!”

Ben shrugged, then grinned. “Okay!”

Sparky led them to the little bathroom. The water made a happy hiss. The toothpaste smelled like minty snow. “Squeeze a pea-sized dot,” Sparky said. “Brush, brush, brush—front teeth, back teeth, little circle swirls.”

Mia giggled with bubbly foam. “It tickles!”

Ben watched in the mirror and copied. Swish-swish-swish. “My teeth feel shiny!”

“Clean and bright,” Sparky chimed. He gave them both warm towels. “Pat, pat, pat. Dry and cozy.”

Back in the guest room, Sparky smoothed the blankets. “Now, soft drum time.”

Ben sat on the rug and tapped. Thup-thup. Thup-thup. It sounded like raindrops on a window. Mia swayed and made her teddy dance. The lamp hummed, and the lemony air felt calm.

“I want to try,” Mia said.

Ben passed the drum. “Your turn.”

Mia tapped, thup… thup… thup. “We’re sharing!”

Sparky’s tail whirred. “That is generous. Sharing makes hearts feel big.”

When the timer on Sparky’s tummy went ping, he said, “All done with drum time. Last step: quiet story.”

Ben handed the drum to Sparky. “Thanks for the soft sticks.”

“You’re welcome,” Sparky said. He put the drum and sticks neatly on a shelf. “Planning helps us know what comes next.”

Sparky turned the lamp lower. The light turned honey-soft. He opened a book that smelled like paper and glue. “Tonight’s story is about a little moon who learned to listen.”

Mia snuggled her bear. “I like listening.”

Ben curled up on the pillow. “Me too.”

Sparky read in a gentle voice. “The moon waited, the stars sang softly, and everyone took turns to shine.” His words were like a blanket—warm and slow. The room felt safe and sleepy.

Mia yawned. “Thank you, Sparky.”

Ben blinked. “I didn’t know soft drums could be fun.”

Sparky smiled with a soft beep. “We used peaceful talk, made a plan, kept clean, and shared. That is a great team.”

Mia whispered, “What if we want different things again?”

Sparky patted the blanket. “We stop. We listen. We think of a plan. We try a strategy, like taking turns, using quiet tools, or finding a new way that feels good for everyone.”

Ben nodded. “And we brush our teeth first.”

“Exactly,” Sparky said. He tucked the blanket around them like a gentle hug. The lemony-clean smell, the soft lamp light, and the quiet breath of the children filled the room.

Mia closed her eyes. “I feel calm.”

Ben smiled. “Me too.”

Sparky sat by the door, his little chest light glowing. “Peaceful solutions make a happy room. Generous hearts make happy friends. Good plans help us know what to do.”

Outside, the night was still. Inside, the guest room was cozy and clean, with two sleepy cousins and one helpful robot dog keeping kind watch.

The clear lesson of the night was simple: When we listen, plan, and share, we can find peaceful ways to solve problems, keep clean, and be kind to each other.

The End.
