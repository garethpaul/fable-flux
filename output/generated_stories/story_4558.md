# Pip's Porch Day

Pip the penguin loved sitting on his peaceful front porch. The wooden boards felt warm under his orange feet, and he could smell the sweet flowers blooming nearby. Today was perfect for watching the world go by.

"What a beautiful morning!" Pip said, stretching his flippers wide. The sun painted everything golden, and a gentle breeze made the wind chimes sing their tinkling song.

Soon, Pip heard *thump, thump, thump* down the path. It was Benny the bunny, hopping as fast as his legs could carry him.

"Hi Pip!" Benny called out, breathing hard. "Can I sit with you? I'm so tired!"

Without waiting for an answer, Benny plopped right down next to Pip. He was so close that his fluffy fur tickled Pip's wing.

Pip felt a little squished. He loved having friends visit, but he also liked having his own space. His tummy felt tight and twisty.

"I need to catch my breath," Benny panted. "I was running from the big kids who were being too loud. They scared me!"

Pip looked at Benny's worried face and droopy ears. His friend looked really upset. Pip's heart felt warm and caring, even though he still felt crowded.

"That sounds scary, Benny," Pip said gently. "I'm sorry that happened to you."

Pip took a deep breath, just like his mama taught him when he had big feelings. He counted to three slowly. Then he had an idea.

"Benny, I can see you're feeling scared and need a safe place," Pip said kindly. "I want to help you feel better. But could you sit on that step over there? I need a little space to feel comfortable too."

Benny's ears perked up. "Oh! I didn't know I was too close. I was just so worried, I wasn't thinking about your feelings."

"That's okay," Pip smiled. "When we're scared or upset, sometimes we forget. But we can always ask each other what we need."

Benny hopped over to the step Pip pointed to. It was still close enough to talk, but now Pip had room to stretch his flippers.

"Is this better?" Benny asked.

"Perfect!" Pip said, feeling much more relaxed. "Now, tell me more about what happened. I want to understand."

As Benny shared his story, Pip listened carefully. He made understanding sounds like "mmm-hmm" and "oh my." When Benny's voice got shaky, Pip said, "You're safe here with me."

"Thank you for listening, Pip," Benny said, his ears standing up straighter. "And thank you for telling me you needed space. I feel better knowing how to be a good friend."

"You ARE a good friend, Benny," Pip said warmly. "Good friends care about each other's feelings AND respect each other's needs."

They sat together peacefully, listening to the birds chirp and watching fluffy clouds drift by. Benny felt calm and safe. Pip felt happy and comfortable.

"Want to share some crackers?" Pip offered, pulling out a small bag from beside his chair.

"Yes, please!" Benny said with a big smile.

As they munched their snack, Pip felt proud. He had helped his friend feel better while also taking care of his own needs. That felt really good.

"This is the best porch day ever," Benny said, and Pip agreed completely.

The End.
