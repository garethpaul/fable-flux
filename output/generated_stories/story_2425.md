# Grace the Giraffe Sets the Table

Grace was a thoughtful giraffe with gentle eyes and a long, long neck. She lived in a sunny home where the dining room was full of warm light and soft smells. The table was smooth and wooden, and the chairs made friendly little squeaks when they moved. Every evening, the dining room became the heart of the home.

One day, Mama called, “Grace, it’s time to set the table.”

Grace loved to help. She stretched her neck and breathed in. Mmm! The air smelled like warm tomatoes and basil. There was the soft clink of bowls and the quiet hum of the fridge. Grace felt calm and proud.

“I can do it,” Grace said. “I will make the table neat.”

Grace started with the placemats. She lined them up, edge to edge. She whispered, “One, two, three, four,” as she counted. She placed shiny forks and smooth spoons. She put cups by each plate, just so. It felt good to be organized. Her tail swished with happy focus.

Knock, knock! There was a sound at the door. Grace looked up.

“Friends are here!” Mama called.

In came Leo the lamb, with soft wool and bouncing steps. Behind him waddled Pip the penguin, cool and quiet, with flappy little feet. Last came Mia the mouse, tiny and quick, with bright curious eyes.

“Hi, Grace!” Leo said. His voice was loud and cheerful. “We brought bread!”

“Hello,” Pip said softly. The word sounded like a gentle wave. “I like your table.”

Mia squeaked, “Can I sit by the window? I like to look at the sky.”

Grace felt a little worry. She had a plan. She wanted everyone to sit in the spots she had placed. The seats were not by the window. Grace’s heart thumped. She liked the table to be just right.

“I worked hard to make the places,” Grace said, trying to smile. “Can we sit in order?”

Leo’s legs were already dancing. He put the bread down with a thump. “I like to sit on the end!” he said.

Pip stood very still. “I would like a chair where my feet can touch the rung,” he murmured.

Mia twitched her whiskers. “I feel safe near the window,” she said.

Grace felt her cheeks grow warm. Her chest felt tight. She wanted to say, “No!” But Mama touched her shoulder and whispered, “Take a breath, dear.”

Grace took a slow breath in and a slow breath out. She listened to the sounds—the clock’s tick-tock, the wind’s hush, the soft crackle of bread crust. She looked at her friends’ faces. Leo’s eyes were bright, Pip’s were careful, and Mia’s were hopeful. Grace felt her tight chest soften.

“Okay,” Grace said slowly. “I hear you. Leo likes the end. Pip needs a rung for his feet. Mia likes the window.”

She thought about acceptance. Acceptance means letting people be themselves. It means saying, “You are okay just as you are.”

Grace nodded. “We can make this work. We can change the plan.”

She moved a placemat to the end for Leo. Scritch, scritch went the mat on the table. She slid a chair with a low rung for Pip. Squeak, squeak went the chair legs. She put a cozy napkin by the window for Mia. The napkin smelled like clean cotton.

Leo beamed. “Thanks, Grace!”

Pip smiled shyly. “Thank you for listening.”

Mia sighed with relief. “I feel good here.”

Grace smiled back. “I like when we all feel okay.” Her heart felt warm and light.

They sat down together. The dining room felt peaceful and happy. Mama brought tomato soup. Steam curled up like little clouds. “Careful, it’s hot,” she said.

Grace passed the bread. “Would you like a slice, Leo?”

“Yes, please!” Leo said. He took a big sniff. “Yum!”

Pip asked, “May I have butter?” His voice was still soft, but it felt stronger now.

“Yes,” said Grace. She placed the butter near Pip. “Let’s keep things tidy,” she added. “We can share and keep the table neat.”

They ate. Slurp, sip, munch. The sounds were friendly. Grace watched crumbs, then gently swept them into a little pile. “We can each use a napkin,” she reminded, smiling. Organization helped everyone enjoy the meal.

“Grace,” Mia said between tiny bites, “thank you for accepting our needs.”

Grace nodded. “When I listen to you, I understand you better. When I accept you, we all feel safe.”

Leo tapped his hoof softly. “Sometimes I get excited,” he said. “Thanks for not getting mad.”

“I can be very quiet,” Pip added. “Thanks for noticing what helps me.”

Grace felt proud and calm. “We are all different,” she said. “Different is good. We can be kind and still keep things in order. We can make space for each other.”

After dinner, they all helped clean up. Scrape, rinse, stack. Grace showed where each cup and spoon went. “Spoons in the small bin, forks in the big bin,” she said. It felt smooth and simple. Working together was fun.

When the table was clear, the wood felt cool under Grace’s hoof. The room smelled like soap and warm bread. The chairs gave one last friendly squeak.

Mama smiled. “What did we learn today?”

Grace lifted her head. “We learned to accept each other. We listened. We kept the table organized. We were patient and kind.”

Leo, Pip, and Mia all said, “Yes!”

Grace’s heart glowed. She had changed her plan, and it was okay. It was better. She had made room for her friends. And in that warm, bright dining room, everyone felt seen, heard, and loved.

The End.
