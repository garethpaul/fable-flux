# Benny Bear's Art Room Adventure

Benny Bear was a small, fluffy cub with soft brown fur and bright curious eyes. Today was his first day in the creative art room at his new school. The room smelled like crayons and paint, and colorful pictures covered every wall.

"Wow!" Benny said, looking around at all the art supplies. There were paintbrushes of every size, boxes of crayons, glue sticks, and sparkly stickers everywhere.

Benny spotted a big box of special markers that changed colors when you drew with them. His paws tingled with excitement as he reached for them.

"Those are mine!" squeaked Ruby Rabbit, hopping over quickly. She grabbed the markers and held them close to her chest. "I was using them first!"

Benny's ears drooped. "But I just wanted to try them," he said softly.

Ms. Owl, their teacher, flew over with a gentle smile. "What's happening here, little ones?"

"Benny tried to take my markers!" Ruby said, her nose twitching nervously.

"I didn't know they were yours," Benny explained. "I just wanted to draw a rainbow."

Ms. Owl nodded thoughtfully. "Ruby, it sounds like you were feeling worried about your art supplies. And Benny, you were excited to create something beautiful. Both feelings are okay!"

She sat down between them. "In our art room, we have a special rule. When someone is using something, we ask before we touch it. That's called respecting boundaries."

"What's a boundary?" Benny asked, tilting his fuzzy head.

"A boundary is like an invisible line that helps us know what belongs to someone else," Ms. Owl explained. "Just like how you wouldn't want someone to take your favorite teddy bear without asking, Ruby doesn't want someone to take her art supplies without asking."

Benny's eyes grew wide with understanding. "Oh! So I should have said, 'May I please use the markers when you're done?'"

"Exactly!" Ms. Owl hooted happily.

Ruby's ears perked up. She looked at Benny's sad face and felt her heart soften. "Benny, I'm sorry I got upset. I was worried there wouldn't be enough markers for everyone."

"That's okay," Benny said. "I should have asked first. I'm still learning!"

Ruby thought for a moment, then smiled. "Would you like to share the markers with me? We could draw a rainbow together!"

Benny's face lit up like sunshine. "Really? That would be amazing!"

The two friends sat side by side at the art table. Ruby showed Benny how the special markers worked, and together they created the most beautiful rainbow either of them had ever seen. The colors seemed to dance and shimmer on the paper.

"This is even better than drawing alone," Ruby giggled as she watched the purple marker magically turn pink.

"It really is!" Benny agreed, carefully staying in his part of the paper. "And now I know to always ask before touching someone else's things."

Ms. Owl watched them work together and smiled proudly. "Look how wonderful things can be when we respect each other's boundaries and share with kind hearts!"

As they finished their rainbow masterpiece, Benny learned that asking permission wasn't just polite – it was a way to show care for his friends. And Ruby discovered that sharing made art time twice as fun.

From that day on, Benny always remembered to ask before touching anything that belonged to someone else, and their art room became the happiest, most creative place in the whole school.

The End.
