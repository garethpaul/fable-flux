# Benny Bunny’s Clean-Up Crew

Benny Bunny was a determined rabbit with bright eyes and soft, snowy fur. He lived in a sunny backyard full of warm grass, fluttering butterflies, and the sweet smell of flowers. The sun tickled his whiskers, and the breeze felt cool on his ears.

One morning, Benny hopped to his favorite carrot patch. “Oh no,” he said, scrunching his nose. “My carrots are dirty!” The carrots were covered with soil and a little sticky mud. The watering can had tipped over, and the bucket was messy too.

Benny tapped his foot. Tap, tap, tap. “I love carrots,” he said softly, “but I must keep clean. Clean hands. Clean tools. Clean food.” He remembered what Mama Bunny always said: “Clean is safe. Clean is healthy.”

Benny took a deep breath. He could smell damp dirt and warm sunshine. He made a plan, because Benny was a thinking rabbit.

Step one, he said to himself, wash my paws.

He hopped to the little garden sink. The water splashed, splash-splash-splash, and felt cool on his paws. “Wet, soap, scrub, rinse, dry,” Benny sang. He rubbed his paws together, making tiny bubbles. The soap smelled like lemon. He rinsed, then shook his paws—drip, drop, drip—and dried them on a clean towel.

“Paws are clean!” Benny smiled.

Step two, wash the carrots.

Benny filled a clean bowl with water. Swish, swish! He gently brushed each carrot. “Hello, carrot. Into the bath you go!” he giggled. The water turned a little brown, but the carrots turned bright and shiny. “All clean,” he said.

Step three, clean the tools and make a clean place to eat.

Benny washed the watering can, the bucket, and the small shovel. He set them in the sun to dry. The metal felt warm when he touched it, and it shone in the light. He spread a clean cloth on the grass for a picnic. The cloth smelled like fresh air.

Just then, Benny heard footsteps. Pat-pat-pat!

It was Tilly Turtle, Sammy Squirrel, and Dottie Duck. “Hi, Benny!” they called.

“Hi, friends!” Benny waved. “I’m making a clean carrot picnic. Would you like to help?”

Tilly nodded slowly. “I can carry the clean bowl,” she said.

Sammy chattered, “I can gather clean napkins!”

Dottie flapped her wings. “Quack! I can sing the washing song!”

Benny smiled. “Great! But first, we all wash our hands.”

They lined up at the garden sink. Benny showed them. “Wet, soap, scrub, rinse, dry,” he said. They sang together:

“Wet, soap, scrub, scrub, scrub,
Rinse the bubbles, rub-a-dub!
Dry with towels, nice and neat,
Now our hands are clean to eat!”

Tilly’s hands were shiny. Sammy’s paws smelled lemony. Dottie’s feathers felt soft and fresh.

Benny checked the time on the little sun clock he made with a stick and a stone. The shadow pointed to the big leaf. “We have ten hops until snack time,” he said. “Let’s use our time well.”

He made a list in his head:
- Wash hands: done.
- Clean carrots: done.
- Set the picnic: almost done.
- Invite neighbors: do now!

“Sammy,” Benny said, “please invite Miss Mole and Mr. Beetle while I set cups. Tilly, can you place the napkins? Dottie, keep singing the song so we remember to be clean.”

Off they went! Sammy darted like a quick breeze. Tilly placed napkins slowly and carefully—neat, neat, neat. Dottie sang cheerfully, “Scrub-a-dub, clean is love!”

Soon, neighbors arrived. Miss Mole sniffed. “Mmm, it smells fresh.” Mr. Beetle clicked happily. “Everything looks so clean and bright!”

Benny pointed to a small sign he drew with a crayon. The sign read: “Clean Hands, Happy Tummies.” He smiled. “We keep the backyard clean to help everyone stay healthy.”

They all sat on the cloth. Benny passed the carrots with a clean spoon. “One at a time,” he said kindly. “Please and thank you.”

Dottie reached for a carrot and was about to take two. Benny shook his head gently. “Let’s share so everyone gets one first.” Dottie nodded. “Quack, you’re right.”

After the picnic, Benny had one more idea. “Let’s do a Clean-Up Crew,” he said. “We can pick up tiny bits of leaves, put tools away, and wash our paws again.”

Everyone cheered. They collected little bits, plink-plink into a basket. They wiped the cloth. They washed hands again. Wet, soap, scrub, rinse, dry!

The backyard looked sunny and tidy. The air smelled like lemon and grass. Benny’s heart felt warm. He had used smart steps to solve the muddy mess. He had shared clean food with friends. They worked together, and they finished on time.

Miss Mole smiled. “Benny, your plan was clever.”

Mr. Beetle clicked. “Your heart was generous.”

Tilly said, “Your backyard is so clean!”

Benny’s ears perked up. “Thank you. Clean hands and clean tools keep us healthy. Thinking in steps helps us fix problems. Sharing and helping make our backyard happy.”

Everyone nodded.

Benny looked at the clean bowl and the bright carrots all gone. He felt proud and calm. “Today we learned,” he said gently, “that hygiene keeps us safe, thinking helps us solve problems, and being generous brings smiles.”

They waved goodbye as the sun made the grass sparkle. Benny tucked away the towel, straightened the sign, and took one last sniff of lemon and flowers.

“Clean is kind,” he whispered, and hopped home.

The End.
