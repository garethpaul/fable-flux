# Ollie's Kitchen Helper Day

Ollie the otter wiggled his whiskers as he smelled something yummy coming from the warm kitchen. The morning sun streamed through the window, making everything glow golden and cozy.

"Good morning, Mama!" chirped Ollie, sliding across the smooth wooden floor on his belly. His soft brown fur was still damp from his morning swim in the pond.

"Good morning, my little splash!" laughed Mama Otter, stirring a big pot of fish soup. The steam rose up like fluffy clouds, and Ollie could smell the delicious herbs floating in the air.

Ollie noticed his little sister Lily sitting quietly in the corner, looking very sad. Her usually bright eyes were droopy, and she wasn't playing with her favorite shell.

"What's wrong, Lily?" asked Ollie, paddling over to her with his webbed paws.

"I wanted to help Mama cook," whispered Lily. "But I'm too small to reach the counter. I can't do anything right."

Ollie's heart felt heavy seeing his sister so upset. He remembered feeling the same way when he was smaller. "Oh, Lily," he said gently, "I felt sad like that too sometimes."

Ollie looked around the warm kitchen with its wooden shelves full of clean dishes and herbs hanging from the ceiling. Then his eyes lit up with an idea!

"Mama," called Ollie, "Lily wants to help cook, but she feels too small. Can we find special jobs just for her?"

Mama Otter smiled warmly. "What a thoughtful brother you are, Ollie! Of course we can."

Ollie gently took Lily's paw. "Come on! I know lots of important jobs you can do."

He showed Lily how to sort the clean river stones they used to keep their food fresh. "See? You're the perfect size to organize these!" The smooth stones clicked softly as Lily arranged them by color.

"And look!" Ollie pointed to some herbs that had fallen on the floor. "You can gather these up for Mama's soup. Your small paws are just right for picking up tiny pieces."

Lily's whiskers twitched with excitement as she carefully collected every little herb leaf. The kitchen filled with the fresh, green smell of the herbs.

"I have the most important job of all for you," said Ollie mysteriously. He pointed to their family's special wooden spoons. "These need to be cleaned and put away. Only someone very careful and gentle can do this job."

Lily's eyes sparkled as she polished each spoon until it gleamed. "I'm helping! I'm really helping!" she squeaked with joy.

"You certainly are," said Mama Otter, giving both her children warm hugs. "Ollie, thank you for using your caring heart to help your sister feel better. And Lily, you're doing such important work!"

As they all worked together, the kitchen filled with happy chatter and the wonderful smells of their lunch. Ollie felt proud and warm inside, knowing he had helped his sister feel good about herself.

"When we care about how others feel," said Mama Otter, "and when we talk about our feelings, we can solve problems together. Just like taking care of our river home, we need to take care of each other's hearts too."

Lily hugged Ollie tight. "Thank you for helping me feel big and important!"

Ollie hugged her back, his heart full of love. "We're a team, little sister. We're always a team."

The End.
