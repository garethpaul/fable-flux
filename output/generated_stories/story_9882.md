# Sunny's Treehouse Promise

Sunny was a friendly bear cub with soft, fuzzy ears and a round, warm belly. Sunny loved the cozy treehouse at the edge of the green forest. The treehouse smelled like pine needles and clean wood. The ladder was smooth, and the windows opened to the breeze. Birds sang, “cheep-cheep,” and leaves whispered, “shhh-shhh.”

Sunny clapped little paws. “I will have a busy day,” Sunny said. “I will invite friends to play.” Sunny made a big sign with a red crayon. It said, “Treehouse Play Time!” Sunny hummed a happy song.

Soon, friends came. Pip the squirrel wiggled his fluffy tail. Dot the chipmunk held shiny marbles. Lila the little owl blinked her wide, golden eyes.

“Welcome!” Sunny said, opening the door wide. “Please come in. Let’s have fun.”

Pip dashed to the shelf. “I want the drum!” he squeaked. He grabbed the drum and banged, bang-bang-bang! The loud sound bounced off the wooden walls.

Dot ran to the marble box. “I want all the marbles!” she chirped. She poured them out. Click-clack-clack! Marbles rolled everywhere.

Lila flapped to the reading nook and picked up a soft, blue book. She whispered, “I want quiet. My ears feel tired.”

Sunny felt a flutter in the tummy. The treehouse, once calm, was too noisy and messy now. The drum was loud. The marbles were everywhere. Lila looked sad and held the book close.

Sunny took a big, slow breath. The air smelled like pine and sunshine. Sunny remembered something Mama Bear said: “Respect means listening and caring. Respect means we share space and take turns.”

Sunny nodded. “Friends,” Sunny said in a calm voice, “we can all be happy if we show respect.”

Pip paused and held the drum. “Respect?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Sunny. “Respect means we listen to each other’s needs. It means we use gentle paws and gentle voices in the treehouse.”

Dot looked at the rolling marbles. “But I want to play right now,” she said.

Sunny smiled kindly. “We can all play. We will take turns. We will practice waiting. Practice helps us get better.”

Lila peeped softly. “I need a quiet corner,” she said. “My ears are small and soft.”

Sunny pointed to the reading nook. “This is our quiet space,” Sunny said. “We will keep it peaceful. That shows respect for Lila.”

Pip’s ears twitched. He tapped the drum softly. Thump-thump. “Is this okay?” he asked.

“Thank you for asking,” said Sunny. “Soft drum in the play area is okay. Loud drum is for outside time.”

Dot bent down and scooped marbles carefully. “I can share,” she said. “Click-click.” She put half the marbles in a little bowl and left the rest in the box. “We can practice taking turns with the marbles.”

Sunny clapped. “Great idea! Let’s make a plan.”

Sunny drew a simple chart on a piece of paper. The paper smelled like crayons. Sunny wrote names with big, round letters.

“Plan,” Sunny read. “First, Pip plays soft drum for two songs. Then Pip has a turn with marbles. Dot has marbles for two songs, then Dot reads a page with Lila. Lila reads two pages, then chooses a quiet puzzle.”

Pip tried the soft drum. Thump-thump. His tail relaxed. “I can do soft,” he said. “I am practicing.”

Dot rolled one marble down a little ramp. Click. “I am waiting my turn,” she said. “I am practicing.”

Lila smiled and read in a whisper. “The leaves go ‘shhh-shhh,’” she read. Her eyes looked bright. “Thank you for the quiet.”

Sunny felt warm and proud. The treehouse wasn’t messy now. It felt calm and happy again. The window let in sweet, green light. The friends played, and the sounds were gentle: thump, click, whisper.

Then a little problem came. Pip wanted one more drum song. Dot wanted one more marble roll. They both started to talk at the same time. “Me!” “Me!”

Sunny raised a paw. “Pause,” Sunny said. “Let’s use respectful words.”

Pip took a breath. “May I have one more soft drum song?” he asked.

Dot looked at the chart. “After Pip’s song,” she said, “it is my turn. May I roll the blue marble then?”

Sunny nodded. “Yes. Thank you for asking kindly. Thank you for waiting. That is respect.”

They followed the plan. Pip finished one soft song. Dot rolled the blue marble down the ramp. Lila turned a page and smiled.

At snack time, the friends sat in a circle. The wooden floor felt smooth under Sunny’s paws. The berries were sweet and juicy. “Mmm,” said Dot. “These are yummy.”

“Thank you for the quiet corner,” Lila said.

“Thank you for the turn,” Pip said.

Sunny looked around the treehouse. It was neat again. The air felt soft and happy.

“Today we learned something big,” Sunny said. “Respect means listening, sharing, and taking turns. We can practice every day. When we understand each other, we all feel good.”

Pip nodded. “I understand.”

Dot smiled. “I can practice.”

Lila blinked kindly. “I feel respected.”

Sunny hugged each friend with gentle arms. “Our treehouse is a place of respect,” Sunny said.

They all whispered together, “We listen. We share. We care.”

The friends waved goodbye as the sun made golden stripes on the floor. Sunny tucked away the drum, the marbles, and the book. The treehouse sighed a happy, quiet “shhh.”

Sunny smiled and whispered, “I will practice respect again tomorrow.”

The End.
