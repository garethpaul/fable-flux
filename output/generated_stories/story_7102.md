# Miko the Mouse Leads the Morning

Miko the mouse lived in a comfortable nursery. The room smelled like clean cotton and warm oatmeal. Sunlight peeked through soft curtains and made little golden squares on the carpet. Toys rested in baskets. Tiny chairs waited by a low table. A clock tick-tick-ticked like a gentle heartbeat.

Miko was a kind mouse with bright eyes and soft gray fur. He loved to help. He loved to share. But today, Miko wanted to try something new.

“I can do my morning by myself,” Miko whispered as he wiggled his whiskers. “I can be independent.”

He stretched his paws. He listened to the clock. Tick-tick. He took a deep breath. The air smelled like crayons and a hint of vanilla soap. His tummy felt fluttery, but in a warm, excited way.

First, Miko looked at the picture chart on the wall. It showed the morning steps: make bed, wash paws, dress, snack, and playtime. The pictures were bright and simple. Miko pointed with a tiny paw.

“I can do it,” he said.

He smoothed his tiny blanket. Swish, swish. He pulled the corners neat. He patted the pillow. Thump. “Bed, done!” he smiled.

Next, he padded to the sink on quiet mouse feet. Drip, drip, the faucet sang. Miko turned the handle carefully. He felt the cool water on his paws. Splash, splash. He rubbed soap until bubbles smelled like lemons. “Wash, done!” He giggled when a bubble popped on his nose. Pop!

In a little drawer, Miko found his red vest. He tried one button. It slipped away. “Oh!” said Miko. “Buttons are tricky.” He tried again, slower this time. “Pinch, poke, push,” he whispered. He used both paws together. Pinch, poke, push. Click! The button slid in. “I did it!” Miko cheered. His heart felt big and bright.

As Miko dressed, he heard friends waking up in the nursery. A soft voice came from a crib. “Miko, can you help?” It was Pip the plush bunny, blinking sleepy eyes.

Miko took a breath. “I am doing my morning by myself,” he said kindly. “But I can show you how.”

He walked to Pip and pointed to the picture chart. “Look, Pip. First bed, then wash, then dress.” His voice was gentle. He showed Pip how to smooth blankets. Swish, swish. Pip copied. Swish, swish.

“You’re a good leader,” Pip said. “You show, and I try.”

Miko’s whiskers twitched happily. He felt proud and calm. He wasn’t doing it for Pip. He was showing Pip how to do it too.

At the snack table, the oatmeal smelled sweet and cozy. Steam curled like tiny clouds. Miko found a small spoon and a little bowl. He held the spoon with one paw and steadied the bowl with the other. Scoop, scoop. Plop. He added blueberries—plip, plip. He lifted the spoon carefully. “Slow and steady,” he told himself. The spoon wobbled, but he kept his paws steady. He took a bite. Warm and yummy. “Snack, done,” he smiled.

Across the table, a baby bear toy named Toko looked sad. “I dropped my spoon,” Toko sniffled. The spoon lay on the floor, quiet and shiny.

Miko thought for a moment. “I can be independent and still share,” he said softly. He slid his extra spoon toward Toko. “Here you go. You can try again.” Toko’s smile came back like sunshine. “Thank you, Miko.”

After snack, it was playtime. The blocks were smooth and colorful. They smelled like wood and a little like the sunshine. Miko wanted to build a tall tower. He took a deep breath. “I can try this on my own,” he said. He set the big square block first. Then a long rectangle. Then a small cube. Tap, tap, tap, he pressed them gently so they would not wobble.

Pip and Toko watched. “Can we help?” Pip asked.

Miko nodded. “I will start, and you can follow my plan,” he said. “We can work together, but I will lead.”

He pointed to the blocks. “Big on bottom. Small on top. Slow and careful.” Pip placed a block. Toko placed a block. Miko placed a block. Their paws moved in turn. One, two, three. They watched the tower grow. Up, up, up!

The tower wiggled. “Oh no!” Toko gasped.

Miko stayed calm. “Freeze,” he said in a soft leader voice. Everyone held still. The tower rested. Miko slid one block to fix the balance. “There,” he said. “We can do hard things when we go slow and think.”

Finally, the tower was as tall as Miko’s ears. They all clapped. Clap, clap, clap! The sound echoed in the cozy room. The clock went tick-tick-tick. The sunlight made the blocks glow.

Miss Lila, the gentle caretaker, peeked in. “What a wonderful morning,” she said. “Miko, I see you made your bed, washed, dressed, served your snack, and led your friends. How did it feel?”

Miko stood tall in his red vest. “I did many things by myself,” he said. “I used my paws and my plan. I shared and I led. It felt brave and good.”

Miss Lila smiled. “That is independence,” she said. “You can try first. You can ask for help if you need it. You can show others how too.”

Miko’s heart felt warm like oatmeal. “I can be independent,” he said. “I can use my own paws, my own plan, and my kind mouse heart.”

They took the tower apart together and put the blocks back in the basket. Rustle, rustle. Sharing the work felt nice. The nursery hummed with happy sounds.

Lesson: Try to do things by yourself first. Go slow. Use both hands. Be a kind leader. Share your tools and your time. You can be brave and independent every day.

The End.
