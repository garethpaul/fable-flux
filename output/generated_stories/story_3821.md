# Rocco's Big Baking Day

Rocco the raccoon loved music more than anything! He had tiny cymbals on his paws that went *clink-clink* when he walked. Today, he wanted to make special musical muffins for his forest friends.

"I need to get ready first," Rocco said to himself, standing in Grandma Bear's big pantry. The shelves were tall and full of colorful jars and boxes. Everything smelled like cinnamon and vanilla.

Rocco's whiskers twitched with excitement. "But where do I start?" The pantry seemed so big and full of so many things!

He took a deep breath and remembered what Grandma Bear always said: "When you prepare well, everything goes smoothly."

"First, I need to find my recipe," Rocco said calmly. He looked around carefully and spotted a red cookbook on the bottom shelf. *Clink-clink* went his cymbals as he picked it up.

The recipe said he needed flour, eggs, milk, and blueberries. "I'll gather everything before I start cooking," Rocco decided. "That's being responsible!"

Rocco looked up at the tall shelves. The flour was way up high in a white container. "How can I reach that?" he wondered. Then he noticed a sturdy step stool tucked behind some boxes.

*Clink-clink, clink-clink!* His cymbals made happy sounds as he carefully moved the step stool. "Safety first!" he said, making sure it was steady before climbing up.

The flour container was heavy. Rocco held it with both paws and climbed down slowly, one step at a time. "Slow and careful," he whispered to himself.

Next, he found fresh eggs in a basket. They felt smooth and cool in his paws. He counted them carefully: "One, two, three eggs!" The milk was in the cold box, white and creamy.

"Now for the blueberries!" Rocco said excitedly. He found a basket of plump, purple berries that smelled sweet and fresh. His tummy rumbled just thinking about them.

Rocco arranged everything on the counter in a neat row. "Flour, eggs, milk, blueberries," he counted. "I have everything I need!"

Just then, Grandma Bear walked into the kitchen. "Oh my, Rocco! You've prepared everything so nicely," she said with a warm smile. "You were very responsible getting ready first."

"I wanted to make musical muffins for my friends," Rocco explained, his cymbals making soft *clink* sounds. "I remembered to prepare everything before starting."

"That's wonderful thinking," Grandma Bear said. "When we prepare well, cooking becomes much easier and more fun. Would you like me to help you bake them?"

"Yes, please!" Rocco said happily.

Together, they mixed the ingredients. Rocco stirred the batter while humming a little tune. *Clink-clink* went his cymbals in rhythm with his song. The kitchen filled with the sweet smell of baking muffins.

When the muffins were done, they looked golden and perfect, with blueberries peeking out like little jewels.

"These will make my friends so happy," Rocco said proudly. "And I learned that preparing first makes everything work better!"

Grandma Bear hugged him gently. "You showed great curiosity, responsibility, and calmness today, Rocco. Your friends are lucky to have such a thoughtful raccoon."

Rocco's cymbals chimed softly as he smiled. He had learned that taking time to prepare made his adventure successful and fun.

The End.
