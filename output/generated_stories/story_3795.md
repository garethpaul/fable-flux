# Rooftop Waddle Dance

Pip was a cheerful penguin with shiny black feathers and a bright orange beak. One sunny morning, Pip waddled up the building’s safe, flat rooftop. The sky was big and blue. A gentle breeze tickled Pip’s cheeks. Far below, the city hummed like a soft drum.

“This is a good place to dance,” Pip chirped. “Tap-tap, flap-flap, wiggle-waddle!”

Pip tapped his little feet on the warm rooftop tiles. Tap-tap! He flapped his wings. Flap-flap! He wiggled his belly. Wiggle-waddle! The dance made a happy sound, like tiny drums and wind whooshes.

Soon, Pip’s friends arrived. There was Mia the mouse with pink ears, Bop the bunny with bouncy feet, and Coco the cat with a swishy tail.

“What’s that fun sound?” asked Mia.

“It’s my Rooftop Waddle Dance!” said Pip, smiling. “Do you want to try?”

“Yes!” said Bop. “I want a turn!”

“Me too!” purred Coco.

Pip clapped his little wings. “We can all dance. But we must take turns. Taking turns keeps our bodies safe and our hearts happy.”

Mia nodded. “Taking turns is kind.”

Pip drew a little dancing line with a piece of chalk. “We’ll go one by one. First, we watch and listen. Then we dance.”

Pip went first. “Tap-tap, flap-flap, wiggle-waddle,” he sang softly. His feet made tiny tick-tick sounds. The wind made a gentle shhhh. Pip smiled. He bowed. “Your turn, Mia!”

Mia stepped up. Her small feet went tippy-tippy on the rooftop. “I’m a little shy,” she squeaked.

“That’s okay,” said Pip. “We practice. We go slow. Practice helps our bodies learn.”

Mia breathed in. She tapped once. Tap. She flapped her tiny paws. Flap. She wiggled her whiskers. Wiggle! She giggled. “I did it!”

“Great job!” cheered Pip. “You were patient and brave.”

Bop the bunny hopped to the line. “Watch this!” He hopped too fast—boing, boing, boing—almost bumping Coco.

Pip held up a wing. “Let’s pause. We must be careful. We wait for space. We keep our friends safe.”

Bop froze, his ears standing tall. “Oops! I forgot to wait.”

“That’s okay,” said Pip kindly. “We can try again. Slow feet, soft hops.”

Bop tried again. Boing… boing… boing. Soft and gentle. “I did it slow,” he said, smiling.

Coco swished her tail. “I want my turn, but I’m not good at flapping.”

Pip stepped closer. The rooftop tiles felt warm under their toes. “We can help each other,” he said. “I will show you one small step at a time.”

Pip showed a simple move. “Tap-tap.” Coco copied. Tap-tap. “Flap-flap,” Pip said softly. Coco lifted her paws. Flap-flap. “Wiggle-waddle!” Pip giggled. Coco wiggled her tail. Wiggle-waddle! Coco purred. “That felt nice.”

They went around again. Pip made a new sound, “Ta-ta-ta,” like tiny rain. Mia added a whisper, “Shhhh,” like the wind. Bop added a soft thump with his big feet, “Thump-thump.” Coco added a swish, “Swish-swish.”

“It sounds like rooftop music!” Pip said. The friends smiled. Their hearts felt warm and light.

A cloud slid over the sun. The air grew cooler. Mia shivered. “I’m a little cold,” she said.

Pip nodded. “Let’s take a break. We can wait for the sun. Patience helps us stay calm.”

They sat in a friendly circle. Pip shared sips of cool water from a little bottle he carried. Coco helped by holding the bottle steady with her paws. Bop used his ears to fan Mia gently. “Thank you,” Mia said. “That was helpful.”

When the sun peeked out again, Pip stood up. “Who wants to try a new step?”

“Me!” said Bop. He waited at the line. He looked left. He looked right. He checked for space. Then he hopped soft and slow. Boing… boing… boing. He finished with a bow. Everyone clapped.

“Your turn, Coco,” said Pip.

Coco waited until Bop was clear. She tapped and flapped and made a smooth tail swish. Swish! She finished with a smile. “I felt calm because I waited,” she purred.

Mia stepped up. “I will try the wiggle longer.” She wiggled and giggled and made a tiny tap at the end. “I practiced,” she said proudly. “Practice makes me stronger.”

Pip danced last. He listened to his friends’ sounds and added them together. “Tap-tap, shhhh, thump-thump, swish!” went their rooftop band. The breeze kissed their faces. The tiles felt warm. The city hummed below. Pip felt grateful.

Pip bowed to his friends. “We danced with taking turns. We practiced. We were patient. We helped each other. That is the Rooftop Waddle Way!”

Mia clapped. “I learned that waiting my turn keeps everyone safe.”

Bop nodded. “I learned that going slow helps me do it right.”

Coco purred. “I learned that helping friends makes dancing more fun.”

Pip smiled his biggest smile. “Dance is for everyone. When we wait, practice, and help, our dance shines.”

They did one last gentle circle dance together, hands and paws and wings moving in time. Tap-tap, flap-flap, wiggle-waddle, swish. Their hearts felt happy and kind.

The End.
