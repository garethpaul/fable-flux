# Daisy's Musical Discovery

Daisy the deer fawn sat quietly in the old attic. Her spotted coat looked soft and brown in the gentle sunlight. She had wandered up here while exploring the big house. Now she felt a little scared and very unsure of herself.

"I don't know how to get down," Daisy whispered. Her voice was so quiet, like a tiny bell. The wooden stairs looked very steep and scary to her little legs.

Daisy's ears drooped sadly. She felt her heart beating fast. "I'm too little," she said to herself. "I can't do anything right."

As she looked around the dusty attic, something caught her eye. In the corner sat an old piano. Its keys were yellow and some were chipped. But it looked magical in the warm sunlight streaming through the window.

Daisy walked closer on her delicate hooves. The floorboards creaked softly under her feet. She could smell the old wood and see tiny dust sparkles dancing in the air.

"I wonder what this does," she said curiously. Daisy gently pressed one white key with her small hoof. DING! A beautiful sound filled the quiet attic.

"Oh my!" Daisy gasped. Her eyes grew wide with wonder. She pressed another key. DONG! Then another. DING!

The pretty sounds made Daisy feel warm and happy inside. Her heart wasn't beating so fast anymore. She pressed more keys, making a simple melody. The music floated around the attic like friendly butterflies.

"I can make music!" Daisy said with surprise. "I didn't know I could do that!"

She played more notes, getting braver with each sound. Some were high and tinkly. Others were low and rumbly. Daisy giggled as she discovered each new sound.

Soon, Daisy was playing a cheerful tune. She felt proud and confident. "I AM good at something," she said with a big smile. "Making music makes me feel strong!"

The beautiful sounds drifted down through the house. Mrs. Robin, who lived in the garden, heard the lovely melody. She flew up to the attic window.

"What beautiful music!" chirped Mrs. Robin. "Who's playing?"

"It's me, Daisy!" said the little fawn proudly. "I found this piano and I can make it sing!"

"You're very talented," said Mrs. Robin kindly. "Would you like help getting downstairs? I can show you the way."

Daisy felt brave now. The music had shown her she could do special things. "Yes, please," she said confidently. "I think I can do it if you help me."

Mrs. Robin guided Daisy down the stairs step by step. "Slow and steady," she encouraged. "You're doing great!"

Daisy carefully placed each hoof on the wooden steps. She felt proud of herself for being responsible and asking for help when she needed it.

When they reached the bottom, Daisy looked back up at the attic. "Thank you, Mrs. Robin," she said. "And thank you, piano, for teaching me I can do wonderful things!"

From that day on, Daisy visited the attic piano often. She learned that everyone has special talents inside them. Sometimes you just need to be brave enough to try new things to discover what makes you special.

Daisy learned that feeling scared was okay, but she shouldn't let fear stop her from trying. Music had helped her find her confidence, and now she felt ready for any adventure.

The End.
