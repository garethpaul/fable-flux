# Daisy's Safe Dining Room Adventure

Little Daisy the deer fawn loved exploring Mama's cozy dining room. The wooden table gleamed in the morning sunlight, and pretty flowers sat in a blue vase.

"I want to help set the table for breakfast!" Daisy said, bouncing on her tiny hooves.

Mama Deer smiled. "That's very helpful, sweetie. But remember our safety rules first."

Daisy nodded eagerly. "Walk slowly, no running!"

"That's right," Mama said. "And what else?"

"Um..." Daisy thought hard, tapping her hoof. "Keep my hooves on the ground?"

"Good! No jumping on chairs. And?"

Daisy's ears perked up. "Ask for help with breakable things!"

"Perfect! Now you can help me."

Daisy felt so proud. She walked carefully to the wooden chairs, her hooves making soft *clip-clop* sounds on the floor. The morning air smelled like warm pancakes from the kitchen.

"I'll get the napkins!" Daisy said. She reached up high for the pretty cloth napkins on the shelf.

*Stretch, stretch, stretch!*

"Almost got it..." Daisy stood on her back hooves, wobbling.

*CRASH!*

Oh no! Daisy had knocked over a small picture frame. It fell to the floor with a loud bang.

"Daisy!" Mama called, rushing in. "Are you hurt?"

Daisy's eyes filled with tears. "I'm sorry, Mama! I forgot the safety rule. I didn't ask for help."

Mama knelt down and hugged her little fawn. "Are you okay? That's what matters most."

"I'm not hurt," Daisy sniffled. "But I broke your pretty frame."

Mama picked up the frame. "Look, it's not broken! Just the glass fell out. We can fix it together."

Daisy wiped her eyes. "Really?"

"Really. But what should you do differently next time?"

Daisy thought carefully. "Ask for help when things are too high?"

"Exactly! Safety first, always."

Daisy felt better. "Can I still help with breakfast?"

"Of course! Let's make a plan together."

Mama and Daisy sat at the table. The wooden surface felt smooth and cool under Daisy's hooves.

"First, I'll get the napkins for you," Mama said. "What can you do safely?"

Daisy's tail wiggled with excitement. "I can put the napkins by each plate! And I can count them - one, two, three, four!"

"Wonderful! And?"

"I can put the forks on the left side, like you taught me!"

Mama smiled. "That's my helpful little fawn."

Together, they set the beautiful table. Daisy walked slowly and carefully, remembering all the safety rules. She felt the soft napkins between her hooves and listened to the gentle *clink* of forks on plates.

"Look, Mama! We did it together!" Daisy said proudly.

The table looked perfect. Sunshine streamed through the window, making everything sparkle. The blue vase of flowers smelled sweet and fresh.

"You were very helpful, Daisy. And you remembered to be safe."

"I made a mistake," Daisy said thoughtfully. "But I learned something new!"

"What did you learn?"

Daisy stood tall. "When I ask for help, I can still be helpful! And saying sorry when I make mistakes is important."

"That's right! Tomorrow, would you like to help again?"

"Yes! And I'll remember all the safety rules. That's my goal!"

Mama gave Daisy a big hug. "I'm proud of you for learning and improving."

As they sat down for breakfast, Daisy felt happy and safe. The dining room felt warm and cozy, filled with love and the sweet smell of pancakes.

From that day on, Daisy always remembered: being helpful means being safe first, asking for help when needed, and learning from mistakes makes you better every day.

The End.
