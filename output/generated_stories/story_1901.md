# Gigi the Giraffe and the Big Bathroom Build

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with long legs and a gentle smile. One sunny morning, she tip-tapped into the bathroom. The tiles felt cool on her hooves. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet. Minty soap made the room smell fresh.

Gigi peered into the mirror. “Hello, me,” she said softly. “Today, I want to learn and help.”

She looked up. Her very long neck reached higher than the shower curtain. “Hmm,” she hummed. “The curtain is too short for me. Water splashes out.” She saw tiny puddles on the floor. Splish, splash.

Gigi blinked and took a deep breath. “I can speak up. I can ask for help.” Her heart felt brave. She called, “Friends, can you come?”

Clara the clever crab clacked in with tiny, careful steps. “Click-click! I’m good with clips,” Clara said.

Benji the busy beaver waddled in, tail thumping softly. Thump, thump. “I can build,” said Benji. “I love to make things strong.”

Pip the polite parrot fluttered to the towel bar. “Squawk—teamwork time!” Pip sang. His feathers brushed Gigi’s nose. It tickled!

Gigi smiled. “Thank you for coming. The shower curtain is too short for me, and the water splashes. I need a taller curtain. I want to stay safe and dry.”

“That’s good self-advocacy,” Pip chirped. “You said what you need.”

Clara tapped the curtain rings. “These are small. We can add more rings and clips.”

Benji looked at the wall. “We can build a little step for us to reach. And we can add a taller rod.”

Gigi nodded. “Let’s make a plan. Everyone has a job.”

They washed their hooves, claws, and wings first. Swish, swish. They sang a handwashing song. “Wet, soap, scrub, rinse, dry!” The warm water felt nice. The fluffy towels were soft on their skin and feathers.

“Teamwork,” Gigi said. “We work together and listen.”

“My job,” said Clara, “is to clip the rings.” She clicked and clacked. “Click, click, click!” The sound was sharp and happy.

“My job,” said Benji, “is to build a little wooden step.” He gnawed, nibble-nibble, and stacked smooth pieces. Tap, tap went his tiny hammer. The wood smelled sweet and clean.

“My job,” said Pip, “is to hold the screws and count. One… two… three… four!” Pip’s voice was bright.

“And my job,” said Gigi, “is to steady the rod with my long neck and keep the tools in a safe basket.”

They started. Gigi held the rod steady. “I am tall,” she said proudly. “I can help reach.”

Benji climbed onto the step he built. “Safe and strong,” he said. Clara clicked the new rings on the tall rod. Pip passed screws and cheered, “You can do it!”

The faucet went drip, drip, drip. The minty soap smelled like a fresh breeze. The window let in warm light. Everyone worked. Everyone listened.

After a while, Pip tilted his head. “Break time!” he chirped. “Our bodies need fuel.”

Gigi brought a small snack. She set it on a clean towel. “We can try crunchy carrots, sweet apple slices, and cool water.” The carrots went crunch! The apples were juicy. Slurp, slurp went the water.

“Good nutrition helps us work and think,” Gigi said. Her tummy felt happy and strong.

Back to the build they went. Benji tightened the last screw. Clara clipped the last ring. Pip fluffed the new, long curtain. “Ta-da!” he sang.

Gigi stepped into the tub and pulled the curtain closed. Swish! The fabric was soft and whispery. “I will test it,” she said. She turned on the shower. Whoosh! Warm water rained down. Gigi giggled as the drops pattered on her back. Pat-pat-pat.

She peeked out. The floor was dry. No splish, no splash. “It works!” Gigi beamed. Her heart felt light and proud. “Thank you, team.”

Benji puffed his chest. “We built it together.”

Clara clicked her claws. “We used our skills.”

Pip nodded. “We listened, took turns, and asked for what we needed.”

Gigi wrapped in a soft, warm towel. “I learned that when I speak up, my friends can help. I learned that healthy snacks give energy. I learned that building takes planning and care. And I learned that teamwork makes big jobs feel small.”

They cleaned up the tools and put them back in the basket. The bathroom was neat. The air was warm and steamy and smelled like mint and wood.

Gigi looked around with her thoughtful eyes. “Friends,” she said, “thank you for helping me make the bathroom safe.”

“Anytime,” said Benji.

“Always,” said Clara.

“Teamwork forever!” sang Pip.

Gigi smiled and nodded. “Teamwork means we share jobs, listen, and help. When we do, everyone feels safe and strong.”

They all gave a gentle cheer. “Hooray for teamwork!”

The End.
