# Benny Beaver’s Backyard Game Plan

Benny was a busy beaver with bright eyes and a big, flat tail. He lived by a sunny backyard where the grass smelled sweet, like fresh apples, and the birds sang cheer-up songs in the trees. The warm sun made the fence glow like honey. Benny loved to work, to build, and to play.

One morning, Benny heard giggles and thumps. Thump-thump! Tap-tap! His friends were in the sunny backyard. Mia the mouse bounced a soft, red ball. Zuzu the zebra stretched her legs. Pip the puppy wagged his tail so fast it swished the daisies.

“Let’s play a sports day!” Mia squeaked.

“I can run fast,” Zuzu said proudly.

“I can fetch!” Pip barked.

Benny smiled. “I can think of a plan,” he said. “I am a busy beaver who thinks.”

“What game first?” asked Mia.

Benny scratched his chin. He took a breath. He looked around. He listened to the birds and felt a soft breeze on his whiskers. “We want a game everyone can play. Let’s use the red ball. Let’s make a goal at the garden gate,” he said. “We need space on the grass. We should move the watering can and the bucket, so no one trips.”

“That’s smart,” Zuzu said. “You saw the bucket and thought ahead.”

They all helped. Clink, clank, swish! The yard was clear. The sun warmed their backs, and the grass tickled their toes.

Benny drew a line with a stick. “Here is the start,” he said. “We will roll the ball to the gate and touch the fence with our tail or hand. No pushing. We take turns.”

Pip’s ears perked up. “Can we run fast?” he asked.

“We can run, but we need control,” Benny said calmly. “If we go too fast, we might miss the goal.”

Mia looked worried. “I am small. What if I can’t roll far?”

Benny nodded kindly. “You can do it. We will make the path small for you,” he said. “We can use leaves to make lanes. We make a small lane for Mia. That is fair.”

Zuzu shifted her hooves. “But I want to run long!”

Benny smiled. “You can run in a big lane. We are all different. We can make a plan that fits.”

Mia’s whiskers twitched. “Thank you, Benny.”

They began. Zuzu went first, clip-clop, clip-clop, rolling the ball with her nose. She giggled and touched the fence with her striped leg. “Goal!”

Pip went next. Patter-patter-patter! His paws drummed. He rolled too fast and whoosh—the ball slipped past the gate. Pip looked down. “I missed,” he said softly.

Benny patted Pip. “Try again. Think step by step,” Benny said. “First, eyes on the ball. Second, small pushes. Third, aim for the gate.”

Pip tried again. Push, push, pause. The ball rolled smooth. Tap! He touched the fence with his paw. “Goal!” Pip barked, tail wag-wag-wagging.

Now it was Mia’s turn. Her small paws trembled. “I’m scared,” she whispered.

Benny looked at her kindly. “You can say what you need,” he said.

Mia took a brave breath. “I need more time,” she told the group. “Please wait for me.”

“We will wait,” Zuzu said.

Mia pushed the ball gently along her little leaf lane. Rustle, roll, rustle. The ball wobbled. She paused. “Think, think,” she said. “Small pushes. Aim for the gate.” She rolled again. Tap! She touched the fence with her tiny hand. “Goal!” Her eyes sparkled.

Benny’s turn came last. He liked to help more than to play, but he wanted to try. He lined up. He felt the sun warm his fur. He listened to the birds. He smelled the flower bed.

“Go, Benny!” his friends cheered.

Benny rolled the ball with little tail taps. Tap, tap, tap. The ball went straight. But then—oh!—a sprinkler clicked on. Click-click! Water sprayed, shhhh, shhhh, making the grass slippery.

Benny slipped and plopped onto the soft ground. Splat! His tail went splash in a puddle. He felt sad for a moment. “Oh no,” he said.

Pip ran over. “Are you okay?”

Benny sat up and took another breath. He put his paw on his chest. “Yes,” he said slowly. “I can try again. I will think of a new plan.”

“What’s the plan?” Zuzu asked.

Benny looked at the sprinkler. “The grass is wet. Wet grass is slick. I should slow down. I can use my tail like a brake, and I can roll the ball around the wet spot.”

Mia nodded. “Smart thinking.”

Benny stood, steady and calm. He nudged the ball to the dry side. Tap, pause, tap. The ball moved in a curvy path. He used his tail to slow it when the ground looked shiny. Finally, the red ball reached the gate. Tap! Benny touched the fence and laughed. “Goal!”

They cheered and hugged. The sun felt warm again. The spray felt cool on their cheeks, like tiny kisses of rain.

Benny gathered his friends. “We used our minds,” he said. “We looked, we planned, we tried, and we tried again. We spoke up for what we needed. We moved things to be safe. We made fair lanes so everyone could play.”

Mia smiled. “Thinking step by step helped me.”

Pip wagged. “Changing the plan helped me.”

Zuzu nodded. “Waiting and listening helped me.”

Benny beamed. “That is logical thinking. We look, we think, we do. And sports are more fun when we help each other.”

They packed up the ball and put the bucket and watering can back in a safe spot. The birds sang a soft good-bye song. The sweet grass smell filled the sunny backyard. Benny felt proud and peaceful.

“Tomorrow,” Benny said, “we can plan a new game.”

“Yes!” everyone cheered.

The End.
