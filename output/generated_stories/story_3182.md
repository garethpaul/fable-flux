# Miko Speaks Up in the Pantry

Miko was a small, courageous monkey with bright eyes and warm brown fur. One sunny afternoon, Miko tiptoed into the pantry. The pantry smelled like ripe bananas, cozy bread, and a tiny hint of cinnamon. The floor felt cool under Miko’s feet. Shelves stood tall, like quiet trees, holding jars and boxes and baskets.

Miko’s tummy gave a soft rumble. “Bananas,” Miko whispered. “I would like a banana, please.”

But the bananas were on the top shelf, way up high. Miko stood on tiptoe. Stretch, stretch! Still too far. Miko shook a little. “I can do this,” Miko told himself. “I can use my brave voice and ask for help.”

Just then, Miko heard a flutter and a squeak. Pip the parrot and Tia the tiny turtle were in the pantry too. Pip flapped his green wings. “Banana party!” he squawked. Tia blinked slowly and smiled. “I like berries,” she said softly.

“Hi, friends,” said Miko. “I want a banana. They are up high. I need help, please.”

Pip tilted his head. “I can fly!” he chirped. “I will get it first.” He zoomed up and tugged a bunch. Plop! Three bananas fell onto a clean cloth. One rolled toward the edge.

Miko reached out fast. He caught the rolling banana. “Got it!” he said. The banana felt smooth and cool in his hands.

Tia looked up. “May I have a turn?” she asked. “I move slow.”

Pip puffed his chest. “I want a big one,” he said. He grabbed the longest banana and tucked it under his wing.

Miko took a deep breath. He felt a flutter in his tummy. He wanted to be fair. He also wanted to be kind to the pantry and not make a mess. Miko remembered a song from school: “Use your words. Use them kind. Share the space. Care for your mind.”

Miko stood tall. “Friends,” he said in a clear, gentle voice, “I want to talk. I have something important to say.”

Pip blinked. Tia lifted her head.

Miko said, “I want a banana, too. I asked for help. I feel worried when we rush and grab. Can we take turns? Can we choose gently so we don’t drop food?”

Pip ruffled his feathers. “You feel worried?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Miko, nodding. “I feel worried when there is a mess. The pantry is our place. We should care for it. We can pick up, and we can not waste. That makes me feel calm.”

Tia smiled. “Thank you for telling us,” she said. “I want a small banana, please.”

Pip looked at the bananas. He looked at Miko. “I got excited,” he admitted. “I forgot to listen. Let’s take turns.” He set the big banana back down. “Miko, you asked first. You choose first.”

Miko felt warm inside. He peeled a banana carefully. The peel made a soft rip sound. The fruit smelled sweet. “I will choose this one,” he said. “And I will save the peel for the compost, so we help the earth.”

“Com-post?” Pip repeated.

“It means we put peels in a special bin,” Miko explained. “Then the peels can turn into good dirt for plants.”

“Oooh,” said Tia. “Good for the earth.”

They each chose a banana. Miko showed how to peel slowly so it didn’t splatter. Pip and Tia watched. “Peel, peel, peel,” sang Pip softly. They each took small bites. “Mmm,” said Miko. “Sweet and soft.”

Suddenly, they heard a tiny tap-tap. A drip of water came from a leaky bottle on a lower shelf. Drip. Drip. Drip. A small puddle was forming.

Miko pointed. “Water is dripping,” he said. “We need to fix it.” He walked closer and felt a cool splash on his toes. “Cold,” he giggled.

Pip hopped down. Tia scooted over. Miko said, “Let’s use kind words and make a plan. I can hold the bottle. Pip, can you twist the cap? Tia, can you bring a cloth?”

“Yes,” said Pip. “Yes,” said Tia.

Together, they moved gently. Miko held the bottle steady. Pip twisted, twist, twist, until the cap was snug. Tia pushed a soft cloth with her little feet. Swish, swish. They wiped up the puddle. The pantry smelled fresh again.

Miko clapped softly. “We did it! We cared for the pantry. We cared for the earth by not wasting water.”

Pip bowed. “And we used words,” he said. “We took turns.”

Tia nodded. “We listened,” she added.

Miko felt brave and calm. “I am proud I spoke up,” he said. “I said what I needed. I asked for help. I used a gentle voice.”

Pip smiled. “Your brave voice helped me slow down,” he said.

Tia touched Miko’s hand. “Your words helped us all,” she whispered.

They put the banana peels in the compost bin. They tucked the cloth to dry. They looked at the neat shelves. Miko closed his eyes and imagined the pantry as a tiny forest. The jars were tree trunks. The boxes were rocks. The baskets were nests. In his mind, they were forest helpers, keeping the forest clean and fair.

“Thank you, friends,” said Miko. “Today we learned to speak up, share, and care.”

“Thank you, Miko,” said Pip and Tia together.

They left the pantry smiling, bellies full and hearts happy.

The End.
