# Pippa the Pantry Parrot

Pippa was a bright, creative parrot with feathers like paint—red, blue, green, and gold. She lived near a busy kitchen. One day, she fluttered into the pantry. It smelled like warm bread and sweet cinnamon. Shelves stood tall with jars and boxes. Crackle, rustle, crinkle—the packages made little sounds.

“Hello, pantry!” Pippa chirped. “I feel a big idea coming!”

She hopped from a low shelf to a higher one. Tap-tap with her claws, swish-swish with her wings. She loved to move and explore. Her eyes sparkled with curiosity.

On the middle shelf sat a big jar of oats, a tiny tin of tea, and a shiny can of beans. Beside them was a box of pasta, shaped like little wheels. A bag of rice rustled when Pippa bumped it.

Then Pippa saw it: a banana hung from a hook, just out of reach.

“Oh! I want a banana snack,” she said. Her tummy gave a small, polite grumble. “I can get it. I have a plan!”

Pippa flapped up, but the hook was high, and the air felt cool on her feathers. She tried again. Flap-flap—almost! She missed and bumped a box. Thump. The pasta box slid and tinkled. A little pasta wheel rolled past her claws.

Pippa felt a hot puff of anger in her chest. Her feathers ruffled. “Squawk!” she said. “I can’t reach!”

She stomped her feet. Stomp-stomp. She flapped hard. Flap-FLAP-FLAP!

The can of beans wobbled. Wobble-wobble—CLUNK! It rolled to the edge.

Pippa froze. “Uh-oh,” she whispered. Her heart beat fast—thump-thump. She took a slow breath in. She let it out, long and soft. “Hooo.”

“I feel mad,” Pippa said, naming her feeling. “Mad because I can’t reach. But I can calm my body. I can think. I am a creative parrot.”

She shook out her wings—shake, shake. She wiggled her toes—wiggle, wiggle. She took two big breaths. In… out. In… out. The hot puff of anger turned into a warm, steady feeling.

“Okay,” said Pippa. “Time to use my brain.” She looked at the things around her. “What do I know? Heavy things don’t tip easily. Flat things can make a step. Round things roll.”

She tapped the pasta box. “Round wheels roll. Not safe.” She nudged the bean can. “Round again. Nope.” She patted the oat jar. “Too heavy.” Her eyes landed on a stack of flat cloth napkins. “Aha! Flat!”

Pippa pulled one napkin to the floor. Swoosh. She pecked at a sturdy wooden spoon lying nearby. “Strong and long.” She slid the spoon under the napkin to make a ramp. Then she pecked a small, empty crate closer. Scrape, scrape. She pushed with her shoulder. “I can move my body to help my mind!”

Step by step, Pippa made a little ladder: crate on the bottom, then the flat napkin to keep it from sliding, then the wooden spoon like a bridge to a low shelf.

She tested it with her claw. Tap. “Steady,” she said. She climbed up. Step, breathe. Step, breathe. “I can do hard things.”

From the spoon, she was closer to the banana. But it was still a tiny bit high. She looked around again. “I need one more safe step.” She found a thick cookbook on the lowest shelf. “A book is flat and strong.”

Pippa tugged the book gently. Thump. She set it on the spoon bridge. She climbed onto the book. “Almost!”

Now Pippa was near the hook. She stretched her neck. She reached with her beak. “Careful,” she whispered. She tapped the banana’s stem. Tap-tap. The stem snapped with a soft pop. The banana swung into her claws.

“I did it!” Pippa laughed. “Thank you, brain. Thank you, body.”

She slid down safely, step by careful step. Swoosh to the napkin, step to the crate, hop to the floor. She peeled the banana. The sweet smell made her smile. She took a tiny bite. Mmm—soft and creamy.

Just then, she heard a voice. It was Chef Ana at the pantry door. “Pippa! What a busy bird. I heard a wobble. Are you okay?”

“I’m okay,” Pippa said. “I felt mad. I took breaths. I used my body to calm down. I looked, I thought, and I built a safe way to reach. I was creative!”

Chef Ana smiled. “That is smart and safe. You used knowledge and kindness to yourself. Will you help me put things back neat?”

“Yes!” said Pippa. She moved with care. She picked up the pasta wheel and put it back in the box. Click. She steadied the bean can. She folded the napkin. She slid the spoon into its drawer. The pantry looked tidy again.

Chef Ana clapped softly. “Problem solved. You turned a big feeling into a smart plan.”

Pippa fluffed her feathers with pride. “When I feel mad, I can stop, breathe, move, and think. I can be creative and safe.”

She finished her banana and did a happy wing wiggle. Swish-swish, tap-tap. “I learned that my body can help my brain. My brain can help my body. And I can use things around me in new ways.”

Chef Ana nodded. “That is the best kind of knowledge.”

Pippa chirped, “I am Pippa the Pantry Parrot, and I make good ideas!”

The pantry smelled sweet and felt calm. Pippa hummed a gentle tune as she flew to the door, ready for her next bright idea.

The End.
