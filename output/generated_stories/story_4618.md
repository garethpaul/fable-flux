# Benny Bunny Checks the Boxes

Morning light peeked through the soft curtains in the comfortable nursery. The room smelled like fresh crayons and warm soap. Pillows were puffy. Shelves were neat and low. A little chart with big boxes hung on the wall. The chart said: “Benny’s To-Do!”

Benny Bunny bounced in, ears high. “Good morning!” he chirped.

Miss Dot, their gentle teacher, smiled. “Good morning, Benny. Today, you can be the Task Captain.”

Benny’s whiskers twitched. “Task Captain?” His heart did a happy thump. “I can do it!”

Miss Dot pointed to the chart. It had pictures and simple words:
1. Put away backpack
2. Wash paws
3. Snack time clean-up
4. Story rug ready
5. Goodbye tidy-up

Benny stared at all the boxes. “That looks like a lot,” he whispered. His tummy felt fluttery, like tiny butterflies. He rubbed his paw on his soft sweater. “I want to finish. But it feels big.”

Miss Dot nodded kindly. “One small step at a time,” she said. “Small steps finish big jobs.”

Benny took a deep breath. He liked the warm smell of the room. He liked the sunny dots on the floor. “Okay. Step one.”

He put away his backpack. The zipper made a zing sound. He set his lunchbox on the shelf. He pressed a sticker on the first box. “Click!” it stuck. Benny grinned. “I did it!”

“Great start,” said Miss Dot.

Benny hopped to the sink. The water was cool and tickly. He rubbed bubble soap until his paws felt squeaky and clean. “Squeak, squeak,” he giggled. He dried with a fluffy towel. He pressed another sticker. “Two done!”

His friend Tilly Tortoise peeked up from a coloring page. “Benny, can I come wash too?”

“Sure!” Benny said. “We can take turns.” He held the towel for Tilly. “All clean!”

Snack time came. Crackers crunched. Apples smelled sweet. Juice made tiny bubbles pop. After they ate, crumbs sprinkled the table. Benny looked at the chart. “Snack time clean-up is next.”

He felt the flutter again. “There are crumbs everywhere,” he said softly. “It looks like a big job.”

Tilly blinked. “Maybe we can make a plan,” she said.

Benny’s ears perked. “A plan! Good idea.” He tapped his chin. “I can wipe the table. You can bring the little bin. Then we can sweep together.”

Tilly smiled. “Team plan!”

Benny squeezed the spray bottle. Pssst! The cloth felt cool and damp. Circles, circles, circles—wipe, wipe, wipe. Tilly held the bin steady. Tap, tap, tap went the crumbs. Benny hummed a little tune, “Small steps, small steps.” When they finished, the table shined.

Benny pressed the third sticker. “Three! We did it,” he cheered. His chest felt warm and proud.

Next was “Story rug ready.” Benny looked at the rug. Pillows were scattered like clouds. A blanket was half rolled, half squished. A tiny car hid under the green cushion.

“I will sort,” Benny said. “Soft things on the rug. Toys on the shelf.” He used his gentle voice to talk to himself. “Soft here, toys there.” He shook the blanket. It whooshed like a tiny breeze. He stacked pillows, pat pat pat. He rolled the blanket snug. He put the car in the toy bin. The rug looked cozy and calm.

“Benny, that rug looks welcoming,” Miss Dot said. Her voice sounded like warm tea. “You thought it through.”

Benny pressed the fourth sticker. Four boxes were filled. Only one box left: “Goodbye tidy-up.”

The afternoon ticked by with stories and songs. When the tidy-up bell chimed, Benny glanced around. Crayons on the art table. Blocks by the bookshelf. A puzzle piece near the window.

His tummy fluttered again. “I want to finish my list,” he told himself. “But there are many little things.”

He took another deep breath. He remembered Miss Dot’s words. “One small step at a time.”

He called out, “Friends, let’s make a tidy train! I will be the engine. We pick up one thing each time we pass a spot.”

“Choo-choo!” giggled the class.

Benny led the train around the room. At the art table, each friend put two crayons in the box. Click, click. At the bookshelf, they stacked blocks in the bin. Clack, clack. By the window, Benny found the puzzle piece. He held it up. “Found you!” He placed it in the puzzle with a gentle snap.

They went around twice. The room grew neat and bright again. Benny’s whiskers tingled with happy sparks.

He pressed the last sticker. “Five!” he said, beaming. “I finished all my boxes.”

Miss Dot clapped softly. “Benny, you used small steps, a plan, and kind words. You asked for help and helped others. How do you feel?”

Benny’s ears stood tall. “I feel proud. I can finish my tasks!”

Tilly nodded. “And your tidy train was fun.”

Miss Dot handed Benny a tiny paper star. “Today you learned: When a job looks big, break it into little steps. Use your words. Make a plan. Ask a friend. Then finish strong.”

Benny hugged the star to his sweater. The nursery smelled like clean tables and apples. The shelves looked peaceful. His heart felt calm and brave.

“I am a Task Captain,” Benny said, smiling. “I can do hard things, one small step at a time.”

He waved to his friends. “See you tomorrow. We can check more boxes together!”

The End.
