# Bunny Steps in a Cozy Room

In a small, cozy bedroom with soft yellow walls, a fluffy rug, and a nightlight shaped like a star, lived a determined rabbit named Rumi. The air smelled like clean sheets and warm cocoa. The window was open just a little, and a gentle breeze made the curtains dance.

Rumi loved music. Every night, Rumi pressed a big button on a tiny music box. Soft twinkly notes filled the room. Rumi’s paws tapped. Tap, tap. Tap-tap-tap. Rumi wanted to learn a special dance called Bunny Steps.

“I can do it,” Rumi said, ears perking up. “I will practice. I will not give up.”

Rumi’s cozy bed had a fluffy blue blanket and a carrot-shaped pillow. There was a round mirror on the closet door. Rumi stood in front of the mirror and tried the first step.

“Step, slide, hop,” Rumi whispered to the music.

Step. Slide. Hop—BOOP! Rumi wobbled and landed on the soft rug with a gentle thump. The rug smelled like lavender. Rumi’s whiskers twitched.

“Ow,” Rumi said, rubbing a knee. “This is hard.”

From the hallway, Mama Rabbit peeked in. “I hear music,” she said with a smile. “Practicing, little dancer?”

“Yes,” Rumi said, eyes bright. “But I keep falling.”

Mama walked in and sat on the bed, making it squeak. “Learning takes time,” she said. “Use patience. Be kind to yourself. Try small steps.”

Rumi nodded. “Small steps,” Rumi repeated.

Rumi stood again. “Step,” Rumi said softly. One paw moved. “Slide,” the other paw swished. “Hop,” Rumi bent knees and hopped—this time, a tiny hop. Rumi landed steady.

“I did a tiny hop!” Rumi giggled. The music twinkled like stars. Rumi felt warm and proud.

Rumi tried again. Step, slide, small hop. Again. And again. But on the fifth try, a big hop snuck out. Rumi slipped and bumped the laundry basket. It toppled. Socks spilled on the floor like little clouds.

“Oh no!” Rumi’s ears drooped. “I made a mess.”

Just then, Rumi’s little cousin, Tula, padded in wearing fuzzy slippers. “I heard a thump,” Tula said. “Are you okay?”

“I’m okay,” Rumi said with a sigh. “I am learning Bunny Steps, but I keep messing up. And now the socks are everywhere.”

Tula picked up a tiny sock and sniffed. “They smell like sunshine,” she said. “Let’s clean together. Then I can watch you. I will cheer.”

They cleaned the socks. Rustle, rustle. Plop. Plop. The basket was full again, and the room looked neat. Rumi felt better.

“Thank you,” Rumi said. “I will try again. I will be patient.”

Tula sat cross-legged on the rug. “Go, Rumi!” she whispered.

Rumi looked in the mirror. “Step,” Rumi said. Paw forward. “Slide,” paw swish. “Hop,” tiny hop. Land. Rumi swayed. Balance, balance. Rumi smiled.

Then Rumi tried the next part: “Turn and wiggle.” Rumi turned too fast. “Whoa!” Rumi bumped Tula’s slipper.

“Ouch,” Tula said, pulling back a paw.

Rumi’s heart felt squishy. “I’m sorry, Tula,” Rumi said. “I did not mean to bump you. Are you okay?”

Tula wiggled her toes. “I’m okay. Maybe make a space,” she said, pointing to a line in the rug pattern. “Dance on this line, and I’ll sit behind the pillow.”

“That’s a good plan,” Rumi said. “Thank you.” They moved the pillow. Tula sat safe behind it.

Mama Rabbit nodded. “That is good problem-solving,” she said. “You talked. You listened. You made a plan.”

Rumi felt calm. The nightlight glowed warm and gold. The music box played again: tink-tink, ting-ting.

Rumi took a deep breath. In, out. “I can learn,” Rumi told the mirror. “I will be patient.”

Step. Slide. Hop. Turn. Wiggle. Pause.

Rumi did the steps slowly. The paws felt lighter. The floor felt soft under foot pads. The mirror showed a small rabbit with bright eyes and brave ears.

But then Rumi forgot the last step. “Oh no,” Rumi whispered. “What comes next?”

“Clap!” Tula said.

“Clap!” Mama said.

Rumi smiled. “Clap,” Rumi said, and clapped twice. Clap, clap.

Rumi tried the whole dance again. Step, slide, hop, turn, wiggle, clap. The music chimed. Rumi swayed and finished with a tiny bow.

“You did it!” Tula cheered, clapping fast.

Mama’s eyes shone. “I saw patience,” she said. “I saw practice. I saw resilience. You kept trying, even when it was hard.”

Rumi’s chest felt warm like cocoa. “I kept trying,” Rumi said. “I can learn new things.”

Tula stood and wiggled her slippers. “Can you teach me?”

Rumi nodded. “Yes. We will go slow.”

Rumi showed Tula one small step at a time. They counted together. “One, two, three.” When Tula slipped, she frowned. Rumi patted her shoulder. “It’s okay,” Rumi said softly. “We can try again. We can be patient.”

Tula took a breath. Step. Slide. Hop. She landed and laughed. “I did a tiny hop!”

They danced the Bunny Steps together in the cozy bedroom. The curtains swished. The nightlight glowed. The music sparkled like little bells. Their feet made soft sounds on the rug. Tap, swish, hop.

When the song ended, they hugged. Rumi felt strong and calm.

Mama tucked them in. “What did we learn tonight?” she asked.

Rumi whispered, “When we try, and try again, and use patience, we can learn new skills.”

Tula added, “And we can fix little bumps with kind words and a plan.”

Mama kissed their foreheads. “That is resilience,” she said. “That is growing.”

Rumi snuggled under the blue blanket. “I will practice again tomorrow,” Rumi said. “I will not give up.”

The star nightlight winked. The room smelled sweet and safe. Rumi closed sleepy eyes, proud and peaceful.

The End.
