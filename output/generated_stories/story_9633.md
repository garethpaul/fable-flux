# Bounce's Big Adventure

Little Bounce was a happy kangaroo joey who loved to hop everywhere. One sunny morning, Bounce was playing on the rooftop of a tall building where his family lived. The rooftop had soft grass and colorful flowers that smelled like honey.

"Mama, look how high I can jump!" Bounce called out, bouncing up and down on his strong little legs.

But when Bounce looked around, he couldn't see Mama anywhere. The rooftop felt very big and quiet. The wind made a soft whooshing sound through the flowers.

"Mama? Papa?" Bounce called out. His voice echoed across the empty rooftop. "Where are you?"

Bounce felt his heart beating fast. He had never been alone on the rooftop before. The sun felt warm on his fuzzy brown fur, but he still felt scared.

"What should I do?" Bounce whispered to himself. He sat down next to a pretty yellow flower and took three deep breaths, just like Mama had taught him. "In... and out... in... and out..."

The breathing made Bounce feel calmer. He looked around the rooftop with clear eyes. There were so many beautiful things to see! Red flowers danced in the breeze. A friendly butterfly with orange wings fluttered nearby.

"Hello, little butterfly," Bounce said softly. "Are you alone too?"

The butterfly landed on Bounce's nose, making him giggle. It tickled!

Bounce stood up and decided to explore. "I can take care of myself," he said bravely. "I'm a big joey now!"

He hopped slowly around the rooftop, looking at everything carefully. Near a blue flower, he found a small puddle of rainwater. Bounce was thirsty, so he took a careful drink. The water tasted fresh and cool.

"I found water all by myself!" Bounce said proudly.

Then he discovered a shady spot under a small tree where the ground was soft and comfortable. Bounce practiced his best hops there, counting out loud. "One hop! Two hops! Three hops!"

While hopping, Bounce heard a tiny "peep peep" sound. He looked down and saw a baby bird who had fallen from its nest.

"Oh no, little bird! Are you okay?" Bounce asked gently.

The baby bird looked scared and couldn't fly yet. Bounce felt sorry for the little creature.

"Don't worry," Bounce said kindly. "I'll help you."

Very carefully, Bounce used his small paws to lift the baby bird. He looked up and saw the bird's nest in the tree above. Taking tiny, careful hops, Bounce climbed up and gently placed the baby bird back with its family.

"Peep peep!" chirped the baby bird happily.

"You're welcome, little friend," Bounce smiled.

Just then, Bounce heard familiar voices calling his name.

"Bounce! Where are you, sweetie?" It was Mama's voice!

"I'm here!" Bounce called back, hopping excitedly toward the sound.

Mama and Papa appeared from behind the rooftop door, looking worried but happy to see him.

"We were getting groceries from downstairs," Mama explained, giving Bounce a big warm hug. "We're so proud of you for staying calm and safe!"

"I took care of myself, Mama!" Bounce said proudly. "I found water, I practiced hopping, and I even helped a baby bird!"

Papa smiled and patted Bounce's head. "You showed great self-reliance, kindness, and calmness, little one. You're growing up to be a wonderful joey."

Bounce felt proud and happy. He had learned that he could take care of himself and help others too. From that day on, Bounce felt more confident and brave, knowing he could handle new situations with a calm heart and helping hands.

The End.
