# Panda Paws and the Spin Cycle

Ping the panda padded into the laundry room with soft, careful steps. His black-and-white paws made tiny pats on the cool tile floor. The room smelled clean, like soap and sunshine. “I want to help,” Ping whispered. “Helping makes my heart feel warm.”

Mama Panda smiled and pointed to a little basket on the floor. “Thank you, Ping. Can you sort the clothes? Whites in one pile, colors in another.”

Ping nodded. “I can do that,” he said. He crouched down and sniffed a fluffy towel. It smelled like fresh rain. “White towel here,” he said, patting a soft white pile. He found a red shirt. “Red is a color,” he said, and made a bright color pile.

Ping moved with gentle swishes and soft scoots. He stretched his arms. He bent his knees. He rolled a sock like a little ball. “Roll, roll, roll,” he sang. His round ears wiggled as he worked. Ping loved to move. Helping felt like a dance.

“Uh-oh,” Ping said. One tiny blue sock was missing its friend. He looked under the basket. He looked behind the door. “Sock, where are you?” he called.

Mama Panda peeked in. “What’s the problem, Ping?”

“One sock is hiding,” Ping said. He took a deep breath. “I can solve this.” Ping clapped his paws softly. “First, I will look with my eyes. Then, I will feel with my paws.”

Ping moved the basket. Clunk. He felt along the floor. Smooth and cool. No sock. He opened the washer door. The big, round window smelled like soapy bubbles. He peered inside. “Hello?” he whispered. No sock.

Ping sat for a moment. He felt a little sad. His shoulders drooped. “This is hard,” he said.

Mama Panda knelt beside him. “It is hard,” she agreed. “What can we try next?”

Ping tapped his chin. “I can check the sleeves!” He shook the red shirt. Wiggle-wiggle. Something soft fell to the floor. Plop! The tiny blue sock!

Ping grinned. “I found it! I kept trying.” His chest felt warm and proud.

“Good problem-solving,” Mama Panda said. “Now, let’s load the washer.”

Ping lifted clothes one by one. “In you go, towel. In you go, shirt.” He counted softly. “One, two, three.” The washer door clicked shut. Ping pressed his ear to the glass. “It’s cool and smooth,” he said.

Mama Panda handed him the soap. “Just a little,” she said.

Ping squeezed the bottle carefully. Plop, plop. The soap smelled like lemons. “Mmm, lemony,” he giggled.

Mama Panda nodded. “Ready to press the button?”

Ping held his paw over the start button. “Yes.” He pressed it. Beep! The washer hummed. The drum began to turn. Swish, swish. The clothes tumbled like dancing clouds.

Ping watched, eyes wide. “They are spinning!” he laughed. He swayed his body side to side. “Swish, swish,” he whispered, moving with the washer. He lifted his paws and did tiny steps. Tap, tap. “Helping can be fun.”

While the washer swished, Ping looked around. He saw clothespins in a jar. Click, clack. He opened and closed them. “Pinch, pinch,” he said. He saw a basket of clean socks waiting to be matched.

“I can help more,” Ping said. He sat on the rug and matched socks. Stripe to stripe. Dot to dot. “You match. You match,” he said softly. He felt calm and patient. When two socks were different, he didn’t frown. He smiled and said, “Not a match yet. I will keep looking.” Ping liked how it felt to keep trying.

The washer beeped. Beep-beep! The door clicked. Warm steam puffed out, smelling fresh and cozy. Ping held the basket. “I’m ready,” he said.

Together, they lifted the wet clothes. Drip, drip. “Heavy,” Ping said, using both paws and his strong legs. He moved slowly and safely to the dryer. Thump, thump went the clothes as they fell in.

Mama Panda showed Ping the lint tray. “We clear this to help the dryer,” she said.

Ping pulled the soft lint. “It’s like a tiny cloud!” he giggled. He tossed it in the bin. “All clean.”

They set the dryer. Whoosh. Warm air flowed. Ping sat and waited quietly. Waiting was part of helping, too. He swung his feet and hummed a little tune. “I can be patient,” he said.

When the dryer stopped, Ping opened the door. A wave of warmth puffed out. “It feels like a hug,” he said. They folded towels. Fold, press, pat. Ping lined up corners with care. He stacked them into neat towers. “Tall and tidy,” he said proudly.

Mama Panda hugged Ping. “You were so helpful today. You moved your body, you solved a problem, and you kept trying.”

Ping smiled. His eyes shone. “Helping makes me happy. When it’s tricky, I can try a new way. I can be patient.”

Mama Panda nodded. “That’s our lesson for today.”

Ping carried a small stack to the shelf. The towels felt fluffy. The shelf smelled clean. He placed the stack just right. “All done,” he said.

“Teamwork,” said Mama Panda.

“Teamwork,” said Ping.

The End.
