# Leo the Lion Cub Learns to Share

In a cozy home office filled with books and papers, a brave little lion cub named Leo loved to play. The office belonged to his mama, who worked there every day typing on her computer. Leo thought the whole room was his special playground.

"This is MY office!" Leo roared proudly, puffing out his fluffy chest. He would knock over pencil holders with his paws and scatter papers everywhere. *Crash!* went the staplers. *Whoosh!* went the papers flying through the air.

Leo's mama tried to work, but it was very hard with all the noise and mess. "Leo, sweetie, I need to finish my work," she said gently. But Leo didn't listen. He was having too much fun!

One morning, Leo's little sister Lily came into the office. She was much smaller than Leo, with tiny paws and a soft voice.

"Can I play too, Leo?" Lily asked politely, her big eyes hopeful.

"NO!" Leo roared loudly. "This is MY space! Go away!" He pushed all the toys toward himself and turned his back to Lily.

Lily's eyes filled with tears. She walked away sadly, her little tail dragging on the floor.

Mama Lion stopped typing and looked at Leo with serious eyes. "Leo, how do you think Lily felt when you yelled at her?"

Leo's ears drooped. He hadn't really thought about Lily's feelings. "Um... sad?" he whispered.

"That's right," Mama said softly. "When we don't share or speak kindly, we hurt others' feelings. How would you feel if someone told you to go away?"

Leo thought hard. His tummy felt funny and warm - not the good kind of warm. "I would feel very sad," he admitted quietly.

Just then, Leo heard soft sniffling from the hallway. Lily was sitting alone, wiping her eyes with her tiny paws.

Leo's brave heart felt heavy. He wanted to be a good big brother, not a mean one. Taking a deep breath, he walked slowly to where Lily sat.

"Lily?" Leo said gently. "I'm sorry I was mean to you. That wasn't nice."

Lily looked up with surprise.

"Would you like to play in the office with me?" Leo asked. "We can share the toys and be quiet so Mama can work."

Lily's face lit up like sunshine. "Really? You want to play together?"

"Yes!" Leo smiled. "And I promise to use my inside voice and listen to your ideas too."

The two cubs returned to the office together. Leo showed Lily how to build towers with the blocks without making too much noise. Lily taught Leo a quiet game with the colorful paper clips. They whispered and giggled softly.

"*Click, click*" went the paper clips as they made pretty chains. "*Whisper, whisper*" went their voices as they shared secrets.

Mama Lion smiled as she watched her cubs playing peacefully together. "Thank you both for being so respectful," she said warmly. "Leo, I'm proud of how you chose to be kind."

Leo felt his chest puff up with pride - but this time it was different. This time he felt proud because he had been brave enough to say sorry and choose kindness.

"Being respectful feels good," Leo realized. "And playing together is much more fun than playing alone!"

From that day on, Leo always remembered to share, speak kindly, and think about others' feelings. He learned that being truly brave meant being kind and respectful to everyone.

The End.
