# Benny Bear's Big Bathroom Game

Benny Bear was a fluffy brown cub who loved to play soccer. Every morning, he would brush his teeth and wash his face in the cozy bathroom before running outside to kick his favorite red ball.

But this morning was different. When Benny turned on the water faucet, nothing came out! Not even a tiny drop.

"Oh no!" said Benny, looking at his toothbrush covered in minty toothpaste. "How will I brush my teeth? How will I wash my face? I need to be clean before my soccer game!"

Benny felt worried. His tummy felt fluttery like butterflies were dancing inside. He sat on the soft bathroom rug and thought hard. "What can I do?" he wondered aloud.

Then Benny remembered something. "Wait! Mama always keeps a big pitcher of water in the kitchen for emergencies!" He padded to the kitchen on his furry paws and found the blue pitcher, full of cool, clear water.

"I can use this!" Benny said happily. He carefully carried the pitcher back to the bathroom, being very careful not to spill any precious drops.

First, Benny poured a little water into his cupped paws to wet his toothbrush. "Scrub, scrub, scrub," he sang as he brushed his teeth, making sure to clean all around. The water felt cool and refreshing in his mouth.

Next, he poured some water into the sink basin to wash his face. The water felt nice and cool on his furry cheeks. "Splash, splash!" he giggled as he cleaned behind his ears.

But then Benny looked at the pitcher. There wasn't much water left, and he still needed to wash his paws after breakfast.

"I need to be smart about this," Benny said to himself. He remembered watching his mama save water by turning off the faucet while brushing her teeth. "I can do that too!"

Benny used just a tiny bit of water to rinse his toothbrush, then set it in the holder. He used a small amount to splash his face one more time, then patted himself dry with his soft yellow towel.

Just then, Papa Bear knocked on the bathroom door. "Benny, are you okay in there? I heard the water isn't working."

"I'm fine, Papa!" Benny called out cheerfully. "I found the emergency water pitcher and figured out how to get ready all by myself!"

Papa Bear opened the door and smiled proudly. "Wow, Benny! You used your thinking cap and found a great solution. That's called being resourceful!"

"What does resourceful mean?" asked Benny, tilting his fuzzy head.

"It means you used what you had to solve your problem all by yourself," Papa explained. "You didn't give up, and you found a way to get ready for your soccer game on time!"

Benny's chest puffed up with pride. He felt happy and confident. "I did it! I solved the problem!"

"And you saved water too," Papa added. "That's very thoughtful."

Soon, the water was fixed, but Benny felt proud that he had been so clever. He ran outside to play soccer with his friends, feeling clean and ready for anything.

"I can solve problems!" Benny told his friend Rosie Rabbit as they kicked the ball back and forth. "When something goes wrong, I just need to think and use what I have!"

From that day on, whenever Benny faced a problem, he remembered to stay calm, think creatively, and use his resources wisely.

The End.
