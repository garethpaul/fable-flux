# Penny Panda and the Rooftop Garden Mystery

High up on the rooftop of the tall apartment building, Penny Panda was watering her beautiful garden. The morning sun felt warm on her black and white fur, and she could smell the sweet flowers blooming all around her.

"Good morning, little tomatoes," Penny said softly, sprinkling water on the tiny green plants. "Good morning, pretty sunflowers!"

Penny loved taking care of her rooftop garden. Every day, she would climb the stairs slowly and patiently, carrying her little watering can. She knew that plants needed water, sunshine, and lots of love to grow big and strong.

Suddenly, Penny heard a small voice crying. "Help! Help me!"

Penny looked around carefully. Where was that sad voice coming from? She listened with her round ears and followed the sound to the corner of the rooftop.

There, stuck behind a big flower pot, was a tiny brown sparrow. The little bird was flapping his wings, but he couldn't get out!

"Oh my!" said Penny gently. "Don't worry, little friend. I'm here to help you."

The sparrow looked scared. "I was trying to get some seeds from your garden, but I got stuck! I'm sorry for taking your food!"

Penny sat down slowly and thought carefully. She could see that the sparrow was very hungry and frightened. Instead of being angry, Penny felt sorry for the little bird.

"Let me understand," said Penny in her calm voice. "You were hungry and looking for food?"

The sparrow nodded sadly. "Yes, I haven't eaten all day. But I know it was wrong to take from your garden without asking."

Penny smiled warmly. "I understand now. Being hungry is very hard. Let me help you get unstuck first, and then we can talk about the food."

Very carefully and patiently, Penny moved the heavy flower pot. The sparrow fluttered out, shaking his tiny feathers.

"Thank you so much!" chirped the sparrow. "You're very kind, even though I tried to take your seeds."

Penny looked at her garden, then at the hungry little bird. She had an idea that would be fair for everyone.

"Little sparrow," said Penny, "I understand that you need food. That's not wrong! But next time, please ask first. That's the right thing to do."

The sparrow's eyes grew wide. "You mean... you're not angry?"

"I'm not angry," said Penny. "But I want to teach you something important. When we take things without asking, it's not fair. But when we ask nicely, good things can happen!"

Penny walked over to a special corner of her garden where she kept extra seeds. "These seeds are for sharing with hungry friends like you. You can come here every morning, but you must ask first. That's how we take care of each other safely and fairly."

The sparrow hopped up and down with joy. "Really? You would share with me?"

"Of course!" said Penny. "Understanding each other and sharing is what good friends do. And now you know the safe way to visit my garden!"

From that day on, the little sparrow visited Penny every morning. He always asked politely, "May I please have some seeds, Penny?" And Penny always shared happily.

Penny learned that when we try to understand why someone does something, we can find kind ways to help. The sparrow learned that asking nicely is always better than taking without permission.

Together on the sunny rooftop, they became the very best of friends, taking care of the garden and each other.

The End.
