# Fawn's Fair Feast

Little Fawn had the softest brown spots and the biggest, kindest eyes in the whole forest. Today was very special because Grandmother Deer had invited all the woodland friends to a big feast in her cozy dining room.

The dining room smelled wonderful! Sweet honey cakes sat on pretty plates, and colorful berries sparkled like jewels in wooden bowls. Fawn's tummy rumbled with excitement as she looked at the long wooden table covered with a cheerful red tablecloth.

"Welcome, everyone!" called Grandmother Deer as the friends arrived. Rabbit hopped in with his fluffy white tail bouncing. Squirrel scampered through the door, chattering happily. Bear shuffled in with a warm smile, and tiny Mouse squeaked hello from near the door.

But when they all sat down, Fawn noticed something that made her heart feel heavy. The biggest, most delicious-looking honey cake was placed right in front of Bear. Rabbit got a medium-sized berry tart. Squirrel had a small acorn muffin. And poor little Mouse? Mouse had only three tiny crumbs on her plate.

"This doesn't look fair," Fawn whispered to herself, her ears drooping sadly.

Bear was already reaching for his enormous cake when Fawn stood up. Her legs felt wobbly, but she knew she had to speak up.

"Wait, please!" Fawn said in her gentle voice. Everyone turned to look at her. "I think we should share the food more fairly. Mouse is our friend too, and she has almost nothing to eat."

Mouse's tiny whiskers twitched with surprise. "Oh my," she squeaked softly, "I didn't want to complain."

Bear looked down at his huge cake, then at Mouse's nearly empty plate. His big brown eyes grew sad. "You're absolutely right, Fawn. I'm sorry, Mouse. I didn't notice."

"I have an idea!" Fawn said, her spots seeming to shimmer with excitement. "What if we put all the food in the middle of the table? Then everyone can take what they need, and we can make sure everyone gets enough."

Grandmother Deer smiled proudly. "What a wise and kind suggestion, little Fawn."

All the friends worked together, moving the delicious treats to the center of the table. The honey cakes, berry tarts, acorn muffins, and even some crunchy leaves that Fawn had brought were arranged beautifully for everyone to share.

"This looks much better," said Rabbit, his pink nose twitching happily.

"And it smells even more wonderful when we share it together," added Squirrel, his bushy tail swishing with joy.

Mouse climbed onto a small cushion so she could reach the food better. "Thank you, Fawn," she said, her voice full of gratitude. "You made sure everyone was treated fairly."

As they ate their feast, the dining room filled with laughter and happy chatter. Bear made sure Mouse got some of the sweet honey cake. Rabbit shared his berries with everyone. Squirrel offered his nuts to his friends.

Fawn felt warm and happy inside, like sunshine was glowing in her chest. She had learned that being fair meant making sure everyone was treated kindly and equally. It didn't matter if someone was big like Bear or tiny like Mouse – everyone deserved to be included and cared for.

"Thank you for speaking up, dear Fawn," said Grandmother Deer, nuzzling her granddaughter gently. "You showed great courage and kindness today."

From that day on, whenever the woodland friends gathered for meals, they always remembered Fawn's lesson about fairness and sharing. And their feasts became even more delicious because they were seasoned with justice, kindness, and love.

The End.
