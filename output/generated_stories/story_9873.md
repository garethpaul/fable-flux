# Poppy the Patient Panda and the Fair Share

Poppy was a patient panda who lived in a cozy treehouse high in a leafy green tree. The treehouse smelled like fresh bamboo and sweet pine. The floor felt smooth under Poppy’s soft paws. A gentle breeze tickled the curtains and made them wave hello. Birds chirped, “Tweet, tweet!” and sunlight made warm, golden squares on the wooden walls.

Poppy loved playing with friends in the treehouse. Today, her friends—Milo the monkey, Tia the turtle, and Bibi the bluebird—came over to build a block city. The blocks were bright red, blue, and yellow. They clacked and clicked as the friends stacked them high.

“Let’s make a bridge!” said Milo, bouncing.

“I will make a slow, strong tower,” said Tia, smiling softly.

“I will make a nest house,” chirped Bibi.

Poppy nodded. “I will make a big, safe road so everyone can play.”

They worked and giggled. Poppy felt glad. Her heart felt warm like sunshine. But then—clatter, clack!—Milo grabbed a big handful of blocks from the middle of the pile.

“I need these! I’m the fastest builder!” he said, hugging the blocks tight.

Bibi flapped her wings. “But I need some too!”

Tia tucked into her shell a little. “I don’t have any big ones now.”

Poppy’s ears twitched. She watched and waited. Poppy was patient. But she saw something that did not feel right in her tummy. It felt like a tiny pebble in her shoe. It felt unfair.

Poppy took a slow breath. She remembered what her grandma panda always said: “Patience helps us see the truth. Justice means making things fair for everyone.”

Poppy softly tapped the floor with her paw. Tap, tap. “Friends, can we talk?” she asked, her voice kind and calm.

Milo swung his tail. “I’m just fast! Fast builders get more blocks!”

Poppy shook her head gently. “Being fast is okay, Milo. But taking all the blocks is not fair. Justice means everyone gets a fair share.”

Milo frowned. “But I want my big bridge.”

Poppy nodded. “I hear you. You want to build something great. We all do. Let’s make it fair.”

Tia peeked out of her shell. “How can we do that?”

Poppy thought, and the breeze whispered in the leaves. She set the blocks in a neat row. “We can share by turns. We can count the blocks. We can each get the same number. That is justice. That is fair.”

Bibi chirped, “I like turns!”

Milo looked at the bright blocks. His cheeks puffed. He looked at Tia’s tiny pile and Bibi’s small stack. He looked at his big armful. He sighed. “I guess it is not fair.”

Poppy smiled. “Thank you for thinking, Milo. That is integrity—doing the right thing, even when it is hard.”

Milo slowly placed some blocks back. Clack, clack, clack. “I’m sorry,” he said. “I will share.”

Poppy’s eyes shone. “Thank you. That shows respect.”

They counted together. “One, two, three, four!” Each friend got the same number of big blocks and small blocks. The piles looked even. The pebble feeling in Poppy’s tummy went away. She felt light and happy.

Now the building was fun again. The blocks clicked like music. Milo built the bridge, but this time he asked, “Does anyone need this block?” Tia made a tall, careful tower. Bibi built a cozy nest house. Poppy made a wide road that touched all the buildings, so everyone could visit.

Suddenly, the bridge wobbled. Wiggle, wobble, wobble—CRACK! It fell with a clatter. Milo’s eyes filled with tears. “My bridge!” he cried.

Poppy patted his shoulder gently. “It’s okay. We can fix it together.”

Tia nodded. “Slow and steady.”

Bibi fluttered. “I will bring a long block for support!”

Poppy looked at the blocks and thought. “Let’s try this: We can put a strong block here, and here, like legs. Then the bridge will stand tall and fair.” She smiled. “We can each give one block from our piles. That is fair for all of us.”

Milo sniffled, then nodded. “I will give one.” He handed over a shiny red block.

Tia pushed a blue block forward. Bibi nudged a yellow one. Poppy added a thick, sturdy block from her pile. Together, they built the bridge again. This time, it stood strong. The road touched the tower, the bridge, and the nest house. Everything connected. Everyone could play.

They cheered. “Hooray for fair play!”

The sun warmed their backs. The blocks felt smooth and cool. The treehouse smelled like happy bamboo soup. Milo grinned. “Sharing felt good!”

Poppy laughed softly. “Justice helps us all. When we are fair, everyone can have fun.”

Bibi chirped, “Integrity is doing the right thing.”

Tia added, “Respect is listening and caring.”

Poppy nodded. “Yes. Today we used patience to see the problem. We used justice to make it fair. We used integrity to fix our mistake. We used respect to listen.”

Milo said, “Next time, I will ask before I take.”

Poppy smiled. “That is the fair way.”

They played until the sky turned peach and pink. The breeze hummed a sleepy song. Poppy felt peaceful. Her heart felt like a soft, warm blanket.

As they cleaned up, Poppy whispered, “Justice means fair for everyone. When we share, we care.”

All the friends agreed. “Fair for everyone!”

The End.
