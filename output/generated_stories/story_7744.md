# Penny Panda's Sorry Letter

In a cozy nursery filled with soft toys and colorful books, lived a gentle panda named Penny. Her black and white fur was as fluffy as cotton balls, and her round eyes sparkled like shiny buttons. The nursery smelled like lavender and felt warm and safe, with sunshine streaming through the polka-dot curtains.

Penny loved playing with her best friend, a little stuffed bunny named Benny. They would have tea parties, build block towers, and read picture books together every day.

One sunny morning, Penny was feeling grumpy. She had woken up too early and her tummy rumbled with hunger. When Benny bounced over with his usual cheerful "Good morning, Penny!" she snapped at him.

"Go away, Benny! I don't want to play today!" Penny said in a mean voice, pushing the little bunny aside.

Benny's button eyes grew sad and watery. His soft ears drooped down like wilted flowers. "Oh," he whispered quietly, and hopped away to sit alone by the window.

Penny continued playing by herself, but something felt wrong. The nursery seemed quieter and less colorful without Benny's giggles. Her toys didn't feel as fun, and even her favorite honey crackers tasted bland.

As the morning went on, Penny watched Benny sitting all alone. She noticed how his shoulders slumped and how he wasn't humming his happy songs like usual. A heavy feeling grew in Penny's chest, like a gray cloud settling over her heart.

"Oh no," Penny said to herself, touching her furry chest. "I hurt Benny's feelings. I was mean to my best friend, and now he's sad because of me."

Penny's patient nature helped her think carefully about what to do. She remembered what Mama Panda had taught her: "When we hurt someone's feelings, the best thing to do is say sorry and show them we care."

Penny had an idea! She waddled over to the little wooden desk in the corner of the nursery. With her chubby paws, she picked up a purple crayon and a piece of sunshine-yellow paper.

Very carefully, Penny drew a big heart with wobbly lines. Inside the heart, she wrote "SORRY" in her best letters, even though the 'S' looked a bit like a snake and the 'Y' was sideways. She drew herself and Benny holding hands, with big smiles and happy flowers all around them.

At the bottom, she wrote "I LOVE YOU BENNY" and drew a picture of them sharing honey crackers.

Penny's heart felt lighter as she colored. The act of making something special for Benny filled her with warm, fuzzy feelings, like drinking hot cocoa on a cold day.

When she finished, Penny walked slowly to where Benny sat. Her paws felt sweaty and her tummy had butterflies, but she knew what she had to do.

"Benny," she said softly, sitting down beside him. "I'm very sorry I was mean to you. I was grumpy, but that's no excuse for hurting your feelings. You're my best friend, and I love you very much."

She handed him the colorful sorry letter. "I made this for you."

Benny's eyes grew bright and sparkly again. "Oh, Penny! Thank you! I forgive you. I love you too!"

They hugged tightly, and Penny felt the gray cloud lift from her heart, replaced by warm sunshine.

From that day on, whenever Penny felt grumpy, she remembered to use her words kindly and to always say sorry when she made mistakes.

The End.
