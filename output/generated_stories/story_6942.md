# Gigi’s Pantry Plan

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with a long, gentle neck and big, kind eyes. She loved to help. She loved to think. She loved to make things pretty with art. One sunny day, Gigi peeked into the community pantry.

The pantry smelled like warm bread and apples. It was filled with boxes and cans and baskets. There were colorful labels and soft paper bags. But today, it looked a little messy. Boxes were sideways. Cans were wobbly. A jar of jam sat without a label.

“Oh dear,” Gigi whispered, tilting her head. “People come here to find food. They need it to be easy. I can help!”

Her friend Penny, a tiny mouse with bright whiskers, scurried by. “Hi, Gigi! What are you doing?”

“I want to clean and label and make art signs,” said Gigi. “We can make it neat for everyone.”

Penny’s whiskers twitched. “That’s a big job.”

“I have time,” Gigi said with a smile. “I will do it all today!”

Gigi started right away. She stacked cans. Clink, clink, clink. She sorted boxes. Rustle, rustle. She made colorful signs with paint. Swish, swish, swish. “Veg-e-ta-bles,” she said slowly, painting big green letters. “Fruit,” she sang in sunny orange.

Her long neck helped her reach tall shelves. Her big heart made her smile. People came in and smiled too.

“Thank you, Gigi,” said Mr. Bear, choosing noodles.

“You are kind,” said Mrs. Rabbit, picking rice.

Gigi felt warm and happy. Helping felt good.

Then, Gigi looked up. The clock on the wall said ten o’clock. At eleven, she had promised to read stories to the little calves. At twelve, she had a meeting to plan a park painting day. At one, she was going to help deliver food to Grandma Duck. Gigi’s ears wiggled. There was so much to do!

“I can finish fast,” Gigi told herself. She painted more signs. She sorted more jars. She even started a big mural with flowers and smiling spoons. It was so pretty. It smelled like lemon paint and fresh paper.

Penny tugged Gigi’s tail. “Gigi, it’s almost eleven.”

Gigi blinked. “Oh no! I forgot the time!”

Her heart thumped. She had not made a plan. She had not set a timer. Now she was late. The little calves waited in the reading corner, holding soft pillows. The park meeting friends sat with empty notepads. Grandma Duck held a basket by her door.

Gigi’s tummy felt tight. “I wanted to help everyone,” she whispered. “But I did too much at once.”

Penny held Gigi’s hoof. “It’s okay to need a plan. We can fix this.”

Gigi took a deep breath. She smelled the sweet apples. She listened to the quiet hum of the fridge. Her shoulders softened. “You’re right. I can make amends. I can be on time if I make a plan.”

She put down her brush and found a little notebook. She drew three boxes.

- Box 1: Read to calves at eleven.
- Box 2: Park meeting at twelve.
- Box 3: Deliver food at one.

She drew a tiny clock next to each box. Then she drew a heart under them and wrote: Be kind. Be honest. Be on time.

Gigi clipped a small bell timer to her scarf. Ting! She set it carefully.

First, she went to the reading corner. “I am sorry I am late,” she said softly. “I lost track of time.” The little calves looked up. One calf said, “We waited! Can we still have a story?”

“Yes,” said Gigi. “Thank you for your patience.” She read a sweet book about sharing. Her voice was gentle. The pages smelled like clean paper. The calves giggled at the funny parts. “Moo!” they said together.

Ting! The bell rang. “Time to go to the meeting,” Gigi said. “Thank you for listening.”

At the park meeting, Gigi said, “I am sorry I was late to start. I made a plan now.” Her friends nodded. They were tolerant and kind. They made a simple list: paint benches blue, plant flowers, make a big welcome sign. Gigi smiled and drew little stars. Ting! The bell rang again.

Gigi trotted to Grandma Duck’s house with a neat basket. “Fresh bread, apples, and tea,” she said. “I am sorry I am a little late. I made you a small art label so you know what is inside.”

Grandma Duck quacked softly. “Thank you, dear. You made amends. That is good.”

Back at the pantry, Gigi looked at the half-finished mural. “I will finish this tomorrow,” she said. “I will put it in my plan.”

Penny clapped her tiny paws. “You did it, Gigi!”

Gigi wrote one last note: Finish mural: two o’clock tomorrow. She set the bell for a few minutes and cleaned up the paint. Swish, wipe, tidy.

The pantry looked neater. The signs were bright and clear. People could find what they needed. Gigi felt calm and proud.

She nibbled a crisp apple slice. Crunch. “Today I learned a big thing,” she said. “Time is like a friend. When I make a plan, I can be kind to others and to myself.”

Penny nodded. “And when we make a mistake, we can make amends.”

Gigi smiled. “Yes. We can be patient and tolerant too.”

They posted a small sign on the pantry door: “Plan, Help, Be Kind.”

The End.
