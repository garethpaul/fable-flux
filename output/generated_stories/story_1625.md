# Riff Raccoon Rides to School

Riff was a musical raccoon with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He loved to tap rhythms on anything he could find. Tap, tap, tappity-tap! This morning, Riff climbed into the family car with Mom. The seats were warm, and the air smelled like strawberries from his shampoo.

“Buckles on,” Mom said gently.

“Click!” Riff said, snapping his seat belt. He liked the safe snug feeling. He drummed a little on the buckle. Tap-tap.

Today, Riff was going to visit a school for the first time. He wanted to learn new songs and new words. But he also felt a flutter in his tummy. What if he made a mistake? What if he forgot the words?

Mom started the car. The engine hummed like a big bee. “How are you feeling, Riff?” she asked.

Riff wiggled his whiskers. “I want to learn, but I feel wiggly inside.”

“It’s okay to feel wiggly,” Mom said. “Learning is an adventure. We can make a plan.”

“A plan!” Riff sat up taller. His tail curled with interest.

Mom handed him a small, clean cloth and a tiny spray bottle. “First, fresh and clean helps our brains feel ready. Spray, wipe, and shine.”

Riff spritzed the cloth. It smelled like lemon. Spritz, spritz! He wiped his little paws. Swipe, swipe! “Ooh,” he said. “My paws feel cool and tingly.”

“Clean paws, clear mind,” Mom said with a smile.

Riff looked at his face in the mirror flap. He could see a little jam spot on his cheek from breakfast. He giggled. “Hello, jam spot.” He wiped it away. “Goodbye, jam spot!” His fur felt smooth and tidy, and he liked it.

The car rolled past trees waving their green hands. Sunlight danced on the dashboard. Riff tapped a gentle rhythm on his knees. Tap, tap, tappity-tap.

“Let’s sing our learning song,” Mom said.

Riff nodded. “Yes, please!”

Together they sang, soft and bright:
“Wash, wash paws,
Brush, brush teeth,
Buckle up safe,
Breathe, breathe, breathe.
I can learn, I can try,
I feel proud, and that’s why!”

Riff took a slow breath in and out. His tummy felt less fluttery.

Mom said, “What do you want to learn today?”

Riff thought. He could almost taste the idea, like a sweet pear. “I want to learn new music words,” he said. “Like beat and rest. And I want to count to four while I play.”

“That’s a great goal,” Mom said. “How will you practice?”

Riff’s ears perked up. “I can clap and count, even in the car.”

He held up his clean paws. Clap, clap, clap, clap. “One, two, three, four!” He paused and whispered, “Rest.” Then he clapped again. Clap, clap, clap, clap. His rhythm felt steady, like the tires on the road. Thrum-thrum, thrum-thrum.

A small worry peeked back in. “What if I clap wrong at school?” he asked.

Mom’s voice was warm. “Mistakes help us learn. Each try is a little bridge. Step by step.”

Riff nodded. He liked the picture of a little bridge made of tries.

They stopped at a red light. Riff could hear other cars, a bird on a wire, and the soft tick-tick of the blinker. He made a tiny song with the sounds. Tick-tick, chirp-chirp, hum-hum. He smiled. “Music is everywhere!”

“Yes,” Mom said. “And your clean paws make a great drum.”

Riff grinned. “And my clean face helps me see the notes!”

They arrived early at the school. The parking lot smelled like fresh rain, even though the sun was shining. Mom turned off the engine. The quiet felt cozy.

“Before we go in,” Mom said, “let’s do our smart-start steps.”

Riff nodded. He:
- Took a deep breath. In, out.
- Smoothed his fur and checked his buckle.
- Gave his paws one more gentle wipe. Swipe, swipe.
- Said, “I can learn new things.”

His heart felt big and brave.

Just then, his little brother Pip, also in the back seat, peeked over. Pip squeaked, “Riff, teach me the counting clap!”

Riff’s tail flicked with pride. “Okay!” He showed Pip: “Clap, clap, clap, clap—one, two, three, four—rest.” Pip giggled and clapped along. They made a small car concert. Clap-clap! Giggle-giggle!

Mom applauded. “Wonderful learners!”

Riff felt taller inside. Teaching made him feel strong and kind. His clean paws made happy, crisp sounds, and his clean face felt fresh, like a new song.

“Ready?” Mom asked.

“Ready,” Riff said. He opened the door and felt the warm sun on his fur. He stepped out with a bounce. At the school door, he whispered to himself, “I can learn. I can try. I am proud.”

Later, when the visit was done, Riff climbed back into the car, glowing. “Mom, I learned beat and rest! And I counted to four! I even showed the class our clap.”

Mom smiled. “How do you feel?”

“Clean, calm, and clever,” Riff said. “Education helps my brain grow. Hygiene helps me feel ready. And I am proud of me.”

Mom squeezed his paw. “That’s the best music of all.”

Riff tapped a soft thank-you rhythm on the seat. Tap, tap, tappity-tap. He looked out at the bright day and hummed their song. He knew tomorrow would bring more steps on his little learning bridge.

The End.
