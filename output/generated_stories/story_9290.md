# Gigi’s Big Helping Neck

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe who loved the cozy, comfortable nursery. Sunlight made warm squares on the soft rug. The air smelled like fresh crayons and clean blankets. Gentle music hummed, and tiny plants on the windowsill wiggled in a soft breeze.

Today was Clean-Up Day. The children, toys, and animal friends worked together to tidy the nursery. There were blocks, books, and soft animal dolls everywhere. A bright poster on the wall read, “We work as a team!”

Gigi blinked her long eyelashes and said, “Let’s help each other. Teamwork makes big jobs feel small.”

Mouse Mimi squeaked, “I can carry tiny things!”

Hippo Hugo yawned, “I can push heavy bins.”

Bunny Bella wiggled her nose. “I can sort colors!”

Gigi smiled. “I can reach high places.”

Teacher Rosa clapped softly. “Thank you, helpers. Remember safety. Walk, don’t run. Lift with care. Share, be fair.”

They began. Blocks clinked. Pages rustled. The rug felt squishy under hooves and paws. Everyone hummed a happy tune: “We work together, do our best, we help our friends and take a rest.”

Suddenly, someone gasped. On the highest shelf sat a shiny, red fire truck toy. It glowed in the sun like a tiny cherry. A note beside it said, “For everyone to share.”

Mimi squeaked, “I love that truck!”

Hugo rumbled, “Me too!”

Bella whispered, “It is beautiful.”

Gigi looked up with her gentle eyes. “We can share it. We can take turns. That is fair.”

Just then, a little wind from the open window whooshed. The red truck rolled to the very edge. It wobbled. It teetered.

“Oh no!” said Bella.

“It might fall!” said Mimi.

“We must keep it safe,” said Gigi. “Let’s make a plan.”

Teacher Rosa nodded. “Use teamwork. Keep your bodies safe. Keep the toy safe. Use what we have before getting something new.”

Gigi bent her long neck, but she was still just a little too far away. She could smell the wood of the shelf and the lemony clean smell, but she could not reach the truck.

Hugo tried to stand on his toes. “Almost,” he said, puffing. The shelf creaked. “Not safe,” he added, stepping back.

Mimi had an idea. “We can build a block ramp!”

Bella clapped. “Yes! That is smart and kind to the Earth. We use the toys we have. That is sustainability.”

They set to work. The blocks made gentle tap-tap sounds. Gigi placed big, sturdy blocks at the bottom. Hugo pushed them close together. Bella sorted by size and color. “Small to big, red to blue,” she sang.

Mimi carried tiny wedges and slid them in so the ramp would not wobble. “Safe and steady,” she said.

Gigi tested the ramp with her hoof. “Solid,” she said. “Good team.”

But the truck still wobbled on the shelf. Gigi looked at the note again. “It says, ‘For everyone to share.’ That means we must be fair. No pushing. No grabbing. Justice means fair turns.”

Hugo nodded. “We will make a turn chart,” he said. He found a scrap of paper that had a blank back. “We reuse,” he added, smiling. He drew simple boxes. Bella wrote names with big, careful letters. Mimi dotted tiny hearts.

“Now, let’s get the truck down safely,” said Teacher Rosa.

Gigi took a deep breath. “I will stretch slowly. Mimi, you watch the ramp. Bella, you hold the base. Hugo, you spot me. If I say stop, we all stop.”

“Teamwork!” they all said.

Gigi stretched her long neck. She felt the cool air on her face and heard the soft swish of the curtain. The red truck shimmered. Gigi whispered, “Almost.” She touched the truck with the very tip of her nose.

“Steady,” said Hugo in a low, calm voice.

“I am here,” said Bella, holding the ramp.

“You can do it,” squeaked Mimi.

Gigi nudged, then gently rolled the truck onto the ramp. The wheels squeaked. Squeak, squeak. The truck began to roll down, slow and safe.

“Catch,” said Gigi.

Mimi guided the front wheel. Hugo kept the ramp still. Bella cheered softly, “You’ve got it, little truck.”

The truck reached the bottom and stopped in Gigi’s hoof. Everyone smiled. The room felt warm and bright. The toy was safe. Their bodies were safe. The shelf was safe. They had used what they had and not wasted a thing.

Teacher Rosa beamed. “That was wonderful teamwork. You made a plan. You were fair. You were careful. You were generous.”

Gigi nodded. “We will all get a turn. That is just and kind.” She placed the truck in the middle of the rug.

Mimi said, “You can go first, Gigi. You reached it.”

Gigi shook her head and smiled. “Thank you, Mimi. But let’s be generous. Hugo can go first because he waited so patiently. Then Bella. Then you. I will go last.”

Hugo’s eyes grew shiny. “Thank you, friends.” He pushed the truck gently. “Nee-naw, nee-naw,” he hummed, and then passed it to Bella without being asked.

The turns were smooth and happy. No one rushed. No one grabbed. They shared smiles, not tears. The nursery felt full of kindness.

At the end, Gigi said, “What did we learn?”

Mimi said, “Teamwork makes hard things safe and easy.”

Bella said, “Justice means fair turns.”

Hugo said, “Generosity means we share.”

Teacher Rosa added, “Sustainability means we use what we have and care for our things. Safety means we care for our bodies and our friends.”

Gigi wrapped her long neck around her friends in a cozy hug. “Together, we can do big things.”

The End.
