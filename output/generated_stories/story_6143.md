# Ollie Otter's Dinner Table Adventure

The big dining room table sparkled with clean plates and shiny forks. Mama Otter called from the kitchen, "Dinner time, everyone!" The smell of warm fish soup filled the air.

Ollie Otter bounced into the dining room on his little webbed feet. His whiskers twitched with excitement. "I'm so hungry!" he squeaked, jumping up onto his chair.

Papa Otter and Sister Otter came in too. Papa carried a steaming pot that smelled wonderful. Sister had a basket of warm bread rolls that made Ollie's tummy rumble.

"Ooh! Ooh! I want the biggest bowl!" Ollie reached across the table with both paws. His chair wobbled back and forth. "Give me soup first! I'm the hungriest!"

"Whoa there, little otter," Papa said gently. His deep voice was kind but firm. "What do we do before we eat?"

Ollie's paws froze in the air. He looked around the table. Mama and Papa and Sister all had their paws folded quietly. They were waiting.

"Oh," Ollie whispered. His cheeks felt warm. "We wait for everyone to sit down."

"That's right," Mama smiled. "And why do we wait?"

Ollie thought hard. His tail swished behind his chair. "Because... because it's respectful?"

"Exactly!" Sister clapped her paws together. "When we wait for everyone, it shows we care about each other."

Papa began serving the soup. The ladle made gentle splashing sounds. "Who would like to be served first?" he asked.

Ollie's paw shot up again. "Me! Me!" But then he stopped. He looked at Mama's patient face and Papa's kind eyes.

"Actually," Ollie said slowly, "maybe Mama should go first? She cooked the yummy soup."

Mama's whiskers twitched with pride. "Thank you, Ollie. That's very thoughtful."

As Papa served Mama first, then Sister, then himself, Ollie waited. His tummy growled, but he kept his paws folded. Finally, Papa filled Ollie's bowl with the steaming, delicious soup.

"This tastes even better when we all eat together," Ollie said, taking a careful sip. The soup was warm and salty and perfect.

"You know what, Ollie?" Sister said, passing him a soft roll. "When you wait your turn, it makes dinner more special for everyone."

Ollie nodded, his mouth full of bread. He had learned something important today. Taking turns wasn't just a rule - it was a way to show love.

"Can I help clear the table when we're done?" Ollie asked. "I want to be respectful like a big otter."

Papa ruffled the fur on Ollie's head. "Of course you can help. That would be wonderful."

As the family ate together, sharing stories and laughs, Ollie felt warm inside. Not just from the soup, but from knowing he had made his family proud. Being respectful and taking turns made everything more fun.

When dinner was over, Ollie carefully carried his bowl to the kitchen. His little paws held it steady and safe.

"Thank you for teaching me about respect," Ollie told his family. "Tomorrow I'll remember to wait my turn right away!"

The End.
