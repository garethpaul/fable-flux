# Ollie the Otter and the Bathroom Bubble Line

Ollie the Otter loved to splash. He had soft brown fur, bright curious eyes, and a wiggle in his whiskers when he was excited. Today was Bath Time Day at the big, shiny bathroom. The tiles were cool and smooth, the mirror gleamed, and the air smelled like sweet soap and warm towels.

“Mmm, bubbles!” Ollie chirped, bouncing in place. He heard the bathwater gurgle and saw tiny bubbles pop-pop-pop. “I want to go first!”

Mama Otter smiled and held up a finned paw. “We take turns, Ollie. We show respect for others. Respect means we listen, we wait, and we care.”

Ollie shifted from paw to paw. Waiting felt big and wiggly. He peeked at his friends. Tia the Turtle sat by the sink, tapping her shell softly, “Tip tap.” Benny the Bunny brushed his teeth, “Swish, swish,” and hummed a little tune. Sunny the Seal folded towels, “Fluff, fluff,” and placed them in a neat stack.

“Can I be first? Please?” Ollie asked, his tail swishing.

Tia the Turtle blinked slowly. “I am almost done washing my shell. You can be next if we make a line.”

“A line?” Ollie asked.

“A line means taking turns,” Mama said gently. “First, second, third. We respect each other’s time and space.”

Ollie looked at the shiny floor and imagined a line made of bubble dots. He took a breath. “Okay. I can wait. I will be next.”

He stood behind Tia and tried to be still, but his paws felt jumpy. He listened to the water go “whoosh,” to the toothbrush go “swish,” and to Sunny gently fold, “fluff.” The bathroom felt busy and cozy, like a warm hug.

“I know a waiting game,” Sunny the Seal said with a smile. “Let’s count bubbles. One, two, three…”

“Four, five, six!” Ollie chimed in, giggling. The bubbles sparkled like tiny stars, and his wiggles slowed. Waiting felt easier with a game.

Tia finished and turned to Ollie. “Your turn,” she said kindly. “Thank you for waiting.”

Ollie stood taller. “Thank you, Tia.” His voice felt proud and warm. He climbed into the tub. The water hugged him, and he felt the silky soap slide across his paws. “Ooh! Warm like sunshine!”

“Let’s learn while you wash,” Mama said. “Knowledge helps us be responsible.”

Ollie nodded, splashing a little. “What is responsible?”

“Responsible means you do what you need to do, even when it’s not the most fun,” Mama explained. “You wash all the places, you use only what you need, and you leave things ready for the next friend.”

Ollie thought and touched his whiskers. “So I respect others by taking turns and by leaving the bathroom nice?”

“Yes,” said Mama. “That is respect too.”

Ollie tried to be careful. He used one small squeeze of soap. “Plip.” He scrubbed behind his ears. “Scrub-scrub.” He washed his toes. “Wiggle-wiggle.” He rinsed his whiskers. “Drip, drip.”

Benny the Bunny hopped closer, eyes bright. “Ollie, may I have the blue cup when you’re done?”

Ollie looked at the blue cup floating by his paw. He wanted to use it again. He felt the tug of wanting. He took a deep breath and remembered. Respect means we listen, we wait, and we care.

“Yes, Benny,” Ollie said. “I will hand it to you when I finish rinsing my head.”

“Thank you,” Benny replied, ears perking up.

Ollie poured one last cup of warm water on his head. “Whoosh.” Then he handed the blue cup to Benny with a smile. “Here you go.”

Benny took the cup. “You’re a good friend.”

Ollie’s heart felt bubbly and big.

After the bath, Ollie stepped onto the soft mat. “Ahh,” he sighed, feeling the towel wrap around him. Sunny passed him a towel from the neat stack. “All dry!” Ollie chirped.

“Now for responsible clean-up,” Mama said. “What do we do?”

Ollie looked around. There were drops on the floor, a soap cap open, and a brush by the sink. He nodded. “I can help.” He put the soap cap on. “Click.” He wiped the tiny puddles. “Swish.” He placed the brush by the cup. “There.” He even straightened the towels. “Fluff, fluff.”

The bathroom looked calm and shiny again. Tia smiled. “Thank you, Ollie. You respected us and the bathroom.”

Ollie felt proud. “Respect means I listen, I wait, and I care,” he said. “And taking turns makes everyone feel happy.”

Mama Otter hugged him, warm and gentle. “You learned with your heart and your mind. That is knowledge.”

Benny waved the blue cup. “My turn is done. Sunny, do you want a turn?”

Sunny nodded. “Yes, please.” They all made a little line again—first, second, third—and the bathroom filled with soft giggles and the friendly sounds of “whoosh,” “swish,” and “fluff.”

Ollie looked in the mirror at his bright, clean face. He wiggled his whiskers and said, “Today I learned respect. I can wait, I can share, and I can help.”

Mama smiled at all of them. “When we respect each other, the day feels smooth and kind.”

Ollie’s tail made a happy swoosh. “I like smooth and kind,” he said.

They all agreed. The bathroom sparkled, their hearts felt light, and the bubble line made bath time fun for everyone.

The End.
