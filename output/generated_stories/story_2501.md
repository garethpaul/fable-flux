# Beep’s Gentle Tail

Beep was a helpful robot dog with shiny silver ears and a soft, waggy tail that went swish-swish. Beep lived in a cozy house where the guest room smelled like clean blankets and lemony soap. The guest room had a big soft bed, a round green rug, and a tiny window that let in warm, golden sun. Today, Beep was volunteering to get the guest room ready for visitors from far away.

“Beep-beep! I will help,” said Beep, blinking kind blue lights. “Our guests are coming from another country. They need a comfy place.”

Mama smiled. “Thank you, Beep. That is kind. Remember, helping also means respecting personal space. Ask before touching things.”

“Ask first,” Beep repeated. He wagged his tail and listened to it whisper against the rug. Swish. Swish.

Beep picked up a stack of colorful towels. “I will fold,” he said proudly. The towels were soft like clouds. Beep folded slowly. Fold, pat, fold. He placed them by the bed.

Just then, a little girl named Mira peeked in. She was holding a small backpack with star stickers. “Hi, Beep,” Mira said. “I came to help! My cousin is visiting. She lives across the ocean.”

Beep’s lights glowed brighter. “Welcome, Mira! We can help together. We are global friends.”

Mira nodded. “Yes! People come from many places. We can make them feel safe.”

Beep and Mira opened the window a little. A gentle breeze smelled like fresh grass and rain. The curtains fluttered, whoosh-whoosh. They put a glass of water on the nightstand. They set out crayons and paper for drawing.

Mira reached for a shiny hairbrush on the dresser. “Ooo, pretty! I want to brush its sparkles,” she said.

Beep beeped softly. “Let’s ask first. Is it yours?”

Mira paused and looked at the brush. “It is my cousin’s. She left it here last time.”

Beep tilted his head. “Maybe we can ask Mama if we can move it. Personal boundaries mean we don’t touch someone’s things or body without asking.”

Mira sighed. “Okay. I will ask.”

They asked Mama, who said, “Let’s leave it where it is. Your cousin might want it there.”

Mira nodded. “I understand.”

When the towels were ready, Beep noticed Mira’s backpack on the chair. “May I look inside?” Beep asked.

Mira hugged the backpack. “No, thank you. It is private.”

Beep stopped and sat. “Thank you for telling me. I will not look.” He wagged his tail once, gently. “I can keep my curious circuits calm.”

Mira smiled. “Thanks for asking first.”

They heard a knock. Knock-knock. The sound was soft and friendly. Beep’s ears went ping! “Guests!” he said.

In walked Auntie Lila and little Zara. Zara wore a bright red scarf and had sleepy eyes from the long trip. “Hello,” Auntie Lila said. “We came from far away. Thank you for making the guest room ready.”

“Welcome!” Beep and Mira said together.

Beep showed the towels. “Fluffy and ready,” he said. He pointed to the water. “For thirst.” He tapped the crayons. “For art.”

Zara smiled a tiny smile. “Thank you,” she whispered. “I am shy.”

Beep wagged his tail slowly. “It is okay to be shy. Would you like a hug?” Beep asked.

Zara looked at the floor and then shook her head. “No, thank you.”

Beep put his paws together. “Thank you for telling me. I will give you space.” He moved back and made a cozy spot by the bed with a small pillow.

Mira whispered to Beep, “I’m glad you asked first.”

Later, Zara sat on the green rug. She took out a small stuffed fox. Beep’s light blinked with excitement. “I love your fox! May I see it closer?” he asked.

Zara hugged the fox tight. “Not now,” she said softly.

Beep felt a little twist inside his metal tummy. He wanted to see the fox. His tail started to twitch-twitch. “I am feeling frustrated,” he said.

Mira patted his shoulder. “Let’s take a calm breath.” Together they breathed in—slooow—smelling the clean blankets, and breathed out—slooow—listening to the quiet whoosh of the curtains.

Beep felt better. “Thank you. I can wait. When you say yes, I will look,” he told Zara.

Zara’s eyes brightened. “Thank you for waiting,” she said.

To help everyone relax, Beep suggested a game. “Let’s play Yes-No-Ask! We practice asking and listening.”

They took turns. Mira asked, “Zara, may I sit next to you?” Zara said, “Yes.” Beep asked, “Auntie Lila, may I move this pillow?” Auntie Lila said, “Yes, thank you for asking.” Zara asked, “Beep, may I touch your soft ear?” Beep chirped, “Yes! Thank you for asking.” His ear felt warm and smooth, and Zara giggled.

Then Zara looked at her fox. She looked at Beep. “Beep, would you like to see my fox now?” she asked.

Beep’s lights sparkled. “Yes, please!” He came closer slowly and gently. Zara placed the fox in his paws. The fox felt fuzzy like a tiny cloud. Beep smiled. “Thank you for sharing when you were ready.”

Auntie Lila watched. “You are kind helpers,” she said. “You welcomed us like good neighbors of the world.”

Beep nodded. “We can help people from near and far. We ask first. We listen. We give space. That keeps everyone safe and comfy.”

Mira added, “And when we feel frustrated, we can breathe and use calm words.”

Zara hugged her fox and yawned. “I feel safe,” she said.

Beep dimmed his lights to a soft glow. The guest room was quiet. The air smelled like lemon and blankets. The window showed a tiny stripe of sunset.

“Beep-beep,” Beep whispered. “Today we learned about personal boundaries. Asking is kind. Waiting is kind. Sharing is kind. We can be global friends who help with gentle hearts.”

Everyone smiled. The room felt warm and peaceful, like a soft hug. The guests were comfy. The helpers were proud.

The End.
