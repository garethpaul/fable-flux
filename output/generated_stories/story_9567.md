# Sora the Helpful Squirrel

Sora was a small, resourceful squirrel with bright eyes and a fluffy, swishy tail. One sunny morning, Sora peeked into the quiet guest room. The room smelled like clean sheets and lemons. The curtains were soft and blue. A big bed sat in the middle, and a little suitcase rested on a chair. Today, Grandma Mei was coming to visit.

Sora tapped her paws together. “I want to help,” she whispered. “Helping feels good.”

Sora climbed onto the bed. “Hello, bed,” she giggled. “Let’s get ready.” She smoothed the blanket with her tiny paws. Swish, smooth, swish. The blanket felt warm and cozy. She puffed the pillows. Poof! Poof! The pillows turned round and puffy like clouds. Sora smiled. “Soft and comfy,” she said.

On the table, Sora found a small notepad and a pencil. She drew a big heart. Then she thought, “I can write a welcome note!” Sora loved words. She loved how they sounded. She loved how they could share feelings.

She wrote slowly, saying each word out loud. “Wel-come, Grand-ma Mei.” She sounded each piece. “Welcome” sounded like a warm hug. “Grandma” felt gentle and kind. “Mei” sounded like a bell. Ding! She added a little squirrel drawing and a flower. “Words are friends,” Sora said softly. “They help us say what we feel.”

Sora looked around. She saw a book on the shelf with shiny gold letters. She ran a paw along the spine. It felt smooth and cool. “Stories!” she chirped. She placed the book on the bedside table for Grandma Mei to read.

Then Sora heard a tiny clink. The vase on the dresser wobbled. “Oh!” Sora hurried over. The vase was empty and a little dusty. She picked a small silk flower from the box in the closet and set it inside. “Color!” she said, smiling at the bright red petals. She used a soft cloth to dust the dresser. Wipe, wipe. The dust tickled her nose. “Ah-choo!” Sora sneezed and giggled.

Sora wanted the guest room to smell nice. She opened the window a little. A gentle breeze floated in. It carried the smell of fresh grass and sunshine. The curtains fluttered like butterfly wings. Whisper, flutter. Sora took a deep breath. “Mmm. Fresh air.”

On the chair, the little suitcase leaned to one side. Sora nudged it upright. She noticed a tiny loose screw on the chair leg. “I can fix that,” she said. Sora found the little tool box in the hall. She gripped the small screwdriver and turned it. Twist, twist. Nice and snug. “All safe,” she said proudly. She was careful and patient. She worked all by herself, but she knew when to be gentle.

Sora looked at the clock. Tick-tock, tick-tock. Grandma Mei would arrive soon. Sora thought about more ways to help. She checked the nightlight. Click. It glowed soft and warm. She placed a glass of water on a coaster. She folded a tiny towel into a simple squirrel shape and put it by the sink. “Surprise!” she whispered.

Then Sora practiced her words again. She wanted to welcome Grandma in a special way. She whispered, “Hello, Grandma Mei. I am glad you are here.” She tried saying it in another language Grandma liked. “Ni hao, Nai Nai,” she said, carefully. The words felt new and shiny on her tongue. She practiced slowly. “Ni hao, Nai Nai.” Sora smiled. “I can learn new words. Words help me be kind.”

Just then, there was a soft knock. Knock-knock. Sora’s tail swished with joy. She ran to the door. Grandma Mei stepped in. She wore a gentle smile and a soft scarf that smelled like jasmine tea.

Sora stood tall and brave. “Ni hao, Nai Nai!” she said.

Grandma Mei’s eyes sparkled. “Ni hao, Sora,” she replied. Her voice was warm like a blanket. “What a lovely room!”

Sora took her paw and showed her everything. “Look, comfy pillows. Look, welcome note. Look, book for reading. I fixed the chair. I made it safe.”

Grandma Mei read the note. “Welcome, Grandma Mei,” she read aloud. “This is beautiful, Sora. Your words are full of love.” She hugged Sora. The hug felt cozy and sweet, like honey on warm toast.

Sora’s cheeks felt toasty. “I wanted to help,” she said. “I can do little jobs. Little jobs make a big happy.”

Grandma Mei nodded. “Helping hands—and helping paws—make a home bright,” she said. She ran her fingers along the fluffy pillow. “You used your words to welcome me. You used your mind to fix the chair. You used your heart to make this room kind.”

Sora’s tail curled with pride. “I will keep learning new words,” she said. “I will keep helping.”

Grandma Mei smiled. “Every day we can learn. Every day we can help. And every day we can get a little better.”

They sat on the bed together. The room felt safe and peaceful. The breeze made the curtains whisper. The nightlight glowed softly. Sora read her note again, sounding out each word. Grandma Mei clapped, and Sora giggled.

Sora thought, Helping makes me strong. Helping makes others happy. Learning words helps me say love. She snuggled close and whispered, “Welcome home, Grandma Mei.”

Grandma Mei kissed Sora’s head. “Thank you, my helpful, resourceful squirrel.”

The guest room shone with care. Sora felt independent and proud. She had used her paws, her tools, and her words. She had made a difference, all by herself, with love.

The clear lesson was bright in her heart: Be helpful, use kind words, try new things, and keep improving, one small step at a time.

The End.
