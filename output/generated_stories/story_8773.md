# Bucky Beaver’s Bubble Balance

Bucky the busy beaver zipped into the bright bathroom. The tiles were shiny and cool under his webby feet. He liked the lemony soap smell and the warm puff of steam from the tub. It was morning, and Bucky had a lot to do.

“Brush, wash, stretch, and chew!” he sang. His tail thumped the floor with a happy thwack-thwack. He was excited, but he was also a little wiggly. His body wanted to go fast, fast, fast!

Bucky climbed onto his little blue step stool by the sink. The mirror showed his big front teeth and his wide, whiskery smile. “Time to practice balance,” he told himself. “Slow and steady, little beaver.”

He squeezed a tiny dot of minty toothpaste. Squeeze… plop! Oops! Too much! The toothpaste splashed on the counter like a white snowflake. Bucky giggled, but then he frowned. “I want to do it right.”

His friend Dot the duck waddled in. Her soft yellow feathers smelled like clean bubbles. “Good morning, Bucky!” Dot quacked. “I came to cheer you on.”

Bucky nodded. “I need help with… coordination.” He said the big word carefully. “That means my body parts work together. Hands, eyes, tail, feet!”

Dot flapped gently. “We can do it together. We can move slow and match our motions.”

Bucky tried again. “Squeeze… tiny dot… stop.” He looked with his eyes. He felt with his paw. Plip! A perfect pea-sized blob sat on his brush. “Yes!” he cheered.

“Now brush in little circles,” Dot said softly. “Round and round. Top and bottom. Left and right.”

Bucky hummed. “Round and round.” His paw moved in smooth loops. The mirror fogged a little. The mint taste tingled. Swish-swish went the brush. He spit into the sink. Patooey! A tiny splash! He wiped his mouth with a soft towel. “That felt good,” he said. “I used my eyes and my paw together.”

Dot smiled. “That’s coordination.”

Bucky hopped down to the bath mat. It was fluffy like a cloud. “Next, washing!” He turned the faucet. The water burbled and sang. Warm steam kissed his nose. He pumped soap. Pump, pump—oops! Too fast! Soap blobs leaped onto the floor. Splish!

Bucky’s tail slipped a little. “Whoa!” His heart beat fast. Dot reached out her wing. “Steady, Bucky. Feet far apart. Tail flat on the mat. Bend your knees. Eyes on the soap.”

Bucky took a slow breath. In… out… He spread his feet. He pressed his tail down like an anchor. He looked at his paws. “Pump… one… rub… rub…” His paws rubbed in circles. Tiny bubbles grew. Pop! Pop! Pop! The bubbles smelled like lemons and sunshine. He rinsed. The water felt warm and silky.

“You did it!” Dot cheered. “You used your tail to balance. That is smart beaver thinking.”

Bucky beamed. “My body can work together when I go slow.”

Dot pointed to a bright green apple on the counter. “Snack time. Good fuel helps bodies move well.”

Bucky’s tummy rumbled. “Crunch time!” He reached too fast, and the apple rolled. It tumbled toward the tub. “Oh no!”

“Eyes on the apple,” Dot quacked. “Soft paws. Bend. Catch.”

Bucky lowered his body. He watched the apple. His paws scooped. Plop! He caught it before it clunked. “Got it!” He laughed, the sound like tiny drums.

He washed the apple with a gentle rinse. “Rub, rub, rinse.” Then he took a bite. Crunch! The apple was juicy and sweet. Juice dripped down his chin. “Mmm. Apples help me grow strong and keep me going,” he said.

Dot nodded. “Good nutrition helps your brain and body. That makes coordination easier.”

Bucky chewed and smiled. “Snack and stretch?” he asked.

“Let’s do a bathroom balance game,” Dot said. “Stand on one foot. Arms out like wings.”

Bucky stood on his step stool with Dot beside him. He lifted one foot. Wobble, wobble. “Whoa…” He spread his paws and lifted his tail. He looked at a dot on the mirror. He took a breath. In… out… His body became still. “I’m doing it!”

Dot lifted one webbed foot too. “We can add a count. One… two… three…”

They counted and giggled. They switched feet. They reached up high and touched the sky. They bent low and tapped the mat. They did five gentle knee bends. Up… down… up… down… “Exercise helps our bodies learn the moves,” Dot said. “Little bits each day.”

Bucky felt warm and proud. His fur smelled clean. The bathroom was full of happy sounds—drips, bubbles, crunches, and little laughs. He washed his cup, put away his brush, and patted the counter dry. His motions were careful now. He looked, he felt, and he moved with calm.

Dot fluffed her feathers. “Look how far you came, Bucky. This morning you were fast and slippery. Now you are steady and smooth.”

Bucky grinned. “I learned to use my eyes, paws, feet, and tail together. I go slow. I breathe. I balance. And I eat a good snack.”

Dot tapped the apple core. “And you had help from a friend.”

Bucky hugged Dot gently. “Friends make practice fun.”

They gave each other a happy high-five—paw to wing. Slap!

Bucky looked in the mirror one more time. He felt strong inside. “Brush, wash, stretch, and chew,” he sang softly. “I can do it, and so can you.”

Lesson: When we move slowly, breathe, and let our body parts work together, we build coordination. Healthy food and kind friends help us learn and grow.

The End.
