# Koda's Recycling Practice

Koda the koala sat on his peaceful front porch every morning. His soft gray fur sparkled in the gentle sunlight. He loved watching the birds chirp in the eucalyptus trees. But today, Koda had a special job to do.

"Today I will sort all the recycling," Koda said to himself. He looked at the big pile of bottles, cans, and paper on his porch. "Mama said I need to practice every day to get better."

Koda picked up a blue bottle. It was smooth and cool in his fluffy paws. He placed it carefully in the blue bin. Then he found a shiny can. It made a tiny "clink" sound as he dropped it in the metal bin.

"This is easy!" Koda smiled. His black nose twitched with excitement.

But then the wind started to blow. "Whoosh!" went the breeze. Papers flew everywhere! Bottles rolled across the wooden porch. Cans scattered like marbles.

"Oh dear," Koda sighed. His ears drooped down. "Everything is mixed up again." He felt like giving up. The job seemed too hard now.

Koda's mama came outside. She had kind, twinkling eyes. "What's wrong, little one?" she asked gently.

"The wind messed up all my sorting," Koda said sadly. "I practiced so hard, but now it's all wrong again."

Mama sat down beside Koda on the wooden porch steps. The boards creaked softly under them. "Do you know what makes someone really good at something?" she asked.

Koda shook his head.

"Practice and trying again, even when things go wrong," Mama said. "Every time you practice, you get a little bit better. Even if the wind blows things around."

Koda thought about this. He watched a little ant carry a crumb. The ant dropped it, then picked it up again. The ant kept trying.

"Will you help me practice again?" Koda asked.

"Of course!" Mama smiled. "But first, let's make this easier. We can put rocks on the papers so they won't blow away."

Together, they found smooth, round rocks. Koda placed them on top of the papers. They felt warm from sitting in the sun.

"Now let's sort together," Mama said. "You can tell me where each thing goes."

Koda picked up a green bottle. "This goes in the glass bin!" he said proudly.

"Perfect!" Mama cheered.

They worked together as the morning birds sang their happy songs. Koda sorted faster and faster. He remembered where everything belonged.

"Red can goes in metal bin," Koda said. "Newspaper goes in paper bin."

When they finished, all three bins were full and neat. The porch looked clean and organized.

"You did wonderful work," Mama said, giving Koda a warm hug. "See what happens when you practice and don't give up?"

Koda felt proud and happy. His chest puffed out with confidence. "Tomorrow I want to practice again," he said. "And the next day too!"

"That's the spirit," Mama laughed. "Practice makes everything easier."

From that day on, Koda sorted recycling every morning on his peaceful porch. Some days were windy. Some days were rainy. But Koda always kept practicing. Soon, he became the best recycling sorter in the whole neighborhood.

The End.
