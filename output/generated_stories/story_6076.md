# Momo’s Colorful Choice

Momo the courageous monkey loved the creative art room. It smelled like fresh paper and lemony soap. Sunlight made bright squares on the floor. Paints in little cups looked like rainbows. Brushes stood in a jar like tiny trees.

“Good morning, colors!” Momo chirped, tail swishing.

Ms. Dot, the gentle art teacher, smiled. “Good morning, Momo. Today we’ll make a big friendship poster. Everyone will add one special picture.”

Momo’s heart did a happy hop. “I want to paint a giant banana boat! With blue waves! Splash, splash!”

On the big table, Momo saw three shiny things:
- A big, soft brush
- A small, pointy brush
- A glittery gold sticker sheet

A sign said, “Pick one tool to share.” Momo’s eyes grew round. “Only one?” he whispered.

Lulu the lamb stood nearby. “I like the gold stickers,” she said softly.

Pip the penguin waddled in. “I need the small brush for tiny stars,” he peeped.

Momo looked at the tools. His paws tingled. The big brush would make fast, splashy waves. The small brush could make tiny foam bubbles. The gold stickers could make the sun sparkle. Momo felt a little twisty inside. So many choices!

Ms. Dot came closer. “When we have choices,” she said gently, “we stop, we think, and we choose with our hearts. We can ask: What do I want? What do others need? What helps our whole team?”

Momo took a slow breath. He listened. He heard water dripping in the sink, a crinkle from the paper roll, and his own calm breath. He looked at Lulu’s hopeful eyes and Pip’s little flippers.

“I want the big brush,” Momo said. His tail curled. “But Lulu really loves the stickers. And Pip needs the small brush.”

Momo tapped his chin. “I can be brave and generous. I can make a kind choice.”

He smiled. “I choose the big, soft brush,” he said, “but I will share it!” He held it up. “We can take turns. I will use it for waves. Then Pip can use the tip for small splashes. And Lulu—” Momo looked at the sticker sheet. “Would you like the gold stickers? They will make your sun shine.”

Lulu’s woolly face glowed. “Thank you, Momo!”

Pip clapped his flippers. “And thank you for sharing the big brush. Taking turns is smart!”

Momo’s tummy felt warm, like cocoa. He had made a decision. He had thought about himself and about his friends.

They started. Shh, shh went the big brush as Momo painted smooth blue waves. Splash went the paint as he dabbed foamy white at the tips. The paper felt cool under his paws. The room smelled like sweet glue and oranges as someone opened a new bottle.

“Your waves sound like the ocean,” Ms. Dot said.

Momo giggled. “Listen! Shh, shh, splash!”

“May I try now?” Pip asked.

“Of course,” Momo said, handing the brush with care. “Your turn.”

Pip made tiny splashes next to the boat. Tap, tap, tap went the brush. Lulu peeled a gold sticker. Peel, peel. She pressed it down. Press! The gold sun gleamed.

“Oh!” Momo gasped. “The sun looks like warm honey.”

Lulu beamed. “Your boat looks strong and brave.”

Just then, Tiny Tessa the turtle rolled in a tray of beads. “I only have one cup of blue beads,” she said. “Who wants them?”

Momo remembered Ms. Dot’s words. Stop. Think. Choose with your heart.

He looked at the blue beads. They would make perfect raindrops. He looked at Tessa’s slow smile. He looked at the poster.

Momo said, “Let’s sprinkle some for rain, and save some for Tessa’s bracelet.” He held out the cup. “We can share.”

Tessa’s eyes twinkled. “That is very kind.”

They sprinkled beads. Tinkle, tinkle. Pip added tiny star dots with the small brush. Dot, dot, dot. Lulu stuck one last gold star near the sun. Shine!

Soon, the poster showed a banana boat riding brave blue waves under a golden sun, with gentle rain and twinkly stars. It looked happy. It felt like teamwork.

Ms. Dot clapped. “Look at your beautiful friendship poster! What did you learn today?”

Momo stood tall. “I learned to make a good choice. I stopped. I thought. I chose with my heart. I shared the brush. I let Lulu have the stickers. I saved beads for Tessa. That felt kind and generous.”

Pip nodded. “And taking turns helped everyone.”

Lulu added, “Sharing made our art better.”

Ms. Dot gave them a big, soft hug. “Courage is not only climbing high,” she said. “Courage is making thoughtful choices that help the whole group.”

Momo’s tail made a happy loop. He looked at his friends. He looked at the shiny poster. He felt proud and peaceful.

He whispered to the colors, “Thank you for helping me decide.”

The poster dried in the sunny square on the floor. The room smelled like paint and a little like magic. Everyone smiled.

And from that day, when Momo saw many choices, he remembered: Stop. Think. Choose with your heart. Be kind. Be generous. Take turns.

The End.
