# Panda Poppy’s Car-Ride Rules

Panda Poppy was a soft, round panda with bright eyes and calm paws. Today, Poppy climbed into the family car with Mama Panda, Papa Panda, and Baby Bean, the little bamboo-colored cub. The sun felt warm on Poppy’s fur, and the seat smelled like clean lemons.

“We are going to the park,” said Mama Panda, buckling her seatbelt with a click. “First, we follow our car-ride rules.”

Poppy nodded. “Seatbelts on. Quiet voices. Kind turns.” Poppy’s voice was gentle, like a soft drum.

Baby Bean wiggled. “Snack, please!” His tummy made a tiny grumble.

“In a moment,” said Papa Panda. “First, we start safe.”

Poppy waited. Waiting was one of Poppy’s best skills. Poppy took a slow, deep breath—smell the flowers, blow the wind. The seatbelt hugged Poppy’s belly. Click. Safe.

The car hummed and rolled down the street. Trees waved green paws outside the window. Poppy watched the leaves dance and felt the cool air from the little vent tickle whiskers.

“Now it is snack time,” said Mama Panda. She held up two small snack boxes. One had crunchy carrot sticks. One had sweet apple slices. “We share and we take turns. That is fair.”

Baby Bean reached fast. “Apples! Mine!” His paw grabbed the apple box.

Poppy blinked and smiled. “Baby Bean, can I please have some apples too?” Poppy’s voice was calm and respectful. “You can have some carrots from me.”

Baby Bean frowned. “But I want all the apples.”

Papa Panda looked at them in the mirror. “We are fair in our family. Everyone gets a turn.”

Poppy nodded. “We can make a plan. Baby Bean, you take three apple slices. I take three apple slices. Then we trade.”

Mama Panda counted out loud. “One, two, three for Bean. One, two, three for Poppy.” The apple slices smelled like sunshine. Poppy crunched and listened to the happy snap. Baby Bean crunched too and smiled, juice shining on his chin.

“Thank you for sharing,” said Mama Panda. “That is respect.”

Poppy felt warm in the chest. Respect felt like a soft blanket. It meant using kind words and kind eyes.

At a red light, the car stopped. “Red means rest,” said Papa Panda. He drummed softly on the steering wheel. Tap, tap.

Baby Bean kicked the back of Poppy’s seat. Thump. Thump. Thump.

Poppy turned and kept a kind face. “Baby Bean, please stop kicking my seat. My back is there. It hurts.” Poppy placed a paw on the seat to show where.

Baby Bean’s eyes grew wide. “Oh.” He tucked his feet back. “Sorry, Poppy.”

“Thank you for listening,” said Poppy. “That is respect too. My body is important.”

The light turned green. Go! The car hummed again. Poppy watched a dog with floppy ears trot on the sidewalk. The dog’s tag jingled like tiny bells. Poppy smiled.

Soon, Papa Panda said, “We need to make a quiet stop at the drive-up window. We will pick up water bottles. Healthy sips!”

The car rolled forward slowly. Baby Bean started to sing very loud. “LA LA LA!”

Poppy held up a paw. “Bean, the person at the window is working. Let’s use inside voices. That is respectful.”

Baby Bean lowered his voice. “la la la.” He giggled.

At the window, a friendly raccoon in a hat passed Papa Panda two cold water bottles and a bag with napkins. “Here you go! Have a sunny day!”

“Thank you,” said Papa Panda.

“Thank you,” said Poppy, eyes bright. The bottle felt cool and smooth. Poppy took a sip. The water was fresh and made Poppy’s mouth happy. “Ahhh.”

Mama Panda passed a small trash bag back. “Apple cores and napkins go in here. We keep the car clean. Healthy habits!”

Poppy carefully put the tiny apple seeds in the bag. “Clean car, calm minds,” Poppy said, patting the bag closed. Baby Bean copied, placing his sticky napkin in the bag. He beamed. “I can help!”

The park sign came into view. Big green letters waved hello. But the parking lot was busy. Several cars waited for spots.

Papa Panda breathed in. “We will be patient. We wait our turn.”

Poppy nodded and whispered to Bean, “Let’s play the Quiet Colors Game while we wait.” Poppy pointed out the window. “I see a blue car.”

“I see a yellow hat,” said Baby Bean.

“I hear chirping birds,” said Poppy, cupping an ear. Tweet-tweet! The sound was like little tickles in the air.

A car pulled out. Another car zoomed toward the open spot. Papa Panda slowed and lifted a paw, letting the other car go first. “They were here before us. Fair is fair.”

Poppy felt proud. “We waited. We respected the line.”

At last, it was their turn. Papa Panda parked. Click went the gear. Everyone unbuckled and stretched. The fresh park air smelled like grass and sunshine.

Mama Panda knelt and looked at the cubs. “What did we learn today?”

Poppy thought. “Respect means kind words, kind turns, and kind bodies.”

“And healthy habits,” added Baby Bean. He wiggled his water bottle. “Sip, buckles, clean-up!”

“And fairness,” said Papa Panda. “Waiting our turn and sharing the road.”

Poppy hugged Baby Bean. “We did it together,” Poppy said softly.

Baby Bean hugged back. “We are a fair and respectful team!”

They walked toward the swings, paws together, hearts light. The day felt bright, and the family felt even brighter.

The End.
