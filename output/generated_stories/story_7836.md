# Momo the Monkey's Safe Room Adventure

Momo was a brave little monkey with soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes. Today, he was staying in Grandma's cozy guest room while his parents went shopping. The room smelled like lavender and had pretty blue curtains that danced in the gentle breeze.

"I want to explore!" Momo said, bouncing on the soft bed. But then he remembered what Mama had taught him: "Always be safe when you're somewhere new, Momo."

Momo looked around the guest room carefully. He saw a tall bookshelf filled with colorful books, a rocking chair with fluffy cushions, and a small table with a pretty lamp. Everything looked so interesting!

First, Momo wanted to climb the bookshelf to reach the highest books. But he stopped and thought, "Is this safe?" The bookshelf was very tall and wobbly-looking. 

"No," Momo said to himself. "That could fall and hurt me. I should ask Grandma for help if I want those books."

Next, Momo noticed some electrical cords near the wall. They looked like fun vines to swing on! But again, he remembered Mama's safety lessons.

"Electrical cords are not toys," Momo said, shaking his head. "They could give me an owie. I'll stay away from those."

Then Momo saw the rocking chair. It looked perfect for a little monkey! He climbed up carefully, holding the armrests with both hands. The chair rocked gently back and forth.

"This feels nice and safe," Momo smiled, enjoying the smooth rocking motion. The chair creaked softly, like a gentle song.

Suddenly, Momo spotted something shiny under the bed. It was small and round, like a button. His curious monkey brain wanted to pick it up and maybe taste it.

But Momo stopped again. "Wait! Small things could be dangerous. I might choke on it, or it might not be good for monkeys to eat."

Instead of picking up the shiny object, Momo called out, "Grandma! There's something small under the bed!"

Grandma's footsteps came down the hallway. "What is it, dear?" she asked, peeking into the room.

"I found something shiny, but I didn't touch it because it might not be safe," Momo explained proudly.

Grandma smiled warmly. "Oh my! That's my lost earring! Thank you for being so careful and smart, Momo. You're absolutely right - small objects can be dangerous for little ones."

Grandma picked up the earring safely. "I'm so proud of you for thinking before acting. That shows real courage!"

"But I didn't do anything brave," Momo said, tilting his head.

"Oh, but you did!" Grandma explained, sitting in the rocking chair and lifting Momo onto her lap. "Being brave doesn't mean doing dangerous things. Real courage means making smart, safe choices even when you want to do something else."

Momo felt warm and happy. The guest room didn't feel new and scary anymore - it felt safe and cozy.

"Can you read me a story from one of those books?" Momo asked.

"Of course! Let me get one from the lower shelf," Grandma said. She chose a book with pictures of animals and began reading in her gentle voice.

As Grandma read, Momo learned that being safe was the best way to have fun adventures. He had explored the guest room, found Grandma's earring, and learned important lessons - all while staying safe and sound.

"Tomorrow, I want to practice more safety skills," Momo yawned sleepily.

"That's my brave little monkey," Grandma whispered, giving him a soft hug.

The End.
