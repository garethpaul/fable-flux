# Mila Mouse and the Lonely Guest Room

In a cozy house with yellow walls and soft carpets, there lived a kind little mouse named Mila. She had the fluffiest gray fur and the tiniest pink nose that twitched when she was thinking hard.

Mila loved exploring every corner of the house, but her favorite place was the guest room. It had a big, bouncy bed with blue pillows that smelled like lavender, and sunshine that danced through the white curtains.

One morning, Mila heard sniffling sounds coming from the guest room. *Sniffle, sniffle.* She poked her whiskers around the door and saw a small brown cricket sitting all alone in the corner.

"Hello there," said Mila softly, her voice gentle like a whisper. "I'm Mila. What's your name?"

The cricket looked up with sad, shiny eyes. "I'm Charlie," he said quietly. "I'm visiting this house, but I don't think anyone wants me here."

Mila's heart felt heavy like a stone. "Why do you think that, Charlie?"

"Well," Charlie's voice got even smaller, "yesterday I tried to join the other bugs playing hide-and-seek in the kitchen. But they said I was too loud when I chirp. Then I wanted to help the ants carry crumbs, but they said I was too small. I don't fit in anywhere."

Mila sat down next to Charlie, feeling the cool wooden floor under her tiny paws. She thought very hard, her pink nose twitching fast.

"Charlie," she said honestly, "I understand how you feel. Sometimes I feel left out too because I'm small. But you know what? Being different makes us special, not less important."

Charlie's antennae perked up a little. "Really?"

"Really!" Mila's eyes sparkled like stars. "Let me tell you a secret. I have a plan, but I need to be honest with you about something first."

Charlie leaned closer, curious.

"I was scared to tell the other animals about my favorite hiding spot because I thought they might take it away from me," Mila admitted. "But keeping secrets made me lonely too. Want to help me share it with everyone?"

Charlie nodded eagerly, his sadness starting to melt away like ice cream in the sun.

Mila led Charlie to a magical space behind the guest room's big dresser. It was like a secret fort with dust bunnies as soft as clouds and streams of golden sunlight.

"This could be our special meeting place," Mila explained. "A place where everyone is welcome, no matter how they look or sound."

Together, they invited all the house creatures: the busy ants, the colorful beetles, the gentle spiders, and even the shy silverfish. At first, some animals weren't sure about including everyone.

"But Charlie chirps too loud," said one ant.

"And the silverfish moves too slowly," added a beetle.

Mila stood up tall on her hind legs. "That's exactly why we need everyone! Charlie's chirping can be our music. The silverfish's careful movements can teach us to be gentle. We're all different, and that makes us stronger together."

Soon, the guest room filled with laughter and happy sounds. Charlie's chirping became the soundtrack for their games. The silverfish taught everyone how to move gracefully. The ants shared their teamwork skills, and the beetles brought beautiful colors to their fort.

As the sun set through the curtains, painting everything orange and pink, Charlie smiled his biggest smile ever.

"Thank you, Mila," he said. "You showed me that being honest about our feelings and including everyone makes everything more fun!"

From that day on, the guest room became the happiest place in the whole house, where every creature was welcomed, valued, and loved.

The End.
