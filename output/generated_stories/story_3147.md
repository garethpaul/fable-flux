# Ellie’s Practice Plan

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, she walked into the musical practice room. The room smelled like polished wood. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Drums, shakers, and a shiny xylophone sat in a neat row. Ellie’s heart went thump-thump with happy beats.

“Today, I want to play a song for everyone,” Ellie said, swishing her tail. “But first, I need a plan.”

She looked up at the big clock with friendly hands. Tick-tock, tick-tock. It was almost practice time for the whole animal band: Mira the monkey, Leo the lion, Aya the ant, and Timo the turtle.

Ellie opened a blue folder. Inside was her practice list. She read slowly, “Step one: warm up. Step two: tune. Step three: practice slow. Step four: practice together. Step five: smile and share.”

She took a deep breath through her trunk. “I can do this,” she whispered.

First, Ellie warmed up. “Bum-bum-bum,” she hummed softly, tapping the drum with two gentle beats. Tap-tap. Tap-tap. Her ears wiggled with the rhythm. She stretched her trunk like a long ribbon and rolled her shoulders. It felt good to get ready.

Next, she tuned the xylophone. “Ding… dong… ting…” Each bar sang a tiny rainbow sound. “I’m listening,” Ellie said. “I’m preparing.” She used her trunk to tap lightly. The notes sounded smooth and bright, like sun on water.

Mira the monkey swung in with a jingling bell bracelet. “Hi, Ellie! Let’s play fast!” Mira shook her bells. Jingle-jingle!

Ellie smiled kindly. “Hi, Mira. I want to play fast too. But first, I need to prepare. I’m on step three: practice slow.”

Mira bounced. “Slow? That’s not fun!”

Ellie nodded. “It helps me learn. Want to try with me?” She tapped a simple beat: tap… tap… tap… Mira listened, then shook her bells slowly, jingle… jingle… jingle…

“Oh! I hear it,” Mira said. “Slow helps me stay steady.”

Leo the lion padded in, his mane soft like a cloud. He carried a small drum. “Roar—um, I mean, hello,” he said shyly. “I like loud drums!”

Ellie giggled. “Loud is fun, Leo. But we are preparing. Let’s try soft first.” She tapped soft-soft, like butterfly feet. Leo copied her. “Soft is cozy,” he said, smiling.

Aya the ant marched in with a tiny triangle that went ting! Timo the turtle followed with a gentle shaker, swish-swish.

Ellie checked her list. “We are on step four: practice together.”

Mira, Leo, Aya, and Timo stood in a circle. Ellie said, “Let’s talk about our plan.” She drew a little map on a white board: warm-up, tune, slow, together, share. “We are a team,” she said. “We can listen and take turns.”

Mira raised her hand. “Can I start the beat?”

Leo raised his paw. “Can I make it strong?”

Aya waved an antenna. “Can I add a tiny sparkle?”

Timo blinked slowly. “Can I keep the steady swish?”

Ellie nodded. “Yes to all! We will prepare by counting. One, two, three, four.” Her trunk pointed kindly around the circle.

They began. Tap… jingle… ting… swish… The room filled with soft sounds. It felt like warm tea in their bellies. But then, Mira got excited and sped up. Jingle-jingle-jingle! Leo heard and got louder. Boom-BOOM! The beat tangled. Aya squeaked, “Oh no!” Timo stopped. Swish…

The music sounded messy. Everyone frowned.

Ellie took a deep breath. “Friends,” she said calmly, “we have a little problem. Our music got too fast and too loud. Let’s solve it together.”

Mira’s shoulders drooped. “I’m sorry. I went too fast.”

Leo looked at his paws. “I’m sorry. I went too loud.”

Ellie gently touched their shoulders with her trunk. “Thank you for saying that. It is okay. We are learning. Let’s use our plan.” She pointed to the board. “Step three: practice slow. Step four: practice together. We can do it.”

She taught a “global count,” a way everyone could use, no matter where they came from. “Let’s try clapping and counting in numbers we all know: one, two, three, four,” Ellie said. “Then we play. We will listen to each other. We will take turns. We will leave space.”

They tried again. Ellie counted: “One… two… three… four…” Mira watched Ellie’s trunk move like a gentle conductor. Jingle… Leo breathed and played soft. Boom… Aya added tiny ting! like stars. Timo kept the heartbeat swish… The sounds fit together like a puzzle.

Soon, the music felt like a friendly walk. Step, step, step, step. Ellie’s ears wiggled. “Yes!” she cheered. “We prepared, and it worked!”

At the end, Ellie looked at her list. “Step five: smile and share.” She turned to her friends. “Would you like to share our song with the school?”

Mira nodded. “We learned to go slow.”

Leo rumbled, “We learned to listen.”

Aya chirped, “We learned to take turns.”

Timo smiled, “We learned to prepare.”

They stood tall and played their song. The musical practice room filled with warm notes and happy hearts. Friends from many places listened, clapped, and smiled. Some wore bright scarves. Some spoke in different words. But everyone understood the steady beat and the friendly faces. The music brought them together.

After the song, Ellie said, “Preparation helps us do our best. When we plan, listen, and work together, we can make kind music for everyone.”

Her friends cheered. “Thank you, Ellie!”

Ellie tucked her blue folder away. She felt proud and calm. Tick-tock, tick-tock, the clock sang. It was time to rest, and time to smile.

The End.
