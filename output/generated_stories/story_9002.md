# Leo's Dance of Sorry

In a quiet attic filled with dusty sunbeams lived Leo, a brave little lion cub with golden fur and a fluffy mane. His whiskers twitched when he was curious, and his tail swished when he was excited.

Leo loved the peaceful attic. Soft shadows danced on wooden floors. Old curtains smelled like lavender. Gentle creaks and squeaks made cozy music.

Every morning, Leo practiced his special dance routine. Step, step, twirl! Pounce, wiggle, roar! He was getting ready for Grandma Lion's birthday party.

"I want to make the perfect dance for Grandma," Leo said, practicing his coordination. "Step, step, twirl!"

But this morning, something went wrong. Leo spun too fast during his big twirl. His paws got tangled up!

CRASH!

Leo tumbled into a stack of Grandma's favorite photo albums. Pictures scattered everywhere like falling leaves. Some photos got bent. Others got dusty.

"Oh no!" Leo's whiskers drooped. "I broke Grandma's special pictures!"

Leo felt terrible. His tummy felt heavy and sad. These weren't just any pictures - they showed all of Grandma's happy memories with her family.

"What should I do?" Leo whispered to himself. "Grandma will be so upset."

Leo thought about hiding the mess. He thought about blaming the wind. But deep in his brave little heart, he knew what was right.

"I need to make amends," Leo said firmly. "I need to fix what I broke."

First, Leo carefully picked up each photo. His paws were gentle and slow. He looked at each picture - Grandma as a young lion, family picnics, birthday parties.

"These memories are so precious," Leo realized.

Next, Leo found a soft cloth. He dusted each photo with tiny, careful movements. His coordination was much better when he went slowly.

Then Leo organized the photos back into the albums. He practiced putting them in the right order. Some showed Grandma growing up. Others showed holidays and celebrations.

But Leo wanted to do more than just clean up. He wanted to make something special to show how sorry he felt.

Leo found colorful paper and crayons in an old art box. He drew a beautiful picture of himself and Grandma dancing together. At the top, he wrote in his best letters: "I'M SORRY, GRANDMA. I LOVE YOU."

When Grandma Lion climbed up to the attic that afternoon, Leo was waiting.

"Grandma," Leo said, his voice small but brave. "I made a mistake. I knocked over your photo albums while practicing my dance. I'm very sorry."

Grandma's eyes were kind and warm. "Thank you for telling me the truth, Leo."

"I cleaned everything up," Leo continued. "And I made this for you." He showed her his colorful drawing.

Grandma's face lit up like sunshine. "Oh, Leo! This is beautiful. I can see how hard you worked to make things right."

"I learned that when I make a mistake, I should fix it and say sorry," Leo said proudly.

"That's exactly right," Grandma purred. "Making amends shows how much you care. Now, would you like to practice your dance routine together? We can go nice and slow."

Leo's tail swished with joy. "Yes! Step, step, twirl - but carefully this time!"

From that day on, Leo always remembered: when you make a mistake, the brave thing to do is make it right. And practicing slowly helps you do your best.

The End.
