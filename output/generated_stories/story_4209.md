# Finn's Garden Shed Adventure

Little Finn the fox kit had bright orange fur and the biggest, most curious eyes in the whole forest. Today, his nose twitched with excitement as he discovered something wonderful behind Farmer Ben's house—a cozy garden shed with its door slightly open.

"Wow!" Finn whispered, his fluffy tail swishing. The shed smelled like fresh wood and dried flowers. Sunlight streamed through a dusty window, making everything glow golden.

Inside, Finn found amazing treasures: colorful watering cans, soft gardening gloves, and best of all—three shiny red wheelbarrows, each just the right size for a small fox kit!

"I want to play with ALL of them!" Finn said, his paws dancing with excitement. He jumped into the first wheelbarrow. "Wheee!" Then he hopped to the second one. "This is fun!" Then the third. "Even better!"

Just then, he heard tiny voices outside.

"Is someone in our special shed?" squeaked a voice.

Three little rabbits hopped through the doorway—Rosie with floppy ears, Benny with a twitchy nose, and Luna with soft gray fur.

"Hi!" said Finn, still sitting in the wheelbarrow. "I found these amazing wheelbarrows! I'm playing with all three!"

Rosie's ears drooped. "But we always play with those wheelbarrows. We take turns pushing each other around the garden."

"Taking turns?" Finn tilted his head. "What's that?"

Benny hopped closer. "Taking turns means we each get to play, but we wait for our turn. First Rosie rides, then me, then Luna, then we start again!"

Finn looked at the three wheelbarrows, then at the three sad rabbits. His tummy felt funny—not hungry funny, but the kind of funny you get when you know something isn't quite right.

"Oh," Finn said quietly. "You mean... I can't play with all of them at the same time?"

Luna nodded kindly. "We can all play together! But we need to share and take turns so everyone has fun."

Finn thought hard. He really wanted to play with all the wheelbarrows, but the rabbits looked so hopeful. His mama had taught him about being kind to others.

"Okay!" Finn said, hopping out of the wheelbarrow. "How do we take turns?"

Rosie clapped her paws. "Let's make a plan! We can each pick a wheelbarrow and take turns being the pusher and the rider."

They spent time planning their game. Finn learned that taking turns meant waiting, but it also meant everyone got to have fun. They decided to count to twenty for each turn—Finn practiced counting with the rabbits.

"One, two, three..." they counted together as Rosie pushed Finn around the garden. The wheelbarrow bumped over soft grass, and Finn giggled as the wind tickled his whiskers.

When they reached twenty, Finn hopped out. "Your turn, Benny!"

As the afternoon went on, Finn discovered something wonderful. Taking turns didn't mean less fun—it meant MORE fun! He made three new friends, learned to count to twenty, and everyone got to play.

When the sun began to set, painting the sky orange like Finn's fur, the four friends sat together in the warm shed.

"Thank you for teaching me about taking turns," Finn said. "And thank you for letting me play with you, even though these are your special wheelbarrows."

"That's what friends do," said Luna. "We share and take turns so everyone feels happy."

Finn's heart felt as warm as the golden sunlight. He had learned that the best adventures happen when everyone gets to play together.

The End.
