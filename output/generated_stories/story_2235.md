# Benny Beaver’s Break-Time Boogie

Benny was a busy beaver with a tidy home office. His desk was smooth and shiny like a lake on a calm day. His pencils were lined up like little logs. His computer made a gentle hum, like a bee saying hello. Tap-tap-tap went Benny’s paws on the keyboard. He loved to build plans and draw maps for dams.

But today, Benny’s tail felt heavy. His shoulders drooped. His eyes felt sleepy. “Hmm,” Benny said. “My body feels slow.”

He wiggled in his chair. He stretched his arms up, then drooped down again. “I have so much to do,” he sighed. “But my body says, ‘No, thank you!’”

His friend, a little bluebird named Pip, chirped at the window. “Cheep-cheep! Hi, Benny! Want to play?”

“I can’t,” Benny said. “I have work. I must be busy, busy, busy!”

Pip tilted her head. “Your whiskers look tired.”

Benny touched his whiskers. They did feel droopy. He took a sip of cool water. He sniffed the lemon smell from a tiny candle on his desk. He listened to the soft hum of his computer. Still, he felt slow.

“I need a plan,” Benny whispered. “A beaver needs a good plan.”

He got out his notebook and drew three boxes. In the first box he wrote, “Move.” In the second box he wrote, “Music.” In the third box he wrote, “More try.”

Pip hopped closer. “What’s that?”

“My exercise plan,” Benny said. “Maybe my body needs a break to move. Maybe that will help my brain. Exercise can make us strong and happy.”

Pip clapped her tiny wings. “Cheep! A break-time boogie!”

Benny smiled. “Yes! A break-time boogie.”

He pressed a button, and soft music floated into the room. The beat was gentle. Thump-thump, like a friendly drum. The melody was bright, like sunshine on water. Benny’s tail swished. His paws tingled.

“Okay,” Benny said. “Step one: Move.”

He stood up. He stretched tall, as tall as a tree. He bent down, as low as a little pebble. He rolled his shoulders. He wiggled his paws. “One, two, three,” he counted softly. “I can do this.”

Pip sang along. “Cheep-cheep-cheep!”

Benny tried some toe touches. He tried slow marches in place. Left paw, right paw. Left paw, right paw. His heart went thump-thump a little faster. Warmth spread through his arms and legs. He felt the wood floor smooth under his feet. The air smelled fresh, like pine.

“Step two: Music,” Benny said. He turned the music a tiny bit louder. The beat made him smile. He swayed side to side. He made up a silly dance. Wiggle-tail, clap-clap, spin! He giggled. Pip giggled too. They laughed and danced around the chair.

“Whew!” Benny puffed. “I feel warm. I feel awake!”

Pip chirped, “You look proud!”

Benny stood tall. “I do feel proud. I am taking care of my body. That makes me strong.”

He sat and drank a sip of water. He wiped his brow with a soft towel. His heart felt happy. His brain felt sparkly, like little stars. He looked at his work. “I can try again now.”

But then, his tail drooped just a tiny bit. “What if I get tired again?” he wondered.

Pip tapped the notebook. “Step three: More try.”

Benny nodded. “That means I keep going. If I feel tired, I take a short move break. I can do small steps. Small steps make big things.”

He set a gentle timer with a soft ding. “Work for a little while. Then move for a little while,” he said. “That is my strategy.”

Tap-tap-tap went Benny’s paws. He drew a neat blue line on his map. Ding! The timer sang. Benny stood up. He marched in place. He made circles with his arms. He hummed with the music. He felt strong again.

He worked and moved, worked and moved. Each time he took a break, he whispered, “I can do this.” His chest felt big and brave. His smile felt bright. His whiskers perked up. Pip cheered, “Cheep-cheep! Go, Benny, go!”

Soon, Benny looked at his desk. The map was done! He had drawn careful lines and placed tiny flags. He clapped for himself. Clap-clap! “I did it,” he said softly. “I kept trying. I used my plan. I used my body and my brain.”

Pip fluttered in a happy circle. “Your plan worked!”

Benny nodded. “Exercise helped me feel good. Music helped me move. My strategy helped me keep going. I took small steps. I did not give up.”

He wrote a note on his notebook: “Break-time Boogie Plan: Move, Music, More Try.” He put a gold star sticker on it. Then he put a tiny star on his chest. “For me,” he said, smiling. “Because I am proud of me.”

Pip sang a soft song. The room felt warm and cozy. The lemon candle smelled sweet. The music faded to a quiet hum. Benny’s heart felt calm and strong.

Benny waved to Pip. “Thank you, friend.”

“Cheep-cheep! Anytime,” Pip replied. “Let’s boogie again tomorrow!”

Benny looked around his home office. It was still tidy. It was still busy. But now it felt happy too. He patted his tail. He stretched one more time. “Exercise helps me work and play,” he said. “When I move, I feel brave. When I try, I grow.”

And that day, Benny learned a big lesson: Little moves make big feelings. With exercise, music, a good plan, and persistence, you can feel strong inside and out.

The End.
