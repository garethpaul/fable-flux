# Boo-Boo Bear and the Safe Song

Boo-Boo Bear was a friendly bear cub with soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes. Today, Boo-Boo padded into the musical practice room, where the floor was smooth and shiny. The room smelled like polished wood and lemon soap. Rows of drums, shakers, and a big, shiny piano waited quietly. Sunlight made little squares on the floor, and dust motes danced like tiny fairies in the light.

Boo-Boo’s heart went thump-thump with excitement. “I want to make music all by myself,” Boo-Boo whispered. “I want to be brave and independent!”

Boo-Boo’s teacher, Miss Melody, was a kind rabbit with long ears and a warm smile. She wore a bright scarf with little notes printed on it. “Good morning, Boo-Boo,” she said in a gentle voice. “Before we play, let’s sing our safety song.”

Boo-Boo swayed and hummed. “I know, I know! Safety first!”

Miss Melody sang, “Check your space, tie your lace, gentle hands in every place.”

Boo-Boo giggled and sang back, “Slow and steady, paws are ready!”

They looked around together. The drumsticks were in a basket. The shakers were in a box. The piano bench was low. A soft mat lay in front of the xylophone. Miss Melody tapped the mat. “This is our safe spot,” she said. “Soft paws, soft sounds, and we keep things where they belong.”

Boo-Boo nodded. “I can do it!”

Miss Melody’s long ears twitched. “I will be right over there, listening,” she said, pointing to a chair near the window. “If you need help, call my name. Asking for help is a smart, safe choice.”

“I want to try on my own,” Boo-Boo said, standing tall. “But I will call if I need you.”

Boo-Boo picked up a shaker. It felt cool and pebbly, like tiny beans inside. “Chika-chika,” it whispered. Boo-Boo shook it softly. “Chika-chika-chik!” The sound made Boo-Boo’s toes wiggle. “This is fun!”

Next, Boo-Boo walked to the xylophone. The rainbow bars looked like candy. Boo-Boo reached for the mallets. “Tap-tap,” said the first bar, bright and sweet. “Plink-plonk,” said the second. Boo-Boo smiled. “I can make a rainbow song!”

Then Boo-Boo saw the big, shiny drum in the corner. It was very tall. It had a silver rim and a smooth, tight top. Boo-Boo’s imagination grew big. In Boo-Boo’s mind, the drum became a giant moon. “Boom!” Boo-Boo imagined. “Boom-boom-boom!”

Boo-Boo took a step. The floor looked slippery. Boo-Boo remembered the safety song. “Check your space, tie your lace…” Boo-Boo looked down. The shoelace was loose and wiggly, like a little snake.

“Oh!” Boo-Boo said. “My lace is not safe.” Boo-Boo sat on the soft mat and made bunny ears with the lace. Loop, swoop, and pull. “Tie your lace,” Boo-Boo sang. The bow looked neat and snug.

Boo-Boo stood up again and reached for the drum. The drum was high. It made Boo-Boo stretch and stretch. Boo-Boo’s paws felt wobbly. Boo-Boo tried to tiptoe. The drum moved a tiny bit. It made a quiet scrape, “skrrr.” Boo-Boo’s tummy felt tight. “This does not feel safe,” Boo-Boo whispered.

Boo-Boo remembered another line from Miss Melody: “Ask for help is smart and safe.” Boo-Boo took a deep breath and called, “Miss Melody? I want to play the big drum, but it is too high. Can you help me, please?”

Miss Melody hopped over, ears bouncing. “I am proud of you, Boo-Boo,” she said. “You checked your space, tied your lace, and asked for help. That is brave and smart.”

She brought a sturdy little step stool and placed it beside the drum. She held it steady with one paw. “Step up when you are ready.”

Boo-Boo put one paw, then the other, on the stool. It felt firm and safe. Miss Melody moved the drum closer so Boo-Boo didn’t need to lean. “Now try a gentle tap,” she said.

Boo-Boo lifted a soft mallet and tapped. “Boom,” went the drum, deep and warm, like a hug you could hear. Boo-Boo’s eyes sparkled. “Boom-boom!” The sound filled the room and made the air quiver.

Boo-Boo grinned. “Thank you, Miss Melody.”

“You’re welcome,” said Miss Melody. “Thank you for keeping yourself safe and for asking.”

Boo-Boo felt proud and independent. “I did it with help,” Boo-Boo said. “That is still me doing it!”

Miss Melody nodded. “Yes. Independence means making good choices. Safe choices are the best choices.”

They made a little song together. Boo-Boo tapped the drum, “Boom.” Miss Melody played one piano note, “Ding.” Boo-Boo shook the shaker, “Chika.” They made a pattern: “Boom, ding, chika. Boom, ding, chika.” It sounded like rain and sunshine holding hands.

Boo-Boo imagined friends marching safely in a line, each friend tying laces, checking space, and asking for help when needed. Boo-Boo felt thankful. “I am grateful for you, Miss Melody,” Boo-Boo said. “And for the mat, and the stool, and the safe song.”

Miss Melody smiled. “I am grateful for you, Boo-Boo. Your careful paws make beautiful music.”

When the music time ended, Boo-Boo put the shaker back in the box, the mallet back on the xylophone, and the stool back by the wall. “Keeping things in their place keeps everyone safe,” Boo-Boo said.

Miss Melody clapped softly. “You learned the lesson well.”

Boo-Boo took one last deep drum breath. The room smelled like wood and lemon and a little bit like happy. Boo-Boo waved. “Safety first, then music,” Boo-Boo said. “I can be independent and safe, and I can say thank you.”

Miss Melody waved back. “And that makes the best song of all.”

The End.
