# Birdie Builds the Cozy Closet Club

Birdie was a small singing bird with bright blue feathers and a warm, wiggly song. “Tweet-a-dee! Tweet-a-do! I can sing and I can do!” Birdie chirped. Birdie lived with Mia, a kind child who had a closet hideaway. The closet was soft and quiet, with a little lamp, a tiny rug, and a box of toys. It smelled like clean cotton and crayons. Birdie loved to visit.

One morning, Mia peeked into the closet. “Oh no,” she said. “It’s so messy! The rug is all bunched up. The blocks are spilled. The ball is stuck behind the shoes.”

Birdie hopped in and looked around. The lamp glowed like a little moon. The rug felt fuzzy under Birdie’s tiny feet. Birdie heard a thump-thump as the ball rolled and bumped the wall.

“I want to make this closet a Cozy Closet Club,” Mia said. “A place to read, build, and play.” She sighed. “But it’s a lot of work.”

Birdie puffed up and sang a brave song. “Tweet-a-dee! We can do it! One step, two step, we will finish what we start!”

Mia smiled. “Okay. We will complete our tasks, one by one.”

They made a simple list:
1. Pick up blocks.
2. Roll out the rug.
3. Find the ball.
4. Build a shelf.
5. Make a sports spot.
6. Share our club with friends.

“Ready?” Mia asked.

“Ready!” sang Birdie.

First task: pick up blocks. The wooden blocks were smooth and cool. “Clack, clack,” they sounded as Mia and Birdie put them in the box. Birdie used her beak to nudge the little ones. “Clack!” She hummed, “Pick up, pack up, we can do it!” In no time, the floor was clear.

Second task: roll out the rug. Mia pulled one edge. Birdie hopped along the other edge. “Swoosh,” went the rug as it flattened. Birdie pressed with her soft tummy and little feet. “There,” said Mia. “Flat and neat!”

Third task: find the ball. Birdie listened. The closet was quiet. Then—tap, tap—Birdie heard it! “Tweet! I hear the ball!” She squeezed behind the shoes. It smelled like leather and a tiny bit like grass. Birdie pecked gently and nudged. “Bump-bump,” the ball rolled free.

“You found it!” Mia cheered. Birdie sang a high, happy trill. “We complete our tasks when we try, try, try!”

Fourth task: build a shelf. Mia had a small, safe kit with wooden pieces that clicked together. “We are building,” Mia said. “This is skill-building!” Birdie watched closely. “Click. Click.” Mia read the picture steps. “Step one, step two, step three.” Birdie held a light dowel steady with her wing while Mia slid it in. “Tap,” went the last piece. The shelf stood tall and firm.

Birdie rubbed her cheek on the wood. It felt smooth like a tree branch in the sun. “Tweet-a-do! We built it!”

Fifth task: make a sports spot. Mia placed a soft hoop on the door and a basket on the floor. “This is for gentle ball toss,” she said. “We can practice our aim.” Birdie flapped and cheered, “Sports time! Toss and catch, gentle and safe!”

Mia tossed the ball. “Swish!” It landed in the basket. Birdie bounced and sang, “Try, try, try, then we get it by and by!”

The closet hideaway was coming together. It looked neat and bright. The shelf held books, the box held blocks, and the rug lay smooth like a calm pond.

Last task: share our club. “Community service means we help others,” Mia said. “Let’s invite our neighbors to use the Cozy Closet Club when they need a quiet place to read or build.”

Birdie nodded. “Tweet! We can make a sign.”

Mia wrote: “Cozy Closet Club—Come Read, Build, and Play. Please put things back. We complete our tasks!”

They opened the door, and two little neighbors, Sam and Noor, came by. “Wow,” said Sam. “It’s so nice!”

“It smells like clean cotton,” Noor said. “And the rug is so soft!”

“We can build a tower,” Mia said. “And we will put the blocks back when we are done.”

Birdie showed them the sports spot. “We toss gently. We take turns. We keep it tidy.”

They all played. Birdie sang a steady work song: “Step by step, task by task, we finish, then we ask, ‘What’s next?’”

When playtime was over, Birdie chirped, “Cleanup time!” Mia and the friends put books on the shelf, blocks in the box, and the ball in the basket. “Clack, swish, tap.” It sounded like a happy band.

The closet hideaway felt warm and calm. The little lamp glowed. Birdie’s song was soft and sweet. “We did it,” Mia said. “We built, we played sports, and we helped our friends.”

Birdie smiled with her bright eyes. “We completed all our tasks. When we finish what we start, we make good things for everyone.”

Mia hugged Birdie gently. “Our Cozy Closet Club is ready. And we will keep it ready, together.”

Birdie sang the final tune, “Tweet-a-dee, tweet-a-do, I can sing and I can do! Helping hands, happy hearts, finish tasks and do our part!”

The End.
