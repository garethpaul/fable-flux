# Ollie Otter’s Tidy-Then-Help Plan

Ollie the playful otter bounced on his soft bed. His cozy bedroom smelled like fresh soap and warm blankets. Sunlight made little sparkles on his shiny shell collection. Toys made gentle clacks as he tapped them. His blanket felt fluffy under his paws.

“I want to play!” Ollie giggled. He rolled like a little log. But in the corner, his chore chart hung on the wall with bright star stickers.

Mama Otter peeked in. “Good morning, Ollie. What is your plan today?” she asked, her voice sweet like honey tea.

Ollie looked at his chart. “Make bed. Put toys away. Eat a good snack,” he read slowly. “Then help the neighbors with the food drive.”

Mama smiled. “That is a big plan. First things first. One task at a time.”

Ollie wriggled. “But playing is fun now.”

Mama touched his paw. “Doing tasks helps us help others. When we finish, we feel proud. And we can play after.”

Ollie took a deep breath. He felt the cool air on his nose. “Okay. I can do it!” he said. His whiskers wiggled with courage.

He tugged the blanket. “Smooth, smooth,” he said, patting it flat. He fluffed his pillow. Poof! It smelled like clean cotton. “One task done!”

“Great leadership, Ollie,” Mama said. “You led your body to finish your job.”

Ollie giggled. “I am the leader of my room!” He picked up blocks. Click, clack. He placed books on the shelf. Thump. He put shells in a box. Shhh, they slid softly. “Two tasks done!”

His belly made a tiny rumble. “Rrrr,” it said.

“Time for a smart snack,” Mama said. She brought a little plate to the bedroom. Bright orange carrot sticks crunched. Green apple slices were sweet and juicy. A small bowl of oat crackers was crisp and light.

Ollie munched. “Crunch, crunch! Apples are sweet. Carrots are crispy!” Juice dripped down his chin. He wiped it with a soft napkin. “Snack task done!”

Mama nodded. “Good nutrition gives us energy to help our community.”

Ollie stood tall. “Now the food drive!” He picked up his little backpack. Inside, he placed a can of beans. Clink. A bag of rice. Rustle. A jar of peanut butter. Plop. “These foods help friends grow strong,” he said.

Mama and Ollie looked at the wall map above his bed. It showed rivers, forests, towns, and faraway oceans. “People near and far need care,” Mama said. “When we help, we are good neighbors to the whole world.”

Ollie traced the river with his paw. “I can be a helper here,” he said. His heart felt warm and brave.

They were almost ready to go, but Ollie saw markers on the floor. “Oops,” he said. “One more thing.” He picked them up and clicked the caps on. Click! Click! “All done. My room is safe and tidy.”

Mama clapped softly. “You finished every task. That shows leadership.”

Ollie smiled wide. “I made a plan. I did the plan.” He felt proud from whiskers to tail.

At the community center, the room buzzed like happy bees. Friends brought food. Baskets rustled. Cans clinked. There was a big sign that said, “Food Friends!”

Ms. Panda waved. “Welcome, Ollie! Can you sort the foods?”

Ollie nodded. “Yes! I can lead a line.” He placed a sign on the table. “Grains here. Beans here. Fruits here.” He spoke in a clear, kind voice. “One at a time. We can do it!”

Friends followed. “Grains here,” said Turtle, sliding in rice. “Beans here,” said Squirrel, rolling a can. “Fruits here,” said Bunny, placing peaches.

Ollie’s paws moved steady. “We are a team,” he said. He listened. He helped. He smiled. The room smelled like warm bread and sweet fruit. The pile grew tall like a gentle mountain.

A little pup looked worried. “I dropped a can,” she said.

Ollie knelt. “It’s okay,” he whispered. “Let’s pick it up together.” They lifted it—clink! “We finished the task. Teamwork!”

Soon, the job was done. Ms. Panda clapped. “You led with kindness, Ollie. You finished your tasks, and you helped our community.”

Ollie’s heart felt big and light. He thought about his cozy bedroom, his chart, and his snack. He thought about friends near and far. He smiled at Mama.

At home, Ollie snuggled into his soft bed. The room was calm. The blanket felt warm. The shells shimmered in the gentle light.

Mama whispered, “What did we learn today?”

Ollie yawned a tiny yawn. “Make a plan. Do one task, then the next. Good food gives energy. Helping others helps the world. I can be a kind leader.”

Mama kissed his head. “Yes. You finished your tasks, you cared for your body, and you served your community.”

Ollie closed his eyes, happy and proud. “Tomorrow, I will do my tasks again,” he murmured.

The End.
