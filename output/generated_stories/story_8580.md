# Rocco Leads the Art Room Band

Rocco was a musical raccoon with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He wore a tiny scarf with colorful notes on it. He loved to make music. He loved to help, too. One sunny morning, Rocco padded into the creative art room. The room smelled like crayons, fresh paper, and a little like glue. Sunbeams danced across jars of brushes, baskets of beads, and shelves of paint. Tap-tap-tap went Rocco’s little drum as he walked. His tail swished to the beat.

“Good morning!” Rocco sang. “I’m ready to make art and music!”

His friends waved. There was Poppy the panda, who liked to paint rainbows. There was Tito the turtle, who loved to count beads. There was Lila the lamb, who enjoyed drawing maps of the world. They all smiled at Rocco.

Today’s plan was special. The teacher, Ms. Aya, clapped her hands. “Friends,” she said in a warm voice, “today we will make a mural together. A big picture that shows people around the world being kind. We will also make sounds to match our art. We will be an art room band!”

Rocco’s ears perked up. He loved bands. He loved helping friends make music. He took a breath. “I can be a helper,” he said, feeling brave.

Ms. Aya nodded. “Thank you, Rocco. That is leadership. A leader helps others, listens, and keeps everyone calm and kind.”

Rocco felt a happy flutter in his chest. He tapped his drum softly. “Boom-boom, I can listen. Boom-boom, I can help.”

They started the mural. Poppy painted smiling faces with big red hearts. Lila drew a blue and green world with tiny hands holding a big flower. Tito glued bright beads into a sunny border. Rocco set out little instruments: bells, shakers, a triangle, and his drum. He made a simple beat so everyone could follow. “Tap-tap… pause… tap-tap,” he hummed.

Soon the room filled with sounds. Jingle-jingle from the bells. Swish-swish from brushes. Snip-snip from scissors. It felt like a kind, busy world.

But then, a wrinkle came. Poppy reached for a brush and knocked a cup. “Oh no!” she cried. “Spill!” Blue water splashed. A blue drip fell on Tito’s beads.

Tito’s eyes got big. “My beads!” he said. His voice shook. His cheeks puffed. “I worked slowly. Now they are wet.”

Lila stopped drawing. “I was making tiny hands,” she said softly. “Now the paper is soggy.”

Rocco’s heart sped up. His drum went bump-bump-bump. He felt a yucky bubble of frustration rise in his tummy. He wanted to shout, “Stop!” But he remembered Ms. Aya’s words. A leader helps. A leader listens. A leader keeps calm and kind.

Rocco put his drum down and took a slow, quiet breath. In and out. He put a paw on his chest. “I can calm down,” he whispered. He tapped his fingers softly. Tap, tap, pause. His body felt better. He looked at his friends with gentle eyes.

“It’s okay to feel upset,” Rocco said. “Let’s say our feelings.”

Tito nodded. “I feel mad and sad,” he said. “I wanted it perfect.”

Poppy said, “I feel sorry. My hands were wiggly.”

Lila said, “I feel worried. Can we fix it?”

Rocco listened. “Thank you for telling me,” he said. “Thank you, Poppy, for saying sorry.” He smiled at Tito. “We can fix it together.”

Ms. Aya smiled from the doorway. “That is leadership, Rocco. You are calm, you listen, and you use kind words.”

Rocco looked at the mess. He had an idea. Leaders make plans. “Team,” he said, “let’s make a clean-up song. We can move together.”

He tapped a gentle beat. “Tap-tap, wipe-wipe. Tap-tap, dry-dry.”

Everyone followed. Poppy dabbed the water with soft towels. “Wipe-wipe.” Tito spread the beads on a tray in the sun. “Dry-dry.” Lila lifted her map gently. “Tap-tap,” she giggled, “I will draw new tiny hands.”

Rocco thanked them. “Poppy, thank you for being careful now. Tito, thank you for trying again. Lila, thank you for your patience.” His words felt warm, like sunshine.

They worked and sang. The blue drip became a little river on the mural. Lila drew children from many places standing by the river, sharing flowers and smiles. Tito added a rainbow bridge with shiny beads. Poppy painted new hearts, even bigger than before.

Rocco added music to match. For the river, he rubbed a shaker softly. Swish-swish. For the bridge, he rang the triangle. Ting! For the big hearts, he beat his drum slow and strong. Boom… boom.

Soon the art room glowed. The mural was even more beautiful. It showed friends from around the world helping each other, holding hands, and saying thank you in many ways. Lila wrote little words: Gracias, Thank you, Merci, Asante. Rocco sang them gently. “Thank you, friends, all around the world.”

Ms. Aya clapped. “You led with care, Rocco. How did you do it?”

Rocco thought. “I felt mad,” he said. “I took a breath. I listened. I made a plan. We worked together. We said thank you.”

Ms. Aya nodded. “That is true leadership. You helped everyone feel safe and strong.”

Tito smiled. “I am grateful,” he said. “Thank you, team.”

Poppy grinned. “I learned to slow down.”

Lila cuddled her crayon. “I learned to try again.”

Rocco hugged his drum. His tail swished to a happy beat. “We are a team,” he said. “We are helpers. We are kind to friends near and far.”

They stood together and looked at their mural. It was colorful, kind, and full of music. Rocco tapped one last rhythm. “Tap-tap, thank you.”

Everyone chimed, “Thank you!”

The End.
