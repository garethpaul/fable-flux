# Nutmeg Waits and Wonders

Nutmeg the resourceful squirrel lived in a cozy bedroom at the top of a sunny house. The bedroom smelled like fresh laundry and warm blankets. A soft rug tickled Nutmeg’s paws, and the little window showed a patch of blue sky. Nutmeg had a tiny toolbox, a basket of buttons, and a jar of shiny acorns lined up like soldiers.

One morning, Nutmeg woke up to the sound of gentle rain tapping on the glass. Tap-tap. Pitter-patter. The drips made a lullaby on the window. “Good morning, rain,” Nutmeg whispered. “I have a big plan today.”

Nutmeg’s plan was to build a bedtime glow lantern from a cardboard tube, paper stars, and a string of tiny lights. “This will help everyone feel calm at night,” Nutmeg said, stroking the smooth cardboard. “It will shine for me, for my friends, and for friends far away too.”

Nutmeg spread out the supplies on the rug. Snip went the scissors. Rustle went the paper. Click went the little toolbox. Everything was ready—except one thing.

“Uh-oh,” said Nutmeg. “I need new sticky dots. The store is far away, and the rain is heavy.”

Nutmeg took a slow breath. In and out. “I can wait. Waiting is hard, but I can do it.”

While Nutmeg waited, there was a soft knock at the toy chest. Out popped a tiny bear named Button, with a blue ribbon around his neck. “Hi, Nutmeg,” said Button. “What are you doing?”

“I’m building a glow lantern,” said Nutmeg. “But I have to wait for sticky dots. I’m practicing patience.”

Button nodded. “Waiting makes my tummy wiggle,” he said. “But we can wait together.”

Nutmeg smiled. “Yes. We can help each other.”

They listened to the rain. They counted drops on the window. “One, two, three… drip!” The room felt cool and quiet. The lamp hummed softly. The blanket smelled like soap and sunshine.

After a while, a little bird peeped from the windowsill. It was Pippa, with bright eyes and damp feathers. “Peep! May I come in? The rain is very splashy.”

“Come in, Pippa,” said Nutmeg. “We are waiting and keeping cozy.”

Pippa shook her feathers. Sprinkly sprinkles flew like tiny diamonds. “What are you building?” she asked.

“A glow lantern,” said Nutmeg. “It will be a kind light for bedtime. It will also remind us to be gentle with our world, and with each other.”

Pippa hopped closer. “I can hold things with my beak,” she said. “I can help.”

Nutmeg nodded. “Thank you. Patience and teamwork make good work.” Nutmeg’s voice was calm, but inside, the squirrel’s heart wiggled with excitement.

They made a plan. “First,” said Nutmeg, “we will sort the paper stars by size.” Button clapped. “Small, medium, big!” Pippa chirped. “Peep!”

They sorted slowly. They sang a waiting song: “Slow and steady, soft and ready. Wait, breathe, try again.” When Nutmeg felt rushy, Nutmeg placed a paw on the warm rug and took a deep breath. In and out. The rushy feeling floated away, like a cloud.

Still, they needed sticky dots. “I can fly to the mailbox,” said Pippa. “Maybe a package came.”

Nutmeg shook their head gently. “The rain is too splishy-splashy. We will wait a little more.” Button nodded. “Waiting keeps us safe.”

They used time well. Nutmeg checked the toolbox. “Screws? Yes. Tape? Yes.” Button told a tiny joke. “What did the star say to the moon? ‘I’m stuck on you!’” They all giggled.

Soon, the rain became a soft whisper. Shhh. The window glowed with a pale light. Tap-tap changed to drip-drop and then to quiet.

Pippa fluffed her feathers. “I hear the mail truck,” she peeped. Beep-beep from the street below.

Nutmeg’s ears perked up. “Let’s use patient feet,” Nutmeg said. “Slow and safe.” They walked to the door, down the hallway, and to the little mail slot. There it was—a small packet with Nutmeg’s name in bright blue letters.

Nutmeg smiled and hugged the packet. “We waited. We did it together.”

Back in the cozy bedroom, Nutmeg opened the packet. “Sticky dots!” The dots smelled a little like lemons. Pluck, pluck, pluck—Nutmeg placed the dots carefully. Pippa held the paper. Button smoothed the edges. Nutmeg gently tucked the tiny lights inside the tube. Click—the switch turned on.

A warm glow filled the room. Golden stars danced on the walls and ceiling. The light felt like a hug. Everyone was quiet for a moment.

“It’s beautiful,” whispered Button.

“It’s kind,” peeped Pippa.

“It was patience,” said Nutmeg. “We waited. We breathed. We helped. We kept trying.”

Nutmeg set the lantern by the window. The glow floated out, soft and friendly. “This light is for us,” Nutmeg said, “and for friends near and far. We are all neighbors on one big Earth. When we are patient and help each other, our light reaches everyone.”

Button yawned a happy yawn. Pippa tucked her head under her wing. Nutmeg wrapped a blanket around them all. The room smelled cozy and sweet. The stars twinkled on the ceiling like sleepy fireflies.

Nutmeg whispered, “Today I learned that patience is waiting with a calm heart. Patience helps me finish, helps me be safe, and helps me be kind. When we wait and work together, good things happen.”

The glow lantern hummed softly. The house was quiet. Outside, a tiny breeze said hello to the leaves.

They rested, warm and proud, with the gentle light shining for everyone.

The End.
