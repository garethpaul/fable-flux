# Daisy's Big Day

Daisy the deer fawn wiggled in her soft nursery bed. Today was the day! Mama had promised to teach her how to jump over the little stream behind their cozy home.

"Is it time yet, Mama?" Daisy asked, bouncing on her tiny hooves. The morning sunlight streamed through the nursery window, making everything glow golden and warm.

Mama Deer smiled gently. "Not quite yet, little one. First, we need to eat breakfast and let our food settle. Jumping takes strong muscles and a calm tummy."

Daisy's ears drooped. "But I've been waiting forever!" She had been thinking about jumping all night long. In her dreams, she soared over streams like a fluffy cloud.

"Forever is a very long time," Mama said softly. "You've been waiting since yesterday. That shows you're growing more patient already."

While Mama prepared sweet clover and fresh berries, Daisy paced around the nursery. She tried jumping over her toy blocks, but her legs felt wobbly. She tried jumping over her pillow, but tumbled onto her side with a soft thump.

"Ow," Daisy whimpered. Her knee had a tiny scrape.

Mama nuzzled her gently. "Jumping takes practice, sweetheart. And practice takes time. Would you like to hear about when I learned to jump?"

Daisy nodded, snuggling close to Mama's warm, fuzzy side.

"I was just like you," Mama began. "So excited and eager. But I tried to jump too big too fast. I fell into a muddy puddle!" Mama's eyes twinkled. "I was covered in mud from my nose to my tail!"

Daisy giggled. "Really, Mama?"

"Really! So my mama taught me something very important. She said patience is like growing flowers. You plant the seed, water it every day, and wait. You can't pull on the flower to make it grow faster."

After breakfast, they walked to the practice area. Mama had set up small sticks on the ground.

"We'll start tiny," Mama explained. "First, we step over the stick. Then we hop over it. Then we jump!"

Daisy wanted to jump over the biggest stick right away, but she remembered Mama's flower story. She took a deep breath that smelled like pine trees and fresh grass.

"Step first," Daisy said to herself. She stepped over the tiny stick. It felt easy!

"Wonderful!" Mama cheered. "How did that feel?"

"Good! My legs feel strong and ready."

Next, Daisy hopped over the stick. Her heart beat fast with excitement, but she stayed calm and focused.

"Now for a small jump," Mama said, moving to a slightly bigger stick.

Daisy felt nervous butterflies in her tummy. "What if I fall again?"

"Then we'll try again," Mama said kindly. "Being brave doesn't mean you're not scared. It means you try even when you feel scared."

Daisy closed her eyes, felt her hooves firm on the soft ground, and jumped! She sailed over the stick and landed safely on the other side.

"I did it! I did it!" Daisy danced in circles.

"You certainly did," Mama beamed. "And you did it by being patient with yourself."

As they walked home, Daisy felt proud and happy. "Mama, patience isn't just waiting, is it?"

"What do you think it is?" Mama asked.

Daisy thought carefully. "It's being kind to yourself while you learn new things."

Mama's heart swelled with love. "That's exactly right, my wise little fawn."

The End.
