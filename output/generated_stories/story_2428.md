# Owl Hoots Help at the Table

A wise old owl named Ollie lived near a little house with a sunny dining room. The dining room smelled like warm toast and sweet oranges. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. The table had plates of many colors—red, blue, green, and purple. Everyone who visited felt welcome.

One morning, the dining room was busy. Friends came to share breakfast. There was Mia, who loved to hum and had curly hair with bright beads. There was Sam, who used a gentle green hearing aid and smiled shyly. There was Noor, who wore a sparkly scarf and laughed like tiny bells. There was Leo, who zoomed in with his shiny blue wheelchair. And there was Ollie the owl, with big round eyes and soft feathers like gray clouds.

“Good morning,” said Ollie. “Hoo-hoo, good morning!”

“Good morning!” the friends cheered.

Plates clinked. Spoons tink-tinked. The toast was crispy. The oranges were juicy. Butter smelled creamy, and the jelly was purple and sweet.

After breakfast, crumbs dotted the table like tiny stars. Cups sat on the table, waiting. Napkins drooped like sleepy flags. The floor had a splash of juice that made a sticky spot.

Mia looked at the mess and felt her tummy twist. “Oh no,” she said softly. “It’s so much.”

Sam tugged his shirt. “I feel worried,” he whispered. “I don’t know where to start.”

Noor frowned. “I don’t like sticky,” she said. “It makes my hands feel ugh.”

Leo tapped his wheels. “I want to help,” he said. “But how can we do it all?”

Ollie blinked slowly. “Hoo-hoo,” he said kindly. “We have a little problem. The dining room needs care. We can feel our feelings, and we can find a way. Let’s breathe.”

Everyone took a slow breath in and a slow breath out. The dining room felt calmer, like a quiet hug.

Ollie ruffled his feathers. “Chores are like small jobs. Chores help our home. Chores help our hearts. We do a little bit, and we do it together.”

“Together,” said Mia, and she smiled.

“Together,” said Sam, touching his hearing aid. His smile grew.

“Together,” Noor sang, and her scarf shimmered.

“Together,” Leo chimed, tapping his wheels lightly.

Ollie looked around. “We are all different. We are all special. We can each choose a job that fits us.”

Mia said, “I like counting!” She picked up the napkins. “One, two, three, four,” she said, folding them into nice small squares. The napkins felt soft, like tiny pillows.

Sam said, “I can listen and look.” He watched for cups and plates. He carried them slowly to the sink. The cups went clink, clink. He felt proud and careful.

Noor said, “I can wipe the sticky!” She dipped a cloth in warm, soapy water. It smelled like lemons. She wiped the juice spot. Swish, swish. “Ah,” she said. “Smooth and clean.”

Leo said, “I can be the helper captain!” He rolled around the table and pointed gently. “Mia, napkins in the basket. Sam, plates to the sink. Noor, wipe the table edge too.” His wheels hummed softly, and he grinned.

Ollie fluffed. “Hoo-hoo! I can sweep the crumbs.” He used a small broom. Brush, brush. The crumbs whispered into a little pan.

They worked and hummed. Mia counted and folded. Sam clinked and stacked. Noor swished and wiped. Leo guided and checked, and Ollie swept and hooted a happy song.

Soon, the table shone. The napkins sat in a neat pile. The floor was smooth. The dining room smelled lemony and bright. Sunlight danced on the clean spoons.

Mia clapped. “We did it!”

Sam nodded. “I feel calm now.”

Noor wiggled her fingers. “No more sticky!”

Leo beamed. “Teamwork!”

Ollie tucked his wings around them like a cozy blanket. “Hoo-hoo,” he said. “How do your hearts feel?”

Mia touched her chest. “My heart feels proud.”

Sam said, “My heart feels brave.”

Noor said, “My heart feels happy.”

Leo said, “My heart feels strong.”

Ollie smiled. “We had a problem. We made a plan. We used our different strengths. We listened, we helped, we accepted each other. That is the magic of chores.”

Mia asked, “Can we make a chore song?”

“Yes!” said Noor, and she sang:
“Fold and wipe,
Clink and sweep,
Helping hands
Make joy run deep!”

They all sang together. The song bounced off the walls like bright bubbles.

Ollie nodded. “Hoo-hoo. Remember our lesson: When there is a mess, we don’t feel bad. We feel our feelings, we make a plan, and we help together. Chores can be kind. Chores can be fun. Chores help everyone.”

Leo raised a hand. “Next time, I’ll set the forks!”

Mia said, “I’ll count the cups!”

Sam said, “I’ll listen for clinks!”

Noor giggled. “I’ll chase the sticky!”

Ollie flapped once. “And I will cheer for you all.”

They looked around their clean, sunny dining room. It felt warm and safe. It smelled like lemons and toast. Their hearts felt light.

“Thank you, friends,” Ollie said. “Hoo-hoo, we did it together.”

The End.
