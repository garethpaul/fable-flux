# The Tall Plan at the Table

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe. She had soft, golden fur with gentle brown spots. Her big eyes blinked slowly when she was thinking. Today, Gigi stood in her cozy dining room. The sun made warm shapes on the floor. The table smelled like fresh wood. There was a small clock on the wall that went tick-tick, tick-tick.

On the table sat a big fruit bowl. It was shiny and full of colorful fruit. Red apples. Yellow bananas. Purple grapes. Gigi took a deep breath. She had a goal.

“I want to make a fruit rainbow,” Gigi said. “Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple. I want to do it myself.”

Her tummy rumbled. Her ears twitched. She stretched her long neck and reached for the fruit bowl. But the slippery grapes rolled away. A banana slid with a soft swoosh. An apple wobbled and bumped her nose.

“Oops,” said Gigi, rubbing her nose. “This is hard.”

Gigi did not give up. Persistence, she remembered, means keep trying. She set a simple plan in her mind.

Goal: Make a fruit rainbow.
Step 1: Get fruit safely.
Step 2: Line up colors.
Step 3: Taste and share.

She whispered, “I can do this, one small step at a time.”

Gigi tried again. She used her gentle lips to lift one red apple. She placed it on a small blue plate. It made a soft thump. “Red,” she said with a smile.

She reached for an orange next, but her hoof nudged the bowl. The grapes rolled again. Tip-tap, tip-tap, they bounced on the table. One grape fell to the floor with a tiny plink.

“Oh no,” Gigi sighed. “I need help.”

Just then, her friends peeked in. Tiko the tiny turtle wore a green bandana. Buzz the bumblebee hummed and glowed like a tiny sun. Mimi the mouse had quick whiskers and kind eyes.

“What are you doing, Gigi?” asked Mimi.

“I have a plan,” said Gigi. “I want to make a fruit rainbow. But the fruit slips, and I keep bumping it.”

“We can help,” said Tiko in a calm, slow voice. “Teamwork!”

“Teamwork,” buzzed Buzz. “Zzz-yes!”

Gigi’s heart felt warm. “Thank you. Let’s make a plan together.”

They made a little team circle by the table. The room smelled sweet, like oranges and grapes. The clock kept going tick-tick. Gigi said, “I will reach the tall fruit. Tiko, you can hold the plate steady with your strong shell. Mimi, you can roll small fruit gently. Buzz, you can buzz near the bowl to remind us to go slow.”

“Slow and steady,” said Tiko.

“Roll and ready,” squeaked Mimi.

“Buzz-buzz, we can do it!” sang Buzz.

They started. Gigi lifted a round orange. Tiko pressed the plate with a tiny scrape-scrape. Mimi rolled two grapes into a little cup. Buzz hummed a soft beat. Hummm, hummm, go slow.

Orange went next to red. Yellow banana followed with a light plop. “Red, orange, yellow,” Gigi said. “We are doing it!”

A green pear waited at the edge of the bowl. Gigi reached. But her neck bumped the spoon, and the spoon made a loud clink! Gigi blinked and froze.

“It’s okay,” said Tiko. “We stay calm.”

Mimi patted Gigi’s hoof. “We keep trying.”

Buzz hovered close. “You’ve got this.”

Gigi took a deep breath. In. Out. “I will try again,” she said. “I will not give up.” Gigi moved slowly. Her long neck was steady. She lifted the green pear. She set it down. The plate did not wobble.

“Green!” they all cheered.

Blue was tricky. There were no blue apples. But there were blueberries in a little bowl. Mimi rolled the tiny berries one by one. Plip. Plip. Plip. They looked like little blue dots in the line.

“Blue!” said Buzz, looping in a happy circle.

Last was purple. Gigi and her friends looked at the grape cup. The grapes shined like little moons. Tiko carefully tilted the cup. Mimi guided with her paws. Buzz hummed a steady tune. Gigi nudged one purple grape into place. Then another. Then one more.

“Purple!” Gigi cheered. “We did it!”

They stepped back. The fruit rainbow was bright and pretty. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple. The dining room felt warm and happy. The fruit smelled sweet and juicy. Gigi’s heart felt full.

“We reached our goal,” Gigi said softly. “We kept trying. We worked together.”

Tiko smiled. “Persistence makes strong hearts.”

Mimi clapped her tiny paws. “Teamwork makes big jobs feel small.”

Buzz twirled. “Cooperation is sweet, like grapes!”

Gigi grinned. “Let’s taste and share.” She gave each friend a piece. Crunch went the apple. Squish went the grape. The banana was soft and creamy. They giggled at the blueberry dots on Mimi’s whiskers.

Gigi looked at her friends. “When something is hard, I can make a plan. I can ask for help. I can keep trying. That is persistence.”

“Yes,” said Tiko. “Step by step.”

“Together,” said Mimi.

“Always,” buzzed Buzz.

Gigi felt proud and peaceful. Her goal was met. Her friends felt happy too. The fruit rainbow sparkled in the sun. The clock went tick-tick, tick-tick, and the room smelled like sweet success.

The End.
