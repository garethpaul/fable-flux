# Poppy the Parrot Plans a Snack

Poppy the Parrot fluffed her bright green feathers and tilted her head. Her pantry was cool and cozy. It smelled like warm oats, sweet bananas, and a tiny bit like cinnamon. Little jars and boxes sat on the shelves like quiet, patient friends.

“My tummy says, squawk, snack time!” Poppy chirped. “I want to make my own banana oat bites.”

She hopped to the low shelf. “I can do it,” she whispered. “I will be safe. I will be slow.” Poppy took a deep breath in. She puffed her chest out, then breathed out soft and calm. “I am ready.”

Poppy looked around. “First, I need oats.” She spotted a small bag. It was on the second shelf, just a little high for her wings. “Hmm,” she hummed. “This is a problem. But I can solve it.”

She flapped carefully to the step stool sitting by the door. The stool was sturdy and had little rubber feet. “Good tool!” Poppy said. She tapped it with her beak. Tap, tap. “I will move it slowly.”

Poppy pushed the stool, scoot, scoot, scoot. She paused. She listened to her heart. Thump, thump. “Slow and safe,” she said. “I can be patient.” She climbed up one step. Then another. She reached, not too far, and hugged the oat bag with one wing. “Got it!”

Next, Poppy needed a banana. She looked at a yellow bunch hanging on a hook. “Hello, bananas,” she giggled. “You smell sweet like sunshine.” She chose one and placed it gently in a little blue bowl.

Poppy set everything on the low counter. “Now I mash. Then I mix.” She peeled the banana. The skin made a soft zip sound. She mashed it with a fork. Squish, squish. “This is fun!” she laughed.

She poured oats into the bowl. “Shake, shake.” A few oats sprinkled onto the counter. Poppy paused. She looked at the tiny mess. “I will stay calm,” she told herself. “I will clean as I go.” She wiped the oats back into the bowl with a small brush. Swish. “Better.”

Poppy wanted a pinch of cinnamon. She turned to the spice rack. The little jar was glass. It was high. “This jar is tricky,” she said. “I can ask for help. Asking is part of doing it on my own, too.” Poppy thought of her friend, Momo the Mouse, who lived near the pantry door.

“Momo?” Poppy called. “Are you close by?”

A tiny nose peeked out. “Squeak! I am here,” said Momo. “What do you need?”

“I am making banana oat bites by myself,” Poppy said with a proud smile. “I can mix and mash. But the cinnamon is high and glass. May you steady the jar while I scoop?”

Momo’s whiskers twitched. “That is a smart plan. I will hold. You scoop.”

Poppy climbed one step on the stool. Momo held the jar steady with both tiny paws. Poppy took a baby spoon and scooped just a little. The air filled with a warm, cozy smell. “Thank you, Momo,” Poppy said. “Teamwork helps me be safe and strong.”

“You’re doing great,” Momo cheered. “You are careful and calm.”

Poppy added the cinnamon and mixed. Stir, stir, stir. The dough felt soft and sticky on her spoon. It looked like little clouds. “Almost ready,” she sang.

She lined a small tray with paper cups. Plop, plop, plop. She dropped little scoops into the cups. One, two, three, four. “I did it!” she chirped. “Now they rest in the cool.”

Poppy slid the tray into the small fridge drawer. She closed it with a gentle thump. She washed her bowl and fork with warm, soapy water. Bubble, bubble. She dried them with a soft towel. “Clean space, happy place,” she said.

While the bites cooled, Poppy felt wiggly. She wanted to peek. She wanted to eat now. She put a wing over her heart and took three slow breaths. In and out. In and out. In and out. “I can wait,” she told herself. “Waiting helps me make good choices.”

Momo sniffed the air. “They smell so good, Poppy!”

Poppy smiled. “Thank you for helping me be safe.”

Ding! The little timer on the counter sang a tiny note. Poppy opened the drawer. The banana oat bites were round and set. She lifted one. It was cool on the outside and soft in the middle.

She took a small bite. Mmm. It tasted sweet and cozy, like a hug. “I made this,” Poppy said softly. Her eyes shone. “I did many things by myself. I asked for help when I needed it. I stayed calm. I solved problems. I can do hard things.”

Momo clapped his tiny paws. “You were independent and also a great teammate,” he said.

Poppy shared a bite with Momo. “We worked together,” she said. “I feel proud. Next time, I can try a new step.”

Poppy packed two bites into a little box for later. She put the stool away. She checked the floor for spills. All clean. She closed the pantry door with a gentle click.

“Today, I learned,” Poppy sang. “Being independent means trying my best, making safe choices, asking for help when I need it, and staying calm. I can plan. I can wait. I can do it!”

Poppy fluffed her feathers and smiled. Snack time felt extra yummy, because she made it herself.

The End.
