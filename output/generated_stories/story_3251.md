# Pip the Mouse Learns to Help Herself

In a cozy corner of Mr. Johnson's garden shed lived a little gray mouse named Pip. She had the softest whiskers, bright black eyes, and a pink nose that twitched when she was thinking hard. The shed smelled like fresh wood and blooming flowers from the garden outside.

Every morning, Pip would wake up and call out, "Mama! I'm hungry! Can you get my breakfast?"

Her mama would scurry around, gathering seeds and crumbs for Pip's meal.

"Mama! I can't reach my water bowl! Can you help me?" Pip would squeak.

Again, Mama Mouse would rush over to help.

One sunny Tuesday morning, Mama Mouse sat down next to Pip. "Little one," she said gently, "I need to visit Grandma Mouse today. She lives in the barn across the field."

Pip's whiskers drooped. "But Mama, who will get my food? Who will help me reach things? I don't know how to do anything by myself!"

Mama Mouse nuzzled Pip's head. "Sweet Pip, you're much more capable than you think. Today is a perfect day to discover what you can do all on your own."

After Mama left, Pip's tummy began to rumble. "Oh no," she squeaked. "I'm so hungry, but I don't know where to find food."

She sat still for a moment, her little nose twitching as she thought. Then she remembered seeing Mama sniff around the shed corners. "Maybe I can try that too," Pip said to herself.

She took a deep breath through her pink nose. What wonderful smells! She could smell sweet sunflower seeds near the gardening tools, and breadcrumbs by the door where Mr. Johnson sometimes ate his lunch.

Following her nose, Pip discovered a small pile of seeds that had fallen from a bag. "I found food all by myself!" she cheered, doing a little mouse dance.

Next, she felt thirsty. Her water bowl seemed so high up on the shelf. Pip looked around and noticed some wooden blocks stacked nearby. "Hmm," she thought, "maybe I can use these like stairs."

Carefully, she pushed one block closer to the shelf with her tiny paws. Then another. Soon she had made little steps leading right to her water bowl. She climbed up and took a refreshing drink.

"I did it! I really did it!" Pip squeaked happily.

When the afternoon sun made the shed warm and golden, Pip felt sleepy. Usually, Mama would fluff her bedding for her. But Pip had an idea. She gathered soft bits of cloth and cotton that had fallen around the shed. With her paws and nose, she made the coziest, fluffiest bed ever.

Just as she was settling down for a nap, Mama Mouse returned.

"Pip! How did your day go, little one?" Mama asked.

Pip's eyes sparkled with pride. "Mama, I found my own food by using my nose! I made steps to reach my water! And I built the most comfortable bed! I can do lots of things by myself!"

Mama Mouse hugged her daughter tight. "I'm so proud of you, Pip. You discovered that you're much stronger and smarter than you knew. When we try to solve problems ourselves, we learn wonderful new things about what we can do."

From that day on, Pip still loved spending time with her mama, but she also enjoyed the good feeling that came from helping herself and solving her own problems.

The End.
