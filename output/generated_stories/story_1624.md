# Ollie Otter's Clean-Up Game

Ollie the otter loved playing in his cozy nursery. The room had soft blue walls with fluffy white clouds painted on them, and sunshine streamed through the big window. His favorite toys were scattered everywhere - colorful balls, building blocks, and his special swimming goggles.

"What a fun morning!" Ollie said, stretching his furry arms. He had been practicing his swimming moves, pretending the soft carpet was a sparkling river. But now his paws felt sticky, and his whiskers had crumbs from his morning snack.

Ollie's mama peeked into the nursery. "Good morning, my little swimmer! Are you ready for your big swimming lesson today?"

"Yes, yes, yes!" Ollie bounced excitedly. "I want to learn how to do underwater flips!"

Mama Otter smiled, but then she noticed something. "Oh my, Ollie. Before we go swimming, we need to make sure you're all clean. Look at your paws!"

Ollie looked down. His paws were covered in sticky jam and had bits of dirt from playing. "But Mama, I'm already good at swimming! Why do I need to be clean?"

"Let me show you something special," Mama said gently. She led Ollie to the bathroom mirror. "What do you see?"

Ollie giggled at his reflection. His face had purple jam smudges, and his fur looked messy. "I look funny!"

"You do look silly," Mama laughed. "But being clean isn't just about looking nice. When we're clean, we feel better, and we don't get sick. Plus, all the best swimmers know that clean bodies work better in the water!"

Ollie's eyes grew wide. "Really? Clean helps me swim faster?"

"Absolutely! Let's make washing up into a fun game," Mama suggested. "First, we'll wash your paws with warm, soapy water. Can you count to ten while we scrub?"

"One, two, three..." Ollie counted as the warm water and bubbles tickled his paws. The soap smelled like fresh lemons, and the bubbles popped with tiny *pop* sounds that made him giggle.

"Now let's brush your teeth to make them sparkle like pearls in the ocean," Mama said, handing him his special otter-sized toothbrush.

Ollie brushed in small circles, just like Mama taught him. "My teeth feel so smooth and clean!" he said, running his tongue over them.

"Perfect! Now let's wash your face and comb your fur," Mama said. The cool washcloth felt refreshing on Ollie's face, and the soft brush made his fur shine.

When they finished, Ollie looked in the mirror again. "Wow! I look like a champion swimmer now!"

"You certainly do," Mama said proudly. "And how do you feel?"

Ollie thought for a moment. "I feel... fresh! And ready! And happy!" He did a little twirl. "Being clean makes me feel strong and confident!"

At the swimming lesson, Ollie's coach, Mr. Seal, noticed right away. "Ollie, you look ready to learn today! Clean otters are the best swimmers because they take care of themselves."

Ollie beamed with pride as he dove into the pool. His clean body moved smoothly through the water, and he felt wonderful. He even learned his underwater flip on the first try!

That evening, Ollie told Mama, "I love being clean! It makes swimming more fun, and I feel like I can do anything!"

From that day on, Ollie made sure to wash up before every activity. He learned that taking care of his body was just as important as practicing his swimming skills.

The End.
