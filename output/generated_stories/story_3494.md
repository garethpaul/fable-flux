# Bubbles, Brushes, and Sharing

Splash was a playful dolphin who loved water, songs, and shiny things. One sunny morning, Splash visited a cozy house with a big, bright bathroom. The bathroom smelled like sweet orange soap. The mirror sparkled. The floor felt cool and smooth like seashells. Little towels hung like flags, and a basket held many brushes and combs.

“Hello!” Splash clicked and chirped, poking his round head through a bubbly tub filled with warm water. “I’m here to play!”

Two friends ran in. Mina wore a bright scarf on her hair with tiny golden dots. Theo had curly hair and a big giggle. Mina held a toothbrush with stars on the handle. Theo held a blue comb shaped like a fish.

“Hi, Splash!” Mina said. “We are getting ready for the day.”

Theo showed his comb. “I’m going to comb my curls!”

Splash’s eyes grew wide. “Ooh, shiny!” He loved shiny.

Splash reached for the starry toothbrush. “I want the sparkly one!”

Mina hugged her brush close. “This is my toothbrush. It’s just for me.”

Splash blinked. “But it is so pretty. I want it now.”

Theo shook his head kindly. “Toothbrushes are not for sharing. We each have our own. It keeps our mouths healthy.”

Splash’s fins drooped. He puffed a tiny bubble. “Oh. I didn’t know. I just like to share fun things.”

Mina smiled. “We can share other bathroom tools! We can share the sand timer, the music, and the bath toys. We can share turns and space.”

Splash bobbed happily. “I like sharing turns!”

Theo held up the blue comb. “We can share this comb after we clean it. I use it for my hair. Mina sometimes uses a soft brush for her scarf.”

Mina touched her scarf gently. “This scarf is special in my family. My grandma showed me how to wrap it. It makes me feel cozy and proud.”

Splash’s eyes twinkled. “I like that. In my pod, we sing whale songs to say hello. That is special to us.”

Mina clapped. “We can celebrate our differences! Different hair, different songs, different clothes!”

Theo turned on the tap. Water hummed and whooshed. “Let’s do our morning jobs.” The warm steam floated like tiny clouds. It smelled like oranges and sea breeze.

“Jobs?” Splash asked, tilting his head.

“Responsibility,” Mina said carefully. “It means we take care of our things and our bodies. We do what needs to be done.”

Theo nodded. “We can make it fun!”

Theo placed the sand timer on the sink. “Two minutes for brushing. We share the timer.”

He pressed a button on a small speaker. Soft drum beats began, then a bright tune with claps and a flute. “This is music from my grandpa’s country,” Theo said. “He taught me the clapping pattern.”

“Clap-clap, pause, clap!” Mina tried, giggling.

Splash swished his tail in the tub. “I’ll add dolphin clicks!” He clicked and squeaked to the beat.

Mina pointed to a basket. “Splash, you can brush with this extra toothbrush that is new and clean. It has dolphins on it!”

Splash beamed. “A dolphin brush for me? Thank you!” He held it gently. “I will take care of it. That’s my responsibility.”

They all began. “Brush, brush, brush!” sang Mina. Foam puffed up like tiny white clouds. It tasted minty and cool.

“Brush the fronts,” Theo sang. “Brush the backs. Brush the itty-bitty ones!” He made a silly face in the mirror. Splash giggled, bubbles popping softly.

The timer sprinkled sand. “Swish and spit!” Mina said. They rinsed. The water made a musical plink-plink.

“Now hair time,” said Theo. He cleaned the comb with warm water and a dab of soap. “We share the comb by taking turns and keeping it clean.”

Theo combed his curls, slow and gentle. “My curls go boing!” he said. Boing!

Mina watched in the mirror. “Now me.” She smoothed the comb over the ends of her hair under her scarf. “I like the soft brush too. It keeps my scarf neat.”

Splash patted his smooth head. “I don’t have hair. I have smooth dolphin skin. I will share the towel to dry my fins.” He picked a small towel from the stack.

Mina nodded. “We can share the towel if we keep it clean and put it in the basket after.”

Theo showed the basket. “Dirty towels go here. That’s being responsible.”

Splash dried his fins and folded the towel. His fins were warm now. He looked at the starry toothbrush again. He felt a tiny tug in his heart. It was so pretty.

“I still like your sparkly brush,” Splash said softly. “But I am happy with mine. I can share the music, the timer, the comb, and the tub. I can share smiles. I can share my songs.”

Mina squeezed his fin. “Thank you for understanding.”

Theo grinned. “Want to hear another song from my grandpa?”

“Yes!” Splash clicked. “And I will teach you my hello song.”

They sang together: clap-clap, pause, clap! Click-click, squeak, splash! The bathroom felt full of happy sounds and warm steam. The mirror fogged with little hearts Mina drew with her finger. They took turns with the comb, folded towels, and put things back where they belonged.

When they were done, the bathroom was tidy. Their teeth were shiny. Their hearts felt light.

Splash wiggled his tail. “Today I learned: some things we share, and some things we don’t. Sharing means taking turns and caring for each other. Being responsible keeps us safe and happy. And our differences make our songs brighter!”

Mina and Theo cheered. “We share the day!”

Splash splashed one last, gentle splash. “And I share a big thank you.”

The End.
