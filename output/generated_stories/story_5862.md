# Milo’s Balcony Garden Helpers

Milo was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright button eyes. He lived in a cozy nest near a sunny balcony garden. The garden had tiny pots, green leaves, and a little watering can that made a happy drip-drip sound.

One warm morning, Milo stretched. He took a deep breath. The air smelled like mint and tomato vines. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” A gentle breeze tickled Milo’s whiskers.

“Today, I will water the garden,” said Milo. “I can do it myself.”

He picked up the small watering can. It was made of shiny blue metal. Milo tried to lift it. “Oof!” It felt heavy.

“I will use my mouse muscles,” Milo said. He took a steady breath and stood tall. He remembered to be calm. He counted, “One… two… three…” counting helped him feel in control.

He tried again. He moved slowly. Drip-drip, the water fell on the mint leaves. The mint smelled cool and sweet. Milo smiled. “I am strong and careful.”

Just then, his friend Bee buzzed by. “Bzz-bzz! Hello, Milo! Need help?” Bee’s wings hummed like a tiny engine.

Milo thought. “I want to do it myself,” he said, “but the can is heavy. Maybe we can work together.”

Bee nodded. “Teamwork! I can guide you. I can show you where the thirsty leaves are.”

“Good idea,” said Milo. “I will hold the can. You can point.”

They started with the tomatoes. The little yellow flowers were bright like stars. Bee hovered. “This one, bzz! And that one, bzz!”

Milo tilted the can just a little. Drip-drip-drop. The soil drank the water. The sun felt warm on Milo’s ears. He wiped a tiny bead of sweat from his brow. “Whew! Watering is good exercise,” he said. His heart felt happy and strong.

Next came the baby lettuce. The leaves were soft and cool, like tiny green hands. Bee buzzed, “Easy now, bzz. Not too much.”

Milo paused and breathed. “Slow and steady,” he said. He poured just a small drink. He smiled. “I am using self-control. I can stop and think.”

Suddenly, a puff of wind pushed a pot of parsley. It wobbled. “Oh no!” cried Bee.

Milo felt his tummy twist. The pot tipped and dirt spilled. Pat-pat, pat-pat, the soil hit the floor. Milo’s whiskers drooped.

He wanted to rush. He wanted to cry. But he remembered his calm counting. “One… two… three…” He took a breath. “We can fix this,” he said. “We will be a team.”

Bee buzzed close. “I can brush the leaves clean.”

“And I can scoop the soil back,” said Milo. He found a tiny cup. Scoop, scoop. He pressed the dirt with his soft paws. Press-press. The parsley smelled peppery. It looked safe again.

“Good job, team!” said Bee.

Milo’s ears perked up. “Thank you, Bee. I felt worried, but I slowed down. I used my plan.”

They moved to the strawberry pot. Little green berries were hiding under the leaves. Milo giggled. “Peek-a-boo berries!”

Bee pointed with a gentle buzz. “Just a sprinkle here.”

Milo’s arms felt tired now. His muscles tingled. “My body says rest,” he said. He set down the can and shook his paws. Shake-shake. He took a sip from his tiny water cup. “This is how I care for myself.”

Bee landed on the rim of a pot like a tiny yellow jewel. “Breaks help us do our best,” she said.

After a short rest, Milo lifted the can again. It felt lighter. “Teamwork makes hard jobs easier,” he said. Drip-drip, they finished the last pot of basil. The basil smelled spicy and bright.

A snail friend peeked out from behind a leaf. “Hello,” said Snail, slow and shy. “The shade here is nice.”

“Welcome, Snail,” said Milo. “We are watering the garden.”

Snail blinked. “I can help by watching. If a pot wobbles, I will tell you.” He looked very carefully with his tiny eyes.

“Thank you,” said Milo. “That is a helpful job.”

With Bee guiding, Snail watching, and Milo pouring, the balcony garden looked fresh and happy. The leaves glistened. The soil was dark and damp. A rainbow shimmered in a tiny puddle.

“We did it!” Milo cheered. He raised the watering can like a trophy. “I am proud. I used my body, my brain, and my friends.”

Bee buzzed a happy song. “Bzz-bzz-bzz!”

Snail nodded slowly. “Good team.”

Milo put the can away. He patted the pots. Pat-pat-pat. “Thank you, garden,” he said. The breeze whispered back. The birds sang. The sun felt like a warm hug.

Milo thought about the morning. “Today I learned something,” he said. “I can try by myself. I can slow down and breathe. And when a job is big, I can ask friends and we can work together.”

Bee smiled. “That is teamwork, bzz. We notice. We plan. We help.”

Milo’s whiskers twitched with joy. “Teamwork makes us strong. Self-control keeps us calm. Trying my best helps me grow.”

He waved to Bee and Snail. “See you tomorrow for more garden exercise!”

“See you,” said Bee.

“See you,” said Snail.

The balcony garden glowed in the afternoon sun, tidy and watered. Milo curled up in his cozy nest, feeling peaceful and proud. He knew he could be brave and kind, and he knew he could count on his friends too.

The End.
