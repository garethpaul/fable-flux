# Ellie's Big Helper Day

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved spending time with Grandma Elephant in the big, cozy pantry. The pantry smelled like sweet bananas, crunchy peanuts, and fresh hay. Tall shelves reached up to the ceiling, filled with colorful jars and baskets.

"Grandma, what are you doing?" Ellie asked, swinging her little trunk curiously.

"I'm getting ready for the family feast tomorrow, dear one," Grandma said, her wise eyes twinkling. "All our elephant family will come to eat together."

Ellie watched as Grandma reached for jars with her long trunk. Some were too high up, and Grandma had to stretch and stretch. "Ooh," Grandma groaned softly.

"Grandma, you look tired," Ellie said, her big ears flapping with concern. "Can I help you get ready?"

Grandma smiled warmly. "That's very sweet, Ellie, but you're still little. This is grown-up work."

But Ellie had an idea! She looked around the pantry with her bright, curious eyes. "What if we work together? I could help in my own special way!"

"Tell me your idea," Grandma said, sitting down to listen.

Ellie's trunk wiggled with excitement. "You're tall and wise, and I'm small and quick! You can reach the high shelves, and I can gather things from the low shelves. We can make a plan together!"

Grandma's trunk curled into a smile. "That sounds wonderful! But first, let's talk about what we need to do."

They sat together on the soft pantry floor. Ellie felt the cool stones under her bottom as Grandma explained. "We need sweet fruits for the young elephants, crunchy nuts for the teenagers, and fresh vegetables for the grown-ups."

"I can remember that!" Ellie said proudly. "Sweet, crunchy, fresh!"

"Very good," Grandma said. "And we need to wash everything, arrange it nicely, and set up the eating area too."

Ellie nodded eagerly. "Let's make a list! Then we can do everything step by step."

Working together, they planned their preparation. Ellie would collect low items while Grandma gathered high ones. Ellie would wash the small fruits while Grandma cleaned the big vegetables. They would arrange everything together.

"This is much better than working alone," Grandma said as they began. "Your young energy helps me, and my experience helps you!"

Ellie felt so proud as she carefully carried apples in her trunk. The red apples felt smooth and smelled crisp. She washed them gently in the water basin, watching the droplets sparkle.

"Look, Grandma! I'm being extra careful," Ellie said, placing each clean apple in the basket.

"You're doing beautifully," Grandma said. "When we prepare together and plan ahead, everything works so much better."

As the sun set, their pantry looked amazing. Colorful fruits filled one basket, golden nuts filled another, and fresh green vegetables made a third pile. Everything was clean, organized, and ready.

"We did it!" Ellie trumpeted happily.

"Yes, we did," Grandma said, hugging Ellie with her trunk. "You showed great initiative by offering to help, and we negotiated a perfect plan together. This is how our elephant family has always worked - by preparing well and helping each other."

The next day, when all the elephants came to feast, everyone praised how wonderful everything looked and tasted.

"Ellie helped prepare everything," Grandma announced proudly.

Ellie felt warm and happy inside. She had learned that preparing ahead and working together made everything better - and much more fun too!

The End.
