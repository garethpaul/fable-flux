# Cubby Cleans the Pantry

In a cozy cave at the edge of the forest, there was a pantry. The pantry smelled like sweet honey, warm oats, and fresh berries. Little jars twinkled in the dim light. The shelves made a soft creak, like “eeek,” when the door opened.

A friendly bear cub named Cubby scooted in. “Good morning, pantry!” Cubby sang. His paws felt cool on the smooth stone floor. He licked his lips. “I would like a snack!”

Cubby reached for a jar of blueberries. The jar slipped. “Oh!” Clink, clatter, roll! The jar bumped an empty box. The box slid onto a bag of flour—poof! A white cloud floated up. Cubby’s nose twitched. “A-choo!” He sneezed a snowy sneeze.

Cubby looked around. “Oh no.” Crinkly bags lay on the floor. Sticky honey drops dotted the shelf. A paper wrapper hugged his paw. There was a little drip, drip from a leaky bottle.

Cubby’s ears drooped. “The pantry is messy,” he whispered. He felt a squeeze in his tummy. “This mess can hurt our home. Ants might come. Food can spoil. And drips waste good honey.” He touched the cool floor and felt a small puddle. “It is not safe.”

Cubby took a deep breath. He remembered what Mama Bear always said, “We care for our home, and our home cares for us.” He remembered the forest ranger’s visit to his den. “We each have a job,” the ranger had said. “We pick up, we sort, and we share. That is how we take care of the Earth.”

Cubby stood tall. “I can help,” he said to himself. “I can use my voice. I can use my paws.”

First, he padded to the doorway and called, “Mama? Papa? Can we talk?”

Mama Bear poked her head in. “Yes, Cubby? I hear your voice.”

Papa Bear followed, wiping his paws. “What do you need, Cub?”

Cubby pointed with a gentle paw. “The pantry is messy. I feel worried. The sticky drips, the loose lids, and the trash on the floor can hurt our home. Can we fix it together, please?”

Mama nodded. “Thank you for telling us how you feel, Cubby. That is brave.”

Papa smiled. “That is good civic care, Cub. We all use the pantry. We can all help.”

Cubby’s tail nub wiggled. “Can I lead a clean-up?” he asked. “I have a plan.”

“Please lead,” Mama said, tapping her chest. “We will follow.”

Cubby clapped. “Step one: We sort! Reuse, recycle, and trash.” He set out three baskets. He made little signs with big letters. He smelled the ink marker—sniff, sniff. “This one says ‘Recycle.’ This one says ‘Reuse.’ This one says ‘Trash.’”

He held up a crinkly bottle. “This can be recycled.” Plunk! It rang softly in the basket.

He found a clean glass jar. “This we can reuse for oats.” He twisted the lid—click.

He picked up the sticky paper on his paw. “This is trash.” Into the bin it went—thump.

“Step two: We stop the drip!” Cubby dabbed the honey bottle with a cloth. Warm honey smelled like sunshine. He tightened the cap—twist, twist. “All done,” he said.

“Step three: We keep it clean.” He wiped the flour with slow circles. Swish, swish. He swept the floor. Brush, brush. The stone felt smooth again. The air smelled fresh, like pine and berries.

Mama Bear said, “I like your steps. Clear and kind.”

Papa Bear added, “Let’s make a shelf map.” He drew simple pictures: a berry for the berry jar, a bee for the honey, an oat for the oats. “Now we all know where things go.”

Cubby grinned. “I can make a rule, too. May I?”

“What is your idea?” Mama asked.

Cubby spoke in a calm, bright voice. “Please close lids tight. Please pick up what you drop. Please ask for help if you need it. That keeps our home and Earth happy.”

Papa gave a thumbs-up. “Good rules. Good voice.”

They stuck the rules on the door, right at Cubby’s eye level. The paper felt smooth under his paw. Cubby read them out loud. His voice sounded brave and clear.

Later, their neighbors—Squirrel, Rabbit, and Raccoon—peeked in. “It smells nice!” said Rabbit, wiggling his nose.

“It looks shiny,” said Raccoon, patting the clean shelf.

Cubby waved. “We cleaned the pantry. We sorted bottles and jars. We made rules. Would you like to learn our steps?”

Squirrel nodded fast. “Yes, please!”

Cubby showed them the baskets. “Recycle, reuse, trash,” he said. He showed them the map and rules. “We can all help the Earth. We are little, but our choices are big.”

Squirrel picked up a loose lid. “I can twist it tight.”

Rabbit folded a box for recycling. “I can flatten it.”

Raccoon rinsed a jar. “I can make it ready to reuse.”

Cubby’s heart felt warm and proud. He had used peaceful words. He had asked for help. He had led with care. The pantry was tidy and safe. The forest friends smiled together.

Mama Bear brought out fresh berries. “Snack time,” she sang.

Cubby took a juicy bite. “Mmm! Thank you. Caring for our home makes it taste even better.”

Everyone laughed, soft and happy. The pantry hummed with good sounds: clink of clean jars, whisper of paper, and the gentle creak of the shelf.

Cubby looked at the sign one more time. “We care for our home, and our home cares for us,” he said.

The End.
