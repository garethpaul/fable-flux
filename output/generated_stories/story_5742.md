# Koala Kiki’s Pantry Parade

Koala Kiki was calm and cozy. She lived near a little house with a bright blue door. Inside the house was a pantry that smelled like warm bread and sweet apples. One sunny morning, Kiki peeked in. Rows of jars sparkled. Crackers crunched softly in their box. The cool tile floor tick-ticked under Kiki’s soft feet.

“Hello, pantry,” Kiki whispered. “I’m here to help with snack time.”

Kiki wasn’t alone. Her friends—Sparky Squirrel, Benny Bunny, and Dotty Duck—came waddling and hopping in. Their tummies made tiny rumble sounds. They all wanted snacks!

Sparky grabbed a shiny jar. Benny reached for a box at the same time. Dotty flapped her wings and said, “Quack! Crackers, please!”

The jars wobbled. The box slid. A spoon clinked and clattered to the floor.

Kiki took a calm breath. “Friends,” she said softly, “we need a plan.”

Sparky’s tail twitched. “I’m hungry now,” he squeaked.

Benny’s ears drooped. “Me too,” he said.

Dotty’s feathers fluffed. “Quack! Can I stand here?” She stepped closer to Kiki.

Kiki held up her paws. “Personal space, please,” she said kindly. “I need a little room to move my paws.”

Dotty blinked. “Oh! Sorry, Kiki.” She stepped back and smiled. “Is this better?”

“Yes,” Kiki said. “Thank you. That is my boundary. I will respect yours too.”

Kiki looked around the pantry at the shelves, the jars, and the boxes. She could hear the soft crunch of crackers inside the box and smell the sweet peanut butter in the jar. She could feel the cool air and see the bright light from the window. The pantry felt full and busy.

“I have an idea,” Kiki said. “Let’s make a Pantry Parade. We will work together, one by one. That is called coordination. We move in order, like a gentle dance.”

Sparky’s eyes lit up. “A dance?”

Benny tapped his foot, thump-thump. “In a line?”

Dotty bobbed her head. “Quack-quack, a parade!”

Kiki nodded. “Yes. We will line up with space between us. We will take turns. We will wait. We will use gentle hands and gentle voices.”

Kiki placed three colorful mats on the floor. “Sparky, you stand on the red mat. Benny, you take the blue mat. Dotty, the yellow mat is for you. I will stand here and help.”

They all stepped onto their mats. Sparky swished his tail but kept it on his mat. Benny’s ears wiggled but stayed in his spot. Dotty fluffed her feathers and held still.

Kiki spoke calmly. “First, Sparky will open the cracker box. Then he will pass it to Benny. Benny will count three crackers, one-two-three, and pass the box to Dotty. Dotty will pass it back to me, and I will close it tight. We will move slow and sure. That is coordination.”

Sparky smiled. “I can do that.”

Benny nodded. “I can count.”

Dotty quacked softly. “I can pass.”

Sparky reached for the box with two hands. “I’m ready,” he said. He looked at Kiki. “Is it my turn?”

“Yes,” Kiki said. “It is your turn.”

Sparky opened the box with a gentle rip. Crunchy smells floated out. “Mmm!” he said. He passed the box to Benny and kept his paws to himself.

Benny counted, “One, two, three!” He placed three crackers on a small plate. He did not take extra. “I will be honest,” Benny said. “Three is three.” He passed the box to Dotty.

Dotty took the box and flapped once. “Quack! My turn to pass.” She kept her wings low and far from Benny’s ears. “Is this your space?” she asked.

“Yes,” Benny said. “Thank you for asking.”

Dotty smiled and passed the box to Kiki. Kiki closed it with a soft snap. “Well done, team,” she said. “We moved in order. We waited our turns. We kept our bodies in our own space. And we were honest with the crackers. That is integrity.”

They tried again with the peanut butter jar. Kiki said, “We need a spoon. We will share the spoon. One scoop each. We will wipe the spoon clean each time.”

Sparky took the spoon when it was his turn. He scooped one time and stopped. “I want more,” he said, “but I will tell the truth. I had my turn.” He licked his lips and handed the spoon to Benny.

Benny took one scoop and gave the spoon to Dotty. Dotty took one scoop and gave the spoon back to Kiki. Kiki wiped the spoon and put it in the sink.

They all sat on the mats with their snacks. Crunch, crunch. Mmm, mmm. The pantry was quiet and happy.

Dotty leaned close, then paused. “Kiki, may I sit next to you?”

“Yes,” Kiki said. “Thank you for asking. That respects my boundary.”

Sparky smiled. “We did a Pantry Parade!”

Benny giggled. “We danced with our hands!”

Kiki nodded. “We coordinated. That means we moved together in a smart way. We used our bodies and our words to help each other. We waited our turns. We kept to our spaces. We told the truth.”

Sparky said, “When we coordinate, we don’t bump or spill.”

Benny said, “When we respect space, we feel safe.”

Dotty said, “When we tell the truth, everyone can trust us.”

Kiki finished her cracker and stood up. “Friends, we solved the pantry problem with calm, care, and coordination.”

They all cheered, “Hooray!”

The pantry smelled sweet and clean. The jars were closed tight. The floor was neat. The friends felt proud and peaceful.

Kiki gave a cozy smile. “Next time, we will make our parade again. We can do hard things when we work together.”

The End.
