# Tilly Turtle Builds at the Big Table

Tilly was a shy turtle with a smooth green shell. She liked quiet. She liked soft things, like warm toast and gentle hugs. One sunny morning, Tilly slid into the dining room. The big table smelled like pancakes and syrup. The chairs were tall, and the window let in golden light. On the table sat a box of bright, colorful blocks.

Clack, clack, clack went the blocks when she touched them. Tilly liked that sound. It was happy and tidy. She wanted to build a little tower. Maybe a turtle house! But her voice was small, like a whisper. Her cheeks felt warm.

Just then, her friend Max the bunny hopped in. His ears bounced. “Hi, Tilly!” he said. “Can we build?”

Tilly took a tiny breath. “Y-yes,” she said. “Let’s build.” Her words felt brave and light.

Sasha the squirrel scampered in next. “Blocks!” she squeaked. “Let’s make the tallest tower ever!”

Everyone climbed onto chairs. The blocks were smooth and cool. Red, blue, green, yellow. They smelled a little like fresh wood. The big table felt steady under Tilly’s small hands.

Tilly held a blue block. “I want to start,” she whispered. Her voice was quiet, but she tried to make it stronger. “May I put the first block?”

Max’s ears twitched. “Let’s take turns,” he said. “That is fair.”

Sasha nodded. “Okay! Tilly first, then me, then Max.”

Tilly smiled. Her heart thumped thump-thump, but she felt proud. She put the blue block in the middle. Clack. It sat straight and safe.

Sasha added a red block on top. Clack.

Max added a yellow block next. Clack.

Tilly took a green block. Her hands were steady now. “I want to make a door,” she said. “For my turtle house.” She looked at her friends. “Is that okay?”

“Yes!” said Max.

“Great idea!” chirped Sasha.

They built together, one by one. Clack. Clack. Clack. The tower was growing. The door was a little gap at the bottom. Tilly could peek through it and see the table.

Tilly’s tummy made a happy flutter. She was being brave and using her voice. She was taking turns, and it felt good.

Then Sasha got very excited. She picked up three blocks at once. “I want to go faster!” she said. “I want to put all of these on now!”

Tilly felt a tight squeeze in her chest. She looked at the tall stack. It wobbled a little. “Um…” she said, soft.

Max put down his block. “Sasha,” he said kindly, “we are taking turns.”

Sasha’s tail twitched. She looked at Tilly. “Is it okay if I go again now?”

Tilly took a slow, brave breath. She thought about the tower. She thought about the door. She thought about how taking turns made the tower safe and strong.

“I would like my turn now,” Tilly said, a little louder. “Let’s keep our turns. That is fair. That is safe.”

Sasha blinked. She smiled. “Okay, Tilly. Your turn.”

Tilly’s shoulders relaxed. “Thank you,” she said. She placed a long flat block across the top. Clack. “This is the roof.”

Max clapped his paws softly. “Nice roof!”

They kept building. They listened to each other’s ideas. Max said, “Let’s make a window.” Tilly said, “Yes, on the side.” Sasha said, “Let’s add a chimney!” and they all giggled.

Clack. Clack. Clack. The tower was tall and straight. It made a little shadow on the table. The colors shone in the morning light. Tilly ran her finger along the smooth blocks. She could feel tiny bumps and cool edges.

Suddenly, the tower shook. A chair bumped the table. Wobble, wobble! “Oh no!” said Max.

Tilly put both hands around the tower, gentle and firm. “Wait,” she said clearly. “Let’s hold it steady. One at a time, let’s add support.”

Sasha and Max nodded. They put their hands near the base. Tilly guided them. “Sasha, a block here. Max, a block there.” Her voice was calm. She felt strong.

Clack. Clack.

The tower stopped wobbling. It stood tall again.

“We did it!” cheered Sasha.

Tilly smiled so wide her cheeks felt warm again, but with happy heat. “We built it together,” she said. “We took turns. We used our voices.”

Max peeked through the door. “Tilly, your house is cozy!”

Tilly laughed. “Let’s invite tiny toys for tea!” They set little cups around the tower. The cups clinked softly. The room smelled like syrup and warm toast again. The light made the blocks glow.

Sasha poured pretend tea. “To builders!” she said.

“To taking turns,” said Max.

Tilly lifted her cup. “To being brave and speaking up,” she said. Her voice was still gentle, but now it carried across the table.

They sipped pretend tea and looked at their work. The tower was bright and strong. It felt like a happy hug in the middle of the dining room.

Tilly touched her shell and took a deep breath. She had started shy. She had stayed kind. She had been brave. She had helped the building be safe and fair.

Lesson learned: When we build, we take turns, we share ideas, and we use our voices kindly. That makes strong towers and happy friends.

The End.
