# Mango’s Music Circle

Mango was a small, courageous monkey with bright eyes and quick fingers. He loved sounds. Tap, tap. Tinkle, tinkle. Boom, boom! The musical practice room at the tree school was full of shiny instruments. The room smelled like polished wood and bananas from snack time. Sunlight made tiny dust sparkles float in the air.

“Today we make a music circle,” said Miss Bell, the kind teacher. She shook a bell. Ding!

Mango bounced. “I want to play the big drum!” he said.

“Everyone can play,” Miss Bell smiled. “We will take turns. We will listen. We will include all friends.”

Around the room, friends chose instruments. Tia the turtle picked a soft shaker. Swish, swish. Pip the parrot chose bright blue maracas. Chik-chik! Nib the mouse held a tiny triangle. Ting!

In the corner, a new friend stood very still. It was Lala the little lizard. Lala hugged herself and looked at the floor. Her tail curled tight.

Mango waved. “Hi, Lala! Do you want to play?”

Lala whispered, “I’m not sure. I feel small. My hands get sticky. I don’t know the songs.”

Mango felt a warm squeeze in his heart. He remembered when he was new. He remembered feeling shy. He took a slow breath. In—out. He wanted everyone to feel welcome.

Miss Bell clapped softly. “Let’s begin with a hello song. We sing, we play, and we wait for our friends.”

Mango reached for the big drum. Boom! The sound was loud, like thunder. Pip laughed and shook the maracas faster. Chik-chik-chik!

“Wait,” said Tia. “I can’t hear Nib.”

Nib tapped the triangle. Ting… but the big drum covered it up.

Mango looked at his hands. He liked the big boom. But he also liked his friends’ sounds. He remembered Miss Bell’s words: We include all friends. We listen.

He whispered to himself, “I can do the right thing.” That was his integrity. He put down the big drum and picked up two small sticks.

Tap, tap. Soft and steady. Now everyone could hear. Swish, chik-chik, ting, and tap!

Miss Bell nodded. “Thank you, Mango. That was kind.”

The music circle felt warm and bright. But Lala still stood in the corner. Her eyes were watery.

Mango thought, I will try again. He walked over slowly. He crouched so his face met Lala’s eyes. “When I was new, I felt twisty inside,” he said. “Would you like to sit by me? We can go slow.”

Lala looked up. “Slow sounds okay.”

Mango found a gentle tambourine with tiny jingles. He wiped it with a soft cloth. “Sometimes hands feel sticky,” he said. “We can clean the tambourine together.” They wiped, wipe-wipe, until it shined.

He showed Lala how to hold it. “You can shake it like a leaf in the wind,” he said, “or tap it like raindrops. Want to try?”

Lala gave a tiny shake. Jing… Then a tiny tap. Jing-tap. Her tail loosened a little.

Miss Bell began a new game. “Echo time! I play, we listen, we copy. Everyone can try. If we make a mistake, we try again. That’s resilience.”

Mango smiled. He loved trying again. He played: tap, tap, tap. The class echoed: tap, tap, tap. Lala watched. Her tongue flicked with focus.

“Lala, would you like to lead an echo?” asked Miss Bell.

Lala froze. “I don’t think I can.”

Mango put a gentle hand near, not touching, just close. “We can do it together,” he whispered. “I will follow you.”

Lala took a breath. She lifted the tambourine. Jing… jing-jing.

Silence. Then Mango echoed, “Jing… jing-jing!” Tia echoed. Pip echoed. Nib echoed. Soon the whole room echoed Lala’s sound. The air filled with soft jingles and happy smiles.

Lala’s eyes widened. “They followed me!” she said.

“They did,” said Mango. “Your sound matters.”

Miss Bell beamed. “We include every voice. We listen with our ears and our hearts.”

The next game was “Mix and Match.” Each friend played a little pattern. Mango wanted to try a fast roll with the drumsticks. He tried. Tap-tap-tap-tap—oops! One stick slipped. Clatter!

Some friends giggled. Mango’s cheeks felt hot. He could have stopped. But he remembered resilience: try again. He picked up the stick, took a breath, and tried slower. Tap… tap… tap. Then a happy roll: tap-tap-tap-tap-tap! The room cheered. Lala clapped her tiny hands. Jing!

“Good trying, Mango,” Miss Bell said. “You kept going.”

Soon the circle’s sound became a song. The room smelled like orange slices now, as snacks came out. The sunlight felt warm on Mango’s fur. He looked around. Every friend had a place. Every sound was heard. Lala was smiling and sitting tall. She led one more echo, and everyone followed.

At the end, Miss Bell asked, “What did we learn today?”

Mango raised his hand. “We learned to include everyone. We listen so small sounds can be heard. We tell the truth and do the right thing. We keep trying when it’s hard.”

Lala nodded. “And my sound matters when friends make space for me.”

Miss Bell tapped her bell. Ding! “Yes. In our music circle, every voice belongs.”

Mango’s heart felt big and bright. “In our circle,” he said, “no one is left behind.”

The End.
