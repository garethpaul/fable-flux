# Monty’s Backyard Workout

Monty was a small, courageous monkey with bright eyes and a happy tail. He lived with his family near a sunny backyard. The grass felt warm and tickly on his toes. The air smelled like sweet oranges from the tree. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” from the fence.

“Good morning, sunshine!” Monty said, stretching his arms up high. He loved to move. He loved to jump. He loved to try new things. Today, he wanted to have a big exercise adventure.

Mama Monkey handed him a little timer shaped like a banana. “Here you go, Monty,” she said with a smile. “This timer can help you plan your fun. You can exercise and still have time for snacks and rest.”

Monty’s eyes sparkled. “A timer? For a plan?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Mama. “A plan helps your day feel calm and strong. Let’s set three little goals.”

Monty clapped. “Three goals! I am ready!”

Mama pointed to the sunny yard. “First, warm-up stretches. Second, a backyard obstacle path. Third, a cool-down and water break.”

Monty nodded. “I can do it!” He pressed the timer. “Five minutes for stretching!”

He stood tall. “Arms up like the sun,” he said, reaching high. The sky felt big and blue. He touched his toes. “Hello, toes!” He twisted side to side. “Wiggle-waggle!” The grass brushed his fingers. A butterfly floated by, soft as a whisper.

Monty giggled. “I feel warm and ready!”

The timer beeped. Beep! Beep! “Stretching done,” Monty said. “Now the obstacle path!”

He looked around the sunny backyard. There was a little path of smooth stones. There was a red hula hoop on the grass. There were chalk lines like balance beams. A small ball waited by the fence.

Monty took a breath. “I will go slow and steady. I will be brave and careful.”

He set the timer. “Ten minutes for the obstacle path!”

First, he hopped on the smooth stones. “Hop-hop-hop!” His feet went light and quick. The stones felt cool on his toes. He counted, “One, two, three, four!” A robin cheered, “Chirp-chirp!”

Next, he lifted the red hula hoop. He swung it around his belly. “Whoosh-whoosh!” The hoop made a soft breeze. It tickled his fur. He laughed and tried again when it fell. “It’s okay,” he told himself. “Try, try, try!” On the third try, the hoop whirled. “Round and round!” Monty smiled wide. “I did it!”

Then he walked on the chalk lines. “Heel-to-toe, heel-to-toe,” he whispered. His arms reached out like airplane wings. The chalk felt dusty and smooth. A bumblebee hummed nearby, “Bzzz,” like a tiny drum. Monty kept his eyes on the line. “Steady, steady,” he said. He wobbled once and then found his balance. “Yes!”

He picked up the small ball last. “Throw and catch,” he said. He tossed it up. “Boing!” It landed in his hands. He tossed to the fence and caught the bounce. “Good hands, Monty,” he cheered. He tried gently. He tried higher. He tried with one clap in between. Clap! Catch! He felt proud and strong.

The timer beeped. Beep! Beep! Monty took a big breath. “Obstacle path done!”

Just then, his friend Pip the puppy peeked through the gate. “Woof! What are you doing?” Pip’s tail swished like a little broom.

“Hi, Pip! I’m exercising,” said Monty. “I made a plan. I used my time. Do you want to try the path with me?”

Pip wagged. “Woof-yes!”

Monty smiled. “We will take turns. We will count. We will cheer.”

They set the timer. “Four minutes for Pip,” Monty said. Pip hopped on the stones. “Hop-hop!” Monty clapped. “Go, Pip, go!” Pip tried the hula hoop on the ground, stepping in and out. “Step-step!” Monty giggled. “Great feet!” Pip walked the chalk line, nose sniffing, ears flopping. “Steady!” Monty encouraged. Pip nudged the ball with his nose and caught it with his paws. “Woof!”

The timer beeped. Beep! Beep!

“Great job, Pip!” Monty said. “Now it’s time for cool-down.”

They set the timer again. “Three minutes for cool-down and water.”

Monty and Pip breathed in and out. “In… and out…” The air smelled like warm grass. The sun kissed their faces. They stretched their arms and legs slowly. They took small sips of cool water. It tasted fresh and sweet.

Mama came outside with a smile and a bowl of orange slices. “How did it go?”

Monty stood tall. “I used my timer. I did my stretches. I did my obstacle path. I cooled down and drank water. I exercised my body and my brain!”

Pip wagged. “Woof-woof!”

Mama hugged Monty. “I’m proud of you. You showed courage. You used time well. You practiced coordination with your stones, hoop, line, and ball.”

Monty grinned. “Exercise helps me feel strong, calm, and happy. A plan helps me finish my fun.”

He shared orange slices with Pip. The juicy fruit popped in his mouth. “Mmm!”

Monty looked at the sunny backyard and the friendly fence and the blue, blue sky. “Tomorrow,” he said, “I will make a new plan.”

And the birds sang, “Cheep-cheep!” like a gentle cheer.

The End.
