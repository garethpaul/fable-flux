# Emi Listens to the Breeze

The afternoon sun made warm golden stripes across the peaceful front porch. Emi, a gentle elephant calf with soft ears and bright eyes, sat on a woven mat. The porch smelled like fresh lemons from the tree nearby. The wooden rail felt smooth under her trunk. Birds chirped little songs, and a sleepy cat purred on the step.

Emi’s mama had set out a tiny basket with picture cards and a little bell. “This is our quiet-play basket,” Mama said with a smile. “You can play on your own, Emi. Listen to your feelings. If you need help, try using your words. I believe in you.”

Emi nodded, but her tummy felt wiggly. She wanted to feel brave and calm, but her feelings were mixed up, like marbles rolling in a bowl.

She picked up a picture card. It showed a sunny face. Another card showed a cloud with raindrops. Another had a smile and a sad tear together. Emi blinked. “What do these mean?” she whispered.

A breeze brushed her cheeks. The leaves whispered shhh-shhh. The bell in the basket winked at her. Emi tapped it with her trunk. Ting! The tiny sound sparkled in the air.

“Hello, bell,” Emi said softly. “I feel…” She looked at the picture cards. She touched the sunny face. “A little happy to be outside.” She touched the cloud. “A little sad that I miss Papa, who is at the market.” She touched the smile with a tear. “A little both.”

Her chest felt lighter. The words helped.

The cat stretched and meowed. “Mrrr?”

Emi nodded to the cat. “I am learning to say my feelings.” The cat blinked slowly and curled back up.

Emi decided to play a listening game. She closed her eyes. She heard a bee buzz by. Bzzzz. She heard the wind chimes sing. Ding-ding-dong. She heard her own breath, soft and slow. In. Out. In. Out. Each sound felt like a color to her. The bee sounded yellow. The chimes sounded silver. Her breath sounded blue and calm.

She opened her eyes and smiled. “I can name what I hear. I can name what I feel.” She touched the sunny card and said, “Happy.” She touched the cloud card and said, “Sad.” She touched another card with a little lightning bolt and said, “A tiny bit mad, too.” Her voice shook, just a little. “I want Papa to come back now.”

Her ears drooped. A tear slipped down her cheek. Plip.

Emi took a deep breath, like Mama taught her. She breathed in for four tiny counts. One, two, three, four. She breathed out for four tiny counts. One, two, three, four. She rang the bell again. Ting.

“I can help myself,” she said, wiping her tear with her soft trunk. “I can use calm breaths. I can use words.” The words felt like gentle steps under her feet.

To practice, she made a feeling song. She tapped the wooden rail with her trunk, tap-tap, and sang in a small, steady voice:

“Sunny happy, rainy sad,
Little waiting makes me mad.
Breathe in slow, breathe out, too,
Hello feelings, I see you.”

Emi’s song floated out into the warm air. The breeze seemed to sing along. The lemon tree leaves clapped softly. The cat purred a bass line.

Mama peeked out the door but did not come onto the porch. She listened and smiled, letting Emi practice on her own.

Emi looked into the basket again. She found a small notebook and a stubby pencil. On the first page, she drew a big round sun. She whispered, “Happy.” On the second page, she drew a blue raindrop. She whispered, “Sad.” On the third page, she drew a small red zigzag. She whispered, “Mad.” She labeled each picture with a careful letter or two, sounding out the words: “Ha-ppy.” “Sad.” “Mad.” The pencil made soft scratchy sounds, skritch-scratch, skritch-scratch.

“I like words,” Emi told the cat. “Words are tools. Words are friends.” She traced the letters with her trunk. The letters felt bumpy and good.

A cloud moved over the sun for a moment, and the porch became cooler and dim. Emi felt the change like a tiny shiver. She hugged herself. “Hello, shiver,” she said. “You mean I am a little worried. I can wait. I can play.”

She decided to build a porch fort. She leaned two cushions together and draped a light scarf over them. The scarf smelled like clean soap and sunshine. Inside the fort, the world sounded soft and close. She set the bell inside. Ting. She placed her picture cards like little flags. Sun. Rain. Zigzag.

“Welcome, feelings,” she said. “This is a safe fort.” Emi smiled at her own idea. She made her own cozy space. She felt proud. Her heart puffed up like a small balloon.

Suddenly, there were footsteps on the path. Clip-clop, clip-clop. Emi’s ears perked up. She peeked from the fort.

“Papa!” she cried.

Papa elephant waved and climbed the porch steps. He smelled like the market—warm bread and ripe fruit. “Hello, Emi,” he said, kneeling down. “How are you feeling?”

Emi took a breath. She thought of the cards. She thought of her song. She stood tall. “I felt happy to be outside,” she said. “I felt sad and a little mad while I waited. I used my words. I sang my feeling song. I built a fort. Now I feel proud and calm.”

Papa’s eyes shone. “You listened to your feelings. You helped yourself. And you used beautiful words.”

Emi handed him the bell. “Ting,” she chimed. “That means I did it.”

Papa hugged her gently. Mama joined them and hugged, too. The porch felt extra warm, like a blanket of light.

Mama asked, “What did you learn today?”

Emi smiled. “I learned that feelings talk, and I can listen. I can name them with words. I can help myself with calm breaths and good ideas. And I can wait.”

Papa nodded. “That is a strong heart and a smart mind.”

Emi rang the bell once more—ting! The cat meowed. The wind chimes sang. The lemon tree swayed. Emi took one more deep breath. In. Out. She felt safe, brave, and loved.

The End.
