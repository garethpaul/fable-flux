# Ellie’s Balcony Garden Parade

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and twinkly eyes. She loved her small balcony garden. The balcony smelled like mint and wet soil. Sunlight warmed the big leaves. Tiny bells on the railing jingled in the breeze. Ellie watered the plants with her little blue watering can and hummed, “Drip, drop, splash-splash!”

Today, Ellie had a big goal. “I want to make a Balcony Garden Parade,” she said. “Plants from all over the world can dance! I will learn about each one.” She clapped her feet and set her goal: learn, clean, and share.

Ellie’s friends came to help. Mei the sparrow fluttered in with a tiny red scarf. Omar the kitten padded over, paws quiet as clouds. Priya the bunny hopped, her ears wiggling. And Nana Lila, Ellie’s wise llama neighbor, leaned over the railing with a smile.

“Hello, friends!” Ellie trumpeted softly. “Our garden has plants from many places. We will learn about them, keep them clean, and have a parade!”

Mei chirped, “I see cilantro! In my family, we use it to make dumplings smell fresh.”

Omar purred, “There’s mint! We put mint in tea at my house. It makes my nose feel cool.”

Priya sniffed, “And turmeric! My mama uses it to make warm golden milk. It smells sunny.”

Nana Lila nodded. “Every plant has a story. Every friend has a way.”

Ellie’s trunk tickled the mint. “Tingle, tingle,” she giggled. She was careful and clean. She washed her trunk in a little basin. Swish-swish. She wiped it with a towel. “Clean trunk, clean plants,” she said. “Good hygiene keeps us safe.”

They placed little flags by each plant. Mei drew a tiny dumpling. Omar drew a steaming cup. Priya drew a bright sun. Ellie drew a globe. The garden looked like a rainbow of ideas.

Then—oh no! A puff of dust blew in. Achoo! The cilantro drooped. The mint looked muddy. The turmeric pot tipped, leaving a yellow streak. Ellie’s ears drooped, too. “I wanted a shiny parade,” she said. “Now everything is messy.”

Mei chirped, “It’s okay.”

Omar flicked his tail. “We can fix it.”

Priya nodded. “We can try together.”

But Ellie felt a little grumpy. She stomped, just a tiny stomp, and a few petals fell. “I worked so hard,” she whispered. “I’m upset.”

Nana Lila breathed slowly. “Feelings are real,” she said. “Let’s wash our hands and try again.”

Everyone washed. Scrub-a-scrub. Soap bubbles smelled like oranges. They sang, “Wash, wash, wash,” for a long, slow count to twenty. Ellie smiled at the bubbles. “I feel calmer,” she said.

They made a plan. Ellie set small goals:
- Goal 1: Clean the leaves.
- Goal 2: Learn one new fact about each plant.
- Goal 3: Ask each friend to share a tradition.

“Teamwork,” Ellie said. “Together is best.”

Mei dipped a soft cloth in water. “Cilantro likes a gentle wipe,” she said. “My grandma says gentle hands make good food.” Wipe, wipe. The leaves shone.

Omar brushed the mint with a clean brush. “Mint likes space,” he said. “At my house, we breathe the mint steam when we have a stuffy nose.” Brush, brush. Ellie leaned close and felt the cool smell. “Ahhh,” she sighed.

Priya patted turmeric soil back into the pot. “Turmeric can color food,” she said. “And we sometimes make pretty patterns on paper.” Pat, pat. The yellow streak looked like sunshine now.

Ellie used her clean trunk to sweep the balcony floor. Swish-swish. “I learned that cilantro seeds are called coriander,” she said proudly. “That is new knowledge!”

“Wonderful,” said Nana Lila. She sprinkled a little water. “Learning about each other is like watering friendship.”

Just then, a tiny beetle crawled from under a leaf. It had knocked the turmeric pot before! Ellie blinked. “Oh,” she said softly. “I got upset at the mess. I thought someone was careless. Friends, I’m sorry.”

Mei fluttered. “We forgive you, Ellie.”

Omar purred. “We know you care.”

Priya smiled. “We all make mistakes.”

Ellie’s heart felt warm. “Thank you,” she said. “I forgive the little beetle, too. It was just exploring.”

At last, the Balcony Garden Parade began. Mei flew in a circle, singing, “Cilantro, cilantro!” Omar marched with mint leaves tucked behind his ears, chanting, “Minty fresh!” Priya twirled with a ribbon the color of turmeric, laughing, “Golden swirl!” Ellie walked slowly, trumpeting a gentle song, “Many places, one garden.”

They shared stories as they paraded.
Mei said, “In my family, we fold dumplings together.”
Omar said, “We pour tea and sit close.”
Priya said, “We celebrate with bright colors.”
Ellie said, “I listen, learn, and keep things clean.”

The balcony felt like a tiny world. It smelled like herbs, sunshine, and orange soap. Bells jingled. Leaves whispered. Everyone’s ways were different. Everyone’s ways were special. Together, they made the garden strong.

Ellie raised her trunk. “Today we learned:
- Different cultures have beautiful traditions.
- Clean hands and clean tools help plants and people.
- Small goals make big dreams happen.
- Saying sorry and forgiving bring smiles.”

Nana Lila winked. “That is wise knowledge for a young elephant.”

Ellie wrapped her trunk gently around her friends. “Our garden is for everyone,” she said. “Let’s keep learning, washing, and sharing.”

The sun dipped low. The flags glowed softly. The friends waved goodbye with happy hearts.

The End.
