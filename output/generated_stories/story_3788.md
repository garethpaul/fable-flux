# Pippa Penguin on the Rooftop Garden

Pippa was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a waddly walk. Today, Pippa climbed the stairs, step step step, all the way to the rooftop. The air felt cool on her feathers. The city sounded soft and far away—hummm from cars, tweet-tweet from birds. The sun was warm and yellow. Puffy clouds drifted like marshmallows.

On the rooftop, there was a small garden. Little pots stood in rows. Green leaves waved hello. The door creaked—squeak—and Pippa popped out with a smile. “Good morning, garden!” she said.

Pippa heard a tiny cry. “Oh no! Oh no!” squeaked Nia the squirrel. Nia’s tail was fluffy and shaking. She pointed with a paw. A bright red kite was stuck on the wiggly clothesline. The wind tugged it—flap, flap, flutter! The string was tied in a knot.

“My kite!” Nia said. “I tried to help, but I can’t reach.”

Pippa waddled closer. She felt the wind brush her cheeks. She smelled mint from a green leaf. “I can help,” said Pippa. “Helping is my favorite.”

Nia blinked. “Really? Thank you!”

Just then, Tiko the tiny sparrow landed with a chirp. “I saw the kite! I can fly. Maybe I can help too!”

Pippa looked at her friends. “We can help together,” she said. “Teamwork!”

Pippa tried first. She stretched on her tip-toes. She reached and reached. Her flippers waved. “Ooof!” She was still too short. The kite danced away from her touch.

“It’s okay,” said Pippa. “Let’s think. We can be resourceful. We can use what we have.”

She looked around. She saw a watering can, a bucket, a soft rope, and a little garden stool. The stool looked steady. The rope looked strong.

“Open minds, open eyes,” Pippa said softly. “Let’s try a new idea.”

Nia’s ears perked up. “What if we stack something?”

Pippa nodded. “Good idea. The stool is small. It can help me be taller.” She set the stool beside the clothesline. The stool made a tiny clunk on the rooftop.

Tiko flapped his wings. “I can fly up and hold the kite still!”

“And I can hold the stool,” said Nia. “I can keep you safe.”

“Thank you,” said Pippa. “Helping hands, helping paws, helping wings!”

The wind puffed. Whish! The kite wiggled. “Ready?” asked Pippa.

“Ready!” chirped Tiko.

“Ready!” squeaked Nia.

Tiko zipped up—whoosh!—and pecked gently at the kite’s ribbon. “I’ve got it!” he called.

Pippa stepped on the stool. It felt firm under her feet. Nia held it tight with both paws. “I’ve got you,” Nia said, tail wrapped around the leg.

Pippa reached again. Stretch, stretch. She could feel the rough string brush her flipper. “Almost,” she whispered.

“Try the rope,” said Nia. “We can loop it!”

Pippa smiled. “Yes! Good idea.” She made a loop in the soft rope, like a big round donut. She tossed the loop up. Whoop! The loop slipped over the clothesline.

Tiko steadied the kite. “Nice throw!” he chirped.

Pippa pulled the rope gently. The loop tugged the knot closer. “Easy does it,” she said. Pull, pause. Pull, pause. The knot slid toward her.

“I can help with the knot,” said Nia. “I am good with tiny ties.” Pippa lowered the knot into Nia’s paws. Nia nibbled and nipped, tiny teeth tapping—tick, tick. The knot opened. The string went free.

“We did it!” said Pippa. The kite dipped into her flippers. It felt smooth and paper-light. The sun made the red color glow.

Nia jumped and clapped. “Thank you, Pippa! Thank you, Tiko!”

The three friends laughed. The wind carried their happy sound across the rooftop—ha ha, hee hee. Pippa handed the kite to Nia. “We helped together,” she said. “Helping feels good.”

Nia hugged the kite. “I was worried,” she said. “I didn’t know what to do.”

“It’s okay to feel worried,” said Pippa. “We stopped. We looked. We tried new ideas. We asked friends. That is resourceful.”

Tiko puffed his chest. “Open minds make strong teams!”

Pippa nodded. “Yes. And helpful hearts make smiles.”

They decided to celebrate. Pippa filled the watering can. Glug, glug. She gave each plant a drink. Nia gently patted the soil. Pat, pat. Tiko sang a soft song—tweet-tweet-twee. The mint leaves smelled fresh. The tomato plant had tiny yellow flowers. The breeze felt cool on their faces.

“Let’s fly the kite once, very carefully,” said Pippa. “We will hold the string together.”

They each held a part of the string—flipper, paw, wing. The kite rose up—whoooosh!—and danced like a happy fish in the sky. They giggled and took turns. No one pulled too hard. Everyone listened.

Soon, they wound the string back in. The kite came down softly into Nia’s arms. “Safe and sound,” Nia said.

Pippa looked at her friends. “Today we learned something,” she said.

“What did we learn?” asked Tiko.

“We learned that being helpful is kind,” said Pippa. “We learned to try new ideas with open minds. We learned that teamwork makes big jobs feel small.”

Nia nodded. “And we learned the rooftop is a nice place to help.”

Pippa smiled. “Yes. We can be helpers every day.”

The sun dipped low. The wind turned gentle. They waved goodbye and headed to the door. The hinge went squeak as it closed.

Pippa waddled down the stairs, heart warm and light. She whispered, “Helping makes the world smile.”

The End.
