# Rosie Rabbit's Big Helper Heart

Rosie Rabbit loved her cozy garden shed. It smelled like fresh dirt and sweet flowers. The wooden walls were warm from the sunshine, and her favorite watering can sat on the shelf.

One morning, Rosie heard a tiny voice crying. "Help! Help!"

She twitched her soft ears and looked around. "Who's there?" she called.

"Down here!" squeaked the voice.

Rosie looked down and saw a little field mouse stuck under a heavy flowerpot. His whiskers were trembling, and his tiny paws couldn't push the pot away.

"Oh my!" said Rosie. "Don't worry, little friend. I'll help you!"

Rosie tried to lift the flowerpot with her paws, but it was too heavy. She pushed and pushed until her fluffy tail was all dusty. "Hmm," she said, scratching her head. "This is harder than I thought."

The mouse sniffled. "I was looking for seeds to eat. Now I'm stuck!"

Rosie's heart felt warm and fuzzy. She wanted to help her new friend so badly! "Let me think," she said, tapping her foot on the wooden floor.

Then Rosie had an idea! She remembered seeing a long stick in the corner of the shed. "Wait right here!" she told the mouse.

Rosie hopped over and grabbed the stick with her teeth. It tasted like bark and felt smooth. She dragged it back to the flowerpot.

"What are you doing?" asked the mouse.

"I'm going to use this stick to lift the pot!" Rosie said proudly. She slid one end under the flowerpot and pushed down on the other end. The pot moved just a little bit.

"It's working!" cheered the mouse. "But I still can't get out."

Rosie tried again, but her paws slipped. She sat down and thought harder. The shed was quiet except for the gentle buzzing of bees outside.

Suddenly, Rosie heard more voices. "Rosie? Are you in there?" called her friends - Benny Bunny and Carla Cat.

"Yes! Come quick! I need help!" Rosie called back.

Benny and Carla rushed into the shed. Their paws made soft patting sounds on the floor.

"What's wrong?" asked Benny, his nose twitching.

"My new friend is stuck!" Rosie pointed to the mouse.

"Oh no!" said Carla, her whiskers drooping with worry.

Rosie explained her stick idea. "But I need more help to make it work."

"We can do it together!" said Benny.

All three friends grabbed the stick. Rosie counted, "One, two, three, PUSH!"

They pushed down hard, and the flowerpot lifted up high enough for the little mouse to scurry out safely.

"Hooray!" they all cheered. The mouse's eyes sparkled with happiness.

"Thank you, thank you!" squeaked the mouse. "You're the kindest friends ever!"

The mouse's fur was covered in dirt, but he was smiling big. "My name is Max. Can we be friends?"

"Of course!" said Rosie, her heart feeling as warm as sunshine. "Friends always help each other!"

From that day on, Max visited the garden shed every morning. The four friends would share seeds, tell stories, and help each other with problems big and small.

Rosie learned that some problems are too big for one rabbit alone, but with good friends working together, any problem can be solved!

The End.
