# The Little Story Circle

The afternoon light was soft in the guest room. The curtains were green and swishy. A tiny fan hummed like a busy bee. A gentle elephant calf named Eli stood on the fuzzy rug. His feet were big and warm. His ears felt soft like pillows.

“This room smells like fresh soap,” Eli said, wiggling his trunk. He saw a low table, a basket of crayons, and a little stack of paper. There were two small chairs and one big cushion that made a little circle.

Auntie Mira smiled. “Welcome to the Story Circle, Eli,” she said. “Today, we will tell a story together. We will take turns. We will write it down. We will finish it as a team.”

Eli’s eyes grew wide. “I like stories,” he whispered. “But… I am small. My words are small.”

Auntie Mira shook her head kindly. “Small words can make big pictures,” she said. She patted the big cushion. “Sit here. Feel the soft seat. Listen to the fan. Breathe in. Out. Good. Ready?”

Eli nodded. His heart beat thump-thump like a quiet drum. He placed his trunk on the table. The paper felt smooth, like a clean path. The crayon smelled waxy and sweet.

Auntie Mira held a blue crayon. “We will play a game,” she said. “I say one line. Then you say one line. We take turns. I write what we say.”

Eli liked that. Taking turns felt fair, like sharing a cookie. “Okay,” he said, with a tiny smile.

Auntie Mira began. “Once upon a time, in a cozy guest room,” she said, “a brave friend found a secret sound.”

Eli blinked. He heard the fan hum. He heard birds peep. He heard his tummy rumble. He giggled. “The sound went hum-hum!” he said.

Auntie Mira wrote the words slowly. Scratch, scratch went the crayon. Eli watched his words become squiggles on paper. It felt like magic painting.

“My turn,” Auntie Mira said. “The brave friend looked under the cushion.”

“My turn,” said Eli. He lifted the cushion with his trunk. He felt a tickle of dust. “Achoo!” he sneezed. “They found… a tiny bell,” he said, eyes shining. “It went ting-ting!”

Scratch, scratch. The blue crayon wrote ting-ting. Eli smiled bigger. He could hear the bell in his mind.

Auntie Mira passed him a red crayon. “Would you like to write one word?” she asked. “We can try together.”

Eli’s ears warmed. “I can try,” he said. He held the crayon. It felt thick and friendly in his trunk. Auntie Mira guided gently. “Write bell,” she whispered. “B… e… l… l.”

The letters came out wobbly. They looked like tiny dancing legs. Eli giggled. “I wrote!” he said proudly.

“Yes, you did,” said Auntie Mira. “Wobbly letters still tell strong words.”

They kept going. Turn by turn. Line by line.

Auntie Mira: “The tiny bell wanted a friend.”

Eli: “The bell wanted a song.”

Auntie Mira: “They listened and listened.”

Eli: “They heard the fan’s hum-hum and the birds’ peep-peep.”

Auntie Mira: “The brave friend shook the bell.”

Eli: “Ting-ting! The bell sang hello!”

Scratch, scratch, scratch. The paper filled with their story. The guest room felt warm and happy. Eli’s shoulders relaxed. He liked the rhythm of turns. It was like a dance: you, me, you, me.

Soon, Eli’s tummy rumbled again. “I am hungry,” he said. “Stories make me hungry!”

Auntie Mira laughed softly. “We will finish the story first,” she said. “That is our task. We can do it.”

Eli nodded. Completing tasks felt good. It felt like putting all toy blocks back in their box, tidy and calm. “Let’s finish,” he said bravely.

Auntie Mira pointed to the page. “We need an ending,” she said. “How can our friends feel safe and happy?”

Eli closed his eyes. He listened to the guest room. The fan hummed. The curtains swished. The floor was warm. He felt calm. “They make a Story Circle,” he said. “They take turns singing.”

“Beautiful,” Auntie Mira said, writing quickly, the crayon soft and whispery. “And then?”

Eli thought. “They share the bell,” he said. “First me, then you. We both play the song.”

Auntie Mira nodded. “And they write the song down,” she said. “So they can remember.”

Eli picked up the red crayon again. He made a big smiling circle for the bell. He wrote two more wobbly letters with help: H I. “Hi,” he read. “The bell says hi.”

Auntie Mira clapped. The sound was gentle, like pat-pat on a drum. “We did it,” she said. “We told a story. We wrote words. We took turns. We finished our task.”

Eli’s chest felt warm and proud. “I am a storyteller,” he whispered.

“Yes,” said Auntie Mira. “Your small words made big pictures. Your turns made the story fair. Your writing made it real.”

She taped the paper on the wall. The story looked bright and brave. The guest room smelled like crayons and clean laundry. Eli reached out and rang a little craft bell from the basket. Ting-ting! The sound sparkled in the air.

“What did we learn today?” Auntie Mira asked.

Eli thought carefully. “Tell with your heart,” he said. “Take turns with your friends. Write it down so you remember. And finish what you start.”

Auntie Mira smiled. “That is a good story lesson,” she said.

Eli smiled back. He felt calm, clever, and kind. He snuggled into the big cushion. The fan hummed a sleepy song. The story on the wall shone like a friendly moon.

The End.
