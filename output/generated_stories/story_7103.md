# Spark the Robot Dog’s Artful Morning

Sunshine peeked through the curtains in a cozy bedroom. A soft rug tickled little toes. Crayons, paper, and stickers sat in a colorful bin. On the bed, Mia stretched and yawned. On the floor, a helpful robot dog named Spark blinked his bright blue eyes and wagged his soft, whirring tail.

“Good morning, Spark,” Mia said, rubbing her eyes.

“Good morning, Mia,” beeped Spark gently. “Today is a good day to try a big-kid thing.”

Mia sat up. “A big-kid thing? Like what?”

Spark’s ears clicked. “Like making your own morning art, and cleaning up all by yourself.”

Mia looked at the art bin. Her tummy fluttered with a tiny worry. “By myself?”

Spark’s tail whirred again, quiet and kind. “I will cheer. You can do it.”

Mia took a deep breath. She slid off the bed, and the rug felt warm and squishy under her toes. She picked up the art bin. It felt a little heavy. “I can carry it,” Mia whispered. “I am strong.”

“Strong and smart,” Spark agreed. “What will you make?”

Mia smiled. “A sunshine picture for Mama.”

She set the bin on the low table by the window. Light spilled over the paper like yellow lemonade. Birds sang softly outside. Spark sat beside her, his metal paws making tiny taps on the wood.

Mia opened the crayon box. “So many colors!” she said. She chose sunny yellow. Scritch, scritch. The crayon felt smooth and warm in her hand. A big round sun grew on the page. She added orange rays—zip, zip, zip—and little hearts—bloop, bloop, bloop.

“Beautiful,” Spark said. “What is next?”

Mia bit her lip. “Maybe clouds. Maybe a tree. I don’t know.”

Spark blinked. “When you are not sure, try small steps.”

Mia nodded. “Small steps.” She drew a tiny cloud—puff. Then another—puff, puff. Soon, fluffy white clouds bumped along the blue sky.

She looked at the green crayon. “Trees are hard,” she said.

“You can do hard things,” Spark said softly. “Break them into little parts.”

Mia drew a brown line for a trunk—straight and steady. Then she made little green circles—pop, pop, pop. The tree grew leafy and full. She stuck a round sticker bird on a branch. “Chirp!” she giggled.

Spark wagged. “You were resourceful. You used stickers to help.”

Mia grinned. “I am a helper to myself!”

When the picture was done, Mia held it up. The paper smelled like wax and fresh paper. “I did it,” she said, her voice glowing.

Spark nodded. “Yes. Now, an artist also knows how to tidy up.”

Mia looked around. Crayons were open. Stickers were scattered like confetti. Paper bits sprinkled the rug. The room felt a little messy. Mia’s shoulders drooped. “It’s a lot.”

Spark’s soft voice hummed. “We can use an organization game. Ready?”

Mia’s eyes lit up. “A game!”

“Game rules,” Spark said, tapping his tail. “Rule one: Sort by color. Rule two: Count to five. Rule three: Put things where they belong.”

Mia clapped. “Okay!”

She picked up five yellow crayons and lined them up like sunbeams. “One, two, three, four, five!” The numbers felt bouncy in her mouth. She placed them in the yellow slot. Click.

Next, she gathered blue crayons. “One, two, three.” They rolled like tiny rivers into the blue slot. Shhh, plink.

Spark cheered. “You are independent. You are doing this by yourself.”

Mia smiled big. “I am a big kid.”

They moved to stickers. Mia sorted star stickers in one cup and heart stickers in another. “Stars here, hearts here,” she chanted. Tiny paper scraps went into the trash bin with soft rustles. The table felt smooth again when she slid her hand across it.

“What about the paper?” Spark asked.

Mia held her picture gently. “This goes on the wall.” She found a bit of tape and pressed it above her bed. The sunshine picture glowed in the morning light. “I love it,” she whispered.

Spark’s eyes twinkled. “How do you feel?”

Mia put her hands on her hips. “I feel proud. I made art all by myself. I cleaned up all by myself.”

Spark tilted his head. “What helped you?”

Mia thought. “Small steps helped. Sorting helped. Counting helped. And you cheered.”

Spark wagged. “You used independence and resourcefulness. You planned, you tried, and you finished.”

Just then, Mama peeked into the cozy bedroom. She smelled like pancakes. “Wow,” she said, eyes wide. “What a beautiful picture! And your room is so tidy!”

Mia ran to Mama and hugged her. “I did it myself!”

Mama kissed her hair. “You did, and you worked so hard. That is wonderful.”

Mia looked at Spark. “Tomorrow I can make a rainbow. I can sort colors again. I can tidy again.”

Spark gave a happy beep. “You can. And you will.”

Mia picked up her favorite crayon and placed it carefully in the box. Click. The cozy bedroom felt calm and bright. The sun warmed the rug. The art on the wall smiled down.

Mia took a deep breath. “Small steps, sort and count, put things away. I can do it,” she said.

“Yes,” Spark whispered. “You can.”

The End.
