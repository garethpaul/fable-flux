# Panda Poppy’s Rooftop Picnic

Panda Poppy lived in a tall, bright house with a flat rooftop. The rooftop was Poppy’s favorite place. It was sunny and breezy. She could hear birds chirp-chirp and feel the warm sun on her fuzzy ears. One morning, Poppy’s tummy made a little rumble. “Grumble-growl,” it said.

Poppy patted her belly. “I am hungry,” she said softly. “I want a yummy snack.” She climbed up the little ladder to the rooftop and took a deep breath. The air smelled like flowers and fresh leaves. Poppy sat on a cozy mat and opened a basket.

Inside the basket were choices! There were bright red apple slices, crunchy carrot sticks, creamy yogurt in a cup, a tiny bowl of nuts, and a little plate with sweet cookies. Poppy’s eyes got wide. So many choices! Her tummy rumbled again. “Grumble-growl,” it said, a little louder.

Poppy felt a flutter in her chest. “Which one should I eat?” she asked. She remembered what Mama Panda had told her. “When you feel fluttery, stop and breathe.” Poppy put her paws on her belly. She breathed in slowly. She breathed out slowly. “Breathe in… breathe out,” she whispered. The flutter slowed down.

Just then, Windy the breeze danced by and tickled Poppy’s whiskers. “Whoosh,” went Windy. “Hello, Poppy! What are you doing?”

“I am choosing a snack,” said Poppy. “I want to make a good choice for my body.”

Windy swirled around the basket. “Snacks can help you grow strong, think clearly, and play happily,” Windy whispered. “Listen to your tummy and your heart.”

Poppy nodded. “I will be patient,” she said. “I will decide with care.”

She looked at the apples. They were shiny and smelled sweet. She looked at the carrots. They were bright and crunchy. She looked at the yogurt. It was cool and creamy. She looked at the nuts. They were small and nutty. Then she looked at the cookies. They smelled like sugar and cinnamon.

Poppy’s tummy said, “Grumble-growl,” but not too loudly. Poppy tapped her chin. “Hmm,” she said. “I can make a plan.”

She used her paws to line up the foods. “Fruits help me feel bright,” she said, touching the apples. “Veggies help me stay strong,” she said, touching the carrots. “Yogurt helps my bones,” she said, tapping the cup. “Nuts help my brain,” she said, touching the bowl. “Cookies are a sometimes treat,” she said, smiling at the plate.

Poppy closed her eyes and listened. She heard birds. She heard Windy say, “Whoosh.” She felt the sun on her fur. She felt her tummy, calm now. “I choose a rainbow plate,” she said. “I can have a little of many good foods.”

She made a snack plan:
- First, apple slices for fruity energy.
- Next, carrot sticks for crunch.
- Then, a few nuts for thinking power.
- A spoon of yogurt to make bones happy.
- Last, one small cookie, as a treat.

Poppy took a bite of apple. “Crunch!” It tasted juicy and sweet. “Mmm,” she said. She munched a carrot. “Crunch-crunch!” It tasted fresh, like the garden. She ate a few nuts. “Nibble-nibble.” They tasted warm and toasty. She scooped some yogurt. “Spoon, swirl, slurp.” It was cool and smooth. She smiled and licked her lips.

Windy danced around. “You made a kind choice for your body,” Windy sang. “You were patient. You made a plan. You listened.”

Poppy nodded. “I feel calm. I feel strong,” she said. “My tummy is happy.” She looked at the little cookie. It waited on the plate. Poppy took one slow breath. “In… and out.” She took a small bite. “Nibble.” The cookie was sweet and cinnamon-y. “This is my treat,” she said, “and that is okay.”

Just then, Neighbor Bird landed on the rooftop rail. “Tweet! What are you eating?” Bird asked.

“My rainbow plate,” said Poppy. “Fruits, veggies, nuts, and yogurt. And a tiny treat.”

Bird tilted her head. “How did you choose?”

Poppy smiled. “I was patient. I breathed. I listened to my tummy. I made a plan. I picked many good foods first. I saved the treat for last.”

Bird chirped happily. “Tweet! That is smart. You are learning a snack skill.”

Poppy giggled. “Yes! Snack skill-building,” she said, clapping her paws. “I can do it again next time.”

The sun made the rooftop warm and golden. Poppy wiped her mouth and put the basket away. Her belly felt cozy. Her heart felt proud. Windy gave her one last “whoosh,” like a hug.

Poppy stood up and looked at the bright sky. “Today I learned to be patient,” she said. “I learned to decide with care. I learned that good foods help my body. I learned that treats can wait, and that is okay.”

She smiled at Bird. “Next time, we can share a rainbow plate,” she said.

“Tweet-tweet!” said Bird. “I accept your plan!”

Poppy laughed. “Thank you, Windy. Thank you, Bird. Thank you, rooftop,” she said. “I feel ready to play.”

And off she went, strong and happy, with a calm belly and a calm mind.

The End.
