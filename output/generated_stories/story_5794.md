# Tilly the Thoughtful Giraffe Sorts It Out

Tilly the thoughtful giraffe had a very long neck and a very gentle heart. One bright morning, she tiptoed into the laundry room. It smelled like warm soap bubbles and clean rain. The washer hummed, “Whirr, whirr,” and the dryer sang, “Thump, thump, thump.”

“Today is chore day,” Tilly said, smiling. “I will help.”

On the floor were three baskets. One basket had tiny socks from baby pandas. One had shirts from children all around the world—bright red, sunny yellow, deep green, and ocean blue. The last basket held big towels that felt soft and fluffy.

Tilly bent her long neck down. “I can sort and fold. That is fair,” she said.

Just then, her friend Poko the puppy skidded in. “I want to push the buttons!” Poko barked, wagging fast.

And Mimi the mouse peeked from behind the detergent box. “I want to fold the little socks,” squeaked Mimi. “They are my size!”

Tilly felt a tight pinch in her belly. She wanted to push the buttons too. She wanted to fold the tiny socks. Her cheeks felt warm. “Hmm,” she breathed in slow. She smelled soapy bubbles and warm air. She breathed out slow. “Pooooof.”

Tilly remembered: When we feel mad, we can pause. We can use gentle words.

“It is fair to share,” Tilly said softly. “We can make a plan.”

Poko tilted his head. “A plan?”

“Yes,” said Tilly. “We can take turns. We can help in ways that fit us.”

Mimi clapped her tiny paws. “How?”

Tilly looked at the baskets. “We can sort by color. We can match socks. We can fold towels. And we can push buttons, one at a time.”

Poko wagged. “I like turns!”

Mimi nodded. “I like matching!”

Tilly smiled. “First, let’s sort the shirts. Look at all the colors from many places.” She held up a red shirt with a little maple leaf. “This one might be from a cold place.” She held up a green shirt with a tiny frog. “This one might be from a warm, rainy place.” She held up a blue shirt with stars. “Clothes travel far. People everywhere do laundry too.”

Poko sniffed the shirts. “They smell like sunshine!”

Mimi touched the buttons. “So smooth.”

Tilly said, “We will be fair. Poko, you can push the washer button first. I will count to three. One, two, three!”

Poko pressed the button. “Beep!” The washer started. “Whirr, whirr.”

Poko’s tail wagged in circles. “My turn is done. Who is next?”

Mimi squeaked, “Me next for folding!” She picked up a tiny panda sock, black and white. She picked up another sock, white and black. “Match, match!” she sang. “They are a pair!”

Tilly used her long neck to reach the top of the dryer. She took out warm towels. Steam puffed. The towels were cozy like clouds. “Ahh,” said Tilly. “Warm.” She folded one, two, three big towels. “I can do the big ones. That is fair, because I am big.”

Poko sat to cool down. “I can help by bringing baskets,” he said. He pushed a basket with his nose. “Scoot, scoot.”

Tilly’s belly felt calm now. She saw Mimi’s pile of socks. “You are a great matcher,” Tilly said.

Mimi blushed. “Thank you.”

Tilly saw Poko waiting nicely. “You are a great turn taker,” Tilly said.

Poko barked, “Thank you!”

Soon the washer beeped, “Beep-beep.” It was time to move clothes. Poko looked at Tilly. “Is it fair if I push the button again?”

Tilly shook her head gently. “It is fair to take turns. Mimi can push this time.”

Mimi’s eyes grew wide. “Me? I am small.”

Tilly nodded. “We can help. I can lift you up.”

Tilly bent down. Mimi climbed onto Tilly’s soft mane. Tilly lifted her up, up, up. Mimi reached. “Beep!” The dryer started. “Thump, thump, thump.”

Mimi giggled. “I did it!”

They listened. The dryer was like a drum. Thump, thump. The room smelled warm and sweet, like fresh cotton.

While they waited, Tilly showed a map on a laundry bag. “Look,” she said. “People in many places wash clothes. Some use rivers. Some use big machines. Some hang clothes in the sun. No matter where we are, we share chores. We help our families. We take turns. That is fair.”

Poko looked at the map. “Wow,” he said.

Mimi traced the lines. “The world is big,” she squeaked.

When the dryer stopped, they folded together. Tilly folded towels. Poko folded shirts—well, he tried. His paws slipped. The shirt flopped.

“That’s okay,” said Tilly, smiling. “We can learn. Fold in, fold up. Press, press.”

Poko tried again. “Fold in, fold up. Press, press.” He did it! “I learned!”

Mimi matched the last socks. “Match!”

They placed everything in the right baskets. Small things on top. Big towels on the bottom. The room was neat and tidy.

Tilly took a deep breath. “I feel proud,” she said. “We were fair. We shared. We calmed our mad feelings. We did our chores.”

Poko wagged slowly, happy and tired. “We helped.”

Mimi squeaked softly, “We cared.”

Tilly nuzzled her friends. “Fairness means everyone gets a turn. Fairness means we help in ways that fit. Fairness feels good for all.”

They smiled together in the warm, clean room. The day felt bright and kind.

The End.
