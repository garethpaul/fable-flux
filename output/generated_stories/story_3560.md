# Squeak’s Treehouse Team Plan

Squeak the resourceful squirrel lived in a cozy treehouse high in a leafy oak. The treehouse smelled like pine cones and warm acorns. Sunlight made dancy dots on the floor. Squeak’s fluffy tail twitched with excitement. Today was Treehouse Sports Day!

Squeak put on a tiny green headband. “I want to play acorn ball,” Squeak said. “I am small, but I am fast. I can help!”

Down on the grass, the friends gathered. There was Bella Bluebird, Max Mouse, and Tippy Tortoise. Coach Owl blinked his big golden eyes. “Teams are ready,” he hooted. “We will toss the acorn, run, and score.”

Squeak’s heart pitter-pattered. The acorn looked big and bumpy. It smelled nutty. Squeak rubbed tiny paws together. “I can do it,” Squeak whispered.

But when the game began, Squeak did not get the acorn. Bigger friends passed to each other. The acorn zoomed by. Whoosh! Thump! Squeak hopped from paw to paw. “I’m open!” Squeak squeaked. No one looked.

Squeak felt a little squish in the tummy, like a squeezed berry. “Maybe I am too small,” Squeak thought. A wind ruffled Squeak’s ears. The tree leaves whispered. Squeak took a deep, slow breath. In…and out. The air tasted cool.

“I need to speak up for me,” Squeak said to self. “That is self-advocacy. I can say what I need.” Squeak made a plan, because Squeak was resourceful.

Squeak’s plan:
- Step 1: Use a big, brave voice.
- Step 2: Tell the team what I can do.
- Step 3: Ask for a fair turn.
- Step 4: Keep trying, even if it is hard.

When the whistle stopped the game for a water break, Squeak padded over to Coach Owl and the team. Squeak stood tall, tail high.

“Excuse me, Coach,” Squeak said. “Friends, can I share?” The voice was small but steady.

Coach Owl nodded. “Yes, Squeak. Tell us.”

Squeak looked at each friend. “I am fast and I can zip under low branches. I can catch the acorn with sticky paws. I want a turn. Please pass to me next time.”

Bella Bluebird fluffed her wings. “I didn’t know you could zip so fast, Squeak!”

Max Mouse wiggled whiskers. “Thanks for telling us. That helps.”

Tippy Tortoise smiled slow. “Good asking, Squeak.”

Coach Owl hooted softly. “That was brave and clear. You said what you need. That is self-advocacy. Team, let’s make a plan.”

They made a strategy. Squeak drew a little map in the dirt with a twig. “Bella flies high. Max runs left. Tippy blocks with a steady shell. I run under the low branch and wait here.” Squeak pointed to a shady spot near a root.

Coach Owl tapped the map with a feather. “Wise plan.”

Back on the field, the grass felt tickly on Squeak’s toes. The sun felt warm. The acorn came to Bella. “Squeak, ready?” Bella called.

“I’m ready!” Squeak called back, voice bright.

Bella tossed. The acorn spun. Squeak zipped—whoosh! The low branch brushed Squeak’s ears. Squeak reached out. Plop! The acorn landed safe in Squeak’s paws. The crowd of forest friends went “Ooooh!”

Max Mouse cheered, “Go, Squeak!”

Squeak scampered to the goal stump, heart humming. Thump! Squeak tapped the acorn to score. The team clapped and chirped and stomped their tiny feet. Squeak’s cheeks felt warm and happy.

But then, the other team got the acorn and scored too. The game was close. Squeak’s team huddled. “I feel wiggly-nervous,” Squeak said. “But I can try again.”

Coach Owl said, “Use your strategy, and remember to speak up.”

On the next play, the other team blocked the path. The low branch was crowded. Squeak paused. “I need to change the plan,” Squeak said. “Friends, new idea! Tippy, can you make a slow, wide turn? Max, sneak behind me.”

“Got it,” said Tippy.

“On it,” squeaked Max.

Squeak clapped twice. “Bella, high pass!”

Bella sent the acorn up, up, up. Squeak waited, paws ready. The acorn fell like a tiny moon. Squeak caught it—plop!—and then gently rolled it to Max. Max scurried and tapped the goal stump. Score!

The forest cheered. The game ended with happy squeaks and hoots. Everyone lined up for high-fives and shell taps.

Squeak’s chest felt big with proud. “Thank you for passing to me,” Squeak said to Bella.

“Thank you for telling us what you needed,” Bella said.

“Thank you for the plan,” Max added.

“Thank you for trying again,” said Tippy.

Coach Owl ruffled Squeak’s headband. “You used your voice. You made a strategy. You kept going. That is resilience.”

Back in the treehouse, the friends shared crunchy apple slices and warm acorn cookies. The air smelled sweet and toasty. Squeak wrapped the tail like a soft blanket and smiled.

Squeak said, “Today I learned: I can speak up with kind words. I can make a plan. I can be thankful for my team. When I ask for a fair turn, I can help.”

The friends nodded. “We learned that too!”

Squeak looked out at the shining leaves. “I feel brave and grateful,” Squeak said. “I can use my voice tomorrow, too.”

The End.
