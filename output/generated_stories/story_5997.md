# Pippa Parrot’s Brave Table Dance

Pippa was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a splash of blue on her wings. She loved to paint with her beak and tap rhythms with her claws. One sunny morning, Pippa sat on the back of a chair in the dining room. The room smelled like warm toast and sweet oranges. Sunlight made little squares of light on the floor.

On the big wooden table stood a music box shaped like a tiny house. When Mama turned the silver key, the music box played a soft, tinkly tune. Pippa shivered with joy. “Cheep! I want to dance!” Pippa chirped. She fluffed her feathers. “I want to dance on the table!”

Mama smiled and said, “You can dance, Pippa. Be brave, be calm, and move slowly. Courage helps us try new things.”

Pippa looked at the table. It seemed very big and very shiny. “But what if I slip?” she whispered.

Papa said, “Take a deep breath. Courage means we try, even when we feel a little scared. We can be calm and careful.”

Pippa put one claw on the table. The wood felt smooth and cool. She took a tiny step. Tap. Tap. Her claws made a gentle sound. “I can do it,” she said softly. “I can be brave.”

The music box began to play. Ting-ting-ting. The soft notes filled the dining room. The air smelled like cinnamon toast, and the sunshine felt warm on Pippa’s wings.

Pippa started to dance. She lifted one foot and then the other. Tap-tap, tip-tip. Her wings made a quiet whoosh. “Cheep-cheep!” she sang. She turned in a little circle.

Suddenly, her claw slid on a shiny spot. “Eep!” Pippa wobbled. Her heart went thump-thump. She froze. “I’m scared,” she whispered.

Mama said gently, “Pause. Breathe in… and out. Calm helps courage.”

Pippa closed her eyes and took a deep breath. In… and out. She felt her heart slow down. She opened her eyes. The table was still there. The music was still sweet. “I can try again,” she said.

She moved one tiny step. Tap. She spread her wings for balance. Whoosh. “I will be calm. I will be brave,” Pippa told herself.

She tried a new move she had imagined while painting. “The Leaf Step,” she said. She slid one claw like a leaf drifting down, then tapped the other foot, tap-tap, like rain on the window. She felt steady. “I did it!” she chirped, smiling.

Her little brother, Pip, clapped his hands. “Go, Pippa! Dance, dance!”

Pippa tried a twirl. But her tail brushed a spoon. Clink! The spoon rattled and rolled. It made a loud sound on the table. Pippa jumped. “Oh no!” she cried.

Papa picked up the spoon and set it by the plate. “It’s okay,” he said. “Sometimes loud sounds surprise us. Take another calm breath.”

Pippa breathed in the sweet orange smell. She listened to the soft ting-ting of the music box. She felt the smooth wood under her claws. “I am safe. I am brave,” she said. She tried the twirl again, a little slower. Turn… and stop. Steady.

“Yay!” laughed Pip.

Pippa’s body felt warm and light. She made a new dance, the “Sunshine Shuffle.” Tap-tap, slide, tap! She swished her wings like a gentle fan. Whoosh-whoosh. She nodded her head with the beat. Her beads on her leg band clicked softly, click-click, like tiny crickets.

Then she tried the “Courage Hop.” Hop—land—pause. Hop—land—pause. Each time she paused, she took a small calm breath. In… out. “Pause helps me,” she said. “Calm and courage are good friends.”

The music got quieter as the little key slowed down. Pippa wanted to finish her dance. She took one last big hop and bowed. As she bowed, she slipped again—just a little. But this time she bent her knees, spread her wings, and steadied herself. “I did not give up,” she said proudly.

Mama and Papa clapped. Pip clapped and giggled. “Brave Pippa!” he cheered.

Pippa puffed her feathers with happy pride. Her cheeks (right under her beak!) felt warm. “I was scared,” she told Pip. “But I stayed calm. I tried again and again. That is courage.”

Mama hugged Pippa. “You showed persistence,” Mama said. “You kept going. You were careful. You were calm. You were brave.”

Pippa hopped down from the table to the chair. She could still feel the smooth wood and hear the soft echo of the music in her ears. She took one more deep breath and smiled. “Tomorrow,” she said, “I will dance again. Maybe I will make a new step called the Orange Peel Wiggle!”

Pip laughed. “I will clap,” he said.

Pippa nodded. “We can all be brave,” she said. “When we feel scared, we can breathe, move slowly, and try again.”

The End.
