# Pippa the Painting Parrot

Every morning, when the sun made the windows glow, Pippa the parrot fluttered into the creative art room. The room smelled like fresh paper and lemony clean tables. Paints sat in little cups like rainbows. Soft brushes waited in a jar. Pippa loved this room.

“Good morning, colors!” Pippa chirped. Her feathers were green and blue with tiny gold speckles. She had a gentle voice and bright, curious eyes. Pippa had a happy routine.

“Step one: apron on!” she sang, tying a tiny red apron around her belly.

“Step two: wash, wash!” She flapped to the sink, and the warm water felt tickly on her claws.

“Step three: plan and play!” Pippa tapped her beak and looked at the blank paper. It felt smooth and cool.

Every day, Pippa made art. Some days she painted dots like raindrops. Some days she made big swirls like windy clouds. Today, Pippa wanted to paint a song. She could feel the music in her wings.

She dipped her brush into sunny yellow. “Swoosh!” The brush slid across the paper. She added sky blue. “Swish!” The colors danced together. Pippa hummed a little tune. “La-la-la!” Her feathers rustled happily.

“Pippa, what are you painting?” asked Benny the turtle, who liked to visit the art room. He wore a striped scarf and moved slowly, but he always smiled.

“I’m painting a song!” Pippa said. “It sounds like sunshine.”

Benny blinked. “I don’t see a song,” he said softly. “I see lines.”

Pippa paused. Her heart felt a tiny pinch. “Oh,” she whispered. “Maybe it doesn’t look like a song to you.”

Just then, Ms. Dot, the art room helper, rolled in a cart of clean cups. “Good morning, artists!” she said. “Remember our routine: apron, wash, plan, and play.”

Pippa nodded, but she didn’t feel as bright as before. She looked at her painting. Yellow and blue swirls smiled up at her. They still felt like a song to her.

Benny stretched his neck. “I like your colors,” he said. “But I don’t understand the song part.”

Pippa took a deep breath. She remembered step three: plan and play. Play means try. Play means share. “Would you like to hear the song in my head?” she asked.

“Yes, please,” said Benny.

Pippa tapped the paper gently with her brush. “This swoosh is the long note. It goes ‘ooooo,’ like the sun coming up. And this swish is the short note. It goes ‘ta-ta,’ like happy feet.” She hummed, and her humming matched the brush marks.

Benny’s eyes widened. “Oh! I hear it now,” he said. “The long note looks big and the short note looks little. That helps me understand.”

Pippa smiled. Warmth fluttered in her chest. She added tiny gold dots. “These are twinkles,” she said. “They go ‘ting, ting!’”

Benny tapped his shell, making a soft “tok.” “Can I add a ‘tok’ spot?” he asked. “I want to help the song.”

Pippa nodded. “Yes! Let’s make music art together.” She put a brown dot where Benny pointed. “Tok!”

They laughed. The art room smelled like oranges when Ms. Dot opened a new pack of clay. Sunlight tickled the glitter. Brushes whispered on paper all around.

Soon, other friends peeked in. Mia the mouse sniffed. “What’s that pretty smell?” she asked.

“Orange clay,” Ms. Dot said. “Would you like to shape something after you paint?”

“Yes!” Mia squeaked. She watched Pippa’s picture. “What is it?”

Pippa stood tall on the table, her claws clicking softly. “It’s our song painting,” she said. “You can see long notes, short notes, and twinkles. Listen.” She hummed the tune and pointed to each mark. Benny tapped his shell right on the “tok” dot.

Mia’s whiskers wiggled. “I understand now,” she said. “I can hear it in my head. Can I add a tiny ‘squeak’ mark?”

Pippa clapped her wings. “Please do!”

They made a small silver line. “Squeak!”

When the painting felt full, Pippa looked at it with happy eyes. She remembered her routine. “Step four: clean and cheer.” She washed her brush, wiped the table, and patted the paper dry. The room sounded like gentle clinks and splashes.

Ms. Dot came over. “Tell me about your art, Pippa,” she said.

Pippa puffed her chest. “Today I learned that my art can feel like a song to me and still look like lines to someone else. When I share my idea, other friends can understand. We can make it together.”

Ms. Dot nodded. “That’s creative expression,” she said. “You showed your feelings with colors and sounds. You followed your routine, and you helped your friends understand.”

Benny smiled. “Your art helped me hear with my eyes.”

Mia giggled. “And sing with my whiskers!”

Pippa’s heart felt bright again. She stuck a tiny sticker of a star in the corner. “Our art is a song we can see,” she said. “We made it by planning, playing, and understanding each other.”

The bell chimed softly. Clean brushes, tidy tables, happy friends. Pippa flapped to the door and turned back. “See you tomorrow, colors,” she whispered.

And the colors seemed to whisper back, “See you tomorrow, Pippa.”

The End.
