# Koko's Thank You Corner

In a cozy study corner filled with soft cushions and colorful books, lived a gentle koala named Koko. The corner smelled like lavender and had warm sunlight streaming through the window. Koko loved this special place where she could read, draw, and think quietly.

One morning, Koko woke up feeling grumpy. Her favorite eucalyptus leaves tasted bitter, and she couldn't find her special purple crayon anywhere.

"This is a terrible day!" Koko huffed, her fluffy ears drooping. She stomped into her study corner and plopped down on her favorite blue cushion with a big *thump*.

Just then, her friend Benny the bunny hopped by the window. "Good morning, Koko! Want to play hopscotch?"

"No!" Koko grumbled. "Everything is going wrong today. I can't find my crayon, my breakfast was yucky, and I feel all mixed up inside."

Benny's ears perked up with concern. "That sounds frustrating. Can I help?"

Koko was about to say no again when she noticed something. Benny's soft brown eyes looked kind and worried. He really wanted to help her feel better.

"Maybe," Koko said quietly. "But I don't know what to do when I feel this angry and sad."

Benny hopped closer to the window. "My mama taught me something special. When I feel upset, I think of three things that make me happy. Want to try?"

Koko's ears twitched with curiosity. "How does that work?"

"Close your eyes and take a deep breath," Benny said gently. "Now, what's one thing that makes you smile?"

Koko closed her eyes and breathed in slowly. The lavender smell in her corner was so peaceful. "I'm thankful for my cozy study corner," she said, her voice getting softer. "It always makes me feel safe."

"That's wonderful!" Benny cheered. "What's another thing?"

Koko thought harder, her breathing becoming calmer. "I'm thankful for you, Benny. You came to check on me when I was grumpy. That was really kind."

Benny's cotton tail wiggled with happiness. "And one more?"

Koko opened her eyes and looked around her study corner. She saw her collection of smooth stones, her soft blanket, and her stack of picture books. "I'm thankful for all my special things that help me learn and feel better."

As Koko spoke, something magical happened. Her angry feelings started melting away like ice cream in the sun. Her shoulders relaxed, and a small smile appeared on her fuzzy face.

"Wow," Koko said, stretching her arms. "I feel so much better! My heart feels warm and happy now."

"That's the magic of gratitude," Benny explained. "When we remember good things, it helps the frustrated feelings go away."

Just then, Koko spotted something purple peeking out from under her blanket. "My crayon!" she gasped with joy.

"Would you like to draw together?" Benny asked. "I brought my colored pencils."

"Yes, please!" Koko said, patting the cushion next to her. "Thank you for teaching me about gratitude, Benny. And thank you for being such a good friend."

As they drew pictures of rainbows and flowers together, Koko felt grateful for her friend, her cozy corner, and the new way to handle big feelings. She learned that even on difficult days, there's always something to be thankful for.

From that day on, whenever Koko felt frustrated or upset, she would sit in her study corner, take deep breaths, and think of three things that made her grateful. It always helped her feel better.

The End.
