# Milo Mouse and the Cozy Corner

Milo was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived in a snug treehouse high in a friendly oak. The treehouse smelled like warm wood and cinnamon tea. Sunlight made little golden dots on the floor.

Milo loved visitors. He set tiny cups on a tiny table. He laid soft leaf-mats on the floor. He whispered to himself, “I like sharing. I like quiet, too.”

One breezy morning, Milo hummed a happy tune. Tap, tap! He painted a blue rainbow on a smooth acorn shell. He felt calm and cozy in his corner by the round window.

Knock, knock!

“Come in!” Milo called.

In came Bella Bunny, bouncing-bounce, and Toby Tortoise, step-step. Bella’s ears were floppy and pink. Toby’s shell was shiny and green.

“Hi, Milo!” said Bella. “Can we play in your treehouse?”

“Yes,” said Milo, smiling. “Let’s have fun.”

Bella giggled and hopped. “Let’s drum!” She patted a tin can. Boom, boom, boom!

Toby wiggled his toes. “Let’s build a tower.” He stacked smooth stones. Clack, clack.

Milo’s whiskers twitched. The boom was loud. The clack was sharp. His cozy corner felt crowded. The blue rainbow wobbled in his paw. Milo’s tummy felt tight, like a knot.

He took a slow breath in. He let a slow breath out. He remembered his words: “I like sharing. I like quiet, too.”

Milo looked at Bella and Toby. He placed his tiny brush down. He said in a calm, kind voice, “I need a quiet space. This is my cozy corner. Please do not play loud here.”

Bella’s ears perked. “Oh!” she said. “We did not know.”

Toby blinked. “Thank you for telling us,” he said.

Milo pointed to a little sign. It had a blue star and soft clouds. “Blue star means quiet,” he explained. “This corner is for painting and reading.”

Bella nodded. “I can drum by the door,” she said. “Booms stay there.”

Toby smiled. “I can build by the sunny spot,” he said. “Clacks stay there.”

Milo felt the knot in his tummy loosen. He smiled. “Thank you,” he said. “That helps me feel safe.”

They moved together. Boom, boom by the door. Clack, clack by the sun. The cozy corner stayed hush-hush. Milo dipped his brush. Swish, swish. He painted a tiny blue bird with silver wings. The paint smelled like berries. The leaves outside went shhhhh in the wind.

After a while, Bella peeked over. “Milo, your bird is pretty,” she whispered.

“Thanks,” said Milo. “Would you like to try painting, too? We can make a quiet art time.”

Bella hugged her drum. “Yes, but where can I put my drum so it does not bother you?”

Milo pointed to a little basket. “Here is a drum basket,” he said. “When the drum is in the basket, it is rest time for the drum.”

Bella smiled. “Okay. Drum, take a nap.” She tucked it in.

Toby tapped his chin. “My tower wants to be taller,” he said. “But I do not want my clacks to bother you.”

Milo showed a soft cloth. “Cloth under stones,” he said. “Soft clacks.”

Toby laid the cloth down. Clack became click, like a tiny clock.

Now the treehouse sounded gentle. Whisper, swish, click. The air felt warm and kind. Milo’s heart felt light.

“Friends,” said Milo, “thank you for listening to my words. I used my boundary words.”

Bella asked, “What are boundary words?”

Milo said, “Boundary words are kind words that say what I need. They help me feel safe and happy. They tell others where to play and how to touch my things.”

Bella nodded slowly. “Like, ‘Please do not grab my drum. Ask first.’”

“Yes,” said Milo. “And like, ‘Please keep loud sounds by the door.’”

Toby raised his hand. “I have boundary words, too,” he said. “Please do not move my stones without asking.”

Milo and Bella answered together, “We will ask.”

They all smiled. It felt good to share and also to have space.

Then Milo had an idea. “Let’s make signs for the treehouse,” he said. “We can use colors!”

He brought out paper leaves, soft string, and sticky berries for glue. They worked as a team.

- Blue star sign: Quiet corner.
- Yellow sun sign: Building spot.
- Red heart sign: Ask before touching.
- Green leaf sign: Music place.

Bella drew a heart. “Ask before touching,” she read. “I like this.”

Toby stamped a leaf print. “Music goes here,” he said.

Milo tied the signs up high. The signs swayed gently. The treehouse looked bright and friendly.

At the end of the day, they sipped warm cinnamon tea. The cups clicked softly. The sky turned pink. Birds chirped sweetly outside.

Bella said, “Today I learned to listen when my friend says, ‘Please.’”

Toby said, “I learned to say what I need.”

Milo said, “I learned that I can be kind and still have my own space.”

They all said, “We can use boundary words. We can listen. We can be friends.”

Milo felt cozy and proud. His treehouse felt safe and happy for everyone.

The End.
