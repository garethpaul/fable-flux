# Melody's Messy Day

In a bright and colorful creative art room, there lived a little yellow bird named Melody. She had the most beautiful voice in the whole school, and she loved to sing while the children painted and drew pictures.

Every morning, Melody would perch on her special branch near the window and sing happy songs. "Tweet-tweet-trill! Good morning, everyone!" she would chirp. The children loved hearing her sweet melodies as they created their artwork.

But one Tuesday morning, something was very wrong. The art room was a terrible mess! Paint tubes were squeezed all over the tables, brushes were scattered on the floor, and colorful papers were everywhere. The room smelled like spilled paint and looked like a rainbow had exploded.

"Oh my!" gasped Mrs. Chen, the art teacher, as she walked in. "What happened here?"

Melody felt her little heart beating fast. She remembered what had happened the night before. She had been practicing a new song and flying around the room. In her excitement, she had knocked over paint bottles and scattered supplies everywhere. But she had been too scared to tell anyone.

As the children arrived for art class, they looked sad and confused. "Where are our clean brushes?" asked little Emma. "I can't find any paper!" said Tommy, looking around with big worried eyes.

Mrs. Chen tried to clean up, but there was so much mess. The children couldn't do their art projects. Some started to cry because they really wanted to paint pictures for their families.

Melody watched from her perch, feeling terrible inside. Her tummy felt all twisty, and she didn't feel like singing at all. She knew she had caused this problem, but saying sorry felt scary.

Then Melody remembered what her mama bird had taught her: "When we make mistakes, the best thing to do is be honest and help fix them."

Taking a deep breath, Melody flew down to Mrs. Chen's shoulder. "Tweet-tweet," she chirped softly. "Mrs. Chen, I need to tell you something important."

Mrs. Chen smiled gently. "What is it, Melody?"

"I made this mess last night when I was practicing my songs. I was flying around and knocked things over. I'm very, very sorry," Melody sang in her sweetest voice. "I should have told you right away."

Mrs. Chen's eyes were kind and understanding. "Thank you for telling me the truth, Melody. That was very brave of you."

"Can I help clean up?" asked Melody eagerly. "I want to make it right."

"Of course!" said Mrs. Chen. "Teamwork makes everything better."

Melody used her beak to pick up small pieces of paper and put them in neat piles. She helped organize the paintbrushes by color. The children joined in too, and soon everyone was working together.

As they cleaned, Melody sang a special cleaning song: "Pick up, clean up, working as a team! Making things tidy makes everyone beam!"

The children giggled and sang along. The room began to sparkle again.

When everything was clean and organized, Mrs. Chen gave Melody a gentle pat. "You made a mistake, but you did the right thing by telling the truth and helping to fix it. That's what good friends do."

Melody felt so much better! Her tummy wasn't twisty anymore, and her heart felt light and happy.

From that day on, Melody always remembered to be careful when flying around the art room. And whenever she made a mistake, she told the truth right away and helped make things better.

The children loved Melody even more because they knew she was honest and caring. And Melody's songs sounded sweeter than ever because her heart was full of wellness and joy.

The End.
