# Joey's Musical Garden Party

Joey the kangaroo was bouncing happily in his sunny backyard. The warm sun tickled his soft brown fur, and the green grass felt springy under his feet. "What a beautiful day!" he said, hopping in circles.

As Joey bounced near the fence, he heard a sad sniffling sound. He stopped and listened carefully with his big ears. The sound was coming from next door.

"Hello?" Joey called softly. "Is someone there?"

A little voice answered, "Yes... I'm Mei. I just moved here from far away, and I feel lonely."

Joey's heart felt heavy when he heard how sad Mei sounded. He knew exactly what to do! "Don't worry, Mei! I'm Joey, and I live right here. Would you like to be friends?"

Through a gap in the fence, Joey saw a small girl with dark hair sitting alone. Her eyes were red from crying, and she looked very tired.

"I miss my old home," Mei whispered. "Everything here is different. Even the music sounds different."

Joey's ears perked up. "You like music? I love music too! What kind of music do you know?"

Mei's face brightened a little. "In my old country, we played beautiful songs with special instruments. But I don't have any here."

Joey thought hard. He wanted to help Mei feel better, but he also needed to take care of himself. He was getting thirsty from all the bouncing in the warm sun.

"Wait right here, Mei! I have an idea!" Joey hopped to his water bowl and took a long, cool drink. "Ahh, that's better," he said, feeling refreshed.

Then Joey gathered some empty flower pots, wooden spoons, and a metal watering can from around the yard. He carried them carefully to the fence.

"Mei, let's make music together! These can be our instruments!"

Mei's eyes grew wide with wonder. "Really? We can make music with those?"

"Of course!" Joey said, tapping a flower pot with a wooden spoon. It made a lovely hollow sound - *tap, tap, tap*. "Try it!"

Mei picked up another spoon and gently tapped a different pot. *Ting, ting, ting* - it made a higher sound.

"That's beautiful!" Joey cheered. "Can you teach me a song from your country?"

Mei smiled for the first time that day. She began to tap a gentle rhythm while humming a sweet melody. Joey listened carefully, then joined in with his own pot, creating harmony.

Soon, other neighbors heard the lovely music floating through the air. Mrs. Robin flew down to chirp along, and even grumpy Mr. Turtle poked his head out to listen.

"This is wonderful!" said Mrs. Butterfly, landing on the fence. "What a beautiful way to welcome our new neighbor!"

As they played together, Joey noticed that Mei looked much happier. Her cheeks were pink with joy instead of tears.

"Thank you, Joey," Mei said. "You helped me feel at home here. You listened to my feelings and cared about me."

Joey's heart felt warm and full. "That's what friends do, Mei. When someone feels sad, we try to help them feel better. And when we take care of ourselves too, we have more energy to help others!"

From that day on, Joey and Mei had musical playtime every afternoon in the sunny backyard. They learned songs from each other's cultures and always made sure to drink water and rest when they needed to.

And whenever they heard someone feeling lonely, they invited them to join their joyful music circle.

The End.
