# Gigi the Giraffe and the Fair Turn Treehouse

High in a friendly tree, there was a cozy treehouse. It smelled like pine and warm sunshine. Inside, a thoughtful giraffe named Gigi bent her long neck to peek through the little window. The breeze tickled her nose. She giggled. “Hello, treehouse,” she said softly. “Today we will have fun.”

Gigi had hung a shiny bell by the door. Ting-ting! The bell chimed as her friends climbed up the ladder: Momo the monkey, Pip the parrot, and Tula the turtle. Their feet and claws made gentle clacks on the wooden floor.

“Look!” said Gigi, her big eyes bright. “We have a new sharing slide!” The slide curled down from the treehouse like a smooth ribbon. It was bright blue and smelled a little like rain.

“I want first!” Momo shouted, bouncing up and down. His tail swished and his eyes sparkled.

“No, me first!” squawked Pip, flapping her green wings. Feathers floated like leaves.

Tula stretched her neck. “I was here early,” she said slowly. “I should go first.”

Gigi’s ears twitched. Her heart felt wobbly. She wanted everyone to be happy. She also wanted to be fair. Justice means fairness, she remembered. Justice means everyone gets what is right.

“Friends,” Gigi said in her calm voice, “We need justice. We need a fair way.”

Momo crossed his arms. Pip puffed up. Tula sighed. The room felt tight and noisy.

Gigi took a deep breath. She could smell the sweet apples in the basket by the wall. “Let’s sit,” she said. “We will talk.” She set the apples in the middle. “Crunch if you are calm.” They each took a bite. Crunch. Crunch. Crunch. The sounds made them smile.

“What is fair?” asked Gigi.

“Me first,” said Momo, softer now. “I’m fast.”

“I want first,” Pip said, “because I can fly down fast too.”

“I am slow,” said Tula. “I may wait a long time.”

Gigi nodded. She listened with her ears and her heart. “Justice is not just loud or fast,” she said. “Justice means we all get a turn. And the turn is safe and kind. We can make a plan.”

Momo blinked. “A plan?”

“Yes,” said Gigi. “We can take turns. We can also think about needs.”

Pip tilted her head. “Needs?”

Tula gave a small smile. “I need extra time,” she whispered.

Gigi tapped her hoof on the floor. Tap. Tap. Tap. “How about this: We use the bell. The bell tells when a turn starts and when a turn ends. Each friend gets two bell rings for their turn. We will go by need today. Tula can go first because she needs extra time. Then Momo. Then Pip. Tomorrow we will change the order so it stays fair.”

Momo’s tail stopped twitching. “Two bell rings?”

Pip shook out her feathers. “And I get a turn after Momo?”

“Yes,” said Gigi. “And we all cheer for each friend. That is cooperation. We solve it together.”

They all looked at the bell. It shone in the soft light. Ting-ting, it seemed to sing, ready to help.

“Okay,” said Momo at last. “I like cheering.”

“Agreed,” said Pip.

“Thank you,” said Tula.

Gigi smiled. “We have a plan.”

She held the bell cord in her teeth. Ting! “Tula’s turn,” Gigi said.

Tula moved to the top of the slide. The wood was smooth and warm. “I feel a little scared,” she said.

“We are here,” said Gigi. “We will count together.”

“One, two, three!” they chanted.

Tula slid. Shoooo! The wind kissed her shell. At the bottom she laughed. “That felt like a small wave!”

Gigi rang the bell again. Ting! “Turn done!” she called.

“Great sliding!” shouted Momo.

“Good glide!” squawked Pip.

Tula climbed back up slowly, smiling. “Your turn, Momo,” she said.

Ting! Momo whooshed down. “Whee!” His fur brushed the slide with a soft hush. Ting! “Turn done!” Gigi rang.

“Nice job!” said Tula.

“Fast and fun!” chirped Pip.

Ting! Pip’s turn came. She tucked her wings and zipped down like a green leaf in the wind. Ting! “Turn done!”

“Beautiful slide,” said Gigi.

They went again, in the same order, with two bell rings each. The treehouse felt light and happy. The bell sang. Their cheers filled the air.

After a while, Gigi said, “Now we will switch the order, so justice stays strong.” She drew a simple chart on a leaf with a little berry. “Tomorrow: Momo, Pip, Tula. The next day: Pip, Tula, Momo. We take turns being first. We think about needs. We listen. That is fair.”

They all nodded. The plan felt safe, like a warm blanket.

Momo grinned. “We did it together.”

Pip fluffed her feathers. “That is cooperation!”

Tula patted the floor gently. “And the turns were just,” she said proudly.

Gigi’s heart felt steady and bright. She looked around the treehouse. She could hear birds singing, leaves whispering, and her friends breathing easy. “When we use justice,” she said, “everyone feels seen. Everyone gets a turn. That is the fair way.”

They shared the last apple slices. Sweet juice dripped on their chins. They laughed and cleaned up together.

As the sun went soft and golden, Gigi rang the bell one last time. Ting! “Thank you, friends. Today, we learned justice. We cooperated. We took turns. That is our treehouse rule.”

“Yes,” said her friends. “Fair and kind.”

The End.
