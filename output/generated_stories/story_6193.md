# Buttons the Rabbit Cleans the Attic

Buttons was a small, determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. One sunny afternoon, Buttons hopped up the stairs to the quiet attic. The attic smelled a little dusty, like old paper and warm wood. Sunlight made golden lines across the floor.

“I promised I would tidy the attic today,” Buttons said out loud. “I can do it. I am responsible.”

Buttons took a deep breath and opened a big box. “Oh!” he whispered. Inside were shiny buttons, ribbons, and tiny bells. They tinkled softly. “These are pretty. But I must clean first. Cleaning comes before playing.” Buttons nodded to himself, trying to be brave and strong.

Then he heard a gentle squeak. “Squeak-squeak?”

“Hello?” Buttons called. From behind a stack of books came a small mouse. She had soft brown fur and a tiny pink nose.

“My name is Mina,” said the mouse. “I live under the hats. It is very messy today. I feel lost.”

Buttons’ ears perked up. “I am Buttons. I promised to tidy the attic. I will help you find your home, and I will clean too.”

“Thank you,” Mina said with a shy smile. “That is very kind.”

Buttons picked up a feather duster. “First, I dust.” Swish, swish. Dust sparkled in the light like fairy snow. “Next, I sort.” He made neat piles: hats here, books there, toys in a basket, ribbons and buttons in a tin. His paws moved fast, but gentle.

Mina watched with wide eyes. “You are careful,” she said.

“I am being responsible,” Buttons replied. “When I say I will do a job, I do it.”

Buttons found a small hat with a blue ribbon. Inside was a soft, cozy nest. “Mina!” he called. “Is this your home?”

Mina’s eyes shone. “Yes! You found it! Thank you, Buttons.”

Buttons smiled. “I am glad. Now I will sweep.” Sweep, sweep, went the broom. The floor felt smooth and clean under his paws.

Just then, the attic window creaked. A gentle breeze blew in. It smelled like fresh grass. Papers fluttered like butterflies. “Oh no!” Buttons cried. “The papers!”

Mina darted and grabbed one paper with her tiny paws. Buttons hopped and caught another with his long ears—flip! Together they giggled. “We are a good team,” Mina said.

Buttons stacked the papers and tied them with a ribbon. He patted the stack. “Safe and tidy.”

At the back of the attic, Buttons found a small drum, a wooden whistle, and a paint box. His whiskers twitched. He wanted to play music and paint a picture. He could almost hear the drum go boom-boom and the whistle sing tweet-tweet.

Buttons touched the drum, then pulled his paw back. “First I finish my job,” he said. “Then I can play. That is being responsible.”

Mina nodded. “I can help, so you can finish faster.”

So Buttons and Mina worked side by side. They stacked books from biggest to smallest. Thunk, thunk, thunk. They folded scarves, soft and warm. They lined up the shiny buttons—click, click, click—like tiny moons.

Buttons paused and looked around. The attic felt calmer now. The light seemed brighter. “We are almost done,” he said.

“Almost,” Mina agreed. “But look.” She pointed to a broken toy wagon with a wobbly wheel.

Buttons knelt down. “I can fix it. Fixing is part of taking care.” He found glue and a small string. He turned the wheel slowly and pressed it tight. “Hold it, please,” he asked Mina.

“I am holding,” Mina said, very still.

They counted together, “One, two, three, four, five.” The wheel stayed on. Buttons smiled so big his nose wiggled. “We did it!”

Now the attic was clean. The boxes were neat. The hats made a pretty mountain. Mina’s home was safe and snug under the blue-ribbon hat.

Buttons clapped his paws. “I kept my promise. I finished the job. Now we can play!”

Mina’s whiskers twitched. “What shall we play?”

Buttons opened the paint box. Colors shone like little rainbows. “Let’s make a thank-you picture,” he said. “You helped me. I want to show I care.” He dipped a brush. Swish! He painted a bright sun. Mina painted a tiny mouse and a rabbit holding hands. They added a clean attic with neat shelves. It looked happy.

Buttons tapped the drum softly. Boom-boom, like a gentle heartbeat. Mina blew the whistle—tweet! It sounded cheerful. Together they made a little song as they painted. It felt warm and peaceful.

When the picture was dry, Buttons hung it on the wall with a small clip. “This picture shows our responsibility,” he said. “We cleaned first. We fixed things. We helped each other.”

Mina nodded. “It also shows friendship,” she whispered. “And kindness.”

Buttons tucked the broom and duster away. He closed the boxes with care. He picked up a tiny bell and gave it to Mina. “For you,” he said. “A friendship bell.”

Mina shook it. Tinkle-tinkle. “I will take good care of it,” she said. “I will be responsible too.”

Buttons felt proud. His heart felt big and light. “Today I learned that doing my job first makes time for fun,” he said. “Being responsible helps me, helps my friends, and keeps our space safe.”

Mina smiled. “And when we care for others, they feel safe too.”

Buttons nodded. He waved good-bye and hopped to the stairs. The attic was quiet again, but not lonely. It was clean, bright, and full of gentle, happy sounds.

The End.
