# Nutkin's Big Voice

High up in the old oak tree, Nutkin the squirrel lived in the coziest treehouse. It had tiny windows that let in golden sunlight and a small door painted bright red. Nutkin loved his home, but today something was very wrong.

"Oh no!" squeaked Nutkin, looking out his window. The wooden bridge that connected his treehouse to the other animals' homes had broken during the night's big storm. Pieces of wood hung down like broken teeth.

Nutkin's tummy rumbled loudly. "Grumble, grumble!" He needed to get to the community food storage, but now he was stuck! The other animals were gathering below, chattering and planning.

"We should wait for the grown-ups to fix it," said Benny Bunny.

"Maybe it will fix itself," whispered Penny Mouse, so quietly no one could hear her.

Nutkin watched from his window, feeling scared and alone. His little heart beat fast like a tiny drum. But then he remembered something his mama had taught him: "When you need help, use your voice, little one. Speak up clearly and kindly."

Taking a deep breath, Nutkin opened his window wide. "Hello, friends!" he called out in his strongest voice. "I'm stuck up here! Can you hear me?"

All the animals looked up. Their furry faces turned toward Nutkin's treehouse.

"I have an idea to fix our bridge!" Nutkin continued, his voice growing more confident. "But I need everyone's help!"

"We're listening!" called back Benny Bunny.

Nutkin felt proud and brave. "I can see everything from up here! Benny, you're strong - can you gather the fallen branches? Penny, you're small and quick - can you find rope in the tool shed? And Ollie Owl, you're wise - can you help us plan which pieces go where?"

The animals looked at each other with bright, excited eyes. "Yes!" they all shouted together.

"But Nutkin," called Penny Mouse, "how will we get the materials up to you?"

Nutkin's bushy tail twitched as he thought hard. "I'll make a basket from these vines by my window! You can tie things to it, and I'll pull them up!"

For the next hour, all the animals worked together like a happy team. Nutkin directed from above, his voice clear and helpful. "A little to the left, Benny! Perfect, Penny! Great job, Ollie!"

The sweet smell of fresh wood filled the air as they built. Birds chirped encouragingly from nearby branches. Soon, a beautiful new bridge stretched from Nutkin's treehouse to the main tree.

"Hooray!" cheered all the animals as Nutkin scampered across his new bridge. His tiny paws pattered happily on the sturdy wood.

"Thank you, everyone!" said Nutkin, his whiskers twitching with joy. "When I spoke up and asked for help, we solved the problem together!"

Benny Bunny patted Nutkin's back. "You were a great leader, Nutkin! You used your voice to help all of us."

"And now we have an even better bridge than before!" added Penny Mouse, no longer whispering.

As the sun set, painting the sky orange and pink, Nutkin sat on his new bridge with his friends. His tummy was full, his heart was happy, and he felt proud.

"I learned something important today," Nutkin said softly. "When I speak up clearly and ask for help, good things happen. My voice matters, and so does everyone else's."

The animals nodded and smiled, watching the first stars twinkle above their treehouse home.

The End.
