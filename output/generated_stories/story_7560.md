# Bella Bird Learns to Listen

Bella was a beautiful blue bird who loved to sing. Every morning, she would perch on the bathroom windowsill and sing her cheerful songs. "Tweet-tweet-tra-la-la!" she would chirp, making the whole bathroom echo with her sweet voice.

But Bella had one big problem. She never listened to directions. When Mama Bird told her to clean her feathers, Bella would fly in circles instead. When Papa Bird asked her to practice her quiet voice, Bella would sing even louder!

One sunny morning, Bella flew into the Johnson family's bathroom. She saw the shiny mirror and thought it would be the perfect place for a concert.

"Tweet-tweet-tra-la-la!" Bella sang at the top of her lungs. Her voice bounced off the white tiles and made everything sound extra loud.

Little Emma Johnson was trying to brush her teeth. "Shh, pretty bird," Emma said gently. "Please sing quietly. I'm trying to brush my teeth."

But Bella didn't listen. She flapped her wings and sang even louder. "TWEET-TWEET-TRA-LA-LA!"

Emma's toothpaste foam went everywhere! Some landed on the mirror, some on the sink, and some on the floor. "Oh no!" Emma cried.

Mama Johnson came running. "What happened, Emma?"

"The bird is singing so loud that I can't concentrate," Emma explained, wiping toothpaste from her chin.

Mama Johnson looked at Bella kindly. "Sweet bird, we love your beautiful voice, but right now Emma needs quiet to brush her teeth properly. Could you please sing softly?"

Bella tilted her little head. She had never thought about how her loud singing might bother others. She watched as Emma tried again to brush her teeth, but Bella's loud songs made Emma's hand shake.

"I understand now," Bella chirped softly. "When someone asks me to do something, it's important to listen."

Bella took a deep breath and sang in her gentlest voice: "Tweet-tweet-tra-la-la." Her song was still beautiful, but now it was soft and peaceful.

Emma smiled. "Thank you, bird! Now I can brush my teeth properly." She moved the toothbrush in small, careful circles, just like the dentist had shown her.

Bella watched Emma follow the directions for brushing teeth: up and down, side to side, and don't forget the back teeth! Bella realized that following directions helped Emma take good care of herself.

"Can you teach me the tooth-brushing song?" Bella asked.

Emma giggled. "Brush up high, brush down low, make those teeth all clean and glow!"

Bella sang along quietly, moving her head up and down like a tiny toothbrush. "Brush up high, brush down low, make those teeth all clean and glow!"

From that day on, Bella became the best direction-follower in the whole neighborhood. When Mama Bird asked her to clean her feathers, Bella listened carefully and cleaned each feather gently. When Papa Bird asked her to practice flying quietly, Bella flapped her wings softly.

Every morning, Bella would visit Emma's bathroom. She would sing her special tooth-brushing song in a gentle voice while Emma brushed her teeth. They became the very best of friends.

"Following directions helps everyone feel happy and safe," Bella would sing. "When we listen and understand, we can all work together!"

And from that day forward, Bella's beautiful songs brought joy to everyone because she had learned the most important lesson of all: listening to others and following directions shows love and respect.

The End.
