# Milo Mouse Makes It Better

Milo was a kind little mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived in a comfortable nursery where sunlight made warm squares on the rug. The room smelled like clean cotton and crayons. On the shelf sat neat baskets for blocks, books, and tiny cars. A big, cozy chair waited by the window.

“Good morning,” Milo squeaked, stretching his whiskers. He loved tidy mornings. He liked to know where things were. It made his heart feel calm and quiet.

Today was puzzle day. The puzzle had big, smooth pieces that felt cool in Milo’s paws. He hummed as he worked. Click, click, click. The pieces fit together like hugs.

Suddenly, a loud clatter rattled the room. Crash! A tower of blocks fell near the door. Little Bunny, with floppy ears and gentle eyes, stood very still. Her nose wriggled. “Oh no,” she whispered. “I didn’t mean to.”

Milo looked up. “It’s okay,” he said softly. He walked over with small, careful steps. “Are you alright?”

Little Bunny nodded, but her eyes were wet. “I’m scared,” she said. “I made a mess.”

Milo took a slow breath and showed her. “Watch me breathe in,” he said, filling his tiny chest. “And breathe out.” The air smelled like warm milk. The room felt quiet again.

Little Bunny copied. In and out. Her ears relaxed. “Thank you,” she whispered.

“Everyone makes messes,” Milo said. “We can fix it together.” He smiled a gentle mouse smile. “That’s compassion. Compassion is caring when someone feels sad or scared.”

Little Bunny blinked. “Com…passion?”

“It means we help,” Milo said. “We are kind.”

They knelt by the fallen blocks. Tap, tap, tap. Milo picked up one block. Little Bunny picked up another. “Blocks go in the blue basket,” Milo said. He pointed to the shelf. “Books go in the green basket. Cars go in the red.”

Little Bunny’s nose twitched. “Blue, green, red,” she repeated.

They made a steady rhythm. Tap—the block in the basket. Swish—the car in its home. Flip—the book on its shelf. The sound was like a calm song. The rug felt soft under their knees. Sunbeams warmed their backs.

“See,” Milo said. “When we put things away, the room feels happy. My heart feels calm.” He patted his chest. “Does your heart feel calm?”

Little Bunny smiled a small smile. “A little.”

Milo nodded. “We can make it more calm.” He set three more blocks in a line and stacked them gently. “Slow and steady. We don’t rush.”

Just then, Bear Cub waddled in, hugging a fluffy blanket. “I heard a crash,” Bear Cub said. “Is everyone okay?”

“We’re okay,” Little Bunny said. “Milo helped me breathe. And we’re cleaning up.”

Bear Cub squeezed his blanket. “Can I help?” His voice was deep and kind.

“Yes,” Milo said. “Thank you.” He loved how friends could help and still do their own part. “You can carry the cars, Bear. Bunny and I will do the blocks.”

They worked together. Bear’s paws were big and careful. Bunny’s ears wiggled as she hummed. Milo kept count. “One, two, three. Blue basket. One, two, three. Red basket.”

When the last block slid into place, Milo clapped his tiny paws. Clap, clap! The nursery looked neat again. The cozy chair looked extra inviting. The sun made the rug sparkle.

Little Bunny took a brave breath. “I want to try the puzzle,” she said. “By myself.” Her voice was small but strong.

“You can do it,” Milo said. “I believe in you.” He sat nearby, calm and ready, but he did not touch the pieces. He let Bunny be independent.

Little Bunny turned a piece and frowned. “It doesn’t fit.”

Milo kept his voice soft. “Try turning it a little. And listen.” He tapped his ear. “When it’s right, it goes click.”

Bunny turned the piece. Click! Her eyes widened. “I did it!” She giggled, light as a bell.

Bear Cub cheered. “Hooray!”

Milo’s heart felt warm. He was proud of Bunny. He was proud of their calm work and their neat room. He was proud of their kind hearts.

A tiny sniffle came from the door. It was Squeak, the baby mouse, holding a crumpled drawing. “It tore,” Squeak said. “Is it bad?”

Milo crouched down, whiskers level with Squeak’s. “It’s not bad,” he said. “It just needs care.” He took a piece of gentle tape and smoothed the paper. “We can fix it. We can be kind to your picture, too.”

Squeak’s eyes shone. “Thank you.”

Milo nodded. “This is compassion,” he said again. “We care for each other. We care for our things. We breathe, we help, we put things away. That keeps our room calm, and our hearts calm.”

The friends cuddled on the big chair. They listened to the soft tick-tick of the clock and the hush of wind outside. The nursery felt safe and warm and very, very tidy.

Little Bunny whispered, “I feel calm.”

Bear Cub said, “I feel strong.”

Squeak said, “I feel loved.”

Milo smiled. “Me too,” he said. “When we are kind and organized, we help everyone feel better. That’s compassion.”

The End.
