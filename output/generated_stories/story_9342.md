# Benny Beaver's Baking Day

Benny Beaver stood in the warm kitchen, his brown tail tapping excitedly on the wooden floor. Sweet smells of vanilla and cinnamon filled the air. Golden sunlight streamed through the window, making the copper pots shine like treasure.

"Today we're making cookies!" announced Mrs. Owl, their cooking teacher. Around the big kitchen table stood Benny's friends: Ruby Rabbit, Sam Squirrel, and Penny Porcupine. Each had their own special mixing bowl.

Benny's whiskers twitched with excitement. He loved mixing and stirring! His strong beaver paws were perfect for kitchen work.

"I want to crack the eggs first!" Benny called out loudly, reaching across the table.

"Wait, Benny," said Mrs. Owl gently. "Let's take turns. Ruby, would you like to start?"

Benny's tail slapped the floor. "But I'm the best at cracking eggs!" he said, his voice getting louder. "I should go first!"

Ruby Rabbit's ears drooped sadly. She had been holding an egg carefully in her small paws.

"Taking turns means everyone gets a chance," Mrs. Owl explained kindly. "Ruby was ready first."

Benny crossed his arms and frowned. He watched Ruby gently tap the egg on the bowl's edge. Crack! The golden yolk plopped perfectly into the fluffy white flour.

"Nice job, Ruby!" cheered Sam Squirrel.

"Now Sam's turn to add the butter," said Mrs. Owl.

Sam's bushy tail wiggled as he carefully measured the creamy yellow butter. It smelled rich and sweet as it dropped into the bowl.

Benny's foot tapped impatiently. "When is my turn?" he asked.

"Very soon," smiled Mrs. Owl. "Penny, would you add the sugar?"

Penny Porcupine carefully poured sparkling white sugar. It made a soft whooshing sound as it fell. "This is fun!" she giggled.

Finally, Mrs. Owl turned to Benny. "Now it's your turn to mix everything together!"

Benny's eyes lit up like stars. He picked up the big wooden spoon. His strong arms stirred the mixture round and round. The ingredients swirled together like a delicious dance.

"Wow, Benny!" said Ruby. "You're really good at mixing!"

"Your strong arms make it look easy," added Sam admiringly.

Benny felt warm and happy inside. "Thanks! You're all good at different things too. Ruby cracks eggs perfectly, Sam measures just right, and Penny pours so carefully."

Mrs. Owl nodded wisely. "When we take turns and work together, we make the best cookies!"

The friends took turns again - rolling the dough, cutting shapes, and placing cookies on the baking sheet. Benny waited patiently for each turn, cheering for his friends.

Soon, the kitchen filled with the amazing smell of baking cookies. Golden-brown treats came out of the oven, perfectly crispy and sweet.

"These are the most delicious cookies ever!" said Penny, taking a warm bite.

"That's because we all helped," said Benny proudly. "Taking turns made them extra special."

Mrs. Owl smiled warmly. "When we respect each other and take turns, everyone feels important and happy."

Benny hugged his friends. His tail wagged contentedly. "Next time, I'll remember to wait for my turn. It makes everything more fun when we all get to help!"

The friends sat together, sharing their warm cookies and laughing. The kitchen felt cozy and full of friendship.

The End.
