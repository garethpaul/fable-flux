# Ollie's Workshop Adventure

Ollie the otter loved to build things with his tiny paws. Today, he bounced into Grandpa's garage workshop, his whiskers twitching with excitement.

"Look, Grandpa! I want to make a toy boat!" Ollie squeaked, pointing at the colorful wood pieces on the workbench.

Grandpa smiled and handed Ollie a simple instruction sheet with pictures. "Follow these steps, little one. First, find the blue piece."

Ollie's dark eyes sparkled as he grabbed the first piece he saw. It was red, not blue. He tried to fit it with another piece, but it wouldn't work.

"Grr!" Ollie huffed, his fluffy tail slapping the floor. "This is too hard!"

He threw the red piece down and it clattered loudly on the concrete floor. The sound echoed through the garage, making Ollie feel even more upset.

"I can't do it!" Ollie cried, his little paws balled into fists. His heart was beating fast, and his face felt hot.

Grandpa knelt down beside Ollie. "I see you're feeling frustrated, buddy. That's okay. Everyone feels that way sometimes."

"But I want to make my boat NOW!" Ollie whimpered.

"Let's try something to help you feel better," Grandpa said gently. "Can you take three deep breaths with me? In through your nose, out through your mouth."

Ollie sniffled and breathed in slowly. The garage smelled like sawdust and oil. He breathed out with a whoosh. They did this three times together.

"How do you feel now?" Grandpa asked.

"A little better," Ollie admitted, his shoulders relaxing.

"Good! When we feel frustrated, our bodies get all tight and our minds get cloudy. Deep breathing helps us feel calm so we can think clearly."

Grandpa picked up the instruction sheet. "Now, let's look at the pictures together. What color does step one say to find?"

Ollie looked carefully at the drawing. "Blue! I need the blue piece first."

"That's right!" Grandpa cheered. "Following directions step by step makes building much easier."

Ollie found the blue piece and then the yellow one that went with it. They fit together perfectly with a satisfying click.

"It worked!" Ollie giggled, doing a little happy dance.

Step by step, Ollie followed the pictures. When he felt frustrated again trying to attach the sail, he remembered what Grandpa taught him.

"Deep breaths," Ollie whispered to himself. "In and out, in and out."

Soon, his breathing slowed down, and he could see the problem clearly. The sail piece was upside down!

"I figured it out by staying calm!" Ollie announced proudly.

Twenty minutes later, Ollie held up his finished wooden boat. It was painted bright blue with a white sail, just like in the picture.

"You did it, Ollie!" Grandpa said, giving him a warm hug. "You learned something very important today."

"I learned that when I feel frustrated, I can take deep breaths to feel better," Ollie said. "And following directions helps me succeed!"

"That's right. Taking care of our feelings is just as important as taking care of our bodies," Grandpa explained. "You're becoming very wise, little otter."

Ollie sailed his new boat in a bucket of water, watching it bob gently. He felt proud and peaceful inside.

From that day on, whenever Ollie felt frustrated, he remembered to breathe deeply and follow directions carefully. It made everything so much better!

The End.
