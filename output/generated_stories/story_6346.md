# Cubby Helps on the Porch

Cubby was a friendly bear cub with soft brown fur and a bright, curious nose. One sunny morning, Cubby sat on the peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like pine wood and warm honey tea. A cool breeze tickled Cubby’s ears. Birds chirped, “Chee-chee!” Leaves rustled, “Shhh.”

“Good morning, porch,” Cubby said with a smile. “I’m ready to help today!”

Mama Bear came out carrying a basket of little seed packets. “Hello, Cubby,” she said. “I want to plant these seeds, but I also need to sweep the porch. Can you help?”

Cubby’s eyes sparkled. “Yes! Helping makes my heart feel warm.”

Mama set the seeds on a small table. “Thank you. I’ll get the broom.” She went inside.

Just then, a butterfly fluttered by. “Hello, butterfly,” Cubby whispered. The butterfly landed near the seeds. A tiny breeze puffed across the porch. Whoosh!

“Oh no!” Cubby gasped. The seed packets slid. One packet slipped under the big porch bench. Scratch, slide!

Cubby lay on his tummy and peeked under the bench. It was dark and dusty. He could smell old leaves. “I see you, little seeds,” he said. But the packet was far back, just out of reach.

Cubby stretched his paw. “Almost… almost…” His claws touched the edge. The packet scooted even farther back. “Oh grapes,” Cubby said, feeling a little frustrated.

Mama came back with the broom. “Everything okay, Cubby?”

Cubby took a slow breath, in and out. “I want to help, but the seeds slid under the bench. I can’t reach.”

Mama nodded kindly. “Thank you for telling me. We can solve this with a plan.”

“A plan!” Cubby sat up. “I like plans.”

Mama smiled. “First, let’s calm our bodies. Let’s count the birds together.”

Cubby listened. “One chirp… two chirps… three chirps…” His shoulders relaxed.

“Good,” said Mama. “Now, what tools do we have on our porch?”

Cubby looked around. He saw the broom, a long wooden spoon by the tea cup, a stack of soft cloths, and some string. He sniffed the air. The wooden spoon smelled like sweet soup. The cloths smelled like soap and sunshine.

Cubby tapped his chin. “Maybe I can use the long spoon to reach.” He lay down again and slid the spoon under the bench. Clink! The spoon touched the packet but pushed it sideways. The packet got stuck behind a wobbly pinecone.

“Hmm,” said Cubby. “That didn’t work the way I hoped.”

“That’s okay,” said Mama. “Trying is brave. What else could we try?”

Cubby’s nose wiggled. “What if we make a gentle grabber?” He picked up the soft cloth and the string. “I can tie the cloth to the spoon. Then it will be soft and sticky for the seeds.”

Mama held one end of the string. Cubby tied a small knot. Tug, tug. “Teamwork,” Cubby said. “That’s helpful.”

“Very helpful,” Mama agreed.

Cubby slid the soft-tipped spoon under the bench. He moved slow and steady. “I’m using my eyes and paws together,” he whispered. “Careful… careful…” The cloth brushed the packet. Fwip! The packet moved toward Cubby.

“Almost there!” said Mama in a quiet voice.

Cubby felt the urge to push fast, but he remembered the plan. “Slow and gentle,” he told himself. He made a tiny scoop. The packet slipped onto the cloth. He pulled it out. Swish!

“I did it!” Cubby cheered. He hugged the packet. It crinkled like tiny rain. “We did it with a plan and a tool!”

Mama clapped softly. “You stayed calm, used a strategy, and helped. Thank you, Cubby.”

Cubby’s tummy felt proud and warm. “Can I help more?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “I’ll sweep. Can you count the seed holes for me? We need ten holes.”

Cubby nodded. He took a small stick and poked the soil in the porch planter. “One… two… three…” The soil felt cool and crumbly. The sun felt warm on his back. “Four… five… six… seven… eight… nine… ten!” He wiggled his paws. “All set!”

Mama sprinkled seeds. “Now we cover them.” Cubby patted the soil with gentle paws. Pat, pat. “Like tucking in for a nap,” he said.

After the sweeping and planting, the porch looked neat and happy. The tea steamed. The air smelled like clean wood and fresh dirt. The butterfly came back and danced above the planter.

Cubby sipped warm tea with Mama. “Helping feels good,” Cubby said. “When I felt stuck, I took a breath, made a plan, and used a tool.”

Mama nodded. “That is innovation and strategy. And you showed coordination with your careful paws. Most of all, you were helpful.”

Cubby smiled. “Next time something is tricky, I will breathe, think, and try again. I can help.”

The butterfly fluttered. The birds sang, “Chee-chee-chee!” Cubby looked at the peaceful front porch and felt brave and kind.

The End.
