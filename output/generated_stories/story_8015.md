# Daisy's Big Helper Day

Little Daisy the deer fawn stood quietly by the big wooden dining room table. Her soft brown eyes looked up at all the grown-up deer getting ready for the forest community dinner. The table was so tall, and Daisy felt so small.

"I want to help too," Daisy whispered to herself, watching her mama arrange the pretty wildflower centerpieces.

Mama Deer noticed Daisy's sad face. "What's wrong, my little one?"

"Everyone is helping with the dinner, but I'm too little," Daisy said, her voice shaky. "I can't reach the table like you and Papa. I can't carry the heavy plates like Uncle Buck."

The dining room smelled wonderful - like fresh pine needles and sweet berries. But Daisy couldn't enjoy it because she felt left out.

Papa Deer heard Daisy and knelt down beside her. "Being small doesn't mean you can't help, sweetie. Sometimes the best helpers come in small packages!"

"Really?" Daisy's ears perked up hopefully.

"Really!" said Grandma Doe, walking over with a warm smile. "I have an idea. Would you like to be our special napkin helper?"

Daisy nodded eagerly. "Yes! But... what if I mess up?"

"Then we'll fix it together," Mama said gently. "That's what families do."

Grandma Doe showed Daisy a basket full of soft, colorful napkins. "These need to go by each plate. You're just the right size to walk under the table and place them perfectly!"

Daisy took a deep breath. "I can do this," she said, feeling a little spark of confidence growing in her chest.

She picked up the first napkin - it was yellow like sunshine. Carefully, she walked to the first chair and placed it neatly beside the plate. "One down!" she said proudly.

"Beautiful work!" cheered Papa Deer.

As Daisy continued around the table, she started to feel more confident. The napkins were soft in her small hooves, and she could smell the lavender scent Grandma had sprinkled on them.

When she reached Uncle Buck's spot, she found him struggling to fold his napkin properly with his big hooves.

"Uncle Buck, would you like me to help?" Daisy asked bravely.

"Oh yes, please! Your little hooves are much better at this than mine," Uncle Buck said gratefully.

Daisy carefully folded his napkin into a perfect triangle. "There! Sometimes being small is helpful!"

Soon, other forest friends arrived for dinner. Mrs. Rabbit needed help reaching napkins for her little bunnies. Mr. Squirrel's tiny paws couldn't grip the napkins well. Each time, Daisy volunteered to help.

"My turn to help someone else!" Daisy would say cheerfully, and each friend thanked her warmly.

By the time all the guests arrived, Daisy had helped everyone and felt wonderful inside. Her chest puffed out with pride as she looked at the beautiful dining room.

"Daisy," announced Grandma Doe to everyone, "was our most important helper today! She showed us that when we work together and take turns helping each other, everything works out perfectly."

All the forest friends clapped their paws and hooves. Daisy's face glowed with happiness.

"I learned something today," Daisy said to her family. "I don't have to be big to be helpful. I just have to be brave and believe in myself!"

"And," added Mama Deer, "you showed everyone how wonderful it feels to volunteer and share the work."

That night, as Daisy curled up in her cozy bed, she smiled. Tomorrow, she would look for more ways to help others, because now she knew - she was capable of amazing things, just as she was.

The End.
