# Sunny Rooftop Rescue

On a bright morning, a resourceful squirrel named Sprig scampered onto a tall, flat rooftop. The roof felt warm under his tiny paws. The sun made the metal railings glimmer, and a soft breeze smelled like fresh bread from the bakery below.

Sprig’s fluffy tail twitched. “Today,” he said, “I will make a safe snack spot up here.” He had found a small pile of nuts near a wobbly drainpipe. He wanted to move them to a safer place.

But as Sprig looked around, he heard a faint sound. “Cheep… cheep…” A little bird hopped near a puddle. The bird’s wing drooped. Her feathers were ruffled.

Sprig paused. “Hello,” he said gently. “I’m Sprig. Are you okay?”

The bird blinked. “I’m Pip,” she chirped softly. “My wing is tired. I can’t fly home yet. And that dark cloud looks scary.”

Sprig looked up. Gray clouds were gathering like soft blankets. A cool, wet smell floated in the air. Drip, drip. A few raindrops landed on Sprig’s whiskers.

Sprig took a deep breath. His heart pitter-pattered. He had two problems now. He had his nuts to move. He had Pip to help. He needed to decide what to do first.

Sprig sat still and tapped his paw. “Think, Sprig. Plan it out,” he whispered. “What is most important? What is safest?” He looked at the wobbly pipe, the slippery puddle, and Pip’s worried eyes.

He nodded to himself. “First, help Pip get safe from the rain. Then, move the nuts. That is my plan.”

Pip peeped, “Are you sure? Your nuts are important.”

Sprig smiled. “You are important too. We can do both, one step at a time.”

The wind brushed against their ears. “Whoosh,” it said. The rain grew stronger. “Pitter-patter, pitter-patter.”

Sprig scanned the rooftop. He saw a sturdy wooden crate by a chimney. He saw a wide, flat board leaning on the skylight. He smelled damp tar and wet leaves. He listened to the raindrops drumming like tiny drums.

“Come with me,” Sprig said. He guided Pip carefully. “Watch your step. The roof is slippery.”

They reached the wooden crate. It was dry inside and cozy. Sprig tugged some soft straw from a corner. He made a little nest.

“Try this,” he said. “Rest here and keep warm.”

Pip settled into the straw. She sighed with relief. “Thank you, Sprig.”

Sprig smiled, but the rain grew louder. “Pitter! Patter!” He peeked at his nuts across the rooftop. They were still near the wobbly pipe. The puddle around them was growing.

Sprig made another decision. “I will move the nuts next. But I need a better way.”

He thought and planned again. “The board could be a bridge,” he said. “A dry path over the puddle!”

Sprig tugged the board inch by inch. “Scrape, scrape,” it went. He rested when his paws felt tired. He kept his tail up for balance. Finally, the board lay across the puddle like a sturdy walkway.

Sprig tested it with one careful paw. “Tap.” It held. He crossed with small steps. “Tip, tip, tip.” The puddle sloshed below. He breathed slowly and stayed steady.

At the nuts, Sprig saw the pipe wobble. “Rattle.” He decided, “I will not carry too many at once.” He filled a tiny leaf pouch with just a few nuts. He walked back over the board. Then he went again. And again.

On the third trip, the wind gusted. “Woooosh!” The board wiggled. Sprig paused and hugged the board. “It’s okay,” he told himself. “Slow is safe. Slow is smart.” He waited for the wind to pass. Then he took another careful step.

Soon, all the nuts were safe by the cozy crate. Sprig shook raindrops from his tail. Pip chirped with joy. “You did it!”

Sprig peeked at Pip’s wing. “How does it feel now?”

Pip stretched gently. “Better. Not perfect, but better.” She smiled. “You made a plan. You changed the plan when you needed to. You kept me safe. You kept your nuts safe too.”

Sprig felt warm in his chest. The storm began to fade. The clouds lightened, and a thin beam of sun made the wet rooftop sparkle.

Pip hopped to the edge of the crate. “May I try a little glide?”

Sprig nodded. “I will watch. If it’s too hard, we will think of another plan.”

Pip opened her wings. She glided to a nearby railing and landed. “Cheep! I did it!” She flapped happily.

Sprig clapped his tiny paws. “Hooray!”

They shared a nut snack inside the crate. The sun peeked out. The breeze smelled fresh and clean.

Pip chirped, “Today I learned to rest before I fly.”

Sprig said, “Today I learned to stop, think, and choose. First safety, then snacks. Little steps make big jobs easier.”

Pip nodded. “Planning helps. Changing your plan can help too.”

Sprig’s tail curled in delight. “Next time, we can plan a rooftop picnic!”

Pip laughed. “With a blanket so it won’t get soggy!”

They both giggled.

And on that sunny, shiny rooftop, Sprig’s good decisions made the day better for everyone. He felt proud, not just for finishing, but for thinking carefully and being kind.

The clear lesson: When a problem comes, pause, make a plan, choose the safest first step, and improve your plan as you go. Little, smart choices lead to big, happy results.

The End.
