# Nutkin's Rooftop Adventure

The warm afternoon sun shone down on the red brick rooftop where Nutkin the squirrel loved to play. His fluffy gray tail twitched with excitement as he scampered across the smooth tiles, collecting acorns for winter.

"This is the perfect spot!" Nutkin chattered to himself, finding a cozy corner near the chimney. He began arranging his acorns in neat little piles, humming a happy tune.

Suddenly, a loud THUMP echoed across the rooftop. Then another THUMP! THUMP! THUMP!

Nutkin's ears perked up. His whiskers twitched with annoyance. The noise was coming from the other side of the roof, where Ruby the robin had built her nest.

"What is all that racket?" Nutkin muttered, his tail bristling. The thumping sounds made it impossible to concentrate on his acorn sorting.

THUMP! CRASH! BANG!

Nutkin's cheeks puffed out with anger. His little paws clenched into fists. "That's it!" he squeaked loudly. "I'm going to tell that noisy bird to be quiet!"

He scurried across the rooftop, his claws clicking on the tiles. Steam seemed to come from his ears as he got angrier and angrier.

When Nutkin reached the other side, he saw Ruby hopping around frantically. Twigs and leaves were scattered everywhere. Her nest looked crooked and messy.

"HEY!" Nutkin shouted, his voice echoing across the rooftop. "Stop making all that noise! I'm trying to work!"

Ruby stopped hopping and looked at Nutkin with sad, shiny eyes. A single tear rolled down her orange breast.

"I'm sorry, Nutkin," Ruby chirped softly. "The big storm last night knocked down my nest. My three little eggs are inside, and I'm trying to fix it before they get cold."

Nutkin felt his anger melt away like ice cream in the sun. His tail drooped with shame. "Oh no," he whispered. "I didn't know. I was only thinking about myself."

Ruby sniffled. "I've been working all morning, but my wings are getting tired. I don't think I can fix it alone."

Nutkin took three deep breaths, just like his mama had taught him when he felt upset. In... out... in... out... in... out. His heart felt calmer.

"Ruby," Nutkin said gently, "I'm sorry I yelled at you. Would you like some help?"

Ruby's eyes brightened like tiny stars. "Really? You would help me?"

"Of course!" Nutkin smiled. "I'm good at building things with my paws, and you're good at knowing where everything should go."

Together, they worked as a team. Nutkin used his strong paws to weave the twigs tightly together. Ruby flew around, finding the softest moss and fluffiest feathers for the inside of the nest.

"A little to the left," Ruby chirped as Nutkin adjusted a branch.

"How's this?" Nutkin asked, patting the moss into place.

"Perfect!" Ruby sang happily.

Soon, the nest was even better than before. It was round and cozy, with thick walls to keep the eggs warm and safe.

"Thank you so much, Nutkin," Ruby said, settling gently onto her eggs. "You're such a kind friend."

Nutkin's chest puffed out with pride, but this time it was good pride. "I learned something important today," he said. "When someone seems to be bothering me, I should find out what's really happening before I get angry."

Ruby nodded. "And I learned that asking for help isn't something to be embarrassed about. Friends help friends!"

From that day on, whenever Nutkin felt his temper rising, he remembered to take deep breaths and think about others' feelings first. And Ruby always remembered that true friends are happy to lend a helping paw.

The End.
