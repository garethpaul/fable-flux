# Fern’s Closet Campout

Fern was a gentle deer fawn with soft, speckled fur and big, bright eyes. One rainy morning, patter-patter went the drops on the roof. Fern peeked into a small closet hideaway under the stairs. It smelled like cedar wood and clean blankets. The closet was cozy, quiet, and safe.

“I will make a camp spot,” Fern whispered. “I will wait for the rain to rest.”

Fern spread a blue blanket on the floor. She stacked pillows. She made a tiny tent with a sheet and a chair. “This is my calm space,” she said, smiling. “I can rest here.”

A little squirrel friend named Pip knocked on the door. Tap-tap. “Hi, Fern! Can I come in?” Pip asked, his tail flicking fast.

Fern took a breath. “You can come in if you are quiet and gentle,” she said. “This is my special hideaway. We use soft voices here.”

“Okay,” Pip said. He tiptoed in. The closet felt warm and smelled like rain and soap. They listened to the raindrops. Pitter, patter. Pitter, patter.

Soon, Sunbeam the bird chirped at the door. “Tweet! May I join you?” Sunbeam asked.

Fern nodded. “Yes, but please ask before touching my things. This is my boundary. My blanket and my pillow are for me.”

“I understand,” Sunbeam said. “I will sit on this corner and use my own scarf.” She fluffed her yellow feathers and settled in.

They sat together, calm and cozy. But Fern’s hooves itched to run. She looked at her friends. “I love my closet, but I also love to move,” she said. “When the rain stops, can we play outside?”

“Yes!” Pip chattered. “Let’s plan a fair game.”

“We can take turns,” Sunbeam chirped. “We can share, and we can rest when we need to. Fair and kind.”

Fern smiled. “That sounds good.”

The rain slowed. Drip… drop… stop. The air smelled fresh, like wet leaves and cool mud. Fern peeked out. A soft breeze kissed her nose. “It is time,” she said.

They stepped outside. The grass was sparkly with tiny drops. “Let’s make an outdoor trail,” Fern said. “We can hop to the rock, tiptoe to the bush, and run to the big tree.”

Pip clapped his little paws. “We can call it the Friendship Trail!”

Sunbeam sang, “Tweet-tweet! We will move our bodies and play in the fresh air.”

Fern set the rules. “We go one at a time. We cheer for each other. We ask before we tag or touch. If someone says ‘stop,’ we stop.”

“That is your boundary and ours,” Pip said. “We will be fair.”

Fern went first. “Hop, hop,” she sang, landing on the rock with a soft thump. She tiptoed to the bush. Crunch went the twigs. She ran to the big tree. Swish went the leaves as she brushed past.

“Great job!” cheered Sunbeam and Pip.

Pip went next. He hopped, he tiptoed, he ran, his tail waving like a flag. “Whee!” he squeaked.

Sunbeam fluttered along the trail, hopping with tiny feet. Tap-tap-tap. She paused. “I need a rest,” she said.

Fern nodded. “We can rest. Our bodies tell us when to stop.” She offered a shady spot. The air was cool. A sweet flower smell drifted by.

After a rest, they tried a new game. “Let’s play Leaf Pass,” Fern said. “We will pass a big green leaf, one by one. No grabbing. We wait our turn.”

Pip reached too fast. The leaf crinkled. Fern held up a hoof. “Please wait, Pip,” she said kindly. “I feel upset when my leaf is grabbed. Please ask me first.”

Pip’s ears drooped. “I’m sorry, Fern. May I have the leaf, please?”

“Yes,” Fern said, smiling. “Thank you for asking.” She passed the leaf. Pip hugged it close and then passed it to Sunbeam.

“Fair turns are fun,” Sunbeam chirped. “I like when we ask and we wait.”

They played and moved and laughed. They ran to the tree, then walked slowly back. They stretched tall like tree trunks. They curled small like acorns. They breathed in the fresh air, and they blew out like the wind. Whoooo.

When the clouds turned pink, they went back to the cozy closet hideaway to say goodbye. The closet felt extra warm now, like a hug after a busy day.

Fern looked at her friends. “Today we had an outdoor adventure,” she said. “We moved our bodies. We breathed fresh air. We were fair and kind. We used our words and our boundaries.”

Pip nodded. “We asked before touching. We took turns. We listened when someone said stop.”

Sunbeam sang softly, “Outdoor play helps us grow strong and calm.”

Fern curled on her blue blanket. “I learned that it’s good to have a calm, safe space,” she said, “and it’s good to go outside to run, hop, and breathe. I can say what I need. I can be fair and kind.”

Pip and Sunbeam smiled. “We can, too.”

Fern closed her eyes, happy and proud. “Tomorrow, we can make a new trail,” she whispered.

The End.
