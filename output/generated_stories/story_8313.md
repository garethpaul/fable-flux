# Tilly Turtle’s Tasty Study Snack

Tilly was a shy turtle with a smooth, green shell. She loved her quiet study corner. It was a cozy space by the window with a small desk, a soft lamp, and a basket of crayons. The air smelled like paper and warm sunshine. On her desk sat a little plant named Sprout. Sprout had bright, happy leaves.

“Good morning, Sprout,” Tilly whispered. “Today I will listen well and eat smart.”

Tilly had a little schedule on the wall. It showed a sun for morning, a book for study time, and a carrot for snack time. She tapped the picture of the carrot with her gentle turtle toe.

Knock, knock. Tilly’s friends peeked in. Buzz the bee hummed softly, Mia the mouse squeaked hello, and Pip the parrot ruffled green feathers that glowed in the sun.

“Can we study with you?” asked Mia.

Tilly blushed and nodded. “Yes, we can learn together.”

They sat on soft cushions. Pip read the picture book aloud in a friendly voice. “Today we learn about food that helps our bodies grow strong,” he said.

Tilly listened. She could hear Buzz’s tiny wings, like a quiet song. She could smell the sweet apple in Mia’s lunch bag. Her tummy made a small “glug-glug” sound. She was hungry.

“It’s snack time,” Buzz hummed.

Tilly opened her lunch box. Inside were bright foods: red strawberries, orange carrot sticks, green cucumber circles, and a little square of cheese. There was also a reusable cup of water with a blue turtle sticker.

Mia smiled. “That looks colorful, Tilly.”

“Thank you,” Tilly said softly. “I learned colors help. Red, orange, green. Many colors make a happy plate.”

Pip nodded. “That’s called eating a rainbow.”

Buzz looked around. “Where is the trash can?”

Tilly pointed to two bins. One said Recycle. The other said Compost. Next to them was a small bag for trash. “We take care of our world,” she whispered. “We put things in the right place.”

Mia took out a crinkly wrapper. She frowned. “Is this trash?”

Tilly thought. She took a breath and listened to the soft rustle of the wrapper. “Hmm. That goes in the trash bag,” she said kindly. “But your apple core can go in the compost.”

“Thank you for helping,” Mia said. “You are a good listener.”

Tilly felt warm inside. She was shy, but she could still help.

They each chose snacks. Pip picked a sweet strawberry. Buzz dipped his tiny feet on a cucumber circle and giggled. Mia crunched a carrot stick. Crunch, crunch.

Tilly looked at the cheese and paused. “Cheese is yummy,” she said. “But I also want more greens.” She put a cucumber circle on a cracker. “I will try a new taste.”

She took a bite. Crunch. Cool. Fresh. “Mmm,” she smiled. “That feels good in my belly.”

Pip asked, “How do we know when we have enough?”

Tilly put a hand on her shell. She closed her eyes and listened to her body. Her tummy felt calm, not too empty, not too full. “My tummy tells me,” she said. “It says, ‘Thank you, Tilly. That is enough.’”

Buzz hummed low. “Listening to your body is smart.”

Mia looked at Sprout, the plant. “Sprout looks thirsty.”

Tilly touched the soil. It felt dry like a cookie crumb. She filled a small watering can and poured slowly. Drip, drip. Sprout’s leaves wiggled with joy.

“We care for our plant,” Tilly said. “Plants give us air, and some plants give us food. We give them water and love.”

Pip pointed to the window. Sunlight made tiny dust sparkles. “The earth is our big home,” he said. “We take care of it together.”

Tilly nodded. She felt brave. “We can use our cups again,” she said, pointing to their reusable cups. “We can eat fruits and veggies. We can put scraps in compost. We can share and listen.”

Mia squeezed Tilly’s hand. “You are a good friend.”

Tilly’s shy smile grew. “You are my friends too. You accept me, even when I am quiet.”

Pip fluffed his feathers. “We like you just the way you are.”

They cleaned up. Apple cores went into the compost. The paper napkin, with a tiny crumb, went into recycle only if clean. It had a big smear, so Tilly shook her head. “This one goes to trash. We try our best.”

Buzz placed the reusable cups back on the shelf. “All ready for next time!”

They sat again in the study corner. It felt cozy and calm. Tilly felt strong in her shell and strong in her heart. She had listened to her body, chosen colorful foods, and helped her friends care for their big home.

Tilly whispered to Sprout, “Today I learned to eat a rainbow, to drink water, to listen to my tummy, and to be kind to the earth.”

Sprout’s leaves seemed to wave hello in the warm light.

Tilly looked at her friends. “Snack time made us smart time,” she said with a giggle.

Everyone laughed. It sounded like little bells.

The End.
