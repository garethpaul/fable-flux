# Koala Kira’s Tidy Time Plan

Kira was a calm koala with soft gray fur and gentle eyes. She lived in a cozy bedroom with a puffy blue rug, a tiny wooden clock that went tick-tock, and a warm lamp that glowed like sunrise. Kira loved to hug her pillow and sniff the sweet smell of eucalyptus tea by her bed.

One bright morning, Kira sat up and stretched her fluffy arms. “Good morning, clock,” she said softly. “Tick-tock, time to rock!” The clock ticked back, friendly and steady. Tick. Tock.

Kira had a big plan. She wanted to clean her room, read a story, and call her cousin Kai who lived far away, across the big ocean. “Today I will try my best,” Kira whispered. “I can do it, step by step.”

She picked up a little notebook with a rainbow on the cover. On the first page, she drew three boxes with a chunky blue crayon.

“Box one,” Kira said. “Tidy toys.” She drew a square with tiny blocks inside.

“Box two. Read a book.” She drew a book with a smile.

“Box three. Call Cousin Kai.” She drew a tiny phone with a heart.

Kira looked at the clock. “I will spend five songs on tidying, two songs on reading, and one song on calling,” she decided. She liked to use songs to count time. It helped her stay calm.

She pressed play. A cheerful tune filled the room—la la la. The music made the lamp feel warmer, and Kira’s paws felt tingly with energy.

“Time to tidy,” she said. She put stuffed animals on the bed. Plop, plop. She sorted blocks by color. Red in the red box. Blue in the blue box. “Hello, little blocks,” she hummed. “You go here. Thank you.”

But under the bed, Kira found a puzzle with a missing piece. Her ears drooped. “Oh no. Where is it?” She felt a tight knot in her belly. “I want to finish now!”

Kira took a slow breath. In and out. The tea smelled sweet and minty. The music kept singing. “It’s okay to feel mad,” she told herself. “I can try a calm plan.”

She made her body soft like a sleepy koala. She counted slow. “One, two, three.” Then she tried again. She checked her backpack. Rustle, rustle. She peeked behind the curtains. Swish. “Aha!” The puzzle piece was under her pillow, cool and smooth. “Found you.” Kira smiled and clicked it into place. Snap!

The first song ended. “One song done,” Kira said. “Keep going.” She gathered crayons. Tap-tap into the cup. She folded little socks—squish, squish—and put them in a drawer. The second and third songs went by. La la la. Kira kept her plan.

After the fifth song, Kira looked around. The room felt open and fresh. The rug was clear. The air smelled clean and cozy. She checked her notebook and put a green check in the first box. “Tidy toys—done!”

Now it was time to read. Kira picked a book about children around the world. On one page, a girl in a red dress waved hello. On another page, a boy in a warm hat built a snow friend. Kira listened to two soft songs while she read, tracing the pictures with her paw. “People live in many places,” she said. “We all sleep, eat, play, and learn. We can be kind to everyone.” Kira felt her heart grow big and warm.

“Last box,” she said. “Call Cousin Kai.” She looked at the clock. It was still daylight in her room, but she remembered that Kai lived far away. “When it is day here, it can be night there,” she said. “I want to respect Kai’s time.”

Kira tapped the world map on her wall. “Kai’s bedtime might be now,” she guessed. She checked the little clock and her calm plan. “I can send a voice message instead,” she decided. “That way I am kind and still on time.”

She pressed the phone button and spoke softly. “Hi, Kai. I cleaned my room with my five-song plan. I read about children around the world. I hope you are sleeping well. I will call when it is your daytime. I love you! Night-night.”

Kira put a green check in the third box. “Call—done, in a kind way,” she said. She felt proud and steady.

Then Kira noticed a tiny mess of crayons on the rug. “Oops,” she said. “I will fix it now.” She put them away without anyone telling her. Click, clack, into the cup. “I can rely on me,” she whispered. “I can take care of my space.”

Kira turned off the music. The room was quiet and snug. She sipped warm tea. Mmm. She hugged her pillow, which felt soft like clouds.

“What did I learn today?” Kira asked the clock.

Tick-tock answered, steady as a heartbeat.

“I learned that a plan helps me use my time,” Kira said. “Songs can guide me. When I feel upset, I can breathe and try again. I can be kind to others’ time, even far away. I can help myself and keep my room nice.”

Kira smiled, calm and bright. “Tick-tock, time well used,” she whispered.

The clock went tick. Tock. And Kira curled up in her cozy bed, happy and proud.

The End.
