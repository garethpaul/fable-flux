# Toby's Rooftop Problem

The warm morning sun shone down on the apartment building rooftop. Toby the tortoise slowly climbed the last step to the top. His shell gleamed green and brown in the bright light.

"Oh my!" Toby said, looking around with his wise old eyes. The rooftop was a mess! Toys were scattered everywhere. A red ball sat in a puddle. Building blocks lay upside down. A jump rope was tangled around a flower pot.

Three little rabbits sat in the corner, looking very sad. Their ears drooped down low.

"Hello there," Toby said gently. "I'm Toby. What seems to be the problem?"

The first rabbit, Rosie, sniffled. "We wanted to play up here, but everything is all mixed up! We don't know where to start."

The second rabbit, Benny, pointed with his paw. "The ball is too wet to bounce. The blocks won't stack because they're upside down. And our jump rope is all twisted!"

The third rabbit, Luna, sighed. "It's too hard. We give up."

Toby nodded thoughtfully. His wise eyes twinkled. "Hmm. This does look tricky. But you know what? Every problem has a solution if we think it through step by step."

"Really?" asked Rosie, perking up her ears.

"Really," Toby smiled. "Let's use our thinking caps. What should we do first?"

Benny scratched his head. "Um... clean up everything at once?"

"Good try," Toby said kindly. "But that might feel too big. What if we picked just one thing to fix first?"

Luna looked around. "The ball in the puddle?"

"Excellent thinking!" Toby said. "Why is that a good choice?"

"Because... because the water will make everything else wet too?" Rosie said slowly.

"Exactly right! You're thinking like a problem-solver now."

The three rabbits hopped over to the puddle. Working together, they carefully lifted the red ball out. Benny found an old towel and dried it off.

"It's clean and bouncy again!" Luna laughed, giving it a gentle test bounce.

"What's next?" asked Toby.

The rabbits looked around more carefully now. Their ears stood up straight as they thought.

"The blocks!" said Benny. "If we turn them right-side up, we can build something!"

"And I can untangle the jump rope while you do that," added Rosie. "My small paws are good at working with knots."

Toby beamed with pride. "Now you're thinking like a team! Each problem becomes easier when we break it down and work together."

The rabbits got busy. Benny and Luna carefully flipped each block and started building a colorful tower. Rosie patiently worked on the tangled rope, loosening one loop at a time.

"This is actually fun!" Luna giggled as their tower grew taller.

"And look!" Rosie held up the jump rope. "All untangled!"

Soon the rooftop was neat and organized. The rabbits had even made it better than before. They'd built a wonderful block castle and arranged the other toys in a special play area.

"You did it!" Toby said proudly. "You used logical thinking to solve each problem step by step."

"And we helped each other too," Benny added. "It was easier when we worked as a team."

"That's right," Toby nodded. "When we think clearly and cooperate, we can solve any problem. Remember: look at the whole situation, choose one thing to fix first, and work together."

The three rabbits spent the rest of the morning playing happily on their clean rooftop. They bounced the ball, jumped rope, and played with their blocks.

As Toby slowly made his way back down the stairs, he could hear their joyful laughter echoing across the rooftop. His wise old heart felt warm and happy.

"Another problem solved with good thinking and teamwork," he said to himself with a satisfied smile.

The End.
