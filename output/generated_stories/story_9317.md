# Tilly Turtle Takes Her Time

Tilly was a shy little turtle who lived with her family. One sunny morning, Tilly peeked into the bright, bubbly bathroom. It smelled like sweet soap and minty toothpaste. The mirror was shiny. The sink dripped, drip… drip… drip. The soft bath mat felt squishy under Tilly’s tiny feet.

Tilly needed to brush her teeth and wash her face. But Tilly was shy in the busy bathroom. Her big brother, Tim, liked to hurry-hurry. Her little cousin, Dot, liked to splash-splash. Tilly liked quiet. She liked to take her time.

“Can I have a turn?” Tilly asked in a small voice.

Tim zoomed in with a whoosh. “I’ll be fast!” he said, grabbing the toothbrush. He squeezed the toothpaste. Squish! A big blob fell on the counter.

Dot giggled. “Bubbles! I like bubbles!” She tapped the soap pump. Squirt! Bubbly foam puffed out and tickled her nose.

Tilly blinked. She felt very small. “I can wait,” she whispered, and tucked her head a little into her shell.

Mama Turtle peeked in. “Good morning, my sweet turtles,” she said in a warm, soft voice. “I see we have a bathroom bustle! Remember, we share. We take turns. We use patience.”

Tim looked at Dot. Dot looked at Tilly. The sink went drip… drip… drip.

“It is fair to take turns,” Mama said. “And we can practice waiting with kind hearts.”

Tilly took a deep breath. In… out. She smelled the minty paste again. She felt the warm light on her shell. “I can be patient,” she said. Her voice was a little bigger now.

Tim nodded. “I can be fair,” he said. He wiped up the extra toothpaste with a towel. “Oops. I’ll fix my mess.”

Dot hopped on the bath mat. “I can practice gentle hands,” she said. She pressed the soap pump slowly. One soft squirt. “I did it!”

Mama smiled. “Let’s make a plan,” she said. “A turn for Tim, a turn for Tilly, and a turn for Dot. We will count together. Ten slow turtle counts for each turn.”

Tilly liked the plan. Plans felt safe. Plans felt fair.

Tim brushed his teeth. Swish, swish. Scrub, scrub. The minty smell danced in the air. Mama and Tilly and Dot counted: “One… two… three…” in slow, calm voices. Tilly watched the bubbles slide down the sink like tiny stars. She felt her toes press the soft mat. Her tummy felt fluttery, but she kept breathing. “Four… five… six…”

When they reached “ten,” Tim put down the brush. “All done!” he said. He smiled at Tilly. “Your turn.”

Tilly stepped up to the sink. The faucet made a gentle hiss. She put a pea-sized dot of toothpaste on her little brush. Plip. She looked in the mirror. A shy turtle looked back. Her eyes were kind. “I can do this,” Tilly told herself.

She brushed in little circles. “Round and round,” she sang softly. “Front teeth, back teeth.” The foam was fizzy and cool. “Minty!” she giggled. Mama and Tim and Dot counted for her. Their voices were slow and steady: “One… two… three…”

Dot peered up. “You’re doing great,” she said. Her eyes were bright.

Tilly brushed all the places. She spat into the sink. Patooey! She rinsed her brush. Swish. Her teeth felt smooth like little stones by a pond.

On “ten,” Tilly stepped back. “All done,” she said. Her shy voice sounded proud.

Dot’s turn came next. She washed her hands. Warm water splashed. Pitter-patter. She rubbed her fingers and thumbs. “Bubbles on my hands,” she giggled. Tilly counted with care. “One… two… three…” She helped Dot rub the tops and the palms. She showed her how to rinse. “All clean!”

When everyone was done, the bathroom was quiet again. The counter was wiped. The towels were straight. The mirror still shone. The air smelled fresh and minty.

Mama Turtle hugged them all. “You did it,” she said. “You used patience. You were fair. You practiced taking turns. You helped each other.”

Tilly felt warm in her shell. She stood a little taller. “Waiting was hard,” she said, “but I used my slow turtle counts.” She tapped her shell gently. “Slow and steady helps me.”

Tim nodded. “I can slow down too,” he said. “I can clean up my mess.”

Dot clapped. “I can use gentle pumps and tiny toothpaste dots!” she chirped.

Tilly looked at the sink. She thought of other busy times: putting on shoes, washing hands before lunch, waiting for toys. “Maybe we can make a turn chart,” she said softly. “A fair-list for the bathroom.”

Mama’s eyes sparkled. “What a helpful idea! We can practice every day.”

They made a simple chart with stickers. A star for waiting. A heart for helping. A smile for cleaning up. Tilly placed a small star by her name. Her shy heart felt brave.

That night, Tilly went back to the bathroom. It was calm. The light was warm. The mat was soft. Drip… drip… drip… The sounds were friendly now. She took her time brushing and washing. She remembered to breathe in and out. She smiled at the shy turtle in the mirror.

“I can be patient,” she whispered. “I can be fair. I can practice. I can help.”

And the bathroom felt like a happy, peaceful place.

The End.
