# Koala Kiki’s Quiet Garden Breath

Koala Kiki lived on a sunny balcony with a tiny garden. The garden had soft green mint, curly parsley, and bright yellow flowers that smelled like warm honey. The wind liked to visit and say, “Whooo,” and the leaves would whisper back, “Shhh.”

Each morning, Kiki held her warm mug of chamomile tea. She felt the mug with both paws. “Warm,” she said. She took a slow breath in and a slow breath out. “Good morning, garden.”

Today was a big day. Kiki wanted to plant new seeds. She had a small blue shovel, a little watering can, and a packet of cherry tomato seeds. She also had her “Calm Plan,” a tiny card she made with drawings: a sun for “breathe,” a water drop for “sip,” a heart for “kind words,” and a leaf for “try again.”

“Hello, seeds,” Kiki said kindly. “We will grow together.” The seeds did not talk, but Kiki imagined they smiled.

Kiki opened the seed packet. Oh! A breeze puffed by. “Whooo!” The packet flipped. Tiny seeds sprinkled across the balcony like freckles. Tap, tap, tap!

Kiki’s heart went thump-thump. “Oh no,” she whispered. Her ears felt hot. Her tummy felt tight.

She looked at her Calm Plan. “First, breathe,” she said. Kiki put one paw on her belly and one paw on her chest.

“In for three,” she whispered. “One, two, three.” Her belly rose like a soft balloon. “Out for three. One, two, three.” The tight feeling loosened. The wind slowed. The leaves whispered, “Shhh.”

“Second, sip,” Kiki said. She took a small sip of water from her little cup. The cool water felt like a gentle river in her mouth.

“Third, kind words,” Kiki told herself. “It’s okay, Kiki. Seeds are small. You are gentle. You can try again.”

Kiki knelt down. “Hello, little seeds,” she said. She used her blue shovel and her soft paws to gather the scattered seeds into a tiny bowl. A ladybug landed on the rim. “Click,” went its tiny feet. “Thank you for visiting,” Kiki whispered. The ladybug opened its red wings and rested.

Kiki checked the soil in her garden box. She poked it with a finger. “Dry,” she said. “We can fix that.” She poured a little water. “Drip, drip, drip.” The soil turned dark and smelled like rain.

Kiki made small homes for the seeds—little holes in neat rows. “You have your own cozy bed,” she said. She dropped one seed in each hole. “Plip, plip.” She covered them with a blanket of soil. “Tuck, tuck.”

“Fourth, try again,” Kiki smiled. She patted the soil. “Soft and safe.”

Just then, clouds gathered. The sky turned a sleepy gray. The wind said, “Whooo-ooo,” a little louder. A fat raindrop fell on Kiki’s nose. “Plop!” She giggled. “Cool!”

But then the rain began to patter harder. “Pitter-patter, pitter-patter.” Kiki felt worry tickle again. “Will my seeds wash away?” she wondered.

She took two more calm breaths. “In… one, two, three. Out… one, two, three.” Her shoulders lowered. “I can prepare,” she said.

Kiki looked around and found two small trays and a clear cover. She made tiny umbrellas by propping the clear cover over the garden box. She tucked the trays at the edges to stop the water from splashing. “This is how I care,” Kiki said. “I prepare, and I am gentle.”

A neighbor pigeon named Pip hopped over, shaking rain from his feathers. “Coo?” he asked.

“Hi, Pip,” said Kiki. “I’m keeping my seeds safe.”

“Coo-coo,” Pip said, tilting his head.

“Would you like some water?” Kiki asked. She set a shallow dish on the balcony. Pip sipped. “Coo!” Pip fluffed, happy.

Kiki sat on her soft mat and listened. The rain made a cozy song. “Pitter-patter, tap-tap, shhh.” The mint leaves smelled extra fresh, like tiny green candy. The cool air brushed Kiki’s fur. She closed her eyes for a moment. “I am safe,” she whispered. “My seeds are safe. Pip is happy. The garden rests.”

After a while, the rain slowed to sprinkles. A thin sun peeked out. The clear cover sparkled with tiny drops like diamonds. Kiki lifted it and checked the soil. “Hello, seeds,” she said. “You stayed snug.”

Pip cooed. A small worm wiggled up to say hello. “Hello, helper,” Kiki smiled. “Thank you for caring for the soil.”

Kiki washed her paws, dried them on a towel, and stretched her arms wide. “I did it,” she said. “I felt big feelings. I used my Calm Plan. I was kind to myself, kind to my seeds, and kind to my friend.”

She drew a new picture on her Calm Plan—a little umbrella for “prepare.” She taped the card to the balcony door where she could see it every day.

That night, Kiki watered just a little. “Sip, not splash,” she sang. She hummed a soft tune, “Hmm-hmm,” and the leaves hummed back, “Shhh-shhh.”

“Good night, garden,” Kiki whispered. “Grow when you’re ready.”

Kiki took one last deep breath. In… one, two, three. Out… one, two, three. She felt peaceful and proud.

Lesson: When things go wrong, stop and breathe. Be kind to yourself. Take small steps. Prepare and try again. Calm helps us care.

The End.
