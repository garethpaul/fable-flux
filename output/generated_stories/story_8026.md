# Polly's Dancing Dinner

Polly the parrot loved to dance. Her bright green feathers would shimmer and shake as she moved to music. But there was one big problem - Polly was too shy to dance in front of others.

Every evening, the Rodriguez family gathered in their cozy dining room for dinner. The wooden table gleamed under the warm yellow light, and the smell of Mama's delicious rice and beans filled the air. Polly perched quietly on her special stand in the corner, watching the family eat and talk together.

"Polly, why don't you come join us?" asked little Sofia, the youngest daughter. "You always sit so far away."

Polly ruffled her feathers nervously. "Squawk! I'm fine here, thank you," she chirped softly.

But Polly wasn't really fine. She wanted to be part of the family dinner, but she was scared they might laugh at her dancing.

One evening, Grandpa Carlos visited for dinner. He brought his old guitar and began playing a beautiful Spanish song. The music floated through the dining room like magic.

"This is a traditional song from my childhood," Grandpa said with a smile. "My grandmother taught it to me when I was very young."

Polly's feet began to tap on her perch. The music was so lovely! Without thinking, she started to sway back and forth. Her head bobbed to the rhythm, and her colorful tail feathers swished.

"Look!" whispered Sofia. "Polly is dancing!"

Suddenly, Polly realized everyone was watching her. She stopped moving and tucked her head under her wing, feeling embarrassed.

"Oh no," she thought. "They saw me dancing. What if they think I'm silly?"

But then something wonderful happened. Instead of laughing, the whole family started clapping!

"That was beautiful, Polly!" said Mama. "You dance just like my grandmother used to!"

"Really?" Polly peeked out from under her wing.

"Sí, really!" said Grandpa Carlos. "Dancing is a way to share joy and tradition. Would you teach us your dance?"

Polly felt a warm, happy feeling in her chest. "You... you want to learn from me?"

"Of course!" said Papa. "Every family member has something special to share."

Slowly, Polly flew down to the dining table. The smooth wood felt cool under her feet. She took a deep breath and began to dance again. This time, she felt proud instead of scared.

She showed them how to move their shoulders like her wings. Sofia giggled as she tried to bob her head like Polly. Even baby Miguel clapped his hands to the beat.

"Now let me share something with you," said Grandpa. He played a different song and showed Polly some traditional steps his family had danced for generations.

"We can combine our dances!" suggested Polly excitedly. "Your family traditions and my creative moves!"

And that's exactly what they did. Every evening after dinner, the dining room became a dance floor. Polly taught her special wing-flapping moves, while the family shared their traditional steps. They created beautiful new dances together.

Polly learned that being different made her special, not strange. Her creative dancing brought joy to the whole family, and she discovered that sharing her talents made her feel confident and happy.

From that day on, Polly never felt shy about dancing. She knew that when you share your gifts with love and respect, wonderful things can happen.

The End.
