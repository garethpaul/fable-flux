# Benny the Beaver's Big Art Day

Benny the Beaver loved to build things with his strong teeth and busy paws. He could chop wood *tap-tap-tap* and stack logs *thump-thump-thump* faster than anyone in the forest. But today was different. Today, Benny was in the bright, colorful art room at his school.

"Welcome, everyone!" said Ms. Robin, their cheerful teacher. "Today we're making special art projects for the school sports day!"

Benny looked around the room. There were paintbrushes of every size, crayons that smelled like berries, and paper that felt smooth under his furry paws. But Benny felt worried. His tummy did little flips.

"I'm good at building dams," Benny whispered to himself, "but I don't know how to make art."

Next to him sat Luna the Rabbit, who was already sketching beautiful flowers. On his other side, Carlos the Squirrel was mixing bright red and yellow paint that looked like sunshine.

"What are you making, Benny?" asked Luna sweetly.

Benny's ears drooped. "I... I don't know. I'm not good at art like you are."

Ms. Robin heard Benny's sad voice and waddled over. "Benny, what do you love to do most?"

"I love building things," Benny said quietly. "And I love playing soccer with my friends."

"Those are wonderful things!" Ms. Robin chirped. "Can you think of a way to put building and soccer into your art?"

Benny's brown eyes lit up. Maybe he could try something different! He picked up some brown and green crayons. Slowly, he began to draw. First, he made a big circle for a soccer ball. Then he drew sticks that looked like the logs he loved to stack.

"Look!" said Carlos excitedly. "Benny's making a soccer goal out of logs!"

As Benny drew, he felt better and better. His paws moved more quickly. He added grass that felt bumpy under his crayon, and a bright yellow sun that looked warm and happy.

"This is taking too long," Benny worried, watching other friends finish their projects.

"Take your time, Benny," said Ms. Robin gently. "Good art, just like good building, needs patience."

Benny took a deep breath that smelled like crayons and paper. He remembered how he felt when he built his first dam - proud and happy. He could feel that same feeling growing in his chest now.

He added little stick figures playing soccer near his log goal. He drew himself as one of the players, with a big smile and strong beaver tail.

"Wow, Benny!" said Luna. "Your picture shows how you love sports AND building!"

When art time was over, Ms. Robin hung everyone's artwork on the wall. Benny stepped back and looked at his picture. It wasn't like anyone else's, but it was perfectly him.

"I did it!" Benny said, his tail slapping the floor with joy. "I made art that shows who I am!"

"You sure did," said Ms. Robin proudly. "You used your special talents to make something beautiful and unique."

That day, Benny learned something important. He didn't have to be good at everything the same way as others. When he used what made him special - his love of building and sports - he could create wonderful things in his own way.

From that day on, whenever Benny felt worried about trying something new, he remembered his special art day and smiled.

The End.
