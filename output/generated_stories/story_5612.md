# Bella Bird and the Bedroom Song

In a cozy bedroom with soft yellow curtains, a little singing bird named Bella hopped on the windowsill. The room smelled like clean soap and warm blankets. Sunlight made happy squares on the rug. Bella puffed her feathers and sang, “Tweet-a-dee, tweet-a-doo! I have big things I must do!”

Bella’s best friend, Max, was a child with curly hair and bright eyes. Max sat on the floor with blocks. The blocks made clack, clack sounds. Max smiled at Bella. “Hi, Bella! Will you sing while I play?”

Bella fluttered down to the bedpost. “I love to sing, Max,” she chirped. “But I have a list today.” She pointed with her tiny wing to a picture chart on the wall. The chart had simple drawings: a sun for “Make the bed,” a shirt for “Put away clothes,” a book for “Clean up books,” and a heart for “Help others.”

Max clapped. “A list! I like lists.”

Bella nodded. “I must finish my tasks. When I finish, my song will be strong and bright.” She hummed a little warm-up tune. “But first, I need to follow the steps.”

Just then, Mama walked in with a gentle smile. She smelled like oatmeal cookies. “Good morning, helpers. First, make the bed,” she said kindly. “Then, put clothes in drawers. After that, clean up books. Last, we will do a small act of kindness for our neighbors. That’s community service. We help our big world, one small thing at a time.”

Bella’s feathers tingled with a little worry. “So many steps,” she whispered. “What if I forget? What if I sing too much and don’t finish?”

Max saw Bella’s worried eyes. “It’s okay to feel worried,” Max said softly. “Let’s do one step at a time.”

Bella took a deep breath. She listened to the sound of the clock: tick, tock, tick, tock. She felt the soft bedspread under her feet. “Step one: make the bed,” she chirped.

“Pull, smooth, tuck,” Mama said. “Follow the directions.”

Bella and Max pulled the blanket. It swished like a small wave. They smoothed the sheet. It felt cool and flat. They tucked the corners. “Pull, smooth, tuck!” Bella sang. “We can do this, we won’t get stuck!” Her heart felt calm. She smiled.

“Check!” said Max, placing a sticker on the sun picture.

“Step two: put away clothes,” Bella chirped.

The clean clothes were warm from the dryer. They smelled like flowers. Max folded a tiny shirt. Bella held a sock with her beak. “Sock in the drawer,” she sang. “Shirt in the closet.” She followed the picture chart and Mama’s gentle voice. “Clothes away, hooray today!”

“Check!” Max said, adding a sticker to the shirt picture.

“Step three: clean up books,” Bella chirped.

Books lay all over the rug. One had a picture of a big blue whale. Another had a map of the world with bright colors. Bella touched the map with her wing. “Many people live all around the world,” she sang softly. “We are all neighbors on Earth.”

Max stacked the books. Tap, tap, tap. The stack felt heavy in his hands. Bella heard the soft slide of covers as they fit on the shelf. “Put books back, spine out,” Mama said. “Follow the directions.” Bella copied, “Spine out!” and made sure the pictures faced front. The shelf looked neat and happy.

“Check!” Max cheered, adding a sticker to the book picture.

Bella stood tall. “We did three steps!” She felt proud. But her tummy fluttered. “Step four is big,” she whispered. “Help others.”

Mama nodded. “Yes. Today, we can make a kindness bag for a neighbor. We’ll add a note, a snack, and a tiny book. We can sing them a song. That’s community service. We help our community and our big world by caring.”

Bella felt brave now. “Let’s do it,” she chirped.

They found a small bag with stars. Max drew a smiley face note: “We care.” Bella picked a tiny book about colors. She listened to the crinkle of the snack wrapper as Mama packed it. The bag rustled like leaves.

“Before we go,” Mama said, “let’s review the directions: Walk to the door. Carry the bag gently. Knock softly. Say hello and share.”

Bella sang the steps like a gentle drumbeat. “Walk, carry, knock, share.” Her voice was warm and bright.

They walked down the hall. Step, step. The floor felt smooth and cool. At the neighbor’s door, Bella’s heart did a little dance. She felt shy. Max squeezed her wing. “It’s okay to feel shy,” he whispered. “We’re doing a kind thing.”

Knock, knock.

The neighbor, Mr. Kim, opened the door. He wore a cozy sweater and smelled like tea. “Oh! Hello, friends,” he said with a soft smile.

Bella held up the bag. “We made this for you,” she sang. “We finished our tasks, and now we share.”

Mr. Kim’s eyes sparkled. “Thank you. Your kindness helps our whole community. We all live together in this big world.” He listened as Bella sang a short song: “We do our tasks, we help and care, we follow steps and love to share.”

They walked back to the cozy bedroom. The bed was neat. The clothes were away. The books were tidy. The room felt peaceful, like a hug.

Bella looked at the chart. All the stickers shone. Her chest felt warm and proud. “I did it,” she whispered. “I followed directions. I finished my tasks. I helped my neighbor.”

Mama hugged them both. “Tasks done make time for fun,” she said.

Bella fluttered to the windowsill and sang a bright, happy song that filled the room with light. Max danced in little steps, tap-tap on the rug.

Bella smiled. “When we finish our tasks, we feel calm. We help our home. We help our neighbors. We help our world.”

Max nodded. “One step at a time,” he said. “We can do it.”

The End.
