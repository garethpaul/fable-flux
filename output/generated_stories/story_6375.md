# Joey's Big Boy Breakfast

Little Joey the kangaroo woke up with a big stretch and an even bigger yawn. The morning sun streamed through the dining room windows, making golden patches dance on the wooden table.

"Good morning, sunshine!" called Mama Kangaroo from the kitchen. "Are you ready for breakfast?"

Joey bounced into the dining room, his fluffy tail wiggling with excitement. But when he saw his usual high chair, he stopped hopping. The chair looked so... little.

"Mama," Joey said, puffing out his chest, "I'm a big joey now! Can I sit in a big chair like you?"

Mama Kangaroo smiled warmly. "Of course, my little love. But sitting in a big chair means doing big joey things. Are you ready for that?"

Joey nodded so hard his ears flopped. "Yes, yes, yes!"

Mama helped Joey climb onto the tall dining room chair. His little paws could barely reach the table, but he felt so proud and tall! The wooden chair felt smooth and cool under his fur.

"Now," said Mama, setting down a colorful plate, "big joeys make healthy choices for their bodies. What do you see here?"

Joey's nose twitched as he smelled the delicious breakfast. There were orange carrot sticks that crunched between his teeth, sweet purple grapes that popped with juice, golden scrambled eggs that were fluffy and warm, and whole grain toast cut into fun triangle shapes.

"I see rainbow food!" Joey giggled. "Orange, purple, yellow, and brown!"

"That's right!" Mama clapped her paws together. "Rainbow foods give our bodies rainbow power. The orange carrots help your eyes see better. The purple grapes give you energy to hop and play. The yellow eggs make your muscles strong, and the brown toast helps your tummy feel happy."

Joey picked up his small fork carefully. At first, it felt wobbly in his paw, but he remembered what Mama always said: "Practice makes progress!"

"Look, Mama! I'm feeding myself!" Joey said proudly as he scooped up some eggs. A few pieces fell on his bib, but he kept trying.

"You're doing wonderfully," Mama encouraged. "Big joeys also listen to their tummies. Eat slowly and think about how the food tastes and feels."

Joey chewed thoughtfully. The eggs tasted creamy and warm. The grapes burst with sweet juice that made him smile. The carrots made a satisfying crunch that echoed in the quiet dining room. The toast was nutty and filled his tummy with cozy warmth.

"My tummy is telling me it's getting full," Joey announced, putting down his fork.

"What a smart big joey you are!" Mama said, giving him a gentle hug. "You listened to your body, made healthy choices, and fed yourself. That's what wellness means – taking good care of yourself."

Joey beamed with pride. His whiskers twitched with happiness as he helped Mama clear his plate, carrying his cup carefully to the kitchen sink.

"Tomorrow, can I sit in the big chair again?" Joey asked hopefully.

"Every day, my wonderful joey. Because every day, you're growing bigger, stronger, and wiser."

That night, as Joey snuggled into bed, his tummy felt perfectly happy and full. He had learned that being a big joey meant making good choices, listening to his body, and taking care of himself. And that felt even better than sitting in a big chair!

The End.
