# Pippa Parrot’s Rooftop Rainbow

On a sunny morning, a creative parrot named Pippa hopped onto the warm rooftop. The tiles felt smooth under her feet. The sky was a big blue blanket, and a soft breeze tickled her green and gold feathers.

“Today I will paint a rainbow!” Pippa chirped. She loved to make art. She brought a small basket with paints, a tiny brush, a water cup, a cloth, and a crunchy seed snack for later.

Pippa dipped her brush into red. “Swoosh!” She made a bright red stripe on the big white rooftop board. She smiled. “Red like strawberries!” She dipped into orange and painted another stripe. “Orange like sweet oranges!” The colors shined in the sun.

But when Pippa tried to paint yellow, the wind said, “Whoooosh!” The brush wobbled and scratched. The yellow bumped into the orange and made a messy smear.

“Oh no,” Pippa squawked. “It’s ruined!” Her chest felt tight. Her feathers fluffed up. Her wings shook. Pippa frowned and stamped her little feet. “I can’t do it. I can’t!”

Just then, a gentle pigeon named Pip shuffled over from the chimney. “Good morning, Pippa,” said Pip softly. “I see bright colors!”

Pippa huffed. “It was a rainbow. Now it’s a mess.”

Pip tilted his head. “I hear a frustrated parrot.”

“I’m not just frustrated,” Pippa said. “I’m mad, and I’m sad, and I want to quit!”

The wind blew again, cooler this time. Pippa shivered. Her tummy rumbled a little. Her mouth felt dry.

Pip nodded. “Big feelings are okay. Let’s care for you first. Want a sip of water?”

Pippa blinked. “Yes.” She took a tiny sip from her cup. The water was cool and kind. She took a breath in, then out.

“Again?” Pip asked.

Pippa breathed in slowly and breathed out. She felt her shoulders drop. Her wings settled by her sides. The sun warmed her back.

Pip smiled. “When I feel frustrated, I do three things. I stop. I sip. I stretch.”

“Stop, sip, stretch,” Pippa repeated. She tried stretching her wings wide—once, twice. “That feels nice.”

“Would you like your snack?” Pip asked.

Pippa pecked a crunchy seed snack. Crunch, crunch. “My tummy feels better,” she said.

They both listened. Far below, a bus hummed. Bees buzzed by a flower pot. The rooftop smelled like sun and dust and a little bit like paint.

“I still want a rainbow,” Pippa whispered. “But I’m scared it will go wrong.”

Pip nodded. “Let’s prepare. We can make a plan.”

“A plan?” Pippa cocked her head.

“First,” said Pip, “we can tape the board so colors stay in lines.” He picked up some sticky tape from Pippa’s basket.

“Second,” said Pippa, “we can put a small rock in the water cup so it won’t tip.” She found a little pebble near a pot and plopped it in. Plink!

“Third,” said Pip, “we can wait for a calm breeze. We can watch the leaves on that little rooftop plant. When they are still, we paint.”

Pippa nodded. “And if I feel frustrated, I stop, sip, and stretch.”

“You’ve got it,” Pip cooed.

They waited. The breeze moved the leaves—swish, swish. Then the leaves grew quiet. The wind was calm.

Pippa dipped her brush into yellow. “Hello, yellow,” she said softly. “You are sunshine.” With slow strokes, she painted a bright yellow stripe. “Swoosh.” It stayed in place.

Pippa’s eyes sparkled. “It’s working!”

Next came green. “Green like the rooftop vines,” she said. She painted smooth lines, then blue like the big sky, and purple like sweet grapes.

When she reached purple, the wind puffed again. “Whoo—” It nudged her wing. The brush made a little wiggle mark.

Pippa felt that tight feeling sneak back. Her tummy squeezed. “Oh—” She stopped. She put down the brush. “Stop, sip, stretch,” she said to herself. She took a small sip. She stretched her wings. She breathed in and out.

The tight feeling grew smaller. Pippa looked at the wiggle mark. “It’s okay,” she said. “I can make this wiggle into a pretty swirl.”

She dipped the brush in purple and gently curved the wiggle into a tiny swirl cloud at the edge of the rainbow. “Ta-da!”

Pip clapped his wings. “Creative and calm,” he said.

Pippa giggled. “Thank you for helping me care for me.”

They stepped back. The rainbow shined in the sunlight—red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple—with a playful purple swirl. It looked happy and bright.

Pippa touched the dry cloth to a small drip. Dab, dab. “Preparation helps,” she said. “Tape, pebble, and waiting for calm.”

“And compassion,” Pip added. “You were kind to yourself.”

Pippa nodded. “I had big feelings, and I did not quit. I stopped, sipped, and stretched. I made a plan. I finished my rainbow.”

They shared the last seeds and watched the colors glow. The breeze felt gentle, like a soft hug. A tiny cloud floated by, and a bee buzzed hello.

Pippa took a final breath and fluffed her feathers in the warm sun. “When I feel frustrated again,” she said, “I will remember: stop, sip, stretch, and make a plan.”

“And I will remind you,” Pip said kindly.

Pippa smiled. “Thank you. Today was a good rooftop day.”

“It was,” Pip agreed. “A colorful one.”

The big blue sky slowly turned to soft gold as the sun began to go down. The rooftop felt cozy. Pippa tucked her brush away and closed her basket.

“Good night, rainbow,” Pippa whispered.

The rainbow seemed to smile back.

The End.
