# Dippy’s Sudsy Sports Day

Dippy was a playful dolphin with a bright smile and a shiny gray back. Today, he wasn’t in the big blue sea. He was visiting his friend Mira’s house. Splash! Dippy slid in through a wide, safe tub in the laundry room where Mira’s family washed clothes. The room was warm and smelled like clean soap and sunny lemons. The washing machine hummed, “Whirr, whirr,” and the dryer rumbled softly, “Rrr-rrr.”

“Hi, Dippy!” Mira said, waving. She wore a tiny shirt with a ball on it. “Mama is washing our team shirts. We have a fun sports day at the park!”

Dippy’s eyes sparkled. “Sports day? I love to play! Can I help?”

Mira’s mama smiled. “Yes, please. We have many shirts from many teams. We must sort them and keep them safe.”

Dippy looked at the baskets. There were red shirts, blue shirts, green shirts, and yellow scarves. There were tiny socks and big socks. Some shirts had swirls. Some had stars. Some had words in different languages.

Mira pointed. “This shirt says ‘Hola’ in Spanish. This one says ‘Jambo’ in Swahili. This scarf is from Tita in the Philippines. We cheer in many ways!”

Dippy clapped his fins. “Wow! So many cheers! I want to learn them all.”

Mira grinned. “We can learn while we help.”

Just then, a tiny sock slipped off a basket and fell—plop—into a puddle. “Oh no,” said Mira. “It will be dirty again.”

“I can help!” Dippy said. He used his gentle nose to nudge a towel over the puddle. “Slurp,” went the towel as it drank up the water. Dippy lifted the sock with care. “Here you go, little sock.”

“Thank you,” said Mira. “You are a good helper.”

Dippy felt warm and proud. Helping felt nice. The washing machine beeped. “Beep-beep!” The door clicked open, and warm steam floated out. It smelled like fresh rain and sweet flowers.

Mira’s mama said, “Time to switch the clothes.”

Dippy peeked in. “It looks like bubbly waves!” he giggled. He and Mira worked together. Dippy passed each shirt to Mira. Mira shook them gently. “Fluff, fluff!” Then she put them in the dryer. The dryer went, “Rrr-rrr spin!”

While they waited, Mira brought out soft balls from a basket. “Let’s practice for sports day,” she said. “We can play gentle games in the laundry room.”

“Yay!” said Dippy, flapping his fins. “What game?”

“Target toss,” said Mira. She put empty laundry baskets on the floor. “We toss the soft balls into the baskets. We cheer for everyone, no matter what.”

Dippy nodded. “I will try, and I will help.”

Mira aimed. “One, two, three!” Plop! The ball landed in the closest basket.

“My turn,” said Dippy. He tossed with his fin. The ball bonked the rim and rolled under a chair. “Oops!”

“It’s okay,” said Mira. “We can improve. Try again.”

Dippy smiled. “I will not give up.” He took a deep breath. “Whoosh.” He tried again—soft and steady. Plop! The ball landed in the basket.

“We did it!” said Mira. “Now let’s cheer in many ways.” She clapped and said, “Bravo!” Dippy tried, “Jambo!” They both giggled and said, “Hola!” Then they bowed and said, “Arigatou,” like Mira’s friend Kenji does after games, to show thanks.

The dryer beeped again. “Beep-beep!” The shirts were warm and cozy. But oh dear—one green shirt was wrinkly and squished.

Mira frowned. “We need it to look nice.”

Dippy looked around. “We can help it.” He remembered the soft steam. “Steam can smooth, like water on waves.”

Mira’s mama brought a spritz bottle. “A little water, a gentle shake, and a pat can help.” Dippy spritzed. “Pss-pss.” Mira shook. “Fluff, fluff.” Mama patted. “Pat-pat.” The wrinkle smoothed out like a calm sea.

“Teamwork!” Dippy cheered.

Just then, a tiny meow came from a laundry basket. “Mrrrp?” It was Noodle the cat, curled on a scarf.

“Oh, Noodle,” said Mira. “That scarf is for Auntie Lila’s dance. It’s special.”

Dippy swam—swoosh—through the air like a soft dolphin hug and nudged the basket. “Excuse me, Noodle. May we have the scarf?” He offered a folded towel. “Here is a cozy bed.”

Noodle sniffed the towel and purred. “Prrr.” She stepped off the scarf and curled on the towel instead.

“Thank you, Noodle,” said Mira. “And thank you, Dippy. You helped with kind words.”

Mira’s mama folded the last shirt. “We are ready for sports day! We will run and toss and dance. We will cheer in many languages. We will help each other.”

Dippy wiggled with joy. “I learned today. Helping makes work light. Helping makes hearts bright.”

Mira nodded. “We practiced sports. We improved with each try. We showed care for clothes and for Noodle. We learned new cheers from different places.”

They stacked the clean shirts neatly. They packed the balls and the scarf. The laundry room felt calm and clean. It smelled like lemons and warm cotton. The machines were quiet now.

Dippy touched fins with Mira. “Let’s always help.”

“Always,” said Mira. “At home, at play, and with friends from everywhere.”

The End.
