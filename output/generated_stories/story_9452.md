# Pippa the Mindful Parrot

Pippa was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a splash of blue on her tail. She loved her cozy study corner. It had a little table, soft pillows, a cup of crayons, and a neat row of picture books. The window let in warm sunshine. The air smelled like oranges from a bowl of fresh fruit.

“Good morning, study corner!” Pippa chirped. “I’m ready to make a sunny poster!”

She fluttered down to her table and spread out her paper. She wanted to draw a big, smiling sun. But as she picked up a yellow crayon, her tummy made a long, low rumble.

Rrrr-rrrumble.

“Oh!” Pippa giggled. “Tummy, are you singing?” She set the crayon down. “I want to draw, but I also want a snack.”

On the shelf, she saw a crunchy snack bag. Near the window, she saw the orange bowl filled with shiny carrots, apples, and little green peas in a cup.

Pippa blinked and felt a little swirl inside. “I can’t choose. I feel jumpy in my feathers.”

Her wings flapped fast. She grabbed a crayon. It slipped. The crayon bumped the cup, and peas rolled everywhere like tiny green beads. Tap tap tap went the peas on the floor. Pippa’s eyes grew wide. “Oh no!”

Her heart beat quick. “I made a mess. I want to hurry and fix it. I want to hurry and draw. I want to hurry and eat.” Everything felt too fast.

Pippa remembered something her friend Mia taught her. “When it feels too fast,” Mia had said, “use your mindful pause. Stop. Breathe. Notice.”

Pippa put a wing on her belly. “I will try,” she whispered. “Mindful pause.”

She closed her eyes. She took a slow breath in. In goes the air, cool and soft. She let a slow breath out. Out goes the air, warm and gentle. She listened. The room was quiet. Sunlight made a golden square on her table. She felt the smooth table under her feathers. She smelled sweet orange and leafy peas.

“I feel calmer,” Pippa said. “I can choose with care.”

She opened her eyes and smiled at the peas on the floor. “Hello, little peas. I will pick you up slowly.”

Pippa picked up one pea. “One.” She picked up another pea. “Two.” Each pea felt cool and round in her claws. She put them in a little bowl. “I am taking my time,” she said. “This is mindful.”

When the peas were back in the bowl, Pippa looked at the snack bag, then at the fruit and veggies. She put a wing on her tummy again and asked, “Tummy, what do you need to help me draw and feel good?”

Her tummy felt calm now. It whispered, Rumble… but gentle.

Pippa nodded. “I want food that helps my body and my brain.” She picked up a bright orange carrot stick. Crunch! It sounded like tiny footsteps on dry leaves. She bit an apple slice. Juicy and sweet trickled on her tongue. She took a sip of water. Cool and clear. “Ahh,” said Pippa. “This is a healthy snack.”

Pippa smiled at the snack bag. “Sometimes you are okay for a small treat,” she told it kindly. “But right now, I choose carrots and apple. That helps me draw my sunny poster.”

She sat tall on a pillow. “Now I will make art with my calm body.” She held the yellow crayon. “Hello, sun,” she said. She drew a big round circle. She added warm rays. She colored slowly, feeling the crayon slide soft across the paper. Swish, swish.

She added smiling clouds with her blue crayon. She stuck on a pea with a tiny dab of glue to make a funny green bird friend in the corner. “Hello, Pea Bird,” Pippa giggled. “Thank you for waiting while I cleaned up.”

There was a gentle tap at the door. Mia peeked in. “Hi, Pippa! Your study corner looks peaceful. What are you doing?”

“I had a problem,” Pippa said. “I felt jumpy and made a mess. I could not choose a snack. So I used my mindful pause. I stopped. I breathed. I noticed. Then I chose a healthy snack. Now my body and brain feel ready.”

Mia smiled. “That is understanding your feelings. That is mindfulness. And that is good nutrition, too!”

Pippa puffed with pride. “Mindfulness helps me take care of me,” she said. “It helps me be kind to my tummy, my thoughts, and my space.”

Mia pointed to the poster. “Your sun is glowing!”

Pippa nodded. “It shines because I took my time.”

She taped the poster above her table. The sun looked bright and happy. The room felt warm and calm. Pippa took one more slow breath in and out. “I can solve problems when I pause,” she said. “I can choose healthy foods for energy. I can understand my feelings.”

Mia clapped softly. “Yes. Little steps, calm breaths.”

Pippa twirled one blue tail feather. “I will remember: stop, breathe, notice, choose.”

She looked around her study corner. Pillows were tidy. The peas were safe in the bowl. Her tummy felt good. Her heart felt light.

“Thank you, mindful pause,” Pippa chirped. “Thank you, carrots and apple. Thank you, study corner.”

And the sun on her poster seemed to smile even bigger.

The End.
