# Pebble Tea on the Porch

On a peaceful front porch, a cheerful penguin named Pippa sat in a rocking chair. The porch smelled like warm wood and lemon soap. A gentle breeze tickled Pippa’s feathers, and wind chimes sang, ting-ting-ting.

Pippa set a tiny table with two cups and a bowl of smooth pebbles. “Penguin pebble tea!” she giggled. “Perfect for a calm porch day.”

Her best friend, Benny the bunny, hopped up the steps. His ears wiggled, and his nose twitched. “Hi, Pippa!” Benny said. “I smelled the lemon soap. It smells clean and happy.”

“Hi, Benny!” said Pippa. “Want to share pebble tea and stories?”

“Yes, please!” Benny said. He plopped into the chair next to Pippa. The chair made a little creak, like a friendly frog.

Pippa poured pretend tea. “Sip, sip,” she said, tapping her cup to Benny’s. “Cheers!”

They listened to the wind chimes. Ting-ting. Ting. The porch felt cozy and safe. A butterfly landed on the railing. Its wings were like tiny orange flags.

Benny leaned closer to see the butterfly. “Wow,” he whispered. “So pretty.”

Pippa smiled. She liked how quiet and peaceful everything was. “Let’s play a game,” she said. “We can stack pebbles and make a tiny tower.”

Benny clapped his paws. “Yes!”

Pippa picked up a blue pebble. She loved how it felt cool and smooth. Benny picked a round gray pebble. “Plop,” it went, right on top.

They stacked and stacked. “Careful,” said Pippa softly. “Slow and steady.”

Benny bounced in his seat. “I’m excited!” he said. His chair creaked again. He reached across the table to grab a shiny white pebble.

Pippa flinched. Benny’s paw brushed her flipper. The pebble tower wobbled. Wibble-wobble—down it fell. Clink, clack, clatter!

Pippa’s tummy felt tight. Her smile slid away. “Oh,” she said. “That was too close to my flippers. I felt squished.”

Benny’s ears drooped. “I’m sorry,” he said. “I wanted the shiny pebble. I forgot to ask.”

Pippa took a slow breath in. One… two… three. She felt the cool air in her beak. She watched the butterfly float up and away. Her tummy began to unknot.

She spoke in a calm voice. “Benny, I like playing with you. I also like my space. Please ask before touching my flippers. That keeps me calm.”

Benny nodded fast. “Thank you for telling me, Pippa. I can do that. May I please have the shiny pebble?”

Pippa’s smile came back. “Yes,” she said. “Thank you for asking.” She passed the pebble gently into his paw.

They rebuilt the tower. Pebble by pebble. Slow and steady. “We can take turns,” Pippa said. “You, then me.”

“Okay,” Benny said. “And I will keep my paws on my side until it’s my turn.” He tucked his paws close, then wiggled one ear. “Is this a good distance?”

Pippa checked in with her body. Her shoulders felt soft. “Yes,” she said. “That feels good.”

The wind chimes played again. Ting-ting-ting. The porch smelled like lemon and a little like daisies. Far away, a bird called, tweet-tweet.

The tower grew as tall as Pippa’s cup. Benny whispered, “We are careful friends.”

Pippa giggled. “Yes. Careful and kind.”

When the tower was finished, Pippa poured more pretend tea. “Sip, sip,” she said.

Benny sniffed his cup. “Mmm,” he said. “Smells like… pep-pep-pebble!” They both laughed.

Benny looked at Pippa. “Can I give you a hug to say thank you?”

Pippa took another slow breath. She noticed the warm sun on her feathers. She noticed her happy heart. “Yes,” she said, “I would like a hug.”

Benny hopped over slowly. He hugged Pippa gently, not too tight. “Is this okay?” he asked.

“Perfect,” said Pippa. She felt cozy and safe again.

A soft thump sounded when a leaf landed on the steps. The butterfly returned and sat on the tower’s top pebble for a quick hello. They watched it together without moving, like statues. The tower did not wobble.

Benny smiled. “We are good at listening to the porch sounds. Ting-ting. Tweet-tweet. Thump.”

Pippa nodded. “And we are good at listening to each other.”

They cleaned up the pebbles, one by one. Clink, clink, clink into the bowl. The breeze gave them a tiny kiss on their cheeks. The chairs creaked goodbye.

Benny waved. “See you tomorrow?”

“See you tomorrow,” Pippa said. “Thank you for being a kind friend.”

Benny hopped down the steps. Pippa rocked gently and took one last deep breath. In… out. She felt proud.

Today, Pippa learned that friendship means sharing, asking, and listening. She learned that saying “please ask first” is caring for herself. Benny learned that good friends respect space. Together, they found calm with slow breaths and gentle voices.

The End.
