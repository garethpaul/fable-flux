# Benny Bunny Washes Words

Benny Bunny lived in a cozy home office with bright windows, a soft green rug, and a little desk that fit his fluffy tail. His mommy worked at a big computer. It hummed like a sleepy bee. Benny had a small bunny-sized keyboard and a stack of picture cards: apple, bubble, cuddle, and more. He loved words. He loved how words felt in his mouth—soft, bouncy, sticky, silly!

“Good morning, words,” Benny said, tapping his tiny keys. “Hello, apple. Hi, bubble. Nice to see you, cuddle!”

Mommy smiled. “Practice makes you strong, Benny. And strong bunnies always wash their paws.”

Benny nodded. He was a determined rabbit. He loved to try, try, try.

One day, a problem hopped in. Tiny dots of dust floated in the sunny air. The trash bin was full of old paper. Little crumbs hid under the keyboard. Benny sniffed. “Sniff sniff. It smells… dusty.” His whiskers twitched. His nose wiggled. His ears perked.

He pressed his favorite key: the B key for “bubble.” But the key stuck. “Oh no!” said Benny. “My words feel sticky.” He pressed again. It made a slow, sad click. The screen showed, “b…..b….”

Benny’s tummy felt tight. He loved words. He wanted them to hop and dance, not get stuck. He took a deep, brave breath. “I can fix this,” he said. “I can use my strong bunny brain.”

He looked around like a tiny detective. “Where did the stickiness come from?” He remembered he had been eating a sweet carrot snack. He had touched the keys with carrot paws.

Benny made a plan with three steps. He even spoke the steps out loud, tapping his paw like a little teacher.

“Step one: wash my paws.
Step two: clean my desk and keyboard.
Step three: practice happy, clean words.”

Mommy’s chair swiveled. “That sounds like smart, logical thinking,” she said. “And very brave. Do you want help?”

Benny stood tall on tip-toes. “Yes, please! I want to do it myself, but I like help for the big parts.”

They hopped to the sink. The water was warm and gentle. It sounded like tiny rain on leaves. Swish, swish. Benny rubbed soap into bubbles. Big bubbles, small bubbles, pop-pop-pop! He sang, “Wash the paws, front and back, between the fingers, pat-pat-pat!” He scrubbed for a long bunny count: “One carrot, two carrot, three carrot, four…” all the way to ten. He rinsed. He shook. Drip, drop, plip! He dried with a soft towel that smelled clean, like sunshine and cotton.

“How do your paws feel?” Mommy asked.

“Fresh!” Benny giggled. “They feel like cloud pillows.”

Back at the desk, Benny looked at the full trash bin. “We should not waste,” he said. “Sustain… sustain…” The word felt big.

“Sustainability,” Mommy said kindly. “We can take the old papers to the recycle box.”

Benny clapped. “Yes! Let’s recycle.” They sorted the papers into a green box with a circle sign. It made a gentle rustle, like leaves in the wind.

Next, they used a soft cloth and a tiny bit of safe cleaner. Benny wiped slowly, left to right. Swipe, swipe. He lifted the keyboard and tapped gently, tap-tap, to let the crumbs fall onto a little sheet. He saved the sheet for the compost bin in the kitchen. “From crumbs to soil,” he whispered. “That helps the garden grow.”

He pressed the B key again. It sprang up with a happy click. “B!” said the screen.

Benny cheered. “My words can dance again!” He tried more: “A for apple,” “C for cuddle,” “W for wash.” The letters lined up like little friends holding hands.

Benny breathed a smile. He looked at his paws. He looked at his clean desk. He smelled the room. It smelled light and bright, like lemon and fresh paper. He felt proud and calm.

He wanted to thank the words. He loved their sounds. He wrote a tiny poem to show his language love:

“Words are warm,
Words are bright,
Clean paws help
Words feel right.”

Mommy clapped softly. “Beautiful, Benny. Your poem is kind and smart.”

The computer hummed a happy hum. The sun made a golden square on the floor, perfect for a little bunny nap. But Benny had one more idea. “Let’s make a Hygiene Helper Chart!” he said. He drew pictures with a thick blue crayon:

- A paw with bubbles.
- A trash bin with a green arrow for recycle.
- A keyboard with a smiling face.

He taped the chart near his desk. “I will wash before I type,” he said. “I will clean up when I am done. I will be gentle with my tools. That is brave and caring.”

Mommy nodded. “That is courage, Benny. Courage to stop, think, and do the right thing.”

Benny sat up tall. “I did not give up. I used my brain. I used my heart. I used soap!”

They both laughed. Then Benny practiced a new word: “Hygiene.” He sounded it out slowly. “Hi-jeen. Hygiene helps me and helps my words.”

He typed his poem again, smooth and quick. He felt safe. He felt strong. He felt clean.

That night, Benny washed his paws before dinner. He washed after, too. He put scraps in compost, papers in recycle, and he thanked the words for a good day. “Good night, apple. Good night, bubble. Good night, cuddle.”

The office was tidy and quiet. The moon made a silver stripe on the rug. Benny curled up in the warm square of moonlight and smiled.

The End.
