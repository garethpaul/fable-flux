# Brave Brushstrokes for Baby Bear

It was art time in the creative art room. Sunlight slid across the colorful tables. Paints gleamed like rainbows in little cups. Paper smelled clean and crisp. Brushes stood like tall grass in a jar.

A friendly bear cub named Bibi tiptoed inside. Her soft paws made quiet pats on the floor. She wiggled her round ears. She looked at the bright paints and felt a flutter in her tummy.

Ms. Lark, the gentle teacher, smiled. “Hello, Bibi! Today we paint. You may choose your own picture.”

Bibi looked at the paints. Red looked bold. Blue looked calm. Yellow looked like sunshine. “What if I make a mistake?” Bibi whispered.

Ms. Lark knelt beside her. “Your picture is your voice,” she said. “You can decide what to paint. You can fix little oops with more color. That’s how artists learn.”

Bibi took a deep breath. She could smell sweet glue and lemony soap. She felt the smooth paper under her paw. “Okay,” she said. “I will try.”

Bibi chose a big sheet of paper. She picked a small brush and a big brush. She tapped them together—tap, tap. “I want to paint a forest,” she decided. “With tall trees and a bright path.” Her heart felt warm. That was her first decision.

Kip the rabbit hopped over. “Hi, Bibi! I’m painting a rocket. Zoom!” He made a zoom sound and giggled.

“I’m painting a forest,” Bibi said. “I think. I hope I can do it.”

“You can,” said Kip. “I’ll zoom, and you can swish!” He swished his brush in the air.

Bibi dipped her big brush into green paint. Swish, swish! She made tall lines for trees. The paint was cool and slippery. It made soft sounds on the paper. But oops—the green line slipped off to the side.

Bibi’s ears drooped. “Oh no,” she said softly. “I made a wrong line.”

Ms. Lark came closer. “I see a brave swish,” she said. “What can you do now?”

Bibi blinked. She looked at the wobbly line. She tapped her chin. “I can make that line into a vine,” she decided. She picked up the small brush and added tiny leaves. Tap, tap, tap. “There,” she said. “It’s a vine!” Her shoulders felt lighter.

“That’s smart,” said Ms. Lark. “You made a choice. You kept going. That is self-confidence.”

Bibi smiled a tiny smile. She kept painting. She made soft brown trunks. She made a bright yellow path like a ribbon. She hummed a little tune. Hummm, hummm.

Then she heard a sniffle. Tori the turtle was by the glitter box. Her eyes were wet. “I spilled my glitter,” Tori said. Silver sparkles made a shiny puddle.

Bibi looked at her painting. The path was half done. She took a breath and made another decision. “I can help,” she said. She found the dustpan and a small brush. She swept slowly. Swish, sweep, pour. The glitter tinkled like tiny bells as it fell back into the jar.

“Thank you,” Tori said. “You are kind.”

“You’re welcome,” said Bibi. She felt proud and warm, like sunshine inside. She washed her paws with soapy water. Bubbles popped. Pop, pop. “Now I go back to my picture,” she said. “I promised myself to finish.”

Bibi checked the time chart on the wall. The clock had a big red hand. “Ten more minutes,” Ms. Lark called. “Artists, please be responsible and start finishing.”

Bibi nodded. “I will finish,” she told herself. She made tiny flowers with dots of purple. Dot, dot, dot. She made a small blue pond that looked cool and quiet. She added a little red bird on a branch. Chirp, chirp, she whispered.

Kip hopped back. “Wow! Your forest looks cozy.”

“Thank you,” said Bibi. She looked at one empty space. It felt too plain. Her tummy fluttered again. “Should I add anything?” she asked.

“What do you think?” Ms. Lark asked. “You can decide.”

Bibi closed her eyes for a moment. She listened to the soft brush sounds, the gentle music, the happy chatter. She opened her eyes. “I will add a sun,” she decided. She dipped into the yellow paint. Circle, circle, rays, rays. The sun glowed.

“Time to clean up,” called Ms. Lark. “Brushes in water, caps on glue.”

Bibi put lids on the paint cups. Click. She wiped the table with a damp cloth. Wipe, wipe. She carried her painting to the drying rack with careful paws. “I made it,” she said, standing tall.

At sharing time, the class sat in a circle. Bibi held her forest. Her paws felt a little shaky, but she took a brave breath.

“Tell us about your art,” said Ms. Lark.

“I was scared to start,” Bibi said. “I worried I would mess up. But I tried. When a line went funny, I made a vine. I helped Tori, and then I finished my path. I decided to add a sun. Now I feel proud.”

The class clapped. Clap, clap, clap. Tori smiled. “Your forest is kind,” she said. “It helped me feel calm.”

Ms. Lark nodded. “Bibi showed self-confidence by trying and by not giving up. She showed responsibility by cleaning and finishing. She made good decisions and shared her time to help a friend. That is generous.”

Bibi’s ears perked up. Her smile grew big. Inside, she whispered, “I can do hard things.”

She looked at her painting again. It shined in the light. It looked like a place she could walk into, step by step, brave and bright.

The End.
