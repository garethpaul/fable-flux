# Owl on the Rooftop

On a warm, golden evening, a wise old owl named Olive perched on a flat rooftop. The roof felt smooth and a little warm on her feet. The sky smelled like rain and sun, and the soft wind tickled her feathers. Olive blinked her big, kind eyes. Hoo-hoo, she sang softly. She loved to watch, listen, and think.

Every day, Olive helped the neighborhood birds settle for the night. She reminded them to be kind, to share, and to say sorry when needed. Olive liked to help. It felt good. But tonight, the wind felt heavier, and her wings felt tired. Her tummy made a small rumble.

“I am hungry,” Olive whispered. “I am sleepy, too.”

Just then, three little pigeons landed on the rooftop with a flutter. Flap-flap! Their feet went tap-tap on the roof.

“Olive, Olive!” they cooed. “We can’t agree. We want a bedtime song. But we all want different songs!”

The first pigeon puffed up. “I want a fast song!”

The second pigeon shook her head. “I want a slow song.”

The third pigeon sighed. “I want a silly song.”

Olive felt her eyes droop. Her wings drooped, too. She took a deep breath. She wanted to help. But her tummy rumbled again.

Olive remembered a quiet voice inside her. Take care of yourself first. That is self-care. When you feel well, you can help well.

She stood tall and spoke gently. “Friends, I hear you. You each want a song. But I am hungry and tired. I need a snack and a sip of water. Then I can sing with a calm voice.”

The pigeons blinked. “But we want a song now,” said the first pigeon.

Olive nodded. “I understand. Let’s talk.” She pointed to a little puddle on the roof. It sparkled like a tiny mirror. “I will drink some water. It will take one minute. Then I will munch my nuts. That will take two minutes. After that, we can choose together. We can compromise.”

“What is compromise?” asked the second pigeon.

“It means we listen, we share, and we meet in the middle,” Olive said. “We make a plan that is fair for everyone.”

The pigeons looked at each other. The wind hummed. The sky turned rosy. They could smell the warm rooftop and hear a soft city buzz below.

“Okay,” said the third pigeon softly. “We can wait three minutes.”

“Thank you,” Olive said. “You are good friends.”

Olive sipped the cool water. Sip, sip. She felt the water slide down her dry throat. Ahh. She ate crunchy nuts from a small pouch. Crunch, crunch. Her tummy felt happy. She took three slow breaths. In, out. In, out. In, out. Her wings felt light again.

She turned to the pigeons and smiled. “Thank you for waiting. Now, let’s use kind words to choose. Tell me what you love about your song.”

The first pigeon said, “Fast songs make me feel excited!”

The second said, “Slow songs make me feel safe.”

The third said, “Silly songs make me giggle.”

Olive nodded. “All those feelings are important.” She placed a feathered wing on her heart. “We will use integrity. That means we will be honest and fair. We will keep our word.”

They all nodded back. Nod, nod.

Olive fluffed her feathers. “Here is a plan. First, I will sing one verse fast. Then one verse slow. Then one verse silly. We will have three verses. Everyone gets a turn. That is our compromise. Does that sound fair?”

The pigeons smiled. “Yes!”

Olive began to sing with her clear, warm voice. The first verse was fast. Tap-tap went little pigeon feet as they danced. The second verse was slow, like a soft blanket. The wind whispered along. The third verse was silly, with giggles and funny hoots. Hoo-hoo-hee!

When the song was done, the pigeons sighed happily. “Thank you, Olive! That was perfect.”

Olive bowed a little. “Thank you for waiting and for talking with me. I took care of myself. You listened. We used good communication. We made a fair plan. We kept our word. That is integrity.”

The pigeons snuggled close. The rooftop felt cool now, and the stars popped out like tiny lights. A gentle night smell, like fresh rain and leaves, filled the air.

“We learned something,” said the first pigeon.

“What did you learn?” Olive asked softly.

“We learned that when we take care of our needs,” said the second pigeon, “we can be calm and kind.”

“And when we listen and share,” added the third pigeon, “everyone can feel happy.”

Olive smiled, her eyes warm and bright. “Yes,” she said. “Self-care helps us help others. Communication helps us understand. Compromise helps us be fair. And integrity helps us keep our promises.”

The pigeons yawned. “Good night, Olive.”

“Good night, friends,” Olive whispered, tucking her wings around herself. The rooftop was quiet. The city hummed low. The stars twinkled. Olive felt peaceful and proud. She had cared for herself, and then she had helped.

And that night, under the kind sky, everyone slept well.

The End.
