# Kiki the Calm Koala Builds a Better Desk

Kiki the calm koala lived in a cozy home with a tiny home office. The room smelled like warm paper and fresh pencils. Sunlight painted soft squares on the floor. Kiki’s round ears twitched as she listened to the quiet hum of a fan.

On the desk sat a wobbly tower of crayons, a little laptop, and a cup full of markers. “Hmm,” Kiki whispered, patting the desk with her fuzzy paw. “This desk is too crowded. I need more space to draw and click.” Her voice was gentle, like a soft blanket.

Kiki looked around the room. There were boxes, jars, and old folders waiting to be used. A cardboard box rested by the window. It smelled like clean paper and a little bit of cinnamon from the kitchen nearby. Kiki’s eyes lit up. “I can build something,” she said. “I can build a new shelf!”

She took a deep breath. In… and out. “I can do this slowly. I can do this calmly.” She smiled. “I will use recycling. I will use care.”

Just then, two friends peeked in. Nia the noisy squirrel bounced in with her fluffy tail wiggling. “Hi, Kiki! What are you doing? Can I help?” Nia’s voice buzzed like a happy bee.

Behind her came Mo the slow snail, sliding gently on a little leaf board. “Hel-lo, Ki-ki,” Mo said in a soft, stretchy voice. “I like to help too.”

Kiki clapped her paws quietly. “Yes! I’m building a shelf from old boxes. We can reuse and make new things. That is called recycling.”

Nia’s eyes sparkled. “Let’s build fast!” she squeaked.

Mo nodded slowly. “Let’s build care-ful.”

Kiki giggled. “We can build fast and careful. We can build together.”

They gathered supplies: two sturdy boxes, a stack of old magazines, four toilet paper rolls, a roll of tape, some colorful bottle caps, and a glue stick. The tape felt sticky like honey on Kiki’s paw. The cardboard felt rough and strong. The bottle caps clicked like tiny drums when Nia tapped them.

Kiki made a plan. She drew a simple picture with big shapes. “First, we make the base,” she said. “Then, we make legs with the rolls. Last, we add a top shelf.”

Nia wriggled with energy. “I can tape! I can zoom!” She zipped around, tearing tape with a rip-rip sound.

Mo smiled slowly. “I can press the tape down. I am good at slow pressing.” He slid over and smoothed each piece of tape with gentle, careful moves.

They worked together. Kiki held the box steady. Nia wrapped tape around the corners. Rip! Stick! Press! Mo pressed the tape so it stayed snug. Smooth, smooth, smooth. The base was set.

“Now the legs,” Kiki said. She showed the toilet rolls. “These will hold the top shelf.”

Nia placed the rolls too close together. “Oops,” she chirped, giggling. “They look like four tiny chimneys!”

Mo measured with his eyes, slow and sure. “Give them space,” he said. “Then the shelf will not wob-ble.”

Kiki nodded. “Good idea, Mo.” She drew dots where the legs should go. Nia lined them up, and Mo pressed them down. Together they glued a flat piece of cardboard on top. The glue smelled a little sweet. They waited. Kiki counted softly, “One… two… three… four… five.” The glue set. The shelf held.

“Time to decorate,” Nia cheered. She sprinkled bottle caps like candy.

Mo chose magazines with calm colors—soft blues and greens. “These look like the sky and the leaves,” he said, smiling.

Kiki said, “We will celebrate our differences. Nia brings speed and excitement. Mo brings care and calm. I bring a plan. All are good. All help us build.”

They made a pattern—blue cap, green cap, rainbow sticker, magazine square. Click. Pat. Press. The shelf looked bright and happy. The home office felt new and tidy.

Kiki placed the crayon tower on the top shelf. It stood steady and straight. She put the markers in a jar on the side and slid the laptop onto the clean desk. There was space to draw. There was space to click. The fan hummed. The sunlight warmed Kiki’s fur. She felt proud.

Nia bounced. “We built it! We did it together!”

Mo nodded. “Re-cycling is smart. Build-ing is kind to our world.”

Kiki hugged them both gently. “We made something new from old things. We listened. We were patient. We used our different ways.”

Nia tilted her head. “My fast was good.”

Mo smiled. “My slow was good.”

Kiki’s eyes shone. “Together is best.”

They high-fived—paw, tiny paw, and soft snail shell. Tap, tap, tap. Then they had a little celebration. Nia shook a paper ribbon. Swish! Mo tapped a bottle cap like a bell. Ting! Kiki poured cool water and shared apple slices. Crunch, crunch. Sweet and crisp.

Kiki looked at the shelf again. “This is our brave builder shelf,” she said. “It reminds us to reuse, to build, and to love how we are different.”

Nia giggled. “What shall we build tomorrow?”

Mo whispered, “Some-thing slow and fast.”

Kiki smiled, calm and bright. “We will plan it. We will try it. We will build it.”

The End.
