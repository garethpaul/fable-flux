# Leo’s Quiet Paw Dance

In a sunny home office, a brave little lion cub named Leo padded across the soft rug. Mama Lion sat at a desk with a big glowing screen and a tall stack of papers. The room smelled like warm tea and new paper. The keyboard made tiny click-click sounds. A fan hummed like a sleepy bee.

Leo’s tail flicked with energy. “Mama, can we play?” he asked, eyes bright and golden.

Mama smiled. “Soon, brave cub. I have a meeting in five minutes. I need quiet while I talk, okay?”

Leo nodded, but inside he felt bouncy like popcorn. He wanted to ROAR and run and dance. His paws tingled. His chest felt full of wiggles. He tried to sit on a cushion. He wiggled. He wobbled. He rolled. The cushion went fwoomp.

The meeting started. Mama put on her headset. “Hello, everyone,” she said softly. “Thank you for joining.”

Leo whispered, “I will be quiet.” He pressed his paws together. He tried to be still. But the wiggles grew bigger. His ears heard Mama talking, but his body said, Move! Move! Move!

“Uh-oh,” Leo whispered. His heart went thump-thump. His breath felt fast, like a little train.

He remembered something Mama taught him. “When you feel too much, use your brave tools,” she had said. “You can help yourself.”

Leo put a paw on his chest. He put a paw on his belly. “Hot cocoa breath,” he told himself. He pretended to hold a warm cup. He breathed in slow—sniff, sniff—like smelling sweet cocoa. He breathed out slow—blow, blow—like cooling the cup. The fan hummed with him. In—sniff, sniff. Out—blow, blow. His heart slowed. Thump… thump. His paws still tingled, but softer now.

He looked around the home office. There were sticky notes in bright colors. There were smooth paper clips that felt cool on his paw. There was a little basket of toys: a soft lion, a quiet puzzle, and his favorite—shiny stars that stuck to the rug.

Leo whispered, “I can choose.” He tried the puzzle. Click. Click. Piece by piece. He felt proud. But the wiggles came back, like a tiny drum inside. Tap-tap-tap.

Leo tapped his paw on the rug. It made a soft thup. An idea popped like a bubble. “A quiet dance!” he thought. “I can move and be kind to the quiet.”

He stood up on the rug. He bent his knees. He whispered to himself, “Soft paws. Slow body.” He started a Quiet Paw Dance. He tiptoed—tip… tip… tip. He swished his tail in slow, careful swirls. He rolled his shoulders like waves. He counted in his head. “One… two… three.” He breathed in and out with the rhythm. In—two—three. Out—two—three.

The rug felt fuzzy. The air felt warm. The fan’s hum became music. Mama kept talking softly, and Leo kept moving softly. He felt proud. “I am brave. I am calm. I can help myself,” he whispered.

After a while, Leo felt the drum inside grow quiet. No more tap-tap. His body felt like still water. He lay down on the rug and put a star sticker on his paw. It stuck with a tiny pat. He smiled.

Just then, Mama’s meeting ended. She took off her headset. “All done,” she said. She looked at Leo. “Thank you for being quiet, brave cub. How did you do it?”

Leo sat up tall. He practiced self-advocacy with his small, strong voice. “Mama,” he said, “my body had big wiggles. I used hot cocoa breath. I did a Quiet Paw Dance on the rug. It helped me feel calm. Next time, can I have a little dance space and a soft music hum?”

Mama’s eyes shone. “Yes! That is a smart plan,” she said. “You knew what you needed and asked for it. That is brave and kind.”

They made a tiny dance corner by the plant and the window. Mama placed a small mat and a jar with star stickers. She turned the fan to a gentle hum. “This can be your calm dance spot,” she said.

Leo placed his paw on his chest and belly again. “In—sniff, sniff. Out—blow, blow,” he said. He did one slow tail swirl. He giggled. “My body feels happy.”

Mama clapped softly. “Me too. Now let’s play five-minute dance freeze!”

They danced together in the home office. When Mama said “Freeze!” Leo stopped and took one hot cocoa breath. In—sniff, sniff. Out—blow, blow. He felt the rug, heard the hum, and smelled the tea. He felt strong and in control.

Later, they cuddled on the cushion. Mama asked, “What did you learn today, brave cub?”

Leo’s eyes were warm and sleepy. “I learned I can calm my body,” he said. “I can breathe slow, move soft, and ask for what I need. That helps me and helps you.”

Mama nuzzled his mane. “Yes,” she whispered. “That is self-regulation and mindfulness. You listened to your body, you used your tools, and you spoke up.”

Leo purred, a low happy rumble. He pressed a star sticker onto Mama’s paw. “For you,” he said. “We are a good team.”

The fan hummed. The tea smelled sweet. The home office felt peaceful and bright.

The End.
