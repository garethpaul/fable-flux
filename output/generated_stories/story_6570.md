# Poppy Penguin’s Shed Surprise

Poppy was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a happy waddle. One sunny morning, she flapped her little wings and said, “Today, I will make something special!” She waddled to the garden shed. The shed smelled like fresh wood and warm sunshine. Dust danced in the light. The door creaked. “Creeeak,” it sang.

Inside, there were boxes and baskets. There were jars of buttons, rolls of ribbon, and soft yarn in many colors. There were safe scissors with round tips, sticky tape, and glue that smelled sweet like paper. Poppy smiled. “This is a good place to craft,” she said. “I will make a welcome sign for Grandma Goose. She is visiting today!”

Poppy set out a big piece of cardboard. She felt it with her flippers. “Smooth,” she said. She looked around. “What should I use?” She tapped her beak and thought. Thinking is a good skill. It helps us plan. Poppy took a deep breath. “I will use what I have.”

She opened a jar. “Click, clack!” went the buttons. She found blue, yellow, and green. She opened a basket. “Rustle, rustle,” went the ribbons. Red and gold ribbons shone like little sunbeams. Poppy picked up a pencil. “I will write WELCOME,” she said. She drew big bubble letters. “W-E-L-C-O-M-E.” She smiled. “Good start!”

Just then, Poppy heard a rustle outside. “Help!” squeaked a tiny voice. Poppy waddled to the door and peeked out. A little field mouse stood by a broken flowerpot. His whiskers shook. “Oh no,” said the mouse. “I broke Mama’s pot. I wanted to plant seeds. Now I am sad.”

Poppy’s heart felt warm. “I can help,” she said. “We can fix it with a craft!” The mouse sniffled. “Really?” Poppy nodded. “Yes! Crafting is for making and mending. We will think, plan, and be careful.”

They went inside the shed. The mouse wiggled his tiny nose. “It smells like crayons,” he said. Poppy giggled. “I like that smell.” She put on a little apron. She gave the mouse a tiny apron, too. “We are a team,” said Poppy.

Poppy looked at the broken pot. There were three big pieces and some tiny chips. “Let’s sort the pieces,” she said. “Sorting helps us see.” They put big pieces in one pile. Tiny chips in another. The mouse’s eyes grew wide. “This is like a puzzle!” Poppy nodded. “Yes, we fit the shapes.”

They tried one big piece. It did not fit. Poppy frowned a little. “Hmm. Not this way.” She turned it around. “Click!” It fit. “We did it,” cheered the mouse. They added the next big piece. “Click!” It fit too. Poppy used glue very carefully. “Dot, dot, not a lot,” she said softly. The mouse smiled and whispered, “Dot, dot, not a lot.”

They held the pot gently until it felt strong. “We will make it pretty, too,” said Poppy. She wrapped a gold ribbon around the pot and tied a neat bow. “Soft and shiny,” she said. The mouse clapped. “Mama will love this!”

Poppy waddled back to her sign. “Now, I must finish my welcome sign,” she said. She dipped a small brush in blue paint. “Swish, swish.” She painted inside the letters. The mouse watched. “Can I help?” Poppy smiled. “Yes! You can hand me buttons.” The mouse carefully picked blue, yellow, and green. “Thank you,” said Poppy. “Helping hands make light work.”

They glued the buttons around the edge of the sign. “Dot, dot, not a lot,” they said together. The buttons made a happy pattern—blue, yellow, green, blue, yellow, green. Poppy felt proud. “Patterns help our brains think,” she said gently. “We can see what comes next.”

They added a red ribbon at the top. Poppy tied it tight. “Now it can hang,” she said. The sign sparkled in the sunbeam. The shed felt cozy and bright. The mouse’s whiskers twitched with joy. “I learned I can fix things,” he said.

Footsteps sounded on the path. “Tap, tap, tap.” Grandma Goose peeked in. She wore a soft shawl that smelled like lavender. “Hello, dears,” she honked kindly. “What a lovely shed!”

Poppy held up the sign. “Welcome, Grandma Goose!” she chirped. Grandma Goose gasped softly. “Oh my, this is beautiful! The letters are clear. The buttons are bright. You planned and you made.”

The mouse held up the fixed pot. “We mended this, too,” he squeaked. “Dot, dot, not a lot!” Grandma Goose smiled big. “What smart helpers! What careful crafters!”

Poppy felt warm and tall inside. She said, “We used our eyes to look, our minds to think, and our hearts to help.” Grandma Goose hugged them gently. “That is the best craft of all,” she said. “You turned a problem into a plan.”

They went outside to the garden. The mouse planted seeds in the fixed pot. Poppy hung the sign on the shed door. It swayed and clicked in the breeze. “Welcome,” it said, without words.

They all sat for a snack—crunchy crackers and sweet apple slices. The sun felt soft, and the air smelled like fresh earth. Poppy took a happy bite. “Crafting can fix, make, and share,” she said. “When we think and help, good things grow.”

The End.
