# Melody's Messy Mistake

In the bright creative art room, a little yellow bird named Melody loved to sing while children made beautiful pictures. Her sweet voice filled the room with happy songs. *Tweet, tweet, la-la-la!* she would sing from her perch by the window.

The art room was Melody's favorite place. Colorful paintbrushes stood in neat rows. Crayons were sorted by color in pretty boxes. Paper was stacked in tidy piles. Everything had its special place.

One sunny morning, Melody felt extra excited. She flapped her wings and sang louder than ever before. "What a beautiful day for art!" she chirped.

But as Melody danced and twirled, her wings knocked over a jar of paintbrushes. *Clatter, crash!* The brushes scattered across the floor. Paint drops splattered on the clean white paper.

"Oh no!" gasped Melody. She tried to fly away quickly, but her tail feathers swept across the crayon box. *Tumble, roll!* Red, blue, and green crayons rolled everywhere.

Mrs. Chen, the art teacher, walked into the room. She saw the mess and frowned. "What happened here?" she asked, looking around at the scattered supplies.

Melody felt her little heart beating fast. She wanted to hide behind the curtain, but she knew that wouldn't be right. The mess was getting bigger as other children started to arrive.

"I'm sorry, Mrs. Chen," Melody said in a small voice. "I was singing and dancing, and I wasn't being careful. I made this big mess."

Mrs. Chen's face softened. "Thank you for telling me the truth, Melody. That was very brave of you."

"I feel terrible," chirped Melody sadly. "I love this art room, and now it's all messy because of me."

"Everyone makes mistakes," said Mrs. Chen gently. "What matters is that you said sorry and want to make it better."

Melody perked up. "Can I help clean up? I want to fix what I did wrong."

"Of course!" smiled Mrs. Chen. "Let's work together."

Melody carefully picked up crayons in her beak, one by one. She sorted them by color - red ones here, blue ones there. Mrs. Chen gathered the paintbrushes and wiped them clean.

"The brushes go in size order," explained Mrs. Chen. "Big ones in back, small ones in front."

Melody watched carefully and helped arrange them properly. She even used her wing to gently wipe the paint drops from the paper.

Soon, other children arrived and saw Melody working hard. "Can we help too?" asked little Emma.

"Yes, please!" chirped Melody. "Many hands make work easier!"

Together, they organized all the art supplies. The room looked even better than before! Everything sparkled and shone in its proper place.

"Thank you, Melody," said Mrs. Chen. "You turned your mistake into something wonderful. You apologized, helped clean up, and taught everyone about working together."

Melody felt warm and happy inside. "I learned something important today," she sang softly. "When I make a mistake, I should say sorry right away and help fix it."

From that day on, Melody still sang beautiful songs in the art room. But now she was extra careful with her wings and always helped keep everything organized. The children loved her even more because they knew she was honest and kind.

"Saying sorry isn't scary," Melody would sing. "It makes everything better!"

The End.
