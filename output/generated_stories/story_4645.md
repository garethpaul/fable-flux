# Sprig the Squirrel’s Spoon Map

Sprig the squirrel lived in a warm kitchen, right above the pantry shelf. The kitchen smelled like cinnamon toast and apples. The sun made golden squares on the floor. The kettle sang a soft song. Sprig loved this cozy place.

One morning, Sprig peeked out from behind the sugar jar. “Good morning, Kitchen!” he chirped. His fluffy tail twitched with ideas.

On the table sat a big mixing bowl, a wooden spoon, and a box of oatmeal. Next to them were little jars with stickers and flags: raisins from California, coconut from the Philippines, cocoa from Ghana, and honey from a local farm. There was also a paper world map on the table, with bright colors and oceans like blue paint.

Sprig clapped his tiny paws. “I want to make a snack,” he said. “And I want to learn about the world!”

He stood on the edge of the bowl and took a deep breath. He smelled sweet honey, warm oats, and a hint of chocolate. His tummy rumbled.

Then a little problem popped up.

“I can only choose three toppings,” Sprig said. “If I add too many, the snack will be sticky and fall apart. What should I pick?”

His whiskers wiggled. Decision time.

He thought of five ideas at once. “Raisins! Coconut! Cocoa! Honey! Nuts!” Sprig’s eyes went big. “Oh acorns, that is more than three.”

Sprig put a paw on his chest. “Slow down,” he told himself. “I can make a plan.”

He skittered across the table and gently tapped the world map with the end of the wooden spoon. Tap, tap.

“Where do these tasty things come from?” he wondered. “Maybe that can help me choose.”

He picked up the jar of raisins. “Raisins grow from grapes in sunny places like California,” he said, sounding out the long word slowly. “They are chewy and sweet.”

He sniffed the coconut. “Coconut comes from tall trees by warm oceans, like the Philippines,” he said. “It’s white and flaky.”

He opened the cocoa jar and took a tiny sniff. “Cocoa is from cocoa beans that farmers grow near the equator, like in Ghana. It smells like chocolate.”

He tapped the honey bear bottle. “Honey is made by bees right here near our home. Buzz, buzz!”

Sprig felt proud. He was learning about the world with his nose and his spoon. But he still had to decide.

The kettle puffed a cloud of steam. The clock ticked. The sunbeam moved a little.

Sprig made a little chart in his head. He said each choice out loud.

“Raisins are chewy. Coconut is light. Cocoa is rich. Honey is sticky and sweet. Nuts are crunchy.”

He touched his heart. “What do I want my snack to feel like?” He rubbed the spoon on his paw. “I want chewy, a little crunch, and a tiny taste of chocolate.”

He smiled. “I have a plan!”

Sprig chose raisins for chew. He found a small bowl of chopped nuts for crunch. And he picked cocoa for a tiny chocolate taste. Three choices. He set the coconut and honey to the side.

But then he paused. “Wait,” he said kindly to the honey bottle. “I still like you. I can save you for tea time.”

Sprig looked at the map again. “I will learn about the Philippines and honey bees later,” he told himself. “Today I will learn about California, Ghana, and nuts from our local trees.”

He poured warm oats into the big bowl. Ploop! He shook in the raisins. Plip! He sprinkled in cocoa. Dusty brown clouds floated up. “Achoo!” He giggled. He added the nuts. Crunch, crunch.

With the wooden spoon, he stirred. Swish, swish. The oats turned cozy and thick. The raisins peeked out like little boats. The cocoa made the oats smell like hot cocoa on a winter day.

“Hello, tiny snack from big places,” Sprig said. “I’m glad I chose with care.”

He hopped onto the world map again. He drew three little dots with a pencil: one on California, one on Ghana, and one near his home for the nuts. He drew a tiny line from dot to dot to dot. “This is my spoon map,” he said. “My snack is a little world trip!”

He tasted a spoonful. “Mmm,” he hummed. “Chewy, crunchy, and just a little chocolate. My plan worked.”

Just then, the kettle clicked off. Sprig patted the honey bottle. “Tea time later,” he whispered. “Thank you, bees.”

He cleaned the table with a little cloth. Wipe, wipe. He put the jars back in a neat row. He folded the map and tucked it under the sugar jar to keep learning for another day.

Sprig sat on the warm windowsill with his bowl. The sun felt like a soft blanket. He thought about farmers in Ghana, groves in California, and nuts from nearby trees. He felt connected, like a thread tying places together.

“I made a good decision,” he said softly. “I looked, I listened, I learned, and I chose.”

His tail curled happily. “Next time, I can try coconut and honey. I can make a new plan. I can keep learning about our big, beautiful world.”

Sprig finished his snack and tapped the spoon like a drum. Tap, tap, tap. “I am a resourceful squirrel,” he sang. “I think before I choose!”

The warm kitchen hummed. The sun shone. Sprig smiled.

The End.
