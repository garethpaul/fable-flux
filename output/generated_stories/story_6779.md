# Nutkin's Big Building Day

The warm sunshine tickled Nutkin the squirrel's fluffy tail as he scampered down the big oak tree in Mrs. Henderson's backyard. Today was special - he wanted to build the biggest, most wonderful nest ever!

"I'll gather the softest leaves and the strongest twigs," Nutkin chattered excitedly to himself. His little paws worked quickly, collecting materials all around the sunny yard.

Just as Nutkin started building on a perfect branch, he heard a tiny "chirp chirp!" Below him sat Ruby, a small red robin, looking very sad.

"What's wrong, Ruby?" Nutkin called down, his bushy tail swishing with concern.

"I was hoping to build my nest right there too," Ruby said softly. "That branch gets the best morning sunshine, and it's safe from the wind. But I see you got here first."

Nutkin looked at his pile of building materials, then at Ruby's sad little face. He scratched behind his ear with his tiny paw, thinking hard.

"Hmm," said Nutkin. "This is quite a problem! We both want the same spot."

Ruby nodded. "My grandmother always built her nest facing east for the warm morning sun. It's a robin tradition."

"And my family has always built nests high up for safety," Nutkin replied. "That's our squirrel way."

They sat quietly for a moment, listening to the gentle rustling of leaves in the breeze. Nutkin's whiskers twitched as he got an idea.

"Wait! What if we share?" Nutkin suggested, his eyes bright with excitement. "This branch is big enough for both of us!"

Ruby tilted her little head. "Share? How would that work?"

Nutkin scampered down to Ruby's level. "Well, you need the east side for morning sunshine, right? And I need to be high up. What if you build on the east end of the branch, and I build on the west end? We could both have what we need!"

"That sounds wonderful!" Ruby chirped, flapping her wings happily. "But I don't know how to build with twigs as big as yours."

"And I don't know how to weave grass as neatly as you do," Nutkin admitted. "Maybe we could teach each other?"

Ruby's eyes sparkled. "Yes! I can show you how robins weave soft grass into cozy circles. It's a special technique my mother taught me."

"And I can show you how squirrels stack twigs to make strong, sturdy walls," Nutkin said proudly. "My father taught me that trick!"

Working together, they started building. Ruby showed Nutkin how to bend grass gently and weave it in tiny circles. "See? Like this - over, under, over, under," she demonstrated with her small beak.

Nutkin taught Ruby how to choose the strongest twigs and fit them together like puzzle pieces. "The secret is finding twigs that curve the same way," he explained, arranging them carefully with his nimble paws.

As the sun moved across the bright blue sky, their homes took shape. Ruby's nest was perfectly round and cozy on the east side, catching the golden morning light. Nutkin's nest was sturdy and safe on the west side, with a wonderful view of the whole backyard.

"Look what we built together!" Ruby sang happily.

"It's even better than what I imagined building alone," Nutkin agreed, munching on an acorn. "We both got what we needed, and we learned new things too!"

From that day on, Nutkin and Ruby were the best of neighbors, sharing building tips and enjoying their perfect branch together.

The End.
