# Benny Bear's Big Mistake

Benny Bear climbed the creaky wooden stairs to Grandma Bear's quiet attic. Dust danced in the golden sunbeams that streamed through the small window. The attic smelled like old books and lavender.

"Wow!" Benny whispered. Boxes filled with treasures sat everywhere. Old photo albums, colorful quilts, and shiny music boxes covered the floor.

Grandma Bear had asked Benny to help organize the attic. "This is called volunteering," she had explained. "When we help others, we feel good inside."

Benny picked up a beautiful snow globe. Inside, tiny white flakes swirled around a little house. He shook it gently and watched the magic happen.

"So pretty!" he said, turning it over and over.

CRASH!

The snow globe slipped from his furry paws. Glass scattered across the wooden floor like tiny diamonds. Water pooled everywhere.

Benny's brown eyes filled with tears. His bottom lip trembled. "Oh no, oh no, oh no!"

He heard Grandma Bear's footsteps on the stairs. Quickly, Benny pushed the broken pieces under a dusty box with his paw. His heart thumped loudly in his chest.

"How's my helper doing?" Grandma Bear asked sweetly as she reached the top step.

Benny looked down at his paws. "Um... good, Grandma."

Grandma Bear's nose twitched. She smelled something different. Her wise eyes spotted the wet spot on the floor.

"Benny, dear," she said gently, "did something happen?"

Benny's ears drooped. He felt scared and ashamed. "I... I broke your snow globe, Grandma. I'm sorry! I tried to hide it because I was scared you'd be mad."

Grandma Bear's face softened. She sat down on an old wooden chair and patted her lap. "Come here, little one."

Benny climbed onto her warm, fluffy lap. She smelled like cookies and kindness.

"Thank you for telling me the truth," Grandma Bear said, stroking his soft fur. "That took courage."

"But I broke your special thing," Benny sniffled. "Aren't you angry?"

Grandma Bear hugged him tight. "Oh, sweetheart, I forgive you completely. Accidents happen to everyone. The snow globe was just a thing, but you are much more important to me."

Benny looked up with surprise. "Really? You're not mad?"

"Not at all," Grandma smiled. "When we forgive someone, it means we still love them even when they make mistakes. Everyone deserves forgiveness."

Benny felt the heavy feeling in his chest float away like a balloon. "I promise to be more careful, Grandma."

"I know you will," she said. "Now, let's clean this up together. That's what families do."

They carefully picked up each piece of glass. Benny learned that cleaning up mistakes was part of growing up. As they worked, Grandma Bear told him stories about times she had made mistakes too.

"Even grown-ups break things sometimes," she chuckled. "What matters is that we learn and try our best."

When the attic was tidy again, Benny felt proud. He had helped organize three whole boxes of books. Volunteering felt wonderful, especially with Grandma Bear.

"Thank you for forgiving me and teaching me," Benny said, giving her the biggest bear hug ever.

"Thank you for being honest and helping me today," Grandma Bear replied. "You're my very best volunteer."

As they walked downstairs together, Benny learned that forgiveness makes hearts feel light and love grow stronger. He couldn't wait to volunteer in the attic again tomorrow.

The End.
