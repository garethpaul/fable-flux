# Cubby’s Little Lab Plan

In a cozy house on Maple Street, there was a quiet basement lab. The floor was cool and smooth. The lights hummed softly. Bottles clinked on shelves, and a little fan went whirr-whirr. In the middle sat a friendly bear cub named Cubby. Cubby had soft brown fur and big curious eyes.

Cubby held a bright blue notebook. “Today I have a big plan,” Cubby said. “I will learn how to clean water for our garden. I will make a tiny filter. That is my goal!”

Cubby took a deep breath. The air smelled like soap and lemon from the clean counters. “First, I will write my steps,” Cubby said. Cubby wrote with a squeaky pencil:
1. Gather friends
2. Gather safe things
3. Build the filter
4. Test the water
5. Share and clean up

Cubby smiled. “A goal is a promise to try,” Cubby whispered. “I can do it, step by step.”

“Hello!” chirped Pip the sparrow from the window grate. Pip’s feathers were soft gray and brown. “What are you doing?”

“I have a goal,” Cubby said, holding up the blue notebook. “I want to make dirty water clean. Will you help?”

Pip hopped in, tiny feet tapping. “Yes! My eyes are sharp. I can look for small bits.”

“Tink-tink!” went a shell on the table. It was a little turtle named Tika, with a green shell and bright eyes. “I heard ‘water.’ I love ponds,” Tika said. “I will help too. I can be patient and careful.”

Then Benny the bunny peeked down the stairs. His ears were long and soft. “Is this the helper club?” he asked. “I can carry things. I am strong.”

Cubby clapped. “We are a team! We are all different. That helps us reach our goal.”

Step one was done.

“Step two,” Cubby read. “Gather safe things.” The friends sniffed and looked around. The lab had clean jars, soft cloth, pebbles smooth as eggs, sand like sugar, and a big spoon.

“This cloth feels fuzzy,” Benny said, rubbing it on his cheek.

“The sand is fine and tickly,” Pip chirped, tapping it with his beak.

“The pebbles are cool and round,” Tika said, holding one on her shell.

Cubby made a neat pile. “We will need a clear jar, pebbles, sand, and cloth,” Cubby said. “We will also need a strainer and a cup.” Cubby checked the list. “Step two is done.”

“Step three,” Cubby said. “Build the filter.” Cubby washed paws. Tika washed flippers. Benny washed paws. Pip shook wings. They sang a tiny song: “Wash, wash, scrub-a-dub! Clean hands for a clean job!”

Cubby put the cloth inside the jar like a soft bed. Benny poured a layer of pebbles. Clack, clack, clack. Tika sprinkled sand on top. Shhhhh. Pip pecked a teeny hole in the cloth for a drip-drip exit. “I can do small pecks,” Pip said proudly.

The filter stood tall and tidy. Cubby drew a little smiley face on the jar. “We built it!” Cubby said. “We are careful and safe.”

“Step four,” Cubby read. “Test the water.” Cubby poured a cup of dirty water with tiny leaves and specks. The water smelled like old leaves and wet dirt. “I do not like the smell,” said Benny, wrinkling his nose.

“Let’s try,” said Cubby. The water went drip… drip… drip through the sand and pebbles. The lab was quiet. They listened. Drip… drip… drip. The clean water came out into a clean cup. It looked clear like glass.

Pip peered closely. “I see no specks!”

Tika touched the cup. “It feels cool.”

Benny sniffed. “It smells like nothing. Much better!”

Cubby smiled wide. “Our goal is almost done!” But Cubby paused. “We must check one more time.” Cubby held up a white paper. “We will use this test strip.” The strip changed color just a little. “Safe for plants,” Cubby read. “Yay!”

Benny hopped high. “We did it!”

Cubby shook a paw. “We did it together. We set a goal, made steps, and finished each step.”

“Step five,” Cubby said. “Share and clean up.” Pip flew to the window and called, “Neighbors! We made a tiny water helper!” The neighbors peeked in—Mina the mouse, Jojo the frog, and Kiki the kitten. They all looked different and smiled.

Cubby showed the jar. “This is a filter. It helps plants by cleaning water. We must also take care of our streams and ponds. We can pick up trash. We can turn off taps.”

Kiki raised a paw. “I can pick up bottles.”

Jojo croaked, “I can tell my pond friends.”

Mina squeaked, “I can turn off the tap when I brush.”

Cubby nodded. “These are great little goals. Let’s write them!” Cubby wrote new steps for each friend. Small goals are still goals.

They all helped tidy the lab. Bottles went clink as they went back on shelves. The cloth went swoosh in the wash bin. The floor felt smooth and clean again.

Pip chirped, “How do you feel, Cubby?”

Cubby looked at the blue notebook. Every step had a check mark. Cubby’s chest felt warm and proud. “I feel happy,” Cubby said. “I made a plan. I asked my diverse friends. We worked as a team. We finished our goal. We helped nature.”

Tika smiled. “Goals help us go, little by little.”

Benny wiggled his ears. “Next goal: carrot snacks!”

Everyone laughed. Cubby closed the notebook gently. “My big lesson,” Cubby said, “is to make a clear goal, make small steps, and do them one at a time. With different friends and kind teamwork, we can finish our tasks and care for our world.”

The friends shared sweet carrot sticks and cool water. The fan went whirr-whirr. The lights hummed softly. The basement lab felt bright and brave.

The End.
