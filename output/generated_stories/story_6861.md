# Ellie's Garden Helper Day

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved spending time on Grandma Rose's sunny balcony garden. The colorful flowers smelled so sweet, and the green leaves felt cool under her tiny trunk. Today was special - Grandma Rose needed helpers to plant new seeds!

"Good morning, Ellie!" called Grandma Rose, wearing her big sun hat. "Would you like to volunteer to help me plant tomatoes today?"

Ellie's ears flapped with excitement. "Yes, yes! I want to help!" she trumpeted softly.

Grandma Rose smiled and pointed to different spots around the balcony. "First, we need to follow directions carefully. Can you help me gather the red pots from that corner?"

Ellie nodded eagerly and wrapped her little trunk around a pot. But oh no! The pot was heavier than she thought. It wobbled and almost fell!

"Don't worry, dear," said Grandma Rose gently. "Sometimes big jobs need teamwork. Let's work together."

Grandma Rose held one side of the pot while Ellie held the other with her trunk. Together, they carried it safely to the planting table. The pot felt much lighter when they cooperated!

"Hooray!" cheered Ellie. "We did it together!"

Next, Grandma Rose showed Ellie the tiny brown seeds. "These will grow into big, red tomatoes! But first, we need to fill the pots with soft soil. Can you help me scoop?"

Ellie tried to use the big shovel, but her trunk wasn't quite strong enough. She felt a little sad. "I can't do it," she sighed.

"Remember, persistence means trying again," encouraged Grandma Rose. "Let's find a way that works for you."

Grandma Rose found a smaller scoop that fit perfectly in Ellie's trunk. "Try this one!"

Ellie scooped and scooped, filling the pot with dark, rich soil that smelled like rain. Her trunk got a little dirty, but she didn't mind. Working together felt wonderful!

"Now comes the most important part," said Grandma Rose. "We need to follow directions exactly. First, make a small hole with your trunk. Not too deep!"

Ellie carefully poked a tiny hole in the soil. Then Grandma Rose showed her how to drop in one small seed and cover it gently.

"Pat, pat, pat," they said together as they covered the seed with soil.

They planted five pots together, taking turns and helping each other. When Ellie's trunk got tired, Grandma Rose helped. When Grandma Rose needed the watering can, Ellie brought it over.

"Last step!" said Grandma Rose. "Let's water our seeds together."

They held the watering can together, sprinkling cool water that sparkled in the sunshine. The drops made tiny "plink, plink" sounds on the soil.

"We make a great team!" said Ellie, dancing a little happy dance on the balcony.

Grandma Rose hugged Ellie with her warm arms. "Yes, we do! When we cooperate and help each other, we can do amazing things. And because you volunteered to help and followed directions so well, our garden will grow beautiful tomatoes!"

Ellie looked at their row of planted pots and felt proud. Her trunk might be small, but when she worked with others and kept trying, she could help make something wonderful grow.

"Can we plant more seeds tomorrow?" asked Ellie hopefully.

"Of course, my dear helper," laughed Grandma Rose. "Gardens always need friends who work together!"

The End.
