# Ella’s Big Think in the Living Room

The playful living room was full of colors and cozy sounds. Sunlight made warm squares on the carpet. Crayons lay in a rainbow row on a low table. A small ball sat by a basket. Soft music hummed, like a friendly bee.

A gentle elephant calf named Ella stood in the middle of the room. Her ears were big and soft, and her tiny tail swished-swish with feelings. She smelled the crayons. They smelled like sweet wax. She bounced the ball. Thump, thump. She felt excited and a little worried all at once.

“I want to learn so many things,” Ella said, her voice like a small trumpet. “I want to draw. I want to play ball. I want to know how.”

Her big feet tapped the rug. Tap. Tap. Tap.

Ella picked up a purple crayon. “I will make art,” she said. She pressed the crayon. Scritch-scratch. She made a circle. Oops! The line wiggled. The circle looked like a potato.

Ella’s ears drooped. “Oh no,” she whispered. “I don’t know how to make it round.”

She put the crayon down and picked up the ball. “I will try sports,” she said. She rolled the ball with her foot. The ball bumped into the basket and bounced away. Boing! It rolled under a chair and made a soft thud.

Ella’s tummy felt fluttery. “I don’t know how to do this either,” she said. A tiny tear tickled her cheek.

She sat. The carpet felt fuzzy on her legs. She could hear birds outside, cheep-cheep, and smell sunshine and soap. She closed her eyes and took a slow breath in. Sloooow. She let it out. Hoo.

“I need help,” she said. “I will ask. I can use my words.”

A tall giraffe pillow leaned on the couch. A teddy bear sat in a basket. A potted plant waved leaf-hands in the breeze from the window fan. The fan went whirr-whirr, like a kind whisper.

Ella looked up. “Living room, can I learn?” she asked. It felt silly, but it felt brave too.

The teddy bear looked soft and wise. Ella hugged the bear. “Teddy, I want to know. I want to learn art. I want to learn sports. Can you help me practice?”

The bear did not talk, but hugging made Ella feel strong and calm. She wiped her tear with her trunk. “I can ask a grown-up,” she said. “And I can try small steps.”

Ella stood tall. “I will be my own helper,” she said. “I will tell my needs.” She called toward the hallway, “Excuse me! I need help learning!”

A kind voice floated back, “I hear you, Ella! What do you need?”

“I need to learn how to make a round circle and how to roll a ball to the basket,” Ella said. “Can we practice, please?”

“Great asking,” the voice replied. “Let’s start with knowledge. First, we watch and think. Then we try.”

Ella smiled. She tried the circle again. “I will use a light touch,” she told herself. She held the crayon gently. She moved her trunk slow. Scritch… scratch… around and around. The line grew smoother.

She giggled. “It’s rounder!” She added eyes and a smile. “Hello, Circle Friend!”

She made more shapes. A triangle—one, two, three sides. A square—one, two, three, four. She counted aloud. “Numbers help me know.” She mixed colors too. Blue and yellow swirled into green. “Art is knowledge,” she said. “I can learn by looking, thinking, and trying.”

Now the ball. Ella set it in front of the basket. She tapped it with her foot. “Too strong?” she wondered. Tap. The ball rolled gently. It kissed the basket edge and slipped in. Plop!

Ella’s trunk curled with joy. “I did it!” She made it a game. She moved a little farther back. Tap. Roll. Plop! She tried from the side. Tap-tap. Roll. Boing off the basket, then in. “Sports is practice,” she said. “I can learn with small steps.”

She took breaks for sips of water. The cup was cool. The water tasted fresh. She felt good and safe.

Soon, the paper was full of shapes and colors. The basket had many balls inside. The living room felt extra bright and happy, like it was cheering. Claps seemed to echo in Ella’s heart. Clap, clap, clap!

Ella looked at her art. “I used knowledge for art,” she said softly. She looked at the basket. “I used knowledge for sports.” She touched her chest. “I used my brave voice to ask for help. That’s self-advocacy.”

The kind voice said, “Ella, what did you learn today?”

Ella stood tall. Her ears made a gentle flap-flap. “I learned to think first, try next, and practice again,” she said. “I learned to ask for help with my words. I learned that knowledge grows when I try.”

She drew a big golden sun in the corner of her paper, with long, happy rays. She rolled the last ball with a soft tap. Plop! She giggled, a sweet trumpet-laugh.

“Learning feels good,” Ella said. “I can keep learning, one small step at a time.”

The living room glowed with late afternoon light. The crayons smelled cozy. The ball was warm from her foot. Ella hugged the teddy and whispered, “Thank you for listening.” She hugged herself too.

Then she placed her art on the wall with a gentle pat and lined up the balls in the basket. “I know more now,” she said. “And I can keep going.”

The End.
