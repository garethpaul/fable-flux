# Ellie's Rainbow Letters

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved to climb up to the rooftop of her apartment building. The rooftop was her special place where she could see the whole colorful city below. Today, she brought her big box of crayons and paper to write letters.

"I want to write to everyone in my neighborhood!" Ellie trumpeted happily. She looked down at all the different houses and buildings. She could smell delicious foods cooking - spicy curry, sweet bread, and something that smelled like cinnamon.

Ellie started writing her first letter with a bright red crayon. "Dear Friends," she wrote in big, wobbly letters. But then she stopped. "Oh no," she worried. "What if they don't understand my letter? Mrs. Chen speaks Chinese, and Mr. Ahmed speaks Arabic, and Rosa's family speaks Spanish."

Ellie's trunk drooped sadly. She wanted to share her letters with everyone, but she only knew how to write in English. The warm sun felt good on her gray skin, but her heart felt heavy.

Just then, she heard footsteps on the rooftop stairs. Up came Mrs. Chen with her little granddaughter Mei, Mr. Ahmed with his son Omar, and Rosa with her baby brother.

"Hello, Ellie!" called Rosa. "What are you doing up here?"

"I wanted to write letters to share with everyone," Ellie said quietly. "But I don't know how to write in your languages. I'm sorry I can't include everyone."

Mrs. Chen's eyes twinkled. "That's very thoughtful, dear. But did you know that kindness speaks every language?"

"What do you mean?" asked Ellie, perking up her big ears.

"Show me your letter," said Mr. Ahmed gently.

Ellie held up her paper. Even though the words were in English, she had drawn colorful pictures all around them - a smiling sun, happy flowers, and children holding hands.

"Look!" said little Mei, pointing at the pictures. "I understand the happy sun!"

"And I see the flowers!" added Omar. "They're beautiful!"

Rosa clapped her hands. "The children holding hands look just like all of us!"

Mrs. Chen smiled. "You see, Ellie? Your pictures tell a story that everyone can understand. Art and kindness are languages that bring people together."

"Really?" Ellie's eyes grew wide and bright.

"Really!" said Mr. Ahmed. "And we can help you learn some words in our languages too. Then your letters will be even more special."

Mrs. Chen taught Ellie how to write "friend" in Chinese. Mr. Ahmed showed her how to write "hello" in Arabic. Rosa helped her write "love" in Spanish.

Soon, Ellie's letter was covered with words and pictures from many different cultures. The rooftop filled with laughter as everyone worked together, sharing crayons and teaching each other new words.

"This is the most beautiful letter ever!" said Ellie, looking at their colorful creation. "It has a little bit of everyone in it!"

"Just like our neighborhood," said Rosa. "We're all different, but we're all friends."

As the golden sunset painted the sky, Ellie realized something wonderful. She didn't need to know every language to share kindness. Pictures, smiles, and caring hearts could bring everyone together.

From that day on, Ellie's rooftop became a special place where all the neighborhood children came to write, draw, and learn about each other's cultures. And every letter they made together was more beautiful than the last.

The End.
