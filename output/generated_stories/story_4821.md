# Oliver Owl's Big Feelings Day

Oliver the wise old owl perched on his favorite branch outside the cozy nursery window. The morning sun painted golden stripes across the soft yellow walls, and he could hear little voices inside getting ready for their day. Oliver loved watching over the children, but today something felt different in his feathered chest - a tight, grumbly feeling that made his wings feel heavy.

"Hoot, hoot," Oliver sighed, ruffling his brown and white feathers. All morning, the children had been extra loud and busy. Baby Emma was crying, toddler Sam was banging his toy drums, and the older children were running around playing tag. The sounds bounced off the nursery walls like ping-pong balls, making Oliver's head feel fuzzy and his heart beat fast.

"Why am I feeling so cranky?" Oliver wondered aloud, his big golden eyes blinking slowly. He noticed his talons gripping the branch extra tight, and his usually smooth feathers were all puffed up. "I love these children, but right now everything feels too loud and too much."

Just then, Mrs. Rose, the kind nursery teacher, stepped outside. She looked up at Oliver with gentle eyes. "Hello there, wise Oliver. You look a bit ruffled today. Are you feeling overwhelmed?"

Oliver nodded his head up and down. "Hoot, yes! I want to help the children, but I feel all mixed up inside. My feathers feel prickly, and I want to fly far away, but I also want to stay and watch over everyone."

Mrs. Rose smiled warmly. "Even wise owls need to take care of themselves, Oliver. When we feel overwhelmed, our bodies are telling us something important. What do you think you need right now?"

Oliver closed his big eyes and took a deep breath, feeling the cool morning air fill his chest. "I think... I think I need some quiet time. Maybe I could fly to the peaceful oak tree for a little while?"

"That sounds very wise," Mrs. Rose said. "Taking breaks when we need them isn't giving up - it's taking good care of ourselves. When we rest, we can come back feeling better and ready to help others."

Oliver felt his tight chest begin to relax. "But what if the children need me while I'm gone?"

"The children will be safe with me," Mrs. Rose assured him. "And you'll be a much better helper when you feel calm and peaceful again. Sometimes the kindest thing we can do is listen to our feelings and give ourselves what we need."

Oliver spread his beautiful wings wide, feeling the tension melt away like morning dew. "You're right! I can take a quiet break, do some gentle stretching, and maybe even take a little nap in the sunshine."

He flew gracefully to the tall oak tree across the garden, where the leaves whispered soft songs in the breeze. Oliver found a perfect sunny spot, closed his eyes, and took slow, deep breaths. He felt his body relax from his beak to his tail feathers.

After his peaceful rest, Oliver felt like himself again - calm, wise, and ready to watch over his little friends. He flew back to his branch outside the nursery window, where the children were now having quiet story time.

"Feeling better?" Mrs. Rose asked with a knowing smile.

"Much better!" Oliver hooted softly. "I learned that taking care of myself helps me take better care of others. When I feel big feelings, I can take a break, breathe deeply, and do what my body needs."

From that day on, whenever Oliver felt overwhelmed, he remembered that it was okay to take breaks, ask for help, and listen to his feelings. And the children in the nursery learned too, watching their wise owl friend show them that self-care is one of the most important lessons of all.

The End.
