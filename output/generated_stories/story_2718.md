# Bunny Beats on the Balcony

On a sunny morning, a determined rabbit named Rumi hopped onto the balcony garden. The air smelled like mint and wet soil. Tiny tomatoes shone like red buttons. Wind chimes tinkled softly, ding-ding, ding-ding.

“I want to make music today,” said Rumi, ears standing tall. “I want my heart to feel calm and brave.”

Rumi tapped a flower pot. Tock. Tock. He rubbed a leaf. Swish. Swish. He plucked a string of chimes. Ting-ting!

“That sounds nice,” Rumi whispered. “But I want a song that makes me feel peaceful and strong.”

He tried to sing, la-la-la, but the sound came out squeaky and wobbly. Rumi’s nose twitched. “Oh no. My song sounds messy.”

Rumi felt a tight squeeze in his tummy. His ears drooped. The wind suddenly felt too loud. The smells felt too big.

Rumi took a slow breath in, and a slow breath out. “Let’s try self-care,” he said. “I can help my body feel okay.”

He placed a paw on his chest. “Breathe in like smelling a flower.” Sniff. “Breathe out like blowing a bubble.” Whooo. He did it three times. Sniff. Whooo. Sniff. Whooo. Sniff. Whooo.

Rumi felt softer inside. “Okay,” he said. “I can think. I can solve this.”

He looked around the balcony garden with curious eyes.

“What sounds do I have?” Rumi asked. He listened. The wind chimes went ting-ting. The watering can dripped, plip-plop. A bee buzzed by the marigolds, bzzzz. A soft leaf brushed his whiskers, shhh.

Rumi smiled. “I will make my song with garden sounds!”

He hopped to a small drum made from a turned-over bucket. Tock-tock. He tapped the terracotta pot with a wooden spoon. Tok-tok-tok. He shook the mint leaves. Shaka-shaka. He added a little bell. Ding!

Rumi tried to play everything at once. Tock-tok, tok-tok, shaka-shaka, ding! But it sounded crowded. His ears flopped. “Too many sounds,” he said.

He paused. He took another bubble breath. Whooo.

“Let’s use critical thinking,” Rumi said. “What is the problem? The sounds are all together. What can I do? I can take turns.”

He made a plan with his paws. “First, a steady beat. Then, a soft sound. Then, a happy ring.”

Rumi counted in a gentle voice. “One, two, ready, go.”

Tock. Tock. Tock. Tock. The bucket drum made a heartbeat sound. Rumi nodded. “This helps me feel steady.”

He added the leaf shaker between beats. Tock. Shhh. Tock. Shhh. It sounded like rain kisses.

Then he added the bell at the end. Tock. Shhh. Tock. Shhh. Ding!

Rumi’s nose wiggled with joy. “I hear a pattern!” he said. “Beat, soft, beat, soft, ring.”

But just then, the wind blew hard. The chimes went TING-TING-TING-TING! The bee buzzed loud. BZZZZ! Rumi’s song broke. His tummy felt tight again.

He stopped. He sat down on a warm tile. The sun hugged his fur. He put his paws on his knees. He closed his eyes.

“It’s okay to feel upset,” Rumi whispered. “My feelings are important.” He named them. “I feel frustrated. I feel loud inside.”

He gave himself a kind hug. “I can take care of me.” He drank a sip of water. He nibbled a small carrot. Crunch. Crunch. He stretched his ears. He smiled a little.

When he felt ready, Rumi opened his eyes. “The wind can join my music,” he said. “I can listen and make space.”

He tried again. He waited for the wind to ding the chimes. Ding-ding. Then he played his beat after. Tock. He let the leaf sound whisper when the wind got quiet. Shhh. He rang the bell at the very end. Ding!

Rumi made a new pattern with the wind. Ding-ding, tock, shhh, ding. Ding-ding, tock, shhh, ding.

His heart felt calm and brave. His feet tapped. His ears danced. The bee buzzed by in time, bzzz, like a tiny friend singing along.

A little voice came from the doorway. It was Rumi’s neighbor, Mia the mouse. “I love your balcony song,” she squeaked. “Can I play too?”

“Yes!” said Rumi. “We can share.” He handed Mia two small spoons. “Tap soft on the pot after the leaf sound.”

They practiced together. Rumi counted. “One, two, ready, go.” The wind chimed. Ding-ding. Rumi tapped. Tock. The leaf whispered. Shhh. Mia tapped softly. Tip. And then the bell rang. Ding!

They smiled big. Their bodies felt relaxed and happy. The garden smelled sweet. The sun felt warm. The music felt just right.

Rumi looked at Mia. “Music helps me calm my feelings,” he said. “When I breathe, think, and listen, I make better sounds.”

Mia nodded. “Music is self-care,” she said. “It helps my heart feel safe.”

Rumi added, “And when a problem comes, like big wind, we can think of new ways. We can make the wind part of the song.”

They played their balcony song three more times, gentle and proud. Ding-ding, tock, shhh, tip, ding. Ding-ding, tock, shhh, tip, ding. On the last ding, they both giggled.

Rumi took a deep breath in, like smelling a flower. Sniff. He blew a bubble breath out. Whooo. He put a paw on his chest and felt it go thump-thump, slow and steady.

“I am determined,” he said. “I can care for myself. I can use my thinking. I can make music that helps me feel good.”

The wind chimes sang a soft yes. The bee hummed a happy goodbye. The balcony garden glowed with sound.

The End.
