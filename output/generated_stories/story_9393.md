# Ruby Rabbit Speaks Up

In the warm dining room with its cheerful yellow walls, Ruby Rabbit sat at the big wooden table. Her fluffy white ears twitched nervously as she looked at her dinner plate. Green broccoli trees covered everything. Not one tiny carrot in sight!

"Eat up, Ruby!" said Mama Rabbit, setting down a glass of milk. "Broccoli helps you grow big and strong."

Ruby's pink nose wiggled. She loved carrots - crunchy, sweet, orange carrots. But she felt too shy to ask for what she wanted. Her small voice seemed stuck in her throat like a whisper.

"I... I..." Ruby started, then stopped. She poked the broccoli with her fork. It smelled earthy and green.

Papa Rabbit noticed Ruby's sad face. "What's wrong, little bunny?"

Ruby looked down at her paws. "Nothing," she mumbled quietly, even though something was definitely wrong.

She tried eating one tiny broccoli piece. It tasted okay, but not like her favorite orange carrots. Her tummy felt empty and disappointed.

"May I please have some carrots too?" Ruby whispered so softly that nobody heard her.

Mama Rabbit was already walking back to the kitchen. Papa Rabbit was reading his newspaper. Ruby's request floated away like a quiet breeze.

Ruby felt frustrated. Her ears drooped down like wilted flowers. She wanted to speak louder, but being assertive felt scary and hard.

Then Ruby remembered something important. Last week, when she wanted to play outside, she had used her clear, confident voice. "Please may I go play?" she had asked. And it worked perfectly!

Ruby sat up straighter. Her ears perked up tall and proud. She took a deep breath that filled her whole chest.

"Mama! Papa!" Ruby said in her clearest, strongest voice. "I have something important to say!"

Both parents looked at her with surprised, happy faces.

"I like broccoli," Ruby continued bravely, "but I really love carrots too. May I please have some orange carrots with my dinner?"

"Of course, sweetie!" Mama Rabbit smiled warmly. "I'm proud of you for asking clearly. Let me get some carrots right away."

Papa Rabbit nodded approvingly. "Good job using your voice, Ruby!"

But then Ruby remembered something else. She had been quiet and maybe a little rude earlier.

"I'm sorry I didn't speak up sooner," Ruby said honestly. "I should have asked nicely from the beginning instead of staying quiet and feeling grumpy."

"Thank you for apologizing," said Mama Rabbit, returning with a bowl of bright orange carrot sticks. "Next time, remember that we want to hear what you need."

Ruby's eyes sparkled with joy. The carrots looked perfect - crispy and colorful. She took a big, satisfying crunch. The sweet taste made her ears wiggle with happiness.

"This is much better!" Ruby said, eating both broccoli and carrots. "Speaking up wasn't scary after all. It was actually easy!"

From that day forward, Ruby Rabbit always used her clear, confident voice to ask for what she needed. She learned that being assertive meant speaking kindly but clearly, and that grown-ups actually wanted to help when she communicated well.

And whenever she forgot to speak up right away, she always remembered to say "I'm sorry" and try again with her brave rabbit voice.

The End.
