# Finley Fox and the Busy Art Room

Finley was a curious fox kit with bright ears and a twitchy nose. Today, Finley padded into the creative art room. The room smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Sunlight splashed on the tables. Paint jars gleamed like little rainbows.

“Wow,” said Finley. “So many colors!”

Brushes clinked in a jar. Snip-snip went the safety scissors. Children chatted softly. Ms. Maple, the art teacher, smiled. “Hello, artists. Today we will make a big friendship banner!”

Finley’s tail swished. “I want to help,” Finley whispered. The banner was very long and very plain. It needed shapes, paint, and glue.

Ms. Maple clapped her hands. “We have three jobs. One, cut paper stars. Two, paint handprints. Three, clean brushes. We need volunteers.”

Finley’s ears perked up. Three jobs? Three choices! Finley’s belly did a little flip. “Which job should I choose?” he wondered.

Finley smelled the sweet paint. He heard the water splish-splash at the sink. He felt the soft paper on his paws. So many sounds. So many things to do.

“Finley,” said Ms. Maple kindly, “you may choose one job first. Think with your head and your heart.”

Finley took a deep breath. “Sniff in. Blow out,” he said to himself. The air tasted like oranges from the soap. “I can decide. I will listen and look.”

Finley looked around. Miri the mouse was near the scissors. She frowned. “These stars are tricky,” she squeaked. “I need a helper.”

Gus the goose stood by the paint. He flapped. “Honkkk! I love red! I love blue!” His big wings made little splat dots everywhere.

Tara the turtle stood at the sink. “I can clean,” she said slowly. “But the brushes are many.”

Finley’s heart tugged three ways. He wanted to paint. He wanted to cut stars. He wanted to clean brushes too. Finley’s paws wiggled.

“Hmm,” Finley said. “What do we need most right now?”

He listened. Snip-snip slowed. Miri sighed. “The banner needs stars first,” Finley whispered. “Then paint can go on the stars. And clean brushes can come after.”

Finley raised his paw. “I will help with stars!” he volunteered.

Ms. Maple nodded. “Good decision, Finley. You thought it through. Thank you for volunteering.”

Finley sat with Miri. The scissors felt cool. The paper felt smooth. Snip. Snip. Uh-oh. One star had a wiggly leg.

“It’s okay,” Miri said. “We can use it as a twinkle.”

Finley smiled. “Twinkle star!” He lined up the paper. He watched carefully. Snip. Snip. Better! Soon they had a little pile of stars. Yellow stars. Blue stars. Rainbow stars.

Gus honked from the paint table. “Help! My wing is too painty!”

Finley looked up. His ears twitched. “I want to help Gus,” he thought. “But I promised stars.” He took a deep breath. “I will finish my job. Then I will help.”

He turned to Miri. “How many stars do we need?”

Miri held up her tiny paws. “Ten!”

“Let’s count,” said Finley. “One, two, three…” They counted together until ten stars made a happy stack.

Finley bounced to Ms. Maple. “Stars are done! What next?”

Ms. Maple winked. “You listened and finished your task. Now choose again.”

Finley looked around. Gus still dripped red paint. Tara still washed slowly. The banner now had twinkly stars, but it needed handprints.

Finley sniffed. The paint smelled fruity. He looked at the sink. The water was gray. “If I help clean,” he thought, “everyone can paint better.”

Finley lifted his paw. “I will help clean brushes!” he volunteered again.

Tara smiled. “Thank you, Finley.”

Finley turned on the tap. Warm water rushed over his paws. Swish-swish went the brushes. The soap made tiny bubbles that popped. Pop! Pop! It tickled Finley’s nose. He giggled.

“Finley,” Tara said, “you are fast.”

Finley grinned. “We are a team.” Soon the brushes were clean and lined up like little soldiers.

Gus honked happily. “Now I can paint a purple heart!”

Finley looked at the banner. Stars sparkled. Handprints bloomed. Hearts and circles danced. But a corner was empty.

Finley’s eyes shone. “I have an idea,” he said. “We can stamp leaf prints with our paws! We can use the clean brushes to paint our paws and press-pat.”

Ms. Maple nodded. “That is resourceful, Finley. You used what we have.”

Finley painted his paw with soft green. It felt cool and slippery. Press-pat! A leaf print! The corner filled with leaf friends. Press-pat, press-pat! Miri, Gus, and Tara joined in.

At last, the banner was bright and full. The room smelled like soap and paint. The water was quiet now. The sun warmed their backs.

Ms. Maple gathered everyone. “Artists, look at our work.” She pointed to the stars, the handprints, the hearts, and the leaf prints. “Finley listened to what was needed. He made choices. He volunteered to help. He used what we had in a smart way.”

Finley’s cheeks felt warm. His tail swished slowly. “I was worried about choosing,” he said. “But I took a breath. I looked. I listened. I decided. Then I helped.”

Ms. Maple smiled. “That is how good decisions are made.”

Finley patted the banner gently. “When I listen and think, my choices help my friends.”

Everyone clapped. Clap, clap, clap!

The banner dried in the sun, full of color and care. Finley’s heart felt big and bright.

The End.
