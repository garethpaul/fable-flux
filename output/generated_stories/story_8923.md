# The Waiting Song of Sunny Squirrel

Sunny the squirrel lived in a cozy bedroom with soft blankets, a tiny pillow, and a window that showed the tall green trees outside. The bedroom smelled like warm laundry and pine cones. Sunny kept a small basket of acorns on the shelf and a bright yellow clock on the wall.

One sunny morning, Sunny bounced on the bed. “Today I will build my very own bedtime lantern,” Sunny said with a happy wiggle. “It will glow like fireflies and help everyone feel calm.”

Sunny gathered supplies: a cardboard tube, shiny paper, a safe little night light, and some friendly stickers shaped like stars. Sunny’s paws were quick, and the tail swished with excitement. “I want it done right now!” Sunny chirped.

But the tape was sticky. It clung to Sunny’s fur. The shiny paper crinkled. The cardboard tube rolled off the bed and thumped to the rug.

Sunny huffed, “Why is this taking so long?” The yellow clock ticked softly: tick-tock, tick-tock.

Sunny’s best friend, Pip the mouse, peeked in from a tiny door near the bookshelf. Pip wore a blue scarf. “Hi, Sunny! What are you making?” Pip squeaked.

“My bedtime lantern,” said Sunny. “But it’s not working. I want it finished fast.”

Pip climbed onto the bed and patted Sunny’s paw. “Maybe we can slow down. We can take one step, then another. We can be patient.”

Sunny took a deep breath. The bedroom air was warm and gentle. “Okay,” Sunny said. “One step at a time.”

Step one: cut the window shapes. Snip, snip went the scissors. Sunny went slow. Round window. Star window. Heart window. “I did it!” Sunny smiled.

Step two: smooth the shiny paper. “Press and glide,” Pip said. Together they pressed and glided. The paper made a soft shhh sound. No wrinkles!

Step three: tape it all together. “Tape is tricky,” Sunny said. The tape stuck to the tail again. “Oh no!”

Pip giggled kindly. “It’s okay. Let’s try again. We can be patient.”

Sunny tried again, slower. Sticky, stick, press. It worked! Sunny’s ears perked up. “Patience helps!” Sunny said.

Just then, they heard sniffles from under the bed. “Sniff, sniff.”

Sunny and Pip peeked down. It was Dot the ladybug, tiny and red. Dot’s small wings trembled. “I lost my cozy button bed,” Dot said. “I can’t sleep without it.”

Sunny felt a gentle tug in the heart. “We can help,” Sunny said. “But it may take time.”

Dot dabbed tears. “Okay,” she whispered.

Sunny looked around the cozy bedroom. There were books, blocks, and a basket of socks. “We need a plan,” Sunny said. “Let’s use teamwork. Let’s be patient, step by step.”

Pip nodded. “I will look in the sock basket,” said Pip.

“I will check behind the books,” said Sunny.

“I will look under the rug,” said Dot.

They searched slowly and carefully. Pip sniffed the basket. “Smells like cotton and lavender,” Pip said. No button bed.

Sunny slid books one by one. “I see a ribbon. I see a crayon. No button bed yet.” Sunny’s tail twitched, but Sunny breathed in and out. “Patience,” Sunny whispered. “We will find it.”

Dot lifted the rug corner. “Dust tickles my nose,” she said. “Achoo! Still no bed.”

Tick-tock went the yellow clock. Sunny smiled at the steady sound. “The clock is singing the waiting song,” Sunny said. “Tick-tock, tick-tock. We can wait. We can try again.”

They looked in new places. Behind the curtain. Inside the toy box. Under the pillow. Still nothing.

Sunny’s shoulders drooped for a moment, but then stood up tall. “We won’t give up,” Sunny said. “We are strong and kind, and we can be patient.”

Pip pointed. “What about the lantern box?”

Sunny opened the empty box where the shiny paper had been. “Look!” Inside was a small round button bed, soft and red, with tiny white dots. “We found it!”

Dot buzzed with joy. “Thank you! You were patient with me. You didn’t hurry past my feelings.”

Sunny placed the button bed gently in Dot’s paws. “We helped together,” Sunny said. “Teamwork makes our hearts warm.”

Dot settled in with a happy sigh. “My bed feels safe,” Dot said. “Your bedroom is cozy. Your hearts are cozy too.”

Pip clapped tiny hands. “Now, let’s finish the lantern.”

Sunny nodded. “Yes. Slow and steady.”

They placed the little night light inside the lantern and set it on the bedside table. Sunny turned it on. Soft golden light floated around the room. Stars and hearts danced on the walls. The cozy bedroom glowed like a warm hug.

Sunny listened to the clock’s tick-tock and smiled. “Patience helped us build. Patience helped us find. Patience helped us be kind.”

Pip yawned and curled up in a tiny blanket. Dot tucked into the button bed. Sunny pulled up the soft covers and felt proud and calm.

Sunny whispered, “When we take our time, we do things well. When we wait with care, we help our friends.”

The lantern shone gently. The room smelled like warm blankets and pine cones. The tick-tock sang its quiet song.

Sunny closed sleepy eyes with a happy heart, knowing that patience, resilience, empathy, helping others, and teamwork made the day bright.

The End.
