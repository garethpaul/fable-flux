# Benny Beaver’s Balcony Plan

Benny was a busy beaver with bright eyes and a big, toothy smile. He lived in a small home with a tiny balcony garden. The balcony had pots of mint, soft basil leaves, little cherry tomatoes, and a shy sunflower that liked to peek at the sky.

One breezy morning, Benny stepped onto the balcony. He sniffed the fresh mint. “Mmm, tingly and cool,” he said. He touched the warm wooden railing. “Today feels like a good day to build.”

He looked at his plants. The pots were in a muddle. The tomato vines drooped. The sunflower leaned on the railing like a sleepy friend.

Benny put his paws on his hips. “I have a big idea,” he said. “I will build a tiny plant shelf. My plants need a cozy place to grow.”

He felt a wiggly feeling in his tummy. It was not fear. It was excitement. “I can do it,” he whispered. Then he smiled. “First, I make a goal.”

Benny took out a small chalkboard. He drew three boxes.

“Goal: Build a plant shelf for the balcony,” he said out loud.

He tapped the first box with his chalk. “Step one: Plan.” Tap. “Step two: Gather.” Tap. “Step three: Build.”

Benny nodded. “One step at a time.”

He sat on a soft cushion and planned. He sketched a simple shelf with two levels, one for the herbs and one for the bigger plants. He imagined the smell of basil, the tickle of tomato vines, the golden face of the sunflower smiling up high.

“Plan is done,” he cheered, and he drew a happy star.

Next came Gather. Benny opened his little toolbox. Click! The latch made a crisp sound. Inside were tiny nails, a small hammer, a measuring tape, and two smooth boards he had saved. He also found some sturdy twine.

He measured the balcony space. The tape went shhhk…shhhk as it slid out. “Not too long, not too short,” Benny said. He measured the boards. “Just right.”

He carried everything to the balcony. The boards felt warm from the sun. The hammer felt heavy but friendly in his paw. “I can do this myself,” Benny said. His heart felt strong.

He took a deep breath. The air smelled like wet soil and sweet basil. The city hummed softly below. Birds chirped on the railing, curious and bright.

Benny talked to his plants. “I am building you a new home. Please be patient.”

Then he began to Build. Tap-tap-tap went the hammer. The tiny nails clicked like raindrops on the wood. Benny kept his paws steady. He hummed a little building song.

“Step by step,” he said. “I can do it. I can do it.”

One board wobbled. “Oh!” said Benny. His tummy fluttered. He stopped and looked at his goal on the chalkboard. He took another deep breath. “It’s okay. I will try again.”

He checked his plan. He measured once more. Shhhk. He tied twine to make the shelf snug and safe. Pull, pull, knot. The twine felt rough and strong. The shelf stood taller and steadier.

“Good job, me,” he said, and he winked at the sunflower. The sunflower seemed to perk up.

Benny tested the shelf with a gentle push. It did not wobble. “Yes!” he cheered. A happy bubble popped in his chest.

Now it was time to move the plants. “Careful,” Benny whispered. He lifted the mint pot. The leaves brushed his paw. They smelled fresh and cool. He put the mint on the top shelf.

Next came the basil. The leaves were soft and silky. “You go up here too,” said Benny. He set the basil beside the mint. The herbs looked like friends at a picnic.

He moved the tomatoes to the lower shelf. The little fruits glowed like red buttons. He gave them a stake to lean on. “There you go,” he said, patting the soil. “Stand tall.”

Last, he helped the sunflower. “You get a sunny spot, my tall friend.” He tied a gentle loop of twine to keep it straight. He felt proud and calm.

Benny stepped back and looked. The shelf was neat and strong. The plants stood happy in their new home. The balcony felt bigger and brighter. A sweet, green smell filled the air.

A sparrow landed on the railing. “Chirp-chirp! Busy day?”

Benny laughed. “Yes! I set a goal. I made a plan. I gathered what I needed. I built it myself.”

The sparrow tilted its head. “Chirp! Looks good!”

Benny’s cheeks felt warm. He put a little sticker star on the last box on his chalkboard. “Goal complete,” he said.

He poured water from his tiny can. The water made a soft glug-glug sound and sparkled in the sun. Drip-drops slid into the soil. The plants seemed to sigh, “Ahhh.”

Benny sat on his cushion with a cup of cool mint tea. He watched the leaves wiggle in the wind. He felt calm, brave, and strong.

“I can set goals,” Benny said. “I can take small steps. I can build with my own paws. When I try, I grow.”

He smiled at the sunflower and at himself.

The End.
