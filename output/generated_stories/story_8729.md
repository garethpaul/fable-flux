# Pippa the Pantry Parrot’s Plan

Pippa was a small, creative parrot with bright green feathers and a splash of sunny yellow on her wings. She loved to say, “Plan, plan, I can!” and flap her wings in happy little wiggles.

One rainy morning, Pippa perched on the doorknob and peeked into the pantry. The pantry smelled like warm oats, sweet bananas, and a tiny hint of cinnamon. Shelves held jars that clicked, bags that crinkled, and boxes that made soft scratchy sounds when she brushed them with her feathers.

Pippa said, “Squawk! My tummy says hungry. But I want to grow strong and brave. I need a plan!” She puffed up proudly. “I will make a Healthy Snack Plan!”

She hopped to a low shelf. A big cookie box winked at her. It smelled sugary and loud. Pippa tilted her head. “Cookie now?” she asked. “Or plan first?” She tapped her beak gently on the wood. Tap, tap. “Plan first!”

Pippa found a little notebook and a red crayon in a drawer. She loved to draw. She drew a big happy star. “Goal!” she chirped. “My goal is: a yummy, healthy snack to help me fly fast.” She said the words slowly, like stepping stones. “Yu-mmy. Heal-thy. Fly fast.”

She broke her goal into steps. “Step one,” she said, writing wobbly letters. “Pick good foods.” She looked around. Bright bananas glowed like small moons. Crunchy carrots whispered, “Crunch, crunch!” A bag of oats gave a soft rustle. A jar of peanut butter smelled nutty and warm.

“Step two,” Pippa said, making a square box on the page. “Make the snack.” She drew a spoon.

“Step three,” she added, making a smiley face. “Taste and check. Did my plan work?”

Pippa felt proud. Her chest felt warm and bubbly, like hot oatmeal. “I can do this,” she told herself. “I am smart. I am strong. I have a plan.”

She picked a banana. “Hello, banana,” she chirped. “You are sweet and soft.” She picked carrots. “Hello, carrots. You are crunchy and bright.” She scooped a little oats. “Hello, oats. You are cozy and kind.” She took a tiny spoon of peanut butter. “Hello, peanut butter. You are sticky and strong.”

Just then, a cookie box slid a little. Scritch-scratch. Pippa looked over. The cookies smelled like fireworks in a candy sky. Her tummy did a happy flip. “Maybe I could eat cookies now,” she whispered. Her feathers drooped. “But I made a plan,” she reminded herself. “My goal is a healthy snack to help me fly fast.”

Pippa took a deep breath. In…and out. The air tasted like cinnamon and brave. “I can wait,” she said. “I can follow my steps.”

She put oats in a small bowl. Plop, plop. She mashed the banana with a fork. Squish, squish. She mixed in tiny carrot curls. Shhh, the carrot ribbons slid like orange rain. She added a small spoon of peanut butter. Scoop, swirl. The bowl smelled sweet and nutty. Pippa hummed, “Mix, mix, mix. Plan, plan, plan.”

“Step two is done,” she said with a happy chirp. “Now step three. Taste and check!”

Pippa took a tiny bite. Mmm. The oats were cozy. The banana was soft and sunny. The peanut butter was brave and sticky. The carrots made a little crunch that sang. Her wings fluttered. “I like it!” she cried. “My plan worked!”

She took another bite and felt strong from beak to tail. “I did it,” she told herself. “I made a goal. I followed steps. I can do big things!”

Pippa flapped to a low perch and tried a short fly across the pantry. Whoosh! Her wings felt steady. She giggled. “Fast like a happy breeze!”

The cookie box whispered again, scritch-scratch. Pippa looked at it and smiled. “Hello, cookies. I can have you another day. Today, I met my goal.”

She drew a star next to her steps. Then she drew another star, even bigger. She said, “New goal: clean up the pantry!” She laughed a soft parrot laugh. “One small job at a time.”

She made a new plan:
- Step one: Put lids on jars. Click!
- Step two: Close bags. Crinkle-zip!
- Step three: Wipe the shelf. Swish-swish!
- Step four: Celebrate with water. Sip, sip!

Pippa followed each step. Click. Crinkle-zip. Swish-swish. Sip, sip. She felt calm and proud. The pantry was neat. The smells were fresh and friendly. Her heart felt like a bright drum. Boom, boom. Happy, happy.

She perched by the door and whispered, “I am Pippa. I make plans. I try my best. I take small steps. I can do healthy things.” She looked at her notebook and hugged it with her wings. “Goals help me grow.”

Outside, the rain slowed to a soft patter. Pitta-patta, pitta-patta. Pippa flapped her wings and sang, “Plan, plan, I can!” She knew she could use her steps for many things: bedtime, playtime, cleanup time, and snack time too.

Lesson learned: When we set a goal, take small steps, and cheer for ourselves, we grow strong inside and out. Healthy choices and proud hearts help us fly.

The End.
