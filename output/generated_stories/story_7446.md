# Bubbles Cleans the Lab

In a cozy house, down the creaky stairs, there was a basement lab. The lab smelled a little like lemons and a little like wet socks. Lights hummed softly. Beakers clinked on metal shelves. Machines blinked with tiny green dots.

Splash! A playful dolphin named Bubbles swam in a round glass tank in the middle of the lab. The tank had warm water, tickly bubbles, and a little rubber duck. Bubbles loved to play tag with the bubbles and sing, “Squeee! Squeee!”

Dr. Dot, a kind scientist with big round glasses, worked in the lab. She wore a white coat and blue gloves. “Good morning, Bubbles,” she said. “Today we test my soap sprayer. It makes hands super clean!”

Bubbles wiggled his tail. “I like clean! Clean feels fresh!” His nose twitched. “Pee-yew. But the lab smells funny.”

Dr. Dot sniffed. “Mm. A little. We must keep things clean. That is called hygiene. Hygiene keeps us healthy.”

Bubbles nodded. He dipped under the water and popped up with a splash. “I can help! I want to be a Clean Team helper!”

Dr. Dot smiled. “Wonderful. First, we wash hands.” She pressed a button. The soap sprayer went whirr… whirr… pfft. A tiny bubble burped out and popped on Dr. Dot’s glove. “Oh,” she said. “That’s not enough soap.”

“I will try!” Bubbles squeaked. He tapped the tank button with his nose. The sprayer went whirr… whirr… pffffft. One wiggly bubble rolled down and plopped. Not enough.

Bubbles frowned. “Hmm. It’s hard.”

Dr. Dot patted the tank glass. “We keep trying. That is called persistence. We try and try, and we learn.”

Bubbles took a big breath. The water felt cool on his fins. He listened to the hum of the lights and the soft drip… drip… drip from a leaky pipe. “I can do it,” he whispered.

Dr. Dot turned a knob. “Let’s check the soap bottle.”

Bubbles peeked through the glass. He saw a bottle with a blue fish sticker. “It looks full,” he said. He pressed the button again. Whirr… wheeze… pfft. Still just a tiny bubble.

Bubbles looked down. He saw suds clinging like white clouds to the bottom of the bottle. He blinked. “Oh!” he said. “The bottle is stuck. The soap is hiding.”

Dr. Dot tilted the bottle, but it didn’t pour. “Hmm. Maybe the tube is clogged.”

Bubbles tapped the glass with his nose. “We can clean the tube! Hygiene for the sprayer!”

Dr. Dot chuckled. “Yes! Clean helps clean.” She got a small brush. “We’ll need to turn off the sprayer and take out the tube.”

Bubbles watched closely. The brush made a tiny scratch-scratch sound. Little gray gunk came out. “Ew,” Bubbles said. “That gunk looks like mud.”

“It’s not mud,” said Dr. Dot. “It is old soap stuck in the tube.”

Bubbles wanted to say, “I cleaned it yesterday.” But his tummy felt twisty. He remembered he had splashed water on the sprayer yesterday and did not dry it. He had hoped no one would notice. He took a deep breath. “Dr. Dot,” Bubbles said softly, “I need to be honest. I made a big splash. I didn’t tell you. Maybe my splash made the gunk stick.”

Dr. Dot looked at him kindly. “Thank you for telling the truth, Bubbles. Honesty helps us fix things. It is safe to say, ‘I made a mistake.’”

Bubbles’ tummy untwisted. He felt warm and brave. “Next time I will dry the sprayer,” he said.

“Good plan,” said Dr. Dot. “Now let’s finish cleaning.” She brushed the tube. Bubbles helped by blowing little streams of water in his tank to match the scratch-scratch rhythm. Swish-swish! Scratch-scratch! It sounded like a tiny cleaning song.

They put the tube back. Dr. Dot held up her hands. “Ready?” she asked.

Bubbles nodded. “Ready!”

Whirr… whirr… whoooooosh! The sprayer puffed a soft cloud of lemony foam. It smelled bright and clean.

“Yay!” cheered Bubbles. He clapped his fins. Dr. Dot rubbed her hands with the foam. “We scrub palms,” she said, “backs of hands, between fingers, and thumbs. Then rinse!” Water splashed. Drip… drip… drip turned into happy splish-splash sounds.

“Hands clean!” Bubbles sang. He pressed his nose to the glass. He wanted clean too. Dr. Dot smiled. “Let’s clean your tank.” She used a gentle hose and a soft brush. Swish! The water shone like a mirror. The rubber duck looked new and bright.

Bubbles twirled. “I feel fresh! Clean helps me zoom!”

Dr. Dot nodded. “Hygiene keeps us healthy. We clean tools, we wash hands, and we tell the truth. And we keep trying.”

Bubbles grinned. “Today I learned three big things,” he said. “Hygiene, persistence, and honesty.”

“What do those mean to you?” asked Dr. Dot.

Bubbles counted on his fins. “Hygiene means we clean and keep the yuck away. Persistence means we try and try. Honesty means we tell the truth, even when it is hard.”

Dr. Dot gave a thumbs-up. “Perfect.”

The lab smelled like lemons now. The lights hummed a happy tune. The beakers clinked like tiny bells. Bubbles floated, belly-up, peaceful and proud.

“Clean Team!” he giggled. “Ready for the next adventure.”

“Tomorrow,” said Dr. Dot, “we test the toothbrush robot.”

Bubbles squealed. “I love brushes!”

They laughed together. The lab felt bright and safe.

The End.
