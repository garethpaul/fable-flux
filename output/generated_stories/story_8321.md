# Fern's Fair Forest Plan

Little Fern the deer fawn loved her cozy study corner. It was tucked behind a big oak tree, where soft sunlight filtered through green leaves. Today, she spread out her favorite books about the forest and munched on sweet clover.

"Munch, munch, munch," went Fern as she chewed the nutritious green leaves. The clover tasted like sunshine and made her feel strong and healthy.

Suddenly, she heard crying. "Boo-hoo! Boo-hoo!"

Fern's big brown eyes looked up from her book. She listened carefully with her soft ears. The sound came from near the berry bushes.

"Someone needs help," Fern said to herself. She carefully marked her place in the book and trotted toward the sound.

Behind the blueberry bushes, she found Chip the chipmunk sobbing. His tiny cheeks were wet with tears.

"What's wrong, Chip?" asked Fern gently, sitting down so she wouldn't seem too tall.

"The big raccoons took all our winter nuts!" Chip sniffled. "They said finders keepers, but we worked so hard to gather them. My family will be hungry when snow comes."

Fern's heart felt heavy. "That's not fair at all," she said. "Everyone deserves enough food for winter."

She thought about what she learned in her books about being fair and just. "Chip, show me where this happened."

They walked together to the old hollow log where Chip's family stored their nuts. Sure enough, it was completely empty. Nearby, Fern could see paw prints leading to the raccoons' den.

"I have an idea," said Fern. "But first, let's listen to everyone's side of the story."

At the raccoons' den, they found Rocky and his brothers sorting through piles of acorns, walnuts, and hazelnuts.

"Hello, Rocky," said Fern politely. "Chip says these nuts belong to his family."

Rocky's masked face looked surprised. "We found them in an empty log. Nobody was around!"

"But we were just getting fresh moss to line our storage!" squeaked Chip. "We've been collecting these nuts for weeks!"

Fern listened carefully to both sides. She could smell the earthy scent of the nuts and see how much work had gone into gathering them. Her nose twitched as she thought.

"I think I know a fair solution," said Fern. "Rocky, you didn't know the nuts belonged to Chip's family. And Chip, you didn't mark your storage clearly."

She stood up tall and proud. "Here's what's fair: Chip's family should get their nuts back because they gathered them first. But Rocky, your family can help gather more nuts, and we'll all share the work and the harvest."

Both families looked at each other, then at wise little Fern.

"That sounds fair," said Rocky slowly.

"Yes!" chirped Chip. "We can work together!"

For the rest of the afternoon, everyone worked as a team. They gathered nuts, sorted them by size, and made sure each family had enough healthy food for winter. Fern shared her knowledge about which nuts were most nutritious.

As the sun set, Fern returned to her study corner. She felt warm and happy inside, like drinking hot cocoa. She had learned that being fair meant listening to everyone and finding solutions that helped everybody.

"Justice means making sure everyone is treated fairly," she said to herself, opening her book again. "And when we work together and care for each other, the whole forest is happier."

The End.
