# Melody's Helping Song

In a cozy study corner by the big window, a little yellow bird named Melody loved to perch on the bookshelf. She had the most beautiful voice in the whole neighborhood! Every morning, Melody would sing sweet songs while children read their books below.

"Tweet-tweet-trill!" sang Melody as the sun painted golden stripes across the wooden floor. The warm light felt so nice on her tiny yellow feathers.

One sunny day, Melody noticed something different outside the window. Mrs. Chen, the elderly lady next door, was struggling to carry heavy grocery bags up her front steps. The bags looked so big and heavy!

"Oh my," chirped Melody softly. "Mrs. Chen needs help!"

Melody watched as young Pablo from across the street rode by on his red bicycle. She saw little Aisha playing with colorful blocks in her yard. Everyone was busy with their own activities, but nobody noticed Mrs. Chen needed help.

Melody's heart felt warm and fluttery. "I know what I can do!" she said, puffing out her little chest proudly.

She flew outside and perched on the fence post. Taking a deep breath, Melody began to sing the most beautiful, attention-getting song she had ever sung.

"Help-help-help! Someone needs a friend! Come-come-come! Let's all lend a hand!" Her melody was so sweet and clear that it floated through the air like musical butterflies.

Pablo stopped his bicycle and looked around. "What a pretty song!" he said, following the sound with his eyes.

Aisha looked up from her blocks, her dark eyes sparkling. "I hear singing!" she giggled, standing up and brushing grass from her purple dress.

Melody kept singing, pointing her tiny wing toward Mrs. Chen. "Look-look-look! A neighbor needs care! Share-share-share! Show her that you're there!"

Pablo saw Mrs. Chen first. "Oh! Mrs. Chen looks tired," he said, quickly parking his bike. He ran over with a big smile. "Hi, Mrs. Chen! Can I help carry your bags?"

"¡Gracias, Pablo!" said Mrs. Chen, her face lighting up with relief. "These bags are very heavy today."

Little Aisha toddled over too, her small hands reaching up. "I help too!" she said in her sweet voice. "I carry the light one!"

Mrs. Chen's eyes filled with happy tears. "You children are so kind! Pablo, you take the big bag. Aisha, here's a small bag of apples just for you."

Working together, the three neighbors carried all the groceries inside. Mrs. Chen's kitchen smelled like cinnamon and fresh bread, making everyone feel warm and happy.

"Thank you both," said Mrs. Chen, giving each child a gentle hug. "Would you like some fresh cookies I baked this morning?"

Melody watched from the window, her tiny heart bursting with joy. Her song had brought the neighborhood together! She saw Pablo and Aisha sitting at Mrs. Chen's kitchen table, sharing cookies and laughing together.

"Tweet-tweet-wonderful!" sang Melody proudly. "When we help each other, everyone feels happy!"

From that day on, whenever Melody saw someone who needed help in the neighborhood, she would sing her special helping song. And every time, friends would come running to lend a hand.

The study corner felt extra cozy that evening as Melody settled on her favorite book. She had learned that even the smallest voice can make the biggest difference when it calls others to help.

The End.
