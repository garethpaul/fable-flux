# Joey's Helping Hands

Little Joey the kangaroo loved to bounce and hop around Grandpa Roo's big garden. His soft brown fur sparkled in the morning sun as he played among the colorful flowers. But today, something was different in the old wooden garden shed.

"Oh my!" said Grandpa Roo, scratching his gray head. "Look at this messy shed, Joey. All my garden tools are scattered everywhere!"

Joey peeked inside the dusty shed. Shovels lay on the floor, flower pots were stacked in wobbly towers, and seed packets were sprinkled all around like confetti. The shed smelled like fresh dirt and old wood.

"It's so messy, Grandpa!" Joey said, wrinkling his little black nose. "How will you find anything?"

Grandpa Roo sighed. "I need to organize everything before the big garden party tomorrow. But my old back is too sore to bend and lift all day."

Joey's ears perked up. He had an idea! "Grandpa, I want to help you! Can I volunteer to clean the shed?"

"That's very kind, little one," Grandpa Roo smiled. "But this is a big job. Are you sure you can do it?"

Joey bounced excitedly. "Yes! I'm small, so I can reach into tiny spaces. And I'm fast at hopping around!"

Grandpa Roo taught Joey how to sort the tools. "Put all the shovels here, the watering cans there, and the flower pots on this shelf," he explained patiently.

Joey started working right away. Hop, hop, hop! He carried small tools in his tiny paws. But when he tried to lift a heavy watering can, it was too big for him.

"This is too heavy!" Joey said, feeling frustrated. His ears drooped down.

"Don't give up, Joey," encouraged Grandpa Roo. "What else could you try?"

Joey thought hard. Then his face lit up! "I know! I can drag it across the floor instead of lifting it!"

Slowly but surely, Joey dragged the watering can to its proper place. When he finished, he felt proud and strong.

Next, Joey organized the tiny seed packets. His small paws were perfect for this job! He sorted them by color - red tomato seeds, yellow sunflower seeds, and green bean seeds.

"Look, Grandpa! I made rainbow rows!" Joey called out happily.

As the day went on, Joey learned that volunteering felt wonderful. Every time he helped, his heart felt warm and fuzzy inside. The shed began to look neat and tidy.

"Joey, you're doing such a great job!" praised Grandpa Roo. "You decided to help, and you didn't give up when things got hard."

When they finished, the shed sparkled clean. All the tools hung in neat rows, and the seed packets stood in colorful lines. Joey felt tired but very happy.

"Thank you for volunteering to help me, Joey," said Grandpa Roo, giving him a big hug. "Because you helped, we'll have the best garden party ever!"

Joey bounced with joy. "Helping feels even better than playing, Grandpa! Can I volunteer to help you again tomorrow?"

"Of course!" laughed Grandpa Roo. "Helpers like you make the world a brighter place."

That night, Joey fell asleep with a big smile on his face, dreaming about all the ways he could help others.

The End.
