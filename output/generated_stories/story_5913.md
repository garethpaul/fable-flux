# Gigi's Honest Heart

High up in the tall oak tree, Gigi the giraffe lived in the most wonderful treehouse. Her long neck made it easy to peek out the round windows and see all her forest friends below. The treehouse had colorful walls, soft cushions, and shelves full of books and toys.

One sunny morning, Gigi was organizing her toy box when she found something that didn't belong to her. It was Benny Bunny's favorite red ball - the one with yellow stars that bounced so high!

"Oh my!" said Gigi, holding the ball carefully in her hooves. "How did this get here?"

She remembered playing catch with Benny yesterday near the big willow tree. The ball must have rolled into her bag when she wasn't looking. Gigi's tummy felt funny - not sick, but worried.

"I should give this back to Benny right away," she said to herself. "It's the right thing to do."

But then Gigi looked around her treehouse. She had been wanting a new bouncy ball for weeks. Her old blue one had a small hole and didn't bounce very well anymore.

"Maybe... maybe I could keep it just for today?" whispered Gigi. "Benny has lots of toys."

The red ball felt smooth and perfect in her hooves. She gave it a little bounce - *boing, boing* - and it flew up to touch the ceiling! It was much better than her old ball.

Gigi tried to play with the ball, but something felt wrong. Every bounce made her heart feel heavy. The bright yellow stars seemed to frown at her. Even the warm sunshine coming through her window couldn't make her feel happy.

"This isn't fun at all," sighed Gigi. "I know this ball belongs to Benny, and keeping it makes me feel sad inside."

Gigi looked at her old blue ball with the tiny hole. "You know what? I can fix you up and make you bounce again!" She found some colorful tape and carefully covered the hole. Then she decorated her ball with stickers of rainbows and flowers.

*Boing!* Her fixed-up ball bounced almost as high as before!

"There!" said Gigi happily. "Sometimes the best way to get something new is to improve what you already have!"

Gigi climbed down from her treehouse, holding Benny's red ball carefully. She found Benny sitting sadly by the pond, looking everywhere for his lost toy.

"Benny!" called Gigi. "I found your ball in my toy box. I think it rolled in by accident yesterday."

Benny's ears perked up and his nose twitched with joy. "My bouncy ball! Thank you so much, Gigi! I looked everywhere for it!"

"I'm sorry I didn't bring it back sooner," said Gigi honestly. "I really wanted to keep it, but that wouldn't have been right."

Benny gave Gigi the biggest bunny hug. "You're such a good friend, Gigi. Thank you for being honest!"

As they played together with both balls - Benny's red one and Gigi's newly decorated blue one - Gigi felt warm and happy inside. Her heart felt light and bouncy, just like the balls they were throwing.

"Being honest feels so much better than keeping something that isn't mine," thought Gigi. "And fixing my old ball was even more fun than I expected!"

From that day on, whenever Gigi found something that wasn't hers, she always returned it right away. And she learned that being creative and improving what she had was often better than wanting something new.

The End.
