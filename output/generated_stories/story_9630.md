# Joey's Big Dinner Problem

Little Joey the kangaroo bounced excitedly into the dining room. His tummy was rumbling like thunder, and he could smell something delicious cooking in the kitchen.

"Mama, I'm so hungry!" Joey called out, his nose twitching at the wonderful smells of carrots and fresh grass soup.

"Dinner will be ready soon, sweetie," Mama Kangaroo replied from the kitchen. "Please set the table for our family."

Joey hopped over to the tall dining table, but when he looked up, his ears drooped. The table was so high! Even when he stretched his little arms as far as they could go, he couldn't reach the top.

"Oh no," Joey whispered, feeling worried. "How can I help if I can't reach?"

He tried jumping as high as he could. Boing! Boing! But he still couldn't reach the table. Joey sat down and took three deep breaths, just like Mama had taught him when things felt hard.

"Think, Joey, think," he said to himself, tapping his furry chin. "There must be a way."

Joey looked around the dining room with curious eyes. He saw chairs pushed under the table, a wooden step stool by the window, and some thick books on a low shelf.

"Wait a minute!" Joey's eyes sparkled with an idea. "Maybe I can use something to help me reach higher!"

First, Joey tried dragging the heavy wooden chair. It made a loud scraping sound on the floor. "Errrrr!" But it was too heavy for his little paws.

Then Joey noticed the step stool. It was much lighter! He pushed it carefully across the room. "This might work!" he said hopefully.

Joey climbed onto the step stool and stretched up again. He was closer, but still not quite tall enough to put the plates on the table safely.

"Hmm," Joey thought. He looked at the thick books on the shelf. "I wonder..."

Very carefully, Joey carried two big books over to his step stool. He placed them on top, making sure they were steady and wouldn't slip.

"Perfect!" Joey cheered. Now when he climbed up, he could reach the table easily!

One by one, Joey placed the colorful plates around the table. He was so proud of himself for figuring out a solution. The yellow plate went here, the blue plate went there, and the red plate went right in the middle.

"Joey!" Mama Kangaroo hopped into the dining room carrying a steaming pot of soup. "You set the whole table! But how did you reach so high?"

Joey pointed proudly at his creation. "I used the step stool AND the books together! When one thing didn't work, I tried another way. And when that wasn't enough, I used them both!"

Mama Kangaroo smiled warmly. "What a clever and resourceful little joey you are! You stayed calm, thought carefully, and found a creative solution."

Papa Kangaroo bounced in just then. "Something smells wonderful! And look at this beautiful table!"

As the family sat down to enjoy their dinner together, Joey felt proud and happy. The soup tasted extra delicious because he had helped make dinnertime special.

"Remember, Joey," Mama said, "when you face a problem, take a deep breath, look around, and think of different ways to solve it. You have everything you need inside you to figure things out."

Joey nodded, his mouth full of yummy soup. He had learned that being resourceful meant using what was around him in new and creative ways. And the best part? He had done it all by himself!

The End.
