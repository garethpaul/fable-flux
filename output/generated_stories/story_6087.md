# The Fawn’s Rainbow Snack Plan

In a cozy home office, a gentle deer fawn named Dap sat on a soft rug. The room smelled like warm paper and a little like pine. Sunlight glowed on the desk where a small laptop hummed. Sticky notes made a bright, happy border around the screen. Click, click, clicked the keyboard.

Dap’s tummy gave a small rumble. “Hello, tummy,” Dap giggled. “You sound like a tiny drum.” The sound made Dap think about snacks. Dap wanted to be independent and choose a good one.

On the wall was a big world map. It had blue oceans and bright, colorful countries. “So many places,” Dap whispered. “Do animals and kids there like good snacks too?” Dap’s ears perked up with curiosity.

A friendly voice came from the laptop. It was Auntie Fern on a video call. “Good morning, Dap! How is my brave fawn?” she asked with a smile.

“I’m hungry,” Dap said. “I want a snack I can pick by myself. I want it to help me grow strong.”

“That is a great plan,” Auntie Fern said. “Let’s use the Rainbow Snack Plan. You choose colors for your plate!”

Dap’s eyes sparkled. “A rainbow on my plate?”

“Yes,” Auntie Fern said. “Red, orange, yellow, green, blue or purple, and something white or brown. Can you do that?”

“I can try,” Dap said. Dap felt proud and a little nervous too. Independence felt exciting and big.

Dap trotted to the kitchen cart by the door. The cart had small baskets for snacks. The air smelled like sweet fruit and crunchy grains. Dap spoke out loud, practicing good communication. “I’m choosing a red food. I will pick… a strawberry. It smells like sunshine!” Dap sniffed. “Mmm, sweet and soft.”

Dap looked again. “Orange… carrot sticks! They are bright and crunchy.” Dap tapped one on the plate. Tap, tap.

“Yellow,” Dap said. “A slice of banana. It feels smooth.” The banana made a soft plop on the plate.

“For green, I choose baby spinach,” Dap decided. “It feels cool and light.” The leaves made a whisper sound, shh-shh, as they slid onto the plate.

“Blue or purple…” Dap looked carefully. “Blueberries!” Dap picked three. “Little blue buttons.” They rolled, bup-bup-bup, and Dap giggled.

“White or brown,” Dap said. “A small scoop of oats in a cup. I can add milk.” Dap poured gently. Glug, glug. The oats made cozy smells, like warm morning.

Dap sat back on the rug with the rainbow plate. “Auntie Fern, I did it!” Dap called toward the laptop. “I chose my snack all by myself.”

Auntie Fern clapped softly. “Wonderful, Dap. You used color groups. That helps your body in many ways. Red and blue fruits can help your heart. Orange helps your eyes. Greens help your body feel strong. Oats give you energy to play and think.”

Dap looked at the world map again. “Do friends in other places eat rainbow foods too?”

“Yes,” Auntie Fern said. “Children everywhere enjoy colors. In Japan, kids may eat seaweed and rice. In Mexico, bright mango and corn. In India, yellow lentils. In Kenya, greens like sukuma wiki. Many places, many colors!”

Dap’s eyes grew wide. “Wow. We all need good food. We are different, and we are the same.”

“That’s right,” Auntie Fern said. “Now try mindful bites. Look, smell, taste, and tell me what you notice. Use your words.”

Dap took a strawberry bite. “It’s juicy. It tastes sweet and a tiny bit tangy.” Juice tickled Dap’s chin, and Dap licked it up, giggling.

Crunch! went a carrot stick. “Loud and crunchy. It tastes fresh, like a garden.”

A banana bite felt soft. “It is creamy and gentle,” Dap said.

A spinach leaf went shh on Dap’s tongue. “Leafy and calm,” Dap whispered.

A blueberry popped. “Pop! It’s sweet and a little tart.” Dap smiled at the sound.

Dap lifted the oat cup. “Warm, cozy, and filling. My tummy is happy.” The tiny drum quieted.

“You did wonderful observing,” Auntie Fern said. “How do you feel now?”

“I feel proud and full of energy,” Dap said. “I chose a balanced snack. I listened to my body. I used my words. And I learned about foods from around the world.”

Auntie Fern nodded. “That is nutrition, independence, and communication working together. You made a smart plan. You can teach others too.”

Dap looked at the sticky notes and picked up a crayon. “I will make a Rainbow Snack Plan sign for the home office,” Dap said. On a note, Dap drew a little plate with colored dots and wrote: “Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Blue/Purple, White/Brown.”

Tap, tap, tapped the crayon. Swish, swish went the strokes. The home office felt bright and peaceful. Dap hung the note by the laptop.

“When my tummy rumbles again,” Dap said, “I can follow my plan. And I can ask friends, ‘What colors are on your plate today?’”

“That is a kind question,” Auntie Fern said. “It helps others share and learn too.”

Dap took one last carrot crunch and gave a happy sigh. “Thank you, Auntie Fern.”

“You’re welcome, my brave fawn. Keep growing, keep tasting, keep talking,” Auntie Fern said.

Dap stood tall on the soft rug. The world map shimmered in the light. Dap felt connected to many places and many plates. Inside, Dap felt strong, kind, and ready for new days.

The End.
