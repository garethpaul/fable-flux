# Tessa Tortoise’s Stretchy Shed Day

In a cozy garden, by a row of bright flowers, stood a wooden garden shed. It smelled like fresh dirt and lemony soap. Inside lived Tessa the wise tortoise. Her shell was smooth and green, and her eyes were kind and twinkly.

“Good morning, little shed,” Tessa said, peeking out her door. She took a slow breath in. Sniff. The air smelled like mint leaves. She let the breath out. Ahhh. “Today, we move our bodies,” she whispered. “Exercise helps us feel strong and calm.”

Tap-tap! Someone knocked on the shed. It was Pip the mouse, holding a tiny paintbrush. “Hi, Tessa! Can we paint today?” Pip asked.

“We can paint, and we can move,” said Tessa with a smile. “We will exercise first. Then we will share art. Moving gets our bodies ready.”

Pip’s nose wiggled. “Okay! What do we do?”

Tessa slid onto her mat. It was soft like moss. “We start with gentle stretches,” she said. “Reach up like a tall sunflower.”

Pip reached high. “Ooo! I feel long!”

“Now we touch our toes,” Tessa said, bending slowly. “Hello, toes.” She giggled.

“Hello, toes!” Pip chirped, bending too.

They did slow shell-arches, arm circles like fluttering butterflies, and tiny turtle-steps across the shed. Step, step, step. Tessa counted, “One, two, three,” in a calm voice.

“Listen to your breath,” Tessa said. “In and out, like waves.” Whoosh, whoosh. Pip’s whiskers relaxed.

Tap-tap-tap! The shed door opened again. Benny the bunny bounced in, ears bobbing. “I heard exercise!” he squeaked. “Can I join?”

“Yes,” said Tessa. “We share this space. We move with care.”

Benny hopped in place, thump, thump. “Oops! I made a loud sound,” he said, blushing.

Tessa nodded kindly. “Strong hops can be soft hops. Let’s try soft.” She showed a gentle bend and a quiet spring. “Bend, breathe, hop… hush.”

Benny tried again. Thup. Much softer. “I did it!” he said.

They made a game called Garden Moves. Tessa called, “Be a tree!” They stood tall and still. The shed felt quiet and steady.

“Be a worm!” They wiggled on the mat and giggled.

“Be a wheelbarrow!” They rolled their arms in big circles. Swoosh, swoosh.

After the moves, Tessa placed a small bell on the floor. “Now we rest for three soft bells,” she said. Ding… ding… ding… They lay still, feeling their hearts slow down. Pip felt warm and calm. Benny felt light and happy. Tessa smiled. “Mindful rest helps our bodies after we move.”

Pip held up his paintbrush. “Is it art time now?”

“Yes,” said Tessa. She opened a box. Inside were paint pots, big paper, and sponges. The paints smelled a little like berries. “We will paint our favorite move,” she said. “We will share the colors and the space.”

Benny reached for the red. Pip reached too. Their paws bumped. They both froze.

Tessa watched with gentle eyes. “We can take turns,” she said. “Pip, you hold the red for three slow breaths. Benny, you hold the blue for three slow breaths. Then switch.” They breathed together. In… out… one. In… out… two. In… out… three. Swap!

Pip dabbed red dots. “These are my soft hops,” he said.

Benny swirled blue waves. “These are my breaths,” he said.

Tessa dipped a sponge in leafy green and pressed it in a circle. “This is my shell stretch,” she said. The sponge made a soft squish sound.

They painted and hummed. The paper shone with colors: red dots, blue waves, green circles, and yellow sun shapes. The shed felt warm and busy. The brush bristles tickled the paper. Swish, swish.

Soon, Pip’s tummy made a tiny rumble. “I’m hungry,” he said.

Tessa nodded. “We moved our bodies. We made art. Now we share a snack.” She opened a basket and passed out carrot rounds and apple slices. Crunch! Crunch! The apples were sweet and crisp.

Benny looked at the painting. “We did this together,” he said.

Tessa touched the corner with her careful paw. “Exercise helped our bodies. Mindfulness helped our hearts. Sharing helped our friends. Art helped our feelings shine.”

Pip smiled. “I like moving first. My hands feel steady when I paint.”

“Mine too,” said Benny. He bounced once, softly. “Can we do Garden Moves again tomorrow?”

Tessa chuckled. “Yes. We can make exercise part of every day. We can move, breathe, and be kind.”

Outside the shed, the flowers nodded in the breeze. Inside, the friends rolled up the mat and washed the brushes, splish-splash. The bell sat quiet. The room smelled like apples and paint.

Tessa looked at her friends. “What did we learn today?” she asked.

Pip said, “Exercise makes me calm and strong.”

Benny said, “Sharing makes room for everyone.”

Tessa added, “Art lets our feelings dance. And mindful breaths help us choose soft hops.”

They all took one more slow breath together. In… out. The day felt bright and good.

The End.
