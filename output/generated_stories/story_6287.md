# Pippa Parrot’s Pantry Parade

Pippa was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a tail that shimmered like a rainbow. She loved to dance and sing. One sunny morning, she fluttered into the pantry. The pantry was cool and quiet. It smelled like sweet honey, warm cinnamon, and crunchy crackers. Jars and boxes lined the shelves like neat little houses.

“Squawk! Pantry parade!” Pippa chirped. She tapped her claws on the floor. Tap-tap. Tap-tap. Her wings wiggled. Her feet wanted to dance fast, fast, fast.

Then Pippa saw a shiny jar of peanut butter. It was big and heavy. Next to it was a small jar of sprinkles, all colors twinkling like stars. She also saw a stack of tins, tall like a tower, and a soft roll of paper towels.

Pippa’s eyes sparkled. “I can make a safe dance stage,” she said. “I will dance slow and steady. I will use my calm plan.”

Pippa had learned a calm plan from her friend, Tio Turtle. Tio had said, “When your body feels fast, stop, breathe, and choose.” Pippa repeated the words to herself. “Stop, breathe, choose.”

Her body felt wiggly. Her wings wanted to flap. But Pippa paused. She placed a claw on her heart and breathed in. Smell the cinnamon. Breathe out. Blow the feathers soft. In. Out. In. Out.

“Ahh,” she said. “Now I can choose.”

First, Pippa looked for safety. She checked the floor. “No slippery oil,” she said. She checked the tins. “They could fall.” She chose to move the tins to a lower shelf with gentle, careful claws. Clink, clink. “Safe,” she said.

Next, she made soft edges with the paper towels. She rolled them out to make a cushion. “Soft landing,” she giggled, patting the fluff. Pat-pat.

She placed the sprinkles away from the edge. “Sprinkles are for later,” she told herself. “I want them now, but I will wait. I can wait.” She felt her tummy flutter like a tiny drum. She breathed again. In. Out. “I am strong. I can wait.”

Pippa made a beat by tapping a wooden spoon on a small box. Tock-tock. Tock-tock. The pantry hummed. The light from the doorway made little golden lines on the floor. Pippa wiggled her shoulders. “Pantry parade, slow and steady,” she sang.

She started to dance. Step, pause. Step, pause. She counted, “One, two. Breathe. One, two. Breathe.” Her wings swished softly. Swish, swish. Her claws clicked. Click, click.

Then Pippa felt the fast feeling again. The music in her head got louder. She wanted to spin and leap! She looked at the tall peanut butter jar. It seemed like a drum. “I can jump on it!” she thought.

But Pippa remembered Tio’s words. “Stop, breathe, choose.” She froze like a statue. She took three slow breaths. In. Out. In. Out. In. Out. She looked at the jar. It was heavy and round. It might roll. That could be unsafe.

“I choose a safer drum,” she said. She found a little empty box with a flat top. She pressed it. It did not wobble. “This is safe,” she said proudly. Tap-tap-tap! She made a gentle beat.

As she danced, she made up a song:
“When I feel fast,
I slow my wings.
I breathe in deep,
And choose good things.”

Pippa felt proud. Her chest felt warm, like sunshine. She marched in a tiny circle, careful and calm. Step, step. Pause. Smile.

Suddenly, the sprinkles sparkled again. The colors were so bright! Red, blue, yellow, pink. Pippa wanted to shake them and watch them fall like rainbow rain. Her feathers tingled. Her claws reached out.

“Stop,” she whispered. “Breathe.” She closed her eyes and smelled the cinnamon again. She counted to three. “One… two… three.” She asked herself, “Is it safe? Is it kind? Is it time?”

She peeked at the soft paper towel floor. “If I shake a tiny bit, over the soft towels, it will be easy to clean,” she decided. “I will ask myself again.” She nodded. “I choose a tiny shake.”

Pippa twisted open the jar with slow, careful turns. Twist, twist. She held it low, over the towels. Tap-tap. Just a few sprinkles fell. They landed like quiet stars. She smiled. “I did it. I kept it small.”

She danced through the sprinkles with tiny steps. Tiptoe, tiptoe. Her tail made a soft whoosh. Her heart felt calm and happy. She finished with a bow. “Ta-da!”

Just then, Tio Turtle peeked into the pantry. “Hello, Pippa,” he said in his gentle voice. “I like your stage.”

Pippa beamed. “I used my calm plan, Tio! I stopped. I breathed. I chose. I made it safe. I waited for sprinkles. I kept it small.”

Tio nodded slowly. “That is self-regulation,” he said. “You listened to your body. You kept yourself safe. You were patient. You did not give up.”

Pippa fluffed her feathers with pride. “I can dance and be safe,” she said. “I can be calm and still have fun.”

Tio clapped his little turtle hands. “And you kept trying. That’s persistence. I am proud of you.”

Pippa looked at the softly shining pantry, the neat shelves, and the tiny sparkle stars on the towels. She felt tall inside. “I am proud of me, too,” she said. “When I feel fast, I know what to do.”

She sang her song one more time, softly:
“When I feel fast,
I slow my wings.
I breathe in deep,
And choose good things.”

Then Pippa cleaned up, one careful sweep at a time. Swish, swish. The pantry was tidy and safe again.

Pippa took a happy breath. “Pantry parade tomorrow,” she chirped. “Slow, safe, and steady!”

The End.
