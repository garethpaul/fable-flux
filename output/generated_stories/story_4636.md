# Momo's Healthy Workshop Adventure

Momo the monkey loved spending time in Grandpa Joe's garage workshop. The smell of wood shavings tickled his nose, and the sound of tools clinking made him feel excited. Today, Momo was helping Grandpa Joe build a special bird feeder.

"Momo, could you hand me that hammer?" asked Grandpa Joe, pointing to the red tool on the workbench.

Momo stretched his long arms and grabbed the hammer. But as he reached over, his tummy made a loud "GRUMBLE!" 

"Oh my!" laughed Grandpa Joe. "Someone sounds hungry!"

Momo's stomach felt empty and rumbly. He looked around the workshop and spotted a bag of colorful candy on the shelf. His eyes grew wide. The candy looked so shiny and sweet!

"Grandpa Joe, can I have some candy? My tummy is so hungry!" Momo asked, pointing at the sugary treats.

Grandpa Joe smiled kindly. "Well, little monkey, let's think about this together. What do you think your body needs when you're hungry?"

Momo scratched his head with his furry fingers. "Um... food?"

"That's right! But what KIND of food helps your body grow strong and gives you energy to build things?" Grandpa Joe asked gently.

Momo thought hard. He remembered what his mama always said about eating good foods. "Healthy food?"

"Exactly!" Grandpa Joe clapped his hands. "Let's go to the kitchen and see what healthy snacks we can find."

In the kitchen, Grandpa Joe opened the refrigerator. The cool air felt refreshing on Momo's face. Inside, Momo saw colorful fruits and vegetables that looked like a rainbow.

"Wow!" Momo exclaimed. "Look at all the pretty colors!"

"These are nature's candy," said Grandpa Joe. "See these orange carrots? They help your eyes see better when we're working with small screws and nails."

Momo picked up a crunchy carrot stick and took a bite. "Crunch, crunch!" It tasted sweet and fresh.

"And these red apples," continued Grandpa Joe, "give you energy to swing your hammer and build strong things."

Momo bit into a juicy apple slice. The sweet juice ran down his chin, and he giggled. "Mmm! This tastes good AND it's good for me!"

"That's right, my clever monkey," said Grandpa Joe. "When we eat healthy foods, our bodies feel strong and our minds can think clearly. That helps us make good choices and learn new skills."

Momo ate his healthy snacks slowly, paying attention to how the foods tasted and how they made his tummy feel happy and satisfied. His stomach stopped grumbling, and he felt full of energy.

"Now I feel ready to build!" Momo announced, flexing his little muscles.

Back in the workshop, Momo helped Grandpa Joe finish the bird feeder. His hands felt steady, and his mind felt clear. He carefully held the wood pieces while Grandpa Joe hammered them together.

"You're such a good helper, Momo," said Grandpa Joe. "Making healthy food choices gave you the energy and focus you needed."

Momo beamed with pride as they hung the beautiful bird feeder outside. "I'm glad I chose healthy snacks instead of candy. Now I know that good food helps me do good work!"

"And the birds will thank you too," winked Grandpa Joe.

Momo learned that taking time to think about his choices and eating nutritious foods helped him feel strong, focused, and ready for any adventure.

The End.
