# Leo's Wobbly Washing Day

Leo the lion cub lived in a cozy house with his family. His favorite room was the laundry room. It smelled like fresh soap and sunshine. The washing machine made fun whooshing sounds.

But Leo had a wiggly problem. His paws felt clumsy and unsteady. When he tried to help with laundry, things went topsy-turvy.

"I want to help!" Leo said one sunny morning. Mama Lion was sorting colorful clothes into baskets.

"Of course, little one," Mama purred. "Can you put the socks in this basket?"

Leo picked up a tiny sock. His paw wobbled left. Then right. The sock flew through the air and landed on his head! "Oops!" Leo giggled, but felt frustrated too.

Next, Leo tried to carry a basket of towels. His legs felt wobbly like jelly. Step, stumble, step, stumble. The soft towels tumbled everywhere!

"My paws don't work right," Leo sighed. His golden ears drooped sadly. "I'm too clumsy to help."

Mama Lion sat down beside her cub. "Leo, coordination takes practice," she said gently. "Your body is learning how to work together."

"Really?" Leo's amber eyes grew wide.

"Really! Let's try something fun," Mama suggested. "First, let's march in place. Left paw, right paw, left paw, right paw."

Leo marched slowly. His paws felt more steady with each step. "This feels good!" he roared softly.

"Now let's practice picking things up," Mama said. She placed colorful socks on the floor. "Go slow and focus."

Leo took a deep breath. He moved his paw carefully toward a red sock. Slowly, gently, he picked it up. Success! "I did it!"

They practiced every day. Leo marched around the washing machine. He balanced on one paw, then the other. He picked up socks, one by one, placing them carefully in baskets.

"Practice makes progress," Mama reminded him each day.

Soon, Leo's movements became smoother. His paws felt stronger and more sure. The wobbly feeling started to fade away.

One morning, Leo woke up excited. "Today I'll help with all the laundry!" he announced.

In the laundry room, Leo moved with new confidence. He sorted socks by color - red ones here, blue ones there. His paws worked together perfectly.

He carried a small basket without dropping anything. Step by steady step, he walked across the room. The towels stayed neatly folded.

"Look, Mama! I'm coordinated now!" Leo beamed with pride.

Mama Lion smiled warmly. "You practiced every day, Leo. Your body learned to work as a team."

Leo helped fold washcloths, matching corners carefully. He put soap in the washing machine without spilling. Everything felt smooth and easy.

"Coordination is like a superpower," Leo realized. "When my paws, legs, and eyes work together, I can do anything!"

From that day on, Leo was Mama's best laundry helper. He learned that with practice and patience, his body could do amazing things.

Every time Leo felt wobbly at something new, he remembered: "Practice makes progress. My body is learning!"

And it always did.

The End.
