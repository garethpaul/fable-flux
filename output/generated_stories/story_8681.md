# The Guest Room with Many Hugs

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with big kind eyes and a long, gentle neck. One sunny morning, Gigi tiptoed into the guest room. The room smelled like fresh soap and sweet tea. The curtains felt soft like clouds, and the blankets were fluffy and warm. “This is the welcome room,” Gigi whispered. “Everyone who visits will feel safe here.”

Today, friends from faraway places were coming to visit. There was Lina the lion from a warm, sandy land. There was Bao the panda from a cool, bamboo forest. There was Sami the seal from a snowy, splashy sea. And there was Asha the sparrow, who loved to fly over busy cities. They were all coming to share stories and to rest in Gigi’s guest room.

Gigi wanted everything to be perfect. She lined up cups for tea. She put out a basket of fruits—yellow bananas, red apples, green pears. She set little baskets of tiny gifts from her town: a woven ribbon, a handmade button, and a small jar of flower seeds for the garden.

“I want it just right,” Gigi said. “I must be patient and kind.” She took a deep breath. The air smelled like cinnamon buns from the kitchen. “I can wait. I can listen.”

Knock, knock.

Lina the lion peeked in. “Hello, Gigi!” Lina’s fur was warm and a little sandy. “I brought a song from my village.”

“Welcome, Lina,” said Gigi. “Please rest. The others will come soon.” Gigi felt excited. Her hooves went tap-tap on the floor. “I want to hear your song now!” she said. Then she stopped. “But I will wait. I will be patient so we can all hear together.”

Knock, knock.

Bao the panda waddled in. He smelled like fresh leaves. “I brought bamboo tea,” he said. “It tastes green and calm.”

“Welcome, Bao,” said Gigi. “Your tea will be lovely.” She looked at the cups and wanted to pour right away. Her tail twitched. “I can pour now,” she thought. “But I will wait. I will be patient so everyone can sip together.”

Knock, knock.

Sami the seal slid in with a smile. His whiskers were wet and tickly. “I brought a shell from my sea,” he said. It sang softly when you held it to your ear—whooooosh.

“Welcome, Sami,” said Gigi. She wanted to show the shell to everyone at once. She reached out, then folded her hooves. “I will wait. I will be patient so we can listen together.”

Flutter, flutter.

Asha the sparrow landed on the windowsill. “I brought a tiny bell from my city,” she chirped. “It rings bright and happy—ting-a-ling!”

“Welcome, Asha,” said Gigi. “Now we are all here.”

Everyone sat in the cozy guest room. The sun was warm and the rug was soft. Gigi smiled. “We will share our traditions,” she said. “We will take turns. We will listen with our hearts.”

Lina lifted her paws. “In my home, we sing before we eat.” She hummed a gentle, golden song. Gigi wanted to clap fast. She waited until the last note, then clapped softly. “Thank you, Lina,” she said. “I heard every note!”

Bao poured the bamboo tea. “In my home, we sip slowly,” he said. “We wait, we taste.” Gigi held the warm cup. She wanted to gulp. She took tiny sips. The tea tasted like quiet rain. “Thank you, Bao,” she said. “Slow is sweet.”

Sami held out the shell. “In my home, we listen to the sea before bedtime.” Everyone pressed the shell to their ear. Whooooosh. Gigi wanted to take the shell outside. She passed it to Asha and waited. “Thank you, Sami,” she said. “The sea sounds calm.”

Asha rang her bell. Ting-a-ling! “In my city, we ring to say hello to neighbors.” Gigi wanted to ring it again and again. She placed the bell back. “Thank you, Asha,” she said. “Hello to all.”

Then everyone looked at Gigi. “What is your tradition?” they asked.

Gigi’s heart thumped. She had been patient. Now it was her turn. “In my home,” she said, “we share before we serve ourselves.” She lifted the basket of fruit. “Please, friends, take some.”

They all reached out. Lina took an apple. Bao took a pear. Sami took a banana slice. Asha pecked a tiny grape. Gigi waited. Only when everyone had fruit did she take a piece. It tasted extra sweet because she had waited.

Suddenly, Gigi saw the tiny gift baskets. She had only three. But there were four friends! Her tummy felt tight. “Oh no,” she whispered. “This is a problem. I want everyone to feel loved.”

She sat very still. She took a slow breath. “I can be patient,” she told herself. “I can think of a kind solution.” She listened to the room—the soft curtain swish, the shell’s whooooosh, the bell’s ting-a-ling.

Then Gigi smiled. She opened the little jar of flower seeds. “We can share,” she said. “One seed for each friend. We will plant them together in the yard. We will wait, water, and watch them grow. This is community service. We will make a garden for everyone.”

Lina’s eyes sparkled. “I will sing to the plants.”

Bao nodded. “I will bring water.”

Sami clapped his flippers. “I will smooth the soil.”

Asha fluttered. “I will ring the bell when it is time to water.”

Gigi gave each friend a seed. She felt calm and proud. “We solved the problem with patience,” she said. “We were gentle and fair.”

That evening, they went outside and planted the seeds. The earth felt cool and crumbly. The air smelled like fresh grass. They watered the seeds and waited. The waiting felt good, like a soft blanket.

Back in the cozy guest room, they snuggled on the rug. Gigi whispered, “Today I learned that patience helps us listen, share, and solve problems. When we wait kindly, everyone feels loved.”

Everyone smiled. “Thank you, Gigi,” they said.

The End.
