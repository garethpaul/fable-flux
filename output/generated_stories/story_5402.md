# Benny Beaver’s Big Pot of Kindness

Benny was a busy beaver. Tap-tap-tap went his tail when he worked. Today, he worked in a warm kitchen. The kitchen smelled like maple pancakes and cinnamon toast. The window let in golden sunshine. The kettle sang a soft hiss. The big pot on the stove went bubble, bubble, bubble.

“I will make a kindness stew,” Benny said. He tied on a tiny red apron. It felt snug and soft. He washed his little paws in warm, soapy water. Splash, splash! He hummed a brave little tune. “Stir, stir, share. Stir, stir, care.”

Benny chopped carrots. Crunch! He sliced apples. Snip! He added sweet corn and thick oats. Plop, plop! He sprinkled a pinch of pepper that tickled his nose. “Ah-choo!” he laughed. “This stew will help my friends. Sharing is caring.”

Just then—knock, knock! The door rattled. Benny’s paws paused. His heart made a quick thump. “Who is there?” he called, with courage in his voice.

“It’s Mimi Mouse,” squeaked a tiny voice. “I got lost in the wind. I am cold and hungry.”

Benny opened the door. Warm steam hugged Mimi’s whiskers. “Come in,” said Benny. “Sit by the stove. Feel the cozy heat.” Mimi’s paws stopped shivering. “Thank you,” she said with big, grateful eyes.

Benny filled a small bowl. “Here you go.” Mimi tasted the stew. “Mmm,” she sighed. “It tastes like hugs.”

Knock, knock! The door rattled again. “Who is there?” Benny asked.

“It’s Gus Goose,” honked a deep voice. “I flew too far. My wings are tired. My tummy says honk-honk-hungry.”

Benny felt a tiny worry. “Do I have enough?” he whispered. He looked at the big pot. Bubble, bubble, bubble. He took a brave breath. “We can share,” he said. He added more water and more oats. He stirred with steady little circles. “Stir, stir, share. Stir, stir, care.” He smiled and gave Gus a bowl. Gus smiled back. “Thank you,” honked Gus. “My heart feels warm now.”

Knock, knock, knock! Three taps this time. “Who is there?” Benny asked, standing tall.

“It’s Tilly Turtle,” came a slow voice. “The road was rough. My shell is dusty. I need a rest.”

“Come in, Tilly,” Benny said kindly. He brushed the dust from Tilly’s shell with a soft towel. Swish, swish. He handed her a warm bowl. Tilly tasted it and closed her eyes. “Thank you,” she whispered. “This tastes like sunshine.”

Soon more friends came. Pip and Pop, two little birds. A shy raccoon named Ray. Even a grumpy squirrel named Sid. The kitchen grew busy and bright. Chairs scooted. Spoons clinked. Everyone said, “Thank you, Benny!”

Benny looked into the pot. Uh-oh. Only a little stew was left. His tummy made a tiny rumble. He had not eaten yet. He felt a pinch of worry. He stirred and stirred, but the stew was low. He felt a tear tug at the corner of his eye.

Mimi saw his face. “Benny,” she said softly, “you shared with us. We can share with you.”

Gus nodded. “We can help!”

Benny took a brave breath. “I am a little hungry,” he said. It felt bold to say it out loud.

“Let’s work together,” Tilly said. “Peaceful teamwork helps everyone.”

Everyone moved into action. Pip and Pop flew to the pantry. “We found beans!” they chirped. Ray the raccoon washed potatoes. Swish, swish in warm water. Sid the squirrel cracked nuts. Crack, crack! Mimi measured oats. Gus brought water in a big jug. Glug, glug! Tilly stirred with slow, strong circles.

The kitchen smelled even better now—like apples, cinnamon, and cozy jokes. Laughter bubbled like the pot. Benny felt his worry float away. He felt strong and steady. He felt grateful.

“I am proud of you all,” Benny said. “You are brave and kind. Thank you for helping.”

“Thank you for sharing,” everyone said. “You showed us how.”

They filled the pot to the brim. Bubble, bubble, bubble! Benny tasted the stew with a wooden spoon. “Mmm,” he said. “It tastes like friends.”

He gave everyone a bowl, and this time he took one too. The stew was warm and sweet. It gave him energy. It made his heart feel big.

Sid, the grumpy squirrel, smiled a tiny smile. “I feel better when I share,” he said. “I guess I was just hungry.”

“It is okay to feel grumpy,” Benny said. “Kindness helps.”

When all tummies were full, Benny wrote a little sign and taped it on the wall. The sign said, “Generous hearts make cozy homes.”

The friends cleaned the kitchen together. Swipe went the cloth. Clack went the bowls. The window glowed with the soft, orange light of evening. Benny’s tail went tap-tap-tap with happy work.

Benny stood by the warm stove and looked at his friends. “Today I learned,” he said, “that generosity can grow. When we share, our kindness comes back to us. We can be brave, we can keep trying, and we can use peaceful solutions.”

Everyone nodded. “Thank you, Benny,” they said. “We will share, too.”

They hugged goodbye and stepped into the soft night. The kitchen stayed warm and quiet. Benny sighed a calm, cozy sigh. He felt thankful, brave, and ready to share again tomorrow.

The End.
