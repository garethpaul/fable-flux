# Milo Mouse’s Car Ride Kindness

Milo was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright button eyes. He lived with a loving family. He rode with them in their family car. The car smelled like apple slices and clean seat belts. The seats felt warm and bouncy. The windows showed blue sky and fluffy clouds.

“Click,” went Milo’s tiny seat belt on his booster seat. “Safety first,” he squeaked.

“Ready to go?” asked Dad. His voice was gentle.

“Ready!” squeaked Milo. He wiggled his whiskers with joy.

Milo liked looking around the car. He saw a crinkly paper cup on the floor. He saw a shiny soda can near the door. He saw some wrappers with bright colors. The wrappers felt sticky. The can made a soft clink when the car turned.

Milo frowned. “The car looks messy,” he whispered. He felt a little sad. He liked a clean, happy car.

Just then, his big sister, Tasha, reached for the radio. “I want loud music!” she said.

Dad said, “Please ask first.”

Tasha sighed. “May I turn on music?”

“Yes,” said Dad, “but not too loud.”

Milo took a deep breath. He remembered a big word: respect. Respect means caring about others. Respect means asking, listening, and being gentle.

Milo asked, “Dad, may I pick up the trash?” His voice was small but brave.

“That is kind,” said Dad. “Thank you for asking.”

Milo smiled. He stretched his little paws. He picked up the paper cup. Crinkle, crinkle. He picked up the soda can. Clink, clink. He picked up the wrappers. Sticky, sticky.

He put them in a small bag. He held the can and said, “This can can be new again. We can recycle!”

Tasha looked back. “Recycle? In the car?”

“Yes,” said Milo. “We can sort things. We can be kind to the Earth.”

Dad nodded. “Good idea, Milo.”

Milo had another idea. “May we make a recycle box for the car?” he asked. “We can use an old shoe box. We can decorate it. It can live right here by the door.”

Tasha blinked. “A shoe box? In the car?”

Milo’s whiskers trembled. He felt a tiny bit shy. “We can try,” he said softly. “We can be open-minded.”

Tasha thought. “Hmm. Okay. We can try.”

The car rolled into the store parking lot. The sun made the hood warm. The air smelled like fresh bread. Dad said, “Let’s look for a box.”

They found an old shoe box in the trunk. It smelled like new rubber and cardboard. Milo clapped his paws. “Yay!”

At home, they got crayons, tape, and stickers. Milo colored a big green circle. He drew arrows that went round and round. Tasha stuck on blue star stickers. Dad added a label that said, “Car Recycle.”

Milo asked, “May I put the can in now?” He waited.

“Yes,” said Dad. “Thank you for asking.”

Clink! The can went into the box. Milo felt proud. His chest felt warm, like sunshine. “We did it!” he squeaked.

The next day, they drove to the park. The car hummed. The box sat by the door. Tasha finished a juice box. She waved it in the air.

Milo smiled. “May I have it for the box?”

Tasha smiled too. “Yes. Thank you for asking.” She squeezed the straw out and handed it to him.

Milo placed the juice box into the recycle box. “Helping is kind,” he said. “Helping shows respect.”

On the way home, the car was calm and clean. The air smelled like grass and wind. Milo heard birds singing outside. Chirp, chirp! He felt safe and happy.

Suddenly, the car hit a little bump. Bump! The recycle box slid. It bumped his paw. “Ouch,” said Milo.

Tasha said, “Let’s fix it.” She got a soft strap and asked, “May I tie the box to the seat so it stays safe?”

Milo nodded. “Yes, please.”

Dad said, “Thank you both for thinking and asking. That is respect.”

That night, Milo had one more idea. He took a clean wrapper and folded it. He folded and folded. Snip, snip went the child scissors. He made a tiny paper mouse with big ears.

“Look!” he squeaked. “Recycling can be art!”

Tasha gasped. “It’s so cute!”

Dad smiled. “You are creative, Milo.”

Milo’s heart felt big. “I am brave and kind,” he whispered. “I can help. I can ask. I can listen.”

He patted the recycle box in the car. It felt smooth and strong. He looked at Tasha. “Thank you for trying my idea,” he said.

Tasha smiled. “Thank you for asking with respect,” she said. “That made it easy to say yes.”

Milo looked out the window at the moon. The moon looked like a shiny coin. The car was quiet and clean. The family felt close.

Milo said, “Respect means we care for people. Respect means we care for our car and our Earth.”

Dad nodded. “And respect helps us solve problems together.”

Milo curled his tail and smiled. He felt proud and calm. He felt like a helpful mouse in a happy car.

The End.
