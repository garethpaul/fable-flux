# Pebble the Penguin’s Nursery Helpers

Pebble the cheerful penguin waddled into the comfortable nursery. The room felt warm and cozy. Sunlight made soft squares on the fluffy rug. Crayons smelled waxy and sweet. A little fan hummed a gentle song. Pebble’s black-and-white feathers were smooth, and his orange feet felt bouncy on the floor.

“Good morning!” Pebble chirped. “I’m ready for a busy day!”

Ms. Lina, the kind teacher, smiled. “Good morning, Pebble. Today we are practicing coordination. That means moving our bodies together, with care, and helping our friends.”

Pebble blinked. “Co-or-di-na-tion,” he tried slowly. “Like a dance?”

“Exactly,” Ms. Lina said. “Body, eyes, ears, and heart working together. And we’ll use our teamwork to help our nursery community.”

Pebble flapped his little wings with joy. “I love helping!”

The children were making a big picture on the floor. Paper rustled. Markers clicked. The picture showed a sunny garden with flowers and a pond. “Wow,” Pebble said, “It looks like a happy day.”

“Our friend Nia needs a resting corner,” Ms. Lina explained. “She feels a bit tired today. Let’s build a soft reading nook. We must move pillows, books, and a small chair. We also need to tidy the crayon table. We will use coordination so everyone stays safe and calm.”

Pebble nodded. “I can do it! My eyes will look. My wings will carry. My feet will step slowly.”

Nia smiled from a cozy beanbag. “Thank you, Pebble,” she said softly. “My head feels a little thumpy.”

Pebble waddled to the pillows. “First, I look,” he whispered. “Then I lift.” He hugged a pillow. It smelled like clean cotton. “Now I walk—slow and steady.” Pebble took small penguin steps. Tap. Tap. Tap. He watched the floor and listened for friends. He placed the pillow next to Nia.

“You did it!” Ms. Lina cheered. “That’s coordination.”

Pebble felt proud. “I will bring two more,” he said. But then he saw Tim zoom by with a stack of books. “Whoa!” Tim slipped, and the books slid. Pebble’s eyes went wide.

Pebble took a deep breath. “Tim, let’s do it together,” he said gently. “We can count our steps.”

Tim nodded. “Okay.”

Pebble picked up one book. “One,” he said. Tim picked up one book too. “Two.” They walked side by side. “Step, step, step.” The books smelled like paper and stories. They set them down by the pillow pile.

Nia laughed quietly. “Thank you, team.”

Next, the crayon table needed love. Some crayons were on the rug. A few had no wrappers. One crayon was broken in half.

Pebble crouched low. “I will sort colors,” he said. “Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple.” He pointed and put each crayon in a small cup. “My eyes look. My flippers pick up. My feet stay still.” He hummed a little song. “Pick and place, pick and place.”

“Can I snap the broken crayon into two cups?” asked Tim.

Ms. Lina nodded. “Good idea. That is understanding and problem-solving.”

Pebble smiled. “We can share the halves!”

Soon the table looked neat. The cups made a rainbow line. Pebble wiped a tiny crayon smear. The cloth felt cool and damp. “All clean,” he said proudly.

“Time for the chair,” Ms. Lina said. “It is small, but we must carry it safely.”

Pebble stood on one side. Tim stood on the other. “Ready?” Pebble asked.

“Ready,” Tim answered.

They lifted together. “Light as a feather!” Pebble giggled. They walked slowly. Pebble looked forward. Tim looked to the side. “Left, right, left, right,” they chanted, like a quiet drum. Their feet tapped gently on the rug. They set the chair beside the pillows.

Nia sat in the chair and hugged a pillow. Her shoulders relaxed. “This is perfect,” she sighed.

Pebble waddled back and noticed his own tummy grumble. “I feel thirsty,” he said.

“That is good self-care,” Ms. Lina said. “Take a sip and a breath.”

Pebble went to the water station. The cup felt cool in his flipper. He took small sips. “Sip, pause, sip,” he said. He dabbed his beak with a napkin. He felt calm and strong again.

Ms. Lina clapped softly. “Look around,” she said.

The nursery looked bright and tidy. The reading nook was soft and safe. The crayon table was ready for art. Everyone’s faces were happy and calm.

Pebble’s heart felt big. “We did it together,” he said.

Tim grinned. “We used our bodies and our brains.”

Nia waved. “And you helped me rest.”

Pebble waddled closer and whispered, “Coordination is a kind of caring. We watch, we listen, we move, we help.”

Ms. Lina nodded. “Yes. Coordination helps us help ourselves and others. Today you practiced understanding, self-care, and community service.”

Pebble puffed his chest. “I’m a Nursery Helper Penguin!”

Everyone laughed gently. Pebble did a tiny penguin twirl—carefully, slowly, with balance. “Twirl and stop,” he said, and he didn’t wobble at all.

“Story time?” Nia asked.

“Story time,” Ms. Lina agreed. Pebble sat on the soft rug, feet tucked, eyes bright. He felt safe, helpful, and ready to listen.

Today Pebble learned to look, listen, and move with care. Coordination helped him help his friends, care for himself, and make the nursery cozy for everyone.

The End.
