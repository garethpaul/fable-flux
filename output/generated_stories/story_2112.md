# Squeaky's Big Voice

In a cozy little bathroom behind the bathtub lived a kind mouse named Squeaky. Squeaky had soft gray fur and tiny pink ears that wiggled when he was excited. He loved his bathroom home because it was warm and safe, with fluffy towels that made perfect blankets.

Every morning, Squeaky would peek out from behind the bathtub to watch the family get ready for their day. He saw Mama brushing her teeth with white, foamy toothpaste. He watched Papa splash cool water on his face. And he giggled quietly when little Emma sang silly songs while washing her hands.

But Squeaky had a big problem. He was very, very shy. His voice was so tiny that even when he tried to speak, only the smallest "squeak" came out. This made him feel sad because he wanted to make friends with the family, especially little Emma who seemed so kind and cheerful.

One Tuesday morning, something terrible happened. While Emma was brushing her teeth, she dropped her favorite yellow toothbrush behind the bathtub - right where Squeaky lived! Emma looked and looked, but she couldn't reach it.

"Oh no!" Emma cried, her eyes filling with tears. "My special toothbrush is gone! Mama gave it to me, and now I lost it!"

Squeaky's little heart felt heavy. He could see the bright yellow toothbrush right next to his tiny bed. He wanted to help Emma so badly, but he was scared to come out. What if she was frightened of him? What if his voice was too quiet?

Squeaky took three deep breaths, just like he had seen Emma do when she was upset. "In... and out... in... and out," he whispered to himself. The breathing made him feel calmer and braver.

Then Squeaky remembered something important his mama had taught him: "Sometimes the smallest voices can make the biggest difference, but only if we use them."

Squeaky stood up tall on his tiny legs and cleared his throat. "Ahem!" he squeaked, but it was still very quiet.

Emma didn't hear him. She was still crying.

Squeaky tried again, this time a little louder. "Excuse me!" he called out in his small but clear voice.

Emma stopped crying and looked around. "Who said that?"

Taking all his courage, Squeaky stepped out from behind the bathtub, holding the yellow toothbrush carefully in his tiny paws. "It's me, Squeaky! I found your toothbrush!"

Emma's eyes grew wide with wonder, not fear. "A talking mouse! And you found my toothbrush! Thank you so much!"

Squeaky felt warm and happy inside. Using his voice wasn't scary at all! "I live behind your bathtub," he explained. "I see you brush your teeth every day, and you always look so happy. I wanted to help."

"You're so kind!" Emma said, gently taking her toothbrush. "Would you like to be my bathroom friend?"

Squeaky's whiskers twitched with joy. "Yes, please!"

From that day on, Squeaky and Emma became the best of friends. Every morning, they would chat while Emma got ready. Sometimes Squeaky would even do a little dance on the bathroom counter, spinning and twirling while Emma giggled.

Squeaky learned that his voice, no matter how small, was important and valuable. When he spoke with kindness and courage, wonderful things could happen.

And Emma learned that the best friends can come in the smallest packages, especially when they're brave enough to speak up and help.

The End.
