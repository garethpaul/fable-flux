# Squeaky’s Cozy Closet

Squeaky the squirrel loved his secret spot. It was a closet hideaway in the corner of the playroom. The door was small and squeaky—just like his name. Inside the closet, it was soft and warm. A fuzzy blue scarf hung from a hook. A box of buttons sat on the floor. A little night-light glowed like a tiny moon.

“Perfect,” Squeaky whispered, patting his fluffy tail. “This is my special place.”

One rainy afternoon, the wind tapped the window, tap-tap-tap. The closet smelled like clean shirts and a little bit like cinnamon. Squeaky curled up under the scarf. He listened to the gentle drip-drop, drip-drop of the rain. His tummy felt happy and safe.

Knock, knock.

Squeaky’s ears perked up. “Who’s there?” he asked, peeking out.

It was Tippy the mouse. Tippy’s whiskers were wet. Her paws were cold. “Hi, Squeaky,” she said softly. “Can I come in? The rain is loud, and I feel scared.”

Squeaky looked at his small space. He liked his quiet. He liked his things just so. He hugged the scarf. “This is my closet,” he said. “It’s very small.”

Tippy’s eyes grew shiny. “I understand,” she whispered. “I can sit in the hallway.”

Squeaky watched her tiny shoulders droop. His heart made a squeeze-squeeze feeling. He remembered a time when he was scared of thunder. He remembered wishing for a friend.

Squeaky took a slow breath. He sniffed the cinnamon smell. He listened to the rain. He felt the squeeze-squeeze again, and he thought, How does Tippy feel right now?

“Wait,” Squeaky said. “I think you are cold. I think you are scared.”

Tippy nodded. “Yes.”

Squeaky’s tail twitched. “I feel that sometimes too,” he said. “Come in. We can share.”

Tippy stepped inside. Drip-drop water fell from her whiskers. “Thank you,” she squeaked.

Squeaky pulled down the fuzzy blue scarf. “Here,” he said. “It is warm and soft.”

Tippy tucked the scarf around her shoulders. “Mmm,” she sighed. “It smells nice.”

Squeaky opened the button box. The buttons clicked and clacked like tiny shells. “Want to sort?” he asked. “Round ones here. Star ones there.”

Tippy smiled. “Yes!”

They worked together. Click, clack. Click, clack. Squeaky made a rainbow line. Tippy made a moon shape. The night-light glowed and the rain hummed a lullaby.

Knock, knock.

Squeaky and Tippy looked up. It was Chatter the chipmunk. His cheeks trembled. “Hi,” he said. “My ball rolled away. The thunder boomed. Can I sit with you until it’s quiet?”

Squeaky looked around. The closet felt even smaller now. He felt a tiny worry wiggle in his chest. Is there room? Will it be too noisy?

He placed a paw on his chest and took a deep breath. In. Out. He remembered the squeeze-squeeze feeling. He looked at Chatter’s shaking paws and thought, How does Chatter feel?

Squeaky nodded. “We can make room,” he said. “We can scoot and share.”

Tippy moved the button box closer to the wall. Squeaky folded the scarf in three. Chatter slipped in and sat crisscross. “Thank you,” Chatter said. “I feel better already.”

Boom! The thunder growled. Tippy flinched. Chatter covered his ears. Squeaky’s heartbeat went thump-thump-thump.

Squeaky spoke softly, like a blanket. “Let’s do a calm game,” he said. “We can count the drips.”

“Okay,” Tippy whispered.

“Okay,” Chatter said.

They listened. Drip. Drip. Drip-drip.

“One,” said Squeaky.

“Two,” said Tippy.

“Three,” said Chatter.

As they counted, their shoulders relaxed. The closet felt warm and bright, even with the storm outside.

Squeaky had an idea. “Let’s make a kindness train,” he said. He held out a button. “We pass a button and say kind words.”

He looked at Tippy. “You are brave,” he said, giving her the button.

Tippy smiled and passed it to Chatter. “You are gentle,” she said.

Chatter passed it back to Squeaky. “You are smart and sharing,” he said.

Squeaky’s cheeks felt warm in a happy way. “Thank you,” he said. He learned a new feeling. His special place felt even more special with friends inside.

Soon, the rain turned from tap-tap to tip-tap to quiet. The thunder rolled away like a sleepy giant.

Chatter stood up. “I will find my ball now,” he said. “Thank you for the safe spot.”

Tippy wrapped the scarf and then gave it back. “Thank you for the warm,” she said. “I feel cozy in my heart.”

Squeaky looked at the closet. The buttons were in neat shapes. The scarf was folded. The night-light still glowed like a tiny moon. He looked at his friends. He felt calm and proud.

“I learned something,” Squeaky said. “When I think about how someone else feels, I can help. Sharing my space did not make it smaller. It made it kinder.”

Tippy nodded. “Empathy,” she said softly. “That is a big word. It means you care about my feelings.”

Chatter grinned. “And acceptance means we like each other just the way we are.”

Squeaky added, “Generosity means we share what we have.”

They all hugged. Their fur felt soft. The closet smelled like cinnamon and smiles.

From that day, Squeaky’s closet hideaway was still his special place. But now, it was also a safe spot for friends who needed it. He kept the scarf ready. He kept the button box open. And he kept his heart open too.

The End.
