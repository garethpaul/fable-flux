# Daisy's Car Ride Apology

Little Daisy the deer fawn sat in the back seat of the Miller family's car, her soft brown eyes wide with worry. Her spotted coat felt warm against the cozy car seat, and she could hear the gentle hum of the engine as they drove through the forest.

"I made a big mistake," Daisy whispered to herself, remembering what had happened that morning.

Mrs. Miller turned around with a kind smile. "What's wrong, sweet Daisy? You've been so quiet."

Daisy's ears drooped low. "I ate all the apples from your picnic basket," she said sadly. "I was so hungry, and they smelled so good and crunchy. But now there's nothing left for our family picnic."

The car smelled like pine trees from outside and the leather seats felt smooth under Daisy's hooves. She could see tall trees whooshing by through the window.

"Oh my," said Mr. Miller gently. "Those apples were for everyone to share at the park. How do you think the children will feel when they find out?"

Daisy thought about little Emma and Sam Miller, who had been so excited about their picnic. "They'll be sad," she said, her voice tiny. "And hungry too. I wasn't being fair. I should have asked first."

Mrs. Miller reached back and patted Daisy's soft head. "It's good that you realize your mistake, dear. But what matters most is what you do next."

"What can I do?" asked Daisy, perking up her ears hopefully.

"Well," said Mr. Miller, "we're passing the farmer's market right now. Would you like to use your allowance to buy some healthy snacks for everyone?"

Daisy's eyes sparkled. "Yes! I want to make it right!"

The car pulled into the colorful market parking lot. Daisy could smell fresh fruits and vegetables - sweet strawberries, crisp carrots, and juicy oranges. The market was full of bright colors and happy sounds.

"Look!" said Daisy, pointing with her hoof. "Fresh apples! And carrots too! Those are good for our bodies and give us energy to play."

With Mrs. Miller's help, Daisy carefully picked out red apples, orange carrots, and green grapes. She even chose some nuts that would help the squirrels they might meet at the park.

"These foods will help everyone grow strong," explained Mrs. Miller. "Fruits and vegetables have vitamins that keep us healthy."

As they walked back to the car, Daisy felt the warm sun on her fur and heard birds chirping happily. "I'm buying extra," she said proudly, "so we can share with the forest animals too. That's being fair to everyone!"

Back in the car, Daisy hugged the bag of fresh food. "I'm sorry I ate all the apples," she said. "I learned that sharing is caring, and I should always ask before taking something that belongs to everyone."

"And now you're making amends by fixing your mistake," said Mr. Miller. "That shows you care about others."

When they reached the park, Emma and Sam cheered when they saw all the healthy snacks Daisy had chosen. They shared the food with some hungry rabbits and squirrels, and everyone had enough to eat.

"Thank you for making it right, Daisy," said Emma, giving the fawn a gentle hug.

Daisy smiled, feeling warm and happy inside. She had learned that when you make a mistake, the best thing to do is say sorry and try to fix it. Making amends feels much better than feeling guilty!

The End.
