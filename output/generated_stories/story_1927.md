# Rosie Rabbit's Mixed-Up Lab

Rosie Rabbit loved to invent things in her basement lab. She had colorful bottles, shiny tools, and sparkly buttons everywhere. But there was one big problem - everything was scattered all around!

"Where did I put my special wrench?" Rosie asked herself, hopping from table to table. She pushed aside piles of screws, moved stacks of papers, and looked under her fuzzy slippers.

The basement smelled like metal and paint. Rosie's ears drooped as she searched. "I know it's here somewhere," she whispered.

Just then, her friend Benny Bear came down the creaky wooden stairs. "Hi Rosie! Are you ready to build our rocket ship?"

Rosie's nose twitched with worry. "Oh no, Benny! I can't find anything in this messy lab. I'm sorry, but we can't build today."

Benny looked around at the scattered tools and bottles rolling on the floor. "Wow, it is pretty mixed up down here. But don't worry, Rosie! We can fix this together."

"Really?" Rosie's ears perked up. "But there's so much mess!"

"My grandpa always says, 'One step at a time makes any job easy,'" Benny smiled. "Let's try organizing everything into groups."

Rosie felt a little spark of hope. "That sounds like a good idea! But what if I mess it up again?"

"That's okay," Benny said gently. "We can always try again. That's how we learn and grow!"

First, they gathered all the tools. Rosie picked up wrenches while Benny collected screwdrivers. The metal tools clinked together as they placed them in a bright red toolbox.

"This feels good!" Rosie giggled, her cotton tail wiggling with excitement.

Next, they sorted the colorful bottles by size. Small blue bottles went on the bottom shelf, medium green bottles in the middle, and tall purple bottles on top.

"Look how pretty they look all lined up!" Benny pointed out.

Then they organized the papers into neat stacks and put the buttons in clear jars. Rosie could hear each button make a tiny "plink" sound as it dropped in.

"Now let's make labels," Rosie suggested. "That way I'll remember where everything goes!"

They drew pictures on small cards - a hammer for the tool box, a bottle for the liquid shelf, and a button for the button jars.

When they finished, Rosie took a deep breath. The basement smelled fresh and clean. Everything had its own special place, and she could see her whole lab clearly.

"Benny, I'm sorry I was grumpy earlier," Rosie said, giving her friend a warm hug. "Thank you for helping me learn that organizing can be fun!"

"You're welcome, Rosie! Now, should we build that rocket ship?"

Rosie hopped over to her organized toolbox and easily found her special wrench. "Yes! And now I know exactly where everything is!"

As they worked together, Rosie felt proud and calm. She learned that when things seem too hard, taking one small step at a time makes everything possible. And keeping things organized helped her mind feel peaceful and ready for new adventures.

From that day on, Rosie always put her tools away after using them. Her lab stayed neat and tidy, and she could invent amazing things with her friends every single day.

The End.
