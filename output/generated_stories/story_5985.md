# Pico the Parrot’s Peaceful Porch Day

Pico was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a splash of blue on his tail. He loved to make art with leaves and little pebbles. He loved to sing soft songs. He lived with Nana on a peaceful front porch that smelled like oranges and warm bread. The porch had a comfy swing, a small table, and pots of mint and flowers. The wind made the chimes sing, “Ting-ting-ting,” like tiny bells.

“Good morning, porch,” Pico chirped. “Today, we make something new!”

Nana poured warm tea that smelled sweet. “Good morning, Pico,” she said. “Let’s have a calm, happy day.”

Pico nodded. “A wellness day,” he said. He didn’t know all the big words, but he knew it meant taking care of his body, his heart, and his mind. He puffed his feathers and took a deep breath. In through his beak, out through his beak. His tummy went up and down, slow and soft.

He spread his art leaves on the table. He had round leaves, long leaves, and tiny, tiny ones like green boats. He had smooth gray pebbles that felt cool. He hummed a gentle song. “Hummm-hummm,” he sang, tapping a pebble, “ting.”

Just then, a little sparrow named Squeak hopped onto the porch rail. “Hi, Pico!” Squeak chirped, flapping excited wings. “Can I help?”

Pico smiled. “Yes, Squeak. We can make a leaf rainbow.” He pointed to the colors: light green, dark green, and a red leaf from the fall. “We will place them just so.”

Squeak nodded and picked up a red leaf. “Like this?”

Pico’s eyes grew wide. “Careful,” he said. “Slow hands.” He showed Squeak how to breathe in and breathe out. “We move slow. We feel the leaf. We look with kind eyes.”

Squeak copied him. In and out. In and out. “I feel calm,” Squeak said.

They worked together. The wind smelled like mint. The sun felt warm on Pico’s feathers. The porch swing creaked gently. “Creak, creak,” it sang.

But then, a sudden loud noise came from the street—“Vroom!” A truck rumbled by. Squeak jumped, and his little foot bumped the table. The leaves blew into the air like tiny boats in a storm. The pebbles rolled. Pico’s neat rainbow fell apart.

“Oh no!” Pico squawked. His heart felt hot. His eyes felt wet. His feathers puffed. “My art!”

Squeak’s wings drooped. “I’m sorry,” he whispered. “I got scared.”

Pico looked at the mess. He felt his tummy twist like a knot. He wanted to shout. But he remembered Nana’s morning words: calm, happy day. He remembered wellness. He remembered his breathing. He placed his feet flat and took a slow breath. In… one, two, three. Out… one, two, three. His tummy softened. His heart slowed down.

Nana stepped onto the porch. Her slippers went “shuffle-shuffle.” She smelled like warm bread. “What happened, my dears?” she asked.

“The truck was loud,” Squeak said. “I bumped the table. I am sorry, Pico.”

Pico blinked. He looked at Squeak’s sad eyes. He listened to the wind. Ting-ting-ting went the chimes. He put a wing on his chest. “My feelings are big,” he said. “I feel mad. I feel sad. But I can breathe. I can choose kind.”

Nana smiled softly. “That is mindful, Pico. You noticed your feelings. You breathed. That helps your body and your heart.”

Pico looked at the leaves on the floor. He looked at Squeak. “I forgive you,” he said. “It was an accident.”

Squeak’s wings lifted a little. “Thank you,” he peeped.

“Let’s make the art again,” Pico said. “But first, we take a wellness break.”

“What is a wellness break?” Squeak asked.

Pico showed him. “We drink water. We stretch. We rest our eyes. We use kind words.”

Nana brought two tiny cups of cool water. Pico sipped. The water felt fresh and clear. Squeak sipped too and burped a tiny “brrrp.” They giggled. Then they stretched their wings and arms up, up, up to the sky and let them fall down, down, down like feathers.

“Close your eyes,” Pico said. “Listen.” They heard bees buzz, “bzzzz.” They heard the wind brush the leaves, “shhh.” They heard a dog bark far away, “woof-woof.” They felt the warm sun and the cool porch wood under their feet. Their bodies felt calm and cozy.

“Now we are ready,” Pico said. He and Squeak picked up the leaves again, one by one. Pico said, “Slow hands.” Squeak said, “Kind eyes.” They smiled and breathed as they worked. The leaf rainbow became even more beautiful. They added a small heart shape in the middle with tiny pebbles.

Nana clapped softly. “What a mindful masterpiece,” she said.

Squeak looked at Pico. “I feel proud,” he said.

Pico nodded. “I feel proud too. We had big feelings. We used our breath. We forgave. We tried again.”

He sang a new soft song. “Hummm-hummm,” he sang, and the chimes answered, “ting-ting.”

Pico looked at the porch and whispered, “Thank you for being peaceful.” The porch looked sunny and kind. The mint leaves smelled sweet. The warm air wrapped them like a hug.

Pico turned to Squeak. “We learned today,” he said. “Wellness is taking care of our bodies and our hearts. Mindfulness is breathing and noticing. Forgiveness is saying, ‘It’s okay,’ when there is a mistake.”

Squeak nodded. “And trying again,” he added.

“Yes,” Pico said. “And trying again.”

They sat on the swing, creak, creak, and shared a slice of warm bread. It tasted soft and buttery. They watched their leaf rainbow shine in the sun. Pico’s heart felt light, like a feather in the sky.

The End.
