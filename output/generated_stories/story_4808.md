# Oliver Owl's Special Day

High up on a tall building's rooftop, a wise old owl named Oliver stretched his fluffy brown wings. The morning sun felt warm and cozy on his feathers. Oliver loved starting each day on his favorite rooftop where he could see the whole city below.

"Good morning, world!" Oliver hooted softly. His big, round eyes sparkled in the sunshine.

But today, Oliver felt a little tired. His tummy was grumbly, and his wings felt heavy. He had been so busy helping other animals all week that he forgot to take care of himself.

"Oh my," Oliver said, looking at his reflection in a puddle of rainwater. "I look quite tired. My feathers are messy, and I feel hungry."

Oliver remembered what his grandmother always told him: "To help others, you must first help yourself, little one."

First, Oliver decided to eat a healthy breakfast. He flew down to his special food box hidden behind the rooftop door. Inside were crunchy seeds, fresh berries, and clean water.

"Mmm," Oliver said as he ate the sweet blueberries. "These taste like little bursts of sunshine!" The seeds went *crunch, crunch* between his beak, and the cool water made his throat feel much better.

Next, Oliver groomed his feathers carefully. He used his beak to smooth each fluffy feather until they were neat and clean. "There," he said, admiring himself in the puddle again. "Much better!"

Then Oliver remembered his favorite self-care activity - writing in his journal. He pulled out a small notebook and a pencil with his talon. The pencil felt smooth and familiar in his grip.

"Dear Journal," Oliver wrote in his careful letters. "Today I learned something important. I was so busy helping my friends that I forgot to take care of myself. But now I feel much better after eating good food and cleaning my feathers."

As Oliver wrote, he felt peaceful and happy. Writing helped him understand his feelings better.

Just then, his friend Bella Bunny hopped onto the rooftop. "Oliver!" she called. "I need help finding my lost carrot!"

Oliver's first thought was to fly away immediately to help. But then he paused. "Bella," he said kindly, "I would love to help you. But first, have you eaten breakfast today? You look tired."

Bella's ears drooped. "No, I was too worried about my carrot to eat."

Oliver smiled wisely. "Let's take care of you first. Come share some berries with me. When we feel strong and healthy, we can think more clearly."

Bella's eyes lit up. "That's a wonderful idea!"

They sat together on the warm rooftop, sharing the delicious berries. The morning breeze felt gentle on their faces, and birds chirped happy songs around them.

"You know what?" Bella said after eating. "I just remembered where I left my carrot - by the garden gate!"

Oliver chuckled. "See? When we take care of ourselves first, everything becomes clearer."

As Bella hopped away happily, Oliver finished writing in his journal: "Taking care of myself isn't selfish - it helps me be the best friend I can be."

Oliver tucked his journal away and spread his wings wide, feeling strong and ready for whatever the day might bring. The sun climbed higher in the sky, and Oliver felt grateful for learning such an important lesson.

The End.
