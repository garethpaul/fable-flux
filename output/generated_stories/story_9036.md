# Nia the Thoughtful Giraffe Builds a Welcome World

In a bright guest room with soft rugs and sunshine on the floor, a thoughtful giraffe named Nia stretched her long neck. The room smelled like clean sheets and warm tea. The window was open, and a friendly breeze whispered, “Hello.”

Nia smiled. “Today is a good day,” she said. “Today, I will make our guest room ready. Friends from around the world are coming to visit.”

Nia looked around. On the shelf was a box of building blocks in many colors—red, blue, green, and yellow. There were soft pillows with tiny stars. There was a small globe that spun with a gentle whirr. Nia tapped the globe with her nose. Whirr, whirr. It felt cool and smooth.

“I want every friend to feel at home,” said Nia. “I will share this room. I will build a welcome place.”

Nia began to move. Clip-clop went her hooves on the wooden floor. She stacked blocks into bridges and houses. “This house can be for Mei,” she said softly. “Mei loves red.” She placed a red block on top, tap, tap.

Nia built a little blue bridge. “This bridge is for Amir. Amir likes to cross bridges. He says, ‘Bridges bring us together.’” Nia smiled at the gentle click of the blocks. Click. Click.

There was a quiet knock at the door. Knock, knock.

Nia took a brave breath. “I can do this,” she whispered. “I can be brave and kind.”

She opened the door. In came three guests. There was Mei the panda with bright eyes. There was Amir the fox with a soft tail. And there was Lila the hummingbird who hummed a happy tune. Bzz-zz-zz.

“Welcome!” Nia said. Her voice was warm. “Please come in. This is our guest room. This is your room, too.”

“It smells like tea and sunshine,” said Mei, taking a deep breath.

“It looks cozy,” said Amir, brushing his tail on a star pillow.

“It sounds friendly,” hummed Lila, zipping near the globe. Whirr.

Nia nodded. “We are friends from many places. We can share. We can build together. We can care for each other. That is being good neighbors to the whole world.”

Mei touched the red house of blocks. “Can I add a window?” she asked.

“Yes,” said Nia. “We can build together.” She lowered her long neck to see.

Amir placed a yellow block. “I will make a door,” he said. “A door that opens for everyone.”

Lila hovered near the blue bridge. “I will add a tiny flag,” she buzzed, placing a blue triangle on top. “It can wave hello.”

They worked side by side. The blocks made happy sounds—click, clack, tap. Nia shared the blocks. Amir shared a gentle song. Mei shared her careful paws. Lila shared her bright ideas.

Soon, they had made a little town of welcome. There was a tall tower with a soft star pillow roof. There was a quiet reading corner with the globe that went whirr. There was a wide path for tiny feet to move and dance.

“Let’s make a path for everyone,” said Nia. “Some friends move fast. Some move slow. All can join.”

They lined up soft rugs in a winding trail. Swish, swish. Nia tested it with big steps. Step, step. Mei tried with tiny panda pads. Pat, pat. Amir trotted. Tip-tap. Lila zipped and hovered. Bzz-zip!

“Look,” said Nia. “We all move in our own way. We all belong.”

Then they heard another knock. Knock, knock.

Nia’s ears twitched. “More guests?”

At the door stood a little tortoise named Timo. He looked shy. His shell was shiny and green. “Hello,” he said softly. “I came from far away. May I rest here?”

Nia bent down so her eyes met his. “Yes, Timo,” she said kindly. “This is our guest room. We share it. We made a path for everyone.”

Timo smiled a slow smile. “Thank you,” he said.

Nia thought for a moment. “Friends,” she said, “let’s make one more thing. Let’s build a welcome sign in all our ways.”

She placed a big block at the front. Mei drew a heart on it with a red crayon. Amir painted a sunny swirl. Lila tapped little dots like stars. Timo added a tiny leaf.

Nia added a gentle message. She spoke it out loud so everyone could hear: “Welcome, friend. We care for you. We share this space. We build together.”

The room felt warm and bright. The tea smelled sweet. The blocks felt smooth. The globe hummed softly. Whirr.

Amir wagged his tail. “Nia, you were brave to invite us.”

Mei hugged a pillow. “You were kind to share.”

Lila zipped in a happy circle. “You helped us build.”

Timo sighed with joy. “You showed us we are one big world.”

Nia’s heart felt big and quiet. “We all did it together,” she said. “That is global citizenship. We care for friends near and far. We share. We build. We move together with courage.”

They sat in their little town of welcome. They sipped warm tea. They told gentle stories. They watched the globe spin. Whirr, whirr.

Nia looked around the guest room and smiled. “Our world fits right here,” she whispered. “And there is room for everyone.”

The End.
