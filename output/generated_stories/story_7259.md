# Bunny Words on the Porch

On a peaceful front porch, a determined rabbit named Rumi sat up tall. His whiskers twitched. The wood under his paws felt warm from the sun. The porch smelled like sweet tea and fresh hay. Birds sang, “tweet-tweet,” from the fence. A soft breeze brushed Rumi’s ears.

“I will learn new words today,” Rumi said. “Words are like little hugs for the heart.”

Rumi tapped his paw. He had a notebook made of big leaves and a tiny carrot-pen. He wrote the word “hello.” Then he smiled. “Hello is a friendly door,” he said. “It opens a talk.”

Just then, Tia the turtle climbed the steps. Her shell clicked softly on the porch. “Hullo,” Tia said in her slow, sweet voice.

Rumi blinked. “Do you mean hello?” he asked.

Tia nodded. “Yes. In my turtle family, we say hullo. It is how we learned.”

Rumi felt his ears warm. “I want to learn it,” he said quickly. “Hullo, Tia!” He wrote “hullo” in his leaf book. “This word feels round, like your shell.”

Tia smiled. “Words can feel like shapes,” she said.

A squirrel named Pip zipped by. He carried acorns and giggled. “Hola!” Pip chirped. “Hola means hello in my grandma’s words.”

Rumi’s eyes went wide. “Hola,” he tried, rolling the sound slowly. “Oh-la.”

Pip clapped his tiny paws. “Yes! Hola, Rumi!”

Rumi wrote “hola.” The carrot-pen squeaked. He sniffed the porch air. It smelled like pine cones now, from Pip’s tail.

A breeze brought new footsteps. Nia the songbird landed on the railing. Her feathers shone blue. “Jambo!” Nia sang. “It means hello too.”

“Jambo,” Rumi sang back. He liked the bounce. He felt happy bubbles in his chest. He wrote “jambo” and drew a tiny music note.

Soon, Benny the bear cub waddled up, holding a jar of honey. Sticky sweetness filled the air. “Hi,” Benny said shyly. “I brought sharing honey.”

“Hi, Benny!” Rumi said. “Thank you.”

Benny dipped a paw and passed a teensy taste to each friend. The honey was warm and golden. Rumi licked his lips. “Mmm,” he said. “Honey is like a smile you can taste.”

The porch was busy and bright with feelings and sounds. Rumi bounced and learned and listened. He felt proud.

Then a new friend stepped onto the porch. It was Lila the lamb. Her wool was soft and white, and her voice was gentle. “Salaam,” she said, bowing her head.

Rumi’s ears stood up. He was excited. He wanted to say all the words first. He rushed and said, “No, say hello first!”

Lila’s eyes grew round. Her smile fell. The porch got quiet. Even the birds looked down.

Rumi froze. He saw Lila’s face, and his heart drooped like a leaf in the rain. He had not listened. He had not been kind.

Tia whispered, “Rumi, different words can be the same love.”

Nia hummed softly. Pip tucked his tail. Benny held out the honey, but no one reached.

Rumi took a slow breath. He felt the porch boards under his paws again. He heard the wind. He remembered his goal: to learn words like little hugs.

He turned to Lila and bowed his head. “I am sorry,” he said in a small voice. “I did not listen. Please forgive me. I would like to learn your hello.”

Lila’s eyes softened. She took a tiny step closer. “I forgive you,” she said. “Thank you for asking. Salaam means peace.”

Rumi smiled, warm and real. “Salaam, Lila,” he said carefully. He tasted the “s” like a soft breeze. He felt the “laam” like a long cuddle.

Lila smiled back. “Salaam, Rumi,” she said. “Your words can be gentle.”

Rumi sat again and wrote “salaam” with slow strokes. He drew a small heart next to it. “Words are bridges,” he whispered. “Words can carry peace.”

Benny held up the honey. “Honey break?” he asked.

“Please,” said everyone.

They licked sweet golden drops. Pip giggled. Tia tapped a rhythm with her shell. Nia sang a tiny tune. The porch glowed with afternoon light. It smelled like honey and warm wood and happy friends.

Rumi looked at his leaf book. It had many ways to say hello: hello, hullo, hola, jambo, salaam, and hi. He tapped each word with his paw.

He said, “Hello is a door. Hullo is a round shell. Hola is a sunny wave. Jambo is a jump. Salaam is a soft peace. Hi is a small hop.”

Lila nodded. “Every word carries a feeling,” she said.

Rumi looked at his friends. “Today I learned to listen,” he said. “I learned to be kind. I learned to forgive and be forgiven. And I learned that every language is a gift.”

“Every language is a gift,” his friends echoed.

The sun began to rest. The porch felt sleepy and safe. Rumi closed his leaf book and hugged it close.

“Tomorrow,” he said, “I will learn thank you.”

“Gracias,” Pip chirped.

“Asante,” Nia sang.

“Shukran,” Lila smiled.

“Thank you,” Rumi said, listening carefully to each sound. He felt brave, calm, and glad.

And the peaceful front porch was full of warm words, sweet honey, and friends who listened with their hearts.

The End.
