# Pico Plans the Quiet

Pico was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a tiny blue hat he made from a folded paper cup. He loved his study corner. It was a cozy spot by the window with a small desk, a soft chair, some crayons in a tin that went tinka-tink when he shook it, and a little plant in a glass jar. The plant smelled like fresh rain. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor, and the air felt gentle and calm.

“Squawk, study time!” Pico chirped happily. He opened his notebook. It was full of drawings of trees, birds, and a big plan for saving paper. Pico cared about not wasting things. He kept a box of scrap paper for doodles and used both sides of every page.

Today, Pico wanted to make a “Save the Paper” poster for the library. He picked up a green crayon. “Plan first,” he told himself. “Planning helps me be clear.”

Pico drew a simple list with little stars:
- Use both sides
- Share your paper
- Recycle scraps
- Plant a tree

He nodded. “Good plan.” He took a deep breath. The room smelled like crayons and the lemon soap he used to clean the desk. He began to draw a friendly tree with round leaves.

Suddenly, there was a loud clatter. CLANG-CLANG! From the hallway, Kiki the kitten banged two spoons together like cymbals. “Ta-da!” Kiki sang. “I made music!”

Pico’s feathers ruffled. His heart made a quick thump. The noise made his head feel fizzy. “Oh,” he said softly. “I can’t think.”

Kiki ran into the study corner. Her whiskers twitched. “Play time?”

Pico wanted to scream, but he stopped. He placed a gentle wing on his chest and took a slow breath in. One, two, three. He felt the warm sunlight on his feathers. He remembered his plan: be clear, be kind.

Pico spoke in a calm, strong voice. “Kiki, I like your music. It sounds shiny. But I need quiet to work. I am making a poster to save paper. Can we make a plan so we both feel good?”

Kiki blinked. “A plan?”

“Yes,” said Pico. He pointed to his list. “I plan before I draw. We can plan our sounds, too.”

Kiki rolled a spoon in her paw. It made a tiny ting. “I want to play.”

Pico nodded. “I hear you. You want to play now. I need quiet now. Here is my idea.” He held up one feather. “Idea one: You can play in the kitchen for ten minutes. I will set the timer. Then I will take a break and listen to one song.” He held up a second feather. “Idea two: We can make soft music together. We can tap the spoons like raindrops, not thunder.”

Kiki’s ears perked. “Raindrop music?”

Pico smiled. “Yes. Soft and steady. Pitter-pat.”

Kiki thought. She looked at the spoons. She looked at the notebook. “I like idea one and idea two,” she said. “Kitchen first. Then raindrop song.”

“Thank you for listening,” Pico said. His voice was warm and firm. “I feel better when I say what I need.”

Pico set the timer. The timer clicked quietly. Kiki scampered away. The study corner grew calm again. Pico’s shoulders relaxed. He heard a little bird outside chirp, peep-peep. He smelled his plant’s fresh green scent. He went back to his poster.

With slow strokes, Pico colored the leaves. He used short lines to make the tree look soft and full. He added a smiling sun and a happy worm under the soil. He wrote, “Use both sides!” in big friendly letters. He practiced his words out loud, clear and brave. “Use, both, sides.”

The timer dinged. Kiki peeked in. “Time?” she asked.

“Break time,” Pico said. “I will listen to one song.” Kiki sat on the floor, paws tucked, and tapped the spoons softly. Ting… ting… ting… like raindrops on a window. Pico listened. His tail swished with the beat.

“That was lovely,” Pico said. “Thank you for playing softly.”

Kiki purred. “Can I help with the poster?”

“Yes,” said Pico. “We will use scrap paper to cut tiny leaves. That saves new paper.” He opened the tin of crayons, and it went tinka-tink again. Together they found small green pieces from the scrap box. Snip, snip. Glue dab, dab. The poster looked bright and alive.

On the bottom, Pico wrote, “Please respect quiet corners.” He read it to Kiki. “Respect means we care about others,” he explained. “We listen. We plan. We speak kindly and clearly.”

Kiki nodded. “Like you did.”

Pico felt proud. His chest felt warm, like sunshine. “Yes. I used my assertive voice. Not loud. Not hidden. Just clear.”

They hung the poster by the window. The plant’s leaves brushed the paper and made a soft shhh sound. Pico looked around the study corner. It felt peaceful, like a friendly hug.

Kiki whispered, “Can we do raindrop music now?”

Pico smiled. “Yes. Two minutes, soft and sweet.” They counted together, one, two, three, tapping like gentle rain. When they finished, Pico said, “Thank you for working with me. Our plan helped.”

Kiki grinned. “Plans and kind words!”

Pico nodded. “Plans and kind words help everyone. When we speak up, listen, and respect each other, we make room for learning and play. And when we use only what we need, we help the Earth too.”

The End.
