# Benny's Musical Garden

Benny the beaver loved building things with his strong teeth and busy paws. On this sunny morning, he waddled into the bright backyard, his fluffy tail dragging behind him. The warm sunshine tickled his brown fur, and he could smell the sweet flowers blooming everywhere.

"What should I build today?" Benny wondered aloud, tapping his chin with his tiny paw.

He started gathering sticks and leaves, making a big pile. But as he worked, something magical happened. The wind blew through the trees, making a soft "whoooosh" sound. A bird sang "tweet-tweet" from above. Even his own tail made a gentle "thump-thump" as it hit the ground.

"Oh my!" said Benny, his ears perking up. "Everything is making music!"

He stopped building and listened carefully. The bees hummed "bzzzz," the leaves rustled "swish-swish," and somewhere nearby, water trickled "drip-drop-drip."

Benny's eyes grew wide with excitement. "I want to make music too!"

He tried banging two sticks together. "Tap-tap-tap!" It sounded nice, but not quite right. He tried slapping his tail on the ground. "Thump!" Better, but still not perfect.

Benny sat down and felt frustrated. His little shoulders drooped. "I'm good at building, but I don't know how to make beautiful music," he sighed.

Then he remembered something his mama always told him: "When you have a problem, take a deep breath and think quietly."

Benny closed his eyes and breathed in slowly through his nose. The air smelled like roses and grass. He breathed out through his mouth and felt his body relax. As he sat still and listened, he heard all the backyard sounds again.

"Wait a minute!" Benny said, opening his eyes. "Maybe I can write down all these sounds and make a song!"

He found a smooth piece of bark and used a sharp stick to scratch marks on it. He drew wavy lines for the wind sounds, dots for the raindrops, and zigzags for the bird songs.

As he wrote, Benny started to hum along with the sounds around him. "Whoooosh, tweet-tweet, bzzzz," he sang softly. His voice mixed with all the natural music in the backyard.

A friendly robin landed nearby. "What a lovely song!" chirped the bird.

"Really?" asked Benny, his face lighting up.

"Oh yes! You're using all the sounds around you. That's the most beautiful kind of music – when you listen to the world and sing along with it!"

Benny felt proud and happy. He had solved his problem by being quiet, listening carefully, and writing down his ideas. He didn't need fancy instruments – the whole backyard was his orchestra!

From that day on, Benny would sit quietly in the sunny backyard every morning. He would listen to all the natural sounds, write them down on pieces of bark, and create new songs. Sometimes he would tap sticks, sometimes he would hum, and sometimes he would just listen.

The other animals loved Benny's music time. They would gather around to hear his gentle songs that mixed his voice with the sounds of wind, water, and wildlife.

Benny learned that making music wasn't about being perfect – it was about listening, being creative, and sharing joy with others.

The End.
