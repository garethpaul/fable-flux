# Cleo the Clever Cat Saves the Songs

Cleo was a clever cat with soft gray fur and bright green eyes. Her tail twitched like a tiny metronome. She loved to visit the musical practice room at the community center. The room smelled like polished wood and lemon cleaner. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Shiny drums, sleepy violins, a big piano, and a rainbow of recorders waited in a neat line.

One afternoon, Cleo padded in and heard, “Thump, thump,” from the big drum. She heard, “Ting-ting!” from the triangle. The piano went, “Plink, plonk, plunk.” Children laughed. Ms. Lina, the music teacher, smiled and clapped.

Cleo purred. Music made her whiskers dance.

But soon, Cleo’s ears twitched. The sink was running. Water rushed and rushed. Paper towels piled up in the trash. The lights were all on, even by the bright window. A fan hummed and hummed, but no one stood near it.

Cleo’s eyes grew wide. “So much waste,” she mewed softly. “We need to take care and share.”

“Meow?” asked Leo, a little boy with curly hair. He knelt to pet Cleo. “What is it, Cleo?”

Cleo sat tall. She had an idea, but she needed a plan. Strategy! She tapped the floor with her paw in a steady beat. Tap… tap… tap.

Ms. Lina noticed. “Is Cleo giving us a rhythm?” she said. “Let’s listen.”

Everyone grew quiet. Cleo purred a low, soft note. She pointed her paw to her ear. “Listen,” she seemed to say.

The children listened. They heard the running water. “Who left the sink on?” asked Maya.

They heard the humming fan. They saw the bright lights, even in sunny spots.

Cleo blinked slowly and looked at Ms. Lina. Ms. Lina nodded. “Class, Cleo wants us to use our ears and our hearts,” she said. “We can care for our room. We can make music and save, not waste.”

“How?” asked Ben.

Cleo had a strategy. She hopped to the whiteboard. With her paw, she tapped out a pattern on the tray: tap-tap, pause, tap. Then she pointed to three pictures on the wall: a droplet, a leaf, and a lightbulb.

“Oh!” said Maya. “A water plan, a planet plan, and a light plan!”

“Let’s make a song to help us remember,” said Ms. Lina. “Cleo will lead!”

Cleo’s tail swished. She stood by the sink. Ms. Lina played a gentle, bouncy tune on the piano: plink, plonk, plink-plink. The children clapped a soft beat. Cleo pressed the faucet closed with her paw. The water stopped. The room sighed, shhhhh, like a calm breeze.

“Verse one,” sang Ms. Lina. “Turn it off when you’re done.”

The children sang, “Turn it off, turn it off, when you’re done!”

Cleo smiled. She padded to the fan and pressed the button. The humming faded.

“Verse two,” sang Ms. Lina. “If no one needs it, off it goes!”

The children sang, “Off it goes, off it goes!”

Cleo trotted to the bright window and looked up at the lights. She blinked at Leo. He understood. He flipped the switch for lights by the sunny spots. The room still glowed warm and happy.

“Verse three,” sang Ms. Lina. “Use the sun, it’s bright and free!”

The children sang, “Bright and free, bright and free!”

Cleo looked at the pile of paper towels. She sniffed the air. Lemon and paper. She pawed at a small cloth towel hanging on a hook. She rubbed her paws and purred.

Maya said, “We can use cloth towels and wash them. We can use one paper towel only if we must.”

“Chorus!” said Ms. Lina. She played a cheerful tune. “Care for our room, care for our world, listen, think, and try. Save a little here and there, reach for low, not high!”

Cleo danced in a slow circle. Her paws made soft pat-pat sounds on the wood floor. The children clapped and giggled. The music felt light and kind.

Then came a problem. They were almost out of recorder reeds and drum sticks. “We need more,” said Ben. “Should we throw away the old box?”

Cleo sniffed the empty box. She pushed it to Leo and sat on it like a queen. “Meow,” she said, and tapped it with her paw. Tap-tap-tap.

“Cleo says reuse!” Leo laughed.

Ms. Lina nodded. “Great strategy. Let’s make a rhythm bin. We can turn this box into a shaker drum. We can store extra rubber bands and string for fixing things. We can mend, not toss.”

They worked together. They listened to Cleo’s cues. They added dry beans to the box. Shake-shake-shake! They wrapped worn drum sticks with soft tape. Tap-tap, not snap-snap. They labeled jars for buttons, screws, and broken parts to fix later.

Soon, the room felt different. It felt calm and bright. The air was cool, not cold. The lights were gentle. The sink was quiet. The fan hummed only when needed. The trash can was not full anymore.

Cleo curled up on the piano bench. She purred, a soft rumble like a tiny motor. Ms. Lina stroked her back. “Thank you, Cleo,” she whispered. “You helped us listen. You helped us make a plan.”

Maya said, “We saved water.”

Leo said, “We saved power.”

Ben said, “We reused and fixed things.”

Cleo blinked and gave a happy, slow kitty wink. The children grinned.

Ms. Lina played the chorus one more time. Everyone sang, “Care for our room, care for our world, listen, think, and try. Save a little here and there, reach for low, not high!”

Cleo’s tail twitched to the beat. She felt proud and warm. She knew that small steps made a big song for the Earth.

The clear lesson was simple: When we listen, make a plan, and use only what we need, we care for our world.

The End.
