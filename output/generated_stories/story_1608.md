# Tall Tilly’s Nursery Plan

In a bright, comfortable nursery with soft rugs and sunshine-yellow curtains, lived a thoughtful giraffe named Tilly. Tilly had kind eyes, long lashes, and a gentle heart. She loved to learn. She loved to plan. She even loved the smell of clean soap at the sink by the window.

One morning, Tilly stretched her long neck and took a deep breath. She could smell crayons, fresh apples, and a tiny bit of sleepy morning air. “Good morning, nursery,” Tilly whispered. “Today, I want to make a Learning Corner.”

Her friends—Benny the bunny, Mo the mouse, and Dot the duck—looked up from the block basket.

“A Learning Corner?” squeaked Mo. “What is that?”

“It’s a special place,” said Tilly, her voice warm and calm. “We can read, count, and draw. We can learn new things every day.”

Dot flapped her little wings. “Quack-yes! But where do we start?”

Tilly blinked slowly and smiled. “We start with a plan.”

She took a big piece of paper and a purple crayon. “Step one,” she said, writing carefully, “Wash hands.” She drew little bubbles and a happy sink.

“Why wash first?” asked Benny, twitching his nose.

“Clean hands help us stay healthy,” said Tilly. “Clean hands help our books and blocks stay happy, too.”

The friends lined up. Tilly turned on the warm water. It sounded like a tiny river—shhh, shhh. She pumped soap into Benny’s paws. “Rub, rub,” she sang softly. “Front and back, between, around. Count to twenty with a bubbly sound.”

They all counted together. “One, two, three…” Their voices made a gentle song. The soap smelled like lemons and the bubbles felt soft and silky. They rinsed and dried with fluffy towels.

“Step two,” Tilly said, drawing a small clock, “We make a schedule.” She printed the words in big, neat letters: Read, Count, Create, Clean Up.

Benny’s ears wiggled. “Schedules make me feel safe,” he said.

“Me too,” whispered Mo. “I like to know what comes next.”

“Step three,” Tilly continued, “We work as a team.” She drew four smiling faces holding a big book.

Dot waddled in a happy circle. “Teamwork! Quack!”

They got to work. The sun made warm shapes on the rug. The crayons smelled waxy and bright. The books felt smooth and cool under their paws and hooves. Tilly stacked picture books about colors and shapes. “Red, blue, yellow,” she hummed. “Circle, square, triangle.”

Mo pushed a basket of counting pebbles. “These feel like tiny river stones,” he said, patting them. “One, two, three…”

Benny lined up soft blocks by size. “Small, medium, big,” he said proudly.

Dot brought a box of clean brushes and paper. She sniffed the paper. “It smells crisp, like snow,” she said, giggling.

Soon the Learning Corner looked cozy and bright. But then—uh-oh! A tower of blocks started to wobble. “Waaa-obbly,” Benny gasped.

Tilly felt a little flutter in her tummy. She wanted to run and grab the blocks fast. But she took a slow breath. In, out. In, out. She put a hoof on her chest. “I can stay calm,” she told herself. “I can think first.”

She turned to the group. “Team, let’s pause,” Tilly said softly. “Let’s use our plan.”

Mo nodded. “Step three: teamwork.”

“Dot, please hold the bottom blocks steady,” Tilly said.

“Quack-okay!” Dot pressed her wings gently on the base.

“Benny, count the blocks as I restack them,” Tilly said.

“One, two, three, four,” Benny counted in a careful rhythm.

Tilly moved slowly and safely. She placed each block in the center. She checked with her eyes. She listened for the quiet thump of a block settling. The tower stood tall and still.

“We did it!” cheered Mo.

Tilly smiled. “Step four,” she wrote, adding a star, “We learn from what happens. The tower needed a strong base. We planned, stayed calm, and worked together.”

After a happy snack of apple slices—crunch, crunch—the friends tried their schedule.

First, Read. Tilly pointed to letters. “A is for apple, B is for bunny, C is for calm.” She looked at Benny and winked.

Next, Count. Mo lined up pebbles. “Ten pebbles feel cool and smooth,” he said. “Let’s count by tapping.” Tap, tap, tap. Their taps sounded like tiny raindrops.

Then, Create. Dot painted shapes with clean brushes. “I made a blue circle that looks like the moon,” she said. “And a yellow triangle like cheese.” Everyone giggled.

Finally, Clean Up. Tilly sang the hand-wash song again. They wiped the table with a fresh, soapy cloth. The room smelled bright and lemony. They put books in the basket and blocks on the shelf. The nursery looked neat and peaceful.

Benny hugged Tilly’s leg. “Your plan helped me feel brave,” he said.

Mo nodded. “Learning is fun when we do it together.”

Dot fluffed her feathers. “And clean hands make shiny art!”

Tilly’s heart felt warm. “Today we learned three big things,” she said gently. “Education helps our minds grow. Hygiene keeps us healthy. Teamwork makes hard jobs easier. When we plan and stay calm, we can solve little problems.”

The sun made a golden path across the rug. The Learning Corner waited, cozy and ready for tomorrow’s new ideas.

Tilly closed her eyes for a moment and breathed in the lemony, crayon-sweet air. “I am proud of us,” she whispered. “We learned with kind hearts.”

The End.
