# Panda Poppy’s Calm Shed Adventure

Panda Poppy was a patient panda with soft black-and-white fur and gentle eyes. One sunny morning, Poppy padded to the garden shed. The shed smelled like fresh soil and pine wood. It was full of colorful pots, shiny tools, and a small window that let sunshine splash on the floor.

Poppy opened the door. “Hello, shed,” Poppy whispered. “Let’s make art today!”

Inside the shed, three friends waited. There was Tico the tiny turtle, slow and green with a bright red shell sticker. There was Mina the mouse, quick and curious, with pink ears that twitched. And there was Gigi the goldfinch, yellow as a sunflower, perched on a broom handle.

“Hi, Poppy!” chirped Gigi. “We brought paints!”

“I brought a drum,” said Tico. “Boom. Boom.” He tapped gently.

“I brought glitter glue,” squeaked Mina, holding a shiny tube.

Poppy smiled. “We will make a kindness sign for the garden,” Poppy said. “It will say, ‘Please Be Gentle.’”

The friends set out brushes. The paint smelled sweet, like berries. The wood sign felt smooth under Poppy’s paw. Sunlight warmed everyone’s backs. Outside, bees buzzed and the leaves rustled softly.

“I will paint the letters,” said Poppy. “Big and clear.”

“I can paint little flowers,” said Mina.

“I can add blue dots,” said Gigi.

“I can drum a slow song,” said Tico. “Tap… tap… tap.”

They began. Poppy dipped a brush in red paint and made a big P. Mina painted tiny purple flowers. Gigi dotted blue dots like raindrops. Tico tapped a steady beat that sounded like a calm heart.

Suddenly, Mina’s tail swished, and a cup wobbled. Splash! Red paint dripped on Poppy’s paw. It felt cold and sticky.

“Oh!” Poppy blinked. Poppy’s chest felt tight. Poppy’s face felt hot. Poppy’s tummy felt rumbly. “I feel mad,” Poppy said softly. “My paw is messy, and my letter is smudged.”

Mina’s whiskers drooped. “I’m sorry, Poppy,” she squeaked. “I didn’t look where my tail went.”

Gigi fluttered closer. “We can fix it,” she said.

Tico looked up. “I can help. We can breathe,” he said.

Poppy nodded. “I will use my calm plan,” Poppy said. “When I feel mad, I stop. I take three belly breaths. Then I use kind words.”

Poppy set the brush down. She put a paw on her belly. “In… one,” Poppy whispered. The air smelled like paint and flowers. “Out… two.” Poppy felt her shoulders soften. “In… three.” Poppy’s tummy felt warm again.

“Thank you for saying sorry, Mina,” Poppy said. “Please ask before moving near my paint. I like my space.”

Mina nodded. “I will keep my tail close. Can we clean your paw?”

“Yes,” Poppy said. “That would help.”

Gigi brought a soft cloth. Tico rolled over a small bowl of water. Swish, swish. The paint came off. Poppy smiled. “Thank you,” Poppy said. “I feel better.”

They went back to work. Poppy painted slowly. “I like big, neat letters,” Poppy said. “It helps me feel calm.”

“I like tiny flowers,” Mina said. “They make my heart feel happy.”

“I like dots that look like rain,” Gigi chirped.

“I like gentle drumming,” said Tico. “It reminds me to be patient.”

Another friend came to the shed door. It was Noura the hedgehog, with soft brown spines and bright eyes. “Hello,” she said. “May I watch? I brought orange yarn.”

“Yes,” said Poppy. “We can use your yarn to make a sunny border. But please ask before touching the brushes. I need my own space while I paint.”

“I understand,” said Noura. “Thank you for telling me your boundary.”

They worked together. Noura tied the yarn around the edges. It felt fuzzy and warm. Gigi sang, “Dot, dot, dot,” as she painted. Mina hummed. Tico drummed a gentle tap-tap.

Then, a little problem happened again. Gigi flapped her wings excitedly and knocked a tiny jar of glitter. Sparkle dust rained down, landing on Poppy’s nose. It tickled. Poppy’s eyes crossed. “Ah-choo!”

Everyone froze.

Poppy felt a quick spark of mad again. “I feel mad,” Poppy said honestly. “I don’t like surprise glitter on my nose.”

Gigi drooped. “I’m sorry, Poppy. I forgot to fold my wings.”

Poppy took a breath. “It’s okay to be mad,” Poppy said. “I will use my calm plan.” Poppy took three belly breaths. “Please keep your wings close in the shed,” Poppy said. “I need space.”

“Thank you for telling me,” Gigi said. “I will keep my wings tucked.”

They cleaned the glitter with a soft brush. Poppy giggled. “It sparkles like stars!”

At last, the sign was done. Big, red letters read, “Please Be Gentle.” Tiny purple flowers danced around the words. Blue dots twinkled like rain. Orange yarn made a sunny frame. Tico added his drum, tap-tap, as they carried the sign outside.

They hung the sign on the garden fence. The breeze smelled like mint leaves. Bees buzzed hello.

Poppy looked at the sign and at her friends—small and big, feathered and furry, all different, all kind. “We made this together,” Poppy said. “We used calm breaths. We said sorry. We asked before touching. We shared our creativity.”

Mina nodded. “We told the truth about our feelings.”

Gigi chirped. “We respected boundaries.”

Tico smiled. “We were patient.”

Noura clapped. “We showed that everyone belongs.”

Poppy patted the sign. “When we feel mad, we can pause, breathe, and use kind words,” Poppy said. “That helps our hearts feel safe.”

Everyone cheered. The garden felt warm and bright. The shed stood cozy and calm, ready for the next adventure.

The End.
