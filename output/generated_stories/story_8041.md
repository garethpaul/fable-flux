# Penny Panda's Musical Mistake

Penny Panda loved music more than anything in the whole world. Every morning, she would waddle into the cozy practice room with her favorite bamboo flute tucked under her fuzzy arm. The room smelled like fresh wood and had soft carpet that felt good under her paws.

"Good morning, room!" Penny would say cheerfully, looking at all the shiny instruments hanging on the walls. There were drums that went *boom-boom*, bells that went *ting-ting*, and a big piano with keys that looked like a smile.

Today, Penny's friend Benny Bear was coming to practice with her. Benny played the little drum, and together they made beautiful music. Penny was so excited that she started playing her flute right away.

*Toot-toot-toooooot!* The happy sounds filled the room.

When Benny arrived, he was carrying his small red drum. "Hi Penny! I brought my drum for our song!"

But Penny was having so much fun playing by herself that she didn't stop. *Toot-toot-TOOOOT!* Her flute got louder and louder.

"Penny," said Benny softly, "can I play too?"

Penny kept playing. She was in her own musical world and didn't hear her friend.

Benny tried again. "Penny, remember we were going to play our song together?"

But Penny's flute sounds were so loud that she still didn't hear Benny. The little bear's ears drooped down, and his eyes got watery. He felt sad and left out.

Finally, Penny stopped playing to take a breath. That's when she saw Benny sitting in the corner, looking very sad with his drum silent beside him.

"Benny?" Penny said, her voice getting quiet. "Why do you look sad?"

"I wanted to play music with you," Benny said with a sniffle. "But you didn't listen when I asked. I felt like you didn't want to be my music friend anymore."

Penny's tummy felt funny - not good funny, but worried funny. She looked at her flute, then at Benny's sad face, and suddenly understood. She had been so excited about her own music that she forgot about her friend's feelings.

"Oh no," Penny said, setting down her flute carefully. "Benny, I made a big mistake."

She walked over to her friend and sat down beside him on the soft carpet.

"I'm really sorry, Benny," Penny said, looking right into his eyes. "I was so excited about playing my flute that I forgot to listen to you. That wasn't kind, and it hurt your feelings. I'm sorry I made you feel left out."

Benny's ears perked up a little. "You are?"

"Yes," nodded Penny. "Friends should listen to each other. Playing music together is much more fun than playing alone. Will you forgive me?"

Benny smiled and hugged his panda friend. "Of course! Thank you for saying sorry, Penny."

"Now," said Penny, picking up her flute, "let's make beautiful music together! You start with your drum, and I'll join in softly."

Benny's face lit up like sunshine. *Tap-tap-tap* went his little drum. Then Penny joined in with gentle *toot-toots* that matched perfectly.

Together, they filled the practice room with the most wonderful music - the kind that sounds best when friends play together.

From that day on, Penny always remembered to listen to her friends and share her music. And whenever she made a mistake, she knew that saying "sorry" was the first note in making things right again.

The End.
