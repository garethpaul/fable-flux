# Tall Spots and Tiny Dots

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with warm brown spots and long, gentle eyelashes. Today, Gigi stepped into the creative art room. The room smelled like orange paint and clean paper. Sunlight danced on jars of brushes. Soft music hummed like a friendly bee.

Gigi whispered, “Good morning, colors.” Her voice was low and kind.

On the big table were paints, glue, scissors, buttons, feathers, and paper that felt smooth under her hoof. There was a tiny easel for small friends, and a tall easel just right for Gigi’s long neck.

Ms. Apple, the art teacher, smiled. “Today, we make a giant picture together,” she said. “We will need teamwork.”

Gigi’s ears twitched. “Teamwork means we help each other,” she said softly. “And we share.”

Her friends hopped in. Pip the puppy wagged his tail. “I like dots!” he barked.

Lulu the ladybug fluttered. “I like glitter,” she buzzed.

Tito the turtle blinked slowly. “I like glue,” he said, careful and calm.

Gigi looked at the blank paper rolled across the wall. It was as long as a train. “What should we make?” she asked.

Pip barked, “A playground!”

Lulu buzzed, “A rainbow!”

Tito said, “A sunny pond.”

Gigi took a deep breath. The room smelled a little like crayons now. “We can make all three,” she said. “A rainbow over a playground by a sunny pond.”

Ms. Apple clapped. “Great plan. Think about jobs. Think about what you can do on your own, and what you can do together.”

Gigi nodded. “I can paint the tall sky because my neck is long.”

Pip wagged. “I can make tiny dot flowers.”

Lulu giggled. “I can sprinkle gentle glitter.”

Tito smiled. “I can glue the big paper sun.”

They began. Gigi dipped a big brush into blue paint. Swish, swish, swish. The sound was like soft rain. Pip dabbed dot, dot, dot with bright pink. Lulu shook glitter like stars. Tito tapped glue in slow, careful circles.

Soon, Gigi saw a problem. “Oh,” she said. “I cannot see the bottom of the paper. My neck is too tall.”

Pip’s dots were all low. “I can’t reach up high,” he panted.

Lulu’s glitter sparkled, but some fell where Tito wanted glue. Tito frowned a tiny frown. “My glue is sticky. The glitter sticks to me.”

Gigi’s heart felt like a tiny drum. Thump-thump. She did not want anyone to be upset. She was thoughtful. She wanted to help.

“Team meeting,” Gigi said gently.

Ms. Apple nodded. “Good idea.”

Gigi lowered her long neck so her face was close to her friends. She could smell the sweet glue and the leafy smell of paper. She spoke slowly. “We each can do some things on our own. And we can also help each other.”

Pip’s ears perked. “How?”

Gigi smiled. “Pip, you can make dot flowers in the low field. When I paint the sky, I can tilt my brush so drips don’t fall on your flowers. Lulu, you can sprinkle glitter after Tito presses the glue, not before. Tito, can you tell Lulu when you are ready?”

Tito nodded. “I can say, ‘Ready for glitter now.’”

Lulu buzzed, “I like that!”

Pip spun in a little circle. “And how can I help you, Gigi?”

Gigi blinked. “I can’t see the bottom. Will you be my eyes down low?”

“Yes!” Pip barked. “I will say, ‘More to the left!’ or ‘A little up!’”

Gigi’s chest felt warm. This was teamwork.

They tried again. Swish, swish went Gigi’s brush across the high sky. Pip called, “More to the left! Now perfect!” Dot, dot went his tiny flowers. Tito pressed the glue for the sun and said, “Ready for glitter now.” Lulu sprinkled, “Shimmer-shimmer!” The glitter fell like quiet snow onto the sticky glue, not onto Tito’s shell. Tito smiled.

Ms. Apple brought a step stool. “Would this help?” she asked.

Gigi used the stool to see the middle of the paper. “Thank you,” she said. “That is good teamwork too.”

As they worked, they each had little moments of independence. Pip chose red and yellow dots all by himself. “I like these,” he said proudly.

Lulu made a tiny glitter trail for a butterfly she imagined. “It’s a pretend friend,” she whispered.

Tito cut a circle sun with steady snips. “Snip, snip,” he said, calm and sure.

Gigi painted fluffy clouds that looked like pillows. “These clouds feel soft to my eyes,” she said, smiling.

At last, the giant picture glowed. A rainbow curved over a bright playground. Swings and slides shone with bold color. A sunny pond sparkled with glittering water. Flowers dotted the ground. The sky was big and blue, with gentle, puffy clouds.

They all stepped back. The room was quiet for a breath. Then everyone cheered.

Ms. Apple clapped again. “You worked together. You shared. You used your own ideas. And you helped each other.”

Gigi felt tall in her heart. “Teamwork makes big art,” she said.

Pip wagged. “And big smiles!”

Lulu twirled. “And shiny friends!”

Tito nodded. “And a happy pond.”

Gigi looked at her friends. “We can be independent and still be a team,” she said softly. “We can use our imagination and our kind words.”

They hung the picture on the wall. It rustled like a small breeze. The room smelled like fresh paint and glitter. Everyone was proud.

Gigi whispered, “Thank you, team.” Her friends whispered, “Thank you, Gigi.”

The lesson was clear in their hearts: When we work together and listen, our ideas can fit like puzzle pieces, and we can make something wonderful.

The End.
