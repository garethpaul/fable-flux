# Kiri Koala and the Friendly Fix-It Day

Kiri the calm koala loved quiet places. One sunny morning, she padded softly to the garden shed. The shed smelled like warm wood and fresh soil. A little window let in a golden square of light. Dust danced like tiny stars. Kiri smiled and whispered, “Good morning, shed.”

Inside, there were pots, a watering can, and a basket of soft rags. There were also tools, like a gentle brush and a small hammer, all resting on hooks. Kiri liked how everything had a home. It made her heart feel steady and slow, like her breath.

Then Kiri heard a rustle-rustle and a clink-clink. “Oh?” she murmured. She peeked behind a stack of flower pots. Two friends were there! Pip the mouse was tugging a string. Beebo the beetle was nudging a shiny bolt. Their whiskers and antennae wiggled with worry.

“Hello, Pip. Hello, Beebo,” Kiri said in her soft, kind voice. “What’s happening?”

Pip squeaked, “Eep! The watering can is stuck! We want to water the new bean plant, but the spout is wobbly. I pull, Beebo pushes. It makes a clank and a clunk, but no fix!”

Beebo buzzed, “Click-click?” He tapped the bolt with a tiny claw.

Kiri took a deep, slow breath. In… and out. She placed her paw on her chest. “Let’s talk calmly. We can fix things with peaceful words,” she said. “First, let’s listen. One at a time.”

Pip’s ears perked. “I feel worried,” he said. “The bean is thirsty.”

Beebo nodded. “I feel rushed,” he buzzed. “I want to hurry, but my taps are too loud.”

Kiri smiled. “Thank you for telling me your feelings. That helps. When we share our feelings, our hearts feel lighter. Now let’s make a plan.”

She showed her paw. “Step 1: We breathe. In… and out.” Everyone breathed together. The shed felt calm, like a soft blanket.

“Step 2: We use gentle words,” Kiri said. “We say, ‘Please,’ and ‘Thank you,’ and ‘Can we try…?’”

“Step 3: We take turns,” she added. “One helper at a time.”

Pip and Beebo nodded. They looked less ruffled.

Kiri examined the watering can. The spout wiggled. The bolt was loose. “Hmm,” she hummed. “We need the small wrench. Where is it?”

Pip pointed to a high shelf. “Up there!”

Kiri took initiative. She found a sturdy stool. Tap, tap went her soft feet as she moved it. “I will climb safely,” she said. “I will go slowly.” She climbed up, feeling the smooth wood under her paws. She smelled the clean metal of the tools. She picked the small wrench. “Got it,” she whispered.

Back on the floor, Kiri said, “Let’s use our calm plan.”

Pip asked, “Can I hold the can steady, please?”

“Yes, thank you,” said Kiri.

Beebo buzzed, “May I shine my light?” He lifted his tiny glow-worm friend from his back. The little light made the bolt glimmer.

“Thank you,” Kiri said. “That helps so much.”

Kiri turned the bolt with the small wrench. Turn, turn, snug. The sound was soft, like a click-kiss. “Almost there,” she cooed.

Suddenly, Pip’s paw slipped. The can leaned. “Oh no!” he squeaked.

Kiri held up a paw. “Pause,” she said gently. “Accidents happen. Let’s try again.” She looked at Pip’s eyes. “Are you okay?”

Pip nodded. “I feel a little scared. I will hold with two paws.”

“Good sharing,” said Kiri. “That is peaceful-communication.”

They tried again. Pip held steady. Beebo kept the light still. Kiri turned the bolt—click, click—until it was just right. “All snug!” Kiri cheered softly.

Pip smiled wide. “Thank you, Kiri.”

Beebo buzzed a happy tune. “Brrrr-rrr!”

“Let’s test the can,” Kiri said. She filled it at the tap just outside the door. The water sounded like a tiny river, splish-splash. The can did not drip. It felt balanced and strong.

Together, they walked to the bean plant. The garden smelled like mint and sunshine. A butterfly flittered by. Kiri tilted the can. The water poured in a gentle arc. The soil turned dark and damp. The bean leaf looked shiny and new.

“My heart feels calm,” said Pip.

“My body feels well,” buzzed Beebo. “No more rush!”

Kiri stretched and smiled. “We did it together. We listened, we used kind words, and we took turns. That is peaceful-communication. And we took initiative by making a plan and getting the wrench. Helping and staying calm is good for wellness.”

They returned to the shed. Kiri wiped the wrench with a soft rag. Pip rolled up the string. Beebo tapped the hook labels. “Home, home, home,” he buzzed, as each tool went back.

Before they left, Kiri placed a small note on the shelf. It read, “Breathe. Use gentle words. Take turns.” She drew a tiny heart and a smiling watering can.

Pip read the note aloud slowly. “Breathe. Use gentle words. Take turns.”

Beebo hummed. “I will remember.”

Kiri gave them a cozy koala hug. Her fur felt warm and fluffy. “When we speak with peace, our work feels easy. When we care for ourselves and each other, we grow strong like the bean.”

Outside, the sun was soft and low. The bean plant shivered in a sweet breeze.

Kiri waved. “See you tomorrow for another calm adventure.”

“See you!” chirped Pip and buzzed Beebo.

They all felt proud, peaceful, and well.

The End.
