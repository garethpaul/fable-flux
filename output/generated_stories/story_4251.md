# Penny Penguin's Special Guest Room

Penny Penguin waddled into Grandma's cozy guest room. The soft blue blanket felt smooth under her flippers. But something was wrong. Penny felt worried and mixed up.

"Everything is different here," Penny said to herself. She looked around the unfamiliar room. The bed was bigger than hers. The lamp was yellow, not pink. Even the window showed different trees.

At home, Penny had her special morning routine. First, she would make her bed. Then she would write in her journal. After that, she would organize her toys. But here in the guest room, Penny didn't know what to do.

"I miss my routine," Penny sighed. Her tummy felt fluttery. "How can I feel happy without my special morning habits?"

Just then, Grandma Goose knocked gently on the door.

"Good morning, sweet Penny," Grandma said with a warm smile. "How did you sleep?"

"The bed was nice," Penny said quietly. "But I feel confused. I don't have my morning routine here."

Grandma's eyes twinkled. "Would you like to create a new guest room routine? We could make it special together."

Penny's black and white head perked up. "Really? We could do that?"

"Of course!" Grandma said. "Routines help us feel safe and happy anywhere we go. Let's start by making this bed together."

Penny and Grandma pulled the soft blue blanket tight. They fluffed the pillows until they were puffy and round. Penny felt a little better already.

"What comes next in your home routine?" Grandma asked.

"I write in my journal," Penny said. "But I forgot to bring it."

Grandma opened the desk drawer. Inside was a small notebook with seashells on the cover. "This guest room journal is for special visitors like you," she said.

Penny's eyes grew wide. "For me?"

"Yes! You can write about your visit. Maybe draw pictures too."

Penny carefully held the pencil in her flipper. She wrote her name at the top of the first page. The letters were wobbly but proud. Then she drew a picture of herself and Grandma making the bed.

"This feels good," Penny said, her voice getting happier.

"Now, what's the last part of your routine?" Grandma asked.

"I organize my toys," Penny said.

Together, they arranged Penny's small suitcase. They put her pajamas in one corner and her books in another. Everything had its place.

"There!" Penny said with a big smile. "Now I have a guest room routine!"

The next morning, Penny woke up feeling excited. She made the bed with the blue blanket. She wrote in her seashell journal about the yummy fish Grandma cooked for dinner. She organized her things in the suitcase.

"My routine works anywhere!" Penny told Grandma at breakfast. "It makes me feel at home even when I'm visiting."

"That's the magic of routines," Grandma said. "They travel with you in your heart."

When other penguin cousins came to visit, Penny showed them the guest room routine. She taught them how to make the bed and write in the journal. Soon, all the little penguins felt happy and comfortable too.

"Routines help everyone feel included and special," Penny realized.

From that day on, Penny knew that good routines could make any place feel like home. She had learned that sharing routines with others made them even more wonderful.

The End.
