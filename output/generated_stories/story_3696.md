# Pippin's Big Voice

In the cozy nursery with soft blue walls and fluffy white clouds painted on the ceiling, lived a cheerful penguin named Pippin. His black and white feathers were always neat, and his orange beak gleamed in the warm sunlight streaming through the window.

Every day, the nursery children played together on the rainbow carpet. They built tall towers with colorful blocks, read picture books, and shared their favorite toys. Pippin loved watching them, but he always stayed quietly in the corner by the rocking chair.

One sunny morning, Pippin noticed something troubling. Big Bear, the largest stuffed animal in the nursery, was taking toys from the smaller animals without asking.

"Give me that red ball," Big Bear said gruffly to Little Lamb. "I want to play with it now."

Little Lamb's soft white wool trembled. "But I was playing with it first," she whispered so quietly that Big Bear pretended not to hear.

Pippin watched as Big Bear took the ball anyway. His orange feet shuffled nervously on the soft carpet. "Someone should say something," Pippin thought. "That's not fair to Little Lamb."

Next, Big Bear marched over to Bunny and grabbed her favorite purple crayon. "I need this for my picture," he announced.

Bunny's pink nose twitched sadly, but she didn't speak up. She just looked down at her half-finished drawing.

Pippin's heart felt heavy watching his friends feel sad. He took a deep breath, puffing out his round white belly. "I need to help them," he decided. "Even though I'm nervous, I can use my voice to stand up for what's right."

Waddle, waddle, waddle. Pippin's orange feet carried him across the soft nursery floor. His flippers felt shaky, but he stood up tall.

"Excuse me, Big Bear," Pippin said in a clear, polite voice. "I noticed you took Little Lamb's ball and Bunny's crayon without asking. That doesn't feel kind."

Big Bear looked surprised. "But I'm bigger than them," he said.

"Being big doesn't mean you can take things without permission," Pippin explained gently but firmly. "We all deserve to be treated with respect, no matter our size."

Pippin turned to his friends. "Little Lamb, Bunny, you can use your voices too. It's okay to say 'no' when someone isn't being fair."

Little Lamb lifted her fluffy head. "You're right, Pippin. Big Bear, please give me back my ball. I was still playing with it."

Bunny straightened her long ears. "And I need my purple crayon to finish my picture, please."

Big Bear looked around at all the faces watching him. His expression softened. "I'm sorry," he said, handing back the toys. "I didn't think about how you felt. Can we all play together instead?"

"That's a wonderful idea!" Pippin smiled, his orange beak bright with happiness.

From that day on, Pippin felt proud of his brave voice. He learned that speaking up for himself and others wasn't mean—it was caring. Little Lamb and Bunny practiced using their voices too, and Big Bear learned to ask before taking things.

The nursery became an even happier place where everyone felt safe to speak up and be heard.

As the golden afternoon sun filled the cozy room, Pippin snuggled into his favorite spot, knowing that even small penguins can make a big difference when they use their voices with kindness and courage.

The End.
