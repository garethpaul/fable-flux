# Penny Penguin Paints and Helps

Penny was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a curious waddle. She loved to sit on her peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like fresh soap and lemon wood. The breeze felt cool on her feathers. Birds chirped, and the porch swing creaked, squeak-squeak, in a friendly way.

Penny had a box of colorful chalk. Red, blue, yellow, green! She tapped each stick, tap-tap, and smiled. “Today, I will make porch art,” she said. “I will make our porch bright and kind.”

She drew a big sun. She drew a cozy house. She drew a smiling fish. “Hello, sunny sun! Hello, cozy house!” she giggled. She hummed a happy tune, la-la-la.

Just then, Mrs. Goose from next door waddled by. She carried a heavy basket of flyers. The papers fluttered like tiny wings. “Oh dear,” Mrs. Goose said. “I need help. We are having a community clean-up. I must tell everyone. But I am just one goose.”

Penny’s heart felt warm. “I can help,” she said. “I like to help. I can volunteer!”

Mrs. Goose smiled. “Thank you, Penny. Your front porch is very peaceful. May I rest a moment?”

“Of course,” said Penny. “Please sit.” Penny brought a cool cup of lemon water and a soft cushion. She felt proud. Helping felt good, like sunshine in her tummy.

Penny looked at her chalk. She had an idea. “Mrs. Goose,” she said, “I can draw signs right here on the porch steps. Big, bright signs! They can say, ‘Come help! We can do it!’ Then neighbors will see and join.”

Mrs. Goose clapped her wings. “What a smart, kind idea!”

Penny drew big letters. She made happy stars and hearts around the words. She added little brooms and smiling trash bags. She drew a path of footprints that pointed to the sidewalk. The chalk felt powdery and soft in her flippers. The porch smelled like lemon and dust.

Soon, Mr. Squirrel came by with a bag of nuts. He stopped and read. “Volunteers needed? I can help!” he said. “I like to sweep.”

“Thank you!” Penny said. She handed him a tiny paper with the time. “See you later.”

Then, Little Bunny hopped over. “Can I help too? I am small, but I am quick.”

“Yes,” Penny said. “Small helpers are big helpers too.”

Penny felt a tug on her flipper. It was Tilly Turtle. “I am shy,” Tilly whispered. “I want to help, but I am slow.”

Penny bent down. She spoke gently. “You can help in your own way. You can pass out water. You can smile at people. Smiles help a lot.”

Tilly smiled a tiny smile. “I can do that,” she said.

Just then, two noisy crows flapped onto the porch rail. “Your chalk is messy,” one crow cawed. “Go play somewhere else.”

Penny’s heart thumped. She held her flippers close. She took a deep breath. The breeze touched her face, cool and kind. She stood tall, as tall as a penguin can.

“I hear you,” Penny said in a clear, calm voice. “I am making art to help our town. I am allowed to draw on my porch. Please do not be unkind. You may watch, or you may go.”

The crows blinked. They shuffled. “Hmm,” said one. “You spoke up nicely.” They flew off, caw-caw, into the blue sky.

Penny felt brave. “I used my strong voice,” she whispered. “I was kind and firm.”

At last, the time for the clean-up came. Neighbors gathered by Penny’s porch because they saw her signs. The group smelled like fresh grass and soap bubbles. Buckets clanked. Brooms swished. “Thank you for volunteering!” Mrs. Goose called.

Penny passed out gloves. She gave Tilly Turtle the water table job. “You are in charge of drinks,” Penny said. “This is important.”

Tilly nodded. “I can do it,” she said, steady and proud.

Everyone worked together. Swish-sweep went the brooms. Plop went the trash into bags. Penny hummed as she drew a big thank-you mural on the porch step. She drew all her friends. She drew Mr. Squirrel with a broom. She drew Little Bunny with a bag. She drew Tilly with cups. She even drew the crows flying in the sky, because she was fair.

A soft rain cloud drifted in. Plip-plop, plip-plop. The chalk colors melted and swirled. The steps turned into a rainbow puddle. “Oh no,” said Little Bunny.

“It’s okay,” Penny said. “The art did its job. It helped people come. Art can be a sign. Art can be a smile. Art can be a plan.”

When the rain stopped, the street looked neat and clean. The air smelled fresh, like after a bath. Mrs. Goose stood on Penny’s peaceful porch and said, “Friends, thank you. Penny, your volunteering and your art helped us all. And you used a strong, kind voice. That was brave.”

Penny’s cheeks felt warm with joy. She waddled to the swing and sat with her friends. Squeak-squeak went the swing. The sun peeked out and made tiny sparkles on the wet steps.

Penny said, “Today I learned three things. Helping others feels good. Art can help people come together. And when I need to, I can speak up with a kind, strong voice.”

Everyone nodded. “We can help too,” they said.

Penny smiled. “Let’s keep volunteering. Little by little, we can make our world bright.”

The End.
