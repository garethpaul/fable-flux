# Ollie the Otter Builds Fair and Square

Ollie the playful otter wiggled into the sunny guest room. The room smelled like clean sheets and warm dust. Light made soft squares on the carpet. A big box of wooden blocks sat by the window.

“Ooo!” Ollie squeaked. “Blocks! Let’s build something amazing!”

He clapped his little paws. Tap tap! His whiskers twitched. His heart felt bubbly and bright.

“First,” he said, “I need a plan.” He spread the blocks on the carpet: long blocks, short blocks, red, blue, yellow. The blocks thunked and clicked together. Ollie liked the sounds.

He picked up a long block. “This will be a bridge,” he told himself. “A strong, fair bridge for everyone.”

Just then, his friends peeked in. Miko the mouse, Tula the turtle, and Pip the puffin stepped softly onto the rug.

“What are you building?” asked Miko, her nose twitching.

“A bridge!” said Ollie. “Do you want to help? I’m volunteering to lead, and I’d love volunteers to build with me!”

Pip flapped happily. “I can stack tall!”

Tula nodded slowly. “I can make things steady.”

Miko smiled. “I can line up little pieces.”

Ollie beamed. “Great! We’ll work as a team. We’ll build with fairness and coordination.”

“What is coor-uh-dah-shun?” asked Miko.

Ollie thought. “Coordination means we move and work together. Like a dance. We take turns. We listen. We help.”

Everyone agreed. Pip puffed out his chest. “Let’s start!”

Ollie drew a simple plan on a notepad with big shapes. “Here is the start. Here is the middle. Here is the end. Everyone gets a job.”

- Tula, you place the base blocks, nice and flat.
- Miko, you line up the small blocks along the sides.
- Pip, you stack the tall posts, one at a time.
- I will guide and carry extra blocks.

“Fair?” Ollie asked.

“Fair!” they all said.

Tula pushed the heavy base blocks. Thunk. Thunk. The carpet felt bumpy under her shell. “Slow and steady,” she hummed.

Miko clicked small blocks in a neat line. Click. Click. “In a row,” she sang softly.

Pip set a tall post. “Up it goes!” he chirped. Plunk!

Ollie carried blocks from the box. The wood felt smooth and warm. “You’re doing great,” he said. “Thank you for volunteering your time.”

Soon, the bridge grew. It was as long as the bed and as tall as a chair seat. Sunlight warmed their backs. The room smelled like wood and a little like Pip’s sea-salt feathers.

But then, Pip flapped too fast. He grabbed three tall blocks at once. “I can stack them quicker! I want the tall job more!”

The blocks wobbled. Wibble wobble… CRASH! The tall post tumbled. Miko squeaked and jumped back. Tula blinked.

Ollie’s tummy felt twisty. He took a slow breath. “It’s okay,” he said gently. “Mistakes happen.”

Pip’s eyes got wet. “I… I wanted more turns. I wanted the tallest job.”

Ollie nodded. “Thanks for saying how you feel. Fairness means everyone gets turns. Not too much for one, not too little for others.”

Miko raised her paw. “Can I try one tall post?”

Tula added, “We can share jobs. We can switch.”

Ollie clapped softly. “Yes! Let’s make a fairness plan.” He drew three big circles on the notepad. “We will rotate. After five blocks, we switch. We will also count together to stay coordinated.”

He smiled at Pip. “You still get turns. And you can help others when it’s their turn.”

Pip sniffled, then nodded. “Okay. I can help steady the tall post with my wings.”

“Team ready?” Ollie asked.

“Ready!” said Tula, Miko, and Pip.

They moved like a gentle dance.

Ollie counted, “One… two… three… four… five.” His voice was calm and kind.

- Tula placed base blocks. Pip held the sides steady.
- Miko tried a tall post. Pip whispered, “Nice and straight.” Plunk!
- Pip lined up small blocks this time, making careful rows.
- Then they switched again.

The bridge grew stronger. It didn’t wobble now. Their hands and paws and wings worked together. The room filled with soft sounds: click, thunk, plunk, and quiet counting. They took little breaks for sips of water and deep breaths. The guest room felt cozy and happy.

At last, Ollie set the last block. “Done!” he said.

They all cheered. The bridge stood straight and fair, with room for everyone to cross. It had bright colors and smooth sides. Sunlight made it shine.

“Let’s test it,” said Tula.

They each rolled a toy car across. Vrrr. Vrrr. No wobbles. No crashes. Just smooth rolling and big smiles.

Pip puffed with pride. “We built it together. Sharing turns felt good.”

Miko giggled. “I liked trying the tall post.”

Tula smiled. “I liked helping steady.”

Ollie’s heart felt warm. “We used a plan. We volunteered to help. We were fair. We coordinated. That made our building strong.”

They put a little sign on the bridge. Ollie wrote in big letters: “Fair and Square Bridge. Built by Friends.”

Then they cleaned up the extra blocks together. They stacked them neatly back in the box. Helping felt like part of the building too.

Ollie turned to his friends. “What did we learn?”

Pip said, “Building is better when we share turns.”

Miko added, “Fairness means everyone gets a chance.”

Tula said, “Coordination is moving together, slow and steady.”

Ollie nodded. “And volunteering means we choose to help. When we help, we make good things.”

They sat on the soft carpet and looked at the bridge. The guest room felt bright and peaceful. Ollie’s whiskers twitched with joy.

“Let’s build again tomorrow,” he said.

“Together,” they agreed.

The End.
