# Pip's Splashy Mistake

Pip the penguin loved bath time! His black and white feathers gleamed under the warm bathroom lights. He waddled happily across the cool tile floor, flippers flapping with excitement.

"Splash time!" Pip chirped, diving into the big white bathtub.

SPLASH! SPLASH! SPLASH!

Water flew everywhere! It splashed on the mirror. It splashed on the fluffy towels. It even splashed on Ruby the rubber duck, who was floating peacefully nearby.

"Oh my!" squeaked Ruby, spinning around in the waves. "That's quite a splash, Pip!"

Pip giggled and splashed even bigger. WHOOSH! More water went flying.

"Wheee! This is so fun!" Pip called out, not noticing Ruby's worried face.

But then Pip saw something that made his happy flippers stop moving. Ruby wasn't smiling anymore. Her bright yellow face looked sad, and she was shivering in the corner of the tub.

"Ruby? Are you okay?" Pip asked, his cheerful voice becoming quiet.

Ruby's small voice trembled. "The big splashes are scary, Pip. They push me around and make me feel dizzy. I was trying to have a peaceful float."

Pip's heart felt heavy, like a stone sinking in water. He looked around the bathroom. Water droplets covered everything. The once-dry towels were now soaked. Poor Ruby looked very upset.

"Oh no," Pip whispered, his orange beak drooping. "I didn't know I was being too splashy. I was having so much fun, I forgot to think about you."

Pip's black eyes filled with tears. He felt sorry in his whole penguin body - from his webbed feet to the tip of his head.

"Ruby, I'm really sorry," Pip said, swimming closer gently. "I didn't mean to scare you or make you feel bad. I should have asked if big splashes were okay first."

Ruby looked at Pip's sincere face. She could see he truly felt sorry.

"Thank you for saying sorry, Pip," Ruby said softly. "It helps my feelings feel better."

"How can I make it up to you?" Pip asked. "I want to fix my mistake."

Ruby thought for a moment. "Maybe we could take turns? You could have splash time, and I could have quiet float time?"

Pip's eyes lit up. "That's a wonderful idea! And I could help you feel safe during splash time too!"

Pip gently moved to one side of the tub. "Would you like me to make a calm zone for you? I'll keep my splashes over here."

"That sounds perfect," Ruby smiled, her yellow face brightening.

From that day on, Pip always remembered to think about others during bath time. He would ask, "Ruby, are you ready for splash time?" And when Ruby wanted to float peacefully, Pip would practice his quiet swimming.

"Thank you for being such a thoughtful friend," Ruby would say.

"Thank you for teaching me how to be better," Pip would reply.

The bathroom became a happy place for both friends. Pip learned that saying sorry and making changes showed he truly cared. And Ruby learned that friends make mistakes sometimes, but good friends always try to make things right.

As the warm water swirled around them, both friends felt happy and loved. The bathroom echoed with gentle splashes and soft giggles - the perfect sounds of friendship.

The End.
