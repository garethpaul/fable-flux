# Momo Mouse and the Bathroom Plan

Momo was a kind little mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, Momo scurried into the bathroom. The floor tiles felt cool on Momo’s tiny paws. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet. The mirror was shiny, and a fluffy blue towel hung on the wall.

“Momo, remember our bathroom plan,” called Mama Mouse from the doorway. “We follow directions to stay safe and clean.”

“What are the directions?” Momo asked, whiskers twitching.

Mama smiled. “First, ears open. Second, eyes looking. Third, body moving carefully. Fourth, we stop and check. And we always show respect—respect for our home, our tools, and each other.”

Momo nodded. “Ears, eyes, careful body, stop and check. And respect!”

Today, Mama asked Momo to help clean the tiny mouse toothbrushes. “We’ll make them sparkle,” said Mama.

Momo squeaked happily. “I can help!”

Mama pointed to a small cup and a tiny bottle of mouse-safe soap. “Direction one,” she said. “Turn the faucet one little twist, not too strong.”

Momo’s paws reached up. Twist. The water trickled. Drip-shhh. “It’s gentle water,” Momo said, smiling at the soft sound.

“Good following,” Mama said. “Direction two: Put the toothbrushes in the cup, then add one drop of soap.”

“One drop,” Momo repeated. Plop! A tiny bubble floated up and tickled Momo’s nose. “It smells like lemons!” Momo giggled. The bathroom smelled bright and fresh.

Momo swished the brushes. Swish, swish, swish. The bristles made a whispery sound.

“Direction three,” Mama continued. “Move the brushes up and down, side to side. Gentle hands.”

“Up and down. Side to side,” Momo said. Momo’s paws moved in a steady rhythm. “This is like a little dance!”

Mama nodded. “Yes, movement can be careful and kind.”

Just then, Momo saw a big, shiny button on the side of the tub. It looked very interesting. Momo’s eyes grew wide. “What does that do?”

Mama Mouse said, “We will learn about it later. Right now, let’s follow the plan.”

Momo felt a wiggle of excitement. The button looked so fun. But Momo took a deep breath. “Ears open. Eyes looking,” Momo whispered. “Careful body.” Momo kept swishing the brushes.

After a moment, Momo asked, “Mama, can I ask about the button now?”

“That is respectful,” Mama said. “Yes, you can ask. The button turns on the bubble bath. We use it when we are ready and an adult says it’s safe.”

Momo nodded. “I will wait.”

When the brushes were clean, Mama said, “Direction four: Stop and check. Are the brushes rinsed? Is the water off? Are the tools put away?”

Momo checked carefully. “Rinsed? Yes! Water off? I will do it now.” Twist. The faucet went quiet. “Tools away? Yes!” Momo patted the soap bottle and put it back.

“You followed all the directions,” Mama said. “You showed respect for the bathroom. You moved your body carefully. I am proud of you.”

Momo’s heart felt warm. “I like following directions. It helps me know what to do.”

Mama pointed to the towel. “Now let’s practice hand washing with our plan.”

Momo clapped softly. “I love washing!”

“Ears open,” Mama said. “Turn on the water a little.”

Twist. Drip-shhh.

“Eyes looking,” Mama said. “See the soap?”

Momo nodded. “One pump.” Squish! The soap felt silky. It smelled like lemons again.

“Move your hands,” Mama guided. “Rub, rub, rub. Palms, backs, in between your fingers. Wiggle, wiggle, wiggle.”

Momo giggled and wiggled fingers. The bubbles tickled. “Rub, rub. Wiggle, wiggle.”

“Stop and check,” Mama said. “Are all the bubbles rinsed?”

Momo looked. “A few are hiding!” Momo rinsed again until the water ran clear. “All clean.”

“Water off,” Mama said. “Towel time.”

Momo patted paws on the soft blue towel. “So fluffy!” The towel felt like a warm hug.

Just then, Mama smiled. “You asked about the button. You waited. Now, because you followed directions, we can press it together and learn.”

Momo’s eyes sparkled. “Really?”

“Really,” Mama said. “Direction one: Stand back from the tub. Direction two: I press the button. Direction three: We watch, not touch. Direction four: We listen for the motor.”

Momo stepped back. “Standing back.” Mama pressed the button. Bloop! Bloop! Soft bubbles fluffed up in the tub, like tiny white clouds. The air smelled sweet and clean. The bubbles made happy popping sounds—plip, plop, pop!

Momo clapped. “It’s so fun to watch!”

“You followed directions and kept us safe,” Mama said. “That is respect for rules and for each other.”

Momo nodded proudly. “Following directions helps me move safely. It helps me learn.”

Mama hugged Momo. “Yes. When we listen, look, move carefully, and stop to check, we do our best.”

Momo took a deep breath. The bathroom was calm and tidy. The brushes were clean, the towel was fluffy, and the faucet was quiet.

Momo smiled. “Today I learned to follow directions, move safely, and respect our home. I can do it again tomorrow!”

“Yes,” Mama said. “And every day.”

The End.
