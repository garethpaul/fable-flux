# Fawn's Fair Share

Little Fawn stretched her soft brown legs and yawned in her cozy bedroom. Sunlight streamed through the window, making her spotted coat shimmer like tiny stars. Today felt special, but she wasn't sure why.

"Good morning, Fawn!" chirped Robin, perching on the windowsill. Her bright red breast gleamed in the morning light.

"Good morning, Robin!" Fawn said, her voice gentle and sweet. "What brings you here so early?"

Robin fluffed her feathers excitedly. "I found the most wonderful thing! A whole pile of shiny acorns by the big oak tree. Want to come see?"

Fawn's ears perked up. She loved acorns! They were crunchy and delicious. "Oh yes, please!"

Together, they hurried outside to the oak tree. There, scattered on the soft grass, were dozens of golden acorns, each one perfect and gleaming.

"Wow!" Fawn gasped. "There are so many!"

Just then, Squirrel came scampering down the tree trunk, his fluffy tail twitching. "Those are mine!" he squeaked. "I dropped them from my nest this morning!"

Robin tilted her head. "But I found them first! Finders keepers!"

Squirrel's whiskers trembled. "But I gathered them yesterday! I worked so hard collecting them for winter."

Fawn watched her two friends argue. Her tummy felt funny, like when she ate too many berries. She knew this feeling - it meant something wasn't right.

"Wait," Fawn said softly. Both friends stopped talking and looked at her with curious eyes.

"Robin, you found the acorns, but Squirrel worked hard to gather them. That means they really belong to Squirrel, doesn't it?" Fawn's voice was kind but clear.

Robin's beak drooped a little. "I... I guess you're right. But I was so excited to find treasure!"

Fawn nuzzled Robin gently with her nose. "I understand. Finding something special feels wonderful. But keeping something that belongs to someone else wouldn't feel good in our hearts, would it?"

Squirrel's eyes grew wide and grateful. "Thank you for being fair, Fawn. And Robin, I'm sorry I squeaked at you."

"I'm sorry too," Robin chirped. "I should have asked whose acorns they were."

Fawn smiled, her heart feeling warm and light. "You know what? Squirrel, since Robin helped you find your acorns, maybe you could share just a few with her?"

Squirrel's tail bounced happily. "What a wonderful idea! Robin, would you like some acorns? And Fawn, you should have some too for helping us be fair!"

Robin's eyes sparkled. "Really? You'd share with us?"

"Of course!" Squirrel gathered several acorns. "Friends share with friends, especially when everyone is kind and honest."

The three friends sat in a circle on the soft grass, crunching their acorns together. The morning air smelled fresh and sweet, and Fawn felt proud and happy inside.

"Being fair feels much better than keeping something that isn't ours," Robin said thoughtfully.

"And sharing makes everything more fun!" added Squirrel.

Fawn nodded, her gentle eyes twinkling. "When we're fair and honest, everyone feels good. That's what real friends do."

As they finished their breakfast, Fawn realized why today felt so special. It wasn't about finding treasure or getting the most acorns. It was about being a good friend and doing what was right, even when it was hard.

The three friends spent the rest of the morning playing together, their hearts full of joy and their friendship stronger than ever.

The End.
