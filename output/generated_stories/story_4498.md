# Leo Listens in the Garden Shed

The brave little lion cub, Leo, padded across the yard with soft, careful paws. The morning felt cool. The grass felt wet like tiny kisses on his toes. He wrinkled his nose. He could smell earth and leaves and a little bit of oil.

He stopped at the garden shed. The wooden door was big and blue. It made a friendly squeak. Inside, the air was dusty and warm. Sunlight slid through a tiny window and made sparkles in the air. Leo’s whiskers wiggled with wonder.

“Good morning, Leo,” said Mama Lion, her voice gentle like a soft blanket. “Today we take good care of the shed. We keep it clean and safe. That is good for wellness.”

“What’s well-ness?” Leo asked, blinking his golden eyes.

“Wellness means taking care of your body, your heart, and your mind,” Mama said. “We listen to our bodies. We respect tools. We show compassion to ourselves and to others.”

Leo puffed his chest. “I’m brave! I can do it!”

Mama smiled. “Brave and careful. First, we listen. What do you hear?”

Leo tipped his ear. “I hear drip, drip,” he said. He pointed to a little bucket with water. “I hear creak,” he said as the door moved. “I hear buzz,” he added, hearing a tiny bee outside the window.

“Good listening,” Mama said. “Now smell.”

Leo sniffed. “I smell wood. I smell dirt. I smell… lemon?” He found a yellow cloth. It was lemony clean.

Mama nodded. “We will use that cloth later. Before we start, we stretch.” She reached her paws up, then down. “This helps our bodies feel good.”

Leo copied. “Reach up! Reach down!” His tail made a happy swish. His body felt warm and awake.

Mama pointed to a small broom. “We sweep slow. We keep paws away from sharp things. We respect tools.”

“I will listen,” Leo said. He picked up the small broom. It felt light and smooth. Swish, swish. Dust danced in the sunlight. Leo giggled. “It looks like tiny snow!”

Then Leo saw a shiny thing. It was a little metal scoop. “Ooo!” He reached for it fast.

Mama’s voice was calm. “Pause, Leo. Eyes first. Then paws. Is it safe?”

Leo froze. He looked closely. The edge was sharp. He pulled his paw back. “It is not for me.”

“That is respect,” Mama said. “Thank you for listening.”

They moved a small box together. “One, two, three, push,” Mama said. Leo pushed with his strong little paws. The box slid with a soft scrape.

Under the box was a small brown spider. It curled into a tiny dot. Leo’s eyes went wide. “A spider! Should I roar?” His mouth made a tiny “o.”

Mama lowered her head. “We show compassion. The spider is scared. We can help.”

Leo took a slow breath like Mama taught him. In, out. In, out. His heart felt calm like a quiet drum. “Hello, little spider,” he whispered. “We will move slow.”

Mama used a jar and a paper. The spider wiggled in. Its legs were like little paintbrushes. Leo watched with soft eyes. “We will take you outside,” he said. They set the spider in a flower bed. The sun warmed Leo’s nose. “Safe now,” he said.

Back in the shed, Mama gave Leo the lemony cloth. “You can wipe the low shelves,” she said. “Small circles. Slow and gentle.”

Leo made small circles. Wipe, wipe. The lemon smell was bright and happy. He hummed a little tune. “My paws are working. My body feels strong,” he said.

“Let’s take a wellness break,” Mama said. They sat on a soft mat. Mama poured water into a little cup. The cup was blue with a lion on it. “Sip, sip,” said Mama.

Leo took a sip. The water felt cool and sweet. He listened to his tummy. “My tummy says thank you,” he giggled.

“Is your body tired or ready?” Mama asked.

Leo wiggled his shoulders and listened inside. “My body says… a tiny bit tired,” he said.

“We can do a lion breath,” Mama said. “Big breath in, gentle breath out.”

Leo tried. In through his nose. Out through his mouth. His whole body felt peaceful and cozy.

They finished the last bit of cleaning together. The tools stood in a neat row. The floor looked clear. The shed smelled like lemon and wood. Leo’s heart felt full.

“You were brave,” Mama said. “You listened. You respected the tools. You showed compassion to the spider. How does your body feel now?”

Leo put a paw on his chest. “My body says proud. My mind says calm. My heart says kind.”

Mama nuzzled him. “That is wellness.”

Leo grinned. “Today, I learned: I can be brave and gentle. I can listen to my body. I can respect tools and show kindness. That keeps me safe and happy.”

They stepped outside. The sun warmed their backs. The grass brushed their paws. The shed stood clean and friendly.

“Tomorrow,” Leo said, “can we do more lion breaths?”

Mama laughed softly. “Yes, little lion. We can listen and breathe every day.”

The End.
