# Pebble the Penguin’s Shed Routine

Pebble was a cheerful penguin who loved a neat plan. Every morning, Pebble waddled to the little garden shed behind the house. The shed smelled like fresh earth and pine wood. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. Pebble smiled and said, “Good morning, Shed!”

Inside, tools hung in a row. A small broom, a tiny rake, and a blue watering can sat on a shelf. Pebble clapped his flippers. “Time for our routine,” he chirped. Routine made Pebble feel calm, like a soft lullaby.

First, Pebble took a deep, slow breath. In… and out. He listened. Drip, drip went a leaky tap. Rustle, rustle went dry leaves in a basket. “I hear you,” Pebble said kindly. “We will take turns.”

Pebble’s routine had steps:
- Sweep the floor.
- Water the seedlings.
- Wipe the tools.
- Snack and story time.

Pebble picked up the small broom. Swish, swish, swish. The straw brushed the dusty floor. Dust sparkled in the light. “Swish, swish,” sang Pebble. The shed felt tidy and safe.

Just then, a soft scritch-scritch sound came from a corner. A little field mouse peeped out, whiskers twitching. “Hello,” said the mouse. “I am Mallow. May I stay in this warm shed?”

Pebble paused. This was new. New things could feel wobbly. He put a flipper on his tummy and breathed in… and out. “We have a routine,” Pebble said gently. “But we can be kind. We can make space.”

Mallow nodded. “I will try to be quiet,” she whispered.

Pebble smiled. “Thank you. You can watch while I sweep.”

Swish, swish. Pebble finished the floor. “Step one, all done,” he cheered.

Next was watering. The blue watering can was cool and smooth in Pebble’s flippers. Pebble tipped it. Pitter-patter went the water over tiny green seedlings in little pots. The soil smelled rich, like warm cookies but not sweet. “Hello, little leaves,” Pebble said. “Just the right drink.”

A flutter and a chirp came from the window. A ladybug buzzed inside and landed on Pebble’s beak. “Oh!” Pebble giggled. “Hello, Dot.” Dot’s wings were shiny red with black spots. Dot zipped to a pot and sat. “I like company,” Pebble said. “But we must keep calm and gentle.”

Mallow shuffled closer, eyes bright. “Can I help?”

Pebble thought. Helping was not in the plan. But Pebble remembered: being tolerant means making room. Pebble nodded. “You can count the drips. Count to three for each pot.”

Mallow counted. “One, two, three.” Pebble poured slowly. Pitter-patter. It felt good to share.

“Step two, all done,” Pebble said.

Now it was time to wipe the tools. Pebble hummed a calm tune as he wiped the tiny rake. Wipe, wipe. He wiped the small trowel. Wipe, wipe. Dot walked along the handle, tiny feet tickling. Pebble giggled again. Mallow brought a clean cloth. “Here you go,” she said. Pebble took it. “Thank you. Teamwork!”

Pebble checked the list on the wall. “Snack and story time next.” He opened a small tin. Inside were crunchy fish crackers for Pebble and seed crumbs for visitors. The crackers smelled salty. Pebble shared with Mallow and placed a crumb near Dot. “Nibble, nibble,” Mallow said. Dot clicked her wings and sat still, like a tiny red button.

As they munched, a loud bang sounded. A gust of wind pushed the door. Slam! Pebble’s heart thumped. New sounds could be scary. Pebble touched the list. Routine helps, he told himself. “Let’s use calm breaths,” he said. “In… and out.”

Mallow copied. Dot buzzed, then settled. Pebble waddled to the door and gently pushed it closed. Click. The shed was quiet again. Pebble smiled. “We stayed calm. We followed our steps.”

“Your routine helps me feel safe,” Mallow said, nibbling a crumb.

“It helps me too,” Pebble said. “Routine is like a map. It shows us what to do.”

After snack, Pebble read a little picture book he kept on the shelf. He traced the pictures with a flipper. “Once there was a seed…” he read softly. The words felt smooth and slow.

When story time ended, Pebble checked the shed. The floor was clean. The seedlings were watered. The tools were shiny. The door was snug. Pebble felt warm and proud.

Mallow stretched. “May I come again tomorrow?”

Pebble nodded. “Yes. We have a routine, and we have room. We can make calm choices together.”

Dot lifted into the sunbeam, making tiny rainbows in the air. Pebble laughed. “See you tomorrow, friends.”

Pebble hung the broom, set down the watering can, and patted the list. “Routine, tolerance, calmness,” he whispered. “We did them all.”

Pebble waddled out, the grass cool under his feet. He turned back and waved at the little shed. “Thank you, Shed. See you in the morning.”

And the shed, quiet and tidy, seemed to glow with a happy hush.

The lesson: Routines help us feel safe and calm. We can be kind and make room for others, even when plans change a little. With calm breaths and gentle hearts, we can finish our steps and enjoy the day.

The End.
