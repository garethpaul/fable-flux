# Pippa Parrot's Quiet Crunch

Pippa the creative parrot loved colors, sounds, and making things. Her feathers were bright green with a splash of blue, and she wore a tiny red ribbon on her tail. Today, the dining room was her studio and stage!

The sun made warm squares on the wooden floor. The round table was set with a little blue placemat, a small cup of water, and a bowl of crunchy carrots and soft banana slices. The air smelled like sweet bananas and fresh bread. Pippa wiggled with happy energy.

“I will make a snack rainbow!” Pippa chirped. “Orange carrots, yellow banana, and… imagination!” She flapped her wings, and her ribbon swished.

“Pippa,” called Nana from the kitchen doorway. Nana had kind eyes and soft hands. “Let’s practice our mindful snack. Remember our three steps? Breathe, Notice, Nibble.”

Pippa hopped onto her chair. “Breathe, Notice, Nibble,” she repeated, but her eyes kept peeking at a box of colorful paper on the sideboard. She wanted to build a paper crown right now. Her toes tapped, tap-tap-tap, on the chair.

“First, we breathe,” Nana said, sitting beside her. “In like smelling a flower. Out like blowing a little cloud.”

Pippa tried. “Innn…” She smelled banana and the wooden table. “Outtt…” A tiny cloud of air tickled her beak. Her shoulders softened. The room felt quiet, like a soft blanket.

“Good,” Nana smiled. “Now we notice. What do you see, hear, and feel?”

Pippa looked at the carrots. They were shiny and orange like tiny suns. The bananas were pale and smooth. She listened. The clock went tick… tock… The refrigerator hummed. A bird sang outside. Pippa touched the cool cup. The water made a gentle swish.

“I see circles and orange suns,” Pippa said. “I hear tick-tock and hum-hum. I feel cool cup and soft napkin.”

“Wonderful noticing,” Nana said. “Now we nibble.”

Pippa picked up a carrot. Crunch! It was loud and snappy in her beak. The carrot tasted sweet and earthy. She smiled. “Crunch like a tiny drum!” she giggled.

She reached for another carrot, but her eyes jumped to the paper again. “Crown now?” she chirped, almost standing up.

Nana shook her head kindly. “Mindful snack first. We practice staying here. We can make a crown after. Let’s try our three steps again.”

Pippa puffed her chest. She wanted to do it. She wanted to make Nana proud. She wanted to feel calm and strong. “I can do it,” she said softly.

“In like a flower… out like a cloud.” Breathing made her tummy feel warm. She noticed the way the sun warmed her feathers. She noticed a tiny banana seed dot. She noticed her toes had stopped tapping. She nibbled a banana slice. It was sweet and soft, like a buttery hug. “Mmm,” Pippa hummed.

Her mind jumped again. “What if my paper crown has stars?” she blurted.

Nana nodded. “That is a bright idea. Hold it gently in your mind, like in a little basket. It can wait there. Come back to your banana.”

Pippa imagined a small basket in her head. She put the star-crown idea inside and closed the pretend lid. She took another breath. In… and out… The room smelled like sweet fruit and wood polish. It felt good to come back.

A tiny drip of water slid down her cup. Pippa watched it wiggle. Plink! It dropped on the placemat. She smiled at the little dark dot it made. “Hello, plink,” she whispered.

Crunch. Nibble. Sip. Pause. Pippa kept going. Every time her mind fluttered away, she noticed it. She breathed. She came back. Sometimes it felt easy like floating. Sometimes it felt wiggly like a worm. But Pippa was persistent. She practiced again and again: Breathe, Notice, Nibble.

Soon, her bowl was almost empty. Her tummy felt comfy, not too full. Her body felt calm and awake. Her heart felt like it was smiling.

“All done,” Pippa chirped, patting her beak with the napkin. “I stayed. I came back. I did it.”

Nana clapped softly. “You were mindful, Pippa. You took care of your body with food. You took care of your mind with breathing. You were patient and kept trying.”

Pippa puffed with pride. “Now crown time?”

“Now crown time,” Nana agreed.

They opened the paper box. Pippa noticed the papers: smooth blue, scratchy gold, shiny silver. She chose yellow for sunshine and blue for sky. She pressed the tape. It made a sticky sound—schlip! She snipped with tiny scissors—snip-snip. She glued a silver star—squish! She placed the crown on her head and breathed in. The paper smelled a little like flowers and rain.

“How do you feel?” Nana asked.

“Calm, happy, strong,” Pippa said. “Mindful parrot power!”

Nana laughed. “Remember our steps?”

“Breathe. Notice. Nibble,” Pippa sang. “And keep trying when my mind flies away.”

Pippa looked at the table and smiled. “Thank you, carrots. Thank you, bananas. Thank you, breath.” She gave Nana a gentle wing hug.

Pippa’s crown shone in the sunlight. Her tummy was content. Her heart was quiet and brave. She had made a snack rainbow and a calm space inside.

The clear lesson: When we breathe, notice, and come back again and again, we feel well and strong. Mindfulness helps us take care of our bodies and our hearts.

The End.
