# Penny Panda's Helpful Workshop

Penny Panda loved her cozy garage workshop. The smell of wood shavings filled the air, and colorful tools hung neatly on the walls. Every morning, she would put on her special blue apron and get ready to build wonderful things.

"What should I make today?" Penny asked herself, looking at her workbench covered with smooth pieces of wood.

Just then, she heard a soft *tap tap tap* at her garage door.

"Come in!" called Penny cheerfully.

Little Benny Bunny hopped inside, his ears drooping sadly. "Hi, Penny," he said quietly. "I broke my favorite toy truck yesterday. The wheel fell off, and now it won't roll anymore."

Penny's kind eyes sparkled. "Oh my! Let me see it, Benny."

Benny pulled out a bright red toy truck with one missing wheel. "I can't play with it anymore," he sniffled.

"Don't worry," said Penny gently. "I think we can fix this together! Would you like to help me?"

Benny's ears perked up. "Really? You'd help me fix it?"

"Of course!" Penny smiled. "Helping friends is one of my favorite things to do. Let's make a plan first."

Penny showed Benny how to think step by step. "First, we need to find the right size wheel. Then, we'll need to attach it carefully. What do you think we should do after that?"

"Test it to make sure it rolls?" Benny guessed.

"Exactly right!" Penny clapped her paws together. "You're a great helper already!"

They searched through Penny's collection of wheels until they found a perfect match. The wheel was smooth and round, just the right size for the little truck.

"Now, let's be very careful," Penny said, showing Benny how to hold the truck steady. "We'll use this special glue that smells like vanilla."

Benny sniffed the air. "Mmm, it does smell like cookies!"

Working together, they carefully attached the new wheel. Penny's patient paws guided Benny's smaller ones as they pressed the wheel into place.

"We need to wait for it to dry," Penny explained. "Good building takes time and patience."

While they waited, Penny taught Benny how to organize tools. "When everything has its place, it's easier to help others quickly," she said.

After the glue dried, it was time to test the truck. Benny gently pushed it across the smooth garage floor. The truck rolled perfectly, its wheels spinning smoothly.

"It works! It works!" Benny jumped up and down with joy. "Thank you so much, Penny!"

"You helped too, Benny," Penny said warmly. "We made a great team. When we work together and help each other, we can fix almost anything!"

Just then, Sally Squirrel peeked into the garage. "I heard happy sounds! What's happening?"

"Penny helped me fix my truck!" Benny said proudly. "And I helped too!"

"That's wonderful!" said Sally. "Penny, could you help me build a bird feeder for my tree?"

Penny's eyes lit up. "Of course! Benny, would you like to help us with our next project?"

"Yes!" Benny said excitedly. "I love helping!"

As the three friends gathered around Penny's workbench, the garage filled with the sounds of planning and laughter. Penny felt warm and happy inside, knowing that helping others was the best way to spend any day.

The End.
