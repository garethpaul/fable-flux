# Daisy the Deer Learns About the World

In a sunny backyard filled with colorful flowers and buzzing bees, lived a gentle deer fawn named Daisy. Her soft brown fur sparkled in the warm sunlight, and her big dark eyes were always curious about everything around her.

One bright morning, Daisy was nibbling on sweet clover when she heard Mrs. Robin chirping excitedly from her nest in the oak tree.

"Oh my! Oh my!" sang Mrs. Robin. "I just flew back from a very long journey, and I saw so many wonderful places!"

Daisy's ears perked up. "Tell me about them, Mrs. Robin! What did you see?"

Mrs. Robin fluffed her red feathers and began to share. "I saw children playing in sandy deserts far away, and other children building snowmen in snowy mountains. Some children were picking mangoes from tall trees, while others were catching fish in sparkling rivers."

Daisy's eyes grew wide with wonder. "Wow! There are so many different places in our world!"

"Yes, dear Daisy," chirped Mrs. Robin. "And all the children, no matter where they live, love to play and laugh just like the children who sometimes visit this backyard."

Just then, Daisy noticed something that made her heart feel heavy. In the corner of the yard, she saw empty food bowls that belonged to Patches, the neighborhood cat, and Max, the friendly dog next door. Their bowls were completely empty, and both animals looked very hungry.

"Oh no!" said Daisy softly. "Mrs. Robin, Patches and Max don't have any food. Just like you told me about children around the world, I think animals everywhere need help sometimes too."

Mrs. Robin nodded wisely. "You're absolutely right, Daisy. When we learn about others, we also learn how to help them."

Daisy felt a little nervous. She was just a small fawn. "But I'm so little. How can I help?"

"Even small acts of kindness can make a big difference," encouraged Mrs. Robin.

Daisy thought hard, her little hooves tapping on the soft grass. Then her face lit up with an idea! She remembered seeing the Johnson family, who lived in the house, always putting out fresh water and food for the backyard animals.

Carefully, Daisy walked to the back door and gently tapped it with her hoof. *Tap, tap, tap.*

Little Emma Johnson opened the door and gasped with delight. "Mommy! There's a baby deer at our door!"

Daisy pointed her nose toward the empty bowls and then looked back at Emma with her gentle eyes.

"Oh!" said Emma's mother, understanding immediately. "You're telling us that Patches and Max need food! What a smart and caring little deer you are."

Together, Emma and her mother filled the bowls with fresh food and clean water. Patches and Max came running, their tails wagging and purring with happiness.

"Thank you, Daisy!" barked Max between bites.

"You're the kindest fawn in the whole world!" purred Patches.

Daisy felt warm and proud inside. She had learned that the world was full of different places and creatures, and that even a small fawn like her could help make it a better place.

Mrs. Robin flew down and perched on Daisy's head. "You see, dear one? When we care about others, whether they're near or far, we make our whole world brighter."

From that day on, Daisy always looked for ways to help others in her backyard and dreamed about all the friends around the world who were doing the same thing.

The End.
