# Joey Roo and the Sunny Backyard Promise

Joey Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with soft brown fur and springy feet. The sun felt warm on his back, and the grass in the backyard smelled sweet and green. Birds chirped, “Chee-chee!” and a soft breeze puffed like a whisper. It was a perfect day for hopping.

“Good morning, sunshine,” Mama Roo said, tying on her wide hat. “Backyard play time. Remember our safety rules, Joey.”

Joey nodded, ears wiggling. “Yes, Mama! Stay inside the fence. Ask before I climb. Keep water by the flowers. Gentle with bugs. And… tell the truth.”

Mama smiled. “That last one is called integrity. It means being honest and doing the right thing, even when no one is watching.”

Joey’s tail thumped the soft dirt. “I can do that!”

He hopped to his chores basket. “Morning routine!” he sang. Joey set out a small water pail, a tiny rake, and a bright yellow trash bag. He liked routines. They made his tummy feel calm.

First, Joey watered the marigolds. Drip, drip. The cool water sparkled in the sun. A butterfly fluttered past, like a floating petal.

“Good morning, butterfly,” Joey whispered. “I will share water with you and the flowers.”

He set the pail down and looked at the old tree stump by the fence. It looked like a little mountain. “That stump looks fun,” he said. His feet bounced. “But I must ask before I climb.” Joey took a breath and placed his paws on his belly. In… out… in… out. He practiced self-regulation, just like Mama taught him.

He hopped back to Mama. “Can I climb the stump, please?”

Mama looked at the stump and at Joey’s bright eyes. “You remembered to ask. Good choice. You may climb if you go slow and keep three points of contact—two paws and one foot, or two feet and one paw.”

“Safety first!” Joey chirped. He climbed carefully, feeling the rough bark under his paws and the sun warm on his shoulders. “I’m King of the Stump!” he giggled, then hopped down safely.

Next, Joey spotted something shiny near the garden bed. “Ooo,” he said. A crinkly bottle cap lay in the grass. He picked it up, feeling the cool metal. “This doesn’t belong here,” he said. “Trash can, not grass.” Joey put the cap in his yellow bag. “I’m helping the backyard,” he told the ants marching by. “We all share this home.”

The breeze lifted and the wind chimes jingled. “Ting-ting!” Joey smiled and chased his shadow across the sunny yard. He hopped past Mama’s flower pots—only a little too close.

His tail brushed a small flower pot. “Uh-oh!” The pot wobbled. Tip-tip—crack! The pot split, and dark soil spilled out like chocolate crumbs. A tiny green sprout fell onto the dirt.

Joey’s heart jumped. His ears drooped. He looked around. Mama was by the gate, talking to a neighbor. No one had seen. Joey’s tummy felt squirmy.

“I could hide the pot,” he whispered. “Or I could tell.” He thought about integrity. He thought about his promise to be honest and kind to their backyard. Joey placed a paw on his belly. In… out… in… out. He looked at the little sprout lying in the warm soil.

“I will tell,” he decided softly. “The right thing is the brave thing.”

Joey hopped to Mama, holding the broken pot. “Mama,” he said, voice small. “I bumped your pot. I’m sorry. I didn’t mean to. The sprout fell out.”

Mama crouched down, looking into Joey’s eyes. “Thank you for telling me the truth right away,” she said gently. “That is integrity. I’m proud of you.”

Joey’s shoulders relaxed. “Can we help the sprout?”

“We can,” Mama said. She brought out a little shovel and a spare pot. “This is a good chance for conservation—taking care of our plants and our home.”

Together, they scooped the soft soil. It felt cool and crumbly on Joey’s paws. Mama showed him how to make a cozy hole. Joey lifted the sprout with careful fingers. “Sorry, little plant,” he whispered. “I will be gentle.”

They tucked the sprout into the new pot. Joey watered it—drip, drip—just enough. He wiped his paws on a cloth and smiled. “It looks happy,” he said.

“It does,” Mama agreed. “And I feel happy that you told the truth. What did we learn?”

Joey thought. The wind chimes sang again. “Integrity means I tell the truth and fix my mistakes,” he said. “Safety means I ask and go slow. Conservation means I care for plants and pick up trash.”

Mama tapped his nose. “And routines help you remember all of that.”

Joey’s tail thumped again. “My routine helped me breathe and choose the right thing.”

They finished their backyard time by checking the fence latch—click—so the yard stayed safe, and by putting the yellow trash bag in the bin. Joey waved to the ants. “Thank you for sharing,” he said. He waved to the butterfly too. “See you tomorrow.”

As the sun warmed their faces, Mama poured cool water into a cup. Joey sipped. Ahh. “Mama?”

“Yes, Joey?”

“Doing the right thing makes my heart feel light.”

Mama hugged him. “That is the sunshine of integrity.”

Joey beamed. “I will keep my promise,” he said. “Every day.”

The End.
