# Squeaky’s Nutritious Bedtime Plan

Squeaky the resourceful squirrel lived in a cozy bedroom at the top of a little house. His bed was a soft nest of blankets. His lamp glowed warm and golden. His window showed the tops of trees and a friendly moon. The air smelled like clean sheets and pine cones. Squeaky loved his room.

One evening, Squeaky’s tummy made a small rumble. “Rrrr,” it said.

“Oh!” said Squeaky. “My tummy is talking. It wants a smart snack.”

Squeaky liked smart snacks. Smart snacks helped him play, think, and sleep. He hopped to his small shelf. On it sat little jars he had labeled with funny drawings: a carrot picture, a leaf picture, a water drop, and a nut. He had made the labels himself out of old paper, so nothing went to waste.

He opened the window a crack. Cool night air drifted in. He heard crickets singing. He smiled. “Hello, night friends!”

Squeaky set out his tiny plate and a small green cup. He spoke softly to himself, “A balanced snack makes me strong. Different foods help different parts of me.” He had learned this from his forest teacher, Ms. Robin. Ms. Robin said, “Eat a rainbow, little ones!”

Squeaky looked in the carrot jar. “Carrot sticks!” he chirped. “Crunch-crunch orange helps my eyes.” He looked in the nut jar. “Almonds! Crunchy nuts help my body grow and go.” He filled his green cup at his little water pitcher. “Water helps everything,” he said, and took a sip. “Ahh!”

He reached for the berry bowl, but it was empty. “Oh,” he said. His tail drooped a little. “No red berries? I wanted a rainbow.”

His tummy rumbled again. “Rrrr.”

Squeaky felt a bubble of frustration in his chest. He stamped one tiny paw. “I wanted berries now!” he squeaked.

He took a breath in. He took a breath out. He remembered Ms. Robin’s calm-down song. Squeaky sang softly, “Smell the flower, blow the cloud. Smell the flower, blow the cloud.” He sniffed like a flower. He blew like a cloud. The bubble of frustration got smaller.

“I can be resourceful,” Squeaky said. “I can use what I have. I can make a plan.”

He tip-tapped to his art bin. Inside were clean jars, paper, and markers. He found a jar with a blue lid. He drew a bright red berry on a tiny label and stuck it on the jar. He smiled. “Tomorrow, I will pick berries with a grown-up,” he said. “I will only pick ripe ones and leave plenty for birds. That is caring for the earth.”

Squeaky set the empty berry jar on the shelf to remind him. He did not throw away the paper scraps. He put them back in the bin. “I reuse and recycle,” he said proudly. “That helps my cozy world.”

He sat on his bed with his plate and cup. “Hello, carrot,” he said. “Hello, almond. Hello, water.” He took a crunchy carrot bite. Crunch! Crunch! The sweet, earthy taste tickled his tongue. He chewed an almond. Munch, munch. It tasted toasty and warm. He sipped his water. Sip. “This snack is simple, but it is smart,” he said.

Just then, the wind whooshed at the window. A tiny paper napkin fluttered off the table and onto the floor.

Squeaky hopped down. “Oops! I pick up to keep my room clean.” He picked up the napkin and put it in his compost cup. He kept a small, tidy compost cup for little food bits and paper napkins. Later, it would go to the garden to feed the soil. “Compost helps the earth,” he said. “The earth helps us.”

He finished his snack. His tummy felt happy. “Thank you, snack,” he said. His heart felt calm. “Thank you, breaths,” he said.

Then he heard a soft knock. Tap-tap. It was his friend, Pippa the mouse, peeking in. “Hi, Squeaky,” Pippa whispered. “I can’t sleep. My tummy is grumbly. But I’m mad because we have no cookies.”

Squeaky nodded. “I felt mad too,” he said. “I wanted berries.” He showed Pippa how to smell the flower and blow the cloud. Together they breathed in and out. Their shoulders softened.

“I feel better,” Pippa said.

“I made a smart snack,” Squeaky said. “Would you like to share a carrot and a nut?”

“Yes, please,” Pippa said.

They sat cross-legged on the bed. Crunch, crunch. Munch, munch. Sip, sip. The lamp made a friendly pool of light. Crickets sang. The moon watched kindly.

“This snack helps my body,” Pippa said. “I don’t need cookies to feel cozy.”

Squeaky smiled. “We can plan for berries tomorrow. We will bring a small basket. We will only pick what we need. We will leave no trash. We will thank the plants.”

Pippa nodded. “That is good care.”

When the plates were clean and the compost cup had little bits to take to the garden tomorrow, Squeaky closed the window. He turned the lamp low. The room smelled like carrots and clean night air.

“I learned something,” Squeaky said. “When I am frustrated, I can breathe. I can make a plan. I can choose a balanced snack to help my body. I can care for my room and the earth.”

Pippa yawned. “Me too,” she said.

They snuggled under the blankets. Their eyelids felt heavy. Their hearts felt light.

“Good night, smart snack,” Squeaky whispered. “Good night, kind earth.”

The moon shone softly. The house was quiet. Squeaky and Pippa drifted to sleep, warm and proud.

The End.
