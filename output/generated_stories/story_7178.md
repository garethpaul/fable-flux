# Bubbles, Brushes, and Brave Little Hooves

A gentle deer fawn named Fern peeked into the bright bathroom. The tiles were cool like smooth pebbles. The mirror shone like a quiet pond. The air smelled like minty soap and warm towels.

Fern wiggled her soft ears. “Good morning, bathroom,” she whispered. “Today, I will care for me.”

On the counter sat a tiny toothbrush with a green handle. Fern touched it with the tip of her hoof. “Hello, Brush,” she said. “Will you help me keep my smile strong?” The brush seemed to nod.

Fern turned on the faucet. Water rushed out with a happy whoosh, like a friendly stream. “Whoosh!” Fern giggled. She wet the brush and squeezed a little bead of toothpaste. “Just a pea,” she said. “That’s enough for me.”

She looked in the mirror. “I can do this,” Fern told herself. “I am brave and gentle.” She brushed in little circles. “Swish, swish, swish,” she hummed. Minty bubbles popped on her tongue. Pop! Pop!

Knock, knock! A small voice called from the hallway. “Fern? Can you help us?”

Fern paused. “Who is there?” she asked.

“It’s Pip the squirrel and Dot the duckling!” Pip squeaked. “We want to learn bathroom care too!”

Fern took a deep breath. She thought with care. “I want to finish brushing,” she said softly, “and I want to help my friends.” She rinsed, spit, and smiled. “I can do both with a plan.”

“Please come in,” Fern said. “But let’s make it tidy and safe.”

Pip and Dot waddled in. Pip’s tail twitched like a fluffy feather duster. Dot’s little feet went pat-pat on the mat.

Fern pointed to the towel hooks. “First, we hang towels so the floor stays dry. Dry floors keep us safe.” She lifted her chin. “Please hang your towels, friends.”

Pip started to toss his towel on the floor. Fern stood tall. “Pip, towels go on hooks, not the floor,” she said in a kind, clear voice. “That keeps everyone from slipping.” Pip blinked and nodded. “Okay, Fern!” He hung it up.

Dot stared at the soap pump. “What does this do?” she asked.

Fern smiled. “Let’s think,” she said. “When do we use soap?” She tapped her hoof and counted. “After the potty, before snacks, after play, and when our hands feel sticky.”

Dot quacked softly. “My wings feel sticky.”

“Then it’s soap time,” said Fern. She pressed the pump. Squish! A gentle bubble of soap sat like a tiny cloud. “Rub, rub, rub,” Fern sang. “Front and back, between, around. Rub until we hear the bubbles squeak.”

They rubbed. Squeak, squeak! The bubbles smelled like oranges. Water whispered as they rinsed. The sink made little silver rivers.

Pip held up a big toothpaste tube. “I want a giant squish!” he cheered.

Fern shook her head kindly. “Let’s use our thinking,” she said. “Too much paste makes too many bubbles, and it’s hard to spit. A little pea is enough.” She held out her hoof. “Would you like me to show you?”

Pip paused. His whiskers trembled. “Yes, please,” he said.

Fern squeezed a tiny dot onto his brush. “See? Small and smart,” she said. “Now brush in little circles. Gums are gentle.”

Dot looked shy. “I’m scared of the noisy faucet,” she whispered.

Fern stepped closer. “I hear you,” she said softly. “Noises can feel big. Let’s make it small.” She turned the handle just a little. The water whispered, not whooshed. “Listen. Shhh. Soft as the wind in leaves.”

Dot’s eyes brightened. “I like the soft sound,” she said. “Thank you, Fern.”

Soon the bathroom sounded happy: swish, hum, pat, squeak. Mint and orange smells danced in the air. The mirror showed three smiling friends.

Fern looked at the floor. “Oh,” she said. A tiny puddle sparkled near the tub. “Water can be tricky. We need to keep it safe.” She stood straight. “Friends, let’s do a quick clean-up so no one slips.”

Pip reached for a towel on the counter. Dot grabbed the step stool. Fern thought again. “Wait,” she said. “Let’s use a small cloth for the floor, not our clean towels. And we can move the stool to the dry side.”

They nodded. Fern handed Pip a cloth and guided Dot to slide the stool. Wipe, wipe. Slide, slide. The puddle was gone.

All done, they washed hands one more time. Rub, rinse, pat. Fern pointed to the trash bin. “Tissues go here,” she said. “Keeping things neat helps everyone.”

Pip saluted with his brush. “Fern, you are a bathroom hero!”

Fern laughed. “We are a team. We care for ourselves, and we help our place stay clean. That is wellness.”

Dot fluffed her wings. “And you used a strong voice, Fern. You told us what to do in a kind way.”

Fern nodded. “Yes. Strong and kind. Ask, think, do. That is our plan.”

They took a deep breath together. In, out. Calm and proud.

Fern looked in the mirror one last time. “I cared for me,” she said. “I helped my friends. I kept the bathroom safe. I feel healthy and happy.”

Pip and Dot cheered. “So do we!”

Outside, the hallway smelled like pancakes. It was time for breakfast.

Fern turned off the light with a soft click. “Wellness starts with small steps,” she whispered, “and small friends can do big good.”

The End.
