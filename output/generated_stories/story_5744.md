# Kora the Koala’s Attic Team

In a quiet attic, soft and still, lived a calm koala named Kora. The attic smelled like warm wood and old paper. Dust sparkles floated in the sunbeams. The floorboards felt smooth under Kora’s gentle paws.

Kora loved the attic. She liked the hush. She liked the tiny window where she could see the sky. One morning, Kora blinked her sleepy eyes and listened. Drip. Drip. Drip.

“Oh,” Kora said softly. “I hear a drip.”

Kora followed the sound. Drip. Drip. She found a little hole in the roof. A tiny raindrop fell into a metal bowl. Plink! The bowl sang a bright note.

Kora touched the bowl. “Thank you, bowl,” she whispered. “You are catching the water. But we need a better plan.”

Kora looked around. There were many things in the attic. A basket of buttons. A stack of books. A roll of string. A soft scarf. A box of small tools. She also saw a shy mouse peeking from a shoe. “Hello,” said Kora, calm and kind. “I’m Kora.”

The mouse twitched his whiskers. “I’m Miko,” he squeaked. “I don’t like drips.”

“Me too,” said a moth from the scarf. “I’m Miri. That plink is loud.”

Kora nodded. “We can fix it. We will work together. We will use our bodies and our minds. That is called coordination.”

“Cor-di-na-tion?” Miko tried the word.

“It means we make a plan,” Kora said. “We take turns. We watch and listen. We help each other. We use our knowledge.”

Miri fluttered her soft wings. “I can flap near the hole so you can see it better.”

“That is helpful,” said Kora. “Helping is kind. And fixing is good for the Earth. If we fix the roof, we do not waste water, and we take care of our home. That is called sustainability.”

Miko nodded fast. “I can run and find things.”

Kora smiled. “Let’s talk, let’s plan.”

They sat in a small circle on a woven mat. The mat felt scratchy and warm. Kora spoke slowly. “We will not throw things away. We will use what we have. We will be gentle.”

Miko squeaked, “I can bring the string!”

Miri said, “I like the scarf.”

Kora pointed to the books. “The books may have answers. Knowledge is in books. Let’s look.”

They opened a big book. The pages smelled like dust and sunshine. Kora traced a picture with her paw. “This page shows a patch. A patch covers a hole. We can make a patch from the scarf.”

Miri landed on Kora’s ear. “I agree,” she hummed.

Miko tugged the scarf over. It was soft and green. Kora rubbed it between her fingers. “Soft is good. It will bend. We need something sticky.”

Miko ran to the tool box. “Tape!” he said, pulling a roll with tiny teeth marks. “I found tape.”

Kora shook her head gently. “Tape may fall off when it is wet. The book says we should sew. See the picture? Stitch, stitch.”

Miri fluttered. “I can watch the hole and say, ‘Here!’”

Kora said, “We must make a safe plan. We need to reach the hole. It is high. We will not climb alone. We will coordinate.”

She looked at a sturdy step stool. She pressed it. It did not wobble. “This is safe. Miko, please hold the bottom. Miri, please shine light with your wings near the hole. I will climb slowly.”

Miko took a deep breath. “I can do that.”

Miri hummed, “I can do that.”

Kora asked, “Is this plan okay for everyone?” She waited.

“Yes,” said Miko.

“Yes,” said Miri.

“That is good negotiation,” Kora said. “We talk. We listen. We agree.”

Kora put on a small cloth hat. “For safety,” she said. She tucked a needle, thread, and the scarf in a little pouch. Step. Step. Step. The stool creaked a tiny creak. Miko’s paws held strong. Miri’s wings made a soft whirr.

“Here,” Miri whispered, circling the hole. Cool air kissed Kora’s nose. She could hear the quiet drip. Drip.

Kora breathed in. She stitched the scarf patch with careful pokes. “In, out. In, out,” she sang. “We are a team. We can do hard things.”

Miko looked up. “You’re doing it!”

Kora tied a neat knot. She pressed the patch flat. The drip stopped. The attic grew still again. Sunbeams warmed their faces. The air smelled like cloth and clean wood.

Miko clapped his tiny hands. “We fixed it!”

Miri danced a twinkly dance. “No more plink.”

Kora climbed down slowly. She hugged her friends with a gentle squeeze. “We did it together. We used coordination. We used knowledge. We cared for our home. That helps our big world too. When we take care of our little place, we help the Earth. We are global citizens.”

“Glow-bull sit-i-zens,” Miko repeated, smiling.

“It means we all share the Earth,” Kora said. “We help. We do not waste. We talk kindly. We make plans.”

Miri nuzzled Kora’s cheek. “I liked our plan. I felt safe.”

Kora nodded. “I felt calm. I felt proud.”

They put the tools away. They folded the books. They saved the extra scarf. “We will use it again,” Kora said. “That is sustainable.”

The attic was quiet and cozy again. The sun made golden shapes on the floor. Kora yawned a happy yawn. “Thank you, team,” she whispered.

Miko curled in the shoe. Miri tucked into the scarf. Kora rested on the mat. Their hearts felt warm.

Lesson: When we listen, plan, and help each other, we can solve problems. That is coordination. Using knowledge and caring for our home keeps the Earth happy too.

The End.
