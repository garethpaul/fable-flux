# Ellie's Colorful Garden Helper

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved spending time on Grandma Rose's sunny balcony garden. The small space was filled with bright red tomatoes, yellow sunflowers, and purple petunias that smelled so sweet.

"Good morning, my dear Ellie!" called Grandma Rose as she watered her plants. "Would you like to help me today? Mrs. Bird next door needs flower decorations for her grandson's birthday party."

Ellie's big brown eyes sparkled with excitement. "Yes, yes! I want to help!" she trumpeted softly.

Grandma Rose smiled and handed Ellie a small watering can painted with rainbow stripes. "First, we need to follow my special directions to pick the prettiest flowers. Can you do that?"

"I can follow directions!" Ellie nodded, her little trunk wiggling with joy.

"Step one," said Grandma Rose, "water the purple petunias gently. They're thirsty!"

Ellie carefully held the watering can with her trunk. Drip, drip, drip went the water. But oh no! She accidentally poured too much water, and it splashed everywhere.

"Oops!" Ellie said, feeling sad. "I made a mess."

"That's okay, sweet Ellie," Grandma Rose said kindly. "Let's try again. Remember, just a little water - like gentle rain."

Ellie tried again, this time very slowly. Drip... drip... drip. Perfect! The purple flowers seemed to dance in the morning breeze.

"Step two," continued Grandma Rose, "pick three yellow sunflowers and two red roses."

Ellie counted carefully on her trunk. "One yellow, two yellow, three yellow!" She picked each flower gently. Then she found the roses. "One red, two red!"

"Wonderful counting, Ellie!" praised Grandma Rose. "Now for step three - the creative part! How should we arrange these flowers to make Mrs. Bird's grandson happy?"

Ellie thought hard, tapping her little foot. She looked at the bright yellow sunflowers and the deep red roses. Suddenly, she had an idea!

"I know! I know!" Ellie trumpeted excitedly. "We can make a rainbow! Yellow in the middle like the sun, and red around it like a hug!"

"What a creative idea!" said Grandma Rose, clapping her hands.

But when Ellie tried to arrange the flowers, they kept falling over. She tried once, twice, three times. Each time, the flowers tumbled down.

"This is hard," Ellie sighed, her little trunk drooping.

"Don't give up, dear one," encouraged Grandma Rose. "Sometimes the best ideas need patience. What if we try a different way?"

Ellie thought again. Then she had another creative idea! "What if we put them in the pretty blue vase? The yellow and red will look like a sunset!"

Together, they carefully placed each flower in the vase. The yellow sunflowers stood tall in the center, while the red roses created a beautiful circle around them.

"Oh my!" gasped Mrs. Bird when they brought the arrangement over. "This is the most beautiful bouquet I've ever seen! Thank you for volunteering to help, Ellie!"

Ellie felt warm and proud inside. Her creative idea had worked! She had followed directions, tried again when things were hard, and helped make someone happy.

"You see, Ellie," said Grandma Rose as they sat together on the balcony, watching butterflies visit their garden, "when we use our creativity, follow directions carefully, and don't give up, we can make beautiful things happen."

Ellie smiled, already thinking of new creative ways to help in the garden tomorrow.

The End.
