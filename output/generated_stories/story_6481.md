# Leo's Laundry Room Parade

Leo was a brave little lion cub with bright eyes and a soft, golden mane. One rainy afternoon, Leo padded into the laundry room. Drip, drop went the rain against the window. Hummmm went the washing machine, and swish-swish went the dryer. Warm, soapy smells filled the air.

“Mmm, it smells like clean clouds,” Leo said with a happy purr.

The laundry room was full of colors. There were blue socks, red shirts, and a long, wiggly green scarf. A basket of clothespins looked like tiny wooden smiles. Leo’s whiskers twitched with curiosity.

“I want to make something,” Leo whispered. “I want to make a show!”

Mama Lion peeked in. “What are you thinking, my brave cub?”

“I want to make a parade,” Leo said. “A laundry room parade! With costumes! And music! And dancing paws!”

Mama Lion smiled. “That sounds very creative. I love your idea. But we must keep the room safe and tidy. The laundry needs to be folded. We also share this space with everyone. Can you promise to be careful and helpful?”

Leo stood tall. “I promise. I will be careful. I will be helpful. And I will make art!”

He began to plan. “I will use soft things and not sticky paint. I will use gentle clothespins, not tapey tape.” Leo tapped his chin. “I will ask before I use anything.”

He took a deep breath, then called, “Mama? May I use these clean towels for costumes?”

Mama Lion shook her head kindly. “Clean towels need to stay clean. How about those old washcloths in the basket? They are for playtime.”

Leo listened. “Okay! Thank you for telling me.”

He made a little cape from a blue washcloth and clipped it with two tiny clothespins. Click, click. He made a crown from the green scarf, but it was too long. It drooped over his eyes.

“Oops!” Leo giggled. “I can’t see!”

Papa Lion walked by with a laundry basket. “Need help, little leader?”

“Yes, please!” Leo said. “May I borrow a ribbon to tie my crown? And I will help fold later.”

“That’s a fair trade,” Papa Lion said. “We can compromise. You get a ribbon, and you give time to help.”

“Compromise,” Leo repeated slowly. “That means we both get something we need.”

Papa Lion tied the scarf gently. “There. Safe and snug.”

Leo twirled. Swish! The scarf crown felt soft on his ears.

Now Leo wanted music. He listened to the room. Hum, hummm went the washer. Thump, thump went the dryer. Drip, drop went the rain. “We have a band!” Leo cheered. “Washer, Dryer, and Rain!”

He set up a stage with a folded bath mat. He used a sock as a microphone. “Testing, testing!” he said in a tiny roar. “Welcome to the Laundry Room Parade!”

His stuffed elephant, Ellie, sat on a laundry basket like a very important guest. Leo bowed. He sang a silly song:

“Swish-swish socks and spin-spin drum,
Hum-hum washer, here we come!
Colors bright, we dance and play,
We make art in kind, safe ways!”

He danced in gentle steps so the baskets would not tip. Tap-tap went his paws on the floor. His tail swished in time with the hum. Leo posed and smiled. He felt brave and proud.

Then he saw Baby Cub crawling toward the open basket of clothespins. Baby Cub reached for a handful.

Leo paused. He remembered what Mama said: “We share this space.” He also remembered civic-responsibility: helping the whole pride.

He knelt down. “Hi, Baby,” he said softly. “These pins can pinch. Ouch! Let’s play with something safe.” He offered a soft washcloth instead. “This is squishy and fun.”

Baby Cub giggled and squeezed the cloth. Squeeeze. Leo carefully pushed the clothespin basket away from the edge. He felt warm inside. Helping felt good.

Mama Lion returned with a smile. “I saw you keep Baby safe. Thank you for taking care of our home.”

“I wanted everyone to enjoy the parade,” Leo said. “That is part of being a helper.”

“Would you like an audience?” Mama asked.

“Yes!” Leo said. “But first, I promised to help fold.”

They folded together. Fold, pat. Fold, pat. Leo made neat little stacks. He felt calm and strong.

When the folding was done, Mama, Papa, and Baby sat on a soft blanket. Leo took a brave breath and spoke clearly. “Welcome to my art show. I made costumes from old cloths. I made music from the room. I will dance in careful steps. If you want to join, please ask, and we can share.”

He looked at Mama. “Is that good communication?”

Mama nodded. “Very good. You told us your plan. You shared the rules. You were kind and clear.”

Leo began the parade. He marched slowly past the washer and nodded to the hum. He tiptoed by the dryer and bowed to the thump. He spun near the window and waved to the tiny rain dots.

“Go, Leo!” cheered Papa. Baby Cub clapped. Clap, clap!

At the end, Leo raised his sock microphone. “Thank you for coming! Art can be anywhere. We can make and share. We can help and care!”

Everyone roared softly—Rrrrrr!—the happy kind.

Mama hugged Leo. “You expressed yourself with your heart and also kept our home safe.”

Leo purred. “I learned that creative-expression is fun when we communicate, compromise, and care for others.”

The End.
