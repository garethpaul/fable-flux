# Tilly Turtle’s Table Team

In a sunny house with a cozy dining room, lived Tilly, a shy turtle. Tilly liked quiet. She liked the smooth wooden table and the soft clink of cups. She liked the tall chair legs that made little tunnels to peek through. The dining room smelled like warm pancakes in the morning and sweet oranges in the afternoon.

One day, Tilly peeked at a bright flyer on the wall. It said, “Table Tennis Fun! Today at 3:00 in the dining room!” There was a ping-pong paddle on the picture. Tilly’s heart thump-thumped. “Sports in the dining room?” she whispered. “I am shy. But maybe I can try.”

Mama Turtle smiled. “You can build your skills, Tilly,” she said gently. “We can make a plan.”

“A plan?” Tilly asked, eyes wide.

“Yes,” Mama said. “A time plan helps us practice. We will make a list.”

They made a simple list on a sticky note:
- 1:00 Practice holding the paddle.
- 1:30 Practice soft taps.
- 2:00 Snack and rest.
- 2:30 Practice serves.
- 3:00 Table Tennis Fun!

Tilly nodded slowly. “I can do that,” she said.

At 1:00, the dining room was quiet and bright. Tilly held a small red paddle. It felt smooth and a little heavy. “Hello, paddle,” she whispered. “Please be my friend.” She wrapped her little turtle fingers around the handle. “Grip, not squeeze,” Mama coached. Tilly breathed in. She breathed out. “Grip, not squeeze,” she repeated, soft and steady.

At 1:30, Mama set a white ball on the table. Tap. Tap. Tap. The ball made a tiny “tick-tick” sound. Tilly giggled. “It sounds like rain drops,” she said.

“Soft hands,” Mama reminded. “Eyes on the ball.”

Tap. Tap. Oops! The ball rolled under a chair. Tilly pulled into her shell for a moment. Then she peeked out. “Try again,” she whispered to herself. She tried again. Tap. Tap. Tap. “I am building a skill,” she said with a small smile.

At 2:00, they had orange slices. The juicy smell filled the room. Tilly licked her lips. “Mmm.” She felt her tummy smile and her body rest.

At 2:30, it was serve time. Mama showed how to toss the ball up and tap it over the net. Toss. Tap. Whoosh. The ball zipped softly. Tilly tried. Toss. Tap. Plop. The ball fell. Tilly took a deep breath. “Try again,” she said. Toss. Tap. Boing! It hopped over the net. “I did it!” she cheered, not too loud.

Soon, friends came in. Benny Bunny bounced with a grin. Sisi Squirrel swished her fluffy tail. Gus Goose waddled with neat steps. “Hi, Tilly!” they said.

“Hi,” Tilly answered, shy but brave.

Coach Panda set up the net on the dining room table. “Welcome, players,” Coach said kindly. “We are here to have fun, to learn, and to be fair.” Coach pointed to a sign: Be Kind. Take Turns. Help Each Other.

“That is justice,” Coach said. “Justice means fair and kind.”

Tilly nodded. Fair and kind felt good.

They began. Tap-tap. Tap-tap. The dining room made happy sounds: the tick of the ball, the squeak of chairs, the soft claps of little paws. Tilly waited when it was not her turn. She watched the clock on the wall. The big hand moved. The small hand pointed. “When the big hand is on the six, it is my turn,” she told herself. Time plan, time plan.

At last, it was Tilly and Benny’s turn. “We are a team,” Benny said.

Tilly’s heart thump-thumped again. “We can do it,” she whispered. She held the paddle. Grip, not squeeze. Eyes on the ball.

Benny served. Whoosh! The ball came to Tilly. Tap! She sent it back. “Nice!” Benny said. Tilly smiled.

Tap-tap. Tap-tap. Back and forth they played. Sisi counted, “One, two, three!” Gus waddled and cheered, “Honk for team!”

Then a little mix-up happened. The ball hit the edge and rolled. Sisi called, “Point for me!” But Gus said, “I think it hit the net.” Everyone looked worried.

Coach Panda raised a paw. “Let’s be fair,” Coach said. “When we are not sure, we replay the point. That is justice.”

Tilly took a tiny breath. “We can replay,” she said softly. Sisi nodded. “Okay! Replay.”

They replayed. Tap-tap. Tap-tap. Tilly made a careful serve. Toss. Tap. Whoosh! The ball landed just right. “Great serve!” said Coach.

Soon, the game ended with giggles and claps. No one shouted. No one cried. Tilly felt warm in her shell. She felt proud. She had used her plan. She had built her skill.

After the game, Coach Panda said, “Friends, we have one more thing. Our community needs clean tables. Can we help?”

“Yes!” everyone said.

They got soft cloths and warm soapy water. Swish, wipe. Swish, wipe. The dining room smelled fresh and bright. Tilly wiped slow and careful. “I am helping my community,” she said. “I can do hard things, one small step at a time.”

Mama hugged her. “You practiced. You managed your time. You were fair and kind. You built your skills.”

Tilly tucked her chin and smiled. “I am still shy,” she said. “But I am brave, too.”

On the wall, Tilly stuck a new note:
- Practice a little each day.
- Be fair and kind.
- Help the team.
- Take turns.
- Try again.

She read the list and felt ready for tomorrow.

The lesson was clear in Tilly’s heart: With a simple plan, kind fairness, and steady practice, small steps make big skills. And helping together makes the whole room shine.

The End.
