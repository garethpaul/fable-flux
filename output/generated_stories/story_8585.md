# Rilo Raccoon’s Big Choice

Rilo the musical raccoon hummed a soft tune as he padded into the creative art room. The room smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Paints sparkled in rainbow cups. Drums, bells, and a tiny xylophone sat on a shelf.

“Good morning, colors! Good morning, sounds!” Rilo sang, tapping his tail like a drum. Tap-tap-tap.

Today, Rilo had two fun plans. Plan A: make a big music-and-paint poster. Plan B: play outside in the park and make music with sticks and leaves. Both sounded wonderful. But he could not do both before lunch. He had to choose.

Rilo’s ears wiggled. “Hmm,” he said. “Which one?” He sniffed the sweet smell of paper. He listened to the soft clink of the xylophone. He peered out the window and saw trees waving hello. The sky looked bright and blue.

Just then, his friends bounced in. Mimi the mouse carried a box of glitter. Gus the goose wore a green scarf. Tavi the turtle brought a basket with snacks.

“Hi, Rilo!” Mimi squeaked. “Do you want to paint with me? We can sprinkle glitter like stars!”

Gus honked gently. “Or we can go outside,” he said. “The wind makes lovely whoosh music.”

Tavi nodded slowly. “We have time for one big thing before lunch,” he said. “Let’s decide.”

Rilo’s whiskers twitched. His heart felt bouncy and busy. “I love painting,” he said. “But I also love the park.” He took a deep breath and put his paw on his chest. “I need to make a good choice.”

Mimi set the glitter down with a tiny tinkle. “Maybe we can talk it out,” she said. “We can share ideas.”

“That’s called negotiation,” Tavi said kindly. “We listen. We take turns. We choose together.”

Rilo nodded. “Okay. I will say what I like, and I will listen, too.” He tapped his tail in a calm beat. Tap… tap… tap.

Mimi spoke first. “I like painting today because the colors are bright. I want to paint sunshine. I can almost taste the lemon-yellow!”

Gus fluffed his feathers. “I like outside because the air smells fresh. I can feel the grass tickle my feet. I want to march and make leaf-crackle music.”

Tavi smiled. “I like both. I want us to feel happy and still have time to eat our snack.”

Rilo looked around. He had an idea. “What if we choose one now and one later? We can go outside first while the sun is warm. After lunch, we can paint the sounds we heard!”

Mimi’s eyes sparkled. “Oh! We can paint wind whooshes and leaf-crackles!”

Gus honked, “Yes! We can be music explorers outside. Then we bring the music inside!”

Tavi clapped his little turtle hands. “That is a smart plan. We made a choice by talking and listening.”

Rilo smiled. His chest felt light. “Decision made,” he said. “Outside first!”

They packed simple things: a small drum, a wooden spoon, and a notebook. Outside, the air smelled like pine and warm dirt. The sun hugged their fur and feathers. A cool breeze kissed their cheeks.

“Listen,” Rilo whispered. The trees said shhhh. Birds chirped sweet notes. Far away, a dog barked woof-woof.

Rilo tapped the drum in time with the birds. Tuk-tuk, chirp-chirp. Mimi shook a tiny bell. Ting-ting. Gus stomped softly in the grass. Step, step. Tavi rubbed two sticks together. Scratch-scratch.

“This is music!” Rilo laughed. He felt grateful. “Thank you, trees. Thank you, breeze. Thank you, friends.”

They found a big leaf that made a flappy sound. They found pebbles that clicked like little castanets. They made a park parade and sang, “We chose well, we chose well, we’re happy, we can tell!”

Soon, their tummies rumbled. Tavi held up the basket. “Snack time,” he said. They sat in the shade. Crunch went the crackers. Sip went the water. Rilo looked at his friends and the sky. “I’m thankful for this day,” he said.

After lunch, they went back to the creative art room. It felt cool and cozy inside. The smell of crayons welcomed them. Rilo placed big paper on the table. “Now we paint the sounds we heard,” he said.

Mimi painted swoopy blue wind lines. Gus dabbed green dots for grass steps. Tavi made tiny brown scratches for stick sounds. Rilo painted golden bird notes and added silver glitter for sunlight. He hummed as he worked. Hum-hum-hummm.

When they finished, the poster sparkled with music and color. It showed their whole day—the choice, the outside fun, and the happy teamwork.

Mimi giggled. “I’m proud of our choice.”

Gus nodded. “I’m grateful we listened.”

Tavi smiled. “We decided together. That made it easier.”

Rilo touched the poster gently. “Today I learned to make a plan, to talk, to listen, and to choose. I can make good choices.”

He tapped his tail one last time. Tap-tap-tap. “Thank you, friends,” he said. “Thank you, park. Thank you, art room. I feel happy.”

They hung the poster by the window, where the sun could make it sparkle. The colors danced. The room felt full of music and kindness.

The End.
