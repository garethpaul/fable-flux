# Dottie the Dolphin's Attic Adventure

Dottie the dolphin loved to explore new places. Today, she swam up through the old pipes of a big house and found herself in a dusty, quiet attic. Sunbeams danced through a small window, making everything look magical.

"Wow!" squeaked Dottie, her fins splashing gently in a puddle of water that had collected there. "What a wonderful place to play!"

As she looked around, she spotted three children sitting in different corners of the attic. They all looked upset and were not talking to each other.

"Hello!" called Dottie cheerfully. "I'm Dottie! What are your names?"

The first child, Maria, had beautiful dark hair and was holding colorful paper. "I'm Maria," she said sadly. "I wanted to make paper flowers like my abuela taught me, but..."

"But I need the scissors first!" interrupted Jin, a boy with kind eyes who was holding a small wooden box. "I want to make origami cranes like my grandmother showed me!"

"Well, I was here first!" said Amara, a girl with curly hair who was clutching some clay. "I want to make pottery like my family does!"

Dottie noticed that all three children's faces were red and their hands were clenched tight. She had felt this way before too.

"Oh my," said Dottie gently, swimming closer. "It sounds like everyone is feeling very angry and frustrated."

"We are!" said Maria, her voice getting louder. "Nobody wants to share!"

Dottie's wise dolphin heart understood. "You know," she said softly, "when I feel angry, my whole body gets tight and hot, just like yours. But I learned something special that helps."

"What?" asked Jin, curious despite being upset.

"First, let's all take three deep breaths together," suggested Dottie. "Breathe in slowly... and out slowly. Feel your shoulders relax."

The children breathed with Dottie. She noticed their tight fists opening up.

"Better?" asked Dottie. "Now, when I feel angry, I remember that sharing makes everything more fun. What if you all worked together?"

"Together?" asked Amara, tilting her head.

"Yes!" chirped Dottie excitedly. "Maria, your paper flowers sound beautiful! Jin, origami cranes are amazing! And Amara, pottery is wonderful! What if you took turns with the scissors and helped each other?"

Maria's eyes lit up. "I could teach you both how to make papel picado like my family does for celebrations!"

"And I could show you how to fold paper cranes for good luck!" added Jin, smiling.

"My clay could make little pots for your flowers!" said Amara, clapping her hands.

Dottie watched happily as the children began sharing their materials and ideas. The quiet attic filled with laughter and excited chatter.

"First, let's use the scissors to cut Maria's paper," suggested Jin. "Then I'll fold some cranes, and then Amara can shape her clay!"

"Taking turns makes everyone happy!" observed Dottie.

As the afternoon passed, the children created the most beautiful art together. Maria's colorful flowers decorated Jin's origami cranes, which sat perfectly in Amara's tiny clay pots.

"Thank you, Dottie," said Maria, giving the dolphin a gentle hug. "You taught us that when we feel angry, we can breathe and share instead of fight."

"And learning about each other's families made everything more special!" added Amara.

Dottie beamed with joy. "Remember, little ones, anger is a normal feeling, but we can choose what to do with it. Deep breaths, kind words, and sharing always make things better!"

As the sun set through the attic window, Dottie waved goodbye to her new friends and splashed back down the pipes, her heart full of happiness.

The End.
