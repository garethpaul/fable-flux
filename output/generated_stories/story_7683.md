# Gigi the Giraffe Learns to Care

In a sunny backyard filled with tall trees and colorful flowers, lived a thoughtful giraffe named Gigi. She had the most beautiful golden spots and the longest, softest neck in the whole neighborhood.

One bright morning, Gigi woke up feeling very tired. Her neck felt stiff, her tummy was grumbly, and she felt sad looking at the messy backyard around her.

"Oh my," said Gigi, stretching her long neck toward the warm sun. "I don't feel very good today."

The backyard was covered with fallen leaves, empty water bowls, and wilted flowers. The bird bath was dirty, and the little garden looked thirsty and droopy.

Gigi's friend Benny the Bunny hopped over. "Good morning, Gigi! You look tired. Are you okay?"

"I feel yucky, Benny," Gigi sighed. "And look at our beautiful backyard. It's all messy and the plants look sad too."

Benny wiggled his nose thoughtfully. "Maybe you need to take care of yourself first, Gigi. When we feel good, we can help others feel good too!"

"Take care of myself?" asked Gigi, tilting her spotted head. "What does that mean?"

"Well," said Benny, "when did you last eat a good breakfast?"

Gigi thought hard. "Yesterday morning, I think."

"And when did you last drink fresh, cool water?"

"Um... I can't remember," Gigi said quietly.

"And when did you last rest in the shade?" Benny asked gently.

Gigi's eyes grew wide. "I've been so busy worrying about everything else, I forgot to take care of me!"

Benny smiled. "Let's fix that! First, let's get you some yummy green leaves and fresh water."

Gigi munched on crispy, sweet leaves from the apple tree. "Mmm, these taste like sunshine!" she said, feeling better already.

Next, she drank cool, refreshing water from the clean part of the stream. "Ahh, that feels so good in my tummy," Gigi said happily.

Then Benny showed her a perfect shady spot under the big oak tree. "Now rest here for a little while," he said.

Gigi lay down in the soft, cool grass. She felt the gentle breeze on her spots and listened to the birds singing. "This feels wonderful," she said, closing her eyes.

After her rest, Gigi felt much, much better! Her neck felt strong, her tummy was happy, and she felt cheerful again.

"Now I have energy to help our backyard!" said Gigi excitedly.

Together, Gigi and Benny cleaned the bird bath with fresh water. Gigi used her tall neck to reach the high branches and gently shake down the old leaves. Benny helped water the thirsty flowers.

"Look!" said Gigi. "When I took care of myself first, I had the energy to take care of our home too!"

The flowers stood up tall and bright. The birds splashed happily in their clean bath. The backyard looked beautiful and healthy again.

"You're so smart, Gigi," said Benny. "Taking care of yourself isn't selfish - it helps you take care of everything else!"

From that day on, Gigi always remembered to eat well, drink water, and rest when she needed to. And because she felt good, she could help keep their sunny backyard beautiful for all her friends.

"Taking care of me helps me take care of everything I love," Gigi would tell her friends with a big, happy smile.

The End.
