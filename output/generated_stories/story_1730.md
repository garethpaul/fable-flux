# Leo's Big Cleanup Adventure

Leo was a brave little lion cub with a golden mane that sparkled in the morning sunlight. He lived in a cozy house with his mama and papa, and his favorite place was their playful living room. The room was filled with soft, colorful cushions, wooden blocks, picture books, and toys from all around the world.

This morning, Leo's mama called from the kitchen. "Leo, sweetie! Your friends Aria and Chen are coming over to play soon. Can you please tidy up the living room?"

Leo looked around at all his scattered toys. There were his red fire truck, blue building blocks, and stuffed animals everywhere. "Aw, Mama," Leo groaned, his little whiskers twitching. "That's going to take forever!"

"Remember, Leo," Mama said gently, "when we respect our space and our friends, we make everyone feel welcome and happy."

Leo sighed but started picking up his toys. First, he grabbed his fire truck and placed it on the toy shelf. The smooth red paint felt cool under his paws. Then he heard the doorbell ring.

"They're here!" Leo called out, dropping his blocks with a loud CLATTER and running to the door.

Aria bounced in wearing a beautiful purple dress with tiny mirrors that jingled softly. "Hi Leo! I brought my special dancing scarves from my grandmother in India!"

Chen followed, carrying a small wooden box. "And I brought my family's tangram puzzle from China! Want to see?"

The three friends ran into the living room, but Chen suddenly stopped. "Oh no," he said quietly, looking at all the toys on the floor. "It's hard to find a good spot to play."

Aria tried to dance with her colorful scarves, but she tripped over Leo's building blocks. "Ouch!" she said, rubbing her knee.

Leo's ears drooped. He felt his cheeks grow warm with embarrassment. "I'm sorry, friends. I didn't finish cleaning up like Mama asked."

"That's okay," Chen said kindly. "We can help you! In my family, we always work together."

"Yes!" Aria agreed, her bracelets jingling as she clapped. "My grandmother says that when we help each other, we show respect and love."

Leo's eyes brightened. "Really? You'd help me?"

"Of course!" his friends said together.

The three cubs worked as a team. Leo put away his blocks, stacking them by color - red, blue, yellow, green. Chen carefully organized the books, arranging them from biggest to smallest. Aria gathered all the stuffed animals and lined them up like they were watching a parade.

"This is actually fun!" Leo laughed as he placed the last toy car on the shelf.

When they finished, the living room looked wonderful. The soft carpet was clear for dancing, the coffee table was perfect for Chen's puzzle, and there was plenty of space for everyone to play.

"Now we can enjoy all our different games!" Chen said, opening his tangram box. The wooden pieces smelled like cedar and felt smooth and warm.

Aria twirled with her silk scarves, the fabric floating through the air like colorful clouds. "And I can dance without tripping!"

Leo felt proud and happy. "Thank you for helping me, friends. I learned that respecting our space means everyone can have more fun!"

Mama peeked into the room and smiled. "Look at this beautiful, tidy space! And look at these wonderful friends working together."

The three cubs spent the rest of the day playing Chen's puzzle, dancing with Aria's scarves, and building tall towers with Leo's blocks. They learned that when they respect their space and help each other, everyone feels special and included.

The End.
