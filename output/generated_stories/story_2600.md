# Benny Bunny’s Curious Practice Corner

Benny Bunny was a small, determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He had a cozy study corner by the window. The corner had a tiny desk, a basket of crayons, a stack of picture cards, and a soft, bouncy ball with blue stars. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. The room smelled like fresh paper and lemon soap. Benny loved this little place.

One morning, Benny twitched his whiskers. “I want to learn new things,” he said. “I’m curious! What can I try today?”

He looked at his basket. There was a picture card with a ball. There was also a card with the letter B. Benny traced the big B with his paw. “B is for ball,” he said slowly. “B, b, b—ball!” His voice sounded round and bouncy, like the ball itself.

Benny picked up the starry ball. “I want to play a sport,” he whispered. He tossed the ball up. It went plip! into his soft paws. “I want to learn to bounce and catch.”

Benny tried. Boing! The ball bounced high. “Oh!” he squeaked as it rolled away. “Come back, ball!” He hopped after it and giggled. He tried again. Boing, plip! Boing, plop! Sometimes he caught it, and sometimes he did not.

“I can do it if I practice,” Benny told himself. His ears stood tall. He set a small goal. “I will catch the ball three times.”

He counted in a gentle voice. “One.” Boing, plip! He caught it. “Two.” Boing, plip! He caught it again. “Three.” Boing, plop! Oops! The ball slipped. Benny felt a tiny frown. His tummy felt twisty.

Just then, Mama Bunny peeked in. She smelled like warm carrot soup. “Hello, Benny,” she said softly. “What are you curious about today?”

“I want to learn a sport,” Benny said. “I want to bounce and catch. But it is hard.”

Mama smiled. “Curiosity helps us try. Practice helps us grow. Would you like a tip?”

“Yes, please,” said Benny.

Mama showed him how to bend his knees a little. “Soft paws,” she said. “Eyes on the ball. Breathe in. Breathe out.”

Benny tried. He bent his knees. He breathed in. He breathed out. Boing, plip! The ball landed snug in his paws. “I did it!” he cheered. His heart felt warm and proud.

A knock came at the door. Tap, tap! It was Benny’s friend, Tessa Turtle. She wore a tiny red headband. “Hello,” said Tessa in a calm voice. “I heard boing, boing! Can I play, too?”

“Yes!” said Benny. “We can take turns.”

They made a plan. “First Benny, then Tessa,” Benny said. “We say, ‘My turn,’ and ‘Your turn.’”

“My turn,” said Benny. Boing, plip! He caught the ball and passed it gently to Tessa.

“My turn,” said Tessa. Boing… plip. She caught it, slow and steady. “Thank you,” she said.

“Great job!” said Benny. He clapped his paws. The clap sounded like little pats on a drum.

They kept playing. Sometimes the ball rolled under the chair and made a dusty, tickly smell. Sometimes it bounced high and made a happy thump on the rug. They laughed and learned. They counted, too. “One, two, three,” they said together.

After a while, Benny felt curious again. He looked at the picture cards. He picked up the B card. “B is for ball,” he said. He found T for turtle. “T is for Tessa.” He found R for rabbit. “R is for rabbit—me!”

Tessa smiled. “We are learning words and a sport,” she said. “We are using our study corner to build skills.”

Benny nodded. “Curiosity helps us ask, ‘What is next?’” His eyes shone.

He thought for a moment. “Let’s make a rhythm with words and bounces.” He tapped the ball gently. “B, b, ball,” he said, tap, tap. Tessa joined in. “T, t, turtle,” tap, tap. They giggled at the silly song. The sounds felt smooth on their tongues, like humming.

Then the ball slipped again. Boing, plop! It rolled under the desk. Benny felt a pinch of worry. “Oh no.”

Tessa said, “Let’s try a plan.” She pointed with a slow, careful claw. “We can move the chair. We can reach the ball together.”

“Good idea,” said Benny. They counted, “One, two, lift.” They slid the chair. Benny reached. He felt cool floor on his paw and a little puff of dust. He grabbed the ball.

“We solved the problem,” Benny said. His whiskers wiggled with joy. “Curiosity, planning, and taking turns helped us.”

Mama Bunny clapped. “What did you learn today?” she asked.

Benny thought. He stood tall. “I learned to bounce and catch. I learned B, T, and R. I learned to take turns with Tessa. When I am curious, I try. When I practice, I grow. When there is a problem, I make a plan.”

Tessa added, “And we have fun!”

Benny hugged the ball. The blue stars felt smooth under his paws. The study corner felt bright and happy. The air smelled like soup and clean paper. Benny’s heart felt brave.

“Tomorrow,” Benny said, “I will be curious again. Maybe I will learn a new word. Maybe I will try a new sport move. Step by step.”

Mama smiled. “Yes, little rabbit. Curiosity is a gentle light. It helps you see the next step.”

Benny nodded. “I am a determined rabbit,” he said. “I keep trying.”

They put the ball back in the basket. They stacked the picture cards. Benny and Tessa high-fived—paw to claw, soft and careful.

“Thank you for playing,” Benny said. “Your turn to pick next time.”

“Deal,” said Tessa.

Benny felt calm and proud. He looked at his study corner and whispered, “Curiosity helps me learn.”

The End.
