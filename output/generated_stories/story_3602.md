# Joey Roo Plans a Promise

The warm kitchen smelled like cinnamon toast and oranges. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. A joyful kangaroo joey named Joey Roo bounced in, his feet thump-thump-thumping softly on the mat.

“Good morning, Mama!” Joey Roo sang.

“Good morning, my sweet jumper,” Mama Roo said, stirring a pot with a wooden spoon. The spoon made a soft tap-tap against the pan. “Today we can make art. We will plan it together.”

Joey Roo’s eyes sparkled. “Art! Can we use shiny things?”

“We can,” Mama Roo smiled. “But first, we must plan. Planning helps us feel calm.” She set out a big calendar, crayons, and a jar full of buttons, ribbons, and colorful bottle caps. Next to it, she placed a little bin labeled Recycle.

Joey Roo sniffed the cinnamon air. He felt warm and happy. “What will we make?”

“We will make a ‘Kindness Collage’ from clean recycled treasures,” Mama Roo said. “We will use boxes, paper rolls, and bottle caps we saved. And we will make a promise to be honest and careful with our supplies.”

Joey Roo bounced once. “I promise!”

Mama Roo nodded. “Let’s plan. Step one: We draw our idea. Step two: We gather our recycled things. Step three: We make the art. Step four: We tidy up.”

Joey Roo drew a sun with smiling bottle-cap eyes. He drew a little heart. He drew a big kangaroo with a tiny joey in her pouch. “This is us!”

Mama Roo clapped softly. “Lovely plan, my joey.”

They washed their hands. The warm water felt smooth. The soap smelled like lemon. Joey Roo giggled as bubbles tickled his paws.

Mama Roo showed him the recycle bin. “These are clean boxes and paper rolls. We use what we have. We do not take more than our plan says.”

Joey Roo nodded. “I will be honest and careful.”

They began to make the collage. Joey Roo glued a shiny blue cap on the sun. Plink! The cap made a tiny sound as it touched the glue. He pressed down, feeling the cool plastic against his paw.

Mama Roo cut a paper heart. Snip, snip. “How many bottle caps does your plan need?” she asked.

Joey Roo looked at his drawing. “One, two, three. Three bottle caps.”

Mama Roo smiled. “Good planning.”

But then Joey Roo saw a very, very shiny red cap in the jar. It sparkled like a cherry. “Oh!” he whispered. He wanted it so much. His paws felt wiggly. He took the red cap and slipped it behind his back.

He used one blue cap, then another, then another. He had used all three, just like the plan. Still, the red cap felt hot in his paw. He looked at Mama Roo. She was humming and placing ribbons.

Joey Roo’s tummy felt twisty. He held the red cap tight. His ears drooped. The kitchen was still warm, but Joey Roo felt a little cold inside. He had promised to be honest. He had promised to follow the plan.

“Joey,” Mama Roo said gently, “your ears are telling me a story. Do you need a calm breath?”

Joey Roo nodded. Mama Roo put a paw on her belly. “In like warm cocoa.” They breathed in together. “Out like a soft wind.” They breathed out. Joey Roo felt a little better.

Mama Roo looked at him kindly. “Is there something you want to tell me?”

Joey Roo took another calm breath. He opened his paws. The shiny red cap glowed like a tiny apple in his hand. “Mama, I took this. I hid it. I wanted it so much. But I promised to be honest. I am sorry.”

The kitchen seemed to sigh with them. The cinnamon air felt sweet again. Mama Roo knelt down and hugged Joey Roo. “Thank you for telling the truth. Honesty helps us feel safe and calm.”

Joey Roo’s eyes were watery. “Will you be mad?”

“I am proud of you for being honest,” Mama Roo said. “When we make a mistake, the best plan is to tell the truth and make it right. What do you think we should do now?”

Joey Roo looked at the plan. He looked at the red cap. “We did not plan to use it. I can put it back and keep my promise.”

“That is a smart plan,” Mama Roo said. “And next time, we can plan for a red cap, too.”

Joey Roo placed the cap back in the jar. Plink. He felt lighter, like a hop with no pack. “I feel calm now.”

They finished the collage with ribbons and boxes. The glue smelled a little like fresh paper. The bottle caps clicked as they settled. Joey Roo made a tiny sign that said, “We Tell the Truth.”

When they were done, they tidied up. Joey Roo put the extra boxes back into the recycle bin. He stacked paper rolls neatly. He wiped tiny glue dots with a soft cloth. The sponge made squish-squish sounds.

Mama Roo held up the art. The smiling sun shone. The heart was bright. The joey in the pouch looked brave and happy.

“What did we learn today?” Mama Roo asked.

Joey Roo stood tall. “Plan first. Use what we have. Tell the truth. When I am honest, my heart feels calm.”

Mama Roo kissed his forehead. “Yes, my joyful joey. Honesty makes our home warm, just like this kitchen.”

Joey Roo bounced once, twice. “Next time, we will plan for the red cap!”

Mama Roo laughed. “Yes. We will plan together.”

They hung the collage on the fridge, next to the calendar. The magnets clicked. The kitchen smelled like oranges again as Mama peeled a snack. Joey Roo crunched a slice. It was juicy and sweet.

“I like honest crunches,” Joey Roo said, giggling.

Mama Roo smiled. “Me too.”

The End.
