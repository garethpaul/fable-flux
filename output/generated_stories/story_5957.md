# Nutkin's Rooftop Garden Adventure

High up on the red brick rooftop of Mrs. Chen's apartment building, a clever little squirrel named Nutkin had made his cozy home. His fluffy gray tail twitched with excitement as the warm morning sun tickled his whiskers. But today, Nutkin noticed something troubling.

"Oh my acorns!" squeaked Nutkin, looking at his empty food bowl. His tummy rumbled loudly. "I've been eating nothing but leftover cookies and cake crumbs from the garbage cans. No wonder I feel so sleepy and grumpy!"

Nutkin's friend Ruby, a bright red cardinal, landed nearby with a gentle flutter of wings. "Good morning, Nutkin! You look a bit droopy today. Are you feeling okay?"

"Not really," Nutkin admitted, his ears drooping. "I keep eating sweet treats, but they make me feel yucky. I want to eat better foods, but I don't know how to find them up here on this rooftop."

Ruby tilted her head thoughtfully. "You know what? I think you need some healthy foods! Fresh fruits, nuts, and seeds give us energy and make us feel strong. Would you like me to help you?"

"Yes, please!" Nutkin's tail perked up. "But how can we find healthy food up here?"

Ruby chirped excitedly, "We can make our own rooftop garden! I'll show you where to find seeds, and you can use your clever paws to plant them."

Together, they flew and scampered around the rooftop. Ruby showed Nutkin some old flower pots left behind by the previous tenants. The pots felt cool and smooth under Nutkin's tiny paws.

"First," said Ruby, "we need good soil." She led him to a corner where fallen leaves had created rich, dark earth that smelled fresh and earthy.

Nutkin filled the pots carefully, his whiskers twitching with concentration. "This dirt feels so soft and squishy!"

Next, Ruby brought seeds she had collected - sunflower seeds, pumpkin seeds, and even some berry seeds. "These will grow into delicious, healthy foods!" she explained.

"But what if the other rooftop animals try to eat our plants before they grow?" Nutkin worried, remembering how the pigeons sometimes took his food.

"We can talk to them nicely," Ruby suggested. "Sometimes when we explain things kindly, others understand and want to help instead of taking."

When a family of pigeons landed near their garden, Nutkin felt nervous. But he remembered Ruby's advice. He took a deep breath and spoke clearly: "Hello, friends! We're growing healthy foods here. Would you like to help us take care of the garden? When the plants grow big, we can all share the healthy snacks together!"

The pigeons cooed happily. "What a wonderful idea! We'd love to help water the plants and keep them safe."

Day by day, Nutkin watered his plants with rainwater he collected in small containers. The fresh water sparkled like tiny diamonds in the sunlight. He watched tiny green sprouts push through the soil, and his heart filled with joy.

Soon, beautiful sunflowers stretched toward the sky, and plump red tomatoes hung like little lanterns. Nutkin munched on crunchy sunflower seeds and juicy tomatoes. "Mmm! These taste so much better than old cookies, and I feel so much stronger!"

Ruby perched beside him, preening her bright feathers. "How do you feel now, Nutkin?"

"Amazing!" Nutkin did a little dance, his fluffy tail spinning. "I have so much energy, and I'm not grumpy anymore. Plus, I made new friends by talking nicely to them!"

From that day on, Nutkin enjoyed his healthy rooftop garden, sharing fresh foods with all his friends and feeling happy and strong every single day.

The End.
