# Leo's Colorful Challenge

Leo the lion cub bounded into the bright art room, his golden fur shining under the warm lights. The room smelled like fresh crayons and sweet glue. Colorful paintings hung on the walls like rainbow butterflies.

"Good morning, Leo!" called Ms. Rabbit, his art teacher. "Today we're making special picture frames for your families!"

Leo's amber eyes sparkled with excitement. He loved making things with his paws! Ms. Rabbit showed everyone the example - a beautiful frame decorated with painted handprints, glittery stars, and colorful buttons.

"First, we paint our handprints," Ms. Rabbit explained. "Then we add decorations. Take your time and finish each step!"

Leo dipped his little paw in the blue paint. It felt cool and squishy between his toes. He pressed his paw onto the cardboard frame. "Look!" he roared softly. "My paw print!"

But when Leo tried to add the second handprint, the paint smudged. The blue mixed with yellow and turned green. "Oh no!" Leo huffed, his whiskers twitching with worry.

"This is too hard!" Leo grumbled, pushing the frame away. His tail swished back and forth - the way it always did when he felt frustrated.

Ms. Rabbit hopped over gently. "Leo, what's making you feel upset?"

"My handprints look messy," Leo said, his voice small. "I want to stop. It's not pretty like yours."

Ms. Rabbit sat down beside him. "Leo, when something feels hard, we can take deep breaths. Want to try with me?"

Leo nodded. Together, they breathed in slowly through their noses - "Smell the flowers," Ms. Rabbit said. Then they breathed out through their mouths - "Blow out the candles."

Leo's shoulders relaxed. His tail stopped swishing.

"Now," Ms. Rabbit said kindly, "let's look at your frame again. What do you see?"

Leo looked carefully. "The green part looks like grass! And the blue part looks like sky!"

"Exactly!" Ms. Rabbit smiled. "Sometimes our best art happens when things don't go as planned. Should we keep going?"

Leo felt braver now. "Yes! I want to finish my frame!"

Step by step, Leo added decorations. He glued on silver stars that twinkled like tiny suns. He stuck on red buttons that looked like ladybugs. Each time something felt tricky, Leo remembered to take deep breaths.

"This star won't stick!" Leo said when the glue seemed stubborn.

Instead of giving up, Leo pressed the star down and counted to ten. "One... two... three..." When he reached ten, the star stayed put!

"I did it!" Leo roared proudly.

Finally, Leo's frame was complete. It had blue and green handprints like sky and grass, twinkling stars, and bright red buttons. It was different from Ms. Rabbit's example, but it was beautiful in its own special way.

"Leo," Ms. Rabbit said, "you did something very important today. Even when you felt frustrated, you took deep breaths and kept trying. You finished your task!"

Leo beamed, his chest puffing out with pride. "I can't wait to show Mama and Papa! They'll love my special frame!"

As Leo carefully carried his artwork home, he felt warm and happy inside. He had learned that finishing something, even when it's hard, feels wonderful. And taking deep breaths helps when big feelings try to take over.

From that day on, whenever Leo faced a challenging task, he remembered his colorful frame and knew he could finish anything if he kept trying.

The End.
