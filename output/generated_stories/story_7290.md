# Leo the Lion Learns to Move and Help

Leo was a brave little lion cub with golden fur that sparkled in the morning sunlight. He lived with his family in a cozy house with a big dining room where they ate their meals together every day.

One sunny morning, Leo woke up feeling very sleepy and slow. "Yawn," he stretched his little paws. "I don't want to move today. I just want to sit still."

Leo shuffled slowly to the dining room where Mama Lion was setting the table. The wooden table gleamed, and colorful placemats made everything look cheerful. But Leo noticed something that made his nose wrinkle.

"Mama, why does our dining room smell funny?" Leo asked, sniffing the air.

Mama Lion looked around and gasped. "Oh my! Look at all this mess, Leo. There are crumbs on the floor, sticky spots on the table, and the recycling bin is overflowing!"

Leo saw banana peels, empty juice boxes, and paper napkins scattered everywhere. The dining room looked very untidy, and it didn't smell fresh anymore.

"But Mama, I'm too tired to help clean," Leo said, plopping down in his chair. "Moving around is hard work."

"Oh, Leo," Mama Lion said gently, "our bodies need to move to stay healthy and strong. When we don't move, we feel sleepy and weak. And when we don't take care of our home, it becomes unhealthy for everyone."

Leo's little sister Lily bounded into the room, her tail swishing happily. "Good morning!" she chirped. "I just did my morning stretches and jumping jacks! I feel amazing!"

Leo watched Lily bounce around the room with so much energy. She looked happy and bright-eyed. "How do you have so much energy, Lily?" he asked.

"Moving my body makes me feel great!" Lily explained. "Want to help me clean up? We can make it fun by dancing while we work!"

Leo thought about it. The dining room really did need help, and his family deserved a clean, healthy space to eat their meals.

"Okay," Leo said, standing up slowly. "But I don't know how to make cleaning fun."

Lily grinned and started marching in place. "Like this! March, march, march while you pick up the crumbs!" She demonstrated, lifting her knees high and picking up banana peels with each step.

Leo tried it too. "March, march, march!" he chanted, feeling his sleepiness start to fade away. As he moved his legs, his heart began to beat faster, and he felt more awake.

"Now let's do the wiping wiggle!" Lily giggled, wiggling her whole body while she wiped the table with a damp cloth.

Leo wiggled too, cleaning the sticky spots. "This is fun!" he laughed, feeling energy flowing through his body.

Together, they sorted the recycling, doing squats each time they put something in the right bin. "Squat for the paper! Squat for the cans!" they sang.

Soon, the dining room sparkled clean. The air smelled fresh, and everything was organized. Leo felt wonderful - his body was warm and energized, and his heart felt happy knowing he had helped his family.

"Wow!" Leo exclaimed, looking around proudly. "Moving my body made me feel so much better! And our dining room looks beautiful!"

Mama Lion hugged both cubs. "You two are wonderful helpers. When we move our bodies and take care of our home, we feel good inside and out."

From that day on, Leo made sure to move his body every morning and help keep their home clean and healthy. He learned that movement wasn't just good for him - it helped his whole family too!

The End.
