# Rooftop Craft Time with Jump-Jump Joey

Jump-Jump Joey was a joyful kangaroo joey with big soft ears and a cozy little pouch. He lived in a tall building with a sunny, safe rooftop. Every morning, Joey had a happy routine. He would stretch his legs, wash his face, drink water, and take three deep belly breaths. In and out. In and out. In and out. Then he would bounce up the stairs to the rooftop. Boing, boing, boing!

Today, the rooftop smelled like warm sun and fresh soap. The sky was bright blue, and the wind felt cool on Joey’s cheeks. He wiggled his nose and smiled. “Today is craft day!” he sang. “Craft day on the roof!”

Joey’s friends were there too. Pip the pigeon fluffed his silver feathers. Mina the mouse squeaked hello from under a tiny hat. Tiko the turtle blinked slowly and gave a little wave. They all liked to make things together.

Joey opened a small craft box. Inside were safe, soft supplies. There were paper shapes, big crayons, chunky glue sticks, fuzzy pom-poms, and round stickers. The glue smelled sweet, like a little flower. The crayons felt warm in the sun.

“Let’s craft a Good-Morning Board,” Joey said. “It will help us remember our kind routines.”

“What is a routine?” Mina asked, tilting her tiny head.

“A routine is what we do every day to take care of ourselves,” said Tiko, slow and wise. “Like wash, drink, breathe, and rest.”

“And craft!” Pip added, puffing up. “Craft helps our busy brains feel calm.”

Joey clapped his paws. “We can craft and care at the same time. We can help each other, too.”

They sat in a cozy circle on a soft picnic blanket. Joey shared the supplies. “One for you, and one for me,” he said. “We take turns. We share.” The friends nodded.

Joey drew a big sun with a yellow crayon. “This sun means morning stretches,” he said.

Pip gently pressed blue stickers in a wavy line. “This is water for drinking.”

Mina glued a paper cloud. “This cloud means soft breathing. In and out.”

Tiko placed a heart shape. “This heart is for rest time and quiet cuddles.”

They were working well together, but Joey’s paws started to go too fast. He wanted to finish right away. He squeezed the glue too hard. Squish! The glue made a sticky puddle.

“Oh no,” Joey gasped. “It is too messy.”

Pip cooed kindly. “It’s okay, Joey. Let’s pause.”

Joey put a paw on his belly. He took a slow breath. In. Out. He felt the wind tickle his nose. He smelled the sunny rooftop. He heard faraway city sounds, beep and hum. His body felt calm again.

“Thank you, Pip,” Joey said softly. “I can slow down.”

Mina brought a little cloth. “We can clean together,” she said. “Wipe, wipe.” Joey and Mina dabbed the glue. Tiko held the paper still. Pip kept the pom-poms safe so they didn’t roll away. Working together made the mess turn into a neat, shiny spot.

Joey smiled again. “Cooperation makes big jobs small,” he said.

Now Joey remembered to use tiny glue dots. Tap, tap. He placed orange pom-poms for a cheerful border. Mina added a small picture of a toothbrush. Pip drew a glass of water with a happy face. Tiko wrote one simple word next to each picture: Stretch. Wash. Drink. Breathe. Rest. Craft. Share.

The wind danced across the rooftop and ruffled their fur and feathers. The blanket felt warm under their feet. The sun made their craft sparkle.

“Time for a small break,” Tiko said. “Self-care is part of crafting.”

Joey nodded. “Yes. Sip of water.” He took a drink and licked a cool drop from his whiskers. “Snack time?” he asked.

Mina passed a tiny bowl of apple slices. Crunch, crunch. Pip pecked at seeds. Tiko nibbled lettuce. Joey chewed slowly and felt his tummy say thank you.

After the break, they checked their Good-Morning Board. It looked bright and friendly. Joey tapped each picture and spoke clearly.

“Stretch,” he said, reaching his paws up to the sky.
“Wash,” he said, patting his face with a clean wipe.
“Drink,” he said, taking a little sip.
“Breathe,” he said, in and out.
“Rest,” he said, closing his eyes for a moment.
“Craft,” he said, holding a crayon.
“Share,” he said, handing a pom-pom to Pip.

Pip flapped with joy. “We made it together!”

Mina squeaked, “It shows how we care for our bodies and our hearts.”

Tiko smiled. “And how we help each other.”

Joey felt proud and peaceful. “When we craft with care, we feel calm,” he said. “When we share and take turns, we make friends. When we slow down and breathe, we can fix sticky spots.”

They hung the Good-Morning Board on a safe wall on the rooftop, where the sun could shine on it every day. Joey took three more deep breaths. In and out. In and out. In and out. His ears wiggled with happy tingles.

“Tomorrow,” Joey said, “we can craft a Good-Night Board!”

“Together,” said Pip.

“Kindly,” said Mina.

“Slowly,” said Tiko.

Joey bounced once, softly. “Together, kindly, slowly,” he echoed. “That is our rooftop way.”

The End.
