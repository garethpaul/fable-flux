# The Mouse Who Solved the Clicky Problem

In a cozy home office, a kind mouse named Mint lived behind a stack of soft notebooks. The office smelled like warm paper and fresh tea. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. The computer hummed, the keyboard clicked, and the big rolling chair squeaked, squeak, squeak.

Mint loved the quiet mornings. He listened to the gentle whirr of the fan and nibbled a tiny crumb of cracker. “Good morning, room,” Mint said softly. “Let’s have a peaceful day.”

Then, CLACK! CLACK! CLACK! The computer mouse on the desk began clicking loudly. Mint’s ears twitched. The clicking was sharp and fast. It made the air feel spiky.

“Ouch,” Mint whispered, covering his ears. “That sound is too much.” He peeked up at the desk. A gray computer mouse with shiny buttons sat by the keyboard. Next to it, a stack of paper wobbled. A pencil rolled and tapped, tap-tap, to the floor.

Mint climbed the long curtain string, paw over paw. He popped up on the desk. “Hello,” he said kindly. “I’m Mint. Can we talk?”

The gray mouse blinked its blue light. “I am Click. I help the human work,” it said in a tiny, buzzy voice. “Click-click-click!”

Mint took a calm breath. He remembered his three steps: stop, think, speak kindly. He stopped and put his paw on his chest. Think, he told himself. Speak kindly, he reminded his heart.

“Click,” Mint said, “your clicks are very loud. My ears feel tired. Can we find a gentle way?”

Click’s light flickered. “But I must click to do my job. The human says I am helpful.”

Mint nodded. “You are helpful. I like helpful. Maybe there is a different plan, a quieter plan. We can solve this together.”

Click was quiet for a second. The room hummed. The fan whispered whoosh-whoosh. “What is your plan?” Click asked.

Mint looked around. He saw a soft cloth by the tea mug. He saw a rubber band. He saw the wobbly stack of paper and the open window letting in a cool breeze. He felt an idea grow, warm and round, like a little sun.

“First,” Mint said, “we make a soft pad so your clicks are not so loud. Soft things help sound be gentle. That is kind to our ears. And if we use what we already have, that is good for the Earth. We don’t waste.”

Click blinked. “Softer is nice. No waste is nice.”

Mint pulled the soft cloth closer. It smelled like lemons. “May I fold this under you?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Click.

Mint folded the cloth into a neat square. He slid it under Click’s belly. Click tried a little click. click. The sound was smaller, like a tiny raindrop.

“Oh,” Mint said, smiling. “That’s better.”

Click wiggled. “Better,” it agreed. But soon, the papers tip-tapped in the breeze and made rustle-rustle sounds. The pencil rolled again. The office felt messy, and the messy sounds stirred up more clicks. Click got busy. CLACK! CLACK!

Mint’s whiskers drooped. He felt a little tight in his tummy. He stopped. He thought. He spoke kindly again.

“Click,” he said, “I feel worried when the papers move. It makes more noise. Can we tidy together? Then we can both feel calm.”

Click’s light brightened. “We can tidy.”

Mint used the rubber band to wrap the papers so they stayed still. “This helps,” he said. “We use what we have. That is smart and kind to the Earth.”

He placed the pencil in a cup so it could not roll. He pulled the window just a tiny bit, so the breeze was soft, not wild.

The room grew quiet and smooth. The fan sang a soft whoosh-whoosh. The keyboard made little tip-taps. Click tried again. click. click. Tiny, gentle sounds. Mint’s ears relaxed. His shoulders felt light.

“Thank you, Mint,” Click said. “You did not shout. You did not hide. You talked to me. You had a plan.”

Mint felt warm pride. “We did it together. We solved the clicky problem with calm words and smart steps.”

Footsteps sounded in the hall. The human came in, sat down, and smiled. “What a tidy desk,” the human said. The chair went squeak, but only once. The human worked. The room stayed peaceful.

Mint curled near the notebooks and listened. He heard the friendly hum of work. He smelled the lemon cloth. He felt safe.

Click whispered, “If I get too loud again, will you tell me kindly?”

“Yes,” said Mint. “I will stop, think, and speak kindly. And we will make a new plan if we need one.”

Click glowed. “I will try to be gentle, too.”

Mint nodded. “That’s teamwork.”

The sun made golden stripes on the desk. The home office felt like a calm nest. Mint took a tiny sip of tea steam in the air and smiled.

The End.
