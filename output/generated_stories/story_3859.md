# Mango's Gentle Plan

Mango the courageous monkey lived near a sunny backyard. The backyard smelled like fresh grass and sweet flowers. Birds chirped. The sun felt warm and cozy, like a soft hug.

One bright morning, Mango swung down the fence and landed on the green lawn with a happy “Whee!” He loved to explore. He loved to learn. He loved to help.

“Good morning, backyard!” Mango said, patting a smooth, sunny rock. “What can we discover today?”

Then he heard a tiny rustle. Scritch, scratch. Scritch, scratch.

Mango tilted his head and listened. “Hello? Who’s there?” he asked gently.

From behind the flower pot popped a small, shivery hedgehog. The hedgehog’s eyes were shiny with worry. One little foot was stuck in a tangled bit of old garden string.

“Ouch,” said the hedgehog in a whisper. “I can’t get free.”

Mango’s heart felt warm and big. “I will help,” he said softly. “My name is Mango. What’s your name?”

“I’m Prickle,” said the hedgehog, sniffing. “I’m scared. The string is tight.”

Mango took a small breath. He wanted to rush in. He wanted to pull the string fast. But he stopped and thought. Mango used his brave heart and his thinking brain.

“First, let’s be calm,” Mango said. He sat down slowly so he would not scare Prickle. The grass tickled his toes. He listened to the birds. He felt the warm sun. “Can we try together?”

Prickle nodded, quivering just a little. “Okay.”

Mango looked closely. He used his eyes like little helpers. He saw the string made a loop. He saw it was wrapped around a little stick. He saw a tiny knot.

“I have a plan,” Mango said. “Step one: move the stick. Step two: loosen the loop. Step three: free the foot.” He held up three fingers and smiled.

“That sounds smart,” said Prickle, a tiny smile peeking out.

“Thank you,” said Mango. “But we will also be gentle. We will be kind.”

Mango spoke softly. “Prickle, can you lift your foot just a bit? Only a teeny bit?”

Prickle lifted, just a teeny bit.

Mango touched the stick with careful fingers. He felt the rough wood and the scratchy string. He wiggled the stick. Wiggle, wiggle. The knot loosened a little.

“You’re doing great,” Mango said. “You are brave.”

Prickle took a slow breath. “You are kind.”

Mango looked at the loop again. “Now the loop,” he said. He did not yank. He did not pull hard. He slipped one finger under and lifted the loop like lifting a tiny bracelet. The loop got bigger.

“Almost there,” said Mango.

Prickle’s foot came free with a soft pop. “I’m free!” Prickle squeaked. He did a happy little wiggle. The grass felt cool and soft under his toes.

Mango clapped. “We did it, together!”

Prickle looked shy. “Thank you. You were careful. You did not scare me. You used a plan.”

Mango nodded. “I used my eyes, my brain, and my heart.”

They both smiled. Then they heard a faint cheep, cheep, cheep from near the garden gate. It sounded like tiny bells.

“What is that?” Mango asked.

“Let’s discover,” said Prickle, feeling braver now.

They walked toward the sound. The sun warmed their backs. A gentle breeze smelled like lemons and leaves. At the gate they found a little bird, a baby sparrow, hopping and peeping. One wing was tucked funny under a loose, floppy leaf.

“Hello, little one,” Mango said softly. “Are you stuck too?”

Cheep, said the baby.

Mango crouched low. He remembered his gentle plan. “Step one: look closely. Step two: make a safe space. Step three: help slowly.”

He looked. The leaf was bendy, not tight. The wing was fine, just pinned. Mango used two fingers and lifted the leaf, like lifting a blanket. He moved slowly so the baby bird wouldn’t be scared.

“You’re safe,” whispered Prickle.

The baby bird shook its feathers. Shiver, shiver. Then it hopped to the open gate where a bigger sparrow waited. The big sparrow chirped, and together they flew up, up, up, their wings whispering whoosh in the sunny air.

Mango and Prickle smiled so wide their faces felt warm. “We helped again,” said Prickle.

Mango nodded. “We were careful, and we were kind.”

They sat on the soft grass and shared a snack: a sweet banana for Mango and a crunchy apple slice for Prickle. They listened to the birds sing. They watched the clouds float like puffy sheep in the big blue sky.

Prickle said, “Today I learned something.”

“What did you learn?” asked Mango.

“I learned that courage is not just being fast,” Prickle said. “Courage is being gentle and kind. And making a plan.”

Mango clapped softly. “Yes. Compassion means caring about how others feel. Logical thinking means using our eyes and our brains to make a plan. Discovery means we look, we listen, and we learn.”

Prickle nodded. “And friends help together.”

Mango grinned. “Friends help together.”

The sun felt warm. The backyard felt safe. Mango felt proud, not just for being brave, but for being gentle too.

And if you listen closely in a sunny backyard, you might hear Mango’s soft voice say, “Let’s look, let’s think, and let’s be kind.”

The End.
