# Polly's Colorful Laundry Day

Polly the parrot loved bright colors more than anything in the whole world. Her feathers were red, blue, green, and yellow - just like a beautiful rainbow! Today, she was helping Mama wash clothes in the warm, cozy laundry room.

"Squawk! Look at all these pretty colors!" chirped Polly as she watched the washing machine spin around and around. The soapy water made swirling patterns that danced before her bright black eyes.

Mama smiled. "Yes, Polly! We have clothes from all our friends around the world. See this red sari from India? And this blue kimono from Japan?"

Polly flapped her wings excitedly. "Oh! And look at this bright yellow shirt from Mexico! It has beautiful flowers on it!"

As Mama sorted the clothes into different piles, Polly noticed something wonderful. "Mama, the colors look like my feathers! Can I help organize them?"

"Of course, sweet Polly! But remember, we must be safe. Stay away from the hot dryer and the electrical cords," Mama said gently.

Polly nodded seriously. "Safety first! I promise!"

Together, they sorted the clothes by colors. Red shirts went in one basket, blue jeans in another, and white socks in a third. Polly used her clever beak to pick up small items like colorful hair ribbons and pretty scarves.

"This is like making art!" Polly exclaimed. "Each pile is a different color painting!"

Suddenly, Polly had a wonderful idea. "Mama! Can we make something beautiful with the extra fabric scraps?"

Mama's eyes twinkled. "What a creative idea, Polly! What would you like to make?"

Polly thought hard, tilting her colorful head. "A friendship quilt! We can use pieces from all our friends' clothes to remember how special they are!"

"That's brilliant!" said Mama. "Creative expression makes everything more beautiful."

They gathered soft fabric scraps - a piece of purple silk from Thailand, orange cotton from Morocco, and green wool from Ireland. Polly carefully arranged them into a rainbow pattern on the folding table.

"Look, Mama! It tells the story of all our friends!" Polly sang happily. "The purple reminds me of Priya's dance costume, and the orange looks like Omar's favorite shirt!"

Mama helped Polly pin the pieces together safely, keeping the sharp pins away from her feathers. "You're so good at organizing colors, Polly. And your creative ideas make our laundry day special!"

As they worked, Polly sang a little song: "Red and blue and green so bright, yellow, purple, orange light! Colors from around the world, make our friendship quilt unfurled!"

When they finished, their small quilt square sparkled with all the colors of the rainbow. It looked just like Polly's beautiful feathers!

"We can add more squares each time we do laundry," said Mama. "Our quilt will grow bigger and more beautiful!"

Polly danced on her perch, spreading her colorful wings wide. "I love being creative! It makes ordinary things like laundry day feel magical!"

From that day on, Polly looked forward to laundry day. She learned that creativity could turn any ordinary task into something wonderful, and that organizing things safely made room for even more beautiful creations.

The End.
