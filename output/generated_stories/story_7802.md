# Benny Beaver's Big Mix-Up

Benny Beaver loved helping in the laundry room. His big front teeth were perfect for carrying laundry baskets, and his flat tail made a great sorting mat. Every day, he helped wash clothes for all the forest animals.

"Swoosh, swoosh!" went the washing machine as Benny carefully separated the whites from the colors. The warm, soapy smell filled the cozy laundry room, and Benny hummed happily as he worked.

One busy Tuesday morning, Benny was folding a big pile of clean clothes when he noticed something wrong. Mrs. Rabbit's beautiful red sweater was mixed in with Mr. Bear's brown shirts!

"Oh no!" Benny said, holding up the sweater. "This doesn't belong here." He looked closer and saw the tiny tag that read "Mrs. Rabbit" in neat letters.

Benny's friend Chippy Chipmunk scurried in, carrying nuts in his cheeks. "Just put it with the brown clothes," Chippy mumbled through his full mouth. "Nobody will notice!"

But Benny shook his furry head. "That wouldn't be right, Chippy. This sweater belongs to Mrs. Rabbit. She worked hard to buy it with her carrot money."

"But it's just one little sweater," Chippy squeaked. "And you're so busy today!"

Benny looked at the tall piles of laundry around him. The washing machines were rumbling, "Whoosh-whoosh-whoosh," and there were so many clothes to fold. It would be easier to just ignore the mix-up.

But deep in his heart, Benny knew what was right. "Mrs. Rabbit trusted me with her special sweater," he said firmly. "I need to make sure she gets it back."

Benny's resourceful beaver brain started working. "I have an idea!" he announced. He used his strong teeth to carefully carry the red sweater, and his webbed feet helped him walk quickly through the forest.

When Benny knocked on Mrs. Rabbit's burrow, she opened the door with a worried face.

"Oh, Benny!" she cried. "I was just about to come see you. My favorite red sweater is missing from my laundry!"

Benny's eyes sparkled as he held up the soft, clean sweater. "Here it is, Mrs. Rabbit! It got mixed up with Mr. Bear's clothes by mistake."

Mrs. Rabbit's ears perked up with joy. "You brought it all the way here? Even though you're so busy?" She hugged the sweater tight. "Thank you for being so honest and caring!"

"It was the right thing to do," Benny said proudly. "Your sweater belongs with you."

Mrs. Rabbit smiled warmly. "You know what? Let me help you with the laundry today. Friends should help friends who do the right thing!"

Together, they walked back to the laundry room. Mrs. Rabbit helped Benny sort clothes and fold towels. The work went much faster with a friend, and they sang songs while the machines hummed their washing tune.

"Thank you for teaching me about being fair," Chippy said when he saw them working together. "Next time, I'll help return things to their right owners too!"

As the sun set, Benny looked around the neat, organized laundry room. Every piece of clothing was clean, folded, and ready to go home to its proper owner.

"Being honest and fair feels even better than getting the work done fast," Benny said to himself, patting his tail with satisfaction.

From that day on, whenever there was a mix-up in the laundry room, all the forest animals knew they could count on Benny Beaver to make things right. And they all learned that doing the fair thing, even when it's harder, always leads to the happiest endings.

The End.
