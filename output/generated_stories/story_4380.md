# Kora Koala and the Fair Share Paints

Kora was a calm koala with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. She loved the creative art room at school. The room smelled like fresh paper and lemon soap. The tables were covered with colorful mats. Shelves held glitter, brushes, and jars of paint. Red, blue, yellow, and green shined like little rainbows.

Today was Art Day. Ms. Sunny, their teacher, clapped her hands. “We will paint a big mural together!” she said. “Everyone will help. We will share the colors. We will take turns. We will be fair.”

Kora’s heart felt warm. “I love sharing,” she whispered.

Her friends gathered at the long paper. Milo Mouse wiggled his whiskers. Tia Turtle tapped her tiny feet. Benny Bear bounced and bumped the table, making the brushes jiggle.

Ms. Sunny placed the paint jars in the center. “Remember,” she said, smiling, “fair means everyone gets a turn.”

Benny Bear grabbed the red and blue. “I’m making a giant rocket!” he roared with excitement. He dipped both brushes at once. Splish, splash! Red dots and blue swirls covered his corner.

Milo reached out. “Can I have blue, please?” he asked.

Benny hugged the jars. “Not yet. I need it,” he said, not looking up.

Tia Turtle stretched her neck. “May I have a turn with red?” she asked softly.

Benny shook his head. “Later.”

Kora watched. Her tummy felt tight. She wanted green to paint leaves on a tall tree, but Benny had covered the green jar with his big paw. The room filled with paint smells and soft music, but Kora’s ears heard little sighs from her friends.

“This does not feel fair,” Kora thought. She was calm, but she felt brave inside. She knew everyone needed a turn. She took a slow breath. In… and out… The way eucalyptus leaves smelled in her mind helped her feel steady.

Kora tried to solve the problem by herself first. “Maybe I can wait,” she thought. She waited one minute. Two minutes. Three minutes. She drew a tiny tree with a pencil while she waited. She was patient, but her friends were still waiting too. Benny kept painting with both colors.

Kora decided to be brave and speak up. She walked closer to Benny. “Benny,” she said in a clear, kind voice, “we all need the paints. We need to be fair. Can we take turns?”

Benny looked surprised. “But my rocket needs blue and red,” he said. His ears drooped a little. “I’m excited.”

“I can see that,” Kora said, nodding. “Your rocket looks fast! We can make a plan so everyone can paint. That’s fair.”

Milo and Tia moved closer, listening. The brushes on the table made tiny clicking sounds. The paint jars glug-glugged when Ms. Sunny gently stirred them.

“What kind of plan?” Benny asked.

Kora thought hard. She was persistent. She did not give up. “We can use a turn timer,” she said. “Ten slow counts each. We pass the color to the next friend. We can also mix colors on a shared plate. See? Blue and yellow make green! We can all try.”

Milo squeaked, “I can count! One… two…”

Tia smiled. “And I can pass the jar when it is time.”

Benny shifted his paws. He looked at his rocket, then at his friends’ empty spots. “I don’t want to be unfair,” he said quietly. “Okay. Let’s try the plan.”

Kora’s heart felt light. “Thank you, Benny,” she said.

They put a clean plate in the middle. Kora poured a tiny splash of blue. Milo added yellow. Swirl, swirl. A soft green appeared like a spring leaf. Everyone smiled.

Milo counted in a happy voice. “One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine, ten!” He passed the blue to Tia. Tia painted a shiny shell. Then she passed it to Kora. Kora dipped her brush and painted smooth green leaves. Swoosh, swoosh. The paper smelled like wet rain on the forest.

Benny waited his turn and bounced a little. When he got the blue again, he added stars around his rocket. “Wow,” he said. “Sharing helps my rocket look even better!”

Ms. Sunny came by and nodded. “I see fairness,” she said. “I see bravery in using your voice. I see persistence in finding a plan that works.”

Kora’s cheeks felt warm and happy. “We did it together,” she said.

The mural grew and grew. Tia’s shiny shell, Milo’s tiny moon, Benny’s big rocket, and Kora’s tall tree with leaves that seemed to whisper. The colors looked bright and balanced. Everyone had a turn. Everyone’s work mattered.

When they were done, they stood back and admired the mural. The room was quiet for a moment, like a soft hug. Then Benny turned to Kora. “Thank you for speaking up,” he said. “I didn’t know I was being unfair.”

Kora smiled. “Fair means we share and take turns,” she said. “Fair means we listen. Fair feels good for everyone.”

Milo nodded. “And brave talking can be kind.”

Tia added, “And trying again and again helps us find a way.”

Ms. Sunny taped the mural on the wall. “Our art shows fairness,” she said. “This is what teamwork looks like.”

Kora held her brush and took one more deep breath. She felt calm, brave, and proud. The art room smelled sweet and clean. The colors on the wall glowed.

The lesson was clear in Kora’s heart: Be fair. Be brave. Keep trying. Speak up kindly. Everyone shines when we share.

The End.
