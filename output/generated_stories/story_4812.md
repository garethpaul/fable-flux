# Oliver's Healthy Rooftop Day

High up on a tall red rooftop sat Oliver the Owl. His soft brown feathers sparkled in the morning sunshine. Oliver was very wise and loved to help others learn important things.

"Hoot, hoot!" Oliver called out cheerfully. "What a beautiful day to take care of myself!"

Oliver stretched his big wings wide. He felt the cool breeze tickle his feathers. Then he noticed his friend Pip the Sparrow flying by, looking very tired.

"Hello, Pip!" Oliver hooted. "You look sleepy. Are you okay?"

Pip landed on the rooftop with a small thud. "Oh, Oliver! I'm so tired. I forgot to eat breakfast, and I stayed up too late playing."

Oliver's wise yellow eyes twinkled kindly. "That sounds like you need to learn about taking care of yourself, just like I do every day!"

"Taking care of myself?" Pip chirped curiously. "What does that mean?"

Oliver smiled and fluffed his feathers. "Well, first I always eat healthy foods. Watch this!" 

Oliver flew down and brought back some crunchy seeds and fresh berries. The berries were bright red and smelled sweet. "These give my body energy and keep me strong," he explained.

Pip's eyes grew wide. "They look delicious!"

"Try some," Oliver said warmly. Pip ate the healthy food and immediately felt better. Her little wings stopped drooping.

"Now," Oliver continued, "I always make sure to rest when I'm tired." He showed Pip a cozy spot on the rooftop where soft moss grew. "Sleep helps our bodies grow and stay healthy."

Oliver then stretched his wings again, reaching them high toward the fluffy white clouds. "And I exercise every day! Moving our bodies makes us feel happy and strong."

"This feels wonderful!" Pip said, copying Oliver's stretches. She could feel her muscles waking up.

"One more important thing," Oliver hooted gently. "I take time to be calm and quiet. Listen..."

They both sat very still. They could hear the gentle wind whooshing through the trees. Birds sang sweet songs in the distance. The warm sun felt cozy on their feathers.

"I feel so peaceful," Pip whispered.

"When we take care of our bodies and minds," Oliver explained, "we feel happy and ready for anything! This is called self-care."

Pip flapped her wings excitedly. "I want to take care of myself too! Will you teach me more?"

"Of course!" Oliver hooted happily. "Every day, remember to eat good food, get enough sleep, move your body, and take quiet time. Your body and mind will thank you!"

From that day on, Pip visited Oliver every morning. Together, they ate healthy breakfasts, did stretches on the rooftop, and enjoyed peaceful moments watching the sunrise.

"Thank you for teaching me, Oliver," Pip chirped. "I feel so much better when I take care of myself!"

Oliver's chest puffed up with pride. "Taking care of yourself is the best gift you can give to your body and mind. When you feel good, you can help others feel good too!"

As the sun climbed higher in the bright blue sky, Oliver and Pip felt strong, happy, and ready for their day.

The End.
