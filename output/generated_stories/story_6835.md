# Bouncy Bubbles in the Laundry Room

The friendly bear cub, named Benny, padded into the laundry room on soft paws. The floor felt cool on his feet. “Ooh!” he said, wiggling his toes. The room smelled like clean soap and warm cotton. The washing machine buzzed and hummed like a busy bee. The dryer whooshed like a soft wind.

Benny’s mama called from the doorway, “Laundry time, Benny! Let’s move our bodies and help with chores.”

Benny’s ears perked up. He loved to move. “I can help!” he cheered. He clapped his paws, and they made a happy, gentle smack.

On a low shelf, there were little baskets with colorful socks. The socks had stripes, spots, stars, and rainbows. Some were tiny, some were long, and some were fuzzy. Benny whispered, “So many socks! So many friends.”

From the hallway came Benny’s friends who were visiting: Mia the mouse with tiny quick feet, Raj the rabbit with bouncy legs, and Poppy the parrot with bright green wings. “We heard chores!” Poppy squawked. “We love to move!”

Benny smiled. “Let’s do a sock dance and sort! We can move our bodies and use our smart brains.”

Mama Bear nodded. “Great idea. First, we move. Then, we match.”

The washing machine beeped. Beep-beep! The round door opened with a warm puff of steam. It smelled like lemons and sunshine. “Whoa,” said Raj, scrunching his nose, “it smells cozy!”

“Step one,” said Benny, holding up one paw, “pull and place.” He pulled out a small towel. Drip! “We shake, we stretch, we place.” He stood tall and shook the towel like a flag. “Shake, stretch, place!”

Mia giggled and copied. “Shake, stretch, place!” She moved her tiny arms wide, then set the towel in a basket.

Poppy flapped gently. “Shake, stretch, place!” The towel made a soft flap-flap sound as it landed.

Benny felt his heart beating fast in a happy way. Moving made him feel strong and calm. “Now socks!” he said. “We hop to the socks.”

They all hopped to the baskets. Hop, hop, hop! Benny’s paws thumped. Raj’s feet thumped faster. Mia made little tip-tip hops. Poppy did tiny hops, wings tucked in.

Benny picked up a rainbow sock. He held it to his cheek. “It’s soft,” he said, smiling. “Let’s find its friend.”

Raj grabbed a striped sock. “Does this match?” he asked.

Benny looked closely. “Hmm. We can use our eyes and think. This one has blue, green, yellow. Yours has red stripes. Not a match. Let’s keep looking.”

Mia found a sock with stars. “This one is small like me,” she said.

Poppy chirped, “Let’s sort by size first! Big pile, medium pile, small pile. Then we match patterns.”

Benny blinked. “Good plan, Poppy! That’s logical thinking.” He felt proud of his friends. “We can all help in our own ways.”

They began to move with a rhythm. Benny stretched high to reach the top of the basket. Stretch! Raj bounced to the side. Bounce! Mia tiptoed around the pile. Tip-tip! Poppy made little side steps, wings tucked. Step-step!

“Big, medium, small,” Benny chanted. “Big, medium, small.” He placed socks gently in piles. He used his eyes to notice shapes and colors. “This star sock is small. This fuzzy sock is big.”

They matched two rainbow socks. “A pair!” cheered Raj. They matched two tiny star socks. “A pair!” sang Mia. They matched two fuzzy socks. “A pair!” squawked Poppy. Each time they made a pair, they did a little move: clap, stomp, wiggle!

Clap, stomp, wiggle! The laundry room filled with giggles and moving feet.

Benny found one polka-dot sock and one plain white sock. He frowned a little. “These do not match. That’s okay,” he said, taking a deep breath. “We can be open-minded. Maybe they can be buddies for now.”

Mama Bear smiled. “Sometimes we don’t find a perfect pair right away. We do our best and keep looking later.”

Benny nodded. “Let’s make a ‘lonely sock’ basket.” He set the two socks in a small basket and patted them. “You will find your friends.”

Soon the room looked tidy. Towels were stacked. Socks were matched or waiting. The dryer sang a soft ding, like a bell. Warm air whooshed. Benny felt a glow in his chest. He had moved his body, used his brain, and helped his family.

“Thank you,” Mama Bear said. “You all moved in different ways, and you all helped. Different bodies, different moves, same teamwork!”

Benny’s tail—just a little nub—wiggled. “I like that. Mia tiptoes. Raj bounces. Poppy flaps. I stretch. We can learn from each other.”

Mia smiled. “Moving feels good.”

Raj nodded. “Moving helps me think.”

Poppy chirped, “Moving makes chores fun!”

Benny took a deep breath. The room smelled like clean cotton. He listened to the soft hum. He felt the cool floor and the warm towels. “I learned something,” he said. “When we move our bodies and use smart steps—shake, stretch, place; big, medium, small—we can do chores and have fun. And it’s good to try new ways and be kind to all our friends.”

Mama Bear gave Benny a soft, warm hug. “That is a wonderful lesson, my cub.”

Benny hugged back. “Next time,” he said, “we can make a sock parade!”

“Parade!” everyone cheered. They marched out of the laundry room—step, hop, tip, flap—smiling, tired in a good way, and proud.

The End.
