# Rascal's Sorry Song

Rascal the raccoon loved making music more than anything in the whole world. His fluffy striped tail would wiggle with joy whenever he heard pretty sounds. Today, he bounced into the bright music practice room, his little paws clapping together.

"Time to practice my drums!" Rascal said happily, looking at the shiny drum set in the corner.

The practice room smelled like wood polish and had colorful music notes painted on the sunny yellow walls. Rascal climbed onto the drum stool and picked up his drumsticks. He was so excited that he started playing right away.

BANG! CRASH! BOOM! The drums were so loud and fun!

But Rascal didn't notice that his friend Bella the bunny was practicing her quiet flute song in the corner. The loud drumming made it impossible for her to hear her own music.

"Oh no," whispered Bella sadly, putting down her silver flute. Her ears drooped down, and her eyes filled with tears.

Rascal kept drumming louder and louder, lost in his own rhythm. BANG! CRASH! BOOM!

Finally, Bella's tears started to fall. "I can't practice my song for the school concert," she sniffled quietly.

When Rascal heard the soft crying sound, he stopped drumming immediately. His ears perked up, and he looked around the room. That's when he saw Bella sitting in the corner, wiping her furry cheeks with her paws.

"Bella? Why are you crying?" Rascal asked, climbing down from his drum stool. His heart felt heavy when he saw his friend so sad.

"I was trying to practice my gentle flute song," Bella explained in a wobbly voice. "But your drums were so loud that I couldn't hear my music at all. Now I won't be ready for tomorrow's concert."

Rascal's face fell. He looked at his drumsticks, then at Bella's sad face. His tummy felt funny and twisted. He realized he had been thinking only about his own fun and hadn't noticed his friend needed quiet time to practice too.

"Oh, Bella," Rascal said softly, walking over to her. "I'm so, so sorry. I didn't mean to make you sad. I was having so much fun drumming that I forgot to look around and see that you were practicing too."

Bella looked up at Rascal with her big, gentle eyes.

"I should have asked if it was okay to play my loud drums," Rascal continued. "That wasn't very kind of me. Will you forgive me?"

Bella's ears lifted up a little bit. "Thank you for saying sorry, Rascal. That makes me feel better."

"How can I help you practice your song?" Rascal asked, his tail wiggling with hope.

Bella smiled for the first time that day. "Maybe you could play very, very quietly while I practice? Like soft raindrops instead of loud thunder?"

"Yes!" Rascal said excitedly. "I can make gentle music too!"

So Rascal went back to his drums and played the softest, most careful beats - tip-tap-tip-tap - like little raindrops on leaves. Bella played her sweet flute melody, and together their music sounded beautiful and peaceful.

"This sounds wonderful!" Bella said, her eyes sparkling with joy.

"It does!" agreed Rascal. "And it feels good in my heart when we make music together and everyone is happy."

From that day on, Rascal always remembered to look around and think about others before making loud sounds. He learned that saying sorry when you hurt someone's feelings, even by accident, helps everyone feel better and become better friends.

The End.
