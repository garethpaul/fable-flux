# Ellie's Fair Garden Day

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved spending time in Mama's beautiful balcony garden. The colorful flowers smelled so sweet, and the green leaves felt cool under her tiny trunk. Today, Mama had given Ellie and her friend Benny Bunny a very special job.

"I need you both to water all the plants," Mama said, handing them each a small watering can. "Every single plant needs water to grow big and strong. Can you work together fairly?"

"Yes, Mama!" Ellie trumpeted happily. Her little trunk wiggled with excitement.

Benny hopped up and down. "We'll do our best!"

The balcony garden was full of wonderful plants. There were red tomatoes, yellow sunflowers, purple petunias, and green herbs that smelled like pizza. Ellie counted carefully with her trunk. "One, two, three... twelve plants total!"

"Let's each water six plants," Ellie suggested. "That would be fair, don't you think?"

But Benny had already started hopping toward the prettiest flowers. "I want to water the sunflowers and the roses! They're the most beautiful!"

Ellie felt her tummy get a little tight. The sunflowers and roses were her favorites too, but she knew that wouldn't be fair. "Benny, wait," she said gently. "If you take all the pretty flowers, what will I water?"

Benny stopped hopping and looked around. All that was left were the plain green herb plants and some small seedlings. "Oh," he said quietly. "I guess you'd get the boring ones."

"That doesn't feel fair to me," Ellie said, using her kind voice. "And Mama said every plant is important. The herbs make our food taste yummy, and the little seedlings will grow into big, beautiful plants too!"

Benny's ears drooped a little. "You're right, Ellie. I wasn't being fair. I just got so excited about the pretty flowers."

Ellie smiled and gently touched Benny's paw with her trunk. "I understand! They are really beautiful. But what if we take turns? You can water one pretty plant, then I'll water one, then you water another. That way we both get to care for all different kinds!"

"That's a great idea!" Benny's ears perked up. "And we can help each other finish the job properly."

So that's exactly what they did. Benny watered the first sunflower while Ellie watered the tomatoes. Then Ellie got to water the roses while Benny took care of the herbs. They took turns, one by one, making sure each plant got just the right amount of water.

"Smell this basil!" Ellie giggled, sniffing the herbs with her trunk. "It smells like Mama's spaghetti!"

"And look how the water makes the soil all dark and rich!" Benny observed, watching the water soak into the dirt.

When they finished, every single plant was watered. The leaves sparkled with water drops like tiny diamonds in the sunshine.

Mama came out to check their work. "Wonderful job, you two! Every plant looks happy and healthy. And I noticed you shared the work fairly."

"It felt good to be fair," Ellie said, swinging her trunk proudily. "Every plant got what it needed."

"And we both got to water beautiful flowers AND important plants," Benny added. "Being fair made the job more fun!"

Mama hugged them both. "When we're fair and work together, everyone feels happy. Just like our garden - every plant is special and important."

As the sun warmed their faces, Ellie and Benny sat together, watching their freshly watered garden sparkle in the light.

The End.
