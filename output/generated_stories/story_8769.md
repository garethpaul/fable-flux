# Bubbles for Everyone

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with big gentle eyes and a long, long neck. One sunny morning, she tip-hooved into the bright, clean bathroom. The tiles felt cool on her hooves. Drip, drip, went the faucet. The mirror sparkled like a shiny puddle.

Gigi hummed a small song. “Wash, wash, wash,” she sang. She loved the warm, soapy bubbles that smelled like oranges. She reached for the little soap pump with her long neck and pressed it. Squish! A sweet orange scent filled the air. “Mmm,” said Gigi, “that smells like a happy day.”

Her friends were in the bathroom too. Pip the penguin waddled on the mat, flapping his little wings. “I like cold water,” said Pip, shivering with joy. “Splish-splash is my favorite!”

Bella the bunny twitched her nose. “I like warm water,” she said softly. “It feels like a cozy blanket.”

Tiko the turtle peeked from his shell. “I like slow water,” he whispered. “Slow, slow, slow.”

Gigi smiled. “We are different, and that is special,” she said. “We can share the bathroom and be kind.”

But then a little problem began. Pip turned the tap to cold. “Brrr! Perfect for me!” Cold water rushed out with a hiss. Bella hopped back. “Oh no! My paws get chilly!” She turned the tap to warm. The water changed to steamy and soft. Pip shivered and wobbled. “Too warm!”

Tiko blinked. “It is too fast,” he murmured. “I can’t keep up.”

The faucet splashed, and suds hopped like tiny white clouds. Pip waddled, Bella hopped, Tiko scooted, and Gigi stood, tall and puzzled. The bathroom was full of sounds—drip, hiss, splish, and plip-plop—like a little music show. But everyone looked worried.

Gigi took a deep breath. She was a thoughtful giraffe. “Friends,” she said in her calm voice, “let’s use peaceful words. We can talk and take turns.” She bent her long neck so her face was close to each friend. “Tell me what you need.”

Pip said, “I need cold water for a bit. It makes me feel awake!”

Bella said, “I need warm water for my paws. Warm feels safe.”

Tiko said, “I need slow water. Slow helps me not slip.”

Gigi nodded. “Thank you for telling me. Our differences are wonderful. Let’s make a plan.”

She found three little cups on the shelf—blue, yellow, and green. She placed them in a line on the counter. “We can share,” she said. “And we can take turns.” Her tail swished, happy with the idea.

First, Gigi turned the tap to a slow, gentle trickle. “Tiko first,” she smiled. “We will count to ten.” The water went drip, drip, drip. Tiko washed his tiny feet. “One, two, three…” Gigi counted in a soft voice. “Ten!” Tiko beamed. “Thank you. That was just right.”

Next, she turned the tap to cold for Pip. “Chilly time!” Pip giggled and dipped his wings. “Ahh! Splish!” Gigi counted again. “One, two, three…” Pip waddled in circles, laughing. “Ten!” Pip clapped. “Thank you. That woke me up!”

Then Gigi turned the tap to warm for Bella. “Cozy time,” Gigi said. Bella sighed happily and washed her paws. “So soft,” she whispered. “One, two, three… Ten!” Bella bounced. “Thank you. I feel calm.”

Gigi washed last. She mixed warm and a little cool. “Just right for me,” she said, pumping orange soap—squish! The bathroom smelled like fruit and joy. She massaged the foam into her spots. The bubbles made tiny pops, pop-pop-pop, like quiet fireworks. She smiled. “We are different, and that is okay.”

When washing was done, Gigi had another thoughtful idea. “Let’s have a snack,” she said. “Good food helps our bodies.” She opened a small basket by the towels. Inside were crunchy carrots, leafy greens, and cool cucumber slices.

Gigi shared with generosity. “Bella, carrots for you?” Bella nibbled. “Crunch-crunch! Thank you!”

“Pip, cool cucumber?” Pip pecked happily. “Munch-munch! So fresh!”

“Tiko, leafy greens?” Tiko chewed slowly. “Nibble-nibble. Perfect.”

Gigi took some leafy greens too. “Greens help us grow strong,” she said. “And water helps us feel clean and healthy.” The friends ate together, tails and wings and ears wiggling with joy. The bathroom felt cozy and bright. The mirror showed four happy faces—each one different, each one smiling.

Pip waddled close to Gigi. “I like cold,” he said, “and you like warm-and-cool. We are different!”

Bella twitched her nose. “We can still be friends,” she said. “We can share and take turns.”

Tiko nodded slowly. “Peaceful talking helps,” he whispered.

Gigi’s eyes sparkled. “Yes,” she said. “When we listen, we can find a plan that works for everyone.”

They put the cups back on the shelf and folded the towels neatly. The faucet made one last drip. The bathroom was quiet again, smelling of orange and fresh water.

Gigi gave a gentle giraffe hug, as much as her long neck allowed. “We celebrate our differences,” she said. “We are kind. We share. We eat good foods. And we talk with peaceful words.”

All the friends cheered, “Hooray for us!”

The End.
