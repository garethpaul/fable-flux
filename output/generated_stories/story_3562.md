# Nutkin's Thank You Plan

High up in the big oak tree, Nutkin the squirrel lived in the coziest treehouse. It had tiny windows that sparkled in the sunlight and a little red door that creaked when it opened. Nutkin loved his treehouse, but today he had a big problem.

"Oh no!" squeaked Nutkin, looking at his empty acorn cupboard. Winter was coming soon, and he had no food stored away. His fluffy tail drooped with worry.

Just then, he heard cheerful voices below. Nutkin peeked out his window and saw his forest friends working together. Benny the beaver was chopping wood with his strong teeth. "Chop, chop, chop!" went the sound. Ruby the rabbit was gathering soft moss for her burrow. Ollie the owl was collecting shiny pebbles for his nest.

"They're all so busy helping themselves get ready for winter," thought Nutkin. "But I've been too busy playing to prepare!"

Nutkin's whiskers twitched as he had an idea. "Wait! My friends have been so kind to me all summer. Maybe I can help them first, and then ask for their help too!"

He scampered down the tree trunk, his tiny claws clicking on the bark. "Good morning, everyone!" called Nutkin with a big smile.

"Hi, Nutkin!" chirped Ruby, her pink nose wiggling. "What are you up to today?"

"I want to help you all!" said Nutkin. "Benny, your wood pile looks heavy. Let me help you carry it!"

Benny's eyes lit up. "That's so thoughtful, Nutkin! Thank you!"

Together, they moved the wood to Benny's dam. The logs felt rough and smelled like fresh forest. Next, Nutkin helped Ruby gather the softest, fluffiest moss he could find. It felt like tiny pillows in his paws.

"This moss is perfect!" said Ruby, giving Nutkin a grateful hug. "You're such a good friend!"

Then Nutkin helped Ollie organize his shiny pebbles by size. They clinked and sparkled like tiny stars.

"You have such a good eye for organizing," hooted Ollie wisely. "Thank you for your help!"

When all the work was done, Nutkin's friends noticed he looked a little sad.

"What's wrong, Nutkin?" asked Benny kindly.

Nutkin's cheeks turned pink. "Well, I was having so much fun this summer that I forgot to gather acorns for winter. I don't know what I'll eat when the snow comes."

"Oh, Nutkin!" said Ruby. "You helped us so much today!"

"And you've been such a wonderful friend all summer," added Ollie.

"We want to help you too!" said Benny. "Let's all work together to fill your treehouse with acorns!"

The friends spent the whole afternoon gathering acorns. They found big ones and little ones, smooth ones and bumpy ones. Soon, Nutkin's cupboard was overflowing with enough food for the whole winter.

"I can't believe how full my cupboard is!" said Nutkin, his eyes sparkling with happy tears. "Thank you all so much!"

"That's what friends do," said Ruby with a warm smile.

As the sun set, painting the sky orange and pink, Nutkin invited everyone up to his treehouse for a celebration dinner. They shared acorn soup and told funny stories. The treehouse filled with laughter that sounded like tinkling bells.

"I learned something important today," said Nutkin. "When we help others and say thank you, good things come back to us. Being grateful makes everything better!"

His friends all nodded and smiled. From that day on, Nutkin always remembered to help others first and to say thank you for all the wonderful things in his life.

The End.
