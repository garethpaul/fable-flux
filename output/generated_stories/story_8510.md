# Milo the Monkey's Rooftop Adventure

Milo was a brave little monkey with soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived on a tall building's rooftop with his family. The rooftop was wide and sunny, with flower pots and a small garden where vegetables grew.

Every morning, Milo loved to swing from the clotheslines and play with his sister Maya. But today felt different. Maya was sitting alone by the flower pots, looking sad.

"What's wrong, Maya?" Milo asked gently, sitting beside her on the warm concrete.

Maya's tail drooped low. "The big pigeons took our favorite sunny spot," she whispered. "They won't let us play there anymore. They flap their wings and make loud noises when we come close."

Milo felt his heart beat faster. He wanted to help his sister, but the pigeons were much bigger than them. The gray birds cooed loudly from their corner of the rooftop, near the best climbing pole.

"We could ask them nicely to share," Milo suggested, though his voice shook a little.

Maya shook her head. "They look too scary. Their beaks are so big!"

Milo took a deep breath. The morning air smelled fresh and clean. He could hear the city sounds below - cars honking and people talking. But up here, it was mostly quiet except for the pigeons' cooing.

"I have an idea," Milo said, standing up tall. "Let's bring them something nice. Maybe they're grumpy because they're hungry."

Together, the monkey siblings picked some fresh berries from their garden. The berries were purple and sweet-smelling. Milo's hands trembled just a little as they walked toward the pigeons.

"Hello, pigeons," Milo called out in his friendliest voice. "We brought you some yummy berries!"

The biggest pigeon puffed up his chest. "This is our spot now!" he cooed loudly.

Milo felt scared, but he remembered what Mama Monkey taught him about using brave words. "We understand you like this spot," he said clearly. "It's very sunny and nice. But this rooftop belongs to all of us. Could we please share it?"

The pigeons looked surprised. They had expected the little monkeys to run away.

"We brought berries to be friendly," Maya added, finding her courage too. "There's enough sunshine for everyone."

The big pigeon tilted his head. The berries did smell delicious. "You... you want to share?" he asked, sounding confused.

"Yes!" Milo said with a big smile. "We could take turns, or play together. The rooftop is more fun with friends."

Slowly, the pigeons waddled over to try the berries. "These are very good," the big pigeon admitted. "Maybe we were being too grumpy."

"Sometimes everyone feels grumpy," Milo said kindly. "But talking helps us feel better."

From that day on, the pigeons and monkeys shared the rooftop happily. They took turns at the sunny spot and even played games together. Milo learned that being brave meant using kind words, not fighting.

"Thank you for helping me be brave too," Maya told her brother as they swung on the clotheslines together.

Milo felt proud and warm inside. The rooftop felt bigger and brighter when everyone was friends. He had learned that peaceful words were much stronger than angry ones.

The End.
