# Pico Parrot and the Table Talk

Pico was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a paint-splash tail. He loved making things. He loved singing rhymes. He especially loved dinner time in the cozy dining room.

The dining room smelled like warm bread and sweet carrots. The round table was shiny and smooth. The chairs were soft and squeaky. The lamp made a gentle, golden glow. Clink, clink, went the spoons. Mmm, went tummies.

“Tonight,” said Mama, setting down a big bowl of noodles, “we will have a family show-and-tell after dinner.”

Pico’s eyes sparkled. “I have a song!” he chirped, flapping. “I made it up! It goes, ‘Twirl and swirl, noodles curl!’”

“I drew a picture,” said Bella Bunny, wiggling her nose. “It’s a sparkly moon.”

“I want to drum,” said Tiny Turtle softly. He tapped the table—tap, tap.

Pico puffed his chest. “I will go first. I will sing and paint and drum and tell a story. All of it!”

Bella’s ears drooped. Tiny Turtle pulled into his shell just a little. The room felt tighter.

Mama smiled gently. “We can all share. We will take turns.”

Pico squawked, “But I have so many ideas! I want to do them all first.” His feathers ruffled.

Papa, slicing apples, said, “Let’s eat first. Crunch, munch, then we plan.”

They ate. Slurp went the noodles. Crunch went the apples. The buttery bread was warm on Pico’s tongue. The carrots tasted sweet like sunshine. Pico’s belly felt happy, but his heart felt buzzy. He wanted to sing now.

When the plates were cleared, Mama set a timer with a soft beep. “Now,” she said, “we choose who goes first. We can pick a fair way. Any ideas?”

Pico flapped. “Pick me!” he squawked. “I have the most things!”

Bella frowned. “That doesn’t feel fair,” she said in a tiny voice.

Tiny Turtle peeped, “I… I can wait, but I want a turn too.”

Pico’s tummy did a twist. He looked at Bella’s big hopeful eyes and Tiny’s shy smile. The dining room was quiet. The lamp hummed softly. Pico listened to his own fast breath.

He took a big, slow inhale. In… and out. “I don’t want anyone to feel sad,” he said. “But I still want to share my ideas.” His voice shook, but he stood tall.

Mama nodded. “This is a good problem to solve. How can everyone feel heard?”

Pico blinked. He thought. He looked at the napkin basket. He had an idea.

“I know!” Pico chirped. “We can make a plan. We can take turns. We can compromise.” He said the big word slowly, tasting each sound. “Com-pro-mise.”

“What does compromise mean?” Tiny asked.

Pico tapped his beak, thinking. “It means we meet in the middle. We all give a little and we all get a little. We make a plan that works for everyone.”

Bella’s ears perked up. “What is the plan?”

Pico felt brave. “I will volunteer to go second,” he said. “Bella can go first with her picture. I can sing after. Tiny can drum in between for a little bit, and then again at the end! We can mix our ideas.”

Tiny peeked out more. “I… I like that.”

Bella smiled. “Me too.”

Papa clapped softly. “Great teamwork.”

Mama set the timer. “Each turn is two beeps long. When it beeps, we switch.”

Bella stood by the shiny table and held up her sparkly moon. “This is my picture,” she said. “The sparkles are stars.” The glitter twinkled under the warm lamp. Pico saw silver dots like tiny bells. He felt a happy tingle in his feathers.

Beep-beep! The timer sang.

Tiny Turtle tapped—tap, tap, tap—soft as rain on a window. Pico swayed. “That sounds nice,” he whispered.

Beep-beep!

Pico stepped up. His heart thumped, but he felt strong. “I kept my promise,” he told himself. “I can do this.” He sang, “Twirl and swirl, noodles curl!” He added, “Slurp and smile, stay a while!” He wiggled and did a silly noodle dance. Everyone giggled. The dining room felt light and floaty, like bubbles.

Beep-beep!

Tiny tapped again, a little louder—tap TAP tap. Bella waved her ears to the beat. Pico clapped his wings.

“Now,” said Pico, “I have one more idea. Bella, can you hold your moon? Tiny, can you drum? I will paint with my words.” He lowered his voice. “The moon sleeps on a noodle cloud. Stars sprinkle like salt. Friends share the sky. We take turns to shine.”

The room was quiet, then filled with warm smiles. Papa wiped a happy tear. Mama hugged herself.

Beep-beep!

They all bowed. The chairs squeaked. The table gleamed. The golden lamp hummed, cozy as a cuddle.

Mama said, “How did that feel?”

Bella said, “Fair.”

Tiny said, “Brave.”

Pico said, “Good. I did not go first, but I still got to shine. We all did.” He puffed, but this time it was soft and proud.

Mama nodded. “That is compromise. You listened. You took turns. You volunteered to change the plan. You believed in yourself, and you helped everyone feel included.”

Pico touched his paint-splash tail. “I can be creative and kind,” he said. “I can share the stage.”

Papa held out a plate. “Apple slices for our stars?”

Crunch, munch! Giggles filled the dining room like music. Pico felt the warm table under his feet and the sweet apple taste on his tongue. He looked at his family and said, “Next time, someone else can go first again. We can make a new plan together.”

Everyone nodded. The lamp glowed. The night felt gentle and bright.

The lesson was clear in Pico’s heart: When we compromise and take turns, everyone gets a chance to shine. And that made dinner time the best show of all.

The End.
