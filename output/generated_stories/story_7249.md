# Poppy the Patient Panda’s Porch Notes

Poppy the panda sat on the peaceful front porch. The porch was cool and shady. The wooden boards felt smooth under her soft paws. A gentle breeze tickled the bamboo chimes. Tink, tink, tink. Birds chirped in the tall trees, and the air smelled like warm tea and fresh rain.

On a low table, Poppy kept a tiny box of smooth stones, a jar of crayons, and a stack of little papers. “Today, I will write kind notes,” Poppy said. Her voice was calm. “I will listen with my heart.”

She dipped her big paw in warm water, dabbed it dry, and picked a purple crayon. She smiled. “Purple feels peaceful,” she whispered.

Just then, a small squirrel scurried up the steps. His tail flick-flick-flicked. His eyes were shiny with worry. “Poppy! Poppy!” he squeaked. “Crow took my acorn! It was shiny and round and mine. I am mad. I am sad.”

Poppy patted the step beside her. “Come sit,” she said. “Take a deep breath with me.” They breathed in slow. They breathed out slow. The wind brushed their cheeks like a soft hug.

Poppy folded her paws. “Tell me more,” she said. “I am listening.”

Squirrel sniffled. “I found the acorn by the big rock. I rolled it and sang to it. Then Crow swooped and grabbed it. He flew to the mailbox post. He would not give it back.”

Poppy nodded. “Thank you for telling me,” she said. “Your feelings matter. Let’s be gentle with our words. Let’s use our listening ears.”

She took a small paper from the stack. On the top, she drew a tiny heart. “Sometimes,” Poppy said, “a kind note can help. We can write to Crow.”

Squirrel blinked. “A note?”

“A note that says what you feel,” said Poppy. “A note that asks kindly. A note that listens, too.”

Poppy picked the purple crayon again. “What would you like to say?”

Squirrel wiggled his paws. “Um… Dear Crow, that acorn is special to me. It is my acorn. Can I please have it back?”

Poppy nodded and wrote the words slowly, saying each word softly so Squirrel could hear. The crayon made a gentle scritch-scratch on the paper. Then Poppy added, “We can also ask a question.”

She wrote, “Why did you take it?” and then, “Can we find a way that feels good for both of us?”

Poppy placed a smooth stone on the paper and drew a small acorn in the corner. “Let’s bring the note to the mailbox post,” she said.

They walked to the edge of the porch. The boards creaked kindly under their steps. The sun felt warm on their fur. At the post, Crow sat with glossy black feathers. He blinked his bright eyes and tilted his head.

Poppy lifted the note. “Hello, Crow,” she said in her soft voice. “We wrote a kind note. May we read it?”

Crow tucked his wings close. “Caw?” he asked.

Poppy read the note slowly. Squirrel stood tall and brave. When Poppy finished, she looked up. “We are ready to listen,” she said. “Can you tell us your side?”

Crow ruffled his feathers. “Caw,” he said, and then he spoke in a gentle voice. “The acorn was shiny. I thought no one wanted it. I love shiny things. I did not know it was special to you. I am sorry you are sad.”

Squirrel’s tail stopped flicking. His eyes softened. “Thank you for telling me,” he said. “I was very sad. I still hope to have it back.”

Crow tilted his head again. “We can share,” he said. “We can look for two acorns together. I can return this one to you now. Then we can search for a shiny leaf for me.”

Poppy smiled. “That sounds like a peaceful plan,” she said. “Thank you for your open heart.”

Crow hopped down and placed the acorn in Squirrel’s paws. The acorn was warm from the sun. Squirrel hugged it close. “Thank you, Crow,” he said. “I will help you find a shiny leaf.”

Poppy took another paper and a green crayon. She wrote a new note: “Thank you for listening. Thank you for caring.” She drew a leaf and a feather.

They all walked together along the porch edge. The bushes rustled. The world smelled like sweet grass. The wind hummed.

Soon they found a leaf that was shiny and gold in the light. Crow cheered, “Caw!” Squirrel giggled. Poppy clapped her soft paws.

Back on the porch, Poppy poured tiny cups of warm tea. They sipped and listened to the bamboo chimes. Tink, tink, tink.

Squirrel touched the note with the heart. “Writing helped me,” he said. “It helped me say my feelings.”

Crow nodded. “Listening helped me,” he said. “It helped me understand.”

Poppy’s eyes sparkled. “Compassion is like a soft blanket,” she said. “It keeps our hearts warm. We can write kind words. We can listen. We can find peaceful answers together.”

They sat in the soft shade, happy and calm. The porch was quiet, and their hearts were quiet, too.

The End.
