# Polly's Discovery Garden

Polly the parrot loved her colorful balcony garden. Every morning, she would spread her bright green wings and fly from her cozy perch to explore the beautiful flowers and plants that grew in pots all around the sunny balcony.

"Good morning, sunflowers!" Polly chirped happily. "Good morning, tomatoes! Good morning, sweet basil!"

The warm sun felt wonderful on her feathers, and the sweet smell of flowers filled the air. Polly loved to write about all the amazing things she discovered in her garden. She kept a special notebook with her tiny pencil, writing down every new thing she found.

But one cloudy morning, Polly had a big problem. She couldn't find her notebook anywhere!

"Oh no!" she squawked, flapping her wings. "Where is my discovery book? I found three new ladybugs yesterday, and I wanted to write about them!"

Polly searched everywhere. She looked under the watering can - not there. She peeked behind the flower pots - still not there. She even checked inside the bird bath, getting her beautiful feathers all wet and messy.

"This is terrible," Polly sighed, sitting on her favorite sunflower pot. Dirty water dripped from her wings, and her bright feathers looked dull and muddy.

Just then, her friend Benny the butterfly landed nearby. "Polly, you look upset. And very messy! What's wrong?"

"I lost my discovery notebook," Polly explained sadly. "Now I can't write about all the wonderful things in my garden. And look at me - I'm all dirty from searching!"

Benny's colorful wings sparkled in the morning light. "Don't give up, Polly! Let's clean you up first, then we'll search together. Sometimes when we're clean and fresh, we can think more clearly."

Polly nodded. That made sense! She flew to the clean water dish and carefully washed her feathers. She used her beak to smooth each green feather until they shone brightly again. The cool, clean water felt refreshing, and soon she looked beautiful once more.

"Much better!" chirped Polly, feeling fresh and ready. "Now I can think clearly. Let me retrace my steps from yesterday."

Polly and Benny searched the garden together. They looked carefully at each plant, each corner, each hiding spot. As they searched, Polly noticed so many wonderful things she had never seen before!

"Look, Benny! Tiny purple flowers on the mint plant!" she exclaimed.

"And see those interesting patterns on the bean leaves!" Benny added.

Finally, behind the biggest tomato plant, Polly spotted something familiar. "My notebook!" she sang joyfully. It had fallen behind the pot when the wind blew yesterday.

Polly picked up her pencil with excitement. "I have so much to write about! Not just the ladybugs, but all the new discoveries I made while searching!"

She wrote about the purple mint flowers, the bean leaf patterns, and even about how good it felt to have clean, shiny feathers. She wrote about how Benny helped her, and how being patient and not giving up led to finding her notebook.

"You know what, Benny?" Polly said, looking up from her writing. "Sometimes losing something leads to finding something even better - new discoveries!"

From that day on, Polly kept her notebook in a special safe spot. She learned that staying clean helped her feel confident, that being resilient meant never giving up, and that every day in her garden brought wonderful new discoveries to explore and write about.

The End.
