# Tilly Turtle’s Treehouse Tune

Tilly was a shy turtle with a smooth, green shell. She liked quiet things, like watching leaves wiggle in the wind and listening to tiny raindrops tap-tap on her shell. One sunny morning, Tilly peeked up at the big treehouse in the middle of the clearing. The wooden ladder smelled like warm pine. Birds chirped. A breeze carried the sweet scent of apples.

“Today is the Treehouse Music Day,” Tilly whispered. Her voice was soft as a humming bee. Inside the treehouse, friends were gathering. There would be drums, bells, and claps. There would be singing and smiling.

Tilly wanted to help. She wanted to play. But she felt shy. Her tummy fluttered like a little moth.

She looked up the ladder. “I can take the first step,” she told herself. “I can try.” That was Tilly’s initiative starting to glow, like a small light inside.

Step. Thump. Her feet touched the first rung. Step. Thump. Her hands held the smooth wood. She climbed slowly—step, breathe, step, breathe—until she reached the doorway.

“Hi, Tilly!” chirped Pippa the parrot, with shiny blue feathers. “We need helpers!”

“Helpers?” Tilly asked, peeking in.

“Yes!” said Benny the bunny, twitching his pink nose. “We need someone to pass the music sticks!”

“And someone to keep the beat,” added Suri the squirrel, flicking her fluffy tail. “Tap-tap, tap-tap!”

Tilly felt that little light inside grow brighter. “I can help,” she said. “I will pass the sticks.” Her voice was still soft, but brave.

The treehouse smelled like fresh wood and sweet fruit. Sunbeams made lines of light on the floor. The friends gathered in a circle. Benny held a soft drum. Pippa had shiny bells. Suri clapped her tiny paws. Tilly found a basket of smooth music sticks. They felt cool in her hands.

“Thank you, Tilly!” Pippa sang. “You are volunteering!”

Tilly smiled. “I am trying.”

The music started. Tap-tap, jingle-jingle, clap-clap. Tilly listened. Her heart thumped with the rhythm. She passed sticks to each friend, one by one.

When Tilly reached Max the mole, he blinked and said, “I want the loud drum.”

“We only have one drum,” Benny said gently. “I am using it now.”

Max frowned. “But I want it.”

Tilly felt the flutter again. This was a problem. She took a slow breath. “We can share,” she said. “We can take turns. Or we can make a new sound.”

Max’s whiskers twitched. “A new sound?”

Tilly nodded. “We can tap the floor with our feet. Stomp-stomp!” She lifted one foot and gave a tiny stomp. It made a soft thud on the wooden floor.

Max tried. Stomp-stomp! He smiled. “That is fun!”

Benny tapped the drum quietly while Max stomped in time. Pippa jingled faster. The sounds blended: thud-thud, tap-tap, jingle-jingle, clap-clap. It felt like rain and sunshine together.

But soon, the beat got wobbly. Pippa sped up. Suri slowed down. The music sounded messy.

Tilly’s eyes grew wide. “We need a steady beat,” she said. Her little light of initiative glowed again. “I can help us keep time.”

She picked up two sticks and rubbed them together—scritch-scritch. The sound was gentle and steady, like crickets at night.

“Follow me,” Tilly said, a little louder. Scritch, scritch, scritch, scritch.

The friends listened. Their feet and hands matched Tilly’s steady scritch. The music came together. It felt smooth, like warm soup in a bowl.

“Great job, Tilly!” Suri cheered.

Tilly’s cheeks felt warm. It was a happy warm, like sunshine on a shell.

Then Pippa wanted to sing her favorite fast song. Benny wanted a slow song. They both spoke at once. “Fast!” “Slow!”

Tilly put one stick down and held up the other like a tiny baton. “Let’s compromise,” she said. “First, a slow song. Then, a fast song. We can take turns.”

Pippa and Benny looked at each other, then nodded. “Okay!”

The slow song came first. Tilly kept the scritch-scritch beat. The friends swayed. The treehouse creaked softly, like it was humming along. Then came the fast song. Tilly sped up. Scritch-scritch-scritch! The friends laughed and stomped. The floor vibrated under their feet—tickly and fun.

After the songs, Pippa pointed to a basket of fruit. “We need someone to pass the snacks.”

Tilly sat up tall. “I can do it,” she said. She passed juicy apple slices and sweet blueberries. The apples were crisp. The blueberries burst with tiny pops.

“Thank you, Tilly,” Max said. “You helped a lot today.”

Tilly took a bite of apple. Crunch! “I was shy,” she said. “But I tried. I took the first step. I kept trying.”

Benny nodded. “You showed initiative. You started helping all on your own.”

“And you helped us compromise,” added Pippa. “You listened to everyone.”

Tilly looked around the bright, happy treehouse. She felt calm and proud. The little light inside her glowed steady and strong.

She tapped her sticks one more time. Scritch-scritch. “When we see a way to help,” she said, “we can start. We can try. We can keep going.”

All the friends smiled. “Yes!”

They ended with a gentle song together. The wind whispered in the leaves. The treehouse smelled like apples and wood. Tilly’s heart felt full.

The End.
