# Ruby's Helping Song

Ruby was a little red bird who loved to sing. Every morning, she perched on the big oak tree in the sunny backyard. The warm sun made her feathers sparkle like tiny jewels.

"Tweet-tweet-trill!" Ruby sang her happy morning song. The flowers seemed to dance when she sang. The butterflies fluttered closer to listen.

One bright Tuesday morning, Ruby noticed something different. Mrs. Chen, the kind lady who lived in the house, was in her garden. But Mrs. Chen looked very sad.

"Oh dear," Mrs. Chen sighed. "My tomato plants are so thirsty, but my watering can is broken."

Ruby stopped singing. She tilted her little head and watched. Mrs. Chen's beautiful red tomatoes were drooping in the hot sun. The green leaves looked very sad too.

"I wish I could help," Ruby chirped softly. But what could a small bird do?

Ruby thought and thought. She flew around the backyard, looking for ideas. Near the fence, she spotted something shiny - it was a small cup that had fallen from the bird bath!

"Tweet-tweet!" Ruby sang excitedly. She had an idea!

Ruby picked up the tiny cup in her beak. It was just the right size for her. She flew to the bird bath and dipped the cup in the cool, clean water.

Carefully, Ruby flew over to the first tomato plant. She poured the water right onto the roots. The plant seemed to say "thank you" by standing up a little straighter.

"One plant helped!" Ruby sang. "Now for the next one!"

Back and forth Ruby flew. Cup after cup of water. Her little wings got tired, but she kept going. The sun was getting hotter, but Ruby didn't stop.

Mrs. Chen looked up from her weeding. "Oh my!" she gasped. "What is that sweet little bird doing?"

Ruby was so busy helping that she didn't notice Mrs. Chen watching. She just kept flying - to the bird bath, to the plants, back to the bird bath again.

After many trips, Ruby felt very tired. Her wings ached and her beak was sore. But when she looked at the garden, all the tomato plants were standing tall and happy again!

"Tweet-tweet-trill!" Ruby sang a tired but joyful song.

Mrs. Chen clapped her hands together. "You wonderful little bird! You saved my garden!"

Mrs. Chen hurried inside and came back with a small dish of fresh water and tiny seeds. She placed them on a special little table just for Ruby.

"This is for you, my helpful friend," Mrs. Chen said with a big smile. "Thank you for caring about my plants."

Ruby felt warm and happy inside. Her tummy was full of delicious seeds, and her heart was full of joy. She had learned something very important that day.

The next morning, Ruby woke up early. She sang her beautiful song as always, but now she also looked around the backyard to see if anyone needed help.

She saw a baby butterfly stuck in a spider web. Ruby gently helped free it with her beak.

She noticed that the bird bath was getting low on water. Ruby called to the other birds to come drink before it was all gone.

When the mailman dropped a letter that blew away in the wind, Ruby flew fast and picked it up, dropping it right by Mrs. Chen's door.

"Thank you, Ruby!" Mrs. Chen called out each time. "You are such a good helper!"

Ruby discovered that helping others made her feel even happier than just singing alone. Her songs became more beautiful because her heart was full of kindness.

From that day on, Ruby was known as the most helpful bird in the whole neighborhood. She learned that even small acts of kindness can make a big difference.

And every evening, as the sun set over the backyard, Ruby would sing a special song - a song about helping, caring, and making the world a little bit better, one small deed at a time.

The End.
