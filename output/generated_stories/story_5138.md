# Pebble the Penguin’s Balcony Garden Fix

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a bouncy waddle. He lived in a cozy home with a sunny balcony garden. The garden had tiny tomato plants, sweet basil, mint that smelled fresh like cool gum, and a little lemon tree that made the air smell zippy and bright. Pebble loved to hum while he watered the plants. “Hello, little leaves,” he said every morning. “Sip, sip, sip!”

One warm day, Pebble waddled onto the balcony. The sun felt tickly on his feathers. The breeze was soft and smelled like basil and wet dirt. Pebble’s tummy felt happy. “Good morning, garden!” he chirped.

But something was wrong. The basil leaves drooped like sleepy umbrellas. The soil looked dry in one pot and soggy in another. A tiny tomato hung low on its vine, pale and shy.

“Oh no,” Pebble said. He touched a leaf with his flipper. “You feel tired.” He listened. He could hear dripping. Plink. Plink. Plink. Water was leaking from a broken watering can. It had a crack!

Pebble took a deep breath. “I can fix this,” he said. “I can make it better. I can improve my garden!”

He looked around. He smelled the mint. He heard a bird chirp, “Cheep-cheep!” He felt the warm sun on his beak. Pebble smiled. “First, I will learn what the plants need,” he said. “Then, I will try new ideas.”

Pebble knelt close. “Basil, what do you need?” he asked.

The basil leaves danced in the breeze. Pebble imagined a tiny whisper: “Not too much water. Just a little here, a little there.”

Pebble nodded. “Tomato, what do you need?”

He looked at the tomato plant. Its stem leaned to one side. Pebble imagined it saying, “I need help to stand tall. I need sunlight too.”

Pebble put his flipper on his chest. “I hear you,” he said. “Let’s solve this, step by step.”

He made a plan.

Step 1: Fix the watering.
Step 2: Help the plants stand tall.
Step 3: Make the sun and shade just right.
Step 4: Keep trying and keep improving.

Pebble waddled inside. He found a clean plastic bottle. He found a straw. He found a rubber band and some tape. “I can make a new water helper,” he said. He poked tiny holes in the bottle cap with a dull toothpick. “Poke, poke,” he said softly. He used the rubber band to hold the straw near the top so air could go in and water could drip out slow. “This is my drip, drip bottle,” Pebble said proudly. “It’s an invention!”

He filled the bottle with water. He turned it upside down into the soil of the dry pot. Drip. Drip. Drip. The water came slow and gentle. “Perfect,” Pebble said. “Not too much. Not too little.”

Next, Pebble looked at the tomato plant. He found two wooden chopsticks in the kitchen drawer. He tied them to the stem with soft string, careful and kind. “There you go,” he said. “Stand tall, little tomato.” The plant felt steadier now, like it was giving a small smile.

Pebble shaded his eyes with his flipper. “Now the sun,” he said. The lemon tree made a bright splash of light. The basil was too hot at noon. Pebble had an idea. He took a small scarf and a clothespin. He clipped the scarf to the railing to make a tiny shade for the basil at lunchtime. The scarf flapped gently. Swish, swish. “Shade for you when the sun is strong,” Pebble said.

Pebble sat and watched. He listened to the drip, drip, drip. He smelled mint and lemon. He felt the breeze. After a while, the basil leaves perked up a little. The tomato stood taller. Pebble clapped softly. “We are improving,” he cheered. “We are solving problems!”

But then he noticed the soggy pot. The soil was heavy and wet. Pebble touched it. “Hmm,” he said. “This pot needs better holes.”

He lifted the pot and peered underneath. Only one tiny hole! Pebble blinked. “I can innovate again,” he said. He found a plastic tray and some small pebbles. He gently moved the plant to another pot with more holes. He put pebbles at the bottom to help the water drain. The soil felt lighter. “There,” Pebble said. “Now you can breathe.”

The bird on the rail chirped, “Cheep-cheep-cheep!” Pebble laughed. “Thank you, little helper,” he said.

The next morning, Pebble came back. The air smelled fresh and green. The basil stood up. The tomato was blushing red at the edges. The mint was fluffy and bright. Pebble’s heart felt warm. “We did it,” he said. “We kept trying. We made a plan. We made new tools. We improved our garden!”

He filled the drip bottle again. He checked the strings on the tomato. He moved the shade scarf just a bit. “Little changes make big help,” he said.

Pebble patted the soil. “When something is not working, we don’t give up. We look, we listen, we try new ideas, and we make it better.”

The bird chirped a happy tune. The leaves rustled softly. Pebble smiled wide. “Today was a good garden day,” he said. “I learned to improve, to solve problems, and to use my penguin brain to invent.”

He gave the basil a tiny wave. “See you tomorrow, garden friends,” he whispered.

The End.
