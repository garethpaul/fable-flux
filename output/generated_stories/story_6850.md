# Benny's Big Helper Day

The warm morning sun shone on Mrs. Robin's balcony garden. Benny the beaver wiggled his flat tail excitedly as he looked through the glass door.

"Today is volunteer day!" chirped Mrs. Robin, opening the door wide. "Would you like to help me take care of my garden, Benny?"

Benny's brown eyes sparkled. "Yes, yes! I want to help!" He clapped his small paws together. "But... what do I do?"

Mrs. Robin smiled kindly. "First, we need to listen carefully to directions. Can you do that?"

Benny nodded his furry head up and down. "I'm a good listener!"

"Wonderful!" said Mrs. Robin. "Step one: we need to water the tomato plants. They're the tall green ones in the red pots."

Benny looked around the sunny balcony. He saw many colorful pots with different plants. Some were short and leafy. Some were tall with tiny flowers. But there! He spotted the tall green plants in bright red pots.

"I found them!" Benny squeaked happily, pointing with his little paw.

"Perfect! Now, take this small watering can," Mrs. Robin said, handing him a blue can with yellow dots. "Pour the water gently at the bottom of each plant. Not on the leaves - just the soil."

Benny carefully took the watering can. It felt important in his paws. He walked slowly to the first tomato plant. The soil looked dry and thirsty.

"Like this?" he asked, tilting the can gently.

"Exactly right!" Mrs. Robin clapped. "You're following directions perfectly!"

The water made a soft trickling sound as it soaked into the brown soil. Benny could smell the fresh, earthy scent. It made him feel proud and helpful.

"What's next?" Benny asked eagerly, his tail swishing with excitement.

"Step two: we need to pick the ripe cherry tomatoes. They're the small red ones that feel soft when you touch them gently."

Benny looked closely at the plants. He saw green tomatoes that felt hard. He saw yellow ones that felt firm. Then he found some beautiful red ones that felt just a little squishy.

"These ones?" he asked, touching them carefully.

"Those are perfect!" Mrs. Robin said. "You're such a good communicator, asking questions when you're not sure."

Benny carefully picked the ripe tomatoes and placed them in a small basket. Each one was warm from the sunshine and smelled sweet and fresh.

"Step three," said Mrs. Robin, "we need to pull the weeds from around the herb plants. Weeds are the plants that don't belong. They take food away from our good plants."

Mrs. Robin showed Benny what weeds looked like. They had different shaped leaves and grew in funny places.

"I see one!" Benny pointed to a tall, skinny plant growing between the basil and oregano.

"Good eye!" Mrs. Robin praised. "Pull it out gently by the roots."

Benny grabbed the weed carefully and pulled. It came out with a satisfying pop, roots and all. The herb plants seemed to stand up straighter without the weed crowding them.

After an hour of working together, Benny and Mrs. Robin sat on small garden chairs. The balcony garden looked beautiful and healthy. The tomatoes were watered, the ripe ones were picked, and all the weeds were gone.

"Thank you for volunteering to help me today, Benny," Mrs. Robin said, offering him a glass of cool lemonade. "You listened to directions so well, and you asked great questions when you needed help."

Benny's chest puffed out with pride. His paws were a little dirty, but his heart felt warm and happy.

"I loved helping!" he said, taking a sip of the sweet, cold lemonade. "Can I volunteer again tomorrow?"

Mrs. Robin laughed her musical laugh. "Of course! Gardens need helpers every day. And you, my dear Benny, are the very best kind of volunteer - one who listens, asks questions, and works with care."

Benny smiled his biggest beaver smile. Helping others felt even better than building dams!

The End.
