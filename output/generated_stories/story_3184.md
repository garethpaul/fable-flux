# Finn's Big Voice

Little Finn the fox kit had the fluffiest orange fur and the most curious black nose. He loved exploring every corner of the cozy farmhouse where he lived with Mama Fox and Papa Fox.

One sunny morning, Finn's tummy rumbled loudly. "Grumble, grumble, GRUMBLE!" it went.

"Oh my!" laughed Mama Fox. "Someone sounds hungry!"

Finn sniffed the air with his wet black nose. Something smelled absolutely delicious coming from the pantry. His little paws patted softly on the wooden floor as he tiptoed toward the wonderful smell.

The pantry was full of amazing things! Tall jars sparkled like jewels on high shelves. Colorful boxes stood in neat rows. And there, on the very top shelf, sat a basket of the most beautiful red apples Finn had ever seen.

"Those apples look so yummy," Finn whispered to himself. "And they smell so sweet and crispy!"

Finn stood on his tippy-toes and stretched his little paws as high as they could go. But the basket was much too high. He tried jumping, but his small fox legs couldn't reach that far.

"Maybe I could climb," thought Finn. He looked at the shelves like a ladder. But as he put one paw on the bottom shelf, it wobbled. The jars clinked together with a scary "CLINK, CLINK!"

Finn's ears drooped. He really wanted those apples, but he didn't want to make a mess or get hurt.

Just then, Papa Fox walked into the pantry carrying an empty basket.

"Hello, little one," Papa Fox said warmly. "What are you doing in here?"

Finn felt shy at first. His whiskers twitched nervously. But then he remembered that Papa Fox always listened to him with kind eyes.

"Papa," Finn said in his small but brave voice, "I'm really hungry, and those red apples up there smell so good. But I can't reach them, and I don't want to climb because it might be dangerous."

Papa Fox's eyes twinkled proudly. "Finn, I'm so glad you told me what you needed! And I'm especially proud that you thought about being safe."

"Really?" asked Finn, his bushy tail wagging hopefully.

"Really," said Papa Fox. "Using your words to ask for help is very brave. And thinking about safety shows you're growing up to be wise."

Papa Fox reached up with his long arms and gently took down two perfect red apples. "These are for our snack time. Would you like to help me wash them?"

"Yes, please!" Finn's whole body wiggled with happiness.

At the kitchen sink, the cool water splashed over the shiny red apples. Finn helped rub them clean with his small paws.

"Papa," said Finn thoughtfully, "I'm glad I used my words instead of trying to climb."

"Me too," said Papa Fox, giving Finn a warm hug. "When we speak up about what we need and think about others' safety too, good things happen."

They sat together at the sunny kitchen table, crunching their sweet, juicy apples. Each bite was crispy and perfect.

"From now on," said Finn with apple juice on his whiskers, "I'll always use my big voice to ask for help when I need it!"

"That's my brave little fox," smiled Papa Fox.

And from that day forward, Finn remembered that his voice was important, his needs mattered, and asking for help was the smartest thing to do.

The End.
