# Ruby Rabbit's Big Voice

Ruby was a small, fluffy rabbit with soft brown fur and bright pink ears. She lived in a cozy burrow, but today she had to go down to the busy basement where all the woodland animals worked together.

*Thump, thump, thump!* Ruby's little paws made quiet sounds as she hopped down the wooden stairs. The basement was very busy and very loud!

"Move those acorns faster!" squeaked a mouse.

"Where did I put my hammer?" grumbled a badger.

*Bang! Crash! Clatter!* Tools dropped everywhere. Animals scurried back and forth carrying boxes, nuts, and building supplies. Ruby felt very small in all the noise and movement.

Ruby needed to ask for help finding her lost carrot lunch, but everyone looked so busy and important. Her tummy rumbled loudly. "Grrowwwl!"

"Maybe I should just go home," Ruby whispered to herself, her ears drooping down. But then her tummy rumbled again, even louder this time.

Ruby watched a big bear carrying heavy logs. She wanted to ask him, but her voice felt stuck in her throat like peanut butter. The bear walked right past her without noticing.

Next, Ruby saw a family of squirrels sorting nuts into colorful bins. She opened her mouth to speak, but only a tiny "um..." came out. The squirrels were chattering so loudly they couldn't hear her small voice.

Ruby's ears drooped even lower. She felt invisible, like a shadow in the corner. But then she remembered what her mama always told her: "Even the smallest rabbit has the biggest heart. Your voice matters, Ruby."

Ruby took a deep breath that filled her whole chest. She stood up tall on her hind legs, making herself as big as she could. Her pink nose twitched with determination.

"Excuse me!" Ruby called out in her clearest, strongest voice.

A few animals nearby stopped and looked at her.

Ruby felt her heart beating fast like a drum, but she kept going. "I lost my carrot lunch somewhere down here. Can anyone help me find it?"

"Oh my!" said Mrs. Owl, flying down from a high shelf. "I saw an orange carrot by the paint cans over there!"

"I'll help you look!" offered a friendly raccoon with a striped tail.

"Me too!" squeaked a little mouse.

Soon, Ruby had a whole team of animal friends helping her search. They looked under boxes, behind tools, and around corners.

"Found it!" called the raccoon, holding up Ruby's bright orange carrot. "It was hiding behind the toolbox!"

"Thank you so much!" Ruby said, her voice now confident and clear. "I was scared to ask for help, but you're all so kind!"

"We're always happy to help," smiled Mrs. Owl. "But we can only help when we know someone needs us. Speaking up was very brave, Ruby!"

Ruby munched her delicious carrot and made new friends in the basement. She learned that her voice was important, no matter how small she felt.

From that day on, whenever Ruby needed something, she remembered to stand tall, speak clearly, and ask for help. And she always found that others were ready to listen and lend a paw.

"Even small voices can make big differences," Ruby told herself proudly.

The End.
