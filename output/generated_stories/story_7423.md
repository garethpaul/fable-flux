# Bolt the Brave Seat-Belt Buddy

Beep-woof! Bolt was a helpful robot dog. His nose glowed soft blue. His ears clicked like tiny spoons. He lived with Mia and her family. Today, they were going for a ride in the family car.

The sun was warm. The car smelled like orange snacks and clean seats. The engine hummed like a sleepy bee. “Beep-woof!” said Bolt, hopping in. His little paws went clink-clink on the floor mat.

“Seat belts, please,” Mom said, smiling. “Safety first.”

Mia climbed into her car seat. “I want to hold my bear,” she said.

“You can hold Bear after your buckle is clicked,” said Bolt. His tail-light blinked. “Click is safe.”

Mia reached for the belt. Click! It locked. She hugged Bear. “I did it!”

Bolt wagged. “Good job. Safe and snug.”

Dad turned on the music. Soft music floated like bubbles. The car rolled down the street. Trees waved. A cool breeze whispered through the open window.

“Window up, please,” said Bolt gently. “Little hands inside.”

Mia pulled her hand back in and lifted the window. “I forgot,” she said.

“That’s okay,” said Bolt. “We remember to be safe.”

They stopped at a red light. A big truck rumbled by. Vroom-vroom! “Red means stop,” Mia said.

“Right,” said Bolt. “Green will mean go. Safety is listening.”

Soon, they reached the grocery store. Dad parked. Mom said, “Stay buckled until I say unbuckle.”

Mia waited. Her legs wiggled with energy. “Now,” Mom said. Mia pressed the button and climbed out. Bolt hopped down, too.

In the parking lot, the ground felt warm and smooth. Cars clicked and doors thumped. Bolt stood tall. “Hold hands,” he said. “Look left. Look right. Listen.”

Mia took Mom’s hand. They walked. A car started moving fast. A boy was not holding hands. He ran between cars. “Stop!” beeped Bolt. His light flashed bright. “Walking feet. Hold hands. Parking lots are busy.”

The boy froze. His eyes grew big. A grown-up hurried over. “Thank you,” the grown-up said. “We forgot our safety rules.”

Bolt’s tail-light blinked kindly. “Everyone learns. Safety is fair for all.”

They shopped. Mia picked apples. Crunchy and red. She picked carrots. Orange and crisp. “These help my body,” she said.

“Yes,” said Bolt. “Self-care is choosing good food and rest and water. It keeps us strong to be safe.”

They paid. The cashier smiled. “What a smart helper.” Bolt beamed. “Beep-woof!”

Back at the car, Mia climbed in. Bolt checked. “Seat belts, please.”

“Click,” said Mia. Bear sat under the belt too. Bolt shook his head. “Bear can sit on your lap, but belts are for people. We don’t share belts. That keeps you super safe.”

Mia moved Bear. “Okay.”

The car started again. The road was bumpy-bump. Mia laughed. Then she felt hot. “I’m thirsty,” she said.

“Ask for water when we stop,” said Bolt. “Sipping while the car moves can make spills. We wait to be safe.”

Mom pulled into a rest stop. The air smelled like fresh grass. “Now you may drink,” she said.

Mia sipped cool water. “Ahh.” She felt better. “That is self-care,” said Bolt. “You listened to your body.”

They got back in. Click! Click! “Ready,” said Dad.

As they drove, they passed a playground. A yellow ball rolled into the road. A little puppy on a leash barked. Yip! A car did not slow down. It swerved and honked. “That driver is not being safe,” said Mia, worried.

“Justice means fairness and rules for everyone,” said Bolt softly. “Drivers follow rules to keep people and pets safe. We will do the right thing.”

Dad slowed. He stopped and let a grown-up pick up the ball and puppy. The grown-up waved. “Thank you!”

“Beep-woof,” said Bolt. “Safe and fair.”

A little later, a new problem started. Mia’s belt felt tight. She pulled her arm out of the strap. “I don’t like it,” she grumped.

Bolt’s ears clicked. “Uh-oh. Alert. We keep our arms inside the belt. That is how the belt protects you.”

“I’m mad,” Mia said. Her cheeks got warm. She crossed her arms.

“It’s okay to feel mad,” said Bolt. His voice was calm and gentle. “Let’s take a belly breath. In… one, two. Out… one, two.” He breathed with her. In… and out… The music was soft like a hug.

Mia’s face relaxed. “Better,” she said.

“Would you like a soft cloth under the strap?” asked Mom. “It can feel cozy.”

“Yes,” said Mia. Mom placed a soft cloth under the belt. Mia slid her arm back in. Click. “Comfy,” she said.

“Problem solved,” Bolt cheered. “You used words. You breathed. You cared for your body and stayed safe. That is smart.”

Soon they were home. The car sang a little chime. Ding-dong! Mom parked. “Unbuckle,” she said.

Mia pressed the button. She climbed out with Bear. Bolt hopped down. The air smelled like dinner. The sky was pink and gold.

Bolt wagged. “What did we learn today?”

Mia held up three fingers. “Safety first: buckle up, hands inside, hold hands. Self-care: drink water, eat good food, take belly breaths. Justice: rules are fair for everyone, even drivers.”

Bolt nodded. His light glowed warm. “Yes. When we follow safety rules, we care for ourselves and others. That is fair. That is kind.”

Mia smiled. “Beep-woof,” she said.

Bolt laughed. “Beep-woof!”

They went inside, feeling proud and peaceful.

The End.
