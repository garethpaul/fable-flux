# Koala Kiki’s Porch Pause

Koala Kiki loved the peaceful front porch. It was warm and sunny. The wooden boards felt smooth under her soft paws. A gentle breeze tickled her fuzzy ears. She could smell sweet flowers and a little hint of eucalyptus tea from her cup.

“This is my quiet time,” Kiki whispered, taking a slow sip. “Sip, sip.” The tea was warm and cozy in her tummy.

Kiki liked to take care of herself. She had a little list on a chalkboard by the rocking chair. It said: “Breathe. Stretch. Sip water. Rest. Listen kindly.” She smiled at her list. She felt calm and ready for the day.

Just then, a tiny tap-tap-tap sounded on the porch rail. Tap, tap, tap! Kiki’s eyes grew curious. “I wonder what that is?” she asked softly.

A brave beetle peeked over the edge. “Hello! I’m Benny!” he squeaked. “Can I play here?”

Kiki nodded. “Hello, Benny. Yes, you may. This is a peaceful porch. We use quiet voices. We are kind.”

Benny tapped his little feet. “I like quiet. But I also like to explore. What are you doing?”

“I am taking care of me,” said Kiki. “I breathe, I stretch, and I rest. Would you like to try?”

Benny’s tiny antennae wiggled. “I’ll try!”

Kiki put her paw on her chest and breathed in. She smelled flowers and fresh air. She breathed out and felt her shoulders relax. “In… and out,” she said.

“In… and out,” Benny copied, his shell going up and down. “Wow! I feel cozy.”

Next came stretching. Kiki reached one arm up to the tall sky. “Hello, sky,” she giggled. She reached the other arm to her toes. “Hello, toes.”

Benny stretched his little legs. “Hello, legs! Hello, floor!” He wobbled, then tried again. “I can do it if I keep trying!”

“That is persistence,” Kiki said kindly. “Keep trying, little by little.”

Just then, a friend peeped from the porch steps. It was Nia, a small nightingale. “Tweet-tweet! May I join?” she sang softly.

“Yes,” said Kiki. “Welcome to the peaceful porch. We share. We listen. We take turns.”

Nia fluttered to the rail and listened to the wind rustle through the leaves. “It sounds like shhh,” she whispered. “So calm.”

Kiki poured a little water into a saucer for Benny and a tiny thimble for Nia. “Sip water time,” she said. “Sipping helps our bodies.”

Benny took a careful sip. “Sip, sip. Thank you.”

Nia dipped her beak. “Sip, sip. Thank you.”

They sat together. The sun felt warm like a hug. The rocking chair creaked a friendly creak-creak. A faraway dog barked, but softly. A bee buzzed by, busy but not loud.

After a while, Benny grew curious again. “What is behind that pot?” he asked.

Kiki’s eyes twinkled. “Let’s find out. But we move with care and respect. We do not disturb others.”

They tiptoed to the tall pot. Benny peeked and saw a sleepy caterpillar curled like a comma. “Oh!” Benny gasped. “I want to say hello!”

Kiki held up a gentle paw. “We can say hello in a quiet voice. The caterpillar is resting. Rest is part of self-care.”

Nia sang a tiny hum. “Hello, sleepy friend,” she crooned. The caterpillar wiggled but stayed asleep.

Benny looked thoughtful. “Sometimes I want to tap-tap-tap. But I can be gentle.”

“That is respect for others,” Kiki said. “And respect for yourself, too.”

They went back to the rocking chair. Kiki felt her tummy rumble—rumbly-rum. “Snack time,” she smiled. She took a crunchy leaf and a small slice of apple from a basket. She shared with her friends.

“Crunch, crunch,” Benny said.

“Peep, peep,” Nia said with joy.

Kiki chewed slowly. “Chew and breathe,” she said. “My body feels happy when I take my time.”

A cloud slipped over the sun, and the porch grew cooler. Nia shivered. “Brrr.”

Kiki folded a soft blanket over Nia’s shoulders. “Here you go. Warm and snug.”

Benny yawned. “I think I need a rest.”

“Listening to your body is smart,” Kiki said. “Let’s have a quiet minute.”

They sat still. The breeze made the wind chime sing, ting-ting-ting. Kiki closed her eyes. She thought of her list. Breathe. Stretch. Sip water. Rest. Listen kindly. Her heart felt calm and strong.

After the quiet minute, Benny stood taller. “I feel ready to explore again!”

Nia flapped her wings. “I feel ready to sing again!”

Kiki smiled. “I feel ready to help again.”

Benny looked at Kiki’s chalkboard. “Can I make a list too?”

“Of course,” Kiki said. She handed him a tiny piece of chalk and a small pebble for writing. Benny wrote: “Breathe. Try again. Be gentle.” Nia added: “Sip. Sing softly.”

They all giggled. “We did it,” Nia chirped.

“We learned,” Benny cheered.

“We took care of ourselves,” Kiki said. “When we care for ourselves, we have energy to be kind and curious. We can keep trying, even when it is hard.”

The sun peeked out again, warm and bright. Kiki rocked in her chair and felt peaceful and proud. “This porch is a good place for self-care,” she whispered.

Benny tapped a happy tap. “Tap, tap.”

Nia sang a happy note. “Tweet!”

Kiki took a deep breath and closed her eyes for one more quiet moment. Then she opened them wide. “Friends, let’s remember our lesson: Take care of your body and your feelings. Be curious. Keep trying. Be gentle with others and yourself.”

They all nodded together.

The End.
