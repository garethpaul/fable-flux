# Mia the Monkey Builds a Team

Mia was a courageous monkey with bright eyes and warm brown fur. She loved to try new things. One sunny day, she danced into the creative art room at Sunshine School. The room smelled like fresh paper and fruity markers. Colorful paints sparkled in little cups. Glue sticks, glitter, and cardboard boxes were stacked high like tiny mountains.

“Good morning, art room!” Mia giggled. “Let’s make something amazing!”

On the wall was a sign: “Build Your Own Game!” Mia clapped her hands. “A game? Like sports? I can do that!”

She looked around. Children of all kinds were busy. A tall giraffe painted with long strokes. A tiny turtle tapped-tapped stickers onto a box. A zebra with zigzag stripes measured a ribbon, nose crinkled.

Mia’s tail swished. “I will build a bouncing ball game,” she said. “We can roll balls through tunnels and score points!”

She set out cardboard tubes, tape, and paper cups. She drew happy stars with a lemon-yellow crayon. “Who wants to help?” she called.

“I do,” said Zuri the zebra. “I like to run fast! Sports are fun.”

“I do,” said Tito the turtle. “I am slow but careful.”

“And me!” said Gia the giraffe, bending her long neck. “I can reach high places.”

Mia smiled. “We are different. That is great! We can all help.”

They started building. Tape made sticky shhh sounds. Scissors went snip-snip. The cardboard smelled like dry leaves. Mia rolled a bouncy ball. “Let’s test,” she said.

The ball plunked into a cup and toppled the whole tower. Cups fell, and glitter puffed into the air like tiny stars.

“Oh no,” whispered Tito. “It fell down.”

Mia felt her cheeks get warm. Her heart thumped. She took a deep breath. “It’s okay,” she said. “We can improve it. We can make it better.”

“How?” asked Zuri.

Mia thought. She tapped her chin. “We need stronger walls. And wider tunnels. We will build again.”

They tried again. Zuri held the tape. Tito pressed the pieces gently. Gia used her long neck to hold a tube up high while Mia added extra tape. They made the tunnel wider. They used two cups to make one strong cup. They added a soft landing made from a sponge.

“Test number two,” Mia said. She rolled the ball. It zoomed through the tunnel, but it got stuck. “Stuck,” said Tito with big eyes.

Mia frowned a little. She felt a tiny squeeze in her tummy. “That’s okay,” she said softly. “We are learning.”

Zuri tapped her hooves. “Maybe the ball needs a little ramp.”

“A ramp!” Mia brightened. “Great idea.”

They folded a piece of cardboard. Snip. Bend. Tape. Now they had a small ramp. Gia decorated it with bright blue swirls. “It looks fast,” Gia said, smiling.

“Test number three,” Mia said. Her voice was steady and brave. She rolled the ball. It zipped up the ramp, through the tunnel, and into the cup. Plunk! The tower stayed up.

“We did it!” cheered Mia.

“Teamwork!” said Zuri.

“Slow and careful helps,” Tito added.

“And long necks can help too,” Gia giggled.

Mia grinned. “Let’s make it into a real sport. We can count points. We can take turns.”

They made a score sign with big numbers. The markers smelled sweet like berries. They drew stars for ten points and hearts for five. Mia showed the rules with simple pictures. “Roll, ramp, tunnel, cup,” she said, pointing.

Children lined up. A panda with gentle paws rolled first. Plunk! Everyone clapped. A rabbit with long ears tried next. The ball wiggled and wobbled. It missed the cup and rolled across the floor.

“It’s okay,” Mia said kindly. “Try again. We can improve with practice.”

The rabbit smiled and tried again. Plunk! “I did it!”

Mia took her turn. Her hands shook a little. She breathed in. The room smelled like orange paint. She breathed out. “I can do it,” she whispered. She rolled the ball. It flew up the ramp and landed just on the edge. Teeter, teeter… then it fell out.

Mia’s shoulders drooped. For a moment, she felt small. Then she remembered. “I am brave when I try again.”

She adjusted the ramp by a tiny bit. “Little by little,” she said. She rolled the ball. Plunk! It landed in the cup. Cheers filled the room.

Coach Lila walked in. She wore a whistle and a big smile. “What a wonderful game,” she said. “You built it together. You improved it step by step.”

Mia stood tall. “We made mistakes,” she said. “But we learned. We kept trying.”

Coach Lila nodded. “That is how we grow stronger. In sports and in life, improvement is a journey.”

They played more rounds. They took turns. They high-fived. Some rolls were fast. Some rolls were slow. All rolls were welcome. The room was full of claps, giggles, and happy shouts. Everyone’s faces glowed with pride.

At clean-up time, Mia touched the game gently. “Thank you,” she whispered. “You helped us learn.”

Tito stacked cups. Zuri rolled up tape. Gia wiped the table with warm, soapy water. The bubbles smelled like lemons. The art room looked neat and shiny again.

Mia looked at her friends. “We are all different,” she said. “We helped in different ways. That made our game better.”

“Yes,” said Gia. “Together, we can build big things.”

“And keep improving,” added Tito.

Mia smiled. Her brave heart felt calm and bright. “When we try, practice, and help each other, we can do amazing things.”

The End.
