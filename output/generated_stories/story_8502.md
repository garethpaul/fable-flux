# Ellie's Big Steps

The warm morning sun painted golden stripes across the peaceful front porch. Little Ellie the elephant calf sat on her favorite cushion, watching butterflies dance in the garden. Her trunk swayed gently as she hummed a soft tune.

"Good morning, sweetie," said Mama Elephant, stepping onto the porch with a smile. "Are you ready for your morning walk today?"

Ellie's big ears drooped a little. Yesterday, she had tried to walk down the front steps by herself, but her legs felt wobbly. She had sat down with a thump instead of walking gracefully like Mama.

"I don't know, Mama," Ellie whispered. "What if I fall again?"

Mama's trunk gently touched Ellie's forehead. "Remember what we say in our family? 'Big steps start with little tries.'"

Ellie loved how Mama always used special words to help her feel brave. She repeated the phrase slowly, tasting each word. "Big steps... start with... little tries."

"That's right, my clever girl," Mama said proudly. "And what's our other saying?"

"Every elephant learns at their own pace," Ellie recited, her voice growing stronger.

Mama nodded. "Exactly! Now, shall we practice together?"

Ellie stood up on her sturdy legs. The wooden porch felt smooth under her feet. She could smell the sweet jasmine flowers blooming nearby and hear the gentle rustle of leaves in the breeze.

"First, let's do our morning stretches," Mama suggested. "Reach your trunk up high!"

Ellie stretched her little trunk toward the sky. It felt good to move her muscles. "Like this, Mama?"

"Perfect! Now touch your toes."

Ellie bent down carefully, her trunk curling to tap her front feet. The stretching made her feel warm and ready.

"Now for the steps," Mama said gently. "Remember, I'm right here beside you."

Ellie looked at the three wooden steps leading down to the garden path. They seemed much smaller now than they had yesterday. She took a deep breath, filling her lungs with the fresh morning air.

"One step," she said aloud, placing her front foot carefully on the first step.

"Wonderful!" Mama cheered. "How does that feel?"

"Good! My foot feels strong," Ellie said with surprise.

"Two steps," Ellie continued, moving to the second step. Her legs felt steadier now.

"You're doing beautifully," Mama encouraged. "What do you notice about walking slowly?"

Ellie thought for a moment. "I can feel my feet better. And I can look around at the pretty flowers."

"That's very wise, Ellie. Taking our time helps us enjoy the journey."

"Three steps!" Ellie announced as she reached the bottom. She had done it! Her whole body felt proud and strong.

"I did it, Mama! I walked down all by myself!"

Mama trumpeted softly with joy. "You certainly did! And do you know what the best part is?"

"What?" Ellie asked, her eyes bright with happiness.

"You didn't give up when it felt hard. That's what makes you resilient – you keep trying even when something seems difficult."

Ellie loved learning new words. "Re-sil-ient," she repeated carefully. "That means I'm brave and I don't give up!"

"Exactly right," Mama said. "And now you know you can trust your strong legs to carry you safely."

As they walked together through the garden, Ellie felt proud of her big accomplishment. She had learned that taking small steps and practicing with patience could help her do amazing things.

"Tomorrow, can we practice walking up the steps too?" Ellie asked eagerly.

Mama smiled. "Of course, my resilient little elephant. Big steps start with little tries, remember?"

The End.
