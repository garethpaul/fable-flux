# Benny Bunny Cleans the Keyboard

Benny Bunny lived in a bright home with a small home office. The office smelled like paper and pencils. The desk was smooth and cool. The computer made tiny humming sounds, like a friendly bee. Benny was a determined rabbit. He liked to help.

One morning, Benny wiggled his whiskers and hopped into the office. “I will help Mama work!” he said. His nose twitched. He looked at the keyboard. The keys were dusty. There were little crumbs in the spaces. The mouse felt sticky.

“Uh-oh,” Benny said softly. “This is not clean.”

Mama Rabbit walked in with a cup of warm tea. The tea smelled like lemon. “Good morning, Benny,” she said. “I see you looking closely.”

Benny pointed with his paw. “Mama, the keyboard is dirty. That is not healthy.”

Mama nodded. “You are right. We use our hands here. Germs can hide in dust and crumbs. Clean hands and a clean space help us stay healthy.”

Benny stood tall. “I can fix it! I am a determined helper.”

Mama smiled. “We will do it together. First, we talk. Then, we plan. Then, we clean.”

Benny took a deep breath. “I feel worried about the germs,” he said. “I want us to be safe.”

Mama rubbed his ear gently. “Thank you for telling me with calm words. That is peaceful talking. I hear you.”

They made a plan. Mama said, “Step one: Wash our paws. Step two: Turn off the computer. Step three: Use a soft cloth. Step four: Throw away crumbs. Step five: Wash again.”

Benny nodded. “I can do that.”

They went to the sink. The water was warm and splashy. “Wet, soap, scrub, rinse, dry,” Mama sang.

Benny sang too. “Wet, soap, scrub, rinse, dry!” He scrubbed each paw. Bubbles smelled like oranges. The bubbles tickled. Benny giggled.

Back in the office, the room felt bright and brave. “Click,” Mama turned off the computer. The screen went dark and quiet.

Benny picked up a tiny brush. Swish, swish, swish. He brushed the crumbs out from the keys. Little crumbs fell like tiny snow. “I am doing it!” Benny said. His heart felt proud and warm.

Mama used a soft cloth. Wipe, wipe, wipe. “We keep the cloth a little damp,” she said. “Not too wet.”

Benny looked very close. “Some sticky spot is here,” he said. “I can be patient.” He pressed his lips together and kept going, slow and careful. Wipe, wait, wipe again.

The mouse went from sticky to slick. The keyboard went from dusty to shiny. The desk smelled fresh, like clean cotton.

Then Benny saw a problem. A key looked loose. It wiggled. “Oh no,” he whispered. His ears drooped. “I think I broke it.”

He felt a heavy lump in his tummy. He wanted to hide under the chair. He took a small breath. Then another. He remembered: talk first, stay calm.

Benny looked at Mama. “I feel scared,” he said. “I touched the key, and now it wiggles. I did not mean to. I want to fix it.”

Mama knelt down. “Thank you for telling the truth,” she said. “You are brave. Let’s look.”

They checked the key together. “It is not broken,” Mama said. “It popped up a little. We can press it back.” She showed him how to line it up. Click! The key sat snug again.

Benny smiled big. “We solved the problem!”

“You were patient,” Mama said. “That is resilience. You kept trying, even when it felt hard.”

They finished cleaning. Then they washed paws again. Wet, soap, scrub, rinse, dry. Benny hummed the little song and watched the bubbles dance. The water felt warm. The towel felt soft and fluffy.

Mama turned on the computer. The gentle hum returned. The keys looked bright. “Thank you, Benny,” she said. “You helped our home office be healthy.”

Benny stood on his tiptoes. He sniffed the clean air. “It smells nice,” he said. “It looks shiny. It feels safe.”

Mama hugged him. Her fur was cozy. “I am grateful for you,” she said.

Benny’s eyes sparkled. “I am grateful for learning,” he said. “I learned to wash my paws. I learned to clean gently. I learned to speak calmly. I learned to keep trying.”

He made a little sign to tape on the wall. The letters were big and friendly. It said: “Clean hands, clean keys, happy bunny!”

Every day after that, Benny sang his song before he touched the keyboard.
“Wet, soap, scrub, rinse, dry!”
He cleaned up little messes right away.
Swish, wipe, swish!

When crumbs came, Benny said, “Let’s make it clean.” When he felt worried, he took a breath and spoke softly. When a small problem popped up, he tried again.

Benny’s home office stayed bright and healthy. Benny felt strong, kind, and proud.

And the keyboard? It went clickity-clack, clean and happy.

The End.
