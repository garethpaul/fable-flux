# Hopscotch Plans at the Dinner Table

Bun-Bun the rabbit sat in the bright dining room. The table smelled like warm carrots and herb soup. The window was open, and a soft breeze tickled Bun-Bun’s whiskers. Outside, the grass looked shiny and green. Birds chirped, “Chee-chee!” in the trees.

Mama Rabbit set a bowl in front of Bun-Bun. “Carrot soup and salad,” she said. “After dinner, you can play outside.”

Bun-Bun’s ears perked up. “I want to hop outside now,” Bun-Bun said. “I want to play hopscotch and leaf chase!”

Mama smiled. “I hear your big feelings,” she said. “It’s hard to wait. Let’s use a plan. Eat dinner, then play. Plans help us do big fun things.”

Bun-Bun wiggled in the chair. “I feel bouncy. My tummy feels twisty,” Bun-Bun said.

“That feeling is excitement,” Mama said. “Take a slow breath in, and a long breath out.”

Bun-Bun breathed in through a tiny pink nose. The soup smelled sweet and cozy. “Mmm,” Bun-Bun said, calmer now. “I can wait with a plan.”

Mama pointed to a small notepad on the table. “Let’s make an Outdoor Plan.”

Bun-Bun’s eyes grew wide. “A plan?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “A plan is a map for our time.”

Bun-Bun dipped a carrot stick in soup and crunched. “What’s first?”

Mama held up a paw. “Step one: Eat our dinner to get strong.”

Bun-Bun nodded. “Step two: Put on sun hat and sneakers.”

“Step three: Fill the water bottle,” Mama said.

Bun-Bun’s whiskers twitched. “Step four: Choose a yard game.”

Mama tapped the table. “Step five: Play outside with safe, kind moves.”

Bun-Bun clapped. “We have a plan!” The words felt good, like a gentle drum in Bun-Bun’s chest. “I can do it.”

Bun-Bun ate soup and salad, one spoon, one bite, steady and proud. The warm soup made Bun-Bun feel strong and ready. The salad leaves were crisp. “Crunch, crunch,” they said.

After dinner, Bun-Bun put on a yellow sun hat and tiny blue sneakers. “Shoes are snug. Hat is on,” Bun-Bun said.

Mama filled a little bottle. “Water is ready. Now, pick a game.”

Bun-Bun thought. The grass outside was soft. The driveway was smooth. “Let’s play hopscotch!” Bun-Bun said. “We can draw boxes with chalk.”

Mama smiled. “Great choice. That is outdoor-activity. It moves your body and your brain.”

They carried chalk to the yard. The evening air was cool and smelled like mint from the garden. The sky was pink and gold. Bun-Bun drew a line of squares and numbers. “One, two, three,” Bun-Bun counted.

“Before we hop, what is our strategy?” Mama asked.

Bun-Bun tilted a head. “Strategy?”

“A strategy is a smart way to do something,” Mama said. “When you hop, look at the next square. Keep your feet light. If you wobble, pause and breathe.”

Bun-Bun nodded. “My strategy: eyes forward, light feet, pause when wobbly.”

Bun-Bun tossed a little pinecone to square one. “Plunk!” Bun-Bun hopped on square two, then three. The ground was cool under Bun-Bun’s sneakers. A bird sang. The pinecone rolled a tiny bit.

On square five, Bun-Bun wobbled. “Oh!” The ears flopped. The heart started to race. “I feel frustrated,” Bun-Bun said, noticing. “My tummy feels tight.”

Mama held up a paw. “You noticed your feeling. That is brave. Use your strategy.”

Bun-Bun took a slow breath. In. Out. The wobble got smaller. “Eyes forward, light feet,” Bun-Bun whispered.

Hop, hop, hop! Bun-Bun reached the end and turned. The breeze lifted the sun hat just a little. Bun-Bun giggled and finished with a big hop to the start.

“I did it!” Bun-Bun cheered. “My plan helped me. My strategy helped me. My feelings are calm now.”

Mama clapped. “You used outdoor play to move your body and your feelings. You used a plan to stay safe and steady.”

“Can we play leaf chase next?” Bun-Bun asked. Leaves skittered across the grass. “Swish-swish!”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Let’s add water sips, too.”

They ran gently after the leaves. Bun-Bun laughed as the sneakers went tap-tap on the path. When Bun-Bun felt warm, Bun-Bun said, “Pause.” One sip of water. One breath. “I’m ready again!”

Soon, the sky turned purple. Crickets began to sing. “Chirp-chirp,” they said.

Mama touched Bun-Bun’s shoulder. “It’s time to go in.”

Bun-Bun looked at the hopscotch squares and the quiet yard. “I feel a little sad. I want more play,” Bun-Bun said softly.

“It’s okay to feel sad,” Mama said. “You can hold that feeling and make a new plan.”

Bun-Bun thought. “Tomorrow after dinner, we can play again. We will use our plan!”

Back in the dining room, the table smelled like mint tea. Bun-Bun sat and sipped warm tea. The chair felt smooth under soft fur. Bun-Bun smiled. “Today I learned: Outdoor-activity helps my body and my big feelings. A plan and a strategy help me do hard things. Breathing helps me when I wobble.”

Mama hugged Bun-Bun. “That is a wise lesson, my determined rabbit.”

Bun-Bun’s ears wiggled. “I am proud of me.”

The End.
