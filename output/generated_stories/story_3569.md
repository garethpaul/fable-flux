# The Little Leader in the Home Office

In a quiet home office, a kind mouse named Miri lived behind a warm, humming computer. The room smelled like paper and lemon cleaner. Sunlight made stripes on the carpet. The keyboard went click-clack, and the printer purred like a sleepy cat.

On the desk was a little basket of crayons, sticky notes, and tiny paper clips. Miri loved to help keep everything tidy. She was small, soft, and silvery gray. Her whiskers twitched when she was excited.

One busy morning, Miri peeked out and gasped. “Oh! What a mess,” she squeaked. The crayons were everywhere. Sticky notes were on the floor. The papers were a jumble. Miri’s tiny paws tingled. “We need to clean before the big video call,” she said.

Her friends popped out to see. Pip the chipmunk skittered onto the chair. “I can stack papers! I am fast!” he said, his tail flicking.

Dottie the ladybug fluttered near the lamp. “I can move small things,” she buzzed in her tiny voice.

Bunty the bunny hopped close to the basket. “I can carry heavy things,” she said, ears tall and proud.

Miri looked at the messy desk and felt a flutter in her tummy. She wanted to help. She wanted everyone to help. But everyone was talking at once.

“I will do the papers!” Pip squeaked louder.

“No, me!” said Bunty. “I am strong.”

Dottie buzzed, “I can do the papers, too!”

Everyone reached for the same pile. Papers slipped. Crayons rolled. The printer beeped, beeped. The mess got bigger. Miri’s whiskers drooped. “Oh dear,” she said softly.

Miri took a deep breath. She listened to the soft whirr of the computer. She heard the tick-tock of a little desk clock. She smelled lemon and fresh paper. She put her paws on her heart. “Kind mouse, be brave,” she whispered to herself. “Be a leader.”

She climbed onto a sticky note pad like a tiny stage. “Friends,” she called gently, “let’s take turns.”

Pip, Dottie, and Bunty stopped. Their eyes turned to Miri.

“We all want to help,” Miri said. “That is wonderful. But we have a problem. We are doing the same job at the same time. It makes a big mess.”

“What do we do?” asked Dottie, landing softly on the pencil cup.

“We make a plan,” said Miri. “I will be the leader. Is that okay?”

Pip nodded. “Okay!”

Bunty smiled. “Okay!”

Dottie twirled her tiny wings. “Okay!”

Miri’s voice was calm and kind. “We will take turns. We will volunteer for jobs we do best.”

“I volunteer for papers,” said Pip, raising a tiny paw.

“I volunteer for heavy things,” said Bunty, thumping her big, soft foot.

“I volunteer for tiny bits,” buzzed Dottie, patting a paper clip.

Miri clapped her paws. “Great choices! Pip, you go first. Count to ten while you stack papers. Dottie, you go second. Clip them and smooth them. Bunty, you go last. Carry the stacks to the tray.”

“What is your job, Miri?” asked Pip.

“I will guide. I will hold the list. I will cheer. I will help anyone who needs help,” said Miri. “Leaders listen and help.”

The work began.

Pip stacked. “One, two, three…” His little paws slid the papers, shhh, shhh. He made a neat pile.

“Time!” called Miri softly. “Great job, Pip. Dottie, your turn.”

Dottie buzzed down. Click! Clip! Click! Clip! She straightened the edges. “All smooth,” she sang.

“Bunty, your turn,” said Miri.

Bunty scooped the stacks with gentle paws. Thump, hop, thump. She placed them in the tray. “Safe and tidy,” she said.

Crayons still rolled on the floor. Miri pointed. “Who will volunteer for crayons?”

“I will!” said Pip. “I want a small job now.”

“Me too!” said Dottie.

“Let’s take turns again,” said Miri. “Pip, you roll them this way. Dottie, you drop them in the basket.”

Pip rolled the red crayon. It went rrrrroll. Dottie caught it. Plop! It landed in the basket. Orange. Plop! Blue. Plop! Yellow. Plop!

Bunty swept the sticky notes into a colorful stack. Swish, swish. She giggled. “They smell like lemons!”

Soon the desk was clean. The computer light blinked softly. The printer hummed a happy hum. The air felt fresh and bright.

Miri looked around. Her heart felt warm. “We solved the problem,” she said. “We took turns. We volunteered. We followed the plan.”

Pip puffed out his chest. “You were a good leader, Miri.”

Dottie made a tiny heart shape with her wings. “You listened to everyone.”

Bunty nodded. “You helped us help.”

A voice came from the speaker. “Video call starting,” it chimed.

Miri smiled. “We are ready,” she said. “Friends, thank you.”

They all stood together, paws and wings and ears side by side. They felt proud. They felt calm. The room was neat and bright.

Miri whispered, “Being a leader means being kind, making a plan, and letting everyone shine. We can all be leaders when we help and take turns.”

Pip, Dottie, and Bunty whispered back, “We can be leaders too.”

The End.
