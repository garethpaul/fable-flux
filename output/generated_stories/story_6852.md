# Benny Beaver and the Balcony Words

Benny the busy beaver loved mornings on the balcony garden. He wore a tiny straw hat and a bright blue apron. The sun felt warm on his fur. The air smelled like mint and tomatoes. Little bees buzzed. The leaves rustled like soft whispers.

“Today I will help,” Benny said. “I will water, I will weed, and I will read.”

Read? Yes! On the balcony, Mama had placed small signs near each plant. The signs had words. Benny liked words. He liked how they sounded. He liked how they told him things.

Benny picked up a watering can. “First,” he said, “I will follow the directions.” He looked at a sign with big letters: MINT. Under it, tiny letters said, “Little water.” Benny whispered the word: “Mint.” He tapped his lips. “Mmm—mint! The word feels cool, like the smell.” He giggled and gave the mint a small sip. “Little water.”

Next, Benny found a sign: TOMATO. Under it, the letters said, “More water.” He read slowly, “To-ma-to.” He patted his tummy. “The word is round and bouncy, like the fruit!” Benny poured more water and smiled when the soil drank it up.

A small snail peeked from a pot. “Hello,” said the snail. “I am Sam. Can I help?”

“Yes, Sam!” said Benny. “We can work together. We will listen, look, and read. We will be a good team.”

Sam slid closer and looked at another sign: BASIL. The little letters said, “Morning water.” Benny said the word out loud. “Ba-sil.” He sang it like a song. “Ba-sil, ba-sil.” He felt the word on his tongue, like a tiny dance. “Morning water,” he read. “It is morning now.” He watered gently.

Just then, a sparrow named Pip landed on the railing. “Tweet! I like songs,” Pip chirped. “What can I do?”

“You can read the clouds,” Benny said. “Tell us if rain is coming.”

Pip puffed up proudly. “Tweet! I will look.” Pip looked at the sky. “Blue and bright. No big clouds.”

“Thank you,” said Benny. “We are cooperating. We are helping the plants together.”

Benny saw a new pot with a picture of a pepper and a sign that said: PEPPER. The small letters read, “No water today.” Benny read it slowly. “Pep-per. No water today.” He nodded. “We will follow the directions.” He put the can down.

Sam the snail pointed his feelers at a tiny sprout with a sign: DILL. The letters said, “Soft water.” Benny touched the sprout. It felt feathery. He whispered, “Dill.” The word felt light, like a breath. He poured water like rain from a cloud, soft and gentle.

A fly buzzed past a little cup and nudged a marker into the soil. The sign for CARROT fell over and landed next to LETTUCE. Benny blinked. “Oh!” he said. “I think something is not right.”

He used logical thinking. “Let me look,” he said. “Let me read. Carrots grow under the soil. Lettuce grows on top. This row has leafy tops. That sign says CARROT, but these are leaves like lettuce.”

Pip peeked. “Tweet! The leaves look like fans. Lettuce!”

Sam nodded slowly. “Yes, lettuce. The sign moved.”

Benny set the signs straight. “This is lettuce. This is carrot.” He smiled. “Words help us know. Words guide us.”

Benny found a small basket with a note. The note said, “Please pick three cherry tomatoes for Granny.” Benny read it out loud. “Please pick three cherry tomatoes for Granny.”

He counted on his paws. “One, two, three.” He picked three red, shiny cherries. They felt smooth and warm from the sun. He placed them in the basket. “We are volunteering,” he said. “We are helping Granny.”

Pip flapped. “Tweet! Good helping!” Sam clapped his feelers together.

Benny noticed a plant with a sign: LAVENDER. The little letters said, “No picking. Smell gently.” Benny leaned close and sniffed. “Ahhh.” The smell was sweet and calm like bedtime. He whispered the word, “La-ven-der.” The word felt long and soft, like a lullaby. He didn’t pick. He followed the directions and just enjoyed the smell.

A breeze brushed the chives, and they chimed against a tiny bell. “Ting-ting,” sang the bell. Benny read the CHIVES sign. “Trim on Friday.” He checked the calendar on the wall. “Today is Wednesday. Not Friday.” He smiled and put the scissors back. “We listen to words. Words are friends. Words tell us when.”

The sun moved higher. The balcony felt warm. Benny’s fur smelled like mint and lavender. His paws were a little wet from the watering can. He looked around. The signs stood tall. The plants looked happy.

Mama stepped onto the balcony. “Oh, Benny! You followed the directions. You used your words. You helped so much.”

Benny’s chest felt big and proud. “We all helped,” he said. “Sam read with his eyes. Pip checked the sky. I read the words. The words helped us think. The words helped us choose.”

Mama nodded. “That is language,” she said. “Language helps us understand. When we read, we learn. When we listen, we solve problems.”

Benny hugged Mama. He looked at the mint and said, “Mint.” He looked at the tomato and said, “Tomato.” He looked at lavender and breathed in. “Lavender.” The words felt like colors and songs in his mouth.

Benny smiled at his friends. “Let’s share words every day,” he said. “We can read. We can think. We can follow directions. We can help.”

Pip chirped. “Tweet! Words are wonderful.” Sam whispered, “Words guide us.”

Benny tipped his hat. “Thank you, words,” he said.

The End.
