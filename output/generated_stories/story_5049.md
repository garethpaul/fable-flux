# Rosie Rabbit's Cookie Challenge

Rosie Rabbit wiggled her pink nose as she hopped into the warm kitchen. The smell of fresh cookies filled the air, making her tummy rumble with excitement. On the counter sat a tall glass jar filled with golden chocolate chip cookies.

"Oh my!" said Rosie, standing on her tippy-toes. "Those cookies smell so yummy!" But no matter how high she stretched her little paws, the jar was much too high to reach.

Rosie sat down and tapped her foot. "Hmm," she said, looking around the cozy kitchen. "I really want a cookie, but I need to think of a smart way to get one."

First, Rosie tried jumping. "One, two, three... jump!" But even her highest bunny hop wasn't enough. The jar stayed far above her head.

"That didn't work," Rosie said to herself. "But I won't give up! Let me try something else."

Next, she spotted a wooden spoon on the counter. Rosie stood on her tippy-toes again and stretched the spoon toward the jar. But the spoon was too short, and she couldn't quite reach.

"Hmm," said Rosie, putting the spoon down. "I need a better plan."

Rosie looked around the kitchen with her bright eyes. She saw a sturdy step stool tucked under the sink. "Aha!" she exclaimed. "That might help me!"

But when Rosie tried to move the step stool, it was too heavy for her small paws. She pushed and pushed, but it barely budged.

"This is tricky," said Rosie, sitting down to think. "Maybe I need to ask for help."

Just then, Mama Rabbit hopped into the kitchen, carrying a basket of fresh carrots. "Hello, my little bunny," she said with a warm smile. "What are you doing?"

Rosie took a deep breath. "Mama, I really want a cookie from that jar, but it's too high. I tried jumping and using a spoon, but nothing worked. Could you please help me move the step stool?"

Mama Rabbit's eyes twinkled. "I'm so proud of you, Rosie! You tried different ways to solve your problem, and when you needed help, you asked nicely. That's very smart thinking!"

Together, they moved the step stool to the counter. Rosie climbed up carefully, holding the sides for safety. Now she could reach the cookie jar perfectly!

"Thank you, Mama!" said Rosie as she opened the jar. The sweet smell of cookies made her whiskers twitch with joy. She took one warm, gooey cookie and climbed down safely.

"You know what, Rosie?" said Mama Rabbit, giving her a gentle hug. "You showed me something very important today. When something seems impossible, we can always find a way if we think carefully and ask for help when we need it."

Rosie took a big bite of her cookie. It was even more delicious than she had imagined! "Mmm! Next time I have a problem, I'll remember to try different ideas and ask for help if I need it."

"That's my smart little bunny," said Mama Rabbit. "Would you like to help me make carrot soup for dinner?"

"Yes, please!" said Rosie, finishing her cookie. "And I know just the right step stool to use!"

They spent the rest of the afternoon cooking together in the warm, cozy kitchen, and Rosie felt proud of how she had solved her cookie problem all by herself.

The End.
