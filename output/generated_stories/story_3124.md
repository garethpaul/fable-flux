# Whiskers and the Wobbly Birdhouse

Whiskers the clever cat loved spending time in Grandpa Joe's garage workshop. The garage smelled like wood shavings and paint, and sunlight streamed through the dusty windows, making everything sparkle.

"Meow! What are you making today, Grandpa Joe?" asked Whiskers, jumping onto the workbench with a soft thud.

Grandpa Joe smiled and patted Whiskers' orange fur. "I'm trying to build a birdhouse for the sparrows in our yard. But look at this mess!" He pointed to a pile of crooked wood pieces. "It keeps falling down. I think I need help."

Whiskers tilted his head and studied the wobbly birdhouse. One wall leaned to the left, the roof was upside down, and the entrance hole was way too big. "Don't worry, Grandpa Joe! I have an idea. Let's work together!"

Just then, they heard tiny chirping sounds from outside. Three little sparrows sat on the windowsill, looking sad and cold.

"Tweet, tweet! We need a warm home for winter," chirped the smallest sparrow. "But we're too little to help build anything."

Whiskers' whiskers twitched with excitement. "Everyone can help! Building is more fun when we all work together."

"But I'm just a small bird," worried the tiny sparrow. "What can I do?"

"You can be our measuring helper!" said Whiskers bravely. "Hop through that entrance hole and tell us if it's the right size."

The little sparrow's eyes lit up. "Really? I can help too?"

Grandpa Joe nodded. "Of course! Every helper is important."

Whiskers showed everyone how to work as a team. "First, let's make the walls straight," he said, using his paws to hold the wood steady. The smell of fresh pine filled the air as they worked.

The biggest sparrow held nails in his beak. "Tweet! I can carry these!"

The middle sparrow used her tiny feet to steady small pieces. "I'll hold this corner!"

The smallest sparrow flew in and out of the entrance hole. "It's perfect now! Not too big, not too small!"

Grandpa Joe hammered carefully while Whiskers guided each piece into place. "Tap, tap, tap!" went the hammer. The workshop filled with happy sounds of building and teamwork.

"Now for the roof!" announced Whiskers. He climbed up high and showed everyone how to angle the wood so rain would roll off. His orange tail swished with pride as he balanced carefully.

The sparrows chirped encouragement. "You're so brave, Whiskers! We could never climb that high!"

"And you're so good at fitting through small spaces!" replied Whiskers. "We all have special talents!"

Finally, they painted the birdhouse bright yellow like sunshine. The paint smelled fresh and clean, and their paws and feathers got delightfully messy.

When they finished, the birdhouse stood tall and strong. It had straight walls, a perfect roof, and an entrance hole that was just the right size.

"We did it!" cheered Whiskers, doing a little dance on the workbench.

The sparrows flew around the beautiful birdhouse, singing with joy. "Thank you for including us! This is the best home ever!"

Grandpa Joe hugged Whiskers. "You taught me something important today. Building isn't just about wood and nails. It's about working together and including everyone."

Whiskers purred loudly. "When we're brave enough to ask for help and kind enough to include others, we can build anything!"

That night, the sparrows slept safely in their new home, and Whiskers curled up happily, dreaming of their next building adventure together.

The End.
