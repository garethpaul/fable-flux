# Ollie Otter’s Treehouse Classroom

Ollie the playful otter loved his treehouse. It smelled like fresh leaves and pine cones. Sunlight slid through the windows and made bright squares on the wooden floor. His books were stacked neatly in a basket. His little chalkboard stood beside a cozy rug. Today, Ollie wanted to have a school day in his treehouse.

“I will learn new things!” Ollie said, wiggling his whiskers. “Learning makes me brave and strong.”

He clapped his paws and looked at his list. He had drawn simple pictures to remember his plan:
- A book for story time.
- A toothbrush for clean time.
- A puzzle piece for thinking time.

“First, story time!” Ollie opened a big picture book. The pages felt smooth. He traced the bright letters with his paw. “A, B, C,” he said slowly. “Letters make words. Words tell stories.”

A soft breeze tickled his nose. He giggled. “My treehouse is a good classroom,” he said. “It is quiet. It is bright. I can think here.”

After the story, Ollie went to clean time. He had a tiny sink in a corner and a cup with a blue toothbrush. He turned the handle. Water sang, splash-splash, and felt cool on his paws.

“First, soap on hands,” he said. He rubbed his paws. “Front, back, between, and thumbs.” Bubbles smelled like oranges. He counted to twenty. “One, two, three…” His paws squeaked when he rinsed them. He smiled. “Clean paws help me stay healthy.”

Then he brushed his teeth. “Small circles,” he said, looking in a little mirror. “Up teeth, down teeth, and tongue.” The mint tasted tingly and fresh. He swished and spit. “Ahh! Sparkly clean.”

“Now I am ready for thinking time,” Ollie said proudly. He set a puzzle on the rug. The puzzle showed a rainbow fish, a leaf, and a moon. But something was wrong. One piece was missing.

Ollie’s tummy felt twisty. “Oh no,” he whispered. “Where is the last piece?” He took a deep breath. “I can solve this. I can use my thinking steps.”

He held up one paw. “Step one: Ask a clear question. Where did I last see the piece?”

He held up a second paw. “Step two: Look for clues.”

He looked around the treehouse. He used his senses. He listened. The tree leaves rustled shhh-shhh. He sniffed. He smelled oranges from the soap and a little nutty smell from acorns. He looked low and high. He found a red crayon under the rug. He found a tiny feather by the window. But no puzzle piece.

He held up a third paw. “Step three: Make a plan.” He drew a simple map on his chalkboard. “I will check the book basket, then the window ledge, then my backpack.”

He checked the book basket. “Not here.” He checked the window ledge. “Not here.” He opened his backpack. Inside, he found a napkin, a snack apple, and… a muddy leaf.

Ollie blinked. “Muddy? In my classroom?” His whiskers twitched. “I must be brave and look outside.”

He opened the little door and stepped onto the treehouse porch. The bark under his paws felt rough. A squirrel chattered nearby. “Hello, Squeaky,” Ollie said. “Have you seen a puzzle piece?”

Squeaky tilted his head. “Chee-chee?” He pointed his tiny paw toward a low branch.

Ollie looked. There, beside a strip of shiny tape, was the missing puzzle piece, stuck to the branch. It had a smudge of mud on it.

Ollie felt a little flutter in his chest. “That branch is high,” he said softly. He placed a paw on the railing. Then he took a brave breath. “I can do this. I will be careful.”

He checked his plan like a teacher. “Step one: Safety. Hold the railing. Step two: Move slowly. Step three: Ask for help if needed.”

“Squeaky, can you hold my chalk?” Squeaky chirped and held the chalk carefully.

Ollie reached out. The wind brushed his fur. He stretched his paw, slow and steady. “Almost there…” he whispered. He touched the piece, peeled it gently, and brought it back. “I did it!”

Back inside, Ollie washed the muddy piece with a damp cloth. “Clean and ready,” he said. He also washed his paws again. “Learning and hygiene go together.”

He clicked the last piece into the puzzle. Snap! The rainbow fish smiled up at him. Ollie’s heart felt warm and big.

“Today I learned many things,” he said. He wrote on the chalkboard in big, simple words:
- Be clean.
- Think in steps.
- Be brave and careful.
- Believe in you.

Squeaky clapped his tiny paws. “Chee-chee!”

Ollie bowed. “Thank you, class,” he giggled. “I am proud of me.”

He packed his toothbrush, soap, and book neatly. He sang a little song:
“Wash, brush, read, and see,
Smart and clean is brave like me!”

The sun made golden stripes on the floor. Ollie felt calm and happy. He hugged Squeaky and looked at his finished puzzle. He knew he could learn new things every day.

The lesson: Education helps us solve problems. Clean habits keep us healthy. Thinking in steps and being brave build self-esteem.

Ollie smiled. “Tomorrow, more learning,” he said. “Today, I did my best.”

The End.
