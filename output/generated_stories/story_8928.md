# The Tortoise Who Tried Again

In a cozy bedroom with soft yellow lamps, a little fan hummed a gentle whirr. The blankets were fluffy like clouds, and the rug felt warm and fuzzy under little feet. On the bed sat a wise tortoise named Tiko. His shell was smooth and green, and his eyes were kind and calm.

Tiko lived with a family who loved stories and bedtime snuggles. Tonight, Tiko wanted to learn something new. On the blanket lay a small wooden puzzle with bright shapes: a red circle, a blue square, and a yellow star.

“I can do it,” Tiko said softly. “Slow and steady.”

He picked up the yellow star with his careful, tiny claws. He tried to fit it in a space. It did not fit. The star bumped the edge and wiggled but would not go in.

Tiko felt his cheeks get warm. His tummy felt tight. “Hmm,” he said. “This is hard.”

From the hallway, the night smelled like warm cocoa. The fan kept humming. The lamp glowed like a cozy sunset. Tiko took a breath. He put the star down and tried the red circle. It rolled and rolled and fell off the bed with a soft plop.

“Oh no,” Tiko whispered. His eyes felt sparkly with tears. “I want to stop.”

Just then, a small voice came from the doorway. It was Mia, the child who shared the bedroom with Tiko. She wore rainbow pajamas and had a sleepy smile. Beside her came Pip, a plush penguin, and Lulu, a fluffy bunny. Everyone was welcome on this bed. Inclusion was the rule.

“Hi, Tiko,” Mia said. “Are you okay?”

Tiko nodded a little. “I feel frustrated,” he said. “The shapes won’t go in.”

Mia sat beside him. The bed creaked a friendly squeak. “It’s okay to feel that way,” she said. “Let’s take a mindful breath.”

“What is a mindful breath?” Tiko asked.

Mia touched her belly. “Breathe in slow like you smell a flower.” She sniffed in. “Breathe out slow like you blow a bubble.” She puffed out.

Tiko tried. In through his nose, he smelled the warm cocoa and clean sheets. Out through his mouth, he blew a gentle bubble breath. His tummy loosened. His shoulders felt softer.

“Again?” Mia asked.

“In… and out,” Tiko said. He smiled a little. “Thank you.”

Pip the penguin flopped onto the rug and nudged the red circle up with his soft wing. “Waddle help,” Pip said in a squeaky play voice. Lulu the bunny leaned close and whispered, “We can do this together.”

Tiko looked at the puzzle. “I will try again.”

He picked up the blue square. He looked for a square space. “I will go slow,” he said. “Slow is okay.”

“Slow is strong,” Mia said. “We can be turtle-strong.”

Tiko lined the edges carefully. Slide, slide—click! The square fit in! Tiko’s eyes shone. “I did it!”

“You did it!” Mia cheered. Pip clapped his flippers. Lulu did a tiny thump with her foot, thump-thump.

Tiko reached for the red circle. “Circle goes in a circle space,” he said. He found the round hole. He tried once. It wiggled. He tried again, turning it just a little. Click! The circle dropped in.

Tiko giggled. The fan made a happy whirr. The lamp made the shapes look shiny and bright. The room felt extra cozy now.

Then came the yellow star again. Tiko held it. His heart beat fast, but it felt brave. “Star, we will try again,” he said.

He looked closely. “The star space has pointy parts,” he said. He lined one point, then another. The star stuck on the last point. It did not go in.

Tiko felt the tight tummy again. “Oh,” he said. “I feel frustrated again.”

Mia nodded. “Feelings come and go,” she said gently. “Let’s do self-care. Sip of water?”

On the nightstand was a small cup with a blue straw. Tiko sipped. The water was cool and sweet. He took one slow breath. He patted his shell with a soft tap-tap. “I am safe,” he whispered. “I can try.”

Pip waddled closer. “Star dance?” he peeped. Lulu wiggled her ears. “Wiggle, wiggle!”

They all did a tiny wiggle dance on the bed. Feet thumped softly on the rug. The blanket rustled like soft leaves. Tiko laughed. His tummy felt light.

“Ready,” Tiko said. “Slow and steady.”

He held the star again. He lined up each point with care. He paused. He breathed in like a flower. He blew out like a bubble. Then he pressed, gentle and firm.

Click!

The star fit perfectly. The puzzle was complete.

Tiko’s smile was big and warm. “I did it! I tried again!”

Mia hugged him gently so his shell felt safe and snug. “You were resilient,” she said. “That means you kept going, even when it was hard. You used mindful breaths. You took care of yourself. You asked for help. You included everyone.”

Tiko nodded. “Resilient,” he said slowly. “I can learn hard things, one slow step at a time.”

The fan hummed a lullaby. The lamp glowed soft. Pip and Lulu snuggled close. Mia pulled up the blanket. The bedroom smelled like cocoa and clean sheets.

Tiko whispered, “When I feel frustrated, I can breathe. I can take a sip. I can ask for help. I can try again.”

Mia whispered back, “And that is how we grow.”

Tiko closed his eyes with a happy sigh. The puzzle lay finished, bright and proud.

The clear lesson glowed in the cozy room: Slow and steady, breathe and try again. That is resilience.

The End.
