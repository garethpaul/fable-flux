# Ruby Rabbit's Colorful Solution

In a cozy study corner filled with books and crayons, lived a little rabbit named Ruby. Ruby had soft brown fur and the biggest, brightest eyes you ever did see. She loved to draw pictures with her colorful crayons every single day.

One sunny morning, Ruby hopped into her study corner and found something very sad. Her three best friends - Benny Bear, Lily Lamb, and Max Mouse - were sitting on the soft reading rug with tears in their eyes.

"What's wrong?" asked Ruby, her nose twitching with worry.

"We wanted to make pictures for our mommies," sniffled Benny Bear. "But we don't know how to draw anything pretty."

"I can only make scribbles," said Lily Lamb sadly, holding up a paper covered in messy lines.

"My circles look like squiggly worms," added Max Mouse, his tiny whiskers drooping.

Ruby's heart felt heavy seeing her friends so upset. She looked around her study corner at all the art supplies - the rainbow box of crayons, the stack of white paper, and the jar of colorful markers. Then her eyes lit up like two little stars!

"I have an idea!" Ruby exclaimed, her cotton-tail wiggling with excitement. "I can teach you how to make beautiful pictures!"

Ruby hopped over to her art box and pulled out the biggest piece of paper she could find. "Watch me," she said in her kindest voice. "First, we start with simple shapes."

She picked up a red crayon and drew a big circle. "This can be the sun!" she said. Then she added yellow lines all around it. "And these are the sunshine rays!"

Benny Bear's eyes grew wide. "That's so pretty, Ruby!"

"Now you try," Ruby said, handing Benny a yellow crayon. "Make your own sun."

Benny carefully drew a wobbly circle. It wasn't perfect, but Ruby clapped her paws together. "That's wonderful, Benny! Every sun is special in its own way."

Next, Ruby showed Lily Lamb how to draw a simple flower. "Start with a circle in the middle," Ruby explained, "then add petals all around like this." She drew five pink petals around a yellow center.

Lily tried it with her purple crayon. Her petals were different sizes, but Ruby smiled big. "Look how unique your flower is, Lily! Purple is my favorite color for flowers."

For Max Mouse, Ruby demonstrated how to draw a house. "A square for the walls, a triangle for the roof, and a rectangle for the door," she said, drawing each shape slowly so Max could see.

Max picked up a blue crayon and carefully copied Ruby's steps. His house was small and crooked, but it had so much character!

"You're all such amazing artists!" Ruby cheered, doing a little bunny hop of joy.

Soon, all four friends were drawing together. The study corner filled with the sound of giggles and the sweet smell of waxy crayons. Ruby helped each friend add details to their pictures - clouds in the sky, grass on the ground, and smiling faces on their suns.

"Thank you, Ruby," said Benny, Lily, and Max together. "You're the best teacher ever!"

Ruby's cheeks turned pink with happiness. "When we help each other, we can create the most beautiful things," she said. "And the best part about art is that everyone's pictures are special, just like we are!"

The four friends hung their colorful masterpieces on the study corner wall, creating their very own art gallery. From that day on, they drew pictures together every week, and Ruby always remembered that sharing what you know with others makes everything more wonderful.

The End.
