# Miko's Big Feelings Day

Miko the monkey loved playing in Grandpa Joe's garage workshop. The big room smelled like wood shavings and had shiny tools hanging on the walls. Today, Miko was building a special birdhouse with colorful blocks.

"Almost done!" Miko said happily, carefully placing a red block on top. But just then, his little sister Luna came running in.

"Miko! Miko! Can I play too?" Luna squeaked excitedly.

"No! I'm busy!" Miko said, not looking up from his project.

Luna reached out to touch the pretty birdhouse. "Ooh, so colorful!"

CRASH! The birdhouse tumbled down, blocks scattering everywhere across the concrete floor.

Miko's face turned red. His hands made tight fists. "LUNA! YOU BROKE IT!" he shouted, his voice echoing in the garage. "I WORKED SO HARD!"

Luna's eyes filled with tears. "I'm sorry, Miko! I didn't mean to!"

Miko felt hot and angry all over. His heart was beating fast like a drum. He wanted to yell more and maybe even throw blocks!

Just then, Grandpa Joe walked over with his gentle smile. "Miko, I can see you're feeling very angry," he said in his calm, warm voice. "When we have big angry feelings, what do we do?"

Miko remembered what Grandpa taught him. He sat down on the soft rug and took three deep breaths. "In through my nose... out through my mouth," he whispered. The cool garage air filled his lungs.

"That's right," Grandpa Joe nodded. "Now, let's wash those dusty hands and face. Sometimes cleaning up helps us feel better inside too."

At the little sink in the corner, Miko splashed cool water on his face and scrubbed his hands with soap that smelled like oranges. The water felt refreshing, and he could feel his anger washing away too.

"I'm sorry I yelled, Luna," Miko said, drying his hands with a fluffy towel. "I was angry, but yelling hurt your feelings."

Luna wiped her nose. "I'm sorry I knocked down your birdhouse. I should have asked first."

"You know what?" said Grandpa Joe, his eyes twinkling. "Building together is more fun than building alone. Would you like to make an even bigger birdhouse?"

Miko looked at all the scattered blocks. Then he looked at Luna's hopeful face. "Yes! Luna, you can help me. You're good at finding the pretty colors."

"Really?" Luna's face lit up like sunshine.

Working together, they built the most wonderful birdhouse ever. Luna found sparkly blue blocks while Miko placed them carefully. When Miko felt frustrated again, he remembered to take deep breaths and ask for help instead of getting angry.

"Look how beautiful it is!" Luna clapped her clean hands.

"We make a great team," Miko agreed, giving his sister a gentle hug.

Grandpa Joe smiled proudly. "When we stay calm and work together, we can build amazing things. And Miko, I'm proud of how you handled your big feelings today."

As the sun set through the garage windows, Miko felt happy and peaceful. He learned that angry feelings are okay, but taking deep breaths, staying clean, and being kind to others makes everything better.

"Tomorrow, can we build a castle?" Luna asked.

"Yes!" Miko laughed. "But let's wash our hands first!"

The End.
