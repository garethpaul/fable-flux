# Momo the Brave Monkey and the Wobbly Tower

Momo was a small, courageous monkey with bright eyes and soft, fuzzy fur. Momo lived in a playful living room filled with cozy pillows, colorful blocks, and a big, bouncy rug. Sunlight peeked through the curtains and made little sparkles on the floor. It smelled like warm cookies and clean laundry. It was a perfect day for a big idea.

“I will build the tallest block tower ever!” Momo said, puffing up with courage.

Momo picked up a red block. Tap. Momo set it down. Then a yellow block. Tap. Then blue, green, and purple. Tap, tap, tap. The tower grew tall, tall, tall.

“It is wobbly,” Momo whispered, holding a breath. The tower trembled just a tiny bit, like it felt tickly.

“I can do it,” Momo said. “I am brave.” Momo reached for one more block—an orange one with a little star sticker.

Clatter! The tower fell down in a noisy rumble. Blocks bumped Momo’s toes. Momo’s little heart thumped. Tears poked at the corners of Momo’s eyes.

“I worked so hard,” Momo said in a tiny voice.

Momo sat on the soft rug and hugged a pillow. The pillow smelled like sunshine. The room was quiet, except for the tick-tick of a clock. Momo felt sad, and a little mad, and a little scared to try again.

Just then, a friendly breeze slipped in through the curtain and made it flap softly. Swish, swish. It felt cool on Momo’s cheek. Momo took a slow breath in. Then out.

“I remember a story,” Momo said. Momo liked to tell stories when feelings were big. Stories helped Momo feel brave again.

Momo put a hand on the heart and spoke in a brave storyteller voice. “Once upon a time, there was a little monkey hero who wanted to climb a mountain. The mountain was tall and wobbly. The monkey tried once. Tumble! The monkey tried twice. Tumble, tumble! The monkey took a breath. The monkey said, ‘I can try again.’ The monkey did not give up. The monkey felt strong inside.”

Momo listened to the words. The words felt warm, like a hug. Momo blinked away the tears and stood up.

“I can try again,” Momo said, with a small smile. “I can be patient. I can be careful.”

Momo picked up the blocks. Tap, tap. The red block went down. Momo pressed it flat with gentle fingers. “Stay,” Momo said softly. The yellow block went on top, but this time Momo put it right in the middle. “Balance,” Momo whispered, learning a new word. The blue block went on. Momo checked from the side. “Straight.” The green block went on. Momo wiggled it just a bit until it was snug.

The tower was growing again.

The floor creaked. The tower wobbled. Momo’s tummy flipped. “I feel nervous,” Momo said, naming the feeling. “I will take a calm breath.”

Momo breathed in slowly, smelling the cookie smell. Momo breathed out slowly, feeling the soft rug under little feet.

“I can be brave and gentle,” Momo said.

Clink. Another block. Clink. Another. The tower shimmered in the sun.

Just then, a rubber ball rolled across the rug. Bump, bump. It nudged Momo’s tail. Momo almost jumped!

“Freeze,” Momo told the body. “Steady.” Momo moved the ball away and smiled. “Thank you, body, for stopping.” Momo felt proud. That was resilient—trying again, staying steady, and not giving up.

A tiny block at the very top slipped sideways. “Uh-oh,” Momo said. “It’s okay. I can fix it.” Momo took it off, turned it around, and set it back. Tap.

The tower stood tall. Taller than the couch cushions. Taller than the pile of picture books. It did not wobble now. It looked strong and bright, like a rainbow.

“I did it!” Momo cheered, clapping little hands. The clap sounded crisp and happy. Clap, clap!

Momo danced around the tower with careful steps. The rug felt bouncy under Momo’s toes. The curtains swished like they were clapping, too. Swish, swish!

Momo sat on a pillow and began to tell the story again, but this time, it was Momo’s own story.

“Once upon a time,” Momo said, “there was a courageous monkey named Momo. Momo built a tower. It fell down. Momo felt sad. Momo told a brave story. Momo tried again. Momo used calm breaths. Momo fixed the wobbles. Momo did not give up. Momo built a strong, tall tower!”

Momo smiled big. The smile felt warm all the way to the ears.

Momo looked at the tower and then at the blocks still in the basket. “I can help others try again, too,” Momo said. “I can share my story.”

Momo held up a block and spoke kindly, as if to a friend. “When things fall down, you can take a breath. You can try a new way. You can be brave inside. That is resilience. That means you keep going.”

Momo placed the last block by the tower like a tiny flag. “I am proud of me,” Momo whispered.

The sun made one last sparkle on the tower. The room felt cozy and calm. Momo curled up on the soft rug, hugging the pillow, and listened to the happy tick-tick of the clock.

“I learned that trying again helps me grow,” Momo said. “I learned that stories make me brave.”

Momo closed eyes for a moment and breathed in and out. The tower stood tall, and so did Momo’s courage.

The End.
