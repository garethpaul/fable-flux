# Koala Kiki’s Rooftop Rest and Rescue

Kiki was a calm koala who loved to sit in soft places. One sunny morning, Kiki climbed up to the rooftop garden. The roof was warm and gentle under her paws. A little breeze kissed her nose. She could smell mint leaves and sweet strawberries growing in pots. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” and a tiny wind chime sang, “Ting-ting!”

Kiki smiled. “Good morning, Rooftop,” she said in a soft voice. “Today, I have a plan.”

Kiki had three small goals for the day. She whispered them out loud, tapping each claw to count.
“Goal one: stretch and breathe.”
“Goal two: drink water and eat a healthy snack.”
“Goal three: help a friend if someone needs me.”

She rolled out a small leaf-green mat. “I care for me first,” Kiki said kindly to herself. “When I feel good, I can do good.”

Kiki stood tall, then curled small. She stretched her arms up, up, up. She bent down, down, down to touch her toes. She breathed in, smelling mint. She breathed out, slow and calm. “In… and out,” she hummed. Her shoulders felt light, like feathers.

After stretching, Kiki sipped cool water from a blue cup. “Sip, sip,” she said. The cup felt chilly in her paws. She nibbled a rosy strawberry. It was juicy and sweet. “Mmm,” Kiki smiled. “My body feels happy.”

Just then, Kiki heard a tiny squeak. “Help! Oh, help!” it cried.

Kiki paused and listened. The sound came from behind the herb pots. She walked softly and peeked. There, a little pigeon chick stood on wobbly feet. Its gray feathers were fluffy, and its eyes were wide.

“Hello, little one,” Kiki said in her calm voice. “I am Kiki. Are you okay?”

The chick peeped, “I tried to fly. I am not ready. My wing feels tired. I am scared.”

Kiki knelt down. She felt the sun on her fur and the smooth tile under her knees. She placed a gentle paw near the chick. “I hear you,” she said. “It is okay to rest. Rest is care.”

The chick sniffled. “But I want to fly now.”

Kiki nodded. “I have goals today. Do you want to make goals, too? Little steps can help.”

The chick blinked. “Little steps?”

Kiki smiled. “Yes. Let’s make three birdy goals.” She held up three claws. “Goal one: breathe and feel safe. Goal two: drink some water and rest your wing. Goal three: practice small hops when your body says yes.”

The chick took a shaky breath. “Cheep… cheep.” The sound was softer now.

Kiki looked around the rooftop. The sun warmed her back, and the mint leaves rustled. “Let’s start with breathing.” Kiki breathed slowly. The chick copied. In… out… in… out. The wind chime went, “Ting,” as if it agreed.

“Very good,” Kiki whispered. “Now water.” She dipped a tiny spoon into her cup and dripped cool drops into a bottle cap. The chick sipped. “Sip, sip,” it said, and perked up a little.

“Do you need rest?” Kiki asked kindly.

“Yes, please,” said the chick.

Kiki folded a soft cloth into a nest and set it near a pot of basil. The basil smelled peppery and fresh. She helped the chick snuggle in. “You are brave,” Kiki said. “Brave means caring for your body.”

They sat together. Kiki took one more strawberry. She chewed slowly, listening to the breeze. The chick closed its eyes, then opened them again. “I feel better,” it peeped. “But I still want to fly.”

“We can try small hops,” Kiki said. “Just tiny goals. We listen to your body.”

Kiki placed a flat cushion on the ground. “Hop here,” she encouraged.

The chick fluffed its feathers. “Cheep!” It made one small hop onto the cushion. Thump. It wobbled, then stood tall. Its eyes sparkled.

“Great job,” Kiki clapped softly. “How do you feel?”

“A little tired,” the chick said.

Kiki nodded. “Then we rest again. Rest is not stopping. Rest is part of the plan.”

They did a little pattern: breathe, sip, rest, hop. The sun moved across the sky, making golden shapes on the roof. The mint leaves swayed. A butterfly floated by, like a tiny kite.

After a while, the chick made two hops. Hop-hop! Its wings flittered. “I did it!” it peeped.

Kiki beamed. “Yes, you did. You followed your goals with care and kindness.”

Just then, a bigger pigeon glided down. “Coo-oo,” the parent pigeon said. “Thank you for helping my chick.”

Kiki bowed her head. “We made gentle goals and listened to the body. Self-care helps us grow.”

The parent pigeon nodded. “Caring hearts make strong wings.”

The chick nuzzled Kiki’s paw. “You were calm and kind.”

Kiki felt warm and proud. She checked her own list. “I stretched and breathed. I drank water and ate a healthy snack. I helped a friend,” she said happily. “I met my goals.”

The sky turned soft orange. Kiki packed her mat and cup. She took one last deep breath. The rooftop smelled like mint and sunshine.

Kiki waved to the pigeons. “Remember: we care for ourselves, we set little goals, and we show compassion. That is how we grow, one gentle step at a time.”

“Cheep! Coo!” the pigeons sang as they flew in a small circle, then settled close together.

Kiki climbed down, heart calm and cozy. She felt strong because she had been gentle.

The End.
