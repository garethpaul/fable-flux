# Leo the Lion Learns to Recycle

Leo was a brave little lion cub with golden fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived in a cozy house with his mama and papa, and his favorite room was the big, sunny dining room where the family ate their meals together.

One morning after breakfast, Leo looked around the dining room and saw something that made him frown. Empty cereal boxes sat on the table, crumpled juice cartons were scattered about, and plastic yogurt containers were piled up near his chair.

"Mama," Leo said, his little nose wrinkling, "why is there so much trash everywhere? It makes our beautiful dining room look messy!"

Mama Lion smiled and patted Leo's fluffy head. "You're very observant, my brave little cub. But do you know what? Not all of this is trash. Many of these things can be recycled!"

"Re-cy-cled?" Leo repeated slowly, rolling the new word around on his tongue. "What does that mean?"

"Recycling means we can turn old things into new things instead of throwing them away," Papa Lion explained as he walked into the dining room. "It helps keep our Earth clean and healthy."

Leo's eyes grew wide with excitement. "Can I help recycle? Please, please, please?"

"Of course!" Mama said. "But first, we need to practice sorting everything into the right groups. Are you ready to move around and learn?"

Leo bounced up and down on his paws. "Yes! I'm ready!"

Mama brought three colorful bins into the dining room - a blue one, a green one, and a yellow one. "The blue bin is for paper, like newspapers and cereal boxes. The green bin is for plastic bottles and containers. The yellow bin is for glass jars and metal cans."

Leo listened carefully, his ears perked up and his tail swishing with concentration.

"Let's practice!" Papa said. He held up an empty cereal box. "Leo, where does this go?"

Leo thought hard, then proudly pointed to the blue bin. "Paper goes in blue!"

"Excellent!" cheered Mama. "Now you try picking something up."

Leo padded around the dining room, his paws making soft tapping sounds on the wooden floor. He spotted a plastic yogurt container and carefully picked it up in his mouth. The smooth plastic felt cool against his teeth.

"Green bin!" he mumbled through the container, then dropped it in with a satisfying *plunk*.

"Wonderful job!" Papa clapped his big paws together.

For the next hour, Leo practiced moving around the dining room, sorting and recycling. He learned to recognize the crinkly sound of paper, the light weight of plastic containers, and the smooth coolness of glass jars. Each time he put something in the correct bin, his family cheered, making him feel proud and strong.

Soon, the dining room was sparkling clean again. Leo sat back on his haunches, panting happily. "Look! Our dining room is beautiful again, and we helped the Earth too!"

"That's right, my brave little recycler," Mama said, nuzzling Leo's head. "And the more we practice, the better we get at taking care of our planet."

Leo grinned, showing his tiny white teeth. "Can we recycle again tomorrow? I want to practice more and more!"

"Every day," Papa promised. "Because taking care of our Earth is something we do every single day."

From that day forward, Leo became the family's recycling helper, always eager to sort, move, and practice keeping their home and planet clean.

The End.
