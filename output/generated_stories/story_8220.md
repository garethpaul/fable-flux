# Ollie's Car Ride Routine

Ollie the otter loved car rides with his family. His soft brown fur felt cozy against the warm car seat. The gentle hum of the engine made him feel safe and happy.

"Time for our trip to Grandma's house!" said Mama Otter, jingling the car keys.

But today, Ollie felt wiggly and excited. He bounced in his car seat. He dropped his favorite toy fish on the floor. He forgot to put on his seatbelt.

"Ollie, we need to follow our car routine," said Papa Otter gently. "Remember what we do first?"

Ollie's whiskers twitched. He was too excited to remember! "I want to go NOW!" he squeaked.

"Let's work together," said Mama Otter with a warm smile. "What's the first thing we always do?"

Ollie thought hard. His little paws fidgeted with his seatbelt. "Um... seatbelt?"

"Yes! Good job remembering," said Papa Otter. "Click!"

The seatbelt made a satisfying sound. Ollie felt proud. "What's next?" he asked.

"You tell me," said Mama Otter. "Look around the car."

Ollie's bright eyes looked everywhere. He saw his toy fish on the floor. "Oh! I pick up my toys!"

"Perfect teamwork!" said Papa Otter. Ollie reached down and grabbed his soft toy fish. He hugged it tight.

"Now what?" asked Ollie. He was starting to remember the routine.

"Think about your tummy," said Mama Otter.

Ollie patted his round otter belly. "Snacks! I get my snacks ready!"

Mama Otter handed him a small bag of fish crackers. Ollie's nose twitched at the yummy smell. He put the bag in his special car cup holder.

"Last step?" asked Papa Otter.

Ollie closed his eyes and thought. "Music! We choose our car songs!"

"You remembered the whole routine!" said Mama Otter. "How do you feel?"

Ollie's tail wiggled happily. "I feel ready! And proud! And... and... calm!"

Papa Otter started the car. The engine purred like a happy cat. "When we follow our routine together, everything goes smoothly," he said.

As they drove, Ollie munched his crackers. He sang along to their favorite songs. He watched the trees and houses go by through his window. His toy fish sat safely in his lap.

"I like our car routine," said Ollie. "It helps me remember everything."

"Routines help families work as a team," said Mama Otter. "When we talk nicely and help each other, trips are more fun."

Ollie nodded. His whiskers bobbed up and down. "Next time, I'll remember without help!"

"We'll always help each other," said Papa Otter. "That's what families do."

The car rolled gently down the road. Ollie felt the warm sun on his fur through the window. He listened to the happy music. His tummy felt full of yummy crackers.

"Thank you for helping me remember," said Ollie. "I love our family team."

"We love you too," said Mama and Papa Otter together.

When they arrived at Grandma's house, Ollie unbuckled his seatbelt carefully. He picked up his toy fish and his empty snack bag. He waited for Mama and Papa to get out first.

"Look!" said Ollie proudly. "I remembered our getting-out routine too!"

Grandma Otter was waiting at the door with open arms. "What a responsible little otter!" she said.

Ollie's heart felt warm and happy. Following routines with his family made everything better. And working together made every car ride an adventure filled with love.

The End.
