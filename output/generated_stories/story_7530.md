# Polly's Big Brave Words

In a cozy house, there lived a bright green parrot named Polly. She had beautiful red feathers on her wings and loved to learn new words every day. Polly's favorite room was the warm, steamy laundry room where Mama did the washing.

"Swoosh, swoosh!" went the washing machine. "Tumble, tumble!" went the dryer. Polly perched on her special swing near the window, watching the clothes spin round and round.

Every morning, Polly would practice her words. "Hello! Good morning! Pretty bird!" she would chirp happily. But there was one big problem that made Polly's tummy feel funny and scared.

The washing machine made a very LOUD noise when it finished washing. "BEEP! BEEP! BEEP!" it would go, so sudden and sharp. Every time Polly heard that scary sound, she would hide her head under her colorful wing and stay very quiet.

"Oh my," said Mama one day, folding warm towels that smelled like sunshine. "Polly, why do you get so quiet when the washer beeps?"

Polly peeked out from under her wing. "Too loud! Too scary!" she whispered in her smallest voice.

Mama smiled gently. "I understand, sweet Polly. But you know what? That beep is just the washing machine's way of talking. It's saying 'I'm finished! Come help me!'"

Polly tilted her head, thinking hard. "The machine... talks?"

"Yes!" said Mama. "Just like you love words, the washing machine has its own special word. Would you like to learn what it means?"

Polly felt curious but still scared. She took a deep breath, smelling the fresh, clean scent of soap bubbles. "Maybe... maybe I could try to be brave?"

"Being brave doesn't mean you're not scared," Mama explained, hanging up a soft blue shirt. "Being brave means you try something new even when you feel scared."

The next morning, Polly sat up tall on her swing. When she heard the familiar "swoosh, swoosh" of the washing machine, she didn't hide. Instead, she practiced her brave words.

"I am brave," Polly said to herself. "I can learn new things."

Then it happened. "BEEP! BEEP! BEEP!"

This time, instead of hiding, Polly spread her beautiful wings wide. She took a big, brave breath and called out, "Hello, washing machine! Thank you for talking to me!"

Mama clapped her hands with joy. "Oh, Polly! You were so brave!"

Polly felt warm and proud inside, like sunshine filling her heart. "The beep isn't scary," she discovered. "It's just saying hello in machine language!"

From that day on, whenever the washing machine beeped, Polly would answer back with her own cheerful words. "You're welcome, washing machine! Good job cleaning the clothes!"

Soon, Polly learned that the dryer had its own special sound too - a gentle "ding!" And the iron made a little "puff" of steam. The whole laundry room was full of different voices, all talking in their own special ways.

"I love learning new words," Polly chirped happily, preening her bright feathers. "Even machine words! Being brave helped me make new friends."

Mama gave Polly a gentle scratch behind her head. "You showed such courage, Polly. Now you can enjoy all the wonderful sounds and words around you."

And from that day forward, the laundry room became Polly's favorite place to practice being brave and discovering new ways that everything around her could "talk."

The End.
