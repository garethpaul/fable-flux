# Owl Wisdom in the Guest Room

In a quiet house at the end of a lane, there was a tidy guest room with a soft green rug and a big window. The air smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap. In the corner stood a tall wooden coat rack. On top of the rack sat a wise old owl named Orla.

Orla had soft gray feathers and bright gold eyes. At night, she hooted softly. During the day, she watched and listened. Orla liked to learn, and she liked to help. Her feathers rustled like paper when she moved.

One sunny afternoon, two cousins came to play in the guest room. Little Mia carried a box of puzzle pieces. Max held a red toy truck. The room felt warm and friendly.

“Let’s build the big picture,” Mia said, opening the box. The puzzle smelled like cardboard and fresh paint. The pieces were smooth and cool in her small hands.

“I want to drive my truck,” Max said, rolling his wheels across the rug. “Vroom! Vroom!”

Mia frowned. “We said we would do the puzzle together, Max.”

Max hugged his truck. “But I really want to drive now.”

Orla blinked. A tiny knot of worry fluttered in the room, like a trapped moth. She fluffed her feathers and spoke in a gentle voice. “Hoo-hoo. I hear a problem.”

Mia looked up. “Orla, we can’t agree.”

Max looked at his shoes. “I don’t want Mia to be mad.”

Orla tilted her head. “Let us use a strategy. Strategy means a smart plan. A plan helps us make good choices.”

“What kind of plan?” Mia asked.

“First, we listen,” Orla said. “Each person tells the truth about feelings. That is honesty. Then we think of ideas. That is knowledge. Then we choose a fair way. That is strategy.”

Mia took a little breath. “I feel sad when we don’t do the puzzle like we said.”

Max rolled his truck slowly and whispered, “I feel excited to drive. The rug is like a road.”

Orla nodded. “Thank you for honest words. Now, ideas. What can we try?”

Mia tapped a puzzle piece on her palm. “Maybe Max can help find the corner pieces. Then we can make a road in the picture. He can drive when we build it.”

Max’s eyes grew wide. “A road in the picture? Vroom! That sounds fun.”

Orla puffed with pride. “A clever idea. Let us add one more. We can use a timer. When the timer sings, we switch.”

Mia smiled. “We can use the little egg timer on the shelf.”

Max looked at Mia. “I’m sorry I didn’t listen first.”

“I’m sorry I frowned,” Mia said. “Let’s try the plan.”

The egg timer went tick-tick-tick. The guest room felt calm again. Sunlight made warm shapes on the rug. Mia and Max searched for corner pieces. Their fingers brushed the cardboard edges. “Here’s one!” Mia giggled.

Max held up a piece with a bit of road on it. “I found the road!”

Orla watched with soft hoots. “Hoo-hoo, teamwork.”

They set the corners, then the straight edges. The puzzle picture grew like a little town. Green trees, yellow houses, and a long gray road curved across the scene. The colors looked bright and happy.

The timer rang, ding-ding! “Switch time,” Orla said.

Max set his truck on the puzzle road very carefully. The wheels made a tiny whisper sound. “Vroom,” he said softly, smiling. Mia clapped. “Good driving, Max.”

After a few gentle laps, the timer dinged again. “My turn to place pieces,” Max said, handing the truck to Mia to hold. Mia held it with two hands. “I will keep it safe.”

They worked and switched, worked and switched. Sometimes they paused and took deep breaths when a piece didn’t fit. Orla reminded them, “When a piece doesn’t fit, try another. That is a strategy too.”

At last, the final piece clicked into place. The puzzle picture was complete. The road made a smooth loop. Max guided the truck one last time, then parked it on the green rug.

Mia and Max looked at each other. Their faces were bright like the sunny window. “We did it,” Mia said.

Max nodded. “We used a plan.”

Orla fluffed her feathers. “You listened with empathy. You told the truth with honesty. You used knowledge to think of ideas. You chose a fair strategy. You solved the problem.”

Mia hugged Max. “Thank you for playing with me.”

Max hugged back. “Thank you for sharing your puzzle.”

Orla smiled with her golden eyes. The guest room felt cozy again. The clean-sheet smell mixed with warm sunshine. The egg timer was quiet. The truck rested. The puzzle town looked peaceful.

Orla hooted softly, “Remember our steps: listen, share feelings, think of ideas, choose a fair plan, and try again if it doesn’t work. Strategy helps hearts feel safe.”

Mia and Max nodded. “We will remember.”

Outside, a breeze tickled the window. Inside, the wise old owl closed her eyes, happy and proud.

The End.
