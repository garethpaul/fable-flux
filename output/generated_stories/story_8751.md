# Kipp the Calm Koala Helps in the Warm Kitchen

The warm kitchen smelled like cinnamon and toast. Sunlight slipped across the floor in yellow stripes. A kettle hummed a soft song. Kipp the calm koala sat on a stool, his round ears wiggling with each little sound.

Kipp had a plan. “My goal today,” he whispered, tapping his furry chest, “is to help make muffins for the community snack table.” He smiled and took a slow, deep breath. In… out… His breath felt like warm cocoa in his tummy.

Grandma Roo, in her blue apron, set out bowls and spoons. “We’re volunteering today,” she said kindly. “We’ll bake muffins for our neighbors. You can be my helper.”

“I will be a gentle helper,” Kipp said. He liked the word gentle. It felt like a soft blanket.

The door creaked, and in came Tilly Tasmanian and Max Wallaby. Tilly’s eyes sparkled. Max bounced on his toes. “We want to help too!” they chirped.

“Wonderful,” said Grandma Roo. “We can all help.”

Kipp nodded. “We can have a goal,” he said. “My goal is to stir slowly and keep the batter inside the bowl.”

“My goal is to crack eggs,” Max said, grinning. “Crack, plop!”

“My goal is to pour sugar,” Tilly said, shaking the bag a little too fast.

The kitchen was warm and bright. The wooden spoon felt smooth in Kipp’s paws. He stirred in slow circles. Swish, swish. The batter smelled sweet and comfy.

Then—oops! Max cracked an egg too hard. SPLAT! Egg slid over the counter. Tilly tipped the sugar too far. WHOOSH! A white puff floated up. It sprinkled Kipp’s nose. He giggled. But then the giggle stopped. The batter wobbled. The sugar covered the floor like snow.

Kipp felt his heart go thump, thump. His cheeks grew hot. His paws squeezed the spoon too tight. He wanted to shout, “Stop!” The kitchen that smelled like cinnamon now smelled a little like worry.

Grandma Roo looked over. “It’s okay,” she said softly. “We can fix this.”

Kipp put a paw on his belly. He remembered his goal: be a gentle helper. He remembered his calm breath. He closed his eyes just for a moment.

“In… one, two, three,” he whispered. “Out… one, two, three.”

His shoulders softened. The spoon felt smooth again.

“I can use my calm,” Kipp said. “Let’s solve the problem.” He opened his eyes and gave a small smile to Tilly and Max. “We are a team.”

Tilly’s ears drooped. “I didn’t mean to make a sugar storm.”

Max’s tail drooped too. “I didn’t mean to make egg slime.”

Kipp nodded. “We all make messes. We can clean and try again.” He took another slow breath. “Let’s set tiny goals. Tiny goals help big jobs.”

“What tiny goal?” asked Tilly.

“First, wipe the egg. Then sweep the sugar. Then try again,” Kipp said. “I will count our breaths while we clean.”

They worked together. Kipp wiped with a warm, soapy cloth. It felt squishy and warm on his paws. Max held the bowl steady. Tilly used a small broom—swish, swish, swish. The floor shone again.

“Now we try again,” Kipp said calmly. “Max, try tapping the egg on the bowl. Tap-tap, little crack. Tilly, pour slow like a sleepy rain.”

Max nodded. He took a breath with Kipp. Tap. Crack. Plop! The egg slid into the bowl. No splat. Tilly took a breath too. Tip… tip… tip… The sugar came down like tiny snowflakes. No whoosh.

“You did it,” said Grandma Roo, clapping softly. “That’s good self-control. That’s self-regulation. You used your inside calm to choose what to do.”

Kipp smiled. “My inside calm helped my outside actions.” He stirred the batter in slow, sunny circles. Swish… swish… swish…

“Can we have a stirring goal?” asked Max.

“Yes,” said Kipp. “We can count to ten. Ten calm circles.” They counted together. “One… two… three…” The numbers felt round and friendly.

Soon, the muffin tray was full. Grandma Roo opened the oven. Warm air hugged their faces. The muffins puffed up like little golden clouds. The smell was cozy—vanilla, cinnamon, and sweet berries.

While they waited, Tilly and Max bumped shoulders.

“My spoon is shinier,” Max said.

“No, mine is,” Tilly said, frowning.

Kipp saw their faces getting tight. He felt a tiny thump in his chest again. He put a paw on his belly.

“In… one, two, three. Out… one, two, three,” he whispered. He smiled at his friends. “Let’s use our calm words.”

He held up both spoons. “We can take turns. I will count to five for Tilly. Then count to five for Max.” His voice was gentle and steady.

Tilly and Max took turns. Kipp counted, “One… two… three… four… five.” They both got to use the shiny spoon. Their faces softened into smiles.

Ding! The oven sang a little bell song. Grandma Roo set the muffins on the counter. The tops were golden and a little crisp. The muffins made tiny crackling sounds as they cooled.

“We did it!” Tilly cheered.

“We kept our goals,” Max added.

Kipp hugged himself with happy paws. “We used self-regulation,” he said proudly. “We calmed our bodies. We solved our problems. We helped others.”

They packed the muffins into baskets and carried them to the community snack table. Neighbors waved and said thank you. Kipp’s chest felt warm and bright, like the sun in the kitchen.

On the walk back, Kipp said, “When I feel wobbly inside, I can pause, breathe, and choose. That helps me be kind and helpful.”

Grandma Roo smiled. “That is a wonderful lesson, Kipp. And your calm helped everyone.”

Kipp nodded. “My calm is my superpower.”

The warm kitchen glowed as they cleaned up together. Swish, wipe, pat. Kipp took one more slow breath and felt proud. He had reached his goal and helped his friends, all with calm, kind choices.

The End.
