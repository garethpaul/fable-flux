# Rocky Raccoon’s Rolling Band

Rocky was a small, musical raccoon with bright eyes and tapping toes. He loved to make music with anything. He could drum on a cup. He could hum like a bee. He could shake a seatbelt buckle like a tiny tambourine. Ding, ding, ding!

Every morning, Rocky hopped into the family car with Mama Raccoon and Baby Roo, his little sister. The car smelled like oranges from Mama’s snack bag. The seats felt warm and cozy. The seatbelt clicked with a happy clack. Click-clack! Rocky smiled and patted the dashboard softly. Tap-tap.

“Good morning, my band,” Rocky giggled. “Are we ready to roll?”

“We’re ready,” Mama said. “Seatbelts on?”

“Click!” said Rocky. “Click!” said Baby Roo.

They had a routine. They sang a morning song, counted to five, and took deep breaths. It felt safe. It felt calm. It felt like a hug.

“One, two, three, four, five,” they counted together.
Breath in. Breath out. Ahhh.

Rocky looked out the window. The sky was soft and blue. The trees whispered swish-swish. The tires hummed on the road, mmmmmm. “Today I want to help,” Rocky said. “I want to help our town.”

“What a kind idea,” Mama said. “Helping is community-service. We can help together.”

Rocky’s ears perked. “Teamwork!” he cheered. Baby Roo clapped, pat-pat-pat.

As they drove, Rocky saw Mrs. Duck and her ducklings by the park. The ducklings peeped, peep-peep. But the sidewalk had crunchy leaves everywhere. People were stepping around the mess.

“Leaves are in the way,” Rocky said. “Can we help?”

“Yes,” Mama said. She parked the car safely. The brakes went hushhhh. They took small brooms from the trunk. The brooms smelled like straw and sunshine.

Rocky started a beat. Tap-tap, swoosh. Tap-tap, swoosh. “Sweep, sweep, tidy street,” he sang.

“Teamwork makes it neat,” Mama sang back.

Baby Roo shook a little bag like a shaker. Shake-shake-shake! Mrs. Duck quacked, “Thank you!” The ducklings peeped, happy and bright. People smiled and walked easily now. Rocky’s chest felt warm. Helping felt good.

Back in the car, they washed hands with a cool wipe. Wipe, wipe, clean. Routine was important. “We help, then we clean,” Rocky said. “That’s our way.”

They rolled on. The car smelled like fresh wipes and oranges again. Rock music from the radio played softly. Doo-doo-doo. Rocky beat a gentle rhythm on his knees. Tap-tap-tap.

Soon they reached the little library box near the corner store. The door of the box was stuck. A child stood nearby with a book in his paws and a worried frown.

“Oh no,” Rocky said. “He wants to read.”

“Let’s help,” Mama said. “Community-service is helping our neighbors.”

Rocky hopped out with Mama. The sun felt warm on his fur. He wiggled the little door gently. Wiggle, wiggle. “Easy does it,” he whispered. Mama held the box steady. Baby Roo hummed a sweet note through the window. Hummmmm.

“Teamwork,” Rocky said. He pushed and Mama pulled. Click! The door popped open. The child grinned. “Thank you!”

“You’re welcome,” Rocky said. His heart beat a happy drum. Bum-bum-bum. “Books for all!”

They waved goodbye and got back in the car. Click went the seatbelts. They drank tiny sips of water. Sip, sip. Routine helps bodies feel good.

On the way home, Rocky saw Mr. Mole by the bus stop. His bag had spilled. Apples rolled everywhere. Thump, thump, thump! One apple rolled under the bench.

“Apples on the move!” Rocky laughed. “Can we help?”

“Yes,” Mama said. They parked again. Rocky made a gathering song. “Roll back, apples, roll back, please!” He tapped the curb with his paw: tick-tick.

Baby Roo pointed. “There!” Rocky reached and gently got the apple from under the bench. His paws felt cool on the smooth apple skin. He placed it back in the bag. Mr. Mole sighed with relief. “Thank you, helpers!”

“Community helpers,” Mama said. “That’s us.”

Rocky felt proud. “Helping is our rhythm.”

They climbed back into the car one last time. The afternoon sun painted the seats with gold. The engine purred, prrrrrr. “We helped the sidewalk, the library box, and Mr. Mole,” Rocky said. “We did it together.”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Teamwork and routine helped us help others safely.”

Rocky nodded. “First, we buckle. Then we help. Then we clean and rest.”

Baby Roo yawned a tiny yawn. “Nap time,” she said, snuggling into her seat. Her fur smelled like baby soap. Rocky stroked her ear gently.

Rocky took a deep breath in. He let a slow breath out. “I learned something,” he said. “Community-service is like music. We listen. We help. We work as a team. We make the day feel better.”

Mama smiled. “Beautiful, Rocky.”

Rocky tapped a soft goodbye beat on the armrest. Tap… tap… tap. “Friends, if you need a hand,” he sang softly, “our rolling band will help the land.”

The car turned onto their street. The tires whispered home. Shhhhhh. They parked. Click went the seatbelts as they unbuckled. The door opened with a gentle creak. The air smelled like grass and dinner.

Rocky looked back at the car and patted the seat. “Thank you, car band,” he said. “See you tomorrow. We will help again.”

He held Mama’s paw, and Baby Roo’s tiny paw held his. Three paws, one team, one happy beat.

The End.
