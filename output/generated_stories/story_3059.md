# Benny Beaver's Porch Helpers

Benny was a busy beaver with bright, shiny teeth and a warm, wiggly nose. Today, he sat on a peaceful front porch that smelled like fresh pine. The porch boards felt smooth under his little paws. A cool breeze tickled his whiskers. Birds sang sweet notes in the maple tree. The morning sun made the porch rail glow like honey.

“I love helping,” Benny said. He tapped his tail gently on the floor. Thump, thump. “But where can I help today?”

Grandma Goose rocked in a chair beside him. Squeak, creak, went the chair. She smiled. “Good morning, Benny. The porch is so peaceful. I like it that way.”

“I can keep it peaceful,” Benny said. “And I can be helpful too!”

Just then, Benny heard a soft clatter. Clink, clink. A basket of apples had tipped over near the steps. Red apples rolled like little suns. One bounced against Benny’s paw.

“Oh!” gasped Benny. “Apples on the run!” He giggled. “I can help.”

Benny scooped an apple into the basket. He used gentle paws and careful eyes. “Roll, roll. Stop, please,” he said kindly to a wiggly apple. It slowed by his tail. He caught it and placed it in the basket.

A tiny chipmunk peeked from under the porch swing. “I like apples,” the chipmunk squeaked. “Can I have one?”

Benny thought. Helping means sharing too. “Yes,” he said. “You may have one apple.” He handed over a small, shiny one. “But the rest go in the basket.”

“Thank you!” chirped the chipmunk, nibbling happily.

Grandma Goose clapped her wings softly. “Well done, Benny. That was helpful.”

Benny felt warm and tall inside. “I did it!” His tail made a soft happy thump.

A minute later, a gentle buzz filled the air. Bzzzz. A big yellow balloon bobbed against the porch post. It was tied to a little turtle’s wrist. The string had wrapped around a flower pot. The turtle pulled and tugged. “Oh dear,” said the turtle. “My balloon is stuck.”

Benny tilted his head. “I can help.” He took a breath. He remembered: move slow, use kind words, be careful. “I will go slow,” he told the turtle. “Is that okay?”

“Yes, please,” the turtle said. His eyes were round with worry.

Benny used his small, strong teeth to nibble the knot on the string. Nibble, nibble—stop. He looked up. “Still okay?” he asked.

“Yes,” said the turtle with a tiny smile.

Nibble, nibble—slide! The string came free. The balloon bobbed higher. “Up it goes,” Benny laughed, holding the string. He placed it gently in the turtle’s hand.

“Thank you,” said the turtle. “You helped me and my balloon.”

Benny’s heart felt bubbly and brave. “I can help,” he whispered to himself. “I really can.”

The porch grew quiet again. The maple leaves whispered shhh-shhh. The breeze smelled like clean soap and flowers. Benny sat and listened. He liked the quiet. He liked helping too.

Then a little rabbit came hopping up the steps. Tap, tap, tap went her feet. Her ears drooped. “My book fell,” she said. “It slid under the porch. I wanted to read on the peaceful porch, but now I can’t reach it.” Her eyes looked shiny with tears.

Benny peered into the dark space under the porch. It smelled like cool earth. He heard a tiny drip, drip, drip. It was far and a little scary. Benny bit his lip. “It is dark,” he said softly.

Grandma Goose rocked and hummed. “Being brave is doing a kind thing even when it feels a little hard,” she said.

Benny put a paw on his chest. Thump-thump went his brave heart. “I want to help,” he told himself. “I can try.”

He lay flat on his tummy and wriggled forward. The boards felt cool on his fur. “I am going slow,” he whispered. “I am being safe.” His whiskers brushed the dusty edge. He reached, reached, reached.

“I can’t see,” he said.

“I can hold your tail so you feel safe,” said the rabbit. She held his tail gently. “I will help you help me.”

Benny smiled in the dark. “Thank you.” He stretched one more inch. His paw touched something soft. “I feel it!” He pulled the small book close and scooted back. He blinked in the warm sunlight and gave the book to the rabbit.

“My book!” she cheered. She hugged it tight. “Thank you, Benny. You were helpful. You were brave too.”

Benny’s chest felt light as a balloon. “I did it,” he said. “I helped you, the turtle, and the apples. Helping makes the porch peaceful.”

Grandma Goose nodded. “You listened. You asked. You went slow. You were kind. That is true helping.”

Benny sat tall. He felt sure and strong. He looked at the porch. The apples were safe in their basket. The turtle waved with his balloon. The rabbit read her book. The breeze whispered happy secrets through the leaves.

Benny made a little sign with a crayon. He wrote: “Porch Helpers.” He taped it by the step. “If anyone needs help,” he said, “we can help together.”

The chipmunk peeked out again and saluted with a tiny paw. The turtle and rabbit smiled. Grandma Goose rocked and hummed.

Benny took a deep breath. The porch smelled like apples and sunshine. His tail tapped a gentle beat. “Helping feels good,” he said. “Helping makes me brave. Helping shows I understand what others need.”

The porch was peaceful, and Benny felt peaceful too.

The End.
