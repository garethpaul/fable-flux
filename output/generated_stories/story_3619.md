# Bun-Bun’s Pantry Plan

Bun-Bun was a small, determined rabbit with bright eyes and soft, twitchy whiskers. One sunny morning, Bun-Bun hopped into the cool, quiet pantry. The pantry smelled like oats and apples. The shelves were tall and neat. Jars made tiny clinks when the door closed.

“I have a big idea,” Bun-Bun whispered. “I want to make pantry art!”

Bun-Bun looked around. There were empty boxes from oatmeal, a clean yogurt cup, a cardboard egg box, and a soft paper towel roll. There were also safe, clean lids from jars that made a fun tapping sound.

“First, I need a plan,” said Bun-Bun, patting a little notebook with a carrot on the cover. “Planning helps me do things step by step.”

Bun-Bun drew a simple picture. “Step 1: Gather clean, safe recycling. Step 2: Sort by size and color. Step 3: Make a picture. Step 4: Clean up.”

Bun-Bun smiled. “I can do it!”

Just then, a warm voice came from the doorway. It was Granny Hare. “What are you up to, little one?”

“I am making art,” said Bun-Bun, ears high. “I made a plan. See?” Bun-Bun showed the notebook.

Granny Hare smiled. “A plan is clever. Planning makes hard jobs easier. I will watch and cheer.”

Bun-Bun hopped to Step 1. The rabbit picked up the empty oatmeal box. “This is clean. Good.” Bun-Bun pressed the soft box. It crinkled. “Crinkle, crinkle!” Bun-Bun giggled.

Next, the yogurt cup. “Clean and safe.” It made a little “plink” when tapped. The cardboard egg box felt bumpy. “Bumpy, bumpy,” Bun-Bun said, rubbing it with soft paws. The paper towel roll was smooth and round. “Roll, roll,” it hummed as it rolled on the floor.

Bun-Bun set everything on the low pantry shelf. “Step 1 is done!”

“Good planning,” said Granny Hare. “What is next?”

“Step 2: Sort by size and color,” said Bun-Bun. The rabbit lined up the pieces. “Big box here. Medium egg box here. Small lids here.” Bun-Bun sorted by color too. “Brown roll with brown box. White cup with white lids.” The rabbit’s ears wiggled. “Sorting feels calm.”

Bun-Bun checked the notebook. “Step 3: Make a picture.”

“What will you make?” asked Granny Hare, eyes twinkling.

“A picture of a garden,” said Bun-Bun. “A garden in the pantry!”

Bun-Bun thought for a moment. “Plan first,” the rabbit whispered. Bun-Bun traced a simple shape in the air. “The oatmeal box can be the ground. The egg box bumps can be bushes. The paper roll can be a tree trunk. The lids can be flowers.”

Bun-Bun set the oatmeal box flat. “Ground.” The egg box went on top. “Bushes.” The paper roll stood tall. “Tree.” The white cup turned upside down to be a cloud. “Fluffy cloud,” said Bun-Bun, touching it. It felt cool and smooth.

Bun-Bun looked at the lids. “These can be flowers.” The rabbit gently placed a red lid, a blue lid, and a yellow lid along the “bushes.” “Tap, tap, tap,” went the lids.

Bun-Bun paused. “Something is missing,” the rabbit said, nose twitching. “Leaves!”

Bun-Bun looked around the pantry. Then the rabbit looked back at the plan. “I can add paper leaves.” Bun-Bun found a clean paper bag in the recycling bin. It was brown and soft. “May I use this?” Bun-Bun asked.

“Yes,” said Granny Hare. “You asked, and that is kind. It is clean and ready to reuse.”

Bun-Bun carefully tore leaf shapes. “Rip, rip,” went the paper. The edges were rough and interesting. “Leaves,” Bun-Bun said, placing them around the tree.

Granny Hare clapped softly. “You are following your plan so well.”

Bun-Bun took a step back. The picture looked good, but the flower lids kept sliding. “Oh no,” Bun-Bun said. “The lids won’t stay.”

Bun-Bun felt a little worry. The rabbit’s ears drooped. “What should I do?”

Granny Hare pointed to the notebook. “What does your plan say when something is tricky?”

Bun-Bun added a new line. “Step 3a: Ask for help and use safe glue.” Bun-Bun looked up. “Granny, do we have safe glue?”

“We do,” said Granny Hare. She reached for a small, non-toxic glue stick on a high shelf. “I will help you use it.”

They worked together. Bun-Bun put a tiny bit of glue on the back of each lid. “Just a dot,” said Granny. “A plan uses just enough.”

“Dot, press,” said Bun-Bun, tapping each flower lid. The lids stayed! The rabbit’s ears popped back up. “It works!”

Bun-Bun added one more touch. With a green crayon, the rabbit drew stems on the box ground. “Swish, swish,” went the crayon. The pantry smelled like paper and a little like glue. It felt cozy and quiet.

Bun-Bun checked the notebook. “Step 4: Clean up.” The rabbit put extra scraps in the recycling bin. The glue stick cap clicked shut. The shelf was neat again.

Granny Hare hugged Bun-Bun. “You planned. You sorted. You reused. You made art. That is wonderful.”

Bun-Bun looked at the pantry garden and smiled. The rabbit felt proud and calm. “Planning helped me finish. Recycling helped me make art. I can make a new plan tomorrow.”

Granny Hare nodded. “Yes. Good plans make big dreams feel small and easy.”

Bun-Bun hopped to the door, then looked back at the pantry garden. The colors were bright. The textures were fun. The little lids shone like tiny suns.

“I did it,” Bun-Bun said. “Plan, then do!”

The End.
