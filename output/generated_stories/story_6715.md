# Joey's Big Workshop Adventure

Joey the kangaroo joey loved bouncing everywhere! Hop, hop, hop! He bounced in the morning. He bounced at lunch. He bounced all day long in his mama's garage workshop.

The garage smelled like wood and paint. It had shiny tools hanging on the walls. There were colorful boxes full of screws and nails. Mama Kangaroo was building a special toy box for children around the world.

"Can I help, Mama?" asked Joey, bouncing up and down. "Please, please, please?"

"Of course, little one," said Mama. "But remember, we must use our calm bodies when we work with tools."

Joey nodded his fuzzy head. But as soon as Mama handed him a small hammer, Joey got SO excited! He started bouncing and bouncing. BANG! He hit his thumb instead of the nail.

"Ouch!" cried Joey. His eyes filled with tears.

"Oh dear," said Mama gently. "Let's take a deep breath together."

Joey sniffled. He wanted to help so badly, but his bouncy body kept getting in the way. When Mama showed him how to measure wood, Joey bounced and knocked over the ruler. When she gave him sandpaper, he bounced and dropped it on the floor.

"I'm too bouncy!" said Joey sadly. "I can't help make toys for other children."

Mama sat down next to Joey. The garage felt quiet and warm. "Joey, your bouncy energy is wonderful. But sometimes we need to use our calm energy too. Would you like to learn a special trick?"

Joey's ears perked up. "Yes, please!"

"When we feel too bouncy, we can take three deep breaths," said Mama. "Let's try together. Breathe in... and out. In... and out. In... and out."

Joey felt his body getting calmer. His heart wasn't beating so fast anymore.

"Now," said Mama, "let's count to five before we move. Can you try that?"

Joey took his deep breaths. He counted: "One... two... three... four... five." Then he picked up the small hammer very carefully. This time, he hit the nail perfectly!

"I did it!" Joey cheered. But instead of bouncing, he remembered to breathe first.

Together, they sanded the wood smooth. Joey's little paws moved slowly and carefully. The wood felt soft under his fingers. They painted the toy box bright blue, like the sky. Joey made steady, even strokes with his brush.

"This toy box will make children happy all around the world," said Mama. "Children in Australia, and Africa, and everywhere!"

Joey felt proud and warm inside. "I helped make something special for other kids!"

As they cleaned up the workshop, Joey practiced his new skill. When he felt bouncy, he took three deep breaths and counted to five. His body felt calm and strong.

"Mama," said Joey, "I learned that I can choose to be bouncy when it's time to play, and calm when it's time to work."

"That's right, my smart joey," said Mama, giving him a big hug. "You learned to control your body and your feelings. That's a very grown-up skill!"

Joey smiled big. He had helped create something beautiful for children everywhere. And best of all, he had learned that he could be in charge of his own body and feelings.

That night, Joey bounced happily to bed, knowing tomorrow he could help Mama build more wonderful things for kids around the world.

The End.
