# Pebble’s Porch Plan

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a happy waddle. Pebble loved to sit on the peaceful front porch and listen. The porch smelled like fresh lemon from the wooden rail. The breeze was soft and cool. Birds chirped. Wind chimes sang, ting-ting-ting.

Pebble clapped tiny flippers. “What a calm day!” Pebble said. “I can hear, I can see, I can feel. I love this peaceful porch.”

Pebble looked around and blinked. “Oh?” Pebble saw a crinkly plastic cup on the porch step. A shiny can rolled under a chair. A paper wrapper flapped-flap near the flower pot.

Pebble’s smile dipped. “Hmm. Trash on the porch?” Pebble tapped the beak, thinking. “I want to keep our porch clean and calm. But how?”

A squirrel peeked from the fence. Her name was Nibble. She held a sunflower seed in her tiny paws. “Hi, Pebble!” Nibble chirped. “Your porch smells nice. Crunch, crunch.”

“Hi, Nibble,” said Pebble. “I found litter. It makes me sad. I want a peaceful porch. I want to help.”

Nibble looked at the can. “I was playing toss with that can,” she said softly. “I did not mean to make a mess.”

Pebble nodded. “It’s okay to play,” Pebble said kindly. “We can fix it together. Let’s use peaceful words. Let’s make a plan.”

“Okay!” Nibble agreed. Her tail swished-swish.

Just then, a breeze rolled by. The paper wrapper slid across the porch, rustle-rustle. A small robin landed on the rail. “Cheep! I was looking for string for my nest,” the robin said. “I pecked the wrapper, but it flew away. I did not mean to litter.”

Pebble smiled gently. “Thank you for telling me. We can try again in a better way.”

Pebble took a deep breath. The air felt cool in the beak. “Step one,” Pebble said. “We will gather trash. Step two, we will sort. Some things are trash. Some things can be recycled.”

Nibble’s eyes grew wide. “Recycled?”

Pebble nodded and waddled to three small buckets by the door. One was blue, one was green, and one was gray. Pebble pointed with a flipper. “Blue is for recycling—like clean cans and clean plastic. Green is for compost, like leaves and apple cores. Gray is for trash that cannot be reused.”

The robin chirped, “Cheep-cheep! I can help pick up little bits.”

Together, they began. Pebble scooped the plastic cup. It felt smooth and cool. “This can be recycled,” Pebble said, and placed it in the blue bucket. Nibble rolled the shiny can. “This too?” she asked.

“Yes,” Pebble said. “But let’s rinse it.” Pebble poured a little water. The can went clink-clink as it dropped in the blue bucket.

The robin tugged the paper wrapper. “What about this?” she asked.

Pebble read the wrapper. “It is clean paper. We can recycle it too.” The robin placed it gently in the blue bucket. “Cheep!”

They worked calmly. The wind chimes sang. The sun felt warm on Pebble’s soft feathers. The porch began to look neat again.

Then a loud clang sounded. A raccoon peeked from behind the porch steps. It was Rascal, with curious eyes and quick paws. He held a shiny jar lid. “I like to collect shiny things,” Rascal said. “I took this. I made a mess. I am sorry.”

Pebble waddled closer with a kind smile. “Thank you for telling the truth,” Pebble said. “We can fix the mess together, and we can still find a good way for you to enjoy shiny things.”

Rascal blinked. “A good way?”

“Yes,” said Pebble. “We can make a shiny art box. We can use clean lids and safe bits to make art. That way, the porch stays peaceful, and you still get your sparkle.”

Rascal’s eyes lit up. “Art!”

Pebble brought out a small box and a bit of glue. They washed the lid, then set it in the box. Rascal clapped. Nibble giggled. The robin fluffed her feathers happily.

When all the pieces were sorted and the porch was clean, Pebble looked around. The rail shone. The air smelled like lemon and flowers again. The wind chimes sang a soft song.

Pebble folded flippers and said, “We did it with peaceful words and a plan. We cleaned up and we recycled. We found a way for everyone to feel okay.”

Nibble nodded. “I will play toss in the yard next time, not on the porch,” she said.

The robin chirped, “I will use bits from the green bucket for my nest, not wrappers.”

Rascal raised a paw. “I will put shiny things in the art box, not on the floor.”

Pebble beamed. “Thank you, friends. When we talk kindly, listen, and make a plan, we solve problems. We can care for our porch, and for the Earth too.”

They sat together on the peaceful front porch. The breeze was cool. The chimes went ting-ting-ting. Pebble poured little cups of cool water for everyone, and they sipped with happy smiles.

Pebble whispered, “Peaceful plans make peaceful places.”

The End.
