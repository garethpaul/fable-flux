# Tiko Tortoise’s Colorful Day

Tiko the wise tortoise peeked into the creative art room. The room smelled like crayons and warm glue. Sunlight made bright squares on the floor. Paintbrushes stood in cups like tiny trees. Paper rustled like leaves.

“Good morning, colors,” Tiko said with a smile. His shell was smooth and patterned, like a little map. He liked to move slowly and think carefully. Today, he wanted to make art for Sharing Day.

Tiko looked around. “First, I will get ready,” he whispered. He opened a small basket. Inside were soft cloths, a tiny jar of water, a clean apron, and his favorite pencil. He laid everything in a neat row.

“Prepare, prepare, take good care,” Tiko hummed. He tied his apron. He set paper on the easel and taped the corners. The tape made a gentle rippp sound. He sharpened his pencil until it made a soft scritch.

Lina the ladybug buzzed by. “Hi, Tiko! What will you make?”

Tiko blinked kindly. “I will paint a Story Shell,” he said. “On my shell, my family paints tiny pictures. It is our tradition. Each picture shows a memory.”

Lina’s wings clicked. “A tradition? What is that?”

“It is something special we do again and again,” said Tiko. “It helps us remember where we come from. It helps us share who we are.”

Lina smiled. “I want to see!”

Tiko dipped a brush in water. Swish, swish. He touched blue paint. The brush tip looked like a blueberry. He spoke softly to the paper. “Hello, blue.”

He made a curvy wave. “This wave is for the river where my grandmother sang,” he said.

Lina leaned close. “I can almost hear the song.”

Tiko hummed a low tune. Mmm-mmm-mmm. The humming filled the room like warm cocoa. “When we prepare our tools and our hearts,” he said, “the art sings, too.”

Pip the bunny hopped in. “Boing! Are you painting the ocean?” Pip wiggled his nose. The room smelled extra painty now.

“Not the ocean,” said Tiko. “A Story Shell. Would you like to watch? First, we wash brushes after each color.” He swished the brush again and wiped it gently on the cloth. “Clean brush, clear colors.”

Pip nodded. “Prepare, prepare, take good care!” he sang with Tiko.

Tiko dipped into sunny yellow. He dotted little suns. Tap, tap, tap. “These are for the warm mornings when my uncle told stories.”

“What kind of stories?” Pip asked.

“Stories of slow steps and brave hearts,” said Tiko. His eyes were bright. “He told me, ‘Slow can be smart. Slow can see.’”

Tiko drew tiny green leaves. Scratch, scratch. “These are for the garden where we learned patience,” he whispered. “We watered. We waited. Then the beans grew.”

Lina clapped her tiny hands. “I like how you remember with pictures!”

Tiko nodded. “Pictures help us share. When we have a tradition, we prepare for it. We gather what we need. We take our time. Then our hearts feel full.”

He added a small red heart. Plip. “This is for love.”

Pip hopped from foot to foot. “Can I make a picture of my grandma’s carrot soup?” he asked.

Tiko smiled. “Yes. Your picture can share your tradition, too. But first, let’s prepare.” He guided Pip to an apron. “Tie it snug.” Pip tied a bow. “Now set your paper.” Pip smoothed it flat. “Now choose colors.” Pip picked orange and green.

Lina buzzed to a fresh page. “I will paint bedtime lullabies!” She chose purple and soft blue.

The room filled with gentle sounds. Swish and tap. Hummmm and giggle. Paper slid. Brushes clinked in water. The sunlight felt warm on their backs.

Pip drew a big orange pot. “This is the soup pot,” he said. “We stir and stir.” He made wavy steam lines. “It smells cozy.”

Lina dotted stars and drew a moon. “My mama sings, ‘Rest now, little wings.’” She shaded the moon softly. “It sounds like a hug.”

Tiko added tiny footprints around his wave and suns. “These are our walking stories,” he said. “Step by step, we arrive.”

Soon, three pictures dried on the rack. The rack felt cool and metal when Pip touched it. Tiko checked the brushes. “We wash. We wipe. We put away.” He lined the paints in a rainbow. “Preparation keeps our space happy.”

The door opened. Friends came in for Sharing Day. Ducklings waddled. A kitten purred. The room buzzed like a soft hive.

Tiko stood by his painting. “This is my Story Shell,” he said. “Each color is a memory. My tradition is to share our stories with pictures.”

“Ooooh,” said the friends.

Pip held up his soup pot. “This is carrot soup day with my grandma. We chop, we stir, we wait. Then we say thank you.”

“Mmm,” said everyone.

Lina showed her lullaby moon. “This is our singing time. It helps me feel safe.”

The teacher smiled. “You prepared so well. You shared your traditions. You used your own ideas. That is creative expression.”

Tiko bowed his head. “When we prepare and share, our art tells who we are,” he said. “And when we listen, we learn who others are.”

Everyone clapped. Tiko’s heart felt warm and calm, like tea in a cup.

That night, Tiko tucked his painting away. He whispered, “Thank you, colors. Thank you, stories.” He placed his clean brushes in their cup and folded his apron flat.

Prepare, share, and care, he thought. That is a good way.

The End.
