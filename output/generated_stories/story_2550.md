# Polly Follows the Plan

Polly was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a splash of blue on her wings. She loved to paint, sing, and make up silly dances. She lived with Mia and Dad in a playful living room filled with cozy pillows, soft rugs, and a big low table for crafts. Sunlight made little squares on the floor, and the air smelled like crayons and fresh paper.

One sunny morning, Mia clapped her hands. “Polly, let’s write a thank-you card for Grandma!” she said.

Polly tilted her head. “Write, write! Thank you!” she squawked happily. She flapped to the table and tapped a piece of paper with her beak. Tap-tap!

Dad smiled. “First, let’s follow the steps so our card turns out just right,” he said gently. “We’ll listen, take turns, and help each other.”

Polly bounced on her feet. “Steps? Like a plan?” Her eyes sparkled. She loved making things.

“Yes,” Mia said. “Step one: We clean the table. Step two: We get our tools. Step three: We write the message. Step four: We decorate. Step five: We put it in the envelope.”

Polly chirped, “One, two, three, four, five! Follow the plan. Follow, follow!”

They wiped the table. The cloth felt warm from the sun. Swish, swish. The table was smooth and ready. Then Mia set out crayons, markers, a glue stick, and stickers. The crayons smelled waxy and sweet. The markers clicked when Mia capped and uncapped them. Click. Click.

Dad said, “Polly, could you please bring the paper?” His voice was calm and kind.

Polly listened. She picked up the paper with care and placed it on the table. “Paper ready!” she said. She felt proud. Following directions felt good.

Mia showed the card. “Let’s write first,” she said. “Then decorate.”

Polly’s eyes danced when she saw the glitter glue. It sparkled like tiny stars. “Shiny!” she squawked. She grabbed the glitter glue and squeezed. Ssssquish! A blob landed on the corner of the card, and a few drops dotted the rug.

“Oh!” said Mia, surprised.

Polly froze. Her feathers fluffed. She felt a little twist in her tummy. “Sorry,” she whispered. “I forgot the plan.”

Dad knelt down. “It’s okay, Polly,” he said softly. “Let’s use peaceful words and fix it together. What was the plan?”

Polly took a breath. She remembered the steps. “Write first,” she said slowly, “then decorate.”

Mia nodded. “Yes. Thank you for listening. Let’s clean the glitter together.”

They worked as a team. Dad brought a damp cloth. The rug felt cool and a little sticky, but soon it was clean. Polly dabbed the card corner gently. Tap, tap. The three of them smiled.

“Good teamwork,” Dad said. “Polly, would you like to help write the message by saying the words?”

Polly straightened her neck. “Yes!”

Mia held a blue marker. “What should we say first?”

Polly said, “Dear Grandma,” in her sweet squawk.

Mia wrote the words. The marker made a soft shhh sound on the paper. “Next?”

“Thank you for the cozy scarf,” Polly said. She rubbed her cheek against the soft scarf around her neck and cooed. “It feels warm like sunshine.”

Mia wrote more. “We love you very much. Love, Mia and Polly.”

Polly looked at the words. They were neat and blue. “Now decorate?” she asked carefully.

“Yes,” Mia said, smiling. “Step four.”

Polly listened closely. “What are the decorating rules?” she asked.

Mia held up one finger. “One: Small dots of glue.”

Dad held up a second finger. “Two: Caps back on after use.”

Polly held up a wing. “Three: Stickers go on the edges.”

They began. Polly dotted tiny sparkles. Tap-tap-tap. The glue made little stars. Mia placed heart stickers around the sides. Dad drew a small sun in the corner. The card looked bright and happy. It smelled like craft time—glue and crayons and a little bit of lemon from the clean table.

“Envelope time,” Dad said. He slid the card in. Fffft. “Polly, would you like to press the flap down?”

Polly pressed with her warm beak. Press. “All done!” she chirped.

Mia hugged Polly gently. Polly felt safe and calm. “We did it because we followed directions,” Mia said. “We listened, used kind words, and helped.”

Polly nodded. “Follow the plan. Listen. Help,” she repeated. Her chest felt light and proud.

Just then, a small crayon rolled off the table. Plink! It bounced near the couch.

Polly started to fly after it fast, but she paused. She remembered the plan of listening and staying gentle. “Mia, may I pick it up now?” she asked.

“Yes, please,” Mia said. “Thank you for asking.”

Polly picked up the crayon and placed it back in the cup. The cup made a soft clink as the crayon settled.

Dad smiled. “Polly, today you discovered something important.”

Polly tilted her head. “What did I discover?”

“You discovered that following directions helps us make beautiful things and feel peaceful,” Dad said.

Polly fluffed her feathers happily. “I like peaceful,” she said. “I like plans. I like helping.”

Mia held the envelope. “Let’s mail this to Grandma.”

Polly sang a little song as they walked to the door. “Follow the plan, do it right. Kind words make the day bright!”

They laughed together.

The card was ready, the room was tidy, and everyone felt calm and proud. Polly had learned to listen, speak kindly, and follow each step. The living room seemed extra sunny.

And Grandma would get a very special card.

The End.
