# Koala Kori Counts the Clues

Kori the koala liked quiet mornings. The sun was warm on her fuzzy ears. The grass in the sunny backyard felt soft under her paws. She breathed in. The air smelled like sweet flowers and fresh leaves. Birds sang, “Cheep-cheep!” A bee hummed by. It sounded like, “Bzzzz.”

Kori smiled. “What a bright day,” she said.

Her friends came to play. There was Pip the puppy, with a waggy tail. There was Mina the mouse, with tiny pink paws. There was Gus the goose, with a gentle honk.

“Let’s have a picnic!” said Pip. His tongue flopped out, and he giggled.

“Yes! Apples and crackers!” Mina squeaked.

“Honk-yes!” said Gus.

They set out a blue blanket. They set a basket on top. Then Pip’s eyes grew wide. “Oh no! My red ball is missing,” he said. “It’s my favorite ball.”

Mina gasped. “Where did it go?”

Gus waddled in a circle. “Honk, maybe it rolled away.”

Kori took another calm breath. She sat very still. “We will find it,” she said softly. “Let’s think. Let’s use our minds like helpers. We can look, listen, and ask good questions.”

Pip’s ears perked up. “Okay,” he said.

Kori held up her paw. “First, we remember. When did you see the ball last?”

Pip thought. He tilted his head. “I was bouncing it by the garden.”

Kori nodded. “Good clue. Next, we look for signs. What does the ball look like?”

“It is red,” said Pip. “It has a white star.”

Kori nodded again. “Red with a star. Very good. Now we check the garden.”

The friends walked to the garden. The sun warmed their backs. The tomatoes smelled sweet and earthy. A tiny bug tickled Kori’s paw. She giggled.

Mina pointed. “Look! There is a round mark in the dirt.”

Kori leaned closer. “This could be from the ball,” she said. “Where do the marks go?”

They all looked. The marks made a little path. Dot-dot-dot in the soft soil. The path went past the tomatoes, past the tall sunflowers, and toward the grapevine.

Gus fluffed his feathers. “Honk. I see something red in the vines.”

They peeked under the big leaves. A red shape was there. But it was a shiny red watering can.

Mina sighed. “Not the ball.”

Kori patted Mina’s paw. “It is okay. We keep thinking. What else rolls?”

“Apples roll,” said Pip.

“Rocks roll,” said Gus.

Kori smiled. “Yes. But our clue is the white star. Let’s look for a white star.”

They turned their heads slowly. They looked high. They looked low. A white flower star shone on a vine. Mina giggled. “A star! But it is not on a ball.”

Kori laughed softly. “It’s a good try. Let’s keep going.”

They followed the dirt marks to the shady tree. Under the tree, the ground was cool. The leaves whispered, shhhh. Pip sniffed. “I smell something sweet. Like strawberries!”

“Me too,” said Mina.

Kori tapped her chin. “If the ball rolled here, what would stop it? The tree roots are bumpy. A bump could stop a ball.”

They peered around the roots. No ball.

Gus honked gently. “Maybe the wind moved it.”

Kori nodded. “Good idea. Which way is the wind blowing?” She held up a leaf. The leaf fluttered toward the fence.

“The wind goes that way,” Pip said.

“Then the ball might go that way too,” Kori said. “Let’s check by the fence.”

They walked to the fence. It was warm and rough. On the other side, the neighbor’s daisies bobbed. Something red peeked from under the gate.

Pip’s tail wagged fast. “I see it! I see the white star!”

Kori smiled. “Let’s test. Is it round? Is it red? Does it have a white star?”

Pip reached under with his paw. He rolled it out. “Yes! Round, red, and a star. We found it!”

Mina clapped. “Yay!”

Gus flapped. “Honk-hooray!”

Pip hugged the ball. “Thank you, Kori. You stayed so calm. You asked good questions. You helped us think.”

Kori’s eyes were kind. “We all helped. We looked for clues. We used our senses. We tried one idea, and then another. That is logical thinking. It is like putting little steps together.”

Pip nodded. “Little steps.”

Mina’s whiskers twitched. “Kori, can I have a turn with the ball?”

Pip looked at his ball. He took a breath. He smiled. “Yes, Mina. We can share.” He rolled the ball to her. “You try.”

Mina giggled and bounced it. “Boing, boing!”

Gus asked, “May I try too?”

“Of course,” said Pip. He rolled it to Gus. “We can take turns.”

Gus bounced it with his beak, very gently. “Honk! Fun.”

Kori lay back on the blanket. The sun felt cozy on her fur. The friends laughed. The ball made soft thumps on the grass. The picnic smelled like apples and crackers. The backyard felt safe and happy.

Kori spoke softly. “Today we learned to be calm, to be kind, and to think with our minds. When there is a problem, we can stop, breathe, look for clues, ask questions, and try little steps. We can also share and help each other.”

Pip nodded. “Calm hearts and smart minds.”

Mina squeaked, “And kind paws!”

Gus honked, “And sharing!”

They all smiled. The sunny backyard glowed. A butterfly fluttered by, as if it said, “Good job!”

The End.
