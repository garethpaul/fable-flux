# Benny Beaver’s Best-Time Plan

Benny was a busy beaver with bright teeth and a big, warm smile. He lived by a sunny backyard with soft green grass, a sandbox, and a shady maple tree that smelled sweet. Birds chirped. Bees hummed. A gentle breeze tickled Benny’s whiskers.

“Good morning, sunny backyard!” Benny said. He tapped his tail on the grass. Tap, tap! “I have so much to do.”

Benny looked at his little notebook. He loved to write. Writing made his heart feel calm. He held a red pencil that smelled like strawberries. He wrote, “Plan.”

“I will stack sticks. I will help friends. I will write a story,” he said. “But when?” His tummy fluttered like a butterfly. He felt busy, busy, busy!

His friend Dot the ladybug landed on his nose. “Hi, Benny! Can you help me move a leaf?” she asked softly.

“Of course!” said Benny. He pushed the crunchy leaf. Swish, swish! Dot smiled and flew away.

Then Pip the puppy ran up, paws pat-patting. “Benny! Will you throw my ball?” he barked.

“Okay, Pip!” Benny tossed the squeaky ball. Squeak! Pip wagged and ran.

The sun climbed higher and felt warm on Benny’s fur. The backyard smelled like fresh dirt and flowers. Benny smiled, but then he looked at his notebook. “Oh no,” he whispered. “I did two things, but I did not write my story yet.”

Benny wanted to write a kind story for his friend Mia the mouse. It was Mia’s birthday. Benny wanted to be generous and give her a special story gift. He wanted his story to be about friendship and helping.

Just then, Mia peeked from behind the flower pot. Her nose twitched. “Hi, Benny,” she squeaked. “I am excited for later. We will have cake!”

Benny felt a squeeze in his chest. “I want to write your story, Mia,” he said, “but I keep doing new things. My day feels wiggly.”

Mia nodded. “Maybe you need a plan that shows time,” she said. “Like a picture plan.”

“A picture plan?” Benny blinked. He loved pictures.

Mia drew a big circle in the dirt with a stick. She made the circle like a clock. “We can make little boxes of time,” Mia said. “First, then, next, last.”

Benny’s ears perked up. “First, then, next, last,” he repeated. The words felt tidy, like lining up blocks.

They sat in the warm sun. Benny wrote in his notebook. He wrote simple steps.

“First: write Mia’s story under the maple tree.
Then: stack sticks for the sandbox border.
Next: help friends who ask.
Last: play ball and rest.”

He drew little pictures next to the words. A book. A stack of sticks. A heart. A ball. He smiled. The plan looked friendly and clear.

Dot flew back. “Benny, can you help me again?” she asked.

Benny took a deep breath. He looked at his plan. “First is writing,” he said kindly. “I will help you in the ‘Next’ time box. I wrote it here.” He showed Dot the little heart picture.

Dot read the plan. “That is smart,” she said. “I can wait. Thank you for telling me when.”

Pip the puppy bounced over, tail wagging. “Ball now?”

Benny patted Pip’s head. “Ball is ‘Last,’ after I help and stack sticks. I will play with you then.”

Pip looked at the pictures and sat. “Okay! I’ll save my barks.”

Benny snuggled under the maple tree. The leaves made dappled, dancing lights on his notebook. He could hear gentle whoosh-whoosh wind and faraway chirps. He smelled sweet sap. He felt calm.

He began to write Mia’s story. “Once there was a tiny mouse with a big, kind heart,” he wrote. Scratch, scratch went his pencil. The words felt like music. Benny smiled. He wrote about sharing, helping, and cheering up friends. He drew a little picture of Mia with sparkly eyes.

When he finished, he added a ribbon sticker. “Done!” Benny said. His tail thumped happily. Tap, tap, tap!

“Now it is ‘Then,’” Benny said. He stacked sticks by the sandbox. Thunk, clack, rustle. He counted. “One, two, three, four.” The sticks made a neat border.

“Next” came, and Benny helped Dot move the leaf to a shady spot. Swish! Dot sighed with joy. “Thank you, Benny. You are very thoughtful.”

At last, “Last” time box arrived. “Pip, it’s playtime!” Benny called.

Pip’s ears perked up. “Ball!” he barked. They ran and laughed. The ball went squeak, squeak, and the grass felt cool on their toes and paws.

Mia came with a tiny party hat. “Is there… a story?” she asked.

Benny handed her the notebook page. “For you,” he said, blushing.

Mia read. Her eyes grew shiny. “This is the best gift,” she whispered. “You used your time to write with love. Thank you.”

Benny felt warm and proud. He looked at his picture plan. It had helped him be kind and calm. He had done each thing, one by one.

He turned to his friends. “I learned something today,” he said. “When we make a plan—first, then, next, last—we can do our jobs and still help our friends. We can be generous and kind without feeling too busy.”

Dot buzzed. “That’s good time management!”

Pip wagged. “And good fun!”

Benny laughed. “Now I can plan tomorrow too!”

The sun began to set, painting the backyard orange and pink. The breeze felt cool. Benny tucked his notebook close. He was a busy beaver, but now he was a smart-time beaver too.

The End.
