# Rosie Rabbit's Big Race Day

In a cozy nursery filled with soft toys and colorful books, lived a little rabbit named Rosie. Her fluffy white fur was as soft as cotton balls, and her pink nose twitched whenever she got excited. Today, Rosie was VERY excited because tomorrow was the Big Bunny Race at the playground!

"I'm going to win!" Rosie declared, hopping around her favorite rocking chair. "I'm the fastest rabbit in the whole nursery!"

But instead of getting ready for the race, Rosie spent the day playing with her toy cars and reading picture books. When her friend Oliver Owl flew by the window, he called out, "Rosie! Are you practicing for tomorrow's race?"

"I don't need to practice," Rosie said proudly, her ears standing tall. "I'm already fast enough!"

Oliver's big round eyes looked worried. "But Rosie, all the other animals are practicing. Benny Bear is stretching his muscles, and Lily Lamb is running laps around the garden."

Rosie just shrugged and continued playing. The nursery smelled like lavender from the little sachets tucked between the cushions, and the afternoon sun made everything feel warm and sleepy.

The next morning, race day arrived! The playground was buzzing with excitement. Colorful flags fluttered in the breeze, and all the animal friends gathered at the starting line. Rosie hopped over confidently, her heart beating fast with excitement.

"Ready, set, GO!" called out Mrs. Hedgehog, the race judge.

All the animals took off running. At first, Rosie was in the lead, her paws thudding against the soft grass. But soon, her legs began to feel heavy and tired. Her breathing became quick and shallow.

"Huff, puff, huff," Rosie panted as Benny Bear ran past her with steady, strong steps.

Then Lily Lamb bounced by with graceful leaps. Even little Tommy Turtle, who had been practicing every day, slowly but surely passed Rosie.

By the time Rosie crossed the finish line, she was last. Her ears drooped down, and her eyes filled with tears. All her friends cheered for her anyway, but Rosie felt sad and disappointed.

"I'm sorry I was so proud," Rosie whispered to Oliver Owl after the race. "I should have listened to you and practiced."

Oliver patted Rosie's paw gently. "It's okay, Rosie. What matters is that you learned something important today."

"What's that?" Rosie asked, wiping her eyes with her soft paw.

"That preparation helps us do our best," Oliver explained kindly. "When we practice and get ready, we feel confident and strong."

Rosie nodded slowly. "You're right. I want to apologize to everyone for being boastful. And next time, I'll prepare properly!"

The next week, Rosie practiced every single day. She stretched her muscles like Benny Bear showed her. She practiced her breathing like Lily Lamb taught her. She even learned patience from Tommy Turtle.

When the next race came around, Rosie felt ready. She had prepared her body and her mind. Even though she didn't win first place, she finished strong and felt proud of her effort.

"Great job, Rosie!" cheered all her friends. "You prepared so well!"

Rosie's nose twitched with happiness. She had learned that being prepared wasn't just about winning – it was about doing your very best and feeling good about your effort.

From that day on, whenever Rosie had something important to do, she always remembered to prepare first. And she never forgot to say sorry when she made mistakes, because that's what good friends do.

The End.
