# Gigi the Giraffe’s Gentle Choices

Gigi the giraffe was visiting her friend Sam’s house. Sam had a cozy guest room just for sleepovers. The guest room smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap. The curtains were soft and green, like leaves in the sun. A tiny fan hummed, “whirr, whirr,” making the air feel cool.

“Welcome, Gigi!” said Sam, a smiling squirrel with a fluffy tail. “This is our guest room. You can see the garden from the window!”

Gigi’s long neck peeked near the window. Outside, butterflies floated over purple flowers. Gigi loved nature. She loved trees and bees and soft, cool dirt. She felt happy and calm.

Sam bounced on a beanbag. “Let’s play a game!” he chirped. On the carpet sat three boxes. One had building blocks. One had animal cards. One had shiny glitter glue.

Gigi’s heart made a tiny tap-tap. Too many choices! “Hmm,” she said softly. “I want to pick a good game. I want us to have fun. And I want to be kind to nature.”

Sam tilted his head. “What do you mean?”

Gigi took a slow breath, in and out. She liked to think before acting. “Glitter can fall on the floor,” she said. “If we open the window, the glitter might fly out to the garden. That could make a mess for the birds. I care about the birds.”

Sam looked at the glitter glue. It sparkled like stars. “Oh,” he said. “I didn’t think of that.”

“It’s okay,” Gigi said, smiling. “We can still choose something fun.” She tapped her hoof on the carpet. Tap. Tap. Tap. She talked to herself, quiet and calm. “First, I notice my choices. Then I think of what matters. I care about you, and I care about the garden.”

Sam’s tail swished. “Let’s use the blocks!”

Gigi nodded, but then paused. She looked at the animal cards. “Wait. I have an idea,” she said. “We can make a garden with blocks. We can build a safe home for tiny bugs. Then we can use the animal cards to tell a story.”

Sam clapped. “Yes! A bug hotel!” His voice sounded bright and happy.

They opened the block box. The wooden blocks smelled like pine trees. Gigi and Sam stacked squares and triangles. They made doorways for beetles and tunnels for worms. “Knock, knock,” Gigi said, gently tapping a block. “Anyone home?”

Sam giggled. “This is Mrs. Ladybug’s room,” he said, placing a small red card. “She likes it quiet.”

Gigi nodded. “And this is Mr. Bee’s resting spot,” she added. “We keep it clean and safe.”

The fan went whirr, whirr. The curtains swished, hush, hush. Gigi felt peaceful. She liked using her long neck to reach the top blocks. She moved slowly and carefully, so the tower stayed steady.

After a while, Sam pointed to the window. “Can we open it a little? The room feels warm.”

Gigi looked at the tall window. She thought again. “If we open it just a little,” she said slowly, “the fresh air will come in. We can keep the glitter glue closed. That way, no sparkles fly away.”

Sam nodded. “Good plan.”

Gigi stood tall and reached the latch. She opened the window a tiny bit. Cool air drifted in, carrying the smell of grass and rain. “Mmm,” said Gigi. “That smells like the garden saying hello.”

They added more blocks and more cards. Gigi used a calm voice to ask, “Sam, do you want to build the roof or the door?”

Sam blinked. “I want the roof.” Then he paused. “Oh! But you reach higher. Maybe you should do the roof.”

Gigi took another slow breath. “Thank you for thinking of that,” she said. “We can share. You can guide me. You tell me where to put the blocks, and I will place them.”

Sam’s eyes sparkled. “Teamwork!” He pointed with his tiny paw. “A little to the left. Perfect!”

Soon the bug hotel was done. It had a roof, a door, and two small windows. Gigi and Sam sat back to admire their work.

“Now the story,” Gigi said. She picked up a frog card. “Mr. Frog hopped to the hotel,” she said in a gentle voice. “He knocked on the door and asked, ‘Is this a kind place?’”

Sam picked up a snail card. He made a slow, stretchy voice. “Yes,” said Mr. Snail. “We think before we do. We keep things clean. We make room for friends.”

Gigi smiled. “Mr. Frog felt safe. He said, ‘Thank you for making careful choices.’”

Just then, Sam’s mom peeked in. “What a wonderful guest room garden!” she said. “I love how you two made thoughtful choices.”

Gigi’s cheeks felt warm with pride. “We thought about the birds,” she said. “We chose blocks and cards. We opened the window a little. We took turns and used calm voices.”

Sam nodded. “We made a bug hotel!”

Gigi stretched her long neck and took one more slow breath. In. Out. She felt steady inside. “I learned that I can stop and think,” she said softly. “I can be kind to friends. I can be kind to nature. That helps me make good choices.”

Sam gave Gigi a tiny high-five on her hoof. “You are a thoughtful giraffe,” he said.

Gigi giggled. “And you are a caring friend.”

They put the blocks away, closed the window, and waved to the garden. The butterflies twirled in the sun, and the room felt calm and clean.

The End.
