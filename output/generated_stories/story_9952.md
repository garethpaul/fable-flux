# Bounce to the Beat, Little Roo

Little Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and a springy tail. Today, Roo hopped into the musical practice room at the community center. The room smelled like lemon-clean floors. Soft lights glowed. Colorful drums, shiny cymbals, and friendly instruments waited on shelves. A big poster said, “Move your body. Move your heart!”

Roo wiggled with excitement. “I love to bounce!” Roo said. “Bounce, bounce, bounce!”

Coach Lina, a kind music-and-movement teacher, clapped her hands. “Hello, Roo! Hello, friends!” Her voice sounded like a warm bell. Around her stood children and joeys from many places. Mei wore a red ribbon. Sam had bright blue sneakers. Amina wore a green scarf. Hugo had a small drum. They all smiled at Roo.

“Today we will exercise with music,” said Coach Lina. “We will use our bodies to make beats. We will listen. We will talk to each other. We will recycle, too!”

Roo’s ears perked up. “Exercise? Like jumping?”

“Yes,” said Coach Lina. “But we will jump safely. We will breathe. We will stretch. Exercise makes us strong and happy.”

Mei raised her hand. “I can show a stretch from my grandma.”

Amina nodded. “I can share a clap rhythm from my dad.”

Hugo patted his drum. “I will keep a soft beat.”

Sam waved a cardboard tube. “And I made a shaker from a bottle. I put dry beans inside. It’s recycling!”

Roo’s tail thumped. “Let’s move!”

Coach Lina passed out more clean, empty bottles and small boxes from a blue recycling bin. “We can make music from old things,” she said. “Shake, shake, shake!”

Everyone shook gently. The bottles whispered, ch-ch-ch. The boxes went tap-tap. Roo laughed. “It sounds like rain!”

“First, warm-up,” said Coach Lina. “Feet flat. Knees soft. Breathe in,” she hummed. “Breathe out.”

Roo breathed in lemony air. In. Out. It felt calm, like floating on a cloud.

“Now stretch tall like a tree,” said Coach Lina.

Roo reached up, up, up. Fingers spread. Toes pressed. “I am a tall tree!” Roo giggled.

Mei showed a side stretch. “Reach to the moon,” she said.

Roo reached to the moon and to the sun. Muscles felt long and comfy.

“Time for our beat walk,” said Coach Lina. “Hugo, soft drum, please.”

Boom… boom… boom.

Step, step, step. Roo felt the floor under big feet. “This is good exercise,” Roo said. “My legs feel strong.”

Amina smiled. “Yes. We move and we listen.”

“Now let’s hop,” said Coach Lina. “Roo, will you lead?”

Roo stood tall. “I can lead!” Heart thumped like a happy drum. “Small hops first.” Roo bent knees and bounced, boing… boing… boing. Everyone followed.

“Good job,” said Coach Lina. “Now big hops, but watch your space. Use your words.”

Roo looked around. “I need more room,” Roo said kindly to Sam.

Sam stepped to the side. “Thanks for telling me,” he said.

Roo hopped big. Boing! Tail swished. Air brushed Roo’s face. It felt fresh and cool.

“Water break,” called Coach Lina. Cups were stacked by the sink. A sign said, “Use, rinse, recycle.” Roo sipped cool water. “Ahhh,” Roo said. “Water helps my body.”

A soft drum roll began. “Let’s share rhythms from our homes,” said Coach Lina. “We will move with each one.”

Amina clapped a gentle pattern: clap, clap, pause, clap. “This is from my family,” she said.

Roo copied the claps and did knee lifts to the beat. Up, down. Up, down. “My knees are working!” Roo said.

Mei shook her ribbon like a wave. “We bow and sway,” she said.

Roo swayed side to side. The ribbon whispered. Roo’s belly felt warm and happy.

Hugo tapped a circle rhythm on his drum. Sam shook his recycled shaker. “This is my beat!” Sam said. The shaker rattled fun and bright.

Roo did star jumps to the rattle—arms out, legs out, big smile. “Star jumps make my heart strong,” Roo puffed.

Coach Lina nodded. “Exercise is for every body, every day. Moving to music is joyful.”

Then Roo saw a spill of paper scraps near the craft table. “Oops,” said Roo. “Trash on the floor.”

“We can help,” said Mei.

Roo bounced over, but stopped. “Friends, can I hop by you?” Roo asked.

“Yes,” said Amina, stepping aside.

They picked up the scraps together. The paper felt dry and crinkly. Roo carried it to the blue bin. “We recycle paper here,” said Hugo.

Roo dropped the scraps in. Plip, plip. “Helping feels good,” Roo said.

“Last game,” said Coach Lina. “The Listening Circle. We talk. We move. We rest.”

Everyone sat. The floor felt cool. “How does your body feel after exercise?” asked Coach Lina.

“Warm!” said Sam.

“Happy,” said Amina.

“Strong,” said Mei.

Roo patted a thumping chest. “My heart says thank you.”

“What did we learn?” asked Coach Lina.

Roo raised a paw. “I learned to breathe, stretch, and move safely. I learned to use words to make space. I learned that friends from many places have beautiful rhythms. I learned to recycle and make music with old things.”

Coach Lina smiled. “Wonderful lessons, Roo.”

“Can we do a goodbye hop?” Roo asked.

“Yes,” everyone said.

They hopped slow, then fast, then slow again. The room filled with soft thumps, rattles, and giggles. The lemony air felt like sunshine.

Roo took a deep breath. “Exercise helps my body. Talking helps my friends. Music brings us all together.”

“Exactly,” said Coach Lina.

Roo waved. “See you next time! I will keep moving every day!”

The End.
