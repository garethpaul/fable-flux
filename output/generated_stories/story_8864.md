# Penny Panda's Rooftop Adventure

High up on a tall building, Penny Panda sat quietly on the rooftop. She loved being up so high where she could see the whole city below. The warm sun tickled her black and white fur, and a gentle breeze made her ears wiggle.

"What a beautiful day," Penny said softly to herself, watching the fluffy white clouds drift by like cotton balls in the bright blue sky.

Suddenly, she heard a tiny voice calling, "Help! Help!" Penny's round ears perked up. She listened very carefully, staying perfectly still.

"Where is that coming from?" she wondered aloud. The voice was so small and squeaky!

Penny looked around the rooftop. She saw flower pots, a small garden, and some tools. But where was the voice coming from?

"Help! I'm stuck!" the tiny voice called again.

This time, Penny listened even more carefully. She closed her eyes and turned her head slowly, using her excellent panda hearing. The sound was coming from... the flower pots!

Penny walked slowly and quietly to the row of colorful flower pots. Red geraniums, yellow marigolds, and purple petunias swayed in the breeze. She knelt down carefully and listened again.

"Down here! In the yellow pot!" squeaked the voice.

Penny looked into the bright yellow marigold pot. At the bottom, she saw a tiny gray mouse with whiskers that twitched nervously.

"Oh my!" said Penny gently. "How did you get down there, little friend?"

"I was looking for seeds to eat," said the mouse, whose name was Max. "I climbed down into this pot, but now the sides are too slippery for me to climb back up! I've been calling for help all morning."

Penny thought carefully. She knew she had to help Max, but she also needed to be safe. The pot was deep, and she didn't want to get stuck too.

"Don't worry, Max," Penny said in her calm, patient voice. "I'm going to help you. But first, let me think of the safest way."

Penny looked around the rooftop. She saw a long wooden ruler near the gardening tools. Perfect!

"Max, I'm going to lower this ruler down to you. Can you climb up it like a ladder?" Penny asked.

"Oh yes!" squeaked Max excitedly. "Mice are very good climbers!"

Penny carefully held one end of the ruler and slowly lowered the other end into the pot. She made sure to hold it very steady.

"Take your time, Max," Penny said encouragingly. "I'll hold the ruler nice and still for you."

Step by tiny step, Max climbed up the ruler. His little claws made soft scratching sounds on the wood. Penny waited patiently, keeping the ruler perfectly steady.

Finally, Max reached the top of the pot and hopped onto the rooftop. He was free!

"Thank you so much, Penny!" Max said, doing a happy little dance. "You saved me! But how did you know I was in trouble?"

Penny smiled warmly. "I listened very carefully. Sometimes when we're quiet and really listen, we can hear when someone needs our help."

Max's whiskers twitched with joy. "You're the best listener ever! And you kept me safe too."

From that day on, Penny and Max became the best of friends. Penny learned that listening carefully can help her help others, and Max learned to be more careful around deep flower pots.

Every morning, they would sit together on the rooftop, listening to all the sounds of the city waking up below.

The End.
