# Paints on the Porch

Pip was a patient panda. Pip loved to sit on the peaceful front porch. The porch was warm and sunny. The wood felt smooth under Pip’s paws. A soft breeze tickled Pip’s ears. Birds sang, “Tweet, tweet!” and a butterfly fluttered by.

Pip set out little cups of paint. Red like a ripe apple. Blue like the big sky. Yellow like warm sunshine. Green like crunchy leaves. Pip held a small brush. “Today I will paint the world,” Pip said with a smile.

Mama Panda brought a cool cup of water. It went, “clink, clink,” on the porch. “What will you paint, Pip?” Mama asked.

“I want to paint places far away,” said Pip. “I want to paint how people say hello. I want to paint kind hands and happy faces. I want to paint the world with love.”

“That is a big idea,” said Mama. “Take your time. Be patient.”

Pip dipped the brush in blue. Swish, swish. Pip painted a big round shape. “This is our big world,” Pip said. The paint was smooth and shiny. The brush tickled Pip’s paw.

Pip tried to paint a little house like the ones in the mountains. The roof went squiggle. “Oh no,” said Pip. “It is not straight.” Pip felt a tiny frown. Pip’s tummy felt tight.

Mama sat beside Pip. “It’s okay to feel frustrated,” Mama said softly. “Take a slow breath with me.” They breathed in and out. The porch smelled like fresh rain and warm wood. Pip’s shoulders felt softer.

“I can try again,” said Pip. Pip dipped the brush in red. Tap, tap. Pip painted a tiny heart. “This is for love,” said Pip.

Next, Pip painted a child waving hello. “In Spanish, we say, ‘Hola!’” Pip said. Pip tried to paint a wave. The line wiggled. “Oh no, it wobbled,” Pip said, feeling a little hot in the face.

“You are learning,” said Mama. “Wobbly lines can dance.” Mama wiggled her paw. Pip giggled. “Wobbly lines can dance!” Pip said. Pip made wiggly lines on purpose. The lines looked happy now.

Pip dipped in yellow and said, “In Mandarin, we say, ‘Nǐ hǎo!’” Pip painted sunshine around the waving child. The yellow glowed. Pip’s heart felt bright.

A breeze whooshed. The paper lifted. “Wait!” Pip cried. The corner flipped. A drop of blue fell into the yellow. It made green swirls. “My sun!” said Pip. Pip’s eyes felt wet.

Mama placed a smooth stone on the corner. “The wind helped you mix a new color,” Mama said. “Green is for growing.”

Pip looked. The green swirls looked like a garden. “In Swahili, we say, ‘Jambo!’” Pip said. Pip painted tall green leaves and red flowers. The red smelled sweet in Pip’s mind. Pip could almost hear drums: boom, boom.

Pip dipped in purple. “In French, we say, ‘Bonjour!’” Pip said. Pip painted a little hat and a big smile. The purple felt cool and calm.

Then Pip wanted to paint a tiny boat on a big blue sea. Pip painted a curve. The boat went splat. A blob! Pip’s ears drooped.

Pip closed eyes. Pip took three slow breaths. In, out. In, out. In, out. The birds sang, “Tweet, tweet,” like a soft timer. Pip opened eyes and smiled. “I can turn a blob into a boat,” Pip said. Pip added a flag. Pip added waves. Swish, swish. Now the blob looked like a brave boat.

A neighbor squirrel peeked up. “Hi, Pip!” squeaked Squeak. “What are you painting?”

“I am painting the world,” said Pip. “People say hello in many ways. Hola. Nǐ hǎo. Jambo. Bonjour.” Pip waved with the brush. A tiny dot of paint jumped and made a star. “Oh!” Pip laughed. “The paint is saying hello too!”

Squeak clapped. “Can I paint?” Squeak asked.

“Yes,” said Pip. “We can paint together.” Pip gave Squeak a small brush. They tapped, tap-tap, like rain on the porch. They painted hands of many colors holding the big blue world. They painted smiles. They painted music notes. They painted a little panda and a little squirrel sitting on a porch, just like them.

Pip said, “Sometimes I feel mad when my lines wiggle.”

Squeak nodded. “Me too.”

Pip said, “I take a breath. I try again. I make a new idea. Wiggly lines can dance. Blobs can be boats.”

Squeak grinned. “That helps me.”

Mama brought a plate of crisp apple slices. Crunch! The apples tasted sweet and cool. The breeze felt soft. The sun made the paint shine.

Pip looked at the big picture. “We painted the world,” Pip said. “We painted hello in many ways. We were patient. We did not give up. We showed our feelings. We found new ideas.”

Mama smiled. “That is creative expression,” she said. “You used your art to share your big heart. You learned about our big, wide world, and you practiced patience.”

Pip hugged Mama and Squeak. “I will paint again tomorrow,” Pip said. “I will try new colors and new hellos.”

The porch was peaceful. The picture dried. Pip felt proud and calm. Pip whispered, “Hello, world. I am ready to make and share.”

The End.
