# Splash the Dolphin Learns to Share

In a cozy nursery with soft blue walls, Splash the dolphin lived in a special water tank. The room smelled like lavender and felt warm and safe. Colorful toys floated around him - a yellow rubber duck, a red beach ball, and his favorite purple starfish.

Splash loved to play all day long. He would splash and spin, making happy clicking sounds. The water felt cool and smooth against his gray skin.

One sunny morning, a new friend arrived. A small sea turtle named Shelly was placed in the tank. Her green shell sparkled in the morning light.

"Hello!" chirped Shelly. "What a wonderful place to play!"

But Splash didn't feel happy. His fins started to flutter fast. His heart beat quickly. "These are MY toys!" he said loudly, swimming in circles around all his things.

Splash pushed the beach ball away from Shelly. He wrapped his fins around the starfish. "No sharing!" he squeaked angrily.

Shelly's eyes filled with tears. She swam to the corner of the tank. The water felt cold and lonely there. "I just wanted to be friends," she whispered sadly.

Splash's anger made the water choppy. Waves splashed over the tank's edge. The nursery floor got wet. Even his favorite toys didn't feel fun anymore.

Mrs. Marina, the kind caretaker, came into the room. She had gentle hands and a soft voice. "Oh my, what's happening here?" she asked.

"Splash won't share," Shelly said quietly.

Mrs. Marina sat by the tank. "Splash, how do you feel right now?" she asked.

"Mad!" Splash clicked. But then he noticed how sad Shelly looked. His anger started to feel heavy, like a rock in his tummy.

"When we're angry, we can take deep breaths," Mrs. Marina said. "Let's try together."

Splash took a big breath through his blowhole. The air felt fresh and cool. He breathed out slowly. His fins stopped fluttering so fast.

"Better?" asked Mrs. Marina.

Splash nodded. The angry feeling was getting smaller.

"Now, what could help both of you feel happy?" Mrs. Marina asked.

Splash thought hard. He looked at Shelly in the corner. She looked so lonely. "Maybe... we could talk?" he said softly.

Splash swam over to Shelly. "I'm sorry I was mean," he said. "I got scared you would take my toys away forever."

"I don't want to take them," Shelly said. "I just wanted to play together."

"We could take turns," Splash suggested. His voice sounded much calmer now. "You can play with the ball while I play with the duck. Then we can switch!"

Shelly's face lit up like sunshine. "That sounds perfect!"

They decided to play for ten minutes each, then trade toys. Mrs. Marina helped them count the time.

Soon, both friends were laughing and splashing together. The water felt warm and happy again. They discovered that playing together was much more fun than playing alone.

"Look!" Shelly giggled, balancing the ball on her nose.

"Wow!" Splash clapped his fins. "Can you teach me that?"

As the day ended, Splash felt proud and peaceful. He had learned that talking about feelings was better than staying angry. Sharing made everything more fun.

"Thank you for teaching me to share," Splash told Shelly.

"Thank you for being a good friend," Shelly replied.

Mrs. Marina smiled as she watched them play peacefully together. The nursery felt calm and full of friendship.

The End.
