# Ellie’s Colorful Breakfast Plan

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft, floppy ears and a curious trunk. One sunny morning, she tiptoed into the warm kitchen. The room smelled like toast and oranges. Sunlight slid across the floor like golden butter.

“Good morning, Ellie,” said Mama Elephant, stirring oatmeal with a wooden spoon. Plip-plop went the oatmeal bubbles.

Ellie’s tummy rumbled. “Good morning, Mama. Can I make breakfast today?” Ellie’s eyes sparkled. “I have a creative plan!”

Mama smiled. “That sounds exciting. Making breakfast is a big job. Can you be responsible?”

Ellie nodded. “Yes! I will try my best.”

Mama tied a tiny apron around Ellie. It had blue stripes and a pocket for her little spoon. “Let’s wash our hands first,” Mama said.

They turned on the warm water. Swish-swash went the soap. Ellie scrubbed carefully. “Clean hands mean healthy hands,” she sang.

Ellie looked around the warm kitchen. The fruit bowl was bright and happy—red strawberries, green kiwis, and yellow bananas. The oatmeal pot made gentle steamy clouds. The toast popped up with a cheerful spring.

Ellie tapped her chin with her trunk. “I will make Rainbow Bowls!” she said. “A Rainbow Breakfast is healthy and fun.”

Mama nodded. “What a creative idea. You took initiative all by yourself.”

Ellie spread a clean towel on the counter. “I will be neat,” she whispered. She placed bowls in a row. “Hello, bowls,” she giggled. “Are you ready?”

“Ready!” she answered in a tiny bowl voice, making herself laugh.

She peeled a banana. The peel went shloop. She sliced it into little moons. “Yellow moons for the rainbow.” She set them carefully into the first bowl.

Next, she washed strawberries. Drip, drip, drip went the water. She patted them dry. Snip, snip went the green tops. “Red stars,” she said, arranging them like a smile.

Then she cut kiwi with Mama’s help. “Green wheels,” Ellie said. “Roll into the bowl!” The sweet, tangy smell made her trunk wiggle.

She stirred the oatmeal. “Plip-plop, plip-plop.” She scooped warm oatmeal into each bowl. Steam tickled her cheeks. “It smells cozy,” Ellie sighed.

Mama set a small dish of nuts and seeds on the counter. “Would you like to add crunchy sprinkles?”

Ellie thought. “Yes! But just a little, to be healthy.” Tap-tap went the spoon as she sprinkled. “Crunch, crunch,” she whispered.

A crumb fell onto the floor. Ellie saw it and paused. “Oops.” She picked it up and put it in the compost bin. “I’m responsible,” she said proudly.

Just then, Papa Elephant peeked in. “Mmm, something smells good!” His trunk sniffed the air. “Is breakfast ready?”

“Almost!” Ellie said. She looked at the bowls. “Everyone likes different things,” she remembered. “I will be tolerant and kind.” She pointed to each bowl. “This one has no nuts, for Papa. This one has extra kiwi for Mama. This one is just right for me.”

Papa clapped softly. “You thought of everyone. That is very caring.”

Ellie looked at the table. The placemats were crooked. She straightened them. She set out little spoons and napkins. She filled cups with cool water. Glug-glug. “We drink water to stay healthy,” she said.

Mama brought the honey jar. “Just a tiny drizzle,” Mama reminded.

Ellie nodded. “I can do that.” She tipped the jar slowly. Drizzle-dazzle, a little swirl of gold. “Pretty and sweet,” she whispered.

They sat together. The chairs made gentle creaks. Ellie looked at her family. “Let’s breathe and be thankful,” she said. They all took a slow breath in and out. The warm kitchen felt like a hug.

Papa tasted a spoonful. “This is wonderful! I taste creativity.”

Mama smiled. “I taste responsibility and healthy habits.”

Ellie took a bite. The oatmeal was warm and soft. The fruit was juicy and bright. Crunchy seeds popped like tiny fireworks. “My Rainbow Breakfast makes my body and my heart happy,” she said.

After eating, Ellie stacked the bowls. Clink, clink. She wiped the table with small, careful circles. Swish, swish. She washed the spoons and set them to dry. “When we make a mess, we clean it up,” she sang softly. “That’s being responsible.”

Mama gave Ellie a gentle hug. “You showed initiative. You made a plan. You were creative and careful.”

Papa nodded. “You thought of everyone’s needs. That is kind and tolerant.”

Ellie’s ears wiggled with joy. “I learned that creativity can make healthy food fun. I learned that being responsible keeps the kitchen tidy. And I learned that caring about others makes breakfast better.”

The sunbeams danced across the clean counter. The warm kitchen smelled like sweet fruit and soap. Ellie hung up her apron. Her tummy was full, and her heart felt big.

“Tomorrow,” Ellie said, “I will think of a new, creative meal!”

Mama chuckled. “We can’t wait.”

The End.
