# Ollie Otter's Helper Heart

In a cozy closet behind the winter coats, little Ollie Otter had made the most wonderful hideaway. Soft blankets covered the floor, and twinkling fairy lights made everything glow like stars. This was Ollie's special place where he loved to play and dream.

One sunny morning, Ollie heard his mama calling from downstairs. "Ollie! Mrs. Rabbit needs help reading stories to the baby animals at the library today. Would you like to volunteer?"

Ollie's whiskers twitched with excitement. "Vol-un-teer?" he asked, practicing the big word. "What does that mean, Mama?"

"It means helping others because you want to make them happy," Mama explained gently. "You don't get toys or treats for volunteering. You do it because it feels good in your heart to help."

Ollie clapped his tiny paws together. "Yes! I want to help!" But then his smile faded. "Wait... I don't know how to read yet."

Mama's eyes sparkled. "You don't need to read words from books, sweetie. You can tell stories from your imagination! That's just as special."

Ollie scurried up to his closet hideaway to think. He touched the soft blankets and looked at his favorite toy boat. "What story should I tell?" he wondered aloud.

Suddenly, Ollie had an idea! He gathered his toy boat, a small shell, and a fluffy stuffed fish. "I'll tell them about my underwater adventures!"

At the library, Ollie saw Mrs. Rabbit looking worried. Three little bunnies, a baby fox, and two tiny mice sat in a circle, but they looked sad.

"Oh dear," Mrs. Rabbit sighed. "The storyteller is sick today, and I don't have any books ready."

Ollie's tummy felt nervous, like butterflies were dancing inside. But then he remembered what Mama said about helping others feel good in your heart.

"Mrs. Rabbit," Ollie said in his sweetest voice, "I came to volunteer! I brought a story to share."

Mrs. Rabbit smiled warmly. "That would be wonderful, Ollie!"

Ollie sat in the middle of the circle, holding his toy boat. The baby animals leaned in close, their eyes wide with curiosity.

"Once upon a time," Ollie began, making his voice sound magical, "there was a brave little otter who sailed across the sparkling blue sea." He moved his boat through the air, making whooshing sounds.

The baby fox giggled. The little bunnies wiggled with excitement.

"The otter found a magical shell," Ollie continued, holding up his shell, "that could grant wishes to help other sea creatures!" He made the shell shimmer in the light.

For twenty minutes, Ollie told his amazing adventure story. He made splashing sounds, acted out swimming motions, and gave each character a funny voice. The baby animals laughed and clapped their paws.

When the story ended, all the little ones cheered. "More! More!" they squeaked.

Mrs. Rabbit hugged Ollie tight. "Thank you for volunteering, dear Ollie. You made everyone so happy today!"

That evening in his closet hideaway, Ollie felt warm and glowy inside. Mama tucked him in among his soft blankets.

"How did volunteering feel?" she asked.

Ollie's whiskers twitched with joy. "It felt like sunshine in my heart, Mama! Helping others made me feel even happier than playing with my favorite toys."

"That's the magic of volunteering," Mama whispered. "When we help others, we help ourselves feel good too."

Ollie snuggled deeper into his blankets, already planning his next volunteer adventure.

The End.
