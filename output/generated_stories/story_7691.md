# Panda Poppy’s Quiet Plan

Panda Poppy lived in a cozy home with a sunny home office. The office had a small desk, a big chair, and a friendly green plant in a blue pot. The plant’s leaves were soft and shiny. The window was open just a little, and a gentle breeze smelled like fresh rain. The computer hummed, “Hmmmm,” like a sleepy bee.

Poppy was patient and calm. She liked to help. She liked to listen. Today, Poppy wanted to finish a puzzle and draw a picture of the plant. She sat down, took a deep breath, and smiled.

“Good morning, plant friend,” Poppy said. “Your leaves look happy.”

The plant was quiet, but Poppy pretended it waved.

Suddenly—plink, plink, plink! Poppy heard a drip. She turned her head. “What is that sound?” she asked.

Plink, plink. A small leak! A tiny drop was falling from the window frame. The drop landed on the floor near the power strip. Poppy’s eyes grew wide.

“Oh dear,” Poppy said softly. “Water and wires do not mix.” Her tummy felt fluttery. The room smelled extra wet now, like a puddle.

Poppy put her paw on her chest. “I can be calm. I can solve this.” She took a slow breath in and a slow breath out. “First, I will keep everyone safe.”

She looked around. “Step one,” she said. “Turn off the power.” Poppy used her long reach to carefully flip the switch on the power strip. Click! The computer stopped humming. The room felt quiet and safe.

“Step two,” Poppy said. “Catch the drip.” She looked for something to help. She saw a small bowl on the shelf. It was smooth and shiny. “Perfect.” She slid the bowl under the drip. Plink! The drop landed in the bowl with a tiny, happy sound.

“Step three,” Poppy said. “Dry the floor.” She found a towel in a basket. The towel was soft and fluffy. She pressed it on the wet spot. Squish! The water soaked into the towel. Poppy wrung it out in the sink with a twist and a squeeze.

The dripping slowed. Poppy smiled. “I can do this.”

But then Poppy noticed something else. The plant’s leaves were drooping a little. “Oh,” she whispered. “Are you thirsty, plant friend?” She touched the soil. It felt dry like sand. Poppy thought again.

“Hmm,” she said. “I should save water and use just what you need.” She looked at the bowl catching the drip. Plink, plink. “Aha! I can use the water from the bowl.”

Poppy waited until the bowl had a little water. She poured it gently into the plant’s blue pot. “Sip, sip,” she said. “Just enough for you.” The plant looked a tiny bit brighter.

Then Poppy heard a soft knock. “Poppy?” called her friend, Nico the squirrel, peeking in. “I heard the plink. Do you need help?”

“Hi, Nico,” Poppy said. “Yes, please. I have a plan, and I am being patient.”

“What is the plan?” asked Nico.

Poppy pointed with a gentle paw. “I turned off the power for safety. I put a bowl to catch the drip. I used the water for the plant, so we do not waste. Now I need to stop the leak for today.”

Nico nodded. “Good idea.”

Poppy found a strip of sticky tape and a small cloth. “Step four,” she said. “Make a dry patch for the window.” She patted the window dry with the cloth. Pat, pat. Then she placed the tape over the tiny gap. Press, press. The tape made a snug seal. The drip slowed to… nothing.

Nico grinned. “No more plink!”

Poppy smiled and felt warm inside. “We did it.”

“Can we open the window a little more for fresh air?” asked Nico.

“Let’s check,” Poppy said. She lifted the window just a tiny bit on the dry side, not near the leak. A clean breeze floated in. It smelled like pine trees. Poppy and Nico breathed in together. In… and out… calm and happy.

“Now we tidy up,” Poppy said. She folded the towel and put the bowl by the plant for the next time it needed a sip. She moved the power strip up onto the desk, far from the floor. Then she wiped the desk with a soft cloth. Swish, swish. The office looked neat and safe.

Poppy sat back down. “Thank you for helping, Nico.”

“You are very patient,” said Nico. “You made a plan, step by step.”

Poppy felt proud. “When I feel worried, I breathe. I think of steps. I use what I have. I care for the earth. I do not waste water. I care for my plant, my friend. I keep the room clean and safe.”

Nico clapped. “Can we do the puzzle now?”

“Yes,” Poppy said. “First, a sip of water for us.” They filled their cups and took small sips. “Sip, sip,” they giggled.

They worked on the puzzle together. The pieces clicked. Click, click. The plant stood tall by the sunny window. The office smelled fresh and clean. Poppy’s heart felt light.

“We solved a problem,” Poppy said.

“And we took care of the earth,” Nico added.

“And we took care of ourselves,” Poppy said. “Breathing, cleaning, and using water wisely.”

Poppy looked at the plant and whispered, “Thank you for being patient too.”

The End.
