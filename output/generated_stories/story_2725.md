# Ruby Rabbit's Calm Art Day

Ruby Rabbit hopped into the bright, colorful art room at her preschool. The walls were covered with happy paintings, and shelves held jars of sparkly paint in every color of the rainbow. The room smelled like crayons and paper, and soft music played in the background.

"Today we're making special paintings!" announced Ms. Chen, their kind teacher. "You can paint anything that makes you happy."

Ruby's ears perked up with excitement. She loved to paint! She quickly grabbed a big paintbrush and dipped it into bright red paint. But when she tried to paint a perfect circle for the sun, it came out wobbly and lopsided.

"Oh no!" Ruby said, her nose twitching with worry. "This doesn't look right at all!"

She tried again with yellow paint, but her hand was shaking because she felt upset. The paint dripped down the paper in messy streaks.

"This is terrible!" Ruby's voice got louder, and she felt her heart beating fast like a drum. Her fluffy tail started to twitch, and she wanted to throw her paintbrush down.

Ms. Chen noticed Ruby getting frustrated and walked over with gentle steps. "Ruby, I can see you're feeling upset about your painting. Would you like to try something that might help?"

Ruby nodded, her ears drooping sadly.

"Let's take three deep breaths together," Ms. Chen said softly. "Breathe in slowly like you're smelling a beautiful flower." She took a long, deep breath through her nose.

Ruby copied her teacher. The air felt cool and fresh in her nose.

"Now breathe out slowly like you're blowing bubbles," Ms. Chen continued, breathing out with a gentle "whoosh" sound.

Ruby breathed out slowly. She felt her shoulders start to relax.

"One more time," Ms. Chen smiled. "In like a flower, out like bubbles."

After the third breath, Ruby noticed something wonderful. Her heart wasn't beating so fast anymore, and her paws had stopped shaking.

"How do you feel now?" asked Ms. Chen.

"Better," Ruby said quietly. "My tummy doesn't feel all twisted up anymore."

"Sometimes when we feel frustrated, our bodies get all tight and wiggly," Ms. Chen explained. "Taking slow breaths helps our bodies feel calm and peaceful again."

Ruby picked up her paintbrush with steady paws. This time, instead of trying to make a perfect circle, she painted swirls and dots and happy squiggles. She added blue for the sky, green for grass, and purple flowers that looked like little hearts.

"Look!" Ruby called to her friend Sam, a little mouse at the next easel. "I made a happy garden!"

"It's beautiful!" Sam squeaked. "I love all the colors!"

Ruby stepped back and looked at her painting. It wasn't perfect, but it was full of joy and bright colors. Most importantly, she felt proud of what she had created.

"I learned something important today," Ruby told Ms. Chen as they cleaned up their paintbrushes. "When I feel upset, I can take deep breaths to help my body feel calm again."

"That's exactly right," Ms. Chen smiled. "And do you know what? Your painting is wonderful because it came from your happy, calm heart."

Ruby hopped home that day with her painting tucked safely under her arm and a big smile on her face. She couldn't wait to show her family her beautiful artwork and teach them about taking calm, deep breaths too.

The End.
