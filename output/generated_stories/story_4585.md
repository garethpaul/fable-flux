# Rico Raccoon and the Treehouse Tune

Rico was a musical raccoon with bright eyes, soft gray fur, and little black paws that loved to tap-tap-tap. He lived in a cozy treehouse high in a big, friendly oak. The treehouse smelled like pine and cinnamon tea. Sunlight peeked through the round window, and little chimes jingled in the breeze. Rico kept all his instruments there: a shiny red drum, a tiny triangle, a wooden flute, and a small guitar he called Pickle.

Today was a big day. Rico’s forest friends were coming for a music circle. “We will play one song together!” Rico said, bouncing on his toes. He could feel the wood floor under his feet. It felt warm and smooth.

Knock, knock, knock!

“Hi, Rico!” chirped Bella Bluebird, fluttering in with a soft whoosh of wings. She carried a neat basket of song cards.

“Hello!” squeaked Milo Mouse, dragging a little tambourine that jingled with every step.

“Howdy!” rumbled Tessa Turtle, slow and steady, with a small bell tied to her shell. Ding, ding.

Rico clapped. “Welcome to my treehouse! We will make music. First, let’s follow directions. We will set up our circle, warm up, and then play together.”

Bella nodded. “I like directions. They help me learn.”

Milo sniffed the air. “Mmm, cinnamon tea!”

Tessa smiled. “I’ll listen carefully.”

Rico showed a bright yellow sign in his paws. The sign said:

1. Sit on your spot.
2. Hands still and eyes on the leader.
3. Play only when you hear, ‘Go!’
4. Use gentle sounds.
5. Stop when you hear, ‘Pause!’

Rico placed soft leaf mats in a circle. “Here are your spots,” he said. “Bella, the blue mat. Milo, the green mat. Tessa, the purple mat. I will sit on the orange mat.”

Everyone sat. The mats felt cushy and cool. Rico’s tail swished with joy. “Let’s try the warm-up,” he said. “Listen to the directions.”

He spoke slowly. “Tap your knees two times. Clap one time. Wiggle your nose.”

Tap-tap. Clap! Wiggle, wiggle.

They laughed. “Again!” said Milo.

Rico smiled. “Okay. Tap your knees two times. Clap one time. Wiggle your nose.”

But this time, Rico’s paws wanted to drum. The red drum looked so shiny. It whispered to him, Play me! Rico picked up the drumsticks and gave a big boom-boom-boom.

Bella blinked. “Rico, the directions said tap knees, clap, wiggle. No drumming yet.”

Tessa tilted her head. “We follow directions so we can learn together.”

Milo clutched his tambourine. “I want to shake it too!”

Rico’s ears drooped a little. “Oh,” he said softly. “I did not follow my own directions. I was excited. I am sorry.” His tail stopped swishing.

Bella’s voice was kind. “It’s okay. Let’s try again.”

Rico took a deep breath. He could smell the cinnamon tea again. It made him feel calm. He put the drumsticks down. “Thank you for reminding me,” he said. “Let’s do it right.”

He raised one paw. “My directions: Tap knees two times. Clap one time. Wiggle your nose.”

Tap-tap. Clap! Wiggle, wiggle.

They did it together. It felt good to make the same sounds at the same time. “Great job,” said Tessa, her bell giving a happy ding.

Rico beamed. “Now, we will learn our song. It is called Treehouse Tune.”

He picked up Pickle, his small guitar. “Directions first,” he said. “Listen, then echo. I will play a soft strum. Then you will play.”

He strummed—bing-bong—like rain on leaves. The sound was warm and gentle.

“Now you,” he said. He pointed to Bella.

Bella chirped a sweet tweet-tweet.

“Good listening,” said Rico. “Now Milo. Gentle sound.”

Milo shook his tambourine softly—jingle-jingle—like tiny stars.

“Tessa next,” said Rico.

Tessa rang her bell—ding—clear and bright.

“Wonderful,” Rico said. His heart felt light. “Now all together, but remember the directions: play only when I say, ‘Go,’ and stop when I say, ‘Pause.’ Ready?”

He held up his paw. “Go!”

Bing-bong, tweet-tweet, jingle-jingle, ding!

“Pause!”

All the sounds stopped. The treehouse grew quiet. A little breeze swirled in. The chimes outside whispered, tinkle-tinkle.

“Go!”

They played again. Rico watched their careful faces. Bella’s eyes were shiny and focused. Milo’s ears wiggled with pride. Tessa sat tall and still.

“Pause!”

Silence, like a cozy blanket.

They did it again and again. Sometimes Rico almost strummed early, but he remembered the sign. Hands still and eyes on the leader. He felt responsible. He was the leader, and leaders follow directions too.

Soon the song sounded smooth, like honey. When they finished, everyone cheered. “We did it!” called Milo.

Rico poured warm cinnamon tea into tiny cups. They sipped and smiled. The tea tasted sweet and spicy. The treehouse felt safe and happy.

Bella said, “Following directions helped us learn.”

Tessa nodded. “And it helped us be kind and take turns.”

Milo giggled. “It made the song sound good!”

Rico wrapped his tail around himself and thought. “Today I learned something important,” he said. “Following directions helps me do my best. It keeps us safe, helps us learn, and shows responsibility. I almost forgot, but I listened, I fixed it, and I tried again.”

He stood and pointed to the yellow sign. “Let’s make this our music promise.”

They all placed a paw, a wing, a little hand, and a shell tip on the sign. “We promise to follow directions, to learn, and to be responsible,” they said together.

Outside, the sun dipped low and painted the sky orange and pink. The chimes sang a gentle goodnight song.

Rico smiled. “Thank you for making music with me. We did it right, and it felt so good.”

They packed up quietly, following the directions on the sign. Spots clean. Instruments safe. Treehouse tidy.

As his friends waved goodbye, Rico gave the drum a soft pat. “We’ll play again tomorrow,” he whispered. “And we’ll follow directions first.”

The End.
