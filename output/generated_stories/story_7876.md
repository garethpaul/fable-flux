# Shelly's Garden Adventure

On a sunny balcony high above the city, there lived a wise old tortoise named Shelly. Her shell was green and brown like the leaves around her, and she moved slowly but surely through her beautiful garden home.

The balcony was filled with colorful flowers, tall tomato plants, and fragrant herbs. Shelly loved her garden dearly and knew exactly how to take care of each plant.

One morning, three young rabbits hopped onto the balcony. "Wow!" said the first rabbit, Pip. "Look at all these yummy plants!"

"Let's eat everything!" squeaked the second rabbit, Dot.

"Wait, wait!" called Shelly in her gentle, calm voice. "Welcome to my garden, little ones. But before you start nibbling, would you like to learn the garden rules?"

The third rabbit, Benny, wrinkled his nose. "Rules are boring! We just want to eat!"

Pip and Dot nodded eagerly. They started hopping around, grabbing leaves from different plants. Pip bit into a spicy pepper plant and immediately started coughing. "Yuck! This tastes terrible!"

Dot tried to munch on a thorny rose stem. "Ouch! This hurt my mouth!"

Benny laughed at his friends but then tried to eat some mint leaves. The strong taste made him scrunch up his face. "Bleh! This is too strong!"

Shelly watched patiently as the three rabbits made mistake after mistake. Her wise eyes twinkled with understanding.

"Oh dear," said Shelly kindly. "Are you ready to listen to the garden directions now?"

The three rabbits looked at each other, feeling silly. Their tummies were upset, Dot's mouth was sore, and they were all very confused.

"Yes, please," said Pip quietly. "We should have listened to you first."

Shelly smiled warmly. "That's perfectly okay, little ones. Everyone learns differently, and I understand that. Now, let me show you the right way."

She moved slowly to a patch of sweet lettuce. "These green leaves are perfect for rabbits," she explained. "They're gentle and tasty."

Next, she pointed to some carrot tops with her wrinkled foot. "And these orange vegetables are buried in the soil. If you dig carefully, you'll find a wonderful treat."

"But how do we know which plants are safe?" asked Benny.

"That's a wonderful question!" said Shelly. "In my garden, I always follow three simple directions: First, ask before you taste. Second, start with small bites. Third, if it looks different or smells strong, wait for help."

The rabbits listened carefully this time. They followed Shelly's directions exactly. Pip asked permission before trying the lettuce. Dot took small bites of the carrot. Benny waited for Shelly's help with the herbs.

"This lettuce is delicious!" said Pip happily.

"And this carrot is so sweet!" added Dot.

"Thank you for being patient with us," said Benny. "Even though we didn't listen at first, you still helped us."

Shelly's eyes sparkled with joy. "I'm proud of you for learning to follow directions. And I'm glad you can now enjoy my garden safely. You've also learned to be independent by asking good questions!"

From that day forward, the three rabbits visited Shelly's garden often. They always remembered to follow her directions, and they learned to take care of themselves by making smart choices.

As the sun set over the balcony garden, Shelly smiled contentedly. She had taught the young rabbits that following directions keeps everyone safe and happy.

The End.
