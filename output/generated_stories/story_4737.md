# Dolly Dolphin’s Quiet Splash

Dolly the playful dolphin loved to splash, flip, and giggle. One sunny morning, Dolly swam up to her special study corner. It wasn’t in the sea today. It was a cozy corner made just for learning, with a little desk, soft pillows, and a round blue rug that felt like smooth sand under her flippers. On the desk were crayons, picture cards of oceans and lands, and a tiny globe that spun with a soft whirr.

“Good morning, Study Corner!” Dolly chirped, her voice bubbly and bright.

Her friend, a small crab named Crisp, waved a tiny red claw. “Good morning, Dolly! Today we learn about respect,” Crisp said, clicking gently so it sounded like raindrops on shells.

Dolly took a deep breath. The air smelled like clean paper and salty sea breeze from a nearby open window. “Respect,” Dolly repeated. “What does it feel like?”

“It feels like listening ears, soft voices, and kind choices,” Crisp said. “It means we care about people, places, and our big blue world.”

Dolly’s eyes sparkled. “Let’s start with a warm-up! My body wants to wiggle.” She bounced lightly on the blue rug. “Exercise time!”

“Great idea,” Crisp agreed. “But gentle, please. This is a study corner.”

Dolly nodded. “Gentle,” she promised. She did quiet dolphin stretches. “Arms up like seaweed,” she said, lifting her fins high. “Sway side to side, swish, swish.” The rug felt warm on her belly when she bent down. She took calm breaths. In, like a wave. Out, like a sigh.

“Respect for the room,” Crisp said, smiling. “Good job.”

Dolly giggled. “I want to jump and splash!” She wiggled, almost knocking the crayons. She stopped. “Ooh! I remember. Gentle.”

She opened the crayon box slowly. The crayons smelled waxy and sweet. She picked the blue one and drew a big ocean. “This is our home,” she said. “Look, Crisp! Fish friends.”

Crisp pointed to the tiny globe. “And here are homes far away. Some friends live where it is very cold. Some where it is very hot. Respect means we care about their homes too.”

Dolly spun the globe softly. It hummed and clicked. “Hello, world!” she whispered. She drew a penguin with a scarf, a camel with a smile, and a child waving from a sandy beach. “We share the Earth,” she said. “We keep it clean.”

Crisp nodded. “That is respect for our planet.”

Knock, knock. Two classmates peeked in: Tiny Turtle and Busy Bee. “Can we join?” asked Tiny Turtle in a slow, gentle voice.

“Yes, please,” Dolly said. She scooted over and patted the soft pillow. “We can all learn.”

Busy Bee buzzed in a happy circle. “Bzzz! Let’s play ‘Quiet Ocean, Loud Ocean’!”

Dolly clapped, then paused. “Let’s ask the room,” she said. “Study Corner, is it okay?”

The room was quiet, but Dolly listened with her heart. She remembered: soft voices, kind choices. “We can play in a quiet way,” she decided. “That is respect.”

They began. Dolly made small waves with her fins—swish, swish. Tiny Turtle tapped the rug—tap, tap. Busy Bee buzzed low—bzzz. Crisp clicked tiny clacks—click, click. It felt calm and friendly. Everyone smiled.

After the game, Dolly felt wiggly again. “Let’s do more exercise!” she said. “Ocean moves!”

They did dolphin jumps without leaving the rug, like little bounces. They did turtle stretches—slow and long. They did bee wings—gentle arm flaps. The air felt warm. Their hearts felt happy. They took water breaks. “Sip, sip,” Dolly said, licking a cool drop off her lip. It tasted fresh.

Suddenly, a crayon rolled to the desk edge. “Oh!” Dolly gasped. She reached fast. The crayon fell and landed with a soft plop. Busy Bee zipped toward it. “Mine!” Bee buzzed.

Dolly held up a fin. “Let’s be respectful,” she said softly. “We can share and take turns.”

Bee hovered, then slowed. “Bzzz… You’re right. You can go first.”

Dolly shook her head kindly. “Thank you, Bee. We can use it together.” She broke the drawing into parts. “I color the water. You color the sky. Tiny Turtle, you add a mountain. Crisp, you make little homes. Homes for penguins, camels, and kids.”

They worked side by side. The paper felt smooth under their careful touches. The colors looked bright and happy. Dolly listened to Bee’s quiet buzz, Turtle’s tiny taps, and Crisp’s click-click. It sounded like a soft song.

When they were done, Dolly looked at the picture. “We made a big world,” she said. “We took turns. We used gentle bodies. We used kind voices. We respected each other, the room, and the planet.”

Tiny Turtle smiled. “I feel calm,” he said.

Busy Bee buzzed softly. “I feel proud.”

Crisp clacked, “I feel safe.”

Dolly’s heart felt warm, like sunshine on water. “I learned that respect is love in action,” she said. “It is listening, sharing, and caring for our big world.”

They cleaned up together. Crayons back in the box. Pillows fluffed. Rug smoothed. Dolly spun the globe one more time. Whirr. “Good night, world,” she whispered.

“Good job, learners,” Crisp said. “You had exercise. You showed respect. You learned about our world.”

Dolly gave a happy wiggle, small and quiet. “I can do respect every day,” she said. “At home, at school, and in our study corner.”

Everyone nodded. They touched fins, claws, wings, and flippers in a gentle group hug. It felt soft and warm.

The End.
