# Fawn's Quiet Craft Morning

In a comfortable nursery, sunlight made soft golden squares on the rug. A gentle deer fawn named Fern blinked her big brown eyes. She stretched her tiny legs and wiggled her soft ears. The room smelled like clean blankets and fresh crayons. The mobile above her crib tinkled a little song.

“Good morning,” Fern whispered to her stuffed owl, Hoot. “Today we make a craft.”

Fern trotted to her craft corner. Baskets held buttons and ribbons. Jars held pom-poms and sticks. Paper sat in a neat pile, smooth and bright. Fern’s hoof tapped happily. But when she pulled out the glue, three little nursery friends popped up.

Pip the mouse squeaked, “Me first! I want shiny stars!”

Bibi the bunny said, “I need the purple ribbon right now.”

Toto the turtle blinked slowly. “I… would like… one blue pom-pom… please.”

All the voices together sounded busy and buzzy. Fern’s heart went thump-thump faster. Her ears felt warm. The room felt too full. The glue smelled sweet and sticky.

Fern took a slow breath in. She let a slow breath out. She remembered what her caregiver, Nana, always said: “Calm is a soft blanket for your mind.”

Fern put a hoof on her chest. “We can be calm,” she said in a gentle voice. “We can be organized. Then we can all make something nice.”

Pip twitched his whiskers. “But I want stars now!”

Fern shook her head softly. “We will take turns. We can make a plan.” She smiled and pointed to the baskets. “First, let’s sort our things. We put ribbons in this basket. We put buttons in this cup. We put pom-poms in this jar.”

Bibi’s ears perked up. “Sorting is fun!”

The friends began to sort. Ribbons felt smooth and silky in their paws. Buttons clicked like tiny rain. Pom-poms felt fluffy and light. Fern hummed a calm tune while they worked. The nursery sounded like soft clinks and little pats.

When the craft table looked neat, Fern drew a big circle on a piece of paper with a fat blue crayon. “This is our plan circle,” she said. “We will make a kindness wreath. We each add three things. We take turns.”

Pip’s nose twitched. “How do we choose who goes first?”

Fern thought for a moment. She took another slow breath. “We can negotiate,” she said. “That means we talk and make a fair choice.”

Toto raised his tiny foot. “I am very slow. Maybe I can go second so I have time to pick.”

Bibi nodded. “I can go last. I like to watch.”

Pip looked at the stars. “I can go first, but I will share the shiny ones.”

Fern smiled. “That is fair. The order is Pip, Toto, Fern, Bibi. Then we go around again.”

The room felt peaceful now. The light was warm. Fern could smell the paper and a little bit of glue. Pip went first. He pressed two stars and one red button on the circle. “They sparkle!” he giggled.

Toto went next. He moved slowly and carefully. He chose one blue pom-pom, one green leaf sticker, and one tiny shell. “I like cool colors,” he said softly.

Fern added a soft ribbon bow, a yellow circle like the sun, and a small cotton cloud. The cotton felt cushy on her hoof. “This feels like a calm sky,” she whispered.

Bibi hopped happily. She added a purple ribbon, a silver heart, and a pink flower. The wreath looked friendly and bright.

Pip reached for more stars, but there were only two left. He frowned. “I wanted three.”

Fern spoke gently. “Let’s use our calm voices. We can share. We can trade.”

Bibi held up her silver heart. “Pip, do you want this heart for one star?”

Pip thought, then nodded. “Yes, please.” They swapped with smiles.

Toto blinked. “I have an extra leaf. Would anyone like it?”

Fern said, “Thank you, Toto. I will trade you a blue dot.”

They traded, too. The wreath grew fuller and prettier with each small, kind choice. The air felt easy. The table stayed tidy. The friends’ paws and hooves stayed clean because they wiped glue drips with little cloths.

At last, the kindness wreath was done. It smelled faintly like paper and a tiny bit like Nana’s soap. Fern held it up. The ribbons swished softly. The stars winked. Everyone clapped.

Nana peeked into the nursery. “What a lovely wreath,” she said. “How did you make it together?”

Fern lifted her chin, proud but quiet. “We stayed calm. We got organized. We made a plan. We talked and traded. We took turns.”

Nana smiled. “That is wonderful. Calm hearts make smart plans.”

Fern hung the wreath near the crib where the light could tickle it. She felt warm and peaceful inside, like a snug blanket. Pip, Bibi, and Toto snuggled close.

Fern whispered, “When we feel busy and buzzy, we can breathe, sort, and make a plan.”

“Calm and kind,” Toto added.

“Share and shine,” Pip said.

“Neat and sweet,” Bibi giggled.

Fern closed her eyes and listened to the soft nursery sounds: the clock’s quiet tick, the mobile’s tiny chime, her friends’ gentle breaths. She smiled.

Today’s adventure was small and slow. It was just right.

The End.
