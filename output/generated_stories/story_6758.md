# Leo’s Cozy Kitchen Cleanup

In a warm kitchen filled with sweet smells, a brave little lion cub named Leo padded in on soft paws. The oven hummed, and the sun shined through the window like a friendly smile. Pots made tiny clinks, and a wooden spoon rested on the counter like a sleepy stick.

Leo wiggled his whiskers. “Mmm! I smell cinnamon,” he purred. His tummy gave a happy rumble. He wanted a snack. He wanted to help, too.

On the table, colorful bowls sat side by side. Flour dusted the air like snow, and shiny spoons peeked out of a big jar. Leo’s friend, Pip the raccoon, stood on a little stool, wearing a small apron with yellow stripes.

“Hi, Pip!” Leo said bravely. “What are you making?”

“Banana muffins!” chirped Pip. “I’m stirring, stirring, stirring.” Pip’s tail swished, and batter made soft plop-plop sounds in the bowl.

Just then, Mama Hen walked in, feathers fluffed, glasses perched on her beak. “Good morning, helpers,” she clucked. “We can bake together. But first—let’s find everything we need.”

Leo looked around. The kitchen felt cozy, but it looked busy. Measuring cups were under a towel. A mixing whisk hid behind a bag of sugar. A trail of flour led to the door like tiny white footprints.

“I can help!” Leo said. His brave heart felt big. He jumped onto a chair and reached for a whisk. “Found it!”

Pip nodded. “I need the small cup. The one that says one-half.”

Leo saw many cups. Big cups. Little cups. Cups with numbers. Leo grabbed three at once. Clatter-clatter! They fell like metal bells. “Oops,” Leo whispered. His ears drooped.

Mama Hen flapped gently. “It’s okay, Leo. We will make a plan. Organization helps us work as a team.”

“Or-gan-i-za-tion,” Leo tried the word. It felt like a long noodle in his mouth.

Mama Hen smiled. “Organization means everything has a place. When we put things where they belong, we find them fast. We show respect for others’ things, too.”

Pip pointed to the counter. “We can make homes for our tools! A home for cups, a home for spoons, a home for bowls.”

Leo’s eyes shined. “Like a map!”

Together they made a simple plan. Pip drew little picture labels on sticky notes: a spoon picture for the spoon jar, a cup picture for the measuring cups, and a whisk picture for the whisk hook. Leo placed the notes gently in their spots. Tap, tap.

“Let’s try it,” Mama Hen said. “Leo, please put the cups in their cup home.”

Leo picked up each cup and looked at the size. He found the one that said 1/2. “This is the small helper,” he giggled. He set it in the cup home and lined the others from big to small. Click, click, click.

Pip clapped. “I can find them so quickly now!”

Next, Leo reached for the sugar bag. He started to tug, but he saw Mama Hen’s careful eyes. “May I pick this up?” Leo asked.

“Thank you for asking,” Mama Hen said. “That is respect.”

Leo lifted the bag slowly. He poured sugar into a bowl. Shhhhh. The sugar sounded like soft sand on a beach. Pip scooped flour—poof! A tiny cloud puffed up, and both friends giggled.

“Time to crack eggs,” Mama Hen said. “One, two.”

Leo saw a little shell piece fall into the bowl. His brave heart thumped. “Oh no! I made a mistake.”

Pip smiled kindly. “We can fix it together.” Pip held the big spoon while Leo used the whisk like a tiny net. Plip! Out came the shell.

“Teamwork,” Mama Hen said warmly. “And when we keep things tidy, we have space to see and fix small troubles.”

They mixed. They poured. The oven ticked and glowed. Soon the kitchen smelled cozy and sweet, like hugs. While the muffins baked, Leo looked at the counter. It was almost clean, but not quite.

“I want to organize more,” Leo said. His tail swished with brave energy. He wiped little drips with a cloth—wipe, wipe. He put the milk back in the fridge—cool air kissed his nose. He stacked the bowls—soft clinks like tiny chimes. He placed the spoon in its spoon home—plunk!

Pip lined up napkins and set cups in a neat row. “This looks calm,” Pip said. “I feel calm too.”

Mama Hen fluffed her feathers. “When our space is organized, our hearts feel peaceful.”

Ding! The timer sang. Mama Hen slid the muffins out. Warm steam puffed up, smelling like bananas and sunshine. She set them on a rack. The tops were golden and soft.

Leo inhaled the sweet smell. “Can we share with Mr. Tortoise next door?” he asked. “He likes soft snacks.”

“That’s thoughtful,” Mama Hen said. “Sharing shows respect and friendship.”

They packed two muffins in a little box. Leo put the box on a clean plate. “Everything has a place,” he said proudly. “Even the muffins have a home.”

Pip giggled. “A muffin home!”

They sat together at the table. Warm muffin crumbs tickled Leo’s whiskers. He felt proud, helpful, and calm.

“I learned something big,” Leo said. “When we organize, we find things fast, we keep them safe, and we show respect for our friends.”

Mama Hen nodded. “Yes. Organization is a kind of kindness.”

Pip lifted a muffin. “To friends who tidy together!”

Leo lifted his, too. “To brave, tidy friends!”

They munched and smiled in the warm kitchen, where everything had a happy home.

The End.
