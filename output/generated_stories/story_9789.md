# Ollie Otter’s Brave Study Corner

Ollie was a playful otter with shiny whiskers and a round, bouncy belly. He loved his cozy study corner. It had a soft rug, a tiny desk, and a lamp that made warm, golden light. His pencils smelled like fresh wood. His books felt smooth and cool. He liked to sit and wiggle his toes while he learned.

Today was Story Sharing Day. Ollie had a special book from Grandma Otter. “It’s a book of songs and stories from our river home,” Grandma had said. The cover showed otters wearing bright bead necklaces and patterned wraps. The pages were full of river songs, family dances, and stories about clean paws and brave hearts.

Ollie smiled. “I will share our song,” he said. He touched the beads on the cover. They felt bumpy and bright. He felt excited and a little nervous too. His tummy did a tiny flip-flop.

His friends waddled in. There was Mia the mouse, Pip the parrot, and Tiko the turtle. Teacher Bear stood by the whiteboard. She smelled like honey and mint tea.

“Welcome, little learners,” said Teacher Bear. “Today, we share, we listen, and we care.”

Ollie held up his book. “I have songs from my family,” he said. “We sing by the river. We wash our paws before we dance.” He swished his whiskers.

Pip flapped his wings. “Sing! Sing!” he squawked. Mia clapped. Tiko nodded very slowly.

Ollie opened to the first page. He took a breath. But then—oh no! He saw smudges on the cover. Sticky jam! His paws felt tacky. His whiskers drooped.

“My book is messy,” Ollie whispered. “And my paws are sticky.” His tummy flip-flopped again.

Teacher Bear knelt beside him. “We have a little problem,” she said softly. “What can we do?”

Ollie looked at the sink by the study corner. The water made a soft hush sound. The soap smelled like oranges. He remembered Grandma’s words: “Clean paws show care. Brave hearts share.”

Ollie stood up tall, as tall as an otter can. “Friends,” he said, “I am nervous. But I will be brave. I will lead a clean-paw parade!”

“A parade?” squeaked Mia.

“Yes,” said Ollie. “We will wash. Then we will share and sing.”

Teacher Bear smiled. “Ollie, that is brave leadership,” she said. “Show us the way.”

Ollie marched to the sink. “Step one,” he said, “roll up sleeves.” He rolled his. “Step two, warm water.” He turned the tap. “Step three, soap! Bubbles on both sides.” He rubbed his paws. Squish, squish, swish. Bubbles tickled. The water felt warm and gentle. “Step four, rinse and shake.” Drip, drip, drip. “Step five, dry with a clean towel.”

Mia copied. Pip fluffed and laughed at the bubbles. Tiko took his time, humming. Soon, their paws were clean and shiny.

Ollie wiped the book cover with a soft cloth. The jam was gone. He took a deep breath. His chest felt light. His tail did a happy wiggle.

“Thank you for washing,” he said. “It shows respect. In my family, we wash before we share food or stories. It keeps us healthy, and it shows love.”

“That is a beautiful custom,” said Teacher Bear. “We appreciate your culture, Ollie.”

Ollie nodded. “Would you like to learn our welcome song?”

“Yes!” everyone chimed.

Ollie began in a gentle voice. “River bright, paws so clean, hearts are brave, and voices keen.” He tapped a rhythm on his desk. Tap-tap, tap. The lamp made a cozy glow. The room smelled like oranges and pencils.

Mia sang softly. Pip added a high chirp. Tiko hummed low and steady. Teacher Bear clapped the beat. Ollie led with a big, brave smile. He showed them the dance steps: step-step, swish-swish, wiggle-wiggle. He wore a beaded ribbon Grandma had tied on his backpack. The beads clicked like tiny river stones.

Pip paused. “What if I sing wrong?” he whispered.

Ollie took a brave breath. “It is okay to try,” he said. “Being brave means trying even when you feel wobbly.”

Pip nodded and sang again. His voice grew clear and bright.

When the song ended, everyone cheered. The study corner felt warm and happy. Ollie’s heart felt big and brave.

Teacher Bear said, “Ollie, you found a problem, and you led a solution. You were brave and kind. You taught us about your family’s ways, and you showed us good hygiene.”

Ollie giggled. “Clean paws, strong hearts,” he said.

Mia wiggled her whiskers. “I learned to wash well.”

Tiko smiled slowly. “I learned to be brave and try.”

Pip flapped. “I learned a new song!”

Ollie hugged his book. He felt proud. He looked at his friends and said, “When we care for ourselves and for each other, we can do brave things.”

Teacher Bear nodded. “That is our lesson today.”

Ollie sat back on the soft rug. The lamp glowed. The room smelled sweet and safe. His study corner was tidy, and his heart was steady. He knew he could be brave again tomorrow.

The End.
