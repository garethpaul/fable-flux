# Benny Bear's Big Feelings

Benny the bear cub stood in front of the big bathroom mirror, his fuzzy brown face looking worried. His tummy felt all twisty, and his paws felt shaky. Today was his first day at forest school, and he had BIG feelings swirling around inside him like a whirlwind.

"Mama!" Benny called out, his voice sounding small and scared. "I don't want to go to school. My tummy feels funny!"

Mama Bear came into the bathroom, her warm smile making the room feel cozier. She knelt down beside Benny and looked at him in the mirror. "Tell me about these feelings, little one. What does your body feel like right now?"

Benny touched his fluffy belly. "It feels like butterflies are dancing in there. And my paws are all trembly, see?" He held up his small paws, which were indeed shaking a little.

"Those are nervous feelings," Mama Bear said gently. "It's okay to feel nervous about new things. Your body is telling you that something important is happening."

"But I don't like feeling this way," Benny whimpered, his brown eyes getting watery.

Mama Bear had an idea. "Let's try something special. When we have big feelings, we can help our bodies feel better with some gentle exercise. Want to try?"

Benny nodded, curious despite his worries.

"First, let's take three deep breaths together," Mama Bear said. "Breathe in through your nose like you're smelling honey." They both took a big sniff. "Now breathe out through your mouth like you're blowing bubbles." They exhaled slowly.

Benny felt a tiny bit calmer. The twisty feeling in his tummy was still there, but not as strong.

"Now, let's do some brave bear stretches," Mama Bear suggested. "Reach your paws up high like you're picking berries from a tall tree!" Benny stretched up, standing on his tippy-toes.

"Good! Now let's march in place like strong forest bears!" They marched together, their paws making soft padding sounds on the bathroom floor. Benny started to giggle as they marched faster and faster.

"How do your feelings look now?" Mama Bear asked, pointing to the mirror.

Benny looked at his reflection. His face wasn't as worried anymore, and his paws had stopped shaking. "The butterflies are still there, but they're not dancing as crazy now," he said thoughtfully.

"That's wonderful, Benny! You were very brave to talk about your feelings and try something new to help yourself feel better. That takes courage!"

Benny stood a little taller. "I was brave, wasn't I?"

"Very brave. And you know what? It's okay to still feel a little nervous about school. Those butterflies might be excited butterflies too - excited to meet new friends and learn new things!"

Benny considered this. "Maybe the butterflies want to go to school too?"

"I think they do," Mama Bear said with a warm hug. "And if you feel nervous again at school, you can take deep breaths and remember that feelings are okay. You can even tell your teacher about your butterflies."

"I can talk about my feelings?" Benny asked.

"Always, sweetie. Talking about feelings helps them feel less scary."

Benny looked in the mirror one more time. He still felt nervous, but now he also felt proud and a little excited. "I think I'm ready for school now, Mama."

"I think you are too, brave little bear."

The End.
