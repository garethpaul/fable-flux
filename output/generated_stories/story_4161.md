# Cubby’s Kitchen Plan

In a warm kitchen that smelled like cinnamon and honey, a friendly bear cub named Cubby wiggled his nose. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. The kettle hummed. The fridge hummed too. Cubby’s tummy rumbled.

“I want to make a snack,” Cubby said. “I want a rainbow fruit bowl!”

Mama Bear smiled, wiping her paws on a soft towel. “That sounds delicious,” she said. “Remember, Cubby, good plans help good goals. What is your goal?”

Cubby thought. He tapped his chin. “My goal is to make a healthy rainbow bowl all by myself,” he said. “With help when I need it.”

“Great goal,” Mama said. “Let’s make a plan. First, we wash our paws. Next, we check what we have. Then we chop safely, and we clean up. Can you say the steps?”

Cubby raised one paw and wiggled his claws. “Wash, check, chop, clean!” he said slowly.

“Perfect,” Mama said. “That is our plan.”

Cubby ran warm water. Drip, drip, drip. He rubbed his paws with soap. Squish, squish. The bubbles smelled like lemons. “Wash!” he said, smiling. He dried his paws on a clean towel.

“Now we check,” Mama said, opening the cool fridge. A gentle puff of cold air touched Cubby’s fur. He peeked inside. “We have red strawberries, orange slices, green kiwi, and blue blueberries,” Cubby said. He looked again. “And we still have half a banana from yesterday.”

Mama nodded. “Good careful checking. Let’s use what we have first. That helps us not waste food. That is called conservation. We save food, we save water, we save our world.”

Cubby’s eyes grew wide. “Con-ser-va-tion,” he said slowly. “I can do that.”

He set the fruits on the counter one by one. Plop, plop, plop. He felt the bumpy orange. He smelled sweet strawberries. He heard the soft buzz of the fridge door closing.

“Next is chop,” Mama said, placing a small safe knife on a little cutting board. “I will help you. You can do big bear hands, and I will guide.”

Cubby held a strawberry. “Red first,” he said. “Like a stop sign. Stop and think.”

“What do we think about?” Mama asked.

Cubby scrunched his face. “We think: Is this safe? Do I know the step? Do I need help?” He looked up. “I need help to chop.”

“Good critical thinking,” Mama said warmly. “I will hold the strawberry. You push gently.”

Slice. The strawberry became little hearts. Cubby giggled. “They smell so sweet!” he said. He put them in a bowl. Plink, plink.

They peeled the banana. The peel went soft in Cubby’s paws. “Mama, where does the peel go?” he asked.

“In the compost bowl,” Mama said, pointing to a small green pail. “We turn scraps into soil. That is conservation too.”

Cubby dropped the peel into the pail. Thunk. “Save, not waste,” he said proudly.

They sliced kiwi. The green smelled fresh, like a tiny forest. Blueberries rolled like marbles. “Catch them!” Cubby laughed, cupping his paws. Plop, plop, plop into the bowl.

“Orange next,” Cubby said. “But the bowl looks full.”

“Let’s think,” Mama said. “What was your goal?”

Cubby looked at the bowl. “A rainbow fruit bowl,” he said softly. “Red, orange, yellow, green, blue. We have red, green, and blue. Banana is yellow. We need orange!”

He looked at the orange slices on a plate. “We can add just a few,” he decided. “Not too many. We share the rest later.” He placed three orange moons on top. “Now it is a rainbow!”

Mama clapped softly. “You followed your plan. Wash, check, chop, clean.”

Cubby looked around. “Clean!” he said. He put the berry tops and kiwi peels in the compost pail. He wiped the counter with a warm, soapy cloth. Swish, swish. He rinsed the knife with Mama’s help. Sssshh. He put the cloth in the wash basket.

Cubby took a deep breath. The kitchen smelled fruity and fresh. The bowl looked bright and happy. His tummy felt excited. “I did my goal!” he cheered.

“You did,” Mama said, hugging him. “How does it feel?”

Cubby smiled so big his cheeks felt warm. “It feels proud,” he said. “It feels strong.”

They sat at the table. The wood felt smooth under Cubby’s paws. He took a bite of strawberry. Sweet and soft. A bite of kiwi. Zing! A blueberry popped. Pop! The banana was creamy. The orange was sunny. “This is yummy and healthy,” Cubby said. “Nutri… nu-tri-tion!”

“Nutrition means food that helps you grow,” Mama said. “Your plan helped you make a good choice.”

Cubby wiggled. “Can I make a plan chart?” he asked. “For next time?”

Mama gave him a big sheet and a crayon. Scratch, scratch. Cubby drew four boxes and little pictures: soapy paws, a fridge, a cutting board, and a broom. He drew a small leaf on the compost pail too.

“This is my Goal Plan,” Cubby said. “I can use it every day.”

Mama nodded. “Goals can be big or small. With steps, you can do them all.”

Cubby looked at his rainbow bowl, his clean counter, and his plan chart. He felt calm and happy. “I can set goals,” he whispered. “I can finish tasks. I can save and not waste.”

He took one more juicy bite and licked a sweet drop from his nose. “Tomorrow,” Cubby said, “my goal is veggie stars!”

“We will make a plan,” Mama said, smiling.

Cubby giggled. “Wash, check, chop, clean!”

The End.
