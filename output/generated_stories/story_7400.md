# Dotty the Dolphin’s Backyard Book

Dotty was a playful dolphin with bright eyes and a big smile. She loved to splash and laugh. One sunny day, Dotty visited her friend Mia’s sunny backyard. The grass felt soft and warm. Bees hummed. A gentle breeze smelled like sweet flowers and fresh soap from the clothesline.

“Hi, Mia!” Dotty clicked and squeaked. “Let’s make a story today!”

Mia clapped. “Yes! We can write a book. We can be backyard writers!”

Dotty wiggled her flippers. “I can’t hold a pencil like you. But I can share ideas. We can write them together.”

They set up a little table under the shade of a big umbrella. On the table were paper, crayons, and a blue pencil. A small bin sat nearby, one side for trash and one side for recycling. A soap pump and a towel waited on a stool by a little bucket of clean water.

Mia opened a notebook. “What should our story be about?”

Dotty closed her eyes. She listened to the leaves whisper. She heard birds sing, “Tweet, tweet!” She smelled the lemony soap on the towel. She felt happy and calm. “Let’s write about a bubble that goes on a trip!” Dotty said.

Mia smiled. “Good idea! I will write the words. You say them.”

Dotty took a deep breath. “Once there was a brave bubble,” she said. “The bubble liked to float over the sunny backyard.”

Scratch, scratch went the pencil. Mia wrote the words. “I drew a bubble,” Mia said. She held up the page. The bubble was round and shiny. Dotty saw tiny rainbow colors.

Dotty giggled. “The bubble met a bird. The bird said hello.”

Mia wrote and drew. “Hello!” she whispered for the bird’s voice. They both laughed. The air felt light and fun.

They worked and worked. They used three pages, then four, then five. Soon, there were crayon bits and tiny paper scraps on the table. A crayon wrapper slipped to the grass.

Dotty noticed. “Oops! We should clean as we go,” she said kindly. “Writers keep their space neat. It helps our brains feel clear.”

Mia nodded. “Yes. We recycle paper. We throw away trash.”

They picked up the wrapper. Dotty read the bin labels out loud. “Paper and cardboard here. Other trash there.” She dropped the wrapper in the trash side. Mia put the paper scraps into the recycling side. The soft clink and rustle sounded tidy and nice.

Dotty touched the towel. “Let’s wash our hands, too,” she said. “Clean hands help our pages stay clean.” She squeezed a little soap. Squish! It smelled fresh like lemons. They washed and rubbed. Splash, splash went the water. They dried with the towel. Their hands felt soft and safe.

Mia smiled. “Now we can write more.”

Dotty took another deep breath. “The bubble met the sun,” she said. “The sun said, ‘Shine, friend!’ The bubble felt brave.”

Mia wrote and drew a bright sun. The yellow crayon slid smooth across the page. Dotty watched the color glow. She felt proud. “Writing is like talking, but on paper,” she said. “It helps us share.”

Mia nodded slowly. “When we write, our ideas can stay. We can read them later.”

A breeze blew. Some pages wiggled. One page flew off the table. “Oh no!” Mia cried.

Dotty leaped and caught it with a gentle flip of her tail. “Got it!” she cheered. The page smelled like crayons and sunshine. She set it back and placed a clean rock on top.

They kept going. Dotty spoke the next lines. Mia wrote carefully. She made spaces. She used simple words. “The bubble came home,” Dotty said softly. “The bubble felt loved.”

Mia put down the pencil. “We did it,” she whispered. “Our book is done.”

Dotty looked around. The table was neat. The recycling bin held paper scraps. The trash bin had wrappers. Their hands were clean. The air felt bright and smooth. The birds still sang. The grass tickled Dotty’s tummy when she leaned down to laugh.

“Let’s read it out loud,” Dotty said.

They read together. Their voices were warm and proud. The pages made a soft swish, swish sound. When they finished, Mia hugged the notebook. “I feel brave like the bubble,” she said.

Dotty nodded. “Writing helps us notice small things,” she said. “Like smells, sounds, and feelings. Writing helps us share kindness.”

Mia smiled. “And writers clean up, wash hands, and recycle. That keeps our backyard happy, too.”

Dotty clapped her flippers. “Today we learned three good things,” she said. “We write our ideas. We keep our hands and pages clean. We recycle to help the Earth.”

The sun began to yawn in the sky. The backyard felt cozy. Dotty and Mia placed their book in a dry, safe box. Mia washed the crayon off her fingers. Dotty helped sort the last scraps with a gentle hum.

“We are backyard writers,” Mia said.

“We are clean and kind helpers,” Dotty added.

They both grinned. Their hearts felt big and bright, like the sun.

The End.
