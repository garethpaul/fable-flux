# Cubby Saves the Porch Day

Cubby was a friendly bear cub with soft, fuzzy fur and bright, curious eyes. He loved to sit on the peaceful front porch of his forest cabin. The porch smelled like warm wood and pine needles. Sunlight made little golden squares on the floor. A cool breeze tickled Cubby’s nose. Birds sang sweet “tweet-tweet” songs, and a mama squirrel chattered hello from the railing.

“Good morning, porch,” Cubby said with a big smile. “Let’s have a clean, green day!”

Cubby’s friend, Pip the raccoon, padded up the steps with soft, tap-tap paws. Pip carried a crinkly bag. “Hi, Cubby! Can we play snack toss?” Pip giggled and tossed a wrapper into the air.

The crinkly wrapper floated down and landed near the porch plants. Cubby’s nose wrinkled. He saw two more wrappers under the rocking chair and a plastic cup rolling in the breeze.

“Oh,” Cubby said softly. His heart felt wobbly. The porch did not look peaceful now. It looked messy. A butterfly fluttered close, then flew away from the cup. A tiny beetle bumped into a wrapper and turned around.

Cubby took a deep breath. He remembered what Mama Bear taught him: “We care for our home. We keep it clean. That’s conservation. We help nature by being careful.”

Cubby stood tall, even though his knees felt shaky. He spoke in a kind, clear voice. “Pip, I like playing with you. But the wrappers and cup are hurting our porch and our animal friends. The butterfly and beetle need a clean place. Please don’t toss trash. Let’s clean up.”

Pip blinked. “I didn’t think about that,” he said. His striped tail drooped. “I’m sorry, Cubby.”

Cubby nodded. “It’s okay. We can fix it together.” He smiled. “We can be porch protectors!”

Pip’s eyes lit up. “Porch protectors! I like that.”

Cubby fetched a small broom that smelled like straw. Pip found a little basket. They picked up the wrappers. “Crinkle, crinkle,” went the plastic. They scooped the rolling cup. “Plop,” it went into the basket. Cubby swept pine needles into a neat pile. “Swish, swish,” went the broom.

A breeze brought the sweet smell of clover. The birds sang brighter. The porch looked happier already.

Mama Bear stepped outside, carrying a jug of cool water that glug-glugged softly. “Hello, helpers,” she said. “What are you two up to?”

Cubby lifted his chin. “We are porch protectors, Mama. We are cleaning and caring for our home. That is conservation.”

Mama Bear smiled and rubbed Cubby’s ears. “That is responsible and kind. Thank you for speaking up, Cubby. Thank you for helping, Pip.”

Pip grinned. “Cubby was brave and kind. He asked me to stop tossing trash. I listened.”

Mama Bear set down three little bins. One had a blue sticker with a bottle. One had a green sticker with a leaf. One had a gray sticker with a trash can. “Let’s sort,” she said. “Blue is for recycling, green is for compost, and gray is for trash.”

Cubby and Pip sorted slowly. They touched each item and decided. “This cup goes to blue,” said Cubby. “It can be made new.” Pip held up an apple core from under the chair. “Green for compost! It will help plants grow.” They clapped when they finished. “We did it!”

A shy bunny hopped up to the porch steps. “Hello,” she whispered. “I like the smell here now.” She twitched her nose. “May I sit?”

“Yes,” said Cubby. “There is space for friends when we care for our place.”

They all sat on the warm boards. The sun felt cozy on their fur. A gentle hum of bees came from the flowers. Mama Bear poured water into little cups. “Sip, sip,” went the friends.

Pip looked around. “I want to make a porch promise,” he said. “I will not throw wrappers. I will use the bins. I will care for our home.”

Cubby placed a paw on his heart. “Me too. I will ask for help when I need it. I will speak up kindly if I see a problem.”

Mama Bear nodded. “That is being assertive and responsible. That is friendship, too—helping each other do what is right.”

They made a small sign together. Cubby drew a leaf. Pip drew a bottle. Mama Bear wrote the words: “Porch Protectors—We Care for Our Home.” They hung the sign by the railing with a soft “click.”

Later, a gust of wind blew a napkin onto the porch. It fluttered like a little cloud. Pip reached for it before it could fly away. “Got you!” he laughed. He put it in the correct bin. Cubby cheered. The bunny clapped her paws.

The porch looked peaceful again. The air smelled fresh. The birds sang a soft “thank you” song. Cubby felt proud, like a warm glow in his chest.

Cubby turned to Pip and the bunny. “Today we learned something big,” he said. “When we keep our porch clean, we help nature. That is conservation. When we speak up kindly, that is being brave. When we follow our porch promise, that is responsibility. And doing it together is friendship.”

They rocked in the chair, creak-creak, and watched the sunset paint the sky peach and pink. The porch was calm and happy, and so were they.

The End.
