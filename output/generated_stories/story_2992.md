# Splash the Dolphin's Thank You Song

In a cozy nursery painted ocean blue, there lived a very special toy dolphin named Splash. The nursery was warm and comfortable, with soft pillows everywhere and gentle sunlight streaming through the windows. Splash wasn't just any ordinary toy - he was magical and could swim through the air as if it were water!

Every morning, baby Emma would wake up and play with Splash, making him dive and spin around the room. "Wheee!" Splash would call out happily as he soared through the air.

But one sunny Tuesday, Splash woke up feeling grumpy. "Hmph," he huffed, floating near the window. "I'm tired of this nursery. I want to see the real ocean where other dolphins live!"

Emma's grandmother was visiting that day. She sat in the rocking chair, watching Splash float around with a frown on his face. "What's wrong, little dolphin?" she asked gently.

"I don't like it here anymore," Splash complained. "I want bigger waves and deeper water. This nursery is too small!"

Grandmother smiled wisely. "Let me tell you a story, Splash. Close your eyes and listen."

As Splash closed his eyes, Grandmother began to speak in a soft, musical voice. "Once, there was a real dolphin who lived in a part of the ocean that became very dirty. The water turned murky, and there was trash floating everywhere. The fish swam away, and the coral turned gray."

Splash opened one eye. "That sounds terrible!"

"It was," Grandmother continued. "That dolphin would have given anything for a clean, safe place like this nursery. A place with fresh air, loving care, and a little girl who played with him every day."

Splash began to think carefully. He looked around the nursery with new eyes. The walls were painted with beautiful sea creatures. The mobile above the crib played gentle ocean sounds. Emma's toys were all clean and bright.

"I... I never thought about that," Splash said quietly.

Just then, Emma toddled into the room. "Splash!" she giggled, reaching up for her favorite toy. Her eyes sparkled with joy when she saw him.

Suddenly, Splash felt a warm feeling in his heart. He realized how lucky he was! Emma loved him so much. The nursery was clean and safe. He had soft blankets to rest on and beautiful music to listen to.

"Thank you, Grandmother," Splash said. "I understand now!"

Splash began to sing a happy song: "Thank you for my cozy home, thank you for the love I'm shown. Thank you for the clean, fresh air, thank you for the gentle care!"

Emma clapped her hands and laughed. The music filled the nursery with joy.

From that day on, Splash made it his mission to teach Emma about taking care of their beautiful world. When they played, he would sing songs about keeping the oceans clean and being grateful for their home.

"We must take care of our Earth," Splash would sing, "so all creatures have a place of worth!"

Emma learned to put her toys away and help keep the nursery tidy. She learned that being thankful made everything more beautiful.

Every night, as the stars twinkled outside the window, Splash would float peacefully in the nursery, singing his thank you song. He had learned that gratitude made every day an adventure, and taking care of your home - whether it's a nursery or an ocean - was the most important job of all.

The End.
