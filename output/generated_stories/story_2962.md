# Joey Roo and the Ticking Attic

Joey Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with a bouncy tail and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, Mama Roo said, “Joey, it’s almost story circle time at noon. Please clean your play corner and make a picture for your friends. Remember to listen to the clock.”

Joey hopped up the wooden stairs to the quiet attic. The attic smelled like cedar and dust and apple tea from a little cup on a shelf. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. In the corner, a gentle clock went tick-tock, tick-tock.

“Hello, attic!” Joey giggled. “Let’s make art!”

Joey saw colorful papers, stickers, crayons, and a box of shiny buttons. He also saw a drum from Ghana, a tiny fan from Japan, a bright scarf from India, and a small maraca from Mexico. The attic held treasures from many places. Joey’s ears wiggled with joy.

“First, I clean,” he said. “Then I draw.” He looked at the clock. The short hand pointed to eleven, and the long hand pointed to twelve. Mama had said story circle at noon. That meant Joey had one hour.

Joey started to pick up crayons. Tick-tock. The drum glowed in the sunlight. “Just one tap,” Joey whispered. He tapped softly. Boom-boom. It sounded warm, like a hug. He smiled. “Okay, back to cleaning.”

He put stickers in a tin. Tick-tock. The little fan peeked from a box. “Just one wave,” Joey said. He opened the fan, swish-swish. Cool air brushed his whiskers. “Mmm, that feels nice.” He giggled and put the fan away.

He stacked papers neatly. Tick-tock. The maraca called, “Shake me!” Joey shook it—cha-cha-cha! “Oh!” he giggled. “It sounds like dancing rain.”

Joey looked at the clock again. The long hand had moved. “Uh-oh,” he said. “I need to use my time.” He took a deep breath and listened to the steady tick-tock. “I can make a plan. Clean, draw, music last.”

Joey clapped his paws softly. “Plan time!” He set three small boxes in a row. He put crayons in the first box. He put papers in the second. He put instruments in the third. He sang, “First clean, then draw, music at the end, hooray, hooray!”

He picked up buttons—click, click. He dusted the shelf—swish, swish. He folded the scarf—smooth and silky under his paws. The attic smelled fresh, like cedar and sunshine.

Joey checked the clock. Tick-tock. “Good job, me,” he said. “Now drawing.”

He sat on a soft rug. He drew a big circle world. He used brown, green, and blue. He drew children holding hands: a girl with braids and beads, a boy with a bright hat, a child with a shiny hair clip, a friend in a wheelchair with star stickers on the wheels. He drew a drum, a fan, a scarf, and a maraca. He drew Mama Roo and himself. He glued shiny buttons like stars around the world. He whispered, “We all belong.”

As he worked, his tummy felt calm. He listened to the clock. Tick-tock. He said to himself, “I am using my time.” He smiled.

Just then, his cousin Kip hopped into the attic. Kip was a little joey with a playful grin. “Joey! Wanna bounce?” Kip asked.

Joey looked at the clock. The long hand was close to the twelve again. “I want to,” Joey said, “but I need to finish. It’s almost story circle. I made a plan.” He pointed to the boxes. “First clean. Then draw. Music at the end.”

Kip’s ears drooped. “Oh,” he said. “I forgot to clean my corner. I got distracted. I played and played and now I’m late.”

Joey nodded kindly. “It’s okay. We can make a quick plan, together.” He patted the rug. “Listen to the clock with me.”

They both got quiet. Tick-tock. Tick-tock. The sound was steady like a gentle heartbeat.

Joey said, “We will pick up for two ticks.” They counted, “One tick, two tick.” They scooped toys—scoop, scoop. “Now we will draw for four ticks.” They colored—swish, swish, crayon on paper. Kip drew his family sharing food from a big pot. “This is jollof rice,” Kip said. “It smells spicy and yummy.” Joey drew a bowl of noodles with chopsticks and a warm bowl of soup. “I like miso soup,” he said. “It smells cozy.”

Joey smiled. “We used our time well!”

Just then, Mama Roo climbed the attic stairs. “How’s it going?” she asked.

Joey stood up. “I listened to the clock. I made a plan. I cleaned, I drew, and now it’s music time.” He showed his picture. “We all belong.”

Mama Roo’s eyes shone. “I am proud of you,” she said. Kip looked shy. “I forgot my time,” he said softly. “I’m sorry.”

Mama Roo knelt and gave Kip a gentle hug. “Thank you for telling the truth,” she said. “I forgive you. You can try again.”

Joey handed Kip the maraca. “Let’s make music together,” he said.

They tapped the drum—boom-boom. They shook the maraca—cha-cha-cha. They waved the fan—swish. They tied the bright scarf around the drum, and it felt soft and silky. The attic filled with sounds and colors. Their hearts felt warm and light.

The clock chimed twelve. Ding! “Story circle time!” Mama Roo cheered. Joey and Kip hopped downstairs, holding their pictures. Joey felt proud. He had listened. He had used his time. He had helped his cousin. And they had all shared a little piece of their worlds.

At story circle, Joey said, “When I listen to the clock and make a plan—first, then, next—I can do what I need and still have fun.” The children smiled and clapped. Kip whispered, “Thank you, Joey.” Joey whispered back, “We can try again every day.”

The End.
