# Tully the Turtle Tells the Truth

Tully was a shy turtle. He liked quiet mornings and warm sun. He carried his shell like a cozy little house. One bright day, Tully peeked up the ladder to the big treehouse in the park. The wood smelled like fresh pine. Leaves whispered shhh shhh in the breeze. Birdsong sprinkled the air like little bells.

Today was Clean-Up Day at the treehouse. Kids and animal friends came with small brooms, soft cloths, and a big blue bin for trash. It was a civic job for everyone. Helping keeps our park healthy, said Ms. Robin, who wore a tiny red scarf. It is good for our wellness, too. Clean places help us breathe and play.

Tully’s cheeks felt warm. He was shy, but he wanted to help. He took a small, soft brush and dusted the steps. Swish, swish. He hummed a tiny tune.

Hi, Tully! said Mia the squirrel, who was quick and kind. I like your careful brushing!

Thank you, Tully said softly. He smiled and kept brushing.

On the treehouse floor lay a shiny marble. It gleamed green and blue, like a tiny world. It rolled and clicked in Tully’s careful hand. He liked the cool, smooth feel. He wanted to keep it. But he did not know whose it was.

He looked around. The treehouse was busy. Mia stacked books. A little bunny wiped the railing. A boy named Jay spread a picnic blanket and set out apple slices and water.

Tully put the marble in his pocket. It felt heavy. His tummy felt heavy, too. He heard Ms. Robin call, If you find something, please speak up. Honesty helps us trust each other. Respect means we care for others’ things.

Tully’s heart thump-thumped. He did not want to speak. He was shy. He looked at the floor and brushed. Swish, swish. But the marble in his pocket felt like a pebble in his shoe. It made his steps feel slow.

Mia came close and whispered, Are you okay, Tully?

Tully nodded, but his eyes looked wet. He took a deep breath in. The air smelled like apples and leaves. He let the breath out. He touched his pocket.

Mia tilted her head kindly. You can tell me anything, she said. Honesty is brave.

Tully looked at his feet. I found a marble, he said, very quiet. I put it in my pocket. I’m sorry.

Thank you for telling me, Mia said with a warm smile. That is honest and kind. Let’s tell everyone together. I will stand with you.

Tully’s belly fluttered like tiny butterflies. But he felt a little lighter. He nodded.

Mia tapped her tail and called, Friends, Tully has something to share.

Tully held up the marble. His hands shook a little. I found this. I put it in my pocket. I felt bad. I want to give it back.

Jay’s eyes grew wide. That is my special marble! he said. My grandpa gave it to me. Thank you for telling the truth, Tully.

Tully’s cheeks warmed again, but this time they felt like sunshine, not worry. I’m sorry, Jay, he said. I should have asked.

Jay smiled and took the marble gently. You were honest. That helps us all. He placed the marble in a small tin and closed it. I will keep it safe.

Ms. Robin fluttered down. Tully, you showed honesty and respect for others. That keeps our treehouse family strong. Also, you took a deep breath and spoke up. That is good wellness for your heart.

Tully nodded. His shell felt light. His chest felt calm. He could breathe easy now.

Let’s have a water break, said Jay. Everyone sipped cool water and crunched apple slices. The apples were sweet and crisp. The water felt fresh.

After the break, Tully helped make a Lost and Found box. He and Mia drew a big label with a friendly sun. They placed it by the door.

Now if we find things, we can return them, said Mia. That is civic responsibility—helping our whole community.

Tully smiled. He found a red button under a chair. He held it up and called, I found a button! He placed it in the box. His voice sounded steady and clear. He felt brave.

At the end of the day, the treehouse shone. The windows sparkled. The floor smelled like lemon. The air felt clean and light.

Ms. Robin clapped her wings. Great job, helpers! Before you go, let’s share what we learned.

Tully raised a small hand. Honesty makes my tummy feel better, he said. When I tell the truth, I feel calm inside. It shows respect for others’ things.

And it helps our place stay happy and safe, said Jay. That is good for everyone.

Mia nodded. Being honest can be hard, but friends can help.

Ms. Robin smiled. Yes. Honesty builds trust. Respect keeps peace. Caring for our park is good wellness and good citizenship.

The sun dipped low, painting the leaves gold. The treehouse looked cozy and proud. Tully tucked into his shell for a moment and then peeked out with a grin.

Thank you for standing with me, he said to Mia.

Always, she said.

Tully waved to his friends. He felt shy, but he felt strong, too. He had told the truth. He had helped his home in the trees. His steps on the ladder were light as he climbed down. Tap, tap, tap.

He whispered to the breeze, I can be honest and brave.

The breeze seemed to whisper back, Yes, you can.

The End.
