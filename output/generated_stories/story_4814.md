# Hoot the Owl’s Quiet Morning

In a comfortable nursery, soft and warm like a hug, a wise old owl named Hoot perched on the window seat. The curtains were creamy and light, the rug felt fluffy under tiny toes, and the air smelled like clean blankets and a hint of lavender. Toys rested in tidy baskets, and a small clock tick-tocked in a gentle, steady beat.

“Good morning,” Hoot hooted softly, his feathers smooth and silver. “Today, we will take good care of ourselves.”

A small child named Mia peeked around a pillow. “Hi, Hoot,” she whispered. “Can we play?”

“We will,” Hoot said with a nod. “First, we practice self-care. When we care for ourselves, we feel calm, strong, and ready to play.”

Mia’s eyes grew bright. “What do we do?”

Hoot blinked his round, golden eyes. “We start with a calm breath.” He spread one wing and counted. “In for one… two… and out for one… two.” His voice was slow and soft, like warm tea.

Mia copied him. “In… two… out… two.” She felt her chest rise like a little balloon, then fall. The tick-tock of the clock matched her breath. Tick-tock, in and out. Her shoulders relaxed.

“Good,” said Hoot. “Now our bodies are calm.”

Mia giggled. “I feel like a quiet balloon.”

Hoot smiled. “Next is washing hands. Clean hands help us stay well.” They went to the tiny sink in the nursery corner. The water made a friendly splash-splash sound. The soap smelled like lemons. “Rub, rub,” Hoot sang. “Front and back, between fingers, round and round. Count to ten.”

Mia counted with Hoot. “One, two, three…” The bubbles popped like little stars. Her hands felt fresh and cool.

They dried their hands on a soft towel. “Fuzzy,” Mia said, rubbing her cheeks. She felt cozy and cared for.

“Now we sip water,” Hoot said, offering a small cup. The water was clear and cool. “Sip-sip,” Hoot hooted. “Water helps us play and think.”

Mia took a sip. “Ahh,” she said. Her tummy felt happy.

A small stuffed rabbit named Binky sat on the rocking chair. Mia picked him up and held him close. “Binky wants to learn too.”

“Of course,” said Hoot. “Friends care for themselves and each other. That is friendship.”

They sat on the rug. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Dust sparkles danced in the light. Outside, a bird chirped, and far away a car hummed softly. Everything felt peaceful.

“Let’s do a calm stretch,” Hoot said. “Reach up like the sun, and down like a sleepy leaf.” Mia reached her arms up, up, up, and then down to touch her toes. “Wiggle your fingers, wiggle your toes,” Hoot added.

Mia wiggled and laughed. “It tickles!”

Hoot chuckled. “Body happy, heart happy.”

Mia looked at the tidy baskets. “Can we play now?” she asked.

“Yes,” said Hoot, “but first, we make a plan. When we choose what to do, it helps us feel calm. That is self-care too.”

Mia thought. “I want to read a book with you and Binky.”

Hoot nodded. “A wonderful plan.”

They picked a picture book with a shiny blue cover. The pages smelled like paper and a bit like crayons. Hoot read in a gentle voice. “Once there was a little seed…” Mia traced the pictures with her finger. Binky leaned against her shoulder. The story felt soft and safe.

After the story, Mia’s tummy made a tiny rumble. “I’m hungry,” she said.

“That is your body talking,” Hoot replied kindly. “Listening is self-care. Let’s have a snack.”

They shared slices of apple that were cool and crisp. “Crunch,” went the apple. “Mmm,” said Mia. The sweet taste made her smile. Hoot had a small nibble too, and Binky got a pretend nibble.

“Now,” Hoot said, “let’s clean up together.” They put the book back, folded the blanket, and set Binky on the chair. “When we tidy, our space feels calm,” Hoot explained. “A calm space is a kind gift to ourselves.”

Mia nodded. “I like our calm space.”

Hoot hopped onto the window seat again. “Before we play again, let us rest. Close your eyes for three slow breaths.”

Mia closed her eyes and hugged Binky. Hoot counted softly. “In… one, two, three. Out… one, two, three.” The nursery felt even warmer and safer, like a soft nest.

When Mia opened her eyes, she felt light and happy. “Hoot, I discovered something,” she said.

“What did you discover?” Hoot asked, tilting his wise head.

“I discovered that when I breathe, wash, drink water, stretch, choose, snack, and tidy, I feel calm. I can be a good friend to me and to Binky.”

Hoot’s eyes shone. “Yes, dear Mia. Self-care helps us feel well. When we care for ourselves, we have more kindness to share. That is friendship and wellness.”

Mia smiled big. “Today is a good day.”

“It is,” Hoot agreed. “Every day can be a good day when we listen to our bodies, our hearts, and our friends.”

They began a gentle game with blocks, building a small cozy house for Binky. The blocks clicked softly. Mia hummed a little tune. Hoot watched with pride, calm and bright in the sun.

The house stood tall, and Binky sat at the door. “Thank you, Hoot,” Mia said.

“Thank you, Mia,” Hoot replied. “We made calm, we found joy, and we practiced self-care. That is our daily adventure.”

The End.
