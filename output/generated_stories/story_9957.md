# Melody's Special Song

In a cozy study corner filled with colorful books and soft cushions, lived a little yellow bird named Melody. She had the most beautiful voice in the whole house, but today she felt a little sad. Her tiny heart was heavy because she missed her grandmother bird who lived far away.

"Tweet, tweet," Melody sang softly, perched on top of a big picture book. But her song didn't sound as happy as usual.

Mrs. Chen, who worked at the desk nearby, noticed Melody's quiet chirping. "Hello there, little bird," she said gently. "You sound different today. Are you feeling okay?"

Melody tilted her small head. "I miss my Grandma Birdie," she chirped. "She used to teach me the most wonderful old songs that her grandmother taught her. Now I'm forgetting them!"

Mrs. Chen smiled warmly. "Oh my! Those sound like very special tradition songs. Would you like to practice them? Maybe I can help."

Melody's bright eyes sparkled with hope. "Really? But I don't have anything to help me remember."

Mrs. Chen looked around the study corner and spotted an empty cardboard box in the recycling bin. "I have an idea! Let's make something special together."

She carefully took out the clean box and some colorful paper scraps. "We can create a memory box for your grandmother's songs! Every time you sing one, we'll put a colorful paper feather inside."

Melody flapped her tiny wings excitedly. "Oh yes! That sounds wonderful!"

Mrs. Chen cut out beautiful paper feathers in red, blue, and green. Then she showed Melody how to decorate the box with the feathers. As they worked together, Melody began to remember her grandmother's songs.

"This one was Grandma's favorite," Melody sang, her voice growing stronger. "Tweet-a-loo, tweet-a-lay, sunshine brings a brand new day!"

"That's beautiful!" Mrs. Chen clapped her hands. "Let's put the first feather in your memory box."

As Melody dropped the red feather into the box, she felt her heart growing lighter. She remembered another song and another, each one making her feel closer to her grandmother.

"Chirp-chirp-choo, the morning dew, makes the flowers fresh and new!" she sang, doing a little dance on the desk. The exercise made her feel even happier.

Mrs. Chen watched as Melody's confidence grew with each song. "You know, Melody, by remembering these songs, you're keeping your grandmother's traditions alive. And one day, you can teach them to other young birds!"

Melody's chest puffed up with pride. "I can share Grandma's songs with everyone! That way, her beautiful traditions will never be forgotten."

By the end of the afternoon, Melody's memory box was full of colorful feathers, and her heart was full of joy. She had learned that traditions are like precious gifts that we can share with others.

"Thank you for helping me remember," Melody chirped to Mrs. Chen. "Now I understand that when we share our family traditions, they become even more special!"

From that day on, Melody sang her grandmother's songs every morning in the study corner, sharing the beautiful melodies with anyone who would listen. She had discovered that the best way to honor her grandmother was to keep her traditions alive and pass them on to others.

The End.
