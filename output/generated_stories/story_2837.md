# Daisy's Big Helper Day

Little Daisy the deer fawn peeked into Grandma Rabbit's guest room. Her spots sparkled in the morning sunlight streaming through the window.

"Oh my!" said Grandma Rabbit, looking around the messy room. "My nieces and nephews visited yesterday. Look at all these toys scattered everywhere!"

Daisy's ears drooped. The room was very messy. Blocks were on the floor. Books were under the bed. Stuffed animals sat in corners looking sad.

"I wish I could help clean up," Daisy said softly. "But I'm too little. My legs are wobbly, and I might break something."

Grandma Rabbit's whiskers twitched kindly. "Oh, sweet Daisy. You're bigger than you think!"

"Really?" Daisy's brown eyes grew wide.

"Let's make a deal," said Grandma Rabbit. "You help me for just ten minutes. If you get tired, we'll take a rest."

Daisy thought carefully. Ten minutes didn't sound too scary. "Okay," she whispered.

First, Grandma Rabbit showed Daisy how to pick up the soft toys. "Use your gentle mouth," she said. "Deer are very careful creatures."

Daisy carefully lifted a small teddy bear. Her teeth were gentle, just like Grandma said. She placed it on the bed. "I did it!" she said, her tail wiggling with joy.

"Wonderful!" cheered Grandma Rabbit. "See how strong and careful you are?"

Next, they worked on the blocks. Daisy used her nose to push them into a pile. The blocks felt smooth and cool against her soft nose. She giggled as they clicked together.

"My nose is like a little bulldozer!" Daisy laughed.

For the books, Daisy had a great idea. "I can use my hooves to slide them out from under the bed!"

She stretched her long legs and carefully pushed the books where Grandma Rabbit could reach them. Her hooves made soft tapping sounds on the wooden floor.

"You're such a smart problem-solver," said Grandma Rabbit proudly.

When they finished, Daisy looked around the clean, cozy room. The soft blue blankets were folded neatly. The wooden toys sat in their basket. Everything smelled fresh and clean.

"I helped clean a whole room!" Daisy said, standing tall on her slender legs.

"You certainly did," said Grandma Rabbit. "And now you've earned some rest time."

They sat together on the soft guest bed. Grandma Rabbit brought Daisy some cool water and sweet clover. The clover tasted like sunshine and made Daisy feel strong.

"I was scared I was too little to help," Daisy admitted, munching the green leaves.

"Sometimes we feel smaller than we really are," said Grandma Rabbit gently. "But when we try our best and take care of ourselves, we discover how capable we are."

Daisy nuzzled Grandma Rabbit's soft fur. "Can I help you again tomorrow?"

"Of course! And remember, asking for breaks when you need them makes you an even better helper."

Daisy's spots seemed to shine brighter as she smiled. She had learned that being little didn't mean being helpless. With patience, rest, and trying her best, she could do amazing things.

From that day on, whenever Daisy felt too small for a task, she remembered her big helper day in the guest room.

The End.
