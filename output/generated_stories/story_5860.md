# Benny Beaver's Fix-It Day

Benny was a busy beaver with bright eyes and quick paws. He loved his garage workshop. It smelled like fresh wood and warm glue. Sunlight peeked through the window and made little dust sparkles dance. Tools hung neatly on the wall. Hammers, wrenches, a tiny red saw, and a bright blue tape measure all had their own hooks.

“Good morning, tools!” Benny said. He patted his wooden workbench. “Today we help friends.”

There was a knock, knock at the open garage door. Squeak the mouse peeked in. She wore a tiny scarf and held a toy car with a wobbly wheel.

“Hi, Benny,” Squeak said softly. “My car goes zig-zag, then flop.”

Benny smiled. “Let’s think it through. What’s the problem? The wheel is wobbly. What do we need?” He tapped his chin. “First, we look. Then we test.”

He rolled the car slowly. The wheel squeaked, “Eee-eee.” Benny listened. He touched the wheel. “Loose,” he said. “We need a small screwdriver.”

Benny showed Squeak. “Turn right to make it tight,” he said. They turned the tiny screw together. Squeak pushed the car. It rolled straight and smooth. “Zoom!”

Squeak clapped. “Thank you!”

“Now you try it,” Benny said. “You did the turning, so you know how. That’s thinking and doing.”

Squeak grinned and shared a little cheese cracker. “For you!”

Benny laughed. “You keep it. But I’ll share a story.” He held up the screwdriver. “This tool is small, but it helps in a big way. Like small ideas that make big fixes.”

Another knock, knock. Hoot the owl waddled in with a wooden puzzle. “Hoo-hoo, Benny,” Hoot said. “My shapes won’t fit.”

Benny wiped his paws and looked closely. “Hmm. Let’s plan. We don’t push. We match.” He spread the pieces. Triangle, circle, square. He pointed. “Find the corners.”

Hoot blinked. “Corners?”

“Yes,” said Benny. He touched the triangle. “Count with me. One, two, three corners.” He pointed at the square. “One, two, three, four.” He traced the round circle. “No corners.”

Hoot hooted happily. “No corners goes in the round hole!”

They took turns. Hoot tried the circle. It slid in with a soft snick. The triangle clicked in next, then the square. Hoot fluffed his feathers. “We did it!”

“How did we do it?” Benny asked.

“We looked, we thought, then we tried,” Hoot said proudly.

“That’s critical thinking,” Benny said gently. “We use our eyes, our brain, and our hands. We make a plan.”

The garage grew cozy with happy feelings. The air smelled like cedar shavings. Outside, a breeze whispered, making the wind chimes sing, “Ting-ting.”

Then came a bonk-bonk-bonk. It was Tilly the turtle, pushing a wiggly wagon with a crooked handle. A pile of blocks was inside. “My wagon tips,” Tilly said, frowning. “My blocks go everywhere.”

Benny nodded. “Let’s slow down and think. What do we see?”

Tilly peered at the handle. “It is crooked.”

Squeak sniffed. “The wheels are not even.”

Hoot leaned close. “The blocks are high on one side.”

Benny smiled. “Great clues! We have three problems. We will fix them one by one.”

He pointed to the handle first. “We tighten the bolt.” Benny showed Tilly the wrench. “Left to loosen, right to tighten.” Tilly turned slowly. The handle steadied.

Next, he looked at the wheels. “This nut is loose.” He let Squeak try the small wrench. “Turn, turn, stop,” Benny coached. The wheel stood straight.

Last, Benny crouched by the wagon. “The blocks are tall on one side and short on the other. What happens when it’s not balanced?”

“It tips,” Hoot and Tilly said together.

“How can we share the blocks so it’s fair and flat?” Benny asked.

Tilly lifted a big block and passed it to Squeak. Squeak shared two small blocks with Hoot. They took turns, left side, right side, left side, right side. The pile became even.

Tilly tried the wagon. It rolled smooth as a song. “It works!” she cheered.

Benny nodded. “We looked, we listened, we made a plan, and we shared. That’s how we fix tricky things.”

Just then, a little drip, drip, drip sounded from the ceiling. Benny looked up. “Oh!” A tiny pipe had a tiny leak. A bright drop fell onto the floor with a plip.

Benny didn’t rush. He took a deep breath. “Step one: find the leak. Step two: stop the water. Step three: fix the crack.”

He set a bucket under the drip. “Plip, plop,” it went. He turned off the little water knob. The drip slowed, then stopped.

“What tool now?” Hoot asked.

“Plumber’s tape,” Benny said. He wrapped the tape snug around the pipe threads. He twisted the pipe back in place and turned the water on slowly. No drip.

Squeak squeaked, “We did it again!”

Benny smiled. “We thought before we fixed.”

Tilly looked at the bucket. “Can we share the water with your plants?”

“Yes,” said Benny. “Great idea.” They carried the bucket carefully and watered the green ferns by the door. The leaves looked shiny and thankful.

Benny handed out apple slices from a little lunch box. “Let’s share a snack,” he said. “One for each friend.” They munched together. Crunch, crunch, crunch.

Squeak whispered, “Benny, you help us. How can we help you?”

Benny’s whiskers twitched. “You already did. You used your eyes and your minds. You asked good questions. You shared and took turns. That helps everyone.”

The sun dipped a little. The workshop felt calm and bright. Tools hung neatly again. The friends waved goodbye, their hearts warm.

Benny patted his workbench and said, “Think first, then fix. Share tools, share time, share smiles.”

And the garage seemed to hum, “Yes.”

The End.
