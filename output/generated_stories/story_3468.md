# Robo-Pup’s Wiggle-and-Help Day

In a sunny house with blue curtains, there was a quiet guest room. The guest room had a soft green rug, a big window, and a little bed with star blankets. It smelled like clean soap and fresh lemons. In the corner sat a helpful robot dog named Robo-Pup. Robo-Pup had shiny silver ears, a wagging antenna tail, and bright, friendly lights for eyes.

One morning, Mama said, “Grandma is coming to stay tonight! We need to tidy the guest room.”

Robo-Pup’s lights blinked. “Woof-beep! I can help!” he said in a cheerful, beepy voice.

But there was a problem. The guest room was messy. A suitcase was stuck under the bed. A basket of toys was tipped over. A little chair was blocking the closet door. And on the rug were tiny socks, soft like clouds. Robo-Pup tried to move, but the little chair wobbled. The basket rolled. The suitcase would not slide. He beeped a worried beep. “Oh dear. How do I move it all?”

Little Mia, who loved to hop, peeped in. “Hi, Robo-Pup! I hear your beeps,” she said. She looked around the room with her big brown eyes. “It’s a lot. But we can move together!”

Robo-Pup’s tail antenna wagged. “Woof-beep! Together is better,” he said.

Mia took a deep breath. The air smelled like lemons and warm sunlight. “Let’s make a plan,” she said. “We move our bodies, and we move the mess. We wiggle, we lift, we roll—slow and safe.”

Robo-Pup nodded. “I can count steps,” he said. “Step one, wiggle room!”

Mia and Robo-Pup stood tall. “Wiggle, wiggle!” Mia sang. Robo-Pup’s little metal paws tip-tip-tapped on the floor. They stretched their arms out wide. They touched their toes. They shook their shoulders like tiny bells. The room filled with happy sounds—tap, swish, giggle.

Then Mia said, “Step two, move the chair.”

Robo-Pup placed his soft rubber nose on the chair and pushed. The chair squeaked—eeeek!—and moved a little. Mia put both hands on the chair. “We push together,” she said. “One, two, push!” Their feet slid on the rug, but they smiled. With a gentle scrape, the chair moved to the wall. “We did it!” said Mia.

Robo-Pup wagged faster. “Woof-beep! Cooperation!”

Next was the basket of toys. It was round and full of blocks and stuffed bears. “It looks heavy,” said Robo-Pup.

Mia nodded. “Let’s make it light. We move little bits.” She picked up a blue block. “Lift, place.” Robo-Pup picked up a soft bear with his little magnetic paw. “Lift, place,” he echoed. Their voices were steady and kind. Lift, place. Lift, place. They hummed a tune: “Move and groove, make some room.” Soon the basket was easy to lift. They rolled it next to the wall. It went rumble-rumble, like a tiny thunder.

“Good job, bodies!” Mia said. She patted her chest. Robo-Pup patted his shiny side. Their faces glowed—Mia’s with a big smile, Robo-Pup’s with a glowing green light.

But the biggest problem stayed: the suitcase under the bed. It was stuck tight, like a sleepy turtle in a shell.

Robo-Pup tried to pull with his paw. “Tug-tug,” he said. Nothing.

Mia lay flat on the cool rug. “I will look,” she whispered. It was dark under the bed, and it smelled like dust and cedar wood. “The suitcase is hooked,” she said. “It is stuck under a slat.”

Robo-Pup blinked. “Hmm. We need a new way.”

Mia thought. “We take turns,” she said. “You lift the bed a tiny bit. I pull slowly. No rush.”

Robo-Pup set his paws and made a gentle hum. “Liiift,” he said. The bed creaked like an old door. Mia wrapped her hands around the suitcase handle. “Pull, pull,” she sang softly. A little at a time, the suitcase slid—shhh, shhh—out into the light.

“Woof-beep! We did it!” Robo-Pup cheered.

Mia clapped. “Teamwork!”

Just then, the tiny socks on the rug seemed to be everywhere. A small voice came from the door. It was Max, Mia’s little cousin. He held a crayon and looked worried. “I dropped my socks,” he said. “I made a mess. I’m sorry.”

Mia knelt down. “It’s okay, Max,” she said kindly. “We all make messes. Do you want to help move?”

Max nodded, his cheeks pink. Robo-Pup lowered his head to Max. “We can work together,” he beeped softly. “We move slow and safe.”

Mia showed Max how to make a game. “Hop to a sock, pick it up, hop back to the basket.” Max giggled. “Hop, pick, hop!” He bounced like a little frog. Robo-Pup matched the rhythm, his paws going tip-tip, his antenna tail swish-swish. Mia hopped too. Their feet thumped softly on the rug—thump, thump, thump. Soon the socks were all in the basket.

They looked around. The guest room was tidy and bright. Sunlight danced on the walls. It smelled like lemons and happy work.

Mama walked in and smiled wide. “Oh! What wonderful helpers,” she said. “You moved your bodies. You moved the mess. You were patient with each other. You worked together. Grandma will love this room.”

Robo-Pup’s lights glowed warm. “Woof-beep! Movement helps!” he said.

Mia hugged Max. “We can do hard things when we move, when we are kind, and when we cooperate,” she said.

Max nodded. “And we can take turns,” he added proudly.

Robo-Pup wagged. “And we can try new ways if the first way is stuck.”

They all laughed. The room felt calm and cozy. The problem was solved, and their hearts felt big.

Lesson: Moving our bodies helps us solve problems. When we are patient, kind, and work together, we can do great things.

The End.
