# Sunny's Treehouse Team

Sunny the squirrel lived in a cozy treehouse high in a friendly oak. The treehouse smelled like pine and warm leaves. A tiny bell by the door went ting-ting when the breeze blew. Sunny was small, soft, and very resourceful. Sunny loved to help friends and loved to lead with kindness.

One bright morning, Sunny looked at a round wooden clock. “Tick-tock,” it said softly. Sunny tapped the clock and smiled. “We have a busy day,” Sunny said. “We will practice leading, helping, and sharing time.”

Sunny rolled out a map made from bark and leaves. It showed the forest, the pond, and the garden. “Friends come from many places,” Sunny said. “We can work together and care for our big, shared world.”

Knock-knock! The little bell sang ting-ting. Friends arrived with happy paws and toes. Pip the chipmunk carried tiny nuts. Maya the mouse had seeds in a pouch. Tiko the turtle wore a bright scarf from a faraway pond. Lila the ladybug fluttered in, humming softly.

“Hello!” Sunny said. “Welcome to the Treehouse Team. Today, we will clean the forest path, plant a peace garden, and share snack time. We will practice leadership, global-citizenship, resourcefulness, and time-management. We will do it together.”

Pip blinked. “Leader? What does a leader do?”

“A leader listens,” Sunny said. “A leader makes a plan. A leader helps everyone feel safe and strong.”

Sunny pointed to a simple list on a leaf board:
- First: Clean the path.
- Next: Plant the peace garden.
- Last: Share snacks and stories.

Sunny clapped paws. “We will use our time well. We will work, then rest, then celebrate.”

The friends cheered. Lila sang, “We can do it!”

Down the rope ladder they went. The forest smelled like fresh dirt and sweet flowers. The sun felt warm on their backs. The path had leaves and twigs all over.

Sunny said, “I will show you how, then you try.” Sunny used a big leaf like a broom. Sweep, sweep. “Gentle hands, kind hearts,” Sunny said.

Pip swept small twigs. “Like this?”

“Yes!” said Sunny. “Great job, Pip.”

Tiko moved slow but steady, pushing a heavy branch. “This branch is big,” Tiko huffed.

Sunny looked around. “We can do it together.” Sunny, Pip, Maya, and Lila pressed their tiny bodies to the branch. “One, two, three, push!” Together they moved it off the path.

Tiko smiled. “Thank you. Teamwork!”

Maya found a shiny can. “Oh no,” she said softly. “Someone forgot this.”

Sunny nodded. “We take care of our home and our planet. We are global citizens. We keep the forest clean so everyone can enjoy it.” Sunny put the can in a bag to recycle.

“Listen,” Lila said. The birds sang tweet-tweet. The wind whispered shhh. The friends breathed in slowly. “The forest is happy,” Sunny said.

Back at the treehouse, Sunny checked the clock. “We used time well. Now, next step.” Sunny led the friends to a sunny spot by the trunk. “We will plant a peace garden for all. Flowers for bees, herbs for butterflies, and a tiny tree that will grow and grow.”

Sunny passed out tiny shovels made from shells. “Dig small holes,” Sunny said. “Gentle and patient.”

Maya patted the soft dirt. “I like this work,” she said.

Pip sprinkled seeds, tap-tap-tap. “Little seeds, please grow.”

Tiko carried water in a leaf bowl. Splash! A little water spilled on his foot. He laughed. “Cool water!”

Lila fluttered and fanned the soil with her wings. “I help too!”

Sunny planted a seed from Tiko’s pond friends. “This plant is from far away. We welcome it. Different places, one big world.”

They set a smooth stone that read: “All friends welcome.”

Sunny checked the clock again. “Last: snacks and stories.” In the treehouse, the air smelled like roasted nuts and sweet berries. The friends sat on soft moss mats.

Sunny poured berry tea. “Sip-sip,” said Pip.

“We worked together,” Sunny said. “How did it feel?”

Maya squeaked, “I felt brave when you showed me.”

Tiko said, “I felt strong when we pushed the branch together.”

Lila said, “I felt happy helping the earth.”

Sunny nodded. “That is leadership. I listened, I made a plan, and I helped you try. You followed, and then you led too. Each of you led in your own way.”

Pip asked, “Can I be a leader?”

“Yes,” Sunny said. “You can lead by sharing ideas. You can lead by being kind. You can lead by using time wisely.”

Sunny pointed to the leaf board. “First, next, last. That helps us plan. Practice makes us better.”

The sun began to set. The sky turned soft orange and pink. The peace garden glowed. Bees buzzed gently. The bell went ting-ting in the evening breeze.

Sunny smiled. “Today we cared for our home, and for our world. We led with kindness. We used our time well. We practiced and learned.”

The friends hugged. “Tomorrow,” Sunny said, “each of you can lead a small task. We will listen and help, just like today.”

“Together,” said the friends.

“Together,” said Sunny, feeling proud and warm inside.

The End.
