# Splash the Dolphin's Big Mistake

Splash the dolphin was very excited! Today, he was visiting his friend Emma's house for the first time. Emma's mommy had filled up a special big bathtub in the guest room just for Splash to swim in.

"Welcome to our home, Splash!" said Emma, clapping her hands. "You can play in the guest room bathtub while I show you all my toys!"

The guest room was cozy and warm, with soft blue curtains and fluffy white towels. The bathtub sparkled with clean, cool water that felt just right. Splash dove in with a happy "SPLASH!"

"Wheee!" Splash squeaked, doing flips and spins. The water felt so good on his smooth gray skin. Emma giggled as she watched from beside the tub.

Emma had brought her favorite toys to share - a red boat, yellow rubber ducks, and her special music box that played gentle songs. She carefully placed the music box on the small table next to the tub.

"This plays my favorite lullaby," Emma said proudly. "My grandma gave it to me. It's very special."

Splash was having so much fun splashing that he didn't listen carefully. He wanted to see the pretty music box up close. With a big jump, he leaped high out of the water.

CRASH! 

Splash accidentally knocked the music box off the table with his fin. It fell to the floor and broke into pieces. The beautiful music stopped forever.

Emma's eyes filled with tears. "Oh no! My music box!"

Splash felt terrible. His tummy felt heavy and sad. He wanted to hide under the water, but he knew that wouldn't help Emma.

"I'm so sorry, Emma," Splash said quietly, his voice shaking. "I didn't mean to break your special music box. I should have been more careful."

Emma wiped her eyes. "It's broken forever now," she sniffled.

Splash's heart hurt seeing his friend so sad. He knew he had to do something to make it better. "Emma, I want to tell you the truth. I was being too excited and not listening when you said it was special. That was wrong of me."

"You're being honest," Emma said softly. "That helps a little."

"I want to make it up to you," Splash said. "I can't fix the music box, but I can sing you the most beautiful dolphin song. My mama taught it to me."

Splash began to sing a gentle, sweet melody that sounded like ocean waves and seagulls. His voice echoed beautifully in the guest room. Emma's face lit up with wonder.

"That's the most beautiful song ever!" Emma exclaimed.

"I'll teach it to you," Splash offered. "Then you'll always have a special song, even without the music box."

Emma smiled. "I'd love to learn your song, Splash."

They spent the rest of the afternoon with Splash teaching Emma the dolphin lullaby. Soon Emma was humming along perfectly.

"Thank you for being honest and trying to make things better," Emma said. "I forgive you, Splash. Your song is even more special because it comes from your heart."

Splash learned that when you make a mistake, the best thing to do is tell the truth, say sorry, and try to make things right. And Emma learned that sometimes the most beautiful gifts come from friendship and honesty.

The End.
