# Penny Panda's Special Guest Room

Penny Panda loved helping her mama around their cozy bamboo house. Today was extra special because Grandma Panda was coming to visit! Mama had asked Penny to help prepare the guest room.

"Penny, sweetie," Mama said with a warm smile, "can you help me make the guest room nice and clean for Grandma?"

"Yes, Mama!" Penny said, clapping her fuzzy black and white paws together. "I want to do a really good job!"

Penny followed Mama into the guest room. Sunlight streamed through the window, making everything look bright and cheerful. But oh my! The room was quite messy. There were books scattered on the floor, pillows tossed about, and dust on the wooden dresser.

"This looks like a big job," Penny said, her round ears drooping a little.

"That's okay," Mama said gently. "When we work together and take our time, we can make anything beautiful. Let's start by picking up the books."

Penny began gathering the colorful books. Some were heavy, and some were light as feathers. She could smell the sweet scent of the pages as she carefully stacked them on the bookshelf. But after a few minutes, Penny started to feel tired.

"Mama, this is taking forever!" Penny sighed. "Can't we just stuff everything under the bed?"

Mama sat down beside Penny and gave her a gentle hug. "Oh, my patient little panda, I understand you're tired. But think about how Grandma will feel when she sees her room. Don't you want her to feel respected and loved?"

Penny thought about her dear Grandma, who always brought her favorite honey treats and told the best bedtime stories. "Yes, I do want Grandma to feel special."

"That's what respect means," Mama explained. "When we take care of someone else's space and do our best work, we show them how much we care."

Penny nodded and picked up another book. This time, she placed it carefully on the shelf, making sure it was straight and neat. "Like this, Mama?"

"Perfect!" Mama beamed.

Together, they fluffed the soft pillows until they were as puffy as clouds. Penny ran her paws over the smooth fabric, making sure each pillow was placed just right. Then they dusted the dresser with a soft cloth that smelled like lavender flowers.

"Now for the final touch," Mama said, pulling out fresh sheets that smelled like sunshine. They worked together to make the bed, tucking in the corners nice and tight.

When they finished, Penny looked around the room. Everything sparkled! The books stood in neat rows, the pillows looked perfectly plump, and the bed was smooth and inviting.

"We did it!" Penny cheered, doing a little happy dance.

Just then, they heard a gentle knock at the front door. "That must be Grandma!" Mama said.

When Grandma Panda saw the guest room, her eyes twinkled with joy. "Oh my! This room looks absolutely wonderful! Someone worked very hard to make me feel welcome."

Penny's chest puffed up with pride. "I helped, Grandma! Mama taught me that when we do our best work, we show respect."

Grandma scooped Penny up in the biggest, warmest hug. "You are such a thoughtful and respectful little panda. Thank you for taking such good care of me."

That night, as Penny drifted off to sleep, she felt happy and proud. She had learned that showing respect by completing tasks with care made everyone feel loved and special.

The End.
