# Rocky Raccoon’s Home Office Band

Rocky was a musical raccoon. He lived with his family in a cozy home. His favorite room was the home office. It had a soft chair, a big desk, a humming computer, and a sunny window. The room smelled like paper and warm tea. Click, click went the keyboard. Hum, hum went the computer. Tap, tap went Rocky’s paws.

“Good morning, home office!” Rocky sang. He wore tiny headphones and a bright red scarf. He liked to make music and help his friends.

One windy day, Rocky heard a small knock. Tap-tap. Tap-tap.

Rocky opened the door. There stood Bella, a shy bluebird. Her feathers were ruffled by the wind. She looked worried.

“Hi, Bella,” said Rocky with a smile. “What’s wrong?”

“My song is lost,” Bella whispered. “The wind blew it away. I was going to sing for our town. It’s Civic Clean-Up Day. I want to help everyone feel good while they tidy the park. But now I can’t sing.”

Rocky’s ears perked up. “We can fix this together,” he said. “We are friends. Friends help.”

Bella blinked. “Really?”

“Really,” said Rocky. “Let’s make a new song right here in the home office!”

Rocky pulled a little box from under the desk. It was full of fun things. There were rubber bands, paper cups, pencils, and a tin can. “We can innovate,” said Rocky. “That means we can try new ideas.”

Bella tilted her head. “New ideas?”

“Yes!” said Rocky. “Listen.” He stretched a rubber band over a box. Twang! Twang! “Guitar sound!” He tapped the tin can. Ting! Ting! “Drum sound!” He blew gently across a paper cup. Hooo! “Wind sound!”

Bella smiled. “I like the wind sound. It sounds like a hug.”

Rocky giggled. “Let’s make a plan. Plan means steps. Step one: make a beat. Step two: add a tune. Step three: sing together.”

They began. Rocky tapped the can. Ting, ting, ting-ting! Bella bobbed and chirped, “Tweet, tweet, tweet!” But then Bella’s voice shook. “I can’t do it,” she said. “I feel shy.”

Rocky stopped. He put a soft paw on Bella’s wing. “It’s okay to feel shy,” he said. “We can take small steps. We can be resilient. That means we keep trying.”

Bella took a breath. “Okay. Small steps.”

Rocky turned down the lights. He closed the curtains a little. The room felt gentle and safe. He turned on a warm desk lamp. The light was golden. The computer hummed like a cat. “Better?” he asked.

“Better,” said Bella.

Rocky tried again. Ting, ting, ting-ting! “I’ll start,” he said. “You join when you are ready.”

Bella listened to the beat. Her tail feathers relaxed. She chirped softly. “Tweet, tweet.” Then louder. “Tweet, tweet, tweet!” Soon she was smiling. “I did it!”

“You did it!” Rocky cheered. “You are brave.”

They worked together. They tried new sounds. They made a shaker with rice in a jar. Shake-a, shake-a! They made a gentle bell with paperclips on a string. Clink, clink! They tested the microphone on the desk. “Hello,” said Rocky. “Hello,” said Bella. The sound came through the speakers clear and warm.

Rocky opened a little notebook. “Words for our song,” he said. “Let’s make words about helping our town.”

Bella nodded. “Friends pick up. Friends care. Friends sing and share.”

Rocky wrote and read it out loud. “Friends pick up. Friends care. Friends sing and share.” He grinned. “I love it!”

They practiced. Rocky counted, “One, two, three!” The beat began. The tune rose. Bella sang, “Friends pick up. Friends care.” Rocky added, “Friends sing and share.” Their voices blended like honey in tea.

Soon it was time to go to the park. The wind was still whooshy, but Rocky and Bella felt strong. They carried their little band in a backpack. The park had leaves on the ground. People had gloves and bags. Everyone was ready to clean.

Rocky set the microphone on a picnic table. He waved. “Hi, friends! We made a song to help us work together.”

Bella took a breath. Rocky winked. The beat began. Ting, ting, ting-ting! Bella sang, “Friends pick up. Friends care.” Children clapped. Grown-ups smiled. Rocky sang, “Friends sing and share!” Everyone joined in. Shake-a, shake-a went the rice. Clink, clink went the paperclip bell. The park felt bright and happy. Cleaning felt fun.

A little bunny dropped a wrapper by mistake. Bella paused and said kindly, “It’s okay. We can help.” The bunny picked it up and smiled. “Thank you,” said the bunny. “I can be a helper too.”

When the park was clean, the sun peeked through the clouds. The wind became soft. Rocky bowed. Bella chirped a happy trill. The crowd cheered.

Bella turned to Rocky. “Thank you for helping me find my song.”

Rocky hugged her with his striped tail. “Thank you for being my friend. We did it together.”

They walked home, backpacks light, hearts full. The home office welcomed them back with a quiet hum. Rocky wrote in his notebook, “Today we learned: friendship helps us be brave. Innovation is trying new ideas. Civic care means helping our town. Resilience is trying again.”

Bella nodded. “And music makes work feel good.”

Rocky smiled. “Friends pick up. Friends care. Friends sing and share.”

The End.
