# The Song of Fair Play

Chirpy was a tiny, bright blue singing bird. Chirpy lived in a cozy creative art room where children painted, glued, and dreamed. The room smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Sunlight made glitter sparkle on the tables. Paintbrushes stood like colorful trees in jars.

One morning, Chirpy hopped onto a soft paint rag and sang, “Tweet-a-dee! Tweet-a-doo! I love to sing for me and you!” Chirpy fluffed his feathers, happy and warm.

Today was Sports Day in the art room. The children had made a tiny indoor course with soft foam hoops, paper cup cones, and a rainbow ribbon finish line. They called it the Gentle Games. Everyone could play, even small friends like Chirpy.

“Welcome, friends!” said Miss May, the teacher, with a smile. “We will listen, take turns, and cheer for each other. Listening helps us be fair.” She tapped a little bell that went ding!

Chirpy hopped in place. “I can win if I sing very loud,” he chirped. “Tweet! Tweet! Out of my way!”

“Remember, Chirpy,” said Miss May gently, “we listen to rules and to each other. That is justice—being fair to everyone.”

Chirpy wiggled his wings. “Justice?” he asked.

“It means we all get the same chance,” Miss May said. “We use our ears and our hearts.”

First came the Hoop Hop. The rule was simple: hop through each foam hoop when Miss May said, “Go.” No bumping. No skipping.

“Ready?” Miss May asked.

Chirpy bounced. He wanted to go fast. He wanted to finish first. He chirped, “Tweet! I’ll start now!” and zipped through a hoop before the bell dinged.

“Chirpy,” said Miss May in a calm voice, “we start on ‘Go.’ Please listen.”

Chirpy paused. His cheeks—well, his feathers—felt hot. “Oh,” he peeped. “I forgot to listen.”

They tried again. Ding! “Go!”

Chirpy hopped, but he sang so loudly that he didn’t hear Benny Bear cub say, “I’m stuck!” Chirpy bumped a hoop, and it wobbled. Benny fell softly on a mat with a whump.

“Oof,” said Benny. He looked sad.

Chirpy’s song stopped. He saw Benny’s small frown and felt a tug in his chest. “Are you okay?” Chirpy asked.

“I needed help,” Benny said. “I said it, but you couldn’t hear me.”

Miss May knelt down. “Chirpy, listening is not just about rules. It is also about friends. When we listen, we understand. That is fair.”

Chirpy blinked. He listened to the room: the soft hum of the air, the rustle of paper, the gentle breaths of friends. He heard little sounds he had missed before.

“I want to try again,” Chirpy said. “This time I will use my big ears… well, my little ears in a big way.”

Next was the Cone Weave. The rule: weave around the cups. Don’t knock them down. And if someone needs help, stop and check.

“Ready?” said Miss May. “Go!”

Chirpy sang very softly now, like a lullaby. “Tweet… tweet…” He listened. He heard Benny’s happy giggle as he weaved. He heard Sumi the snail’s tiny slide sound. He heard Miss May clap, clap, clap.

Suddenly, Sumi whispered, “Oh… I’m slow.”

Chirpy paused. He looked at the line. “Friends,” he called gently, “let’s cheer for Sumi.”

“Go, Sumi, go!” said Chorpy in a sweet, clear voice.

“Go, Sumi, go!” the children echoed.

Sumi smiled and finished the weave. Chirpy’s chest felt warm and proud. Listening had helped him be kind.

Now came the Rainbow Ribbon Relay. The rule: pass the ribbon when you hear your name. If you pass too soon, the ribbon may drop.

Miss May rang the bell. “Team A: Benny… then Chirpy… then Sumi!”

Benny ran, soft feet pat-pat on the mat, and held the rainbow ribbon. “Chirpy!” he called.

Chirpy’s ears perked. He waited until he truly heard his name. Then he reached out, careful and ready. The ribbon slid into his wing tips like a silky rainbow snake. “Thank you, Benny!”

Chirpy listened again. “Sumi!” he called only when she looked up.

Sumi reached. The ribbon landed safe and sound. The team crossed the finish line together. The room filled with cheers and the gentle shake-shake of glitter pompoms. It sounded like rain on leaves.

Miss May smiled wide. “That was wonderful listening. You waited. You helped. You were fair. That is justice.”

Chirpy stood tall. “When I sing too loud, I can’t hear my friends,” he said. “Listening helps me play fair and understand.”

Benny nodded. “You listened to me, and I felt safe.”

Sumi added, “You cheered softly so I could hear my own brave heart.”

Chirpy’s tail twitched with joy. “I can sing and listen,” he said. “Soft when I need to hear. Strong when it’s time to cheer.”

Miss May gave everyone a tiny paper medal with a star. On Chirpy’s star, she wrote, “Great Listener.”

Chirpy sang a new song, gentle and bright. “Tweet-a-dee, we listen and share. Tweet-a-doo, we all play fair.”

The art room glowed with color and kindness. The paintbrush trees seemed to dance. The glitter sparkled like tiny suns. Chirpy heard every happy sound: claps, giggles, and the soft bell that said, “Well done.”

Chirpy took a deep breath. He felt calm. He felt strong. He felt heard, and he felt ready to hear others, too.

Lesson: Listening helps us be fair, kind, and part of the team. When we listen, everyone gets a turn, everyone feels safe, and everyone has fun.

The End.
