# Kippy's Big Choice Day

Kippy the kangaroo joey bounced excitedly into Grandpa's garage workshop. The morning sun streamed through the dusty windows, making everything sparkle like magic. Tools hung neatly on the walls, and the sweet smell of wood shavings filled the air.

"Good morning, Kippy!" called Grandpa Kangaroo, his whiskers twitching with a smile. "I have something special planned for us today."

Kippy's ears perked up. "What is it, Grandpa? What is it?"

Grandpa pointed to his workbench where three different projects waited. "Today, you get to choose what we build together. But," he said gently, "you can only pick one. We need to take our time and think carefully."

Kippy hopped closer to look. The first project was a bright red birdhouse with tiny windows. The second was a wooden boat that could float in puddles. The third was a garden box for growing carrots.

"Wow!" Kippy gasped, his eyes growing wide. "They all look so fun! I want to make them all right now!"

"I understand," Grandpa chuckled, his warm paw patting Kippy's shoulder. "But good decision-making means taking time to think. Let's look at each one carefully."

Kippy took a deep breath, just like Grandpa taught him. He closed his eyes for a moment and listened to the gentle sounds of the workshop - the tick-tock of the old clock and birds chirping outside.

First, he examined the birdhouse. "The birds would love this," he said thoughtfully. "They could stay warm and dry."

Next, he picked up the boat plans. "This would be perfect for sailing in rain puddles! I could watch it float."

Finally, he studied the garden box. "Mmm, I could grow my own crunchy carrots to eat!"

Grandpa watched patiently as Kippy hopped from project to project. "What's your heart telling you, little one?"

Kippy sat quietly on his tail, thinking hard. He remembered how much he loved playing outside after rain, splashing in puddles and feeling the cool water on his paws.

"I choose the boat!" Kippy announced with a happy bounce. "I want to take it outside and watch it sail!"

"Wonderful choice!" Grandpa beamed. "Now, let's work together. You can hand me the tools when I ask, and I'll show you each step."

They worked side by side all morning. Kippy carefully handed Grandpa the hammer when asked, then the sandpaper, then the paintbrush. He learned that taking turns and being patient made the work more fun.

"Feel how smooth the wood is now," Grandpa said, guiding Kippy's paw over the sanded boat.

When they finished painting the boat bright blue, Kippy clapped his paws together. "We did it! We really did it!"

After lunch, they took the boat outside to a big puddle from yesterday's rain. Kippy gently placed his boat in the water and watched it float perfectly.

"You made a thoughtful choice today," Grandpa said proudly. "You took time to think, you were patient, and you worked well with others."

Kippy watched his boat bob in the puddle, feeling proud and happy. "Making good choices feels really good inside," he said with a big smile.

As the boat sailed in circles, Kippy knew he had learned something important: the best decisions come when you take time to think and listen to your heart.

The End.
