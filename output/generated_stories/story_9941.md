# Joey Roo’s Porch of Helping

Joey Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey. He loved to bounce, hum a happy tune, and watch the world from his peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like warm wood. The soft breeze tickled his whiskers. Birds chirped, “Cheep, cheep,” and the sun felt cozy on his fur.

“Good morning, porch!” Joey Roo said, patting the railing with his little paw. “Today, I will help someone.”

Mama Roo brought a cool cup of lemon water. It clinked with tiny ice cubes. “Helping is kind,” Mama Roo said. “Helping can be small or big.”

Joey Roo nodded. “I can help!”

Just then, a tiny ant marched across the porch with a crumb bigger than its head. The crumb wobbled and slipped. “Oh no!” squeaked the ant.

Joey Roo crouched low. “Hello, little ant. Do you need help?”

“Yes, please,” said the ant in a teeny voice. “This crumb is too heavy.”

Joey Roo gently nudged the crumb with his soft paw, just a little push. The ant smiled. “Thank you!”

Joey Roo felt warm and proud. “Helping feels good,” he whispered.

A gentle “coo” floated down from above. A white pigeon with shiny eyes landed on the porch rail. A tiny cloth bag hung from her neck. “Hello,” she cooed. “I am Pippa the pigeon. I carry messages around the world.”

“Wow!” Joey Roo’s eyes grew wide. “Around the world?”

Pippa nodded. “From town to town, and friend to friend. Today, I lost a letter.” She peeked into the bag and sighed. “I’m worried. Someone is waiting.”

Joey Roo put his paw on his chest. “I can help!”

Pippa flapped her wings. “Thank you. The letter has a blue stamp with a small globe on it.”

Joey Roo bounced to look around. He looked under the porch chair—dust tickled his nose. “Achoo!” He looked behind a flower pot that smelled sweet like honey. No letter.

“Let’s use our words,” Mama Roo said kindly. “Good helpers listen and ask questions.”

Joey Roo nodded. He turned to Pippa. “Where did you fly before here?”

“I flew over the big gum tree and the white fence,” Pippa said. “The wind was strong.”

Joey Roo listened. He looked at the gum tree. Its leaves whispered. He looked at the fence, bright and smooth. Something fluttered near the fence post. A tiny corner of blue peeped out!

“I see it!” Joey Roo cried. He hopped carefully and picked up a small envelope. It had a blue stamp with a globe. “Found it!”

Pippa cooed with joy. “You helped me, and you helped the person waiting far away. That is being a good neighbor to the whole world.”

Joey Roo smiled so big his cheeks felt round. “Helping can go far!”

Just then, a little wombat named Wally waddled onto the porch. His fur was dusty, and his eyes looked sad. “Hi, Joey,” he mumbled. “I… I stepped on your chalk by the steps. It broke. I am sorry.”

Joey Roo blinked. His favorite chalk! He felt a small pinch in his tummy. He took a deep breath. The lemon water smelled fresh. Mama Roo’s hand felt gentle on his shoulder.

Joey Roo looked at Wally. “Thank you for telling me,” he said softly. “I feel sad. But I forgive you.”

Wally’s eyes brightened. “I can help fix it,” he said. “I have extra chalk at home.” He held out a small box. “For you.”

Joey Roo’s ears perked up. “Thank you, Wally!” He smiled. “Let’s draw together.”

They sat on the warm porch step. Scratch, scratch went the chalk. Joey Roo drew the big gum tree. Wally drew the white fence. Pippa watched and cooed. “I will draw a globe,” Joey Roo said. He made a round circle and colored blue. Then he added green shapes. “This is our world.”

Pippa nodded. “The world is big, but helping makes it feel close.”

Mama Roo brought a small plate of crisp apple slices and crunchy crackers. “Sharing snack?” she asked.

“Yes!” Joey Roo said. He passed the plate to Wally. “Here you go.” He passed it to Pippa. “Here you go.” The crackers were salty. The apples were sweet and juicy.

Pippa tucked the letter safely back in her bag. “Because you helped me, a child in another town will feel happy. Your help traveled far.”

Joey Roo felt his heart glow. He looked at Wally. “You helped me too. You said sorry and shared.” He looked at Pippa. “You taught me to ask good questions.”

The sun slid lower, painting the porch with soft gold. The birds sang sleepy songs. Joey Roo hugged Wally and bumped wings with Pippa.

“I learned,” Joey Roo said. “Helping others can be small, like a crumb, or big, like a letter to another town. I can say sorry. I can forgive. I can listen and share. That is good for my porch and for our whole world.”

Mama Roo smiled. “That is global citizenship,” she said. “We care for neighbors near and far.”

Joey Roo bounced once more and giggled. “Today, I helped. Tomorrow, I will help again.”

The End.
