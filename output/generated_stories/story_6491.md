# The Song That Fixed the Blocks

Chirpy was a small, bright blue singing bird. Chirpy lived in a playful living room with soft rugs, bouncy pillows, and a rainbow box of toys. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. The room smelled like fresh oranges, and a fan hummed a gentle, whirring song.

One morning, Chirpy hopped onto a stack of soft blocks. “Tweet-a-dee! Tweet-a-doo!” sang Chirpy. Chirpy loved to sing. Singing made the room feel happy. The blocks wobbled under Chirpy’s tiny feet. The stack was very, very tall.

“Careful, Chirpy,” said Teddy, a cuddly teddy bear who sat on the couch. “The tower is wobbly.”

“I can do it! I want the tallest tower!” Chirpy chirped. Chirpy flapped and tried to set one more block on top. Plop! The block slid. The whole tower tumbled down with a clatter-clack, clatter-clack!

Chirpy’s heart thumped. Chirpy’s feathers puffed. “Oh no!” chirped Chirpy. “My tower fell!” Chirpy felt hot and mad. Chirpy stomped, tiny claws tapping the floor. “I worked so hard! I want it now!”

Teddy watched with kind button eyes. “It’s okay to feel mad,” he said softly. “Let’s use our calm tools.”

Chirpy huffed. “I don’t want calm tools. I want a tower!” Chirpy’s cheeks felt hot. Chirpy’s tummy felt tight. Chirpy opened beak to shout—but stopped.

The fan went whirr, whirr. The clock went tick-tock. Chirpy heard them. Chirpy took a breath. In, out. Chirpy sang a small song, very quiet. “Breathe in, breathe out, little bird, little bird.” Chirpy felt the tight feeling get smaller.

“That’s it,” Teddy said. “Sing your calm song.”

Chirpy sang again, a little louder. “Breathe in, breathe out.” Chirpy’s feathers settled. The hot feeling cooled. The room felt friendly again.

“I still want a tall tower,” Chirpy told Teddy, voice soft now. “But I don’t want it to fall.”

Teddy nodded. “Let’s think. What do we know?”

Chirpy tilted head. “Tall towers can fall if they are wobbly.”

“Good knowledge,” said Teddy. “How can we make it less wobbly?”

Chirpy looked around. The rug was squishy. The blocks were smooth. The big book on the table was flat. “A flat base!” Chirpy chirped. “A strong bottom helps the top!”

“Resourceful idea,” Teddy said with a smile.

Chirpy hopped to the table and gently slid the big book onto the floor, next to the rug. Thump. It made a flat, sturdy spot. “We will build here,” Chirpy said.

Chirpy picked the biggest blocks for the bottom. “Big ones first,” Chirpy sang. “Big, then medium, then small.” The blocks felt cool and smooth on Chirpy’s feet. Chirpy pressed each block and checked. “Is it wiggly?” Chirpy gently tapped. If the block wiggled, Chirpy moved it. “Not here,” Chirpy said. “Here is better.”

Teddy clapped softly. “You are using your eyes, your hands, and your brain.”

Chirpy added more blocks. “I’m also using my song,” Chirpy said. Chirpy made up a building tune. “Press-press, check-check, stack-just-right.” The song made the work feel fun and slow, not rushed.

When the tower got tall, Chirpy felt a small spark of hurry. “I want to finish now!” chirped Chirpy. The spark got hot. Chirpy stopped, closed eyes, and sang the calm song again. “Breathe in, breathe out.” The hot spark cooled.

“Great anger-management,” Teddy whispered.

Chirpy opened eyes and smiled. “I can wait. I can think.” Chirpy looked around for help. The pillow was soft. “A pillow wall!” Chirpy chirped. Chirpy pushed two pillows close to the tower. “If it wobbles, the pillows will catch it.”

“That’s very resourceful,” Teddy said. “You found what you needed right here.”

Chirpy added the last small block, very slowly. Tap. Tap. Tap. Chirpy checked the sides. Not wiggly. Chirpy stepped back and looked. The tower stood tall and steady on the big book, with pillows hugging it.

Chirpy’s heart felt light and floaty. “I did it!” Chirpy sang. “Tweet-a-dee! Tower for me!”

Teddy raised his soft paw. “High-five?” Chirpy tapped the paw with a tiny wing. Slap! They both laughed.

Chirpy flew in a gentle circle, singing a bright, sweet song that filled the living room. Sunlight made the blocks glow like candy colors. The fan whispered, the clock ticked, and everything felt calm and safe.

“I learned something,” Chirpy said. “When I feel mad, I can breathe and sing my calm song. I can use my brain and look for helpful things. I can ask, ‘What do I know?’ and ‘What can I use?’”

Teddy nodded. “That is knowledge and resourcefulness. You also talked to me. That is good communication.”

Chirpy puffed a happy feather puff. “I will remember,” Chirpy chirped. “Big base, slow steps, calm song, and friendly help.”

“Exactly,” said Teddy. “Now your tower is strong, and so are you.”

Chirpy sat by the tower and hummed a gentle tune. The living room felt warm and safe. The tower did not wobble. Chirpy felt proud, smart, and peaceful.

The End.
