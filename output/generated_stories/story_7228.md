# Panda Poppy’s Study Corner Plan

Poppy the panda had a cozy study corner. It was soft and warm, with a tiny lamp that glowed like a firefly. A small wooden desk sat by the window. Crayons smelled sweet like berries. Paper felt smooth under Poppy’s paws. A ticking clock went tick-tock, tick-tock.

Today, Poppy had a goal. “I will learn to trace my name,” Poppy said, patting her round belly. “P-O-P-P-Y!”

Poppy took a deep breath. “I can do it. One step at a time.”

Mama Panda peeked in. “What’s your plan, little one?”

“My plan is to trace my name,” Poppy said. “First, I will get ready.”

Poppy made a checklist. She drew simple pictures: a pencil, a paper, a cup of water, and a small snack. “This is my preparation,” Poppy said proudly.

Together, Poppy and Mama set out the things. Pencil? Check. Paper? Check. Water? Check. Snack? Check. Poppy lined them up neatly. The pencil rolled, but Poppy gently stopped it with her paw. “Stay,” she giggled.

“Do you have a goal for today?” Mama asked.

“Yes,” said Poppy. “My goal is to trace my name two times.”

“That’s a clear goal,” Mama said with a smile. “If you feel wiggly or stuck, remember S-T-O-P.”

“What is S-T-O-P?” Poppy asked, tilting her fuzzy head.

“Stop. Take a breath. Observe. Proceed,” Mama said softly. “It helps when we feel frustrated.”

Poppy nodded. “I will try.”

Poppy placed her paw on the pencil. She started with P. The line went up… and then it went squiggle. “Oh no,” Poppy sighed. Her ears drooped. Her cheeks felt hot. “It’s not perfect.”

Her tummy felt tight, like a knot. The tick-tock felt loud. The crayon smell felt too strong. Poppy’s eyes grew wet.

Mama pointed to a tiny blue card on the desk. It said, “S-T-O-P.”

Poppy whispered, “Stop.” She put the pencil down.

“Take a breath,” she said. Poppy breathed in slowly. In through the nose. Out through the mouth. In… and out. Her shoulders softened.

“Observe,” Poppy said. She looked around. The lamp was warm. The chair was comfy. Her paper was clean on the other side. Her first try was bumpy, but it was still a try. Poppy touched the paper. It felt smooth again.

“Proceed,” Poppy said. “I will try again.”

Poppy turned the paper over. This time, she traced a big P with slow, steady moves. “Up, around, and stop,” she murmured. Scratch, scratch went the pencil. Poppy smiled. “That felt better.”

She moved to O. “Round and round,” Poppy sang softly. The O looked like a donut. She giggled. “Yum.”

Then P again. Her paw slipped. The curve looked flat. “Oh,” she frowned. Her tummy squeezed tight. “I feel frustrated.”

Poppy remembered S-T-O-P. “Stop,” she said, and put the pencil down. “Take a breath.” She breathed in and out, counting to three with her paw taps: one, two, three. “Observe,” she whispered. “I feel better when I sit tall.” She wiggled her tail and sat straight. “Proceed,” she said, and tried the P again, slowly. Scratch, scratch. Better!

She traced the Y with care. “Down and slide and tail,” she said. The tail looked wiggly, like a fish. Poppy clapped softly. “I did it once!”

Poppy took a little sip of water. The cool sip made her mouth feel fresh. She nibbled one crunchy bamboo bite. “Snack break helps me stay calm,” she said.

“Would you like help?” Mama asked.

“I think I can try by myself,” Poppy said. “My goal is two times.”

Poppy started her second try. P… O… P… P… Y… The lines were not perfect, but they were steady. The clock went tick-tock, nice and gentle now. The lamp glowed like a hug. Poppy’s paws felt warm and sure.

She looked at both papers. “I met my goal!” Poppy cheered. “I traced my name two times.”

Mama clapped. “You prepared your space, you set a clear goal, and you coped with frustration. You were patient.”

Poppy smiled big. “When I felt tight and hot, I used S-T-O-P. I took breaths. I tried again. I can learn new things.”

Mama gave Poppy a soft paw bump. “Yes. When we prepare and take it step by step, our brains feel safe and strong.”

Poppy put her papers on the wall. She drew a gold star beside them. “Tomorrow, my goal will be three times,” she said. “I will use my plan again.”

The study corner felt extra cozy. The lamp hummed. The paper smelled like sunshine. Poppy felt calm and proud.

She whispered to the room, “I am patient. I can try, pause, and try again.”

And the room seemed to whisper back, tick-tock, “Yes, you can.”

The End.
