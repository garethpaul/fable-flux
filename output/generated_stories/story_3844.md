# Benny Beaver’s Sunny Backyard Plan

Benny was a busy beaver with a big, warm smile. He lived by a sunny backyard where the grass felt tickly, the flowers smelled sweet, and the birds sang, “Tweet-tweet!” every morning. Benny loved to build things. He loved to help. He loved to do everything… all at once!

One bright day, Benny woke up and stretched his little paws. “I have three big jobs today,” he said. “I will build a tiny wooden bridge by the flowers. I will water the tomatoes. And I will make a cozy reading spot in the shade.”

Benny’s tail thumped happily on the soft ground. Thump, thump! He hurried to the wood pile. He hurried to the water can. He hurried to the blanket for the reading spot. He hurried so much that his whiskers wiggled!

Then he stopped. “Oh no,” he whispered. “I feel too busy.” His tummy felt fluttery, like butterfly wings. He held his chest and took a slow breath. In… and out. The sun felt warm on his fur, and a gentle breeze brushed his cheeks.

Benny’s friend, Maya the robin, hopped down from the fence. “Hi, Benny! You look worried,” she chirped.

Benny nodded. “I want to do everything. But my head feels like a big, messy toolbox.”

Maya tilted her head and listened. “Maybe we can make a plan,” she said softly. “We can do one thing at a time.”

Benny blinked. “A plan?” He liked how that word sounded. Plan. It felt calm and neat.

Just then, Tilly the turtle peeked out from under a leaf. “Plans are nice,” Tilly said in a slow, kind voice. “I make small steps. Small steps make big things.”

Benny’s shoulders relaxed. “Small steps,” he repeated. He found a smooth stick and drew lines in the dirt. Scratch, scratch. “Step one: build the tiny bridge. Step two: water the tomatoes. Step three: make the cozy reading spot.”

Maya flapped her wings. “Good list!” she sang. “Do you need help?”

Benny looked at his friends. He felt warm inside. “Yes, please. But I can try to do my part first.”

They all smiled. Benny picked up little sticks and placed them gently by the flower bed. Tap, tap, tap went Benny’s paws. He measured with his eyes and lined up the sticks. “This one here. That one there.” He tied them with soft grass. He tested the tiny bridge with his paw. “It’s steady!”

Maya cheered, “Tweet! You did it!”

Benny grinned. “Step one is done.” He put a small dot next to step one in the dirt. Dot.

Next, Benny lifted the watering can. Slosh, slosh. The water sparkled like tiny stars in the sunlight. “I will count,” he said. “One… two… three little pours.” The tomatoes looked happy and shiny. A sweet, fresh smell rose from the wet soil. Benny took another slow breath. In… and out. His tummy felt calm.

“Step two is done,” he said. Dot.

Tilly smiled. “You are doing great, Benny.”

Now it was time for the cozy reading spot. Benny found the blue blanket. It felt soft like a cloud. He spread it under a shady tree. He added two pillows, a basket of books, and a cup of cool water with a slice of lemon. The lemon smelled bright and sunny.

Benny looked at his list in the dirt. “All done,” he said. His eyes shone. His tail gave a happy thump. Thump!

Then Benny heard a tiny sound. “Oh!” squeaked a little ant near the tiny bridge. “The bridge is lovely, but a leaf is in the way.”

Benny felt the flutter again. “A problem,” he said. His cheeks grew warm. He put his paw on his heart. “I feel worried,” he said out loud. “I can name my feeling. I can take a breath.” He breathed in and out. “Now I can think.”

Maya hopped closer. “What’s your plan, Benny?”

Benny looked around. He found a small twig and a little shell. “I will use the twig to lift the leaf,” he said. “And the shell can hold it like a tiny bowl.”

With a careful wiggle, he lifted the leaf. With a gentle slide, he tucked it into the shell. The path was clear. The ant waved its tiny arms. “Thank you!”

Benny smiled. “We did it!”

“You solved the problem,” Tilly said, slow and proud.

Benny sat on the blanket with his friends. They shared cool water and looked at the sunny backyard. The air smelled like grass and lemon. A soft breeze rustled the leaves. The tiny bridge looked neat. The tomatoes glowed red. The reading spot felt safe and cozy.

Maya asked, “Benny, what did you learn today?”

Benny thought. “I learned to make a plan,” he said. “I learned to do small steps. I learned to name my feelings and breathe. I learned I can ask for help and also help myself.”

Tilly nodded. “That is smart time-using,” she said with a smile.

Benny giggled. “Time-management,” he tried the big word. It felt funny and good. “I can be busy and calm.”

They opened a book together. Benny read the pictures while Maya chirped and Tilly hummed. The sun made golden shapes on the blanket, and the day felt just right.

The End.
