# Niko the Elephant Learns to Ask and Try

In a playful living room, soft sunlight made warm squares on the rug. Blocks were stacked like tiny towers. Crayons lay in a rainbow row. A small drum and a soft ball sat by a basket. The room smelled like fresh oranges and clean soap.

A gentle elephant calf named Niko waddled in. His ears were floppy and kind. His feet made soft thump-thump sounds on the rug. Niko whispered, “Good morning, toys.” He liked to say hello.

Niko looked at the soft ball. He looked at the crayons. He looked at the drum. His tummy felt fluttery. “I want to play,” he said, “but I don’t know how to start.” He rubbed his trunk on his cheek. His eyes felt watery, like tiny ponds.

Mama Elephant sat on the couch with a bright book. The book had pictures of shapes and games. She looked up and smiled. “Hi, Niko. What do you need?”

Niko took a deep breath. “Mama, I want to learn. I want to know how to play ball and how to draw. I want to know the rules.” His voice was small but brave.

Mama nodded. “That is self-advocacy,” she said gently. “You told me what you need. Asking for help is smart.” She patted the couch. “Let’s learn together.”

First, they picked up the soft ball. It felt squishy, like a pillow. Niko pressed it with his trunk. Squeak! It made a happy sound.

“What are the rules?” Niko asked.

“Let’s use knowledge,” Mama said. She opened the bright book. The page showed a picture of a ball and two feet. “Rule one: Eyes on the ball. Rule two: Gentle taps. Rule three: Try again and again.”

Niko put the ball on the rug. His big feet felt tickly. “Eyes on the ball,” he whispered. He tapped. The ball rolled and bumped a block tower. Clink-clink! Blocks wobbled and fell.

Niko’s ears drooped. “I didn’t do it right.”

Mama touched his shoulder. “Remember rule three. Try again.”

Niko sniffed. He stood tall. “I will try again.” He tapped the ball with a little foot. Tap. It rolled to the couch, slow and straight. “I did it!” Niko laughed. His laugh felt like bubbles.

“Let’s make it sport time,” Mama said. She put two pillows on the floor like goals. “Kick to the pillow.”

Niko lined up. “Eyes on the ball. Gentle tap.” He kicked. The ball slid into the pillow with a soft thump. “Goal!” The room felt bright and sunny in his chest.

“Knowledge helps,” Mama said. “You learned the rules and used them.”

Niko nodded. “I used knowledge.” He felt proud, like a big, warm blanket around his heart.

Then Niko looked at the crayons and paper. “I want to make art. But my trunk feels wiggly. I don’t know how to draw a ball.”

“Let’s ask and learn again,” Mama said.

Niko lifted his trunk. “Mama, please teach me how to draw a circle.”

Mama smiled. “Great asking. Step one: Hold the crayon steady. Step two: Start small. Step three: Go slow.”

Niko chose the blue crayon. It smelled like wax and sky. He held it with his trunk. He touched the paper. Scritch-scratch. The sound was soft, like tiny rain.

His line went bumpy. He frowned. “It is not a circle.”

Mama pointed to the book. The next page had shapes. “Let’s trace a lid first. That is knowledge too. We use tools.”

Niko put a round lid on the paper. He traced slowly. Scritch… scratch… all the way around. He lifted the lid. A circle! “I did it!” He giggled. He colored it yellow. He added a little smile. “Hello, Sunny Ball!”

“Beautiful art,” Mama said. “You used tools. You asked questions. You tried.”

Niko’s tummy rumbled. He gave a shy grin. “Can I drum a little?”

“Sure,” Mama said. “What do you need this time?”

Niko thought. “I need to know how to make a steady beat.”

“Good asking,” Mama said. “Rule: Count 1-2-3-4. Tap on each number.”

Niko tapped the drum. Tum… tum… tum… tum. The sound was deep and friendly. He felt the beat in his feet. Thump-thump, thump-thump. He smiled with his whole face.

Soon, Niko had an idea. “Let’s make a game! I will kick the ball to the pillow, then draw a circle, then drum four beats.” He wiggled with joy.

“Game on,” Mama said.

Niko kicked. Thump! Goal.
He drew. Scritch-scratch! Circle.
He drummed. Tum… tum… tum… tum!

They clapped together. The living room felt like a happy song. The orange smell and the crayon smell mixed into something cozy.

Niko hugged Mama. “I learned so much. I asked for help. I used rules. I tried again.”

Mama hugged back. “That is knowledge in action. Sports for the body. Art for the heart. Asking for help for your voice.”

Niko stood tall. “I am small, but I can learn big things.”

“Yes,” Mama said. “You can.”

The End.
