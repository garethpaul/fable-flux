# Benny Bear's Fair Share

Benny Bear sat in the warm, sunny backyard. His fluffy brown fur felt toasty in the golden sunlight. The grass smelled fresh and green. Pretty flowers danced in the gentle breeze.

Benny had three honey cookies on a blue plate. They were golden and sweet, just the way he liked them. His tummy rumbled with excitement.

"Yum, yum!" Benny said, picking up the first cookie. "These look so good!"

Just as he was about to take a big bite, he heard sniffling sounds. Behind the big oak tree, he saw Ruby Rabbit. Her soft gray ears drooped down. Tears sparkled in her brown eyes.

"What's wrong, Ruby?" Benny asked, setting down his cookie.

"I'm so hungry," Ruby whispered. "I forgot my lunch at home. My tummy feels empty and growly."

Benny looked at his three cookies. Then he looked at sad Ruby. His heart felt warm and caring.

"Would you like a cookie?" Benny asked with a big smile.

Ruby's ears perked up. "Really? But those are yours!"

"Friends share," Benny said proudly. He gave Ruby one golden cookie. "Now we both have treats!"

Ruby took a small bite. "Mmm! This tastes like sunshine and sweetness! Thank you, Benny!"

Soon, they heard tiny squeaking sounds. Little Pip Mouse peeked out from under a leaf. His whiskers twitched nervously.

"Oh my," Pip squeaked. "Those cookies smell wonderful. I haven't eaten anything all day."

Benny looked at his two remaining cookies. Ruby looked at hers. They both smiled at the same time.

"Here, Pip!" Ruby said. "You can have half of mine!"

"And half of mine too!" Benny added cheerfully.

Now all three friends had cookies to share. They sat together on the soft grass under the shady tree.

"This is the best picnic ever!" Pip squeaked happily.

"Sharing makes everything taste better," Ruby said, wiggling her nose.

Benny felt proud and happy inside. His tummy was full, but his heart felt even fuller. "When we're fair and share with friends, everyone feels good!"

The three friends munched their cookies slowly. They listened to birds singing sweet songs. Butterflies danced around the colorful flowers. The warm sun made everything feel perfect.

"I was worried I wouldn't have enough," Benny said thoughtfully. "But sharing made me feel really good inside."

"You were so kind to us," Ruby said, giving Benny a gentle hug.

"And fair too!" added Pip, climbing onto Benny's soft paw.

After they finished eating, the friends played together. They rolled in the grass and chased butterflies. They laughed and giggled until the sun began to set.

"Thank you for sharing, Benny," Ruby said. "You taught me that being fair makes everyone happy."

"And you taught me that sharing is caring!" Pip squeaked.

Benny's chest puffed up with pride. He had made a good choice. Being fair wasn't always easy, but it always felt right.

"Tomorrow, let's all bring snacks to share!" Benny suggested.

"Yes!" Ruby and Pip cheered together.

As the golden sun set behind the trees, Benny waved goodbye to his friends. He skipped home with a big smile on his furry face.

That night, Benny dreamed of more sunny days filled with friendship, fairness, and sharing. He learned that when you're fair to others, you feel good about yourself too.

The End.
