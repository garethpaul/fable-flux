# Bella Bird Learns to Save Water

Bella was a bright blue bird who loved to sing beautiful songs. She lived in a cozy nest near Mrs. Garcia's house and often flew inside to explore. Her favorite room was the warm, steamy laundry room where she could hear the washing machine go "whoosh-whoosh" and smell the fresh, clean scent of soap bubbles.

One sunny morning, Bella flew into the laundry room and noticed something wonderful - a shiny silver faucet that sparkled in the sunlight.

"Oh my!" chirped Bella excitedly. "What a pretty water fountain!"

She turned the handle with her tiny orange feet, and crystal-clear water began flowing out. "Splash, splash, splash!" The water made such a lovely sound as it hit the sink.

Bella was so happy that she began to sing her favorite song while the water kept running. "Tweet-tweet-trill, what a thrill! Water flowing down the hill!"

Mrs. Garcia heard Bella's sweet singing and came into the laundry room. When she saw the water running and running, her eyes grew wide.

"Oh, Bella!" said Mrs. Garcia gently. "The water is still flowing, little one."

Bella tilted her tiny head, confused. "But Mrs. Garcia, I love the pretty water sounds! Listen - splash, splash, splash!"

Mrs. Garcia sat down and spoke softly to Bella. "Sweet bird, water is very precious. When we leave it running, we waste it. Other animals and people need water too."

Bella's bright black eyes blinked with surprise. "Really? But there's so much water!"

"Let me show you something," said Mrs. Garcia. She took Bella to the window and pointed outside. "See how the flowers look droopy? They need water to drink. And the little squirrels need clean water too."

Bella looked at the sad, thirsty flowers and felt her heart flutter with worry. "Oh no! Did I take their water?"

"When we waste water, there's less for everyone," explained Mrs. Garcia kindly. "But you can help! Always remember to turn off the water when you're done."

Bella felt frustrated and sad. Her little wings drooped. "But I don't know how to remember! It's too hard!"

Mrs. Garcia smiled warmly. "I have an idea! How about you sing a special water-saving song? That will help you remember."

Bella's eyes brightened. She loved to sing! "What kind of song?"

"Try this," said Mrs. Garcia. "Turn it on, turn it off, save the water, nice and soft!"

Bella practiced the song several times, flapping her wings happily. "Turn it on, turn it off, save the water, nice and soft! Tweet-tweet!"

From that day on, whenever Bella wanted to play with water, she would sing her special song. She would turn on the faucet, enjoy the water for just a moment, then sing "Turn it on, turn it off, save the water, nice and soft!" and carefully turn it off with her feet.

Mrs. Garcia was so proud of Bella. The flowers outside grew tall and green, and all the animals had plenty of fresh water to drink.

Bella learned that being careful with water was a way to show love for all her friends - the flowers, the squirrels, and everyone who needed water.

Now Bella was the happiest singing bird in the whole neighborhood, knowing she was helping take care of the world around her.

The End.
