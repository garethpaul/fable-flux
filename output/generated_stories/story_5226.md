# Milo Mouse Rides Calmly

Milo was a kind little mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. Today, Milo climbed into the family car with Mama and Papa. The seat was smooth and cool. The car smelled like lemons and a little like peanut butter from yesterday’s snack. The seatbelt clicked, “click!” across Milo’s tiny belly.

“Ready, Milo?” asked Mama.

“Yes!” squeaked Milo. “We are going to the market!”

The car hummed, “vrrrmmm,” and the trees outside drifted by. Milo liked watching the clouds. He saw one that looked like a fluffy cheese puff. His tummy gave a little rumble.

“Papa,” Milo said, “my tummy is hungry.”

“We packed snacks,” Papa said. “We’ll eat when we stop.”

Milo nodded. He wanted a snack now, but he took a slow breath in…and out. He felt the air tickle his nose. That felt calm and nice.

Soon the car bumped over a little pothole. “Bump!” Milo’s tail twitched. He felt a bubble of worry in his chest.

“Mama,” Milo whispered, “the bumps make me feel wiggly.”

Mama turned and smiled. “Thank you for telling me. Let’s use our calm breaths together. In… and out…”

Milo and Mama breathed slowly. In… and out… The worry bubble got smaller. Milo felt his shoulders soften.

The sun shone through the window, warm and golden. Milo rubbed the foggy glass with his tiny paw and drew a happy face. He giggled.

Then Milo smelled something sweet. It smelled like apples and cinnamon. His tummy rumbled again, louder this time. “Rrrr-rrr.”

“Mama, can I have a cookie?” Milo asked. “I smell cookies.”

Papa chuckled. “That’s Mrs. Rabbit’s bakery. We’re driving by.”

Milo pressed his nose to the window. “It smells so good!”

“Milo,” Mama said gently, “we will have a snack when we stop. We brought crunchy carrots and a little cheese.”

Milo made a small frown. “But I want a cookie,” he said in a tiny voice.

Milo looked at Mama and then at Papa. He remembered the calm breaths. He remembered being honest. He squeezed his paws together.

“I feel disappointed,” Milo said. “I want a cookie, but I can wait. Can I have a carrot first?”

Mama’s smile grew warm and wide. “Thank you for telling us how you feel. That is good communication. Yes, a carrot first is a healthy choice.”

“We’ll stop at the park ahead,” Papa said. “Almost there.”

The car kept humming, “vrrrmmm.” Birds swooped past the window. Milo listened. Tweet, tweet! He felt better. He was proud he used his words.

Suddenly, he felt a small crinkle under his leg. He reached down and pulled out a wrapped candy. It was sticky on one corner. His eyes went big.

“Oh!” Milo gasped. The candy looked yummy. He thought, I could open it now. But he also thought about honesty. He took one calm breath. In… and out…

“Mama,” Milo said softly, “I found a candy under the seat. It is sticky.”

“Thank you for telling us,” Mama said. “That was honest. Sticky candy is not a good car snack.”

Papa nodded. “Candy can wait. Carrots help our bodies grow strong. Let’s throw the sticky candy away at the park.”

Milo put the candy in a little trash bag. His chest felt light, like a feather. He was glad he told the truth.

At last, the car rolled to a stop. “We’re here!” Papa said.

Click went the seatbelts. Milo hopped out. The air smelled fresh like grass. The wind brushed his whiskers. The sun warmed his ears.

Mama opened the snack box. Crunch! Milo bit a carrot. It was juicy and sweet. He nibbled a tiny square of cheese. It was creamy and smooth. His tummy felt happy.

“Thank you for waiting, Milo,” Mama said. “You were calm and patient.”

Milo smiled. “I used my breathing. I used my words. And I told the truth.”

Papa handed him a small apple slice. “Good choices help us feel good.”

After snack time, Mama said, “We can share a small cookie later. First, let’s play.”

Milo clapped his little paws. “Yay!”

They played a short game of I-Spy beside the car. “I spy something blue,” said Milo.

“My hat!” Papa laughed.

Milo giggled and twirled. The wind whispered, “whoosh.” He felt peaceful and proud.

Back in the car, Milo buckled up. “Click.” He took one calm breath just because it felt nice.

“Mama?” he asked.

“Yes, dear?”

“When I feel wiggly, I can breathe in and out. I can say how I feel. I can tell the truth. And I can choose a healthy snack.”

“That’s right,” Mama said. “Calmness helps our words come out kind. Communication helps us be understood. Honesty makes trust. Good nutrition helps our bodies.”

Milo’s eyes sparkled. “I am a calm, kind mouse!”

The car hummed home. Milo watched the clouds again. They looked like pillows in the sky. His tummy was full, his heart was light, and his mind felt quiet and cozy.

Today’s ride taught Milo a big lesson: when we stay calm, use our words, tell the truth, and choose healthy foods, little bumps feel small, and the ride feels safe and happy.

The End.
