# Polly Paints the Closet

Polly the parrot loved to make things. She had bright green feathers, a sunny yellow beak, and a big, happy, “Squawk!” When Polly flapped her wings, tiny breezes tickled her face. She liked to say, “I can make it! I can make it!” every time she had an idea.

One rainy day, Polly and her friends, Ben and Mia, played inside. The house smelled like warm soap and fresh bread. Pitter-patter, the rain tapped the windows. Ben opened a small door and said, “Look! The closet is our hideaway.”

Inside the closet hideaway, it was cozy and soft. There were fluffy blankets, a small lamp, and a basket of crayons. Mia giggled and whispered, “Shh! This is our quiet place.” The lamp glowed like a friendly firefly.

Ben’s mom peeked in and smiled. “You can play in here,” she said, “but please follow these directions: only draw on paper, keep the blankets clean, and ask for help if you need it.”

Polly bobbed her head. “Directions, check! I can make it even better!” She saw the plain brown boxes on the shelf and the white closet wall. Her creative feathers tingled. She imagined stars on the wall, and a rainbow on the boxes. Her heart felt fizzy with excitement.

Mia held up paper. “Let’s draw stars here,” she said, patting the paper.

But Polly was already flapping up. “I can make it extra special!” she chirped. She dipped a blue crayon and started drawing a big star right on the wall. Scritch-scritch! The crayon made a crunchy sound. Blue dust sprinkled down.

Ben gasped. “Polly, the directions said paper.”

Polly paused, then smiled. “I’m helping! It will be beautiful.”

She drew a rainbow on a blanket. Swoosh, swoosh, the soft fabric moved under her wing. The blanket smelled like lavender. The colors spread wide and bright.

“Polly,” Mia said softly, “the directions said to keep the blankets clean.”

Polly fluttered down, her cheeks warm. “But it looks so nice,” she said. Her tummy felt wiggly. The closet hideaway was messy now. Blue on the wall. Colors on the blanket. Crayons on the floor. The fun fizzy feeling faded.

Ben’s mom came back. She looked at the wall. She looked at the blanket. She took a deep breath. “Oh dear,” she said. Her voice was calm but firm. “Polly, did you follow the directions?”

Polly’s feathers drooped. “No,” she whispered. “I wanted to make it special. I forgot to listen. I’m sorry.”

Mia put a kind hand on Polly’s wing. “We can fix it together.”

Ben nodded. “We are friends. Friends help.”

Ben’s mom smiled a little. “Thank you for being honest, Polly. When we follow directions, we keep things safe and kind. Let’s make a plan.”

Polly perked up. “A plan?” She loved plans!

Ben’s mom said, “First, wipe the wall with a damp cloth. Only on the crayon spots. Second, wash the blanket with cold water. Third, make art on paper. Tape the paper to the wall. That way, the wall stays clean.”

Polly listened carefully. She repeated, “Wipe wall, wash blanket, draw on paper.” She tapped her beak three times. “Wipe, wash, draw.”

Together, they worked. The cloth went swish, swish, and the blue star faded. The blanket went plop into a tub. Bubbles popped and smelled like lemons. Polly giggled when a bubble landed on her beak. Pop!

“Thank you for helping,” Mia said, smiling.

“Thank you for teaching me,” Polly said, smiling back.

When the wall was clean and the blanket was drying, Ben brought more paper and tape. Polly’s eyes shone. “Now I can make it special and follow directions!”

She drew a sky full of stars on the paper. She drew gentle rain, a bright rainbow, and a little parrot with a happy beak. Scritch-scritch on paper sounded friendly. They taped the drawings to the wall. The closet looked colorful and neat. The lamp glowed on the pictures, and the colors danced softly.

Ben’s mom peeked in again. “You followed the directions,” she said. “I’m proud of you.”

Polly stood tall. “Following directions helps everyone,” she said. “It keeps our hideaway safe. It keeps our things clean. And it helps friends feel happy.”

Mia hugged Polly. “You are a creative parrot and a kind friend.”

Polly hugged her back with her wings. “Next time, I will listen first,” she said. “Then I will create.”

They cuddled on the fluffy blankets. The closet smelled fresh and lemony. The rain outside slowed to a hush. Polly looked at their art and felt warm inside.

“Polly,” Ben asked, “what did we learn today?”

Polly chirped, “We learned to follow directions, to be kind when mistakes happen, and to fix problems with friends.”

Ben grinned. “That’s a good lesson.”

Polly nodded. “I can make it! And I can listen, too.”

The End.
