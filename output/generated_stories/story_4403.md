# Benny Beaver’s Cozy Clean-Up

Benny was a busy beaver with a big, flat tail and quick little paws. He loved building block towers, lining up toy boats, and making tiny twig bridges. Benny’s cozy bedroom smelled like fresh wood shavings and warm blankets. Sunlight made soft squares on the floor. It was a happy, safe place.

One morning, Mama Beaver peeked in. “Good morning, Benny!” she said, her voice gentle like a stream. “It’s clean-up time before playtime.”

Benny’s ears twitched. “But I want to build a super-tall tower now,” he said. He picked up a block and tapped it. Tap-tap.

Mama smiled. “First we follow directions. Then we play. We can do it together. Cooperation makes it faster and more fun.”

Benny puffed his cheeks. He felt a little squirmy inside. “I don’t want to,” he whispered. His tail thumped the rug. Thump.

Mama sat beside him on the soft blanket. “I hear your big feelings,” she said. “Let’s take belly breaths to help your frustration.” She placed a paw on her tummy. “Breathe in… and out.”

Benny put a paw on his tummy too. In… and out. He felt his body relax, like a calm pond after a splash. “Okay,” he said, a tiny smile growing. “Let’s try.”

Mama clapped softly. “We’ll work as a team. I’ll pick up the puzzle pieces. You put the blocks in the blue bin. We can count! Ready?”

“Ready!” Benny chirped.

They began. Clink! went the blocks into the bin. Click! went the puzzle pieces into the box. Benny liked the sounds. The room felt calmer with each little clink and click.

His cousin, Tilly Turtle, poked her head through the door. “Hi, Benny,” she said in her slow, sweet voice. “Can I play too?”

Benny’s tail swished. “We are cleaning first,” he said. “We’re following directions so we can be safe. Want to help?”

Tilly smiled. “Yes. I can roll up the soft socks.”

“Thank you,” said Benny. He liked that Tilly moved slowly. It helped him remember to go slow and careful too.

They worked together. Benny carried two blocks. Tilly rolled socks into neat little balls. Mama put books back on the shelf. The room smelled like lemon soap. The blanket felt warm under their knees.

Then Benny found his toy hammer under his pillow. “Ooo! Hammer time!” He lifted it high.

Mama shook her head gently. “Pause, Benny. Hammers are only for the work mat. That keeps everyone safe.”

Benny froze. He remembered the rule. Safety first. He lowered the hammer. “To the work mat,” he said, and he walked carefully, one, two, three steps, and set it down. “I followed directions!”

“You did,” Mama said. “That’s smart and safe.”

Benny felt proud. His chest felt big and light, like a bubble. “Tilly, do you want to try my block tower later?” he asked.

Tilly nodded. “Yes, but I like short towers,” she said. “Tall ones scare me. They go crash.”

Benny blinked. He liked tall towers. But he remembered tolerance—being kind when someone likes things a different way. “We can make a short tower together,” he said. “Short towers don’t crash as hard.”

Tilly’s eyes shone. “Thank you.”

They kept going. Clink. Click. Roll. Benny hummed a little tune. “Clean, clean, clean, so we can play. We do it together, hip-hip hooray!”

Soon, only the blanket was left. Benny grabbed one corner. Tilly grabbed another, slow and steady. “Ready, lift!” Benny called.

“Lift,” Tilly echoed. They folded the blanket like a big soft sandwich. It smelled like sunshine.

“All done,” said Mama, looking around the cozy bedroom. The floor was clear. The bins were full. The shelf was tidy.

Benny’s whiskers wiggled. “We did it! Cooperation made it fast.”

Mama nodded. “And safe,” she added. “You followed directions, even when you felt frustrated. You showed patience with Tilly, and Tilly helped with care.”

Benny bounced on his toes. “Now can we play?”

“Yes,” said Mama. “What’s the plan?”

Benny glanced at Tilly. He remembered her feelings. “Let’s build a short, strong tower,” he said. “We can take turns.”

“I place the first block,” Tilly said. Plunk.

“I place the next,” Benny said. Plunk.

They took turns. The tower grew to four blocks high—just right. Benny wanted to add more. His tail wiggled. He felt the urge like a fizzy soda pop. He took a belly breath. In… and out. “Your turn to decide, Tilly,” he said.

Tilly patted the top block. “All done,” she said softly.

Benny smiled. “All done,” he agreed. He liked how calm the room felt. He liked that Tilly felt safe.

Mama clapped. “What wonderful teamwork!”

Benny stretched out on the clean rug. It was smooth and comfy. “I learned something,” he said.

“What did you learn?” Mama asked.

Benny counted on his paws. “One: Cooperation makes jobs quick and fun. Two: Following directions keeps everyone safe. Three: When I feel frustrated, I can breathe and try again. Four: It’s kind to let friends like different things.”

Tilly tapped her shell happily. “I learned those too.”

Benny grinned. “Team Beaver-Turtle is the best!”

They giggled and built another small tower, side by side, in the cozy bedroom that felt bright, tidy, and full of friendly, helpful sounds.

The End.
