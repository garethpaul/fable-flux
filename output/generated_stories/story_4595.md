# Ollie's Big Sports Day

Ollie the otter loved to play! He had soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes that sparkled when he was excited. Today, Ollie was very excited because he wanted to practice sports in Grandpa's old garden shed.

"I want to be the best at every sport!" Ollie said, clapping his little paws together. The shed smelled like old wood and fresh grass from outside. Sunlight peeked through the dusty windows, making everything glow.

Ollie found a red ball and tried to bounce it like basketball players do. *Bounce, bounce, CRASH!* The ball knocked over a flower pot.

"Oops!" said Ollie. He tried to kick the ball like soccer players do. *THUMP!* It hit a rake, which fell down with a loud *CLANG!*

Then Ollie tried to throw the ball like baseball players do. *WHOOSH!* It flew right into a spider web in the corner.

"Oh no!" cried Ollie, his whiskers drooping. "I'm not good at any sports! This is too hard!"

Just then, he heard a gentle voice. "Hello there, little one."

Ollie turned around and saw Mrs. Robin perched on the windowsill. Her red breast was bright and cheerful.

"I'm trying to play sports, but I keep making messes," Ollie said sadly. "I want to be good at everything right away!"

Mrs. Robin tilted her head thoughtfully. "You know, Ollie, I wasn't always good at flying. When I was little, I had to practice every day. Would you like to learn something special about sports?"

"Yes, please!" said Ollie, his eyes getting bright again.

"Sports aren't just about being the best," Mrs. Robin explained gently. "They're about having fun, learning new things, and being kind to others. Let me show you something wonderful."

Mrs. Robin flew down and picked up three small stones with her beak. "Watch this!" She dropped them into three different containers - *plink* in a tin can, *splash* in a water bowl, and *thud* in a wooden box.

"That sounds like music!" giggled Ollie.

"Exactly!" chirped Mrs. Robin. "You can create your own sport! Use your imagination and make something new."

Ollie's eyes grew wide with wonder. He looked around the shed and saw old tennis rackets, colorful scarves, and empty boxes. "I have an idea!"

Ollie set up the boxes in a line. He took the soft scarves and made them into gentle targets. Then he found some small, soft balls that wouldn't break anything.

"I'll call it 'Gentle Toss!'" announced Ollie proudly. "The goal is to land the soft balls in the boxes, but if I miss, that's okay too!"

He practiced his new sport, tossing the balls gently. Sometimes they went in the boxes - *plop!* Sometimes they didn't, and that was perfectly fine.

"This is fun!" laughed Ollie. "And look - nothing is broken!"

Mrs. Robin clapped her wings. "You've learned something very important, Ollie. Sports are about trying your best, being creative, and having fun. You don't have to be perfect right away."

From that day on, Ollie played his Gentle Toss game every day. He even taught it to his friends when they visited. They all learned that the best part about sports wasn't winning - it was playing together, being kind, and using their imaginations.

"Thank you for teaching me about peaceful sports," Ollie told Mrs. Robin. "Now I know that being creative and kind is just as important as being strong!"

The End.
