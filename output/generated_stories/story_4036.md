# Otter Oliver’s Colorful Chorus

Otter Oliver splashed his paws in a bowl of cool, blue paint. “Sploosh!” he giggled. The creative art room smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Sunny light danced on shiny scissors. Brushes stood in neat cups, like little trees. Music hummed softly from a small speaker, a bouncy tune that made Oliver’s whiskers wiggle.

“Today, I will make a big, bright poster!” Oliver said. “I will paint and sing!” He tapped his tail to the beat. Tap-tap-tap.

Friends arrived with happy steps. Tia the turtle rolled in her glitter glue. Ravi the rabbit carried soft pom-poms. Mia the mouse brought tiny bells that chimed, ting-ting! Each friend smiled in a different way. Each friend liked different colors and sounds.

Oliver felt so excited. He saw the biggest paintbrush, smooth and sturdy, perfect for big swirls. He saw the longest sheet of paper, snowy and clean. He scooped them up fast. “Mine!” he cheered. “I’m first!”

Ravi blinked. “Can I try the big brush after you?” he asked.

“Maybe later,” Oliver said, swishing blue in wide waves. “I need it now.”

Tia held up her glitter glue. “We can make sparkly stars,” she said.

“Not yet,” Oliver answered. “I have a plan.”

Mia’s bells jingled gently. “We can add music,” she said. “We can paint to the beat!”

Oliver shook his head. “I want quiet so I can think,” he said, even though he was tapping.

The room got very still. The song played softly, but the friends did not dance. Ravi stood near a small brush. Tia held her glitter. Mia kept her bells in her lap. Oliver painted big swirls and big dots all by himself. Swish, swash, splat.

Then Oliver reached for yellow paint. The jar was stuck. He tugged. Tug-tug. The lid popped off too fast. “Oh!” A drop landed on his nose. Another drop splashed on the floor. Plop! He slipped a little and smeared his blue swirl. “Oh no,” he whispered.

He tried to wipe it with a paper towel. The towel ripped. The big brush fell with a thud. Oliver’s tail drooped. His poster was messy, and his heart felt messy too. He looked at his friends. Ravi’s ears drooped. Tia’s shell sparkled quietly. Mia’s bells were silent.

Oliver felt a tight, twisty feeling in his tummy. “I didn’t share,” he said softly. “I took the big brush. I kept the long paper. I said no to your music and your glitter.” He looked at his blue paws. “I made a mess by myself.”

Tia rolled closer. “It’s okay to make a mess,” she said. “We can clean it.”

Ravi nodded. “We can help.”

Mia gave a tiny smile. “We can make it fun.”

Oliver took a deep breath. He listened. The song on the speaker picked up. Clap, clap, clap. He heard Mia’s bells, ting-ting, in his mind. He saw Tia’s glitter shining. He saw Ravi’s pom-poms, soft like clouds. He saw his friends’ different ideas, like many colors in one box.

“I am sorry,” Oliver said. “I did not share. Can we do this together? Can we use a plan? We can put tools in order. We can take turns.”

Ravi’s ears perked up. “Yes! Let’s make stations.”

“Stations?” Oliver asked.

Ravi smiled. “A brush station. A glue station. A music station. We can go one by one.”

Tia clapped slowly. “And we can keep the lids on when we are not using them,” she said. “That keeps things tidy.”

Mia rang her bells softly. “And I can lead the beat. We can paint to the music!”

Oliver’s whiskers twitched. “I love that,” he said. “Let’s do it.”

Together, they made a plan. Oliver lined up the paint jars by color: red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple. He wiped the lids and twisted them on snug. Click, click. Tia set out paper towels and a small trash bin. Ravi sorted brushes: big brushes in one cup, small brushes in another. Mia put the bells, a shaker, and a little drum on a low table.

“Welcome to our art and music stations!” Oliver announced. His voice felt warm. His heart felt light.

Mia rang the bells. Ting-ting-ting. “Brush station, start!”

Oliver used the big brush for two swirls. “One, two!” he counted. Then he passed the big brush to Ravi. “Your turn.”

Ravi painted soft circles and smiled. He passed it to Tia. “Your turn!” Tia made little star shapes with a small brush and then added a tiny dab of glitter glue. “Sparkle, sparkle,” she whispered.

At the music station, Mia tapped the drum, thum-thum, like a heartbeat. The beat helped everyone move in time. Paint, pass. Glue, pass. Bell, ring. “We are a team,” Mia sang.

Oliver tried the shaker. Chika-chika! He laughed. “Your bells sound like tiny raindrops,” he told Mia.

Mia beamed. “Your shaker sounds like crunchy leaves,” she said.

Tia smiled at Ravi’s pom-poms. “They look like soft snowballs,” she said.

They celebrated each other’s ideas. Oliver loved how different they were. The poster became a colorful chorus: blue swirls, glitter stars, gentle circles, and pom-pom puffs. The neat stations kept the room clean. The lids stayed on when not in use. The big brush was shared. No more spills. No more droops.

When they finished, the music ended with a happy ding. They stepped back together. The poster shone in the sun. It looked like a party on paper.

Oliver felt proud and calm. “Thank you for sharing with me,” he said. “Sharing makes more fun. Sharing lets us use everyone’s ideas. And being organized helped us all take turns.”

Ravi nodded. “We solved the problem together.”

Tia patted the trash bin. “And we kept the room tidy.”

Mia rang one last bell. Ting! “Different sounds and different styles make the best song,” she said.

Oliver hugged his friends with painty paws. “We share. We celebrate our differences. We stay organized. That is our art room song!”

They all sang, “Share and care, one by one, we make art and we have fun!”

The End.
