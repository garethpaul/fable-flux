# Della the Dolphin Starts the Art

Della was a playful dolphin with bright eyes and a big, happy smile. One rainy day, she swam through a wide, blue door into the creative art room. The room smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Colorful brushes stood in cups. Paints glowed like little rainbows. Soft music hummed, “La-la-la,” from a tiny speaker.

Friends were there too. Timo the turtle peeked from his shell. Bella the bunny wiggled her nose. Sami the seal clapped his flippers. But everyone stood still.

“Hi, friends!” Della chirped, flicking water drops like tiny stars. “Let’s make art!”

Timo looked shy. “I don’t know what to paint.”

Bella whispered, “There are so many colors.”

Sami said softly, “What do we do first?”

Della felt her heart bounce like a happy bubble. She loved to play. She loved to help. She took a slow, calm breath in and out, just like the soft music. In… and out… The room felt peaceful.

“I can start,” Della said with a grin. “I’ll take initiative! That means I begin, and I help, and I invite.”

She swam to a big table. The paper was smooth under her fin. “Step one,” Della said, “we set up.” Her fin tapped the brushes. Tap-tap. She lined them up: big brush, medium brush, tiny brush. She set out cups of water. Plip-plop. She put red, yellow, blue, and green paint in small trays. “Now it is easy to begin!”

Bella’s ears perked up. “You made it ready. I like that,” she said.

Della smiled. “Step two, we think. What helps us feel calm? Let’s close our eyes. Let’s listen.” The music sang softly. The rain pitter-pattered on the window. Everyone breathed in and out together.

Timo peeked one eye open. “I feel less wiggly,” he said.

“Me too,” said Sami. “Splash-sigh!”

Della clapped her fins gently. “Step three, we share. I will share the brushes and the colors.” She pushed the trays toward her friends. “Here you go. Everyone can use them.”

Timo touched the tiny brush. “Can I try the yellow?”

“Yes,” said Della. “Yellow for you, and red for Bella, and blue for Sami, and green for me. We can switch later.”

Bella’s nose twitched. “Thank you for sharing.”

Della dipped her brush in green. Swish! It felt cool and smooth. She made a big circle. “This is a sea circle,” she giggled.

Sami dipped his brush in blue. “I can make ocean dots,” he said. Dot-dot-dot. The paper went blip-blip with happy dots.

But then a small problem splashed into the room. A new friend stood by the door. It was Nia the narwhal. She looked at the floor. She held her tail close. “Hi,” she whispered. “May I paint too?”

Timo moved behind his cup. Bella looked at the full table. “We don’t have space,” she said softly.

Sami bit his lip. “There is only one tiny brush,” he added.

Della felt her tummy twist. A worry wave came. She took a slow breath. In… and out… She listened to the rain. She listened to the music. “I can take initiative,” she told herself. “I can make a plan.”

Della looked around the room. She saw a clean, small table in the corner. It had a white cloth and a single cup. “I have an idea!” she said. “We can make more space.”

She swam to the corner table and pushed it gently with her nose. Scoot, scoot. “Sami, can you help me slide it closer?”

“Yes!” Sami clapped and scooted the table. The legs made a soft shh-shh on the floor.

“Timo,” Della asked, “can you bring two papers?”

“I can,” Timo said. He carried the papers carefully on his shell.

“Bella,” Della smiled, “can you share your tiny brush first, for two minutes? Then we switch.”

Bella took a breath and nodded. “I can share.”

Della set out another cup of water. Plip. She poured a little paint into a clean tray. Plop-plop. “Nia,” Della said, “this spot is for you. We’re happy you’re here.”

Nia’s eyes brightened. “Thank you,” she whispered. Her voice grew brave. “I like purple.”

Della grinned. “We can make purple by mixing red and blue.” She dipped her brush. Swish. Red met blue and turned to a deep, happy purple. “Ta-da!”

Nia smiled. “It’s beautiful.”

Everyone began to paint together. They talked and laughed.

Timo said, “I am painting a sunny turtle shell.”

Bella said, “I am painting a rainbow carrot.”

Sami said, “I am painting a splashy wave.”

Nia whispered, “I am painting a purple star.”

Della painted green bubbles. Swirl, swirl, pop! The room was full of color and soft sounds and smiles.

Soon, Bella handed the tiny brush to Nia. “Your turn,” she said.

“Thank you,” Nia replied. “I will share it back.”

When the paintings were done, Della looked at them all. Her heart felt warm. “We did it,” she said. “We started. We shared. We made space for everyone.”

The teacher dolphin peeked in and clapped. “What beautiful art!” she said. “And what beautiful kindness.”

Della beamed. “Taking initiative helped us all.”

They hung the paintings on the wall. The colors shined like candy lights. The rain slowed. The music hummed soft and sweet.

Della turned to her friends. “What did we learn today?”

Timo said, “We can start when others feel unsure.”

Bella said, “We can share what we have.”

Sami said, “We can make room for everyone.”

Nia said, “We can breathe, and then we can be brave.”

Della nodded. “Yes. When we take initiative with kind hearts, we help ourselves and we help our friends.”

The End.
