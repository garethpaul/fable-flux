# Gigi the Giraffe Builds a Helper

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with big kind eyes and a long, spotty neck. One sunny morning, she stepped into Granny’s garage workshop. It smelled like fresh wood and lemon soap. Sunbeams shined on shiny tools. The floor felt cool under Gigi’s hooves, and tiny dust sparkles danced in the light.

Gigi took a deep breath. “Today, I want to do something by myself,” she said softly. “I want to make a Snack Snatcher to reach my crunchy leaves on the high shelf.”

The shelf was tall, even for a giraffe. Gigi had always asked Granny for help. Granny smiled and patted Gigi’s shoulder. “You can do it, dear. I’ll be nearby if you need me. What is your goal?”

Gigi lifted her head. “My goal is to build a simple tool. It will be safe and strong. I will plan, try, and finish.”

Granny nodded. “That is a smart plan.”

Gigi looked around. She heard the quiet hum of a small fan. She smelled sweet pine wood. She saw wood blocks, rubber bands, soft sandpaper, and a basket of bright screws—blue, red, and yellow. There was a gentle saw, a tiny drill with a funny whir, and safety goggles that made Gigi giggle when she tried them on.

She made a list on a big paper. “Step one: draw. Step two: choose pieces. Step three: build. Step four: test.” She pressed her hoof on each step and smiled.

Gigi drew a picture with a crayon. “A long handle with a little grabber,” she said. “Squeeze the handle, and the grabber closes.” She held her drawing up to the light. “This is my brave plan.”

She found two wooden sticks, a small hinge, and soft rubber pads. She felt nervous. Her tummy fluttered like tiny butterflies. “I can try,” she whispered. “I can be brave.”

Granny walked by and gave a thumbs-up. “You’re doing great, Gigi.”

Gigi put on her goggles. “Safety first,” she said. The goggles felt snug, and she liked the clicky sound they made when she adjusted the strap. She picked up the small screwdriver. It felt cool and smooth in her hoof.

She lined up the hinge. “Screw, screw, screw,” she sang softly as she turned the screwdriver. The screw squeaked a little. “Eee-eee.” Gigi giggled. “Hello, little squeak.”

She added the rubber bands to make the grabber close. Snap! The band stretched and settled. She tested the handle. The grabber opened and closed. Click, click. “It works,” she cheered.

Gigi walked to the tall shelf. The crunchy leaves rustled in their basket. They smelled green and fresh. She lifted her Snack Snatcher. Her knees wobbled. The handle felt heavier than before. “What if I drop it?” she worried.

She took a slow breath. In and out. “I can do hard things by myself,” she reminded herself. “I made a plan. I can be brave.”

She reached up. The grabber touched the basket. The rubber pads squeaked on the wicker. She squeezed. The grabber slipped. The basket wobbled and settled back. Thump.

Gigi felt her eyes get wet. “I didn’t do it.” Her voice was small.

Granny stepped closer. “How does your plan help now?”

Gigi sniffed and looked at her list. “I can try again,” she said. “I can fix one small thing.”

She checked the grabber. The rubber pads felt a little slippery. She found a strip of soft felt and wrapped it around the pads. “Now they are grippy,” she said.

She stood tall and lifted again. “Slow and steady,” she whispered. She aligned the grabber and squeezed gently. Click. The pads hugged the basket. She pulled down slowly. The basket slid off the shelf and rested in her hooves. Rustle, rustle.

“I did it!” Gigi laughed. Her spots seemed to sparkle. She took a crunchy leaf. Munch, munch. It tasted cool and sweet.

Granny clapped. “You set a goal. You made a plan. You tried, fixed, and tried again. You were brave and independent.”

Gigi hugged her Snack Snatcher. The wood felt warm from her touch. “I did it by myself,” she said proudly. “I asked for help only when I needed it. I can make a plan next time, too.”

She hung her tool on a peg. The peg clicked as it held the handle. Gigi drew a gold star next to “Step four: test.” She added one more line: “Step five: share.” She turned to Granny. “I can teach my friends to make tools, too.”

Granny smiled. “That is a wonderful idea.”

Gigi looked around the bright garage workshop. She felt brave, calm, and strong. “When I set a goal, make a plan, and keep trying, I can do big things,” she said. “Being independent feels good.”

The End.
