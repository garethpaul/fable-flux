# Splash the Dolphin's Laundry Day Adventure

Splash the dolphin loved to play and swim all day long. But today was different. Today, Mama Dolphin had asked him to help with the laundry in their special underwater laundry room.

"Splash, can you please help me sort the clothes?" Mama asked, pointing to a big pile of colorful sea-clothes floating in bubbles.

"Sure, Mama!" Splash said excitedly. He loved helping, but he had never done laundry before. The room smelled like fresh ocean breeze and soap bubbles tickled his nose.

Splash dove right in without thinking. He grabbed armfuls of clothes and started stuffing them into the washing machine. Red shirts went in with white shells. Dirty socks mixed with clean towels. Soon, the laundry room was a mess!

"Oh my!" said Mama Dolphin, swimming over quickly. "Splash, we need to stop and make a plan first."

Splash's tail drooped. "I was just trying to help fast, Mama."

"I know, sweetie," Mama said gently. "But when we don't plan, we can make bigger messes. Let's think together. What should we do first?"

Splash thought hard, his little dolphin brain working. "Um... maybe we should look at all the clothes first?"

"Great idea!" Mama smiled. "Planning means thinking before we act. What do you notice about these clothes?"

Splash looked carefully. The clothes were all different colors - some were bright red, others were white as sea foam, and some were very dirty with sand.

"They're different colors!" Splash said proudly.

"Exactly! And we need to wash similar things together. Can you help me sort them into groups?"

Splash nodded eagerly. This time, he swam slowly and looked at each piece. "This red shirt goes with the other red things," he said, placing it carefully in one pile. "And this white shell goes with the white pile."

"You're doing wonderfully!" Mama encouraged. "Now, what about these very dirty, sandy clothes?"

"They need their own pile because they're extra messy?" Splash guessed.

"Perfect! You're such a good planner now."

As they worked together, Splash felt proud. Planning made everything easier! The sorting was fun when he took his time.

"Mama," Splash said as they loaded the first group into the washing machine, "I'm sorry I made a mess before. I should have asked for help."

"It's okay to ask questions, Splash," Mama said, giving him a gentle hug with her fin. "Everyone needs to learn, and it's perfectly fine to say 'I don't know how' or 'Can you teach me?' That's called knowing your boundaries."

Splash watched the clothes swirl around in the bubbly water. "So planning means thinking first, and boundaries means knowing when to ask for help?"

"Exactly right, my smart little dolphin!"

The washing machine hummed a happy tune, and soon their clothes came out clean and bright. Splash had learned that taking time to plan made everything work better.

"Next time I'll remember to think first, then act," Splash said proudly.

"And I'll always be here to help you learn," Mama replied.

From that day on, whenever Splash faced a new task, he remembered to stop, think, plan, and ask for help when he needed it. And his laundry day adventures were always successful!

The End.
