# Bubbles in the Treehouse Band

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to make music. Splash-splash, clap-clap, click-click went Bubbles’ sounds. One bright morning, Bubbles wiggled her shiny fins and bounced on a soft, wet leaf pad at the base of a tall tree. At the top was a cozy treehouse. It smelled like pine and sweet apples. A little ladder dangled down. Bubbles giggled. “I want to play music in that treehouse!”

Bubbles took a deep breath. Swish! With a gentle whoosh of magic, a rainbow bubble lifted her up, up, up. Pop! The bubble set her softly on the wooden floor. The boards felt warm under her belly. Sunlight made gold stripes on the walls.

Inside the treehouse were three friends. Mimi the mouse tapped a tiny drum. Tom-Tom the turtle strummed a small guitar. Kia the kitten rang a bell that went ding-ling-ling. They were a band. They smiled at Bubbles.

“Hi! I’m Bubbles,” said the dolphin, clicking happily.

“Welcome to our treehouse band,” said Mimi. “We make music and share.”

“I love to play,” said Bubbles. She saw a shiny pan. She saw a bright pot lid. She saw a basket of shakers. Her tail wiggled with joy. She made a big splashy click and zoomed around. Thump! Her fin bumped the pan. CLANG!

Kia winced. “That was loud.”

Bubbles didn’t notice. “Listen to my song!” she shouted. “Clack-clack-clap! Splash-click-CLANG!”

Tom-Tom pulled his head back a little. “Um… we play together,” he said softly. “We take turns.”

But Bubbles felt so excited. She spun and made loud sounds. CLANG! CLANG! CLANG! The treehouse shook. Pinecones outside rattled. Mimi dropped her drumstick. Kia’s ears went flat.

“Stop, please,” said Mimi, covering her tiny ears. “It hurts.”

Bubbles froze. She saw Mimi’s sad eyes. She saw Kia’s drooped whiskers. She saw Tom-Tom’s worried face. The air felt heavy. The treehouse did not feel warm now. It felt tight and still.

Bubbles’ heart thumped. She hadn’t wanted to make her friends sad. She had only wanted to play. Her cheeks felt hot. Her tail stopped wiggling.

“I didn’t mean to hurt you,” Bubbles whispered.

Mimi took a small breath. “We know,” she said. “But our ears are tender. We like soft sounds first.”

Bubbles looked down at the floor. She saw a tiny crack where the pan had bumped. She saw her fast fin. She listened to her own loud click in her head. She thought about how the sounds felt to her friends.

Bubbles took another breath. She remembered something: When we make a mistake, we can say sorry. A real, gentle sorry.

She scooted close, fins low. “I am sorry,” Bubbles said, slow and clear. “I got too loud. I did not listen. Are you okay?”

Kia’s ears lifted a little. “Thank you for saying sorry,” said Kia. “My ears still feel buzzy.”

Bubbles nodded. “What can I do to help?” she asked. Her voice was soft, like a tiny wave on the sand.

Tom-Tom peeked out. “Maybe we can have quiet time,” he said. “Then we can try again. We can make a plan.”

“I like plans,” Mimi said. She put her drum down and took a sip of apple water. “We can use a turn stick. When you hold the stick, you play. When you don’t, you listen.”

Bubbles felt a warm bubble of hope. “I can do that,” she said. “And I can start with soft sounds.”

They sat together. The treehouse was calm. The wind whispered through the leaves: shhh, shhh. A bird sang a tiny tweet. Bubbles closed her eyes and listened. She felt how quiet could be music too. She felt her friends relax.

“Ready?” asked Mimi, smiling again. She handed Bubbles a smooth, shiny stick.

Bubbles held the stick gently. She picked up a small shaker. Shake-shake. Very soft. Like rain on a leaf. Kia smiled and rang her bell, soft too: ding… ding. Tom-Tom plucked his guitar: plink… plonk. Mimi tapped: tip… tap.

They sounded like a gentle morning. The treehouse felt warm again. Sunlight tickled Bubbles’ back.

“I like this,” whispered Bubbles.

When it was Tom-Tom’s turn, Bubbles listened. She kept her fins still. She watched his happy eyes. She felt proud. She was part of the band. When her turn came again, she wanted to make a splash. She remembered her promise. She made one big sound—CLANG!—then looked up.

Mimi giggled. “Maybe one big sound at the end,” she said. “That can be our special finish.”

“Okay,” said Bubbles. “I will save my big splash for the end.”

They played their song again. Soft-shake, soft-ding, soft-plink, soft-tap. At the very end, Bubbles lifted the pan lid high. She looked at her friends. “Ready?” she asked.

“Ready!” everyone cheered.

CLANG! The sound rang like a shiny bell and then faded. The treehouse hummed with happy feelings. The friends clapped and laughed. Bubbles’ heart felt light and bubbly.

“Thank you for saying sorry,” Mimi said. “That helped us feel safe.”

“Thank you for telling me what you needed,” Bubbles said. “I will listen and play with care.”

Kia nuzzled Bubbles’ fin. “That is empathy,” she purred. “You felt how we felt.”

Bubbles smiled. “Empathy helps our music,” she said. “And our friendship.”

From then on, the treehouse band had a new rule: Soft first, then strong together. They took turns. They listened. They said sorry when they made mistakes, and they asked how to make it better. Their music felt like rain, sunshine, and one happy splash at the end.

The lesson they learned was clear: When we make a mistake, we can say a true sorry, listen with care, and try again. That is how hearts—and songs—feel good.

The End.
