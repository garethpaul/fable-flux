# Roo Leads the Balcony Garden

Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and a bouncy tail. Roo lived with Mama on the fourth floor, where there was a sunny balcony garden. The garden smelled like mint and sweet strawberries. Little pots lined the rail, and tiny bells tinkled in the warm breeze. Bees hummed. A watering can sat by the door, shiny and blue.

“Good morning, garden!” Roo chirped, nose twitching. “Today we water, feed, and pick!”

Mama smiled. “That sounds like a plan, Roo. You can be the leader today.”

Roo’s heart felt fizzy and warm. “I’m the leader!” Roo clapped tiny paws. “I will help, I will guide, and we will be safe.”

Friends arrived for garden time. Tilly the tiny turtle slid across the mat. Pip the parakeet fluttered to the rail. Squeak the mouse peeked from behind a pot.

“Hi, Roo!” Pip sang.

“Hello!” Tilly whispered, her shell shiny like a little moon.

“Hi, hi!” squeaked Squeak, whiskers wiggling.

Roo stood tall. “Welcome, team! I am Leader Roo today. We will take turns. We will stay safe. We will have fun!”

“What do we do first?” asked Tilly.

Roo sniffed the air. The soil smelled dry, like toast. “Step one,” Roo said, pointing to the watering can. “Water. But we must be safe. We walk, we don’t run. We keep paws and feet away from the edge.”

Mama nodded. “Good leadership, Roo. Remember, leaders use kind words and listening ears.”

Roo tapped Roo’s ear. “Listening ears on!” Roo giggled. Everyone smiled and touched their ears, even Pip with his feathers.

“Who goes first?” asked Squeak.

Roo thought. The leader’s tummy fluttered. “We will take turns. Tilly first, then Squeak, then Pip, then me.” Roo lined up the friends and drew a chalk line on the floor away from the edge. “We wait behind the line.”

Tilly lifted the blue can with Roo’s help. “It’s heavy,” she said.

“We can share the job,” Roo said. “Leaders share.” Together, they tilted the can. Trick, trick, trickle went the water. The dirt turned dark and smelled fresh.

“My turn!” Squeak squealed.

Roo held up a paw. “Squeak, please wait by the line. Your turn is next.” Roo smiled gently. “Thank you for waiting.”

Squeak took a deep breath. “Okay,” he said, bouncing softly in place.

When it was Squeak’s turn, Roo guided him. “Slow and low,” Roo said. “Water the roots. Keep paws safe.” Squeak poured with care. Pip cheered, “Tweet-tweet! Nice pouring!”

Now Pip’s turn. Pip dipped his beak into the can and flicked tiny drops like rain kisses. “Sprinkle-sprinkle!”

Roo laughed. “That’s perfect for the mint. It likes a gentle rain.”

At last, Roo watered the strawberries. Red berries peeked under green leaves. They smelled sweet, like warm jam.

“Step two,” said Roo. “We feed the plants.” Roo held a small scoop. “Only one scoop each.” Roo handed the scoop to Tilly. “Tilly, you start.”

Tilly nodded and sprinkled food. Squeak watched closely. His tail twitched.

“I want two scoops,” Squeak said.

Roo took a calm breath. “Leaders are fair,” Roo said softly. “We all get one scoop so everyone has a turn. You can help me count, Squeak.”

Squeak beamed. “One!” he counted as each friend fed one scoop. “Two for Roo! I mean… one for Roo!” He giggled.

“Step three,” Roo said. “We pick the ripe berries. But we must be safe. We keep our tummies away from the rail. We hold the pot with one hand, and we pick with gentle fingers.”

Pip fluffed his feathers. “I can watch from the rail, but I won’t lean,” he chirped.

Roo nodded. “Good safety, Pip.”

They picked together. Pop! Pop! Pop! The berries dropped into a little bowl with soft thumps. The sun warmed their shoulders. A breeze smelled like basil and rain.

Suddenly, Squeak reached for a berry that was very close to the edge. His toes inched forward.

Roo stepped in front, calm and firm. “Stop, Squeak. Safety first,” Roo said, holding out a paw. “Please step back to the chalk line.”

Squeak froze. His eyes grew wide. “Oh,” he said. “I forgot.”

“It’s okay,” Roo said kindly. “Leaders remind friends. Let’s pick that berry together from the inside.” Roo moved the pot so the berry came closer. “See? Safe and easy.”

Squeak smiled with relief. “Thank you, Leader Roo.”

When the bowl was full, Mama brought tiny cups. “Berry-mint tea for my helpers,” she said.

Roo raised a cup. “Team, we did it! We took turns, we stayed safe, and we helped the garden grow.”

“How did it feel to be leader?” asked Mama.

Roo thought. The tea was warm and sweet. The mint tingled on Roo’s tongue. “It felt big,” Roo said. “I used kind words. I listened. I made a plan. I kept us safe. And I shared jobs.”

Pip chirped, “You were a fair leader!”

Tilly nodded. “And a gentle one.”

Squeak hugged Roo’s leg. “And you helped me remember.”

Roo’s tail thumped with joy. “Thank you, team. Next time, someone else can lead, and I will take turns.”

Mama smiled. “That’s true leadership—guiding with care, keeping everyone safe, and making room for others to lead, too.”

They sat in the warm sun, sipping tea and nibbling sweet berries. The bells tinkled. Bees hummed. The plants looked happy and tall.

Roo looked at the garden and whispered, “I can lead with kindness.” Roo felt brave, fair, and full of bounce.

The End.
