# Fawn’s Puzzle in the Playful Living Room

Tap, tap, tap went little hooves on the soft rug. A gentle deer fawn named Fawn stood in a bright, playful living room. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Pillows were puffy and colorful. Wooden blocks sat in a basket. A puzzle with big pieces sparkled on the coffee table. Fawn’s ears perked up. “Today feels like a thinking day,” Fawn whispered, feeling brave and curious.

Mama’s voice floated from the kitchen, gentle and warm like cocoa. “Have fun, little one. Use your thinking ears and your calm heart.”

Fawn nodded. “Thinking ears. Calm heart.” The words felt like a hug.

Fawn climbed onto a soft beanbag. The puzzle on the table showed a bright house, a tree, a kitty, and a smiling sun. But some pieces were mixed up. The sun piece was near the rug. The kitty tail was under a pillow. The tree top was upside down!

Fawn took a deep breath. Sniff. The air smelled like oranges and clean soap. “I can do this,” Fawn said softly. “I will look. I will listen. I will try.”

First, Fawn looked with careful eyes. “What do I see?” Fawn asked out loud. “I see colors. I see shapes.” Fawn touched a round, yellow piece. It felt smooth and warm from the sun. “Round and yellow. That could be the sun.”

Fawn tried to fit it. Click. It did not fit. Fawn did not frown. “Hmm,” Fawn hummed. “Let’s try a new way.”

Fawn made a plan: sort first, then try. “Sort by color!” Fawn said. “Yellow pieces here. Green pieces there. Kitty pieces with stripes over here.” Fawn’s little hooves pat-patted on the table as pieces moved into little groups.

Suddenly, a tiny squeak came from under the couch. Fawn’s ears twitched. “Hello?” Fawn bent down and sniffed. Dust smelled tickly. A toy mouse peeped out. “Squeak!”

“Hi, Mouse,” Fawn said kindly. “I’m solving a puzzle. Do you want to watch?”

“Squeak!” said Mouse, eyes round and shiny.

Fawn smiled. “I will use my calm heart. I will use my smart brain.” The words felt good and steady.

Next, Fawn asked a question. “What goes first?” Fawn looked at the picture on the box. “Edges go first. They are flat on one side.” Fawn found flat pieces and lined them up. Tap, tap, tap went the pieces. They made a soft, friendly sound. The frame began to show.

Mouse wiggled whiskers. “Squeak-squeak?”

“You are right, Mouse,” Fawn said gently. “We can try one piece at a time.”

Fawn picked up the round, yellow piece again. “Where do you live?” Fawn asked the piece. Fawn looked at the top of the puzzle. “The sun goes in the sky.” Fawn tried the top corner. Click. It fit! Fawn’s heart felt warm and proud.

“I did it!” Fawn said. “I used my eyes, and I used a plan.”

Fawn kept going. The kitty’s striped tail needed a body. Fawn looked under the pillow. Swish. “Here you are,” Fawn giggled, finding the kitty face. “You look happy.” Fawn tried to push the tail into the body. It did not fit. Fawn paused. “No push,” Fawn said calmly. “I will turn it. I will look again.” Turn, turn. Click. It fit!

Mouse clapped tiny paws. “Squeak!”

A little problem popped up. Two pieces looked the same: both green and leafy. Fawn felt a tiny worry in the tummy. “Which one?” Fawn asked. Fawn took a slow breath. In… and out… like waves. “I can think. I can try.”

Fawn looked closely. “This one has a small bird. This one has a big leaf.” Fawn checked the box picture. “The bird sits near the top.” Fawn tried the bird piece up high. Click. Perfect.

Fawn smiled bigger. “Thinking helps. Questions help. Looking close helps.”

Then a new problem came. The last piece was missing! Fawn looked in the basket. Rustle, rustle. No piece. Fawn looked under the rug. Flop. No piece. Fawn looked at Mouse. Mouse shrugged. “Squeak?”

Fawn felt a sad droop in the ears. But Fawn remembered: calm heart. smart brain. “I will make a plan,” Fawn said. “Step one: look where I played. Step two: listen for a sound. Step three: ask for help if I need it.”

Fawn listened. The living room hummed softly. Somewhere, something went clink. Fawn followed the sound to the curtain. A tiny sparkle peeped out from the folds. “There you are!” Fawn chirped. The last piece was hiding like a shy star.

Fawn placed it in the puzzle. Click! The big picture was complete: house, tree, kitty, sun. Everything smiled back.

Mama walked in, wiping her hands. “I heard happy clicks,” she said. “What a beautiful puzzle!”

Fawn’s chest felt tall and light. “I used my thinking ears and my calm heart,” Fawn said. “I made a plan. I looked close. I asked gentle questions. I tried again. I did not push. I solved it peacefully.”

Mama stroked Fawn’s soft head. “That is wonderful,” she said. “You used critical thinking. You stayed calm and kind. That builds strong, brave feelings inside.”

Mouse squeaked a happy squeak. Fawn giggled and hugged a pillow. The room smelled like oranges and sunshine. The puzzle picture glowed.

Fawn nodded. “When I have a problem, I can slow down, make a plan, look and listen, and try again. That helps me feel brave. That helps me help others too.”

Mama smiled. “Yes, little one. That is the smart, peaceful way.”

Fawn beamed. “I can think. I can be kind. I can do it.”

The End.
