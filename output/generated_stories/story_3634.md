# Hoot's Sparkly Soap Song

In a warm kitchen, a kettle hummed and the air smelled like cinnamon toast. A wise old owl named Hoot perched on the back of a wooden chair. Hoot’s feathers were soft and speckled, like tiny snowflakes. His big golden eyes blinked kindly at the sunny window.

Today, Hoot had helpers: Pip and Poppy, two little friends with jam on their cheeks and flour on their noses. “We’re making snacks!” cheered Pip, clapping his sticky hands.

“Snacks are fun,” Hoot hooted gently, “but clean hands are even better.”

Poppy scrunched her face. “My tummy is rumble-rumble. Washing takes too long!”

Hoot tilted his head. “Your tummy is important. So are your hands. Clean hands keep bellies happy.”

Pip wiggled. “But the sink makes my sleeves wet,” he said softly. “And the soap is slippery.”

Hoot listened. He felt their feelings. “I hear you,” he said. “Washing can feel tricky. Let’s solve this together.”

The kettle puffed a tiny whistle. The warm kitchen smelled like toast and lemons now. Sunlight made the faucet shine. Hoot hopped closer and tapped the counter with a claw. “We will make a plan,” he said. “A gentle plan. A musical plan!”

“Music?” Poppy’s eyes sparkled.

“Yes,” Hoot nodded. “A Soap Song to help us remember. Music can be a helper for our feelings.”

Hoot fluffed his feathers. “Step one: sleeves up!” he hooted. Pip and Poppy pushed up their sleeves. Swish, swish.

“Step two: save water,” Hoot said, turning the tap on low. “A little splash is enough. We don’t need a river.” The stream was soft and quiet, like a tiny whisper: shhhhh.

“Step three: wet hands,” Hoot said. Pip and Poppy put their hands under the gentle stream. Warm water tickled their fingers.

“Step four: turn water off while we scrub,” Hoot added, twisting the knob. “This is called saving water. It helps the trees, the fish, and the sky.”

Pip smiled. “We are helping the whole world?”

“Yes,” said Hoot. “Little helpers make a big world better.”

Hoot set a bright bar of soap in their hands. It smelled like lemon and mint. “Now we sing,” he said, smoothing his feathers.

Hoot began in a soft, friendly voice:
“Bubbles, bubbles, round we go,
Front and back and between, just so.
Thumbs go twisty, wrists go whoosh,
Scrub-a-dub, swish-swish-swoosh!”

Pip giggled and followed. Poppy danced a tiny dance on her toes. They rubbed fronts and backs, thumbs and wrists. The soap made happy squeaks. Bubbles sparkled like tiny moons.

Hoot hooted the next part:
“Count to twenty, nice and slow,
Clean hands help our bodies grow.
Turn the water on again,
Rinse the bubbles, one to ten.”

They counted together, slow and calm. “One… two… three…” The warm kitchen felt safe and cozy. The lemon-mint smell filled the air. Pip’s shoulders relaxed. Poppy’s scrunched face smoothed out.

“Now rinse,” Hoot said, turning the water on low again. The bubbles slipped away, plop, plop, plop. The water sang a little song as it ran. Hoot nodded. “Good saving. No splashing flood.”

Pip shook his hands gently. “My sleeves are dry!” he said with proud eyes.

Poppy wiggled her fingers. “My hands feel tingly and happy,” she laughed.

Hoot pointed to a small cloth towel hanging by the sink. “Step five: pat-pat dry,” he said. “This towel is for today. We will wash it later to keep it fresh.” Pip and Poppy patted their hands until they were warm and dry.

They all looked at the snack table. Apples shone red, carrots crunched orange, and toast was golden. “Now,” Hoot said, “clean hands can help make and eat healthy snacks.”

Pip sliced a soft banana with a safe, dull butter knife. Poppy spread yogurt with a tiny spoon. Hoot sprinkled a pinch of cinnamon. Crunch, munch, yum! The kitchen filled with happy chewing and a gentle kettle hum.

Pip wiped a tiny drip from the table with a cloth. “We can clean up too,” he said.

Poppy put peels in the green food scraps bin. “We can help the garden,” she added. “Compost is food for dirt!”

Hoot’s eyes shone. “That is smart sustainability,” he said. “We save water, we make less trash, we feed the soil. And we wash our hands to keep our bodies strong and our hearts calm.”

Poppy touched Hoot’s wing. “I felt worried,” she admitted. “Now I feel proud.”

Pip nodded. “I felt frustrated. The song helped me feel brave.”

Hoot smiled. “Feelings are like clouds. They float by. Our plan and our song help the sun come out.”

Before another snack, they sang the Soap Song again, just for fun. The bubbles danced. The water whispered. The towel went pat-pat. Everything felt fresh and bright.

Hoot blinked slowly. “What did we learn today?”

Pip stood tall. “Wash hands with our Soap Song!”

Poppy clapped. “Save water and clean up!”

Hoot hooted happily. “Yes. Clean hands keep us healthy. A small plan solves big worries. And caring for our world is a kind, musical thing to do.”

The warm kitchen glowed. The friends munched their snacks with clean, happy hands, and the kettle hummed like a cozy friend.

The End.
