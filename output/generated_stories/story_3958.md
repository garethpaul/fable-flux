# Panda Paints in the Garage

Poppy the panda was patient. Poppy liked to take slow breaths and think kind thoughts. One sunny morning, Poppy padded into Grandpa Bao’s garage workshop. The door rolled up with a rumbly rattle. Inside, the air smelled like wood shavings and lemon oil. Light made tiny dust sparkles dance.

“Good morning, Grandpa Bao,” Poppy said.

“Good morning, little brush,” Grandpa Bao said with a soft smile. He called Poppy “little brush” because Poppy loved to paint. “Today is busy. The neighborhood parade is tonight, and our old lion dance drum needs help.”

Poppy looked at the big round drum. The leather top was faded. The red paint on the sides was chipped. Gold swirls had lost their shine.

Poppy touched the drum gently. “Hello, drum,” Poppy whispered. “We will help you.”

Grandpa Bao nodded. “This drum has a story. My grandmother taught me to paint it when I was small. She said, ‘We share our traditions by caring for them and by helping others learn.’”

Poppy’s ears perked up. “Can I learn? Can I help?”

“Yes,” said Grandpa Bao. “We will fix and paint together. We will also help our friends make parade art.”

Poppy felt warm and happy inside. Helping made Poppy’s heart feel big.

Grandpa Bao opened a wooden box. Inside were smooth brushes, little cups, and safe, bright paints that smelled like oranges. Poppy wrinkled a nose and giggled. “It smells like a fruit party.”

“Careful and patient,” Grandpa Bao said. “First, we clean.” He gave Poppy a soft cloth. They wiped the drum gently. Swish, swish. Dust tickled Poppy’s paws.

Knock, knock. The garage door shook lightly. Miko the mouse peeked in with big eyes. “Hi! My kite is torn. Can you help?”

Poppy looked at Grandpa Bao. Grandpa Bao smiled. “Helping others comes first.”

Poppy nodded. “We will help, Miko.” Poppy took slow breaths. “We will use tape and care.”

Poppy held the kite while Grandpa Bao pressed tape along the rip. Press, press. Smooth, smooth. The tape made a little crinkle sound. Miko wiggled with joy. “Thank you! Now I can fly it in the parade!”

“You’re welcome,” Poppy said. “We helped together.”

When Miko left, Poppy and Grandpa Bao went back to the drum. “Tradition time,” Grandpa Bao said. He dipped a brush into red paint. Drip, drop. “Hold your brush like this. Paint slowly. Think about the music.”

Poppy copied Grandpa Bao. The brush felt soft. The paint felt cool on the drum. “Swirl… swirl…” Poppy whispered. Poppy made a careful curve. The red looked bright like a happy apple.

Soon, another friend came. Lina the lizard carried a banner. “Oh no. My banner is blank,” Lina said. “I want flowers on it. But I don’t know how.”

Poppy smiled. “We can help. Grandpa taught me swirls. I can show you dots and petals.”

Poppy dipped a brush in pink. Tap, tap. Dot, dot, dot. “Petals are dots that hold hands,” Poppy said. Lina tried. Dot, dot, dot. Petals bloomed. Poppy added green stems. Swish! The banner looked alive.

“Wow!” Lina clapped. “Thank you. Now I can share beauty in the parade.”

“You did it,” Poppy said. “We helped together.”

They all hummed a little song as they worked. The garage sounded like a tiny parade: hums, taps, swishes, and happy giggles.

When Lina skipped away, Poppy’s tummy made a small growl. Grandpa Bao chuckled. “Snack time helps artists.” He peeled a sweet orange. The peel made a zippy sound. The air smelled sunny and bright. They shared slices. Juice dripped down Poppy’s chin, and Poppy laughed.

Back to the drum they went. “Now gold,” Grandpa Bao said. “Gold is for joy. My grandmother taught me to paint gold swirls for luck.”

Poppy listened with big, careful eyes. “I will try. Slow and kind.”

Poppy dipped into gold paint. The brush tickled the drum. Swirl… swirl… swoop! One swirl got a little wiggly.

“It’s okay,” Grandpa Bao said softly. “Being patient means trying again.”

Poppy breathed in. Poppy breathed out. Poppy fixed the swirl with a tiny touch. “There,” Poppy said. “Helping the swirl.”

Soon, Benji the bunny hopped in. “My lion mask lost a whisker,” he said.

“We can help,” Poppy said. Poppy found soft string. Snip! Snip! With glue and gentle paws, they added whiskers. The glue smelled a little sweet. Benji wiggled his nose. “Perfect! Thank you.”

At last, the drum was bright red with shiny gold swirls. It looked proud and ready. Poppy patted it softly. “We helped you,” Poppy said.

Evening came. The garage door opened to cool air and twinkly lights. Outside, neighbors laughed and talked. Miko’s kite fluttered. Lina’s banner waved with pink flowers. Benji’s lion mask whiskers shone.

Grandpa Bao rolled out the drum. Boom, boom, boom! The sound was deep and happy. It thumped in Poppy’s chest like a kind heartbeat.

Grandpa Bao lifted Poppy up. “Little brush,” he said, “today you learned to paint, and you helped our friends. You shared our family tradition with gentle hands.”

Poppy smiled wide. “Helping feels good. Sharing art makes love grow.”

Everyone cheered. Poppy tapped the drum once more. Boom! The parade moved down the street with color, music, and joy. Poppy waved. “If someone needs help,” Poppy said, “I will listen. I will be patient. I will try.”

Grandpa Bao nodded. “That is the lesson. Helping others brings us together, and traditions live when we share them.”

Poppy’s heart felt big again, warm like a hug. The stars winked as if to say, “Yes.”

The End.
