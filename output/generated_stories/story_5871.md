# Benny Beaver's Garden Day

Benny Beaver woke up early on his balcony garden home. The morning sun felt warm on his brown fur. He stretched his little paws and looked around at all the plant pots.

"I want to do EVERYTHING today!" Benny squeaked excitedly. He saw the watering can, the seed packets, and the small shovel. His tail slapped the wooden deck with a loud thwack-thwack.

Benny grabbed the watering can first. Water splashed everywhere! Then he dropped it and picked up seeds. Seeds scattered across the deck like tiny raindrops. Then he grabbed the shovel and started digging holes everywhere.

"Dig, dig, dig! Plant, plant, plant! Water, water, water!" Benny chattered fast. But soon, his garden looked very messy. Water puddles mixed with dirt. Seeds were in the wrong places. Some plants looked droopy.

Benny sat down with a big sigh. His whiskers drooped. "Oh no," he said sadly. "My garden is all mixed up. I wanted to help it grow, but I made a mess instead."

Just then, Mrs. Robin landed on the balcony railing. Her red chest feathers gleamed in the sunlight. "Hello, Benny," she chirped sweetly. "You look upset. What happened?"

"I wanted to do everything at once," Benny explained. "But now my garden is messy and I feel all jumbled inside."

Mrs. Robin tilted her head thoughtfully. "When I feel jumbled, I take three deep breaths," she said. "Want to try?"

Benny nodded. Together, they breathed in slowly through their noses. "Smell the fresh morning air," Mrs. Robin said. They breathed out slowly through their mouths. "One... two... three."

Benny's shoulders relaxed. "I feel calmer now," he said softly.

"Good!" Mrs. Robin smiled. "Now, what if we make a plan? One job at a time?"

Benny's eyes lit up. "That's a great idea! First, I'll clean up the spilled water." He carefully wiped the puddles with a small towel. The deck felt smooth and dry again.

"Next, I'll put the seeds in the right pots," Benny said. He picked up each tiny seed gently. His paws moved slowly and carefully now. "Tomato seeds go here. Flower seeds go there."

Mrs. Robin helped by pointing with her wing. "You're doing wonderfully, Benny!"

Finally, Benny watered each plant just a little bit. The water made a gentle pitter-patter sound. The plants seemed to dance happily.

"Look!" Benny pointed to a neighbor's balcony. "Mr. Rabbit looks like he needs help with his garden too."

"Would you like to share what you learned?" Mrs. Robin asked.

Benny nodded eagerly. He called out, "Mr. Rabbit! Want to garden together? I learned how to take deep breaths and make plans!"

Mr. Rabbit hopped over with a big smile. "That sounds wonderful, Benny!"

The three friends spent the morning gardening together. They took turns with tools. They breathed deeply when they felt rushed. They made plans for each plant.

As the sun climbed higher, Benny looked at his beautiful, organized garden. His plants stood tall and happy. His heart felt calm and proud.

"I learned something important today," Benny said to his friends. "When I slow down and breathe, I can do things better. And sharing makes everything more fun!"

Mrs. Robin and Mr. Rabbit nodded. "You're a very wise little beaver," Mrs. Robin said.

Benny's tail gave a gentle, happy tap on the deck. His garden was perfect, and his heart felt peaceful.

The End.
