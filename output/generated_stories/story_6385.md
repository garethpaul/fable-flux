# Finn the Fox Kit Fixes a Wobble

Finn was a curious fox kit with bright, shiny eyes and a soft, fluffy tail. One sunny morning, he padded into the garage workshop with a little clack-clack of his tiny claws. The air smelled like wood and orange soap. Sunlight made stripes on the floor. Tools hung on the wall, shiny and still.

Finn’s small wooden wagon sat by the workbench. “Oh no,” said Finn, tapping it with his paw. The wheel went wobble-wobble. The wagon made a squeaky sound like a mouse. Finn’s ears perked up. “I will learn how to fix it,” he whispered. His heart felt brave and bouncy.

Grandma Owl lived in a cozy nest in the rafters. She peeped down. “Good morning, Finn,” she hooted softly. “What do you need?”

“My wagon wobbles,” said Finn. “I want to learn to make it strong again.”

“Learning is a gift,” said Grandma Owl. “You can do it. I will watch and cheer.”

Finn smiled. He put on a little apron. It felt smooth and snug. He picked up a cloth and wiped the wagon. Swish, swish. Dust made tiny clouds in the warm light. He took a deep breath. The garage smelled like pine wood and warm rubber.

Next, Finn looked at the tool bench. There was a small blue screwdriver. There was a silver wrench. There was a tiny red oil can. “Which one?” Finn asked himself. He tapped his chin. “I can learn step by step.”

He rolled the wagon slowly. Scritch-scratch went the wobbly wheel. Finn knelt and looked close. He saw a little nut, not tight. “Aha,” said Finn. “This nut is loose.”

Grandma Owl nodded. “You noticed a clue. That is smart learning.”

Finn reached for the silver wrench. It felt cool in his paw. He turned it on the nut. Twist, twist. It moved a little. “It’s tight now,” he said. He pushed the wagon. Wobble-wobble again!

Finn’s tail drooped. “Oh,” he said, “it still wobbles.”

From under the workbench came a tiny squeak. Pip the field mouse popped out with bright whiskers. He held a tiny screw like a treasure. “Hi, Finn,” said Pip. “I like to help. I can share this extra screw.”

Finn smiled. “Thank you, Pip. That is kind and generous.”

Pip squeaked, “Let’s look together.” He pointed with his nose. “See the little peg? It’s missing a screw.”

Finn’s ears stood tall. “I see it! I am learning to look all around.”

Pip held the screw. Finn chose the small blue screwdriver. He lined up the screw and turned it slowly. Turn, turn. The sound was soft and steady. He felt the screw go in snug and safe.

Finn tried the wagon. The wheel moved smooth. No wobble! He laughed. “It works!” His tail swished like a happy broom.

Grandma Owl clapped her wings. “You learned by looking, thinking, and trying. Well done.”

Finn hugged Pip gently. “Thank you for sharing your screw. You were kind.”

Pip blushed. “You did the work. You learned how.”

Just then, a bee buzzed at the open door. Bzzzz. The bee bumped the screen and buzzed in a worried way. “Oh dear,” said Finn. “The bee wants to go out.”

Finn remembered: be kind and be calm. He picked up a clear jar and a soft card. “We can help,” he said. He moved slowly. Scoop went the jar over the bee. The bee hummed like a tiny drum. Finn slid the card under the jar. He carried the bee to the sunny door. Warm light touched his paws. He lifted the card away. The bee flew out, free and happy. Bzzzz!

“Kindness feels warm,” said Finn. His chest felt light and proud.

He turned back to his wagon. “Now I can carry books to the little library!” Finn packed two picture books and a small jar of bright buttons. “I will share,” he said. “Sharing is generous.”

Pip smiled wide. “May I ride?” he asked.

“Hop in,” said Finn. He checked the wheels. He checked the handle. “I can be self-reliant,” he said. “I can take care, and I can ask for help when I need it.”

They rolled out, rumble-rumble over the smooth garage floor. The wagon felt steady and strong. The air outside smelled like grass. The sun felt warm on Finn’s fur.

At the little library box, Finn placed the books inside. He left the jar of buttons for craft time. A small raccoon kit came by. “Thank you,” the kit said. “These books look fun.”

“You’re welcome,” said Finn. His heart felt full. “Today I learned to fix, to notice, to try, to ask, and to share.”

Back in the garage workshop, Finn put away the tools. The wrench went clink. The screwdriver went click. He wiped the bench. Swish, swish. He hung up his apron. He looked at Grandma Owl and Pip.

“I learned by doing,” said Finn. “Learning helps me help others. Kindness and generosity make the day bright.”

Grandma Owl hooted softly. “And when you learn, you grow strong inside.”

Finn patted his wagon. “No more wobble,” he said. He smiled, big and brave.

The End.
