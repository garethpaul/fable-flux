# Polly's Rooftop Adventure

High up on a sunny rooftop, a bright green parrot named Polly sat with her colorful feathers ruffled and her beak turned down in a big frown. She had been trying to write a story in her special notebook, but the words just wouldn't come out right.

"Squawk! This is too hard!" Polly screeched, throwing her pencil down with a loud clatter. Her orange feet stomped on the warm rooftop tiles. "I can't think of any good words!"

Polly felt angry steam coming out of her ears like a little teapot. Her heart was beating fast, and her wings felt all tight and squished against her body. She wanted to tear up her notebook and fly away!

Just then, her friend Benny the bluebird landed softly beside her. "What's wrong, Polly?" he chirped gently.

"I'm trying to write about my adventures, but I'm stuck!" Polly squawked loudly. "My brain feels all foggy, and I can't think!"

Benny tilted his little blue head. "You know what always helps me when I feel stuck? Moving my body! Want to try some rooftop exercises with me?"

Polly looked at him with one curious eye. "Exercises? But I'm too angry to move!"

"That's exactly when moving helps the most!" Benny said with a cheerful tweet. "Come on, let's try!"

Even though she felt grumpy, Polly decided to be brave and try something new. Benny showed her how to stretch her beautiful wings wide like an airplane. "Feel the warm sunshine on your feathers," he said.

Polly stretched her wings out wide. The gentle breeze felt cool and tickly on her green feathers. "Ooh, that feels nice," she admitted.

Next, Benny had her march in place, lifting her orange feet high. "Stomp, stomp, stomp!" they chanted together. The angry feelings in Polly's chest started to bounce away with each step.

Then they did jumping jacks, flapping their wings up and down. "One, two, three!" they counted. Polly began to giggle as she felt her heart pumping in a good way now.

"Now let's fly in circles around the rooftop!" Benny suggested.

Polly took a deep breath and spread her wings. Flying felt wonderful! The wind whooshed through her feathers, and she could see the whole neighborhood below. Her angry feelings flew away like dandelion seeds in the breeze.

After their exercise, Polly and Benny sat down to rest. Polly's breathing was calm and steady now. Her mind felt clear and sparkly, like a clean window.

"How do you feel now?" asked Benny.

Polly picked up her pencil with excitement. "I feel amazing! And guess what? Now I have so many ideas for my story!" She began writing quickly: "Once upon a time, a brave parrot learned that moving her body helped her angry feelings fly away..."

The words flowed like a happy river. Polly wrote about flying, stretching, and jumping. She wrote about friendship and trying new things.

"Thank you, Benny!" Polly chirped. "I learned that when I feel stuck or angry, moving my body helps my mind feel better too!"

From that day on, whenever Polly felt frustrated, she remembered to stretch, march, and fly around the rooftop. Exercise became her special helper for both her body and her creative mind.

The End.
