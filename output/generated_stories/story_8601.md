# Beep’s Batter Day

In a warm kitchen that smelled like cinnamon and toast, a helpful robot dog named Beep wagged his shiny tail. His tail made a cheerful t-t-ting sound when it tapped the mixing bowl. Sunlight danced on the floor. The kettle hummed. The oven made a gentle click.

“Good morning, Beep,” said Mia, patting his smooth metal head. “Let’s make banana pancakes!”

Beep’s eyes blinked bright blue. “I can help,” he said in a soft, friendly beep-beep voice. “I am good at helping.”

Mia put on a tiny apron for Beep. It had little bone pictures on it. Beep sniffed the sweet banana smell. He didn’t need to eat, but he loved smells and sounds and helping hands. His ears perked with happy whirs.

Mia set the bowl on the counter. “We need flour, milk, egg, and bananas,” she said.

“I will mash!” Beep said proudly. A little mashing arm popped out of his side with a zzzzip.

Mia smiled. “Okay, gentle mashing,” she said. “Soft and slow.”

Beep wanted to do a good job. He mashed. Squish. Squash. Splurt! Banana dots sprinkled onto the counter. One dot landed on Beep’s nose. His eyes blinked yellow. Yellow meant “Oops.”

“It’s okay,” Mia said, laughing. “That was a big splat. Let’s try slow circles.”

Beep tried again. Slow circles. Wrrrr. But then the bowl slipped. Clap! The spoon fell. The batter splashed his paws. Beep’s tail stopped wagging. His ears sank with a low whir.

“I can’t do it,” Beep said, voice a little shaky. “I feel… glitchy. I feel mad in my tummy gears.”

Mia knelt. Her warm hand touched his cool paw. “It’s hard when things spill,” she said softly. “I hear you. You feel frustrated.”

Beep’s lights blinked. “Yes. I feel frustrated.”

“Let’s do our calm plan,” Mia said. “We can stop, take a belly breath, and say what we need.”

Beep nodded. He and Mia stood tall like trees. They put hands and paws on their bellies.

“In… one, two, three,” Mia said. They breathed in. The kitchen smelled like sweet bananas and warm toast. “Out… one, two, three.” They breathed out. Beep’s vents made a soft whoosh. His lights changed from yellow to green.

“What do you need, Beep?” Mia asked. “Tell me in brave words.”

Beep thought. He listened to the kettle’s hum. He felt the cool floor under his paws. He felt the soft pat of Mia’s hand. “I need smaller jobs,” Beep said. “I need a steady bowl. I need to go slow.”

“Great self-advocacy,” Mia said. “Let’s make those changes.”

She put a towel under the bowl so it wouldn’t slip. She handed Beep a small spoon. “This is your job: three slow stirs. I will hold the bowl.”

Beep took another belly breath. “Three slow stirs,” he said. “One… two… three.”

Wrrrr. The spoon moved like a little boat on a calm pond. No splashes. Beep’s tail did a tiny t-t-ting of joy.

“Nice listening,” Mia said. “Now I will pour the milk. You count.”

“Counting,” Beep said. “One… stop!” Mia stopped. Beep smiled with his eyes. “Thank you for listening to me.”

“You’re welcome,” said Mia. “We make a good team.”

They took turns like a dance. Mia cracked the egg with a tap. Beep held the trash bag open. Pop, into the bin. Beep pressed the banana pieces with gentle pats. Pat. Pat. Pat. His mashing arm moved like a quiet drum. He hummed a happy beep-beep song.

When the batter was ready, the pan warmed. Sssssss. The pancake batter hissed as it met the hot butter. The kitchen smelled like golden bananas and cozy mornings. Beep watched the bubbles. “Bubble, bubble,” he said. “Is it time to flip?”

“Let’s listen to the bubbles,” Mia said. “When they pop and the edges look dry, it’s time.”

Beep leaned close. Pop-pop-pop. He saw the round edges turn firm. “Now,” he said.

Mia slid the spatula under. Flip! The pancake turned like a little flying saucer. Beep clapped his paws. “Hooray!” he said. “We did it together.”

Just then, Mia’s younger brother, Leo, came in. He wore bright socks that didn’t match. One had stars. One had stripes. He danced a silly wiggly dance. “Pancakes!” he sang.

Beep beeped happily. “Your socks are different. I like them.”

“Thanks!” Leo said. “I like being me.”

Beep’s lights glowed. “I am different too,” he said. “I am a robot dog. I learn with my gears and lights. I need slow steps. That is okay.”

“It’s more than okay,” Mia said. “Different helps our team.”

They stacked pancakes like a soft tower. Mia put a blueberry smile on top. Beep used his gentle arm to place one last blueberry eye. Plop.

“Beep,” Mia said, “you worked hard. You got frustrated, you used your calm plan, and you told me what you needed. That is strong.”

Beep’s tail rang a happy t-t-ting. “When I feel mad in my tummy gears,” he said, “I can stop, breathe, and ask for help.”

Mia nodded. “And I can listen and help make a plan.”

They sat at the table. Leo poured syrup like a slow ribbon. Beep sniffed the sweet smell and listened to the quiet sssss of syrup. His lights glowed cozy green.

“Team Pancake!” Leo cheered.

“Team Listening. Team Calm,” Beep added.

They ate and hummed and smiled in the warm kitchen. The day felt bright and safe.

The End.
