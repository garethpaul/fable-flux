# Bubbles’ Dinnertime World Tour

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to wiggle, giggle, and splash. Today, Bubbles had a big plan: practice a new dinnertime routine. But Bubbles didn’t live in the sea. Bubbles lived with a kind family who had a cozy dining room with warm lights, a shiny wooden table, and chairs that made soft squeaks. The air smelled like warm rice, sweet carrots, and a little lemon. Plates were round and smooth. Forks and spoons clinked like tiny bells.

“Dinnertime!” called Mama. “Routine time!”

Bubbles swam through the air—well, that’s what it looked like when Bubbles wiggled—into the dining room. Bubbles loved to explore. But tonight, Bubbles wanted to practice the steps. Step 1: Wash fins. Step 2: Sit in the chair. Step 3: Napkin on lap. Step 4: Taste new food. Step 5: Talk, not shout. Step 6: Help clean up.

Bubbles hummed, “Routine helps me. Routine sets me free!”

Papa smiled. “We can learn about the world at dinner, too.”

Bubbles’ eyes sparkled. “A world tour?”

“A gentle, quiet tour,” Mama said with a grin.

Bubbles sprinkled drops of water from washed fins, then slid onto the chair. The napkin felt soft like a cloud on Bubbles’ lap. The spoon was cool, smooth, and shiny like a moon.

At the table sat friends from many places: Asha wore bright bangles that jingled softly. Leo had a tiny soccer pin. Mei had a red ribbon, and Raf wore a cozy scarf. They all smiled.

“Welcome, everyone,” Papa said. “Tonight, we try tiny tastes from around the world.”

Bubbles bounced a little. “I want to try them all!”

“Remember Step 3 and Step 4,” Mama whispered. “Napkin, then taste.”

First came rice with lemon and herbs. Steam rose like little clouds. “This is from Greece,” said Papa.

Bubbles took a small bite. “Mmm! It tastes sunny!” The lemon tickled Bubbles’ nose.

Next came carrots with warm spices. “This is from India,” Asha said. “It smells like flowers and sunshine.”

Bubbles sniffed. The scent was sweet and cozy. Bubbles tried a nibble and smiled. “Like a hugging blanket!”

Then came dumplings. Mei giggled. “These are from China. They are soft and chewy.”

Bubbles poked a dumpling. It was squishy and smooth. Bubbles tasted and clapped fins. “Chewy clouds!”

Raf shared a little saucy bean dish. “From Brazil,” he said. “It is gentle and yummy.”

Bubbles tasted and nodded. “Beans are strong and kind!”

Leo passed a small cup. “This is cool water. From our tap, but water is everywhere. We share the same Earth.”

Bubbles took a sip. The water was fresh and clear. “Hello, world!”

But then, a new plate arrived. It had tiny green trees. “This is broccoli,” Mama said. “Lots of kids eat it, all around the world.”

Bubbles’ smile fell. “I don’t like tiny trees,” Bubbles whispered. Bubbles’ tummy felt tight. The chair squeaked. A little growl bubbled inside. Bubbles wanted to shout, “No!”

Bubbles remembered Step 5: Talk, not shout. Bubbles took a slow breath. In. Out. In. Out. The napkin felt soft in Bubbles’ lap. The spoon felt cool in Bubbles’ fin.

“I feel mad,” Bubbles said softly. “The smell is strong. I don’t want to try.”

Mama nodded. “Thank you for telling us with calm words.”

Asha said, “When I feel mad, I blow a bubble.” She puckered and blew a pretend bubble. Pop!

Mei added, “I tap the table gently three times.” Tap, tap, tap.

Leo said, “I count to five.” One, two, three, four, five.

Raf smiled. “We can all try a tiny bite. We can be brave together.”

Bubbles looked at the tiny tree. “A tiny bite,” Bubbles said. “I can try.” That was Step 4: Taste new food.

Bubbles touched the broccoli with the fork. It was bumpy like coral. Bubbles took a little bite. Chew, chew. The taste was green and a little peppery. Bubbles blinked. “It’s… okay. Not my favorite. But I tried!”

Everyone clapped softly. The forks made happy clinks.

Mama gave a thumbs-up. “That was brave. That was calm. That was our routine.”

They talked about people around the world who eat together, who try, who share, and who listen. They spoke kindly, waiting their turns. The lights felt warm. The room felt safe.

When dinner was over, Bubbles remembered Step 6: Help clean up. Bubbles carried plates, one by one, to the sink. Swish, swash, splash—the water sang. Friends helped too. Many fins and hands made light work. This was inclusion: everyone joined in.

Papa said, “What did we learn on our world tour?”

Bubbles raised a fin. “Routine helps me feel calm. I can try new things, even if I feel mad. I can use gentle words. People around the world eat many foods, and we can share and help together.”

Mama smiled. “Yes. And when we practice, we grow.”

Bubbles hummed the routine song. “Step by step, day by day. Try, breathe, help, and play.” The dining room smelled clean and sweet. The table shone. The napkins were folded like little boats.

Bubbles wiggled and giggled. “Tomorrow, we do it again!”

The End.
