# Rocco’s Honest Song

Rocco was a musical raccoon. He wore big red headphones and a tiny blue scarf. He loved to tap-tap his paws and hum-hum a tune. One rainy afternoon, Rocco visited Dr. Dot’s basement lab. The lab was bright and busy. Lights blinked. Buttons beeped. The air smelled like warm popcorn and lemon soap. A gentle fan made a shhhh sound.

“Hello, Dr. Dot!” Rocco sang. “My paws are ready to play!”

Dr. Dot smiled. She was a kind friend with curly hair and green glasses. “Hi, Rocco. Today we’ll make a Beat Box. It goes boom, tap, shake! We will test it, fix it, and dance.”

Rocco’s whiskers twitched. “Boom, tap, shake!” he echoed, clapping his paws.

On a shiny table sat a small metal box with rainbow wires. A big red button glowed. A little screen blinked 1-2-3. Beside it was a chart with simple pictures: a paw, a drum, a lightbulb, a heart.

Dr. Dot pointed. “This chart helps us think. We look, listen, try, and care. That is how we solve problems.”

“Look, listen, try, and care,” Rocco repeated.

Dr. Dot turned a dial. The Beat Box sang, “Boom… tap… shake!” Rocco danced. He wiggled his tail. He slid his feet. He swished his scarf. “This is fun!” he said, laughing.

Then—pop! The Beat Box went quiet. The light went dark. The room was so still they could hear the fan go shhhh and a drip drop from a pipe.

Rocco froze. “Oh no,” he whispered.

Dr. Dot nodded. “We have a problem.”

Rocco looked at the chart. “We look,” he said. They leaned in. The red button was a little crooked.

“We listen,” said Dr. Dot. They listened. No beep. No hum. Only shhhh.

“We try,” said Rocco. He pressed the red button. Nothing.

“We care,” said Dr. Dot. She patted the box. “We keep it safe, and we keep each other safe.”

Rocco’s cheeks felt warm. He remembered. A minute ago, when Dr. Dot reached for a tool, Rocco had tried a move. He called it the Super Spin. He had spun fast. His tail had bumped the red button. He had not told Dr. Dot. Now the Beat Box was quiet.

His tummy felt twisty. He did not like the twisty feeling. He looked at the heart on the chart. He thought about honesty. Honesty means telling the truth. It means saying what really happened.

Rocco took a deep breath in… and out. “Dr. Dot,” he said, “I need to tell you something.”

Dr. Dot looked up. “I’m listening.”

“I tried my Super Spin,” Rocco said, voice small. “My tail bumped the red button. I was afraid you would be mad. I did not say it. I feel sorry.”

Dr. Dot’s face softened. “Thank you for telling the truth, Rocco. That is honest. I am not mad. I am glad you told me. Now we can fix it together.”

Rocco’s tummy un-twisted a little. “We can?” he asked.

“Yes,” she said. “Honesty helps friends. When we know the truth, we can make a good plan.”

They looked again. The button was bent to one side. Dr. Dot handed Rocco a soft cloth. “You can hold the wires. Hold them steady. I will fix the button.”

Rocco nodded. He held the wires gently. They felt cool and smooth. “Steady, steady,” he hummed. He kept his body still. No Super Spins now. Dr. Dot used a tiny tool. Click. Click. The button was straight.

“Let’s test,” she said.

Rocco pressed the red button. The light blinked. The screen flashed 1-2-3. The Beat Box sang, “Boom… tap… shake!” Rocco and Dr. Dot cheered.

Rocco smiled wide. “It works!”

Dr. Dot laughed. “It does. You helped. You were honest, and you used careful moves.”

Rocco looked at the chart. He pointed to the paw. “We looked.” He pointed to the lightbulb. “We listened and thought.” He pointed to the heart. “We cared, and I told the truth.”

“Perfect,” said Dr. Dot. “Now we add one more step: Safe Movement. We move big when it is safe and small when we need to.”

Rocco wiggled his shoulders in a tiny dance. “Tiny moves near the box,” he said. Then he stepped back and did a gentle toe-tap. “Big moves over here.”

“Great plan,” Dr. Dot said. “You used critical thinking.”

Rocco stood tall. “I also want to say sorry for not telling you right away.”

“I accept your apology,” said Dr. Dot. “Thank you for being brave.”

They made a new game. The Beat Box played boom, tap, shake. Rocco moved in three ways:
- Boom: Stomp-stomp in place.
- Tap: Clap-clap softly.
- Shake: Wiggle-wiggle shoulders.

When the light turned blue, Rocco used tiny moves. When it turned green, he used big moves. He watched. He listened. He thought. He stayed honest with his words and careful with his body.

Soon the rain stopped. Sunlight slid across the lab floor like warm butter. The fan still went shhhh, but now it was a happy sound.

Rocco took off his headphones. “Dr. Dot, today I learned a big thing.”

“What did you learn?” she asked.

“I learned that honesty helps friends fix problems. Telling the truth made my tummy feel better. It helped us think and make a plan. It helped the Beat Box sing again.”

Dr. Dot nodded. “Yes. Honesty brings trust. Trust brings good teamwork.”

Rocco hugged his scarf. “And good teamwork brings music!”

They laughed. They danced one last time. Boom. Tap. Shake. Rocco moved with care, with thought, and with truth in his heart.

The End.
