# Bibi’s Big World Wall

Bibi was a friendly bear cub with soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes. One rainy morning, Bibi padded down the creaky stairs to the basement lab. The lab was full of interesting sounds and smells. Drip… drip… went a leaky pipe. Hummmm went a little fan. The air smelled like crayons and warm paint. On one long wall, there was a giant, blank paper. It was as wide as a bus!

Bibi giggled. “This is perfect for art!” he said, clapping his paws. His voice echoed, “Art… art… art!”

On a table, Bibi found friendly art helpers: jars of paint, thick brushes, stickers shaped like stars, and soft sponges. There was also a big round globe, smooth and cool under Bibi’s paw. It spun with a soft whirr. Blue swirls, green shapes, and tiny dots sailed past Bibi’s nose.

“What are all these places?” Bibi asked the globe. He tapped it gently. “Hello, world.”

Grandma Bear came down the stairs with a warm smile. She wore bright yellow glasses and a test-tube apron dotted with paint. “Good morning, Bibi,” she said. “What do you see?”

“I see the world,” Bibi said. “But I don’t understand it. The wall is empty. My heart feels big. I want to draw… but what?”

Grandma Bear nodded. “Let’s make a Big World Wall,” she said. “Art can help us learn. We can learn about people and places all around the world.”

Bibi’s ears perked up. “Yes! But… I have a problem,” he said softly. “I don’t know how to show the whole world. It feels too big.”

Grandma Bear squeezed his paw. “We will solve it step by step. We will listen, look, and learn. Then we will paint.”

First step: listen. Grandma Bear turned on the lab radio. Gentle music filled the basement. A drum went dum-dum. A flute sang la-la. A singer hummed in a language Bibi didn’t know. The sound was new and sweet.

“What is that?” Bibi asked.

“That music comes from far away,” Grandma Bear said. “People everywhere make music.”

Bibi closed his eyes. He felt the drum in his chest. “I feel brave!” he said.

Second step: look. Grandma Bear opened a box of picture cards. There were children with smiling faces, wearing clothes Bibi had never seen before: bright scarves, cozy hats, colorful shirts. Some children stood by tall trees. Some stood by smooth sand. Some stood in snow that looked like white sugar.

Bibi pointed. “She has flowers on her dress! He has a round drum! There is a big blue sea!”

Third step: learn. Grandma Bear rolled the globe to a green shape. “This is a forest far away,” she said. She rolled to yellow sand. “This is a desert.” She rolled to white swirls. “This is ice and snow.”

Bibi took a deep breath. The air smelled like paper and hope. “Now I can make art,” he said.

He dipped a brush in blue paint. Swish, swish! Big waves rolled across the bottom of the wall. He patted a sponge in green. Dab, dab! Forests grew in soft, leafy shapes. He pressed star stickers for cities that twinkled—plink, plink!

Bibi talked as he painted. “This blue is the sea we all share. Boats can float. Fish can swim. People can visit.”

He added orange dots. “These are deserts. They are hot. People drink water and cover their heads.”

He stamped white puffs. “These are snowy places. People wear warm coats and sip hot soup.”

Bibi drew children with simple, happy faces. One child wore beads. Another wore a hat with a puff. Another carried a book. He gave them all big smiles and open hands.

Grandma Bear clapped. “You are showing how different and how same we all are,” she said. “We all sing. We all learn. We all play.”

Bibi paused. He held a purple brush but looked worried. “What if I make a mistake?” he whispered. “What if I draw someone wrong?”

Grandma Bear put a paw on Bibi’s shoulder. “Good question,” she said. “When we learn about the world, we listen and show care. We ask, ‘What makes you you?’ We look for true things. We do not laugh at what is new. We respect.”

Bibi nodded slowly. “I can add labels,” he said. He wrote simple words under his pictures: Forest. Desert. Snow. City. Music. Friends. He added speech bubbles. “Hello!” said one child. “Hola!” said another. “Jambo!” “Ni hao!” “Bonjour!”

The basement felt warm with colors and kind words. Tap-tap went the brush. Swish-swish went the sponge. Bibi’s heart felt calm and bright.

At last, Bibi stood back. The Big World Wall glowed. It showed oceans, trees, sand, and snow. It showed children from many places, waving hello. It showed books, drums, and crayons. It showed that the world is big—and connected.

Bibi smiled. “I solved my problem,” he said. “The world felt too big. But step by step, I learned. Art helped me understand. We are different, and we are alike. We can care for our big home.”

Grandma Bear hugged him. “Yes,” she said. “When we learn about others, we become good neighbors. When we share art, we share hearts.”

Bibi turned off the radio. The drum’s echo faded. The fan hummed softly. The paint smelled sweet. The stairs creaked as Grandma Bear went to make tea.

Bibi waved at the wall. “Hello, world,” he said. “I will take care of you. I will listen, look, and learn.”

The End.
