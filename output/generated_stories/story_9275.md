# Roo's Safe Song

Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey. His tail thumped softly as he hopped into the musical practice room. The room smelled like fresh wood and lemon polish. Sunlight made bright squares on the floor. Shiny drums, a small piano, and rows of tiny bells waited quietly.

Roo’s ears twitched. “Hello, music!” he giggled. “Let’s play!”

Coach Kiki, a kind koala with round glasses, waved. “Hi, Roo! Before we play, we do our safety check.”

Roo nodded. “Safety first!”

Coach Kiki pointed to the wall. “Step one: soft ears.” She handed Roo a pair of little sound-muff headphones. They were blue and comfy.

Roo slipped them on. “Mmm! They feel like clouds.”

“Step two: clear floor,” said Coach Kiki. They looked around. A long cord lay snaking across the floor like a sleepy vine.

Roo tilted his head. “That cord looks tricky.”

“Good eye,” said Coach Kiki. “We tuck cords away so no one trips.” Together, they rolled the cord and clipped it safely along the wall.

“Step three,” Coach Kiki smiled, “gentle hands.”

Roo placed his paws on the piano keys. “Gentle,” he whispered. He pressed one key. Plink. The sound was soft and bright, like a tiny star.

Roo’s friend Tilly, a tiny turtle, peeked in. “Hi, Roo. Can I listen?”

“Yes!” Roo said. “We can play together.”

Coach Kiki clapped softly. “We also care for our room, like we care for the forest. That’s conservation. Put things back, share tools, and don’t waste. It’s fair and kind—justice for everyone and everything.”

Roo’s chest puffed with pride. “I can do that!”

They began. Roo tapped a small drum. Tilly rang a bell. Ding! Tap-tap. Ding! The room filled with friendly sounds.

Suddenly, Roo saw something wrong. A stack of heavy music books leaned sideways. It looked wobbly.

“Coach Kiki,” Roo said, “those books look like they might fall.”

Coach Kiki nodded. “Thank you for speaking up. That is brave and fair. Justice means we protect each other.” She placed the books flat on a low shelf. “Now everyone is safe.”

Roo beamed. His tail thump-thumped. He pressed three piano keys in a row. Do-re-mi. Tilly hummed along. Mmm-mmm-mmm. The melody felt warm, like sunshine on fur.

Then there was a loud clatter. Crash! A little cymbal rolled to the edge of the table.

Roo froze. “Oh no! It might fall.”

“Let’s pause,” said Coach Kiki. “We use our safety plan. Stop. Look. Think.”

Roo took a slow breath. He looked. The cymbal was near the edge. He thought. “I will ask for help.”

“Coach Kiki,” Roo said, “can you reach it safely?”

“I can,” she replied. She gently moved the cymbal to the center and added a soft mat beneath the table. “Now if it slips, it has a safe landing.”

Roo felt calm. “I like our plan.”

Coach Kiki smiled. “Me too. Music is fun, and safety helps us enjoy it.”

Tilly raised her tiny hand. “Can we make a safety song?”

“Yes!” Roo cheered. He bounced once, softly, so his headphones stayed put.

They made a chant:

“Stop, look, think,
Keep cords in a link.
Soft ears, clear space,
Gentle hands, safe place!”

Roo played a steady beat on the drum. Thum-thum. Tilly rang her bell at each rhyme. Ding! Coach Kiki hummed a low, cozy note. Mmmmmm.

Roo imagined the forest. He thought of trees, birds, and clean water. “Coach Kiki,” he asked, “is safety like helping the forest?”

“Yes,” she said. “When we take care of the room, we take care of our world. We don’t break, we don’t waste, and we share. That is justice and conservation. It keeps everyone and everything safe.”

Roo looked at the recycle bin by the door. A crumpled paper sat on the floor. He picked it up and put it in the bin. “I can help!”

“Thank you, Roo,” said Tilly. “That was fair.”

Roo grinned. He felt tall and brave. He made a soft ending on the piano. Plink-plonk-plink…plonk. The final note hung in the air like a bubble, then popped into quiet.

Coach Kiki clapped gently. “You followed every step. You listened. You spoke up. You cared for your friends and your space.”

Roo hugged Tilly. “Music is more fun when we are safe.”

Tilly hugged back. “And fair.”

Roo patted the drum. “And kind to our room and our world.”

They put the instruments away, one by one. Drum on the shelf. Bells in the box. Headphones on the hook. They turned off the lights. Click. The room felt peaceful and proud.

As they left, Roo sang their chant softly. “Stop, look, think…” His voice sounded happy and strong.

Today, Roo learned that safety comes first, fairness helps friends, and caring for our space protects the world. With gentle hands and brave voices, music can shine.

The End.
