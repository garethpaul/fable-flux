# Nutkin's Big Fix

High up in the tall oak tree lived a clever little squirrel named Nutkin. His treehouse was the coziest home in the whole forest, with soft moss cushions and tiny acorn bowls for snacks.

One sunny morning, Nutkin heard crying from below. "Wah! Wah!" It was his friend Bunny, sitting by the tree trunk with tears in her fuzzy eyes.

"What's wrong, Bunny?" called Nutkin, scampering down the rough bark.

"The bridge to my burrow broke!" sobbed Bunny. "Now I can't get home, and neither can Raccoon or Hedgehog. We're all stuck on this side of the stream!"

Nutkin's bushy tail twitched as he thought. The old log bridge had indeed snapped in two. The rushing water below made a loud "whoosh" sound, and it looked very deep.

"Don't worry," said Nutkin, puffing out his chest. "I'm the smartest squirrel in the forest. I'll fix this myself!"

Nutkin tried pushing a big rock toward the stream. "Oof! Ugh!" But it was too heavy. Then he tried dragging a long stick, but it was too short to reach across.

Soon, Raccoon and Hedgehog arrived, looking sad and worried.

"Can we help?" asked Raccoon, his black mask making him look very serious.

"No, no!" huffed Nutkin, wiping sweat from his furry forehead. "I can do this alone. I'm the leader here!"

But as the sun climbed higher in the bright blue sky, Nutkin grew more and more tired. His little paws were sore, and he still hadn't solved the problem.

Finally, Nutkin sat down on a smooth rock and hung his head. "I'm sorry, friends. I thought I could fix everything by myself, but I can't."

Bunny hopped over and patted his paw gently. "That's okay, Nutkin. We all make mistakes."

"But I wanted to be a good leader," whispered Nutkin.

"Good leaders ask for help," said wise old Hedgehog, his spiky back glinting in the sunlight. "They work with their team."

Nutkin's ears perked up. "You're right! Will you help me make a plan?"

"Yes!" cheered all his friends together.

Nutkin climbed onto a tall mushroom so everyone could see him. "Okay, team! Raccoon, you're strong. Can you help me move rocks? Hedgehog, you're small. Can you check if the rocks are steady? Bunny, you're fast. Can you gather soft leaves to make the bridge safe?"

"Great plan!" said Raccoon, clapping his tiny paws.

Working together, they built a wonderful bridge. Raccoon and Nutkin rolled big stones into the water with loud "splash" sounds. Hedgehog tested each rock by walking carefully across. Bunny covered everything with soft, green leaves that smelled like springtime.

"We did it!" cheered Nutkin as they all crossed safely to the other side.

"Thank you for being such a good leader," said Bunny, giving Nutkin a warm hug.

"Thank you for teaching me what that really means," said Nutkin. "A real leader works with friends, not alone."

From that day on, whenever there was a problem in the forest, Nutkin would gather his friends and say, "Let's solve this together!" And they always did.

Back in his cozy treehouse that evening, Nutkin smiled as he looked out at the beautiful bridge they had built. He had learned that the best leaders are those who bring everyone together.

The End.
