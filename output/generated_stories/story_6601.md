# Rosie Rabbit's Garden Adventure

Rosie Rabbit loved her special balcony garden. Every morning, she hopped outside to water her tiny tomato plants and smell her pretty flowers. The sun felt warm on her soft brown fur, and the sweet scent of lavender tickled her pink nose.

"Good morning, little plants!" Rosie said cheerfully, filling her small watering can with fresh water.

But one day, Rosie noticed something terrible. Her beautiful flowers were drooping, and their petals were turning brown. The leaves on her tomato plants looked sad and wilted.

"Oh no!" Rosie cried, her ears drooping with worry. "What's wrong with my garden?"

She looked closer and gasped. Plastic bottles and candy wrappers were scattered everywhere! Someone had thrown trash over the balcony railing, and it had landed right in her precious garden.

Rosie felt her whiskers twitch with anger. Her little paws clenched into fists. "This makes me so mad!" she huffed, stamping her foot. "Who would hurt my plants like this?"

For a moment, Rosie wanted to throw the trash right back over the balcony. But then she remembered what Mama Rabbit had taught her about taking deep breaths when she felt upset.

"Breathe in... breathe out," Rosie whispered to herself, closing her eyes and counting to five. The anger in her tummy started to feel smaller.

When she opened her eyes, Rosie looked at her garden again. "Getting mad won't help my plants," she said thoughtfully. "But I know what will!"

Rosie hopped inside and got her little gloves, a small trash bag, and her gardening tools. She worked carefully, picking up every piece of litter. The plastic crinkled in her paws, and she wrinkled her nose at the yucky smell.

"Don't worry, little flowers," she said gently. "I'm going to take good care of you."

After cleaning up all the trash, Rosie gave her plants extra water and sang them a happy song. She could already see them starting to perk up!

But Rosie wasn't finished. She had an important idea.

The next day, Rosie made colorful signs with pictures of flowers and happy faces. "Please keep our earth clean!" one sign said. "Plants need love, not litter!" said another.

She asked Papa Rabbit to help her hang the signs around the building. "What a wonderful idea, Rosie!" Papa said proudly. "You're taking such good care of our environment."

Soon, other families in the building saw Rosie's signs. They started being more careful with their trash and even helped keep the area clean.

Mrs. Mouse from upstairs brought Rosie a new packet of sunflower seeds. "Thank you for caring about our home," she squeaked kindly.

A week later, Rosie's garden was more beautiful than ever. Her tomatoes were growing big and red, and her flowers danced in the gentle breeze. Best of all, there wasn't a single piece of litter anywhere!

"I'm so proud of you, Rosie," Mama Rabbit said, giving her a warm hug. "When you felt angry, you chose to help instead of hurt. And look how you've made our whole neighborhood better!"

Rosie smiled, her heart feeling as sunny as her garden. She had learned that taking care of the earth meant taking care of everyone who lived on it - including herself.

The End.
