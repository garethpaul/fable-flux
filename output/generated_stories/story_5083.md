# Brave Song in the Art Room

The creative art room smelled like fresh paint and orange peels. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Paper chains in many colors hung from the ceiling. Brushes, crayons, and clay sat on a long table.

On a small window perch sat Lila, a singing bird with soft blue feathers. Her chest had a tiny gold patch that shone when she breathed in. Lila loved to sing when no one was around. Her voice was sweet, like a flute. But when people came close, her heart fluttered fast.

“Today is Sharing Art Day,” said Ms. Ana, the teacher. Her smile was bright and kind. She wore a scarf with patterns from many places—tiny stars, leaves, and little boats. “We will make art and share it. We will listen to each other. We will be brave.”

Lila felt her feet wiggle on the perch. “Brave,” she whispered. “I want to be brave.”

Children came in with happy steps. Amir wore a green shirt and brought stamps from his grandma’s country. Mei had folding paper to make fans. Amina had beads that clicked softly. Luis carried a small drum. Zoe had crayons that smelled like fruit—cherry, lemon, and blueberry.

Ms. Ana clapped gently. “First, we listen. Then, we share. Our room is a circle of care.”

Lila closed her eyes and listened. Tap-tap-tap went Luis’s drum as he warmed up. Swish-swish went Mei’s paper as she folded. Click-click went Amina’s beads sliding over the string. Thump-thump went Lila’s own brave heart.

“I want to sing,” Lila told herself. “But my beak feels tight.”

Ms. Ana put a little easel by the window. “Lila, would you like to sit near us?” she asked softly. “You can watch and listen. When you feel ready, you can sing.”

Lila hopped closer. The lemony crayon smell tickled her nose. She saw a rainbow of paints, thick and shiny. She heard whisper-laughs and kind words.

Amir showed his stamps. “These are from my grandma,” he said. “She says they tell stories.” He pressed a round stamp onto blue ink and printed a moon shape. “The moon watches over us,” he explained.

“Beautiful,” said Ms. Ana. “We listened well.”

Mei held up her paper fan. “This fan can make a soft wind,” she said. She waved it gently. Cool air brushed Lila’s feathers. It felt nice. Lila sighed, and the gold on her chest glowed a little brighter.

Amina shook her bead necklace. “My beads make music,” she said. The beads clicked like tiny raindrops. Zoe sniffed her crayons. “My colors smell yummy! Want a sniff?” she asked. Everyone giggled and took a turn. “Mmm, blueberry!”

Luis tapped his drum. Tum-tum. Tum. He looked at Lila. “I like to play so others can sing,” he said. “Do you like to sing?”

Lila’s feet trembled. “Yes,” she peeped. “But my tummy is fluttery.”

Ms. Ana knelt so her eyes met Lila’s. “Courage means doing something even when you feel fluttery,” she said. “We will listen with our ears, our eyes, and our hearts. We will wait. We will cheer softly.”

The room grew calm. Brushes rested. Hands were still. Eyes were kind. Lila took a deep breath. She felt the cool fan wind. She smelled blueberry crayons. She heard the soft click of beads and the quiet hum of lights. The room was ready.

“Little song,” Lila told herself, “we can try.”

She opened her beak. A tiny note floated out, light and shy. Luis tapped once, softly. Tum. The note felt braver. Lila sang another note, and then another. Her voice flowed like warm sunlight. She sang about paint that shines, paper that dances, beads like raindrops, and friends from many places sharing one room.

Amir pressed his moon stamp on his paper, slowly, in time. Mei waved her fan to the beat. Amina let her beads click-click like gentle rain. Zoe drew a big heart with the cherry crayon. The art room became a soft, colorful orchestra.

Lila’s gold chest patch shimmered. Her tummy felt steady. She sang louder, and her song reached the corners of the room and the high paper chains. Her song felt like a hug.

When her song finished, the room was quiet for a breath. Then Ms. Ana placed her hand on her heart. Everyone did the same. It was their silent cheer. Then came small claps, soft as butterfly wings.

“You were so brave,” said Ms. Ana.

“I was scared,” Lila admitted, “but I did it.”

Amir smiled. “Your song sounded like the moon on water.”

Mei nodded. “And like a cool wind.”

Amina held out her beads. “Can you sing while I click-click?”

Luis grinned. “I can drum for you again.”

Zoe drew a gold star. “This is for your brave heart,” she said, handing Lila the picture.

Lila’s eyes felt warm. “Thank you for listening,” she said. “Listening helped me be brave.”

Ms. Ana hung the star picture by the window. “In our art room,” she said, “we share many colors and many sounds. We come from many places. We listen. We help each other be brave.”

Lila hopped to the perch. Her feathers felt light. “Tomorrow,” she chirped, “I will sing again. And maybe I will teach a tiny song to anyone who wants to learn.”

“We will listen,” said the children together.

The sun made new warm squares on the floor. The room smelled like orange peels and fresh courage.

The End.
