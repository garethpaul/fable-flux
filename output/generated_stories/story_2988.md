# Bubbles’ Thank-You Art

In a cozy bedroom with soft pillows and twinkly night-lights, a playful dolphin named Bubbles popped his head out of a big blue toy box. He was a stuffed dolphin, soft and squishy, with a stitched smile and sparkly eyes. Every night, when the room grew quiet and warm, Bubbles liked to wriggle and wiggle, play, and learn.

“Good evening, room!” Bubbles chirped, flipping his plush tail. The room smelled like lavender soap and crayons. The window hummed with a gentle breeze. The lamp made a golden glow.

On the bed sat Mia, a little child with curly hair and cozy pajamas. Mia yawned and hugged Bubbles. “Hi, Bubbles,” Mia whispered. “I had a big day.”

“What happened?” asked Bubbles, tilting his head.

“I spilled my snack, and Ms. Star helped me,” Mia said. “Grandpa read me a story. And you, Bubbles, you kept me brave at nap time.”

Bubbles bounced. “So many helpers! That sounds important.”

Mia nodded. “I feel warm and happy. I want to say thank you.”

Bubbles clapped his flippers. “Let’s make thank-you art! Art can show feelings. Art can say, ‘I’m grateful.’”

Mia smiled wide. “Yes! But… I only have a little paper,” she said, holding up a small stack. “And only three crayons.”

“That is a little problem,” Bubbles said. He blinked his sparkly eyes and thought. “We need to be smart. Let’s use knowledge. Let’s think step by step.” He tapped his fin gently. “Who do we thank? What do they like? How can we share?”

Mia sat crisscross on the bed. “We thank Ms. Star, Grandpa, and you.”

Bubbles giggled. “Okay! Three people, three crayons. That matches. Great start!”

Mia laid the crayons in a row. “Blue, yellow, and green.”

Bubbles listened to the quiet hum of the night. He could hear the soft tick-tock of the clock, like a little heartbeat. “Ms. Star loves sunflowers,” he said, remembering. “They are yellow.”

Mia nodded. “Grandpa likes trees. Trees are green.”

“And I am a dolphin,” Bubbles said, swishing his tail. “I am blue!”

Mia clapped. “We can make three thank-you pictures with just three colors!”

Bubbles grinned. “Yes! We solved the problem. Now, let’s make grateful art.”

They spread the small papers on the bed. The blanket was warm and fuzzy under their knees. Mia drew a big round yellow circle. “This is a sunflower for Ms. Star,” she said. “Thank you for helping me.”

Bubbles added little yellow dots around it. “Dot, dot, dot,” he sang. “We are showing thanks with sunshine color.”

Next, Mia used the green crayon. She drew a tree with a tall trunk and big leaves. “This is for Grandpa,” she whispered. “Thank you for reading to me.” She could almost hear Grandpa’s soft voice saying, “Once upon a time…”

Bubbles drew tiny green hearts on the leaves. “Hearts mean love,” he said.

Then Mia picked up the blue crayon and drew a smiling dolphin shape. “This is for you, Bubbles. Thank you for being brave with me.”

Bubbles wiggled with joy. “I feel seen! I feel grateful too.”

But then Mia frowned a little. “I don’t have envelopes,” she said. “How do we give the art?”

Bubbles took a little breath. He closed his eyes and listened. The clock ticked. The breeze shhh-ed. “Let’s think,” he said softly. “We can use what we have. We can share in a new way.”

Mia looked around the room. She saw a ribbon on her dresser. It was soft and red. She saw tape on her desk. “We can make a gallery!” she cheered. “A thank-you wall!”

Bubbles squeaked. “Yes! We can hang the art and invite them to see.”

Mia and Bubbles carefully taped the sunflower, the tree, and the dolphin above the bed. The ribbon made a little bow at the top. The pictures glowed in the lamplight. The room smelled like crayons and warm blankets.

Mia took a deep breath. “I feel thankful,” she said. Her tummy felt cozy and light, like a small balloon.

Just then, Mama peeked in. “Oh! What beautiful art,” Mama said. “What is it for?”

“It is a thank-you gallery,” Mia said. “For Ms. Star, Grandpa, and Bubbles.”

Mama smiled. “That is kind and smart. In the morning, we can take a photo and send it to Ms. Star and Grandpa.”

Bubbles beamed. “We used knowledge and thinking. We solved problems. We helped others feel happy.”

Mia hugged Bubbles close. “Thank you, Bubbles.”

Bubbles whispered, “Thank you, Mia. Thank you, cozy room. Thank you, night breeze. Thank you, crayons.” He looked around and felt his heart grow warm. “Gratitude is noticing good things and saying thank you.”

Mia nodded. “Gratitude is a gift.”

She yawned a big, sleepy yawn. The lamp clicked softer. The night was gentle and sweet. Bubbles snuggled in the crook of Mia’s arm. The pictures fluttered just a little in the breeze, like tiny flags of thanks.

Bubbles closed his sparkly eyes. “Tonight we learned: When we feel grateful, we can show it with words, with art, and with kind actions. We can think, we can share, and we can help.”

Mia whispered, “Thank you for today.”

And the cozy bedroom hummed back, “You’re welcome.”

The End.
