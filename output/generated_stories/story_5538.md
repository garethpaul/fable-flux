# Berry Bear Finds the Beat

Berry was a friendly bear cub with soft brown fur and bright curious eyes. Today, Berry padded into the musical practice room. The room smelled like polished wood and lemon soap. Sunlight made rainbow shapes on the floor. Drums, bells, and a small piano sat in neat rows. A big sign said, “Clean, Play, Share!”

Berry wiggled with joy. “I want to make music!” Berry said.

But oh no! The room was messy. Sticks were scattered. Drum skins were dusty. The piano bench was sticky. A box of shakers had spilled. The floor was a clatter of clinks and clanks.

Berry’s ears drooped. “How can I play if everything is a mess?” Berry asked.

The teacher, Ms. Finch, a kind finch with a red scarf, chirped, “First we do our chores. Then we make music together. We can do it as a team. Let’s think.”

Berry took a deep breath. The room was quiet except for a tiny drip-drop sound by the sink. Berry rubbed paws together. “I can help,” Berry said. “I can use what we have.”

Berry looked around. No big mop. No big duster. Just small things on shelves. Berry’s eyes lit up. “I will be resourceful!”

“So smart,” Ms. Finch chirped. “Use your clever cub brain!”

Berry found a soft scarf hanging on a chair. “This can be a duster,” Berry said. Swish, swish! Berry gently wiped drum skins. Dust flew like little gray clouds. “Ah-choo!” Berry giggled.

Next, Berry looked at the sticky bench. “Hmm,” Berry hummed. The sink went drip, drop, drip. Berry took a clean cloth and a tiny bowl. Berry held the bowl under the drip. Plink, plink! The bowl filled with water. “Aha! Water to clean.” Berry dipped the cloth and wiped the bench. Wipe, wipe, wipe. The bench squeaked clean.

Ms. Finch clapped her wings. “Resourceful and safe. Well done!”

Two friends peeked in. It was Pip the mouse and Tali the turtle. Pip’s whiskers twitched. Tali smiled shyly. “Can we help?” they asked.

“Yes!” Berry said. “We all belong. We all help. We all play.”

Berry looked at the spilled shakers. “We need a plan,” Berry said. “Let’s sort and count. Let’s think step by step.”

- “I will sweep with this paper fan,” Pip squeaked.
- “I can carry small things,” Tali said slowly. “I am steady.”

Berry nodded. “I will be the picker-upper with this bucket.”

Together they sang a little chore song:

“Pick, pack, pat,
We can clean like that.
Sort, scoop, sweep,
Music we will keep!”

Pip fanned gently. Whoosh, whoosh! Little beads rolled into a corner. Tali waddled over and scooped them with a cup. Scoop, plop, scoop, plop. Berry used the bucket to catch the rolling shakers. Clink, clink!

A shaker was broken. Beads leaked like tiny rain. “Oh no,” Pip said.

Berry tapped a chin. Think, think. “Let’s be creative,” Berry said. “We can fix it.”

Berry found a short cardboard tube and some paper circles. Berry also found a ribbon and a bit of tape in a drawer. “This can be a new shaker,” Berry said.

Tali held the tube steady. Pip poured beads in—tink, tink, tink. Berry taped the ends with care and tied a ribbon around the middle. Shake, shake! It made a soft rain sound.

“We made an instrument!” Tali smiled.

Ms. Finch beamed. “You used what you had. That is resourcefulness.”

Soon, the room looked lovely. The drums were shiny. The piano bench was clean. The shakers were in a tidy box with a new homemade shaker on top. The air smelled like lemon and a little like paper and ribbon.

Berry’s heart felt warm. “We did our chores,” Berry said. “Now we can play.”

Ms. Finch handed each friend a part. “Berry on drum. Pip on bell. Tali on shaker. I will count us in.”

Berry picked up two sticks. Tap, tap on the drum. Pip went ding, ding on the bell. Tali shook the new shaker—shhh, shhh, shhh like soft rain. They listened to each other. They smiled.

“One, two, three, play!” Ms. Finch chirped.

The music filled the room. It sounded clear and happy. Berry felt proud. “We used our brains. We used our paws. We used what we had. We made something good,” Berry said.

After the song, Berry hugged friends gently. “Thank you for helping. Everyone can help in a way that fits them.”

Ms. Finch nodded. “Today we learned a big lesson.”

Berry raised a paw and said, “When there is a problem, we can stop, think, and use what we have. We can be resourceful. We can do our chores. Then we can create and play.”

Pip squeaked, “And we can include everyone!”

Tali added, “Slow or fast, big or small, we all belong.”

Berry looked around the bright, clean room. The instruments glowed. The floor shone. The new shaker sparkled with its ribbon. Berry’s ears perked at the happy silence before the next song.

“Let’s make more music,” Berry said.

They all laughed and began again, tapping, dinging, and shhh-shh-shaking with joy.

The End.
