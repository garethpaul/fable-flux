# Kira the Koala Shares the Colors

Kira the koala loved the creative art room at her school. The bright, sunny room smelled like crayons and had colorful paintings hanging on every wall. Today, Kira was excited to paint a picture of her favorite eucalyptus tree.

She walked slowly to the art table, her soft gray fur brushing against the chair as she sat down. "What a beautiful day to make art!" Kira said to herself, taking three deep breaths like her teacher had taught her.

At the table sat two little rabbits, Rosie and Rex. They were both reaching for the same box of paint brushes at the exact same time.

"I need the blue brush!" squeaked Rosie, pulling on one end of the box.

"But I was here first!" cried Rex, tugging on the other end. "I want to paint the sky!"

The box shook back and forth. Paint brushes rattled inside like marbles in a jar. Both rabbits' faces were getting red and scrunchy.

Kira watched quietly, her dark button eyes taking in the whole situation. She could feel her heart beating steady and slow, just like when she hung upside down from tree branches at home.

"Oh my," Kira said gently, standing up from her chair. "It looks like you both really want to paint something special."

Rosie and Rex stopped pulling and looked at the calm koala.

"We both need the blue brush," Rosie explained, her voice still wobbly from being upset.

"And there's only one," Rex added, pointing at the box.

Kira nodded thoughtfully, her fluffy ears twitching. She could smell the sweet scent of watercolors and hear the soft hum of the air conditioner. These sounds always helped her think clearly.

"You know what?" Kira said, sitting down between the two rabbits. "When I have a problem like this, I like to take deep breaths and think of fair solutions. Want to try with me?"

The three friends breathed in slowly through their noses, then out through their mouths. One... two... three times.

"Much better," smiled Kira. "Now, what if we shared? Rex, what are you painting?"

"A big blue sky with fluffy clouds," Rex said, calmer now.

"And Rosie, what about you?"

"A blue ocean with fish swimming," Rosie replied.

Kira's eyes sparkled with an idea. "What if Rex paints his sky first for five minutes, then Rosie paints her ocean for five minutes? We can take turns!"

"But what if I forget my idea?" worried Rosie.

"We can draw a quick sketch first!" suggested Kira. "I have pencils we can all share."

Rex and Rosie looked at each other, then smiled. "That sounds fair!" they said together.

Kira helped them set up a timer that looked like a colorful butterfly. Rex painted his beautiful sky while Rosie sketched her ocean scene. When the timer's wings fluttered, they switched.

"This is so much better than fighting," giggled Rosie as she dipped the blue brush into the paint.

"And more fun too!" agreed Rex, admiring his finished sky.

While they painted, Kira worked on her eucalyptus tree with green and brown brushes. The art room filled with happy humming and the gentle swoosh of brushes on paper.

"Thank you, Kira," said both rabbits when they finished. "You helped us solve our problem!"

Kira hugged her new friends gently. "When we're calm and think about fairness, we can solve almost any problem together. Sharing makes everything more fun!"

The three friends hung their beautiful paintings on the wall, right next to each other, where everyone could see them.

The End.
