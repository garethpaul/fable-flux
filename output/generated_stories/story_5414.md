# Leo Finds His Roar

In a sunny backyard, a brave little lion cub named Leo padded onto the soft grass. The sun felt warm on his fur. The air smelled like fresh flowers and sweet oranges. Birds chirped, “Tweet-tweet!” and a gentle breeze whispered, “Shhh.”

Leo’s family had a big plan. Today was Garden Day! Mama Lion set down a basket of tiny seeds. Papa Lion rolled over a watering can. Big Sister Lila carried small gardening tools that clinked and clanked.

“Let’s make a happy garden,” said Mama. “We’ll plant, water, and watch it grow.”

Leo’s heart thumped with excitement. He wanted to help. He wanted to do a big job.

“I can carry the seeds,” Leo said.

Lila smiled. “You’re small, Leo. Maybe you can watch the tools.”

Leo’s ears drooped. He felt a tiny knot in his tummy. He wanted to say more, but his words felt stuck. He looked at the bright marigold seeds. He looked at the tall watering can. He took a deep breath. The breeze tickled his whiskers.

“I can do it,” Leo said softly.

Mama Lion knelt low so her eyes met Leo’s eyes. “What do you need, little lion?”

Leo reminded himself: I can use my words. I can say what I need.

He lifted his chin. “I want a job that fits me,” he said. “I want to help for real.”

Mama smiled. “That is good self-advocacy, Leo—using your voice to share your needs. Let’s find the right job.”

Leo looked around. The dirt felt cool under his paws. He smelled mint leaves and the bright, peppery scent of tomatoes. He heard the clink of tools and the splash-splash of water. He thought hard.

“I can make the seed lines,” Leo said. “My paws are small. I can make little rows.”

“That sounds responsible,” said Papa. “Let’s try it together.”

Papa showed Leo how to draw a gentle line in the soil. “Not too deep,” he said. “Just a cozy bed for the seeds.”

Leo pressed his paw into the soft earth. “Like this?” he asked.

“Perfect,” said Papa.

Lila came over, her tail swishing. “I didn’t know you could do that, Leo.”

Leo looked up. “I can, and I’m proud. Can I also carry the seed basket if it’s not too heavy?”

Lila lifted the basket. “It’s light,” she said, and handed it to him. “Thank you for telling us what you need.”

Leo felt warm inside. “Thank you for listening,” he said. “I’m grateful.”

They worked as a team. Leo made small lines—swish, swish. Lila dropped in seeds—plip, plip. Mama covered them with soft soil—pat, pat. Papa watered—drip, drop, splish.

A little ladybug landed on Leo’s nose. He giggled. “Hello, tiny helper!”

When they reached the last row, a gust of wind blew. Whoosh! The seed basket tipped, and seeds sprinkled everywhere—on the grass, on the path, even on Leo’s toes.

“Oh no!” said Lila.

Leo’s eyes grew wide. He felt a flutter in his tummy. He wanted to hide. But then he took a slow breath. He remembered his new skill: use words, ask for help, take responsibility.

“I spilled the seeds,” Leo said. “I made a mistake. I am responsible. Can you please help me pick them up?”

Mama nodded. “Thank you for being honest and brave.”

Papa smiled. “We can fix this together.”

Lila clapped. “Teamwork time!”

They knelt close. Leo used his small paws to scoop the tiny seeds—scoop, scoop. Lila held a leaf like a little tray. Papa brushed the path clean. Mama showed Leo which seeds could still be planted.

Soon, the seeds were safe again. The garden rows looked neat and cozy.

Leo sat back and wiped his paws. He felt proud and calm. The sun warmed his face. He could smell the damp earth and tiny green plants waking up.

“I did it,” Leo said. “I used my voice. I asked for help. I fixed my mistake.”

Mama hugged him. “That is self-advocacy, gratitude, and responsibility all together.”

Lila bumped her shoulder to his. “And teamwork!”

Papa handed Leo the watering can. “Would you like to give the last gentle pour?”

“Yes, please,” said Leo.

He tilted the can. Water sparkled in the sunlight and whispered onto the soil—shhh, shhh. The garden looked happy.

Later, they sat on a picnic blanket. They ate orange slices that dripped sweet juice. Bees hummed softly by the flowers. The sky was bright blue, and the backyard felt safe and sunny.

Mama asked, “Leo, what did you learn today?”

Leo licked a bit of juice from his whiskers. “I learned to say what I need. I learned to be honest when I make a mistake. I learned to thank my team. When I use my voice, people can help me, and I can help them.”

Everyone cheered, “Roar for Leo!”

Leo gave a small, happy roar: “Rooaaar!” It wasn’t loud, but it was brave and true. He felt strong and kind.

The garden glowed softly in the sun, and Leo knew he would watch over it with care. He would speak up when he needed help, say thank you when others helped, and do his part every day.

The End.
