# Panda on the Rooftop Garden

On top of a tall building, there was a soft, green rooftop garden. The air smelled like fresh leaves and warm sunshine. Bees hummed, flowers nodded, and a tiny wind chime tinkled, tinkle-tinkle. A patient panda named Pema liked to sit there every afternoon.

Pema was fluffy and gentle. She loved to water the plants with her small silver watering can. “Sip, sip,” went the water. “Thank you,” she whispered to the flowers. Pema liked quiet. She liked slow. She liked safe.

One day, a little red fox named Fizz zoomed up the stairs. The door clanked. “Bang!” Fizz burst onto the rooftop. He ran fast, fast, fast. His paws skittered on the tiles. “I am a rocket!” he shouted. “ZOOM!”

Pema blinked. Her ears twitched. “Hello, Fizz,” she said in a calm voice. “It is nice to see you.”

Fizz did not hear. He hopped on the low garden wall. He waved his tail. “I can jump! I can spin!” He spun and wobbled. The wind blew his fur. The wall was near the edge.

Pema’s heart thump-thumped. She took a slow breath. “Safety first,” she told herself. “Use calm words. Use kind eyes.”

She walked closer. “Fizz,” she said softly, “the wall is not safe. My body feels tense when I see you there. Let’s keep our bodies safe.”

Fizz frowned. “I want to play big!” he said. He jumped again. His foot slipped a little. “Eep!” He caught himself and laughed, but his laugh sounded shaky.

Pema set down her watering can. “Let’s make a plan,” she said. “We can play big. We can also be safe. Will you listen?”

Fizz shuffled his paws. He looked at the plants. He looked at Pema’s warm, steady eyes. “Maybe,” he said. “But I feel squiggly inside.”

Pema nodded. “I hear you,” she said. “Your body has big energy. That can feel squiggly. My body likes slow. We are different, and that is okay.”

Fizz’s ears perked up. “You hear me?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Pema. “I want to understand. When I feel big feelings, I stop and breathe.” She put a paw on her belly. “Smell the flower.” She breathed in. “Blow the windmill.” She breathed out. “Do you want to try with me?”

Fizz looked at the bright yellow sunflowers. He took a little sniff. “Smell the flower,” he said. Then he blew out, slow. “Whooo.” His tail twitched less.

“How does your body feel now?” asked Pema.

Fizz thought. “Less bouncy,” he said. “But I still want to play big.”

Pema smiled. “We can make a play zone,” she said. She pointed to a soft mat in the middle of the garden. “Big moves here. Safe feet, safe paws. No climbing the edge. That is a boundary. Boundaries help our bodies stay safe.”

“What is a boundary?” asked Fizz.

“It is a friendly line,” said Pema. “It says: This is okay. That is not okay. It keeps us safe and kind.”

Fizz nodded. “Okay. Big moves on the mat!”

They moved to the mat. The grass tickled their toes. The mat was springy. “Boing!” went Fizz as he hopped. Pema clapped. “I can cheer,” she said. “I like slow, but I can cheer for you.”

Fizz did a gentle spin. He giggled. “Watch me, Pema!” he called.

“I am watching,” said Pema. “I see your happy paws. I hear your happy laugh. I care about your feelings.”

Then Fizz’s tail bumped the watering can. Water splashed. “Splash!” Pema’s fur got wet and cold. She gasped. Her cheeks felt hot. She felt a small grumpy bubble in her tummy.

Fizz froze. “Oh no,” he whispered. “Are you mad?”

Pema put a paw on her belly. She took a breath. “Smell the flower.” In. “Blow the windmill.” Out. The grumpy bubble got smaller. “I feel upset,” she said, “because my fur is wet. I need a towel. I also know it was an accident.”

Fizz’s eyes were big. “I am sorry,” he said. “I did not look where my tail was. How can I help?”

“Thank you for saying sorry,” said Pema. “Please bring me the towel.”

Fizz brought a soft blue towel. Pema patted her fur. Pat, pat. “That feels better,” she said. “Next time, let’s look with our eyes and think with our tails.”

Fizz giggled. “Think with our tails!”

Pema smiled. “You can ask, ‘Is this okay?’ and I can say, ‘Yes,’ or ‘No,’ or ‘Please change.’ That is using words and boundaries.”

Fizz nodded. “Is it okay if I do three big jumps on the mat?”

“Yes,” said Pema. “Three is okay.”

Fizz counted, “One, two, three!” He landed with a soft thump. He looked at Pema. “How do you feel now?”

“I feel calm and proud,” said Pema. “You are being safe and kind. You listened to my feelings. I listened to yours.”

The sun felt warm on their faces. The wind chime sang. Tinkle-tinkle. Bees hummed by the flowers. The rooftop felt happy and safe.

Pema poured more water. “Sip, sip,” went the can. Fizz held the plants steady with gentle paws. They worked like a team.

Pema said, “Today we learned to use calm breaths, kind words, and clear boundaries. We kept our bodies safe. We cared about each other’s feelings. That is empathy.”

Fizz smiled wide. “Empathy means I try to feel what you feel,” he said. “And I make choices that are kind.”

“Yes,” said Pema. “That is the best play.”

They finished their garden time. Then they sat side by side, sharing a sweet pear. It was juicy and cool. They crunched and smiled. The city below hummed softly.

Fizz said, “Thank you for being patient.”

Pema said, “Thank you for listening.”

They looked at the sky and felt peaceful.

The End.
