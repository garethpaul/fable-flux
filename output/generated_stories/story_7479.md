# Mango the Monkey Finds His Calm

The warm kitchen smelled like sweet bananas and cinnamon toast. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. Pots clinked softly, and a kettle hummed a happy tune.

Mango the monkey sat on a stool by the big wooden table. His tail curled like a question mark. He was small and brave, with bright eyes and soft brown fur. Today, Mango wanted to help make banana muffins.

“I can do it!” Mango said. He puffed his cheeks and stood tall. “I am a courageous helper.”

His friend Pip, a tiny green parrot, fluttered down to the counter. “I’ll help too!” chirped Pip. “We can be a team. One, two, me and you!”

Auntie May, who wore a red-and-white apron, smiled. “We’ll need good listening and slow hands,” she said. “And we will use our calm voices.”

Mango nodded fast. “I can be calm,” he said. His toes wiggled with excitement. The bowl shone like a little silver moon. The yellow bananas smelled extra sweet.

Auntie May set out the things they needed. Flour like soft snow. Sugar like sparkly sand. Eggs in a smooth brown carton. A little spoon of cinnamon. And a tall cup of milk.

“First,” Auntie May said, “we mash the bananas.”

“I mash! I mash!” Mango grabbed the fork and poked the bananas. Squish! Squash! Banana mush slid and plopped into the bowl. Mango giggled. “This is fun!”

Pip danced on the counter. “Tap, tap, tap!” He pecked the edge of the bowl like a tiny drum.

“Next,” said Auntie May, “we pour the milk.”

Mango reached for the tall cup. His hands felt buzzy. His tail swished. He wanted to pour it all at once. Fast. He wanted to be the best pourer ever.

“Careful, Mango,” Pip chirped. “Slow hands. Calm minds.”

“I can do it,” Mango said again. He lifted the cup. The milk wobbled. The cup shook. Plip! Plop! Splash!

Milk spilled over the bowl and onto the table. It ran like a little white river. It dripped onto Mango’s toes. The floor felt cold and wet. The sound was a big sploosh.

Mango’s cheeks got hot. His tummy felt tight. “Oh no,” he whispered. “I messed up.”

Auntie May picked up a towel. She did not yell. “Spills happen,” she said softly. “Let’s clean together.”

Pip fluttered near Mango’s ear. “We can fix it. One wipe at a time.”

Mango’s heart thumped. He wanted to hide. He wanted to throw the cup. He wanted to cry. His tail lashed the air.

He stopped. He remembered Auntie May’s words: calm voices, slow hands.

“I need a pause,” Mango said in a small voice.

Auntie May nodded. “A pause is a smart tool.”

Mango put the cup down. He placed his palm on his belly. “Slow like a turtle,” he told himself. He took a long breath in. Smell the cinnamon. He breathed out. Blow out the birthday candle. In and out. In and out. He counted softly. “One… two… three.”

His tummy softened. His cheeks cooled. His tail went still. The kitchen felt warm again, not too hot. He heard the kettle hum. He smelled the bananas. Pip’s feathers brushed his shoulder like a little hug.

“I feel better,” Mango said. “I can try again.”

Together, they wiped the milk. Squish went the towel. Swish went the cloth. The table was dry and shiny once more.

“Now,” Auntie May said, “we pour with teamwork. Pip, you watch. Mango, you hold the cup with two hands. I will steady the bowl.”

Mango nodded. He wrapped both hands around the cup. Pip counted, “One… two… pour!”

Mango tilted the cup slowly. The milk made a soft glug-glug sound. The white stream went right into the bowl. Not a drop on the table.

“You did it!” Pip cheered. “Slow hands!”

Mango grinned. Pride felt warm, like a small sun in his chest. “Thank you for helping,” he told his friends.

They cracked the eggs. Tap, tap—crack! Mango used gentle thumbs. Pip leaned close and said, “You’ve got this.” They added flour and cinnamon, and Mango stirred in circles. Big circle, little circle. His arms moved like a dance. Auntie May hummed. Pip kept the beat. Tap-tap, stir-stir.

The muffin tray went into the oven. The kitchen filled with a cozy smell. Banana, cinnamon, and a little bit of brave. Mango’s tummy rumbled with a happy growl.

While they waited, Mango practiced his calm. He sat on the stool and breathed. In like the smell of muffins. Out like blowing bubbles. He wiggled his toes, then stilled them. He shook his hands, then put them quiet in his lap. Pip sat beside him and matched his breaths. Auntie May smiled at their teamwork.

Ding! The oven sang. Auntie May took out golden muffins. Steam curled up like tiny ghosts. She set one on a small blue plate. Mango tore it open. Warm. Soft. Sweet.

He took a bite. “Mmm!”

Auntie May poured a little milk into a small cup. Mango held it with two hands. He took a sip. No spill.

“I learned a new trick,” Mango said. “When I feel wiggly, I pause and breathe. I can try again with slow hands.”

Pip fluffed his feathers. “That’s self-regulation,” he chirped proudly. “You noticed, you paused, you chose.”

Mango smiled. “And we did it together. Teamwork and calm make good muffins.”

They clinked their cups—clink!—and shared the warm treats.

The End.
