# Fawn’s Feelings Helper

In a comfortable nursery with soft rugs and sunny windows, a gentle deer fawn named Fawn stood by a basket of toys. The room smelled like warm soap and orange slices. Outside, a breeze made the curtains wave hello. Inside, tiny chairs, a cozy reading nook, and a big feelings chart waited on the wall.

“Good morning, Fawn,” said Miss Lila, the caregiver, with a kind smile. “Today, we tidy toys, share snack, and have story time.”

Fawn smiled back. “Good morning!” Fawn’s hooves were small and careful. Fawn loved quiet songs and gentle hugs. But today, Fawn’s tummy felt fluttery, like a butterfly.

“Friends,” said Miss Lila, clapping soft. “We need a helper to pass out nap blankets later. Who wants to volunteer?”

Fawn’s ears perked up. Fawn liked helping. Helping felt warm, like holding a cup of cocoa. Fawn raised a hoof a little bit. But then Fawn saw a big stack of puzzles and blocks on the floor. There were so many. Fawn’s hoof went down.

“I can,” said Bear Cub, waving a paw.

“That’s kind,” said Miss Lila. “Thank you, Bear.”

Fawn’s tummy fluttered more. Fawn wanted to help too, but Fawn felt shy. Fawn didn’t know how to say, “Me too, please.”

“Let’s tidy,” Miss Lila said. “We sing while we tidy.” She sang, “Pick up, pick up, one by one,” and the room filled with humming voices.

Fawn picked up a soft ball and smelled its clean rubber. Fawn stacked blocks, click, click, click. But Fawn’s eyes got wet. A little drop rolled down Fawn’s cheek.

Miss Lila knelt. “Fawn, I see tears. What are you feeling?”

Fawn pressed a hoof to their chest. “I feel… fluttery. I want to help. I don’t know how to say it.”

“That is a big feeling,” said Miss Lila softly. “Let’s take a slow breath together. In through the nose… out through the mouth.” They breathed. Fawn felt the air cool in and warm out. The flutter slowed.

Miss Lila pointed to the feelings chart. It had little faces: happy, sad, brave, shy, mad, proud. “Which face looks like your feeling?”

Fawn touched the shy face, then the hopeful face. “I feel shy and… wanting.”

“That’s brave to say,” Miss Lila said. “When you want to help, you can use your strong voice. That is called being assertive. Not loud, not mean—just clear. Try this: ‘I want to help, please.’”

Fawn nodded. “I… I want to help, please.”

“Wonderful,” Miss Lila said. “Let’s find a way to help right now.”

At the snack table, Apple Squirrel was stacking cups but had too many. The cups wobbled and clacked. “Oh no,” said Apple Squirrel. “My paws are tiny!”

Fawn took one breath. “I want to help, please,” Fawn said, kind and clear.

Apple Squirrel smiled. “Thank you, Fawn.” The cups made a soft pop as they stacked together. Fawn felt a warm glow. Helping made the room feel even sunnier.

“Friends, hand-wash time,” Miss Lila called. The sink water was warm and bubbly. Fawn rubbed hooves together—front, back, between—singing the hand-wash song. The soap smelled like lemons. Bubbles popped like tiny kisses. “All clean,” Fawn said, proud. Healthy hands help everyone feel good.

At snack, carrot sticks crunched. “Crunch, crunch,” went Bear Cub. “Yum,” said Apple Squirrel. Fawn chewed slow and felt calm. “Chewing slow helps my belly,” Fawn whispered.

After snack, Miss Lila held up a basket. “We still need a blanket helper for nap time. Bear, you volunteered before, but we can have two helpers.”

Fawn’s ears stood tall. Fawn remembered the feeling words. Fawn placed a hoof on the table and made eye contact with Miss Lila. “I want to help, please,” Fawn said, steady and clear.

Miss Lila’s face lit up. “Thank you, Fawn. That is assertive and kind. Bear, can you and Fawn share the job?”

Bear Cub nodded. “Yes! We can be Blanket Buddies.”

Fawn giggled. “Blanket Buddies!”

When nap time came, the room turned soft. Lights dimmed, and a lavender scent floated in the air. Fawn and Bear Cub carried blankets. The blankets were fluffy and cool to the touch. “Here you go,” Fawn said to Bunny, placing a blanket like a cloud. Bunny smiled and whispered, “Thank you.”

Fawn felt a big, calm feeling. The fluttery tummy was gone. Fawn’s heart felt full, like a basket with just-right toys.

“Fawn,” Miss Lila whispered when all were tucked in, “how do you feel now?”

Fawn thought. “I feel proud. I used my words. I helped. I was kind to my body with clean hands and slow bites. I was kind to my friends.”

Miss Lila nodded. “You named your feelings. You volunteered to help. You used an assertive voice. That is good communication.”

Fawn rested on a small pillow. Outside, the breeze still waved the curtains. Inside, soft snores and tiny sighs rose and fell like gentle music. Fawn closed their eyes, feeling safe, helpful, and brave.

When rest time was over, Miss Lila gathered the friends. “What did we learn today?”

Fawn lifted a hoof. “When my tummy flutters, I can breathe slow. I can point to a feeling. I can say, ‘I want to help, please.’ Helping feels good. Sharing jobs is kind. Washing hands and chewing slow keeps us healthy.”

Apple Squirrel clapped. Bear Cub clapped, too. Everyone smiled.

Miss Lila said, “Those are wonderful lessons.”

Fawn’s tail flicked happily. “I am a Feelings Helper,” Fawn said.

“Yes,” Miss Lila agreed. “And your strong, kind voice helps everyone.”

The End.
