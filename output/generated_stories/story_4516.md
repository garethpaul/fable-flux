# Tilly Turtle’s Guest Room Groove

Tilly was a shy turtle with a small, shiny shell. She was staying in Aunt Mia’s house for the weekend. Aunt Mia smiled and said, “Tilly, you can sleep in the guest room.”

The guest room was cozy and quiet. The curtains were soft and blue. A little lamp made a warm, golden glow. A fluffy rug felt like clouds under Tilly’s toes. Tilly whispered, “Thank you,” and tucked herself in.

In the morning, Tilly peeked out from her shell. “Good morning, room,” she said. But when she looked in her bag, her eyes grew wide. “Oh no,” she gasped softly. “I forgot my music box!” The tiny music box helped Tilly feel brave. She loved to listen and do a slow, happy dance.

Without her music, Tilly felt shy and wiggly inside. Her tummy fluttered. Her feet felt still, like stones. She wanted to dance, but she didn’t know how to start.

Aunt Mia knocked gently. Knock, knock. “Tilly, would you like to play?” she asked.

“I want to dance,” Tilly said, peeking from her shell. “But I forgot my music. I feel too shy.”

Aunt Mia sat on the edge of the bed. “Hmm,” she said kindly. “When we have a problem, we can use what we have. That is called being resourceful.”

“Re… source… ful?” Tilly tried the word.

“It means we can find new ways,” Aunt Mia said. “Let’s look around.”

They looked at the guest room together. The window was open a little. A soft breeze came in. Rustle, rustle went the curtains. The rug made a gentle swish when Tilly moved her feet. The lamp’s click made a tiny tick sound when Aunt Mia turned it on and off. Click. Tick. Click. Tick.

“I hear sounds,” Tilly whispered.

“Yes!” Aunt Mia nodded. “We can make our own music.”

Tilly stood very still and listened. The clock on the wall said, “tick-tock, tick-tock.” The window made the curtains whisper, “whoosh, whoosh.” Tilly began to smile.

Aunt Mia tapped two wooden hangers together. Tap, tap. Tap, tap. “These can be drums.”

Tilly picked up a small box of tissues. She shook it gently. Swish-a-swish! “This is a shaker,” she said.

Her shy feeling moved away a little. Her shell didn’t feel so tight. “I can try,” she said in a small voice.

“Let’s make a song,” said Aunt Mia. “I’ll tap a beat. You add sounds. Then you can dance.”

Tap, tap. Tap, tap. went the hangers. Tick-tock, tick-tock, said the clock. Whoosh, whoosh, sang the curtains. Swish-a-swish, swish-a-swish, went Tilly’s tissue box.

Tilly’s toes began to tingle. The rug was soft and warm. She moved one foot forward. Swish. She moved the other foot to the side. Swish. She swayed her shell left. She swayed her shell right.

“I’m dancing!” Tilly giggled. “I made music from the room!”

Aunt Mia clapped softly. Clap, clap. “You are very resourceful, Tilly.”

Tilly’s cheeks felt warm and happy. She twirled slowly, like a leaf. Her shy tummy flutter turned into a giggle bubble. She hummed a little tune. “La, la, la.”

“May I try a new sound?” Tilly asked. She looked around. She saw a glass of water on the night stand. She dipped her finger in and rubbed the rim. Hmmm-mmmm, sang the glass.

“Ooh!” Tilly’s eyes sparkled. “The glass sings!”

Aunt Mia smiled. “You found another sound. You are using what you have.”

Tilly put the sounds together. Tap, tap. Tick-tock. Whoosh, whoosh. Swish-a-swish. Hmmm-mmmm. She moved slow and smooth. She bent and stretched. She did a tiny hop. Her shell made a soft thump on the rug. Thump.

“I feel brave now,” Tilly said. “I can dance with my own music.”

Just then, a guest cat named Coco peeked in. “What’s that lovely sound?” Coco purred.

“It’s our guest room band!” Tilly said. “Do you want to dance?”

Coco swished her tail. “Yes!” She padded in, light as a feather. Coco tapped her paw on a small box. Tap, tap, tap. Tilly laughed and danced in a circle with Coco. Aunt Mia tapped the hangers in time.

Soon the whole guest room buzzed with gentle music. The breeze felt cool on Tilly’s face. The lamp glowed like sunshine. The rug cushioned every step. The clock kept steady time.

When the song ended, Tilly took a deep, happy breath. “Thank you, guest room,” she said. “Thank you, sounds.”

Aunt Mia gave Tilly a hug. “You solved your problem by being resourceful. You used the things around you to make music. You found a way to dance.”

Tilly nodded. “When I feel shy, I can look and listen. I can use what I have.”

“That’s a wonderful lesson,” Aunt Mia said.

Tilly tucked into her cozy bed that night with a smile. She knew she could be brave and creative. With ears to hear and eyes to see, she could make music anywhere, and dance her own dance.

The End.
