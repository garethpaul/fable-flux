# Koala Kori Shares the Cookies

In a warm kitchen, the sun made little gold squares on the floor. The air smelled like sweet vanilla and cinnamon. A calm koala named Kori stood on a safe, sturdy step-stool by the counter. Kori wore a tiny apron with a big, blue pocket.

“Breathe in. Breathe out,” Kori whispered softly. “I am calm. I can help.”

Kori’s friends were coming to play: Bibi the bunny, Tiko the turtle, and Momo the mouse. Kori wanted to make cookies for everyone. The bowl was big, and the spoon was shiny. The butter was soft, and the sugar felt like tiny sand.

“Tap, tap,” went the eggs. “Swish, swish,” went the spoon.

Kori hummed a little song. “Mix, mix, swirl, swirl.” The dough smelled so yummy.

Soon, Bibi hopped in. “What can I do?” Bibi asked, ears wiggling.

“You can pour the flour,” Kori said. “We can all help. We can all take turns.”

Tiko slowly slid in. “How can I help?” Tiko asked in a gentle, slow voice.

“You can add the chocolate chips,” Kori smiled. “One scoop for the bowl, and none for the floor.”

Momo zipped in with fast feet. “I want to help! I want to help now!”

Kori took a calm breath. “We will have turns,” Kori said kindly. “We will be fair.”

They all stood by the warm oven. “Dip, drop,” went the spoon, making little dough circles on the baking tray.

“I want to make a big cookie for me!” Momo squeaked.

Bibi twitched her nose. “I want extra chips in mine.”

Tiko blinked. “I am slow. Will I get a turn?”

Kori felt a little swirl inside. The kitchen got noisy. It was hard to think. The warm air pressed like a soft blanket. Kori put a paw on the apron pocket and took a calm breath.

“Breathe in. Breathe out,” Kori said. “Let’s be fair. Fair means everyone gets a turn. Fair means we share.”

“How?” asked Momo, bouncing.

“We can use a simple plan,” Kori said, tapping the spoon. “We will count to three. Each friend gets three drops of dough. Then we switch. Like this!”

Kori showed them. “One, two, three,” and passed the spoon to Bibi.

Bibi smiled. “One, two, three,” and passed it to Tiko.

Tiko took his time. “One… two… three,” and passed it to Momo.

Momo zipped. “One, two, three!” and passed it back to Kori.

Everyone helped. Everyone had a turn. The tray filled up with little round cookies.

Then, ding! The oven sang a tiny bell. Warm smells floated out like soft clouds. The cookies were golden with melty chips. Kori used a safe mitt. “Sss,” went the hot air. They waited. Waiting was hard.

“I want to eat one now,” Momo said, nose twitching.

Kori shook a gentle head. “We wait so no one gets a burn,” Kori said. “Waiting is part of being kind to our bodies.”

They all peered at the cookies. “They look like little suns,” Bibi said.

“They smell like hugs,” Tiko added.

When the cookies cooled, Kori put them on a plate. There were twelve cookies. Kori counted slowly, touching each one. “One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine, ten, eleven, twelve.”

“How do we share?” asked Bibi.

“Let’s be fair again,” Kori said. “There are four friends. We can each have the same number. We can each have three.”

Kori began to give out cookies. “One for Bibi, one for Tiko, one for Momo, one for Kori.” The plate went around and around until each friend had three cookies.

Momo’s eyes were big. “But I am very hungry,” Momo said. “Can I have more?”

Kori was quiet for a moment. The kitchen hummed with the low sound of the fridge. The plate was empty now. Kori took a calm breath.

“Let’s think of a fair way,” Kori said. “We can volunteer to help with more cookies. If we work, we can make more. If everyone helps, we can all have a little extra later.”

“I will help wash the bowl!” Bibi said, hopping toward the sink.

“I will wipe the table,” Tiko said, finding a soft cloth.

“I can carry the spoons,” Momo said, standing tall. “I can help by putting them away.”

Kori smiled. “Thank you. Helping is called volunteering. It feels good to help.”

They each did their job. Splash, splash, went the water. Wipe, wipe, went the cloth. Clink, clink, went the spoons. Kori mixed a small, new batch. This time, they used the same turn plan. “One, two, three,” around the circle.

Soon, there were four tiny cookies, one for each friend. Kori put them on a plate.

“Now we can have one more,” Kori said. “This is fair, because we all helped.”

They each ate a warm, tiny cookie. “Mmm,” went Bibi. “Yum,” went Momo. “So good,” said Tiko. Kori smiled and took a calm breath. The kitchen felt happy and safe.

Kori looked at the friends. “Today we learned something,” Kori said. “Fair means we share and take turns. Fair means we all help. When we are calm and kind, we can make a good plan.”

Momo nodded. “I like fair.”

Bibi clapped. “I like helping.”

Tiko patted the table. “I like being calm.”

Kori hugged each friend. “We can be fair, we can volunteer, and we can do things on our own too. That is independence. And when we breathe and think, that is mindfulness.”

They all took one quiet breath together. “Breathe in. Breathe out.”

The warm kitchen glowed. The friends felt full, proud, and kind.

The End.
