# Momo’s Quiet Chat in the Study Corner

Momo was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and tiny pink paws. Momo loved the study corner. It was a cozy spot with a small desk, a sunny window, and a big basket of crayons. The lamp made a warm, yellow glow. The air smelled like paper and lemon soap. It was so calm.

Each morning, Momo whispered, “Good morning, study corner,” and the room felt happy. Today, Momo wanted to make a colorful chart to practice letters.

“Tap, tap,” went Momo’s paws on the paper.
“Scritch, scratch,” went the crayon.

Just then, a cool breeze whooshed through the window. The papers lifted. One sheet flew up, then another, like little birds!

“Oh no!” said Momo. “My chart!”

Momo’s whiskers wiggled. Momo felt a flutter in the tummy. The room sounded loud for a moment: flutter-flap, flap-flutter. The crayons rolled and clicked.

Momo took a slow breath in. One… two… three.
Momo breathed out, soft and warm. One… two… three.

“I can be calm,” Momo whispered. “I can use my words. I can ask for help.”

Momo peeked around. In the study corner, there were friends. Pip the paperclip sat on the desk. Rula the ruler leaned near a book. Nib the pencil rested by the lamp, with a sharp tip and a kind smile.

Momo said, “Friends, the wind took my papers. I feel a little worried. Can you help me, please?”

Pip clicked. “I can clip the papers together!”

Rula said, “I can show where they slid.”

Nib said, “I can write numbers on each page, one to five, so we know the order.”

Momo’s ears lifted. “Thank you. That helps me feel better.”

Together, they listened. Rustle, rustle. One paper hid under the chair. Swish, swish. One paper slid by the rug. Momo crawled on soft toes. The rug felt fuzzy. The floor felt cool. The sun made the desk warm on Momo’s back.

“I found one!” said Momo.
“I found another!” said Rula.

Momo said, “Great job, team!”

But then, a big sheet was stuck by the window. The breeze still whooshed. The paper flapped like a little flag.

Momo felt another tummy flutter. “I feel a bit scared to go near the windy window,” Momo said.

Nib nodded. “It’s okay to feel scared. Let’s think of a plan.”

Momo took another slow breath. In… out. “I will say what I need. I need the window to be less windy,” Momo said.

Momo called, “Excuse me, Window. May we close you a little, please? The wind is too strong for my papers.”

The window made a tiny creak, like it understood. Momo pulled the handle just a bit. The whoosh became a soft whisper. “Hoo… hoo…”

“Thank you, Window,” said Momo.

Momo reached for the last paper. The edge tickled Momo’s paw. “Got it!” Momo laughed. “It feels smooth and cool!”

Pip clicked the stack tight. “All safe!”
Rula lined them up. “All straight!”
Nib wrote, “1, 2, 3, 4, 5,” in neat, gentle marks.

Momo smiled. “We did it because we talked and worked together. Thank you for listening to my words.”

Now it was time to make the chart. Momo said, “I will say my plan out loud, so we all know.”

Momo pointed. “First, I draw big letters. Next, I color. Last, I hang the chart with sticky tape.”

Pip said, “Clear plan!”
Rula said, “Ready to help!”
Nib said, “I will be careful and slow.”

They began. Scritch, scratch went Nib. Swish, swash went the crayons. Momo hummed a tiny song. The lamp’s warm light made the colors glow: bright red, sunny yellow, calm blue, soft green.

Suddenly, the tape roll slipped and stuck to Momo’s whiskers. “Eep!” Momo giggled. “This is tricky.”

Momo felt a small pinch of worry. Then Momo remembered: be calm, use words, keep trying.

“I need help,” Momo said. “The tape is on my whiskers. Please peel it slowly.”

Rula and Pip worked together. Peel, peel. The tape came free without a tug.

“Thank you,” said Momo. “I felt worried, but I asked for help and stayed gentle.”

At last, the chart hung on the wall. It looked bright and happy. The letters stood tall. The colors smiled.

Momo clapped tiny paws. “We did it!”

Nib said, “How do you feel now?”

Momo put a paw on the chest. “I feel calm. I feel proud. When things were hard, I used my words. I asked kindly. I listened. I kept trying. That helped me be brave.”

The study corner felt cozy again. The lamp hummed. The papers were safe. The room smelled sweet and clean.

Momo whispered, “Thank you, friends. Talking helps. Calm helps. Trying again helps.”

Then Momo added, “Next time the wind comes, we will talk, make a plan, and do our best.”

All the friends agreed. “Yes!”

And every morning after that, Momo still said, “Good morning, study corner,” and also said, “Let’s use our words today.”

The End.
