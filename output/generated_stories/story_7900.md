# Joey Jumps to Sparkly Clean

The sun was warm and bright in the sunny backyard. A joyful kangaroo joey named Kip bounced-bounced on the soft grass. The grass tickled his toes. The flowers smelled sweet, like honey. Birds sang, “Chirp-chirp!” and the breeze felt gentle on Kip’s fur.

“I want to make a mud castle!” Kip cheered. He saw a puddle by the garden. It was shiny and brown like chocolate milk. He giggled and jumped in. Sploosh! Mud splattered on his belly. Splash! It dotted his cheeks. Kip clapped his paws. “It feels squishy!”

Mama Roo peeked out the back door. “Kip,” she said kindly, “remember our hygiene rules. Clean body, clean paws, clean face.”

Kip puffed his cheeks. “But I’m not done. My castle needs a tall tower!”

Mama Roo smiled. “I respect your playtime. How about a compromise?”

Kip blinked. “Com-pro-mise?”

“It means we both get something we want,” Mama Roo said. “You can build your tower. Then you show initiative and wash up by yourself.”

Kip thought. He tapped his muddy paw on his chin. “Okay! I’ll make the tower, then I’ll wash. That’s a good plan!”

He rolled mud into a tall, wobbly tower. He used leaves for flags and sticks for windows. “Ta-da!” he sang. The tower looked grand and silly. Mud squished between his toes. His fur felt sticky and heavy. “Ew,” he giggled, “I’m a muddy muffin!”

Mama Roo pointed to the wash station by the garden hose. There was a small step stool, a bubbly soap pump, a soft towel, and a tiny brush for paws. The soap smelled like oranges. The towel felt fluffy like a cloud.

Kip stood tall. “I can do it,” he said. He took initiative. He turned the hose knob slowly. Water hissed and splashed. “It’s cold!” he squeaked, hopping back. He took a deep breath. “I can try again.” He tested with his paw. “Warm now,” he said when Mama Roo turned the knob a little more.

Kip hummed. “Wash, wash, wash,” he sang. He made bubbles in his paws. “Squish-squish, scrub-scrub!” The soap made tiny rainbows. He rubbed his paws, front and back. He cleaned between his fingers. “Hello, clean paws!” he giggled.

He washed his face with gentle circles. “Soft and sweet,” he said, smelling the orange soap. He wiped his nose. “Boop!” He scrubbed his belly, his knees, and his tail tip. “Don’t forget behind the ears,” Mama Roo reminded.

Kip lifted his ears. “Scrub, scrub, little ears!” He rinsed until the water ran clear. The mud swirled away like brown clouds. He shook his fur. “Drip-drip, drop!”

Mama Roo handed him the towel. “Pat, don’t rub,” she said.

“Pat-pat-pat,” Kip repeated, pressing the fluffy towel on his cheeks and paws. He looked at his reflection in the shiny watering can. “Sparkly!” His fur looked bright. His smile looked big.

Just then, his friend Dot the ladybug buzzed by and landed on a leaf. “Hello, Kip,” Dot said. “Play with me?”

Kip glanced at the muddy puddle and then at his clean paws. He wanted to jump again, but he also remembered the plan. He showed respect to himself, to Mama Roo, and to Dot.

“I want to stay clean,” Kip said. “Let’s play a clean game.” He looked around. “We can make a leaf train! Or bounce on the dry grass!”

Dot nodded. “I like the leaf train,” she said.

Kip picked big, dry leaves. He lined them up in a row. “Choo-choo!” he called. He and Dot pushed the leaf train around the yard. The birds sang along. The sun warmed their backs. Kip’s clean paws felt light and comfy.

Mama Roo watched with a happy smile. “Kip, you took initiative to wash. You used a good compromise. And you showed respect by keeping your promise.”

Kip stood tall. “Clean feels good,” he said. He sniffed his fur. “I smell like oranges!”

Dot buzzed a circle. “I like your clean hug,” she giggled as Kip gave a gentle cuddle.

When playtime ended, Kip said, “I have one more idea.” He set a small basket by the hose. He put in the soap, the towel, and the brush. He made a little sign with a stick and paper. He drew a big bubble and a happy face. “This is our Wash-Up Station,” he announced. “Friends can use it after playing.”

Mama Roo clapped softly. “That’s smart initiative, Kip. You help others stay healthy.”

Kip nodded. “Hygiene means wash your body, wash your paws, wash your face,” he said proudly. “It keeps us safe. It shows respect for our friends, our toys, and our home.”

The sun began to dip, and the backyard glowed golden. Kip bounced once, twice, then hugged Mama Roo. “Thank you for the compromise,” he said.

“Thank you for keeping the promise,” Mama Roo replied.

Kip smiled. “I can play and be clean. I can choose clean games. I can wash all by myself.”

He took a deep, happy breath. The air smelled like grass and oranges and a little bit like sunshine. Kip whispered, “Sparkly clean feels like a hug.”

The End.
