# Ollie's Colorful Calm

Ollie the otter loved to play in the bright, cheerful art room at his school. The walls were covered with colorful paintings, and shelves held jars of sparkly paint, soft brushes, and rolls of paper that crinkled when touched.

"Today we're making friendship pictures!" announced Ms. Robin, their kind teacher. "Everyone will work together to create something beautiful."

Ollie's whiskers twitched with excitement. He loved art time! But as soon as the other animals started gathering around the art table, things got very busy and loud.

"I want the red paint!" squeaked Penny the mouse.

"No, I need it first!" chattered Charlie the chipmunk.

"Move over! I can't reach the brushes!" called Benny the bear.

Soon, everyone was reaching and grabbing. Paint jars clinked together, brushes scattered across the table, and voices got louder and louder. Ollie felt his tummy get tight and fluttery. The noise made his ears hurt, and he couldn't think clearly.

"Oh no," whispered Ollie to himself. "This doesn't feel good at all."

He watched as Penny accidentally knocked over a jar of blue paint. SPLASH! It spilled across Charlie's paper, making him very upset.

"My picture is ruined!" Charlie cried.

Ollie's heart felt sad seeing his friend so worried. He took a deep breath, just like his mama had taught him when things felt overwhelming. The sweet smell of the art supplies filled his nose, and he felt a little calmer.

"Wait, everyone!" Ollie called out in his gentle voice. "I have an idea that might help us all."

The other animals turned to look at him, their faces still worried and frustrated.

"What if we take turns?" Ollie suggested softly. "We could make a plan together, so everyone gets what they need."

Ms. Robin smiled warmly. "That sounds like a wonderful idea, Ollie. How do you think we could do that?"

Ollie's dark eyes sparkled as he thought. "Well, we could put all the paint jars in the middle where everyone can see them. Then we could take turns picking one color at a time. That way, nobody has to worry about not getting their turn."

"And," added Ollie, getting more excited, "we could share the brushes by putting them in this pretty cup. When someone is done with a brush, they can put it back for the next friend."

Penny's whiskers perked up. "That sounds much nicer than grabbing!"

"And less messy too," agreed Charlie, looking at his paint-splashed paper.

Working together, they arranged the art supplies just as Ollie had suggested. They placed the colorful paint jars in a neat circle in the center of the table. The brushes went into a cheerful yellow cup where everyone could reach them easily.

"Now let's try again," said Ms. Robin. "Remember to use your calm voices and take turns."

This time, the art room felt peaceful and happy. The animals spoke in soft, kind voices. They said "please" and "thank you" as they shared the supplies. Ollie felt his tummy relax, and his whiskers stopped twitching nervously.

"Ollie, would you like the green paint next?" asked Benny politely.

"Yes, please! Thank you for asking," Ollie replied with a big smile.

As they painted, the room filled with gentle sounds: brushes swishing on paper, quiet conversations, and happy giggles. Even Charlie's paint-splashed paper turned into something beautiful when he added more colors on top.

"You know what?" said Penny as she carefully cleaned her brush. "This is much more fun when we're calm and take turns."

Ollie nodded, his sleek fur shining in the warm light. "When we prepare and make a plan together, everyone feels better. And when everyone feels better, we can make the most beautiful art!"

Ms. Robin hung all their friendship pictures on the wall, where the bright colors seemed to dance together in perfect harmony.

The End.
