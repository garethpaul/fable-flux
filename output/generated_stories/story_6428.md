# Ollie Otter’s Gentle Plan

Ollie the playful otter loved to wiggle, giggle, and splash. Today, there was no river to splash in, but the nursery was warm and cozy. The soft rug felt fluffy under his paws. The sun made little squares of light on the floor. The air smelled like clean soap and warm milk. Toys sat in tidy baskets, and tiny shoes lined up like little ducks.

Ollie’s teacher, Miss Nia, smiled. “Good morning, Ollie. Today we practice self-care,” she said in a gentle voice. “That means we take care of our bodies, our feelings, and our friends.”

Ollie clapped his paws. “I can do that!”

He saw his friends stretching on the rug. “We are doing our morning wiggles,” said Mia, a small mouse with bright eyes.

Ollie loved to wiggle. He bounced high. He rolled fast. His tail swished-swish across the floor. “Wheee!” he squeaked.

But Ollie did not look where he rolled. His tail knocked into the water cup. Splash! Water tipped onto the rug and dripped over Pip’s drawing.

Pip, a puff of a bunny, held up the soggy paper. “Oh no,” Pip whispered. His long ears drooped.

Ollie’s tummy felt twisty. His cheeks felt hot. The room was quiet. The rug felt cold and squishy under his paws now. Ollie looked at Pip’s sad face. He felt a heavy thump in his chest. “I… I didn’t mean to,” he said softly.

Miss Nia knelt beside them. “We can fix problems,” she said. “What can we do first?”

Ollie took a big breath in. He felt the air go in, soft and cool. He let it out, slow and warm. “I can help,” he said. “I can say sorry.”

Ollie looked at Pip. “I’m sorry I splashed your picture. I was too bouncy. Are you okay?”

Pip blinked and sniffed. “I feel sad,” he said. “But thank you for saying sorry.”

Ollie’s eyes felt watery. “I want to make it better. Can I help dry it?”

Miss Nia handed Ollie a soft towel. “Gentle pats,” she said.

Ollie and Pip patted the wet paper. Pat-pat, pat-pat. The towel felt warm and fuzzy. The paper was still wrinkly, but the colors showed again. “Look! The sun in your picture is smiling,” Ollie said.

Pip’s ears lifted. “It is!”

Miss Nia nodded. “That was kind, Ollie. What else can we do for self-care now?”

Ollie thought. His tummy felt less twisty. “I can do calm wiggles,” he said. “I can move and be safe.”

Miss Nia smiled. “Great idea. Exercise helps our bodies. Calm moves help our feelings.”

Ollie stood on the soft rug. “Friends, let’s do gentle moves,” he said. “Otter waves!”

They all stood in a line. Ollie showed small waves with his paws. “Swoosh… swoosh,” he hummed. The sound was like the sea. The room felt quiet and happy. “Now otter stretches,” he said. He lifted his paws up, up, up. “Reach for the sun squares.”

Mia giggled. “I see the light on my paws!”

“Now we roll like a slow log,” Ollie said. He rolled just a tiny bit. “Little rolls, safe and slow.”

Pip smiled and rolled, too. “Little rolls are fun!”

They made a circle shape. Ollie set out a “safe space” with the towel. “When we move, we keep our space,” he said. “We look with our eyes. We listen with our ears.” He touched his chest. “We feel our hearts go thump-thump.”

Miss Nia clapped softly. “You are leading with care, Ollie.”

Ollie felt proud and warm inside. “After moving, we drink water,” he said. He held the cup with both paws. Sip. The water was cool and sweet. “We take little sips.”

Pip sipped, too. “My body says thank you,” Pip said.

Ollie turned to Pip. “Would you like to make a new picture together?” he asked.

Pip nodded. “Yes, please.”

They sat at the little table. The wood felt smooth. The crayons smelled like wax and fruit. Ollie drew a sea with tiny waves. Pip drew a bright sun and a bunny boat. Their hands moved slow and steady. Scritch-scritch went the crayons.

Ollie said, “When I feel too bouncy, I can stop, breathe, and choose gentle moves.”

Pip said, “When I feel sad, I can tell a friend and ask for help.”

Miss Nia put their picture on the wall. “This shows problem-solving,” she said. “You made a plan. You used your words. You cared for your bodies and your feelings. You cared for each other.”

Ollie’s tail gave a happy swish. “I’m glad I said sorry,” he whispered. “I’m glad we made a safe plan.”

The room felt cozy again. The rug was warm. The sun squares shone bright. Ollie and Pip shared a soft hug. It felt like a blanket around their hearts.

Ollie looked at his friends. “Self-care is three things,” he said, holding up one paw, then two, then three. “Care for my body with exercise and water. Care for my feelings with calm breaths. Care for my friends with kind words and an apology when I make a mistake.”

Everyone nodded. “We can all do that,” said Mia.

Ollie grinned. “Let’s end with one more otter wave.” Swoosh… swoosh… They all waved, gentle and slow.

Ollie felt peaceful and strong. He learned that being playful is lovely, and being careful is loving. When problems splash, he can make a plan, say sorry, and take good care.

The End.
