# Busy Beaver’s Table-Trot

In a sunny dining room, a busy beaver named Bixie woke up with a stretch. The big table stood in the middle like a wide brown island. The chairs were tall trees. The window let in warm light that smelled like toast. Bixie’s tail thumped softly, tap-tap, on the wooden floor.

“I have a big day,” Bixie said. “I will use my strong body and my smart brain.” Her whiskers twitched with excitement.

On the table, a bowl of bright apples shone like little red suns. A basket of napkins felt soft as clouds. A pitcher of water sparkled. Bixie patted her chest. “My heart goes thump-thump. It likes to move. Exercise helps it stay happy!”

She looked at the chairs. “Hmm,” she hummed. “I can sit and watch the dust float. Or I can make a fun movement game.” Bixie tapped her chin. “I will decide. I choose to move!”

Bixie set some rules, just like a kind leader. “Safe steps. Quiet feet. Big smiles,” she said. “I will care for the table and the chairs. I will care for me, too.”

She took a deep breath. “Sniff!” The room smelled like cinnamon. “Ahh.” Her body felt ready.

“First, warm-up,” said Bixie. She marched around the table, one, two, one, two. Her paws went pad-pad-pad on the floor. “Hello, legs,” she giggled. “Wake up, arms!” She lifted her paws and reached to the sky. “Stretch like the sun.” The window breeze kissed her cheeks.

Clink! A spoon on the table wiggled. Bixie stopped. “Oh! Did I bump you?” she asked the spoon softly. It did not answer, but it stayed still. “I will move gently,” she decided. “I want to be kind to my home.”

Next, Bixie made a little course. “Chair hop, napkin step, apple reach,” she planned. She spread napkins in a squiggly path on the floor. She made sure they were flat and safe. “I use what I have,” she smiled. “I can make fun from simple things.”

“Ready, set, go!” Bixie hopped from napkin to napkin. “Boing, boing!” The wood felt smooth under her paws. She reached up high for the air near the apples. “Stretch tall!” she laughed. “Hello, shoulders!” She bent down low. “Hello, toes!”

She stopped to drink a sip of water. “Glug, glug,” went the pitcher. “Exercise and water are friends,” she said. Her tail thumped again, happy and cool.

Suddenly, Bixie saw a crumb on the floor. Then another. “Oh dear,” she said. “Crumbs can make hands sticky and floors dirty.” She looked at her fun course. She looked at the crumbs. “What should I do?” She thought for a moment.

“I can keep playing and leave the mess,” she said. “Or I can help and clean.” She placed a paw on her heart. “I choose to help. I care for my home. I care for our world. Helping is part of being a good neighbor.”

Bixie grabbed a small brush. Swish-swish. She swept the crumbs into a little pile. Tap-tap. She put them in the bin. “There,” she smiled. “Exercise for my arms, and care for my space. When we all help a little, the world shines a lot.” She waved to the window, thinking of friends far away who also eat and play and clean. “We share one big home called Earth.”

Back to the course she went. “Round two!” she cheered. She added a gentle twist. “Twist like a river.” She wiggled her hips. “Wiggle-wiggle!” She counted her breaths. “In… one, two. Out… one, two.” The room felt bright and brave.

Then, a chair wobbled. “Uh-oh,” said Bixie. She steadied it with both paws. “Safety first.” She checked each chair foot. “Stable and strong,” she nodded. “I take time to make good choices.”

She invited a friend in her mind. “Hello, friends everywhere,” she said to the air. “Let’s move together! You can march in your dining room. You can stretch in your kitchen. We are different and the same. We can all try our best.”

Bixie finished with a cool-down. She lay her belly on the warm floor. “I feel my heart go thump-thump.” It slowed to a calm beat. She listened to the quiet tick-tick of the clock. She smelled the sweet apples again. She smiled. “My body feels strong. My mind feels kind.”

At the end, Bixie placed the napkins back in the basket, straight and neat. She lined up the spoons. She filled the water pitcher for later. “That was fun,” she said. “I used exercise to help my body. I made choices to keep us safe. I cared for my room and our world.”

Her tail gave one last happy thump. “Tomorrow, I will move again,” Bixie promised. “I can lead, I can help, and I can try. When I move my body, I help my heart. When I make good choices, I help my home. When I care for the Earth, I help everyone.”

The End.
