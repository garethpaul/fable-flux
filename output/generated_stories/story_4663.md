# Bella Bird Plans the Song Party

Bella was a tiny singing bird with soft blue feathers and a bright, sunny voice. She lived in a playful living room where pillows made little mountains, a rug had rainbow stripes, and sunlight danced on the walls. The room smelled like fresh cookies, and soft music hummed from a small radio. This was Bella’s favorite place to sing.

One morning, Bella fluttered to the windowsill. “Tweet, tweet! Today I want to share my family’s Song Day!” she chirped. Song Day was a special tradition from her bird family. They sang old songs and taught new ones. They also shared stories about where the songs came from. Bella’s heart felt warm when she thought about it.

She hopped onto a soft pillow. “I will have a Song Party!” she said. “I’ll invite my friends. We will sing, clap, and share.” But then Bella blinked. The room looked very big. The rug looked very long. Her tummy felt wiggly. “Oh no,” she whispered. “How do I start?”

Bella took a deep breath. “I can plan,” she said, standing tall. “Planning helps me know what to do.” She found a little notepad by the toy box and a bright blue crayon. On the first page she wrote: PLAN.

She whispered as she wrote. “Step one: Pick a time. Step two: Make a list. Step three: Ask for help. Step four: Practice.” Her voice sounded steady and brave.

Bella called to the room, “Friends, I’m planning a Song Party. Can you help me?” Her friend Tuffy, a soft teddy bear with a tiny bowtie, looked up from the couch. “I can help carry things,” Tuffy said in a deep, cuddly voice.

Pip the wooden duck rolled over and squeaked, “Quack! I can be the audience!”

Luna the lamp blinked her warm light. “I can make the room cozy,” she hummed.

Bella smiled. “Thank you. That matches my plan.”

She checked step one. “Pick a time. How about after snack?” The radio clicked and said, “Good idea, little bird. After snack is calm time.” Bella wrote: After snack.

Step two was “Make a list.” Bella tapped the crayon on her beak. “We need songs, seats, a tiny stage, and a way to share our tradition.” She drew small pictures: a music note, four squares for seats, a line for a stage, and a heart for sharing. The crayon smelled waxy and sweet.

Tuffy pointed to the rug. “This rainbow stripe can be the stage line,” he said.

“Good thinking,” Bella chirped. “Let’s put pillows for seats.” She and Tuffy puffed and pushed the fluffy pillows. “Huff, huff,” they giggled, feeling the tickly fabric on their feathers and fur.

Pip squeaked, “Quack! I will sit here and clap!” He practiced clapping with his wooden wings: click-clack, click-clack.

Now step three: “Ask for help.” Bella flew to the plant by the window. Green leaves whispered, “Rustle, rustle.” Bella spoke clearly, “Dear Plant, may I hang this banner on your branch?” She held up a string of paper notes she had made.

The plant rustled yes, and Bella tied the banner. She felt proud. She was being assertive—she asked in a strong, kind voice.

Luna the lamp warmed the room with a golden glow. The radio turned soft. The living room felt cozy and ready.

Finally, step four: “Practice.” Bella stood on the rainbow stage line. She sang a small song from her family: “Loo-loo, la-la, we sing together, near or far, in any weather.” Her voice was sweet like honey. The notes floated like bubbles.

She paused and said, “I will also share the story. In my family, we sing to say ‘We belong.’ It is our tradition.” She looked at her friends and said, “You can share your traditions too.”

Tuffy raised a paw. “In my family, we have Hug Time every night,” he said proudly.

Pip squeaked, “We roll around the rug after breakfast!”

Bella clapped her wings. “Wonderful! We can share them at the party.”

Soon, snack time was done. It was calm time. The living room was ready. The pillows made soft clouds. The banner fluttered like tiny birds. The air smelled like apples. Bella felt a little flutter in her tummy. She stood tall and spoke clearly, “Welcome to Song Day. I planned this party. We will sing, listen, and share.”

Tuffy and Pip clapped. Click-clack. Clap-clap.

Bella sang the family song again, louder and brighter: “Loo-loo, la-la, we sing together.” Her voice sparkled. She told the story of her grandma bird who taught her the song while sitting on a warm branch. “This song means we are together,” she said. “Now you can sing it too.”

“Loo-loo, la-la,” Tuffy and Pip sang back. Their voices mixed like colors on the rug. The whole room felt happy.

Then Bella said in her firm, gentle voice, “Now it is your turn to share.” Tuffy stepped onto the rainbow line. “In my family, we say, ‘Please’ and ‘Thank you’ at dinner,” he said. “It makes our hearts calm.” Pip rolled up and squeaked, “In my family, we dance after cleaning up!” He wiggled, and everyone laughed.

Bella nodded. “Thank you for sharing. You were brave.” She checked her notepad. All steps were done. The plan worked!

They ended with one more song, a new one they made together. “Plan, plan, step by step. Ask for help, take a breath. Share your ways, kind and strong. We belong in every song.”

As the lamp dimmed to a soft glow, Bella felt proud and peaceful. She had used planning to solve her big problem. She shared her tradition and used an assertive voice to ask for help and to speak up. Her friends learned and shared too.

Bella fluttered to the windowsill and smiled. “Planning helps me do big things, one small step at a time,” she said.

The End.
