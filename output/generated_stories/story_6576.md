# Splash the Dolphin's Garden Helper Day

Once upon a time, there lived a cheerful dolphin named Splash who loved to help everyone around him. One sunny morning, Splash was swimming near the shore when he heard a sad voice coming from a little garden shed.

"Oh dear, oh dear!" cried Mrs. Rose, an elderly lady who was standing outside her wooden shed. "I dropped all my gardening tools in the water, and now I can't take care of my beautiful flowers!"

Splash's big, kind heart felt sorry for Mrs. Rose. He swam closer to the edge of the water and called out, "Hello! I'm Splash the dolphin. What's wrong?"

Mrs. Rose looked surprised to see a talking dolphin. "My gardening tools fell into the deep water when I was watering my plants by the shore. Without them, I can't help my flowers grow big and strong!"

"Don't worry!" said Splash with a happy click. "I love to help! Let me dive down and find your tools."

Splash took a deep breath and dove down, down, down into the cool blue water. He used his strong tail to swim fast and his smart eyes to look everywhere. The water felt refreshing on his smooth skin as he searched.

"I found a rake!" Splash called out as he came up for air, holding the tool carefully in his mouth.

"Wonderful!" clapped Mrs. Rose.

Splash dove down again. This time he found a small shovel buried in the sandy bottom. Up and down he went, practicing his diving skills and getting better each time. Soon he had found a watering can, some flower pots, and even Mrs. Rose's favorite gardening gloves!

"You're such a helpful friend, Splash!" said Mrs. Rose as she gathered all her clean tools. "But now I have another problem. These heavy bags of soil need to go into my shed, and I'm too tired to carry them all."

Splash thought for a moment. "I have an idea! I may live in the water, but I can still help on land!"

Using his strong nose, Splash gently pushed the bags of soil across the grass toward the wooden shed. He moved his whole body back and forth, wiggling and sliding on the soft grass. It felt funny and tickly, and Splash giggled with joy.

"This is like dancing!" laughed Splash as he pushed another bag. "Moving my body in new ways is fun!"

Mrs. Rose opened the shed door wide. The shed smelled like fresh wood and earth. Together, they worked as a team. Mrs. Rose pulled while Splash pushed, and soon all the soil bags were safely inside the cozy shed.

"Thank you so much, Splash!" said Mrs. Rose. "Because you helped me, I can now plant beautiful flowers that will make clean air for all the fish and animals to breathe!"

Splash felt warm and happy inside. "Helping others makes me feel wonderful! And I learned that practicing new movements makes me stronger!"

From that day on, Splash visited Mrs. Rose every week. He helped her water the plants from the water side, and she shared stories while her flowers grew tall and colorful. The garden became the most beautiful spot by the shore, and all the sea creatures and land animals enjoyed the fresh, clean air together.

"Helping others is the best feeling in the whole ocean!" Splash would say with a joyful splash of his tail.

The End.
