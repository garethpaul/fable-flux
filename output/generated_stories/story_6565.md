# Pippa Penguin’s Crafty Shed Day

Pippa was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a little red scarf. One sunny morning, she waddled to the garden shed. The wooden door smelled like pine. The floor felt cool on her webbed feet. “Hello, shed!” Pippa chirped. “Today, I will craft!”

Inside the shed, there were boxes and baskets. Snip-snips of shiny scissors. Soft yarn in rainbow colors. Glue sticks that smelled sweet. Paints like tiny suns and stars. Pippa clapped her flippers. “I will make a wind chime for the garden,” she said. “It will sing in the breeze.”

Pippa set out her tools. She said, “I ask my body first. Am I safe?” She looked around. The scissors were closed. The glue had a cap. The floor was clear. “Yes,” she said. “I am safe.”

She took a deep breath. “In through my beak, out slow.” Pippa smiled. “I can craft with care.”

Just then, a tap-tap sounded on the door. “Pippa? Can I come in?” It was Benny Bunny with soft gray ears.

“Yes, Benny,” Pippa called. “The door is open.”

Benny hopped in. “What are you making?”

“A wind chime,” said Pippa. “I will use sticks, strings, buttons, and bells. Would you like to help?”

“Yes!” said Benny.

Pippa added, “Let’s ask first. We use our words. We share tools. We keep our bodies safe.” Pippa pointed to a little sign she painted. It said: Ask. Share. Safe.

Benny nodded. “I will ask. I will share. I will be safe.”

Pippa showed a little tray. “This is my tray. Please ask before touching.” She smiled kindly. “I like to know who is touching my things.”

Benny said, “I will ask first.”

Pippa tied two sticks into an X. She pulled the string snug. “Tug-tug. Not too tight,” she said. “Just right.” She held the sticks up. “Benny, can you hold them steady?”

“Yes,” said Benny. He held them with gentle paws.

Tap-tap again. It was Mia Mouse with bright whiskers. “Hi! Can I come in?”

“Yes, Mia,” Pippa said. “We are crafting.”

Mia sniffed the glue. “It smells sweet like honey.” She twitched happily. “Can I use the blue yarn?”

“Please ask Pippa,” said Benny. “It is on her tray.”

Mia turned to Pippa. “Pippa, may I use the blue yarn?”

Pippa’s heart felt warm. “Yes, you may. Thank you for asking.”

They all worked. Pippa tied a bell. Tinkle-tinkle. Benny sorted buttons by color. “Red, blue, yellow.” Mia cut yarn pieces. Snip-snips, soft and quiet.

Soon, the table felt crowded. Benny’s paw reached for the scissors on Pippa’s tray.

“Benny,” said Pippa in a calm voice, “please ask before taking from my tray.”

Benny pulled his paw back. “Oops. I forgot. Pippa, may I use the scissors?”

“Yes,” said Pippa. “Thank you for asking.”

Then Mia placed buttons in a big pile near the glue. The pile tipped. Buttons rolled like tiny marbles.

Clink-clink, plip-plip!

“Uh-oh,” said Mia. “What do we do?”

Pippa took a deep breath. “We can think. We can solve this.” She looked at the floor. The buttons rolled toward a little bump in the wood. “Let’s make a button fence!”

“How?” asked Benny.

Pippa looked around. She saw a ruler and two cardboard pieces. “Aha,” she said. “We place the cardboard like a wall.” She pushed them gently to stop the buttons. “Benny, can you hold the wall? Mia, can you scoop the buttons into a bowl?”

“Yes!” said Benny and Mia.

They worked as a team. Scoop, plink, scoop, plink. Soon, the buttons were safe in the bowl.

Pippa smiled. “We did it! We used our brains. We kept calm.”

The wind chime was almost done. Pippa tied on the last bell. Ting! She swung the chime. The bells sang: tinkle-ting, tinkle-ting.

“Beautiful,” whispered Mia.

“It sounds like happy rain,” said Benny.

“Let’s test it outside,” said Pippa.

They opened the shed door. Fresh air kissed their faces. The garden smelled like soil and mint leaves. Pippa waddled carefully. Waddle-waddle. Benny hopped. Hop-hop. Mia scurried. Skitter-skitter. They moved together and kept space. “Arms out,” said Pippa, stretching her flippers. “We give each other room.” Mia copied with tiny arms. Benny spread his paws. They giggled and kept safe.

They hung the chime on a low hook. A soft breeze blew. The bells danced. Tinkle, tinkle, tinkle.

Pippa’s eyes shone. “We made it with care.”

Just then, a crow cawed from the fence. Caw-caw! The wind blew stronger. The chime twirled fast. One string loosened.

“Oh no,” said Mia.

“It’s okay,” said Pippa. “Crafts can need fixes.” She thought. “What will make it stronger?”

“More knots,” said Benny.

“Glue on the knot,” said Mia.

“Good ideas,” said Pippa. “Let’s use both.”

Back in the shed, Pippa tied a double knot. Pull-pull. She dabbed a tiny dot of glue. Tap. “Now we wait,” she said. “Crafts need patience.”

They counted together. “One… two… three…” The glue set. The knot held firm.

Outside again, the wind chime sang. Tinkle-ting, tinkle-ting. Pippa felt proud. Her friends clapped.

“Thank you for helping,” Pippa said. “You asked before touching. You shared. You kept safe. We solved problems.”

Benny smiled. “I learned to ask before I take.”

Mia nodded. “I learned to think and try again.”

Pippa patted her chest. “I learned that saying my rules is kind. It helps me feel safe.”

They hugged gently. Pippa added, “We can craft many things. With care, with asking, and with kind hearts.”

The chime sang in the breeze. The garden smelled sweet. The shed stood cozy and bright. Pippa, Benny, and Mia waved.

The End.
