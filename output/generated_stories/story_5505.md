# Pebble the Penguin’s Bathroom Race

Pebble was a cheerful penguin who loved to waddle fast and sing little songs. “Waddle-waddle, hum-hum!” Pebble chirped. One morning, Pebble slid into the bathroom. The floor felt cool and smooth under tiny penguin feet. The air smelled like minty toothpaste and soapy bubbles. The mirror was foggy from a warm shower, and little water drops made soft plip-plop sounds in the sink.

Today, Pebble wanted to play. Pebble wanted to build a pillow fort. Pebble wanted to draw a rocket. Pebble wanted to dance. But first, Pebble needed to get ready.

Mama Penguin called from the hallway, “Pebble, it’s time to brush, wash, and go!”

Pebble’s tummy fluttered. “But I want to play now!” Pebble said, fluffing little wings. Pebble looked at the toothbrush. It was blue and shiny. Pebble looked at the soap. It smelled like oranges. Pebble looked at the towel. It was soft and stripey.

Pebble felt a tiny grump. “I don’t want to spend all day in the bathroom,” Pebble muttered.

Mama waddled in gently. “I hear you, little feather,” she said in a calm voice. “Let’s make a plan. We can use our time like a treasure. When we plan, there’s time for fun.”

Pebble blinked. “A treasure?”

Mama nodded and hugged Pebble. “Yes. We’ll use the bathroom timer. We do three things: brush teeth, wash face and flippers, and potty. We do each thing until the little sand runs out. Then—playtime!”

Pebble looked at the tiny hourglass timer on the counter. The golden sand sparkled. The glass felt cool when Pebble touched it. “It looks like desert snow,” Pebble whispered.

“Want to try?” Mama asked.

Pebble puffed up with courage. “Yes! Let’s have a Bathroom Race!”

Mama smiled. “A gentle race. We do it right, not fast-fast. We do it just right.”

Pebble set the timer and picked up the blue toothbrush. “Round and round, little brush,” Pebble sang. The mint toothpaste tasted tingly and fresh. Pebble’s mouth felt bubbly and cool. “Mmm! It tastes like winter,” Pebble giggled.

Pebble brushed the front, brushed the back, brushed the tiny nibble teeth. The sand slipped down, down, down. Pebble felt a little rush. “Oh no, the sand is going!”

Mama’s voice was warm. “Breathe, Pebble. Slow and steady. You are doing great.”

Pebble took a big penguin breath. In… out. “I can do it,” Pebble said softly. Pebble brushed a little more, then spit—sploosh! The sink water swirled like a tiny ice whirlpool. Pebble peeked at the timer. The last sparkle of sand fell. “I did it!”

Mama clapped gently. “Yes! You used your time well.”

“Now wash time,” Pebble said. Pebble turned the faucet. The warm water felt like a hug on Pebble’s flippers. The orange soap made bubbles that smelled sunny. Pebble rubbed-rubbed, making a soft squish-squish sound. Bubbles popped with tiny kisses. Pop! Pop!

Pebble looked at the timer again. The sand was sliding. A little worry tickled Pebble’s feathers. “What if I can’t finish?” Pebble asked.

“Listen to your plan,” Mama said. “Practice makes easy. You did brushing. You can do washing. One, two, three—rinse!”

Pebble counted, “One, two, three!” and rinsed the bubbles away. The towel was fluffy and warm, and made a cozy whoosh when Pebble dried. The timer chimed. “Done!”

“Potty time,” Mama reminded.

Pebble waddled to the potty. The seat felt cool. Pebble swung feet and hummed. “Waddle-waddle, hum-hum.” Pebble waited, and waited. The timer sand slipped. Pebble felt frustrated again. “I want to be done!”

Mama took Pebble’s flipper. “Let’s imagine. Close your eyes.”

Pebble closed eyes. “What do I see?”

“A brave penguin sailing a bubble boat,” Mama whispered. “The bubbles carry you across a shiny sea. You are calm. You are patient.”

Pebble smiled and breathed slowly. The bathroom sounded soft—drip, drip, drip. It smelled clean and sweet. Pebble felt the cool seat and the warm towel on knees. And then—hooray! Pebble finished.

The toilet flushed with a cheerful whoosh. Pebble washed and dried once more. Pebble peeked at the timer. The last shake of sand fell with a tiny hush.

Pebble stood tall. “I did it! I followed my plan!”

Mama hugged Pebble tight. “You listened. You practiced. You used your time with care. Now there is time for play.”

Pebble’s eyes sparkled. “Pillow fort?”

“Pillow fort,” Mama said.

Pebble waddled out, then stopped. “Wait! I want to set a play timer, too.”

Mama smiled. “What a smart idea.”

Pebble turned the hourglass again. “When the sand is gone, I clean up,” Pebble said. “Then snack time!”

“Exactly,” Mama said. “That’s time-management.”

Pebble repeated the new big word slowly. “Time. Man. Age. Ment.” It felt bouncy and important. Pebble felt proud. Pebble learned that a plan makes room for fun. Pebble learned that breathing helps when feelings get big. Pebble learned that practice makes things easier.

Pebble looked back at the bathroom. “Thank you, bathroom,” Pebble whispered. “You smell nice. You sparkle. You help me grow.”

Pebble built a pillow fort, just like a bubble boat. When the timer finished, Pebble cleaned up without a fuss. Pebble smiled. The day felt smooth and shiny, like a polished shell.

Pebble snuggled into Mama’s side. “I can use my time like treasure.”

Mama kissed Pebble’s head. “Yes, little feather. And when you do, there is time for everything you love.”

The End.
