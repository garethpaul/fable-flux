# Benny Bear’s Big Table

Benny was a friendly bear cub with soft, honey-brown fur and bright curious eyes. This morning, Benny padded into the dining room on his gentle paws. The room smelled like warm toast and sweet berries. Sunlight made sparkles on the shiny wooden table. Chairs stood like tall friends waiting for hugs.

Mama Bear placed a big bowl down. “Good morning, Benny,” she said with a smile. “Today we have berry pancakes!”

“Berry pancakes!” Benny clapped his paws. “Yum, yum!”

At the table were Benny’s friends from the forest. Mia the mouse sat on a pillow so she could see. Tino the turtle wore a tiny napkin like a cape. Lila the lamb had a ribbon in her wool. And Kiki the kitten purred softly, tail swishing like a little feather.

Mama set a basket of pancakes in the middle. “We eat together,” she said. “We wait. We share. We listen. Everyone belongs.”

Benny nodded. He wanted to help. He wanted everyone to feel welcome.

“Buenos días,” said Tino in a slow, happy voice. “That means good morning!”

“Bonjour!” squeaked Mia. “Good morning in French.”

“Nǐ hǎo!” meowed Kiki.

“Wow,” said Benny. His ears wiggled. “So many ways to say hello!”

Lila smiled. “Baa-baa! That is my lamb hello.”

Benny giggled. “Hello, everyone! I like your words.” He tried them one by one. “Buenos días. Bonjour. Nǐ hǎo. Baa-baa!” The words felt like tiny songs on his tongue.

Mama poured syrup that smelled like sunshine. “Who would like pancakes?” she asked.

“I would! I would!” Benny bounced in his chair. “Me first!”

Mia looked down. “I am little,” she whispered. “I can wait.”

Benny’s paws paused. He looked at his friends. He saw Mia’s small paws. He saw Tino’s slow smile. He saw Lila’s patient eyes. He took a deep breath. His tummy felt wiggly, like jelly. He remembered Mama’s words: We share. We listen. Everyone belongs.

Benny sat up tall. “I have an idea,” he said in a brave, kind voice. “Let’s make room for everyone. Mia can have a pancake first. The basket is big enough for all of us.”

Mia’s ears perked. “Really?”

“Yes,” Benny said. “You belong at the big table. We all do.”

Mia smiled. “Thank you, Benny.”

Kiki purred louder. “Mrrr. That was very thoughtful.”

Mama nodded. “That is inclusion,” she said. “Inclusion means making space so everyone feels welcome.”

Benny felt warm inside, like a hug. He passed the basket to Mia with careful paws. “Here you go.”

Mia took a small pancake. “Merci,” she said. “That means thank you.”

“Gracias,” Tino added when he took one. “Thank you.”

“Xièxie,” Kiki meowed softly.

Benny’s eyes shone. “So many thank-yous!” He tried them too. “Merci. Gracias. Xièxie.” He smiled. “All the words are friendly.”

As they ate, the butter melted like little yellow puddles. The pancakes were fluffy and sweet. The syrup made sticky smiles. “Mmm,” said Lila. “This tastes like a warm morning.”

When the basket came back to Benny, he took his pancake. He felt proud. His tummy was happy, and his heart was happier.

Then Benny saw something. “Oh!” A chair was a bit far from the table. Tino’s short legs could not reach the plate well.

Benny stood up. “I can help,” he said. He pushed the chair gently, paws steady. “Here you go, Tino.”

“Gracias,” said Tino. “You are very kind.”

Benny nodded. “Everyone should be comfy. Everyone should be able to reach.”

After breakfast, crumbs danced on the table like tiny snow. Mama brought a soft cloth. “Time to clean up,” she said.

Kiki looked shy. “I sometimes make crumbs,” she whispered.

Benny remembered inclusion again. He also remembered being strong in his kind ideas. He lifted his chin. “We all help,” he said, speaking clearly. “It’s our table. We can do it together.”

Mia squeaked, “Yes! I can wipe the little spots.”

Tino said, “I can carry the napkins. Slow and steady.”

Lila hummed. “I can gather forks.”

Benny picked up plates with careful paws. They worked side by side. The cloth felt cool and damp. The table smelled like lemon and clean wood. Their voices made a soft music: swish, hum, pat, purr.

When they finished, the dining room shone. Mama clapped. “Look at that! You included each other. You spoke up kindly. You used words from many places. And you took responsibility.”

Benny’s cheeks felt warm. “We did it together,” he said. He looked at his friends. “You belong here. Always.”

“Siempre,” said Tino. “Always.”

“Pour toujours,” said Mia.

“Yǒngyuǎn,” purred Kiki.

“Forever,” baa’d Lila.

Benny smiled so big his eyes crinkled. He took a deep breath. The room smelled like lemon and love. “My new words feel like hugs,” he said.

Mama hugged him for real. “That is the lesson for today,” she said. “Inclusion means making space, using kind words, and helping so everyone can join. Being assertive means speaking up for fairness in a gentle way. Responsibility means we all take care of our space.”

Benny nodded. “I can do that every day.”

And he did.

The End.
