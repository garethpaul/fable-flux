# Bubbles the Dolphin Beats the Clock

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to splash paint almost as much as splashing in the sea. Today, Bubbles swam into the creative art room at the Sea School. The room smelled like fresh paper and fruity crayons. Colorful brushes stood like tiny trees in cups. Soft music hummed like the ocean.

“Good morning, Bubbles,” said Ms. Starfish, the teacher. “Today, we paint, we move, and we manage our time.”

Bubbles clapped his fins. “Paint and move? Yay! I love both!”

A big, friendly clock ticked on the wall. Tick-tock. Tick-tock. Ms. Starfish pointed. “We have three parts today,” she said. “First, warm-up exercise. Then, painting. Last, clean-up. We will use the clock to help us.”

Bubbles nodded, but his eyes twinkled at the rainbow paints. They looked so bright and splashy. “Can I paint first?” he asked.

Ms. Starfish smiled gently. “I understand that you’re excited. Painting is fun. But our bodies need to wake up first. Let’s exercise. It will help your mind and your muscles. It will make painting even better.”

Bubbles wiggled. He took a deep breath. He smelled the salty air from the open window. “Okay,” he said. “I will try.”

Ms. Starfish rang a little bell. Ding! “Warm-up time. Five minutes,” she said, tapping the clock. The hand pointed to the number 1. “When it points to the 2, we paint.”

Bubbles and his friends stood on soft mats. “Stretch like starfish!” called Ms. Starfish.

Bubbles stretched his fins wide. His skin felt tingly and warm. “Ooo, that feels nice,” he giggled.

“Swim in place!” called Ms. Starfish.

Bubbles swam without moving forward, swoosh-swoosh, swoosh-swoosh. His heart beat faster, thump-thump, thump-thump. He smiled. “I feel strong!”

“Deep breaths,” said Ms. Starfish. “In through the blowhole, out slow.”

Bubbles breathed in. The air smelled like lemons from the art soap. He breathed out. Ahhh. He felt calm and ready.

The clock hand moved. Tick-tock. Tick-tock. It pointed to the 2.

“Painting time!” cheered Ms. Starfish.

Bubbles swam to his easel. He dipped a brush into blue paint. The paint was cool and smooth. Swish! He painted big waves. He dabbed yellow dots like sunshine. Dot, dot, dot. He added green seaweed that wiggled like fingers. Wiggle-wiggle.

“I am painting a strong ocean,” Bubbles said. “The ocean moves. Like exercise!”

His friend, Tiny Turtle, peeked over. “I like your waves,” said Tiny. “They look fast.”

“Thank you,” said Bubbles. His cheeks felt warm with joy.

The clock ticked again. Ms. Starfish rang the bell. Ding! “Two more minutes,” she said. “Time to finish up.”

Bubbles saw a purple color he loved. He wanted to add more dots. Many, many dots! He dipped his brush again.

Ms. Starfish swam over. “Bubbles,” she said kindly, “remember our plan. We had time for exercise, time for painting, and time to clean. When the hand points to the 3, we clean together.”

Bubbles frowned a little. “But I want more dots.”

“I understand,” said Ms. Starfish. “Your art is special. You can add dots tomorrow. Right now, we show care. We care for our bodies with exercise. We care for our art room with clean-up. We care for our time by stopping when it is time.”

Bubbles looked at the clock. Tick-tock. He took a deep breath. “Okay,” he said. “I will add three dots. One, two, three. Then I stop.”

He tapped the brush gently. Dot. Dot. Dot. The last dot made a tiny plop sound. He smiled. “Done!”

The bell rang. Ding! “Clean-up time,” said Ms. Starfish.

Bubbles rinsed his brush. Swish in the water cup. The water turned light blue, like morning sky. He wiped the table with a soft sponge. It smelled like oranges. Squeak, squeak. Tiny Turtle folded paper towels. Crinkle, crinkle. The room began to shine.

“Teamwork!” said Tiny Turtle.

“Time-work!” Bubbles laughed. “We are good at both.”

After clean-up, everyone sat in a circle on the soft floor. The mats felt cozy under Bubbles’s belly. Ms. Starfish smiled. “How do you feel after exercise, painting, and clean-up?”

Bubbles raised a fin. “My body feels strong and awake,” he said. “The exercise made my heart go thump-thump. It made me calm. It helped my painting. And I stopped when the clock said stop. I can paint more tomorrow.”

Tiny Turtle nodded. “I understand the plan now,” Tiny said. “I like the bell. It helps me know what to do.”

Ms. Starfish clapped her arms. “You did it! You moved your body, you made art, and you followed time. That is good time-management. That is understanding.”

Bubbles felt proud. His chest felt warm, like sunshine on water. “I learned that exercise helps me,” he said. “I learned that time helps me. When I move first, I can make better art. When I stop on time, I can start again next time.”

Ms. Starfish gave Bubbles a shiny star sticker that smelled like strawberries. “You are a time-smart, healthy artist,” she said.

Bubbles giggled. “I am a splashy, strong dolphin!” he cheered.

On the way out, Bubbles looked back at his painting. The waves seemed to dance. He waved a fin. “See you tomorrow, dots,” he whispered.

He swam happily down the hall, feeling light and steady. Tick-tock, ding-ding, swoosh-swoosh. He knew the rhythm now: move, make, and manage time with care.

The End.
