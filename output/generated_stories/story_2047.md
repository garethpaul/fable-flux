# Mango the Monkey's Kitchen Safety Adventure

Mango the monkey loved helping in Grandma Rosa's warm kitchen. The sweet smell of cinnamon filled the air. Pots bubbled happily on the stove. Colorful spices lined the shelves like tiny rainbows.

But today, something was wrong.

"Oh dear!" said Grandma Rosa, stirring a big pot of soup. "I forgot to put away the sharp knives. And look - that pot handle is sticking out!"

Mango looked around the kitchen with his bright brown eyes. He saw knives on the counter. A pot handle pointed toward the walking path. Hot steam rose from the stove. The floor had spilled water near the sink.

"This kitchen isn't safe," said Mango bravely. "Someone could get hurt!"

Grandma Rosa smiled. "You're very smart, Mango. But I'm too busy cooking for the family dinner. What should we do?"

Mango stood up tall. His fluffy tail twitched with determination. "I can help make it safe! I want to be a kitchen safety leader!"

"That's wonderful," said Grandma Rosa. "But remember - always ask before touching anything hot or sharp."

Mango nodded seriously. He had a goal: make the kitchen safe for everyone.

First, Mango carefully pointed to the knives. "Grandma Rosa, can you please put these away?"

"Good eye!" said Grandma Rosa. She placed the sharp knives safely in the drawer. "What else do you see?"

Mango pointed his furry finger at the pot. "That handle could bump someone!"

Together, they turned the pot handle toward the back of the stove. Now no one would bump into it.

"The floor is wet and slippery," observed Mango. "May I get a towel?"

"Yes, but be very careful," said Grandma Rosa.

Mango slowly wiped up the spilled water. The kitchen floor was safe to walk on again.

"What about the hot stove?" asked Mango.

Grandma Rosa showed him the special mitt. "This keeps my hands safe from hot things. And see this red light? It means the stove is hot. We never touch when it's red."

Mango's eyes grew wide with understanding. "Red means stop! Just like traffic lights!"

"Exactly right!" said Grandma Rosa proudly.

Mango felt warm and happy inside. He had learned so much! Sharp things go in safe places. Pot handles turn away from people. Wet floors get dried. Hot things need special mitts.

Soon, the whole family arrived for dinner. Cousin Carlos ran into the kitchen.

"Wait!" called Mango. "Let me check if it's safe first!"

Mango looked around carefully. No knives on the counter. Pot handles turned safely. Floor was dry. He gave a big thumbs up.

"All safe!" announced Mango proudly.

"Wow!" said Cousin Carlos. "You're like a kitchen safety superhero!"

During dinner, Grandma Rosa told everyone about Mango's helpful leadership. "Mango saw problems and helped solve them. He made our kitchen safe for everyone."

Mango's chest puffed out with pride. He had set a goal and reached it! He had shown initiative by speaking up. He had been a leader by helping others stay safe.

"Tomorrow, can you teach me about kitchen safety too?" asked Cousin Carlos.

"Yes!" said Mango excitedly. "Safety is everyone's job!"

From that day on, Mango became the family's kitchen safety helper. He learned that being brave means speaking up when something isn't safe. Being a leader means helping others learn important things.

And every time he smelled Grandma Rosa's delicious cooking, Mango felt proud knowing he helped keep everyone safe and happy.

The End.
