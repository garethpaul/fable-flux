# Joey Hops to Quiet

Joey was a joyful kangaroo joey. He loved to hop, wiggle, and sing, “Boing, boing, boing!” He lived with Mama Roo in a cozy home. Today, Mama Roo worked in the home office. The office smelled like warm paper and lemon-clean. The keyboard clicked, click-click, like tiny raindrops. The printer hummed a soft bzzzz.

“Mama, can I play?” Joey asked, peeking into the office.

“Yes, my sweet hopper,” Mama Roo said, smiling. “I have a meeting soon. It’s a quiet time. Let’s make a plan together.”

Joey’s ears perked up. “A plan?”

Mama Roo pointed to a little chart on the wall. It had three pictures: a sun for Play Time, a moon for Quiet Time, and a star for Help Time. “We follow our routine,” Mama said. “First Play, then Quiet, then Help. When we follow the plan, our day feels calm.”

Joey nodded. “Boing! I can do it!”

He skipped to the rug with his blocks. He built a tall tower—click, clack, stack! He made a red roof and a blue door. His tail thumped softly on the floor. He felt proud and giggly.

The tall tower wobbled. Joey wanted to cheer loud. “YAY—” He stopped. He looked at the chart. Quiet Time was next. Joey put a finger to his lips. “Shh,” he whispered to himself. “I can be calm.”

But being calm was hard! His feet wanted to hop. His voice wanted to sing. He could smell Mama’s tea—sweet and minty—and he wanted to ask for a sip. His tummy fluttered like a little bird.

Joey took a slow breath in. He puffed a gentle breath out. “Smell the flower, blow the candle,” he said softly, like Mama taught. His body felt softer, like a pillow.

Mama came to the door with her headset. “It’s Quiet Time now, little one. What helps your body be calm?”

Joey thought. “A cuddle rock?” He hugged himself and rocked. Sway, sway. “And my soft scarf?” He wrapped a silky blue scarf around his shoulders. It felt cool and smooth.

Mama nodded. “Great tools. You are using self-regulation. That means you help your body and feelings get calm.”

Joey beamed. “I can help me!”

Mama smiled. “I’ll be in my meeting. After, it’s Help Time.”

She closed the glass door. Joey could see her on the screen, talking. Her voice was soft and kind. Joey felt a wiggle try to bounce out. He took another breath. Smell the flower. Blow the candle.

He whispered, “I can wait.”

Joey set a tiny timer egg that went tick, tick, tick. He looked at his picture books. He traced the bumpy scales on the dinosaur page—rough and scratchy. He rubbed the smooth sun on the next page—warm and shiny in the light.

Suddenly, the printer made a louder bzzzz and spit out a paper. It slid to the floor with a whispery swish. Papers fluttered like butterflies. Joey’s eyes grew wide. “Uh-oh,” he said. He wanted to run in and shout, “Mama, papers!”

He remembered the chart. He put his hands on his belly. Pat, pat. “First Quiet, then Help,” he told himself. “What can I do?”

Joey looked around. He saw his toy basket. He saw his blocks. He saw a long, soft scarf. “I can be resourceful,” he whispered, trying the big word slow. “Re-sour-ce-ful!”

He made a plan. He slid the scarf under the door, like a gentle snake. He used the scarf to pull the paper closer—shh, shh—without going in. He stacked the papers neatly on the mat. Tap, tap. He felt proud and calm.

The timer went ding!

Mama opened the door. “Help Time!” she said. Then she saw the neat stack. “Oh! You helped already. Joey, you used calm breaths. You waited. You solved a problem. That is wonderful self-regulation.”

Joey’s chest felt warm like sun. “I smelled the flower. I blew the candle. I rocked. I used my scarf. I helped!”

Mama knelt and gave him a gentle snuggle. He smelled her mint tea again. “Thank you for helping me,” she said. “Now we can work together. Will you hand me the papers?”

“Yes!” Joey said.

They sat side by side. Joey passed each page, one by one. Rustle, rustle. Mama stapled—click. Joey counted softly, “One, two, three.” He liked the soft sounds and the steady rhythm. It felt like music.

When they finished, Mama pointed to the star on the chart. “Help Time is done. You followed our routine. You worked with me. That is cooperation.”

Joey grinned. “Co-op-er-a-tion!”

“Let’s end with a wiggle break,” Mama said. They hopped in place. Boing, boing, boing! Then they took three calm breaths together. In and out. In and out. In and out.

Mama said, “What did you learn today, my joyful joey?”

Joey patted his heart. “I can help my body be calm. I can wait. I can use tools. First Play, then Quiet, then Help.”

Mama nodded. “That’s self-regulation. When we practice, we feel safe and strong, and we can help others too.”

Joey smiled big. “I am a calm helper!”

The End.
