# Panda Poppy’s Big Bite of Brave

Poppy was a small, patient panda with soft black ears and a gentle smile. One sunny morning, Poppy padded into the dining room. The room smelled like warm noodles and sweet carrots. The table was bright with a yellow cloth, and a little spoon shone like a tiny moon.

“Good morning, table,” Poppy giggled. “I’m ready for breakfast!”

Mama Panda set a bowl down. “Noodle twirls with crunchy carrots,” Mama said. Steam curled up, tickling Poppy’s nose. “Careful, it’s hot.”

Poppy’s tummy rumbled. “My tummy says hurry-hurry,” Poppy whispered.

Mama smiled. “Let’s use calm and courage. We can be brave and patient at the same time.”

Poppy picked up the little spoon. The noodles were slippery and wiggly like tiny snakes. “Uh-oh,” Poppy said. “This is tricky.”

Poppy scooped. Plop! A noodle slid over the edge and onto the tablecloth. Poppy tried again. Splat! A carrot bounced away. Poppy’s cheeks felt warm. Poppy’s paw felt tight.

“I can’t do it,” Poppy huffed. “I feel mad-mad.”

Mama sat beside Poppy. “It’s okay to feel frustrated,” Mama said softly. “Let’s take a slow panda breath.” She showed Poppy: in through the nose, out through the mouth—slow and gentle like a breeze.

Poppy breathed in. Sniff. Poppy breathed out. Whooo. The warm steam smelled like cozy soup. The tight feeling began to melt like a snowflake on a paw.

“You are brave when you try again,” Mama said. “Courage means trying, even when it is hard.”

Poppy looked at the spoon. “Hello, Spoon,” Poppy said. “Let’s be a team.”

“Team Spoon-Panda!” Mama cheered.

Poppy held the spoon with both little paws. Scoop. Hold steady. “Slow and calm,” Poppy whispered. The spoon wiggled. A noodle tried to run away.

“I’m scared it will fall,” Poppy said.

“That scared feeling is okay,” Mama said. “Your brave is bigger than your scared. Count with me. One… two… three.”

Poppy counted. One… two… three. The spoon stayed steady. Poppy marched the spoon to the bowl’s edge—careful, careful—and into Poppy’s mouth. Slurp!

“Mmm!” Poppy smiled. The noodle tasted warm and soft. A carrot crunched like a tiny drum. Crunch-crunch.

“I did it!” Poppy clapped. The spoon clinked on the bowl. Clink!

But the next scoop went splat on the table again. Poppy felt a little mad bubble. “I want to throw the spoon,” Poppy grumbled.

Mama nodded. “I hear your mad bubble. Let’s pop it with calm. Breathe, count, and try again.”

Poppy put the spoon down. Paw to belly. Sniff… whooo. One… two… three. The mad bubble felt smaller.

“I am brave. I can try again,” Poppy said.

Poppy scooped slowly. A noodle climbed the spoon like a tiny slide. Poppy held steady. “Steady, spoon.” Slurp! Another bite!

“Courage!” Mama cheered softly, clapping her big paws.

Poppy felt proud and tall, even while sitting in the small chair. The yellow cloth looked brighter. The spoon looked friendly. The noodles looked like silly twirls dancing in the bowl.

Soon there were only two noodles left. Poppy’s paw felt a little tired. The spoon felt heavy.

“I want to stop,” Poppy sighed.

“Rest is part of brave,” Mama said. “Shake your paws. Wiggle your ears.” Poppy giggled and wiggled. The tired feeling tiptoed away.

Poppy held the spoon again. “Hello, last noodles,” Poppy said. “I will be calm. I will be brave.”

Scoop. Steady. Slurp! The last noodles were sweet and soft and made Poppy’s tummy warm and happy. Poppy licked the spoon. It tasted like carrot and sunshine.

“All done!” Poppy beamed.

Mama wiped a little noodle stripe from Poppy’s chin. “You showed courage. You felt frustrated, but you breathed, you counted, and you tried again.”

Poppy nodded. “My brave is bigger than my mad,” Poppy said. “When I feel tight, I can breathe slow. When I feel scared, I can count. I can try again.”

Mama hugged Poppy. The hug was warm like a blanket. The dining room felt safe and calm. The spoon lay on the table, shiny and proud.

Poppy looked around. The window showed a bright blue sky. The chair made a soft scrape on the floor as Poppy scooted back. Scritch-scratch.

“Today, I was a patient panda,” Poppy said. “I was calm. I used courage.”

Mama smiled. “Yes. And next time something is hard, what will you do?”

Poppy held up three fuzzy fingers. “Breathe slow. Count to three. Try again.”

They both laughed. The laughter sounded like little bells.

Poppy patted the bowl. “Thank you, noodles,” Poppy said. “You helped me practice my brave.”

“Every day is practice,” Mama said. “And you are getting stronger.”

Poppy’s tummy felt full, and Poppy’s heart felt full, too. The dining room smelled sweet and cozy. Poppy hopped down from the chair, ready for the day, ready for small hard things—and ready to be calm and brave.

The lesson: Courage means trying again, even when you feel frustrated. Calm breaths and counting can help.

The End.
