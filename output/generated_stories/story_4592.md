# Ollie the Otter's Bubble Trouble

Ollie the otter loved bath time more than anything in the whole wide world. Every evening, he would slide into the warm, bubbly water with a big splash and a happy giggle.

"Wheee!" Ollie squeaked as he dove under the fluffy white bubbles. The water felt silky smooth against his soft brown fur, and the bubbles tickled his whiskers in the most delightful way.

But tonight was different. When Ollie turned the shiny silver faucet, only a tiny trickle of water came out.

"Drip... drip... drip," went the faucet, making sad little sounds.

Ollie's big round eyes grew wide with worry. "Oh no!" he said, watching the drops fall slowly into the empty bathtub. "How will I have my bubble bath? How will I splash and play?"

He tried turning the handle harder. Still just tiny drops. He tried the other handle too. Nothing but more slow drips.

Ollie sat on the edge of the bathtub, his fluffy tail drooping sadly. For a moment, he felt like crying. But then he remembered what Mama Otter always told him: "When something seems impossible, that's when we get creative!"

"I won't give up," Ollie said firmly, puffing out his furry chest. "There has to be another way!"

He looked around the bathroom with his bright, curious eyes. There was the sink! Ollie scrambled over and turned that faucet. Water gushed out beautifully.

"Hooray!" he cheered. But the sink was much too small for a proper otter bath.

Ollie thought and thought, tapping his little paw against his chin. Then his eyes lit up like sparkly stars.

"I have an idea!" he announced to his rubber duck friend sitting on the counter.

Ollie found the biggest cup in the bathroom - a bright blue one that Mama used for rinsing her hair. He filled it with warm water from the sink, then carefully carried it to the bathtub.

Splash! In went the first cupful.

"This is working!" Ollie giggled, already feeling proud of his clever solution.

Back and forth he went - fill the cup, carry it carefully, pour it in the tub. His little paws worked steadily, and soon he had a rhythm going.

"Fill and pour, fill and pour," he sang happily. "I'm making my bath with my own four paws!"

The water level in the tub grew higher and higher. Ollie added his favorite bubble soap, and soon the bathroom smelled like sweet strawberries.

After many trips, Ollie had enough water for a wonderful bath! He slipped into the warm bubbles with the biggest smile.

"I did it!" he splashed joyfully. "I didn't give up, and I found my own special way!"

As he played with his floating toys, Ollie felt proud and happy. The bath felt even more special because he had worked so hard to make it happen.

When Mama Otter came to check on him, she found Ollie beaming with pride.

"Look, Mama! The big faucet was broken, but I used the sink and this cup to fill my whole bathtub!" Ollie explained excitedly.

"Oh my clever little otter!" Mama said, her eyes twinkling with pride. "You didn't give up when things got difficult. You used your smart thinking to solve the problem all by yourself!"

Ollie splashed happily in his hard-earned bubbles, knowing that with creativity and determination, he could overcome any challenge that came his way.

The End.
