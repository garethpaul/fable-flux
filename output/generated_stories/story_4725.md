# Polly Paints a Plan

The family car hummed softly down the road. The seats were warm, and the air smelled like orange slices and crayons. In the back seat sat a bright green parrot named Polly. Polly wore a tiny blue scarf and had clever eyes. She loved to make art and sing little songs.

“Squawk! Road trip!” Polly chirped, tapping her beak on the window. She saw trees, a sparkling lake, and birds swooping in the sky.

Mia and Max, the children, giggled. “Polly, can you sing us a color song?” Mia asked.

Polly puffed up with joy. “Red apple, green leaf, blue sky, tweet!” she sang, making the car feel happy and bouncy.

As the car turned toward the park, the smell changed. It wasn’t oranges anymore. It smelled yucky, like old socks and sticky soda. Polly’s eyes grew wide.

“Squawk? What’s that smell?” Polly asked.

Dad rolled down the window a little. “Oh no,” he said softly. “The park by the lake looks messy today.”

They parked the car. Everyone looked out. The grass had wrappers. The path had cups. The water had floating bits. Ducks quacked sadly. A breeze carried a papery rustle and a plastic crinkle.

Polly’s feathers drooped. “Poor lake,” she whispered. “Poor ducks.”

Mia frowned. “I don’t like this,” she said.

Max crossed his arms. “Who will fix it?”

Polly tapped her beak. Tap, tap, tap. When Polly tapped, she was thinking. She was a creative parrot, and creative parrots like to make plans.

“I have an idea!” Polly chirped. “We can help. We can volunteer! Volunteer means we use our time to help others and the Earth.”

Mom nodded. “That is a kind idea, Polly.”

“But we need a plan,” Dad said. “And we should be safe.”

Polly bobbed her head. “Plan, plan, plan!”

She looked around the car. There were paper bags. There were extra gloves. There was a big water jug. Polly’s eyes sparkled. “We can make a clean-up team,” she said. “We can pick up trash and sort it. We can make signs to teach people.”

Mia smiled. “I can wear gloves and pick up wrappers!”

Max grinned. “I can hold the bag and help sort!”

Polly flapped her wings. “And I can sing and paint signs with words and pictures. I will use kind colors and big letters.”

Dad said, “We will stay together. We will not touch sharp things. We will ask a park helper for help if we see something tricky.”

“Team Clean!” Polly cheered. “Squawk! Let’s go!”

They slipped on gloves. The ground felt soft under their feet. The sun was warm on their backs. The ducks quacked, as if saying, “Thank you!”

Polly started a happy beat by tapping her beak on the bag. Tap-tap, tap. “Pick it up, pop it in, help the lake and make it grin!” she sang.

Mia picked up a wrapper. “In the trash bag!”

Max found a bottle. “Recycling bag!”

Polly pointed her wing. “Leaf pile stays. Leaves are for bugs and soil,” she said. “We protect nature.”

A park ranger walked by and smiled. “What a great team,” she said. “Thank you for volunteering.”

Polly’s heart felt warm. “We are helping the Earth,” she chirped.

Soon Polly set up a tiny art station on the car’s back seat. She dipped a brush in blue paint and swished it across a big paper. Swish, swish. The paper smelled like new books. “I will make a sign,” Polly said. “It will say: ‘Please Keep Our Lake Happy.’”

She painted a smiling lake with little fish and a duck. She added a picture of a hand dropping trash into a bin. She added a heart. “Love the lake,” she said softly.

Mia made a sign too: “We Care!” Max drew a big green leaf and a bright sun.

They taped the signs near the path with help from the ranger. People slowed down to look. “Oh!” said a jogger. “I will throw my cup away.” A family clapped. “Thank you, helpers!”

Polly felt proud. “Volunteering is working together,” she told Mia and Max. “We see a problem, we make a plan, and we help.”

After a while, the bags were full. The grass looked clean and shiny. The water sparkled again. The ducks swam in circles, quacking happily. The breeze smelled fresh, like pine and water.

Polly hopped back into the car and took a big sip of cool water. “Ahh,” she sighed. “Team Clean did it!”

Dad smiled. “We solved the problem by helping.”

Mom patted Polly’s wing. “And you used your creativity to make signs and songs. That helped everyone learn.”

Polly nodded. “When we volunteer, we show love with our time,” she said. “We can help parks, people, and animals.”

Mia hugged Polly gently. “Can we volunteer again next week?” she asked.

Max nodded. “We can bring friends!”

Polly fluffed her feathers, happy and bright. “Yes! We will sing, paint, sort, and care. We will keep our world clean.”

As the car hummed home, the air smelled sweet and light. Polly sang a final soft song: “Little hands, big hearts, we all can do our parts.”

And everyone in the family car smiled, knowing they had made a difference together.

The End.
