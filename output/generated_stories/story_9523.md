# Bear Cub Timmy and the Tidy Time Plan

Bear cub Timmy had soft brown fur and bright curious eyes. He loved to help. He loved to play. He loved to dream. One sunny morning, Mama Bear said, “Timmy, the neighborhood clean-up starts at noon. We will meet at the garden shed.”

Timmy clapped his paws. “I will help! I will sweep! I will plant!” He could smell the sweet grass and the lemony soap Mama used for buckets. He could hear the bees buzzing by the flowers. His heart felt bouncy with happy plans.

But Timmy also wanted to build a pretend rocket from boxes in the garden shed. “I will have time,” he said. “I can do everything!” He skipped outside with his small red watch on his paw.

At the garden shed, the wooden door creaked. Inside, it smelled like fresh dirt and mint. Sunlight made dust sparkle in the air. Rakes and brooms leaned on the wall. Flower pots stacked like tiny towers. Timmy giggled. “Hello, tools!”

His friend, Squirrel Sam, waved his fluffy tail. “Hi, Timmy! Clean-up is at noon. Don’t be late,” said Sam.

Timmy nodded. “I will be on time.”

Sam pointed to the clock on the shed wall. “Now it is ten o’clock. That is two hours.”

“Two hours is big!” said Timmy. “I can play and help.”

Timmy found a big box. He climbed in. “Zoom!” he said. “My rocket is ready.” He put a shiny spoon as a pretend lever. He wrapped a scarf as a seatbelt. “Five… four… three… two… blast off!” He felt the smooth cardboard under his paws and the cool scarf on his shoulders. He giggled so loud that a robin chirped back.

Knock, knock. The shed door opened. It was Hedgehog Holly. “Hi, Timmy. I came to borrow a small rake. I need it for the clean-up.”

“Okay!” Timmy said. He looked around. Oh no. He had moved the rake to make a rocket wing. The rake was under two boxes and a bucket.

“I will find it fast,” said Timmy. He tugged the bucket. Clank! He pushed the boxes. Thump! Time ticked.

“Thank you,” Holly said, smiling when he handed her the rake. “See you at noon!”

Timmy looked at his watch. “Oh! Now it is eleven o’clock.” His tummy did a tiny flip. “I still have time,” he told himself. He went back to the rocket and added a flower pot as a pretend engine. He hummed a space song. “La-la-zoom!”

The shed door opened again. Turtle Tia peeked in slowly. “Excuse me, Timmy. Do you have the broom? I need to sweep the path for everyone.”

Timmy blinked. “Yes, but… where is it?” He had propped the broom as a rocket tail. He checked under a tarp. Rustle. He checked behind the door. Squeak. He found it at last.

“Thank you,” Tia said. “See you at noon.” Her smile was patient.

Timmy looked at the wall clock. The hands pointed up and to the side. “Eleven-thirty,” he read. His heart thumped. “I am running out of time,” he whispered.

Just then, Mama Bear called from outside, “Thirty minutes, Timmy!”

Timmy sat on a soft bag of soil. He felt the rough burlap and took a deep breath. He looked at his rocket. He looked at the messy tools he moved. He thought about his friends waiting. He thought about the clean-up at noon.

“I need a plan,” he said. “A tidy time plan!”

He spoke his plan out loud, counting on his paw pads:
- “First, ten minutes to put tools back. That shows respect.”
- “Next, ten minutes to get my bucket and gloves ready. That helps everyone. That is civic responsibility.”
- “Last, ten minutes to meet friends at the shed door. That means I am on time.”

Timmy set his red watch. “Beep!” he said, pretending it made a sound. He worked fast but gentle. Clink went the rake on its hook. Swish went the broom in the corner. Tap-tap went the flower pots in a neat line. He whispered, “Thank you, tools, for helping.” The shed felt calm again. It smelled like clean wood and mint.

He put on his small gloves. They were soft and a little sandy. He filled his bucket with seed packets and a tiny shovel. “Ready!”

He checked the clock. “Five minutes left.” He smiled. “I did it!”

Squirrel Sam peeked in. “Wow, Timmy! The shed is neat.”

Timmy stood tall. “I made a tidy time plan. Now I am ready to help.”

At noon, friends gathered at the shed door. Mama Bear smiled. “Right on time, Timmy.”

Timmy grinned. “I was almost late. I played first and lost track. Then I made a plan. I put tools back. I got ready. I came on time.”

Holly nodded. “That helped me find the rake.”

Tia added, “And the path is clean because I had the broom.”

They all worked together. Swish went the brooms. Pat-pat went the soil. Sprinkle went the water. The air smelled fresh and wet. Laughter bubbled like a sweet stream.

When they finished, the garden path shone. Flowers stood tall. The shed was tidy. The friends felt proud.

Timmy patted his rocket box and whispered, “We will play later.” He looked at his friends and said, “Plans help us be kind with time. When we are on time, we show respect. We help our town. We help our friends.”

Mama Bear hugged him. “That is a big lesson.”

Timmy’s eyes sparkled. “Next time, I will plan playtime and help time. I can do both!”

Everyone cheered. “Hurrah for Timmy’s tidy time plan!”

The End.
