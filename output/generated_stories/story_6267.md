# Leo the Lion Cub Makes Things Right

In a warm, sunny kitchen, little Leo the lion cub was having a big problem. His golden fur was messy, and his whiskers drooped down sadly. He had made a terrible mess while trying to help Mama Lion make breakfast.

"Oh no, oh no!" Leo whimpered, looking around the kitchen. Flour covered the counter like snow. Broken eggshells scattered across the floor. Sticky honey dripped from the cabinet door. The sweet smell of honey mixed with the powdery scent of flour.

Leo's little paws had knocked over everything when he tried to reach the mixing bowl. He wanted to surprise Mama by making pancakes all by himself. But now the kitchen looked like a tornado had visited!

"Leo, what happened here?" Mama Lion asked as she walked into the kitchen. Her eyes grew wide when she saw the mess.

Leo's bottom lip trembled. "I wanted to make you breakfast, Mama. But I made everything yucky instead." A tiny tear rolled down his furry cheek.

Mama Lion's voice was gentle but firm. "Leo, making a mess happens sometimes. But what do we do when we make mistakes?"

Leo sniffled and thought hard. "We... we say sorry?"

"That's right, little cub. And what else?"

Leo's ears perked up a little. "We help clean up!"

"Very good!" Mama smiled. "Making amends means saying sorry AND helping to fix what went wrong."

Leo stood up tall on his little legs. "I want to make amends, Mama! I'll clean everything up!"

But when Leo looked at the big mess again, he felt scared. "It's too much, Mama. I'm just a little cub."

"Remember, Leo," Mama said, kneeling down to his level. "We can do hard things when we take them one step at a time. Let's organize our cleaning into small jobs."

Mama helped Leo make a plan. First, they would pick up the eggshells. Then wipe the honey. Finally, sweep up the flour. Leo nodded eagerly, his brave lion heart growing stronger.

"I can pick up these shells!" Leo said, carefully gathering each piece. His small paws worked slowly but steadily. The shells made tiny clicking sounds as he dropped them in the trash.

Next came the sticky honey. Leo used a warm, damp cloth to wipe the cabinet. The cloth felt soft and squishy in his paws. "Look, Mama! The honey is gone!"

"You're doing wonderful, brave cub," Mama encouraged.

The flour was the biggest job. Leo swept with a little broom, pushing the white powder into neat piles. Some flour puffed up in tiny clouds, making Leo giggle. "It's like snow!" he laughed.

After lots of hard work, the kitchen sparkled clean again. Leo's fur was dusty, but his heart felt light and happy. The kitchen smelled fresh and lemony from the cleaning.

"I did it, Mama! I made amends!" Leo announced proudly, puffing out his little chest.

"Yes, you did, my brave lion cub," Mama said, giving him a warm hug. "You said sorry, you worked hard to fix your mistake, and you didn't give up. That's what making amends means."

Leo learned something important that day. When we make mistakes, we can make things right by saying sorry and helping to fix the problem. Even little cubs can do big things when they try their best and don't give up.

From that day on, whenever Leo made a mess, he remembered to make amends. He would say sorry, clean up, and always ask for help when jobs seemed too big. His brave little heart grew stronger with each good choice he made.

The End.
