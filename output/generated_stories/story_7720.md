# Leo the Helper Lion

Leo was a brave little lion cub with a golden mane that sparkled in the sunshine. One morning, he heard strange sounds coming from Grandpa's garage workshop. *Clang! Bang! Thump!*

"Oh my!" said Leo, his ears perking up. "Someone might need help!"

Leo padded into the garage on his soft paws. The workshop smelled like wood shavings and motor oil. Tools hung neatly on the walls, and sunlight streamed through the dusty windows.

There was Grandpa, sitting on a little stool with his head in his hands. A broken toy train lay scattered on the workbench.

"What's wrong, Grandpa?" asked Leo, his voice gentle and caring.

"Oh, Leo," sighed Grandpa. "Little Emma's toy train is broken, and I promised to fix it before her birthday party today. But my old hands are shaking, and I can't see the tiny screws very well anymore."

Leo looked at the broken train. The wheels had fallen off, and some small parts were scattered around. He took a deep breath and thought carefully. "Maybe I can help!" he said with a big smile.

"But Leo, you're just a little cub," said Grandpa kindly. "This is very detailed work."

Leo sat down and looked closely at the train. He noticed his small paws were just the right size for the tiny pieces. His young eyes could see all the little details clearly.

"Grandpa, what if we work together?" suggested Leo. "You can tell me what to do, and I can use my small paws to help!"

Grandpa's face lit up. "What a wonderful idea, Leo! Teamwork makes everything better."

First, Leo carefully picked up each tiny screw with his paws. "I found them all!" he said proudly.

"Good job! Now, can you see the little holes where the wheels attach?" asked Grandpa.

Leo looked very closely, being mindful and patient. "Yes! I can see them perfectly!"

Step by step, Leo helped put the train back together. When a screw was tricky, he didn't give up. He tried again and again until it fit just right.

"This is hard work," puffed Leo, "but I won't stop until we finish!"

Grandpa smiled warmly. "Your persistence is amazing, Leo. You're being such a good helper."

When the last wheel was attached, Leo and Grandpa tested the train. It rolled smoothly across the workbench with a cheerful *choo-choo* sound.

"We did it!" cheered Leo, jumping up and down. "Emma will love her train!"

"Leo," said Grandpa, giving him a gentle hug, "you made the right decision to help today. Your kind heart and helpful hands saved the day."

Later, when Emma received her fixed train at the party, she squealed with joy. "It works perfectly! Thank you!"

Leo felt warm and happy inside. He learned that even little cubs can make a big difference when they choose to help others. Sometimes the best solutions come from working together and never giving up.

That night, as Leo curled up in his cozy bed, he smiled. Tomorrow, he would look for more ways to be helpful, because helping others made his heart feel as bright as sunshine.

The End.
