# Milo the Monkey Learns to Focus

Milo was a little brown monkey with bright, curious eyes and a fluffy tail that never stopped wiggling. He lived with his family in a cozy house where Mama Monkey worked in her home office.

One sunny morning, Milo bounced into Mama's office while she was typing on her computer. The room smelled like fresh coffee and had tall bookshelves filled with colorful books.

"Mama! Mama! Look what I can do!" Milo shouted, doing three somersaults in a row. His tail knocked over a stack of papers, sending them flying like white butterflies.

"Oh my!" said Mama gently. "Milo, I'm working right now. I need quiet time to finish my important project."

But Milo's body felt wiggly and bouncy. He couldn't sit still! He climbed on the desk chair, making it spin round and round. "Wheee!" he squealed with delight.

"Milo," Mama said with a patient smile, "your body seems very excited today. But when I'm working, I need you to use your calm voice and gentle movements."

Milo's ears drooped. He wanted to be good, but his body felt like it had springs inside! "I can't stop wiggling, Mama. My body won't listen to me!"

Mama knelt down and looked into Milo's eyes. "Sometimes our bodies feel very energetic, and that's okay. But we can learn to help our bodies calm down. Would you like me to teach you some special tricks?"

Milo nodded eagerly, his tail still twitching with excitement.

"First, let's try the 'Monkey Breathing,'" Mama said. "Put your hands on your belly and take three slow, deep breaths. Breathe in through your nose like you're smelling a banana, and breathe out through your mouth like you're blowing bubbles."

Milo placed his small hands on his tummy. "In... and out... in... and out..." he whispered. His shoulders began to relax, and his tail stopped wiggling so much.

"Great job!" Mama praised. "Now let's try 'Statue Monkey.' Stand very still like a statue for ten seconds, then you can move again."

Milo stood as still as he could, counting slowly. "One... two... three..." It was hard, but he felt proud when he reached ten.

"Now," said Mama, "let's find something quiet for your hands to do while I work. You can use your resourcefulness to choose an activity."

Milo looked around the office thoughtfully. He spotted some colorful blocks in a basket. "Can I build a tower with these soft blocks?" he asked politely.

"Perfect choice!" Mama smiled. "Building will keep your hands busy and your mind focused."

Milo sat on the soft carpet and began stacking the blocks carefully. When he felt his energy bubbling up again, he remembered to take his monkey breaths. In through his nose, out through his mouth.

After a while, Milo had built a magnificent tower. He felt calm and proud. Mama finished her work too.

"Milo, you did such a wonderful job regulating your body today," Mama said, giving him a warm hug. "You learned to notice when you felt wiggly, you asked for help, and you used your breathing and building to help yourself feel calm."

"I did it!" Milo beamed. "My body listened to me!"

From that day on, whenever Milo felt too energetic, he remembered his special tools: monkey breathing, statue time, and finding quiet activities. He learned that he could help his own body feel calm and focused.

The End.
