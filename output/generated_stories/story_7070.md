# Pepito and the Gentle Switch

Pepito was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a tiny blue scarf. He loved to squawk ideas and paint with his wings. Today, Pepito flapped down the stairs to the basement lab. The lab was cool and a little dusty. It smelled like lemon soap and old books. Lights hummed softly. Machines blinked with red, yellow, and green dots.

“Hello, lab!” Pepito chirped. “Let’s make something kind!”

Pepito liked kind inventions. He wanted to help the Earth. He wanted to be fair to everyone. He also wanted to listen, even when he felt excited.

On the table sat a big glass jar, a stack of paper, and a little wind-up fan. Pepito tapped the jar. Tap tap. “I will make a Gentle Switch,” he said. “A switch that turns things off when we don’t need them. That saves power. That helps the Earth.”

Pepito pulled out crayons. Scratch, scratch. He drew a sunny flower on the jar. He drew a tiny leaf on the switch. He loved the soft crayon smell.

Down in the corner, a small spider named Dot peeked out. Dot had shiny eyes and careful legs. She made thin, neat webs.

“Hello, Dot,” said Pepito.

“Hello,” said Dot in a sweet, small voice. “What are you making?”

“A Gentle Switch!” said Pepito. “It will listen to the room. If the room is quiet and empty, it will turn off the light. If someone comes in, it will turn it on again.”

Dot wiggled a leg. “That sounds helpful. But please listen to me. My web is by the light cord. Be careful.”

Pepito felt a happy flutter. “I hear you,” he said. “I will be careful.” He took a slow breath. He looked closely with his bright parrot eyes. He saw the delicate web shimmer like silver hair.

Pepito found a tiny bell. Ding. He found a soft button. Boop. He found a little paper sign that said, “Save a bit. Share a bit.” He smiled. “Saving is like sharing with the Earth,” he said. “That is fair.”

He began to build. He wrapped a string around the bell. He stuck the soft button to the jar. He clipped the Gentle Switch to the light cord. He moved slowly, like a cloud, so he would not break Dot’s web. He listened to Dot’s tiny breaths. In and out. In and out.

“Thank you for listening,” Dot said.

“You are welcome,” Pepito said. “Listening helps me be kind.”

Just then, the basement door creaked. Creak! Two mice ran in. Pip and Pop wore little caps. They squeaked with joy. “We want to test the fan! We want wind!” Pip shouted. “Me first!” Pop squeaked back. They reached for the fan at the same time.

Pepito fluffed his feathers. “Wait, friends,” he said gently. “Let’s be fair. We can take turns.”

“But I want it now,” Pip said with a pout.

“I want it now, too,” Pop said, crossing tiny arms.

Pepito tapped his beak. Tap tap. “Let’s listen to each other. Pip, you speak. Pop, you listen. Then Pop, you speak. Pip, you listen.”

Pip took a breath. “I am hot. I want a breeze.”

Pop nodded. “I hear you,” Pop said. “I am hot too.”

Pepito smiled. “We can share. We can use the Gentle Switch. The fan will turn on for one song, then turn off to save power. Then the other mouse can have a turn.”

“That is fair,” said Pip.

“That is fair,” said Pop.

Pepito turned the Gentle Switch to Fan Time. Ding! The little bell sang a clear note. The fan spun. Whoosh. The air felt cool and soft on their faces. It smelled like clean fabric and a little bit like wood. Pip giggled. Pop squeaked with joy. When Pepito sang his “One Little Song” tune, the fan slowed. Whirr… click. Off. The Gentle Switch winked. It had saved a bit of power.

“My turn!” said Pop. Ding! The fan spun again. Whoosh. Pepito counted slowly so Pop had the same time. When the song ended, the fan clicked off once more.

“Thank you,” said Pip.

“Thank you,” said Pop.

“You are welcome,” Pepito said. “Sharing is fair. Saving is kind.”

Dot peeped from her web. “Your switch is gentle, Pepito. You listened. You saved power. You were fair.”

Pepito felt warm inside. He looked at the light. The room was bright, but no one was near the switch. He flapped to the door and called, “Anyone still here? Do we need the light?”

The mice and Dot looked around. “We can clean up now,” said Dot. “Then we can go outside and use the big sun.”

Pepito nodded. “Good plan.” He pressed the soft button. Boop. The light dimmed to low. It felt calm, like evening. They picked up paper scraps. They put lids on jars. They put the fan away. The lab grew quiet. The Gentle Switch listened. Click. The light turned off. The room rested.

They climbed the steps. The sun felt warm on their faces. The breeze smelled like grass. The birds sang in the trees. Chirp chirp tweet!

Pepito smiled. “Today I learned that listening keeps friends safe. Sharing makes things fair. Saving power helps the Earth. Little choices help a lot.”

Dot nodded. “Small steps are strong steps.”

Pip and Pop high-fived with tiny paws. “We can do this every day!” they squeaked.

Pepito fluffed his blue scarf. “Yes,” he said. “We can be gentle with our world.”

The End.
