# Penny Penguin and the Treehouse Helpers

Penny was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a bright orange beak. She lived near a big, friendly tree with a cozy treehouse. The treehouse smelled like pine and warm wood. The floor felt smooth under Penny’s little webbed feet. Sunlight made dappled spots that danced on the walls. Penny loved it there.

One morning, Penny waddled up the ladder, one careful step at a time. “Step, step, step!” she sang. “Good morning, treehouse!” She opened the little window and felt a soft breeze. It smelled like leaves and a tiny bit like rain. She heard birds chirp, “Tweet, tweet!”

Penny had a plan. “Today I will make the treehouse tidy,” she said. “I will sort the blocks by color and shape.” She set out red, blue, and yellow baskets. She smiled wide. “This will be fun!”

Then she heard a rustle. A small squirrel peeked in. His name was Squeak. His tail was fluffy and twitchy. “Hi, Penny,” said Squeak. “I need help. I lost my shiny nut.”

Penny paused. She looked at the baskets. She felt a tiny tug inside. She wanted to keep sorting. But she saw Squeak’s worried eyes. She put a flipper on her chest and took a slow breath in and out. “I can wait,” she told herself. “My friend needs me.”

“Of course, Squeak,” said Penny gently. “Let’s find your shiny nut together.”

“Thank you,” said Squeak. “It is smooth and golden. It makes a ‘click’ sound when I tap it.”

Penny nodded. “We will use our thinking. First, we look. Then, we listen.”

They looked under the tiny table. “No nut,” said Penny. They listened near the window. “No click,” said Squeak. They checked the toy shelf. “Nope,” said Penny with a little giggle.

Just then, a soft hoot came. “Hoo-hoo?” It was Olive the little owl. Her feathers were fluffy like clouds. “What are you doing?” she asked.

“We are helping,” said Penny. “We are looking for Squeak’s shiny nut.”

Olive blinked. “I can help too.” She tilted her head and listened. “I hear something… tap-tap… click.” She pointed her wing at the rug.

Penny knelt and lifted the rug corner. There was a tiny bell. “Not the nut,” she said, “but good listening, Olive!”

Squeak’s tail drooped. “Maybe I lost it forever,” he whispered.

Penny scooted close. “I hear your feelings,” she said softly. “You feel sad and worried.”

Squeak nodded. “Yes.”

Penny took another slow breath. She remembered her plan. “Let’s try steps,” she said. “Step one: Think where you had it last. Step two: Trace your steps back. Step three: Ask for help kindly.”

Squeak thought. “I had it when we played store,” he said. “We said, ‘Ding-ding! Fruit is ready!’”

Penny clapped her flippers. “Great clue! Let’s go to the play store.”

They walked to the corner with the wooden boxes. The boxes smelled like apples and pretend bread. Penny opened the fruit crate. “Apples, lemons, grapes,” she counted. “No shiny nut.”

Olive peered into a tiny cup. “I see a marble,” she said. “Still not it.”

Penny looked at the cash register toy. She pressed a button. It made a cheerful “ding!” She opened the drawer. “Look!” she cried.

Inside, wrapped in a little leaf, was the shiny nut. It was smooth, golden, and made a soft “click” when Penny tapped it with her beak.

“My nut!” Squeak squealed. His eyes sparkled. He hugged the nut and then hugged Penny. “Thank you for helping me. You were patient and kind.”

Penny felt warm and happy. “You’re welcome,” she said. “Friends help friends.”

Olive hooted happily. “Teamwork!” she said. “We listened and used steps.”

Penny nodded. “Yes! We used our thinking and our hearts.”

Squeak looked at the baskets. “You were cleaning,” he said. “I made you stop.”

Penny smiled. “It’s okay. My baskets can wait. Helping you was important.”

“I want to help you now,” said Squeak. “We can sort together.”

“Me too,” said Olive. “I can read the labels. Red, blue, yellow!”

They all worked side by side. Penny picked up a red block. “Red in the red basket,” she said. “Plop!” Squeak rolled a blue ball. “Blue in blue,” he said. “Boop!” Olive gently placed a yellow star into yellow. “Soft and safe,” she whispered.

The room began to look neat and bright. The sun made warm patches on the floor. Penny’s heart felt calm. She took one more slow breath. In, out. She smiled at her friends.

When they finished, Penny opened the window again. A cool breeze whooshed in. It smelled like sweet leaves. “We did it,” she said. “We helped Squeak. We kept calm. We used steps. We worked together.”

Squeak held up his nut. “And I will keep it safe,” he said. “I will put it in this tiny box with a leaf.”

Penny tapped the box and giggled. “Click!”

Olive fluffed her feathers. “Today was a good day,” she said.

Penny nodded. “Today I learned that compassion means caring about how others feel and helping them. When we slow down, think, and work together, everyone feels better.”

They sat in the cozy treehouse, listening to the birds sing “tweet-tweet” and feeling the gentle breeze. Penny’s cheerful laugh filled the room, light as a feather.

The End.
