# The Porch Pick-Up Parade

Bunbun the rabbit loved the peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like warm wood and mint tea. The breeze tickled Bunbun’s whiskers. The rocking chair creaked a friendly creak. Birds sang softly in the maple tree.

Every morning, Bunbun stretched tall, wiggled his nose, and said, “I can do good things today!” He was a determined rabbit with big ears and a bigger heart.

One sunny day, Bunbun hopped onto the porch and saw three little bins by the steps. One was blue, one was green, and one was brown. The blue bin said, “Recycling.” The green bin said, “Compost.” The brown bin said, “Trash.”

Bunbun peered inside the blue bin. “Oh dear,” he said. “A banana peel is in recycling. That belongs in compost.” He sniffed the peel. It smelled sweet and mushy. “We can make soil from this!” Bunbun smiled.

He checked the green bin. “Oh no,” he said softly. “A shiny can is in compost. The can belongs in recycling.” He tapped the can. Clink-clink! It sounded like a tiny bell.

Bunbun felt a thump-thump in his chest. “I can fix this,” he said. “I can help.”

Just then, his friend Squirrelly zipped onto the porch with a crinkly bag. “Hi, Bunbun! I brought snacks!”

“Thank you,” said Bunbun. “Let’s sort, too. We can be Porch Helpers.”

Squirrelly opened the crinkly bag and munched. “Crunch-crunch!” Crumbs fell on the mat. He tossed the empty bag toward the green bin.

Bunbun gently caught the bag. “Wait, friend. This bag is not food. It goes in trash or sometimes in a special store drop-off. Today, it goes in trash.”

Squirrelly’s tail drooped. “I did it wrong.”

Bunbun patted his shoulder. “That’s okay. We are learning together.”

From the steps, Tilly Tortoise slowly climbed up, carrying a jar. “Hello, little ones,” she said in a warm, round voice. “I brought mint tea for wellness. It makes minds calm and bellies cozy.”

Steam rose from the jar and smelled fresh and cool. Bunbun breathed it in and felt peaceful.

Tilly set down three tiny cups. “Sip with me on the porch,” she said. “And I will share a tradition. My grandma taught me about sorting. She called it the Pick-Up Parade.”

“A parade?” Squirrelly’s eyes sparkled.

“Yes,” said Tilly. “We sing a little song. We move in a line. We check each bin with care. It turns work into joy.”

Bunbun clapped. “Let’s do it!”

But just then, Rocco Raccoon shuffled by with a cardboard box. “I’m fast,” he bragged. “I can toss this in any bin.” He flipped open the brown bin.

“Wait!” Bunbun called.

Rocco frowned. “Why wait? I’m helping fast.”

Bunbun took a slow breath, enjoying the minty steam. “I feel worried,” he said. “When we toss without looking, the bins get mixed. Then the recycling truck gets sad. The earth gets tired.”

Rocco crossed his arms. “I like fast.”

Tilly poured tea. “Sip first,” she said kindly. “Then listen.”

Rocco sniffed the cup. “Mmm. Cool and calm.” He took a small sip. “Okay. I can listen.”

Bunbun spoke softly. “We want clean air and happy animals. Sorting helps. Cardboard goes in recycling. Banana peels go in compost. Broken things and plastic bags go in trash or a special place.”

Rocco’s ears perked. “Special place?”

“Some stores take bags,” said Squirrelly. “We can ask.”

Rocco looked at his box. He touched the cardboard. “It feels dry and papery. So… blue bin?”

“Yes!” Bunbun smiled. “You did it.”

Rocco grinned. “Maybe I can be fast and careful.”

“Now,” said Tilly, tapping her cup, “the Pick-Up Parade begins.”

They lined up on the porch. The rocking chair creaked the beat. The breeze was their drum. Tilly sang the tradition-sharing song:

“Blue for bottles, cans, and box,
Green for peels and veggie stalks,
Brown for things we cannot use,
We look, we think, we kindly choose.”

Bunbun sang too, his voice bright. Squirrelly hummed and swished his tail. Rocco clapped the rhythm. They marched in a small circle, step-step, hop-hop, clap-clap.

Bunbun gently moved the banana peel to compost. He placed the shiny can in recycling. He put the crinkly bag in the trash for now. He held up an apple core. “Green bin!” he cheered. Plop! It made a friendly soft sound.

Rocco found a glass jar. “Blue bin,” he said, tapping it—ting! Squirrelly picked up a paper napkin with food on it. “Green bin,” he whispered, and tucked it in.

They worked together, singing and smiling. The porch felt neat and fresh. The bins sat in the sun, tidy and proud.

Tilly raised her cup. “Well done, Helpers. This is wellness for the earth and for us. When we care, our hearts feel light.”

Rocco nodded. “I learned to slow down.”

Squirrelly said, “I learned to look first.”

Bunbun puffed his chest. “I learned to lead with kindness.”

He posted a simple sign by the steps with three colors and little pictures. “This will help everyone,” he said.

The friends sat and sipped the last of the mint tea. The breeze smelled like leaves and clean wood. A bluebird chirped, “Cheer-cheer!”

Bunbun looked at the happy bins and felt proud. “Today,” he said, “we helped the earth from our peaceful front porch.”

Tilly smiled. “And tomorrow, we can share the tradition again.”

The End.
