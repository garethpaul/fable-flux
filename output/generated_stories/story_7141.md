# Benny's Big Art Day

In the bright art room, Benny Beaver sat at a small wooden table. Colorful paints lined the shelves. Brushes stood tall in glass jars. The smell of fresh paper filled the air.

Benny's big front teeth clicked together excitedly. Today was art day! He wanted to paint the biggest, most beautiful tree picture ever.

"I need ALL the brown paint!" Benny said loudly. He grabbed every brown paint jar with his little paws. The jars clinked together as he pulled them close.

Behind him, a small mouse named Mia watched sadly. She wanted to paint her tiny house brown too. But Benny had taken all the brown paint.

"Excuse me," Mia squeaked softly. "Could I have some brown paint please?"

Benny's fluffy tail twitched. "No! I'm painting a HUGE tree. I need every drop!" He hugged the paint jars tighter.

Mia's whiskers drooped. She sat down with just her red and yellow paints. Her paper stayed mostly white.

Soon, a friendly rabbit named Ruby hopped over. "What's wrong, Mia?" she asked, her pink nose twitching.

"I wanted to paint my house brown, but Benny took all the paint," Mia whispered.

Ruby looked at Benny. He was struggling with his big paintbrush. Brown paint dripped everywhere. His tree looked messy and wobbly.

"Benny," Ruby called gently. "How's your painting going?"

Benny's ears drooped. "It's too hard! The brush is too big, and the paint keeps dripping. I can't make my tree look right."

Ruby smiled kindly. "Maybe Mia could help you. She's really good with small details."

Benny looked at tiny Mia. Her small paws held her brush perfectly steady. "But I need all this brown paint," he said uncertainly.

"What if we shared?" Mia suggested. "I only need a little bit for my house. You could keep most of it for your tree."

Benny thought hard. His tree did look messy. Maybe sharing wasn't so bad.

"Okay," Benny said slowly. "You can have some brown paint. But can you help me with the tiny branches? Your small paws are much better than mine."

Mia's eyes sparkled. "Yes! And you could help me paint my big roof. Your brush makes perfect wide strokes!"

They moved their papers together. Benny carefully poured some brown paint into a small cup for Mia. The sweet smell of paint mixed with friendship.

Mia painted delicate twigs on Benny's tree. Each branch was perfectly straight and neat. Benny painted Mia's roof with smooth, even strokes. His big brush worked wonderfully for large spaces.

"This is fun!" Benny laughed. His tail wagged happily.

"Much better than painting alone," Mia agreed, her whiskers twitching with joy.

Ruby watched them work together. "Look how beautiful both paintings are now!"

Benny's tree had a strong brown trunk and tiny perfect branches. Mia's house had a smooth brown roof and bright red walls. Both pictures were better than they could have made alone.

"I'm sorry I didn't want to share," Benny said. "Working together made everything better."

"That's okay," Mia smiled. "Now we're both happy with our paintings!"

They hung their artwork side by side on the wall. The tree and house looked like they belonged together - just like their new friendship.

From that day on, Benny always remembered that sharing and working together made everything more fun and beautiful.

The End.
