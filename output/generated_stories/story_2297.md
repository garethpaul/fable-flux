# Poppy the Patient Panda and the Pantry Parade

Poppy was a patient panda with soft black-and-white fur and bright, curious eyes. She lived with her family in a cozy home where the pantry smelled like warm bread, sweet honey, and crunchy rice cakes. Today was Pantry Parade Day, and Poppy had a big job. She would help put snacks on the lower shelf so everyone could reach them.

Mama Panda opened the pantry door. “We will work together,” Mama said with a smile. “We will take turns and finish our task.”

Poppy nodded. “I can do that,” she said softly. She felt calm and ready.

Just then, her friends arrived. Nia the squirrel zoomed in with a bag of nuts. Tiko the turtle carried a jar of peanut butter slowly, step… step… step. Lila the little leopard held a tiny tin of tea leaves that smelled like flowers. “We came to help!” they cheered.

Poppy clapped her paws. “Thank you! Let’s line up. We can take turns. I will go first, then Nia, then Tiko, then Lila.”

Nia’s tail twitch-twitch-twitched. “But I am fast! I want to go first,” Nia said, bouncing.

Poppy took a deep breath. She remembered to be both patient and brave. “Nia,” she said kindly, “we can take turns. I will go first just once. Then it will be your turn. We will all get a turn.”

Nia wiggled but nodded. “Okay,” she said, “I can wait.”

Poppy stepped into the pantry. The floor felt cool under her paws. She lifted a box of rice cakes. Crunchy crumbs rattled inside, like tiny rain. She placed the box on the lower shelf. “My turn is done,” Poppy said with a smile.

Nia hopped forward. “My turn!” She set the nuts beside the rice cakes. “Listen,” she giggled, shaking the bag. “Nut-nut rattle!” The sound made everyone laugh.

Tiko the turtle moved next. “I am slow, but steady,” he said. He placed the peanut butter down with a soft thump. “Done,” he whispered, a little proud smile on his face.

Lila held up the tiny tea tin. “This is from far away,” she said. “My grandma sent it from a country called India. It smells like flowers and warm sun.” Lila opened the tin for just a second. The pantry filled with the gentle smell of tea leaves. Poppy closed her eyes and breathed in. “It smells like a quiet garden,” she said.

They were doing great, but then the honey jar rolled to the edge of a high shelf. The golden honey shivered inside. “Oh no!” Nia squeaked. “It might fall!”

Poppy looked up. She felt a little flutter in her tummy. “We need a plan,” she said calmly. “Let’s take turns to fix it.”

“I can climb,” said Nia, twitching.

“I am steady,” said Tiko, standing firm.

“My paws are strong,” said Poppy.

“My eyes are sharp,” said Lila.

Poppy stood tall. “I will go first and hold the stool. Tiko, you hold the base. Nia, you can climb. Lila, you tell us when we are close.” She spoke clearly and kindly. She was being assertive, using her voice to help everyone.

They moved into place. Poppy held the little stool. It felt smooth and cool. Tiko pushed his shell to keep it steady. Nia climbed slowly, step… step… step. Lila watched closely. “Closer,” Lila said softly. “A little more. Stop! You are there.”

Nia hugged the honey jar to her chest. “Got it!” she chirped. Carefully, she climbed down. Poppy and Tiko kept the stool still. When Nia reached the floor, everyone cheered.

“Now, we’ll take turns again,” Poppy said. “My turn to place the honey, then Nia, you can line up the crackers. Tiko, you can set the napkins. Lila, you can put the tea in a safe spot.”

They followed the plan. The pantry began to look neat and friendly. Boxes lined up like a little parade. Crackers stood like tiny houses. Napkins made a soft, snowy stack. The tea tin sat like a small treasure from far away. Poppy felt warm and proud.

At last, Mama Panda came back. She peeked into the pantry and smiled. “You finished the task together,” she said. “I see taking turns. I see strong voices and kind listening. I see careful hands.”

Poppy’s heart felt light. “We did it by taking turns,” she said. “We used our voices to share ideas. We were patient. We were safe.”

Nia nodded. “Waiting was hard,” she said, “but it felt good.”

Tiko added, “Slow and steady helped.”

Lila hugged the tea tin. “And our world is big,” she said. “Snacks and tea can come from many places. We can all share.”

Poppy looked at her friends. “When we take turns, we all get a chance. When we use kind voices, we finish our tasks. And when we listen to each other, our pantry—and our world—feels bright and peaceful.”

The friends sat on the soft rug and shared a simple snack: rice cakes with a tiny swirl of honey. The crunch was happy, and the sweet taste made them giggle. Poppy licked a drop of honey from her paw. “Mmm,” she said. “Teamwork tastes sweet.”

The End.
