# Tully the Turtle Tidies Up

In a cozy little house, the laundry room hummed and whirred. The washing machine sang a soft swish-swish. The dryer made a warm whoosh. Soap bubbles sparkled like tiny stars on the floor.

Tully was a shy turtle who liked quiet corners and slow mornings. He wore a small blue bandana. He kept a paintbrush tucked in his shell, just in case he felt brave enough to make art. Today, Tully peeked into the laundry room and blinked.

“Oh,” Tully whispered. “It’s messy.”

There were socks on the floor, towels in a heap, and a basket flipped on its side. A little bottle of lavender soap lay on its belly, and a sweet smell floated in the air.

Tully’s heart beat thump-thump. He pulled his head in, just a little. “I’m shy,” he told himself. “I don’t like big messes. They feel loud.” He sat very still and took a slow breath in through his nose. He blew it out like he was cooling soup. In. Out. Slow and soft.

He peeked again. “I can help,” he said in a tiny voice. “Helping is a healthy habit.”

Just then, a friendly mouse named Miri scampered in, carrying a cloth. “Hi, Tully,” Miri chirped. “I’m here to sweep crumbs. Oh! The laundry looks upset.”

Tully nodded. “It feels too big for me,” he said. His cheeks felt warm. “When things are big, I get tight in my shell.”

Miri sat beside him. “Let’s use peaceful words with the mess,” she said. “We can tell the room what we need.”

Tully blinked. “Talk to the room?”

Miri smiled. “It helps us know our plan. Ready?”

Tully took another soft breath. “Laundry room,” he said, “I feel worried when you are messy. I need a calm space to move and paint. I can help one little step at a time.”

The washing machine swish-swished like it agreed.

Miri clapped. “That was kind and clear.”

Tully looked at the socks. They were all different colors—red, yellow, green. They felt silky in his hands. “Step one,” he said. “Sock pairs.”

He sang a tiny song as he worked. “Find a friend, sock at the end. Red meets red. Green meets green.” The socks made a soft flup when they matched. That sound made Tully smile.

“Step two,” said Miri. “Towels!”

Tully pressed the towels flat with his gentle turtle hands. They were warm from the dryer. “Mmm,” he hummed. “Like sunshine.” He folded one, then two, then three. Each fold felt like a hug.

He started to feel braver. His head came out from his shell a little more. His shoulders softened.

“Step three,” Miri squeaked, pointing to the spilled soap. “We clean the spill.”

Tully looked at the lavender soap. The scent was sweet and calm. He took a breath in. He blew a breath out. “We can do that,” he said. He dabbed with the cloth. He wiped in little circles. The floor went from slippery to safe.

Suddenly, the dryer buzzed. Bzzz! Tully jumped and hid in his shell. His eyes felt watery.

Miri placed her tiny paw on his shell. “It’s okay,” she said softly. “That sound surprised us. Let’s try a mindful pause.”

Tully listened to his breath. In. Out. He counted to three. One, two, three. He felt his feet on the floor. He felt the warm room. He peeked out. “Thank you, Miri,” he said. “I feel better.”

They opened the dryer door together. Warm air puffed out like a friendly cloud. Tully giggled. “It smells like clean cotton candy, but not sticky.”

Now the room looked neat. The basket stood tall again. The socks were happy in pairs. The towels were stacked like a soft castle. The bottles were closed and safe.

Miri tilted her head. “You did a lot, Tully.”

Tully touched his paintbrush. “I want to make a laundry-room picture,” he said. “Art helps me share feelings.”

He spread a small paper on the folding table. With gentle strokes, he painted a smiling washing machine, a tidy basket, and a tiny turtle breathing with a little heart by his nose. He added dots of purple for lavender, and a sunny yellow corner to show the warm feeling in his chest.

Miri watched. “It’s beautiful,” she whispered.

Tully looked at his picture. “When I feel shy,” he said, “I can breathe slow. I can say what I need. I can do one small step, then the next. That is a healthy habit.”

Miri nodded. “And I can listen and help. That is a peaceful habit.”

Tully taped the picture to the wall. The tape made a soft rip-rip sound. He stood back. The laundry room seemed to smile.

He patted his shell. “Thank you, body,” he said. “You told me when you were tight. I listened and took care.” His words were soft and kind.

Miri wriggled her whiskers. “Snack time?”

Tully laughed. “Yes. But first, hands.” They washed their hands with warm water and bubbly soap. The bubbles popped like tiny kisses. Pop, pop, pop.

“Clean room, clean hands, calm hearts,” Tully said.

“Teamwork,” Miri added.

They left the laundry room tidy and bright. The humming turned to a soft lullaby behind them.

The End.
