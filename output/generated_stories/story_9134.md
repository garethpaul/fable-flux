# Pebble’s Nursery Story Circle

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a tiny orange beak. He waddled into the comfortable nursery, where soft rugs cushioned his feet and warm sunlight made the room glow. The air smelled like crayons and clean soap. Gentle music hummed like a lullaby, and books lined a low shelf like colorful friends.

“Good morning!” Pebble chirped, flapping his little wings. His friends—Mina the mouse, Toby the turtle, and Lila the lamb—looked up from building a tower with big, soft blocks.

“Want to play?” Mina squeaked.

“Yes!” Pebble said. He waddled faster, his feet making a tap-tap sound on the mat. He picked up a red block. It felt smooth and cool in his flippers.

“Let’s build a castle,” Toby said in a slow, friendly voice.

“A castle for stories,” Lila added. “We can sit inside and tell tales!”

Pebble’s eyes sparkled. He loved stories. He loved the swish of pages and the whispers of words.

They stacked blocks—blue, yellow, green. Up, up, up!

Then, suddenly—bump! The tower wobbled. Pebble had pushed his block a little too hard. The castle leaned like a sleepy tree.

Crash!

Blocks scattered with a clatter. Mina’s whiskers drooped. Toby pulled into his shell. Lila’s wooly forehead wrinkled.

Pebble’s tummy felt squirmy. “Oh no,” he whispered. “I’m sorry.”

Mina crossed her tiny arms. “You knocked it down.”

“I didn’t mean to,” Pebble said, eyes wide and wet. “I got too excited.”

Miss Star, the teacher with a gentle smile and star-shaped earrings, walked over. She smelled like oranges and hugs. “I see a problem,” she said softly. “The castle fell, and feelings are hurt.”

Pebble nodded. “I made a mistake.”

Miss Star knelt so she could see everyone’s faces. “Let’s use our calm breaths,” she said. “In like smelling a flower, out like blowing a bubble.”

They all breathed in slowly. The room felt softer. They breathed out slowly. The room felt safe.

“Now,” Miss Star said, “let’s use our words. Pebble, what happened?”

Pebble held the red block close. “I pushed too fast. I’m sorry for knocking it down.”

Mina’s ears lifted. “I felt mad,” she said. “I worked hard.”

“I felt scared,” Toby added in his quiet voice. “The crash was loud.”

“I felt sad,” Lila said. “We wanted a story castle.”

Miss Star nodded. “Thank you for sharing your feelings. That is knowledge. When we know our feelings, we can solve problems.”

Pebble’s flippers trembled. “How can I fix it?” he asked.

Mina looked at the blocks. “Maybe we can build again.”

“But this time,” Toby said, peeking out, “slow and gentle.”

“And we can make a plan,” Lila suggested.

Miss Star smiled. “That sounds like a good solution. Let’s plan with our storytelling minds.”

Pebble’s eyes brightened. “We can tell the castle a story while we build! The story will help us go slow.”

Miss Star clapped softly. “Wonderful. Stories can guide our actions.”

Pebble began, his voice sweet and steady. “Once upon a time, a careful penguin built a castle with friends. He took one small block and set it down, tap.”

Pebble placed a block gently. Tap.

Mina continued, “A brave mouse added a block, tip-toe.”

Tip-toe. She set a yellow block on top.

Toby nodded. “A wise turtle checked the sides, slow and steady.”

Slow and steady, he lined up a green block, making sure it was straight.

Lila sang, “A kind lamb waited her turn and smiled.”

She waited, then placed a blue block with a soft smile.

Together, they told the story and stacked the blocks. The tower grew. Pebble felt calm. He listened to the words. He felt the smooth blocks. He watched his friends’ happy faces. The nursery smelled like fresh paper, and the sunlight warmed his feathers.

“Almost there,” Miss Star whispered. “Remember our plan.”

Pebble took a breath, in like a flower, out like a bubble. He placed the last red block gently. The castle stood tall and cozy, with a doorway just big enough for them to sit.

“We did it!” Mina squeaked.

“We solved the problem,” Toby said.

“We used knowledge and kind words,” Lila added.

Pebble beamed. “We told a story to help us.”

They crawled inside the castle. The soft rug tickled Pebble’s feet. The castle made a quiet echo. Pebble opened a picture book. The pages smelled like new adventures.

Miss Star sat nearby. “What did we learn?” she asked.

Pebble raised his flipper. “When there is a problem, we can breathe, share feelings, and make a plan.”

Mina nodded. “We can use our words.”

Toby smiled. “We can go slow.”

Lila whispered, “We can tell stories to remember what to do.”

Miss Star hugged them with her eyes. “That is conflict-resolution—solving problems with calm hearts and smart choices.”

Pebble felt warm and proud. He looked at his friends. “Thank you for helping.”

“And thank you for saying sorry,” Mina said, patting his flipper.

“Now,” Pebble giggled, “let’s read our castle a bedtime story.”

They read together, voices soft as feathers. The castle stood strong. The nursery glowed. Their hearts felt light and happy.

The End.
