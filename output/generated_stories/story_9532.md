# Bucky Beaver’s Bathtub Gallery

Bucky was a busy beaver with bright teeth and a happy tail. He loved to make things. He loved to build. He loved to draw. One sunny morning, Bucky waddled into the bathroom. Drip, drip, drip went the sink. Plink, plink, plink went the tub.

“Good morning, bathroom!” Bucky said. He took a big sniff. “Mmm, it smells like bubble soap and minty toothpaste.”

Bucky saw a foggy mirror. He saw soft towels. He saw a shiny faucet that sparkled like a star. Then he saw something else—an empty white wall right by the tub.

Bucky’s eyes grew wide. “A blank wall is like a blank page,” he whispered. “It’s a place for art!”

He opened the little cabinet. Inside were cotton swabs, a hairbrush, and some colorful bath crayons. “Art time!” Bucky cheered. “I will make a bathtub gallery!”

Bucky dipped a sponge in warm water. Squish, squish. He wiped the wall. “Clean wall, happy art,” he said. He felt excited in his tummy and a little wiggly in his tail.

He picked a blue bath crayon. “Hello, blue,” he said. “Let’s draw the river.” He drew soft, wavy lines that curved like a smile. Swish, swish, swish.

He picked green. “Hello, green,” he said. “Let’s draw tall trees.” He made long trunks and round tops. Plop, plop, plop went drops from the wet sponge. The bathroom smelled warm and soapy.

He picked yellow. “Hello, sunny sun,” he giggled. He drew a bright circle with little lines for rays. “Sunshine in the bathroom!”

Bucky hummed. He felt calm and proud. He listened to the gentle drip and his own happy hum. “I am making art,” he said. “Art helps me show my feelings.” He drew a big heart. “Today I feel thankful.” He put a tiny smile inside the heart. “This is my gratitude heart.”

Bucky stepped back. “Hmm,” he said. He felt a little flutter in his chest. “Is it okay to draw on the wall?” He looked at the bath crayons. The box said, “For tub and tile. Washes off with water.”

Bucky nodded. “These are safe for the bathroom,” he said. “I can make art and still take care of our home.” He patted the wall gently. “Art and care can go together.”

He added a beaver dam made of brown lines. “This is my home,” he said. Then he drew a little toothbrush with sparkles. “This is caring for me.” He drew a tiny trash can with a lid. “This is caring for our place.”

Just then, Mama Beaver peeked in. “Hello, Bucky,” she said with a smile. “I hear humming. What are you making?”

“My bathtub gallery!” Bucky said. His tail thumped softly on the rug. “Look! A river, trees, a sun, a heart, a home, and a toothbrush! I used bath crayons. They wash off. I checked.”

Mama looked closely. She touched the heart. “I see your gratitude heart,” she said. “What are you thankful for?”

“I’m thankful for our clean water, our bright towels, and you,” Bucky said. His cheeks felt warm. “I feel happy and a little shy.”

Mama nodded. “Thank you for sharing your feelings,” she said. “That is emotional awareness. You noticed your feelings and said them out loud.”

Bucky smiled. “Art helps me tell my feelings,” he said. “And I want to be careful. I want to be responsible.”

“That’s civic responsibility,” Mama said softly. “We take care of our space so everyone can enjoy it. We make art in the right places. We clean up when we are done.”

Bucky looked at his gallery. He felt proud like a tall tree. “Can I finish one more picture?” he asked. “I want to draw a friend.”

“Yes,” said Mama. “Then we’ll tidy together.”

Bucky picked the red crayon. He drew a little fish with big eyes. “Hello, fish friend,” he said. “Thank you for swimming in my river.” He added bubbles. Pop, pop, pop.

“Now,” Bucky said, “I will put the crayons back.” He clicked each cap on. Click, click, click. He lined them up. He put them in the cabinet.

He turned on the warm water. Whoosh. He dipped the sponge. Squeeze, squeeze. “Time to clean,” he said. He wiped the wall gently. Swish, swish, swish. The sun, the trees, the fish, and the heart faded like a soft cloud. The tiles shone again.

Mama wiped, too. Together they watched the water run clear. “Thank you for helping,” Bucky said.

“Thank you for sharing your art,” Mama said. “What did you learn today?”

Bucky took a deep breath. He smelled the minty toothpaste again. He felt calm and steady. “I learned that art is fun and helps me show feelings. I learned to be thankful. I learned to use the right tools in the right place. I learned to clean up and care for our home.”

Mama hugged Bucky. “That is a big day,” she said.

Bucky grinned. “Tomorrow I will make new art,” he said. “Maybe on paper. Maybe outside with chalk. I will ask first. I will share. I will clean up. I will say thank you.”

The bathroom was quiet. Drip, drip, drip. Bucky placed a small paper sign on the counter. It said, “Bucky’s Bathtub Gallery—Closed for Cleaning. Thank you!”

He felt proud, responsible, and loved. His heart felt like a warm sun.

“Good night, bathroom,” Bucky whispered. “Thank you for being my art place today.”

The End.
