# Timmy Turtle’s Pantry Plan

Timmy was a shy turtle who liked quiet places. He lived near a cozy kitchen. One day, Timmy peeked into the pantry. It smelled like warm bread and sweet cocoa. The shelves were tall and tidy. Jars of beans clicked softly. Boxes of crackers made a papery rustle. Timmy’s shell felt calm in the cool, dim light.

“Hello?” Timmy whispered. “Is anyone here?”

“Down here!” squeaked Pip the pantry mouse. Pip had bright eyes and a tiny tool belt. “I’m building a snack shelf for sharing. But I need help. It must be done before the big Snack Time bell rings!”

Timmy tucked his head a little. “I’m shy,” he said softly. “I move slow.”

“That’s okay,” Pip said kindly. “We can plan. Good time plans help everyone.”

Timmy’s head peeked out. “A plan?”

“Yes!” Pip pointed to a sticky note. “We have three jobs. One, sort. Two, stack. Three, sweep. The bell rings in one hour. If we use our time well, we can build the shelf and be ready.”

Timmy took a gentle breath. He liked steps. Steps felt safe. “Let’s make a plan,” he said.

Pip nodded. “First, we use ten minutes to collect. Then twenty minutes to build. Then twenty minutes to put snacks on the shelf. Last, ten minutes to sweep and check. That makes… sixty minutes!”

Timmy smiled a small smile. “That is the whole hour.”

Pip set a tomato-shaped timer on a flour tin. Tick-tick-tick went the timer. Timmy’s heart went tick-tick-tick too, but in a softer way.

“Job One: Collect,” Pip said. “Ready, go!”

Timmy looked left and right. He made a list out loud. “Smooth jars. Light boxes. Round fruit cups.” He moved slow but steady. He used his strong turtle legs to push a basket. The floor felt cool under his feet. A jar clinked. “I will carry the heavy things,” he said. “Slow can be safe.”

Pip zipped around like a breeze. “I’ll get the light stuff!” he said, scooping napkins and plastic spoons. “We are a good team.”

“Time check,” Pip called. He tapped the timer. “Two minutes left!”

Timmy saw a wobbling syrup bottle. He carefully hugged it to his shell. “Safe and sound,” he said. The ten minutes ended with a ding. They had a neat pile.

“Job Two: Build,” said Pip. “We have boards and pegs. Let’s think first.”

Timmy nodded. “Think first. Move next.” He touched the boards. They were smooth and smelled like pine. “Big board on the bottom. Little boards on top.” He counted softly, “One, two, three holes.”

Pip read the picture guide. “Peg goes here. Screw goes there. We can match shapes.”

Timmy held pieces steady while Pip turned a tiny screwdriver. Twirl-twirl-twirl went the screw. Tap-tap went Timmy’s careful claw as he nudged a peg. The shelf stood a little crooked.

Timmy tilted his head. “It leans.” He felt shy again. “Did I do it wrong?”

Pip patted his shell. “You noticed it. That’s good thinking. Let’s try again.” They looked at the picture. “Ah! The long peg goes in the wide hole.”

Timmy swapped the peg. He pressed down gently. The shelf settled flat. “It stands!” he said. His smile grew.

“Time check—ten minutes left for building,” Pip said. “We’re right on time.”

They added the last board. Pip tested it. It did not wobble. “Strong and safe!”

“Job Three: Put snacks on the shelf,” Pip said. “We have twenty minutes.”

Timmy liked this part. He made a simple rule. “Big things down low. Light things up high.” He placed the large soup cans on the bottom. Thunk. He put crackers on the middle. Rustle. He set apples on top. Thump-thump, sweet smell.

Pip sorted by shape and color. “Red boxes together. Round jars together.” He made labels that said Soup, Crackers, Fruit. He used big, clear letters.

They worked in a gentle rhythm. Timmy hummed, “Slow and steady.” Pip sang, “Sort and ready!”

“Time check—five minutes left,” said Pip.

“Job Four: Sweep and check,” Timmy said. He liked saying the plan. It made him brave. They swept crumbs. Swish-swish. They wiped the shelf. Wipe-wipe. They checked the labels. “Can we read them?” Timmy asked.

“Yes,” Pip said, pointing. “Soup. Crackers. Fruit.”

Ding! The Snack Time bell rang. The pantry felt bright, even in the dim light. The shelf looked neat and strong. It smelled like apples and clean wood.

Neighbors peeked in. Buzz the beetle, Flo the moth, and Gus the ant came to see. “Wow!” said Buzz. “So tidy!”

Flo fluttered. “And safe!”

Gus nodded. “Good building.”

Timmy tucked his head for a second, then lifted it. “We used a plan,” he said slowly. “We used our time. We did each job.”

Pip smiled. “Timmy, your slow and steady was perfect for the time plan. You noticed the lean. You kept the heavy jars safe. You helped us finish on time.”

Timmy’s shell warmed with pride. He felt shy, but also brave. “Thank you,” he whispered.

They shared snacks. Crunch went the crackers. Sweet went the apples. The friends chewed and laughed softly. The pantry felt like a small, kind world.

Buzz asked, “What is the trick?”

Timmy thought. He touched the tomato timer. Tick-tick. “Make a plan. Do one thing at a time. Check the time. Check your work.”

Pip added, “And help each other. Different speeds can fit together.”

Timmy nodded. “Slow can be smart.”

Everyone smiled. The shelf stood tall. The plan had worked. Timmy’s shy heart felt strong.

The End.
