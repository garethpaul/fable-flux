# Cubby’s Cozy Bedroom Dance

Cubby was a friendly bear cub with soft brown fur and a round, wiggly belly. He loved hugs, honey toast, and warm blankets. Cubby’s cozy bedroom smelled like clean sheets and a little like cinnamon from last night’s snack. Sunlight made golden squares on the rug, and the small blue night-light still blinked like a sleepy star.

“Good morning, bedroom,” Cubby said, rubbing his eyes. He stretched his paws up, up, up, and his toes peeked from under his blanket. He heard birds chirp outside. Chirp, chirp! He felt happy and bouncy.

Mama Bear opened the door with a smile. “Good morning, Cubby. Time to make your bed and put your toys away.”

Cubby looked at his bed. The blanket was crumpled like a mountain. His fluffy pillow had slid to the floor with a soft thump. Blocks and little cars waited under the chair like tiny neighbors.

“Chores…” Cubby’s ears drooped. He wanted to play, not tidy. “Do I have to?”

Mama Bear nodded gently. “Yes, Cubby. Little chores help us care for our home. But we can make it fun. We can dance!”

Cubby blinked. “Dance… and do chores?”

Mama Bear laughed softly. “Dance makes bodies strong and hearts happy. Dancing can help you move, clean, and smile all at once. Want to try?”

Cubby felt curious. He took a deep breath and smelled the clean blanket and cinnamon. His paws tingled with new energy. “Okay! Let’s try a cleaning dance!”

Mama Bear clapped a steady beat. Clap, clap… clap, clap. “We’ll start with the Blanket Swoosh.”

Cubby grabbed one corner of the blanket. It felt soft and warm. Mama Bear hummed a gentle tune, like a bumblebee. Mmm-mmm, la-la! Cubby stepped to the side—slide, slide—and swooshed the blanket up like a big wave. The blanket floated and fell softly onto the bed. Swoosh… whoosh… plop!

Cubby giggled. “The Blanket Swoosh is fun!”

“Now the Pillow Pop,” Mama Bear said.

Cubby bent his knees. He felt the carpet tickle his feet. “Pop!” He bounced and gently tossed the pillow into the air. It landed just right. Pop! Pop! Cubby clapped. “I did it!”

“You’re moving with the music,” Mama Bear said. “Your body likes to move.”

Cubby nodded. His cheeks felt warm and happy. He wanted more. “What’s next?”

Mama Bear looked at the toys. “The Toy Tippy-Toe.”

Cubby put his paws together like a little dancer. Tip, tip, tip! He tippy-toed to the blocks. The blocks were smooth and cool. He picked them up and set them in the bin. Plunk, plunk. He swayed his hips. Wiggle, wiggle! He felt light and bouncy, like a bubble.

He danced to the cars. He hummed a silly car song. “Vroom, vroom, zoom, zoom!” He rolled each car into its garage box. Click! He spun in a tiny circle and laughed. The room looked neater already. Cubby breathed in and smelled the clean air. It felt good inside his chest.

Mama Bear smiled. “You’re doing the Clean-up Cha-Cha.”

Cubby tried the steps. “Cha-cha-cha!” Step, tap, step, tap. He lifted the socks from the floor—soft, warm—and put them in the laundry basket. Cha-cha-cha! He wiggled his shoulders and shook his tail. Shake-a-shake! His ears bounced.

“Now the Book Boogie,” Mama Bear said, pointing to the little books. “Gentle feet, careful paws.”

Cubby picked up a picture book with shiny pages. He hugged it to his chest. The cover felt smooth. He skipped once, twice. Skip, skip! He placed the book on the shelf. “Good night, book,” he whispered, even though it was morning. He boogied to the next book and the next. Boogie, boogie, place! Soon the shelf was neat.

Cubby looked around. His cozy bedroom was tidy and bright. The rug was clear for more dancing. The golden sunlight looked even more sparkly. He felt proud. His heart beat thump-thump, happy and strong.

“I did it!” Cubby said. He raised his paws like a dancer on a stage.

Mama Bear clapped. “Bravo, Cubby! You were open to trying something new. You moved your body, and you helped our home.”

Cubby’s eyes were wide. “I didn’t think chores could be fun. Dancing made it fun!”

Mama Bear nodded. “When we are open-minded, we can find joy in new ways. Dance helps us learn rhythm, balance, and listening. It helps us feel our feelings and move them.”

Cubby listened to his breath—whoosh in, whoosh out. He felt calm and strong. “Can we have a good-bye dance?”

“Of course,” said Mama Bear. She turned in a slow circle and whispered, “This is the Thank-You Twirl.”

Cubby copied her. Twirl… twirl… stop! He didn’t get dizzy because he kept his eyes on Mama Bear’s smile. He felt safe and loved.

They did one last move together—the Big Bear Hug Dip. Mama Bear held Cubby close. They bent their knees, dipped a little, and came back up. Hug, dip, up! Cubby giggled. “That tickled my tummy!”

Mama Bear kissed his forehead. “Dance can help you start the day, clean your room, or calm your body. Movement is a friend.”

Cubby looked at his neat bed, the tidy toys, and the shiny shelf. He looked at his paws. “My paws can dance and help. I like that.”

He took a small bow to the room. “Thank you, cozy bedroom.”

Mama Bear bowed too. “Thank you, Cubby Dancer.”

Cubby beamed. “I’m ready for the day!”

Lesson: Trying new things, like dancing while doing chores, can make work fun. Being open-minded and moving our bodies helps us learn, help, and feel happy.

The End.
