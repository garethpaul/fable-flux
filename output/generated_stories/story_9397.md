# Bella Bird Plans a Concert

Bella was a small, blue singing bird. She lived near a bright dining room with a big wooden table, soft chairs, and a shiny window. The dining room smelled like warm toast in the morning and sweet apples in the afternoon. Sunlight made little squares on the floor.

Bella loved to sing. “La-la-la!” she chirped. Her voice was soft like a feather. One day, she wanted to give a tiny concert in the dining room. “I want to make everyone smile,” Bella said.

She hopped onto a chair and looked around. The table had a bowl of oranges. The window was open a little, and a cool breeze tickled Bella’s feathers. She felt excited. “Tonight, I will sing!”

Then Bella paused. Her tummy felt fluttery. “What if my song is too quiet?” she whispered. “What if I forget the words?” Her heart went thump-thump.

Bella took a deep breath. “I can prepare,” she said bravely. “When I get ready, I feel strong.”

She made a plan.

First, Bella warmed up her voice. “Mee-mee-mee,” she sang softly. “Mah-mah-mah,” she sang a little louder. She drank a tiny sip of water from a spoon on the table. The water was cool on her tongue. “Ahh,” she sighed. Her voice felt smooth.

Next, Bella practiced her song. She chose a simple tune with happy words. She sang about sunbeams, orange peels, and the comfy chairs. “Sunshine squares on the floor, friends and smiles, I sing more!” she chirped.

She practiced slow. She practiced fast. She tapped her little feet. Tap-tap. Tap-tap. She counted her breaths. “In… out… sing,” she said. The counting helped her remember.

Bella also prepared the room. She nudged the bowl of oranges to the side so she had space to stand. She asked the chairs, “May I sing here?” Of course, the chairs were quiet, but Bella liked to be polite. She closed the window just a little, so the wind would not blow her music away. The breeze became gentle, like a hug.

Soon, her friends arrived. Pip the mouse peeked from behind a napkin. “Hi, Bella!” he squeaked. “I heard you will sing.”

Lulu the ladybug landed softly on the table. Her tiny wings made a pap-pap sound. “I love concerts,” she said. “Do you need help?”

“Yes, please,” said Bella. “I am getting ready. I feel a little shy. Will you sit close?”

“We will,” said Pip and Lulu together. They scooted near Bella’s spot. Pip brought a crumb of cracker to share, and it smelled toasty. Lulu dusted a sprinkle of sugar from the table, making it sparkle.

Just then, a big voice boomed. It was Benny the vacuum, resting by the doorway. “Concert? I like loud!” he grumbled. “I can hum along. Vroooom!”

Bella blinked. Her tummy fluttered again. She wanted a quiet concert, but she also wanted Benny to feel welcome.

Bella took another breath. “I can be brave and kind,” she told herself. “I can make a plan with my friends.”

She hopped to Benny. “Benny,” she said gently, “thank you for loving music. I am a small bird. My voice is tiny. If you hum too loud, no one will hear my song.”

Benny’s light blinked. “Oh,” he said softly. “I did not know.”

Bella smiled. “Would you like to help in a quiet way? Maybe you can make a soft whoosh at the end, like wind in the trees.”

Benny hummed a whispery “whoooo.” It sounded like a sleepy cloud. “I can do that,” he said.

“That is a great compromise,” Pip said, clapping his little paws.

Lulu nodded. “You cared about Bella’s needs. That is empathy.”

Bella felt warm inside. Her preparation and her brave talk helped. “Thank you, friends,” she said. “I am ready.”

Bella fluffed her feathers and stood tall. “In… out… sing,” she whispered. She began. “Sunshine squares on the floor, orange peels and something more…” Her song floated like a ribbon. The dining room smelled like apples and felt cozy and bright. Pip swayed. Lulu hummed. Benny waited quietly.

Bella forgot one word and paused. Her heart thumped. She looked at Pip and Lulu. Pip smiled. Lulu nodded. Bella remembered her plan. “When I get stuck, I breathe.” She took a little breath. “Friends and smiles, I sing more!” she finished with a sweet trill.

At the end, Bella nodded to Benny. He made a soft whoosh, like leaves dancing. It was perfect. Everyone cheered, “Yay, Bella!”

Bella’s cheeks felt warm with happy pride. “Thank you for listening. Thank you for helping me prepare,” she said.

Pip squeaked, “Your plan worked!”

Lulu said, “You were brave and kind.”

Benny rumbled, “I liked the quiet whoosh.”

Bella fluttered her wings. “Today I learned that preparation helps me feel ready. I learned I can be brave and ask for what I need. I learned we can compromise so everyone belongs.”

The sun slipped lower, and the dining room looked golden. Bella packed up her little spoon of water. She pushed the oranges back. She opened the window a tiny bit more to let in a happy breeze.

“Tomorrow,” Bella chirped, “I will sing again. I will practice. I will plan. And I will listen to my friends.”

Her friends smiled. The dining room felt full of music and care.

The End.
