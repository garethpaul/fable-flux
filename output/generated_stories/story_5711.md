# Joey’s Sunny Backyard Surprise

Joey was a joyful kangaroo joey with big feet and a bigger smile. He lived in a sunny backyard with soft grass, bright flowers, and a gentle breeze that smelled like oranges. The sky was blue, and butterflies danced like tiny kites.

“Good morning, backyard!” Joey said, bouncing lightly. Boing, boing!

He packed his little pouch. In went a water bottle, a tiny broom, and a snack of crunchy carrot sticks. Joey liked to try things on his own. Today felt like a “do-it-myself” day.

Joey looked around. “Who wants to play?” he called. He heard chirping, buzzing, and a quiet squeak.

Under the lemon tree, a small brown hedgehog peeked out. Next to the daisies, a colorful parrot perched on the fence, feathers bright red, green, and blue. Near the veggie patch, a snail blinked slow, its shell shiny like a tiny rainbow.

“Hi!” Joey waved. “I’m Joey. Do you want to play?”

“Hola!” squawked the parrot. “I’m Pico.” He flapped. “I like to sing!”

“I’m Nia,” said the hedgehog in a tiny voice. “I like quiet.”

The snail lifted its eyes. “I am Sam. I move slow. Very slow.”

Joey bounced closer. “Let’s have a backyard parade!”

Pico sang a happy tune. “La-la-la!” It sparkled in the air like sunshine. Nia covered her ears. “Too loud,” she whispered. “My heart feels jumpy.”

Joey stopped and watched Nia’s face. He put a paw on his chest to feel his own heartbeat. He looked at Pico. “Pico, Nia likes quiet. Can we make a soft song?”

Pico tilted his head. “Soft? Like this?” He sang, “La… la… la…” very gently, like a feather.

Nia smiled. “That’s better,” she said. Her shoulders relaxed like melting butter.

Joey grinned. “Thank you, Pico. That was kind.” He felt warm inside. This was empathy—listening and caring about how friends feel.

They started the parade. Joey tapped a tiny drum softly—tap, tap. Pico hummed. Nia pushed a leaf like a float. Sam slid slowly, drawing a shiny trail that sparkled in the sun.

“Wait,” said Joey, sniffing the air. Something smelled yucky. He followed the smell to the garden bed. There were crinkly wrappers and a plastic bottle under the flowers. A ladybug sat on a leaf, looking worried.

“Oh no,” Joey said. “Trash in the garden. The flowers can’t breathe.”

Nia’s nose twitched. “It makes my tummy feel funny.”

Pico squawked softly. “We must help.”

Joey straightened up. “We can clean it! We will care for our backyard. I can do it myself,” he said. He grabbed his tiny broom.

He started to sweep, but the bottle rolled away. Joey chased it—boing, boing—then tripped and fell on his soft tail. “Oof!” He felt a little frustrated and a little silly.

Sam inched closer. “I can block the bottle with my shell,” he said slowly.

Nia spoke up. “I can hold the bag open.”

Pico flapped. “I can carry light paper with my beak.”

Joey took a breath. He looked at his friends. He felt a soft tug in his heart. “I wanted to do it by myself,” he said. “But the garden needs help. Can we do it together?”

“Yes,” said Nia gently.

“Sí,” chirped Pico.

“Slow and steady,” said Sam.

They worked as a team. Joey swept gently. Pico picked up tiny bits. Nia held the bag wide. Sam blocked the rolling bottle until Joey scooped it up. The ladybug buzzed a happy little buzz. The sun felt warmer. The garden smelled fresh again—like lemons and earth.

When they finished, Joey clapped his paws. “We did it! Thank you.”

Pico bowed. “We make a good team.”

Nia smiled shyly. “I feel safe.”

Sam blinked. “The ground is happy.”

Joey poured water into a bowl. “Let’s have a snack.” He opened his pouch and saw only one carrot stick left. He felt a pinch of worry. “Oh. Just one.”

He looked at his friends’ faces—hopeful, hungry, kind. Joey thought about empathy again. He broke the carrot into little pieces—snap, snap, snap. “We can share.”

They crunched together. Crunch, crunch. It tasted extra sweet.

Pico fluffed his feathers. “In my family,” he said, “we say gracias after we share.”

Nia nodded. “In mine, we say thank you.”

Sam added, “In snail talk, we say gl-glee.” He made a tiny happy sound.

Joey giggled. “Gracias. Thank you. Gl-glee!” The words felt like a rainbow of sounds. He loved learning about his friends.

The parade started again, softer than before. Tap, hum, swish, slide. Joey noticed Sam moving slowly. He matched Sam’s pace. “I can go slow, too,” Joey said. “I want to be with you.”

Sam smiled with his eyes. “Thank you for waiting.”

Joey’s heart felt big and bright, like the sun. He learned that empathy means listening, noticing, and caring. He learned that being independent is trying his best, and asking for help when it helps others. He learned that caring for the earth keeps everyone safe. And he learned that sharing makes snacks and smiles grow.

Joey looked around at his friends and the clean, sunny backyard. “Today was a good day,” he said.

“It was a kind day,” said Nia.

“A green day,” said Pico.

“A shared day,” said Sam.

Joey nodded. “Let’s remember: We listen. We help. We share. We care for our world.”

They all took a deep, happy breath. The lemon tree swayed. The butterflies twirled. The sun hugged them with warm light.

The End.
