# Benny Bear Fixes It Right

Benny Bear was a friendly bear cub with soft brown fur and bright curious eyes. One sunny morning, he padded into Grandpa Grizzle’s garage workshop. The garage smelled like warm wood and orange soap. Sunbeams made tiny dust sparkles float in the air.

“Good morning, Grandpa!” Benny said.

“Good morning, Benny,” rumbled Grandpa Grizzle. “Today we fix your red toy truck.” Grandpa winked. “We’ll do it the right way.”

Benny’s red truck was missing a wheel. It wobbled and clacked when he pushed it. Benny loved his truck. He wanted it to zoom!

The workshop was full of sounds and colors. Hammers went tap-tap. A fan went whirr. Jars held shiny screws. Paint cans sat in a row like a rainbow. There was a big wooden table, smooth and cool.

Grandpa set the truck on the table. “We need the small silver screw,” he said. “Please bring the screw box.”

Benny nodded. He found the box and opened it. Click! Inside were tiny screws, big screws, and medium screws. They glittered like little fish.

Benny picked a screw. “This one!” he said.

Grandpa looked close. “Hmm. That’s not the right size. We must tell the truth about what fits. That’s called integrity,” Grandpa said kindly. “Integrity means doing the right thing, even when it’s hard.”

Benny’s ears perked. “Inte… gri… ty,” he tried.

“Good trying,” Grandpa smiled. “Let’s measure.”

They held the screw next to the wheel. It was too short. Benny felt a warm grumble in his tummy. He wanted the truck to be done now. His paws squeezed tight.

“I want it to zoom now!” Benny said, his voice a little growly.

Grandpa nodded. “I hear your big feelings. Let’s take a slow breath together.” They breathed in… and out. In… and out. Benny felt the grumble shrink like a balloon letting out air.

“Better,” Benny said softly.

“Better,” Grandpa agreed. “Now let’s keep trying. That is persistence.”

Benny tried a longer screw. Still not right. He tried a thin screw. Wobble. Not right. He tried a thick screw. Too tight.

Benny’s face felt hot. His paws wanted to slam the box. He almost did… then he remembered the breathing. In… and out. In… and out. He also remembered integrity. Do it right. Tell the truth. Do not pretend.

“I want to say it fits,” Benny whispered. “But it doesn’t.”

Grandpa gave him a gentle pat. “That is integrity. Thank you for telling the truth.”

Just then, Benny saw a small silver screw under the table. “Look!” he squeaked. He picked it up. It was dusty. He wiped it on a soft cloth. It shone.

They tried the screw. It fit! Click. The wheel sat snug and straight.

“Yay!” Benny clapped. “We did it right!”

A soft knock came at the open garage door. It was Millie Mouse, Benny’s friend, holding a blue toy car. “Hi, Benny. Hi, Grandpa,” Millie peeped. “My car won’t roll. Can I watch?”

“Of course,” Grandpa said.

“Let’s share the space,” Benny added, moving over. “You can sit by me.”

Millie smiled and sat. She touched the smooth table. “It feels cool,” she said.

Benny wanted to zoom his fixed truck now. He pushed it a little. Vroom! It felt so good. Then he bumped the screw box by accident. Crash! Screws spilled and rolled like tiny marbles. One tapped Millie’s car and it fell off the table. Plop!

Millie’s eyes grew big. “Oh!”

Benny’s heart thumped. He wanted to say, “It wasn’t me!” But he stopped. He remembered integrity. He remembered doing the right thing, even when it was hard.

Benny took a breath. “It was me,” he said. “I bumped the box. I am sorry, Millie. Are you okay? Is your car okay?”

Millie picked up her car. A little paint chip had popped off. “I’m okay,” she said. “My car is a little sad.”

Grandpa handed Benny a tiny paintbrush and blue paint. “What can we do now?” he asked.

Benny nodded. “I can help fix it. I can make it right.” He dipped the brush and carefully dabbed the blue spot. “I will be gentle.” His paws were steady. He felt calm and proud.

Millie watched. “Thank you, Benny,” she said. “That was kind.”

Benny smiled. “I was mad before,” he said softly. “I used my breaths. I told the truth. I said sorry. And I helped.”

Grandpa Grizzle beamed. “That’s integrity, anger-management, and apology. You also shared space and kept trying.”

When the paint was dry, Millie’s car looked bright again. Benny and Millie rolled their toys on the table. Vroom. Zoom. Clickety-click. The sounds made them giggle.

“Let’s share the tools,” Benny said. He passed Millie a tiny cloth. “We can polish together.”

They polished. They laughed. They took turns. The fan went whirr, and the sunbeams danced.

At the end of the day, Benny put the screws back in the box. He closed it carefully. Click. He looked at Grandpa. “We did it right.”

Grandpa nodded. “Doing it right feels good.”

Benny hugged Millie. “Today I learned: Tell the truth. Breathe when I’m mad. Say sorry. Share and keep trying.”

Millie hugged back. “Me too!”

Benny pushed his red truck. It rolled smooth and straight. Vroom! He felt proud inside, like a warm light.

The End.
