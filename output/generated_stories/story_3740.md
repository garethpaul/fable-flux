# Melody's Fair Play Day

In a bright and colorful art room filled with paintbrushes, crayons, and sparkly paper, lived a little yellow bird named Melody. She had the most beautiful singing voice and loved to chirp happy songs while she painted pictures.

One sunny morning, Melody heard excited voices coming from the playground outside the art room window. "Tweet, tweet! What's all that noise?" she wondered, fluttering to the windowsill.

Outside, she saw children playing different sports - some kicking a red soccer ball, others bouncing orange basketballs, and a few throwing soft, white baseballs. But something didn't look right. The bigger children were taking all the balls and not sharing with the smaller ones.

"That's not fair!" chirped Melody. She watched as little Emma stood sadly by herself, wanting to play soccer but having no ball. Tommy was reaching for a basketball, but the older kids wouldn't let him have a turn.

Melody's heart felt heavy. She knew this wasn't right. Everyone should get a chance to play and have fun with sports!

"I need to help," Melody sang softly to herself. But how could a small bird make a big difference?

Then Melody had a wonderful idea! She looked around the art room and saw something perfect - a big piece of poster board and colorful markers. Her tiny claws gripped a red marker, and she began to draw.

First, she drew a big circle and divided it into sections like a pie. "This will be a sharing schedule!" she chirped proudly. She drew pictures of soccer balls, basketballs, and baseballs in different sections, along with clock faces showing different times.

Next, Melody drew happy children of all sizes playing together. She made sure to include both big and little kids sharing the same ball.

When her colorful chart was finished, Melody carefully carried it in her beak and flew outside to the playground.

"Tweet, tweet! Listen everyone!" she sang in her sweetest voice. The children stopped playing and looked up at the little yellow bird.

"I made something special for you!" Melody announced, hanging her chart on the fence where everyone could see it. "This is a Fair Play Schedule! See? Everyone gets a turn with each ball!"

The children gathered around, their eyes wide with wonder. "Look!" said Sarah, pointing to the chart. "At 10 o'clock, the little kids get the soccer ball!"

"And at 10:30, we all share the basketballs!" added Marcus excitedly.

Emma clapped her hands. "Now I can play soccer too!"

The older children looked at each other and smiled. "That's a really good idea, Melody," said Jake, the tallest boy. "We should have been sharing all along."

From that day forward, the children followed Melody's Fair Play Schedule. They learned to listen to each other, take turns, and organize their games so everyone could join in. The playground became filled with laughter and happy voices.

Melody perched on her favorite branch, watching all the children play together. Her heart felt warm and full of joy. She began to sing her prettiest song - a tune about friendship, fairness, and fun.

"When we share and play together, everyone can have fun! Sports are better when we're fair, under the bright, warm sun!"

The children heard her song and began to sing along, their voices mixing with Melody's in perfect harmony.

The End.
