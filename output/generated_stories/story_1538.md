# Trunks Up for Tiny Tasks

The gentle elephant calf, named Nuru, climbed into the family car with a soft thump. The seat felt cool and smooth under Nuru’s legs. The car smelled like oranges from Mama’s hand wipes. Sunlight danced across the windows, making warm squares on Nuru’s gray knees.

“Today is a little trip,” said Mama Elephant, buckling Nuru in. Click. “We have tiny tasks to do.”

“I can do tiny tasks,” Nuru said, nodding. Nuru’s ears fluttered like soft fans.

Papa Elephant started the engine. Vroom. The car hummed, and the steering wheel looked like a big donut. Nuru giggled and wiggled toes. “What are the tasks?”

Mama held up a small card with pictures. “We have a plan. One, deep breaths. Two, snack. Three, choose music. Four, help with map. Five, say thank you. We can check each task off as we go.”

Nuru liked the little boxes to check. It felt like a game.

“First task,” Mama said. “Mindful breaths.”

Nuru sat tall. “In like smelling a flower,” Nuru whispered, “and out like blowing a bubble.” Inhale. Exhale. The air tasted like orange and a little like vanilla from the car air freshener. Nuru’s belly rose and fell. Calm. Warm.

“Check!” said Papa, tapping the card. “Good start.”

On the sidewalk, they passed friends. Mr. Panda waved with a bamboo snack in hand. A giraffe family stooped to duck under a tree branch. A kangaroo pushed a stroller, the baby joey peeking out. The world was big and different, and Nuru smiled. “So many friends,” Nuru said. “All different. All going places.”

“Just like us,” Mama said. “We all have tasks, and we all help each other.”

The car turned the corner. The seatbelt hugged Nuru’s tummy snug and safe. The sun peeked from a cloud, bright and friendly.

“My tummy says hello,” Nuru giggled. It grumbled softly. “Is it snack time?”

“Task two,” Mama said with a grin. She opened the little cooler. Crinkle. “Apple slices for crunch, cucumber for cool, and a small water bottle.”

Nuru took tiny bites. Crunch, crunch. The apple was sweet and juicy. The water went glug, glug, cool down the throat. “Thank you, Mama.”

“Check,” Papa said, and he smiled in the mirror. His eyes were kind. “You remembered kind words.”

“Task three,” Mama said. “Choose music.”

“Something soft,” Nuru decided. “Soft like a cloud.” Click. A gentle song filled the car, twinkly like little stars. Nuru swayed, ears flopping a bit. “This helps me focus.”

A stoplight turned red. The car paused. Nuru watched colors—red, yellow, green—like traffic fruit. “What is next?” Nuru asked.

“Task four,” Mama said, “help with the map. We need to find the library drop box to return our book.”

Nuru carefully held the picture map. Streets were lines. The library was a blue square with a tiny book drawing. “Turn left at the big oak tree,” Nuru said. “Then go straight until the store with the rainbow flag.”

Papa nodded. “Good strategy, Nuru. You notice landmarks.”

Outside, the rainbow flag fluttered. A fox family smiled near the store door. A sign said, “All are welcome.” Nuru felt a warm glow. Different faces, different tails, different stripes. All welcome.

The car turned left. There was the library! A brick building with wide steps and a green lawn. The drop box was shiny and silver.

“Task five,” Mama said, handing Nuru the book. “Please put the book in the slot.”

Nuru held the book to a small chest. “Thank you, book, for stories,” Nuru whispered. Clunk! The book slid in—soft, sure. It felt good to finish. Nuru’s heart felt like a small drum—tap, tap—happy and proud.

“Check!” Papa cheered. He clapped his big hands softly so the sound was like gentle rain. “All tasks done.”

Nuru’s eyes were bright. “We did it, step by step.” Nuru wiggled. “Can we add one more? A thank-you task?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Who will you thank?”

Nuru waved through the window. “Thank you, Mr. Panda! Thank you, giraffes! Thank you, road helpers and bright lights. Thank you, rainbow sign. Thank you, Mama and Papa.”

“Thank you, Nuru,” Mama said. “You were calm, kind, and focused.”

The car hummed on. Vroom. The sky was soft blue like a cozy blanket. Nuru felt sleepy and safe. “When I breathe, snack, pick music, and follow the map, I can do my tasks,” Nuru said. “That is my strategy.”

“That is a smart strategy,” Papa said. “Mindful steps help us finish.”

Nuru yawned, a little trumpet sound. “Trunks up for tiny tasks,” Nuru whispered, lifting a small trunk.

Mama touched Nuru’s ear. “Trunks up,” she echoed.

As the car rolled toward home, Nuru looked out at friends of every shape, color, and size sharing the road and the day. Nuru closed eyes and smiled, warm and content.

“We can do big things,” Nuru murmured, “one tiny task at a time.”

“Yes,” Mama said softly, “and we can care for ourselves and others along the way.”

The End.
