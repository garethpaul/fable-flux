# Pippa Parrot’s Plan

Sunlight made little squares on the carpet in the playful living room. Pillows looked like bright clouds, and crayons waited in a tin that went ting-ting when it moved. Pippa Parrot fluffed her green and blue feathers and squawked happily.

“Today,” Pippa said, tapping her beak on a toy drum, “I will make a Wellness Fun Fort! We will play, drink water, and stretch our wings!”

Pippa’s friend, a small stuffed bunny named Bop, wobbled on the couch arm. “What’s the plan?” Bop asked in a soft voice.

Pippa blinked. “Plan?” She looked around. “I can just do it!”

She swooped to the pillow pile. “Pillows here!” she chirped. She knocked three pillows onto the floor. They landed with a fwump! She grabbed a blanket and tugged and tugged. The blanket fell on her head. “Mmph!” she mumbled, her feathers tickling her beak.

Bop giggled. “You look like a blue burrito.”

Pippa popped out. “I am okay!” She zoomed to the crayons. “Signs! I need signs!” She scribbled a big W on a scrap of paper. She dropped the crayon. It rolled under the couch.

Pippa’s tummy made a tiny rumble. Her wings felt a little heavy. She looked at the clock with big bubble numbers. “So much to do,” she whispered. “And I feel wobbly.”

Bop tilted his head. “Maybe we need a plan, Pippa.”

Pippa puffed her chest. “I can do it myself. I am a creative parrot!” She flapped to the water cups. One cup tipped and splashed cold water on her toes. “Eep!” The floor sparkled with little puddles.

Bop hopped down and slipped, landing softly. “Oof,” he said. His button eyes looked worried.

Pippa’s heart pinched. “Oh, Bop. I’m sorry,” she said. “I hurried. I didn’t plan. Are you okay?”

Bop nodded and patted his tummy. “I’m okay. Maybe we can plan together.”

Pippa took a slow breath. She listened to the quiet hum of the house. She smelled the warm blanket and the lemony floor. “Yes,” she said. “Plan first. Then play.”

She flapped to the low table and found a small notepad. She wrote with a purple crayon, saying the words out loud:

“Step 1: Snack and water.
Step 2: Gentle stretch.
Step 3: Build pillow fort.
Step 4: Make signs.
Step 5: Clean up.”

Pippa smiled. “This plan helps my body and my brain,” she said. “It keeps us safe and happy.”

“Step 1!” Bop cheered.

Pippa poured water carefully this time. “Slow and steady,” she said. They sipped. The water felt cool and kind. Pippa shared apple slices from a little cup. Crunch, crunch. Her tummy stopped rumbling.

“Step 2,” Pippa said. She stood tall. “Stretch like a tree.” She reached her wings up, up. Bop reached his soft arms up too. “Now breathe in,” Pippa said, “and breathe out.” Their breath sounded like a tiny ocean.

“Step 3!” Pippa flapped to the pillows. “Two for walls, one for a door.” She stacked them gently. “Blanket on top,” she said. The blanket floated down like a quiet cloud. No more bonks. No more burritos.

Bop clapped. “Cozy!”

“Step 4,” Pippa sang. She drew simple, happy signs: a water drop for Drink Water, a little stick bird stretching for Stretch Time, and a heart for Be Kind. She taped them to the fort with a smile. The tape made a soft rip-rip sound.

“Step 5,” Pippa said. She picked up the rolled-away crayon from under the couch using a wooden spoon. “Got you!” She wiped the tiny water spots with a towel. The floor felt safe again.

They both crawled into the fort. It smelled like cotton and crayons. Light peeked in through a little door. Pippa set the toy drum inside and tapped a gentle beat. Bop hummed along.

A small voice came from the doorway. It was Max, the little child who lived there. He peeked in with wide eyes. “Can I play?” he asked.

Pippa nodded. “Yes! We made a plan. First we drink water, then we stretch, then we play.”

Max smiled and took a sip from his cup. He stretched his arms like a tall giraffe. “Ready!”

They played a quiet rhythm game. Tap, tap. Tap-tap-tap. Pippa led the game and watched the signs. When Max got excited and started to wiggle a lot, Pippa pointed to the heart sign. “Be kind,” she chirped. “Gentle taps.” Max nodded and slowed down.

After play, Pippa looked at her list. “Clean up time,” she said. Everyone helped. Pippa folded the blanket. Bop hugged a pillow into place. Max put the crayons back in the tin. Ting-ting!

Pippa turned to Bop. “Thank you for the idea to plan,” she said. “I’m sorry I didn’t listen at first. I hurried, and that made little spills. Planning helped me help everyone.”

Bop smiled. “Planning keeps us well.”

Pippa fluffed her feathers and felt calm and proud. “Today I learned: when we make a simple plan, we feel better, we are helpful, and we can do things by ourselves and together.”

They all touched the heart sign and giggled. The living room felt bright and safe, like a happy nest.

The End.
