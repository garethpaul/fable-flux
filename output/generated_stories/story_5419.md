# Leo’s Sunny Backyard Dance

The sun was warm and bright in the sunny backyard. The grass felt soft and tickly on little paws. A brave little lion cub named Leo blinked at the sparkly light and took a deep breath. “Today,” Leo said, “I will learn to dance!”

Birds chirped, “Cheep, cheep!” The breeze whispered, “Whoosh.” The flowers smelled sweet and sunny. Leo’s tail swished. His heart felt thumpy with both excitement and a tiny bit of worry.

On the porch, Mama Lion clapped her paws gently. “You can do it, Leo,” she said with a smile. “Dancing is fun. Dancing is practice. Dancing is feelings.”

Leo nodded. “I want to feel brave,” he said. “I want my feet to go tap-tap-tap and my tail to swish-swish-swish.”

Leo put on his bright blue scarf. It felt smooth on his fur. He stood tall in the soft grass. He lifted one paw. He wiggled his hips. He tried a big jump.

Splat! Leo slipped and landed with a soft plop on his bottom.

“Ow,” said Leo. His ears drooped. “Maybe I can’t dance.”

Mama Lion knelt beside him and stroked his back. “New things can feel hard,” she said kindly. “Remember the growth mindset: We say, ‘I can’t do it yet.’ That little word, ‘yet,’ is brave.”

Leo sniffed. He looked at the bright sky. He felt the warm sun. He listened to the birds. “I can’t dance yet,” he whispered. “But I can try again.”

Leo stood up. He took a slow breath. In, out. He tried little steps. Tap. Tap. Tap. He counted softly, “One, two, three.” He swished his tail. Swish. Swish. Swish. He bent his knees and hopped. Boing!

A little squirrel named Nib stood on the fence and clapped. “Go, Leo! I love your tail swish.”

“Thank you,” said Leo with a grin. His worry melted a bit like ice in the sun.

Then there was a new sound—thump, thump, thump. A big kangaroo named Roo hopped into the backyard. She wore shiny tap shoes. Clickity-clack! Clickity-clack!

“Hello!” Roo said. “I came to dance. I like to dance fast. Super fast!”

Leo’s eyes went wide. “I want to dance too,” he said. “But I don’t know the steps yet.”

Roo smiled. “We can share. We can make a dance together.”

Nib squeaked, “Yay! Team dance!”

But when Roo started, she hopped so fast that the ground shook a little. Thump-thump-thump! The flower pot wobbled. Leo’s paws slipped again. He bumped into Roo’s tail.

“Oops!” said Leo. “I’m sorry.”

“Ouch!” said Roo. “My tail!”

Nib waved his tiny paws. “Wait! Don’t bump! Don’t pout! We can solve this peacefully.”

Leo took a slow breath. “We have a problem,” he said. “I am learning slowly. Roo moves very fast. How can we both dance?”

Roo tapped her chin. Click, click. “We can use kind words,” she said. “We can make a plan.”

Mama Lion nodded. “That’s a peaceful solution. Talk it out. Take turns. Try again.”

Leo said, “Roo, can you show me one small step first?”

Roo said, “Yes. Watch.” She lifted one foot and did a tiny tap. Click. “Your turn.”

Leo tried. Tap. The sound was softer than Roo’s, but it made his chest feel warm. “I did it!”

Roo cheered, “Great job! Now let’s add a hop.” She hopped once. Boing.

Leo hopped. Boing. His paws landed steady. He smiled big. “I can do it when I go slow.”

Roo nodded. “Slow is okay. Slow helps our bodies learn.”

Nib squeaked, “Add a swish! Add a swish!”

Leo swished his tail. Swish. Roo swished her long tail. Swoosh. They both laughed.

Together they made a plan:
- First, one tiny tap. Click.
- Then, one gentle hop. Boing.
- Then, a tail swish. Swish.
- Then, a clap. Clap!

Leo and Roo practiced the steps. Tap, hop, swish, clap. Tap, hop, swish, clap. The rhythm felt like sunshine in their paws. Leo’s ears perked up. His heart felt happy and brave.

After a while, Roo said, “Ready for a little faster?”

Leo took a breath. “Yes. I will try. If I slip, I will try again.”

They danced a bit faster. Tap-hop-swish-clap! Tap-hop-swish-clap! The birds chirped along. Cheep-cheep-cheep! The breeze hummed. Whoosh. The flowers nodded like a tiny audience.

Leo almost slipped, but he bent his knees and found his balance. “I did not give up!” he said proudly.

Roo beamed. “That’s the growth mindset. You learned with practice.”

Mama Lion clapped. “And you solved the problem with kind words and a plan. That is a peaceful solution.”

Nib did a little spin. “Spin!” he cried.

Roo giggled. “Let’s add a spin at the end.”

Leo’s eyes sparkled. “Okay! Tap, hop, swish, clap, spin!” He twirled slowly, feeling the sun on his face and the grass between his toes. Round and round, and stop!

Everyone cheered. “Bravo, Leo!”

Leo bowed. “Thank you for helping me. Thank you for going slow. Thank you for talking it out.”

Roo said, “Thank you for trying again and again.”

Mama Lion hugged Leo. Her fur was warm and soft. “How do you feel?” she asked.

Leo smiled. “I feel brave. I feel happy. I feel like a dancer.”

He looked at the sunny backyard. He listened to the birds. He felt the breeze. He held his head high.

“I can learn new things,” Leo said. “I can use kind words. I can make a plan. I can dance!”

The End.
