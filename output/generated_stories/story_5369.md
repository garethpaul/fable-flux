# Nutkin's Big Problem

In a cozy study corner filled with soft cushions and tall bookshelves, lived a little squirrel named Nutkin. His fluffy gray tail twitched with excitement every morning as golden sunlight streamed through the window.

Today felt different. Nutkin's tummy rumbled loudly. "Grumble, grumble, GRUMBLE!" 

"Oh my!" squeaked Nutkin, patting his empty belly. "I'm so hungry, but I don't have any nuts left!"

He looked around his tiny home in the corner bookshelf. His acorn jar was completely empty. The walnut box had nothing but crumbs. Even his emergency peanut pouch was gone.

"What will I do?" Nutkin worried, his whiskers trembling.

Just then, his friend Chippy the chipmunk scurried up. "Good morning, Nutkin! You look sad. What's wrong?"

"I ate all my nuts," Nutkin admitted, his cheeks turning pink. "Now I have nothing for breakfast, lunch, or dinner!"

Chippy's eyes sparkled kindly. "I have extra nuts! I can share some with you."

Nutkin's tail perked up, but then he stopped. "That's very nice, Chippy, but I should learn to take care of myself. Can you help me figure out how?"

"What a smart idea!" chirped Chippy. "Let's think together."

Nutkin sat on his favorite blue cushion and put his tiny paw to his chin. "Hmm... where do nuts come from?"

"Trees!" they both said at the same time, then giggled.

"The big oak tree by the window has lots of acorns," Nutkin said, his dark eyes brightening. "I could gather some myself!"

"That's perfect!" Chippy clapped his small paws. "And I know where the best walnut tree is. We could go together!"

Nutkin felt proud and excited. "Yes! But I want to do the collecting myself. That way, I'll learn how to take care of my own needs."

The two friends scampered to the window. The morning air smelled fresh and sweet, like dewdrops and flowers. Nutkin carefully climbed down to the oak tree. Its rough bark felt good under his tiny claws.

"Look at all these beautiful acorns!" Nutkin gasped. Brown acorns with little caps covered the ground like nature's treasures.

Carefully, Nutkin picked up each acorn, checking to make sure it was good and fresh. He filled his cheek pouches until they bulged like balloons.

"Great job!" cheered Chippy from above. "You're being so responsible!"

Back in the study corner, Nutkin organized his nuts into different containers. "I'll put some in my breakfast jar, some in my lunch box, and some in my dinner tin," he planned out loud. "And I'll save extra for emergencies!"

"That's very wise," said Chippy admiringly.

Nutkin felt warm and happy inside. "Thank you for offering to share, Chippy. That was very kind. But I'm glad I learned to gather food myself!"

"You did wonderfully," Chippy smiled. "Now you know you can take care of yourself!"

As they shared a delicious lunch of fresh acorns, Nutkin felt proud and confident. The nuts tasted extra special because he had gathered them with his own paws.

"From now on," Nutkin declared, "I'll collect a little bit every day so I never run out again!"

The two friends spent the afternoon reading books in the sunny study corner, their bellies full and their hearts happy.

The End.
