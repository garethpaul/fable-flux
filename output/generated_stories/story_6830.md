# Tidy-Time Tango

In a cozy bedroom with soft, squishy pillows and a warm honey-yellow rug, a friendly bear cub named Benny woke up and stretched. He sniffed the sweet smell of clean blankets and listened to the gentle hum of the wind outside the window.

“Good morning, room,” Benny said with a sleepy smile. “Today will be busy!”

Toys were sprinkled across the floor like colorful leaves. Blocks made little towers. Crayons rested in a sleepy rainbow line. A tiny drum sat beside a teddy who wore a scarf. Benny’s fuzzy ears perked up. “Oh! It’s chore day,” he remembered.

Mama Bear peeked into the room. Her voice was soft like a blanket. “Good morning, Benny. We have ten little minutes to tidy before breakfast,” she said, tapping a small timer. Tick… tick… tick… The sound was gentle and steady.

Benny’s nose twitched. Ten minutes sounded short. He looked at the towers, the crayons, the drum, and his bed with the blanket all rumpled. His tummy made a tiny flip. “Mama, it’s a lot,” he whispered.

Mama Bear knelt and gave his paw a squeeze. “We’ll do it step by step. We’ll use our bodies and dance our chores. We can move, we can wiggle, and we can finish on time,” she said with a smile. “Ready for the Tidy-Time Tango?”

Benny’s eyes grew wide. “A dance?”

Mama Bear pressed the timer. “Ten minutes. Let’s go!”

Tick… tick… tick…

“First move,” Mama said. “Blanket Burrito Roll!” She rolled her shoulders, and Benny giggled. Together they shook the blanket—swish, swish—the fabric felt cool on Benny’s paws and smelled like sun and soap. They laid it smooth. Benny patted the corners. “Puff, puff, perfect!” he cheered.

Tick… tick… tick…

“Second move,” Mama sang. “Sock Hop!” Benny bounced on his toes. “Hop, hop, hop,” he said, picking up his striped socks. The socks felt soft and a little warm from the night. He placed them in the drawer. His feet went tap-tap on the rug. He giggled again. “Sock Hop is silly!”

“Third move,” Mama whispered with a grin. “Block Slide!” Benny slid the blocks into their basket. Clack, clack, clack. The sound was like tiny wooden raindrops. He pushed the basket under the shelf—shoop! His body swayed left and right.

Tick… tick… tick…

Benny peeked at the timer. The little red line was smaller. “Mama, is there enough time?” he asked, his voice a little wobbly.

Mama nodded. “Yes, if we keep moving. You’re doing great.”

“Fourth move,” Benny said, feeling brave. “Crayon Rainbow Sweep!” He gathered crayons, one by one—red, blue, green, gold. Their waxy smell tickled his nose. He placed them gently in their box. “Sleep well, crayons,” he whispered.

“Fifth move,” Mama said softly. “Teddy Twirl.” Benny picked up his teddy with two paws and spun once. The room looked like a happy blur. He set Teddy on the pillow and tucked the tiny scarf in a neat bow. “You look cozy,” Benny told Teddy.

Tick… tick… tick…

A soft drumbeat in Benny’s head said, Bum-bum… bum-bum. He tapped the tiny drum. Thum! It made a round, friendly sound. “Drum March!” Benny marched to the shelf. Left, right, left, right. He placed the drum in its special spot. The shelf felt smooth and cool.

Benny took a breath. The room already looked brighter. Sunlight painted warm stripes on the rug. He felt proud, like his chest was a warm muffin.

Then he saw it—under his bed, a little trail of puzzle pieces. “Oh no,” he said. “We forgot the puzzle.”

Tick… tick… tick…

Mama knelt. “We still have a few minutes. Try the Gentle Scoop.”

Benny lay on his fuzzy tummy and reached under the bed. The floor was cool against his fur. He gathered the pieces with careful paws. Click, click—they fit into the box. “Gentle Scoop worked!” he said, popping up.

A tiny yawn came from the doorway. Little Sister Bear, Bella, rubbed her eyes. “Benny, I can’t find my rainbow ribbon,” she said in a small voice.

Benny looked at the timer. Tick… tick… only a sliver of red. He took a breath. “Kindness move,” he said. “Ribbon Rescue!”

He scanned the room with sharp, shiny eyes. He crouched low, then stretched tall. He listened. Swish… a soft sound near the chair. Benny peeked behind it and found the ribbon, silky and smooth, like a little river. “Here you go, Bella,” he said, tying it gently into her hair.

Bella beamed. “Thank you, Benny. You’re fast and kind.”

Tick… tick… The timer chimed with a gentle ding!

Benny and Mama froze, then looked around. The bed was neat. The blocks were tucked away. The crayons were safe. Teddy smiled from the pillow. The drum sat on the shelf. The puzzle box was closed. The rainbow ribbon sparkled in Bella’s hair.

“We did it on time!” Benny cheered. He did a tiny Tidy-Time bow. “Ta-da!”

Mama hugged him, her fur warm and soft. “You danced your chores. You moved your body. You helped your sister. That is smart time use and a very kind heart.”

Benny’s tummy rumbled. He laughed. “Breakfast time?”

“Breakfast time,” Mama said. “And after breakfast, we can dance again.”

As they walked to the kitchen, Benny looked back at his cozy bedroom. It felt calm and happy, like it was smiling. He felt calm and happy, too.

Benny whispered, “When I move and tidy on time, my room shines, and my heart does, too.”

The End.
