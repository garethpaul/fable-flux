# Benny's Big Share

The warm sunshine made the green grass sparkle like tiny diamonds. Benny the bear cub rolled his favorite red ball across the soft lawn. His fluffy brown fur felt cozy in the morning light.

"This is the best day ever!" Benny said, catching his ball with both paws. The ball was shiny and bouncy, just perfect for playing.

Soon, Benny heard little footsteps. Two small rabbits hopped into the backyard. Their names were Rosie and Ruby. They had soft gray fur and twitchy pink noses.

"Hi Benny!" called Rosie. "Can we play with your ball?"

Benny hugged his ball tight. "No way! This is MY ball," he said loudly. "Find your own toy!"

The little rabbits looked sad. Their ears drooped down. "We don't have a ball," whispered Ruby. "We just wanted to play together."

"Too bad!" Benny said. He turned his back and rolled the ball far away from them.

Rosie and Ruby sat under the big oak tree. They watched Benny play all by himself. Soon, Benny noticed something. Playing alone wasn't very fun anymore.

The ball rolled toward a flower bed. Benny ran to get it, but it was stuck behind the tall sunflowers. He pushed and pulled, but his little paws couldn't reach it.

"Help!" Benny called. "My ball is stuck!"

Rosie and Ruby heard him. They looked at each other, then hopped over quickly.

"We can help," said Rosie kindly. "Ruby is small enough to squeeze behind the flowers."

Ruby wiggled through the green stems. She pushed the red ball with her nose. Pop! Out it came, rolling right to Benny's paws.

"Thank you," Benny said quietly. His tummy felt funny and warm. The rabbits had helped him even though he wasn't nice to them.

Benny looked at Rosie and Ruby. They had been kind when he was mean. That wasn't fair at all.

"I'm sorry," Benny said, his voice soft and gentle. "I was wrong to keep my ball all to myself. That wasn't fair to you."

"It's okay," said Rosie with a smile. "Everyone makes mistakes."

Benny thought hard. How could he make things better? Then he had a wonderful idea!

"Would you like to play ball together?" Benny asked. "We could take turns and share!"

"Really?" Ruby's eyes sparkled with joy.

"Yes! We can roll it to each other," Benny said. "That sounds much more fun than playing alone."

The three friends made a triangle on the grass. Benny rolled the ball to Rosie. She giggled and rolled it to Ruby. Ruby squeaked with happiness and rolled it back to Benny.

"This is amazing!" laughed Benny. "Sharing makes everything better!"

They played until the sun was high in the sky. They took turns being the roller and the catcher. When someone missed the ball, the others cheered them on.

"You're getting so good at catching!" Benny told Ruby.

"And you're the best ball roller ever!" Rosie told Benny.

As the friends played together, Benny felt proud and happy. His heart was full of warm, fuzzy feelings. Sharing his ball made the game twice as fun!

When it was time to go inside, Benny hugged his new friends.

"Thank you for teaching me about sharing," he said. "Being fair makes everyone happy!"

"And thank you for saying sorry," said Rosie. "That was very brave."

Benny learned that being fair and kind felt much better than being selfish. From that day on, he always remembered to share and play fairly with his friends.

The End.
