# Benny Beaver's Messy Guest Room

Benny Beaver loved to build things. He built dams with sticks, towers with blocks, and bridges with books. But there was one big problem - Benny's guest room was very, very messy!

"Oh my!" said Mama Beaver as she peeked into the guest room. "Benny, your cousin Bella is coming to visit tomorrow. Where will she sleep with all these toys everywhere?"

Benny looked around the room. His wooden blocks were scattered on the floor like fallen leaves. His toy cars were hiding under the bed. His crayons rolled around loose, making colorful trails on the carpet. The guest bed was covered with puzzle pieces that sparkled in the sunlight.

"I don't know, Mama," Benny said, his tail drooping sadly. "I like building, but I don't know how to clean up!"

Mama Beaver sat down next to Benny. "Would you like me to help you learn?" she asked gently.

"Yes, please!" Benny's eyes lit up like twinkling stars.

"First," said Mama Beaver, "let's make homes for all your toys. Just like how we beavers have our cozy lodge, your toys need special places too."

They started with the blocks. "These blocks are different sizes," Mama explained. "Let's put the big ones in this box and the small ones in that box." Benny carefully sorted each block, listening to them clink and clank as they found their new homes.

"This is like building!" Benny said excitedly. "I'm building organized piles!"

Next, they gathered the toy cars. "Vroom, vroom!" Benny made car sounds as he drove each one into a special basket. The cars seemed happy to be together, like a parking lot for toys.

"Now the crayons," said Mama Beaver. They found a colorful tin box with different sections. "Red crayons here, blue crayons there," Benny said as he sorted them by color. The crayons looked like a beautiful rainbow in their new home.

The puzzle pieces were trickier. "These all go together to make one big picture," Mama explained. "Let's put them in this special puzzle box so they don't get lost."

As they worked, Benny discovered something wonderful. "Mama, when everything has a place, I can find what I want to play with!"

"That's right, sweetie," Mama smiled. "And when Cousin Bella comes, she'll have a clean, comfortable place to sleep. Plus, you can share your organized toys with her!"

Finally, they made the bed with fresh, soft sheets that smelled like spring flowers. The pillows were fluffy like clouds, and the blanket was smooth and warm.

"Wow!" said Benny, looking around the room. "It's so clean and pretty! And I know where everything is!"

Just then, the doorbell rang. "Bella's here!" called Papa Beaver.

Cousin Bella waddled into the guest room, her eyes wide with wonder. "Benny, this room is amazing! Everything is so neat and organized. Will you teach me how to keep my room this way?"

"Of course!" Benny beamed proudly. "And we can play with all my toys together. I know exactly where everything is!"

That night, both little beavers slept peacefully in the organized guest room. Benny learned that being organized wasn't just about cleaning up - it was about making space for others, finding things easily, and feeling proud of his space.

From that day on, Benny always remembered to give his toys special homes, making room for friends and keeping his space welcoming for everyone.

The End.
