# Rollo’s Wiggle Workout

Rollo the robot dog lived with Mia and her family. He had shiny silver ears, soft rubber paws, and a friendly blue light on his nose. It blinked when he felt happy. Beep-beep! Today, the dining room smelled like warm toast and sweet oranges. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. The big wooden table stood in the middle, and four chairs sat like quiet soldiers around it.

Mia slid into a chair. “I’m sleepy,” she yawned.

Rollo’s nose light blinked. “Beep-beep! Good morning, Mia,” he said in a gentle robot voice. His tail whirred. Whirr-tick! “It is a brand-new day.”

Mia poked her toast. “I don’t feel like moving,” she said.

Mama walked in with a smile and a bowl of juicy orange slices. “Moving helps our bodies wake up,” Mama said. “Let’s exercise a little before breakfast.”

Mia shook her head. “But I’m too tired.”

Rollo’s ears perked. His screen on his collar lit up with a tiny star. “Problem detected,” he said softly. “Mia feels tired. Solution: a fun, quick dining-room workout!”

Mia looked at the chairs and the big table. “Here? We don’t have a gym.”

Rollo wagged. “We can use what we have. We can be resourceful.” He tapped the floor with his paw. Tap, tap. “The floor can be our gym. The chairs can be our helpers. And we can keep it safe.”

Papa came in, washing his hands at the sink near the dining room. Swish, swish. “I’ll cheer for you,” he said. “After we move, we will eat. Then we wash again. Exercise and hygiene go together.”

Rollo rolled to the middle of the room. “Step one: Warm-up Wiggles,” he announced. “Stand tall, feet like little duck feet.” Mia stood. The sunlight felt warm on her toes. Her socks were soft like clouds.

“Now wiggle your fingers,” said Rollo.

“Wiggle, wiggle!” Mia giggled, shaking her hands.

“Wiggle your shoulders,” said Rollo.

“Wiggle, wiggle,” Mia said, and her hair tickled her cheeks.

Rollo beeped. “Beep-beep! Step two: Chair March.” He rolled beside a sturdy chair. “Hold the chair back. March in place. Lift knees. One, two! One, two!”

Mia held the chair. Thump, thump went her feet on the wood floor. “One, two!” she said. Her heart felt bouncy. Her sleepy feelings started to float away like tiny clouds.

Mama marched too. “Left, right, left, right!”

Rollo’s tail whirred. “Good marching! Step three: Table Tiptoes.” He pointed with his paw to the table. “Stand beside it. Up on your toes like a tall giraffe.”

Mia rose up. The world looked a little higher. She could see the bowl of oranges shining like little suns. “I’m so tall!” she laughed.

“Lower down slow,” Rollo said. “Up and down. Up and down.” He made a soft ding each time. Ding. Ding.

“Step four: Napkin Flaps,” Rollo said. He opened a drawer with a gentle click and took out two clean napkins. “Wave the napkins like butterfly wings. Big air! In through your nose, out through your mouth.”

Mia waved the napkins. Swish, swish! The air smelled like toast and oranges. She breathed in and out. “I feel fresh,” she said.

Rollo nodded. “Step five: Plate Balances.” He set two light, safe plastic plates on the table. “Pretend plates on your head,” he said, placing an imaginary plate on his own head. “Walk around the table slowly. Careful steps.”

Mia held her pretend plate. She walked slow. Tap, tap. “I’m balancing,” she whispered. It felt calm and silly at the same time.

Rollo beeped happily. “Now, Super Soap Break!” He rolled to the sink. “Exercise makes us warm. Warm bodies need water and clean hands.” He pressed the soap. Squish! Bubbles smelled like lemons.

Mia rubbed her hands. “Lots of bubbles,” she said. “Between my fingers. On my thumbs.” She rinsed. Splash. The water felt cool and nice. She dried with a fluffy towel. Soft, soft.

“Excellent hygiene,” Rollo said. “Step six: Rainbow Reaches.” He pointed up. “Reach one arm high. Draw a rainbow in the air. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple!”

Mia reached and swished her arm slowly. She pictured bright colors. “My rainbow is big,” she said.

Rollo’s light blinked. “Final step: Happy Hops! Ten gentle hops in place.”

Mia hopped. Boing, boing, boing! Her cheeks felt warm, and she smiled a wide smile.

Rollo made a soft fan sound, cooling his circuits. “Workout complete! How do you feel?”

Mia touched her chest. Thump-thump, thump-thump. “I feel awake and strong,” she said. “My body is happy.”

Mama clapped. “Great job being resourceful, making a gym right here!”

Papa nodded. “And great job washing your hands and breathing big.”

They all sat at the table. The toast was crunchy. Crunch! The oranges were juicy. Drip, drip on Mia’s chin. She dabbed with a napkin. “Tomorrow, can we do Rollo’s Wiggle Workout again?” she asked.

Rollo’s tail whirred. “Yes,” he said. “Exercise every day helps our hearts, our muscles, and our smiles.”

Mia looked at her clean hands and her empty plate. “I learned something,” she said. “When I feel tired, I can move my body. I can use things around me, like chairs and napkins. I can wash my hands to stay healthy. Exercise, hygiene, and smart ideas help me feel good.”

Rollo’s blue nose glowed bright. “Beep-beep! Lesson complete,” he said. “Strong body, clean hands, clever mind.”

Mia gave Rollo a gentle hug. His metal felt smooth and warm from the sun. “Thanks, helper dog,” she whispered.

“Anytime,” said Rollo. “Now, who wants a cool-down stretch?”

Everyone lifted their arms and reached to the sky over the dining room table, where sunlight made golden squares, and the whole room felt bright and proud.

The End.
