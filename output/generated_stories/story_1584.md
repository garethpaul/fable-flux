# Leo the Lion Cub Learns to Share

In a cozy, warm kitchen that smelled like fresh-baked cookies, lived a brave little lion cub named Leo. The kitchen had sunny yellow walls and a big wooden table where Leo loved to play with his favorite toy cars.

One morning, Leo's mama was making pancakes. The sweet smell of vanilla and maple syrup filled the air. "Mmm!" Leo purred, his little nose twitching with excitement.

*Ding dong!* The doorbell rang.

"That must be your friends Bella Bunny and Charlie Chipmunk," said Mama Lion, wiping her paws on her flowery apron. "They're coming for breakfast!"

Leo's eyes grew wide. He quickly grabbed all his toy cars and hugged them tight against his fuzzy chest. "These are MINE!" he roared softly. "I don't want to share!"

When Bella and Charlie arrived, their faces lit up seeing Leo's colorful cars scattered across the warm kitchen floor.

"Wow, Leo! Can we play with your cars?" asked Bella, her bunny ears bouncing with excitement.

"No!" Leo said loudly, pulling all the cars closer. "They're all mine! Find your own toys!"

Charlie's little chipmunk face looked sad. Bella's ears drooped down. They sat quietly at the table, watching Leo play alone.

Mama Lion noticed the problem. She knelt down next to Leo, her voice gentle and warm. "Leo, sweetie, how do you think your friends feel right now?"

Leo looked at Bella and Charlie. Their faces looked very sad. His tummy started to feel funny - not the good kind of funny from smelling pancakes, but the worried kind.

"They look... sad," Leo whispered.

"That's right," Mama said softly. "When we don't share, it makes our friends feel left out. How would you feel if you visited a friend and they wouldn't share their toys with you?"

Leo thought hard. His little lion heart felt heavy. "I would feel very sad too," he admitted.

Just then, Leo had an idea! His face brightened like sunshine. "I know! We can take turns! That way everyone gets to play!"

Leo jumped up and carefully divided his cars into three equal piles. "Bella, you can have the red cars first. Charlie, here are the blue ones. I'll play with the yellow ones. Then we can switch!"

"Really?" Bella's ears perked up again.

"Yes!" Leo said proudly. "We can build a big race track together! It'll be even more fun!"

The three friends spent the morning building the most amazing race track around the kitchen table. They took turns being the race announcer, the mechanic, and the driver. Leo discovered that sharing made everything much more exciting!

"Breakfast is ready!" called Mama Lion, setting down a plate of golden pancakes.

"Can we share the pancakes too?" asked Leo, remembering his new lesson.

"Of course!" laughed Mama. "Sharing food makes it taste even better!"

As they munched on the delicious pancakes, Leo felt warm and happy inside - warmer than the cozy kitchen, warmer than the sunshine streaming through the windows.

"Thank you for sharing, Leo," said Charlie, syrup on his whiskers.

"Thank you for teaching me that sharing is more fun!" Leo replied, his little chest puffed out with pride.

From that day on, Leo became known as the most generous little lion cub in the whole neighborhood. He learned that when you share with others, you don't lose anything - you gain friendship, fun, and a happy heart.

The End.
