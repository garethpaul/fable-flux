# Fox Steps and Friendly Beats

Flicker was a curious fox kit with bright eyes and quick paws. Today, Flicker padded into the musical practice room. The room smelled like polished wood. Sunlight striped the floor. Shiny drums, a tall piano, and tiny bells waited quietly. The room felt warm and safe.

Flicker wiggled his nose. “Hello, room,” he whispered. “I want to move and learn.” His tail swished with little swish-swish sounds.

Bam-bam! A small raccoon named Rilla tapped a drum with soft sticks. “Hi, Flicker!” she said. “I like the drum. It is loud and fun.”

Ting-ting! A hedgehog named Pip shook a bell bracelet. “Hi, friends,” Pip said. “I like the bells. They are light and shiny.”

Flicker’s ears perked up. “I like to run and hop,” he said. “Can we move our bodies with the music? Exercise can be fun!”

Rilla smiled. “Yes! But I want to practice my drum rolls.”

Pip nodded. “And I want to make pretty bell songs.”

Flicker felt a small twist in his tummy. He wanted to move. They wanted to play. Could they do both? He took a deep breath. “Maybe we can share and make a plan,” he said. “We can take turns. We can mix music and moves.”

Rilla tilted her head. “What kind of plan?”

“First we warm up,” Flicker said. “Then we drum and move. Then we ring and stretch. We all get a turn.”

Pip’s eyes sparkled. “That sounds fair.”

Flicker laid a soft mat on the floor. The mat was smooth and cool. “Warm-up time,” he said. “Let’s be kind to our bodies.”

He showed big, slow arm circles. “One, two, slow and round,” he said. Rilla and Pip circled their arms. They giggled when their fingers whooshed through the air.

“Now touch the sky,” Flicker said. They reached up, up, up. The ceiling lights blinked gently. “Now touch your toes.” Their paws, hands, and tiny feet stretched down. They felt their backs lengthen like sleepy cats.

Rilla’s drum gave a soft tap-tap. “Can I add a beat?” she asked.

“Yes,” Flicker said kindly. “Let’s count together.” Rilla drummed, “Tap, tap, tap, tap.” Flicker marched in place. “Step, step, step, step.” Pip shook bells with a happy “ting-ting-ting-ting.”

Flicker’s heart felt light. His legs warmed. His tail wagged. “Let’s hop like bunnies,” he said. “Four hops!” They hopped—one, two, three, four—while Rilla drummed four taps and Pip bell-rang four tings. Their sounds and steps matched. It felt neat and together.

After a few minutes, Rilla rubbed her arms. “I’m getting tired,” she said.

“That is okay,” Flicker said. “Exercise helps us get strong. We listen to our bodies. We can drink water.” He brought small cups. The water was cool and sweet.

Pip smiled at Flicker. “Thank you for the water. That was kind.”

Flicker smiled back. “Now it’s bell time,” he said. “Pip, you lead. We will move gently.”

Pip’s cheeks glowed. “I will make a slow bell song,” he said. Ting… ting… ting… The sound felt like raindrops. Flicker showed slow stretches. “Reach like a tree,” he said. “Sway in the breeze.” They swayed side to side. Their bodies felt calm and long. The floor was smooth under their feet. The air smelled like lemon polish.

Rilla watched Pip and Flicker. She tapped her drum softly, just a whisper, so Pip’s bells could shine. That was kind.

After the stretch, Flicker said, “Now we make a plan for the last part.” He looked at his friends. “Rilla wants a real drum roll. I want fast steps. Pip wants a bright finish.”

“How do we choose?” Pip asked.

“We can negotiate,” Flicker said. “We can talk and find a way that helps each friend.”

Rilla nodded. “I can drum fast for eight beats.”

Pip added, “I can ring the bells on the last two beats.”

Flicker wagged his tail. “I can do quick fox steps for eight beats, then a big bow.”

They all agreed. “Yes!”

“Ready?” Flicker asked.

“Ready!” said Rilla and Pip.

Rilla started: tap-tap-tap-tap, tap-tap-tap-tap! Flicker’s paws pitter-pattered—step-step, step-step—light and quick. Pip counted in his head, then rang, “TING! TING!” at the end. Flicker bowed low. Rilla smiled wide. Pip clapped his little hands. The room rang with happy sounds. Their hearts felt warm and proud.

Flicker took a breath and felt his body glow. “We moved our bodies. We got strong. We were kind. We made a plan together.”

Rilla patted the drum. “My arms feel worked and good.”

Pip hugged his bells. “My shoulders feel long and calm.”

Flicker nodded. “Exercise can be fun with music and friends. We can listen, share, and take turns. That is kindness. When we talk and choose together, that is negotiation.”

They cleaned up the room. Sticks in a cup. Bells in a box. Mat rolled snug. The room grew quiet and neat again.

Flicker looked at his friends. “Same time tomorrow?”

“Yes!” they said.

They bumped paws and hands, gentle and happy.

The End.
