# Tessa Tortoise and the Tidy-Up Team

In a cozy bedroom with soft yellow walls, a small lamp glowed like a friendly moon. The blankets smelled like clean cotton. Toys made gentle clinks as they rested in baskets. A wise tortoise named Tessa lived there. She had a warm shell the color of tea and honey, and she wore tiny round glasses that slid down her nose.

Tessa stretched her little legs. “Good morning, cozy room,” she said in a calm voice. She looked around. Blocks were on the rug. Crayons were on the bed. Books were open like sleepy butterflies.

Tessa nodded. “I have a plan. I will finish my tasks: make my bed, put away toys, and read my list.”

She had a small chart on the wall. It was smooth and shiny. It said:
1. Make bed
2. Put away toys
3. Feed Goldie the fish

Tessa picked up the blanket. Swish. She smoothed it with her gentle hands. “Task one,” she said. “Done.”

She smiled and felt proud. Her heart felt warm, like cocoa.

Then she heard voices outside the window. Tap, tap, tap. The sound was like soft rain. Tessa peeked out. Neighbors were hanging posters for a community clean-up. Mrs. Mouse called, “We need helpers to make the garden bright!”

Tessa wanted to help. Her feet wiggled with excitement. But she looked back at her chart. Two more tasks waited.

“I want to go now,” she whispered. Her tummy fluttered. “But I must finish what I started.”

She turned to the toys. Clink, clack, click. Blocks went into the box. Crayons slipped into their tin with a happy tap-tap. “Task two,” Tessa said. “Done.”

She went to Goldie the fish. The fish bowl sparkled by the window. Goldie swam in circles, swish, swish. “Good morning, Goldie,” said Tessa. She sprinkled food—plip, plip. Goldie bubbled with joy. “Task three,” said Tessa. “Done!”

Just then, Tessa noticed something under the bed. A shiny marble. It did not belong to her. She rolled it in her hand. It felt cool and smooth. Tessa frowned a little. “This is Ben Bunny’s marble. He lost it yesterday.”

She could hide it and keep it. The marble was pretty like a tiny moon. But Tessa shook her head. “I will be honest,” she said softly. “Honesty is kind.”

Tessa tucked the marble in a small pouch and headed to the door. She stopped and looked back. Her bed was neat. Toys were away. Goldie was fed. The room felt calm and clean. Tessa took a deep breath. It smelled like lemon soap and sunshine.

Outside, the neighbors were gathering. “Hello, Tessa!” said Mrs. Mouse. “We are cleaning the garden. Can you help?”

“Yes,” said Tessa. “I finished my tasks. I can help now.”

Ben Bunny hopped over. “I lost my marble,” he said sadly. “It is shiny and blue.”

Tessa opened her pouch. “I found it under my bed,” she said. “I waited to tell you. I wanted to be honest.”

Ben’s eyes grew bright. “Thank you!” he said. He hugged the marble. “You told the truth. That helps my heart.”

Tessa smiled. “We can help the garden now.”

The team got to work. Tessa wore small garden gloves. They felt a little big, but cozy. She pulled tiny weeds, tug, tug. The soil smelled rich and earthy. The sun felt warm on her shell. Birds sang, “Chee-chee-chee!” It sounded like happy giggles.

Mrs. Mouse swept the path. Swish, swish. Ben Bunny picked up litter with a grabber. Clink, clank. Tessa placed new flowers in soft holes. She patted the dirt, pat-pat-pat.

“Community service is helping together,” Tessa said. “It makes our street smile.”

After a while, the garden shimmered with colors—red tulips, yellow daffodils, and tiny bluebells. The path was clean. The neighbors clapped. “Hooray for the tidy-up team!”

Mrs. Mouse handed Tessa a little ribbon. It was green and silky. “For your honest heart and your helpful hands,” she said.

Tessa felt a warm glow in her chest. She thought about her cozy bedroom and her finished chart. She thought about giving the marble back. She thought about the garden, bright and happy.

Ben Bunny hopped close. “How did you do so much?” he asked. “You did your chores. You told the truth. You helped us, too.”

Tessa nodded. “I made a plan. I did each task, one by one. When I finish my tasks, I feel calm. Then I can help others with a happy heart.”

Ben smiled. “I want to try that.”

When Tessa went home, she stepped into her tidy room. It felt peaceful and safe. She wrote a new list:
1. Water plant
2. Read a book
3. Help a friend

She tapped her pencil, tap-tap. “I will do my tasks. I will be honest. I will help my community,” she said.

Her lamp glowed softly. Goldie swished in her bowl. Tessa curled under her neat blanket and whispered, “One by one, I get things done.”

She closed her eyes with a gentle smile, ready for tomorrow’s list and tomorrow’s helping hands.

The lesson was clear in her calm heart: finish your tasks, tell the truth, and help your community. It makes the world feel cozy, like a warm, tidy room.

The End.
