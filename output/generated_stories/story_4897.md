# Gigi the Giraffe Says What She Needs

Gigi the giraffe lived in a bright, playful living room. Pillows were puffed like little clouds. A soft rug tickled her hooves. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor, and music hummed from a small speaker. It smelled like fresh oranges and clean laundry.

Gigi was thoughtful. She liked to think before she did things. Today was dance day. “Dance helps my body grow strong,” Gigi told herself, “and helps my heart feel calm.” She stretched her long neck and took a deep breath in. She breathed out slow. “I can listen to my body. I can use my words.”

Her friend, Bop the blue rubber ball, bounced over. “Boing! Dance time!” squeaked Bop.

“Hi, Bop,” Gigi said with a smile. “Yes, dance time. Let’s make it gentle and fun.”

Gigi pressed play. A happy beat filled the room—tap, tap, tap! The floor felt smooth under her hooves. She swished her tail. “Step, step, wiggle,” she sang softly. Her ears wiggled to the music.

Bop bounced faster. “Let’s spin super fast!” Bop cheered.

Gigi felt the music in her chest like a tiny drum. But her tummy felt a little tight. “Hmm,” she thought. “Fast spinning makes me dizzy.” She put her hoof on her chest and took another breath. “I can say what I need.”

“Bop,” Gigi said kindly, “fast spins make me dizzy. I want to dance slow right now. Let’s do gentle wiggles.”

Bop paused mid-bounce. “Oh! Okay. Thanks for telling me,” Bop said. “Wiggles are great too!”

They wiggled together. Swish, swish! Gigi smiled. Her body felt happy. The music changed to a clappy rhythm. Clap, clap, clap! Gigi clapped her hooves and giggled. The sound echoed in the cozy room. She felt proud. “I used my voice,” she thought.

Knock, knock! The toy box lid tapped. Out popped Maki the sock monkey with a jingle. “Let’s do a jump dance!” Maki said, jingling tiny bells. “Big jumps!”

Gigi checked in with her body. Her legs felt strong, but she heard her tummy make a tiny grumble. “I need water,” she thought. She felt her mouth a little dry. “I can ask for a break.”

“Friends,” Gigi said in a gentle voice, “I need a water break. My body needs a sip. Then we can jump.”

“Good idea,” Maki said, jingling softly. “Healthy bodies need water!”

Gigi walked to her bright blue cup. She took small sips. The water felt cool and fresh. “Ahh,” she said, feeling calm. She wiped her mouth with a soft napkin. “Thank you, water,” she whispered.

She turned to her friends. “Thanks for waiting,” she said.

“You’re welcome!” Bop and Maki chimed.

Now the music played a quick drum beat. Gigi tried one small hop. Boing! She felt steady. “I’m ready for little jumps,” she said. “Little jumps feel safe for me.”

“Little jumps together!” Maki cheered.

They hopped in a line: hop, hop, hop. The rug felt bouncy under Gigi’s hooves. Her tail swished, and the room smelled like orange peels again when the sunlight warmed the bowl on the table.

Soon, the music got very loud. BOOM, BOOM, BOOM! Gigi’s ears twitched. The loud beat felt too big in her head. She felt a frown starting.

She took a deep breath and placed a hoof on the speaker. “Too loud for me,” Gigi said clearly. “I need the music softer.”

Bop nodded. “Let’s turn it down.” The knob clicked. The sound became gentle again—tap, tap. Gigi’s shoulders relaxed. “Thank you,” she said. “I feel better.”

They started a new dance. Gigi led with a calm voice. “Step, step, stretch up tall. Smile, and say what you need.” They all stretched tall, taller, tallest! Gigi felt the warm air on her cheeks. She felt strong and brave inside.

After the dance, Gigi’s tummy made a bigger grumble. “Snack time,” she decided. She asked, “Friends, do you want orange slices?” The room smelled extra bright as she peeled the orange. The peel made a soft tearing sound. “Zzzzip!” The juice was sweet and sticky on her tongue.

“Yum!” said Bop. “Thank you!”

“Thank you,” said Maki, jingling happily.

Gigi wiped her hooves. “You’re welcome,” she said. “I am grateful for my friends. I am grateful for my body. I am grateful for my voice.”

Bop bounced closer. “Gigi, I liked when you told us your needs. That helps me know what to do.”

Gigi felt warm in her heart. “Using our words keeps us safe and happy,” she said. “We can ask for water, ask for soft music, and choose gentle moves. We can listen to our bodies. We can say yes, and we can say no.”

Maki clapped. “Let’s end with a thank-you dance!”

They swayed slow, like leaves in the sun. “Thank you, feet,” said Gigi. “Thank you, ears. Thank you, friends.” The dance was calm and sweet. The room felt peaceful and bright.

Gigi took one last deep breath. In and out. She smiled. “Today I learned that self-advocacy means using my words to share my needs,” she said. “That helps me stay healthy, kind, and brave.”

Her friends nodded. “And we learned to listen,” said Bop.

Gigi wrapped her long neck in a gentle hug around her friends. “I feel proud,” she whispered.

The End.
