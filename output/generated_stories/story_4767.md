# Koko's Garden Helper Day

The warm sun shone brightly in the backyard where Koko the koala sat under his favorite eucalyptus tree. He watched the colorful flowers dance in the gentle breeze.

"What a perfect day to help in the garden," Koko said softly to himself.

Koko's friend Bella the bunny hopped over, looking worried. "Oh no, Koko! I made a big mistake."

"What happened, Bella?" Koko asked in his calm, gentle voice.

Bella's ears drooped. "I was watering Mama's vegetable garden, but I left the hose running. Now all the water is gone from the rain barrel!"

Koko could see that Bella felt very sad. The empty rain barrel sat in the corner of the yard, and water puddles covered the ground.

"Don't worry," Koko said kindly. "We can fix this together. But first, let's turn off that hose."

They walked over to the green garden hose. Water was still flowing out, making more puddles. Koko gently turned the handle to stop the water.

"There," he said. "Now, what can we do to help the garden and save water too?"

Bella sniffled. "I don't know. I wasted so much water!"

Koko thought carefully. "Look around, Bella. What do you see?"

Bella looked at the puddles sparkling in the sunshine. "Lots of water on the ground?"

"Exactly!" Koko smiled. "We can collect this water and use it again. That's called recycling water."

They found two small watering cans in the garden shed. The metal cans felt warm from sitting in the sun. Together, they carefully scooped up the clean puddle water.

"This is fun!" Bella giggled as she filled her little red watering can.

"And we're helping the Earth too," Koko explained. "When we don't waste water, there's more for everyone - the plants, the animals, and all our friends."

They carried their watering cans to the thirsty vegetable plants. The tomatoes looked droopy in the heat. The carrots needed a drink too.

"Pour slowly," Koko showed Bella. "Just a little bit for each plant."

The cool water made a gentle splashing sound as it hit the soil. The plants seemed to perk up right away.

"Look, Koko!" Bella pointed. "The flowers are standing up tall again!"

"Plants are just like us," Koko said. "They need just the right amount of water - not too much, not too little."

As they worked together, more friends came to help. Charlie the chipmunk brought acorns to plant. Sara the squirrel helped dig small holes for new seeds.

"Working together makes everything easier," Koko noticed. "And more fun too!"

When they finished watering, Bella looked much happier. "Thank you for helping me fix my mistake, Koko."

"That's what friends do," Koko replied. "And you learned something important today."

"I learned to turn off the water when I'm done," Bella said proudly. "And that we can reuse water instead of wasting it!"

The afternoon sun felt warm on their fur as they sat in the shade. The garden looked green and healthy. The rain barrel would fill up again with the next shower.

"Tomorrow, let's check on our plants together," Koko suggested.

"Yes!" Bella agreed. "And I'll remember to save water every day."

Koko smiled his gentle smile. Taking care of the Earth felt good, especially when friends worked together.

The End.
