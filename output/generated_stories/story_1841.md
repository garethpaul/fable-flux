# Benny Beaver and the Busy Pantry

Benny was a busy beaver with a flat, paddle tail and bright, curious eyes. One rainy morning, he waddled into the cozy pantry. The pantry smelled like warm oats and sweet apples. Jars clinked softly. The shelves were full of crunchy crackers, soft bread, shiny cans, and a little jar of sticky honey.

“I will help!” Benny said. “I will make the pantry neat.”

He stacked boxes. He scooted jars. Tap-tap went his little claws on the wooden floor. His tail swished, swoosh-swish. He hummed a happy tune.

Just then, his friends peeked in. Mia Mouse wiggled her whiskers. Gus Goose fluffed his feathers. Lila Ladybug landed gently on a flour sack.

“Hi, Benny!” said Mia. “Can we help?”

Benny’s eyes sparkled. “Yes! Let’s work together.”

Mia squeaked, “I can carry tiny things.”

Gus honked softly, “I can reach tall shelves.”

Lila chimed, “I can look closely and find labels.”

They all smiled. The pantry felt warm and friendly.

Benny pointed. “Jars here. Boxes there. Bread on top.”

Mia nodded and took a small jar. “Okay!”

Gus stretched his long neck and picked up a big box. “Got it!”

Lila fluttered near a row of cans, reading the tiny words. “Peas, corn, peaches,” she said.

But suddenly, clink-clank! The jars got too close. Mia squeaked, “Oops!” A jar wobbled.

Thunk! Gus tried to help, but his wings brushed a box. It slid. The box bumped the bread. The bread bumped the honey. The honey jar rolled… roll, roll… bonk!

Honey dripped like golden rain. Sticky sweetness spread on the shelf. Benny’s eyes went wide. His tail drooped.

“Oh no,” Benny whispered. “This is a mess.”

Mia’s whiskers trembled. “I didn’t mean to.”

Gus’s feathers sagged. “I was trying to help.”

Lila landed on Benny’s finger. “Let’s slow down.”

Benny took a deep breath. In… out… in… out. He felt his heart go from fast-fast to slow-slow.

He remembered something his grandma beaver said: “Use peaceful words. Ask. Listen. Make a plan.”

Benny nodded. “Friends, let’s talk.”

They formed a little circle on the clean floor. They could hear the soft patter of rain outside and smell the sweet honey.

Benny said in a calm voice, “I feel worried when things tip over. I want our pantry to be safe.”

Mia put her tiny paw on Benny’s paw. “I feel sad that I knocked the jar. I want to try again.”

Gus took a gentle breath. “I feel confused about where to put things. I want clear steps.”

Lila’s wings shimmered. “I feel ready to help by looking and guiding.”

Benny smiled. “Thank you for sharing. Let’s make a plan together.”

He held up one finger. “Step one: We clean up the honey, slowly and safely.”

“Step two,” said Mia, “We carry small things, one at a time.”

“Step three,” honked Gus, “I wait for a clear space before I lift big boxes.”

“Step four,” chimed Lila, “I say the labels out loud so we all know where things go.”

Benny added, “And step five: We use kind words. We ask before we pass. We listen.”

Everyone agreed. “Yes!”

They started with the sticky honey. Swish-swish went a soft cloth. Drip-drop, the sweetness disappeared. The shelf felt smooth again.

“Ready?” Benny asked.

“Ready,” said Mia, carrying a tiny jar that smelled like cinnamon.

“Ready,” honked Gus, standing back until Benny said, “Okay, go!”

“Peaches on the middle shelf,” Lila called. “Crackers near the bread. Heavy cans on the bottom.”

“Thank you,” Gus said. He moved slowly. His big feet went step, pause, step.

“Passing the jar,” Mia squeaked. “Is now a good time?”

“Yes, now,” Benny said. He held out both paws. “I’ve got it.”

Tap-tap-tap, they worked in a calm rhythm. They smiled. They listened. They asked and answered. The pantry began to shine.

After a while, Lila landed on the last box. “Almost done!”

Benny placed the final jar. He stepped back. The shelves were tidy and strong. The crackers were crisp in their spot. The bread rested soft and safe. The honey sat snug, with a little note that said, “Carry with care.”

Benny’s tail made a happy thump. “We did it!”

Mia’s whiskers twitched with joy. “We did it together.”

Gus flapped his wings gently. “I liked our plan.”

Lila giggled. “Peaceful words are powerful.”

Benny looked at his friends. “When we had a problem, we stopped. We talked. We listened. We made a plan. That helped.”

Mia nodded. “I felt heard.”

Gus smiled. “I knew what to do.”

Lila beamed. “We kept each other safe.”

Benny gave a little bow. “Thank you for your care and your kindness.”

They all shared a group hug. The pantry felt cozy and calm. The rain outside turned to a soft drizzle, pitter-patter, like a happy song.

Benny said, “Our lesson today is peaceful communication. We use gentle voices, we ask before we act, and we listen. That is how we solve problems.”

Everyone agreed. “Yes!”

They left the pantry, proud and clean and calm, ready for a snack together: crunchy crackers, a slice of soft bread, and a tiny taste of honey—passed with care.

The End.
