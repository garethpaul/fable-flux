# Bubbles Plans in the Quiet Attic

Bubbles was a playful dolphin with bright eyes and a happy squeak. One sunny day, Bubbles swam up a swirl of stairs in a seaside house. At the top was a quiet attic. It smelled like old wood and warm dust. Sunbeams peeked through a tiny window and made little sparkles dance in the air.

“Hello, attic!” Bubbles squeaked. “I want to build a cozy reading corner!”

The attic was calm. A soft moth fluttered by. Boxes sat like sleepy whales. A fuzzy blanket peeked from a basket. Bubbles felt excited. But everything was spread out. Where should Bubbles start?

Bubbles took a deep breath. “Let’s make a plan,” Bubbles said. “Planning helps me do big things, one small step at a time.”

Bubbles found a big piece of paper and a chunky blue crayon. The crayon smelled a little like sweet wax. “Step one,” Bubbles said, writing slowly. “Find a clean, safe spot.” Bubbles smiled. “Step two: Gather things I need. Step three: Put it all together. Step four: Try it out!”

Bubbles clapped tiny fins. “I have a plan!”

First, Bubbles looked for a clean, safe spot. The floor was dusty, and a little creaky. “Creak, creak,” sang the floor. Bubbles pressed a fin gently. “This part feels strong,” Bubbles said. “I will put my reading corner here.”

Next, Bubbles wanted to gather things. Bubbles peeked into boxes. “What do I need?” Bubbles asked. “I need a soft place to sit, a light to see, and books to read.”

Bubbles lifted the fuzzy blanket. “Soft!” Bubbles giggled, rubbing it on a cheek. It felt warm and snuggly, like a hug. Bubbles found a small pillow with stars. “Hello, pillow.” Bubbles found a little lamp with a switch. Click! The lamp made a gentle glow, like a jar of fireflies.

Bubbles wiggled happily. Then a tall box blocked the path. Bump! Bubbles tried to move it. “Ooof, this is heavy,” Bubbles said, feeling a little worried. Bubbles paused. “I can speak up for myself. I can ask for help.”

Bubbles swam downstairs and called, “Grandma? May I please have help moving a box in the attic?”

Grandma smiled. “Thank you for asking so clearly, Bubbles. That is good self-advocacy.” Together they went up. The stairs went tap, tap, tap under Grandma’s shoes. In the quiet attic, they pushed the box a little to the side. Slide, slide. “There,” said Grandma. “Safe and easy.”

“Thank you, Grandma,” Bubbles said. Bubbles’ heart felt light and proud. Asking for help was a smart part of the plan.

Back to gathering! Bubbles found a basket of picture books. The pages smelled like paper and stories. “Perfect,” Bubbles said. Bubbles carried the books carefully, one by one. “Slow and steady,” Bubbles whispered. “That is good planning.”

Now it was time to put it all together. Bubbles spread the fuzzy blanket on the strong floor spot. The stars on the pillow looked like tiny sugar cookies. Bubbles set the lamp on a small, low table. Click! The glow made the attic feel friendly.

Bubbles checked the plan. “Step four: Try it out!” Bubbles sat on the blanket. The blanket felt cozy. The pillow felt soft. The lamp made a warm circle of light. Bubbles opened a book. Swish went the page.

“Hmm,” Bubbles said. “It is a little chilly.” Bubbles shivered. “What can I do?” Bubbles tapped a fin to the chin. “I can add ‘extra warmth’ to my plan.”

Bubbles wrote, “Step five: Add a sweater.” Bubbles swam to a hook and found a small, knitted sweater. It smelled like clean soap. Bubbles slipped it on. “Ahh, warm,” Bubbles sighed.

Bubbles smiled. “Planning helps me notice what I need,” Bubbles said. “I can speak up and make changes.”

Just then, the moth fluttered near the lamp. Its wings went whisper, whisper. “Careful, little friend,” Bubbles said gently. “The lamp is warm. You can rest by the window.” Bubbles opened the window a tiny bit for a cool breeze, and the moth drifted to the light on the wall, safe and calm.

Grandma peeked in. “How is your reading corner, Bubbles?”

Bubbles beamed. “I made a plan. I followed my steps. I asked for help. I added a sweater. Now it is just right!”

Grandma nodded. “That is wonderful planning and skill-building. You solved problems one by one.”

Bubbles opened a book and read the pictures out loud. “Splash, splash,” Bubbles giggled at a dolphin drawing. The attic felt peaceful. The light was soft. The air smelled like old wood and happy stories.

When the story ended, Bubbles cleaned up and checked the list. “All done,” Bubbles said. “Planning made a big job feel easy.”

Bubbles looked at the crayon paper and added one more line: “Next time: Add a tiny snack.”

Bubbles winked. “I can plan again tomorrow!”

The End.
