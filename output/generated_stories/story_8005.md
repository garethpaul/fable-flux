# Pippa Parrot’s Table Talk

Pippa was a bright green parrot with rainbow tail feathers. She lived with Nana Nelly in a cozy house. Every evening, the dining room filled with warm yellow light and the yummy smell of soup. The wooden table felt smooth, and the chairs made little squeaks when they moved. Tonight, Nana Nelly called, “Dinner time!”

Pippa flapped in, singing a little tune. “Squawk! I have a big idea!” Her eyes shone. Pippa loved ideas. She was a creative parrot. She liked to paint, to stack napkins like mountains, and to make up funny rhymes.

Nana Nelly smiled. “Hello, Pippa dear. Let’s set the table. It’s our chore. We do it together.”

Pippa puffed her feathers proudly. “I will make the table beautiful! I will build a napkin castle. I will make spoon trains. Choo-choo!”

Nana Nelly looked at the clock. The clock ticked softly, tick-tock, tick-tock. “We need the table ready in ten minutes,” she said kindly. “The soup is hot and the bread is warm.”

Pippa’s head tilted. She pictured towers of napkins and long lines of spoons. “Ten minutes is not a lot,” she chirped. “I want to make art with the table.”

“Art is lovely,” Nana said. “But we also need to eat. We must respect our tummies and the time.”

Pippa’s feathers drooped. “But my idea is so big,” she whispered. Her chest felt tight like a tiny knot. She wanted to help. She wanted to feel proud. She wanted the table to look like a parade.

Just then, the front door opened. The neighbors, Mr. Lee and little Mia, came in with a basket. “Soup night!” Mia giggled. “We brought carrots.” The basket smelled sweet and earthy.

Mia peered at the empty table. “Can I help?” she asked.

Pippa hopped from foot to foot. “I want to make a napkin castle,” she told Mia. “But Nana needs the table set fast.”

Mia nodded. “I like castles,” she said. “I also like soup.”

Mr. Lee smiled. “In our building, we help at the community room,” he said. “We set tables for everyone. We have a trick.” He tapped his chin. “We mix fast and fun. We use a plan.”

Pippa blinked. “A plan?” Her heart felt lighter. Plans sounded helpful. They sounded smart.

Nana Nelly nodded. “Let’s find a compromise,” she said gently. “A compromise means we meet in the middle. We share ideas. We both feel heard.”

Pippa whispered the word slowly. “Com-pro-mise.”

Mr. Lee said, “We can set the table quickly for eating. Then, we can save a little time at the end for your art.”

Mia clapped softly. “Yes! First we line up plates, cups, spoons, and napkins. Then we make a small castle centerpiece.”

Pippa’s eyes sparkled. “A tiny castle?” she asked.

“A tiny castle,” Nana agreed. “Fast first, fancy later.”

Pippa breathed in the warm soup smell. She felt brave. “Okay,” she said. “That is our plan.” Her feathers fluffed with new self-confidence. She could do this. She could help and be creative too.

They started. Pippa counted the plates with a cheerful chirp. “One, two, three, four.” The plates felt cool and smooth on her claws. Mia placed cups. They made little tap sounds. Tap, tap, tap. Nana set down the spoons. Mr. Lee passed the bread. The room hummed with soft work sounds and friendly whispers.

Pippa kept the beat. “Count and set, count and set,” she sang. She liked the rhythm. She liked the team. She liked being part of something that helped everyone. It felt like community service right in the dining room. Her chest felt warm and proud.

In just a few minutes, the table was ready. The soup steamed. The bread crust crunched when Nana sliced it. “Now,” said Nana, “you have two minutes for art.”

Pippa and Mia folded napkins into little roofs. Pippa bent one napkin into a tiny flag. She stuck it in a clean salt holder. “Ta-da!” she chirped. “A small castle!”

Mia grinned. “It’s cute.”

Nana laughed softly. “It is perfect. Thank you for your hard work.”

They sat down together. The chairs made happy squeaks again. Pippa dipped her spoon. The soup was warm and carrot-sweet. “Mmm,” she said. “This tastes like a hug.”

Mr. Lee raised his cup of water. “To teamwork!”

“To compromise!” Nana added.

“To castles and chores!” Mia giggled.

“To me,” Pippa said with a shy smile, “feeling brave and helpful.”

They ate and talked. Pippa noticed how calm everyone felt. No one was rushed. No one was upset. Her heart felt like a soft pillow. She had respected Nana’s time. Nana had respected her art. They had shared and listened.

When dinner was done, Pippa hopped up. “I will help clear the table,” she announced. “Chores can be fun with a plan.”

Mia helped stack cups. Mr. Lee wiped a small drip. Nana hugged Pippa with her eyes. “Thank you, dear.”

Pippa fluffed her rainbow tail. “Today I learned a new word,” she said proudly. “Compromise. It means we make space for each other. We can do chores and still be creative.”

Nana nodded. “And you learned to trust yourself.”

Pippa smiled. “Yes. I can help. I can make art. I can do both.”

The tiny napkin castle waved its little flag. The dining room smelled like soap and bread crumbs. The clock ticked a soft, happy tick-tock. Pippa sang a gentle tune. “Fast first, fancy later,” she chirped. “We meet in the middle, and dinner is greater.”

The End.
