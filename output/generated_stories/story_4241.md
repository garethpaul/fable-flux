# Ruby Rabbit's Clean and Green Day

Ruby Rabbit hopped into her cozy bathroom one sunny morning. Her fluffy white fur was dusty from playing in the garden, and her pink nose twitched as she looked in the mirror.

"Oh my!" Ruby said, touching her dirty paws. "I need a good wash before I visit Grandma Bunny today."

Ruby turned on the water faucet. Whoosh! The water rushed out fast and loud. She giggled as the warm water splashed everywhere - on the sink, on the floor, and even on the wall!

"This is fun!" Ruby laughed, letting the water run while she looked for her special carrot-shaped soap.

But then Ruby heard a tiny voice. "Help! Help!" squeaked someone very small.

Ruby looked around. There, by the drain, was a little water drop with worried eyes.

"Who are you?" asked Ruby, turning off the faucet.

"I'm Dewey the Water Drop," said the little voice. "I live in the pipes with all my water drop friends. When you leave the water running, we get scared we might all disappear!"

Ruby's ears drooped. "Oh no! I didn't know I was making you scared. I just wanted to get clean."

Dewey smiled. "You can still get clean! But if you turn off the water while you soap up, you'll save lots of us water drops. We can help other animals and plants too!"

"Really?" Ruby's eyes sparkled. "How?"

"Well," Dewey explained, "when you save water, there's more for the flowers in your garden to drink. And more for the birds to splash in their baths. And more for your friend Squirrel when he gets thirsty!"

Ruby clapped her paws together. "I want to help everyone! Will you teach me how to wash the smart way?"

"Of course!" chirped Dewey.

Ruby turned on the water just a little bit - not too much. She wet her paws and face with the gentle stream. Then she turned the water off.

"Now soap up!" said Dewey.

Ruby rubbed the carrot soap all over her paws, making sweet-smelling bubbles. She scrubbed behind her ears and cleaned her fluffy tail. The soap felt smooth and slippery, and smelled like fresh carrots from her garden.

"This feels wonderful!" Ruby said, enjoying the warm, sudsy feeling.

"Now rinse!" said Dewey.

Ruby turned the water back on - just enough to wash away all the soap. The clean water felt cool and refreshing as it washed the bubbles down the drain.

"All clean!" Ruby announced, looking at her sparkling white fur in the mirror.

"And you saved lots of water drops!" cheered Dewey. "Thank you for being so kind and caring!"

Ruby felt proud and happy. She dried herself with her soft yellow towel and brushed her fur until it was fluffy and shiny.

"Taking care of myself AND helping others feels amazing!" Ruby said.

Later that day, Ruby taught her little sister how to brush teeth with the water turned off. She showed her cousin how to take quick showers. And she told Grandma Bunny all about Dewey and the importance of saving water.

"When we take good care of ourselves AND take care of our Earth," Ruby learned, "everyone feels happy and healthy!"

From that day on, Ruby always remembered to be gentle with water, and she felt wonderful knowing she was helping all her friends - both big and small.

The End.
