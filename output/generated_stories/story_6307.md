# Timmy Turtle’s Quiet Song

In a cozy bedroom with soft blue walls and a star nightlight, a shy turtle named Timmy peeked out from his shell. His bed felt warm like a hug, and his favorite blanket was fluffy and green, just like a leaf. The room smelled like clean laundry and a little bit like crayons. On a small shelf sat a tiny toy drum, a bright yellow shaker, and a shiny harmonica.

Timmy loved music, but he was shy. He whispered to himself, “I like to play, but I like it quiet.” He touched the harmonica with his gentle turtle paw. It was cool and smooth. He took a tiny breath and blew a soft note. Hummm. The sound was sweet and small, like a bee.

Timmy’s big sister, Bella, bounced into the room with a smile. “Hi, Timmy! Can I play music with you?” Her bracelet jingled like little bells.

Timmy wiggled in his shell. He wanted to say yes. He also wanted his quiet song. He felt a little twist in his tummy. That feeling was worry. He took a slow breath, just like Mama Turtle taught him. In, out.

“Okay,” Timmy said softly. “Let’s play.”

Bella picked up the toy drum. Boom-boom! It was loud. The sound filled the cozy bedroom. The blanket shook a little. Timmy’s eyes got wide. His heart thumped fast. He pulled his head back into his shell just a bit.

Bella didn’t see. She was having fun. Boom-boom-boom! “Listen, Timmy! Marching music!”

Timmy squeaked, “Too loud.” But the drum went boom again.

He felt another twist in his tummy. This feeling was mad and sad mixed together. He wanted to play. He wanted to be heard. He wanted quiet.

He took another slow breath. In, out. He remembered Mama’s words: “Use your words. Share your feelings. Then find a way to work together.”

Timmy moved his head all the way out of his shell. He put the harmonica down. He looked at Bella and said, “Bella, I feel upset. The drum is too loud for me. I like soft music. Can we make a plan?”

Bella stopped mid-boom. She looked at Timmy’s eyes and saw they were shiny. “Oh,” she said, gentle now. “I didn’t know. Thank you for telling me.”

Timmy nodded. “I want to play with you,” he said. “I also want quiet. Can we share our sounds?”

Bella tapped the drum very lightly. Tap-tap. “Like this?” she asked.

Timmy smiled a little. “Yes, soft taps.”

Bella thought for a moment. “We could take turns. First quiet song. Then a little louder song. We can practice both. We can share.”

Timmy’s shoulders relaxed. “That is a good plan,” he said.

They set a timer shaped like a duck. Quack! It made a silly sound when it started. “First quiet,” Bella said.

Timmy picked up the harmonica. He took a slow breath and made a soft hummm. He closed his eyes. The note felt like warm tea. Bella tapped the drum very gently. Tap… tap… tap. Timmy added a tiny shake with the yellow shaker. Shhh-shhh. The room felt calm and cozy again. The star nightlight glowed like a tiny moon.

Quack! The timer beeped. “Now my turn,” Bella said. She looked at Timmy. “Is a little louder okay?”

Timmy held his blanket and nodded. “A little,” he said. “But not too much.”

Bella smiled. She practiced a medium beat. Boom-tap, boom-tap. Timmy covered his ears just a bit, then peeked. He could still hear the beat, but it was not too big. He tried a stronger harmonica note. HUUUMM. It sounded brave. Timmy felt proud. He was sharing the space with his sister. He was practicing being brave.

After a while, they tried something new. “Let’s make a feelings song,” Bella said. “We can play how we feel.”

Timmy said, “Okay. I feel shy and happy.” He made soft notes and a tiny shake. Hummm, shhh.

Bella said, “I feel excited.” She made tap-tap-boom with a bouncy rhythm. Tap, tap, boom!

They listened to each other’s sounds. They watched each other’s faces. If Timmy’s eyes got big, Bella made her drum softer. If Bella’s feet started to dance, Timmy made his harmonica a little stronger. They were sharing, listening, and practicing.

When Mama Turtle peeked in, she heard sweet music. Not too loud, not too quiet. “What a lovely song,” Mama said. “I hear good problem-solving. I hear kindness.”

Timmy smiled. “We talked about feelings,” he said. “We made a plan. We took turns.”

Bella nodded. “We practiced quiet and medium. We shared.”

Mama Turtle hugged them both. The room smelled like warm cookies now. “How do you feel?” she asked.

Timmy placed a hand on his chest. “I feel calm and proud,” he said. “I used my words. I can be brave and still be me.”

Bella said, “I feel happy. I can play fun music and also be gentle.”

They put the instruments back on the shelf. The star nightlight twinkled. Timmy tucked into bed with his green blanket. Bella gave him a soft fist bump. “Team music,” she whispered.

“Team music,” Timmy whispered back.

Lesson: When sounds or feelings get too big, use your words. Share how you feel. Listen to others. Make a plan together. Practice taking turns. That is how we solve problems with kindness.

The End.
