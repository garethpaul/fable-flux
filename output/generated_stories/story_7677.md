# Milo's Big Discovery

Milo Monkey sat in his cozy study corner. The room smelled like old books and fresh pencils. Sunshine streamed through the window, making golden spots on his fuzzy brown fur.

"This puzzle is too hard!" Milo said, pushing away the colorful pieces. He had been trying to build a picture of a rainbow for a long time. The pieces just wouldn't fit together the way he wanted.

Milo's tail swished back and forth. He felt frustrated and grumpy. "I only like the red pieces," he mumbled. "Red is the best color. I don't need any other colors."

He picked up only the red puzzle pieces and tried to make a picture. But it looked strange and empty. Something was missing.

"Hmm," Milo scratched his head. His soft fingers felt confused.

Just then, he heard a gentle voice. "Hello, little monkey. What are you working on?"

Milo looked up. Mrs. Owl sat on the bookshelf above him. Her feathers were speckled brown and white, and her big round eyes sparkled with kindness.

"I'm making a rainbow," Milo said. "But I only want to use red pieces. Red is my favorite!"

Mrs. Owl hooted softly. "That sounds interesting. May I see?"

Milo showed her his red-only puzzle. It had big holes where other pieces should go.

"It looks like your rainbow is missing something," Mrs. Owl said gently. "Real rainbows have many colors working together."

"But I don't like the other colors," Milo said, crossing his arms.

Mrs. Owl flew down to sit beside him. "Have you tried looking at the other colors closely? Sometimes things surprise us when we give them a chance."

Milo felt curious despite himself. He picked up a yellow piece. It was bright and warm, like sunshine. It felt smooth in his fingers.

"This yellow piece is pretty," he admitted quietly.

"What about that blue one?" Mrs. Owl asked, pointing with her wing.

Milo touched the blue piece. It was cool and calm, like the sky on a peaceful day. "It feels nice too," he said, his voice growing more excited.

Soon, Milo was examining all the colors. The orange pieces reminded him of sweet mangoes. The green pieces looked like jungle leaves. The purple pieces sparkled like evening stars.

"Wow!" Milo exclaimed. "Each color is special in its own way!"

He began fitting the pieces together. Red next to orange, orange next to yellow, yellow next to green. The colors looked beautiful side by side.

"Look, Mrs. Owl!" Milo shouted with joy. "My rainbow is complete! All the colors make it perfect!"

The finished puzzle showed a bright, full rainbow stretching across a blue sky. Every color had its place, and together they created something magical.

"You discovered something wonderful today," Mrs. Owl said, her eyes twinkling. "When we keep our minds open to new things, we often find beauty we never expected."

Milo hugged his completed puzzle. His heart felt warm and happy. "I'm glad I tried all the colors. Being open-minded helped me solve my problem!"

From that day on, Milo always remembered to give new things a chance. Whether it was trying different foods, meeting new friends, or learning new games, he kept his mind open to wonderful possibilities.

The study corner became his favorite place to explore and discover new things, one colorful piece at a time.

The End.
