# Benny Beaver's Safe Dance Party

In a playful living room with soft pillows and a bright, cozy rug, a busy beaver named Benny wiggled his tail with excitement. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. The air smelled like vanilla cookies from the kitchen. Music played softly—tap, tap, tippy-tap—from a little speaker.

“I want a dance party!” Benny chirped. His big front teeth showed when he smiled. He loved to build and he loved to dance.

Mama Beaver peeked in, wiping cookie crumbs from her paws. “A dance party sounds fun, Benny,” she said. “But first, let’s make it safe.”

“Safe?” Benny asked, tilting his head.

“Safe means we move and play without getting hurt,” Mama said gently. “Let’s get the room ready.”

Benny nodded. He felt a warm, happy bubble in his chest. “I can help!”

They looked around the playful living room together. Toys were scattered—blocks, a tiny drum, and a toy boat. The blanket on the couch hung low, almost touching the floor.

Mama said, “Step one: Make space.”

Benny picked up his blocks. Clack, clack, clack. He put them in a basket. “Blocks go here!”

“Great job,” Mama cheered. “Step two: Soft floor.” She rolled up a slippery little mat. “We don’t want slips.”

Benny patted the big rug. It felt fluffy and safe under his paws. “Soft,” he said with a grin.

“Step three: Safe feet,” Mama said, pointing. Benny’s socks were loose and twisty.

Benny wiggled his toes and changed into snug, grippy socks with tiny stars. “Sticky stars!” he giggled, tapping his feet. Tap, tap.

They made a simple rule sign together on a piece of paper. Mama drew pictures and Benny colored them in:
- Keep space around us.
- Look where we step.
- Stop if we feel wobbly.
- Water sips between songs.

Benny held up the sign. “Rules help us have fun!”

Mama smiled. “Now, let’s dance around the world.”

Benny’s eyes grew wide. “Around the world? In our living room?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “We can learn dances from many places. It helps us know our big, beautiful world. And we dance safely.”

The first song began. It had a bouncy beat with soft drums. “This is a dance from West Africa,” Mama said. “Strong feet, happy hands.”

Benny bent his knees and stomped carefully on the rug. Stomp, stomp. He clapped above his head. Clap, clap! He watched his paws and stayed in his space. “I feel strong!” he cheered.

“Good watching,” Mama said. “You kept space.”

Next came a swishy song with jingles. “This music is from India,” Mama said. “Let’s move our hands like flowers opening.”

Benny opened and closed his fingers slowly. Open, close. The jingles made tiny rain sounds. “It’s like petals,” he whispered. He looked down at his feet to be sure they were steady. “Safe steps,” he reminded himself.

Then a quick, happy tune filled the room. “This dance is from Ireland,” Mama said. “Fast feet, light hops.”

Benny’s eyes sparkled. He loved to hop. He held Mama’s paw and tried small hops. Hop, hop, hop! He stayed on the soft rug and kept his tail away from the table. “I’m watching. I’m safe,” he said proudly.

They took a water sip break. Benny’s cup felt cool in his paws. “Cold,” he giggled, feeling the chilly touch on his lips.

“Listening to our bodies keeps us safe too,” Mama said. “Are you warm? Do you need a rest?”

Benny shook his head with a smile. “I’m ready!”

A gentle, floaty song began. “This is a dance from China,” Mama said. “Smooth arms, slow steps.”

Benny moved his arms like a calm river. Whoosh, whoosh. He breathed in and breathed out. He felt peaceful.

Then, a cheerful beat with maracas rattled—chi-chi, cha-cha. “This music is from Latin America. Let’s try a little cha-cha.”

Benny swayed his hips and counted softly. “One, two, cha-cha-cha!” He remembered the rule sign. He looked around, gave space to the toy basket, and smiled at Mama. “Safe sways!”

Suddenly, Benny’s tail brushed the low blanket hanging from the couch. The blanket slipped—shhh—and drooped onto the floor.

Benny stopped right away. “Pause,” he said. He pointed. “That blanket can make us trip.”

Mama nodded. “Good eyes, Benny.” Together, they folded the blanket and tucked it in. “You remembered to stop when something didn’t feel safe.”

Benny’s chest filled with proud, warm feelings. “Safety is my dance partner,” he said.

For the last song, they made up their own “Beaver Boogie.” Benny tapped a rhythm on his tiny drum—dum, dum, dum! He and Mama danced in a circle, keeping their steps light and their space clear. They laughed and twirled slowly so they wouldn’t get dizzy.

When the music ended, the living room was still neat, and Benny felt calm and happy. He hugged Mama. Her fur smelled like cookies.

“What did we learn today?” Mama asked.

Benny held up the rule sign. “We learned to make space, wear safe socks, look where we step, stop if it’s wobbly, and drink water.” He added softly, “We also learned that people around the world dance in different ways. That makes the world special.”

Mama smiled. “And being safe helps us enjoy it all.”

Benny wiggled his tail. “I can dance, I can learn, and I can keep us safe!”

They turned off the music and put the drum away. The playful living room felt extra cozy now, like a hug.

“Tomorrow,” Benny said with a yawn, “maybe we’ll visit more dances.”

“Tomorrow,” Mama agreed, tucking the rule sign on the wall, “we’ll be safe and ready.”

Benny closed his eyes, hearing the soft echo of clap, stomp, and whoosh in his mind. He felt proud, careful, and kind.

The End.
