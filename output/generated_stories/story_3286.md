# Sunny Sings and Finds

Sunlight made warm squares on the carpet of the comfortable nursery. Soft pillows lay in a cozy reading corner. A tiny breeze smelled like clean blankets and crayons. In the window, a little singing bird named Sunny fluffed her yellow feathers and sang, “Tweet-a-leet! Good morning!”

Sunny loved to sing. She liked how her song made the room feel sparkly. Today, she chirped a little tune and hopped from the window to the crib rail, then to the shelf. On the shelf sat a shiny blue box with a red ribbon.

“Ooh,” Sunny whispered, tilting her head. “What is that? I want to find out!”

She pecked the ribbon gently. The bow wiggled. “Hello, ribbon,” Sunny giggled. “May I see what you’re hiding?”

No one answered, of course. Sunny took a tiny breath. “Tweet!” She tugged, and the ribbon slid free with a soft shhhh sound. The box lid lifted. Inside were colorful stickers, a small mirror, and a tiny comb.

Sunny’s eyes went wide. “A discovery!” she sang. “I found special things.”

She looked into the mirror and saw a little bird with fluffy feathers, bright eyes, and a happy beak. “Hello, Sunny,” she told herself. “You look chirpy today.”

Sunny touched the comb. “Is this for me?” She paused, thinking of her friend Momo the stuffed monkey who sat by the pillows. “Hmm. Maybe I should ask.”

She flew down and nudged Momo. “Momo, may I try this comb? I want to care for my feathers.” Momo smiled with his stitched mouth, and Sunny imagined him saying, “Yes, but be gentle.”

“Deal,” Sunny chirped. “I will be gentle, and I’ll put it back.”

Sunny combed her feathers slowly. Swipe, swipe. It tickled! She hummed a quiet song. “Self-care time, tweet-a-leet.” Her feathers felt smooth. Her body felt calm.

Just then, the nursery door opened. Nurse Nana, with soft slippers and a kind smile, stepped in. She smelled like warm soap and orange slices. “Good morning, Sunny,” she said in a whispery voice. “What lovely singing.”

Sunny puffed up, a little shy. “Good morning, Nana. I made a discovery,” she said. “I found the blue box.”

Nurse Nana nodded. “I see. Those are our care things. We use them to help ourselves feel good and clean.”

Sunny hopped closer. “May I use them? I want to care for me. I also want to sing a new song.”

Nurse Nana knelt, and her bracelet jingled softly. “Let’s make a plan,” she said. “You may use the comb and a sticker now. After we clean up, we can open the window a little so you can sing your new song to the plants.”

Sunny tilted her head. “A plan is a deal?”

“It’s a kind deal,” said Nana. “That’s called negotiation. We talk and choose together.”

Sunny smiled. “I agree. Comb, one sticker, then clean up, then singing to the plants.”

“Perfect,” Nana said.

Sunny combed one more time. “Swipe, swipe.” She chose a star sticker. It felt smooth and cool on her feather. “I feel special,” Sunny chirped.

Nana handed Sunny a tiny cup of water. “Sips help your voice,” she said.

Sunny took a tiny sip. The water felt cool and fresh. “Ahh. My throat feels happy,” she said. “Self-care helps singing!”

Nana opened the window a little. A soft breeze brought in a leaf smell and a tiny faraway car sound. The plant on the windowsill wiggled, like it was waving.

Sunny hopped to the sill. “Hello, plant. I made a discovery,” she sang. “I learned that asking is kind. I learned to care for me. I learned to make a plan.”

She made up a new song. It went like this:

“Find and see, gently be,
Ask and share, take care of me.
Talk it out, sing it through,
I learn each day, and so can you!”

Her voice sounded clear and bright. Nana swayed. Momo watched, still and happy. The nursery felt full of soft humming and sunshine.

After the song, Sunny put the comb back in the box. She pressed the lid down and wrapped the ribbon loosely. “All tidy,” she said.

Nana clapped softly. “Sunny, you discovered new things and took care of yourself. You also made a fair deal. How do you feel?”

Sunny fluffed her feathers and touched her star sticker. “I feel proud,” she said. “My body feels calm. My heart feels warm. And my song feels brave.”

Nana smiled. “Those are wonderful feelings.”

Sunny hopped to the cozy corner and nestled by Momo. She closed her eyes for a moment and took three slow breaths. In, out. In, out. In, out. The room smelled like crayons and sunshine. The blanket felt soft on her feet.

She opened her eyes and whispered, “Tomorrow, I will discover something new again. I will ask. I will care. I will make a plan.”

Nana nodded. “That’s a beautiful idea.”

Sunny gave one last gentle tweet. “Discovery makes my day bright.”

The End.
