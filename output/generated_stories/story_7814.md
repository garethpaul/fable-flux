# Buzzy the Beaver’s Laundry Plan

Buzzy the Beaver had a shiny orange hat and a tail that went thump-thump when he was excited. Today, he stood in the laundry room. The room was warm and hum-hummy. The washing machine hummed, “Whirr, whirr,” and the dryer went, “Tum-tum-tumble.” The air smelled like soap bubbles—fresh and clean.

Buzzy wiggled his whiskers. “I want clean clothes for the week,” he said. “But there is so much to do! It feels big.” A little knot of worry sat in his tummy.

On the floor sat a mountain of clothes. Socks peeked out like little bunnies. Shirts flopped like sleepy birds. Towels made a soft, fluffy hill.

Buzzy took a deep breath. “I am a busy beaver,” he said. “I can make a plan.” He smiled. His tail went thump-thump.

He grabbed a big sheet of paper and a crayon. “Step one, sort the clothes,” he said. He drew three boxes and labeled them with big letters he could read: DARKS, LIGHTS, TOWELS. He clapped. “I made a goal: three baskets sorted!”

Buzzy sang as he worked. “Socks together, shirts together, towels fluffy, light as feathers.” He held the dark clothes to one side and the light ones to the other. He made a special pile for towels. The clothes whispered, “Shhh,” as they slid into the baskets. The soft pile made his paws feel cozy.

He stopped and looked at his paper. “Goal check,” he said. “Did I sort?” He peeked into the baskets. “Yes! I did it. One goal done.” His smile grew bigger.

Next, he pointed to the washing machine. “Step two, wash the first load,” he said. He chose the DARKS basket. “Only to the line,” he told himself. “Not too full.” He poured a tiny cup of soap. The soap smelled like lemon clouds. “Glug, glug,” went the bottle. He pushed the button. “Beep!” said the washer. “Whirr, whirr,” it sang.

Buzzy wiggled in happy circles. “While we wait, we plan the next step,” he said. He drew a little clock on his paper. “When the washer stops, I will move the clothes to the dryer.” He drew a square and wrote DRY. “Then I will fold,” he said, drawing folded squares. “One step at a time.”

His neighbor, Dot the Dust Bunny, peeked from under a shelf. “Hi, Buzzy,” Dot squeaked. “What are you doing?”

“I am doing chores with a plan,” Buzzy said. “I made a goal. I follow my steps. It helps me feel calm.”

Dot twitched her fuzzy ears. “Can I help?” she asked.

“Yes!” Buzzy said. “You can be my timer friend. When the humming stops, tell me.”

Dot nodded. “I will listen with my big ears.”

The washer hummed and swished. Water splashed: “Swish, swish.” Buzzy used the waiting time to tidy the soap shelf. He lined up bottles—big, medium, small. He wiped a spill. “Wipe, wipe,” went the rag. It smelled like fresh rain.

“Stop!” Dot squeaked. “The hum is done!”

Buzzy checked his plan. “Move clothes,” he read. He opened the washer. Warm steam whooshed out. “Ahh, cozy!” He lifted the wet clothes. “Plop, plop,” they fell into the dryer. He turned the knob. “Click!” The dryer tumbled. “Tum-tum-tumble,” it sang.

“Next load,” Buzzy said. He washed the LIGHTS. Then he washed the TOWELS. He checked each box on his paper. Check, check, check. Each check made his heart feel proud and steady.

Soon, the dryer beeped. “Beep-beep!”

“Folding time,” Buzzy said. He made a folding space on a clean table. The table felt cool and smooth under his paws. He folded shirts into neat little squares. “Fold, smooth, pat,” he said softly. He matched socks like friends holding hands. “You go together,” he told them. He rolled towels into cozy logs. “Good night, towels,” he whispered with a giggle.

Dot hopped closer. “This is fun,” she said.

“It is,” Buzzy agreed. “Chores can be fun when we plan and play.”

Soon, the mountain of clothes was gone. In its place stood tidy stacks—shirts, pants, socks, and fluffy towels. The laundry room looked bright and clear. The air smelled like sunshine on lemons.

Buzzy looked at his paper. The last box said PUT AWAY. He lifted one stack at a time. “Light and steady,” he said. He put shirts in the drawer. He put socks in the basket. He hung towels on a hook. “All done,” he whispered.

He drew a big star at the bottom of the page and wrote, GOAL DONE! His tail thumped a happy drum. “I set a goal. I made a plan. I did my chores,” he said. “I feel proud.”

Dot wiggled with joy. “What did you learn, Buzzy?”

Buzzy smiled. “Big jobs feel small when we make a plan. One step, then the next. We can do it,” he said. “That is goal-setting!”

Dot clapped her tiny paws. “I will make plans too!”

Buzzy hung his paper on the wall. The star sparkled in the warm light. “Tomorrow, I will plan snack time,” he said. “Today, I did laundry with a plan.”

He took a deep, happy breath. The room hummed softly, like a bedtime song. “Good work,” he told himself. “I can do hard things, one little step at a time.”

The End.
