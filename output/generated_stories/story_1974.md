# Polly's Helping Wings

In a cozy dining room with yellow walls, a bright green parrot named Polly perched on her favorite chair. The morning sun streamed through the big window, making her colorful feathers shine like a rainbow. Polly loved to watch the world outside while her family ate breakfast.

"Good morning, Polly!" said little Jake as he sat down for cereal. The spoon clinked against his bowl. Polly bobbed her head and squawked happily.

Through the window, Polly saw something that made her feathers ruffle with worry. Old Mrs. Chen from next door was in her backyard, trying to build a birdhouse. She had wooden pieces scattered on the grass, but they kept falling over. Mrs. Chen looked tired and confused.

"Help! Help!" Polly squawked, flapping her wings. She wanted to tell Jake's family about Mrs. Chen, but they were busy getting ready for work and school.

Polly thought hard. Her bright black eyes sparkled with an idea. She could help Mrs. Chen herself! Polly was very good at building things. She often helped Jake stack blocks and sort his toys.

"I'll be back!" Polly called out, though no one understood her parrot words. She flew out through the open dining room door and soared into the sunny backyard.

Mrs. Chen looked up in surprise as the colorful parrot landed near her pile of wood. "Hello there, pretty bird," she said softly. Her voice sounded tired.

Polly picked up a small nail in her strong beak and placed it exactly where it needed to go. Then she used her clever feet to hold a piece of wood steady. Mrs. Chen's eyes grew wide with wonder.

"Oh my! You want to help me build this birdhouse?" Mrs. Chen asked with a gentle smile.

"Yes! Yes!" Polly squawked, even though it sounded like parrot chatter to Mrs. Chen.

Working together felt wonderful. Polly held pieces while Mrs. Chen hammered. The sound went "tap, tap, tap" in a happy rhythm. When Mrs. Chen dropped screws in the soft grass, Polly used her sharp eyes to find them quickly.

"You're such a good helper," Mrs. Chen said, patting Polly's soft green head. "In my home country, we always believed that helping others brings good luck to everyone."

As they worked, other neighbors came outside. They saw Mrs. Chen and the helpful parrot building together. Soon, Mr. Garcia brought his drill. Little Sarah carried over some paint. Even grumpy Mr. Thompson offered to help hang the finished birdhouse.

The warm sun felt good on everyone's backs. The smell of fresh wood mixed with blooming flowers. Laughter filled the air as neighbors shared stories and worked side by side.

By lunchtime, they had built the most beautiful birdhouse anyone had ever seen. It was painted bright blue with yellow trim, just like a tiny house for bird families.

"This is wonderful!" Mrs. Chen clapped her hands. "Polly, you started something magical. When we help each other, amazing things happen."

Polly felt proud and happy inside. Her chest puffed out with joy. She had learned that helping others doesn't just make them feel good - it brings the whole neighborhood together.

Jake's family came outside to see what all the excitement was about. "Polly!" Jake called. "You helped build that beautiful birdhouse!"

"Help! Help!" Polly squawked proudly, spreading her wings wide.

From that day on, whenever someone in the neighborhood needed help, Polly was always ready to lend a wing. And Mrs. Chen's birdhouse became home to a family of cheerful blue birds who sang thank-you songs every morning.

The End.
