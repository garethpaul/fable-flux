# Joey Roo’s Task-Tidy Adventure

In a cozy bedroom with soft, sunny curtains, a joyful kangaroo joey named Joey Roo bounced on his bed. His blanket felt warm and fuzzy. His pillow smelled like clean soap. Sunlight made dancing dots on the wall.

“Boing! Boing!” Joey Roo giggled. He held a little list on bright yellow paper. “Mama, I have so many jobs today! It’s a Task-Tidy Day.”

Mama Roo peeked in with a smile and a gentle swish of her tail. “Good morning, Joey Roo. Let’s look at your list.”

Joey read the simple words with Mama. “One: Make my bed. Two: Pick up toys. Three: Sort books. Four: Pack care bag for the community drive.” He traced the last line with his finger. “And five: Tell the truth, even when it’s hard.”

Mama nodded. “Those are good tasks. Doing tasks helps you grow strong and kind. And the care bag will help neighbors. That’s community service.”

Joey Roo felt proud. “I can do it! I can do it!” He bounced once more, then stopped. “Wait. Should I start with the fun part?”

Mama Roo smiled. “Start at the top. One task at a time. Little hops make big journeys.”

Joey Roo took a deep breath. He patted his fuzzy blanket smooth. “Task one: Make my bed.” He tugged the corners. Swish, swish. He fluffed his pillow. “Poof!” The pillow made a soft sound and a little puff of powdery smell. He grinned. “Done!”

“Great job,” said Mama Roo.

Joey Roo looked around. Toys were everywhere. Blocks, cars, and a tiny drum. “Task two: Pick up toys.” He made a song to help. “Pick and plop, pick and plop, toys go home with a happy hop!” He picked up a red car. Click! Into the bin. He picked up blocks. Clack-clack! Into the box. He tapped the tiny drum. Dum-dum-dum! Then he put it on the shelf. The room felt calmer, like a gentle hum.

“Two tasks done,” Joey Roo whispered. He peeked at his books. They were piled like a crooked tower. “Task three: Sort books.” He ran his paw along the covers. Some were smooth, some were bumpy. He sniffed a page. It smelled like paper and a little bit like sunshine. “Big books on the bottom, little books on top,” he said. He made a neat line and felt a small happy spark in his chest.

“Three tasks!” he cheered. “I’m skill-building! I’m getting better!”

Mama Roo clapped softly. “You are learning, Joey Roo.”

Now came the care bag. Joey Roo got a soft blue backpack. “Task four: Pack care bag.” He looked at his toys and books, then at a basket for the community drive.

“Mama, what should we pack?” he asked.

“We can share a warm scarf, a spare toothbrush, and a book you’ve outgrown,” Mama said. “This helps our neighbors nearby and far away. We are global citizens. We care for the world.”

Joey Roo held a small bear with one ear slightly bent. “What about Mr. Hops?” He hugged it. The fur felt cozy against his cheek. He loved Mr. Hops.

Mama Roo knelt. “You choose. It must be honest sharing. Keep what you need. Give what you truly want to give.”

Joey Roo looked at the bear. His tummy felt wiggly. He remembered the last task: tell the truth, even when it’s hard. “I want to help,” he said softly. “But I still need Mr. Hops at bedtime. I can share the red car and the warm scarf. And this bouncy ball too.” He put them in the bag. Zip! “That is my honest share.”

Mama Roo hugged him. “Thank you for being honest. Honest giving feels good.”

Joey Roo smiled. “Task four: Done!”

He checked the list again. “Task five: Tell the truth, even when it’s hard.” He thought and thought. Then his ears drooped. “Mama, I need to tell you something. I almost hid my toys under the bed, not in the bin. I wanted to be done fast. But that is not finishing the task.”

Mama Roo looked under the bed. Dust bunnies wiggled like tiny clouds. “Thank you for telling me the truth,” she said. “Would you like to finish it the right way together?”

“Yes, please,” Joey Roo said. They pulled out two tops, one sock, and a rolling marble. Roll, roll, roll—click! Into the bin. “Now the task is complete,” Joey Roo said, feeling light and proud.

They stood in the cozy bedroom. It looked neat and calm. The sun felt warm on Joey Roo’s fur. The air smelled like fresh sheets. Joey Roo’s heart felt big and brave.

“Mama,” he said, “little hops make big journeys. I did my tasks!”

“Yes,” said Mama. “You made your bed, picked up toys, sorted books, packed a care bag, and told the truth. You built skills, helped others, and finished what you started.”

Joey Roo bounced once, softly. “I can help our neighbors. I can help the world. I can finish my tasks, one by one.”

Mama Roo gave him the list. Joey Roo drew a big smiley face at the bottom. “Tasks complete!” he cheered.

They carried the care bag to the door. The zipper made a friendly zzzip. Joey Roo felt ready and strong.

He whispered, “Pick and plop, pick and plop, little hops won’t ever stop.”

Mama Roo winked. “And that is how we grow.”

The End.
