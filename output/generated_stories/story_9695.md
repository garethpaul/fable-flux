# Mango's Balancing Day

Mango the monkey woke up in his cozy treehouse. The morning sun peeked through the green leaves. His treehouse swayed gently in the warm breeze.

"What a beautiful day!" Mango stretched his arms wide. But when he tried to stand up, something felt different. His legs felt wobbly like jelly.

Mango looked down at his hands and feet. "Oh my! I forgot to practice my coordination yesterday," he said. "Now I feel all mixed up!"

Mango tried to walk across his treehouse floor. Step, wobble, step, wobble. His arms swung too much. His tail got tangled around his legs.

"This won't do," Mango said firmly. "I need to take care of my body and practice moving smoothly."

Mango remembered what his grandmother taught him. "Start slow and steady," he whispered to himself.

First, Mango sat down and touched his toes. One, two, three times. His muscles felt warm and stretchy. The wooden floor felt smooth under his bottom.

Next, Mango stood up very slowly. He placed one foot carefully in front of the other. Left foot, right foot, left foot, right foot. His balance got better with each step.

"Much better!" Mango smiled. His confidence grew like a flower opening in the sunshine.

Mango decided to practice his favorite coordination game. He picked up three soft, yellow bananas from his fruit basket. The bananas smelled sweet and felt smooth in his hands.

"Time to juggle!" Mango threw one banana up in the air. He caught it with both hands. Then he tried two bananas. Up, catch, up, catch. His eyes followed the bananas carefully.

Just then, his friend Chirpy the bird flew to the treehouse window. Her bright blue feathers sparkled in the sunlight.

"Wow, Mango! You're getting so good at that!" Chirpy chirped happily.

"Thank you!" Mango beamed. "I'm practicing my coordination. It helps my body work together like a team."

"Can you teach me?" asked Chirpy. "I want to learn too!"

Mango felt proud and excited. "Of course! Sharing knowledge makes it even more fun."

Mango showed Chirpy how to start small. "First, try hopping on one foot," he demonstrated. Hop, hop, hop on his left foot. Then hop, hop, hop on his right foot.

Chirpy tried hopping on one tiny bird foot. She giggled as she wobbled. "This is tricky but fun!"

"Now let's try walking in a straight line," Mango suggested. He walked carefully along a wooden beam. His arms stretched out for balance. The beam felt solid under his feet.

Chirpy flew alongside him, practicing smooth, straight flying. Her wings moved in perfect rhythm.

"You're doing great!" Mango encouraged. "Coordination takes practice, just like learning to fly or climb."

They practiced together all morning. Mango taught Chirpy how to stretch her wings properly. Chirpy showed Mango how to move his arms like wings for better balance.

As the sun reached high in the sky, both friends felt proud of their progress. Their bodies felt strong and coordinated.

"Thank you for teaching me," Chirpy said. "Taking care of our bodies is important!"

"And sharing what we know makes us both better," Mango agreed. He felt happy and accomplished.

Mango looked at his hands and feet again. They felt steady and strong. His whole body worked together like instruments in a band.

"Every day is a chance to practice and grow," Mango said wisely. "When we take care of ourselves and help our friends, we all become stronger."

From that day on, Mango and Chirpy practiced coordination together every morning. Their treehouse filled with laughter, learning, and friendship.

The End.
