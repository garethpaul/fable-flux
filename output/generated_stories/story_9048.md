# Polly's World Adventure Room

Polly the parrot loved bright colors and new adventures. Her feathers were rainbow-colored - red, blue, green, and yellow. Today, she was staying in Grandma's cozy guest room while her family visited from far away.

The guest room had something very special - a big world map on the wall! Polly flapped her wings excitedly as she looked at all the different countries painted in pretty colors.

"Squawk! Look at all these places!" Polly chirped. She could see blue oceans, green forests, and sandy deserts. "I wonder what children do in these faraway lands."

Polly noticed the room was a bit messy. There were towels on the floor and soap by the sink. She remembered what Mama always said: "A clean space makes everyone feel welcome."

"I want to be a good guest," Polly said to herself. She picked up the soft white towels with her beak and hung them neatly on the rack. The soap went back in its special dish.

As Polly cleaned, she imagined children around the world. "In Japan, children probably fold their clothes neatly," she thought, smoothing the bedsheets with her colorful wings.

She looked at Africa on the map. "Children there might help their families get clean water." Polly filled a cup with fresh water and placed it by the window for any thirsty birds outside.

"Squawk! In Mexico, children might help wash dishes after dinner!" Polly carefully wiped the sink until it sparkled like a mirror.

Suddenly, Polly heard voices downstairs. Her cousin Roberto had arrived! He was visiting from Spain and spoke both Spanish and English.

"¡Hola, Polly!" Roberto called as he flew into the guest room. His feathers were bright green and orange.

"Hello, Roberto!" Polly replied happily. "Look, I made our room clean and welcoming, just like children do all around the world!"

Roberto's eyes grew wide as he saw the tidy room and the world map. "¡Qué bueno! That's wonderful! In Spain, my friends and I always clean our classroom together."

"Really?" Polly asked, her head tilting curiously.

"Sí! Yes! When we work together and keep things clean, everyone feels happy and healthy," Roberto explained. "It doesn't matter what language we speak or where we come from."

Polly felt warm and proud inside. "So being clean and helpful connects us to children everywhere?"

"Exactly!" Roberto squawked joyfully. "Good hygiene and helping others makes the whole world a better place."

Together, the two parrots looked at the big world map. They pointed their wings at different countries and imagined children brushing their teeth, washing their hands, and helping their families.

"Tomorrow, let's teach the other birds how to keep their nests clean," Polly suggested.

"¡Perfecto! Perfect!" Roberto agreed. "We can show them that taking care of ourselves and our spaces is something all birds can do together."

That night, as Polly snuggled in the clean, cozy guest room, she felt connected to children all around the colorful world. She had learned that being independent and keeping clean wasn't just good for her - it was a way to show kindness to everyone she met.

"Good night, world," Polly whispered softly, looking at the map one more time. "Tomorrow I'll be an even better global citizen."

The End.
