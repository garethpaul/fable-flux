# The Fair Cookie Game

In a warm kitchen, a gentle deer fawn named Della stood on a soft rug. The rug felt fuzzy under her little hooves. The kitchen smelled like cinnamon and sugar. The oven hummed a happy song. Della’s ears wiggled. Her nose twitched.

“Mama, are the cookies ready?” Della asked.

“Almost,” said Mama Deer, smiling. “They need one more minute.”

Della peeked through the oven window. “They look round and golden,” she whispered. “They look like sunny coins!”

Ding! The timer sang. Mama Deer took the tray out. Steam rose up. The cookies crackled softly. Della breathed in. “Mmm, warm and sweet,” she said.

Mama set the cookies on a cooling rack. “We will share,” she said. “We must be fair.”

Della nodded. “Fair means everyone gets the same,” she said.

Mama Deer poured two cups of milk with tiny bubbles on top. “Let’s play the Fair Cookie Game,” she said. “You imagine the rules.”

Della clapped her small hooves. She liked to imagine. She closed her eyes. “I imagine the table is a ship,” Della said. “The cookies are treasure. We must share the treasure.”

Mama Deer smiled. “Good. What is the problem?”

Della counted. “One, two, three, four, five cookies,” she said. “But there are two of us. Five is an odd number. Someone gets more.” Della’s ears drooped. “That is not fair.”

Mama Deer nodded. “How can we solve it?”

Della thought. She imagined the cookies as sun coins. She imagined a friendly cookie dragon guarding them. “Dragon says the treasure must be shared,” Della said. “How do we share five?”

She tried an idea. She put two cookies by Mama. She put two by herself. One cookie sat alone. “The lonely cookie,” she said softly. “It feels left out.”

Della frowned. Her tummy felt tight. She wanted to be fair. She wanted to be kind. “Mama, I want to improve my idea,” Della said. “I will try again.”

She thought of slicing the last cookie. But the cookies were warm and soft. They might crumble. She imagined the table ship rocking on sweet milk waves.

Then Della had a sparkly thought. “We can use turns!” she said. “We can take turns with the last cookie. We share it with time.”

“How will that work?” asked Mama.

Della lifted her chin. “First, we let the cookie cool,” she said. “Then we break it in half.” She smiled. “Fair is not always the same. Fair can mean everyone feels cared for.”

Mama Deer nodded. “That sounds fair and kind.”

Carefully, Della touched the last cookie. It was warm like sunshine on a leaf. “It smells like hugs,” she said. She broke it gently. Crack. Two halves. Crumbs sparkled like tiny stars.

Della put one half near Mama and one half near herself. “Now it is fair,” she said. “Two cookies for you, two cookies for me, and a half for you, and a half for me.”

Mama Deer’s eyes shone. “You used your imagination to find a fair way,” she said. “You improved your plan when the first one did not work.”

Della giggled. “Thank you, Friendly Cookie Dragon,” she whispered to her pretend friend. She sipped her milk. It was cool and creamy. The bubbles tickled her nose. She ate a cookie. It was soft inside and a little crunchy outside. “Mmm,” she said. “It tastes like a cozy day.”

They munched and smiled. Crumbs danced on the plate. Della licked a crumb from her nose. “I like this game,” she said. “Can we play again?”

Mama Deer pointed to a bowl of fruit. “Here is a new problem,” she said. “Three strawberries and one banana. How can we be fair?”

Della tapped her hoof. Tap, tap. She imagined the strawberries wearing tiny hats. She imagined the banana as a yellow moon boat. “We can make a fruit parade,” she said. “We slice the banana into little moons. We give each of us one strawberry and some banana moons. Then we share the last strawberry by taking turns.”

Mama Deer clapped. “You are getting better and better,” she said. “You are improving.”

Della felt proud. Her chest felt warm, like the oven. “Imagination helps me,” she said. “It shows me new ways.”

They made a fruit parade across the plates. Red, yellow, red. They shared and laughed. Della patted the table ship. “Thank you, ship,” she said. “You help us be fair.”

When the snack was done, Della cleaned up with a small cloth. She wiped the crumbs. She put the cups in the sink. “I can help,” she said. “Helping is fair, too.”

Mama Deer gave Della a gentle nuzzle. “Today you learned three big things,” she said. “Use your imagination, be fair, and keep improving.”

Della smiled. Her ears stood tall. “I can do that,” she said. “I can imagine kind ideas. I can share. I can try again.”

The warm kitchen hummed. The oven cooled with a soft sigh. The rug felt cozy on Della’s hooves. Della looked at the empty plates and felt full in her heart.

“Tomorrow,” she said, “we can play again.”

“Yes,” Mama Deer said. “And you will imagine even more.”

The End.
