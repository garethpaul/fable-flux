# Joey’s Pantry Art Party

Joey the kangaroo joey woke up with a bounce. His tail thumped softly on the floor. “Today feels like an art day!” he said, his eyes bright.

He hopped into the pantry. The pantry smelled like warm oats and sweet raisins. Shelves were full of boxes, jars, and shiny tins. The light was soft and golden. Crinkly paper bags whispered when he brushed past them.

“Mama?” Joey called. “Can I make art?”

Mama Roo peeked in with a smile. “Art is wonderful, Joey. Let’s plan first.”

Joey nodded. “Plan, then art. Okay!” He clapped his little paws.

Mama Roo got a small chalkboard and a stub of blue chalk. “What do we need?” she asked.

Joey thought. “We need safe things. We need clean things. We need colors!”

“Good plan,” Mama Roo said. “We can use recycling. We can make art and help the Earth.”

Joey looked around. He saw empty cereal boxes, a paper egg carton, a cardboard tube, and two clean yogurt cups. He touched the cardboard. It felt smooth and a little bumpy. He tapped the cups. “Tup, tup!”

“May I use these?” Joey asked.

Mama Roo nodded. “Yes, these are clean and ready. And we will put back what we do not use. That is honest and kind.”

Joey hopped in place. “I will be honest. I will ask first. I will put back things.”

They laid a big newspaper on the pantry floor. It felt cool and crackly under Joey’s toes. Mama Roo gave Joey a small smock. It was soft and smelled like soap.

“We need a plan for our picture,” Mama Roo said. “What will you make?”

Joey closed his eyes. He saw colors in his mind. Swirls, dots, and a big happy sun. “I will make a Pantry Friends collage!” he said. “With shapes and colors and all our snacks.”

Mama Roo laughed. “Without the real snacks, dear. But we can make pretend snack shapes.”

Joey giggled. “Yes! Pretend snacks!”

Together they drew a simple plan on the chalkboard: 1) Gather. 2) Sort. 3) Glue. 4) Share.

Joey gathered. He heard the soft swish of paper bags and the gentle clink of jars. He found the cardboard tube and held it to his eye. “I spy a cookie!” he said.

Mama Roo shook her head with a smile. “First we sort,” she reminded him.

They sorted by shapes and textures. Joey put flat pieces in one pile. He put round pieces in another. He rubbed a strip of cardboard. “It is ridgy,” he said. “It tickles my paw.”

Next came glue. The glue smelled a little sweet. Joey squeezed a tiny dot on a cereal box square. “Plop!” He pressed it to a big paper. “Squish!” He added a yogurt cup lid for a moon. He cut (with Mama’s help) the egg carton into bumpy hills.

As they worked, friends came to the pantry door. A small mouse with tiny whiskers peeped in. “Squeak! Can I help?”

Joey smiled. “Yes! This is a Pantry Friends collage. We share.”

A colorful beetle crawled in, its shell shiny green and blue. “Click-click,” it said softly. A robin hopped to the shelf, singing a bright note. Joey’s heart felt warm. “We all look different,” he said. “Different is good.”

Mama Roo nodded. “Diversity makes our art beautiful.”

Joey gave the mouse soft paper bits. He gave the beetle a shiny foil piece from a clean chocolate wrap. He handed the robin a red ribbon from an old bag. “We are using recycling,” Joey told them. “We are kind to the Earth.”

They glued and pressed. Joey made a big orange sun with cereal box circles. The mouse made tiny crumb-paint dots using a dry brush and a little spice. “It smells like cinnamon!” Joey said, wiggling his nose. Warm and sweet filled the air.

The beetle placed the shiny foil in little stripes. “It looks like water,” Joey said. The robin tucked the red ribbon around the moon. “It looks like a hug,” Joey whispered.

Joey saw a bright jar of sprinkles on the shelf. His eyes went wide. He wanted to use them. They were so pretty. He reached out, then stopped. He put his paw back.

“Mama,” he said softly, “I did not ask about sprinkles. I wanted to take them. But I will be honest. May I use them?”

Mama Roo smiled and shook her head. “Those are for baking. Thank you for telling the truth. Let’s find a safe, okay sparkle.”

Joey felt proud. “Honesty feels good,” he said.

Mama Roo found a scrap of shiny paper. “This can be our sparkle.”

They made tiny stars from the paper. Snip, snip. Joey glued them on. “Twinkle!” he said.

At last the collage was done. Joey sat back and looked. The pantry smelled like cinnamon and paper and glue. The art felt bumpy and smooth. It showed a happy sun, a silver river, a ribbon moon hug, and tiny spice dots.

Joey clapped. “We planned. We used recycling. We made art together. We told the truth.”

Mama Roo hugged him. “Yes. Art can be a plan, a promise, and a party.”

The mouse squeaked, the beetle clicked, and the robin sang. Joey bowed. “Thank you, friends. Different friends make bright art.”

Mama Roo hung the collage on the pantry door with soft tape. It fluttered when the cool air moved. Joey read the chalkboard again. Gather. Sort. Glue. Share.

He smiled. “I can do this again. I can plan my art. I can use old things in new ways. I can be honest.”

Mama Roo kissed his forehead. “You are an artist, Joey.”

Joey’s tail thumped, thump, thump, a happy beat in the warm pantry.

The End.
