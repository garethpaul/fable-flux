# Sunny Sticks and Friendly Fixes

In a sunny backyard, a resourceful squirrel named Sprig woke up on a warm, bright morning. The grass sparkled with tiny drops of dew. The air smelled like sweet flowers and fresh leaves. Birds chirped, “Tweet-tweet!” and a gentle breeze said, “Whoosh,” through the tall sunflowers.

Sprig stretched his little paws. “Today, I will build a cozy bridge,” he said. His fluffy tail flicked with excitement. “Then I can carry acorns over the tiny creek without getting my toes wet!”

Sprig gathered sticks. Tap, tap, tap went his paws on the soft ground. He found smooth sticks, bumpy sticks, and a long, strong stick. He hummed a happy tune, “Build, build, build,” as he placed the sticks near the creek.

Just then, a blue jay named Jingle flew down. “Caw! Those are shiny sticks,” Jingle said, tilting his head. “I want some for my nest.”

Sprig held a stick and smiled. “Hello, Jingle! I am building a bridge. I need these sticks. The creek is splashy and cold.”

Jingle puffed his feathers. “But I want them. They look perfect.”

Sprig took a slow, calm breath. He remembered: respect means listening and using kind words. “Jingle,” Sprig said softly, “I hear you. Your nest is important. My bridge is important too. Maybe we can share.”

Jingle blinked. “Share? How?”

Sprig wiggled his whiskers. “Let’s look together. We can pick sticks for your nest and sticks for my bridge. We can choose fair and kind.”

Jingle looked at the pile. He shuffled his feet. “Okay. We can try.”

They sat by the creek. The water whispered, “Swish-swish,” over round stones. The sun warmed their backs. Sprig touched each stick. “This long, strong stick can be the bottom of the bridge,” he said. “It will carry weight.”

Jingle pecked gently at a soft, bendy twig. “This one feels cozy for a nest,” he chirped.

Sprig nodded. “Good choice. How about we take turns picking? You pick one, then I pick one.”

Jingle smiled. “Yes. That sounds fair.”

They took turns. Jingle chose a twig. Sprig chose a sturdy stick. They made two neat piles—one for nest building, one for bridge building. When they found a curly stick, they both paused.

Jingle said, “I like that curly one.”

Sprig said, “I like it too.”

Sprig thought. His tail made a question mark. “What if we use it in both? You can bend a little piece for your nest, and I can use a little piece on my bridge rail.”

Jingle tapped his beak. “We can break it gently and share.”

Sprig placed the curly stick on a flat rock. “We can do it together,” he said. “Slow and careful.”

“Slow and careful,” echoed Jingle.

Snap! The stick made two neat pieces. They each took one. “Thank you,” said Jingle.

“Thank you,” said Sprig. His heart felt warm. Respect feels good.

Just then, a bunny named Dottie hopped over. “Boing, boing! What are you building?” Her nose wiggled. “I smell fresh wood.”

“I’m building a bridge,” said Sprig. “And Jingle is building a nest. We are sharing and taking turns.”

Dottie’s ears stood tall. “Can I help?”

Sprig nodded. “Yes, please. We can all help. But first, let’s talk. We should listen to each other.”

Dottie sat and listened. Sprig shared his plan. Jingle shared his needs. Dottie said, “I can hold sticks with my paws.”

They began to build. Sprig laid the long, strong stick across the creek. Dottie held one end steady. Jingle placed small twigs like tiny rails. The creek went, “Plip-plip,” as water touched the stones.

Sprig said, “I will test it.” He took a careful step. The bridge felt firm. “One paw. Two paws.” He crossed to the other side and waved. “It works!”

Jingle flapped his wings. “Now I will add twigs to my nest.” He flew to a low branch and tucked the soft, bendy twigs inside. “Cozy!”

Dottie clapped her paws. “We built and we shared. And no one got toes wet!”

A squirrel from the fence, old Mr. Whisk, peeked over. “I watched you,” he said in a gentle, rusty voice. “You used kind words. You listened. You made a plan. That is respect.”

Sprig’s cheeks felt proud. “We talked. We took turns. We found a fair way.”

Jingle nodded. “We did not grab. We did not shout. We asked.”

Dottie smiled. “And we built something good.”

The sun felt warm on their fur and feathers. The air smelled like sweet grass. Sprig carried acorns across the little bridge. They made a soft “clack-clack” in his paws. Jingle tucked the last twig into his nest. Dottie splashed one paw in the creek and giggled.

Sprig looked at his friends. “Thank you for helping. Thank you for listening.”

Jingle said, “Thank you for sharing.”

Dottie said, “Thank you for being kind.”

Sprig’s tail swished happily. “When we respect each other, we can build big things, even with small sticks.”

They sat together in the sunny backyard, watching the light dance on the water. The bridge stood strong. The nest looked snug. Their hearts felt full.

And that day, they learned: Respect means listening, taking turns, and finding a fair way together.

The End.
