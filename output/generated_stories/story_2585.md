# Hoot’s Safe and Gentle Words

In a quiet attic, where dust danced in sunbeams and floorboards whispered little creaks, lived a wise old owl named Hoot. Hoot had soft, speckled feathers, big bright eyes, and a warm, gentle voice. He kept a tiny library in the attic: small books, big books, and books with simple pictures. The attic smelled like old paper and pine wood. It felt cozy and safe.

One morning, Hoot fluffed his feathers and said, “Hoo-hoo! Today we learn about safety and kind words.” He placed three shiny buttons on the floor. One was red, one was yellow, and one was green.

Just then, a little mouse named Pip peeked in. Pip had tiny paws, twitchy whiskers, and curious eyes. “Hi, Hoot!” Pip squeaked. “What are those buttons?”

Hoot smiled. “These buttons are our helpers. Red means stop. Yellow means think. Green means go. They will help us be safe and finish our tasks.”

Pip’s nose twitched. “I want to help! What is the task?”

Hoot opened a small box. Inside were soft feathers, little labels, and a roll of string. “We will tidy the attic,” Hoot said. “We will label the boxes and put books on the shelf. We will use safe bodies, safe tools, and safe words.”

Pip clapped his paws. “Yay!”

Hoot pointed to a wobbly ladder. “Problem,” he said gently. “The ladder is shaky. That is not safe.”

Pip frowned. “Oh no.”

Hoot tapped the red button. “Red means stop. We do not climb a shaky ladder.”

Pip nodded. “Stop.”

Hoot looked around. He saw a sturdy stool. He wiggled it. It did not wobble. He tapped the yellow button. “Yellow means think. How can we reach the high shelf safely?”

Pip looked up. He looked down. He took a slow breath. “We can use the sturdy stool. We can ask for help. We can move slowly.”

Hoot tapped the green button. “Green means go. Safe plan. Let us do it.”

They moved the stool close to the shelf. Hoot held the stool steady with his strong wings, and Pip climbed up one step. “One step at a time,” Hoot said. “Slow is safe.”

Pip reached the shelf. He placed three books gently. His paws made soft thumps. “I did it!” Pip cheered.

“Good job,” Hoot said. “You used your plan. You were safe.”

Next, they needed to tie the books with string. Pip grabbed the sharp scissors. “I can cut!” he shouted.

Hoot touched the red button. “Stop. We use gentle words and careful hands.”

Pip blinked. “Gentle words?”

Hoot nodded. “Words can be safe too. Loud, pushy words can rush us. Gentle words help us move slow and smart. Try this: ‘May I please cut the string?’”

Pip took a breath. “May I please cut the string?”

“Yes,” Hoot said. “Now, yellow think. What keeps us safe with scissors?”

Pip looked at the shiny blades. “Point them down. Hold them by the handle. Keep paws away from the tips.”

“Green go,” Hoot hooted softly.

Snip, snip. The sound was small and neat. Pip smiled. “Snip sounds like drizzle!” He giggled and whispered, “Snip, snip, safe and quick.”

Hoot chuckled. “You are enjoying words. Words can be like music. Gentle words help the mind and keep the body safe.”

They moved to the tiny library. Hoot held up labels that said “Big,” “Small,” and “Stories.” Pip traced the letters with a paw. “These letters feel bumpy,” he said.

“They tell us where things go,” Hoot said. “Language helps us understand. Labels help us finish our task.”

Pip placed the labels on the boxes. He spoke each word softly. “Big. Small. Stories.” His voice was calm and careful. The attic felt even cozier.

Suddenly, a window rattled. A breeze blew in and made a book fall with a thud. Pip jumped. “Oh!”

Hoot pressed the yellow button. “Think. What do we do when something surprises us?”

Pip put a paw on his chest. “We stop. We breathe in. We breathe out. We look. We listen.”

“Good,” Hoot said. “Now I will close the window. You stand back at a safe space.” He moved slowly, checked the latch, and closed it with a soft click.

Pip clapped. “Problem solved!”

They looked around the attic. The shelves were neat. The boxes were labeled. The string was tucked away. The scissors were in a safe tin. The room smelled clean and warm.

“We finished our task,” Hoot said. “We used red stop, yellow think, and green go. We used gentle words. We stayed safe.”

Pip’s whiskers danced. “I learned three things! One: Stop when it is not safe. Two: Think of a safe plan. Three: Go slowly and finish the job with kind words.”

Hoot gave a happy hoot. “Yes. Safety first, kind words always, and tasks completed with care.”

Pip curled on a soft feather pillow. “Thank you, Hoot. I like how words can help. They can be soft like a whisper, or quiet like a hug.”

Hoot tucked a small blanket over Pip. “And they can guide us to safe choices. You did very well today.”

The sunbeam stretched, and the attic glowed. Hoot and Pip rested, feeling proud and peaceful in their tidy, safe space.

The End.
