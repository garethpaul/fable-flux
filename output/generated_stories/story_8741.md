# Koko's Reading Routine

In a cozy study corner filled with colorful books and soft cushions, lived a calm koala named Koko. The study corner smelled like vanilla candles and had sunshine streaming through the big window. Koko loved this special place, but there was one big problem.

Every morning, Koko would look at all the books on the shelves and feel scared. "There are so many words," Koko whispered to himself. "What if I can't read them? What if I'm not smart enough?"

Koko's tummy would feel fluttery, like butterflies were dancing inside. He would pick up a book, look at the first page, then put it right back down. "Maybe tomorrow," he would say sadly.

One sunny Tuesday, Mama Koala came into the study corner. She saw Koko sitting on his favorite blue cushion, looking worried.

"What's wrong, my little eucalyptus leaf?" Mama asked gently, sitting down beside him.

"I want to read all these wonderful books," Koko said, pointing to the tall shelves. "But there are so many words! They look like tiny ants marching across the pages."

Mama Koala smiled warmly. "Oh, Koko! Do you know the secret to becoming a great reader?"

Koko's ears perked up. "A secret?"

"Yes! It's called a reading routine. We start with just one small step every day, like climbing a eucalyptus tree one branch at a time."

"Really?" Koko asked, his eyes growing wide and curious.

"Let's try together," Mama said. She picked up a book with bright pictures. "Every morning after breakfast, we'll read just one page. Just one!"

The next morning, Koko felt nervous but excited. After eating his eucalyptus leaves, he walked to his study corner. The soft cushions felt warm and welcoming under his fuzzy bottom.

"One page," he said bravely to himself. "I can do one page."

Koko opened the book. The words looked friendlier somehow. He read slowly: "The... little... bird... flew... high."

"I did it!" Koko cheered, clapping his paws together. The sound echoed happily in the quiet corner.

Day after day, Koko followed his new routine. After breakfast, he would waddle to his study corner, sit on his blue cushion, and read one page. Sometimes the words were tricky, but he would sound them out slowly.

"Beau-ti-ful," he would say carefully. "Beautiful!"

After one week, something magical happened. Koko realized he wanted to read more than one page! His routine had made him feel confident and brave.

"Mama!" he called excitedly. "Can I read two pages today?"

Mama Koala hugged him tight. "Of course! Your daily routine helped you build your reading skills, one page at a time."

Soon, Koko was reading whole chapters! He discovered stories about flying elephants, talking trees, and brave little mice. His study corner became his favorite place in the whole world.

"I learned something important," Koko told his friend Penny Possum. "When something feels too big and scary, I can make it smaller by doing a little bit every day. My routine made me brave!"

Now Koko reads every morning after breakfast, just like brushing his teeth or combing his fur. His daily routine turned his big scary problem into his greatest joy.

The End.
