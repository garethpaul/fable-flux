# Fern's Fair Share

In a cozy basement beneath the old oak tree, a gentle deer fawn named Fern lived with her woodland friends. The basement was always busy, filled with the sounds of scurrying feet, rustling leaves, and cheerful chatter. Wooden shelves lined the walls, holding acorns, berries, and seeds for winter.

Every morning, the animals worked together to sort their food. Rabbit would hop around counting carrots, Squirrel would organize nuts by size, and Mouse would arrange tiny seeds in neat rows. But Fern felt left out.

"I want to help too!" Fern said, her big brown eyes sparkling with hope.

"You're too big, Fern," squeaked Mouse. "You might step on our tiny seeds."

"And your hooves are too hard," added Rabbit. "You could crack our delicate carrots."

Fern's ears drooped sadly. The basement smelled like earth and dried leaves, but it didn't feel cozy anymore. She watched her friends work while she stood alone in the corner.

"That's not fair," Fern whispered to herself. "I want to help too."

Wise old Owl, who lived on the highest shelf, heard Fern's quiet words. "What seems to be the problem, little one?" he hooted gently.

"Everyone gets to help except me," Fern explained. "They say I'm too big and clumsy. But I want to be useful too!"

Owl's golden eyes twinkled. "Being fair means everyone gets a chance, doesn't it? But sometimes we need patience and practice to find the right job for everyone."

"But what can I do?" Fern asked.

"Let's think together," said Owl. "What are you good at?"

Fern thought hard. "Well, I'm tall. And I'm gentle, even though I'm big."

"Excellent!" hooted Owl. "Now, let's practice being patient while we figure this out."

The next day, Fern noticed something important. The highest shelves in the basement were covered in dust and cobwebs. Mouse couldn't reach them, Rabbit couldn't hop that high, and Squirrel was afraid of the wobbly ladder.

"I have an idea!" Fern announced. "I can clean the high shelves! My height is perfect for reaching up there."

Fern practiced moving very slowly and carefully. She used her soft nose to gently brush away the dust. Her long neck helped her reach every corner without knocking anything over.

"This is wonderful!" cheered Mouse. "Now our storage space is much cleaner!"

"And we have more room for food!" added Rabbit, thumping his foot happily.

"You found the perfect job, Fern!" chittered Squirrel.

From that day on, Fern had her own special job in the busy basement. She cleaned the high places that no one else could reach. The work made the basement smell fresh and clean, like a spring morning.

"Thank you for helping us be fair," Owl said to all the animals. "Everyone deserves a chance to help, and with patience and practice, we can find the right job for everyone."

Fern's heart felt warm and happy. The basement buzzed with activity, and now she was part of it all. She had learned that being fair meant including everyone, and that with patience and practice, there was always a way for everyone to help.

"Being fair feels good," Fern said, smiling as she gently dusted another high shelf. "And being patient helped me find my perfect job!"

The End.
