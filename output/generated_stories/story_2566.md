# Bubbles in the Basement Lab

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to splash, sing, and click-click with joy. Today, Bubbles visited a special place: a basement lab. The lab was under a big house, and it smelled like lemons and clean soap. The lights hummed softly. Machines blinked little green and blue dots, like tiny stars. The floor felt cool and smooth under Bubbles’ fins as he slid in a shallow practice pool made just for him.

Dr. Luna, a kind scientist with curly hair and bright eyes, waved. “Hi, Bubbles! Today we practice talking with our words. We are learning to be brave and kind.”

Bubbles chirped, “Eee-eee! I’m ready!” He loved learning. He loved fun. He loved being heard.

In the lab, there were three helpers: Max the mouse, Tiko the turtle, and Mimi the robot. They all wore tiny badges that said, “We help each other!”

Dr. Luna clapped softly. “We will take turns. We will listen. We will use strong, kind voices. That is called being assertive.”

Bubbles blinked. “As-ser-tive?” he asked, tasting the new word.

“It means you say what you need in a clear and kind way,” Dr. Luna said. “Not too loud. Not too quiet. Just right.”

Max squeaked, “Let’s play the button game!” There was a big red button that made bubbles float up from the pool with a happy pop-pop-pop. Bubbles loved that button. He flapped his fins with excitement. Pop! Pop! Pop! The bubbles smelled like fresh water and tickled his nose.

But soon, Max pressed the button again and again. Pop-pop-pop! Pop-pop-pop! Bubbles giggled, but his eyes felt a little tired. He wanted a turn to press the button himself.

Bubbles took a deep breath. He remembered Dr. Luna’s words. Not too loud. Not too quiet. Just right. He lifted his head and looked at Max.

“Max,” Bubbles said in a clear, kind voice, “I want a turn. Please give me the button now.”

Max paused. He looked at the button. He looked at Bubbles. “Oh! I didn’t know you wanted a turn,” Max said. “Thank you for telling me.” He handed the button to Bubbles.

Bubbles smiled. “Thank you, Max.” He pressed the button once. Pop! A big bubble rose up and went boop on his nose. Bubbles giggled. He felt proud. His chest felt warm, like sunshine. He had used his words.

Next, Mimi the robot rolled over with a tray that smelled like oranges. “Snack time!” Mimi beeped. “Orange slices and cool water!”

Tiko the turtle reached for all the orange slices at once. “Mine!” he said. The tray wobbled. One slice slipped and splashed into the pool with a plip.

Dr. Luna knelt beside Tiko. “Let’s think. We share snacks. We think of everyone.”

Bubbles wanted an orange slice too. He looked at Tiko. He kept his voice steady and soft, like a friendly wave. “Tiko, I would like an orange slice. Please leave some for me.”

Tiko slowed down. He blinked. “I was excited,” he said. “Thank you for telling me. Here is one for you.” He pushed a bright orange slice toward Bubbles. The orange smelled sweet and juicy. Bubbles took a bite. Mmm! His tongue tingled.

“Good using your words, Bubbles,” Dr. Luna said. “That is self-advocacy. You said what you needed.”

Bubbles swished his tail happily. Then the lab lights flickered. Click. Click. The humming grew quiet. Mimi’s eyes turned soft blue. “Power saving mode,” Mimi said. “We must help the lab.”

Dr. Luna looked around. “Team, we have a small job. We must clean up the splashed water, turn off extra lights, and put things away. We care for our place. That is civic responsibility.”

Bubbles nodded. The floor had little puddles. He loved splashing, but he knew the lab should be safe and dry. He swam to the edge and said, “Friends, I can push the big towels with my nose. Please help me.”

Max squeaked, “I will pick up the cups!”

Tiko said, “I will put the snack tray back.”

Mimi beeped, “I will dim the lights to safe mode.”

Bubbles pushed a fluffy towel with his nose. Squish-swish. The towel soaked up the puddles. He could feel the cool water through the towel. “There,” he said. “Dry and safe.”

When they finished, the lab felt calm and cozy. The lights glowed warm and soft. Dr. Luna smiled. “You used your voices. You took turns. You shared. You cared for the lab. I am proud.”

Bubbles felt brave and peaceful. He had asked for a turn. He had asked for a snack. He had asked for help to clean up. He had been assertive, and kind, too.

Max waved his tiny paw. “Bubbles, can we press the button one more time together?”

Bubbles grinned. “Yes. Let’s take turns. I press once, then you press once.”

They pressed the button in turns. Pop! Pop! Pop! The bubbles floated up like little moons and made soft plop sounds when they popped. Everyone laughed.

Dr. Luna clapped. “What did we learn?”

Bubbles raised his fin. “Use strong, kind words. Say what you need. Listen to others. Share and help.”

“Exactly,” Dr. Luna said. “That is assertiveness, tolerance, and caring for our place.”

Bubbles’ heart felt light as foam. He hummed a happy dolphin song. “I can be brave and kind,” he said. “I can speak up and listen too.”

And the lab, with its gentle lights and clean lemon smell, felt like a safe place for everyone.

The End.
