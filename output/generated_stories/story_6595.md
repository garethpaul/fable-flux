# Bear Cub’s Porch Promise

On a peaceful front porch, a friendly bear cub named Bibi rocked in a wooden chair. The chair creaked softly, creak, creak, creak, and the morning smelled like warm pine and fresh air. Sunlight made golden stripes on the porch floor. Bibi’s paws held a big cup of cool water. She liked to watch the breeze tickle the leaves and listen to the birds sing, tweet-tweet!

“Good morning, porch,” Bibi said. “Good morning, trees. Good morning, birds.”

Mama Bear opened the porch door with a smile. “Good morning, Bibi. Today is a good day to care for our home.”

“I love our porch,” Bibi said. She rubbed the smooth railing. “It feels safe and quiet.”

Mama Bear nodded. “Our porch is part of our home. And our home is part of our forest. We care for the forest, and the forest cares for us.”

Bibi’s ears perked up. “How do we care for it?”

Mama Bear pointed to the steps. A crinkly bag, a bottle cap, and a paper cup sat by the flower pots. A little ant tried to walk around them. The ant’s legs wiggled fast.

“Oh no!” Bibi said. “Trash on our porch!”

“The wind blew it in,” Mama Bear said gently. “This is a problem.”

Bibi felt a tight feeling in her tummy. “I don’t like this problem. The porch looks messy. The ant looks confused.”

Mama Bear took Bibi’s paw. “We can solve problems. Let’s make a Porch Promise. Every morning, we will do a little care. Just a little. We will pick up trash, water the plants, and sweep. A small routine can help a big world.”

Bibi took a deep breath. She smelled the sweet flowers and the dusty leaves. “A Porch Promise,” she said. “I can do that.”

They made a plan. Bibi would hold the small trash bag. Mama would hold the broom. They would use soft steps and slow breaths. “We move together,” Mama said. “That’s coordination. We work as a team.”

Bibi nodded. “Teamwork!”

They started with the bottle cap. “Clink!” it went into the bag. Bibi smiled. “One piece. I can do one more.” She picked up the paper cup. “Rustle.” Then the crinkly bag. “Crisp, crisp.”

“Good job,” Mama said. “You are being gentle and careful.”

Bibi watched the tiny ant. “Hello, little friend,” she said. “Now you can walk.”

The ant waved its feelers and wiggled away.

Next, they watered the plants. The watering can made a soft glug-glug sound. Cool drops shined on the green leaves. The flowers smelled sweet and spicy. “Thank you, plants,” Bibi whispered. “We will care for you every day.”

They swept the porch. Swish, swish, swish. Bibi and Mama moved together like a dance. Bibi swept the small spots. Mama swept the big spots. Their paws made tap-tap sounds on the boards.

Soon, the porch looked neat. The sunlight stripes looked brighter. The air felt lighter. Bibi’s tummy felt calm and warm.

“We did it,” Bibi said, proud. “Our porch is clean.”

Mama Bear hugged her. “You kept your Porch Promise today. That is persistence. You kept going until it was done.”

Bibi grinned. “I can do it again tomorrow. And the next day. That’s routine!”

They sat in the rocking chairs. Creak, creak, creak. Bibi sipped water. A hummingbird zipped by with a soft buzzzz. The flowers nodded as the wind whispered.

Just then, a new breeze blew. A tiny napkin fluttered onto the steps. Bibi blinked. “Oh no. The trash came back.”

Mama Bear patted her paw. “Trash can come again. That is okay. We don’t give up.”

Bibi stood up. “I will try again.” She picked up the napkin and put it in the bag. She looked at the yard and saw two more bits of paper by the path.

“This is bigger than our porch,” Bibi said slowly. “What about the path? What about the grass?”

Mama smiled. “We start small and keep going. Small care grows.”

Bibi had an idea. She made a sign with big, friendly letters: “Please help keep our porch and forest clean.” She drew a smiling tree and a tiny ant.

She taped the sign near the steps. Tape made a soft rip sound. “There,” Bibi said. “We can invite others to help.”

Soon, Neighbor Squirrel scampered by. “Chitter! What a nice porch! Oh! A sign!”

Bibi waved. “Hi! We are caring for our porch and forest. We pick up a little every day.”

Neighbor Squirrel picked up a leaf wrapper. “I can help,” he said. “Chitter-chitter!”

Bibi felt joy fizz in her chest, like bubble water. “Thank you!”

As the sun moved higher, the porch was bright and tidy. The ant marched along, the flowers sparkled with tiny drops, and the air smelled clean. Bibi rocked once more and made a quiet promise.

“Every morning,” she whispered. “I will care, I will try, I will not give up. I will be gentle with the earth.”

Mama Bear squeezed her paw. “That is how we love our home,” she said.

Bibi smiled at the trees, the birds, and the little ant. “We can all work together,” she said. “When we care a little, every day, the world feels better.”

The End.
