# Leo’s Gentle Paws in the Guest Room

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, Mama Lion said, “We have a guest coming today. Auntie Giraffe will sleep in the guest room. Let’s make it cozy!”

Leo’s ears perked up. “I want to help! I want to be brave and kind,” he said, swishing his tail.

Mama smiled. “That’s wonderful, Leo. We will do our chores together. We will take turns. And we will be kind.”

They padded down the hall to the guest room. The door opened with a soft creak. The room smelled like fresh soap and clean blankets. A little breeze tickled the curtains, and sunlight made warm spots on the floor.

Leo’s paws pressed into the soft rug. “It feels like fluffy clouds,” he giggled.

Mama pointed with her paw. “First, we tidy toys. You take the blocks. I’ll fold the blankets.”

Leo loved blocks. He picked up a red block. “Clack.” He picked up a blue block. “Clack.” He wanted to stack them high. He started to build a tall tower.

Mama said gently, “We are cleaning now, little cub. We can build later. Let’s take turns. First tidy, then play.”

Leo blinked and nodded. “Okay, Mama. My turn to tidy now. Play later.”

He put the blocks in the basket. “Plop, plop, plop.” The sound made him smile. He found a stuffed zebra under the chair. “Hello, Zebra,” he whispered, hugging it close. The zebra felt soft against his whiskers. He placed it on the pillow. “You can nap here,” he said kindly.

Mama folded a blanket. “Smooth and flat,” she said. “Feel, Leo.”

Leo pressed his paw. The blanket was warm and soft. “Smooth,” he said. “It feels like sunshine.”

Papa Lion peeked in with a broom. “Who is my brave helper today?”

“Me!” Leo stood tall. “I am helping! I am being kind. I am taking turns.”

Papa smiled. “Let’s sweep. I’ll sweep the floor. Then it will be your turn to dust the table.”

Leo watched Papa sweep. “Swish, swish,” went the broom. Little dust bunnies danced in the air.

When Papa finished, he gave Leo a soft cloth. “Your turn,” he said.

Leo dusted the little table by the window. “Wipe, wipe.” He found a tiny crumb. He picked it up and put it in the bin. “Goodbye, crumb,” he giggled. “The table is clean now.”

A little spider sat in the corner, hanging from a thin, shiny web. Leo tilted his head. “Hello, Spider,” he said softly. “We are cleaning the guest room. Auntie Giraffe is coming.”

The spider wiggled its legs. Leo whispered to Mama, “Should we move Spider?”

Mama said, “We can be kind. We can help Spider find a safe place.” She opened the window a little. A sweet breeze whooshed in. They used a paper cup and a leaf. “Your turn,” Mama said.

Leo held the cup carefully. The spider tickled his paw through the cup. “Hee-hee,” Leo giggled. He carried Spider to the garden outside. “Here is a cozy leaf,” he said. “Goodbye, Spider.”

Back in the guest room, Leo saw the bed. The sheets were crinkled like waves. “Can I help make the bed?” he asked.

Mama nodded. “Yes. I tuck one side. You tuck the other. We take turns.”

“Tuck,” said Mama.

“Tuck,” said Leo. The sheets became smooth. Leo patted the pillow. “Puff, puff,” he said, making it fluffy.

Then Leo found a paper on the floor. It was a little picture he had drawn—a sunny sun and a smiley face. He picked it up. “This can be a welcome sign,” he said.

Mama beamed. “What a kind idea!”

Leo placed the picture on the table with a tiny flower in a cup. He smelled the flower. It smelled like lemons and rain. “Auntie will like this,” he said softly.

Soon, there was a gentle knock at the door. “Knock-knock.” Auntie Giraffe stepped in, her long neck bending to see the room. “Oh my,” she said, her voice warm like cocoa. “What a lovely guest room! It smells fresh. It looks so cozy. And what is this beautiful picture?”

Leo’s chest felt warm and proud. “I drew it for you. We all helped. We took turns. We did our chores. We were kind.”

Auntie Giraffe lowered her head and nuzzled Leo gently. “Thank you, brave little lion cub. Your kind paws made this room shine.”

Papa clapped softly. “Teamwork makes chores feel light.”

Mama said, “Yes. Taking turns helps everyone.”

Leo curled his tail with joy. “Kindness feels good,” he said. “And helping is fun!”

Auntie Giraffe lay down on the bed. “The pillow is so puffy,” she sighed happily. “The blanket feels like a hug.”

Leo smiled and whispered, “Good night, guest room. Be kind to Auntie.”

That evening, they all shared warm milk and crunchy crackers. They laughed. They told gentle jokes. The house felt calm and happy. Leo’s eyelids grew heavy. He snuggled into Mama’s fur and listened to the soft night sounds. “Cricket-crick, hoot-hoot,” sang the night.

Mama stroked his ear. “Today you learned something special.”

Leo yawned. “I learned to be kind, to do my chores, and to take turns,” he said sleepily. “Kindness makes home cozy.”

Mama kissed his head. “Yes, my brave little cub. Kindness is the softest pillow of all.”

Leo purred. “I can be kind again tomorrow,” he murmured.

The End.
