# Birdie Sorts the Nursery

Birdie was a small, singing bird with bright blue feathers and a warm, happy voice. Birdie lived in a comfortable nursery with soft rugs, a sunny window, and shelves that smelled like fresh wood. There were blocks, books, and teddy bears. There were crayons in a rainbow of colors. It was cozy and calm—most of the time.

One morning, Birdie woke up on the curtain rod and sang, “Tweet-tee-dee! Good morning to me!” Birdie looked down. Oh my! Toys were all over the floor. Blocks were under a blanket. Crayons were rolling away like little logs. The rug felt bumpy under Birdie’s tiny feet.

“This feels messy,” Birdie said. “I can’t find my song cards!” Birdie liked to read song cards and sing the words.

From the shelf, Bear-Bear yawned. “Good morning, Birdie,” said Bear-Bear, soft and sleepy.

“Good morning,” Birdie said. “I want to sing, but I can’t find my cards.”

Rattle-Rabbit shook with a jingle. “Maybe they are under the blocks,” he said.

Birdie hopped to the floor. “Let’s look,” Birdie said. Birdie peeked under a pillow. “Not here.” Birdie peeked behind a book. “Not here.”

Birdie felt a little sad. The sunny window warmed Birdie’s wings, but the room still felt tangled. Birdie took a deep breath. The air smelled like crayons and clean cotton.

“I have an idea,” Birdie said. “We can organize.” Birdie said the word slowly. “Or-gan-ize means put things in their place.”

Bear-Bear nodded. “Where is the place?”

“We can make places,” Birdie said. Birdie fluttered and sang a tiny planning song. “Sort, sort, sort! Find a home for every sort!”

Rattle-Rabbit jingled happily. “Let’s make homes!” he said.

Birdie looked around. “Blocks can live in the blue bin. Books can sit on the low shelf. Crayons can sleep in the green cup. Soft toys can rest in the basket.”

Bear-Bear hugged a block. “But I like blocks near me,” he said in a slow, gentle voice.

Birdie smiled. “We can talk and make a plan. We can use our words.” Birdie sat close. “How about this: We keep two blocks by you for snuggling, and the rest go in the blue bin. Is that fair?”

Bear-Bear thought. “Two blocks for me, many blocks in the bin. That sounds fair.”

“That is good negotiation,” Birdie said kindly. “We listened. We found a way that works for everyone.”

Rattle-Rabbit wiggled. “I like to shake near the window,” he said. “Can I stay there?”

Birdie chirped, “Yes! You can stay by the window on the little tray. Your tray will be your place.”

Birdie clapped tiny wings. “Now, sort!”

They began. Birdie sang as they worked. “Blocks in the bin, books on the shelf, crayons in the cup, soft toys to rest!” The song filled the nursery, bright and cheerful. The blocks clicked and clacked. The books made soft thumps. The crayons made a little rattly sound in the cup. The basket of toys felt warm and fuzzy.

Birdie found a shiny corner under the rug. Something was peeking out. Birdie tugged gently. “My song cards!” Birdie chirped with joy. The cards smelled like paper and sunshine. Birdie’s heart felt light.

“Yay!” said Bear-Bear. “You found them!”

“We did it together,” Birdie said. “Because we organized.”

Birdie looked at the shelf. “Let’s make signs,” Birdie said. “Signs help us remember.” Birdie took a big paper and a fat crayon. Birdie drew a blue square for blocks. Birdie drew lines like little books for the shelf. Birdie drew a green circle for crayons. Birdie drew a basket with a smile for soft toys.

Rattle-Rabbit tapped the tray. “Can I have a sign?” he asked.

“Yes,” Birdie said. “A small bell for you.” Birdie drew a tiny bell and placed it on the tray by the window.

The nursery looked neat and friendly. The rug was smooth. The shelves were tidy. The sun felt warmer, and the room smelled like fresh paper and clean cotton. Birdie’s song floated up like a happy ribbon.

Then they heard a little “clink, clink.” Two crayons rolled out of the cup. Birdie picked them up and paused. “Should we keep all the crayons in the green cup?” Birdie asked.

Rattle-Rabbit tilted his head. “Sometimes the cup spills.”

Birdie nodded. “Let’s talk.” Birdie’s voice was calm. “We can use a bigger cup, or we can put the cup on the shelf. What do you think?”

Bear-Bear patted the table. “A bigger cup, please,” he said.

Rattle-Rabbit jingled. “And a little tray under it!”

Birdie smiled. “Good ideas! We listened, we shared, we chose.” They found a bigger cup and a small tray. The crayons sat snug and safe.

Birdie opened the song cards. “Now I can sing.” Birdie sang a sweet song: “When we sort and put away, we find our things and we can play.”

Everyone swayed. The nursery felt peaceful and bright.

Birdie looked around and said, “Organization helps us. We talk, we plan, we tidy, and we care. When everything has a place, our hearts feel calm.”

Bear-Bear hugged Birdie. “I feel calm,” he said.

Rattle-Rabbit shook softly. “I feel happy.”

Birdie chirped, “Me too.”

And every evening after play, Birdie sang the tidy-up song. They worked together, used their words, and made fair plans.

The nursery stayed neat, and their days felt easy and fun.

The End.
