# Panda Poppy’s Quiet Closet Plan

Panda Poppy loved cozy places. One rainy afternoon, she found a closet hideaway behind a soft blue curtain. Inside the closet were fluffy scarves, a stack of pillows, and a tiny lamp that glowed like a friendly firefly. It smelled like clean cotton and warm vanilla.

“This is perfect,” Poppy whispered, patting a pillow. “A quiet, snuggly spot.”

Soon, her friends came to play. Tiko the tiger cub trotted in with bouncy steps. Lila the little lemur swung by the door. Benji the bunny hopped after them, his ears flopping.

“Wow!” said Tiko, eyes wide. “A secret fort!”

“I want the sparkly scarf!” Lila squeaked, reaching for a shiny, silver one.

“I want the little lamp!” said Benji, poking the switch. Click. Click. Click.

The tiny lamp blinked on and off. The closet filled with shadows and flickers. The friends all spoke at once. Words piled up like blocks.

“I saw it first!”

“No, me!”

“Give it back!”

The closet felt tight and noisy. Poppy’s round ears twitched at the loud voices. Her tummy felt wiggly, like jelly.

Poppy took a slow, deep breath. In…and out. She remembered her calm plan: Stop. Breathe. Use gentle words.

She held up her paw. “Friends,” she said softly, “I feel wobbly when it’s loud. Can we try quiet voices?”

Tiko blinked. Lila paused. Benji clicked the lamp off and hugged his ears.

“Okay,” Benji whispered. “Quiet voices.”

“Thank you,” said Poppy. “We have a little problem. We all want the same things at the same time. Let’s use peaceful words and work together.”

Tiko’s tail swished. “I feel jumpy,” he said. “I want to play fast.”

Lila wrapped the scarf around her shoulders, then stopped. “I feel excited,” she said. “I want to twirl.”

Benji patted the lamp. “I feel curious,” he said. “I want to press the button.”

Poppy nodded. “Thank you for telling me your feelings. I feel calm when we listen. We can share. We can take turns. We can make a plan.”

“How?” asked Lila, tilting her head.

Poppy rubbed the soft scarf between her paws. “First, we take a Breath Break,” she said. “Smell the flower.” She pretended to smell a flower. “Blow the candle.” She gently blew out.

The friends copied her. In…and out. Smell the flower. Blow the candle. The closet felt softer again. The tiny lamp gave a warm glow. The pillows felt extra squishy.

Poppy smiled. “Now, we use kind words. We say what we need. We ask, not grab.”

Tiko nodded. “Can I please have a fast game?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Poppy. “Let’s play ‘Pass the Pillow.’ We pass it in a circle, one by one. When you hold it, you can do one fast wiggle. Then you pass it on.”

Lila giggled. “Can I please have a twirl turn with the scarf?”

“Yes,” said Poppy. “After Tiko’s wiggle, you twirl once with the scarf. Then pass.”

Benji raised a paw. “Can I please click the lamp one time?”

“Yes,” said Poppy. “After Lila’s twirl, you click the lamp one time. Then stop.”

Tiko, Lila, and Benji clapped quietly. “That’s a plan!”

They sat in a circle on the fluffy carpet. The closet smelled like warm cotton and a little bit of lemon from the wooden hangers. Poppy put the pillow in Tiko’s paws.

Tiko wiggled fast-fast-fast and then stopped. “My turn is done,” he said proudly, and passed the pillow to Lila.

Lila wrapped the silver scarf around her shoulders. She twirled once, like a soft spinning top. The scarf flashed in the lamplight. “My turn is done,” she sang, and handed the pillow to Benji.

Benji clicked the lamp. Click. On. The glow felt like a hug. He clicked it off. “My turn is done,” he said, smiling.

Poppy hugged the pillow to her belly. “Now it’s my turn to share a feeling,” she said. “I feel happy when we take turns.”

Around and around they went. Wiggle. Twirl. Click. Breath Break. Wiggle. Twirl. Click. The game was smooth like a song. The friends watched with kind eyes and waiting paws. When it was hard to wait, they put hands on hearts and took one slow breath.

After a while, Lila said, “I feel a little tired.”

“My legs feel soft,” said Tiko.

“My paws feel warm,” said Benji.

Poppy nodded. “Let’s use our quiet closet for a rest plan.”

They made a tiny nest with pillows. The scarf became a gentle blanket. The lamp glowed low, like a sleepy moon. Poppy whispered, “Thank you for using peaceful words. Thank you for sharing. Thank you for telling your feelings.”

Tiko yawned. “I liked the plan.”

“I liked the turns,” said Lila.

“I liked the breaths,” said Benji.

Poppy patted each friend’s paw. “When we have a problem, we can stop, breathe, and use gentle words. We can make a plan and work together. That is peaceful talk.”

All three friends smiled. The closet felt safe and calm. The rain outside made a soft tip-tap song.

Poppy closed her eyes and whispered, “Peace begins with our voices, our breaths, and our hearts.”

The friends rested, snug and proud. They had solved the problem with patience, coordination, and care.

The End.
