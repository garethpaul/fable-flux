# Benny Beaver's Garden Helper Day

Benny Beaver wiggled his flat tail excitedly as he looked out at Mrs. Robin's beautiful balcony garden. The morning sun made the red tomatoes shine like little lanterns, and the green lettuce leaves looked soft and crinkly.

"Good morning, Benny!" chirped Mrs. Robin from her nest near the flower pots. "I could really use some help in my garden today. Would you like to be my special helper?"

Benny's brown eyes sparkled. "Oh yes! I love helping!" he said, clapping his small paws together. "What should I do first?"

Mrs. Robin fluttered her wings thoughtfully. "Well, I need someone to water the plants very gently. Can you follow my directions carefully?"

"I can do that!" Benny nodded eagerly.

Mrs. Robin showed him a tiny blue watering can. "First, fill this with just a little water. Then, pour it slowly near the roots, not on the leaves. The plants get thirsty, just like you do!"

Benny picked up the watering can. It felt smooth and cool in his paws. He filled it with water that sparkled in the sunlight and began pouring it quickly over the first plant.

"Whoa there!" called Mrs. Robin gently. "Remember, slowly and near the roots. Let me show you."

She demonstrated with her beak, pretending to pour water drop by drop. "See? Nice and easy."

Benny tried again, this time moving the watering can slowly and carefully. The water made a soft tinkling sound as it touched the soil. "Like this?" he asked.

"Perfect!" Mrs. Robin sang happily.

Just then, a fuzzy yellow bee buzzed over to the flowers. "Buzz-buzz! Those flowers smell so sweet!" said Bella Bee. "But I can't reach the nectar because some leaves are blocking my way."

Benny immediately reached out to move the leaves with his paws. "I'll help you!"

"Wait!" said Mrs. Robin kindly. "Let's think of a peaceful way to solve this problem together. Benny, what if we gently move the leaves without breaking them?"

Benny paused and thought. "Oh! I could carefully lift them up instead of pushing them?"

"That's a wonderful idea!" Mrs. Robin chirped. "And Bella, what if you help us by showing us exactly which leaves need moving?"

Working together, the three friends created a peaceful solution. Bella pointed with her tiny legs to show which leaves were in her way. Benny gently lifted each leaf with his soft paws, being very careful not to hurt the plant. Mrs. Robin watched and gave helpful suggestions.

"There!" said Benny proudly as he held up the last leaf. "Now you can reach all the flowers, Bella!"

"Buzz-zing! Thank you both!" Bella hummed happily as she flew from flower to flower. "This is much better than fighting with the leaves!"

The three friends spent the rest of the morning working together in the garden. They followed Mrs. Robin's directions to plant new seeds, helped each other reach high places, and solved every problem with kind words and teamwork.

As the sun climbed higher, Benny sat back and looked at their beautiful work. The plants stood tall and happy, the flowers bloomed brightly, and everything smelled like fresh earth and sunshine.

"Thank you for being such a wonderful helper, Benny," said Mrs. Robin. "You learned to follow directions and find peaceful solutions to every problem."

Benny's tail wiggled with joy. "I learned that working together and being gentle makes everything better!" he said with a big smile.

The End.
