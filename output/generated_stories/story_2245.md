# Gigi the Giraffe Finds Her Words

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe. She loved to think and look and listen. Her favorite place was the study corner—a cozy nook with a small desk, a soft blue rug, a tall lamp, and a basket full of crayons and smooth, clean paper. The lamp made a warm golden glow, and the crayons smelled a little like sweet wax. The room was quiet, except for the soft tick-tock of a clock.

One sunny afternoon, Gigi wanted to write her very first story. She stretched her long neck and whispered, “I can do this.” She picked a purple crayon, her favorite. It felt sturdy in her hoof. She drew a gentle line. But her letters were wiggly. Her words were tiny and crooked.

“Oh no,” she sighed. “My writing looks messy.”

Her friend Dot, a small ladybug, buzzed by the window and landed on the desk. “Hi, Gigi!” Dot chirped. “What are you doing?”

“I’m trying to write,” Gigi said softly. “But it doesn’t look good. Maybe I’m not good at writing.”

The words made Gigi’s tummy feel heavy. Her ears drooped.

Dot looked at the paper. “It looks like a beginning,” Dot said. “Beginnings can be bumpy. We can use a plan. Do you want a writing plan?”

Gigi blinked. “A plan?”

“Yes,” Dot said, tapping the paper with a tiny foot. “First, we breathe. Then, we break the big job into small steps. And we say kind words to ourselves.”

Gigi took a big, slow breath in… and out. The warm air smelled like paper and crayons.

Dot smiled. “Step one: breathe. Good! Step two: draw a picture of your idea. Step three: add one word. Step four: add two more words. Step five: read it out loud. See? Small steps.”

Gigi hugged the purple crayon. “I like small steps.” She drew a sun with long, smiling rays. The crayon made a soft scratch-scratch sound on the paper.

Just then, Tip the monkey peeked around the door. “Whatcha doing?” Tip giggled, swinging his tail. He hopped onto the rug and looked at Gigi’s page. “Those letters look funny,” Tip blurted without thinking.

Gigi’s heart sank. “Funny?” she whispered. Her eyes stung a little.

Tip saw her sad face. His smile faded. “Oh,” he said, ears drooping. “I didn’t mean to hurt you.”

Gigi took another breath. “I am learning,” she said, slow and calm. “I am trying my best.”

Dot buzzed between them. “We are using a plan,” she told Tip. “Want to help kindly?”

Tip nodded fast. “I’m sorry, Gigi,” he said. “I spoke without thinking. Will you forgive me?”

Gigi looked at Tip’s sorry eyes. She felt the warm lamp light on her neck. She nodded. “I forgive you. Please be gentle with your words.”

Tip sighed with relief. “Thank you.”

Dot clapped her tiny hands. “Great feelings team! Now, step three: add one word.”

Gigi thought. She said, “Sun.” She wrote S-U-N, slow and careful. The letters were still a little wiggly, but the word was there. She smiled a small smile. “Sun,” she read.

“Step four: add two more words,” Dot reminded.

Gigi whispered, “Sun is…” She added, “bright.” She wrote B-R-I-G-H-T. It was hard, but she kept going. The crayon made tiny swishing sounds.

Tip watched quietly. “I can get you a yellow crayon,” he said. “Yellow can make the sun glow.”

“Thank you,” Gigi said. “That is helpful.” Tip brought the yellow crayon. Gigi colored in soft circles. The sun looked warm and happy.

Gigi read her page out loud: “Sun is bright.” Her voice felt strong. The words sounded like sunshine.

Dot buzzed in a little circle. “Listen to that! You did it.”

Gigi’s chest felt light. She added one more line: “Gigi smiles.” She wrote G-I-G-I and then S-M-I-L-E-S. She drew a small giraffe with a smile and long, gentle lashes.

Tip pointed. “That looks like you!”

Gigi giggled. “It is me.”

She looked at her page. It was not perfect. The letters tilted. Some lines shook. But the picture was sunny, and the words were true. She touched her chest. “I am proud of me,” she said. “I can learn. I can grow.”

Dot nodded. “That is self-esteem,” she said. “That means you know you are special and strong inside, even when you are learning.”

Tip sat cross-legged on the rug. “I want to try too,” he said. “Can you show me the plan?”

Gigi smiled. “Yes.” She spoke in a kind, steady voice. “Step one: breathe. Step two: draw. Step three: one word. Step four: two more words. Step five: read out loud. And we use kind words to ourselves and to friends.”

Tip breathed in and out. He drew a banana and wrote, “Banana is yummy.” His letters were wiggly too.

Gigi clapped her hooves softly. “Great job, Tip.”

Tip beamed. “Thanks! I’m proud of me.”

Gigi taped both pages to the wall in the study corner. The tape made a soft rip sound. The papers fluttered gently like little flags.

Under the warm lamp, the study corner felt safe and bright. Gigi looked at her work and whispered, “I can do hard things. I can be kind to me. I can forgive friends. I can make a plan.”

Dot buzzed happily. “And you can shine.”

Gigi’s smile grew big. “We can all shine,” she said.

The End.
