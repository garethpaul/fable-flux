# Polly Plans the Day

Polly was a bright green parrot with sunny yellow cheeks and a soft red tail. She lived in a playful living room filled with cozy pillows, a bouncy rug, and a little table with crayons, paper, and stickers. Sunlight danced on the floor. The clock tick-tick-ticked, and the air smelled like fresh soap and oranges.

“Good morning!” chirped Polly. “Today, I help. Today, I write. Today, I do my part!”

Polly flapped to the window perch and looked down at her little list on the wall. The list was made of big pictures: a broom, a book, a cup, a heart, and a mailbox. Polly loved her list. It was her routine.

She traced each picture with her wing. “Sweep. Read. Share. Say sorry if I make a mistake. Send a note,” she said softly. “That is civic responsibility. That means we take care of our home and our friends.”

Polly hopped to the shelf and saw a tiny toy car on the floor. “Uh-oh,” she said. “Someone might trip.” She picked it up with her beak and put it in the toy basket. Clink! It felt good to help.

Next, Polly grabbed the small broom. Swish, swish. The broom brushed crumbs into a dustpan. “I can do it!” Polly sang. The rug felt bouncy under her feet, and the room smelled clean. Helping care for the living room was part of the day, like sunshine and snacks.

“Polly, do you want some water?” asked Mia, a gentle voice from the hallway.

“Yes, please!” Polly replied. Mia brought a small cup. Polly held it steady with her claws. Sip, sip. “Thank you,” said Polly. “I will put my cup in the sink when I am done. That is my job.”

Mia smiled. “You make our home nice. That helps everyone.”

Polly puffed with pride. “I help our home. That is civic responsibility. We all share.”

After water, Polly sat at the little table. She picked a blue crayon and a sheet of paper. “I will write a kind note,” she said. Scratch, scratch went the crayon. The paper felt smooth, and the crayon smelled a little waxy. “Dear Neighbors,” she said aloud as she wrote in big loopy letters, “Please put toys in the basket after play. Thank you! Love, Polly.”

She added a sticker of a smiling sun. “A kind note helps us remember our shared rules,” Polly said. “Rules keep us safe and happy.”

Polly looked around. The room was tidy. She felt calm and proud. Then she heard a thump! A tower of blocks wobbled and fell—clatter, clatter, clack!

“Oh no,” Polly whispered. “I bumped it.”

Mia peeked in. “Are you okay?”

Polly nodded, but her feathers sagged. “I made a mess.”

Mia knelt beside her. “It was an accident. What can we do now?”

Polly took a deep breath. “I can fix it. I can say sorry.” She turned to the blocks and said, “I am sorry, blocks, for the big fall.” She gently picked them up, one by one. Click, clack, stack. The tower stood again, even taller.

“Good repair,” said Mia. “You took responsibility. That is kind.”

Polly felt warm and light. “When we make a mess, we help to fix it. That is civic responsibility too.”

The clock went tick-tick. It was reading time. Polly sat by the window with a soft book. The pages rustled like leaves. She read a little story about friends sharing a sandbox. “They take turns,” Polly said. “They care for the sand toys.”

Soon it was snack time. Crunch, crunch went Polly’s apple slice. After snack, she put the napkin in the bin and the plate by the sink. “I can clean up after me,” she said. “I can rely on me.”

Polly checked her list again. Broom? Done. Book? Done. Cup? Done. Heart? “Oh!” Polly remembered. “I forgot to say sorry to Mia for the noise.” She flew to the hallway. “Mia, I am sorry I made a loud crash.”

Mia hugged Polly gently. “Thank you for telling me. I forgive you.”

Polly smiled. Forgiveness felt like a soft blanket.

Last was the mailbox picture. Polly folded her kind note and slipped it into a little toy mailbox on the table. “Mail delivered!” she chirped. She also wrote one more note to herself: “You did it, Polly.” She added a star sticker. “I can keep this routine every day.”

Mia clapped softly. “Polly, you showed civic responsibility. You cared for our space, you followed a routine, you helped, you fixed a mistake, and you wrote kind notes.”

Polly flapped her wings. The living room looked bright and friendly. The rug was clear. The toys were cozy in the basket. The air smelled like apples now.

Polly sang, “We help, we share, we say sorry, we care. We can do it, here and there!”

Polly felt happy and strong. She knew she could help again tomorrow. She knew her routine helped everyone feel safe. She knew she could rely on herself to do kind things.

And she knew this lesson: When we take care of our home and each other, we make a good place for all.

The End.
