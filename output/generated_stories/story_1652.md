# Miko Monkey Finds His Calm

Miko was a small, courageous monkey with bright eyes and soft, fuzzy fur. One rainy day, Miko visited Nana’s house. The guest room was cozy and warm. A soft rug tickled his toes. A tiny window clicked with little raindrops. Drip, drop, drippity-drop. The air smelled like clean blankets and lemon soap.

Miko loved to move. He loved to jump and spin. Today, he had a big plan. “I will make a welcome dance for Nana!” he said. “It will be my creative show!”

He wiggled his tail, tapped his toes, and twirled around the guest bed. His heart beat fast, thump-thump! His chest felt fluttery like a butterfly. He pulled out crayons and paper to draw a poster for his show. But the crayons rolled off the desk. The blue one went under the bed. The red one fell with a clack. The paper crinkled. The rain got louder. Drip, drop, plop-plop-plop.

Miko’s eyes got big. “Oh no,” he whispered. “It’s all too much!” His tummy felt tight. His hands felt hot. He tried to jump his worries away, but his feet bumped the nightstand. The lamp wobbled. “Eek!” Miko caught it just in time. His breath came fast and choppy.

Nana knocked softly and peeked in with a gentle smile. “How’s my brave Miko?”

Miko’s voice wiggled. “I wanted to make a dance and a poster, but the rain is loud, the crayons roll, and I feel twisty inside.”

Nana sat on the soft rug. “You are courageous to tell me,” she said. “When the room feels busy and our body feels twisty, we can choose calm. Calm helps us think. Calm helps us create. Would you like to learn a calm plan?”

Miko nodded.

“First, we pause,” Nana said. “Feet on the rug. Feel the soft squish.”

Miko pressed his toes down. The rug felt warm and fuzzy, like hugging clouds.

“Second,” Nana whispered, “we breathe like the rain. In slow, out slow.”

Miko breathed in. He smelled lemon and clean blankets. He breathed out. His shoulders dropped a little.

“Third,” said Nana, “we move in quiet ways. Gentle movements help our body feel safe. Would you like to copy me?”

Miko smiled a tiny smile. “Yes.”

Nana showed him calm monkey moves. “Stretch arms up like tall trees.” They stretched. “Sway side to side like leaves in the wind.” They swayed. “Roll your shoulders like small circles.” They rolled. “Pat your chest soft, tap-tap, to say, ‘I’m okay.’”

Miko copied each move. His tummy felt less tight. The rain still dripped, but it sounded like a song now. Drip, drop, drippity-drop.

“Fourth,” Nana said, “we make a calm space.” She put two pillows by the window and a blanket on the rug. “This is your calm corner. You can sit, breathe, and create.”

Miko’s eyes shone. “Can I try my poster here?”

“Of course,” said Nana.

Miko took a deep breath and spoke to himself. “I can be calm. I can move gently. I can make art.” He placed a book by the edge of the desk so the crayons would not roll. He put the paper flat on the blanket. He chose colors one by one. Blue for raindrops. Yellow for warm lights. Green for brave leaves. He drew a big smile and wrote, with help, “Welcome, Nana!”

When the rain thumped louder, Miko paused and did his calm monkey moves. Stretch. Sway. Shoulder circles. Tap-tap. He breathed like the rain. In slow, out slow. His chest felt easy again.

Soon, it was time for his dance. Miko stood in the guest room. The soft rug hugged his feet. The poster cheered from the wall. Nana clapped quietly. “Go, Miko!”

Miko began with calm steps. He tiptoed like raindrops. Pitter-pat. He swayed like trees. Whoosh-whoosh. He made big circle arms like rainbows. He ended with a brave monkey bow.

Nana’s eyes sparkled. “What a creative, calm dance! You moved with care. You solved your problem.”

Miko beamed. “When I felt twisty, I used my calm plan. Pause, breathe, gentle moves, and a calm corner. Then I could make art and dance!”

Nana hugged him. Her sweater felt soft and warm. “That is wise. Calm helps us do our best. Calm helps us feel safe. Calm helps us improve.”

Miko giggled. “I am a courageous monkey who knows how to be calm!”

He hung the poster a little straighter. He took one more breath in and out. The rain slowed to a tiny tap-tap. The guest room felt cozy and bright.

Miko whispered to the room, “Thank you, calm.” Then he looked at Nana. “Tomorrow, I can teach you my calm monkey moves.”

“I would love that,” Nana said.

Miko smiled his biggest smile. “Me too.”

The End.
