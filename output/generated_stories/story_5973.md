# Pebble the Penguin Finds Their Voice

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and soft, shiny feathers. Pebble’s cozy bedroom was warm and snug. A little night-light glowed like a tiny moon. The bed was fluffy, and the blanket was soft like a cloud. Pebble’s favorite pillow smelled like clean soap and a little bit like the sea.

One evening, as the wind hummed gently against the window, Pebble was building a puzzle on the rug. The pieces clicked together—click, click, click. “I’m almost done,” Pebble whispered, smiling.

Mama Penguin poked her head in. “Bedtime soon, little pebble,” she said kindly.

Pebble nodded. “Okay, Mama. Just two more pieces.”

Suddenly, Pebble’s kid cousin, Pico, waddled in with a toy drum. “Boom-boom! Let’s play a marching song!” Pico giggled and tapped the drum.

The sound felt loud in the cozy room. Boom. Boom. Boom. Pebble’s tummy felt tight. Pebble wanted quiet to finish the puzzle and get ready for sleep.

Pebble took a slow breath in. In… and out. Pebble placed a flipper on the belly and felt it rise and fall. This helped Pebble think. Pebble remembered: Use calm words. Speak up. Be kind and clear.

Pebble said softly, “Pico, I like your drum. It sounds brave. But my ears feel too loud right now. I need quiet to finish and get sleepy.”

Pico blinked. “Oh,” Pico said, holding the drum. “I want to play. I didn’t know your ears felt loud.”

Mama nodded. “Thank you, Pebble, for saying what you need. That is self-advocacy.”

Pebble looked at Pico. “Can we make a plan?” Pebble asked. “Maybe we take turns?”

Pico tilted the drum. “How?”

Pebble said, “You can play a soft song for one minute—tap-tap, like tiny snowflakes. Then we have one minute of quiet. I will finish my puzzle. After that, I can clap for you. We can trade. Is that fair?”

Pico thought. Pico liked to play. Pico also liked fair plans. “Okay,” Pico said. “I will try soft taps.”

Pico breathed in and out, like Pebble. Then Pico tapped gently: tap, tap, tap. The sound was like raindrops on leaves. Pebble’s shoulders felt loose again. Click, click—two puzzle pieces fit. Pebble smiled. “Thank you, Pico.”

After a minute, Pebble held up a flipper. “Quiet minute now.” The room felt calm and warm. The night-light glowed softly. Pebble heard the wind hum and the clock tick. Tick-tock, tick-tock. This quiet felt good.

When the minute was done, Pebble clapped. “Your turn, Pico!”

Pico beamed and played a tiny tune—tap-tap-ta-tap! Pebble swayed and laughed. “I like your music.”

Mama Penguin watched with a smile. “You both made a good agreement. That is negotiation.”

Pebble finished the last puzzle piece. Click! “I did it!” Pebble cheered. The picture showed a map with penguins from many places—north, south, east, west. Each penguin wore a bright scarf in a different color.

Pebble touched the map. “Look, Pico. Penguins all around the world. We are all different. We all share the same sky.”

Pico nodded. “We can share the room, too.”

Pebble said, “Yes. In our home, in our world, we can listen, take turns, and care for each other.”

Mama turned down the lamp. The night-light glowed a little brighter. “Time for bedtime cuddles,” she said.

Pebble pulled the soft blanket up to the chin. The blanket felt warm like sunshine on feathers. Pebble took three slow breaths. In… out. In… out. In… out. The wind sang a sleepy song outside the window.

Then Pebble remembered something. “Mama, I feel a little thirsty,” Pebble said. “May I have a small sip of water, please?” Pebble used a calm, clear voice.

“Thank you for telling me,” Mama said. “Yes, a small sip.” She brought a tiny cup. The water was cool and fresh. Pebble’s mouth felt happy.

Pico yawned a big, squeaky yawn. “I feel sleepy, too,” Pico said. “Can I stay for the bedtime song?”

Pebble looked at Mama. “I like a very soft song at night,” Pebble said. “It helps my body rest.”

Pico nodded. “I can sing soft,” Pico whispered.

Mama began to sing a gentle lullaby. Pico hummed softly, like a bee in a flower. Pebble listened. The room was peaceful. The puzzle sat finished on the rug. The drum rested quietly by the door. Pebble felt proud and calm.

Pebble smiled and whispered, “Today, I used my voice. I asked for what I need. We made a plan together. We shared, like good neighbors in our big world.”

Mama kissed Pebble’s head. “That’s right,” she said. “You practiced self-advocacy, mindfulness, and kind negotiation. That is how we care for ourselves and for others.”

Pebble’s eyes grew sleepy. “Good night, Pico. Good night, Mama.”

“Good night,” they whispered back.

The room glowed, the wind hummed, and Pebble drifted into dreams—dreams of friendly penguins everywhere, taking turns, listening, and using kind words.

The End.
