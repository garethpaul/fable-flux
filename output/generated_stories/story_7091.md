# Bella Bird Saves the Art Room

Bella was a small singing bird with bright blue feathers and a tiny golden beak. She lived near a school and loved to visit the creative art room. The art room smelled like fresh paper and warm crayons. Sunlight danced on jars of paint. Brushes stood like little trees, and colorful paper fluttered like leaves.

“Good morning!” sang Bella as she flew in through the open window. “Tweet-a-dee, tweet-a-do!”

Children from many places sat around big tables. Mei wore a red scarf. Sam had curly hair. Amina’s bracelet jingled softly. Luis hummed a tune from home. Ms. Rosa, the teacher, smiled with paint on her hands.

“Today we make nature collages!” Ms. Rosa said. “We will use safe things, like old paper, empty boxes, and fallen leaves.”

Bella landed on a pencil cup and listened. She loved nature. She loved singing to trees and thanking the river for water. She puffed her feathers with joy.

But then Bella saw something that made her heart thump. A big gray trash bag sat by the door. Inside were pretty scraps of paper, bottle caps, and cardboard rolls. She also saw someone toss a plastic bottle into the wrong bin.

“Oh no,” Bella whispered. “So many things can be used again. So many things can be sorted. If we throw them away, the earth feels sad.”

Bella felt a flutter of worry. She was small. Could a small bird help? She took a tiny breath. “I can try,” she told herself. “I can be brave.”

She hopped to the center of the table and sang a bright, brave tune. “Tweet-a-dee! Friends, may I share a song?”

The children looked up. “Hi, Bella!” said Mei. “What’s your song about?”

“It is about caring for our world,” Bella said. “Listen with your hearts.”

Bella sang softly at first, like raindrops: “Save a little, sort a little, use it one more day.” She pecked the plastic bottle gently and rolled it toward a blue bin. “Plastics here, please,” she chirped.

Sam clapped. “I can do that!” He picked up two more bottles and put them in the blue bin. Clink, clink!

Bella sang a little louder, like wind in the trees: “Paper to the paper place, keep it clean and dry.” She nudged the paper scraps toward a green bin. They rustled like leaves.

Amina smiled. “Paper goes here,” she said, placing the scraps in the green bin. “We use paper kindly.”

Luis held up the cardboard rolls. “And these?” he asked.

Bella hopped closer. “Cardboard to cardboard,” she sang. “Or we can make something new!”

Ms. Rosa nodded. “Yes! We can turn these into art. Let us make instruments from the Earth’s gifts.”

The room buzzed with happy sounds. Tape tore with a rip. Paint lids popped. Brushes swished. The children created shakers with bottle caps and paper tubes. They made drums from boxes and old fabric. Mei added beads from a broken necklace. Amina shared bright patterns from her home. Luis painted waves like the ocean he remembered. Sam drew tall trees from the park.

“Listen,” Ms. Rosa said. “Each of us brings a song from our home. Together, we make a band.”

Bella’s heart felt warm. She sang to keep the rhythm: “Boom-bada, shake-shake, tap-tap, tweet!”

Then Bella saw the big gray trash bag again. It still bulged with mixed things. She felt a pinch of worry. “We must be brave,” she told herself.

Bella flew to the bag and tugged the string with her beak. “Excuse me,” she chirped. “May we check this bag?”

Ms. Rosa knelt beside her. “Of course, little helper.”

Together they opened the bag. The children gathered around. “We can sort this,” said Mei.

They worked as a team:
- Paper to the green bin. Swish.
- Plastic to the blue bin. Clink.
- Cardboard to a box for art. Thump.
- Food scraps to the compost pail. Plop.

“Where does this go?” asked Amina, holding a shiny wrapper.

“Trash,” said Ms. Rosa gently. “But we try to have very little.”

Bella sang a soft thank-you song. “When we sort with care, the earth can breathe.”

Soon the trash bag was small and light. The bins were neat and tidy. The art table was full of new instruments and collages. The room felt bright and calm.

Ms. Rosa smiled. “Bella, you showed courage. You spoke up kindly. You taught us to care for our world.”

Mei wrapped a tiny scarf around the shaker. “This is for you, Bella,” she said. “You helped us learn.”

Bella bowed. “Thank you, friends. We all helped.”

They formed a circle. Luis counted, “Uno, dos, tres!” and they began to play. The drum went boom. The shakers went sha-sha. The brushes tapped like rain. Bella’s song rose clear and happy.

“Save a little, sort a little,
Use it one more day.
Many hearts from many homes
Help the Earth this way.”

When the music ended, the room felt peaceful. The sun painted warm stripes on the floor. The air smelled like crayons and orange peels from the compost pail. Everyone was smiling.

Ms. Rosa said, “What did we learn today?”

Sam said, “Sort our trash.”

Amina said, “Make new things from old.”

Mei said, “Listen to each other.”

Luis said, “Every home can help.”

Bella chirped, “Small voices can make big change.”

They all nodded. The art room was clean, colorful, and kind to the Earth. Bella flew to the window and looked back with joy.

“We did it together,” she sang. “Tweet-a-dee, tweet-a-do. Caring for our world is something we can all do.”

The End.
