# Nutmeg’s Song and the Team Drum

Nutmeg was a small, resourceful squirrel with a fluffy tail and bright, curious eyes. Today, Nutmeg scurried into the musical practice room at the community center. The room smelled like polished wood and lemon soap. Sunlight danced on shiny drums, a sleepy piano, and rows of tiny bells that twinkled like stars. Outside, Nutmeg’s soccer team would soon start their big game, and Nutmeg had promised to bring the team drum.

“Tap-tap, boom-boom!” Nutmeg whispered, patting the big drum with gentle paws. “This is my family’s tradition drum. We play it before every brave day.”

Nutmeg’s grandma had taught a special song. “We share this song to share love,” Grandma had told Nutmeg. “We share it to be brave together.” Nutmeg hummed the tune: “Tum-tum, ta-da, tum-tum!” It sounded warm and cozy, like a soft hug.

Just then, Coach Rabbit peeked in. “Nutmeg, the team is waiting,” Coach said. “Are we ready for our pre-game cheer?”

Nutmeg stood tall. “Yes! I have our tradition drum. We will share our family song. It will help us be brave and ready.”

But when Nutmeg reached for the drumstick, it wasn’t there. Nutmeg checked the floor, the chair, and the drum case. No drumstick.

“Oh no,” Nutmeg whispered. “The stick is missing.” Nutmeg’s tail drooped. The team needed the drum. The old song needed the drum. Nutmeg’s heart thumped, fast like little feet.

Nutmeg took a deep breath. “I can be brave. I can prepare. I can find a way.” Nutmeg looked around the musical practice room. The piano keys were black and white like little teeth. The bells lined up like silver fish. A box of extra music things sat on a shelf.

Nutmeg opened the box. “Ribbons, felt pads, and a rubber ball,” Nutmeg said. “No stick.” Nutmeg checked under a music stand. A dust bunny tickled Nutmeg’s nose. “Ah-choo!”

A soft voice came from behind a drum. “Are you okay?” It was Penny the Porcupine, who loved the triangle. She held a shiny triangle and a metal beater.

“I’m okay,” Nutmeg said. “But I lost the drumstick, and our team needs the drum for our tradition song.”

Penny nodded. “Traditions are important. They help us feel together. You can borrow my triangle beater!”

Nutmeg tried the beater. Tap. Tap. Tap. “It makes a good sound,” Nutmeg said, “but the drum still needs a soft boom.”

Nutmeg remembered Grandma’s words: “Prepare with care.” Nutmeg searched again and found two wooden rhythm sticks. “These are too hard,” Nutmeg said. Then Nutmeg spotted a small sock in the lost-and-found basket. It was striped blue and green and smelled like clean soap.

“I have an idea!” Nutmeg said. “Resourceful time!” Nutmeg wrapped the sock around one rhythm stick and tied it with a ribbon. The head looked soft and round, like a little cloud.

“Let’s test it,” Penny said.

Nutmeg tapped the drum. “Boom.” The sound was warm. Nutmeg tapped again. “Boom, boom.” The room seemed to smile.

Coach Rabbit clapped. “Great preparation, Nutmeg! You solved the problem.”

But Nutmeg still felt a wiggly worry. “Is it okay to share my family song with the team?” Nutmeg asked softly. “It is special.”

Penny tilted her head. “You can ask your heart,” she said. “And remember what your grandma said.”

Nutmeg closed eyes and listened inside. The song felt like a blanket shared on a chilly day. Nutmeg felt brave. Nutmeg felt ready. Nutmeg nodded. “Sharing a tradition can make us strong together.”

Nutmeg carried the drum outside to the soccer field. The grass was cool and smelled sweet. The team—Fox, Duck, Bear, and Bunny—gathered in a circle. Their cleats clicked and their jerseys rustled.

“What’s that drum?” Duck asked.

“It is my family’s tradition drum,” Nutmeg said. “We play this song to share courage, care, and joy. I want to share it with you, too. Is that okay?”

The team smiled. “Yes!” they cheered.

Nutmeg lifted the soft-headed stick and began: “Tum-tum, ta-da, tum-tum.” The drum boomed like a friendly thunder. Penny tinged the triangle, bright and clear: “Ting!”

The team joined in. Fox hummed. Bear swayed. Bunny patted paws. Duck sang, “We are brave, we are kind, we are ready!” The sound wrapped around them like a big warm scarf.

Nutmeg felt proud. Nutmeg had prepared. Nutmeg had shared the tradition. The team felt brave together.

They finished the song with a soft “boom.” Coach Rabbit smiled. “Team, that was wonderful. Thank you, Nutmeg, for sharing your tradition and for your brave heart. Now let’s play!”

During the game, Nutmeg dribbled the ball, tap-tap across the grass. Fox passed, Duck kicked, and Bear blocked with gentle paws. They worked as a team, remembering the song. When they felt nervous, they hummed, “Tum-tum,” inside their heads and felt brave again.

After the game, the team gathered in the musical practice room. The air was warm, and the drum shone softly. Nutmeg stroked it with love.

Coach said, “What did we learn today?”

Nutmeg smiled. “We learned that sharing a tradition can bring us together. We learned to prepare and solve problems. We learned that being brave can be gentle, like a drum boom that says, ‘You can do it.’”

The team nodded. Penny said, “And we learned that a sock can save the day!”

Everyone laughed. Nutmeg’s tail curled happily. “Next time,” Nutmeg said, “we will share the song again. We will prepare again. We will be brave again.”

The room seemed to hum with joy. The drum waited, ready to boom with love.

The End.
