# Melody's Mindful Morning

In a cozy nursery with soft yellow walls and fluffy white clouds painted on the ceiling, lived a little singing bird named Melody. Her feathers sparkled like sunshine, and she had the most beautiful voice in the whole house.

Every morning, Melody would wake up and immediately start singing her loudest, happiest songs. "Tweet-tweet-TWEET! La-la-LA!" she would chirp at the top of her lungs, bouncing excitedly on her perch.

But there was a problem. Baby Emma, who slept in the crib below, was still trying to sleep. When Melody sang her loud morning songs, Baby Emma would wake up crying. "Waaah! Waaah!" Baby Emma would sob, rubbing her sleepy eyes.

Melody didn't understand why everyone seemed upset. She loved singing! It made her feel happy and free.

One morning, Mama came into the nursery looking very tired. Her hair was messy, and she had big yawns. "Oh, Melody," she said gently, "your singing is beautiful, but Baby Emma needs her sleep to grow big and strong."

Melody tilted her little head. "But singing makes me happy!" she chirped softly.

"I know, sweet bird," Mama said, stroking Melody's soft feathers. "Let me teach you something special called mindfulness. It means paying attention to what's happening around you and thinking about others' feelings too."

Mama sat in the rocking chair and spoke in a calm, quiet voice. "Close your eyes, Melody. Take a slow, deep breath. Can you hear the gentle sounds around you?"

Melody closed her bright little eyes and listened carefully. She could hear the soft tick-tock of the clock, the gentle whoosh of air from the window, and Baby Emma's peaceful breathing as she slept.

"Now," whispered Mama, "can you feel how quiet and peaceful the room is right now?"

Melody nodded. The nursery felt warm and cozy, like a soft hug. She noticed how the morning sunlight made gentle patterns on the wall and how the mobile above Baby Emma's crib turned slowly and silently.

"When we're mindful," Mama explained, "we notice how our actions affect others. Your loud singing wakes Baby Emma when she needs to sleep. But there are perfect times for your beautiful songs too."

Melody opened her eyes and looked at sleeping Baby Emma. The baby looked so peaceful with her tiny hands curled up and a little smile on her face.

"I want Baby Emma to feel happy too," Melody whispered.

From that day on, Melody practiced being mindful every morning. She would wake up, take three slow, deep breaths, and listen to the sounds around her. If Baby Emma was still sleeping, Melody would sing very softly or wait until the baby woke up naturally.

When Baby Emma was awake and happy, Melody would sing her most joyful songs. Baby Emma would giggle and reach her little hands toward Melody, clapping along to the music.

"Tweet-tweet-tweet," Melody would sing gently, and Baby Emma would coo back, "Goo-goo-gaa!"

Mama was so proud. "You're being very mindful and kind, Melody. You're thinking about others while still doing what you love."

Melody learned that being mindful meant taking time to notice, breathe, and think about others. She could still sing her beautiful songs, but now she chose the right times to share her gift. The nursery became an even happier place where everyone felt respected and loved.

The End.
