# Bella Bird's Laundry Day Song

Bella was a bright yellow bird who loved to sing. Every morning, she would perch on the windowsill of the cozy laundry room and watch the family get ready for their day.

"Tweet, tweet, what a lovely day!" Bella sang as the warm sunshine streamed through the window.

But today, something was different. The laundry room was very messy! Dirty clothes were scattered all over the floor like colorful puddles. Clean clothes sat in wrinkled piles on the counter. The washing machine was full of wet clothes that needed to be hung up to dry.

"Oh my!" chirped Bella. "This room needs some help!"

Just then, little Emma walked into the room with her mom. Emma was only four years old, with curly brown hair and a big smile.

"Mommy, there are so many clothes everywhere!" Emma said, looking around with wide eyes.

"Yes, sweetie," said Mom with a tired sigh. "We have lots of laundry to do today. It's a big job for just one person."

Bella fluttered down from her perch. "Tweet, tweet! I have an idea!" she sang. "Chores are more fun when we work together!"

Emma giggled. "Did you hear that, Mommy? The pretty bird wants to help us!"

Mom smiled. "Well then, let's all work as a team! Emma, can you help me sort the clothes by colors?"

"Yes!" Emma clapped her hands excitedly.

Bella began to sing a cheerful sorting song: "Red with red, and blue with blue! Yellow clothes go together too! Sorting colors is what we do, la-la-la!"

Emma danced along as she picked up red shirts and put them in one pile. The blue jeans went in another pile. Soon, all the clothes were organized by colors.

"Look how neat it looks now!" Emma said proudly.

"Great job!" said Mom. "Now let's put the dark colors in the washing machine first."

Bella sang another helpful tune: "Wash the darks, then wash the lights! Keep them separate, that's just right! Clean and fresh, oh what a sight, tra-la-la!"

As they worked, Bella noticed that Emma was doing her fair share of the work. She wasn't letting Mom do everything by herself.

"You're being such a good helper, Emma," chirped Bella. "When everyone helps with chores, the work gets done faster and it's more fun too!"

Emma beamed with pride as she helped move the wet clothes from the washer to the dryer. The clothes felt heavy and damp in her small hands, but she was determined to help.

"One, two, three!" counted Emma as she dropped each piece of clothing into the dryer.

Mom added the fabric softener that smelled like fresh flowers. "Mmm, our clothes will smell so nice!" she said.

While the clothes tumbled and dried, they folded the clean clothes together. Bella sang a folding song: "Fold them neat, fold them tight! Every shirt looks just right! Socks in pairs, what a sight, tra-la-la!"

Emma carefully folded her small t-shirts, making sure the edges matched just like Mom showed her.

When they finished, the laundry room sparkled! Everything was clean, organized, and put away in its proper place.

"We did it!" cheered Emma, spinning around happily.

"Thank you, Bella Bird, for making chores so much fun with your songs!" said Mom.

Bella puffed out her yellow chest proudly. "When we all work together and share the chores fairly, everything gets done with love and care!" she sang.

From that day on, Emma always remembered Bella's lesson: chores are easier and more fun when everyone helps, and keeping things organized makes life better for the whole family.

The End.
