# Ruby Rabbit's Big Garden Project

Ruby Rabbit hopped into the old garden shed. The wooden walls smelled like fresh dirt and flowers. Sunlight peeked through the dusty window, making everything glow.

"Today I'm going to build the best carrot garden ever!" Ruby announced to herself. She looked around at all the gardening tools scattered everywhere. There were shovels, rakes, watering cans, and seed packets all mixed up.

Ruby's nose twitched with excitement. She grabbed a big shovel and started digging right in the middle of the shed floor.

"Wait, wait!" called a wise old voice. It was Oliver Owl, perched on a shelf. "Ruby, what are you doing?"

"I'm planting carrots!" Ruby said proudly, dirt flying everywhere.

Oliver hooted softly. "But dear rabbit, you can't plant carrots inside the shed. And you need to follow the directions first."

Ruby stopped digging. Her ears drooped a little. "Directions? But I want to start now!"

"Let me help you," Oliver said kindly. He pointed his wing at a colorful packet. "See that seed packet? It has step-by-step pictures that show you exactly what to do."

Ruby picked up the packet. The pictures showed: first, find a sunny spot outside. Second, dig holes in the dirt. Third, plant the seeds. Fourth, water gently. Fifth, wait patiently.

"Oh!" Ruby's eyes grew wide. "I need to go outside first!"

"That's right," Oliver nodded. "And you'll need the right tools in the right order."

Ruby looked at the messy shed. "But how do I know which tools to use when?"

Oliver flew down and perched on a small garden cart. "Let's make a plan together. What does step one need?"

Ruby studied the pictures carefully. "A shovel for digging!"

"Perfect! Put the shovel in the cart first."

Ruby carefully placed the shovel in the cart. "What's next?"

"Step three shows watering. What tool do you need?"

"The watering can!" Ruby hopped excitedly and added it to the cart.

"And step five says to mark where you planted. What could help with that?"

Ruby looked around and spotted some small wooden signs. "These markers!"

Soon, Ruby's cart was organized with everything she needed, in the right order. Her whiskers twitched happily.

"Now I'm ready!" she announced.

Ruby followed each step carefully. She found a sunny spot outside the shed. She dug small holes just like the picture showed. She planted each seed gently. She watered them with just the right amount. Finally, she put up markers to remember where each row was.

"I did it!" Ruby cheered, brushing dirt off her paws.

Oliver flew over and smiled. "You followed every direction perfectly. And look how organized your garden is!"

Ruby's garden had neat, straight rows. Each area was clearly marked. Everything looked perfect.

"Following directions made everything so much easier," Ruby realized. "And now I know my carrots will grow in the best way possible!"

"That's the magic of following directions," Oliver said. "When you take time to read and follow each step, your projects turn out wonderful."

Ruby hugged the seed packet. "Next time, I'll always read the directions first. Then I'll gather my tools in the right order. It's like having a treasure map!"

From that day on, Ruby always followed directions carefully. Her garden grew the most beautiful carrots in the whole neighborhood, and she became known as the best little gardener around.

The End.
