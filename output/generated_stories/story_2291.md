# Nutkin's Honest Choice

Nutkin the squirrel loved exploring new places. Today, his fluffy tail twitched with excitement as he discovered a warm, cozy room filled with interesting machines that hummed and bubbled.

"What a wonderful place!" Nutkin squeaked, his tiny paws patting the smooth washing machine. The laundry room smelled like fresh soap and clean towels.

As Nutkin scampered around, he noticed something shiny on the floor near the dryer. It was a beautiful golden button that sparkled in the light.

"Oh my!" Nutkin gasped, picking up the button with his small paws. "This is the prettiest thing I've ever seen!"

The button felt smooth and warm. Nutkin's whiskers twitched as he imagined how wonderful it would look in his tree house. No one was around to see him take it.

But then Nutkin heard a sad voice from the next room.

"Oh no," sighed Mrs. Chen, the kind lady who lived in the house. "I can't find the special button from my grandmother's coat. It must have fallen off when I was doing laundry."

Nutkin's ears perked up. His little heart began to beat faster. The button in his paws suddenly felt very heavy.

"This button belongs to Mrs. Chen," Nutkin whispered to himself. "But it's so pretty, and she doesn't know I have it."

Nutkin sat on a pile of soft towels, thinking hard. His mama had always taught him that being honest was the right thing to do, even when it was difficult.

"What should I do?" he asked himself, looking at the golden button.

Then Nutkin remembered how sad Mrs. Chen sounded. The button wasn't just pretty – it was special because it came from someone she loved.

Taking a deep breath, Nutkin made his choice. He would be brave and do the right thing.

"Mrs. Chen!" Nutkin called out in his loudest, most assertive squirrel voice. "Mrs. Chen, I found something!"

Mrs. Chen appeared in the doorway, looking surprised to see a little squirrel in her laundry room.

"Oh my goodness!" she exclaimed. "A talking squirrel!"

Nutkin held up the golden button with both paws. "I found this on the floor. I think it belongs to you. It fell from your grandmother's coat."

Mrs. Chen's eyes filled with happy tears. "Oh, you wonderful little squirrel! This button is so precious to me. It's the only thing I have left from my grandmother's favorite coat."

She gently took the button from Nutkin's paws. "Thank you for being so honest. You could have kept it, but you chose to do the right thing."

Nutkin's chest puffed up with pride. Doing the honest thing felt much better than keeping something that wasn't his.

"You know what?" Mrs. Chen said with a smile. "I have some delicious nuts in my kitchen. Would you like some as a thank-you gift?"

"That would be wonderful!" Nutkin chirped happily.

As Mrs. Chen led him to the kitchen, Nutkin felt warm and proud inside. He had been honest, even when it was hard. He had spoken up assertively to help someone. And he had taken care of something precious that belonged to another person.

From that day on, whenever Nutkin faced a difficult choice, he remembered how good it felt to be honest and helpful.

The End.
