# Gertie's Colorful Heart

Gertie the giraffe loved the creative art room at the Rainbow School. Her long neck helped her reach the highest shelves where the sparkly paints lived. The room smelled like crayons and glue, and colorful pictures covered every wall.

Today, Gertie wanted to paint a picture of her favorite tree back home in Africa. She stretched her neck up high to get the green paint. "Perfect!" she said, her brown spots wiggling with excitement.

But when Gertie looked around, she saw something that made her heart feel heavy. Little Benny Bunny sat in the corner with tears in his fuzzy eyes. His paper was empty, and his paws were folded sadly.

"What's wrong, Benny?" Gertie asked gently, bending her long neck down to his level.

"I wanted to paint a carrot garden," Benny sniffled. "But all the orange paint is gone, and without orange, my carrots won't look real."

Gertie looked at her palette. She had lots of orange paint – more than she needed for her tree. Her first thought was to keep it all for herself, just in case. But then she remembered what her mama always said: "A kind heart is the most beautiful color of all."

"I have orange paint!" Gertie said. "Would you like to share with me?"

Benny's ears perked up. "Really? But don't you need it?"

Gertie thought for a moment. "I only need a tiny bit for the oranges on my tree. You can have the rest!"

As Gertie squeezed orange paint onto Benny's palette, she noticed Maya Mouse watching from nearby. Maya's whiskers twitched nervously.

"Maya, do you need something too?" Gertie asked.

"Well," Maya squeaked quietly, "I wanted to paint my cheese collection, but I need yellow paint for the cheese and orange for the cheddar."

Now Gertie had a problem. She only had enough orange for one friend. She tapped her hoof thoughtfully on the colorful floor.

"I have an idea!" Gertie exclaimed. "Benny, what if we mix some of your orange with white to make a peachy color for some of your carrots? Then Maya could use the extra orange for her cheddar cheese!"

Benny's nose twitched as he thought. "That sounds wonderful! Carrots come in different colors anyway!"

Maya clapped her tiny paws. "And I can share my yellow with both of you!"

Soon, all three friends were painting together. Gertie's tree had orange fruits and yellow sunshine. Benny's garden had orange carrots, peachy carrots, and yellow flowers. Maya's cheese collection sparkled with golden yellows and bright oranges.

"Look how beautiful our paintings are!" said Gertie, stepping back to admire their work.

"They're even more beautiful because we made them together," added Benny.

"And because we shared," whispered Maya with a big smile.

When their teacher, Mrs. Owl, saw the paintings, her feathers fluffed with pride. "These are magnificent! I can see that you used your hearts as much as your brushes."

Gertie felt warm and happy inside. Her tree painting was lovely, but the best part was seeing her friends' joy. She learned that sharing didn't make her painting worse – it made everything better.

As they cleaned their brushes in the sink, Gertie said, "Tomorrow, let's paint a picture together – all three of us on one big paper!"

"Yes!" cheered Benny and Maya.

Gertie's heart felt as colorful as a rainbow. She discovered that compassion was like paint – the more you share it, the more beautiful the world becomes.

The End.
