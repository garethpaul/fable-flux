# Polly's Green Adventure

Polly the parrot loved riding in the family car with her friend Emma and Emma's mom. Her bright green and red feathers sparkled in the sunshine streaming through the windows. Today felt extra special because they were driving to the park for a picnic.

"Look, look!" squawked Polly, pressing her orange beak against the cool glass window. "So many trees and flowers!"

Emma giggled. "Yes, Polly! The trees are so green and pretty."

But as they drove further, Polly noticed something that made her colorful feathers droop. Pieces of trash were scattered along the roadside - plastic bottles, candy wrappers, and old newspapers fluttering in the wind.

"Oh no!" chirped Polly sadly. "The pretty earth looks messy. Why is there trash everywhere?"

Emma's mom looked in the rearview mirror at the worried parrot. "Sometimes people forget to put their trash in the right place, Polly. It makes our beautiful world dirty."

Polly's bright eyes grew wide. "That's not good for the trees and animals! We need to help!"

"You're absolutely right, Polly," said Emma's mom with a warm smile. "What do you think we could do?"

Polly flapped her wings excitedly, thinking hard. Suddenly, her eyes lit up like tiny stars. "I have an idea! Let's be Earth helpers!"

When they reached the park, instead of just having their picnic, Polly had a wonderful plan. She spoke gently to Emma and her mom, using her sweetest voice.

"Please, can we pick up the trash first? Then our picnic will be even more beautiful!"

Emma clapped her hands. "Yes! Let's clean up together!"

Emma's mom found some gloves and trash bags in the car. Polly couldn't hold the bags with her wings, but she became the best trash-spotter ever! She flew from spot to spot, calling out in her cheerful voice.

"Here's a bottle! There's a wrapper! Oh, look - a can hiding under that bush!"

Emma and her mom followed Polly around, picking up every piece of litter she found. Other families at the park saw what they were doing and wanted to help too.

"What a smart parrot!" said one dad. "Can we join your cleanup?"

Soon, five families were working together, making the park sparkle clean. Polly felt so proud watching everyone work as a team. The grass looked greener, the flowers seemed brighter, and the birds in the trees chirped happily.

After their cleanup, they finally had their picnic. The sandwiches tasted extra delicious, and the lemonade was extra sweet. Polly perched on Emma's shoulder, preening her feathers in the warm sunshine.

"Thank you for helping me take care of our Earth," Polly said softly to Emma. "When we work together and talk kindly to each other, we can solve big problems!"

Emma's mom gave Polly a gentle pat. "You taught us something important today, Polly. Taking care of our environment is everyone's job."

As they drove home in the car, Polly looked out at the clean park through the window. She felt warm and happy inside, knowing that by speaking up peacefully and volunteering to help, they had made their world a little bit better.

"Tomorrow, can we help clean up another place?" asked Polly hopefully.

"Of course!" laughed Emma. "We're Earth helpers now!"

The End.
