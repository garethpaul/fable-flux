# Ollie's Soccer Ball Surprise

Ollie the otter bounced excitedly in his car seat as Mom drove to the park. His fluffy brown fur wiggled with each bump in the road. Today was soccer practice, and Ollie couldn't wait to play with his friends!

"Are we there yet, Mom?" Ollie asked, pressing his little nose against the cool window. He could see trees whooshing by and birds flying in the bright blue sky.

"Almost there, sweetie," Mom said with a smile. "Remember to listen to Coach Bear and have fun!"

Ollie nodded eagerly. He loved kicking the black and white soccer ball with his webbed feet. In the back seat next to him sat his special water bottle and his favorite orange soccer ball that squeaked when he squeezed it.

As they turned the corner, Ollie got so excited that he started bouncing even more. "Soccer! Soccer! Soccer!" he chanted, pumping his little paws in the air.

But then - BONK! His elbow accidentally hit his water bottle, and it tumbled right onto Mom's head in the front seat.

"Ouch!" Mom said, rubbing her head where the water bottle had bumped her.

Ollie's eyes grew wide. The car felt very quiet all of a sudden. Mom's smile had turned into a surprised frown, and Ollie's tummy felt funny - like he had eaten too many fish crackers.

"Oh no," Ollie whispered, his whiskers drooping. He hadn't meant to hurt Mom. He was just so excited about soccer that he forgot to be careful in the car.

Mom pulled safely into the parking lot and turned around. Ollie could see that her forehead was a little red where the water bottle had hit.

"Mom, I... I..." Ollie started, but the words felt stuck in his throat like peanut butter.

"What is it, Ollie?" Mom asked gently.

Ollie took a deep breath. "I'm sorry, Mom. I didn't mean to hit you with my water bottle. I was just so excited about soccer that I forgot to sit still. Are you hurt?"

Mom's face softened into a warm smile. "Thank you for saying sorry, Ollie. That was very brave of you. My head feels a little sore, but I'm okay."

"Can I help make it better?" Ollie asked, unbuckling his seatbelt carefully this time.

"That would be wonderful," Mom said.

Ollie gently patted Mom's forehead with his soft paw, just like she did when he got hurt. "Better?" he asked hopefully.

"Much better," Mom said, giving him a big hug that smelled like her lavender perfume. "And I'm proud of you for apologizing. That shows you're growing up to be a kind and responsible otter."

Ollie felt the funny feeling in his tummy disappear, replaced by warm, happy bubbles. "I'll try to remember to be more careful, even when I'm excited."

"That's all anyone can ask for," Mom said. "Now, are you ready for soccer practice?"

"Yes!" Ollie cheered, but this time he kept his paws in his lap. "And Mom? Thank you for forgiving me."

"I love you, Ollie," Mom said as they walked toward the field together, paw in paw.

At practice, Ollie told Coach Bear about what happened. "Good job apologizing," Coach Bear said. "That's just as important as scoring goals!"

Ollie smiled proudly. He had learned that saying sorry when you make a mistake helps everyone feel better.

The End.
