# Sunny Seeds and the Backyard Helpers

Sunny the squirrel woke up to warm light on his whiskers. The sun peeked over a tall fence. Birds sang tiny songs. A soft breeze smelled like grass and flowers. “Good morning, sunny backyard!” Sunny chirped. He flicked his fluffy tail. He was a resourceful squirrel. He liked to fix things and help friends.

Sunny scampered along the fence. He looked down. “Oh no,” he whispered. The yard had little bits of paper and a shiny can by the birdbath. A plastic bag hugged a bush. A long string was stuck in the grass like a snake. “This backyard is home,” Sunny said. “We must take care of it.”

A bee buzzed near Sunny’s ear. “Bzz, careful,” said the bee. “The bag scared my flowers.” The bee sounded worried.

“I will help,” said Sunny. He remembered three rules. “Care for our home. Be patient. Be safe.” Sunny touched the string with one paw and pulled back. “Hmm,” he said. “This looks tricky. I will not rush.”

He ran to the oak tree where Owl liked to nap. The bark felt rough under his paws. “Owl,” Sunny called softly, “may I ask for help?”

Owl blinked slow, kind eyes. “Hoo-hoo, what is the plan, little friend?”

“The yard is messy,” said Sunny. “I want to clean it. But I must be safe. Some things might be sharp.”

Owl nodded. “Good thinking. Use gentle paws. Watch with careful eyes. Be patient like a slow breeze.”

Sunny smiled. “Thank you, Owl.” He took a deep breath. He let it out slowly. He felt brave.

He went first to the plastic bag. It flapped and crinkled. “Hello, bag,” Sunny said kindly. “You do not belong in the bush. Come with me.” He stepped on one corner to stop the wind. The bag smelled a little like soap. Rustle, rustle. Sunny folded it with careful paws. He did not bite it. He remembered safety. He tucked the bag under a rock so it would not fly away.

Next, Sunny saw the shiny can. A robin hopped close. “Chirp-chirp! That can rolls and bumps my nest,” said the robin.

Sunny watched the can. It shone bright in the sun. He touched it with a stick first. Tap, tap. It rolled a little. “I will not use my teeth,” said Sunny. He pushed it with the stick to the side by the fence. “There you go, can. We will find your home later.”

Then Sunny found the long string. It wiggled in the grass. “This could tangle tails,” he said. He could feel his heart beat fast. He stopped. He sat down. “Be patient.” He waited and watched. A ladybug climbed onto a tiny leaf. The breeze made a whisper sound. Sunny felt calm again.

“Okay,” he said. “I will go slow.” He held one end of the string in his paw. He pulled gently. The string slid free, soft like a noodle. “Got you,” he giggled. He coiled it like a little circle and placed it with the can.

“Bzz, thank you,” said the bee, landing on a yellow flower. “Now I can visit my blossoms.”

“Chirp-chirp, thank you,” said the robin, fluffing her feathers.

Sunny looked at the last little bits of paper. They were light like feathers. He scooped them into a leaf bucket—just a big leaf he folded into a cup. Swish, swish. He carried the leaf bucket to the fence corner. There was a small bin there, left by the people who lived in the house. It had a picture of arrows in a circle. Sunny remembered Owl’s words. “Use careful eyes.” He read the picture with his mind. “This is for things that can be used again.”

He sorted with care:
- Paper pieces into the paper box.
- Shiny can into the metal box.
- Plastic bag into the bag bin.

“I am caring for our home,” Sunny said. His tail swished in a happy S-shape.

Just then, a little chipmunk named Pip peeked out from a flower pot. “Hi, Sunny! Can I help too?”

“Yes,” Sunny said with a grin. “But let’s be safe. No sharp edges. Use your paws, not your teeth.”

Pip nodded. “I will be careful. I will go slow.” Pip gathered tiny leaves that had blown into the birdbath. Plip-plop, the water made soft sounds. Pip hummed. Sunny hummed too. Work felt like a song.

When they were done, the backyard looked bright and clean. The air smelled like mint and warm dirt. The grass glittered with little drops of water. The birds sang louder. The bee danced.

The people in the house opened the door. A child clapped. “Look, the yard is tidy!” The child placed a small sign by the fence. It had a picture of a heart hugging the earth. “Please keep our backyard clean,” the sign said.

Sunny read the picture and felt proud. “We did it,” he told Pip. “We cared for our home. We were patient. We were safe.” He patted the oak tree. “Thank you for shade,” he whispered.

Owl hooted softly from a branch. “Hoo-hoo, good helpers.”

Sunny curled his tail and smiled. “Little paws can do big care,” he said. “We can help every day.”

And every day after that, Sunny checked the sunny backyard. He went slow, stayed safe, and cared for the earth. He taught Pip and the birds a gentle tune: “Take your time, keep it clean, work with care, kind and keen.”

The lesson was clear in Sunny’s bright eyes: when we are patient and safe, we can care for our world, one small backyard at a time.

The End.
