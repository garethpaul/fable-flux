# Leo Prepares the Roaring Story

In a cozy study corner, soft and warm like sunshine, a brave little lion cub named Leo sat on a small cushion. The cushion was bright red and squishy. The desk smelled like clean paper and lemon wood. A tiny lamp made a gentle golden glow. Leo’s tail swished with a little thump-thump on the rug.

Tonight was Family Story Night. Grandma Lala would visit and tell the old lion family roar-story, a special tradition. This time, Grandma asked Leo to share a new part too. Leo felt brave, but also a bit wiggly inside.

“I want to do a good job,” Leo whispered. “But how do I get ready?”

Mama Lion padded in with quiet paws. She set down a basket of things: a little bell, a soft scarf, colored cards, and a shiny pencil. “Preparation helps us feel brave,” Mama said, smiling. “Let’s make a plan in your study corner.”

Leo’s ears perked up. “A plan?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Step by step. First, we think. Then, we practice. Then, we check.”

Leo took a deep breath. He could smell warm tea and honey. He felt a bubble of courage. “Okay!”

Step one: Think. Leo sat tall. “What is our tradition?” he asked.

Mama nodded. “Our tradition is sharing a roar-story about kindness and courage.”

Leo tapped the cards with his paw. “I will tell about the time I helped Baby Bird find her nest. That shows kindness and bravery!”

Mama clapped softly. “Good idea.”

Step two: Practice. Mama tied the soft scarf around Leo’s neck. It felt snug and safe. “This scarf is from Grandma Lala,” she said. “It reminds us of our family.”

Leo rang the tiny bell. Ding! His whiskers tingled. “I will begin with the bell,” he said. “It will tell everyone, ‘Listen, please.’”

He stood behind the desk. The golden lamp made his fur glow. He held a card with a picture he had drawn: a little bird, a big tree, and a friendly lion cub. “Hello,” he practiced, “my name is Leo. I have a brave story to share.”

Mama smiled. “Use your big voice. Use your calm breath.”

Leo puffed his chest. “Hello, my name is Leo. I have a brave story to share.” His voice sounded stronger. He felt proud.

Step three: Check. “Do I know what to do if I forget?” Leo asked.

Mama nodded. “Yes. If you forget, look at your picture cards. Take a deep breath. Smile. You are ready.”

Leo practiced again. He told how the wind was whoosh-whoosh and how the tree smelled like pine. He told how Baby Bird chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” He told how he felt a thump-thump in his chest but kept going. He practiced saying, “I asked for help,” and, “I took careful steps.” He practiced ending with, “We can be brave and kind when we prepare.”

Then Leo packed his story kit: bell, scarf, picture cards, and pencil. He put them in a small blue bag. The zipper made a soft zzzip! He checked the bag twice. “Prepared!” he said.

Soon, Grandma Lala arrived. Her fur was silver, and her eyes were warm. She brought a drum that smelled like cedar. “Hello, my brave cub,” she purred. “Are you ready to share our tradition?”

Leo felt a flutter in his belly. He looked at his bag. He looked at Mama. He touched the scarf at his neck. “Yes,” he said. “I prepared.”

They sat in the study corner. The lamp glowed. The room was quiet and safe. Grandma Lala began. “Long ago, our family shared stories to teach courage.” She tapped the drum: dum, dum, dum. “Tonight, Leo will add a new part.”

Leo rang the bell. Ding! He stood up. His paws felt steady on the soft rug. “Hello,” he said. “My name is Leo. I have a brave story to share.”

He showed his first card. “One windy day, I heard, ‘Cheep-cheep!’ It was Baby Bird. She could not find her nest.”

Leo took a slow breath, just like he practiced. “I felt scared,” he said. “But I made a plan.”

He showed the second card. “I asked Mama Bird for help. I looked for the big tree. I walked carefully. I sang, ‘Cheep-cheep, it’s okay.’”

Grandma’s eyes sparkled. Mama nodded. Leo smiled. He felt brave.

He showed the last card. “We found the nest. Baby Bird was safe. I learned that preparation helps me be brave and kind.”

He bowed a little. The scarf brushed his cheek, soft and warm. The room felt cozy and bright. Grandma tapped the drum. Mama clapped. They both cheered, “Hooray for Leo!”

Grandma Lala hugged him. “You honored our tradition,” she said. “You were brave, and you prepared.”

Leo hugged back. He could smell Grandma’s cedar drum and Mama’s honey tea. His heart felt big and light. “Preparation helps me be brave,” he said. “I can share, I can help, and I can try.”

That night, Leo put his cards back in the bag. He set the bell on the desk. He folded the scarf with care. He smiled at his cozy study corner. “I am ready for next time,” he whispered.

The lesson was clear in Leo’s happy heart: When we prepare, we feel brave. When we share our traditions, we grow together. And when we are kind, we shine.

The End.
