# Daisy's Garden Dream

Daisy the gentle deer fawn stood in the sunny backyard. Her soft brown spots glowed in the warm morning light. She sniffed the sweet air with her tiny black nose. Birds chirped happily in the tall oak tree above.

"I want to grow the most beautiful flower garden ever!" Daisy said with excitement. Her little hooves danced on the soft grass. She had watched Mrs. Robin plant colorful flowers all spring. Now Daisy wanted her very own garden.

But Daisy didn't know how to start. She looked around the big yard. There were so many places to choose from. The sunny spot by the fence? The shady area under the tree? Daisy felt confused and a little sad.

"This is too hard," she whispered. Her ears drooped down. "I don't know anything about gardens."

Just then, Benny the brown bunny hopped over. His fluffy tail wiggled as he moved. "Hi Daisy! You look worried. What's wrong?"

"I want to make a garden, but I don't know how," Daisy explained. "It seems impossible."

Benny's whiskers twitched thoughtfully. "My mom always says big dreams need small steps. What's one tiny thing you could do first?"

Daisy tilted her head. She thought hard. "Maybe... pick a spot?"

"Great idea!" Benny cheered. "Let's look around together."

They walked slowly around the yard. Daisy noticed how the sun moved across different areas. She felt the soil with her hooves - some spots were hard, others soft and crumbly.

"This spot gets morning sun but afternoon shade," Daisy observed by the garden fence. "The dirt feels just right - not too wet, not too dry."

"You're being very smart about this," Benny said proudly.

Next, Squeaky the gray squirrel scampered down from the oak tree. "Did someone say garden? I love gardens! But I only like nut trees."

Daisy's heart sank a little. "Oh. I wanted to plant flowers."

"That's okay!" Squeaky chattered. "Different gardens for different friends. Your flower garden will be beautiful here. Maybe I'll plant my nut tree over there by the shed."

Daisy smiled. She was learning that everyone could have different dreams in the same space.

Mrs. Robin flew down with a gentle flutter. "I couldn't help but hear about your garden plan, dear Daisy. Would you like some flower seeds? I have extras."

"Really?" Daisy's eyes sparkled with joy.

Mrs. Robin nodded. "When we share our dreams, wonderful things happen."

Daisy carefully took the tiny seeds in her mouth. They felt smooth and full of promise. She dug small holes with her hooves, just like she had watched Mrs. Robin do. One by one, she planted each seed with care.

"Now what?" Daisy asked.

"Now we wait and water," Mrs. Robin explained. "Gardens take time and patience."

Every morning, Daisy visited her garden spot. She sprinkled water from the nearby birdbath. She talked softly to her seeds, telling them about the sunshine and her hopes.

Benny often joined her. Squeaky would chatter encouragingly from his tree. Mrs. Robin shared gardening tips.

After many days, tiny green shoots poked through the soil. Daisy gasped with delight. "They're growing! My dream is coming true!"

Soon, colorful flowers bloomed - red roses, yellow sunflowers, and purple violets. The garden was more beautiful than Daisy had ever imagined.

"I did it!" Daisy danced around her flowers. "I made my dream real by taking one small step at a time."

Her friends gathered to admire the gorgeous garden. Everyone felt proud and happy.

"See?" Benny said with a warm smile. "Big dreams really do come from small steps."

Daisy nodded, her heart full of joy and accomplishment.

The End.
