# Sunny the Singing Bird and the Quiet Attic Feast

In a quiet attic, where dust danced like tiny stars in the shafts of light, a small singing bird named Sunny lived by a round window. The attic smelled like old wood and warm tea. It was still and calm, except for Sunny’s gentle song.

“Tweet-a-dee, tweet-a-doo,” Sunny sang each morning. Her voice was bright and sweet. She loved to sing for the moon at night and the sun in the morning.

One day, Sunny’s tummy made a funny sound. “Grumble, rumble,” it said. Sunny blinked. “Oh! I am hungry,” she chirped. Sunny pecked at a little bowl near her nest. It had only a few crumbs. The crumbs were dry, and they did not help much.

Sunny tried to sing, but her song felt small. “My voice sounds tired,” she whispered. “Why does my song feel weak?”

She hopped to the window and looked out at the rooftops. The city below was busy. People walked, children laughed, and a baker rolled dough. Sunny pressed her cheek to the cool glass. “Maybe I need good food,” she said. “Food that helps my body and my voice.”

Sunny put on her brave face. “I will find help,” she sang softly. “I will make a plan.”

Sunny fluttered down the attic stairs and peeked into a big room below. It was a community hall. People were setting up a long table under colorful paper flags. The tablecloth was bright and clean. It smelled like apples, warm bread, and spice.

A kind woman with curly hair smiled up at Sunny. “Hello, little singer,” she said. “I am Mira. We are making a community food share. Would you like to help?”

Sunny’s eyes shone. “Tweet! I would love to help. I am also hungry, and my voice feels weak,” Sunny said. “I think I need foods that help me sing.”

Mira nodded. “Food is fuel,” she said. “It helps our bodies grow strong and our hearts feel bright. Today, friends are bringing dishes from many places.”

Soon, people came in with baskets and bowls. A boy in a blue hat carried a bowl of sweet orange carrots. “These are crunchy and good for eyes,” he said.

A grandpa with a kind smile brought a pot of soup. “This is lentil soup,” he said. “Warm and full of strength.”

A girl with a braid placed a plate of rice and beans. “This is from my family’s kitchen,” she said. “It smells like home.”

A baker with flour on her cheeks set down small whole-grain rolls. “Chewy and nutty,” she said. “Good for energy.”

A tall teen held a basket of glossy apples. “Crisp and juicy!” he laughed.

Sunny’s tummy went “grumble, rumble” again. “All these foods look so nice,” she chirped. “But I do not know what to choose.”

Mira crouched down. “We can make a rainbow plate,” she said. “Eating many colors helps your body in many ways.”

Sunny flapped her wings. “A rainbow plate! Yes, please!”

Together they made a small, balanced plate for Sunny: a scoop of lentil soup for warm strength, a little rice and beans for steady energy, a handful of bright carrots for crunch, a piece of a whole-grain roll for fiber, and slices of red apple for sweet juiciness. Mira also poured a cup of cool water. “Water helps your voice,” she added.

Sunny sniffed deeply. The soup smelled cozy, like hugs. The carrots were crisp and sweet. The roll was soft inside and toasty outside. The apple was juicy and bright. Sunny took small bites and sips.

“Yum,” Sunny chirped. “My tummy feels happy. My body feels warm.”

Mira smiled. “Food from many friends can make a strong song.”

But then the door creaked. A gentle gust blew out a string of paper flags. They fluttered down onto the floor and tangled around the legs of the table. Some cups wobbled. A little child looked worried. “Oh no,” she said. “Our table!”

Sunny took a deep breath. “I can help,” she said. “I can sing a steady song. We can work together.”

Sunny hopped up to a beam and began to sing, “Steady, steady, one by one. We will fix it. It can be done.” Her song was clear and bright now. People smiled and moved in rhythm. The boy in the blue hat held the flags up. The grandpa tied new knots. The baker moved the rolls to a safe spot. Mira wiped the table and set the cups again. The little child picked up napkins.

“Tweet-a-dee, we did it!” Sunny chirped when the table stood neat and pretty once more. Everyone clapped. The attic felt warm with joy.

“Sunny,” Mira said, “would you share a song for us all?”

Sunny puffed her feathers and sang a new tune: “Food gives strength, friends give cheer. From many homes, love gathers here.” Her voice rang through the quiet attic, bright and strong like sunshine.

After the song, everyone made their own rainbow plates. They said “please” and “thank you.” They tried new foods from different homes. The room filled with happy chattering and soft crunching and the sweet smell of apples, spices, and warm bread.

Sunny felt proud. She had asked for help, learned about healthy foods, and helped her community too. She looked around at the smiling faces—so many different people, all sharing and caring.

Sunny whispered, “When I eat a rainbow and drink water, my voice is strong. When we help each other, our hearts are strong.”

The lesson was clear: Good food helps our bodies and voices. Sharing with friends helps our whole community. And trying foods from many places helps us learn and love.

Sunny tucked into her cozy attic nest that night, tummy full and heart happy. She hummed a soft bedtime song.

“Tweet-a-dee, tweet-a-doo,” she murmured, and the quiet attic felt like home.

The End.
