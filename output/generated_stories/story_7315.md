# Nutkin's Workshop Adventure

Nutkin the squirrel loved making things with his tiny paws. His favorite place was Grandpa Joe's garage workshop, where colorful tools hung on the walls and wood shavings smelled like pine trees.

"Good morning, Nutkin!" called Grandpa Joe as the little squirrel scampered through the open door. "What would you like to make today?"

Nutkin's fluffy tail twitched with excitement. "Can I make a birdhouse? Please, please, please?"

"Of course!" Grandpa Joe smiled. "But remember our workshop rules. What do we always do first?"

"Ask before touching tools!" Nutkin squeaked proudly.

Just then, Nutkin's friend Chippy the chipmunk bounded in. "Wow! Look at all these shiny hammers!" Chippy reached for a big hammer on the workbench.

"Wait!" Nutkin said gently. "We need to ask Grandpa Joe first. That's the rule."

Chippy's whiskers drooped. "But I want to use it now!"

Grandpa Joe knelt down to their level. "Chippy, these tools can be dangerous if we don't use them properly. Asking first keeps everyone safe. Would you like to help Nutkin make his birdhouse?"

"Really?" Chippy's eyes sparkled. "But whose turn is it first?"

"I was here first," Nutkin said, "so I'll start. Then you can have a turn!"

Grandpa Joe handed Nutkin a small, safe hammer. "This one is just your size." The hammer felt smooth and warm in Nutkin's paws. *Tap, tap, tap* went the hammer as Nutkin carefully nailed two pieces of wood together.

"My turn now?" asked Chippy hopefully.

"Yes!" Nutkin passed the hammer to his friend. "Hold it like this, near the bottom."

Chippy's first nail went in crooked. "Oh no! I messed up!"

"That's okay," Nutkin encouraged. "My first nail was wobbly too. Try again!"

*Tap, tap, tap* - this time Chippy's nail went in straight. "I did it!"

The friends took turns all morning. Nutkin sanded the wood until it felt silky smooth. Chippy painted the birdhouse bright yellow like sunshine. The paint smelled fresh and made their whiskers tickle.

"Now for the roof," said Grandpa Joe, helping them attach the final piece.

When they finished, both friends stepped back to admire their work. The little birdhouse was perfect, with a round door and a cheerful yellow coat.

"Thank you for sharing your turn with me," Chippy said to Nutkin. "And thank you for teaching us, Grandpa Joe!"

"Thank you for letting me use your workshop," added Nutkin. "And thank you, Chippy, for helping make it even more beautiful!"

Grandpa Joe's eyes twinkled. "You both learned something very important today. When we respect each other's space and take turns, we can create wonderful things together."

They hung the birdhouse on a branch outside the workshop. Soon, a little blue bird peeked inside.

"Look!" whispered Nutkin. "Someone already wants to live in our house!"

As they cleaned up the tools together, putting each one back in its special place, Nutkin felt warm and happy inside. He had made something beautiful, shared with a friend, and learned that following rules and taking turns made everything more fun.

"Same time tomorrow?" asked Chippy.

"Absolutely!" grinned Nutkin. "I can't wait to see what we'll create next!"

The End.
