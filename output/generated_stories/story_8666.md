# Paco Plans a Pop-Up Playground

Paco the Parrot loved colors, sounds, and making new things. He was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a red beak. One sunny morning, Paco waddled into the dining room. The room smelled like warm toast and sweet orange juice. The table felt smooth under his feet, and the chairs stood like tall trees.

“Squawk! Today, I will build something!” Paco chirped. “But first, I will make a plan.”

Paco knew that planning helps dreams become real. He flapped his wings with excitement and looked around. “This dining room is my building place,” he said. “I will make a pop-up playground!”

He got a paper and a chunky blue crayon. He drew a big square for the table, small circles for the chairs, and a happy star for his playground. He whispered, “Step one: draw the plan. Step two: gather things. Step three: build. Step four: check safety. Step five: play!”

Paco’s friend, Mia the Mouse, peeked from under a chair. “What are you doing, Paco?”

“I am planning,” Paco said proudly. “Do you want to help?”

Mia’s whiskers twitched. “Yes! I can carry little things.”

Paco nodded. “Thank you! But first, personal rules.” He puffed his feathers, brave and clear. “We only use safe things. We ask before touching other people’s things. We keep space for others. We use gentle bodies and kind words.”

Mia squeaked, “I agree!”

Paco looked at the shelves. There were napkins, soft cloths, paper cups, and big spoons. He pointed with his wing. “Mia, please ask Mama for the paper cups and the napkins. I will use the boxes from the corner.” The boxes smelled like cardboard and sounded crinkly when he touched them.

Mia came back with Mama’s smile and the paper cups. “Mama says yes!”

“Thank you,” Paco said. He checked his list. “Step two: done.”

They started to build. Paco stacked paper cups into short towers. Tap, tap, tap. He put napkins on top like tiny flags. He slid a box near a chair to make a tunnel. Rustle, rustle. He tied a ribbon from one chair to another to make a soft gate. Swoosh.

Suddenly, Timo the Toddler toddled in with sticky jam hands. “Play now!” Timo shouted. He reached for the towers.

Paco took a brave breath. He stood tall, even though his heart thump-thumped. “Stop, please,” he said kindly. “We are still building. We need space. Please wipe hands first.” He pointed to a cloth. “That is my boundary.”

Timo frowned. “I want to play.”

“You will,” Paco said, gentle and firm. “We will be ready soon. You can help by wiping your hands and counting the cups with me.”

Timo looked at the cloth. He looked at Paco’s calm eyes. “Okay.” Swish, swish. His hands were clean. “One, two, three!” he counted with Paco, touching only with one clean finger.

Paco smiled. “Thank you for respecting my boundary. That was brave.”

They worked together. Paco checked his plan. “Step three: build. Step four: safety check.” He wiggled each cup tower. “Is it steady?” Wiggle-wiggle. “Yes.” He tested the ribbon gate. “Is it soft?” Boing-boing. “Yes.” He looked at the tunnel. “Is it big enough?” He poked his head through. “Yes!”

Mia pointed to the table. “What about this space?”

Paco nodded. “We need a quiet corner for resting.” He placed a folded cloth on a chair seat. “Rest spot,” he said softly. He drew a little heart on his plan. “A rest spot is part of planning, too.”

Mama walked in. The room smelled like lemon and fresh bread now. She saw the playground. “What a smart plan,” she said. “Did you ask before using things?”

“Yes, Mama,” Mia squeaked.

“And we set boundaries,” Paco added. “We used clean hands, kind words, and gentle bodies.”

Mama clapped. “Wonderful!”

Paco held up his plan and read. “Step five: play!” He opened the ribbon gate. “Welcome to Paco’s Pop-Up Playground! First, we slide feathers down the napkin ramp.” He shook a feather. It floated like a soft whisper. “Then we crawl through the box tunnel.” Rustle-rustle! “Then we hop around the cup towers.” Hop, hop, hop!

Timo giggled. “This is fun!”

They played carefully. When Timo moved too fast, Paco said, “Slow, please.” When Mia needed space, she said, “I need a little room.” Everyone listened. The room was full of happy sounds—giggles, soft taps, and tiny boings.

After playing, Paco looked at his plan again. He drew a little check mark. “We did it,” he said. “We planned, we built, we respected boundaries, and we were brave.”

Mia leaned on the rest spot with a sigh. “I liked the plan. It helped me know what to do.”

Timo hugged Paco. “Planning makes play better.”

Paco felt warm and proud. “Planning helps our ideas grow strong. Building is safer with a plan. Boundaries keep bodies and feelings safe. And bravery helps us speak up.”

He carefully took down the towers, folded the napkins, and put the cups away. Tap, fold, stack. The dining room felt calm and neat again. Paco wrote one more note on his paper: Plan, Build, Check, Play, Clean Up.

He smiled at his friends. “Tomorrow, we can plan again!”

The End.
