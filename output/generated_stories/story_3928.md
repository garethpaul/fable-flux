# Nutkin's Special Thank You Day

Nutkin the squirrel lived in a cozy tree right outside Mrs. Chen's home office window. Every morning, he would peek through the glass and watch her work at her big wooden desk. The office smelled like fresh coffee and had soft, warm light from a yellow lamp.

One sunny Tuesday morning, Nutkin noticed something wonderful. Mrs. Chen had left a small bowl of mixed nuts on her windowsill! His fluffy tail twitched with excitement.

"Oh my!" squeaked Nutkin. "Those nuts look delicious! I'm so hungry from gathering acorns all morning."

But as he reached for the bowl, Nutkin stopped. He remembered what his mama had taught him: "Always be grateful and think of others, little one."

Nutkin sat back on his tiny paws and thought carefully. "Mrs. Chen must have put these here on purpose. But maybe she meant to share them with other animals too?"

Just then, he heard a soft chirping sound. A little blue jay named Bella landed on the windowsill, looking very tired.

"Hello, Nutkin," Bella said sadly. "I've been flying all morning looking for food for my baby birds. The wind blew down our nest yesterday, and we lost all our stored seeds."

Nutkin's heart felt warm and fuzzy. He knew exactly what to do.

"Bella, look! Mrs. Chen left us this wonderful bowl of nuts. Would you like to share them with me?" Nutkin asked with a big smile.

Bella's eyes sparkled. "Really? You would share with me?"

"Of course!" said Nutkin. "Mama always says that good things taste even better when we share them with friends."

The two friends carefully divided the nuts into equal piles. Nutkin made sure Bella got the biggest cashews since she needed to feed her babies too.

"Thank you so much, Nutkin," chirped Bella. "You're the kindest squirrel I know!"

As they munched their crunchy treats, Nutkin had another idea. "We should thank Mrs. Chen too! She was so thoughtful to leave these for us."

Together, they gathered some pretty yellow leaves and small smooth pebbles. They arranged them in a heart shape on the windowsill, right next to the empty bowl.

When Mrs. Chen came back to her desk, she saw the beautiful leaf-and-pebble heart. She smiled so wide that her eyes crinkled at the corners.

"What a lovely surprise!" she said, opening her window. "Thank you, little friends. This makes my heart happy."

She gently placed a few more nuts in the bowl. "For being so grateful and kind to each other," she whispered.

Nutkin and Bella did a little happy dance on the windowsill. The afternoon sun felt extra warm on their fur and feathers.

"I feel so good inside," said Nutkin, patting his full, happy tummy.

"That's because being grateful and sharing makes our hearts grow bigger," explained Bella wisely.

From that day on, Nutkin always remembered to say "thank you" and share with others. He learned that gratitude isn't just about saying words – it's about showing kindness and thinking of others too.

Every morning, Mrs. Chen would find little thank-you gifts from her animal friends, and every morning, she would smile and share her nuts with grateful hearts.

The End.
