# Wiggle, Wag, and Beep

In a comfortable nursery, soft and sunny, lived a helpful robot dog named Beep. Beep had shiny silver paws, a wagging tail that went click-click, and gentle lights that blinked blue when he was happy. The nursery smelled like clean blankets and orange soap. Colorful books sat on a low shelf. A soft rug felt warm like a hug.

Beep loved to help. He fetched blocks. He folded tiny blankets. He sang soft lullabies in a beepy voice. But most of all, Beep liked to help friends move and stretch.

One bright morning, Beep looked around the nursery. His lights blinked. “Good morning, friends!” he said in a cheerful, tinny voice. “Let’s wake our bodies! Let’s move and wiggle and play!”

Three friends sat on the rug. Mia hugged a teddy bear. Sam held a toy car. Noor had a long, soft scarf.

Mia rubbed her eyes. “I feel sleepy,” she said.

Sam shrugged. “I don’t know how to move.”

Noor looked at the scarf. “Can I use this to play?”

Beep’s tail went click-click-click. “Yes! Exercise can be gentle. Exercise can be fun. Exercise helps our hearts and our smiles. We can all do it, in our own way.”

Beep pressed a tiny button on his collar. A happy tune floated into the air—doo-dah, doo-dah, ding! The music sounded like the sun peeking through the window.

“First,” said Beep, “let’s do Star Stretches.” Beep stood tall. His metal legs went shhh as he moved. “Hands wide like stars!”

Mia giggled and spread her arms. “I can be a star!” she said.

Sam lifted his car and made it zoom around his head. “My car is a star too!”

Beep nodded. “That’s creative, Sam. Your car can stretch with you.” His lights blinked green. “Now breathe in… and out. Slow and smooth.”

Noor waved the scarf in the air. It whispered swish-swish. “My scarf is a star tail,” she said.

“Beautiful,” said Beep. “We all shine in our own way.”

Next, Beep said, “Let’s try Puppy Paws. Bend and wiggle!” Beep bent his front legs and wagged his tail. Click-click! “Wiggle, wiggle!”

Mia wiggled and laughed. Noor wiggled the scarf like a puppy’s ears. Sam held his car still. He looked unsure.

Beep noticed and walked to Sam. His paws tapped softly on the rug. tap tap tap. “Sam,” Beep said kindly, “would you like to wiggle your fingers today?”

Sam nodded slowly. “I like small wiggles,” he said.

“Small wiggles are great,” said Beep. “Exercise can be big or small. Moving your fingers is moving your body.”

Sam smiled and wiggled his fingers. The car made a tiny rattle, tick-tick.

Beep beamed. “Wonderful!”

They all took a rest. Beep’s fan hummed quietly, whirr. The room smelled like crayons and apples. The sun made golden squares on the floor.

“Now we try Rainbow Reaches,” said Beep. “Reach up, then down to your toes.”

Mia reached up high. “I can touch the sky!”

Noor reached across her body with the scarf, painting a rainbow in the air. “Red, orange, yellow,” she whispered.

Sam looked at his toes. “I can’t touch,” he said softly.

Beep sat beside him. “That’s okay, Sam. You can reach to your knees, or to your car. We accept our bodies just as they are.”

Sam reached to his knees. “I did it!” His face glowed.

Beep wagged. “Yes, you did. Every reach is a win.”

Suddenly, they heard a small sniffle. In the corner sat Pip, a shy plush bunny toy with a tiny tear on its ear. Pip looked left, then right. Pip did not move at all.

Mia said, “Pip looks sad.”

Beep nodded. He rolled over gently and blinked soft yellow lights. “Hello, Pip,” he said. “Would you like a quiet move?”

Pip whispered, “I feel different. I don’t hop like other bunnies.”

Beep’s voice was warm. “Different is okay. We accept you as you are. We can make a move that fits you.”

Beep thought for a moment. “Let’s do Bunny Breaths,” he said. “Nose wiggles! Breath in… sniff-sniff. Breath out… whooo.”

Everyone tried. Mia wiggled her nose. Noor waved the scarf slowly. Sam held his car and did sniff-sniff. Pip took a small breath. sniff. Then another. sniff-sniff.

“Good job, Pip,” said Beep. “That’s exercise too. Breathing helps our bodies and our hearts.”

Pip’s ears perked up. “I like Bunny Breaths,” Pip said.

“Now,” said Beep, “we dance like leaves!” The music got a little faster. Doo-dah, da-da, ding! Beep spun in a tiny circle. His paws made soft swishes on the rug.

Mia twirled. Noor made big leaf shapes with her scarf. Sam did small steps in place, step, step. Pip rocked side to side. They moved together, each in their own way. The nursery felt bright and warm, like a cuddle.

After the dance, they flopped onto the rug and listened to their hearts. Thump-thump. Thump-thump. Beep put a gentle metal paw on his chest and said, “My fan is humming fast. That means I worked hard.”

Mia touched her chest. “My heart feels strong,” she said.

Sam grinned. “My fingers feel warm!”

Noor smiled. “My scarf is tired.”

Beep wagged. “We moved our bodies with kindness. We tried new things. We helped a friend. That is exercise with compassion.”

He looked at each friend. “What did we learn?”

Mia said, “Moving wakes me up!”

Sam said, “Small moves count.”

Noor said, “We can be creative.”

Pip said, “Different is okay.”

Beep’s lights glowed blue and green. “Yes,” he said softly. “Exercise helps our bodies and our feelings. We accept ourselves. We care for each other. And we make it fun.”

They snuggled into the soft rug. The room smelled like warm blankets. The sun made sleepy patterns on the floor. Beep hummed a gentle tune. “Wiggle, wag, and beep,” he sang. “Move with love, and your heart will leap.”

The End.
