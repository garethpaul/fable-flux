# Nutkin's Big Voice

Nutkin the squirrel lived in a cozy oak tree right outside Mrs. Chen's home office window. Every morning, he watched her type on her computer while he munched his breakfast acorns. The clickety-clack of the keyboard sounded like a gentle song.

But one day, something terrible happened. Big yellow machines rumbled into the yard with loud VROOOM sounds. Men in hard hats pointed at Nutkin's beautiful tree.

"We need to cut this down to make room for the new garage," said one man.

Nutkin's fluffy tail twitched with worry. "Oh no!" he squeaked. "This is my home! All my winter nuts are buried here!" But the men couldn't hear his tiny voice over the noise of their machines.

Nutkin scampered down and tried jumping up and down. He chattered as loudly as he could. "CHIT-CHIT-CHIT! Please don't cut my tree!" But still, nobody noticed him.

Feeling scared and small, Nutkin sat on a branch and thought hard. His whiskers twitched as he remembered something important. "Mama always said I have a voice that matters," he whispered to himself. "I just need to find the right way to use it."

Suddenly, Nutkin had a brilliant idea! He scurried to Mrs. Chen's office window and began tapping with his tiny paws. TAP-TAP-TAP!

Mrs. Chen looked up from her computer. "Hello there, little friend," she smiled.

Nutkin pressed his paws against the glass and pointed toward his tree. He made big, worried eyes and pointed to the yellow machines. Then he pretended to cry, wiping his eyes with his paws.

"Oh my!" said Mrs. Chen, understanding. "Are you trying to tell me something about your tree?"

Nutkin nodded so hard his whole body wiggled. He ran in circles, then pointed to all the little holes where he'd buried his acorns. He showed her his cozy nest with soft leaves and twigs.

Mrs. Chen's eyes grew wide. "You live in that tree! And you've stored your food there!" She watched as Nutkin showed her three baby squirrels peeking out from the nest. "And you have a family!"

Nutkin clapped his tiny paws together and nodded again.

Mrs. Chen rushed outside to the construction workers. "Stop!" she called. "We need to talk about this tree!"

She explained how the tree was home to Nutkin's family and showed them all the acorn storage spots. "This squirrel found a way to tell me his tree is important. We need to find another solution."

The workers scratched their heads. "Well," said the boss, "we could build the garage on the other side of the house instead."

"Perfect!" said Mrs. Chen.

Nutkin was so happy he did three backflips on his branch! He gathered some of his prettiest acorns and left them on Mrs. Chen's windowsill as a thank-you gift.

From that day on, whenever Nutkin saw Mrs. Chen, he waved with his little paw. She always waved back and said, "Thank you for speaking up, brave Nutkin!"

Nutkin learned that even though he was small, his voice was important. When something mattered to him, he could find creative ways to speak up and ask for help. And Mrs. Chen learned to listen carefully to all the creatures who shared her yard.

The End.
