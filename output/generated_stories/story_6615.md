# Owl Hoots for Healthy Hearts

In a playful living room full of color, a wise old owl named Olive sat on the back of a soft, bouncy couch. The room smelled like fresh oranges and clean soap. Sunlight made golden squares on the fluffy rug. Toys giggled in a pile, and a tiny drum sat near a bright blue ball.

“Good morning, little friends,” Olive Owl hooted gently. “Hoo-hoo, are we ready for a healthy day?”

Two children, Mia and Ben, clapped their hands. “We want to play!” said Mia, twirling. “We want snacks!” said Ben, patting his tummy.

Olive nodded, her soft feathers rustling. “Play is wonderful. Snacks are yummy. But let’s make a plan. A plan helps us grow strong and kind.” Her yellow eyes twinkled. “Today we make a wellness plan!”

Mia’s curls bounced. “What is wellness?”

Olive blinked slowly. “Wellness is feeling good in your body, your mind, and your heart. It is moving, eating healthy foods, resting, and being kind.”

Ben sniffed the air. “I smell oranges.”

“That is a healthy snack,” said Olive. “We can make goals to help us have a healthy day.”

“What is a goal?” asked Mia, tilting her head.

“A goal is a small promise we try to keep,” Olive explained. “Not too big. Not too hard. Just right for you.”

Olive spread her wings like a cozy blanket. “Listen to my three goals for today: Move our bodies. Eat rainbow foods. Help our neighbors.”

Mia and Ben cheered. “We can do that!”

Olive hopped onto the drum with a soft thump. “First, we move! Let’s do gentle stretches.”

They reached to the sky. They touched their toes. Their feet felt warm on the rug. The blue ball rolled and bumped Ben’s toe. “Boop!” he giggled.

“Now Owl Jumps,” Olive said, flapping. “Bend… and hop!” They hopped like little owls. The couch squeaked. The rug felt tickly. Mia’s hands were happy and clapped. Ben’s cheeks got rosy.

“Water sip,” Olive reminded, pointing to cups. “When we move, we drink.”

They sipped cool water. It tasted like a tiny river. “Ahh,” said Mia. “My body feels awake.”

“Goal one: check!” Olive hooted softly.

“Snack time?” Ben asked.

“Yes. Rainbow time,” said Olive, gliding to the table. A basket held orange slices, green cucumber circles, red strawberries, yellow banana coins, and purple grapes.

“The rainbow is pretty,” Mia whispered.

“We eat many colors to help our bodies,” Olive said. “Orange for strong eyes. Green for power. Red for hearts. Yellow for happy energy. Purple for calm.”

They made silly fruit faces on plates. Two grape eyes. A banana smile. A strawberry nose. They crunched and munched. The cucumbers were cool and crisp. The oranges were juicy and sweet. Juice dripped on Ben’s chin. He giggled.

“Goal two: check!” Olive smiled with her eyes.

Mia looked at the window. “What about helping neighbors?”

Olive gave a gentle hoot. “We can do community service even from our living room.”

“From here?” Ben asked, surprised.

“Yes,” said Olive. “We can make kind cards for the people who keep our park clean and safe.” She set out paper, crayons, and stickers that sparkled like tiny stars.

Mia drew a big green tree. Ben drew a broom and a smiling sun. Olive wrote simple words: “Thank you. You help us play.” The markers smelled like fruit. The paper felt smooth and warm from their hands.

They taped the cards on a small box. Inside the box, they placed a bag of oranges and bottles of water.

“This is our Health Helper Box,” Olive explained. “We will leave it by the front desk in our building for the helpers.”

Ben bounced. “We are helpers too!”

“Goal three: check!” Olive sang, like a soft bell.

Suddenly, Mia frowned. “But Olive… sometimes I forget my goals. I just want cookies. I want to sit and watch shows. I feel little.”

Olive nodded, kind and calm. “That is a real feeling,” she said. “Goals can be hard. When it is hard, we make our goals small again. We try again with gentle hearts.”

“How?” asked Ben, eyes wide.

“We use the Hoot Plan,” said Olive. “H stands for ‘Have a small goal.’ O stands for ‘One step at a time.’ O stands for ‘Offer yourself kindness.’ T stands for ‘Tell a helper.’”

Mia whispered, “I can have one cookie and also one orange.”

“That is a strong plan,” Olive said. “One step at a time.”

Ben said, “I can move for five minutes, then rest.”

Olive fluffed her feathers. “Offer yourself kindness. Say, ‘I am learning.’ And tell a helper.” She tapped her chest. “I am here. Hoo-hoo.”

They put the Health Helper Box by the door. Mia and Ben gave thumbs up. The room felt sunny and safe. The rug was soft under their toes. Their bellies felt comfy, not too full, not too empty. Their hearts felt warm like a hug.

Olive looked at the list: Move. Rainbow food. Help others. Try small. Be kind. Ask for help.

“Our wellness plan worked,” Mia said, smiling.

“It worked because we worked together,” Olive replied.

Ben yawned a tiny yawn. “Can we rest now?”

“Yes,” Olive said. “Rest is part of wellness too.”

They curled up on the couch. The room was quiet and cozy. Olive hummed a gentle hoot lullaby. “Hoo-hoo, you did your best. Hoo-hoo, now time to rest.”

As they drifted to sleep, Olive whispered, “Wellness is moving your body, feeding it colors, helping your community, and being kind to yourself. Small steps make strong hearts.”

The End.
