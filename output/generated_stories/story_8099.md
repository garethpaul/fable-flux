# Ellie's Rainbow Garden

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved her family's balcony garden. Every morning, she would spray water from her trunk onto the colorful flowers and green vegetables growing in pots. The sweet smell of jasmine and the bright red tomatoes made her happy.

One sunny day, Ellie's mama said, "Ellie, we're having special visitors today! Children from different countries are coming to see our garden."

Ellie felt excited but also a little nervous. "Will they like our garden, Mama?" she asked, curling her trunk around a sunflower stem.

"I think they will love it," Mama smiled. "But first, let's make sure we're clean and ready. Remember to wash your trunk and feet!"

Ellie giggled as she splashed in the water bucket. The cool water felt refreshing on her gray skin. She scrubbed behind her big ears and made sure her trunk was sparkly clean.

Soon, three children arrived with their families. There was Mei from China, who had long black hair and wore a red dress with golden dragons. There was Kofi from Ghana, who had beautiful dark skin and a bright yellow shirt. And there was Sofia from Mexico, who had curly brown hair and a colorful woven bag.

"Wow!" said Mei, looking at the garden. "These flowers are so pretty!"

But Ellie noticed something. Each child looked at different plants with special interest. Mei got very excited when she saw the bok choy growing in a corner pot.

"My grandma grows this too!" Mei said, gently touching the green leaves. "We use it to make soup!"

Kofi's eyes lit up when he spotted the okra plants. "Look! These are just like the ones in my uncle's garden back home. We cook them with rice and chicken!"

Sofia clapped her hands when she saw the cilantro and tomatoes. "¡Qué bonito! These smell just like my abuela's salsa garden!"

Ellie felt confused. "But I thought all gardens were the same," she said to her mama quietly.

Mama wrapped her trunk around Ellie lovingly. "Every family grows what they love to eat, sweetheart. That's what makes the world so wonderful - we're all different!"

Ellie thought about this. Then she had an idea! "Would you like to help me plant something new?" she asked her new friends.

The children cheered, "Yes!"

Together, they planted seeds that each family had brought. Mei shared snow pea seeds, Kofi brought watermelon seeds, and Sofia had marigold seeds. Ellie carefully watered each new planting with her trunk.

"Now our garden has foods and flowers from around the world!" Ellie trumpeted happily.

As they worked together, the children taught Ellie words in their languages. "Water is 'agua' in Spanish," Sofia said. "And 'shui' in Chinese," added Mei. "And 'nsu' in my language!" laughed Kofi.

When it was time to say goodbye, all the children hugged Ellie's trunk. "Thank you for teaching us about gardens!" they said.

That evening, as Ellie watered their new international garden, she felt proud and happy. The different plants looked beautiful together, just like their new friendships.

"Mama," Ellie said, "I learned that being different makes everything more interesting and beautiful!"

"That's right, my wise little elephant," Mama said. "The world is like our garden - full of different, wonderful things that all grow together."

From that day on, Ellie loved learning about new cultures and making friends from everywhere. Her balcony garden became a rainbow of plants from around the world, and her heart became full of love for all the different, special people she met.

The End.
