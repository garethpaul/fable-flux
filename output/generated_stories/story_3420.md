# Toby the Turtle's Big Pantry Adventure

Toby the turtle lived in a cozy house with his family. He was very shy and often hid inside his shell when things felt scary. Today, something wonderful smelled in the kitchen, and his tummy rumbled loudly.

"Grumble, grumble!" went his belly.

"Oh my," said Toby softly, patting his shell. "I'm so hungry, but I'm too shy to ask Mama for a snack."

Toby peeked around the corner and saw the pantry door was open just a crack. The sweet smell of cookies drifted out like a gentle breeze. His nose twitched with excitement.

"Maybe I can find something yummy in there," he whispered to himself. "But it looks so big and dark."

Toby took a deep breath. "I can do this," he said, even though his voice was very quiet. "I just need to be brave like the big turtles."

He slowly pushed the pantry door open with his little green foot. *Creak!* The door made a sound, and Toby quickly pulled his head into his shell.

"It's okay, Toby," he told himself. "That was just the door saying hello."

Inside the pantry, tall shelves reached up, up, up to the ceiling. Boxes and jars sat everywhere, and everything smelled like different foods. Toby's eyes grew wide with wonder.

"Wow!" he gasped. "Look at all these treasures!"

But there was one problem. All the good snacks were on the high shelves, and Toby was very small. He stretched his neck as long as he could, but he couldn't reach anything.

"I'm too little," Toby sighed sadly. "Maybe I should give up."

Then Toby remembered what his grandpa turtle always said: "When you can't reach something, use your thinking cap!"

Toby looked around carefully. He saw a sturdy wooden step stool in the corner. It had pretty flowers painted on it and looked just the right size.

"That's perfect!" Toby said with a smile. "I can use that to help me reach higher!"

Slowly and carefully, Toby pushed the step stool across the floor. *Scrape, scrape, scrape!* It made a scratchy sound on the tiles, but Toby kept going.

"I'm doing it!" he said proudly. "I'm solving my problem all by myself!"

He climbed up on the step stool, one little foot at a time. Now he could see a jar of his favorite crackers shaped like fish. They were orange and smelled like cheese.

"Perfect!" Toby cheered quietly. He carefully took the jar and climbed back down.

Just then, Mama Turtle came into the kitchen. "Toby! What are you doing, little one?"

Toby felt shy again and started to pull his head into his shell. But then he stopped. He had been so brave and smart!

"I was hungry, Mama," Toby said in a stronger voice. "I found the crackers all by myself! I used the step stool and everything!"

Mama Turtle smiled warmly. "Oh, Toby! I'm so proud of you for being brave and using your thinking skills. You took good care of yourself by finding a healthy snack."

They sat together at the little table, sharing the fish crackers. Each one crunched happily in their mouths.

"You know what, Toby?" said Mama. "You're much braver than you think. Sometimes being brave just means trying, even when you feel shy."

Toby's shell felt warm and happy. "I did it!" he said with a big smile. "And next time I'm hungry, I know I can help myself!"

From that day on, Toby felt a little bit braver every day, knowing that he could solve problems and take care of himself.

The End.
