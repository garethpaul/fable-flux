# Bunny Bounce and the Big Book Corner

In a cozy study corner, by a sunny window, lived a determined rabbit named Bunny Bounce. The study corner smelled like fresh paper and warm crayons. Soft pillows sat on a little rug. A tiny desk held colorful markers, a shiny pencil, and a big, big book with a blue cover.

Bunny Bounce wiggled his whiskers. “Today, I will read this big book,” he said, patting the cover. His ears stood tall. He put his paw on his chest. “I can try. I can do it.”

His friend, Turtle Tim, peeked in slowly. “Hello, Bunny. That book is very big,” Tim said kindly.

Bunny Bounce nodded. “Yes, it is big. But I am brave, and I will try,” he said with a smile.

Bunny pulled the book closer. The cover felt smooth and cool. He opened to the first page. The paper made a soft swish. The letters looked small and wiggly.

Bunny’s nose twitched. “Hmm,” he said. “Some letters are hard.” He felt a little flutter in his tummy.

Just then, Birdie Blue fluttered down to the windowsill. “Cheer-cheer! I can chirp the ABC song with you,” she sang.

Bunny Bounce took a deep breath. “Thank you, Birdie. I will try first,” he said. “I believe in me.”

Bunny pointed to the first letter. “A,” he said. His voice was small but steady.

“B,” he said next, a little louder.

“C!” he laughed, clapping his paws. He wiggled with joy. The rug felt soft under his feet.

Turtle Tim smiled. “Slow and steady is good,” he said.

Bunny Bounce kept going. Some letters were easy. Some were not. When he saw a tricky one, his ears drooped a tiny bit. Then he remembered, “I am brave. I will try.”

“Let’s take a wiggle break!” Bunny said. He hopped up and did gentle jumps—one, two, three—soft thumps on the rug. “Moving helps my brain,” he giggled.

Birdie Blue chirped a happy beat. Turtle Tim swayed left and right. “Exercise makes us strong,” Tim said.

After the wiggles, Bunny felt bright and ready. He sat tall at the desk. He held the shiny pencil. “I will trace a letter,” he said.

He tried the letter B. His paw shook a little. The pencil made a scratchy sound on the paper. The first B was bumpy.

Bunny looked at the bumpy B. He felt a pinch of worry. “Oh,” he whispered. “It’s not perfect.”

Turtle Tim put a gentle flipper on Bunny’s paw. “It’s okay,” he said softly. “Trying is brave.”

Birdie Blue nodded. “Cheer-cheer! Try again. Your best is enough.”

Bunny took a slow breath. In, out. He tried again. The pencil slid smoother this time. “B!” he said with joy. “I did it!”

He smiled wide. His cheeks felt warm and happy. “I believed in me,” he said.

A tiny mouse named Mina peeked around the book. “Excuse me, Bunny,” she squeaked. “May I sit on the pillow too?”

Bunny Bounce scooted over right away. “Yes, Mina. There is room. We can share. We take turns,” he said. He patted the pillow. “You can watch me read a little.”

“Thank you,” Mina said, settling in. “You are kind.”

Bunny turned another page. The paper whispered again. He pointed and read the letters he knew. When he found a hard word, he paused. “This part is tricky,” he told his friends. “I feel a little nervous, but I can try.”

Birdie Blue hummed softly. Turtle Tim nodded slow. Mina folded her tiny paws. “We believe in you,” they said together.

Bunny’s heart felt strong. He tried the word, sound by sound. “Hop,” he said at last. “It says hop!”

He bounced once on the rug. “Hop!” The friends laughed. They all did a small hop together.

Suddenly, a loud wind whooshed at the window. The curtain swished, and the big book almost closed. The sound made Mina squeak. Birdie ruffled her feathers. Bunny’s ears perked.

“That was loud,” Bunny said. He put his paws on the book to keep it open. “I am brave. I can help.”

He looked at Mina. “Are you okay?”

Mina nodded. “Yes. Thank you for asking.”

Bunny smiled. “We are safe. Let’s keep going.” He spoke in a calm voice. The room felt calm again. The sun peeked back in, warm on their faces.

Bunny read one more line and traced one more letter. He drew a little star next to it. “I did my best,” he said proudly. “I can learn. I can grow.”

Turtle Tim clapped slowly. “You were brave and patient.”

Birdie Blue sang a sweet tune. “Cheer-cheer for Bunny dear!”

Mina grinned. “You shared the pillow and helped me feel safe. Thank you.”

Bunny Bounce put his paw on his heart. “I believed in me. I was kind to you. I tried and tried,” he said. “That is what makes me strong.”

They cleaned the desk together, put the pencil away, and fluffed the pillows. The study corner smelled like crayons and sunshine again. Bunny took one last hop. “Tomorrow, I will try a new page,” he said.

The friends nodded. “We will cheer for you,” they said.

Bunny smiled. “And I will cheer for you too.”

The End.
