# Whiskers’ Calm-Down Corner

In a cozy study corner by the window, a clever cat named Whiskers liked to learn. The corner smelled like fresh paper and lemony clean. Sunlight made warm squares on the soft rug. A little desk held crayons, a tiny water cup, and a stack of picture cards. Whiskers’ fluffy tail swished as he purred, “Today I will draw and read!”

Whiskers put on his blue glasses. They felt cool on his nose. He opened a book with bright pictures of fruits. “Apples, bananas, grapes,” he read in a gentle voice. He touched the smooth pages. They made a soft swish-swish sound.

Then Whiskers saw a tricky puzzle card. It showed shapes to match. He wanted to finish it fast. “I can do it!” he said. He picked up a triangle piece. It kept slipping from his paw. The piece fell to the floor with a tiny click. Whiskers’ ears drooped.

“I feel grumpy,” he said. His whiskers twitched. His tail flick-flicked. He tried again. The triangle did not fit. His tummy felt tight. His head felt hot. “This is hard!”

Whiskers remembered something his Grandma Cat had said: “When you feel upset, take care of you.” Whiskers looked around his study corner. He saw his “self-care basket.” It held a small brush, a water bottle with a star sticker, and a soft, blue squeeze ball.

“I can take care of me,” Whiskers whispered.

He put the puzzle down. He sat crisscross on the rug. He took a slow breath in, and a slow breath out. “In… out…” His chest rose and fell. The air smelled like books and sunshine. He squeezed the soft ball. Squish. Squash. His paws relaxed.

Next, he brushed his fur, long and slow. Swish… swish… It felt warm and gentle. His shoulders dropped. His face softened. “That feels better,” he said. He took a sip of cool water. Sip, sip. The water made his mouth feel fresh and happy.

Just then, his friend, Tiny Mouse, peeked in. “Hi, Whiskers! I heard a click,” Tiny Mouse squeaked. “Are you okay?”

Whiskers nodded. “I felt mad at my puzzle. But I am doing self-care.” He pointed to his basket. “I breathed. I brushed. I drank water.”

Tiny Mouse’s eyes got big. “Wow. That is smart. Can I help?”

Whiskers smiled. “Yes. But first, let’s share a stretch.” They stood tall. They reached their paws up, up, up. They wiggled their fingers. They touched their toes. Their bodies felt loose and light.

“I feel ready now,” said Whiskers.

Whiskers and Tiny Mouse looked at the puzzle again. “Triangle pieces can be tricky,” said Tiny Mouse. “Let’s go slow.”

Whiskers nodded. “Slow is kind.” He lined up the triangle and turned it a little. Click! It fit. His eyes shone. “I did it!”

Tiny Mouse clapped his tiny hands. “Hooray! You didn’t give up.”

Whiskers purred. “Self-care helped me calm down. When I calm down, I can try again.”

They moved to the coloring page. It showed a big, smiling apple. Whiskers chose a red crayon. He colored slowly. Tiny Mouse chose a green crayon for the leaf. “Let’s share colors,” said Whiskers. He passed the red crayon. The paper felt bumpy under their paws. The crayons made a soft scritch-scratch sound.

Tiny Mouse sniffed the air. “I smell snack time!” he giggled.

Whiskers looked at the clock. “Yes! Healthy snack time.” He opened a small tin. Inside were crunchy carrot sticks and sweet apple slices. He washed his paws with a damp cloth. “Clean paws first,” he said. “That is a healthy habit.”

Tiny Mouse washed his paws too. “Clean paws for me!” They shared the snack. Crunch, crunch. The carrots were crisp. The apple tasted juicy. Whiskers poured a little water into a tiny cup for Tiny Mouse. “I like to share,” he said.

“Thank you,” said Tiny Mouse. “You are generous.”

After snack, Whiskers tidied the study corner. He put crayons in a row. He stacked the books. He placed the brush and ball back in the basket. “A clean space helps my brain feel calm,” he said.

Tiny Mouse pointed at the puzzle. “What did you learn today?”

Whiskers touched his chest. He could feel his steady heartbeat. “I learned to listen to my body. When I feel tight or hot, I can stop. I can breathe. I can brush. I can drink water. I can ask a friend for help. Then I can try again. That is self-care.”

Tiny Mouse smiled. “Self-care helps me too.”

The sun moved across the window, and the warm square on the rug slid to the door. Whiskers stretched and yawned a happy yawn. “I feel proud,” he said.

Tiny Mouse hugged Whiskers’ paw. “I feel proud of you too.”

Whiskers put on his blue glasses again. “Tomorrow, I will learn more,” he said. “And I will care for me, and share with you.”

They both waved goodbye to the study corner, neat and bright. The room smelled like lemon and apples. The day felt calm and sweet.

The lesson was clear and kind: When big feelings come, take care of your body, take a breath, and ask for help. Be gentle with yourself. Share with friends. Healthy habits make every day feel better.

The End.
