# Splash the Dolphin's Garden Adventure

On a sunny morning, Splash the dolphin wiggled and splashed in her special water tank on Mrs. Chen's balcony garden. The garden was full of beautiful plants from around the world - red roses from England, yellow sunflowers from Mexico, and purple lavender from France.

"Good morning, Splash!" said Mrs. Chen, watering her colorful flowers. "Today we have visitors coming to see our special garden."

Splash loved meeting new friends. She clicked and whistled happily, making tiny rainbows in her water droplets.

Soon, three children arrived with their families. There was Emma with blonde hair, Carlos with dark curly hair, and Aisha wearing a bright yellow headscarf.

"Wow! A real dolphin!" Emma squealed, running to the tank.

But when Carlos tried to look too, Emma pushed him away. "I was here first! Go away!" she shouted, her face turning red like the roses.

Carlos felt sad. His brown eyes filled with tears. "I just wanted to see the dolphin too," he whispered.

Splash saw Emma's angry face and Carlos's sad tears. The smart dolphin knew she needed to help. She swam to the corner of her tank and made soft, gentle clicking sounds.

"Splash looks worried," noticed Aisha, pointing to the dolphin.

Mrs. Chen knelt down beside the children. "When we feel angry, we can take deep breaths, just like Splash takes deep breaths before she dives."

Splash demonstrated by taking a big breath and slowly letting it out through her blowhole. "Whoooosh!"

"Let's all try together," Mrs. Chen suggested. "Breathe in slowly... and out slowly..."

Emma watched Splash and took a deep breath. Her red face began to feel cooler. "I'm sorry, Carlos," she said quietly. "I was excited and forgot to share."

Carlos smiled. "That's okay. We can all watch Splash together!"

Splash was so happy! She spun in a circle and splashed gently, creating sparkles in the sunlight.

"Look!" said Aisha. "The water drops look like tiny diamonds!"

The three children stood side by side, watching Splash perform graceful flips. Emma pointed to the Mexican sunflowers. "These are like the ones in my grandmother's garden!"

"My family grows lavender like this in Morocco," Aisha shared, touching the purple flowers gently.

"And my abuela has roses just like these!" Carlos added excitedly.

Mrs. Chen smiled. "Our garden brings together beautiful things from many places, just like our friendship brings together wonderful children from different families."

Splash clicked happily and swam to each child, letting them gently touch her smooth, gray skin. The dolphin's calm energy helped everyone feel peaceful and connected.

"When I feel angry again, I'll remember to breathe like Splash," Emma promised.

"And we'll always share and take turns," added Carlos.

"Just like the flowers in this garden, we're all different and beautiful," said Aisha wisely.

As the sun climbed higher, the children helped Mrs. Chen water the plants. Splash watched proudly as her new friends worked together, sharing the watering can and complimenting each other's careful work.

"Thank you, Splash," Emma whispered to the dolphin. "You taught us how to be calm and kind."

Splash responded with a gentle whistle and a small splash that felt like a cool, refreshing hug.

From that day on, whenever any of the children felt angry or upset, they remembered Splash's lesson: take deep breaths, share with others, and celebrate the beautiful differences that make everyone special.

The End.
