# Benny Beaver's Big Art Project

Benny Beaver hurried into the bright art room, his big flat tail dragging behind him. The room smelled like fresh paint and glue. Colorful papers covered the tables, and paintbrushes stood in tall jars like tiny trees.

"I'm going to build the BEST art project ever!" Benny said loudly, his brown fur bristling with excitement. He grabbed blocks, paper, glue, and crayons all at once. His small paws were so full that some things tumbled to the floor with soft *thud* sounds.

Miss Rabbit, the art teacher, smiled gently. "Remember, Benny, we have plenty of time to create something wonderful."

But Benny wasn't listening. He wanted to build a castle, paint a rainbow, AND make a paper airplane all at the same time. His tail *slap-slapped* on the floor as he rushed from table to table.

"Move, move!" Benny said, bumping into Lily Lamb who was carefully painting pink flowers. Her paintbrush wobbled, and pink paint dripped onto her white wool.

"Oh no!" Lily said sadly, looking at the pink spots.

Benny barely noticed. He was too busy trying to stack blocks while holding a paintbrush in his mouth. The blocks wobbled and crashed down. *Crash! Clatter!*

"This isn't working!" Benny groaned, his ears drooping. He looked around the messy art room. Paint was everywhere, blocks were scattered, and his friends looked upset.

Little Mouse was trying to glue paper, but Benny had taken all the good glue. Owl was waiting patiently for the crayons that Benny had piled up at his workspace.

Benny's whiskers twitched as he realized what had happened. His tummy felt heavy and sad. "I wanted to make everything perfect," he whispered, "but I made everything messy instead."

Miss Rabbit sat down next to Benny. "Sometimes when we try to do too much too fast, we don't do our best work," she said kindly. "What do you think you could do differently?"

Benny looked at his friends. Lily Lamb still had pink paint in her wool. His heart felt sorry and warm at the same time.

"I'm really sorry, Lily," Benny said, walking over to her. "I was rushing and bumped into you. That wasn't kind."

Lily smiled. "It's okay, Benny. Accidents happen."

"Maybe I could help you clean up?" Benny offered. Together, they gently washed the pink paint from Lily's wool with a damp cloth.

Then Benny took a deep breath. "Miss Rabbit, could you help me make a plan? I want to build something special, but I want to do it the right way."

"Of course!" Miss Rabbit said. "Let's choose just ONE project to start with."

Benny decided to build a small wooden bridge with blocks. He worked slowly and carefully, placing each block with his steady paws. When he needed glue, he shared it with Mouse. When Owl wanted to borrow crayons, Benny happily shared those too.

*Click, click, click* went the blocks as Benny built his bridge piece by piece. It felt good to take his time and do careful work.

"Look!" Benny called out proudly when he finished. His bridge was strong and beautiful, with red blocks and blue blocks making a pretty pattern.

"That's wonderful, Benny!" said Miss Rabbit. "You took your time and built something really special."

Benny's chest puffed up with pride, but this time it felt different. He felt proud because he had been kind, patient, and careful. His friends gathered around to admire his bridge, and Benny felt warm and happy inside.

"Tomorrow I want to paint," Benny said with a big smile. "Just painting. One thing at a time!"

The End.
