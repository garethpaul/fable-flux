# Koala Kai’s Clickety-Clack Day

Koala Kai was a calm koala with soft gray fur and big, gentle ears. Kai lived in a cozy home with a sunny home office. The office smelled like warm tea and paper, and the window let in bright morning light. The keyboard went clickety-clack, and the little desk fan hummed, whirr, whirr.

“Good morning, desk,” Kai whispered. “Good morning, computer.” Kai liked to talk softly to the things that helped each day feel friendly.

Today, Kai wanted to make a little poster on the computer that said, “You Can Do It!” Kai wanted to share the poster with friends to help them feel brave and strong.

Kai sat in the comfy chair. The chair went squeak. Kai’s paws felt the smooth keys. Click. Clack. Tap. The mouse felt cool and round. The screen glowed bright and blue.

“I can do this,” Kai said calmly. “I will try my best.”

Kai began to type. But the letters looked too small. The colors looked too dull. Kai’s ears drooped just a little.

“Hm,” said Kai. “I feel a bit stuck.”

Just then, a tiny friend peeked in. It was Miri the moth, flutter-flutter by the lamp.

“Hi, Kai!” Miri buzzed softly. “I like your glow-y screen!”

“Hi, Miri,” said Kai. “Thank you. I am making a poster. But it doesn’t look right yet. I feel frustrated.”

Miri landed on the pencil cup. “Sometimes I flap and miss the light,” she said. “I try again. Maybe you can try again too.”

Kai took a slow, deep breath. In and out. “Yes,” said Kai. “I will keep trying.”

Kai clicked on a big yellow button. The color on the screen turned sunny and warm.

“Oooh,” said Miri. “Sunny like honey!”

Kai smiled. “Thank you for the idea, Miri.”

Just then, Tiko the tiny turtle toddled in, tap, tap, tap on the wooden floor. “Hello,” Tiko said in a slow, gentle voice. “What are you making?”

“A poster that says, ‘You Can Do It!’” said Kai. “I want it to help people.”

Tiko nodded. “That is kind.” Tiko looked at the screen. “Would you like my idea?”

Kai respected Tiko’s slow way. “Yes, please,” Kai said. “I would like to hear your idea.”

“Maybe,” said Tiko, “you can add a bright star. Stars help me feel brave at night.”

“That’s a good idea,” said Kai. “Thank you.”

Kai clicked and drew a star. The star shined like a tiny lemon cookie. Kai felt a warm, happy wiggle in the tummy. “I can do hard things,” Kai said to self. “I can learn.”

Then—oops!—Kai pressed a wrong key. The star vanished. The screen went blank-white.

Kai’s paws froze. The fan still went whirr, whirr. Kai’s heart made a quick thump.

“I feel frustrated again,” Kai said out loud. “I want to stop.”

Miri and Tiko looked at Kai with kind eyes.

“It is okay to feel big feelings,” said Tiko.

“Maybe take a tiny break,” said Miri. “Flap-flap, sip-sip.” She pointed her tiny wing to a cup of warm tea.

Kai nodded. “Yes. I will take a calm break.” Kai stood up, stretched arms and legs, and sipped tea. The tea tasted sweet and cozy like a hug. Kai looked out the window at a small cloud drifting by.

“I can try again,” Kai whispered. “I believe in me.”

Kai sat back down and pressed the undo button. Poof! The star came back. Kai smiled wide. “I did it!”

Next, a new friend poked his head in. It was Pip the parrot with teal and green feathers. “Squawk! Need help?” Pip asked, tilting his head.

Kai thought for a moment. “Thank you, Pip. I am practicing doing it myself first. If I still need help, I will ask. It is honest to say what I need.”

Pip nodded. “Respect! I’ll watch quietly.” Pip sat on the chair arm and stayed calm.

Kai clicked and typed. “YOU CAN DO IT!” appeared in big, bold letters. Kai picked friendly colors: sunny yellow, sky blue, grassy green. Kai added a small line at the bottom that said, “Made by Kai.” Kai wanted to be honest about who made the poster.

Miri fluttered. “It looks bright!”

Tiko smiled. “It looks brave.”

Pip squawked softly, “It looks kind.”

Kai read the words out loud. “You Can Do It! Made by Kai.”

Kai’s chest felt light and proud. “I tried. I felt frustrated. I took a break. I tried again. I believed in myself.” Kai turned to friends. “Thank you for your ideas. Thank you for being respectful and patient.”

“You’re welcome,” said Tiko.

“Happy to help,” said Miri.

“Squawk! You did it yourself!” said Pip.

Kai printed the poster. The printer went brrr-brrr, click-clack. The paper felt warm and smooth. The colors were bright and cheerful. Kai taped the poster above the desk. The star twinkled, and the letters looked strong.

Kai sat back in the squeaky chair and smiled. “Every day, I can learn. Every day, I can try. I am honest. I am kind. I respect others. And I believe in me.”

The friends all nodded. Outside, the tiny cloud drifted past the sun, and light filled the home office with a happy glow.

Kai whispered to the room, “You can do it,” and the room seemed to whisper back, “Yes, you can.”

The End.
