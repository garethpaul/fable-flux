# Pippa the Parrot’s Guest Room Plan

Pippa was a bright, creative parrot with green wings and a splash of blue on her tail. She liked to say, “Plan, write, and share!” Today, Pippa flapped into the cozy guest room. The room smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap. The sun shone in, warm and golden, across a soft red rug.

“Squawk! Today I help friends,” Pippa said. She hopped onto a small desk. On the desk was paper, crayons, and a big world map. The map showed many places with tiny pictures: a drum, a kite, a rice bowl, a snowflake, and a beach umbrella.

Pippa tilted her head. “Our friends come from many places,” she chirped. “We all share the world. We can be kind and plan together.”

Pippa took a deep breath. “First, I make a plan.” She drew a simple chart with three boxes. In the first box she wrote, “Listen.” In the second box she wrote, “Plan.” In the third box she wrote, “Share.”

Knock, knock. Two toy friends sat on the bed: Benny Bear and Lila Llama. They were not real animals, but in Pippa’s heart they were real friends. Today, they would practice being global citizens.

“Hello, Benny Bear,” Pippa said softly.

“Hi, Pippa,” Benny said in a deep, gentle voice. “I want a quiet story.”

“Hello, Lila Llama,” Pippa added.

“Hi, Pippa,” Lila said in a bright, bouncy voice. “I want dancing music!”

Pippa blinked. “Oh,” she said. “You want different things. We can listen. We can plan. We can share.”

She pointed her wing at the map. “Many people around the world like different things. We can learn, be kind, and work it out.”

Benny Bear hugged a pillow. “I feel sleepy,” he murmured.

Lila Llama wiggled. “I feel excited!”

Pippa put her wing on her heart. “I hear you,” she said. “Let’s write our ideas.”

She picked up a blue crayon. Scratch, scratch. On the paper she wrote, “Benny: quiet story. Lila: dancing music.” She drew a little bear and a llama. She smiled. “Writing helps us remember and be fair.”

Then Pippa asked, “What do we both need?” She tapped her beak and listened. Outside, a soft breeze moved the curtains. The lemon soap smell made the room feel fresh. Pippa felt calm.

“Time,” Benny said.

“Fun,” Lila said.

Pippa nodded. “Yes! We need time and fun. We can plan a schedule.” She drew a clock with a big hand and a little hand. “First quiet story, then dancing,” she said. “Ten minutes each. Is that okay?”

Benny Bear said, “That is fair.”

Lila Llama said, “Okay! After the story, I will dance. I can wait.”

Pippa ruffled her feathers. “We made a plan by listening and negotiating.” She smiled. “Negotiation means talking kindly and finding a way for everyone.”

She opened a small notebook and wrote a title: “Guest Room Peace Plan.” Then she read aloud: “We listen. We plan. We share. We take turns. We care about the world.” She drew a big heart around the words.

“Now, story time,” Pippa whispered. She turned down the lamp to make soft light. She rubbed the red rug with her toes. It felt fluffy. She made a warm nest of pillows for Benny. She cleared a little dance spot for Lila for later.

Pippa read a short, gentle story about a child sharing tea with a friend from far away. “They say hello in different ways,” Pippa read. “They smile and try each other’s tea.” Her voice was slow and calm. Benny’s eyes drooped. Lila listened too, hugging her knees.

When the story ended, Benny Bear yawned. “Thank you,” he said. “I feel safe.”

Pippa checked the clock she drew. “Now it’s dance time!” she chirped. She clapped softly. Tap-tap-tap. Lila Llama began to dance, swish-swish, around the room. Her hooves made a little sound on the rug. Pippa hummed a gentle tune—la, la, la—so it wouldn’t be too loud for sleepy Benny.

Benny smiled. “I like your quiet dance,” he said.

Lila giggled. “I like your cozy story,” she said.

Pippa fluffed her wings with joy. “We did it! We listened to each other. We planned and wrote our ideas. We shared time. We were kind,” she said. “This is global citizenship. We live in one big world. We are different, and we care for each other.”

She wrote a final note in her notebook: “We listened. We planned. We shared. It worked!”

Then Pippa asked, “What can we do next to help our world?”

Benny Bear said, “We can learn words from other places.”

Lila Llama said, “We can write a welcome note for guests.”

Pippa nodded. She wrote a simple welcome note on bright yellow paper: “Welcome, friends! We listen. We plan. We share.” She drew a tiny globe with smiling faces around it and taped the note to the guest room door.

The sun dipped a little. The room glowed warm and soft. Pippa felt proud. “Today we practiced being global citizens,” she said. “We used planning, writing, compassion, and negotiation. Our guest room is a friendly room for everyone.”

Benny Bear and Lila Llama snuggled into the pillows. Pippa tucked the crayons away. The lemon scent and the cozy rugs made everyone calm.

Pippa whispered, “Plan, write, and share.”

“Plan, write, and share,” her friends echoed.

And the guest room felt like a tiny, loving world.

The End.
