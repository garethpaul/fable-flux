# Pippa Parrot’s Pantry Parade

Pippa was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a splash of sunny yellow on her cheeks. She loved colors, patterns, and making things neat. One morning, she fluttered into the pantry. The pantry smelled like warm bread and sweet cinnamon. It was cool and quiet inside.

“Oh dear,” Pippa squawked softly. “What a jumble!”

Cans rolled to one side. Crackers hid behind the flour. A bag of rice lay open like a sleepy pillow. A small drip of honey made a sticky spot on the shelf.

Pippa blinked her round eyes. “I can help,” she said. “I can make a plan.”

She tapped her beak gently on a wooden spoon. Tap-tap-tap. “Friends,” she called, “can we work together?”

Tiny feet scurried. Out came Milo Mouse, wearing a tiny blue cap. “I can carry small things,” said Milo. “I am careful and quick.”

Bella Bunny hopped in, ears bouncing. “I can stack boxes,” said Bella. “I am strong and steady.”

Gus Gecko peeked from a jar label. “I can make labels,” said Gus. “My writing is neat.”

Pippa fluffed her feathers. “Thank you,” she said. “Let’s be safe. No climbing too high. No sharp things. We work slow and kind. We keep the floor clear.”

“Yes, Captain Pippa!” they cheered.

Pippa smiled. “We will organize by groups. Crunchy snacks together. Breakfast food together. Cans together. Spices together.”

The friends nodded. It felt good to have a simple plan.

First, Pippa pointed to the floor. “Let’s make four baskets.” She placed four little bins in a row. “Snacks here. Breakfast here. Cans here. Spices here.”

Milo Mouse sniffed. “Mmm, crackers.” He picked up a small box. The box felt smooth. “Snacks,” he said, and set it in the snack bin.

Bella Bunny hugged a bag of oats. “Breakfast,” she giggled. The bag rustled like soft rain. She put it in the breakfast bin.

Gus Gecko stuck a tiny note on a can. “Peas,” he wrote carefully. The marker smelled a little sweet. He lined up the cans. “Cans go here.”

Pippa fluttered to the sticky honey spot. “Safety first,” she said. She dabbed it with a clean cloth. “We don’t want a slippery spot.”

“Good idea,” said Milo. “My paws are happier now.”

Pippa made up a song. “Sort, sort, sort and smile. We can do it, mile by mile.” She sang in a gentle voice. Her words sounded like a soft drum.

They all joined in. “Sort, sort, sort and smile!”

Pippa lifted a jar of cinnamon and breathed in. “Mmm,” she said. “Spice. This goes with spices.” She placed it gently in the spice bin.

Suddenly, they heard a tiny clink. A tower of cans wobbled. Wobble-wobble. “Oh!” cried Bella.

Pippa raised a wing. “Freeze,” she said calmly. “Hands and paws still.” The friends stopped. The cans settled and grew quiet.

Pippa nodded. “Let’s make wide stacks, not tall stacks. Wide stacks are safe.” She showed them how to make a short, strong row. “Like a little wall,” she said.

They kept going. Sort and smile. Sort and smile.

Soon the bins were ready. Pippa looked at the shelves. “Now we put things back in order.” She flapped to the lowest shelf. “Snacks on the bottom. Easy to reach. Safe for little paws.”

Milo grinned. “I can help with the bottom shelf.” He arranged crackers, nuts, and raisins. He lined them like little soldiers. Click, click, click went the boxes.

“Breakfast in the middle,” said Pippa. Bella placed oats, cereal, and pancake mix in a neat row. The bags made friendly rustles. “All cozy,” Bella said.

“Cans on this side,” said Pippa. “Spices on the other.” Gus stuck labels on the edges: SNACKS, BREAKFAST, CANS, SPICES. His letters were big and clear.

Pippa clapped her wings. “Let’s make a Safety Corner,” she added. “Heavy things low. Glass in the back. Lids on tight.”

They checked lids. Twist, twist. They moved heavy jars low. Thump, safe. They kept the floor clean. Sweep, sweep.

“Community time!” Pippa chirped. She made a little basket of extra snacks. “We can share these with our neighbors,” she said. “Helping others is kind.”

Milo nodded. “We have enough. Sharing feels warm.”

Bella hugged the basket. “I will deliver with careful hops.”

Gus added a label that said, “For Friends.”

They stepped back. The pantry looked bright and calm. It smelled like cinnamon and clean shelves. Everything had a home. Pippa’s heart felt light.

Milo whispered, “It’s so neat. I can find things.”

Bella smiled. “I feel safe.”

Gus puffed his chest. “I feel proud.”

Pippa said, “We made a plan. We worked as a team. We were safe. We helped our community. This is good leadership. This is good organization.”

She hung a small chart on the door with happy pictures: sort, stack, label, share, and clean. “We can follow this every day,” she said.

The friends sang one more time, soft and sweet. “Sort, sort, sort and smile.”

Pippa gave a final nod. “A tidy pantry helps everyone. When we put things in their place, we save time, we stay safe, and we can share more.”

They all agreed. Their tummies rumbled. Pippa laughed. “Snack time! Crackers and raisins from the bottom shelf.”

They ate together, neat and cozy in the cool pantry, and felt proud of their kind, careful work.

The End.
