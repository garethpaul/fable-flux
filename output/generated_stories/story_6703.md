# Joey's Big Building Day

Joey the kangaroo was bouncing around Grandpa's garage workshop. The little joey loved to hop and jump everywhere he went!

"Bounce, bounce, bounce!" Joey said as he hopped from one corner to another. His big feet made loud THUMP sounds on the concrete floor.

Grandpa Kangaroo was working on a special project. He was building a beautiful birdhouse with his tools. "Joey," said Grandpa gently, "would you like to help me build this birdhouse?"

"Yes, yes, yes!" Joey bounced up and down excitedly. "I want to help! I want to help!"

Grandpa smiled and handed Joey a small hammer. "This is for you, little one. But first, we need to practice being still and careful."

Joey took the hammer and immediately started swinging it around. BANG! CLANG! He hit the workbench by accident.

"Whoa there, Joey!" said Grandpa. "When we use tools, we need to move slowly and think first."

But Joey was too excited. He kept bouncing and swinging the hammer. CRASH! A jar of screws fell down and scattered all over the floor.

"Oh no!" cried Joey. He felt his tummy get tight and worried. "I made a big mess!"

Grandpa knelt down and put his warm paw on Joey's shoulder. "It's okay, little joey. Everyone makes mistakes when they're learning. But let's try something special."

"What?" asked Joey, his ears drooping a little.

"Let's practice the 'Stop and Breathe' game," said Grandpa. "When we feel too bouncy or excited, we can stop our bodies and take three big breaths."

Grandpa showed Joey how to stand still like a statue. "Breathe in slowly through your nose... smell the sawdust and wood," he said. "Now breathe out through your mouth... whoosh!"

Joey tried it. "In... I smell the wood! Out... whoosh!" He felt his bouncy feeling slow down.

"Good job!" said Grandpa. "Now, let's clean up together, and then try building again."

Joey and Grandpa picked up all the screws. Joey moved slowly and carefully, remembering to breathe when he felt bouncy.

"Now," said Grandpa, "let's hold the hammer with two hands and tap gently."

Joey held the small hammer carefully. Tap, tap, tap. He helped Grandpa nail a piece of wood to the birdhouse. "I did it!" Joey whispered proudly.

"You sure did," said Grandpa. "You used your 'Stop and Breathe' skill!"

They worked together for the whole afternoon. Whenever Joey felt too excited and bouncy, he would stop, take three deep breaths, and then continue working carefully.

When they finished, the birdhouse was beautiful! It had a red roof and a small round door for the birds.

"We make a great team," said Grandpa, giving Joey a big hug.

"I learned that when I stop and breathe, I can do hard things!" said Joey happily.

They hung the birdhouse outside the garage, and soon a little blue bird came to look at their work.

"Thank you for teaching me, Grandpa," said Joey. "Now I know how to be bouncy AND careful!"

From that day on, whenever Joey felt too excited or bouncy, he remembered to stop, breathe deeply, and think before acting. And he became the best little helper in Grandpa's workshop!

The End.
