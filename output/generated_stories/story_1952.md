# Brave Bubbles and the Big Brush

In a bright, cozy bathroom, a friendly bear cub named Bebo stood on a soft blue bath mat. The mirror was shiny. The soap smelled like sweet berries. Warm light made tiny sparkles on the faucet.

Bebo wiggled his round ears. “Tonight I can do my bedtime jobs all by myself,” he said. His voice sounded brave, but his tummy felt a little fluttery.

Mama Bear peeked in with a smile. “You’ve got this, Bebo,” she said gently. “Wash paws, brush teeth, and tidy your towel.”

Bebo nodded. “I can do it.” He took a deep breath that smelled like bubble soap. His heart thumped a happy beat.

He stepped on his little stool. The sink was still a bit high. Bebo’s paws felt small. He looked at the big toothbrush. He looked at the tall faucet.

A tiny worry crept in. “Maybe I can’t,” he whispered.

The faucet dripped. Plink. Plink. It sounded like a kind little drum. Bebo tapped his paw on the soap dish. “Tap, tap.” The sounds made him smile.

“Hello, sink,” Bebo said. “I’m Bebo. I’m learning.” He liked talking to the things around him. It made the room feel friendly.

He tried to turn the faucet. It was hard. His paw slipped. “Oh!” Water splashed and sprayed cool drops on his nose. He giggled. “That’s chilly!”

Bebo steadied himself. “I will try again.” He wrapped both paws around the handle and turned slowly. “Creeeak.” Water rushed out, making a soft whoosh. He felt proud. His chest felt warm, like a hug.

“Step one,” he said. “Wash paws.” He rubbed his paws with the berry soap. Tiny bubbles popped. Pop, pop, pop! They smelled sweet and fun. “Hello, bubbles!” he laughed. “You make me brave.”

He rinsed and shook his paws. “Drip, drop!” Water danced on the sink. He reached for the towel but it hung a bit high. He frowned. “Hmm.”

Bebo looked around. “I can try a new way.” He pulled his stool closer to the towel. Step, scoot. Step, scoot. He climbed up and patted his paws dry. “Soft and warm,” he said. “I did it!”

Mama Bear’s voice came from the hall. “How’s my smart cub?”

“I’m okay!” Bebo called back. “I’m learning and trying.”

“Good communication,” Mama Bear replied. “I like hearing your plan.”

Bebo smiled. He loved sharing his steps out loud. It made the bathroom feel like a team.

“Step two,” Bebo said. “Brush teeth.” He squeezed the toothpaste. A little too much came out. “Oops!” A minty blob slid onto the counter. “Uh-oh.”

He took a small breath. “It’s okay, Bebo,” he told himself softly. “Mistakes are for learning.” He wiped the extra paste with a tissue and dropped it in the bin. “Tidy, tidy,” he said.

He put a pea-sized dot of paste on the brush. “This much is just right.” He looked in the mirror. “Hello, Bebo,” he said to his reflection. “You are brave. You are kind. You can do this.”

He started to brush. “Swish, swish,” went the brush. “Top teeth, bottom teeth, back teeth,” he sang in a little song. The mint tasted cool, like tiny snowflakes. His tail did a happy wiggle.

He hummed as he brushed, “Around the world we care and share. Clean teeth show that we prepare.” He liked thinking about kids in other homes getting ready for bed too. “We are all different,” he said, “and we are all doing our best.”

When his mouth felt sparkly, he spit into the sink. “Ptoo!” He rinsed his mouth with a small cup of water. “Sip, swish, swish, and spit!” Water splashed softly. He wiped the sink with a small cloth. “I can help keep our water place clean,” he said. “That helps everyone.”

“Step three,” Bebo said. “Tidy towel.” He folded the towel with careful paws. Fold, smooth, pat. He hung it neatly. The bathroom looked calm and cozy.

Bebo looked around. “I finished my tasks!” His voice was proud and bright. Then he saw one more thing: his toothbrush cup could tip over. He had an idea.

“I can make it steadier,” he said. He took a wide soap box lid from the shelf and set the cup inside it. The cup fit snug. “Ta-da!” he cheered. “An invention!” He clapped. “Innovation means trying new ideas to solve a problem.”

Mama Bear walked in, paws clapping softly. “I heard your brushing song,” she said. “It sounded strong.”

Bebo’s eyes shone. “I made a steadier cup, Mama. And I did all my steps.”

Mama Bear knelt and hugged him. She smelled like honey. “You believed in yourself, you tried new ways, and you finished your tasks. That’s real bear bravery.”

Bebo stood taller. He felt big inside, like a bright lantern. “Sometimes I felt worried,” he said, “but I talked kindly to myself. I asked for help with my voice, and I helped our bathroom stay clean. That helps our home, and our big world too.”

Mama Bear kissed his ear. “Yes, little cub. Caring for your space is part of caring for the planet. And speaking kindly to yourself grows your self-esteem.”

Bebo looked in the mirror again. “Hello, Bebo,” he said. “You are able. You matter.” His smile was warm and wide.

He turned off the water. “Good night, sink,” he said. He switched off the light with a soft click.

As Mama Bear tucked him in, Bebo whispered, “I can do hard things.”

“Yes,” Mama Bear said. “And when you try, learn, and finish, you shine.”

The End.
