# Splash's Special Porch Day

Splash the dolphin loved visiting Grandma Ocean's peaceful front porch. The wooden boards felt warm under his smooth gray skin. Colorful wind chimes sang gentle songs in the breeze. Sweet jasmine flowers made the air smell like magic.

Today, Splash brought his collection of shiny bottle caps. He loved sorting them by color on the porch steps. Red ones here, blue ones there, silver ones in a special pile.

"Good morning, Splash!" called his friend Crab from the corner of the porch. "What are you doing with all those caps?"

"I'm making rainbow patterns," Splash said proudly. "Look how they sparkle in the sunshine!"

But then Seagull swooped down with a loud SQUAWK! "Those are mine!" she cried. "I saw them first in the tide pools!"

Splash's fins drooped. "But I found them on the beach yesterday. I cleaned them all up!"

Crab scuttled closer. His little claws clicked on the wooden boards. "Oh no! Fighting makes my shell feel all tight inside."

The three friends stared at each other. The wind chimes stopped singing. Even the jasmine seemed to smell less sweet.

Splash took a deep breath of the salty air. "Wait," he said slowly. "Maybe we don't have to fight. Grandma Ocean always says the best treasures are shared treasures."

"What do you mean?" asked Seagull, tilting her white head.

"Well," Splash said, his eyes twinkling like the bottle caps, "these caps came from the ocean. That's all of our home! Maybe we can use them together."

Crab's eyes lit up. "Ooh! My family has an old tradition. We make music with shells and caps!"

"Really?" Seagull flapped her wings excitedly. "My grandmother taught me to make beautiful patterns with shiny things!"

Splash clapped his fins together. "Perfect! And I know how to sort them so they're extra special. We can combine all our ideas!"

The three friends worked together on the sunny porch. Crab showed them how to tap the caps gently to make tinkling music. Seagull arranged them in swooping patterns like waves. Splash organized them by size and color.

"This sounds like ocean songs!" laughed Crab as they tapped out a rhythm.

"And it looks like sunset over the water!" added Seagull, admiring their swirling design.

Splash felt warm happiness bubble up in his chest. "It's even more beautiful than when I worked alone. We're recycling these old caps into something brand new!"

Grandma Ocean peeked out from her kitchen window. Her wise old eyes crinkled with joy. "What lovely music and art! You've turned trash into treasure together."

"And we learned each other's traditions too," said Splash. "Crab's music, Seagull's patterns, and my sorting!"

As the sun climbed higher, the three friends sat on the peaceful porch. Their bottle cap creation sparkled and chimed in the gentle breeze. The jasmine smelled sweet again. The wind chimes joined their melody.

"Fighting felt yucky," said Crab. "But sharing feels warm and happy."

"Next time we disagree, let's remember to listen and work together," agreed Seagull.

Splash nodded, watching their beautiful creation dance in the sunlight. "The best solutions come when we share our different ideas. Just like the ocean shares its treasures with all of us!"

The End.
