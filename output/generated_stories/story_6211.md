# Milo’s Rooftop Morning

Every morning, when the sun peeked over the tall buildings, a courageous monkey named Milo climbed up the ladder to the rooftop. The rooftop was smooth and warm. The air smelled like fresh bread from the bakery below. Pigeons cooed, “Coo-coo,” and a soft breeze tickled Milo’s fur.

Milo had a happy routine. He patted the little blue mat. He watered the tiny rooftop garden. He counted the clouds. “One, two, three fluffy clouds,” he said with a smile. Routine made Milo feel calm and brave. He liked knowing what came next.

One bright morning, Milo saw a new bird on the roof. The bird was small with shiny black feathers and bright eyes. The bird chirped, “Chirp-chirp!” and hopped near Milo’s mat.

Milo blinked. “Hello,” he said kindly. “I am Milo. This is my morning mat.”

The bird tilted its head. “I’m Soot,” she chirped. “I am new. I don’t know where to sit.”

Milo looked at his mat. He liked things to be the same. He liked the mat to be just so. His tummy felt tight. He didn’t know what to do. He crossed his arms. “This is my place,” he said too quickly. “Please don’t sit here.”

Soot’s wings drooped. She scooted back, her little feet scratching the roof. The rooftop felt suddenly quiet. Even the pigeons stopped and listened.

Milo tried to count clouds again. “One, two—” But he saw Soot sitting alone by the vent. The metal vent hummed, and the edge looked cold and rough. Soot wrapped her wings around herself, and her eyes looked wet.

Milo’s tummy felt tight again, but in a different way. He felt a warm twist in his chest. He thought about his routine. He thought about brave choices. Brave didn’t mean only sticking to the same. Brave could mean being kind and trying new things.

He walked over slowly. The sun warmed his back. He could smell the sweet flowers in the garden. He could hear Soot’s soft little sniff.

“Um, Soot?” Milo said gently. “I’m sorry. I spoke too fast. I didn’t think about your feelings.” He touched his chest. “I feel sorry in here. I wanted my routine, but I forgot to be kind.”

Soot looked up. “You said sorry?” she chirped, a tiny smile starting.

“Yes,” said Milo. “I can share my mat. We can make a new routine together. Would you like to sit with me?”

Soot’s eyes sparkled like the morning sun. “Yes, please,” she chirped.

They walked to the blue mat. It felt soft under their toes. The mat smelled a little like soap and sunshine. Milo patted one side. “This side is for you,” he said.

Soot hopped on and nestled her feathers. “Thank you,” she said. “I am new to this rooftop. I don’t know the morning routine.”

Milo smiled. “I can show you! First, we water the garden.”

Milo handed Soot the tiny watering can. It was green and light. The water made a soft trickle sound. The flowers lifted their faces, and the leaves looked shiny. “Listen,” Milo whispered. “The water says, shhh-shhh.”

Soot giggled. “Shhh-shhh,” she echoed.

“Next,” said Milo, “we do our stretches. Reach high!” Milo stretched his long arms up, and his fingers tingled. Soot stretched her wings wide. The breeze brushed their faces.

“Now we count clouds,” Milo said. “Counting helps me feel calm.”

They looked up. “One fluffy rabbit cloud,” said Soot.

“Two puffy pillow clouds,” said Milo.

“Three silly snail clouds,” they said together, laughing. Their laughter bounced off the rooftop walls and felt warm in the air.

A pigeon waddled near them. “Coo-coo?” it asked.

Milo waved. “This is Soot,” he said. “She is part of our morning now.”

The pigeon cooed happily and pecked by the garden. Soot chirped a cheerful note. The rooftop felt full and friendly again.

When they finished counting, Soot looked at Milo. “Thank you for sharing,” she said. “I felt sad before. Now I feel accepted and safe.”

Milo nodded. “I felt scared to change,” he said. “But I can be brave and kind. My routine can grow. There is room for you.”

They made a new plan together. Every morning, they would water, stretch, count clouds, and share the blue mat. They added one more step. Milo said, “We check our feelings.” He touched his chest. “How do you feel today?”

Soot chirped, “I feel happy and calm.”

Milo took a deep breath. The air smelled like bread and flowers. “I feel proud,” he said. “I said sorry, and I accepted a friend.”

The sun climbed higher, and the rooftop shimmered with light. The breeze hummed softly, and the pigeons sang, “Coo-coo, coo-coo.”

Milo smiled at Soot. “Our morning is even better now,” he said. “Because we have each other.”

Soot chirped, “Yes. Acceptance makes space for friends.”

Milo nodded. “And a good routine helps our hearts feel safe.”

They sat together on the blue mat, warm and cozy, watching the clouds drift by. Milo felt brave, kind, and calm. He knew that saying sorry and accepting others made his day brighter.

The lesson was clear in his brave monkey heart: when we accept others and say sorry when we forget, our routines can grow, and our friendships can bloom.

The End.
