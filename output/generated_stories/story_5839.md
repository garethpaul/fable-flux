# Milo's Moving Day

In a cozy closet behind the winter coats lived a kind little mouse named Milo. His hideaway was perfect - soft fabric scraps for his bed, a tiny acorn cap for his water bowl, and colorful buttons he'd collected scattered around like decorations.

One sunny morning, Milo woke up feeling grumpy. His whiskers twitched with frustration. "Everything is WRONG today!" he squeaked loudly, stamping his tiny paws.

His best friend, a cricket named Chirp, hopped over from behind a shoebox. "What's wrong, Milo?" Chirp asked gently.

"I can't find my favorite cheese crumb! My nest is messy! And I'm tired of sitting still!" Milo's tail swished angrily as he threw his button collection across the closet floor. CLINK! CLANK! PING!

Chirp watched sadly as the beautiful buttons rolled everywhere. "Oh my," he whispered.

Milo immediately felt sorry. His ears drooped down. "I'm sorry, Chirp. I didn't mean to make such a mess. I just felt so... so... MAD inside!"

"It's okay to feel angry sometimes," Chirp said kindly. "But what can we do when those big feelings come?"

Milo thought hard, his little nose wiggling. "Maybe... maybe I need to MOVE these feelings out?"

"That's a wonderful idea!" Chirp chirped excitedly. "Let's try it!"

First, Milo did tiny mouse jumping jacks. Up and down, up and down! His arms reached high toward the closet ceiling. "One, two, three!" he counted. The angry feeling started to wiggle away.

Next, he did mouse marching around his hideaway. Left, right, left, right! His little paws made soft patting sounds on the closet floor. "This feels good!" he squeaked happily.

Then Milo tried spinning in circles until he felt dizzy and giggly. "Wheee!" he laughed, his whiskers tickling his cheeks.

"How do you feel now?" asked Chirp.

Milo stopped and took a deep breath. The fresh air filled his tiny lungs. "Much better! The angry feelings moved right out of my body!" He wiggled his toes and stretched his arms. "But I still need to clean up my mess."

Together, Milo and Chirp picked up every single button. They made it into a fun game, rolling the buttons into piles by color. Red buttons here, blue buttons there, shiny silver ones in their own special spot.

"Moving my body helped me think clearly," Milo said as they worked. "And saying sorry to you felt good too."

"You're such a good friend, Milo," Chirp smiled. "You made things right."

As they finished organizing, Milo found his missing cheese crumb hiding under a green button! "Look what moving around helped me find!" he laughed.

That evening, Milo told Chirp a story about a little mouse who learned that when big feelings come, moving your body can help them flow away like water down a stream. "And," he added with a yawn, "saying sorry and fixing mistakes makes everything better."

From that day on, whenever Milo felt angry or frustrated, he remembered to move his body first - jumping, marching, or dancing - until the feelings passed. Then he could think clearly and make good choices.

The End.
