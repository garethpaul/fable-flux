# Pippin’s Rooftop Wiggle Words

Pippin was a creative parrot who loved colors, songs, and making things with his beak. He lived on a sunny rooftop garden with pots of mint and cherry tomatoes. The air smelled like fresh leaves. The wind felt cool and tickly on Pippin’s feathers.

Every morning, Pippin sat on a chair and wrote stories with a fat blue crayon. He wrote on big paper taped to the table. “I love writing, I love writing,” he sang. He did not flap. He did not hop. He did not stretch.

His friend, Mimi the mouse, peeked out from a flower pot. “Hi, Pippin! Want to play hop-hop?” she squeaked.

Pippin waved a wing. “Not now, Mimi. I am writing a wonderful story.”

Mimi nodded, then danced away. Pippin wrote and wrote. His feet felt sleepy. His wings felt heavy. His back felt stiff like a board. He frowned. “Hmm. My feathers feel grumpy,” he clucked.

Next came Sunny the snail, sliding across a smooth stone. “Hello, Pippin,” Sunny hummed. “Want to do slow stretches with me?”

Pippin shook his head. “No, thank you, Sunny. I must finish my story. It is very important.”

He scribbled and scribbled. The sun grew warm. The mint smelled minty. The tomatoes looked shiny red. But Pippin’s body felt tight. His neck went “ow.” His toes went “ouch.” He puffed his chest. “I do not like this feeling.”

Mimi came back and tapped his chair. “Pippin, are you okay?”

Pippin sighed. “My body is tired. My words are stuck. My stories feel wiggly and wrong.”

Mimi put a tiny paw on his wing. “Maybe your body needs to move.”

Sunny nodded slowly. “Move, then write. Exercise can help.”

Pippin crossed his wings. “I don’t need exercise. I need more writing.”

He tried to write again. The crayon slipped. Scribble! Scribble! The blue line went across a page that was not his. It went onto Mimi’s clean page that she saved for later.

Mimi gasped. “Oh!”

Pippin’s eyes grew wide. “Oh no! I made a messy scribble on your page.” His cheeks felt hot. His tummy felt twisty. He looked at Mimi’s page. It was messy and blue. He looked at Mimi. Her whiskers drooped.

Pippin took a slow breath. “I am sorry, Mimi,” he said softly. “I did not mean to scribble on your page.”

Mimi blinked and whispered, “Thank you for the apology.”

Pippin nodded. “I will fix it. I will make it right.” He thought and thought. “I can write you a new page! A beautiful page.”

Mimi smiled a little. “That would be nice.”

Sunny lifted his eyes. “Before writing, maybe we can try some gentle moves. Exercise can help your body and your brain.”

Pippin looked at his stiff wings. He looked at his sleepy toes. He remembered how sad Mimi’s page looked. He wanted to do better. “Okay,” he said. “Let’s move.”

Mimi clapped. “Rooftop wiggles!”

They stood in the warm breeze. Pippin spread his wings wide. “Big wing stretch,” he said. He lifted his wings up to the sky. The sky was bright and blue. The wind whooshed softly. His feathers ruffled. “Ahh, that feels nice.”

Mimi showed toe taps. “Tap, tap, tap,” she sang, tapping her tiny toes on the stone. Pippin copied with his claws. Tap, tap. His toes woke up.

Sunny showed slow neck rolls. “Slow and gentle,” he hummed, moving like a tiny moon. Pippin rolled his neck. He felt the tight spot loosen. “Mmm,” he chirped.

They hopped in a circle. Hop, hop, hop! Pippin laughed. They reached up high. They touched the ground. They did ten soft knee bends. “One, two, three,” counted Mimi. Pippin felt his heart beat happy and strong. He felt the sun on his back. He smelled mint in the air. He heard his own giggles.

After moving, Pippin stood tall. His wings felt light. His mind felt clear like the sky. “I feel good!” he cheered. “Now I can make things right.”

He picked up a fresh, clean page. He looked at Mimi. “I will write you something special.”

Pippin wrote with care. He made big letters. He drew gentle shapes. He wrote a poem just for Mimi:

“Dear Mimi, you are quick and bright,
Thank you for your smile so light.
I am sorry for the scribble blue.
Here’s a shiny page for you.”

He added a little picture of Mimi hopping and a tiny snail with a happy face. He used blue, but this time inside the lines. He blew on the page. “All done!”

Mimi took the page. Her whiskers perked up. “I love it,” she squeaked. “Thank you, Pippin.”

Sunny nodded. “A kind apology. A kind fix.”

Pippin smiled wide. “I learned something today. Exercise helps my body and my brain. Moving first helped me write better. And when I make a mistake, I say sorry and try to fix it.”

Mimi held up her new page. “Let’s make rooftop wiggles every day!”

Pippin flapped happily. “Yes! Move, then write!” he sang.

The wind hummed. The mint leaves danced. Pippin, Mimi, and Sunny did one last happy hop. Then Pippin sat to write another story, his wings loose and his heart light.

The End.
