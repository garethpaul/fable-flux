# Ellie's Big Helper Day

Ellie the elephant calf loved her cozy study corner. Soft purple cushions surrounded her. Colorful books lined the wooden shelves. The morning sunlight streamed through the window, making everything warm and bright.

Today was special. Mrs. Robin had asked all the animal children to help prepare for the new students arriving next week. Each child would get a job to help the newcomers feel welcome.

"I want to help too!" Ellie said, swishing her little trunk excitedly.

But when Mrs. Robin handed out the jobs, Ellie's heart sank. "I'm sorry, Ellie," Mrs. Robin chirped gently. "All the jobs are taken. Maybe next time."

Ellie's ears drooped. Her trunk felt heavy. She watched as Benny Bear got the job of carrying heavy boxes. Rosie Rabbit would hop around delivering welcome letters. Tommy Tiger would use his loud roar to call everyone together.

"What can I do?" Ellie wondered sadly. "I'm not as strong as Benny. I'm not as fast as Rosie. I'm not as loud as Tommy."

Ellie slumped in her study corner. The soft cushions didn't feel comforting anymore. She picked up a book about friendship, but the words looked blurry through her worried eyes.

Then she heard something. "Sniff, sniff." Someone was crying softly outside.

Ellie peeked out the window. A small mouse sat alone under the big oak tree. His tiny shoulders shook as he cried.

"Hello," Ellie called gently through the window. "Are you okay?"

The mouse looked up with wet eyes. "I'm Max. I'm new here. I don't know anyone, and I'm scared about starting school."

Ellie's kind heart felt warm. She had an idea! "Wait right there, Max. I'll be right out."

Ellie hurried outside. She sat down carefully next to Max, making sure not to scare him with her size.

"I know how you feel," Ellie said softly. "Sometimes I feel scared too. But you know what? I think I can help you feel better."

Ellie used her trunk to gently pick some pretty flowers. "These are for you," she said, placing them near Max.

"Wow!" Max squeaked. "They smell so sweet!"

"And look," Ellie said, using her trunk to reach high branches. "I can get you the ripest berries for a snack."

Max's eyes grew wide. "You can reach so high!"

Ellie smiled. "My trunk can do lots of helpful things. Watch this!" She used her trunk like a gentle shower, spraying cool water from the nearby fountain to help Max wash his dusty paws.

"You're amazing!" Max giggled. "I feel so much better now."

Soon, other new students heard Max's happy laughter. A shy turtle, a nervous squirrel, and a worried bunny all came over to see what was happening.

Ellie realized something wonderful. She was being resourceful! Her trunk wasn't just different - it was special. She could reach high places, pick up delicate things, and spray water. She could help in ways no one else could.

"I may not be the strongest or fastest," Ellie thought happily, "but I can use what makes me special to help others."

Mrs. Robin flew over and saw Ellie surrounded by happy new students. "Ellie!" she chirped with delight. "You found the perfect job all by yourself. You're our official Welcome Helper!"

Ellie's trunk wiggled with joy. She had learned that being resourceful meant using her own special gifts to solve problems and help others.

From that day on, whenever new students arrived, they knew they could count on Ellie's gentle trunk and kind heart to help them feel at home.

The End.
