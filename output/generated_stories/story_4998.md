# Pebble the Penguin and the Balcony Garden

Pebble was a cheerful penguin who loved her little balcony garden. It was a small space with sunny pots and green leaves. Pebble watered the herbs with a tiny blue can. She hummed, “Drip, drop, hello mint! Drip, drop, hello tomatoes!”

One warm morning, Pebble waddled outside and sniffed the air. It smelled like wet soil and sweet basil. Bees buzzed softly. A breeze tickled her feathers. Pebble smiled. “Good morning, garden!”

Just then, Pebble’s neighbor, Mr. Parrot, flapped onto the railing. His feathers were green and gold. He squawked, “Too much water! Squawk! The balcony floor is splishy-splashy. My toes are cold!”

Pebble blinked. She looked down. Little puddles dotted the floor. “Oh,” she said softly. “I didn’t see that. I’m sorry, Mr. Parrot.”

From the other side, Mrs. Tortoise peeked over a flower box. “Excuse me,” she said in a slow, gentle voice. “The sun is very bright. The tomato plant grew big and now it blocks my sun. I need warm light for my nap.”

Pebble’s tummy fluttered. “Oh dear,” she whispered. “I didn’t know.”

A small chick from across the courtyard chirped, “Cheep! The wind is blowing soil. It tickles my nose!” The chick sneezed—“Ah-choo!”—and a tiny cloud of dirt sparkled in the air.

Pebble felt her cheeks get hot. Her happy garden was making trouble. She hugged her blue watering can. “I wanted to help my plants grow,” she said. “But I didn’t think about everyone else.”

Pebble took a deep breath. The breeze smelled like rosemary now. She listened to the bees. She listened to her neighbors. Her heart said, I can fix this. I can be kind and fair.

“I want our balcony to be good for all,” Pebble said. “Let’s solve this together.”

Mr. Parrot bobbed his head. “Talk, talk. Good plan.”

Mrs. Tortoise smiled. “Slow and steady solutions.”

Pebble waddled to a shelf of pots. She tapped her beak and thought. “First,” she said, “I will water slowly. Drip, not splash. I will use the small cup, not the big one.”

She poured a tiny stream over the mint. Drip, drip, drip. The sound was soft, like rain on a leaf. No puddle grew.

“Better,” Mr. Parrot said. “No cold toes.”

“Next,” Pebble said, “I will move the tall tomato pot.” She looked around. Her wings were small, but her mind was brave and bright. “I can do it,” she told herself.

She scooted the pot a little to the left, then a little to the right. Grrrriiiip, went the pot on the mat. The tomato leaves rustled like whispering fans. Now the sun shone on Mrs. Tortoise’s favorite nap spot.

“Ah,” Mrs. Tortoise sighed, closing her eyes. “Warm and nice.”

Pebble saw dry soil on the edge of a pot. “And this,” she said, “needs a fix.” She tucked soil deeper with a tiny spade. She pressed it gently. Pat, pat, pat. She made a small fence from smooth pebbles around the pot, so the wind would not blow soil away.

The little chick chirped, “Cheep! No tickle!”

Pebble smiled. “Now our balcony is cleaner and calmer. I will put a tray under each pot to catch extra water. I will sweep after I garden. That is taking care of our shared space. That is being a good balcony neighbor.”

Mr. Parrot fluffed his feathers. “That is civic responsibility. Squawk! Caring for all.”

Pebble stood tall. “I learned something,” she said. “We are different. We have different needs. I can listen and make space for everyone. That is tolerance.”

“Tolerance is kind,” Mrs. Tortoise said. “It makes room for others’ sunny spots.”

They all worked together. Pebble placed trays under the pots. Mr. Parrot held the broom with his claw while Pebble swept. Swish, swish, swish. Mrs. Tortoise pushed the pebble fence straighter with her careful shell. Click, click.

When they were done, the balcony garden looked neat and happy. The air smelled like basil and lemon thyme. The floor was dry. The sun warmed three cozy corners.

Pebble laughed a cheerful penguin laugh. “Thank you for telling me your worries. Thank you for helping.”

“Thank you for listening,” said Mr. Parrot.

“Thank you for trying,” said Mrs. Tortoise.

The little chick called, “Cheep! I like this balcony. Can I visit again?”

Pebble nodded. “Yes. We can water, and watch bees, and share the sun. We can speak up with gentle words. We can listen with gentle hearts.”

As the day ended, the sky turned pink and gold. Pebble tucked a small sign into the garden. It read, “We Share Here.”

She patted the soil one last time. Pat, pat. “Together,” she whispered. “We grow better.”

The End.
