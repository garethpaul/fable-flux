# Otter Ollie’s Cozy Fix-It Night

Otter Ollie was a playful otter who loved splashing in the tub before bed. His cozy bedroom felt warm and safe. The nightlight glowed like a tiny moon. Soft blankets smelled like clean soap. A little breeze tickled the curtains. It was bedtime, and Ollie hummed a gentle tune.

“Good night, Moon Lamp,” Ollie whispered. He reached to click on his sleepy star projector, the one that sprinkled stars on the ceiling.

Click. Nothing.

The ceiling stayed plain and quiet. No stars. No sparkle.

Ollie’s whiskers drooped. “Oh no,” he said softly. “I want my star lights.”

He felt a big feeling rise up, like a wave. His paws squeezed his blanket. Then he remembered something Mama Otter always said: “Be mindful. Take a breath. Then look for a gentle plan.”

Ollie sat crisscross on his rug. He placed a paw on his chest and one on his belly. In, two, three. Out, two, three. His tummy went up and down like a little boat.

“Okay,” he whispered. “I can be patient. I can be resourceful.”

He looked around his cozy bedroom. What did he have? A cardboard box from new socks. Some crayons. A roll of tape. A soft scarf. A tiny flashlight shaped like a fish. A stack of picture books. A jar of smooth shells. Ollie’s eyes twinkled.

“I can make my own stars,” he said. He stood a little taller. “I can ask for help if I need it.”

He padded to the door. “Mama?” he called, kind but strong. “Can I make a safe star light with things I already have?”

Mama Otter peeked in with a smile. “That is very resourceful, Ollie. Let’s be mindful and safe. I’ll watch and help with the tricky parts.”

Ollie clapped his paws. “Thank you, Mama!”

First, Ollie drew little stars and moons on the cardboard with his crayons. Scratch, scratch went the crayons. “Yellow star. Blue moon. Pink comet!” He giggled.

Then he asked, “Mama, may I use the blunt scissors?” Mama nodded and sat close. Snip, snip. Ollie cut out star shapes. Some were lumpy. Some were tiny. “That’s okay,” he said. “I can be patient. I can try again.” He trimmed the lumpy ones. Snip, snip. Better!

He poked tiny holes in another piece of cardboard, like tiny dots in the night. Tap, tap. “This will be my star screen,” he said proudly.

Mama found an empty jar that used to hold jam. It was clean and shiny. “We can reuse this,” Mama said. “That is sustainability. Using what we have is kind to our Earth.”

Ollie nodded. “Kind to Earth. I like that.”

He slipped the soft scarf over the lamp on his bedside table, then paused. “Mama, is this safe?” He was assertive and careful.

Mama shook her head gently. “Scarves and lamps don’t mix. That could be hot.”

Ollie took the scarf away. “Thank you for helping me be safe.” He thought again. “What about my fish flashlight and my star screen?”

“Good idea,” Mama said.

Ollie taped the star screen over the box opening. He placed the fish flashlight inside, pointing up. Click! Soft dots of light sprinkled on the ceiling and walls. Tiny stars danced across his room.

“Ooooooh,” Ollie breathed. He hugged himself. The room felt like a quiet sea of light. The fish flashlight made a friendly hum.

“Now for decorations,” he said. He taped the crayon stars and moons around his bed. Rustle, tape. Rustle, tape. He placed the smooth shells in the jam jar and set it by the window. The shells clicked softly. “This can be my moon jar,” he whispered.

He opened a picture book to a page with a calm night sky and propped it up. He sat on his rug again for a mindful moment. In, two, three. Out, two, three. He listened to the quiet. He smelled his clean blanket. He felt the soft rug under his paws. His heartbeat slowed. He smiled.

Mama tucked him in. “You did that with patience and resourcefulness,” she said. “You used what you had. You asked for help. You kept it safe. You were kind to Earth.”

Ollie’s whiskers wiggled. “I felt upset. I took a breath. I made a plan. I used my things again. I fixed my problem.”

Mama kissed his nose. “That is a big otter skill.”

Ollie looked at the tiny stars twinkling on the ceiling. “Good night, new stars,” he said. The fish flashlight shined softly through the little holes. The room glowed gentle and calm.

His eyes felt heavy. His body felt warm. He yawned a small otter yawn. “I can be patient,” he murmured. “I can be mindful. I can be resourceful.”

The stars winked like little friends. The moon jar gleamed. The night felt safe.

Ollie curled into his blankets, happy and proud. He had turned a problem into a cozy plan.

And that is how Otter Ollie learned that with a calm breath, a kind voice, and the tools around you, you can make your own sparkle.

The End.
