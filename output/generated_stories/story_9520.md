# Benny Bear's Sharing Shed

Benny Bear was a fluffy brown cub who loved to explore. One sunny morning, he discovered an old wooden shed behind his house. The shed smelled like fresh wood and had dusty sunbeams dancing through the windows.

"Wow!" said Benny, pushing open the creaky door. "Look at all these wonderful things!"

Inside the shed, Benny found colorful balls, jump ropes, hula hoops, and art supplies. There were paintbrushes, crayons, and sheets of white paper. His brown eyes sparkled with excitement.

"I can play with everything all by myself!" Benny said happily. He bounced a red ball, spun a yellow hula hoop, and drew a picture of a rainbow. The shed felt cozy and warm.

Soon, Benny heard voices outside. Through the window, he saw his friends Rosie Rabbit and Tommy Turtle looking sad. They were sitting under a tree with nothing to do.

"I wish we had something fun to play with," sighed Rosie, twitching her pink nose.

"Me too," said Tommy slowly. "It's such a beautiful day for playing."

Benny felt his tummy get tight and twisty. He had so many fun things in the shed, but he wanted to keep them all to himself. He quietly closed the shed door and kept playing alone.

But as Benny bounced the ball by himself, it didn't feel as fun anymore. When he drew pictures, they seemed less colorful. Even the hula hoop felt heavy and boring.

"Playing alone isn't as much fun as I thought," Benny mumbled to himself.

He peeked out the window again and saw his friends still sitting sadly under the tree. Benny's heart felt warm and fuzzy. He knew what he needed to do.

"Rosie! Tommy!" called Benny, running outside with his arms full of toys. "Come see what I found!"

Rosie's ears perked up. "Oh my! What are all those colorful things?"

"I found a shed full of toys and art supplies!" said Benny with a big smile. "Would you like to play with me? We can share everything!"

"Really?" asked Tommy, his eyes growing wide. "You want to share with us?"

"Yes!" said Benny. "Playing together will be much more fun!"

Soon all three friends were in the shed, laughing and playing. Rosie jumped rope while counting, "One, two, three!" Tommy painted a picture of flowers with bright purple and yellow paint. Benny taught them both how to spin hula hoops.

"Let's make up an exercise game!" suggested Benny. They took turns calling out fun movements: "Hop like a bunny! Stretch like a turtle! Dance like a bear!"

The friends giggled as they moved their bodies and got good exercise. The shed filled with the sounds of joy and friendship.

"This is the best day ever!" said Rosie, breathing hard from all the fun exercise.

"Thank you for sharing with us, Benny," said Tommy. "You're such a good friend."

Benny felt his heart grow warm and happy. "Sharing makes everything more fun! When we play together, we can be more creative and help each other learn new things."

From that day on, Benny's shed became a special place where all the friends came to play, create, and exercise together. Benny learned that the best part about having wonderful things isn't keeping them to yourself – it's sharing them with friends and making everyone happy.

The End.
