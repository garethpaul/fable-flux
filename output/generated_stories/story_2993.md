# Sunny’s Workshop Surprise

Sunny the squirrel had bright eyes, quick paws, and a fluffy tail that curled like a question mark. Sunny lived near a small house with a big, creaky garage. Inside the garage was a workshop. It smelled like sawdust, warm glue, and orange soap. Sunbeams made dust sparkle in the air. Tools hung on the wall like shiny silver fish, and colorful boxes waited on a shelf.

“Good morning, workshop!” Sunny chirped, peeking through a tiny squirrel door by the floor. “I have a big idea today!”

Thump-thump-thump went Sunny’s tiny feet on the wooden floor. The workbench felt smooth and cool. A soft hum came from a little fan. A radio played gentle music. Sunny wiggled with excitement. “I will make a Cozy Cart,” Sunny said. “A cart to carry nuts, and leaves, and maybe a picnic blanket!”

Sunny looked around carefully. There was a sign above the bench: Ask before using. Please put tools back. Respect others. Sunny nodded. “I will be careful. I will be kind.”

On a low shelf, Sunny saw small boxes of scraps. One box had short wood pieces. Another had soft cloth bits. A jar held loose wheels from old toy cars. Sunny clapped tiny paws. “Perfect!”

Sunny took a deep breath in. In. Out. In. Out. The breath felt slow and warm. “I will use mindful paws,” Sunny whispered. “I will move slowly and think.”

“Hello?” came a voice from outside the door. It was Miko the mouse, carrying a tiny paintbrush. “What are you making, Sunny?”

“A Cozy Cart!” said Sunny. “I want it to roll and make a happy sound.”

Miko’s whiskers twitched. “May I watch?”

“Yes,” said Sunny. “Please watch. And please remind me to be careful.”

Sunny sorted the wood pieces by size. “Short, shorter, shortest,” Sunny sang. “I can build a small frame.” Sunny used a little wooden ruler. “Measure, measure, one and two. Make it even, make it true.”

Miko pointed to the tools hanging high. “There’s a screwdriver up there. But the sign says ask.”

Sunny nodded. “We need to respect the rules.” Sunny pulled a string to ring a tiny bell. Ding-a-ling-ling!

Ms. Dot, the kind human who owned the workshop, opened the door. She wore blue overalls and a friendly smile. “Well, hello, friends. What do you need?”

Sunny stood tall. “May I please use the small screwdriver and some wood glue to build my Cozy Cart? I will be gentle. I will put things back.”

Ms. Dot smiled. “Thank you for asking. Yes, Sunny. Please use the small screwdriver and the glue. Keep your paws safe. Move slowly.” She set the tools on a low stool. “I respect your careful plan.”

“Thank you,” Sunny said. Sunny felt warm and proud.

Sunny dabbed glue on the wood and pressed the pieces together. The workshop smelled sweet and tangy. “We wait and breathe,” Sunny said. “In. Out.” Sunny and Miko listened to the soft fan and the tiny ticking clock. Tick. Tick. Tick. The glue set.

“Now wheels!” Sunny chose four wheels from the jar. But when Sunny tried to fit them on, the cart tipped and clattered. Clack! Clack! The back wheels were wobbly.

Sunny’s tail drooped. “Oh no.”

Miko put a gentle paw on Sunny’s arm. “Let’s pause.”

Sunny closed their eyes and breathed. “In. Out. I can use my imagination. I can picture a fix.”

Sunny imagined the cart as a little turtle. “The turtle needs strong legs,” Sunny thought. Sunny looked around. Bottle caps? Too slippery. Buttons? Too small. Then Sunny saw two tiny washers and a short axle from a toy car. “Aha!”

Sunny checked with Ms. Dot. “May I please use these two washers and this axle?”

“Yes,” said Ms. Dot. “Great thinking.”

Sunny slid the washers on the axle to hold the wheels steady. With careful turns of the screwdriver—twist, twist—the wheels sat straight. “Try again,” Sunny said.

Sunny pushed the cart. It rolled smooth and soft. “Shhhrrrrr,” went the wheels on the floor. Sunny grinned. “It works!”

“Can we paint it?” Miko asked. “I brought a brush.”

“Yes,” Sunny said, “but first we should ask if we may use paint here.”

Ms. Dot nodded. “You may use the washable paints. Please put paper under the cart.”

Sunny spread paper, like a picnic blanket. Miko dipped the brush in sunny yellow. Sunny dipped a finger in sky blue. Swish, swish. Dot, dot. They painted a big blue cloud on the side and tiny yellow stars on the wheels. The cart looked happy and bright.

Then, footsteps. Thump, thump. Bruno the big brown dog peeked in, tail wagging. “Hi, friends! What is that shiny cart?”

“It’s my Cozy Cart,” Sunny said. “Would you like to try a gentle pull?”

Bruno wagged faster. “Yes, please!” Bruno took the string, but Bruno tugged too hard. The cart bumped. Bump! Bump!

Sunny held up a paw. “Wait, Bruno. Gentle, please. We respect the cart and each other.”

Bruno paused and took a slow breath. “In. Out. Gentle,” Bruno said. He pulled softly. The cart rolled just right. Bruno smiled. “Thank you for reminding me.”

Sunny placed a soft cloth inside the cart, along with three acorns and a tiny bell. Ding! The bell sang a quiet hello.

Ms. Dot clapped. “You solved problems with calm breaths and big imagination. You asked before using. You were kind to each other.”

Sunny felt the warm glow of pride. “I learned that imagination helps me see new ways. Mindful breaths help me stay calm. Respect helps everyone share the space.”

Sunny, Miko, and Bruno took turns pulling the Cozy Cart around the workshop. The fan hummed, the radio sang, and the little bell chimed. Sunny’s tail curled like a happy comma.

“Today was a good day,” Sunny said. “We made, we breathed, we shared.”

“And we will put tools back,” Miko added.

They cleaned up, wiped the glue bottle, washed the brushes, and set the screwdriver on the stool. The workshop looked neat and peaceful again.

Sunny parked the Cozy Cart by the door. “Thank you, workshop,” Sunny whispered. “Thank you, friends.”

The End.
