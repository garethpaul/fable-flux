# Cubby’s Cozy Kitchen Plan

In a warm kitchen that smelled like cinnamon and honey, a friendly bear cub named Cubby wiggled his nose. The sunlight made little squares on the floor. The kettle hummed, and the wooden clock went tick-tock, tick-tock. Cubby loved this cozy place.

“I want to make pancakes!” Cubby said, clapping his paws. His tail nub wiggled with joy.

Mama Bear smiled and set a soft paw on the table. “Pancakes sound yummy. But first, we follow directions. That keeps our kitchen safe and our Earth happy.”

“Our Earth?” Cubby blinked. He looked at the shiny water tap and the big blue recycling bin by the door. He could smell fresh apples in a basket. He heard a little bee buzz at the window, visiting the flowers outside.

Mama Bear nodded. “Yes, Cubby. When we cook, we can be kind to our Earth. We call that sustainability. It means using only what we need and not wasting.”

Cubby stood tall. “I can do that! What do we do?”

Mama Bear slid a little notepad and a thick blue crayon across the table. “Let’s make a plan. A plan is a kind of strategy. We write it so we can follow directions.”

Cubby pressed the crayon to the paper. It felt a little waxy and warm in his paw. “What do I write?”

“Step one,” Mama Bear said. “Check what we already have.”

Cubby opened the cool, gentle-breathing fridge. “Eggs, milk, and blueberries!” He sniffed the berries. “They smell sweet.” He wrote big, bouncy letters: Eggs, milk, blue-ber-rees.

“Step two,” Mama Bear went on. “Use the oldest food first. That way, nothing gets wasted.”

Cubby peered into the fruit bowl. One banana had brown freckles. “This banana is ready now!” he said.

“Perfect,” said Mama Bear. “Banana pancakes today.”

Cubby wrote: Use freckle banana.

“Step three,” Mama Bear said. “Save water. Only turn on the tap when we need it.”

Cubby drew a little drop. Drip! He could almost hear it plink in the sink.

“Step four,” Mama Bear said. “Sort the trash. Food peels go in the compost, clean paper in recycling, and very little in the trash.”

Cubby nodded and drew three boxes. He colored one green for compost, one blue for recycling, and one gray for trash.

Cubby held up the paper. “I wrote our plan!” His chest felt warm and proud.

“Now we follow directions,” Mama Bear said. “Let’s begin.”

Cubby washed his paws, turning the water on, then off. “On to get them wet. Off while I scrub. On to rinse. Off again!” The water felt cool and slippery, and the soap smelled like lemon.

“Good saving,” Mama Bear said.

Cubby peeled the freckled banana. The peel made a soft rip. The banana was mushy and sweet. He put the peel in the green compost bin. Plop!

He poured the milk carefully. “Oops!” A little splash fell on the counter.

“Accidents happen,” Mama Bear said kindly.

Cubby frowned. “I got messy.”

“We make amends,” Mama Bear said, handing him a cloth. “That means we fix our mistake.”

Cubby wiped the milk in slow circles. The cloth felt squishy. “All clean,” he said, smiling again.

They mixed the batter. Cubby stirred with a wooden spoon. Swish, swish. The batter looked thick and creamy. The pan warmed on the stove. It made a little hiss. Cubby could feel the cozy heat on his fur.

“Now,” Mama Bear said, “we don’t need too much heat. Medium is enough. That saves energy.”

Cubby watched tiny bubbles pop on the pancake. Pop-pop-pop. He sniffed the sweet smell. “Time to flip?” he asked.

“Check your plan,” Mama Bear said.

Cubby read his paper. “We followed step one, two, and three. Now we are on step four, sorting. But flipping comes first, I think.”

Mama Bear laughed softly. “You’re right. Sometimes we add a step. Say it, and write it.”

Cubby spoke carefully. “Step five: Flip carefully.” He wrote Flip with a big F. He slid the spatula under the pancake. Flip! It landed with a gentle thwap. “I did it!”

They cooked a stack of pancakes with blueberries poking out like little moons. Cubby put the egg shells in the green compost and the empty milk carton in the blue recycling. He wiped the counter again and turned off the stove click.

They sat at the table. Cubby poured honey in a tiny swirl. The pancakes were warm and soft. The blueberries burst with juiciness. “Mmm,” he said. “We cooked and saved!”

Mama Bear nodded. “Tell me our strategy.”

Cubby pointed to each line with his crayon. “We wrote a plan. We used what we had. We saved water. We sorted trash. We used just enough heat. And when I made a mess, I made amends.”

“And we followed directions,” Mama Bear added. “That keeps us safe and helps the Earth.”

Cubby smiled big. He looked at the green compost bin by the door. He imagined the peels turning into rich dirt for the garden. He waved at the little bee outside the window. “Grow flowers, little bee. We’re helping!”

After breakfast, Cubby taped his plan to the fridge. The tape made a tiny rip-snap sound. “We can use this plan again,” he said. “For muffins. For soup. For anything!”

Mama Bear hugged him. Her fur was soft and smelled like honey. “Yes, Cubby. Small smart steps help every day. That is sustainability.”

Cubby took a breath of warm, cinnamon air. His tummy was full, and his heart felt proud and calm.

“I like our cozy kitchen,” he said. “I like our Earth, too.”

“We can care for both,” Mama Bear said. “One kind choice at a time.”

Cubby nodded. “Today I learned to make a plan, follow directions, fix my mistakes, and use only what I need. That helps the Earth.”

Mama Bear smiled. “That is a big, kind lesson.”

Cubby grinned. “Let’s write more plans!”

The End.
