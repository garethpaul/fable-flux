# Sunny the Seatbelt Bird

Sunny was a small, yellow bird who loved to sing. Her feathers were soft like a dandelion, and her voice sounded like tinkling bells. Sunny lived in a cozy nest near a quiet street. Every Saturday, she flew to visit her friend Mia and Mia’s family. Today, Mia’s family was going on a drive in the family car, and Sunny was invited.

“Chirp, chirp! I love car songs!” Sunny sang, fluttering her tiny wings.

Mia giggled and opened the car door. The car smelled like vanilla air freshener and warm sunshine. The seats were smooth and cool. Mia climbed in the back seat and clicked her seatbelt. Click! It sounded like a little “tick!” Sunny hopped onto the headrest and looked around with bright, curious eyes.

“Sunny,” Mia said gently, “you need to be safe too.”

Sunny puffed her chest. “But I’m a bird! I can balance,” she sang. She hopped and twirled. “I can sing and slide!”

Mia’s dad looked in the mirror. “Sunny, even birds need safety. Everybody stays buckled in the car.”

Mia’s mom nodded. “Safety is how we take care of ourselves and each other.”

Sunny felt a little confused. She loved to sing and dance. She did not like to sit still. She flapped her wings and chirped a quick tune. “Chirp-chirp, zoom-zoom! I want to sing and move!”

Mia reached out her hand. “Sunny, we can sing with you. But we sit safely first.” She pointed to a small pet carrier with a soft, blue towel inside. “We brought a cozy bird seat. It has a tiny seatbelt just for you.”

Sunny peeked inside. It smelled like clean cotton and a little like birdseed. The towel felt fluffy and kind. She rubbed her cheek on it and smiled. “It is soft,” she sang. “But can I still sing?”

“Of course,” said Mia’s dad. “We all can. Safety first, songs second.”

Sunny thought hard. She liked the soft towel. She liked Mia’s family. She did not like the idea of sitting still, but she wanted to be a part of the team. “Okay,” she chirped. “I will try.”

Mia’s mom gently lifted Sunny into the little carrier seat and clicked the tiny bird seatbelt. Click! Sunny felt snug, like a chick in a nest.

The car started. Vroom. The engine hummed like a big bumblebee. Trees waved hello outside the window. Sunny sang a gentle song. “Chirp, chirp, safe and snug. Chirp, chirp, comfy hug.”

Mia sang with her. “Safe and snug, comfy hug!”

They drove down the road, and the sun dazzled like a bright lemon. Suddenly, a red ball rolled into the street. A puppy chased the ball. Mia’s dad pressed the brakes. Squeak! The car slowed quickly. Sunny felt the seatbelt hold her gently in place. She did not slide. She did not bump. She stayed safe.

“Whoa,” Mia said, eyes wide. “That was close!”

Sunny’s heart fluttered fast, like tiny drumbeats. She took a deep breath. In and out. “My seatbelt kept me safe,” she whispered, surprised. “I am glad I tried it.”

The puppy’s owner held up a hand and mouthed “Thank you!” from the sidewalk. Mia’s dad nodded and drove slowly again. “We share the road,” he said. “That’s justice. Being fair means keeping others safe, too.”

Sunny tilted her head. “Justice is being fair and careful?”

“Yes,” Mia said. “We stop for puppies. We stop for people. We keep rules so everyone is safe.”

Sunny liked that. It felt kind. It felt right.

A few minutes later, another car zoomed by in a rush. Zoom! The driver was not wearing a seatbelt. Sunny could see the strap dangling. Mia frowned. “That’s not safe.”

Sunny felt a tiny tug in her heart. She wanted to help. She wanted to sing a safety song. But the other car was far away now. Sunny thought and thought. “What can we do when others don’t follow safety?” she asked.

Mia’s mom spoke softly. “We do our best. We follow the rules. We set a good example. We don’t shout. We keep calm. If someone is close to us and not safe, we can speak kindly.”

Sunny nodded. “Kind words help,” she said. “Chirp, chirp, kind and safe.”

They reached the store and parked. The air outside smelled like fresh rain and oranges. Mia unbuckled. Click. Sunny’s mom—no, Sunny’s seatbelt—clicked too. Sunny hopped out and stretched her wings. She felt proud. She had taken care of herself.

“Sunny,” Mia said, smiling, “you were brave and smart.”

Sunny fluttered. “I sat. I sang. I stayed safe.”

On the way home, Sunny had an idea. “Let’s make a safety song,” she chirped.

Mia clapped. “Yes!”

Sunny sang in her bell-bright voice:
“Click, click, seatbelts on,
Safe and snug till the ride is done.
Deep breaths in, voices kind,
Help our friends, be fair and mind.”

Mia’s family sang along. The car felt warm and happy. The sunlight made golden dots on the seats. Sunny’s heart felt light.

When they got back to Mia’s house, Sunny perched on the open door. She looked into the little bird seat with the blue towel. “Thank you, seatbelt,” she said. “You kept me safe. That is self-care. That is love.”

Mia hugged Sunny gently. “And when we are safe, we can help others be safe too.”

Sunny winked. “Justice and kindness, chirp by chirp.”

They all smiled. From then on, Sunny always clicked in before a song. She still twirled and sang, but now she did it with a cozy belt and a caring heart.

Lesson: Safety first means we take care of ourselves, we stay calm, and we are fair to others. That is how we solve problems together.

The End.
