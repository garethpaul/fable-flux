# Benny Beaver's Rooftop Garden

Benny was a busy beaver with bright eyes and a warm, woodsy smell. He wore a little green cap and a tool belt that jingled softly when he walked. Today, Benny climbed the stairs to the rooftop with a plan. The sun felt gentle on his fur, and a cool breeze tickled his whiskers. He could hear city birds chirp and feel the soft hum of traffic below.

“I’m going to make a rooftop garden,” Benny said to himself. “Plants will grow. Bees will visit. The air will feel fresh. This will help the earth.”

Benny opened his bag. He had small wooden planters he crafted himself, shiny watering cans, and seed packets with pictures of flowers and herbs. He set them out neatly in a row. Tap, tap, tap, went his little hammer as he fixed the planters to the rooftop railing to keep them safe.

Just then, Pippa Pigeon fluttered down and landed with a coo. She tilted her head and pecked at a seed packet. “Coo! What’s all this, Benny?”

“It’s a garden, Pippa,” Benny said with a smile. “Would you like to help plant?”

Pippa hopped closer. “Coo! I love seeds. But I also love my spot over there.” She pointed with her wing to the sunny corner. “That is my warm place.”

Benny looked. His planters were lined up… right in Pippa’s sunny spot. His tail gave a little thump. He felt proud of his hard work, but he also felt a tug in his heart.

“I didn’t know it was your spot,” Benny said softly. He imagined how Pippa felt—how nice the sun must feel on her feathers. He took a deep breath. “I’m sorry, Pippa. I can move some planters so you keep your warm place.”

Pippa’s eyes shone. “Coo, thank you!”

While they talked, Mia Mouse peeked out from a tiny vent with her brother Max. “Hi, Benny,” Mia squeaked. “We come out at night. We like it quiet. Will your garden be noisy?”

Benny thought about hammers and watering cans. He imagined how loud it might sound to small ears. He knelt down so he was closer to Mia and Max. “I hear you,” he said gently. “I will do the loud work in the daytime. At night, I will tiptoe. Is that okay?”

Mia and Max nodded, whiskers twitching. “That helps,” said Max.

Benny smiled. “I want us all to be comfy.”

He moved some planters away from the sunny corner. He found a new spot that was still bright. He placed the tall plants on the windy side, to block strong gusts. He set the shorter plants in the calm middle. He marked a quiet path with smooth stones, so he could walk softly at night. Tap, tap—no, not tap. Benny put down his hammer and pressed the last nails with gentle thumbs, saving the tapping for tomorrow.

Soon, their friend Ravi Raccoon popped up from the stairwell with a clatter. “Whoa! A garden!” His paws were dirty from exploring. “Need help, Benny?”

“Yes, please,” Benny said, standing tall but kind. “We are making a garden to help the earth. But this corner is Pippa’s warm spot. And Mia and Max like quiet. We can work together.”

Ravi grinned. “Got it! I’ll carry soil. Soft steps. Soft voice.”

Together they filled the planters. The soil smelled like rain and forests. It felt cool under Benny’s paws. “Squish, squish,” went the soil. Pippa gently tapped seeds with her beak. Mia and Max pat-pat-patted the earth with tiny paws. Ravi poured water, and the cans made a “glug-glug” sound. Benny read the seed packets in a slow, steady voice. “Lavender. Basil. Sunflowers. Marigolds.”

When Ravi reached for the last sunflower seeds, his paws moved too fast. The packet ripped, and seeds scattered across Pippa’s warm spot.

“Oh no!” Ravi gasped, ears low. “I made a mess.”

Pippa fluffed her feathers, ready to fuss. Benny stepped in. He put a paw on Ravi’s shoulder and spoke clearly. “Accidents happen. Let’s fix it together.”

Benny looked at Pippa with kind eyes. “I know your warm place is special. Can we gather the seeds and leave your spot clean and sunny?”

Pippa took a breath. She felt the warmth on her feathers. She also saw Ravi’s worried eyes. “Coo,” she said softly. “Yes. We can fix it together.”

They all got to work. Benny swept gently. Mia and Max picked up tiny seeds like treasure. Ravi held the bag open. Pippa guided them with her wing, “There’s one. And there.”

Soon, the spot was clean, and the seeds were safe in a new packet that Benny crafted from extra paper. He drew a big sunflower on it with a bright yellow crayon.

At last, the garden was ready. Little labels stood in the soil. The plants waved in the breeze. A soft, sweet smell rose up. Bees buzzed hello. The rooftop felt alive.

Benny stood with his friends. His heart felt wide and warm. “We did this together,” he said. “We listened. We were brave and kind. We kept the rooftop cozy for everyone. And we helped the earth.”

Pippa cooed happily in her sunny spot. Mia and Max squeaked, “Thank you!” Ravi grinned and gave a gentle thumbs-up.

Benny smiled. “When we care about each other’s feelings, we make better plans. That’s empathy. When we speak up kindly, that’s being brave and clear. That’s assertive. And when we work together, that’s cooperation. Our garden is our gift to the world.”

The sun dipped lower, and the air felt soft and sweet. Benny packed his tools quietly. He waved to his friends and took one last look at the green, growing rooftop.

“See you tomorrow,” he said. “We’ll water, we’ll watch, and we’ll keep caring.”

The End.
