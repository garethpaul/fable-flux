# Whiskers Chooses a Snack

The home office was quiet and cozy. The window was open a little. A soft breeze smelled like warm paper and fresh pencils. The computer made a tiny hum. Tick-tock went the clock.

A clever cat named Whiskers sat on the desk. His fur was soft like a blanket. His eyes were bright and curious. He loved to watch the mouse on the screen. He also loved snack time.

“Snack time!” called Maya from the doorway. She was Whiskers’s person. She wore big glasses and a kind smile.

Whiskers perked up. His tail swished. “Mrrr-ow?” he asked.

Maya held up two bowls. “We can choose a snack together,” she said. “We will make a good choice. We will think about our bodies. We will think about our home. We will think about the Earth.”

Whiskers sniffed the first bowl. Crunch-crunch treats that smelled sweet and salty. He sniffed the second bowl. Soft tuna with carrots and peas. It smelled like the ocean and a garden.

Maya put the bowls on the floor. “We have a sugar cookie treat,” she said, tapping one bowl. “And we have a tuna-veggie mix,” she said, tapping the other. “Let’s decide.”

Whiskers looked at the cookie treat. It was shiny in a plastic wrap. He poked it with his paw. Crinkle, crinkle.

Then he looked at the tuna-veggie mix. It sat in a little glass bowl with a green lid. Maya had made it at home. The lid had a small leaf on it.

“What should we choose?” asked Maya.

Whiskers tilted his head. “Mrrr?” He liked sweet smells. He liked easy snacks. But his tummy rumbled in a big way, not a small way.

Maya sat on the rug. “Let’s think,” she said kindly. “Good food helps our bodies grow strong. It helps our brains think well. It helps us feel calm. What food does that?”

Whiskers placed a paw on his chest. Thump, thump went his heart. He looked at the veggie mix again. He imagined crunchy peas. He imagined soft carrots. He imagined fish that made his whiskers wiggle.

Maya nodded. “Veggies and fish have good things inside. They give us energy. They help our eyes, our fur, our smiles.”

Whiskers peeked at the plastic wrap. He noticed the little trash can by the desk was full of wrappers. It looked sad and crowded.

Maya pointed to a small bin with a green sticker. “This is our compost bin,” she said. “Veggie bits and peels go here. They go back to the Earth. The Earth turns them into soil. That is fair to the Earth.”

Whiskers blinked slowly. Fairness felt warm in his chest. He liked fair. He liked taking turns with the world. He liked helping.

“Let’s decide together,” said Maya. “We can be a team. We can be fair to our bodies. We can be fair to the Earth. We can be kind to our tummies.”

Whiskers touched the glass bowl with his nose. Tap. He chose the tuna-veggie mix.

“Great choice,” Maya said. Her voice was happy like sunshine. “This is a smart snack. It is also less trash.”

Whiskers purred. Rrrr-rrr. Maya put a small spoonful on a little plate. The plate was blue with tiny stars. The food smelled gentle and good. Whiskers took a bite. Soft, smooth fish. Sweet carrots. Pop of peas. His mouth felt busy and happy.

“Yum,” said Maya, tasting a carrot from a separate bowl. “I like it too.”

Whiskers licked his lips. He felt full but not too full. He felt calm. His whiskers tingled in a nice way. He rubbed his head on Maya’s knee.

Then Whiskers heard a small rustle. He turned. A little spider sat by the window, watching a tiny fly. The spider waited. The fly buzzed away. The spider did not chase. It looked patient.

Whiskers watched with big eyes. “Mrrr?”

Maya whispered, “The spider waits. It makes careful choices too. Careful choices can be kind and smart.”

Whiskers nodded in his kitty way. He felt proud. He had waited and chosen.

Maya picked up the plastic wrap from the cookie treat. She paused. “We can save this for later, for a tiny sweet bite,” she said. “We can share it with friends. That is fair. And we can choose treats that make less trash next time.”

Whiskers tapped the compost bin with his paw. Maya laughed softly. “Yes,” she said. “We will compost the carrot peel. We will wash the glass bowl. We will use it again. Reuse means less waste.”

Tick-tock went the clock. The home office felt bright and clean. The breeze smelled like leaves. The computer hummed like a quiet bee.

Whiskers curled on the chair. He felt strong, smart, and kind. Maya stroked his back. “Today we learned,” she said gently. “Good food helps our bodies. Careful choices help our hearts. Reusing and composting help our Earth.”

Whiskers purred louder. He closed his eyes. Snack time had become think time. Think time had become feel-good time.

The End.
