# Pebble Paints on the Porch

Pebble the cheerful penguin loved to paint. He had a tiny blue hat, bright orange feet, and a happy waddle. One sunny day, Pebble sat on the peaceful front porch. The wooden boards felt warm and smooth under his feet. The breeze smelled like sweet flowers. Birds chirped soft songs in the trees.

On the porch, Pebble set out his art things: thick paper, bright paints, a cup of water, and three favorite brushes. “Today I will paint my best picture!” Pebble said. He dipped a brush into sunny yellow. Swish, swish. The brush made soft sounds. Pebble smiled. “I love art. Art helps my feelings.”

Pebble’s friend, Bobo the bunny, hopped over with a soccer ball. “Hi, Pebble! Want to play?” Bobo’s ears wiggled with joy.

Pebble looked at his paints. “I want to finish my art first,” he said kindly. “Then we can play sports.”

Bobo nodded. “Okay! I’ll practice kicks on the grass.” He bounced the ball and ran to the yard.

Pebble kept painting. He made a big round sun. He added a small house with a red door, just like his porch door. He painted Bobo kicking the ball. He painted himself waving hello. Swish, tap, dot! The colors were bright and happy.

Then, drip! Oh no. A flash of black and white—the neighbor’s kitten, Squeak, jumped onto the porch. Squeak’s tail swished. The kitten bumped the cup of water. Splash! Water spilled across the paper.

Pebble froze. The wet paper sagged. The yellow sun turned into a blurry blob. “My picture!” Pebble shouted. His chest felt tight. His cheeks felt hot. His flippers squeezed into little fists.

Bobo heard and ran back. “What happened?”

Squeak meowed and hid under the chair.

Pebble stomped once. Thump. “I’m so mad! I worked so hard.” A tiny tear rolled down his feathered cheek.

Bobo took a slow breath. “It’s okay to feel mad,” he said softly. “Try belly breaths. In like a balloon, out like a balloon.”

Pebble nodded. He put a flipper on his belly. In… puff. Out… whoosh. In… puff. Out… whoosh. His body felt softer. His chest felt calm.

Pebble looked at the wet paper. “I still feel sad,” he said. “But I feel better.” He looked under the chair. “Squeak, I know you didn’t mean it.”

Squeak peeked out and meowed. The kitten rubbed Pebble’s foot. Pebble patted the kitten’s head. “I forgive you.”

Bobo said, “You can start again. That is a growth mindset!”

Pebble blinked. “A growth mindset means I can learn and try again.”

Bobo smiled. “Yes! You can make a new plan.”

Pebble sat up tall. “New plan: I will make a new picture. I can use the splash to make art.” He dabbed a brush into blue and swirled it over the water spot. Swish, swirl, dab! The drip became a sparkling pond. Pebble added ducks. Quack, quack! He added lily pads, and a big green frog with round eyes.

He added Bobo kicking the soccer ball to the side of the pond. He added himself painting on the porch. He laughed. “I made art from a mistake!”

Bobo clapped. “It looks even better!”

Pebble felt proud. “Thank you, Bobo. Thank you, Squeak, for the new idea.”

Squeak purred and curled up on a dry corner of the porch.

“Now,” Bobo said, holding up the soccer ball, “sports time?”

Pebble looked at his brushes. He looked at the ball. “Yes! Let’s play for a bit, then I’ll finish the sky.”

They set a rule. “Honesty time,” Pebble said. “If the ball rolls near the paints, we tell the truth and stop.”

“Honest and safe,” Bobo said.

They played gentle soccer on the grass by the porch. Thump, roll, giggle! Pebble kicked with careful penguin feet. Bobo hopped and tapped the ball with soft paws. The grass tickled their toes. The breeze cooled their cheeks. When the ball rolled near the steps, Pebble called, “Stop!” They both stopped. “Thank you for being honest,” Pebble said.

After a short game, they sat back on the porch. Pebble washed his brushes. Swish in the water cup. Tap-tap on the edge. He painted a soft blue sky and tiny white clouds. He added a bright orange sun with friendly rays.

Bobo watched. “Your art shows your feelings.”

Pebble nodded. “Art helps me share my heart. When I was angry, I used belly breaths. I told the truth. I tried again. I grew.”

He signed his name at the bottom: Pebble P. Penguin. He held up the picture. “Ta-da!”

Bobo cheered. Squeak meowed. The birds sang. The porch felt peaceful again.

Pebble smiled wide. “Today I learned: Art can turn mistakes into magic. I can calm my anger with breaths. I can be honest. I can play and be safe. With a growth mindset, I can try again.”

Bobo said, “And your painting is beautiful.”

Pebble hugged his friends. “Thank you. Let’s hang the painting by the door.”

They taped it up where the sun could shine on it. The colors glowed warm and bright. Pebble’s heart felt warm too.

“Now,” Pebble said, “who wants a snack?”

“Me!” Bobo and Squeak said together, and they all laughed on the peaceful front porch.

The End.
