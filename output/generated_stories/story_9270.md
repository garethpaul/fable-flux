# Roo’s Band of Hops

Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and a bouncy tail. Today was music day! Roo hopped into the musical practice room, ears perking up at the sounds inside. The room smelled like fresh wood and lemon polish. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. Shiny drums, soft shakers, and colorful xylophones waited on a big table.

“Good morning!” Roo chirped. “I’m ready to hop and play!”

Roo’s friends were there too. Tiko the turtle tapped a drum with gentle flippers. Bella the bluebird fluffed her feathers and held tiny bells. Max the mouse hugged a small triangle. Mia the monkey twirled a ribbon and picked up a shaker.

Coach Song, a kind teacher with a warm smile, clapped softly. “Hello, band! Today we will make music together. We will use teamwork.”

Roo bounced high. “I can play the drum! I can play the bells! I can play all of them!”

Coach Song shook her head kindly. “We take turns. We listen. We share. That is teamwork.”

Roo wiggled, trying to stand still. The drum looked so fun. The bells looked sparkly. The triangle went ding!

Coach Song raised a hand. “First, we will play a steady beat. Listen to my clap.” Clap, clap, clap. It sounded like footsteps on a path. “Who would like the drum?”

Tiko lifted a flipper. “I can try.”

Roo’s tail twitched. “But I want the drum.”

Tiko looked shy. “I can keep it soft.”

Roo stared at the shiny drum. His heart felt wiggly. He took a big breath. Coach Song smiled. “Roo, we respect our friends. Tiko asked first.”

Roo nodded. “Okay. I can wait.” He picked up a shaker instead. It felt smooth in his paw and made a soft swish-swish.

Coach Song counted, “One, two, three, four.” Tiko tapped the drum: bom, bom, bom, bom. Roo shook the shaker: swish, swish, swish, swish. Bella chimed the bells: ching, ching. Max went ding on the triangle. Mia’s ribbon swooshed like a breeze.

The room filled with gentle sound. Roo smiled. “We sound good!”

Coach Song said, “Now let’s try louder, then softer. We will follow signals. Watch my hands.”

Her hands lifted high. The band played louder. Her hands floated low. The band played soft, soft, soft. Roo listened and matched the sound. It felt like riding a wave. His feet stopped bouncing so much. He felt calm and proud.

Then Coach Song said, “Let’s switch instruments. It’s Roo’s turn for the drum.”

Roo’s ears stood tall. “Really? Thank you, Tiko!”

Tiko nodded. “We can share.”

Roo sat up straight and held the drumsticks. The drum felt warm and smooth. “I will keep the beat,” he said. “I will be steady.”

“Ready,” Coach Song said. “One, two, three, four.”

Roo tapped: bom, bom, bom, bom. He kept it slow and strong. The beat felt like a heartbeat. Bella added ching-ching. Max dinged at the end of each four. Mia shook the shaker like falling rain. Tiko clapped gently to help.

Roo watched Coach Song’s hands and listened to his friends. When Bella needed space, Roo played softer. When Max was ready to ding, Roo paused a tiny bit so the triangle could sparkle. Roo felt proud in his chest. He was being a good teammate.

Suddenly, Roo got excited. He started to drum faster. Bom-bom-bom-bom-bom!

Bella fluttered. “Too fast!”

Max squeaked, “I cannot find my ding!”

Roo stopped. His cheeks felt hot. “Oh. I went too fast. I’m sorry, friends.” His voice was small but honest. “I will slow down and listen.”

Coach Song nodded. “That is integrity, Roo. You told the truth and fixed it. Now try again. Watch. Listen. Work together.”

Roo tried again. Bom... bom... bom... bom. He looked at Bella’s wings, at Max’s little paws, at Mia’s ribbon. He heard Tiko’s claps. He felt the group. The music came together like puzzle pieces.

Coach Song beamed. “Beautiful teamwork! Now, let’s make a song about hopping.”

“Hop, hop, hop,” sang Bella.

“Ding at the top,” sang Max.

“Shake, shake, shake,” giggled Mia.

“Steady beat, don’t stop,” clapped Tiko.

Roo added words. “We listen. We share. We care!”

They played and sang. The drum was steady. The bells were bright. The triangle sparkled. The shaker whispered. The room hummed with happy sound. Roo’s paws tingled, and his tail swished with the beat.

When the song ended, the last ding floated in the air like a tiny star. The band was quiet. Everyone was smiling.

Roo said, “We did it together!”

Coach Song gave a gentle clap. “Yes. Teamwork makes music strong. Respect helps friends belong.”

Roo nodded. “I learned to wait, to listen, to share, and to tell the truth.”

Tiko tapped his shell. “And to keep the beat!”

Bella chirped, “And to leave space for others.”

Max squeaked, “And to watch and coordinate.”

Mia twirled. “And to have fun!”

Roo laughed. “When we work together, our song feels happy in my heart.”

Coach Song said, “Remember this: good teammates listen, take turns, and help each other shine.”

Roo hugged the drum. “Next time, I can help count us in.”

“Teamwork!” they all cheered.

The End.
