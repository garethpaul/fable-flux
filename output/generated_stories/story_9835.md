# Mimi Mouse Makes Music

Mimi was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright button eyes. She lived near a creative art room where children painted, glued, and sang. The art room smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Sunlight made rainbow shapes on the floor through colorful window stickers. Brushes clinked in jars. Paper rustled. And today, instruments waited on a low shelf: a small drum, shakers, chimes, and a tiny xylophone with rainbow bars.

Mimi’s whiskers twitched. “I love music,” she whispered. Her heart made a happy thump-thump. “Maybe I can make a gentle song for everyone.”

She tiptoed inside. The room was quiet before class. Mimi listened. She could hear the hum of the air vent and the soft tick-tock of a wall clock. She smiled and patted the little drum. Tap-tap. It sounded like raindrops. She shook a blue shaker. Chk-chk! It sounded like crunching leaves.

“Hello,” said a deep voice from behind a stack of paper. A tall puppet with yarn hair popped up from a puppet theater. It was Mr. Wiggles, the classroom puppet. “What brings you here, little friend?”

“I’m Mimi,” she said. “I want to make music that feels kind.”

“That is a lovely plan,” said Mr. Wiggles. “Remember: be safe and gentle. Use instruments with care.” He pointed his soft puppet hand at a poster that said, “Kind Hands. Safe Sounds. Take Turns.”

Mimi nodded. “Kind hands. Safe sounds. Take turns. I can do that.”

Just then, the door opened. Children came in, giggling and stomping in squeaky shoes. Their teacher, Miss Lila, clapped softly. “Welcome, artists! Today we will paint to music. Choose an instrument and a friend.”

The room buzzed with excitement. A boy named Max grabbed the big drum. Boom-BOOM! It was very loud. A girl named Aya took the chimes and made a twinkle-twinkle sound. Another child, Jo, shook two shakers at the same time, fast and faster, until it sounded like a storm. Mimi’s ears folded back. The loud boom-BOOM made her tummy feel wiggly. She hid behind the paint easel.

“I want to play too,” Mimi said in a tiny voice. “But it’s too loud for me.”

Miss Lila noticed the little mouse peeking. “Oh! Hello, little visitor,” she said softly. “Are you listening to our music?”

Mimi nodded. “Yes. I like gentle sounds. But the big drum scares me.”

Max heard her. He stopped for a moment. “I like loud,” he said. “Loud feels strong!”

Aya smiled at Mimi. “I like soft,” she said. “Soft feels calm.”

Mr. Wiggles popped up again. “We can all like different sounds,” he said. “That’s okay. Let’s celebrate our differences. How can we keep everyone safe and happy?”

Mimi took a deep breath. She remembered “Kind Hands. Safe Sounds. Take Turns.” She felt brave. “Excuse me,” she said, stepping out from the easel. “Can we make a plan? I like music too. Can we have loud-and-soft time?”

Miss Lila nodded. “Wonderful idea, Mimi. That is assertive and kind. Class, let’s try a music rainbow. Red time is loud, bright sounds. Blue time is quiet, gentle sounds. Green time is a mix. We will take turns.”

Max grinned. “I can boom in red time!”

“And I’ll chime in blue time,” Aya said.

Mimi felt warm and brave. “I will play the xylophone in blue time,” she said. “I will use one soft mallet.”

Miss Lila set a sand timer. “When the sand is at the top, it’s red time. When it’s halfway, green time. When it’s almost gone, blue time. Ready?”

The sand whooshed. Red time began. Max played the drum, but Miss Lila reminded, “Safe hands, safe ears.” Max patted the drum with the flat of his hand. Boom-boom, not BOOM-BOOM. Jo shook the shakers in a steady beat. Chk-chk, chk-chk. Mimi covered one ear with her paw and counted her breaths. She felt okay. The sound was big but not scary.

Then came green time. Max softened. Aya added chimes. Jo shook slower. Mimi touched a rainbow bar. Ting! It sounded like a friendly bell. Together they made a garden of sounds—big flowers and tiny seeds.

Finally, blue time. The room grew quiet. Brushes swished on paper. Mimi played the xylophone with little taps. Ting-ting, tiiiing. Aya brushed the chimes like wind in leaves. The children painted swirls and stars. Max rested his hands on the drum and hummed. “Hmmmm.”

Mimi’s whiskers trembled with joy. “Listen,” she said. “We are making a song with our differences.”

When the sand ran out, Miss Lila clapped softly. “Beautiful music, everyone. How did that feel?”

Max smiled. “I felt strong in red time. I felt calm in blue time. I can do both.”

Aya nodded. “I liked being gentle. It helped my painting.”

Mimi stood tall. “I felt safe because we had a plan. I asked for what I needed.”

Mr. Wiggles wiggled. “And you all learned to listen, take turns, and care for each other. That is music for the heart.”

Miss Lila wrote on the board: Our Music Rules
- Kind hands
- Safe sounds
- Take turns
- Ask for what you need
- Celebrate differences

The children cheered. Mimi tapped the last note—ting!—and the sound hung in the sunny air like a tiny star.

Mimi smiled at her new friends. “We made music that fits everyone,” she said.

“Yes,” said Miss Lila. “Music is for all of us. Loud and soft. Fast and slow. Different and together.”

Mimi’s heart sang. She had been brave. She had asked for safety. She had shared her gentle song. And the whole room had learned to listen and care.

The End.
