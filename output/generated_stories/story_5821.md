# Ollie Otter's Big Day

Ollie the otter loved to play in the old garden shed behind his house. The wooden walls smelled like fresh pine, and sunshine streamed through the dusty windows, making everything sparkle.

One morning, Mama Otter called from the kitchen. "Ollie! Today we need to clean the shed, organize your toys, and plant flowers. We have lots to do!"

"Okay, Mama!" Ollie squeaked happily. But when he got to the shed, he saw his favorite ball sitting in a sunny spot. *Bounce, bounce, bounce!* Soon he was rolling around, completely forgetting about his jobs.

Time ticked by. The sun moved higher in the sky.

"Ollie?" Mama called. "How's the cleaning going?"

Ollie looked around. Toys were scattered everywhere. Dirt covered the floor. Nothing was done! His tummy felt tight and worried.

"Um... almost finished!" he called back, even though it wasn't true.

But Ollie felt bad about not being honest. His whiskers drooped, and his fluffy tail stopped swishing. He started throwing toys into boxes quickly - *crash, bang, clatter!* This wasn't working at all.

Soon Mama Otter appeared in the doorway. She saw the messy shed and Ollie's sad face.

"Oh, sweetie," she said gently. "What happened?"

Ollie's eyes filled with tears. "I'm sorry, Mama. I played instead of working. I said I was almost done, but I wasn't being honest. Now I'm angry at myself, and everything is still messy!"

Mama Otter sat down and pulled Ollie close. Her fur was warm and smelled like lavender soap. "Thank you for telling me the truth now. That was brave and honest."

"But I messed up everything!" Ollie sniffled.

"Everyone makes mistakes," Mama said. "The important thing is learning from them. Let's figure out how to use our time better."

Mama helped Ollie take three deep breaths to feel calmer. "When we have big jobs, we can break them into small pieces," she explained. "And we can set a timer to help us stay focused."

They found an old kitchen timer that went *tick-tick-tick*. "Let's clean for 10 minutes, then take a 5-minute play break," Mama suggested.

*Ding!* went the timer.

Ollie picked up blocks and put them in the blue box. The timer helped him remember to keep working. When it rang again, he got to bounce his ball for five whole minutes!

*Ding!* Time to work again.

This time, Ollie organized his books on the shelf. The colorful covers looked like a rainbow. Working felt good when he could see his progress!

After three work sessions, the shed looked amazing. Toys were sorted, the floor was swept clean, and everything had its place.

"Now for the flowers!" said Mama.

They planted bright yellow marigolds outside the shed. The soil felt cool and crumbly in Ollie's paws. The flowers smelled sweet and sunny.

"We did it!" Ollie cheered, doing a happy little dance.

"Yes, we did," Mama smiled. "You learned to use time wisely, follow directions, and be honest about your feelings. That shows real leadership!"

That evening, Ollie sat in his clean, organized shed. He felt proud and happy. Tomorrow, he would remember to use his timer and break big jobs into small pieces.

"Time management is like swimming," he told his reflection in the window. "When you take it one stroke at a time, you can go anywhere!"

The End.
