# Pippa the Parrot and the Sock Day Surprise

Pippa the parrot loved colors. Her feathers were red, blue, and green, like a rainbow. She liked to say, “Bright and bold!” when she saw pretty things.

One sunny morning, Pippa hopped into the laundry room. The room smelled like lemon soap and warm cotton. The washing machine hummed, “Whirr, whirr,” and the dryer went, “Bump, bump.” A basket sat on the floor, full of clean socks—striped socks, polka-dot socks, tiny socks, and long socks.

Pippa tilted her head. “So many colors! So many shapes!” she chirped. “Let’s craft something bright and bold!”

She picked up a sock with little stars. She rubbed it against her cheek. “Soft,” she said, smiling. She picked up a blue sock. “Blue like the sky,” she sang. Her claws clicked on the tile as she danced. “Crafting time! Crafting time!”

Just then, a gray cat named Tilly padded in. Her tail swished, and her whiskers twitched. “Pippa, those are clean socks,” Tilly said softly. “We need to match them. They belong in pairs.”

Pippa fluffed her feathers. “I have an idea,” she said. “I want to make a sock snake. It will be long and silly. It will help us remember to be brave and kind.”

Tilly tilted her head. “A sock snake? Hmm.”

Pippa took a deep breath and stood tall. “I feel excited about my craft,” she said clearly. “I want to try. Can we make it together?”

That was Pippa being assertive. She used her voice. She said what she wanted, kind and clear.

Tilly sat beside the basket. She listened. “I hear you,” Tilly purred. “A craft could be fun. But we must ask before using other people’s things.”

Pippa blinked. She had not asked. Her tummy felt twisty. “You are right,” she said. She looked at the door. “Mama is in the kitchen. I will ask.”

Pippa hopped to the doorway. “Mama!” she called. “May I use some socks for a craft? Just the ones who lost their pairs?”

Mama’s voice floated back, warm as the dryer. “Yes, Pippa. You may use the lonely socks in the bottom of the basket. Thank you for asking.”

Pippa’s tummy untwisted. She flapped her wings happily. “Thank you, Mama!”

Back in the laundry room, Pippa and Tilly picked lonely socks. A red sock with no match. A yellow sock with a tiny hole. A long green sock with stripes. The pile smelled fresh, like sunlight.

Pippa said, “We need string.” She found a roll of soft yarn on a shelf. She found buttons in a small tin. “Clink, clink,” went the buttons. She found safety glue that was safe for crafts. It smelled a little sweet.

“First, we line up the socks,” Pippa said. “Red, blue, green, yellow. Bright and bold!”

Tilly nodded. “I will hold the string with my paws.”

They worked together. Pippa tied knots slowly. “One loop. Pull tight.” Tilly pressed the socks flat with gentle paws. “Smooth, smooth,” she said. Pippa squeezed glue on buttons for eyes. “Blink, blink!”

The washing machine sang, “Whirr,” like a happy song. The dryer made warm air. It felt cozy on Pippa’s feathers. She hummed as she worked. Tilly purred a soft beat.

But then Pippa rushed. She skipped checking a knot. The sock snake fell apart—plop! Buttons rolled across the floor. “Clickety-click!”

Pippa’s eyes went wide. Her chest felt tight. “Oh no,” she said. “I messed up.”

Tilly touched her with her tail. “It’s okay. We can learn. Try again, slow and steady.”

Pippa took a deep breath. In. Out. She felt the warm air on her beak. “I can try again,” she said. “I can learn. One step at a time.”

She picked up the socks. “I’m sorry, Tilly,” Pippa said. “I hurried. I did not check the knot.”

“I forgive you,” Tilly purred. “Thank you for saying sorry.”

Pippa tried again, slower this time. “Loop. Pull. Check.” She tugged each knot, gentle but firm. Tilly counted, “One, two, three,” and kept the line straight. Pippa added soft felt for a tongue. “Flick, flick!” She glued the button eyes and waited. “Patience,” she said. “We let it dry.”

They listened to the dryer, “Bump, bump,” and watched the glue set. The sock snake grew long—red, blue, green, yellow, polka dots at the end. It was silly and wonderful.

Pippa lifted the snake. It wiggled and waggled. “Sss-hello!” she chirped in a tiny snake voice. Tilly giggled.

Mama stepped into the doorway. She smiled. “What a bright and bold snake! Tell me what you learned.”

Pippa stood tall. “I used my voice to share my idea,” she said. “I asked before taking things. I said sorry when I rushed. I tried again and did not give up.”

Tilly added, “We worked together. Slow and steady makes strong knots.”

Pippa wrapped the sock snake around the laundry basket like a hug. The room smelled like clean cotton and sweet glue. The hum of the machines sounded like a happy purr.

Pippa chirped, “Crafting can be kind. Crafting can be brave. Crafting can grow our hearts and our hands.”

Mama nodded. “Yes. You were thoughtful, assertive, and patient.”

Pippa fluffed her bright feathers. “Bright and bold!”

The sock snake seemed to smile with button eyes. Everyone laughed.

The End.
