# Splash's Big Mistake

Splash the dolphin loved visiting the study corner at the aquarium. It was a quiet place with soft blue lights and floating books that never got wet. Children came here to read and learn about ocean life.

Today, Splash swam excitedly through the glass tunnel. The study corner smelled like fresh sea salt and had the gentle sound of bubbles floating up. Splash's favorite teacher, Miss Marina, was setting up for story time.

"Good morning, Splash!" said Miss Marina. "I have healthy kelp snacks for everyone today."

Splash's tummy rumbled. The green kelp strips looked crunchy and delicious. But there was a big sign that said "Please wait for snack time."

When Miss Marina turned to arrange the books, Splash couldn't help himself. He quickly grabbed three kelp strips with his flipper and ate them. *Crunch, crunch, crunch!* They tasted salty and sweet.

Soon, other sea creatures arrived. There was Shelly the turtle, Finn the small fish, and Ollie the octopus. They all sat in a circle, ready for story time.

"Time for our healthy snacks!" announced Miss Marina. She counted the kelp strips. "Oh my! We're missing some snacks. Did anyone see what happened?"

Splash's tummy felt funny. Not because the kelp was bad, but because he felt worried. He looked at his friends' faces. They looked confused and sad.

"Maybe they fell on the floor?" suggested Shelly.

"Or floated away?" said Finn in his tiny voice.

Miss Marina looked around carefully. "These snacks are important. They help us grow strong and healthy. Without enough for everyone, some friends might go hungry."

Splash felt bubbles of worry in his chest. His friends were trying to solve the mystery, but he knew the truth. He watched Ollie use his eight arms to look under every book. He saw Finn swimming in circles, searching everywhere.

"I have an idea!" said Ollie. "Let's all work together to find them!"

But Splash couldn't watch his friends work so hard anymore. His heart felt heavy like a big anchor.

"Wait," said Splash, swimming to the middle of the circle. "I need to tell you something."

Everyone stopped and looked at him with their kind eyes.

"I... I ate the missing kelp strips," Splash said quietly. "I was hungry and couldn't wait. I'm really sorry."

The study corner became very quiet except for the gentle bubble sounds.

Miss Marina swam closer to Splash. "Thank you for telling the truth, Splash. That took courage."

"But now there isn't enough for everyone," said Splash sadly. "I made a big mistake."

"You did make a mistake," said Miss Marina gently. "But telling the truth was the right thing to do. That's what matters most."

Shelly swam over and patted Splash's fin. "We all make mistakes sometimes."

"What can I do to fix it?" asked Splash.

Miss Marina smiled. "Well, I have extra kelp in the storage area. But next time, what could you do differently?"

"I could ask first," said Splash. "And wait my turn, even when I'm really hungry."

"That's exactly right!" said Miss Marina.

She brought out more kelp strips, and everyone shared the healthy snacks together. They were even more delicious because everyone had enough.

"Telling the truth feels much better than keeping a secret," said Splash, munching happily.

"And we're still friends," added Finn with a big smile.

From that day on, Splash always remembered to be honest, even when it felt scary. He learned that truth-telling made friendships stronger and helped everyone feel safe and happy together.

The End.
