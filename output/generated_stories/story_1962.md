# Piko Builds a Kind Day

Piko was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a splash of blue on his wings. He lived with Mia and her family. One sunny morning, Piko sat on the back of a chair in the dining room. The table was smooth and shiny, and it smelled like warm toast and oranges.

“Good morning, Piko,” Mia said, patting his soft feathers. “Do you want to build something today?”

“Build! Build!” Piko squawked, bobbing his head. His eyes sparkled. “Blocks! Boxes! Big ideas!”

On the dining room table, there were wooden blocks, empty cereal boxes, paper tubes, and safe child scissors. There was tape and big square napkins that felt soft and crinkly. The sunlight made shapes on the table, like little warm squares.

Mia smiled. “Let’s make a tiny town. Then we can take it outside and play.”

Piko flapped his wings gently. “Tiny town! Chirp-chirp town!” He picked up a red block with his beak and set it down carefully. Tap. He listened to the small sound it made on the table. Tap. Tap.

They stacked blocks for houses. They taped boxes to make garages. Piko put a blue napkin on top of a box. “Roof! Blue roof!” he said. He rubbed his cheek on it. “Soft.”

Mia cut windows in a paper tube. “This can be a tower,” she said. “Look, Piko. It needs to be steady.”

“Steady,” Piko repeated. He pressed the tape with the tip of his beak. “Stick, please.” The tape made a sticky zzzt sound as Mia pulled it. Piko giggled. “Zzzt!”

They made a wide road with brown paper. Piko used a crayon to draw a tiny yellow line down the middle. His feathers brushed the paper, whisper, whisper. He sniffed. The crayon smelled waxy and sweet.

Suddenly, Piko heard a small sniffle from the doorway. A little boy, Tom, from next door stood there with his dad. Tom’s eyes were watery. He held a broken toy truck in his hands.

“Hi, Tom,” Mia said. “What’s wrong?”

“My truck wheel fell off,” Tom said softly. “I can’t play.”

Piko tilted his head. His heart felt warm, like the sun on the table. He hopped closer. “Sad?” he asked gently.

Tom nodded. “Yes.”

Piko looked at the tiny town. He looked at Tom’s truck. “We build,” Piko said. “We can help.”

Mia smiled. “Great idea, Piko. Tom, would you like to build a new truck garage while we figure out the wheel?”

Tom sniffled and smiled a little. “Okay.”

They cleared a space on the dining room table. Piko slid over a small box. “Garage,” he chirped. He pressed the box flat so it sat steady. Mia gave Tom some tape. “You can tape the door,” she said.

Tom pressed the tape. Zzzt. He giggled. “It sounds funny.”

“Zzzt!” Piko echoed, and everyone laughed.

Mia gently looked at the toy truck. “The wheel can go back on with a clip,” she said to Tom’s dad. “I’ll grab one.” She walked to the drawer and came back with a small clip.

Piko watched with wide eyes. “Fix. Fix,” he whispered. He felt happy to help. He liked seeing Tom’s face get brighter.

While Mia fixed the wheel, Piko and Tom made a sign. Piko held a crayon in his claws. Tom held the paper. Together they drew big letters. “T-O-M,” Mia read. “Tom’s Garage!”

Tom beamed. “That’s me!”

The air smelled like crayons and oranges. The table felt smooth under their hands and claws. Piko listened to the soft snip of scissors and the tap of blocks. The town was almost done.

Click! Mia snapped the wheel in place. The truck rolled smoothly across the table. Tom gasped. “It works!”

Piko bounced. “Vroom! Vroom!” He gently rolled the truck into the garage. “Home.”

Tom hugged the truck. “Thank you, Piko. Thank you, Mia.”

Mia looked out the window. The backyard trees waved in the soft wind. Birds chattered. “Let’s take our tiny town outside,” she said. “The grass will be a park.”

“Outdoor play!” Piko cheered.

They carried the town to the patio. The warm sun touched their cheeks. The grass felt cool and tickly on their toes. The breeze smelled like leaves and fresh dirt. They placed the roads on the patio stones and set the tower near a flowerpot.

Tom drove his truck along the paper road. Vroom. Piko set up little block benches. “For friends,” he said. “Sit, sit.”

A ladybug crawled across the blue napkin roof. Piko whispered, “Hello, tiny builder.” He watched the ladybug’s little feet. Tick-tick-tick.

Mia clapped. “We built together. We took it outside. We helped a friend.”

Piko puffed his feathers proudly. “Build with care,” he said. “Help makes happy.”

Tom nodded. “I felt sad. You helped me. Now I feel good.”

They played until the shadows got long like stretched-out blankets. When it was time to go in, they carefully lifted the town back to the dining room table. Piko smoothed the paper road with his wing. “Safe and steady,” he said softly.

Mia looked at Piko and Tom. “What did we learn today?”

Piko raised a wing. “We build strong. We play outside. We care for friends.”

Tom smiled. “Building can fix problems. Sharing and helping feels good.”

Piko snuggled onto the chair back. His eyes were sleepy and warm. “Tomorrow,” he whispered, “we build more.”

The End.
