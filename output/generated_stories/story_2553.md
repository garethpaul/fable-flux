# Milo's Cozy Plan

Milo the courageous monkey woke up in his cozy bedroom. His room was warm and soft, with a fuzzy blue rug and a little night lamp that glowed like a tiny moon. The morning light peeked through the curtains, making golden stripes on the floor. Milo stretched his arms and tail. He took a deep breath. The air smelled like clean blankets and banana muffins from the kitchen.

“Good morning, Milo,” he said to himself, smiling. “Today will be a good day.”

Milo loved adventures, even small ones. But today, he wanted to learn something big: how to make a plan. He patted his chest. “I am brave and smart,” he whispered. “I can make a plan.”

He hopped to his little desk. On the desk were crayons, a calendar with stickers, and a notepad that said, “Milo’s Plan.” He picked a green crayon and drew three big boxes.

“Step 1: Take care of me,” he said. “Step 2: Help my home. Step 3: Be kind and forgive.”

Milo smiled. “A plan helps my day feel calm,” he said. “A plan helps me be well and kind.”

He looked at Step 1. “Wellness first!” Milo said. He listened to his tummy. Grumble, grumble. “I hear you, tummy,” he giggled. He planned a healthy breakfast. He drew a banana, a bowl of oats, and a cup of water.

Then he did his morning stretch on the soft rug. “Reach up high, touch the sky,” he sang. His arms felt warm. His toes felt tingly. He brushed his teeth and felt the minty bubbles pop-pop-pop. He washed his face with warm water. Drip, drip, drip. “I feel clean and strong,” Milo said. “Step 1 is done!”

Milo looked at Step 2. “Help my home,” he read. “That is civic responsibility,” he said slowly, practicing the big words. “It means I help my place and my people.”

He planned to clean up his cozy bedroom. He made a list: “Pick up toys. Fold blanket. Water plant.” He put on some bouncy music and began. “Pick, fold, water,” he sang. He placed blocks in a box. Click, clack. He folded his yellow blanket so it was smooth and neat. He gave his little green plant a tiny drink. “Sip-sip,” he said, pretending to be the plant. The room looked bright and happy.

Just then, there was a soft knock. Tap, tap.

“Hi, Milo,” said Tika the tiny squirrel, peeking in. “Can we play?”

“I planned to help my home first,” Milo said kindly, pointing to his list. “Then we can play.”

“I can help,” said Tika.

“Thank you!” said Milo. They worked together. They laughed when a sock tried to hide under the bed. “Sneaky sock!” Milo said.

Soon, the room was tidy. “Step 2 is done!” Milo cheered. He put a star sticker on his plan.

Milo looked at Step 3. “Be kind and forgive.” He thought about yesterday. His friend Bobo the bear cub had bumped his block tower and it fell down. Crash! Milo had felt mad. His cheeks got hot. He had crossed his arms. But last night, in bed, he had thought about it. Maybe it was an accident. Maybe Bobo needed a friend.

Milo planned to talk to Bobo today. He practiced in the mirror. “I felt sad when my tower fell,” he said softly. “But I forgive you.” He looked at his brave eyes in the mirror. “I can say how I feel. I can forgive.”

Tika tugged his sleeve. “I hear footsteps,” she whispered.

There was another knock. Tap, tap.

“Hi, Milo,” Bobo said, eyes looking down at his paws. “I am sorry about the tower.”

Milo took a breath, just like his plan said. In, out. Calm and steady. “I felt sad,” Milo said, “but I forgive you, Bobo.”

Bobo’s face brightened. “Thank you,” he said.

Milo’s tummy felt warm, like a blanket hug. “Would you both like to help me with one more plan?” he asked. “Let’s make a kindness chart for our street. We can plan ways to help.”

“That is civic responsibility!” Tika said proudly.

They sat on the fuzzy rug. Scratch-scratch went the crayons. They drew boxes and hearts. They planned: “Pick up litter with grown-ups.” “Share toys.” “Say please and thank you.” “Rest when tired.” “Drink water.” “Use calm words.”

Milo added little pictures: a tiny trash bag, a smiling water cup, a sun wearing sunglasses. They giggled.

“Before we go outside later,” Milo said, “we check Step 1 again. Are we well?” They each drank water. Gulp, gulp. They each took two slow breaths. In, out. Their bodies felt ready.

Milo looked around his cozy bedroom. It smelled clean. The plant leaves looked shiny. The rug felt soft under their feet. The lamp glowed gently.

“I like our plan,” Milo said. “It helps us feel safe. It helps us do good.”

Tika nodded. “It helps me know what to do next.”

Bobo smiled. “It helps my heart feel light.”

Milo placed one more star on the page. “Planning helps me be brave and kind,” he said. “When we plan, we take care of ourselves, help our home, and forgive.”

They all bumped fists. “Team Plan!” they cheered.

That night, Milo tucked into bed. The blankets were warm. The room was calm and cozy. He looked at his plan with shiny star stickers. He felt proud.

“Tomorrow,” he whispered, “I will make a new plan.”

He closed his eyes and smiled. His heart felt quiet and happy.

The End.
