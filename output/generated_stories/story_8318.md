# Shelly's Fair Share Garden

In the cozy study corner of the classroom, a shy little turtle named Shelly sat quietly by herself. She loved to read books about gardens and healthy foods. The soft cushions felt warm under her green shell, and the morning sunlight streamed through the window.

Shelly watched as the other animals played together. They were planning a classroom garden! But when it came time to share the seeds, something wasn't right.

"I want ALL the carrot seeds!" squeaked Max the mouse, grabbing the whole packet.

"And I'll take ALL the lettuce seeds!" chirped Penny the parrot, snatching them away.

Poor Benny the bunny sat sadly with empty paws. "But what about me?" he whispered. "I love carrots and lettuce too."

Shelly's heart felt heavy. This wasn't fair! Everyone should get to plant seeds. She pulled her head into her shell, feeling too shy to speak up.

From inside her shell, Shelly heard Benny sniffle. "I guess I won't have any vegetables to eat," he said sadly.

That made Shelly feel even worse. She knew it was wrong when someone didn't get their fair share. Taking a deep breath, she slowly poked her head out.

"Um... excuse me," Shelly said in her quiet voice.

Max and Penny didn't hear her tiny voice.

Shelly tried again, a little louder. "Excuse me, please!"

This time, everyone looked at the shy turtle.

"That's not fair," Shelly said, her voice getting stronger. "Everyone should get some seeds. When we share fairly, everyone can grow healthy food."

Max's whiskers twitched. "But I really want lots of carrots!"

"Sharing means everyone gets some," Shelly explained gently. "If we each take only what we need, there's enough for everyone. That's what being fair means."

Shelly crawled over to the seed packets. With her small claws, she carefully divided the seeds into equal piles. "See? Now Max gets carrot seeds, Penny gets lettuce seeds, and Benny gets some of each!"

Benny's ears perked up. "Really? For me too?"

"Of course!" smiled Shelly. "Everyone deserves healthy food to grow."

The teacher, Mrs. Owl, hooted approvingly. "Shelly is absolutely right. When we share fairly, everyone wins!"

Max looked at his pile of seeds, then at Benny's happy face. "You know what? This feels much better than taking everything."

"Me too!" agreed Penny. "Sharing makes me feel warm and happy inside."

Together, all four friends planted their seeds in small pots. They took turns watering them with the little green watering can. The soil smelled rich and earthy, and the water made soft pattering sounds.

"Thank you, Shelly," said Benny, giving her a gentle hug. "You were very brave to speak up for what's right."

Shelly's cheeks turned pink under her shell. "Sometimes being fair is more important than being shy," she said proudly.

As the weeks passed, beautiful green sprouts grew in each pot. The friends shared their vegetables when they were ready - crunchy carrots, crisp lettuce, and sweet peas. Everything tasted extra delicious because it was grown with fairness and friendship.

From that day on, Shelly wasn't quite so shy. She learned that speaking up for what's right - even when it feels scary - helps everyone feel happy and included.

And whenever someone in their classroom wasn't being fair, Shelly would remember her brave moment and gently remind them: "When we share fairly, everyone gets what they need."

The End.
