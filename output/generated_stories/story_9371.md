# Kiki the Koala’s Porch Pause

Kiki the Koala liked quiet mornings on the peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like sweet eucalyptus tea, and a soft breeze tickled Kiki’s fuzzy ears. Birds chirped gently from the gum tree. The wooden steps felt warm under Kiki’s paws.

“Good morning, porch,” Kiki said in a calm, cozy voice. She rocked in the small chair and took a slow breath in, then let it out. “Ahh. This feels nice.”

Kiki had a little plan for the day. “Today,” she said, tapping her tummy, “I will care for me. I will rest, sip tea, and stretch.” She smiled and began a gentle stretch. “Reach up, reach down,” she hummed.

Just then, footsteps pattered on the path. Benny the Busy Bandicoot zoomed up, carrying a bright red ball. “Kiki! Let’s play bounce-bounce-roll!” he shouted, the ball thumping on the porch floor.

Kiki’s ears twitched at the loud thump. She placed a paw over her heart and took a breath. “Hi, Benny. I am happy to see you,” she said softly. “Today I need a calm morning. My body is saying, ‘Slow, please.’”

Benny bounced on his toes. “But I want to play now!” he said. The ball bumped a flower pot. The pot wobbled, and a little dirt sprinkled out. Kiki blinked and set the pot gently back in place.

“Hmm,” Kiki said, thinking. “Let’s make a plan together. That is called negotiation. We can find a way that works for both of us.”

Benny tilted his head. “A plan?”

“Yes,” said Kiki. “How about this: I take ten calm breaths and finish my warm tea. Then we play a quiet porch game.”

Benny hugged the ball. “But I like big bounces,” he said, a little frown on his face.

Kiki patted the chair beside her. “Sit and breathe with me,” she said. “Then we will choose a gentle game. We can roll the ball slowly. Or we can play ‘tap-tap-toe’ with leaves.”

Benny climbed onto the porch step. “Okay,” he said. Kiki led him.

“In and out,” Kiki whispered. “Like waves.”

They breathed together: in through the nose—sniff, sniff—out through the mouth—whoo. The birds sang. The breeze brushed their fur. The tea smelled sweet and warm. Benny’s shoulders softened.

“That was nice,” Benny said. “I feel less bouncy and more steady.”

Kiki smiled. “That is self-care,” she said. “We listen to our bodies. We rest when we need to. Then we can play better.”

“Can we roll now?” Benny asked, tapping the ball lightly.

“Yes,” said Kiki. “Let’s roll softly so the porch stays peaceful.”

They rolled the red ball back and forth—slow, smooth, quiet. Thump… shh. Thump… shh. Kiki’s paws felt the cool rubber. Benny giggled when the ball tickled his toes.

Soon, another friend arrived. Mia the Little Magpie flew down and landed on the railing with a flap-flap. “Hello! Can I join?” she chirped. Her shiny eyes sparkled.

“Welcome, Mia,” Kiki said. “We are playing a gentle game.”

Mia bobbed. “Gentle is good,” she said. She pecked the ball softly, and it rolled to Benny. They played in a calm rhythm.

Then Mia saw something bright. A silver button rested near the flower pot. “Ooh! Shiny!” Mia picked it up quickly with her beak. “Mine!” she chirped, hopping away along the railing.

Benny’s eyes grew wide. “Hey! That is from my backpack,” he cried. “Please give it back!”

Mia hugged the button to her chest. “But it is so pretty,” she said. Her wings trembled. “I wanted it.”

Kiki placed a paw on her chest again, taking a slow breath. “Let’s talk,” she said kindly. “We can use our words. We can share feelings.”

Benny nodded. “I feel sad,” he said. “The button is special to me. My Nana gave it to me.”

Mia looked down. “I feel excited by shiny things,” she whispered. “I did not ask. I am sorry.”

Kiki nodded. “Thank you for your honest words,” she said. “Mia, can you return the button now?”

Mia placed the button in Benny’s paw. “I forgive you,” Benny said softly, after a pause. “Thank you for giving it back.”

Mia’s feathers smoothed. “Thank you for forgiving me,” she chirped. “Can we make a plan for shiny things next time?”

Kiki smiled. “Yes,” she said. “Here is a good plan. Benny keeps his special button safe in his backpack. Mia, if you see something shiny, you can ask, ‘May I look?’ We will say yes to looking, no to taking.”

“That is a fair plan,” Benny said.

“I agree,” Mia chirped.

They played again, rolling the ball and tapping toes. Kiki felt calm and warm. She sipped the last of her tea—sweet, soft, and cozy—and stretched her arms.

When the sun rose higher, Kiki stood. “I cared for me first,” she said. “I rested and breathed. Then I played and shared. That helped me be kind and strong.”

Benny smiled. “I learned to wait and to ask.”

Mia nodded. “I learned to say sorry and to forgive.”

Kiki hugged her friends. The breeze whispered through the leaves. The porch felt peaceful and happy.

“We did it together,” Kiki said.

The End.
