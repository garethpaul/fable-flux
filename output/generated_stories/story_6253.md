# Leo’s Warm Kitchen Art Day

The sun peeked in through the window of the warm kitchen. The room smelled like cinnamon toast and sweet berries. Pots and pans shined. A little clock tick-tick-ticked. On the big wooden table sat paints, paper, brushes, and a cup of water that went swish-swish when it moved.

A brave little lion cub named Leo stood on a stool by the table. His fur was soft and golden, and his whiskers wiggled when he smiled. “Today is Art Day!” Leo roared, but only a tiny roar, because he was inside. “I will make a sunny picture.”

Mama Lion set down a plate of toast. “Art needs a full tummy,” she said with a smile.

Leo took a crunchy bite. “Crunch! Mmm.” He felt brave and excited. He dipped his brush into bright yellow paint. Plop! The paint felt cool and slippery on the paper. “I’m making the sun,” he said.

Just then, Leo’s cousin, Lila the lamb, trotted in. Her hooves went clip-clop on the floor. She wore a little blue scarf. “Hi, Leo,” Lila said. “May I paint too? I brought glitter.”

Leo looked at the paints. He saw three brushes, two big sheets of paper, and one little cup of water. He wanted to use the glitter first. He wanted to be the first artist. His tail twitched.

Mama Lion said, “There is room for two artists in a warm kitchen.”

Leo took a deep breath. “Okay,” he said. “We can share.”

They both climbed onto stools. Lila opened the glitter. It sparkled like tiny stars. “Ooo,” Leo whispered. “Shiny!”

Lila dipped a brush into blue paint and made big swirls. Swoosh-swoosh. “I’m painting the ocean,” she said.

Leo made yellow circles. Swirl, swirl. “I’m painting the sun.” He smiled. “Our pictures can be friends.”

Then—oops! Leo’s elbow bumped the cup. The water went tip-tip-tip… and then SPLASH! The cup fell. Water spilled on Lila’s paper. The blue swirls turned into a big blur. The glitter floated and stuck to her scarf.

Lila’s eyes got big and wet. “Oh no,” she said. “My ocean is gone.”

Leo’s heart felt heavy. His ears went flat. “I’m sorry,” he said. “I did not look. I bumped the cup.”

Lila sniffed. “I worked hard.”

Mama Lion handed them soft towels. “Let’s take calm breaths,” she said. “In… and out.” They breathed together. In… and out. The kitchen was warm and quiet again. The clock ticked. The toast smelled sweet.

Leo thought with his brave brain. He looked at the mess. He looked at the supplies. He asked, “What is the problem?” He said it out loud so he could hear his plan. “The problem is the water spilled. Lila’s picture is blurred.”

Lila nodded. “Yes.”

Leo said, “What can we do?” He tapped his chin. Tap, tap. “We can make the blur into waves. We can use glitter for foam. We can share a towel and pat it dry. We can use the other paper and start again.” He took another breath. “We can fix it together.”

Lila looked at the paper. The blur looked like soft waves. “Maybe my ocean is not gone,” she said. “Maybe it is a stormy sea.”

Mama Lion smiled. “That sounds like logical thinking.”

Leo picked up the towel. “May I help you, Lila?” he asked.

“Yes,” Lila said. “That is fair. We will both help.”

They patted the paper, pat-pat-pat. The water soaked into the towel. The picture stayed wavy, like a windy day. Leo sprinkled just a little glitter along the top. It sparkled like foam. Lila added white paint, dab-dab-dab. “Look,” she said. “Splash!”

Leo nodded. “Splash!” He giggled. Then he looked at the cup. “We need a safe place for the water.”

Mama Lion pointed to the corner of the table. “Over here is safe, far from elbows.”

Leo moved the cup. “I will be careful. I will look with my eyes and think with my brain.”

They painted side by side. Lila’s stormy sea rolled and shone. Leo’s sun glowed bright and round. He added orange rays—zig-zag, zig-zag. The warm kitchen hummed with happy sounds: brush swishes, little laughs, and the soft tick of the clock.

When they finished, they held up their pictures. “Ta-da!” Leo said.

“Ta-da!” Lila said.

Mama Lion clapped. “Two wonderful artists,” she said. “And two good problem solvers.”

Leo turned to Lila. “I am sorry I spilled the water,” he said again. “It was not fair that your work got ruined. I wanted to make it right.”

Lila smiled. “You did make it right. You helped me fix it. That is making amends.”

Leo’s whiskers lifted. “We can hang our art together.”

They taped the pictures on the fridge. The sun and the sea looked like friends, side by side. The kitchen felt even warmer, like a hug.

Leo poured a fresh cup of water and set it far, far back. He counted the brushes. “One, two, three.” He pushed one toward Lila. “We share.”

Lila giggled. “Fair and square.”

Leo took a small bow. “Artists think, share, and care,” he said.

Mama Lion gave them each a cozy hug. “Today you learned about art and also about being kind,” she said. “Art can change a mistake into something new. Making amends feels good. Fairness means everyone gets a turn and a chance.”

Leo looked at the sparkles on his paw. “Art is shiny,” he said.

Lila nodded. “Art is brave.”

Leo smiled. “And we are, too.”

The End.
