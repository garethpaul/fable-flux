# Pip's Rooftop Adventure

High up on a snowy rooftop, a cheerful penguin named Pip waddled back and forth. The cold wind ruffled his black and white feathers. Below him, the city sparkled like tiny lights.

"What a perfect day for sliding!" Pip said happily. He had discovered the most wonderful thing - the rooftop had a long, slippery slope perfect for penguin sliding.

Pip took a running start. "Wheee!" He slid down the icy rooftop on his round belly. The wind tickled his beak as he zoomed along. At the bottom, he flapped his flippers with joy.

Just then, Pip heard a tiny voice. "That looks so fun," someone whispered sadly.

Pip looked around. Behind a chimney, he spotted a small sparrow shivering in the cold. Her brown feathers looked ruffled and wet.

"Hello there!" Pip called out cheerfully. "I'm Pip! What's your name?"

"I'm Stella," the sparrow said quietly. "I got caught in the rain and my wings are too wet to fly home."

Pip's heart felt warm with kindness. "Oh no! You must be so cold and scared."

Stella nodded. "I saw you sliding and it looked so happy. I wish I could have fun like that."

Pip thought for a moment. The rooftop was his special sliding place, but Stella needed help more than he needed to play alone.

"Would you like to slide with me?" Pip asked with a big smile. "We could take turns, and sliding might help warm you up!"

Stella's eyes grew bright. "Really? But I don't know how to slide like a penguin."

"That's okay!" Pip said confidently. "I can teach you. We'll figure it out together."

Pip waddled up the slope with Stella hopping beside him. "First, you sit down like this," he showed her, settling on his belly. "Then you push off with your feet!"

Stella tried sitting on her tiny bottom. She looked nervous.

"You're doing great!" Pip encouraged. "I'll go first to show you, then it's your turn."

Pip slid down slowly this time, calling back, "See? Just like that! Now you try!"

Stella took a deep breath. She pushed off gently and slid down the slope. "Wheee! This is amazing!" she chirped with delight.

"Your turn again!" Stella said when she reached the bottom.

Pip and Stella took turns sliding down the rooftop. Sometimes they slid together, Stella riding on Pip's back. The little sparrow's feathers began to dry and fluff up from all the movement and laughter.

"I feel so much warmer now," Stella said gratefully. "And my wings are almost dry!"

"That's wonderful!" Pip clapped his flippers. "You're such a good slider now!"

As the sun began to set, Stella flapped her wings. They worked perfectly! "I can fly home now, thanks to you, Pip. You shared your special place and helped me feel better."

"That's what friends do," Pip said proudly. "Being kind makes me feel happy inside."

Stella gave Pip a gentle hug with her wing. "You're the kindest penguin ever. Can I come back and slide with you again?"

"Of course!" Pip said. "This rooftop is even more fun when we share it!"

Stella flew up in a happy circle. "Thank you, Pip! See you tomorrow!"

As Pip watched his new friend fly safely home, his heart felt full of joy. Sharing his favorite sliding spot had made everything twice as fun. He had helped someone in need and made a wonderful friend.

Pip slid down the rooftop one more time, feeling proud and happy. Tomorrow would bring another day of sliding and friendship on his special rooftop.

The End.
