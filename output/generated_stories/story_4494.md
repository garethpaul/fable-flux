# Rainbow Songs in the Nursery

Rusty the raccoon loved music. He wore a tiny blue cap and a gentle smile. His little paws could tap, clap, and strum. Today, Rusty visited a comfortable nursery full of soft rugs, squishy pillows, and big windows that let in warm, golden light. The room smelled like crayons and apple slices. Paintings made by children lined the walls, full of swirls, dots, and bright rainbows.

“Good morning, friends!” Rusty sang as he plucked his small ukulele. Ting-ting-ting! “Let’s make art and music together!”

The children clapped. “Yay, Rusty!”

Miss Lina, the teacher, rolled out big sheets of paper. She placed paints, crayons, and brushes in little cups. The brushes felt soft, and the paints looked shiny and wet. Rusty’s whiskers twitched with excitement.

“I will paint a rainbow song,” Rusty said. He dipped a brush into red paint. Swish! He hummed a red note. “Laaa!” He moved to orange. “La-laaa!” His tail swayed to the beat.

Milo, a boy with curly hair, watched. “I want to make a rainbow song too!”

Jia, a girl with bright braids, nodded. “Me too!”

Rusty smiled. “Art is for everyone. Let’s each make a rainbow song. Your song can look different from mine.”

They each took a paper. Rusty painted neat arcs. Milo splashed big dots. Jia made gentle zigzags. The room filled with happy sounds: swish, dab, tap, ting! The sunshine warmed their backs. The paints smelled a little like sweet berries and a tiny bit like soap.

After a while, Milo frowned. “Rusty, your rainbow song looks perfect. Mine is messy.”

Rusty sat beside Milo and listened. “What does your heart want to paint?”

Milo looked at his dots. “My heart hears drum sounds. Boom. Boom-boom!”

Rusty tapped his ukulele body like a drum. “Boom-boom!” He nodded. “That is your art. It is honest. That’s called integrity—being true to you.”

Milo’s frown turned into a giggle. “Boom-boom!”

Jia looked worried too. “I want to use all the purple. It makes my zigzags sing. But Milo wants purple dots.”

Milo held the purple cup tight. Jia reached for it. The brush wobbled. A drip fell. Plop.

Rusty raised a gentle paw. “Let’s pause.”

Miss Lina came closer. “We can share. We can listen.”

Rusty took a deep breath. “We want everyone’s art to sing. That takes kindness and compromise. Compromise means we find a way that feels okay to both.”

Jia squeezed the brush handle softly. “Maybe I can use purple for three zigzags. Then Milo can use it for ten dots.”

Milo counted on his fingers. “Three zigzags, ten dots. That feels okay.”

Rusty grinned. “You found a fair plan together.”

They tried it. Jia made three singing zigzags. Swoosh, swoosh, swoosh. She smiled. “Purple sings!”

Milo dotted—one, two, three… ten! Tap, tap, tap! He bobbed his head. “Boom-boom dots!”

Rusty strummed a happy chord. Ting-ting-ting! The nursery seemed to hum with color and sound.

After more painting, Rusty noticed a tiny smear of purple at the corner of Jia’s paper. It was on the edge of Milo’s spot. Milo looked down. “Oh! I dripped on your art, Jia. I didn’t mean to.”

Jia blinked. She touched the drip with her brush and made it into a little purple star. “It’s okay. Thank you for telling the truth.”

Rusty nodded. “That is integrity too—being honest when you make a mistake.”

Milo smiled with relief. “I feel better.”

Rusty looked up at the wall. “I have an idea. Let’s learn something new. Does anyone know the colors of the rainbow?”

Jia raised her hand. “Red, orange, yellow, green…”

Milo added, “Blue and purple!”

Rusty clapped softly. “And indigo, a deep blue. Now let’s sing them.”

They sang together:
“Red and orange, yellow, green,
Blue and indigo, purple sheen!”

Their voices were soft and sweet. The ukulele sparkled: ting-ting-ting! The children swayed. The paintbrushes danced.

When the paintings dried, Rusty said, “Let’s make a gallery.” They taped the art on the wall, side by side: arcs, dots, and zigzags. The nursery looked like a sunrise.

Miss Lina whispered, “What do we see?”

Jia pointed. “We see different art.”

Milo said, “We see honest hearts.”

Rusty added, “We see kindness and compromise.”

They all looked proud. Rusty felt a warm glow in his chest. He had shared music, and the children had shared art and care.

Jia held up her brush. “Rusty, will you help us make one big painting together?”

Rusty nodded. “Yes! We will listen, share, and be true.”

They rolled out one giant paper. The children chose colors in turns. They used gentle voices. “Can I have blue, please?” “Yes, after I finish this line.” They mixed a little red with blue to make purple. “Look! New color!” They learned that mixing colors is like making new songs—soft, bright, surprise notes.

At the end, they all stepped back. The big painting sang with arcs, dots, and zigzags, and one purple star. It looked and felt happy.

Rusty strummed the final chord. “Today we learned:
- Art is for everyone.
- Be honest and be you.
- Share and compromise.
- Learn new things together.”

The children clapped and hugged. The nursery felt warm and safe. Rusty’s tail swished. “Your art has a kind heart,” he said.

“Thank you, Rusty!” the children cheered.

Rusty tipped his blue cap. “Keep your art singing.”

The End.
