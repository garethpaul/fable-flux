# Bunny Beats in the Pantry

Tap, tap, tap.

A little rabbit named Rumi stood on soft paws in the cool, quiet pantry. The pantry smelled like oats and apples. It was dim and cozy, with jars that glowed like tiny moons when the light peeked in. Rumi’s whiskers twitched. His long ears stood tall and determined.

“I can do it,” Rumi whispered. “I can make a calm song.”

Rumi wanted to make a pantry song to help calm his heart and help his friends feel brave. He felt a flutter in his tummy. It felt like butterflies dancing. He took a slow bunny breath in and out.

“Hello, jars,” Rumi said. “Hello, oats. Hello, apples. Will you be in my song?”

The metal lids went ting, ting when his paw touched them. The flour bag went puff! A wooden spoon leaned by a bowl, and it looked like a drumstick. The shelf hummed softly, like it was waiting.

Rumi smiled. “I will try. I will keep trying,” he said, standing tall.

First, he tapped a jar with one paw. Ting. Then he tapped a tin with the wooden spoon. Tonk. He shook the rice bag gently. Shhh, shhh, shhh. He liked the sounds.

“Listen,” Rumi said to himself. “Ting, tonk, shhh. Ting, tonk, shhh.”

He tried to sing along, but his voice came out tiny. “La… la?” He cleared his throat and took another slow bunny breath. “I can do it,” he said again.

From the doorway, a mouse named Pip peeked in. “Hi, Rumi,” Pip squeaked. “What are you doing?”

“I’m making a calm pantry song,” Rumi said. “But I feel a little wobbly.”

Pip nodded. “I feel wobbly sometimes too. Can I listen?”

“Yes,” Rumi said. He felt warm inside. A friend was here.

Rumi tried again. He tapped a jar. Ting. He tapped the tin. Tonk. He swished the rice. Shhh. He hummed, very softly. “Mmmm… mmmm…”

Pip closed his eyes. “That sounds like rain,” Pip said.

Rumi’s ears perked up. “Rain is calm,” he said. “Let’s make rain.”

He wiggled his toes and did a tiny hop. Hop, hop. His feet made the softest thump on the floor. He smiled bigger. He was having fun now.

But then the wooden spoon slipped. Clatter! It fell to the floor. Rumi’s heart jumped. His whiskers drooped. “Oh no,” he said.

Pip picked up the spoon and handed it back. “It’s okay,” Pip whispered. “Try again.”

Rumi hugged the spoon to his chest and counted. “One… two… three.” He took a slow breath. In. Out. Calm. “I can do it.”

He tapped the tin. Tonk. He tapped the jar. Ting. He swished the rice. Shhh. He hummed. “Mmmm… la-la… mmmm.”

A sweet smell drifted from a bag of apples. Rumi sniffed. “Apples smell happy,” he said. He rubbed the apple gently. It made a tiny squeak. Squeak!

Pip giggled. “Put the squeak in the song!”

Rumi tried. Ting, tonk, shhh, squeak. “La, la, la,” he sang, a little louder this time. His voice felt steady. His tummy felt warm and brave.

A turtle named Tulla slid in slowly. “I heard music,” Tulla said in a calm, low voice. “May I join?”

“Yes,” said Rumi. “We are making a calm pantry song.”

Tulla tapped a wooden bowl with her shell. Thum. It sounded like a heartbeat. Thum… thum…

Rumi listened. He let the sounds wrap around him like a soft blanket. Ting, tonk, shhh, squeak, thum. He smiled and nodded to the beat. His ears swayed like soft flags.

“I feel calm,” Pip said, breathing slow.

“I feel steady,” Tulla said.

Rumi stood taller. “I feel strong,” he said. “I can do it.”

He tried a tiny solo. “La-la-la, I can try. La-la-la, I can be calm. La-la-la, I can be me.” His voice rang clear in the pantry, like a little bell. He looked at his friends. They were smiling.

The spoon didn’t slip this time. The jars didn’t wobble. Rumi moved slowly, with care. He tapped and hummed and listened. He felt proud.

Suddenly, the light grew brighter as the door opened. Mama Bunny peeked in. “What lovely music,” she said warmly. “It sounds like raindrops and hearts.”

Rumi’s ears blushed pink. “We made a calm pantry song,” he said. “I kept trying. I believed I could do it.”

Mama nodded. “You were determined,” she said. “You took deep breaths. You listened. You were kind to yourself. That is good for your heart and your body.”

Rumi’s chest felt big with happy. “I am brave,” he said softly.

Pip squeaked, “You are brave!”

Tulla added, “You are calm and strong.”

Rumi tapped a final ting, tonk, shhh. Then he bowed. “Thank you,” he said. “Thank you, pantry.”

They all took one more slow breath together. In. Out. The pantry was peaceful. The apples smelled sweet. The jars shone. The shelves hummed.

Rumi whispered, “I can do hard things when I keep trying. I can be calm. I can be me.”

And the friends hummed the pantry song as they left, feeling safe, steady, and proud.

The End.
