# Milo's Colorful Choice

The bright morning sun streamed through the windows of the creative art room. Milo the monkey swung from the colorful paper chains hanging from the ceiling. His fluffy brown tail curled with excitement as he looked around at all the art supplies.

"Good morning, Milo!" called Mrs. Chen, the art teacher. Her paint-splattered apron was covered in rainbow colors. "Today we're making special pictures for the children at the hospital. Would you like to help?"

Milo scratched his head thoughtfully. He had planned to paint a big picture of bananas - his favorite food! His tummy rumbled just thinking about those yellow, yummy treats.

"But what about my banana painting?" Milo asked, pointing to the blank canvas he had saved for himself.

Mrs. Chen smiled warmly. "You can choose, little friend. You could paint your banana picture, or you could help make art for children who are feeling sick. They would love to see cheerful pictures on their walls."

Milo's nose twitched as he smelled the fresh paint. Red, blue, yellow, and green - all the colors looked so inviting! He watched other children dipping their brushes and creating beautiful flowers, sunshine, and happy faces.

"Tell me about the children at the hospital," Milo said softly.

"Well," Mrs. Chen explained, "some children have to stay in bed for many days. The walls in their rooms are very plain and white. A colorful picture would make them smile and feel less lonely."

Milo's heart felt warm and fuzzy, like when he got a big hug. He thought about feeling sad and lonely. Then he imagined how happy he would feel if someone made something special just for him.

"I want to help!" Milo announced, jumping up and down. His decision felt right in his chest, like a happy bubble.

Mrs. Chen clapped her hands together. "Wonderful! What would you like to paint for the children?"

Milo picked up a soft, fluffy brush. He dipped it in bright yellow paint. "I'll paint a big, smiling sun! And maybe some happy monkeys swinging on vines!"

As Milo painted, the colors felt smooth and cool under his brush. Yellow for sunshine, green for jungle vines, and brown for playful monkeys. Each stroke made his picture more cheerful and bright.

"This is even more fun than painting bananas!" Milo giggled as he added tiny flowers around his sun.

Other children gathered around to watch. "That's beautiful, Milo!" said Sarah, a little girl with pigtails. "The sick children will love it!"

When Milo finished his painting, it was full of joy and color. The sun had the biggest smile, and the monkeys looked like they were having the best day ever.

"You made a very kind choice today, Milo," Mrs. Chen said, helping him clean his brushes. "When we help others, we feel good inside too."

Milo's chest puffed up with pride. He felt proud and happy, like he had done something really important. "Can we make more pictures for the children?" he asked eagerly.

"Of course!" Mrs. Chen laughed. "There are many children who would love your cheerful art."

As Milo started his second painting - this time of dancing bananas to make the children laugh - he realized something wonderful. Helping others felt even better than keeping all the fun for himself.

That night, Milo dreamed of children smiling at his colorful pictures, and his heart felt as bright as the sunshine he had painted.

The End.
