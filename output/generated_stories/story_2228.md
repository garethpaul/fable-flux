# Benny Bear's Big Game Plan

Benny Bear was a fluffy brown cub who loved to play in his cozy living room. The soft carpet felt nice under his paws, and colorful toys were scattered everywhere like a rainbow on the floor.

"Today I want to do EVERYTHING!" Benny announced, bouncing on his toes. He wanted to play catch with his red ball, do jumping jacks like the frogs in his picture book, practice his soccer kicks, and build the tallest block tower ever.

Benny grabbed his bouncy red ball first. "Catch time!" he said, throwing it high in the air. BONK! It hit the ceiling light and rolled under the couch.

"Okay, jumping jacks then!" Benny started jumping and waving his arms. One, two, three... CRASH! He knocked over his juice cup on the coffee table.

"Maybe soccer?" Benny kicked his soft soccer ball across the room. THUMP! It knocked down the block tower he had started yesterday.

Benny sat down on the fluffy rug with a big sigh. His living room looked messy, and he felt tired already. "This isn't working," he said sadly. "I can't do everything at once!"

Just then, Mama Bear walked in. She saw Benny's confused face and the scattered toys. "What's wrong, little one?" she asked gently, sitting beside him.

"I want to play all my games, but everything keeps going wrong!" Benny explained, his brown eyes looking worried.

Mama Bear smiled warmly. "Sounds like you need a game plan, Benny. Do you know what that means?"

Benny shook his fuzzy head.

"A game plan is like a map for your day," Mama Bear explained. "First, you pick one activity. Then you make space for it. Then you play! After that, you clean up and pick the next activity."

"Oh!" Benny's eyes lit up like stars. "So I do one thing at a time?"

"Exactly! Let's try it together," Mama Bear said.

Benny thought carefully. "I want to start with jumping jacks!"

First, they moved the coffee table to make a safe space. The carpet felt soft and bouncy under Benny's feet. "One, two, three, four!" Benny counted as he jumped. His arms felt strong, and his heart beat fast like a happy drum.

"Great job!" Mama Bear clapped. "Now what's next in your game plan?"

"Soccer practice!" Benny said excitedly.

They moved to the other side of the room and rolled up the small rug. Benny practiced gentle kicks against the soft couch cushions. "Goal!" he cheered as the ball bounced back to him.

After soccer, Benny chose ball catch. They cleared space near the tall bookshelf. Benny threw the red ball gently and caught it with both paws. "I'm getting better!" he giggled.

Finally, Benny built his block tower in the corner where it was safe. Red blocks, blue blocks, yellow blocks - up, up, up! "Look how tall it is!" he said proudly.

As the sun painted golden stripes through the window, Benny looked around his living room. Everything was organized, and he had played all his favorite games!

"My game plan worked!" Benny said, giving Mama Bear a big, warm hug. "I did everything I wanted, and it was so much fun!"

"That's the magic of having a strategy," Mama Bear said. "When you plan your activities and do them one at a time, you can enjoy each one fully."

From that day on, Benny always made a game plan before playing. He learned that being organized made everything more fun!

The End.
