# Benny Beaver’s Brave Dinner Plan

Benny was a busy beaver with a bright, shiny smile and big, careful paws. He loved to build, stack, and stir. Tonight, Benny hurried into the dining room. The table was smooth and cool. The wooden chairs smelled like fresh trees. The room was warm, and a soft lamp made a golden glow.

“Dinner time!” called Mama Beaver, setting bowls on the table. “We have crunchy carrots, green peas, warm brown rice, and a tiny treat.”

Benny’s nose twitched. He smelled sweet chocolate from the tiny treat. He saw peas pop in the bowl like little green buttons. He heard the rice go swish as Mama scooped it.

Benny felt his tummy rumble. “I want the treat first!” he said.

Mama smiled. “Let’s make a smart dinner plan,” she said. “Healthy bites help our bodies grow strong. We can decide together.”

Benny blinked. He liked deciding. He liked planning too. But he also liked chocolate. He tapped his tail on the chair. Tap, tap, tap.

Papa Beaver sat down and took a deep breath. “I like to start with a brave bite,” Papa said. “A brave bite is a bite I’m not sure about yet. I taste, I chew, I try.”

Benny looked at the bowl of peas. He felt a little worried. The peas were round and shiny. What if they rolled off his spoon? What if he didn’t like them?

Benny took a slow breath in. In… and out. “I can be brave,” he whispered.

“Let’s make a plan,” Mama said. She held up her paw like she was counting stars. “First: water sip. Second: brave bite. Third: crunchy chew. Then: rice, rest, and treat.”

Benny giggled. “That sounds fun! Water sip.” He lifted his cup. The water was cool and tickled his tongue. Sip!

“Brave bite,” he said. He scooped one pea. Plink! It sat on his spoon. He opened wide. Chomp. The pea was soft and sweet, like a tiny hug. “Hey,” Benny said, his eyes wide. “That pea is friendly!”

Mama clapped softly. “You did it.”

“Crunchy chew,” Benny said. He picked up a carrot stick. Crunch! Crunch! It snapped like a little stick in the forest. It tasted fresh. It tasted sunny. Benny felt proud.

“Now rice,” Papa said, smiling. “Swish, scoop, chew.”

Benny scooped warm rice. It felt cozy in his mouth. He wiggled in his chair. “My tummy feels happy,” he said. “My body is getting strong.”

After a few bites, Benny saw the tiny treat again. It winked at him. He wanted it. His tail thumped faster. Tap-tap-tap-tap.

He paused. He remembered the plan. He remembered being brave. He put his paw on his chest. Thump. Thump. “I can wait,” he told himself. “I can choose.”

Mama leaned close. “What does your body say?”

Benny closed his eyes. He listened. His tummy said, “More peas, please!”

Benny grinned. “My tummy wants peas.” He took two more peas. Plink, plink. Chomp, chomp. “Yum!”

“Good listening,” Papa said. “That is healthy decision-making.”

Benny looked at his plate. There were three carrot sticks left. The carrots were orange like sunset. He felt brave again. “I will try a carrot with rice,” he said. He mixed a tiny bit. The rice was soft, the carrot was crisp. His mouth felt happy-silly. “It’s a crunchy cloud!”

“Smart chef,” Mama said. “You made a new taste.”

Benny ate until his tummy felt just-right. Not too full. Not too empty. He put his paw on his belly. “Comfy,” he said.

Mama smiled. “You followed your plan. You were brave. You made good choices.”

Papa nodded. “Now, tiny treat?”

Benny looked at the treat. He looked at his water. He thought. He decided. “Yes, tiny treat now.” He took one small bite. Sweet and soft. He smiled. “Mmm.” He took another sip of water. “I will save the last bite for later,” he said. “I feel done.”

“That is a healthy habit,” Mama said. “Stopping when your tummy says ‘all set’.”

Benny helped clean the table. He wiped a little spill. He stacked the cups. The cloth felt damp and cool. He liked helping. It made him feel strong inside.

Before bed, Benny brushed his teeth. Scritch-scritch, scrub-scrub. He watched the bubbles in the mirror. His smile shone bright.

In his cozy bed, Benny thought about dinner. He remembered the brave bite, the crunchy chew, and the happy, just-right feeling.

Benny whispered, “I can make a plan. I can be brave. I can choose healthy.”

The lamp clicked off. The room was soft and quiet. Benny’s tail gave one sleepy thump.

“Good night, smart chef,” Mama whispered.

“Good night,” Benny said, smiling. “Tomorrow, more peas, please.”

The End.
