# Ollie's Big Room Adventure

Ollie the otter loved his cozy bedroom with its soft blue walls and fluffy white carpet. The morning sun streamed through his window, making everything sparkle like water droplets. But today, something was different. His mama had exciting news!

"Ollie, sweetie," Mama called from the doorway. "Your cousin Benny is coming to stay with us for a whole week!"

Ollie's whiskers twitched with excitement. He loved visitors! He bounced on his bed, making the springs squeak happily. "Yay! When does he come?"

"Right now!" Mama smiled as a small badger peeked around the corner.

Benny was very different from Ollie. Where Ollie was sleek and brown, Benny was fuzzy and black-and-white striped. Benny walked slowly and spoke quietly, while Ollie loved to splash and chatter.

"Hi," Benny whispered, clutching a worn teddy bear.

"Hi! Want to play water games?" Ollie asked loudly, diving under his covers like he was swimming.

Benny shook his head. "Too noisy. I like quiet games."

Ollie's tail drooped. This wasn't what he expected. He tried again. "Let's build a fort!"

"I don't know how," Benny said softly.

Ollie felt frustrated. His paws clenched into little fists. Why couldn't Benny just play his way? It would be so much easier!

"But... but I always build forts!" Ollie huffed, his voice getting louder.

Benny's eyes filled with tears. He hugged his teddy bear tighter and sat in the corner.

Ollie's mama gently touched his shoulder. "Ollie, remember what makes our river special?"

Ollie thought about their home by the water. "All the different fish?"

"That's right. The fast fish, the slow fish, the colorful ones, and the plain ones. They all make the river beautiful together."

Ollie looked at Benny sitting quietly in the corner. Slowly, he walked over and sat down beside his cousin.

"I'm sorry I was loud," Ollie said gently. "What games do you like?"

Benny peeked up. "I like puzzles. And reading. And... and drawing."

Ollie's eyes lit up. "I have crayons! And my mama reads to me every night!"

Together, they spread out on the soft carpet. Benny showed Ollie how to draw careful, detailed pictures. Ollie helped Benny put together a big puzzle of ocean animals. When Benny got frustrated with a tricky piece, Ollie encouraged him.

"You can do it! Try turning it this way," Ollie said patiently.

When Ollie got excited and started chattering too fast, Benny gently reminded him, "Slower, please. I want to understand."

By bedtime, they had created beautiful pictures together and finished two whole puzzles. As Mama read them a story, both friends snuggled under Ollie's cozy blankets.

"Benny," Ollie whispered, "I'm glad you're different from me."

"Why?" Benny asked.

"Because you taught me new things! And we make a good team."

Benny smiled. "You taught me things too. Like how to be brave and try new games."

Mama tucked them in and kissed their foreheads. "Sweet dreams, my different and wonderful boys."

As the room grew quiet and peaceful, Ollie realized something important. Being different wasn't bad – it was special. Just like their river needed all kinds of fish, their friendship was better because they each brought something unique.

"Tomorrow, want to draw AND build a fort?" Ollie asked sleepily.

"Yes," Benny yawned. "That sounds perfect."

The End.
