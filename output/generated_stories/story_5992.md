# Polly's Dancing Dream

Polly the parrot lived in the coziest bedroom you could imagine. Soft pink curtains hung by the window, letting in warm golden sunlight. A fluffy purple rug covered the wooden floor, and colorful toys were scattered everywhere.

Polly had bright green feathers that sparkled in the morning light. She loved to create new things - drawing pictures with her beak, building towers with blocks, and most of all, dancing!

"Today I'm going to learn a special new dance!" Polly chirped excitedly, flapping her wings. She had seen a beautiful butterfly dance outside her window yesterday, spinning and twirling gracefully through the air.

Polly stood on her favorite perch and tried to copy the butterfly's moves. She stretched one wing high up to the ceiling. "One, two, three... spin!" she said.

But when Polly tried to spin, she tumbled right off her perch! THUMP! She landed on the soft rug with her feathers all ruffled.

"Squawk! That didn't work!" Polly felt her chest getting tight and her face getting warm. She was frustrated. The dance looked so easy when the butterfly did it!

Polly tried again. This time she flapped both wings and jumped. But instead of a graceful twirl, she crashed into a pile of stuffed animals. "Why can't I do this?" she squawked, feeling even more frustrated.

Her friend Teddy Bear noticed Polly's upset face. "What's wrong, Polly?" Teddy asked kindly.

"I want to dance like the butterfly, but I keep messing up!" Polly's voice was shaky. "It's too hard!"

Teddy smiled warmly. "When I feel frustrated, I take three deep breaths. Want to try with me?"

Polly nodded. Together they breathed in slowly through their noses - "Ahhhh" - and out through their mouths - "Whooooo." They did this three times.

"Feel better?" asked Teddy.

"A little," Polly admitted. Her chest didn't feel as tight anymore.

"Maybe we can break the dance into tiny pieces," suggested Teddy. "What if you just practice moving one wing first?"

Polly liked this idea. She stood on the rug and slowly lifted her right wing. "I did it!" she cheered.

"Great! Now try the left wing," encouraged Teddy.

Polly lifted her left wing. It felt good to succeed at something small.

"Now try a tiny step to the right," said Teddy.

Step by step, Polly practiced each part of the dance. When she felt frustrated again, she remembered to take her deep breaths. "Breathe in... breathe out," she whispered to herself.

After lots of practice, Polly put all the moves together. She lifted her right wing, then her left, took three small steps, and did a gentle turn. She didn't fall down!

"I'm dancing!" Polly sang happily, her green feathers shimmering as she moved around the cozy room. "Look, Teddy! I'm really dancing!"

The warm sunlight made rainbow patterns on the wall as Polly danced. She felt proud, calm, and joyful all at once.

"You did it, Polly!" cheered Teddy. "You didn't give up, even when it was hard."

Polly beamed with pride. "I learned that when I feel frustrated, I can take deep breaths and try smaller steps. Breaking big things into little pieces makes them easier!"

From that day on, whenever Polly faced something challenging, she remembered her dancing lesson. Take deep breaths, break it into small pieces, and keep trying. Soon she was creating the most wonderful dances in her cozy bedroom, filling it with joy and beautiful movement.

The End.
