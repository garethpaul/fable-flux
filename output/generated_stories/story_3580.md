# The Turn-Taking Tea Party

In a sunny little house, there was a cozy guest room with a soft rug, a tall window, and a tiny table set for tea. A kind mouse named Mimi lived behind the baseboard. Mimi had shiny eyes, gentle paws, and a big heart. She loved to help. She loved to learn. She loved to say, “Please,” and “Thank you.”

One morning, warm light fell across the guest room. It smelled like fresh laundry and lemon soap. The window breeze made the curtains whisper, swish-swish. Mimi peeked out and saw a toy tea set on the little table—two cups, one teapot, and one tiny cookie. Her whiskers twitched with curiosity.

“Oh!” said Mimi softly. “A tea party!”

Just then, a friendly ladybug named Dot landed on the windowsill. Dot had shiny red wings with black dots, and a giggly, buzzy voice. “Hello, Mimi!” Dot chirped. “Is that a cookie I see?”

Mimi smiled. “Yes! And tea! Let’s have a tea party in the guest room.”

Dot buzzed closer and sniffed the cookie. “Mmm. Sweet. Crumbly. Yum.”

Mimi climbed up the table leg using a ribbon like a rope. She was clever and very independent. She did not ask for a big ladder. She thought, “I can do this safely.” Paw over paw, she pulled herself up. Dot used her tiny wings to help by steadying the ribbon. “I will hold it,” Dot said.

“Thank you,” Mimi said. “We make a good team.”

They reached the table. Mimi poured pretend tea into one cup. “Sip,” she said, and smiled. Dot buzzed in a circle. “Me next! Me next!” she said, zipping toward the teapot.

Mimi reached for the teapot too. “I want to pour more!”

Both tiny friends touched the teapot at the same time. The cups rattled. The cookie wobbled. The teapot wiggled. Mimi felt her tummy twist. Dot’s wings hummed fast-faster-fast.

“Wait,” Mimi said, taking a slow, deep breath. She remembered mindfulness. She put a paw on her chest and felt it go up and down. “In… and out,” she whispered. The smell of lemon soap helped her feel calm.

Dot watched. She slowed her wings. “In… and out,” she echoed. The room felt still and happy again.

Mimi nodded. “We both want to pour,” she said kindly. “We need to take turns.”

Dot tilted her head. “What are turns?”

“Taking turns means we share time,” Mimi said. She spoke slowly, with gentle words. “First, one friend goes. Then the other friend goes. We both get a try.”

Dot blinked. “But I really, really want to pour now.”

Mimi had an idea. She was resourceful. She looked around and spotted two shiny buttons on the floor by the rug. “Let’s make turn-tokens!” she said. She nudged one button to Dot. “Blue button means it is your turn. Red button means it is my turn. When we finish, we pass the button.”

Dot touched the smooth blue button. “It feels cool,” she said. “Okay. I have blue. I pour first.”

Mimi smiled. “I will wait with red. I can wait. I can be patient.” She folded her paws in her lap and listened to the tiny tick-tick of the hallway clock. Waiting felt hard, like holding a wiggly giggle. But Mimi breathed in and out. In… and out. She watched the sun sparkle on the teapot.

Dot lifted the teapot very carefully. “Tilt… tilt… pour!” A soft trickle sound came, like gentle rain. “I did it!” Dot buzzed with joy and set down the pot. She passed the blue button to Mimi. “Your turn.”

“Thank you,” Mimi said. She held the teapot and poured for Dot’s cup. “Tilt… tilt… pour!” The pretend tea made a tiny glug-glug sound. Mimi felt proud. She passed the red button back to Dot. “Your turn for the cookie?”

Dot looked at the tiny cookie. “How do we share one cookie?” she asked.

Mimi thought again. She used a clean leaf from a plant on the windowsill as a little plate. “We can break it. I will make two pieces,” she said. She pressed gently. Snap! Crumbs sprinkled like golden sand. She placed half on Dot’s leaf and half on hers. “We took turns pouring. Now we take turns biting. You first.”

Dot took a small bite. Crunch. “Mmm.” She smiled and passed the cookie piece back. “Your turn.”

Mimi nibbled. Crunch. “Yum.” She passed it back again. Bite by bite, they finished the cookie, taking turns. No crumbs were wasted. Their bellies felt warm and happy.

After tea, they wanted to look out the tall window. But it was high. Mimi stood on a stack of soft pillows, one by one, testing each. “Safe and steady,” she said. She climbed slowly, all by herself, feeling proud and independent. Dot flew beside her, ready to help if needed.

At the top, they took turns looking out. Mimi held the curtain for Dot. Dot held the curtain for Mimi. Outside, leaves shimmered, and the sky was bright and blue. They listened to a far-away bird sing. Tweet-twee! Tweet-twee!

Dot whispered, “Taking turns feels fair.”

Mimi nodded. “Taking turns helps us both feel happy. When we wait, we can listen, breathe, and notice. We can be mindful.”

They climbed down, putting the pillows back neatly. Mimi coiled the ribbon rope and tucked it by the baseboard. The guest room looked tidy again. The air smelled like lemon and a little like cookie crumbs.

Dot smiled. “Today I learned to take turns.”

Mimi smiled back. “Me too. Taking turns means share time, wait kindly, and help each other. It makes play better.”

They bumped friend-to-friend—paw to wing. Then they waved good-bye, feeling calm and proud.

The End.
