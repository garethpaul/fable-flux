# Nutkin's Colorful Discovery

In a bright creative art room filled with paintbrushes and colorful paper, lived a little squirrel named Nutkin. His fluffy gray tail twitched with excitement as he looked around at all the wonderful art supplies. Rainbow crayons sat in neat rows. Glitter sparkled in the morning sunlight streaming through the windows.

But today, Nutkin had a big problem. His tummy was rumbling loudly, and he felt very tired and grumpy.

"Grrrrowl!" went his belly. "I'm so hungry, but I only want cookies and candy!" Nutkin whined. He had been eating nothing but sweet treats all week, and now he felt yucky and sleepy.

Nutkin tried to paint a picture, but his paws felt shaky. The paintbrush felt too heavy. His beautiful painting looked all wobbly and messy.

"Why can't I make pretty art today?" Nutkin wondered aloud. He sat down on a soft cushion, feeling frustrated.

Just then, Ms. Robin the art teacher flew into the room. Her bright red feathers gleamed as she carried a basket full of colorful foods.

"Good morning, Nutkin!" she chirped cheerfully. "I brought some special art supplies today - rainbow foods!"

Nutkin's nose twitched curiously. "Rainbow foods? What are those?"

Ms. Robin smiled and opened her basket. "These are nature's crayons! Look - red strawberries, orange carrots, yellow bananas, green broccoli, blue blueberries, and purple grapes!"

"But those aren't art supplies," Nutkin said, scratching his head with his tiny paw.

"Oh, but they are!" Ms. Robin explained. "When you eat rainbow foods, they give your body energy and help your brain think clearly. Then you can create the most amazing art!"

Nutkin looked at the colorful foods. They did look pretty, like a real rainbow. "Will they really help me feel better?"

"Let's try!" Ms. Robin suggested. "First, let's wash our paws - clean paws make the best art!"

Nutkin washed his little paws with warm, soapy water. The bubbles tickled and smelled like fresh flowers.

Then he tried a sweet strawberry. "Mmm! It tastes like sunshine!" he giggled.

Next came a crunchy orange carrot. "Crunch, crunch! It sounds like music!" Nutkin said, wiggling his ears.

He nibbled on green broccoli that looked like tiny trees, and popped purple grapes that burst with sweet juice.

As Nutkin ate his rainbow foods, something magical happened. His tummy stopped grumbling. His paws stopped shaking. His eyes became bright and sparkly.

"I feel so much better!" Nutkin exclaimed, jumping up and down. "My body feels strong and happy!"

"Now let's create art!" Ms. Robin said.

Nutkin picked up his paintbrush again. This time, his paws were steady and strong. He painted beautiful trees with green leaves, bright yellow suns, and red flowers that looked just like the strawberries he had eaten.

"Look! My painting is as colorful as the rainbow foods!" Nutkin cheered.

Ms. Robin clapped her wings. "When we feed our bodies healthy foods, we can do amazing things! Healthy foods are like fuel for our imagination."

From that day on, Nutkin always ate his rainbow foods before creating art. He learned that taking care of his body helped him think better, feel happier, and make the most beautiful artwork in the whole art room.

"Healthy foods make everything more fun!" Nutkin would tell his friends, as he painted masterpiece after masterpiece with his strong, steady paws.

The End.
