# Miri Mouse and the Bubble of Calm

In a cozy basement lab, lights hummed softly. Little machines blinked. Bottles lined the shelves like rainbow soldiers. Miri Mouse, a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright eyes, loved to help here. She wore a tiny blue apron and a smile.

“Good morning, lab!” Miri chirped. She sniffed the warm, dusty air. It smelled like lemons, books, and a hint of popcorn from last night.

Today, Miri wanted to build a Bubble Catcher. It was a gentle tool that could scoop big, shiny bubbles without popping them. She set out a spoon, a small fan, and a clear cup. “I will be patient,” she told herself. “I will be careful.”

Her friend, Pip the cricket, hopped in. “Chirp-chirp! Need help, Miri?”

“Yes, please,” said Miri. “We will make a Bubble Catcher and share it with everyone.”

They mixed soap and water. Swish-swish went the spoon. The water looked like liquid pearls. Miri dipped the ring and blew. A bubble grew. It shimmered with pink and green swirls. “Oooh,” said Miri. “So shiny!”

She slid the clear cup under the bubble. Pop! The bubble disappeared.

Miri’s whiskers drooped. “Oh,” she said softly.

“Try again,” chirped Pip.

Miri made another bubble. She moved the cup too fast. Pop!

Her ears grew warm. Her tail twitched. “Why won’t it work?” she squeaked. Her tummy felt tight, like a knot.

Pip tilted his head. “Do you feel mad?”

Miri stamped one tiny foot. “A little,” she said. “I tried two times!”

Pip nodded. “It’s okay to feel mad,” he chirped. “Let’s use a calm plan.”

Miri took a slow breath in. The lab smelled like soap and lemons. She let the breath out like a soft “whooo.” She put her paws on her belly. In and out. The knot began to loosen.

“I will count,” said Miri. “One… two… three.” Her voice got gentle again.

“Good plan,” said Pip. “Anger is a big feeling. We can listen to it and be gentle.”

Miri looked at the tools. “I think I moved too fast,” she said. “I will be honest with myself. I rushed.”

“That’s smart,” said Pip. “Honesty helps us learn.”

Miri dipped the ring again. A bubble grew. She held the cup steady. She whispered, “Slow and soft.” She brought the cup close like a feather. Almost there—

Pop!

Miri’s eyes filled with tears. Her tail curled tight. “I want to scream!” she squeaked. Her paws shook.

She set the cup down and stepped back. “I feel very angry,” she said honestly. “I need a break.”

Miri sat on a small mat. She hugged a bean bag. It felt squishy and cool. Pip sat beside her, quiet and kind. They listened to the soft hum of the lights. Miri counted to five. She breathed in. She breathed out. The anger felt like a red ball shrinking and turning pink, then warm and small.

“I feel calmer,” she said.

Pip smiled. “What will you try next?”

Miri wiped her eyes. “I will ask for help,” she said. “Pip, can you hold the fan on low?”

“Yes!” chirped Pip.

They tried again. Miri blew a bubble. It wobbled, big and round, like a tiny planet. Pip held the fan on the lowest click. The air moved like a soft whisper. Miri slid the cup under, slow as a cloud.

Plop! The bubble slipped into the cup and did not pop. It sat there, shining and still.

“We did it!” Miri squeaked. She clapped her paws. Pip chirped a happy tune. The lab seemed brighter.

Miri made two more bubbles. Plop. Plop. Three bubbles in three cups. They looked like crystal moons.

Just then, Tavi the turtle peeked in. “Hello,” he said, voice low and kind. “I heard happy sounds.”

Miri’s cheeks warmed. “We made a Bubble Catcher,” she said. “But I got very mad first. I wanted to yell. I took a break and told the truth about my feelings. I asked for help. Then it worked.”

Tavi nodded slowly. “That is brave,” he said. “Anger is a feeling. It is not a boss. You were patient. You were honest. You chose calm.”

Miri grinned. “When I felt mad, I breathed. I counted. I used gentle hands. I tried again.”

Pip added, “And we worked as a team!”

They shared the bubbles with Tavi. They watched them glow and float inside the cups. The lab smelled sweet and sounded like soft humming and quiet giggles.

Miri patted her chest. “I am proud,” she said. “I can have big feelings. I can make safe choices. I can wait and try again.”

Pip chirped, “Today we learned: When anger comes, we can stop, breathe, tell the truth about our feelings, and be patient. Then we can try again.”

Miri nodded. “That is our Bubble of Calm.”

They cleaned up the lab. Swish-swish went the sponge. Click went the fan. The basement felt cozy and kind. As they left, Miri whispered, “Thank you, feelings. Thank you, patience. Thank you, honesty.”

The End.
