# Benny Bear's Big Backyard Adventure

Benny the bear cub stretched his fuzzy arms up high as the warm sunshine tickled his nose. The bright yellow sun was shining in the big blue sky, and the soft green grass felt wonderful under his paws.

"What a perfect day to play outside!" Benny said with a happy wiggle.

As Benny looked around the sunny backyard, he saw his friend Rosie Rabbit hopping near the flower bed. But Rosie looked sad, and her ears were drooping down.

"Hi Rosie!" called Benny, waving his paw. "Want to run and play with me?"

Rosie shook her head slowly. "I can't, Benny. Look at all these weeds growing in Mrs. Garden's flower bed. They're making the pretty flowers sick, but I'm too small to pull them all out."

Benny walked over and saw the tall, prickly weeds choking the colorful flowers. The red roses and yellow daisies looked droopy and sad.

"Hmm," said Benny, scratching his furry head. "That's not fair to the flowers! They need help. Maybe we can work together?"

"But there are so many weeds," sighed Rosie. "And I promised Mrs. Garden I would take care of her flowers while she's away."

Benny's eyes lit up with an idea. "I know! Let's make it fun! We can turn pulling weeds into an exercise game!"

"An exercise game?" asked Rosie, her ears perking up.

"Yes!" Benny bounced excitedly. "We can stretch up high to reach the tall weeds - that's good for our arms! We can squat down low for the small ones - that's good for our legs! And we can take turns so it's fair for both of us!"

Rosie's whiskers twitched with interest. "That does sound fun! But how should we take turns?"

Benny thought carefully. "How about this - you pull one weed, then I pull one weed? That way we both do the same amount of work, and we both get the same amount of exercise!"

"That's very fair!" agreed Rosie, clapping her paws together.

The two friends got to work. Benny reached way up high, stretching his arms like a tall tree. "One, two, three - PULL!" he grunted, yanking out a big weed. His muscles felt strong and warm.

Then Rosie squatted down low like a little ball. "My turn!" she said, pulling out a small weed near the ground. "This is good exercise for my legs!"

They took turns pulling weeds, counting together. "One for Rosie, one for Benny, two for Rosie, two for Benny!" Their hearts beat faster, and they felt energized and happy.

Soon, other friends came to see what they were doing. Tommy Turtle and Sally Squirrel wanted to help too!

"Can we join your exercise game?" asked Tommy.

Benny and Rosie looked at each other and smiled. "Of course! The more friends who help, the better! We can all take turns fairly."

The four friends worked together, pulling weeds and exercising their bodies. They stretched high, squatted low, and even did jumping jacks between the flower rows. Their bodies felt strong and healthy from all the movement.

When they finished, the flower bed looked beautiful again. The roses stood tall and proud, and the daisies danced in the breeze.

"Look what we did together!" said Benny, wiping his happy, sweaty forehead. "We helped the flowers, we exercised our bodies, and we all worked fairly as a team!"

"And it was so much more fun than doing it alone," added Rosie.

Mrs. Garden returned that evening and gasped with joy when she saw her beautiful, weed-free flowers.

"Thank you for being such responsible, caring friends," she said, giving them all warm hugs.

That night, Benny fell asleep with a big smile, his body tired but happy from a day full of exercise, friendship, and helping others.

The End.
