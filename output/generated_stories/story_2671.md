# The Lion Cub’s Dinner Game

In a sunny dining room with a big wooden table, a brave little lion cub named Lio sat with his paws folded. The room smelled like warm bread and sweet mango. The curtains were bright and swishy. The chairs made tiny squeaks on the floor.

Lio’s Mama placed bowls on the table. “We are sharing traditions tonight,” she said with a smile. “Our friends will bring foods and a game from their homes.”

Lio’s whiskers twitched. “I like games,” he said. “I want to win!”

“Winning is fun,” Mama said. “But tonight is about learning and sharing. Let’s be mindful. We can breathe slow and notice new things.”

Ding-dong! The bell rang. Lio’s friends stepped in. Mei the panda wore a red ribbon. Kofi the baby elephant had a green scarf. Ana the kangaroo joey hopped with a tiny drum. The dining room filled with happy voices and clinks of dishes.

“It smells yummy,” Lio said, sniffing. He smelled ginger, coconut, and warm tomato sauce. His tummy rumbled.

Mei set down a steaming bowl. “This is congee,” she said. “My family eats it to feel cozy.”

Kofi lifted a pot. “This is jollof rice,” he said. “We dance while we stir!”

Ana placed a plate of lamingtons. “Sweet treats from my home,” she said, grinning.

Mama set out bread and a salad. “We also have our family soup,” she said. The soup smelled like carrots and herbs.

They all sat. The chairs squeaked again. “Let’s say hello in our ways,” Mama said.

Mei put her paws together. “Ni hao,” she said softly.

Kofi lifted his trunk. “Akwaaba!” he trumpeted.

Ana waved. “G’day!”

Lio puffed out his little chest. “Roar! That means hello to me,” he giggled.

They tasted the foods. “Mmm,” said Lio, feeling the warm, soft congee on his tongue. “It’s like a hug.”

“The rice is spicy,” Ana said, eyes wide. “But in a good way!”

Kofi dipped bread into soup. “So cozy,” he said, wiggling his toes.

After dinner, Mama clapped. “Now, a tradition-sharing game. Each of you will teach us a small sport move from your culture. We will try, one by one.”

Lio’s eyes shone. “I will be so fast,” he said.

Mei stood. “In my family, we practice mindful tai chi. Slow and calm,” she said. She floated her paws like clouds. “Breathe in… breathe out.”

Lio tried to move slowly. His tail swished. But he wobbled and stepped on a chair leg. “Oof!” he said. “Slow is hard!”

Kofi smiled. “That’s okay. Now my turn. We play keepy-uppy with a little ball.” He bounced a soft ball on his trunk. “Tap, tap, tap.”

Lio bounced the ball on his paw. It fell. “Oh no!” he said. His ears drooped.

Ana hopped up. “We practice footy kicks,” she said, placing a small foam ball on the floor. She tapped it forward. “A gentle kick!”

Lio kicked. The ball rolled under the table. “Grr,” he grumbled. His face felt hot. His paws felt tight. “I can’t do it!”

The room grew quiet. Lio’s chest felt tight, like a knot. He wanted to roar, a big roar. But he remembered Mama’s words: be mindful.

Mama put a gentle paw on Lio’s back. “Let’s take three belly breaths,” she said softly. “Feel the air go in. Feel it go out.”

Lio closed his eyes. He felt the warm light on his face. He smelled sweet mango again. He breathed in—one, two, three—and out—one, two, three. His paws loosened. The knot in his chest slowly untied.

“I was frustrated,” Lio said. “My body felt hot.”

“That happens,” Mei said kindly. “We all learn new things.”

“In my home,” Kofi said, “we sing when we feel stuck.” He hummed a soft tune. “Mm-mm-mm.”

“In mine,” Ana said, “we try again with a smile.”

Lio opened his eyes. “Can you show me slow again, Mei?”

Mei nodded. “Cloud hands,” she said. “Move like a cloud.” Lio lifted his paws, floating slow. He breathed in and out. He didn’t wobble this time. He felt calm.

“Kofi, your ball?” Lio asked.

Kofi tapped the ball to Lio. Lio bounced it once, twice. Plop—it fell. Lio took a breath and tried again. Tap, tap. “I did two!” he laughed.

“Ana, a tiny kick?” Lio asked.

“Tiny kick,” Ana said. Lio tapped the foam ball. It rolled to Mama’s paw. “Goal!” Mama cheered.

Everyone clapped. The dining room rang with happy sounds—claps, giggles, and tiny chair squeaks.

Lio’s whiskers lifted. “I learned your moves,” he said. “They are different and fun. Thank you for sharing.”

Mei smiled. “And you shared your roar hello.”

Kofi bobbed his head. “And your brave try.”

Ana bumped paws with Lio. “Teamwork!”

Mama brought little cups of warm tea. “What did we learn tonight?” she asked.

Lio sipped. “We learned that friends have different foods and games. We can try them with calm breaths. We can share our own ways, too.”

“And when we feel frustrated,” Mei added, “we breathe.”

“And sing,” Kofi said.

“And smile and try again,” Ana finished.

Lio nodded. “Different is special. Sharing makes us strong.” He touched his heart. “I felt mad. I breathed. I tried. I learned.”

They all lifted their cups. “To sharing!” they said.

The dining room felt cozy and bright. The sweet mango smell mixed with warm tea. Lio’s heart felt big and soft.

He smiled and gave a small, happy roar.

The End.
