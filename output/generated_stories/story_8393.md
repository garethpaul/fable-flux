# Benny Bear's Helpful Workshop Day

Benny Bear was a fluffy brown cub who loved to explore. One sunny morning, he heard interesting sounds coming from Grandpa Joe's garage workshop. *Tap, tap, tap!* went a hammer. *Whirrrr!* went a drill.

"What's happening in there?" Benny wondered aloud, his round ears perking up with curiosity.

He peeked through the open garage door and saw Grandpa Joe working at a big wooden table covered with colorful tools. There were screws scattered on the floor, and old bicycle parts piled in the corner.

"Hello, Grandpa Joe!" called Benny cheerfully. "Can I help you?"

Grandpa Joe looked up with a warm smile. His gray beard was speckled with sawdust. "Well, hello there, Benny! I'm fixing broken toys to give to children who need them. Would you like to be my helper?"

Benny's eyes sparkled with excitement. "Yes, please! I love helping!"

Grandpa Joe showed Benny a red wagon with a wobbly wheel. "This wagon just needs a new bolt. Can you find me a shiny silver bolt from that jar?"

Benny carefully looked through the jar of bolts. They clinked and clanked as he searched. "Is this one good?" he asked, holding up a perfect silver bolt.

"Perfect!" said Grandpa Joe. "You have very good eyes, Benny."

As they worked together, Benny noticed his paws getting dirty from the tools. "My paws are all dusty," he said.

"That's okay," Grandpa Joe chuckled. "When we work hard, we get a little messy. But we should wash our paws when we're done to take care of ourselves. Clean paws are healthy paws!"

Benny nodded seriously. "Taking care of myself is important too!"

Soon, Mrs. Robin flew into the garage with her three little chicks. "Tweet, tweet! We heard you were fixing toys. Could you help us too? Our nest swing broke in the wind."

"Of course!" said Benny immediately. "Everyone is welcome in our workshop!"

Grandpa Joe smiled proudly at Benny. "That's the spirit! Let's all work together."

The robin family perched on the workbench while Benny and Grandpa Joe examined the tiny swing. It was made from twigs and string.

"Look," said Benny, pointing to some colorful yarn scraps in the recycling bin. "We could use this pretty yarn instead of the old string!"

"Wonderful idea!" chirped Mrs. Robin. "Recycling those scraps will make our swing even more beautiful!"

They carefully rewove the swing with the soft yarn. The little chicks tweeted happily as they tested their new swing.

"Thank you for including us," said Mrs. Robin gratefully. "You made us feel so welcome!"

As the sun began to set, Benny and Grandpa Joe washed their paws with warm, soapy water. The workshop smelled like wood shavings and fresh air.

"Benny," said Grandpa Joe, "you were such a wonderful helper today. You showed kindness to everyone who needed help."

Benny's chest puffed up with pride. "Helping others makes my heart feel warm and happy! And I learned that taking care of myself helps me help others better."

"That's exactly right," Grandpa Joe agreed. "When we help others, share our space, and take care of ourselves, we make the whole world a little brighter."

Benny looked around the tidy workshop, filled with newly fixed toys ready to bring joy to children. He felt proud knowing that his helpful paws had made a difference.

The End.
