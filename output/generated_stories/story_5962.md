# Leo the Lion Cub and the Tidy Time Plan

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived with his family in a playful living room. The rug was bouncy and blue, the couch was a big, cozy mountain, and the toy basket was a colorful rainbow box. Sunlight sparkled through the window and made warm squares on the floor.

One morning, Leo roared a tiny roar. “Rrr!” he said. “I want to build a tower as tall as the couch!” He pulled out blocks, cars, puzzle pieces, crayons, and his favorite world globe ball. The living room looked like a busy city. Clack, clack went the blocks. Vroom went the cars. Scritch-scratch went the crayons on paper.

Mama Lion walked in and smiled. “I see big play,” she said. “And I see a big mess.”

Leo looked around. “It is a big mess,” he said, ears twitching. He felt a little flutter in his tummy. He liked big play, but the mess felt big too.

“Play is fun,” said Mama. “And being responsible is important. Responsible means we take care of our things and our space.”

Leo thought. He loved his toys. He loved his living room. He wanted to be responsible. “I can do it,” he said, standing tall. “I am a brave cub.”

Just then, Leo’s cousin Lila called on the family video screen. Her face popped up with a smile. “Hi, Leo! Want to play tower together?” she asked, holding up her own blocks. Lila lived far away, across the sea. Leo loved that they could play at the same time.

“Yes!” Leo said. “But first, I need to make a plan. I want to clean as I play.”

“A plan?” Lila asked. “That sounds smart.”

Leo nodded. “I will be a problem solver,” he said. “My problem is: big mess. My goal is: clean and fun.”

He looked around and used his nose to sniff. The room smelled like crayons and warm sunshine. He used his eyes to sort. Blue blocks here. Red cars there. Puzzle pieces in the box with a click. Crayons in the cup with a tap-tap.

“I will make tidy zones,” Leo said. “Blocks by the couch. Cars near the rug road. Art at the table. Globe ball on the shelf.”

Mama clapped softly. “You are using critical thinking,” she said. “You are making a plan and taking steps.”

Leo puffed his chest. “I have feelings,” he said. “I feel proud. I also feel a little tired.”

“It’s okay to feel both,” Mama said. “You can take small steps.”

Leo took three slow lion breaths. In… out. In… out. In… out. His shoulders relaxed. “I feel calm now,” he said.

Lila held up a sign she drew: “Tidy Time Plan.” She said, “Let’s say it together!”

Leo smiled. “Step one: Play in one zone,” he said.
“Step two: Put toys back before moving to a new zone,” Lila added.
“Step three: Ask for help if it feels too big,” said Leo.
“Step four: Be kind and brave,” said Lila.

They started. Leo played in the block zone. He stacked blocks: click, clack, balance. Lila counted on the screen, “One, two, three, four!” When he finished, he put the blocks back in the basket. Plunk, plunk, plunk.

Then Leo moved to the car zone. “Vroom, beep, zoom!” he sang. He made a little road with tape. When he was done, he parked each car in a neat line. “Good night, car,” he whispered to each one.

Next was the art zone. Leo colored a picture of the Earth, round and blue and green. “We share one big world,” he said. “We all take care of it.” He put the crayons in the cup and the paper in the art bin. “Taking care is being a global citizen,” Mama said. “We start at home, and it helps the world.”

Suddenly, Leo saw a spill. His cup had tipped. Water dripped on the rug. Drip, drip, drip. Leo’s heart beat fast. “Oh no!” he said. He felt worried, like a little cloud in his chest.

He took a lion breath. In… out. “I can handle this,” he said. “I will be responsible. I will be assertive and ask for what I need.”

“Mama,” Leo said, using a strong, kind voice. “I spilled water. I need a towel, please.”

“Thank you for telling me,” said Mama, bringing a soft towel. “Let’s clean together.” Leo pressed the towel. Squish. The water soaked up. The rug felt warm and dry again.

Lila cheered from the screen. “You did it! You asked for help. You solved the problem.”

Leo smiled wide. His whiskers wiggled. He looked around. The living room was neat. The rainbow box was full. The floor was clear. The couch mountain was ready for a cozy rest.

Mama gave Leo a gentle hug. “How do you feel now?” she asked.

“I feel proud,” said Leo. “My body feels calm. My heart feels happy. I took care of my things. I took care of my space. I took care of our world in a small way.”

“And you were brave and kind,” said Mama.

Leo waved to Lila. “Thank you for the plan,” he said.

“We did it together,” Lila said. “Across the sea!”

Leo curled on the couch mountain with his globe ball. The sunlight felt warm on his fur. He whispered, “I can play. I can plan. I can clean. I can ask for help. I am responsible.”

And in the playful living room, everything felt safe, bright, and ready for more adventures.

The clear lesson: When we make a plan, take small steps, care for our things, and ask for help when we need it, we are being responsible. We can solve problems, feel our feelings, and help our big world—starting right at home.

The End.
