# Benny Beaver's Big Bathroom Problem

Benny Beaver was a very busy little beaver. He loved to build things with his wooden blocks and splash in his bathtub. But today, something was making Benny feel very frustrated.

"I can't reach the soap!" Benny said, standing on his tippy-toes in the bathroom. The soap dish was too high on the sink counter. Benny stretched and stretched, but his little paws couldn't quite touch it.

"Grrrr!" Benny growled, feeling his cheeks get warm. His tail started to slap against the bathroom floor. *Thump, thump, thump!* The sound echoed off the shiny white tiles.

Mama Beaver heard the noise from the kitchen. "Benny, what's wrong?" she called.

"Everything is too high!" Benny shouted. "I can't wash my paws! I can't brush my teeth! I can't do anything by myself!"

Benny felt so frustrated that he wanted to cry. His little heart was beating fast, and his paws were getting sweaty. He sat down on the soft blue bathroom mat and took a deep breath. The mat felt fuzzy under his bottom.

"When I feel frustrated, I can stop and think," Benny said to himself, remembering what Mama had taught him. He closed his eyes and counted to five. "One... two... three... four... five."

The bathroom was quiet except for the gentle *drip, drip* of the faucet. Benny opened his eyes and looked around. That's when he made an amazing discovery!

"Wait a minute," Benny said, his brown eyes getting wide. "Maybe I can find a way to reach!"

Benny noticed his sturdy wooden step stool in the corner by the bathtub. It was yellow and had little flowers painted on it. "I wonder if this could help me," he thought.

Benny pushed the step stool across the smooth bathroom floor. *Scrape, scrape, scrape!* It made a funny sound that made him giggle. He positioned it right in front of the sink.

"This is like exercise!" Benny said happily, feeling his muscles work as he pushed. "I'm getting stronger!"

Carefully, Benny climbed onto the step stool. Now he was tall enough to reach the sink! The soap smelled like fresh lemons, and the water felt warm and bubbly on his paws.

"I did it!" Benny cheered, washing his paws all by himself. "I found my own solution!"

Just then, Mama Beaver peeked into the bathroom. "Benny, you look so proud! What happened?"

"I was frustrated because I couldn't reach," Benny explained, drying his paws with the soft green towel. "But I stopped, took deep breaths, and found a way to help myself! I used my step stool!"

Mama Beaver smiled. "That's wonderful, Benny! You learned that when we feel frustrated, we can take a moment to calm down and look for solutions."

"And I learned I can ask for help if I need it," Benny added. "But sometimes I can figure things out on my own too!"

From that day on, whenever Benny felt frustrated, he remembered to stop, breathe, and think. Sometimes he found his own solutions, and sometimes he asked for help. Both ways were perfectly okay!

Benny kept his step stool in the bathroom, and every time he used it, he felt proud and independent. He had learned that frustration was just a feeling that would pass, and there was always a way to solve problems with patience and creativity.

The End.
