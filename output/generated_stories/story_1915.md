# Benny Bunny’s Bright Choice

Benny Bunny was a determined rabbit with soft gray fur and big curious eyes. He loved to tinker and think. His favorite place was the basement lab under his cozy burrow. It smelled like warm carrots and clean earth. Little lights blinked. Tools clicked. Bottles clinked. A gentle hum filled the air.

“Today I will make a Helper Machine,” Benny said. His whiskers twitched with excitement. “It will clean up the garden. It will water plants. It will help friends!”

Benny hopped down the wooden steps. Tap, tap, tap went his paws. He switched on a lamp. Click! The bright light made the shiny gears sparkle. He put on his tiny goggles. He tied his fluffy ears back so they would not fall in his way.

On the bench sat two batteries. One was big and easy. One was small and green with a leaf picture. A note read, “Green one: saves energy.”

Benny looked from one battery to the other. He wiggled his nose. “I want the machine to be fast,” he said. “Fast, fast, fast!” He tapped the big battery. It felt heavy. He tapped the green one. It felt light.

His friend, Dot the ladybug, flew in through the window and landed on a beaker. “Hi, Benny! What are you building?” she asked. Her tiny wings made a soft bzz.

“A Helper Machine!” Benny smiled. “Should I pick the big battery or the green one?”

Dot hovered. “Hmm. The green one uses less energy,” she said. “That helps the Earth.”

Benny’s ears wiggled. He liked fast things. He pictured the big battery zooming the machine around the yard. He also pictured the little green leaf. He had a choice to make.

Benny took a deep breath. “I will try the big battery first,” he decided. “Just for now.”

He clicked the big battery in place. Click, clack! He turned the knob. Whirr! The Helper Machine shook. It buzzed louder and louder. “Zoom!” Benny cheered.

But the machine raced too fast. Vroom! It bumped a box. Thump! It knocked over a jar of water. Splash! Cold water ran across the table and dripped to the floor. Drip, drop, drip.

“Oh no!” Benny cried. His belly felt tight. Dot flew back with a gasp. “My wings are wet!”

Benny pressed the off button. Beep. The room grew quiet, but the floor was messy. A little carrot picture book got wet. The pages curled. Benny’s ears drooped.

He looked at the puddle, then at Dot’s damp wings, then at the two batteries. He blinked. He felt sorry. He had chosen fast over careful. He had chosen big over green. It was time to fix the problem.

“I am sorry, Dot,” Benny said softly. “I made a quick choice. I did not think about safety. I did not think about saving energy.” He handed Dot a tiny napkin. “Can I help dry your wings?”

“Thank you, Benny,” Dot said. She smiled. “Saying sorry is a good start. Now you can make a better choice.”

Benny nodded. He grabbed a towel and wiped the floor. Swish, swish. He patted the book. Tap, tap. He set the jar upright and tightened the lid. Twist! He took out the big battery. Then he picked up the small green one with the leaf.

“This time, I will choose the green battery,” he said. “I want to help, not hurry.”

He clicked the green battery in place. Click, clack! He turned the knob a little. Whirr... but soft. The Helper Machine hummed like a happy bee. Mmmm.

Benny added a tiny water cup so it would pour slowly. Drip, drip, sip. He added a gentle brush to pick up crumbs without knocking things over. Brush, brush. He put little bumpers on the sides so it would not hurt the jars. Boing, boing.

“Let’s test it,” he said.

The Helper Machine rolled around the lab. It sipped up small spills. It brushed dust into a bin. It used just a little power. The green battery light blinked, “Thank you.”

Dot clapped her tiny hands. “It works! And it’s gentle.”

Benny grinned. His chest felt light and warm. “I made a better choice,” he said. “I slowed down. I thought about safety. I thought about the Earth.”

He carried the machine up to the garden. The morning sun felt warm on his fur. The air smelled like fresh grass. The machine watered each plant with a soft mist. Pssst, pssst. The leaves sparkled. No splashes. No bumps.

Benny turned to Dot. “I am sorry again for the splash before,” he said. “Thank you for helping me think.”

“I forgive you,” Dot said. “You learned. You fixed the mess. You chose the green way.”

Benny patted the machine. “When I make choices, I can stop, think, and choose what is kind to friends and kind to our Earth,” he said. “That is a bright choice.”

Dot buzzed happily. “Bright like the sun,” she said.

Benny smiled and wiggled his whiskers. “Bright like a bunny brain,” he giggled.

They watched the garden drink the soft mist. The Helper Machine hummed quietly. Benny felt proud, not just because he had a machine, but because he had made a good decision, said sorry, and cared for the planet.

The End.
