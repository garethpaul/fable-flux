# Joey’s Gentle Helper Hops

Joey was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and a soft, warm pouch. One sunny morning, he hopped to the garden shed. The shed smelled like fresh wood and soil. It was a cozy place with shelves, pots, and tools.

Joey peeked inside. “Hello, shed!” he giggled. His ears twitched. He heard a tiny rustle. “Who’s there?”

“Cheep, cheep!” A little bird peeked from behind a flower pot. A soft feather floated through the air and tickled Joey’s nose. “Oh! Hello, little bird,” said Joey. “Are you okay?”

The bird fluttered its wings. “Cheep,” it said, sounding worried. Its nest had slipped onto a low shelf. Some twigs were scattered on the floor.

Joey took a slow breath in and a slow breath out, just like Mama taught him. “I can help,” he said kindly. “Helping feels good.”

He looked around the shed. It was a little messy today. A watering can lay on its side. A small rake leaned at a funny angle. A bright red glove sat all alone on the floor.

“First, I will be calm,” Joey whispered. He placed his paws on his chest and counted. “One… two… three… calm.” He smiled. “Now I can think.”

Joey spoke softly to the bird. “Let’s fix your nest. We will do it step by step.” The bird blinked and chirped, “Cheep-cheep,” a happy sound.

Joey picked up the twigs on the floor. “Crunch, crunch,” they sounded in his paws. He placed them gently in the nest. “Soft and snug,” he said. He added a leaf that smelled like lemon. “Mmm, fresh!”

He looked at the shelf. “This shelf is too low. Is there a safer spot?” Joey tilted his head and listened. He could hear the wind outside. “We need a spot out of the wind,” he said. He scanned the shed and saw a little hook near a small window. Sunlight made a warm, golden square on the wall.

“This place is bright and safe,” Joey decided. “But how can I get the nest up there?” He tapped his chin. Tap, tap. “I will problem-solve!”

Joey found a sturdy wooden box. He checked it with his paw. “Not wobbly,” he said. He set the box under the hook. Then he moved the little rake out of the way. “Safety first,” he said, nodding. “I will be careful.”

He took another slow breath. “I can do this.” He gently lifted the nest. “We are going up now,” he told the bird in a calm voice. He stepped onto the box. The wood felt smooth under his paws. “Step, step.” He reached up and set the nest on the hook, right in the warm, golden square.

The bird fluttered to the nest and snuggled in. “Cheep-cheep! Thank you!”

Joey smiled. “Helping feels warm in my heart,” he said, patting his chest.

Then Joey noticed the shed still looked a bit messy. “I can help more,” he said. “A tidy shed is a safe shed.” He picked up the red glove and found its partner on a shelf. He put the gloves together like friends. “Together is better,” he sang.

He stood the watering can upright. “Glug-glug,” it sounded. He set it near the door for easy watering later. He leaned the little rake and a small broom on the wall, with the bristles facing down so they would not fall. He found a soft cloth and wiped a dusty jar. “Wipe, wipe,” he said. The air smelled cleaner.

“Mindful and gentle,” Joey reminded himself. He moved slowly and noticed little things: the cool floor on his feet, the warm sun on his ears, the happy cheeps from the tiny bird. When he got thirsty, he paused and took a sip from his water bottle. “Listening to my body is kind,” he said.

Just then, a tiny mouse peeked out from behind a toolbox. “Squeak?” it said.

“Hello, Mouse,” said Joey. “Do you need help too?”

The mouse pointed with its whiskers to a small bag of seeds. The top was stuck. “Squeak-squeak!”

Joey checked the bag. “Hmm,” he hummed. “This knot is tight.” He took a breath in. “I can try.” He gently pulled one end. Tug. Then the other end. Tug. The knot didn’t move. He thought for a moment. “I will use a tool safely.”

He found a blunt wooden stick on the shelf. He slipped it into the loop of the knot and wiggled. Wiggle, wiggle. The knot loosened. “Yes!” Joey said softly. He opened the bag and poured a few seeds into a tiny dish. “Here you go.”

The mouse squeaked with joy and nibbled. “Squeak-squeak!”

Joey looked around. The shed was tidy. The bird was cozy. The mouse was fed. Joey’s heart felt big and happy. “Helping helps everyone,” he said. “It helps me feel peaceful too.”

As the sun got lower, Mama called, “Joey! Time to hop home!”

Joey waved to his new friends. “Good-bye, Bird! Good-bye, Mouse! Be safe!” He took one more slow breath, smelling the clean, woodsy air. He listened to the quiet, gentle sounds. He smiled.

Outside, he told Mama, “I helped today. I was calm. I solved problems. I was gentle.”

Mama hugged him. “That is wonderful, Joey. Helping makes the world brighter.”

Joey nodded. “I will keep helping, one careful hop at a time.”

The End.
