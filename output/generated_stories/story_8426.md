# Jump-Jump Joey Follows the Steps

In a comfortable nursery with soft yellow walls and gentle sunlight, a joyful kangaroo joey named Joey loved to bounce. The floor felt warm under his big feet. The air smelled like clean blankets and apple snacks. A quiet clock went tick-tock, tick-tock, like a friendly drum.

“Boing!” Joey laughed, hopping from a fluffy rug to a beanbag chair. His tail swished. His ears wiggled. “I’m ready to play!”

Miss Bree, the gentle teacher with a calm voice, smiled. She wore a blue apron with tiny stars. “Today we will build a Rainbow Tower,” she said. “We will follow directions. We have steps. When we follow the steps, our tower will stand tall.”

Joey clapped. “I love towers!”

Miss Bree showed a picture chart. Each step had a little drawing:
1. Place the big red block on the mat.
2. Add the big orange block on top.
3. Then add yellow, green, blue, and purple.
4. Count to three each time to make it steady.
5. Stop and check. Hands still. Eyes looking.

Joey bounced once more. “Boing! I can do it all at once!”

Miss Bree gently shook her head. “We do it step by step, Joey. Directions help us make good choices. They are like a map.”

Joey nodded. He wanted to follow directions, but his body felt like a happy drum. Boom-boom, bounce-bounce! The blocks were smooth and cool in his paws. The red one was heavy and shiny. It made a tiny thump on the mat.

“Step one,” Miss Bree said. “Place the big red block.”

Joey put the red block down. “Thump!” He smiled. “Easy!”

“Step two,” Miss Bree said. “Add the orange block on top.”

Joey tried to stack the orange block, but he felt wiggly. “Boing!” He did a little hop. The orange block slid. Clack! It fell to the side.

Joey’s ears drooped. “Oh no.”

Miss Bree knelt beside him. “It’s okay, Joey. Let’s use a strategy. Strategy means a plan. Our plan is to follow each step and be still for three counts. Try a still body, soft breath, slow hands.”

Joey took a soft breath. In… out… His chest felt warm. He put his paws on his knees to make his body still. “I can do it,” he whispered.

He picked up the orange block. “One… two… three,” he counted softly. He placed it on the red block. This time it stayed. Joey’s eyes grew wide. “It worked!”

“Great decision,” Miss Bree said. “You chose to go slow.”

“Step three,” Joey said to himself. “Yellow on top.” He touched the yellow block. It felt smooth and a little cool. He lifted it carefully. “One… two… three.” Plop. The tower grew taller.

Other children watched. A tiny koala friend, Kiki, whispered, “You can do it, Joey.” Joey felt proud and warm inside.

“Step four,” Miss Bree reminded. “Green.”

Joey’s tail twitched. He almost bounced, but he remembered the plan. “Still body. Soft breath. Slow hands,” he said, tapping his nose. He placed the green block. “One… two… three.” Steady.

“Blue next,” said Miss Bree.

Joey picked up the blue block. It smelled faintly like the wooden shelf. “One… two… thr—” He heard a giggle and turned his head. The blue block wobbled. “Oops!”

Miss Bree put a gentle hand near the tower, not touching it. “Eyes looking,” she said softly. “Finish your count.”

Joey faced the blocks again. “Three.” The blue block settled with a tiny click.

“Last one,” said Kiki. “Purple!”

Joey felt excited. His feet tippy-tapped. He wanted to hop and shout. But he remembered the directions. Steps make success. “Slow hands,” he said. He lifted the purple block and felt its weight. He set it on top. “One… two… three.”

He and Kiki didn’t breathe for a second. The tower stood tall and bright like a rainbow in the sun. Joey’s smile spread wide. “We did it!”

Everyone clapped. Clap, clap, clap! The sound was cheerful. Joey’s heart felt floaty and light. Miss Bree beamed. “You followed directions. You used a strategy. You made good decisions. How does it feel?”

Joey wiggled his ears. “It feels… steady. Like my tower!”

Miss Bree nodded. “When we follow directions, things go more smoothly. We stay safe. We help friends. We reach our goals.”

Joey looked at Kiki. “Do you want a turn? I can help. We can say the steps together.”

Kiki nodded. “Yes, please.”

Joey became the helper. He pointed to the chart and spoke slowly. “Red on the mat. Orange on top. Count to three. Stop and check. Hands still. Eyes looking.” His voice was kind.

Kiki placed the red block. Joey whispered, “One… two… three.” They breathed together. They stacked orange, yellow, and green. When Kiki’s paw shook, Joey said, “Soft breath.” Kiki smiled and steadied her hand. Blue and purple went on with tiny clicks.

Two rainbow towers stood side by side. The nursery felt calm and happy. The clock said tick-tock, tick-tock, like a soft song. The air smelled like apple slices and fresh crayons.

Miss Bree took a photo for the wall labeled, “We Follow Directions.”

Joey patted his chest. “When I go fast, things fall. When I follow steps, I stand tall.”

Miss Bree said, “That is a smart rhyme, Joey.”

Joey nodded. “My choice is to follow directions. My strategy is still body, soft breath, slow hands. I can do it.”

He gave Kiki a gentle high-five. No bump, no crash—just a soft tap. Peaceful and proud.

The Rainbow Towers glowed in the sunlight. Joey did a tiny, careful hop and whispered, “Boing,” with a quiet giggle.

The End.
