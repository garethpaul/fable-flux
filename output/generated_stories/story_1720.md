# Miri Mouse and the Balcony Garden Band

Miri was a kind mouse who lived on a sunny balcony garden. The garden smelled like mint leaves and warm tomatoes. The wind made the wind chimes sing, “Ting-ting!” Birds chirped. Bees buzzed. Miri’s whiskers twitched with joy.

“I love our garden,” Miri said, patting a soft leaf. “I want to help.”

On the balcony, friends gathered every morning. There was Bella the butterfly, Pip the pigeon, Tico the tiny turtle, and Numi the snail. They liked to water plants, count seeds, and make a little band with pots and spoons.

One morning, Miri had an idea. “Let’s make garden music for the seedlings,” she said. “Music helps them feel happy.”

“Yes!” said Bella, fluttering. “I can flap like a drum!”

“I can coo like a horn,” said Pip. “Coo-coo!”

“I can tap the pot,” said Tico. “Tup-tup!”

Numi smiled. “I can shake these dry seed pods. Chika-chika.”

Miri found a tiny leaf harp. She plucked it. “Plink, plink.” It sounded sweet, like a small raindrop.

They set up the Balcony Garden Band. But just as they began, Pip flapped his wings too close to the mint. “Coo! Make way, small friends,” he said, stepping on a seedling.

“Pip!” cried Miri. “Please be careful. The baby plant is small.”

Pip puffed up. “I am big and fast. I don’t have to wait.”

Miri’s ears drooped. The seedling bent. Tico frowned. “That was not kind.”

Miri took a deep breath. She remembered her special word: respect. Respect means we care for others’ feelings, space, and things. Respect means we listen and wait our turn.

Miri placed her tiny paw on the railing. “Friends,” she said softly, “our garden is for everyone. Big and small. We need to be gentle. We need to respect the plants and each other.”

Pip looked away. “But I like to go first,” he muttered.

Miri nodded. “I like to go first too sometimes. But we can take turns. Taking turns shows respect.”

Bella fluttered close. “We can practice,” she said.

Tico tapped the pot. “We can think it through,” he added. “A plan!”

Miri made a plan using critical thinking. She looked at the space. She noticed the sunny corner and the shady corner. She saw where the pots were and where the seedlings slept. She drew a simple map in the dirt with her tail.

“Here is our music circle,” Miri said. “We each have a spot. We wait for the leaf signal.” She held up a little leaf. “When I wave the leaf, we play. When I put it down, we stop. If someone forgets, we forgive and try again. We practice respect.”

Pip shuffled his feet. He peered at the bent seedling. “I am sorry, little plant,” he cooed. “I did not respect your space.”

Miri smiled. “Thank you for saying sorry. We forgive you.”

Numi gently propped the seedling with a tiny stick. “There,” he said softly. “We help, we heal.”

They began to practice. Miri waved the leaf. Plink-plink went the leaf harp. Coo-coo went Pip, from his spot. Tup-tup went Tico. Chika-chika went Numi. Flutter-flutter went Bella. The sounds mixed like warm sunshine and cool water.

But then Pip got excited. He started early. “Coo!” he cried.

Miri put down the leaf. Everyone stopped. They looked at Pip.

Pip’s feathers drooped. “Oh,” he said. “I forgot.”

Miri walked over and smiled. “It’s okay. We practice. We can try again. Thank you for stopping when you saw the leaf.”

They tried again. This time, Pip waited. He watched the leaf. He listened. When Miri waved it, he joined in at the right time. The music felt calm and happy. The wind chimes chimed along, “Ting-ting!”

Later, a tiny ant peeked from a crack. “Can I listen?” the ant asked.

“Yes!” said Miri. “Everyone is welcome. We include all friends.”

The ant smiled and sat by a basil plant. More ants came. A ladybug came. Even a shy moth came at dusk. The balcony garden felt full of gentle sounds and gentle hearts.

When they finished, Miri put down the leaf, and everyone stopped together. It felt good to be in a team.

Pip waddled over to the seedling and brushed it softly with his wing. “I respect you,” he whispered.

Miri’s whiskers twinkled. She turned to her friends. “What did we learn today?”

Bella fluttered. “Respect means taking turns.”

Tico tapped. “Respect means being gentle with plants and friends.”

Numi nodded. “Respect means we listen and stop when asked.”

Pip raised a wing. “Respect also means saying sorry and practicing to do better. Thank you for forgiving me.”

Miri hugged him. “We all make mistakes. We can forgive, include, and practice again.”

They watered the seeds together. The watering can made a soft glug-glug. The soil smelled rich and clean. The sun felt warm on their backs. The garden seemed to hum with peace.

Miri looked around the balcony garden band and smiled. “Respect makes our music sweet. Respect makes our garden safe. Respect makes our hearts kind.”

Everyone cheered, “Respect!”

And the seedlings, small and brave, stood up a little taller, as if they heard and felt the love.

The End.
