# Benny Bunny Breathes

Benny was a small, determined rabbit with soft gray fur and long ears that twitched when he was curious. One sunny morning, Benny hopped into the dining room. The table was big and wooden. It smelled like warm toast and sweet berries. Sunlight made bright squares on the floor. The chairs looked tall, like friendly giants.

“I can do it! I can pour the juice all by myself,” Benny said. His whiskers wiggled with excitement. He wanted to help set the table for breakfast.

Mama Rabbit smiled. “You are very determined, Benny. Remember our calm plan if you feel wobbly.”

“What’s the calm plan again?” Benny asked, tilting his head.

“Bunny breaths,” said Mama. “In through your nose, out through your mouth. Slow and soft.”

Benny nodded. He stood on a little step stool. He felt brave. He picked up the heavy orange juice jug. The jug was cold and slippery. “I got this,” he whispered.

But oh no! The jug tipped. A splash of orange juice sloshed onto the table. It made a sticky puddle. Some juice dripped to the floor with a soft plip, plip.

Benny’s heart thumped fast. His tummy felt tight like a knotted string. His ears drooped. “I messed up!” he squeaked. “I can’t do it. I’m bad at pouring.”

Mama Rabbit knelt beside him. Her voice was warm like toast. “Feelings are okay, Benny. Let’s notice them.”

Benny blinked. “My tummy is tight. My face is hot. I feel mad and sad.”

“That is emotional awareness,” Mama said kindly. “You noticed your feelings and your body. Now, let’s try our calm plan.”

Benny put a paw on his belly. “Bunny breaths,” he said softly.

“In,” Mama whispered.

Benny breathed in through his nose. He smelled oranges and pancakes. He felt his belly rise.

“Out,” Mama whispered.

Benny breathed out through his mouth. He felt the air tickle his whiskers.

They breathed together. In…and out. In…and out. Benny’s heart slowed. His tummy untied. His ears lifted a little.

“I feel calmer,” Benny said. “The juice is still sticky, but I feel okay.”

“That is brave,” Mama said. “You stayed with your feelings and took care of them.”

Benny looked at the puddle. It shone like a small orange lake. He listened to the quiet drip, drip. “We can clean it,” he said. “Even if I made a mistake, I can try again.”

Mama handed him a soft towel. It felt fluffy in his paws. “Let’s work together.”

Swipe, swipe. The towel made a gentle whisper sound. Benny giggled when the cold juice touched his paw. “It’s silly and slippery!” he said.

Soon the table was clean, and the floor was dry. The sun made warm stripes again. Mama set out a smaller cup with a little spout. “This cup is a helper,” she said. “It pours slowly.”

Benny stood tall on his stool. He held the helper cup with two paws. He took one bunny breath in…and one bunny breath out. “I am calm and careful,” he told himself.

He tilted the cup. The juice flowed in a thin, quiet stream. Glug…glug… The cup filled to the line. Not a drop spilled.

“I did it!” Benny cheered. His ears bounced. His eyes sparkled like morning dew.

Mama clapped softly. “I accept you just as you are, Benny, whether you spill or pour perfectly.”

Benny’s smile grew wide. “I accept me, too,” he said. “Sometimes I feel big feelings. I can notice them. I can breathe. I can try again.”

They sat at the table. The pancakes were fluffy and smelled like vanilla. The berries were sweet and cool. Benny took a bite. “Mmm,” he said. “Calm tastes yummy.”

Mama laughed. “Calm feels safe.”

Benny looked around the dining room. He heard the soft clink of forks and the rustle of napkins. He felt the smooth wood under his paws. Everything felt steady.

After breakfast, Benny practiced pouring water into a little plant. He breathed in and out. Not a drop spilled. The plant’s leaves sparkled with tiny beads of water. “Hello, plant,” Benny whispered. “I poured calmly for you.”

Mama gave Benny a gentle nose-kiss. “When things spill, we clean. When feelings spill, we breathe.”

Benny nodded. He placed his paw on his belly one more time. In…and out. He felt brave. He felt calm. He felt proud.

Then Benny hopped down from the stool. “I am a determined rabbit,” he said. “And I can be calm, even when juice goes plip, plip!”

Mama smiled. “That is the lesson for today.”

Benny wiggled his whiskers. “Notice feelings. Bunny breaths. Try again.”

“Yes,” said Mama. “Calm helps us do hard things.”

Benny took one more bunny breath. The dining room felt bright and friendly again. It smelled like oranges and pancakes and a new day.

The End.
