# Dolly the Dolphin's Thank You Dance

Dolly the dolphin lived in a magical place where sea creatures could visit land whenever they wanted. Her favorite spot was Mrs. Garcia's peaceful front porch, where colorful flowers bloomed and gentle breezes carried the sweet smell of jasmine.

One sunny morning, Dolly splashed up onto the wooden porch, her smooth gray skin glistening with water droplets. She loved how the warm sun felt on her back and how the porch creaked softly under her flippers.

"Good morning, Dolly!" called Mrs. Garcia from her rocking chair. "What brings you here so early?"

Dolly wiggled her tail excitedly. "Mrs. Garcia, I woke up feeling so happy! The ocean was sparkly, the fish were singing, and I could smell your delicious pancakes from far away. But I don't know how to show how thankful I feel."

Mrs. Garcia smiled warmly. "Well, dear, when I feel grateful, I like to move my body in special ways. Would you like to learn a thank you dance?"

"Oh yes!" Dolly clapped her flippers together, making a cheerful splashing sound.

"First," said Mrs. Garcia, standing up slowly, "we start by reaching our arms up high to thank the sky." She stretched her arms toward the fluffy white clouds.

Dolly lifted her flippers up high, wobbling a little as she balanced on her tail. "Like this?"

"Perfect! Now we spin around slowly to thank the wind." Mrs. Garcia turned in a gentle circle.

Dolly tried to spin, but her flippers got tangled and she tumbled onto the soft porch cushions with a giggle. "Oops! This is harder than swimming!"

"That's okay," laughed Mrs. Garcia. "Moving on land is different from moving in water. Let's try again, but this time, go very slowly."

Dolly took a deep breath and spun very carefully, using her tail to help her balance. The wooden boards felt smooth under her belly as she turned. "I did it! I can feel the breeze on my face!"

"Wonderful! Now we touch our hearts to thank everyone we love." Mrs. Garcia placed her hand over her heart.

Dolly pressed her flipper to her chest. "I'm thankful for you, Mrs. Garcia, and for my fish friends, and for my mama and papa!"

"Last, we reach down low to thank the earth that holds us up." Mrs. Garcia bent down and touched the porch floor.

Dolly leaned forward, touching the warm wooden boards with her flipper tips. She could feel the solid porch supporting her and smell the earthy scent of the flower boxes.

"Now let's do the whole dance together!" said Mrs. Garcia.

They moved together - up to the sky, spinning with the wind, touching their hearts, and reaching down to the earth. Dolly's movements became smoother and more graceful with each try. Her flippers and tail worked together like a beautiful ocean wave.

"I feel so warm and happy inside," said Dolly, her eyes sparkling. "The thank you dance makes my grateful feelings come out through my whole body!"

Mrs. Garcia hugged her gently. "That's exactly right, dear. When we move our bodies to show gratitude, we feel even more thankful than before."

From that day on, whenever Dolly felt grateful, she would visit the peaceful porch and do her thank you dance. And every time she moved her flippers up, around, to her heart, and down, she remembered all the wonderful things in her life that made her smile.

The End.
