# Kip the Kangaroo Learns to Care

In a cozy study corner filled with colorful books and soft cushions, little Kip the kangaroo joey was having snack time. His fluffy brown fur bounced as he hopped excitedly around the room, munching on crispy apple slices.

"Crunch, crunch, crunch!" went the juicy apple pieces between his tiny teeth. Kip loved the sweet taste and the way the juice made his whiskers tingle. But when he finished eating, he tossed the apple core right onto the soft carpet.

"There!" said Kip cheerfully, wiping his paws on his fuzzy belly. "All done!"

Just then, Mama Kangaroo hopped into the study corner. Her warm brown eyes looked sad when she saw the apple core on the floor.

"Oh, Kip," she said gently, her voice soft like a whisper. "What happened to your apple core?"

Kip's ears drooped a little. "I threw it down, Mama. I was finished with it."

Mama Kangaroo sat down beside her little joey, her pouch making a cozy spot for him to snuggle. "Sweet Kip, do you know what happens when we don't take care of our Earth?"

Kip shook his head, his big eyes curious.

"Come, let me show you," Mama said, reaching for a picture book with a bright green cover. She opened it to show pictures of beautiful forests, sparkling rivers, and happy animals.

"Our Earth is like a big, wonderful home for everyone," Mama explained, pointing to the colorful pictures. "The trees need clean soil to grow strong. The fish need clean water to swim and play."

Kip touched the pictures with his small paw. "The trees look so tall and green, Mama!"

"Yes, they do," Mama smiled. "But when we throw things on the ground instead of putting them where they belong, it can hurt our Earth home. The apple core has seeds that could grow into a beautiful apple tree, but only if we put it in the right place."

Kip's eyes grew wide. "Really? My apple could become a tree?"

"It could!" Mama nodded. "Apple cores belong in our compost bin, where they can help make rich, dark soil for new plants to grow. That's called taking care of our environment."

Kip felt his tummy flutter with excitement and a little bit of worry. "I didn't know, Mama. I'm sorry I didn't take care of our Earth home."

Mama Kangaroo wrapped her warm arms around her little joey. "It's okay, sweet one. Learning is how we grow. Do you want to help fix it?"

"Yes, yes!" Kip bounced up and down, his tail thumping softly on the cushion.

Together, they picked up the apple core. Kip held it carefully in his small paws as they hopped to the kitchen. Mama showed him the special green compost bin.

"In here, your apple core will help make food for the soil," Mama explained as Kip dropped it in. "And maybe someday, a new apple tree will grow!"

Kip clapped his paws together. "I want to always take care of our Earth home, Mama!"

From that day on, whenever Kip had a snack in his study corner, he always remembered to put his scraps in the right place. He learned that taking care of the Earth was like giving it a big, warm hug.

"Taking care of our Earth makes everything grow happy and strong," Kip would say proudly, "just like me!"

The End.
