# Bounce, Breathe, and Share

The sun was warm and bright in the sunny backyard. A joyful kangaroo joey named Kip bounced on the soft grass. Boing, boing! The air smelled like sweet flowers, and a gentle breeze brushed Kip’s ears. Birds chirped cheep-cheep, and a bee buzzed by.

“I am full of bounce!” Kip laughed. His little paws patted the ground. His tail swished like a paintbrush.

Kip had a shiny red ball. It felt smooth and cool in his paws. He tossed it up. “Whee!” The ball sparkled in the sun and landed with a soft thump.

Just then, Kip’s friend, Willa the wombat, peeked through the gate. “Hi, Kip!” Willa called. “Can I play, too?”

Kip hugged the red ball. He loved his ball. He loved bouncing. He didn’t want to stop. “Um… maybe… I’m still playing,” Kip said. His tummy felt tight, and his paws squeezed the ball. He felt a little hot in his cheeks.

Willa’s ears drooped. “Oh,” she said softly. “I can watch.”

Kip looked at Willa’s face. He could see her small frown. His chest felt a little heavy. He knew he liked playing with friends. But he also felt a big want in his body. He wanted to keep the ball.

Kip remembered something Mama Roo had taught him. “When big feelings come, stop and check in,” Mama had said. “We can bounce, breathe, and choose.”

Kip put one paw on his chest. “Hello, feeling,” he whispered. “You feel tight.” He took a slow breath in. Sniff… and a long breath out. Ffffff. The air smelled like sunshine and grass. He bounced very slowly. Boing… pause… boing… pause. His tail made a soft swish-swish.

Willa watched quietly. The birds still sang. The breeze cooled Kip’s warm cheeks.

Kip took another breath. “Sniff… fffff.” He felt the tight feeling soften, like a knot turning into a loose ribbon. “I feel calmer,” he said to himself. “I can choose.”

He looked at the red ball. He looked at Willa. “I can share,” Kip said. His voice got brighter. “We can take turns!”

Willa’s eyes sparkled. “Really?”

“Really,” Kip grinned. “I will roll it first. Then you roll it back.”

Kip set his paws wide. He felt the grass tickle his toes. He rolled the ball. It went whoosh across the yard and stopped by Willa’s paws.

“My turn!” Willa laughed. She rolled it back. The ball hummed softly over the grass. Kip caught it and smiled.

They made a little game. Kip called, “Slow like a turtle!” and rolled the ball s-l-o-w. Willa giggled and rolled it slow, too. Then Willa called, “Fast like a bird!” and they rolled it quick, zoom!

Kip felt his body happy and light. Sharing made the game more fun. “Thank you for waiting,” he told Willa.

“Thank you for sharing,” Willa said. She scratched her ear and looked at the flowers. “Can we try bouncy rolls?”

Kip nodded. “Yes! But let’s make a plan.” He remembered Mama’s words: “Plans help our bodies feel safe.” Kip held up a paw. “We each get three bounces,” he said. “Then we switch.”

“One… two… three!” Kip counted as he gently bounced the ball on the grass. Boing, boing, boing! The ball made soft thumpy sounds. Then he passed it to Willa. One… two… three! Willa’s turn.

They followed the plan. When Kip felt the big want sneak back—Mine! mine!—he noticed it. His paws felt a little grabby. He paused. “Bounce, breathe, and choose,” he whispered. He took a slow breath. Sniff… fffff. The grabby feeling faded. He smiled and waited for his turn.

Soon, a butterfly floated by, yellow like a tiny sun. It landed on the red ball and tickled Willa’s paw. Willa giggled. “It likes our game!”

Kip laughed. “Maybe it likes our calm turns,” he said. “We are gentle.”

After a while, Willa sat on the grass. “I’m getting tired,” she said. “My legs feel wobbly.”

Kip checked his body, too. His tail felt slow. His breath felt deep and steady. “I feel ready for water,” he said. “Let’s rest.”

They sat under a small tree. The shade felt cool. Kip shared his water bottle. The water tasted clean and fresh. He also shared a crunchy apple slice from his pouch. Crunch, crunch. Willa smiled with apple juice on her chin.

“Today I learned something,” Kip said softly. “When my feelings get big, I can stop, bounce slowly, breathe, and choose kind.”

Willa nodded. “And sharing makes games better,” she said. “Thank you for the apple, too.”

Kip patted his chest. His heart felt warm, like a little sun. “I’m proud of me,” he said. “I used my calm plan.”

Mama Roo waved from the back door. “Snack time, joeys!”

Kip stood up and held out a paw. “Let’s go together,” he said.

Willa took his paw. “Together,” she agreed.

They bounced, not too fast, not too slow, just right. They felt the soft grass. They heard the birds. They smiled big smiles.

Kip whispered, “Bounce, breathe, and choose.” He liked the way the words felt in his mouth—soft and strong. He knew he could use them any time his feelings got big.

The End.
