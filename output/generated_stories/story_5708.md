# Sunny Hops in the Backyard

Sunny was a joyful kangaroo joey with big, bouncy feet and a soft, warm nose. One bright morning, Sunny peeked out into the sunny backyard. The grass was tickly on his toes. Birds chirped, “Tweet-tweet!” The breeze smelled like sweet flowers and fresh leaves.

“Mama, can I hop by myself today?” Sunny asked, his eyes wide with excitement.

Mama Kangaroo smiled. “Yes, Sunny. You can explore. Remember to listen to your feelings, and be kind to others. I’ll be right here if you need me.”

Sunny gave a happy wiggle. “I feel brave!” he said, patting his chest. “Brave feels warm and strong.”

He bounced to the big sunflower patch. The yellow petals looked like tiny suns. “Hello, Sunflowers!” Sunny giggled, giving them a gentle sniff. They smelled sunny and soft.

Suddenly, Sunny heard a rustle. A little beetle was trying to climb a leaf. The leaf was shaky. The beetle slipped and wobbled.

“Oh no!” Sunny said. He felt a little pinch in his tummy. “I feel worried,” he whispered. “Worried feels tight.”

He took a slow breath, in and out, just like Mama taught him. “I can help. I can be gentle,” Sunny said. He placed a small twig near the beetle, like a tiny bridge. The beetle tapped its tiny feet across and reached the leaf. It wiggled its antennae as if to say, “Thank you!”

Sunny smiled. “I feel proud. Proud feels big and tall.”

He hopped on, past a puddle that sparkled like a mirror. Plip-plop! His toes made soft splashes. “Splash!” Sunny laughed. Then, he saw some wrappers near the fence. The wrappers crackled in the wind. A little ant slipped under one and peeked out.

Sunny frowned. “These don’t belong in the grass,” he said. “The backyard is our home.” He felt a tug in his heart. “I feel caring. Caring feels warm and gentle.”

Sunny picked up the wrappers and put them in the bin by the back door. “There you go,” he told the ant. “Now you have a clean path.” The ant marched on, tiny feet tapping, tap-tap-tap.

“Good job taking care of your space,” Mama called from the porch. “You are being a helper.”

Sunny’s ears perked up. He hopped to the tall lemon tree. The bright lemons smelled zesty. A breeze blew, and a lemon rolled toward a patch of prickly weeds. “Yikes!” Sunny jumped back. His chest felt jumpy. “I feel scared,” he said aloud. “Scared feels shaky.”

He put his paws on his belly and breathed slowly. “In, out,” he whispered. “I can be careful.” Sunny found a stick and nudged the lemon away from the prickles. “Safe now,” he said, calmer. The shakiness faded, like clouds moving away from the sun.

Just then, a small blue bird landed on the fence. “Chirp?” it asked softly. One wing was droopy.

Sunny tilted his head. “Hi, little bird. Are you okay?” He listened. He looked at its wing. He felt a tug of sadness. “I feel sad for you,” he said. “Sad feels heavy.”

He remembered what Mama said: Be kind to others. Sunny dipped his paw in the birdbath and gently splashed fresh water in. “Here is cool water,” he said. He found a leaf and made a tiny shade tent by leaning it against a smooth stone. “Here is shade.”

Mama hopped closer, careful and calm. “You noticed the bird’s feelings,” she said. “That is empathy. You are being a good friend.”

Sunny nodded. “I can feel my feelings and see others’ feelings too.”

The little bird sipped water, chirped a soft “Thank you,” and rested. Sunny’s heart felt bright and glowy. “I feel happy,” he said. “Happy feels light, like bubbles.”

Sunny hopped to the veggie patch. He saw a carrot peeking out. “Hello, carrot!” he giggled. He brushed some dry leaves away and tucked them into the compost bin. “These can make new soil,” he said. “That helps the earth.” He felt helpful and strong.

The sun was lower now, warm and sleepy. Sunny’s legs felt wobbly. “I feel tired,” he said. “Tired feels slow and heavy.” He looked at Mama and took a deep breath. “I did many things by myself today.”

Mama opened her pouch a little and patted the soft edge. “You did. You listened to your feelings. You helped the beetle, the bird, and the backyard. That is independence, empathy, and care for our world.”

Sunny snuggled close, but stayed on his own two feet. “I learned that feelings help me,” he said. “When I feel worried or scared, I can breathe. When I feel caring, I can help. When I feel proud, I can smile and keep going.”

Mama hugged him gently. “That is a wonderful lesson.”

Sunny looked at the glowing sky and the tidy backyard. The beetle waved its antennae. The bird chirped a soft, sweet note. Sunny’s heart felt calm and bright.

“I am Sunny,” he said, “and I can feel my feelings, help friends, and take care of my home.”

He smiled a big, joyful smile and gave a happy hop.

The End.
