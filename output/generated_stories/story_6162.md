# Benny Beaver’s Big Table

Benny the busy beaver bustled into the dining room. His little paws pat-pat-patted on the wooden floor. He sniffed the air. It smelled like warm pancakes and sweet berries. The room was bright and cozy, with sunshine on the chairs and a big round table in the middle.

“Today,” Benny said, puffing his chest, “I will build the best breakfast table ever!”

He had a bundle of sticks, soft napkins, and a shiny spoon. He tapped the table with the spoon. Ting, ting! It sounded happy.

Benny loved to build, and he built fast. He stacked napkins like little logs. He lined up cups like tiny towers. He made a centerpiece with flowers and pine cones. He even made place mats from woven twigs. Swish, swish went the twigs. Benny smiled. He liked the neat lines and straight rows.

Just then, the door creaked. “Hello?” said a gentle voice.

It was Dot Duck, waddling in, her feet going slosh-slosh even on the dry floor. She wore a blue scarf with silver dots. Dot loved dots best.

“What a pretty table,” Dot quacked. “May I help?”

Benny nodded. “Yes, please. Put napkins here. Straight. Like this.” He placed one perfectly in front of a chair. “All the same.”

Dot tilted her head. “I like them curvy,” she said. She folded her napkin into a wavy shape and set it down. “It looks like a splash!”

Benny’s whiskers twitched. “But I like straight,” he said. “Straight is best.”

Before Dot could answer, someone else peeked in. “Yoo-hoo!” sang Mimi Mouse. She slid a colorful paper crown onto her head. “I brought sparkle stickers.” Her tiny feet made tip-tap sounds.

“Stickers?” said Benny. “On the table?”

“On the cups!” squeaked Mimi. She stuck a star on one cup and a moon on another. “Each cup can be special.”

Benny took a deep breath. His heart thumped. He liked things the same. He liked things quiet and neat. Now the cups looked different. One shiny star. One sleepy moon. His tail gave a little thump on the floor. Thump.

“Different can be good,” Dot said softly, seeing Benny’s face. “Different is like a song with many notes.”

Benny looked at the cups. He listened to the dining room. He heard the clock tick-tick. He smelled the syrup, sweet and warm. He felt the soft napkins and the cool spoon. He thought about songs and notes and splashes and stars.

He looked at his friends. Mimi’s eyes sparkled. Dot’s scarf shone.

“Maybe,” Benny said slowly, “we can make a table that is straight and splashy and sparkly.”

Mimi bounced. “We can share ideas! We can take turns. That’s fair.”

Dot nodded. “We can talk and plan. That’s how we can be a team.”

Benny’s whiskers relaxed. “Okay. Let’s make a plan.”

They sat around the big round table. Benny drew a little map with his spoon in a tiny puddle of syrup. “Here,” he said, “we put straight napkins.”

Dot smiled. “Here,” she said, pointing to the centers, “we add wavy flowers.”

Mimi clapped. “And here,” she squeaked, placing stickers gently, “we give each cup a different sticker. A sun, a cloud, a heart, a leaf!”

They all nodded. It felt fair. It felt kind. It felt like everyone was heard.

They worked together. Benny lined up napkins so they were neat. Dot fluffed the flowers until they danced. Mimi stuck one sticker on each cup. Not too many, not too few.

“Listen,” said Dot. The flowers rustled. “Shh, shh.” The cups clinked. “Clink.” The chairs bumped softly. “Bump.” It sounded like a friendly rhythm.

Benny giggled. “Our table sings!”

Soon, more friends came in. Gus Goat came with crunchy carrots. Poppy Puppy brought jam that smelled like strawberries. Each friend looked at the table and smiled.

Gus said, “I like the straight lines. They make me feel calm.”

Poppy wagged her tail. “I like the stickers. They make me feel happy.”

Dot dipped her head. “I like the waves. They make me feel free.”

Benny felt warm inside. He had learned something big. Different likes can fit together. Different ways can make a new way.

They all sat down. Mimi poured milk. Swish. Dot passed pancakes. Flop. Benny drizzled syrup. Drip-drip. They ate and giggled. They talked and listened.

“Thank you,” Benny said to his friends. “I wanted everything the same. But now I see. Different makes our table better. We shared. We took turns. We made a plan.”

Mimi licked a bit of jam from her whiskers. “We used our imaginations, too!”

Dot quacked a happy quack. “We celebrated everyone.”

Benny’s tail thumped, but now it was a happy thump. “Next time,” he said, “let’s build a lunch table with shapes! Squares, circles, triangles!”

“Hooraaaay!” everyone cheered.

The dining room glowed with sunshine and smiles. The table was not just neat. It was neat, and splashy, and sparkly. It was theirs, made together.

And the big lesson sat at the table, too: We are all different, and that is something to celebrate. When we talk kindly, share ideas, and make a plan, we can build something wonderful together.

The End.
