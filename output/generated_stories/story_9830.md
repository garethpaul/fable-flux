# Stretch, Sip, and Smile, Koala Kai

Koala Kai was a calm koala with soft gray fur and gentle eyes. He loved the creative art room. The room smelled like fresh paper and crayons. Sunlight danced on the floor. Paints glowed like little rainbows. Brushes stood in cups like tall grass.

“Today I will make a big sun,” Kai whispered. He picked up a round, yellow crayon. “Hello, Sun,” he said kindly.

Kai sat on a low stool. He drew big circles and tiny dots. He glued orange triangles for sun rays. He hummed a happy tune. But after a while, his shoulders felt tight. His neck felt stiff. His tummy made a little gurgle.

“Hmm,” said Kai. “My body feels tired.”

His friend, Mia the mouse, peeked in from behind a stack of colored paper. “Hi, Kai!” she squeaked. “Your sun is bright!”

“Thank you,” said Kai softly. “But my back feels crunchy like a crumpled paper ball.”

Mia wiggled her whiskers. “Maybe you need a wiggle break!”

Just then, Teacher Luz clapped her hands gently. She wore a paint-splashed apron and a warm smile. “Artists, it’s time for a wellness pause,” she said. “Healthy bodies help bright ideas!”

Kai blinked. “A wellness pause?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Teacher Luz. “We take care of our bodies while we make art. We move, we sip water, and we breathe.”

Kai looked at his sun picture. He wanted to keep drawing. He also wanted his back to feel better. He felt stuck like glue.

Mia gave his paw a tiny pat. “Let’s try together,” she said.

Teacher Luz showed them a soft mat. “First, we stretch like gentle paint strokes,” she said.

Kai stood up slowly. The mat felt warm under his feet. He lifted his arms high. He reached to the sky as if he were a tall, tall tree.

“Reach up,” said Teacher Luz. “Say hello to the ceiling.”

“Hello, ceiling,” Kai giggled. He felt his sides open like curtains. The tight feeling started to melt.

“Now we twist,” said Teacher Luz. “Twist left like a curly ribbon. Twist right like a swirly brush.”

Kai twisted left. He could smell the lemony soap on the sink. He twisted right. He heard the soft swish of paper. His body felt awake, like a fresh page.

Mia hopped beside him. “Hop-hop,” she said. “Little hops keep us bouncy!”

Teacher Luz nodded. “Let’s try star jumps. Arms out, legs out. Be a bright star!”

Kai tried a slow star. “Shine,” he whispered. He did three gentle jumps. His heart thumped a happy beat. His cheeks felt warm.

“Now let’s breathe,” said Teacher Luz. “Smell the flowers.”

Kai breathed in through his nose. He smelled crayons and a hint of orange paint.

“Blow the bubbles,” said Teacher Luz.

Kai breathed out softly. He pretended to blow rainbow bubbles. “Pop, pop,” he said in a tiny voice. He felt calm and cozy.

“Time to sip water,” said Teacher Luz. She pointed to the water station. Tiny cups waited like shiny thimbles.

Kai took a small drink. The water felt cool and tingly. “Ahh,” he sighed. His tummy gurgled again, but now it sounded like a happy purr.

Mia smiled. “How do you feel, Kai?”

Kai wiggled his fingers. “Loose like wet paint,” he said. “And calm like a cloud.”

Teacher Luz nodded. “Healthy habits help our bodies and our art. When we move, sip, and breathe, we feel strong and focused.”

Kai looked at his sun again. He picked up an orange marker. His paw moved smoothly. He added soft lines and gentle dots. He hummed a brave little hum.

But soon he felt a tiny pinch in his neck. “Oh,” he said. “Pinch again.”

Mia squeaked, “Mini break?”

Kai smiled. “Yes. A mini break.” He rolled his shoulders, took two flower sniffs, and blew two bubble breaths. He did one small star jump.

The pinch faded away. Kai felt proud. “I can help my body,” he said. “I can choose healthy ways.”

When the picture was done, the sun looked warm and bright. It seemed to smile at the whole room.

Teacher Luz clapped softly. “Beautiful work, Kai. You cared for your art and your body.”

Kai held up his picture. “Thank you, Teacher. Thank you, Mia,” he said. “Today I learned a new art trick.”

“What trick?” asked Mia.

Kai grinned. “Stretch, sip, and smile. Move a little. Breathe a little. Then make bright art.”

Mia nodded. “That’s a clever trick for every day!”

Kai hung his sun on the drying line. The yellow paper fluttered like a happy flag. He felt peaceful in his chest and light in his legs.

He took one last sip of water. He gave the room a gentle hug with his eyes. The creative art room felt safe and shiny. Kai whispered, “Healthy habits help me shine.”

The End.
