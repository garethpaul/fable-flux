# Sunny-Day Cleanup and the Little Bird Bath

In a sunny backyard, a friendly bear cub named Bobo woke up from a cozy nap. The grass was warm. The air smelled like sweet flowers and fresh dirt. Birds sang, “Tweet, tweet!” and a soft breeze tickled Bobo’s ears.

Bobo stretched. “Good morning, sun!” he said. He loved his backyard. It had tall sunflowers, smooth pebble paths, and a shady tree with a swing.

Bobo picked up his little red pail and wooden spoon. “Today I will build something,” he said. His tummy felt fluttery with happy excitement. He wanted to make a water splash place for birds.

As Bobo walked to the pebble path, he saw something that made his smile fade. “Oh no,” he whispered. A crinkly bag blew by his paws. A cup lid sat in the grass. A shiny wrapper was stuck under a flower leaf.

Bobo’s ears drooped. “The yard looks messy. The plants might be sad,” he said. He touched a sunflower stem. It felt sticky where the wrapper had touched. “I feel worried,” Bobo said softly. “This is not good for the earth.”

He took a deep breath. The air smelled a little like plastic now. Bobo didn’t like that smell. He put his paw on his chest. “I can help,” he told himself. “I can use my thinking.”

Bobo looked around carefully. “What belongs with nature? Leaves, sticks, and pebbles,” he said. “What does not belong? Bags, lids, and wrappers.” He made two neat piles. He hummed a little tune to feel calm:

“Pick it up,
Sort it right,
Make the backyard
Clean and bright.”

A blue jay hopped nearby. “Peep?”

“It’s okay,” said Bobo. “I am cleaning. I will make it safe for you.” The blue jay tilted its head and watched.

Bobo found his little baskets. One had a green leaf picture. One had a blue bottle picture. One had a brown apple core picture. “Green basket for paper and clean cardboard. Blue basket for bottles and cans. Brown bin for food scraps,” Bobo said. He pointed with his paw. “Logical thinking helps me choose.”

He picked up the crinkly bag. “Is this clean paper?” He shook his head. “No. It is soft plastic. It can’t go in the green basket.” He put it in a trash bag instead. He found a clean paper cup. “This can go in the green basket.” He found an apple core under the swing. “Brown bin,” he said, plop!

Bobo felt proud. His heart felt warm, like sunshine. “I am caring for my backyard,” he said.

As he worked, he smelled the flowers again. He heard bees buzz-buzz in a happy way. He felt the cool dirt on his paws. The backyard began to shine.

Then Bobo saw a small puddle by the pebbles. “Splash, splash,” he tapped it with his spoon. A tiny bird fluttered down. “Chirp!” It hopped near the water and then stepped back. The puddle was muddy.

“I can build a clean bird bath,” said Bobo. “Building time!”

He looked at what he had. A shallow clay bowl. Flat stones. A pitcher of water. He made a plan:

- Step one: Find a level spot.
- Step two: Place flat stones so birds don’t slip.
- Step three: Fill with fresh water.
- Step four: Add a little shade with a leaf.

Bobo patted the dirt flat with his paws. He placed the bowl. He set smooth stones gently inside. Plink, plonk. He poured water slowly. Swish. He tucked a big leaf on one side to make a tiny shade. The water sparkled in the sun.

The tiny bird hopped close. “Chirp?”

“It’s safe,” Bobo said softly. “It’s for you.”

The bird dipped its feet. Splash! It fluffed its feathers. Bobo giggled. “It works!”

Just then, a breeze pushed another wrapper across the yard. Bobo felt a pinch in his tummy. “I feel a little mad,” he said. He took a slow breath in and a slow breath out. “I can choose kind actions.” He picked up the wrapper and put it where it belonged.

Bobo made a small sign with a stick and a leaf. He drew a big heart and a tiny tree with his spoon. “Please keep our yard clean,” he said, placing the sign near the gate. “We share this space with birds and bees.”

The blue jay flew to the shady tree and sang loudly. The tiny bird splashed again. The sun felt warm on Bobo’s fur. The backyard smelled sweet and clean.

Bobo clapped his paws. “Today I cleaned up, I sorted right, and I built a bird bath. I used my feelings to make kind choices. I used my brain to make a plan. I cared for the earth.”

He took one last look at the neat baskets and the sparkling water. “Little actions can help a lot,” he said. “I will care for my backyard every day.”

Bobo swung on the swing, back and forth, back and forth. He felt calm, happy, and proud.

The End.
