# Nutkin's Busy Day

Nutkin the squirrel loved his cozy study corner. Soft cushions sat by the window where golden sunlight streamed in. Books lined the wooden shelves, and his favorite acorn snacks waited in a small blue bowl.

"I have so much to do today!" Nutkin chattered excitedly. He wanted to read three books, practice writing his letters, and organize his acorn collection.

Nutkin grabbed the first book and started reading. His fluffy tail twitched with excitement as he turned each page. When he finished, he immediately picked up another book.

"Just one more chapter," he said to himself. His eyes felt a little tired, but he kept reading.

Hours passed. Nutkin's tummy started to rumble loudly. "GRUMBLE, GRUMBLE!" But he ignored it and reached for his third book.

His friend Chippy the chipmunk peeked into the study corner. "Nutkin, want to play outside? The sun feels so warm and nice!"

"Not now, Chippy," Nutkin said without looking up. "I'm too busy reading."

Chippy's ears drooped. "Okay," she said sadly and walked away.

Nutkin kept reading and reading. His eyes began to hurt. His back felt stiff from sitting so long. His tummy rumbled even louder. "GRUMBLE, RUMBLE, GROWL!"

When Nutkin finally looked up, the sun was setting. Orange and purple colors painted the sky. He felt dizzy and weak.

"Oh no," Nutkin whispered. He had forgotten to eat lunch. He had forgotten to drink water. He had forgotten to stretch and move around. Worst of all, he had hurt Chippy's feelings.

Nutkin's paws shook as he tried to stand up. "I don't feel good at all," he said. His body felt tired and achy.

Just then, Chippy returned with a glass of cool water and some fresh berries. "I brought you these," she said kindly. "You look like you need them."

"Chippy, I'm so sorry," Nutkin said, his voice wobbly. "I was mean to you. I forgot to take care of myself too."

Chippy smiled warmly. "It's okay, Nutkin. Sometimes we all forget important things."

Nutkin drank the cool water. It felt refreshing in his dry throat. The sweet berries made his tummy feel better. "Thank you for being such a good friend," he said.

"What did you learn today?" Chippy asked gently.

Nutkin thought carefully. "I learned that my body needs food and water to feel strong. I need to move around and rest my eyes. And I should never be mean to my friends, even when I'm busy."

"Those are very wise lessons," Chippy nodded.

From that day on, Nutkin made a special plan. Every hour, he would stop reading to drink water and eat a healthy snack. He would stretch his arms and legs and look out the window to rest his eyes. And he always made time to play with Chippy.

"Taking care of myself helps me learn better," Nutkin discovered. "And being kind to friends makes everything more fun!"

Now Nutkin's study corner was filled with laughter and healthy habits. He still loved to read and learn, but he did it in a way that kept his body happy and his friendships strong.

The End.
