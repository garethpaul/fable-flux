# Roo's Rhythm Jumps

Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and big, bouncy feet. Today, Roo hopped into the musical practice room at the community center. The room smelled like fresh wood and clean floor. Sunlight slid across shiny drums and sleepy violins. Soft dust sparkled in the air. It was warm and friendly.

“Boing, boing!” sang Roo as he bounced. His tail swished like a soft broom. “I love to move!”

Ms. Melody, the music helper, clapped her hands. “Hello, Roo! Today we practice gentle songs,” she said in a sweet voice. “We can make soft sounds with our bodies. Tap, clap, and breathe.”

Roo’s ears perked up. “I can jump!” he said. “Jumping is my favorite.”

A tiny turtle named Tilly stretched her neck. “I can’t jump,” she whispered.

A small bird named Pip fluttered to a stand. “I can flap, but not jump,” chirped Pip.

Roo blinked. He felt a small squeeze in his tummy. “Oh,” he said. “That’s okay. We can still move together!”

Ms. Melody smiled. “You’re a leader when you help friends move in their own way,” she said. “Roo, will you help us make a movement song?”

Roo took a deep breath. He felt proud and a little shy. “I can try,” he said. His voice was soft like a feather.

Dum-dum on the drum. Ding on a triangle. The room made friendly sounds.

Roo hopped to the front. “Friends,” he said, “let’s make a move song and take turns. We will choose moves that help our bodies get strong. Exercise helps our hearts and smiles!”

“Yay!” chirped Pip. Tilly nodded slowly, eyes bright.

Roo made a decision. “First, we warm up,” he said. “Breathe in, like smelling a flower.” Everyone sniffed. “Breathe out, like blowing a bubble.” Everyone puffed. The air felt cool on their tongues. Roo’s chest felt calm.

“Next,” said Roo, “tap-tap shoulders.” Tap. Tap. It felt like little raindrops.

“Now clap three times,” Roo said. “One, two, three!” Clap clap clap! The sound echoed happy and round.

Roo looked at Tilly and Pip. “What moves do you like?”

Tilly raised a gentle foot. “I like slow stretches,” she said. She reached her neck long. “Stretch up like the sun.” Everyone stretched. Muscles felt long and warm.

Pip flapped tiny wings. “I like wiggles!” he chirped. “Wiggle fingers, wiggle toes.” Everyone giggled and wiggled. The room shook with little laughs.

Roo’s tail thumped a beat. Thump, thump. “Let’s try jumps,” he said. “Little jumps for little feet, big jumps for big feet. Or you can flap or stretch instead.”

Pip flapped high. Tilly did a small, slow bounce. Roo bounced big and light. “Boing, boing, boing!” His fur brushed the air. His feet thudded soft on the floor. It sounded like a friendly drum.

Ms. Melody watched with happy eyes. “Great leadership, Roo. You made choices so everyone can move.”

Roo nodded. He felt warm and brave. “Moving is for every body,” he said. “We are different. That is good.”

The door opened. More friends peeked in: a mouse with quick whiskers, a shy snail, and two children from down the hall. “Can we join?” asked the children.

Roo stood tall. “Yes! This room is for all,” he said. “Let’s take care of our space. We share the floor, and we listen.”

“That is civic responsibility,” Ms. Melody said. “We care for our place and each other.”

Roo pointed to tape lines on the floor. “We each pick a spot,” he said. “No bumping. No pushing. If someone needs space, we give it.”

The mouse squeaked, “I can scurry steps!” The snail bubbled, “I can slide slow.”

Roo smiled. “Perfect. We make a movement parade.”

He led the group with a gentle chant:
“Smell the flower,
blow the bubble.
Stretch up tall,
shake out trouble.
Tap-tap shoulders,
clap one, two, three.
Move how you can,
strong as can be!”

Feet pattered. Shells slid. Wings fluttered. Hands clapped. The drums hummed like a purring cat. The triangle went ding-ding like a tiny star. The floor felt smooth under Roo’s paws. The room was full of kind energy.

Then a drum rolled too loud. BOOM! The snail flinched. Pip tucked his wings. Tilly pulled into her shell.

Roo paused and put up a paw. “Stop,” he said softly. Everyone stopped. “We can choose a sound that feels safe,” he said. “Big boom or soft boom?”

“Soft, please,” said Tilly.

Roo nodded. “Good decision,” he said. “Soft boom keeps friends comfy.” He tapped the drum with two fingers. Boom…boom…like a heartbeat. The room calmed. Smiles returned.

They finished with a cool-down. “Hug your body,” Roo said. “Say, ‘Thank you, body.’” Everyone hugged. The room smelled like warm wood and happiness.

Ms. Melody clapped once. “Roo, you were a kind leader,” she said. “You made choices, you helped friends, and you kept the room safe and clean.”

Roo’s cheeks felt sunny. “We moved together,” he said. “Exercise made us strong and happy. I will lead again tomorrow!”

Everyone cheered. “We will move with you!”

Roo bounced one last gentle boing. “Move your way,” he said. “Every day is a good day to move.”

The End.
