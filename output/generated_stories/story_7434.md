# Bubbles in the Study Corner

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to learn. Today, Bubbles visited a quiet study corner. The study corner was cozy and bright. There was a soft blue rug like the sea, a round table, and shelves with books that smelled like fresh paper. Sunlight made warm stripes on the floor. A little fan hummed, “whirr, whirr,” and pencils clicked, “tap, tap.”

Bubbles splashed his tail in a small water tub made just for him. “I’m ready to study!” he chirped. He wore a tiny backpack with star stickers from friends around the world—stars from Kenya, Japan, Brazil, and India. They made Bubbles feel happy and included.

Friends came to the study corner, too. Mina wore a bright scarf with tiny flowers. Leo had curly hair and a big smile. Noor had shiny glasses. Mateo brought a drum from his family’s country. “Hi, Bubbles!” they said.

“Hi!” Bubbles squeaked. “Let’s learn shapes and sounds!”

Ms. Sunny, the teacher, clapped her hands softly. “Today we will build a puzzle together,” she said. “We will take turns. We will use calm words. We will help each other.”

Bubbles felt excited. He loved puzzles. The pieces were smooth and colorful. One looked like a yellow sun. Another was a blue splash like the ocean. He could almost taste the salty air when he breathed in.

Mina placed the first piece. “My turn,” she whispered.

Leo placed the next piece. “Click!”

Bubbles held the blue splash piece close. He waited. He wanted to go fast. He wanted his piece to fit now. His tummy felt tight like a knot. His fins twitched. “When is my turn?” he asked.

“Soon,” said Ms. Sunny. “We are sharing.”

Noor placed a green leaf piece. “Click!”

Bubbles’ face felt hot. His cheeks puffed. “I want my turn now!” he shouted, and a little splash jumped from the tub.

The room grew quiet. The fan still went “whirr,” but it sounded far away. Bubbles’ heart thumped, “thump, thump.” He felt big feelings. He felt angry.

Ms. Sunny knelt by Bubbles and spoke gently. “Breathe with me, Bubbles. Smell the warm cocoa.” She breathed in slow. “Now blow the cool wind.” She breathed out slow.

Bubbles tried. In… out. In… out. The angry heat cooled a little. “I still want my turn,” he said, softer now.

“It’s okay to feel mad,” Ms. Sunny said. “What can we do with mad feelings?”

Bubbles thought. “We can use calm words. We can wait and count.” His voice was small.

“Good,” Ms. Sunny smiled. “Let’s try.”

Bubbles put his fin on his chest. He counted, “One… two… three…” He squeezed a soft squishy ball. Squeeze, squeeze. He hummed a bubble song from his ocean home, “Boo-boo-ba-bloop.” The tune felt like a hug.

Mina looked up. “Bubbles, I hear your song. It is pretty.”

“Thank you,” Bubbles said. “It’s from my family.”

Mateo tapped his drum once, soft like a heartbeat. “We can take turns faster,” he said. “Short turns for everyone.”

“Noor added, “We can say, ‘May I have a turn, please?’”

Bubbles nodded. He took a slow breath. “May I have a turn, please?”

“Yes,” Leo said. “After me, it’s you.”

Leo placed a triangle. “Click!”

“Your turn, Bubbles,” Ms. Sunny said.

Bubbles smiled. The angry knot had loosened. He lined up his blue splash piece. He checked the edges. He pressed it down gently. “Click!” It fit!

Everyone clapped. “Yay, Bubbles!”

Bubbles felt proud, like a sunbeam inside. “Thank you for waiting with me,” he said. “I used calm breaths. I used kind words.”

They kept building. Noor showed a piece with swirly lines. “In my home, we draw patterns like this,” she said.

“It looks like waves,” Bubbles said. “In my home, we sing bubble songs.”

Mina held up a red piece shaped like a little door. “In my family, we take off our shoes at the door. It feels cozy.”

Mateo tapped his drum again, a tiny rhythm. “In my family, we dance when we finish a puzzle.”

Bubbles listened. He liked learning about each friend. All their ways made the study corner bright and friendly. He felt included, and he made sure everyone felt included too. When a smaller child peeked in, Bubbles waved his fin. “Want to help?” he asked.

“Yes,” the child said, smiling shyly.

They finished the puzzle together. The last piece was a golden star. Bubbles looked at his friends. “Who wants to place it?” he asked.

The little child said, “You can.”

Bubbles shook his head. “Let’s do it together.” They all touched the star and pressed. “Click!” The puzzle shone in the sunlight.

Ms. Sunny clapped. “You showed courage, Bubbles, to face your big feelings. You managed your anger with calm breaths and kind words. You included everyone. You helped others.”

Bubbles felt calm and brave. He splashed his tail softly. “When I feel mad,” he said, “I can breathe in cocoa and blow out wind. I can count. I can use gentle words. I can wait and share.”

Everyone cheered. Mateo tapped a happy beat. Mina twirled her scarf. Noor’s glasses sparkled. The fan hummed, the pencils tapped, and the study corner felt safe and warm.

Bubbles giggled. “Learning together is fun,” he said. “Different homes, one big team!”

The End.
