# Bolt the Robot Dog Solves the Big Problem

In the cozy home office, Bolt the robot dog's blue lights blinked happily. His shiny silver body gleamed in the morning sunshine. "Beep-boop! Good morning!" he chirped in his friendly robot voice.

Daddy sat at his big desk, typing on his computer. "Click-clack, click-clack" went the keyboard. But something was wrong. Daddy's face looked worried and his shoulders were tight.

"Oh no," Daddy sighed loudly. "The Johnson family and the Smith family are both upset about the playground schedule. They both want the same time slot!"

Bolt's antenna ears perked up. His caring circuits started buzzing. "Woof-beep! What's wrong, Daddy?" Bolt asked, rolling closer on his smooth wheels.

Daddy looked down at his helpful robot friend. "Two families are arguing about playground time, Bolt. They're both being mean to each other. I don't know how to help them be fair."

Bolt's screen-face showed a thinking emoji. His computer brain whirred softly. "Hmm-beep! When friends fight, we need to listen to everyone," Bolt said wisely.

"You're right, buddy," Daddy said, patting Bolt's metal head. "But how can I make it fair for both families?"

Bolt's tail-antenna wagged excitedly. "Beep-boop! Let's call them together! When everyone talks nicely, we can find good solutions!"

Daddy smiled for the first time that morning. He picked up his phone and called both families. "Hello, this is about the playground schedule. Could we all talk together?"

Soon, both families were on a video call. Bolt could see their faces on the computer screen. Mrs. Johnson looked frustrated. Mr. Smith seemed angry. Their children looked sad behind them.

"Woof-beep! Hello everyone!" Bolt said cheerfully, appearing on the screen. "I'm Bolt! I love helping friends solve problems!"

The children's faces brightened when they saw the friendly robot dog. "A robot dog!" little Timmy Johnson gasped.

"Now," Daddy said gently, "let's listen to everyone. Mrs. Johnson, can you tell us what you need?"

Mrs. Johnson took a deep breath. "We need playground time after school because that's when Timmy gets his energy out."

"And Mr. Smith?" Daddy asked kindly.

Mr. Smith's voice was calmer now. "Sarah needs playground time then too, for the same reason."

Bolt's screen showed a lightbulb. "Beep-boop! What if both families play together? Friends can share the big playground!"

The children on screen started bouncing excitedly. "Yes! We can play together!" Sarah Smith called out.

Mrs. Johnson smiled. "That's actually a wonderful idea. The kids could be friends!"

Mr. Smith nodded. "And we parents could take turns watching them. That would help everyone!"

"Woof-beep! Sharing makes everyone happy!" Bolt said, doing a little spin. "When we listen and work together, problems become solutions!"

Both families agreed to try sharing the playground time. They even planned a playdate for the weekend.

After the call ended, Daddy hugged Bolt tight. "You're amazing, buddy. You helped everyone feel heard and find a fair solution."

Bolt's lights twinkled with joy. "Beep-boop! When people are upset, we listen with our hearts. Then we find ways to be fair to everyone!"

Daddy leaned back in his chair, feeling much better. "You know what, Bolt? All this problem-solving made me tired. I think I need a break."

"Woof-beep! Self-care is important too!" Bolt said. "Let's get some fresh air and recharge our batteries!"

They walked outside together, feeling proud that they had helped two families become friends.

The End.
