# Polly's Special Space

Polly the parrot loved her cozy treehouse. She had painted the wooden walls with bright rainbow colors. Her art supplies sat neatly on small shelves. Soft cushions made perfect spots for drawing and thinking.

"This is my special place," Polly chirped happily. She spread her colorful feathers and picked up her favorite paintbrush. The morning sun warmed her back through the treehouse window.

Soon, Polly heard rustling leaves outside. Squirrel bounced through the window without asking.

"Wow, what a cool place!" Squirrel chattered loudly. He jumped on Polly's cushions with his muddy paws. "Can I play here?"

Before Polly could answer, Rabbit hopped up the rope ladder. "This looks fun!" she said, grabbing Polly's paintbrush. Blue paint dripped everywhere.

Polly's feathers ruffled with worry. "Wait, please—" she started to say.

But then Raccoon climbed in too. "Party time!" he called out. He knocked over Polly's neat supply box. Crayons rolled across the floor.

Polly felt her heart beating fast. Her beautiful, peaceful treehouse was becoming messy and loud. She wanted to screech, but instead she took a deep breath.

"Stop!" Polly said in a calm but firm voice. "This is my special space."

The three friends froze. They looked at Polly with surprise.

"I like having friends visit," Polly explained gently. "But I need you to ask before coming in. And please be careful with my things."

Squirrel's ears drooped. "Oh! I'm sorry, Polly. I didn't ask first."

"I made a mess with your paint," Rabbit said sadly. "That wasn't nice."

Raccoon looked at the scattered crayons. "I was too excited. I should have been more careful."

Polly's feathers smoothed down. She felt much calmer now. "It's okay. Everyone makes mistakes. But my treehouse is special to me. I need rules to keep it safe and peaceful."

"What rules?" asked Squirrel politely.

Polly smiled. "Always ask before coming in. Clean your paws first. Use art supplies gently. And speak in quiet voices."

"Those are good rules," said Rabbit. "Can we help clean up?"

"That would be wonderful," Polly chirped.

Working together, they picked up every crayon. Rabbit carefully cleaned the spilled paint. Squirrel wiped his muddy paw prints from the cushions. Raccoon organized the art supplies back on the shelf.

"Now," said Polly, "would you like to paint together? We can make a friendship picture."

"Yes, please!" said all three friends.

They sat quietly on the clean cushions. Polly shared her brushes and paints. Each friend painted their favorite thing. Squirrel painted acorns. Rabbit painted carrots. Raccoon painted shiny rocks. Polly painted all her friends together.

"This is much more fun," whispered Rabbit.

"And peaceful too," added Squirrel softly.

The warm afternoon sun made their painting glow. Polly felt happy and calm in her special space. Her friends had learned to respect her boundaries. And together, they had created something beautiful.

"Thank you for teaching us about your rules," said Raccoon. "Now we know how to be good guests."

Polly's colorful feathers sparkled in the sunlight. "And thank you for helping me keep my treehouse special. Good friends respect each other's spaces."

From that day on, Polly's friends always asked before visiting. They cleaned their paws and spoke softly. The treehouse stayed peaceful and beautiful. And their friendship grew stronger because everyone felt respected and safe.

The End.
