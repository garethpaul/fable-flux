# Beatrix Beaver’s Big Bedtime Boost

In a cozy bedroom with soft lamplight and warm blankets, a busy beaver named Beatrix bounced on the rug. Her tail thumped gently—thump, thump—like a tiny drum. Pillows smelled like fresh cotton. Her quilt felt fuzzy and snug. The window hummed with a quiet nighttime breeze.

Beatrix had had a big day, but her heart felt small. “I wish I could do everything perfectly,” she whispered, chewing her lip. “But today I dropped my blocks. I fumbled my buttons. I forgot a word in my song.” Her whiskers drooped.

Mama Beaver peeked in. “I hear a thump-thump-tail,” she said with a smile. “What’s on your mind, my busy Beatrix?”

“I don’t feel big or brave,” Beatrix said. “I feel wobbly.”

Mama sat beside her on the quilt. “Your feelings are welcome here,” she said softly. “Let’s give them a hug.” They pressed their cheeks together—warm, whiskery, safe.

Just then, a gentle knock came from the door. “May we come in?” asked a small voice.

Beatrix’s friends had arrived for a bedtime stretch-and-story. Nia the squirrel wore cozy socks. Theo the turtle had a shiny shell blanket. Mei the mouse carried a tiny lantern that glowed buttery yellow. They all looked different, and they all smiled the same friendly smile.

“Hi, Beatrix!” chirped Nia. “We came to move, breathe, and feel proud.”

“I don’t know if I can feel proud,” Beatrix said, twisting her paws.

Theo tucked beside her. “We can try together,” he said, slow and kind. “Slow is okay.”

Mama spread out a soft mat on the floor. “Let’s play ‘I Can.’ It’s a moving game for cozy rooms. We use our bodies and our brave words.” She turned the lamp lower. The room felt calm and glowy.

“First,” Mama said, “we shake out the day.” She shook her hands—shake-shake-shake. Beatrix copied. Her tail thumped softly. Everyone giggled when it made a drumbeat on the mat.

“I can shake!” Beatrix said, a tiny smile peeking out.

“Next,” Mama said, “we take a big breath in, like smelling warm toast.” They sniffed—mmmm. “And blow it out like a birthday wish.” Whooooo.

Beatrix felt her chest rise and fall. “I can breathe,” she whispered.

Nia twirled slowly like a leaf. “I can twirl.”

Mei tiptoed, lantern glowing. “I can tiptoe.”

Theo stretched his neck, long and gentle. “I can stretch.”

Beatrix tried each move. Her feet felt tingly on the rug. The air smelled sweet, like clean sheets. She heard the lantern’s tiny glass clink and the hush of the night. Her body felt warm and helpful.

Mama clapped softly. “Wonderful. Now, we play the ‘Proud Round.’ We each say one thing we like about ourselves.”

Nia patted her chest. “I like that I am quick and kind.”

Mei squeaked, “I like that I am small and brave.”

Theo smiled. “I like that I am careful and strong.”

All eyes turned to Beatrix. Her tummy fluttered. She looked at her sturdy paws and her wide, wonderful tail. “I… I like that I keep trying,” she said. “Even when I drop blocks. Even when I fumble buttons. I try again.”

Her words felt like warm cocoa. Her heart stretched bigger.

Mama nodded. “Trying again is brave. That is beaver-strong.”

Beatrix stood tall on the mat. “I can do three strong steps,” she said. “Step. Step. Step.” Her tail went thump-thump-thump. Everyone cheered.

“Let’s make a bedtime bridge,” said Nia. “We’ll build it together!” They didn’t need wood. They used their bodies and their ideas.

Theo lay down like a steady stone. Mei knelt like a tiny pillar. Nia held out her hands like a railing. Beatrix placed a pillow in the middle like a plank.

“Is it okay that I’m small?” asked Mei.

“Small is perfect,” said Beatrix. “Small fits where big can’t.”

“Is it okay that I’m slow?” asked Theo.

“Slow is perfect,” said Beatrix. “Slow keeps us safe.”

“Is it okay that I’m quick?” asked Nia.

“Quick is perfect,” said Beatrix. “Quick helps us get things done.”

They crossed their pretend bridge, one by one, with giggles and careful steps. The bed looked extra cozy now, like a soft boat in a moonlit pond.

Mama dimmed the lamp a little more. “One last thing,” she said. “Beatrix, would you like to lead the ‘I Am’ song?”

Beatrix blinked. “Me? Lead?”

“You?” Nia echoed, eyes bright.

“Please,” Mei said.

Theo nodded. “We will follow.”

Beatrix felt the room’s calm hug. She hummed a tiny tune, low and sweet.

“I am me,” she sang, patting her chest.
“I am strong,” she sang, stomping once—thump.
“I am kind,” she sang, hands on heart.
“I keep trying, all night long,” she finished, with a shy smile.

Her friends repeated each line, their voices soft and cozy. The song wrapped around them like a quilt.

Beatrix climbed into bed, quilt up to her chin. Mama kissed her forehead. “What do you know now?” Mama asked.

Beatrix thought of shaking hands, deep breaths, and brave words. She thought of quick Nia, tiny Mei, slow Theo, and how they all fit together like puzzle pieces.

“I know I’m good just the way I am,” she said. “I can move. I can try. I can lead. I can be me.”

Mama’s eyes sparkled. “That is a very big know.”

The friends waved goodnight in their different ways—quick, tiny, slow—and tiptoed out. The room smelled like quiet and clean blankets. The window breeze whispered, Good job.

Beatrix’s tail made one last happy thump. Her heart felt full and sure. She closed her eyes, warm and proud and ready for dreams.

The End.
