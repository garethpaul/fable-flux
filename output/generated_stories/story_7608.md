# Squeaky's Big Rooftop Mix-Up

High up on a red brick rooftop, a kind little mouse named Squeaky lived in a cozy corner under the chimney. Squeaky had soft gray fur and the tiniest pink nose that twitched when he was excited.

Every morning, Squeaky loved to do his exercises on the rooftop. He would stretch his little paws up to the sky, run around the chimney three times, and hop over the roof tiles. "One, two, three!" he would count as he jumped.

But today was different. When Squeaky woke up, he found a big mess on his rooftop! Someone had scattered breadcrumbs everywhere, knocked over his neat pile of acorns, and left muddy footprints all around his exercise area.

"Oh no!" squeaked Squeaky, his whiskers trembling. "Who made this mess? Now I can't do my morning exercises!"

Just then, he heard a soft "coo, coo" from behind the chimney. Out waddled Penny the pigeon, her feathers looking ruffled and her head hanging low.

"Go away!" squeaked Squeaky without thinking. "You probably made this mess!"

Penny's eyes filled with tears. "I... I..." she started to say, but Squeaky had already turned away.

All day, Squeaky felt grumpy. He couldn't exercise properly because of the mess. He couldn't find his favorite acorns because they were all mixed up. The warm sun on his fur didn't feel nice anymore.

As the sun began to set, painting the sky orange and pink, Squeaky heard quiet sniffling sounds. He peeked around the chimney and saw Penny sitting alone, looking very sad.

Squeaky's kind heart began to feel heavy. "Maybe I should talk to her," he thought. "I didn't even let her explain."

He took a deep breath and walked over to Penny. "Hi, Penny," he said gently. "I'm sorry I was mean to you this morning. I should have listened. Can you tell me what happened?"

Penny looked up with surprised, hopeful eyes. "Oh, Squeaky! Thank you for asking. A big storm came last night, and I got scared. I flew to your rooftop to hide, but the wind was so strong! It blew everything around. I tried to clean up, but I made it worse. I'm so sorry!"

Squeaky felt his anger melt away like ice cream in the sun. "Oh, Penny! I'm sorry too. I should have asked you first instead of being angry. You must have been so frightened!"

"I was," Penny cooed softly. "But I should have told you right away what happened."

Squeaky's whiskers twitched with an idea. "Let's clean up together! Then we can both exercise on the rooftop. Would you like that?"

Penny's feathers fluffed up with happiness. "Yes! I love to exercise too!"

Working together, they organized the scattered acorns into neat piles. They swept away the breadcrumbs with their wings and paws. Soon, the rooftop was even cleaner than before!

"Now for exercises!" squeaked Squeaky happily. "Let's stretch our arms... I mean, wings and paws... up high!"

They exercised together as the stars began to twinkle. Squeaky did his hops while Penny did her wing flaps. They counted together: "One, two, three!"

"This is so much more fun together," said Penny.

"It really is," agreed Squeaky. "And I learned something important today. When there's a problem, it's always better to talk about it first. Communication helps us understand each other."

From that day on, Squeaky and Penny exercised together every morning, and they always talked through any problems that came up. The rooftop became a place of friendship, filled with laughter and the sound of counting: "One, two, three!"

The End.
