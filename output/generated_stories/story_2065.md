# Fawn Finds Her Voice

In a soft, comfortable nursery, a gentle deer fawn named Fawn curled up on a round rug. The rug felt fluffy like a cloud. The walls were the color of warm milk. Sunlight slipped through the curtains and made golden stripes on the floor. A clock on the wall went tick-tock, tick-tock, like a tiny woodpecker.

Fawn loved her nursery. There were baskets of blocks, shelves with picture books, and a cozy reading chair that squeaked a little when you sat down. Fawn’s ears were velvety, and her nose twitched when she was curious. Today, she had a big plan.

“I will build a tall block tower, read my favorite book, and water my little fern before snack,” Fawn whispered. She looked at the clock. “I can do it if I take my time and use my time.”

She set three soft cards on the rug. Each card had a picture she drew herself: a tower, a book, and a watering can. “First tower, then book, then water,” she said, tapping each card.

Just then, her friends came to play. Squeaky Mouse rolled in a toy train. Chirpy Sparrow fluttered near the window. “Let’s play train all morning!” squeaked Squeaky.

Fawn’s hooves tapped the rug. She wanted to play, but she also wanted to finish her plan. Her tummy felt fluttery like a moth. She took a slow breath and listened to the clock. Tick-tock. Tick-tock.

“I want to play,” Fawn said softly. “But I have a plan. First I will build my tower. Then I can play train for a little while.”

Squeaky blinked. “Now?”

“Not yet,” Fawn said. She pointed to her cards. “First tower. Then play. I need time.”

Chirpy tilted her head. “Can we help you build?”

Fawn’s ears perked up. “Yes, please. Thank you for asking.”

They stacked smooth blocks. The blocks smelled like clean wood. Fawn placed a red block, then a blue one. “Slow and steady,” she said. Squeaky handed her a square. Chirpy balanced a tiny cube on top. The tower wobbled.

“Oh no!” squeaked Squeaky.

Fawn took another breath. “I can fix it,” she said. “I will try again.” She pressed the blocks gently. Tap, tap. “I need a flat base,” she told her friends. “And I need a calm body.”

Tick-tock. Tick-tock. The tower grew taller. Fawn felt proud and warm inside. She smiled. “We did it!”

Chirpy chirped. “Now train?”

Fawn looked at the clock and her cards. She remembered her plan. “Next is my book,” she said kindly. “I will read two pages. Then I can play train.”

Squeaky looked disappointed. “But I want to go fast.”

Fawn used her gentle voice. “I hear you. I want to go fast too. But I need to do my plan. It helps me feel good.” She patted her chest. “I am telling you what I need.”

Fawn curled into the squeaky chair and opened her favorite book. The pages smelled like paper and lemon, because the room was clean. She read aloud in a soft whisper. “Once there was a bright moon…” Chirpy and Squeaky listened. They swayed like grass in a breeze.

After two pages, Fawn closed the book. “Thank you for waiting,” she said. “Now we can play train for five minutes.” She set a sand timer on the floor and flipped it. The sand hissed down like tiny rain.

They chugged the train around the rug. “Choo-choo!” sang Chirpy. “Toot-toot!” squeaked Squeaky. Fawn laughed and clapped her hooves. The train wheels clicked on the track. Click, click, click.

When the last grain of sand dropped, Fawn pointed. “Time is up,” she said. “Last, I will water my fern.”

She picked up a small watering can. The water splashed softly, like a whispering stream. The fern’s leaves looked shiny and bright. Fawn smiled and patted the pot. “You had a drink,” she said.

Squeaky tugged her sleeve. “I’m hungry.”

“Me too,” chirped Chirpy.

Fawn nodded. “Snack time fits now.” She looked at the clock. Tick-tock. “I did my plan. I asked for what I needed. I took turns with my time.”

They sat on the rug with apple slices and tiny oat cookies. The apple smelled sweet. The cookies were warm and crunchy. Fawn felt calm and happy. The fluttery feeling was gone. In its place was a steady, cozy glow.

Squeaky nibbled and said, “You told us your plan. That helped me know what to do.”

Chirpy added, “You used your voice. You were kind and clear.”

Fawn’s tail flicked. “When I speak up about my needs, it helps everyone,” she said. “I can be gentle and strong at the same time.”

Squeaky squeaked, “Next time, I will say my plan too.”

Chirpy chirped, “And I will listen.”

Fawn tucked her cards into a small basket. She hugged her friends. The nursery felt even more comfortable now, like a safe nest.

Fawn looked at the clock one more time. Tick-tock. She whispered, “I can plan my time. I can ask for help. I can say what I need.” She took a deep, happy breath. “I am learning. I am growing.”

And the golden stripes of sun on the floor seemed to smile back.

The End.
