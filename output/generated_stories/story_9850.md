# Leo's Colorful Truth

The art room sparkled with rainbow colors. Paintbrushes stood tall in glass jars like tiny soldiers. Leo the lion cub bounced on his paws as he walked inside.

"Good morning, artists!" called Miss Rabbit cheerfully. "Today we'll paint pictures of our families!"

Leo's golden tail swished with excitement. He loved art time! The smell of fresh paint made his nose twitch happily. He picked up his favorite blue brush and dipped it in the paint.

Swish, swish! Leo painted big circles and wiggly lines. His paws moved in happy dancing motions as he worked. "This is fun!" he roared softly.

But then... SPLASH! Leo's elbow bumped the water jar. Cold water spilled everywhere! It dripped onto Emma Elephant's beautiful painting.

"Oh no!" Emma trumpeted sadly. "My purple flowers are all runny now!"

Leo's tummy felt tight and twisty. His ears drooped down. Everyone was looking at the mess. Miss Rabbit hurried over with paper towels.

"What happened here?" Miss Rabbit asked gently.

Leo looked at his wet paws. He looked at Emma's sad face. His heart beat fast like a drum. He wanted to hide under the art table.

"Maybe... maybe the water jar just fell over by itself?" Leo whispered quietly.

Emma's trunk drooped even more. "But I saw your elbow bump it, Leo."

Leo's cheeks felt hot and red. The truth felt heavy in his chest like a big rock. He took three deep breaths, just like Mama Lion taught him.

"Wait," Leo said, standing up tall on his hind legs. "I need to tell the truth."

He walked over to Emma with small, careful steps. "Emma, I'm really sorry. My elbow bumped your water jar when I was painting. I made the mess. I was scared to say it."

Emma's eyes grew wide and sparkly. "Thank you for telling me the truth, Leo. That was very brave!"

Miss Rabbit smiled her biggest smile. "Leo, honesty takes real courage. I'm proud of you for being truthful."

Leo's tummy felt much better now. The heavy feeling flew away like a butterfly. "Can I help clean up? And maybe help Emma fix her painting?"

"Of course!" Miss Rabbit clapped her paws together. "Teamwork makes everything better!"

Leo and Emma worked together, moving their brushes in gentle swirls. They cleaned up every drop of water. Then Leo helped Emma paint new purple flowers, even prettier than before.

"Your flowers look magical now!" Leo said, bouncing on his toes.

"They do! And Leo, I like that you told the truth. That makes you a really good friend," Emma said with a happy trumpet sound.

Miss Rabbit gathered all the little animals in a circle. "Leo showed us something important today. When we make mistakes, telling the truth helps us fix them together."

Leo's chest puffed out with pride. "Telling the truth felt scary at first, but then it felt really good! Like sunshine in my heart!"

From that day on, Leo always remembered his art room lesson. Every morning during their creative time, he practiced being honest about little things and big things. His friends trusted him more, and his heart felt light and happy.

When the art bell rang each day, Leo would bounce into the colorful room, ready to create and ready to be truthful, no matter what.

The End.
