# Fair Turns in the Treehouse

Sunlight peeked through green leaves, making tiny spots of light dance on the wooden floor. In a cozy treehouse high in a big oak, a friendly bear cub named Bramble wiggled his paws. He could smell pine and sweet sap. He could hear birds chirp, peep, peep, in the branches.

“Today is play day!” Bramble said. His fur felt warm from the sun. His heart felt jumpy-excited.

Bramble’s friends climbed up the rope ladder. Squeak the mouse came first, twitching her whiskers. Hoot the owlet fluffed soft feathers. River the otter patted the wood with his wet paws. They all smiled.

“Let’s play the Leaf Glide,” said River, pointing to a smooth slide made from wide leaves. “Whoosh!”

“Let’s build a lookout nest!” chirped Hoot. He tapped sticks into a tidy pile.

“Let’s draw maps!” squeaked Squeak, holding a tiny red crayon.

Bramble looked at the Leaf Glide. It looked fast and fun. He wanted the first turn. His paws tingled. “Me first!” he said. He sat at the top and whooshed down. “Wheee!” Wind brushed his ears. The slide smelled like cool leaves and morning dew.

When he climbed back up, Squeak sat at the top, ready to glide.

Bramble wanted to go again. “Me again,” he said, stepping close.

Squeak’s whiskers drooped. “But I didn’t have a turn yet.”

Hoot blinked slow. “We can take turns,” he hooted softly.

Bramble crossed his arms. “But I’m big. I can go faster. It will be quick.”

River’s tail flicked. “Fair is fair,” he said. “We all want a turn.”

Bramble huffed. Inside, a prickly feeling grew. He really wanted another whoosh. His paws felt stomp-stomp on the floor. “I don’t want to wait,” he muttered.

Hoot tilted his head. “When we share, we all feel good,” he said. “When we don’t share, someone feels small.”

Squeak’s little voice was quiet. “I feel small,” she said.

Bramble looked at Squeak’s tiny paws shaking on the slide. He looked at Hoot’s careful eyes and River’s hopeful smile. The prickly feeling changed into a heavy rock in his tummy.

“I… I’m sorry,” Bramble said. His voice was soft now. “You can go, Squeak.”

Squeak smiled. “Thank you.” She slid down, squealing, “Eeeeee!” She landed in a pile of dry leaves that rustled shhh-shhh. Her nose twitched with joy.

River went next. “Whoosh!” He splashed a little water from his fur at the bottom and giggled.

Then Hoot glided, wings tucked in, saying, “Hoo-hoo!” He landed with a gentle thump.

At last it was Bramble’s turn again. He took a deep breath. Waiting was hard. But he did it. He climbed up and whooshed with a big laugh. “Wheee!”

“Let’s try the lookout nest,” Hoot said. “We need sticks and vines.”

Bramble reached for the biggest sticks. “I can do all the big ones,” he said. He felt strong. He piled them high. But the pile kept falling. Clatter-clatter! A stick bonked his paw. “Ow,” he said, shaking it.

River touched the pile. “Maybe we can build together,” he said. “One big, one small, one bendy.”

Squeak held up a thin vine. “And use vine like a ribbon,” she said. “I can tie knots.”

Bramble breathed out slow. He wanted to do it himself. But the pile did not stay. He looked at his friends’ busy paws. He nodded. “Let’s do it together. We can take turns tying and placing.”

They made a plan. Hoot placed a big stick. Squeak tied a vine. River slid a smooth twig in the right spot. Bramble pressed it tight with his warm paw. The nest grew strong. It smelled like fresh bark and green vines. The wind hummed happy through the leaves.

Suddenly, a gust of wind woooshed through the tree. The slide wiggled. The nest shook. “Hold on!” Hoot hooted.

Bramble’s heart thumped. “We can fix it,” he said. “We can do it.” He took a deep breath. “I’ll hold the nest.”

“I’ll tie more vines,” Squeak squeaked.

“I’ll check the slide,” said River.

They worked together. Bramble’s arms felt tired. The wind pushed, but he pushed back, steady and brave. The vines tickled his nose. River’s wet paws felt cool on the leaf slide as he smoothed it flat. Hoot tucked feathers around loose twigs. Squeak tied quick, neat knots. Tug-tug. Tie-tie.

At last the wind calmed. The treehouse felt snug again. The slide was smooth. The nest was safe and round. The friends looked at one another with big smiles.

Bramble felt light as a feather. “We did it,” he said. “We were fair. We took turns. We shared jobs. We fixed things together.”

Squeak patted his paw. “You waited so nicely.”

Hoot blinked. “You were brave and kind.”

River grinned. “And that was fun!”

Bramble’s belly felt warm, like sunshine through leaves. He looked at the slide, then at his friends. “Let’s make a turn list,” he said. He drew simple marks on a flat piece of bark. “Squeak, then River, then Hoot, then me. Then we go again.”

“Fair turns!” they all cheered.

They glided. They built. They rested in the nest and watched the sky turn soft orange. They smelled the sweet evening air. They heard crickets sing, chirp-chirp.

Bramble hugged his friends gently. “Fairness makes play feel good,” he said. “When we wait, share, and help, everyone shines.”

The End.
