# Dotty’s Fair Picture Plan

Dotty was a gentle deer fawn with soft ears and a speckled back. She lived in a cozy home with a sunny home office. The home office smelled like warm paper and crayons. The window let in light like honey. The desk felt smooth under Dotty’s little hooves.

“I love this room,” Dotty said. “It is calm. It is quiet. It is my art place.”

On the desk were crayons, pencils, and a big, blank paper. A cup held paintbrushes like tiny trees. Dotty’s tail flicked with happy wiggles.

Today, Dotty had a big plan. “I will make a picture book,” she said. “I will use art and writing! I will share my feelings.”

She opened the crayons. “Snap!” went the box. She smelled the waxy, bright colors. “Mmm, red smells like berries,” she giggled. She picked a blue crayon. “Blue feels cool.”

Dotty drew a sun with a smile. She wrote one big word under it: “Hello!” Her letters were bouncy and a little wiggly.

Just then, her friend Pip, a chubby chipmunk, peeked in. Pip wore tiny glasses for reading. “Hi, Dotty!” Pip chirped. “What are you making?”

“A picture book!” said Dotty. “It will help others feel calm and well. That is wellness.”

Pip smiled. “Can I draw too?”

Dotty looked at the paper. There was still lots of space. “Yes,” she said kindly. “We can share.”

They drew together. Dotty drew a tree with gentle leaves. Pip drew a tiny nest. Their crayons made soft scratch-scratch sounds. The paper felt a little bumpy under Dotty’s hoof.

Soon, their friend Buzz the bumblebee buzzed by the window. “Bzz-zzz!” Buzz hummed. “What fun! Can I add a flower?”

“Yes, Buzz,” said Dotty. “We are making art and writing a book.”

Buzz dipped a small brush in pink paint. “Tap, tap,” went the brush. A lovely flower bloomed on the page.

Then, a new friend, Rollo the raccoon, wandered in. Rollo had bright eyes and busy paws. He saw the pretty paper. He saw the crayons. He saw the paints.

“I want to make it all super big!” Rollo said. He grabbed the biggest brush. Swoosh! He painted a giant gray cloud across the whole page.

Dotty gasped. Pip squeaked. Buzz tilted in the air.

“Oh,” Dotty said softly. “Rollo, we were all sharing. Your big cloud covered our sun and flower.”

Rollo blinked. “I wanted to help. Big is fun. I did not mean to cover it.”

Dotty felt a twist in her tummy. Her ears drooped. She took a slow breath in and a slow breath out. “I feel sad,” she said. “My art got covered. That is not fair.”

Pip hugged the edge of the paper. “We need fairness,” Pip said. “We need justice.”

Buzz buzzed gently. “Justice means we all get a turn. We all get space.”

Rollo looked at the page. He saw the sun and flower hiding under the cloud. He touched his chest. “I feel sorry,” he said. “I did not ask. I did not listen.”

Dotty nodded. “Thank you for saying sorry,” she said. “We can fix this. We can make a plan.”

She took a clean sheet and taped it next to the first page. “We can make a book with many pages,” she said. “We can write rules that are kind and fair.”

Dotty picked up a pencil. The pencil made a soft scratch. She wrote, slowly and big:

1. Ask before you paint.
2. Share space.
3. Take turns.
4. Be gentle.
5. Say sorry and fix it.

She read the words aloud. “These are our justice rules,” Dotty said. “They help everyone.”

Rollo nodded. “I will follow the rules,” he said. “May I have a corner to paint a small cloud?”

“Yes,” said Dotty. “A small cloud can bring rain for the flower.”

Pip smiled. “I will draw a rainbow after the rain.”

Buzz hummed. “I will paint tiny drops. Drip, drop, drip.”

They worked together. Rollo painted a small, soft cloud. Pip drew a bright rainbow. Buzz added tiny shiny drops. Dotty drew faces on the drops. “Plip-plop smiles!” she laughed.

They wrote little words under each picture. Dotty wrote, “Hello, Sun.” Pip wrote, “Nest rests.” Buzz wrote, “Kind rain.” Rollo wrote, “Fair cloud.” The letters were simple and sweet.

The home office felt warm and safe again. The light on the desk looked like butter. The air smelled like paper and paint. Dotty’s tummy untwisted.

“We used art to share feelings,” Dotty said. “We used writing to share rules. We used justice to be fair. We used understanding to listen.”

Rollo’s eyes shone. “I learned to ask and to take turns,” he said.

Pip patted the page. “I learned that small can be strong.”

Buzz twirled. “I learned that gentle art makes hearts calm. That is wellness.”

Dotty smiled. “I learned that when we make a plan together, our art gets better.”

They turned their pages like a real book. Flip, flip. Dotty wrote a title on the first page: “Our Fair Art Book.” She added a heart.

They looked at their book and felt proud. Dotty’s tail made happy taps on the chair. Tap, tap, tap.

“Art helps us be kind,” Dotty said. “Writing helps us remember. Justice helps us share. Understanding helps us grow.”

Everyone nodded.

They placed the book on the desk by the window. The sun smiled on it.

The End.
