# Panda Poppy Says Sorry and Makes It Right

Poppy was a patient panda with soft, fluffy fur and big, gentle eyes. Poppy lived in a comfortable nursery with bright rugs, cozy blankets, and walls painted the color of warm sunshine. The room smelled like clean soap and sweet bananas. Soft music tinkled from a little speaker, and the window let in a friendly breeze.

Today was craft day. Poppy and friends sat at a low table with crayons, safety scissors, and glitter glue. The table felt smooth under Poppy’s paws. The glue was sticky and cold, and the crayons smelled waxy and bright. Miss Maya, the kind teacher, clapped her hands. “Friends, we will make thank-you cards,” she said. “We will listen to the steps and get ready. We will practice patience, and we will say thank you.”

Poppy nodded and smiled. “I will listen,” Poppy whispered.

Miss Maya said, “Step one: Take one paper. Step two: Write a name. Step three: Add a picture. Step four: Put away the glue when you are done.”

Poppy took one paper. Poppy wrote “Grandma” with careful, round letters. Poppy drew bamboo and a heart. Poppy hummed a happy tune. Everything felt calm and cozy.

Then Poppy saw a shiny, silver sticker on Leo Lion’s spot. “Ooo!” Poppy said. “A star!” Poppy reached across the table. Poppy did not ask. Poppy did not wait. Poppy did not listen.

Poppy’s paw bumped a cup of glitter glue. The cup tipped. The glue slid like purple jelly. It splashed onto Mia Mouse’s card. “Oh no!” Mia squeaked. Her tiny whiskers trembled. Her card was sticky and wet.

Poppy’s tummy felt twisty. Poppy’s ears got warm. Leo frowned. “You didn’t listen,” he said. Mia sniffled. “My card was for Nana,” she said softly. “Now it is messy.”

Miss Maya came close. She knelt so her eyes were level with Poppy’s. Her voice was calm. “Poppy, I saw what happened. What do we say when we make a mistake?”

Poppy took a deep breath. The nursery smelled like glue and bananas. Poppy remembered being patient. Poppy remembered listening. Poppy looked at Mia. “I am sorry,” Poppy said. “I did not ask. I did not listen. I bumped the glue. I made your card messy.”

Mia looked at Poppy. Mia’s nose twitched. Miss Maya nodded. “Saying sorry is the first step,” she said. “Now, how can we make amends? How can we make it right?”

Poppy’s paws felt tingly. Poppy thought. “I can help clean,” Poppy said. “I can make a new card with Mia. I can wait my turn for the star.”

Miss Maya smiled. “That is preparation,” she said. “You have a plan. Let’s get ready and do it.”

Poppy brought soft paper towels. The towels felt squishy and cool. Poppy dabbed the glue carefully. “Dab, dab,” Poppy said. “I am cleaning.” Mia dabbed too. “Dab, dab,” Mia echoed. They worked slowly. The music chimed like tiny bells.

They set the messy card aside to dry. Poppy took a new paper and slid it to Mia. “Would you like to draw together?” Poppy asked. “I will listen. You can go first.”

Mia’s eyes brightened. “Yes,” she said. “Let’s draw Nana’s garden.”

Poppy folded paws in her lap. Poppy waited. Mia drew pink flowers. Poppy watched and listened. Mia said, “Now your turn.” Poppy drew a sunny yellow circle. “Sun,” Poppy said. “Do you like it?”

“I like it,” Mia said. “Thank you for waiting.”

Poppy smiled. “Thank you for letting me help,” Poppy said. Poppy felt warm and proud.

Miss Maya brought a clean cup of glue. “What is our rule?” she asked.

Poppy and Mia said together, “One dot, not a lot!” They giggled. Poppy squeezed one tiny dot. It felt cool. It looked like a shiny bead. They pressed a small, gold star in the corner. Poppy asked Leo, “May I please use a star?” Leo grinned. “Yes. Thank you for asking,” he said.

Soon, Mia’s new card looked even better than before. There were pink flowers, a bright sun, and a shiny gold star. Mia wrote “For Nana” in careful letters. She wiggled her whiskers with joy. “It’s beautiful,” she said. “Thank you, Poppy.”

Poppy’s heart felt light, like a balloon. “I am happy to help,” Poppy said. “I will remember to listen. I will prepare. I will ask first.”

Miss Maya gathered the class on the soft rug. It felt like a fluffy cloud under Poppy. The room was quiet and warm. Miss Maya said, “Friends, what did we learn?”

Poppy raised a paw. “When we make a mistake, we say sorry. Then we make amends. We clean up. We help fix it. We listen, and we wait our turn.”

Mia nodded. “I feel grateful,” she said. “Thank you, Poppy.”

Leo added, “Being ready and asking first keeps things safe.”

Miss Maya smiled big. “I am proud of you,” she said. “You listened. You prepared. You made amends. That is kindness.”

Poppy hugged Mia gently. The glue on their paws was dry and a little sparkly. The nursery smelled sweet. The music tinkled. The sun shone. Poppy felt calm and brave.

Poppy whispered, “I can be patient. I can say sorry. I can make it right.”

The End.
