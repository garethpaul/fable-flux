# Benny the Beaver's Colorful Team

Benny the beaver loved to build things with his strong teeth and busy paws. Today, he waddled into the bright creative art room at the community center. The room smelled like fresh paint and colorful crayons. Sunshine streamed through big windows, making everything sparkle.

"Wow!" said Benny, looking around with wide eyes. The walls were covered with beautiful pictures made by children from all over the neighborhood.

Ms. Rosa, the art teacher, smiled warmly. "Hello, Benny! We're making team posters today for our new sports club. Would you like to join us?"

Benny's tail slapped the floor with excitement. "Yes, please! I love sports!"

Around the room, different animals were already working. There was Maya the mouse drawing tiny soccer balls, Carlos the cat painting basketball hoops, and Zara the zebra making colorful handprints.

But when Benny looked closer, he felt his smile fade. All the posters showed the same kinds of animals playing sports - only cats playing basketball, only mice playing soccer, and only zebras playing tennis.

"Um, Ms. Rosa?" Benny said quietly, his voice wobbly. "I don't see any beavers in these pictures. Maybe I'm not good at sports because I'm different."

Ms. Rosa knelt down next to Benny. "Oh, Benny, what makes you think that?"

"Well," Benny said, touching his big flat tail, "I have this funny tail, and my teeth are so big. I don't look like the other animals."

Maya the mouse stopped drawing and scurried over. "Benny, your tail is amazing! I bet you could swim really fast!"

Carlos the cat nodded, his whiskers twitching. "And your strong teeth could help you play hockey! You could hold the stick really well!"

Zara the zebra galloped over, her stripes shimmering. "Everyone is different, and that's what makes sports fun! Look at my stripes - they help me run fast because they confuse other players!"

Ms. Rosa clapped her hands together. "What a wonderful idea! Let's make a poster that shows ALL of us playing sports together!"

Benny felt warmth spread through his furry chest. "Really? All of us?"

"Of course!" said Ms. Rosa. "Sports are for everyone, no matter what you look like or where you come from."

The friends got to work on a big, beautiful poster. Benny painted himself swimming with his powerful tail. Maya drew herself scoring goals with her quick little legs. Carlos painted himself jumping high to make baskets. Zara added herself running fast around a track.

"Look!" squeaked Maya. "We're all different, but we're all athletes!"

Benny stepped back to admire their work. The poster was full of bright colors - blue for the swimming pool, green for the soccer field, orange for the basketball, and purple for the running track. It smelled like happiness and teamwork.

"I love how we all look different but we're all on the same team," purred Carlos.

"Being different makes us special," added Zara, doing a little dance.

Benny's chest puffed up with pride. "I can't wait to try all these sports with my new friends!"

Ms. Rosa hung their poster on the wall next to all the others. "This is the most beautiful poster yet, because it shows that everyone belongs in sports, no matter what makes them unique."

As they cleaned up their art supplies, Benny realized something important. His big tail, strong teeth, and busy paws weren't things to hide - they were his superpowers! And having friends who looked different from him made everything more fun and exciting.

"Thank you for helping me see that being different is wonderful," Benny said, giving everyone a big group hug.

The End.
