# Rainbow Lunch in the Leafy Loft

High in a leafy tree, a brave little lion cub named Lumo lived in a cozy treehouse. The treehouse smelled like pine needles and sweet fruit. Sunlight made golden patches on the floor. Lumo’s whiskers twitched with excitement. Today was Lunch Day with friends!

Lumo peeked into the wooden cupboard. “Hmm,” he murmured. “Only crunchy crackers and a jar of honey.” His tummy gave a soft rumble. “I want a yummy, strong-lion lunch that helps me grow.”

He looked at the basket by the door. Empty. Lumo took a brave breath. “I will make a rainbow lunch,” he said. “A rainbow gives me many colors and many good foods. I can do it.”

Just then, his friends arrived, climbing the ladder one by one. There was Tiko the monkey, wearing a bright scarf. Mei the red panda, with a tiny bell on her wrist. Amina the giraffe, with warm brown eyes and a gentle smile. And Zaid the parrot, feathers shining green and blue.

“Hello!” chirped Zaid. “What’s for lunch? I can smell something sweet.”

“Only honey and crackers,” Lumo said, ears drooping. “But I want a rainbow lunch.”

Tiko swung from a branch and clapped. “Let’s make one! We can bring foods from our families. Different foods, different colors!”

Amina nodded. “Sharing is caring.”

Mei rang her bell softly. “We can try new tastes. That helps our bodies learn.”

Lumo smiled. “Yes! Let’s use our treehouse kitchen. We will be brave and try new things.”

They set to work. The little kitchen was full of happy sounds—clink, clatter, tap, tap. The air smelled like sweet fruit, leafy greens, and warm spices.

Lumo washed his paws with bubbly water. “Clean paws for cooking,” he said. “That keeps us healthy.”

He opened the window and the breeze carried in a gentle rustle. “Listen,” Lumo whispered. “The leaves are cheering us on.”

Tiko brought a basket of bright bananas and red cherry tomatoes. “From my grandma’s garden,” he said proudly. “Grandma says red foods help hearts feel strong.”

Mei placed a small bowl on the counter. Inside were little orange cubes. “Steamed pumpkin,” she said. “My family likes it with a tiny sprinkle of ginger. Orange foods help our eyes see in the dusk.”

Amina stretched her long neck and set down a bundle of dark leafy greens and juicy purple grapes. “We eat these after long walks,” she said kindly. “Greens help us feel steady and grapes are sweet like hugs.”

Zaid fluttered to the table with a pouch of chickpeas and a jar of lemon. “My auntie makes hummus,” he squawked happily. “Creamy and zesty! It helps our muscles.”

Lumo’s eyes shone. “So many colors! Red, orange, green, purple, and yellow bananas.” He patted the empty basket. “Let’s add the crunchy crackers and a tiny drizzle of honey.”

They began to build the rainbow lunch. Tiko sliced the tomatoes, tiny seeds glistening. Mei mashed the pumpkin with a soft squish. Amina rinsed the greens; they smelled cool and grassy. Zaid squeezed lemon with a bright squeeze-sound and stirred the chickpeas—scoop, mash, swirl.

Lumo tried to spread hummus on a cracker. Oops! It splatted on his paw. He giggled, then sighed. “I’m not good at this.”

Amina leaned down. “You are learning,” she said gently. “Try again, little lion.”

Lumo took a brave breath. “I will keep trying.” He held the cracker steady. Scoop. Spread. Smooth. “I did it!”

Zaid cheered, “Persistence! Keep going!”

They made a colorful platter: red tomatoes, orange pumpkin, yellow banana slices, green leaves, purple grapes, golden crackers, and creamy hummus. It looked like a rainbow road on a wooden plate.

But then—tap tap—rain began. The roof went patter-patter, and the room grew dim. A small shiver ran down Lumo’s back. “What if my friends don’t like my food?” he whispered.

Mei touched his paw. “It’s okay to be nervous. We try with kind hearts.”

Tiko nodded. “Taste with curiosity!”

Lumo picked up a leaf with hummus and a tomato. Crunch. Creamy. Juicy. His eyes widened. “Mmm! Fresh and cozy!”

One by one, they tasted. Amina munched pumpkin with banana. “Soft and sweet,” she said. Zaid dipped grapes in a tiny dot of hummus and laughed. “Surprise delicious!”

Mei mixed greens and tomatoes. “Color makes me brave.”

They ate slowly, listening to the rain sing. Between bites, they sipped cool water. Lumo felt warm and strong inside. He noticed the colors on the plate getting smaller and smaller.

A little knock came from the window. A shy field mouse peeped in, whiskers wet from the rain. “Excuse me,” she squeaked. “I smelled something nice. I am very hungry.”

Lumo’s heart glowed. “Come in,” he said. “We will share.”

They made a tiny rainbow plate for the mouse. She nibbled and smiled. “Thank you. This tastes like sunshine.”

When lunch was done, they cleaned the table together. Wipe, swish, stack. The treehouse smelled like lemon and honey and happy friends.

Lumo stood tall. “Today,” he said, “we learned that eating many colors helps our bodies. We learned to keep trying, even when it’s hard. And we shared with kind hearts.”

Amina smiled. “Nutrition makes us strong.”

Mei chimed, “Persistence helps us learn.”

Zaid fluttered, “Compassion makes friends.”

Tiko grinned. “And different foods from different families make a rainbow!”

Lumo felt brave and proud. He looked at the empty plates and the bright sky after rain. “Tomorrow,” he said, “we will make another rainbow.”

The End.
