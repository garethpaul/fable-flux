# Blue Blanket, Warm Tea, and the Story Star

In a quiet attic, where the roof whispered pitter-pat when the wind blew, a friendly bear cub named Bumi opened a small wooden door. The attic smelled like old pine and cinnamon. Dust sparkles floated in the sunbeams, and soft boxes sat like sleepy mountains.

“Hello, quiet place,” Bumi said. “Today is a calm day.”

Bumi climbed up and found a cozy corner. There was a blue blanket, a tiny rocking chair, and a basket of story cards with pictures. The cards showed rivers, stars, and friendly faces. Bumi loved stories. Bumi also loved taking care—of toys, of friends, and sometimes, of Bumi’s own big bear feelings.

Today, Bumi’s tummy felt a little twisty. Maybe it was the tall stairs. Maybe it was a loud noise from outside. Bumi put a paw on the tummy and took a slow breath in, then let it out like a soft “whooo.”

“I will take care of me,” Bumi whispered. “I can do small, kind things.”

Bumi made a self-care plan, just like Mama Bear had taught. Step one: pause. Step two: breathe. Step three: choose a gentle activity. Bumi wrapped up in the blue blanket. It felt warm and smooth, like a hug. Bumi rocked in the chair. Creak, creak, creak. The sound was slow and friendly.

“Better,” Bumi said.

Then Bumi heard a tiny scritch-scratch. It came from behind a tall box. Bumi peeked over and saw a little mouse with big ears. The mouse’s whiskers shook.

“Hello,” said Bumi in a soft voice. “I’m Bumi. Are you okay?”

“I am Miri,” squeaked the mouse. “The wind was noisy. My heart is thumpy.”

Bumi nodded. “My tummy felt twisty too. Would you like to do self-care with me?”

Miri blinked. “What is self-care?”

“It means we take care of our bodies and feelings,” Bumi said. “We can make a safe space. We can rest. We can tell a story.”

Miri’s ears perked up. “A story?”

Bumi opened the basket of story cards. The first card showed a bright star. “Let’s make a Story Star. You pick one card, then I pick one, and we make kind words.”

Miri chose the star. “This star is warm,” she said. “It says we are safe.”

Bumi chose a card with a cup of tea. “This tea is sweet and gentle,” Bumi said. “It helps our tummies relax.”

Miri smiled. “I like that.”

Bumi set a tiny cloth on the floor like a picnic. Miri sat on the edge. Bumi found a little tin cup in the attic and pretended to pour warm tea. “Sip, sip,” Bumi said. “We can pretend, and we can breathe.”

They took five slow breaths together. In through the nose, out through the mouth. Bumi counted with a soft paw. “One… two… three… four… five.”

Miri’s whiskers stopped shaking. “I feel calmer,” she said.

Bumi nodded. “Me too.” Then Bumi looked at Miri’s tiny paws. “Do your paws need a rest?”

“Yes,” Miri said. “They are tired.”

Bumi folded a corner of the blue blanket into a mouse-sized nest. “Here is a cozy spot,” Bumi said. “Self-care can be rest.”

Miri curled up. “Thank you, Bumi. You are very kind.”

Bumi smiled. “That is compassion. Compassion is kindness for you and for me. We can share it.”

The wind outside grew softer. In the attic, it smelled like warm wood. The rocking chair whispered creak, creak, creak. Bumi picked another card with a picture of a forest path.

“Let’s tell a story about a brave mouse and a careful bear cub,” Bumi said. “They listen to their bodies. They take breaks. They help each other.”

Miri nodded. “Once there was a mouse named Miri,” she began. “Her heart was thumpy.”

“And a bear cub named Bumi,” Bumi added. “Bumi wrapped up in a blanket and made tea.”

“They breathed together,” Miri said.

“They felt better,” Bumi said.

“They learned that self-care is not a fuss,” Miri said.

“It is a gift,” Bumi finished. They both giggled.

Bumi found a small bell that made a soft, happy ding. “When the bell rings, we stretch,” Bumi said. “Gentle stretches help our bodies.” They lifted paws high and wiggled toes. They rolled shoulders. They shook out tiny worries like crumbs.

Miri stood tall. “I feel brave now,” she said.

Bumi patted Miri’s back. “I feel calm. When feelings get big, we can pause, breathe, rest, and tell a kind story. We can ask for help. We can help friends.”

The sunbeam moved across the floor. Dust sparkles danced like tiny stars. The attic felt bright and safe.

“I will remember this,” said Miri. “Self-care helps me care for you too.”

Bumi nodded. “Yes. When we care for ourselves, we have more love to share.”

They packed the story cards back into the basket. Bumi folded the blue blanket. Miri touched the bell and smiled at its ding.

“Good-bye, quiet attic,” Bumi said. “Thank you for being our cozy place.”

“Good-bye,” squeaked Miri. “See you for tea and stories.”

Bumi climbed down the stairs with a light heart. The wind outside was gentle now. Bumi took one more soft breath and smiled.

Today’s lesson was clear: Caring for yourself is kind and brave. Stories can soothe. Compassion makes friends feel safe.

The End.
