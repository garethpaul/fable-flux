# Rosie Rabbit Gets Ready

In a cozy nursery with soft yellow walls and fluffy white clouds painted on the ceiling, lived a little rabbit named Rosie. Her fur was the color of warm chocolate, and her ears twitched whenever she got excited. Today was very special - it was the day of the Great Garden Race!

"Oh my!" squeaked Rosie, hopping out of her tiny bed. "The race is today, and I haven't prepared at all!"

Rosie looked around her comfortable nursery. Sunlight streamed through the window, making rainbow patterns on the soft carpet. She could smell the sweet scent of morning flowers drifting in from outside.

"I need to get ready," Rosie said to herself, her whiskers twitching with worry. "But I don't know how!"

Just then, her friend Oliver the owl flew to the windowsill. His feathers were a beautiful brown and white, and his big round eyes sparkled with wisdom.

"Good morning, Rosie!" hooted Oliver gently. "You look worried. What's wrong?"

"The Garden Race is today, and I'm not prepared!" Rosie's ears drooped sadly. "I don't think I can run fast enough."

Oliver tilted his head thoughtfully. "Preparation isn't just about being fast, Rosie. Would you like me to help you get ready the right way?"

Rosie's ears perked up. "Yes, please! But I thought preparation was just about practicing running."

"Let me show you," Oliver said kindly. "First, let's take care of your body."

Oliver taught Rosie some gentle stretches. "Touch your toes like this," he demonstrated. "Now reach up high like you're picking apples from a tree!"

Rosie giggled as she stretched. Her muscles felt warm and loose. "This feels good!"

"Now let's prepare your mind," Oliver continued. "Close your eyes and imagine yourself running. Feel your paws on the soft grass."

Rosie closed her eyes and took deep breaths. She could almost feel the cool morning air and hear the cheering from her friends.

"Next, we need to prepare your body with good food," Oliver suggested.

Together, they found some fresh carrots and crisp lettuce in the nursery's little play kitchen. "Eating healthy food gives us energy," Oliver explained as they munched.

"I feel stronger already!" Rosie said, her nose twitching happily.

"One more thing," Oliver said. "Let's prepare by asking for help."

Rosie looked confused. "Asking for help is preparation?"

"Of course! Smart rabbits know when to ask friends for support."

They hopped outside where Rosie's friends were gathering. There was Benny the bear, Luna the ladybug, and Sam the squirrel.

"We came to cheer for you!" said Benny, his voice warm like honey.

"And we brought water for after the race!" added Luna, her tiny voice tinkling like bells.

When the race began, Rosie felt ready. Her body was stretched and strong, her mind was calm and focused, her tummy was full of good food, and her heart was full of her friends' support.

She didn't win first place, but she ran her very best race ever. As she crossed the finish line, all her friends cheered loudly.

"You did it, Rosie!" Oliver hooted proudly. "You prepared in all the right ways!"

Rosie's whiskers twitched with joy. "I learned that preparation means taking care of my whole self - my body, my mind, and asking friends for help too!"

From that day on, whenever Rosie had something important to do, she remembered to prepare by stretching, eating well, thinking positive thoughts, and spending time with friends who cared about her.

The End.
