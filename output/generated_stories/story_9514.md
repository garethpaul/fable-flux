# Bear Cub Benny’s Busy Garden Shed

Benny was a friendly bear cub with soft, fuzzy ears and warm honey-brown fur. He loved the sunshine on his nose and the grass tickling his toes. One bright morning, Benny padded over to the little garden shed behind his forest home. The shed smelled like wood and fresh soil. It felt cool and quiet inside, with sunlight peeking through the window like golden stripes.

“Hello, Garden Shed!” Benny said, smiling. “I want to play outside today!”

He opened the creaky door. Creak! Inside, he found a small shovel, a watering can, some packets of seeds, a jump rope, and a red wagon with wobbly wheels. A spider in the corner waved a tiny leg. Benny waved back. He loved being kind.

Benny picked up the watering can. “Glug, glug,” it went as he filled it at the rain barrel. He looked at the packets of seeds. “Carrot seeds,” he read. “Sunflower seeds.” He sniffed a seed packet. It smelled like dust and sunshine.

“I will grow a garden,” Benny said. “I will play outside and help things grow!”

Just then, his friend Dotty the duckling waddled over, her little feet going pat-pat-pat on the path. “Hi, Benny!” Dotty quacked. “Can we play?”

“Yes!” Benny giggled. “Let’s plant seeds. We can jump, dig, water, and watch.”

Dotty clapped her soft wings. “I like to splash! I can help water.”

They stepped into the yard. The ground felt warm, and the air smelled sweet, like flowers and fresh grass. Buzz, buzz, a bee hummed past. “Hello, Bee,” Benny said kindly.

Benny and Dotty tried to push the red wagon full of tools. The wheels squeaked and wobbled. “Squeeeak!” It was hard to move. Benny puffed and pushed. Dotty pulled the handle. The wagon bumped and dumped. Clonk! The shovel fell out.

“Oh no,” Benny said. “This is a problem.”

Dotty tilted her head. “We can fix it,” she said softly. “But how?”

Benny thought. He sat on the grass and felt the warm sun on his fur. He listened to the leaves whisper shhhh in the breeze. He looked at the garden shed. “Maybe there is a way inside,” he murmured.

Back in the shed, Benny found a little toolbox. It had a tiny brush, a rag, and a small bottle of oil. The bottle smelled like nuts. “We can make the wheels smooth,” he said.

Dotty nodded. “We can work together.”

Benny brushed dirt from the wheels. Swish, swish. Dotty held the rag and wiped them clean. Wipe, wipe. Benny dripped a drop of oil on each wheel. Drip, drip. He gently spun them. Whirrr. The wagon did not squeak anymore.

“Let’s try,” Benny said. They pushed the wagon again. The wheels turned easy and quiet. “We did it!”

“You solved the problem,” Dotty quacked. “With kind hands and careful work.”

Benny grinned. “And teamwork!”

They took the wagon to a sunny patch. Benny scooped soft soil with the little shovel. The dirt felt cool and crumbly between his paws. Dotty pecked tiny holes with her beak. Tap, tap.

“Seeds go in,” Benny said. He dropped in carrot seeds. Plink, plink. Dotty dropped in sunflower seeds. Plunk, plunk. They tucked the seeds under a blanket of soil. Pat, pat.

Benny lifted the watering can. “Glug, glug,” the water whispered as it fell like gentle rain. Dotty splashed her feet in a tiny puddle. Splish, splash! They laughed and felt the cool drops on their toes.

Just then, Squeaky the mouse peeked around the shed door. “Hi,” squeaked Squeaky. “I want to play, too. But I don’t have a shovel or seeds.”

Benny looked at his tools. He felt a warm hug in his heart. “You can use my shovel,” he said. “And we can share our seeds.”

Squeaky’s eyes shone. “Thank you,” he squeaked. “You are generous.”

Benny smiled. “Friends share.” He handed the shovel to Squeaky and opened another packet of seeds. Together, they planted a little mouse-sized row. Squeaky patted the soil with tiny paws. Pat, pat.

They all took a break in the shade of the shed. The wood smelled warm, and the breeze felt soft on their faces. They ate crunchy apples and sweet berries. “Crunch, munch,” went Benny. “Yum, yum,” went Dotty. Squeaky licked berry juice from his whiskers. “Mmm!”

Benny stood up and stretched. “Let’s move our bodies,” he said. “We can jump rope, pull the wagon, and dance like the leaves.”

They jumped. They hopped. They pulled. They twirled. They ran in circles, giggling. Their hearts thumped in a happy beat. Thump-thump, thump-thump. The sun felt warm and kind.

When the sun slid lower, Benny put the tools back in the shed, nice and neat. The wagon sat ready, its wheels clean and smooth. The garden beds were tucked in, damp and dark. Buzz, buzz, the bee came back and hovered over the new patch.

Benny touched the soil with a gentle paw. “We worked outside. We moved and played. We solved a problem. We shared,” he said. “That is a good day.”

Dotty nodded. “We will watch the seeds grow tall.”

Squeaky smiled. “We will grow, too.”

Benny’s heart felt full like a basket. “Thank you, friends.”

Lesson: Playing outside is healthy and fun. We can solve problems with teamwork. We can be kind and generous by sharing what we have. When we care for nature and for friends, we all grow strong.

The End.
