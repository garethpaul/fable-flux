# Rocky Raccoon Builds a Beat

Rocky was a musical raccoon with bright eyes and a fluffy striped tail. He loved to tap, clap, and sing. One sunny morning, Rocky padded into the musical practice room. The room smelled like fresh wood and lemons. Soft light shone on drums, bells, and small blocks. The floor felt cool under his paws.

“Good morning, room!” Rocky chirped. He wiggled his whiskers and smiled. “Today I will build a beat!”

Rocky looked around. He saw wooden blocks for building, a tiny drum set, and bright safety cones in a little basket. He also saw a big sign on the wall with smiling stars. The sign said, “Be Safe. Be Strong. Be Kind.”

Rocky clapped his paws. “I will build a music fort,” he said. “A strong fort for big sounds!”

He picked up wooden blocks. Clink, clank, clunk! The blocks made happy sounds. Rocky stacked them high, higher, highest. “Look at my tower!” he giggled.

Just then, his friend Coach Penny the parrot flew in with a gentle flap-flap. Coach Penny wore a tiny whistle. “Hello, Rocky! I hear tapping tunes,” she chirped.

“Hi, Coach Penny!” Rocky said. “I’m building a music fort. I want to drum inside it and make big beats!”

Coach Penny tilted her head. “That sounds fun. Let’s talk about safety first.”

Rocky blinked. “Safety?”

“Yes,” said Coach Penny. “Safety keeps you strong. Safety keeps you smiling.” She pointed to the cones. “We use cones to make a safe space. We build low and wide, not wobbly and tall.”

Rocky looked at his tall tower. It shook a little. Wibble. Wobble. “Oh,” he said softly. He felt a tiny twist in his tummy. “I want to be safe.”

Coach Penny smiled. “Good choice. Let’s fix it together.”

Rocky took a deep breath. In and out. His ears perked up. “I can do it.”

“First,” Coach Penny said, “exercise time! We warm up our bodies and brains.”

Rocky grinned. He liked to move. “What do we do?”

Coach Penny blew her whistle. Tweet! “Reach up high like a tall tree,” she said.

Rocky reached up. His paws stretched to the ceiling. He felt his back stretch. “Ahhh,” he said.

“Now touch your toes,” said Coach Penny.

Rocky bent down. He touched his little toes. “Hee hee! That tickles.”

“Shake your paws,” Coach Penny cheered.

Rocky shook his paws. Shake-shake-shake! His tail swished. Swish-swish!

“Now we are ready,” Coach Penny said. “Let’s build a safe music fort.”

Rocky nodded. He took down the tall, wobbly tower. Clunk, clunk, clunk. He put cones in a circle to make a safe line. “This is our safe space,” he said.

“Great,” said Coach Penny. “We build low walls, not tall towers. We make a wide base.”

Rocky placed big blocks on the bottom. Thump. He pressed them gently. “Strong and steady,” he whispered. He stacked smaller blocks on top. Tap, tap. He left a doorway big enough to walk through. He made sure no blocks stuck out.

Rocky listened. The room was calm. His paws felt warm. He smelled the wood. He heard the soft hum of the lights.

He stood back. “It looks safe,” he said.

Coach Penny checked the walls. She gave a happy nod. “Safe and strong! Now let’s add music.”

Rocky rolled his tiny drum inside the fort. He set a shaker on the floor. He put a bell on a sturdy block. Ding!

Before he played, he saw his drumsticks on the floor. One stick lay across the doorway. “Oops,” he said. “Someone could trip.” He picked it up and put it in a small basket. “Sticks rest here when we don’t use them.”

Coach Penny clapped. “Excellent safety!”

Rocky stood in the doorway. “May I go in?”

“Yes,” said Coach Penny. “Walk, don’t run. Look with your eyes. Move with your paws.”

Rocky walked slowly. Step-step-step. He felt proud. He sat on a soft mat. He breathed in and out again. In. Out. Calm.

“Ready to build your beat?” Coach Penny asked.

Rocky smiled. “Ready!”

He tapped the drum. Dum-dum. He shook the shaker. Shhhh-shhh. He rang the bell. Ding-ding! He made a steady beat. Dum, shhh, ding. Dum, shhh, ding.

He sang, “Safe and strong, we build it right. We move our bodies, we feel so light!”

Coach Penny danced a happy parrot dance. She flapped and bobbed. “What a safe sound!” she chirped.

Rocky tried a big loud hit. BOOM! A block wiggled. Rocky paused. He checked the wall. “Is it okay?” he asked.

Coach Penny pointed. “Press it back gently.”

Rocky used both paws. Press, press. The block was steady again. “I fix problems safely,” he said.

They played together. The room filled with warm sounds. Dum, shhh, ding. Flap, clap, sing! Rocky’s heart felt bouncy with joy.

When the song was done, Rocky cleaned up. He put drumsticks in the basket. He stacked blocks back in the bin, low and neat. He put cones away. He checked the floor. “Clear and safe,” he said.

Coach Penny gave him a tiny high-five. “You built. You moved. You stayed safe.”

Rocky’s whiskers twitched. “Safety first, then music and fun!”

Coach Penny nodded. “Safety helps us play every day.”

Rocky waved at the room. “Thank you, practice room. Thank you, Coach Penny.”

He picked up his shaker and hummed. He felt strong, calm, and proud. “I can build. I can exercise. I can be safe.”

And Rocky tapped one last gentle beat. Dum. Shhh. Ding.

The End.
