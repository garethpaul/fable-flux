# Polly Speaks Up

Polly the parrot sat quietly in her travel cage in the back seat of the family car. Her bright green feathers ruffled as the car bumped along the road. Through the window, she could see tall buildings and busy streets whizzing by.

"Look at all those different people!" Polly chirped softly to herself. She saw children with different colored skin walking to school. Some wore colorful clothes from faraway places. "The world is so beautiful and different!"

But Polly felt sad. Every time she tried to share what she saw, the family didn't listen. When she squawked "Pretty flowers!" they said "Shush, Polly." When she called out "Happy children!" they told her to be quiet.

Polly's head drooped down. Her orange beak touched her chest feathers. "Nobody wants to hear what I have to say," she whispered. The car seat felt bumpy under her cage, and the air smelled like vanilla from Mom's coffee.

Just then, little Emma in the front seat started crying. "I'm scared about my new school," she sniffled. "What if the other kids don't like me because I'm different?"

Polly's bright eyes perked up. She knew exactly how Emma felt! Taking a deep breath, Polly stood up tall on her perch. Her claws gripped tightly as she found her brave voice.

"Emma!" Polly called out clearly. "Different is wonderful!"

The whole family turned around in surprise. Dad even pulled the car over safely.

"Polly?" Mom asked. "What did you say?"

Polly fluffed her feathers proudly. "I see different people every day! Different colors, different clothes, different languages. They're all beautiful! Emma should be proud to be different too!"

Emma's tears stopped. "Really, Polly? You think being different is good?"

"Yes!" Polly squawked happily. "In my travels, I learned that every country has special children. Some speak Spanish, some speak French, some speak Chinese. All special! All wonderful!"

Dad smiled in the rearview mirror. "Polly, we're sorry we didn't listen before. You have important things to say!"

"Thank you for speaking up, Polly," Mom added warmly. "Your words helped Emma feel better."

Polly's chest puffed out with pride. She had used her voice to help someone! "When we speak up with kindness," she chirped, "we can help others feel brave too!"

Emma giggled and reached back to gently touch Polly's cage. "Will you tell me more about the different children you've seen?"

"Of course!" Polly sang happily. As the car started moving again, she told Emma about children from around the world. She described kids playing soccer in Brazil, children flying kites in Japan, and little ones dancing in Africa.

"The world is full of amazing, different people," Polly explained. "And you, Emma, are amazing too! Being yourself is the best gift you can give."

Emma sat up straighter in her seat. "I'm going to be brave at my new school. I'll use my voice to make friends, just like you did, Polly!"

From that day on, the family always listened when Polly had something important to share. And Polly learned that speaking up with respect and kindness could make a big difference.

"Remember," Polly would often say, "your voice matters! Use it to help others and share the beautiful things you see in our big, wonderful world!"

The End.
