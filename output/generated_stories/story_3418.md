# Tully the Turtle Writes in the Pantry

Tully was a shy turtle with a soft green shell and gentle eyes. He liked quiet places. His favorite spot was the pantry, where the air smelled like cinnamon, oats, and warm bread. The cool tile felt smooth under his little feet. It was calm there. It felt safe.

One sunny afternoon, Tully peeked into the pantry and whispered, “Hello, jars. Hello, baskets.” The jars did not answer, of course, but the rice made a soft shhh when he brushed by, and the sugar looked like tiny white snow.

Tully carried a small notebook and a short, stubby pencil. He loved to write little stories, but he felt shy about sharing them. “What if my words are too small?” he asked the shelves.

He opened his notebook and wrote, in careful, slow letters: “Today I am brave.” The pencil made a soft scratch-scratch sound. Tully listened to his breathing—slow in, slow out. Calm in, calm out. That made his heart feel steady.

From the doorway, a friendly voice said, “Hi, Tully! What are you doing?” It was Pip the mouse, with whiskers like tiny paintbrushes.

Tully tucked the notebook close. “I… I’m writing,” he said in a quiet voice.

“I like writing!” Pip said. “Can I hear a line?” Pip sat on a bag of flour. A little puff of flour floated up like a cloud and tickled Tully’s nose.

Tully took a deep breath. “I feel a little shy,” he said.

Pip nodded. “It’s okay to feel shy. Let’s take slow turtle breaths together.”

They breathed in. They breathed out. The pantry was calm. Tully felt the cool tile. He heard the clock go tick-tock. He smelled sweet vanilla.

“Okay,” Tully said, feeling a tiny spark of courage. “I can try.”

Just then, Nia the cricket chirped from behind a box of oatmeal. “Hi, friends! I love stories too. I can make a quiet cheer.” Nia rubbed her wings to make a soft trrrr sound, like a happy hum.

Tully smiled. “A quiet cheer is nice.”

He opened his notebook and read, “Today I am brave. I sit in the pantry. I hear soft sounds. I feel calm.”

“That is lovely,” Pip said. “Your words make me feel calm too.”

Tully felt a warm glow in his shell. “Thank you,” he whispered. He wrote another line: “My heart is gentle. My voice is small. But my words are strong.”

Suddenly, the pantry door creaked, and a breeze made the spice jars clink. Tully’s notebook fluttered open, and a page slipped under a basket. “Oh no!” he cried.

Pip scampered. Nia hopped. Tully tucked into his shell for a moment, listening to his breath. Slow in. Slow out. “I can do this,” he told himself. “I am brave.”

He peeked out and stretched his little legs. He moved slow and steady to the basket. He pressed his feet on the cool floor and pushed, push, pushed. The basket wiggled. Pip and Nia cheered softly, “Go, Tully, go!”

The page slid free. Tully picked it up. There was a smudge of flour on the corner, like a tiny white moon. He giggled. “My story has a moon now,” he said.

“You did it,” Pip said. “You were calm, and you did it.”

Tully nodded. “I stayed calm and I helped myself. That feels good.”

He wrote again: “When I feel shy, I breathe. I can be brave and kind to myself.”

Nia chirped, “Can I add a word?”

Tully smiled. “Yes.”

Nia said, “Hope.”

Tully wrote: “I have hope.” He liked how that sounded. He felt brave enough to share more.

“Would you like to hear my pantry poem?” Tully asked.

“Yes!” Pip and Nia said together.

Tully read, his voice soft but steady:
“Pantry quiet, pantry bright,
Cinnamon day and vanilla light.
Soft little sounds, tick-tock tune,
Sugar snow and flour moon.
I am small, but I am strong.
In my heart, I sing my song.”

Pip’s eyes shined. Nia’s wings hummed. The pantry seemed to smile, full of cozy smells and tiny sounds.

“Tully,” Pip said, “your writing helped me feel calm.”

Nia added, “Your words helped my heart sit down and rest.”

Tully’s shy smile grew bigger. He felt tall, even though he was small. “My words can help,” he said. “I can help.”

They decided to make a little “Calm Corner” in the pantry. Pip brought a soft napkin for a mat. Nia found a cinnamon stick that smelled sweet. Tully set his notebook on a small lid like a desk. “This is our quiet place,” he said.

Tully wrote one last line for the day: “I am brave when I try. I am calm when I breathe. I am proud when I share.”

He closed his notebook with a gentle pat. “Thank you for listening,” he told his friends.

“Thank you for sharing,” Pip replied.

“Thank you for writing,” Nia chirped.

Tully took one more slow breath and felt the calm all the way to the tip of his tail. He knew he was still shy. That was okay. He also knew he could write, breathe, and try. That made him feel strong inside.

That night, as the pantry grew quiet and cozy, Tully whispered, “Today I learned I can be brave. My small voice matters.” The sugar sparkled like stars, and the flour moon watched over the Calm Corner.

The lesson was clear: When you feel shy, breathe slow, write your feelings, and try. You can be calm. You can be well. You can be brave, just like Tully.

The End.
