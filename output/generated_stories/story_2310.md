# Pebble the Penguin Paints Together

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with bright, shiny eyes and a wiggly little waddle. Today, Pebble waddled into the creative art room. It smelled like fresh paper and sweet glue. The tables were covered with soft brushes, bright paints, smooth clay, and bowls of sparkly buttons. Sunlight made the glitter shine like tiny stars.

“Hello, colors!” Pebble chirped, flapping happy wings. Pebble loved to paint. Pebble loved to try new things. Pebble was curious.

Other friends were there too: Mia the mouse, Finn the fox, and Tessa the turtle. They were all ready to make art. A big sign on the wall said, “Art Time Is For Everyone!”

Pebble saw a super shiny silver paint pot. It looked like moonlight. Pebble gasped. “Ooo! I want to use the silver!” Pebble scooted quickly and held the paint pot close. “Mine!” Pebble said softly, hugging it.

Mia the mouse walked over with tiny careful steps. “Can we share the silver?” Mia asked. “I want to make moon dots.”

Pebble shook a little head. “I need it. My picture will be the most sparkly.”

Finn the fox flicked his tail. “Maybe we can all use it,” he said gently. “We can take turns.”

Pebble clutched the pot. “No… I want my painting to be perfect.”

Tessa the turtle blinked slowly. “Perfect can be shared,” she whispered.

Pebble waddled to a corner with the silver paint and a big white paper. Pebble dipped a brush and made soft swishy sounds. Swish, swish. The silver paint glided like smooth ice. Pebble smiled. But then Pebble heard quiet footsteps stop. No one was laughing. No one was singing. The room felt still, like a snow day without snowflakes.

Pebble peeped back at the table. Mia was tapping an empty spot. Finn was stirring red and blue but not smiling. Tessa was pushing a plain brown button back and forth. Their faces looked a little droopy. Pebble felt a small pinch inside.

Pebble thought, My picture is shiny. But my friends look dim. Is this fun for everyone?

Pebble’s wings drooped. The silver paint sparkled, but the quiet did not feel good. Pebble took a deep breath, like a soft puff of winter air, and waddled back.

“Um… I was not kind,” Pebble said, voice tiny. “I kept the silver. I did not include you.”

Mia looked up. “We felt left out,” she squeaked.

“I’m sorry,” Pebble said. “I want to try again. Let’s make a big picture together! We can all use the silver. We can all share.”

Finn’s ears perked up. “A team painting?”

Tessa nodded slowly. “Together is warm.”

Pebble placed the silver paint in the middle. “We can take turns,” Pebble said. “We can mix colors. We can make space for everyone.”

Mia smiled. “Let’s draw a night sky!”

Finn wagged his tail. “I can paint purple mountains.”

Tessa lifted a brush carefully. “I can make a long, steady river.”

Pebble’s heart felt light and bubbly. “I will paint a bright moon with the silver,” Pebble said. “But I will wait my turn.”

They started. Swish, tap, dot. The brushes made soft music on the paper. Pebble watched Mia make tiny silver stars. “Twinkle, twinkle,” Mia sang. Pebble waited, and it felt okay. Pebble noticed how happy Mia looked. Pebble’s wings wiggled.

Finn mixed red and blue into a deep purple. He added silver on the edges. “Look! The mountains shine like bedtime,” he said.

Tessa painted a wide river with slow strokes. She added silver ripples. “The water looks calm,” she whispered, smiling.

It was Pebble’s turn for the silver. Pebble dipped the brush gently and painted a big, round moon. The paint felt cool and smooth. The moon glowed. Pebble laughed, a bright, happy laugh. “Our picture is so beautiful!”

They added buttons for flowers, cotton balls for clouds, and clay for little rocks. The paper filled with color and texture. The room filled with giggles and soft chatter.

A teacher penguin waddled by and clapped. “Oh! What a caring team,” she said. “What a kind and inclusive group. You made space for everyone.”

Pebble puffed with pride, but not the alone kind. It was the together kind. Pebble looked at friends. “I learned something,” Pebble said. “When I keep all the shiny things, hearts feel dim. When I share and include, everyone shines.”

Mia patted Pebble’s flipper. “Including feels good.”

Finn nodded. “Sharing makes more fun.”

Tessa added, “Curiosity brings new ideas. Look at our sky!”

Pebble’s tummy felt warm like cocoa. Pebble thought about the quiet from before and the music of now. Pebble wanted to remember this day.

They hung the big picture on the wall. The sign beside it said, “Art Time Is For Everyone!” Pebble picked up a marker and wrote, “We share. We include. We make art together.”

Pebble turned to the friends. “Tomorrow, do you want to try new paints? Maybe bumpy paint? Maybe smelly lemon paint?”

“Yes!” everyone cheered. “We’ll explore together!”

Pebble grinned. “Together is best.”

And the silver paint still sparkled, but now it sparkled on all their smiles, too.

The End.
