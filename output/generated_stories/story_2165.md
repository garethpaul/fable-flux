# Tumble the Tortoise in the Pantry

Tumble the tortoise lived with a gentle family. He was old and very wise. His shell was smooth like a river stone, and his eyes were kind and bright. One quiet morning, Tumble waddled into the pantry.

The pantry was cool and cozy. The floor felt smooth under Tumble’s tiny feet. He could smell cinnamon, apples, and a little bit of garlic. Sunlight slipped under the door, making a golden line on the tiles.

Tumble took a deep breath. “Today,” he said softly, “I will practice calm.”

He looked around. On a low shelf sat a basket of bright yellow bananas. On another shelf, there was a jar of crunchy crackers. Tumble smiled. “A small snack and a big lesson,” he whispered.

Then he heard a rustle. “Squeak-squeak!” Two little pantry mice popped out from behind a flour bag. They wore crumbs like tiny hats.

“Hello,” Tumble said in his slow, warm voice. “Good morning.”

“Good morning!” said Mimi Mouse. “We love the pantry. It smells like stories.”

“We love the crackers,” added Max Mouse. “Crunch, crunch!”

Tumble nodded. “Crackers are nice. But we must ask before we take. We share and we care.”

Max reached for the jar. The lid wobbled. Crack! The jar tipped and fell. Crumbs flew like snow. Mimi gasped. Max squeaked, “Oh no!”

Tumble felt his chest get tight. His face felt hot. His heart thump-thumped. He wanted to shout, “Stop!” But he remembered his promise.

He pulled his head in, just a little. He took a slow breath. In through the nose. Out through the mouth.

“In... and out... I can be calm,” Tumble whispered. He touched his shell. It was cool and steady. “Shell breath,” he said. “Slow and safe.”

Mimi looked worried. “Tumble, are you mad?”

Tumble peeked out and nodded gently. “I feel mad inside. My tummy feels twisty. My cheeks feel warm. That is anger. It is okay to feel it. I will not roar. I will breathe. Then I will use my words.”

He took another slow breath. In... and out...

Max’s whiskers drooped. “I wanted crackers so much. I forgot to ask. I am sorry.”

“Thank you for saying sorry,” said Tumble. His voice was calm and soft now. “Let us make a plan. We can fix this together.”

They picked up the crumbs. Brush, brush, brush. Tiny paws and steady shell. The pantry smelled like flour dust and cinnamon again.

When the floor was clean, Tumble pointed with a gentle nod. “These are my boundaries,” he said. “A boundary is a safe line. The crackers are not for taking without asking. It keeps the pantry neat and kind.”

Mimi tilted her head. “How do we remember?”

Tumble thought. Then he smiled. “We can make a sharing map. A map shows where to ask and where to take.”

He found a small sticky note on the door. He drew a big happy face and a tiny cracker. “This shelf,” he said, tapping the note, “is for asking. If you want a cracker, say, ‘May I have one, please?’ The top shelf is a no-touch place. That is a boundary.”

Mimi and Max nodded. “May we practice?” asked Mimi.

“Yes,” said Tumble. He set one cracker on a plate on the low shelf. “Try now.”

Mimi stood tall. “May I have one, please?”

Tumble smiled. “Thank you for asking. Yes, you may have one.”

Max shuffled his feet. “May I have one too, please?”

“Yes,” Tumble said, “and next time, we ask first.”

They crunched together. Crunch, crunch. The sound was happy and small.

Suddenly, the pantry door swung open. The family’s little child peeked in. “Hi, Tumble!” The child stepped closer, hands reaching fast for Tumble’s shell.

Tumble blinked. His body felt tight again. He pulled in just a bit and took a shell breath. In... and out...

He used his calm voice. “Please use gentle hands. My shell is for soft touches only.”

The child stopped and nodded. “Gentle?” They reached out, slow and soft. Tap, tap. A kind pat.

“Thank you,” Tumble said. “That keeps me safe.”

Mimi whispered, “That was a boundary too!”

Tumble nodded. “Yes. Boundaries help everyone feel safe.”

They heard the clock tick-tock in the hall. The pantry felt peaceful again. It smelled like apples and good choices.

Mimi smiled. “Tumble, what do we do if we feel mad again?”

Tumble showed them. “Stop. Breathe like a turtle. In... and out. Name the feeling: ‘I feel mad.’ Then use kind words to say what you need. Ask, don’t grab. And remember your boundary map.”

Max tapped the sticky note. “Ask shelf. No-touch shelf. We can do it.”

Tumble’s eyes sparkled. “That is wise.”

They all shared a slice of banana, which smelled sweet and sunny. They giggled when the peel made a tiny squeak on the plate.

Tumble looked at his friends. “Today we learned to be calm, to ask, and to keep safe spaces. That is a big adventure for a small pantry.”

Mimi and Max chimed, “In... and out... we can be calm.”

Tumble smiled, warm as a sunbeam. “When we breathe and use our words, anger turns into a good plan.”

The End.
