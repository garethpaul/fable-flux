# Pippa Penguin Solves the Beat

Pippa was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and quick feet. She waddled into the musical practice room, humming a happy tune. The room was warm and cozy. Colorful drums lined the wall, shiny bells twinkled in a basket, and a big round drum sat in the middle like the moon. A soft rug felt squishy under her webbed feet. It smelled like clean wood and lemon polish.

“Good morning, room!” Pippa chirped. “I’m ready to practice!” She flapped her little wings with a happy swish-swish.

Today, Pippa wanted to play a song for her friends. She picked up two small drumsticks. Tap-tap! Tap-tap! The sound was crisp and bouncy. She smiled.

But when Pippa tried to play her song and do her fun exercise hops, everything got mixed up. Hop-tap-tap. Tap-hop—oops! The beat went bumpity-bump and the hops went flippity-flop. The bells jingled at the wrong time. Pippa frowned a tiny frown.

“Oh no,” she said softly. “My hands and my feet are not working together.”

She sat on the rug to think. She took a deep breath. In-out. In-out. The room was quiet except for the soft hum of a heater and the faint jingle of a bell rolling in the basket.

“I can solve this,” Pippa told herself. “I will use my penguin brain. I will think, test, and try again.”

First, Pippa looked at the big round drum. “Step one,” she said. “Listen to the beat.” She tapped the big drum with one stick. BOOM… BOOM… BOOM… Each sound felt like a gentle wave. Pippa closed her eyes and nodded her head. Nod… nod… nod… “This is my steady beat,” she whispered.

Next, Pippa used her feet. “Step two,” she said. “Move with the beat.” She stood tall, bent her knees, and hopped softly on the rug. Hop… hop… hop… Her feet landed when the drum said BOOM. It felt good. Her body warmed up. She giggled. “This is fun exercise!”

Now Pippa added her other drumstick. “Step three,” she said. “Hands join in.” She tried tap-tap while she hopped. But she went too fast. Hop-tap-tap-tap—whoops! The sticks clacked together and dropped. Clatter!

Pippa paused. She put a flipper on her cheek. “That didn’t work. Why?” she asked. She thought carefully. “Maybe my hands need a different job. Maybe I can make a pattern.”

She clapped two times and counted out loud. “One, two. One, two.” The words felt steady and kind.

“New plan,” she said. “Feet on the big beats. Hands on the small beats.”

She tried again. BOOM with a hop. Then tap-tap with her sticks. Hop… tap-tap… Hop… tap-tap… Her feet and hands started to smile together. The sounds filled the room—deep boom, light tap, happy breath. Pippa’s cheeks felt warm. Her heart felt brave.

“Yay!” Pippa cheered. “It’s working!”

Then Pippa wanted to add bells. She slipped a jingle band over her flipper. The tiny bells chimed like little stars. “Step four,” she said. “Add one new sound.”

She tested the pattern. Hop (BOOM)… tap-tap… jingle! Hop (BOOM)… tap-tap… jingle! The bells made a bright, shiny sparkle. But after a few rounds, Pippa wobbled and the bells jingled at the wrong time.

She stopped and smiled. “It’s okay to stop. I can think again.”

Pippa talked to herself kindly. “What do I know? I know the big beat. I know the small taps. I need a place for the jingle. Maybe the jingle comes after the taps.”

She made a plan out loud, slow and clear. “Hop on BOOM. Tap-tap with sticks. Pause. Jingle once.” She pointed to each thing with her flipper. “Hop. Tap-tap. Wait. Jingle.”

She tried the plan. Hop… tap-tap… (quiet)… jingle! Hop… tap-tap… (quiet)… jingle! The quiet pause felt like a soft breath. The jingle felt like sunshine at the end.

Pippa danced a little circle. Swish, swish went her feet. Ding-a-ling went the bells. “I did it!” she laughed.

Just then, her friend Tiko peeked in. “Hi, Pippa! I heard music. It sounds great!”

Pippa beamed. “Hi, Tiko! I used my brain to solve a problem. I listened. I moved. I made a plan. I tested. I tried again.”

Tiko clapped. “Can you teach me?”

“Yes!” said Pippa. “First, we find the steady beat. Listen.” BOOM… BOOM… BOOM… “Now hop with me.”

Tiko hopped with Pippa. Their feet made soft thumps on the rug. Thump… thump… thump…

“Now tap-tap,” said Pippa. “And remember our pause. Then jingle.”

Together they played: Hop… tap-tap… (quiet)… jingle! Their music filled the room. It sounded clear and bright. It felt strong and gentle at the same time. It made their bodies warm and happy.

When the song ended, Pippa and Tiko took deep breaths and high-finned. Their flippers touched with a friendly slap.

Pippa smiled. “We solved it by thinking, moving, and trying again.”

Tiko nodded. “Thinking helps our music. Thinking helps our bodies move. Thinking helps us feel brave.”

Pippa looked around the cozy room. “I love music. I love exercise. And I love using my brain.”

They put away the sticks and bells. The room was quiet and tidy. Pippa hummed her tune as they waddled out, proud and calm.

Lesson: When something is hard, stop, think, make a plan, and try again. Use your brain, your body, and your heart.

The End.
