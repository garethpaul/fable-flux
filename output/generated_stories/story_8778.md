# Bubbles, Squeaks, and the Truth

Mimi the kind mouse lived in a cozy wall nook near a bright, shiny bathroom. The bathroom smelled like soap and warm towels. It sounded like drips, plinks, and soft hums from the fan. Every morning, the mirror smiled at the sun, and the blue rug felt squishy like a cloud.

One rainy day, Mimi scurried in to visit her friends. “Squeak hello!” she sang.

Her friends were very different, and that made Mimi happy. Pip the porcupine had soft, bendy quills that looked like tiny paintbrushes. Bella the goldfish swished in a round bowl by the sink, her tail sparkling like orange ribbons. Toot the toothbrush had rainbow bristles and liked to tickle teeth. They were all different, and that made their games extra fun.

“Let’s play bubble boats!” Mimi squeaked. She climbed onto the edge of the tub where Mama Human had left a little sea of bubbles. The bubbles smelled like strawberries. Pop! Pop! Pop!

“I can make a bubble hat,” Pip said, tapping the suds. A fluffy crown wobbled on his head. “Ta-da!”

“Oh! I want one too,” Mimi said. She dipped her paws and made a tiny bubble hat. “Do I look fancy?”

Bella blew small bubble kisses from her bowl. “Glub-glub! You look lovely,” she bubbled.

Toot laughed in a tinny voice. “Boat race time! On your marks, squeak, set, go!”

Mimi and Pip used little bottle caps as boats. They blew softly. The caps danced across the foam. Mimi’s whiskers twitched with joy. Pip’s eyes shone bright.

Then—oops! Mimi leaned too far. Her tiny paw bumped a tall, wobbly bottle of shampoo. It slid… it tilted… it tipped. Plop! The bottle fell into the tub, and a shiny blue wave splashed out over the edge.

Splash! A warm sheet of soapy water rolled onto the floor and over the squishy blue rug. The rug looked like a little lake now.

Mimi’s heart thumped. Her ears drooped. “Oh no,” she whispered. “I made a mess.”

Footsteps echoed in the hallway. Thump-thump. Mama Human was coming. Pip’s quills trembled. Bella swirled in a circle. Toot froze in his cup.

Pip peeked at Mimi. “We can hide,” he whispered. “No one will know.”

Mimi’s tummy felt twisty. She looked at the wet rug. She looked at her friends. She felt a little hot puff of anger at herself. “I should have been careful,” she thought. The anger fizzled into her cheeks.

She remembered something Mama Mouse always said: “Breathe in, breathe out. Tell the truth. That is what kind mice do.”

Mimi took a slow breath. In. Out. Her body felt calmer. Her whiskers stopped shaking. “I need to be honest,” she said softly. “Even if I feel scared.”

Mama Human stepped into the bathroom. She saw the shiny puddle. She saw the fallen shampoo. She looked at the little friends. Her eyebrows lifted like little bridges. “Oh dear,” she said. “What happened here?”

Mimi’s tail swished. She stood up straight, even though she was small. “I did it,” she squeaked. “I bumped the bottle. I made the splash. I’m sorry.”

Pip blinked. “Mimi, you told the truth,” he whispered.

Mama Human knelt down. Her voice was gentle and warm like a towel from the dryer. “Thank you for being honest, Mimi,” she said. “Accidents happen. We can clean it up together.”

Mimi’s eyes brightened. “Together?”

“Together,” Mama Human said. She handed Mimi a tiny corner of a towel. Pip dabbed with a washcloth using his brushy quills. Bella blew little bubble signals—“Glub-glub!”—to show where the biggest puddles were. Toot sang a scrub-scrub song to keep them moving.

“Wipe, wipe, swish,” sang Toot.
“Dab, dab, wish,” hummed Pip.
“Glub-glub, here!” bubbled Bella.
“Squeak-squeak, cheer!” giggled Mimi.

Soon the floor was dry. The rug felt cozy again. The bathroom smelled like clean strawberries and warm soap.

Mama Human smiled. “You all did a great job. Mimi, I’m proud you said the truth. That helps everyone feel safe.”

Mimi’s chest felt light like a bubble. “I was scared,” she said. “I felt mad at myself for the mess. But I took a slow breath. Then I told the truth.”

Pip nodded. “I get prickly when I’m mad,” he said. “I will try slow breaths too.”

Bella flicked her fin. “Glub! When we are honest, we can fix things.”

Toot wiggled. “And then we can play again!”

Mimi looked at her friends. They were all different—their shapes, their sounds, their ways to help. And that made their team strong. “I like that we are not the same,” Mimi said. “Your prickles help dab. Bella’s bubbles help point. Toot’s song helps us smile. My squeaks help us tell the truth.”

Mama Human patted the rug. “Different friends, same big hearts,” she said. “Now, bubble boats again—but with careful paws.”

Mimi set the little boat on the water. She looked at the shampoo bottle and stepped back a tiny bit. “Ready?” she asked.

“Ready!” Pip grinned.

“Glub-glub, go!” Bella cheered.

Toot chimed, “Race time!”

They blew softly. The boats glided. Mimi’s laugh sparkled like the bubbles. She felt brave, calm, and kind.

Mimi whispered, “Honesty helps us learn, fix, and feel good inside.”

Everyone nodded and played on, happy and safe in the shiny, clean bathroom.

The End.
