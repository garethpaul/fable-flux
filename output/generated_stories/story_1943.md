# Benny Beaver Makes It Right

Benny was a busy beaver with bright, shiny teeth and a warm, thumpy tail. One sunny morning, Benny visited Granny Willow’s house. “Today,” Granny said, smiling, “I need help in the guest room. We have a visitor tonight.”

Benny puffed up with pride. “I can do it! I’m brave and busy!”

The guest room smelled like clean soap and soft flowers. Sunlight made the curtains glow. The blankets were fluffy like little clouds. Benny wiggled his whiskers. “First, I will stack the pillows,” he said.

As Benny stacked, he hummed a happy tune. Tap-tap went his tail on the wooden floor. He set one pillow, then two, then three—very tall! But the tower wobbled.

“Careful,” whispered Benny. “Steady, steady—”

Uh-oh! The tower fell. Whoomp! A pillow bounced into a lamp. The lamp tipped, slid, and toppled onto a small glass bowl on the table.

Clink. Crack. Oh no.

The bowl had a tiny chip on the edge. Benny’s heart went thump, thump. His whiskers drooped. “I didn’t mean to,” he whispered. He felt a little swirl in his tummy.

Just then, his friend Dotty Duck waddled in. “Quack-hello! Need a hand?”

Benny stood very still. “I made a mistake,” he said in a small voice. “I chipped Granny’s bowl. I feel scared.”

Dotty looked at the bowl. She tilted her head. “It is a small chip,” she said gently. “You are still a good beaver.”

Benny blinked. “But what if Granny is mad?”

Dotty took a brave breath. “Let’s tell her the truth. I will stand with you.”

Benny swallowed. He could smell Granny’s cinnamon tea from the kitchen. He could hear the soft tick-tock of the hallway clock. He felt Dotty’s warm wing on his paw.

Together they walked to Granny. Step, step, step.

“Granny,” Benny said, voice shaky. “I was trying to help in the guest room. I stacked the pillows too high. The lamp fell. The bowl got a chip. I am sorry.”

Granny looked at Benny. Her eyes were kind and bright. She took his paw in her wrinkly hand. “Thank you for telling me the truth,” she said. “Thank you for being brave.”

Benny’s eyes got watery. “Are you mad?”

Granny shook her head. “I am not mad. Things can break. Hearts do not have to. I forgive you, Benny.”

Benny let out a big breath. “Hooo.” His shoulders relaxed. Dotty smiled and squeezed his paw.

Granny touched the bowl. “We can still use it for buttons,” she said. “And next time, we can stack pillows low and safe.”

Benny nodded. “Yes. Low and safe.”

Dotty fluffed her feathers. “We can finish the guest room together!”

Benny’s tail gave a happy thump. “Teamwork!”

They went back to the guest room. The sun felt warm on their fur and feathers. The sheets smelled like fresh air. Benny folded the blankets slowly, pat-pat, smooth-smooth. Dotty arranged the pillows two by two, soft and snug. Granny brought a little basket for the chipped bowl. “New job for you,” she said to the bowl. “Button keeper!”

Benny giggled. “Hello, Button Keeper.”

While they worked, Benny said, “Granny, thank you for forgiving me.”

Granny smiled. “Forgiveness helps our hearts feel light. It helps friends stay close.”

Dotty nodded. “And telling the truth is brave.”

Benny felt warm and strong. “I like being brave and honest. I like fixing mistakes.”

They checked the room. The blanket corners were neat. The pillows were perfect. The lamp stood steady. A tiny breeze made the curtains whisper shhhh. Everything looked just right.

Knock, knock! The visitor arrived early. It was Mr. Mole with round glasses and a gentle smile. “Oh my,” he said, sniffing. “It smells so clean and cozy!”

Benny’s chest lifted with pride. “Welcome, Mr. Mole! Your room is ready.”

Mr. Mole patted Benny’s paw. “Thank you, young helper.”

Granny winked at Benny. “You finished the task very well.”

Benny’s tail thumped softly. “I made a mistake, but I told the truth. Granny forgave me. We worked together. We finished.”

Dotty quacked, “That is real friendship.”

Benny looked at his friends. He felt light, like a little leaf on a stream. “When I make a mistake, I can be brave. I can say sorry. I can fix it. I can forgive others, too.”

Granny poured cinnamon tea. The warm smell filled the room. They clinked cups—clink, clink—and smiled.

Benny whispered to himself, “Forgiveness makes room in my heart for friendship.”

And with that, the guest room was ready, the visitor was happy, and Benny learned a big lesson in a small, sunny room.

The End.
