# Nutkin's Big Pantry Helper Day

Nutkin the squirrel woke up with his fluffy tail twitching with excitement. Today, Mama Squirrel had asked him to help organize the family pantry before winter came.

"Good morning, Nutkin!" chirped Mama from the kitchen. "Are you ready to be my special helper today?"

"Yes, yes, yes!" squeaked Nutkin, bouncing on his tiny paws. "I want to help with ALL the chores!"

The pantry smelled like cinnamon and dried berries. Shelves stretched high above Nutkin's head, filled with jars of nuts, seeds, and sweet acorns. But oh my! Everything was scattered and messy.

"Where do we start, Mama?" asked Nutkin, his big brown eyes wide.

"First, we'll sort all the acorns by size," Mama explained gently. "Big ones go in this basket, medium ones here, and tiny ones in this small jar."

Nutkin grabbed an acorn and examined it carefully. *Plink!* It went into the big basket. Then another. *Plink, plank, plink!* Soon he had a rhythm going.

"This is fun!" he giggled, his whiskers twitching with joy.

But then Nutkin saw his friend Chippy the chipmunk peeking through the pantry window, playing outside in the sunshine. Nutkin's paws slowed down. The acorns didn't seem as interesting anymore.

"Can I play outside now?" Nutkin asked, leaving half the acorns unsorted.

Mama's face looked a little sad. "Oh, Nutkin. When we start a job, it's important to finish it. Our family needs these acorns organized for winter."

Nutkin felt his tummy do a little flip. He had made Mama sad by not finishing his chore. "I'm sorry, Mama. I forgot about finishing."

"It's okay, little one," Mama said, giving him a warm hug that smelled like pine needles. "Everyone makes mistakes. What matters is what we do next."

Nutkin thought hard. "Can I try again? I want to be a good helper!"

"Of course!" Mama smiled. "And maybe we can ask Chippy to help too. Many paws make light work!"

Nutkin scampered to the window. "Chippy! Want to help us organize acorns? We can make it a game!"

Chippy's eyes sparkled. "Yes! I love sorting games!"

Soon both friends were working together. They counted aloud: "One, two, three!" as they sorted. They made up silly songs about acorns. The work felt like playing!

"Look, Mama!" called Nutkin proudly. "We finished ALL the acorns, AND we organized the sunflower seeds too!"

The pantry now looked beautiful and tidy. Every jar sparkled clean, and everything had its perfect place. The sweet smell of organized nuts filled the air.

"You both did such wonderful work!" Mama beamed. "Nutkin, you learned something very important today."

"What's that?" asked Nutkin, his tail curled with curiosity.

"When we finish our chores, we help our whole family. And when we include our friends, work becomes more fun!" Mama explained.

Chippy nodded eagerly. "Can we help organize tomorrow too?"

"Absolutely!" laughed Mama. "Good helpers are always welcome!"

That evening, as Nutkin snuggled into his cozy nest, he felt proud and happy. His tummy was full of warm acorn soup, and his heart was full of joy. He had learned that finishing chores felt even better than starting them, and sharing work with friends made everything more fun.

"Tomorrow I'll be an even better helper," Nutkin whispered sleepily.

The End.
