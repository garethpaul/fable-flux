# Riff the Raccoon Finds the Right Words

Riff was a small raccoon with bright eyes, soft gray fur, and a striped tail that swished to the beat. He loved to make music in the sunny backyard. Each morning, warm sunbeams tickled the grass. Bees buzzed softly. Birds sang sweet “tweet-tweet” songs from the fence. Riff would tap on a tin can, shake a tiny bell, and strum a rubber-band guitar he made himself. “Ting-ting! Shake-shake! Boing-boing!” he hummed, smiling wide.

Riff’s friends lived in the same backyard. There was Dot the ladybug, slow and careful. There was Gus the snail, shiny and shy. And there was Pip the blue jay, loud and fast. They liked to play together. They liked Riff’s music too. But one sunny morning, something went wrong.

Riff had practiced a new song. He wanted to share it at a backyard music show. He had practiced and practiced. Tap, tap. Ting, ting. Boing, boing. He made a little poster with a crayon: “Riff’s Sunny Song Show! Today!” He put the poster by the big flower pot.

Pip the blue jay swooped down. “Caw! Caw! I can sing loud! I will sing first!” Pip grabbed Riff’s rubber-band guitar with his beak.

“Wait, Pip,” Riff said. “That is my guitar. I need it for my show.”

Pip fluffed his feathers. “But I want it now!” He struck the rubber bands. Twang! Twang! The sound was sharp and squeaky.

Dot the ladybug whispered, “Um, maybe we can take turns?”

Gus the snail peeped, “I don’t like loud fighting.”

Riff felt his heart thump. He felt warm in his cheeks. He wanted to shout. He wanted to grab. But he took a deep breath. He remembered what Mama Raccoon taught him. “Use your words. Say how you feel. Ask for what you need. Listen to others.”

Riff held his paws still. He spoke slowly. “Pip, I feel upset when you take my guitar without asking. I worked hard. I practiced. I want a fair turn. Can we talk about it?”

Pip blinked. He tilted his head. “Talk? But I want to sing now.”

Riff nodded. “I hear you. You want to sing now. Singing is fun. I want to play my song too. We can make a plan. We can be fair. That is called justice. Justice means we all get a fair turn.”

Dot nodded. “Fair is good!”

Gus smiled a tiny smile. “I like plans.”

Pip hopped from foot to foot. He looked at the shiny guitar. He looked at Riff’s kind eyes. The sun made the rubber bands sparkle like little rainbows. Pip took a small breath. “Okay. Let’s make a plan.”

Riff spread out a leaf like a paper. He drew three boxes with a stick. “First, I play my new song. Second, you sing your song, Pip. Third, we play together. We can clap for each turn. Clap, clap, clap!”

Dot added, “I can be the time keeper. I will count to ten for each turn.”

Gus said, “I will ring the bell when it’s time to switch.”

Pip’s feathers settled. “That sounds fair. I didn’t mean to grab. I’m sorry, Riff.”

Riff smiled. “Thank you for saying sorry. I forgive you.” He felt lighter. His tail swished to the beat again.

The show began. The sun felt warm on Riff’s back. The grass smelled sweet and green. A tiny breeze brushed his whiskers. Dot counted, “One, two, three…” Gus’s bell went “ding!” Birds on the fence watched with bright eyes.

Riff played his new song. “Ting-ting! Tap-tap! Boing-boing!” It sounded like sunshine and happy feet. Pip listened. He clapped his wings. “Clap, clap!” Dot swayed. Gus hummed softly.

Then it was Pip’s turn. He sang, “Caw-caw, tweet-tweet, trrrr!” His voice was strong like the wind through leaves. Riff listened and smiled. Gus rang the bell. “Ding!” They switched.

For the last turn, they played together. Riff kept a steady beat. Pip sang a soft part and then a loud part. Dot tapped a pebble like a tiny drum. Gus shook the bell gently. Their music blended. It sounded like a backyard hug. Neighbors peeked over the fence. A squirrel cheered. “Yay!”

When the music stopped, everyone clapped. Riff felt proud. Pip hopped close. “Riff, your song was so good. How did you learn it?”

Riff grinned. “Practice. I practiced a little every day. Practice makes our skills grow.”

Pip nodded. “I will practice too. And next time, I will ask before I use your things.”

Riff said, “And I will listen when you have ideas. We can always talk and make a fair plan.”

Dot smiled. “Today we used kind words.”

Gus added, “We used fair turns.”

Riff wrapped his striped tail around his friends in a cozy circle. “We learned that good communication helps everyone. When we say how we feel, listen to others, and make a plan, we find justice and joy. And when we practice, we get better and better.”

The sun dipped a little lower. The grass felt cool. The bees buzzed a sleepy buzz. Riff packed his guitar. Pip tucked his wings. Dot and Gus yawned tiny yawns.

“See you tomorrow,” Riff said. “We can make a new song.”

“Tomorrow!” chirped Pip.

“Tomorrow,” whispered Dot and Gus.

They all smiled, calm and happy, knowing they could talk things through, be fair, and keep practicing together.

The End.
