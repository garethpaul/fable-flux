# Leo the Lion Learns to Ask Nicely

In a cozy study corner filled with colorful books and soft cushions, a brave little lion cub named Leo sat with his mama. The afternoon sun streamed through the window, making golden patches on the carpet where Leo liked to play.

"It's time for reading practice, Leo," said Mama Lion gently, opening a picture book with bright red covers.

Leo's whiskers twitched as he looked at the book, then at his favorite toy truck sitting on the nearby shelf. The shiny blue truck seemed to call to him. "But Mama," Leo said with a small roar, "I want to play with my truck RIGHT NOW!"

Mama Lion's warm brown eyes looked at her little cub. "I understand you want to play, sweetie. But we need to practice reading first."

Leo's tail swished back and forth. He felt frustrated, and his first thought was to stomp his little paws. But then he remembered something Papa Lion had taught him about using his words.

"Mama," Leo said, sitting up tall like a brave lion, "may I please tell you how I feel?"

"Of course, my brave cub," Mama smiled, her voice as warm as honey.

Leo took a deep breath. "I really, really want to play with my truck. Reading feels hard today, and playing would make me happy. Could we maybe find a way to do both?"

Mama Lion's eyes sparkled with pride. "Thank you for telling me how you feel, Leo. That was very brave. Let me think..." She tapped her paw thoughtfully. "What if we read a story about trucks first? Then you could play with your truck for ten minutes before we read another book?"

Leo's golden eyes lit up like sunshine. "Really? A truck story?" He bounced on his cushion, making it squeak softly.

"Yes, and look!" Mama pulled out a book with a big yellow truck on the cover. "This book has trucks that go 'BEEP BEEP' and 'VROOM VROOM!'"

"That sounds perfect!" Leo clapped his little paws together. "Thank you for listening to me, Mama."

As they read the truck book together, Leo made all the engine sounds - "VROOM! BEEP! HONK!" The words seemed easier when they were about something he loved. The pages felt smooth under his paws, and the pictures were so bright and colorful.

After the story, Leo got to play with his blue truck, making it zoom around the soft carpet. "Vroom, vroom!" he giggled, feeling happy and satisfied.

When playtime was over, Leo surprised himself by saying, "Mama, I'm ready for the next book now!"

"My goodness, Leo!" Mama Lion nuzzled his fuzzy head. "You used your words so well today. Instead of getting upset, you told me how you felt and asked for what you needed. That's what big, brave lions do!"

Leo puffed out his little chest proudly. "I used my voice like a roar, but with kind words!"

"Exactly right," Mama Lion said, giving him a warm hug that smelled like sunshine and love. "When we talk about our feelings and listen to each other, we can find solutions that make everyone happy."

From that day on, whenever Leo wanted something, he remembered to use his brave voice and kind words. He learned that asking nicely and explaining his feelings was much better than stomping or roaring angrily.

The End.
