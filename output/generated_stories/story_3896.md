# Splash's Special Room

Splash the dolphin lived in a magical house where sea creatures could visit land. Today was his first day staying in the cozy guest room. The room had soft blue walls that reminded him of the ocean. A big window let in warm sunshine.

But Splash felt confused. At home in the ocean, he had his daily routine. He would swim with his pod, play with seaweed, and rest in his favorite coral cave. Now everything was different.

"I don't know what to do first," Splash said sadly. He flopped on the soft carpet. The room felt too quiet without the sound of ocean waves.

Just then, Bella the sea turtle peeked through the doorway. She was staying in the room next door.

"Good morning, Splash!" Bella said cheerfully. "You look worried. What's wrong?"

"I miss my routine," Splash explained. "At home, I always know what to do. Here, I feel lost."

Bella's wise eyes sparkled. "Maybe we can make a new routine together! Routines help us feel safe and happy."

"Really?" Splash perked up. "But I don't know how to make a routine on land."

"That's okay," Bella said gently. "We can work as a team. I've been here before."

First, Bella showed Splash how to make his bed. The soft blankets felt strange under his flippers, but Bella helped him smooth out the wrinkles.

"Every morning, we can start by making our beds," Bella explained. "It helps us feel organized."

Next, they practiced brushing their teeth at the little sink. Splash kept dropping his toothbrush, but Bella encouraged him to keep trying.

"Don't give up," Bella said. "Persistence means trying again and again until we learn."

Splash tried five more times. Finally, he held the toothbrush just right. "I did it!" he squeaked happily.

For breakfast, they went to the kitchen together. Bella taught Splash how to pour juice without spilling. The sweet orange smell made Splash's tummy rumble.

"After breakfast, we can read books," Bella suggested. "Then we'll have quiet time, just like your rest time in the coral cave."

As the days passed, Splash and Bella worked together every morning. They made their beds, brushed their teeth, ate breakfast, and read stories. When Splash felt frustrated, Bella reminded him to be patient.

"Routines take practice," she would say. "But they make each day feel special."

Soon, Splash began to love his new routine. The guest room started to feel like home. He could make his bed all by himself now. He brushed his teeth without dropping anything. He even helped Bella reach high shelves with his long nose.

"Thank you for teaching me," Splash told Bella one sunny morning. "I was scared of change, but you helped me learn."

"That's what friends do," Bella smiled. "We work together and help each other grow."

Splash looked around the guest room. The blue walls felt cozy now. The soft carpet was perfect for morning stretches. The big window showed him beautiful gardens instead of scary new places.

"I love our routine," Splash said happily. "It makes me feel brave and ready for each new day."

From that day on, Splash knew that routines weren't just rules to follow. They were special ways to take care of himself and feel confident anywhere he went.

The End.
