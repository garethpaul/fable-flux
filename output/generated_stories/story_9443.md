# Polly's Musical Laundry Day

Polly the parrot lived in a cozy house with her family. She loved making music with her beautiful voice. Every morning, she would sing happy songs that made everyone smile.

But today was different. Polly felt grumpy and tired. She had stayed up too late last night playing instead of going to bed on time.

"Squawk! I don't want to help with laundry today!" Polly said crossly when Mom asked her to come to the laundry room.

The laundry room smelled like fresh soap and felt warm from the dryer. Clothes tumbled around and around with a gentle whooshing sound. But Polly just sat on her perch with her feathers all ruffled up.

"I'm too tired to sing," Polly grumbled. "And laundry is boring!"

Mom looked at her little parrot friend. "Oh my, someone sounds cranky today. Did you get enough sleep last night?"

Polly's head drooped down. "No... I played with my toys until very late. Now I feel yucky."

"I understand," Mom said gently. "When we don't get enough sleep, everything feels harder. But maybe we can make laundry time more fun together."

Mom started sorting the colorful clothes into piles. "Look, Polly! Red shirts here, blue pants there. It's like a rainbow!"

Polly perked up a tiny bit. The bright colors were pretty.

Then Mom had an idea. "What if we made up a sorting song? Music makes everything better!"

Polly's eyes sparkled. "Really? A laundry song?"

"Yes! Listen to the washing machine," Mom said. "Swish, swish, swish. Can you make music with that sound?"

Polly listened carefully. The washing machine did make a rhythm! "Swish, swish, swish," she sang softly. "Sort the clothes, sort the clothes, swish, swish, swish!"

"That's beautiful!" Mom clapped. "Now let's add the dryer sound. Tumble, tumble, tumble!"

"Tumble, tumble, tumble," Polly sang louder. "Clean and fresh, clean and fresh, tumble, tumble, tumble!"

Soon Polly was singing a whole laundry song. She made up verses about folding towels and matching socks. Her voice filled the warm room with happy music.

"This is fun!" Polly chirped. "The laundry room sounds like a music room!"

As they worked together, Mom talked to Polly about making good plans. "Tomorrow night, let's plan to put your toys away earlier. Then you can get good sleep and feel happy in the morning."

"Yes!" Polly agreed. "Good sleep helps me sing my best songs!"

They finished folding all the clothes while singing together. Polly felt much better now. Her grumpiness had melted away like soap bubbles.

"Thank you for helping me feel better," Polly said, nuzzling Mom's hand. "I learned that music makes everything more fun, even chores!"

"And what else did you learn?" Mom asked with a smile.

"That getting good sleep is important! And when I feel cranky, I can talk about it instead of being grumpy," Polly said proudly.

From that day on, Polly always planned her bedtime better. And whenever the family did laundry, they sang Polly's special laundry song together. The washing machine and dryer became their backup band!

Polly learned that music could turn any ordinary day into something magical, and that taking care of herself helped her share her gifts with others.

The End.
