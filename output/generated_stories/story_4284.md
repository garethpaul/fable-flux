# Little Eko’s Balcony Garden Parade

Eko was a gentle elephant calf with soft ears and curious eyes. He lived with his family in a cozy home. Outside their window was a small balcony garden. It smelled like mint and sweet basil. Tiny wind chimes tinkled, and colorful pots lined the rail. Sunlight warmed the leaves, and butterflies flickered like tiny flags.

One sunny morning, Mama said, “Eko, our neighbors are bringing plants from their homes. We will make a garden parade!”

Eko’s tail swished. “A parade? On our balcony?” he asked.

“Yes,” Mama smiled. “Everyone brings a plant from their culture. We will learn from each other.”

Soon, neighbors arrived. Mei came with a bamboo plant in a red pot. “In my family, bamboo means strength,” she said.

Ravi brought a small curry leaf plant. “We use these leaves to cook. It smells yummy,” he said, rubbing a leaf. The air filled with spicy, lemony scent.

Ava carried a lavender plant wrapped in purple cloth. “We use lavender for calm. It helps me breathe slow,” she whispered, taking a long, gentle breath.

Samir wheeled in a little olive tree. “My grandma told me olives are for peace,” he said.

Eko clapped his big soft feet. “Welcome, welcome!” he cheered. He showed them his tiny marigold. “My marigold is bright. My grandma says marigold is for happy days.”

They placed the plants along the balcony. The colors made a friendly rainbow: green bamboo, silver olive leaves, purple lavender, glossy curry leaves, and sunny marigolds.

Then the wind pushed the bamboo pot. It wobbled toward the edge.

“Oh no!” cried Mei.

Eko reached out with his trunk. He caught the pot just in time. “Got it!” he said.

But his marigold got bumped. A petal fell. Eko felt his cheeks get hot. His heart thumped. “My flower!” he squeaked. He wanted to stomp.

Ava touched her lavender and said softly, “Smell with me, Eko. In…and out.” She breathed in slow. Eko copied. In…out. The lavender scent was sweet and calm. His tummy felt softer. His feet stopped stomping.

“Thank you,” Eko said. “I felt mad. But I can cool down.” He flapped his ears like little fans.

Mei knelt. “I am sorry about your flower. Let’s fix it together.”

Eko nodded. He looked closely at the fallen petal. “We can try again,” he whispered. He brought fresh water in a small blue cup. He watered the marigold slowly—drip, drip—like tiny rain. He patted the soil with care.

Ravi smiled. “In my family, we say, ‘Try, try again.’ That is persistence.”

Eko repeated the word slowly. “Per-sis-tence. Keep trying.” He felt proud.

They made plant labels. Mei wrote “Bamboo—Strength.” Ravi wrote “Curry Leaf—Flavor.” Ava wrote “Lavender—Calm.” Samir wrote “Olive—Peace.” Eko wrote “Marigold—Joy.”

The balcony smelled like many places at once: spicy, sweet, fresh, and warm like sunshine. The wind chimes sang a tiny song. A butterfly landed on the olive tree, then on Eko’s trunk. It felt like a soft tickle. He giggled.

“Let’s have the parade,” Mama said.

They lined up the pots. Eko led with his marigold. “Step, step, step,” he said. The others followed, gently moving each pot a little, then stopping to share.

Mei bowed and said, “In my family, we say ‘Ni hao’ for hello.” Everyone tried. “Nee how,” they said. They laughed at the new sound.

Ravi said, “We say ‘Namaste’ with our hands.” They pressed hands together. Eko tried. His trunk curled, and everyone smiled.

Ava said, “We count in French: un, deux, trois.” They counted to three. Eko’s ears wiggled on “trois.”

Samir shared a story from his grandma about olive trees and peace. Eko listened with round eyes.

Eko looked around. “Our garden is like a team,” he said. “Different plants. Different words. All friends.”

Just then, a bee buzzed near Eko’s ear. “Bzzzz!” He flinched. The marigold wobbled again. Eko felt a quick spark of anger. He remembered lavender breathing. “In…and out,” he said to himself. He calmed and held the pot steady.

“You did it, Eko,” said Mama. “You used your calm. You cared for your flower and your friends’ plants too.”

“We helped each other,” Mei said.

“That is compassion,” Ava added. “We notice feelings and we are kind.”

They finished the parade with a small snack. Ravi sprinkled curry leaves on rice. Ava set a little lavender cookie. Mei poured cool tea for the grown-ups. Samir shared tiny olives. Eko sniffed, tasted, and smiled. “So many flavors,” he said. “Like our plants.”

As the sun began to rest, the balcony glowed golden. The plants looked cozy. The neighbors waved good-bye in many ways: “Bye-bye!” “Zài jiàn!” “Namaste!” “Au revoir!” “Ma’a salama!”

Eko waved his trunk. His heart felt big and warm. “Today I learned,” he said, “that people have different plants, words, foods, and stories. When we listen and share, we grow together. When I feel mad, I can breathe and try again. I can be gentle and keep going.”

Mama hugged him. The wind chimes sang one more tiny song. The garden was quiet and happy.

The End.
