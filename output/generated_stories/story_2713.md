# Rosie's Rainbow Project

Rosie the rabbit hopped into the bright art room. Colorful paintings covered the walls. Brushes and crayons sat on every table. The room smelled like fresh paint and paper.

"Today we're making a big rainbow mural!" announced Mrs. Bear, their teacher. "Everyone will work together."

Rosie's ears perked up with excitement. She loved rainbows! Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple - all the pretty colors dancing together.

"I can do it all by myself!" Rosie said, grabbing a big paintbrush. "I'm really good at painting!"

She dipped her brush in red paint and started making a huge stripe across the paper. Drip, drip went the paint.

"Wait, Rosie!" called out Sam the squirrel. "We're supposed to work together!"

But Rosie kept painting. She wanted her rainbow to be the best one ever. She reached for the orange paint next.

"Can I help with the orange?" asked Mia the mouse in her tiny voice.

"No thank you," Rosie said quickly. "I've got it!"

She painted and painted. But something wasn't right. Her red stripe was bumpy. The orange was too thick in some spots and too thin in others.

Rosie frowned. Her whiskers twitched with worry. The rainbow didn't look as beautiful as she had imagined.

Across the room, she heard happy giggling. She looked over and saw Ben the bear, Lily the lamb, and Tom the turtle all painting together. Their rainbow was smooth and bright.

"Your red looks perfect!" Ben was saying to Lily.

"And your blue is so pretty!" Lily replied to Tom.

They were smiling and laughing. Their rainbow sparkled like a real one in the sky.

Rosie's ears drooped. Her rainbow looked messy compared to theirs. Paint dripped on the floor. Her paws were covered in colors.

"Oh no," she whispered. A tear rolled down her furry cheek.

Mrs. Bear walked over with a kind smile. "What's wrong, Rosie?"

"My rainbow looks terrible," Rosie sniffled. "I thought I could do it all by myself, but it's too hard."

Mrs. Bear knelt down. "You know what? Rainbows in nature are made of many colors working together. Maybe your art rainbow needs friends too."

Rosie looked at her classmates again. They were still helping each other, sharing brushes and mixing colors.

"Could... could I ask for help?" Rosie asked softly.

"Of course!" Mrs. Bear said. "That's what friends are for."

Rosie took a deep breath. She hopped over to Sam, Mia, Ben, Lily, and Tom.

"Um, excuse me," she said, her voice small. "I'm sorry I didn't want to work together before. Could you help me fix my rainbow?"

"Yes!" they all said together.

Sam helped smooth out the bumpy red stripe. Mia carefully painted thin yellow lines. Ben mixed the perfect purple. Lily added sparkly touches. Tom painted a beautiful blue sky.

Rosie felt warm and happy inside. Working together felt much better than working alone.

"Look!" said Mia, pointing at their rainbow. "It's the most beautiful one ever!"

She was right. Their rainbow was bright and smooth, with perfect colors flowing together.

"Thank you for helping me," Rosie said. "I learned that teamwork makes everything better!"

Mrs. Bear clapped her paws. "What a wonderful rainbow! And what wonderful teamwork!"

All the friends smiled. They had created something beautiful together, and they had helped each other too.

From that day on, Rosie always remembered that the best projects - just like the best rainbows - are made when friends work together.

The End.
