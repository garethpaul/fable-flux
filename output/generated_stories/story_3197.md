# Fawn's Special Dinner Party

Little Fawn had soft brown fur with white spots that sparkled like tiny stars. Her big brown eyes were always kind and gentle. Today was very special because Mama Deer said Fawn could help set the dining room table for their forest friends' dinner party.

"Oh, how exciting!" Fawn said, bouncing on her small hooves. The dining room smelled like fresh pine and sweet berries. Sunlight streamed through the windows, making everything glow golden and warm.

Fawn carefully carried the pretty blue plates to the long wooden table. One, two, three, four plates. But then she heard a knock at the door.

"Hello!" called Rabbit, hopping into the dining room. "I'm here for the party!"

"Me too!" squeaked Mouse, scurrying behind Rabbit.

"And me!" honked Goose, waddling through the doorway.

Fawn looked at the table, then at her three friends. Oh no! She had only set four places - one for herself, one for Mama, and two extras. But now there were three friends who needed seats too!

"I don't know what to do," Fawn whispered to herself, feeling worried. Her ears drooped down. "There aren't enough places for everyone."

Rabbit's nose twitched as he smelled the delicious carrot soup. "Mmm, I'm so hungry!"

Mouse's tiny stomach rumbled loudly. "Me too!"

Goose flapped her wings. "When do we eat?"

Fawn felt sad. She wanted everyone to feel welcome, but she didn't have enough plates or chairs. Then she had a wonderful idea!

"Wait here, friends!" Fawn said. She trotted to the kitchen and found more plates, cups, and napkins. But there still weren't enough chairs.

Fawn thought hard, tapping her hoof on the floor. Then her eyes lit up! She remembered the soft cushions in the living room and the small stools from Mama's sewing corner.

"I can make this work!" Fawn said to herself.

She gathered the cushions and stools, arranging them around the table. The cushions were perfect for Mouse, and the stools were just right for Rabbit. Goose could stand comfortably at the tall end of the table.

"Friends, come see!" Fawn called happily.

When everyone entered the dining room, their eyes grew wide with joy.

"What a creative table!" said Rabbit, settling onto his soft cushion.

"This is perfect for me!" squeaked Mouse from her cozy spot.

"I love standing here!" honked Goose. "I can see everyone!"

Mama Deer smiled proudly at Fawn. "You found a way to make everyone comfortable and welcome. That shows great respect for your friends."

As they shared the warm soup and crunchy salad, Fawn felt her heart grow warm too. She had learned something important: when you care about others and use your creativity, you can solve problems and make everyone feel special.

"Thank you, Fawn," said all her friends together. "You made us feel so welcome!"

Fawn's spots seemed to shimmer even brighter as she smiled. "When we respect each other and work together, everything turns out wonderfully!"

From that day on, whenever Fawn had friends over, she always made sure everyone had a special place at the table, no matter how different their needs were.

The End.
