# Koala Kipp and the Porch Parade

Koala Kipp loved the peaceful front porch. The porch had soft wooden boards that felt warm under Kipp’s paws. A gentle breeze tickled Kipp’s fuzzy ears. The air smelled like sweet leaves and fresh rain. Birds sang, “Tweet-tweet!” and the wind chimes whispered, “Ting-ting.”

Kipp sat on a comfy cushion and took slow, calm breaths. In, out. In, out. His heart felt quiet and happy.

“Good morning, porch,” Kipp said softly.

Kipp liked to move his body every morning. He called it Stretch-and-Sway Time. He reached his paws up high. “Hello, sky.” He touched his toes. “Hello, toes.” He swayed side to side. “Sway, sway,” he hummed. Exercise made Kipp feel strong and cozy at the same time.

Soon, friends came by the porch.

First came Penny the puppy, her tail going thump-thump on the steps. “Hi, Kipp! Can I join your stretches?” she asked, panting happily.

“Yes,” said Kipp with a calm smile. “You are welcome here.”

Penny wagged harder. “I like to jump!” She bounced—boing, boing—making the boards tremble a little.

Kipp nodded. “Jumping is fun. Please jump gently on the porch,” he said. “We share this space.”

Penny listened. “I can be gentle,” she said, and made her jumps small. Boing… boing… soft and light. Kipp felt proud. Respect means caring about others and the place we share.

Next came Tia the turtle, slow and steady. Tap… tap… tap. She carried a bright orange scarf. “Hello, friends,” Tia said. “I am slow. Can I still play?”

Kipp’s eyes were kind. “Yes, Tia. We accept all friends. We move together.”

Penny wagged. “We can match your pace!”

Kipp showed a new move. “Let’s do Turtle Stretches,” he said. “We reach… and we wait.” They reached. They waited. They breathed. In, out. It felt good to go slow.

A shadow slid across the porch. It was Rafi the raccoon, with curious eyes and striped paws. He sniffed the air. “What smells so nice?” he asked.

“Eucalyptus tea,” Kipp replied. The steam curled up like a tiny cloud. “Would you like to join us?”

Rafi shifted his feet. “I like fast moves!” He spun in a circle—whoosh!—and bumped the wind chimes. The chimes clanged loud and sharp.

Penny’s ears drooped. Tia tucked her head a little. Kipp put a paw to his heart. “Rafi,” he said calmly, “we are glad you are here. On the porch, we move with care. Respect means checking on others. Are they safe? Are they comfortable?”

Rafi blinked. “I didn’t check,” he said. “I was too excited.”

“It’s okay to be excited,” said Kipp. “Let’s try a new plan. When we want to do a big move, we ask first.”

Rafi nodded. “Can I do a spin if I stand over here?” He pointed to a wide space by the rail.

Kipp smiled. “Yes, thank you for asking.” Penny and Tia smiled too. Respect feels warm, like sunshine on fur and shell.

They made a Porch Parade. Kipp clapped a gentle beat. Clap… clap… clap. Penny did tiny jumps. Boing… boing. Tia waved her orange scarf, slow and bright. Rafi spun in his safe space—whoosh—softly this time. Kipp swayed and stretched, keeping time. The porch felt peaceful again.

Just then, a breeze curled by and lifted Tia’s scarf. The scarf floated toward the steps. “Oh!” Tia cried softly.

“I’ll get it!” said Rafi, ready to dash.

Kipp raised his paw. “Wait. Porch rules. Please ask.”

Rafi took a breath. “May I run to catch the scarf?”

“Yes,” said Kipp. “Thank you for asking.”

Rafi zipped, but with careful feet. Tap-tap, soft and quick. He caught the scarf and brought it back with a proud grin.

“Thank you, Rafi,” Tia said. “That was kind.”

“I felt fast and respectful,” Rafi said. He puffed in and out, happy from the exercise.

They took a water break. Kipp poured cool water into little cups. Sip, sip. The cups made tiny clinks on the table.

Penny wagged. “We are different,” she said. “I like to jump. Tia likes slow. Rafi likes fast. And Kipp likes calm.”

Kipp nodded. “And we respect each other. Respect means listening, asking, and taking turns. Acceptance means we welcome our friends just as they are. Exercise means we move our bodies in ways that help everyone feel safe.”

The sun peeked from behind a cloud and warmed the porch. In that golden light, they tried one more move together. Kipp said, “Let’s do the Kindness Pose.”

They stood tall. They stretched their arms wide like open doors. They smiled. “We make space,” Kipp said. “We care. We share.”

The wind chimes sang softly, ting-ting-ting. The birds answered, tweet-tweet. The porch felt peaceful and proud.

Penny whispered, “I feel calm inside.”

Tia said, “I feel strong.”

Rafi said, “I feel respected.”

Kipp looked at his friends. “I feel grateful,” he said. “Thank you for joining me on the porch today.”

They cleaned up together—folded the scarf, stacked the cups, and patted the cushion. The boards were tidy. The air was sweet and quiet again.

Kipp took one more calm breath. In, out. He smiled at his friends. “Remember,” he said, “respect means we look, we listen, and we ask. When we do that, everyone belongs.”

They waved goodbye and promised to meet again for another Porch Parade.

The End.
