# Ollie Otter’s Tidy Time

In a sunny backyard, there was a small garden shed that smelled like fresh wood and minty soap. The shed had a squeaky door, a cool dirt floor, and neat shelves with jars, brushes, and shiny tools. A playful otter named Ollie lived nearby by the pond, and he loved to visit the shed.

“Ooo, look at all the fun things!” Ollie giggled, wiggling his whiskers. He held a soft paintbrush to his cheek. “It’s tickly!” He tapped a tin watering can. “Clink, clank!” He sniffed a bag of sweet soil. “Mmm, it smells like rain.”

Today, Ollie had a big job. Grandma Dot, a sweet old turtle with bright eyes, asked, “Ollie dear, can you help tidy the shed before the garden picnic? We need the shed nice and neat by snack time.”

Ollie clapped his paws. “Yes! I can help! I’m a helper otter!”

Grandma Dot smiled and gave him a list. “Let’s follow directions. First, stack the flower pots. Next, sweep the floor. Then, sort the tools into bins. Last, wipe the table. We have one hour. I’ll check in after each step.”

Ollie nodded hard. “Stack, sweep, sort, wipe. I can do it on time!”

He bounced into the shed. The clock on the wall went tick-tock, tick-tock. Sunlight made warm stripes on the floor. Ollie picked up one pot, two pots, three pots. “Stack, stack, stack!” he sang.

Then he saw a long ribbon. It was shiny blue. “Ooo! Ribbon game!” Ollie wrapped it around a broom like a bow. He twirled. He giggled. Time went by. Tick-tock, tick-tock.

Grandma Dot peeked in. “How’s step one?”

Ollie looked at the floor. Pots were wobbly and the ribbon was everywhere. His whiskers drooped. “Oh… I got busy with the ribbon.”

Grandma Dot nodded kindly. “It’s okay, dear. Let’s lead our time. You can be the leader. Leaders follow directions and help others. Try making a plan.”

“A plan?” Ollie blinked.

“Yes. A simple plan. Say the step. Do the step. Then say ‘done!’ and check it off. I’ll be your helper. You can tell me what to do.”

Ollie took a brave breath. “Okay! Step one: stack the pots. I’m the leader. Grandma, please hold the big pot. I will put the medium one inside. Plop! Now the small one. Plip!”

Together they went plop, plip, plop, plip. The pots made a friendly clunk. Ollie pointed to the list. “Step one, done!”

Tick-tock, tick-tock.

“Step two: sweep the floor,” Ollie said. He held the broom. “Grandma, please move the box. I will sweep the dirt pile.” Swish, swish. The broom smelled dusty and clean. Swish, swish. The floor felt smooth under his paws. “Step two, done!”

Tick-tock, tick-tock.

“Step three: sort the tools into bins,” Ollie said, feeling taller. He read the labels. “Green bin is for garden gloves. Red bin is for rakes. Blue bin is for brushes.” He touched each tool. “Gloves are soft. Brushes are tickly. Rakes go clink!”

Grandma Dot asked, “Where does the little trowel go?”

Ollie looked at the picture. “It goes in the red bin with rakes? Hmm.” He paused. “No, the picture shows small hand tools in the yellow bin. Thanks for asking! We are following directions.”

Clink, rustle, tap. “Step three, done!”

Tick-tock, tick-tock.

“Step four: wipe the table,” Ollie said. He dipped a cloth in soapy water. The bubbles smelled like lemons. Pop, pop, pop! He wiped in little circles. “Round and round. Round and round.” The wood shone. “Step four, done!”

Grandma Dot smiled wide. “Listen. Do you hear it?”

Ollie listened. The clock went tick… tock… and stopped at the hour. Birds chirped. The shed felt cool and calm. “We finished on time!” he cheered.

Just then, two friends peeked in: Benny the bunny and Mia the mouse. “Wow, it’s so tidy,” said Benny. “Who was the leader?” asked Mia.

Ollie stood straight. “I was the leader. I made a plan. I said the step. I did the step. I said ‘done!’ We followed directions. That saved time.”

Benny’s ears wiggled. “Can you lead us to set up the picnic?”

Ollie nodded. “Yes! We will use our plan.” He tapped the list and made a new one on a small card. “First, lay the blanket. Next, put out cups. Then, bring the fruit. Last, clean up.”

He looked at his friends. “Benny, please shake the blanket. Mia, please stack the cups. I will carry the strawberries.”

They worked together. Fluff, clatter, sweet berry smell. Ollie checked each step. “Blanket, done. Cups, done. Fruit, done.” When they finished, he said, “Last step: clean up,” and they put away the basket.

Grandma Dot brought lemonade and crunchy carrot sticks. “You used your time well,” she said. “You led with kindness. You followed directions. You finished on time.”

Ollie sipped. “Lemon is zippy!” He felt proud and warm inside. The shed was neat. The picnic was ready. His friends were smiling.

He looked at the clock and at his list. “A plan helps me do my jobs. One step, then the next. Say it, do it, done.”

Grandma Dot nodded. “That’s smart time-management.”

Ollie giggled and wiggled his whiskers. “I’m a helper otter and a leader otter!”

The friends laughed and shared strawberries as the sun made the garden glow.

The End.
