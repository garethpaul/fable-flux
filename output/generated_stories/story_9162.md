# Bunny's Beautiful Garden

Bunny loved her little balcony garden. She had planted colorful flowers in pretty pots and watched them grow every day. The red roses smelled sweet, the yellow sunflowers stood tall, and the purple violets looked like tiny jewels.

One sunny morning, Bunny hopped outside to water her plants. "Good morning, my beautiful flowers!" she said cheerfully. But when she looked around, her happy smile turned into a big frown.

"Oh no!" Bunny gasped. Her neighbor's cat had knocked over three flower pots! Dirt was scattered everywhere, and her precious flowers were lying on their sides.

Bunny's ears turned red with anger. Her little paws clenched into fists. "That naughty cat ruined my garden!" she shouted. "I'm so mad! I want to hop over there and tell that cat exactly what I think!"

Bunny stomped her foot hard on the balcony floor. *Thump!* She was about to march next door when she heard a gentle voice.

"Hello, little rabbit," said Mrs. Chen, the elderly lady who lived downstairs. She was tending to her own beautiful garden filled with colorful lanterns and bamboo plants. "I heard some noise. Is everything okay?"

"My garden is ruined!" Bunny cried, pointing to the mess. "I'm so angry I could scream!"

Mrs. Chen climbed the stairs slowly and looked at the scattered dirt and fallen flowers. "I can see why you're upset," she said kindly. "When I feel very angry, I take three deep breaths. Would you like to try it with me?"

Bunny sniffled. "Will that really help?"

"Let's see," Mrs. Chen smiled. "Breathe in slowly through your nose... smell the sweet flowers... now breathe out through your mouth. Let's do it together."

Bunny breathed in deeply. She could smell the roses and feel the warm sunshine on her fur. She breathed out slowly. They did this three times together.

"How do you feel now?" Mrs. Chen asked.

"A little better," Bunny admitted. Her ears weren't red anymore, and her paws had unclenched.

"Good! Now, when we feel calmer, we can think of good solutions," Mrs. Chen explained. "In my home country, we have a saying: 'A peaceful heart grows the most beautiful garden.'"

Bunny looked at the mess again, but this time she felt more hopeful. "Maybe I can replant my flowers! But what about the cat?"

"Perhaps we could build a small fence around your garden," Mrs. Chen suggested. "And maybe leave some cat toys on the other side so the kitty has something fun to play with instead."

Bunny's eyes lit up. "That's a wonderful idea! Could you teach me how to make a pretty fence like the ones in your garden?"

Together, they carefully replanted Bunny's flowers. Mrs. Chen showed her how to make a small bamboo fence with colorful ribbons. They even made a little play area with jingling bells for the neighbor's cat.

When they finished, Bunny's garden looked more beautiful than ever! The flowers stood proudly in their pots, protected by the lovely fence.

"Thank you for helping me feel calm and find a good solution," Bunny said, giving Mrs. Chen a big hug.

"Remember," Mrs. Chen said with a warm smile, "when we feel angry, we can always take deep breaths and think of peaceful ways to solve our problems."

From that day on, whenever Bunny felt angry, she would take three deep breaths and look for kind solutions. Her garden grew more beautiful each day, and she even became friends with the neighbor's cat!

The End.
