# Momo’s Warm Kitchen Promise

Momo was a courageous monkey with bright eyes and quick hands. One sunny morning, Momo padded into a warm kitchen. The kitchen smelled like cinnamon toast and sweet oranges. Pots hummed softly on the stove. Sunlight danced on the floor.

Momo’s tummy rumbled. “I want breakfast!” he said.

On the counter, Momo saw two choices. One bowl had colorful sugar sprinkles. The other had a rainbow of fruits—red strawberries, green kiwi, orange slices, and a banana. There was also a little bowl of oats and a cup of cool milk.

Momo scratched his head. “Hmm. Which is better for my body?” he wondered.

Nana Bird fluttered in through the window. “Good morning, Momo!” she chirped. “What are you choosing?”

“I want the sprinkles,” said Momo. “But the fruit looks friendly too.”

Nana tilted her head. “Let’s breathe first,” she said. “In… out… in… out.” They took slow belly breaths. The warm kitchen felt calm.

“Now,” Nana said softly, “what does your body need?”

Momo placed his hand on his tummy. He listened. Gurgle, gurgle. “I think it needs energy to climb, jump, and help.”

“Fruits and oats give strong, slow energy,” said Nana. “Sprinkles are a sometimes treat.”

Momo nodded. “I can be brave and choose the fuel I need.”

He washed his hands with warm, bubbly soap. Rub-a-dub, rinse, dry. “Clean hands help keep everyone safe,” he said. “That’s my kitchen promise.”

Momo looked at a small safety poster on the fridge. It had a picture of hands and a smile. “Clean hands are kind hands,” it read. Momo grinned. “I like helping my kitchen stay healthy.”

Momo set out a little cutting board and a child-safe knife. “I will practice,” he said. “Skill-building time!”

He peeled the banana. Soft and sweet. He sliced the strawberries. Red and juicy. He scooped oats into a bowl. Scratch, scoop, plop. He poured milk, slow and steady. Splash!

“Listen,” Momo said. “The oats whisper.” The pot on the stove went blub-blub as the oats warmed. The kitchen filled with a cozy, toasty smell.

Tap-tap! Neighbor Turtle knocked at the door. “Good morning,” he said slowly. “May I come in?”

“Yes!” said Momo. “But please wash your hands.” He showed Turtle the sink and the soap. They sang a short wash song together. “Wash the tops, wash the bottoms, between the fingers too!”

“That is good for our town,” Turtle said. “We care for each other.”

Nana smiled. “That’s civic responsibility—helping our community be healthy.”

Momo stirred the oats. “I’m making a rainbow bowl,” he said. “Red, orange, green, yellow!” He sprinkled in tiny chia seeds like black stars. He added a spoon of yogurt, thick and cool.

Turtle sniffed. “Mmm. It smells warm and safe.”

Momo looked again at the sprinkles. “They look fun,” he said. “But I will choose fruit today.” He took a mindful breath. “I feel proud.”

Nana clapped her wings. “Good decision-making!”

Momo set the table. Plink, plink went the spoons. He put napkins in neat squares. He poured water into cups. Glug, glug. He sliced an extra orange to share.

“Sharing is caring,” Momo said. “Everyone gets a turn.”

They sat together. “Before we eat, let’s pause,” Nana said. They all closed their eyes for a moment.

Momo whispered, “I see the colors. I smell the cinnamon. I feel the warm bowl. I hear the gentle bubbles. I taste… ready!”

He took a bite. “Mmm!” The banana was creamy. The strawberries were bright. The oats were soft and cozy. His body felt happy and strong.

Turtle chewed slowly. “This food helps my feet feel steady.”

After breakfast, Momo washed the bowls. Swish, swish. He wiped the table with a clean cloth. Wipe, wipe. He put peels in the compost bin. “The garden will like this,” he said.

“That is helping the Earth,” Nana said. “Another good choice.”

Suddenly, Momo heard a rumble outside. The little playground sign had fallen over in the wind. “Oh no!” he said. “Kids might trip.”

Momo tied on his tiny apron like a superhero cape. “I can help.” He took a mindful breath. “Safe first.” He asked Nana and Turtle to come along.

They carried the sign carefully together. Lift, steady, set. Momo used a little hand shovel to pack the soil firm. Pat, pat, pat. He felt strong and calm from his good breakfast.

“Teamwork!” cheered Nana.

“Thank you, Momo,” said a neighbor bunny. “You helped our whole street.”

Momo smiled. His tummy felt cozy, and his heart felt big. He waved. “I made a good choice. I ate food that helps me think, move, and help others.”

Back in the warm kitchen, Momo drew a picture of his rainbow bowl and put it on the fridge. He wrote, with big careful letters, “Fuel for brave bodies.”

Nana read it aloud. “Fuel for brave bodies.”

Momo nodded. “Today I learned: I can pause, breathe, and choose foods that help me grow. I can practice skills like washing, cutting, and cleaning. I can help my home and my town.”

Turtle smiled. “Small choices make a big, healthy day.”

Momo hugged them both. The kitchen smelled like oranges and cinnamon. The sun made golden squares on the floor. Momo felt ready for new adventures.

The End.
