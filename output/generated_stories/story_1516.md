# Polly's Bright Idea

Polly the parrot loved colors. Her bright green feathers sparkled, and her orange beak gleamed as she perched on the washing machine in the cozy laundry room. The room smelled like fresh soap bubbles and warm, clean clothes.

"Squawk! What a mess!" Polly chirped, looking around with her bright black eyes.

The laundry room was very messy. Dirty clothes were piled high on the floor. Empty soap bottles sat in corners. Old towels were scattered everywhere. The washing machine hummed loudly, but it looked sad and tired.

Polly's family was always so busy. Mom rushed in to throw clothes in the washer. Dad hurried to move wet clothes to the dryer. Big sister Emma grabbed clean clothes and ran to her room. Nobody had time to organize or clean up properly.

"This room needs help," Polly said to herself, ruffling her colorful feathers. "But I'm just a small parrot. How can I make a difference?"

Polly thought and thought. Then she had a wonderful idea! She began to squawk loudly and clearly.

"Help! Help! Cooperation needed! Squawk!"

Mom came running. "What's wrong, Polly?"

Polly pointed her wing at the messy room. "Squawk! Room needs teamwork! Family help together!"

Mom looked around and gasped. "Oh my! You're absolutely right, Polly. This room is a disaster!"

Soon, Dad and Emma came to see what was happening.

"Polly says we need to work together," Mom explained. "She's right – we all use this room, so we should all help take care of it."

"But I don't know how to organize," Emma said, looking worried.

Polly flew to Emma's shoulder. "Squawk! Polly help! We learn together!"

Dad smiled. "That's a great idea. Let's make it fun!"

The family began working as a team. Polly helped by sorting clothes with her beak – whites in one pile, colors in another. She was very good at spotting tiny socks hiding under big towels!

"Look!" Polly squawked excitedly. "Save empty bottles! Make bird feeders!"

"What a smart idea!" said Mom. "We can reuse these instead of throwing them away."

Emma carefully cut holes in the plastic bottles while Dad helped. They filled them with birdseed to hang outside. "We're helping the environment AND the birds!" Emma cheered.

Polly taught everyone her special system. "Squawk! Dirty clothes in basket! Clean clothes folded neat! Soap bottles in recycling!"

As they worked together, the room began to sparkle. The washing machine seemed to hum more happily. The sweet smell of lavender soap filled the air. Sunlight streamed through the clean window, making everything glow.

"This is so much better when we all help," said Dad, wiping his forehead.

"And it's more fun too!" added Emma, giving Polly a gentle pet.

From that day on, the family had a new rule. Every Saturday morning, they would spend thirty minutes together in the laundry room. Polly would perch on her special stand and guide them with happy squawks.

"Thank you, Polly," Mom said, giving her a sunflower seed treat. "You taught us that speaking up for what's right is important. And working together makes everything easier!"

Polly preened her feathers proudly. "Squawk! Together we're strong! Cooperation makes everything better!"

The laundry room stayed clean and organized, and the whole family felt proud of their teamwork.

The End.
