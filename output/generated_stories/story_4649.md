# Sunny's Kitchen Helpers

The warm kitchen smelled like cinnamon toast and sweet apples. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. A soft kettle hummed on the stove. In the middle of it all stood Sunny, a small, resourceful squirrel with a fluffy tail and bright, curious eyes.

Sunny lived with Nana May, who hummed happy songs and wore a blue apron with tiny sunflower buttons. This morning, Nana May looked around and sighed. “Oh dear,” she said kindly. “The kitchen is a jumble.”

Sunny twitched his whiskers. He saw bowls on the counter, flour on the rug, and spoons hiding under napkins. The fruit basket tipped to one side, and apples had rolled under a chair.

Sunny felt a tiny flutter in his tummy. “It’s a big mess,” he whispered. “Maybe too big.”

Nana May smiled and patted Sunny’s paw. “We can do it together,” she said. “Small steps make big help.”

Sunny took a deep breath. He loved to help. He loved to solve problems too. He looked around with sharp squirrel eyes. “First,” Sunny said, “we will sort. Sort means put like things together.”

Nana May nodded. “That sounds smart.”

Sunny scampered across the warm floor. Tap-tap, went his quick feet. He picked up spoons and placed them in a shiny bowl. Clink, clink! He gathered napkins in a neat stack. Fold, fold!

Then Sunny heard a soft bump. The apples! They sat under the chair, like round red moons. “I can get you,” Sunny said. He stretched his little body and rolled an apple out with his paw. Thump. “One!” He reached for another. Thump. “Two!” He made a fun game of it. His tail flicked side to side. It felt like exercise for a strong, helpful squirrel.

Nana May laughed. “You are a rolling champion,” she said.

Sunny puffed his chest with pride. Helping felt good. He liked that feeling.

He looked at the flour on the rug. “We need a sweep-sweep dance,” Sunny decided. He picked up a small brush and began to sweep in soft circles. Swish, swish. He made the broom sway like music. “Dance, dance, sweep, sweep!” he sang.

Nana May joined him, tapping her toe. “I like your dance,” she said. “You found a fun way to move and help.”

Just then, the door clicked. Two little friends came to visit. Dot the ladybug buzzed in with tiny wings. Pip the puppy trotted in, tail wagging. “Hi!” Pip barked. “It smells like apples.”

“It smells like hugs,” Dot giggled.

Sunny felt his whiskers wiggle. He wanted to show them the kitchen. But he also felt shy about the mess. Would they think it was too messy?

Dot landed softly on Sunny’s shoulder. “We can help,” she said. “Helping makes friends feel good.”

Pip nodded. “We are a team!” His paws made soft, happy thuds on the floor.

Sunny smiled. “Thank you,” he said. “We have a plan. First sort, then put away.”

Sunny pointed with his paw. “Dot, can you gather the tiny things? Like buttons and clips?”

“Yes!” Dot zipped gently from place to place. Tap, tap, went her tiny feet. “Little things, come with me,” she sang.

“Pip,” Sunny said, “you are strong. Can you push the fruit basket so it stands tall?”

“Woof-yes!” Pip pushed carefully with his nose. The basket sat straight. The apples looked safe and comfy. Pip wagged so hard his ears flopped. “I did it!”

Sunny watched, then went back to sweeping. Swish, swish. He made tidy lines. “When we help, we feel calm,” he said softly. “We can see what we need.”

He opened a drawer. It was messy, too. Forks mixed with crayons, and a ribbon curled around a potato chip clip. Sunny frowned, then nodded. “Organize time,” he said. “Forks here. Clips here. Crayons in a cup.”

Nana May clapped once. “Wonderful organizing,” she said. “Everything has a home.”

Dot paused. “What about the ribbon?” she asked.

Sunny thought. He didn’t want anyone to feel left out, not even a ribbon. “The ribbon can tie the napkins,” he said. “Every helper has a job.”

He tied the napkins with a gentle tug. They looked neat and pretty. Sunny felt warm inside, like the sunlight squares on the floor.

At last, the counters gleamed. The spoons bundled together. The apples shone in their basket. The rug felt soft and clean under their feet and paws.

Nana May opened the oven. Warm air floated out, smelling like cinnamon and sweet apples. “Apple toast for my helpers,” she said.

They sat at the little table. Crunch went the toast. Mmm went everyone. Pip’s tail thumped a happy beat. Dot dabbed a crumb with her tiny foot. Sunny licked a bit of cinnamon from his whisker and giggled.

Nana May raised her cup of milk. “To helpful hearts,” she said.

Sunny raised his cup, too. “To helpful hearts,” he echoed. “Helping makes work lighter, friends closer, and rooms happier.”

Dot buzzed. “And organizing helps us find things fast!”

Pip wagged. “And cleaning can be a fun exercise,” he barked, bumping the broom with his nose.

Sunny nodded. “And we accept every helper,” he said. “Big or small, winged or waggy. Everyone can help in their own way.”

The kitchen was warm and bright. Sunny’s heart felt the same.

The End.
