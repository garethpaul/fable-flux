# Otter Ollie’s Porch Plan

Otter Ollie liked to wiggle and giggle. He lived by a quiet river, but today he sat on a peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like warm wood and sweet flowers. The wind made the chimes sing, “Ting-ting-ting,” and the sun made little sparkles on the steps.

Ollie wiggled his whiskers. “I have a big day!” he said. “I want to paint a picture, build a block tower, and share a snack with friends.” He tapped his paws on the porch. Tap-tap. “But when should I do each thing?”

Mama Otter brought out a small basket. It carried crayons, blocks, and juicy apple slices. “Good morning, Ollie,” she said softly. “You can plan your time. A little plan helps a big day.”

Ollie’s eyes shone. “A plan? Like a map?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Let’s make a porch plan. First, we paint. Next, we build. Then, we share a snack.”

Ollie clapped. “Paint, build, snack! I can do that.” He set out paper. The paper felt smooth. The crayons smelled waxy and bright. “Swish, swish!” Ollie drew a yellow sun and a blue river.

Just then, friends came up the porch path. Tilly Turtle waved slowly. “Hello, Ollie.” Her shell was green and shiny. Benny Bunny bounced. “Hi, hi, hi!” His ears wiggled like tiny flags. Sora Sparrow chirped. “Tweet! Can we play?”

Ollie smiled big. “Yes! I am painting first. Then I will build. Then we will snack.” He pointed to the basket like a helper.

Benny bounced closer. “I want to build now! Block tower! Tall, tall!”

Tilly blinked. “I move slow. I like to paint careful lines.”

Sora fluffed her small feathers. “I like to sing while we snack. Tweet-tweet!”

Ollie felt a wiggle in his tummy. His plan felt wobbly. Everyone wanted something different. He squeezed his crayon. “What do we do?” he asked. The wind went “whoosh,” and the chimes went “ting.”

Mama Otter knelt down. “You can use peaceful words,” she said. “Ask, listen, and find a way that feels fair. You can take turns. You can do a little of each. You can make room for differences.”

Ollie took a deep breath. In… out… “Okay.” He turned to his friends. “Let’s use gentle words. Let’s listen.”

Benny’s feet thumped. “I want blocks now.”

Tilly tapped her brush. “I like to paint now.”

Sora peeped. “Snack time songs!”

Ollie nodded. “We all want different things. That’s okay. We can make a porch plan together.” He put the crayons down and held up one paw. “How about this: We paint for five little songs. Then we build for five little songs. Then we snack and sing.”

Tilly tilted her head. “Five little songs? I can count them slowly.”

Benny wiggled. “Can I tap the chimes to count?”

Sora chirped. “I will sing the songs!”

Ollie smiled. “Yes! We can do it together.”

They began with painting. Sora sang a soft song, “La-la, tweet-tweet,” and the wind made the chimes go “ting” with the beat. Ollie drew a river otter with big sparkly eyes. Tilly painted tiny flowers with careful dots. Benny didn’t love sitting still, but he used a big blue crayon to make a fast sky. “Swish! Swish!” he said, giggling.

After five little songs, Ollie said, “Time to build!” Benny cheered, “Blocks!” Tilly smiled. “I will help with the base.” Sora flapped. “I will count the blocks.”

They stacked blocks on the warm porch boards. Tap. Tap. Tap. Benny put a tall block on top. It wobbled. “Uh-oh,” he said. Tilly slid a flat block under the tower. “I am slow, but steady,” she said.

Ollie looked at the wobble. “I have an idea. We can use two towers and a bridge.” He handed blocks to everyone. Benny made one tower fast. Tilly made one tower strong. Ollie made a bridge that clicked into place. Sora cheered, “Tweet! We did it!” The tower stood tall and safe.

After five songs, Sora chirped, “Snack time!” Ollie brought out the apple slices. They smelled sweet and crunchy. He set out four cups of cool water. “Clink,” went the cups on the porch.

They sat in a circle. Benny munched, “Crunch-crunch.” Tilly nibbled, “Munch.” Sora sang a happy snack song. Ollie licked a drop of juice from his paw. “Mmm!”

Mama Otter smiled from the door. “How did your porch plan go?”

Ollie puffed with pride. “We used peaceful words. We took turns. We made room for our differences. Benny likes fast. Tilly likes slow. Sora likes songs. I like plans. We worked together.”

Benny nodded. “I built tall!”

Tilly patted her brush. “I painted fine.”

Sora fluffed. “I sang time!”

Ollie hugged his friends. The porch felt warm and kind. The chimes sang, and the wind smelled like flowers and wood. “I learned something,” he said. “When we listen and share, we find a way that is fair.”

Mama Otter said, “That is a wonderful lesson. Peaceful solutions make happy hearts.”

Ollie packed the crayons, stacked the blocks, and put the cups in the basket. “Plan, play, and peace,” he said. “That is my porch way.”

They waved goodbye as the sun made long golden lines across the steps. Ollie curled up on the porch and sighed a soft, happy sigh.

Peaceful words, kind hearts, and good plans make a bright day.

The End.
