# Bunny’s Cozy Clean-Up

Bunny lived in a cozy bedroom with soft pillows, warm light, and a little window that let in the morning sun. The bedroom smelled like clean blankets and sweet soap. On the shelf were crayons, paper, and a small drum. On the floor were blocks, books, and a red toy car. Bunny was a determined rabbit. When Bunny had an idea, Bunny tried and tried until it worked.

One bright morning, Bunny bounced out of bed. “Today I will make a beautiful picture for my friend Squirrel,” Bunny said. “Squirrel is kind and funny. I want to show I care.”

Bunny hopped to the shelf. “Oh no,” Bunny whispered. The crayons were mixed with buttons. Papers were under socks. The drum was hiding under a blanket. Bunny wrinkled a little nose. “It’s too messy. I can’t find my blue crayon!”

Bunny’s ears drooped. “I want to make Squirrel’s picture,” Bunny said softly. “But I don’t know where to start.”

Tap, tap! Bunny heard little paws at the door. “It’s me,” squeaked Squirrel. “Can I come in?”

Bunny opened the door. Squirrel peeked into the cozy bedroom and blinked. “Wow,” Squirrel said kindly. “You have many fun things.”

“I want to draw you a picture,” Bunny said. “But I can’t find my things. It feels too big.”

Squirrel nodded. “Let’s do it together,” Squirrel said. “Friends help friends.”

Bunny took a deep breath. The room smelled like fresh air from the window. “Yes,” Bunny said. “We can do it. I am determined!”

They set a plan. Bunny said, “We will make little homes for things.” Squirrel clapped. “Homes for things! That is smart.”

Bunny found three baskets. One was soft and fuzzy. One was smooth and shiny. One had tiny stars. Bunny labeled them with big, simple drawings. Bunny drew a crayon on one basket. Bunny drew a block on another basket. Bunny drew a book on the last basket.

“Crayons in the crayon basket,” Bunny said. “Blocks in the block basket. Books in the book basket.”

Squirrel wiggled a fluffy tail. “I can help. I will put the blocks in the block basket.” Squirrel picked up blue blocks, red blocks, and yellow blocks. They clacked together with a happy sound.

Bunny gathered crayons—tick, tick, tick—as the crayons tapped the basket. “I found the blue crayon!” Bunny sang.

They hummed a soft song while they worked. The paper made a whisper sound. The soft blanket swished as Bunny folded it. The little drum went tap-tap as Squirrel moved it to the shelf.

Suddenly Squirrel saw the red car. “Vroom?” Squirrel asked politely. “Is it okay if I roll it to the shelf?”

Bunny smiled. “Thank you for asking. Yes, please. That is respectful.”

Squirrel rolled the car slowly, so it did not bump the books. “I go slowly so I don’t hurt your things,” Squirrel said.

Bunny felt warm and happy. “You are a good friend,” Bunny said. “You are careful and kind.”

Soon, the floor was clear. The bed was smooth. The shelf looked neat. The air felt light and nice.

“Now we can make your picture,” Squirrel said.

Bunny set out the crayons and paper. “Would you like to draw too?” Bunny asked.

Squirrel nodded. “Yes, please. I will draw you a picture too.”

They sat together on a soft rug. Bunny’s paw went swish-swish on the paper. Squirrel’s paw went tick-tick. Bunny drew a tall tree, a bright sun, and Squirrel holding a nut. Bunny used the blue crayon, the green crayon, and a little bit of orange. Squirrel drew Bunny with big ears and a smile.

“I love your picture,” Bunny said. “Thank you.”

“I love yours too,” Squirrel said. “Thank you for sharing your crayons.”

Bunny put the finished pictures on the wall with gentle tape. The pictures looked cheerful and colorful on the clean wall.

“Do you know what helped?” Bunny asked.

Squirrel tilted a head. “What?”

“Friends work together,” Bunny said. “We talked. We asked. We listened. We were careful with each other’s things. We made little homes for our toys. Now we can find everything.”

Squirrel nodded. “That is organization,” Squirrel said slowly, trying the big word. “Or-ga-ni-za-tion!”

Bunny giggled. “Yes! Organization helps us play and create.”

They made a simple chart with stickers. Bunny drew a sun for morning clean-up and a moon for evening clean-up. They put star stickers for each time they tidied up. “This will help us remember,” Bunny said. “We can be resourceful.”

Squirrel pointed to the stickers. “And it is creative too!”

They hugged. The room felt cozy and bright. The window let in the last warm light of the day. Bunny’s heart felt full.

“Thank you for helping me,” Bunny said. “You are my friend.”

“And you are mine,” Squirrel said. “I respect your things. You respect mine. We help each other.”

Bunny smiled a big, calm smile. “When we are kind and organized, we have more time to make beautiful things.”

They looked at their pictures one more time. The colors shined softly. The room smelled like paper and crayons and clean blankets. It felt safe and happy.

Together they whispered, “Friends help friends.”

The End.
