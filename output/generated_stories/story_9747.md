# Owl Who Set the Table for the World

In a cozy dining room with a sunny window, a wise old owl named Olive fluffed her soft gray feathers. The table was big and round. It smelled like warm toast and sweet apples. Sunlight made tiny dust sparkles dance. Olive blinked her bright golden eyes and said, “Hoo-hoo, today we set the table for the world!”

Her little friends, Pip the puppy and Miri the mouse, ran in on quick feet. Pip’s paws went tap-tap on the wooden floor. Miri’s whiskers twitched. “For the world?” asked Pip. His tail wagged fast. “That is a very big table!”

Olive smiled. “We can think big even in a small room. We can care for friends near and far. That is called being a global citizen.”

Pip tilted his head. “Glow-ball? Does it glow?”

Miri giggled. “Global means the whole wide world.”

Olive nodded. “Yes. We use kind words. We share. We keep things clean and safe. We move our bodies to help. We work together.”

Pip bounced. “I can move! I can help!” He did three little jumps. “Hop, hop, hop!”

“Wonderful,” said Olive. “First, we set the table. Each plate is for a place in our world.”

Olive took out plates that smelled like soap and sunshine. “This blue plate is for the big blue oceans,” she said. “This green plate is for forests. This yellow plate is for sunny deserts. This white plate is for snowy mountains.”

Miri patted the blue plate. “It’s cool and smooth,” she said. “Like a calm wave.”

Pip reached for the green plate. “I want this one!” he barked.

Miri also reached. “I want the green one too!”

They both grabbed the plate. It slipped with a squeak. Pip frowned. Miri squeaked. The room felt tight and quiet.

Olive raised one gentle wing. “Stop. Breathe in like the wind.” Pip and Miri breathed in slowly. “Breathe out like the sea.” They exhaled. Their shoulders relaxed.

Olive said softly, “We have a problem. Let’s solve it with kind words. Pip, you speak. Then Miri, you speak.”

Pip wagged slowly. “I want the green plate because I love trees.”

Miri nodded. “I want the green plate because I love grass to run in.”

Olive hooted. “You both love green places. You are the same in your hearts. How can we share?”

Pip thought. “We can take turns?”

Miri squeaked, “Yes! Pip can carry it to the table. Then I can place the fork next to it.”

Olive smiled. “A good plan. You solved the conflict.” Pip carefully carried the green plate. Miri placed the fork with a tiny clink.

Olive said, “Now, exercise time! We will march around the table to bring each plate. Marching helps our bodies grow strong and our minds feel calm.”

They marched—step, step, step—around the dining room. Pip’s paws went tap-tap. Miri’s feet went pat-pat. Olive’s soft wings swish-swashed as she marched. They placed the blue, yellow, and white plates.

“Listen,” said Olive. “Do you hear the gentle clinks? Do you smell the apples? Our senses help us notice and care.”

Miri looked at a big stack of napkins. “Oh! So many!”

Olive said, “We will use only what we need. That is called conservation. When we save things, we help the whole world.”

Pip counted slowly, “One, two, three.” He took three napkins for three friends. “No waste,” he said proudly.

Olive pointed to a small basket of fruit. Apples were red and shiny. Bananas were sunny yellow. “We will share food choices from many places,” Olive said. “Some foods come from far away. We say thank you to helpers everywhere.”

Miri whispered, “Thank you, farmers. Thank you, drivers.”

Pip chimed in, “Thank you, rain and sun.”

They placed fruit in the center. The table looked bright and neat. Olive clapped her wings. “Organization helps us find, use, and share.”

Just then, a little spill! A drop of water slid across the wood. Pip gasped. “Oh no!”

Olive stayed calm. “Accidents happen. We fix them together.” She pointed to a cloth. “Team clean!”

They moved their bodies again: stretch, reach, wipe! The cloth felt damp and cool. The wood felt smooth and dry. Pip laughed. “We did it!”

Olive said, “You are strong, smart helpers. Pip, you solved a problem. Miri, you shared with your heart. We all moved our bodies. We saved napkins. We set a table that cares for the world.”

Pip looked around. “But where is the world?”

Olive tapped her head gently. “The world is out there, and in here.” She tapped Pip’s chest too. “Being a global citizen means we think of others, even if they are far away. We share. We listen. We take turns. We keep our home clean. We save what we can. Our small actions help the big world.”

Miri smiled wide. “We can do this every day.”

“Yes,” Olive said. “Every day can be a tiny world-helping day.”

They all sat together. The chairs made soft scrapes on the floor. The room felt warm and happy. Sunlight hugged them like a blanket. Pip wagged. Miri nibbled an apple slice. Olive took a small sip of water.

Olive raised her wing like a toast. “To caring for our big, shared home.”

Pip and Miri chimed, “To our world!”

The End.
