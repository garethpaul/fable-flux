# Ollie Otter’s Closet Camp

Ollie was a playful otter with bright eyes and smooth whiskers. He loved to splash, he loved to giggle, and today he loved to plan. “Let’s have Closet Camp!” he squeaked, pointing to the cozy closet hideaway by the coats and shoes. The closet smelled like clean laundry and cedar wood. It was soft and dark, like a little cave.

Ollie wiggled inside and clapped his paws. “Friends, follow me!” His friends—Mina Mouse, Pip Pup, and Tiko Turtle—peeked in. They saw blankets, a flashlight, and a small basket.

“It’s snug,” said Mina, twitching her nose.

“It’s quiet,” said Pip, wagging his tail.

“It’s perfect,” said Tiko, slow and warm.

Ollie’s whiskers twitched. He wanted to be the leader. He wanted to make Closet Camp the best camp ever. “Let’s start!” Ollie said. He flicked on the flashlight. Click. A circle of soft light danced on the coats.

“Do we have snacks?” asked Pip.

“Do we have a plan?” asked Tiko.

Ollie blinked. He felt a little ping in his tummy. “We can just play,” he said quickly. “It will be fine!”

They crawled in. Coats brushed their cheeks. The carpet felt fuzzy under their toes. Ollie bounced. “Let’s build a blanket fort!” he said.

“Where are the clips?” asked Mina.

Ollie looked around. No clips. “We can hold the blanket,” he said. He lifted one corner. The blanket slid down. Floomp.

“Hmm,” said Tiko.

“Again!” said Ollie. He tried the other corner. Floomp. The blanket piled on his head. He huffed. His whiskers drooped.

Next, Ollie said, “Let’s have a quiet story time.” He sat down. “Once upon a—”

“Water,” Pip whispered. “I’m thirsty.”

“Oh,” said Ollie. No water.

“Also,” Mina sniffed, “it’s a little dark.”

Ollie clicked the flashlight. Click. Click. It dimmed. “Oh no,” he said in a tiny voice. “The batteries…” He felt warm in his cheeks. His tummy felt tight. His paws clenched. He wanted to stomp. He wanted to say, “This is too hard!”

He squeaked instead, “Everything is ruined!”

Mina touched his paw. “It’s okay to feel mad,” she said softly.

Pip licked his paw. “We can fix it.”

Tiko nodded, slow and sure. “Let’s pause. Breathe with me.”

Ollie took a deep breath, in and out. He listened. He heard the quiet tick-tick of the hallway clock. He smelled the clean coats. He felt his heart slow down. “I feel better,” he said. “Thank you.”

Ollie looked around the closet. He saw the basket. He saw a little notebook and a crayon. He saw clothespins, a roll of tape, and a small bag. He blinked. “I think,” he said, “we need a plan. We need to prepare.”

“That is smart leadership,” said Tiko.

Ollie sat up tall. “Okay, team,” he said in a clear voice. “We will make a list. Then we will gather. Then we will come back.” His friends smiled. Ollie felt brave.

He wrote in the notebook with the crayon, big and simple:
- Clips for blankets
- Fresh batteries
- Water and snacks
- Soft pillows
- A little bell to call for help

“Good plan,” said Mina.

“Let’s go,” said Pip, tail wag-wag-wag.

They padded out. The hallway felt bright and warm. First, they found the clip bag in a drawer. Click, click—Ollie tested them. “Strong,” he said.

Next, they found a small pack of batteries on a shelf. “Ask before we use,” Tiko reminded. Ollie asked the grown-up. The grown-up smiled and said, “Yes, and good planning, Ollie.” Ollie beamed.

They filled a little bottle with water. Pip carried it carefully in his mouth. Mina chose crackers and apple slices and put them in the small bag. Tiko hauled two soft pillows, one under each arm. Ollie found a tiny bell in the craft box. It jingled, bright and happy.

Back to the closet they went. The air felt cool and cozy again. Ollie clipped the blanket to a hanger. Click. He clipped it to a shelf. Click. The blanket stayed up! “Fort!” he cheered.

He changed the flashlight batteries. Click. The light shone strong and white. He set the water and snacks in the basket. He put the bell by the door. Ding-ling!

“Story time now?” asked Mina.

“Camp leader says yes,” said Ollie, smiling. He felt calm. He felt proud.

They crawled under the blanket fort. The light made soft shadows on the coats. The pillows felt squishy and kind. The apple slices smelled sweet. Ollie told a gentle story about a river and a moon. Pip crunched quietly. Mina hugged a pillow. Tiko hummed a low tune.

When the story ended, Ollie rang the bell softly. Ding. “Team check,” he said. “Do we need anything else?” His voice was steady.

“I am comfy,” said Mina.

“I am happy,” said Pip.

“I am safe,” said Tiko.

Ollie took another slow breath. He remembered how he felt before—tight, hot, mad. He remembered how he felt now—loose, cool, calm. “Friends,” he said, “I learned something.”

“What did you learn?” asked Mina.

Ollie grinned. “When we prepare, problems feel smaller. When we lead with a plan, we help our friends. When we feel mad, we can breathe, ask for help, and try again.”

Pip wagged. “That is a good camp rule.”

Tiko nodded. “A wise leader prepares.”

Ollie tucked the notebook in the basket. “This is our Camp Kit,” he said. “Next time, we will be ready.”

They snuggled in the closet hideaway, warm and proud. The blanket fort held strong. The light glowed softly. The bell sat by the door, waiting. Ollie’s whiskers curled into a smile.

Closet Camp was cozy, and Ollie was prepared.

The End.
