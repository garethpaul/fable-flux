# The Quiet Roar at Dinner

The brave little lion cub was named Lio. Lio had soft golden fur and bright curious eyes. One evening, Lio padded into the dining room on gentle paws. The air smelled warm and yummy—carrot soup, sweet bread, and a tiny bowl of honey. The table was smooth and cool under his paws, and the chairs were just the right size for the family.

Mama Lion set down a bowl. “Dinner time,” she said with a smile.

Papa Lion tapped his spoon. “Let’s sit and eat together.”

Lio felt his tummy rumble. “Roar!” he said, but it was a small roar, like a purr with a wiggle. He climbed onto his chair. The chair made a little squeak. Lio giggled.

But then Lio saw something new. A green cup sat by his plate. It was not his usual yellow cup. His whiskers twitched. He liked his yellow cup. He liked it every night. He felt a little hot bubble in his chest.

“Where is my yellow cup?” Lio asked, his voice getting louder. “I want my yellow cup!”

Mama Lion looked kindly at Lio. “We are trying the green cup tonight,” she said. “It is special too.”

Lio’s tail swished. His face felt tight. He wanted to roar very loud. The room felt too big. The spoon clinked on the bowl, and the sound felt sharp. The carrot soup steamed and tickled his nose. He felt his paws curl.

Papa Lion spoke softly. “Brave cub, can you notice your feelings?”

Lio blinked. “I feel mad,” he said. “I feel mad about the green cup.”

“That is okay,” Mama Lion said. “Feelings are welcome here.”

Lio’s ears drooped. “But I want to roar.”

Papa Lion nodded. “When we feel mad, we can use our quiet roar. Want to try?”

Lio tilted his head. “What is a quiet roar?”

Papa Lion showed him. He put a paw on his belly. “Breathe in slow… one… two… three. Breathe out slow… one… two… three. That is a quiet roar. It helps the big feelings calm down.”

Lio looked at the green cup. He put a paw on his belly. He breathed in. The carrot soup smell was sweet and warm. He breathed out. The tight feeling in his face got a little softer.

“In,” Mama Lion said, smiling. “Out.”

Lio tried again. “In… out.” His tail stopped swishing so fast. The room felt less sharp and more cozy. The little lamp made a soft golden light on the shiny table. He could hear the gentle tick-tock of the clock. His paws relaxed.

Papa Lion whispered, “You are brave when you are calm.”

Lio looked at the green cup. It had a tiny leaf painted on it. He traced the leaf with one claw. “It’s pretty,” he said softly. “I still miss my yellow cup.”

“That is acceptance,” Mama Lion said. “You can miss your yellow cup and still try the green one.”

Lio lifted the green cup. The water was cool and smooth. “Sip,” he said, and took a tiny sip. It felt nice in his mouth.

Papa Lion smiled. “How does your belly feel?”

“Warm and soft,” Lio said. “The quiet roar helped.”

They began to eat. The soup tasted sweet and a little salty. The bread was fluffy and made a soft “tear” sound when Mama broke it. Honey dripped like sunshine onto Lio’s slice. He licked his lips. “Mmm,” he said.

Halfway through dinner, a little drop of soup fell on Lio’s fur. “Oh no!” he gasped. His eyes got wide again.

Mama Lion handed him a napkin. “Accidents happen,” she said. “Let’s breathe and dab.”

Lio dabbed the spot. He took one slow breath. The worry floated away like a little cloud. “I did it,” he said.

Papa Lion tapped his nose. “That is patience with yourself.”

Lio nodded. “I can be patient.”

They talked about their day. Papa Lion said, “I fixed a wobbly chair.” Mama Lion said, “I read a new book.” Lio said, “I learned a quiet roar.”

When the bowls were empty, Mama Lion brought a small plate of orange slices. They smelled bright and juicy. Lio peeled one. The peel made a tiny “zip” sound. The juice felt cool on his tongue.

“Thank you for dinner,” Lio said. “And thank you, green cup.”

“You were calm and brave,” Mama Lion said.

Lio smiled. “Sometimes I feel mad or worried,” he said. “But I can breathe in and out. I can notice my feelings. I can try new things.”

Papa Lion raised his cup. “To calm hearts,” he said.

Lio raised his green cup. “To calm hearts and quiet roars!”

The dining room felt warm and safe. The clock ticked softly. The table was clean and shiny. Lio’s belly was full, and his heart felt light.

That night, before bed, Lio looked at both cups—the yellow cup and the green cup. He touched them gently. “I can like both,” he whispered. “I can be calm.”

He took one last slow breath. In… out. He smiled.

The End.
