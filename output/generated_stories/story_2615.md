# Joey’s Big Craft Circle

In a bright, comfortable nursery, a joyful kangaroo joey named Kip bounced, boing, boing, boing! Soft rugs tickled his toes. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. The air smelled like crayons, fresh paper, and a little like apples.

“Today we make a big rainbow poster,” said Miss Lila, their gentle teacher. Her voice was calm and kind. “We will use paper, glue, and lots of colors. We will work together. That is called cooperation.”

Kip’s ears perked up. “Co-op-er-a-tion,” he said slowly. “That means we help each other!”

“Exactly,” Miss Lila smiled. “We also use our words to share our ideas.”

Kip’s friends gathered around the low table. There was Mia the mouse, Tiko the turtle, and Bibi the bluebird. In the middle sat a big sheet of paper, shiny glue sticks, and a basket of soft paper shapes—red circles, orange squares, yellow stars, green leaves, blue raindrops, and purple hearts.

Kip sniffed. “Mmm! The glue smells sweet and sticky.” He giggled. “Let’s make the brightest rainbow!”

Mia squeaked, “I like red!” She reached for the red circles.

Bibi chirped, “Blue raindrops for me!”

Tiko blinked slowly. “I can smooth the paper,” he said. “I am careful.”

Kip bounced once. “I can glue!”

Miss Lila nodded. “Let’s make a plan first. We talk. We listen. Then we craft.”

Kip took a deep breath. “Okay. I think the rainbow goes red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple.”

Mia wiggled her whiskers. “I agree!”

Bibi fluffed her feathers. “Me too!”

Tiko smiled. “Yes.”

They began. Mia placed red circles in a gentle curve. “Round and bright,” she said.

Kip dabbed glue, press, press, press. The glue was cool on his fingertips. “Sticky but fun!” he laughed.

Tiko used his soft shell to smooth the bumps. “Nice and flat,” he hummed.

Bibi dropped blue raindrops into a little puddle shape. “Plip-plip!”

Soon they reached the orange squares. Kip was excited. He bounced a little higher. “My turn to pick shapes!” he said. He grabbed a handful and started to stick them anywhere. The squares went up, down, and sideways. A few stuck to his paw. One stuck to his ear!

Mia squeaked, “Oh!”

Bibi tilted her head. “The rainbow looks wiggly now.”

Tiko blinked. “I feel a little mixed up.”

Kip froze. His ears drooped. “Did I make a mess?” he asked in a small voice.

Miss Lila knelt beside them. “It is okay. We are learning. What can we do?”

Kip remembered her words. “We can talk. We can listen.”

Mia nodded. “Kip, can we try putting orange in a line, like the red?”

Kip took a breath. “Yes,” he said. “Thank you for telling me. Can someone help me?”

Bibi chirped, “I can help! I will lift the squares from your ear.” She pecked gently. “Boop!”

Tiko said, “I can sort the squares. Straight ones here. Corners there.”

Kip smiled again. His tail thumped softly on the rug. “I will glue where you point.”

They moved together like a little team. Mia pointed. “Here, here, and here.” Kip pressed the squares in a neat curve. Tiko smoothed. Bibi sang a happy note.

When they reached the yellow stars, Kip asked, “Who wants to place the stars?”

Mia raised her paw. “I do! But I need help.”

“Let’s do it together,” Kip said. “We can share.”

They worked slowly, carefully. The stars shone bright. The green leaves made a soft, rustly sound as Tiko pressed them down. “Sh-sh-sh,” went the paper.

Bibi placed the blue raindrops in a clean line this time. “Drip, drop, line and stop!” she chirped.

At last, the purple hearts. Kip held one up. “I feel happy,” he said. “I want to put hearts near friends.”

Mia smiled. “That is sweet.”

They placed a purple heart by each name on the bottom. Kip’s paws were a little sticky, but his heart felt warm. He looked at the big rainbow. It was smooth. It was bright. It made the nursery feel extra cozy.

Miss Lila clapped softly. “You did it! You cooperated. You crafted. You used your words. How does it feel?”

Kip bounced once, gently. “It feels good. When I went too fast, I listened. When I needed help, I asked. When my friends needed help, I helped.”

Tiko nodded. “We each had a job.”

Bibi fluffed her wings. “We shared ideas.”

Mia squeaked, “We made something beautiful together.”

Miss Lila put a small gold sticker in the corner. “Team Rainbow,” she read. “What is our lesson today?”

Kip looked at his friends. He smiled a big, joyful smile. “When we cooperate, we can do big things. We talk. We listen. We help. That is how we shine.”

The rainbow seemed to glow in the warm light. The glue had a soft, sweet smell. The room was quiet for a moment, full of happy hearts.

Kip whispered, “Thank you, team.”

“Thank you, Kip,” his friends said.

They hugged, soft and careful, so no paper would crinkle. Then they bounced, squeaked, chirped, and hummed their way to snack time, ready to share apples and stories.

The End.
