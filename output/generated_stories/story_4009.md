# Finn's Big Mistake

The quiet attic smelled like old wood and dusty books. Sunlight peeked through a small round window, making golden spots on the floor. Little Finn the fox kit loved exploring up here when Grandma Owl was taking her afternoon nap downstairs.

Finn's orange fur was soft and fluffy. His black nose twitched as he sniffed around. So many interesting things lived in the attic! Old boxes, shiny picture frames, and... 

"Ooh!" Finn's green eyes grew wide. On a special shelf sat the most beautiful music box he had ever seen. It was painted with tiny blue flowers and had a golden key on the side.

Finn knew he should ask before touching Grandma Owl's things. But his paws reached up anyway. The music box felt smooth and cool.

"I'll just look," Finn whispered to himself. "Just for one tiny second."

But as Finn lifted the music box, his paws slipped. CRASH! The pretty box fell to the wooden floor. The lid popped open. The tiny dancing lady inside broke into three pieces.

Finn's heart beat fast like a drum. His ears drooped down. "Oh no, oh no, oh no!" he cried softly.

From downstairs came the sound of footsteps. Grandma Owl was coming up to the attic!

Finn wanted to hide behind a big box. He wanted to run away. But something in his tummy told him that wasn't right.

"Finn?" called Grandma Owl's gentle voice. "Are you up there, little one?"

Finn took a deep breath. His paws shook as he picked up the broken pieces. When Grandma Owl's wise face appeared at the top of the stairs, Finn was sitting by the music box with tears in his eyes.

"Grandma Owl," Finn said in a very small voice. "I did something bad. I touched your special music box without asking. Now it's broken, and I'm so, so sorry."

Grandma Owl's feathers ruffled softly as she walked over. She looked at the broken music box, then at Finn's sad face.

"Oh, my dear little kit," she said gently. "Thank you for telling me the truth. That was very brave."

"But I broke your beautiful music box!" Finn sniffled. "Are you mad at me?"

Grandma Owl sat down next to Finn. Her wing wrapped around his small shoulders. "I'm not mad, sweet one. Everyone makes mistakes. What matters is that you told the truth and said you're sorry."

"Really?" Finn looked up with hopeful eyes.

"Really," Grandma Owl smiled. "This music box belonged to my grandmother. But you know what? I love you much more than any music box."

Finn felt warm and happy inside. "Can I help fix it?"

"We can try together," Grandma Owl said. "And next time, just ask before touching special things, okay?"

"I promise!" Finn nodded hard. "I'll always ask first."

They spent the rest of the afternoon carefully gluing the tiny dancer back together. When they wound the golden key, sweet music filled the attic once again.

"See?" Grandma Owl said as they listened to the gentle tune. "Sometimes broken things can be made beautiful again, just like hurt feelings can be healed with a sincere apology."

Finn snuggled close to Grandma Owl. He learned that saying sorry and telling the truth felt much better than hiding. And the best part was that Grandma Owl's love for him never broke at all.

The End.
