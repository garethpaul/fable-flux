# Dotty the Dolphin Paints and Plays

Dotty was a playful dolphin who loved to splash, giggle, and hum little songs. One sunny day, Dotty visited the creative art room. The room smelled like fresh crayons and salty clay. Paints glowed like rainbows. Paper rustled softly. Brushes tickled little jars with soft, tap-tap sounds.

“Hello, brushes!” Dotty giggled. “Are you ready to swim in color?”

Dotty swam in a big tub of water placed for wet art, swoosh-swoosh, and flicked droplets that sparkled like tiny stars. Friends were in the room too: Mia the mouse, who wore tiny sneakers; Tico the turtle, strong and steady; and Buzz the bee, who buzz-buzzed a happy tune. A big sign read, “Art and Sports Day!”

Coach Sunny, a gentle seal, clapped her flippers. “Today, we make art about our favorite sports, and then we play friendly games. We also tell stories about our art. Remember our big rule: Kindness.”

Dotty flapped her fins. “I love sports! I love stories! I love kindness!” She zoomed in a circle and knocked a brush. Splat! Blue paint dotted the floor.

Mia squeaked, “It’s okay, Dotty. We can wipe it up.” She handed Dotty a soft cloth. It felt warm and fuzzy in her fin.

“Thank you, Mia,” Dotty said. “That was kind.”

Everyone started painting. Dotty painted a big, shiny ball. “This is for water polo,” she said. “I splash, and I pass!” She added waves that smelled like the sea and a smiling sun that looked like a juicy orange.

Mia painted a tiny track for running. Tico painted a soccer goal. Buzz dabbed yellow dots like sunshine. The room hummed with happy sounds: swish of brushes, gentle giggles, and the soft thump-thump of little feet moving around.

Then Coach Sunny announced, “Time to share our stories!”

Dotty went first. She lifted her picture carefully. “In my story, I splash and pass the ball to my friends. We cheer for each other. We take turns. We try our best.”

The friends clapped. Tico said, “I like your waves. They look cool and fast!”

Dotty smiled so big her cheeks felt bubbly.

Next was Buzz. Buzz buzzed to the front with a small poster and said softly, “My wings are small. I can’t run fast. I can’t splash like Dotty. I feel left out.”

Dotty’s heart went thump. She remembered the rule: Kindness. “Buzz,” she asked gently, “what do you like?”

Buzz pointed at the dots. “I like counting. I like telling stories. I like cheering.”

Mia squeaked, “That is very important!”

Coach Sunny nodded. “Sports are for everyone. We all have different strengths.”

Then came the games. First was a passing game with a soft, bouncy ball. Dotty zipped and caught the ball easily. Swoosh! Pass! Catch! Buzz hovered near the side.

“Pass to me!” Dotty called to Tico. Tico kicked the ball gently. It rolled to Buzz and bumped his tiny legs. Buzz wobbled and backed away. “I’m scared,” he whispered.

Dotty stopped. She saw Buzz’s small wings tremble. She saw his eyes look shiny and wet. Dotty took a slow breath. She wanted to win the game, but she wanted to be kind more.

Dotty swam over, slow and gentle. The floor felt cool under her fin. “Buzz,” she said, “would you like to be our Story Coach and Cheer Captain? You can tell us who to pass to, and you can cheer for us. Your voice is strong and bright.”

Buzz blinked. “Really? That counts?”

“It counts a lot,” said Dotty. “We need you.”

Buzz smiled, warm as honey. “Okay! Dotty to Mia. Mia to Tico. Tico to Dotty. Go team!”

“Go team!” everyone cheered.

Buzz waved his tiny pom-pom made of yarn. “Kind pass! Kind catch!” he sang.

Dotty passed gently to Mia, like giving a soft hug. Mia passed to Tico. Tico tapped the ball back to Dotty. They moved together, slow and steady, fast and fun, listening to Buzz’s bright voice.

When they finished, Coach Sunny blew her whistle. Tweet! “Great teamwork,” she said. “You all used kindness.”

“Buzz helped us,” Dotty said. “We listened to his story and made space for him.”

Buzz buzzed in a happy circle. “I feel brave now,” he said. “Can I try a tiny pass?”

Dotty nodded. “We will make it safe.”

They used a lighter, squishy ball. Dotty held her fin steady. Buzz tapped the ball with one gentle toe. Boop! It rolled into Dotty’s fin. Everyone clapped. Buzz beamed and hugged his own little self with his wings.

Later, they added to their art. Dotty painted Buzz as a bright star on the team. Buzz added tiny dots that looked like cheers. Mia drew big ears for listening. Tico drew a wide, open door.

At the end, they all sat in a circle. Coach Sunny asked, “What did we learn today?”

Dotty said, “Kindness means seeing how friends feel and helping them join.”

Mia said, “Kindness means listening.”

Tico said, “Kindness means making space.”

Buzz said, “Kindness makes me brave.”

Coach Sunny smiled. “Kindness makes sports and stories better for everyone. We celebrate our differences. We cheer for each other. That is our win.”

Dotty felt warm, like sunshine on the sea. She hugged her friends with a splashy tail hug and whispered, “Kindness is our best sport.”

The End.
