# Rabbit Rosa’s Pantry Parade

Rosa was a determined rabbit with soft gray ears and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, she hopped into the pantry. The pantry smelled like warm bread, sweet cinnamon, and crunchy carrots. Shelves stood tall like little mountains. Jars clicked and clacked when Rosa nudged them.

“I will make a snack to share,” Rosa said. “A snack from many places in our world!”

She wiggled her whiskers and looked up. “Hmm. What foods do friends around the world like?” Her tummy gave a happy rumble.

On the bottom shelf, Rosa found a bag. “Rice,” she read slowly. The grains felt smooth and tiny. “Rice grows in many countries,” Rosa said. “My friend Mei likes rice.” Rosa smiled. “Rice can be part of our snack.”

She reached higher and saw a shiny green bottle. “Olive oil,” she whispered. “Sofia’s family uses olive oil.” The bottle glowed like sunshine through leaves. Rosa put it in her basket.

Next, she spotted a jar of chickpeas. She shook it gently. Rattle, rattle! “Omar loves hummus made from chickpeas,” said Rosa. “These can be mashed and tasty.”

On the top shelf, a small jar of seaweed flakes rested like a tiny forest. “Kai told me about seaweed from the ocean near his home,” Rosa said. She touched the jar. It felt cool. “Maybe a sprinkle will be nice.”

She found cocoa powder too. The smell was rich and chocolatey. “Ana bakes cocoa cookies,” Rosa said. “We can make a little sweet treat to share.”

Rosa set everything on the pantry floor. “I want to make a snack that shows our big world,” she said. “Everyone belongs.”

Just then, her friend Lila the ladybug buzzed in through the door. “Hi, Rosa! What are you doing?”

“I’m making a World Snack,” said Rosa. “Will you help?”

“Yes!” Lila cheered. “What’s first?”

“First, we rinse the rice,” Rosa said. She washed the rice in a bowl. Splash, swish, swish! Cool water tickled her paws. She cooked the rice until it was soft and fluffy. It smelled warm and gentle.

“Now chickpeas,” said Rosa. She mashed them with olive oil and a tiny squeeze of lemon. Squish, mash, smoosh! “This is hummus,” Rosa explained. “It is smooth and creamy.”

Lila tasted a dot. “Mmm! It’s soft like a cloud.”

Rosa put a tiny pinch of seaweed flakes on the rice. “Just a sprinkle,” she said. “Seaweed can be salty and special.”

She paused and looked at the cocoa powder. “We can make cocoa rice balls,” she said. She mixed a little cocoa and honey into a small cup of rice, rolled it into little balls, and set them on a plate. They looked like tiny moons.

Soon, their friends peeked into the pantry. Mei hopped in first. “I smell rice!” she said.

“Come in,” said Rosa. “I made snacks with foods from different places.”

Sofia smiled. “Olive oil! It reminds me of my grandma’s kitchen.”

Omar sniffed the hummus. “That smells like family picnics,” he said.

Kai looked at the seaweed flakes. “Those are like the ones from the sea near my home,” he said softly.

Ana giggled at the cocoa rice balls. “They look yummy!”

Rosa’s ears stood tall. “Before we eat, let’s share how we say hello.” She clapped her paws. “We can learn from each other.”

Mei waved. “Ni hao!”

Sofia waved. “Hola!”

Omar bowed his head. “Marhaba!”

Kai smiled. “Aloha!”

Ana said, “Olá!”

Rosa said, “Hello!” She beamed. “Many ways to say hello. Many foods to try. Our world is big, and there is room for everyone.”

They all sat in a circle on a small pantry rug. The floor felt cool, and the air smelled like lemons and cocoa. Rosa passed plates. “Please try a little bit,” she said. “If you don’t like it, that’s okay. We can be kind.”

Mei tasted the rice with seaweed. “It is soft and salty. I like it.”

Omar dipped a carrot into the hummus. “Creamy! It tastes like home.”

Sofia drizzled a drop of olive oil on her rice. “Mmm, smooth.”

Kai tried a cocoa rice ball. “Sweet and chewy!”

Ana nibbled hummus, then a cocoa ball. “I like both.”

Rosa watched her friends enjoy. Her heart felt big and warm. “Our snack is made from many places,” she said. “We are different, and we can share.”

Lila buzzed. “What did we learn today?”

Rosa wiggled her nose. “We learned that our world is full of wonderful foods and ways to say hello. When we listen, share, and show respect, we make room for everyone. That is inclusion.”

Mei added, “Trying new things helps us understand our friends.”

Omar nodded. “Respect means we are gentle with each other’s favorites.”

Sofia said, “Global means big world, and we are all part of it.”

Kai smiled. “We can be kind, even when something is new.”

Rosa clapped her paws. “Yes! Our big world fits in our small pantry today. When we share and learn, we bring the world closer.”

They finished their snacks and cleaned up together. Jars clicked closed. The floor got a quick wipe. The pantry felt calm and bright.

Rosa hugged her friends. “Thank you for helping,” she said. “Let’s keep learning from our big, beautiful world.”

Everyone waved. “See you next time!”

The End.
