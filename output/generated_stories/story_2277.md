# Ellie's Big Helper Heart

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved exploring the garden behind her house. Her gray trunk swayed happily as she walked through the colorful flowers. The sweet smell of roses tickled her nose, and the warm sunshine felt wonderful on her back.

One sunny morning, Ellie heard a tiny voice coming from the old garden shed. "Help! Help me, please!" squeaked the little voice.

Ellie's big ears perked up. She followed the sound with careful steps, her feet making soft *thump-thump* sounds on the grass. The wooden shed smelled like dirt and old tools.

"Hello?" called Ellie gently. "Who needs help?"

"Down here! I'm stuck!" came the voice again.

Ellie looked down and saw a small field mouse trapped under a heavy flower pot that had fallen over. The mouse's whiskers were trembling, and his tiny paws were trying to push the pot away.

"Oh my!" said Ellie. "Don't worry, little friend. I'll help you!"

But then Ellie stopped. The flower pot belonged to Mrs. Garden, the kind lady who lived next door. What if Mrs. Garden got upset if Ellie moved her pot?

Ellie's tummy felt funny with worry. Should she help the mouse or leave the pot alone?

The mouse looked up with big, scared eyes. "Please, I can't get out!"

Ellie knew what she had to do. Helping someone in trouble was more important than worrying about getting in trouble herself.

"I'll get you out right now!" Ellie said bravely.

She wrapped her strong trunk around the flower pot. It felt heavy and cool. With a gentle lift, she moved it to the side. The mouse scurried out, brushing dirt off his brown fur.

"Thank you, thank you!" squeaked the mouse, dancing in a happy circle. "You saved me!"

"I'm so glad you're okay," said Ellie, feeling warm and happy inside.

Just then, Mrs. Garden walked into the shed. She saw the moved flower pot and looked puzzled.

Ellie's heart beat fast. She could pretend she didn't know what happened, but that wouldn't be honest.

"Mrs. Garden," said Ellie in her sweetest voice, "I moved your flower pot to help a mouse who was stuck. I'm sorry I touched it without asking."

Mrs. Garden smiled kindly. "Oh, Ellie! Thank you for telling me the truth. And thank you for helping the little mouse. That was very brave and kind of you."

The mouse peeked out from behind a watering can. "She's the best helper ever!" he squeaked.

Mrs. Garden patted Ellie's trunk gently. "You know what, Ellie? When someone needs help, it's always right to help them. And I'm proud of you for being honest with me."

Ellie's trunk curled up in a happy smile. Her whole body felt light and joyful.

"Would you like to help me water the flowers?" asked Mrs. Garden.

"Yes, please!" said Ellie excitedly.

As they watered the garden together, the mouse played nearby, and Ellie learned something wonderful: When you help others and tell the truth, good things happen. Her heart felt as big and warm as her smile.

From that day on, Ellie always listened for anyone who needed help, and she always told the truth about her actions.

The End.
