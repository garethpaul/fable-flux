# Koko the Calm Koala Tries Something New

Koko the calm koala woke up in a cozy bedroom with soft blue walls and a little nightlight that glowed like a tiny moon. The morning sun peeked in, warm and gold, and the air smelled like clean sheets and eucalyptus tea. Koko stretched slowly, his fluffy ears wiggling. He loved mornings that felt gentle.

“Good morning, Pillow,” Koko whispered, patting his favorite star-shaped pillow. “Today will be a good day.”

Mama Koala opened the door with a smile. “Good morning, Koko. Time to get ready. We have a playdate after breakfast.”

Koko sat up, blinked, and smiled back. “Okay, Mama.” He took a deep breath in and out. Calm breaths made his tummy feel soft and happy.

First, Koko made his bed, smoothing the quilt with his little paws. The quilt felt warm and bumpy under his fingers. He liked helping. Helping was part of his healthy habits.

“Brush time,” Mama said.

Koko hopped to the bathroom and brushed his teeth. Scrub, scrub, scrub—minty bubbles tickled his tongue. “Sparkly,” he said, grinning in the mirror. He washed his paws with warm, soapy water. “All clean.”

Back in the cozy bedroom, Koko chose his favorite soft sweater. It was gray, like a cloud. He liked how it felt, snug and friendly. “I’m ready,” he told Mama.

At breakfast, the table smelled like toast and bananas. Mama poured oatmeal into a little bowl. “I added blueberries,” she said. “They’re new. Want to try?”

Koko looked at the blue circles. He had never tried blueberries. His ears twitched. “I like my oatmeal plain,” he said softly.

Mama smiled and sat down. “You can have it plain. But remember, open minds help us grow. We can listen, look, taste, and try. It’s okay to not like something. It’s okay to like something new.”

Koko took a calm breath. He put one blueberry on his spoon and tasted it. Pop! It was sweet and a little tangy. His eyes widened. “Oh! I like it,” he said. “It tastes like a tiny sweet raindrop.”

“See?” Mama said. “Your open mind helped you discover something new.”

After breakfast, Koko and Mama set up the cozy bedroom for the playdate. They put out blocks, a big storybook, and a soft basket of plush toys. The room smelled like cedar and clean rugs. The nightlight still glowed softly in the corner.

Knock, knock!

“Come in,” called Mama.

Two friends came to play: Lina the little lizard and Benji the bunny. Lina wore bright socks. Benji wore a red bow tie. “Hi, Koko!” they said together.

“Hi,” Koko replied, calm and warm. “Let’s play.”

Lina ran to the blocks. “I want to build a very tall tower!” she said.

Benji hugged the plush basket. “I want to play doctor with the plushies,” he said.

Koko looked at both friends. They wanted different things. His heart thumped. He wanted everyone to be happy.

Koko took a slow breath. “We can take turns,” he said. “We can be open. First, we build a tower. Then, we play doctor.”

Lina nodded. “Okay.”

Benji nodded too. “Okay!”

They sat on the soft rug. The blocks clicked and clacked. “Red block on top,” Lina said.

“Blue block next,” Koko said.

Benji held a yellow block. “I think this one goes sideways,” he whispered.

Lina blinked. “Sideways? Towers go up.”

Koko felt the room get quiet. He placed his paw on the rug. The rug felt fuzzy and calm. “Let’s keep open minds,” he said gently. “We can try sideways and see what happens.”

Lina tilted her head. “Okay. We can try.”

Benji placed the block sideways. The tower looked different—wider and strong. “It works!” Benji giggled.

Lina smiled. “It looks like a castle now.”

They all laughed. The blocks made little tapping sounds as they finished the castle. “Ta-da!” Koko said, clapping softly.

“Now my turn?” Benji asked.

“Yes,” Koko said. “We take turns.”

They played doctor with the plushies. Benji checked the bear’s heartbeat with a toy stethoscope. Thump-thump, thump-thump. “Bear is healthy,” he said.

Lina wrapped a bandage on the giraffe’s long neck. “All better,” she sang.

Koko washed the plushies’ tiny paws with a pretend wipe. “Clean and cozy,” he said. “Healthy habits help.”

Soon, Mama brought a plate of apple slices. The apples were shiny and smelled sweet. “Snack time,” she said.

“Thank you, Mama,” Koko said. He took a bite. Crunch! Juice ran over his tongue. He passed the plate to Lina. “Your turn,” he said. Lina took some and passed it to Benji. “Your turn,” she said. They all shared.

After snack, Lina pointed to the storybook. “Can we read?”

Koko nodded. “Yes. We can try something new.” He opened to a page he had never read. The pictures were bright with stars and rivers. He read slowly, and Mama helped with big words. The room felt safe and warm. Their voices sounded like soft music.

When the story ended, Lina said, “I liked our sideways block idea.”

Benji said, “I liked trying new things.”

Koko smiled, his ears wiggling. “Me too. Having an open mind helped us listen, try, and share. Taking turns made it fair. Being gentle made it kind.”

Mama nodded. “That’s a wonderful lesson.”

As the sun moved across the floor, the playdate ended. Hugs felt soft and sweet. “Bye, friends!” Koko waved.

Back in his cozy bedroom, Koko cuddled his star pillow. He felt proud and peaceful. “Today I tried new food and new play,” he whispered. “My open mind helped me learn. Taking turns helped us have fun. Being kind helped us get along.”

He yawned a sleepy yawn. The nightlight glowed like a tiny moon again. Koko closed his eyes, warm and calm.

The End.
