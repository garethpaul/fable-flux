# Nila’s Quiet Spoon

In a warm kitchen that smelled like cinnamon and toast, a gentle elephant calf named Nila stood on a soft mat. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. The kettle hummed. The clock tick-tocked like a sleepy drum.

Nila’s trunk wiggled with excitement. “Today I make fruit bowls!” she said. Her ears flapped happily. On the table were bright bananas, juicy strawberries, tiny blueberries, and a round orange that looked like a little sun.

Mama’s voice floated from the doorway. “Slow and gentle, Nila,” she said. “Use your mindful trunk.”

Nila blinked. “Mindful?” she asked. “What is mindful, Mama?”

Mama smiled. “Mindful means we notice. We listen. We feel. We breathe. We do one thing at a time with a calm heart.”

Nila nodded. “I can try.”

She picked up a wooden spoon. It felt smooth and warm. “Hello, spoon,” she whispered. “You are light like a feather.” She smiled. The spoon did not answer, but it sat quietly and waited.

Nila washed the fruit in cool water. Splash, splash. The water tickled her trunk. “I hear the splash,” she said softly. “I feel the cool.” Her belly felt calm, like a small pond.

She put the strawberries on a towel. Red and shiny. She sniffed them. “Sweet!” Her eyes grew wide. The sweet smell made her think of summer and pink clouds.

Nila picked up the banana. She peeled it slowly. Rip, rip. “I see yellow,” she said. “I hear rip.” She felt proud. “One thing at a time.”

She began to slice. One banana circle. Two banana circles. Her trunk moved a little too fast. The slices slid and tumbled. Plip-plop! One piece dropped onto the floor.

“Oh no!” Nila’s cheeks grew hot. Her ears drooped. “I made a mess,” she whispered.

The clock ticked kindly. The kettle hummed. Mama stepped closer. “Breathe, Nila,” she said gently. “Let’s do our belly breath.”

Nila put her trunk on her belly. She breathed in. Her belly grew big like a balloon. She breathed out. Her belly got small like a lemon. In. Out. In. Out.

“I feel softer now,” Nila said. Her ears lifted a little.

Mama nodded. “Good noticing. Now try again. Slow and gentle.”

Nila wiped the floor with a towel. “I can help the kitchen feel better,” she told the floor. “I am sorry, floor.” The floor felt warm under her feet.

She placed the cutting board steady. She looked at the knife. “I use you safely,” she said. “One slice at a time.” She breathed in. She breathed out. Slice. Pause. Slice. Pause. The banana circles made a neat little line like a tiny train.

Next came the strawberries. “Hello, little hearts,” Nila giggled as she cut them. Red juice sparkled in the sun. “I see shiny. I smell sweet.”

Then the blueberries. “You are round and brave,” she whispered to the blueberries. She rolled them into a small blue mountain. They made soft tap-tap sounds on the bowl.

Nila looked at the orange sun. “Time to peel,” she said. She started fast again—rip! The peel flew and bumped the sugar jar. The jar wobbled. Wibble-wobble! Nila’s eyes went big.

“Stop,” she told herself. “Feel the floor. Feel my feet.” She pressed her feet down. She felt the mat squish. She felt steady. “Breathe.” In. Out.

The jar stopped wobbling. Nila smiled. “Thank you, breath.”

She peeled the orange slowly now. The peel curled like a ribbon. The tiny orange puffs smelled bright and happy. “I smell sunshine,” said Nila. She laughed a little laugh that sounded like a tiny trumpet.

With all the fruit ready, Nila had an idea. “I can make a picture!” she said. “I can be creative and kind.” She thought of Mama and of Mr. Cat, who liked to nap by the window.

She made a smiling face in one bowl. Banana circles for eyes. A strawberry slice for a mouth. Blueberry freckles. “This is for Mama,” she said.

She made a tiny paw shape in another bowl with blueberries and a little orange slice. “This is for Mr. Cat,” she whispered. “I am thinking of you.”

Mama clapped softly. “It looks lovely, Nila. How did you make it so calm and pretty?”

Nila stood tall. “I used my mindful trunk,” she said. “I noticed. I breathed. I went slow. I felt my feet. I did one thing at a time.”

Mama hugged her carefully. “That is wellness,” Mama said. “It helps your body and your heart.”

They sat at the table. Nila lifted one spoonful. She looked at it. “Hello, spoon,” she whispered again. She smelled the fruit. She listened to the quiet tick-tock. She tasted. Sweet, soft, juicy. “Thank you, fruit,” she said.

Mr. Cat padded in and purred. He sniffed his bowl and meowed a happy meow. Nila petted him gently. “Here you go,” she said. “I made this with love.” Her touch was soft and kind.

Mama smiled. “How do you feel now, Nila?”

Nila put her trunk on her belly and closed her eyes for one breath. In. Out. “I feel peaceful,” she said. “When I am mindful, I can be calm. I can be careful. I can be creative and kind.”

The kitchen felt extra warm, like a hug. The sun squares on the floor looked like little gold boats. The kettle was quiet now. The clock ticked on, happy and slow.

Nila’s ears flapped. “Tomorrow,” she said, “I will be mindful when I tie my scarf.”

Mama nodded. “One breath at a time.”

The End.
