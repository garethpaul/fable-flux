# Cubby Helps at Home Office

Cubby was a friendly bear cub with soft, brown fur and bright eyes. He lived with Mama Bear and Papa Bear in a cozy den. One sunny morning, the den felt busy. The home office hummed softly. The computer made little clicks. Papers rustled like leaves. The desk lamp glowed warm and gold.

Cubby peeked in. “Hi, Mama,” he said. “What are you doing?”

Mama Bear smiled. “I’m working, Cubby. I help our neighbors by sending notes. The notes tell them about a park clean-up. It is our job to care for our home and our town.”

Cubby’s ears perked up. “Caring is a job?”

Mama nodded. “Yes. It’s called civic responsibility. We help our community. We listen, we speak kindly, and we work together.”

Cubby thought. He looked at the computer, the mail pile, and the calendar with colorful dots. He wanted to help too.

Papa Bear walked in with a basket of letters. “These letters need stamps,” he said. “We must send them today so everyone knows when to help.”

Cubby stood tall. “I want to help!” he said. “I can do a job.”

Mama Bear patted his paw. “Thank you, Cubby. That is self-advocacy. You said what you can do. That helps us know.”

Cubby smiled. He took a deep breath. He smelled paper and fresh ink. He heard a bird chirp outside. The home office felt busy, but also safe.

Mama Bear said, “Let’s make a plan. We will work as a team. We will use peaceful communication. That means we use calm words, we listen, and we take turns.”

Cubby nodded. “I can do that.”

Papa Bear showed a small sheet of bear-stamps. “Cubby, you can place a stamp on each letter.”

Cubby pressed one stamp down. Smush! It was sticky and smooth. He giggled. “I’m helping our neighbors get the park clean-up note!”

Suddenly, Cubby spotted a problem. The big calendar showed the clean-up on the same day as Story Time at the den. Cubby loved Story Time. He felt a twist in his tummy. He wanted to help the park. He also wanted Story Time.

Cubby took another deep breath. He remembered peaceful words. “Mama, Papa,” he said softly, “I see a problem. The clean-up is on Story Time day. I feel worried. I want to help the park, and I want Story Time too. What can we do?”

Mama Bear looked at the calendar. “Good noticing, Cubby. You spoke up kindly. That is self-advocacy.”

Papa Bear nodded. “When we see a problem, we talk and find solutions.”

They all sat at the desk. The chair was big and squishy. Cubby’s paws made tiny taps on the wood. The room felt calm.

Mama asked, “What are our choices? Let’s list them.”

Cubby counted on his claws. “One, we go to the clean-up first and Story Time later. Two, we ask neighbors if we can start earlier. Three, we trade jobs. Maybe I pick up little sticks at home, while you clean the park.”

Papa Bear smiled. “Great ideas. Which helps the community the most?”

Cubby thought. “If we all help the park together, the park gets clean faster. Maybe we can ask to start earlier so we can still have Story Time.”

Mama Bear said, “Let’s write a note to our neighbors. We will use peaceful communication.”

She opened a new message. Tap, tap, tap went the keys.

Mama read aloud, “Hello, neighbors. We are excited to help clean the park. We have Story Time later. Can we start the clean-up one hour earlier? This helps our family take part and also keep our routine. Thank you for listening. From, the Bear Family.”

Cubby added, “Please and thank you.”

Mama sent the message with a click.

While they waited, Cubby kept stamping letters. Smush, smush, smush. He felt proud. He felt helpful. He hummed a little tune.

Soon, a reply came. Ding!

Papa Bear read, “Yes, we can start one hour earlier. Thank you for sharing your needs, Bear Family. See you there!”

Cubby bounced. “We did it! We used peaceful words. We helped our community. We also kept Story Time.”

Mama Bear hugged Cubby. “That is civic responsibility, Cubby. We care for our shared places. We plan. We speak kindly. We listen.”

Papa Bear added, “And you used self-advocacy. You said what you needed. You helped find a fair plan.”

Later that day, Cubby went to the park with Mama and Papa. The grass smelled sweet. The sun felt warm on Cubby’s fur. He picked up little sticks and leaves with his small paws. He dropped them into a big bag. Swish! Other neighbors waved and smiled.

“Thank you, Cubby!” they said.

Cubby waved back. “We are a team!”

When the park was clean, the Bear Family went home for Story Time. Cubby curled up with a soft blanket. The pages of the book made a gentle whisper. He felt cozy and proud.

Cubby said, “I learned something. Helping our town is everyone’s job. We use calm words. We listen. We speak up. We work together.”

Mama and Papa smiled. “Yes,” they said. “That is how we make our home and our town better.”

The End.
