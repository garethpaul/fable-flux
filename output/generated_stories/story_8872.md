# Rooftop Tick-Tock, Hop-Hop

Bibi was a small, determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. Bibi lived in a cozy home under a rooftop garden. The rooftop was warm from the sun and smelled like mint and tomatoes. The breeze made the little wind chimes sing, ting-ting, ting-ting.

One morning, Bibi stretched her long ears and said, “Today, I will do three things! I will water the plants. I will learn new words. And I will make art with recycling.” Her nose wiggled with excitement.

Mama Rabbit smiled. “That sounds busy, Bibi. Let’s make a plan. Plans help us use our time.”

Bibi nodded. “Yes! A plan.” Mama drew a simple picture chart on a big piece of cardboard. She used a green crayon and made three boxes.

“Box one,” Mama said, pointing, “water plants. It takes this much time.” She drew a short blue line.

“Box two,” Mama said, “learn new words. It takes this much time.” She drew another line.

“Box three,” Mama said, “recycling art. It takes this much time.” She drew the last line.

Bibi clapped her paws. “Tick-tock, hop-hop!” she sang. “I can do it!”

Up on the rooftop, the sun felt warm on Bibi’s fur. A ladybug crawled on a leaf. The soil smelled earthy and wet. “Good morning, mint,” Bibi said. “Good morning, tomatoes.” She picked up her small watering can.

“Remember,” Mama called from the door, “ten slow pours. Then stop.”

Bibi counted out loud. “One… two… three…” The water made soft trickles, shh-shh, into the dirt. “Four… five…” She wanted to keep going, but she looked at Mama’s picture chart. “I must stop at ten,” she said to herself. “I have more to do.”

“Six… seven… eight… nine… ten.” Bibi set the can down. She felt proud. “I used my time!” she said.

Next, Bibi sat on a sunny mat with her little word cards. She loved words. She loved the sounds they made. She picked up a card with a picture of the sun. “Sol,” she said softly, rolling the sound. “In Spanish, sun is sol. Sol feels warm in my mouth.” She smiled.

She picked up another card with a picture of a cat. “Gato,” she said. “Gato is cat.” She listened to the wind chimes and said, “Campanas. Bells. Campanas go ting-ting.”

Mama sat beside her. “Beautiful words, Bibi,” she said. “Let’s set a time. We learn words for five songs.” Mama tapped the chimes gently. “Each ting-ting is a beat.”

Bibi nodded. “Five songs,” she said. They sang together. “Sol, sun, sol. Gato, cat, gato. Agua, water, agua.” The words felt like tiny gifts. The wind felt cool on Bibi’s whiskers. After five songs, Bibi put the cards away. She wanted to keep going, but she remembered the chart. “I can learn more tomorrow,” she whispered. “I am saving time.”

Now it was art time. Bibi had a small box filled with clean recycling—paper tubes, bottle caps, and a shiny tin lid. She spread them out on the rooftop. The tin lid flashed in the sun. The paper tubes smelled a little like cardboard and rain.

“I will make a clock!” Bibi said. “A clock helps me plan my time.” She glued the tin lid to a cardboard circle. She drew numbers with her green crayon, big and round. She made two little hands from a popsicle stick and a twist tie. “Tick-tock, hop-hop,” she giggled.

Her friend, Roo the pigeon, landed nearby with a soft flutter. “Coo! What are you making, Bibi?”

“A clock from recycling,” Bibi said. “It helps me stop and go.”

Roo pecked at a bottle cap. “Coo, coo! Can I help?”

“Yes,” Bibi said. “Please hand me the cap.” Roo nudged it over. Bibi glued it as a button on the clock. “Press here to start. Press here to stop,” she explained, tapping. “Start for plants. Stop at ten. Start for words. Stop after five songs. Start for art. Stop when my chart is done.”

Roo cooed happily. “You are good at time.”

Bibi’s ears perked up. “I am learning.” She checked the picture chart again. “One, two, three. All done!” She felt a warm, fizzy joy in her tummy. It felt like hot cocoa on a cool day.

Mama came up with a plate of apple slices. The apples were crisp and sweet. “How did it go?” she asked.

Bibi pointed to the garden. “Plants are happy.” She pointed to the cards. “I learned sol and gato and agua.” She held up her clock. “And I made this with recycling!”

Mama hugged Bibi. “You used your time with care. You started and stopped. You focused on one thing at a time. That is smart time-management.”

Bibi nodded. “When I plan, I have time for everything. And I can enjoy each thing.”

Roo flapped his wings. “Coo! Can we learn a word for friend?”

Bibi thought. “Amigo,” she said. “Amigo means friend.”

Roo puffed up. “Coo! Amigo!”

The sun began to set, painting the rooftop pink and orange. The wind chimes sang softly. Bibi placed her recycling clock near the door. She looked at the picture chart and drew a big green check mark.

She whispered, “Plan, do, stop, and smile.” Her nose wiggled. “Tomorrow I will plan again.”

Mama kissed her head. “Good plan, little rabbit.”

Bibi felt calm. She had time to rest, time to learn, and time to help the Earth by reusing. Her heart felt full.

And the rooftop felt like the perfect place to hop into a new day, one tick-tock at a time.

The End.
