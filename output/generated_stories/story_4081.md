# Slow Steps in the Dining Room

Tomo was a shy turtle. He lived with Mia and her family. Tomo liked quiet corners and soft rugs. He liked to watch and think.

One sunny morning, the dining room smelled like warm toast and sweet strawberries. The table shone in the light. The chairs stood like tall trees. The floor felt cool and smooth under Tomo’s little feet.

“Tomo,” Mia said, smiling. “Breakfast is ready. Do you want to join us?”

Tomo peeked out from his shell. His heart thumped softly. He wanted the strawberries. He wanted to sit near Mia. But the dining room felt big. The chairs made long shadows. The sounds were loud—clink, clink, clink went the spoons. Tap, tap, tap went the cups.

Tomo took a slow breath. He smelled toast and honey. He heard Mia’s giggle. He felt the cool floor on his belly. He did not rush. He waited and watched.

Mia set a tiny plate on the floor by her chair. “I saved you a strawberry slice,” she said. “It’s sweet and red.”

Tomo thought, Should I go now? Or should I wait? He looked at the path. Chair legs were close together. A napkin was on the floor like a little hill. Tomo made a plan.

“I will move in slow steps,” Tomo whispered to himself. “I will look, listen, and then go.”

He took one step. Tap. He paused. He listened to the spoons. Clink. Clink. He looked left and right. He felt okay.

He took another step. Tap. He paused again. He looked at the napkin hill. “I can go around,” he decided.

Mia saw him inching forward. “You can do it, Tomo,” she said softly. “Slow is okay.”

Papa smiled from the table. “Good planning, little friend,” he said.

Tomo moved like a tiny boat in a calm pond. Tap. Pause. Tap. Pause. His shell rubbed gently on the floor—scritch, scritch.

Halfway there, a chair moved. Squeeeak! Tomo pulled into his shell. The sound felt big in his ears. The world went quiet and round.

“It’s okay,” Mia said in a whisper. She waited. She did not push him. She took a deep breath so he could hear. In. Out.

Tomo listened to the breathing. In. Out. The big feeling got smaller. He peeked out again. The chair had stopped.

“I can wait. I can try again,” Tomo told himself. He made another choice. “I will move when it is quiet.”

He waited. He watched the shadows. He listened for clinks. He felt the cool air on his face. When the room felt calm, he took the next step. Tap.

Soon, Tomo reached the napkin hill. He touched it with his toe. It felt soft. He could climb it, but the edges were bumpy. He thought, Which way is best?

He looked left. He looked right. The right side was narrower. The left side was wide. “Left is safer,” Tomo decided.

He went left. Tap. Pause. Tap. Pause. He tasted the air. Sweet strawberry smell danced on his tongue. His tummy rumbled. “Almost there,” he said.

Mia clapped softly. “I’m proud of your slow steps,” she said.

At last, Tomo reached the tiny plate by Mia’s chair. The strawberry slice shone like a red smile. He touched it with his nose. It was cool and juicy. He took a small bite. Squish! The juice dripped on his chin. It tasted like summer and sunshine.

“Mmm,” Tomo hummed. His cheeks felt warm with happy.

Mia put a small cup of water beside him. “Sip?” she asked.

Tomo looked at the cup. It wobbled a little on the rug. He waited for it to be still. He took a tiny sip. The water felt fresh and smooth.

When the table shook and a spoon clinked loudly, Tomo paused again. He tucked in for a moment. He breathed. In. Out. The big sound passed. Then he peeked out and smiled at Mia.

“I did it with patience,” Tomo said.

“Yes,” Mia said. “You made a plan. You took slow steps. You waited when you needed to. That is patience.”

Papa nodded. “And you made good choices. That is decision-making.”

Tomo wiggled his toes. “And I moved my brave little feet,” he added.

After breakfast, Mia placed a tiny path of napkins on the floor like stepping stones. “These can help you practice,” she said. “One slow step at a time.”

Tomo tried the napkin path. Tap. Pause. Tap. Pause. He listened, looked, and moved. He felt the soft napkin under his toes and the smooth floor in between. He felt calm. He felt proud.

When he reached the end, Mia cheered, “Hooray for patient turtles!”

Tomo lifted his head high. “Slow steps help me do big things,” he said.

He took one more bite of strawberry. Sweet. Juicy. Bright.

“I can wait. I can choose. I can move,” Tomo whispered to himself.

And in the quiet dining room, with warm light and happy hearts, Tomo the shy turtle learned that patience makes brave moments bloom.

The End.
