# Brave Paws in the Warm Kitchen

In a warm kitchen that smelled like sweet honey and cinnamon, a friendly bear cub named Benny stood on a small, sturdy stool. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. The kettle hummed softly. The big round clock tick-tock, tick-tock. Benny’s ears perked up. He wiggled his fuzzy paws.

“I want to help make breakfast!” Benny said, his eyes bright.

Mama Bear smiled and tied a tiny apron around Benny. “Helping is wonderful,” she said. “But first, safety.”

Benny blinked. “What is safety, Mama?”

Mama Bear tapped her nose. “Safety means keeping our bodies safe. We use our eyes, ears, nose, and paws to notice and to be careful. We follow rules so we don’t get hurt.”

Benny nodded. “I can be careful. I am brave!”

“Brave and safe,” Mama said. She pointed to the shiny stovetop. “Hot. We do not touch.” She put a bright red sticker near it. “Red means stop.”

Benny gazed at the sticker. “Red means stop. I remember.” He pressed his paws together so he wouldn’t reach out.

Mama Bear set a mixing bowl on the table. “Let’s make honey pancakes. We will build your cooking skills. I will do the hot parts. You will do the cool parts.”

Benny sniffed the honey. It smelled warm and flowery. He giggled and licked a little drippy drop from his paw. “Mmm!”

“First,” Mama said, “we wash paws.” She turned on the water, not too hot, not too cold. Benny felt the gentle splash. Swish, swish. Soap bubbles popped with tiny snaps. Pop! Pop!

“Clean paws are safe paws,” Benny said proudly.

They counted ingredients together. “One cup flour. Two splashes of milk. Three drips of honey,” Mama said.

Benny poured slowly. “One… two… three,” he counted, tongue peeking out with careful thinking. The spoon scraped the bowl—scritch, scritch. Benny stirred and stirred. The batter was thick and stretchy.

“You are building skills,” Mama said. “Stirring, counting, and listening.”

Benny puffed up with pride. “I am a skill builder!”

Suddenly, the kettle made a sharp whistle. Wheeeeee! Benny jumped, and the bowl wobbled. A little batter splashed onto the floor with a plop.

Benny’s ears drooped. “Oh no.”

Mama Bear held up a paw. “It’s okay. Accidents happen. We stop. We breathe. We think.”

Benny took a slow breath in. He let it out. “Stop. Breathe. Think.”

“Now, what is our safe choice?” Mama asked gently.

Benny looked down. The floor was sticky. “We clean the spill so no one slips.”

“Good plan,” Mama said. She gave Benny a soft cloth. Together, they wiped the floor. Wipe, wipe. Benny smiled again. “Safe and clean!”

Mama turned to the stove. “I will pour the batter on the hot pan. You stay on your stool, paws on the table. Eyes watching.”

Benny nodded. “Red means stop. I do not touch hot.”

The batter sizzled—szzzz! The smell of warm honey filled the air. Benny’s tummy rumbled. “When do I flip?” he asked.

“You are brave to ask,” Mama said. “But flipping is hot. Today, I flip. You will learn to flip when you are bigger, and with help.”

Benny’s heart felt a tiny squeeze. He wanted to do everything. He looked at the pan. The edges bubbled. The heat shimmered.

“I can wait,” he said softly. “Waiting is brave, too.”

Mama smiled. “Yes, it is.” She slid the spatula under a pancake—flip! “Brave is not doing risky things. Brave is making the safe choice, even when you really want to do more.”

Benny’s face lit up. “I can be brave and safe!”

They set cooked pancakes on a plate. Steam curled like little clouds. Mama moved the pan away, clicked off the stove, and pointed to the red sticker. “Hot is off. Now it is safe.”

Benny clapped. “What can I do now?”

“You can add the toppings,” Mama said. She placed sliced berries and a small honey pot on the table. “Use this little spoon.”

Benny drizzled honey in gentle lines. Drip… drip… drip. He placed berries in a smiley face. “Heehee!”

Then Mama Bear said, “Let’s learn one more skill: how to check for heat.” She set a pancake on Benny’s plate and waved her paw over it. “Hold your paw above. Feel the warm air?”

Benny held his paw over the pancake. “Warm!” He waited for a count of five. “One, two, three, four, five.” The steam slowed. He blew softly—whooo. “Ready now.”

They ate together. The pancakes were sweet and soft. Benny wiggled his toes. “I helped! I was safe! I was brave!”

Mama Bear hugged him. “You used your senses. You followed rules. You cleaned up spills. You waited for help with hot things. That is the smart kind of brave.”

Benny nuzzled into the hug. “Brave and safe,” he repeated.

After breakfast, Benny drew a picture. He drew a red sticker, a little stool, soap bubbles, and a big smiling pancake. At the top, he wrote—very big—SAFE.

He taped the picture on the kitchen wall. “This is our Safety Picture,” Benny said. “So we always remember.”

Mama Bear nodded. “Our warm kitchen is a safe place because we make safe choices.”

Benny felt proud from his nose to his paws. He had learned skills. He had been brave by waiting and asking for help. He had kept himself safe. His smile was as warm as the sunlit floor.

The End.
