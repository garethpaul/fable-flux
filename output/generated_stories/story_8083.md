# Leo's Colorful Heart

Leo the lion cub bounced into the bright art room. Sunlight streamed through big windows, making the colorful paints sparkle like jewels. Today was special - it was Art Day at school!

"Good morning, little artists!" said Ms. Giraffe, their tall teacher. "Today we're painting pictures of our favorite healthy foods!"

Leo's golden eyes grew wide with excitement. He loved to paint! He picked up his brush and dipped it in sunny yellow paint. "I'm going to paint bananas!" he announced proudly.

At the table next to him sat Ruby, a quiet little rabbit. She had fluffy gray fur and the tiniest voice. Ruby stared at her blank paper with sad eyes.

"What's wrong, Ruby?" Leo asked, his brush dripping with paint.

"I... I forgot my lunch money again," Ruby whispered. "And I don't have any crayons or paints at home. I can't think of any healthy foods to paint."

Leo's heart felt heavy like a big rock. He looked at his own art supplies - a whole box of bright crayons, three paintbrushes, and squeeze bottles full of every color imaginable.

"That's not fair," Leo said. He set down his brush and thought hard. His mama always said, "A brave heart shares what it has."

Leo had an idea! His whiskers twitched with excitement.

"Ruby, want to share my paints?" Leo asked. "We can paint together!"

Ruby's pink nose wiggled with surprise. "Really? But then you won't have enough for your own picture."

"There's plenty for both of us!" Leo grinned, showing his tiny white teeth. "And I know lots about healthy foods! My mama packs me apples and carrots every day. They make my teeth strong and my fur shiny!"

Ruby's eyes sparkled like dewdrops. "I love carrots too! They make a nice crunching sound."

Leo scooted his chair closer to Ruby. "Here, use my orange paint for carrots. And this red is perfect for apples!"

Together, they painted the most beautiful picture. Leo painted yellow bananas that looked like smiling moons. Ruby painted orange carrots that looked like magic wands. They shared the green paint for broccoli trees and the red paint for strawberry hearts.

"Look at all these healthy foods!" Ruby giggled. "They give us energy to run and play!"

"And strong bones to climb trees!" Leo added, painting purple grapes that looked like tiny balloons.

Ms. Giraffe walked over and smiled her biggest smile. "What a wonderful painting! I can see you two worked together beautifully."

"Leo shared all his paints with me," Ruby said softly. "He's the kindest friend ever."

Leo's chest puffed up with pride, but not because everyone was looking at him. He felt proud because Ruby was happy. Her smile was brighter than all the paints in the whole art room.

"Sharing makes everything more fun," Leo said. "And now we both have the most colorful picture!"

When it was time to clean up, other students came to admire their artwork. Everyone loved seeing all the healthy foods painted in rainbow colors.

"Next time, I want to share my supplies too," said Max the monkey.

"Me too!" chirped Penny the parrot.

Leo learned something important that day. When you share what you have, you don't get less - you get more! More friends, more fun, and more happiness in your heart.

And Ruby learned that asking for help isn't scary when you have kind friends around you.

The End.
