# Joey Roo’s Pantry Plan

Joey Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey. He loved to hop and hum. One sunny morning, he opened the pantry door. Creak! The pantry smelled like warm oats and sweet honey. Little jars clinked. Paper bags rustled. Joey’s tummy said, “Rumble!”

“Oh my,” said Joey. He saw a mess. Flour had spilled like white snow. A bag of oats was torn. Raisins had rolled under the shelf. “Friends could slip,” he said. “We need a plan!”

Mama Roo peeked in. “It’s okay, Joey,” she said kindly. “We can clean together.”

Joey’s eyes sparkled. “Let’s make it fun! Let’s invent a way to clean fast. A helpful way!”

Mama smiled. “That sounds like innovation. Innovation means trying new ideas to solve a problem.”

Joey clapped. “I can try a new idea!”

He sniffed the air. “Flour smells soft and dusty.” He touched it with one finger. “Poof!” White puff on his nose. He giggled. “I need a scooper.”

Joey looked around. He found a small plastic cup. He found a cardboard tube. He found a soft brush. He thought, and thought some more. “Hmm,” he hummed. “What can I make?”

He set the cup on the tube and wrapped it with a rubber band. “Look, Mama! A long scooper!” He brushed the flour into a little hill and used the scooper to lift it. “Swoop!” Into the bowl it went.

“Great thinking,” said Mama. “That is innovation. You used what you had.”

Joey’s ears wiggled. “Now the raisins!” He listened. Plink, plink, plink went the raisins as he dropped them into a jar. He made a game. “Hello, little raisin. Plink! Hello, little raisin. Plink!”

Tap-tap. There was a sound at the door. It was Wally Wombat and Kiki Koala. “Can we play outside?” asked Wally.

Joey looked at the pantry mess. He felt a little sad. “I want to play, but I need to help,” he said. “The floor is slippery.”

Kiki nodded. “We can help! That is being kind.”

Joey smiled wide. “Thank you! Let’s be helpers together.” He showed them his long scooper. “I made this to clean the flour.”

“Wow,” said Wally. “You invented a tool!”

Kiki sniffed. “I smell oranges.” Joey found a basket of oranges in the pantry. He had an idea. “Fresh air helps dry spills. Let’s take the oranges outside for a snack when we finish,” he said. “Then we will have outdoor activity and a rest.”

They worked as a team. Joey scooped. Wally used the soft brush. Swish, swish. Kiki picked up raisins. Plink, plink. Mama wiped the shelf. Wipe, wipe. They sang a little song:

“Scoop and swish, plink and wipe,
Helpful hearts make work feel light!”

Soon the floor looked clean and safe. Joey felt proud. “We did it!” he cheered.

But the bag of oats was still torn. “Uh-oh,” said Wally. “The oats will fall again.”

Joey thought. He looked at the cardboard tube. He looked at a clean jar. “I have a new idea!” he said. “Let’s make a funnel.”

He cut the tube shorter. He put it in the mouth of the jar. He held the torn bag over the tube. “Ready?” he asked.

“Ready,” said Kiki. “I will hold the jar.”

Joey poured very slowly. Shhhhhh went the oats like soft rain. The jar filled up. No spills!

“You made a funnel,” Mama said. “That is smart innovation.”

Joey beamed. “We solved the problem with a new idea. We were careful and kind.”

Kiki clapped. “Now can we go outside?”

“Yes,” said Mama. “You were very helpful.”

They hopped to the backyard. The grass was cool under their feet. The sky was bright and blue. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” A gentle breeze smelled like flowers and clean air. Joey placed a blanket on the ground. He set out the oranges. Peel, peel. The skin made a zesty pop. The juice felt sweet and sticky on their fingers.

“This orange is sunny!” said Wally, giggling.

Joey passed slices to everyone. “Here you go,” he said. “We share and care.”

They played hop-and-stop on the grass. Hop, hop, stop! They raced leaves in a tiny stream. Swish, swish, whoosh! They stretched their arms up to the sky. “Thank you, outside,” Kiki whispered.

After play, they sat and breathed. In, out. Calm and happy.

Mama looked proud. “Joey, what did you learn today?”

Joey thought. He touched his nose, still a little floury. He smiled. “I learned I can try new ideas to fix a problem,” he said. “That is innovation. I learned that helping friends is kind. I learned that outdoor play helps us feel strong and calm.”

Kiki nodded. “And oranges are yummy!”

Wally laughed. “And a tube can be a funnel!”

Joey hugged his friends. “When we see a mess, we can make a plan. We can think, try, and try again. We can help each other. We can play outside and rest after work.”

Mama gave Joey a gentle pat. “That is a wonderful lesson,” she said.

Joey looked at the bright sky. He felt brave, helpful, and smart. “Tomorrow,” he said, “I will find a new idea again.”

The End.
