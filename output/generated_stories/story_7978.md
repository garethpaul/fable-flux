# Mango's Thank-You Day

In a sunny jungle, a courageous monkey named Mango lived in a cozy treehouse. The treehouse smelled like sweet leaves and warm wood. It had a soft leaf bed, a tiny rope ladder, and a window where the breeze tickled Mango’s cheeks.

One bright morning, Mango rubbed his eyes and smiled. “Good morning, Treehouse! Good morning, jungle!” he chirped. Birds sang, “Tweet, tweet!” and the sun painted the floor with golden stripes.

Mango had a plan. “Today is Thank-You Day,” he said. “I will find things to be grateful for. I will help my friends. I will be honest and kind.”

He grabbed his little leaf bag and climbed down the rope ladder. It felt rough and a little warm in the sun. As he reached the ground, he heard a tiny sniffle.

“Sniff… sniff…” It was Pip, a small parrot with green feathers. Pip’s kite string was stuck on a branch near Mango’s treehouse window.

“My kite!” Pip said, flapping his wings. “It is stuck. I pulled too hard.”

Mango looked up. The kite danced and tugged. “I can help,” he said bravely. He climbed the tree, paw over paw, tail steady, heart strong. Leaves brushed his fur. He reached the window and gently untangled the string.

“Here you go, Pip,” Mango said, handing down the kite.

“Thank you, Mango!” Pip chirped. “You are very brave.”

Mango felt warm inside. “I am grateful I can climb,” he said. “I am grateful we help each other.”

He waved goodbye and walked along a leafy path. Crunch, crunch went dry leaves under his feet. He sniffed the air. It smelled like sweet mangoes and cool water.

Soon Mango found Tumba the turtle near a puddle. Tumba’s wagon was stuck in the mud. “Oh dear,” Tumba sighed. “My wagon won’t move.”

“I can help,” Mango said. He pushed and pushed. “1, 2, 3… push!” The wagon rolled free with a squelch.

“Thank you,” Tumba said with a slow smile. “You are strong and kind.”

Mango grinned. “I am grateful for my strong arms,” he said. “And I am grateful for your smile.”

They laughed together. The jungle felt extra friendly.

Mango walked on and heard a gentle hum. “Mmm-mmm.” It was Sana the squirrel, carrying shiny nuts. One nut slipped and rolled to Mango’s feet.

“Oh!” Mango picked it up. The nut looked so smooth and tasty. His tummy rumbled. He wanted to keep it. But then Mango took a deep breath. “Honesty,” he whispered. “Tell the truth.”

“This is your nut,” Mango said, holding it out. “It rolled to me.”

Sana’s eyes sparkled. “Thank you for being honest, Mango. That helps me a lot.”

Mango’s heart felt light and happy. “I am grateful I can tell the truth,” he said. “It makes my heart feel good.”

Sana smiled. “Would you like to share a nut with me?” she asked.

“Yes, please!” Mango said. They sat under a shady leaf and shared a crunchy snack. It tasted salty and sweet. Mango licked his fingers and giggled. “Yum!”

After snack time, Mango looked up at the sky. “I will go home now,” he said. He climbed back to his treehouse and sat by the window. The breeze was cool on his face. The jungle sounded cozy—birds singing, leaves whispering, and faraway frogs saying, “Ribbit, ribbit.”

Mango took out a small leaf journal. He liked to draw pictures of his day. With a berry stick, he drew a kite in the sky. He drew a wagon rolling free. He drew a shiny nut and a big smile.

Then Mango said out loud, “I am grateful for my safe treehouse. I am grateful for my brave body. I am grateful for my honest heart. I am grateful for my friends.”

Knock, knock! “Who is there?” Mango asked.

“It’s us!” Pip, Tumba, and Sana called. They climbed the rope ladder, step by step, and peeked in with big smiles.

“We came to say thank you,” Pip chirped. “You helped us.”

“We brought you a gift,” Tumba added. He held a tiny basket made of grass.

Inside the basket were three small things: a bright feather from Pip, a smooth stone from Tumba, and a sweet berry from Sana. They smelled like sunshine and love.

Mango’s eyes grew wide. “For me?” he asked.

“For you,” they all said.

Mango felt tears of happy. “Thank you,” he whispered. “I am so grateful. I will keep these treasures safe.”

They all sat together in the treehouse. Mango poured cool water from a shell cup. They sipped and smiled.

Pip said, “You were brave.”

Tumba said, “You were helpful.”

Sana said, “You were honest.”

Mango nodded. “Today I learned that gratitude makes my heart big. Helping others makes the day bright. Honesty makes trust. I am thankful for you.”

The sun yawned and turned the sky pink and orange. The friends hugged. “Good night,” they said.

Mango curled up in his leaf bed. The treehouse was quiet and warm. He closed his eyes and whispered, “Thank you for this day.”

His heart felt full, and his smile was soft.

The End.
