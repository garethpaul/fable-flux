# Rooftop Hops and Friendly Stops

Sunny Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with big soft ears and a bouncy tail. One bright morning, Sunny Roo hopped up the safe, flat rooftop of the community center with a gentle thump-thump-thump. The rooftop garden smelled like mint and warm tomatoes. A soft breeze tickled Sunny Roo’s whiskers. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” and the sky looked like a big blue blanket.

“Hello, rooftop!” Sunny Roo giggled. “I’m ready to hop!”

Sunny Roo wasn’t hopping alone. A little mouse named Pip peeked from a planter. “Squeak! Can I ride in your pouch?” Pip asked, eyes shiny.

Sunny Roo smiled. “Hi, Pip! My pouch is special. It is my space. Today, I want to keep it just for me.”

Pip blinked. “Oh. Okay,” he squeaked softly.

Sunny Roo felt warm and brave. Saying no felt new, like trying a fresh, crunchy carrot. It was Sunny Roo’s personal boundary—an important line that kept the joey feeling safe and comfy.

Just then, a cheerful parrot, Lolly, flapped down and ruffled Sunny Roo’s fur with a feathery wing. “Tickle, tickle! Let’s play!” Lolly squawked.

Sunny Roo scooted back and placed small paws on the warm rooftop tiles. “Lolly, I like to play,” Sunny Roo said in a calm voice, “but I don’t like surprise tickles. Please ask first.”

Lolly paused, head tilting. “Ask first?”

“Yes,” said Sunny Roo. “Please ask, ‘Can I tickle you?’ Then I can say yes or no.”

Lolly blinked, then smiled. “Okay! Can I tickle you?”

Sunny Roo thought. The breeze brushed gently. The sun felt cozy. “No thank you,” said Sunny Roo. “Let’s flap and hop instead!”

“Flap and hop it is!” Lolly laughed, and they flapped and hopped around a square chalk drawing. Their feet went pat-pat, their shadows danced, and both friends giggled.

Soon, Sunny Roo saw a shiny red ball roll near the edge of the rooftop. A raccoon kit named Rocco chased it, paws skittering. “My ball! My ball!” Rocco cried.

Sunny Roo’s ears perked. The edge was not a safe place for play. “Stop, Rocco!” Sunny Roo called. “We need a safe space.”

Rocco skidded to a stop. “But I want my ball,” he sniffled.

Sunny Roo took a slow breath. The joey’s heart thump-thumped, not from hopping but from thinking. “We can get your ball together,” Sunny Roo said. “First, let’s make a safe plan. The edge is a ‘no-go zone.’ That is a boundary for all of us.”

Pip squeaked, “Cooperation time!”

Sunny Roo nodded. “Yes. Lolly, can you fly to block the ball? Pip, stand with me and wave so Rocco sees the ‘no-go zone.’ Rocco, please wait here.”

“Okay,” Rocco said, wiping his eyes.

Lolly swooped gently and used a wing to nudge the ball away from the edge. Sunny Roo and Pip waved their arms and tail like tall grass. “No-go zone! No-go zone!” they chanted.

The ball rolled back to the middle. Rocco hugged it tight. “Thank you,” he said, cheeks rosy. “I didn’t know about the no-go zone.”

Sunny Roo smiled. “That’s okay. We learned it together.”

Then, a new problem popped up. A big kangaroo cousin, Boomer, bounced onto the rooftop with a boing-boing. “Sunny Roo! Let’s do super-swoop hugs!” Boomer scooped Sunny Roo up in a giant squeeze.

Sunny Roo’s nose smushed against Boomer’s fur. It smelled like pine and dust. Sunny Roo felt squished and wiggly. “Boomer,” Sunny Roo said, voice muffled, “I need space. Please put me down.”

Boomer blinked, surprised, and set Sunny Roo gently on the warm tiles. “Did I make you uncomfy?” Boomer asked.

Sunny Roo nodded. “I like hugs when I’m ready. Please ask first. My body is mine.”

Boomer’s ears drooped. “I’m sorry, Sunny Roo. May I give you a gentle hug?”

Sunny Roo thought again. The joey checked in with the inside feeling—like looking at a little weather map in the heart. Today felt “breezy okay.” “Yes, a gentle hug,” Sunny Roo said, opening small arms.

Boomer gave a soft, slow hug. “Thank you for telling me your boundary,” Boomer said. “You were brave.”

Sunny Roo felt tall inside. Resilience was like a spring in the tail—bounce back, try again, speak up kindly.

As the sun began to slide and the rooftop shadows grew long, Sunny Roo drew three simple signs with chalk:

- “My Space” with a picture of a pouch.
- “Ask First” with a question mark.
- “No-Go Zone” with a line near the edge.

Sunny Roo showed the signs to everyone. “These help us remember,” Sunny Roo said. “We can be kind to ourselves and kind to our friends.”

Pip squeaked, “Can I sit beside you?”

“Yes,” Sunny Roo smiled. “Thank you for asking.”

Lolly chirped, “Can I tickle your ear?”

Sunny Roo giggled. “Not today. Let’s sing instead.”

Rocco bounced his ball in the center. “Can I roll it to you?”

“Yes, in the safe zone,” Sunny Roo said.

Boomer asked, “Can I take a picture of our signs?”

“Yes,” Sunny Roo said, “and please hold my paw, not my arm.”

Boomer nodded and did just that. Everyone smiled. The air smelled like mint again. The rooftop felt safe, warm, and friendly.

Sunny Roo looked around and said, “When we say what we need, and listen to what friends need, everyone feels safe. Boundaries help our hearts feel happy.”

The friends flapped, squeaked, bounced, and sang. They made room for each other. They asked first. They took turns. They stayed away from the no-go zone. And they had so much fun that even the clouds seemed to smile.

The End.
