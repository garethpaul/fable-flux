# Ready, Set, Paws!

Sunny, the helpful robot dog, woke up with a soft beep-beep and a happy wag of her shiny tail. She lived with her friends on a small balcony garden. The garden smelled like mint and sweet strawberries. Little bells on the tomato vines tinkled in the breeze. Today was Sports Day on the balcony!

Sunny said, “Good morning, team! Today we run, hop, toss, and cheer. Let’s get ready!”

Her friends came outside. Maya wore a yellow headband. Jamal had bright blue socks. Lina rolled onto the balcony in her speedy purple wheelchair. Kenji carried a small drum. Amira wore a green scarf with tiny stars.

Maya asked, “Can we start now?”

Sunny’s ears glowed. “First, we prepare. Preparation helps us do our best. Step one: check our bodies.”

Sunny led them in a gentle stretch. “Reach up like tall sunflowers,” she said.

“Like this?” Jamal giggled, wiggling his fingers toward the sun.

“Yes! Breathe in… and out,” Sunny said, taking slow robot breaths that sounded like whooo… click… whooo… click. Everyone breathed slowly. The mint leaves rustled. The air felt cool and kind.

“Step two: check our space,” Sunny said. She glanced around with her bright blue eyes. “We make it safe.”

They moved the watering can out of the way. Lina tested the balcony ramp. “Smooth and steady,” she said, tapping the wheels. Kenji tied back the tomato vines with soft ribbon so no one would trip. Amira checked the flower boxes. “Bee friends are busy,” she whispered. “We will share the space.”

Sunny nodded. “Step three: check our tools.” She opened her belly pocket with a gentle click. Out came small cones, a soft beanbag, and a roll of rainbow tape.

They marked a zig-zag path with the rainbow tape. The colors shone bright—red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple. Maya clapped. “It looks like a happy snake!”

“Ready?” asked Sunny.

“Ready!” everyone said.

The first game was the Beanbag Balance. Each friend balanced a beanbag on their head or lap while rolling or walking along the rainbow path. Sunny wagged and cheered, “Slow and steady. Eyes forward. Breathe.”

Lina focused. “In… out…” She rolled smoothly, beanbag steady on her lap. Kenji beat a soft drum rhythm: dum… dum… dum. The sound helped them move at the same calm pace.

Next was the Hop-and-Toss. Jamal hopped like a bunny and tossed the beanbag into a basket. Maya wiggled her toes and whispered, “I can do it.” Amira took a mindful moment, placing a hand on her belly. “I feel my breath. In… out…”

When it was Maya’s turn, the beanbag missed the basket and landed in the basil, rustling leaves. Maya’s face got tight. “I can’t do it!” she cried.

Sunny knelt and blinked softly. “It’s okay to feel upset. Let’s pause.” Sunny tapped her paw and the small bell on her collar chimed. “Mindful moment. Smell the basil.” They all leaned in. The basil smelled fresh and peppery.

“Now, let’s prepare again,” Sunny said. “We can try three steps: pause, plan, practice.”

“Pause,” Maya said, breathing in and out. “Plan,” she decided, “I will stand with my feet apart. I will look at the basket. I will toss gently.” She practiced tossing to Sunny first. The beanbag made a soft puh sound against Sunny’s paw.

“You prepared,” Sunny said. “Now try.”

Maya tossed. Plop! The beanbag landed in the basket. Everyone cheered. Sunny’s tail lights twinkled. “Preparation helps us do our best!” she sang.

Then, a tiny problem. A bee buzzed near the path. Jamal froze. “I’m scared.”

Sunny nodded. “Thank you for saying how you feel. Let’s solve it together.” Amira suggested, “We can give the bee space.” Kenji pointed to the marigolds. “The bee likes those.” Lina gently rolled a small flower box closer to the marigolds. Sunny waved a safety flag. “We will wait. Breathe in… out…”

The bee drifted to the marigolds, happy and busy. “Problem solved with kindness,” Sunny said.

For the last game, they made a team relay. Lina rolled the first part fast, hands strong on her wheels. She tagged Kenji with a gentle tap. Kenji drummed a beat for Maya, who skipped along the rainbow path. Maya tagged Amira, who walked small, careful steps and smiled. Jamal finished with a big hop and a big laugh. Sunny ran beside them, making happy beep-beeps.

They ended with a cool-down. Sunny said, “Feel the sun on your cheeks. Hear the leaves whisper. Taste the mint on your tongue if you like.” Everyone picked one small mint leaf and smiled at the bright, cool taste.

Sunny gathered them in a circle. “What did we learn today?”

Maya said, “Pause, plan, practice!”

Jamal said, “Preparation helps me feel ready.”

Lina said, “Everyone can play. We just make space and tools work for us.”

Kenji said, “Kind breathing helps my body.”

Amira said, “We can share with bees.”

Sunny wagged, happy and proud. “We prepared our bodies, our space, and our tools. We solved problems with calm hearts. We played as a team. That is how we do our best.”

They packed up the cones and tape. They watered the mint and tomatoes. The balcony smelled fresh and green. The sun felt warm, like a hug.

Sunny looked at her friends. “Ready for tomorrow?”

“Ready!” they cheered.

The End.
