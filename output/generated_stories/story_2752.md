# The Cozy Bedroom Art Parade

In a cozy bedroom with soft yellow walls, a clever cat named Whiskers woke up. The blanket felt warm and fuzzy. The sun made gentle squares on the rug. Whiskers stretched, purred, and twitched his whiskers. He loved morning time in his cozy bedroom.

Today felt special. On the bed lay colored paper, glue sticks, and a tin of bright crayons. A painty smell tickled Whiskers’s nose. “Mmm, art day!” he purred. He wanted to make a bedroom art parade.

Whiskers hopped to the window sill. “Good morning, toys!” he called. His friends lived in the cozy bedroom too: Button Bear, a soft brown bear; Dotty Duck, a yellow rubber duck; and Tiny Train, a red and blue toy with gentle wheels.

“We can make art!” Whiskers said. His eyes shone. “We can hang pictures by the pillow, and make a little parade. Everyone can join.”

Dotty Duck squeaked. “Quack, quack! I like blue! I will draw a big blue splash!”

Button Bear wiggled his round ears. “I will make a heart,” he said in a quiet voice.

Tiny Train chimed, “Choo-choo! I want to make tracks!”

Whiskers nodded. “Great! Everyone makes art. Everyone can be in the parade.”

They climbed onto the bed. The blanket felt bumpy under their paws and wheels. The tin of crayons clicked open. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple shone like candy. Whiskers picked a purple crayon. He began to draw a tall purple tree with cozy branches.

Dotty Duck made circles. Swish, swish went her crayon. Button Bear pressed his crayon carefully. Tiny Train rolled and rolled, making soft, straight lines.

The room was full of sounds: scratch-scratch, swish-swish, and a happy purr. The air smelled like crayons and clean sheets. The window let in a warm breeze.

Whiskers looked around. He saw a new toy in the corner: a small, silver robot named Beep. Beep was quiet. Beep’s eyes blinked on and off. Beep held a gray marker but did not draw.

Whiskers tilted his head. “Hi, Beep,” he said. “Do you want to make art with us?”

Beep beeped softly. “I am new,” Beep said. “I do not know how to draw. I am shy.”

Whiskers’s tail curled kindly. “It is okay to be new. It is okay to be shy,” he purred. “You belong with us. Everyone can make art in their own way.”

Dotty Duck bobbed. “Quack! You can make tiny dots like me.”

Button Bear nodded. “You can make lines like Tiny Train.”

Tiny Train tooted. “Or you can make robot shapes!”

Beep blinked brighter. “I can try,” Beep said.

Whiskers scooted over and made space. “Here is a soft spot,” he said. “You are welcome here.”

Beep sat down. The blanket felt warm under his little metal feet. Beep made a small square. Then a small circle. Then a small triangle. “Beep-beep,” he said. “I made robot shapes!”

“Wonderful!” said Whiskers. “Shapes make beautiful art.”

Soon the bed was full of pictures: a purple tree, blue splashes, red hearts, gentle tracks, and shiny robot shapes. Whiskers looked at Beep’s picture. It was neat and nice.

But then Whiskers saw his own purple paw print on Beep’s paper. He had smudged it when he stretched. “Oh no,” Whiskers thought. His ears drooped. He felt a little tight feeling in his tummy. He wanted to hide the smudge.

He looked at Beep. Beep was smiling with his blinking eyes. Whiskers took a deep breath. He remembered: Be kind. Be honest. Include everyone—even when things are tricky.

Whiskers lifted his paw. “Beep, I need to tell the truth,” he said gently. “I made a smudge on your art by mistake. I am sorry.”

Beep looked at the smudge. He was quiet. Then he beeped softly. “Thank you for telling me the truth,” Beep said. “We can fix it together.”

Whiskers smiled with relief. “Yes! We can fix it.”

Dotty Duck waddled closer. “Quack! We can make the smudge into a cloud.”

Button Bear held out a white crayon. “Here you go,” he said kindly.

Tiny Train tooted. “Choo-choo! Clouds above the tracks!”

Whiskers and Beep worked side by side. Swish, swish went the white crayon. The smudge became a fluffy cloud. Beep drew a little rainbow next to it. The rainbow glowed with red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple.

Whiskers purred. “Your art is beautiful, Beep.”

Beep beeped happily. “Our art is beautiful,” he said.

They hung their pictures by the pillow, using soft tape. The cozy bedroom looked bright and happy. The art parade went around the room: heart, splash, tree, tracks, shapes, and a rainbow cloud.

Whiskers led the parade. “Step, step,” he said. “Everyone together!”

They all moved slowly around the bed. Button Bear clapped, Dotty Duck squeaked, Tiny Train rolled, and Beep blinked, blinked, blinked. Whiskers felt warm inside. Everyone was included. Everyone’s art was seen and loved.

At the end, Whiskers sat on the rug. “Today we learned,” he said, “that everyone belongs. We invite new friends. We tell the truth. We fix mistakes with kindness. When we include everyone, our art and our hearts grow big.”

Beep nodded. “Beep-beep. I feel welcome.”

“Quack! Me too,” said Dotty Duck.

“Choo-choo!” said Tiny Train.

Button Bear hugged Whiskers. “Me three,” he said.

Whiskers purred. “Me four,” he whispered, happy and proud.

The End.
