# Buzzy Timer and the Bath-Time Plan

Buzzy was a friendly bear cub with soft brown fur and a little round belly. He loved to splash, play, and sing. One evening, Mama Bear called, “Buzzy, it’s bath time!”

Buzzy looked at his toy cars and his blocks. He felt a little tug in his tummy. “But I want to keep playing,” he whispered. His ears felt warm, and his paws wiggled. He didn’t want to be late, but he didn’t want to stop either.

Mama Bear padded to the bathroom with a smile. “Let’s make a plan,” she said gently. “We can use peaceful words and think it through.”

Buzzy followed, sniffing the warm, sweet smell of bubble soap. The bathroom was cozy. The light was soft. The tub was shiny white. Buzzy heard the water rush, shhhhhh, like a little river. He touched a towel. It was fluffy and soft on his paws.

Mama Bear turned the taps. “Listen,” she said. “The water sounds like a song. We can make bath time fun and on time.”

Buzzy took a deep breath. “I feel wiggly,” he said. “I want to play more. I also want bubbles.”

“That’s good sharing of feelings,” Mama Bear said. “We can solve this. Let’s talk and plan.”

Buzzy tilted his head. “How?”

Mama Bear showed him a small, friendly timer shaped like a bee. It had a happy face and tiny wings. “This is Buzzy Timer,” she said. “Buzzy Timer helps us manage time. We set it. It buzzes when time is done.”

Buzzy giggled. “Like me!”

Mama Bear smiled. “Yes, like you. Here is our problem: We need a bath before bedtime, and you want to play. Let’s use steps.”

She held up her paw. “Step one: Pick a number. How many minutes to finish play?”

Buzzy thought. He looked at his blocks. He looked at the tub. He squinted and puffed his cheeks. “Three minutes,” he said. “Three is small. I can do three.”

“Good thinking,” Mama Bear said. “Step two: Set Buzzy Timer to three.” They turned the dial together. Tick-tick-tick.

“Step three: When Buzzy Timer buzzes, we stop playing and start the bath.”

Buzzy nodded. “I can do that.”

The timer ticked softly, like a tiny drum. Buzzy zoomed his car. Vroom. He stacked two blocks. Clack. He took a big breath. He peeked at the timer. Tick-tick.

Buzz! The timer sang a busy little song. Buzzy’s tummy fluttered. He wanted one more vroom. His paw reached for the car, then stopped. He put the car down and looked at Mama Bear.

“I feel a little sad,” he said. “But I remember the plan.”

Mama Bear gave him a warm hug. “Thank you for using your plan. That is time-management.”

Buzzy smiled. “Time-man-age-ment,” he said slowly. The word felt big and bouncy in his mouth.

Mama Bear turned off the taps. The bubbles were tall and fluffy. They smelled like sweet pine. Buzzy dipped a paw. “It’s warm!” he said. He climbed in. Splash!

“Step four,” Mama Bear said. “We choose bath time jobs. Wash, rinse, brush, and dry. We can use a song to help.”

Buzzy clapped. “I like songs!”

Mama Bear sang softly, “Wash my ears, one-two-three. Wash my paws, look at me.” Buzzy rubbed his ears. He counted, “One, two, three.” The bubbles tickled his nose. He giggled, “Hee-hee!”

“Rinse,” Mama Bear said, pouring warm water. “It whooshes like rain.” The water went shhh over Buzzy’s fur. It felt smooth and cozy.

“Brush teeth next,” Mama Bear said, holding a small blue toothbrush. It had a little bear on it. Buzzy squeezed a tiny dot of minty paste. “Only a pea,” he said. “Like a pea pod!” He brushed in little circles. Swish, swish, swish. The mint taste was cool and tingly.

The timer sat on the counter. “Let’s set Buzzy Timer again,” Mama Bear said. “Two minutes for brushing.”

Tick-tick-tick.

Buzzy watched in the mirror. He saw his round cheeks and his bright eyes. He brushed top teeth and bottom teeth. He sang, “Brush the fronts, brush the backs.” The timer buzzed. Buzz!

“All done,” Mama Bear said. “You followed the plan again!”

Buzzy felt proud. His chest felt warm and big. “I can manage time,” he said. “I can make a plan. I can follow steps.”

They dried with the fluffy towel. Pat, pat, pat. The towel smelled like sunshine. Mama Bear helped Buzzy put on cozy pajamas with tiny stars.

Buzzy looked at the timer. “Can I set one more?” he asked. “One minute for picking tomorrow’s book.”

Mama Bear nodded. “That is smart planning.”

Buzzy turned the dial. Tick-tick. He looked at two books. “Bear’s Blue Balloon or Night Songs?” He touched both, thinking. “Night Songs,” he decided before the buzz.

“Good choice,” Mama Bear said. “You used thinking and time.”

Buzzy climbed into bed. He felt calm. He had played. He had bathed. He had brushed. He had chosen a book. He had used his plan and his timer. He took a big, happy breath.

“Mama,” he said softly, “when I feel wiggly, I can say how I feel. I can make a plan. I can use Buzzy Timer. Then I can do things on time.”

Mama Bear kissed his forehead. “Yes, little cub. You solved the problem with peaceful words and smart steps.”

Buzzy smiled and closed his eyes. “I can do it tomorrow too,” he whispered.

The End.
