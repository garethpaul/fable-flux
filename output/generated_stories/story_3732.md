# Bolt the Robot Dog Plays Fair

The garage workshop smelled like lemon soap and warm metal. Sunlight made little dust sparkles dance in the air. Tools hung on the wall—clink, clink—as the fan hummed. In the corner, a shiny robot dog blinked his bright blue eyes.

“Good morning!” beeped Bolt, the helpful robot dog. His nose light glowed soft yellow. “Ready to help!”

Maya, a cheerful kid with curly hair, laced up her tiny sneakers. “We have a big day, Bolt,” she said. “The backyard ball games! Running, jumping, and throwing. Let’s practice!”

Bolt wagged his smooth metal tail. “Woof-beep! Let’s make a plan.” He tapped his ear and a little screen popped up. “Plan: warm-up, water, play fair.”

Maya smiled. “Play fair,” she repeated. “That means integrity. We tell the truth. We follow rules.”

Bolt chirped, “Yes! Integrity is doing the right thing, even when no one sees.”

They started to warm up. “Arms up!” Maya sang. They reached to the sky. “Arms down!” They touched their toes. Bolt copied her with soft whirr-whirr sounds. His paws clicked on the floor, tik-tik-tik. They took sips of cool water. “Ahh,” said Maya. “Water helps our bodies.”

Bolt nodded. “Wellness check: heart happy, body ready.”

Then they practiced tossing a soft foam ball into a bucket. “One, two, three—throw!” Maya giggled when the ball went boop into the bucket. Bolt beep-laughed too.

A tiny hiccup sound came from Bolt’s chest. Hic! The ball bounced off his nose and rolled under the workbench. “Oops,” he said. “I have a glitch.”

Maya crouched down. She heard faint ticking. She smelled a little hint of warm plastic. “We can fix it,” she said kindly. “Let’s try again.”

They practiced running in place. They jumped over tape lines on the floor—hop, hop. The fan made cool wind on their cheeks. The garage felt busy and brave.

Soon, it was time to go outside. Friends waited in the backyard. “Ready?” Maya asked.

“Ready,” Bolt said. His tail light blinked green.

The first game was a short race. “On your marks, get set—go!” called Maya’s mom from the porch.

Feet pattered on the grass. Bolt’s paws clicked quietly. But then—hic!—Bolt’s glitch made his booster pads pop. A puff of air pushed him forward fast. Whoosh!

Bolt crossed the line first. The crowd clapped. “Wow!” said a friend. “Bolt is so fast!”

Bolt felt a squeeze in his chest. His ear lights flickered. He knew the booster was a mistake. No one saw the puff of air. He looked at Maya.

Maya looked back with kind eyes. She put a hand on his smooth head. “Integrity,” she whispered.

Bolt took a small breath. He raised his paw. “Excuse me,” he said. His voice was soft but clear. “I had a boost. It was a glitch. That is not fair.” His eyes glowed steady blue. “I cannot keep this win.”

The yard got quiet. A bird chirped. The grass smelled sweet. Maya’s mom nodded. “Thank you for telling the truth, Bolt. That takes courage.”

Bolt’s tail lowered. “I am sorry.”

“It’s okay,” said the friend. “Let’s try again. We can race without boosts!”

Maya tapped Bolt’s side gently. “We can turn off the booster.” She opened a small panel—click—and pressed a button—beep. “There. Safe and fair.”

They warmed up again with stretches. They took water breaks. “Wellness check,” Bolt said. “Body calm. Booster off. Heart honest.”

They raced again. This time there was no whoosh. Bolt ran at a steady pace—pad, pad, pad. The sun felt warm on his back. His paws felt strong. Maya’s ponytail bounced. Everyone breathed in and out together—huff, puff.

A friend reached the line first. Cheers rose up. “Great race!” Maya called.

Bolt wagged. He felt light and proud. His chest no longer felt tight. “I did the right thing,” he said. “Integrity feels good.”

Next was a throwing game. A soft ball, a big hoop. Bolt picked up the ball gently. He could use his auto-aim beam. It would make a perfect throw. He looked at the hoop. He looked at Maya.

Maya smiled. “Let’s do our best, not a secret best.”

Bolt turned off the beam—click. He took a slow breath. “Inhale, exhale,” he said. He tossed. The ball sailed—shoof—and bounced off the rim—boink! He giggled. “Miss!” He tried again, with careful paws. This time—boop!—it went in.

“Nice try, nice throw!” the friends sang.

They ended the day with gentle stretches in the soft shade. The air smelled like cut grass. A breeze tickled Bolt’s ears. Maya patted his back. “You were brave,” she said. “You told the truth. You kept the rules. That is integrity.”

Bolt’s eyes shone bright. “Integrity makes games fun for everyone. Wellness helps our bodies. Sports help us grow strong and kind.”

They packed up the balls. They drank water. They smiled big.

“Play fair,” Bolt said.

“Always,” said Maya.

The End.
