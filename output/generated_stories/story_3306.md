# Benny Beaver Helps at Dinner

Benny was a busy beaver with bright, shiny teeth and a big, warm heart. He loved to build, to balance, and to be helpful. One rainy afternoon, Benny was in the dining room with his family. The dining room smelled like warm bread and sweet apples. The wooden table felt smooth under his paws. The chairs made tiny squeaks on the floor, eek-eek, as they moved.

Mama Beaver said, “We are having the community dinner tonight. Many neighbors will come. We need helpers!”

Benny’s tail thumped-thump-thumped with excitement. “I want to help! I want to volunteer!” he said, standing tall.

“Thank you, Benny,” Mama said with a smile. “Volunteering means helping because you care. You can set the napkins, stack the plates, and greet our guests.”

Benny puffed his chest. “I can do it all!” he chirped.

He started with the napkins. They felt soft like clouds. He folded them into neat triangles. Fold, fold, fold. He hummed a tune. Then he stacked the plates. One, two, three, four. The plates were cool and smooth. Next, he placed cups by each plate. Clink, clink, clink. Everything looked lovely, and Benny felt proud.

Then the doorbell rang. Ding-dong!

Neighbors came in, shaking off raindrops. “Hello!” Benny said with a big smile. “Welcome! Please come in!” He showed them to the dining room. The room filled with happy sounds—soft chatter, light laughter, and the gentle clink of spoons.

But soon, a little problem popped up.

Benny tried to carry a big bowl of salad. It was slippery and heavy. The bowl wobbled. “Careful, Benny,” Mama warned.

“I can do it!” Benny insisted. He wanted to help so much. But the bowl slipped. Oh no! Lettuce and tomatoes toppled onto the table. Plop, plop, plop! Some cherry tomatoes rolled like tiny red marbles. One rolled onto the floor and squished. Splat!

Benny’s face got hot. His cheeks felt like toast. “I messed up,” he whispered. His tail twitched. His paws clenched. He felt a storm of mad feelings rumble inside. He wanted to stomp. He wanted to shout, “This is too hard!”

Mama touched his shoulder gently. “It’s okay to feel mad,” she said softly. “Take a belly breath. In… and out.” She demonstrated. Benny copied her. Slowly, he breathed in-and-out. In… and out. His shoulders softened. The mad storm quieted to a sprinkle.

Mama smiled. “Thank you for calming your body. That is anger-management. And we can fix this together. Helping means trying again.”

Benny nodded. He wiped a little tomato juice off the floor. “I can try again,” he said in a small voice. His cheeks cooled down. His heart felt steady.

“Let’s be resilient,” Mama said. “Resilience means you keep trying, even when it’s tough.”

“I can be resilient,” Benny whispered.

Benny volunteered to solve the salad problem. “I will make a new salad,” he said. “I will ask for help.”

He turned to Mr. Otter, who was standing nearby. “Will you help me wash the lettuce?” Benny asked.

“Of course!” chuckled Mr. Otter. “Volunteering together is fun.” They swished the lettuce in cold water. Swish, swish! It felt crisp and squeaky.

Benny asked Ms. Duck, “Can you slice the cucumbers?” Slice, slice went the knife, making soft tapping sounds on the board. “Quack-yes!” she said.

Benny asked Little Squirrel, “Can you put tomatoes in the bowl?” Pat, pat, plop. Little Squirrel giggled. “I’m a tomato helper!”

Benny mixed the new salad gently. Toss, toss. It smelled fresh and green. He carried the bowl with both paws and a careful step. Slow, steady, safe. He set it on the table. Everyone clapped. Clap, clap, clap!

“You did it,” Mama said, giving him a warm hug. “You calmed your feelings, asked for help, and kept trying. That is resilience. And you volunteered to fix the problem.”

Benny beamed. “Volunteering together makes big jobs feel smaller,” he said.

Soon, it was time to serve dinner. Benny stood by the stack of plates. “Welcome, friends! I will pass you a plate,” he said. He handed a plate to each neighbor with a cheerful, “Here you go!” The plates went out in a smooth rhythm. Slide, pass, smile. The dining room glowed with soft lights and happy voices. Someone poured apple cider. It fizzed lightly. Fizz-fizz. The warm bread made a cozy crackle when it broke. Crack!

A little bunny dropped her spoon. Clatter! She looked worried. Benny knelt down and smiled kindly. “It’s okay. Everyone drops things sometimes,” he said. “I will get you a clean spoon.” He brought one over, and the bunny sniffled, then smiled. “Thank you,” she whispered.

“That is volunteering, too,” Mama said. “You saw a need and helped.”

At the end of the meal, Benny looked around. The table was messy with crumbs and tiny lettuce bits. He clapped his paws. “Let’s make a clean-up team!” he called. “Volunteers, please!”

Hands and paws went up. They wiped the table—wipe, wipe. They stacked chairs—eek-eek. They washed dishes—swish, swash. Benny led with a happy voice: “We can do it together!”

When the dining room was tidy again, everyone cheered. Benny felt warm and light, like sunshine after rain.

Mama knelt beside him. “What did you learn today?” she asked.

Benny thought for a moment. “I learned that volunteering is helping because you care,” he said. “When I got mad, I took belly breaths. I asked for help. I tried again. That is resilience. And helping together makes our hearts feel big.”

Mama smiled. “Yes, my busy beaver. You made our dinner a happy place.”

Benny wiggled his tail. “I love to volunteer,” he said. “Tomorrow, I will help again.”

The End.
