# Buzzy Beaver’s Wiggle Workout Dinner

Buzzy the busy beaver woke up with a wiggle in his tail. He loved to help, he loved to move, and he loved to tell stories. Tonight, Buzzy’s family was having dinner in their cozy dining room, and Buzzy had a big job: set the table!

The dining room smelled like warm carrot stew and fresh bread. Sunshine made little squares on the floor. The wooden table felt smooth under Buzzy’s paws. He took a deep breath. “I can do it,” he said. “I can learn and move!”

Mama Beaver peeked in. “Buzzy, would you set the table, please?”

“Yes!” said Buzzy. His tail thumped, thump-thump, on the floor. “I will make it fun. I will make it moving!”

Buzzy picked up a stack of plates. They were cool and heavy. “Step, step, place,” he sang. “Step, step, place.” He put one plate, then another. He counted softly. “One, two, three, four.” His paws went tap-tap on the floor. His whiskers tickled in the warm air.

Little Sister Beebee rolled in with her tiny wagon. Beebee couldn’t walk yet, but she zoomed with her wagon wheels. “Can I help?” she asked in a squeaky voice.

“Yes!” said Buzzy. “We are different, and we can help in different ways. You can pass the spoons!”

Beebee giggled. “Vroom!” She pushed the wagon. The spoons chimed, ting-ting, like little bells.

Buzzy made a plan. “We will do an exercise game,” he said. “We will move our bodies in safe, small ways. We will have fun while we set the table!”

Papa Beaver came in with a big bowl. “An exercise game? In the dining room?” His eyes smiled.

“Yes,” said Buzzy. “A wiggle workout! We will get strong and ready to eat.”

Mama nodded. “Let’s try.”

Buzzy stood tall. “First, the Plate March! Lift your knees. March soft. One, two, place a plate!” He marched slowly to the table. “Left, right, place.” Beebee rolled beside him, wheels humming, “Brrrrr.” She nudged a spoon onto the table with her tiny paw. “Ting!”

Papa moved too. “March, march,” he said, keeping his steps quiet so the plates stayed safe.

Buzzy’s tail swished. He felt proud. “Next is the Napkin Stretch. Reach up high, up to the sky. Reach down low, put the napkin by the plate.” He reached up. He reached down. The napkin felt soft, like a cloud.

Beebee reached her paws up and laughed. “Up, up! Down, down!”

Buzzy smiled. “You are great at reaching in your wagon.”

Beebee beamed. “I am helping my way.”

Buzzy nodded. “We all help in our own ways.” His heart felt warm.

He looked at the empty cups. “Now the Cup Twist,” he said. “Hands on the cups. Twist, twist to the side. Place the cup. Good job!” His paws twisted left, then right. The cups made a gentle clink.

Beebee hummed and spun her wagon in a tiny circle. “Twist, twist!” She placed a tiny cup for her doll. “Clink!”

Papa laughed softly. “I like this workout.”

Mama smiled. “I feel my body waking up.”

Buzzy took a deep breath. The room smelled extra yummy now. His tummy rumbled. “Oops! One more move,” he said. “The Fork Wiggle. Wiggle fingers like little fish. Place the forks.”

Beebee wiggled her toes. “Toe wiggle!” She set the last spoon with a happy “ting.”

Buzzy was almost done. But he looked at the chairs. They were not in a neat line. “I can do it,” he whispered. “I can keep trying.” He pushed one chair in. Squeeeak. It was heavy. He tried again. Squeak. He felt a little tired.

Papa touched Buzzy’s shoulder. “You can take a small rest,” he said.

Buzzy nodded. He took three slow breaths. In. Out. In. Out. In. Out. “I will try again.” He pushed with steady paws. Squeak, slide. The chair moved in. “I did it!” he said.

Mama clapped. Beebee rang a spoon like a bell. “Yay!”

Buzzy’s eyes shone. “That is a growth mindset,” he said carefully. “I keep trying. I can learn. My body gets stronger when I practice.”

“Tell us a story while we eat,” said Papa. “A story about your wiggle workout.”

The family sat down. The chairs felt warm. The bread was soft. The carrot stew made little bubbles, blub-blub.

Buzzy began. “Once upon a time, a busy beaver had a wiggle in his tail. He made exercise into a game. He marched with plates. He stretched with napkins. He twisted with cups. He wiggled with forks. He took a rest when he needed. He kept trying. His sister helped in her own way, rolling and ringing and reaching. They were different, and that made the team strong.”

Beebee added, “And they had stew!” She slurped. “Mmm!”

Mama smiled. “Exercise can happen in small, safe ways every day.”

Papa nodded. “Moving our bodies helps our hearts and minds.”

Buzzy took a bite of bread. Crunch. He felt calm and proud. “I like to move,” he said. “I like that we are all different. I like telling stories about our day.”

After dinner, Buzzy stood and did one last gentle stretch. “Up to the sky. Down to the toes.” He giggled. “I feel good.”

Mama hugged him. “We learned, we moved, and we helped.”

Buzzy hugged back. “I can try, I can move, and I can grow. Little steps make big strength.”

Everyone smiled. The dining room felt cozy and bright.

The End.
