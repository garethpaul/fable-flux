# Benny Beaver’s Big Band Helper

Benny was a busy beaver. He loved to build, fix, and tidy. His tail went thump-thump when he worked. Today, Benny scurried into the musical practice room at the community center. The room smelled like wood polish and fresh paper. Sunlight made bright squares on the wooden floor. Instruments rested everywhere—drums, tiny triangles, shiny flutes, and a tall bass that hummed when the floor wiggled.

“Good morning, room!” Benny chirped. He patted the broom like it was a friend. “Time for chores.”

He swept little dust bunnies. Swish, swish. He wiped sticky spots from the drum. Wipe, wipe. He sorted song cards by color. Red, blue, yellow, green. Benny liked when things felt neat and ready.

As he cleaned, he heard soft footsteps and gentle voices. Children came in, one by one. Mei wore a blue scarf with stars. Amir rolled in with his chair, wheels whispering on the floor. Lila had bright maracas that rattled like rain. Mateo carried a tiny drum from his grandma’s country. “It’s called a bombo,” he said, smiling.

Benny’s eyes sparkled. “Welcome! Welcome! I am getting the room ready for Music Club.” He pointed to colorful tape lines on the floor. “Please wait here while I set up seats.”

Benny tried to do everything fast. He lined up chairs, but they bumped together. Clack. He stacked music stands. Clatter. He reached for the heavy bass, but it wobbled. Wobble… wobble… “Oh no!” Benny gasped, catching it with both paws. His tail thumped the floor. Thump!

“Do you need help?” Amir asked kindly.

Benny shook his head. “I can do it. I am a busy beaver. I do all the chores!” He puffed his chest and lifted a drum too big for his arms. The drum slipped. Bump! It rolled under a chair.

Lila’s maracas stopped rattling. “It’s okay, Benny. We can help.”

Benny’s whiskers drooped. He wanted to be helpful. He wanted everything to be perfect before the music started. “I’m sorry,” he said softly. “I made a mess.”

Mei smiled. “We can fix it together.” She pointed to her scarf. “My grandma says, ‘Many hands make music bright.’”

Benny listened. His ears perked up. “Together?” he asked.

“Together,” Amir said. “I can show where the chairs fit so my wheels can move.”

“And I can share a new idea,” Mateo added. “Let’s make a map.”

“A map?” Benny tilted his head.

“Yes!” Mateo spread big paper on the floor. He drew the room with a thick blue marker. “We can make a stage path for rolling, a quiet corner with soft shakers, and a loud corner for drums.”

“That’s innovation!” Lila giggled. “A new plan!”

Benny felt a warm glow in his chest. “Let’s try.”

Everyone joined the chores. Benny swept while Amir rolled tape lines for smooth paths. Zip! Mei dusted the flutes with gentle strokes. Soft, soft. Lila tested sounds in the quiet corner. Shake, shake, whisper-shake. Mateo set the bombo on a mat so it wouldn’t slide. Thud, but gentle.

They talked and laughed. The room felt alive and friendly. Benny’s tail tapped a happy beat. Thump-thump-thump.

A little hedgehog named Pippa peeked in, clutching a tiny bell. “Can I come too?” she asked, voice small.

“Of course!” Benny said. “All friends are welcome.”

Pippa looked at the big instruments and hugged her bell. “I only have this,” she whispered.

Mei bent down so their eyes met. “Your bell is special. It has a bright voice.”

“Every sound matters,” Amir added. “We play together.”

Benny nodded. “We include everyone. That is our big rule.”

Soon, the teacher, Ms. Sora, walked in with a gentle smile and a drum shaped like the moon. “Hello, musicians! I see new ideas here.”

Mateo pointed to the map. “We made a plan so everyone can play.”

Ms. Sora clapped softly. Clap, clap. “I hear innovation and inclusion. Wonderful!”

Benny raised his paw. “I tried to do all the chores by myself. I made mistakes. I am sorry.”

Ms. Sora nodded kindly. “Thank you for saying sorry. We forgive, we learn, we try again. Working together is a beautiful sound.”

Benny felt light, like a leaf on a stream. “Friends, will you help me start the beat?”

They made a circle. Amir tapped his wheel. Tick-tick. Lila shook her maracas. Shika-shika. Mateo played his bombo with a deep dum-dum from faraway lands. Mei hummed a song her grandma taught her, soft as wind. Pippa rang her bell. Ting! Ms. Sora’s moon drum answered. Boom… boom.

Benny added a wooden block beat. Tok-tok. He listened to the different sounds—near and far, loud and soft, high and low. He thought about all the places these songs came from. The room felt like a big, gentle world where every voice fit.

“Listen!” Benny said, smiling wide. “We sound like one big family.”

They played a happy tune. They stopped together. Silence felt warm and full. Everyone grinned.

Benny looked around the tidy, bright room with careful paths, cozy corners, and the new map on the wall. “Today I learned something important,” he said. “Chores are better together. New ideas help everyone. And every friend belongs.”

“Yes,” Ms. Sora said. “Inclusion makes music shine.”

Benny’s tail tapped one last soft beat. Thump. “Thank you for helping. Thank you for forgiving. Let’s always make space for each other.”

All the children and critters nodded. “Always.”

The End.
