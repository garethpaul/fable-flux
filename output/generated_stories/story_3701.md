# Brave Little Bear Says "Please"

In a comfortable nursery, sunlight made golden squares on the floor. Soft rugs felt fuzzy under paws. Crayons smelled waxy and sweet. A friendly bear cub named Bramble wiggled his nose and smiled. He loved this cheerful place.

Today was art time. The teacher, Miss Maple, clapped softly. “Friends, let’s draw our feelings,” she said. She set out big paper, chunky crayons, and glittery stickers that sparkled like tiny stars.

Bramble’s heart fluttered. He had a big idea in his belly. He wanted to draw a strong mountain and a brave bear standing tall. He picked a green crayon and began to draw a hill. Scratch-scritch went the crayon. He added a bright sun with gentle yellow rays.

“Wow,” whispered Bramble. “My picture is strong.”

Just then, his friend Pip the raccoon shuffled close. Pip’s tail fuzzy-whooshed over the paper. Pip reached for the same blue crayon Bramble was holding.

“I need blue,” Pip said quickly. “I’m taking it.”

Bramble’s paws paused. The blue crayon was important for the brave bear’s sky. Bramble felt a tangle in his tummy. He did not want to grab or shout. He remembered Miss Maple’s words from yesterday: “Your voice is your tool. Use it kindly. Use it clearly.”

Bramble took a slow breath. He felt the warm air go in and out. “Pip,” he said in a steady, gentle voice, “I am using the blue crayon right now. Please wait your turn, or we can take turns.”

Pip blinked. His paw hovered. The nursery was quiet except for the soft hum of the heater. Bramble held the crayon, not too tight, not too loose. He stood up a little taller.

Miss Maple smiled from across the room. “That was assertive, Bramble,” she said. “You used your voice kindly and clearly.”

Pip’s ears wiggled. “I want blue too,” he said, softer now. “My ocean needs blue.”

Bramble nodded. “I hear you. How about this: I can color for two minutes, then you can color for two minutes. We can set the timer.”

Pip looked at the small, round timer on the shelf. “Okay,” he said. “Two minutes, then me.” His tail swished happier now.

Miss Maple set the timer. It tick-tick-ticked. Bramble colored the sky. He used long, smooth strokes. He listened to the crayon squeak and smelled the paper. When the timer dinged, he smiled and handed the blue to Pip. “Your turn.”

“Thanks,” Pip said. He colored swishy waves. The room filled with quiet coloring sounds and tiny giggles.

A moment later, a bigger problem popped up. The sticker box had only one sparkly star left. Bramble’s eyes widened. His brave bear needed a star over the mountain. At the same time, Mina the bunny pointed. “I want the star,” she said, hopping closer.

Bramble felt the tummy tangle again. He didn’t want to snatch. He didn’t want to say nothing. He touched his chest and took a breath. “Mina,” he said, looking at her kindly, “I would like that star for my brave bear picture. It’s important to me. Can we find a way to share?”

Mina tilted her head. “But my night sky needs it,” she replied. Her whiskers quivered. She looked a little worried.

Bramble thought. His ears twitched. He had an idea that felt bright and warm. “What if we cut the star in half?” he asked. “Or we can make a new star together with glitter and glue. Then we each can have one.”

Mina’s eyes sparkled like the stickers. “Make a star together?” she asked.

“Yes,” said Bramble. “We can draw a big star, cut it out, and share the glitter. We can both have shiny stars.”

Mina smiled. “Let’s do it.” They carried paper to the craft table. Snip-snip went the safe scissors. Squish-squish went the glue. Sprinkle-sprinkle went the glitter, like tiny bells without sound. They made two stars that shimmered softly.

Bramble gently placed his star above the mountain. The brave bear in the picture stood tall, chest out, paws open. Mina stuck her star in her night sky. Pip added blue bubbles to his ocean. The nursery smelled like glue and crayons and a little bit like orange slices from snack time.

Miss Maple walked over. “Friends, I saw the way you used your voices,” she said. “Bramble, you were assertive. You said what you needed in a calm, kind way. You offered choices and made a plan. That is strong. That is brave.”

Bramble’s cheeks felt warm and happy. “Thank you,” he said.

Mina nodded. “I liked making stars together,” she said. “It felt fair.”

Pip grinned. “I liked the timer,” he said. “Taking turns worked.”

They all stood back to admire their art. Bramble’s picture showed a brave bear saying, “Please,” with a steady smile. He added one last touch: with a brown crayon, he wrote small, friendly words at the bottom. “My voice can be kind and strong.”

As clean-up time started, Bramble helped pick up crayons and wiped glittery sparkles from the table. The room felt calm and bright. The golden sunlight made the new stars twinkle.

Miss Maple whispered, “Remember, being assertive means using your words to say what you need, without hurting others. You did that today.”

Bramble hugged himself gently. “I can do it again,” he said.

The End.
