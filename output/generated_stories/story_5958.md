# Nutkin's Rooftop Adventure

High up on Mrs. Miller's red brick house lived a little gray squirrel named Nutkin. His bushy tail twitched with excitement as he scampered across the warm rooftop tiles in the morning sunshine.

"What a beautiful day!" chirped Nutkin, stretching his tiny paws toward the bright blue sky.

But as Nutkin explored his rooftop home, he noticed something troubling. Colorful candy wrappers, empty chip bags, and sticky soda cans were scattered everywhere. The wind had blown trash from the street below right onto his clean rooftop!

"Oh no!" squeaked Nutkin, his whiskers drooping. "My home is all messy. The birds won't want to visit, and the rain might wash this trash into the garden below."

Nutkin's little heart felt heavy. He could smell the sour smell of old food and see how the shiny wrappers made his beautiful rooftop look ugly. But the pile of trash looked so big for such a small squirrel.

"Maybe someone else will clean it up," Nutkin thought, curling up in a sunny spot.

But as the day went on, more trash blew onto the rooftop. A plastic bottle rolled past his ear with a loud rattle. A greasy napkin stuck to his favorite acorn-hiding spot.

"This is my home," Nutkin said bravely, standing up tall on his hind legs. "I need to take care of it!"

Even though he felt scared about the big job ahead, Nutkin knew it was the right thing to do. He started small, picking up one wrapper at a time with his tiny teeth. The paper tasted yucky, but he kept going.

"Excuse me," called a gentle voice. Robin Red-Breast landed nearby, tilting her orange head. "What are you doing, little friend?"

"I'm cleaning up this trash," puffed Nutkin, dragging a heavy can. "It's making our rooftop dirty and unsafe."

"That's very responsible of you," chirped Robin. "But this job looks too big for one small squirrel. May I help?"

Nutkin's eyes sparkled. "Would you really?"

"Of course! And I know others who would help too," Robin said kindly.

Soon, Blue Jay swooped down, followed by Chipmunk Charlie from the nearby tree. Even Benny Bee buzzed over to see what was happening.

"We all live in this neighborhood," said Blue Jay, picking up bottle caps with his strong beak. "We should all help keep it clean."

Working together, the friends sorted the trash into neat piles. Nutkin learned to ask politely: "Blue Jay, could you please carry the heavy cans? Robin, would you help me with the small pieces?"

As they worked, Nutkin felt proud and strong. His muscles grew tired, but his heart felt happy. The sweet smell of fresh air returned as they cleared away the last piece of trash.

"Look how beautiful our rooftop is now!" exclaimed Nutkin, doing a little happy dance.

The clean tiles sparkled in the afternoon sun. Birds could land safely, and the garden below would stay protected from falling trash.

"Thank you all for helping," said Nutkin gratefully. "I learned that taking care of our home is everyone's job."

"And asking for help when we need it is very smart," added Robin with a warm smile.

From that day on, Nutkin checked his rooftop every morning. When he found new trash, he didn't feel scared anymore. He knew that being responsible meant taking care of his home, and that good friends would always help when asked nicely.

The rooftop stayed clean and beautiful, and all the neighborhood animals felt proud of their special place.

The End.
