# Patches Plans a Sleepover

Patches was a friendly bear cub with soft, speckled fur. He was visiting Grandma’s house. The guest room smelled like clean sheets and pine soap. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. A quilt with many colors lay on the bed.

Patches wiggled his nose. “This room feels cozy,” he said. His voice was small and happy.

Grandma set a basket on the rug. “I have friends coming,” she said. “They are all different. They need a safe, comfy place to rest. Can you help me get the guest room ready?”

Patches clapped his paws. “Yes! I like helping.”

Grandma smiled. “Helping means thinking and listening. We will make a room where everyone belongs.”

Patches looked at the basket. It held pillows of many shapes—soft round ones, squishy long ones, and flat square ones. It also held little name cards, a nightlight, and a small fan.

“Who is coming?” Patches asked.

Grandma read the cards. “Nia the squirrel, who is very small and likes quiet. Dot the duck, who gets chilly. Max the mole, who sees best with gentle light. Rami the rabbit, who hops a lot and likes space to move.”

Patches rubbed his chin. “Hmm. They are all different,” he said. “How can one room fit everyone?”

“That is our puzzle,” Grandma said. “Let’s use our thinking caps.”

Patches pretended to put on a hat. “Thinking cap on!” He giggled. The giggle sounded like soft bells in the sunny room.

He pat-pat-patted the rug. “This rug feels tickly,” he said. “Maybe Nia will like a small, quiet nest.”

He found a tiny corner by the bookshelf. He put a little round pillow there and a folded blanket like a nest. “Nia can feel snug,” he said.

Next, he held a square pillow. “Dot gets chilly. How can we help?” He touched the window. “Cold wind lives here. We need warmth.”

Grandma nodded. “What can keep Dot warm?”

Patches sniffed the quilt. It smelled like sun and fresh air. “We can use this colorful quilt for Dot,” he said. “And the fan can be off when Dot rests.”

“Good thinking,” Grandma said.

Patches set the quilt at the foot of the bed and placed a sign: “Dot’s warm spot.” He felt proud.

He read the next card. “Max needs gentle light.” Patches flipped the switch, and the bright ceiling light hummed. He shaded his eyes. “Too bright,” he said. He tried the nightlight. It glowed like a tiny moon.

“Oooh,” said Patches. “Soft light for Max.” He put the nightlight near the bed and made a small tent with a long pillow and a sheet. “Max can rest under here.”

Last, he read, “Rami likes to hop and needs space.” Patches looked around. The floor had toys and a big trunk. He frowned. “Not much space. What can we do?”

He tugged at the trunk. It was heavy. “I cannot move it alone,” he said. His paws felt warm, and he puffed a little.

Grandma said, “When we need help, we ask. That is safe and smart.”

“Grandma, can you help me?” Patches asked.

Together they scoot-scoot-scooted the trunk to another wall. Patches rolled the toy basket under the bed. The floor looked open and clear.

“Hop space!” Patches cheered. He put down a soft mat so Rami’s feet would not slip. Safety first.

“Let’s check,” Grandma said. “Is it safe for all?”

Patches looked around. He tucked lamp cords behind a table. He closed the window just a little so the curtain would not flap and scare Nia. He set a small water bowl near Dot’s quilt and put a bell by the door so Max could find the way with sound. He smiled. “All safe.”

Knock, knock! The friends arrived.

Nia peeked in with bright eyes. “Is it quiet here?” she whispered.

Patches showed her the tiny nest corner. “It’s your quiet spot, Nia.” Nia’s tail twitched with joy. “Thank you,” she said softly.

Dot waddled in, shivering a bit. “Brrr.”

Patches wrapped her in the colorful quilt. “Warm and cozy.” Dot sighed, “Ahh. This feels like a hug.”

Max the mole sniffed the air. “Is the light gentle?” he asked.

Patches clicked off the big light and pointed to the glowing nightlight. “Moonlight for you, Max.” Max smiled and crawled into the pillow tent. “Perfect.”

Rami bounced at the doorway. “I want to hop!” Patches showed the open mat. “Hop here. It is safe and soft.” Rami boing-boing-boinged with big happy feet.

Everyone was different. Everyone had a place.

Patches listened. He heard soft hopping, gentle quacks, quiet chitters, and slow, happy sniffs. The room felt warm and kind.

Grandma whispered, “How did you make this happen?”

Patches puffed his chest. “I used my thinking cap. I listened. I asked for help. I made safe spots. I made warm spots. I made quiet and light and space. I made a room for all.”

Grandma hugged him. “That is diversity,” she said. “We are not the same, and that is wonderful. When we think and cooperate, we make room for everyone.”

Patches looked at his friends. “You belong here,” he said.

“We belong here,” they all replied.

Patches curled up on a little pillow near the door. He felt the soft quilt under his paw. He smelled pine soap and warm feathers. He heard peaceful sounds.

He smiled and said, “Different needs. One cozy room. We can care for all.”

The End.
