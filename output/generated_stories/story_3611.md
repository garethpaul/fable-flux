# Oliver Owl's Recycling Art Plan

The warm kitchen smelled like fresh cookies. Sunlight streamed through the yellow curtains. Oliver Owl sat at the big wooden table, looking at all the empty boxes and bottles around him.

"Oh my!" hooted Oliver, ruffling his brown feathers. "Look at all this stuff we were going to throw away!"

Oliver's friend Bunny hopped into the kitchen. "What are you doing, Oliver?" she asked, twitching her pink nose.

"I want to make something special," said Oliver. "But I don't know where to start. There are so many things here!"

Bunny looked around. She saw empty cereal boxes, plastic bottles, and colorful bottle caps scattered everywhere. "Wow! That is a lot of stuff," she agreed.

Oliver felt confused. His big round eyes blinked slowly. "Maybe I should just put it all in the trash," he sighed.

"Wait!" said Bunny. "Remember what Teacher Rabbit always says? When we have a big job, we need to make a plan first!"

Oliver's feathers perked up. "A plan? How do we make a plan?"

Bunny smiled. "First, we think about what we want to make. Then we choose what we need. Then we take it step by step!"

Oliver thought hard. He tapped his wing on the table. "I want to make a beautiful bird feeder for my garden! The little birds get so hungry."

"That's a wonderful idea!" cheered Bunny. "Now, what do we need?"

Oliver looked carefully at all the recycled things. "I need something to hold the seeds," he said slowly. "And something to hang it up."

Together, they picked out a big plastic bottle and some colorful string. Oliver felt much better now that he had a plan.

"Step one," said Oliver. "I'll cut holes in the bottle so the birds can get the seeds."

Bunny helped him make perfect round holes. The plastic made a soft *snip-snip* sound as they worked.

"Step two," continued Oliver. "I'll decorate it with these pretty bottle caps!"

They glued red, blue, and green caps all around the bottle. Oliver's wings got a little sticky, but he kept going. "This is fun!" he hooted happily.

"Step three," said Oliver. "I'll put the string through the top so we can hang it up."

Oliver tried to thread the string, but his wings were too big. It was tricky! The string kept slipping out.

"This is hard," Oliver said. His voice sounded a little sad.

"Don't give up!" encouraged Bunny. "Remember, good plans take time. Let's try together!"

Bunny held the bottle steady while Oliver carefully pushed the string through. *Pop!* It went right through!

"We did it!" Oliver danced around the warm kitchen. His feathers fluffed up with excitement.

They filled the feeder with tiny seeds. Oliver carried it outside and hung it on a low tree branch. The colorful bottle caps sparkled in the sunshine.

"Look, Oliver!" whispered Bunny. "Here come some birds already!"

Three little sparrows landed on the feeder. They chirped happily as they ate the seeds. Oliver felt warm and proud inside.

"Making a plan really helped," said Oliver. "First I thought about what I wanted. Then I chose what I needed. Then I took it step by step, even when it got tricky!"

Bunny nodded. "And look how beautiful it turned out! You turned trash into treasure!"

Oliver smiled his biggest owl smile. "Next time I have a big job, I'll make a plan first. Planning makes everything easier!"

The little birds sang sweetly in the garden, and Oliver felt very wise indeed.

The End.
