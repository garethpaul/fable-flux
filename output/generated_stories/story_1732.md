# Pebble the Penguin Takes a Calm Breath

Pebble was a cheerful penguin who loved to waddle and giggle. Today, Pebble was in the playful living room. The rug was soft like marshmallows. Colorful blocks were stacked like tiny houses. Sunlight danced in the window and made rainbow sparkles on the floor.

“Let’s build a tall tower!” Pebble chirped. Pebble’s friends were there too—Rae the rabbit with floppy ears, Tiko the turtle with a shiny shell, and Zara the zebra with bold stripes. Everyone was different, and everyone had something special to share.

Pebble lined up the blocks. Tap, tap, tap went the blocks. The tower grew taller and taller. Pebble’s flippers fluttered with joy. “Look! So tall!”

Rae clapped. “I like your tower, Pebble!”

Tiko nodded slowly. “I can add a block.”

Zara smiled. “I can find the blue block. It matches the sky!”

Pebble felt proud. Pebble put on one more block. Then—uh-oh! The tower wobbled. Wibble. Wobble. Crash! Blocks tumbled everywhere. Clackety-clack! The sound surprised Pebble’s ears.

Pebble’s tummy felt tight. Pebble’s face felt hot. Pebble stomped a little foot. “I worked so hard! It fell!” Pebble’s voice wiggled. A little tear rolled down a feathery cheek.

Rae touched Pebble’s flipper. “It’s okay to feel mad.”

Tiko whispered, “When I feel mad, I go slow like a turtle.”

Zara took a gentle breath. “We can calm our bodies.”

Pebble felt the mad feeling like a fizzy soda inside. Pebble wanted to push the blocks away. Pebble wanted to say, “No one touch it!” But Pebble remembered a song from Grandma Penguin.

“When I feel tight,
I pause, not fight.
Smell a flower,
Blow a light.”

Pebble closed eyes and tried. “Smell a flower.” Pebble sniffed softly. In, one, two. Pebble smelled the living room smells—warm laundry, a lemony clean couch, and a sweet candle that smelled like vanilla. “Blow a light.” Pebble blew out slowly. Out, one, two. Pebble imagined a tiny candle on a cupcake. Pffff.

Pebble’s tummy felt softer. The fizzing felt smaller. Pebble put a flipper on the heart. Pat, pat. “I can be calm,” Pebble said. “I can try again.”

Rae brought a green block. “Can I help?”

Pebble nodded. “Yes, please. Everyone can help. We all take turns.”

Tiko slid a block carefully. “Slow and steady.”

Zara looked around. “Some blocks rolled under the chair. That’s not fair. We all need blocks to share.” Zara peeked under the chair and found three blocks—red, yellow, and blue. “Here they are! Now we all have what we need.”

Pebble smiled. “Thank you for finding them. Everyone should have a chance. That is fair.”

They made a plan. Pebble said, “I will put the base blocks.” Rae said, “I will count.” Tiko said, “I will check if it’s steady.” Zara said, “I will match colors.”

They worked together. Pebble pressed big square blocks at the bottom. Thump. Thump. Rae counted, “One, two, three.” Tiko touched the sides. “No wobble!” Zara handed a blue block. “Sky blue for the top!”

Pebble felt calm and focused. When a block tilted, Pebble felt a little grumpy fizz again. Pebble paused. “Smell a flower.” In. “Blow a light.” Out. The block settled. Pebble smiled. “I did it.”

Suddenly, the room filled with music from the toy radio—bouncy drum beats and happy bells. Ding-ding! The friends wiggled their shoulders. Pebble wiggled too and then set the last block on top. The tower stood tall and strong, almost as tall as Pebble!

Everyone cheered. “Hooray!”

Just then, the door opened and Milo the mouse rolled in with a toy car. Vroom! The car bumped the rug. The tower shook. Wibble. Wobble. The friends froze.

Pebble took a big breath. In. Out. Pebble put up a flipper. “Milo, please slow down near our tower. We worked hard. We all want it to be safe.”

Milo’s whiskers twitched. “Oh! I didn’t see. I will go slow.” Milo rolled the car gently. “I want to help too.”

Pebble nodded. “Thank you for being careful. Everyone matters.”

Together, they added a tiny flag to the top. Pebble used a soft voice. “I felt mad before. I took breaths. I asked for help. I used gentle words. That helped me.”

Rae hugged Pebble. “You calmed your body.”

Tiko smiled. “You tried again.”

Zara said, “You were fair and kind.”

Pebble looked at all the friends—soft ears, shiny shell, bright stripes, quick whiskers. “We are all different. We can all help. We all belong.”

They sat around the tower and shared crunchy apple slices. Munch, munch. The apples were sweet and juicy. Pebble felt proud and peaceful. Pebble hummed the calm song one more time.

“When I feel tight,
I pause, not fight.
Smell a flower,
Blow a light.”

Pebble patted the tower and whispered, “I can handle big feelings. I can be kind and fair.”

The tower gleamed in the sunshine. The living room felt warm and happy. And Pebble the cheerful penguin waddled in a little circle, giggling with friends.

The End.
