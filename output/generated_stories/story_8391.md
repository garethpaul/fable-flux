# Benny Bear's Special Workshop Day

Benny Bear was a fluffy brown cub who loved to help his Papa Bear in the garage workshop. The workshop smelled like wood shavings and had shiny tools hanging on the walls. Benny's favorite sound was the gentle *tap-tap-tap* of Papa's hammer.

One sunny morning, Benny bounced into the workshop. "Papa! Papa! Can I help you build something today?" he asked, his little paws clapping together.

Papa Bear looked down at his eager cub and smiled. "Of course, Benny! But first, what did you forget to do this morning?"

Benny tilted his furry head. "Um... I brushed my teeth!"

"That's good," Papa said. "But what about washing your paws? And combing your fur? And eating your breakfast?"

Benny looked down at his dusty paws and felt his messy fur. His tummy gave a little *grumble*. "Oh no! I was so excited to help that I forgot to take care of myself first!"

Papa Bear knelt down and gave Benny a warm hug. "Taking care of yourself is very important, little one. When we feel clean, fed, and healthy, we can help others much better!"

Benny nodded. "You're right, Papa. But I really want to help you today. What should I do?"

"Let me tell you a story," Papa said, sitting on his workbench. "Once upon a time, there was a little bird who wanted to help build nests for all the other birds. But the little bird was so busy helping that he forgot to eat seeds and drink water. Soon, he became too tired and hungry to help anyone at all!"

"Oh no!" Benny gasped. "What happened to the little bird?"

"Well," Papa continued, "his wise mama bird taught him that taking care of himself first wasn't selfish—it was smart! When the little bird ate his seeds, drank fresh water, and rested his wings, he had much more energy to help his friends."

Benny's eyes grew wide. "So the bird helped everyone better when he took care of himself first?"

"Exactly!" Papa Bear said. "Now, why don't you go wash your paws with warm, soapy water, comb your soft fur, and eat some honey toast? Then you'll be ready to be my best helper!"

Benny raced to the house. He washed his paws until they were squeaky clean, combed his brown fur until it was fluffy and neat, and ate two pieces of honey toast that tasted sweet and delicious.

When he returned to the workshop, Benny felt wonderful. His paws were clean, his fur was soft, and his tummy was happy and full.

"Now I'm ready to help!" Benny announced.

Papa Bear beamed with pride. "You look fantastic, son! Now you can help me build this birdhouse. Your clean paws won't get the wood dirty, and since you ate breakfast, you'll have energy to work!"

Together, they hammered and sawed and painted. Benny felt strong and happy because he had taken good care of himself first.

As they worked, Papa Bear said, "Remember, Benny, taking care of yourself helps you take care of others. It's not selfish—it's responsible!"

Benny smiled as he painted the little birdhouse blue. "I'll always remember to take care of myself first, Papa. Then I can be the best helper ever!"

The End.
