# Joey's Special Art Day

Joey the kangaroo joey bounced into the bright art room. His soft brown fur gleamed under the colorful lights. The art room smelled like crayons and paint. Joey's big eyes sparkled with excitement.

"Good morning, Joey!" said Ms. Rabbit, the art teacher. "Today we're making pictures of our favorite activities."

Joey looked around the room. Red paint sat in little cups. Blue crayons were lined up in neat rows. Paper covered every table. Other animal children were already drawing.

"I don't know what to draw," Joey said softly. He bounced from foot to foot, feeling worried.

Benny the bear was drawing a big cake. "I'm drawing my family's birthday tradition," Benny said proudly.

Lily the lamb painted purple flowers. "These are from my grandma's garden," she explained. "We pick them every spring together."

Joey's ears drooped down. "I don't have special traditions like you do," he whispered.

Ms. Rabbit hopped over to Joey. "Tell me, what do you love to do with your family?" she asked gently.

Joey thought hard. His nose twitched as he remembered. "Well, my mama taught me to hop really high. And my papa shows me how to bounce in zigzag patterns."

"That sounds wonderful!" Ms. Rabbit said. "What else?"

"We play bounce-ball every evening," Joey said, getting excited. "Mama and Papa taught me the special Joey family way to play. We bounce the ball with our tails!"

Joey's eyes grew wide. "That IS a tradition! My family has been playing bounce-ball for many, many years!"

Joey grabbed a big piece of white paper. He picked up a brown crayon and started drawing. First, he drew himself with his strong legs bent, ready to jump. Then he drew his mama and papa.

"What's that round thing?" asked Benny, pointing to Joey's picture.

"That's our bounce-ball!" Joey said proudly. "See how we use our tails to hit it? My great-grandpapa taught grandpapa, grandpapa taught papa, and papa taught me!"

Joey added green grass and a yellow sun to his picture. He drew motion lines around the ball to show it bouncing high in the air.

"Can you teach us how to play?" asked Lily, her wool bouncing as she nodded.

Joey's chest puffed up with pride. "Yes! I can show you at recess!"

When recess time came, Joey led all his friends outside. He brought a soft red ball from the playground box.

"First, you bounce like this," Joey demonstrated, hopping high with his powerful legs. "Then, when the ball comes to you, you swing your tail like this!" Joey's fluffy tail swept through the air.

The other animals tried their best. Benny used his paws instead of a tail. Lily used her woolly bottom. Everyone giggled and had fun.

"This is the best game ever!" laughed Benny as he caught the ball.

"Thank you for sharing your family tradition with us, Joey!" said Lily.

Joey felt warm and happy inside. His tradition wasn't just special for his family anymore. Now his friends could enjoy it too!

That evening, Joey showed his parents his artwork. "I shared our bounce-ball tradition with everyone today," he said proudly.

"That's wonderful, Joey," said Mama, nuzzling his head. "When we share our traditions, they become even more special."

Papa smiled. "And now more families can enjoy bounce-ball together!"

Joey learned that traditions are meant to be shared. When you share something special from your family, it brings everyone closer together.

The End.
