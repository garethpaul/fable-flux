# Sunny's Word Seeds

On a small balcony garden, high above a busy street, lived a resourceful squirrel named Sunny. Sunny’s tail was fluffy like a soft broom, and her eyes were bright and curious. The balcony smelled like mint and wet soil. Tiny flowers bobbed in the warm breeze.

Every morning, Sunny checked her garden. She patted the soil with her little paws. She watered the herbs with a tiny shell. She had a new goal today. Sunny said, “I will learn three new words before sunset.” She tapped her paw and counted, “One, two, three!” Her whiskers twitched with excitement.

Sunny loved words. She said them out loud and tasted how they felt. “Mint,” she whispered. It smelled fresh and cool. “Lavender,” she sang. It sounded like a lullaby. “Tomato,” she giggled. It felt round and bouncy on her tongue.

Just then, a soft flutter landed on the railing. It was Pippa, a small pigeon with gray feathers like a cloudy sky. “Coo,” said Pippa. “Bonjour!”

Sunny blinked. “Bon… jour?” The word felt like a kiss in the air.

“It means hello in French,” Pippa cooed kindly.

Sunny’s ears perked up. “Oh! That’s a new word.” She held up one finger. “Bonjour.”

A breeze danced through the balcony. The basil leaves waved. The little wind chime tinkled, like tiny bells saying hello too.

Sunny was about to water the tomatoes when a ladybug climbed onto her paw. The ladybug’s red shell shone like a tiny sunset. “Salam!” chirped the ladybug.

“Sa… lam?” Sunny tried. The word felt warm and smooth.

“It means hello in Persian,” said the ladybug. “I’m Laleh.”

Sunny smiled. “Bonjour, Laleh. Salam, Pippa!” She counted on her paw. “Two new words!”

“Coo!” Pippa puffed her feathers. “You are very good with words.”

Sunny felt proud, but she had more work to do. She looked at her plants. The mint was thirsty. The strawberries were shy and small. She had a goal: help the plants and learn words. Sunny thought, I can do both. I can help and learn.

Sunny filled her shell to water the mint. A bee buzzed near her ear. The sound was like a tiny drum. “Bzzzz… Hola!” hummed the bee.

“Hola,” Sunny answered. “That means hello in Spanish, right?”

“Yes!” buzzed the bee. “I am Beto.”

Sunny’s tail swished with joy. “Bonjour, Salam, Hola!” She counted three words. Goal met! But Sunny wanted to keep learning. Words were like seeds. The more she planted, the more she grew.

Sunny noticed the strawberry plants drooping. Their leaves were tired. “They need a little shade,” Sunny said. She looked at Pippa, Laleh, and Beto. “Friends, can you help me?”

Pippa cocked her head. “Coo? How can we help?”

Sunny thought carefully. She remembered another goal: be helpful. She took a deep breath. “Let’s make a leafy tent for the strawberries. But I need to ask nicely. I can use my new words.”

She faced Pippa. “Bonjour, Pippa. Will you hold this small leaf with your beak?”

Pippa cooed happily and picked up a leaf.

Sunny looked at Laleh. “Salam, Laleh. Can you ask your ladybug friends to gather tiny sticks?”

Laleh’s wings clicked. “Salam! Yes, we can.”

Sunny turned to Beto. “Hola, Beto. Can you buzz by the basil and help the flowers feel brave?”

“Hola! I can,” said Beto, zooming to the tiny blooms.

They began to work, but there was a problem. Pippa wanted the leaf to go left. Sunny wanted it to go right so it could shade more berries. The leaf wobbled. The strawberries sighed.

Sunny paused. She remembered another skill: negotiation. She said, “Let’s talk.” She used calm words and a gentle voice. “Pippa, I think right will shade the berries. What do you think?”

Pippa tilted her head. “Coo. Left keeps the wind away.”

Sunny nodded. “I hear you. How about this: we place one leaf to the right for shade, and one leaf to the left for wind. Deal?”

Pippa smiled. “Deal.”

Laleh returned with two tiny sticks. “We can hold both leaves,” she chirped.

They worked together. Sunny tied the leaves with a thin, dry grass string. The grass felt rough on her paws. The shade lay soft and cool over the strawberries. The wind still danced, but not too strong.

Beto buzzed back with a happy hum. “The basil smells brave,” he said.

Sunny clapped her paws. “We did it!”

The strawberries perked up. A sweet smell floated in the warm air. The wind chime sang a little song. Everyone rested on the railing. The sun felt like a warm hug.

Sunny took a deep breath. “Bonjour means hello in French,” she said. “Salam means hello in Persian. Hola means hello in Spanish.” She wiggled her whiskers. “Words help us be kind. Words help us work together. Words help us make good plans.”

Pippa cooed, “Coo-rect!”

Laleh giggled. “Salam, hola, bonjour!” Beto buzzed a happy tune.

Sunny smiled. “Today, I met my goal. I learned new words. I was helpful. We made a plan together.”

She looked at the little strawberry tent. “Words are seeds,” she said softly. “When we plant them with care, good things grow.”

The friends snuggled in the shade and watched the sky turn pink. Sunny’s heart felt full and light. She whispered, “Thank you, words.”

The End.
