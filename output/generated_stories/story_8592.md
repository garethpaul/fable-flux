# Beep’s Batter Day

Beep was a helpful robot dog with shiny ears that blinked blue. He lived in a warm kitchen that smelled like cinnamon toast and sweet strawberries. The oven hummed softly. The sun made little squares of light on the floor. Beep’s metal paws went click-clack on the tiles.

“Good morning, Kitchen!” Beep said in a cheerful beep-bop voice.

“Good morning, Beep,” hummed the kettle. “Pip and Poppy are coming to bake muffins.”

Beep wagged his little antenna tail. “I love helping!”

Soon, the door squeaked open. Pip and Poppy, two small siblings, stepped in with big smiles. Pip wore a yellow apron. Poppy wore a blue one with tiny stars.

“It smells warm and cozy!” Poppy said, rubbing her hands together.

“My tummy says yum,” Pip giggled, patting his belly.

Beep clicked a tray onto the counter. “Beep will help. Beep is good at measuring.” His screen showed a happy face.

Pip picked up a wooden spoon. “I like to stir.”

Poppy held the muffin tin. “I like to scoop.”

They gathered the things they needed. Beep read the list in a soft beep-bop voice. “Flour. Milk. Eggs. Blueberries. Sugar. A pinch of salt.”

The bag of flour went whoosh! A white puff floated in the warm light. It smelled soft and dusty. Poppy sneezed. “Ah-choo!”

“Bless you,” said Beep. He pressed his nose light, and a tiny fan whirred. The flour cloud drifted away.

Pip cracked an egg. “Uh-oh,” he said. “A shell fell in.”

Poppy frowned. “Oh no.”

Beep blinked kindly. “It is okay. Mistakes help us learn. We can try again.” He used a little magnet paw to lift out the shell. “See? We can fix it.”

They smiled and kept going. Pip stirred the bowl. Swish, swish. The batter made soft plop-plop sounds. Poppy scooped slow circles. Beep measured with a steady whirr. “Two cups of flour,” he said. “Beep is very good at counting.”

Pip’s face wrinkled. “My arms feel tired,” he said. “Stirring is hard.”

Poppy huffed. “My scoops look messy.”

Beep paused. His ears blinked blue-blue. He remembered his job: to help, and to celebrate what made each friend special.

“Let’s take a pause,” Beep said gently. “We can breathe. In.” He made a soft whoooo sound. “Out.” He made a gentle pfffff sound. Pip and Poppy breathed with him. Their shoulders relaxed.

“Let’s try again with our own best ways,” Beep said. “We are different. Different is good.”

Pip’s eyes lit up. “I like to sing when I stir,” he said. He sang a silly song, “Stir, stir, stir the bowl,” and his spoon went round and round. The batter swished like a little river.

Poppy grinned. “I like tiny scoops,” she said. “Plop, plop.” She made small, neat circles. Each cup in the tin filled just right.

Beep wagged. “Beep likes to count and beep. Beep can count the scoops.” He pointed a gentle laser dot at each cup. “One, two, three…” The numbers made Poppy giggle.

The kitchen felt warm and busy in a good way. The blueberries smelled sweet. The sugar sparkled like snow. The oven hummed a cozy tune.

Then the batter splashed. A big blue blob landed on Beep’s nose light. “Oh!” Beep chirped. “Beep is… drippy.”

Pip and Poppy burst into laughter. “Blueberry nose!” Pip said.

Beep laughed too, beep-bop-beep. “This is funny. Beep can wipe.” He dabbed his nose with a soft towel. “Everyone is learning. Even Beep.”

They were almost done when Poppy’s scoop slipped. Batter plopped onto the floor. Poppy’s face crumpled. “I’m frustrated,” she said.

Beep rolled close and sat beside her. His metal side felt cool; the kitchen air felt warm. “It is okay to feel that,” he said. “Let’s make a plan. We can clean, then try again.”

Pip brought a cloth. Swish, swish. Poppy wiped her eyes and smiled a little. “Thank you.”

“You are careful and neat,” Beep said to Poppy. “Your tiny scoops are perfect.”

“You are strong and musical,” Beep said to Pip. “Your stirring song helps the batter.”

“And you are smart and steady,” Poppy said to Beep. “You count so well.”

They put the tin in the oven. Tick-tick went the timer. The kitchen filled with a sweet, warm smell. Pip’s tummy rumbled. Poppy tapped her toes. Beep’s ears blinked in a happy pattern.

Ding! The muffins were ready. Golden tops, little purple dots, soft steam rising. They waited for them to cool. Pip breathed in. “Mmm.” Poppy hugged herself. “Cozy.”

They sat together at the table. Each friend took a muffin. Pip took a big bite. Poppy took a tiny one. Beep did a gentle taste test with a flavor sensor. “Delicious,” he said. “Teamwork tastes good.”

Poppy looked at her muffin. “We did it,” she said. “I used tiny scoops.”

Pip nodded. “I sang and stirred.”

Beep wagged. “Beep counted and helped.”

They smiled at each other. “We are different,” said Pip.

“And that makes our team strong,” said Poppy.

Beep’s ears blinked bright. “Different is wonderful,” he said. “When we use our own best ways, we learn, we practice, and we improve. When we feel frustrated, we can breathe, make a plan, and try again.”

They clinked their muffins together like cups. Crinkle, crinkle went the paper. The warm kitchen hummed, happy and full.

The End.
