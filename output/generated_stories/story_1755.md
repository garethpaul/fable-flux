# Benny Bear's Moving Day

Benny Bear was a fluffy brown cub who loved sunny days. This morning, the warm sunshine streamed through his bedroom window, making golden patches on his floor.

"What a beautiful day!" Benny said, stretching his arms high above his head. "I want to go outside and play!"

He padded to the backyard on his soft paws. The grass felt cool and tickly between his toes. Birds chirped happy songs in the tall oak tree, and butterflies danced around the colorful flowers.

But as Benny looked around, he felt a little sad. "I want to play, but I don't know what to do," he said to himself. "Maybe I'll just sit here."

Benny plopped down on the soft grass and watched a busy bee buzz from flower to flower. The bee worked so hard, flying from place to place.

"That bee is moving so much!" Benny noticed. "It looks happy flying around."

Just then, a red cardinal landed on a branch above him. "Good morning, little bear!" chirped the cardinal. "Why do you look so sleepy?"

"I feel tired even though I just woke up," Benny said. "I don't know what to do today."

The wise cardinal tilted her bright red head. "When I feel that way, I like to move my body! It makes me feel awake and happy. Want to try?"

Benny thought about this. "How do I move my body?"

"There are so many fun ways!" the cardinal sang. "You could march like a soldier, hop like a bunny, or dance like the butterflies!"

Benny felt curious. He stood up slowly and took a deep breath. The fresh air smelled like sweet flowers and green grass.

"I'll try marching!" Benny said. He lifted his left paw high, then his right paw. "Left, right, left, right!" Soon he was marching in a big circle around the yard.

"This feels good!" Benny giggled. His heart started beating faster, and he felt more awake.

Next, he tried hopping like a bunny. "Boing, boing, boing!" Benny bounced across the yard, his fluffy fur bouncing with each hop. The warm sun felt wonderful on his back.

Then Benny saw the yellow butterflies dancing near the roses. He spread his arms wide like wings and spun in gentle circles. "I'm a dancing bear!" he laughed.

As Benny moved, he noticed how his body felt. His muscles felt strong and stretchy. His breathing was deeper. Most importantly, he felt happy and full of energy!

"You look much more awake now!" called the cardinal from her branch.

"I feel amazing!" Benny said, doing three more little hops. "Moving my body made me feel so much better!"

Benny decided to make moving a special part of every day. He created his own morning routine: five marching steps, ten bunny hops, and three butterfly spins.

"When I move my body, I feel strong and happy," Benny said to himself. "And when I pay attention to how I feel, I can take care of myself better!"

From that day on, whenever Benny felt sleepy or sad, he remembered to move his body. Sometimes he would run, sometimes he would stretch, and sometimes he would dance. Each time, the exercise made him feel wonderful.

The sunny backyard became Benny's favorite place to move and play, and he was never bored again.

The End.
