# Kipp the Kangaroo Joey’s Sunny Backyard Share

The sun was warm and bright in the sunny backyard. The grass smelled sweet and tickly. Little Kipp, a joyful kangaroo joey, bounced in happy hops. His soft ears wiggled. His pouch felt cozy and safe.

“Boing, boing!” Kipp giggled. “Today is a play day!”

In the yard, there was a shiny yellow ball, a tiny sand box, and a blue watering can. Kipp loved the yellow ball most. It was squishy and made a funny squeak when he hugged it.

Squeak!

“Mine!” Kipp cheered, hugging the ball tight.

Just then, friends arrived. Tilly Tortoise moved slowly with a gentle smile. Miko Magpie fluttered to the fence, black-and-white feathers sparkling. Benny Bunny hopped in with soft thumps, nose wiggling fast.

“Hi, Kipp!” they all said.

“Hi, friends!” Kipp waved. “Let’s play in my sunny backyard!”

Tilly looked at the sand. “May I make tiny sand cakes?” she asked softly.

Miko tilted his head. “May I tap the watering can? I like drip-drop sounds.”

Benny’s eyes lit up. “Can I bounce the yellow ball with you, Kipp?”

Kipp hugged the ball tighter. He liked the squeak. He liked the shine. He liked that it was his.

He frowned a little. “Um… I want the ball. I had it first.”

Benny’s ears dipped. “Oh.”

Miko shuffled his feet. “We can take turns,” he chirped.

Tilly nodded slowly. “Taking turns feels fair.”

Kipp’s tummy felt twisty, like a tiny knot. He heard the breeze brush the leaves. He smelled wet dirt by the garden. He looked at his friends’ faces.

He remembered Mama Roo’s words: “Respect means we care about others’ feelings. Justice means we play fair. Integrity means we do what is right, even when no one tells us.”

Kipp looked at the ball. He looked at Benny. He looked at Miko and Tilly. His heart made a thump-thump that felt kind.

Kipp took a deep breath. “I want to do the right thing,” he whispered to himself. “I can make a good choice.”

He bounced the ball once. Squeak! Then he smiled. “Friends, let’s make a play plan!”

“A play plan?” Benny asked, ears perking up.

“Yes,” Kipp said. “We will take turns. I will count to five hops. Then I pass the ball. That is fair. That is just.”

Miko chirped, “And honest too! You’re saying what you will do, and doing it.”

Tilly smiled. “I like the plan.”

Kipp started. “One hop, two hop, three hop, four hop, five hop!” He bounced. Squeak, squeak, squeak! Then he stopped, held the ball out, and said, “Your turn, Benny.”

Benny’s eyes sparkled. “Thank you, Kipp.” He bounced the ball gently. Squeak! Squeak! Then, “One hop, two hop, three hop, four hop, five hop!” He passed it to Miko.

Miko tapped with his wing and giggled. “Squeak!” He counted too and passed to Tilly.

Tilly made careful, tiny bounces. “Squeak.” She counted to five very slowly and passed the ball back to Kipp.

They played and laughed. The sun felt warm on their backs. A soft breeze smelled like flowers. The ball squeaked like a happy mouse.

After a while, Miko pointed to the watering can. “Drip-drops?”

Kipp nodded. “Yes. We can take turns there too.” He picked up the can and listened. Drip, drop, drip! It sounded like tiny rain. He smiled and handed it to Miko.

Miko let water rain on a small patch of dirt. “Drip, drop,” he sang. Benny and Tilly clapped.

Then Tilly asked, “May I make sand cakes now?”

Kipp said, “Yes. Let’s make them together. We will use the scoop one by one.”

Tilly pressed the sand with her little feet. Pat, pat. Benny held the scoop and counted, “One cake, two cake!” Miko decorated with tiny leaves.

Kipp watched his friends. His chest felt light and warm. He had made a good choice. He had respected others. He had been fair.

Just then, a gust of wind whooshed! The yellow ball rolled toward the fence.

“Oh no!” Benny cried.

“I’ll get it,” Kipp said. He hopped fast. Boing, boing! He stopped the ball with his tail tip and brought it back.

Kipp looked at the ball and then at his friends. He could keep it. He could grab it and bounce and bounce. But he remembered his plan.

Kipp took a deep breath and smiled. “It’s your turn, Benny,” he said. He handed the ball over without waiting.

Benny beamed. “Kipp, you kept your word.”

Miko chirped, “That is integrity!”

Tilly nodded. “And that is respect.”

They played until the sun stretched long shadows across the grass. Their cheeks felt warm. Their feet felt sandy. Their hearts felt happy.

Mama Roo peeked out the back door. “I hear giggles,” she said with a grin. “How is the play day?”

Kipp hopped over. “We made a plan. We took turns. We were fair and kind.”

Mama Roo hugged him. “I am proud. Respect, justice, and integrity make playtime good for everyone.”

Kipp smiled and looked at his friends. “When we respect others, everyone feels safe. When we are fair, everyone gets a turn. When we keep our word, everyone trusts us.”

Benny, Miko, and Tilly cheered, “Yes!”

Kipp bounced one last happy hop. “Let’s always play this way.”

The End.
