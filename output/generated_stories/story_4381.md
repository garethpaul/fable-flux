# Leo’s Fair Share Dance

The warm kitchen smelled like cinnamon toast and orange jam. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. A brave little lion cub named Leo stood on a soft rug, his whiskers twitching. “I’m ready to help!” he roared in a tiny voice.

Mama Lion hummed at the counter, stirring a big bowl. “We’re making fruit toast,” she said. “Strawberries, bananas, and sweet honey.”

Leo’s tummy rumbled. “Yum! Can I have the biggest piece?” he asked, bouncing on his paws. His tail swished with excitement, and his ears perked up.

Just then, Leo’s friends padded in. Tori the tiny turtle wore a bright ribbon. Zig the zebra foal had zigzag socks. Pip the puppy wagged so fast his whole body wiggled.

“Fruit toast!” Pip barked happily. “I’m so hungry.”

Tori smiled. “I move slow, but I’m here to share.”

Zig stomped a rhythm on the floor. “Let’s make it fun!”

Mama Lion sliced the toast into four pieces. Each piece had shiny strawberries and banana circles. The honey made a golden gloss. “We will share,” she said, placing the plate on the table.

Leo saw the biggest slice. It had one more strawberry than the others. His paws felt tingly. “I want that one,” he blurted. He stretched his paw toward it.

Pip’s ears drooped. Tori tucked her head into her shell a little. Zig stopped stomping.

Mama Lion lifted a gentle paw. “Hmm,” she said softly. “How do we know what is fair?”

Leo tilted his head. “Fair is… I get the biggest, because I am brave?”

“Being brave is great,” Mama Lion said, smiling. “But fair means everyone gets their share. Fair feels good to all, not just one.”

Leo looked at his friends. Pip’s nose twitched. Tori’s eyes shone. Zig’s stripes looked a bit dull now. Leo’s chest felt warm and wobbly. He wanted everyone to smile.

“I want it to be fair,” he said. “But how do we make it fair?”

Zig flicked his tail. “We can count!” he said, pointing with a hoof.

Tori nodded. “And we can wait our turn.”

Pip barked twice. “We can move! We can play a game to help us choose.”

Leo’s eyes lit up. “A game in the kitchen?” He looked at Mama.

Mama Lion chuckled. “Yes. But safe and gentle. I love your big ideas. That’s innovation—thinking of a new way.”

Leo stood tall. “Let’s make a Fair Share Dance!” He tapped the floor. Tap, tap. “We dance in a circle around the table. When the music stops, we choose in order. We’ll count strawberries to make sure each piece is the same.”

“Great plan,” Mama said. “I can hum for music.”

They made a quick rule list:
- Gentle steps, no bumping.
- Count together.
- If one piece has more, we move one fruit to another.

Mama Lion hummed a cozy tune. “La-la, la-la.” The hum tickled the air. Warm bread smell hugged the room. The friends moved in a slow, happy circle.

Step, slide. Step, slide. Tori’s ribbon bobbed. Pip’s paws pattered. Zig’s hooves clicked softly. Leo stretched his paws in a brave, bouncy step, then slowed to match Tori’s pace. “We move together,” he said kindly. “Fast friends help slow friends. Slow friends help fast friends.”

Mama stopped humming. The room grew still and giggly.

“Now we count,” Leo said. He pointed to the first slice. “One, two, three strawberries!”

The second slice: “One, two, three.”

The third slice: “One, two, three.”

The fourth slice—the biggest one—had “One, two, three, four!”

Pip’s eyes widened. “That’s more.”

Leo took a breath. His tail curled and uncurled. He wanted that extra berry, but he wanted fairness more. “We can fix it,” he said. He carefully moved one strawberry from the big slice to a smaller one. “Now each has three.”

Tori clapped her tiny feet. “Fair!”

Zig grinned. “Smart solution.”

Mama Lion nodded. “You used movement, counting, and a clever idea to make it fair.”

“Who chooses first?” Pip asked.

Leo lifted his chin. “I had the first idea. But fair means we can try a new way.” He thought for a moment. “How about this: Tori can choose first, because she moved slow and still joined the dance. Then Pip, then Zig, then me.”

Tori blinked. “Thank you, Leo. That is kind.”

Pip wagged. “I like this.”

Zig tapped a happy beat.

They danced one more gentle circle to celebrate. The warm floor felt friendly under their feet. The honey smell made their noses wiggle.

Tori chose a slice. Pip chose next. Zig picked his with a happy hoof tap. Leo took the last piece and held it up. “We all have three strawberries,” he said proudly. “Fair and square!”

They ate together. Crunch, munch. The toast was warm. The fruit was sweet and soft. Honey kissed their tongues like sunshine.

After the snacks, Leo wiped his whiskers. “I learned something,” he said. “Fair is not ‘me first.’ Fair is ‘we all share.’ We can move, think, and try new ways to help everyone.”

Mama Lion gave him a gentle hug. “That is a brave heart,” she said. “Brave enough to be fair.”

Pip licked his nose. “Can we play the Fair Share Dance again?”

Zig laughed. “Next time, with apple slices!”

Tori smiled. “I will wear my ribbon again.”

Leo nodded, his eyes bright. “Yes! We can use our bodies, our minds, and our kindness.”

The warm kitchen glowed. The friends felt full and happy, not just in their tummies but in their hearts.

And from that day on, when it was time to share, Leo would say, “Let’s dance it fair!”

The End.
