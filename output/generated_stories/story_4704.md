# Polly's Car Trip Colors

The family car hummed softly down the sunny road. In the backseat, a bright green parrot named Polly sat in her special travel cage. Her colorful feathers shimmered in the morning light.

"Are we there yet?" asked little Emma from her car seat.

"Not yet, sweetie," said Mom from the front. "We still have a long drive to Grandma's house."

Polly ruffled her beautiful red and blue feathers. She had packed her favorite things for the trip - shiny crayons, sparkly stickers, and her precious drawing pad. These were HER special art supplies, and she didn't want to share them with anyone.

"I'm bored," Emma sighed, looking out the window at the passing trees.

Polly's sharp eyes noticed Emma's sad face. The little girl's hands were empty - she had nothing to do during the long car ride.

"Squawk! Pretty colors!" Polly chirped, holding up her bright yellow crayon.

Emma's eyes grew wide with wonder. "Wow, Polly! That's so pretty! I wish I could draw too."

Polly clutched her crayon tighter. These were HER special things. But then she saw Emma's disappointed face and felt a warm feeling in her feathered chest.

"Polly share?" the parrot asked herself quietly.

Dad's gentle voice came from the driver's seat. "Sharing makes car trips more fun for everyone, Polly."

Polly looked at her pile of art supplies, then at Emma's hopeful smile. Slowly, she picked up a purple crayon with her clever beak.

"Here, Emma! Purple for you!" Polly squawked cheerfully.

Emma clapped her hands with joy. "Thank you, Polly! You're so kind!"

Polly felt her heart grow warm and happy. Sharing felt even better than keeping everything to herself! She passed Emma some paper too.

"Let's draw together!" Polly chirped excitedly.

Emma giggled as she made purple circles on her paper. "Look, Polly! I'm drawing grapes!"

"Beautiful grapes!" Polly squawked, using her green crayon to draw leaves. "Polly draw tree!"

The two friends worked side by side, their crayons making soft scratching sounds on the paper. The car filled with happy laughter and creative energy.

"Can I try the sparkly stickers?" Emma asked politely.

"Yes! Sharing is caring!" Polly replied, carefully placing a shiny star sticker on Emma's drawing.

Mom smiled in the rearview mirror. "You two are being such good friends back there."

As they drew and decorated together, the long car ride flew by quickly. Polly discovered that sharing her art supplies made everything twice as fun. Emma's grateful smile made Polly's heart sing louder than any song she could chirp.

"Thank you for sharing, Polly," Emma said softly. "You made this trip so special."

Polly preened her feathers proudly. "Thank YOU, Emma! Sharing makes everything more beautiful!"

When they finally arrived at Grandma's house, both friends had created wonderful artwork together. Their papers were covered in colorful drawings, sparkly stickers, and happy memories.

"Look what we made together!" they called to Grandma, holding up their collaborative masterpiece.

Grandma hugged them both. "Sharing and working together creates the most beautiful art of all!"

Polly learned that day that the best part of having special things wasn't keeping them all to herself - it was sharing them with friends and seeing their faces light up with joy.

The End.
