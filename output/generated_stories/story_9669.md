# Pip's Big Game Day

Pip the penguin wiggled in his car seat. His flippers pressed against the cool window. "Are we there yet, Mama?" he asked.

Mama Penguin smiled in the front seat. "Almost, little one. The ice hockey rink is just around the corner."

Pip's tummy felt funny. Not hungry funny. Worried funny. Today was his first hockey game with the other young penguins. His orange beak trembled a little.

"What if I'm not good enough?" Pip whispered. "What if I fall down? What if the other penguins are better than me?"

Papa Penguin looked in the rearview mirror. His kind eyes met Pip's. "Remember what we practiced, Pip. Take deep breaths. In through your beak, out through your beak."

Pip breathed slowly. The warm air from the car heater felt nice on his feathers. But his flippers still shook.

"I don't think I can do it," Pip said sadly. "Maybe we should go home."

The car stopped at a red light. Mama turned around. "Pip, do you remember when you first learned to slide on your belly?"

Pip nodded. "I fell down lots of times."

"But what happened next?" Papa asked gently.

"I... I got back up," Pip said quietly. "And then I could slide really fast!"

"That's right!" Mama said. "You were brave then. You can be brave now too."

The car started moving again. Pip watched the snowy trees go by. He thought about sliding down hills. He thought about swimming in the cold ocean. He had learned to do those things. Maybe he could learn hockey too.

"But what if the other penguins laugh at me?" Pip asked.

"If someone laughs, what could you do?" Papa asked.

Pip thought hard. "I could... I could ask them to help me instead?"

"What a wonderful idea!" Mama said. "Asking for help shows you're smart and brave."

The car pulled into the parking lot. Through the window, Pip could see other young penguins waddling toward the rink. Some looked excited. Some looked nervous too.

"Look, Pip," Papa pointed. "That little penguin over there looks worried just like you."

Pip saw a small penguin standing alone by the door. The penguin's flippers were shaking.

"Maybe I could help him feel better," Pip said. His own worry started to feel smaller.

"That's the spirit!" Mama said. "When we help others, we feel braver too."

Pip unbuckled his seatbelt. His flippers weren't shaking anymore. "I think I'm ready now."

As they walked to the rink, Pip waddled over to the nervous penguin. "Hi! I'm Pip. Are you scared too?"

The little penguin nodded. "I'm Penny. This is my first game."

"Mine too!" Pip said. "Want to practice together? We can help each other!"

Penny's face brightened. "Yes, please!"

On the ice, Pip fell down three times. But each time, Penny helped him up. When Penny dropped her hockey stick, Pip picked it up for her. Soon they were both giggling and having fun.

The coach penguin clapped his flippers. "Great teamwork, everyone! Remember, hockey is about helping each other and doing your best."

After the game, Pip climbed back into the car. His cheeks were pink from the cold, and his heart felt warm and happy.

"How did it go?" Mama asked.

"I was scared at first," Pip said. "But then I remembered to breathe. And I helped Penny feel better. That made me feel brave!"

"You learned something very important today," Papa said. "Being brave doesn't mean you're not scared. It means you try anyway."

Pip snuggled into his car seat. "And helping others helps me too!"

As they drove home, Pip watched the snowy world outside. He couldn't wait for the next hockey game.

The End.
