# Koko's Big Brave Day

Koko the koala loved his quiet life in the big eucalyptus tree. Every morning, he would wake up, stretch his fuzzy gray arms, and eat his favorite eucalyptus leaves. Then he would take a long, peaceful nap. This was Koko's happy routine.

One sunny morning, Koko's friend Bella the bird flew to his tree. "Koko! Koko!" she chirped excitedly. "My grandmother is visiting today, and she needs a place to stay. Could she use the guest room in your tree?"

Koko's tummy felt funny and fluttery. He had never had a guest before! His guest room was small and cozy, with soft moss for a bed and pretty flowers by the window. But what if he did something wrong? What if his guest didn't like him?

"I... I don't know, Bella," Koko said quietly, his voice barely louder than a whisper. "I've never had a visitor before."

"Please, Koko?" Bella asked. "Grandma Robin is very kind, and she just needs somewhere safe to rest."

Koko wanted to help his friend, but his heart was beating fast like a little drum. He was scared! But then he remembered what his mama always told him: "Being brave doesn't mean you're not scared. It means you do the right thing even when you feel scared."

Koko took a deep breath that smelled like sweet eucalyptus. "Okay, Bella. Grandma Robin can stay in my guest room."

Soon, Grandma Robin arrived. She was a lovely bird with silver feathers that sparkled in the sunlight. "Thank you so much, dear Koko," she said in a warm, gentle voice.

But oh no! Koko was so nervous that he forgot all about his good manners. He didn't offer Grandma Robin any water. He didn't show her where the bathroom was. He didn't even say "welcome" properly! Instead, he just pointed to the guest room and mumbled, "There's your room."

Grandma Robin looked a little sad, but she smiled kindly and went to rest.

Koko felt terrible. His tummy hurt, and his eyes felt watery. He had been rude to a nice guest! He sat on his branch, thinking about what to do.

"I need to be brave again," Koko whispered to himself. "I need to make things right."

Koko gathered some of his prettiest eucalyptus leaves and picked fresh berries. He filled a small bowl with cool, clean water. Then, even though his paws were shaking a little, he knocked gently on the guest room door.

"Grandma Robin?" he said softly. "I'm sorry I wasn't a good host. I was scared because I've never had a guest before. Would you like some snacks and water?"

Grandma Robin's eyes twinkled. "Oh, Koko! That's very thoughtful. And you know what? It takes courage to say sorry. I'm proud of you."

They shared the snacks together, and Grandma Robin told Koko wonderful stories about her travels. Koko discovered that having a guest was actually fun!

From that day on, Koko added something new to his daily routine. Along with eating leaves and napping, he would also think about ways to be kind and brave. And whenever friends needed help, Koko would take a deep breath and say yes, even when he felt a little scared.

"Being brave and making things right always feels good," Koko would tell himself, smiling as he watched the sunset paint the sky in pretty colors.

The End.
