# Mimi Mouse Makes a Plan

Mimi was a kind little mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. She lived in a cozy bedroom at the corner of a sunny house. The bedroom smelled like clean blankets and warm cookies, and the window let in golden morning light. Mimi loved her room, but today it looked very, very messy.

Toys were piled like tiny mountains. Socks were hiding under the bed like sleepy worms. Books were scattered on the floor like leaves. Mimi sighed and wiggled her whiskers. “Oh my,” she said. “I can’t find my red ribbon for the Friendship Parade!”

Tonight, Mimi wanted to wear her red ribbon to wave hello to friends around the world on the computer. The parade would happen on the screen, with families from many places singing and clapping together. “We say hello to everyone,” Mimi said happily. “We share songs and smiles.” But first, she had a problem. Her ribbon was lost in the mess.

Mimi took a deep breath. “I feel a little worried,” she said. She put a paw on her heart and listened to the quiet tick-tock of the bedside clock. Tick. Tock. Tick. Tock. “I can do this,” Mimi whispered. “I will make a plan.”

She climbed on her soft bed. “Step one: Look closely,” she said. She opened her eyes wide and sniffed the air. She smelled clean soap, crinkly paper, and a tiny hint of strawberries from her blanket. “No ribbon yet,” she said.

She hopped down and opened her little drawer. “Step two: Make places.” She set out three small baskets. She stuck a label on each one with a crayon. “Toys,” Mimi read. “Clothes.” “Books.” She smiled. “Organization helps me find what I need.”

Then Mimi called, “Friends, can you help me? We can coordinate!” Her friends were far away, but they could still help. On the computer, a tiny bell chimed, ding-ding. Mimi’s screen lit up with friendly faces: Lina the ladybug from a sunny garden, Bao the bear cub from a snowy hill, and Amara the cat from a sandy beach. They all waved.

“Hi, Mimi!” they said.

“I need my red ribbon for the Friendship Parade,” Mimi said. “My room is so messy. I made baskets. Can you help me make a plan?”

Lina buzzed softly. “We can do it together. First, sort things by type. That’s coordination!”

Bao nodded and clapped his paws in a slow beat. Clap, clap. “We can move together to the rhythm. Step, sort, place!”

Amara purred. “We can make it fun. We can sing a tidy-up song.”

Mimi’s whiskers twitched with joy. “Yes! We can help each other, even from far away. That is being good friends to the whole wide world.”

They began. Mimi sang, “Pick it up, find its home, we can do this, we’re not alone!” Her friends sang with her, each in their own voice. Their song sounded like honey and sunshine. Mimi picked up a toy car. “Toys basket,” she said, plop! She folded a sock. “Clothes basket,” she said, flop! She gathered books and patted their covers. “Books basket,” she said, tap!

“Pause and check,” said Lina. “Is the floor clear in one small square?”

Mimi looked at a square of the floor. “Yes!” she said. She wriggled with pride.

“Great coordination,” said Bao. “Now the next square. Clap, clap.”

Mimi moved to the next spot. She felt calm. She felt strong. The tick-tock clock seemed to cheer for her. Tick, tock, you can do it. Tick, tock, nice and slow.

She found a shiny marble. She found a tiny bell. Ding-ding! She found a long red ribbon peeking from under a pillow. “I found it!” she squeaked. She rubbed the ribbon on her cheek. It felt smooth and cool. “Thank you, friends!”

Amara smiled on the screen. “Look how neat your room looks, Mimi. You planned. You sorted. You worked together.”

Mimi tied the ribbon around her ear in a sweet bow. She looked at her cozy bedroom. The bed was made, the baskets were full, and the floor felt soft under her tiny feet. The room smelled like fresh air and happy plans.

“Let’s do a quick check,” said Lina. “Do toys live with toys?”

“Yes!” said Mimi.

“Do clothes live with clothes?” asked Bao.

“Yes!” said Mimi.

“Do books live with books?” asked Amara.

“Yes!” said Mimi. She giggled. “Everything has a home. That makes it easy to share my space.”

Evening came. The screen filled with families from many places—snowy hills, sunny gardens, sandy beaches, and busy cities. People waved and smiled. Mimi waved back with her red ribbon dancing like a little flame. She felt like part of one big family.

“Hello, world!” Mimi said. “We worked together today. We made a plan. We listened and helped. That’s coordination. That’s organization. That’s being kind to friends near and far.”

Her friends clapped and sang. Mimi’s heart glowed warm like a nightlight. She learned that when a problem feels too big, making a plan and coordinating with friends can make it small. She learned that being organized helps everyone share and find things, and that being a global friend means helping and cheering across any distance.

Mimi cuddled into her pillow. “I can do it, and we can do it together,” she whispered. The clock went tick-tock, the window hummed with a gentle breeze, and her red ribbon lay safe on her bedside table, right where it belonged.

The End.
