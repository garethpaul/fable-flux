# Leo's Cookie Mystery

Leo the lion cub loved spending time in Mama Lion's warm kitchen. The sunny room smelled like fresh bread and cinnamon, and colorful spoons hung from little hooks on the wall. Leo's golden fur sparkled in the morning light streaming through the window.

"Good morning, my brave little cub!" said Mama Lion, giving Leo a gentle nuzzle. "I baked special honey cookies yesterday. Would you like one with your milk?"

Leo's tummy rumbled happily. "Yes, please, Mama!"

But when Mama Lion opened the cookie jar, her face looked puzzled. "Oh my! There were six cookies here yesterday, and now there are only three. That's strange."

Leo's ears drooped a little. He remembered something from last night, but his paws felt tingly and nervous. After dinner, when Mama was reading to his baby sister, Leo had climbed onto the counter. The cookie jar smelled so sweet and delicious. He had taken just one cookie... well, maybe two... or was it three?

"Maybe the cookies walked away by themselves!" Leo said with a little giggle, hoping Mama wouldn't ask more questions.

Mama Lion tilted her head. "Cookies can't walk, silly cub. Someone must have eaten them." She looked around the kitchen, checking the floor for crumbs.

Leo's tummy felt twisty now, not hungry anymore. He watched Mama Lion search the kitchen, moving chairs and looking under the table. She even checked if Papa Lion had eaten them, but Papa was still sleeping.

"This is a real mystery," Mama said, scratching her head with her paw.

Leo's heart felt heavy like a big rock. Mama looked worried, and it was all his fault. He thought about telling the truth, but what if Mama got upset? His little paws fidgeted with his tail.

Then Leo remembered what Mama always said: "Brave lions tell the truth, even when it's hard."

Leo took a deep breath that smelled like warm kitchen air. "Mama," he said in a small voice, "I need to tell you something important."

Mama Lion knelt down so her warm brown eyes were level with Leo's. "What is it, my brave cub?"

"I... I ate the cookies last night. After dinner. I climbed up when you weren't looking." Leo's voice got even smaller. "I'm sorry, Mama. I should have asked first."

Mama Lion was quiet for a moment. Then she wrapped her strong, warm arms around Leo in the biggest, softest hug.

"Thank you for telling me the truth, Leo. That was very brave and honest of you."

"But aren't you mad?" Leo asked, his voice muffled in Mama's fuzzy chest.

"I'm proud of you for being honest," Mama said gently. "Taking cookies without asking wasn't the right choice, but telling the truth was exactly the right thing to do."

Together, they cleaned up the kitchen and organized the cookie jar properly. Mama showed Leo how to ask permission next time he wanted a treat.

"Honest cubs get to help make new cookies," Mama said with a smile. "Would you like to be my special kitchen helper today?"

Leo's whole face lit up like sunshine. "Yes! And next time, I'll always ask first!"

As they mixed flour and honey together, Leo felt warm and happy inside. Telling the truth felt much better than keeping a secret.

The End.
