# Koda's Colorful Kitchen Adventure

Koda the koala stretched his fuzzy gray arms and yawned as the warm morning sun streamed through the kitchen window. The kitchen smelled wonderful - like fresh bread and sweet honey. Koda's tummy rumbled loudly.

"Good morning, sleepyhead!" said Mama Koala, stirring something delicious in a big blue bowl. "Are you ready for breakfast?"

Koda climbed onto his special chair at the kitchen table. The wooden seat felt warm from the sunshine. "I want cookies for breakfast!" he said, pointing to the cookie jar on the counter.

Mama Koala smiled gently. "Cookies are yummy, but they're a sometimes food. Let's make a rainbow breakfast instead! It will give your body energy to play all day."

"A rainbow breakfast?" Koda's dark eyes grew wide with curiosity. "How do we make a rainbow?"

"With colorful foods!" Mama explained. "Each color gives our bodies different vitamins to help us grow strong and healthy."

Together, they walked to the refrigerator. Koda loved the cool air that whooshed out when Mama opened the door. Inside, fruits and vegetables sparkled like jewels.

"Red strawberries for vitamin C," Mama said, handing Koda three plump berries. They felt smooth and cool in his small paws.

"Orange carrots for healthy eyes," she continued, giving him baby carrots that crunched when he bit them.

"Yellow bananas for energy," Mama peeled a banana that smelled sweet and tropical.

Koda giggled as he helped arrange the foods on his plate. "What about green, Mama?"

"Spinach leaves! They make our muscles strong like a superhero," Mama said, adding fresh green leaves to their colorful creation.

"And blue?" Koda asked, though he couldn't think of any blue foods.

"Blueberries!" Mama sprinkled tiny blue gems across the plate. "They help our brains think better."

Finally, Mama added purple grapes. "Purple foods help keep us from getting sick."

Koda looked at his beautiful rainbow plate. The colors were so bright and pretty! "It looks like art, Mama!"

As they sat together at the warm kitchen table, Koda took his first bite. The strawberry was sweet and juicy. The carrot was crunchy and fun to chew. Each food tasted different and special.

"Mama, why do we need to eat rainbow foods every day?" Koda asked between bites.

"Because our bodies are like gardens," Mama explained patiently. "Gardens need different things to grow - sunshine, water, and good soil. Our bodies need different colored foods to grow strong and healthy."

Koda nodded thoughtfully. He understood now why Mama wanted him to eat vegetables even when he preferred cookies. "So if I eat my rainbow foods, I'll grow big and strong?"

"That's right, my sweet koala. And you'll have energy to climb trees, play with friends, and learn new things!"

After finishing his rainbow breakfast, Koda felt wonderful. His tummy was happy and full, and he felt ready for anything.

"Thank you for teaching me about rainbow foods, Mama," Koda said, giving her a big, warm hug. "Can we make rainbow lunch too?"

Mama Koala smiled and hugged him back. "Of course! Taking care of our bodies is one of the most important jobs we have."

From that day on, Koda looked forward to creating colorful, healthy meals. He learned that taking responsibility for his health made him feel proud and strong.

The End.
