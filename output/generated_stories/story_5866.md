# Benny Beaver’s Balcony Plan

The morning air on the balcony felt cool and fresh. Benny the busy beaver wiggled his whiskers and sniffed. He could smell wet soil and sweet basil. He could hear tiny bees buzzing by the purple flowers. He could feel the warm sun touch his fur.

“Good morning, little garden,” Benny said. He patted a pot of cherry tomatoes. “You look thirsty.”

Benny loved his balcony garden. He had small pots of lettuce, carrots, herbs, and a big pot with a baby lemon tree. He also had a little blue watering can and a stack of neat wooden sticks for plant markers. Benny liked to be helpful. He liked to finish his jobs. Sometimes he got so excited that he hurried and spilled water everywhere.

Today, Benny had a plan. He wiggled his tail and whispered, “Think first. Then do.” He had taught himself that when he felt rushy inside, he could slow down and make a plan. That helped his body and his brain work together.

Benny took a deep breath. In, out. He put his paw on his chest. “Calm body. Clear brain,” he said softly.

“Water, weed, snack, share,” he told himself. “That’s my plan!”

Just then, a tiny voice squeaked from the other balcony. It was Mimi Mouse, peeking through the railing. Her whiskers twitched.

“Hi, Benny! Your tomatoes look shiny. Mine look sleepy,” Mimi said. “I only have one carrot today.”

Benny looked at his garden. He had four carrots ready. He had many tomatoes almost red. He felt a tug inside. He wanted to help Mimi. He also wanted to water his own plants first. He pressed his paw to his chest again.

“Calm body. Clear brain,” he whispered.

He thought, What do my plants need first? What does Mimi need? How can we both feel good?

Benny picked up his blue watering can. “First, I water,” he told himself. He checked each pot with his finger. “Dry or wet?” he said out loud. The lettuce soil felt dry. “Slurp, slurp,” went the water. The basil soil felt damp. “No water now,” Benny decided. He felt proud. He was using his brain and his senses.

Next, he pulled three little weeds from the carrot pot. He smelled the fresh, green scent. He placed the weeds in a tiny bucket. “Weeds out, carrots happy,” he said.

Mimi watched quietly. “You are good at caring,” she whispered.

Benny smiled. “I practice,” he said. “I made a plan. It helps me not rush.”

He looked at his four ready carrots. He looked at Mimi’s face. He imagined how she felt. He made a new plan in his head.

“Step one,” he said. “Count the carrots.” He touched each one. “One, two, three, four.”

“Step two. Save some. Share some.” He thought about tomorrow. He thought about the next day. He wanted carrots for later soup. He also wanted Mimi to have a carrot today.

“Step three. Talk it out,” Benny said.

He turned to Mimi. “I can share two carrots with you,” he said. “I will keep two to save and cook later. Does that sound fair?”

Mimi’s eyes grew bright. “Oh, yes! Thank you, Benny!”

Benny carefully tugged two carrots from the soil. He shook off the dirt. The carrots smelled earthy and sweet. He used a little brush to clean them. He passed them through the railing to Mimi’s tiny paws.

Mimi bounced. “Crunch, crunch!” she said, taking a nibble. “Delicious!”

Benny felt warm in his chest. His body felt calm. His brain felt smart. He had followed his plan, and he had been kind.

Just then, a small cloud slid over the sun. The wind wiggled the lemon tree. The baby lemons bumped against each other.

“Oh!” Benny said. “The stakes!” He had planned to use his wooden sticks to help the lemon tree stand tall.

“Step one. Get the sticks,” he said. He set them near the pot. “Step two. Tie gently.” He used soft ribbon to make loose loops. He checked with his fingers. “Not too tight,” he murmured. He felt the bark. It was smooth and cool.

Mimi watched with big eyes. “You think before you do,” she said. “You make steps.”

Benny nodded. “When my tummy feels bubbly, I stop, breathe, and plan,” he said. “It helps me make good choices.”

The sun peeked out again. A ladybug landed on Benny’s paw. It tickled. Benny giggled.

“Snack time!” he said. He picked one cherry tomato. It was red and shiny. He rolled it in his paw. “Pop!” It burst with sweet juice. He savored the taste. He felt excited to eat more, but he paused.

“Self-control,” he reminded himself. “One for now. Save some for later.” He chose two more to put in a small bowl for lunch. He covered the bowl and set it in the shade.

Mimi waved her carrot. “Thank you for sharing,” she said. “I will bring you mint leaves later.”

Benny beamed. “We can trade and plan together!” he said.

They both looked at their tiny gardens. The leaves rustled softly. The air smelled like sunshine and soil.

Benny stretched his paws. “Today I used my senses. I made a plan. I shared. I saved. I feel proud.”

Mimi nodded. “Me too.”

Benny patted the lemon tree. “Think first, then do,” he said. “It helps the garden grow. It helps kindness grow, too.”

The End.
