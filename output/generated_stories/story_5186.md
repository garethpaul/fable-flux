# Poppy the Patient Panda and the Gentle Goggles

In a quiet house, down squeaky stairs, there was a basement lab. The air felt cool on fur, and little lights blinked like sleepy stars. Bottles sat in rows, lids snug. Labels had simple pictures: a sun, a moon, a leaf, and a raindrop. A soft humming came from a tiny fan. It smelled like lemons and clean soap.

Poppy the patient panda loved the lab. Poppy wore round, gentle goggles. They were not too tight. They were just right. Poppy liked to build small helpful things. Poppy liked to test and try, and wait and see. Poppy’s paws were careful. Poppy’s heart was kind.

“Good morning, lab,” Poppy said in a calm voice. “We will learn something new today.”

On the table sat a little toy robot named Dot. Dot had a bright yellow button and two cheerful eyes. Next to Dot was a puzzle box with spinning shapes and crinkly knobs.

Poppy patted Dot. “Today we will make the puzzle box open for everyone,” Poppy said. “Then more friends can play and learn.”

Dot blinked. “Beep. I like learning,” Dot said in a small, tinny voice.

Poppy nodded. “Me too.”

Poppy turned the first knob. It made a click. Turned the second knob. It made a squeak. Turned the third knob. It made no sound. The lid stayed shut.

Dot tilted its head. “Beep? Why no open?”

Poppy felt a little heat in the cheeks. It was frustration trying to grow. Poppy took a slow breath, in and out. “I will be patient,” Poppy said. “I can think. I can try again.”

Just then, a small figure stomped down the stairs. It was Riff, a little raccoon with a striped tail. Riff’s fur was ruffled. Riff’s paws were tight fists.

“I want it open now!” Riff shouted. The shout bounced off the pipes. “I want the lid open. Now, now, now!”

Dot flinched and shook a little. “Beep,” Dot said softly.

Poppy felt the heat again. Loud words can make feelings big. Poppy touched the goggles and made a choice. “I hear you, Riff,” Poppy said gently. “You really want it open. Waiting is hard.”

Riff sniffed. “It is very hard,” Riff said. “My tummy feels twisty.”

“Let’s help your tummy,” Poppy said. “We can breathe like turtles. Slow and small.” Poppy showed a breath in, and a breath out. Riff copied. In. Out. In. Out. The stomp in Riff’s feet became a sway.

“My tummy is softer now,” Riff said. “But the lid is still shut.”

Poppy smiled. “We will use knowledge. We will use kind hands. We will use calm feelings.” Poppy pulled a simple picture chart closer. It showed steps: Look. Think. Try. Check. Help.

“First, we look,” Poppy said. Poppy peered at the puzzle box. “I see pictures by the knobs. A sun, a leaf, and a raindrop.”

“I see them too,” Riff said. “The sun knob is happy to click.”

Dot wiggled. “Beep. The leaf squeaks.”

“The raindrop is quiet,” Poppy said. “Maybe the quiet knob is stuck.”

“Second, we think,” Poppy said. “What helps a stuck thing?”

“Oil?” Riff asked.

“Sometimes. Or a wiggle,” Poppy said. “Or asking for help.”

“Third, we try,” Poppy said. Poppy got a tiny drop of safe, lemon oil. The smell was fresh and bright. Poppy dabbed the raindrop knob. “We will wait.” Poppy counted, “One… two… three…”

Riff bounced. “Is it ready now?”

Poppy nodded. “We can try a gentle wiggle.” Poppy’s paws wiggled the knob left, then right, not too fast.

Click.

Dot beeped big. “Beep beep!”

“Fourth, we check,” Poppy said. Poppy turned the sun knob to the little sun picture. Turned the leaf knob to the leaf picture. Turned the raindrop knob to the raindrop picture. The lid popped up with a pop like bubble wrap. Inside were shiny blocks and soft gears.

Riff’s eyes grew wide. “It opened!”

Poppy clapped softly. “You did turtle breaths. You watched. You learned. You helped.”

Riff looked down. “I yelled before,” Riff said. “I scared Dot.” Riff’s ears drooped.

Poppy knelt to Dot. “Dot, did you feel scared?”

Dot blinked twice. “Beep. Yes. The loud was big.”

Riff took another turtle breath. “Dot, I am sorry,” Riff said. “I was angry. Waiting hurt. I can use quiet words next time.”

Dot’s light turned warm. “Beep. Thank you.”

Poppy nodded. “That is empathy,” Poppy said. “Empathy is feeling with someone. We listen. We care. When we care, we choose gentle.”

Riff touched the puzzle box. “I want to learn the steps again,” Riff said.

Poppy pointed to the chart. “Look. Think. Try. Check. Help,” Poppy said slowly. “And breathe.”

Riff grinned. “I can do it,” Riff said. Riff turned the knobs to close the lid, then opened it again, slowly, kindly. “I did it!”

“You used your own paws,” Poppy said. “That is self-reliance.”

They built a small ramp with the shiny blocks. Dot rolled down and said, “Wee-beep!” The fan hummed, the lights blinked, and the lemon smell made the room feel sunny.

Riff looked at Poppy. “When I feel mad, I can breathe, talk, and ask for help,” Riff said. “I can care about how others feel.”

Poppy smiled. “That is our big lesson,” Poppy said. “Empathy makes our lab gentle. Knowledge helps us solve problems. Patience helps us try again.”

They cleaned the table. Lids clicked closed. Poppy took off the gentle goggles and patted the chart.

“Thank you, lab,” Poppy said. “We learned today.”

Riff waved to Dot. “Bye, Dot. I will use turtle breaths.”

Dot waved a tiny arm. “Beep. Friends.”

The End.
