# Mimi Shares the Music

Mimi was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. She loved music. She loved the tick-tock of the metronome, the tap-tap of drumsticks, and the hum-hum of the big bass. Every afternoon, Mimi scampered to the musical practice room to play.

The room was full of sounds and smells. Shiny drums stood in a circle. Smooth wooden maracas sat in a basket, smelling like warm pine. A bright red tambourine lay on a blue mat, jingling a little when the window breeze came in. On the wall were posters of children from many places, smiling and wearing colorful clothes. Some had saris. Some had kimonos. Some wore bright shirts with patterns. The posters made the room feel friendly.

One sunny day, Mimi found a sparkly triangle with a silver beater. “Ting-ting!” she sang as she hit it gently. The note rang like a star. “Oh, I love this triangle,” Mimi said, her whiskers wiggling.

Soon, friends came in. Tiko the turtle carried a small drum from his island. Aisha the bird wore a tiny scarf and brought a shaker egg. Benji the bunny bounced in with two wooden sticks. Hana the hedgehog rolled a recorder on the floor. “Hello!” they all said. “We are ready to make music!”

“Let’s play together!” Mimi squeaked. “I have the triangle. It makes a pretty sound.”

Aisha tilted her head. “May I try the triangle too?” she asked softly.

Mimi hugged the triangle close. “Um… not yet,” she said. “I found it first.” Her nose scrunched. She felt a tight squeeze in her tummy. She liked the triangle. She did not want to let it go.

Tiko tapped his drum. “In my island, we share the beat,” he said. “We take turns.”

Benji twirled his sticks. “Sharing is caring,” he added.

Mimi’s ears drooped. The tight feeling grew warmer. It felt like a little fire. “But I want it!” she squeaked. Her voice got loud. Her tail twitched and the beater slipped and clinked on the floor.

Aisha stepped back and fluffed her feathers. “That sound scared me,” she said. “Let’s be safe, friends.”

Hana nodded. “We can keep our voices calm. We can hold sticks and beaters with gentle hands.”

Mimi took a deep breath in. She smelled the clean wood of the maracas. She listened to the soft tick-tock of the metronome. In… out. In… out. The little fire in her tummy cooled. “I feel mad,” she said in a small voice. “I really like this sparkly triangle.”

Tiko smiled kindly. “Thank you for telling us,” he said. “That is good anger care.”

Aisha chirped, “We can make a plan. We can take turns.”

Benji pointed to the blue mat. “We can use the mat as our turn spot,” he said. “We can count to ten taps.”

Mimi looked at her friends. She looked at the posters on the wall. So many smiles. So many ways to make music. She remembered the teacher’s words: “We share to make the song strong.”

“Okay,” Mimi said. “Let’s be safe. Let’s be kind. Let’s share.” She picked up the beater carefully. “Aisha, you can have a turn after ten ting-ting taps. I will sit on the mat when I wait.”

Aisha’s eyes shone. “Thank you, Mimi!”

Mimi tapped the triangle: “Ting! Ting! Ting!” The sound was clear and bright. Everyone listened. After ten taps, Mimi stopped. She set the beater down gently. “Your turn, Aisha,” she said. “I will hold the maraca and shake softly while I wait.”

Aisha played: “Ting-ting-ting!” Tiko drummed a steady “dum-dum-dum.” Benji clicked “clack-clack.” Hana blew a sweet “toot-toot.” The room filled with a rainbow of sounds. They smiled and bobbed.

When Aisha finished ten taps, she gave the triangle to Benji. “Your turn!” she said. Benji laughed and shared it with Hana next. Everyone took turns. The music grew and grew.

Mimi felt light, like a feather. The tight feeling was gone. Sharing made the song bigger. Sharing made the room safer and calmer. She liked this feeling.

Then the beater rolled near the edge of the table. “Careful!” Hana said. Mimi reached out slowly and stopped it with her paw. “Safe hands,” Mimi whispered. She put it back on the mat. “We keep our tools in a safe place.”

They played a final song together. Tiko began, “dum-dum.” Aisha followed, “shake-shake.” Benji clicked, “clack-clack.” Hana sang, “toot-toot.” Mimi added the star-bright “ting!” They counted together, “One, two, three, four,” and ended with a soft “ta-da!”

Everyone clapped. Mimi’s heart felt warm and wide. “I learned something,” she said. “When I feel mad, I can breathe in and out. I can use calm words. I can share and take turns. Sharing keeps us safe. Sharing makes music better.”

Aisha chirped, “And sharing makes friends happy.”

Tiko nodded. “We shared our sounds from many places,” he said. “Together, we made one song.”

Mimi smiled. “Let’s keep sharing, every day.”

The End.
