# Koko the Koala's Treasure Hunt

Koko the koala loved quiet places. Today, she climbed up to the old attic above Grandma's house. The wooden floor creaked softly under her fuzzy paws. Dust danced in the golden sunbeams that peeked through the small window.

"What a peaceful place," Koko whispered to herself, taking a deep, calm breath.

As Koko looked around, she saw something that made her gasp. Old boxes were scattered everywhere, and papers were spilling out onto the floor. Empty bottles rolled in corners, and plastic bags were tangled up like spider webs.

"Oh my," said Koko sadly. "This beautiful attic is all messy!"

Just then, she heard tiny voices coming from behind a dusty trunk.

"Help! Help!" squeaked the voices.

Koko moved slowly and carefully toward the sound. Behind the trunk, she found three little field mice trapped under a pile of plastic wrappers.

"Don't worry, little friends," Koko said gently. "I'll help you."

With her careful paws, Koko lifted each wrapper one by one. The mice wiggled free and brushed off their whiskers.

"Thank you, Koko!" said the smallest mouse. "We're Pip, Pop, and Pep. We live up here, but all this trash makes it hard to find our way home."

Koko sat down and thought quietly. She watched how the sunlight made everything look golden and beautiful, even the messy attic. Then she had an idea.

"Would you like to help me clean up our attic?" asked Koko. "If we work together, we can make it beautiful again."

"Yes!" chirped all three mice. "But how?"

Koko smiled. "We'll sort everything into different piles. Papers here, bottles there, and plastic over here. Then we can decide what to keep, what to recycle, and what to give away."

The four friends began their work. Koko picked up the bigger items with her strong paws, while the mice scurried around collecting small pieces. They worked slowly and mindfully, making sure not to miss anything.

"Look!" said Pip, holding up a shiny button. "This could be useful!"

"And these newspapers can be recycled," added Pop, dragging a stack with his tiny paws.

As they worked, Pep found an old photo album. "These pictures are beautiful! Someone will want to keep these."

After an hour of careful work, the attic looked completely different. Everything was sorted into neat piles. The floor sparkled in the sunlight, and fresh air flowed through the window.

"Wow!" said all the mice together. "It's like a treasure room now!"

Koko felt warm and happy inside. "When we take care of our spaces, we take care of each other too," she said wisely.

The mice built a cozy new nest in a clean corner, using soft fabric scraps they had found. Koko made a reading spot by the window with some old cushions.

"Thank you for teaching us about conservation," said Pip. "Now we know how to keep our home clean and safe."

"And thank you for showing us that working together makes everything easier," added Pop.

Koko hugged her new friends gently. "Taking care of our world is everyone's job. When we're mindful about what we use and where we put things, we help all the creatures who share our spaces."

As the sun set, the attic glowed with golden light. Koko and her mouse friends had turned a messy space into a beautiful, organized home for everyone to enjoy.

The End.
