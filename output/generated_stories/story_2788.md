# Sparky's Clean-Up Adventure

In a cozy nursery filled with soft toys and colorful books, lived a special robot dog named Sparky. His shiny silver body gleamed in the morning sunlight, and his bright blue eyes twinkled like stars. Sparky loved helping children learn new things, especially how to stay clean and healthy.

One sunny morning, Sparky heard little Emma crying in her crib. "Woof, woof!" he barked cheerfully, rolling over on his four wheels. "Good morning, Emma! What's wrong?"

Emma sniffled and pointed to her hands. "Sparky, my hands are all sticky from my breakfast! And my face feels yucky too."

Sparky's tail wagged with excitement. His sensors detected that Emma needed to learn about staying clean. "Don't worry, Emma! I know exactly how to help you feel fresh and happy again. Let's go on a clean-up adventure!"

Emma giggled as Sparky gently lifted her from the crib with his soft, padded arms. They rolled together to the little sink in the nursery corner, where a step stool waited just for Emma.

"First," said Sparky, his voice warm and encouraging, "we need to wash our hands! Can you turn on the water? Make it nice and warm, but not too hot."

Emma reached for the faucet and turned it on. The water made a pleasant splashing sound as it flowed into the sink. "Like this, Sparky?"

"Perfect!" Sparky's eyes lit up with pride. "Now, let's put some soap on your hands. See how it makes bubbles?"

Emma squeezed the soap dispenser, and white, fluffy bubbles appeared on her palms. She rubbed her hands together, watching the bubbles grow bigger and bigger. "It tickles, Sparky! And it smells like flowers!"

"That's right! Now rub your hands together for twenty seconds. Let's count together: one, two, three..." Sparky counted along with Emma, making it fun by barking a little "woof" after each number.

After rinsing her hands with the warm water, Emma dried them on a soft, fluffy towel. "My hands feel so much better now!" she said, holding them up to show Sparky.

"Wonderful! But we're not done yet," Sparky said with a gentle beep. "Now let's wash your face to get rid of that sticky feeling."

Sparky helped Emma splash cool water on her face, then showed her how to pat it dry with a clean washcloth. "Gentle pats, just like this," he demonstrated with his soft paws.

Emma looked in the mirror and smiled. Her face was clean and rosy, and her hands smelled fresh and sweet. "I feel so much better, Sparky! Thank you for teaching me."

Sparky's circuits hummed with happiness. "You did such a good job, Emma! Remember, washing your hands and face helps keep germs away and makes you feel wonderful. It's one of the most important things you can do every day."

From that day on, Emma remembered Sparky's lesson. Every morning and after meals, she would wash her hands and face, thinking of her robot friend's encouraging words. She even taught her teddy bears how to pretend-wash their paws!

Sparky watched proudly as Emma grew stronger and healthier, knowing that good hygiene habits would help her feel her best every single day.

The End.
