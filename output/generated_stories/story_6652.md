# Pip's Big Sorry

Pip the mouse loved his cozy treehouse home. The wooden walls smelled like sweet oak, and sunlight danced through the round windows. Every morning, Pip would scurry up the rope ladder, his tiny paws patting against each wooden rung.

Today felt extra special because his friends were coming to play! Pip had built the most amazing block tower that reached almost to the ceiling. Red blocks, blue blocks, yellow blocks - all stacked up high like a rainbow mountain.

"Knock, knock!" called Bunny from outside.

"Come in, come in!" squeaked Pip excitedly.

Bunny hopped through the door, followed by Squirrel who bounded in with his fluffy tail swishing. Their eyes grew wide when they saw Pip's magnificent tower.

"Wow!" gasped Bunny. "Can we play with the blocks too?"

But Pip felt protective of his creation. "No, no! This is MY tower. You can't touch it!"

Squirrel's ears drooped. "But we want to build something too."

"Find your own blocks!" Pip said, crossing his tiny arms.

His friends looked sad, but Pip didn't notice. He was too busy admiring his tower. Then Bunny and Squirrel quietly sat in the corner, watching Pip play all by himself.

After a while, Pip started to feel lonely. Playing alone wasn't as fun as he thought. The treehouse felt too quiet without his friends' happy chatter and giggles.

"Um, Bunny? Squirrel?" Pip called softly. "What are you doing?"

"Just watching," Bunny said with a small voice. "We can't play with anything."

Pip's heart felt heavy like a stone. He looked at his friends' sad faces, then at his beautiful tower. Suddenly, his tower didn't seem as wonderful anymore.

"Oh no," whispered Pip. "I was being mean, wasn't I?"

He scurried over to his friends, his whiskers twitching nervously. "Bunny, Squirrel, I'm really, really sorry. I should have shared my blocks with you. That wasn't kind at all."

Bunny's ears perked up a little. "Do you really mean it?"

"Yes!" squeaked Pip. "I have an idea! What if we take turns? We can work together and make something even MORE amazing!"

Squirrel's tail started swishing with excitement. "What kind of something?"

Pip's eyes sparkled. "A block city! We can each build a different part. Bunny, you could make a park with green blocks. Squirrel, you could build a tall tree house. And I'll make roads to connect everything!"

"That sounds wonderful!" Bunny clapped her paws.

They carefully took down Pip's tower together, sharing every single block. Bunny built a beautiful park with green blocks arranged like bushes and trees. Squirrel constructed an amazing tree house with brown blocks for the trunk and green ones for leaves. Pip created winding roads that connected all their buildings.

"Look what we made together!" Pip squeaked with joy.

Their block city was much more colorful and interesting than anything Pip could have built alone. The treehouse filled with happy laughter as they played together, taking turns adding new buildings and making up stories about the tiny people who lived in their city.

As the sun set through the treehouse windows, painting everything golden, Pip learned something important. "Sharing and saying sorry makes everything better," he told his friends.

"And more fun too!" agreed Bunny and Squirrel.

From that day on, Pip always remembered to share and take turns. And whenever he made a mistake, he knew that a sincere "I'm sorry" could fix almost anything.

The End.
