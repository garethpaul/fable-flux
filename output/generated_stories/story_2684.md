# Benny Beaver's Moving Morning

Benny Beaver woke up with a big yawn and stretched his furry arms high above his head. The sun was shining bright through his dining room window, making golden patches dance on the wooden floor.

"What a beautiful morning!" Benny said, patting his round belly. "Time for breakfast!"

Benny loved eating pancakes at his big dining room table, but he had been sitting there every morning for weeks. His little beaver body felt stiff and sleepy, and his tail felt heavy when he tried to wag it.

Just as Benny was about to sit down with his stack of fluffy pancakes, he heard a gentle knock at his door.

"Help! Help!" called his friend Rosie Rabbit. "My garden gate is stuck, and I can't get to my carrots!"

Benny looked at his yummy pancakes, then at the door. His tummy rumbled loudly. "But I'm so hungry," he mumbled. "And I'm too tired to help right now."

"Please, Benny!" Rosie called again. "I really need your strong beaver teeth to fix it!"

Benny sighed and shuffled slowly to the door. When he tried to help Rosie, he felt out of breath after just a few minutes. His arms felt weak, and his legs were wobbly.

"Oh my," puffed Benny, sitting down heavily. "I'm so sorry, Rosie. I used to be strong, but now I feel like a soggy pancake!"

Rosie's soft ears drooped with worry. "Benny, when was the last time you moved your body and played outside?"

Benny thought hard, tapping his furry chin. "I can't remember! I've been sitting at my dining room table eating and reading every day."

"That's the problem!" Rosie said with a gentle smile. "Our bodies need to move and exercise to stay strong and healthy!"

Benny's eyes grew wide. "Really? But how do I start?"

"Let's begin right here in your dining room!" Rosie suggested. "We can make exercise fun!"

Rosie showed Benny how to march in place, making their feet go "stomp, stomp, stomp" on the wooden floor. Then they did jumping jacks, clapping their hands high above their heads. The dining room filled with happy sounds of movement.

"This feels wonderful!" Benny giggled, his tail starting to wag again. "My body feels tingly and alive!"

Next, they did silly animal stretches. Benny reached up like a tall giraffe, then crouched down like a tiny mouse. They pretended to swim like fish around the dining room table.

After ten minutes of moving, Benny felt amazing. His cheeks were rosy pink, and his heart was beating happily in his chest.

"Now I feel strong enough to help you!" Benny said proudly.

Together, they marched outside where Benny easily fixed Rosie's garden gate with his strong teeth. His body felt powerful and ready for anything!

"Thank you for teaching me about exercise, Rosie," Benny said. "And I'm sorry I almost didn't help you. From now on, I'll start every morning with moving time before breakfast!"

"That's a wonderful plan!" Rosie cheered.

From that day forward, Benny began each morning with fun exercises in his dining room. He would march, stretch, and dance before eating his pancakes. His body grew stronger, his mind felt clearer, and he always had energy to help his friends.

"Exercise makes everything better!" Benny would say, doing a happy little dance around his dining room table.

The End.
