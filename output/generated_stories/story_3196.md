# Pebble the Penguin’s Polite Picnic

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a shiny black-and-white belly. One sunny afternoon, Pebble waddled into the dining room. The room smelled like warm bread and sweet berries. The wooden table felt smooth under Pebble’s flippers. Plates gleamed like little moons.

“Woo-hoo! Snack time!” Pebble chirped. His tummy made a happy rumble.

On the table were bowls of blueberries, crunchy carrot sticks, and tiny sandwiches shaped like stars. Pebble’s friends, Tiko the turtle and Bella the bunny, joined him. Tiko’s shell made a soft tap-tap sound on the floor. Bella’s whiskers twitched with joy.

“I’m hungry!” Bella giggled.

“Me too,” said Tiko, slow and kind.

Pebble hopped up on his chair. His feathers fluffed with excitement. He reached for the berries. “I will take the biggest bowl!” he shouted.

Bella’s ears drooped. Tiko blinked. Pebble scooped and scooped. Blueberries popped and rolled. Plop! Plop! A few bounced onto the tablecloth.

“Pebble,” said Tiko gently, “we are sharing.”

“I want lots!” Pebble said. He stuffed a sandwich in his beak. Crunch! Crumbs sprinkled like snow.

Bella looked at the messy table. “I feel sad,” she whispered. “There are no berries left for me.”

Pebble paused. He looked at Bella’s small, empty plate. He heard his loud crunching. He saw the crumbs on his friends’ spots. The dining room felt a little too quiet now.

Pebble’s cheeks felt warm. “Oh,” he said softly. “Did I forget to be kind?”

Tiko nodded slowly. “We can show respect,” he said. “Respect means we care about each other. We wait our turn. We keep the table tidy. We use gentle voices.”

Bella added, “We also ask, ‘Would you like some?’ That makes hearts feel happy.”

Pebble took a deep breath. He wanted to fix it. He wanted to learn. He wanted to show respect.

“I’m sorry,” Pebble said. His voice was small but brave. “I forgot to share. I forgot to use my gentle voice. I can do better.”

Tiko smiled. “We can try a plan!”

Bella clapped softly. “A teamwork plan!”

Pebble’s eyes shined. “A plan means we use our knowledge,” he said. “We think and we remember.”

They made three simple rules:
- Gentle hands, gentle voices.
- Take turns and count to three.
- Keep the table neat and help each other.

Pebble nodded. “I can do that!” He picked up a napkin. Swipe, swipe. He wiped the crumbs. The tablecloth felt clean again. He rolled the blueberries back into the bowl. Plip… plip… plip. He set the bowl in the middle.

“Bella, would you like some berries?” Pebble asked.

“Yes, please,” Bella said. Her ears perked up.

“Let’s count to three,” said Tiko. “One… two… three.” Pebble took a small scoop. Bella took a small scoop. Tiko took a small scoop. The bowl still had plenty. Pebble felt proud. His heart felt light, like a bubble.

They reached for the carrot sticks next. Pebble moved slowly, using good coordination. He kept his flippers steady. “I will wait my turn,” he said. Tap-tap went Tiko’s shell. Swish-swish went Bella’s whiskers. Crunch, crunch went the carrots, but in gentle bites.

“I like our plan,” Bella said. “It shows respect for others.”

“It helps us all,” Tiko added. “We used our knowledge to solve the problem.”

Pebble smiled. “And we used our bodies with care,” he said, sitting tall. “That’s coordination!”

Soon they made tiny sandwich towers. Pebble placed star sandwiches carefully, one on top of another. “Steady,” he whispered. His flippers stayed still. He giggled when the tower wobbled and then stood strong. Bella clapped. Tiko cheered, “Well done!”

After they ate, Pebble passed the napkins. “Let’s clean together,” he said. Swish, wipe, pat. The dining room smelled fresh again. The plates were shiny. The table looked happy.

Bella hopped in a little circle. “Thank you, Pebble,” she said. “You listened. You waited. You asked.”

Pebble’s eyes sparkled. “Thank you for teaching me,” he said. “Respect makes snack time better.”

Tiko nodded. “Respect helps friends feel safe and glad.”

Pebble placed his flipper over his heart. “I will remember: gentle hands, gentle voices, take turns, keep it neat.”

The three friends shared a soft group hug. Pebble felt warm and proud. The dining room was full of smiles. The afternoon light made golden dots on the floor. Pebble’s tummy was full, and so was his heart.

Pebble chirped, “Today I learned a big thing: When we respect others, we all win!”

The End.
