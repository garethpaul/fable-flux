# Gigi the Giraffe's Paper Problem

Gigi the giraffe loved working in her cozy home office. She had a big wooden desk that was just the right height for her long neck, and colorful pictures from around the world covered her walls. There were photos of elephants from Africa, pandas from China, and butterflies from Brazil.

One sunny morning, Gigi sat at her computer, typing with her hooves. *Click-clack, click-clack* went the keyboard. She was writing letters to children all over the world, sharing stories about animals and nature.

"Oh dear," Gigi sighed, looking around her office. Paper was scattered everywhere! Used sheets covered the floor like a white carpet. Empty boxes that once held paper towered in the corner like cardboard mountains.

Gigi's friend Benny the bunny hopped through the door. "Wow, Gigi! What happened here?" he asked, his pink nose twitching as he looked around.

"I've been so busy writing letters that I forgot to be careful with paper," Gigi admitted, her cheeks turning pink. "Look at all this waste! Trees had to be cut down to make all this paper, and I just threw it away."

Benny's ears drooped. "That doesn't sound very good for the forests where our animal friends live."

Gigi nodded sadly. "You're right, Benny. In the letters children send me, they tell me about the beautiful trees in their countries. Maria from Mexico wrote about colorful trees with singing birds. Kenji from Japan told me about cherry blossom trees that look like pink clouds. I should take better care of the paper that comes from trees!"

"Maybe we can fix this together," Benny suggested, his ears perking up again.

Gigi's brown eyes sparkled with an idea. "Yes! Let me think..." She tapped her chin with her hoof. "First, I'll follow the three R's my friend taught me: Reduce, Reuse, and Recycle!"

Gigi picked up a piece of paper that was only written on one side. "Look, Benny! I can use the back of this paper. That's reusing!"

"Great idea!" Benny cheered, clapping his small paws.

Next, Gigi gathered all the completely used papers. "These can go in the blue recycling bin. They'll be made into new paper!" The papers made a satisfying *rustle* as she sorted them.

"And from now on," Gigi announced, "I'll only print what I really need. That's reducing!"

Benny helped Gigi organize her office. They made neat piles and put the recycling bin right next to her desk where she could see it.

"You know what, Benny?" Gigi said, looking at her clean office. "I want to write a special letter to all my pen pals around the world. I'll tell them how we can all save trees together!"

Gigi sat down and carefully wrote on the back of used paper: "Dear Friends Around the World, today I learned that taking care of paper means taking care of trees. Trees are homes for animals and give us fresh air to breathe. Let's all use paper wisely!"

"Perfect!" said Benny. "You're helping children everywhere learn about conservation."

Gigi smiled, feeling proud and happy. Her office was tidy, and she had learned an important lesson. From that day on, she always remembered to reduce, reuse, and recycle. Every time she saved paper, she thought about the tall trees swaying in the wind and all the animals who called them home.

The End.
