# Nutmeg's Quiet Plan

In a small, sunny study corner, a resourceful squirrel named Nutmeg kept her pencils in a cup and her books in a neat stack. The study corner smelled like fresh paper and warm wood. Sunlight made tiny dust sparkles dance in the air.

Today, Nutmeg wanted to finish her picture book about trees. She wiggled her tail and opened to a clean page. “I can do this,” she whispered. “Slow and steady.”

Tap-tap-tap! Her best friend, Pip the chipmunk, peeked in. “Nutmeg! Let’s play tag!” Pip bounced on his toes. His cheeks puffed with excitement.

Nutmeg felt a wiggle of wanting to run. Her paws tingled. Her heart did a happy thump-thump. But she looked at her book. “I want to play,” she said softly, “but I need to make my tree picture first. I made a plan.”

“A plan?” Pip’s eyes grew round.

Nutmeg nodded. She pulled out a small card. On it she had drawn three boxes. Box one said: Breathe. Box two said: Draw. Box three said: Snack and Play.

“First, I breathe to calm my body,” Nutmeg said. She put one paw on her chest and one paw on her belly. “In and out… like the wind in the leaves.” She breathed in through her nose. She breathed out slowly. The room felt quiet and safe. Her shoulders softened.

Pip copied her. “In… out…” he whispered. His whiskers twitched. “That feels nice.”

“Second,” Nutmeg went on, “I draw five leaves on my tree. Just five. Then I rest my eyes.” She smiled at Pip. “Third, we have a snack. A crunchy, healthy one. Then we can play.”

Pip looked at the sunny window. “Okay. I can wait,” he said. “Let’s do your plan together. Teamwork!”

Nutmeg’s tummy made a tiny growl. She remembered self-care. “Before we start, let’s drink some water,” she said. “It helps my brain think.” She poured two little cups. The water was cool and smooth. Pip giggled when a drop tickled his chin.

They sat at the small desk. Nutmeg picked a green crayon. Scritch-scratch, scritch-scratch, the crayon made gentle sounds. One leaf, two leaves. Pip counted softly, “Three, four…” The paper smelled like crayons and pine.

At leaf five, Nutmeg put down the crayon. She closed her eyes and rubbed her paws together like she was warming them. “Eye rest,” she said. “I see colors on the inside!” Little swirls of green danced behind her eyelids.

Pip did it too. “I see purple swirls!”

But just then, outside the door, they heard laughter. “Tag! Tag!” The other squirrels were playing in the hallway. Nutmeg’s ears perked up. Her tail twitched hard. Her body wanted to jump. Her paws started to bounce.

“My body is wiggly,” she said. “I feel it. I want to run.”

Pip bounced too. “Me too!”

Nutmeg placed her paws flat on the desk. “Let’s use our calm tools,” she said. “Breathe like leaves. Squeeze, then ease.” She made fists and let them go. Squeeze… ease… Squeeze… ease… Her paws felt warm and soft. Her heart slowed to a gentle thump.

Pip copied her. “Squeeze… ease… That helps!”

Nutmeg peeked at her plan. “We did five leaves. We rested eyes. Now it’s snack time.” Her eyes shone. “Healthy snack gives steady energy.”

She opened her snack box. Inside were crunchy carrot coins, a tiny apple, and three little nuts. The apple smelled sweet. The carrots snapped when they bit. Crunch, crunch. “Yum,” said Pip. “This snack is strong fuel.”

Nutmeg chewed slowly. “Chew, chew,” she said. “My belly says thank you.” She offered Pip a carrot coin. “Teamwork means sharing.”

They sipped more water. Glug, glug. The cool water made their mouths feel fresh.

Nutmeg looked at her book again. “I will draw five more leaves,” she said. “Then I’m done.” She picked up the green crayon. Scritch-scratch. Pip cheered quietly, “Go, Nutmeg, go!”

When the last leaf was done, Nutmeg added a tiny brown bird on a branch. She smiled so big her cheeks felt round and warm. “I did it. I followed my plan,” she said. “I kept my body calm and my brain focused.”

Pip clapped. “You did it! And you took care of you. Water, snack, breathe!”

Nutmeg closed the book gently. “Now it’s play time!” She and Pip stood up. They stretched tall like trees. Stretch… tall… sway… The room smelled like apples and crayons.

They ran to the hallway. “Tag!” Pip laughed. Their feet pattered on the floor. Pat-pat-pat. Nutmeg’s tail swished happily. Her chest felt light. She had time for work and time for play.

Later, back in the study corner, Nutmeg taped her tree picture on the wall. The leaves looked calm and bright. “When I feel wiggly,” she said, “I can use my plan. Breathe. Do a little bit. Take care of my body. Then have a healthy snack and play. That’s self-regulation.”

Pip nodded. “And teamwork helps, too.”

Nutmeg touched the picture. “Slow and steady wins in a friendly way. I can be kind to my body and my mind.”

They smiled, feeling proud and peaceful in the sunny study corner.

The End.
