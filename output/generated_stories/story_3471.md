# Puddles the Penguin and the Fair Share Splash

Puddles was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a wiggly walk. He loved bath time. The bathroom was warm and steamy, and bubbles smelled like sweet soap. The shiny tiles felt cool under his webbed feet. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet. Swish, swish went Puddles’ tail as he waddled in.

“Bath time! Splish-splash!” Puddles sang. He hopped into the tub with a gentle plop. The water hugged him. “Ahh,” he sighed. He liked the soft washcloth, the foamy bubbles, and the rubber duck that squeaked.

Squeak! went the duck.

“Hello, Ducky!” Puddles said, making the duck bob on the water. “Let’s make a bubble mountain!”

The door creaked. In waddled two neighbor bath buddies from the hallway: Tippy the tiny turtle and Bongo the bouncy bunny. They came for their turn after Puddles. They waited on a fluffy blue rug. The rug felt squishy like clouds.

“We’re next,” said Tippy in a small, careful voice. “We need warm water too.”

Bongo hopped in place. Thump, thump. “I want bubble hats!” he giggled.

Puddles puffed up with joy. “I love bubble hats!” He scooped a big, big pile of bubbles. He piled them high on his head. The bubbles made a silly crown.

But the water kept running. Glug, glug. The tub was almost full, and the warm water swirled down the drain. The bathroom mirror fogged up like a white cloud. Tippy and Bongo watched with wide eyes.

“Um,” Tippy said softly. “If the water is all used now, there won’t be any left for us. That does not feel fair.”

Puddles paused. His flippers slowed. He heard the drip of the faucet and the hush of the fan. He looked at the almost-empty warm-water knob. He saw Tippy’s worried eyes and Bongo’s wiggly feet stop hopping.

Puddles felt a little pinch in his tummy. “Oh,” he said. “I got very excited. I didn’t think about turns. That does not feel fair to you.”

Bongo nodded. “I want bubble hats too,” he whispered.

Puddles took a deep breath. “Justice means fair,” he said, remembering a poster in the hallway with a big heart and big letters. “Fair means everyone gets a turn. Fair means we share the good things.”

He put his flippers on the tub edge. “I will fix this,” he said. “We can make it fair.”

“How?” asked Tippy.

Puddles thought. He tapped his beak. Tap, tap. Then he smiled. “We can use a timer and share water and bubbles. We can take turns. We can save warm water for you.”

He reached for the faucet and turned it off. Click. The drip stopped. He scooped half the bubble mountain into a small bucket. Swish. “This half is for you,” he told Tippy. “Justice means I don’t take it all.”

Bongo’s ears perked up. “We can help!” he said.

“Yes,” said Puddles. “We can all help. That is cooperation.”

Puddles carefully climbed out of the tub. Plop, squish went his feet on the mat. He wrapped himself in a soft towel. “I had a turn,” he said kindly. “Now you have a turn.”

Tippy smiled. “Thank you, Puddles.” His tiny shell gleamed under the bathroom light. He slid into the tub with a small splash. Ploop!

Puddles turned the warm water on for just a little bit. He counted slowly. “One… two… three.” He turned it off. “Now the water is saved. That is civic responsibility,” he said proudly. “We care for the things we all use.”

Bongo clapped. “Bubble hats for all!” He dipped the bucket and placed a soft bubble hat on Tippy’s head. Squeak went the duck, like it approved.

Puddles set a little sand timer on the sink. The sand whispered down. “When the sand is done,” Puddles said, “it’s Bongo’s turn. Then, if we still have time and warm water, I can have a tiny turn too.”

The bathroom filled with happy sounds. Splash, giggle, swish. Tippy made a bubble beard. Bongo made a bubble bow tie. Puddles danced a little penguin wiggle on the rug. His feet went tap-tap, slide-slide. That was movement and fun without taking more water.

Soon the sand ran out. “My turn!” said Bongo. He hopped in, splash! Tippy scrubbed gently, then climbed out and dried with a hug-soft towel. Puddles helped by passing soap and singing a scrub song.

“Scrub, scrub, scrub,
We care and we share.
We move, we help,
We show we are fair!”

When Bongo’s turn was done, a small bit of time and warm water were left. Puddles took a tiny, quick rinse—just enough to feel fresh. He did not take more bubbles. He smiled at his friends. They all felt clean, warm, and happy.

Tippy patted Puddles’ flipper. “That was fair,” he said. “You made it right.”

Bongo nodded and wiggled his nose. “Justice feels kind,” he said.

Puddles’ heart felt big and bright. “Justice means we make things fair,” he said. “Compassion means we care about how others feel. Cooperation means we work together. Civic responsibility means we take care of shared things, like water and time.”

They hung up towels neatly. They wiped little splashes from the floor with a soft cloth. Swish, swish. The mirror cleared, and they could see three happy faces.

Puddles giggled. “And movement,” he said, doing one last penguin wiggle, “keeps bath time fun!”

They all laughed. The bathroom felt calm, clean, and fair.

The End.
