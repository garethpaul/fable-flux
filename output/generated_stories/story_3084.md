# Polly's Colorful Kindness

Deep inside a cozy closet, behind the winter coats and fuzzy sweaters, lived a bright green parrot named Polly. She had made the perfect hideaway with soft pillows, shiny buttons, and colorful ribbons she had collected.

"Squawk! This is MY special place!" Polly said, fluffing her beautiful feathers.

One rainy Tuesday, Polly heard tiny sniffles coming from outside her closet door.

"Boo-hoo-hoo," cried a small voice.

Polly peeked through the crack. Little mouse Max sat on the floor, tears rolling down his whiskers. His tiny paws held broken crayons.

"Go away!" Polly squawked loudly. "This is MY hideaway!"

Max jumped and scurried behind a shoe. "Sorry," he squeaked. "I just wanted to color, but my crayons broke."

Polly felt her chest get hot and tight. She didn't want anyone in her special space! She flapped her wings hard.

"Find somewhere else!" she screeched.

Max's little shoulders shook as he cried harder. The sound made Polly's tummy feel funny and twisty.

Polly sat in her closet, surrounded by all her pretty things. But something felt wrong. The ribbons didn't seem as sparkly. The buttons didn't shine as bright.

She remembered when Grandma Bird taught her: "When someone is sad, kindness makes everything better."

Polly took three deep breaths, just like Grandma showed her. In... out... in... out... in... out.

Her angry feelings started to melt away like ice cream in sunshine.

"Wait!" Polly called softly.

Max peeked out from behind the shoe, his tiny nose twitching.

"I have crayons in my hideaway," Polly said gently. "Would you like to use them?"

Max's eyes grew wide and sparkly. "Really? You would share with me?"

Polly nodded. "Come see my special place."

Max tiptoed into the closet. His mouth made a perfect "O" shape.

"Wow! It's like a rainbow cave!" Max whispered.

Polly felt warm and happy inside. "Here are my crayons. They're all the colors!"

"Thank you, Polly! You're so kind!" Max said, carefully taking a blue crayon.

They colored together on big pieces of paper. Max drew his family. Polly drew beautiful flowers.

"This is fun!" giggled Max. "Could we make pictures for the other animals?"

Polly's eyes lit up. "Yes! Let's make happy pictures for everyone!"

They worked together all afternoon. Polly used her creativity to think of wonderful ideas. Max helped organize the crayons by color.

When they finished, they had made twenty beautiful pictures!

"Let's give these to our friends," said Polly.

They walked through the house together. They gave a sunshine picture to sad Bunny Betty. They gave a flower picture to lonely Cat Charlie.

Each animal smiled big smiles when they received their gifts.

"You made my day better!" said Bunny Betty.

"This is the prettiest flower ever!" purred Cat Charlie.

That evening, Polly and Max sat in the closet hideaway, surrounded by thank-you hugs and new friends.

"Sharing made everything more fun," said Polly, her green feathers glowing in the soft light.

"And being kind made my sad feelings go away," added Max.

Polly learned that when we feel frustrated or angry, taking deep breaths helps us feel better. She also learned that sharing and being kind makes our hearts feel warm and happy.

From that day on, Polly's closet hideaway became a place where all the animals could come to create, share, and help each other.

"The best hideaway," Polly would say, "is one filled with friends and kindness."

The End.
