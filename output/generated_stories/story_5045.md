# Ruby Rabbit’s Kitchen Plan

In a warm kitchen that smelled like cinnamon and sunshine, a determined rabbit named Ruby stood on a little stool. Her soft ears wiggled with focus. The kettle hummed. The floor felt cozy on her paws. “Today,” Ruby said, “I make a healthy snack all by myself!”

Her tummy gave a tiny rumble. “Grumble, grumble,” Ruby giggled. She peeked into a bright blue bowl. Inside were carrots, apples, and leafy greens. “Colorful foods help me hop and think,” she said. “That’s my healthy habit.”

Just then, a tiny squeak came from the window. It was Pip the field mouse. “Hi, Ruby! Can I watch?” Pip asked, eyes shiny like little buttons.

“Sure!” said Ruby. “But no sneaking candy.” She pointed to a jar of chewy sweets on the high shelf. “We save those for special times.”

Pip nodded, but the candy sparkled in the sun like tiny stars. “They look so yummy,” Pip whispered.

Ruby washed her paws with warm, soapy water. “Swoosh, swish.” She dried them on a soft towel. “Clean paws first. That’s fair and safe,” she said.

“Fair?” Pip asked.

“Justice means fair,” Ruby explained gently. “We follow rules that help everyone feel good and safe. Clean paws for cooking is a fair rule.”

Ruby set a timer that went tick, tick, tick. “We will chop, mix, and move our bodies while we wait,” she said. “Movement keeps us strong!”

She brought out a little plastic knife and a cutting board. “I use safe tools,” Ruby said. “And I cut slowly.”

Chop, chop, chop went the carrots. Snip, snip went the greens. The apples smelled sweet, like a hug. Pip’s whiskers twitched. “Mmm!”

Ruby paused. “Let’s do wiggle jumps! Ten hops!” She hopped: boing, boing, boing! Pip giggled and hopped too. Their ears swished. Their feet pat-patted the warm floor. “Moving is part of healthy habits,” Ruby said, smiling.

When they returned to the bowl, the candy jar seemed even shinier. Pip climbed onto a spoon to peek. “Maybe just one?” he asked, reaching.

Ruby’s ears stood tall. “Wait, Pip,” she said calmly. “Taking candy before snack time is not fair. Our bodies need fuel that helps us grow. Candy is sometimes food.”

Pip sat on the spoon and swung his little feet. “But I feel sad,” he said softly. “I wanted sweet.”

Ruby nodded. “I hear your feelings.” She patted his tiny paw. “We can make sweet and healthy. Let’s imagine we are chefs in a rainbow café!”

Pip’s eyes lit up. “A rainbow café?”

Ruby closed her eyes and whispered, “I am Chef Ruby, with a tall hat. You are Chef Pip, with a tiny hat.” Pip giggled. “What is our plan, Chef?”

“Strategy time,” said Ruby. She tapped her chin. “Plan step 1: Make a sweet dip with yogurt and a little honey. Plan step 2: Make crunchy dippers with carrots and apples. Plan step 3: Dance while the flavors mix!”

Pip clapped. “A plan! That is smart.”

Ruby measured yogurt into a yellow bowl. Plop! She added a tiny drip of honey. Drip, drop. She stirred with smooth circles. Swirl, swirl. The mix smelled creamy and soft.

Pip washed his paws too. “Swoosh, swish,” he sang. “Clean is fair!”

Together they arranged carrot sticks and apple slices like sun rays. Ruby sprinkled a pinch of cinnamon. The warm spice tickled their noses. “Ahh,” said Pip.

Timer ticked. “Movement break!” Ruby cheered. They marched around the table. March, march. They stretched like tall trees. They wiggled like noodles. They breathed in and out. “In… out…” The kitchen felt happy.

Pip glanced at the candy jar again and then looked at the rainbow plate. “Our snack looks pretty,” he said.

“It smells good and feels good,” Ruby added. “Foods with colors help our hearts and brains. That’s justice to our bodies—fair care!”

They dipped. Crunch! Slurp! Pip’s eyes went wide. “It is sweet!” he squeaked. “And crunchy!”

Ruby nodded. “Sweet can be healthy when we plan.”

Just then, the front door creaked. Mama Rabbit peeked in. “I heard chopping and giggles,” she said. “What are my chefs making?”

Ruby stood tall. “We made a healthy snack with a fair plan. We washed paws, used safe tools, moved our bodies, and chose colorful foods. We used strategy!”

Mama smiled. “I am proud. You solved a problem with kindness and fairness.”

Pip looked at the candy jar and then at Mama. “We saved candy for another day,” he said. “We chose food that helps us hop.”

Mama nodded. “That is a just choice—for your bodies and your feelings.”

They shared the snack together. The yogurt was cool. The apples were crisp. The carrots sang, crunch-crunch. The warm kitchen hummed. Ruby felt strong and calm inside.

Pip whispered, “Ruby, when I wanted candy, you listened and helped. That felt fair.”

Ruby squeezed his paw. “We can want things and still make a good plan.”

After the last dip, Ruby wiped the table. Swish, swish. Pip put the bowls in the sink. Clink. They did one more happy hop. Boing!

Ruby said, “Today I learned: healthy habits mean clean hands, colorful foods, and moving our bodies. Justice means fair choices for everyone, even our tummies. Strategy means making a plan.”

Pip nodded. “And imagination makes it fun!”

They smiled at the warm, sunny kitchen. Their hearts felt light and brave.

The End.
