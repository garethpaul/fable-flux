# Leo’s Clever Art Room Adventure

Leo was a brave little lion cub with bright, curious eyes and soft, golden fur. Today, Leo padded into the creative art room. The room smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Sunlight danced on colorful paints. Brushes stood like tiny trees. Paper rustled like leaves. Leo’s whiskers wiggled with excitement.

“I want to make art that helps me feel strong and kind,” Leo said. “I want to help the Earth, too!”

Leo’s friends were there. Tia the turtle tapped a paintbrush. Pip the parrot fluffed his green feathers. Zuzu the zebra bounced in place. “Let’s make posters for a Wellness Play Day!” piped Pip. “We can show how to move our bodies, care for our planet, and be calm.”

Leo smiled. “Yes! We can make a poster, a game, and a quiet corner.”

But then Leo saw a problem. The art room was messy. Paper scraps covered the floor. Caps were off markers. A water cup was tipped, and blue water puddled like a tiny pond.

“Oh no,” said Leo. “We can’t make healthy art in a messy room. Someone might slip. The brushes might dry out. And we are wasting water!”

Leo took a deep breath. “Brave cubs solve problems,” he whispered. “We can fix this.”

Step 1: Clean and Care
Leo lifted the marker caps and clicked them on. “Markers need their caps to stay healthy,” he said. Tia wiped the blue puddle with a rag. “We can wash and reuse this cloth,” she said with a smile. Zuzu picked up paper scraps. “Let’s sort them!” Zuzu said. “Big pieces for new art. Tiny bits for a recycle bin.” Pip opened a window a little. Fresh air floated in. It smelled like rain and leaves.

Leo’s paws felt steady. His heart felt calm. “Cleaning up keeps us safe,” he said. “This is wellness!”

Step 2: Make Earth-Friendly Supplies
Leo asked, “Can we make our own glue?” Tia nodded. “We can mix flour and warm water.” They stirred, swish-swish, until it was smooth. “Sticky!” giggled Zuzu. “And safe,” said Pip. Leo rinsed the paint cups and saved the water for plants. He patted the soil of a little art-room fern. “Sip, sip,” he said. The fern looked happy.

Step 3: Move Our Bodies
“Let’s make a sports poster,” said Leo. He drew a big sun with yellow chalk. “The sun reminds us to play outside.” Zuzu drew a soccer ball—circle, circle, black and white. Pip added a jump rope—swish, swish. Tia painted a little running track.

They tried the moves in the room. “Run in place!” called Pip. Feet pattered. “Big stretch!” said Zuzu. Arms reached high. “Lion breaths,” Leo said. “In through the nose, out with a soft roar.” They all breathed together. In, out. Calm and strong.

Step 4: Quiet Corner
Leo made a cozy corner with a soft mat and a pillow. He placed a smooth pebble there. “This is the Calm Pebble,” he said. “When we feel wiggly, we can hold it and breathe.” The room felt peaceful.

But then, another problem! The lights flickered. Buzz, buzz. “What if the lights go out?” Tia asked. “We won’t see our art,” Zuzu said.

Leo blinked, then grinned. “We can use the sun!” He pulled the tables closer to the window. The sunlight made the paints sparkle. Pip hung a shiny foil circle to bounce the light. “Reflect, reflect!” chirped Pip. Warm, bright light filled the room. “That’s innovation,” said Leo proudly. “Using what we have in a smart way.”

Step 5: The Big Share
They finished their posters:
- “Move Every Day!” with running, jumping, and stretching.
- “Care for Earth!” with recycle bins, water-saving tips, and a happy fern.
- “Feel Calm!” with lion breaths and the Calm Pebble.

“Let’s invite everyone,” said Leo. Friends came to see. Monkey clapped. Bunny wiggled her nose. “What do we do first?” asked Gigi the giraffe.

Leo led them in a tiny sports parade around the room. “Knees up, knees down!” he called. Feet went tap-tap on the floor. Then they made art from saved scraps—soft, crinkly, colorful. They cleaned as they went. Finally, they sat in the quiet corner and did lion breaths together. In, out. In, out. The room felt gentle, like a hug.

Gigi smiled. “I feel strong in my body, kind to the Earth, and calm in my heart.”

Leo’s tail swished. “That’s wellness,” he said. “We move our bodies, care for our home, and care for our feelings.”

The teacher peeked in. “I see a clean room, bright art, and happy friends,” she said. “You solved problems with care and clever ideas.”

Leo stood tall. His brave heart felt warm. “We can keep doing this every day,” he said. “Clean up, save water, use sunlight, move our bodies, and breathe.”

Everyone cheered. The art room glowed with color, laughter, and calm.

Lesson: Wellness means caring for our bodies, our feelings, and our Earth. We can solve problems with kindness, clean up our space, play and move, breathe to feel calm, and use smart, Earth-friendly ideas.

The End.
