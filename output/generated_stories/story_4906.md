# Benny Bear's Big Bathroom Adventure

Benny Bear was a little brown cub with the softest fur and the biggest smile. He loved to splash and play, but today something was very wrong in his cozy bathroom.

"Oh no!" Benny said, looking at the shiny white bathtub. The water wasn't going down the drain! It just sat there, making little ripples when he touched it with his paw.

Benny's mama had gone to the store, and he really wanted to take his evening bath. The warm, soapy water always made him feel so clean and happy. But now the tub was full of old, cold water that wouldn't go away.

"What can I do?" Benny wondered aloud, sitting on the fluffy blue bath mat. He put his furry chin in his paws and thought very hard.

First, Benny tried pushing the water with his hands. "Go down, water, go down!" he said, making big swooshing motions. But the water just sloshed around and stayed put.

Then Benny remembered something. "Maybe there's something blocking the drain," he said to himself. He was a little scared to put his paw in the cold water, but he took a deep breath and felt around carefully.

"Icky!" Benny said, pulling out a big clump of his own brown fur mixed with soap. It felt slimy and gross between his toes, but he was brave and kept going.

He found more fur, and even a small rubber duck that had gotten stuck! "There you are, Ducky!" Benny giggled, setting the yellow toy on the bathroom counter. "You were causing trouble!"

But the water still wouldn't drain. Benny looked around the bathroom with his bright brown eyes. He saw the toilet plunger standing in the corner like a tall soldier.

"Hmm," Benny said, remembering how Papa Bear used it. "Maybe this can help!"

Benny was too small to use the big plunger the regular way, so he got creative. He turned it upside down and used the handle to gently poke at the drain. He was very careful not to scratch the tub.

"Come on, drain, you can do it!" Benny encouraged, just like Mama encouraged him when he was learning new things.

Suddenly, there was a funny gurgling sound. "Glub, glub, glub!" The water started spinning around and around, making a little whirlpool.

"It's working! It's working!" Benny cheered, clapping his paws together. The water went down, down, down until the tub was completely empty and sparkling clean.

Benny felt so proud of himself. His chest puffed out and his tail wagged with joy. "I did it all by myself!" he said, doing a little happy dance on the bath mat.

When Mama Bear came home, she found Benny sitting proudly by the clean, empty tub.

"Mama, I fixed the drain!" Benny announced. "The water was stuck, but I didn't give up. I used my brain and my paws and the plunger handle, and I solved the problem!"

Mama Bear gave Benny the biggest, warmest hug. "I'm so proud of you, little one," she said. "You were brave, you thought carefully, and you didn't give up. That's what good problem-solvers do!"

That night, Benny's bath was extra special. As he splashed in the warm, clean water, he felt happy knowing that when problems come up, he could think of ways to solve them.

The End.
