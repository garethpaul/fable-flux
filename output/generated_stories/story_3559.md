# Nutmeg Leads the Way

Nutmeg the squirrel lived in a cozy treehouse high in a big, kind oak. The treehouse smelled like fresh leaves and warm wood. Soft moss made the floor feel springy. Sunlight danced in through a little round window. Nutmeg loved her treehouse. She kept tiny baskets, neat ropes, and small tools on a pegboard. She liked to be ready. She liked to help.

One breezy morning, Nutmeg looked out and sniffed the sweet air. “Mmm, it smells like pine and rain,” she said. She heard birds chirp, “Cheep-cheep!” and leaves whisper, “Shhhh.”

Then—crack! A little bridge of twigs down on the ground snapped. The bridge helped the forest friends reach the berry bushes. Now it hung wobbly, broken, and sad. Nutmeg’s ears perked up.

“Oh no,” said Bluebird, fluttering near. “How will we get to the berries?”

Bunny hopped in place. “I am hungry. But I am scared of the big stream.”

Turtle blinked slowly. “We must be careful,” he said. “Slow and safe.”

Nutmeg put a paw to her chin. Her tail swished. She felt a tiny squeeze in her tummy. This was a big problem. But she took a deep breath. “I can help,” she said. “Let’s make a new bridge. We will work together.”

“Who will lead?” asked Mouse, squeaking softly.

Nutmeg stood tall. “I will,” she said, voice gentle and bright. “I will listen. I will plan. We will be a team.”

The friends smiled. They trusted Nutmeg. She always shared, and she always thought things through. Nutmeg clapped her paws. “First, we need a plan. Let’s look. Let’s think.”

They all walked to the stream. Water rushed and glittered. Splash-splash! The smooth stones were cool under Nutmeg’s paws. The air felt damp and smelled like clean rain.

Nutmeg pointed. “The stream is fast in the middle, but slow near the logs,” she said. “We can build the bridge where it is slow. That is safer.”

“That is smart,” said Turtle. “Good plan.”

Nutmeg nodded. “Thank you. Now, jobs! Bluebird, please fly and find strong vines. Vines will tie the bridge. Bunny, please gather straight sticks. Mouse, you can carry little pebbles to make steps. Turtle, can you check that the ground is flat? I will bring rope and tools from my treehouse.”

Bluebird flapped. “On it!” Bunny thumped. “Yes!” Mouse squeaked, “I can do that!” Turtle smiled. “Slow and steady.”

Nutmeg hurried up the tree. Her paws felt the rough bark. Scritch-scratch! In the treehouse, she grabbed two ropes, a tiny hammer, and soft cloth for paws. She whispered, “We can do this,” and felt brave.

Back at the stream, she saw her friends working. Bluebird had a beak full of green vines. Bunny laid sticks in a neat pile. Mouse lined up pebbles like little buttons. Turtle patted the dirt smooth. Pat-pat.

Nutmeg set the tools down. “Great job, team. You are amazing,” she said. “Now we build. First, two big logs on the sides. Bunny, can you help me roll this log?”

Bunny pushed. Nutmeg pushed. “Heave-ho!” they said together. The log rolled with a deep, woody thump. Turtle guided it. “A little left,” he said. “Good.”

Nutmeg tied vines in strong knots. “One loop, two loop, pull,” she said. “Bluebird, please check from above. Are the logs straight?”

Bluebird swooped. “A little more to the right,” she chirped.

Nutmeg listened. She moved the log. “How about now?”

“Perfect!” sang Bluebird.

Mouse brought pebbles. “For the steps!” he squeaked.

“Great idea,” Nutmeg said. “That will help little feet.”

They laid sticks across the logs. Tap-tap went the tiny hammer. Swish went the vines as Nutmeg tied careful knots. The air smelled like green vines and fresh wood. The friends worked and hummed. “We are a team. We are kind. We are smart,” they sang softly.

Soon, the bridge was almost done. The stream chuckled below, not so scary now. But then the wind blew. Whoooo! One stick slid. Plop! It fell into the water.

Bunny’s eyes went wide. “Oh no!”

Nutmeg held up a paw. “It’s okay,” she said in a calm voice. “We can fix it. Let’s stop and think.”

She looked at the wet edges. “We need more ties on the windy side,” she said. “Bluebird, more vines, please. Mouse, can you hold the stick? Bunny, help me tie.”

They worked together, slow and careful. “One loop, two loop, pull.” The knots held tight. Nutmeg checked each tie with a gentle tug. Tug-tug. “Safe and strong,” she said.

At last, the bridge stood steady. It looked simple and neat. Nutmeg placed the pebble steps at the ends. “Now, we test,” she said. “I will go first.”

She stepped on the bridge. Cre-e-eak. It held. She took another step. Cre-e-eak. It held again. Nutmeg smiled. “It feels strong,” she said. “Now, Bunny, your turn. Slow and careful.”

Bunny hopped across. “Boing, boing.” He reached the berries. “Yay!” he cheered. Bluebird zipped over. Turtle followed, step by slow step. Mouse scampered. Everyone made it safely.

They all picked berries. The berries were shiny and sweet. “Mmmm,” said Bunny, mouth purple. “Thank you, Nutmeg!”

Nutmeg’s cheeks felt warm. “Thank you, team,” she said. “We did this together. I listened. We planned. We helped. That is leadership. Leadership means caring, thinking, and guiding with kindness.”

Turtle nodded. “You were calm,” he said. “You gave us jobs. You thanked us. That helped us do our best.”

Bluebird chirped, “You saw the problem. You made a plan. You led with love.”

Mouse squeaked, “We can help others too!”

Nutmeg looked at her friends and the safe new bridge. She felt proud and grateful. The stream whispered, “Thank you,” and the leaves rustled happy songs. The sun slid warm over their backs.

Nutmeg said, “When we have a problem, we can stop, look, listen, and plan. We can be leaders by being kind and brave. We can help each other.”

Everyone smiled. Their hearts felt light. The forest felt safe.

They carried berries to share with friends who could not come. They thanked the oak tree for holding Nutmeg’s cozy home. They thanked each other for trying their best.

Nutmeg gave a happy twirl. “We did it,” she said.

“Yes,” said Bunny. “Together.”

The End.
