# Toby the Turtle Learns to Share

In a cozy bedroom with soft blue walls and fluffy white clouds painted on the ceiling, lived a shy little turtle named Toby. His shell was the prettiest shade of green, with yellow spots that sparkled like tiny suns. Toby loved his special corner by the window where warm sunlight streamed in every morning.

Toby had a favorite toy - a shiny red ball that bounced perfectly and made a gentle *boing* sound when it hit the soft carpet. He hugged it close to his shell every night and played with it every day. The ball made him feel happy and safe.

One sunny morning, Toby's friend Luna the bunny came to visit. Her soft gray ears twitched with excitement as she hopped into the bedroom.

"Hi Toby!" Luna said cheerfully. "What a beautiful day to play!"

Toby peeked out from his shell just a little bit. "Hello, Luna," he whispered quietly.

Luna's bright eyes spotted the shiny red ball. "Wow! What a wonderful ball! Can I play with it too?"

Toby's heart started beating fast. He pulled his head deeper into his shell. "No," he said in a small voice. "It's mine."

Luna's ears drooped down. "Oh," she said sadly. "I just wanted to bounce it once."

Toby clutched the ball tighter. He felt scared that if Luna played with his ball, something bad might happen to it. What if she took it away? What if it got lost?

Luna sat down on the soft carpet, looking disappointed. "I brought my jump rope to share," she said quietly. "I thought we could play together."

Toby watched from inside his shell as Luna sat alone. He could see that her whiskers were drooping and her ears were flat against her head. She looked very sad.

Suddenly, Toby felt a funny feeling in his tummy - not a good funny, but a worried funny. He realized that keeping his ball all to himself was making his friend feel bad.

"Luna," Toby said softly, poking his head out a little more. "Are you sad?"

"Yes," Luna nodded. "I feel left out when friends don't want to share with me."

Toby thought about this. He remembered how sad he felt when other animals didn't include him in their games because he was shy. 

"I'm sorry, Luna," Toby said, his voice getting a little stronger. "I was scared you might lose my ball or take it away."

Luna's ears perked up. "Oh Toby! I would never take your special toy. I just wanted to play together."

Toby took a deep breath. Slowly, he crawled out of his shell completely. "Would you like to bounce the ball back and forth?" he asked.

Luna's whole face lit up like sunshine. "Yes! That sounds wonderful!"

Toby gently rolled the red ball to Luna. She caught it with her soft paws and bounced it back to him. *Boing, boing, boing!* The happy sound filled the cozy room.

"This is so much fun!" Luna giggled. "And look - we can use my jump rope too! We can bounce the ball over it!"

They played together for hours, sharing both toys and creating new games. Toby discovered that playing with a friend was even more fun than playing alone.

As the sun began to set, painting the bedroom walls with warm orange light, Luna gave Toby a gentle hug.

"Thank you for sharing with me, Toby," she said. "You're such a good friend."

Toby smiled widely. "Thank you for being patient with me, Luna. I learned that sharing makes everything more fun!"

From that day on, Toby always remembered that sharing with friends made his heart feel warm and happy, just like the sunshine in his cozy bedroom.

The End.
