# Rocco’s Quiet Song Plan

Rocco was a musical raccoon with bright eyes and stripey paws. He loved to make songs with everything he found. Tap-tap on a cup. Ting-ting on a spoon. Hummm on a roll of tape. He wore his favorite red scarf and carried a tiny drum on a string.

One sunny morning, Rocco tiptoed into the home office. The room smelled like warm paper and orange tea. The computer hummed, “Mmmmm.” The printer blinked little green lights, like tiny fireflies. A big plant rustled in the corner.

Rocco’s friend, Maya, sat at the desk. She typed, click-click, click-click. “I have a big call,” Maya whispered. “I need quiet, please.”

Rocco nodded. “Okay,” he said softly. “Quiet.” He pressed a paw to his drum and shushed his whiskers. He wanted to be independent and helpful. He wanted to show he could make good choices.

But then, he saw the shiny paper clips. They looked like little silver fish. “Ting!” he tapped one with his spoon. “Ting-ting!” The sound was so pretty. His paws itched to play more.

Rocco looked at the desk. There were sticky notes in rainbow colors, a smooth stapler, and a cup of pens. His heart felt bouncy. “Just one tiny song,” he whispered to himself. “A very, very quiet song.” He did not prepare. He did not check the time. He did not ask.

Tap-tap on the cup. Ting on the clip. Clap—the stapler went shut! “Oh!” Rocco squeaked.

The stapler made a loud clack. Maya jumped. “Rocco,” she said, surprised. “I’m on my call. I asked for quiet.”

Rocco’s ears drooped. His tail curled around his feet. He felt sorry. He looked at the desk. Papers were crooked. A pen rolled to the floor. “I… I wanted to make music,” he said in a tiny voice. “I forgot to prepare. I didn’t ask. I made noise.”

Maya took a breath. “I know you love music,” she said kindly. “But right now I need peace. Please help me fix this.”

Rocco nodded. “I will make amends,” he said. The words felt big, but they were strong. “I will fix what I did.”

He picked up the pen and placed it gently in the cup. He straightened the papers with smooth, careful paws. He set the stapler down softly—no clacks. He even found the paper clip that had jumped and put it back with a tiny “ting” that sounded like a whispering star.

“I’m sorry,” Rocco said, looking at Maya. “I forgot. Next time, I will prepare. I will make a quiet plan.”

Maya smiled. “Thank you for making amends, Rocco,” she said. “That helps a lot.”

Rocco tiptoed to the doorway. He sat on a small rug and closed his eyes. He took a slow breath in—sniff, the room smelled like orange tea and pencil wood. He breathed out—hummm. He thought and thought. “I can make a Quiet Song Kit,” he whispered. “I can be independent and prepared.”

He found a soft box on a shelf. Inside, he placed quiet things: a fuzzy scarf to rub, a small shaker with rice wrapped in a sock, and a paper book to tap very gently. He drew a plan on a sticky note with a blue crayon:

1. Ask first.
2. Check the time.
3. Choose quiet tools.
4. Play in the hallway.
5. Stop if someone needs quiet.

He waited until Maya looked up. “Excuse me,” Rocco said politely. “May I make a quiet song in the hallway? I have a plan.”

Maya glanced at the clock and nodded. “Yes, Rocco. I have five minutes before my call. Thank you for asking.”

Rocco padded to the hallway. The carpet felt soft on his paws. He held the shaker—shhh, shhh. He rubbed the scarf—swish, swish. He tapped the book—tup-tup. His song sounded like gentle rain. He smiled. He felt proud. He was persistent. He kept trying until he found a way to do what he loved and still be kind.

When the five minutes were done, Rocco peeked into the office. “All done,” he whispered.

Maya finished her call and came to the hallway. “Thank you for waiting and for your plan,” she said. “You made amends. You cleaned up. You asked. You prepared. You did it all by yourself.”

Rocco’s tail swished happily. “I learned,” he said. “When I make a mistake, I can fix it. I can say sorry. I can make a plan. I can try again.”

Maya nodded. “That is how we grow.”

Rocco tapped the book—tup—and the sound was small and sweet. “Next time,” he said, “I will ask first.” He held up the sticky note plan. “I will follow my steps.”

They both smiled. The office felt calm. The plant rustled like applause. The computer hummed a soft “Mmmmm,” like a bedtime lullaby.

Rocco wrapped the fuzzy scarf around his drum. “I can make music and be kind,” he said. “That is my best song.”

The End.
