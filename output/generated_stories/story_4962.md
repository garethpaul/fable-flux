# Shelly's Mindful Morning

Shelly the wise tortoise loved her cozy study corner. Soft morning light came through the window. Her special cushion was purple and fluffy. Books lined the wooden shelves all around her.

Every day, Shelly practiced something very special. She called it "mindful breathing." Shelly would sit very still. She would close her eyes gently. Then she would breathe in slowly and breathe out slowly.

"In... and out," Shelly whispered softly. "In... and out."

This morning felt different. Shelly heard tiny footsteps. Three little field mice peeked around the corner. Their names were Mimi, Max, and Molly. They looked curious but shy.

"What are you doing, Miss Shelly?" asked Mimi in a small voice.

Shelly opened her kind eyes slowly. She smiled warmly at the mice. "I'm practicing mindfulness, little ones. It helps me feel calm and happy."

"Mind-full-ness?" repeated Max, tilting his tiny head.

"Yes," said Shelly gently. "It means paying attention to right now. To your breathing. To how your body feels. To the sounds around you."

Molly's whiskers twitched with interest. "Can we try too?"

Shelly's heart felt warm and happy. "Of course! Mindfulness is meant to be shared. My grandmother taught me when I was very young, just like you."

The three mice scurried closer. They sat in a small circle around Shelly's cushion. Their little paws folded in their laps.

"First, let's sit up tall like strong trees," said Shelly. She showed them how to sit straight but comfortable. "Feel your back nice and long."

The mice straightened their tiny spines. They looked very serious and important.

"Now, close your eyes like sleepy flowers," Shelly continued. "Listen to the sounds around us. What do you hear?"

"Birds singing!" whispered Mimi.

"The wind in the trees!" said Max quietly.

"My tummy rumbling," giggled Molly softly.

Shelly chuckled warmly. "All perfect sounds. Now let's breathe together. Put one paw on your chest. Feel it go up and down."

The mice placed their tiny paws on their chests. They breathed in slowly. Their little chests rose up. They breathed out slowly. Their chests went down.

"In... and out," Shelly guided them. "Like gentle waves on a calm lake."

For five whole minutes, they breathed together. The study corner felt peaceful and quiet. Sunbeams danced on the walls. A gentle breeze rustled the curtains.

When they opened their eyes, the mice looked different. Their faces were relaxed and peaceful. Their eyes sparkled with wonder.

"I feel so calm!" said Mimi, stretching her tiny arms.

"My body feels warm and happy," added Max.

"Can we do this every day?" asked Molly hopefully.

Shelly nodded slowly. "Mindfulness is a gift we can practice anytime. When we feel worried, excited, or even angry. It helps us remember to be kind to ourselves and others."

The mice hugged Shelly's large, gentle foot. "Thank you for sharing this special tradition with us!"

"Thank you for letting me teach you," said Shelly. "Sharing wisdom makes it grow stronger. Just like my grandmother shared with me, and now I share with you."

From that day on, every morning was special in the study corner. Shelly and her three little friends would sit together. They would breathe together. They would practice being mindful together.

And whenever other forest friends felt worried or upset, the mice would share what they learned. Soon, the whole forest knew about mindful breathing.

Shelly smiled proudly. The best gifts are the ones we share with others.

The End.
