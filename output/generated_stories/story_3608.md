# Ruby's Rainbow Art Project

In a cozy warm kitchen, a little rabbit named Ruby hopped around excitedly. The morning sun streamed through the window, making everything glow golden. Ruby's nose twitched as she smelled fresh bread baking in the oven.

"Today is the perfect day for art!" Ruby announced, clapping her small paws together.

Ruby wanted to make a beautiful rainbow painting for her mama's birthday. But when she looked around, she couldn't find any art supplies.

"Oh no," Ruby said, her ears drooping. "I don't have any paints or brushes."

Ruby sat down and thought carefully. She remembered what her teacher said: "Good artists plan ahead and use what they have."

Ruby's nose wiggled as she got an idea. She hopped to the recycling bin and peeked inside.

"Look at all these treasures!" she exclaimed.

Ruby found an empty cereal box, some bottle caps, and old newspapers. In the kitchen drawers, she discovered cotton swabs and small cups.

"I can make my own art supplies!" Ruby said proudly.

She carefully cut the cereal box into a flat canvas. The cardboard felt smooth under her paws. Then Ruby looked in the refrigerator for natural paints.

"Red from strawberry juice, yellow from lemon, and blue from blueberries," she planned out loud.

Ruby squeezed the fruits into the small cups. The kitchen filled with sweet, fruity smells. She dipped the cotton swabs into the colorful juices.

As Ruby started painting, her friend Sam the squirrel peeked through the window.

"What are you making, Ruby?" Sam asked curiously.

"A rainbow for Mama's birthday," Ruby replied, carefully painting a red stripe.

"Can I help?" Sam asked hopefully.

Ruby paused. She had planned to do this all by herself. But then she remembered another important lesson about sharing and including friends.

"Of course!" Ruby smiled. "You can help me paint the orange stripe."

Sam climbed through the window and washed his tiny paws. Together, they dipped bottle caps in the fruit paints and made perfect circles of color.

"This is so much fun!" Sam giggled as he painted.

Ruby's friend Maria the mouse soon joined them too. She helped paint the purple stripe using grape juice.

"Teamwork makes art even more beautiful," Maria said wisely.

The three friends worked together, planning each color carefully. They recycled old newspapers to protect the counter. The warm kitchen buzzed with happy chatter and creativity.

When Mama Rabbit came home, she gasped with delight.

"Oh, Ruby! This rainbow is absolutely wonderful!"

"I made it myself," Ruby said proudly, then quickly added, "Well, Sam and Maria helped too. We used recycled materials and fruit paints."

Mama Rabbit hugged all three friends. "I love it even more knowing you planned it so thoughtfully and worked together. And using natural materials was so clever!"

Ruby beamed with pride. She had learned that being honest about getting help made her art project even more special. She discovered that planning ahead and recycling could create beautiful things.

"Art is about creativity, not fancy supplies," Ruby said happily.

The rainbow painting hung proudly on the kitchen wall, reminding everyone that determination, planning, and friendship could create the most beautiful art of all.

The End.
