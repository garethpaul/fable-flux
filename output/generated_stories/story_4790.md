# Fawn’s Sharing Shed

Every sunny morning, a gentle deer fawn named Fawn padded to the garden shed. Tap, tap, tap went Fawn’s tiny hooves on the stepping stones. The air smelled like fresh grass and sweet flowers. The shed door was wooden and warm from the sun. Fawn had a happy routine: sweep the floor, open the windows wide, and set out a little mat for outdoor crafts.

Inside the shed were baskets of pinecones, smooth rocks, dried leaves, and bright ribbons. “Good morning, supplies,” Fawn said in a soft voice. “We will make something lovely today.” Fawn liked to craft. Fawn liked to plan. Fawn liked to keep things neat.

One morning, Fawn found a shiny bell on the shelf. It jingled like tiny stars. “I will make a special wind chime,” Fawn whispered. Fawn lined up five pinecones, four ribbons, three rocks, and the shiny bell. “I will make it all by myself,” Fawn said, feeling proud.

A soft breeze blew in the window. It smelled like wet earth and mint. Outside, Fawn heard rustles and tiny footsteps. Tap-tap. Pitter-patter. Friends were near.

Bunny poked in her nose. “Hi, Fawn! What are you making?” Bunny’s whiskers twitched.

“I am making a wind chime,” said Fawn. “It will go by the window.”

“I love wind!” said Bunny. “Can I help tie a ribbon?”

Squirrel skittered in and held a smooth rock. “I can help too! I am good with knots.” Squirrel’s tail brushed the floor like a soft broom.

Robin hopped on the windowsill and sang, “Tweet-tweet! I can bring a tiny twig to hang the bell.”

Fawn looked at the neat piles. Fawn remembered the plan: do it alone, keep it tidy. Fawn felt a tight squeeze inside. Fawn liked to share snacks and games, but this project felt special. Fawn’s ears drooped a little. “I… I wanted to do it myself,” Fawn said in a small voice.

Bunny’s ears bent. Squirrel’s tail drooped. Robin quieted her song. The shed felt still. The bell did not jingle.

Fawn saw Bunny’s kind eyes, Squirrel’s hopeful paws, and Robin’s bright feathers. Fawn heard the wind whisper, whoosh, like a gentle hug. Fawn took a deep breath. It smelled like cut wood and lavender. Fawn thought, We are friends. Friends help. Sharing can make joy grow.

Fawn lifted the shiny bell. “I feel proud of my plan,” Fawn said. “But I also want to be generous. We can make the wind chime together. We can share. We can include everyone.”

Bunny smiled wide. Squirrel squeaked, “Yes!” Robin chirped, “Tweet-tweet!”

They made a new plan. Fawn said, “We will each have a job. We will take turns. We will be gentle.”

- Fawn held the twig steady.
- Bunny tied blue ribbons. The ribbons felt silky and cool.
- Squirrel looped the pinecones with careful little paws.
- Robin tucked tiny leaves between knots, like green kisses.

“Listen,” said Fawn. “Tie, tie, pull. Tie, tie, pull.” It became a happy routine. They moved together, slow and kind. When the ribbon slipped, Squirrel said, “Oops!” and giggled. Bunny helped fix it. When Robin dropped a leaf, Fawn said, “That’s okay,” and smiled.

They hung the wind chime by the window. The breeze blew. The chime sang, ding-ding, tink-tink, soft and sweet. Sunlight made the ribbons glow. The scent of pine filled the shed.

“It is lovely,” said Bunny.

“It is strong,” said Squirrel.

“It sings,” said Robin.

“It is ours,” said Fawn. Fawn’s heart felt warm and wide. Sharing did not make the craft less special. Sharing made the joy grow bigger.

Just then, Mouse peeked in, shy and small. “May I join your shed time?” Mouse asked in a tiny voice. “I am new. I have never crafted before.”

Fawn remembered the tight feeling from before. Fawn also remembered the warm feeling of sharing. Fawn nodded. “Yes, Mouse. We have room. We can make a garden flag next. We can use this soft fabric. We will show you how.”

Mouse’s eyes shone. “Thank you for including me.”

They worked in their kind routine again. Fawn held the fabric smooth. Bunny painted a yellow sun. Squirrel dotted little acorns. Robin stamped leaf prints. Mouse glued a red heart right in the middle. Glue felt sticky on little paws, and everyone laughed when a leaf stuck to Squirrel’s whiskers.

At the end, they planted the flag outside the shed. The flag flapped, flap-flap, in the wind. It showed the sun, leaves, acorns, and a bright heart.

Fawn looked around. The shed was still neat, and it was also full of happy friends. The wind chime sang. The flag danced. The garden smelled sweet. Fawn said, “Today I learned something. Generosity means I share my things, my time, and my plans. When I include friends, the day feels brighter.”

Bunny, Squirrel, Robin, and Mouse all said, “Me too!”

Fawn added, “We can make this our shed routine. Every morning, we open the windows, share our supplies, include new friends, and make something together.”

Everyone nodded. The breeze said, whoosh. The bell said, tink. Their hearts said, yes.

The End.
