# Milo Thanks the Morning

Milo was a courageous monkey who lived in a cozy treehouse. His treehouse smelled like fresh leaves and sweet bananas. The floor felt smooth and warm from the sun. Birds sang, “Tweet-tweet!” in the branches, and the wind whispered, “Whoosh.”

One sunny morning, Milo stretched his arms wide. “Good morning, treehouse!” he said. He liked to do exercise every day. He hopped, he skipped, and he did tiny monkey jumps. “Boing! Boing!” His tail swished happily.

Milo looked down from the window and saw his friend, Tia the tiny turtle, trying to push a heavy basket of books. “Oh! That looks hard,” said Milo. He zipped down the rope ladder. “Hi, Tia! Do you need help?”

“Yes, please,” Tia said softly. “These books are for the forest school. I want to share them so everyone can learn.”

Milo smiled. “I will help. Learning is good. Sharing is kind.” He picked up the basket and felt the rough straw on his hands. It was heavy, but Milo was strong and brave. “Let’s go!”

They walked along the soft moss path. Squirrels chattered. The air smelled like pine. The basket bumped against Milo’s legs, thump-thump, but he kept going. “We can do it!” he said. “Exercise makes us strong!”

At the forest school, a circle of animals waited. “Books!” chirped a blue bird. “Stories!” squeaked a little mouse.

Just then, a big baboon named Bruno rushed in. He grabbed the basket and said, “Mine!” He pulled it close.

Milo took a deep breath. His heart went thump. He felt brave, but he also felt a little nervous. He remembered: Use kind words first. “Bruno,” said Milo, “these books are for everyone. It is fair to share.”

Bruno frowned. “I want all the pictures.” His voice was loud.

Tia blinked and whispered, “We brought them for the school. We want to help all our friends learn.”

Milo stood tall. “Bruno, taking without asking is not fair. Justice means we do what is right for everyone. We share. We take turns. We say thank you.”

The animals nodded. The mouse squeaked, “That is fair.” The bird chirped, “Let’s share!”

Bruno looked at the circle. He looked at Tia, who was small but brave. He looked at Milo, who was strong but kind. The room was quiet. Milo could hear the leaves rustle outside. “Whoosh.”

Bruno’s shoulders sagged. “I… I didn’t think about everyone,” he said. “I just wanted the pictures.” He rubbed his hands on the basket. “I am sorry.”

Milo smiled gently. “Thank you for saying sorry. That is brave too.”

Tia said, “We can make a plan. We can take turns with the books. We can read together.”

Milo said, “We can even have a reading circle in the treehouse. I will bring banana snacks.”

The animals cheered. “Hooray!”

They made a chart on a big leaf with berry ink. Milo wrote slowly: “Take Turns.” Tia drew little stars. Bruno added a smiley face. Everyone chose a time. It felt fair and kind. It felt like justice.

After the plan, Milo led an exercise game to celebrate. “Reach to the sky!” he said. Tiny arms and paws reached up. “Touch your toes!” They giggled and wiggled. The floor felt cool under their feet. “Shake-shake-shake!” Their bodies felt strong and happy.

Then it was reading time. The books smelled like paper and pine. Pages whispered, “Flip-flip.” Milo helped Bruno sound out words. “C-a-t,” said Bruno slowly.

“Cat!” Milo cheered. “You did it!”

Bruno smiled. “Thanks, Milo.”

Milo felt warm in his heart. He looked at Tia, who looked proud. He looked at the circle of friends. “I am grateful,” said Milo. “I am grateful for friends who learn. I am grateful for sharing. I am grateful for saying sorry. Thank you, everyone.”

Tia nodded. “I am grateful for your help, Milo.”

Bruno took a breath. “I am grateful for a second chance,” he said. “And for banana snacks.”

They all laughed. “Crunch, munch,” went the bananas.

When the sun began to set, they climbed back to the treehouse for one last story. The treehouse glowed orange and gold. The wind was soft. The birds sang a sleepy song. Milo tucked a blanket around Tia and handed Bruno a book.

“Today we learned,” said Milo. “We learned to share. We learned to be fair. We learned to say thank you. Gratitude makes our hearts gentle and strong.”

Bruno nodded. “Justice feels good,” he said. “It feels like a big hug for everyone.”

“And exercise helps our bodies,” Milo added, doing one last tiny jump. “Boing!”

They all hugged. “Thank you, day,” Milo whispered. “Thank you, friends. Thank you, books. Thank you, treehouse.”

The moon peeked through the leaves. The treehouse was calm and sweet. Milo’s eyes felt sleepy. He smiled and felt safe.

He knew that tomorrow, they would read more, share more, and say thank you again.

The End.
