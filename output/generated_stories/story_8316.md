# Shelly's Fair Share

In the cozy study corner of the library, a shy little turtle named Shelly sat quietly at her favorite table. She loved this special spot with its soft cushions and warm lamp that made everything glow like sunshine. Today, she had brought her lunch box filled with yummy carrots, apple slices, and a peanut butter sandwich cut into perfect triangles.

"Crunch, crunch," went Shelly's carrots as she nibbled them slowly. She always ate her healthy foods first, just like Mama Turtle taught her. The orange carrots were sweet and made her shell feel strong.

Suddenly, Shelly heard sniffling sounds coming from behind the tall bookshelf. She peeked around the corner with her little green head and saw Benny Bunny sitting on the floor, looking very sad.

"What's wrong, Benny?" Shelly whispered softly, even though her heart was beating fast. She wasn't used to talking to other animals, but Benny looked like he needed help.

Benny wiped his eyes with his fluffy paw. "I forgot my lunch today, and my tummy is making grumbly sounds. But Max Mouse took the only apple from the sharing basket and won't give anyone else a turn. He said 'finders keepers' and ran away with it."

Shelly's kind heart felt heavy. She knew it wasn't fair when someone took everything for themselves. That's not how sharing worked! She listened carefully as Benny's tummy made a tiny "grrowwwl" sound.

"That doesn't sound right," Shelly said, her voice getting a little braver. "Everyone should get a fair turn with the sharing basket."

Shelly looked at her lunch box, then at hungry Benny. She knew what was right. "Here, Benny. Would you like to share my lunch? I have plenty of good foods."

Benny's ears perked up. "Really? But it's your lunch!"

"Mama always says sharing makes food taste even better," Shelly smiled, opening her lunch box wider. The smell of fresh apples and peanut butter filled the air.

Just then, Max Mouse scurried by, still holding the big red apple. Shelly took a deep breath and called out, "Max! Wait, please!"

Max stopped and turned around, looking surprised that quiet Shelly was talking to him.

"That apple belongs to everyone," Shelly said gently but firmly. "The sharing basket is for all of us when we're hungry. It's not fair to take it all."

Max's whiskers twitched. He looked at the apple, then at Benny's sad face, then at Shelly's kind eyes. "But... but I was really hungry too," he said in a small voice.

"We can all share together!" Shelly suggested. "I have food, and we can cut the apple into pieces. Everyone gets enough when we share fairly."

Max's tail wiggled with excitement. "That sounds much better than eating alone!"

Soon, all three friends sat together in the study corner. They shared Shelly's healthy lunch and cut the apple into three perfect pieces. The study corner filled with happy munching sounds and giggles.

"This tastes so much better than eating by myself," Max admitted, crunching his apple slice.

"And my tummy feels happy and full," Benny added, enjoying his carrot stick.

Shelly felt warm and proud inside. By listening to her friend's problem and standing up for what was right, she had helped everyone have a fair and yummy lunch.

From that day on, the three friends always shared their snacks in the study corner, making sure everyone got their fair turn and plenty of nutritious food to help them grow big and strong.

The End.
