# Polly's Rooftop Rainbow

High up on a sunny rooftop, a bright green parrot named Polly was very busy. She had colorful feathers that sparkled in the morning light, and today she had a big dream.

"I want to make the most beautiful rainbow ever!" Polly squawked excitedly, flapping her wings. She had found some old paint cans on the rooftop and wanted to paint a giant rainbow across the wall.

Polly dipped her wing into the red paint. "Red first!" she chirped. But when she tried to paint a long, smooth line, the paint dripped and made messy blobs. "Oh no!" Polly huffed. "This isn't working!"

She tried again with blue paint, but her wing was too small to make the wide strokes she wanted. The blue line was wobbly and thin. Polly felt her feathers ruffle with frustration.

"This is too hard!" she squawked, sitting down with a thump. "I can't do it by myself!"

Just then, she heard a gentle voice. "What's wrong, Polly?" It was Max, a friendly pigeon who lived nearby.

"I want to paint a beautiful rainbow," Polly explained, pointing her wing at the messy wall. "But I can't make it look right. My wings are too small, and the paint keeps dripping!"

Max tilted his gray head thoughtfully. "Hmm, maybe I can help! What if we work together?"

"Together?" Polly perked up her bright yellow crest.

"Yes! I could hold the paintbrush in my beak while you tell me where to paint," Max suggested. "And look, here comes Rosie the robin! She's great at making straight lines."

Soon, a small red robin landed on the rooftop. "What are you two doing?" Rosie chirped sweetly.

"We're making a rainbow!" Polly explained excitedly. "Will you help us?"

"I love rainbows!" Rosie sang. "I can help too!"

The three friends made a plan. Polly would be the director, telling everyone what colors to use and where to paint. Max would use his strong beak to hold the big paintbrush for the wide strokes. Rosie would use her tiny beak to add the delicate details.

"Let's start with red at the top," Polly instructed. "Max, can you paint a big curved line?"

"I can do that!" Max said, carefully holding the brush.

"Now orange next to it," Polly continued. "Rosie, can you help make it smooth?"

"Of course!" Rosie chirped, using her small beak to fix the bumpy parts.

They worked together all morning. When Polly felt frustrated because a color wasn't quite right, Max and Rosie encouraged her. "We can fix it together!" they would say.

Slowly, beautifully, their rainbow grew. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple stripes curved across the wall like a real rainbow after rain.

"We did it!" Polly squawked with joy, dancing on her little feet. "Look how beautiful it is!"

The rainbow was perfect - bright and smooth and cheerful. Other birds from the neighborhood flew over to admire their work.

"You know what?" Polly said, looking at her friends. "This rainbow is much more beautiful than anything I could have made alone. Thank you for helping me!"

"That's what friends are for," Max cooed.

"Working together makes everything better!" Rosie added.

Polly learned that when something feels too hard, asking friends for help can make it possible. And the best part was sharing the happy feeling of finishing their goal together.

The End.
