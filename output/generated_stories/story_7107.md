# Kiki Joey’s Cozy Room Day

In a cozy bedroom with soft lamplight and a window that showed the pink morning sky, a joyful kangaroo joey named Kiki bounced out of bed. Her blanket felt warm and fuzzy. Her nose twitched. She smelled clean sheets and a tiny hint of lavender.

“Good morning, room!” Kiki giggled. She patted her pillow. Pat pat. “Today I do things by myself.”

Kiki slid to the floor with a little thump. The carpet was tickly under her toes. She looked at her room. Toys peeked from a basket. Books sat in a little line. Her favorite red socks hid under the bed like sleepy mice.

Mama Roo poked her head in the doorway. “Good morning, Kiki,” Mama Roo said softly. “Would you like help getting ready?”

Kiki puffed her cheeks, then smiled big. “I will try by myself first, please,” she said. “If I need help, I will ask.”

Mama Roo nodded. “That sounds independent,” she said warmly. “I’ll be in the kitchen if you need me.”

Kiki felt proud. Her chest felt like bubbly soda pop. She opened her sock drawer. “Hello, socks,” she whispered. “I can do this.” She found her red socks and slipped them on. One sock was inside out. “Oops!” Kiki giggled. She tugged and smoothed. “There. I did it!”

Next, Kiki looked at her bed. The blanket was all wiggly. “Blanket, let’s get cozy.” She pulled the blanket up. She smoothed it with her paws. Swish, swish. The pillow went right in the middle. “Ta-da!” Kiki clapped for herself. The bed looked neat and happy.

On the floor, her blocks were scattered like little rainbows. Kiki took a deep breath. “I can make a home for blocks,” she said. She picked up blue, then yellow, then green. The basket made a soft plunk sound each time a block dropped in. Plunk. Plunk. Plunk. It sounded like a tiny drum. Kiki liked the sound. “Organized feels calm,” she sang in a small tune.

Just then, Kiki’s plush koala, Coco, lay on the pillow. Kiki reached for Coco. “Coco, do you want hugs?” she asked.

Silence. Kiki paused. “I will ask first and listen,” she told herself. “I practice boundaries.” She held Coco gently. “Hug now or later?” she whispered. In her heart, she imagined Coco saying, “Later, please. I want to rest.”

Kiki nodded. “Okay, Coco. I will give you space.” She tucked Coco under the blanket and gave a soft pat to the pillow instead. Her paws made a whoosh sound on the pillowcase.

Kiki hopped to her low bookshelf. The books smelled like paper and a little like sunshine. She ran her paw along the spines. “Morning book goes here. Night book goes here,” she said, placing them in a neat line. Her room felt peaceful, like a quiet song.

A small rattle sounded from her toy drum by the door. Her baby cousin, Poppy, peeked in with bright eyes. “Kiki!” Poppy squeaked. “Play?”

Kiki smiled. “Hi, Poppy! We can play after I finish my room job,” she said kindly. “I am doing it by myself right now.” She pointed to a soft blue rug. “You can watch from the rug. Please keep your toes on the rug. That is my boundary.”

Poppy put her toes on the rug and hugged her drum. Thump-thump, very quiet. Kiki picked up two crayons from under the chair and placed them in a cup. Click. Click. “Almost done,” Kiki said. “Thank you for staying on the rug.”

Poppy nodded. “Okay.”

Kiki opened her dresser and chose her favorite yellow shirt with tiny stars. She liked how it felt smooth and cool. She put it on all by herself. Her ears wiggled with pride. “I did it,” she whispered.

Mama Roo returned with two cups of warm oat milk. The steam curled like tiny clouds. “I see a made bed, tidy blocks, and dressed Kiki,” Mama Roo said. “How do you feel?”

Kiki bounced once, then stood tall. “I feel big and calm,” she said. “I used independence. I set boundaries with Poppy. I made a place for things.”

Mama Roo smiled. “You organized your room. You respected your space and Poppy’s. And you asked yourself what you needed. That is wonderful.”

Kiki handed Poppy a soft teddy from the toy basket. “Poppy, would you like to hold Teddy? It’s okay to say yes or no.”

Poppy hugged the teddy. “Yes!”

Kiki laughed. “Now we can play a quiet game.” She put a puzzle on the rug. “We keep pieces on the rug. That is our puzzle boundary.” The pieces made a soft clack as they fit together. Kiki and Poppy worked side by side. When a piece was tricky, Kiki took a breath. “I try first,” she said, “then I can ask for help.” She wiggled the piece. Click! It fit.

When the puzzle was done, Kiki put the pieces back in the box. “Puzzle lives here,” she said. Poppy helped, dropping the last piece in with a happy plunk.

Kiki sipped her warm oat milk. It tasted sweet and cozy. She looked around at her tidy room, her comfy bed, and her happy cousin. Her heart felt light.

“I learned something,” Kiki said. “When I do things by myself, I feel proud. When I say my boundaries and listen to others’ boundaries, we feel safe. When I put things in their places, my room feels calm.”

Mama Roo nodded. “That is a big lesson for a small joey.”

Kiki hugged Mama Roo, then waved to Coco on the pillow. “Thank you, room,” she said. “We did it together.”

The End.
