# Toby's Big Mistake

Toby the tortoise loved exploring Grandma Rose's cozy pantry. The shelves were filled with colorful jars that sparkled like jewels, and the sweet smell of cookies always made his tummy rumble happily.

One sunny morning, Toby stretched his long neck to reach a shiny jar of honey on the high shelf. "Just a tiny taste," he whispered to himself. But as he stretched higher and higher, his shell bumped into the jar next to it.

CRASH! Down tumbled a beautiful glass jar filled with Grandma Rose's special rainbow sprinkles. The jar shattered on the floor, and tiny colorful sprinkles scattered everywhere like confetti.

"Oh no!" Toby gasped, pulling his head into his shell. The sprinkles crunched under his feet as he tried to hide behind a bag of flour. His heart felt heavy and sad.

Soon, Grandma Rose's footsteps echoed in the hallway. "What happened in here?" she asked, looking at the mess of broken glass and scattered sprinkles.

Toby stayed very still behind the flour bag. Maybe she wouldn't notice him. Maybe she would think the jar fell by itself.

But Toby's wise old heart knew better. His grandmother had always taught him that telling the truth was important, even when it was scary.

Slowly, Toby poked his head out. "Grandma Rose," he said in a small voice, "I made a big mistake. I was trying to reach the honey, and I knocked over your special sprinkles jar. I'm very, very sorry."

Grandma Rose's eyes were kind but serious. "Thank you for telling me the truth, Toby. That took courage. But I'm sad about my sprinkles. I was saving them for your cousin Lily's birthday cake tomorrow."

Toby's eyes filled with tears. "I want to make it better, Grandma. What can I do?"

Grandma Rose sat down on her little stool and thought. "Well, making amends means trying to fix what went wrong. What ideas do you have?"

Toby thought hard, his wise little brain working. "I could help you clean up the mess! And... and maybe we could go to the store together to buy new sprinkles?"

"That's a wonderful start," Grandma Rose smiled. "But the store only has plain sprinkles, not rainbow ones like these."

Toby looked at the colorful mess on the floor. Suddenly, his eyes lit up. "Grandma! What if we pick up all the sprinkles that aren't broken glass? We could wash them and sort them by color. Then we could make our own rainbow sprinkles that are even more special!"

Grandma Rose clapped her hands. "What a creative solution! That shows real integrity, Toby."

Together, they carefully swept up the glass and saved every clean sprinkle they could find. Toby's small paws worked patiently, sorting red sprinkles from blue ones, yellow from green.

"This is actually more fun than buying new ones," Grandma Rose laughed as they worked. "And Lily will love knowing you helped make them extra special."

When they finished, they had five small jars of different colored sprinkles, each one sparkling clean.

"Thank you for being honest and working hard to make things right," Grandma Rose said, giving Toby a warm hug. "That's what making amends really means - taking responsibility and finding ways to help."

Toby smiled, feeling proud and happy. He had learned that mistakes happen, but what matters most is being honest and working together to make things better.

The End.
