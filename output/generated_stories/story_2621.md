# Ellie's Special Bathroom Helper

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved helping others. Her trunk was perfect for reaching high places, and her big heart was even bigger than her ears! One sunny morning, Ellie heard her friend Mouse squeaking sadly from the bathroom.

"Squeak, squeak! Help me, please!" called Mouse in a tiny voice.

Ellie hurried to the bathroom with her big feet going *thump, thump, thump* on the floor. "What's wrong, Mouse?" she asked gently, lowering her trunk to listen better.

Mouse was sitting by the sink, looking very upset. "I can't reach the soap to wash my paws," Mouse squeaked. "And I promised to help make cookies for the forest picnic today!"

Ellie thought carefully. She could easily reach the soap with her long trunk, but she wanted to help Mouse in a special way. "Let's talk about this together," Ellie said kindly. "What if we make something that helps you reach things all by yourself?"

Mouse's whiskers twitched with excitement. "Really? We could make something together?"

"Yes!" trumpeted Ellie softly. "Let's use our words to plan it out first."

They sat on the soft bathroom rug and talked about their ideas. Ellie listened as Mouse explained exactly how tall the sink was and what would help the most. Mouse listened as Ellie shared her ideas about building.

"I have colorful blocks in my toy box," said Ellie. "And you have that pretty ribbon from your art box. If we work together, we can make you special steps!"

Mouse clapped his tiny paws. "That sounds wonderful! But how do we start?"

Ellie smiled. "First, let's talk through our plan step by step." She used her trunk to point as she spoke. "We'll stack the blocks, then tie them together with your ribbon so they're safe and won't fall."

Working together, they gathered the supplies. Ellie carefully placed each colorful block while Mouse directed her. "A little to the left," squeaked Mouse. "Perfect! Now the blue one on top!"

The bathroom filled with happy sounds - Ellie's gentle trumpeting and Mouse's excited squeaking. The blocks made soft *click* sounds as they fit together, and the ribbon made a satisfying *swish* as they tied it tight.

"Let's test our creation!" said Ellie.

Mouse climbed the beautiful block steps. *Step, step, step* went his little paws. "I can reach the soap!" he cheered. "And the towel! And everything!"

But then Mouse had another idea. "Ellie, what about our other small friends? Bunny and Squirrel might need help reaching things too!"

Ellie's eyes sparkled. "You're right! Let's make more step-stools for everyone!"

They spent the morning crafting together, talking through each step and listening to each other's ideas. Soon they had made step-stools in different sizes for all their forest friends.

"Thank you for listening to me and helping me solve my problem," said Mouse, giving Ellie's trunk a tiny hug.

"Thank you for sharing your ideas with me," replied Ellie. "When we use our words and work together, we can help our whole community!"

At the forest picnic that afternoon, all the small animals could reach the cookie-making station thanks to their new step-stools. Everyone thanked Ellie and Mouse for their thoughtful gift.

Ellie learned that the best way to help others is to listen carefully, share ideas with words, and work together as a team.

The End.
