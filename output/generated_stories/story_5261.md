# Poppy Penguin’s Cozy Bedtime Plan

Poppy was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a big, happy waddle. She loved her cozy bedroom. Her bed was soft like a cloud. Her blanket was fluffy and blue. The nightlight glowed warm and gentle, like a tiny moon. The air smelled like clean, cool snow and minty toothpaste.

Tonight, Poppy wanted quiet. She was sleepy. She hugged her favorite pillow and whispered, “I need calm to sleep.”

But her bedroom was very busy. Her stuffed friends were excited. The fuzzy bear bounced on the bed. The crinkly octopus shook its rattly arms. The squeaky bunny made squeak-squeak sounds. The room went thump! rustle! squeak!

Poppy blinked. Her happy waddle slowed. “Oh,” she said softly. “It is very loud.”

“Party time!” squeaked Bunny. “Let’s hop!”

“Wiggle-wiggle!” rattled Octopus.

“Boing!” said Bear, bouncing again.

Poppy’s tummy felt tight. She wanted to be kind. She also needed rest. She took a slow, deep breath, in and out. The nightlight glowed on her feathers. She remembered: I can use my words.

Poppy stood tall on her little penguin feet. “Friends,” she said in a calm voice, “I like you. I like fun. But my body needs quiet now. I feel tired.”

Bunny paused. “Squeak?”

Bear stopped mid-bounce. “Boing?”

Octopus made a small crinkle. “Do you need help?”

“Yes,” Poppy said. “I need quiet sounds and gentle moves.” She pointed to a picture chart on the wall with soft stars. “This is my bedtime plan. It says: hush voice, soft music, slow hugs, lights low.”

Bear frowned a little. “But I want to bounce.”

Poppy nodded. “I hear you. Bouncing is fun. We can bounce tomorrow morning. Right now is bedtime. Can we choose quiet play?”

Bunny wiggled her long ears. “What is quiet play?”

Poppy smiled. “We can do whisper story. We can count tiny stars. We can pat the blanket soft-soft.” She showed them how to pat the blanket with gentle flippers. Pat, pat. It sounded like snowflakes.

Octopus crinkled softly. “I can shake very tiny.” Crinkle… pause… crinkle. The sound was light, like a little rain.

Bear looked at his paws. “I feel disappointed,” he said. “I wanted big boings.”

Poppy walked over and wrapped Bear in her wings. “Thank you for telling me your feeling,” she said. “Your feeling matters. My feeling matters too. We can be different and still be kind.”

Bear took a deep breath. “Okay. I can try small boings. Maybe one tiny bounce?” He bent his knees and made the softest boing. The bed barely moved. Poppy giggled.

“Thank you for trying,” Poppy said. “That is very generous.”

Bunny peered at the nightlight. “Can we share the light?”

“Yes,” Poppy said. She turned the nightlight so it shined on everyone a little. “We can share the glow.”

Poppy put on calm music. It sounded like ocean waves, shhh… shhh… She opened her window a crack. Cool air kissed her cheeks. The room smelled fresh and quiet.

“Let’s do star counting,” Poppy whispered. She stuck glow stars on the ceiling last week. “One… two… three…” The friends counted in tiny voices. Their words were soft and slow.

After counting, Poppy yawned. “I feel better now,” she said. “Thank you for listening to my needs.”

Bunny smiled. “Thank you for telling us your needs. You asked in a kind way.”

Octopus handed Poppy a small shell. “For you,” he said. “A sleepy gift.”

Poppy took the shell and placed it by her pillow. “That is generous,” she said. “I will share my blanket edge.” She tucked a corner over Bunny, and another over Bear. Octopus slid under the warm fold with a happy crinkle.

The room became very calm. The nightlight was a soft circle. The music washed in gentle waves. Poppy’s eyes grew heavy.

Before sleep, Poppy whispered, “We are all different. We like different things. That is okay. We can listen. We can share. We can be kind.”

Bear whispered back, “I learned to wait.”

Bunny whispered, “I learned to whisper.”

Octopus whispered, “I learned to crinkle small.”

Poppy smiled. “I learned to use my words.”

They all took a deep breath together. In… out… The blanket felt warm. The pillow smelled like soap and snow. The cozy bedroom was peaceful at last.

Poppy closed her eyes. “I am glad we listened,” she murmured. “Tonight we chose tolerance. We gave space for each other. We were generous. We used our voices.”

The waves went shhh… shhh…

And all the little friends drifted into soft, happy sleep.

The End.
