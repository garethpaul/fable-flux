# Milo's Calm Morning

Milo the monkey woke up feeling excited. The sun was shining through his bedroom window, and he could smell pancakes cooking downstairs. He wanted to jump and swing and play right away!

"Time for my morning routine," Milo said to himself, remembering what Mama Monkey had taught him. He took three slow, deep breaths. "In... and out... in... and out... in... and out."

Milo walked calmly to the bathroom, even though his feet wanted to run. The bathroom smelled fresh and clean, like the lavender soap Mama loved. The morning light made everything sparkle.

"Good morning, bathroom!" Milo said cheerfully. He looked in the mirror and saw his fuzzy brown fur sticking up in funny directions. "Oh my! I look silly!"

Just then, his little sister Luna appeared in the doorway. She was rubbing her sleepy eyes and yawning.

"Milo, I need to brush my teeth too," Luna said softly.

Milo's first thought was to say "I was here first!" But then he remembered what Papa Monkey had taught him about being kind and sharing.

"Of course, Luna!" Milo said with a warm smile. "We can share the bathroom. There's room for both of us."

Milo moved over to make space at the sink. "Would you like to brush your teeth first, or should we do it together?"

Luna's sleepy face brightened. "Together would be fun!"

They stood side by side at the sink. Milo helped Luna reach her toothbrush and squeezed a tiny bit of strawberry toothpaste on it. The sweet smell made them both smile.

"Let's brush gently," Milo said, showing Luna how to move the brush in small circles. "Gentle circles keep our teeth happy and healthy."

They brushed their teeth together, making quiet humming sounds. Milo felt proud of himself for staying calm and patient, even when Luna accidentally dropped her toothbrush.

"Oops!" Luna giggled.

"No worries," Milo said peacefully. "Accidents happen. Let's rinse it off."

After brushing, they took turns washing their faces with warm, soapy water. The bubbles felt soft and tickly on their fur.

"Now for hair brushing," Milo said. He gently brushed Luna's fur first, being extra careful around her ears. "There! You look beautiful, sister."

"Thank you, Milo! You're the best big brother," Luna said, giving him a gentle hug.

When Luna left to get dressed, Milo finished his own grooming routine. He brushed his fur slowly and carefully, feeling calm and ready for the day.

Mama Monkey peeked into the bathroom. "Milo, I heard you and Luna sharing so nicely. I'm very proud of how patient and kind you were."

"It felt good to be calm and helpful," Milo said. "When I take deep breaths and move slowly, everything feels easier."

"That's a very wise discovery," Mama said, giving him a warm hug. "Calmness is like a superpower that helps us be our best selves."

Milo nodded, feeling happy and peaceful inside. He had learned that being calm and respectful made morning routines much more pleasant for everyone.

As he walked downstairs for breakfast, Milo felt proud of his calm morning. Those delicious pancakes would taste even better because he had started his day with kindness and patience.

The End.
