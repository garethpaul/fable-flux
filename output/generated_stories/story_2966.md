# Bubbles in the Attic

Binky was a playful dolphin with bright, curious eyes and a giggle that sounded like tiny splashes. One sunny afternoon, Binky flapped his smooth fins and peeked into a quiet attic. The air felt cool and a little dusty. Sunbeams made golden stripes on old boxes. The wooden floor felt warm under Binky’s belly, and the air smelled like pine and a bit like crayons.

“Oooh,” Binky whispered. “A secret art room!”

In the corner, Binky found a big box of art things: soft brushes, chunky crayons, sticky glue, and a rainbow of paper. There was also a round mirror with a shiny silver frame, a small drum, and a little fan that hummed like a bee when it was on.

Binky’s best friends, Tilly the tiny turtle and Pip the puffball kitten, climbed up the stairs behind him. Tilly’s shell made a soft tap-tap-tap on the steps. Pip’s paws went pitter-patter. Their noses twitched. They smelled the dusty attic and sweet glue, too.

“Let’s make art!” Pip purred.

“Let’s make music!” Tilly chirped.

Binky flapped and laughed. “Let’s do both!”

They made a plan. “We will take turns,” Binky said. “First, we draw. Then we drum. Then we dance with the fan. Everyone gets a turn.”

Tilly nodded. “Taking turns is kind.”

Pip smiled. “I like kind.”

They started with drawing. Binky picked a blue crayon. He loved blue. He drew big ocean waves and a sunny sky. Pip picked pink. She drew a fluffy cloud. Tilly chose green and made a tiny turtle. The crayons felt waxy in their hands and paws. The paper made a soft scratch-scratch sound.

When it was time to drum, Binky remembered the plan. “Tilly’s turn first,” he said.

Tilly tapped the drum with gentle claws. Tum-tum-tum. Pip clapped along. Clap, clap! Binky swished his tail like a ribbon and hummed, “La-la-la!”

Then Pip took her turn. She played fast. Tum-tum-tum-tum! The attic vibrated a tiny bit, and the drum felt bouncy under her paws.

“Now it’s my turn,” Binky said. He lifted his fin and—oh no!—his fin bumped the round mirror. The mirror wobbled. Wobble… wobble… CRACK!

The sound was sharp and scary. Tiny lines ran across the glass like spider webs. Binky’s heart thumped. His tummy felt tight, like a knot. He swallowed.

“My mirror!” Tilly squeaked. “It was my grandma’s!”

Pip’s eyes went big. “Oh no, Binky!”

Binky felt hot tears. His cheeks felt warm like sunshine. “I’m sorry,” he whispered. “I didn’t mean to. I forgot to watch my fin.” He took a deep breath. “I should have moved slower. I will fix this. I will be honest.”

Tilly’s face was scrunched and red. She stamped one little foot. “I feel mad,” she said. Her voice was loud. “I feel big mad!”

Binky nodded. “It’s okay to feel mad,” he said softly. “I can wait. I can listen.”

Pip put a paw on Tilly’s shoulder. “Let’s take a calm breath,” she said. “In…and out.” They all breathed together. In…and out. The attic felt a little softer.

Tilly wiped a tear. “I am still sad,” she said, “but I can talk.”

Binky looked into Tilly’s eyes. “I am sorry,” he said again, slow and clear. “I will not hide it. I will tell the grown-ups. I want to help make it better.”

“That is honest,” Tilly said. She touched the crack with her claw. “Maybe we can make art with it.”

Pip’s whiskers quivered. “A shiny collage!”

Binky’s eyes lit up. “A forgiveness frame,” he said. “We can make something new together.”

They found safe gloves in a drawer and a roll of sticky tape. They asked a grown-up to help with the sharp parts, because sharp things are not for little friends. With careful hands, they placed the broken glass behind clear plastic from an old picture sleeve. They glued colorful paper around the edge—blue waves, pink clouds, and a tiny green turtle. Pip added a heart. Tilly drew a small sun. Binky wrote, “We forgive and we fix,” in big block letters, with help on the spelling.

Tilly took a slow breath. “Binky, I forgive you,” she said. Her voice was calm now. “Thank you for telling the truth.”

Binky’s tummy knot untied. He felt light, like he was swimming. “Thank you for forgiving me.”

Pip smiled. “We took turns helping, too,” she said. “Now it’s dance time!”

They turned on the little fan. It hummed like a bee. Whooooosh. The air tickled their faces. The paper scraps fluttered like leaves. Binky swished his tail and spun. Tilly did tiny turtle steps. Pip leaped and landed soft. They laughed together. Their laughter sounded like bells and bubbles.

When the dance was done, they hung the forgiveness frame on the attic wall. The crack looked like a silver river. It shone in the sunbeams.

Binky looked at his friends. “Today I learned,” he said, “that telling the truth is brave, that taking turns is kind, and that anger can get smaller with calm breaths. Most of all, forgiveness makes hearts feel better.”

Tilly nodded. “Forgiveness is love.”

Pip purred. “And love makes art.”

They packed up the crayons and the drum. They cleaned the floor. The attic felt warm and happy, like a hug.

“Daily adventures can be sweet,” Binky said, “when we share, say sorry, and forgive.”

All three friends smiled. “Yes,” they said together.

The End.
