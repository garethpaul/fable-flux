# Finn the Fox Finds His Voice

Finn was a curious little fox kit with bright, pointy ears and a soft, orange tail. Today, he sat in the family car, snug in his seat. The car hummed and purred. The sun warmed the windows. Finn pressed his nose to the cool glass and watched trees zip by like green brushes.

Mama Fox drove and hummed a happy tune. Papa Fox sat in the front, tapping the map with his paw. “We’re going to the park,” Papa said. “They have a Build-It Box with blocks!”

Finn’s paws tingled. “Blocks!” he squeaked. “I want to build a tall tower.”

The car turned. The tires made a shhh sound on the road. Finn smelled the sweet scent of pine trees and the warm, cozy smell of the car seats. He wiggled with joy. Then he felt something else inside—small and fluttery. What if bigger kids took all the blocks? What if he didn’t get to build?

Finn took a brave breath. He remembered Mama’s words: “Use your strong, kind voice.”

“I feel a little nervous,” Finn said softly.

Mama looked in the mirror and gave a gentle smile. “Thank you for telling me, Finn. That is being assertive. Assertive means you say what you need in a calm, kind way.”

Papa nodded. “Your voice matters, little builder.”

At the park, the car door clicked open. Fresh grass smelled like green rain. Finn heard children laughing and blocks clacking. The Build-It Box was big and blue, filled with colorful blocks—red, yellow, blue, and green. The sun made the blocks shiny and warm to the touch.

Two raccoon kits were already stacking blocks. “Let’s make a fort!” one said. A rabbit and a chipmunk hopped over too. The box was busy!

Finn’s heart pitter-pattered. He held his paws to his chest. He wanted to join. He wanted to build. He took a slow, steady breath. “Strong, kind voice,” he whispered.

Finn stepped closer. His tail swished. “Hello,” he said, a little wobbly. “I’m Finn. I want to build a tall tower. Can I please help?”

The raccoon kits looked up. One smiled. “Hi, Finn! I’m Rocket. This is Rilla. We’re making a fort. Maybe the fort needs a tower!”

Finn’s chest felt warm and proud. He had asked. He had used his voice. “Yes, a tower on the fort!”

The rabbit wiggled her nose. “I like doors,” she said. “Can we make a door?”

The chipmunk clapped. “And a window!”

Finn looked at the blocks. He felt his courage growing. “We can do it if we work together,” he said. “Let’s plan.” The word plan felt big and smart in his mouth.

They sat around the box. The blocks made click, clack sounds. Finn’s paws felt the bumpy edges and the smooth tops. He showed a red block. “Red blocks for the base,” he said. “Yellow blocks for the tall tower.” He stood up straight and showed how high the tower could go. “Blue blocks for a strong door. Green blocks for the window.”

Rocket nodded. “Good plan.”

Rilla added, “I can carry the heavy blocks. I’m strong.”

The rabbit smiled. “I can build the door.”

The chipmunk chirped, “I can count blocks!”

Finn took another brave breath. “I can be the tower builder,” he said clearly. “I will stack slowly and safely.”

They began to build. The base went down—one, two, three, four red blocks. The tower started to rise—yellow on yellow. The door took shape with sturdy blue. The green window peeked like a bright leaf.

Click. Clack. Tap. The sounds made a happy building song. Warm sun kissed Finn’s fur. A light breeze smelled like flowers. Finn’s tower wobbled a tiny bit. He paused. “I need a steady paw,” he said, calm and firm. “Can someone help hold it?”

“I can!” said Rocket, stepping in.

“Thank you,” said Finn. He put another yellow block on top. The tower stood tall and straight.

A dog pup ran by and bumped the base. The tower shook—shiver, shiver. Finn’s ears twitched. He felt a little scared. He wanted to shout, “Stop!” But he remembered: strong and kind. He stood tall and said, “Excuse me, please be careful. We are building.”

The pup slowed and wagged. “Okay! Sorry!”

Finn smiled. “Thank you.”

The tower reached as high as Finn’s ears, then his nose, then even higher—almost to Papa’s chin! The door opened and closed. The window let sunbeams in. It was a fort-tower, a cozy place to play.

“Team high-five!” Rilla cheered.

Finn lifted his paw. “We did it together!”

Mama and Papa came over. “What a wonderful build,” Mama said. “Finn, I heard your strong, kind voice.”

Papa nodded proudly. “You were assertive. You shared your plan, asked for help, and spoke up safely.”

Finn’s heart felt big and bright. “When I say what I need,” he said, “we can build great things.”

They played in the fort-tower. The door went swish. The window made square shadows. The friends laughed and took turns. Finn felt calm and brave. He learned that being assertive is not being bossy or loud. It is using a clear, kind voice to share feelings and needs. It helps everyone work together.

As the sun dipped low, Finn gave the tower a gentle pat. “Thank you, tower,” he whispered. “Thank you, team.”

On the ride home, the car hummed again. Finn leaned back, cozy and sleepy. He smiled at the window and watched the trees swish by. “Mama,” he said, “tomorrow I will use my strong, kind voice again.”

Mama squeezed his paw. “That’s a wonderful plan, little builder.”

The End.
