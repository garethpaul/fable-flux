# Benny Beaver Discovers a Better Way

Benny was a busy beaver with a cozy home office. His desk was smooth and wooden. His lamp made a soft, warm glow. His chair was squeaky, squeak-squeak! Benny loved to tap-tap-tap on his little keyboard and stack papers neatly. He liked to help friends with big and small jobs. He was always busy, busy, busy!

One sunny morning, Benny heard a ping on his computer. “Ping! New message!” said the screen. A note popped up from Owl Officer, who kept the forest fair. “Dear Benny,” it read, “please make a poster about forest rules. We need justice and kindness.”

Benny smiled. “I can do that!” he said. “I will work fast. I will make it perfect.”

He took a deep breath. He smelled pine and fresh paper. He felt his tail swish-swish on the chair. He opened a box of bright markers—red, blue, yellow, and green. He took out a big sheet of paper. “This will be art!” Benny said proudly.

Just then, his cousin Bella poked in her head. “Hi, Benny! Can I help?” she asked.

Benny shook his head. “No, Bella. I am too busy. I can do it alone. Please don’t touch my things.”

Bella frowned a little, but she nodded. “Okay,” she said softly. “I’ll be in the kitchen drawing.”

Benny started to draw. He drew tall trees and a clear stream. He wrote: “Be kind. Take turns. Keep water clean.” He hummed a happy tune. But then, his marker slipped. Squiggle! A big blue line went right over the word “kind.”

“Oh no!” Benny said. His cheeks felt hot. He wanted to finish fast, but now the poster looked messy. He grabbed another marker. Scribble-scribble! The line got bigger. The paper wrinkled. “Oh no, oh no!”

Benny’s heart thumped. He forgot to drink his water. He forgot to blink. His head felt tight. His tail drooped. He looked at the clock. Tick-tock, tick-tock. The poster was due soon.

Bella walked in with a shy smile. “Benny, I made a flower drawing,” she said. “Do you want to see?”

“I don’t have time!” Benny snapped. “I made a mistake. Go away!”

Bella’s eyes got wet. “Okay,” she whispered, and she turned to go.

Benny looked at her small, sad shoulders. He listened to the quiet room. He heard his squeaky chair and his fast, fast breath. He put his paw on his chest and took a slow breath in, and a slow breath out. In… out… The air smelled like pine again. His belly felt softer.

“I’m sorry, Bella,” Benny said. “I was unkind. That was not fair. Can you please forgive me?”

Bella turned back. She nodded and gave a tiny smile. “I forgive you,” she said. “We can fix it. We can discover a better way.”

Benny blinked. “Discover?” he asked.

“Yes!” Bella said. “We can try new things and find what works. That’s discovery! It’s like exploring with your eyes and your heart.”

Benny felt a warm bubble of hope. “Okay,” he said. “Let’s discover.”

They looked closely at the messy poster. Bella touched the wrinkled paper. “It feels bumpy,” she said. “Let’s smooth it with our paws.” Swish-smooth, swish-smooth. The wrinkles calmed down.

Benny sniffed the markers. “They smell like berries,” he giggled. “But they are slippery. Maybe we can use paint stamps!”

Bella clapped. “I have leaf stamps!” She ran to the kitchen and came back with leaf shapes, a sponge, and paints. The paints were bright and cool. Plop, plop went the sponge in the paint tray.

Together, they stamped green leaves over the squiggle. Tap-tap, press-press. The blue line turned into a little river. Benny added a beaver brushing teeth by the river. “Wellness,” he said. “We take care of our bodies.”

Bella stamped a heart near two animals sharing a log. “Forgiveness,” she said. “We say sorry and forgive.”

Benny drew a wise owl with a tiny badge. “Justice,” he said. “We follow fair rules.”

Bella smiled. “This is art,” she said. “And it tells a story.”

Benny read the words out loud: “Be kind. Take turns. Keep water clean.” He added one more line: “When we make mistakes, we slow down, breathe, and discover a better way.”

The room felt calm. The lamp glowed warm and soft. The paint smelled gentle. Benny took a big sip of water. “Ahhh,” he said. His tail made a happy thump-thump.

They sent a picture to Owl Officer. Soon they heard, “Ping! New message!” The screen read, “Wonderful work, Benny and Bella! Your art shows kindness and fairness. Thank you.”

Benny grinned. “We did it,” he said. “I learned something today.”

“What did you discover?” asked Bella.

Benny tapped his chin. “I discovered that help can make art better. I discovered that a mistake can become something new. I discovered that saying sorry and forgiving brings peace. And I discovered that taking a breath helps my body and my mind.”

Bella nodded. “Me too,” she said.

Benny put the poster to dry. The colors shone. The leaves looked fresh. The river looked bright.

“Next time,” Benny said, “I will invite you to help from the start.”

Bella giggled. “I’ll bring more stamps!”

They hugged. The home office felt happy. The chair went squeak-squeak in a friendly way. The clock went tick-tock, nice and slow.

The End.
