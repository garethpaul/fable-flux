# Whiskers Builds a Ride-Along City

The family car hummed softly. The seats were warm, and the air smelled like oranges from a little air freshener. Outside the window, trees whooshed by in a green blur. Inside, a clever cat named Whiskers sat in his booster basket with a little seatbelt clip.

Whiskers flicked his ears. “I feel wiggly,” he said. “I want to jump! I want to climb!”

Mama turned the wheel gently. “We are moving, Whiskers,” she said in a kind voice. “But we must stay buckled in the car. It keeps us safe.”

Whiskers sighed. His paws itched to move. His tail swished back and forth. He didn’t want to be still, and his tummy felt tight like a knotted string.

“I can’t do anything,” he mewed. “This is too hard.”

Papa glanced in the mirror. “Hmm,” he said. “I hear your busy body. Let’s help it calm and play in a car-safe way.”

Whiskers took a small breath in through his nose. He let it out slowly through his mouth. “Okay,” he said. “But how?”

On the seat beside him was a soft bag of safe car toys. It made a cozy rustle. Mama said, “You can use what’s in your bag. You can make your own fun with your clever brain.”

Whiskers peeked in the bag. He found fuzzy pipe cleaners, felt squares, chunky blocks with Velcro, and two stickers. He also had a small cardboard box for snacks. The box was empty now.

His whiskers twitched. “I can build!” he purred. “I can make a Ride-Along City!”

He set the empty box on his lap. He pressed a felt square on top. Rip, rip went the Velcro block sound as he pulled and stuck pieces. The car purred. The tires sang a low shhh on the road. The smells of oranges and a tiny hint of peanut butter swirled in the air.

Papa said, “Tell us your plan.”

Whiskers’ eyes shone. “First, I build a road on my box. Then, I make buildings that don’t fall when the car bumps.”

Mama smiled. “What a creative idea.”

Whiskers tried to stack three blocks high. The car rolled over a bumpy patch—thump-thump! The tower wobbled and tumbled. “Oh no!” Whiskers cried. His tail frizzed.

He felt mad heat in his cheeks. His paws curled. He wanted to push the blocks away.

Mama’s voice was soft like a blanket. “Pause. Put your paws on your belly. Breathe in… one, two. Breathe out… one, two.”

Whiskers pressed his paws to his soft fur. He breathed in slowly. He breathed out slowly. His body felt less tight.

“I can try again,” he said. “I can change my plan.”

He looked at the blocks. He looked at the felt. Tap, tap went his paw as he thought. “I will build wide, not tall,” he said. “I will give my buildings flat feet.”

He placed a big felt square as a base. He stuck Velcro blocks side by side like little houses. He bent a blue pipe cleaner into a gentle arch and tucked the ends under two blocks. “A bridge!” he purred. “A bendy bridge stays safe.”

The car turned. The sunlight slid across the seat. His bridge stayed put. “It works!” Whiskers said with a happy chirp.

He added more. He pressed the stickers on the box road—one circle was a pond, and one star was a playground. He rolled a round bottle cap like a tiny car. Tip-tap, tip-tap went his paw as the cap drove around the city. He made swishy sounds with his mouth. “Vrrrroom. Beep-beep. Slow at the bridge. Stop at the star.”

His paws moved, and his body felt busy but safe. “My body wants to move,” he said, “and my brain helps it move in a seat-belt way.”

Papa nodded. “That is self-control. You are using your energy in a safe, smart way.”

Suddenly, the cap-car bumped the bridge and slid to the felt pond. Splash! Whiskers giggled. “The car got wet. It needs a towel.”

He folded a tiny square of tissue into a towel. Pat, pat, pat. “All dry,” he said. “Let’s build a boat.” He bent a silver pipe cleaner into a circle and set it on the pond. He blew gently—fooo—and the boat drifted a little. “Wind!” he whispered.

A small voice came from the back seat next to him. It was Baby Mina, in her own seat. “Me play?” she asked, wiggling her toes.

Whiskers blinked. He liked his city. It felt special. He held it close for a moment. Then his ears softened. “Yes,” he said. “We can build together.” He handed Mina a soft block. “You can make a park.”

Mina pressed the block down. “Park,” she said, smiling big.

Whiskers showed her how to press, not push. “Gentle paws,” he said. “Flat feet for the building.” Mina copied him. Together, they built a tiny slide with two blocks and a felt ramp. “Weee!” they both said.

Mama listened to their happy sounds. “I love your teamwork,” she said. “You turned a hard moment into a creative game.”

Whiskers’ body felt calm and proud. His tail made a slow, happy curl. He looked at Mina’s grin. He looked at his strong, wide bridge. He looked at the road with stars and circles. He had built a whole city in his lap while the family car hummed along.

He patted his chest. “When I feel wiggly, I can breathe, think, and build,” he said. “I can use my creativity to help my body.”

Papa smiled in the mirror. “That is a big skill, Whiskers.”

The car rolled into the driveway. Click went the seat belts. Whiskers carefully lifted his Ride-Along City. “We can keep building inside,” he said. “I have ideas!”

“Me too!” Mina cheered.

Whiskers purred. “We can make room for everyone.”

The End.
