# Fawn's Special Guest Room

Little Fawn lived in a cozy house at the edge of the forest with her mama and papa. She had big brown eyes that sparkled like dewdrops and soft spotted fur that felt like velvet. Today was very special because Grandma Deer was coming to visit!

"Fawn, sweetie," called Mama from downstairs. "Grandma will sleep in the guest room. Can you help make it nice for her?"

Fawn bounced on her tiny hooves. "Yes, Mama! I want Grandma to feel happy!"

She tiptoed into the guest room and gasped. "Oh my!" The room was very messy. Old magazines were scattered on the floor like fallen leaves. Empty boxes sat in corners gathering dust. Toys from last winter were piled on the bed in a wobbly mountain.

Fawn's ears drooped. "This doesn't look cozy at all. Grandma might feel sad in here."

She sat down and thought hard. "What would make Grandma smile?" Then she remembered how Grandma always said, "A tidy space makes a happy heart."

"I know what to do!" Fawn said, standing up tall.

First, she picked up all the magazines. Her small hooves made soft patting sounds on the wooden floor. "These pictures are pretty, but they're old," she said, looking at each one carefully. "Maybe someone else would like them." She made a neat pile to give to the library.

Next, she tackled the empty boxes. Some were torn and crinkled. "You can become something new," she whispered gently, putting them in the recycling bin. The good boxes she folded flat and stacked neatly in the closet.

The toys were tricky because they brought back happy memories. Fawn held a stuffed bunny and smiled. "I remember playing with you, but now other little ones need toys too." She sorted them into two piles - some to keep and some to share with children who didn't have many toys.

As she worked, Fawn felt warm and tingly inside. "Helping feels good," she said to herself.

When the floor was clear, Fawn fluffed the pillows until they looked like fluffy clouds. She smoothed the soft blue blanket and placed Grandma's favorite lavender soap on the nightstand. The sweet smell filled the room like a gentle hug.

"Almost done!" Fawn said, stepping back to look.

She picked wildflowers from the garden - purple violets and yellow buttercups. Their sweet smell made her nose twitch happily. She put them in a small vase by the window where morning sunlight would make them glow.

Just then, she heard a car in the driveway. "Grandma's here!" Fawn called, running downstairs.

Grandma Deer had silver fur and kind eyes that crinkled when she smiled. "My dear little Fawn! I've missed you so much!"

After warm hugs and dinner, Fawn led Grandma upstairs. "I made your room special!"

Grandma stepped inside and her eyes grew wide. "Oh, Fawn! This room feels like a warm hug. Everything is so neat and peaceful."

"I wanted you to feel happy here," Fawn said, snuggling close. "I organized everything and shared things with others who need them."

"You know what?" Grandma said, stroking Fawn's spotted fur. "The most beautiful thing in this room is your kind heart. When we organize with love and think of others, we make the whole world a little brighter."

That night, as Fawn drifted off to sleep, she felt proud and happy. She had learned that organizing wasn't just about making things neat - it was about showing love and caring for others.

The End.
