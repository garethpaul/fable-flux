# Milo's Big Helper Day

Milo the mouse peeked his tiny whiskers around the corner of the dining room. The morning sun streamed through the big window, making golden patches on the wooden floor. But oh my! What a mess he saw.

Crumbs were scattered everywhere like tiny pebbles. Napkins lay crumpled on chairs. The salt and pepper shakers stood far apart from each other, looking lonely.

"Good morning, Milo!" called Mama Mouse from the kitchen. "Would you like to help me clean up after breakfast?"

Milo's ears perked up. "Yes, Mama! I want to help!"

He scurried into the dining room, his little paws pattering softly on the floor. First, he noticed the sad-looking salt and pepper shakers.

"Don't worry, little friends," Milo squeaked gently. "You belong together!" He pushed the salt shaker with his tiny shoulder - push, push, push - until it sat right next to the pepper shaker. "There! Now you're not lonely anymore."

Next, Milo saw all the crumbs scattered about. They looked like a messy puzzle that needed solving. He found a small brush that was just his size.

"Sweep, sweep, sweep!" Milo sang as he brushed the crumbs into a neat little pile. The bristles tickled his paws, making him giggle. "All the crumbs are going to their new home in the dustpan!"

Mama Mouse watched from the doorway, her heart warm with pride. "You're being such a wonderful helper, Milo!"

But then Milo noticed something that made his whiskers twitch with concern. A napkin had fallen under the big dining table, and it looked scared and forgotten in the shadows.

"Oh no!" Milo gasped. "Poor napkin! You must be frightened all alone under there."

Even though the space under the table was dark and a little scary for a small mouse, Milo knew he had to help. He took a deep breath and crawled under the table.

"It's okay, napkin," he whispered softly. "I'm here to help you." His little heart beat fast, but he felt brave knowing he was doing something kind.

Carefully, Milo picked up the napkin with his teeth and pulled it out into the bright dining room. Then he folded it neatly - fold, fold, fold - just like Mama had taught him.

"There you go!" Milo said, placing the napkin in its proper spot. "Now you're clean and ready for the next meal!"

As Milo worked, something wonderful happened. The dining room began to sparkle and shine. The table gleamed in the sunlight. The chairs stood straight and proud. Everything had its special place.

"Milo, come see!" called Papa Mouse, entering the room. His eyes grew wide with amazement. "Did you help make our dining room so beautiful?"

Milo's chest puffed up with pride, but he felt something even better than proud - he felt happy and warm inside, like drinking hot cocoa on a cold day.

"I helped everything find where it belongs," Milo said with a big smile. "The salt and pepper are friends again, the crumbs found their way home, and the napkin isn't scared anymore!"

Mama and Papa Mouse hugged their little helper tight. "When we all work together and help each other," Mama said, "we build something wonderful - a happy, clean home where everyone feels loved."

From that day on, Milo always remembered that helping with chores wasn't just about cleaning up. It was about caring for others and building a happy home together, one small act of kindness at a time.

The End.
