# Rocky's Musical Mess

Rocky the raccoon loved making music in Grandpa Joe's garage workshop. The cozy space smelled like sawdust and oil, and it was filled with wonderful things that made the most amazing sounds when Rocky tapped them with his tiny paws.

*Ting-ting-ting!* went the metal tools hanging on the wall.
*Boom-boom-boom!* echoed the empty paint cans.
*Clang-clang-clang!* sang the wrenches in their box.

Every day after lunch, Rocky would scamper into the garage and create his own special concerts. But Rocky had one big problem – he never put anything back where it belonged.

"I'll clean up later," Rocky would say to himself as he left hammers on the floor and scattered screws everywhere. "Music time is too much fun to stop!"

One sunny Tuesday, Rocky bounded into the garage, ready for his daily concert. But something was wrong. Grandpa Joe was sitting on his workbench, looking very sad.

"What's wrong, Grandpa Joe?" Rocky asked, his fluffy tail twitching with worry.

"Oh, Rocky," sighed Grandpa Joe. "I can't find my special wrench anywhere. Mrs. Chen's bicycle has a wobbly wheel, and I promised to fix it today. Without my wrench, I can't help her."

Rocky's tummy felt funny and tight. He looked around the messy garage. Tools were scattered on the floor like fallen leaves. Paint cans sat in the wrong places. And somewhere in all that mess was Grandpa Joe's special wrench.

"This is my fault," Rocky whispered, his ears drooping. "I made music with everything and didn't put anything back."

Rocky took a deep breath and thought hard. *What should I do?* he wondered. *I could run away and hide, or I could stay and help fix this mess.*

"Grandpa Joe," Rocky said bravely, "I think I know where your wrench might be. I used it yesterday for my drum solo. Let me help you find it!"

Together, they began searching. Rocky carefully picked up each tool and put it back in its proper place on the pegboard. The screws went back in their little containers. The paint cans returned to their shelf.

"There it is!" Rocky squeaked with joy. The special wrench was hiding under a pile of washers near the workbench.

Grandpa Joe's face lit up like sunshine. "Thank you, Rocky! You're such a good helper."

"I'm sorry I made such a mess," Rocky said, hanging his head. "I was having so much fun making music that I forgot to be responsible."

Grandpa Joe gave Rocky a gentle pat. "Everyone makes mistakes, little friend. What matters is that you chose to fix the problem instead of running away. That shows you're growing up to be very responsible."

From that day on, Rocky still made beautiful music in the garage workshop. But now he had a new song to sing while he played: the "Clean-Up Song."

*"Put it back, put it back, where it goes!"* Rocky would sing as he returned each tool to its home. *"Clean and tidy, that's how it goes!"*

Mrs. Chen got her bicycle fixed, Grandpa Joe could always find his tools, and Rocky learned that being responsible made his music sound even sweeter.

The End.
