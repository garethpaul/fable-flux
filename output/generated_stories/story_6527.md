# Polly's Big Truth

In a busy basement filled with boxes, tools, and old toys, lived a beautiful green parrot named Polly. The basement was always buzzing with activity as Mama and Papa worked on their projects. Polly loved watching them paint, build, and create wonderful things.

*Tap, tap, tap* went Papa's hammer. *Whirr, whirr* went Mama's drill. The basement smelled like fresh wood and paint, and colorful feathers from Polly's bright wings would sometimes float through the dusty air.

One sunny morning, Polly noticed something special through the small basement window. Outside, children were playing in the park across the street. They were running, laughing, and flying colorful kites high in the blue sky.

"Oh my!" squawked Polly. "That looks like so much fun! I wish I could play outside too."

But Polly had a problem. She had never told Mama and Papa that she wanted to go outside. You see, Polly was a little scared. What if they said no? What if the big world was too scary for a basement parrot?

Day after day, Polly watched the children play. She saw them ride bikes, play catch, and have picnics on soft green grass. Her heart felt heavy, like a stone in her chest.

"Why do you look so sad, Polly?" asked Mama one afternoon, wiping paint from her hands with a colorful rag.

Polly's feathers ruffled nervously. "Oh, nothing, Mama. I'm just... um... tired from all the noise down here." 

But that wasn't the truth, and Polly knew it. Her tummy felt funny when she didn't tell the truth.

Papa looked up from his workbench. "Maybe you need some fresh air, little one. This basement can get stuffy with all our projects."

Polly's heart jumped! This was her chance! But still, she felt scared to ask for what she really wanted.

The next day, Polly watched a little girl teaching her grandmother how to fly a kite. The grandmother was learning something new, and she looked so happy! Polly realized that being honest about wanting to try new things was important.

"Mama! Papa!" Polly called out, her voice clear and strong. "I need to tell you something true!"

Mama and Papa stopped their work and looked at their colorful feathered friend.

"I've been watching the children play outside," Polly said, her voice a little shaky. "I really, really want to try playing in the park too. I want to feel the warm sunshine on my feathers and see how high I can fly!"

Mama smiled the biggest smile. "Oh, Polly! Why didn't you tell us sooner?"

"I was scared you might say no," Polly admitted, feeling much better now that she had told the truth.

Papa chuckled warmly. "Sweet Polly, we would love to take you outside! We just didn't know you wanted to explore beyond our busy basement."

That very afternoon, Mama and Papa took Polly to the park. Polly felt the warm sunshine on her bright green feathers and the gentle breeze under her wings. She flew higher than she ever had before, making friends with other birds and even helping a little boy find his lost ball.

"Thank you for being honest with us, Polly," said Mama as they watched the sunset paint the sky in beautiful colors.

Polly learned that telling the truth, even when it feels scary, always leads to wonderful things. From that day on, she spent her mornings helping in the busy basement and her afternoons exploring the great big world outside.

The End.
