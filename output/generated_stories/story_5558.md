# Toby the Brave Little Turtle

On a sunny morning, a little green turtle named Toby sat quietly on Mrs. Garcia's peaceful front porch. The wooden boards felt warm under his shell from the golden sunshine. Pretty pink flowers bloomed in pots all around him, and he could smell their sweet scent in the gentle breeze.

Toby loved sitting on the porch, but he had a big problem. He was very, very shy. When other animals came to visit, Toby would quickly pull his head and legs into his shell and hide.

"Good morning, Toby!" chirped Rosie the red robin as she landed on the porch railing. Her bright feathers sparkled in the sunlight.

*Pop!* Toby disappeared into his shell faster than a blink.

"Oh dear," sighed Mrs. Garcia, the kind lady who lived in the house. She had gray hair and wore a cozy blue sweater. "Toby, sweet turtle, you're missing all the fun by hiding in your shell."

From inside his shell, Toby's voice sounded muffled and small. "But I'm scared, Mrs. Garcia. What if the other animals don't like me? What if I say something silly?"

Mrs. Garcia sat down in her rocking chair, which creaked softly as it moved back and forth. "You know, Toby, being brave doesn't mean you're never scared. Being brave means trying even when you feel scared."

Toby peeked one eye out of his shell. "Really?"

"Really," smiled Mrs. Garcia. "Would you like to hear about my plan to help you practice being brave?"

Toby's head came out a little more. "What kind of plan?"

"Well," said Mrs. Garcia, "we could start small. Tomorrow morning, when Rosie comes to visit, you could try just saying 'hello.' Just one little word. If that feels too scary, you can go back in your shell."

Toby thought about this. One word didn't sound too hard. "Just 'hello'?"

"Just 'hello,'" nodded Mrs. Garcia. "And if that goes well, maybe the next day you could try two words."

That night, Toby practiced in the mirror. "Hello. Hello. Hello." His voice got a little stronger each time.

The next morning, Rosie landed on the porch railing again. "Good morning, Toby!"

Toby's heart beat fast like a tiny drum. He wanted to hide, but he remembered Mrs. Garcia's words. Being brave means trying even when you feel scared.

Taking a deep breath, Toby whispered, "H-hello, Rosie."

Rosie's eyes lit up like stars. "Oh, Toby! How wonderful to hear your voice! You have such a nice, gentle way of speaking."

Toby felt warm and happy inside. He didn't hide in his shell at all!

Over the next few days, Toby kept practicing. First "hello," then "hello, how are you?" Then he shared that he liked the smell of the flowers. Soon, he was having whole conversations with Rosie and the other porch visitors.

"I'm so proud of you, Toby," said Mrs. Garcia one sunny afternoon. "You didn't give up, even when you felt scared. You kept trying, and look how much you've grown!"

Toby beamed with pride. "I learned that my shell is good for keeping me safe, but staying inside it all the time means I miss out on making friends. Now I know I can be brave, one small step at a time!"

From that day on, Toby became known as the friendliest turtle on the whole street, and his favorite spot was still the peaceful front porch where he learned to be brave.

The End.
