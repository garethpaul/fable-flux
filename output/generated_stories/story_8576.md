# Leo's Big Art Room Adventure

Leo was a brave little lion cub with a fluffy golden mane and bright, curious eyes. Today was his first day at the creative art room in his school, and he was so excited! The room smelled like sweet crayons and fresh paper. Colorful paintings hung on every wall, and there were tables covered with brushes, paints, and sparkly stickers.

"Good morning, everyone!" said Miss Rabbit, their art teacher. "Today we're going to make beautiful nature pictures!"

Leo bounced on his paws. He loved drawing trees and flowers! But when he looked around, he saw something that made his whiskers droop. There was only one green crayon left, and it was sitting right next to Emma the elephant.

"Oh no," Leo whispered to himself. "I really, really need that green crayon to draw my favorite oak tree!"

Leo's first idea was to just take the crayon when Emma wasn't looking. But then he remembered what his mama had taught him about being brave and kind. Taking things wasn't the right way to solve problems.

His second idea was to roar very loudly until Emma gave him the crayon. But that didn't seem very nice either, and it might hurt Emma's big, floppy ears.

Leo took a deep breath and walked over to Emma's table. His paws felt a little shaky, but he was determined to be brave.

"Hi Emma," Leo said in his friendliest voice. "I see you have the green crayon. I really need it to draw my special tree. Could we maybe share it?"

Emma looked up from her drawing of purple butterflies. "But Leo, I need it too! I want to draw grass for my butterflies to land on."

Leo's tail twitched as he thought hard. Then his eyes lit up with an idea!

"What if we take turns?" Leo suggested, his voice getting more excited. "You could use it for five minutes, then I could use it for five minutes. We could even help each other with our pictures!"

Emma's trunk swayed as she considered this. "That sounds fair! But what if we run out of time?"

Leo had another wonderful idea. "Oh! What if we work together on one big picture? We could draw a beautiful garden with trees AND grass AND butterflies! Then we could both use the green crayon whenever we need it!"

Emma clapped her trunk against her knee. "That's a great idea, Leo! We could make it even more special!"

"How?" Leo asked, his golden eyes sparkling.

"Well," Emma said with a giggle, "after we finish our garden picture, we could do a happy dance around it! And then maybe we could ask Miss Rabbit if we can hang it outside in the real garden!"

Leo's whole body wiggled with joy. "Yes! We could dance like the butterflies in our picture! And seeing our art in the outdoor garden would be amazing!"

The two friends worked together, sharing the green crayon and all their other art supplies. They drew the most beautiful garden picture anyone had ever seen, with tall oak trees, soft grass, and colorful butterflies dancing in the sunshine.

When they finished, Leo and Emma held hands and danced around their masterpiece, spinning and giggling. Miss Rabbit was so impressed that she helped them hang their picture right outside the classroom window, where everyone could see it in the real garden.

"You know what, Emma?" Leo said as they admired their work. "Sharing and working together made this so much better than if I had just used the crayon by myself."

Emma nodded, her trunk curling into a smile. "And now we're friends too! Talking about our problem instead of fighting was the best idea ever!"

The End.
