# Buckle-Up, Brush-Up Beaver

Benny the busy beaver pattered to the family car with a backpack full of sticks and snacks. The morning sun made the silver door warm to touch. “Click, clack,” went Benny’s little claws on the driveway. He loved routines. He liked when things were the same each day.

“Car time is builder time!” Benny said, climbing into his booster seat. He wiggled, he wriggled, and he reached for his bundle of sticks. “I will build a tiny dam on my lap.”

Mama Beaver smiled and buckled his seat belt. “Safety first,” she said. The belt made a snug “snick.” Benny sniffed. The car smelled like piney soap and a hint of yesterday’s apples. The soft seat felt cool against his fur.

“Wait,” Mama said, holding out a small blue pouch. “Before we go, it’s Brush-and-Wipe time.”

Benny’s round eyes blinked. “Brush-and-Wipe? In the car? But our routine is sing-a-song, crunch-a-snack, then build-a-dam!” He hugged his sticks tighter. His tail thumped, thump-thump, on the seat.

Papa Beaver turned around and wiggled his whiskers. “We have a new routine for car days. Quick tooth-brush and paw-wipe. It keeps us clean and healthy.”

Benny’s tummy made a twisty feeling. He did not like new. New felt wobbly, like standing on a floating log. “But I always build first,” he whispered.

His sister, Bella, buckled beside him. She held up the blue pouch. “Smell this, Benny.” She unzipped it. Minty air whooshed out. “Mmm! It smells like snow.”

Benny sniffed again. It did smell fresh and cool, like the river in winter. He peeked inside. A tiny toothbrush, a little tube of toothpaste, and soft wipes with pictures of stars. He wiggled his nose. “Toothbrushes are for sinks,” he said, worried. “And wipes are for bath time.”

Mama nodded. “Yes, and they can help in the car too. We will try. We can be open-minded,” she said gently. “Open-minded means trying new ideas to see if they help.”

Benny looked at his sticks. He loved his plan. But he looked at Mama’s kind eyes and Bella’s hopeful smile. The car hummed as the engine started. The seat belt hugged him safe. He took a slow breath. The air smelled like mint and pine and a little apple.

“Okay,” Benny said. “I can try a tiny try.”

“Great choice,” Papa said. “Tiny tries are brave.”

Bella squeezed a pea-size squiggle of toothpaste onto the brush. “Here,” she said. “Open wide.” Benny opened. The brush went, “swish, swish, swish.” It tickled his teeth. The mint felt tingly and cool, like little snowflakes dancing. Benny giggled. “It’s sparkly!” he said. He spit into a little travel cup. “Ptoo!” The toothpaste made bubbly clouds.

Mama handed him a starry wipe. “Now wipe your paws and your face,” she said. “Good hygiene keeps us healthy.” The wipe felt soft and damp. It smelled like lemon and sunshine. Benny wiped his paws. They felt clean and not sticky. He wiped a tiny smudge from his nose.

“Ta-da!” Bella cheered. “Shiny teeth! Clean paws!”

Benny touched his smooth paws. He tapped his clean teeth with his tongue. “My mouth feels like winter,” he said, smiling. He looked at his sticks again. “Can I build now?”

“Snack first,” Papa said, passing a little bag of apple slices. “Crunch, crunch.” The apples were crisp and juicy. Benny chewed happily. No sticky paws. No fuzzy teeth. He grinned. “This new way is nice,” he said, surprised. “I can hold my sticks without icky.”

The car rolled down the road. Trees whooshed by the windows. Sunlight sprinkled his lap like little gold coins. Benny lined up three thin twigs and one fat stick. He made a tiny triangle dam. “Tap, tap,” went his careful claws. His tail thumped, this time a happy thump.

At a red light, Mama peeked in the mirror. “How’s our new routine?”

Benny sat up tall. “Brush-and-Wipe, then snack, then build,” he said. “It felt strange at first. But I tried, and now it feels good.”

Bella nodded. “Open minds help us learn.”

A little later, Papa stopped at the park. “Before we hop out,” he said, “what do we do?”

Benny zipped open the blue pouch. “Brush for bedtime, wipe for mess time, and in the car we Brush-and-Wipe before snack and build.” He handed a wipe to Bella like a helper. “Here you go.”

They all giggled. The car smelled like mint and lemon and happy apples. Benny felt proud. His tummy was calm, not twisty. He had made space in his routine for something new that helped.

As they unbuckled, Benny patted the blue pouch. “Being open-minded is like opening a new door,” he said. “Sometimes the new door leads to fresh snow in your mouth and clean paws for play.”

Mama gave him a warm hug. “Yes,” she said. “You tried, you learned, and now your routine is even better.”

Benny beamed and thumped his tail softly. “I’m a busy beaver with a clean smile,” he said. “Brush, wipe, snack, build, play!”

They skipped into the sunshine, ready for a bright, clean day.

The End.
