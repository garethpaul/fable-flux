# Brave Steps in the Guest Room

A gentle deer fawn named Dottie stood at the doorway of the guest room. The room smelled like fresh soap and clean sheets. Sunlight made soft squares on the carpet. A big suitcase sat near the bed, and a tiny nightlight glowed like a warm star.

Dottie’s little hooves clicked softly on the floor. “Tap, tap.” She peered inside. “It looks new,” she whispered. Her ears wiggled. She felt a flutter in her tummy. “I feel a little scared,” she said, “but I can be brave.”

On the bed lay a soft blue blanket. It looked like a calm pond. A stuffed squirrel sat on the pillow with a stitched-on smile. From the hallway came the sound of friendly voices and the smell of warm cinnamon rolls.

“Dottie,” called Mama Deer, “our friend Lila the Hedgehog is coming to stay. She’s shy. The guest room needs to feel cozy. Can you help?”

Dottie took a deep breath. “I will try,” she said. “I will be brave.”

She stepped into the room. “Tap, tap, tap.” She touched the blanket with her nose. It felt fluffy and cool. “Hello, room,” she said. “We can make you kind and cozy.”

Just then, a tiny squeak came from the door. Lila the Hedgehog peeked in. Her little eyes were shiny, and her paws held a small red scarf. “H-hi,” Lila said. “This room is big. I feel prickly-nervous.”

Dottie nodded. “I felt nervous too. Let’s be brave together.”

“How?” asked Lila, twirling the red scarf.

Dottie’s ears perked up. “We can fix the room with courage and teamwork. We can use our bodies and our hearts.” She smiled. “That means courage, generosity, and coordination.”

“Big words,” Lila said.

“We can learn,” Dottie said gently. “Courage means try, even if you feel scared. Generosity means share and give. Coordination means move together, slow and steady.”

Lila took a small breath. “Okay.”

First, Dottie looked at the tall curtains. They were crooked. The window made a tiny whistling sound. “Whoooo.” Dottie felt a wiggle of worry, but she lifted her chin. “I can do it.”

She bent her knees. “I will reach the curtain cord. Lila, can you hold the chair steady?”

“I can try,” Lila said. She pressed her paws to the chair legs. “I am small, but I am strong.”

Dottie climbed carefully. Her hooves touched the seat. “Tap.” Her knees shook. “I’m a little scared,” she whispered.

Lila looked up. “You are brave, Dottie. I am here.”

Hearing that, Dottie’s heart felt warm. She stretched her neck and nudged the cord. “Swish.” The curtain slid straight. Sunlight spread like a golden hug across the bed.

“We did it together!” Lila cheered. “That was coordination.”

Dottie grinned. “Yes. We moved safely, step by step.”

Next, Dottie saw the stuffed squirrel. “Lila, would you like a cuddly friend for the night?”

Lila hugged her red scarf. “I forgot my teddy. I feel lonely.”

Dottie felt generous. She gently placed the squirrel in Lila’s paws. “You can borrow my friend. Sharing is generosity.”

Lila’s quills lay flat. “Thank you,” she said softly. “I feel better.”

They both heard a soft rattle. “Click-click.” A loose drawer handle wobbled on the dresser. “It makes a funny sound,” Dottie said. “It might scare us at night.”

Dottie looked at the tiny toolbox on the shelf. “We can fix it. I will hold the screw. Lila, can you turn the little wrench?”

Lila’s eyes grew big. “I’ve never used a wrench.”

Dottie smiled. “We can learn together. We will go slow.”

They crouched by the drawer. Dottie held the handle steady. Lila wrapped her paws around the wrench. Her paws trembled. “I’m nervous.”

“You are brave,” Dottie whispered. “Try one turn.”

“Click.” The screw tightened a little.

“One more,” Lila said. “Click.” The handle stopped wobbling.

“We fixed it!” Dottie laughed. They bumped noses gently. “That was courage and coordination.”

The guest room felt different now. The curtains were straight. The drawer was quiet. The bed looked friendly. The air smelled sweet like cinnamon and clean linen.

Mama Deer peeked in with a plate. “You two did wonderful work,” she said. “Cinnamon roll for Dottie. Cinnamon roll for Lila.”

Dottie broke her roll in half and held out a piece. “Lila can have some of mine too.”

Lila smiled and offered part of her red scarf for the stuffed squirrel. “And the squirrel can wear a tiny blanket. I can give, too.”

They sat on the soft carpet. The rolls were warm and sticky. “Mmm,” said Dottie. Sugar sparkled on her tongue. Lila giggled as the frosting touched her nose. They both laughed, happy and safe.

When night came, the tiny nightlight glowed like a friendly firefly. Lila curled up in the bed with the squirrel. “Thank you,” she whispered. “I feel brave now.”

Dottie stood tall, even though she was small. “Today I learned that courage is trying, even when you feel scared. I learned that sharing helps hearts feel big. And moving together, step by step, makes big jobs easier.”

Lila nodded. “Courage, generosity, coordination,” she said slowly. “We can do them.”

“Yes,” Dottie said, smiling. “We can do them, together.”

The guest room was ready. The friends were ready too—brave, kind, and steady.

The End.
