# Pippa Penguin’s Sunny Song

Pippa was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and tiny waddling feet. Today, the sun was warm and happy in the sky. Pippa stood in the sunny backyard and took a deep breath. The grass smelled fresh. A soft breeze tickled her feathers. She smiled. “I want to make music today,” she said.

Pippa clapped her flippers. Clap, clap! Birds chirped from a fence. Chirp, chirp! “Hello, birds,” Pippa giggled. “Let’s make a song together.”

Pippa waddled to a blue bin by the porch. It was the recycling bin. Inside were clean bottles, a cardboard tube, and a shiny tin can. “We can make instruments!” Pippa said. Her eyes sparkled. “We can reuse and not waste. That is kind.”

She set the items on a blanket. “First, I need a plan,” Pippa said. She picked up a piece of chalk and a small notebook. The notebook was soft and smooth. “I will write my music ideas.” She drew simple shapes. She wrote little words: tap, ding, hum. “Writing helps me remember,” she said. She felt proud.

Pippa tapped the tin can with a wooden spoon. Ting, ting! The sound was bright and high. She smiled. “This can is a bell.”

She shook a plastic bottle with a few dry beans inside. Shaka-shaka! It made a friendly, soft sound. “This is my shaker,” Pippa said.

She blew across the top of a glass bottle. Woo-woo! It sounded like the wind. “This bottle is a whistle,” she said.

Pippa held the long cardboard tube like a drum and patted it gently. Pum, pum. The sound was deep and warm. “This is my drum,” she said.

Pippa lined up her instruments. She tapped her beak with a flipper. “How will my song go?” she wondered. She listened to the backyard. The leaves whispered. The birds sang. A far-away dog barked, woof, woof. Pippa wrote in her notebook: birds, leaves, dog. “My music can tell a story,” she said. “It can be about my sunny backyard.”

She tried to play her song. She shook the shaker. Shaka-shaka! She hit the drum. Pum, pum! She tapped the bell can. Ting! But the sounds were not together. Pippa frowned. “Hmm,” she said. “It does not sound like a song yet.”

She sat on the warm step and took a slow breath. “I can solve this problem,” Pippa said. “I will make a pattern.” She wrote a big number 1 and a number 2 in her notebook. “First, shaker. Then drum. Then bell. Repeat.” She drew little pictures too: beans, circle, star. Her flipper felt the bumpy paper. It helped her think.

Pippa tried again. “One, two, ready, play!” she said. Shaka-shaka. Pum, pum. Ting! Shaka-shaka. Pum, pum. Ting! She smiled. “Yes! Now it sounds like a song.”

A butterfly fluttered close. Its wings were yellow like a small sun. Pippa laughed. “You like my music?” she asked. The butterfly danced up and down.

Pippa added the bottle whistle. Woo-woo. It sounded like the breeze. She wrote a new word in her notebook: rest. “Sometimes music needs quiet too,” she whispered. She paused. She listened. The quiet felt soft and calm.

Just then, her friend Mia the mouse peeked through the fence. “Hi, Pippa!” squeaked Mia. “What are you doing?”

“I’m making music,” said Pippa. “I used recycling to make instruments. Do you want to play with me?”

“Yes, please!” Mia said. Her tiny paws clapped. Pippa handed her the shaker. “Shake on the ones,” Pippa said. She pointed to the number 1 on the page. “I will drum on the twos.” Pippa showed the number 2.

Mia nodded. “One, two, ready, play!” Pippa said. Shaka-shaka on one. Pum, pum on two. Ting at the end. Woo-woo and a rest. The sound was happy and bouncy. The sun felt warm on their faces. The grass felt tickly under their feet.

They played again and again. They changed the pattern. “Let’s try slow,” Pippa said. They counted slow. One… two… “Let’s try fast,” Mia said. They counted fast. One-two! They giggled when the shaker shook extra quick. They wrote the words slow and fast in the notebook. Pippa drew a turtle for slow and a rabbit for fast. Mia hugged herself. “I feel brave trying new sounds,” she said.

Pippa looked at the recycling bin. “We can make one more instrument,” she said. She found a small metal lid and tied it to a string. Ding-a-ling! It sounded like a tiny bell. “We did not waste,” Pippa said. “We turned old things into music.”

Mia’s ears wiggled. “Can we sing too?” she asked.

“Yes!” said Pippa. She wrote simple words: sun, fun, friends. “We can sing our song.” Pippa tapped the beat on the drum. Pum, pum. Mia shook the shaker. Shaka-shaka. They sang, “Sun in the sky, friends by my side, music and smiles, happy inside.” Their voices felt smooth and warm.

They bowed to the birds and the butterfly. The birds chirped back like clapping. Chirp, chirp, chirp! Pippa’s heart felt full. “We solved our music problem with a plan,” she said. “We used recycling to make instruments. We wrote our ideas. We listened and made patterns. Music helps us share and be kind.”

Mia nodded. “Music makes me brave and calm,” she said. “And writing helps me remember.”

Pippa closed her notebook and smiled at the sunny backyard. “Today we learned that music is everywhere,” she said. “We can make music with our voices, with our hands, and with things we reuse. We can listen, plan, and try again.”

They high-fived. The sun yawned and the breeze whispered. Pippa hugged her drum. “Tomorrow,” she said, “we will make a new song.”

The End.
