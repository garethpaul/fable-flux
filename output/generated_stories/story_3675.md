# Polly's Friendship Helper

In a cozy home office with tall windows and colorful books, lived a bright green parrot named Polly. Her feathers sparkled like emeralds in the morning sunlight, and she loved to perch on the big wooden desk next to the computer.

Polly was very creative. She could paint with her beak, arrange paper clips into pretty patterns, and even help organize the pencils by color. But today, something was different. Her human friend Sarah sat at the desk looking very sad.

"What's wrong, Sarah?" chirped Polly, tilting her head to one side.

Sarah sighed deeply. "My best friend Maya is upset with me. We had an argument yesterday about which game to play, and now she won't talk to me. I don't know how to fix it."

Polly's bright black eyes sparkled with an idea. "Squawk! I want to help! But how can a parrot help with friendship problems?"

Sarah smiled a little. "I don't know, Polly. This seems too big to solve."

But Polly wasn't giving up. She flapped her colorful wings and said, "Wait! Let me think like a detective!" She tapped her orange beak against the desk. "Tap, tap, tap."

"First," said Polly, "what made Maya upset?"

Sarah thought carefully. "Well, she wanted to play dress-up, but I really wanted to build with blocks. I said her idea was boring."

"Ooh!" squawked Polly. "That would hurt my feelings too. What if you told her you're sorry?"

"But what if she's still mad?" Sarah worried.

Polly hopped excitedly on her perch. "Then we make a friendship plan! What does Maya love most?"

Sarah's eyes lit up. "She loves drawing pictures of flowers!"

"Perfect!" chirped Polly. "Let's make her something special!" 

Polly flew to the art supplies and carefully picked up a purple crayon in her beak. "I'll help you draw!"

Together, they created a beautiful card. Sarah drew a big sunflower, and Polly added colorful dots around the edges with different crayons. The paper smelled like fresh wax, and their fingers got wonderfully messy with bright colors.

"Now," said Polly, "what words should we write?"

Sarah thought hard. "Dear Maya, I'm sorry I hurt your feelings. Your ideas are wonderful. Can we play together again? From Sarah and Polly."

When they finished, the card was covered in rainbow colors and glittery stickers. It felt smooth and bumpy at the same time from all their decorations.

"This is beautiful!" said Sarah, hugging Polly gently. "But what if it doesn't work?"

"Then we try something else!" squawked Polly confidently. "Good friends never give up on each other!"

The next morning, Sarah took the card to school. When she came home, she was smiling so big that her cheeks hurt.

"Polly! Maya loved our card! She said sorry too, and we played both dress-up AND blocks together!"

Polly did a happy dance on her perch, flapping her wings and bobbing her head. "Squawk! Friendship problems can be solved when we think carefully and try our best!"

"Thank you, Polly," said Sarah, giving her feathered friend a gentle scratch behind the head. "You helped me remember that good friends listen to each other and say sorry when they make mistakes."

From that day on, whenever Sarah had a problem, she and Polly would put their heads together and think of creative solutions. And their friendship grew stronger every single day.

The End.
