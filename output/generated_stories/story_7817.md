# Bubbles the Dolphin’s Spin-Cycle Goal

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to splash, swish, and sing. One day, Bubbles visited a bright, warm laundry room. The room hummed and thumped. The washing machine went whirr-whirr. The dryer went whoosh-whoosh. The air smelled like clean cotton and lemon soap.

Bubbles flapped a fin. “Ooo, listen! The washer sings!” he chirped.

On a low shelf sat a little chart with bright stickers. It said: “Goals!” Bubbles blinked his big dolphin eyes. “Goals help us grow,” he whispered, feeling a tiny flutter in his tummy. “I want a goal today.”

He looked around. The laundry room floor was smooth and shiny. Socks made a rainbow pile—blue, red, green, yellow. A basket of clothespins clicked like tiny wooden teeth. The washer window was round like a porthole, and inside, clothes danced in circles, swish-swish-swish.

Bubbles tapped the chart with his nose. “My goal is to learn a new spin trick,” he said. “A safe spin on the floor, like the washer spins the clothes!”

A friendly voice came from the door. It was Nana, with soft slippers and gentle eyes. “Hello, Bubbles,” she said. “What is your goal today?”

“I want to do a Big Brave Spin,” Bubbles said. “But I will learn it in little steps.”

Nana smiled. “That is a smart plan.”

Bubbles made his plan out loud:
- “Step one: Warm up my body.”
- “Step two: Practice a tiny spin.”
- “Step three: Try a bigger spin.”
- “Step four: Add a song.”
- “Step five: Rest and check my chart.”

He took a deep breath. He wiggled his tail. He swished his fins. He stretched—up, down, left, right. “Warm-up done!” he cheered.

The washer whirled. Swish-swish. Bubbles heard the rhythm and tried a tiny spin. He slid a little on the shiny floor. “Whoops!” His fins flapped. His heart went thump-thump. He felt a wobble.

Nana clapped softly. “Trying is brave,” she said.

Bubbles nodded. “I can try again.” He took another breath. “Step two: tiny spin.” He placed a small rug on the floor so it wasn’t too slippery. He bent his fins and spun just a little circle. He felt steady. “Tiny spin done!”

He put a shiny star sticker on the chart. The sticker made a happy snap sound. Bubbles grinned.

“Step three: bigger spin,” he said. The washer hummed. The dryer swooshed warm air that felt like a hug. Bubbles listened to the sounds: whirr, swish, whoosh. He matched the rhythm. Around he went—one, two, almost three turns!

Then—bump! He tapped a basket. Socks tumbled like sleepy clouds. Bubbles stopped. His eyes went wide. “Oh no,” he whispered. “I made a sock storm.”

Nana chuckled kindly. “It’s okay. You are learning. Let’s tidy together.”

Bubbles took a breath and picked up the socks with his snout, one by one. “Blue sock, red sock, green sock,” he sang. Cleaning felt calm. It was a pause, not a stop.

When the socks were snug again, Bubbles patted his chest. “My heart feels brave.” He added a small smile sticker to the chart. “I kept going,” he said.

“Step four: add a song,” he decided. Bubbles loved creative expression. He sang a simple tune: “Swish and spin, gentle and slow. I can learn, watch me grow.” His voice was soft and bubbly. The room smelled fresh. The floor felt smooth under his fins. The dryer’s warm air brushed his back.

Bubbles tried the Big Brave Spin again. He moved with the music. Around he went—one, two, three turns! He landed steady on the little rug. “I did it!” he squeaked. His tail tapped-tapped-tapped on the floor.

Nana clapped louder. “You set a goal. You made a plan. You tried small steps. You fixed a mistake. You finished with a song. That is brave learning.”

Bubbles placed a golden star sticker on the chart next to “Big Brave Spin.” He looked at it and felt proud. His body felt warm and happy. His breath felt slow and smooth.

He bowed to the washer. “Thank you, swish-swish teacher,” he giggled. He waved to the dryer. “Thank you, whoosh-whoosh helper.”

Nana handed Bubbles a soft towel. “Cool down time,” she said.

Bubbles wrapped the towel around his back. It felt cuddly and safe. He stretched again—up, down, left, right. He took a sip of water. He whispered, “I am strong. I am brave. I can learn new things.”

Nana asked, “What did you learn today?”

Bubbles smiled. “I learned that a goal is like a path. I make small steps. I keep trying. I move my body. I am brave even when I wobble. And I can make it fun with a song.”

The washing machine beeped. Beep-beep! The laundry was done. So was Bubbles’s goal. He did a tiny happy spin, just one turn, and gave Nana a gentle fin bump.

“Tomorrow,” Bubbles said, “I will pick a new goal.”

Nana nodded. “One step at a time.”

Bubbles looked at his chart, full of bright stickers. He felt ready for new things, one little step, and one brave spin at a time.

The End.
