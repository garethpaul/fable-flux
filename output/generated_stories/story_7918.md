# Sunny Songbird’s Art Day

Sunny was a small, yellow singing bird who lived in a playful living room. The rug was soft like a cloud. The curtains were bright and bouncy, like happy sunshine. Toys made gentle clinks and clacks when they moved. The air smelled like clean soap and a hint of crayons.

“Cheep-cheep! Today is Art Day!” Sunny sang. His voice was sweet and warm, like a morning hug.

Sunny fluttered to the window ledge and wiggled with excitement. He wanted to paint a big picture with colors and music. He wanted to make a rainbow song on paper. But the living room was busy. Pillows were stacked in a silly tower. A toy drum lay on its side. Crayons were rolling under the couch.

Sunny took a deep breath. “Cheep,” he whispered. “First, I need to be ready. Preparation helps me do my best.”

He zipped to the table and looked around. “What do I need?” he asked out loud.

From the couch, a soft stuffed bear replied, “What do you think, Sunny?”

Sunny tapped his beak. Tap, tap. “I need paper. I need crayons. I need a little cup of water and a small towel. I need a snack and water for my tummy, too.”

“Good plan,” said the stuffed bear.

Sunny smiled. “Let’s prepare!”

He started with his body. He stretched his wings. Stretch, stretch. He shook his tail. Shake, shake. He sipped cool water from a little bird cup. Sip, sip. It felt fresh and sweet. He nibbled crunchy apple slices from a tiny plate. Crunch, crunch. The apple smelled fruity and bright. “Healthy habits help me feel ready,” Sunny sang. “Cheep, cheep!”

Next, he washed his little claws in a shallow dish. Splash, splash. He patted them dry with a soft towel. Pat, pat. “Clean claws keep my art clean,” he chirped.

Sunny looked at the table. He placed a big sheet of paper in the middle. Swish. He lined up crayons in a neat color row: red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple. “Hello, colors!” he chirped. He put a small trash cup nearby for paper bits. He set a napkin for quick clean-ups. He even put a tiny bell next to him to ring when he needed help.

He hummed a happy tune. “When I prepare, I feel calm. When I prepare, I feel strong.”

Sunny hopped to the bookshelf and took a small box of paints. He checked the labels. “Non-toxic. Safe,” he read slowly. “Good!” He poured a little water into a small cup. Drip, drip. He laid a towel under the cup so spills would be okay.

He looked at the floor. “Uh-oh,” he chirped. A crayon rolled under the couch. He bent down and felt a dust bunny tickle his beak. “Achoo!” Sunny giggled. “Let’s make space.” He pushed the toy drum to the side. Tump, tump. He stacked the pillows neatly. Plop, plop. Now the living room looked clear and friendly.

“Time to sing and paint!” Sunny said.

He dipped a brush in blue. Swish. He stroked the paper. The brush felt soft and cool. He sang, “Blue like the sky, cheep-cheep!” He added yellow. Swish. “Yellow like the sun, cheep-cheep!” When the colors met, they made green. “Oh!” Sunny chirped. “Blue and yellow make green. Preparation helps me see new things.”

A tiny drip rolled toward his feathered toes. “Drip!” Sunny used the napkin. Dab, dab. “I am ready, so it’s easy to fix,” he said proudly.

Sunny drew a big round shape. He dotted two eyes and a bright smile. He painted music notes floating above. He listened to his own song. It sounded like warm honey and little bells. “I hear happy notes,” he said. He smelled the soft paint and clean towel. He felt the smooth paper under his claws.

Soon, the paper was full of colors—soft pink clouds, a green hill, and a rainbow path of music notes. He added a small basket in the corner with apple slices, just like his snack. “Art can show healthy habits, too,” he chirped. “Look! A snack break in my painting.”

Sunny paused. “Time to rest my eyes.” He blinked slowly. Blink, blink. He took a sip of water. Sip. He took three deep breaths. In, out. In, out. In, out. “Breaks help me do my best,” he sang softly.

When he was done, Sunny rang the tiny bell. Ting! The stuffed bear smiled from the couch. “Sunny, your art is bright and kind.”

“Thank you,” said Sunny. “Preparation helped me. I cleaned my claws, I set my tools, I made space, I had water and a snack, and I took breaks.”

He looked around. The paper was drying. The room was tidy. The crayons were back in their box. The towel was folded. Sunny felt proud and peaceful. His heart felt warm, like a sunny window.

Sunny sang a gentle song. “Prepare, prepare, and you will see, art feels happy and worry-free.”

He hung his painting on the wall with a small clip. The colors glowed in the playful living room. Sunny wiggled with joy.

“What did we learn today?” asked the stuffed bear.

Sunny smiled. “When I prepare, I make safe, healthy choices. I can create, I can clean, and I can care for me. Preparation makes art fun!”

The End.
