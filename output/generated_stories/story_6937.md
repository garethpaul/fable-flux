# Gertie's Colorful Creation

Gertie the giraffe loved to peek into the big pantry with her long, long neck. The pantry was full of wonderful things - shiny cans, colorful boxes, and jars that sparkled in the light.

"Mama," said Gertie one sunny morning, "I want to make something special all by myself!"

Mama Giraffe smiled warmly. "That sounds wonderful, dear. What would you like to make?"

Gertie thought and thought, tapping her spotted chin with her hoof. "I want to make art! Beautiful, colorful art!"

"The pantry has many things," said Mama. "But remember, you must be careful and think about what's safe to use."

Gertie nodded eagerly. She stretched her neck high into the pantry and looked around. There were so many choices! Her brown eyes sparkled with excitement.

First, she tried to reach for a jar of colorful sprinkles on the top shelf. But even with her long neck, it was too high and too heavy. "Oh no," she sighed. "This is harder than I thought."

Then Gertie noticed some empty cardboard boxes on a lower shelf. "These feel light and safe," she said to herself. She carefully pulled them down with her gentle mouth.

Next, she spotted some colorful pasta shapes in clear containers. Red bow-ties, yellow shells, and green spirals! "These look like tiny treasures," Gertie whispered with wonder.

But how could she make art with these things? Gertie sat down and thought very hard. She closed her eyes and imagined all the beautiful things she'd seen - flowers in the meadow, butterflies dancing, and rainbows after rain.

"I know!" she exclaimed. "I can glue the pasta to the boxes to make pictures!"

Gertie asked Mama for some safe, washable glue. Then she spread newspaper on the floor and got to work.

She pressed red bow-tie pasta onto one box to make flower petals. The pasta felt bumpy and smooth under her hooves. She arranged yellow shell pasta in a big circle to make a bright, happy sun.

"Look at all these beautiful colors!" Gertie said, stepping back to admire her work. The green spirals became grass, and she made a rainbow with pasta of every color.

As she worked, Gertie felt proud and happy. She was creating something all by herself! Her neck swayed gently as she concentrated, and her tail swished with joy.

"Mama, come see!" called Gertie when she finished.

Mama Giraffe gasped with delight. "Oh, Gertie! You made this beautiful art all by yourself! Look at your wonderful flower garden and that bright sunshine!"

Gertie beamed with pride. "I thought about what I wanted to make, I found safe things to use, and I used my imagination to create something new!"

"You were so thoughtful and independent," said Mama, nuzzling Gertie's long neck. "You solved the problem all by yourself and made something truly special."

Gertie hung her pasta art on the kitchen wall where everyone could see it. Every time she looked at it, she remembered how good it felt to create something beautiful with her own hooves and imagination.

"I can do things by myself," Gertie said happily. "All I need to do is think, be safe, and let my imagination help me!"

From that day on, whenever Gertie wanted to create something new, she remembered to think carefully, choose safe materials, and trust in her own wonderful ideas.

The End.
