# Miko’s Plan in the Workshop

Miko was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He lived in a cozy corner of a big garage workshop. The workshop smelled like fresh wood and warm oil. Sunlight slid in through the window and made dust sparkle like tiny stars. Tools hung on the wall—hammers, wrenches, and a shiny red toolbox that clicked shut with a snap.

One morning, Miko heard a squeak-squeak-squeak. He peeked out and saw a wobbly wagon with one loose wheel. The wagon belonged to his friends: Bella the beetle, Pip the puppy, and Tiko the turtle. They wanted to use the wagon to carry apples from the crate to Miko’s tiny pantry.

“The wheel is wobbly,” Bella buzzed.

“We need apples now!” barked Pip. “Let’s push it!”

Tiko blinked slowly. “If we push too fast, it might fall,” he said.

Miko’s whiskers twitched. He felt a little nervous, but he remembered something important. Planning helps problems feel smaller. He took a deep breath. He could be both kind and strong with his words.

Miko stood tall on a spool of thread. “Friends,” he said in a clear voice, “I have a plan.”

Pip wagged his tail. “A plan? Is it fast?”

“It is careful and smart,” Miko said. “First, let’s look closely. Then we will choose tools. Then we will fix the wheel. Then we will test it. If we plan our steps, we can do it safely.”

Bella buzzed again, this time with a happy hum. “I like steps,” she said.

Tiko nodded. “Slow and steady is good.”

They all gathered around the wobbly wagon. Miko touched the wheel. It wiggled and made a tiny creak. “Step one,” Miko said, “is to look and listen.” He pressed his ear close. “Creak-creak. The bolt is loose.”

Pip bounced. “Step two! Step two! What is step two?”

“Step two is to choose tools,” Miko said. He pointed to the wall. “We need a small wrench and some soft cloth.”

Bella flew up and gently tugged at a little wrench. It went tink-tink against the hooks. Tiko carried a folded cloth on his shell like a cushion. Pip sniffed the air. “The cloth smells like clean cotton!” he said, wagging.

Miko smiled. “Great teamwork.” He put the cloth under the wagon to keep it steady. “Step three is to fix the wheel. I will hold the wrench. Bella, please shine your light with your glow wings. Pip, please hold the wagon still. Tiko, please count to help us go slow.”

Tiko began, “One… two… three…” His voice was calm, like a slow drum.

Miko turned the wrench a little. Click. Click. The bolt felt tighter. The metal felt cool and smooth. Bella’s soft light made the wheel shine. Pip held the wagon with gentle paws and a serious face.

When the bolt was snug, Miko gave the wheel a spin. It went round and round with a happy whirr. “Step four is to test it,” he said. “Let’s try a tiny push.”

Pip gave the wagon a soft nudge. The wheel rolled straight. No wobble. No creak. Only a smooth whisper sound.

“Hooray!” Bella buzzed.

“Good plan,” Tiko said, smiling.

Pip barked, “Apples now?”

Miko nodded. “Yes, apples now. But let’s plan that too.”

Pip’s ears perked. “Plan for apples?”

“Yes,” Miko said kindly. “We do not want spills. Let’s use peaceful words and take turns. We can be assertive and say what we need, and we can listen to each other.”

Bella raised a tiny leg. “I will carry small apples,” she said.

Tiko said, “I will walk behind and make sure the wagon stays straight.”

Pip said, “I will pull slowly. I will say ‘ready’ before I move.”

Miko said, “I will place apples in a line. I need space, please.”

Pip took a breath. “Ready,” he said. He looked at Miko. “Do you have enough space?”

“Not yet,” Miko said calmly. “Please take one step back.”

Pip stepped back. “Is this good?”

“Yes,” Miko said. “Thank you for listening.”

They worked together. The wagon rolled smoothly over the warm floor. The apples smelled fresh and sweet. Tap-tap went their tiny feet. Swish went Bella’s wings. Clack went the toolbox as the workshop rested around them.

On the last trip, a small apple slipped. It wobbled on the edge of the wagon. Pip gasped.

Miko lifted his paw. “Pause,” he said in a firm but gentle voice. “Let’s fix this together. Bella, please steady the apple. Tiko, please hold the side. Pip, please stop and breathe.”

They did. The apple settled. No spill. No rush.

When the pantry was full, Miko looked at his friends. “We did it,” he said. “We made a plan. We used strong, kind words. We listened. We took turns.”

Bella hummed, “Planning helps us feel calm.”

Tiko nodded. “Planning makes hard things easier.”

Pip wagged. “Planning makes my tail happy!”

Miko laughed. His eyes shone like the little stars of dust in the sun. “Today we learned: When there is a problem, we can make a plan. We can speak up kindly. We can listen and help.”

They shared a crunchy apple snack. The wagon sat still and proud, its wheel straight and true. The garage was quiet and cozy, with the soft smell of wood and apples all around. Miko curled his tail and felt warm inside.

“We planned,” he whispered. “And it worked.”

The End.
