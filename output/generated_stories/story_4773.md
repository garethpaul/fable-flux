# Daisy's Sharing Garden

In a sunny backyard filled with colorful flowers and buzzing bees, lived a gentle deer fawn named Daisy. Every morning, Daisy would wake up when the warm sunshine tickled her soft brown fur.

"Good morning, sun!" Daisy would say, stretching her long legs. "Good morning, flowers! Good morning, world!"

Daisy loved her daily routine. First, she would nibble on the sweet clover near the fence. Then she would visit her friend Ruby the robin, who lived in the old oak tree.

"Tweet, tweet! Good morning, Daisy!" chirped Ruby from her nest. "The berries on the bush are extra juicy today!"

"Thank you for telling me, Ruby!" Daisy replied, her tail wiggling with excitement.

As Daisy munched on the delicious berries, she noticed something new. A small gray bunny was hiding behind the garden shed, looking very hungry and sad.

"Hello there," Daisy called softly. "I'm Daisy. What's your name?"

The little bunny peeked out shyly. "I'm Benny," he whispered. "I'm new here, and I don't know where to find food."

Daisy's heart felt warm and fuzzy. She remembered how scary it was to be in a new place. "Would you like to share my berries?" she asked kindly.

Benny's eyes grew wide. "Really? You would share with me?"

"Of course!" Daisy smiled. "There are plenty for both of us!"

They ate the sweet, purple berries together. The juice was so tasty that it made their tongues turn purple, and they giggled at each other's silly faces.

"These berries are the best I've ever tasted!" said Benny, his tummy finally full.

After breakfast, Daisy had an idea. "Benny, would you like me to show you all the best spots in our backyard? I know where the sweetest grass grows and where the coolest shade is!"

"That would be wonderful!" Benny hopped with joy.

Daisy showed Benny the patch of tender green grass near the sprinkler, where the water made everything smell fresh and clean. She showed him the cozy spot under the big sunflower where they could rest when the sun got too hot.

"You're so kind, Daisy," said Benny. "How can I ever thank you?"

"Being friends is all the thanks I need," Daisy replied, nuzzling Benny gently with her nose.

Ruby the robin flew down to meet them. "Who's your new friend, Daisy?"

"This is Benny! He's new to our backyard," Daisy explained. "Benny, this is Ruby. She knows where all the best berries grow!"

"Welcome to our backyard family, Benny!" chirped Ruby happily.

From that day on, Daisy, Benny, and Ruby shared everything together. They shared food, they shared stories, and they shared lots of laughter. Daisy discovered that sharing made everything taste better and feel more fun.

Every morning, Daisy would still do her routine, but now she had friends to share it with. And whenever a new animal came to the backyard, Daisy was always the first to say hello and offer to share.

"Sharing makes my heart feel as warm as the sunshine," Daisy would tell her friends.

And it was true. The more Daisy shared, the happier she felt, and the more friends she made. The sunny backyard became a place where everyone felt welcome and loved.

The End.
