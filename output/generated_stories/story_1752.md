# Cubby’s Music Room Moves

Cubby was a friendly bear cub with soft brown fur and twinkly eyes. Today, Cubby padded into the musical practice room at the forest school. The room smelled like polished wood. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. Drums, bells, and a shiny piano waited quietly.

Cubby wiggled his paws. “Time to move!” he said. He liked to make his body strong and happy with exercise. He also loved music. “I can do both,” he decided.

He pressed a piano key. Ting! The note sounded like a tiny star. Cubby smiled. “I will march to the beat.” He started marching. Left paw, right paw. Thump, thump. His paws felt the cool floor. His shoulders lifted tall. His heart said, I’m awake!

Just then, his friends came in. Tippy the turtle rolled in with a little drum on wheels. Flutter the butterfly floated near the chimes, her wings whispering shhh-shhh. Mimi the mouse carried a bright yellow ribbon. “Hi, Cubby!” they sang together.

“Hi, friends!” Cubby waved. “Let’s do music moves! Exercise can be fun.”

Tippy looked shy. “I’m slow,” he said. “I can’t march fast.”

Flutter fluttered excitedly. “I am light. I cannot stomp.”

Mimi squeaked, “I am very small. Big jumps make me wobble.”

Cubby thought for a moment. He tapped his chin. “We are all different,” he said kindly. “That is special. We can each move our own way.”

“Really?” asked Tippy.

“Really,” Cubby said. “Let’s make a plan.” He pointed to the piano. “I will play a sound. We’ll choose moves that feel good to our bodies.”

Cubby pressed three low keys. Bom, bom, bom. The room hummed. “These are slow beats,” Cubby said. “Tippy, do you want to lead?”

Tippy’s eyes shone. “Yes.” He tapped his little drum. Dum… dum… dum… Tippy took slow steps. Slide, pause. Slide, pause. His shell made a soft tapping sound on the floor.

Cubby marched slowly with him. Flutter swayed side to side, wings brushing the chimes, ting-ting. Mimi twirled her ribbon in a tiny circle. Everyone smiled. Their bodies felt warm and calm.

Next, Cubby pressed quick, high keys. Plink-plink-plink! “Fast beats!” he giggled. “Flutter, your turn.”

Flutter zipped in loops. “Zoom-zoom!” she sang. Her wings whooshed like a breeze. Cubby ran in place, knees up high. Mimi did quick little hops, hop-hop-hop! Tippy moved his drum sticks faster, but kept his steps gentle. “I can go fast with my sticks,” he said proudly, “and slow with my feet.” He beamed. He had made a smart choice for his body.

Cubby clapped. “Great decision, Tippy! Exercise means listening to our bodies. We can choose moves that help us feel good.”

Mimi tugged Cubby’s paw. “Can we try stretchy moves?” she asked. “Like reaching for the moon?”

Cubby nodded. He held a soft note on the piano. Mmmm. “Stretch time,” he said. Everyone reached up. Cubby felt his back lengthen. Mimi stood on tiptoes. Flutter stretched her wings wide. Tippy reached one arm, then the other, steady and strong.

Suddenly, Cubby noticed something. Flutter’s wings drooped. “Are you okay?” he asked.

Flutter landed on the piano bench. “I feel tired,” she said. “My wings need a rest.”

Cubby gave a kind smile. “Let’s choose a cool-down,” he said. “Exercise is strong. Rest is strong too.”

He played soft, sleepy notes. Mimi whispered, “Let’s breathe.” In they breathed. In… and out… Cubby felt his chest rise and fall. Tippy closed his eyes. Flutter smiled. “Thank you,” she said. “That feels better.”

When the music stopped, everyone listened to the quiet. They heard the tiny hum of the room and their own happy breathing.

“Friends,” Cubby said, “what was your favorite move?”

“I liked sliding slow,” Tippy said. “It matched me.”

“I loved zooming,” Flutter said. “My wings sang!”

“I liked tiny hops and big stretches,” Mimi said. “Both felt right.”

Cubby nodded. “I liked marching and breathing.” He patted his chest. “My heart feels strong. My body feels calm.”

Mimi looked at the yellow ribbon. “We should make a friendship pattern,” she said. “One move from each friend!”

“Good idea,” Cubby said. “Decision time. What should our pattern be?”

They thought together. Cubby tapped a light rhythm to help them decide. Ting-ting… ting…

Tippy raised his drumstick. “Slide, pause,” he offered.

Flutter chimed, “Zoom, circle!”

Mimi squeaked, “Hop, hop!”

Cubby added, “Stretch and breathe.”

They tried it. Slide, pause. Zoom, circle. Hop, hop. Stretch and breathe. The room filled with gentle taps, soft whooshes, tiny hops, and a long, happy sigh. The pattern felt just right.

“We did it!” Cubby cheered. “We exercised with music, we chose moves that fit us, and we were kind.”

Tippy smiled. “We celebrated our differences.”

Flutter twinkled. “And we helped each other.”

Mimi hugged Cubby’s leg. “Friends make exercise fun.”

Cubby hugged them back. His fur was warm and cozy. “Let’s remember our lesson,” he said. “Move your body every day. Listen to your body. Be kind to yourself and your friends. Different is wonderful.”

They put away the drum, ribbon, and chimes. The piano made one last soft note. Ting.

“See you tomorrow,” Cubby said.

“See you!” his friends sang, paws and wings waving.

The End.
