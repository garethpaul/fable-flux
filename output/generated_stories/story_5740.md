# Toby the Tortoise and the Car Ride Adventure

Toby the Tortoise was very excited! Today, he was going on a long car ride with the Miller family. Mrs. Miller had invited him because she knew Toby was very wise and good at solving problems.

"Buckle up, everyone!" called Mrs. Miller from the front seat. In the back sat Emma, who was five years old, and her little brother Sam, who was three. Between them sat Toby the Tortoise in his special travel shell.

As the car started moving, Emma pulled out a shiny red toy car. "Vroom, vroom!" she said, making it zoom across her lap. The toy made soft clicking sounds as its wheels rolled.

Sam's eyes grew big and round. "I want to play too!" he said, reaching for the toy car.

"No!" Emma said quickly, pulling the toy away. "It's mine! You can't have it!"

Sam's bottom lip started to wobble. "But I want to play," he said sadly. His voice was getting louder and shakier.

Toby watched with his kind, dark eyes. He had seen this problem many times before. The wise tortoise slowly turned his wrinkled green head toward the children.

"Emma and Sam," Toby said in his gentle, slow voice. "May I tell you a story about sharing?"

"Yes, please!" said Sam, wiping his eyes.

Emma looked curious too, even though she was still holding her toy car tightly.

"Once upon a time," Toby began, "there were two little rabbits who found a beautiful, juicy carrot. Each rabbit wanted the whole carrot for themselves."

"What happened?" asked Emma, leaning closer to Toby's shell.

"Well," said Toby, "they pulled and tugged until... SNAP! The carrot broke into tiny pieces that were too small for anyone to enjoy."

Sam gasped. "Oh no!"

"But then," Toby continued with a smile, "a wise old owl flew down and said, 'Why don't you share the carrot? Half for each of you!' So they did, and both rabbits had a wonderful, tasty lunch."

Emma looked down at her red toy car. She could feel its smooth, cool surface in her hands. "But what if Sam breaks my car?" she asked quietly.

"That's a good question," said Toby kindly. "When we share, we can make rules together. Maybe you could take turns? You play for five minutes, then Sam plays for five minutes?"

Sam clapped his little hands. "Yes! I'll be very careful!"

Emma thought for a moment. She could hear the gentle humming of the car engine and see the trees whooshing by outside the window. Sharing did sound better than listening to Sam cry.

"Okay," Emma said with a small smile. "Sam, you can play with my car. But please be gentle."

"I will! I will!" Sam said happily. Emma carefully placed the red car in his hands.

Sam made soft "vroom" sounds as he rolled the car gently back and forth. After five minutes, he gave it back to Emma without being asked.

"Thank you for sharing!" Sam said, giving Emma a big hug.

"You're welcome," Emma said, feeling warm and happy inside.

Toby smiled his wise tortoise smile. "When we share and listen to each other, everyone feels good. And look—now you both have a friend to play with instead of just a toy!"

For the rest of the car ride, Emma and Sam took turns with the toy car and played together happily. They learned that sharing made everything more fun.

"Thank you, Toby," whispered Emma. "Sharing really is better!"

The End.
