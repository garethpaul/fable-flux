# Benny Bear's Big Clean-Up

In the cozy study corner, Benny Bear sat surrounded by a big mess. Paper scraps covered the soft carpet. Empty juice boxes lay scattered around. Broken crayons rolled under the wooden desk.

Benny's fuzzy brown ears drooped down. His round tummy felt sad. "Oh no," he said, looking at all the trash. "I made such a mess while drawing!"

The morning sun shone through the window. It made the mess look even bigger. Benny's nose twitched as he sniffed. The study corner smelled like old apple juice and waxy crayons.

"I should just throw everything away," Benny mumbled. He started gathering the paper pieces in his small paws. But then he stopped. Something didn't feel right in his heart.

Mama Bear had taught him about taking care of the Earth. "Remember, little one," she always said, "we must be kind to our planet."

Benny sat down and thought hard. His furry forehead wrinkled up. "What would Mama do?" he wondered aloud.

Then Benny's brown eyes lit up! "I know! I can recycle!"

He jumped up on his hind legs. His paws clapped together with excitement. "This will be fun!"

First, Benny sorted everything into piles. He put all the paper scraps together. The juice boxes went in another pile. The broken crayons made a third group.

"Paper goes in the blue bin," Benny said, remembering Mama's lessons. He carried the paper pile across the room. His little legs worked hard. Up and down, up and down he went.

"This is good exercise!" Benny huffed happily. His heart beat fast like a drum. Sweat made his fur feel warm and damp.

Next came the juice boxes. "These go in the green bin," Benny said. He stretched up high to drop them in. His arms reached like tree branches.

The broken crayons were trickier. "Hmm," Benny thought. "These can't be recycled. But maybe I can reuse them!"

Benny gathered all the crayon pieces. He pressed them together in his paws. The waxy bits stuck to each other. Soon he had made new, colorful crayons!

"Look what I made!" Benny cheered. He did a little dance around the study corner. His paws tapped on the clean carpet.

Just then, Mama Bear walked in. Her eyes grew wide with surprise. "Benny! This room looks wonderful!"

"I recycled everything, Mama!" Benny said proudly. His chest puffed out big. "I sorted the paper and juice boxes. And I made new crayons from the broken ones!"

Mama Bear scooped Benny up in a warm, fuzzy hug. "I'm so proud of you," she said. "You took responsibility for your mess. And you helped our Earth too!"

Benny snuggled into Mama's soft fur. He felt happy and tired from all his hard work. "It felt good to do the right thing," he said.

"That's what integrity means," Mama explained. "Doing what's right, even when it's hard work."

Benny nodded. His eyelids felt heavy. "Can we draw with my new crayons now?"

"Of course!" Mama laughed. "But this time, let's put trash in the right bins as we go."

Benny and Mama sat down together. They drew beautiful pictures of the Earth surrounded by hearts. The study corner stayed clean and tidy.

From that day on, Benny always remembered to recycle. He felt proud knowing he was helping take care of the planet for all his forest friends.

The End.
