# Whiskers Welcomes Everyone

In a sunny house, there was a cozy guest room with soft pillows, a round rug, and a window that let in warm light. The guest room smelled like fresh soap and clean sheets. A clever cat named Whiskers loved this room. It was quiet, bright, and perfect for ideas.

Whiskers had silky gray fur and bright green eyes. He liked to think, to listen, and to make little plans that helped his friends. One morning, Whiskers swished his tail and said, “I will make an art day! Everyone can make something special in the guest room.”

He set out colorful paper, crayons, stickers, and safe glue sticks on a low table. He added shiny buttons, soft yarn, and tiny bells that jingled. The guest room felt happy. The round rug was ready for paws and feet. The window was open a crack, and a sweet breeze drifted in.

Friends came to the door. “Hi, Whiskers!” chirped Pip the chick. “Cheep, cheep! I want to draw.”

“I want to paint stars,” said Dot the puppy, wagging her tail.

“I bring a drum,” boomed Bongo the bear cub, patting a small drum. “Boom, boom!”

Whiskers purred. “Welcome, friends! This is Art Day. We take turns. We share. We include everyone.” He put a paw to his heart. “Everyone belongs.”

The friends sat on the rug. Pip drew yellow suns. Dot painted blue swirls. Bongo tapped a gentle beat. The guest room twinkled with color and sound. Whiskers made a crown from yarn and buttons. “Ta-da!” he said, placing it on his head. The bells jingled softly.

Then the door creaked again. In peeked Sloane the snail, small and shy, with a shiny shell. Sloane whispered, “Um… may I come in too?”

Whiskers smiled. “Yes, Sloane! Come in. You are part of Art Day.” He moved a pillow to make space. “We include everyone.”

Sloane slid in slowly. Dot’s ears twitched. “But Sloane is slow,” Dot said. “Will we have to wait a long time?”

Bongo frowned. “And my drum is loud. Will Sloane like it?”

Pip held her paper tight. “We will run out of stickers,” she peeped.

Whiskers felt his whiskers tickle. He took a deep breath and listened to the room. He could hear the drum’s boom, the crinkle of paper, and the tiny slide of Sloane’s shell. He could smell glue and crayons. He looked at his friends’ faces. They were worried.

Whiskers spoke kindly. “We can solve this. We can include Sloane and still have fun. Let’s make a plan.”

Pip asked, “What plan?”

“First,” said Whiskers, “we share stickers by counting. One for Pip, one for Dot, one for Bongo, one for Sloane, and one for me. That is fair.”

“Second,” he said, “Bongo can play a soft drum for now.” Whiskers tapped the drum gently. “Boom… boom…” The sound was like a heartbeat.

Bongo tried it and smiled. “I can do soft,” he said, tapping with gentle paws.

“Third,” Whiskers added, “we make a ‘Slow Spot’ with a comfy pillow for Sloane to work at the best speed. We can visit the Slow Spot and cheer.”

Dot’s tail wagged. “I can set the Slow Spot!” She put a pillow near the window, where the light was warm. “There!”

Sloane looked at the art table. “I… I have never made art with friends,” Sloane whispered. “I am nervous.”

Whiskers gave Sloane a small brush. “You belong here. Paint with us. Your way is welcome.”

Sloane took a tiny dip of blue paint. The brush made a soft swish. “Swish…” Sloane painted slow, gentle waves on a card. Pip cheeped, “That is pretty!” Dot wagged. “I like those waves!” Bongo tapped a soft beat that matched the swish, “Boom… swish… boom… swish…”

Soon, Whiskers noticed something. He had used extra stickers for his crown. He looked at the sticker sheet. Only one shiny star was left, and Sloane had none yet. Whiskers felt a little twist in his tummy. He remembered honesty.

He took off his crown and spoke up. “Friends, I need to be honest. I used too many stickers for my crown. That was not fair. I am sorry.”

Pip tilted her head. “What do we do?”

Whiskers smiled. “We can fix it.” He peeled two stickers off his crown and put them back on the sheet. “Now Sloane can have a star and a heart.” He handed them to Sloane. “I want to include you. You matter.”

Sloane’s eyes shone. “Thank you,” Sloane said softly. “I feel welcome.”

The room felt even warmer, like a hug. They all kept creating. Pip drew a family of suns. Dot painted tiny paws on a rainbow. Bongo’s soft drum kept the beat. Sloane added the star and heart to the blue waves. Whiskers made a new crown with fewer stickers and more yarn. It still jingled.

At the end, Whiskers said, “Let’s share our art!”

Pip held up her suns. “These are for everyone,” she peeped.

Dot barked, “This rainbow has a path for all friends!”

Bongo drummed, “I made a song that is soft and kind.”

Sloane showed the card. “I made waves that carry friends together.”

Whiskers purred, “I made a crown that shines with sharing.”

They placed their art on the round rug. The guest room glowed with color and care. They all clapped and jingled and tapped. Whiskers looked at his friends. “Today we learned that everyone belongs. We listened, we shared, we told the truth, and we made room for each other.”

Sloane smiled wide. “I felt slow,” Sloane said, “but I felt loved.”

Whiskers nodded. “Inclusion means we welcome every friend, every way. That is how art, and hearts, grow.”

They snuggled on the pillows as the light turned golden. The sweet breeze brushed their cheeks. The room hummed with friendship.

The End.
