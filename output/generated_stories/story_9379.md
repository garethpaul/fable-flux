# Pebble Problems on the Porch

Pip was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a tiny orange beak. Pip loved to sit on the peaceful front porch, where a soft breeze tickled his feathers. The porch smelled like fresh lemon tea, and the wooden boards felt warm under his little penguin feet. Birds chirped, leaves rustled, and Pip’s rocking chair creaked, creak… creak.

Pip had a favorite game. He liked to make a pebble path. Every pebble was smooth and cool, like little round ice cubes. He lined them up from the porch steps to the blue flower pot.

“Plink, plink, plink,” he said happily, placing each pebble.

Pip hummed a gentle tune. “La-la, pebble path, la-la, happy math.” He wiggled with joy.

Then a small puff of wind blew, whoosh, just a little. A pebble rolled off the step. “Oh!” said Pip. He put it back.

Another pebble slid sideways. “Oh no,” Pip whispered. He put it back, again.

Just then, Dottie the duckling waddled up the porch path. Her feet went flap, flap, flap. “Hi, Pip!” Dottie quacked. “Can I see your pebbles?”

“Yes!” Pip smiled. “Look, Dottie. A straight line.”

Dottie peered closely. “So shiny,” she said. She leaned in, and her feather brushed a pebble. It wobbled and fell—plink!

Pip’s belly felt tight. His smile drooped. “My path,” he said, frowning.

“I’m sorry,” Dottie whispered, her eyes big and sad.

Pip’s wings felt hot, and his heart went thump-thump. He squeezed his flippers. He wanted to shout. His pebbles were not in a line now. They were crooked and messy. His cheeks puffed, puff, puff.

He remembered Mama Penguin’s calm voice. “When you feel big feelings, Pip, you can stop, breathe, and choose kind words.”

Pip rocked in his chair one time. Creak. He closed his eyes and took a slow breath in. In… one, two, three. He let the breath out. Out… one, two, three. He did it again. In… and out…

The wind felt cool on his face. He heard the birds again. He smelled lemon tea and the sweet porch flowers.

Pip opened his eyes. “I feel upset,” he said softly. “But I can use gentle words.”

Dottie nodded. “I’m listening.”

Pip took another breath. “Dottie, I worked hard on my pebble path. When the pebbles moved, I felt frustrated. Can you help me fix it?”

“Yes!” Dottie quacked. “We can fix it together.”

Pip’s shoulders relaxed. His frown flipped to a small smile. “Thank you.”

They knelt on the warm wooden boards. Pip showed Dottie how to line up the pebbles. “We put them like this,” he said, tapping gently. “Plink, plink.”

Dottie copied. “Plink, plink.” Her voice was soft and careful.

A bumblebee buzzed by. Bzzzz. The porch wind chime sang a tiny song. Ting-ting-ting. Pip and Dottie worked in a quiet rhythm. Plink, plink. Ting-ting. Bzzzz.

Dottie’s feather brushed another pebble. It wobbled again. Pip felt a little poke in his tummy. “Oof,” he said. He paused, took a breath in, and a breath out. He used his words.

“Let’s take our time,” Pip said kindly. “We can be slow and steady.”

“Slow and steady,” Dottie echoed. She tucked her feathers close and moved her flippers carefully.

Soon, the line was straight again. It was even better than before. They made a little curve around the flower pot. Pip clapped his flippers. “We did it!”

Dottie clapped too. “Teamwork!”

Pip poured cool water into two tiny cups. “Lemon tea for us,” he said. They sipped. Slurp. The tea tasted sunny and sweet. The porch felt peaceful again. The rocking chair creaked happily.

“Thank you for telling me how you felt,” Dottie said. “I didn’t know I bumped it. I want to be gentle.”

“Thank you for helping me fix it,” Pip said. “I felt frustrated, but I used my breath and my words.”

Mama Penguin opened the front door and smiled. “I see a beautiful pebble path,” she said. “And I see kind friends.”

Pip waddled to the edge of the path. He looked at Dottie. “If the wind blows again, we can fix it again,” he said. “We can always choose peaceful solutions.”

Dottie nodded. “We can take a breath, use gentle words, and work together.”

They laughed. The breeze whispered. The chimes sang. The pebbles shone like tiny moons.

Pip felt proud inside. His big feelings had been big, but he made a calm choice. He learned that when something goes wrong, he can pause, breathe, and speak kindly. He learned that friends can help.

He gave Dottie a pebble with a white stripe. “This is for you,” he said.

Dottie hugged it close. “I will remember,” she said, “slow and steady, kind and ready.”

Pip sat back in his rocking chair. Creak… creak… He watched the pebble path glow in the afternoon light. He smiled a bright penguin smile.

Today’s lesson, he thought, is simple: When frustration comes, I can choose peace.

The End.
