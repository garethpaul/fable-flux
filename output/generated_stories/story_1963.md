# Polly’s Picnic Parade

In a bright dining room, a creative parrot named Polly sat on the back of a chair. The room smelled like warm toast and sweet strawberries. Sunlight made little gold dots on the tablecloth. “Squawk! Good morning!” Polly chirped. Her feathers were green like fresh leaves, and her tail was blue like the sky.

Polly watched her family set the table. Mama put out bowls. Papa sliced apples. Little Mia hummed a song and lined up spoons. The window was open, and a soft breeze tickled Polly’s wings. She could hear birds outside. She could smell the grass after a light morning mist.

“Let’s eat in the dining room,” said Papa.

Polly tilted her head. “Squawk! Outdoor adventure?” she asked, her eyes bright. “Picnic? Parade?”

Mia giggled. “A picnic parade? What’s that, Polly?”

Polly hopped to the window. “We eat outside! We march! We sing! We move our bodies. We feel the sun. We share old ways and make new fun. Squawk!”

Mama smiled. “My grandma loved picnics,” she said softly. “We can share that tradition with a new twist.”

“What twist?” asked Mia.

Polly puffed her chest. “A Creative Picnic Parade! We make it together. We use our hands. We use our voices. We try new ideas. We care for nature.”

Papa looked around the dining room. “We have food and plates,” he said. “How do we carry everything?”

Polly flapped her wings, thinking. “Innovation time! We can make parade packs.”

Mia clapped. “Parade packs!”

They looked at what they had. There were clean cloth napkins, wooden spoons, and a basket. There was string in a drawer. The dining room smelled like soap and lemons.

“Let’s tie napkins to make little bags,” said Mama. “We can tuck in fruit and bread.”

“Squawk! I’ll help!” Polly picked up the string in her beak and tugged. Mia folded a napkin and tied it into a soft pouch. Papa made a big bag for the apples. Mama filled tiny jars with water and screwed the lids on tight.

Polly listened to the breeze. She could hear leaves whisper, “Come play.” She felt excited. Her feet danced on the chair. “Parade time!” she called.

They stepped out the door and into the warm day. The grass felt cool under Mia’s toes. The sun hugged their shoulders. A friendly cloud drifted by like a cotton ship. “March, march, march,” sang Mia.

Polly led the way down the path. She tapped a wooden spoon on a jar, tinka-tink, tinka-tink! “Listen,” Polly said. “Nature music! Birds sing. Leaves swish. Feet thump. That is our parade band.”

They reached a shady tree. The bark was rough like a little puzzle. The air smelled green and bright. Mama spread the cloth napkins on the ground. Papa set apples in the middle. Mia placed cups in a circle.

“Before we eat,” said Mama, “I will share a tradition. My grandma said, ‘Give thanks to the sky, the ground, and the hands that helped.’”

Polly bowed her head. “Thank you, sky,” she chirped. “Thank you, ground. Thank you, hands.”

Mia smiled. “Thank you, tree,” she said, patting the trunk gently.

They ate crunchy apples. They nibbled bread. Juice dripped, sweet and sticky. “Mmm,” said Mia. “It tastes like sunshine.”

After eating, Polly had an idea. “Let’s make art with nature, but leave nature safe,” she said. “We can use fallen things.”

They looked around and found leaves on the ground, smooth pebbles, and tiny twigs. Mia arranged brown and yellow leaves into a flower shape. Papa made a little path with pebbles, curvy like a river. Mama made a heart with twigs. Polly set one bright feather in the middle.

“It’s a picture on the earth,” Mia whispered. “It’s gentle art.”

Polly nodded. “Creative expression! We can make beauty and still be kind to the park.”

They sang a simple song, soft and happy:
“Step outside, feel the breeze,
Stretch your arms, bend your knees.
Share a smile, share some bread,
Try new ways,” Polly said.

Papa stood and stretched. “I feel strong and calm,” he said. “Outdoor time helps our bodies.”

Mia spun in a slow circle. “I feel brave to try new things,” she said.

Just then, a little ant crawled near their cloth. Mia watched it carefully. “Hello, tiny friend,” she said. “We will not scare you.”

Polly tilted her head. “We share the outdoors with small friends,” she said. “We can be gentle.”

When it was time to go home, they cleaned up. They packed their jars. They checked the ground. No trash. Only the leaf art stayed, soft and natural. The wind would carry it away later.

Back in the dining room, the air felt cozy again. The table waited, neat and clean. Mia hugged Polly. “Thank you for the parade,” she said. “I learned to go outside and move my body. I learned to try new ideas. I learned to share old traditions too.”

Polly ruffled her feathers, happy and proud. “Every day can have a little outdoor adventure,” she chirped. “We can make, we can move, we can share.”

Mama nodded. “Tomorrow, we can plan another small parade,” she said. “Maybe we bring a neighbor. We can teach and learn together.”

Polly hopped to her chair and looked at the window. The breeze waved hello. “Squawk! See you soon, sunshine,” she said.

And the dining room felt like a start, not an end.

Lesson: Going outside helps our bodies and hearts. We can be creative and kind, try new ideas, and honor family traditions—all in one happy day.

The End.
