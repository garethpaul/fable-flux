# Squeaky Squirrel Says It Nicely

Squeaky the squirrel lived in a cozy bedroom with soft blankets, a tiny acorn pillow, and twinkly star stickers on the ceiling. The room smelled like fresh laundry and warm toast. Morning sun slipped through the curtains in golden stripes. Squeaky stretched, wiggled his furry tail, and took a deep breath.

“Good morning, room,” Squeaky said. “Today I will use my brave, kind voice.”

He made his bed with little pats, pat pat pat. He brushed his fuzzy teeth until they felt squeaky clean, scrub scrub scrub. He washed his paws with warm water and gentle soap, bubble bubble bubble. Healthy habits made Squeaky feel strong and calm inside.

Just then, his friend Pip the mouse tumbled in with a toy drum. Boom-boom-boom! The drum echoed around the cozy bedroom. Squeaky’s ears tickled from the loud sound.

“I want to play now!” Pip squeaked. Boom-boom-boom!

Squeaky felt a hot puff of mad in his tummy. His tail twitched. He wanted to shout, “Stop it!” But he remembered his plan. He put a paw on his chest and took a slow, deep breath in. One… two… three. Then he breathed out. Shhhh.

“I can be calm,” Squeaky whispered to himself. “I can be clear.”

He looked at Pip and used his brave, kind voice. “Pip, I like playing with you,” Squeaky said. “The drum is too loud in my cozy bedroom. My ears hurt. Please play softly or let’s choose a quiet toy.”

Pip blinked. Boom… boom. He tapped softer. “Like this?” tap-tap.

“Yes,” Squeaky said with a small smile. “That feels better. Thank you.”

They tried a puzzle on the rug with bright pieces that clicked together. Click, click. Squeaky liked how it felt to ask for what he needed. He felt tall and steady inside.

After the puzzle, Pip saw Squeaky’s shiny acorn jar on the shelf. He reached up. Clink. “Mine!” Pip said.

Squeaky felt the hot puff again. His face got tight. He wanted to grab the jar. He stopped. He put a paw on his chest. Deep breath in. One… two… three. Breathe out. Shhhh.

He kept his feet still and his voice steady. “Pip,” he said, looking into Pip’s eyes, “that is my special acorn jar. I am not ready to share it right now. Please put it back.”

Pip’s whiskers drooped. He held the jar carefully. “Okay,” he said softly, and set it back on the shelf. Clink.

Squeaky nodded. “Thank you. We can share the toy blocks instead.” He pointed to a basket. “Let’s build a calm fort.”

They stacked blocks in gentle colors: blue, green, and yellow. Thunk, thunk, thunk. The fort grew with little windows. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Squeaky’s breathing felt slow and smooth.

Mother Squirrel peeked in with a warm smile. “I hear calm building,” she said. “Time for a snack.” She brought apple slices and crunchy carrot sticks. Crunch, crunch. The apple was juicy. Squeaky licked his lips.

After snack, Pip bounced on the bed. Boing! The blankets wiggled. The acorn pillow flew off and thumped to the floor. Squeaky felt surprised. His heart beat fast. Boing, boing!

He held up a paw. “Pause,” he said. He took a deep breath. One… two… three. Shhhh. He used his brave, kind voice again. “Pip, my bed is for resting. Please stop bouncing on it. It could break, and I don’t like it.”

Pip froze mid-boing and sat down. “Oh,” he said. “I didn’t know. I can stop.”

“Thank you,” Squeaky said. “Let’s bounce on the floor with our knees, like quiet frogs.” They laughed and made soft little hops. Ribbit, ribbit.

The room felt peaceful again. The star stickers seemed to twinkle brighter. Squeaky felt proud. He had stayed calm. He had said what he needed in a clear, kind way. He had been assertive.

Soon, it was tidy-up time. Squeaky sang a soft song while he put blocks in the basket. Pip hummed and stacked books on the shelf. Swish, swish. Mother Squirrel handed them a tiny duster. “Clean room, calm mind,” she said. The dust smelled like lemon. Squeaky’s cozy bedroom sparkled.

Pip picked up the toy drum and looked at Squeaky. “Is this okay?” he asked.

Squeaky listened to his feelings. He put a paw on his chest and checked his breath. In… and out… He smiled. “Yes, but please use your quiet taps.”

tap-tap-tap.

They sat on the soft rug. Pip tapped a gentle beat. Squeaky tapped his paws on the floor. Tap-tap, pat-pat. It felt friendly. It felt safe.

Mother Squirrel brought a small mirror. “Look at your brave faces,” she said.

Squeaky looked. His eyes were bright. His mouth made a proud smile. “I used my words,” he said. “I used my calm. I took breaths. I was kind and clear.”

Pip nodded. “I listened,” he said. “I can be gentle.”

Squeaky wrapped his tail around himself like a warm scarf. He felt cozy and strong. “When I feel mad,” he said, “I can stop, breathe, and say what I need. That helps me, and it helps my friends.”

Mother Squirrel hugged them both. The room smelled like apples and soap and love. The star stickers watched from above, quiet and happy.

And in the cozy bedroom, with soft blankets and tidy shelves, Squeaky’s brave, kind voice made the day feel just right.

The End.
