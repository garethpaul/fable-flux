# Bubbles in the Basement Lab

In a cozy house by the sea, there was a basement lab full of soft lights and gentle hums. The air smelled like salty water and lemons. Machines beeped quietly. Shelves held jars of seashells and shiny buttons. In the middle was a big round tank, like a tiny ocean. In that tank lived Dippy, a playful dolphin with bright eyes and a happy squeak.

“Good morning, Dippy,” said Dr. Lina, the kind scientist who worked in the lab. She wore a blue coat and green rubber boots. “Today we will test the Bubble Helper. It makes big bubbles to carry small things. It will help us clean the bay.”

Dippy clicked and spun in the water. “Squee! I love bubbles!” he sang. His fins splashed, and little waves tickled the tank sides.

Dr. Lina smiled. “Remember, if something is hard, we use our calm tools. Breathe in, breathe out. Count to four.”

Dippy nodded. “Breathe in. Breathe out. Count to four,” he echoed.

Dr. Lina pressed a button. Whirr. Pop! The Bubble Helper made a shiny rainbow bubble. It rose and twinkled. “Ooo!” Dippy chirped. He booped it with his nose. Boop! The bubble floated, then bumped a string of metal beads. Clink-clink! The beads tangled.

“Oh no,” said Dr. Lina. “The bubble popped too soon. We must try again.”

They tried again. Whirr. Pop! Another bubble. Dippy swam fast to guide it. His tail swished. Splash-splash. But the bubble stuck to his fin. “Hey!” Dippy squeaked. He shook his fin. Pop! The bubble burst, and salty drops sprinkled his face.

Dippy scrunched his snout. “This is not fun,” he muttered. His heart thumped. His cheeks felt hot. He slapped the water with his tail. Splash! A wave sloshed over the tank, onto the floor. Dr. Lina stepped back.

“I see big feelings,” Dr. Lina said softly. Her voice was calm like a lullaby. “Are you feeling angry, Dippy?”

Dippy huffed. “Yes. I want it to work now!” His eyes were shiny with mad tears.

“That feeling is okay,” said Dr. Lina. “Anger is a feeling. It does not have to be a problem. What can we do to help your body calm?”

Dippy wiggled. He wanted to splash again. He wanted to shout. But he remembered. “Calm tools,” he whispered. He lifted his head. “I will breathe.”

He closed his eyes. In through his blowhole—slow and cool. Whooo. Out with a soft hum. Hmmm. He counted. “One… two… three… four.” He did it again. Whooo… Hmmm. “One… two… three… four.”

His heart slowed. His cheeks cooled. The lights in the lab looked less bright. He opened his eyes. Dr. Lina smiled and held up four fingers. “You did it,” she said. “You were brave. It takes courage to calm.”

Dippy nodded. “I feel better. I can try again.”

Just then, the lab lights flickered. Click-click. The Bubble Helper made a funny buzz. From a corner cage came a tiny squeak. “Eep!”

“Pip!” cried Dr. Lina. A small brown mouse was stuck behind a rolling cart. Its whiskers twitched. Its little foot was caught in a twist of string.

Dippy’s eyes went wide. “We must help,” he said.

Dr. Lina hurried to the corner, but the cart was heavy. She pushed. It groaned and rolled a little. The string was still tight.

Dippy’s tail flicked. “Bubble Helper can lift the string!” he called. “But I must aim it right.”

He felt a flutter in his chest. What if it pops? What if I mess up? He felt a warm bubble of worry, and a spark of anger tried to rise. “No,” he told himself gently. “Breathe first.”

He took a breath. Whooo… Hmmm. “One… two… three… four.” Calm washed over him like cool water.

“Ready,” said Dippy.

Dr. Lina nodded. She tapped the button. Whirr. Pop! A clear bubble grew, big and steady. It floated toward the string. Dippy swam slowly, guiding with his nose. He moved like a feather in water. “Easy, easy,” he whispered. His fin brushed the bubble—so soft! The bubble slid under the string and lifted it. The string rose, light as air.

“Pip, pull back,” said Dr. Lina.

The mouse squeaked, wiggled, and slipped free. “Eep!” Pip dashed into a tiny house made of a shoe box. He peeked out and squeaked, “Thank you!”

Dippy laughed, a happy series of clicks. Dr. Lina clapped. “You helped Pip. You helped me. You used calm and courage.”

Dippy splashed a little, gentle this time. He felt proud, like sunshine in his chest. “My anger wanted to shout,” he said. “But I breathed. Then I could help.”

Dr. Lina wiped the floor and checked the Bubble Helper. “Now we can practice more,” she said. “Slow and steady wins.”

They tried again. Whirr. Pop! Dippy guided the bubble around the metal beads. No pop. No splash. The bubble carried the beads across the tank and set them in a tray with a soft plink. Dippy cheered. Dr. Lina beamed.

The lab smelled like lemons and ocean. The hum was calm. Pip nibbled a crumb. Everyone felt safe.

Dr. Lina held up a card with a picture: a little dolphin breathing with numbers. “This is our Calm Card,” she said. “When we feel mad, we can pause, breathe in and out, and count to four. Then we choose kind actions.”

Dippy pressed his nose to the glass. “I will remember,” he said. “Breathe. Count. Then help.”

He did a happy flip. Splish! “Today I learned,” Dippy added, “that anger is a feeling, and I can be the boss of my body. I can be brave and help others.”

Dr. Lina gave a thumbs-up. “That’s the lesson,” she said. “You did great, Dippy.”

The lights glowed warm. The machines purred. The little lab felt like a hug. Dippy floated, calm and proud, ready for the next small adventure.

The End.
