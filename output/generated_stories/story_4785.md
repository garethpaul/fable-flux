# Daisy's Special Garden Gift

Little Daisy the deer fawn loved playing in Mrs. Chen's sunny backyard. The warm grass tickled her tiny hooves, and the bright yellow flowers smelled like honey. Every morning, she would hop and skip through the garden, careful not to step on the pretty plants.

"Good morning, flowers!" Daisy would say, touching each bloom gently with her soft nose.

One sunny Tuesday, Daisy was playing a new jumping game. She leaped high over the daisies, then over the roses. "I'm flying like a butterfly!" she giggled, spinning in circles.

But when Daisy tried to jump over the vegetable patch, her back legs got tangled in the tomato plants. CRASH! Down she tumbled, rolling right through Mrs. Chen's beautiful garden.

"Oh no!" Daisy cried, looking around. Tomatoes were squished on the ground. Carrot tops were bent and broken. Dirt was scattered everywhere. "What have I done?"

Mrs. Chen heard the noise and came outside. When she saw her messy garden, her face looked very sad. "My poor vegetables," she whispered, kneeling down to touch a broken tomato plant.

Daisy's big brown eyes filled with tears. "Mrs. Chen, I'm so sorry! I was just playing, and I didn't mean to hurt your garden. Please forgive me!"

Mrs. Chen looked at the little fawn's worried face. She could see that Daisy felt very sorry. "I know you didn't mean to, sweet Daisy," she said softly. "Accidents happen when we're having fun."

"But I ruined everything!" Daisy sniffled. "Your beautiful vegetables are all broken."

Mrs. Chen smiled gently. "You know what? I forgive you, little one. And maybe we can find a way to fix this together."

Daisy's ears perked up. "Really? How?"

"Well," said Mrs. Chen, tapping her chin thoughtfully, "we could make something special with these vegetables. Even broken ones can be useful!"

Together, they gathered all the squished tomatoes and bent carrots. Mrs. Chen brought out colorful paints, brushes, and big sheets of paper.

"We're going to make vegetable stamps!" Mrs. Chen explained. She cut the tomatoes in half, showing Daisy the star-shaped pattern inside. "Look! Nature made beautiful designs for us."

Daisy dipped a tomato half in red paint and pressed it on the paper. "It looks like a flower!" she exclaimed with delight.

They used carrot pieces to make orange circles and celery ends to make perfect green dots. Soon, they had created the most beautiful garden painting ever.

"This is even prettier than the real garden!" Daisy laughed, her tail wiggling with joy.

Mrs. Chen hung their artwork on the fence for everyone to see. "Sometimes the most wonderful things come from mistakes," she said, giving Daisy a gentle pat.

"Thank you for forgiving me," Daisy said, nuzzling Mrs. Chen's hand. "And thank you for showing me how to make something good from something broken."

"That's what friends do," Mrs. Chen replied. "We forgive each other and help each other learn."

From that day on, Daisy was more careful when playing in the garden. But she also learned that when we make mistakes, saying sorry and finding creative solutions can make everything better. And their special vegetable art reminded them both that forgiveness and friendship can create the most beautiful things of all.

The End.
