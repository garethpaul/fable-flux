# Bubbles and Beeps

Click-clack, click-clack went the metal paws of Spark, the helpful robot dog, as he trotted into the bathroom. The floor felt cool and smooth under his tiny treads. The room smelled like minty soap and warm towels. A soft whoosh came from the bath faucet, and bubbles piled up like little white mountains.

“Beep-beep! Bath time team!” Spark chirped, his eyes blinking gentle blue.

Maya, a little girl with curly hair, stood on a stool by the sink. She held a bright yellow toothbrush. Her brother, Theo, held a green one and made a silly face in the mirror. Their baby cousin, Joy, clapped and giggled from her bath seat, splashing the warm water. Splash, splash, plip!

“I want to brush first!” Theo said, bouncing.

“It’s my turn,” Maya said, crossing her arms. “I asked first.”

Spark tilted his shiny head. “Beep. Let’s be fair. We can take turns.” He wagged his metal tail, which made a soft whirr. “But first, we all need patience.”

“What is patience?” Maya asked.

“Patience is waiting kindly,” Spark said. “Patience is a soft breath in and a soft breath out. Let’s try it.”

They breathed in. They breathed out. The mirror fogged a tiny bit. Joy blew a bubble and laughed, “Buh-buh!”

“That tickles my nose,” Theo said, but he smiled.

“Beep. Good waiting,” Spark said. “Maya, you asked first, so you may start. Theo, you will be next. I will help Joy after. That is fair.”

Maya nodded. “Okay.”

Spark pressed his gentle nose to the soap pump. Squeak. A tiny dot of minty paste landed on Maya’s toothbrush. “Now we practice,” Spark said. “Practice makes us better.”

Maya brushed up and down. “Mmm, it tastes like snow!” she giggled.

Spark watched the timer light on his collar. “Beep. Slow circles. One, two, three.” He hummed a calm, happy tune. The tune sounded like soft raindrops. The bathroom felt cozy, like a warm towel hug.

Theo bounced again. “Is it my turn? Is it my turn?”

Spark blinked green. “Patience, friend. Let’s cheer for Maya while we wait.”

Theo clapped. “Go, Maya! Brush-the-brush!”

Maya finished and rinsed. Swish, swish. “All clean!” she said, showing her shiny smile.

“Your turn, Theo,” Spark said. He added a tiny dot of paste to Theo’s brush. “Remember, slow circles.”

Theo started fast. Scrub-scrub-scrub! Splash! Toothpaste flew like tiny stars onto the mirror.

“Oh!” Theo gasped. “I went too fast.”

“That’s okay,” Spark said kindly. “Practice is how we learn. Patience helps us slow down.” He handed Theo a soft cloth with his paw. “We can clean together.”

Theo wiped the mirror. Swipe, swipe. “I can try again.”

“Beep-beep! Try again,” Spark said.

This time, Theo brushed slow. Circle, circle, circle. The minty smell made the air feel fresh. The timer light blinked softly. “I did it,” Theo said, grinning with foam on his lips.

“Rinse and smile,” Spark said. Swish, swish. Theo’s teeth glowed.

Joy splashed and squealed. “Me! Me!”

Spark rolled to the tub and lowered a tiny ramp. “It’s Joy’s turn for gentle hair washing,” he said. “We will be careful and kind.”

Joy touched the water. “Warm!” she said.

Spark dipped a small cup into the bath. Scoop. “We pour slowly,” he said. “We keep water away from eyes. If we wait, we keep it comfy.”

Maya and Theo knelt by the tub. “We can help!” Maya said. She held a soft washcloth. Theo held the baby shampoo.

“Beep. Teamwork,” Spark said. “Maya, hold the cloth on Joy’s forehead. Theo, I will say when to pour.”

They waited. Drip, drip went the water from the cup. Joy blinked. “No ouch,” she said, smiling.

“Now a tiny bit of shampoo,” Spark said. “Squeeze.” Plop. The bubbles were fluffy like clouds. They smelled like peaches.

Theo looked ready to dump fast, but he stopped. He took a slow breath. “Patience,” he whispered. He poured a little. “Like that?”

“Just right,” Spark said.

Maya dabbed Joy’s forehead. “Are you okay, Joy?”

Joy giggled. “Okay!”

They rinsed. They waited. They sang Spark’s soft raindrop song together. The bathroom felt calm and happy. The bubbles twinkled. The mirror shone. The minty smell mixed with peach, and everything felt fresh and clean.

“All done,” Spark said, drying Joy’s hair with a warm towel. Whirr went his gentle dryer. Joy yawned and leaned on Spark’s side. “Soft,” she murmured.

Maya hugged Spark. “You helped us wait.”

Theo hugged him too. “You showed us how to practice.”

Spark wagged. “Beep. You all helped each other. We took turns. We used fairness. We included everyone. Patience made it peaceful.”

Maya looked at Theo. “Next time, you can go first.”

Theo nodded. “And I will wait kindly.”

Joy clapped. “Team!”

Spark sat with a happy chirp. “When we are patient, we feel safe. When we practice, we get better. When we are fair and help others, everyone shines.”

They turned off the faucet. Drip, drip slowed to quiet. The towels felt warm. Their smiles felt bright.

“Beep-beep,” Spark said. “Bath time team, mission complete.”

The End.
