# Squeak’s Soapy Boat

In a bright, clean bathroom, a kind mouse named Squeak peeked out from behind a fluffy towel. The air smelled like lemon soap. Warm steam floated up, and little water drops clung to the mirror like tiny stars.

Squeak’s whiskers twitched. “Hello, bubbles,” he giggled, watching a big bubble wibble and wobble in the sink. Plink, plink, went the drip from the faucet. The sound made Squeak hum a happy tune.

“Today,” Squeak said to himself, “I will make a tiny boat. I will sail it in the sink!” His heart felt bouncy with excitement. He loved to craft, and he loved to share.

Squeak tugged at a small soap box on the shelf. It was light and smooth. He sniffed it. “Mmm, smells fresh,” he said. He found a cotton swab for a mast and a little square of tissue for a sail. He patted the soft tissue. “So soft like a cloud,” he whispered.

Just then, he heard a soft rustle. A shy spider named Dot peeked from behind the soap dish. Dot had tiny, shiny eyes and wiggly legs. “H-hello,” Dot said in a teeny voice. “What… what are you making?”

“A boat!” Squeak squeaked. “Would you like to watch?”

Dot’s eyes blinked. “I want to help,” she said, “but I am not sure how. I am small.”

Squeak smiled kindly. “We can talk about it. We can plan. We can work together. That is called cooperation.” He sat down by the sink. “First, let’s share our ideas with our words. That is called communication.”

Dot felt braver. “Okay,” she said. “I can make tiny knots. I am good with silk.”

Squeak clapped his paws. “Great idea! Could you tie the sail to the mast?”

Dot spun a silky thread, soft as a whisper. Swish, swish, went her silk as she tied the tissue sail to the cotton swab. Squeak used a bit of tape from a small roll he kept in a thimble. “Tape, please!” he said. Dot handed it to him.

Squeak set the soap-box boat in the sink. The water was calm like a little lake. The boat bobbed gently. “Float, little boat,” he said. The sail fluttered as a warm breeze from the vent tickled it.

Suddenly, the faucet dripped a bigger drop. PLOP! The boat wobbled and bumped the side. Dot squeaked, “Oh no! It might tip!”

Squeak took a deep breath. “Let’s not panic. Let’s use our words. What should we do?”

Dot thought. “Maybe… we need a rudder. And maybe we need calm water.”

“I agree,” said Squeak. “I will turn the faucet handle off more.” He reached up and gently turned the shiny handle. The plink, plink slowed to a tiny tinkle and then stopped. “Now the water is calm.”

Dot spun another silky thread. “I can help make a rudder,” she said. Squeak found a small craft stick near the cotton balls. He held it up. “This can be our rudder!” Together, they taped the stick to the back of the boat. Dot tied a neat silk bow to keep it steady.

Squeak asked, “How does it look?”

Dot smiled. “It looks strong. It looks ready.”

“Let’s test it,” Squeak said. He blew softly. “Fffff.” The boat glided across the water, smooth and straight. Dot clapped her tiny legs. “It works!”

At that moment, a little rubber duck named Bubbles peeked over the tub. “Quack? What are you two doing?” Bubbles asked in a gentle quack.

“We made a boat,” Squeak said. “We talked, we shared, we made a plan.”

Bubbles bobbed happily. “Quack! Can I join?”

“Yes!” Squeak nodded. “We can cooperate. We can take turns. Would you like to be the horn?”

Bubbles puffed his chest. “Quack-quack!” he tooted proudly.

Squeak said, “Dot, you call the wind. Bubbles, you quack the horn. I will steer with a spoon.”

They all took a deep breath. “Three, two, one,” Squeak said. “Sail!”

Dot whispered, “Wind, please,” and waved her tiny legs. Squeak blew a gentle breeze. Fffff. Bubbles quacked, “Toot-toot!” The boat sailed in a circle and then straight across the sink. It made a tiny wake like lace on the water.

“Teamwork!” Squeak cheered. “We did it because we talked and listened.”

But then the tissue sail got a little wet and droopy. The boat slowed. Dot’s eyes grew worried. “What now?”

Squeak said calmly, “We can share feelings. I feel a little sad, and a little brave. How do you feel?”

“I feel worried,” Dot said, “but I want to try again.”

Bubbles quacked softly. “I feel helpful.”

Squeak nodded. “Thank you for telling me. When we share, we understand.” He looked around and found a small piece of plastic from a wrapper. “This can be a new sail. It won’t get soggy.”

Dot tied the plastic sail with fresh silk. Squeak dried the boat with a soft washcloth. Pat, pat, pat. Bubbles hummed a happy quack song.

They tried again. The boat zipped across the water, bright and shiny. The three friends laughed. The bathroom smelled fresh and sounded like giggles and gentle splashes.

Squeak turned to his friends. “Today we learned to talk kindly, to listen, and to make a plan. Communication helps us know what to do. Cooperation helps us do it. Crafting helps us create something new.”

Dot nodded. “We used creativity.”

Bubbles quacked, “And compassion! You were kind to me and to Dot.”

Squeak’s heart felt warm. “We did it together.”

They sailed one more lap. Then Squeak dried the boat and tucked it safely by the soap dish, ready for another day.

The End.
