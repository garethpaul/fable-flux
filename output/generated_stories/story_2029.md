# Panda Poppy’s Team Tidy

Panda Poppy was a patient panda with soft, fuzzy fur. She lived in a cozy home with a bright little home office. The chair was squishy. The desk smelled like fresh paper. The window let in warm, golden light. Birds chirped outside. Tap, tap, tap went Poppy’s keyboard as she smiled and hummed a gentle tune.

“Today is Plan Day,” Poppy said. “We will make a poster to care for Earth.” She patted the big poster paper and took a deep breath. “Slow and steady,” she whispered. Poppy liked to plan and prepare.

Her friends peeked in. There was Buzz the busy bee, Skip the squirrel, and Dot the duck. “Hi, Poppy!” they sang.

“Hi, friends,” Poppy said. “Let’s work together. Teamwork makes it fun.”

“Let’s start now! Zoom, zoom!” said Skip, bouncing on his toes.

Buzz buzzed around the desk. “I can bring markers!”

Dot waddled over, flapping. “I can bring glue and stickers!”

Poppy nodded. “Thank you. First, we prepare. We clean the space. We put paper, markers, and glue in a neat line.”

They looked around. The home office was a little messy. Crinkly paper was on the floor. A cup had tipped over. Some recycling sat in a wobbly stack. Poppy smiled kindly. “We can tidy together. We can recycle and reuse.”

Skip hopped to the pile. “I’ll do it fast!” he said. He grabbed too much. The wobbly stack tumbled—crash! Paper floated like snow. A marker rolled across the floor—tap, tap, tap. The sound made Dot jump, and a little glue line squiggled onto the desk.

Buzz blinked. “Oh dear,” he said softly.

Poppy took a slow breath in. She let it out. “It’s okay. Accidents happen. We can fix it, together.”

Skip’s ears drooped. “I’m sorry, Poppy,” he whispered. “I went too fast.”

“Thank you for your apology,” Poppy said, patting his paw. “Next time, we prepare and move slowly. We can be a team now.”

Dot nodded. “Team tidy?” she asked.

“Team tidy!” everyone cheered.

Poppy made a simple plan:
- Sort paper for recycling.
- Wipe the desk.
- Line up supplies.
- Make the Earth-care poster.

They sang a tidy song as they worked. “Pick up, sort up, team up, hooray!” The paper made rustle-rustle sounds. The cloth went swish-swish as they wiped. The window breeze smelled like clean rain. They placed the blue and green markers side by side. The glue cap clicked on—click!

“Nice and neat,” Poppy smiled. “Thank you, team.”

Now they were ready. Poppy drew a big round Earth. She colored blue water and green land. Buzz drew a happy tree with a bird nest. Dot added a pond with a duck. Skip drew a sunny sun and little recycling arrows.

“Let’s write our Earth-care steps,” Poppy said. She spoke slowly, and Buzz wrote the words in big, friendly letters:

1. Reduce
2. Reuse
3. Recycle
4. Be kind and share

“What do they mean?” asked Dot.

Poppy explained with action. “Reduce means use less.” She turned off a lamp they did not need. Click. The room still felt warm and bright from the window.

“Reuse means use again,” Poppy said. She held up a clean jar. “We can use this jar for crayons.” The crayons made a happy clink when they slid in.

“Recycle means sort paper, glass, and cans,” Buzz buzzed, pointing to the neat bins. The bins made soft thunks as they placed things in.

“And be kind and share means teamwork,” Poppy said, smiling at her friends.

Skip raised his paw. “I can ask: ‘May I help?’ and ‘May I take turns?’”

“Yes,” Poppy said. “That is teamwork.”

They glued leaves around the poster. The glue smelled sweet and sticky. They stuck on little stars. The stars felt bumpy under their paws and wings. Dot quacked a happy quack.

Then—oh no!—Buzz’s wing knocked the jar of crayons. It tipped—clatter! Crayons rolled like colorful raindrops. Buzz’s eyes got wide. “I’m sorry,” he buzzed. “I didn’t see it.”

Poppy nodded gently. “Thank you for saying sorry. We can fix it, together. Team tidy again!”

They all knelt and picked up crayons. Click, clack, clink—back into the jar they went. Skip moved slower this time. Dot used her beak gently. Buzz hovered carefully. Poppy held the jar steady.

When they were done, the poster looked bright and beautiful. The Earth smiled. The words were clear. The desk was clean. The room smelled fresh and felt calm. Poppy’s heart felt warm.

“Teamwork helped us,” Poppy said. “We prepared. We cleaned. We said sorry. We cared for Earth.”

Skip grinned. “I learned to slow down.”

Buzz buzzed happily. “I learned to look and share space.”

Dot quacked, “I learned to reuse and recycle.”

Poppy hugged them. “I learned that friends make work light and fun.”

They hung the poster near the window. Sunlight hugged the colors. The birds outside chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” as if they liked it too.

Poppy clapped softly. “Team, we did it!”

“Teamwork!” they cheered. “Care for Earth! Be kind!”

The End.
