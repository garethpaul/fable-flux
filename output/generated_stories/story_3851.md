# Miko's Big Thinking Adventure

In a sunny backyard filled with tall green trees and colorful flowers, lived a little monkey named Miko. Miko had soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes that sparkled in the warm sunshine.

One beautiful morning, Miko swung down from his favorite apple tree, feeling very hungry. His tummy made a loud "grumble, grumble" sound.

"Oh my!" said Miko, patting his belly. "I need some yummy fruit for breakfast!"

Miko looked up at the apple tree where he usually found his food. But something was wrong. All the low branches were empty! The only apples he could see were way up high, much too far for him to reach.

"Hmm," thought Miko, scratching his head. "How can I get those apples? They smell so sweet and look so red and juicy!"

Miko tried jumping as high as he could. "One, two, three... JUMP!" But even his biggest jump wasn't high enough. The apples were still too far away.

Next, Miko tried climbing the smooth trunk of the tree. But it was too slippery, and he kept sliding back down. "Whoosh!" he went, landing softly on the grass with his bottom.

"I need to think about this," Miko said to himself. He sat quietly in the warm sunshine and looked around the backyard carefully. "What can help me reach those apples?"

As Miko looked around, he noticed something interesting. There was a wooden garden bench near the tree. Next to the bench was a big, empty flower pot. And leaning against the fence was a long garden rake.

Miko's eyes lit up! "Wait a minute! I have an idea!"

First, Miko pushed the heavy bench closer to the tree. "Push, push, push!" he huffed, using all his strength. The bench made a scraping sound on the ground as it moved.

Then, Miko carefully carried the flower pot and placed it on top of the bench. It made a soft "thump" sound.

"Now I'm getting taller!" Miko said happily.

Finally, Miko climbed up onto his tower and used the long rake to gently pull down the apple branches. "Come here, little apples!" he called.

One by one, the beautiful red apples fell softly into his hands. They felt smooth and cool, and they smelled absolutely wonderful!

"I did it! I did it!" Miko cheered, doing a little happy dance on his tower. "I used my thinking brain to solve my problem!"

Miko sat in the shade and enjoyed his delicious breakfast. The apples were crisp and sweet, and they made his tummy feel full and happy. As he ate, he felt proud of himself for not giving up.

"You know what?" Miko said to a friendly butterfly that fluttered by. "When I have a problem, I can stop and think. I can look around and find things to help me. I don't need to wait for someone else to help me – I can figure it out myself!"

The butterfly seemed to agree, dancing around Miko's head in the warm sunshine.

From that day on, whenever Miko faced a tricky problem, he remembered his apple adventure. He would stop, think carefully, look around for helpful things, and try different ideas until he found a solution.

And every morning, Miko enjoyed fresh, healthy apples that he had gotten all by himself using his very own thinking brain!

The End.
