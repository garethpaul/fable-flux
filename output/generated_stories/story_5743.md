# Nutkin's Green Car Adventure

Nutkin the squirrel lived in a big oak tree next to the Johnson family's driveway. Every morning, he watched as Mom, Dad, and little Emma got into their blue car. The car made loud rumbling sounds and puffed gray smoke from its back end.

"Achoo! Achoo!" Nutkin sneezed as the smoky air tickled his tiny nose. The gray puffs made his eyes water too. "That doesn't smell very nice," he said, twitching his fluffy tail.

One sunny day, Nutkin noticed something different. The car wouldn't start! Dad turned the key over and over. Click, click, click! But the engine stayed quiet.

"Oh no!" said Emma from inside the car. "How will we get to school?"

Nutkin's ears perked up. He loved solving problems! He scampered down his tree and peeked through the car window. The family looked worried.

"Excuse me," squeaked Nutkin politely, tapping on the glass with his tiny paw. "I might have an idea to help!"

Emma's eyes grew wide with wonder. "A talking squirrel! Mom, Dad, look!"

"Hello there, little friend," said Mom with a smile. "What's your idea?"

Nutkin pointed his fluffy tail toward the garage. "I've been watching you every day. Your car makes smoky air that makes me sneeze. But I know something better! What if we use bikes instead?"

Dad scratched his head. "Bikes? But Emma's school is far away."

"Not too far for bikes!" chirped Nutkin. "And bikes don't make any smoky air at all! They're quiet like whispers. Plus, you'll feel the warm sunshine and hear the birds singing!"

Emma clapped her hands. "That sounds fun! Can we try, please?"

Mom nodded thoughtfully. "You know what? The little squirrel is right. Bikes would be much better for the air we all breathe."

The family got their bikes from the garage. Emma's was pink with streamers on the handles. Mom's was green with a basket. Dad's was blue and tall.

"Follow me!" called Nutkin, scampering ahead. "I know all the best paths!"

They rode through the neighborhood together. The morning air smelled fresh and clean. Birds chirped happy songs from the trees. Emma giggled as her streamers danced in the breeze.

"This is wonderful!" laughed Mom. "No smoke, no noise, just fresh air!"

"And look how happy the trees look without all that gray smoke," added Dad, pedaling beside them.

When they reached Emma's school, other families were arriving in cars. The parking lot was full of rumbling engines and gray puffs.

"Maybe we can tell our friends about bike riding too!" suggested Emma excitedly.

Nutkin beamed with pride. "That's a great idea! When everyone helps keep the air clean, all the animals and people feel better. We're all taking care of our Earth together!"

From that day on, the Johnson family rode their bikes whenever they could. Soon, their neighbors started biking too. The air became cleaner and fresher. Nutkin never had to sneeze from car smoke again.

Every morning, Nutkin would wave from his oak tree as his friends pedaled by. "Good morning, Earth helpers!" he would call out cheerfully.

And the whole neighborhood felt proud to be taking care of their beautiful world together.

The End.
