# Pippa Parrot’s Sunny Craft Day

Pippa was a bright green parrot with a rainbow tail. She loved to flap her wings and say, “Squawk! Let’s make something new!” One sunny morning in the backyard, the grass felt tickly, the flowers smelled sweet like honey, and the breeze whispered, “Whoooosh.” Pippa blinked her shiny eyes and looked around.

“Today is a craft day,” Pippa said. “I feel it in my feathers!”

She hopped to a little table under a big umbrella. On the table were safe, simple craft things: soft paper, big stickers, short yarn, chunky beads, paper cups, and a glue stick that smelled a little like bubble gum. Next to the table sat Pippa’s friends: Benny the bunny, Tilly the turtle, and Max the mouse.

“What will we make?” asked Benny, twitching his nose.

“We will make Windy Wings!” Pippa chirped. “They will flap and spin in the breeze. We can hang them by the garden to cheer everyone up.”

Max’s whiskers twitched. “I don’t know how,” he squeaked.

Tilly tucked in her head a little. “I move slowly,” she said. “I might be too slow.”

Pippa puffed up with courage. “We can do it together. Step by step. Slow is okay. Trying is brave!”

The friends clapped their paws and flippers. The sun warmed their backs. A blue jay sang from the fence, and the scent of fresh grass made everyone smile.

Pippa showed the first step. “Take a paper cup. Make three little holes near the rim.” She tapped with a safe, round-tipped stick to mark dots. “One, two, three.”

Benny’s ears wiggled. “One, two, three,” he repeated. He carefully copied the dots.

“Next,” said Pippa, “we add yarn for tails.” She tied a short piece of yarn through each hole. The yarn was red, yellow, and blue. It felt soft like kitty fur.

Max held his yarn but his paws shook. “I am nervous,” he whispered. The yarn slipped and fell.

Pippa touched his shoulder. “That’s okay,” she said, gentle as a breeze. “Let’s try again together.” She showed him how to make a big, easy loop. Max took a deep breath. In and out. He tried again. Loop, pull, tie.

“I did it!” Max giggled. His eyes sparkled.

“Good learning,” Pippa said. “Learning takes time. Trying is brave.”

Tilly moved slowly but steady. “I can tie one knot,” she said, and she did. The knot was neat and tight. “Slow can be strong,” Pippa cheered.

Now, Pippa showed the next step. “We make wings!” She cut big leaf shapes from the soft paper. Snip, snip. The paper made a tiny crinkle sound. “We stick the wings to the sides of the cup with glue.” She rubbed glue on the edges. The glue felt cool and sticky.

Benny pressed on a wing. Squish. It held. “It works!” he laughed.

Max added big star stickers. “Stars for speed,” he said.

Tilly added circle stickers. “Dots for calm,” she said.

They made four Windy Wings. The colors shimmered in the sun. Pippa looked at them with pride. “Now we hang them on the fence,” she said. She tied one long yarn to the top of each cup so they could dangle and spin.

Just then, a gust of wind rushed by. Whooosh! One Windy Wing blew off the table and rolled across the grass. It bounced toward the flower bed.

“Oh no!” cried Max.

Pippa’s heart thumped. The flower bed looked busy with bees. They buzzed like tiny drums: bzz-bzz. Pippa took a careful breath. “Bees are our friends,” she said softly. “We can be brave and gentle.”

Pippa stepped slowly. “Excuse me, bees,” she whispered. “We are just getting our craft.” She moved with soft feet and soft wings. She reached the rolling cup and held it close. The bees buzzed on the flowers and didn’t mind. Pippa felt proud. “Bravery can be quiet,” she thought.

Back at the table, she showed a new idea. “Let’s make a little handle for windy days,” she said. “Innovation means trying a new way.” She looped yarn around the cup and tied it like a belt. “Now it won’t roll away.”

Benny copied the handle. Tilly did too, very slowly. Max added an extra knot. Together they hung all four Windy Wings on the fence. The wind spun them gently. Swish, spin, swish. The colors flashed red, yellow, blue, and green. The garden looked happy.

The friends sat on a soft blanket and shared apple slices. Crunch, crunch. They watched their craft dance in the sun. Pippa fluffed her feathers. “We learned new knots,” she said. “We fixed a problem. We worked as a team. And we were brave.”

Max smiled. “I learned to try again.”

Tilly nodded. “Slow and steady can help.”

Benny wiggled his nose. “New ideas can make things better.”

Pippa chirped, “Crafting is making with our hearts and hands. Learning is trying, step by step. Innovation is thinking of new ways. Cooperation is helping each other. Bravery is trying even when we feel a little scared.”

The wind sang softly through the leaves. The Windy Wings spun and made a tiny flutter sound. The backyard smelled like apples and flowers. Everyone felt calm, proud, and bright as the sun.

Pippa looked at her friends. “Squawk! Let’s make something new again tomorrow,” she said.

“Yes!” they all cheered.

And the sunny backyard glowed with color, kindness, and the gentle spin of Windy Wings.

The End.
