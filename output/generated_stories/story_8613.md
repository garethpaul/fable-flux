# Dotty the Dolphin Learns to Try

In a cozy, comfortable nursery, soft lamps glowed like warm stars. Pillows felt squishy and smelled a little like fresh soap. Toys rested in tidy baskets. On the wall, a big blue poster showed letters and numbers. In a round tub filled with warm water, a playful dolphin named Dotty splashed gently.

“Whee!” squeaked Dotty, flipping her tail. Water went swish-swish and tiny drops tickled her nose.

Ms. Blossom, the kind teacher, smiled. “Good morning, Dotty. Today we’ll learn some letters and practice saying sorry when we make a mistake.”

Dotty bobbed up. “I like to splash and play! Do letters splash too?”

Ms. Blossom giggled. “Letters don’t splash, but they sing. Listen.” She pointed to the poster. “A says ‘aaa,’ B says ‘buh.’”

Dotty tried, “Aaa… buh!” She clapped her fins. Clap, clap!

Soon the children sat in a circle on a soft rug. The rug felt fuzzy under their toes. Dotty slid her tub close so she could listen. Ms. Blossom handed out little letter cards. “We will practice. Practice helps our brains grow strong.”

Dotty wanted to help, too. She flicked her tail to move nearer. But—swish! A tiny wave spilled over the tub. A little boy’s paper got wet and wrinkly.

“Oh no!” he said, frowning.

Dotty’s tummy felt twisty. “I didn’t mean to,” she whispered.

Ms. Blossom put a gentle hand on the boy’s shoulder. “Accidents can happen. Dotty, what can we do now?”

Dotty took a small breath. The nursery smelled like crayons and clean books. She remembered what Ms. Blossom said: Today we learn letters and saying sorry. Dotty lifted her fin. “I’m sorry I splashed your paper,” she said softly. “I will be careful.”

The boy’s face softened. “Thank you, Dotty.”

Ms. Blossom brought a new, dry paper. “That was a kind apology. Now, let’s practice together.”

They began again. “A says ‘aaa,’” everyone chanted. Dotty hummed along. But when she tried to draw A with a water-safe marker on her board, her fin wobbled. The line went squiggly.

Dotty sighed. “My A looks like a wiggly fish.”

Ms. Blossom knelt beside her. “It’s okay to wobble when we learn. We practice. Let’s try slowly. Up… down… across.”

Dotty felt the smooth board. She moved slow. Up… down… across. The marker made a soft squeak. She smiled. “It looks better!”

“Practice makes strong,” said Ms. Blossom.

They moved to numbers. “One,” said the children. Dotty tried to count her bubble rings. “One… two… three.” She blew bubbles. Pop, pop, pop! The children giggled at the popping sounds.

“Let’s keep the bubbles small,” Ms. Blossom reminded gently. “Small bubbles help papers stay dry.”

Dotty nodded. “I will practice small bubbles.” She took a tiny breath and blew. Pop. Just one little bubble. It made a soft plip when it reached the water. Dotty felt proud.

Later, Ms. Blossom gave a turn-taking game. Each child would match a letter to a picture. “D for dolphin,” said Ms. Blossom, holding up a card with a big D.

Dotty bounced. “That’s me!”

She swished again—too excited—and a drip hopped out of the tub onto a girl’s sleeve. The girl looked surprised.

Dotty’s cheeks felt warm. She remembered. Stop. Breathe. Say sorry. “I’m sorry for the drip,” Dotty said kindly. “Are you okay?”

The girl smiled. “I’m okay.”

Ms. Blossom nodded. “Good job noticing, Dotty. Thank you for your apology. Now, let’s practice gentle swishes.”

Dotty practiced a tiny swish. The water whispered, shhhh. No drips escaped.

“Wonderful,” said Ms. Blossom. “Who can match D?”

Dotty held up the D card. “D says ‘duh.’ D is for dolphin. D is for dot.” She tapped her board with her fin tip, dot, dot. The children repeated, “Duh, duh.”

They clapped. The room sounded like soft raindrops—pat, pat, pat.

At snack time, the nursery smelled like apples and crackers. Dotty nibbled a seaweed snack. It was salty and crunchy. She thought about the morning. She had made mistakes. She had said sorry. She had practiced. She felt a warm glow in her chest.

Ms. Blossom came by with a gold star sticker. “Dotty, you tried hard. You practiced your letters, your counting, and your gentle swishes. You apologized when you splashed. That is smart learning.”

Dotty beamed. “Learning feels good. I will keep practicing.”

Before nap time, the lights dimmed. Soft music floated like slow ripples. Dotty whispered to the children, “Let’s say our lesson.”

Together they said, “We learn by trying. We fix with sorry. We grow by practice.”

Dotty curled in her tub. The water hugged her like a warm blanket. She gave one last tiny bubble. Pop.

“Tomorrow,” she whispered, “I will learn more letters. I will be careful. I will practice.”

Ms. Blossom tucked a blanket over the children. “Yes,” she said softly. “Education is an adventure. Every day we try again.”

Dotty closed her eyes, happy and calm. In the quiet nursery, the letters on the wall seemed to smile back.

The End.
