# Kora the Calm Koala on the Porch

Kora the koala liked to sit on the peaceful front porch every morning. The porch was warm and wooden. The boards felt smooth under her fuzzy paws. The breeze smelled like eucalyptus tea. The birds sang little chirps that sounded like tiny bells.

Kora took a deep breath. “Ahh,” she said softly. “This is my calm spot.”

She had a blue puzzle on a small table. The puzzle had a picture of the moon. The pieces were shiny and cool. Kora loved puzzles because they were like little mysteries. Today she wanted to finish the round moon piece right in the middle.

Her neighbor, a small possum named Pip, tiptoed up the porch steps. His tail swished. His eyes were bright.

“Hi, Kora!” Pip squeaked. “Can I help with the puzzle?”

Kora smiled kindly. “Hello, Pip. Yes, we can do it together. Let’s use gentle hands and quiet voices.”

Pip nodded quickly. “Gentle, quiet. Got it!”

They picked up the tiny pieces. The porch smelled like sweet leaves. The wind whispered around the posts. Kora hummed a soft tune as she looked for edge pieces.

But Pip felt excited. He grabbed three pieces at once. “I’ll find the moon!” he said loudly. He pushed the pieces together. They did not fit. The cardboard bent with a little crinkle.

Kora’s heart squeezed. Her ears got warm. She felt frustrated. She wanted to shout, “Stop!” But she remembered her calm breath.

She put a paw on her chest. In and out. In and out. “I can use my words,” she told herself. “I can be kind and clear.”

“Kora?” Pip waved a piece. “Is this the moon middle?”

Kora looked at the bent piece. She felt a bubble of tears, but she blinked them back. “Pip,” she said in a gentle voice, “I feel upset when the pieces get bent. I need us to go slower.”

Pip blinked. “Oh.” He looked at his paws. “I didn’t know. I’m sorry.”

“Thank you for saying sorry,” Kora said. She patted the table. “We can fix it. Let’s learn how to check the shapes. Then we can save the pieces.”

Pip’s ears perked up. “Learn? Okay!”

Kora picked up a piece. It was smooth on the edges and had a tiny star. “Look,” she said. “First, we look for the flat sides for the edges. Then we look for colors. See the silver line? That might be the moon shadow.”

She held the piece to Pip’s nose. “Does it feel smooth? Does it match the shape?”

Pip rubbed it with his thumb. “It feels cool and smooth,” he said. “The shape is like a little hill!”

“Yes,” Kora said. “Now turn it slowly until it fits. No rushing.”

Pip turned the piece gently. It clicked into place. “We did it!” he squealed, but softer this time.

Kora smiled. “Great job. Thank you for being gentle.”

They kept going. Kora led with calm steps. “Let’s take turns,” she said. “You try one, then I try one.” Pip agreed and waited when it was Kora’s turn. They listened to the birds together. They felt the tiny bumps on each piece. They smelled the clean morning air.

After a while, a little gust of wind whooshed over the porch. A piece slid off the table. Pip jumped to grab it and bumped Kora’s cup of tea. The cup wobbled. A drop splashed on Kora’s paw. It was warm and sticky-sweet.

Kora paused. Her tummy tightened. She wanted to say, “Pip, why did you do that?” But she took a slow breath. “I can speak up kindly,” she reminded herself.

“Pip,” she said, “the tea splash surprised me. I need your help to keep the table steady. Can you hold the edge while I pick up the piece?”

Pip nodded fast. “Yes! I can help!” He pressed his little paws on the table edge and stood very still. Kora picked up the fallen piece and wiped her paw with a napkin.

“Thank you,” she said. “You listened. That helps me feel calm.”

Pip smiled. “I like helping.”

They finished the puzzle together. The moon picture shone in the middle. It looked like a bright button in the sky. Kora and Pip sat back and listened to the porch creak softly. The birds sang a sweet song, like “cheep-cheep, tweet-tweet.”

Pip whispered, “Kora, I learned something.”

Kora tilted her head. “What did you learn?”

“I learned that going slow is kind to the puzzle,” Pip said. “And using gentle hands is kind to you. And when I don’t know, I can ask.”

Kora’s eyes shone. “I learned too. When I feel upset, I can breathe and use my words. I can say what I need. That’s being kind to me and kind to you.”

They bumped paws in a quiet little high-five. The moon on the table glowed softly in the morning light.

Kora poured a fresh cup of tea and a tiny cup of warm water for Pip. Together they sipped. The tea smelled like lemon and leaves. The warm cups felt cozy in their hands.

Kora looked at the puzzle and then at Pip. “Kindness makes our porch feel peaceful,” she said. “Kindness to others and to ourselves.”

Pip nodded. “Kindness helps the puzzle too!”

They both laughed, a small happy sound that floated down the steps and into the day.

The End.
