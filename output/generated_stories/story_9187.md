# Tully Turtle’s Quiet Study Corner

In a cozy study corner, Tully the shy turtle liked to sit very still. The corner had a soft blue rug, a little desk, and a warm lamp that glowed like a friendly firefly. On the desk were smooth crayons, a stack of paper, and a tiny jar of shiny buttons that clicked when the jar moved. The air smelled like fresh paper and lemony clean wood.

Tully whispered, “Good morning, desk. Good morning, crayons.” His voice was soft like a leaf landing on grass. Tully was shy, but he had a big heart. Today, he had a small list on a sticky note. It said: draw a picture, tidy the books, and say thank you.

“I can do it,” Tully said, taking a deep breath. He pulled out a red crayon. It felt warm and waxy in his little turtle hand. He began to draw a sunny shell.

Just then, a gentle paw tapped the doorway. “Hi, Tully,” said Miso the mouse. Miso’s whiskers wiggled, and her tiny feet pat-patted on the rug. “Can I color too?”

Tully felt his cheeks warm. Sometimes, people made him feel small like a pebble. But he remembered: respect means being kind and sharing space and things. He nodded. “Yes. Please come in. We take turns.”

Miso smiled. “Thank you!” She sat beside Tully and smelled the crayons. “Mmm, yummy colors,” she giggled.

Tully pointed to the sticky note. “I am doing my tasks. First, draw. Then, tidy. Then… say thank you.” He looked at Miso. “We can do it together.”

Miso’s eyes sparkled. “I respect your plan,” she said. “You go first.”

Tully colored the sun on his turtle shell. He said, “My turn is done. Your turn.”

Miso picked a blue crayon and drew a little sky. “Soft strokes,” she whispered, making the crayon swish-swish. The paper felt smooth under their fingers, and the crayons made a gentle scratchy sound.

Soon, Sori the snail peeked in, her shell shiny and speckled. “Hello,” she said slowly. “May I sit near the lamp? It is warm.”

Tully swallowed. More friends! He felt a flutter in his tummy like tiny birds. But he looked at his sticky note again and smiled. Respect means we welcome others and listen.

“Yes,” Tully said. “Please sit. We are drawing. We take turns.” He pushed a green crayon toward Sori. “Would you like this?”

Sori nodded. “Thank you, Tully. I accept your kind share.” She drew a green leaf and hummed softly. The hum sounded like a happy bee.

When their picture was finished, the paper looked bright and friendly. There were suns and shells, skies and leaves. Tully clapped softly with his little turtle hands. “We did it. The picture is done.” He put a small check next to “draw a picture.”

Next task: tidy the books. The study corner had three books on the floor. One book was big and blue. One was small and red. One had a shiny silver star, and when Tully touched it, it felt cool and smooth.

Miso hopped up. “I can grab the small one!”

Sori slid forward. “I can push the big one… slowly.”

Tully picked up the star book. “We put them on the shelf with care,” he said. “We respect our things.”

They moved carefully. The books made soft thumps on the shelf. Tully lined them up straight and neat. His heart felt calm, like lake water.

Miso looked at the desk. The crayons were in a little cup, but some were on their sides. “Can we tidy these too?” she asked.

“Yes,” Tully said. “Thank you for asking.” He liked that Miso spoke kindly and waited. Respect is waiting and asking, not grabbing.

Together, they placed each crayon gently into the cup. Tap, tap, tap. The colors looked like a rainbow standing in a row.

Finally, Tully looked at the last task: say thank you. He turned to his friends. His voice was still soft, but it was steady and warm. “Miso, thank you for sharing and waiting your turn. Sori, thank you for helping with the big book and humming a happy song. I feel safe when we respect each other.”

Miso’s whiskers twitched. “Thank you, Tully, for letting us join your plan.”

Sori nodded. “Thank you for the warm lamp and the green crayon.”

They all smiled. The study corner felt extra cozy now, like a hug. The lamp glowed, the rug was tidy, and the picture lay in the center of the desk, bright and beautiful.

Tully took a button from the jar, shook it gently, and listened to the soft click. “Let’s add a shiny button to our picture,” he said. “It will remind us to be kind.” He glued the button in the corner. It sparkled like a tiny star.

Miso whispered, “I like how we asked, waited, and helped.”

Sori said, “That is respect.”

Tully’s shy heart felt big and brave. He looked at the sticky note. All the tasks had neat little checks. He felt proud, not loud, but proud inside like a warm light.

“We learned today,” Tully said. “Respect means we listen, we share, we wait, and we say thank you. When we do that, everyone feels welcome.”

The friends sat quietly for a moment, enjoying the calm of their clean, bright corner. The crayons smelled sweet. The lamp hummed softly. The picture sparkled.

Tully tucked the sticky note into the star book and closed it gently. “Tomorrow, we can learn again,” he said.

“Yes,” said Miso and Sori together. “Together.”

The End.
