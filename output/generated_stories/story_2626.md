# Toby's Bubble Boat Adventure

Toby the turtle peeked around the bathroom door. His green shell gleamed under the bright lights. The big white bathtub looked like an ocean waiting for him.

"Bath time, Toby!" called Mama Turtle from down the hall.

Toby's little head pulled back into his shell. "Do I have to go in alone?" he whispered.

The bathroom felt so big and echoey. Water droplets sparkled on the faucet like tiny diamonds. The fluffy towels hung like soft clouds on their hooks.

Mama Turtle walked in with a warm smile. "What's wrong, little one?"

"The tub is so big," Toby said quietly. "And I'm so small."

Mama Turtle knelt down. "Would you like to make something special for bath time?"

Toby's eyes grew wide. "Make something?"

"Yes! We can craft a special boat just for you," Mama said. She gathered some supplies from the cabinet. "But we'll need to work together."

Toby watched as Mama pulled out a plastic bottle, some tape, and colorful foam shapes. The bottle was clear and smooth. The foam pieces felt soft and squishy.

"First, we need to plan our time," Mama explained. "Bath water takes five minutes to fill. Can we make a boat in five minutes?"

Toby nodded eagerly. "I think so!"

"Great! You can stick these foam shapes on the bottle while I prepare the water," Mama said.

Toby carefully peeled off the first foam star. It was bright yellow like sunshine. He pressed it onto the bottle. It stuck perfectly!

"Look, Mama! It's working!" Toby called out.

"Beautiful job!" Mama replied as she turned on the faucet. Warm water began to flow. "Tell me about your boat."

Toby added a red circle. "This is the captain's wheel!" Then he stuck on a blue triangle. "And this is the sail!"

The water gurgled and splashed as it filled the tub. Steam rose up like gentle clouds. Toby kept crafting, his shell no longer hiding his excited face.

"Two more minutes," Mama announced. "How are we doing?"

"Almost done!" Toby said. He added green squares for windows and an orange heart for good luck. His little flippers moved quickly but carefully.

"Time's up!" Mama said, turning off the water. "Let's test your boat!"

Toby climbed into the warm, bubbly water. It felt cozy and nice. He gently placed his crafted boat on the surface.

The bottle bobbed and floated perfectly! The foam decorations stayed on tight. Toby's boat sailed across the tub like a real ship.

"It works!" Toby cheered. "My boat really works!"

He pushed it gently with his flipper. The boat glided through the bubbles, making tiny waves. The yellow star caught the light and sparkled.

"You were such a good helper," Mama said. "You communicated what you wanted, we cooperated together, and you kept trying even when it felt hard."

Toby smiled proudly. "Can we make another boat tomorrow?"

"Of course! What kind would you like to make next?"

"Maybe a rocket ship!" Toby giggled, sailing his boat in happy circles.

The bathroom didn't feel big and scary anymore. It felt like his special crafting place where wonderful things could happen. Toby splashed gently, his boat dancing on the waves, knowing that with a little creativity and help from someone who cared, he could make anything possible.

The End.
