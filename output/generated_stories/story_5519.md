# Pippin's Bubble Friends

Pippin the penguin waddled into the bathroom with a big smile. His orange feet made soft slapping sounds on the cool tile floor. Today was bath time, and Pippin loved making bubbles!

"Time for my bubble adventure!" chirped Pippin. He turned on the warm water. Steam rose up like fluffy clouds. The water felt nice and cozy on his flippers.

Pippin squeezed the bubble soap. Pop! Pop! Pop! Tiny bubbles danced in the air. But then Pippin noticed something special. Each bubble looked different!

"Hello there!" said a round, sparkly bubble floating by. "I'm Shimmer!"

"And I'm Zigzag!" called a wobbly, wiggly bubble. "I dance differently than everyone else!"

"I'm Tiny!" squeaked the smallest bubble. "Some penguins think I'm too little to play."

Pippin's eyes grew wide with wonder. "You can all talk! How amazing!"

But then Pippin saw Rainbow, a bubble with beautiful colors swirling inside. Rainbow floated alone in the corner, looking sad.

"Why aren't you playing with the others?" asked Pippin gently.

Rainbow's colors dimmed. "The other bubbles say I'm too different. My colors make them feel strange. They don't want to play with me."

Pippin's heart felt heavy. He knew how it felt to be different. Sometimes other animals thought penguins were odd because they couldn't fly like other birds.

"Being different makes you special!" said Pippin. He swam closer to Rainbow. "Your colors are the most beautiful thing I've ever seen!"

Pippin had an idea. "Would you like to be my friend? We could play together!"

Rainbow's colors brightened. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" Pippin splashed happily. "Let's show everyone how wonderful you are!"

Pippin called to all the bubbles. "Come see Rainbow's amazing colors! They're like a tiny sunset!"

Shimmer floated over first. "Wow! Those colors are incredible!"

"They make pretty patterns!" added Zigzag, dancing around Rainbow.

"You're so cool!" squeaked Tiny. "I wish I had colors like yours!"

Soon all the bubbles were playing together. They made a bubble parade around the bathtub. Rainbow led the way, showing off beautiful swirls of red, blue, and yellow.

"Thank you, Pippin," whispered Rainbow. "You helped me make friends by being kind."

"Everyone deserves friends," said Pippin. "When we're kind to others, we discover how wonderful they are!"

The bubbles played until the water grew cool. One by one, they popped with tiny giggles. But Rainbow stayed the longest.

"I'll never forget your kindness," said Rainbow before floating away.

Pippin climbed out of the tub feeling warm and happy. His feathers were clean and fluffy. But the best part wasn't being clean – it was helping Rainbow feel loved.

"Mom!" called Pippin as he dried off with his soft towel. "I made new friends today!"

"That's wonderful, sweetie," said Mom, wrapping him in a cozy hug. "How did you make friends?"

"I was kind to someone who felt different," said Pippin proudly. "And I learned that being different makes everyone special!"

From that day on, whenever Pippin took a bath, he always looked for bubbles who might need a friend. He learned that the smallest act of kindness could make the biggest difference.

And Rainbow? Well, Rainbow visited Pippin's bath every week, bringing new bubble friends who had learned that being different was something to celebrate!

The End.
