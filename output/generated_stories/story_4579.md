# Bella Bird Finds Her Song

In a quiet attic, high above a sleepy house, lived Bella, a small singing bird. The attic smelled like old wood and soft dust. Sunbeams slipped through a tiny window and made golden stripes on the floor. Boxes sat in neat stacks. A round clock ticked, tick-tick, tick-tick. The air felt calm and warm.

Bella loved to sing. She sang when the morning sun peeked in. She sang when the rain tapped on the roof. Her song was sweet and clear, like little bells. But today, Bella felt a flutter in her tummy. She wanted to learn a new song. A big, brave song. A song with high notes and low notes, and a strong, steady rhythm.

“I want to grow my voice,” Bella whispered. “I can try. I can practice.”

She hopped onto an old wooden trunk and took a deep breath. “La-la-la—” Her voice squeaked. “Oh!” She blinked. The note felt too high.

From a dusty painting on the wall, a mouse named Pip peeked out. He had bright eyes and soft gray fur. “Hello, Bella!” Pip squeaked. “Sing your biggest song! Louder, louder!”

Bella smiled. “I will try.” She stood tall and tried again. “La-la—” Her note wobbled and wiggled, then dropped with a tiny plop. Bella’s feathers drooped.

Pip clapped his tiny paws. “Try again! Don’t stop!”

Bella took another breath. “La-la-laaa—” Her throat felt tight. The clock went tick-tick, tick-tick. Bella’s heart went thump-thump. She shook her head. “This is hard,” she said softly. “I’m not there yet.”

From a cozy box, a sleepy cat named Luna lifted her head. “Mrrr?” Luna purred. “Sing softly, little bird. My ears are resting.”

Bella looked at Luna. “I am practicing,” she said kindly. “I want to grow. I will sing, but I will also be gentle. My song can wait for loud.”

Pip hopped closer. “But I want to hear it now!” he squeaked. “Sing big! Sing fast!”

Bella felt her tummy flutter again. She liked Pip. She wanted to please him. But her throat still felt tight, and Luna needed quiet. Bella took a slow breath. The sunbeam felt warm on her wings. She remembered something she told herself: I can try, I can learn, I can rest. I can say what I need.

“Pip,” Bella said in a clear, kind voice, “I am learning. I need to go step by step. And I want to keep the attic quiet for Luna. I will practice softly today. You can listen if you like.”

Pip’s whiskers twitched. He frowned, then nodded. “Okay,” he said. “Step by step.”

Bella smiled. She made a plan. “First, low notes,” she whispered. “Then a little higher. Then a tiny rest.”

She sang a soft “loo-loo-loo,” like little bubbles. The notes felt smooth. She paused and took a sip of water from a tiny lid. She hummed “mmm-mmm-mmm,” like a warm hug. Her throat relaxed.

“Good plan,” Luna purred, curling her tail. “My ears like your soft song.”

Bella tried a higher note. “La—” It wobbled, but not as much as before. She felt proud. “I’m getting better,” she said. “Not yet, but closer.”

Pip clapped again, softer this time. “I hear it! You grew a little!”

Bella nodded. She kept her steps small. Low notes, rest. Medium notes, rest. Higher notes, rest. She listened to her body. She listened to the quiet attic. She listened to her heart.

At noon, the sunbeam moved across the floor. Dust sparkled like tiny stars. Bella took one more deep breath. “I will try the brave part now,” she whispered. She looked at Luna. “Is this okay?”

Luna opened one eye and smiled. “Yes, little bird. I am ready.”

Bella sang her new song. It started soft and low, like a warm blanket. Then it rose, bright and clear, like sunshine on snow. The high note came. It wobbled, then steadied. Bella held it, gentle but strong. The clock went tick-tick, and Bella’s song danced around it.

Pip’s eyes shone. “You did it!” he cheered.

Bella landed near her friends. Her chest felt light and proud. “I did it,” she said. “I practiced. I rested. I said what I needed. I kept trying.”

Luna nuzzled her paw against Bella’s wing. “You grew your song, and you cared for yourself and for others.”

Bella chirped happily. “When something is hard, I can take small steps. I can say, ‘Not yet, but soon.’ I can set gentle rules to keep my space and my friends safe. I can try again and again.”

Pip grinned. “Next time, can you teach me your song?”

“Yes,” Bella said. “We will learn together. We will be patient. We will listen. We will grow.”

The attic felt cozy and bright. The sun warmed the wood. The air smelled sweet, like old books and soft dust. Bella tucked her head under her wing for a moment, then lifted it high.

“I can learn new things,” she sang softly. “I can take my time. I can say what I need. And I can try again.”

The End.
