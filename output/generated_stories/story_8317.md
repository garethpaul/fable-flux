# Shelly's Fair Share Adventure

Shelly the turtle lived in a cozy study corner. She had a soft green shell and tiny black eyes. Shelly was very shy. She liked to hide in her shell when other animals came to visit.

The study corner had tall bookshelves. It had a big wooden desk. It had a sunny window too. Every day, different forest animals came to read books and learn new things.

One morning, Shelly peeked out from behind a book. She saw Rabbit eating a big pile of carrots. She saw Squirrel munching on nuts. She saw Mouse nibbling cheese. Shelly's tummy rumbled. She was hungry too.

"Excuse me," whispered Shelly in her tiny voice. "Could I have some food please?"

But the other animals were talking loudly. They did not hear her small voice. Rabbit kept eating his orange carrots. Squirrel kept crunching his brown nuts. Mouse kept nibbling his yellow cheese.

Shelly felt sad. Her tummy rumbled again. "That's not fair," she thought. "Everyone else gets to eat, but I don't."

Then Shelly remembered something important. Her mama had taught her about being fair. "When something is not right, you must speak up," Mama had said. "Use your voice to help make things better."

Shelly took a deep breath. She stuck her head out of her shell. "Hello everyone!" she said in a louder voice. "I'm hungry too. Could we please share the food?"

Rabbit's ears perked up. "Oh my! I didn't see you there, Shelly!"

Squirrel stopped crunching. "We're sorry! We didn't hear you before."

Mouse smiled. "Of course we can share! Sharing is the right thing to do."

The animals looked at all their food. They had so much! Rabbit had twenty carrots. Squirrel had fifteen nuts. Mouse had ten pieces of cheese.

"Let's make it fair for everyone," said Rabbit. He counted out five carrots for each animal.

"Good idea!" said Squirrel. He gave everyone three nuts.

"And here's cheese for all of us," said Mouse. He shared two pieces with each friend.

Now everyone had the same amount of food. Shelly felt happy. Her tummy felt full. The orange carrots were crunchy and sweet. The brown nuts were nutty and good. The yellow cheese was creamy and yummy.

"Thank you for listening to me," said Shelly. "And thank you for sharing."

"Thank you for speaking up," said Rabbit. "It's important to tell us when something isn't fair."

"We want everyone to feel happy and included," said Squirrel.

"Next time, we'll remember to ask if anyone else is hungry," said Mouse.

From that day on, the study corner became an even better place. When animals brought food to share, they always listened carefully. They made sure everyone got a fair amount. They took care of each other.

Shelly learned something wonderful. Even shy animals can use their voice for good things. When something isn't fair, it's important to speak up. When we listen to each other, we can make things better for everyone.

The study corner stayed clean and beautiful. The animals took good care of their special place. They shared their healthy foods. They listened to each other's needs. And they all became the very best of friends.

The End.
