# Mango the Brave and the Busy Garden Shed

Mango the monkey loved to help. Mango was small, soft, and very brave. He had bright eyes and warm, brown fur. One sunny morning, Mango heard a little clatter. Clink, clank, clink!

“What is that sound?” Mango asked, tilting his head.

The sound came from the garden shed. The shed smelled like fresh soil and sweet grass. The wooden door was a bit squeaky. Creak! Mango peeked inside.

It was dim and cool. Sunlight made little golden spots on the floor. Shelves held pots, seeds, and a red watering can. A broom leaned in the corner. In the middle of the shed stood a large, wobbly bucket. It was tipped on its side, and something under it was shaking.

“Hello?” Mango called softly. “Is someone in there?”

“Peep! Peep!” came a tiny sound.

Mango’s heart thumped. “I will help,” he said. “But I must be careful and brave.”

Mango remembered what Mama Monkey always said: “Be safe. Be kind. Be helpful. Use your imagination to find a way.”

Mango took a deep breath. He smelled the earthy dirt. He felt the cool floor under his feet. He listened. Clink, clank. “Peep! Peep!”

“The bucket is too heavy,” Mango said. “I need a plan.”

He used his imagination. Mango looked at the tools. He saw a long wooden stick. He saw a small garden shovel. He saw thick yellow gloves. He imagined he was a gentle builder. He imagined the stick was a strong lever. He imagined the gloves were brave monkey hands.

“I can make a lever!” Mango said. “I can lift the bucket.” He put on the gloves. They felt snug and safe.

Mango slid the stick under the edge of the bucket. He placed the small shovel under the stick to make a little fulcrum. “I am ready,” he whispered. “One, two, three—lift!”

The bucket tipped. A little bird hopped out! It had soft gray feathers and bright black eyes.

“Peep!” said the bird. It fluffed its wings and looked up at Mango.

“Hello, little friend,” said Mango. “Are you okay?”

“Peep,” said the bird, and it blinked. It looked thirsty.

Mango felt proud and careful. He thought about the garden. The garden is for everyone, he knew. The shed is part of the garden. We all must take care of it. That is civic responsibility—taking care of the places we share.

“I will help more,” Mango said. “You need water.”

Mango found the red watering can. It was cool and smooth. He filled a small cup from the can. Drip, drip, drip. He set the cup on the floor. The bird sipped. Sip, sip. Its little beak made tiny tapping sounds.

Mango smiled. “I am glad you are safe.”

Just then, Mrs. Daya, the gardener, walked by the shed. She wore a big hat and had kind eyes. “Mango? Are you in the shed?” she called.

“Yes!” Mango said. “I used a lever! I helped a bird.”

Mrs. Daya peeked in and gasped softly. “Oh, you brave monkey! You used your imagination and your mind. You solved a problem and helped another. Thank you.”

Mango’s ears felt warm with pride. “I also want to keep the shed tidy,” he said. “The bucket was wobbly. That is not safe.”

“That is a smart idea,” said Mrs. Daya. “Let’s work together.”

They set the bucket upright and put it on a bottom shelf. They swept the floor, swish, swish, swish. Mango stacked the flower pots. Clink, clink. Mrs. Daya checked the door and oiled the squeak. Squeak… quiet!

The little bird hopped to the door and looked at the bright garden. Mango spoke gently. “Little bird, would you like to go outside?” he asked.

“Peep!” said the bird. It hopped across the warm threshold and fluttered into the sun. It chirped a happy note, high and sweet. Mango waved. “Goodbye, friend!”

Mrs. Daya placed a small sign by the buckets: “Keep Lids On. Store Safely.” She smiled at Mango. “You helped the bird. You helped the shed. You helped our garden. That is being a good neighbor.”

Mango felt a glow in his chest. “I used my imagination,” he said. “I thought of a lever. I used safe tools. I asked for help when you came.”

Mrs. Daya nodded. “You also spoke kindly. You listened. That is good communication.”

She handed Mango a little blue ribbon that smelled faintly of lavender. It said, “Helper.” Mango’s tail curled with joy.

“Thank you,” Mango said.

“Thank you, Mango,” said Mrs. Daya. “I am grateful.”

They stepped outside. The sun warmed Mango’s fur. Bees hummed. The bird sang in a nearby tree. Mango looked at the shed, now neat and safe. He felt brave, kind, and ready to help again.

Mango said, “When we see a problem, we can imagine a way to fix it. We can be careful. We can help others. We can take care of our shared places.”

Mrs. Daya smiled. “Yes. Little ideas can make big help.”

Mango grinned. “I will keep imagining and helping.”

The bird chirped, “Peep-peep!” as if to say, “Thank you!”

Mango waved and whispered, “You’re welcome.”

And the garden shed stayed tidy, safe, and happy—because a courageous monkey used imagination, spoke kindly, and cared for others and the place they all shared.

The End.
