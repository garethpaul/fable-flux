# Robo-Pup Plays Fair

In a quiet study corner, with soft rugs and little shelves of books, lived a helpful robot dog named Robo-Pup. His nose glowed a tiny blue, and his ears went “beep” when he was excited. The study corner smelled like crayons and warm paper. Sunlight made gentle squares on the floor.

Robo-Pup loved to help. He loved to learn. He had a small screen on his collar that said helpful words. Today, it said, “Be fair.” Robo-Pup wiggled his metal tail. “Be fair,” he barked in a friendly beep-bop voice. “That means justice!”

Robo-Pup’s friends, Maya and Leo, came into the study corner. They carried soft balls and colorful cones. “Let’s play a mini sport inside,” said Maya. “Let’s play roll-and-run!” The game was simple: roll the ball across the rug, run to touch a cone, then clap three times.

“I like games,” said Robo-Pup. His feet made a click-click on the floor. “Games are fun. Justice means we all get turns.”

Maya rolled first. The ball made a quiet “whirr” on the rug. Maya ran, touched the cone, and clapped. “Yay!” she cheered.

Leo rolled next. The ball bumped into a book, then went around it. “Oops!” he said, and giggled. He ran, touched the cone, and clapped. “Yay!”

Robo-Pup wagged. “My turn?” he asked.

“Yes!” said Maya. “But wait,” said Leo. He pointed to the cone. “I want it to be closer to me.”

Maya frowned. “That’s not fair,” she said softly. The room felt a little heavy, like a quiet cloud.

Robo-Pup’s ears went “beep.” His screen blinked, “Fair turns.” He tilted his shiny head. “Justice means the same rules for everyone,” he said gently.

Leo crossed his arms. “But I want to win,” he said. His face felt hot, and he stomped one foot. The ball made a tiny hop.

Robo-Pup sat and took a slow robot breath. He could hear the hum of the lamp. He could smell the lemony clean on the table. “Leo,” he said, “let’s think. How can we make this just and kind?”

Leo looked at the cone. He looked at Maya. He looked at Robo-Pup’s kind blue nose. “I don’t know,” he whispered.

Robo-Pup tapped his collar. The screen showed simple pictures: a cone in the middle, three smiling faces, a big green check mark. “We put the cone in the middle,” said Robo-Pup. “We all play by the same rule. That is justice. That is fair.”

Maya nodded. “And we can all cheer,” she said. “Even when it’s not our turn.”

Leo’s arms came uncrossed. “Okay,” he said. “Let’s try.”

They moved the cone to the middle. The rug felt soft on their knees. The ball felt smooth and cool in their hands.

“Robo-Pup, you roll,” said Maya.

Robo-Pup put his paw on the ball. “Boop!” The ball rolled with a gentle “whirr.” He ran—click-click—touched the cone, and clapped three times. “I did it!” he beeped.

Maya’s turn. Roll, run, touch, clap! “I did it!” she laughed. Her laugh sounded like tiny bells.

Leo’s turn. He rolled carefully. The ball went straight. He ran, touched the cone, and clapped. “I did it too!” he said. His smile felt warm, like the sun on the rug.

Then a small problem came. The ball knocked a pencil off the table. It made a “tap-tap” on the floor. “Oh no,” said Maya. “Is that okay?”

Robo-Pup looked at the pencil. He looked at the rules. His screen blinked, “Fair fix.” “Justice means we fix little problems the same way for all,” he said. “If the ball hits something, we pause. We pick it up. We try again.”

Leo nodded. “For everyone,” he said. “Even me.”

“Even me,” said Maya.

“Even me,” said Robo-Pup, wagging.

They tried again. Roll, run, touch, clap! Each turn had cheers. “Go, Maya!” “Go, Leo!” “Go, Robo-Pup!” Their voices filled the study corner with happy sounds.

After a while, Maya said, “I am tired.” Leo said, “Me too.” Robo-Pup’s ears gave a soft “beep-beep.” He brought a small water bottle. “Water for all,” he said. “Justice means we share and care.”

They sat on the rug. They sipped water. The cool water felt nice. Their cheeks were pink and happy. Robo-Pup looked at his friends. “We all played,” he said. “We all had turns. We all followed the same rules. How do you feel?”

“I feel proud,” said Maya. She hugged the ball.

“I feel calm,” said Leo. He patted the cone. “I like playing fair. It feels good in my heart.”

Robo-Pup wagged. “That is justice,” he said softly. “Justice means fair turns, fair rules, and kind fixes. It means we accept each other. In sports and in study time, we play fair.”

Maya smiled at Leo. “I accept you, even when you want to win.”

Leo smiled back. “I accept you, even when you roll fast.”

Robo-Pup’s screen showed a warm yellow star. “We learned,” he said. “We used our knowledge to make fair rules. We used acceptance to make smiles.”

They cleaned up the study corner together. The cones made a soft “clack” as they stacked. The ball made a “thump” in the basket. The room smelled like crayons again. The sun made golden lines on the floor.

Robo-Pup wagged one more time. “Be fair,” he said. “Be kind. Use justice every day.”

Maya and Leo said, “Yes!”

The End.
