# Koko's Peaceful Garden Helper Day

Koko the koala stretched his fluffy gray arms and yawned. The morning sun peeked through the windows of the cozy garden shed where he lived. He could smell the sweet scent of flowers and hear birds chirping outside.

"What a beautiful day to help in the garden!" Koko said softly to himself.

As Koko stepped outside, he saw his friend Bunny hopping around frantically. Her white fur was all messy, and her ears were drooping.

"Oh no, oh no!" Bunny cried. "The big storm last night knocked over all the flower pots! The garden is such a mess!"

Koko took a deep breath and walked slowly over to Bunny. "Hello, Bunny," he said in his gentle voice. "I can see you're feeling worried. Would you like some help?"

Bunny stopped hopping and looked at Koko. "But there's so much to do! We need to fix everything right now!"

Koko nodded calmly. "You're right, there is a lot to do. But when we feel overwhelmed, it helps to take slow, deep breaths first." He showed Bunny how to breathe in slowly through his nose and out through his mouth. "Can you try this with me?"

Bunny copied Koko, taking three deep breaths. "That does feel better," she said, her ears perking up a little.

Just then, Squirrel scampered down from a tree. "I heard there was trouble! I want to help too!" he chattered quickly.

"Wonderful!" said Koko. "But first, let's all take a moment to feel calm and peaceful inside. When our minds are calm, we can work together much better."

The three friends sat in a circle on the soft grass. Koko taught them to close their eyes and listen to the gentle sounds around them - the buzzing bees, the rustling leaves, and their own quiet breathing.

"Now," said Koko, opening his eyes, "let's make a plan together. Bunny, what do you think needs to be done first?"

"Well," Bunny said thoughtfully, "we should pick up the broken pots and save any flowers that aren't hurt."

"That's a great idea," said Koko. "Squirrel, what would you like to do?"

"I can gather the scattered seeds!" Squirrel said excitedly but more calmly than before.

"Perfect," smiled Koko. "I'll get fresh soil from my shed to replant everything. But remember, we don't need to rush. We can work at a peaceful pace."

As they worked together, Koko showed his friends how to stay calm when things felt difficult. When Bunny got frustrated with a stubborn pot, Koko said, "It's okay to feel frustrated. Let's take three deep breaths and try a different way."

When Squirrel started working too fast and dropped some seeds, Koko gently reminded him, "Slow and steady helps us do our best work."

By lunchtime, the garden looked beautiful again. The friends sat under a shady tree, feeling proud and peaceful.

"Thank you, Koko," said Bunny. "You taught us that taking care of ourselves helps us take care of others too."

"And working together peacefully made everything easier!" added Squirrel.

Koko smiled warmly. "When we stay calm, breathe deeply, and communicate kindly, we can solve any problem together. Taking care of our feelings is just as important as taking care of our garden."

The three friends enjoyed a quiet lunch, listening to the happy sounds of their beautiful, peaceful garden.

The End.
