# Joey's Big Helper Day

Joey the kangaroo was a bouncy little joey who loved to hop around his mama's home office. The office had a big wooden desk, colorful books on shelves, and a comfy rolling chair that spun around and around.

"Hop, hop, hop!" Joey giggled as he bounced between the desk legs. His soft gray fur felt warm in the morning sunlight that streamed through the window.

Mama Kangaroo was typing on her computer, making clickety-clack sounds with her fingers. "Good morning, my sweet Joey," she said with a smile. "I have so much work to do today."

Joey noticed that Mama looked tired. Her shoulders were droopy, and she kept rubbing her eyes. "Mama, are you okay?" Joey asked, tilting his fuzzy head to one side.

"Oh, Joey, I'm just feeling a bit tired and hungry. I forgot to eat breakfast because I was so busy," Mama sighed. "And I have three important meetings today."

Joey's little heart felt sad seeing his mama feeling low. He wanted to help, but he was just a small joey. What could he do?

Then Joey remembered something. Last week, Mama had taught him how to make a healthy snack! "I know, I know!" Joey bounced excitedly. "I can help you, Mama!"

"That's very sweet, Joey, but you're still learning," Mama said gently.

"Please, Mama? You taught me how to wash apples and spread peanut butter. I practiced and practiced!" Joey's eyes sparkled with determination.

Mama thought for a moment. Joey had been working hard to learn new skills. "Alright, my helpful joey. But we'll do it together, step by step."

In the small kitchen next to the office, Joey carefully washed two red apples under the cool running water. The apples felt smooth and smelled fresh and sweet. "Scrub, scrub, scrub," Joey sang as he cleaned them.

Next, with Mama watching closely, Joey used a safe plastic knife to cut the apples into slices. "Slow and careful," he whispered to himself, remembering Mama's teaching.

Then came the tricky part - spreading peanut butter on whole wheat crackers. Joey's little paws were shaky at first, but he took his time. "I can do this," he said, focusing hard.

"You're doing wonderfully, Joey!" Mama cheered. "Look how much you've learned!"

Joey felt proud and warm inside. He arranged the apple slices and peanut butter crackers on a pretty blue plate, adding some orange carrot sticks for extra crunch and color.

"Here you go, Mama!" Joey presented the healthy snack with a big hop. "This will give you energy for your meetings!"

Mama's face lit up like sunshine. "Oh, Joey! This looks delicious and so nutritious! You made this all by yourself!" She took a big bite of the crunchy apple. "Mmm, perfect!"

As Mama ate her healthy snack, her energy returned. Her eyes became bright again, and she sat up straight in her chair.

"Thank you, my wonderful joey," Mama said, giving him the biggest hug. "You saw that I needed help, and you used the skills you've been learning to take care of me. That shows such kindness and responsibility!"

Joey's chest puffed up with pride. He had learned that practicing skills and caring for others felt even better than just hopping around. From that day on, Joey loved helping Mama in the office, and he kept practicing new skills every single day.

The End.
