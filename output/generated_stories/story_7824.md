# Splash's Big Laundry Plan

In the warm, steamy laundry room lived a small toy dolphin named Splash. His blue body sparkled like ocean water. His friendly smile was bright white. But today, Splash felt worried.

"Oh no!" Splash squeaked, looking around the messy room. "The laundry is everywhere!"

Dirty clothes covered the floor like colorful islands. Towels hung from every corner. The washing machine hummed loudly. The dryer tumbled and rumbled. It was chaos!

Splash's friend, a little girl named Maya, would be home from school soon. Her mom had asked her to help with the laundry. But Maya always forgot to make a plan. She would get overwhelmed and give up.

"This isn't fair to Maya," Splash said firmly. "She needs help! But how can a little dolphin like me help with such a big job?"

Splash closed his eyes and thought hard. His plastic fins wiggled as he concentrated. Then his eyes popped open with excitement.

"I know! I need to make a plan!" Splash declared. "Plans make big jobs feel smaller!"

First, Splash looked at everything that needed to be done. He counted the piles of clothes. He noticed which ones were light colors and which were dark. He saw that some clothes were already clean in the dryer.

"Step one," Splash said to himself, "sort the dirty clothes by color!"

Using his imagination, Splash pretended he could swim through the air. He swooshed around the room, pointing his nose at different piles.

"Light colors go here! Dark colors go there! Maya will know exactly what to do!"

Next, Splash noticed the clean clothes in the dryer were getting wrinkled.

"Step two," he chirped, "fold the clean clothes first! That way they won't get messy again!"

Splash imagined diving and flipping through the air above each piece of clothing. He practiced the motions Maya would need - smooth, gentle folds that would make everything neat and tidy.

"Step three," Splash continued, "wash one load at a time! Not everything at once!"

The front door opened with a jingle. Maya's footsteps echoed down the hall.

"Oh no, the laundry!" Maya groaned as she entered the room. She saw the big mess and her shoulders drooped. "This is too much work!"

But then Maya noticed something special. The clothes seemed to be sorted into neat groups. Light colors here, dark colors there. It looked like someone had started organizing!

"That's strange," Maya said, picking up Splash. "It's like someone made a plan for me!"

Maya looked at Splash's smiling face. "Did you help me think, Splash? This doesn't look so scary when it's organized!"

Following the plan she saw, Maya started with the clean clothes in the dryer. She folded them carefully, one by one. Then she put one load of dark clothes in the washer. The job felt much easier!

"Wow!" Maya laughed as she worked. "Having a plan makes everything better! I know exactly what to do next!"

As Maya folded the last towel, she hugged Splash tight. "Thank you for helping me see that big jobs need good plans. Now I know - first I sort, then I fold what's clean, then I wash one load at a time!"

Splash felt proud and happy. He had used his imagination and planning skills to help his friend. The laundry room was now clean and organized. Maya had learned that planning makes hard work much easier.

"Every big job is just lots of little jobs," Maya said, giving Splash another squeeze. "And little jobs are easy when you plan them out!"

The End.
