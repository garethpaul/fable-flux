# Koala Kori and the Gentle Garden Shed

Kori the koala liked quiet mornings. The sun felt warm on Kori’s fuzzy ears, and the air smelled like lemon and fresh leaves. Today, Kori padded softly to the garden shed. The shed was small and cozy, with a green door and a window that sparkled. Inside, tools hung like shiny fish, and pots stacked like tiny drums.

Kori breathed in. “Mmm,” Kori said. “It smells like soil and sunshine.” Kori’s voice was soft and calm.

Inside the shed, friends were busy. Pip the mouse tugged on a big watering can. Buzz the bee hummed around the flower seeds. Tilly the turtle pushed a small rake with slow, steady steps.

“We are planting today!” Pip squeaked.

“That feels good,” Kori said. “I love planting.”

Suddenly, the watering can clanked on the floor. “Clang!” Pip hopped back. Buzz buzzed louder. Tilly blinked and stopped moving.

“I wanted the red pot!” Pip cried.

“No, I need the red pot,” Buzz buzzed. “It is perfect for tiny petals.”

Tilly pointed a careful claw. “We can share,” she said, but her voice was very small.

Pip stomped. “I had it first!” Pip’s whiskers trembled.

Buzz zipped in quick loops. “I saw it first!”

Kori felt the air get tight, like a knot. The shed that had felt cozy now felt crowded. Kori put a paw on the wooden bench and took a slow, deep breath. In… and out. Kori listened to the sound of the breath, like soft wind in the trees.

Kori spoke gently. “Friends, let’s pause.”

Pip and Buzz didn’t pause. They both reached for the red pot. It tipped. “Plop!” A little bit of soil spilled on the floor. Tilly jumped with a small, “Oh!”

Kori touched the floor with a soft paw to feel steady. “I hear big feelings,” Kori said. “Let’s use peaceful words. One at a time.”

Pip looked down at the soil. “I… I am mad,” Pip said. “I want the red pot.”

Buzz hovered and rubbed little bee legs together. “I feel left out,” Buzz said. “I wanted it too.”

Tilly nodded slowly. “I feel worried,” she said. “Spills make my tummy wobbly.”

Kori smiled kindly. “Thank you for telling your feelings. That is brave.”

Kori reached for a tiny brush and swept the soil into a neat pile. Swish, swish. The soft sound made the shed feel calm again. The sun made a warm square of light on the floor. Dust sparkles danced.

“Let’s try listening ears,” Kori said. “Pip talks. We listen. Then Buzz talks. We listen. Then we find a plan.”

Pip held the red pot with careful paws. “I want to plant my strawberry seed in this pot,” Pip said. “It is just the right size. I was excited.”

Buzz buzzed softly. “I want to plant tiny blue flowers,” Buzz said. “The red pot would look pretty with blue petals.”

Kori nodded. “I hear excitement. I hear ideas. Both are special.”

Tilly lifted a green pot. “This one is nice too,” she said. “It is round and gentle.”

Kori tapped a paw on the bench. Tap, tap. “What if we take turns?” Kori asked. “We can make a plan together.”

Pip’s ears perked up. “A plan?”

Buzz tilted to the side. “What plan?”

Kori counted on paws. “Plan A: We share the red pot. One friend plants today. One friend plants tomorrow. Plan B: We find two pots that feel special. We decorate them to make them just right.”

Pip’s nose twitched. “Decorate? With what?”

Kori pointed to the shelf. “We have stickers, twine, and paints.” Kori sniffed the air. “I smell lemons and paint. It smells like ideas.”

Buzz buzzed a happy buzz. “Paint! I like swirls.”

Tilly smiled, slow and warm. “I like dots.”

Pip looked at the red pot, then at the green pot, then at the paints. Pip’s eyes shone. “Let’s try Plan B,” Pip said. “We can make two special pots!”

Everyone clapped in their own way. Pip patted paws. Buzz buzzed bright. Tilly tapped her shell. Kori’s eyes crinkled in a smile.

They set to work. Kori laid out paper. Pip chose a sunny yellow paint. Buzz chose sky blue. Tilly placed the pots on the paper, steady and sure. Kori wrapped twine around one pot and tied a soft bow. Pip painted strawberries—dot, dot, heart. Buzz painted swirls—loop, loop, loop. Tilly added gentle green dots like tiny leaves.

Soon the shed smelled like fresh earth and a lemony paint tickle. The light moved across the floor like a slow dance.

Pip hugged the yellow strawberry pot. “It feels like me,” Pip said.

Buzz hugged the blue swirl pot. “This one feels like me,” Buzz buzzed.

Kori clapped paws softly. “Now we can plant.”

They filled the pots with soil. Plunk, plunk. The soil felt crumbly and cool. Pip placed a strawberry seed inside, like tucking in a baby. Buzz sprinkled tiny blue flower seeds, like little stars.

Tilly watered both with the now-steady watering can. “Sip, sip,” the water went into the soil.

Kori looked around the cozy shed. “We used peaceful words,” Kori said. “We listened. We made a plan. We worked as a team.”

Pip nodded. “Talking helped my mad go small.”

Buzz agreed. “Listening made me feel seen.”

Tilly smiled. “Sharing made the worry go away.”

Kori breathed in, then out. “When feelings get big, we can pause, use gentle voices, and listen. That is peaceful-communication.”

They set the pots by the sunny window. The seeds were tucked in safe and snug. The shed felt warm and calm again. Outside, a bird sang a tiny song. Inside, friends smiled.

Kori’s heart felt light. “Different ideas can grow side by side,” Kori said. “Just like our plants.”

Everyone nodded. The garden shed was full of kindness, color, and quiet joy.

The End.
