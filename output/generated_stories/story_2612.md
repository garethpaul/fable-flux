# Ellie’s Busy Basement Craft Day

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and bright curious eyes. One rainy afternoon, Ellie and her friends went down to the busy basement. The basement was full of sounds and colors. Drip-drop went the rain on the small window. Click-clack went the craft scissors. Crinkle-crinkle went the paper. The air smelled like glue and fresh crayons.

Ellie looked around and smiled. “Let’s make a big rainbow poster!” she said in her warm, rumbly voice.

Her friends cheered. Mia the mouse squeaked, “I can cut tiny shapes!” Benji the bunny said, “I can glue!” Tiko the turtle slowly nodded, “I can bring the colored paper.”

On the big table, there were markers, yarn, buttons, and shiny stickers. There were safe, child-size scissors and soft brushes. Ellie’s trunk wiggled with joy. “We can do it together,” she said. “We will craft. We will listen. We will talk kindly.”

Mia rushed to the paper pile. Snip-snip went her little scissors. “I will cut all the red,” she squeaked.

Benji hopped to the glue bottle. Squeeze-squish went the glue. “I will glue everything!” he said.

Ellie stood still and took a deep breath. She listened. She heard the drip-drop rain and the crinkle-crinkle paper. She watched Tiko bring slow stacks of blue, green, and yellow. “Thank you, Tiko,” Ellie said. “You are careful. That helps.”

Ellie spread a big white poster on the table. “Let’s plan,” she said gently. “We will make a rainbow line by line. First red, then orange, then yellow, then green, then blue, then purple.”

Mia stopped snipping. “Plan?” she asked.

“Yes,” Ellie said, smiling. “We talk. We listen. We take turns. That is how our craft will shine.”

Mia’s ears perked up. “Okay,” she squeaked. “I will cut red and share.”

Benji wiggled his nose. “I will glue a little at a time,” he said. “I will wait for the pieces.”

They all nodded. The busy basement felt calm and happy.

Ellie used her trunk to point. “Red on top,” she said. Mia handed over soft red strips. Rip-rip went the paper as they made gentle curves. Benji dabbed tiny dots of glue. Tap-tap went his paw. “I am using small glue drops,” he said proudly. “Small dots are neat.”

“Good listening,” Ellie said. “Good talking, too.”

Next came orange. Tiko slowly pushed the orange pile. “Here you go,” he said. “I took my time so it is not messy.”

“Thank you,” Ellie said. “Slow can be good.” They laid the orange strip. It smelled a little like oranges from the crayon.

When they reached yellow, Mia squeaked, “I want to glue now!” Benji’s ears drooped. “But gluing is my job,” he whispered.

Ellie saw their faces. She paused. She listened to the quiet. She heard Benji’s small sigh. She heard Mia’s excited squeak. Ellie spoke softly. “We can share. We can trade. We can take turns. Mia, you can glue this yellow row. Benji, you can cut the next green pieces. Is that okay?”

Benji’s nose twitched. “Okay,” he said. Mia nodded fast. “Okay!”

Mia squeezed the glue bottle gently. Squeeze… pause… squeeze. “Small drops,” she said. Benji snipped the green paper. Snip-snip-snip. “I am trying my best,” he said.

Ellie smiled. “You are doing great. Thank you for listening and talking kindly.” The basement felt warm and bright, even with the rain.

They glued on green, then blue that shimmered like a calm pond. When they reached purple, Tiko said, “I would like to stick on buttons for stars.”

Ellie looked at the plan. “Our rainbow can have stars in the sky,” she said. “That is a lovely idea, Tiko. Thank you for saying it.”

Tiko placed purple buttons one by one. Click. Click. Click. The poster sparkled.

Just then, the glue bottle tipped. Oh no! A little drip rolled toward the rainbow. Benji gasped. Mia squeaked. Tiko blinked.

Ellie took a slow breath. “It’s okay,” she said. “Accidents happen. Let’s fix it together.”

“Paper towel!” Mia squeaked, rushing to bring a soft white towel. Benji held the bottle upright. Tiko slid the poster a tiny bit away from the drip. Ellie dabbed the spot with her trunk and the towel. Pat-pat-pat. The drip was gone.

“We did it,” Ellie said. “We helped each other. We spoke. We listened. We stayed calm.”

They stepped back. The rainbow shined on the big white poster: red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple stars. Shiny stickers twinkled like tiny lights. The basement sounded happy—soft hums, little giggles, and the friendly crinkle of leftover paper.

Ellie’s heart felt full. “Friends,” she said, “our craft is beautiful because we made it together. We planned. We took turns. We used kind words. We listened.”

Mia squeaked, “I learned small glue dots!”

Benji said, “I learned to share jobs.”

Tiko nodded. “I learned that slow is okay.”

Ellie wrapped her trunk around them in a gentle group hug. “We learned to craft with care. We learned to talk and listen. That makes our work strong.”

They hung the rainbow on the basement wall. Every time they looked at it, they remembered their team and their kind voices.

The End.
