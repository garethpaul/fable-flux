# Bubbles the Dolphin and the Leafy Treehouse

Bubbles was a playful dolphin who loved to splash, flip, and giggle. One sunny morning, Bubbles popped up from the blue, sparkly sea and saw a little treehouse on the hill. The treehouse smelled like sweet leaves and fresh wood. Birds sang, “Tweet-tweet!” and the breeze whispered, “Whoooosh.” Bubbles wiggled her tail and said, “I want to visit!”

“How can I get there?” Bubbles wondered. Just then, her friend Timo the turtle paddled by. Timo had a shiny green shell. “Hello, Bubbles,” he said. “Use the bubble-splash path!”

“What’s that?” Bubbles asked.

“Follow the big bubbles that float to the treehouse!” Timo smiled.

Bubbles took a deep breath. “One, two, three—whoosh!” She made a string of big, bouncy bubbles that stacked like soft steps. She hopped from bubble to bubble, feeling the warm sun on her back. “Boing! Boing! Boing!” she laughed as she reached the treehouse deck.

“Welcome!” said Mira the monkey, hanging by her tail. Mira had brown fur and bright, curious eyes. Next to her stood Piko the parrot with red, blue, and green feathers. From a window peeked Nia the rabbit with long ears, and Jo the little human child waved shyly. “Hi, Bubbles! Come in!”

Inside, the treehouse felt cool and cozy. The floor was smooth wood. A pot of mint leaves released a fresh, tingly smell. “We keep our treehouse clean and kind,” Jo said. “We take care of our tree. We share. We save.”

Bubbles tilted her head. “Save what?”

“Water, light, and things we can use again,” Nia said. She held up a jar full of pretty buttons. “We make art from old things.”

Piko pointed to a big sign that said, “We love our Earth.” He squawked, “We recycle!” Then he laughed, “Re-cy-cle!”

Mira clapped. “Today, we build a leaf fan to keep us cool. We won’t use the big noisy fan unless we must.”

Bubbles grinned. “Let’s do it!”

They set out bowls of smooth sticks, fallen leaves, and ribbon scraps. The table felt a little sticky with glue. “Ew, sticky!” Bubbles giggled. Together they tied leaves to sticks. Swish-swish went the leaf fan, and the room smelled more leafy and sweet.

Suddenly, they heard a clatter. “Clink! Clank!” Nia’s ears twitched. “What was that?”

Bubbles peered outside. “Oh no! There is a pile of plastic cups and old bottles by the tree roots.” The tree’s leaves drooped a little.

Mira frowned. “Litter makes the tree sad.”

“Maybe someone made a mistake,” Jo said softly. “We can help.”

Bubbles felt a squeeze in her chest. “I know this feeling. In the sea, trash makes it hard to swim. It scares fish.” Her voice was small. “It scares me, too.”

Piko fluffed his feathers. “Let’s talk to whoever left it. Kind words first.”

Down on the grass, they saw two raccoons, Rika and Rico, with striped tails and curious paws. Rika held a bottle. Rico held a cup. “Hi,” Jo said, waving. “I’m Jo. This is Bubbles and our friends.”

Rika blinked. “We were having a snack. We didn’t know where to put these.”

Rico shuffled his feet. “We feel sorry.”

Bubbles took a gentle breath. “Thank you for telling us. The tree needs clean roots to drink water. Can we show you what to do?”

Rika and Rico nodded. “Yes, please.”

Back in the treehouse, Nia showed three bins. “Blue for recycle. Green for compost. Gray for trash.” She tapped each bin. “Listen: tin-ting for cans in blue, soft thump for apple cores in green, and clunk in gray.”

Bubbles smiled. “Plastic bottles go in blue.” She dropped one in. “Tin-ting!”

Rika tried with a cup. “Tin-ting!” Rico dropped a banana peel in green. “Thump!”

Mira cheered. “You are learning!”

They all worked together. The sun warmed their backs. The grass tickled their toes and paws. The air smelled clean again. The tree’s leaves lifted and rustled, “Shhh-shhh,” like a happy song.

When they finished, the pile was gone. Jo held up a small poster they drew together: pictures of bins, a smiling tree, and the words, “We take care. We share. We save.”

Bubbles looked at everyone—feathers, fur, shells, soft skin, and her smooth blue-gray back. “We are all different,” she said. “But we all love this tree.”

Piko bobbed. “And the sea, too!”

Rika raised her paw. “Can we join your clean-up club?”

“Of course!” said Nia. “Everyone is welcome.”

“Let’s make a plan,” Timo said, popping his head up from a puddle. “We can have a quiet hour each day. We turn off lights, open the window, and use the leaf fan.”

Mira added, “We bring our own cups. No throw-away cups.”

Jo said, “We fix broken things if we can.”

Bubbles clapped her fins. “And we teach others, with kind words.”

They all signed the poster with tiny paw prints, feather marks, leaf rubbings, and one bubbly splash from Bubbles. The treehouse glowed with soft light. The breeze felt cool on their faces. Everyone smiled.

Rika looked at Bubbles. “Thank you for helping, even when we made a mistake.”

Bubbles nodded. “We all learn. When we care for Earth, Earth cares for us.”

They shared crunchy apple slices and cool water in real cups. The mint plant tickled their noses with a fresh smell. Laughter bubbled like little streams, “Hee-hee! Ha-ha!”

Bubbles leaned on the window. The sea shimmered. The tree swayed. Her heart felt calm and full.

Today’s lesson was clear: use less, reuse, and be kind. Ask, listen, and help.

“Daily adventure,” Bubbles giggled, “and daily care.”

Everyone cheered, “We love our Earth!”

The End.
