# Pebble the Penguin’s Tidy Treehouse

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a wiggly walk. Pebble lived in a cozy treehouse with round windows, soft rugs, and a tiny bell that went ting-ting when the door opened. The treehouse smelled like fresh pine and warm toast. Sunlight made dancing dots on the wooden floor.

“Good morning!” Pebble chirped. “Today I will paint a big, happy picture!”

Pebble waddled to the art corner. But—oh no! Paints were under pillows. Brushes were in a boot. Paper was crinkled behind a chair. Pebble’s tummy felt wobbly. The room looked messy. Pebble blinked fast.

“I can’t find anything,” Pebble whispered. “I feel busy in my head.”

Pebble took a slow belly-breath. In… and out. The pine smell felt calm. Pebble patted a flipper on the heart. “It’s okay. I can fix this.” Pebble liked to smile and solve problems.

First, Pebble tried to paint anyway. “Red paint? Red paint?” Pebble peeked under a blanket. “Not here.” Pebble looked behind a drum. “Not here.” Puddles of paint made sticky spots. A brush rolled, tap-tap-tap, across the floor. Pebble slipped—whoops!—and landed on a soft rug.

Pebble sat up and listened. The tiny bell went ting-ting in the breeze. Birds chirped outside. Pebble took another belly-breath. “When things are messy, my heart feels busy. When things are tidy, my heart feels calm,” Pebble said softly. “I need a plan.”

Pebble made a plan with simple steps.

Step one: Sort.
Step two: Put things in a home.
Step three: Keep it calm and kind.
Step four: Check and smile.

Pebble clapped. “Let’s do it!”

Pebble scooped all the brushes into one pile. The brushes felt smooth and tickly. “Brushes together,” Pebble sang. Next, Pebble gathered paints. The paint lids clicked, click, click. “Paints together.” Pebble smoothed paper, pat-pat, and stacked it. “Paper together.” Pebble even found the missing red paint under the pillow. “Hello, Red!”

Pebble looked around. “Now, homes.” Pebble found three boxes. One box smelled like oranges. “Brush Home,” Pebble said, drawing a little brush picture on the box. The next box was sunny yellow. “Paint Home,” Pebble said, drawing a round paint dot. The last box felt crinkly. “Paper Home,” Pebble said, drawing a paper square. Pebble slid each pile into its home. Swish. Click. Pat.

“Ahh,” Pebble sighed. The room felt bigger. The floor felt safe. The air felt quiet. Pebble’s shoulders softened. “My treehouse is calm.”

Just then, knock knock.

“Pebble! Can we play?” called friends from the ladder. It was Tilly the tiny turtle and Buzz the bumblebee.

“Come in!” Pebble rang the bell. Ting-ting!

Tilly looked shy. “I brought stickers.”

Buzz buzzed, “I brought sparkles!”

Pebble smiled. “We can paint and stick and sparkle. But first, let’s keep our plan.”

“What plan?” asked Tilly, blinking.

“Our calm plan,” Pebble said. “We sort. We give things a home. We play. Then we put them back.”

Tilly nodded slowly. “Okay.”

Buzz zoomed. “Okay!”

They started to paint. Swoosh went the brush in cool blue. Plop went the red paint. The paper felt smooth under Pebble’s flipper. Tilly’s sticker sheets made soft crinkle sounds. Buzz sprinkled sparkles—shhh—like tiny stars.

When a brush finished, Pebble hummed, “Brushes go home.” Pebble put it in the Brush Home box. Tilly copied and smiled. “Stickers go home.” Buzz zipped and said, “Sparkles stay closed when we don’t use them!” The lids made a happy click.

The treehouse stayed tidy. Pebble’s heart felt calm. Everyone could find what they needed.

After a while, the sun made long stripes on the floor. Pebble looked at the picture. It was a big, happy tree with a cozy house and three tiny friends. Pebble giggled. “It looks like our day!”

“Time to clean,” Pebble said kindly.

Tilly put stickers in the sticker book. “Stickers home.”

Buzz brushed sparkles into a jar. “Sparkles home.”

Pebble stacked papers. “Paper home.” Pebble wiped a small paint spot. “Goodbye, paint dot.”

They checked the room together.

“Brushes?” Pebble asked.

“In Brush Home,” said Tilly.

“Paints?” Pebble asked.

“In Paint Home,” buzzed Buzz.

“Paper?” Pebble asked.

“In Paper Home,” said Pebble, patting the stack.

Pebble smiled big. The treehouse smelled fresh and felt calm again. The tiny bell went ting-ting, like a little cheer.

Tilly hugged Pebble. “I like your plan. My head feels quiet.”

Buzz did a happy loop. “I can find everything fast!”

Pebble’s eyes sparkled. “When we give things a home, our hearts feel calm. When we clean a little as we play, we can enjoy more. Being organized helps us be ready for fun.”

Pebble hung the painting on the wall. “Now our art has a home too.”

The friends shared crunchy apple slices. Crunch, munch. The day felt cozy and bright.

Pebble whispered, “Sort, home, calm, check.” Pebble tapped the boxes, one, two, three. “Organization helps me feel safe and happy.”

Tilly and Buzz said together, “Me too!”

They waved goodbye at the window. Pebble rang the bell one more time. Ting-ting!

Pebble looked around the tidy treehouse and took a peaceful breath. “I am prepared for tomorrow,” Pebble said. “And tomorrow, I will build a puzzle. I know right where it is.”

Pebble turned off the lamp. Click. The room rested, neat and quiet. Pebble’s heart felt as soft as a feather.

The End.
