# Leo’s Cozy Bedroom Cleanup

One sunny morning, in a cozy bedroom with soft blankets and a warm, sunny window, a brave little lion cub named Leo woke up with a big stretch. His whiskers tickled his nose. He sniffed. “Hmm,” Leo said. “My room smells like crayons and paper. Today is a good day to write!”

Leo loved to write. He had a small table with pencils, crayons, and a blue notebook. His notebook had stars on the cover. He opened it and whispered, “Dear Notebook, today I will write a story.” He pressed a crayon to the page. Scritch, scritch, scritch went the crayon. The sound made him smile.

Mama Lion peeked in. “Good morning, brave cub,” she said, giving Leo a gentle nuzzle. “I see you are writing.”

“Yes, Mama! I am writing a story about a hero who helps the Earth,” Leo said proudly.

“That sounds wonderful,” Mama said. “When you finish, please follow directions and clean up your paper scraps. Recycling helps our home and our town.”

Leo nodded. “I can do that,” he said.

Leo wrote and drew. He used green for trees and blue for the sky. He cut little paper stars, snip snip, and they fell like tiny snowflakes onto the carpet. He hummed a happy song. His cozy bedroom felt busy and bright.

Just then, his friend Pip the parrot fluttered in through the open window. “Squawk! Writing time?” Pip chirped.

“Yes!” Leo said. “I’m making a story.”

Pip found a shiny candy wrapper under the bed. “Ooh! Shiny!” Pip tossed it in the air. The wrapper landed with the paper scraps.

Leo blinked. “Pip, that goes in the trash, not with the paper,” he said.

Pip tilted his head. “Why?”

“Because we recycle paper,” Leo explained. “Paper goes in the blue bin. Trash goes in the small bin. We follow directions to help the Earth.”

Pip nodded slowly. “Follow directions. Blue bin for paper. Got it!”

Leo kept writing. He wrote, “The hero sees a messy world. The hero helps.” He smiled at his words. Then he stood up and looked around. “Oh,” he said softly. The floor was covered with white paper stars, string bits, and the shiny wrapper. His cozy bedroom didn’t look so cozy now.

Mama Lion came back with a gentle smile. “Time to tidy, brave cub.”

“I will do it,” Leo said. He picked up the paper stars and put them in the blue bin. “Paper goes here,” he said. He found the shiny wrapper and put it in the small bin. “Trash goes here.” He found a plastic bottle from yesterday’s water. He tapped it. “Plas-tic,” he said slowly. “Plastic goes in the yellow bin.”

Pip flapped his wings. “I want to help! Where does this cardboard tube go?” he asked, holding up a tube from a finished roll of tape.

“Cardboard is like paper,” Leo said. “Blue bin!”

They sorted together. Rustle, rustle went the paper. Clink, clink went the bottle. The room began to look neat again. Leo’s heart felt warm and light. “I like helping,” he said. “It feels good.”

Just then, Pip dropped a handful of paper stars by mistake. They scattered everywhere. Pip’s eyes grew wide. “Oh no! I made a mess,” he squeaked.

Leo took a deep breath. He remembered being brave and kind. “It’s okay, Pip,” he said softly. “I forgive you. Accidents happen. We can fix it together.”

“Thank you,” Pip whispered.

They picked up every star. Pip followed directions and put them in the blue bin. “I am learning,” Pip said. “I can help the Earth too.”

When they were done, the cozy bedroom was clean. The sunny window made bright spots on the floor. The air smelled like fresh paper and a little bit like oranges from Leo’s snack.

Mama Lion clapped her paws. “I am proud of you both,” she said. “You wrote. You followed directions. You cleaned up. You recycled. That is civic responsibility. That means helping our home and helping our town.”

Leo stood tall. “I am a helper,” he said. He opened his notebook and wrote a big ending. He read aloud, “The hero cleans, sorts, and recycles. The Earth smiles.”

Pip cheered. “Squawk! The Earth smiles!”

Leo put his crayon down and listened. The room was quiet and peaceful. He felt brave, happy, and strong. He looked at Pip. “Friend,” he said, “thank you for helping.”

“Thank you for forgiving me,” Pip replied. “I will remember: paper in blue, plastic in yellow, trash in small.”

Leo nodded. “We can teach others, too,” he said. “We can write a sign!”

Together they wrote a simple sign: “Please recycle: Paper—Blue Bin. Plastic—Yellow Bin. Trash—Small Bin. Thank you!”

They taped the sign near the bins. It looked neat and clear.

Mama Lion smiled. “What a wonderful idea.”

Leo curled up on his soft blanket. “Today was a good day,” he whispered.

“It was a helpful day,” Pip chirped.

Leo closed his eyes. He felt proud of his brave heart and his tidy, cozy bedroom. He knew he could be a hero every day by recycling, following directions, and forgiving friends.

The End.
