# Leo's Big Game Song

Leo the lion cub sat in the bright musical practice room, his golden fur shining under the warm lights. His little paws held drumsticks, but they felt heavy and clumsy. Tomorrow was the big jungle soccer game, and all the animal families would sing the special victory song together. Leo wanted to play the drums just like his papa did.

"Boom, boom, CRASH!" The cymbals fell over with a loud noise. Leo's ears drooped down.

"This is too hard!" Leo growled, throwing his drumsticks on the floor. "I can't do it right!"

His mama lioness walked into the room, her gentle paws making soft sounds on the wooden floor. "What's wrong, my little cub?"

"I keep messing up the victory song," Leo sniffled. "My paws won't do what I want them to do. The other cubs will laugh at me during the big game!"

Mama sat down next to Leo, her warm fur tickling his side. "Tell me, little one, what happens when you fall down while playing soccer?"

Leo thought for a moment. "I get back up and try again."

"That's right!" Mama smiled, showing her gentle teeth. "Learning drums is just like learning to kick the ball. It takes practice and patience."

She picked up the drumsticks and showed Leo how to hold them. "See? Gentle grip, like holding a butterfly. Your great-grandpapa taught this song to grandpapa, grandpapa taught it to papa, and papa taught it to me. Now I'm teaching it to you!"

Leo watched carefully as mama played a simple beat. "Boom-tap, boom-tap, boom-tap-tap!" The rhythm sounded like a happy heartbeat.

"Can I try again?" Leo asked, his tail starting to wiggle with excitement.

"Of course! Remember, when you feel frustrated, take three deep breaths. Like this." Mama breathed in slowly through her nose, making a soft "whoosh" sound, then breathed out. "This helps our minds feel calm and ready to learn."

Leo copied mama's breathing. The air smelled like warm wood and the sweet flowers outside the window. His shoulders relaxed, and his paws felt lighter.

"Boom-tap, boom-tap," Leo played slowly. The drums made a deep, rich sound that vibrated through his chest. It felt good!

"Wonderful!" Mama clapped. "Now, let's practice the soccer cheer part. When our team scores, we play 'boom-tap-tap-BOOM!'"

Leo giggled and tried the pattern. His first try was wobbly, but he took another deep breath and tried again. This time, it sounded much better!

"I'm doing it!" Leo roared happily. "Listen, mama! Boom-tap, boom-tap, boom-tap-tap!"

They practiced together as the afternoon sun painted golden stripes across the room. Sometimes Leo made mistakes, but he remembered to breathe deeply and try again. Each time, he got a little better.

The next day at the soccer game, Leo sat proudly with the other musicians. When their team scored the winning goal, Leo played the victory song perfectly. His family cheered, and all the animals sang along to his drumming.

"I did it!" Leo beamed, his chest puffed out with pride. "I learned that when things feel hard, I can breathe, stay calm, and keep trying!"

Papa lion hugged him tight. "You learned something very important, son. Frustration is just a feeling that visits us, but it doesn't have to stay. With practice and patience, we can do amazing things!"

The End.
