# Milo's Missing Sock Mystery

Milo the monkey loved helping his mama with the laundry. The warm, sudsy smell of soap bubbles always made him happy. Today, he bounced excitedly as Mama Monkey loaded the washing machine with colorful clothes.

"Can I help? Can I help?" Milo chattered, his tail swishing back and forth.

"Of course, sweetie," Mama smiled. "You can sort the socks into pairs."

Milo climbed onto the big basket of clean, fluffy socks. They felt so soft and warm against his furry hands! He began matching them carefully - red with red, blue with blue, striped with striped.

"One, two, three..." he counted proudly. But then he stopped. "Mama! There's only ONE polka-dot sock. Where's the other one?"

Mama looked puzzled. "That's strange. I put two polka-dot socks in the wash this morning."

Milo scratched his head, thinking hard. "Maybe the washing machine ate it!" he said, peering into the big, round door.

"Washing machines don't eat socks, silly," Mama laughed. "But let's think about this like detectives. What could have happened?"

Milo's eyes lit up. He loved solving puzzles! "Well..." he said slowly, "maybe it fell behind the machine?"

Together, they looked behind the washing machine. No sock.

"What else could have happened?" Mama asked encouragingly.

Milo thought and thought. Then he snapped his fingers. "Maybe it's still in the machine! Sometimes socks hide in the corners."

They checked every corner of the washing machine. Still no sock.

"Hmm," said Milo, tapping his chin like a real detective. "Let me think of another idea." He looked around the laundry room carefully. His sharp monkey eyes noticed something colorful peeking out from under the dryer.

"Wait!" he exclaimed, getting down on his hands and knees. "I see something!"

He reached under the dryer and pulled out... the missing polka-dot sock! It was a little dusty but perfectly fine.

"You found it!" Mama cheered. "You're such a good problem-solver, Milo!"

Milo felt proud and excited. "I used my thinking brain!" he said, pointing to his head. "First I guessed, then I looked in different places, and then I really looked carefully with my eyes!"

"That's exactly right," Mama said, giving him a big hug. "You didn't give up when your first idea didn't work. You kept thinking of new ideas to try."

As they finished folding the laundry together, Milo told Mama a story about the sock's adventure. "Maybe the sock wanted to explore," he said with a giggle. "It rolled under the dryer to see what was there, but then it got scared and wanted to come home!"

"What a creative story!" Mama said. "You're not only good at solving problems, but you're also a wonderful storyteller."

Milo beamed with happiness. He had learned that when something puzzling happens, he could use his brain to think of different solutions. And even if the first idea doesn't work, he could try another one, and another one, until he finds the answer.

"Next time something goes missing," Milo announced proudly, "I'll be ready! I'll think, and look, and think some more until I solve the mystery!"

Mama smiled and ruffled his fuzzy head. "That's my smart, brave little monkey!"

The End.
