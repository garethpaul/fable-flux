# Poppy the Penguin Practices

Poppy was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a wiggly walk. One morning, Poppy waddled into the bathroom. The tiles felt cool and smooth under her little penguin feet. Drip, drip, drip went the shiny faucet. The mirror was foggy from warm steam, and the room smelled like minty soap.

“Today, I will practice,” Poppy said with a happy flap. “I will practice brushing, washing, and listening.”

Poppy picked up her tiny toothbrush. It was blue with a little star. “Hello, Brush,” Poppy giggled. “Let’s practice together!”

She squeezed out a small pea of toothpaste. “Pea-sized,” she said, nodding. The toothpaste smelled like sweet mint. Poppy looked in the mirror and took a deep breath.

“Slow and gentle,” Poppy reminded herself. “Top teeth, bottom teeth, all around.”

Swish, swish, swish went the brush. “One, two, three,” counted Poppy softly. “Four, five, six.” The foamy bubbles tickled her beak. Poppy started to giggle.

From the soap dish came a tiny voice. “Hello, Poppy,” it sang. It was the round, friendly soap. In Poppy’s big imagination, the bathroom came alive!

“Hello, Soap,” said Poppy. “I am practicing.”

“Practicing is kind,” said Soap. “It is kind to your body and kind to your day.”

The faucet hummed, “Drip, drop, don’t stop. Practice makes you proud.”

Poppy listened carefully. Listening made her feel calm and strong. “I will finish my count,” she said. “Seven, eight, nine, ten!”

She rinsed her mouth. Swish, swish, swish, and spit! The water was cool, and the sound went plip-plop in the sink.

Next, Poppy turned to the sink to wash her little wings. She turned the water to warm. “Not too hot,” she whispered, listening for the soft hum of the water. She wet her wings and rubbed the soap. Bubbles grew like tiny clouds. They smelled like oranges!

“Hello, Bubbles,” said Poppy. “Will you help me practice?”

“We love to help,” the Bubbles chimed. “Rub, rub, rub. Front and back. Between the little flippers!”

Poppy rubbed and rubbed, slow and careful. “Practice means doing it again and again,” she said. “I can do it!”

Just then, Poppy heard a gentle knock. Tap, tap.

“Poppy,” called Mama Penguin from the door. “Are you doing okay?”

“Yes, Mama,” Poppy answered. She listened to Mama’s warm voice. “I am practicing brushing and washing.”

Mama opened the door a little. She smiled. “I am proud you are practicing,” she said. “Do you need help?”

Poppy looked at her sudsy wings. “I want to try by myself first,” she said. “Then I will ask if I need help.”

“That is good listening,” Mama said. “I am right here.”

Poppy rinsed off the bubbles. The water felt like a soft rain on a spring day. She shook her wings. Drip, drop, went the drops. She patted herself dry with a fluffy towel. It felt warm and cozy.

Poppy saw the bathroom rug. It was shaped like a big, friendly fish. In her imagination, the rug winked. “Hello, Captain Poppy!” it said. “Ready for a practice voyage?”

“Yes!” said Poppy. She hopped on the rug boat. “I am sailing to Tooth Island and Soap Sea.” She used her toothbrush as a little flag and her towel as a sail. The mirror was the bright sky. Poppy steered with a giggle. “We will be kind sailors. We will listen to the wind.”

The faucet wind whispered, “Slow and steady. You are learning already.”

Poppy stood tall. “I can practice. I can be kind. I can listen.”

She spotted a tiny toy cup on the sink. It looked lonely. Poppy picked it up and smiled. “Hello, Cup. Would you like to sail too?”

“Yes, please,” the Cup squeaked in Poppy’s imagination. “Thank you for asking.”

Poppy placed Cup gently on the rug boat. “We share,” she said. “Sharing is kind.”

When the voyage was done, Poppy set the cup back in its spot. She put the toothbrush in its holder and folded the towel neatly. She listened for Mama.

“All finished?” Mama asked.

“All finished,” Poppy said. “I practiced brushing. I practiced washing. I practiced listening. And I was kind.”

“How do you feel?” Mama asked.

Poppy tapped her beak and thought. She felt clean and warm. She felt proud and peaceful. “I feel brave,” she said. “Practice helps me grow.”

Mama gave Poppy a gentle hug. “Yes,” she said. “Practice makes things easier. Practice shows kindness to yourself. And listening helps you learn.”

Poppy looked at the mirror. She saw a cheerful penguin smiling back. “Tomorrow,” she said, “I will practice again.”

The bathroom was quiet and happy. The faucet gave one last drip. The soap sat round and ready. The bubbles rested, like tiny clouds.

Poppy waved. “Thank you, bathroom friends. Thank you for helping me practice.”

“You did it,” the room seemed to whisper. “Slow and steady. Kind and ready.”

Poppy waddled out with a little song. “Practice, practice, every day. Listen, share, and find your way.”

The End.
