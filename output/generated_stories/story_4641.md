# Sunny's Rooftop Rescue

On a warm, breezy morning, a resourceful squirrel named Sunny scampered across a rooftop. The roof was flat and wide, with tiny pebbles that tickled her paws. The sun felt like a gentle hug. A soft whoosh of wind ruffled Sunny’s fluffy tail.

“I love the rooftop,” Sunny chirped. “So many things to learn up here.”

Sunny liked to practice new skills. She balanced on the roof ledge. She listened to the wind. She sorted small shiny leaves by size. She felt proud when she learned something new.

One day, Sunny heard a squeaky cry. “Eee! Help!”

Sunny turned her ears. She followed the sound to a big puddle by the gutter. A little paper boat floated in circles. On the boat sat a tiny beetle with shiny green wings.

“I’m Bree,” squeaked the beetle. “The wind pushed my boat! I can’t get back!”

Sunny’s heart pinged with care. “I’m Sunny. I can help,” she said. “I like to build and try.”

Sunny looked around. Her nose twitched. She saw twigs, string from a kite tail, and a small plastic cup near a potted plant. She wanted to rush, but she took a slow breath. She remembered her skill-building steps.

“Step one,” Sunny said, tapping her paw. “Stop and look.”

She looked at the puddle. It was too wide to jump. The boat was near the middle.

“Step two,” Sunny said. “Think and choose.”

She had ideas. She could poke the boat with a long stick. She could sip the puddle with the cup. Or she could build something new.

Sunny smiled. “I will build a gentle scoop.”

“Will it work?” asked Bree, shivering on the boat.

“I don’t know yet,” Sunny said kindly, “but I will try. If it doesn’t work, we can try something else. That’s how we learn.”

Sunny gathered the twigs and the kite string. She tied the twigs in a long, flat shape. Her little paws tucked and pulled, tug tug, snug snug. She tied the plastic cup to the end. It made a soft click.

“Step three,” Sunny said. “Do it safely.”

She lay on her belly and stretched the scoop to the puddle. The cup touched the water with a gentle plip. Sunny’s tail swished for balance. She breathed slowly. In, out. In, out.

“Almost there,” she whispered.

Sunny tried to lift the boat with the cup. The boat slipped away. Bree gasped. Sunny’s heart thumped, but she kept calm.

“It’s okay,” Sunny said. “New tries make new skills.”

Sunny adjusted her scoop. She aimed at the side of the boat, not the bottom. She moved slowly. Plip, plip. This time the cup nudged the boat toward the edge.

“Yay!” Bree cheered. “You did it!”

“Almost!” Sunny grinned. “A little more.”

With one last careful push, the boat bumped the puddle’s edge. Bree hopped onto the dry roof. He shook his tiny wings. They made a tiny whirr like a toy fan.

“Thank you,” Bree buzzed. “You are very clever.”

Sunny blushed under her fur. “We did it together,” she said. “You stayed calm and cheered. That helped.”

Bree looked at the little boat. “I was scared,” he said softly. “The wind was not nice.”

Sunny nodded. “The wind wasn’t trying to be mean. It just moved fast. Wind is wind. We can be patient with things we don’t control.”

Bree tilted his head. “Patient and kind,” he said. “I can learn that.”

Sunny smiled. “And I can teach you skills! Do you want to practice with me?”

“Yes!” Bree buzzed.

Sunny and Bree made a tiny practice pond in a shallow pan by the potted plant. Sunny said, “We’ll learn three skills: look, think, and try.”

They took turns guiding the boat with the scoop, moving it gently. Sometimes the boat bumped the side. Sometimes it went too fast. Each time, they paused, breathed, and tried again.

Bree laughed when he got it just right. “I did it!”

Sunny clapped her paws. “Great choice-making! You looked. You thought. You tried.”

Soon, a shy pigeon waddled closer. Her feathers were gray like soft rain clouds. “Coo,” she said. “Can I watch?”

“Of course,” said Sunny. “Everyone can learn at their own pace.”

The pigeon blinked. “I’m slow,” she cooed.

“That’s okay,” Sunny said gently. “We are patient here. We can take turns.”

Sunny showed the pigeon how to hold the string with her beak. Bree cheered. They practiced. They waited. They tried again. The rooftop buzzed with happy sounds: plip, whirr, coo, and Sunny’s bright giggles.

As the sun set and made the puddle glow gold, Sunny gathered her friends. “Today we learned a big lesson,” she said. “When we have a problem, we can build a skill. We stop and look. We think and choose. We try, and we try again. We are kind to each other and to ourselves.”

Bree buzzed, “And we are patient with the wind and with friends.”

The pigeon cooed, “And we make room for everyone.”

Sunny’s tail curled with joy. Her heart felt full and warm, like the sunset. She looked at her new friends and the rooftop that felt like a school and a playground.

“We did it,” she said. “Together.”

The End.
