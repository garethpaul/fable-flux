# Benny's Big Bathroom Feelings

Benny the beaver stood in the bright bathroom. The white tiles felt cool under his furry paws. The mirror showed his worried brown eyes.

"I don't want to brush my teeth!" Benny said loudly. His voice echoed off the bathroom walls.

Mama Beaver was getting his toothbrush ready. The minty toothpaste smelled fresh and clean. But Benny felt big, angry feelings growing in his chest.

"It's time to brush, sweetie," Mama said gently. Her voice was calm and kind.

"NO!" Benny shouted. He stamped his foot hard. The sound went THUMP on the bathroom floor. His heart was beating fast like a drum.

Benny felt his feelings getting bigger and bigger. His face felt hot. His hands made tight fists.

"I can see you're having big feelings," Mama said. She sat down on the bathroom rug. "Let's take some deep breaths together."

"I don't want to!" Benny said. But his voice was quieter now.

Mama showed him how to breathe slowly. "In through your nose," she said softly. "Out through your mouth. Like blowing bubbles."

Benny tried it. The air felt cool going in. Warm going out. His fast heart started to slow down.

"How are you feeling now?" Mama asked.

Benny touched his chest. "Still a little mad," he said honestly. "But not as big mad."

"That's good self-control," Mama smiled. "You used your breathing to help your feelings get smaller."

Benny looked at his toothbrush. It had his favorite blue color on the handle. "Why do I have to brush my teeth every day?" he asked.

"That's a great question," Mama said. "Brushing keeps your teeth healthy and strong. Just like how you eat good food to keep your body strong."

Benny thought about this. He liked having strong teeth for eating his favorite wood chips.

"Can I choose which tooth to brush first?" Benny asked.

"What a wonderful idea!" Mama said. "You can make that choice."

Benny felt proud. Making his own choice felt good. "I choose my front teeth first," he decided.

He picked up his toothbrush. The bristles were soft against his paw. "Will you count while I brush?" he asked Mama.

"Of course," Mama said. "One, two, three..."

Benny brushed his front teeth carefully. The toothpaste tasted like strawberries. It made his mouth feel tingly and clean.

"Now I choose my back teeth," Benny said with a smile.

When he finished brushing, Benny looked in the mirror. His teeth sparkled white and clean. He felt proud of himself.

"You did such a good job managing your big feelings," Mama said. "You took deep breaths, asked questions, and made good choices."

Benny hugged Mama tight. She smelled like warm vanilla. "I felt really mad at first," he said.

"All feelings are okay," Mama said. "But we can choose how we handle them. You chose to breathe and use your words."

Benny nodded. "Next time I feel big mad feelings, I'll remember to breathe like bubbles."

"And you can always ask questions if you don't understand something," Mama added.

Benny rinsed his mouth with cool water. The bathroom felt peaceful now. His angry feelings had gotten small and quiet.

"I'm ready for my bedtime story now," Benny said happily.

As they left the bathroom, Benny felt proud. He had learned that big feelings were normal, but he could choose how to handle them. Taking deep breaths, asking questions, and making choices helped him feel better.

The End.
