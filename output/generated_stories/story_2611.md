# Ellie's Special Paper Garden

Little Ellie the elephant calf sat in her cozy nursery, surrounded by soft pillows and warm sunlight streaming through the window. Her trunk wiggled with excitement as she looked at the colorful papers scattered on her small table.

"What should I make today?" Ellie wondered aloud, touching the smooth papers with her gentle trunk tip.

Just then, her friend Benny the bunny hopped into the nursery. "Hi Ellie! What are you doing?"

"I want to make something special," Ellie said, "but I'm not sure what."

Benny's nose twitched as he sniffed the air. "I smell fresh flowers outside! Maybe we could make paper flowers?"

Ellie's eyes sparkled. "That's a wonderful idea! But I don't know how."

"We can learn together!" Benny said cheerfully. "My mama taught me yesterday. First, we need to pick the right colors."

Ellie carefully selected green paper for stems and bright yellow, pink, and purple papers for petals. The papers felt crisp between her trunk fingers, and they made a soft rustling sound as she moved them.

"Now," said Benny, "we fold the colored paper like this." He showed Ellie how to make small accordion folds. "Back and forth, back and forth."

Ellie tried to copy Benny, but her first attempts were wobbly. "Mine doesn't look right," she sighed.

"That's okay!" Benny encouraged. "Practice makes better. Try again, and I'll help you."

Together, they folded and creased the papers. Ellie's trunk was very good at making the folds once she practiced. Soon, she had beautiful fan-shaped pieces.

"Now we twist them in the middle," Benny explained, "and spread out the ends like flower petals."

"Oh my!" Ellie gasped as her paper transformed into a lovely flower. "It's beautiful!"

They worked side by side, making flower after flower. The nursery filled with the gentle sounds of paper crinkling and their happy chatter.

"Let's make stems too," suggested Ellie. She rolled the green paper into long, thin tubes. "These feel smooth and sturdy."

As they crafted, more friends arrived. Rosie the rabbit and Danny the deer peeked into the nursery.

"Wow! What pretty flowers!" exclaimed Rosie.

"We're making a paper garden," Ellie explained proudly. "Would you like to help us?"

"Yes, please!" said Danny. "But we don't know how."

"We can teach you," offered Benny. "Ellie learned really fast!"

Ellie felt warm and happy inside. She carefully showed Rosie how to fold the paper, speaking slowly and clearly. "See? Fold this way, then that way. You're doing great!"

Soon, all four friends were working together, sharing papers and helping each other. The nursery buzzed with friendly conversation and the soft sounds of crafting.

"Look at our beautiful garden!" Ellie said when they finished. Dozens of colorful paper flowers covered the table, creating a rainbow of joy.

"And the best part," added Benny, "is that we didn't pick real flowers. The real ones can stay outside for the bees and butterflies!"

"That's very kind to nature," agreed Ellie, feeling proud of their thoughtful choice.

As their friends headed home, Ellie arranged her paper flowers in a special corner of her nursery. Every time she looked at them, she remembered the fun of learning something new, the joy of working with friends, and the good feeling of being kind to the environment.

"Tomorrow," Ellie whispered to herself, "I want to learn how to make paper butterflies!"

The End.
