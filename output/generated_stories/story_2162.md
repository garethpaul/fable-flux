# Shelly's Big Feelings Adventure

In a cozy basement lab filled with colorful bottles and bubbling beakers, lived a shy little turtle named Shelly. The lab smelled like vanilla and cinnamon, and soft lights twinkled like stars on the ceiling.

Shelly loved her quiet lab home, but today felt different. Her friend Benny the bunny was visiting, and he was VERY excited about everything.

"WOW! Look at all these cool things!" Benny shouted, hopping from table to table. "Can I touch this? What about this shiny button?"

Shelly pulled her head into her shell a little. "Please be careful," she whispered. "Those are my special science things."

But Benny didn't hear her quiet voice. He bumped into her favorite rainbow crystal, and it rolled across the floor with a loud CLINK!

"Oh no!" Shelly's face turned red. Her heart started beating fast like a little drum. She felt angry bubbles growing in her tummy.

"STOP!" Shelly shouted, surprising herself. "You're being too loud and messy!"

Benny's ears drooped. "I'm sorry, Shelly. I was just excited."

Shelly felt the angry bubbles getting bigger. She wanted to hide in her shell forever, but then she remembered what her grandpa turtle had taught her about big feelings.

"When you feel angry bubbles," she whispered to herself, "take three deep breaths like a sleepy dragon."

Shelly breathed in slowly... "Whoooosh." Then out... "Ahhhhhh." She did this three times, and the angry bubbles started to shrink.

"Benny," Shelly said in her normal voice, "I felt angry when you touched my things without asking. But I should have spoken louder the first time."

Benny nodded. "I'm sorry I didn't listen. I get excited and forget to be gentle."

Shelly thought for a moment. "Would you like to do science together? But we need to make some rules first."

"Yes!" Benny's eyes sparkled. "What kind of rules?"

"Well," Shelly said, feeling braver, "we should ask before touching things, use quiet voices in my lab, and take turns. Does that sound fair?"

"That sounds perfect!" Benny agreed. "What should we make first?"

Shelly smiled and pulled out her special mixing bowls. "Let's make rainbow slime! You can help me measure the colors."

Together, they carefully mixed blue and yellow powders. Benny remembered to use his quiet voice and ask, "May I pour this in now, Shelly?"

"Yes, thank you for asking," Shelly replied, feeling proud of how they were working together.

As they stirred the mixture, it turned the most beautiful green color. The slime felt squishy and smooth between their fingers.

"This is amazing!" Benny whispered excitedly. "And I like how we're taking turns."

Shelly beamed. "I like it too. When we talk about our feelings and make fair rules, everyone can have fun."

They spent the rest of the afternoon creating colorful slime rainbows, each taking turns and asking permission. The basement lab filled with happy giggles and the sweet smell of their successful experiments.

When it was time for Benny to go home, he gave Shelly a gentle hug. "Thank you for teaching me about being careful and for sharing your amazing lab."

"Thank you for listening to my feelings," Shelly replied. "Even shy turtles can speak up when something is important."

That night, as Shelly cleaned her lab, she felt proud. She had learned that angry feelings were okay, but she could use her breath and words to handle them. And the best part? She had made her friendship with Benny even stronger.

The End.
