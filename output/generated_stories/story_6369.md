# Panda Poppy’s Dinnertime Dance

Poppy was a patient panda with soft black-and-white fur and bright, kind eyes. She lived in a cozy house where the dining room smelled like warm rice and sweet bamboo soup. The table was smooth and shiny. The chairs made little squeaks on the floor. Sunlight peeked through the curtains and made golden squares on the rug.

“Dinner time is soon,” said Mama Panda, stirring a pot that went bubble-bubble. “Poppy, can you help set the table?”

Poppy took a deep breath. In... and out. It felt calm, like floating on a little cloud. “Yes, Mama. I can help,” she said.

Poppy liked to help. She liked to move slowly and carefully. She liked to learn new skills. Today, she had a new idea.

“I will make it a dance!” Poppy whispered to herself. “A dinnertime dance!”

She tapped her paw on the floor. Tip-tap, tip-tap. The floor felt cool under her pads. It made her smile.

First, Poppy picked up the napkins. They were soft and smelled like clean sunshine. “One for Mama, one for Papa, one for me,” she sang. She twirled in a little circle. Whoosh! The napkins fluttered like butterfly wings. She placed them gently by each plate spot.

“Well done, Poppy,” said Mama Panda. “You are careful and kind.”

Poppy felt proud. Her chest felt warm and big, like a balloon. “Thank you, Mama,” she said. “I am doing my best.”

Next, Poppy carried the spoons. Clink-clink, they went in her paw. She counted, “One, two, three.” She made a gentle spoon slide across the table. Sliiide. It sounded like a tiny song. She wiggled her hips and stepped side to side. Step, step, step. It was her spoon slide dance!

Papa Panda peeked in with a smile. “I see a dancer and a helper,” he said. “What a skill! Setting the table with joy.”

Poppy’s ears felt tingly-happy. “I am learning,” she said. “I am building big skills.”

When it was time for the cups, Poppy paused. The cups were shiny and smooth. They could tip and clatter. Poppy felt a little flutter in her tummy. “I want to be safe,” she said softly.

Mama Panda nodded. “Wellness means taking care of your body and your feelings. What can help you feel steady?”

Poppy put a paw on her belly. In... and out. Breath in like smelling a flower. Breath out like blowing a feather. She imagined her feet were roots, strong and still. “I am steady,” she whispered. “I can do it.”

She picked up one cup at a time. Tap-tap went her paws on the floor in a slow beat. She made a tiny cup-curtsy and placed each one carefully. Plink. Plink. Plink.

“You did it,” Mama Panda said, giving Poppy a gentle paw-hug. “You listened to your body. You breathed. That is wellness.”

Poppy’s eyes shone. “Wellness helps me dance and help!”

Papa set down the bamboo soup. Mmm! It smelled sweet and cozy. Steam curled up like little ribbons. Poppy’s tummy gave a happy grumble. “Rumble-rumble,” she giggled.

There were a few crumbs on the rug. Poppy grabbed a small brush. Swish, swish. The brush made soft whispers. She bent her knees and made a cleaning dance—brush-hop, brush-hop. It felt fun to move her body and help her home feel neat.

“Chores can be a game,” Papa Panda said. “You are turning work into play.”

Poppy nodded. “I am strong. I am careful. I am kind.” Saying these words felt like a warm blanket. She smiled at her reflection in a shiny spoon. “I can do hard things.”

Mama clapped softly. “Let’s have a dinnertime dance party before we eat!”

Poppy tapped a rhythm on the table with her paw. Tap-ta-tap! Papa hummed a tune. Mmm-mmm-mmm. Mama swayed her shoulders. The dining room filled with gentle music, the smell of soup, and the glow of happy faces. Poppy led the dance: step, breathe, smile; step, breathe, smile. Her tail gave a little wag.

After the dance, Poppy placed her paw over her heart. Thump-thump. “Thank you, body,” she said. “Thank you, breath. Thank you, helpers.”

They sat down to eat. The bowls were warm in their paws. The soup tasted sweet and soft. Poppy chewed slowly. Crunch of veggies, sip of soup, happy hum.

Papa asked, “What did you learn today, Poppy?”

Poppy thought and felt the answer. “I learned that wellness is taking care of my body and feelings. I can breathe to feel calm. I can move and dance to feel strong. I can help with chores and build skills. That helps me feel brave.”

Mama smiled. “And you trusted yourself.”

Poppy nodded. “I feel proud.”

The dining room was cozy and clean. The chairs squeaked, the spoons clinked, and the family giggled. Poppy’s heart felt light, and her tummy felt full.

“I am a patient panda,” Poppy said. “I can dance through my day.”

And she did.

The End.
