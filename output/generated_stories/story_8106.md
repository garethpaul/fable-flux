# Mango the Monkey and the Attic Adventure

Mango was a small, courageous monkey with bright eyes and gentle hands. One sunny afternoon, Mango climbed the stairs to a quiet attic. The attic was still and cozy. Dusty sunbeams made golden lines in the air. The wooden floor felt cool on Mango’s toes, and the air smelled like old books and clean cedar.

“I am ready for a little adventure,” Mango whispered. His voice sounded soft and echoey in the quiet.

All around were boxes, baskets, and a wobbly tower of hats. There were buttons in jars, ribbons in a tin, and a broken toy drum. Mango’s tail swished with excitement. “So many things!” he said. “I’ll find something special today.”

Mango opened a box. Inside were puzzle pieces of many colors. He tried to put them together, but the pieces were mixed up. “Hmm,” Mango said, “these pieces are all jumbled.” He felt a tiny bubble of frustration in his tummy.

He took a deep breath. In…and out. “I can stay calm,” Mango told himself. “I can be resourceful. I can find a way.”

Mango looked around. He spotted empty muffin cups in a baking tray. “Aha!” he said. “These can help me sort.” He set the tray on the floor. Tap, tap, tap went the metal on the wood.

“Blue pieces in this cup, red pieces in that cup,” he said. He hummed a happy tune as he worked. Hmmm-mmm, hmmm-mmm. Little by little, the puzzle looked less messy. Mango smiled. “Sorting helps my brain feel calm,” he said.

Just then, a tiny mouse peeked from behind a basket. “Hello,” squeaked the mouse. “I’m Mimi. What are you doing?”

“I’m Mango,” said the monkey. “I am making order. I’m being resourceful! Would you like to help?”

Mimi nodded. “I can carry small pieces,” she squeaked.

Together, they sorted. Plink went the pieces into the cups. Swish went Mango’s tail. The attic felt friendly now, not so quiet and big. “Thank you for sharing the work,” Mango said.

“My whiskers like to help,” Mimi giggled.

When the puzzle was sorted, Mango tried the pieces again. The edge pieces clicked together. Click. Click. Click. But one piece was missing. Mango’s ears drooped. “Oh no,” he said, “I can’t finish.”

Frustration bubbled again. He felt warm in his cheeks. He wanted to throw his hands up. Instead, Mango sat crisscross, closed his eyes, and breathed. “In…and out,” he whispered. “I can stay calm. I can try a new idea.”

Mango opened his eyes and looked carefully. He listened to the soft hum of the attic. He smelled the cedar again. He looked under the hat tower. He peeked behind the drum. “Where could it be?” he wondered.

Mimi tugged on a ribbon. “Maybe the piece is hiding,” she squeaked. The ribbon led to a small, messy drawer. Mango pulled it open. Rustle, rustle. Inside were buttons, paper clips, and a little notebook.

“I can be organized,” Mango said. He found a small bowl and sorted the drawer. Buttons here, clips there, and papers in a neat stack. At the bottom, he found a folded cloth. He lifted it and gasped. “The missing piece!”

Mimi clapped her tiny paws. “You did it!”

Mango grinned. “We did it, together.” He placed the final piece. Click! The puzzle made a picture of a banana tree under the sun. The colors were bright and warm. Mango’s heart felt bright and warm too.

“Now what?” Mimi asked.

Mango looked at the other boxes. He had an idea. “Let’s make the attic friendly for everyone,” he said. “We can share the space. We can label the boxes. We can make a calm corner.”

They found sticky notes and a crayon. Mango wrote simple words and drew pictures: “Hats,” with a little hat. “Ribbons,” with a bow. “Puzzles,” with a puzzle piece. Mimi helped by placing the notes. Tap went her tiny paws.

They made a cozy calm corner with a soft blanket and the little notebook. “This is where we breathe when we feel big feelings,” Mango said. He showed Mimi how to put a hand on the belly. “In…and out.” They practiced together. Their shoulders relaxed. The attic felt peaceful.

Finally, Mango held up the puzzle box. “Let’s share this with others,” he said. They placed the puzzle on a low shelf so any friend could reach it. Mango felt proud. “When we share and get organized, we help everyone.”

Mimi smiled. “You were brave and clever today,” she squeaked.

Mango nodded. “I felt frustrated, but I used calm breaths. I looked for tools. I sorted and organized. I asked for help and shared the work. That is being resourceful.”

The sunbeams in the attic turned softer and pink. Mango and Mimi sat together in the calm corner. They listened to the quiet. It sounded like a gentle hush.

Mango whispered, “Today I learned that when things feel hard, I can slow down, use my tools, and try a new idea.”

Mimi whispered back, “And I learned that sharing makes the work lighter and the attic nicer.”

They smiled. Their hearts felt light and steady.

The End.
