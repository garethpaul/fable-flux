# Ollie Otter’s Rooftop Picnic Promise

Ollie was a playful otter with bright eyes and fast paws. He lived by a tall building with a flat, safe rooftop garden. The roof had soft grass, flower boxes, and a little blue bench. The air smelled like tomatoes and mint. Bees buzzed. Pigeons cooed.

Today, Ollie wanted to host a picnic for friends. He clapped his paws. “A rooftop picnic! Yum yum!” he sang. He invited Maya the mouse, Tariq the turtle, and Bella the bird.

“I will bring crunchy carrots,” said Maya.
“I will bring juicy berries,” chirped Bella.
“I will bring a big water jug,” rumbled Tariq.
“I will bring napkins, plates, and my words,” said Ollie. “I will greet everyone in many languages! Hola! Bonjour! Jambo!”

The sun felt warm. The breeze was gentle. Ollie bounced around, giggling. He chased a fluttering leaf. He rolled on the soft grass. He made a silly whistle. He did not get the plates. He did not get the napkins. He did not wash his paws. He did not set the little table. He forgot to prepare.

Soon, footsteps tapped on the stairs. “We’re here!” called Maya.
“Tweet-tweet!” sang Bella.
“Knock-knock,” said Tariq, opening the rooftop door with a slow smile.

They looked around. No plates. No napkins. No clean table. Ollie’s whiskers drooped.

Maya held a basket. “Where do I put the carrots?” she asked.
Bella flapped. “Where are the cups?”
Tariq blinked. “Is the water spot ready?”

Ollie’s tummy felt twisty. His paws felt sticky from playing. The wind brought the smell of mint and a little dust. He knew he had made a mistake. He had not prepared.

Ollie took a deep breath. He put his paws on his chest to feel it rise and fall. He remembered his promise to use good words. His voice was small but brave. “I am sorry,” he said. “I did not get ready. I forgot the plates and napkins. I did not wash my paws. I was playing. I made a mistake.”

He looked at each friend. “Maya, Bella, Tariq, will you forgive me?”

Maya’s whiskers twitched. “Thank you for saying sorry, Ollie,” she said kindly.
Bella tilted her head. “Saying sorry shows a brave heart.”
Tariq nodded slowly. “An apology is the first step. The next step is to fix it.”

Ollie’s eyes felt wet, but his heart felt lighter. “Yes,” he said. “I will fix it. I will prepare now.”

He washed his paws with warm, soapy water from the rooftop sink. Splash, swish! He dried them on a clean towel. He set the little table. He spread a bright cloth. He counted, “One, two, three, four,” and placed four plates and four cups. He laid napkins like soft, white squares. He made a shade spot for Tariq with a big leaf umbrella. He poured cool water with care.

As he worked, he used gentle words in many languages to show care and to say sorry again. “Lo siento, Maya,” he said in Spanish. “Merci, Bella,” he said in French, for helping hold the cloth. “Asante, Tariq,” he said in Swahili, for waiting. “Thank you,” he added, smiling at them all.

The picnic began. Crunch! went Maya’s carrots. “Healthy and orange!” she squeaked.
Bella dropped berries into a bowl. Plop, plip! “Sweet and full of juice,” she chirped.
Tariq poured water. Glug, glug. “Cool and good for our bodies,” he said.
Ollie added slices of green cucumber and yellow banana from a little basket he kept on the rooftop. “Fresh and yummy,” he said.

The sun warmed their fur, feathers, and shell. A cloud drifted like a soft ship. The mint leaves smelled bright. Ollie felt calm. He spoke with care and joy. He said, “Please,” and “Thank you,” and “Excuse me.” He also shared more words. “Hola means hello. Bonjour means hello, too. Jambo means hello in Swahili. Words can be friends. Words can help.”

Bella smiled. “Your words made me feel seen.”
Maya nibbled. “Your apology made me feel safe.”
Tariq sipped. “Your preparation made us all comfortable.”

Ollie nodded. “I learned something today. Playing is fun, but promises come first. When we forget, we can say sorry, then make it right.”

They finished with a courage game. Each friend said one brave thing they would try.
“I will try a new food,” said Bella, pecking a piece of cucumber. “Crunch! I like it!”
“I will try to speak a new word,” said Maya. “Gracias!”
“I will try to carry the heavy jug next time,” said Tariq, flexing his strong legs.
Ollie puffed up his chest. “I will make a list next time so I remember to prepare. Plates, napkins, water, wash paws!” He giggled. “And I will keep kind words ready on my tongue.”

As the sky turned soft pink, Ollie looked at his friends. “Thank you for forgiving me,” he said, clear and warm.
“You are welcome,” they answered together.

They watched a pigeon land on the blue bench. Coo, coo. The rooftop felt peaceful and good. Ollie’s heart felt full. He had said sorry. He had fixed his mistake. He had learned to prepare. He had used loving words in many languages. And he had shared a healthy meal.

The friends packed up. The breeze smelled like mint and sunshine. Ollie waved. “Adiós! Au revoir! Kwaheri! See you soon!”

The End.
