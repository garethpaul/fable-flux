# Nutkin's Big Apology

In a cozy, quiet attic filled with dusty boxes and golden sunbeams, lived a little gray squirrel named Nutkin. The attic smelled like old wood and lavender sachets, and Nutkin loved exploring every corner.

One sunny morning, Nutkin was busy organizing his collection of acorns when he heard tiny squeaks coming from behind a big cardboard box.

"Help! Help!" came a small voice.

Nutkin's fluffy tail twitched with curiosity. He scampered over and found a little field mouse named Pip trapped under a fallen book.

"Oh my! Let me help you!" Nutkin said, quickly pushing the heavy book away with his strong paws.

Pip scrambled to his feet, brushing dust from his whiskers. "Thank you so much, Nutkin!"

But instead of saying "you're welcome," Nutkin puffed out his chest proudly. "Of course I saved you! I'm the strongest, smartest squirrel in this whole attic. You're lucky I was here!"

Pip's ears drooped. He looked sad and small. "Oh... okay," he whispered, and quietly scurried away to his tiny nest in the corner.

Nutkin felt confused. Why didn't Pip seem happy? He had helped, hadn't he?

The next day, Nutkin was trying to reach some sunflower seeds on a high shelf when he slipped and tumbled down, scattering seeds everywhere.

"Ouch!" Nutkin rubbed his sore tail.

Pip heard the crash and peeked out from his nest. For a moment, he looked like he wanted to help. But then he remembered how Nutkin had acted the day before, and he stayed hidden.

Nutkin spent the whole morning picking up seeds by himself. His back ached, and he felt lonely. The quiet attic seemed too quiet without a friend to talk to.

That afternoon, Nutkin sat by the round attic window, watching raindrops race down the glass. He thought about yesterday and how sad Pip had looked.

"I was mean," Nutkin said out loud to himself. "Pip needed help, and I helped him, but then I bragged and made him feel small. That wasn't kind."

Nutkin's tummy felt twisty and uncomfortable. He knew what he had to do.

He gathered some of his favorite sunflower seeds and walked slowly to Pip's corner. The little mouse was reading a tiny book made from a folded piece of paper.

"Pip?" Nutkin said softly. "Can we talk?"

Pip looked up with cautious eyes. "What do you want, Nutkin?"

Nutkin took a deep breath. "I want to say I'm sorry. Yesterday, when I helped you, I said mean things afterward. I bragged and made you feel bad, and that was wrong. You deserved kindness, not bragging."

Pip's whiskers twitched with surprise.

"I brought you some sunflower seeds to share," Nutkin continued, holding out his paws. "And I promise to be a better friend. Will you forgive me?"

Pip's face brightened like sunshine after rain. "Yes, Nutkin! Thank you for apologizing. That took courage."

From that day on, Nutkin and Pip became the best of friends. They explored the attic together, shared snacks, and helped each other with big and small problems. And whenever Nutkin made a mistake, he remembered that saying "I'm sorry" and meaning it was one of the most important things a friend could do.

The quiet attic was never lonely again, filled instead with laughter, kindness, and the warm feeling that comes from true friendship.

The End.
