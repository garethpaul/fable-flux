# Pebble the Penguin’s Paint-Day Plan

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with bright eyes and a bobbly waddle. Today, Pebble slid into the creative art room, where the air smelled like sweet glue and orange paint. Sunlight made shiny spots on the floor. Brushes stood like tiny trees in cups. Paper rustled like soft snow.

“I have a big goal,” Pebble chirped, tapping a fin to a list. “Step one: paint a rainbow fish. Step two: make it sparkle. Step three: share it with friends!”

Pebble set out the colors. “Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple,” Pebble sang. The paint felt cool and smooth under Pebble’s fin. “Splish, splash, swish!” The brush hissed softly across the paper.

But oops! The blue paint dripped. It splatted a big blue drop on the rainbow fish’s eye.

Pebble’s tummy felt tight. “Oh no,” Pebble whispered. “I wanted it just right.” Pebble’s face scrunched. The brush shook. The more Pebble tried to fix it, the bigger the smear grew.

“Grr… I feel mad,” Pebble said, flapping. Pebble wanted to push the paper away.

Just then, Ms. Maple, the gentle art teacher, waddled over in soft slippers. She smelled like lavender and crayons. “I see a big feeling,” she said in a calm voice. “Would you like to try the calm plan?”

Pebble nodded. “Yes, please.”

“Let’s take three slow breaths,” Ms. Maple said. “Smell the flower.” Pebble breathed in. “Blow the candle.” Pebble breathed out. They did it again. In, out. In, out. Pebble’s tummy softened. The brush stopped shaking.

“Now, tell your feeling a sentence,” Ms. Maple said. Pebble whispered, “I feel frustrated because my picture got a big blue drop.”

Ms. Maple smiled. “Good listening to your feelings. Would you like to try a fix, or take a small break?”

Pebble looked at the paper. “Small break,” Pebble decided. Pebble closed eyes and listened to the room: tap-tap of scissors, swish-swish of brushes, giggles like bubbles. The break felt like a cool sip of water.

When Pebble opened eyes, something new caught the light. A bowl of shiny buttons sat on the shelf. They gleamed like tiny moons. “Discovery!” Pebble chirped. “Maybe sparkles can help my mistake.”

Pebble waddled over, but stopped. Another penguin, Finn, stood there, scooping a big pile of sparkly stars into a bag. His eyes were wide. “Stars are for my picture,” Finn said. “All of them.”

Pebble’s beak tilted. “But we share,” Pebble said softly. “Everyone needs some.”

Finn hugged the bag. Ms. Maple came close. “Let’s listen,” she said. “Pebble, what do you need?”

“I need a few stars to cover my big blue drop,” Pebble said. “I have a goal to finish my rainbow fish.”

“Finn, what do you need?” Ms. Maple asked.

“I need stars to make my night sky,” Finn said. “Lots and lots.”

Ms. Maple nodded. “We use kind justice here. That means fair for everyone,” she said. “Let’s make a fair plan. We can count and share.”

She poured the stars on the table. They made a soft tinkling sound. “Let’s each take ten,” she suggested, “and we will save the rest for others.”

Pebble and Finn counted together. “One, two, three…” The stars clicked like tiny bells. Finn looked at Pebble’s smeared picture and frowned. “I didn’t see your big blue drop,” he said. “I can share two extra.”

Pebble smiled. “Thank you. I will save two if I don’t need them,” Pebble answered. “We can listen and share.”

Back at the easel, Pebble made a plan. “Step one: dab, not drag,” Pebble said. Pebble dipped a small brush into glue. “Step two: place stars gently.” Pebble’s fin pressed the stars on the blue drop. The stars felt cool and bumpy. “Step three: add silver swirls around the fish.”

Pebble hummed, “Swish-swish, twirl-twirl,” and dotted tiny bubbles with a cotton tip. The rainbow fish looked like it swam in a starry sea. Pebble’s chest felt warm and proud.

Finn came by. “Wow,” he whispered. “Your mistake became magic.”

Pebble giggled. “It wasn’t magic. It was a calm plan, listening, and a fair share.”

Ms. Maple clapped softly. “You set a goal. You felt frustrated. You used breaths, words, and a break. You discovered a new idea. And you practiced justice by sharing. That is strong art and strong heart.”

Pebble held up the picture. It smelled like fresh paint and sparkles. Friends gathered around. “Ooooh,” they said. Pebble bowed. “Thank you.”

Finn showed his night sky. Pebble looked closely. “I have two stars left,” Pebble said. “Do you want them?” Finn beamed. “Yes, please!”

At cleanup time, the sink water gurgled. The sponge felt squishy. Pebble sang a tidy-up song. “Wash-wash, dry-dry, we did our best, we tried-tried!”

When the room was neat, Pebble sat quietly and patted the list. “Goal done,” Pebble said. “I painted a rainbow fish. I made it sparkle. I shared it with friends.”

Ms. Maple asked, “What did we learn today?”

Pebble thought. “When I feel frustrated, I can stop, breathe, and use words. I can take a small break. I can make a new plan. I can listen and share so it’s fair for everyone. Mistakes can turn into discoveries.”

Ms. Maple nodded. “That is a bright lesson.”

Pebble’s eyes twinkled like stars. “I can try again tomorrow,” Pebble said. “New goals, new ideas, and a calm heart.”

The End.
