# The Giraffe Who Wrote Fair Rules

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with long, gentle eyelashes and a cozy home office. Her office was sunny and bright. A soft rug tickled her hooves. Her desk smelled like fresh wood. Pencils stood like tiny trees in a cup. Tap-tap went her keyboard as she typed and hummed, “Be fair, be kind, be safe.”

Every morning, Gigi followed her routine. First, she stretched her long neck. Pop-pop went her joints. Then she sipped warm tea that smelled like sweet leaves. Finally, she opened her notebook and wrote, “Today I will help.”

One day, Gigi heard a rumble in the hall. It sounded like rolling thunder. “Bump, bump, bump!” She tilted her head. “What is that sound?” she asked.

Gigi walked to the door and peeked out. She saw her friends, Tiny Tortoise and Buzzy Bee. Tiny was crying big, slow tears. “Plop… plop…” Buzzy buzzed in worried zigzags.

“What’s wrong?” Gigi asked softly.

Tiny sniffed. “B-Buzzy took my blue crayon. She did not ask,” he said.

Buzzy buzzed louder. “I only wanted to draw a sky! I did not mean to be unkind,” she said, wings a-tremble.

Gigi nodded. “Hmm,” she said. “We have a problem. But we can find a fair way. We can use justice.”

Tiny blinked. “What is justice?” he asked, voice small.

Gigi smiled kindly. “Justice means fair and right for everyone. It keeps us safe. It helps us fix problems the kind way.”

Buzzy’s wings slowed. “Can justice help us share?” she asked.

“Yes,” said Gigi. “Let’s talk. Let’s listen. Let’s make a plan.”

Gigi led them into her home office. Sunlight made warm stripes on the floor. She pulled out her big notebook. It smelled like new paper. She wrote with a soft scratch-scratch, “Fair Rules.”

“First,” Gigi said, “we take turns to speak.”

Tiny raised a little foot. “I feel sad,” he said. “The blue crayon is special. It was a gift. When Buzzy took it, I felt small.”

Buzzy nodded. “I hear you, Tiny,” she said. “I feel sorry. I was excited to draw. I forgot to ask. I want to fix it.”

Gigi smiled. “Good listening. Good honesty. That is part of justice. Now let’s think of a safe and fair plan.”

Gigi wrote, “Ask first.” She wrote, “Use a timer.” She wrote, “Keep hands and hooves gentle.”

Tiny peered at the page. “A timer?” he asked.

Gigi held up a little sand timer. Swish went the golden sand. “We can share time. Tiny uses the blue crayon for one turn. Then Buzzy uses it. And every time we share, we ask with kind words.”

Buzzy buzzed happily. “May I please have a turn after Tiny?” she asked.

Tiny smiled. “Yes, Buzzy. After my turn, it is your turn,” he said.

Gigi clapped her hooves softly. “That sounds fair,” she said. “But there is more. Justice means we fix what went wrong.”

Buzzy nodded. “Tiny, I am sorry I took your crayon without asking,” she said. “How can I make it better?”

Tiny thought. He tapped-tapped his shell. “Maybe we can draw together,” he said. “We can make one big sky picture.”

Gigi beamed. “That is teamwork!” she said.

They set up a safe space at the low table. Gigi put the crayons in a little basket. “We will keep the basket in the middle,” she said. “We will ask before we take. We will use gentle hands.”

Tiny took the blue crayon. He drew a wide, blue sky. The crayon felt smooth. Scritch-scratch. Buzzy drew fluffy clouds that looked like cotton candy. Swirl-swoosh. Gigi added a tall giraffe with a smile.

“Look!” said Gigi. “We are working as a team. We are being fair.”

Tiny glanced at the sand timer. “The sand is almost gone,” he said. When the last grains slid down, he passed the blue crayon to Buzzy. “Your turn,” he said.

“Thank you,” said Buzzy. She drew a safe, bright sun with short rays. “I feel happy,” she said. “Justice feels good.”

Gigi wrote one more line in her notebook: “Say sorry. Fix it. Be fair.” She read the rules aloud:
“Ask first.
Take turns.
Use gentle hands.
Say sorry and fix it.”

Tiny and Buzzy nodded. “These are good rules,” Tiny said. “They keep us safe.”

Buzzy buzzed a little song. “Fair for you and fair for me,” she sang. “That is how we like to be.”

Gigi taped the “Fair Rules” on the wall. The tape made a soft rip sound. “Now our home office has clear rules,” she said. “We can follow them every day as part of our routine.”

They finished the picture together. Tiny drew a tiny kite. Buzzy added a bright ribbon tail. Gigi wrote the title at the bottom: “Our Fair Sky.”

Tiny smiled a big, slow smile. “Thank you, Gigi,” he said. “Justice helped my heart feel calm.”

Buzzy nodded. “Justice helped me make it right,” she said. “And teamwork made it fun!”

Gigi closed her notebook with a gentle thump. “Today we learned that justice is being fair and kind, keeping everyone safe, and fixing mistakes together,” she said. “We ask, we listen, we share, and we care.”

The friends hugged. It was a warm, soft hug, like a cozy blanket. The sun shone brighter, and the room smelled like crayons and tea.

Gigi whispered, “Be fair, be kind, be safe.” And they all smiled.

The End.
