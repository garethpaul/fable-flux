# Panda Poppy’s Treehouse Tasks

Panda Poppy lived in a cozy treehouse that smelled like fresh bamboo and sweet leaves. Her soft black-and-white fur felt warm in the morning sun. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” and the wind whispered, “Whooo,” through the branches.

Today was Task Day. A little bell on the wall went, “Ting!” Poppy smiled and clapped her paws. “Today I finish my three tasks,” she said. “I can do it. I will be patient. I will be fair.”

She read the list on a big leaf:
1. Sweep the floor.
2. Sort the blocks.
3. Share the swing.

Poppy picked up her straw broom. “Swish, swish,” went the broom on the wooden floor. Dust danced like tiny stars in the sunbeams. Poppy hummed, “La-la-la,” and kept her paws steady.

Her friend, Squirrel Sunny, peeked in. “Poppy! Come play chase!” Sunny chattered.

Poppy looked at her list. She felt a wiggle of “I want to play now!” in her tummy. But she took a deep breath. “I will be patient,” Poppy said. “I need to finish sweeping first.”

Sunny nodded. “Okay. I will wait and help!” Sunny grabbed a small brush. Together they swept. “Swish, swish, brush, brush.” The dust went into a little pan. Poppy smiled. “Thank you, Sunny.”

“Task One is done,” Poppy said, tapping the leaf list. Her heart felt proud and calm.

Next was sorting the blocks. The blocks were red, blue, and yellow. They felt smooth and cool in Poppy’s paws. Poppy said, “Red blocks in the red basket. Blue in blue. Yellow in yellow.”

Sunny reached for the red basket. A tiny mouse named Mimi scurried in. “Squeak! I want the red basket!” Mimi cried, grabbing it too.

Sunny and Mimi both held the basket. Their eyes got big. “Mine!” said Sunny.
“Mine!” said Mimi.

Poppy put her paws up. “Let’s be fair,” she said gently. “We can take turns. Sunny, you can put in five red blocks. Then Mimi, you can put in five red blocks. We will count together.”

Sunny and Mimi looked at Poppy. Poppy’s voice was kind and steady. “One, two, three, four, five,” they counted as Sunny dropped the blocks. “Plunk, plunk, plunk.”

Then Mimi had a turn. “One, two, three, four, five,” they counted. “Plunk, plunk, plunk.”

They smiled. “That was fair,” said Sunny.
“That felt good,” squeaked Mimi.

Poppy felt the warm glow of happy fairness. “Thank you for taking turns,” she said.

They finished the blocks. “Task Two is done!” Poppy cheered. The baskets looked neat. The treehouse felt tidy and calm.

Now it was time to share the swing. The swing was bright green and smelled like leaves. It moved with a soft creak, “Creeeak,” in the breeze.

Soon, more friends came: Bunny Bella and Bird Pip. “We all want to swing!” they said together.

Poppy looked at the long line of friends. Her tummy felt a flutter of worry. “So many friends,” she thought. “How will we all swing?” She took a slow breath in and out. “I can do this. I will be patient. I will be fair.”

Poppy had an idea. She found her little sand timer. “We will take turns,” she said. “Each friend gets one turn until the sand runs out. Then we switch.”

Pip chirped, “Me first!”
Bella said, “Me too!”
Sunny squeaked, “Me three!”
Mimi squealed, “Me four!”

Poppy nodded and smiled. “We will go in the order you came,” she said calmly. “Pip, then Bella, then Sunny, then Mimi. I will go last.”

Pip hopped on. The swing went “Whoosh!” The wind tickled his feathers. He laughed, “Wheee!” When the sand ran out, Poppy rang the bell. “Ting!”

“Switch,” Poppy said kindly. “Thank you, Pip.”

Bella swung next. Her ears flapped. Sunny and Mimi waited with wiggly feet and twitchy whiskers. It was hard to wait. Sunny stomped once. Mimi squeaked, “Is it my turn yet?”

Poppy knelt down. “Waiting is hard,” she said softly. “Let’s breathe together.” They breathed in and out. Poppy counted, “One, two, three.” Their bodies relaxed.

“Ding!” went the timer. Sunny’s turn, then Mimi’s. Finally, all eyes turned to Poppy.

Poppy sat on the swing. She felt the smooth rope in her paws, the cool air on her face. “Whoosh,” went the swing. Her heart felt light. “I waited my turn,” she thought. “I finished my tasks. I was patient, fair, and strong.”

When the sun turned golden, Poppy rang the bell once more. “Friends,” she said, “we did it! We swept, we sorted, and we shared. We were fair and we finished our tasks.”

Sunny grinned. “You were very patient, Poppy.”
Mimi squeaked, “You helped us be fair.”
Bella nodded. “We all finished together.”

Poppy hugged her friends. “When things are hard,” she said, “we can breathe, take turns, and keep going. That is resilience. We try, we wait, and we finish.”

Everyone cheered. “We are resilient!” they said, giggling.

Poppy looked around her tidy treehouse. It smelled sweet and clean. The evening breeze sang, “Whooo,” and the bell made a happy “Ting.” Poppy’s heart felt full.

“Tomorrow,” she said, “we can do it again.”

The End.
