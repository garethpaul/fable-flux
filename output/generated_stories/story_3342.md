# Gigi the Giraffe Tries a Team Game

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with big gentle eyes and a long, warm neck. She loved to think, plan, and draw. Today, Gigi stepped into the creative art room. The room smelled like fresh paper and sweet crayons. Bright paints winked from little cups. Soft music hummed, tap-tap-tapping like raindrops.

“Good morning,” Gigi said softly. She touched a paintbrush and smiled at the tickly bristles. “Today will be colorful.”

Her friends were already there. Timo the tiny tiger rolled a ball in his paws. Bella the bunny stacked cups into a rainbow tower. Pio the parrot flapped and chirped, “Game time! Game time!”

Ms. Dot, the friendly teacher, clapped her hands. “Friends, we will make art and play a team sport today,” she said. “We will paint a big poster together. Then we will try a gentle ball game called Roll-and-Goal. We will use teamwork. We will use courage.”

Gigi’s heart thumped. She liked painting. She was not sure about a ball game. “What if I trip?” she whispered. “What if I miss?”

Ms. Dot smiled. “Courage means trying, even when you feel a little scared,” she said. “Your team will help.”

Gigi took a slow breath. She felt the cool air in her nose and the warm air out. “I can try,” she said, a tiny bit braver.

First, they worked on the poster. The paper was as big as a door. Swoosh, swoosh, went the brushes. Gigi painted tall yellow stars. Bella dotted pink circles. Timo made bold orange stripes. Pio dabbed blue clouds and chirped, “Pretty! Pretty!”

The paint smelled like lemons and rain. Their hands went swish and pat. Their eyes shined. Gigi felt calm and happy.

On the top, Gigi wrote, “Go Team!” very slowly. “This is our team poster,” she said. “It shows that we try together.”

Next, Ms. Dot rolled out a soft mat. She set up two small goals made of foam. The ball was squishy and red. “Roll-and-Goal,” she said, “is a gentle sport. We roll, we pass, we cheer. We do not push. We help.”

Teams were made. Gigi stood with Bella and Pio. Timo was on the other team with two little bears. Gigi’s knees felt wiggly like jelly. Her spots felt warm.

Bella noticed. “Gigi,” she said, “you can stand near me. I will pass to you.”

Pio added, “We share. We care. We’re a team!”

Ms. Dot blew a soft whistle. “Peep!”

Timo rolled the ball first. It went whoosh across the mat. Pio blocked it gently with a flap and sent it to Bella. Bella gave it a tiny push to Gigi.

The ball bumped Gigi’s hoof. Gigi froze. “Oh,” she gasped. The ball rolled away.

“It’s okay!” said Bella. “Try again.”

Gigi felt her belly flutter. She took another slow breath. In…and out. She lifted her hoof and nudged the ball. Tap. It moved. Tap. It rolled. Gigi’s eyes grew wide.

“You did it!” Pio cheered.

The ball came back fast. Timo rolled it with a spin. It zipped by Gigi and went into their goal. “Goal!” shouted Timo.

Gigi’s heart sank a little. “I missed,” she said. Her ears drooped.

Ms. Dot called, “New try! Remember, courage is trying again.”

Gigi stood taller. She felt her long neck reach up, up. “I will try again,” she whispered.

This time, the ball came slowly. Bella guided it with a gentle bump. “Gigi, ready?” she asked.

Gigi nodded. She watched the red ball. She moved her hoof. Tap, tap, tap. She passed to Pio. Pio squawked happily and rolled it back. Together, they made a little triangle. Tap. Tap. Tap. It sounded like a soft drum.

“Teamwork!” shouted Ms. Dot.

Gigi felt warm and strong. Her fear felt smaller, like a tiny pebble in a big pond. “I can do this,” she said.

The ball came again. Gigi lined up her hoof. She aimed for the foamy goal. She took a brave breath. “For our team,” she said. Tap!

The ball rolled straight and smooth. It spun like a red marble. It kissed the foam and slipped in.

“Goal!” cried Bella.
“Goal!” chirped Pio.
Gigi’s heart leaped. She giggled. “I did it! We did it!”

Timo clapped his paws. “Great job, Gigi!”

They kept playing. Sometimes they scored. Sometimes they missed. Each time, they tried again. They passed. They shared. They cheered for each other. The room filled with happy sounds—rolls and taps, giggles and claps, and the soft peep of Ms. Dot’s whistle.

When the game was done, Ms. Dot gathered them by the big poster. The paint was dry and bright. “What did we learn today?” she asked.

Gigi lifted her head. “Courage means I try, even when I feel a little scared,” she said. “And sports are more fun with teamwork.”

Bella added, “We help each other.”
Pio chirped, “We cheer!”
Timo smiled. “We try again.”

Ms. Dot nodded. “Yes. Courage, sports, and teamwork go together. You were all brave. You were all kind.”

Gigi looked at the poster. Go Team! Her chest felt light and proud. She patted the squishy ball and whispered, “Thank you, courage.”

Everyone gave a gentle high-five. Slap, slap, slap. The art room smelled like crayons and victory.

Gigi smiled. “Today, I was brave,” she said. “Tomorrow, I can be brave again.”

The End.
