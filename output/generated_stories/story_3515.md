# Tapping Time with Benny Bear

Benny was a friendly bear cub with soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes. Today, Benny walked into the musical practice room. The room smelled like fresh wood and warm tea. Sunlight made the drums shine. Little bells jingled on a shelf. A big clock went tick-tock on the wall.

Benny smiled. “Hello, room!” he giggled. “I have a busy day!” He looked at his paper schedule. He had written it with his thick, wobbly pencil. The paper said:
- 1. Warm-up song
- 2. Drum practice
- 3. Help friends
- 4. Write thank-you notes
- 5. Clean up

Benny clapped. “I can do it if I use time well,” he said. He set the round, red timer. “Ten minutes for warm-up!”

He hummed, “La, la, la,” and shook a tiny rattle. The rattle went shhh-shhh. His paws felt warm. His ears felt happy. Tick-tock, went the clock.

A friend peeked in. It was Mei, a panda cub with soft black-and-white fur. “Hi, Benny,” said Mei. “My drum sticks rolled away. Can you help?”

Benny looked at the timer. Seven minutes left. He took a deep breath. “Yes, I can help for two minutes,” he said. “Then I must come back.” He knelt and looked under the piano. “I see a stick!” he said. “It is hiding like a little mouse.” Mei giggled. “Thank you!” Benny smiled. “You’re welcome. I will come back later if you need more.”

Benny returned and finished his warm-up. The timer dinged. “All done!” he said. “Next is drum practice. Fifteen minutes.” He set the timer again.

Tap-tap, went the drum. Tap-TAP! Benny tried soft taps. He tried big taps. He listened to the deep boom. His paws tingled. The room hummed.

The door opened. Amir, a lion cub with a fluffy gold mane, looked shy. “Benny, I spilled my shaker seeds,” Amir said. “They are everywhere.”

Benny peeked at the timer. Ten minutes left. He nodded. “I can help for three minutes,” he said. “Let’s scoop fast.” They scooped tiny seeds that felt like sand. “Thank you,” said Amir. “Shukran,” he added in a warm voice. “Shukran means thank you in Arabic.” Benny smiled. “Shukran!” Amir beamed. “Good time plan, Benny.”

Benny finished his drum practice. The timer dinged again. “Next is help friends,” he read. He set the timer for ten minutes. “Now I can help more.”

He found Mei again. “Do you still need help?” he asked. Mei nodded. “Can you hold the music?” Benny held the paper. The paper felt smooth, like a leaf. They played a gentle beat together. “Xie xie,” said Mei. “That means thank you in Chinese.” Benny repeated, “Xie xie!” They both laughed.

Then Benny helped Amir shake the shaker in a steady way. Shake-shake, pause. Shake-shake, pause. “Great rhythm,” said Amir. The timer dinged. “Help time is done,” Benny said kindly. “Now it is writing time.”

Benny set the timer for ten minutes. He took out bright markers that smelled a little sweet. He wrote thank-you notes on small cards. “Dear Mei,” he wrote slowly, saying each word. “Thank you for playing with me. Love, Benny.” He drew a smiling drum. He wrote to Amir, too. “Dear Amir, thank you for teaching me ‘shukran.’ Love, Benny.” He drew a tiny shaker with seeds.

Just then, the big clock went tick-tock faster in Benny’s head. He gasped. “Oh no! I forgot clean-up time yesterday,” he said softly. “I left a bell on the floor.” He felt sad. He went to the bell and picked it up. “I am sorry,” he said to Mei and Amir. “I forgot to clean up yesterday.”

Mei patted his paw. “It’s okay,” she said. Amir nodded. “We forgive you,” he said gently. “You remembered today.” Benny felt warm inside. “Thank you for forgiving me,” he said. “I will use my time plan better.”

He set the timer for five minutes to clean. He made a clean-up song. “Clean, clean, one-two-three,” he sang. “Put away, nice and free!” Mei and Amir sang too. They stacked drums. They lined up shakers. They folded music papers that rustled like soft rain. The room looked neat. The air felt calm.

Benny checked his paper. “I did warm-up, drum practice, help friends, writing, and clean up,” he said. He drew a little star next to each one. Star, star, star, star, star! He smiled. “My plan helped me do everything.”

Ms. Lark, the music teacher, came in. She wore a colorful scarf that swished. “I heard beautiful sounds,” she said. “And I see a tidy room. Great time management, Benny. You helped others, you wrote kind notes, and you finished your list.”

Benny’s cheeks felt rosy. “Thank you,” he said. He handed out the notes. “Xie xie,” said Mei. “Shukran,” said Amir. “Gracias,” added Ms. Lark with a smile. “That means thank you in Spanish.”

Benny listened to the words from different places. They sounded like music too. He hugged his friends. “When I make a plan,” he said, “I can help, I can write, and I can play. If I forget, I can say sorry and try again.”

They all nodded. The clock went tick-tock, soft and steady. Benny packed his pencil and markers. He waved. “See you next time!” The room glowed in the warm light, neat and happy.

The End.
