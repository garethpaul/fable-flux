# Pippa the Pantry Parrot

In a cozy kitchen pantry, where it smelled like warm bread and sweet cinnamon, lived a creative parrot named Pippa. The pantry was full of jars and boxes. There were jars of beans that clicked, boxes of pasta that rustled, and baskets of fruit that smelled bright and juicy.

Pippa loved the pantry. She loved to look, to listen, and to learn. She had bright green feathers and a red beak. Her eyes were curious and kind.

“Squawk! I love to know,” Pippa would say. “I love to help.”

One rainy morning, Pippa heard a soft sniffle. “Oh dear,” she said. “Who is sad?”

She hopped along a shelf and found her friends. Mousy the mouse sat by a fallen bag of rice. Bunny the rabbit tapped her foot, and Tilly the tiny turtle blinked slowly.

“What is wrong?” Pippa asked, tilting her head.

“We want to make soup,” said Bunny. “Warm, yummy soup to share.”

“We have a big pot,” said Mousy. “But we don’t know what we need. We don’t know how.”

Tilly sighed. “I do not know which jar is what. Everything looks like tiny pebbles to me.”

Pippa felt a little flutter in her belly. “I can help,” she said softly. “I know things. I have watched and learned.”

Bunny’s ears perked up. “You know how to make soup?”

“I know where things live,” said Pippa. “And I know how to ask good questions.”

Pippa spread her wings and looked around. She listened to the rustle of boxes and the soft pat of rain on the window. She smelled garlic and onions. She spotted the vegetable shelf.

“First, we need to understand what soup needs,” Pippa said. “Soup needs water. Soup needs flavor. Soup needs soft and warm.”

She hopped to a shelf. “Onions and carrots make good smell,” Pippa explained. “Beans make soup strong. Rice makes soup cozy.” She pointed with her bright red beak.

“But I can’t tell which jar is rice,” said Tilly.

Pippa nodded. “Let’s use our senses and our words. Let’s learn together.”

She tapped a big glass jar. It made a light ping sound. “This is rice,” Pippa said. “It is small and white. It feels smooth.” She rolled a few grains into Tilly’s little paw. “Feel that?”

Tilly smiled. “Oh! Smooth.”

“And this,” Pippa said, tapping another jar that made a click-click sound, “is beans. It is round and firm.”

Mousy sniffed. “What about flavor?”

Pippa flapped once. “Onion and garlic help. They make good smell. They make happy bellies.” She gently nudged an onion. It was smooth and papery. “Listen,” she whispered, and rubbed the onion skin. It made a soft crinkle sound.

Bunny’s nose twitched. “I smell yummy already!”

They carried the food to the little stove by the pantry door. Pippa looked at the pot. “Water first,” she said. “Then onion. Then carrots. Then rice. Beans can go in after.”

Mousy frowned. “What if we do it wrong?”

Pippa smiled kindly. “We can learn as we go,” she said. “That is what knowing is. We try. We watch. We ask.”

They all got to work. Bunny carefully chopped carrots—chop, chop, chop. Mousy measured rice with tiny scoops—scoop, tip, plink. Tilly washed beans in a bowl—swish, swish. Pippa read the little labels on the shelf and said the words out loud. “C-a-r-r-o-t-s. Carrots. R-i-c-e. Rice.”

The pot began to bubble. Blub-blub! Steam rose up and smelled warm and cozy. Pippa stirred with a wooden spoon. “We share jobs,” she said. “Sharing helps us know more.”

As the soup cooked, the rain slowed to a soft patter. Bunny tasted a spoonful and made a face. “It is not very salty.”

Pippa nodded. “Good noticing. We learned something new. A pinch of salt.” She sprinkled a little. “Now try again.”

Bunny tasted and smiled. “Much better!”

Just then, Hedgehog peeked into the pantry. “Hello. I smell soup,” he said shyly. “May I… may I join?”

Mousy and Bunny looked at Pippa. Tilly blinked. Pippa’s eyes were warm. “Yes,” she said. “We share our soup and our knowing.”

Hedgehog shuffled in. “I don’t know much about cooking,” he said.

“That’s okay,” Pippa said. “You can learn with us. Will you hold the spoon so it doesn’t fall?” Hedgehog held the spoon carefully. He looked proud.

Soon the soup was ready. They sat on a soft rug. The pot made tiny puffs of steam. The soup smelled like onions and sunshine. Everyone had a warm bowl.

“To soup!” Bunny cheered.

“To learning!” said Mousy.

“To friends,” Tilly whispered.

Pippa tasted her soup. It was cozy and kind. She felt happy.

Hedgehog looked around. “Thank you for sharing,” he said. “I learned how rice feels. I learned about salt. I learned that asking is smart.”

Pippa fluffed her feathers. “Knowledge is for sharing,” she said. “When we share what we know, we help everyone. When we listen and try, we understand more.”

They finished their bowls. The rain stopped. The pantry felt bright and safe.

Pippa cleaned the spoon and put the jars back. She made little picture labels with her beak and a crayon so Tilly could tell the jars apart. A rice picture for rice. A bean picture for beans.

“Now we can all learn,” Pippa said. “Now we can all help.”

Everyone smiled. Their bellies were warm, and their hearts were full.

And the pantry lived in happy, helpful peace.

The End.
