# Paws That Wiggle in the Attic

The brave little lion cub, named Lio, padded up the creaky wooden stairs. Step, step, step! The quiet attic smelled like old books and soft dust. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. Lio’s whiskers twitched. His paws tingled. He wanted to move!

“I hear the attic whispers,” Lio giggled. “It says, ‘Wiggle, wiggle!’”

A gentle breeze slipped through a tiny window and kissed Lio’s nose. He sneezed, “Achoo!” and laughed. “Let’s play!”

In the corner, a tall mirror stood like a shiny door. Lio looked at his reflection. His little mane was poofy. His eyes were bright. He felt brave and strong. “Hello, Lio,” he said to the mirror. “Want to move with me?”

He found a cardboard box of dress-up clothes. Feathers, scarves, and a little bell. Ting-a-ling! He put a red scarf around his neck. It felt soft and warm. He slipped the bell on his paw. Ting-a-ling! “I am a dancing lion,” Lio said.

Lio stretched tall like a tree. He reached his paws up high. “Up, up!” he cheered. Then he bent down low and touched his toes. “Down, down!” His body felt awake and happy.

He took tiny steps. Tip-tap, tip-tap. He took big steps. STOMP, STOMP! He hopped like a bunny. Hop, hop, hop! He swished the scarf. Swish, swish. The bell sang. Ting-a-ling!

“Listen, feet,” Lio whispered. “We can make music.”

He made a little game. “Freeze!” he told himself. He stood still like a statue. The attic was very quiet. He could hear his heart, thump-thump. “Go!” he said, and twirled in a slow circle. Around and around, the scarf painted red circles in the sunbeams. Lio smiled. “Moving feels good. Moving helps me think.”

On a shelf, he saw a small drum. Tap, tap. The sound was soft. “Hello, drum,” Lio said. He tapped a pattern with his paw. Tap-tap, pause. Tap-tap, pause. His tail swayed to the beat. He marched in place. Left paw, right paw. March, march! “My body is a band,” he giggled.

Lio wanted to try a big leap. He crouched down. He wiggled. He sprang—whoosh!—and his bell rang loud. Ting-a-LING! His paw bumped a stack of paper birds. The birds fluttered down in a big whooosh. They scattered all over the floor.

Lio froze. “Oh no,” he whispered. “I knocked them down.” His ears drooped. He felt a warm, twisty feeling in his tummy.

From the stairs, a soft voice came. “Lio?” It was Aunt Mara, who kept the attic neat and cozy. She stepped into the golden light. Her feet made gentle shh, shh sounds on the floor.

Lio lowered his head. “I am sorry,” he said softly. “I was leaping. I was not careful. I made a mess.”

Aunt Mara knelt beside him. Her smile was kind. “Thank you for telling me,” she said. “It was an accident. I forgive you.” She stroked his little mane. “Your body was learning and playing. Movement helps you grow.”

Lio’s tummy felt lighter. “Can I help fix it?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Aunt Mara. “Let’s turn this into art.” She handed him a roll of tape and a blue crayon. “We can make a new flock of birds. We can draw, we can dance, and we can tidy together.”

Lio’s eyes sparkled. “We can move and make!”

They set a simple rhythm. Tap the drum. Pick up a bird. Step to the side. Place the bird. Clap. They moved together like a gentle wave. Tap-pick-step-place-clap! Lio hummed. Aunt Mara softly sang, “Pick and place, pick and place, moving bodies, happy space.”

Lio added a wiggle. Wiggle-wiggle! He made the scarf swoop like a bird. “Look, Aunt Mara! I am a flying lion!” He fluttered his paws and glided across the attic. Swish, swish, swish.

Aunt Mara laughed. “Beautiful! Your body tells a story. That is creative expression.”

They stuck the paper birds on a string. The birds hung in the window and danced in the breeze. The attic shimmered with flapping shadows. The bell on Lio’s paw chimed softly. Ting-a-ling.

“Let’s make a movement picture,” Lio said. He drew a big sun with the blue crayon. He drew his paws jumping. He drew arrows that showed up, down, around. “This is my movement map,” he explained. “Stretch, hop, march, freeze, twirl, breathe.”

Aunt Mara nodded. “That is a smart plan. Moving with care keeps things safe.”

Lio took a deep breath in—sniff—and a slow breath out—whooo. He placed the drum back on the shelf. He folded the scarf. He hugged Aunt Mara. “Thank you for forgiving me.”

“You are welcome,” she said. “You were honest, you helped, and you made art. That is brave.”

Lio looked at the birds, the sun picture, and the tidy floor. His heart felt big. “Today I learned,” he said. “My body loves to move. When I make a mistake, I can say sorry and fix it. I can use my moves to make music and art.”

Aunt Mara smiled. “Yes, little lion. Move with joy. Move with care. And forgive yourself and others.”

Lio padded to the stairs. Step, step, step. The attic was still quiet, but now it hummed with happy air. He rang his little bell one last time. Ting-a-ling!

“See you tomorrow, attic,” he whispered. “I will bring my brave paws.”

The End.
