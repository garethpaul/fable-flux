# Rizzo’s Quiet-Time Song Plan

Rizzo the raccoon loved music. His striped tail tapped like a drum. His paws strummed rubber bands. His whiskers twitched whenever he heard a beat. Rizzo lived in a cozy home with a small office where Mama worked at a desk with a big, bright screen.

One sunny morning, the home office smelled like warm tea. The window let in a soft breeze that whispered, “Whoosh.” Papers sat in neat stacks. A tiny clock on the wall went tick-tick-tick.

Rizzo wiggled in with his little guitar. “I made a new song!” he said. “It goes strum, strum, hum-hum, ta-da!”

Mama smiled. “I can’t wait to hear it, Rizzo. Right now I have work time. I need quiet until the little hand on the clock points to the 3.”

Rizzo looked at the clock. The little hand pointed to the 2. “But I feel the music in my toes!” he said, tapping his feet. Tap-tap-tap.

“I understand,” Mama said kindly. She put her hand on her heart. “Let’s use peaceful words. I need quiet to think. You want to play music. How can we both feel okay?”

Rizzo’s ears drooped. “I don’t know,” he whispered.

Mama nodded. “Let’s make a plan. We can use a time plan to help.”

She took out sticky notes in bright colors. They smelled like new paper. She drew three simple pictures: a clock, a guitar, and a heart.

“Here is our plan,” Mama said. “Quiet Time until the little hand is on the 3.” She pointed to the clock picture. “Then Music Time for Rizzo.” She pointed to the guitar. “And we check feelings.” She pointed to the heart.

Rizzo blinked. “A time plan?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “A time plan helps us do things at the right time. It is kind to others and kind to you. It helps you wait and still feel happy.”

Rizzo took a slow breath. He smelled the sweet tea and the lemony wipes on the desk. “Okay,” he said softly. “I can wait.”

To help himself wait, Rizzo made a Quiet Time rhythm with his paws. Pitter-pat on his knees. Whisper-hum under his breath. He looked at the clock. Tick-tick-tick.

A minute felt big. Rizzo’s tail wiggled. He wanted to strum. He squeezed his guitar gently. “I feel wiggly,” he whispered.

He walked to Mama and used peaceful words. “Mama, my body wants to play,” he said. “How can I wait?”

Mama smiled and nodded. “Thank you for using kind words, Rizzo. Let’s find calm waiting tools.” She opened a drawer. The drawer smelled like wooden pencils. She gave Rizzo a soft scarf and a tiny sand timer.

“Shake the scarf. Watch the sand,” she said. “When the sand is done, check the clock.”

Rizzo shook the scarf. Swish-swish. He watched golden sand slide down. Shhhhhh. He felt calmer. He checked the clock. The little hand was almost on the 3.

“Almost time,” he whispered. He hugged his guitar and imagined the happy notes waiting inside, like bubbles.

Then the little hand reached the 3. Ding! The tiny clock chimed. Mama clapped softly. “It is Music Time!”

Rizzo grinned. His eyes sparkled. “I kept the time plan!” he said.

“You did,” Mama said. “Thank you for being kind and using quiet waiting.”

Rizzo hopped once, twice. He set the sand timer for one song. He strummed gently. Strum, strum. He sang in a light voice, “Wait, then play, we share the day.” His tail kept a soft beat on the rug. Thump, thump, thump.

Mama listened and smiled. “Your song is lovely,” she said. “Thank you for playing softly in the home office.”

Rizzo finished the last note—ta-da—like a tiny bell. He set his guitar down. “I have an idea,” he said. “Can we make a music schedule for every day?”

Mama’s eyes lit up. “Yes! Let’s make a daily plan.”

Together they drew a simple chart with pictures:
- Sun for morning quiet
- Drum for music time
- Book for reading time
- Heart for check-in time

They taped it near the clock. The tape made a sticky rip sound. Rizzo patted the paper flat. “I like our plan,” he said.

Later, Rizzo heard Mama’s voice get small. “I have a tricky task,” she said, rubbing her forehead.

Rizzo used peaceful communication. He walked over slowly. “Mama, do you need quiet now?” he asked.

Mama nodded. “Yes, please.”

Rizzo looked at the chart. “It is quiet time now,” he said. He placed his guitar in its basket and wrapped the soft scarf around his shoulders. He tapped his knees in a tiny rhythm and hummed a whisper-hum. Mama worked. Rizzo waited.

When the clock’s hand moved again, it was music time. Rizzo played a thank-you song: “We talk and plan, we wait and share, we show kind hearts because we care.”

Mama clapped. “Rizzo, you managed your time so well,” she said. “You were kind to me and kind to yourself.”

Rizzo felt warm and proud. He learned that time plans help everyone feel calm. He learned that kind words and waiting tools make big feelings smaller. He learned that talking peacefully makes home a happy place.

That night, Rizzo tucked his guitar in and whispered, “Tomorrow I will check the clock, check my heart, and follow our plan.”

The End.
