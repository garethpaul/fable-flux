# Tilly Turtle’s Shed Song

The rain made soft patter-patter sounds on the garden shed roof. Inside, it smelled like fresh soil and warm wood. Tilly Turtle peeked out from her shell. She was a shy turtle with gentle eyes and a tiny green shell that felt safe and snug.

Her friends were visiting the shed today. Buzz the Bee hummed near a flowerpot. Pip the Mouse wiggled his whiskers by a stack of seed packets. Dot the Ladybug sat on a shiny trowel. On the shelf were old jars, a watering can, and a row of pots that made a clinky sound when tapped.

Pip squeaked, “Let’s make music while it rains!”

Buzz buzzed, “Yes! Music time!”

Tilly pulled her head in a little. Music time sounded big and loud. What if she played wrong? What if everyone looked at her?

Dot fluttered her tiny wings. “We can take turns,” she said. “We listen first, and then we play.”

Tilly took a slow breath in and out. In, out. The air smelled like leaves. The rain kept patter-patter on the roof.

Pip found two little sticks. “Tap, tap!” he tried on a pot. It sounded like tick-tick-tick.

Buzz tapped the watering can with his tiny feet. “Ting-ting!” he giggled.

Dot rubbed a jar with a leaf. “Whoosh,” she said softly. “It sounds like wind!”

Tilly watched with wide eyes. Her heart went thump-thump. She wanted to try, but she felt very small. She tucked her chin into her shell.

Dot noticed and floated down beside Tilly. “It’s okay to feel shy,” Dot said kindly. “We can listen to our friends. Then, when it’s your turn, we will listen to you.”

Pip nodded. “I will wait my turn. I’ll listen with my big mouse ears.”

Buzz landed on Tilly’s shell. “I can hum a slow song. That can help us breathe and feel calm.”

“Okay,” whispered Tilly. She liked calm.

Buzz hummed a soft bzz-bzz-bzz. Pip counted, “One, two, three,” in a tiny voice. Dot held up a leaf like a little green flag.

“We’ll go in a circle,” Dot said. “Pip plays first, then Buzz, then me, then Tilly.”

Pip tapped his sticks on the pot. Tick-tick. He stopped and smiled. “Your turn, Buzz,” he said.

Buzz listened, waited, and then went, “Ting-ting,” on the watering can. He stopped and smiled at Dot. “Your turn!”

Dot listened, then rubbed her leaf on the jar. Whoosh. She stopped and looked at Tilly with warm eyes. “Now it’s your turn, Tilly.”

Tilly could feel her heart race. Her little feet wiggled on the wooden floor. “What if I make a silly sound?” she whispered.

Dot said, “All sounds are welcome.”

Pip added, “We will listen to you.”

Buzz buzzed softly, “You are brave, even when you feel shy.”

Tilly took another slow breath. In. Out. She reached for a small clay pot. It felt cool and smooth. She tapped it with her tiny turtle toe. Plok.

Everyone was quiet, listening.

Plok.

Tilly tried a softer tap. plok.

She tried a louder tap. PLOK!

Pip clapped his paws. “I like your plok!”

Tilly’s cheeks felt warm, but in a good way. She smiled a tiny smile. “Thank you,” she said.

Dot waved her leaf. “Let’s make a rain song. We will take turns. We will listen. We will keep the beat.”

They made a pattern: tick-tick (Pip), ting-ting (Buzz), whoosh (Dot), plok (Tilly). The rain on the roof went patter-patter, and the shed filled with happy sounds.

After a while, Buzz said, “Let’s try Tilly as the leader.”

Tilly blinked. “Me? The leader?”

“Only if you want to,” Dot said gently. “You can choose the sound and the speed.”

Tilly felt the smooth pot under her toe. She looked at her friends’ bright faces. She listened to the kind quiet. She stood a little taller. “I can try,” she said.

She tapped slow. Plok… plok… plok.

Pip listened carefully and matched: tick… tick… tick.

Buzz followed: ting… ting… ting.

Dot added the soft wind: whoosh… whoosh… whoosh.

The slow song felt like deep breathing. Tilly’s shell didn’t need to hide. She felt calm and proud.

“Now a little faster,” Tilly said with a giggle. Plok-plok-plok!

Tick-tick-tick! Ting-ting-ting! Whoosh-whoosh-whoosh!

They all laughed. The shed seemed cozy and bright, even with the rain.

When the song ended, Tilly said, “Thank you for listening to me.”

Pip bowed. “Thank you for leading us.”

Buzz buzzed, “You were brave and kind.”

Dot smiled. “You took your turn, and you gave turns too.”

Tilly tucked her head out, all the way. “I felt shy,” she said, “but I tried. Listening helped. Taking turns helped. Now I feel strong inside.”

The rain slowed to a soft drip-drop. The shed smelled like sunshine after rain. Tilly’s heart felt steady and warm.

She looked at the pot, the jar, and the watering can. “Tomorrow,” she said, “we can make a new song. I can lead sometimes, and I can listen too.”

Her friends cheered, “Yes!”

They put the sticks away, stacked the pots carefully, and took one last slow breath together. In. Out. Calm and happy.

Tilly smiled and gave the pot one gentle, proud plok.

The End.
