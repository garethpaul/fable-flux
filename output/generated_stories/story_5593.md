# Ruby Rabbit's Big Helper Day

Ruby Rabbit hopped into Grandma's guest room with her fluffy white tail wiggling. She loved visiting Grandma's cozy house! But when she looked around, her brown eyes grew wide.

"Oh my!" Ruby gasped. The guest room was very messy. Empty boxes were scattered everywhere. Crumpled papers covered the floor like fallen leaves. Old magazines sat in wobbly towers.

Just then, Grandma walked in carrying a heavy trash bag. "Phew!" Grandma sighed, wiping her forehead. "I have so much cleaning to do before my friend visits tomorrow."

Ruby's ears perked up. She wanted to help, but there was a problem. Her little brother Benny Bunny was already in the room, and he was making things worse! Benny was jumping on the boxes, making loud THUMP sounds.

"Benny, stop!" Ruby called out. "You're making more mess!"

"I'm helping!" Benny squeaked, kicking a paper ball across the room.

Ruby felt frustrated. Her whiskers twitched. How could she help Grandma when Benny was being so silly?

Then Ruby had an idea. Instead of getting angry, she hopped over to Benny with a gentle smile.

"Benny," Ruby said softly, "I can see you want to help too. But jumping isn't the kind of help Grandma needs. Want to be my helper partner instead?"

Benny's ears drooped. "But jumping is fun," he whispered.

Ruby nodded. "Jumping IS fun! But we can make helping fun too. Look at all these papers and boxes. We can sort them into different piles!"

She showed Benny three colorful bins Grandma had brought. "This blue bin is for papers we can recycle. The green bin is for boxes we can reuse. And the red bin is for real trash."

Benny's eyes lit up. "Like a sorting game?"

"Exactly!" Ruby clapped her paws together. "And the best part is, we're helping our Earth by recycling!"

The two rabbits got to work. Ruby picked up magazines and newspapers. "These go in the blue recycling bin," she explained, dropping them in with a satisfying PLOP sound.

Benny carefully carried small boxes. "These can be used again!" he said proudly, placing them in the green bin.

Together, they worked as a team. Ruby would find items, and Benny would decide which bin they belonged in. Soon, they were both giggling and having fun.

"You're such a good helper, Benny!" Ruby said, giving her little brother a warm hug.

Grandma watched with a big smile. "Look at my wonderful helper rabbits! You two make such a great team when you work together."

As they finished cleaning, the guest room sparkled. The floor was clear, the bins were full of sorted items, and everything smelled fresh and clean.

"Ruby," Grandma said, "thank you for finding a way to include Benny. That was very kind and smart."

Ruby's chest puffed up with pride. "When we work together and talk nicely to each other, we can solve any problem!"

Benny nodded eagerly. "And recycling helps our planet stay healthy!"

That evening, as they shared carrot cake, Ruby felt happy and proud. She had learned that when there's a disagreement, talking kindly and finding ways to work together makes everything better.

"Tomorrow, let's help Grandma in the garden!" Ruby suggested.

"Yes!" Benny cheered. "We're the best helper team ever!"

And they certainly were.

The End.
