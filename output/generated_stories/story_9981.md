# Kora the Calm Koala on the Rooftop Garden

Kora was a calm koala with soft gray fur and a sweet, sleepy smile. She lived on the rooftop of a tall building. The rooftop had a little garden. There were green leaves, bright flowers, and tiny tomatoes that smelled sunny and warm. The wind up high felt cool on Kora’s nose. The city below hummed softly, like a purring cat.

Every morning, Kora checked the garden. She watered the plants with a little blue watering can. It made a tinkly sound, like rain in a small song. She hummed, “Sip, sip, sip,” as the leaves drank.

One day, Kora looked at the tallest plant. It was a big sunflower. Its head was droopy and tired. The petals looked shy. The sunflower leaned so far, it touched the edge of the roof fence.

“Oh dear,” said Kora, patting the flower. “You look sleepy and thirsty. Are you okay?”

A gentle voice floated on the wind. “I’m trying to grow, but I feel wobbly,” the sunflower whispered.

Kora blinked. “I hear you,” she said softly. “I can help.”

Just then, friends came up the stairs to visit the rooftop garden. There was Pip the pigeon, with shiny feathers that caught the sun. There was Tiki the tiny turtle, who moved slowly but smiled fast. There was Bee-Bee the little bee, buzzing warm and happy.

“Hello, Kora!” called Pip. “Anything fun today?”

Kora nodded. “Our sunflower needs help. It is wobbly,” she said in her calm voice. “Let’s think.”

Bee-Bee buzzed in a circle. “Buzz-buzz! I bring the news! The sunflower is big and brave. But the wind is strong up here.”

Tiki touched the soil with his small foot. “Hmm. The dirt is dry near the stem,” he said.

Kora sat on a soft mat and took a slow breath. “We can use gentle thinking,” she said. “We will look, listen, and plan.”

She pointed with her paw. “First, we check the soil. Then, we give water. Then, we make the stem safe.”

Pip puffed up. “I can bring a string from the stair rail,” he said. “I saw a spare one by the door.”

Bee-Bee buzzed again. “I can look for a stick. A strong stick! Not poky, but kind.”

Tiki nodded. “I can pat the dirt. Pat, pat, pat. Make it cozy.”

Kora smiled at her friends. “Thank you. We all help. This is our garden,” she said.

They got to work. Kora watered slowly. The water smelled fresh. The soil turned dark and cool. Tiki patted the dirt around the sunflower’s base. “Pat, pat, pat,” he said, making a snug bed for the roots. Bee-Bee zipped over a planter and found a smooth bamboo stick. Pip returned with a soft, long string that felt like a cloud.

Kora spoke to the sunflower. “We will stand you up, gently,” she said. “Is that okay?”

“Yes,” whispered the sunflower. “Thank you. I want to face the sun.”

Together, they lifted the sunflower, slow and careful. The petals shook like little yellow bells. Kora held the stem. Pip wrapped the soft string around the stem and the bamboo stick. Not too tight, not too loose. Tiki checked the knot with a tiny tap. Bee-Bee buzzed near Kora’s ear. “It looks good! It looks safe!”

A warm breeze brushed their faces. The sunflower lifted its head, higher and higher. The center looked golden and happy. The petals brightened, like a smile.

“You did it,” Kora said softly. Her heart felt warm.

Pip flapped his wings. “Teamwork!”

Tiki beamed. “We helped our friend.”

Bee-Bee danced in the air. “Buzz-buzz hooray!”

Then they heard a small sniffle near the door. Mia, a little girl from the building, peeked out. She held a tiny pot with a plant. The plant had curly leaves and small white buds.

“Hi,” Mia whispered. “I wanted to bring my plant to the garden. But... my plant looks different. It’s not a flower like the others. I’m afraid it won’t fit.”

Kora smiled and waved her paw. “All plants are welcome here,” she said. “What is your plant’s name?”

Mia stroked a leaf. “This is Mint. She smells minty.”

Kora leaned in and sniffed. Cool, sweet air tickled her nose. “Mmm,” she said. “Mint can make the air fresh. Mint can make tea. Mint is special.”

Pip nodded. “Different is good.”

Tiki added, “Our garden is big in heart.”

Bee-Bee buzzed, “I love mint! I can visit the flowers and the mint.”

Kora patted the ground near the tomatoes. “Let’s make a spot,” she said. Together, they dug a little hole. The soil felt crumbly and rich. Mia placed Mint inside. They patted the dirt, pat, pat, pat. Kora gave Mint a sip from the blue watering can.

Mia smiled. “Thank you for accepting Mint.”

Kora touched Mia’s hand with her soft paw. “In our garden, we accept all friends,” she said. “Tall, small, leafy, or buzzy. We all belong.”

They all sat quietly and listened. The city hummed. The leaves rustled. The sunflower turned to the sun, steady and proud. Mint sent a cool, friendly smell into the air.

Mia said, “We solved a big problem. We helped the sunflower stand. And we made a space for Mint.”

Kora nodded. “We used careful thinking, kind hearts, and helping hands. When we accept each other, our garden grows strong.”

The friends shared a small picnic. They munched tiny tomato slices. They sipped minty water. The sky turned pink and soft. Everyone felt safe and glad.

Kora looked around the rooftop garden. “Today we learned this,” she said. “When we accept others and work together, we can solve problems. We can help our community. We can make beauty grow.”

The sunflower glowed in the sunset. Mint swayed gently. Pip, Tiki, Bee-Bee, Mia, and Kora smiled together.

The End.
