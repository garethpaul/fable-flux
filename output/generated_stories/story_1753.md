# Jump, Joey, Jump!

The garden shed smelled like fresh soil and lemon soap. Sunlight made little dust sparkles dance in the air. A joyful kangaroo joey named Kipp skipped inside on soft, springy feet. His ears perked up. His tail swished. “Today is a jump day!” he said, wiggling with excitement.

Kipp loved to move. He loved to hop, stretch, and breathe big belly breaths. Exercise made his legs feel strong, his heart feel happy, and his mind feel calm. “Jump, jump, jump!” he hummed.

Inside the shed, there were rakes, watering cans, and a big box of jumprope and hoops. There was also a neat row of little brooms for sweeping. Kipp clapped his tiny paws. “A perfect place for practice!”

But when he hopped closer, Kipp’s smile wobbled. The jumprope box had tipped over. Ropes made a snaky pile on the floor. A hoop was stuck under a wheelbarrow. A soft ball had rolled into a dusty corner.

Kipp’s nose wrinkled. “Oh no. It’s so messy! I can’t jump in this jumble.” His tail drooped.

From outside, Ms. Wren, the neighbor, called, “Hello, Kipp! The garden club kids are coming later to clean the park path. Will you help?”

Kipp wanted to help. Helping the park felt big and important. That was civic responsibility. But first, he wanted his exercise. He stomped a little foot. “I feel frustrated,” Kipp whispered.

He put a paw on his chest and took a deep breath in. The shed smelled like mint leaves hanging to dry. He breathed out slowly. “I can calm down. I can make a plan,” he said. His ears relaxed.

“First, I will stretch,” Kipp told himself. He stretched his legs like long rubber bands. He wiggled his toes. He rolled his shoulders. “Warm-up time keeps my body safe.”

Then Kipp looked at the mess again. “I can organize,” he said. “One thing at a time.” He picked up a hoop and slid it onto a hook with a happy click. He shook the dusty ball and wiped it with a towel. He gathered the ropes and looped them gently. Swish, swish went the little broom as he swept a clear space on the floor.

Kipp smiled. “I did it! Now I can move.”

He set up a tiny exercise path. “First hop, then jump, then balance.” He placed a hoop on the floor and hopped in and out. “Boing, boing!” The floor felt cool under his feet. He jumped rope slowly, the rope whispering, whoosh-whoosh. He balanced on a line of tape, arms wide like airplane wings. “I feel strong. I feel happy,” he laughed.

Just then, a small mouse peeked in. “Hi, Kipp. I’m Mip,” she squeaked shyly. “I heard whoosh-whoosh. It sounded fun.”

Kipp waved. “Hi, Mip! I’m exercising. Do you want to try the hoop hop?”

Mip looked at the hoop. “I’m tiny. I might trip.” Her whiskers trembled.

Kipp nodded kindly. “It’s okay to feel nervous. Let’s go slow. We can try together.” He held the hoop steady and counted, “One, two, three.” Mip hopped in. Her feet made a soft tap. She hopped out. Tap, tap. She giggled. “I did it!”

“You did!” said Kipp. His heart felt warm. Sharing felt good. That was generosity.

A beetle walked by with a crumb on its back. The crumb wobbled. “Oh!” said Kipp. “The floor is cleaner now. It’s safe to carry your snack.” The beetle waved its little legs happily and kept going. Kipp felt proud. Organizing helped everyone.

Ms. Wren called again, “Kipp! The kids are here now. We have gloves and bags. We will pick up litter and sweep the park path.”

Kipp bounced. “That is also exercise!” he told Mip. “We can move our bodies and help our town.”

Mip’s eyes shone. “Let’s go!”

Before they left, Kipp looked around the shed. He put the ropes back in the box just right. He hung up the broom. He said, “A place for each thing, and each thing in its place!” It made him feel calm and ready.

At the park, the sun felt warm on their backs. The path made tiny crunch sounds under their feet. Kipp bent and stretched to pick up a cup. He reached and twisted to grab a paper. He pushed the broom in long, smooth swooshes. “This is good exercise,” he puffed with a smile. “I feel my heart beating. Thump-thump.”

A little boy sighed nearby. “This bag is heavy. I feel frustrated.”

Kipp nodded. “Let’s take a breath together.” They breathed in the sweet smell of grass and flowers. They breathed out slowly. “We can take small steps and rest when we need to,” Kipp said.

The boy smiled and tried again. Everyone worked together. Bags filled up. The path began to sparkle. Birds chirped. “Tweet, tweet! Thank you!”

When they finished, Ms. Wren clapped. “Great teamwork, everyone! The park is clean and safe.”

Kipp felt tall, even for a little joey. He had jumped, stretched, balanced, and helped. He had shared and stayed calm. He had organized the shed and cared for the park.

Mip bumped his paw. “We did it!”

Kipp grinned. “Exercise helps my body. Being generous helps my heart. Staying calm helps my mind. Working together helps our home.”

They hopped back to the shed. Kipp drank cool water. He wiped the little broom and tucked it away. He waved to Mip and the beetle and the birds.

“Jump, jump, jump,” he hummed softly, his tail swishing in a happy rhythm. “Move your body, share your joy, and help your world.”

The End.
