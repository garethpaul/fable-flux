# Dolphin Drums on the Balcony

Flip the playful dolphin peeked over the edge of a sunny balcony garden. The air smelled like mint leaves and wet soil. Tiny wind chimes went ting-ting-ting. A soft breeze ruffled the basil and made the marigolds nod hello.

“Good morning, balcony!” Flip chirped, splashing his tail in a little bucket of water. “I want to build. I want to make music!”

On the balcony, there were pots, wooden blocks, a watering can, and a big cardboard box. Flip’s friends were already there. Pip the sparrow hopped along the railing. “Cheep-cheep! Let’s make a band,” Pip sang.

Mina the kitten stretched in the sun. “Mrrrrow. I want to build a tall tower,” she purred, tapping the blocks.

Flip’s blue eyes twinkled. “Let’s do both!” he said. His voice made the wind chimes dance. “We can build a music stage and play a song.”

Pip fluffed his feathers. “Cheep! I want the drum. I want the drum now.”

Mina patted the cardboard box. “I want to stack blocks here,” she said. “I want the space now.”

Flip felt his tummy flip-flop. He wanted the drum too. He wanted the space too. He wanted everything at once. His tail splashed-splish, and water droplets sparkled like tiny stars.

He took a deep breath. He listened to the wind. Ting-ting-ting.

“Hmm,” said Flip. “We can take turns.”

Pip hopped. “What is taking turns?”

Flip smiled. “Taking turns means we share time. You go, then I go, then Mina goes. Everyone gets a turn.”

Mina’s ears perked up. “That sounds fair,” she purred.

Flip tapped the cardboard box with a wooden spoon. Boom-boom! It sounded like a drum. Pip’s eyes went round. “Cheep! I still want to drum.”

Flip nodded. “Let’s make a Turn Plan,” he said. He drew a happy face in the damp soil with his fin. “First, Pip drums for the count of five. Then I drum for the count of five. Then Mina drums for the count of five. After that, we build. We take turns with the blocks too.”

Pip tilted his head. “Cheep-cheep. I like a plan.”

Mina swished her tail. “Me too.”

Flip clapped his fins. “Ready? Pip first!” He held up a fin. “One, two, three, four, five!”

Pip tapped-tapped-tapped with the spoon. Boom, boom, boom-boom! The sound was deep and happy. Flip counted slowly. “One... two... three... four... five!” He smiled at Pip. “Great drumming!”

Pip passed the spoon to Flip. “My turn is done,” he chirped. “Your turn now.”

Flip took the spoon. His fin felt warm from the sun. He drummed softly, then loudly. Boom... BOOM! The balcony plants shook with joy. Mina giggled. “That tickles my whiskers!” she said.

“One... two... three... four... five!” counted Pip. “Flip’s turn is done.”

Mina took the spoon. Tap-tap-tap! Boom-boom! Her paws moved fast, then slow. “I like the sound,” she purred. Flip counted for her. “One... two... three... four... five!”

“Now we build!” Flip said. He pushed the blocks toward the sunny spot. “We take turns with the big square blocks, and the little triangle blocks too.”

Pip picked up a square. “Cheep. My turn first?” He looked at Flip.

Flip nodded. “Yes, Pip. Put one block down.”

Pip set a square block on the cardboard box. Thunk. “I’m done,” he chirped.

“Now my turn,” said Flip. He put a rectangle on top. Clack.

Mina squinted at the edges. “My turn,” she purred. She placed a triangle like a roof. Tip-tap. It made a tiny shadow on the box.

They kept going. Pip placed, then Flip placed, then Mina placed. The tower grew. It looked like a little castle on the balcony garden. Mint leaves brushed it and made it smell fresh and cool.

Suddenly, a gust of wind swooshed. The wind chimes sang fast. Ting-ting-ting-ting! The top triangle wobbled. “Oh no!” Pip chirped.

Flip’s heart thumped. “Problem!” he said. “We can fix it. We can think.”

Mina touched the wobbly triangle. “It needs a strong base,” she purred.

Flip looked around. He saw the watering can. He saw smooth stones by the herbs. “Let’s try stones,” he said. “We will take turns placing stones to make it steady.”

Pip nodded. “Cheep. I can put one here,” he said, sliding a stone near the bottom. The tower felt firmer.

“My turn,” said Flip. He put a stone on the other side. The cardboard box made a soft scritch sound.

“My turn,” said Mina. She pressed a stone gently. The triangle stopped wobbling.

“We solved the problem together,” Flip said, smiling. “We took turns to fix it.”

“Now, music time on our stage,” Pip cheeped.

They made a simple song. Pip drummed for five. Flip shook the wind chimes gently for five. Mina tapped two small pots for five. The sounds blended—boom, ting, ping-ping! The sun felt warm. The mint smelled bright. The friends felt proud.

At the end, Flip bowed. “Thank you for taking turns,” he said. “Taking turns helped us share, build, and solve.”

Pip fluffed his feathers. “Cheep-cheep! Taking turns made it fun.”

Mina purred. “Taking turns made it fair.”

Flip’s tail splashed one happy splash. He whispered to the balcony garden, “We learned a big lesson today.”

The friends hugged the air around the tower so they would not knock it over. They smiled at each other. The problem was solved. The music was sweet. The building was strong. Everyone had a turn.

The End.
