# Cubby Helps in the Workshop

Cubby was a friendly bear cub with soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes. Today, Cubby padded into Grandpa Bear’s garage workshop. The workshop smelled like warm wood and pine soap. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. Tools hung on the walls: shiny wrenches, red-handled pliers, and a big blue toolbox.

“Good morning, Grandpa!” Cubby said, his nose twitching at the smell of fresh sawdust.

“Good morning, Cubby,” Grandpa Bear rumbled kindly. “I’m fixing our wobbly wagon. Would you like to help?”

Cubby’s ears perked up. “Yes! I want to help!”

Grandpa Bear smiled. “Helping is wonderful. Helping means we use kind hands and careful listening.”

Cubby nodded. “I can do that.”

First, Grandpa Bear showed Cubby a small broom. “We always start by making a safe space,” he said. “We sweep so our paws don’t slip.”

Cubby gripped the broom. Swish, swish! The broom made soft sounds on the floor. Dust danced in the sun. Cubby giggled. “I made the floor sparkle!”

“You did,” said Grandpa Bear. “Thank you for your helpfulness.”

Cubby’s chest felt warm and proud. “What next?”

Grandpa Bear tapped the wagon’s wheel. “We need to tighten this bolt. Can you hand me the small wrench?”

Cubby looked at the tools. There were big wrenches and small ones. He squinted and thought, I can try by myself. He picked up a tiny silver wrench. “Is this the small one?”

“Yes,” said Grandpa Bear. “Great thinking.”

Cubby watched Grandpa turn the wrench. Click, click. The wagon stopped wobbling. “We did it!” Cubby cheered.

“We did it together,” Grandpa Bear said. “Helping makes jobs lighter.”

Cubby noticed his paws felt dusty. He rubbed them on his fur, then paused. “Grandpa, my paws are dirty.”

Grandpa Bear nodded. “Good noticing. Helping also means taking care of ourselves. Clean paws help us hold tools safely.”

They walked to the little sink. The water was cool and splishy-splashy. Cubby rubbed his paws with soap. Bubbles popped like tiny stars. “Pop, pop!”

“Nice washing,” said Grandpa Bear. “That’s self-care.”

Cubby felt fresh. “I’m ready for more!”

Grandpa Bear pointed to a jar of screws. “Can you sort the long ones and the short ones? You can do it by yourself.”

Cubby sat on a small stool. He lined up the screws like little soldiers. “Long, short, long, short,” he sang softly. His ears listened to the quiet hum of the workshop. A bird chirped outside. A breeze carried the smell of sweet oil and wood.

“Done!” Cubby said, showing two tidy rows.

“Excellent independence,” Grandpa Bear said. “You tried first, and you did it.”

Cubby’s tummy rumbled. “Grandpa, I feel hungry.”

“That is your body talking,” Grandpa Bear said. “Let’s take a snack break. Helping works best when we rest and eat.”

They shared apple slices and cool water at a small workbench. Crunch, crunch. The apples were sweet and juicy. Cubby licked a drop from his chin and giggled.

After the snack, Cubby saw a small box of safety goggles. “What are these?”

“Goggles keep our eyes safe,” Grandpa Bear said. He handed Cubby a tiny pair. Cubby put them on. The world looked shiny. “I’m Safety Cub!” Cubby announced.

Grandpa Bear laughed. “Safety Cub is very helpful.”

Cubby spotted a loose handle on the blue toolbox. “Grandpa, I can help fix this!”

Grandpa Bear nodded. “Tell me your plan.”

Cubby thought. “First, I look. Then I ask for the right tool. Then I try carefully.”

“Good plan,” Grandpa Bear said. “What tool do you need?”

Cubby examined the screws on the handle. “A small screwdriver,” he said. He found one with a yellow tip.

“Lefty loosey, righty tighty,” Grandpa Bear reminded.

Cubby turned the screwdriver slowly. Twist, twist. The handle snugged tight. Cubby tested it. “It’s not wiggly now!”

Grandpa Bear clapped softly. “You were helpful and careful. You listened, you tried, and you took care of yourself.”

Cubby felt proud and cozy. He looked around the tidy workshop. The floor was clean. The wagon was steady. The tools were in their places. The air smelled like apples and wood.

“Grandpa,” Cubby said, “I like helping. It makes my heart feel big.”

Grandpa Bear gave Cubby a gentle hug. “Helping is love in action. When we help, we make things better. When we care for ourselves, we can help again and again. And when we try by ourselves, we grow strong.”

Cubby nodded. “Today I learned three things,” he said, holding up his soft paws. “One: Be helpful. Two: Take care of me. Three: Try by myself.”

Grandpa Bear winked. “That sounds just right.”

Cubby put the broom away, washed his paws one more time, and hung up his little goggles. He waved at the wagon and whispered, “See you later, wobbly-no-more.”

As they turned off the workshop light, the room grew quiet and cozy. Cubby’s steps felt bouncy and brave.

“I can’t wait to help again tomorrow,” Cubby said.

“And you will,” Grandpa Bear replied. “One kind job at a time.”

The End.
