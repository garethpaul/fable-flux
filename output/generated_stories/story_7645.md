# Pebble the Penguin’s Colorful Clock

Pebble the penguin waddled into the creative art room with a happy wiggle. “Good morning, paints! Good morning, paper!” Pebble chirped. The room smelled like fresh crayons and glue. Sunlight made the glitter sparkle like tiny stars.

Pebble loved to make art. Today, Pebble had a big plan. “I will paint a giant rainbow fish, make a paper crown, and glue macaroni into a smiley sun!” Pebble clapped flippers. “All before snack time!”

Mrs. Maple, the gentle art teacher, smiled. “That’s a lot of fun, Pebble. Let’s use our time wisely. First, let’s make a plan.”

Pebble tilted a feathery head. “A plan?”

“Yes,” said Mrs. Maple. She pointed to a colorful clock with numbers and big, friendly hands. “We will choose what to do first, second, and third. We will use the clock to help.”

Pebble’s eyes shone. “Okay! First: rainbow fish. Second: paper crown. Third: macaroni sun.”

Mrs. Maple put three small cards on the table. Each had a picture: a fish, a crown, and a sun. “We will follow the cards,” she said. “And remember, have fun and be kind to yourself.”

Pebble dipped a brush into blue paint. Swish, swish! The paper whispered as the brush moved. “I love the cool blue,” Pebble giggled. “It feels like the ocean.” Pebble added red, yellow, and green. The rainbow fish looked very bright and very happy.

Then Pebble frowned. “Oh no! I splashed paint on the fish’s tail.”

Mrs. Maple nodded softly. “That happens. Let’s try something creative.”

Pebble tapped the beak. “Maybe… the splashes can be bubbles!” Pebble dabbed white dots around the tail. Pop, pop, pop! “Now the fish is blowing bubbles!”

“That’s clever,” said Mrs. Maple. “You used creativity to make a change.”

Pebble smiled. “I can turn oops into art.”

The clock ticked. Tick-tock, tick-tock. Mrs. Maple pointed. “It’s time for the paper crown now.”

Pebble looked at the fish. “But I want to paint more.”

“We can come back later,” Mrs. Maple said kindly. “We have time for each part if we follow the plan.”

Pebble took a deep breath. “Okay.” Pebble gently set the fish aside to dry. The paper crown was shiny gold. Pebble folded it, and the paper made a crisp, crunchy sound. “I want stars and stickers!”

A friend, Tessa the turtle, rolled over with a glue stick. “Hi, Pebble. Can I help?”

“Yes, please!” Pebble said. “I have big ideas and small flippers.”

Tessa smiled. “I have slow, careful hands.”

Together, they placed blue stars and silver hearts. Sticky, sticky, stick! A star landed sideways.

Pebble blinked. “It’s crooked.”

Tessa nodded. “It’s okay. I like it that way.”

Pebble looked again. The sideways star looked like it was twinkling. “I accept it,” Pebble said. “It makes the crown special.”

Mrs. Maple clapped softly. “You are being patient and kind. That helps you improve.”

The clock ticked again. Tick-tock. Mrs. Maple whispered, “Last, it’s time for the macaroni sun.”

Pebble scooted to the glue table. A sweet glue smell floated in the air. Pebble squeezed the bottle. Squeeze… plop! “Oops, too much.”

“That’s alright,” said Tessa. “Use a little next time.”

Pebble tried again. Squeeze… dot, dot, dot. Much better. Pebble pressed little noodles in a circle. Tap, tap, tap. Then Pebble placed yellow glitter. The glitter shimmered like warm sand.

Pebble sat back. “We did it! Fish, crown, sun!”

Mrs. Maple showed the picture cards. “You followed your plan. You used the clock. And you used creativity when things changed.”

Pebble looked around the room. Brushes clinked in jars. Paper rustled. Friends chatted and laughed. Pebble felt proud and warm, like the glittery sun.

Just then, Benji the bunny hopped over. “Pebble,” he said softly, “my painting has a big smudge. I feel sad.”

Pebble waddled closer. The smudge was dark and wide. Pebble smiled gently. “Sometimes a smudge can be a new idea. Maybe it can be a shadow, or a cave, or a nighttime sky.”

Benji sniffled. “A nighttime sky?”

Pebble nodded. “Add stars. Make the smudge the sky.”

Benji dotted tiny stars. His eyes grew bright. “I did it!”

Mrs. Maple beamed. “You both showed acceptance and creativity.”

The snack bell rang. Ding-ding! Pebble glanced at the clock. “We finished before snack because we used our plan.”

Tessa placed the crown on Pebble’s head. “You are the Time-Plan King!”

Pebble giggled. “I am the Creative King too!”

Mrs. Maple said, “What did we learn today?”

Pebble held up flippers. “I learned three things. One: Be creative. Turn oops into art. Two: Use time well. Make a plan and follow the clock. Three: Accept yourself and others. Crooked stars can still shine.”

Everyone cheered. Pebble waved the paintbrush like a tiny flag. Colors swirled. Friends smiled. The room felt happy and safe.

Pebble looked at the rainbow fish, the twinkly crown, and the glittery sun. “I can make beautiful things,” Pebble whispered, “one step at a time.”

The End.
