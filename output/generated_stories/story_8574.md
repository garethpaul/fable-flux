# Roary’s Cozy Living Room Plan

Roary was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and a tiny tuft of a mane. One sunny afternoon, he padded into the playful living room. The carpet felt squishy under his paws. Sunlight made warm puddles on the floor. His toy drum, stackable blocks, and a shiny red ball waited by the couch. The air smelled like oranges from the snack bowl on the table.

“Today, I’m building the tallest block tower ever!” Roary roared a tiny roar. “Rawr!”

He set his blocks with careful paws. Tap, tap, tap. The tower grew. Roary’s whiskers twitched with pride. “I can do it,” he whispered.

Just then, his friend Pip, a giggly little penguin, waddled in, belly sliding across the rug. “Hi, Roary! Let’s play ball!” Pip chirped, rolling the shiny red ball with a soft thump.

Roary frowned. The ball bumped the rug near the tower. The blocks wobbled. “Careful!” Roary said, eyes wide. “My tower!”

Pip stopped and blinked. “Oh! I didn’t see.” Pip hugged the ball. “I like rolling. It makes my belly feel bouncy.”

Roary took a deep breath. He felt warm inside, like a tiny storm. He wanted to roar big. But he remembered he was brave and kind. He looked at Pip and listened to his own feelings. His heart said, “I want to build.” He guessed Pip’s heart said, “I want to roll.”

Roary whispered, “Hmm. We have different plans.”

Mama Lion walked by with a gentle smile. She smelled like oranges too. “What’s the problem, cubs?”

Roary said, “I want to build my tower.” He touched his blocks. “Pip wants to roll the ball.”

Pip nodded. “I didn’t mean to wobble it.”

Mama Lion knelt, her fur warm and soft. “Thank you for telling me. Let’s use brave listening and kind words.” She patted the rug. “Can we find a way that helps both of you?”

Roary rubbed his chin with a paw. He thought and thought. Decision-making time! He looked around the playful living room. There was a space by the window with a fuzzy blue mat. There was also a space by the couch with the big pillow fort.

“I have an idea,” Roary said. “We can make a Ball Zone and a Block Zone!” He pointed with a paw. “Blocks by the pillow fort. Ball by the blue mat.”

Pip tilted his head. “Will the ball roll far?”

“We can put cushions like a soft wall,” Roary said. “Bump! The ball will stop.”

Pip smiled. “That sounds fun.”

Mama Lion clapped softly. “That’s good negotiation. You both shared ideas.”

Roary picked up a cushion. Pip picked up another. Thump, thump. They made a cozy cushion wall. Roary moved his blocks to the pillow fort side. He felt calm now. The little storm inside got quiet.

“Ready?” Pip asked.

“Ready,” Roary said.

Pip rolled the red ball. Thump, thump, bump! It kissed the cushions and stopped. Pip giggled. “Belly slide!” He slid on the rug. Swish!

Roary stacked his blocks. Tap, tap, tap. The tower got tall. He could hear Pip’s happy giggles. He could hear his own soft breaths. He smelled the orange slices again and felt proud and peaceful.

Suddenly, the ball slipped under the couch. “Oh no!” Pip peeped. “My ball is stuck.”

Roary looked at his tall tower. He looked at Pip’s worried eyes. He felt a tug in his heart. He was brave. He could help.

“I will help,” Roary said. He got on his belly. The rug tickled his whiskers. He reached under the couch. Dust bunnies tickled his nose. “Ah-choo!” He laughed. “Got it!” He pulled the ball out.

Pip clapped his flippers. “Thank you, Roary!”

“You’re welcome,” Roary said. “I’m glad to help.”

Pip looked at the tower. “It is very tall. You did it.”

Roary felt warm again, but this time it was a happy warm. “Thank you for rolling in the Ball Zone,” he said. “That helped me build.”

Pip nodded. “Thank you for making space for me.”

They shared orange slices with Mama Lion. The slices were juicy and sweet. Drip, drip. Pip wiped his beak. Roary licked his paw. They both said, “Thank you, Mama.”

“You worked together,” Mama Lion said. “You accepted each other’s play. You used kind words. You made a good plan. That is very brave.”

Roary and Pip grinned. Roary placed the last block on top. The tower didn’t wobble. It stood tall, like a tiny castle. Pip rolled the ball to Roary’s paw. “Want to roll for a turn?” Pip asked.

Roary thought for a moment. He smiled. “Yes! After I take a tower picture in my mind.” He blinked his eyes, click! “Okay, your turn to teach me rolling.”

Pip showed him a gentle push. Roary tried. Thump, thump. “Wee!” he laughed.

They played until the sun puddles on the rug turned soft and sleepy. The living room felt cozy and kind.

Roary whispered, “I learned something today. When we accept each other and talk, we can both have fun.”

Pip whispered, “And say thank you, too.”

They bumped paws and flippers. “Friends,” they said.

The End.
