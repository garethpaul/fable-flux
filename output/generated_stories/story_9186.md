# Rocco's Thank-You Notes

Rocco the raccoon sat in his cozy study corner. Soft yellow light came from the little lamp on his wooden desk. Colorful pencils stood up straight in a red cup. White paper waited in a neat stack.

But Rocco felt worried. He tapped his tiny paws on the smooth desk. The sound went *tap-tap-tap* in the quiet room.

"I have so many thank-you notes to write," Rocco said with a big sigh. "Mrs. Owl helped me learn to read. Mr. Bear fixed my broken bicycle. And Grandma Squirrel baked me cookies when I was sick."

Rocco picked up a blue pencil. It felt heavy in his small paw. Writing seemed so hard! He started to draw one letter, then stopped.

"This is too much work," he grumbled. "Maybe I don't need to write thank-you notes."

Just then, Rocco remembered something special. He opened his desk drawer with a soft *creak*. Inside was a small music box that played gentle tunes. The sweet melody filled the air like floating bubbles.

"Music always makes me feel better," Rocco smiled. He wound up the music box again. The pretty sounds danced around the room.

As the music played, Rocco thought about Mrs. Owl's kind face when she taught him new words. He remembered Mr. Bear's strong hands fixing his wobbly wheel. He could almost smell Grandma Squirrel's warm, sweet cookies.

"They were so nice to me," Rocco whispered. "I want to say thank you properly."

Rocco picked up his blue pencil again. This time, it felt lighter. He wrote his first letter slowly and carefully. Each letter looked like a little drawing.

"Dear Mrs. Owl," he wrote in big, round letters. "Thank you for teaching me to read. Now I can read stories about adventures and faraway places. You are the best teacher ever!"

The pencil felt smoother now. Rocco's paw moved more easily across the paper. Writing wasn't so scary anymore!

He wrote to Mr. Bear next. "Dear Mr. Bear, thank you for fixing my bicycle. Now I can ride to the park and play with my friends. You have magic hands!"

Finally, Rocco wrote to Grandma Squirrel. "Dear Grandma Squirrel, your cookies made me feel so much better when I was sick. They tasted like sunshine and hugs. Thank you for caring about me!"

Rocco looked at his three letters. The words weren't perfect, but they came from his heart. Each letter showed how grateful he felt.

"I did it!" Rocco cheered. He did a little dance around his study corner. His striped tail swished back and forth with joy.

The next day, Rocco delivered his thank-you notes. Mrs. Owl's eyes sparkled when she read hers. Mr. Bear's chest puffed up with pride. Grandma Squirrel gave Rocco the biggest, warmest hug.

"Your thank-you note made my whole day brighter," Mrs. Owl hooted happily.

"Nobody ever thanked me so nicely before," Mr. Bear rumbled with a huge smile.

"You have such a grateful heart, dear Rocco," Grandma Squirrel said softly.

That evening, Rocco sat in his study corner again. The yellow lamp glowed warmly. His music box played its gentle tune. But now Rocco felt proud and happy inside.

"When we say thank you to people who help us, it makes everyone feel good," Rocco said to himself. "And finishing important tasks feels wonderful too!"

From that day on, Rocco always wrote thank-you notes when people were kind to him. And he learned that completing tasks, even hard ones, made him feel proud and strong.

The End.
