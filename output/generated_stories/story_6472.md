# Leo's Big Bath Adventure

Little Leo the lion cub stood outside the bathroom door. His golden fur was messy from playing all day. Mud covered his paws, and leaves stuck to his mane.

"Bath time, Leo!" called Mama Lion from inside the bathroom.

Leo's ears drooped. "Do I have to, Mama? I don't want to get wet!"

The bathroom felt warm and steamy. Leo could hear water running into the big white tub. It made a splashing sound like a waterfall. The room smelled like lavender soap and fresh towels.

Mama Lion smiled at her little cub. "Before we start, we need to prepare everything. That's what makes bath time special!"

"Prepare?" Leo tilted his fuzzy head. "What does that mean?"

"It means getting ready the right way," Mama explained. "First, let's gather our supplies!"

Leo watched as Mama Lion placed soft towels on the counter. She arranged colorful bath toys in a neat row. There was a yellow duck, a blue boat, and a green frog.

"Can I help prepare too?" Leo asked, his tail starting to swish with excitement.

"Of course! You can choose which soap we use tonight."

Leo sniffed three different bottles. One smelled like sweet oranges. Another smelled like fresh mint. The third smelled like vanilla cookies.

"This one!" Leo pointed to the orange soap with his little paw. "It smells like sunshine!"

"Wonderful choice," Mama said. "Now, what do we do next?"

Leo thought hard. "Check if the water is just right?"

"Exactly!" Mama Lion dipped her paw into the warm water. "Not too hot, not too cold. Perfect for a little lion!"

As Leo climbed into the tub, something magical happened. The orange soap made fluffy white bubbles that sparkled in the light. Leo's eyes grew wide with wonder.

"Mama, look! I can make bubble mountains!" Leo scooped up handfuls of bubbles and shaped them into tall peaks.

"That's beautiful, Leo! You're being very creative." Mama Lion watched as Leo made bubble castles and bubble clouds.

While they washed, Mama Lion shared a special story. "When I was a little cub like you, my mama taught me this same bath routine. Her mama taught it to her too. It's a special tradition in our family."

"Really?" Leo splashed gently. "So Grandma Lion prepared for baths just like us?"

"Yes, and someday you might teach your own cubs about preparation too."

Leo felt proud and important. He carefully washed behind his ears and scrubbed his paws clean. He even helped rinse the soap from his mane.

When bath time was over, Leo felt fresh and cozy. His fur was fluffy and clean. He smelled like sunshine oranges.

"I learned something important today," Leo said as Mama wrapped him in a warm towel.

"What's that, my brave little cub?"

"Preparing makes everything better! When we get ready the right way, even things that seem scary can be fun."

Mama Lion hugged Leo tight. "You're absolutely right. Good preparation helps us feel confident and ready for anything."

That night, Leo dreamed of bubble mountains and sunshine soap. He couldn't wait for tomorrow's bath time adventure.

From that day on, Leo always helped prepare for bath time. He learned that taking time to get ready made every experience more special and enjoyable.

The End.
