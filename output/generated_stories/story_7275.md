# Leo Leads the Dance

Leo was a brave little lion cub with soft golden fur and bright, curious eyes. Today, he padded into the musical practice room on quiet paws. The room smelled like polished wood and fresh paper. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Drums, a piano, and shimmery scarves waited by the wall. Leo’s whiskers twitched with excitement.

“I want to dance!” Leo said, tail swishing.

His friends were there too—Mina the monkey, Tiko the turtle, and Bella the bluebird. They all looked at the big sign on the wall. It read, Practice Time: One at a Time.

Mina hopped. “I want to jump high!”

Tiko smiled slowly. “I want to learn the steps.”

Bella fluttered. “I want to flap and twirl!”

Leo took a deep breath and listened to the quiet hum of the piano. He felt brave, but he also felt something else—a little bit of worry. If everyone danced at once, it might get noisy and messy. The room was for everyone, and everyone needed a turn.

Leo thought hard. He remembered what his mama said: “Use your paws and your pause. Stop. Think. Then choose.”

He pressed his paws together. Pause. Think. Choose.

“I have an idea,” Leo said gently. “Let’s make a plan so we all can dance.”

Mina tilted her head. “A plan?”

Tiko nodded. “That sounds fair.”

Bella chirped. “What is the plan, Leo?”

Leo looked around. He saw the scarves, the drums, and the piano bench. He saw the big clock on the wall with a steady tick-tick. He made a small chart on the floor with three scarves—red, blue, and gold.

“Let’s take turns,” Leo said. “Red scarf is for jumping, blue is for twirling, and gold is for slow steps. We can each pick a scarf and do our dance when the clock hand moves to the next number.”

Mina clapped. “I like red!”

Bella sang, “Blue for me!”

Tiko smiled. “Gold is good.”

Leo smiled. “I will help count and keep the beat.”

He tapped the drum softly. Dum, dum, dum. The sound felt warm in his chest. Mina bounced with the red scarf. She jumped high, then she paused. Bella twirled with the blue scarf, light as a breeze. Tiko took slow, careful steps with the gold scarf, heel-to-toe, heel-to-toe.

When it was Leo’s turn, he stood tall. He lifted his paws and felt the cool floor on his pads. He swayed his tail. He bent his knees. He listened to the drum and the tick-tick clock. Then he danced! He made brave lion steps—light, strong, and steady.

“Roar-rrr!” Leo laughed softly. “This is fun!”

Suddenly, the door creaked open. Ms. Cora, the music teacher, peeked in. “Hello, dancers,” she said with a warm smile. “I see you made a plan.”

Leo’s ears perked. “We are taking turns so everyone can dance.”

Ms. Cora nodded. “That is smart thinking. You used your pause and your plan. That helps our room stay calm and kind.”

Mina said, “But I want to jump again right now!”

Bella fluffed her feathers. “And I want to twirl again!”

Tiko tapped his shell. “I still like slow steps.”

Leo felt the worry again. He did not want anyone to feel left out. He puffed his chest. “Let’s talk,” he said. “What do we all need?”

Mina said, “More jumping time.”

Bella said, “More twirling time.”

Tiko said, “More slow steps.”

Leo nodded. “We all need more dance time. But the room is for everyone. We share it. That is our job.”

Ms. Cora said, “This is called civic responsibility. It means we take care of our space and each other.”

Leo’s eyes shone. “What if we make a group dance?” he suggested. “We can mix the steps. Jump, twirl, slow. We can count to four together.”

He tapped the drum. “One, two, three, four.”

Mina bounced on “one.”

Bella twirled on “two.”

Tiko stepped slow on “three.”

Leo leaped soft and strong on “four.”

They tried it again. One, two, three, four. Their feet made tiny sh-sh sounds on the wood. The drum went dum, dum, dum, dum. The piano hummed as Ms. Cora touched the keys. The room felt bright and happy.

Mina giggled. “This is fun!”

Bella sang, “We are together!”

Tiko beamed. “We are balanced.”

Leo grinned. “We listened, we talked, we made a plan, and we danced.”

They practiced three more times. Each time, they took turns leading. When Mina led, she added a hop-hop. When Bella led, she added a gentle spin. When Tiko led, he added a careful bow. When Leo led, he added a brave lion pose—paws wide, heart open.

At the end, they all took a breath in and a breath out. The room was quiet again. Leo felt proud and calm.

Ms. Cora clapped softly. “You solved a problem with kind words. You used your brains and your hearts. And you danced!”

Leo nodded. “Dancing helps us think and share.”

Bella said, “We learned to listen.”

Mina said, “We learned to take turns.”

Tiko said, “We learned to care for our room.”

Leo’s tail swished. “We learned to lead and to follow.”

They put the scarves away and wiped the drums with soft cloths. The room smelled clean and sweet. The sun squares on the floor turned golden-orange. Leo looked at his friends and smiled.

“Tomorrow,” he said, “we can make a new dance. We can plan, we can share, and we can have fun.”

All his friends cheered. “Yes!”

Leo felt brave and happy. He knew that dancing, talking, and thinking together could make any day bright.

The lesson: Dance with your heart, use kind words to make a plan, and take care of each other and your space.

The End.
