# Koala Kim’s Sunny Craft Time

Koala Kim was a calm koala with soft gray fur and big, gentle eyes. She lived near a sunny backyard that smelled like fresh grass and sweet flowers. The birds sang, “Tweet, tweet!” and a warm breeze tickled the leaves. Today, Kim wanted to make a big friendship banner.

“It will say, ‘Friends Work Together!’” Kim whispered with a smile. She set out bright paper, safe glue, colorful yarn, and shiny stickers on a cozy picnic blanket.

Her friends, Benny Bunny and Tia Turtle, came bouncing and shuffling in.

“Hi, Kim!” said Benny, wiggling his nose. “What are we making? I’m ready to hop and help!”

“Hello, friends,” Kim said in her calm voice. “We are making a banner. We have to finish before the sun reaches the tall fence. That is our time. When the sun touches the fence, we stop and clean up.”

Tia Turtle blinked slowly. “I can help, but I move slowly,” she said. “Is that okay?”

Kim smiled. “Yes, Tia. We will go slow and steady together. That is how friends work.”

They all sat on the warm blanket. The blanket felt soft on their toes. The paper felt smooth, and the glue was a little sticky. The sun shone bright, and the air smelled like lemony soap from the garden hose.

Benny bounced up. “I want to put on all the stickers!” he said.

Kim held up a paw. “Let’s make a plan first,” she said. “We have three jobs. Cut shapes, glue letters, and add stickers. We will take turns. That helps us finish on time.”

Benny’s ears wiggled. “A plan!” he said. “Okay!”

Tia nodded. “I can start with letters,” she said. “I am slow, but careful.”

Kim clapped softly. “Great idea. Benny, you can cut big circles. I will help with glue and counting the time.”

They started. Snip, snip went the safe scissors. Peel, press went the letters. “R… I… E…” Tia said, sounding out each letter in a gentle voice. Kim dabbed glue. “Dot, dot, not a lot,” she sang.

Kim looked at the sun. It was bright and warm on her fur. “We have time for two songs,” she said. “When we finish two songs, we switch jobs.”

They sang a happy song. “Stick and glue, me and you! Friends make crafts, one and two!” Benny giggled. Tia smiled and hummed.

After two songs, Kim said, “Switch time!” Benny handed the scissors to Kim. Tia passed the letters to Benny. Kim gave the glue to Tia.

Benny frowned. “But I want more stickers now,” he said.

Kim took a deep breath. She felt calm. “I know you love stickers,” she said softly. “We will get to stickers soon. First, we finish letters. Our plan helps us finish before the sun reaches the fence.”

Benny looked at the fence. The fence made a long, cozy shadow on the grass. “Okay,” he said. “I can wait. Friends take turns.”

They worked and sang. Snip, snip. Dab, dab. Press, press. The banner grew colorful. Red circles. Blue squares. Yellow stars. Tia placed letters slowly. “F… R… I… E… N… D… S,” she said carefully.

Then, a little breeze came. Whoosh! It made the papers fly like butterflies. “Oh no!” Benny cried. A yellow star floated away.

Kim stayed calm. She closed her eyes and breathed in. She smelled the grass and flowers. “It’s okay,” she said. “We can solve this. Tia, could you hold the corners with smooth stones? Benny, can you gently chase the star?”

“Yes!” said Benny. He hopped softly and caught the star. “Got it!” Tia found round stones and placed them on the corners. “All safe now,” she said.

Kim smiled. “Great teamwork. Thank you, friends.”

They checked the sun again. It was closer to the fence. “We have time for one more song,” Kim said. “Then we do stickers.”

Benny beamed. “Sticker time soon!” Tia nodded. “I am ready.”

They sang their song once more. Then Kim said, “Sticker time!” Benny placed shiny hearts. Tia added little turtles. Kim added tiny silver stars. The banner sparkled. It felt bumpy with shapes and smooth with paper. It looked bright and happy.

The sun touched the fence. Kim clapped. “Time to stop and clean up,” she said.

Benny blinked. “But I want one more sticker.”

Kim pointed to the fence. “We said we stop when the sun touches the fence. Keeping our plan helps us be fair and finish on time.”

Benny looked at the banner. It was beautiful. “You’re right,” he said. “I can save this sticker for next time.”

They cleaned together. Crinkle went the paper scraps. Snap went the glue cap. The blanket smelled like sunshine.

Kim held up the banner. “Friends Work Together!” it read. All three smiled.

“We did it,” Tia said softly. “We took turns. We had a plan.”

“And we helped each other,” Benny added.

Kim nodded. “That is friendship. We stayed calm, we managed our time, and we made a lovely craft. When we plan and share, we all feel good.”

They hugged. The birds sang again. The sunny backyard felt warm and proud.

“Next time,” Benny said, “I will wait my turn even better.”

“And I will keep holding the corners,” Tia said.

Kim smiled. “And I will keep us calm and on time. Friends make great things—together.”

The End.
