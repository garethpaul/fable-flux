# Polka the Parrot’s Plate Parade

Polka was a creative parrot with green feathers that shimmered like leaves. She loved to sing, paint, and make up silly rhymes. Today, Polka wanted to play a new game in the dining room. The sun made warm squares on the floor, and the table smelled like fresh pancakes and sweet syrup.

“Polka plays Plate Parade!” she chirped, tapping her beak on a shiny spoon. “Drum-drum, tap-tap!”

Her friends waddled in. Benny the bear cub padded softly on the rug. Miri the mouse zipped up a chair leg, sniffing the buttery toast. Lolo the ladybug landed on a napkin, tiny wings glinting in the light.

“Let’s use the dishes to make music!” Polka said. She fluffed her feathers and spread out the tools: spoons, cups, a little bell, and a wooden whisk. “We can take turns and make a band.”

Benny’s eyes grew big. “I want the bell! Ding-ding!”

Miri squeaked, “I want to tap the cup! Tap, tap!”

Lolo buzzed, “I want to roll the pea! Roll-rolly!”

Polka felt a flutter. She wanted to do everything first. “Polka first!” she squawked. “I start all the sounds!”

Benny’s ears drooped. “But I wanted the bell…”

Miri’s whiskers trembled. “I wanted the cup…”

Lolo’s wings slowed. “I wanted a turn, too…”

The dining room got quiet. The clock ticked. The toast smell drifted. Polka looked at her friends’ faces. Their eyes were soft and hopeful. Her tummy felt twisty, like a spoon stirring.

Polka took a deep breath. The air tasted like warm pancakes. “Hmm,” she said softly. “Polka can be creative and kind.” She tapped her beak gently. “We can take turns.”

“What does that mean?” Benny asked.

“It means we share the time,” Polka explained. “One friend plays, then another friend plays. Everyone gets a go. We can make a plan.”

“Like a line?” Miri asked.

“Like a line,” Polka nodded. “And we can use a turn timer. Listen.” She picked up the wooden whisk and tapped the table softly—tap, tap, tap. “Three taps means switch.”

Benny smiled. “I can wait for three taps.”

Miri nodded. “I can, too.”

Lolo buzzed happily. “I love the switch-taps!”

Polka spread her wings. “Let’s choose together. I wanted the bell first, but I hear you.” She looked at Benny. “Benny, you can start with the bell. Ding-ding!”

Benny’s ears perked up. “Really?”

Polka nodded. “Yes. Then Miri can tap the cup, and Lolo can roll the pea, and I will tap the spoon. After three taps, we switch!”

“That’s a fair plan,” Miri squeaked.

“That’s respect!” Lolo buzzed. “You listened.”

Polka smiled. “We all listen.”

The music began. Benny rang the bell: ding… ding! The sound was bright and happy. Miri tapped the cup: tap-tap, tap-tap. It sounded like tiny feet. Lolo rolled the pea across a plate: roll-rolly, tick-tick. Polka tapped the spoon on the table: ting-ting.

The dining room filled with soft sounds and giggles. The plates clinked. The napkins swished. The butter smelled warm and melty.

Tap, tap, tap—Polka made the three switch-taps.

“My turn?” Miri asked.

“Your turn!” Benny said, handing over the bell.

Miri held the bell carefully. “Thank you,” she said.

“You’re welcome,” Benny replied.

They played again. The bell, the cup, the pea, the spoon—each friend had a turn. When Lolo’s tiny legs got tired, she said, “Polka, can we trade? I want to try the spoon.”

Polka thought. “Yes, we can trade. That’s negotiation. You ask. We make a plan.” She offered the spoon. “I’ll try the pea.”

Lolo giggled. “Deal!”

They switched and tried new sounds. The pea made a soft tick, tick on the plate. The spoon made a shiny ting, ting in Lolo’s tiny hands.

After a while, Benny said, “I want one more turn with the bell.”

Polka tilted her head. “I want a turn, too. How can we both be happy?”

“Two rings each!” Miri suggested.

“Two rings each,” Polka agreed. “Fair and square.”

Benny rang twice: ding, ding. He smiled and handed it over. Polka rang twice: ding, ding. Her heart felt light. Everyone was smiling.

At the end, Polka fluttered her green wings. “We did it! We took turns, we listened, we traded, and we made music.”

Benny hugged the bell to his chest. “It felt good to wait and then play.”

Miri wrapped her tail around the cup. “It felt kind to share.”

Lolo buzzed in a little circle. “It felt fair to make a plan together!”

Polka’s tummy felt warm now, not twisty. “Polka learned something,” she said. “Taking turns lets everyone shine. Respect is listening. Negotiation is talking and finding a plan.”

They all nodded. The clock ticked softly. The room smelled like syrup and smiles.

“Again tomorrow?” Benny asked.

“Yes,” Polka chirped. “Tomorrow we’ll make a Pancake Parade! And we’ll take turns flipping, too.”

Everyone laughed. The dining room glowed like a cozy hug as they cleaned up together, passing the dishes one by one, taking turns even with the towels.

Polka ruffled her feathers and sang a happy rhyme: “Share the sound, share the fun, turns for all, and joy for one-by-one!”

The End.
