# Polly's Pantry Discovery

Polly was a bright green parrot with beautiful red feathers on her wings. She lived in a cozy house with her family and loved to explore new places with her curious eyes.

One sunny morning, Polly flapped her colorful wings and landed on the kitchen counter. "What's behind that door?" she wondered, tilting her head to look at the pantry.

The pantry door was slightly open, and Polly could smell something interesting inside. "Mmm, it smells like cinnamon and vanilla!" she chirped excitedly.

Polly hopped closer and peeked inside. The pantry was full of shelves with jars, boxes, and bags. Everything looked so mysterious and wonderful!

"I wonder what's in all these containers," Polly said to herself. Her curiosity was growing bigger and bigger.

She flew to the first shelf and saw a clear jar filled with colorful round things. "These look like little rainbows!" Polly exclaimed. They were jelly beans, all shiny and smooth.

On the next shelf, she found a box that rattled when she touched it with her beak. "Shake, shake, shake!" The sound made her laugh. Inside were crackers that looked like tiny squares.

Polly's tummy started to rumble. "I'm getting hungry from all this exploring," she said. But then she remembered what her mama always told her: "Always ask before taking food that isn't yours."

Just then, Polly heard footsteps. Her friend Emma walked into the kitchen looking sad.

"What's wrong, Emma?" asked Polly, flying down to sit on her shoulder.

"I'm supposed to make snacks for my class today, but I don't know what to choose," Emma sighed. "I want everyone to have something healthy and yummy."

Polly's eyes sparkled with an idea. "I can help you! I just explored the pantry and saw lots of wonderful things!"

"Really? You explored all by yourself?" Emma smiled.

"Yes! I was very curious about what was inside," Polly said proudly. "I saw colorful jelly beans, crunchy crackers, and so many other things!"

Emma and Polly went into the pantry together. Polly showed Emma everything she had discovered.

"Look, Emma! These nuts are good for growing strong," Polly pointed with her wing. "And these dried fruits are sweet and healthy!"

"Wow, Polly! Your curiosity helped you learn so much," Emma said happily. "You're helping me take care of my classmates by finding healthy snacks."

Together, they filled a basket with nuts, dried fruits, and whole grain crackers. Polly felt proud that her curious exploring had helped her friend and would help feed other children too.

"Thank you for being so curious and helpful, Polly," Emma said, giving her a gentle pet. "You discovered exactly what we needed!"

Polly preened her feathers proudly. "Being curious helped me learn about new foods and help my community," she chirped. "Tomorrow I want to explore the garden and see what I can discover there!"

Emma laughed. "Just remember to always ask permission first, and stay safe while you explore."

"I will!" promised Polly. "Curiosity makes every day an adventure!"

That evening, Polly told her family all about her pantry adventure. She had learned that being curious could help her discover new things, take care of herself by making good food choices, and even help her friends and community.

From that day on, Polly continued to explore with curiosity, always remembering to be safe and ask questions. Her curious nature made her a wonderful helper and friend to everyone around her.

The End.
