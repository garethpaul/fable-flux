# Kori the Koala’s Cozy Guest Room Day

Kori the koala was calm and kind. One sunny afternoon, Kori visited a friend’s house. “You may rest in the guest room,” said Maya, Kori’s friend. “Make yourself comfy!”

The guest room was soft and quiet. The curtains were light and floaty. The quilt on the bed felt fluffy like clouds. A little table held crayons, paper, and a tiny plant that smelled sweet, like mint. Kori took a deep breath. The room smelled clean and gentle. “Mmm,” Kori murmured. “This feels safe.”

Kori loved to draw. “I will make a welcome picture for Maya,” Kori said. “It will be bright and happy.” Kori picked up a blue crayon. Scratch, scratch went the crayon on the paper. “Hello, sunshine!” Kori giggled. Then Kori used yellow. Swish, swish. Then orange and pink. Tap, tap, tap. “I’m using all my colors!”

Kori hummed a soft song. “Bum-bum, la-la.” Drawing felt good. It made Kori’s heart calm and warm. Kori’s ears wiggled with joy. “I am making art.”

Kori reached for green. But the green crayon rolled off the table. Plunk! It bumped a little cup of water. The cup wobbled. “Oh!” said Kori. Splash! The water spilled onto the quilt. A dark circle spread out like a puddle moon.

Kori’s eyes grew wide. Kori’s tummy felt tight. “Oh no,” Kori whispered. “I made a mess.” Kori looked at the door and then at the quilt. Kori felt sad and a little scared.

Kori took a slow breath in. One, two, three. Then a slow breath out. One, two, three. “I can be calm,” Kori told self. “I can be creative to fix this. I can make it right.”

Kori looked around the guest room. “What can help?” Kori asked. Kori saw soft towels stacked in a basket. Kori saw tissues on the nightstand. Kori saw a tiny fan on the floor. Kori saw a little note on the wall: “We all make mistakes. Try your best.”

Kori nodded. “I will try my best.”

First, Kori gently patted the quilt with a towel. Pat, pat, pat. The towel felt thick and thirsty. It soaked up the water. Kori pressed down more. Pat, pat, pat. The puddle moon got smaller and lighter.

Next, Kori waved the quilt to let it breathe. Whoosh, whoosh. The air felt cool on Kori’s nose. Then Kori turned on the tiny fan and pointed it at the damp spot. The fan hummed, “Hmmmm.”

Kori whispered, “Good job, little fan.”

Kori still felt a pinch in the tummy. “I should tell Maya,” Kori said. “I should make amends.” Kori walked to the kitchen, paws quiet on the floor. “Maya?” Kori called softly.

Maya looked up with a smile. “Hi, Kori! How is the guest room?”

“I spilled water on the quilt,” Kori said. “I am sorry. I am cleaning it. I used a towel and the fan. May I do more to help?”

Maya’s face was kind. “Thank you for telling me,” she said. “You are being brave and honest. Water is okay. You did the right steps. Let’s check.”

Back in the guest room, they touched the quilt. It was only a little damp now. “Nice work,” Maya said. “You were resourceful. You used what you had.”

Kori felt lighter. “I also made a picture for you,” Kori said, holding up the drawing. It showed a big happy sun, a green tree, and a cozy window. There were tiny hearts by the window.

Maya’s eyes sparkled. “It is lovely! The colors make me smile.” She sniffed the mint plant. “And your careful breath helped, too.”

Kori nodded. “When I felt worried, I used calm breaths. In… and out… It helped my body and my mind.”

“Would you like to add a frame?” asked Maya. She opened a small box. Inside were cardboard shapes, yarn, and stickers.

“Yes, please!” said Kori. They sat on the rug. The rug felt warm and bouncy. Kori wrapped blue yarn around the cardboard frame. Wrap, wrap, wrap. Maya placed star stickers. Stick, stick. Kori added a little label at the bottom that said, “Welcome.”

Together they hung the picture on the wall. Kori stepped back. “It looks happy,” Kori said. The fan still hummed. The curtains still floated. The quilt now looked smooth and dry.

Kori patted the quilt. “I am sorry, quilt,” Kori said softly. “Thank you for drying.”

Maya smiled. “You made amends by helping and by telling the truth. You also took care of your feelings with calm breaths. And you used your creativity to make something beautiful.”

Kori’s heart felt warm again. “Today I learned I can fix mistakes,” Kori said. “I can be calm, creative, and kind.”

Maya nodded. “That is a big lesson.”

Kori curled up on the bed for a rest. The mint plant smelled fresh. The quiet room felt safe and cozy. Kori whispered, “Thank you, guest room.”

“Thank you, Kori,” said Maya. “Your art makes this room shine.”

They smiled together. The welcome picture glowed on the wall like a little sun.

The End.
