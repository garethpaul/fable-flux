# Pippa Penguin’s Practice Plan

Pippa was a cheerful penguin with a bright yellow scarf. She waddled into the musical practice room, flapping her little wings. The room was cozy and warm. Soft lights twinkled. A big drum sat in the corner. A shiny xylophone gleamed with rainbow bars. A small piano went plink-plonk when Pippa tapped a key.

“Today, I will play a song,” Pippa chirped. “I will play it all by myself!”

Pippa picked up two gentle mallets. Tap, tap, tap went the xylophone. The bars felt cool and smooth. Ting! went the high note. Tong! went the low note. Pippa smiled. “So pretty!”

In waddled her friend, Benny Bear, with fuzzy paws. “Hi, Pippa,” he said. “Can I play the drum?”

Pippa blinked. “I want to play my song now,” she said. “I have to practice.”

Benny tapped the drum lightly. Boom-boom. “I want to play now too,” he said.

Pippa’s tummy felt twisty. She wanted to be kind. She also wanted to practice. She looked at the clock with big round numbers. She took a deep breath. In through the nose. Out through the beak. “We have a problem,” Pippa said softly. “We both want to play at the same time.”

Benny nodded. “Yes. A problem.”

Pippa shuffled her feet on the soft rug. She had an idea. “Let’s make a plan,” she said. “A plan helps us take turns.”

Benny tilted his head. “A plan?”

“Yes,” Pippa said. She drew a simple chart on a big paper with a fat blue marker. Squeak, squeak went the marker. “I will play for five little minutes,” she said, pointing to a sun picture. “Then you play for five little minutes,” she said, pointing to a moon picture. “We can use the sand timer.” She flipped a small sand timer. Swish went the sand, falling gently.

Benny smiled. “Okay! A turn for you. A turn for me.”

Pippa felt calm. “Thank you,” she said. “That is good conflict resolution.” She said the big words slowly. “It means we solve our problem with kind words and a plan.”

Pippa started to practice. Tap-ting, tap-ting, tap-ting-ting. The notes were not quite right. “Oh,” she said. “That sounds bumpy.”

Her teacher, Ms. Seal, slid in with a happy clap. “I hear brave practice,” Ms. Seal said. “Practice makes us strong.”

Pippa tried again, slower. Tap… ting… tap… ting-ting. She looked at her chart. She looked at the sand. “I will break the song into tiny steps,” she said. “First the start. Then the middle. Then the end.”

She sang the steps in a whisper. “Start is soft. Middle is louder. End is sweet.”

Benny listened. Boom-boom, he echoed softly on the drum, only on his turn. When the sand ran out, swish, he said, “My turn now?” Pippa nodded and handed him the mallets for a moment to feel. “Thank you,” Benny said. He boomed softly, boom… boom, and counted slow.

While Benny played, Pippa did self-care. She sipped cool water. Sip, sip. She stretched her wings and rolled her shoulders. “Healthy habits help me practice better,” she said. “Drink water. Stretch. Breathe.”

When it was Pippa’s turn again, she felt fresh. She smiled and played the start. Tap… ting… tap… ting. “Better!” she cheered.

But then—clang! She hit the wrong bar. Her eyes got watery. “I made a mistake,” she whispered.

Ms. Seal nodded kindly. “Mistakes are part of learning,” she said. “Say: I can try again.”

Pippa took a calm breath. “I can try again.” She slowed down. She watched her flippers. Tap… ting… tap… ting-ting. The notes rang like tiny bells. “I did it!”

Benny clapped his paws. Clap clap! “Good job, Pippa!”

“Thank you,” Pippa said. “Our plan helps me practice. Taking turns helps us both.”

They checked the chart. One more round each. Pippa played the middle. Benny did a soft drum beat to keep the beat: boom… boom… boom. They were a team. The room filled with warm, happy sounds. Ting-ting, boom-boom, plink-plonk.

At the end of the sand, they took a short rest. They munched crunchy apple slices. Crunch, crunch. “Snacks and breaks help our bodies,” Pippa said. “Then we can focus.”

“Healthy habits!” Benny said, licking juice from his paw.

For the last turn, Pippa tried the whole song. She whispered her steps. “Start soft. Middle louder. End sweet.” Tap… ting… tap… ting-ting… tap-tap… ting. The notes danced like tiny stars. Ms. Seal beamed. “Beautiful! You practiced with a plan, you shared, and you cared for your body. That is smart.”

Pippa bowed with her wings wide. “Practice helps me grow,” she said. “When we have a problem, we can talk, make a plan, and take turns.”

Benny bowed too. “And take breaks and drink water!”

They laughed. The room glowed with music and friendship. Pippa’s tummy felt warm and proud.

That night, Pippa tucked her chart into her scarf. “I will use my plan again tomorrow,” she said. “Little steps. Kind words. Healthy habits. Practice, practice, practice.”

The End.
