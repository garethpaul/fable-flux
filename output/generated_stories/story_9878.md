# Panda Pao’s Fair Turn Band

Panda Pao lived in a cozy treehouse. The treehouse smelled like fresh leaves and warm bamboo tea. Wind chimes tinkled softly at the window. Pao was a patient panda who loved music. He had a tiny drum, a shiny triangle, and a twangy little ukulele.

One bright morning, Pao clapped his paws. “I want to make a band!” he said. His whiskers wiggled with excitement.

Tap-tap-tap! Someone climbed the ladder. It was Mimi Monkey, with a jingling bell bracelet.

“Hi, Pao!” Mimi chirped. “Can I play too?”

“Yes!” said Pao with a big smile. “We will be a fair band. Everyone gets a turn.”

Soon, Tiko Tortoise peeked in. He carried two soft maracas. “May I join?” he asked in a gentle voice.

“Yes,” said Pao. “We will share and take turns. That is fair.”

Last, Bibi Bird swooped through the window, feathers rustling. She brought a bright blue whistle. “Tweet! I want to play!” she sang.

“Welcome,” said Pao. “We will listen, we will practice, and we will be fair.”

Pao set the instruments in a line on a woven mat. The mat felt rough under his paws. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor.

Pao spoke kindly. “We will make a song. First, we choose who plays first. Then we switch. Everyone gets the same time. That is fairness.”

Mimi bounced. “Me first! Me first!” Her bell bracelet jingled loud-loud-loud.

Tiko blinked slowly. “I can wait,” he said. “I am slow, but I can practice.”

Bibi fluttered. “Tweet! I want a turn too!”

Pao held up a paw. “Let’s use the Fair Turn Leaf.” He picked a big, green leaf from the window box. It smelled leafy and cool. “We pass the leaf. The one who holds it plays. We count to ten. Then we pass it on.”

Everyone nodded. “Fair!” they said.

Pao handed the leaf to Mimi. “You first.”

Mimi grinned and shook her bell bracelet. It went jingle-jangle, bright like tiny stars. Pao counted gently. “One, two, three…” Mimi danced. “Eight, nine, ten.” Pao smiled. “Great job! Now pass the leaf.”

Mimi gave the leaf to Tiko. Tiko held his maracas. Swish-swish, soft like the ocean. He was slow and careful. “One, two, three…” Pao counted. “Eight, nine, ten.” Tiko smiled. “My turn is done.”

Bibi got the leaf next. She blew her blue whistle. Tweet-toooo! The sound was high and shiny. “One, two, three…” counted Pao. “Eight, nine, ten.”

Then Pao held the leaf and strummed his ukulele. Pling-plong, warm and bouncy. “One, two, three… eight, nine, ten.”

“Let’s make our song now,” said Pao. “We will build it in turns.”

Pao tapped a steady beat on his tiny drum. Boom-boom, like a friendly heartbeat. “This is the base,” he said. “Practice the beat with me.”

Mimi wanted to shake fast. She shook too soon. Jingle-jangle tripped over boom-boom. The sounds bumped into each other.

Bibi tweeted loud over everyone. Tiko swished at the wrong time. It sounded messy. It made Mimi frown. It made Bibi fluff her feathers. Tiko tucked his head.

Pao took a calm breath. He smelled the sweet bamboo tea. “Friends,” he said softly, “let’s be fair and listen. Fairness means we wait and respect each other’s turns. We practice so we can play together.”

Mimi looked at her paws. “I got excited,” she said. “I can wait.”

Bibi nodded. “Tweet. I can be softer and take my turn.”

Tiko smiled. “I will keep the slow swish.”

Pao raised the Fair Turn Leaf again. “Beat first, then bells, then maracas, then whistle. We count to ten for each part. We listen to the friend who is playing.”

They tried again.

Pao went first. Boom-boom, boom-boom. The drum felt deep and cozy. “One, two, three…”

Mimi waited, bouncing a little. When the leaf came, she shook softly. Jingle-jingle, like rain on a tin roof. Her eyes shone. “Eight, nine, ten.” She passed the leaf.

Tiko swished next. Swish-swish, like leaves hugging. The room felt calm. “Eight, nine, ten.”

Bibi took a deep breath and blew a gentle tweet. High and sweet, like a little sunrise. “Eight, nine, ten.”

They smiled at each other. The song sounded smooth and bright. The wind chimes at the window rang along, ting-ting.

“Let’s add words!” said Pao. He sang, “We share, we care, we take our turn.”

Mimi sang, “We listen and learn.”

Tiko sang slowly, “We practice, we grow.”

Bibi sang, “We’re fair, and it shows!”

They laughed and sang again, louder and prouder. Their music filled the treehouse. It felt like warm sunshine and happy hugs.

When they finished, Pao poured tiny cups of bamboo tea. It smelled sweet and grassy. They munched crunchy apple slices.

Mimi said, “Waiting was hard. But it felt fair.”

Tiko said, “Practice made me strong.”

Bibi said, “Respect made our music sweet.”

Pao nodded. “Fairness means everyone gets the same chance. We listen. We wait. We share. With patience and practice, our band can play anything.”

Outside, the leaves clapped softly. Inside, four friends smiled.

They put the instruments away, each in its spot. Pao patted the Fair Turn Leaf and set it on the windowsill.

“Tomorrow,” he said, “we make a new song, and we will be fair again.”

“Yes!” cheered the friends.

The End.
