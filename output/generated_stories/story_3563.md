# Sunny's Smart Acorn Plan

Sunny the squirrel lived in a cozy treehouse high in a big green oak. The walls smelled like fresh leaves. The floor felt warm from the sun. A tiny window let in a soft breeze that tickled Sunny’s whiskers.

One morning, Sunny peeked into her acorn basket. “Oh no,” she whispered. “Only three acorns! Winter will come soon. I need more.”

She looked out the window and saw her friends. Pip the mouse was nibbling a seed. Willa the bluebird sang a bright song. Bump the bunny thumped his soft feet.

Sunny took a deep breath. “I need a plan,” she said. “A smart plan.”

She pulled out her little chalkboard and a stubby piece of chalk. Scratch, scratch. Sunny drew a simple map with big shapes: the oak tree, the pond, the berry bush, and the old stump. She drew a sun for morning and a moon for evening. She made three happy circles for her friends.

Pip climbed up the ladder. “What are you drawing?” he squeaked.

“My plan,” Sunny said. “We need more acorns. Let’s use strategy. That means we think first, then we do. We choose the best way.”

Willa fluttered to the window. “Strategy,” she sang. “Think, then do. I like that!”

Bump’s nose twitched. “What’s the plan?”

Sunny smiled. “First, we check the old stump in the cool morning. Acorns fall there early. Next, we go to the sunny side of the pond. The wind rolls acorns there. Last, we visit the quiet hill when the sun is low.”

Pip clapped his tiny paws. “That sounds smart!”

Sunny added simple jobs. “Pip, you have quick eyes. You will spot acorns. Willa, you can fly high. You will watch for clouds and show us safe paths. Bump, you are strong. You will carry the basket. I will guide us with the map.”

Bump puffed with pride. “I can carry a lot!”

Willa chirped, “And I can sing a signal if I see rain.”

Sunny nodded. “We work together. We use our plan. If something changes, we stop, think, and choose again. That is strategy.”

They set off. Leaves rustled like soft whispers. The air smelled nutty and sweet. At the old stump, Pip squeaked, “There! There!” Tiny acorns hid in the grass like little brown buttons.

Bump lowered the basket. Plink, plonk, plink! The acorns made happy sounds as they dropped in. Sunny checked the chalk map. “Good job, team,” she said. “Step one is done.”

They walked to the pond. Sunlight danced on the water. Willa flew above and called, “Watch your paws! The ground is slippery.”

Bump nodded and took slow steps. Pip pointed to the shore. “Look! The wind rolled a pile of acorns!”

Plink, plonk, plink! The basket grew heavy. Sunny smiled. “Our strategy is working,” she said. “We planned. We listened. We stayed safe.”

Clouds bunched up like gray cotton. Willa sang a soft alert. “Rain might come.”

Sunny paused. She tapped her chin. “New idea,” she said. “Let’s use a quick path through the berry bush shade. We will save time and stay dry.” She drew a short line on the map with her chalk.

They followed the new path. The berries smelled sweet. A raindrop kissed Pip’s ear. “Eep! Cold!” he squeaked. They hurried along and reached the quiet hill just as the clouds drifted away.

On the hill, the grass felt cool and springy. But there were not many acorns. Bump frowned. “Oh. Only a few.”

Sunny sat and breathed. “Sometimes a plan needs a change,” she said gently. “Let’s look for a hidden tree.”

Willa circled in the sky. “I see one! A little oak with low branches.”

They went to the little oak. Acorns lay all around like tiny hats. Plink, plonk, plink! The basket filled to the top.

Back in the treehouse, the friends shared crunchy acorns and sweet berries. The room smelled cozy and warm. Sunny set the chalkboard down.

“Thank you, team,” Sunny said. “Pip, your quick eyes helped us. Willa, your song kept us safe. Bump, your strong paws carried our basket. I am grateful for each of you.”

Pip smiled. “Thank you for the plan, Sunny. You led us kindly.”

Willa chirped, “You listened to us, too. That is good leadership.”

Bump nibbled a berry. “And when the hill was empty, you accepted it and made a new plan.”

Sunny’s tail swished with joy. “Yes. We used strategy. We thought first. We worked together. We changed the plan when we needed to. We were safe, and we were kind.”

She drew a big heart on the chalkboard and wrote in simple letters: Think, Plan, Try, Change if Needed, Thank Your Team.

Sunny read it aloud, slow and clear. “Think. Plan. Try. Change if needed. Thank your team.”

The friends touched paws, wings, and paws again. The sun dipped low, and the treehouse glowed golden.

Sunny looked at the full basket and at her happy friends. “We did it,” she said softly. “Together.”

The End.
