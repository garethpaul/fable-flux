# Milo Mouse’s Cozy Table Tent

Milo was a kind mouse who lived in a snug hole near the dining room. Every morning, Milo peeked out to see the big round table, the shiny spoons, and the soft blue napkins. The dining room smelled like warm toast and oranges. Sunlight danced on the wooden floor. It was a happy place.

One day, Milo rubbed his tummy. “I want a cozy spot to stretch, breathe, and rest,” he said. “A wellness nook!” He squeaked the word wellness slowly, like a song. “Well-ness.”

Milo looked around the dining room. He noticed a small box by the chair. He saw clean napkins folded on the table. He saw a paper tube from a finished roll. He saw a loose button on the floor. Milo did not take anything that was in use. He only picked up safe, clean, and leftover things. “I will build gently,” he said. “I will use what is not needed. That is kind to the Earth. That is sustainability.”

First, Milo sat very still. He took three slow belly breaths, in and out. “When I breathe, I think clearly,” he whispered. “Breathing helps my body and my brain.” He smiled and felt brave.

Milo began to build. “I need a plan,” he told himself. “Step one, make a base. Step two, make a roof. Step three, make it comfy.” He tapped his whiskers and nodded. Logical thinking felt good.

He slid the small box under the table. “This is my base,” he said. He tested it with one paw. “Is it steady?” He shook it gently. “Hmm. It wobbles.” He looked around and found a flat coaster on the floor. He asked aloud, “Is anyone using this?” The room was quiet. He placed the coaster under one corner. “Now try again,” he said. The box stood steady.

“Step two, a roof!” Milo looked up at the blue napkins. They were folded and waiting for dinner. He did not take them. “I should not use things that people need,” he said. “That is empathy. I can think about others.” Then he spotted a soft cloth scrap near his mouse hole. It was clean and free to use. “Perfect!”

Milo draped the cloth scrap from a chair leg to the box. It made a small roof. A stripe of sunlight shone through like a golden ribbon. The roof slipped a little. Milo tried the paper tube. “Maybe the tube can be a pole,” he said. He balanced the tube between the box and the chair leg. The roof stayed up.

“Step three, make it comfy.” Milo found a tiny piece of cotton from an old pillow fluff that had fallen to the floor. He puffed it into a little pillow. He carried one safe blue napkin ring he found under the chair and made it a window frame. He made a tiny bell from the button and a string, so he could ring for help if he needed it.

“Ding!” went the button-bell. Milo giggled. “Welcome to my cozy table tent!”

He crawled inside. It felt cool and soft. The floor smelled like lemon clean and a little like toast. Sunlight made warm shapes on the cloth roof. Milo stretched his paws. “Streeetch,” he said. He took three more slow breaths. “In… out… in… out…” His shoulders felt soft. His heart felt calm.

Just then, a tiny crumb friend, Ant, walked by. “Hello, Milo! What is this?” Ant asked with bright eyes.

“It is my wellness nook,” Milo said. “I breathe here. I stretch here. I drink water here.” He showed a thimble cup filled with clean water he had brought from the kitchen drip. “Water helps my body.”

Ant looked worried. “I am tired. My legs feel busy.”

“You can rest here,” Milo said kindly. “But first, let’s check if it is safe and strong.” Milo tapped the roof. “Roof on?” He tapped the base. “Base steady?” He nodded. “All good.”

Ant smiled and stepped inside. “It is cozy,” Ant said. “It smells nice.”

Milo shared a tiny seed. “Snack?” he asked. “It is a healthy seed. Crunch, crunch.” They both chewed slowly.

Suddenly, a small breeze came through the window. “Whoosh!” The roof fluttered.

“Oh no,” said Ant. “Will it fall?”

Milo placed a clothespin he found on the floor onto the roof edge. “Pin and win!” he said. The roof stayed still. “When things change, we can think and try again,” he told Ant. “That is logical thinking.”

Ant nodded. “I feel calm now,” Ant said. “Thank you for sharing.”

“You are welcome,” Milo said. “Wellness means care for our bodies and feelings. We rest. We drink water. We breathe. We move. We think kind thoughts. We also take care of our home and Earth by using what is left over and asking before we use new things.”

Soon, the dining room clock ticked softly. Tick-tick. It was time for a gentle stretch. “Arms up,” Milo said. He and Ant stretched high, then rolled their shoulders. They both laughed. “I feel strong,” Ant said.

Before leaving, Milo tidied the nook. He put bits back in their spots, kept the button-bell, and folded the cloth scrap neatly. “When we finish, we clean up,” he said. “That is caring too.”

Ant waved. “Bye, Milo! I will make a tiny rest spot in my tunnel. I will use safe, leftover bits, and I will ask before I take.”

Milo smiled. “That is wellness. That is building. That is thinking.”

He crawled into his cozy table tent one last time, took a deep breath, and whispered, “I am calm. I am kind. I am well.”

The End.
