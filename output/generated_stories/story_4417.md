# Nutty Plans in a Cozy Room

In a cozy bedroom, soft and warm like a muffin fresh from the oven, a resourceful squirrel named Sprig woke up. The curtains were sunny yellow. The blanket was fluffy and blue. Sprig wiggled his tiny nose and listened. He heard birds chirp outside and the gentle hum of a fan. His tail made a little swish-swish sound on the sheets.

“Good morning, me,” Sprig said with a smile. “Today is a plan day!”

Sprig loved plans. He loved thinking, “What should I do first? What should I do next?” He kept a little notebook made of square sticky notes. He kept crayons in a cup, and nuts in a jar with a lid that went click.

Sprig’s friend, Pip the mouse, peeked in the door. “Hi, Sprig! Can we make a snack? My tummy says rumble-rumble.”

Sprig patted the bed. “Yes! But let’s use a strategy,” he said. “A strategy is a smart plan. First, we think. Then, we do. Then, we check.”

Pip’s eyes grew big. “Think, do, check,” he repeated.

Sprig opened his notebook. “Step one: a healthy snack. Step two: art time. Step three: clean up.” He drew simple pictures: a carrot, a paintbrush, and a tiny broom.

Pip clapped. “Good plan!”

They went to the little snack tray in the cozy bedroom. Sprig could smell apples—sweet and crisp. He could feel the smooth skin of a banana. He opened the nut jar—click! It smelled toasty.

Sprig said, “Let’s make rainbow snack faces. They are yummy and good for our bodies. Apples for red cheeks. Banana for a smile. Nuts for crunchy eyes. Carrot sticks for silly hair.” He set each food in a neat row.

Pip reached for all the nuts at once. “I want lots!”

Sprig put a gentle paw on Pip’s hand. “Let’s use our strategy. We share. We balance. We choose colors.” He pointed to his plan. “Think first.”

Pip nodded. “Think, do, check.”

They sliced apple moons. Pip placed banana smiles. Sprig counted nuts, “One, two,” and set them like tiny buttons. The carrot sticks made orange hair that tickled their noses. Giggle, giggle!

Pip took a big crunchy bite. “Mmm! Sweet and crunchy.” Sprig tasted a carrot. “Crunch! Strong snack!” Their tummies felt happy and calm.

After snack, Sprig said, “Step two: art.” He spread a soft old sheet on the carpet. He opened a box of crayons. It smelled waxy and warm. He set out paper and a little cup of glue. “We will make a plan poster,” he said. “We will draw our strategy.”

Pip’s whiskers twitched with joy. “I will draw a big S for Strategy!”

Sprig drew three boxes: Think. Do. Check. He drew a light bulb in the Think box. He drew two friends in the Do box. He drew a big smile in the Check box. Pip glued a tiny leaf on the corner. “Leaf means nature snack,” he said.

They hummed a little song. “Think, do, check, hooray! We plan and then we play!”

Suddenly, Pip bumped the glue cup. Plop! A drop fell onto Sprig’s plan. The paper wrinkled. The leaf slid off. Sprig’s eyes went wide. His ears drooped.

“I am sorry, Sprig,” Pip said, tail low. “I did not mean to spill.”

Sprig took a slow breath. He thought, “What is my strategy for a mistake?” He tapped the Think box on the poster. “We think: It was an accident. We do: We fix it together. We check: Is it better?”

Pip sniffled. “Teamwork?”

“Teamwork,” Sprig said with a nod.

They used a tissue to dab the glue—pat, pat. Sprig held the poster flat. Pip smoothed the corners—swish, swish. Sprig added a sun with bright rays to cover the wrinkle. Pip drew a tiny heart near the leaf. The poster looked even happier than before.

Sprig smiled. “Thank you for the apology, Pip.”

Pip smiled back. “Thank you for the plan.”

They held up the poster to the window. Light shined through like gold. The colors glowed. Their cozy bedroom felt safe and bright. Sprig’s tail did a proud wiggle.

Sprig said, “Step three: clean up.” He pointed to the tiny broom. They sang as they worked. “Sweep, sweep, little crumbs. Brush, brush, sticky lumps.” They put crayons in the cup. They closed the nut jar—click. They folded the sheet. The room looked neat again.

Pip rubbed his tummy. “I feel full and strong. Apples and carrots help me grow. Nuts help my brain think.”

Sprig nodded. “Yes. Healthy foods help our bodies and our plans. Strategy helps our day.”

Pip asked, “What next, Sprig?”

Sprig peeked at his notebook. He drew a star. “We check: Did our plan work?”

They looked around. Snack—done. Art—done. Clean up—done. Feelings—calm and happy.

“It worked!” Pip cheered.

Sprig hugged his friend. “Today we learned: Think, do, check. Use kind words. Eat rainbow foods. Fix mistakes with teamwork. That is good strategy.”

Pip giggled. “And we made art!”

Sprig tucked the plan poster on the wall by the bed. He whispered, “Tomorrow, we make a new plan.”

The cozy bedroom was quiet and warm. The fan hummed a lullaby. Sprig and Pip felt proud, smart, and loved.

The End.
