# Panda Poppy’s Plan for Pancakes

Poppy was a patient panda. She lived in a warm kitchen with sun on the floor, soft rugs, and the sweet smell of cinnamon. The kettle hummed. The oven ticked. The wooden spoon felt smooth in Poppy’s paw. Today, Poppy wanted to make pancakes with friends.

“Friends make cooking more fun,” Poppy said, smiling. Her round ears wiggled with joy.

Poppy looked at the big recipe card. It said: “Plan, Prepare, Pour, Flip, Share.” She tapped each word with her paw. Tap, tap, tap. “A plan helps us work together,” she whispered.

She set out bowls, a whisk, and little aprons. The aprons were soft and warm from the sun. She lined them up in a neat row—red, blue, and yellow. Poppy liked neat rows. They made her feel calm.

Soon there was a knock. Knock, knock!

It was Tilly the tiny turtle. She moved slowly, with a shy smile. “Hi, Poppy,” Tilly said, peeking in. “I want to help.”

“You are just in time,” Poppy said. “We have a plan. We will do it step by step.”

Another knock. Knock, knock!

It was Benny the bouncy bunny. He hopped in, ears flopping. He sniffed the air. “Mmm! Pancakes!” Benny said. “I want to pour! I want to flip! I want to eat!”

Poppy took a deep breath. She felt excited too, but she stayed calm. “We all get a turn,” she said in her gentle voice. “We will plan together. That is how friends work.”

They gathered around the big table. The wood felt smooth under their paws. The bowl was cool and shiny. Poppy’s whisk made a soft swish-swish sound.

“Step one: Plan,” Poppy said. She made a chart on a paper with simple pictures. A spoon for mixing. A cup for pouring. A pan for flipping. A plate for sharing.

“Step two: Prepare,” Poppy said. “Tilly, will you crack the eggs? Benny, will you measure the flour? I will warm the pan.”

Tilly blinked. “I crack eggs slowly,” she said.

“That is okay,” Poppy said. “Slow can be good.”

Benny bounced. “I can do it fast!” He scooped a big heap of flour. Some puffed out. Poof! White dust floated in the warm air.

Poppy smiled and wiped the counter with a soft cloth. “It is okay,” she said. “We will try again. Let’s level the cup to the line.”

Benny watched closely. He held the cup steady. “Like this?” he asked.

“Yes,” Poppy said. “Great job, Benny! You are careful.”

Tilly tapped the egg. Crack. A little shell fell in. “Oh no,” Tilly whispered.

“We can fix it,” Poppy said kindly. She used a bigger shell to scoop out the tiny piece. “See? We help each other.”

The batter was ready—smooth, creamy, and sweet-smelling. Swish-swish went the whisk. The pan was warm. A tiny hiss sounded when Poppy poured the first pancake. Tssss.

“Step three: Pour,” Poppy said. “Benny, would you like to pour this time? Slow and steady.”

Benny nodded. He took a breath in… and out. He poured a circle. “I did it!” he said.

“Step four: Flip,” Poppy said. “Tilly, this is your turn.”

Tilly slid the spatula under the pancake. “I am slow,” she said.

“That is perfect,” Poppy said. “Slow helps us be safe.” Tilly lifted, turned, and flip! The pancake landed with a soft plop.

They made pancake after pancake. The kitchen smelled like warm vanilla. They listened to the gentle sizzle. They felt the cozy heat on their cheeks. They worked in order. They took turns. They waited patiently, and they cheered for each other.

Benny stopped bouncing. “Waiting is hard,” he said.

Poppy nodded. “Waiting shows care for friends,” she said. “We wait so everyone can help.”

“Step five: Share,” Poppy said at last. She put pancakes on plates. She added berries on top—blue, red, and purple. She drizzled a little honey that shone like gold.

They sat at the small table. The chairs made tiny creaks. Poppy held up her fork. “What did we learn?” she asked.

Benny said, “I learned to slow down and measure to the line.”

Tilly said, “I learned to be brave and flip, even if I am slow.”

Poppy said, “I learned that friends help and accept each other. With a plan and good organization, we make something yummy together.”

They clinked forks gently. Ting! They took bites. The pancakes were soft and fluffy. The berries popped sweet and tart.

“Friends, plan, and patience,” Poppy said with a smile. “That is our recipe.”

Benny giggled. “And pancakes!”

Tilly giggled too. “And kindness.”

They cleaned up together, humming a happy tune. Poppy folded the aprons and lined them up again—red, blue, and yellow. Everything was tidy, warm, and calm.

Poppy looked at her friends. “Thank you for cooking with me,” she said.

“Thank you for your plan,” said Tilly.

“Thank you for helping me wait,” said Benny.

Poppy’s heart felt full. “We are a great team,” she said. “When we plan, stay organized, and are patient with each other, we can do anything.”

The End.
