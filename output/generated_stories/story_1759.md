# The Gentle Beat of Roo

The musical practice room was bright and cozy. Soft rugs covered the floor. Shiny drums, tiny bells, and a sleepy piano sat in the corner. A joyful kangaroo joey named Roo hopped in with a happy thump-thump. Roo’s ears twitched. Roo’s nose wiggled. “I hear music!” Roo chirped.

“Hello, Roo,” said Ms. Melody, the music teacher. She had warm eyes and a scarf with little music notes. “Today we will listen, breathe, and play together.”

Roo clapped little paws. “I want to drum! Boom-boom-boom!”

Ms. Melody smiled. “We will all have a turn. First, we breathe. In…and out.” She touched a tiny chime. Ting!

Roo closed eyes. In…and out. The room smelled like lemon polish and soft wood. Roo felt calm.

Friends came in. There was Pip the pink parrot chick, Tilly the tiny turtle, and Max the mouse with bright whiskers. “Hi, friends!” Roo chirped.

“Hi, Roo!” they said.

Ms. Melody set a small drum in front of Roo. “This drum is for gentle hands,” she said. “Kind hands make kind sounds.”

Roo’s tail wiggled. “I can do that.” Tap…tap…tap. The drum sounded like soft raindrops. Roo smiled.

Pip shuffled to the little bells. “Jing-jing!” Pip sang.

Max sat by the piano keys. “Plink-plonk,” he said, giggling.

Tilly touched a calm, deep tone block. “Tok,” she whispered.

They played together, slow and sweet. Roo felt the gentle beat in the tummy. Boom…boom…like a happy heart.

Then Roo saw a big shiny drum in the corner. It looked like a full moon. “I want the big drum!” Roo hopped over. Thump, thump.

Ms. Melody raised a hand. “Wait, Roo. Let’s ask who needs it.”

Roo blinked. “Ask?”

“Kindness means we think of others,” Ms. Melody said. “We use our words. We listen.”

Roo looked at friends. Pip stared at the bells. Max tapped the keys. Tilly’s eyes were on the big drum. Tilly’s little head tucked in, then peeked out again.

Roo felt a warm flutter. “Tilly, do you want the big drum?” Roo asked softly.

Tilly nodded slowly. “Yes, please.”

Roo smiled. “You can go first.” Roo stepped back and put paws behind the back. The floor felt snug under Roo’s feet.

Tilly climbed to the big drum and touched it with soft, kind hands. Boom…booom. The sound filled the room like a big hug. “Thank you, Roo,” Tilly said.

“You’re welcome,” said Roo. Roo’s chest felt light and bright. Ms. Melody’s eyes crinkled with a happy spark.

They kept playing. Pip’s bells jingled like little stars. Max’s piano notes twinkled like raindrops. Tilly’s big drum boomed like slow thunder far away. Roo tapped the small drum and kept the gentle beat. Tap…tap…tap. Roo whispered, “Kind hands, kind sounds.”

Then a string on the tiny guitar went twang and twirl. Pip jumped. “Oh! That was loud!” Pip’s wings fluffed.

Roo saw Pip’s wide eyes. Roo remembered the breathing. “Let’s breathe together,” Roo said. “In…and out.”

Pip breathed in. Pip breathed out. Wings settled. “Thank you, Roo,” Pip chirped.

Max squeaked, “I dropped my music card.” He searched under a stool. “I can’t reach.”

Roo crouched low and peered into the soft shadows under the stool. “I see it!” Roo reached with gentle paws and pulled the little blue card out. “Here you go.”

Max’s whiskers wiggled with joy. “Thanks, Roo! That was kind.”

Roo felt warm again. Kindness made the room glow. Roo noticed the glow felt like sunshine in the heart.

Ms. Melody clapped. “Friends, listen.” Everyone paused. The room grew quiet. Roo heard the tiny hum of the lights. Roo smelled lemon and a hint of chalk. Roo felt the drum skin cool under paws.

“When we pause and listen,” Ms. Melody said, “we practice mindfulness. Mindfulness helps us notice our friends and our feelings. It helps us choose kindness.”

Roo nodded. “I can listen. I can choose.”

Ms. Melody passed around rainbow scarves. “Let’s make a kindness song. We will play soft and share turns.”

Roo watched Tilly start with the big drum: boom…boom. Pip added a little jingle: jing…jing. Max played two shiny notes: plink-plink. Roo waited, breathing in and out. Then Roo added gentle taps: tap…tap. The sounds fit together like puzzle pieces.

Roo looked at Tilly and smiled. Tilly smiled back. Roo swished the rainbow scarf slow and smooth. Colors waved like a quiet sunset. Roo felt proud and calm.

At the end, Ms. Melody touched the chime. Ting! “How did that feel?” she asked.

“Safe,” said Tilly.

“Happy,” said Pip.

“Cozy,” said Max.

Roo hugged the small drum. “It felt kind,” Roo said. “When we are kind, the music is kind too.”

Ms. Melody nodded. “Yes, Roo. Kindness is a gentle beat in our hearts. Mindfulness helps us hear it. Compassion helps us share it.”

Roo put a paw on the drum and closed eyes. In…and out. Roo thought, Today I shared, I helped, and I listened. The thoughts felt soft and sweet.

As they packed up, Roo hopped to Tilly. “Would you like the big drum next time too?” Roo asked.

Tilly smiled shyly. “Let’s take turns,” Tilly said. “You can go first, and then me.”

Roo’s tail made a happy swish. “Deal!”

They all waved goodbye. The room was calm and bright. Roo hopped out, paws light, heart steady. Tap…tap…tap. Roo whispered, “Kind hands, kind sounds, kind friends.”

The End.
