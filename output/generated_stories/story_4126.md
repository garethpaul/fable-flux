# Gemma the Giraffe Makes It Cozy

Gemma the giraffe had the coziest bedroom. Her tall window let warm sunbeams slide in like golden ribbons. Her soft blanket felt like a cloud, and her favorite stars-and-moons rug tickled her hooves. She loved to look around and think. Gemma was a very thoughtful giraffe.

One morning, Gemma woke up and stretched her long neck. “Good morning, room,” she whispered. She heard the gentle hum of the fan. She smelled clean soap from the laundry basket. She saw her toys—blocks, books, and a tiny tea set—spread all over the floor like a busy parade.

“Oh dear,” said Gemma, tilting her head. “It looks messy.” She wanted to play tea party, but there was no space on the rug. Her puzzle pieces were under the bed like little moon rocks. Her blanket was bunched up like a mountain.

Mama Giraffe peeked in and smiled. “Good morning, Gemma. Time for morning chores,” she said softly. “We make the bed, put toys in bins, and place books on the shelf.”

Gemma’s ears twitched. “But Mama,” she said, “I want to play now.”

Mama nodded. “I hear you. Play is fun. Chores help us feel calm and cozy. Let’s choose one small thing to start.”

Gemma took a deep breath. She felt a tiny knot in her tummy. “I feel a little worried. It looks like a lot.”

Mama leaned close and nuzzled her cheek. “You are brave. You can do hard things, one step at a time.”

Gemma looked at her blanket mountain. “I can make my bed,” she decided. She pulled the blanket up. Swish. She smoothed it with her hooves. Smooth, smooth. The blanket smelled like sunshine. “I did it!” she cheered.

“That’s step one,” Mama said. “What’s next?”

Gemma looked at the floor. Blocks in bright colors made a rainbow trail. “Blocks go in the red bin,” Gemma said. She picked up one block. Clunk. Then another. Clunk. Her legs felt a little tired, but she kept going. “I am strong and steady,” she told herself. “I can finish.”

After a few trips, the rug peeked out, blue and soft. Gemma smiled. “I can see the moon on my rug again!”

“Beautiful progress,” Mama said. “Would you like a water sip?”

“Yes, please,” Gemma said. She took a cool sip. It felt like tiny bubbles on her tongue. She took a small rest and looked around. There were still books and puzzle pieces.

Just then, her little brother, Gus, toddled in. “Play?” he asked, reaching for the puzzle pieces.

Gemma stood tall. She took another deep breath. “Gus,” she said kindly but firmly, “I’m doing my chores. I will play after. Please wait, and please don’t scatter the pieces.”

Gus blinked. He reached again.

Gemma put a gentle hoof near the puzzle. “No, Gus. Not now,” she said in a clear voice. “You can help me put the pieces in the box. Then we can play tea party.”

Gus’s face crumpled, then smoothed. “Help,” he agreed.

“Thank you,” Gemma said. She showed him where the pieces went. Plink, plink, plink. The sound was like soft raindrops in a tin can. Gus giggled. Plink!

Soon the puzzle was safe. Gemma lined up her books. The covers felt smooth and cool. She ran her hoof over the letters. “Books stand tall on the shelf,” she sang. “Tall like a giraffe on the savanna!”

When they finished, Gemma looked around. The sunbeams made bright squares on the tidy rug. Her bed was smooth. Her toys were tucked in their bins like happy bears in a cave. The room felt calm. Gemma felt calm too.

“I did it,” she whispered. She felt warm and proud inside her chest, like holding a cup of cocoa.

Mama clapped softly. “You showed resilience,” she said. “You kept going, even when it felt big. You were assertive with Gus, and you were kind. I am proud of you.”

Gemma nodded. “Chores make our room cozy. I like that.” She looked at Gus. “Thank you for helping, Gus.”

Gus hugged her leg. “Cozy,” he said.

Mama smiled. “Now there is time to play.”

Gemma set up the tiny tea set on the clean rug. The cups clicked together. Click, click. She poured pretend tea. “Would you like more, Gus?” she asked.

“Yes, please,” said Gus, and he slurped with a happy “Mmmm!”

Gemma laughed. The fan hummed. The room smelled like soap and sunshine. Everything felt just right.

Before nap time, Gemma thought again. “Sometimes chores feel hard,” she said, stroking the smooth blanket. “But I can do them step by step. I can say what I need. I can ask for help. And I can accept that some days are messier, and that’s okay. I can tidy again.”

Mama kissed her head. “That is a wise giraffe.”

Gemma snuggled into bed. “Tomorrow, I will do my morning chores,” she whispered. “My room will be cozy, and my heart will be calm.”

And with that, Gemma and Gus drifted to sleep, their cozy bedroom neat and peaceful, ready for more sunny days and tiny tea parties.

The End.
