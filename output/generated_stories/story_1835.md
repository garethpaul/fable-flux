# Splash in the Pantry

Splash was a playful dolphin who loved words. He loved the whoosh of wind, the swish of water, and the hush of quiet rooms. Today, Splash wiggled his fins and clicked happily. He was helping his friends plan a picnic.

But the ocean was having a windy day. “Whoooosh,” sang the waves.

Splash blinked. “We need snacks,” he said. “Let’s check the pantry!”

The pantry was a cozy room on a little sea farm, right by the shore. It was cool and smelled like warm oats and sweet apples. Jars lined the shelves—click, clack—when the door opened. Splash slid in carefully on a soft, wet mat so he wouldn’t drip too much.

His friends were there: Tilly the turtle, Benny the bunny, and Nori the little crab. They all wore tiny aprons. They giggled when Splash made bubbly dolphin sounds.

“Eee-eee! Hello!” Splash chirped.

“Hi, Splash,” said Tilly, smiling. “Let’s get our picnic food. We’ll be ready in no time.”

They looked around the pantry. They saw jars of oats, a basket of apples, a bowl of carrots, and a box of cups. There was also a big jug of water and a neat stack of napkins.

Splash wiggled. “We need oat bars and apple slices. Yum!” He loved saying the words slowly. “Oat. Apple. Slice. Crunch.”

Benny hopped to a low shelf. “Uh-oh,” he said softly. “The oat jar lid is stuck.”

Nori clacked her little claws. “And… and… the apples are at the very top! We can’t reach!” The apples sat in a basket on a high shelf. The shelf creaked a tiny bit—cree-eeak.

Splash felt a flutter in his tummy. His friends looked worried. The wind outside went whoooosh again. If they didn’t get food soon, the picnic might be too late.

Splash took a deep breath. “We can do this,” he said. “We will be brave. We will be kind. We will use our brains.”

Tilly nodded. “Peaceful plan time?”

“Peaceful plan time,” Splash agreed. He loved saying plan words. “First, we talk. Then, we try.”

They all sat on a soft rug in the pantry. The room was cool. The floor felt smooth. The air smelled like cinnamon.

Splash spoke in a calm voice. “What is our problem?”

Benny held up the jar. “Stuck lid.”

Nori pointed up. “High apples.”

Tilly added, “We want to be safe. No climbing high. No breaking jars.”

Splash clapped his fins gently. “Yes! Safe is smart. We will be resourceful. That means we use what we have.”

He looked around. “We have a wooden spoon. We have a towel. We have a step stool.” He read the labels out loud, slowly, because he liked the sound. “Spoon. Tow-el. Stool.”

Benny’s ears perked up. “What do we do first?”

“Step one,” said Splash. “Breathe in. Breathe out.” They all breathed. In. Out. Calm.

“Step two,” Splash continued. “For the lid: wrap the towel around the lid for grip.” He held the towel with a fin. Tilly held the jar steady with her strong turtle feet. Benny turned the lid—slowly, steadily.

“Twist, twist,” Nori whispered.

POP! The lid opened. The room filled with the toasty smell of oats. “We did it!” said Tilly.

“Step three,” said Splash, smiling. “For the apples: we do not climb high. We keep our world safe. We use the stool and the spoon to bring the basket low, slowly.”

They set the stool down. It went tap-tap on the floor. Nori checked that it was steady. “Steady,” she said.

Benny hopped up. He was still a little short.

“I can help,” said Splash. He placed the wooden spoon under the basket handle like a friendly lever. “We will gently slide, not poke.”

“Together,” Tilly said. “One, two, three.”

Benny held the spoon. Tilly held the stool. Nori guided from below with her small claws ready to catch. Splash kept his voice soft and steady. “Slow. Low. Safe.” The basket slid, squeak, squeak, right into Nori’s tiny claws.

“We got it!” Nori cheered. Apples smelled sweet, like sunshine.

They giggled and clapped. The pantry felt warm and happy now. The wind outside still went whoooosh, but inside, their plan went whoosh in a gentle way too.

“Now we prepare,” said Splash. “Preparation means we get things ready.” He liked that long word. “Pre-pa-ra-tion.” He smiled.

They washed the apples with clean water. Splash hummed a bubbly tune. “Splish, splash, swish.” They dried them with the towel and sliced them safely with a kid-friendly cutter Tilly found in a low drawer. Chop, chop—soft and slow.

Benny measured oats. Tilly stirred with the wooden spoon. Nori sprinkled tiny raisins. Splash read the labels out loud. “Oats. Raisins. Water. Mix.” He loved how words felt like friends.

“Let’s also care for our world,” said Splash. He pointed to the recycle basket and the compost bin. “Peels go to compost. Paper goes to recycle.” They sorted everything neatly. Nori tapped the compost lid. “Good for the earth,” she said.

When the oat bars set and the apples gleamed, Splash packed napkins and cups. “We are ready,” he said. “We planned. We prepared. We were kind. We were careful.”

They went to the door. The wind had softened to a gentle shhhh. Splash felt proud. His heart went thump-thump in a happy way.

On the shore, they had their picnic. Crunch went the apples. Mmm went the oat bars. They shared, they laughed, and they said thank you.

Benny smiled. “We solved our problem.”

Tilly nodded. “We used what we had.”

Nori clacked softly. “We kept it safe and clean.”

Splash beamed. “We were resourceful. We talked calmly. We prepared well. And we cared for our world.”

He looked at his friends. “That is our lesson: When a problem pops up, breathe, talk kindly, look around, and use what you have. Prepare, stay safe, and take care of the earth.”

Everyone cheered. The pantry door made a happy click as it closed, like it was saying, “Good job.”

The End.
