# Gigi the Giraffe Says “I’m Sorry”

In a sunny, comfortable nursery, soft rugs hugged the floor, and colorful blocks made little towers. The room smelled like fresh crayons and warm soap. A gentle hum from a tiny fan whispered, “Whirr, whirr.” In the corner sat Gigi, a thoughtful giraffe with long lashes and a long, long neck. She loved to think, and she loved to write.

Gigi held a chunky blue crayon in her hoof. “I will write a kind note today,” she said. “I will practice my letters.” She dipped her head low to the small table. “Ssss… a circle… a line,” she whispered, smiling.

Her friends were nearby. Benny the bunny bounced on a cushion. Mia the mouse stacked soft blocks. “Hi, Gigi!” Benny said. “What are you writing?”

“A happy note,” Gigi said proudly. “It will say, ‘Hi, friend!’”

Mia smiled. “I like notes,” she squeaked. “They make me feel seen.”

Gigi felt warm and glad. She wrote slowly, pressing hard. The crayon made a squeaky sound. Squeak, squeak, squeak.

Then Gigi saw an empty wall, smooth and big and white. It was right by the table. “Hmm,” Gigi thought. “It is so big! I can write a big H! I can draw a big sun!” Her neck stretched up, up, up. She felt excited and forgot to think about rules.

Squeeeak! The crayon slid across the wall. Gigi drew a huge smiling sun. She wrote a giant H. She swished a bright blue rainbow. “Look!” Gigi laughed. “So tall!”

Benny stopped bouncing. Mia stopped stacking. Their eyes grew round.

“Gigi,” Benny whispered, “the wall is not paper.”

Mia’s whiskers trembled. “We only write on paper,” she said softly. “Miss Poppy said so, for safety.”

Just then, Miss Poppy, the kind nursery teacher, walked in. She smelled like oranges and wore a soft, green sweater. She looked at the wall. Her eyes were calm, but her face was serious. “Oh, Gigi,” she said gently. “What happened?”

Gigi’s tummy felt wobbly, like jelly. Her ears grew hot. “I… I wanted to write a big note,” she said in a small voice. “I forgot the rule.”

Miss Poppy knelt down. “Thank you for telling me,” she said. “Walls are not for writing. Crayons belong on paper. Writing on walls can be unsafe. Paint can chip. Little pieces can fall. And others might copy and get hurt or make a big mess.”

Gigi’s heart thumped. She looked at the blue on the wall. She looked at the blue on her hoof. She took a deep breath. “Miss Poppy,” she said, “I am sorry. I am truly sorry for writing on the wall. I will fix it.”

Miss Poppy nodded. “That is a kind apology,” she said. “An apology has three parts: say ‘I’m sorry,’ say what you did, and ask how to make it better.”

Gigi stood tall and tried again. “I’m sorry I wrote on the wall. How can I make it better and safe?”

Benny and Mia listened. The fan went whirr, whirr.

Miss Poppy smiled. “We can clean together,” she said. “And we can make a safe writing spot.”

Gigi felt hope sparkle like tiny stars. “Yes, please,” she said.

They put on small aprons and gentle, safe cleaning gloves. The gloves felt cool. The cloth felt squishy. Swoosh, swoosh, they wiped the wall. The blue faded to white. Gigi worked carefully, slow and steady. “I made a mistake,” she said, “and I am fixing it.”

Benny held the trash bin. Mia sang a soft song. “Wipe and rinse, wipe and rinse,” she hummed.

When the wall was clean, Miss Poppy brought out big sheets of paper and some sticky tape. “Let’s make a writing wall,” she said. “Paper on the wall keeps you safe and keeps the room clean.”

They taped the paper low and high. The paper crinkled softly. Gigi felt the smooth paper with her hoof. “It feels nice,” she said.

Miss Poppy gave Gigi a fresh crayon. “Now,” she said, “what will you write?”

Gigi thought carefully. She looked at her friends. She looked at the clean wall and the new paper. She took a breath that smelled like orange and crayon. “I will write an apology note,” she said.

She wrote slowly, with love:

“Dear friends,
I am sorry I wrote on the wall.
I will write only on paper.
Love, Gigi”

She added a small sun on the paper, not the wall. Benny clapped. Mia smiled. “That note makes me feel safe,” Mia said. “Thank you.”

Gigi felt lighter, like a balloon in the sky. “When I say sorry and fix my mistake,” she said, “I learn and grow.”

Miss Poppy nodded. “That is the lesson,” she said. “We say sorry, we fix it, and we choose a safe way.”

The fan whispered. The crayons clicked in their box. The nursery felt warm and kind.

Gigi looked at her friends. “Does anyone want to write with me?” she asked.

“Yes!” Benny said. “Let’s write happy notes!”

“Yes!” Mia said. “Let’s write safe and sweet.”

Together they wrote kind words on paper hearts. They taped them to the paper wall. The room filled with soft colors and soft smiles.

Gigi tucked her long neck around her friends. “I’m glad we learned together,” she said.

The End.
