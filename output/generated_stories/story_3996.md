# Gigi’s Closet Dance Plan

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with long legs, kind eyes, and a soft, warm smile. She loved to think, to listen, and to learn. One rainy day, big raindrops drummed on the roof like a drumbeat. Tap-tap, tap-tap-tap. Gigi’s ears twitched. “That sounds like music,” she whispered.

Gigi found a cozy closet hideaway in her house. The closet was small for a giraffe, but it felt safe and snug. There were soft scarves on a shelf, a glow-y nightlight, and a basket of buttons that clicked and clacked. The air smelled like clean cotton and a little bit like peppermint from a stray candy wrapper. Gigi liked it there. It was her quiet thinking place.

“I want to practice dancing,” Gigi said to herself. “I want to learn a new dance for my friends.” She lifted one hoof and then another. But the closet was tiny. Her long legs bumped a broom. Thump. Her head touched a hanger. Clink. The floor was slippery. Whoops!

Gigi felt a little worried. “How can I dance in this small space?” she asked. “I am so tall.” She took a deep breath. In, out. The nightlight glowed like a little moon. The rain kept tapping a beat. Tap-tap, tap-tap-tap.

“I will use what I have,” Gigi decided. “I can be resourceful.” She liked that word. It meant, “I can find a smart way.”

First, Gigi made space. She gently moved the broom and leaned it in the corner. She pushed the basket of buttons to the side. The buttons made a happy clackety sound. “Thank you, buttons,” Gigi giggled. “You can be my maracas.”

Next, Gigi looked at the scarves. They were soft, silky, and bright. One was sunny yellow. One was ocean blue. One was green like a leaf. “These can help me learn,” she said. She tied the yellow scarf to the door handle. She tied the blue scarf to a low hook. She tied the green scarf to a shelf. “Now I have dance spots.”

She tapped each scarf with a hoof. “Yellow, step. Blue, slide. Green, turn.” She sang the words softly to the rain’s rhythm. Tap-tap, tap-tap-tap. “Yellow, step. Blue, slide. Green, turn.”

Gigi tried again. She lifted her front hoof to yellow. Step. She slid her back hoof to blue. Slide. She turned her body toward green. Turn. Her neck bobbed gently. Her tail swished. The hanger did not clink this time. The broom stayed quiet. Gigi smiled. “It works!”

But then the floor felt slippery again. “I need grip,” Gigi said. She looked around. She saw a dry towel folded on a shelf. She placed the towel on the floor like a little mat. She pressed it flat with her careful hooves. “There,” she said. “Safe toes.”

Gigi listened to the rain. Tap-tap, tap-tap-tap. She tapped the buttons in the basket with her hoof. Clack, clack, clack-clack. “That can be my drum,” she said. She liked the sound. It was bright and cheerful.

She made a plan. “I will learn in small steps,” she told herself. “First, slow. Then, a little faster.” She took a breath and counted softly. “One, two, three.” She stepped to yellow. “One.” She slid to blue. “Two.” She turned to green. “Three.” She did it again. And again. Each time, she felt braver and steadier. Her heart felt light, like a balloon.

Soon, Gigi added arm moves. She lifted a scarf with her mouth and let it float down. Whoosh. It tickled her nose. “Hee-hee!” she giggled. She bent her knees a tiny bit. Bend. She stretched her neck tall. Stretch. She swayed her hips slow, like a tree in soft wind. Sway.

Knock, knock. A small voice called, “Gigi, what are you doing?” It was her friend, Pip the mouse.

“I am learning a dance in my closet hideaway,” Gigi said. “I used scarves for spots, a towel for grip, and buttons for drums. I made a plan.”

Pip peeked in. His whiskers wiggled. “It smells like peppermint,” he said, smiling. “Can I watch?”

“Yes,” Gigi said. “And you can learn too. You can dance in small spaces when you have a smart plan.”

Gigi showed the steps. “Yellow, step. Blue, slide. Green, turn.” Pip followed with tiny feet. Step. Slide. Turn. The buttons went clackety-clack, the rain went tap-tap-tap, and the nightlight made the scarves glow.

Pip clapped. “Gigi, you are so clever,” he said.

Gigi felt warm and proud. “I used what I had,” she said. “I solved my problem.”

They tried a new move. Gigi said, “What if we add a clap?” Pip nodded. Step. Slide. Turn. Clap! The sound was crisp and happy. They laughed. They tried fast. They tried slow. They learned together, listening, counting, and smiling.

At last, the rain grew soft. The closet felt cozy and bright. Gigi bowed. Pip bowed too. They both felt calm and glad.

Gigi said, “Today I learned that I can make a plan, use what I have, and keep trying. That is being resourceful.” She touched the yellow scarf. “Step.” She touched the blue scarf. “Slide.” She touched the green scarf. “Turn.” Then she touched her heart. “And be kind to yourself while you learn.”

Pip nodded. “Resourceful, dance, and learning,” he said. “We did all three!”

Gigi smiled her soft, warm smile. “Yes. You can dance anywhere when you think, plan, and try.”

The End.
