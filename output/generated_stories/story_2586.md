# Bubbles in the Quiet Attic

Bubbles was a playful dolphin with shiny gray skin and a happy flip-flop tail. One day, she swam up a wiggly rainbow puddle and—whoosh!—she popped into a quiet attic. The attic smelled like dry wood and old lemon polish. Dust sparkles danced in sunlight that peeked through a tiny window.

“Oh!” Bubbles chirped. “What a new place! I want to explore!”

The attic was calm and still. Boxes were stacked like tall brown castles. A soft blue trunk sat in the corner. A yellow lamp with a string stood on a stool. The floor creaked gently under Bubbles’ smooth belly as she scooted along.

Bubbles loved to play. She loved to try new things. But today, her tummy felt fluttery. Her eyes were big with curiosity. “What’s inside the blue trunk? What’s under the boxes?” she asked herself.

A small voice answered from the window. “Hello! I am Breezy, the attic breeze.” The air brushed Bubbles’ fin like a cool feather. “I like when visitors come,” Breezy whispered. “But we must be careful in the attic.”

“Careful?” Bubbles blinked. “Why?”

“Because the attic is quiet,” Breezy said, “and quiet things can hide little dangers. We use safe steps and gentle moves.”

Bubbles nodded, but her playful tail swish-swish-swished. She scooted to the tallest box castle. A wobbly ladder leaned against it.

“I want to see the top!” Bubbles said. “I can climb fast!”

“Wait,” Breezy said softly. “Let’s make a plan. Safety first.”

Bubbles stopped. Her tummy flutter slowed. “A plan? Like what?”

Breezy tickled the lamp’s string. The lamp clicked and glowed warm and yellow. “First, we turn on the light. We need to see.”

“Oooh,” Bubbles said. The attic looked safer and kinder with light. Shadows ran away.

“Second,” Breezy continued, “we make sure the ladder is steady.”

Bubbles pressed her fin on the ladder. Creak. Wiggle. “It shakes,” she said.

“Then we don’t climb,” Breezy said. “We choose another way.”

Bubbles took a deep breath. She listened to her heart. Thump-thump. “Okay,” she said. “Safety first.”

She looked around and saw a small step stool with rubber feet. It felt firm and friendly. Bubbles asked, “Is this a safe helper?”

“Yes,” Breezy hummed. “And we keep our body slow and our eyes open.”

Bubbles placed the stool near a short stack of boxes. She moved one small box at a time, sliding it towards the floor. “One,” she counted. “Two.” The boxes smelled like old blankets and crayons. She heard a tiny jingle inside. “Three.”

“You are being careful,” Breezy said. “You are understanding your space.”

Bubbles smiled. “I am completing my task!” she chirped.

Inside the top box was a soft helmet with a bright pink stripe. It had a chin strap like a hug.

“Oh!” Bubbles squeaked. “A head hat! For safety?”

Breezy giggled. “Yes. Try it.”

Bubbles snugged the helmet on. Click! It felt comfy. “I care for me,” she said. “This is self-care!”

With her helmet on and the lamp glowing, Bubbles gently opened the blue trunk. The hinges went squeeeak. Inside were colorful ribbons, a tiny first-aid kit, cozy gloves, and a list taped to the lid.

The list said:

- Turn on light
- Ask for help if needed
- Move slowly
- Wear gear
- Put things back

Bubbles traced the letters with her fin. “A safety list. I can follow it!”

She picked up the ribbons and smiled at their soft, silky feel. “I want to make a ribbon dance,” she said.

“Let’s make a clear space,” Breezy suggested. “We keep the floor tidy so we don’t trip.”

Bubbles nodded. She pushed empty boxes to the wall, lined up the trunk, and rolled the stool away. She took a sip from a little water bottle in the trunk, then wiped a bit of dust off her nose. “Self-care break,” she giggled.

Then she twirled the ribbons slowly. Swish. Swish. Colors painted quiet rainbows in the warm light. Bubbles laughed. “I’m having fun, and I’m safe!”

Suddenly, the lamp flickered. The yellow string wiggled. Bubbles felt worried. “Is the lamp okay?” she asked.

“Let’s pause,” Breezy whispered. “Safety check.”

Bubbles stopped her dance. She clicked off the lamp. The attic grew softer again, but not too dark, because the window gave gentle light. She looked at the lamp’s cord. It was tucked under the stool leg.

“Oh,” Bubbles said. “The cord got pinched. That is not safe.”

“You noticed a problem,” Breezy said. “Now we solve it.”

Bubbles lifted the stool and freed the cord. She placed the cord along the wall. She made a little path with tape from the trunk. “There,” she said. “No pinches. No trips.”

She turned the lamp back on. The light came steady and calm.

Bubbles beamed. “I did it! I was curious, and I was careful. I understood the rules. I finished my tasks. I took care of me.”

Breezy swirled around her like a hug. “You learned the attic way.”

Bubbles put the ribbons, helmet, and kit back in the trunk. She closed the lid gently. She checked the list and smiled. “All done,” she said.

The window winked with late sunshine. Bubbles waved her fin. “Thank you, Breezy. Safety makes play more fun.”

“Anytime,” the breeze replied, soft as a lullaby.

Bubbles slid back down the rainbow puddle. Splash! She swam in small circles, happy and proud. Her heart felt calm and brave.

Today, Bubbles learned: Be curious, make a plan, move slow, and check for safety. When we care for ourselves and finish our tasks, playtime is bright and kind.

The End.
