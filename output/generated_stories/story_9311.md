# Benny's Big Build

In the quiet attic of an old farmhouse, a little beaver named Benny had made his home. The attic was cozy and peaceful, with dusty sunbeams dancing through the small window. Benny loved it there because it was the perfect place to practice building.

Benny wanted to be the best builder in the whole world, just like his papa. But every time he tried to stack his wooden blocks, they tumbled down with a loud CRASH!

"Oh no!" Benny squeaked as his tower fell again. His little paws felt shaky, and his eyes filled with tears. "I'll never be a good builder!"

Mrs. Mouse, who lived in the corner of the attic, poked her tiny head out. She had been watching Benny try and try all morning.

"What's wrong, little one?" she asked kindly, her whiskers twitching.

"I keep practicing and practicing, but my towers always fall down," Benny sniffled. "Maybe I should just give up."

Mrs. Mouse scurried over and patted Benny's furry paw. "Oh, dear Benny. Building takes lots and lots of practice. Even your papa had to practice when he was little."

"Really?" Benny's ears perked up.

"Really! Let me show you something." Mrs. Mouse pointed to a corner where spider webs glistened. "See how Spider practices her weaving every single day? Some webs break, but she keeps trying."

Benny looked around the attic with new eyes. He saw how the old wooden beams fit together perfectly. He noticed how patient the dust motes were as they danced slowly in the warm sunlight.

"Will you help me practice?" Benny asked hopefully.

"Of course!" Mrs. Mouse smiled. "But remember, we must be fair. If I help you, you can help me gather seeds later."

"That sounds perfect!" Benny clapped his little paws together.

Mrs. Mouse taught Benny to start with just two blocks. "Feel how steady they are," she said. "Listen to the soft 'click' when they fit together."

Benny took a deep breath. The attic smelled like old wood and sunshine. He carefully placed one block, then another. They stayed!

"I did it!" Benny whispered, not wanting to disturb the quiet peace of the attic.

"Now try three," Mrs. Mouse encouraged.

Block by block, Benny practiced. Sometimes the towers fell, and that was okay. Each time, he took a deep breath, felt the smooth wood in his paws, and tried again.

After many tries, Benny built a tower five blocks high! It wobbled just a little but stayed standing.

"Look, Mrs. Mouse! I'm getting better!" Benny's tail wiggled with joy.

"Yes, you are! Practice makes us stronger and better," Mrs. Mouse said proudly.

Just then, they heard a tiny cry from below. A baby bird had fallen into the attic and couldn't find his way out.

"I know how to help!" Benny said. Using his new building skills, he carefully stacked blocks to make steps leading to the window. The baby bird hopped up each step and flew safely outside.

"Thank you, Benny!" chirped the bird.

Benny felt warm and happy inside. His practice had helped someone else!

That evening, as the attic grew golden with sunset light, Benny helped Mrs. Mouse gather seeds, just as he had promised. They worked together quietly, sharing the peaceful space.

"Thank you for teaching me about practice and patience," Benny said.

"And thank you for being fair and helping others," Mrs. Mouse replied.

From that day on, Benny practiced building every day in the quiet attic. Sometimes his towers fell, but he never gave up. He learned that practice, patience, and helping others made him feel wonderful inside.

The End.
