# Koa the Koala’s Porch Puzzle Day

Koa the calm koala loved mornings on the peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like warm eucalyptus tea. The wooden floor felt smooth under Koa’s fuzzy feet. Birds chirped softly, and a cool breeze tickled Koa’s ears.

Koa took a slow breath in. “Smell the tea,” Koa whispered. Koa blew a soft breath out. “Blow a cloud.” This was Koa’s favorite mindful game.

“Good morning, Koa!” called Neighbor Nell, a friendly squirrel, from the walkway. Her little tail swished fast. “We have a problem!”

Koa sat up tall. “Let’s listen.” Koa’s voice was gentle. “What is the problem, Nell?”

Nell hopped closer. “The community snack stand is closed! The sign blew away. The jars look mixed up. And the littles are coming for snack time soon!”

Koa blinked slowly. “We can help,” Koa said. “Helping is good for our street.” Koa placed the tea cup down and patted the porch. “Let’s think first.”

Nell wiggled. “Think first, then do?”

“Yes,” Koa smiled. “We will use our curious brains.” Koa stood and stretched. “Step one: be calm. Step two: look and listen. Step three: make a plan.”

Nell took a deep breath with Koa. “Smell the tea,” Nell said, smiling. “Blow a cloud.”

Together, they walked to the snack stand at the end of the porch. The stand had small jars of nuts, berries, and crackers. A jar wobbled on the edge. A tiny ant marched by. A paper string hung loose where a sign should be.

“Clue one,” said Koa. “The sign string is loose.” Koa touched it gently. “Clue two: a wobbly jar. That is not safe.” Koa pointed to a tiny trail of crumbs. “Clue three: crumbs.”

Nell’s eyes got big. “What does it mean?”

Koa tapped a soft rhythm on the porch rail. Tap-tap, think-think. “Hmm. What happened first? The wind blew. The sign blew away.” Koa nodded. “What happened next? The jars slid, and one wobbled. The crumbs tell us some snacks may have spilled.”

Nell clapped. “You are a good thinker!”

“We are good thinkers together,” Koa said. “Now we make a plan.”

Koa raised one furry finger. “Plan part one: make the area safe. Move the wobbly jar.” Koa slid the jar to the center. The glass made a tiny clink.

Koa raised a second finger. “Plan part two: find the missing sign.” Koa and Nell looked around. The breeze whispered. A paper rustled under the porch bench.

“I see it!” Nell scooted under the bench and pulled out the sign. It said, “Community Snack Stand—Please Share.”

Koa smiled. “Plan part three: fix the sign so it stays.” Koa found a bit of string and a clip in the porch toolbox. “We need strong ties.” Koa showed Nell how to loop and clip. “Like a hug for the sign.”

While they worked, Little Pip the possum peeked up the steps. “Is snack time soon?” Pip asked, holding a tiny cup.

“Soon,” said Koa. “We are fixing the stand. Want to help?”

Pip nodded. “I can sweep!”

“Great idea,” Koa said. “Mindful sweeping helps everyone.” Koa showed Pip how to sweep slow and steady. Swish, swish. The broom made a soft sound like rain.

Nell sorted the jars by picture labels. “Berries here. Nuts here. Crackers here.”

Koa noticed a small puddle of sticky berry juice near the edge. Koa paused to think. “Sticky floors can be tricky. What can we do?”

“Wipe it up!” Pip said.

“Yes,” Koa smiled. “And then dry it.” They wiped and patted until the wood felt smooth again.

The breeze cooled their cheeks. The porch smelled sweet now, like berries and tea. Koa took another slow breath. “Smell the tea. Blow a cloud.”

Nell held up the sign. “All fixed!”

Koa looked at the stand. “Let’s check our plan. Safe jars? Yes. Clean floor? Yes. Strong sign? Yes.” Koa nodded. “We solved it.”

Soon, neighbors lined up: a blue jay with a tiny bag, a turtle with a little bow, and a bunny with bright eyes. Koa greeted each one. “Please take a small snack and share with a friend.”

The blue jay chirped, “Thank you for fixing the stand!”

“It was a team job,” Koa said. “We used our calm minds and kind hands.”

Pip shared crackers with the bunny. “One for you, one for me,” Pip said. They giggled at the crunchy sound.

Nell leaned on the rail. “Koa, what if something goes wrong next time?”

Koa’s eyes were warm. “We can use our steps again. Be calm. Look and listen. Make a plan. Try it. Check it. That is critical thinking.”

Nell tried the words. “Be calm. Look and listen. Make a plan. Try it. Check it.”

The sun peeked from behind a cloud. The porch felt cozy and safe. Koa poured a fresh cup of warm tea. “Our street is happier when we help,” Koa said. “That is community service.”

“And breathing helps me think,” Pip added, taking a slow breath.

Koa smiled. “Yes, breathing helps our brains. Mindfulness helps us notice clues.”

They all sat for a moment, quiet and peaceful. The birds sang a soft song. The wind hummed. Their hearts felt light.

Koa raised the cup. “To helping together, and to thinking with care.”

“Together!” everyone cheered.

The End.
