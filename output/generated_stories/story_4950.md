# Nutkin's Big Art Project

Nutkin the squirrel sat in his cozy study corner. His fluffy gray tail twitched with excitement. Tomorrow was Art Day at forest school, and he wanted to make something special.

"I need to plan!" Nutkin said, looking at his empty desk. The wooden surface felt smooth under his tiny paws. Sunlight streamed through the window, making dust particles dance in the air.

Nutkin's friend Chippy hopped by. "Just grab some leaves and sticks tomorrow," Chippy chirped. "That's what I always do."

But Nutkin shook his head firmly. "No, I want to be ready. Good art takes planning!"

Nutkin opened his notebook. The pages smelled like fresh paper. He drew pictures of what he might create. First, he sketched a colorful leaf collage. Then he drew a twig sculpture. His pencil made soft scratching sounds on the paper.

"What supplies do I need?" Nutkin asked himself. He made a list in his neat squirrel handwriting:
- Bright red leaves
- Smooth brown acorns  
- Bendy twigs
- Sweet-smelling pine cones
- Sparkly morning dew

Nutkin looked at his list. His whiskers twitched with determination. "Time to gather everything!"

Outside, the forest smelled like crisp autumn air. Nutkin scampered from tree to tree. He found perfect red leaves that felt papery in his paws. He collected round acorns that made clicking sounds in his pouch.

"These twigs are just right," Nutkin said, bending them gently. They curved like tiny rainbows. He spotted pine cones that smelled like Christmas morning.

Back in his study corner, Nutkin arranged everything carefully. The red leaves made a rustling sound as he sorted them by size. The acorns rolled smoothly across his desk. The twigs felt flexible and strong.

"Now I can practice," Nutkin said proudly. He tried making different designs. His paws worked quickly and carefully. The materials felt wonderful as he arranged them into patterns.

The next morning at forest school, all the animals gathered for Art Day. Teacher Owl hooted, "Time to create your masterpieces!"

Chippy looked around frantically. "Oh no! I forgot to bring anything!"

Nutkin opened his well-organized art kit. Everything was exactly where it should be. "Would you like to share my supplies?" Nutkin asked kindly.

"Really?" Chippy's eyes grew wide. "You have so much!"

"I prepared extra," Nutkin smiled. "Good planning means helping friends too!"

Together, they created a beautiful woodland scene. Nutkin's red leaves became autumn trees. His acorns rolled into perfect little animals. The twigs formed a cozy log cabin. Pine cones became fluffy clouds in their art piece.

"This is amazing!" gasped Bunny, hopping over to see.

"How did you make it so beautiful?" asked Fox, his orange tail swishing with curiosity.

Nutkin stood up straight and proud. "I planned ahead! I thought about what I wanted to make. I gathered my supplies early. I even practiced!"

Teacher Owl nodded approvingly. "Excellent preparation, Nutkin! Your planning made this artwork possible."

All the forest friends gathered around to admire the masterpiece. The colors looked bright and cheerful. The textures felt interesting under their paws and wings.

"Next time, I'll plan ahead too," said Chippy gratefully.

Nutkin's chest puffed out with pride. His study corner had been the perfect place to prepare. Planning ahead had made everything work perfectly.

That evening, Nutkin sat back in his study corner. The setting sun painted everything golden. He opened his notebook and wrote: "Planning makes dreams come true!"

His whiskers twitched happily. Tomorrow, he would start planning his next big adventure.

The End.
