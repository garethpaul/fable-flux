# Nutmeg Sets a Line

Nutmeg the squirrel lived behind a small, bright garden shed. The shed smelled like warm wood and pine. On sunny mornings, golden light made the dust sparkle in the air. The shed was full of crinkly bags, clinky tools, and soft, cozy straw in the corner. That cozy corner was Nutmeg’s special spot.

“This is my nest,” Nutmeg would whisper, patting the straw with her little paws. “Soft and safe.”

One day, Nutmeg gathered crunchy acorns. She stacked them in a neat, round pile near her nest. Tap, tap, tap went her paws. Rustle, rustle went the straw. She hummed a happy tune. Then she sniffed. She smelled carrot tops, sweet and earthy. She heard little feet. Hop, hop, hop!

Thumper the rabbit poked his nose inside the shed. “Hi, Nutmeg!” he said. “We’re playing hide-and-squeak!”

Behind him came Pip the chipmunk and Bree the bluebird. Pip’s cheeks were puffy with seeds. Bree shook her feathers. “Chirp! It’s a good day to play,” Bree sang.

Nutmeg smiled. “I like playing,” she said. But her eyes looked at her nest and her neat acorn pile. Her tummy did a tiny twist.

“Let’s hide here!” Pip squeaked. He ran straight to Nutmeg’s straw and rolled around. Bree fluttered down and pecked at an acorn. Thumper hopped close, right onto the edge of the nest.

Crunch.

Nutmeg’s heart beat fast. The straw squished. One acorn rolled away. A little lump rose in her throat.

Nutmeg took a slow breath. She remembered what Grandma Squirrel once said: “Your space is your safe place. You can say what you need.”

Nutmeg stood up tall, as tall as a small squirrel can be. Her tail fluffed. “Friends,” she said in a calm, clear voice, “I like you. I like playing. But this is my special spot. I need it to be safe and neat. Please don’t step in my nest or touch my acorns without asking.”

Pip froze mid-roll. Bree hopped back. Thumper tucked his paws. The shed was quiet except for the soft tick-tick of rain starting on the roof.

“Oh,” said Thumper. His ears drooped. “I didn’t know. I’m sorry.”

“I’m sorry too,” Pip squeaked. “I was excited.”

“Chirp-sorry,” Bree sang, tilting her head.

Nutmeg’s shoulders softened. “Thank you for listening,” she said. “We can still play. But we need a plan.”

Bree fluttered to a high hook. “Strategy time!” she chirped. “What is a plan?”

“A plan is how we can all have fun and keep my space safe,” Nutmeg said. She looked around the shed. The floor had a big, sunny square near the door. The tools hung on a wall with a long, straight board under them. The board felt smooth when Nutmeg touched it.

“I have an idea,” Nutmeg said. “We can make a kind line. A boundary line. It will show where my nest starts.”

“A line?” Thumper asked. “Like a path?”

“Like a friendly fence,” Nutmeg said.

They worked as a team. Bree found a long piece of blue twine in a basket. “Chirp! Twine!” Pip carried tiny pebbles in his cheeks and dropped them in a neat row. Plink. Plink. Plink. Thumper used his big paws to push straw bits back from the line. Shhh-shhh.

Nutmeg tied the twine to two little nails on the board, just above the floor. She stretched it into a tidy square around her nest and acorns. The blue twine made a bright, kind shape. It did not block the air. It did not feel mean. It just said, “This is Nutmeg’s.”

“Let’s practice asking,” Nutmeg said. “If you want an acorn, what can you say?”

Pip raised his paw. “Nutmeg, may I please have one acorn?”

Nutmeg smiled. “Yes, you may have one. Thank you for asking.”

Bree fluffed her feathers. “If I want to sit near your nest, I can say: May I sit close but not inside?”

“Yes,” said Nutmeg, “you may sit near the line.”

Thumper wiggled his whiskers. “If I forget and hop too close, you can say, ‘Stop, please,’ and I will stop.”

Nutmeg nodded. “And I will say it kindly and clearly.”

Rain tapped louder on the roof. Tap-tap-tap. The shed smelled like wet earth and leaves. “It sounds like music,” Bree sang.

“Let’s play a game right here,” Nutmeg said. “We can play Roll and Stop. We roll the acorn on the floor. When it gets to the blue line, we say, ‘Stop!’ and it must stop.”

They laughed and played. Pip rolled an acorn. “Roll-oll-oll!” Thumper stopped it right at the line with a gentle paw. Bree chirped, “Stop!” in a sing-song voice. Nutmeg clapped her tiny paws. Her nest stayed soft and neat. Her acorns stayed safe. Her heart felt calm and warm.

When the rain slowed, sun peeked through the window and made the blue twine glow. Bree looked at the line and said, “It is kind to tell friends what you need.”

Pip nodded. “And it is kind to listen.”

Thumper smiled. “And it is smart to make a plan.”

Nutmeg’s whiskers twitched with joy. “Today we learned three things,” she said. “One: I can use my voice to share my boundaries. Two: We can work together to make a strategy that helps everyone. Three: Asking and listening help friends feel safe.”

They all touched paws, wing, and paw to the floor in a little team tap. Tap.

Nutmeg curled into her cozy nest. “Thank you for helping,” she said.

“Thank you for telling us,” Thumper said.

“Chirp-yes,” Bree added. Pip gave a tiny thumbs-up with his paw.

Outside, the garden smelled fresh and clean. Inside, the shed felt safe and friendly. The blue line shone like a ribbon of respect.

Nutmeg closed her eyes and sighed. “My space is my safe place,” she whispered, “and my friends care.”

The End.
