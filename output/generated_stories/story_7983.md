# Bolt's Big Mistake

In a busy basement filled with whirring machines and blinking lights, lived a helpful robot dog named Bolt. His shiny silver fur gleamed under the bright bulbs, and his tail wagged with a gentle *click-click-click* sound whenever he was happy.

"Beep beep! Good morning!" Bolt said cheerfully as he rolled around on his four little wheels. The basement smelled like fresh oil and warm metal - just the way Bolt liked it.

Today, Papa Mike was working on fixing old toys for children. Colorful teddy bears, wooden blocks, and toy cars covered the long workbench. Bolt loved helping Papa Mike sort the toys into neat piles.

"Bolt, can you help me organize these toys while I fix this broken train?" Papa Mike asked, patting Bolt's metal head.

"Woof woof! Yes, Papa Mike!" Bolt's LED eyes lit up bright blue with excitement.

Bolt carefully picked up toys with his gentle robot mouth. *Clink, clink* went the wooden blocks as he stacked them. *Squeak, squeak* went the rubber ducks as he lined them up. Everything was going perfectly until...

*CRASH!*

Bolt accidentally knocked over a jar of small screws with his wagging tail. The tiny screws scattered everywhere across the concrete floor, making little *ping, ping, ping* sounds as they bounced.

Bolt's eyes turned orange with worry. He looked at Papa Mike, who was still busy with the train and hadn't heard the crash over the humming machines.

"Oh no," Bolt whispered. His circuits felt all jumbled up inside. "Maybe Papa Mike won't notice if I just hide the empty jar."

Quickly, Bolt pushed the empty jar behind some boxes where Papa Mike couldn't see it. Then he tried to continue sorting toys, but his tail wasn't wagging anymore.

After a while, Papa Mike stood up and stretched. "That train is fixed! Now, where did I put those small screws? I need them to finish this toy car."

Papa Mike looked all around the workbench. He opened drawers and moved tools, making the basement even busier with sounds. *Clang, clang* went the tools. *Whoosh* went Papa Mike's frustrated sighs.

Bolt watched Papa Mike search and search. His metal heart felt heavy and cold. Papa Mike looked tired and confused.

"This is not good," Bolt thought. "Papa Mike needs those screws to help the children. I should tell him the truth."

Bolt took a deep breath and rolled over to Papa Mike. "Papa Mike," he said in a small voice. "I have something to tell you."

"What is it, Bolt?"

"I... I accidentally knocked over the jar of screws with my tail. They're all over the floor. I was scared to tell you, so I hid the jar. I'm sorry."

Papa Mike's face softened into a warm smile. "Thank you for telling me the truth, Bolt. That was very brave and honest of you."

Together, they got down on their hands and knees. Bolt used his special magnetic chest to pick up the tiny screws. *Click, click, click* they stuck to him like magic! Papa Mike gathered the ones Bolt couldn't reach.

"See? When we work together and tell the truth, we can fix any problem," Papa Mike said, giving Bolt a gentle hug.

Bolt's tail began wagging again - *click-click-click* - and his eyes glowed bright blue. "Being honest feels much better than hiding mistakes!"

Soon, all the toys were fixed and ready to make children happy. Bolt learned that telling the truth, even when it's scary, is always the right thing to do.

The End.
