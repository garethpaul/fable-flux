# Nutmeg’s Busy Study Corner

Nutmeg was a small, resourceful squirrel with bright eyes and a fluffy tail. She loved to learn new things. She had a cozy study corner under the big window. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. The air smelled like crayons, paper, and cinnamon tea.

On the desk sat colored pencils, a tiny ruler, and sticky notes. A little clock tick-tick-ticked. Nutmeg whispered, “Today, I will draw a map of our park.” She wanted to help her neighbors find the clean-up bins and reading bench. She wanted to make the park better for everyone.

Nutmeg opened her notebook. “Oh no,” she said. “My pencils are short and wobbly. The eraser is tiny. The paper is crinkly.” She tried to draw a straight path. The pencil broke. Snap!

Her tail drooped. “I can’t do it,” she sighed.

Tick-tick-tick went the clock. Then Nutmeg took a deep breath. She remembered something she often said, “I can try. I can think. I can make it work.”

She tapped her head. “Be creative, Nutmeg.”

She looked around her study corner. She felt the smooth ruler. She smelled the cinnamon tea. She heard the soft wind at the window. These little things gave her an idea.

“First, I can fix my pencils,” she said. She found a tiny carrot sharpener she made from a safe bottle cap and sandpaper. “Scritch-scratch,” went the sharpener. The pencils became pointy again.

“Next, I can smooth my paper,” she said. She laid the crinkly page under a heavy storybook. Thump. She waited and hummed, “La-la-la.” When she lifted the book, the paper was flatter.

“Now, the eraser.” Nutmeg rubbed two small erasers together to make one big block. It felt squishy and new. “I did it!” she cheered.

She drew the tall trees with soft green swirls. She drew the pond with shiny blue loops. She made little hearts for the reading bench and stars for the clean-up bins. She used the ruler so the paths looked neat. “Zip, zip,” went her pencil. “Tap, tap,” went her paw.

Suddenly she frowned. “But how will my friends see it? The map is small.” She wanted everyone in the park to know where to put litter and where to sit and read. That would help the whole town. That was being responsible.

Nutmeg blinked. “I need a bigger map. But I have only small paper.” She felt worried, like a tiny cloud in her tummy.

She breathed in. She breathed out. “Be creative,” she told herself again.

She looked in her drawer. She found old envelopes, a paper bag, and a cereal box. “I can make big paper,” she said. She cut the boxes into flat pieces with safe scissors. Snip-snip. She opened the paper bag and pressed it flat. Rustle-rustle.

She taped the pieces together on the floor. “Smooth and steady,” she said. The big paper felt bumpy but strong. She smiled. “This will work!”

Nutmeg placed her small map next to the big one. “I will copy and improve it,” she said. “I can add numbers and arrows.” She dipped a brush in water and paint. Swish-swish. The blue pond looked cool. The green trees looked soft. She added a bright orange arrow that said, “This way to the clean-up bins!” She added a purple heart that said, “Reading bench here. Shhh, quiet and cozy.”

Her friend, Wren the bird, peeked in the window. “Chirp! What are you making?”

“A big park map,” said Nutmeg. “It will help everyone. We can keep the park clean and kind.”

Wren tilted her head. “Chirp-chirp! I can help. I can sing a song so friends will look.”

“Great idea,” said Nutmeg. They practiced. Wren sang, “Look at the map, do your part!” Nutmeg clapped. Clap-clap.

Nutmeg checked her work. She corrected a wiggly line with her big eraser. She added numbers: 1 for the gate, 2 for the pond, 3 for the bins, 4 for the reading bench. She drew little paws to show the path. She felt proud. Her tail flicked with joy.

“Time to share,” she said. She rolled up the big map. It smelled like paper and paint. Together, Nutmeg and Wren carried it to the park notice board. The wood felt rough under her paws. She taped the map up. Press-press.

Neighbors came by. “Ooh!” said Bunny. “Now I know where to put my snack wrapper.”

“Ahh!” said Hedgehog. “Now I can find the quiet bench to read.”

Wren sang the song. “Look at the map, do your part!” Everyone smiled. Nutmeg’s heart felt warm.

Mayor Tortoise nodded slowly. “Good work, Nutmeg,” he said. “You used what you had. You made it better. You helped the whole park.”

Nutmeg blushed. “Thank you,” she said. “I learned I can solve problems with creativity. I can improve things step by step. I can help my community.”

She went back to her study corner. The clock went tick-tick-tick. The sun made soft gold on her desk. She wrote a note and stuck it up high: “Be creative. Try. Improve. Help others.”

Nutmeg sipped her cinnamon tea. It tasted warm and sweet. She smiled. “I did it,” she whispered. “And I can do it again.”

The End.
