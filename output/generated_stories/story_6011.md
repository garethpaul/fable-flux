# Bubbles Waits and Wonders

Bubbles was a playful dolphin with bright eyes and a happy flip of the tail. One rainy day, Bubbles visited a cozy house. The guest room was soft and quiet. The bed had a warm, yellow blanket. The curtains were blue like the sea. A little lamp glowed like a tiny sun. The air smelled like clean soap and fresh towels.

“Click, click!” went Bubbles’s little flippers on the wooden floor. “I want to play! I want to see everything!” Bubbles chirped, making little dolphin squeaks.

On the bed sat a big, round box. It was wrapped in shiny paper with stars. A tag said, “For later.”

“Later?” Bubbles tilted a fin. “I want to open it now.” Bubbles wiggled and poked the ribbon. It made a soft swish sound.

Just then, Grandma Mia walked in. She smiled and patted Bubbles’s smooth head. “Hello, Bubbles. This is the guest room. It’s a quiet place to rest. That box is for later.”

Bubbles’s eyes sparkled. “But I want to see what is inside!”

Grandma Mia nodded kindly. “I know it’s hard to wait. Waiting is called patience. Patience means we can be calm while we wait. When we wait, we also learn. We notice small things. We discover new things.”

Bubbles took a deep breath. In… and out. “How do I wait?”

“We can wait with our senses,” said Grandma Mia. “Let’s use our eyes, ears, nose, and hands. Let’s discover the room while we wait.”

Bubbles blinked and looked around. The lamp made a warm hum. The rain tick-tick-ticked on the window. The blanket felt soft like sea foam. The air smelled sweet, like a tiny flower.

“I hear the rain,” Bubbles whispered. “Tick, tick, tick.”

“Yes,” said Grandma Mia. “That sound can help us feel calm.”

Bubbles looked at the blue curtains. “They look like waves,” Bubbles said. “Whoosh.” Bubbles swayed side to side like swimming.

Grandma Mia chuckled. “Good swimming. What else can we find?”

Bubbles spotted a picture on the wall. It showed a sandy beach and a red bucket. “I know that,” Bubbles chirped. “Sand is warm. Buckets hold shells!”

“What do you think is in the box?” asked Grandma Mia.

Bubbles thought. “Maybe it is a shell. Maybe it is a book. Maybe it is a fluffy towel.”

“It could be,” said Grandma Mia. “But we will open it later. For now, let’s learn something new.”

Grandma Mia took a small hourglass from the shelf. Golden sand sat inside. “This is a tiny timer. We will wait until the sand falls. Then it will be time.”

Bubbles peered close. The sand began to slip, soft like whispery sugar. “Shhh…” Bubbles listened. “I can wait,” Bubbles said, though the tail flicked and flicked.

“To help, let’s play a gentle game,” said Grandma Mia. “We will find three blue things. Then two soft things. Then one thing that smells nice.”

Bubbles clapped flippers. “I like this game!”

“Three blue things,” said Grandma Mia.

Bubbles pointed. “Blue curtains! Blue picture frame! Blue pillow!”

“Good discovery,” said Grandma Mia. “Now two soft things.”

Bubbles patted the yellow blanket. “Soft!” Then Bubbles touched a teddy bear on the chair. “Extra soft!”

“Wonderful,” said Grandma Mia. “Now one thing that smells nice.”

Bubbles sniffed. “Mmm. The fresh towel!” The towel smelled clean and light.

“You are learning to notice,” Grandma Mia said. “How does your body feel now?”

Bubbles listened to the body. The tail felt slower. The heart felt steady. “I feel calm,” Bubbles said with a happy chirp.

Bubbles looked at the hourglass. The sand was half gone. “Still waiting,” Bubbles said. “What can we do next?”

“We can tell a quiet story,” said Grandma Mia. “You start.”

Bubbles smiled. “Once there was a little dolphin named Bubbles. Bubbles wanted to open a shiny box. But Bubbles waited. Bubbles looked, and listened, and learned. Bubbles found blue, and soft, and sweet smells. Bubbles felt calm.”

Grandma Mia clapped softly. “That is a lovely story.”

The rain grew softer, like a lullaby. The lamp glowed warm. The last gold grains fell in the hourglass. “Tink,” it went as the top emptied.

Bubbles’s eyes grew wide. “The sand is done!”

“Yes,” said Grandma Mia. “You were patient. You discovered many things. You learned how to wait.”

Bubbles nodded proudly. “I did it. I can wait.”

Grandma Mia set the box on the bed. Together they untied the ribbon. Swish, swish. The paper crinkled. Crinkle, crinkle. Inside was a bright, blue book with shiny pages. The title said, “Ocean Wonders.”

“A book!” Bubbles squeaked. “It is blue! It smells like paper. It feels smooth.”

Grandma Mia opened the book. Pictures of starfish, coral, and friendly fish smiled up. “This book is a gift for guests,” she said. “We can read it now.”

Bubbles snuggled against the soft blanket. Grandma Mia read in a gentle voice. They turned pages slowly. They learned new words like coral, current, and kelp. Bubbles touched the pictures and listened with calm joy.

“I’m glad we waited,” Bubbles said. “Waiting felt hard at first. But patience helped me see, hear, and learn. I found calm inside me.”

Grandma Mia hugged Bubbles. “Yes. Patience helps us discover. Patience helps us be kind to ourselves and others.”

Bubbles smiled, tail swishing softly. “Next time I will wait with my senses. I will breathe. I will notice. I will learn.”

The rain stopped. The room felt bright and peaceful. The guest room was still and kind, like a gentle sea.

The End.
