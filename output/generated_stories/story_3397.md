# Joey’s Jumble of Jumps and Stories

In a sunny, playful living room, a joyful kangaroo joey named Kip bounced on a soft rainbow rug. The rug felt warm under his paws. It smelled faintly of soap and oranges because Mama had cleaned it that morning. Toys sat in tidy baskets. Books smiled from a low shelf. The window let in a gentle breeze that whispered, “Whoooosh.”

Kip giggled. “I want to jump, and I want to tell a story, and I want to build a block tower!” His eyes were bright and shiny. His tail flicked with happy wiggles.

Mama Roo peeked in with a friendly smile. “That sounds fun, Kip. What will you do first?”

Kip froze. His ears went boing-boing. “Um… I don’t know.” He bounced in a tiny circle. “Jump! No, story! No, tower!” His tummy fluttered like a butterfly. He felt stuck.

Mama Roo knelt and put a soft paw on his shoulder. “It’s okay to feel mixed-up. Let’s use our smart steps.”

“Smart steps?” Kip blinked.

“Yes,” said Mama. “We can plan. Planning is a skill. It helps your brain and your body work together.”

Kip nodded slowly. “I want to learn that skill.”

Mama pointed to the baskets, the books, and the rug. “First, we choose. Second, we practice. Third, we celebrate.”

Kip took a deep breath. He could smell the oranges again. It made him feel calm. “Okay. Step one: choose.”

He looked around the playful living room. He listened to the breeze. He touched the soft rug with his paws. He whispered, “What should I do first?”

Just then, his tummy made a little growl. It sounded like “grrr-hello!” Kip laughed. “My tummy says it wants to move. I will jump first!”

Mama clapped once. “Good decision.”

Kip set up a simple jumping path with three seat cushions. One, two, three. He lined them up like islands in a bright blue sea. “I will jump from red to yellow to green,” he said. “That is my plan.”

He wiggled his toes. He bent his knees. “Jump!” Kip hopped from red to yellow to green. Thump, thump, thump. His paws felt springy. The cushions puffed with a soft “poof.” Kip landed and cheered, “I did it!”

Mama smiled. “Step two: practice. Try it slow. Then try it fast.”

Kip tried it slow. He counted, “One… two… three.” Then he tried it fast. “One-two-three!” He tried it while humming a little tune. He tried it with tiny hops and with big hops. He tried it sideways with a giggle that sounded like bubbles. Each time, he felt his legs growing strong and steady.

“Now I will tell a story,” Kip said, eyes bright. He took a deep breath. “Once upon a time, a brave little joey hopped across the rainbow sea to help a friend.”

Mama cupped her chin. “What happens next?”

Kip looked at the cushions. He touched the green one. It was cool and smooth. He decided, “The friend is a shy teddy who lost his roar.”

“Ooh,” said Mama. “How does the joey help?”

Kip paused. He remembered the steps. “I choose. I practice. I celebrate.” He grinned. “The joey chooses to listen. He practices kind words. Then they celebrate with a happy dance.”

Kip hopped gently and spoke in a clear, warm voice, “Little Teddy, I hear you. Let’s practice a tiny roar. Rrr… rrr… rrr-ROAR!” He made a small, silly roar that tickled his throat. It sounded like a purring kitty mixed with a tiny lion. He and Mama danced a wiggly victory dance. Their tails swished-swish on the rug.

Kip clapped. “That was my story!”

Mama nodded. “You used your skills. You planned your jumps. You made a beginning, a middle, and an end. You made a good decision and helped your teddy friend.”

Kip’s eyes went to the block basket. Smooth wooden blocks waited, smelling like fresh wood and sunshine. “Now I will build a tower.” He breathed in and out. “Step one: choose. I choose a wide base. Step two: practice. I stack slowly. Step three: celebrate. I cheer when it stands.”

He placed four big blocks on the bottom. Tap, tap, tap. His paws were gentle. He put smaller blocks on top. His ears listened to the tiny click of wood on wood. He paused to check if it wobbled. “Hmm.” He added one more block in the center. The tower stood tall and straight.

“I did it!” Kip whispered, not too loud. The tower felt proud. He grinned and gave a quiet, happy bounce.

Mama hugged him. “How do you feel?”

Kip’s heart felt warm like the sunny window. “I feel strong. I used planning. I used kind words. I made good choices.”

Mama smiled. “What did you learn today?”

Kip held up three fingers. “Choose. Practice. Celebrate. That’s my skill. I can use it for jumping, for stories, and for building.”

He winked at the teddy on the couch. “And for helping friends, too.”

They put the cushions and blocks back in their places. The room looked calm and cozy. The breeze whispered again. Kip snuggled beside Mama with a book. He whispered, “Next time I feel mixed-up, I will use my smart steps.”

Mama kissed his forehead. “That is a great plan.”

Kip closed his eyes and smiled. He could almost hear the tower saying click, the cushions saying poof, and the teddy saying a tiny brave “roar.” Kip felt ready for new skills and new stories, one smart step at a time.

The End.
