# Joey’s Plan in the Study Corner

Joey was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and bouncy feet. He loved to hop, tap his tail, and hum a happy tune. One sunny morning, he hopped into the quiet study corner at home. The study corner smelled like fresh paper and lemon-clean wood. Sunbeams made warm squares on the floor. Shelves held books, crayons, a tiny plant, and a jar of smooth pebble erasers.

“Today, I want to make a memory book,” Joey said. “I want to share Nana Roo’s stories, take care of nature, and plan it all myself!”

Mama Roo smiled from the doorway. “That sounds wonderful, Joey. What is your plan?”

Joey blinked. “Um… I will hop and do it.” He spread paper and crayons everywhere. Crayons rolled. Papers slid. The tiny plant wobbled.

“Oops,” Joey whispered. His ears drooped.

Mama Roo walked over and knelt beside him. “Planning helps. Planning is like a map for your day.”

“What is a plan?” Joey asked, tail swishing softly on the rug.

“A plan is a list of steps,” Mama Roo said. “First, we think. Then, we do. You can make small steps.”

Joey took a deep breath. He smelled the wood desk. He felt the smooth paper. “I can make steps,” he said. “I can plan!”

He tapped a crayon on a sticky note. “Step 1: Tidy the study corner.” He looked at the plant and whispered, “You will be safe.” He moved the plant to the sunny side of the desk. The leaves felt cool and soft. He put the crayons in a low cup. He stacked the papers in a neat pile. He put the pebble erasers back in the jar. Click! The jar lid felt snug.

“Step 2: Ask Nana Roo for a story,” Joey said.

He hopped to the video phone. Beep! Nana Roo’s kind face popped up. “Hello, little hopper!”

“Nana, can you share a family tradition?” Joey asked. “I want to put it in my memory book.”

Nana Roo chuckled. “We have a tea-and-tales tradition. Every spring, we sip mint tea and tell stories about planting trees.”

Joey’s eyes shone. “Planting trees helps the Earth!”

“It does,” Nana said. “We plan where to plant. We care for the soil. We share the job.”

Joey smiled. “Thank you, Nana. I will write it down.”

He hopped back to the study corner. “Step 3: Draw and write.” He held a green crayon. Scritch-scratch! He drew a cup of tea with steam curls. He drew little trees with happy faces. He wrote big simple words: “Tea and Trees.” He felt proud and calm.

Then, a problem came. Joey wanted to glue leaves into the book. He opened a glue stick and sniffed. It smelled sweet and sticky. But he looked out the window and saw his favorite shady tree waving. He did not want to take its leaves.

Joey’s ears wiggled. “How can I care for nature and still make my book?” he asked.

He read his sticky note plan. He added a new step. “Step 4: Think of choices.” He put on his thinking face. He tapped his tail gently. Tap, tap. “I can draw leaves. I can use paper I have. I can use old paper from the recycle box.”

He opened the recycle box. Rustle, rustle. He found a green envelope and a brown paper bag. He cut leaf shapes. Snip, snip. He glued them to the page. Smooth, smooth. The leaves looked nice, and the tree outside kept dancing.

“Step 5: Check my work,” Joey said. He turned the pages. He read his words. He looked at his pictures. He fixed a smudgy line with a pebble eraser. Rub, rub. Clean!

Mama Roo peeked in again. “How is your plan, Joey?”

Joey grinned. “My plan works! I made a memory book page. I kept the plant safe. I used recycle paper. I listened to Nana’s tradition.”

Mama Roo clapped softly. “You planned each step. You were independent. You used your thinking to solve a problem and care for the Earth.”

Joey hugged his book. It felt warm from his hands. “I want to do our tea-and-tales too,” he said. “We can plan it.”

Mama Roo nodded. “Let’s add a last step.”

Joey wrote, “Step 6: Make a tea-and-tales day.” He drew a tiny calendar with a star. “We will drink mint tea with Nana on video. We will tell stories. We will plant seeds in cups by the window.”

Joey cleaned the desk. He put the glue away. He watered the tiny plant with a drip, drip from a little cup. The plant’s leaves shivered with joy.

He sat back in the sunny square and hugged his tail. He felt peaceful and proud. “Plans help me know what to do,” he said softly. “Plans help me be careful and kind.”

Mama Roo whispered, “Yes, little hopper. Planning is a map for your day. With a plan, you can do big things, one small step at a time.”

Joey bounced once, twice, three times. “I can plan! I can think! I can care!”

And the study corner felt bright, calm, and ready for more stories.

The End.
