# Panda Poppy’s Plan in the Workshop

Panda Poppy was a patient panda. Poppy loved to take slow breaths and think kind thoughts. One sunny morning, Poppy padded into the garage workshop. The floor felt cool and smooth. The air smelled like sawdust and orange soap. Sunlight made little dust sparkles dance.

“Good morning, tools,” Poppy whispered. “Clink, clank, clunk,” the tools seemed to say back.

Poppy had a goal. Poppy wanted to make a toy cart for playtime. The cart would carry blocks, books, and a tiny plant. Poppy patted her soft belly and smiled. “I can do it,” she said. “I will make a plan, and I will take my time.”

On the workbench, there was a box of old things. There were wooden scraps, loose wheels, a bent spoon, bottle caps, and ribbon. Poppy’s friend, a little robin, hopped in through the open door and chirped, “Tweet! What are you making?”

“I am making a cart,” Poppy said. “I will use what we already have. That is called being sustainable. It means we care for our world. We use, reuse, and do not waste.”

The robin tilted her head. “Tweet-tweet! How will you start?”

Poppy took a slow breath. “First, I set my goal. I will build a safe, strong, and kind-to-Earth cart. Next, I make a list.”

She wrote on a big notepad with a stubby pencil:
- Find a base
- Add wheels
- Make a handle
- Decorate with reused things
- Clean up and recycle

Poppy’s paw pads felt the grain of a flat wooden board. It was smooth on top and rough on the edges. “This will be the base,” she said. “We will sand it.”

Rrr-rrr-rrr went the sandpaper. Dust made a light swish in the sun. “Nice and smooth,” Poppy hummed. “We take our time. We stay safe.”

Next, Poppy picked up two little wheels from an old toy. They were dusty but strong. She wiped them with a cloth that smelled like lemon. “Reusing is smart,” she said. “We give old things new jobs.”

The robin chirped, “Tweet! Wheels on!”

Poppy used a reused dowel for an axle. “Click!” went the wheel as it slid on. “Click!” went the other wheel. She turned the cart base. The wheels spun with a soft “whirr.” Poppy clapped softly. “We are doing it!”

For a handle, Poppy found a sturdy broomstick that was too short to sweep. “Perfect,” she said. She used twine from an old package to tie it to the base. Knot by knot, she worked. “Over, under, pull and tuck,” Poppy sang. Her song was gentle and slow.

The robin peeped, “Tweet, can we make it cute?”

“Yes,” said Poppy, “but let’s be kind to Earth.” She found a bent spoon. She tapped it with her paw. “Tink-tink!” It made a happy sound. “We can make a bell!” She tied the spoon with red ribbon from a gift. “Ding!” went the spoon bell.

Poppy looked in the box and found bottle caps. “We can make flowers,” she said. She dabbed a little non-toxic glue and pressed the caps on the sides. “Pop!” went the caps as they stuck. She drew green stems with a crayon. The crayon smelled a little like wax and apples.

The cart looked bright and cheery. Poppy took a sip of cool water and smiled. “We set a goal. We made a plan. We reused. We were patient,” she said.

The robin hopped into the cart and wiggled. “Tweet! Test ride!”

Poppy giggled. She pulled the handle. The wheels went “whirr, whirr.” The spoon bell went “ding-ding!” The floor made a soft “thump-thump” under Poppy’s careful steps.

Suddenly, the twine on one side slipped. “Oh!” said Poppy. The cart leaned a little.

The robin flapped. “Oh no!”

Poppy took a slow breath in. She let it out. “It is okay. We can fix it. We stay calm. We try again.” She checked her list. “We learn. We do not waste.”

She found an old metal bracket in a jar. It was cool and a little rusty. She cleaned it with a brush until it shone. “We will use this to make the handle strong,” she said. She lined it up and tightened it with a small, reused screw. “Twist, twist,” went the screwdriver. The handle felt firm. “Good job, team,” Poppy said.

The robin chirped, “Tweet! Ready!”

Poppy pulled the cart again. “Whirr, ding, whirr.” It rolled straight and true. Poppy placed a tiny potted plant inside. The soil smelled sweet and earthy. A green leaf waved hello.

“Now we clean up,” Poppy said. She sorted the scraps into bins. “Wood here. Metal here. Plastic here. Paper here. We recycle and reuse what we can. We keep our world tidy.”

The robin fluffed her feathers. “Tweet! What did we learn?”

Poppy smiled, her eyes warm and kind. “We learned to set a goal. We learned to make a plan. We learned to be patient. We learned to reuse and not waste. That is sustainability. It helps our Earth.”

They rolled the cart into the sunlight. The plant leaf shivered in a tiny breeze. The spoon bell sang a soft “ding.”

Poppy hugged the robin gently. “We did it,” she said. “When we care, plan, and reuse, we help our world. Little steps make a big difference.”

The robin chirped, “Tweet-tweet! Let’s share!”

“Yes,” said Poppy. “We can show friends. We can teach them to set goals, craft with care, and be kind to Earth.”

Poppy took one more slow breath. The garage was neat and bright. The cart was ready for adventures. And the world felt just a little greener.

The End.
