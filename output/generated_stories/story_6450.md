# Fawn's Tidy Day

Little Fawn had soft brown spots and the biggest, kindest eyes in the whole nursery. Her tiny hooves made gentle tapping sounds on the wooden floor as she walked around her cozy room.

The nursery was warm and bright. Sunlight streamed through yellow curtains. A rocking chair sat by the window with a fuzzy blue blanket. Colorful toys were scattered everywhere - blocks here, books there, and crayons rolling under the table.

"Oh my," said Fawn softly, looking around. "Everything is so mixed up!"

Fawn's friend Bunny hopped over. "What's wrong, Fawn?" Bunny asked, twitching her pink nose.

"I want to play, but I can't find anything," Fawn said sadly. "My favorite red ball is hiding somewhere. My puzzle pieces are all over the floor."

Bunny nodded. "Let's think about this like a game! What should we do first?"

Fawn thought carefully. She touched her chin with her tiny hoof. "Maybe we can put things where they belong?"

"That's a great idea!" said Bunny. "Let's start with the books."

Together, they picked up the colorful picture books. Fawn smelled the sweet paper scent as they stacked them neatly on the low shelf. "These books have stories from many different places," Fawn said. "This one is about children in Japan. This one shows families in Mexico."

"How wonderful!" Bunny said. "Now where should the blocks go?"

Fawn looked around thoughtfully. "The blocks are square, so maybe they go in the square basket?" She felt proud of her logical thinking.

They sorted the smooth wooden blocks by color - red ones here, blue ones there. The blocks made satisfying clicking sounds as they dropped into the basket.

Next came the art supplies. Fawn's hooves carefully placed each crayon in the rainbow holder. "Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple," she counted softly.

"You're so good at organizing!" Bunny said with a smile.

As they worked, more friends joined them. Squirrel helped with the puzzle pieces. Mouse organized the tiny toy cars. Everyone worked together, sharing the job and helping each other.

"This is actually fun!" laughed Fawn. The room began to look neat and tidy. Everything had its special place.

Finally, Fawn spotted something red under the rocking chair. "My ball!" she cried happily. She rolled it gently with her nose. It was smooth and bouncy, just like she remembered.

"Now we can really play!" said Bunny.

But something even better happened. With everything organized, Fawn could see all her toys clearly. She could choose exactly what she wanted to play with. The room felt calm and peaceful.

"Look how beautiful our nursery looks," Fawn said, taking a deep breath. The air smelled fresh and clean. Sunlight danced on the organized shelves.

All the friends sat in a circle on the soft carpet. They felt proud and happy. Fawn's heart felt warm and content.

"When we work together and put things in order, everything feels better," Fawn said wisely. "And we can find what we need when we want to play."

Bunny hugged Fawn gently. "You taught us something important today. Being organized helps us feel calm and happy."

From that day on, Fawn always remembered to put her toys away after playing. The nursery stayed neat and tidy, and playtime became even more fun because everything was easy to find.

The End.
