# Benny Beaver's Honest Day

In a cozy study corner by the sparkling river, Benny Beaver sat surrounded by his favorite books and wooden blocks. The morning sun streamed through the window, making his brown fur shine like chocolate. Benny loved learning new things in his special corner!

"Today I want to build the biggest dam ever!" Benny said excitedly, stacking his wooden blocks high. His little paws worked quickly, placing block after block.

CRASH! Down tumbled all the blocks, scattering across the soft rug.

"Oh no!" Benny gasped. One block had a small chip in it now. His tummy felt funny and tight.

Just then, Mama Beaver peeked into the study corner. "Good morning, little builder! How are your blocks today?"

Benny looked at the chipped block in his paw. His whiskers twitched nervously. He could hide the broken block under the rug, or he could tell Mama what happened.

"I... I..." Benny started, feeling his heart beat fast like a tiny drum.

"Take a deep breath, sweetie," Mama said gently, noticing Benny looked worried. "Deep breathing helps us feel calm and think clearly."

Benny took a big breath in through his nose, smelling the fresh pine scent from outside. He breathed out slowly through his mouth. His tummy felt a little better.

"Mama, I was building really high, and my blocks fell down. This one got a little chip," Benny said, holding up the block. "I'm sorry I wasn't more careful."

Mama Beaver smiled warmly and sat down beside him. "Thank you for telling me the truth, Benny. That was very honest of you."

"But I broke it," Benny said sadly, his brown eyes looking down.

"Accidents happen when we're learning and playing," Mama explained. "What matters most is that you told the truth. Honesty makes our hearts feel light and happy."

Benny's tummy felt much better now. "It does feel good to tell the truth," he said, surprised.

"Would you like to learn something special about our beaver family?" Mama asked. "Your grandpa taught me that when we make mistakes, we can often fix them or make something even better."

Mama showed Benny how to sand the chipped block gently with soft sandpaper. "See? Now it's smooth again, and we learned how to take care of our things."

"Can we use this block to build something to help the river animals?" Benny asked, remembering how his family always cared for their forest home.

"What a wonderful idea!" Mama said. "Honest hearts often think of ways to help others."

Together, they built a small shelter for the river frogs using all the blocks, including the one they had fixed. Benny carefully placed each block, moving slowly and thoughtfully.

"Look, Mama! The frogs will love this!" Benny clapped his paws as three little green frogs hopped over to explore their new home.

"Ribbit! Thank you, Benny!" the frogs seemed to say as they splashed happily.

As the sun climbed higher, Benny felt proud and peaceful. His study corner was tidy, the frogs had a new home, and best of all, he had been honest with Mama.

"Being honest feels so much better than hiding the truth," Benny said, giving Mama a big, warm hug.

"That's the most important lesson of all," Mama said, stroking his soft fur. "Honest beavers build the strongest dams and the happiest families."

The End.
