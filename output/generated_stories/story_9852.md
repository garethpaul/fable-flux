# Tippy Toes in the Art Room

In a sunny creative art room, a kind mouse named Mimi wiggled her whiskers and sniffed the air. The room smelled like crayons and glue. Paper rustled like soft leaves. Paints shone in red, blue, and yellow. Glitter sparkled like tiny stars. Mimi loved this room. It made her paws feel tingly with happy energy.

“I want to move and make art!” Mimi squeaked. “My body wants to wiggle. My paws want to paint.”

Mimi’s friends were there too. There was Gus the snail, slow and careful. There was Lila the ladybug, small and bright. There was Bruno the bunny, bouncy and bold. Their teacher, Ms. Dot, wore a paint-splattered apron and a warm smile.

“Today,” said Ms. Dot, clapping gently, “we will make paint dances. We will move our bodies and make shapes on big paper. Remember: we each have a space bubble. We ask before we enter someone’s bubble.”

Mimi nodded. A space bubble sounded safe. It sounded kind.

Ms. Dot rolled out a long sheet of paper like a white river. She put washable paint in shallow trays. “We will use our feet, our paws, and our tippy toes,” she said. “We will move like the music.”

Mimi dipped her little mouse paw in blue paint. It felt cool and slippery. “Ooh!” she giggled. She stamped a tiny paw print. Splat! Sweet blue dots!

Bruno bounced forward and dipped his big bunny foot in red paint. “Boing!” he laughed, hopping a red hop-hop-hop.

Lila tapped her tiny feet in yellow. Tap, tap, tap, like sunshine sprinkles.

Gus slid slowly in green. Sloooow, smooth lines.

Music began—soft drums and a happy flute. Mimi’s body woke up like popcorn popping. Her tail swished. Her whiskers twitched. “I want to slide! I want to spin!” she said.

Mimi moved on her tippy toes, light as a leaf. She slid her blue paw and made a long sky-blue line. She spun in a small circle. Swish! Spin! Her heart felt brave. Her body felt strong.

But Bruno bounced big bunny hops. Hop! Hop! Hop! His big feet landed close to Mimi’s paint line.

“Careful,” Mimi said. “My space bubble is small.”

Bruno did not hear. Hop! He splashed red into Mimi’s blue. The colors swirled into purple. Mimi stopped moving. Her ears drooped.

“I didn’t want purple,” she whispered. “I wanted my own blue line. I feel squished.”

Ms. Dot saw Mimi’s droopy ears. She paused the music. “Let’s take a breath,” she said. Everyone placed a hand on their belly and breathed in and out. In… and out…

Mimi felt her belly rise and fall. She felt a tiny calm.

Ms. Dot knelt by Mimi. “What is your problem, Mimi?” she asked softly.

Mimi said, “I want to move. I love to move. But I also want my space. Bruno hopped into my bubble. I feel sad.”

Ms. Dot nodded. “Your feelings matter. This is your body. This is your movement. You can say what you need.”

Mimi looked at Bruno. Bruno’s ears drooped now too. “I didn’t mean to,” he said. “I got excited. I forgot about bubbles.”

Mimi took another breath. “Bruno,” she said in a clear, small voice, “I like you. I like your hops. I need my space bubble. Please ask before you come close.”

Bruno nodded. “I can do that. May I hop near you, but not on your line?”

Mimi felt her chest warm like morning toast. “Yes,” she said, “thank you for asking. You may hop near, not on.”

Ms. Dot smiled. “Let’s try again. We will make movement paths. Mimi, choose your path. Bruno, choose yours. Lila, choose a tiny path. Gus, choose a slow path.”

The music started again. Mimi lifted her chin. She felt brave. She felt proud. She dipped her paw in blue and made a gentle arch, like a rainbow. She whispered to herself, “My body, my bubble, my brave.”

Bruno hopped on a new red path, a little farther away. “I’m hopping near, not on,” he sang.

Lila tapped tiny sun-dots along the edges. Tap tap tap. “I will stay tiny,” she chimed.

Gus slid a slow green river down the middle. “Sloooow and steady,” he hummed.

Mimi began a new move. She bent her knees and did mouse tippy-toe steps. Tip, tip, tip. She made small spins that stayed in her bubble. Spin, pause, smile. She felt strong and gentle at the same time.

Ms. Dot clapped softly. “Look at all these paths. We are moving with care. We are moving with joy.”

Mimi tried a brave hop over her own line. Boop! She landed with a giggle. “I can hop too,” she said. “I can try new moves and still keep my space.”

Bruno cheered, “Go Mimi!”

When the music ended, the long paper looked like a happy map. Blue arches, red hops, yellow dots, green rivers, and a cozy purple swirl where mistakes turned into magic.

Mimi looked at the art. She looked at her friends. Her heart felt big and bright.

“I solved my problem,” she said. “I used my words. I asked for space. I moved my body in a way that felt good. I can be kind to me and kind to you.”

Ms. Dot nodded. “That is our lesson. Move your body. Know your bubble. Use brave words. That makes room for everyone.”

Mimi wiped her paws. The soap felt bubbly and warm. She dried them on a soft towel. She did one last tippy-toe step and smiled.

“My body is strong,” she whispered. “My voice is strong too.”

The End.
