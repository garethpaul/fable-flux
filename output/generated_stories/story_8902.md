# Nutmeg’s Nighttime Helping Hands

Nutmeg was a small, resourceful squirrel with soft gray fur and a fluffy tail. She lived in a cozy bedroom at the top of a tall, friendly house. The bedroom smelled like warm blankets and cinnamon tea. Moonlight made silver shapes on the soft rug, and the bedside lamp glowed like a tiny sun.

Every night, Nutmeg checked her little tool belt. It had a string, a tiny wooden spoon, a cloth, and a button she used like a wheel. “Ready to help!” she chirped, patting each tool with her tiny paws.

One evening, as the clock went tick-tock, Nutmeg heard a sniffle from the bed. “Sniff, sniff.” It was Pip, the little child who loved to read bedtime books. Pip’s nose was stuffy, and their eyes were sleepy.

“Nutmeg,” Pip whispered, “my favorite bedtime book fell under the bed. It’s too dark. I can’t reach.”

Nutmeg’s heart felt warm. She wanted to help. “I can do it,” she said softly. “Helping is my favorite.” She scurried to the edge of the bed and peered into the shadowy space. It was dark and dusty. She smelled a tickle of dust and heard a tiny creak from the bed springs.

Nutmeg took a breath. “Be patient,” she told herself. “Go slow and steady.” She tied her string to the little button wheel and rolled it under the bed. “Clip, clack,” went the button. But it rolled past the book and bumped the wall. “Oh no,” said Nutmeg. “That didn’t work.”

Pip watched with big, hopeful eyes. “It’s okay,” Pip said in a gentle voice. “You can try again.”

Nutmeg nodded. “Thank you for waiting.” She dusted her paws and tried plan number two. She slid the wooden spoon under the bed, feeling with the tip. “Tap, tap.” She felt something soft. “Is it the book?” she wondered. She pulled it back, but out came a fluffy slipper. Pip giggled. “That’s my slipper!”

Nutmeg smiled. “Found one thing! I won’t give up.” Her tail swished with determination. She remembered the cloth. “Dust makes me sneeze,” she said, “so I’ll clean as I go.” She tied the cloth to the spoon and swished it under the bed. “Swish, swish.” Dust bunnies danced in the lamplight, and the room smelled cleaner.

At last, Nutmeg saw the corner of the book. “There you are!” she cheered. She reached her paw, but the space was too tight. Her whiskers tickled. She wanted to hurry, but she remembered patience. “Slow and steady,” she whispered. She wiggled the spoon to slide the book closer. “Come on, friend,” she encouraged. “We can do this.”

The book moved a little. “Scoot, scoot.” Nutmeg tried to pull it with the string, but the string slipped. “Oh,” she said, feeling a tiny droop in her tail. “This is hard.” She took a small breath in, and a small breath out. “Helping can be tricky,” she told herself, “but I can try again.”

Pip leaned over the bed, careful and kind. “Nutmeg, I believe in you,” Pip said. “I can hold the lamp closer.”

The warm light shone brighter under the bed. Nutmeg could see clearly now. “Thank you,” she said. “Teamwork helps!” She looped the string around the book’s corner and tied a little knot—right over left, pull tight. “Ready, set, tug!”

Together, Pip and Nutmeg pulled gently. “Tug, tug.” The book slid out with a soft whoosh. Nutmeg hugged it. The cover felt smooth and cool, and it smelled like paper and cozy nights.

“You did it!” Pip clapped. “Thank you for helping me.”

Nutmeg beamed. “You helped me too. You waited. You were patient. And you gave me light.” Her tail twitched happily.

Pip snuggled into the pillows and opened the book. “Would you like to listen?” Pip asked.

“Yes, please,” Nutmeg said, curling up on the blanket. As Pip read, the room felt even cozier. The lamp hummed softly, and the clock went tick-tock, tick-tock. Nutmeg felt proud and peaceful.

Then they heard another tiny sound. “Squeak?” It came from the window. A little toy mouse had rolled off the sill and was stuck between the curtain and the wall. It wobbled and squeaked again, “Squeak?”

Nutmeg hopped up. “Looks like someone else needs help.” She smiled at Pip. “Are you ready for another team rescue?”

Pip nodded. “Yes!”

Nutmeg climbed the curtain carefully, gripping with gentle paws. “Slow and steady,” she reminded herself. She used the spoon to guide the toy mouse, and Pip held out their hand. “Almost there,” said Nutmeg. “We don’t rush. We care.”

“Got it!” Pip said as the toy mouse slid into their hand. The mouse gave a last happy “Squeak!” and Pip set it safely on the nightstand.

Nutmeg’s eyes sparkled. “Helping others makes the room feel brighter,” she said. “And working together feels good.”

Pip yawned and smiled. “You helped me. I helped you. We helped the mouse. We were patient. We didn’t give up.”

Nutmeg nodded. “That is how helpers grow strong and kind.” She patted her tool belt and tucked herself near the pillow.

Pip turned off the lamp. The moonlight was soft and silver again. The cozy bedroom felt safe and warm. “Good night, Nutmeg,” Pip whispered.

“Good night, Pip,” Nutmeg replied. “Tomorrow, we can help again.”

The End.
