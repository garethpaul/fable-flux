# Dolly Dolphin's Kitchen Dance

Dolly the dolphin wiggled her shiny gray tail as she floated in her special water bowl in the warm, sunny kitchen. The smell of fresh cookies filled the air, and golden sunlight streamed through the window, making everything feel cozy and bright.

"Today I want to help make breakfast!" Dolly chirped happily, splashing her fins. She had watched her friend Sarah crack eggs and flip pancakes so many times. It looked easy!

Dolly tried to crack an egg with her nose. *SPLAT!* Egg goo went everywhere except in the bowl. "Oh no!" she squeaked.

Next, she tried to pour milk. *SPLASH!* The milk spilled all over the warm counter. Dolly's fins started to feel hot and tingly. Her heart began beating fast like a drum.

"This is too hard!" Dolly huffed, her voice getting louder. She felt angry bubbles rising in her tummy. "I can't do anything right!"

Sarah noticed Dolly's frustrated squeaks. "What's wrong, sweet Dolly?" she asked gently, kneeling by the water bowl.

"I wanted to help cook, but everything goes wrong!" Dolly cried, her eyes filling with salty tears. "I'm just a silly dolphin who can't do kitchen things!"

Sarah's warm hand touched the edge of Dolly's bowl. "Oh, Dolly, I understand feeling frustrated. When I feel upset like that, I do something special. Want to try it?"

Dolly sniffled. "What is it?"

"We dance our feelings away!" Sarah said with a smile. "Dancing helps our angry feelings float away like bubbles."

Sarah began moving her arms like gentle waves. "Breathe in slowly through your blowhole," she said softly. "Now breathe out and move your fins like ocean waves."

Dolly took a deep breath. The warm kitchen air felt good in her lungs. She wiggled her fins slowly, copying Sarah's wave movements.

"Now let's dance out the frustration!" Sarah giggled, spinning around the kitchen. "Shake your tail! Wiggle your nose!"

Dolly began spinning in her bowl, her tail making happy splashes. The warm water felt soothing on her skin. She wiggled her nose and clicked her tongue, making cheerful dolphin sounds.

"I'm dancing like ocean waves!" Dolly laughed, feeling the angry bubbles in her tummy pop and disappear. The kitchen felt bright and peaceful again.

"How do you feel now?" Sarah asked, still swaying gently.

"Much better!" Dolly beamed. "The dancing made my mad feelings swim away!"

Sarah clapped her hands. "That's wonderful! Now, would you like to help cook in a dolphin way?"

Dolly's eyes sparkled. "Yes, please!"

Sarah filled a special spray bottle with water. "You can spray water to clean the counter while I cook. That's perfect for a dolphin!"

Dolly squeezed the bottle with her fins, spraying gentle mists of water. "Look! I'm making it rain in the kitchen!" she giggled.

As Sarah flipped golden pancakes, Dolly sprayed the counter clean. The kitchen smelled like sweet syrup and felt warm with friendship.

"We make a great team," Sarah said, placing a tiny piece of pancake near Dolly's bowl.

"Yes!" Dolly agreed, munching happily. "And I learned something important today. When I feel frustrated and angry, I can dance and breathe to feel better. Then I can find new ways to help!"

The warm kitchen filled with the sound of Dolly's happy clicks and Sarah's gentle laughter.

The End.
