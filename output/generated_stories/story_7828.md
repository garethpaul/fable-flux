# Brave Cub in the Guest Room Adventure

The guest room was quiet and sunny. Soft curtains swished in the window breeze. A friendly bear cub named Coco stood at the door, holding a tiny broom. The guest bed had a bright quilt. A basket of clean towels sat on the chair. Dust sparkled in a sunbeam like tiny stars.

Mama’s voice floated from the hall. “Coco, please tidy the guest room. Aunt Maple is visiting tonight.”

Coco’s ears perked up. “Okay, Mama!” he said. He wanted to be helpful. But the room felt big and new. Coco took a small step in. The wooden floor felt cool on his paws. The air smelled like fresh soap and lemon.

Coco looked around and whispered, “I can use my imagination to make this fun.” He tapped the broom like a magic wand. “Broom, we are a brave clean-up crew!”

He marched in tiny steps—tap, tap, tap—moving his paws and tail. “Left paw, right paw. Sweep, swoosh!” The broom made a soft, shhh-shhh sound across the floor. Coco giggled. “We are sweeping sparkles off the moon!”

A gentle creak came from the closet. Coco’s round eyes grew wide. “Is someone there?” His heart beat thump-thump. He stood very still. Then he took a brave breath. “I can be brave and careful,” he told himself. “I can ask for help if I need it.”

He called toward the hall, “Mama? I heard a noise.”

Mama peeked in. “Closet doors can creak, little cub. Do you want me to open it with you?”

Coco nodded. “Yes, please.”

They opened the closet together. Inside were extra pillows, a folded blanket, and a box of puzzle pieces. Coco touched one pillow. It felt fluffy and soft. “Thank you, Mama,” he said.

“You’re welcome,” Mama smiled. “If you feel unsure, you can always call me. It’s brave to ask.”

Coco smiled back. “I will.” He felt warm and safe. Mama went to the kitchen, and Coco returned to his mission.

He picked up the guest pillows and patted them—puff, puff. “Pillows, you are clouds. Please float neatly on the bed.” He carefully placed them at the top. “Clouds in a row!” he cheered.

Next, he saw the towels on the chair. “Towels, you are sleepy caterpillars,” he whispered. He rolled one gently—roll, roll—and put it on the shelf. He rolled the next one. “Two cozy caterpillars.”

The sunbeam moved across the rug. Coco stretched his arms wide and took big, slow steps. “I am a quiet giant,” he said. “I use gentle feet.” He practiced movement—tiptoe, tiptoe—so the floor didn’t thump. He liked how the room felt peaceful when he was careful.

On the nightstand, Coco found a small basket with tiny soaps and a note. The note said, “Welcome.” Coco traced the letters with a claw. The soap smelled like oranges. “Mmm, it smells happy,” he said, sniffing again.

He noticed the guest bed’s quilt was a little crooked. “Quilt, let’s be a rainbow river,” he told it. He straightened the edges—smooth, smooth—and smoothed the top with slow paws. The fabric made a soft whisper sound. “There,” he said. “A calm river.”

Coco looked at the window. The curtain moved like a dancer. He danced too—swish, swish—then stopped. He thought about personal boundaries. “This is a guest room,” he told himself. “I can look, but I won’t touch the special drawer.” He pointed to the bedside drawer and nodded. “I ask before touching people’s things.”

To practice, he said out loud, “May I move this book to the shelf?” He imagined Aunt Maple answering, “Yes, thank you for asking.” Coco smiled and placed the book carefully on the shelf.

A soft bump came from under the bed. Coco felt a little shiver. “I can be brave,” he whispered. He got down on his belly and looked. The floor felt cool on his fur. Under the bed was a dust bunny and a toy button. Coco blinked. “Hello, dust cloud,” he said kindly. He swept it out—shhh—then gently scooped the button. “This might be for Aunt Maple’s sweater. I will put it in the dish so it is safe.”

He set the button in a small dish on the nightstand. “There you go.”

Coco stood up tall. He took a deep breath. The room smelled clean and bright. He listened. No more creaks. Just the soft tick of the hallway clock. He looked around. The bed was neat. The towels were snug. The floor was smooth. The closet was tidy. The window danced.

Mama came back with a smile. “Coco, this room looks ready and welcoming. How did you do it?”

Coco’s eyes sparkled. “I used my imagination to make chores fun. I was brave when I felt unsure. I asked for help. And I remembered personal boundaries. I didn’t open the drawer. I moved carefully.”

Mama hugged him. Her fur was warm. “I’m proud of you,” she said. “You used your body with care, your mind with creativity, and your heart with kindness.”

Coco’s tummy rumbled. “Can we have a snack?”

Mama laughed softly. “Yes, helper cub. Let’s celebrate your good work.”

As they walked away, Coco looked back at the guest room. It felt friendly, like it was smiling. He waved. “See you later, rainbow river bed. Sleep well, cloud pillows.”

Lesson: Imagination can make chores fun. Being brave means asking for help and moving carefully. Personal boundaries keep everyone’s things safe. When we use our minds, bodies, and hearts, we can do big things, even in a quiet guest room.

The End.
