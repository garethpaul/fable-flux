# Polly Parrot and the Painty Problem

Polly was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a big, curious beak. She loved the creative art room. It smelled like sweet glue and orange peel soap. Sunlight made shiny dots on the floor. Paints sat in little cups like rainbow puddles. Brushes stood up like tiny trees.

“Squawk! Today I make a masterpiece!” Polly said, puffing her chest.

Her friends were in the room too. Mia the mouse held a tiny brush. Ben the bunny wore a smock with stars. Kai the kitten had soft paws and a quiet purr.

Polly dipped her brush in red paint. Swish, swish. She wanted to paint a big red circle. But the circle looked wobbly.

“Oh no,” Polly said. Her feathers drooped. “It does not look like I want.”

She tried again. Swish. The brush slipped. The red circle looked like a tomato splat.

“Grrr, squawk!” Polly felt hot in her tummy. Her wings flapped fast. “I can’t do it!”

Mia looked up. “It’s okay, Polly. Take a slow breath with me.”

Polly puffed in. She puffed out. In… out… in… out.

She listened to the room. Drip, drip went the paint. Scratch, scratch went Kai’s crayon. Soft hum went the sunny window.

Polly felt a little calmer, but still sad. “I want a round circle. My brush is not listening.”

Ben hopped closer. “When I feel upset, I wiggle my toes and count. Want to try?”

Polly put one claw down, then the other. “One, two, three,” she said softly. Her body slowed down. Her shoulders relaxed.

Kai purred. “Sometimes I draw it big with my finger in the air first.”

Polly traced a big round circle in the air with her wing. It felt smooth and loopy. “Hmm,” she said. “That feels nice.”

She tried again with the brush. Swish… almost round. But the line bumped. Polly’s eyes filled with tears.

“I am frustrated,” she said. “I want to give up.”

Mia touched her wing. “You can do it, Polly. Let’s make a plan.”

Polly blinked. “A plan?”

“Step one: calm body,” Mia said. “Step two: try a new way. Step three: ask for help if you need it. Step four: keep trying.”

Polly nodded. “Okay. Step one, calm body.” She took two big breaths. In… out.

“Step two,” Ben said. “Try a new way. What else can make a circle?”

Polly looked around. On the shelf was a round sponge. On the table was a cup with a round rim. On the floor, a big roll of tape. Circles everywhere!

“Ooo!” Polly picked up the sponge. It felt soft and cool. She dipped it in red paint. Squish. She pressed it on the paper. Pop!

A perfect circle!

Her eyes got wide. “I did it!” she said. Her voice sounded bright and tingly.

Kai smiled. “You found your own way. That’s self-reliance.”

Polly made another circle with the cup. She tapped gently. Tap, tap. Another circle!

She made big circles, small circles, and tiny dot-circles with a pencil eraser. The paper looked like a field of red suns.

Then Polly tried blue. Drip, drop. She pressed the sponge again. Pop! Blue moons next to red suns. Yellow circles became lemons. Green circles became peas.

“Squawk, this is fun!” Polly sang. She flapped her wings softly, making little wind whooshes.

But then, her wing knocked a cup. Uh-oh! Splash! Blue paint spilled in a wiggly river.

Polly felt the hot tummy again. “Oh no, I made a mess!”

She squeezed her eyes. She remembered the plan. “Step one: calm body.” In… out.

“Step two: try a new way,” she said. She grabbed a towel and patted the spill. Pat, pat, pat. The paint made soft squishy sounds.

“Step three: ask for help,” she added. “Friends, can you help me?”

“Of course,” Mia said, bringing more towels.

“On it,” Ben added, hopping with a bucket.

Kai spread paper under the wet spot. “We’ve got this.”

Together they cleaned. The room smelled like soap again. The floor felt dry under Polly’s claws.

Polly smiled. “Thank you, friends. I can do hard things, but it’s nice to have help.”

She looked at her painting. It wasn’t just circles now. The blue river spill had made a wavy stripe. Polly dipped her brush in white and added little fish with smiley faces.

“This happy accident is part of my art,” she said. “I can use mistakes.”

Mia clapped. “It’s beautiful.”

Ben nodded. “You kept trying.”

Kai purred. “You stayed calm.”

Polly stood tall. “Today I learned a big thing. When I feel frustrated, I can breathe, make a plan, try new ways, and ask for help. I can be my own helper, and I can be a good friend too.”

The sun moved on the floor. The shiny dots danced. Polly signed her name with a swoop: P-O-L-L-Y.

She hung her art on the wall. The room felt warm and bright. Her heart felt calm and proud.

Polly fluffed her feathers. “Squawk! I did it.”

“Yes,” her friends said together. “You did.”

The End.
