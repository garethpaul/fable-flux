# Penny's Colorful Choice

**Penny the Parrot's Art Adventure**

Penny was a bright green parrot with beautiful red and blue feathers. She loved making art more than anything in the whole world. Her favorite place was the sunny art room at the community center.

"Good morning, art room!" Penny squawked happily as she flew in. The room smelled like fresh crayons and sweet glue. Colorful paintings hung on every wall. Tables were covered with paper, brushes, and jars of paint that sparkled like jewels.

Penny landed on her special perch near the window. Warm sunshine tickled her feathers. She could see all the art supplies from here - purple scissors, orange markers, and rolls of shiny stickers.

Today felt extra special. Penny wanted to make the most beautiful painting ever. She spread her wings wide and flew to the paint table.

"Oh my!" Penny gasped. There was only one jar of red paint left. It was her very favorite color - bright like strawberries and fire trucks.

Just then, little Maya walked into the art room. Maya had curly brown hair and wore a yellow dress with tiny flowers. She looked sad, with tears in her big brown eyes.

"What's wrong, Maya?" Penny asked gently, tilting her colorful head.

"I wanted to paint a picture for my mommy," Maya sniffled. "She loves red roses. But I can't find any red paint anywhere."

Penny looked at the jar of red paint in her wing. Her heart felt warm and fluttery. She really, really wanted to use that red paint for her special painting. But Maya needed it too.

Penny took a deep breath. The air smelled like chalk and paper. She could hear children laughing in other rooms. She closed her eyes and thought hard.

"Maya," Penny said softly, "would you like to share the red paint with me?"

Maya's face lit up like sunshine. "Really? You would share with me?"

"Of course!" Penny chirped. "We can take turns. You can paint your mommy's roses first."

Maya clapped her hands. "Thank you, Penny! You're so kind!"

They sat together at the big round table. Maya carefully painted beautiful red roses on white paper. The paint was smooth and thick. It made soft swooshing sounds as Maya moved her brush.

"Your roses are perfect!" Penny said, watching Maya work. "Your mommy will love them so much."

When Maya finished, she smiled big. "Now it's your turn, Penny!"

Penny dipped her brush in the red paint. But then she had an even better idea. "Maya, would you like to paint together? We could make one big picture!"

"Yes!" Maya giggled. "That sounds wonderful!"

Together, they painted the most amazing picture. Maya painted red roses. Penny added red butterflies with her brush held carefully in her beak. The painting was full of love and friendship.

When they finished, both friends stepped back to look. The painting was more beautiful than anything Penny could have made alone.

"Sharing made everything better," Penny said, her feathers ruffling with happiness.

Maya hugged Penny gently. "Thank you for being so kind. This is the best day ever!"

From that day on, Penny and Maya painted together every week. They learned that sharing art supplies made their creations even more special. The art room filled with their laughter and beautiful pictures.

Penny discovered that being kind felt even better than having all the paint to herself. And Maya learned that asking for help can lead to wonderful friendships.

The End.
