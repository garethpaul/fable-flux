# Koko the Koala Learns to Share

Koko the koala had soft, fluffy gray fur and the kindest brown eyes. She lived in a cozy house with her family, and today was very special - her cousin Penny was coming to visit!

"Penny will sleep in our guest room," said Mama Koala as she fluffed the pillows. The guest room had a comfy bed with a yellow blanket, a small wooden table, and a big window that let in warm sunshine.

When Penny arrived, she had a bright red suitcase and a big smile. "Hi, Koko!" she called out, giving her cousin a gentle hug that smelled like eucalyptus leaves.

Koko was excited to show Penny around. They played with blocks, read picture books, and had tea parties with stuffed animals. But as the day went on, something started to bother Koko.

Penny had brought her favorite teddy bear, Mr. Buttons, who had shiny black button eyes and a soft blue bow tie. All the other toys seemed to like Mr. Buttons better than Koko's toys.

"Can I play with your puzzle?" Penny asked politely.

"I guess so," Koko said, but her voice sounded a little grumpy. She watched as Penny carefully put the colorful pieces together.

Later, when they were in the guest room getting ready for bed, Koko saw Penny arrange Mr. Buttons on the pillow next to her other toys - including some of Koko's favorites that Penny had borrowed.

"Those are MY toys," Koko said, her voice getting louder. "You can't just take them to your room!"

Penny's eyes grew wide and sad. "But... I thought we were sharing," she whispered.

Koko felt her cheeks get warm. She wanted to grab all her toys back, but something made her stop. Penny looked upset, and that made Koko's tummy feel funny.

Koko sat down on the soft carpet and took three deep breaths, just like Mama had taught her. "I'm sorry I got mad," she said quietly. "I was feeling upset because I thought you liked your toys better than mine."

"Oh, Koko!" Penny said, sitting down next to her. "I love playing with your toys because they remind me of you. And I wanted Mr. Buttons to meet all your toy friends."

Koko tilted her head. "Really?"

"Really! And you know what? Tomorrow, would you like to sleep in the guest room with me? We could have a sleepover!"

Koko's eyes sparkled. "That sounds wonderful! And Penny... you can keep those toys in here tonight. I want you to feel happy and comfortable."

"Thank you, Koko. You're the best cousin ever," Penny said, giving her another warm hug.

That night, both koalas felt cozy and content. Koko had learned that being respectful meant thinking about how others feel, and that sharing made everyone happier. The guest room felt full of love and friendship.

From then on, whenever someone visited, Koko remembered to be welcoming and kind. She discovered that when you treat others with respect and understanding, you make the best memories together.

The End.
