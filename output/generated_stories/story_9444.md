# Tessa Turtle’s Study Corner Song Plan

Tessa was a shy turtle with a smooth, green shell. She liked quiet places. Her favorite spot was the study corner by the window. It had a small desk, a comfy pillow, and a basket of crayons. Today, the study corner smelled like new paper and orange peel. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor.

On the desk lay a little drum, a tiny xylophone, and a cup full of pencils. Tessa looked at them and took a deep breath. “I want to make a song,” she whispered. “But I feel shy. What if my song sounds silly?”

Her heart went thump-thump, like a soft drum. She touched the xylophone with one careful toe. Ting! A bright, tiny note popped into the air like a bubble. Tessa smiled. “That sounded happy,” she said.

She looked at a big sheet of paper. “Maybe I need a plan,” Tessa told herself. “A plan can help me be brave.” She wrote on the top: My Song Plan.

Step 1: Choose a beat.
Step 2: Add notes.
Step 3: Use my imagination.
Step 4: Practice, practice.
Step 5: Share if I feel ready.

Tessa nodded. “Okay. Step 1.” She tapped the desk gently with her toe. Tap… tap… tap-tap. She listened. The desk made a soft woody sound. She tried again. Tap… tap… tap-tap. “That’s my beat,” she said. “It sounds like walking on soft sand.”

A breeze moved the window curtain. It made a hush sound, like “shhh.” Tessa closed her eyes. “That is my whisper wind,” she said. She added a little drawing of a swirly breeze next to Step 1.

“Step 2: Add notes.” Tessa picked up the tiny mallet. Ting… tong… ting… The notes were like shiny marbles rolling in a bowl. She tried slow. She tried fast. She tried high and low. “I like high-low-high,” she decided. She drew three little stars on the paper: up, down, up.

“Step 3: Use my imagination,” Tessa read. She placed the pillow on the floor and sat. “Close eyes,” she told herself. “What do I see?” In her mind, she saw a blue pond. The water was smooth. Plink, plink, raindrops kissed the top. Frogs sang, ribbit-ribbit, like tiny drums. Fireflies blinked like bells.

“I hear the pond,” she whispered. “My song can be the pond.” She opened her eyes and hugged herself, feeling warm and brave.

Tessa tapped the drum very softly. Dum… dum… dum. She added the xylophone. Ting… tong… ting. She made the breeze sound with her lips: “Shhh.” She giggled. “That’s my whisper wind again.”

Her neighbor, a small paper bird taped on a pencil, wobbled as the breeze came in. “Hello, Paper Bird,” Tessa said. “Would you like to listen?”

The paper bird was quiet, but Tessa pretended it nodded. Pretend is part of imagination, she thought. She drew a little bird on her plan and wrote: Audience: Paper Bird.

“Step 4: Practice, practice,” she said. She played her beat and notes again. Tap… dum… ting… tong… shhh. Sometimes she missed. Sometimes the notes bumped into each other. Tessa took a slow breath. “It’s okay to try again,” she whispered. “Practice helps my song grow.”

Tap… dum… ting… tong… shhh.
Tap… dum… ting… tong… shhh.

The sounds began to fit together like puzzle pieces. Tessa’s shy feeling got softer. Her shell felt lighter. She swayed a little on her pillow. “This feels nice,” she said.

She added one more sound: she snapped her tiny turtle fingers. Snap… snap. “Raindrops!” she said. Snap… snap. The study corner filled with gentle noises: the drum’s soft dum, the xylophone’s bright ting, the desk’s tap, the whisper wind shhh, and the raindrop snaps.

Tessa looked at Step 5. “Share if I feel ready.” She peeked at the doorway. Mama Turtle was nearby, folding towels. “Mama?” Tessa called in a small voice. “I made a song. Would you like to hear it? It is my pond song.”

Mama Turtle smiled. “I would love to, Tessa. You planned it so carefully,” she said, sitting on the floor. “I’m listening.”

Tessa felt a flutter in her tummy. She placed her toe on the drum. “I will follow my plan,” she told herself. She began:

Tap… dum… ting… tong… shhh… snap… snap.

She played slowly. She imagined the blue pond, the frogs, and the fireflies. The study corner smelled like orange peel and felt warm and safe. The notes were little lights. They floated around Tessa and Mama like fireflies in the evening.

When she finished, Tessa looked up. “How did it sound?” she asked, her cheeks pink inside her shell.

Mama Turtle clapped softly. “It sounded beautiful,” she said. “I could hear your pond. I could feel your plan. You used your ears, your hands, and your heart.”

Tessa beamed. “I was shy, but planning helped me feel brave,” she said. “And imagination helped me make pictures in my song.”

Mama nodded. “Music can tell stories. Planning helps the music grow. Imagination makes it sparkle.”

Tessa wrote one last note on her paper: Lesson: Music + Planning + Imagination = Brave and Happy.

She taped her plan to the wall. “Tomorrow,” she said, “I will make a morning sunshine song.”

The paper bird wobbled again, as if to cheer.

Tessa took a deep breath. “Tap… dum… ting,” she hummed, feeling proud.

The End.
