# The Porch Song and the Gentle No

On a peaceful front porch, a small singing bird named Lulla landed on the wooden rail. The porch smelled like warm bread from the kitchen and fresh rain from the garden. The morning air felt soft, and the breeze tickled Lulla’s feathers.

“Chee-chee! Good morning!” Lulla sang. Her voice was sweet and bright like sunshine on a puddle.

A little raccoon named Rocco peeked from under a big potted fern. He had curious eyes and gentle paws. “Hi, Lulla! I love your songs,” he said, clapping softly. “Can I tell a story while you sing? I have a story about a moon cookie!”

Lulla smiled. “I like your stories, Rocco. But I need to practice my song for the porch concert tonight. I want it to sound smooth and kind.”

Rocco bounced. “I can help! I’ll tap the rail. Tap, tap, tap! It will be a drum. And I’ll sit very close. Like this!” He scooted right next to Lulla and started tapping, fast and loud.

Tap-tap-tap! Tap-tap-tap!

Lulla’s feathers trembled. The sound was too close and too fast. “Oh,” she said softly, “that is a lot of tapping.”

Rocco didn’t notice. He grinned and began his story at the same time. “Once there was a moon cookie that wanted to roll—”

Lulla tried to sing with the tapping and the story. Chee—che—ch… Her voice got tangled. Her tummy felt wiggly, not in a happy way. She took a small breath. The porch was still peaceful, but inside, Lulla felt a little storm.

She remembered her plan: practice kindly, sing calmly, and be a good friend. She also remembered something her feathered aunt had told her: “You can say stop in a gentle way. That is a boundary. Boundaries keep hearts safe.”

Lulla looked at Rocco. “Friend,” she said in a warm, clear voice, “I like your tapping and your story. But I need some space to practice. The tapping is too loud for me. And I can’t sing and listen at the same time. Can we take turns?”

Rocco froze. His ears drooped. “Oh. Did I make it bad?” He put his paws behind his back. “I didn’t mean to.”

Lulla shook her head and sang a soft note like a hug. “You did not mean to. Thank you for being excited. I just need a little quiet, and a little space, so my song can grow. After I practice, I want to hear your moon cookie story.”

Rocco’s eyes lit up like little porch lights. “We can take turns!” he said. “You sing first. I will sit on the step over there.” He pointed to a step a few hops away. “I’ll hold my paws still.”

“Thank you,” said Lulla. “That feels good to me.” Her wings relaxed.

Lulla closed her eyes and listened to the breeze. She smelled the sweet flowers and a hint of cinnamon from the kitchen. She felt the smooth rail under her feet. Then she began to practice. “Chee… chee-lee… la-la-la…” Her notes floated like bubbles. She tried again and again. A squeak here, a wobble there. She didn’t stop. She practiced slowly and kindly.

Rocco watched from the step. He swayed a little, but he kept his paws in his lap. He whispered, “You can do it, Lulla.”

Lulla tried the tricky part. “Chee-lee-lee—lo!” The note cracked. She giggled. “Oops! Practice time,” she said to herself. She breathed in, long and gentle, and tried again. “Chee-lee-lee—looo.” This time the note was smooth like warm honey.

“Wow,” Rocco whispered, eyes wide.

When Lulla finished practicing, she hopped down. “Your turn!” she chirped. “I am ready to listen. Do you want the rail drum?” She nudged the rail with her wing.

Rocco shook his head. “No thank you. The drum was too loud for you. I will use my soft paws.” He held up his paws and smiled. “Can I sit a little closer now?”

Lulla thought. She felt calm. “Yes, you can sit one hop closer,” she said. “That feels good to me.”

Rocco sat one hop closer and began. “Once there was a moon cookie that wanted to roll…” His voice was cozy, like a blanket. Lulla listened with bright eyes. She asked questions. “What color was the moon cookie?” He said, “Silver with sugar sparkles.” Lulla laughed. “Did it roll fast?” He said, “So fast it said wheee!”

They took turns all morning. Lulla sang a small song. Rocco told a small story. The porch felt peaceful again. The breeze clapped softly through the leaves.

That evening, neighbors gathered on the front porch for the concert. Fireflies blinked like tiny lamps. Lulla sang her practiced song, smooth and kind. Rocco sat on the step, smiling, paws in his lap. When Lulla finished, she bowed to her friend. “Now the moon cookie story,” she said.

Rocco told his story with gentle paws and a happy heart. Everyone cheered. Lulla chirped, “Thank you for taking turns, Rocco.”

Rocco nodded. “Thank you for telling me your boundary, Lulla. It helped me be a better friend.”

They bumped gently, wing to paw. The porch glowed with joy.

The End.
