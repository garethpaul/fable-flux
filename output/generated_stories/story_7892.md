# Rooftop Hops Around the World

Sunny the kangaroo joey loved to hop. Sunny’s paws were bouncy, his pouch was tiny, and his smile was big and bright. One breezy afternoon, Sunny climbed the stairs, hop-hop-hop, to the rooftop garden of his building.

The rooftop was wide and warm. The sun felt cozy on Sunny’s fur. Mint leaves smelled fresh. A little fountain went splish-splash. Colorful pots lined the edges like a rainbow path.

“Hello, sky!” Sunny chirped. “Hello, city!”

Down below, cars hummed. Pigeons cooed. In the corner, a small group of neighbors gathered around a big blue barrel and a stack of soap. They were setting up for a “Clean Hands, Happy Hearts” day.

Sunny bounced closer. “What’s that?” he asked.

Mina, a kind neighbor, waved. “Hi, Sunny! Today we are helping our building and our friends. We are making a handwashing station for everyone. Clean hands keep us healthy!”

Sunny’s ears perked up. “Can I help? I love to help!”

Mina smiled. “Yes! We need joyful helpers.”

Sunny puffed his chest. “I am a joyful helper!” He hopped, then stopped. In the corner of the rooftop, the wind rattled a cardboard box. Inside were crumpled flyers. A faded sign said: “Global Friends Night.”

Sunny tilted his head. “What’s Global Friends Night?”

Mina brushed dust off the sign. “It’s a special evening. We share music, food, and stories from many countries. We learn about the world and how we can care for one another.”

Sunny’s eyes sparkled. “Can we do it today? Can we learn about the world and wash hands too?”

Mina looked around. “We can try. But first we need water, soap, and a table. We also need to invite our neighbors.”

Sunny took a deep breath. His tummy fluttered. “That sounds big.” He looked at his small paws. “I am just a little joey.”

Mina knelt beside him. “Little paws can do big good. One hop at a time.”

Sunny nodded. “Okay. One hop at a time!”

First, the problem came. The handwashing barrel was too heavy. “Ooof,” groaned Mina. “It won’t move.”

Sunny tapped his chin. “We need many helpers.” He hopped to the edge and called, “Hello, neighbors! We need strong arms and happy hearts!”

Feet thumped up the stairs. Mr. Lee brought a dolly. Aunty Amara brought a bright cloth. A kid named José brought stickers shaped like stars.

“Push together!” called Sunny.

Clunk. Roll. Push. Together, they moved the barrel next to the fountain.

Next, they needed clean water. The hose was twisted like a snake. Sunny touched it. “It feels bumpy.” He twisted left. He twisted right. “It’s stuck!”

“Try again,” said José. “Slow and gentle.”

Sunny breathed in slowly. Twist-twist. The hose sighed free. Water rushed—whooosh! It sparkled in the sun. Sunny giggled as cool drops kissed his nose. “It tickles!”

Mina showed a song for handwashing. “Scrub palms, scrub backs, scrub thumbs, scrub nails,” she sang.

Sunny sang too. “Scrub-scrub, rub-rub, bubbles everywhere!” The soap smelled like lemons. Bubbles floated up, shiny and round. Pop! Pop! Pop!

Now for Global Friends Night. Sunny opened the dusty box. “Oh no,” he whispered. “The flyers are messy.” He felt a tiny pinch of worry. “Can I fix this?”

Mina put a gentle hand on his shoulder. “You can try, Sunny. That is how we learn.”

Sunny straightened his ears. He asked, “Who can share a song from their home?”

Aunty Amara lifted a drum. “I can share a soft beat from Ghana.” Thump-thump, pat-pat. The sound was warm like a hug.

Mr. Lee smiled. “I can show a bow from Korea.” He bowed slowly. Sunny tried it too. Bend, smile, bow. “I feel calm,” Sunny said.

José held a book. “I can say ‘hello’ in Spanish: Hola!”

Sunny clapped. “Hola!” He bounced to a little girl named Lila. “How do you say hello at your home?”

Lila beamed. “Shalom!” The word felt like a song. “Shalom!” Sunny sang back.

Together, they made new flyers with bright stickers and big letters. Sunny asked politely, “Please come to the rooftop! Learn, wash hands, and share!”

Evening came with pink clouds. The rooftop glowed with string lights. Neighbors arrived: tall, small, young, old. The fountain whispered. The mint leaves swished.

Sunny stood on a crate. His heart beat fast. “Welcome, friends!” he called. “We are from many places. We have many stories. We take care of each other. We wash our hands to stay strong. We share to stay kind.”

He showed the handwashing station. Children lined up. “Scrub palms, scrub backs,” they sang. Bubbles shimmered like tiny moons. The rooftop smelled lemony and fresh.

Then the sharing began. Aunty Amara’s drum spoke. Thump-thump. Mr. Lee bowed. José read a short poem. Lila taught a hello song. Sunny hopped in happy circles, his paws light and quick. He felt brave and useful and proud.

A little boy whispered, “Sunny, I was shy. But now I want to help too.”

Sunny grinned. “Helping feels good. We can all help.”

At the end, everyone held hands. They looked at the big, gentle moon. Sunny said, “Our world is wide, like the sky. We are different and the same. Clean hands keep us healthy. Kind hearts keep us close. When we volunteer together, we make our home bright.”

Neighbors nodded. Mina smiled. “You did it, Sunny. One hop at a time.”

Sunny giggled. “One hop, two hop, world-happy hop!”

The End.
