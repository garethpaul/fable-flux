# The Cinnamon Star Share

In a warm kitchen, a curious fox kit named Fenn padded on soft paws. The air smelled sweet, like cinnamon and sugar. The oven hummed a happy hum. Sunlight danced on shiny spoons. Fenn’s whiskers twitched. His tummy made a tiny rumble.

“Mama, what is that smell?” Fenn asked, nose wiggling.

Mama Fox smiled and stirred a big bowl. “Cinnamon star cookies,” she said. “They are warm, soft, and sprinkled with sparkles of sugar.”

Fenn’s eyes grew wide. “Can I help?” he asked.

“Yes,” Mama said. “Helping paws are good paws. You can press the star shapes.” She rolled the dough. Fenn pressed a star cutter into the dough. Squish! He lifted a perfect star. “I did it!” he giggled.

Soon, a tray of stars slid into the oven. Tick, tick, tick went the timer. Fenn listened and watched. He imagined the stars twirling inside, dancing in the warm light, singing, “We are cookies! La-la-la!”

The timer dinged. Mama opened the oven. Whoosh! Warm air puffed out, and Fenn smelled cinnamon hugs. The cookies were golden and glittery. “They look like tiny suns,” Fenn said.

Just then, Fenn’s cousin, Pip the fox kit, trotted in. Pip’s ears perked up. “Cookies?” Pip asked. “I love cookies.”

Fenn blinked. He looked at the tray. He saw one cookie that was just a little bigger than the rest. It was the biggest star. “I want the big star,” Fenn said, tail swishing.

Pip saw the big star too. His tail swished, swish-swish. “I want the big star,” Pip said.

Fenn’s tummy fluttered. His cheeks felt hot. “But I helped,” he said. “I pressed the stars.”

Pip’s ears drooped for a second, then popped up. “I came to visit,” he said. “I have a hungry tummy.”

The warm kitchen grew quiet. Only the tick of the cooling oven and the soft clink of a spoon sounded. The big star cookie sat in the middle, sparkly and still. Fenn felt a bubble of mad inside. Pip’s eyes looked shiny and hopeful. Both fox kits wanted the same cookie.

Mama Fox set down her spoon and knelt to their level. “Hmm,” she said. “We have a problem.”

Fenn nodded. “We both want the big star.”

Pip nodded too. “I want it very much.”

Mama Fox touched a paw to her chest. “Let’s take a slow breath.” They all breathed in. One, two, three. They breathed out. Whooo. The bubble of mad felt smaller.

“Now,” Mama said gently, “let’s use kind words. Let’s listen. Fenn, you go first.”

Fenn rubbed his paws. “I want the big star because I helped make it. I feel proud.”

“Thank you for sharing,” Mama said. “Pip, your turn.”

Pip held his tummy. “I want the big star because I am very hungry and the big one will help my belly feel full. I feel excited.”

Mama nodded. “You both have good reasons. You both have big feelings. How can we solve this so it feels fair?”

Fenn thought. Pip thought. The warm kitchen smelled safe and sweet. Fenn looked at the big star. He remembered the stars singing. He imagined the cookie was a real star ship, sailing through cinnamon clouds. “We could… share it?” Fenn said slowly. “We could make two halves.”

Pip’s ears wiggled. “Two halves can be fair,” he said. “But it will not look like a star anymore.”

Fenn’s eyes lit up. “What if we make a game? We can pretend the big star is a star ship. We can break it into two space wings! One wing for you. One wing for me. We can fly and nibble.”

Pip smiled. “Zoom! Space wings!”

Mama Fox smiled too. “That sounds like a kind and clever plan. It is fair, and it uses your imagination.”

Fenn picked up the big star. It was warm on his paws. Crunch! He gently broke it into two even wings. “One for you,” he said, handing a wing to Pip.

“And one for you,” Pip said, handing a wing back to Fenn, just to be sure it was fair. They both laughed.

They held their cookie wings up high. “Three, two, one—zoom!” they shouted. They ran around the kitchen island, soft paws pat-pat on the floor. They made whooshing sounds. “Whoooosh! Space foxes!”

They took little bites. Crumble, munch. Cinnamon sparkles tickled their tongues. “Mmm,” Fenn said. “My space wing tastes like stars and hugs.”

“Mmm,” Pip said. “My wing tastes like warm clouds.”

After the space flight, they came back to the tray. Many cookies still waited, small and shiny. Fenn picked a small star and gave it to Pip. “For your pocket,” he said.

Pip picked a small star and gave it to Fenn. “For later,” he said.

Mama poured little cups of milk. The cups clinked. The milk was cool and sweet. The fox kits dipped their cookies. Dip, crunch. They smiled with milk mustaches.

Mama Fox gave them each a soft napkin. “I am proud of you,” she said. “You used calm breaths. You used kind words. You listened. You made a fair plan. You used your imagination. You solved the problem.”

Fenn felt warm and happy inside. Pip did too. They bumped noses. “Friends share,” Fenn said.

“Friends care,” Pip said.

They both looked at the tray of stars and the warm kitchen that smelled like cinnamon. Fenn whispered, “When we have a problem, we can slow down, talk, listen, and find a fair way.”

Pip nodded. “And we can make it fun.”

The End.
