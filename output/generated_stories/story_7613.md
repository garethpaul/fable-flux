# Many Ways to Move on the Porch

The sun was warm, and the air smelled like fresh lemonade. A kind mouse named Miko sat on a peaceful front porch. The porch boards were smooth under Miko’s tiny paws. A soft breeze brushed Miko’s whiskers. Birds sang, “Chee-chee!” from the trees.

“I love this porch,” Miko said. “I want to play and move my body.”

Miko made a little plan. “I will lead an exercise game,” Miko squeaked. “We will jump. We will stretch. We will have fun!”

Soon, friends came by the porch steps. There was Lila the long cat with fluffy white paws. There was Bop the big frog with green spots. There was Didi the duck with a bright yellow beak. There was Sam the snail with a shiny, spiraled shell.

“Hello, friends!” Miko waved. “Do you want to exercise with me?”

“Yes!” quacked Didi.

“Ribbit! Yes!” said Bop.

“Meow, yes,” purred Lila.

Sam the snail smiled slowly. “I will try,” Sam said, “but I move slow.”

“That is okay,” said Miko kindly. “We all can move in our own way.”

Miko clapped tiny paws. “First, we jump like popcorn!”

Miko bounced up and down. Bop the frog leaped high. Didi hopped and flapped. Lila tried to jump but liked to stretch her long legs instead. Sam wiggled and made tiny lifts with the front of their soft body.

Miko looked around. “Hmm,” Miko thought. “Not everyone is jumping. Is that a problem?”

Lila looked shy. “I am not a jumper,” Lila said. “I like slow stretches.”

Sam looked worried. “I cannot jump,” Sam said. “I do not have legs like you.”

The porch went quiet. A bee buzzed by. The lemon smell grew sweet and strong.

Miko felt a pinch in the tummy. “I want us all to feel good,” Miko said softly. “But I only picked jumping. That was not open-minded.” Miko took a brave breath. “Friends, I am sorry.”

Bop blinked. “Ribbit? Sorry for what?”

“I did not think about our different bodies,” Miko said. “We are all different. We move in many ways. Different is good. Different is beautiful.”

Didi smiled. “Quack! I like moving in water and on land,” Didi said. “I can flap my wings too.”

Lila lifted her tail. “I can stretch and balance.”

Sam nodded. “I can glide and wiggle and make tiny waves.”

Miko’s ears perked up. An idea popped like popcorn. “Let’s make a Many Ways to Move game! We can take turns. Each friend can show a move that feels good for their body. We will try, and we will cheer.”

“Ribbit! That sounds fun,” said Bop.

“Quack-yes!” said Didi.

“Meow-mazing,” said Lila.

“I can do that,” said Sam.

Miko smiled big. “I will go first. My move is tiny jumps.” Miko made little bounce-bounce steps. Everyone tried their own way. Bop leaped. Didi hopped. Lila lifted one paw and did a soft bounce. Sam lifted the front of their body and made a happy wiggle.

“Now my turn,” said Lila. “I choose a stretch.” Lila reached her long front legs forward and arched her back. The porch smelled like warm wood. The breeze felt cool on their fur and skin.

“I can stretch a little,” said Miko, reaching paws forward.

“Ribbit,” said Bop, spreading legs wide.

“Quack,” said Didi, opening wings.

Sam made a slow, gentle bend. “This feels nice,” Sam said.

“Ribbit! My turn,” said Bop. “Frog leaps!” Bop leaped, but then looked at Sam. “We can make it small leaps or big leaps.”

Miko did tiny hops. Lila did a careful spring. Didi flapped and stepped. Sam made a small lift and glide. They all cheered, “Yay!”

Didi quacked, “Now we flap!” Didi flapped wings and swayed side to side. Miko swung arms like little wings. Lila swished her tail and lifted her paws. Bop moved his arms out like fins. Sam waved their eyestalks gently.

The porch was full of soft thumps, happy taps, and swish-swish sounds. The sun felt warm. The wind felt tickly. Everyone laughed.

Last, Sam said, “My turn. I choose glide and pause.” Sam glided slowly across a porch board and then paused to breathe in and out. “In. Out.”

Miko closed eyes and breathed. “In. Out.” The lemon smell made Miko smile. Bop breathed. Didi breathed. Lila breathed. It felt calm and good.

When the game ended, they sat in a circle. Miko passed tiny cups of cool water. The cups clinked softly. They sipped and rested.

“Friends,” Miko said, “today I learned something big. We are all different. We can move in many ways. When we listen and try new ideas, we all have fun.”

Lila purred. “Different is good.”

Bop said, “Ribbit! We did exercise together.”

Didi said, “We were open-minded.”

Sam said, “I felt welcome.”

Miko’s heart felt warm. “On this peaceful porch, everyone belongs,” Miko said. “We can be kind, we can be brave, and we can learn from each other.”

They all nodded. The birds sang again. The breeze whispered. And the porch felt even more peaceful than before.

The End.
