# Moonlight Fix-It on the Rooftop

On a quiet night, a wise old owl named Orla sat on a rooftop. The roof tiles were cool and smooth under her feet. The moon was big and round, like a shiny white cookie in the sky. The breeze smelled like rain and flowers. Orla blinked her golden eyes and hooted softly, “Hoo-hoo. What a peaceful night.”

Suddenly, tiny footsteps pattered up the stairs. A little child named Max popped out onto the roof. Max wore bright red pajamas and fuzzy socks. His cheeks were pink, and his eyes looked worried.

“Hello, little one,” said Orla, tilting her head. “What brings you to the rooftop so late?”

Max sniffled. “I did a bad thing,” he said. “I knocked over my sister Mia’s block castle. I was mad. Now she is sad. I ran away.”

Orla’s feathers rustled in the breeze. “Feelings can be big,” she said kindly. “But we can make amends. Do you know what that means?”

Max shook his head.

“It means we fix what we can,” Orla explained. “We say sorry with our words and our actions. We try to make it better. That is making amends.”

Max wiped his nose. “But I feel scared,” he whispered. “What if Mia stays mad?”

Orla blinked slowly. “We can be brave and gentle. We can use imagination and try again. That is resilience—getting up, even after a fall.”

Max looked at the moon. “Can we practice?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Orla. She tapped the roof with one soft wing. “Let us imagine the moon is a big round block. We will build a new castle out of moonlight.”

Max’s eyes widened. “A moon castle?”

Orla smiled. “Close your eyes. Breathe in the cool night air. Hear the wind whisper. Feel the roof under your feet. Now, pick up a moon block.”

Max reached out. In his mind, he held a glowing block. It hummed like a quiet song. “It’s warm,” he giggled. “And tingly.”

“Good,” said Orla. “Set it down gently. Pat-pat. Now another block. Tap-tap.”

They placed block after block. Orla hummed a soft hoot song. The stars twinkled like tiny bells. Max’s shoulders relaxed. He breathed in and out. “I like building,” he said. “It feels calm.”

“Sometimes,” Orla said, “we break things by accident or in anger. But our hands can also build and help. That is how we make amends.”

Max opened his eyes. The moon was still bright. The rooftop felt safe. “What should I say to Mia?” he asked.

Orla ruffled her feathers. “You can say: ‘I am sorry I knocked down your castle. I was mad. I will help you rebuild.’ Then you show it with your hands. You help.”

Max whispered the words. “I am sorry… I will help.”

“Very good,” said Orla. “Would you like to practice more?”

Max nodded. “What if Mia cries?”

“Then you listen,” said Orla. “You can say, ‘I hear you. Your castle was special.’ Listening is part of making amends too.”

Max took a deep breath. The night air felt cool and fresh in his nose. He could hear a faraway dog bark and the soft shush of leaves. “I think I can do it,” he said.

Orla gave a gentle hoot. “That is resilience. You are trying again.”

Just then, the rooftop door creaked. Mia peeked out, clutching a small stuffed bunny. Her eyes were shiny with tears. “Max?” she asked in a tiny voice.

Max stood up. His fuzzy socks slipped a little on the smooth tile, but he steadied himself. “Mia,” he said softly, “I am sorry I knocked down your castle. I was mad. I will help you rebuild.”

Mia sniffed. “You will?”

Max nodded. “Yes. I will use careful hands. We can make it even taller.”

Mia hugged her bunny. “Okay,” she said. “It made me sad. My castle was special.”

Max nodded again. “I hear you. Your castle was special.” He reached out his hand. “Can we build together?”

Mia took his hand. “Together,” she said.

Orla watched with warm eyes. “You have brave hearts,” she said. “Let us practice right now. Here on the rooftop, we can plan our new castle.”

Max and Mia sat on the cool tiles. They pretended to pick up moon blocks and star flags. “Pat-pat,” said Max. “Tap-tap,” said Mia. They giggled as a soft breeze brushed their faces, smelling like rain.

“We can add a window,” Mia said. “For Bunny.”

“And a tall tower,” Max said. “For you.”

“And a door that goes boom-boom when it closes,” Mia added, laughing.

Orla nodded. “You are using imagination to build. You are using your hands to help. This is making amends.”

When they were ready, the children stood up. Max looked at Orla. “Thank you,” he said. “You helped me be brave.”

Orla bowed her head. “You did the brave part,” she said. “Remember: When we break, we can help fix. When we hurt, we can help heal. That is how we make amends.”

The moon slipped behind a cloud, and the night felt soft and sleepy. Max and Mia held hands and went back downstairs to rebuild the real block castle. Orla stayed on the rooftop, eyes shining like tiny lamps.

“Hoo-hoo,” she sang. “Build with kind hands. Try again.”

The End.
