# Benny's Water-Saving Discovery

Benny the beaver loved his cozy bathroom. It had a big white bathtub, a shiny faucet, and fluffy blue towels. Every morning, Benny would brush his teeth with the water running. *Splash, splash, splash!*

But Benny had a big problem. He used too much water! When he brushed his teeth, the faucet ran and ran. When he washed his paws, the water flowed like a waterfall. Even when he wasn't using it, the water kept pouring down the drain.

"Water, water everywhere!" Benny sang happily, watching it swirl away.

One day, Benny's friend Penny the duck came to visit. She noticed something strange outside Benny's window. The pond where they loved to swim was getting smaller and smaller.

"Oh no, Benny!" Penny quacked sadly. "Look at our pond! There's barely any water left for the fish and frogs."

Benny looked outside and gasped. The pond that used to sparkle in the sunshine was now just a tiny puddle. The lily pads were dry and brown. A little frog sat on a rock, looking very thirsty.

"But I need water to stay clean," Benny said, his tail drooping. "I'm a beaver! Water is important to me."

Penny nodded. "Water is important to all of us, Benny. That's why we need to use it wisely."

Benny felt confused. How could he stay clean AND save water? It seemed impossible!

"I don't know how to fix this," Benny sighed. "Maybe I'm just not smart enough."

"That's not true!" Penny said kindly. "You're very smart, Benny. You just need to learn new ways to do things. Let me show you some tricks!"

Penny taught Benny to turn off the faucet while brushing his teeth. *Click!* The water stopped flowing.

"Turn it on to rinse, then turn it off again," Penny explained. "You still get clean teeth, but you use much less water!"

Next, Penny showed Benny how to fill a small cup with water for washing his paws instead of letting the faucet run. The water felt just as warm and soapy.

"Wow!" Benny said, his eyes growing wide. "I never thought of that!"

They practiced taking shorter baths too. Benny learned to wet his fur, turn off the water while he scrubbed with soap, then turn it back on to rinse. It felt like a fun game!

"This is actually easier than I thought," Benny said proudly. "And I still feel squeaky clean!"

Every day, Benny practiced his new water-saving habits. Turn off, turn on. Fill the cup, wash the paws. Short baths with lots of bubbles but less water.

At first, it felt strange. Benny had to remind himself each time. But soon, it became as natural as swimming!

A week later, Benny and Penny looked out the window. The pond was filling up again! The water sparkled blue and clear. Fish jumped happily, and the frogs croaked cheerful songs.

"Look what we did together!" Penny cheered.

Benny felt warm and proud inside. "I learned something new, and it helped everyone!"

From that day on, Benny became the best water-saver in the whole forest. He taught other animals his special tricks. Soon, all his friends were saving water too.

"I used to think I couldn't change," Benny told his mama that night. "But when I tried new ways and practiced, I got better and better!"

Mama Beaver hugged him tight. "That's how we grow, my dear Benny. We learn, we practice, and we help our world stay beautiful."

Benny smiled as he brushed his teeth with the water turned off. Saving water wasn't hard at all - it just took a little thinking and a lot of practice!

The End.
