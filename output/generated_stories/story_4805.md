# Otto Otter’s Balcony Day

Otto was a playful otter with bright eyes and soft whiskers. He lived with Nana on the top floor, where a small balcony had a tiny garden. There were pots of mint, cherry tomatoes, and happy yellow flowers. Bees hummed. The air smelled like fresh leaves and warm soil.

“Good morning, little garden!” Otto chirped, peeking through the rails. His paws felt the cool floor. “Today will be busy and fun.”

Nana smiled. “Remember, Otto,” she said kindly, “we take care of ourselves first, so we can help others well.”

Otto nodded. He wanted to volunteer today. The neighbor, Mr. Kim, needed help watering his plants while he visited his sister. Otto loved to help. Helping felt like sunshine inside his tummy.

Otto rushed to the door, but his tummy rumbled. “Oh!” he giggled. “My belly is singing.”

Nana handed him a small bowl of oatmeal with blueberries. “Fuel first,” she said. “Then play and help.”

Otto took a deep breath. The oatmeal smelled sweet. He ate slowly. “Mmm,” he hummed. His body felt warm. His brain felt bright. “Thank you, Nana.”

“Also,” Nana added, “bring your water bottle. Helping takes energy. And don’t forget your sun hat.”

Otto tossed his little blue hat on his head. He hugged his water bottle. “I am ready!”

He stepped onto the balcony garden. Sunlight tickled his whiskers. A cool breeze brushed his fur. “Hello, mint!” he whispered, rubbing a leaf. “You smell like yummy tea.” He listened to the soft buzz of a bee. “Hello, little bee. I will be gentle.”

Otto checked his list. “First: water our plants. Second: water Mr. Kim’s plants. Third: rest and stretch. Fourth: snack and a story.”

He filled his small watering can. “Swish-swash,” the water went. He gave the tomatoes a drink. He patted the soil. “Not too soggy, not too dry,” he said, remembering Nana’s words.

Then Otto noticed a tiny snail on the rim of a pot. The snail had a shiny shell like a small moon. “Oh! A new friend,” Otto whispered. “What is your name?”

The snail wiggled its feelers. Otto giggled. “I will call you Moon.” He watched Moon slide slowly, leaving a silver line. Discovery made Otto’s heart bounce. He wanted to tell Nana right away.

But he looked at his list. “Water first, then talk,” he told himself. “I can be honest with my plan.” That was integrity: doing what he said he would do.

He finished watering the balcony garden. “All done,” he said, feeling proud. He took a sip from his water bottle. “Ahh.” He stretched his arms. “Reach for the sky, touch the toes,” he sang.

Now it was time to volunteer. Otto went next door with Nana. Mr. Kim’s balcony had rosemary, lettuce, and a small lemon tree. The lemons were green and bouncy. The air smelled piney and bright.

“Thank you, Otto,” said Mr. Kim on a video call. His voice was warm. “Water the soil until it is dark, not muddy. And please check the leaves.”

“I will,” said Otto. “I will be careful and kind.”

He looked closely. He found a leaf with tiny dots. “Hmm,” he said softly. “I will tell Nana. I will not hide it.” He wanted to be honest, even if it was small.

“Nana,” Otto said, “this leaf has dots.”

Nana nodded. “Good eyes, Otto. We will wipe the leaf gently.” They used a soft cloth and water. The leaf looked happier.

Otto gave each plant a careful drink. “Here you go, rosemary. Hello, lettuce. You are crunchy and green.” He hummed a watering song. “Drip, drop, do not stop. Sip, sip, little tip.”

His paws felt a bit tired. His shoulders drooped. “I need a break,” he said to himself. He remembered: care for me, so I can care for you.

“Let’s rest,” Nana said. They sat on a cushion in the shade. The breeze kissed Otto’s cheeks. He took three slow breaths. “In… out… in… out… in… out…” His body felt calm and cozy.

After resting, Otto noticed a fallen lemon flower on the floor. It was white and smelled sweet, like candy and sunshine. “This is pretty,” Otto said. “I will make a tiny thank-you bouquet for Mr. Kim.” He picked a flower that had already fallen and a small rosemary sprig from the clippings.

They placed the tiny bouquet in a bottle cap with a drop of water. “A gift of care,” Nana said.

Otto smiled big. “Volunteering is helping. Helping is kind. And taking care of me helps me help more!”

They finished watering. Otto took another sip of water and nibbled two apple slices. Crunch! Crunch! His energy came back. He felt bouncy again.

Back at their own balcony, Otto looked for Moon the snail. Moon was resting under a leaf. “Good nap, Moon,” he whispered.

Otto washed his paws with warm, soapy water. The bubbles smelled like oranges. He dried them on a soft towel. His paws felt clean and happy.

Nana hugged him. “You watered, you rested, you were honest, and you were kind. How do you feel?”

Otto placed his paws on his heart. “I feel proud. I learned: take care of me, tell the truth, and help with a smile.”

Nana nodded. “That is a big lesson.”

Otto waved toward Mr. Kim’s balcony. “Bye, plants! See you tomorrow!” He twirled in a small circle. The sun made his hat shine. The garden buzzed and breathed, and Otto did too.

Today, Otto discovered new things, helped his neighbor, and cared for himself. When we rest, eat well, drink water, and speak honestly, we can help others even better.

The End.
