# Fawn’s Rolling Team Adventure

A gentle deer fawn named Dapple peered out the window of the family car. The car was quiet and warm. The seat was soft. The sun made tiny sparkles on the dashboard. “Good morning, car,” Dapple whispered. “Let’s have a kind day.”

Mama sat in the front seat. “Buckle up, Dapple,” she said kindly.

“Click!” went Dapple’s buckle. Dapple loved that safe sound.

Outside, a breeze moved the leaves, swish-swish. Birds sang, tweet-tweet. Dapple’s ears twitched. “I like those bird songs,” Dapple said. “They say, ‘Hello!’ in their bird language.”

Mama smiled in the mirror. “Yes, many ways to say hello. In English we say, ‘Hello.’ In Spanish, ‘Hola.’ Can you try?”

Dapple nodded. “Hello! Hola!” The words felt bouncy in Dapple’s mouth. “It’s fun to learn words,” Dapple giggled.

The family car hummed softly, mmmmm. The road was smooth. Then, suddenly, the car slowed. “Uh-oh,” Mama said. “A tiny trash bag is stuck under the car. We need to take care of it.”

The car stopped by a shady tree. The air smelled like pine and fresh grass. Dapple heard a little flap-flap under the car.

“We will work as a team,” Mama said. “Teamwork makes big jobs feel smaller.”

Dapple’s heart beat fast, thump-thump. “I can help,” Dapple said, taking a brave breath.

Mama nodded. “We need to move safely and gently. First, we turn off the car. Then we look. Then we help our Earth by putting trash in the right bin. That is called sustainability. It means we take care of our home, together.”

Dapple’s tail flicked. “Together!” Dapple liked that word. It felt warm.

Mama opened the door. The day felt cool on Dapple’s fur. Dapple hopped down carefully. Hop, hop. “I will be the Look Helper,” Dapple said.

“I will be the Lift Helper,” Mama said. “Let’s talk about the plan. Planning is fair. Everyone has a job.”

“That is fairness,” Dapple said. “We share the jobs.”

Dapple crouched low and peered under the car. It was dark and a little dusty. “I see the bag,” Dapple whispered. “It is stuck on a little hook.”

Mama knelt. “Thank you, Look Helper. I will lift the edge of the bag. You watch and tell me if it moves.”

“Okay,” said Dapple. “One, two, three… move!”

The bag rustled, crinkle-crinkle. “It moved!” Dapple said, tail wiggling. “But it is still a little stuck.”

“We can try a gentle wiggle,” Mama said. “Gentle is kind.”

“Wiggle-wiggle,” Dapple said, making a little dance with tiny hooves. The movement made Dapple giggle and brave at the same time.

The bag slipped free. “We did it!” Dapple cheered.

“Teamwork!” Mama cheered back. They bumped noses softly. Boop!

Dapple picked up the bag by the clean corner. “Where does it go?” Dapple asked.

“Good question,” Mama said. “Let’s be fair and put it in the right place. This bag is not for recycling. It goes in the trash bin. That helps our Earth.”

They walked to the park bin nearby. Step, step, step. The path felt smooth. The air smelled like flowers. A squirrel chattered, chirp-chirp, like it was saying, “Gracias!” Dapple smiled. “That means ‘Thank you’ in Spanish,” Dapple said.

“Sí,” Mama said with a grin. “And ‘Thank you’ to you, too.”

Dapple dropped the bag in the bin. Plop! “We used our bodies and our words,” Dapple said proudly. “We moved, we talked, we helped.”

Back at the car, the seat felt cozy again. Mama started the engine. “Now we can keep going.”

Dapple leaned back and listened to the soft hum, mmmmm. “Teamwork is fair,” Dapple said. “We share jobs. We take turns. We help the Earth.”

Mama nodded. “You are a wonderful teammate. How do we say ‘good job’ in Spanish?”

“Buen trabajo!” Dapple sang.

They drove past a little garden. A sign showed a green leaf. Dapple read slowly, “Save… our… Earth.” Dapple clapped tiny hooves. “I want to save our Earth.”

“We do it with small, kind actions,” Mama said. “Picking up trash. Using less. Reusing things. Sharing. Moving our bodies to help, and using kind words to plan.”

Dapple looked at her hooves. They felt strong and helpful. “My body can help,” Dapple said. “My words can help, too. Hello. Hola. Thank you. Gracias.”

Mama smiled. “Words are tools, and so are gentle hooves.”

They came to a stoplight. Red. Yellow. Green. Dapple watched the colors glow. “The light takes turns,” Dapple said. “That is fair.”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Everyone gets a turn. That’s teamwork on the road.”

Dapple’s eyes felt sleepy from the warm sun. But Dapple was happy. The car hummed. The world rolled by. Dapple whispered, “Today we learned: work together, move safely, be fair, and take care of our Earth.”

Mama squeezed Dapple’s hoof. “That is the lesson.”

Dapple smiled a small, cozy smile. “Teamwork feels good,” Dapple said. “Teamwork is love.”

“Teamwork is love,” Mama echoed.

And the family car rolled on, gentle and green, carrying a fawn who knew how to help.

The End.
