# Tilly Turtle’s Trip Plan

Tilly was a shy little turtle. Her shell was smooth and green, like a shiny leaf after rain. Today, Tilly sat in the family car with Mama Turtle and Papa Turtle. The car smelled like lemon wipes and warm seat cushions. Sunlight made tiny sparkles on the window.

“We are going to visit Grandma!” Papa Turtle said. His voice was gentle and happy.

Tilly tucked her head a little. “I hope I do it right,” she whispered. Her tummy felt fluttery, like soft bubbles.

Mama Turtle smiled and passed a small blue bag to Tilly. “Let’s make a plan,” she said. “Planning helps us feel calm.”

Tilly peeked inside the blue bag. It was empty. The zipper made a zzziip sound. “A plan?” she asked.

“Yes,” Mama said. “We think first, we pack next, and we check last.”

Papa Turtle tapped the steering wheel. “Three steps! Think, Pack, Check,” he said, tapping three times: tap, tap, tap.

Tilly took a slow breath. The car air felt cool. “Okay,” she said softly. “Step one: Think.”

“What do we need for the car trip?” Mama asked.

Tilly looked around. She felt the seat belt across her shell. Click! “We need snacks,” she said. Her tummy made a tiny rumble. “We need wipes for clean hands. We need a book. And we need a little trash bag.”

“Great thinking,” Mama said.

“Step two: Pack,” Tilly said, a little louder.

Mama passed a small snack box. Inside were apple slices that smelled sweet and crunchy, and little crackers shaped like stars. Tilly placed them in the blue bag. “Snacks,” she said.

Papa handed over a soft pack of lemon wipes. They felt cool through the plastic. “For hygiene,” Papa said. “Clean hands keep us healthy.”

Tilly nodded and put them in. “Wipes,” she said.

She added her favorite picture book. The pages made a whisper sound when she flipped them. “Book,” she said.

Mama passed a tiny roll-up bag. “Trash bag,” Mama said. Tilly slipped it in.

“Step three: Check,” Tilly said. She pointed and counted with her little turtle finger. “Snacks. Wipes. Book. Trash bag.” She looked up. “All ready.”

The car hummed down the street. Houses went by like slow blocks. Birds sang outside: chirp, chirp. Tilly felt proud. The fluttery feeling got smaller.

Then Papa said, “Uh-oh.” He looked in the mirror. “Traffic is very slow.”

Tilly peeked out. So many cars! Red lights glowed like tiny strawberries. The car stopped. Tilly heard the soft tick-tick of the blinker.

“My hands feel sticky,” Tilly said, looking at her crackers. “And I want to share a snack with Baby Bunny,” she added, pointing to the car seat next to her. Baby Bunny was Tilly’s little cousin, coming with them to see Grandma. Baby Bunny wiggled his ears and squeaked.

Tilly remembered the plan. “Think,” she said to herself. “What do we do?”

She opened the blue bag and took a wipe. It felt cool and smelled lemony. She wiped her hands slowly. Swish, swish. She smiled. “Clean hands,” she said. “Hygiene!”

She broke a cracker star in half. “Here you go, Baby Bunny,” Tilly whispered. Baby Bunny took it and giggled, “Nom nom!” Crumbs sprinkled like tiny snow. Tilly opened the little trash bag and caught the crumbs. “We keep the car clean,” she said. “Organization.”

Mama looked back. “Tilly, you are planning and helping,” she said. “That is generous.”

Tilly’s cheeks felt warm, but in a happy way. “I like our plan,” she said.

After a while, Baby Bunny rubbed his eyes. “Sleepy,” he squeaked.

Tilly thought again. “Think,” she whispered. “What will help?” She took her picture book. “I will read softly,” she said.

She turned the pages gently. “Once there was a blue bird,” she read in a tiny voice. The paper sounded shhh, shhh. Baby Bunny’s eyes got heavy. He yawned and snuggled into his seat.

Papa Turtle smiled at the road. “Green light,” he said. The car rolled forward. Vroom, smooth and slow.

Soon they reached Grandma’s house. The driveway smelled like flowers and fresh grass. Grandma Turtle came out with open arms. “My sweet travelers!” she said.

Tilly climbed out with her blue bag. She felt brave. She had a plan, and it worked. She handed Grandma a clean, lemony wipe for her hands. “For hygiene,” Tilly said shyly.

Grandma laughed softly. “Thank you, kind Tilly. You planned so well.”

Inside, Grandma asked, “What did you learn today?”

Tilly stood tall on her little feet. “Three steps,” she said. “Think, Pack, Check. Planning helps me feel calm. It keeps us clean and organized. And it helps me share.”

Grandma nodded. “A smart turtle and a generous heart,” she said.

Tilly took a deep breath. It smelled like Grandma’s warm bread. Her tummy felt cozy. “Next time,” Tilly said, “I will make a list with little pictures. That will help me plan even more.”

Mama and Papa clapped softly. Baby Bunny clapped too, pat-pat-pat.

Tilly smiled. She was still a little shy, but now she felt ready for many trips, one good plan at a time.

The End.
