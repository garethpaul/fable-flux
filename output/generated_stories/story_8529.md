# Panda on the Rooftop

Poppy was a patient panda. She lived in a cozy home with a flat rooftop. The rooftop was sunny and warm. A soft breeze tickled her ears. Birds chirped, “Tweet-tweet!” and clouds floated like cotton candy.

Poppy liked to exercise on the rooftop. She stretched her arms. She wiggled her toes. She took slow panda breaths. “In,” she said. “Out,” she said. She wanted to learn to jump rope. She had a bright yellow rope with blue stripes. It smelled like new rubber. It felt smooth in her paws.

“I can do it,” Poppy whispered. “I will try and try.”

Poppy put the rope behind her feet. She swung it over her head. Swish! The rope tapped her toes. Tap! She did not jump in time. The rope made a tiny slap on the ground. Poppy blinked. Her tummy felt wiggly.

“It’s okay,” she told herself. “I am patient. I will try again.”

She swung the rope. Swish! Tap! The rope touched her toes again. Poppy’s nose scrunched. She felt a little hot in her cheeks. “Hmm,” she hummed. “I feel frustrated.”

Poppy put a paw on her heart. “Hello, feelings,” she said softly. “You can be here. I am safe.” She took three slow breaths. In. Out. In. Out. In. Out. The breeze cooled her fur.

Her friend, Miko the sparrow, landed on the rooftop rail. “Tweet! What are you doing, Poppy?”

“I am learning to jump rope,” Poppy said. “It is hard. I keep missing.”

“Tweet! You are trying,” Miko said. “Trying is exercise for the brain!”

Poppy smiled. “Yes. I will keep going. I can be calm and patient.”

Poppy had an idea. She would break the task into little steps. “Step one: swing the rope. Step two: bend my knees. Step three: jump!” she said.

She tried again. Swish. Bend. Jump! The rope bumped her heels. Tap. Poppy giggled a tiny giggle. “Oops!”

Miko chirped, “Let’s warm up.” He hopped from foot to foot. “Hop-hop, hop-hop!”

Poppy copied. She hopped on one paw. She hopped on two paws. She swung her arms like windmills. She touched her toes. She reached to the sky. The rooftop smelled like fresh rain and warm stone. The sun made her fur feel toasty.

“Now I feel ready,” Poppy said.

She held the rope. “I will use slow swings,” she said. “I will listen to the rope.”

Swish. The rope whispered over the ground. Poppy bent her knees. She watched the rope. She jumped a tiny jump. Boing! The rope slid under her feet.

“I did one!” Poppy cheered.

Her heart beat fast. Thump-thump. She felt proud. She felt brave. “I will try for two.”

Swish. Boing. Swish. Tap. The rope touched her toes again. Poppy’s smile drooped. She felt a little sad.

She sat on a soft mat. “I need a rest,” she said. “My body is tired. My feelings are big.” She sipped cool water. She listened to the city sounds—honk, hum, and distant laughter.

Miko peeped, “What helps you when feelings are big?”

Poppy thought. “Breathing helps. Kind words help. Moving helps. Music helps, too.”

“Let’s make a rope song,” Miko chirped.

Poppy clapped a slow beat. Clap… clap… clap. “Swing, bend, jump,” she sang. “Swing, bend, jump.” Miko sang along, “Tweet, tweet, thump!”

They added a rhythm. Swish-swish, clap. Swish-swish, clap. Poppy felt her body relax. Her tummy felt calm. Her paws felt ready.

“I will try again,” she said. “I will be patient with my practice.”

She picked up the rope. She sang her song. “Swing, bend, jump.” Swish. Boing. “Swing, bend, jump.” Swish. Boing. “Swing, bend, jump.” Swish. Boing.

One jump. Two jumps. Three jumps! Poppy laughed, a happy panda laugh. “Ha-ha! I did three!”

The wind whooshed gently past her ears. Her feet made soft pat-pat sounds on the mat. Her rope made a sweet swish like a whispering ribbon.

“I will try for more,” she said. “If I miss, I can try again.”

She tried again. Swish. Boing. Swish. Boing. Swish. Tap. She missed. She stopped. She breathed. “It’s okay to stop and breathe,” she said. “I am learning. I am growing.”

Miko fluffed his feathers. “I see your persistence,” he said. “You keep going in small steps. That is strong.”

Poppy nodded. “I am practicing patience. I am listening to my body. I am moving with care.”

She tried one more time. This time, she felt the rhythm in her paws. She heard the rope’s swish. She bent her knees just right. She jumped five times in a row!

“I did five!” she shouted. “I kept trying. I was calm. I used my song. I used my breath. I used my plan.”

Miko flew in a little circle. “Tweet-tweet, hooray!”

Poppy put down the rope. She stretched her arms wide. She felt proud, strong, and peaceful. The sky looked bright and blue. The rooftop felt like a happy place.

Poppy smiled. “Today I learned that persistence helps me grow. When I feel big feelings, I can stop, breathe, and try again. Exercise is fun. Creativity helps me find my rhythm.”

She patted the yellow rope. “I will practice tomorrow, too.”

Miko chirped, “With patience and practice, you can do big things, little by little.”

Poppy nodded. “I will keep going, one hop at a time.”

The End.
