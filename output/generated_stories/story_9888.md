# Bear Cub’s Treehouse Story Day

The sun peeked through green leaves, and a soft breeze tickled the wooden steps of a cozy treehouse. Inside, a friendly bear cub named Bumble rubbed sleepy eyes and sniffed the warm, sweet smell of pine. Tap-tap-tap! Raindrops from last night still plinked from leaves onto the roof. “Good morning, treehouse,” Bumble said with a smile. “Today, I will write a story!”

Bumble loved stories. He wiggled his soft paws and set out paper and a big blue crayon. He could hear birds chirping, “Cheep, cheep!” and feel the smooth wood floor under his toes. “I will write about my friends,” he said. “Everyone will be in it.”

Just then, Squeak the squirrel popped his head in the window. “Hi, Bumble! What are you doing?”

“I’m writing a story,” Bumble said. “Do you want to help?”

Squeak’s tail swished. “Yes! I can think of a big adventure.”

Soon, Tilly the turtle climbed up the ladder, slow and steady. “Hi,” she said in a soft voice. “May I join?”

“Of course,” Bumble said. “Everyone is welcome.”

Bumble held the big blue crayon and wrote the title. He pressed hard, and the crayon made a thick line. “Oops,” he said. “That line is crooked.”

Squeak giggled. “That’s okay. We can fix it.”

Bumble took a deep breath. “I can try again.” He flipped the page. “New page. New try.” His heart felt braver.

Tilly smiled. “That is resilience,” she said gently. “You keep trying.”

Bumble nodded. “Yes. I will keep trying.”

They began. Bumble wrote slowly. “Once there was a treehouse—”

Just then, a gust of wind whooshed through the window. The paper fluttered. The blue crayon rolled, rolled, rolled and fell through a crack in the floor. Clunk!

Bumble blinked. “Oh no. My crayon.”

Squeak peered into the crack. “I can’t reach it.”

Bumble’s ears drooped. He felt a tight knot in his tummy. He wanted to stomp and say, “This is not fair!” But he paused. He put a paw on his chest and breathed in, then out. He remembered to be calm and kind. “I feel sad,” he said, “because my crayon fell. Can we find a fair way to keep writing?”

Tilly lifted a small bag. “I brought crayons,” she said. “There is red, green, and purple. We can share.”

Bumble’s face brightened. “Thank you, Tilly. Sharing is fair.”

They each picked a color. Bumble took purple. Squeak chose green. Tilly held red. “We can take turns,” Bumble said. “We each say a line.”

Squeak squeaked happily. “I go first!”

Bumble thought. “Let’s make a plan. We go in a circle. Squeak, then Tilly, then me.”

They all agreed. Bumble wrote Squeak’s line: “Once there was a treehouse with friends inside.” He listened as the crayon whispered skritch-scratch on the page. It felt nice.

Tilly spoke slowly. “The friends used calm words when they had big feelings.”

Bumble smiled and wrote Tilly’s line. Then it was his turn. “They worked together and did not give up.” He wrote carefully, even when the letters wobbled.

Suddenly, a small knock came at the door. Tap-tap. A shy rabbit named Dot peeked in. Her ears were long and soft. “Hello,” she whispered. “May I come in? I heard about the story.”

Bumble said, “Yes, Dot! Everyone can help. We take turns, and we listen.” He scooted over to make space. The floor felt cool and smooth as Dot sat down. He noticed Dot’s paws trembled a little. “Would you like a turn after me?” he asked.

Dot nodded. “Thank you.” Her ears perked up.

They kept going. Dot’s line was sweet and gentle: “The friends used kind voices and shared tools.” Bumble wrote it slowly, sounding out each word. Sometimes he made a squiggle instead of a letter. “Oops,” he said softly, “I made a squiggle.”

Tilly touched his shoulder. “It’s okay to make mistakes,” she said. “You are learning.”

Squeak added, “Mistakes help us grow!”

Bumble’s tummy knot loosened. He breathed in the piney air and smelled the warm sunlight. “I will try again,” he said. “I can fix it.” He drew a small star next to the squiggle. “This star means I kept trying.”

They wrote about a rainy day, and about sharing snacks. They wrote about building a tiny ramp so Tilly’s little wheels on her book cart could roll over a bump. Bumble drew the ramp with purple lines. He said, “This helps everyone. That is inclusion.”

Squeak chirped, “And it’s fair!”

Dot smiled. “And it feels kind.”

At last, the story was done. Bumble read it out loud. His voice was warm and proud. The words felt like soft blankets. “Our story says: We use calm words. We share. We wait our turn. We find ways for everyone to join. And when things go wrong, we try again.”

The friends clapped. The claps were gentle, like pat-pat-pat on the wood. Bumble’s heart glowed. “Today, I learned resilience,” he said. “I kept going, even when my crayon fell. I took deep breaths. I asked for help. I tried again.”

Dot said, “I learned fairness. We took turns.”

Tilly added, “I learned inclusion. We made a ramp and space for everyone.”

Squeak laughed, “I learned that writing can be teamwork!”

Bumble looked around the bright, happy treehouse. “We did it together,” he said. “When we keep trying and use kind words, we can do big things.”

They taped the story to the wall. The purple, red, and green letters shone in the light. Outside, the wind was soft again. Inside, the friends felt safe and strong.

The End.
