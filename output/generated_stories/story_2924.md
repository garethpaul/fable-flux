# Polly's Special Space

High up in the tallest oak tree lived Polly, a bright green parrot with rainbow feathers on her wings. She loved her cozy treehouse home with its wooden walls that smelled like fresh pine and windows that let in warm, golden sunlight.

Polly was very creative. She painted beautiful pictures with berry juice and made music by tapping on hollow nuts. Her treehouse was filled with colorful artwork hanging from the branches and tiny instruments she had made herself.

One sunny morning, Polly was working on a special painting when she heard loud voices below.

"Look at that cool treehouse!" shouted Benny the bear. "Let's go up there!"

"Yeah!" called out Ruby the raccoon. "I want to see everything inside!"

Before Polly could say anything, Benny and Ruby came climbing up the rope ladder. They burst through her door without knocking.

"Wow, this place is amazing!" Ruby said, grabbing one of Polly's paintings. "Can I have this?"

"And I want to play with these instruments!" Benny said, picking up Polly's favorite drum made from a coconut shell.

Polly felt her tummy get tight and twisty. Her friends were touching her special things without asking! The treehouse felt too crowded and noisy.

"Um, excuse me," Polly said quietly, but her friends didn't hear her over the noise they were making.

Polly's heart beat fast like a little drum. She wanted to hide, but this was her home. She took a deep breath, spread her beautiful wings wide, and spoke up louder.

"Stop, please!" Polly said in her clear, strong voice.

Benny and Ruby stopped and looked at her with surprise.

"This is my special space," Polly explained, her voice gentle but firm. "I like having friends visit, but I need you to ask before touching my things. And please knock on my door first next time."

Ruby's ears drooped. "Oh! I'm sorry, Polly. I didn't know."

"Me too," said Benny, carefully putting down the coconut drum. "We were just so excited to see your treehouse."

Polly felt proud of herself for speaking up. Her tummy didn't feel twisty anymore.

"It's okay," she smiled. "Would you like me to show you around properly? I can tell you about my paintings and maybe we can make music together."

"That sounds wonderful!" said Ruby.

"But first," said Benny, "we should go back down and knock on your door the right way."

Polly giggled as her friends climbed back down the ladder. Soon she heard a gentle "knock, knock" on her door.

"Come in!" Polly called happily.

This time, Benny and Ruby entered quietly. They listened as Polly told them about each painting and asked permission before touching anything.

"May I try playing this drum?" Benny asked politely.

"Of course!" Polly said. "And Ruby, would you like to paint with me?"

They spent the whole afternoon creating art and making music together. The treehouse filled with laughter and beautiful sounds.

As the sun began to set, painting the sky orange and pink, Polly's friends thanked her for sharing her special space.

"Thank you for teaching us about asking first," Ruby said.

"Your treehouse is even more fun when we're respectful," added Benny.

After they left, Polly looked around her treehouse. Everything was just as she liked it, and she felt happy and proud. She had learned that speaking up for herself made everyone feel better.

From that day on, all of Polly's friends always knocked first and asked before touching her things. And Polly always felt comfortable sharing her special creative space with them.

The End.
