# Miko Mouse Says Thank You

Miko was a kind little mouse who lived in a tidy nest behind a bookshelf in a home office. The office smelled like warm paper and lemon polish. The computer made a soft hum, and the window let in gentle light. Miko loved to listen to the click-click of the keyboard and the whirr of the printer. He felt safe there.

Every morning, Miko peeked out to see his friend, Sam, who worked at the desk. Sam wore soft sweaters and smiled with his eyes. “Good morning, little neighbor,” Sam would say. Miko would wave his tiny paw.

One sunny day, Miko had a big idea. “I want to make a thank-you card for Sam,” he squeaked. “I feel grateful for this cozy home office. Sam keeps it clean. He shares warm light. He is kind to me.”

Miko took a small scrap of paper that had fallen near the recycling bin. He picked up a tiny crayon stub he had found and saved. “I will use only what I need,” Miko said. “That is conservation. I can practice using small things.”

Miko set the scrap on a little bottle cap to keep it steady. He took a breath. “I can do this if I practice,” he told himself. He drew a big heart. The crayon felt bumpy and scratchy on the paper. “Scritch, scritch,” it went. Miko smiled.

Just then, he saw a shiny stack of new paper on Sam’s desk. “Oh! So clean and smooth,” Miko whispered. He wanted to use the shiny paper. But he remembered. “Sam needs that paper for work. I will respect his things. I can use my scrap. That is a good boundary.”

Miko kept drawing. His paws wobbled. The heart looked a little crooked. He tried again on the other side. This time, it was rounder. “Practice helps me grow,” Miko said. “I am proud.”

Miko sniffed the air. He smelled tea—warm and sweet. He heard the soft tick-tock of the clock. He felt happy and calm. He wrote tiny words: “Thank you, Sam. From Miko.”

He wanted to add a bow. He saw a bit of ribbon hanging from a gift bag in the trash. “I can reuse this,” Miko said. He tugged the ribbon gently. It came free with a whispery swish. He tied a small knot. “There. Pretty and careful.”

Miko felt brave. He wanted to give the card to Sam. But sometimes, when Sam was busy, he said, “Please wait, little friend.” Miko had learned to accept that. “I can wait,” he told himself. “I can respect personal boundaries.”

Miko watched. Sam was on the phone, talking softly. Miko sat on the rug and practiced breathing. In and out. He listened to the hum of the heater. He tapped his toes to the gentle sound of rain on the window. Plip-plop. Plip-plop.

When the call ended, Sam stretched and smiled. “Hello, Miko,” Sam said. “What are you doing today?”

Miko stepped forward, holding up the tiny card. “I made this for you,” he squeaked. “Thank you for the warm light. Thank you for the quiet hum. Thank you for sharing your space. I feel safe here.”

Sam’s eyes got shiny. “For me?” he asked softly. He bent down. Miko nodded. “I used scrap paper and old ribbon,” Miko said. “I wanted to save new things for your work. I practiced my drawing. I tried hard.”

Sam opened the little card. The heart made him smile a big, warm smile. “Miko, this is the kindest card,” he said. “Thank you for your gratitude. Thank you for taking care of our space and our things. That is very thoughtful.”

Miko felt a glow in his chest, like a warm cup of cocoa. “I am happy,” he said. “Saying thank you makes my heart feel big.”

Sam placed the card by the keyboard. “I will keep it right here,” he said. “Would you like a tiny corner of the soft cloth for your nest? I have a clean scrap just for you.”

Miko blinked. “Is it okay to take it?” he asked.

“Yes,” Sam said. “This piece is extra. I am offering it.”

Miko accepted with a smile. “Thank you,” he said. He tucked the soft cloth into his bag. It felt cozy and gentle.

“Would you like to practice drawing together for a few minutes?” Sam asked. “I have some small, used sticky notes we can reuse.”

“Yes, please!” Miko squeaked. They drew shapes and clouds and smiles. “Scritch-scratch,” went the crayons. They used only a few notes and put the rest back. They cleaned up the tiny bits of paper and put them in the recycling bin. The office looked neat and calm.

When it was time to rest, Miko waved. “I learned today,” he said. “I learned to be grateful. I learned to save and reuse. I learned to practice, even when it is hard. I learned to ask, and to wait, and to accept ‘yes’ or ‘no.’”

Sam nodded. “Those are big lessons, little friend.”

Miko curled up in his nest. The lemon smell was soft. The computer hummed like a lullaby. Miko closed his eyes and whispered, “Thank you, thank you, thank you.”

The End.
