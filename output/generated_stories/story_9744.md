# Ollie Owl’s Tidy Garden Towers

Ollie was a wise old owl with soft, gray feathers and bright, golden eyes. He lived on a cozy balcony garden high above the busy street. The balcony smelled like mint and sweet strawberries. Tiny wind chimes tinkled, and little pots sat in rows—well, almost rows.

One sunny morning, Ollie blinked at the pots and sighed. “Hoo-hoo,” he said softly. “My garden is all mixed up.” The small shovel hid under a leaf. The watering can lay on its side. Seeds were in tiny paper packets, some open, some closed. The blocks for building plant towers were scattered everywhere.

Ollie fluffed his feathers. “Today, I will fix it! I will organize. Then I will build!”

Just then, his friend Pip, a curious ladybug, landed on a tomato leaf. “Good morning, Ollie!” Pip squeaked. “What are you doing?”

“I am getting ready,” Ollie said. “I want to build strong plant towers. But first, we must tidy up.”

Pip buzzed in a circle. “I like to build! Where do we start?”

Ollie smiled. “We start with a plan.”

He tapped his wing to his head. “First, we look. Then, we sort. After that, we stack. Then we label. Last, we build!”

Pip clapped tiny wings. “Look, sort, stack, label, build!”

They looked around. The sun warmed their backs. The soil smelled rich and earthy. A breeze danced over the leaves. Ollie blinked again. “I see tools, seeds, pots, and building blocks.”

“Let’s sort!” Pip said.

Ollie nodded. “Tools in one basket, seeds in a tin, pots in a row, blocks in a box.”

They worked together. Pip tugged a tiny trowel. “Oof! It is heavy.”

“You can do it,” said Ollie. “Use your strength and your smarts.”

Pip rolled it across the soil. “I did it!”

Ollie scooped the lost shovel and placed it in the tool basket. He gently closed the seed packets and slid them into a bright blue tin. “Seeds go here,” he said. “Safe and snug.”

They lined the pots from smallest to biggest. “Small, medium, big,” Pip sang.

“Excellent,” said Ollie. “Now we stack the blocks.”

The blocks were smooth and wooden. They felt warm from the sun. Tap, tap, tap went Ollie’s claws as he tested each one. “Flat ones at the bottom,” he said. “Then the medium ones. Then the little ones.”

“Why, Ollie?” asked Pip.

“So our tower will be strong,” said Ollie. “When we organize, we make building easier.”

Pip nodded. “Organize first. Build better.”

Ollie brought out paper circles and string. He wrote simple labels with a thick, black marker. The marker smelled a little like berries. “Tools,” he said. “Seeds.” “Pots.” “Blocks.”

He tied the labels on each basket and box. Pip traced the letters with a tiny toe. “Now we know where things live!”

“Exactly,” said Ollie. “Everything has a home.”

“Can we build now?” Pip asked, eyes bright.

“Yes,” said Ollie. “Let’s build plant towers!”

They set the biggest pots near the railing, where the sun kissed the leaves. Ollie picked up the flat blocks and set them under the pots to make steady feet. “Listen,” he said. They both were quiet. The city hummed below. The wind chimes chimed. The pots did not wobble. “Steady,” said Ollie.

“Now medium blocks,” Pip said, placing them with care. “Tap, tap.” She smiled. “They feel strong.”

They built little steps with the small blocks so the small pots could climb up and get sunlight, too. Ollie and Pip worked slowly. They checked each step. They fixed little wobbles. They asked questions and tried ideas.

“Hmm,” said Ollie. “This one leans.”

Pip thought. “What if we flip the block?”

They tried it. The pot stood straight. “Good problem-solving,” Ollie said.

When the towers were done, the balcony looked neat and happy. The mint leaves waved. The tomatoes gleamed red. The strawberries peeked out like tiny hearts.

Pip spun in the air. “It is beautiful!”

Ollie spread his wings. “Now we water.” He reached for the watering can. It was right in the tool basket. “Aha! Easy to find,” he said.

They watered each pot. Drip, drip, drip. The soil drank. The plants sighed, or at least it sounded like it.

Pip asked, “What about the seeds?”

Ollie smiled and opened the blue tin. “We saved a spot.” They filled two small pots with soil. It felt cool and soft. Pip dropped in basil seeds—plop, plop. Ollie covered them with a gentle wing. “Sleep well, little seeds,” he whispered.

They put the seed tin back in its home. They put the tools back, too. Pip read the labels out loud. “Tools here, seeds here, pots here, blocks here.”

Ollie nodded. “We looked, sorted, stacked, labeled, and built. That is organization.”

Pip puffed with pride. “We did it together!”

Ollie hooted happily. “When we organize first, we can solve problems and build strong things. And we can find what we need.”

Pip landed on Ollie’s head like a tiny hat. “I will remember.”

The sun began to set. The sky turned peach and gold. The wind chimes sang a soft song. Ollie and Pip sat and watched their tidy garden towers.

Ollie whispered, “Everything in its place. A calm, happy space.”

Pip whispered back, “Look, sort, stack, label, build.”

They both smiled.

The End.
