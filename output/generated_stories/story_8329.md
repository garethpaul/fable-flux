# Leo's Special Sign

Leo was a brave little lion cub with golden fur that sparkled in the morning sun. He lived in a cozy house with a big, sunny backyard where he loved to play. The grass felt soft under his paws, and the warm sunshine made him feel happy and strong.

One bright morning, Leo noticed something that made his little heart sad. The pretty flowers in his backyard were getting trampled! People walking their dogs were cutting through his yard and stepping on Mama's beautiful roses and daisies.

"Oh no!" Leo said, his voice small but determined. "The flowers are getting hurt. They won't be pretty anymore!"

Leo ran inside to find his mama. "Mama, Mama!" he called. "The flowers need help! People are walking on them!"

Mama Lion smiled at her brave little cub. "That is a problem, Leo. What do you think we could do to help?"

Leo thought very hard, tapping his little paw on his chin. "Maybe... maybe we could make something to tell people about the flowers?"

"That's a wonderful idea!" Mama said. "What could we make?"

Leo's eyes lit up like sunshine. "A sign! We could make a sign with words and pictures!"

Mama gathered colorful paper, crayons, markers, and glue. The craft supplies smelled like new possibilities, and Leo felt excited as he touched each item.

"First," Leo said proudly, "I want to write 'Please.'" He carefully drew the letters, his tongue sticking out in concentration. "Please is a nice word that shows we're being polite."

"Very good!" Mama encouraged. "What else should our sign say?"

Leo thought about the beautiful flowers. "Don't step on our flowers," he said, drawing each letter slowly. "And maybe... 'Thank you!'"

Next, Leo drew pictures. He made bright yellow suns, pink and red flowers, and even a little lion family. His crayons felt waxy and smooth as he colored.

"I want to draw a path too," Leo said, adding a winding walkway. "So people know where they can walk!"

When the sign was finished, Leo held it up proudly. The paper felt sturdy in his paws, and the colors were bright and cheerful.

"Now where should we put it?" Mama asked.

Leo carried his sign outside, feeling the warm grass under his feet. He looked around carefully. "Right here!" he said, pointing to the front of the flower garden. "Where everyone can see it!"

Mama helped Leo stake the sign in the ground. It stood tall and proud, with its colorful letters and pictures dancing in the gentle breeze.

The very next day, Leo watched from his window. A lady walking her dog stopped to read the sign. She smiled and walked around the flowers on the grass path instead!

"It worked!" Leo roared happily, bouncing up and down. "My sign helped save the flowers!"

More people read Leo's sign throughout the week. They all walked carefully around the flower garden, and the roses and daisies grew tall and beautiful again.

"You solved the problem all by yourself," Mama said, giving Leo a big hug. "You used your words, you made something helpful with your paws, and you cared about your community."

Leo purred with pride. He had learned that when you see a problem, you can speak up and use creativity to help. Making things with your hands and using kind words could make the whole neighborhood a better place.

From that day on, Leo always looked for ways to help others with his crafting and his brave, caring heart.

The End.
