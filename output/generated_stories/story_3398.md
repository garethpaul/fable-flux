# Jump-Jump Joey and the Puzzle Party

Jump-Jump Joey was a joyful kangaroo joey. He loved to bounce and giggle. Today, the playful living room was warm and bright. Sunlight made little squares on the soft rug. The rug felt squishy under Joey’s big feet. The air smelled like fresh crayons and warm toast.

On the floor sat a big, colorful puzzle. The pieces were shiny and smooth. They made a soft click when they touched. Joey clapped his paws. “I can do it! Jump-jump!” he cheered.

Mama Kangaroo smiled. “You can try, Joey. Remember to be patient. Remember to be kind.”

Joey nodded. He picked up a blue piece. “This goes here,” he said. He tried to fit it, but it did not click. “Hmm.” He tried again. No click.

His friends hopped in through the open door. Pip the Possum waved her tail. “Hi, Joey!”

Roo-Roo the Roo, a little older than Joey, hopped quietly. He wore big glasses. Click the Cricket chirped from a small toy drum. “Chirp-chirp! Puzzle time!”

Joey’s tail thumped with joy. “Let’s play together!”

They all sat on the rug. Joey liked to go fast. He grabbed three pieces. “I’ll put them all!”

Roo-Roo spoke softly. “May I try one, Joey?”

Joey frowned. He wanted to do it himself. “I’m fast. I’m best,” he said.

Pip tilted her head. “We can help. We can take turns.”

Click chirped a tiny beat on the toy drum. Tap-tap-tap. “Team, team, team!”

Joey took a deep breath. He felt wiggly and hot in his cheeks. He wanted the puzzle to be done. He wanted it to be perfect. He wanted it to be his.

Mama Kangaroo said gently, “Joey, we all play in our own ways. Some go fast. Some go slow. Tolerance means we make room for everyone’s way. We listen. We wait. We are kind.”

Joey looked at Roo-Roo’s big glasses. Roo-Roo smiled shyly. “I look for corners,” Roo-Roo whispered. “I like corners. It helps me feel calm.”

Joey’s ears perked up. “Corners?” He looked at the puzzle and saw four pieces with pointy corners.

Pip sniffed the pieces. “These smell like fresh paint,” she said, giggling. “I like the red ones!”

Click hopped onto a green piece and chirped, “I sort by color. Chirp!”

Joey felt his heart slow down. Tap-tap, steady. “Okay,” he said. “We can try your ways too.”

They made a plan:
- Roo-Roo looked for corners.
- Pip sorted red pieces.
- Click tapped a beat for turns.
- Joey looked for blue pieces with waves.

Click chirped, “First Roo-Roo. Tap-tap!”

Roo-Roo picked up a corner piece. His paws shook a little. He tried once. It didn’t fit. He tried again. Click! It fit. Roo-Roo smiled so big his glasses slid down. “Oh!” he said softly.

Joey clapped. “Good job! You did it!”

Now Pip’s turn. She found a red piece with a big flower. She tried to push it in fast. It bent a little. “Oops,” she said. She took a slow breath. She lined it up. Click! It fit.

“Kind and careful,” Mama said. “That is skill-building.”

Click chirped a happy drum riff. “Joey’s turn! Tap-tap-tap!”

Joey took a blue piece with curvy waves. He tried one way. No click. He tried another way. Still no click. He felt his cheeks get hot again. “I can’t,” he whispered.

Roo-Roo nudged the piece gently. “Turn, turn,” he said.

Pip smiled. “We can wait. Take your time.”

Joey tried one more time, slow and gentle. Click! It fit. His chest felt light, like a bubble. “I did it!”

They kept going. The living room filled with happy sounds: soft clicks, tiny taps, quiet chirps, and giggles. The sun moved across the rug. The puzzle picture began to grow: a bright garden with flowers, a shiny pond, and a rainbow.

Then they reached a tricky spot. Two pieces looked the same. Joey and Pip both reached out.

“I want that one,” Joey said.

“I want it too,” Pip said.

They looked at each other. Joey remembered Mama’s words: “Tolerance means we make room for everyone’s way.”

Joey pulled his paws back. He took a deep breath. “Pip, you try first,” he said.

Pip’s eyes sparkled. “Thank you, Joey.” She tried the piece. It didn’t fit. She laughed. “Oops! Your turn!”

Joey tried the other piece. Click! Perfect fit. They both giggled.

At last, there was one piece left. The final piece was shiny and shaped like a little cloud.

Click chirped, “Group turn! Tap-tap!”

They all held the piece together—small paws, big paws, and tiny cricket legs. Together, they pressed it in.

Click! The puzzle was complete. The picture shone with colors. The rug felt extra soft under them, and the room smelled like warm toast again. Joey’s heart felt warm too.

Mama Kangaroo clapped. “You did it! You were patient. You were kind. You cooperated.”

Joey looked at his friends. “I learned to wait. I learned to listen. I learned that we can play different ways. That is tolerance.” He smiled. “And when we keep trying, we get better. That is persistence and skill.”

Roo-Roo nodded. “Corners help.”

Pip giggled. “Red is fun!”

Click chirped a happy beat. “Team!”

Joey bounced once, softly. “I like our team.”

The End.
