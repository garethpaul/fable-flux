# Ellie’s Rooftop Rest

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and bright, curious eyes. One sunny afternoon, Ellie climbed the wide stairs to the rooftop garden of her building. The rooftop was a special place with warm tiles, fluttering flags, and pots of green mint and sweet basil. The air smelled like fresh leaves and sunshine.

“I want to help everyone today!” Ellie said, swishing her little tail. “I can water the plants, carry the toys, and sing songs to make friends smile.”

Her friend Pip, a tiny pigeon with shiny feathers, landed on the railing. “Coo-coo! That is a big plan,” Pip said. “Do you feel ready?”

Ellie nodded fast. “Yes! I can do it all.”

First, Ellie picked up a big watering can. It was heavy. She felt the cool water slosh. “I can carry it,” she told herself. She poured water on the mint. Drip, drip, drip. The mint leaves sparkled and smelled extra yummy.

Then, Mimi the mouse poked out from behind a flower pot. “Ellie, can you carry my basket up the ladder?” Mimi asked. “It’s full of picnic snacks.”

“I can help!” Ellie said. She lifted the basket with her trunk. Crunch, crunch went the crackers inside. The basket was heavy too. Ellie’s legs felt wobbly, like jelly.

Next, Tiko the turtle called, “Ellie, can you push my toy car? It is stuck.”

“I can help!” Ellie said again. She pushed the little car. Her feet slipped on a sunny tile. Her heart thumped. Her ears felt hot.

Pip watched with soft eyes. “Coo-coo, Ellie, how do you feel right now?”

Ellie stopped. She listened to her body. Her trunk felt tired. Her tummy made a tiny rumble. She took a slow breath. In… out…

“I feel… tired,” Ellie said quietly. “And a little hungry.”

Pip nodded. “That is important to know. What can you do to take care of you?”

Ellie looked around. The breeze tickled her cheeks. A shady umbrella flapped gently. The mint smelled kind and cool. “Maybe I can rest,” she whispered. “Maybe I can have a snack.”

Mimi came closer. “It’s okay to rest,” Mimi said. “We can wait.”

Tiko nodded. “Slow is good,” he said with a smile.

Ellie felt a little worried. “But I promised to help,” she said. “If I stop, I will let you down.”

Pip fluttered to her shoulder. “Coo-coo. When you care for you, you grow strong. Then you can help even better. Self-care is not selfish. It is kind.”

Ellie thought about that. She placed the basket down gently. She set the watering can by the mint. She sat under the umbrella. The shade felt cool on her skin. The tiles were warm under her feet. She took three slow breaths. In… out… in… out… in… out…

“I need a break,” Ellie told her friends. “My body feels tired, and my tummy is hungry. I will rest and drink water. Then I can try again.”

“Thank you for telling us,” Mimi said. “That is good communication.”

Pip brought a small cup. “Water for you,” he chirped.

Ellie sipped. The water was cold and sweet. She nibbled a crunchy cracker Mimi shared. She chewed slowly and smiled. Her tummy felt happy. Her ears cooled down. She closed her eyes and listened to the rooftop sounds: the soft hum of bees, the flap-flap of flags, and the faraway whoosh of a bus.

After a short rest, Ellie stood up and stretched. “I feel better,” she said. “I can help again, but I will go slow.”

“That’s a growth mindset,” Pip said. “You learned, and now you try again.”

Ellie picked up the watering can, but this time she asked, “Mimi, can you help me carry it?”

“Yes!” said Mimi, holding one side while Ellie held the other. Together they watered the basil. Drip, drip, drip. The leaves smelled peppery and bright.

Then Ellie looked at Tiko’s toy car. “Tiko, let’s count to three and push together,” she suggested.

“One, two, three!” they said. Push! The car rolled free. Tiko cheered.

Ellie felt proud and calm. “Taking care of me helped me take care of you,” she said. “I can help, and I can rest. Both are good.”

Pip winked. “Coo-coo. And you shared your feelings. That is brave.”

Ellie had one more generous idea. She picked some mint leaves, with permission, and shared a tiny minty tea with her friends. The steam smelled fresh. The cups were warm in their paws and wings.

“To self-care,” Ellie said, tapping cups gently. “To listening to our bodies. To asking for help. To trying again.”

Everyone smiled. The rooftop felt full of sunshine and friends.

That evening, as the sky turned pink, Ellie folded a soft blanket and made a cozy corner for next time. She placed a small sign that read, “Quiet Rest Spot.” She put extra cups and a water jug nearby.

“If anyone feels tired,” Ellie said, “this spot is for you, too.”

Mimi hugged her trunk. Tiko patted her foot. Pip cooed happily.

Ellie looked at the stars beginning to twinkle. Her heart felt light. She had helped, learned, and grown. She had taken care of herself and shared care with others.

The lesson was clear in her gentle smile: When we listen to our bodies and speak our feelings, we grow strong. Rest, food, water, and asking for help are acts of kindness. With self-care, we can be generous and brave, together.

The End.
