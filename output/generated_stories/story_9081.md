# Benny Beaver’s Busy Idea

Benny was a busy beaver. He worked in a cozy home office by the window. The sun made warm stripes on his desk. His desk smelled like fresh wood. His tail thumped soft and happy on the floor.

Benny had big front teeth and a big job. He liked to build and make new things. He called it “innovating.” He said, “Innovating means trying new ideas!” He wiggled his whiskers and smiled.

On his desk, Benny had paper, pencils, a squeaky stapler, and a little clock that went tick-tick-tick. He also had a laptop that hummed. “Mmmmmm,” it sang like a tiny bee.

Benny was making a “Turn-Taker,” a new tool to help friends take turns. He said, “When we share and take turns, we all get a turn to smile.” He wanted to help his friends on a big team call.

Benny opened a box. “Clink!” went the buttons. “Clack!” went the blocks. He held a shiny red knob. “Ooo, smooth,” he said. He sniffed a glue stick. “Minty!” He giggled.

But the clock went tick-tick faster, and Benny felt busy, busy. “I need help,” he said. He tapped the keys. “Click, clack.”

Just then, his friends popped onto the screen. “Hi, Benny!” said Dot the Duck with a happy quack. “Hi!” said Max the Mouse with a squeaky squeal. “Hello,” said Tilly the Turtle, very slow and calm.

Benny showed them his plan. “This is the Turn-Taker. It lights up and sings. It tells us whose turn is next.”

Dot flapped her wings. “Quack! I want to try first!”

Max bounced. “Me first! Me first!”

Tilly blinked. “We can take turns,” she said. “First, we listen. Then, we do.”

Benny nodded. “Yes. We will use the Turn-Taker to help us take turns testing it.”

He pressed the red knob. The Turn-Taker blinked blue. “Beep!” it sang. “Dot’s turn,” said Benny. “Let’s see if the light helps.”

Dot tapped the tool. The light blinked blue-blue-blue. “Quack! It works!” Dot clapped her wings. But then the light stayed blue and would not change.

Max frowned. “It’s not my turn. I’m waiting, but it’s stuck.”

Benny’s tail gave a worried thump. He felt a tight twist in his tummy. “Oh no,” he said. “We have a problem.”

Tilly smiled kindly. “Problems help us learn,” she said. “We can cooperate.”

Benny took a deep breath. In and out. In and out. “We will work together,” he said. “We will try a new idea. That is innovation.”

He clicked the top. “Click.” He looked inside. “I see one wire. I think we need two.” He drew a simple picture. “Wire to light. Wire to knob.”

Dot said, “I can count. One, two!” She waddled in place. “I will count the blinks.”

Max said, “I can listen. I will listen for the beeps.” His ears perked up. “Beep?”

Tilly said, “I can wait and watch. I will remind us to take turns.”

Benny smiled. “Thank you. This is cooperation.”

He added one more wire. “Snip, twist, tap.” He pressed the knob. The Turn-Taker blinked blue. “Beep!” Dot clapped. “Now green,” said Benny. He waited.

Dot counted, “One blink, two blinks.”

Max listened. “Beep-beep!”

The light turned green. “Max’s turn,” said Tilly, very calm.

Max tapped. The light turned yellow. “Beep-beep-beep!” He giggled. “It changed again!” Max smiled big. He felt proud for waiting and for helping.

“Now Tilly’s turn,” said Benny. The light glowed soft and warm. Tilly tapped gently. “Beep!” The light turned purple. Tilly nodded. “It works for me too.”

Benny wrote notes. “Blue for Dot. Green for Max. Purple for Tilly.” He added a soft bell sound. “Ding!” It was gentle and kind on the ears.

Dot said, “The ding is sweet, like tiny rain.”

Max said, “The colors are bright, like candies!”

Tilly said, “The waiting feels calm.”

Benny felt happy heat in his cheeks. His tail thumped a steady rhythm. “We solved the problem with a new idea,” he said. “We tried, we tested, and we fixed it.”

Dot asked, “Can the Turn-Taker help at story time?”

Max asked, “Can it help at snack time?”

Tilly said, “It can help any time.”

Benny nodded. “Yes. Innovation helps when we make new things to solve problems. We share ideas. We try again. We take turns. We cooperate.”

They all took turns one more time. Dot tapped. Max tapped. Tilly tapped. The Turn-Taker blinked and dinged. No one rushed. No one pushed. Everyone smiled.

Benny looked around his cozy home office. The clock went tick-tick softly. The laptop hummed. The sun made golden stripes. He felt proud and peaceful.

“Today we learned,” Benny said, “that new ideas can help us share. Taking turns makes time fair. Cooperation makes work light.”

Dot quacked, “Working together is fun!”

Max squeaked, “Trying new ideas is brave!”

Tilly whispered, “Waiting is kind.”

Benny waved. “Thank you, friends. Let’s use our Turn-Taker tomorrow.”

They all agreed. “Yes!”

Benny closed his laptop with a gentle click. He put away the glue. He washed his paws. He whispered, “Good job, team.”

The Turn-Taker glowed a calm little light, like a night star on his desk.

The lesson was clear: Try new ideas, take turns, and work together. That is how problems turn into happy plans.

The End.
