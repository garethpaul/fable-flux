# Ollie the Brave Little Otter

Ollie the otter loved playing in the sunny backyard. The warm sunshine tickled his whiskers, and the soft grass felt wonderful under his tiny paws. He would splash in his kiddie pool and roll around on the green lawn all day long.

One bright morning, Ollie was having so much fun that he forgot to be careful. He was chasing butterflies and spinning in circles when—CRASH! He accidentally knocked over Mrs. Robin's beautiful flower pot. The pretty purple flowers scattered everywhere, and dirt spilled across the clean patio.

"Oh no!" squeaked Ollie, his brown eyes wide with worry. His tummy felt all twisty inside. "What have I done?"

Mrs. Robin flew down from her nest in the oak tree. She looked at her broken flower pot and the mess on the ground. Ollie's heart beat fast like a little drum. He wanted to run and hide behind the big garden shed.

"I... I didn't mean to," whispered Ollie, his voice shaky and small.

Mrs. Robin's feathers ruffled in the gentle breeze. "Ollie, accidents happen," she said kindly. "But what matters most is what we do next."

Ollie's whiskers twitched nervously. He knew he should help clean up, but he felt scared. What if Mrs. Robin was angry? What if he made an even bigger mess?

Then Ollie remembered what his mama always told him: "Being brave doesn't mean you're not scared. It means you do the right thing even when you feel scared."

Ollie took a deep breath that smelled like sweet flowers and fresh grass. "Mrs. Robin," he said, his voice getting stronger, "I'm very sorry I broke your flower pot. It was an accident, but I should have been more careful."

Mrs. Robin smiled warmly. "Thank you for saying sorry, Ollie. That took courage."

"I want to help fix this mess," said Ollie, standing up tall on his hind legs. "I want to be responsible."

Together, they carefully picked up the scattered flowers. Ollie's small paws worked hard to gather every purple petal. The sunshine felt good on his fur as he helped sweep up the dirt with a tiny broom Mrs. Robin brought.

"These flowers can be planted in the garden," explained Mrs. Robin. "They'll grow even bigger and more beautiful there."

Ollie's eyes sparkled with excitement. "Really? Can I help plant them?"

"Of course! Taking care of our garden helps take care of our whole neighborhood," said Mrs. Robin. "That makes you a good citizen of our backyard community."

They dug small holes in the soft, dark soil. Ollie gently placed each flower in its new home. The cool earth felt good between his toes, and he could smell the rich, earthy scent of the garden.

When they finished, Ollie looked at the beautiful garden with pride. "I feel much better now," he said, his tail wagging happily.

"You should feel proud," said Mrs. Robin. "You showed real courage today. You admitted your mistake, said sorry, and worked hard to make things right."

From that day on, Ollie always remembered to be more careful when playing. And whenever he made a mistake, he found the courage to take responsibility and make things better.

The sunny backyard felt even more wonderful when Ollie knew he was being brave and responsible.

The End.
