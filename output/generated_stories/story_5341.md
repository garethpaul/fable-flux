# Kira's Special Space

Kira the koala loved her cozy closet hideaway. It was tucked behind the hanging clothes in her bedroom, with soft pillows on the floor and her favorite stuffed animals lined up against the wall. The closet smelled like lavender from mama's sachets, and sunlight peeked through the crack under the door, making everything feel warm and safe.

"This is my special thinking place," Kira whispered to her teddy bear, Mr. Buttons. She had decorated the walls with her drawings and kept her most treasured books in a little basket.

One morning, Kira's little brother Benny came bouncing into her room. "Kira! Kira! Can I play in your closet? It looks so fun!"

Kira felt her tummy get a little tight. She loved Benny, but her closet was her special place where she went when she needed to think or feel calm. "This is my quiet space, Benny," she said gently. "I need it to be just mine."

Benny's bottom lip started to wobble. "But I want to play there too! It's not fair!"

Kira took a deep breath, just like mama had taught her. She thought carefully about what to say. "Everyone needs their own special space, Benny. This closet is mine. But maybe we can think of a special space just for you!"

"Really?" Benny's eyes lit up like stars.

Kira nodded and smiled. "Let's make a plan! We can ask mama and papa to help us find the perfect spot for you."

Together, they walked through the house. Kira pointed to different places. "What about under the kitchen table? We could hang a blanket to make it cozy."

Benny crawled under and giggled. "It's like a tent! But it's too close to all the cooking sounds."

"Good thinking," said Kira. "Let's try somewhere else."

They checked the corner of the living room, but it was too busy with people walking by. They looked at the space under the stairs, but it felt too dark and scary.

Finally, they discovered the perfect spot – a sunny corner in Benny's own room, right by the big window. "We could put cushions here and hang up your artwork," Kira suggested.

"And my toy cars!" Benny added excitedly.

Mama and papa helped them set up Benny's new hideaway with soft blankets, his favorite stuffed dinosaur, and a small basket for his treasures. Benny clapped his hands with joy. "This is the best space ever! It's all mine!"

"Now we both have our own special places," Kira said, feeling proud and happy. "When you need quiet time, you can come here. And when I need quiet time, I'll go to my closet."

"And we can still play together in the playroom!" Benny added.

That afternoon, when Kira felt tired from playing, she went to her closet hideaway. She could hear Benny in his own special space, humming quietly to his dinosaur. Both children felt peaceful and happy.

"It feels good when everyone respects each other's special spaces," Kira told Mr. Buttons, snuggling into her soft pillows. "Now we all have places where we can feel safe and calm."

From that day on, Kira and Benny always asked before entering each other's special spaces. They learned that saying "no" was okay, and that everyone deserves to have their own peaceful place.

The End.
