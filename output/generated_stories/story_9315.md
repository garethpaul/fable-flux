# Beep-Boop’s Bubble-Time Turn

Beep-Boop was a helpful robot dog with a shiny silver nose and soft rubber paws. He lived with Mia and Max. One sunny morning, it was time to brush teeth and wash hands in the bathroom.

The bathroom smelled like mint and warm soap. The light made tiny sparkles on the faucet. The mirror was tall and shiny. Beep-Boop’s ears blinked blue when he wagged his little metal tail. “Beep boop! Tooth time!” he said in his gentle robot voice.

Mia ran in with her pink toothbrush. Max ran in with his green one. Beep-Boop rolled in behind them, his wheels going whirr.

“I go first!” Mia said, standing on the step stool.

“I go first!” Max said, reaching for the faucet.

Both children grabbed the handle. The faucet felt cold and smooth. The water made a loud whoosh! It splashed on Mia’s hand. Drip, drip, drop.

Beep-Boop paused. His ears blinked yellow. “Error detected,” he said softly. “Two friends. One sink.”

Mia frowned. “I want bubbles now.”

Max pouted. “I want bubbles now too.”

Beep-Boop took a calm little breath. His chest light glowed green. “We can be fair,” he said. “We can take turns. We can be patient.”

Mia crossed her arms. “But waiting is hard.”

Max stomped his foot. “Waiting takes too long.”

Beep-Boop nodded. “Waiting can feel big. But we can make it small with a plan.”

He pressed his nose. Beep! A tiny timer popped up on his back, shaped like a smiling soap bubble. “Turn Timer: two minutes,” Beep-Boop said. “Two minutes for Mia, then two minutes for Max.”

Mia looked at the bubble timer. “Is two minutes short?”

Beep-Boop wagged. “Two minutes is just enough for brushing. It goes by fast when we practice patience.”

Max looked at his green toothbrush. “Practice patience?”

“Practice means we try, even if it’s tricky,” Beep-Boop said. “Patience means we wait kindly.”

Mia nodded. “Okay. I can try.”

Max nodded too. “Me too.”

“Beep!” said the timer. “Mia’s turn.” Beep-Boop guided her hands. The toothpaste was minty and cool. Squish! A tiny line of paste landed on her brush. The mint smell tickled her nose. She giggled.

“Brush, brush, brush,” Beep-Boop sang softly. “Top and bottom, swirl and swish.”

Mia brushed in little circles. “It feels tingly!” she said. Foam made funny white bubbles around her smile. In the mirror, her eyes looked bright.

Max watched and held his toothbrush. He squeezed it. He felt a little wiggly inside. His tummy fluttered. He wanted his turn. Beep-Boop noticed.

“You’re doing great, Max,” Beep-Boop said. “You are practicing patience. Slow and steady.”

Max took a steady breath. He bounced on his toes, then held still. “I’m waiting kindly,” he whispered.

“Beep!” went the timer. “Turn complete.”

Max smiled wide. “My turn!”

Mia hopped off the stool. “I waited for my turn too,” she said. “Now I can wait for you.” She stood next to Beep-Boop and hugged his smooth shoulder.

Max climbed up. Squish! A pea-sized dot of paste on his brush. The minty smell came again. “Brush, brush, brush,” Beep-Boop sang. Max brushed up and down, then side to side. He spit into the sink. Splish! “It tastes cold,” he said.

“You’re doing it,” Mia cheered. “You’re so good at brushing.”

Max beamed. “I am practicing.”

When the timer beeped again, Max rinsed his brush. He handed the faucet to Mia with an open palm. “Your turn to wash hands.”

“Thank you,” Mia said. She turned the warm water. It felt cozy on her fingers. The soap made soft bubbles. Plop, plop, plop. The sink smelled clean. She rubbed front and back, between fingers, and around her thumbs.

Max waited, hugging Beep-Boop. “Is waiting still hard?” he asked.

Beep-Boop thought with a soft whirr. “Waiting can be hard,” he said. “But fair turns make it easier. Your patience is strong.”

Mia finished and stepped aside. “Your turn to wash,” she said to Max.

Max washed his hands. The water made a happy hiss. The bubbles popped like tiny kisses. He dried his hands on the fluffy towel. “All clean!” he said.

Beep-Boop’s ears blinked rainbow. “Mission complete. Fair turns. Patient hearts. Practiced skills.”

Mia smiled at the mirror. “I can be patient,” she said. “I felt wiggly inside, but I waited kindly.”

Max nodded. “I can be patient too. My turn comes, even when I wait.”

Beep-Boop tapped his nose. “When we wait with kind bodies and kind words, we feel proud. Patience helps everyone.”

Mia took a deep breath. She put her hand on her heart. “I feel proud.”

Max did the same. “Me too.” He turned to Mia. “Tomorrow, you can go first.”

Mia grinned. “And the next day, you can go first.”

Beep-Boop wagged. “That is a fair plan. Taking turns is sharing time. Practice makes patience stronger, like a muscle.”

Mia and Max giggled. “Patience muscles!”

They rolled their shoulders and made silly strong faces. Beep-Boop mimicked them with a playful beep and a wiggle. The bathroom glowed warm and calm.

“Thank you, Beep-Boop,” Mia said.

“Thank you,” Max said.

“Beep boop,” said the helpful robot dog. “You did it. You were patient, you practiced, and you were fair.”

Mia and Max high-fived. “We can do it again tomorrow,” Mia said.

“And the next day,” Max added.

Beep-Boop nodded. “Always. One turn at a time.”

They turned off the light. Click. The bathroom was quiet and clean, and their hearts felt big and steady, full of patience and pride.

The End.
