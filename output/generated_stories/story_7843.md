# Kira the Koala's Special Visitors

Kira the koala loved the cozy guest room. The soft blue blankets smelled like lavender. Sunlight danced through the white curtains. This was where special visitors stayed.

Today, Kira's family was expecting guests. Three animal friends were coming to visit for the whole weekend!

"I want to make the room perfect," Kira said softly. She fluffed the pillows with her fuzzy paws. The pillows felt so fluffy and warm.

Soon, the doorbell rang. *Ding dong!*

"Hello!" called Benny the bear. He was big and brown with a loud, happy voice.

"Hi there!" squeaked Penny the penguin. She waddled in with her black and white feathers shining.

"Greetings!" hissed Sam the snake in a quiet, gentle voice. He slithered smoothly across the floor.

Kira felt excited but also a little worried. Everyone was so different! How could she make them all happy in the same room?

"The guest room is this way," Kira said, leading them down the hall.

Benny bounced on the bed. *Bounce, bounce!* "This is great! I love soft beds!"

But Penny looked uncomfortable. "Oh my," she said. "This room feels too warm. Penguins like it cold."

Sam looked around with his bright eyes. "The bed is too high for me," he said sadly. "I need to stay close to the ground."

Kira's ears drooped. How could she help everyone feel welcome?

She sat quietly and thought hard. *Think, think, think.* What did each friend really need?

"I have an idea!" Kira said suddenly. "Let's work together!"

First, Kira opened the window. Cool, fresh air flowed in. "Is this better, Penny?"

Penny flapped her flippers happily. "Much better! Thank you for thinking of me!"

Next, Kira found extra blankets from the closet. She made a cozy nest on the floor for Sam. "How does this feel?" she asked.

Sam curled up in the soft blankets. "Perfect! You're so thoughtful, Kira!"

Then Kira looked at Benny. "You can keep the bouncy bed," she said with a smile.

Benny grinned. "This is the best! We each have what we need!"

That night, everyone was comfortable. Penny stayed cool by the window. Sam was cozy in his floor nest. Benny bounced gently on the bed. And Kira curled up in her favorite chair.

"Thank you, Kira," said Penny. "You listened to what we each needed."

"You didn't try to make us all the same," added Sam. "You respected our differences."

"And you found a way for everyone to be happy!" said Benny.

Kira felt warm and proud inside. "I learned something important today," she said. "Being different makes us special. When we respect each other's needs, everyone can be happy together."

The four friends talked and laughed until they fell asleep. The guest room was filled with the gentle sounds of different kinds of breathing - bear snores, penguin chirps, snake hisses, and koala sighs.

In the morning, they all agreed it was the best sleepover ever.

"We're all different, but we're all friends," Kira said as they ate breakfast together.

From that day on, Kira always remembered to listen to what others needed and to respect their differences. She learned that the best way to care for friends is to understand what makes each one special.

The End.
