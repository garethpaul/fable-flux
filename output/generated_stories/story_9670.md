# Ellie's Big Car Adventure

Ellie was a small gray elephant calf with the softest ears and the kindest eyes. Today was a very special day. She was going on her first long car ride with Mama and Papa Elephant to visit Grandma Elephant.

The family car was big and blue, with cozy seats and windows that showed the whole wide world outside. Ellie climbed into her special car seat, her trunk wiggling with excitement.

"Are you ready for our adventure, little one?" asked Mama Elephant, buckling Ellie's seatbelt with her gentle trunk.

"Yes!" trumpeted Ellie happily. She pressed her small trunk against the cool window glass.

As they drove down the bumpy road, Ellie watched trees dance in the wind. She saw fluffy white clouds that looked like cotton balls. Everything was wonderful and new.

But then the car started going up a very tall, winding mountain. The road twisted and turned like a wiggly snake. Ellie's tummy began to feel funny and wobbly.

"Mama," Ellie whispered, her voice small and worried. "My tummy feels strange. The road is so twisty."

Her little trunk trembled, and her ears felt hot. The car seemed to rock back and forth like a boat on water.

Papa Elephant looked in the mirror with caring eyes. "It's okay, sweet Ellie. Sometimes our bodies feel different when we travel. That's perfectly normal."

"But I don't like feeling this way," Ellie said, her voice getting smaller. "Maybe I'm not brave enough for big car trips."

Mama Elephant reached back and gently touched Ellie's trunk with her own. "Oh, my dear little elephant, you are much braver than you know. Even the biggest, strongest elephants sometimes feel uncomfortable in cars."

"Really?" asked Ellie, her eyes wide with surprise.

"Really," said Papa Elephant with a warm chuckle. "When I was a little calf like you, I felt the same way. But I learned some special tricks."

Ellie perked up her ears. "What kind of tricks?"

"First, take three slow, deep breaths through your trunk," Mama said gently. "In... and out... in... and out... in... and out."

Ellie tried it. The cool air felt good in her trunk. Her tummy felt a little better already.

"Now," said Papa, "let's play the Looking Game. Can you find something red outside the window?"

Ellie looked carefully. "I see a red barn!" she said, pointing with her trunk.

"Wonderful! Now find something that flies," said Mama.

"A bird! A pretty blue bird!" Ellie giggled, feeling much better.

They played the Looking Game for many miles. Ellie found yellow flowers, white horses, and even a rainbow after a quick rain shower. Her tummy stopped feeling wobbly. Her trunk stopped trembling.

"I did it!" Ellie said proudly as they pulled into Grandma's driveway. "I went on a big car adventure, and I felt better all by myself!"

Grandma Elephant was waiting with open arms and the biggest smile. "My brave little granddaughter!" she trumpeted joyfully.

As Ellie hugged Grandma tight, she felt proud and strong. She had learned that feeling uncomfortable was okay, and that she could help herself feel better. She was braver than she thought, and that made her trunk tingle with happiness.

"I can't wait for our next car adventure," Ellie said with a confident smile.

The End.
