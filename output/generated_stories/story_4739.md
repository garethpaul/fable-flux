# Ruby Rabbit’s Treehouse Celebration

Ruby was a determined rabbit with bright eyes and quick feet. She lived near a big tree with a cozy treehouse. The treehouse smelled like pine and sunshine. It had a little ladder, soft blankets, and a round window that showed the blue sky.

One morning, Ruby wiggled her nose and said, “Today, I will host a Treehouse Celebration! Friends from many places will come. I must be ready.”

Ruby made a list. “Snacks, music, decorations, and a welcome sign,” she said. She put on her red scarf for bravery and tied it tight. She liked being prepared.

Ruby hopped to the meadow for snacks. She picked sweet strawberries and crunchy carrots. The strawberries felt cool and juicy in her paws. “These will taste like summer,” she said. She wrapped them in big leaves.

Next, Ruby looked for music. She found a tiny drum, a wooden whistle, and a string for a simple guitar. Tap-tap, woo-woo, twang! “We will make happy sounds together,” Ruby said, smiling.

Then Ruby made decorations. She cut paper stars and moons. She colored them in many colors: red, blue, yellow, green, and purple. “So many colors,” she said. “Just like our different stories.”

Finally, Ruby painted a welcome sign. It said, “Hello, Friends!” She added small words she learned from her neighbors: “Hola!” “Bonjour!” “Jambo!” “Ni hao!” She tried each one slowly, and giggled at the new shapes in her mouth. “I will try my best to say hello in many ways,” she whispered.

Soon, footsteps and gentle voices came near the tree. Rustle, rustle. Tap, tap. Laughter floated up like bubbles.

First came Tiko Tortoise with a bright patterned scarf. “Jambo, Ruby,” said Tiko, smiling slow and warm.

“Jambo, Tiko!” Ruby said. “Welcome to my treehouse.”

Then Mei Mouse arrived with a tiny lantern that glowed soft gold. “Ni hao, Ruby,” said Mei. The lantern smelled like tea leaves.

“Ni hao, Mei,” said Ruby, carefully. “Your lantern is beautiful.”

Next came Lila Lamb with flower bracelets. “Hola, Ruby,” sang Lila. The flowers smelled sweet, like honey and rain.

“Hola, Lila!” said Ruby, her ears tall and happy.

Finally, Pierre Pup padded over with a little blueberry tart. “Bonjour, Ruby,” he said with a wagging tail.

“Bonjour, Pierre,” said Ruby. “Thank you for the tart. It smells yummy.”

Everyone climbed into the treehouse. The floor felt warm from the morning sun. Ruby passed around cups of cool water and plates of fruit and carrots. The friends sat in a circle on soft blankets.

“I want our celebration to show how we are different and the same,” Ruby said. “We have different words and foods and music. But we can share, listen, and play together.”

Tiko nodded. “In my family, we sing before we eat.” He hummed a deep, gentle tune. The sound was like a hug.

Mei lifted her lantern. “In my house, we light this to show welcome.” The light made golden shapes on the wall.

Lila clapped a rhythm. “In my family, we dance to say hello!” Tap, tap, tap! Her hooves made happy beats.

Pierre smiled. “In my home, we share a tart to show love.” He cut the blueberry tart into tiny slices and passed them around. It tasted sweet and a little tart, like a giggle.

Ruby listened closely. Her heart felt big and warm. She took a brave breath. “Can I try your ways, too?” she asked.

“Yes!” everyone cheered.

Ruby hummed with Tiko. Mmm-mmm-mmm. She held the lantern with Mei and watched the soft glow. She danced with Lila—hop, hop, twirl! She tasted the tart with Pierre. “Delicious,” she said, licking a bit of blueberry from her whiskers.

Then Ruby shared her way. She played the drum, the whistle, and the little string guitar. Tap-tap, woo-woo, twang! “In my family,” Ruby said, “we make music together to say, ‘We are happy you are here.’”

After music, the friends made a Friendship Flag. Each friend colored a square. Tiko drew waves and turtles. Mei drew lanterns and stars. Lila drew flowers and hearts. Pierre drew a sun and a tart. Ruby drew a treehouse with friends inside. They stitched the squares with red string. The flag fluttered near the window, soft and proud.

Suddenly, a breeze blew. Whoosh! The welcome sign slipped and wobbled. It was about to fall. Ruby stood tall and brave. “I will fix it,” she said. She grabbed a ribbon and tied the sign tight. “We prepared,” she said. “Now our welcome will stay strong.”

They shared stories as the sky turned peach and pink. The treehouse smelled like fruit, flowers, and warm wood. Their voices made a gentle music.

“I learned new words,” said Ruby. “Hola, Bonjour, Jambo, Ni hao. Each word is a gift.”

“I learned your music,” said Tiko.

“I learned your dance,” said Lila.

“I learned your light,” said Mei.

“I learned your sharing,” said Pierre.

Ruby’s eyes shone. “We are all different, and that makes us special. When we listen, we learn. When we respect and show care, we become good friends.”

They all nodded. They hugged, soft and kind. The Friendship Flag waved. The welcome sign glowed in the sunset.

Ruby looked around her treehouse. “Our differences make our day bright,” she said. “We are better together.”

The End.
