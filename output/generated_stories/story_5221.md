# Miko Mouse and the Quiet Questions

In a cozy study corner, a kind mouse named Miko lived behind a little blue bookshelf. The study corner smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Sunlight made warm, golden squares on the rug. A gentle clock went tick-tock, tick-tock.

Miko loved the study corner. He loved the soft chair, the pencil cup, and the big window. He loved how quiet it felt in the morning, like the room was taking a calm breath.

One day, Miko found a shiny note on the desk. The note said, “Snack Time Plan.” There was a tiny drawing of apples, carrots, and cheese. Miko’s whiskers twitched with excitement. “Healthy snacks!” he squeaked. “Crunch, munch, yum!”

Just then, Miko heard a rustle. His friend, Tilly the turtle, peeked from a little basket. “Good morning, Miko,” Tilly said slowly. “What’s that?”

“It’s a plan for snacks,” said Miko. “I want to help set up. But I don’t know who wrote the note.”

Tilly blinked. “We can ask. Calm voices help. Clear words help too.”

Miko nodded. “You’re right.” He took a deep breath in and out. “Calm and clear.”

They padded to the pencil cup. A small brown ant named Antsy waved from the rim. “Busy day! Busy day!” Antsy chirped. “I saw a fox with a list.”

“A fox?” Miko asked. “Where did the fox go?”

Antsy pointed to the stack of picture books. “Over there! But hurry!”

Miko wanted to run, but he remembered Tilly’s words. Calm and clear. He walked with slow, steady steps. Tilly followed with tiny toe taps—tap, tap, tap.

By the books, they found a friendly fox named Fern. Fern wore round glasses and a bright green scarf. She was arranging books about colors and food. “Hello,” Fern said with a smile. “I’m glad you’re here.”

Miko dipped his head. “Hi, Fern. We found a note. Is it yours?”

Fern nodded. “Yes, it is. I am planning a snack share for everyone. Apples, carrots, and cheese. But I need help. The bowls are too big for me to lift alone.”

Miko’s heart felt warm. “We can help! We like helping our study corner.”

Tilly added, “We can make signs. We can take turns. We can use calm voices.”

Fern’s eyes shone. “Wonderful. Thank you. Let’s talk about the plan.”

They sat on the soft rug. It felt like a cloud under their toes. Miko wiggled his nose. “First, we wash our paws,” he said. “Then we set out bowls. Then we call everyone.”

Fern nodded. “Good plan. And one more thing—everyone gets a turn. We listen. We wait. We say what we need.”

Miko raised his paw. “I need a tiny knife to cut the apple. I will be careful.” He looked into Fern’s eyes. “I will be honest. I have small paws. I can cut small pieces.”

“Thank you for telling me,” Fern said. “Honesty helps us be safe.”

Antsy popped up again. “I can carry crumbs! I am very strong for my size.”

Tilly smiled. “I can make signs. I will write ‘Crunchy Carrots’ and ‘Cheesy Squares.’”

They washed their paws in a little bowl with warm water. Splish, splash. It smelled like soap bubbles. Miko gently cut the apple. Chop, chop, chop. The slices were thin like sunbeams. Tilly drew big letters with a blue crayon—scritch, scritch. Fern arranged the carrots. Antsy carried tiny crumbs with great pride.

Soon the bowls were ready. Red apples. Orange carrots. Little squares of cheese like yellow doors.

Miko stood tall on a book. “Friends,” he called, using a calm, clear voice. “Snack time is ready. Please line up. We will all get some. We will use kind words.”

A little squirrel hurried up and blurted, “I want the cheese! All of it!”

Miko felt a bump of worry in his tummy. He took a slow breath. In and out. “We can share,” he said gently. “We have a plan. You will get some cheese. Others will too. Let’s take turns.”

The squirrel’s tail twitched. “Okay,” he said, softer. “I’ll wait.”

A shy bluebird whispered, “Are the apples sweet?”

Miko smiled. “Let’s taste. You can try a small slice first.” He offered a piece. Crunch! The bluebird smiled wide. “It is sweet!”

Everyone chewed happily. Crunch, munch, yum. The room felt warm like sunshine. The clock went tick-tock, tick-tock, and the study corner hummed with happy sounds.

When the bowls were almost empty, Miko looked around. “Fern,” he said, “we need more carrots. Should I get more?”

Fern nodded. “Yes, please. Thank you for asking. Your clear words make it easy.”

Miko fetched a few more carrots from the little fridge. He told the truth when one piece fell. “Oops, I dropped this. I will wash it or choose another.” Fern smiled. “That is honest and safe.”

After snack time, everyone helped clean up. Wipe, wipe. Sweep, sweep. Antsy carried one last crumb with a proud grin.

Fern gathered the friends. “Today was good because we talked,” she said. “We asked, we listened, and we told the truth.”

Miko’s whiskers tingled with joy. “When we use calm, clear words, we can help our whole study corner,” he said. “We can share snacks. We can solve little problems. We can be kind.”

Tilly nodded slowly. “Communication helps hearts feel safe.”

Miko hugged his friends. The study corner smelled like apples and soap. The rug was warm under their feet. The sunshine made the room glow.

They all said together, “Talk kindly. Listen well. Be honest. Share.”

The End.
