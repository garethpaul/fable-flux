# Gertie's Clean Garden Day

High up on a sunny balcony, Gertie the giraffe stretched her long neck toward the morning sky. Her beautiful balcony garden was full of colorful flowers, green herbs, and tiny vegetables growing in pretty pots.

"What a lovely day to tend my garden!" Gertie said, looking at her tall sunflowers swaying in the gentle breeze. She could smell the sweet lavender and fresh mint growing nearby.

Gertie was about to start watering her plants when she looked down at her hooves. "Oh my!" she gasped. "My hooves are quite dirty from yesterday's gardening. I should clean them first."

She remembered what her mama always told her: "Clean hooves make for happy gardening, dear Gertie."

Gertie walked carefully to her special washing station - a shallow basin she had placed on the balcony just for cleaning. She turned on the gentle water and watched it sparkle in the sunlight.

"First, I'll wash my front hooves," Gertie said thoughtfully. She dipped them in the warm, soapy water and scrubbed gently. "Scrub, scrub, scrub! All the dirt is washing away!"

The water felt wonderful and tickled her hooves. Gertie giggled as she cleaned between each toe. "Now my back hooves!" She lifted each one carefully and gave them the same gentle, thorough cleaning.

"There!" Gertie said proudly, looking at her clean, shiny hooves. "Now I'm ready to care for my garden properly."

But then Gertie had a wonderful idea. "I should make a schedule so I remember to wash my hooves every day!" She was very good at thinking ahead.

Gertie found a colorful chart and drew pictures of the sun for morning and the moon for evening. "I'll wash my hooves when I wake up, and again after I finish gardening," she decided. "That way, my garden and I will always be clean and healthy!"

She hung the chart where she could see it every day. The bright colors made her smile.

Now it was time for gardening! With her clean hooves, Gertie gently watered each plant. The water made a soft "pitter-patter" sound as it fell on the leaves. Her tomatoes seemed to dance with joy, and her marigolds turned their faces toward her as if saying "thank you!"

"When my hooves are clean, I don't bring any germs to my plants," Gertie explained to a little butterfly that had come to visit. "Clean hooves help my garden stay healthy and strong!"

The butterfly fluttered around her clean hooves and seemed to agree.

As Gertie worked, she felt calm and happy. She took deep breaths of the fresh garden air and listened to the peaceful sounds around her. Taking time to clean herself first had made her feel ready for anything!

When the sun began to set, Gertie looked at her beautiful, well-cared-for garden. All her plants were healthy and growing strong.

"Time to wash my hooves again!" she said cheerfully, looking at her helpful chart. She cleaned her hooves once more, washing away all the garden soil.

"Tomorrow I'll start with clean hooves again," Gertie said contentedly. "Clean hooves, clean garden, happy me!"

That night, Gertie fell asleep peacefully, knowing that keeping clean had helped her take the very best care of her beloved garden.

The End.
