# Coco the Brave Builder Cat

Coco was a clever cat with bright green eyes and soft orange fur. Coco loved the garage workshop. It smelled like pine wood and warm metal. The floor felt cool on Coco’s paws, and little dust motes danced in the sunlight.

On a tall shelf sat many tools. There were screws that went tick-tick in a tiny jar. There was a tape measure that went zip! A hammer rested quietly. Coco knew the names of the tools because Coco listened carefully.

One morning, Coco heard a small, scratchy cry. “Help! Help!” The sound came from behind a big box.

Coco’s ears perked up. “Who is there?” Coco asked.

A tiny mouse peeked out. The mouse had a blue scarf and a shaky tail. “I’m Mina,” the mouse said softly. “I lost my family. They are visiting from far away. We traveled many places. We are world travelers,” she squeaked proudly, then her whiskers drooped. “But I got scared by a loud bang and ran. Now I can’t find them.”

Coco’s heart felt warm and brave. “I will help you,” Coco said. “We will build a sign to guide them. We can make a bright, strong, friendly sign.”

Mina sniffled. “But the garage is noisy. And I am small.”

Coco nodded. “Bravery is not about size. Bravery is trying, even when you feel small. We will try together.”

Coco and Mina walked to the workbench. The wood smelled sweet, like cookies. Coco gently lifted a thin board with careful paws. “This will be our sign,” Coco said.

Mina touched the smooth wood. “It feels nice,” she said.

Coco found bright paint. Red, yellow, and blue. “We will paint a globe,” Coco purred. “The world is big, but we all share it. Your family will know this sign is for travelers.”

Mina’s eyes shone. “Yes! Let’s do it.”

Coco dipped a brush in blue paint. Swish, swish. The brush made soft sounds. Coco painted a big blue circle. Mina used a tiny brush to add green shapes like little islands. “We are all connected,” Coco said. “We are neighbors in one big world.”

They added arrows that pointed to the door. They used yellow stars for sparkle. Coco wrote “Welcome, friends!” in big, friendly letters. Mina added a tiny heart.

Then—clang! A tool slipped off the shelf. Mina jumped. “I’m scared!” she squeaked, hiding behind the paint.

Coco took a deep breath. The air smelled like paint and metal. “I am a little scared too,” Coco said honestly. “But we can be brave. Let’s use our ears. Is the danger real, or is it just a sound?”

They listened. The garage hummed softly. A bird chirped outside. The tool lay still. “It was just a sound,” Mina said. She peeked out and stood a little taller. “I can do this.”

“That is brave,” said Coco, smiling. “We keep going, even when we feel wobbly.”

They found string and made a stand. Coco measured. Zip! Zip! Mina tied careful knots. The knots looked like tiny bows. Coco tapped the sign’s frame with the hammer. Tap-tap, tap-tap. “Strong and steady,” Coco said.

Soon the sign was ready. It showed the blue globe, the bright stars, and the big welcome words. Coco and Mina moved it near the open garage door. Warm air brushed their faces. They could smell grass and hear a faraway train.

“Now we wait,” Mina whispered.

They waited with brave hearts. Coco told stories about travelers. “Some friends ride buses, some fly in planes, some walk with backpacks. We help each other. That is global friendship.”

Mina nodded. “Even if we live in different places, we can be kind.”

That’s when they heard tiny feet and happy squeaks. “Mina! Mina!” Two mice in small scarves ran in. One scarf was red. One scarf was green.

Mina squealed with joy. “Mama! Papa! I’m here!”

Her parents hugged her tight. “We saw the bright sign,” said Mama Mouse. “We saw the globe and the words, Welcome, friends!”

Papa Mouse smiled at Coco. “Thank you for being brave and kind, clever cat.”

Coco purred. “We built it together. Mina was brave too.”

Mina’s tail swished. “I felt scared, but I tried. Coco taught me to listen, breathe, and keep going.”

Mama Mouse wiped a happy tear. “Around the world, we help each other. We are all neighbors.”

Coco nodded. “Yes. Brave hearts build bridges.”

They all looked at the sign. The colors glowed in the sun. The wood felt warm now. The letters looked strong and friendly.

Mina turned to Coco. “What is our lesson today?”

Coco smiled. “Today we learned that bravery is trying, even when you feel small. We learned that building can help solve problems. And we learned that our big world is full of friends. When we help, we make the world better.”

Mina beamed. “I can be brave. I can build. I can be a good neighbor to everyone, near and far.”

Coco purred a happy purr. “Yes,” Coco said. “We all can.”

The End.
