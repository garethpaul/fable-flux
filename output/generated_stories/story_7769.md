# Polly's Colorful Kindness

In a cozy bedroom filled with soft pillows and warm sunlight, lived a creative parrot named Polly. Her feathers were bright green and blue, and she loved to paint beautiful pictures with her tiny brush.

Every morning, Polly would wake up excited to create art. She had a special easel by the window where she painted flowers, rainbows, and happy faces.

But there was one big problem. Polly's paintings always made a mess! Paint dripped on the carpet, splashed on the walls, and got all over her feathers.

"Oh dear," Polly sighed, looking at the colorful mess around her easel. "I've made everything dirty again."

The bedroom belonged to a little girl named Emma, who was always very neat and tidy. Polly worried that Emma would be upset about the paint everywhere.

"Maybe I should stop painting," Polly whispered sadly. "I don't want to make Emma angry."

That afternoon, Emma came into her bedroom after school. She saw the paint splatters and Polly hiding behind her pillow, looking very sorry.

"Polly, what happened here?" Emma asked gently.

"I'm sorry, Emma," Polly chirped quietly. "I love to paint, but I always make a mess. I tried to be careful, but the paint keeps dripping."

Emma sat down on her bed and looked at Polly's paintings. They were beautiful! There was a sunny yellow flower, a purple butterfly, and a rainbow with every color imaginable.

"Polly, these are wonderful!" Emma said with a big smile. "But I can see why you're worried about the mess."

Emma thought for a moment. She wanted to help her feathered friend.

"I have an idea," Emma said kindly. "What if we work together to solve this problem?"

Emma brought old newspapers and spread them under Polly's easel. She gave Polly a tiny apron to protect her feathers and showed her how to dip her brush gently in the paint.

"Let's practice together," Emma said patiently. "I'll help you learn to paint without making such a big mess."

Day after day, Emma and Polly practiced. Emma was very patient, never getting upset when Polly made mistakes. She showed Polly how to wipe her brush and how to use just a little bit of paint at a time.

"You're getting so much better!" Emma encouraged. "Look how neat your painting area is today!"

Polly felt her confidence growing. Emma's kindness made her feel brave enough to keep trying.

Soon, Polly could paint beautiful pictures without making any mess at all. Her paintings were more colorful and cheerful than ever before.

"Thank you for being so kind and patient with me," Polly chirped happily. "You helped me learn that making mistakes is okay when someone cares about you."

Emma hugged Polly gently. "That's what friends do, Polly. We help each other and are kind, even when things get messy."

From that day on, Polly painted every morning in her clean, organized space. And whenever Emma had a problem, Polly was there to help her too, just like Emma had helped her.

The cozy bedroom was filled with beautiful paintings and, most importantly, filled with kindness and friendship.

The End.
