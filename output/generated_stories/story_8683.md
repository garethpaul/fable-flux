# Shelly's Special Tea Party

Shelly the turtle peeked out from behind the big armchair in Grandma's guest room. Her little green head barely showed above the soft blue cushion. Today was special - her cousin Maya was visiting from far away, and Grandma had asked Shelly to show Maya their family's special tea ceremony.

But Shelly felt scared. Her tummy felt fluttery, like butterflies were dancing inside her shell. "What if Maya doesn't like our tradition?" she whispered to herself. "What if I do it wrong?"

Shelly pulled her head deeper into her shell, making herself as small as possible. Through the doorway, she could hear Maya's cheerful voice talking with Grandma in the kitchen.

"Shelly, dear," called Grandma's warm voice. "Come meet your cousin Maya!"

Shelly's feet felt heavy as stones. She slowly walked to the kitchen, her shell making soft tapping sounds on the wooden floor.

"Hi Shelly!" said Maya brightly. She was a young turtle too, with beautiful golden spots on her green shell. "Grandma told me you know a very special tea ceremony!"

Shelly's head disappeared into her shell again. "I... I don't know if I remember it right," came her muffled voice.

Grandma bent down and gently touched Shelly's shell. "Sweet Shelly, our family tea ceremony has been passed down for many, many years. Your great-great-grandmother taught it to me, and I taught it to your mama, and your mama taught it to you."

"But what if I mess it up?" Shelly peeked one eye out.

Maya scooted closer. "You know what? In my family, we have a special song we sing before meals. I was scared to sing it for new friends too. But when I shared it, everyone loved learning something new!"

Grandma smiled. "Maya, would you like to hear about our tea ceremony? It's all about taking care of each other and feeling peaceful."

Shelly's curiosity grew stronger than her fear. She poked her whole head out. "Really? You want to learn?"

"Yes, please!" Maya clapped her flippers together.

Slowly, Shelly began to explain. "First, we arrange the cups in a circle, like this." She carefully placed three small cups on the low table in the guest room. "Then we pour the warm chamomile tea very slowly, so we can smell the sweet flowers."

Maya watched with wide, interested eyes. "It smells like sunshine and honey!"

Shelly felt her confidence growing. "Now we sit quietly for three deep breaths." All three turtles sat in a circle, breathing in the lovely tea scent. "This helps our bodies feel calm and happy."

"I feel so relaxed!" Maya said softly.

"The last part," Shelly continued with growing excitement, "is we each say one thing we're grateful for before we sip."

"I'm grateful for meeting my wonderful cousin Shelly," Maya said.

"I'm grateful for our beautiful family traditions," added Grandma.

Shelly thought for a moment. "I'm grateful for being brave enough to share something special."

As they sipped their warm tea together, Shelly felt proud and happy. Her tummy wasn't fluttery anymore - instead, it felt warm and peaceful.

"Thank you for teaching me your family tradition, Shelly," Maya said. "Now I can share it with my friends back home!"

Grandma hugged both young turtles. "When we share our traditions with others, they grow stronger and spread more love in the world."

Shelly smiled the biggest smile of her life. She had learned that sharing traditions wasn't scary at all - it was a wonderful way to bring families and friends closer together.

The End.
