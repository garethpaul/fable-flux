# Ruby Rabbit's Peaceful Picture

Ruby Rabbit hopped into the bright art room. Colorful paintbrushes stood in tall jars. Paper and crayons covered every table. The room smelled like fresh markers and clay.

"Today I will make the most beautiful picture ever!" Ruby said, thumping her back foot with excitement.

Ruby grabbed a big piece of white paper. She picked up a red crayon and started drawing fast, fast, fast. She wanted to draw a rainbow, flowers, and butterflies all at once.

But the crayon broke. "Oh no!" Ruby cried. Her ears drooped down.

She tried again with a blue crayon. This time she pressed too hard. The paper tore right down the middle.

"This isn't working!" Ruby's whiskers twitched with worry. Her heart beat quickly like a drum. She felt hot and frustrated.

Ruby was about to give up when she noticed something. In the corner sat a wise old owl named Ms. Hoot. The owl was painting slowly and carefully. Her brush moved like a gentle dance.

"Ms. Hoot," Ruby called out, "how do you make such pretty pictures?"

Ms. Hoot looked up with kind, round eyes. "Come here, little rabbit. Let me show you something special."

Ruby hopped over, her fluffy tail bouncing.

"First, take three deep breaths with me," Ms. Hoot said softly. "In through your nose... out through your mouth."

Ruby breathed in. The air smelled like sunshine and creativity. She breathed out slowly. Her shoulders relaxed.

"Again," Ms. Hoot whispered. "Feel your paws on the table. Listen to the quiet sounds around you."

Ruby listened. She heard the soft tick of the clock. She felt the smooth table under her paws. Her heart slowed down to a gentle rhythm.

"Now," said Ms. Hoot, "try drawing just one thing. Pick something that makes you happy."

Ruby thought carefully. "I love carrots," she said with a small smile.

"Perfect. Draw one carrot. Take your time. There's no hurry."

Ruby picked up an orange crayon. This time, she held it gently. She moved slowly, feeling each line as she drew. The crayon felt smooth and warm in her paw.

She drew one simple carrot. It had a round top and a pointy bottom. She added green leaves that looked like little flags.

"Beautiful!" said Ms. Hoot. "How do you feel now?"

Ruby looked at her picture. It wasn't fancy, but it was hers. "I feel... calm," she said, surprised. "And happy!"

"When we slow down and breathe, our best work comes out," Ms. Hoot explained. "Your mind is like a still pond. When it's calm, you can see clearly."

Ruby nodded. She understood now. She drew another carrot next to the first one. Then she added a smiling sun. Each line was careful and peaceful.

Other animals in the art room noticed Ruby's calm energy. A little mouse came to watch. A squirrel asked if she could sit nearby.

"Ruby, your picture is wonderful," said the mouse. "You look so peaceful when you draw."

Ruby's chest filled with warm pride. "I learned that taking deep breaths helps me do my best," she shared. "Want to try breathing with me?"

Soon, Ruby was teaching her new friends how to breathe slowly and draw mindfully. The art room became a peaceful, happy place.

As Ruby hopped home that day, she carried her carrot picture proudly. She had learned something more valuable than perfect art. She had learned how to find calmness inside herself.

"Tomorrow I'll remember to breathe first," Ruby whispered to herself, smiling under the setting sun.

The End.
