# Momo's Big Feelings Adventure

Momo the monkey loved his cozy study corner. It had soft blue cushions, colorful picture books, and a special basket filled with crayons. Every morning after breakfast, Momo would swing over to his study corner to practice writing his letters.

But today felt different. Momo's pencil wouldn't make the shapes he wanted. His letter "A" looked like a wobbly mountain, and his "B" had bumps in all the wrong places.

"Grrrr!" Momo growled, his fluffy brown fur standing up. His face felt hot and red. "This is too hard!" He threw his pencil across the room with a loud CLATTER.

Momo's chest felt tight, like a balloon ready to pop. His hands made tight fists, and he wanted to knock over his crayon basket. These big, angry feelings made his whole body feel jumpy and wild.

"Whoa there, little monkey," said a gentle voice. It was Grandpa Monkey, peeking into the study corner. "I can see you're having some very big feelings right now."

"Everything is WRONG!" Momo shouted, his voice echoing in the quiet corner. "I can't write pretty letters like the big monkeys!"

Grandpa Monkey sat down on the soft rug beside Momo. "When I have big angry feelings," he said quietly, "I like to do something special. Would you like to try it with me?"

Momo nodded, even though his heart was still beating fast like a drum.

"First, let's take three deep breaths together," Grandpa said. "Breathe in through your nose like you're smelling sweet bananas." They both took a long, slow breath. "Now breathe out through your mouth like you're blowing bubbles."

Momo felt his shoulders start to relax. The tight feeling in his chest began to melt away like ice cream in the sun.

"Now, let's do some monkey movements!" Grandpa said with a smile. "Stretch your arms up high like you're reaching for the tallest tree!" Momo stretched and stretched, feeling his muscles wake up.

"Now shake your hands like leaves in the wind!" They wiggled their fingers and shook their arms. "And stomp your feet like you're dancing in the jungle!" 

Stomp, stomp, stomp! Momo giggled as he marched around his study corner. The angry feelings were floating away like clouds.

"How do you feel now?" Grandpa asked.

Momo touched his chest. The tight feeling was gone. His face didn't feel hot anymore. "Better," he said with a small smile. "My body feels calm and happy again."

"That's wonderful!" Grandpa said. "Now, would you like to try your letters again? Remember, practice makes progress, not perfect."

Momo picked up his pencil and sat at his little desk. This time, he took a deep breath before starting. His letter "A" still looked a bit wobbly, but that was okay. He was learning!

"When big feelings come back," Grandpa said, "remember our special routine: breathe like you're smelling bananas, move your body like a dancing monkey, and then try again with a calm heart."

From that day on, whenever Momo felt angry or frustrated in his study corner, he would stop, breathe, move, and then continue with his work. He learned that big feelings are normal, but he had special tools to help himself feel better.

The End.
