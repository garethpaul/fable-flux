# Otto the Otter’s World Song

Otto was a playful otter with bright eyes and soft, whiskery cheeks. He loved splashing, he loved giggling, and today he loved something new—his cozy study corner. The study corner had a tiny desk, a soft pillow, a map with colorful countries, a little drum, and a big jar of crayons. Sunlight made warm squares on the rug. The air smelled like crayons and paper. It felt safe. It felt happy.

“Good morning, study corner!” Otto chirped. He patted the map. “Hello, world!”

On the wall, there was a simple list with three pictures: a hand, an ear, and a heart. Otto’s teacher, Ms. Tide, had shown him the list at school. Follow directions with hands, ears, and heart.

Ms. Tide had said, “Hands ready to help. Ears ready to listen. Heart ready to care.” Otto remembered. He liked the way the words felt, like a calm song.

Otto wanted to make music for the world. He wanted to sing a song that said, “We all belong.” He put on his little headphones and felt the soft pads on his ears. He tapped his drum. Tap-tap. He shook his maraca. Shaka-shaka. He hummed, “La-la-la.”

Otto looked at the map. “This is a big world,” he whispered. “People live in many places. People speak many words. People eat many foods. People dance many dances.” Otto’s whiskers wiggled. “I want to make a world song!”

He opened his music book. There were simple pictures and arrows. “Step one,” Otto read slowly, “listen.” He put his ear close to his drum. He tapped softly. Tap. He listened. Then he tapped loudly. TAP! He listened again.

“Step two,” he read, “follow the beat.” Otto nodded. “I can do that.” He clapped softly. Clap. Clap. Clap. He followed the steady beat. He smiled. “My hands can follow directions.”

“Step three,” he read, “use your heart to care.” Otto pressed his paws to his chest. “My heart can care about friends, even far-away friends,” he said. He looked at the map and waved. “Hello, far-away friends!”

The door creaked. “Hi, Otto!” called his friend Nia from next door. Nia had curly hair and a gentle voice. She carried a tiny bell. It went ding-ding-ding.

“What are you doing?” Nia asked.

“I am making a world song,” said Otto. “Do you want to help?”

“Yes!” Nia smiled. “My grandma taught me a rhythm from her island.” She tapped the bell with a stick. Ding, ding, diiiing. Ding, ding, diiiing. The sound was bright, like sunshine on water.

Otto listened with his ears. “It sounds happy,” he said. “Let’s listen first. Then we can play.”

Nia nodded. “We listen, we follow, we care.”

Otto tapped his drum softly with Nia’s bell. Tap, tap, taaap. Ding, ding, diiiing. Their sounds met and held hands. The study corner filled with tiny echoes. The rug felt warm under their toes. The air jingled with the bell and hummed with the drum.

Otto looked at the map again. “People in many places have music,” he said. “We can listen to each other. We can share.”

Nia said, “We can be kind and fair. We can take turns.”

Otto pointed to the list. “Hands help. Ears listen. Heart care,” he said. “These directions help us play together.”

They tried a new part. Otto counted, “One, two, three.” Nia smiled and waited for her turn. Then she played her bell. Otto listened. He followed. He did not rush. He was patient. He felt proud of his calm heart.

Soon, their friend Bao joined. Bao brought a soft shaker that went sh-sh-sh. “My aunt sings a lullaby from far away,” Bao said. “It is slow and gentle.”

Otto’s ears perked up. “Let’s make room for slow,” he said. “We can listen closely.”

The three friends played. Ding, tap, sh-sh-sh. Slow and gentle. The study corner sounded like morning rain and tiny stars. Otto closed his eyes and felt the beat in his paws. He felt safe. He felt connected.

“Now,” said Otto, “let’s add words.” He looked at the map and sang softly:

“We all belong,
Near and far,
You are you,
Just as you are.
Hands help, ears hear,
Hearts care, we cheer!
Around the world,
We all belong here.”

Nia and Bao sang with him. Their voices were small but strong. Otto felt joy bubble up, like little water pops.

They finished the song and giggled. Bao said, “I liked how we listened first.”

Nia said, “I liked how we followed directions.”

Otto said, “I liked how we cared for each other and for friends far away.”

They looked again at the map. Otto whispered, “We are global friends. We can learn from everyone.”

Nia nodded. “We can share space and sounds.”

Bao added, “We can help when it’s our turn, and wait when it’s not.”

Otto cleaned up the drum, the bell, and the shaker. He followed the clean-up picture chart: put away, zip up, wipe hands. He did it by himself. He felt strong and calm.

The study corner was quiet again. Otto patted the map. “Thank you, world,” he said. “Today I learned to listen, to follow, and to care. That’s how I can be a good friend to people everywhere.”

Nia smiled. “We made music with our hands, our ears, and our hearts.”

Bao hugged his shaker. “We belong together.”

Otto beamed. “Yes! We belong. We can be kind. We can be curious. We can sing for the whole world.”

They held hands for a moment. The sun made the rug glow. The room smelled like crayons and clean paper. Their hearts felt warm, like a hug.

The End.
