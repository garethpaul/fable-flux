# Rooftop Garden, Hoppy Heart

Bibi was a determined rabbit with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. She lived in a cozy house with a flat rooftop. One sunny morning, Bibi climbed the little ladder to the roof. The sky was blue like a shiny marble. The city sounds hummed below—honk, rumble, chirp. A gentle breeze brushed Bibi’s whiskers.

“I want to grow a rooftop garden,” Bibi said. “I want flowers, herbs, and a tiny carrot patch. I can do it!” Her heart thumped with brave, hoppy beats.

Bibi carried a watering can. Slosh, slosh. She had a small bag of seeds. Jingle, shake. She found some empty pots, but there was a problem. The soil bag was very heavy. When Bibi tried to lift it, she wiggled and tugged. The bag would not move.

“I can do this by myself,” Bibi whispered. She pulled again. The bag slid and bumped her paw. “Ouch!” Bibi sighed. “Maybe I need a plan.”

Just then, Mr. Khan, the friendly neighbor, stepped onto his own rooftop next door. He wore a green cap and waved. “Salaam, little gardener!” he called with a warm smile. “What are you growing today?”

“I want to make a garden,” Bibi said. “But the soil is too heavy. I want to be strong and do it alone.”

Mr. Khan nodded. “Being strong is good,” he said. “Working together is strong too.” He pointed to a shiny, blue watering can on his roof. “In my country, Pakistan, we grow mint for tea. Would you like some mint? We can share.”

Bibi’s ears perked up. “Mint tea smells nice. But… I wanted to do it by myself.”

A soft thump sounded behind her. Mei, a girl from downstairs, popped up with a small basket. “Ni hao, Bibi!” she said. “I heard you on the ladder. I brought sweet basil seeds from my grandmother’s kitchen. They are from our family in Taiwan. May I help?”

Bibi looked at the heavy soil bag. She looked at her watering can. Her nose twitched. The breeze carried the warm scent of sun on brick and the faint peppery smell from Mei’s seeds. Bibi took a deep breath.

“I am determined,” Bibi said. “I am also smart. Smart rabbits can ask for help.”

Mr. Khan clapped. “That is wisdom.”

“Okay, friends,” Bibi said. “Let’s cooperate!”

They made a plan. Mei held the bag open. Mr. Khan lifted the heavy soil with his strong arms. Bibi scooped with a little cup. Scoop, pat. Scoop, pat. The soil felt cool and crumbly between Bibi’s paws. The pots filled up, one by one.

“Listen,” Mei said. “The pigeons are cooing.” Coo-oo. Coo-oo.

“And I smell cumin from my kitchen,” Mr. Khan said, sniffing the air. “Lunch is cooking. Mmm.”

Bibi giggled. “I smell adventure!”

They planted seeds together. Bibi planted tiny carrot seeds. Tap, tap. Mei sprinkled basil. Mr. Khan tucked mint cuttings into the soil and hummed a soft song.

“What song is that?” Bibi asked.

“It is a folk song from my home,” said Mr. Khan. “It is about rain helping the garden grow.”

Bibi swayed her ears to the tune. Mei hummed too. They shared stories as they worked. Mei told how her grandmother uses basil in noodle soup. Mr. Khan told how mint is served with hot tea to welcome guests. Bibi shared that rabbits love carrots but also love friends.

When they were finished planting, clouds gathered. A cool shadow fell over the rooftop. Plip. Plip. Rain began to fall. The drops were light and tickly on Bibi’s fur. The wet air smelled fresh, like clean leaves.

“Oh no,” Bibi said. “Will the rain wash our seeds away?”

“We can protect them,” Mei said. She held a small umbrella. Mr. Khan fetched two clear bowls. Together, they covered the smallest pots. Bibi used her body to shield the carrot pot, ears open like little tents.

The rain made a soft drumming sound. Dum, da-dum. Bibi felt calm. The team stood close, sharing the umbrella, sharing smiles. When the rain slowed, the rooftop sparkled. Tiny rainbows danced in the puddles.

“Thank you,” Bibi said. “We did it!”

“You did it,” said Mr. Khan. “You led with your big heart.”

“You asked for help,” Mei said. “That is brave.”

Every morning that week, Bibi checked the pots. She watered gently. She whispered to the soil, “Grow, grow.” One day, green tips peeked out. “Look!” Bibi cried. “Hello, little leaves!”

Mr. Khan brought small cups of warm mint tea. “To our garden,” he said.

Mei brought a bowl of noodles with bright green basil. “A taste of my family,” she said.

Bibi nibbled a tiny carrot leaf—just a little taste—and then smiled. “We learned so much,” she said. “We learned about mint tea and basil soup. We learned songs and words. We learned that together is stronger.”

That evening, the sun painted the sky orange and pink. The city hummed softly. The garden leaves glowed. Bibi’s heart felt full and steady.

“I am still determined,” Bibi said, ears high. “I can do many things on my own. But I can do even more with friends. Cooperation helps everyone grow.”

She looked at her friends, then at the plants, and gave a happy little hop.

The End.
