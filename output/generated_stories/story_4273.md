# Pico the Fair-Feathered Fixer

In a bright, busy garage workshop, a creative parrot named Pico loved to help. Pico had shiny green feathers, a red beak, and a tiny tool belt. The air smelled like warm wood and orange soap. The floor felt cool under Pico’s little feet. Sunlight made dust sparkle like tiny stars.

Every morning, Pico had a routine. Pico sang, “Good morning, tools!” then checked the list on a big board. The list said: Sweep, Sort, Share. Pico liked the word “share.” It made Pico’s chest feel warm and happy.

Friends from all over the neighborhood came to the workshop. Mr. Kareem brought a drum from Egypt. Mei brought a kite from China. Abuela Rosa brought maracas from Mexico. Samir brought a toy truck. Kumi brought a little robot. They all came with smiles, and they all came with care.

One sunny day, a new friend came. Her name was Laila. She wore a bright scarf, and it smelled like roses. “Hello! My name is Laila,” she said. “I have a wind-up camel toy from my family.” The camel had gold paint and tiny bells that jingled. It went clip-clop, clip-clop when it walked. Everyone clapped. Pico puffed up with joy. “Welcome, Laila! We fix, we share, we care,” Pico said.

Pico put Laila’s camel on the shelf with a little tag: Laila’s Camel. The tag was blue. Pico loved bright blue tags.

After snack time—crunchy apples and sweet dates—something felt wrong. The shelf looked different. The blue tag sat alone. The camel was gone.

“Oh!” Laila’s eyes got wet. “Where is my camel?”

Pico felt a twist in his tummy. “We will find it,” Pico said softly. “We will be fair. We will be kind. We will listen.”

Pico clapped his wings. “Team, we have a problem. We will use our routine.” Pico pointed to the list: Sweep, Sort, Share. “And today we add: Speak and Be Fair.”

Mr. Kareem tapped his drum. “Justice means we treat everyone with care,” he said. Abuela Rosa nodded. “We do not blame. We find the truth.” Mei held Laila’s hand. “We help together.”

Pico asked in a calm voice, “Did anyone see the camel?” Samir shook his head. Kumi looked at the floor. “I heard jingling,” Kumi said. “Near the tool box.”

They all listened. The workshop hummed with soft sounds: a gentle fan, tiny clicks, and then—jingle-jingle.

Pico tiptoed. “Let’s look,” Pico said. They peered behind the big red toolbox. There was a box of buttons, a roll of tape, and a shiny bell. But no camel.

“We will not guess,” Pico said. “We will look with our eyes, and we will ask with kind words.”

Pico asked the tools, “Seen a camel?” Pico made a funny voice. Everyone giggled. Laila smiled a tiny smile.

Then Mei pointed. “Look! Wheel prints.” Small round marks led under a workbench. Pico crouched low. The floor smelled dusty and sweet.

Under the bench was a tiny robot. It was Kumi’s robot. The robot held a string in its little clamp. On the string were tiny bells. The bells from the camel!

Kumi’s eyes went wide. “Oh no. My robot loves shiny things. It must have rolled over, took the bells, and kept going.” Kumi’s voice was small. “I am sorry, Laila. I did not mean for this to happen.”

Pico nodded gently. “Thank you for telling the truth,” Pico said. “Justice is fair. We listen. We make it right.”

Kumi picked up the robot and handed the bells to Laila. “Can we fix the camel together?” Kumi asked.

“We still need the camel,” Laila whispered.

Pico fluffed his feathers. “We will sort and sweep.” Everyone joined. They moved slowly, eyes kind, hearts steady. They sang a little song: “Look and listen, side by side. We are fair, with gentle pride.”

Samir checked the low shelves. Abuela Rosa checked the cloth bin. Mr. Kareem tapped a soft rhythm to keep the pace. Pico swept under the big blue cabinet. Scritch, scritch. Something bumped the broom. Clip-clop!

“The camel!” Pico chirped. The camel had rolled under the cabinet when the bells came off. Pico held it up. The gold paint glowed, and the tiny legs went clip-clop when Pico wound it. Laila laughed, clear as a bell.

Together, they fixed the bells to the camel, slow and careful. Mei held the light. Kumi steadied the tiny screws. Pico tightened gently. Click. The camel jingled again: clip-clop, jingle-jingle.

Pico looked at everyone. “We did not blame. We asked. We listened. We told the truth. We made it right. That is justice.”

Laila hugged Kumi. “Thank you for being honest.”

Kumi smiled. “Next time, I will set a robot rule. No taking shiny things.” Everyone giggled.

They added a new step to the list: Speak, Be Fair, Fix, and Make a Plan. Pico drew a little heart next to it.

Before they left, they all took turns winding the camel. Each friend said thank you in a different language. “Shukran,” said Laila. “Gracias,” said Abuela Rosa. “Xie xie,” said Mei. “Thank you,” said Samir. “Arigato,” said Kumi. The words sounded like a happy song in the warm, bright workshop.

Pico perched on the shelf. “Our routine helps us. Justice keeps us kind. We are different, and we all belong,” Pico said.

Everyone nodded. The camel jingled and walked, clip-clop, jingle-jingle, right down the middle of the clean floor.

The End.
