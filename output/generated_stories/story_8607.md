# Dolphin Bubbles and the Nursery Band

In a comfortable nursery full of soft pillows and bright picture books, a playful dolphin named Bubbles visited every morning. Bubbles was small and shiny, with smooth gray skin and a cheerful smile. Even though dolphins live in the sea, Bubbles loved to splash in a big, warm water tub set beside the reading rug. The tub smelled like clean soap and ocean breeze.

“Good morning!” sang Bubbles, popping her head from the water. “Click-click, squeak! I’m ready to play!”

The nursery children clapped. Mira shook a tiny bell. Theo tapped a gentle drum. A sleepy teddy sat on a shelf, watching with button eyes. Miss June, their kind teacher, smiled and said, “Today we will make a music band and clean up our toys after. Let’s talk about our plan.”

Bubbles twirled and splashed. “I want to play the loud drum!” she squeaked. “Boom-boom!”

Theo hugged his drum. “That’s my drum,” he whispered. His eyebrows dipped down. His hands shook a little.

Bubbles didn’t hear his soft voice. She splashed again, excited. Water sparkled like little stars in the sunlight. “Boom-boom!” she chirped.

Miss June knelt by the tub. “Bubbles, let’s use our listening ears,” she said gently, tapping her ear. “What is Theo saying?”

Bubbles paused. She blinked her big eyes and took a tiny breath. “Click?” she asked, tilting her head. She looked at Theo. Theo looked back.

“I feel sad,” Theo said, a tiny tear starting. “I want my drum. Please ask first.”

Bubbles’ round cheeks warmed. “Oh,” she squeaked softly. “I didn’t ask. I’m sorry, Theo.” She wiggled her fins. “May I please try your drum after you?”

Theo sniffled, then nodded. “Yes. After me.”

Miss June clapped quietly. “That is good communication. We use words to share our feelings and ask for turns.”

Mira rattled her bell. “We can all have turns! I can count,” she said. “One, two, three… then switch!”

Bubbles smiled. “Thank you, Mira!” Her tail made a gentle swish-swish in the water. The room felt warm and happy again.

They started their nursery band. Theo tapped the drum, boom-boom, like a soft thunder cloud. Mira rang the bell, ding-ding, like tiny raindrops. Bubbles hummed and clicked, “Eee-eee, click-click,” like waves rolling on sand. Miss June shook maracas, shh-shh, like wind in trees.

Bubbles wanted to sing louder and faster. Her sounds splashed on top of everyone else’s sounds. The music turned messy. It made Teddy wobble on the shelf.

“Wait,” Mira said, covering her ears. “Too noisy!”

Theo pressed the drum with his palms. “I can’t hear my beat,” he said.

Bubbles stopped mid-click. Her heart thumped fast. She didn’t want to ruin band time. “I’m excited,” she said, taking a deep breath. “I want to sing. How can we play together?”

Miss June nodded. “Great question. Let’s practice talking and taking turns. Communication helps music. We will make a plan.”

She held up her fingers. “Plan: First, Theo’s drum. Next, Mira’s bell. Then, Bubbles sings. We listen to each part. After that, we play together, softer.”

Bubbles repeated, slowly, “First drum. Next bell. Then sing. Together, softer.” She wiggled proudly. “I can do it.”

Theo boom-boomed a steady beat. Bubbles listened carefully. She kept her fins still and counted with her clicks, “One… two… three.” She felt calm, like floating. Mira’s bell chimed next, bright and clear, ding-ding. Bubbles smiled. The sounds felt like sunshine.

Then it was Bubbles’ turn. She peeked at her friends. “Ready?” she asked.

“Ready,” said Theo.

“Ready,” said Mira.

Bubbles sang, “Eee-eee, click-click,” sweet and gentle. Her voice floated around the nursery like bubbles popping. Her song told a story of blue water and friendly fish. Theo and Mira watched and smiled.

“Now together, softly,” Miss June whispered. “Use your listening ears.”

They played all at once, but quietly. Drum, boom-boom. Bell, ding-ding. Dolphin song, eee-eee. The sounds fit like puzzle pieces. The teddy on the shelf looked very pleased.

When the song ended, everyone clapped. Bubbles splashed a tiny splash. “We did it!” she squeaked. “We used words. We listened.”

Miss June nodded. “That’s communication. You asked. You listened. You took turns. You practiced. That made the music beautiful.”

After music time, toys lay all over the rug. Blocks, cars, and crayons waited. “Now it’s clean-up time,” Miss June said. “Let’s make another plan.”

Bubbles lifted a block with her nose. “I can help,” she said. “Where does this go?”

Theo pointed. “Blocks in the red bin,” he said.

Mira added, “Cars in the blue bin. Crayons in the cup.”

Bubbles repeated, “Red bin, blue bin, cup. Got it!” She moved slowly and carefully. She asked when she wasn’t sure. “Is this a car?” she giggled, holding up a toy potato.

Mira laughed. “No, that’s pretend food! It goes in the kitchen basket.”

Bubbles placed it gently. “Thank you for telling me.” Together, they sang the clean-up song, soft and happy. Swish, plunk, click! The room began to shine. The air smelled like crayons and soap.

When they finished, Miss June gave them each a sticker. “Teamwork feels good,” she said.

Bubbles beamed. “Today I learned to talk and listen. I felt my feelings and used my words. We practiced, and we finished our tasks.”

Theo patted the drum. “I felt heard.”

Mira jingled her bell. “I felt helpful.”

Bubbles swished her tail. “I feel proud. Tomorrow, let’s make more music with our words.”

They all nodded. The nursery felt warm and calm. Teddy leaned back, cozy on the shelf. Bubbles gave one last happy click and curled in her tub, water whispering around her.

The End.
