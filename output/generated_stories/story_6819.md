# Tiko’s Ticktock Tune

Tiko was a kind little mouse with soft gray fur and bright, curious eyes. He loved music. He loved the shiny drum, the tiny triangle, and the big, warm piano that went plink, plonk, plunk. Every afternoon, Tiko scurried to the musical practice room to listen and learn.

One sunny day, Tiko squeaked, “I want to help the music sound extra special today!” He twitched his whiskers and looked around. The room smelled like fresh wood and lemon polish. Sunbeams made the dust sparkle like tiny stars. On the wall, a big round clock went tick… tock… tick… tock.

In the room, the instruments waited. The drum had a bouncy top. The violin slept in its case. The flute lay on a soft cloth. And on a chair sat a neat stack of music papers with little black notes dancing across them.

Tiko had an idea. “I will tidy the music!” he said. He picked up the papers. They felt smooth and slightly cool. But Tiko was small, and the papers were tall. The stack wobbled. The clock said tick… tock… and Tiko felt a little rush in his tummy.

“Oh no!” Tiko squeaked as the papers slid. Whoosh! The pages scattered like white leaves. Some hid under the piano. Some fluttered by the drum. One landed on Tiko’s head!

Just then, Miss Lark the music teacher walked in, humming a happy tune. “Hello, little friend,” she said kindly. “It’s almost time for the children’s song circle.”

Tiko’s ears drooped. “I made a mess,” he said in a tiny voice. “I tried to help, but I rushed. I mixed up the music papers.”

Miss Lark knelt down. “Thank you for telling me the truth,” she said. “That is very brave. We can fix this. But we must be quick and smart. The children will come soon.”

Tiko felt a warm flutter in his heart. “I want to make amends,” he said. “I want to make it right.”

Miss Lark smiled. “Let’s use our time well. First, we breathe. In…and out.” They both took a slow breath. The room felt calm again.

Tick… tock… said the clock.

“Second,” said Miss Lark, “we make a plan.”

Tiko’s whiskers wiggled. “Plan!” he squeaked. “I can look for page numbers. I can sort from 1 to 10.”

“Good idea,” said Miss Lark. “I will check the titles. We will match the pages together. Teamwork!”

They began. Tiko scampered to the piano. He sniffed the floor—dusty and sweet—and tugged a page from under the pedals. “Page 3!” he called.

“Wonderful,” said Miss Lark. She tapped the pages into a neat pile. Tap-tap. “Here is page 2.”

Tick… tock…

Tiko hurried to the drum and slid under it. His soft paws felt the smooth floor. “Page 4!” he sang. He dashed to the chair and found page 6. He tiptoed to the window and grabbed page 5 from the curtain.

Tick… tock…

“Maybe we need a basket,” Tiko said. He spotted a small blue basket by the crayons. He dragged it with all his might. Scrape, scrape. “We can put found pages in here. Then we won’t drop them.”

“That is very resourceful,” said Miss Lark. “You found a tool to help us use our time.”

Tiko’s chest puffed with a tiny proud feeling. They sorted and matched. 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7… “Where is page 8?” Tiko wondered.

He listened. The room was quiet. Then—flutter, flutter—he heard a whisper from the music stand. Tiko climbed up the stand with careful little steps. There it was. Page 8 was stuck under the clip.

“I found it!” he squeaked, tail swishing. “Now we have 1 through 10.”

Miss Lark clapped softly. “You worked hard. You fixed your mistake. That is making amends.”

Tiko nodded. “I am sorry I rushed. Next time, I will ask before I move the papers. I will take my time.”

“That’s a smart plan,” Miss Lark said. She looked at the clock. “We still have a few minutes. Would you like to help set the metronome?”

Tiko’s eyes sparkled. The metronome was a small wooden triangle with a swinging arm. It went tick-tick-tick in a steady beat. Tiko pressed the button. The arm swung: tick-tick-tick. The sound felt like a gentle heartbeat.

“The beat helps us with time,” said Miss Lark. “It keeps us steady, not rushed.”

Tiko swayed to the rhythm. “Steady like this,” he giggled.

Soon the children arrived. They sat on colorful mats. Miss Lark handed each child a shaker or a small drum. “Today we learned about fixing mistakes and using our time,” she said, smiling at Tiko. “Our friend helped us make things right.”

Tiko waved his paw. “I made a mess,” he told the children, “but I made amends. I said sorry. I helped fix it. I used a plan. I used tools. And I moved with the beat, not too fast.”

The children smiled and shook their shakers. Chika-chika! Boom-boom! The room filled with happy sound. Tiko felt peaceful and proud.

As the music rose, the clock went tick… tock… and Tiko’s heart kept the steady beat. He had learned to slow down, to be kind, and to make things right.

Lesson: When you make a mistake, say sorry, make a plan, use your time well, and be kind. That is how we make amends.

The End.
