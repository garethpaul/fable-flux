# Pippa the Parrot and the Busy Basement

“Squawk! Good morning!” chirped Pippa, a bright green parrot with a yellow crown of feathers. Her feathers glowed like leaves in sunshine. She flapped down the stairs to the busy basement.

The basement hummed and clinked. The washing machine hummed. The dryer clicked. Boxes rustled, and a small fan whirred. The air smelled like warm soap and cardboard. Light peeped in from a tiny window. It made little dust sparkles dance.

“Today is Fix-It Day,” said Maya, Pippa’s friend. Maya wore blue overalls and a big smile. “We need to sort, fix, and save. Ready, Pippa?”

Pippa bobbed her head. “Sort, fix, save!” she sang. Her song was like little bells.

They looked around. There were piles of things. A box of buttons. A basket of bottles. A tangle of strings and wires. A wobbly chair. A stack of old papers.

Pippa’s tummy fluttered. There was so much to do. “Squawk… what if I can’t help?” she whispered. Her heart beat fast. She tucked her wings.

Maya knelt beside her. “You are creative, Pippa. You see new ways. That is your superpower.”

Pippa blinked. “I have a superpower?”

“Yes,” said Maya. “Let’s use it.”

They began. Maya clapped. “Step one: sort.” She pointed to three big bins with pictures.
- A bottle on one bin.
- A leaf on one bin.
- A hammer on one bin.

Pippa tapped her beak. “Think, think,” she said softly. “Bottles in the bottle bin. Leaves and food in the leaf bin. Broken things for fixing go in the hammer bin.”

She picked up a crinkly bottle. It was clear and cool. “Bottle bin!” she chirped, dropping it with a happy clink.

She found a soft apple core on a plate. It smelled sweet and a little sour. “Leaf bin!” she said, plopping it in.

She lifted a wobbly toy car with a missing wheel. “Hammer bin,” she declared.

Maya smiled. “Great thinking, Pippa. You used your brain like a helper.”

Pippa’s chest puffed. A warm glow filled her. “I can do this,” she said.

“Step two: fix,” said Maya.

They looked at the wobbly chair. Pippa tapped it with her beak. “Wobble, wobble,” she sang. She looked at the legs. One screw was loose. It winked like a tiny moon.

Pippa thought. “Loose screw. Needs twist.” She flapped to the toolbox. The tools smelled like metal. She chose a small shiny screwdriver. “Twist-twist helper!”

Maya held the chair. Pippa put her foot on the screw and turned the tool with her beak. Twist, twist. The screw tightened with a little squeak.

Pippa hopped back. “Try now!”

Maya sat. The chair stood strong. “It works!” Maya cheered.

Pippa bobbed. “I did it! My ideas work!” Her feathers ruffled with joy.

They went to the tangle of strings and wires. It looked like a spaghetti monster. Pippa took a breath. “Step by step,” she said.

She followed one red wire with her beak. “Red goes here,” she said, laying it straight. She found a green string. It was silky and smooth. “Green goes there.”

She hummed a steady song: “Sort, fix, save. Be brave, be brave.”

Soon, the spaghetti monster was calm. The wires were neat. The strings were in a loop. Pippa placed them in little jars: one for wires, one for strings. Clink, clink.

Maya clapped again. “Pippa, you are careful and smart.”

Pippa felt taller. “I am careful and smart,” she echoed. Saying it made her heart feel big and bright.

“Step three: save,” said Maya. She pointed to the stack of old papers. “What can we do?”

Pippa sniffed the paper. It smelled like books and dust. She pecked a piece with drawings on one side and blank on the other. “We can draw on the blank side!” she said. “No waste.”

“Good idea,” said Maya. “That helps the trees.”

Pippa nodded. “Trees need leaves. Leaves need us to save.” She flapped to the markers. Swish-swish. She drew a big green tree and a little blue bird. “Saves make smiles,” she sang.

They kept saving. Pippa put matching buttons in a tin. Click. She put tiny boxes inside big boxes. Nest, nest. She made a “Reuse” bin with a sign. She wrote, with help, R-E-U-S-E. “Re-use,” she practiced, pecking each letter.

At last, the busy basement felt calm. The air smelled clean. The humming sounded friendly now. The fan made a soft breeze on Pippa’s feathers. The bins were full in the right way, and the chair stood tall. The Fix-It Day chart had checkmarks.

Maya crouched down. “How do you feel, Pippa?”

Pippa placed a wing on her chest. “Proud,” she said. “I used my ideas. I helped the Earth. I can think things through.”

Maya nodded. “Yes. When you feel small, remember: one clear thought, one kind choice, one little fix. You can do big things, step by step.”

Pippa smiled so wide her eyes sparkled. “I am creative. I am helpful. I am brave.”

From upstairs, they heard someone call, “Snack time!”

Pippa laughed. “Squawk! Snack time!” She took one more look at the neat bins and the sturdy chair. She whispered, “Good job, me.”

Then she flew up the stairs, singing, “Sort, fix, save! Be brave, be brave!”

The End.
