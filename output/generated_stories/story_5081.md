# Melody's Morning Routine

In a cozy guest room with sunshine streaming through the window, lived a little yellow bird named Melody. Every morning, Melody would wake up and sing her beautiful songs to welcome the day.

"Tweet-tweet-tweet! Good morning, world!" Melody chirped happily from her perch near the window.

But today was different. Melody felt confused and a little scared. She had forgotten her morning routine! Usually, she knew exactly what to do first, second, and third. But this morning, her mind felt all jumbled up like puzzle pieces scattered on the floor.

"Oh no!" Melody said, fluffing her bright yellow feathers nervously. "What am I supposed to do first? I can't remember!"

Melody flew around the guest room in circles, feeling worried. The soft carpet below looked fuzzy and warm, and the pretty blue curtains danced gently in the breeze. But nothing felt right without her routine.

Then Melody took a deep breath and listened carefully. She could hear her heart beating fast - thump, thump, thump. She needed to think step by step.

"Wait," Melody said to herself, closing her eyes tight. "Let me listen to my body and think. What do I always do when I wake up?"

First, she remembered stretching her wings. Melody spread her wings wide and felt the morning air tickle her feathers. "Ahh, that feels good!" she chirped.

"What's next?" Melody asked herself, using her thinking skills. She listened to her tummy. "Rumble, rumble!" it said. "Oh yes! I eat my breakfast seeds!"

Melody flew to her little food dish on the windowsill. The seeds looked like tiny brown and black jewels sparkling in the sunlight. She pecked at them carefully, one by one. Crunch, crunch, crunch!

"Now what?" Melody wondered. She looked around the guest room and saw the pretty mirror on the dresser. "Oh! I clean my feathers!"

Melody flew to the mirror and began grooming her yellow feathers with her beak. She worked carefully, making sure each feather was neat and tidy. She could see her reflection getting prettier and prettier.

"Last step," Melody said with growing confidence. "I practice my songs!"

Melody took a deep breath and began to sing. "La-la-la-tweet-tweet!" Her voice filled the guest room with beautiful music. The sound bounced off the walls and made everything feel magical.

As she sang, Melody felt proud and happy. She had used her courage to stay calm when she felt scared. She had listened to her body and used her thinking skills to remember each step. Her wings, her voice, and her mind all worked together perfectly - that's coordination!

"I did it!" Melody sang joyfully. "I remembered my whole routine! Stretch, eat, clean, and sing!"

From that day on, whenever Melody felt confused or forgot something, she would take a deep breath, listen carefully, and think step by step. She learned that having a routine made her feel safe and happy, and that she could always figure things out if she was brave and used her thinking skills.

The guest room filled with Melody's cheerful songs, and she knew that every morning would be wonderful when she followed her special routine.

The End.
