# Ellie’s Treehouse Team

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf. She had soft gray ears and a tiny, happy trumpet. “Prrr-reep!” she squeaked when she felt excited. Ellie had a cozy treehouse. It smelled like wood and leaves. The floor felt warm from the sun. Wind chimes sang, ting-ting, in the breeze.

Today was a special day. Ellie put out little cups of cool water and crunchy apple slices. “Friends, come play!” she called. Her voice was kind, like a hug.

Soon, friends climbed up the ladder. Tiko the monkey swung in. “Oo-oo! I love the chimes!” he said, clapping his hands. Zara the zebra trotted up the steps. Her stripes shone in the sunlight. “Clip-clop! I brought story cards.” Pippa the parrot fluttered down. Her feathers were bright red, blue, and green. “Squawk! I brought songs!” Gus the turtle came last. He moved slowly, tap-tap, tap-tap. “Hi,” he said in a gentle way. “I like puzzles.”

Ellie smiled. “We are all different,” she said. “That makes our team strong.”

They decided to make a banner. “Let’s write Treehouse Team!” said Tiko. He twirled a paintbrush. Drip! A red dot landed on his nose. He giggled. Zara lined up the paints. “We can make patterns,” she said. “Stripe, dot, stripe, dot!” Pippa hummed a tune. “We can sing while we paint. La-la-la!” Gus took out a small box. “I brought shapes for a border,” he said. “Squares and circles.”

They started to paint. The treehouse was full of happy sounds—soft brushes swooshing, Pippa’s song, and Ellie’s small trumpet. “Prrr-reep!” The air smelled like sweet apples and paint.

But, oh no! The brush got stuck in a knotted string of bells. Tiko pulled, and the bells tangled more. The banner edge tore a little. “Uh-oh,” whispered Tiko. “I made a mess.”

Pippa flapped. “Squawk! What do we do? The bells are twisty!” Zara frowned. “We need the banner for our team.” Gus blinked. “This looks like a puzzle.”

Ellie felt a flutter in her belly. Her ears warmed. For a moment, she wanted to hide. Then she took a slow breath. In and out. She remembered: We are a team. We are different. We can help.

“We can fix this,” Ellie said softly but surely. “Let’s use our different skills. We can think. We can try. We can practice.”

Zara nodded. “First, we need a plan.” She tapped her hoof. “Plan steps help me.”

Ellie smiled. “Step one: Look carefully. Step two: Work slowly. Step three: Help each other.”

They all looked. The bells were looped around the brush and the banner. The knot looked like a tiny nest. Pippa tilted her head. “I can use my beak to lift the small bell,” she said. “I am good at tiny things.”

“Good idea,” said Ellie. “Tiko, you can hold the brush steady. You are strong and quick.”

“Got it!” Tiko said. He held the brush with gentle hands.

“Zara,” Ellie said, “you are good at patterns. See where the string goes.”

“I see it,” said Zara. “Under, over, under, over.”

“Gus,” Ellie asked, “can you bring the square tape? It is sticky, and you are careful.”

“Yes,” said Gus. “And I will go slow.”

Ellie took another breath. “I will keep the banner safe with my trunk. Prrr-reep!” She wrapped her trunk softly around the torn edge.

They began. Pippa lifted the tiny bell with a careful peck. Tiko held the brush very still. Zara traced the string with her hoof, humming the pattern. Gus inched over with the tape, peel, stick, pat. Ellie steadied the banner and whispered, “We can do it. We can do it.”

The bells wiggled free. Tinkle-tinkle! The knot opened like a little smile. Everyone sighed with joy.

“We did it!” Tiko danced. Pippa squawked a happy note. “Beautiful teamwork!” Zara beamed. Gus grinned, slow and wide.

Ellie felt warm and proud. “We used our different strengths,” she said. “We helped each other. We made a plan. We practiced patience.”

They fixed the tear with tape and a bright blue star. Then they finished the banner. It read: Treehouse Team! Around the words were shapes and stripes and dots. It was colorful like all of them.

“Let’s hang it high,” said Ellie. They lifted together—trunk, tail, wing, hoof, and careful turtle feet. The banner swayed gently. The wind carried the smell of apples and paint and leaves. The chimes sang again, ting-ting, with the bells, tinkle-tinkle.

Tiko passed the apple slices. Crunch, crunch. Pippa poured cool water. Zara told a story about friends who learn. Gus finished a small puzzle. Ellie listened. She felt calm and brave.

Pippa looked at the banner. “We are not the same,” she said. “And that is good.”

Zara nodded. “We need each part.”

Gus tapped the tape roll. “Different helps.”

Tiko patted Ellie’s shoulder. “And you led us with a gentle plan.”

Ellie smiled and trumpeted a tiny cheer. “Prrr-reep! Today we learned: Our differences make us strong. When we help each other, think together, and practice, we can solve problems.”

Everyone hugged in their own way—wing, hoof, hand, trunk, shell. The treehouse felt safe and bright. Outside, the leaves whispered, Yes.

The End.
