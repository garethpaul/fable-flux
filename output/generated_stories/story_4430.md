# Ollie Otter's Brave Day

In a sunny backyard lived a playful otter named Ollie. He loved splashing in the blue kiddie pool. The warm water felt wonderful on his soft brown fur.

Ollie had many friends who played with him every day. There was Rosie Rabbit, Tommy Turtle, and Penny Pig. They all loved playing together in the bright sunshine.

One beautiful morning, Ollie wanted to show off. He saw his friends playing catch near the flower garden. The red roses smelled so sweet in the warm air.

"Watch me!" Ollie called out loudly. "I can catch better than anyone!"

Ollie jumped high to catch the yellow ball. SPLASH! He landed right in the pool. Water flew everywhere! It soaked Rosie's pretty pink dress and Tommy's green shell.

"Oh no!" cried Rosie. Her dress was dripping wet.

"My shell is all soggy!" said Tommy sadly.

Penny stepped back from the water. "That wasn't very nice, Ollie," she said quietly.

Ollie felt his tummy drop. His friends looked upset. He had wanted to impress them, but instead he made them sad. His whiskers drooped down low.

"I... I didn't mean to," Ollie whispered. He felt very small and sorry.

Ollie swam to the edge of the pool. The water felt cold now, not fun anymore. He looked at his wet friends with big, sad eyes.

"I'm really sorry," Ollie said in his gentlest voice. "I wanted you to think I was amazing. But I made a big mess instead."

Rosie's ears perked up. "You wanted to impress us?"

"Yes," Ollie nodded. "Sometimes I don't feel special enough. I thought if I showed off, you'd like me more."

Tommy's kind eyes grew warm. "But Ollie, we already think you're wonderful!"

"You're the best swimmer we know," added Penny with a smile.

"And you always share your toys," said Rosie.

"Really?" Ollie's eyes grew bright. He felt warm sunshine in his heart.

"Really!" all his friends said together.

"But I still made a mess," Ollie said. "How can I fix it?"

"We can work together!" suggested Tommy. "Teamwork makes everything better!"

Ollie's face lit up. "Yes! Let's clean up together!"

Rosie hopped to get the soft towels. Tommy slowly carried the soap. Penny brought the warm water from the house. The grass felt tickly under their feet.

Ollie helped Rosie dry her pretty dress. He gently cleaned Tommy's shiny shell. He wiped up all the splashed water with Penny.

"Thank you for helping me," Ollie said. "And thank you for being such good friends."

"That's what friends do," smiled Rosie.

As they finished cleaning, Ollie felt proud. Not because he showed off, but because he said sorry and worked hard to fix his mistake.

"I learned something important today," Ollie said, sitting in the warm sunshine. "I don't need to show off. You like me just the way I am!"

"Exactly!" laughed his friends.

From that day on, Ollie still loved to play and splash. But he never forgot that being kind and saying sorry made him feel much better than showing off ever could.

The four friends spent the rest of the sunny day playing gentle games together. The backyard filled with happy laughter and the sweet smell of friendship.

The End.
