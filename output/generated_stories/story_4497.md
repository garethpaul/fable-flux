# Poppy the Patient Panda’s Playday

In a cozy, comfortable nursery with soft rugs and sunny windows, lived Poppy the Patient Panda. The nursery smelled like fresh soap and warm oatmeal. Tiny sneakers lined up by the door. A little whistle hung on a hook, and a basket of balls sat by a fluffy beanbag.

“Good morning, Poppy,” said Miss Lila, the kind teacher. “Today is Sports Playday!”

Poppy’s round ears perked up. “Sports?” he asked. “Do we run and jump?”

“We do,” Miss Lila said with a smile. “But first, we practice healthy habits. We drink water, we warm up, we listen, and we wait our turn.”

Poppy nodded. He was a patient panda, and he liked to do things safely.

His friends waddled in. Tiko the turtle wore tiny knee pads. Bree the bunny had a bright yellow headband. Max the mouse squeaked with joy, holding a small blue ball.

Poppy sniffed the air. “I smell oranges,” he said.

“That’s our snack for later,” Miss Lila said. “But first, stretch time.”

They reached up high. “Hello, sky!” said Miss Lila.

“Hello, sky!” the friends echoed, wiggling their fingers. Poppy felt his arms stretch and his back feel warm. They touched their toes. “Hello, toes!” The carpet felt soft and tickly under Poppy’s pads. He giggled.

“Water break,” said Miss Lila. She held out little cups. Poppy took a sip. The water was cool and quiet in his mouth. “Ahh,” he said, feeling calm.

“Now we play gentle soccer,” Miss Lila announced. She set a foam goal at the end of the rug. “We pass the ball. We cheer for friends. We use kind bodies. That is respect.”

Bree bounced. “I go first!” she said.

Poppy lifted his paw. “We can make a line,” he suggested. “Everyone gets a turn.”

“Good idea, Poppy,” said Miss Lila. “That is fair. That is justice.”

They made a line. Poppy waited with his paws folded. He felt excited bubbles in his tummy, but he breathed in and out, slow and steady. In. Out. He smiled at Tiko. “You can do it,” he whispered.

Bree kicked gently. “Goal!” she cheered. Everyone clapped. Max went next. He tapped the ball with his tiny foot. The ball rolled and rolled. “So close!” said Miss Lila.

Poppy’s turn came. He felt a little worried. “What if I miss?” he asked softly.

Miss Lila knelt beside him. “We try. We learn. We improve.” She held up one finger. “Step one: eyes on the ball.” She held up two fingers. “Step two: slow, steady kick.” She held up three fingers. “Step three: kind heart, no matter what.”

Poppy took a breath. He looked at the ball. He tapped it with his paw. The ball bumped the goal post and rolled away. “Oh,” Poppy said. His ears drooped.

Bree hopped up. “Great try, Poppy! You looked so steady!”

Max squeaked, “You can try again after me!”

Poppy felt warm in his chest. “Thank you,” he said. “I will keep practicing.”

They played and passed. They said “please” and “thank you.” When someone bumped into someone else, they said, “Sorry,” and “Are you okay?” The room was full of soft thumps, happy claps, and little giggles.

Then a small problem popped up. Tiko had not had a turn in a while. Bree and Max were passing back and forth very fast.

Poppy raised his paw. “Friends,” he said gently. “Tiko needs a turn. Let’s be fair.”

Bree’s ears drooped. “Oh! We forgot.”

Max nodded. “Sorry, Tiko.” He rolled the ball to Tiko.

Tiko smiled slow and wide. “Thank you,” he said. He took a breath and kicked. The ball slid right into the goal. “Goal,” he whispered, proud.

“Justice is fair turns for everyone,” Miss Lila said. “Thank you, Poppy, for speaking up with respect.”

Soon it was snack time. Miss Lila brought orange slices and crunchy carrots. The oranges smelled bright and sweet. Poppy peeled a slice and felt the juice tickle his paw. He licked his lips. “Mmm! Fruit helps me play,” he said.

“Healthy fuel,” Miss Lila agreed. “And don’t forget to wash.”

They lined up at the sink. The water splashed like a tiny waterfall. Poppy rubbed his paws. “Scrub, scrub,” he said, humming. The soap smelled like lemons. He dried his paws on a soft towel.

After snack, they tried again. Poppy stepped up to the ball. He remembered: eyes on the ball, slow, steady kick, kind heart. He took a deep breath. Tap.

The ball rolled smooth and true. It kissed the back of the foam goal. “Goal!” everyone cheered.

Poppy’s eyes sparkled. He felt proud, warm, and calm. “I did it,” he said. “I kept trying. I waited my turn. I ate a healthy snack. I drank water. I was kind.”

Miss Lila clapped. “That is the way, Poppy. Healthy habits help our bodies. Respect helps our friends. Practice brings improvement. Fair turns make justice.”

Poppy hugged his friends. The nursery felt safe and happy. The sun painted gold squares on the floor. Poppy took one more sip of water and smiled.

“Tomorrow,” he said, “we will try again.”

“And we will be patient,” said Tiko.

“And fair,” said Bree.

“And kind,” squeaked Max.

Poppy nodded. “Healthy habits, every day.”

The End.
