# Koko the Koala Learns to Share

In a cozy study corner filled with soft cushions and colorful books, lived a calm koala named Koko. The study corner smelled like fresh eucalyptus leaves and had warm sunlight streaming through the window. Koko loved spending his mornings there, reading picture books and drawing with his favorite crayons.

One sunny Tuesday, Koko was coloring a beautiful rainbow when he heard tiny footsteps. "Tap, tap, tap!" went the little feet across the wooden floor.

"Hello, Koko!" squeaked Penny the mouse, peeking around a big book. "What are you drawing?"

"I'm making a rainbow," said Koko gently, holding up his paper. "See? Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple!"

Penny's whiskers twitched with excitement. "Oh my! That's so pretty! I wish I could color too, but I don't have any crayons."

Just then, Benny the bunny hopped into the study corner. His fluffy tail wiggled as he sniffed the air. "Something smells like... crayons! Oh wow, Koko, you have so many colors!"

Koko looked at his big box of 24 crayons. He had been using his favorite blue crayon to color the sky in his picture. The warm crayon felt smooth in his paws.

"Can we please use some crayons too?" asked Penny politely. "We promise to be very careful."

Koko hugged his crayon box tighter. "But... but these are MY crayons," he said. "What if you break them? What if you use them all up?"

Penny's little ears drooped down. Benny's nose twitched sadly. They both looked at the floor.

Koko felt a funny feeling in his tummy. It wasn't a good feeling. He watched his friends looking sad, and suddenly he remembered how happy he felt when he was coloring his rainbow.

"Wait," said Koko softly. He thought about how much fun it was to create colorful pictures. Wouldn't it be even MORE fun if his friends could create pictures too?

"You know what?" Koko said, his voice getting brighter. "I have an idea! There are so many crayons in this box. I think there are enough for all of us to share!"

Penny's eyes sparkled like tiny stars. "Really? You mean it?"

"Yes!" said Koko, opening his crayon box wide. "Penny, would you like the red and pink crayons? And Benny, how about the green and brown ones for drawing carrots?"

"Thank you, Koko!" squeaked Penny, carefully taking the crayons. "You're so kind!"

"This is wonderful!" said Benny, already starting to draw an orange carrot. "Sharing makes everything more fun!"

The three friends spread out their papers on the soft rug. The study corner filled with the gentle sounds of coloring - the soft "swoosh" of crayons on paper and happy giggles.

"Look!" said Penny, showing her picture of a red flower. "I made this for you, Koko, to say thank you!"

"And I drew you a carrot!" said Benny. "Because friends share with friends!"

Koko felt warm and happy inside, like drinking hot cocoa on a cold day. "I have an idea," he said. "Let's make one big picture together! We can each color a part!"

So they worked together, sharing all the crayons and creating the most beautiful picture ever - a garden full of rainbow flowers, orange carrots, and a bright blue sky.

"Sharing is the best," said Koko, smiling at his friends. "When we share, everyone gets to be happy, and we can make even more beautiful things together!"

From that day on, the study corner became a place where all the friends came to share, create, and learn together.

The End.
