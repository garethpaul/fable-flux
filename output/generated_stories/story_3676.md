# Polly's Peaceful Painting

Polly the parrot loved to paint. Her bright green feathers shimmered in the soft nursery light. The room smelled like lavender and felt warm and cozy. Polly had her own special easel next to the rocking chair.

Today, Polly wanted to paint a beautiful rainbow. She dipped her brush in red paint. But when she tried to make a curved line, it came out all wobbly.

"Oh no!" squawked Polly. Her feathers ruffled up. "This is not right!"

She tried again with orange paint. The line was still bumpy and crooked. Polly's heart began to beat faster. Her wings started to flap.

"Why can't I paint a perfect rainbow?" Polly cried. She felt angry and frustrated. The brush shook in her wing.

Polly threw her brush down. It made a little splat on the floor. Paint drops scattered everywhere. Polly's beautiful nursery now had orange spots on the soft carpet.

"I'm a terrible painter!" Polly said loudly. Her voice echoed in the quiet room.

Just then, Mama Bird flew through the open window. She saw Polly's ruffled feathers and the paint on the floor. The room felt tense and upset.

"Oh my little artist," Mama Bird said gently. "What happened here?"

"I can't paint a good rainbow!" Polly sniffled. "Everything I make looks wrong!"

Mama Bird perched beside Polly. She spoke in a soft, calm voice. "Let's take some deep breaths together. Can you breathe in slowly with me?"

Polly breathed in through her beak. The air felt cool and fresh. She let it out slowly. Her feathers began to settle down.

"That's wonderful," Mama Bird said. "Now, let me show you something special."

Mama Bird pointed to Polly's painting. "Look at your wobbly lines. They look like dancing ribbons! And see how the colors blend together? That's magical!"

Polly tilted her head. She looked at her painting with new eyes. The wobbly red line did look like it was dancing. The bumpy orange line looked bouncy and fun.

"Every artist paints differently," Mama Bird explained. "Your way of painting is special because it's yours. There's no wrong way to be creative."

Polly felt her anger melting away like ice cream in sunshine. Her heart felt lighter and happier.

"Can I try painting again?" Polly asked.

"Of course!" Mama Bird smiled. "Remember to breathe deeply if you feel upset. And remember that practice makes us better, not perfect."

Polly picked up her brush again. This time, when she painted a yellow line that wiggled, she smiled. It looked like a happy snake! She painted a green line that curved like a smile.

"Look, Mama!" Polly chirped excitedly. "My rainbow is dancing!"

"It's absolutely beautiful," Mama Bird said. "You stayed calm and kept trying. That shows how strong and wonderful you are."

Polly's chest puffed up with pride. She felt good about herself again. The nursery felt peaceful and happy once more.

"Next time I feel angry, I'll remember to breathe deeply," Polly said. "And I'll remember that my art is special just the way it is."

Mama Bird gave Polly a gentle wing hug. "That's my wise little artist. You learned something very important today about staying calm and believing in yourself."

Polly hung her dancing rainbow on the nursery wall. Every time she looked at it, she remembered to be patient with herself and to breathe deeply when things felt hard.

The End.
