# Little Fawn Finds Her Song

In a sunny musical practice room, a gentle deer fawn named Fira stood on soft hooves. The room smelled like polished wood and lemon oil. Bright posters showed drums, flutes, guitars, and a shiny piano. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor.

Fira’s ears twitched. “Today, I will learn a song,” she whispered. Her voice was tiny but brave.

On the table lay small instruments: a shaker egg, a wooden block, a triangle, and a little drum with a soft beater. A round rug showed a circle of flags from many places. Fira liked to look at the colors and think of faraway songs.

Coach Mira, a kind music teacher, smiled. She wore a scarf with bright patterns. “Hello, Fira,” she said softly. “We will make gentle music today. We will listen, we will try, and we will stay safe.”

Fira nodded. “I want to try the triangle,” she said. The silver triangle sparkled. “But what if I play it wrong?”

Coach Mira knelt to Fira’s eye level. “Your try is important,” she said. “We learn with patience. We keep our bodies safe. And we cheer for our brave hearts.”

Fira took a slow breath. She remembered the safety rules posted on the wall: “Soft hooves on the floor. One instrument at a time. Ears ready to listen.”

Ting! Fira tapped the triangle very lightly. The sound was bright and sweet, like a tiny star. Her tail gave a happy wiggle.

“That was careful and kind,” Coach Mira said. “Would you like to tap to a beat?” She clapped slowly: clap… clap… clap….

Fira tried to match the rhythm. Ting… ting… ting… She missed a clap and frowned. Her ears drooped. “I made a mistake.”

Coach Mira tapped her heart. “Mistakes are steps,” she said. “Each step helps you grow. Can you try again with patience?”

Fira closed her eyes and listened. She felt the warm sun on her back. She felt the smooth beater in her hoof. Ting… ting… ting… This time, she stayed with the claps.

“I did it!” Fira said, her eyes bright.

“You did,” Coach Mira agreed. “Your careful listening helped. Now, would you like to learn a short song from my family? It is a hello song. In my language, we say, ‘Salaam.’ It means peace.”

Fira’s ears perked. “Salaam,” she tried, rolling the word gently. It felt like a soft blanket.

Coach Mira hummed a simple tune: laa-laa, la-la-la, laa-laa. She tapped the wooden block in a soft pattern. “We go slow. We breathe. We keep the room safe and calm.”

Fira switched to the shaker egg. She held it with both hooves so it wouldn’t fly. Shake… pause… shake-shake… pause. The tiny seeds inside went shh-shh, like rain on leaves.

“Beautiful,” Coach Mira said. “Let’s add the word ‘Salaam’ at the end.”

Fira swallowed. Her voice felt shy. “What if my voice is too small?”

“Small can be sweet,” Coach Mira said. “Your voice belongs.”

Fira took a breath in. She let the breath out. She hummed with Coach Mira: laa-laa, la-la-la, laa-laa… “Salaam,” she whispered at the end. The room felt warm. The sound felt friendly.

“Again?” Coach Mira asked.

“Yes,” Fira said, a little louder. They sang together: laa-laa, la-la-la, laa-laa… “Salaam.” Fira smiled. “I like that word. It feels kind.”

“It is,” Coach Mira said. “Some friends say ‘Hola,’ some say ‘Namaste,’ some say ‘Jambo.’ Each hello holds a story.”

Fira’s eyes shone. “Music lets us visit many stories.”

Coach Mira nodded. “And patience helps us hear them.”

They tried the little drum next. “Remember safety,” Coach Mira reminded. “Gentle taps. Keep space around you.”

Fira looked left and right. No one was too close. Tap-tap… tap. The drum sounded like soft hoofbeats on a path. Fira felt steady. “I can do hard things,” she whispered.

“You can,” Coach Mira said. “Let’s put it all together. Triangle, shaker, voice. Small steps, one at a time.”

Fira set the triangle on a mat so it wouldn’t roll. She held the shaker egg. She hummed. She tapped the triangle at the end. Ting! The last note sparkled in the air.

Fira’s chest felt warm and proud. “Coach Mira,” she said, “I finished my song!”

Coach Mira clapped gently. “You completed your task with care. You stayed safe. You were patient. And you believed in yourself.”

Fira’s tail swished. “At first, I felt scared. Now I feel strong inside.”

“That feeling is your self-esteem,” Coach Mira said. “It grows when you try, when you wait, and when you keep going.”

Fira put the instruments back in their places. She checked that the beater was safe on the hook and the shaker egg was snug in its tray. She looked at the flag rug and touched a tiny blue square with her hoof. “Hello, music from everywhere,” she said.

Coach Mira wrapped her colorful scarf around Fira like a quick hug. “Your song is part of the world now.”

Fira took one more gentle breath. “I am Fira. I am careful. I am patient. I am brave. I can learn new things.”

And the little fawn walked out of the musical practice room with soft, happy steps, carrying her bright new hello in her heart.

The End.
