# Giraffe Long-Neck Makes a Choice

In the sunny creative art room, paint pots smiled with color. Red, blue, green, and gold glitter sparkled like tiny stars. Paper rustled softly. Brushes whispered, swish-swish, in little cups of water. A thoughtful giraffe named Long-Neck tiptoed in. His hooves made gentle clack-clack sounds on the floor.

“I want to paint,” Long-Neck said. “I also want to practice ball-rolling for giraffe games.” He blinked his big soft eyes. He liked to think before he did things. He liked to make good choices.

On a big table, there was a round ball. It was orange and bouncy. Boing! It made a happy sound when it tapped the floor. There was also a tall easel with clean paper. It smelled like fresh trees. A sunny breeze came in the window and tickled Long-Neck’s ears.

Friend Zebra trotted in and smiled. “Come race with me!” Zebra said. “Let’s play now!”

Long-Neck felt a wiggle in his tummy. He wanted to play. He also wanted to practice. He also wanted to paint a picture of the sun.

“I will think,” Long-Neck said. He took a deep breath. In… and out. He touched his heart with his hoof. “What is the best choice?”

Teacher Owl fluffed her feathers and hooted softly. “Hoo-hoo. You can decide. What is your plan? You can be independent. You can make a schedule.”

“A schedule?” asked Long-Neck.

“It is a little plan,” Owl said. “You choose. Then you stick to it. That is integrity. That means you do what you say.”

Long-Neck nodded. “I will practice first,” he said. “Then I will paint. Then I will play.”

Zebra stamped a hoof. “Okay! I will wait and cheer,” Zebra said. Zebra sat nearby with a swishy tail and clapped softly.

Long-Neck rolled the orange ball. His long legs made careful steps. Roll… stop. Roll… stop. The ball hummed over the floor, smooth like a purr. “I can do it,” he whispered. He tried again, a little faster. Boing… roll… boing! The ball bumped a paint cup. The water made a tiny splash. Plip!

Long-Neck froze. “Oh!” he said. He looked at the floor. A drop of blue water made a dot like a blueberry.

Teacher Owl tilted her head. “What do we do when we make a little mess?” she asked.

Long-Neck thought. He felt the wiggle in his tummy again. “We clean it up,” he said. He got a soft cloth. Wipe, wipe. The blue dot went away. “I will be careful next time,” he said.

He tried again. He kept his eyes on the ball. He made his steps slower. Roll… stop… roll… stop. Zebra cheered, “You’re getting it!”

Long-Neck smiled. His heart felt warm. Practice made the roll smooth. His legs felt strong and sure. He tried a gentle turn. The ball curved around a small cone. He tried another turn. The ball curved around a paint pot, nice and safe.

“Good choice,” Owl said. “You practiced. You stayed calm. You used integrity.”

Long-Neck felt proud. “Now I will paint,” he said.

He washed his hooves and padded to the easel. He picked a soft brush. Swish-swish. The brush felt tickly in his hoof. He dipped it in yellow paint. The bright yellow smelled sweet like sunshine on lemons. He painted a round sun. Circle, circle. He added bright orange rays. Flick, flick. He listened to the paper make a whisper sound. His picture looked happy.

Zebra peeked. “It glows!” Zebra said.

Long-Neck smiled. He added a little orange ball near the sun. He painted a tiny path, curving like his practice rolls. He added his long neck in the corner, smiling. He giggled at his own long spots.

“Now we can play,” Zebra said.

Long-Neck looked at Teacher Owl. He looked at the ball. He looked out the window at the soft grass. He took a breath. “Yes,” he said. “I practiced. I painted. Now I will play.”

Outside, the grass felt cool. The sky was a gentle blue. Long-Neck rolled the ball to Zebra. The ball hummed low as it moved. Zebra rolled it back. They made a little game of turns. “My turn,” said Long-Neck. “Your turn,” said Zebra. They both waited and watched. They both used kind words. They both laughed, ha-ha-ha.

When the game was done, they went back to the art room. Long-Neck looked at his painting. He looked at the ball. He felt calm and happy.

Teacher Owl nodded. “What did you learn today?”

Long-Neck stood tall. “I learned to make a plan,” he said. “I learned to practice first. Practice makes me better. I learned to clean up my mess. That is integrity. I learned I can choose. I can be independent.”

Zebra bumped shoulders with him. “And we had fun!” Zebra said.

Long-Neck hugged his friend gently. “Yes,” he said. “Practice, good choices, and play. I can do it.”

He hung his sun painting to dry. The glitter sparkled like tiny stars again.

The End.
