# Song Snacks and Seatbelt Tracks

Rafi the musical raccoon wiggled his striped tail and tapped his tiny drum. “Tap, tap, tap!” he sang. Rafi loved music more than anything. Today, he climbed into the family car with Mom, Dad, and his baby sister, Lina. The seats were soft and warm from the sun, and the air smelled like oranges from a little car freshener.

“Seatbelts, friends!” Dad said.

“Click!” Rafi buckled his seatbelt. “Safety first is smart and strong,” he sang in a bouncy voice.

Lina giggled and clapped. “Song!” she squeaked.

“We’re going to the library,” Mom said. “We’ll learn new things.”

Rafi’s ears perked up. “Learn? I love to learn! I can practice reading signs. And I can practice clean hands and clean snacks,” he added, patting his little backpack.

The car hummed down the road. “Mmm,” said Rafi, “I smell crunchy crackers.” His tummy rumbled. “Snack time?”

Mom smiled. “Yes, but first, hygiene. Clean hands help our bodies. Can you show Lina how?”

Rafi nodded. He took out a small bottle of hand wipes and a trash bag for tiny bits. “This is for clean,” he told Lina. “We wipe, wipe, wipe, and toss the wipe in the bag.”

“Wipe-wipe!” Lina said, holding out her hands.

Rafi sang a hand song. “Top and bottom, all around, keep our hands so nice and sound.” The wipes felt cool and a little wet. They smelled like lemons. Rafi showed Lina her palms and fingers. “All clean!” he said. Lina smiled and wiggled her fingers.

“Great cooperation,” Dad said. “Working together makes it easier.”

Rafi puffed with pride. “We are a team.”

The family car rolled past a red sign. Rafi leaned forward. “That sign says STOP,” he announced. “S-T-O-P. Stop means we wait. Waiting is wise.”

Mom glanced back in the mirror. “Nice reading, Rafi! That’s education. You are learning new letters and what they do.”

Rafi grinned. “I like learning words that keep us safe.”

They took a turn. The sun peeked through the windows and made tiny sparkles on Rafi’s drum. “Time for a rhythm snack,” Rafi said. He handed Lina a cracker and held one for himself. “First, napkin on lap,” he reminded. He spread a napkin and showed Lina how to hold her cracker with clean hands. “Crunch, crunch!” The crackers tasted salty and crisp. Rafi chewed with his mouth closed. “I’m practicing manners,” he told Mom.

“That’s another skill,” Mom said. “Good table—car—skills.”

Lina’s cracker dropped. “Uh-oh,” she said, looking sad.

Rafi reached for the little trash bag. “It’s okay,” he said gently. “We do not eat food from the floor. It is not clean. We can get a new cracker.” He smiled and gave her another. “We keep the car tidy.” Lina’s face lit up.

“That was kindness,” Dad said. “You helped your sister and kept her safe.”

Rafi bobbed his head and tapped his drum softly. “Kindness is a happy beat,” he sang.

They drove by a school. Children played on a bright green field. “School!” Rafi said. “School is where we learn. We can learn anytime, anywhere, even in the car.” He looked out the window. “I see a yellow sign with people walking. That means crosswalk. We slow down.”

Dad nodded. “Learning signs helps everyone. Thank you for watching.”

Rafi felt warm and proud. He opened his backpack again. “I brought my picture book,” he said. “I can read to Lina.” He turned pages, rubbing his fingers over the smooth paper. “A is for apple. B is for bath. Baths make us clean and fresh.” He sniffed his fur and giggled. “I like bath time bubbles!”

Lina clapped. “Bubbles!”

Rafi made soft bubble sounds with his drum. “Pop-pop-pop!”

Suddenly, the car bumped over a little pothole. “Bump!” Lina frowned.

Rafi leaned close and sang in a calm voice. “When bumps come by, we breathe and try.” He took a slow breath in and out. Lina copied him and smiled again.

Mom said, “You’re teaching calm breathing. Another important skill.”

They reached a red light. “Red means stop,” Rafi said. He looked at Dad’s hands. “Two hands on the wheel is careful driving.”

“Right,” Dad laughed. “That’s good observation.”

Rafi looked around the car. A tiny crumb hid by his seat. He picked it up and put it in the trash bag. “We clean as we go,” he said. “Clean car, clear mind.”

The car finally pulled into the library lot. “We made it!” Rafi cheered. He unbuckled when the car stopped and handed the trash bag to Mom. “Trash goes in the bin.”

“Thank you for helping,” Mom said. “You showed hygiene, kindness, and learning all ride together.”

Rafi patted his drum. “Today I learned: keep hands clean, read signs, chew politely, breathe calmly, and help my family. Education makes me strong and kind.”

Lina hugged his arm. “Smart Rafi,” she whispered.

Rafi smiled and sang a happy tune as they walked toward the library doors. “Every ride can teach us new, when we work and learn as two… or three… or four!” He laughed, and everyone laughed with him.

The End.
