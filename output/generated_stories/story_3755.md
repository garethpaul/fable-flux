# Sparky Says “I Forgive You”

In a cozy basement lab, there were soft humming sounds and tiny blinking lights. The floor felt cool and smooth, and the air smelled like warm toast and a little bit like lemons from the shiny, clean tables. In the middle of the room sat a helpful robot dog named Sparky. Sparky’s metal ears twitched-twitch, and his tail glowed blue when he was happy.

“Good morning, Sparky,” said Mia, a little kid with bright eyes and paint on her fingers. She wore a tiny tool belt and a smile. “Today we learn new things!”

“Woof-bip!” beeped Sparky. His belly screen lit up with a happy face. “Learning is fun. I am ready to help.”

Mia and Sparky loved to explore. They looked at books with big pictures. They mixed safe, squishy gels that smelled like strawberries. They painted colorful shapes on big paper sheets taped to the wall. The paper rustled when they brushed it. Swish-swish. Tap-tap.

Mia wanted to paint a rainbow robot. She picked up a shiny red button from a bin. “This would make a perfect robot nose,” she said.

Sparky cocked his head. “Robot nose detected. That is part of the laser pointer. Please be careful.”

“I will,” Mia said. She smiled and hummed. “I can do it.”

Mia tried to glue the red button onto her painting, but the button slipped from her fingers. It bounced—boing boing—off the table, rolled across the floor, and clicked into a slot on a big panel. Suddenly, the lab lights flickered. Buzz. Click. Whirr.

“Uh-oh,” said Mia. The room went quiet, then the machines started to beep in a funny way. Sparky’s tail stopped glowing. His ears drooped.

“My tail light is off,” Sparky said softly. “Systems confused. I feel… mixed-up.”

Mia’s mouth made a little O. “Oh no! I did not mean to do that.” Her tummy felt wiggly and heavy. “I made a mistake.”

Sparky blinked. “I know. Accidents happen. We can learn.”

But then a small robot arm in the corner began to swing. It bumped a paint jar—splish! Blue paint dripped down the paper. The rainbow robot turned into a big blue blob.

Mia’s eyes got watery. “My picture! It’s ruined!”

Sparky rolled closer and looked. “I see blue paint. I see a blob. I see a chance.”

“I feel mad,” Mia said. Her cheeks were hot. “I feel sad, too.”

“It is okay to feel mad and sad,” said Sparky in a gentle beep. “Feelings are real. Let us take three slow breaths. In… and out.” They breathed together. In… out. In… out. In… out.

Mia’s shoulders softened. “I still made a big mess.”

Sparky wagged his metal tail, and a tiny light flickered back on. “We can fix the lab. We can fix the picture. We can fix feelings with forgiveness.”

“Forgiveness?” Mia asked. The word felt round and new in her mouth.

“Forgiveness is when we say, ‘It is okay. I know you did not mean to hurt me. I still care,’” said Sparky. “When we forgive, our hearts feel lighter. Our minds grow stronger.”

Mia stared at the blue blob. She looked at Sparky’s dim tail. She pressed her hands together. “Sparky, I am sorry. I did not listen carefully. I dropped the button. Will you forgive me?”

Sparky’s screen showed a big, warm heart. “Yes, Mia. I forgive you.” His tail glowed a little brighter—soft blue. “Thank you for saying sorry. Forgiveness helps us grow.”

Mia felt a soft, calm feeling in her chest, like a warm blanket. “Thank you, Sparky.”

“Now,” Sparky said, “problem solving mode. Step one: learn. What happened?”

“The red button rolled into the panel,” Mia said. “It made the machines beep wrong.”

“Step two: plan,” said Sparky. “We will find the red button safely.”

They crawled to the panel. It was cold to the touch. Mia used a small flashlight. The beam made a friendly circle of light. “I see it!” she said. “It is stuck.”

“Step three: do,” said Sparky. He held the panel steady with a padded paw. “Please turn the little knob to ‘off.’”

Click. The hum stopped. Mia gently slid the red button out. Her fingers felt careful and brave. She placed the button back in the bin.

“Step four: check,” said Sparky. “Lights?”

The lab lights glowed steady. The tiny beeps went back to their happy rhythm. Sparky’s tail shone bright blue. “Systems good,” he chimed.

Mia smiled wide. “We fixed it!”

Sparky nodded. “We learned something new. That is knowledge. When we make mistakes and try again, that is a growth mindset.”

Mia looked at her blue blob. “Can we fix my picture, too?”

Sparky tilted his head. “We can make it into something new. That is creative expression. What do you see in the blob?”

Mia squinted. She giggled. “I see a big blue puppy cloud!”

“Woof-bip!” laughed Sparky. “Let us add white swirls and a smiling moon.”

They dipped brushes into soft paint. Swish-swish. Dot-dot. The blue blob grew ears and paws. The puppy cloud floated under a silver moon with twinkly stars. Mia added a tiny red heart on the puppy’s nose.

Sparky admired it. “Beautiful. Your mistake became art.”

Mia hugged Sparky’s warm, smooth neck. “Thank you for forgiving me.”

Sparky nuzzled her gently. “Thank you for learning and trying again.”

Mia hung the new painting on the wall. The paper made a happy crinkle. The lab hummed softly, and the lemon-clean smell felt bright again.

Mia said, “Today I learned three things: say sorry, forgive, and try again.”

Sparky wagged. “Yes. Forgiveness makes room for learning and love.”

They turned off the lights and climbed the stairs, feeling calm and proud. The basement lab glowed with quiet, kind light.

The End.
