# Benny Bear's Colorful Bath Time

Benny Bear was a fluffy little cub with soft brown fur and the biggest smile in the forest. Every evening, Mama Bear would call, "Bath time, Benny!" But tonight was different. Tonight, Benny had a big problem.

"I don't want to take a bath!" Benny said, crossing his tiny arms. "It's boring! I just stand there while the water splashes. There's nothing fun to do!"

Mama Bear's gentle eyes twinkled. "Hmm," she said, tapping her chin. "What if we made bath time more exciting? What do you like to do best, little one?"

"I love to paint and draw!" Benny said, his ears perking up. "And I love making music! But you can't do those things in the bathtub."

"Are you sure about that?" Mama Bear asked with a mysterious smile. "Let's see what we can discover!"

Together, they walked into the warm, steamy bathroom. The white tiles sparkled, and the bathtub looked big and empty. Mama Bear turned on the faucet, and the water made a cheerful gurgling sound as it filled the tub.

"Close your eyes, Benny," Mama Bear said. "I have a surprise!"

When Benny opened his eyes, he gasped with delight. Floating in the water were special bath crayons in red, blue, yellow, and green! There were also colorful foam letters and numbers bobbing around like tiny boats.

"Wow!" Benny squealed, clapping his paws together. "The bathtub looks like a rainbow!"

"These special crayons work on the bathtub walls," Mama Bear explained. "You can draw pictures, and they wash right off! And listen..." She squeezed a rubber duck, and it made a happy squeaking sound. "You can make music too!"

Benny climbed into the warm, bubbly water. It felt like a cozy hug all around his furry body. He picked up a blue crayon and drew fluffy clouds on the tile wall. Then he used yellow to make a big, bright sun.

"Look, Mama! I'm making the sky!" Benny giggled as soap bubbles tickled his nose.

Next, he arranged the foam letters to spell his name: B-E-N-N-Y. He was so proud! Then he squeezed the rubber duck in rhythm: "Squeak, squeak, squeak-squeak-squeak!" It sounded like a happy song.

"I'm a bath time artist!" Benny announced, drawing green grass and red flowers. The warm water made his muscles feel relaxed and happy.

His friend Rosie Rabbit peeked through the bathroom window. "Benny! What are you doing? I can hear music!"

"I'm having the best bath ever!" Benny called out. "I'm painting pictures and making songs! Bath time isn't boring anymore—it's creative time!"

Rosie's eyes grew wide. "That sounds amazing! Can you teach me tomorrow?"

"Of course!" Benny said, adding purple butterflies to his wall masterpiece. "We can be bath time artists together!"

When Benny finished his bath, he felt clean, relaxed, and very proud of his artwork. Mama Bear helped him wash off the crayon drawings, and they swirled down the drain in colorful spirals.

"Thank you, Mama," Benny said, giving her a big, damp hug. "I learned that even everyday things like baths can be fun when you use your imagination!"

"That's right, sweetheart," Mama Bear said, wrapping him in a fluffy towel. "Creativity can make any time special time. Tomorrow, what new ideas will you try?"

Benny's eyes sparkled with excitement as he thought of all the possibilities.

The End.
