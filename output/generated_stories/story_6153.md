# Tiko the Tortoise Takes a Slow Breath

The playful living room was full of cozy sounds. Soft music hummed. Pillows were piled like little hills. Sunlight made warm squares on the rug. The air smelled like cinnamon tea and fresh oranges.

Tiko the tortoise blinked his gentle eyes. His shell was smooth and green, like a leaf after rain. Tiko moved slowly, but he thought deeply. He was a wise tortoise.

“Today is play-sharing day,” Tiko said kindly. His voice was low and calm. “We will build, we will dance, and we will listen.”

Three friends clapped their hands. Ami wore a bright scarf with tiny stars. Ben had bouncy shoes with blue laces. Mei wore a soft red sweater. All three brought toys from their homes and cultures.

Ami set down a small drum from Ghana. “It goes doom-doom,” she said with a smile.

Ben showed colorful blocks. “We can build a bridge,” he said.

Mei held a paper fan painted with flowers. “It makes cool wind,” she whispered, waving it gently. Whoosh, whoosh.

The living room was full of ideas. It was also full of big feelings. Everyone wanted to go first.

“My drum now!” Ami said, hugging it.
“My blocks now!” Ben said, grabbing the box.
“My fan now!” Mei said, holding it high.

The air felt tight. The music sounded too loud. Tiko felt the jumpy energy. He raised a front foot and breathed in, slow and deep.

“Friends,” Tiko said softly, “let’s try a turtle breath.”

“What’s a turtle breath?” Ben asked.

“It is a slow breath,” Tiko said. “We breathe in like we smell a flower. We breathe out like we blow a bubble. This helps our bodies feel safe. It helps our minds get clear.”

Ami looked at her drum. Mei looked at her fan. Ben looked at his blocks.

“Let’s try,” Mei said.

Tiko nodded. “Smell the flower.” They breathed in through their noses. “Blow the bubble.” They breathed out, slow and steady.

The room felt softer. The sun squares on the rug looked brighter. The soft music hummed like a happy bee.

“Better,” Ami said. “My tummy is not tight now.”

“Mine too,” Ben said. “I can think.”

Tiko smiled. “When we feel calm, we can talk and make a plan.”

They sat in a small circle on the rug. The cinnamon tea smelled sweet. The oranges shone like little suns in a bowl.

“We all want to go first,” Tiko said. “How can we make it fair?”

Ben wiggled his blue laces. “We can take turns.”

Ami patted her drum. “We can count.”

Mei held her fan. “We can mix our games.”

Tiko’s eyes twinkled. “We can do all three. We will take turns with counting, and we will mix our games to share our cultures with respect.”

He drew a plan on the rug with his finger:
- First, Ami plays three doom-dooms.
- Then, Ben places three blocks.
- Then, Mei waves the fan three times.

“And we repeat,” Tiko said. “We listen to each other’s turn. We wait, we watch, we learn.”

“Okay,” said Ami. “Doom-doom, doom.” The drum sounded warm and round. Ben and Mei smiled at the rhythm.

“My turn,” Ben said. He placed a red block, a blue block, and a green block. “We are building a bridge to each other.”

Mei waved her fan: whoosh, whoosh, whoosh. Cool air touched everyone’s cheeks. It felt like a tiny breeze from a garden.

They went around again. Ami tapped a soft doom-doom. Ben added a tall block tower. Mei fanned the tower with a gentle wind.

“Listen,” Tiko whispered. “We made a song, a bridge, and a breeze.”

Ami’s eyes shone. “My drum shares a beat from my family.”

Ben nodded. “My blocks can build any shape. Today they build together.”

Mei smiled. “My fan keeps us cool and calm. I like when we share.”

They paused. Tiko took another turtle breath. In like a flower. Out like a bubble.

“What do we do if we both want the same thing next time?” Ben asked.

“We ask,” said Tiko. “We use kind words. We can say, ‘May I have a turn after you?’ We can wait with a calm body. We can listen for yes or no. We can offer a trade. That is negotiation with respect.”

Ami looked at Mei. “May I fan after you?”

“Yes,” Mei said. “And may I try the drum after you?”

“Yes,” Ami said, handing the drum with care. Their hands touched gently. Their smiles were bright.

Ben held up a block. “Ami, want to place this one on my bridge?”

Ami nodded. “Yes, please.” She placed it slowly. The tower stood tall and proud.

They all giggled. The room felt peaceful and playful. The sun squares moved on the rug. The music hummed like a friendly cat.

Tiko tucked his head in and out, happy. “When we slow down and breathe, we can think. When we listen, we can share. When we respect our different things, we learn more.”

Ami, Ben, and Mei sat quietly for one more turtle breath.
In like a flower. Out like a bubble.

“Thank you, Tiko,” they said.

“You did it,” Tiko replied. “You found calm, you made a plan, and you shared your beautiful ways.”

The living room glowed warm and safe. The bridge stood high. The drum rested. The fan folded like a red butterfly.

They cheered softly, “We did it, together!”

The End.
