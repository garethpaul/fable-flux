# Bella Bear Leads the Band

Bella was a friendly bear cub with soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes. Her ears perked up when she heard music. Today, Bella padded into the musical practice room. The room smelled like shiny wood and lemon polish. Sunlight made warm stripes on the floor. There were drums, bells, a little piano, and a basket of maracas. There were also dust bunnies, scattered sticks, and music sheets on the floor.

Bella blinked. “Oh my,” she said. “Our practice room is messy.”

Her friends came in behind her. Pip the puppy wagged his tail. “Let’s play drums!”

Tilly the turtle peeked out of her shell. “I like the soft bells,” she whispered.

Mina the mouse squeaked, “We can make a song!”

Bella felt excited too. She could almost hear a happy beat in her head. But she looked at the clutter. A drumstick sat in a puddle of juice. The piano keys had crumbs. The maracas were under a chair.

Bella took a deep breath and listened. The room was quiet, like it was waiting. She felt a little flutter in her tummy. “We can’t make music yet,” she said gently. “First, we need to take care of our space. Let’s make it clean and ready.”

Pip tilted his head. “But I want to play now.”

Bella nodded. “Me too. But leaders help everyone do what needs to be done first. I can be the leader. We will do our chores together. Then we can make the best music.”

Tilly blinked slowly. “What do we do?”

Bella looked around. She used her nose to smell the lemon polish on the shelf. She had an idea. “We will make a plan! I will give each friend a small job. We’ll sing while we work.”

Mina squeaked, “A chore song?”

Bella clapped her paws. “Yes! I will lead the song.”

She hummed a soft beat. “Tap-tap, sweep-sweep, pick-pick, wipe-wipe.” The rhythm made Pip’s tail thump like a small drum.

“Pip,” Bella said kindly, “you are fast. Can you pick up the sticks and put them in the stick jar?”

Pip grinned. “I can do that! Pick-pick!” He zipped around, scooping up drumsticks. Click, clack, clink—the sticks sounded happy as they dropped into the jar.

“Tilly,” Bella continued, “you move carefully and see small things. Can you gather the music sheets and stack them neat?”

Tilly smiled. “I can.” She slid from page to page, smoothing each one with her tiny foot. Swish, smooth, stack. The papers made a soft whisper.

“Mina,” said Bella, “you are brave and tiny. Can you crawl under the chair and find the maracas?”

Mina’s whiskers twitched. “Yes!” She crawled under the chair, nose twitching. Rattle-rattle! She rolled two maracas out, green and red.

“And me,” Bella said, “I will wipe the piano keys and sweep the crumbs.” She found a cloth that smelled like lemon. Wipe-wipe, shine-shine. The keys looked bright and clean. She held a small broom and swept the crumbs into a little dustpan. Swish, brush, tap.

They all sang together. “Tap-tap, sweep-sweep, pick-pick, wipe-wipe!” Their voices were light and cheerful. The chores felt like a game.

Pip paused. “I found a broken stick,” he said, holding a splintered drumstick.

Bella thought. Her eyes sparkled. “That’s okay. We can still use it. Let’s make it a shaker.” She wrapped it in paper, poured a few beans inside from the snack jar, and taped it shut. Shake-shake! It made a soft, sandy sound.

“Resourceful!” Tilly said slowly, tasting the new word. “That means we use what we have.”

Bella nodded. “Yes. Leaders find ways to make things work. We can be resourceful and kind.”

Soon the floor was clear, the air smelled fresh, and the instruments were in their places. The room felt happy and bright. Pip looked around and wagged. “Now can we play?”

Bella smiled. “Yes! We did our chores together. We took care of our space. Now our music will shine.”

She lifted her paw. “One, two, three!”

Pip tapped the drum, thump-thump. Tilly rang the bell, ding-ding. Mina shook the maracas, rattle-rattle. Bella pressed the piano keys, plink-plonk. The sounds blended like a rainbow of noises—soft, loud, high, and low. Their song felt warm and cozy, like a hug.

After the song, Bella said, “Great job, team! You listened. You helped. You tried new ideas.”

Mina’s eyes were bright. “You showed us what to do. You asked kindly.”

Pip nodded. “You led us, and we all helped.”

Tilly smiled. “The room is clean, and the music is sweet.”

Bella felt proud, but also grateful. “I led, but we all did it together,” she said. “That is good leadership. Leaders make a plan, use kind words, and help everyone join in. We did our chores, and we used what we had. We were resourceful. Now we can make beautiful music.”

They played one more joyful song. When they finished, they put every instrument back in its spot. Pip closed the stick jar with a click. Tilly stacked the music sheets straight. Mina tucked the maracas into the basket. Bella covered the piano with a soft cloth.

“Chores first, music next,” Bella said with a grin. “That is our team rule.”

Everyone cheered. “Hooray for Bella! Hooray for our team!”

Bella’s heart felt light. She heard the quiet room hum, like it was saying thank you.

The bear cub waved her paw. “Let’s lead with kindness every day,” she said. “We can help, we can plan, and we can make good choices—together.”

The End.
