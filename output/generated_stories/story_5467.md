# Penny Panda's Rooftop Garden

High up on a tall building, there lived a patient panda named Penny. Her fluffy black and white fur sparkled in the morning sunshine as she stood on her favorite rooftop garden. The wind tickled her round ears, and she could smell the fresh air mixed with the sweet scent of flowers.

"Good morning, little seeds!" Penny said cheerfully to the empty flower boxes. "Today, I'm going to plant a beautiful garden for everyone to enjoy!"

Penny had been dreaming of this garden for weeks. She wanted to grow colorful flowers that would make the gray rooftop bright and happy. The other animals in the building could look out their windows and see something wonderful.

She carefully opened her bag of sunflower seeds. They felt smooth and small in her big, gentle paws. "These will grow into tall, sunny flowers," she whispered to herself.

Penny dug little holes in the soft dirt with her claws. Scoop, scoop, scoop! The dirt felt cool and crumbly. She placed one seed in each hole, then covered them with soil like tucking them into bed.

"There!" she said, wiping her paws clean. "Now we wait."

Every day, Penny climbed up to the rooftop. She carried a small watering can that made a pleasant sloshing sound. Splash, splash went the water as she sprinkled it over the soil.

"Drink up, little seeds," she would say softly. "Grow big and strong!"

But day after day, nothing happened. The soil looked the same. No green shoots poked through.

"Maybe I'm not watering them enough," Penny wondered. So she watered them twice a day.

Still nothing.

"Maybe they need more sunshine," she thought. She moved the flower boxes to the sunniest spot on the rooftop.

Still nothing.

After two weeks, Penny felt sad. Her friend Benny Bunny hopped up to visit.

"Why do you look so glum?" Benny asked, his nose twitching.

"My seeds won't grow," Penny sighed. "I've tried everything. Maybe I should give up."

"But Penny," Benny said gently, "remember when I was learning to hop? It took me many tries before I could hop as high as you. Good things take time."

Penny's ears perked up. "You're right! Maybe my seeds just need more time."

The next morning, Penny gasped with joy. Tiny green shoots were poking through the soil! They looked like little green fingers reaching toward the sky.

"You did it!" she cheered, dancing around the flower boxes. "You grew!"

Every day, the shoots grew taller. Penny kept watering them and talking to them. Soon, they had big green leaves that rustled in the breeze.

After many more patient days, beautiful yellow sunflowers bloomed! They were as big as dinner plates, with bright yellow petals that looked like sunshine.

All the animals in the building gathered on the rooftop. "Penny, these are magnificent!" squeaked Mrs. Mouse. "Thank you for not giving up!"

Penny beamed with pride. The flowers swayed gently in the warm breeze, and bees buzzed happily around them.

"I learned something important," Penny told her friends. "When we keep trying and don't give up, wonderful things can happen. It just takes patience and time."

From that day on, Penny's rooftop garden bloomed with many different flowers. And whenever someone felt like giving up, they would visit Penny's garden and remember that good things come to those who keep trying.

The End.
