# Ellie’s Rooftop Rainbow

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and a tiny pink hat. She lived in a tall, cozy house with a flat rooftop. The rooftop felt warm in the sunshine and cool in the shade. Today, Ellie climbed the little stairs, step, step, step, up to the roof. She carried a small basket of paints and a cup of water that went slosh, slosh.

“I will paint a rainbow,” Ellie said. Her voice was soft and happy. The air smelled like fresh soap from the laundry line, and a little breeze tickled her trunk. Far below, she could hear birds chirp. “Tweet-tweet! Paint with me?” Ellie giggled and tapped her paintbrush on the rim. Tap-tap.

Ellie laid out her paints: red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple. The colors looked bright like candy. She dipped her brush in the cup. Swish! She began to paint on a big paper tape-sticked to the rooftop floor. Red made a warm swoosh across the paper. Orange made a sunny curve. Yellow shined like a smile.

As Ellie worked, paint splashed. A dot landed on her cheek. A splash got on her trunk. Even her toes got little speckles. “Oops!” Ellie laughed. “I am a colorful elephant now.” She didn’t mind. The rainbow grew and grew, and she felt proud. Her heart felt warm, and she hummed a little song. “Rainbow, rainbow, bright and new, red and orange, yellow too…”

But after a while, Ellie felt tired. Her tummy made a soft rumble. “Grumble-grumble,” it said. Her trunk drooped. Her eyes blinked slow. “I want to finish,” she told herself, “but I feel sleepy. My hands are sticky. My face is messy.” She sniffed and smelled paint, and her fingers felt icky and dry.

Ellie tried to keep painting. She dipped the brush again. Swish… drip. The brush slipped and made a big splat right on the rainbow. “Oh no!” Ellie’s ears drooped. A blue puddle covered the green. “I think I need help,” she whispered.

Just then, a friendly pigeon landed on the railing. Flap-flap. “Coo?” the pigeon asked, tilting its head. Another friend, a tiny rooftop cat named Miso, stretched in the sun and meowed. “Mrrr? Are you okay, Ellie?”

Ellie sighed. “I am painting a rainbow, but I am sticky and tired, and I made a splat.”

Miso blinked slow. “Self-care time,” the cat purred. “Taking care of you helps your art.”

The pigeon nodded. “Coo-coo! Wash, drink, rest, then paint.”

Ellie touched her cheek. It felt bumpy with dried paint. She looked at her cup. The water was all murky now. “Maybe I do need to stop and take care of me,” she said. “I can still finish later.”

Ellie set down her brush. She took a big, gentle breath in through her trunk. In... and out. The breeze felt cool on her face. “First, I will clean up,” she said.

On the rooftop, there was a little sink for watering plants and washing hands. Ellie turned the handle. The water whispered. “Shhh.” She let the water run over her trunk and hands. It felt warm and nice. She used a tiny bar of soap. Squish, squish. Bubbles grew like soft clouds. “Pop-pop!” She scrubbed her trunk, her cheeks, and each toe. “Scrub-a-dub, scrub-a-dub,” she sang. The paint slipped away. The soap smelled like oranges. She rinsed until the water ran clear. Drip, drip. Then she patted dry with a soft towel. “Ahh,” she said, smiling. “Clean feels good.”

“Coo!” said the pigeon. “Shiny and clean!”

“Now, a drink,” Ellie said. She poured fresh water into her little cup and sipped. Gulp, gulp. The water was cool and sweet. She felt her tummy say, “Thank you.”

Miso purred. “Snack time is good, too.” Ellie nibbled a small banana from her basket. Peel, peel. Mmm—sweet and soft. Her body felt stronger. Her eyes blinked bright again.

Ellie stretched her legs and wiggled her toes. “Stretch up, stretch down,” she said. Her muscles felt happy. She looked at her painting. The blue splat was still there. This time, Ellie smiled. “I can make the splat into a cloud!”

She dipped a clean brush into white paint. Swish-swish. She dabbed around the blue splat, soft and fluffy. The blue became the middle of a puffy cloud. She added little sparkles of yellow like sun dots peeking through. The rainbow curved under the cloud, smooth and bright. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple all smiled at her.

“Beautiful,” Miso purred, wrapping a tail around Ellie’s ankle.

“Coo-coo! Art and care,” the pigeon said.

Ellie washed her brush and her hands again. She put the paints away, clicked the lids tight. Click. Click. She wiped the rooftop floor where drops had fallen. Wipe-wipe. The rooftop smelled fresh again.

Ellie stood tall. The sun felt warm on her back, and the breeze felt like a hug. “I took care of me,” she said. “I cleaned up, I drank water, I had a snack, and I stretched. Now my art is better, and I feel better.”

She looked at her rainbow and whispered, “Thank you, me.” Her heart felt light, and her smile spread wide.

From the railing, the pigeon sang. From the corner, Miso purred. Ellie tapped her pink hat and laughed. “Self-care helps me do my best,” she said. “When I wash, rest, and eat, I can make beautiful things.”

She picked up her picture and held it to the sky. The colors glowed. Ellie felt proud, calm, and clean. It was a happy rooftop day.

The End.
