# Polly Paints the Road

Polly was a small, creative parrot with bright green feathers and a curious yellow beak. She loved to make things, sing little songs, and try new ideas. Today, Polly sat in her cozy perch in the family car, right between Mia and Max in their car seats. The car hummed, “Vmmm, vmmm,” as it rolled down the road. The sun warmed the windows, and the air smelled like oranges from a snack.

“Where are we going?” Polly chirped, tilting her head.

“To Grandma’s!” said Mia.

“With a surprise!” said Max.

Polly fluffed her feathers. “I love surprises!” she said. She peered out the window. Trees swooshed by. The sky was bright and blue.

But soon, the car slowed down. It went, “Vmmm…mmmm…stop.” A long line of cars stretched ahead. The family car didn’t move.

“Oh no,” said Mia. “We might be late.”

Max sighed. “I’m bored.”

Polly looked around. She heard little honks, soft and far away. She saw people in other cars tapping the steering wheel. She felt the seat shake slightly. A tiny worry fluttered in her tummy.

Polly said, “I can help! I’m a creative parrot. I can make a plan.” But then she stopped. Help with a traffic jam? Could she do that?

Polly’s feathers felt prickly. “What if my plan is silly?” she whispered.

Mama turned and smiled. “It’s okay to try, Polly. We learn by trying.”

Papa nodded. “That’s a growth mindset. We keep going, even when it’s hard.”

Polly breathed in. She smelled the orange again. Sweet and bright. “Okay,” she said. “I will try.”

First, Polly tried singing. She sang a cheerful song, “Chirp-chirp-cheep, don’t you weep, we will move, beep beep!” Her voice was clear and happy. Mia clapped. Max giggled. But the car still didn’t move.

Polly puffed up. “That didn’t fix it. But I can try something else.”

She looked down. In the pocket on the door, there were crayons and paper. Polly’s eyes sparkled. “A new idea,” she chirped. “I will draw a plan!”

She took a purple crayon with her beak and drew big arrows. She drew smiling cars taking turns. She drew a big, friendly sign: “Please take turns. We can do it!”

Polly held up the paper. “Mama, can we show this to other cars?”

Mama thought. “We can hold it up to the window,” she said. “We can be kind and clear.”

Polly pressed the paper to the glass. The sun made the purple bright. A driver in the next car looked. She smiled and gave a thumbs-up. Another car rolled down a window. “Good idea!” said a man.

Polly felt warm and brave. “It’s working!” she chirped.

Still, the line of cars moved just a tiny bit. Polly’s wings drooped. “It’s not done yet,” she said softly.

Papa said gently, “Sometimes solutions take time. Keep going, little helper.”

Polly nodded. “I will persist,” she said, trying the big word slowly. “Per-sist.”

She grabbed a green crayon. She drew a second sign: “One car goes, then the next. Like a dance!” She drew happy feet going step-step.

Mia laughed. “A car dance!”

Max wiggled. “Step-step! Beep-beep!”

Polly held up the new sign. Mama turned on the hazard lights—blink, blink—to get attention. People looked. Polly waved a bright feather. “We can do it! One by one!” she called.

Slowly, like a gentle line of ducks, the cars began to move, one, then the next. The family car rolled forward. “Vmmm,” it hummed again.

Polly peeked out. She saw the reason for the stop: orange cones and a worker fixing a small hole in the road. The worker looked tired but focused. Polly felt curious.

“What is that?” Polly asked.

“It’s road work,” Papa said. “They fix the road to keep us safe.”

Polly nodded. “They are helping, too.”

As they passed, Polly chirped, “Thank you!” The worker smiled and waved back.

Soon, the car moved smoothly. The wind whispered, “Whoosh,” through the tiny crack in the window. The sky seemed even bluer. Mia and Max cheered.

“You did it, Polly!” Mia said.

“You were brave,” said Max.

Polly felt proud and calm. “We all did it,” she said. “We used our minds. We tried, and then tried again. We learned.”

When they reached Grandma’s house, the door opened. Warm bread smell floated out. Grandma hugged everyone tight. “You’re here!” she sang. “What happened on the road?”

Polly stood tall on her perch. “There was a big stop,” she said. “I was worried. My first try didn’t work. But I kept trying. I made signs. I asked for help. We took turns. We moved!”

Grandma smiled. “That is a growth mindset,” she said. “You were curious, you were brave, and you did not give up.”

Polly’s eyes shone. “I learned that small steps help. One, then the next. Like a dance!”

Grandma nodded. “And when something is hard, we breathe, we think, we try, and we try again.”

Polly puffed her feathers in a happy way. “I can learn anything if I keep trying,” she said. “And I can help others, too.”

Everyone went inside for warm bread and orange slices. Polly tasted the soft bread—mmm, cozy and sweet. She felt safe, clever, and kind.

Polly whispered to herself, “When I don’t know what to do, I will stay curious, be brave, and keep trying.”

The End.
