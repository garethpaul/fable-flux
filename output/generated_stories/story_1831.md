# Momo’s Pantry Plan

Momo was a small, courageous monkey with bright eyes and quick hands. One sunny morning, he peeked into the big pantry at the edge of the village. The door creaked. Inside, it was cool and a little dark. The shelves smelled like ripe bananas, crunchy nuts, and sweet spices. Momo sniffed. “Mmm,” he said. “So many good foods!”

But the pantry was messy. Some jars were open. A bag of rice had spilled. A banana peel lay on the floor.

Momo scratched his head. “Oh no,” he whispered. “This is not safe. This is not clean. I want to help.”

Momo felt brave. He loved helping. He took a deep breath and made a plan. He held up his finger and said, “Goal number one: Clean the pantry. Goal number two: Save the food. Goal number three: Tell the truth.”

He drew his goals on a little chalkboard he found by the door. He drew a broom, a basket, and a smiley face. “I can do it,” he said. “Step by step!”

Just then, his friend Tiko the turtle peeked in. “Hello, Momo,” Tiko said slowly. “What are you doing?”

“I have a plan,” said Momo. “We will clean. We will sort. We will be honest. Will you help?”

Tiko nodded. “Yes. I like plans.”

They started with goal number one: Clean the pantry.

Momo picked up the banana peel with two fingers. It felt slippery. “This goes in the compost,” he said. “Compost helps the earth. It makes the soil happy.” He put the peel in a green bin.

Tiko used his steady shell to push the broom. Swish, swish, swish. The rice made a tiny tapping sound as it slid into a dustpan. “This rice is not clean,” Tiko said. “We cannot eat it.”

Momo agreed. “We will not waste good food. But we will not eat food from the floor. The compost will turn it into new life.” He smiled. “That is caring for our planet.”

The pantry air smelled fresher. The floor looked bright. “We did it,” said Momo. “Goal one is done.” He drew a big check mark on the chalkboard.

Next was goal number two: Save the food.

Momo looked at the open jars. “Some nuts fell out,” he said. “Let’s sort the good from the bad.” He tasted one carefully. Crunch! “Fresh,” he said. He put the good nuts in a clean jar and closed the lid tight. Click!

Tiko read the labels slowly. “Seeds… beans… tea.” He used his careful turtle hands to close each lid. “Tight lids keep food safe,” he said. “And tight lids stop bugs.”

Momo found a torn paper bag. “We can use a cloth bag instead,” he said. He tied it with a small string. The string felt rough. “This is better for the earth. Less trash.” He felt proud.

They lined up the jars and bags: tall ones, short ones, red, green, and blue. The colors looked cheerful. “Everything has a place,” said Tiko.

“Goal two is done!” Momo said. He drew another check mark.

Then came goal number three: Tell the truth.

Momo took a deep breath. He looked at a banana sticker on the floor. He remembered. Yesterday, he had hurried in and grabbed a banana. He had dropped the peel and left in a rush. He had made part of the mess.

Momo’s cheeks felt warm. His tummy fluttered. “Tiko,” he said softly, “I have to be honest. I dropped a peel yesterday. I forgot to clean it. I am sorry.”

Tiko blinked kindly. “Thank you for telling the truth, Momo. That is brave.”

Momo nodded. “Honesty is part of my plan.”

Just then, Miss Bee, the pantry keeper, buzzed in. “Bzzz! What do I see?” She looked around. “The pantry is clean and neat! Who did this?”

Momo stood tall. “We did, Miss Bee. And I need to tell you something. I left a peel yesterday. I made a mess. Today I cleaned it with Tiko. I am sorry.”

Miss Bee smiled. “Thank you for helping. Thank you for your honesty. That is very responsible.” She patted Momo’s head. “You set goals. You worked step by step. You cared for the food. You cared for the earth. You told the truth. That is wonderful.”

Momo felt warm and happy. His heart thumped like a soft drum. “It was our plan,” he said. “And we did it together.”

Tiko nodded. “Cooperation is strong.”

Miss Bee buzzed toward the door. “Let’s make a list to keep the pantry clean,” she said. “A little plan every day.”

Momo picked up the chalk. “Plan time!” he cheered. He wrote:
- Sweep after snacks.
- Close lids tight.
- Use cloth bags.
- Compost peels.
- Tell the truth.

They all read the list out loud. Their voices sounded bright and clear. The pantry smelled clean and sweet. The shelves looked neat and safe.

Momo grinned. “Goals help me do good things,” he said. “When I make a plan and follow the steps, I can help my friends. I can help the earth. I can be honest and brave.”

Miss Bee nodded. “Small goals, big good.”

Tiko smiled slowly. “Step by step.”

Momo wrapped his tail around the broom and laughed. “We did it! New goal: Snack time.”

They shared a banana, a few nuts, and a cup of warm tea. Sip. Crunch. Yum.

The pantry felt like a happy place.

The End.
