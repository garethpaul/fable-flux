# Koala Kori’s Calm Clean-Up

Morning light made the comfortable nursery glow soft and warm. The rug felt fluffy, like a cloud. A gentle smell of soap and crayons filled the air.

Kori the koala blinked her sleepy eyes and stretched her soft gray arms. “Good morning,” she whispered. Kori was a calm koala. She liked slow breaths and gentle smiles.

Her friends were already busy. Benny the bunny hopped and scattered blocks. Sumi the squirrel zipped around with tiny steps. Tiko the turtle moved slowly, humming.

Teacher Mia clapped softly. “Friends, it’s clean-up time,” she said. “We will get the nursery tidy together. Calm bodies, calm breaths.”

Benny bounced. “I can do it fast!” he giggled, bumping a tower of blocks. Crash! Blocks rolled under the table.

Sumi chattered, “I’ll grab everything!” She reached for crayons, books, and a ball at once. The crayons slipped and clattered to the floor.

Tiko pulled a basket. “I can help,” he said, but the basket was too full and heavy for him alone. It dragged with a squeak.

Kori watched with kind eyes. Her ears felt warm, and she put her paws on her belly. In and out, she breathed. In… and out. Calm and slow.

Teacher Mia smiled at Kori. “Show us your calm way, Kori.”

Kori nodded. “We can do it together,” she said in a quiet voice. “One thing at a time. Breathe in… breathe out.”

Benny hopped closer. “But I want to go super fast!”

Kori patted the rug. “Let’s try slow-fast,” she said. “We breathe slow, and we move steady. Like a gentle train. Choo… choo.”

Benny giggled. “Choo… choo.” He breathed in and out with Kori. His shoulders softened.

Sumi swished her tail. “I can’t hold everything,” she sighed.

Kori pointed to the baskets. “We can make teams,” she said. “Blocks in the block basket. Crayons in the crayon tin. Books on the shelf. Ball in the ball bin.”

Tiko nodded. “I like teams.”

Kori made a plan. “Benny, you and I will roll the blocks to the basket. Sumi, you pick up crayons, one color at a time. Tiko, you guide the basket with your slow, strong push.”

They all put their paws and hands in the center. “Team Calm!” they cheered, but in a soft, inside voice.

Kori showed them. She sat tall, took a breath, and rolled one red block across the rug. The block made a soft tap when it reached the basket.

Tap. Tap. Tap.

Benny copied her, rolling a blue block. “Tap!” he said, smiling.

Sumi picked up one yellow crayon. “Yellow,” she whispered. Plink. It dropped into the tin. “Red.” Plink. “Green.” Plink. The little sounds were like raindrops.

Tiko pushed the basket slow and steady. Squeak… roll… squeak… roll. He hummed a low tune. It made everyone feel cozy.

Kori’s voice was gentle. “We are calm. We are friends. We can do it together.”

Benny saw a block under the table. “Oops,” he said. He felt another rush to zoom, but then he put his paw on his belly and breathed. In… out. He got down on his knees and rolled the block out. Tap.

Sumi reached for too many crayons again. They wobbled in her paws. She paused and listened to Tiko’s hum. “One at a time,” she told herself. Plink. Plink. Plink.

Kori noticed the heavy basket near Tiko. “Need help?” she asked.

“Yes, please,” Tiko said.

Kori and Tiko held the basket together. “One, two, three,” Kori said. They lifted with careful hands. The basket felt smooth and cool against their paws. They set it by the shelf.

Benny stacked three books on the shelf. “Look! They stand tall,” he said.

“Let’s check our list,” Kori said. She pointed with her paw. “Blocks? Done. Crayons? Done. Books? Done. Ball?”

Sumi looked around. “Where is the ball?”

Kori listened. The room was quiet. She heard a soft thump under a blanket. “I hear it,” she said. She lifted the blanket. The ball was shiny and blue.

“Found it!” Sumi sang. She walked the ball with tiny pushes. Bump, bump. It rolled into the bin. Plop.

Teacher Mia came back with a happy smile. “The nursery looks wonderful,” she said. “How did you do it?”

Kori’s cheeks felt warm and proud. “We used calm breaths,” she said. “We worked as a team. One thing at a time.”

Benny hopped once, then stood still. “Calm feels nice,” he said.

Sumi wiggled her tail. “Calm helps me hold things,” she added.

Tiko nodded slowly. “Calm helps me keep going.”

Teacher Mia clapped softly again. “You showed calmness, friendship, and good coordination,” she said. “You did your chores with care.”

Kori looked around at her friends. The rug was neat. The shelves were tidy. The air smelled like clean wood and crayons again. The room felt quiet and safe, like a hug.

Kori took one more breath and smiled. “Calm makes hard jobs feel easy,” she said.

Everyone smiled back. They sat together on the fluffy rug, shoulders touching, hearts steady. Teacher Mia read a cozy story while Tiko hummed, Sumi traced the edge of the rug, and Benny stroked a soft pillow.

Kori closed her eyes for a moment. In… out. Calm and happy with friends.

The End.
