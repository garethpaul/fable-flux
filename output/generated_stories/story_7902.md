# Melody's Important Letter

In a cozy living room with soft cushions and colorful toys, lived a little yellow bird named Melody. She had bright orange feet and a beautiful singing voice that filled the room with joy.

Melody loved to perch on the big blue sofa. She would sing happy songs while watching the sunlight dance through the windows. The warm rays felt wonderful on her feathers.

One morning, Melody noticed something troubling. The bird feeder outside the window was completely empty! Her friend Robin was pecking at the ground, looking very hungry.

"Oh no!" chirped Melody. "Robin has no seeds to eat!"

Melody knew she had to help. She remembered what Mama Bird had taught her: "When you see a problem in your community, it's your responsibility to help fix it."

But how could a little bird help? Melody thought and thought. Then she had an idea!

"I'll write a letter to the nice family who lives here," she decided. "They always fill the bird feeder when they know it's empty."

Melody had never written a letter before. She felt nervous. Her tiny wings weren't very good at holding pencils.

"I need to practice first," she said to herself.

Melody found a crayon that had rolled under the coffee table. She practiced making marks on a piece of paper. At first, her lines were wobbly and crooked.

"This is hard work," she puffed. But Melody didn't give up. She kept trying.

After lots of practice, Melody's lines got straighter. Her circles got rounder. She was getting better!

Now she was ready to write her important letter. Melody thought carefully about what to say. She wanted to be polite and clear.

She drew a picture of the empty bird feeder. Then she drew Robin looking sad and hungry. Finally, she drew herself with a big question mark above her head.

"Dear Kind Family," she wrote in her best bird handwriting. "The bird feeder is empty. Robin is hungry. Can you please help? Thank you! Love, Melody the Bird."

Melody felt proud of her letter. She had prepared well and practiced hard. Now she needed to deliver it!

She carefully carried the letter in her beak to the kitchen table where the family would see it. The paper felt smooth against her beak, and she was very careful not to drop it.

Soon, the little girl who lived in the house found the letter. Her eyes grew wide with surprise and delight.

"Mom! Dad! Look! The bird wrote us a letter!" she called excitedly.

The family read Melody's letter together. They smiled at her drawings and kind words.

"What a thoughtful bird," said the mother. "She saw a problem and found a way to help her friend."

Right away, the family went outside and filled the bird feeder with fresh, crunchy seeds. Robin chirped with joy and began eating happily.

Melody watched from the window, her heart full of happiness. She had seen a problem in her community and taken action to solve it. Even though she was small, she had made a big difference.

"Thank you, Melody!" sang Robin from outside. "You're a wonderful friend and a responsible member of our bird community!"

Melody puffed out her chest with pride. She had learned that being a good citizen means caring about others and taking action when help is needed. Practice and preparation had helped her succeed.

From that day on, Melody always kept watch over the bird feeder. Whenever it was empty, she would write another letter. The family loved getting mail from their special feathered friend.

The End.
