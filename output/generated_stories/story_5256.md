# Fawn's Quiet Plan

In a cozy bedroom, the moon made a soft silver square on the rug. A little lamp glowed warm and yellow. The curtains were blue and swishy. The air smelled like clean blankets and a hint of pine from a tiny wooden toy tree.

A gentle deer fawn named Fawn lay on a soft quilt with leaf patterns. Her big ears twitched. Her hooves were tucked under the covers, but her eyes were wide. “I can’t sleep,” Fawn whispered. “I want to play now.”

On the shelf, a small clock tick-tick-ticked like soft taps. “Tick… tock… tick… tock…” It sounded like a calm heartbeat. Fawn’s tail flicked. “I don’t want to wait,” she said.

Mama’s voice floated from the hallway. “Sweet Fawn, what do we do when we can’t sleep?”

Fawn thought. She wanted to jump and run. But Mama’s words were gentle in her mind. “We use patience,” Fawn said to herself. “Patience means wait and be calm.”

Fawn looked around the cozy bedroom. The rug was fluffy under her hoof. The lamp hummed softly. The curtains whispered shhhh when the night breeze came. Fawn took a slow breath. In…and out.

“Maybe I can be the leader of my bedtime,” Fawn said. “I can make a plan.”

She sat up and patted her quilt. “Step one,” Fawn said, holding up a tiny hoof. “Quiet body.” She wiggled her shoulders and then made them still. “Quiet,” she whispered, smiling.

“Step two,” she said. “Quiet mind.” She looked at her toy basket. There were blocks, a drum, and a little book. The drum felt too loud. The blocks felt too busy. The little book had a moon on the cover. “The moon book is calm,” Fawn decided. “That is a good choice.”

She opened the book slowly. The pages made a soft swish. “Hello, Moon,” Fawn read in a tiny voice. The moon in the picture glowed white and round. “The moon waits all night,” she said. “The moon is patient too.” Fawn felt proud. She was being a leader and making good choices.

But then—tap, tap, tap! Her hooves wanted to tap. “Oh no,” Fawn said. “I still want to play.”

She put her hoof on her chest. “Thump-thump,” went her heart. “It is okay,” she told herself. “I can wait. I can think.” She closed the book and looked at the clock. Tick… tock… tick… tock…

“Step three,” Fawn said softly. “A calm game.” She looked at her shelf again. There was a jar with smooth, shiny buttons. The buttons were blue, green, and gold. They made a tiny clink when she moved the jar. “I can sort them,” she said. “I will sort them by color. That is smart thinking.”

Fawn poured the buttons onto a small tray. They felt cool and smooth under her hoof. “Blue here,” she said, sliding a blue button into a circle she drew with her hoof. “Green here. Gold here.” She moved slowly. She took a breath each time. In… slide. Out… slide. The buttons made a faint chink-chink that sounded like rain on leaves.

Her eyelids felt heavy. Her body felt warm and soft like the quilt. “Step four,” Fawn whispered. “Rest eyes.” She put the buttons back in the jar. Clink, clink. She set the jar on the shelf with a gentle pat.

Mama peeked in. The door made a tiny creak. “How is my patient fawn?” Mama asked, smiling.

“I made a plan,” Fawn said. Her voice was sleepy and proud. “I was the leader. I used quiet body, quiet mind, calm game, and rest eyes.”

Mama sat on the bed and stroked Fawn’s soft ear. “That is wonderful leadership,” Mama said. “You used patience and smart, calm steps. How do you feel?”

“Sleepy,” Fawn whispered. “And happy.”

Mama tucked the quilt under Fawn’s chin. “Would you like a lullaby?”

Fawn thought. She took a small breath. “Yes, please,” she said. “But slow and soft.”

Mama sang a gentle song. It sounded like wind in tall grass. It smelled like warm blankets and pine. The lamp hummed. The clock ticked. The curtains whispered shhhh.

Fawn’s eyes blinked slow. She felt proud of her plan. She had a problem—too much wiggle—and she found a solution with her patient steps. She had led herself to calm. She had used her thinking to choose quiet things.

“Thank you, Mama,” Fawn murmured. “I can wait. I can lead. I can think.”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Patience gives you time to choose. Leadership helps you guide yourself. Thinking helps you find the next right step.”

Fawn smiled a tiny smile. The moon’s silver square slid across the rug like a sleepy snail. Tick… tock… tick… tock… The sounds were safe and steady.

Her body sank into the soft bed. Her hooves were warm. Her ears were still. Her heart went thump… thump… slow and calm.

Before the song was done, Fawn whispered, “I did it.” Then she drifted into sweet sleep.

The lesson is clear: when we feel wiggly or rushed, we can be patient, lead ourselves with a simple plan, and use calm thinking to find the best next step.

The End.
