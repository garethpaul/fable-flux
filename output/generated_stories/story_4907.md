# Buzzy Bubbles and the Bath-Time Plan

In a cozy den, a friendly bear cub named Buzzy loved bath time. The bathroom was warm and steamy. The mirror was foggy. The lemon soap smelled bright, like sunshine. Buzzy liked to splash, to hum, and to make tall bubble hats.

One afternoon, Buzzy’s best friend, a little mouse named Pip, came to play. Pip squeaked, “Buzzy, can we build a block tower after your bath?”

Buzzy looked at the clock on the wall. The big hand was on the 12. The little hand was on the 4. Mama Bear had said, “Bath first. Ten minutes, then play.”

Buzzy nodded. “Yes, Pip! Ten minutes. Then tower time!”

Pip smiled and sat on a soft towel. “I’ll watch,” Pip said. “I’ll cheer.”

Buzzy climbed into the tub. Warm water hugged Buzzy’s paws. Bubbles popped like tiny drums. “Pop, pop, pop!” Buzzy giggled. “Listen, Pip!”

“I hear it!” Pip squeaked, clapping. “Pop-pop party!”

Buzzy began to play. “I’m a boat,” Buzzy sang, “floating on a bubble sea.” A rubber duck squeaked. “Squeak!” The smell of soap swirled in the steam. The lights made the bubbles shine like little moons.

The clock ticked. “Tick-tock, tick-tock.”

Pip peeked up. “Buzzy, I think five minutes are gone,” Pip said kindly.

Buzzy paused. “Five already?” Buzzy felt a tiny pinch in the tummy. It was the feeling of “uh-oh.” Buzzy wanted to keep playing. The bubble hat grew tall, tall, tall!

“Just one more minute,” Buzzy said. “One more bubble hat.”

Pip nodded. “Okay. One more hat.”

The clock ticked again. “Tick-tock, tick-tock.” The mirror fogged more. The air smelled like minty toothpaste now. Buzzy made a bubble beard and laughed, “Ho-ho-ho!” But then Buzzy saw the clock. The big hand moved.

Pip’s ears drooped a little. “Buzzy,” Pip whispered, “I feel a little sad. I want to build the tower with you.”

Buzzy’s paws felt still. The “uh-oh” feeling grew. Buzzy took a deep breath. “Pip, I hear your feelings,” Buzzy said softly. “You feel sad because the time is going.”

Pip nodded. “Yes.”

Buzzy looked at the clock, then at Pip, then at the bubbles. Buzzy wanted fun in the tub, and fun with Pip. What could they do?

Buzzy tried to think with a calm mind. “Hmm,” Buzzy hummed. “Let’s use a plan.” Buzzy remembered Mama’s words: “Name the time. Make it clear. Keep your word, my dear.”

Buzzy said, “Pip, I want to be a good friend. I said ten minutes. I want to be honest and keep my word.” Buzzy pointed to the clock. “Let’s make a bubble timer.”

Pip blinked. “A bubble timer?”

Buzzy dipped a cup in the suds. “I will fill this cup three times. Three cups, then I’m done. One cup is like one minute.” Buzzy smiled. “You can count with me!”

Pip’s ears perked up. “I like that plan.”

“Cup one,” Buzzy said, scooping. The bubbles went “shhh.” Pip counted, “One!” Buzzy rinsed, then scooped again.

“Cup two,” Buzzy said. “Two!” Pip chimed.

“Cup three,” Buzzy said. “Three!” they shouted together.

Buzzy felt proud. “Time to finish,” Buzzy said. Buzzy washed quickly—arms, legs, belly, ears—swish, swish! The lemon soap smelled clean and bright. The water felt silky and warm. Buzzy pulled the plug. The bath made a gurgle song, “glug-glug-glug,” as the water swirled away.

Buzzy wrapped up in a soft blue towel. It felt like a hug. “Thank you for waiting, Pip,” Buzzy said. “I kept my word. I used a plan. I want to be a good friend.”

Pip smiled big. “You were honest, and you listened to my feelings. That makes me feel safe.”

Buzzy nodded. “I felt the ‘uh-oh’ feeling when I wanted to play longer. I took a deep breath and used thinking. That helped.”

They moved to the mat on the floor. The bathroom smelled fresh and warm. “Now,” Buzzy said, “tower time!”

Together they stacked blocks—red, blue, yellow. “Tap-tap,” went each block. The tower grew tall, tall, tall, like Buzzy’s bubble hat had been. Pip giggled. “Our tower is a bubble tower now!”

Buzzy laughed. “Friendship tower!”

Mama Bear peeked in. “I see clean fur and kind hearts,” she said. “What did you learn today?”

Buzzy stood tall. “I learned to be a good friend,” Buzzy said. “I listened to Pip’s feelings. I told the truth about time. I made a plan and kept it.”

Pip added, “And we had fun!”

Mama Bear smiled. “That is the best kind of day.”

Buzzy and Pip high-fived. “Slap!” They looked at the clock. There was still time to play. They took turns. They counted. They helped when blocks fell with a soft “plop.” When the tower tumbled, they both said, “It’s okay. We can try again.”

Buzzy felt warm inside, like the soft towel hug. Friendship felt like that—warm, safe, and happy. Buzzy whispered, “Friends listen. Friends tell the truth. Friends make plans and keep them.”

Pip squeezed Buzzy’s paw. “Friends think together.”

They smiled and built again.

The End.
