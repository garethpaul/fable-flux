# Ruby Rabbit's Rooftop Garden

Ruby Rabbit lived in a tall apartment building with her family. Every morning, she would hop up to the rooftop and look out at the busy city below. The rooftop was empty and gray, with nothing but concrete and a few old boxes.

"I wish we had a beautiful garden up here," Ruby said to herself, twitching her pink nose. "But this job looks too big for just one little rabbit."

Ruby had a wonderful idea. She wanted to turn the empty rooftop into a vegetable garden where all the animals in the building could grow fresh carrots, lettuce, and herbs. But when she looked at all the work that needed to be done, her ears drooped down.

"I need to move these heavy boxes, clean all this dirt, and plant so many seeds," she sighed. "My little paws can't do all of this alone."

Just then, her friend Max Mouse poked his head up from the stairwell. "Hi Ruby! What are you doing up here?"

"I want to make a garden," Ruby explained, "but there's so much work to do. I don't think I can do it by myself."

Max's whiskers twitched with excitement. "That sounds amazing! Can I help you?"

"Really?" Ruby's ears perked up. "But it's going to be a lot of exercise and hard work."

"I love moving around and staying active!" Max squeaked. "Let's get started!"

Soon, other friends heard about the garden project. Bella Bear came up with her strong arms to help move the heavy boxes. "Teamwork makes everything easier!" she said with a big smile.

Oscar Owl flew up with his sharp eyes to help plan where each plant should go. "I can see the best spots for sunshine," he hooted wisely.

Penny Pig brought soap and water to clean their paws and tools. "We need to keep everything clean and tidy for our healthy garden," she oinked happily.

All the friends worked together, taking turns and sharing the tasks. Ruby felt her heart fill with joy as she watched everyone cooperate.

"Let's stretch our arms up high like tall sunflowers!" Ruby called out, and everyone reached toward the sky.

"Now let's hop like bunnies to get our seeds!" Max giggled, and they all bounced around the rooftop.

They dug holes with their paws, planted tiny seeds, and watered them gently. The friends took breaks to wash their dirty paws and drink cool water.

"Working together is so much more fun than working alone," Ruby said, wiping her clean paws on a towel.

"And we're getting lots of good exercise too!" added Bella, stretching her tired but happy muscles.

As the sun began to set, the friends looked at their beautiful new garden. Neat rows of planted seeds covered the once-empty rooftop, and everything was clean and organized.

"We did it!" Ruby cheered, doing a little happy dance. "When we all worked together, the big job became easy and fun!"

"And tomorrow we can come back and take care of our garden together," said Oscar.

From that day on, the rooftop garden became the favorite place for all the animal friends. They learned that when friends cooperate, share the work, and help each other, they can accomplish anything. And Ruby never felt lonely on the rooftop again.

The End.
