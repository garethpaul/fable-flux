# Leo's Garden Fix

Leo was a brave little lion cub who lived in a cozy apartment with his mama. Every morning, he loved to visit their beautiful balcony garden. The garden had bright red tomatoes, yellow sunflowers, and green herbs that smelled like pizza!

One sunny morning, Leo was playing with his favorite bouncy ball. "Bounce, bounce, bounce!" went the ball as Leo giggled and chased it around the garden.

"Be careful, Leo!" called Mama Lion from inside. "Remember to watch where you play!"

But Leo was having so much fun that he forgot to listen. He threw his ball extra high and... CRASH! The ball knocked over Mrs. Rabbit's special flower pot that sat on the next balcony. The pretty purple flowers scattered everywhere, and the pot broke into pieces.

"Oh no!" gasped Leo, his little heart beating fast. He could see Mrs. Rabbit through her window, looking very sad at her broken flowers.

Leo's tummy felt funny and twisted. He had made a big mistake. He wanted to hide, but he remembered what Mama always said: "Brave lions face their problems and make things right."

Leo took a deep breath that smelled like his mama's lavender plants. Then he walked slowly to Mrs. Rabbit's door and knocked softly. "Knock, knock, knock."

Mrs. Rabbit opened the door. Her eyes looked watery and sad. "Hello, Leo," she said quietly.

"Mrs. Rabbit," Leo said in his smallest voice, "I'm really, really sorry. My ball broke your beautiful flower pot. I wasn't being careful, and now your flowers are all messed up."

Mrs. Rabbit's ears drooped. "Those were my grandmother's special flowers, Leo. I've been taking care of them for many years."

Leo felt even worse. A tear rolled down his furry cheek. "I want to make it better. Can I help fix it? Please?"

Mrs. Rabbit looked at Leo's sincere face and kind eyes. "Well," she said thoughtfully, "making amends means more than just saying sorry. It means helping to fix what went wrong."

"I'll do anything!" Leo said, his little tail swishing with determination.

Together, they carefully picked up each purple flower. Leo's paws were gentle as he helped replant them in a new pot that Mrs. Rabbit found. They watered the soil, and Leo even shared some plant food from his mama's garden.

"You know, Leo," said Mrs. Rabbit as they worked, "when we live close together like neighbors, we need to take care of each other and our shared spaces. That's called being responsible."

Leo nodded seriously. "I understand. From now on, I'll play more carefully and think about how my actions affect others."

When Mama Lion came to check on Leo, she found him and Mrs. Rabbit laughing together as they arranged the replanted flowers.

"Leo made a mistake," Mrs. Rabbit told Mama Lion, "but he was brave enough to apologize and help make it right. That shows real character."

Leo beamed with pride. His tummy didn't feel twisted anymore. Instead, it felt warm and happy.

From that day on, Leo always played carefully in the garden. And whenever he saw Mrs. Rabbit watering her flowers, he would wave and ask if she needed any help. Sometimes the best friendships grow from learning to make things right.

The End.
