# Pico Parrot’s Living Room Surprise

Pico was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a tiny blue hat. He lived with Mia, a kind girl, in a playful living room. The room was full of colors and cozy sounds: soft pillows, a bouncy rug, and a window that let in warm, golden light. Every morning, Pico chirped, “New day! New ideas! Squawk!”

One sunny day, Pico saw a shiny basket on the table. Inside were smooth apples, crunchy carrots, and little grapes that looked like purple marbles. “What’s this?” Pico wondered, tilting his head. He loved to discover new things.

Mia walked in with a smile. “Good morning, Pico! Today we try healthy snacks,” she said. “Apples, carrots, and grapes. Yum!”

Pico flapped his wings. “Healthy snacks? Let’s make art! Let’s make music! Let’s discover!”

Mia laughed. “We can discover together. First, we wash our hands. Then we wash the fruit.”

Pico hopped to the sink. The water made a happy hiss. “Shhhhhh,” he mimicked, giggling. Mia gently helped Pico scrub with bubbly soap. The bubbles smelled like lemons. “Clean hands! Clean fruit!” Pico cheered.

They set the food on a bright green plate. Pico tapped the carrots with his beak. “Tock tock! Drum!” He tapped the apples. “Thunk thunk! Bass!” The grapes went “plip plip” as they rolled. The living room felt like a music show.

“I have an idea,” Pico chirped. “Let’s build a fruit tower!” He stacked apple slices and carrot sticks. He set grapes on top like tiny stars. The tower wobbled. “Careful,” Mia said softly. “Slow and steady.”

Pico puffed up with excitement. “I can do it! I discover balance!” He reached with his wing to add one more grape. The tower trembled… and then—plop, plip, plap! The fruit slid onto the rug.

Pico froze. The grapes made purple dots. The carrots made orange lines. The apples left a sticky spot. The room smelled sweet, but the rug felt wet. Mia’s eyes went wide.

Pico’s tummy felt twisty. “Oh no,” he whispered. He looked at Mia. “I’m sorry,” he said in a small voice. “I got too excited. I didn’t ask. I didn’t go slow.”

Mia knelt down and gave Pico a gentle pat. “Thank you for saying sorry, Pico. That was brave. We can fix this together.”

Pico’s feathers relaxed. “Fix together,” he echoed. “Discover how to clean.”

Mia brought a cloth and a little bowl of soapy water. “First, we take a deep breath,” she said. “In… and out.” They breathed together. In and out. Pico felt calmer.

“Now we clean,” Mia said. She showed Pico how to dab, not rub. “Dab, dab,” she said. “Dab, dab,” Pico sang, pressing the cloth with his wing. The rug grew dry and bright again. They put the fruit back on the clean plate. Pico washed his beak with a tiny cloth. “Fresh and clean!” he chirped.

Mia smiled. “We discovered something new today.”

Pico nodded. “We discovered that I need to ask first. We discovered slow and steady. We discovered cleaning skills!” He stood tall. “And we discovered healthy snacks!”

Mia laughed. “Yes! Let’s make a new plan. We can still create. We’ll use a cutting board on the table. And we’ll sit.”

Pico hopped onto a little stool. The table felt smooth and safe. “Plan is good,” he said.

Together they built a happy snack picture. Apple slices became a smiling mouth. Carrot sticks became sunny rays. Grapes became twinkly eyes. The living room filled with the crunchy sound of bites and the sweet smell of fruit.

“Mmm,” said Mia. “The apple is juicy.”

“Crunch crunch,” said Pico. “Carrots are strong sticks for my beak!”

They sipped cool water from cups. The cups clinked lightly. “Water helps our bodies,” Mia said. Pico nodded. “Water for wings, water for songs!”

After snack time, Pico looked at the clean rug and the tidy table. He felt proud. “I’m glad I said sorry,” he said. “I’m glad we made a plan.”

Mia hugged him carefully. “I’m proud of you, Pico. You used kind words, a calm breath, and careful hands. That’s healthy, too.”

Pico fluffed his feathers and spread his wings like a tiny hero. “Today’s discoveries,” he announced, “are: ask before using, go slow and steady, wash hands, clean up spills, drink water, eat fruits and veggies, and say sorry when I make a mess.”

Mia clapped. “Wonderful list!”

Pico looked around the playful living room. The sun made warm squares on the floor. The rug felt soft again. The air smelled clean and lemony. He chirped a bright tune. “New day! New ideas! I can learn, I can try, I can be kind!”

Mia chimed in, “We discover more when we work together.”

Pico nodded. “Together is best.”

The End.
