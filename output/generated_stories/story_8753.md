# Koala Kori’s Quiet Corner Plan

Kori the koala loved soft leaves and soft sounds. Kori was calm and kind. Each morning, Kori padded to a cozy study corner. The corner had a little desk, a comfy cushion, a basket of crayons, and a tiny clock that went tick-tick. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. It smelled like paper and fresh eucalyptus.

But today, Kori’s ears drooped. The crayons were scattered. Papers were crinkly and mixed up. The tiny clock ticked fast, or so it felt. Kori’s tummy made a fluttery wiggle.

“Oh no,” Kori whispered. “I can’t find my blue crayon. I can’t find my reading card. I don’t know what to do first.”

Kori’s paws felt too busy. The calm inside felt wobbly.

Mama Wren, a small bird with kind eyes, hopped onto the window ledge and chirped, “Good morning, Kori! You look puzzled.”

“I am,” Kori said softly. “I want to draw. I want to read. I want to help tidy. But it is all messy. I don’t know where to start.”

Mama Wren tilted her head. “Let’s make a plan. A plan is a kind routine. A routine is like a song we sing every day. It helps our minds feel safe.”

Kori nodded. “A routine. Like steps?”

“Yes,” said Mama Wren. “Small steps. First, we breathe.”

Kori sat on the cushion. “In… and out.” The air smelled cool, with a hint of eucalyptus. Kori’s shoulders relaxed. The fluttery wiggle slowed down.

“Good,” said Mama Wren. “Now, let’s make your Quiet Corner Plan.”

Kori picked up a green crayon and a clean sheet. “Step 1?” Kori asked.

“Step 1: Hello Corner,” chirped Mama Wren. “Say hello to your tools. Put crayons in the cup. Smooth the papers.”

Kori smiled and said, “Hello, crayons.” Clink, clink, clink went the crayons into the cup. “Hello, papers.” Swish, swish went Kori’s soft paw, smoothing the sheets. It felt good. It sounded neat.

“Step 2: Warm-Up,” sang Mama Wren. “One easy job. Then one learning job.”

Kori drew a tiny sun to show “Warm-Up.” “My easy job is to count three blue crayons,” Kori said. “One, two, three!” The blues shone like little lakes.

“My learning job is to read my card,” Kori added. Kori found the card with big letters. “K-O-R-I,” Kori read, tapping each letter. “That’s my name!” A happy bubble popped in Kori’s chest.

“Step 3: Help a Friend,” chirped Mama Wren. “That is volunteering. We give our time. We show respect.”

Kori blinked. “I can help in my study corner?”

“Yes,” said Mama Wren. “The bookshelf is a friend. The trash bin is a friend. The neighbor’s little joey, Jip, is a friend too. You can help them.”

Kori nodded. “I can put the books back by size.” Thump, thump, thump went the books, tallest to smallest. “I can toss the tiny paper bits.” Rustle, drop. The bin said plink!

Just then, Jip the joey peeked in. “Hi, Kori,” Jip whispered. “I cannot open my glue stick. My paws are sticky.”

Kori felt calm and steady now. “I can help. Let’s use the routine.”

Kori breathed in and out. “Step 1: Hello Glue,” Kori said gently. “We hold you and twist.” Twist, pop! The glue stick opened. “Step 2: Warm-Up: dab-dab,” Kori showed. “Step 3: Help a Friend: I’ll wipe your paws.”

Jip giggled. “Dab-dab!” The glue smelled sweet, like a sugar flower. Kori gave Jip a soft wipe. “All clean,” Kori said. “We did it together.”

Jip bounced. “Thank you! You are so calm, Kori. I feel calm too.”

After Jip left, the corner looked bright and tidy. The clock went tick-tick in a friendly way. Kori’s heart felt like a soft drum. Kori picked up a blue crayon and drew a little list:

- Hello Corner
- Warm-Up
- Learning Job
- Help a Friend
- Goodbye Tidy

Kori read it aloud. “This is my routine. It helps me know what to do.”

Mama Wren chirped with pride. “That is self-regulation. You made a plan. You followed steps. You used gentle words. You showed respect for your things and for Jip.”

Kori added a last line. “Goodbye Tidy.” With slow, happy movements, Kori put the crayons back. The cup went clink. The papers stacked with a soft shhh. The chair pushed in, bump-kiss against the desk. It all felt peaceful.

Kori smiled. “I can do my plan every day. I can build my skills. I can help others. My routine is my friend.”

The tiny clock ticked like a quiet song. The sun made one more warm square on the floor. Kori curled on the cushion, calm and proud.

“Tomorrow, I will say, ‘Hello Corner,’ again,” Kori whispered. “I know what to do.”

The End.
