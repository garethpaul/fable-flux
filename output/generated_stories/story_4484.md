# Penny Panda's Pantry Adventure

Penny Panda loved to help in the kitchen. She had soft black and white fur and the biggest, kindest brown eyes. Today, Mama Panda asked her to get some honey from the pantry.

"I need honey for our bamboo pancakes," said Mama Panda. "Can you help me, Penny?"

"Yes, Mama!" said Penny, clapping her paws together. "I know where everything is!"

Penny walked to the pantry door. It was tall and wooden, with a shiny brass handle. She turned the handle slowly and stepped inside. The pantry smelled like cinnamon and vanilla. Shelves reached high up to the ceiling, filled with jars, boxes, and bottles.

"Hmm," said Penny, looking around. "Where is the honey?"

She saw apple sauce on the bottom shelf. She saw crackers on the middle shelf. But where was the honey? Penny stood on her tippy-toes and looked up high.

"Oh my!" she gasped. "The honey jar is way up there!"

The honey jar sat on the very top shelf, golden and glowing in the pantry light. It looked so far away! Penny felt a little scared. How could she reach it?

Then Penny remembered what Papa Panda always said: "When you don't know what to do, think about what you know!"

Penny sat down and thought carefully. "What do I know about getting things from high places?" she wondered aloud.

She remembered seeing Mama use the step stool in the kitchen. "A step stool!" she said. "That's what I need!"

Penny looked around the pantry. In the corner, she spotted a small wooden step stool. It had two steps and felt sturdy under her paws.

"Perfect!" said Penny. She carefully carried the step stool to the shelf with the honey.

But when Penny climbed up, she still couldn't reach the honey jar. She needed to be even taller!

Penny climbed down and thought some more. "What else do I know?" she asked herself.

Then she remembered Papa Panda using a long wooden spoon to reach things. Penny looked around and found a clean wooden spoon on a lower shelf.

"Now I'm prepared!" she said proudly.

Penny climbed back up the step stool. She held the wooden spoon carefully and gently pushed the honey jar closer to the edge. Inch by inch, the jar moved toward her.

"Almost there," she whispered, being very patient and careful.

Finally, the honey jar was close enough! Penny set down the spoon and used both paws to lift the jar safely.

"I did it!" she cheered softly. "I used my knowledge and was brave!"

Penny climbed down the step stool slowly, holding the honey jar tight. She put the step stool back in its corner and the wooden spoon back on its shelf.

"Mama! Mama!" called Penny, running to the kitchen. "I got the honey!"

"Wonderful!" said Mama Panda, giving Penny a big hug. "How did you reach it?"

"I used my brain!" said Penny proudly. "I thought about what I knew, found a step stool and a wooden spoon, and was very careful!"

"You were so smart and brave," said Mama Panda. "And you prepared well by getting the right tools!"

Soon, the kitchen filled with the sweet smell of bamboo pancakes and honey. Penny felt proud and happy. She had learned that when you use your knowledge, stay brave, and prepare well, you can solve any problem!

The End.
