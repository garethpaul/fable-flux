# Whiskers Leads the Snack Time Plan

In a cozy guest room with soft blue walls, a tiny window, and a fluffy green rug, lived a clever cat named Whiskers. The guest room smelled like clean sheets and sunshine. Sunbeams made warm squares on the floor. Whiskers liked to sit in the warm spots and listen to the house sounds—footsteps, giggles, and the hum of the fridge far away.

One afternoon, Whiskers flicked his striped tail and blinked his bright eyes. “Mrow?” he said. He felt a little hungry. His tummy made a small rumble like a purr. From the hallway, he heard the voices of his friends: Pip the puppy, Dot the duck, and Tilly the turtle. They all slipped into the guest room to play.

“Hi, Whiskers!” Pip wagged his tail so fast it thumped the rug. “I’m hungry. Let’s have a snack!”

Dot flapped her little wings. “Quack! I want crunchy chips.”

Tilly peeked from inside her shell. “Maybe cookies?” she asked softly.

Whiskers sat up tall. His whiskers twitched. He was a clever cat, and he liked to solve problems. He also liked to be a good friend. “We can’t eat all sweet and salty snacks,” he said gently. “We need strong bodies and bright brains. Let’s think.”

Dot tilted her head. “Think?” she asked.

“Yes,” said Whiskers. “Let’s use a plan. Good leaders make plans. We can be leaders for our own bodies. We will use our eyes, our noses, and our minds.”

Pip sniffed the air. “I smell bananas,” he said. The smell was sweet and sunny, like a yellow smile.

Tilly pointed to a basket on the guest room table. “There are apples and carrots, too,” she said. The apples were shiny and red. The carrots were bright orange and crisp.

Dot waddled closer. “But chips are tasty,” she whispered.

Whiskers nodded. “Chips are tasty. But we need to think. What do we need today? We played tag. We built block towers. We need energy that lasts. Let’s make a snack that helps us play more.”

“How do we choose?” asked Pip. His ears perked up.

“Step one,” said Whiskers, tapping his paw. “We look at choices.” He pointed with his paw. “Bananas, apples, carrots, yogurt cups, and a small bowl of chips.”

“Step two,” he continued, “we ask questions. Which snacks help our bodies grow strong? Which help our minds think? Which help our tummies feel good?”

Dot blinked. “I don’t know,” she said.

Whiskers smiled. “Let’s test with our senses. Smell.” They all leaned in. The apples smelled fresh, like a cool breeze. The carrots smelled earthy, like the garden after rain. The yogurt smelled creamy and sweet.

“Touch,” said Whiskers. Pip gently tapped an apple. “Firm,” he said. Dot touched a carrot. “Crunchy,” she quacked. Tilly held a yogurt cup with both hands. “Cool and smooth,” she said.

“Listen,” said Whiskers. He took a small bite of carrot. Crunch! He took a tiny chip. Crackle. He took a bite of apple. Crunch-snap!

“Now think,” said Whiskers softly. He sat very still. The room was quiet, except for the soft tick of the clock. Sunlight warmed his back. “Carrots have crunch and help our eyes. Apples are juicy and help our teeth stay busy. Yogurt is creamy and helps our tummies. Chips are fun, but not too many.”

Pip wagged. “Let’s make a snack plan!” he barked.

“Good idea,” said Whiskers. “Step three: Make a plan. We choose a rainbow snack. We pick one fruit, one veggie, one protein or dairy. Then, if we want, a tiny bit of chips for fun at the end.”

Dot clapped her wings. “I pick carrots!” she said. “Crunch!”

“I pick apples,” said Pip, licking his nose. “Juicy!”

“I pick yogurt,” said Tilly with a shy smile. “Creamy!”

Whiskers nodded. “I will lead by making plates.” He lined up four little plates on the guest room table. He washed paws and reminded friends to wash hands too. They sang a hand-washing song. “Scrub, scrub, scrub, wash, wash, wash!” The water felt cool, and the soap smelled like lemons.

Whiskers sliced apples into safe little moons. He placed carrot sticks like orange sunbeams. He opened the yogurt cups with a soft pop. “Plop!” went the spoon as it dipped in. He added three tiny chips on each plate. “Just a little,” he said. “We thought it through.”

They all sat on the green rug. The sun felt warm on their fur and feathers and shell. They ate slowly and noticed. “Crunch,” said Dot. “It sounds like footsteps in leaves.” “Mmm,” said Pip. “Apple juice tastes like sweet rain.” “The yogurt is cool,” said Tilly. “It feels calm in my tummy.”

Whiskers licked his whiskers. “Our plan worked,” he said. “We led with our minds. We asked questions. We used our senses. We chose foods that help us play and learn.”

Dot smiled. “I feel strong!” Pip bounced. “I feel fast!” Tilly nodded. “I feel brave.”

Whiskers purred. “That is critical-thinking,” he told his friends. “It means we stop, look, and think before we choose. Leaders use critical-thinking to make good plans. We can do it together every day.”

Pip tilted his head. “What about tomorrow?”

“Tomorrow,” said Whiskers, “we will think again. We will look at our choices. We will ask questions. We will make a smart plan.”

Dot fluffed her feathers. “Quack! I can help lead.”

Tilly raised her little hand. “Me too.”

Whiskers’ eyes shone. “We are a team,” he said. “Thinking friends, healthy friends, kind friends.”

They cleaned up their plates, stacked them neatly, and wiped the table. The guest room felt fresh and happy. The sun slid slowly across the rug, and Whiskers curled into the warm square with a soft, proud purr.

Today, they solved a snack-time problem with thinking, care, and courage. They learned that good leaders make plans, ask questions, and choose foods that help bodies and brains.

The End.
