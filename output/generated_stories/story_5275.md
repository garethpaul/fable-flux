# Polly Parrot Fixes It Right

In a sunny house with a squeaky door, there was a garage workshop. It smelled like pine wood and a little like oranges from the soap. Tools hung on the wall. Nuts and bolts clinked in tidy jars. A gentle fan hummed, and a tiny radio played soft music.

Polly was a creative parrot who loved the workshop. She had bright green feathers, a sunny yellow beak, and clever eyes. Polly wore a tiny blue tool belt. She liked to say, “Plan it, build it, clean it!” Her wings fluttered when she was excited.

One morning, Polly heard a rattle-rattle from the corner. “Oh!” she said, tilting her head. “The red wagon is wobbly.”

Tito, a small turtle with a shiny shell, peeked out from under a workbench. “My wagon wheel is loose,” Tito said softly. “Can you fix it, Polly?”

Polly puffed up. “Yes! I can make it fast and fun!” She tapped the wobbly wheel. Clack, clack. “I have an idea,” she chirped. “I will use this new shiny screw!”

She reached for a brand-new screw from a box. It sparkled in the light. But on the bench sat a little bag labeled, “Old screws—still good.” The old screws were clean and strong. They just were not shiny.

Grandma Dot, with silver hair and kind eyes, stepped into the garage with a cup of tea. “Good morning, Polly. Good morning, Tito,” she said. “Remember, we use what we have when we can. That is good for our Earth.”

Polly nodded, but her eyes went back to the new shiny screw. “Shiny is pretty,” she whispered. She felt a warm flutter in her tummy. She wanted the wagon to look amazing. She wanted Tito to clap. She wanted Grandma Dot to say, “Wow!”

Polly picked up the new shiny screw. Then she stopped. Her tummy felt twisty. Her cheeks felt hot under her feathers. “Hmm,” she said. “This doesn’t feel right.”

Tito blinked. “What is wrong?”

Polly took a slow breath. She listened to the fan hum. She smelled the pine wood. She looked at the bag of old screws. “Grandma Dot said to use what we have. I said I would fix it right. I want to be honest. I want to do the right thing, even when no one is watching.”

Grandma Dot smiled softly and sipped her tea. She did not say a word.

Polly put the shiny screw back. Clink. She opened the bag of old screws. “These are still good,” she said. “Using them is kind to our Earth. That is called sustainability. It means we take care of things and do not waste.”

Tito nodded. “I like kind.”

Polly spread a clean rag on the floor. “Step one: Plan it,” she chirped. “We will tighten the wheel with a good old screw. Step two: Build it. Step three: Clean it.”

She checked the wheel, turning it slowly. Squeak, squeak. She found the right old screw. She held it steady. “Lefty loosey, righty tighty,” she sang. The screwdriver went squeee-eeek as she turned. The wheel got snug. No wobble now.

“Try it, Tito,” Polly said.

Tito pushed the wagon. The little wheels went rumble-rumble over the concrete. “It works!” he cheered. His eyes were bright. “Thank you, Polly!”

Polly felt light and calm inside. The twisty feeling was gone. “I did it right,” she said. “I finished the job. I told the truth with my actions.”

Grandma Dot put down her tea. “Polly,” she said, “you showed integrity. That means you do the right thing, even when it is hard. You also used what we had. That helps the Earth. And you finished your task. I am proud.”

Polly fluffed her feathers. “I am proud too,” she said. “It feels good to be honest.”

Just then, they heard a tiny clatter. A small box of nails had slipped to the floor. Tink, tink, tink.

“Oh dear,” Tito said.

Polly looked at the nails on the floor. She was ready to play with the wagon. But she remembered her rule. “Plan it, build it, clean it,” she said. “We finish what we start.”

She picked up each nail. Plink in the jar. Plink, plink. Tito helped with his careful turtle claws. Grandma Dot held the jar open. They worked together. The floor was clean again.

Polly took a deep breath. The garage felt tidy and happy. The radio played a bouncy tune. She smelled the orange soap as she wiped the bench. Swish, swish.

“Polly,” Tito said, “can we decorate the wagon too?”

“Yes,” Polly said, smiling. “We can use old ribbons and buttons from the reuse box.” She found colorful scraps in a little bin labeled, “Use again.” They tied a blue ribbon on the handle and glued a yellow button by the side. The wagon looked cheerful.

Tito beamed. “It is perfect.”

Polly nodded. “We used what we had. We told the truth with our choices. We finished our work.”

Grandma Dot clapped softly. “Today we learned: Integrity is doing the right thing, even when no one is looking. Completing tasks keeps us safe and happy. Using what we have is good for our Earth.”

Polly flapped her wings. “Plan it, build it, clean it!” she sang.

They rolled the wagon into the sunshine. The wheel did not wobble at all. Rumble, rumble, smooth and strong. Polly felt peaceful and proud.

The End.
