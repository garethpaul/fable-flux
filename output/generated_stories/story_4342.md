# Gigi’s Tidy-Time Adventure

Gigi the thoughtful giraffe lived in a sunny, playful living room. The rug was soft like a cloud. The window let warm, golden light dance on the floor. Toys were everywhere! Blocks made little towers. Puzzle pieces peeked from under the couch. A tiny drum sat by a basket of crayons that smelled like sweet wax and a little bit of grapes.

Gigi loved to play. “I have time for everything!” she said with a smile, swishing her tail. She tapped the drum. Boom-boom! She reached her long, gentle neck to place a block on a tower. Clink! She picked up a crayon and drew a happy sun. Scritch-scratch!

But soon, the tower wobbled. The drum rolled. The crayons mixed with puzzle pieces. “Oh dear,” Gigi whispered. “So many things at once. My toys feel squished. I feel squished!”

Her friend, a soft, blue timer shaped like a friendly egg, sat on the shelf. Gigi called it Tock. “Hello, Tock,” Gigi said. “I want to play, but I also want to put things away. I want to do it all right now.”

Tock blinked its little light. Beep! “One thing at a time,” it seemed to say.

Gigi took a deep breath. She smelled orange peels from a snack on the table. She felt the rug under her hooves. “One thing at a time,” she repeated. “That is time management. I can choose what to do first, and what to do next.”

She smiled. “Tidy-Time, then Play-Time!” Gigi decided. “We can do both.”

Just then, her buddy, a bouncy ball named Dot, bounced in. Boing, boing! “Play now?” Dot chirped.

Gigi touched the ball softly. “I want to play with you, Dot,” she said kindly. “But first, I need Tidy-Time. I feel better when I do one thing at a time. That is my plan.”

Dot paused. “One thing at a time?” Dot asked.

“Yes,” said Gigi. “Please wait for me. That is my boundary. I will be ready soon.” She added a grateful smile. “Thank you for understanding.”

Dot rolled to a cozy corner. “I can wait,” Dot said. “Beep me when it’s Play-Time!”

Gigi felt warm inside. “Thank you, Dot.”

She tapped Tock. Beep! “Five minutes for Tidy-Time,” Gigi said. “Ready, set, go!”

She sang a little song. “Sort, sort, sort. Put it in the right place. We can do it at a calm and steady pace.” Her voice was soft and cheerful.

She used her long neck to reach the crayons. “Crayons in the rainbow box,” she said. Click! The box closed with a happy snap.

She nudged puzzle pieces into their tray. “Edges here, middle there,” she said. Swish-swish. The pieces felt smooth and cool.

She stacked blocks by color. Red, yellow, blue. “You wait here for Building-Time,” she told them. The blocks made a gentle clack as they touched.

Tock went beep-beep! Time was up. Gigi looked around. The room felt open and calm. The air smelled clean and sunny. Her heart felt light.

Dot bounced back. “Is it Play-Time now?” Dot asked.

“Yes!” Gigi laughed. She tapped Tock again. “Ten minutes for Play-Time.” Beep!

“Let’s play a coordination game,” said Gigi. “Roll, catch, and pause!”

Dot rolled. Gigi stretched her neck and caught the ball with her soft hoof. “Catch!” she giggled. Dot made a happy boing. They played in a rhythm: roll, catch, pause; roll, catch, pause. Gigi listened to the patterns, like music. Boom, boing, hush. She felt her body move in time. Left hoof, right hoof, gentle stop. It was fun!

After a while, Dot panted, “I’m a little tired.”

Gigi nodded. “Me too. Tock says one more minute.” Beep-beep!

They did one last roll and one last catch. “Play-Time is done,” Gigi said. “Thank you for playing and waiting, Dot.”

“Thank you for telling me your plan,” Dot said. “I like to know what happens next.”

Gigi smiled. “Me too. Knowing what happens next helps my heart feel calm.”

She set the ball on the shelf and looked at Tock. “Next is Snack-Time,” Gigi said. She peeled a small orange, and the room filled with a bright, juicy smell. Peel, pop! “Would you like a slice, Dot?”

“Yes, please,” Dot said.

They munched quietly. Squish, sweet, yum. Gigi wiped her mouth and patted the rug. “Today I learned something big,” she said. “When I choose what to do first and what to do next, I feel good. When I speak my needs, my friends understand. And when we move together in time, we play better.”

Dot nodded. “Time helps us be kind and have fun.”

Gigi hugged Dot. “Time helps us be calm,” she added. “One thing at a time, and we can do it all.”

The living room glowed. The toys rested in their spots. Tock blinked softly. Beep… beep… slow and gentle.

Gigi took a deep breath. “I am grateful for you, Dot. I am grateful for Tock. I am grateful for time.”

Dot bounced once. “Me too.”

The End.
