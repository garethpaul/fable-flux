# Fiona's Healthy Road Trip

Fiona was a gentle deer fawn with soft brown spots and big curious eyes. Today was extra special - she was going on her very first family road trip! She climbed into the back seat of the cozy blue car with her mama and papa.

"Are you excited, little one?" asked Mama Deer, buckling Fiona's seatbelt with her gentle hooves.

"Yes! Yes! Yes!" Fiona bounced in her car seat. The soft fabric felt warm against her fuzzy coat.

Papa Deer started the engine. It hummed like a happy bee. "We have a long drive ahead," he said kindly. "Let's make sure we eat well to keep our energy up!"

As they drove past green meadows and tall trees, Fiona's tummy began to rumble. "I'm hungry, Mama," she said, touching her spotted belly.

Mama reached into a special cooler bag. "What would you like, sweetie? We have crunchy carrots, sweet apples, or these colorful crackers?"

Fiona looked at the choices. The crackers had bright pictures and looked fun. "I want the pretty crackers!" she said excitedly.

But after eating just a few crackers, Fiona felt tired and grumpy. Her head felt heavy, and her spots seemed less bright. "I don't feel good," she whispered.

Papa looked in the rearview mirror with worried eyes. "What's wrong, little fawn?"

"My tummy feels funny, and I'm still hungry," Fiona said sadly.

Mama nodded wisely. "Sometimes foods that look exciting don't give our bodies what they really need. Would you like to try something different?"

Fiona thought about this. She wanted to feel better and have energy to enjoy their trip. "What makes me strong like other deer families?" she asked.

"Well," said Mama, "carrots help your eyes see far like Papa's. Apples give you energy to jump high. And these nuts help your brain think smart thoughts."

Fiona's ears perked up. She wanted to be strong and smart! "Can I try the carrots, please?"

Mama handed her a crunchy orange carrot. Fiona took a big bite. *Crunch!* It tasted sweet and fresh. Soon her tummy felt much better.

"More, please!" Fiona said happily. She tried a juicy apple slice next. The sweet juice made her feel bright and energetic again.

As they continued driving, Fiona made a goal. "I want to choose foods that make me feel good," she announced proudly.

"That's a wonderful goal," said Papa. "Every deer is different, but we all need good food to feel our best."

They passed other animal families in cars. A family of rabbits munched on leafy lettuce. A bear family shared honey and berries. A family of squirrels enjoyed nuts and seeds.

"Look!" Fiona pointed excitedly. "Everyone eats different healthy foods, but we're all happy!"

"That's right," smiled Mama. "We can celebrate how different families eat different healthy foods."

For the rest of their journey, Fiona chose fruits, vegetables, and nuts from their cooler. She felt strong, happy, and ready for adventure. Her brown spots seemed to sparkle in the sunlight streaming through the car windows.

When they reached their destination - a beautiful lake surrounded by pine trees - Fiona jumped out of the car with energy and joy.

"I feel amazing!" she said, dancing on her little hooves. "Good food helped me enjoy our whole trip!"

From that day on, Fiona always remembered to choose foods that made her body feel strong and her mind feel bright. She learned that taking care of herself was the best way to enjoy all of life's adventures.

The End.
