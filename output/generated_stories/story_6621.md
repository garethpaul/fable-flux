# Olive Owl’s Little Plan

In a quiet attic, where soft dust danced in the sunbeams, a wise old owl named Olive lived in a cozy nest on a wooden beam. The attic smelled like cedar boxes and warm wool. The floor creaked like a tiny song when Olive hopped. Outside, the wind whispered. Inside, it was calm and still.

Olive blinked her big golden eyes. “Hoo,” she sighed softly. “My wings feel sleepy. My tummy feels rumbly. My mind feels muddled.” She wanted to feel strong and bright again. She wanted to fly smooth circles and read her small book of poems without yawning.

“I need a plan,” Olive said. Her voice was gentle, like a soft blanket. “A little plan with little steps.”

She looked around the attic. There was a tiny bell on a ribbon. There was a toy drum. There were three buttons in a jar. The sunbeam made a warm yellow square on the floor.

Olive set a tiny goal. “I want to feel better in my body and my heart,” she said. “I will make three small steps. Step one, move my wings every day. Step two, drink water and choose good foods. Step three, rest and breathe.”

She fluffed her feathers. “I can do it,” she whispered. “Little steps make big changes.”

But when Olive tried to start, a little problem came. Her mind said, “Do it all now!” and her tummy said, “I want cookies!” Olive felt pulled in two ways. Her wings drooped. “Oh dear,” she said. “This feels too big.”

She paused. She closed her eyes. She listened to the attic. Creak. Hush. Creak. Hush. The quiet helped her think.

“I will make my steps even smaller,” Olive said. “I will make a tiny plan for today only.”

She pecked three dots on a card: dot, dot, dot. “Dot one: flap for one song. Dot two: drink one cup of water. Dot three: rest for five slow breaths.”

“Let’s try,” Olive told herself kindly.

She rang the tiny bell. Ding! “This bell will help me start,” she said.

Olive put the toy drum in the sunbeam. She flapped her wings to a gentle beat. Thump, flap, thump, flap. The air smelled like sunshine and cedar. Her feathers rustled like leaves. “One song,” she whispered. She hummed a little tune. “La, la, la.” Her heart felt warm. “I did it,” she hooted softly.

Next, she sipped cool water from a small blue cup. Sip, sip. The water felt like raindrops on her tongue. “Hello, water,” she giggled. “Thank you for helping me.” She chose a crunchy apple slice from a basket. Crunch. Sweet juice tickled her beak. Her tummy smiled.

“Now for rest,” Olive said. She nestled in her nest. The wool felt cozy and safe. She closed her eyes and counted five slow breaths. “In… out. In… out.” She felt the air go cool in and warm out. Her busy thoughts floated away like tiny feathers.

When she finished, Olive peeped at her card. Three dots done! She felt proud. “I took my little steps,” she said. “My plan worked.”

The next day, the attic was rainy-quiet. Pitter-patter on the roof. Olive felt a little fussy. “I don’t feel like moving,” she mumbled. Her wings sagged.

She looked at the bell. “Ding?” she asked it. Ding! went the bell, bright as a smile.

“I will make it playful,” Olive decided. She made a gentle game called Circle Time. She set three buttons in a ring on the floor. “I will flap to each button and say, ‘You can do it, Olive!’”

Flap to the first button. “You can do it, Olive!”
Flap to the second. “You can do it, Olive!”
Flap to the third. “You did it, Olive!”

She laughed. The game made her feel strong. She sipped water. Sip. She munched carrot sticks. Crunch. She breathed five slow breaths. In… out. The rain sounded like clapping.

Day by day, Olive kept her tiny plan. Some days felt easy like a soft pillow. Some days felt wiggly like a bouncy spring. On the wiggly days, she made her steps even smaller. “Half a song is still good,” she said. “One sip is still good. Three breaths are still good.” She was kind to herself.

One evening, the attic glowed orange with sunset. Olive fluttered in a smooth circle. She glided, light and happy. “Hoo-hoo!” she sang. She opened her poem book. The words felt clear and bright. She did not yawn. Her wings felt awake. Her tummy felt calm. Her mind felt peaceful.

A small mouse peeked from behind a box. “Hello, Olive,” the mouse squeaked. “You look strong today.”

Olive smiled with her eyes. “I made a little plan,” she said. “I chose small steps. I moved, I drank water, I ate good foods, and I rested. When it felt hard, I made the steps even smaller. That helped me keep going.”

The mouse nodded. “Little steps are friendly,” he said.

Olive rang the bell. Ding! “Little steps, big hearts,” she said. “We can do it, one dot at a time.”

And in the quiet attic, where sunbeams and moonbeams took turns, Olive kept her gentle plan. She felt better in her body and in her heart, day by day.

The lesson was clear: make a small goal, take tiny steps, be kind to yourself, and keep going.

The End.
