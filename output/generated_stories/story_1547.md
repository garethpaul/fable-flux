# Ellie's Big Decision Day

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved spending time in Mama's cozy home office. The room smelled like fresh flowers from the window garden, and warm sunlight streamed through the big windows. Today, Ellie had three very important things to do before lunch time.

"Good morning, my sweet Ellie," said Mama Elephant, adjusting her reading glasses. "What would you like to do first today?"

Ellie looked around the office with her bright, curious eyes. On the desk sat three special boxes. The red box held art supplies for making Earth Day cards. The blue box had puzzle pieces that needed sorting fairly between her and her little brother. The green box contained seeds for planting in their conservation garden.

"I want to do everything!" trumpeted Ellie excitedly, flapping her little ears. "But I don't know what to choose first."

Mama smiled warmly. "That sounds like you need to make some good decisions, little one. Remember, when we have many things to do, we can think about what's most important."

Ellie tapped her trunk thoughtfully on her chin. "Hmm... Brother Benny is coming to play soon. Maybe I should sort the puzzle pieces first so we can share them fairly?"

"That's very thoughtful," said Mama. "Fairness is important when we share with others."

Ellie carefully opened the blue box. The puzzle pieces felt smooth and cool in her trunk. She counted them slowly: "One for me, one for Benny, one for me, one for Benny." The colorful pieces made soft clicking sounds as she sorted them into two equal piles.

"Perfect!" said Ellie, feeling proud. "Now Benny and I can each build our own puzzle."

Next, Ellie looked at the clock on the wall. The big hand pointed to the six. "Mama, we're planting seeds with Grandpa Elephant at noon. Should I plant these seeds now so they're ready?"

"What a smart decision!" said Mama. "Thinking ahead about time helps us be prepared."

Ellie opened the green box and gasped with delight. Inside were tiny sunflower seeds that felt like little pebbles in her trunk. She gently pressed each seed into small pots filled with dark, rich soil that smelled like rain.

"These seeds will grow into big, beautiful flowers that feed the bees," said Ellie, sprinkling water drops that sparkled like diamonds in the sunlight. "I'm helping take care of our Earth!"

Finally, Ellie picked up the red box. Inside were crayons that felt waxy and smooth, and paper that crinkled softly. She drew a big, green Earth with blue oceans and wrote "I LOVE YOU, EARTH" in her very best letters.

"Look, Mama! I made good decisions about my time, I was fair with the puzzle pieces, and I helped with conservation by planting seeds!"

Mama wrapped Ellie in a warm, gentle hug that smelled like lavender. "You made wonderful choices today, my dear. You thought about others, planned your time wisely, and cared for our planet. Those are the signs of very good decision-making."

Just then, little Benny arrived, his tail swishing with excitement. "Ellie! What should we do first?"

Ellie smiled proudly. "I already made some great decisions! Want to do puzzles together? I sorted the pieces fairly for both of us!"

As the two little elephants played happily together, Ellie felt warm and proud inside. She had learned that good decisions come from thinking about others, planning ahead, and caring for the world around her.

The End.
