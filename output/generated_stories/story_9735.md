# Owl Hoot’s Tidy-To-Do Day

In a cozy home office at the top of a tall tree, a wise old owl named Owl Hoot blinked his golden eyes. The morning sun made warm stripes on his wooden desk. Papers fluttered like soft leaves. Crayons rolled, click-clack, on the floor. His laptop hummed, mmmmmm, like a friendly bee.

“Oo-hoo,” said Owl Hoot, tilting his head. “Today I have many things to do.”

He looked at his desk. It was messy. A tiny bell, a blue pencil, sticky notes, and a pinecone sat all jumbled together. Owl Hoot’s feathers felt fuzzy and busy. “When things are messy,” he whispered, “my mind feels messy too.”

He took a deep breath. In came the warm smell of tea and the sweet smell of paper. “Let’s get organized,” he hooted kindly. “Organized means everything has a place.”

He clapped his wings. “First, I will make a plan.”

Owl Hoot pulled out three bright bins. One red, one yellow, one green. He placed them in a row. Tap, tap, tap.

“Red is for now,” he said. “Yellow is for later. Green is for give.”

He drew a simple list on a big white card:
- Sip tea.
- Sort desk.
- Send a thank-you note.
- Read a story to a friend.

“Oo-hoo! A tidy plan helps me use my time,” he smiled.

Just then, he heard a soft knock. Knock-knock.

“Come in,” Owl Hoot called.

A small squirrel peeked in. “Hi, Owl Hoot. I have a question. But you look busy.”

Owl Hoot’s eyes twinkled. “I am organizing my day. That helps me have time to help friends. Please, sit.”

The squirrel sat on a little cushion. She sniffed the air. “It smells like cinnamon tea!”

“It is,” Owl Hoot said, pouring a tiny cup. “Would you like a sip?”

“Yes, please,” she squeaked. She took a tiny sip. “Mmm.”

Owl Hoot pointed to the bins. “Watch me sort. It is easy and fun.”

He picked up the blue pencil. “Do I need this now?” he asked. “Yes. Red bin.” Drop!

He picked up the tiny bell. “Do I need this later?” Ring-a-ling! “Yes. Yellow bin.” Drop!

He looked at an extra notepad. “Do I have two? I can share one.” He smiled and put it in the green bin. Drop!

“Sharing feels warm,” Owl Hoot said, touching his chest feathers. “Sharing is generous.”

The squirrel clapped her paws. “I like the green bin! It is the give bin.”

“Yes,” Owl Hoot nodded. “When we organize, we can also be generous.”

Together they sorted. Papers went “shuff-shuff” into a neat stack. Crayons clicked into a cup. The pinecone rolled, plunk, into the green bin to give to a nest-building friend.

Owl Hoot checked his list. “Sip tea. Done.” He put a tiny star next to it. “Sort desk. Done!” Another star.

“Now I have time to help you,” he said. “What is your question?”

The squirrel held up a small clock. Tick-tick-tick. “I forget what to do first. I run and run. Then I feel tired.”

Owl Hoot’s voice was soft and wise. “When we make a simple plan, time feels friendly. We can do first things first.”

He showed her his list. “One, two, three, four. We can make a list for you.”

They wrote:
- Eat a little nut snack.
- Tidy toy box.
- Practice acorn counting.
- Play outside.

The squirrel smiled wide. “This list makes my tummy feel calm.”

“Good,” said Owl Hoot. “A tidy plan and a tidy space help our minds rest.”

He looked at the green bin. “It is time to give.” He packed a small bag with the extra notepad, a spare blue pencil, and the pinecone.

“Let’s deliver,” he said.

They stepped into the hall. The wood was smooth under their feet. Outside the office door, the air smelled like leaves. Owl Hoot hooted a gentle tune, and the squirrel hummed along.

First, they visited Robin, who loved to write songs. “A gift for you,” said Owl Hoot, handing over the notepad.

“For me?” Robin chirped. “Thank you!” She sang a happy trill.

Next, they found Mouse, who liked to draw. “A spare pencil for your art,” said Owl Hoot.

Mouse squeaked, “So kind!” and twitched his whiskers.

Finally, they tucked the pinecone near Hedgehog’s door for building a cozy den. The squirrel giggled. “Surprise!”

Back in the home office, the desk looked calm and clear. The laptop hummed softly. The tea smelled like gentle cinnamon. Owl Hoot checked his list again.

“Send a thank-you note,” he read. He typed, click-click, “Thank you, friends, for being you.” Send!

“Read a story to a friend,” he read next. He opened a little book. “Squirrel, would you like a story?”

“Yes!” she said, tail swish-swish.

Owl Hoot read in a warm, steady voice. The words flowed like a soft river. The room felt peaceful and bright.

When the story ended, the squirrel sighed happily. “Today was a good day.”

“It was,” Owl Hoot agreed. “We made a plan, we organized, we shared, and we used our time well.”

The squirrel nodded. “Organization helps me feel calm. Generosity makes my heart warm. Time-management helps me play and rest.”

Owl Hoot smiled. “Oo-hoo. That is our lesson.”

They put one last star on the list: “All done!”

The End.
