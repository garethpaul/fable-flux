# Gigi Listens With Her Long Ears

Gigi was a thoughtful giraffe with a long, gentle neck and big, soft ears. She loved the creative art room at Sunshine School. The room smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Sunlight made little sparkles on jars of buttons and beads. Paintbrushes stood like flowers in cups. There was a quiet hum from the air vent, like a sleepy song.

“Good morning, art friends!” said Ms. Luna, the art teacher. She wore a paint-splashed apron and a smile. “Today we will make nature collages. We will use safe, clean scraps. We will not waste. We will listen with our ears and our hearts.”

Gigi nodded slowly. “I can listen,” she whispered. Her ears wiggled. She liked to think before she spoke. She liked to notice small sounds: the tap-tap of scissors, the rip-rip of paper, the soft swish of a brush.

On the table, there were baskets of paper bits. Some were shiny blue. Some were green like leaves. There were tiny cork circles and little fabric squares. A big sign said, “Use a little. Share a lot.”

Pip the panda grabbed a handful of shiny blue paper. “I will make the ocean!” he shouted.

Tia the tiger bounced. “I will make a sky! I need all the bright stars!” She pulled a whole string of golden beads.

Gigi took a deep breath. She wanted to make a tall tree with gentle leaves. She touched a green paper scrap. It felt smooth and cool. She looked at Ms. Luna. Ms. Luna said, “First, let’s listen to the plan. Step one: choose a small base. Step two: take only what you need. Step three: share and check in with friends.”

Gigi listened. She repeated in her head, “Small base, take a little, share.” She felt calm and ready.

But the room grew noisy. “More glue! More beads!” called Tia. Beads rattled like tiny rain. Pip’s blue paper fluttered like a wave.

Gigi saw the green basket getting empty. Her heart thumped. “Oh no,” she said softly. “I need just a few greens for leaves.”

She took a slow step. “Excuse me, Pip,” she said in a clear, kind voice. “I am making a tree. Can I have two of the green pieces when you are done?”

Pip paused. He looked at the mountain of blue in his paws. “Um,” he said. He looked at Gigi’s calm eyes and listening ears. “Yes. I can share. I took too much.”

Tia tugged at the golden beads. “I want all the stars!” she said.

Gigi felt a little squeeze in her tummy. She wanted some sparkles for her tree, too—just a tiny sun. She stood tall and took a breath. “Tia,” Gigi said, “I hear you love stars. I do, too. I need one bead for the sun. Can we listen to each other and share?”

Tia blinked. The beads felt cool in her paws. She listened to Gigi’s voice. It was gentle and steady. “Okay,” Tia said. “One bead for you. I can make my sky bright without taking all of them.” She smiled and put a few beads back in the jar. They clinked softly.

Ms. Luna nodded. “I see good listening. I hear kind, assertive words. You used your voice and your ears.”

Gigi’s cheeks felt warm and happy. She picked a small piece of cardboard for her base. She glued a brown strip for the trunk. She pressed three green scraps to make leaves. The glue was cool and sticky on her hoof. She placed one golden bead at the top. It looked like a tiny sun peeking through the branches.

Around her, the room slowed down. Pip laid his blue paper flat and smooth. “I can make a small ocean and leave blue for others,” he said. He smiled and put extra blue back into the basket. Tia made a sky with dots of light, not a giant sparkle storm. The room sounded like soft snips and happy hums.

Suddenly, a little breeze came from the open window. It smelled like wet grass. The breeze tickled the leaves on Gigi’s collage. “Rustle, rustle,” it seemed to say.

Gigi looked at the trash bin. It was full of big paper pieces that were not used. “Ms. Luna,” Gigi said, lifting her head, “can we save the big scraps? We can use them another day. That helps the Earth.”

Ms. Luna beamed. “What a thoughtful idea. That is conservation. Let’s make a ‘Save Box’ for clean scraps.”

Gigi and her friends found a shoebox. It felt smooth and a little dusty. They decorated it with a green heart. They put in the big, clean pieces. The box made a soft thump when it closed.

At the end, everyone shared. Pip held up his ocean. “I listened to the plan. I used a little, and it worked!”

Tia held up her sky. “I shared my stars and still made it bright!”

Gigi held up her tree. The golden bead sun winked. “I listened with my ears and my heart,” she said. “I used my voice kindly. I made my own art by myself. I also helped our Earth.”

Ms. Luna clapped. “Today we learned: Listen first. Use kind, brave words. Do your own work. Care for the world. When we listen, we make space for everyone.”

Gigi felt tall inside and out. Her ears felt warm with pride. The room smelled like glue and crayons and a little bit of sunshine. She took a deep breath and smiled.

“I can listen,” Gigi whispered. “And I can speak up, too.”

The End.
