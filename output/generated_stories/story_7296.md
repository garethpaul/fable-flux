# Leo the Lion Cub Learns About Differences

Leo was a brave little lion cub with a fluffy golden mane and bright curious eyes. He lived with his family in a cozy house with a big dining room where they ate all their meals together.

One sunny morning, Leo's mama announced, "We're having special guests for lunch today! The Elephant family is coming to visit."

Leo bounced excitedly in his chair. "Yay! I love making new friends!"

When the doorbell rang, Leo rushed to greet their guests. But when he saw the Elephant family, he stopped and stared. Baby Ella Elephant had a long, wiggly trunk and big floppy ears. She looked so different from Leo!

"Hello, Leo!" said Mama Elephant kindly. "This is our daughter, Ella."

Leo felt a little scared. He had never seen anyone with a trunk before. "Why does she look so funny?" he whispered to his mama.

"Shh," Mama Lion said gently. "Different doesn't mean funny, Leo. Ella is just different from us, and that's perfectly wonderful."

At lunchtime, everyone gathered around the big wooden dining table. The room smelled delicious with fresh fruit and vegetables. But Leo watched nervously as Ella used her trunk to pick up her food.

"That's gross!" Leo said loudly. "She's touching her food with her nose!"

Ella's big eyes filled with tears. "I can't help it," she sniffled. "This is how elephants eat."

Leo's mama looked disappointed. "Leo, that wasn't kind. How would you feel if someone said mean things about how you eat?"

Leo thought about this. He felt sorry when he saw Ella looking sad. "I... I would feel hurt," he admitted quietly.

"Ella," said Leo's mama, "would you like to show Leo how elephants eat? Maybe he can learn something new."

Ella perked up. "Really? Well, first I always wash my trunk very carefully." She demonstrated how she cleaned her trunk with a wet napkin. "Elephants are very clean! Then I use my trunk like you use your paws."

Leo watched with wonder as Ella gently picked up a banana with the tip of her trunk. "Wow! That's actually really cool! Can you pick up tiny things too?"

"Watch this!" Ella giggled, carefully lifting a single grape. "My trunk is very strong and very gentle."

"That's amazing!" Leo's eyes grew wide. "I'm sorry I was mean, Ella. I didn't understand that you were just being yourself."

"It's okay," Ella smiled. "Want to try eating like an elephant? You can pretend your paw is a trunk!"

Leo giggled and carefully picked up a piece of fruit with just his paw, moving it slowly like Ella's trunk. "This is fun! But your trunk is much better at it than my paw."

"And your teeth are much better for crunching than mine!" Ella laughed.

The two new friends spent the rest of lunch sharing stories and laughing together. Leo learned that Ella loved to paint with her trunk, and Ella discovered that Leo could roar tiny, cute roars.

"Thank you for teaching me about being different," Leo said as they cleaned up together. "Different is actually pretty awesome!"

Mama Lion smiled proudly. "When we're kind and curious about others who are different from us, we can make wonderful new friends and learn amazing new things."

From that day on, Leo and Ella became the best of friends, and Leo always remembered to be tolerant and kind to everyone he met, no matter how different they seemed.

The End.
