# Milly Mouse Makes a Plan

In a cozy nursery with soft yellow walls and fluffy white curtains, lived a little mouse named Milly. Her tiny home was tucked safely behind the wooden toy chest, where she could hear the gentle sounds of babies sleeping and mobiles spinning overhead.

Every morning, Milly would peek out to watch the nursery come alive. She loved seeing the colorful toys, hearing the soft lullabies, and smelling the sweet scent of baby powder in the air. But there was one big problem that made Milly's whiskers twitch with worry.

"Oh dear," Milly squeaked to herself one Tuesday morning. "The baby's crackers are way up on the high shelf, and I'm getting so hungry! But how can a tiny mouse like me reach something so far up?"

Milly's tummy rumbled loudly. She had tried jumping many times before, but the shelf was much too high for her little legs. She had tried climbing the smooth wall, but her tiny paws kept slipping down.

"I need to think about this differently," Milly said, sitting down on her soft cotton bed. "Maybe if I make a good plan, I can solve this problem!"

Milly closed her eyes and thought carefully. She imagined the nursery in her mind, picturing every toy, every chair, and every possible path to the shelf.

"Aha!" she squeaked excitedly. "I can use the rocking chair, then climb onto the changing table, then walk across the mobile string, and finally reach the shelf!"

But as Milly started her journey, she realized her plan had problems. The rocking chair was too wobbly, and she felt scared. The mobile string was too thin and swayed too much.

"This plan isn't working," Milly sighed. "But I won't give up! Let me think of a better way."

Milly sat down again and looked around more carefully. This time, she noticed the stack of soft building blocks near the bookshelf.

"Wait a minute," she whispered. "What if I stack those blocks to make stairs?"

Milly's eyes sparkled with excitement. She scurried over to the colorful foam blocks and began pushing them with her tiny paws. The red block went first, then the blue one on top, then the yellow one.

"This is hard work," Milly puffed, "but my plan is working!"

One by one, she carefully stacked the soft blocks. They were just the right size for her little paws to grip, and they felt squishy and safe under her feet.

Step by step, Milly climbed her block staircase. Up, up, up she went, until finally she reached the shelf!

"I did it!" Milly cheered quietly, so she wouldn't wake the sleeping babies. "My plan worked perfectly!"

She nibbled on a delicious cracker, her heart full of pride and her tummy happy at last.

When Milly climbed back down, she felt different – stronger and more confident.

"I learned something very important today," she said to herself. "When I have a problem, I can stop and think of a good plan. If my first plan doesn't work, I can try again with a better one. Planning helps me reach my goals, even when they seem impossible!"

From that day forward, whenever Milly faced a challenge, she would sit quietly, think carefully, and make a plan. And you know what? Her plans almost always worked!

The End.
