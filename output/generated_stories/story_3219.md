# Penny Panda's Problem-Solving Night

Penny Panda loved her cozy bedroom. Soft moonlight streamed through her window. Her fluffy pillow smelled like lavender. Everything felt just right for bedtime.

"Good night, Penny," whispered Mama Panda, tucking her in with a warm blanket.

But just as Penny closed her eyes, she heard a tiny sound. "Sniff, sniff, sob."

Penny sat up and listened carefully. The sound came from under her bed.

"Hello?" Penny called softly. "Is someone there?"

A small voice answered. "It's me, Benny Bunny. I lost my favorite carrot toy!"

Penny climbed out of bed and looked underneath. There sat Benny, his eyes full of tears.

"Don't worry," said Penny kindly. "Let's think about this step by step. When did you last see your carrot?"

Benny wiped his nose. "I had it when I played in the toy box. Then I hopped around the room. Then... I don't remember!"

Penny thought carefully. "Let's be detectives! First, let's check the toy box."

They looked through all the toys. No carrot.

"Hmm," said Penny, tapping her chin. "You said you hopped around the room. Where did you hop?"

"By the bookshelf!" Benny remembered.

They searched behind the books. Still no carrot.

"Think harder," encouraged Penny. "What else did you do?"

Benny's ears perked up. "Oh! I played hide-and-seek with myself!"

"Where did you hide?" asked Penny.

"In the closet!" Benny exclaimed.

They opened the closet door. Penny moved her dress-up clothes aside. There, behind a sparkly hat, sat the orange carrot toy!

"You found it!" Benny squeaked with joy. "Thank you, Penny!"

Just then, they heard another small voice. "Help! Help!"

This time, it came from the windowsill. A tiny field mouse named Milly was stuck behind Penny's flower pot.

"Oh my!" said Penny. "We need to help Milly too!"

Benny nodded. "Let's think together!"

Penny looked at the problem. "The pot is too heavy for me to move alone. But if we work as a team..."

"I can push from this side," offered Benny.

"And I'll pull from the other side," said Penny.

Together, they carefully moved the flower pot. Milly scurried out, safe and sound.

"Thank you both!" squeaked Milly. "You're such good problem-solvers!"

Soon, a sleepy hedgehog named Harry appeared. "I heard voices. Is everything okay?"

Penny smiled. "We've been helping friends! But now I think we all need sleep."

"Can we have a sleepover?" asked Benny hopefully.

Penny looked around her cozy room. There was plenty of space for everyone.

"Of course!" she said. "But first, let's think about how to make everyone comfortable."

Working together, they gathered extra pillows and blankets. Penny shared her softest stuffed animals. Benny found cozy spots for everyone. Milly and Harry helped arrange everything perfectly.

As they all settled down, Penny felt proud and happy. Her room was full of friends, and her heart was full of joy.

"Penny," whispered Benny, "you taught me something important tonight."

"What's that?" Penny asked sleepily.

"When we have a problem, we can think step by step to solve it. And helping others feels really good!"

Penny smiled in the moonlight. "And working together makes everything easier!"

Soon, all four friends were fast asleep in the cozy bedroom, dreaming of their next helpful adventure.

The End.
