# Polly's Perfect Plan

Polly the parrot loved the big dining room. Sunlight streamed through tall windows onto the shiny wooden table. The room smelled like fresh flowers from Mom's vase. Polly's colorful feathers sparkled red, blue, and green in the morning light.

"Time to clean up!" called Mom from the kitchen. "The neighbors are coming for dinner tonight."

Polly watched from her perch as the family rushed around. Dad wiped the table with a soft cloth. Sister Sarah swept crumbs from under the chairs. Brother Ben folded napkins into neat squares.

"I want to help too!" squawked Polly loudly. Her voice echoed in the big room.

Everyone stopped and looked at her.

"Oh Polly," said Mom kindly. "You're too small to help with dinner preparations."

"Birds might make a mess," added Dad, polishing a silver spoon.

Polly's bright eyes grew sad. Her beautiful feathers drooped. She felt left out and unimportant. "But I have good ideas!" she chirped softly.

The family continued working. They moved chairs and plates. They arranged forks and knives. Polly watched quietly, feeling smaller and smaller.

Then Polly had a wonderful thought. She spread her wings wide and flew to the center of the table.

"Listen please!" she called out. "I know I'm little, but I can help in special ways!"

Sarah stopped sweeping. "What do you mean, Polly?"

Polly puffed out her chest with confidence. "I can greet our guests! I know everyone's names. I can say 'Welcome!' and 'Please sit down!' in my loudest, happiest voice."

Ben looked interested. "That's actually a good idea."

"And," continued Polly excitedly, "I can tell jokes during dinner! I know funny riddles that make people laugh. Happy guests eat better!"

Mom smiled warmly. "Those are creative ideas, Polly. But we still need you to be careful around the food."

Polly nodded her head up and down. "I promise to stay on my special perch during eating time. No flying over plates!"

Dad thought for a moment. "What if we make a deal? You can be our official greeter and entertainment. We'll set up your perch near the door."

"Yes!" chirped Polly. "And I can help fold napkins too! I'll hold one corner with my beak while Sarah holds the other."

Sarah giggled. "That sounds fun! We can work together."

The family looked at each other and smiled.

"It's a deal," said Mom. "Everyone can contribute something special."

That evening, Polly sat proudly on her decorated perch by the dining room door. When the neighbors arrived, she called out clearly: "Welcome to our home! Please come in!"

The guests laughed and clapped. "What a smart parrot!" they said.

During dinner, Polly told her best riddle: "What do you call a sleeping bull? A bulldozer!" Everyone giggled and asked for more jokes.

Polly felt warm and happy inside. Her family had listened to her ideas. They found ways for her to help that worked for everyone.

After the guests left, Mom gave Polly a gentle pat. "Thank you for being such a wonderful host, Polly. Your ideas made tonight extra special."

"We all worked together," said Polly proudly. "That made it perfect!"

The dining room glowed softly in the lamplight. Polly had learned that even small helpers can make big differences when families listen to each other and find ways to work together.

The End.
