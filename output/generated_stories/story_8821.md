# Clever Cat Says, "I Can Ask!"

In a sunny, playful living room, a clever cat named Whiskers stretched on a soft, squishy rug. The rug felt warm and fuzzy under her paws. Toys were scattered like bright candies. There were blocks, soft balls, crayons, and big sheets of paper. Sunlight made little dust sparkles dance in the air.

Whiskers purred. “Today I will learn new things,” she said. “I will try art. I will try sports. I will use my clever brain.” Her tail swished with happy flicks.

Whiskers picked up a red crayon. “Scritch-scratch,” it went on the paper. She drew a round sun with smiling eyes. Yellow, orange, and red made the paper bright. She sniffed the waxy crayon smell. “Mmm, art smells funny and nice,” she giggled.

Just then, her friend Pip, a bouncy puppy, ran in. “Woof! Let’s play ball!” Pip nudged a soft blue ball. “Kick, bounce, spin!”

Whiskers looked at her drawing. She looked at the ball. Her whiskers twitched. “I want to finish my art,” she thought. “But I also want to try sports.” Her tummy felt wiggly, like jelly.

Pip wagged hard. “Come now! Run, jump, zoom!”

Whiskers took a breath. She remembered something she had learned. “I can speak up. I can say what I need.”

She smiled at Pip. “Pip, I want to finish my picture. Then I will play ball. Is that okay?” Her voice was gentle and strong.

Pip paused. “Woof?” He tilted his head.

Whiskers pointed to her drawing. “Art is important to me. I want to finish the sun and a cat. I will play in five minutes.”

Pip’s tail slowed. Then he grinned. “Okay, five minutes! I will count. One… two… I can wait!” Pip curled on a pillow and hummed a puppy hum.

Whiskers felt proud. Her chest felt warm and big. “I did it,” she said softly. “I asked for what I need.”

She added orange rays. She added a cat with a happy smile. She wrote her name with careful letters: W-H-I-S-K-E-R-S. “I used my knowledge,” she said. “I know my letters!”

“Time!” Pip barked, bouncing up. “Ball time!”

Whiskers held up her picture. “Ta-da!”

“Wow!” said Pip. “So bright! You are an art star!”

Whiskers set the picture to dry. Then she padded to the ball. “Now I try sports.”

Pip rolled the ball with his nose. “Ready, set, go!”

Whiskers tapped the ball with her paw. Thump! It rolled to a tower of blocks. The tower wobbled. “Uh-oh!” she squeaked. The tower tumbled with a clatter. Crash, clink, clunk!

Whiskers’ ears drooped. “I didn’t mean to knock it,” she said. Her tail went still. “I don’t know how to aim well.”

Pip gave a kind woof. “It’s okay. We can learn.”

Whiskers took another breath. “I can ask for help,” she told herself. “That is smart.”

She looked up at Pip. “Pip, can you teach me? I want to learn to aim the ball. Please show me how.”

Pip beamed. “Yes! Stand with your paws like this.” He made his front paws wide and steady. “Look, look at the spot. Tail low, eyes on the ball. Gentle tap.”

Whiskers copied. The rug felt tickly between her toes. She saw the spot—a soft pillow goal. She tapped. Thump. The ball rolled, slow and straight. Plop! It kissed the pillow.

“I did it!” Whiskers purred, buzzing like a tiny engine.

“Great job!” Pip barked. “You used knowledge. You asked. You learned!”

Whiskers laughed. “Again!” They played tap, roll, and goal. Their paws and nose made soft thumps and happy pats. The room felt full of play sounds and warm light.

After a while, Whiskers got warm and thirsty. Her tongue felt dry. She stopped and put a paw on her chest. “I need a drink and a rest,” she said.

Pip bounced, then paused. “Do you want to keep playing?”

Whiskers stood tall. “I will speak up. Pip, I need water and a break, please.”

Pip nodded. “Yes, let’s rest!” He fetched a small bowl. Slosh, slosh. Whiskers sipped cool water. It felt like tiny snow on her tongue. “Ahh,” she sighed.

They sat by the window. Whiskers looked at her art. She looked at the ball. She looked at her friend. Her whiskers lifted in a smile.

“Today I learned three big things,” she said.

Pip perked up. “Tell me!”

“One,” said Whiskers, holding up a paw, “I can say what I need. I can ask for time to finish my art.”

“Two,” she said, “I can ask for help to learn. That makes me strong and smart.”

“Three,” she said, “I can take a break when my body needs it. That keeps me healthy.”

Pip wagged. “You are a clever cat!”

Whiskers purred. “And you are a helpful friend.”

They taped the sun picture on the wall. They made a little goal spot with the pillow. The playful living room looked like a happy art-and-sports place.

Whiskers stretched, long and lazy. “I feel proud,” she said. “When I use my words, I take care of me. When I learn new things, I grow.”

Pip nuzzled her ear. “Let’s play and make more art tomorrow!”

Whiskers’s eyes shone. “Yes. Tomorrow I will ask, learn, and create again.”

The End.
