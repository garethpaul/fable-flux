# Koala Kori’s Car Ride Kindness

Kori the koala was calm and cozy in the family car. The seat felt soft under her, and the seat belt hugged her like a gentle tree branch. The car hummed, vrrrmmm, like a sleepy bee. Sunlight peeked through the window and warmed Kori’s fuzzy ears.

Mama drove with careful hands. “Good morning, Kori,” Mama said. “Snack box ready?”

“Yes!” Kori smiled. “Crunchy carrots, please.” She opened her little box. Crunch, crunch. The carrots tasted sweet and crisp. “Carrots help me grow strong,” Kori said. “And water helps me feel good.” She sipped from her cup. Glug, glug.

In the back seat, Baby Kip, Kori’s little cousin, kicked his feet. Tap-tap. “Hi, Kori!” Kip squeaked.

“Hi, Kip,” Kori said softly. “We use gentle feet in the car.” She wiggled her toes and giggled.

The car passed tall trees. Whoosh. The leaves waved like friendly hands. Kori took a slow breath. In through the nose. Out through the mouth. “I feel calm,” she whispered. “Breathing helps.”

Mama turned on the radio. A song in Spanish floated out. “Buenos días,” the singer sang. “Good morning,” Mama translated. “Buenos días means good morning.”

Kori’s ears perked up. “Buenos días,” she tried. The sound felt like sunshine on her tongue. “I like new words,” Kori said. “They are like new friends.”

“Sí,” Mama smiled. “Yes. Words in any language can be kind.”

The car stopped at a red light. Click. Kori watched children walking with bright backpacks. One child waved. Kori waved back. “Hola,” she practiced softly. “That means hello.”

“Hola!” Baby Kip chirped. “Hola!”

“Great try, Kip,” Kori said. “We can learn together.”

The light turned green. The car rolled on. Vrrrmmm. Kori peeked at the snack box again. There was a cookie inside. It smelled like warm cinnamon. “May I have a cookie after my carrots?” Kori asked.

“Yes,” Mama said. “Healthy foods first, sweet treat last.”

Kori nodded. “Healthy habits help my body and brain.” She ate another carrot. Crunch. Crunch.

Suddenly, Baby Kip reached over with a fast little paw and squeezed Kori’s cookie. Squish! The cookie crumbled. Crick-crack. Pieces fell like tiny stars in the snack box.

Kori’s eyes grew wide. Her tummy felt tight. “Oh!” she gasped. “My cookie!”

Baby Kip’s eyes got big and wet. “Sowwy,” he whispered. “I wanted to look.”

Kori felt hot in her cheeks. She took a slow breath. In through the nose. Out through the mouth. She put her hand on her belly. “I am calm,” she told herself. “I can use gentle words.”

Mama glanced in the mirror. “Accidents happen,” she said softly. “We can fix it together.”

Kori looked at the crumbled cookie. It still smelled like cinnamon. She looked at Kip’s small, worried face. “I forgive you, Kip,” Kori said. “Thank you for saying sorry.” Her tummy loosened. She felt lighter, like a leaf.

Kip sniffed. “F’give?”

“Forgive means we try again,” Kori said. “Next time, we ask first.”

Kip nodded. “Ask first,” he repeated.

Kori smiled. “We can share the cookie pieces,” she said. “We can use a spoon.” She held out a little spoon from the snack box. Scoop, scoop. They each took a tiny taste. “Mmm,” Kori said. “Still yummy.”

“Gracias,” Mama said. “That means thank you.”

“Gracias,” Kori told Kip.

“G’acias,” Kip tried. He giggled.

Kori clapped softly. “We are learning new words and new ways.”

Mama parked at the store. Beep, beep went the cars around them. A warm, bread-y smell floated in from outside. Kori stretched in her seat. “Time to move!” she said. “My body likes to wiggle.”

“First, we do our car check,” Mama reminded. “Seat belt off carefully. Hands to self. Feet on floor.”

Kori clicked her belt open. Click. She wiggled her shoulders. Wiggle, wiggle. “Healthy habit,” she sang. “Move, drink water, eat colors, breathe slow.”

Kip bounced. “Breathe slow,” he copied. He breathed in too fast and made a silly face. “Whoops!”

Kori showed him. “Smell the flower.” She sniffed. “Blow the candle.” She blew out a gentle puff. Kip did it too. Sniff. Puff. They both smiled.

When they came back to the car, Mama gave Kori a small apple. The skin was shiny and red. “For a strong snack,” Mama said.

Kori rubbed the apple and took a bite. Crunch! Juicy and sweet. “Delicious,” she said. “Delicioso,” Mama added.

“Delicioso,” Kori repeated. “I love words.” She offered a tiny slice to Kip. “Here you go.”

“Thank you. Gracias,” Kip said proudly.

As they drove home, the sky turned soft and pink. The car hummed its cozy song. Vrrrmmm. Kori leaned back and closed her eyes for a moment. She felt peaceful.

“I learned three things today,” Kori said. “One: Healthy habits help me feel good—eat colors, drink water, move, and breathe. Two: New words are fun—hola, gracias, delicioso. Three: Forgiveness is kind. We can fix mistakes and try again.”

Mama smiled. “That is a wonderful lesson, Kori.”

Kip clapped his little hands. “Try again!”

Kori squeezed his paw gently. “Try again,” she agreed. “We can always try again.”

The car rolled on, safe and warm. Kori took one more slow breath and watched the sunset glow. Her heart felt calm, kind, and strong.

The End.
