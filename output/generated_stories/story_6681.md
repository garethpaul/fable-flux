# Gigi's Listening Corner

Gigi the giraffe had a special secret place. In the back of her bedroom closet, behind the hanging dresses and under the soft blankets, she had made a cozy hideaway. The closet smelled like lavender and felt warm and safe.

Every morning, Gigi would duck her long neck down low and crawl into her hideaway. She called it her "Listening Corner" because it was where she practiced her very best skill - listening with her heart.

"Good morning, Listening Corner," Gigi whispered, settling onto her favorite purple cushion. The closet was quiet and peaceful, with just tiny streams of sunlight peeking through the crack under the door.

Today felt different though. Through the walls, Gigi could hear something that made her ears perk up. It sounded like... crying?

Gigi pressed her ear against the wall and listened carefully. Yes, someone was definitely sad. The sound was coming from next door, where her neighbor Mrs. Rabbit lived.

"Oh my," Gigi said softly. "Mrs. Rabbit sounds upset. I wonder what's wrong?"

Gigi's mama had always taught her that good listening meant paying attention with your ears AND your heart. So Gigi closed her eyes and listened even more carefully.

She heard Mrs. Rabbit sniffling and saying, "I just can't reach those high shelves anymore. How will I ever organize my kitchen?"

Gigi's heart felt warm and tingly. She knew exactly how to help! Her long neck was perfect for reaching high places.

But then Gigi felt a little nervous. What if Mrs. Rabbit didn't want help? What if she was too busy? Gigi had always been shy about speaking up.

"Be brave, Gigi," she whispered to herself in the closet. "Listening is only half of helping. Sometimes you need to be brave and offer to help too."

Gigi took three deep breaths - in through her nose, out through her mouth - just like her mama taught her. Then she crawled out of her Listening Corner and walked next door.

*Knock, knock, knock.*

"Mrs. Rabbit? It's Gigi from next door," she called in her gentlest voice.

The door opened, and there was Mrs. Rabbit with tears in her eyes and flour all over her whiskers.

"Oh, hello Gigi," Mrs. Rabbit said, wiping her eyes. "I'm sorry, I'm just having a difficult morning."

"I heard you were sad," Gigi said kindly. "I was in my special listening place, and I couldn't help but notice. Would you like some help with your high shelves?"

Mrs. Rabbit's eyes lit up like sunshine. "Oh my goodness, would you really? That would be wonderful!"

For the next hour, Gigi used her tall neck to help Mrs. Rabbit organize all her kitchen shelves. They put the sugar bowls up high, arranged the colorful plates, and even hung some pretty dish towels.

"Gigi, you're such a good listener," Mrs. Rabbit said, giving her a warm hug. "Thank you for hearing that I needed help and being brave enough to ask."

That evening, back in her Listening Corner, Gigi felt proud and happy. She had learned something very important: listening with your heart is wonderful, but sometimes the best part of listening is knowing when to speak up and help.

"Tomorrow I'll listen again," Gigi said, snuggling into her soft cushions. "And if someone needs help, I'll be brave and offer it."

The End.
