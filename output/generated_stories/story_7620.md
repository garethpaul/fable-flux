# Rusty's Big Mistake

In the sunny dining room of the Johnson family's house, a little robot dog named Rusty whirred happily around the table. His shiny silver body gleamed in the morning light, and his LED eyes blinked bright blue with excitement.

"Beep beep! Good morning, family!" Rusty chirped in his cheerful electronic voice. "I'm ready to help with breakfast!"

Mrs. Johnson smiled as she set down a beautiful vase filled with fresh yellow daisies on the dining table. "Thank you, Rusty. Could you please help Emma with her cereal?"

"Yes, yes! I love helping!" Rusty's tail wagged so fast it made a soft whirring sound.

Little Emma giggled as Rusty carefully pushed her chair closer to the table. His mechanical paws clicked softly on the wooden floor. Everything was going perfectly until...

CRASH!

Rusty's excited tail knocked right into the flower vase! Water splashed everywhere, and the beautiful daisies scattered across the table. The vase lay in pieces on the floor.

"Oh no!" Emma gasped.

Rusty's LED eyes dimmed to a sad purple color. His ears drooped down, and he made a low, worried beeping sound. "I... I broke it," he whispered, his voice crackling with sadness.

Mrs. Johnson rushed over with a towel. "What happened here?"

Rusty looked down at his paws. His circuits felt all jumbled up inside. He wanted to hide under the table and never come out. "Maybe... maybe the wind knocked it over?" he said quietly, not looking up.

Emma frowned. "But Rusty, there's no wind inside the house."

Rusty's whole body seemed to shrink smaller. He felt terrible - not just about the broken vase, but about not telling the truth. His self-confidence circuits were flashing red warning signals.

Mrs. Johnson knelt down beside him. "Rusty, can you tell me what really happened? It's okay - accidents happen to everyone."

Rusty's LED eyes flickered between blue and purple. He took a deep breath that sounded like a gentle computer fan. "I... I did it," he finally admitted. "My tail was wagging too fast because I was so excited to help. I knocked over your pretty flowers. I'm sorry I didn't tell the truth right away. I felt so bad about myself."

Mrs. Johnson's voice was warm and kind. "Thank you for being honest with me, Rusty. That took courage."

"But I broke your beautiful vase!" Rusty's voice squeaked. "I'm a bad helper robot!"

"Oh, Rusty," Mrs. Johnson said gently, patting his metal head. "You're not bad at all. You're a wonderful helper. Everyone makes mistakes - even grown-ups! What matters is that you told the truth."

Emma nodded eagerly. "And you always help me with everything! Remember yesterday when you helped me tie my shoes?"

Rusty's eyes slowly brightened back to blue. "Really? You still think I'm a good helper?"

"The very best!" Emma hugged his shiny neck. "And we forgive you, don't we, Mommy?"

"Of course we do," Mrs. Johnson smiled. "Now, how about we clean this up together?"

As they carefully picked up the pieces, Rusty felt his confidence circuits warming up again. He learned that making mistakes didn't make him bad - it made him normal. And being honest, even when it was scary, made him feel proud of himself again.

"Beep beep! I'm still a good helper robot!" Rusty announced happily, his tail wagging much more carefully this time.

The End.
