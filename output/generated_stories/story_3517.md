# Bobo Bear Says “I’m Sorry”

Bobo was a friendly bear cub with soft brown fur and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, he padded into the laundry room with a happy hum. The laundry room smelled like warm soap and clean cotton. It was bright and cozy, with a gentle whoosh from the washing machine and a soft hum from the dryer.

Mama Bear smiled. “Good morning, Bobo. Today, we have a laundry day! We will wash, fold, and put away our clothes. It’s a team job.”

Bobo clapped his paws. “Team job! I can help!”

Mama Bear nodded. “Yes, you can. We need coordination. That means we work together, step by step.”

Bobo stood up tall. “Step by step!”

He looked around. There were baskets of clothes, a box of soap, and a big bottle of soft-smelling liquid. The washing machine door was round and shiny like a moon.

“First,” Mama Bear said gently, “we sort. Whites in this basket, colors in that basket.”

Bobo reached for a tiny red sock. It felt warm and fuzzy. “Red sock in colors!” he said, dropping it in.

They sorted together. Swish, swish went the clothes as they landed in baskets. Bobo giggled when two socks stuck to his fur. “Sticky socks!” he laughed.

“Static,” Mama Bear chuckled. “They like you.”

When the baskets were sorted, Mama Bear said, “Second, we wash. Clothes go in, soap goes in, then we press the button.”

Bobo was excited. He wanted to be fast. He grabbed the big bottle of soft-smelling liquid. It sloshed and made a glug-glug sound. Bobo tipped it too far. Oh no!

Sploosh!

Blue liquid poured out. It splashed on the floor. It dripped down Bobo’s paw. It made the floor slippery.

Bobo’s eyes grew wide. His ears drooped. His tummy felt wobbly. “Oh,” he whispered. “I made a mess.”

Mama Bear looked at the blue puddle. She took a deep breath. “Accidents happen, Bobo.”

Bobo bit his lip. His cheeks felt hot. He wanted to hide behind the baskets. He held the bottle tight and whispered, “I’m sorry, Mama. I spilled it.”

Mama Bear knelt down and smiled softly. “Thank you for saying sorry, Bobo. That is brave and kind.”

Bobo blinked. “It is?”

“Yes,” Mama Bear said. “Apology means you know something went wrong, and you want to make it better.”

Bobo nodded slowly. “I want to make it better.”

Mama Bear pointed to a stack of towels. “Third, we clean. We can fix this together. I will hold the bottle. You can place towels on the puddle.”

Bobo put down the bottle carefully. He picked up a fluffy white towel. It felt soft on his paws. He laid it on the puddle. Squish! The towel drank the blue liquid. Bobo pressed with both paws. Squeeze, squeeze. He used another towel. Squish, squeeze.

“Good work,” Mama Bear said. “You are helping.”

Bobo felt a little lighter. His tummy didn’t wobble as much. “We coordinate!” he said, smiling a tiny smile.

“Yes,” Mama Bear replied. “We coordinate.”

Soon the floor was dry and safe. Bobo washed his blue-sticky paws with warm water. Drip, drip. He dried them on a small hand towel. Rub, rub.

“Now,” Mama Bear said, “we finish the job. Clothes in. Soap—just a little. Would you like to pour?”

Bobo took a slow breath. He held the soap cup with two paws. He poured only to the line. “Just a little,” he whispered. The soap made a soft gloop sound.

“Perfect,” Mama Bear said. “Now the button.”

Bobo pressed the round button with one careful paw. Beep! The machine began to swish and swirl. Whoosh, whoosh. Bubbles danced behind the glass like tiny clouds.

Bobo’s smile grew big. “We did it! We made it better.”

Mama Bear hugged him. Her fur smelled like clean cotton. “You said sorry. You helped clean. You finished the task. That is wonderful.”

When the wash was done, they moved the clothes to the dryer. The dryer hummed, mmmmmm, like a big sleepy bee. Later, warm clothes tumbled out in a soft, cozy pile. Bobo giggled and put a warm sock on his paw. “Paw sock!”

Mama Bear laughed. “Silly Bobo. Let’s fold.”

They folded together. Fold the shirt, fold the pants, match the socks. Bobo looked for pairs. “Two blue! Two yellow!” He tapped them together. Tap, tap. He liked the feeling of a job getting done.

At last, the baskets were full of neat, clean clothes. The room smelled fresh. The floor was dry and safe. The machines were quiet. Bobo felt proud and calm.

“Mama,” Bobo said, “I learned something.”

“What did you learn?” Mama Bear asked.

Bobo thought. “When I make a mess, I can say, ‘I’m sorry.’ I can help fix it. I can slow down. We can work together.”

Mama Bear kissed his forehead. “That is a big lesson, my little bear.”

Bobo puffed out his chest. “I am a helper.” He carried a small basket to his room. Step by careful step, he put his clothes away.

Mama Bear called, “Team job complete!”

Bobo cheered, “Team job complete!” His voice rang happy and bright.

That night, Bobo snuggled into his blanket. He felt safe and warm. He remembered the whoosh of the washer and the hum of the dryer. He remembered the blue puddle, the towels, and his brave words: “I’m sorry.”

Bobo smiled. “Tomorrow, I will help again,” he whispered. “Step by step.”

The End.
