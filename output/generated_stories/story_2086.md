# Ollie's Honest Day

Ollie the otter loved spending sunny mornings on Grandma Willow's peaceful front porch. The wooden boards felt warm under his fuzzy belly, and he could smell the sweet flowers blooming in the garden nearby.

"Good morning, Ollie!" called Grandma Willow as she stepped outside with a basket of colorful bottles and cans. "I'm getting ready to sort these for recycling. Would you like to help?"

Ollie's whiskers twitched with excitement. "Yes, please! I love helping!"

Grandma Willow smiled and set down three different bins on the porch. "The blue bin is for bottles, the green bin is for cans, and the yellow bin is for paper. We take turns putting one item in at a time. That way, we can be mindful and make sure everything goes in the right place."

Ollie nodded eagerly. He picked up a shiny red can and *clink* - dropped it into the green bin. "My turn!" he squeaked happily.

Grandma Willow took a clear bottle and gently placed it in the blue bin. "Your turn again, Ollie."

They worked together peacefully, taking turns and listening to the gentle *clink* and *plop* sounds as each item found its home. The morning breeze rustled the leaves above them, and birds chirped cheerful songs.

When they were almost finished, Ollie spotted something special - a beautiful purple bottle that sparkled like a jewel in the sunlight. His eyes grew wide. "Ooh, pretty!" he whispered.

For a moment, Ollie thought about hiding the bottle behind his back. It was so shiny and beautiful! But then he remembered Grandma Willow's kind words about working together and taking turns.

Ollie took a deep breath and felt his heart beating fast. He looked at the purple bottle, then at Grandma Willow, then back at the bottle.

"Grandma Willow," Ollie said slowly, "I found this really pretty bottle. Part of me wants to keep it, but I know it should go in the recycling bin."

Grandma Willow's eyes twinkled with pride. "Thank you for being honest with me, Ollie. That shows real integrity - doing the right thing even when it's hard."

"What's integrity?" asked Ollie, tilting his furry head.

"Integrity means being truthful and doing what's right, even when no one is watching," explained Grandma Willow. "You could have hidden that bottle, but you chose to tell me the truth instead."

Ollie felt warm and proud inside, like sunshine filling his chest. "It feels good to be honest," he said with a big smile.

"And you know what?" said Grandma Willow with a wink. "When we recycle this bottle, it might become something even more beautiful - like a new bottle for someone else to enjoy!"

Together, they mindfully placed the purple bottle in the blue bin. Ollie listened to the gentle *clink* as it settled with the other bottles.

"We make a great team," said Grandma Willow, giving Ollie a gentle pat. "You showed integrity today, and that makes me very proud."

Ollie's tail wiggled with joy. As they sat together on the peaceful porch, watching butterflies dance around the flowers, he felt happy knowing he had done the right thing.

"Being honest feels even better than keeping pretty things," Ollie said contentedly.

Grandma Willow hugged him close. "That's the most beautiful thing of all, dear Ollie."

The End.
