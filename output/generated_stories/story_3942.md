# Kora Koala’s Balcony Banner

Kora was a calm koala who loved her sunny balcony garden. The garden was full of gentle things. Soft mint leaves brushed her paws. Tiny bells hung from a little string and went ting, ting in the wind. The air smelled like sweet basil and wet soil. Kora took a deep breath and smiled.

“Good morning, little leaves,” she whispered. “I am happy you are here.”

Kora had a kind heart and quiet eyes. She liked to sip eucalyptus tea and listen to the city birds. Pigeons cooed on the railing. A busy bee buzzed near the flowers, zzzzz, like a tiny motor. The balcony felt safe and gentle.

One bright morning, Kora had an idea. “I will make a Friendship Banner!” she said. “A banner to welcome everyone who visits the balcony.”

She set out a basket of craft things. There were paper shapes—circles, triangles, and hearts. There were soft yarns—blue, green, and sunshine yellow. There were buttons that clicked like little pebbles. She had glue that smelled like sweet apples and a pair of round scissors.

Just then, Kora heard a faint tap-tap. A small snail peeped around a pot. “Hello,” said the snail. “I am Nib. I move slow.”

Kora smiled. “Hello, Nib. I am Kora. I move calm. Would you like to help me craft?”

Nib’s feelers wiggled happily. “Yes, but I am slow. Is that okay?”

“It is more than okay,” Kora said. “Slow is a good speed.”

They began to craft. Kora cut shapes softly. Snip, snip. Nib placed a heart on the string. Plop. The bell went ting in the wind. Kora hummed a little tune, “La-la-la.” The balcony felt warm with gentle busy sounds.

Soon, Flutter the butterfly drifted down. Flutter’s wings were not the same. One wing was big with purple spots. The other was small with golden specks. Flutter hovered near the banner.

“Hi,” said Flutter, voice like a whisper. “I flap in a wobbly way. Can I help, too?”

Kora nodded. “Your wings are beautiful. Wobbly is welcome.”

Nib smiled. “We can make spots and specks on the banner!”

Flutter tapped tiny dots of paint. Tap, tap, tap. Purple and gold sparkled in the sunlight.

Then Pippo the pigeon hopped closer. Pippo had a raspy coo, like gravel and honey. “Coo-rrr,” he said. “I sing loud. Is loud okay?”

Kora patted a pot of mint. “All sounds have a place. Loud can be joyful.”

Pippo puffed with pride and brought a shiny bottle cap. “A gift,” he cooed. “For the banner.”

They glued the cap in the center. It shined like a little moon.

As they worked, a tiny ant line marched by. The ants moved quick-quick-quick. Nib watched with wide eyes. “I am not quick,” he said softly.

Kora touched Nib’s shell. “Quick and slow can both make beautiful things.”

Nib took a deep breath. “Okay,” he said. “I will place one more heart.”

Flutter fluttered near the string. “Sometimes my small wing gets tired,” she said.

Kora nodded gently. “We can rest. Resting is part of making.”

They paused. The sun warmed their backs. The mint leaves smelled fresh. Pippo’s feathers tickled Kora’s arm. The city sounds hummed far below.

After a sip of tea and a tiny rest, Kora said, “Let’s add our names.”

Nib traced a soft line with leaf juice. Flutter dotted tiny spots. Pippo pressed his stamp—coo!—with his foot. Kora wrote each name carefully: Kora, Nib, Flutter, Pippo.

Just then, Breeze the wind swirled in. Whoosh! The banner lifted and twirled like a ribbon. The little bells sang, ting-ting!

“Oh!” cried Flutter. “It moves like me!”

Nib laughed. “And it rests when the wind rests. Like me!”

Pippo puffed. “And it shines, like my song!”

Kora’s quiet eyes sparkled. “Our banner shows all of us,” she said. “Different, together.”

They stepped back to look. The banner had big shapes and tiny dots, slow-placed hearts, and a shiny cap in the middle. It was not the same on each side. It was wobbly here, smooth there, bright here, soft there. It was beautiful.

A ladybug landed on the railing and stared. “I have different spots from my sister,” she said. “Can I be here?”

Kora nodded. “Yes. Your spots are you.”

The ladybug smiled and rested by the mint.

Nib whispered, “Sometimes I feel strange because I am slow.”

Flutter added, “And I flap in my own way.”

Pippo cooed, “And I sing raspy.”

Kora held the string of the banner and spoke softly. “Being different makes our garden big and bright. We accept each other. We listen. We make space. When we do that, we make something wonderful—like this banner.”

Nib took another deep breath. “I feel warm inside,” he said.

Flutter dipped in the air. “I feel brave.”

Pippo bobbed his head. “I feel proud.”

Kora smiled. “I feel calm and happy.”

They hung the banner high across the balcony. It waved gently. The sun kissed the colors. The mint and basil swayed. The bells sang a soft song. Friends from near and far peeked in—an ant, a ladybug, a shy moth, and a busy bee. Each one found a spot to sit or hover. No one had to be the same. Everyone was welcome.

Kora poured tiny cups of eucalyptus tea and mint water. “Here,” she said, “for all our helpers.”

They clinked cups—clink, clink—and watched the banner dance.

Kora whispered the lesson out loud, so the garden could hear. “We accept each other as we are. Our differences are not problems. They are gifts. When we craft with care and listen with love, we can make beautiful things together.”

The balcony felt extra sunny. The friends sat close, happy and still. The banner swayed, telling their story to the breeze.

The End.
