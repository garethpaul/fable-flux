# Dash the Dolphin at Dinner Time

Dash was a playful dolphin who loved to splash, laugh, and sing. One sunny day, Dash visited his friend Mia for a special dinner in her bright dining room. The room smelled like warm bread and sweet carrots. The table was smooth and shiny. A little plant sat in the center, green and happy.

“Welcome, Dash!” Mia said with a big smile. “We’re having a kindness dinner.”

Dash clapped his flippers. “Splash-tastic! I love dinner.”

Mia’s dad brought in a tray. It had bowls of veggie soup, a basket of bread, and a pitcher of water. The soup steamed and smelled like tomatoes and herbs. Dash took a deep breath. “Mmm!”

At the table sat Mia, her dad, her baby brother Ben, and a guest named Tilly the Turtle. Tilly was slow and careful. She wore a tiny bow on her shell.

“Hi, Tilly!” Dash said. “Let’s eat fast! I’m so hungry.”

Tilly smiled softly. “I eat slowly,” she said. “I like to chew and rest. My shell is heavy.”

Dash felt his tummy rumble. He wanted the bread basket right away. He reached fast, but his flipper bumped the pitcher. The water wobbled. “Woah!” Splash! Cold water spilled on the table. The green plant got a shower.

Ben squeaked, “Uh-oh!”

Dash frowned. “I didn’t mean to. I’m just so hungry.” His face felt hot. His heart thumped. He wanted to shout at the pitcher, at the table, at the slow waiting. He took a big breath in. His chest rose like a wave. He let the breath out, slow and quiet, like bubbles. Mia whisper-giggled, “Bubble breaths help when we feel mad.”

Dash tried again. “In… and out…” He felt a little calmer. “Sorry, everyone,” he said. “I can help.”

Mia’s dad handed Dash a soft towel. “Thank you for saying sorry,” he said. “Let’s clean together.” Dash stroked the table with the towel. The water made tiny rainbow dots in the light. The plant’s leaves dripped, plip-plop.

Tilly reached for her soup spoon very slowly. Dash watched. His flipper twitched. He wanted to rush, but he remembered his bubble breaths. In… out… He waited. He listened to the gentle clink-clink of spoons and the soft hum of the fridge. He smelled warm bread. He heard Ben giggle and tap his cup.

Mia said, “This is our kindness dinner. We practice patience and care.”

Dash nodded. “I’m trying.”

After a few sips, Mia’s dad said, “We also care for our home, our bodies, and our big ocean home.” He placed a small green basket on the table. “This is for food scraps. Later we will compost them. It helps the earth.”

Dash peered inside. “Com-post?” he asked.

“Compost,” Tilly said, smiling. “Old peels and crumbs turn into new soil. It’s like magic that helps plants.”

Dash’s eyes widened. “That is splash-tastic!”

Mia passed the bread basket to Dash. He reached slowly this time. He broke a piece in half. Crumbs rained softly onto his plate. He saved the crumbs for the green basket. “I can care for the earth,” he said. “Little things help.”

Then Ben banged his spoon. Soup splashed on Dash’s flipper. It was warm and wet. Dash’s cheeks felt hot again. His flipper wanted to slam the table. He heard a shout inside his head: “No more splashes!”

Dash paused. He closed his eyes. In… out… in… out… bubble breaths. He felt his body soften, like a calm wave. “It’s okay,” he said gently to Ben. “Splashes happen.”

Ben smiled and offered Dash a napkin. “Sowwy,” Ben said.

“Thank you,” Dash said, wiping his flipper. His voice felt friendly again.

After dinner, Mia’s dad brought out a small bowl of apple slices. “We practiced patience,” he said. “Now let’s practice sharing.”

Dash took one slice and passed the bowl to Tilly. “I can wait,” he said. “I can share.”

Tilly nodded. “And I can be slow. That is okay.” She chewed, crunch, crunch, with a happy face.

Mia pointed to the green basket. “Let’s put our cores in there.”

Dash placed his apple core in the basket. He looked at the plant in the center of the table. The leaves were dry again. He touched one gently. “Sorry for the splash,” he whispered. “We will help you grow.”

Mia smiled. “That is tolerance,” she said. “We all go at different speeds. We all make spills. We all can learn.”

Dash tilted his head. “Tol-er-ance?”

“It means we are kind and patient with differences,” Mia said. “We listen. We don’t yell. We give space.”

Dash felt proud. “I can do that. When I feel mad, I can use bubble breaths. I can wait. I can help the earth. I can practice, practice, practice.”

Tilly tapped her spoon, clink. “And I can say thank you for patience,” she said.

Ben waved his spoon. “Team!” he cheered.

They all put their hands, flippers, and little spoon together. “Team Tolerance!” they said, laughing.

Dash looked around the cozy dining room. It smelled like apples and warm bread. It sounded like soft giggles and gentle clinks. His heart felt calm, like the ocean at sunset.

“I had a good dinner,” Dash said. “Not just food for my tummy, but food for my mind and heart.”

Mia’s dad nodded. “When we practice patience, care for the earth, and breathe through big feelings, we feel well.”

Dash smiled. “Tomorrow, I will teach my ocean friends bubble breaths. We will make a seaweed compost pile. We will be kind, even when it’s slow.”

They cleaned up together, sorted scraps, and watered the plant with the rest of the pitcher. The plant looked extra shiny. Everyone felt proud.

Dash wiggled his flippers. “Thank you for the kindness dinner,” he said. “I learned that tolerance makes room for everyone.”

Mia and Tilly said together, “That’s the best kind of dinner.”

The End.
