# Miko Mouse and the Porch of Peace

Miko the kind mouse loved to sit on the peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like warm wood and lemon soap. A soft breeze tickled Miko’s ears. The sun made tiny gold dots on the floor. Chirp, chirp, sang the birds. Tap, tap, went Miko’s little feet.

“Good morning, porch,” Miko said with a smile. “I am calm. I am ready.”

Miko liked calm. Calm felt like a cozy blanket. Calm felt like a slow, deep breath. In and out. In and out.

Just then, a little ant hurried past Miko’s toes. “Oh no! Oh no!” the ant squeaked. “I lost the crumb for my family!”

Miko put a gentle paw on the porch. “Hello, friend. Let’s use calm. When we are calm, we can think and help. Breathe with me. In and out.”

The ant copied Miko. In and out. The ant’s tiny shoulders dropped. “Thank you. I can look now,” the ant said.

Miko looked too. The porch smelled like sweet bread crumbs. Miko listened. Crunch. A crumb! It was hiding under the porch chair.

“There it is,” Miko said softly. “I will roll it to you.”

Miko used a dry leaf, like a tiny shovel. Scritch, scritch. The crumb rolled free. The ant clapped its tiny hands. “You are kind! Your calm helped me see,” the ant said, and carried the crumb away.

Miko sat back on the step. The breeze sighed. A neighbor leaf skittered by. Everything felt peaceful again.

Then a small bluebird landed on the railing. Her wings fluttered fast. “Tweet! My song won’t come out right. I feel all twisty,” she peeped.

Miko nodded with care. “It is okay to feel twisty. Let’s talk. What do you want your song to say?”

The bluebird blinked. “I want to say, ‘Good day, sky! Good day, friends!’ But my voice wobbles.”

“Let’s use calm,” Miko said. “We can tell a little story to your breath. First, soft voice. Then big voice.”

Miko put a paw on his chest. “In and out. Small song first.”

The bluebird tried. Tweet… tweet. The notes were tiny and sweet.

“That sounds lovely,” Miko said. “Now, let’s make a story. Once there was a brave bluebird who sang to the morning. The sun listened. The trees listened. The friends listened. The song grew strong.”

The bluebird puffed up. She sang the story, slow and clear. Tweet, trill, tweet! The sound floated like a ribbon in the air. The porch felt warm and happy.

“I did it!” the bluebird cheered. “Thank you for talking with me and telling a story. Calm helped my song.”

Miko smiled and patted the railing. “You did the singing. Calm helped you hear your own brave voice.”

The day went on. The porch smelled like fresh mint now. Somewhere, a bell went ding-dong. Miko rocked on the step and hummed.

Suddenly, a soft thump came from the porch basket. A tiny bunny peeked out, ears flopping. “Hi,” the bunny whispered. “My kite string is stuck on a nail. I pulled and pulled. Now the string is a knot. I feel grumpy.”

Miko scooted closer. “Thank you for telling me. When we feel grumpy, we can still use calm. Let’s breathe. In and out. Then we can think of a plan.”

The bunny tried a breath. The grumpy feeling got smaller.

“Good job,” Miko said. “Now, we will be resourceful. That means we will use what we have. I have a paperclip, a button, and a ribbon in my pocket.”

Miko showed the little things. Click went the paperclip. “The paperclip can lift the knot. The button can hold the loop. The ribbon can be a new tail.”

Together, they worked slowly. Miko used the clip to pick, pick, pick at the knot. The bunny held the string steady. The porch smelled like clean air and dust, and the wood felt smooth under their paws.

“At your pace,” Miko said. “Calm hands. Calm hearts.”

Pick, pick. Wiggle, wiggle. The knot came loose. Miko tied the ribbon to the kite. It fluttered and made a happy shhh sound.

“You did it!” the bunny laughed. “I want to tell this story at snack time.”

“That is a wonderful idea,” Miko said. “Stories help us remember. Stories help us share. Stories help us stay calm next time.”

The bluebird returned and sang a proud little song for the bunny. The ant marched by and waved with a crumb on its back.

Miko looked at his friends. “Today, we used calm. We talked about our feelings. We told stories. We used simple things to solve big problems.”

The friends nodded. The porch glowed gold in the afternoon light. The breeze felt like a hug.

Miko took one more deep breath. “When I feel wiggly or worried,” he said, “I can be calm. I can talk. I can tell a story. I can look around and use what I have. Then, I can help, and I can be helped.”

“Me too,” said the bluebird.

“Me too,” said the bunny.

“Me too!” squeaked the ant.

They all sat together on the peaceful front porch. They listened to the leaves whisper. They watched the sky turn soft and pink. Their hearts were quiet and strong.

The End.
