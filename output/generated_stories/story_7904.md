# Bella Bird Finds Her Voice

In a cozy living room filled with soft cushions and colorful toys, lived a little yellow bird named Bella. The room smelled like fresh flowers from the garden outside, and warm sunlight streamed through the big windows. Bella loved this special place where she could practice her singing every day.

But today, Bella felt worried. Her tiny heart was beating fast as she perched on her favorite red pillow. Tomorrow was the big Spring Concert, and all the animals in the neighborhood would be there to listen.

"Tweet, tweet," Bella tried to sing, but her voice came out very quiet and shaky. She looked around the empty living room and sighed. "I wish I could sing loud and beautiful like the other birds," she whispered to herself.

Just then, Mama Bird flew into the room and landed beside her. "Good morning, my little songbird! Are you ready for your practice today?"

Bella's feathers drooped down. "Mama, I don't think I can sing in the concert tomorrow. My voice is too small, and I get scared when I think about all those animals watching me."

Mama Bird gently touched Bella's wing with her own. "Oh, sweet Bella, what do you need to feel better about singing?"

Bella thought for a moment. This was her chance to speak up for herself! "Well," she said slowly, "I think I need more practice time. And maybe... maybe I could practice in front of just one friend first?"

"That's wonderful thinking!" Mama Bird chirped proudly. "You're being very brave by telling me what you need. Let's call your friend Benny Bunny to come listen."

Soon, Benny Bunny hopped into the living room, his soft gray ears bouncing. "Hi, Bella! I heard you want to practice singing!"

Bella felt her tummy flutter with butterflies, but she took a deep breath. "Yes, Benny. I'm nervous about the concert, but I want to try singing for you first. Would that be okay?"

"Of course!" Benny said, settling down on a blue cushion. "I love hearing you sing!"

Bella stood tall on her pillow and began to sing. "La-la-la, the sun is bright! La-la-la, what a beautiful sight!" Her voice started quiet but grew stronger with each note.

"That was amazing!" Benny clapped his paws together. "Your voice sounds like sunshine!"

Bella felt warm and happy inside. "Thank you, Benny! I asked for what I needed, and now I feel much better."

The next day at the Spring Concert, Bella remembered how good it felt to speak up for herself. When she felt nervous again, she raised her wing and said to the concert leader, "Excuse me, could I take three deep breaths before I start?"

"Of course, little one," smiled the wise old owl leading the concert.

Bella breathed deeply three times, just like she had asked. Then she sang her heart out, her voice clear and sweet like morning bells. All the animals cheered and clapped!

After the concert, Mama Bird hugged Bella tight. "I'm so proud of you for asking for what you needed, sweetheart. That's called self-advocacy, and it helped you succeed!"

Bella beamed with pride. She had learned that speaking up for herself wasn't scary – it was helpful! From that day on, whenever Bella needed something, she remembered to use her voice not just for singing, but for asking too.

The End.
