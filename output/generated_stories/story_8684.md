# Flossy Helps in the Laundry Room

Flossy was a gentle deer fawn with soft, speckled fur and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, Flossy trotted into the community laundry room. The room was warm and busy. Machines hummed. Water swished. Soap smelled like fresh lemons. Flossy twitched her nose. “Mmm, clean and cozy,” she said.

Neighbors came and went with baskets. Ms. Owl folded tiny socks. Mr. Raccoon sorted colors and whites. Baby Squirrel giggled at the turning washers. The floor was shiny and cool on Flossy’s little hooves.

Suddenly, a loud CLUNK! made everyone jump. One big washing machine stopped spinning. Its light blinked red. A small puddle began to spread. Drip, drip, drip. The floor got slippery.

“Oh dear,” said Ms. Owl. “This is not safe.” She looked worried. Mr. Raccoon frowned. “I need to wash blankets for the shelter,” he said. “They must be warm and clean tonight.”

Flossy’s heart felt tingly and brave. “We can help,” she said softly. “We can do community service. We can work together!”

Baby Squirrel looked at Flossy with wide eyes. “How?” he asked.

Flossy took a calm breath. “First, safety,” she said. “We keep everyone well.” She looked at the puddle. “We need towels.” She hopped to the shelf and nudged a basket with her nose. “Here are old towels!”

Ms. Owl nodded. “Good thinking.” Together, they spread towels on the puddle. Flossy pressed with her hooves. Squish, squish. The water soaked in. The floor felt less slippery.

Mr. Raccoon pointed to a sign. “The rule says: If a machine breaks, turn it off and tell the helper.” Flossy blinked. “Justice means fair rules for everyone,” she said. “We follow the rule to help all.”

Mr. Raccoon turned off the machine. Click. Flossy trotted to the desk and rang the little bell. Ding! “Excuse me,” she said kindly. “A washer is broken. We need help, please.”

The Laundry Helper, a friendly beaver named Ms. Beaver, arrived with a tool belt. “Thank you for telling me,” she said. “That was responsible.” She put up a bright yellow sign that read, “Wet Floor.” She smiled. “You kept everyone safe. That is wellness.”

Flossy felt warm and proud. “What about the blankets?” she asked.

Ms. Beaver checked her list. “We can share the other machines,” she said. “But it will take time.”

Flossy flicked her ears. “We can make a plan,” she said. “A fair plan, so everyone gets a turn.”

Ms. Owl found a marker. “Let’s make a turn chart,” she hooted. On a big piece of paper, they drew little boxes. Flossy added tiny pictures: a sock, a towel, a blanket. “We take turns,” Flossy said. “That is fair. That is justice.”

Neighbors gathered. “I have only one small load,” said Ms. Hedgehog. “Mr. Raccoon can go first.” She smiled. “The shelter needs blankets.” Everyone agreed.

Flossy wagged her tail. “Thank you for being kind,” she said. “Compassion means we care about others.”

They got to work. Swish went the washers. Fwump went the dryers as warm air whooshed. The room smelled like soap and sunshine. Flossy helped Baby Squirrel carry a tiny basket. “It feels heavy,” Baby Squirrel said.

“Let’s lift together,” said Flossy. “Bend knees. Careful steps.” They counted, “One, two, three.” They set the basket down safely. Baby Squirrel beamed. “We did it!”

Flossy checked the chart. “Mr. Raccoon’s blankets are next,” she said. Mr. Raccoon placed the blankets inside. “Thank you, friends,” he said. “You are helping many animals stay warm.” The machine hummed. Hmmm. Flossy listened. “Happy humming,” she giggled.

Ms. Beaver fixed the broken washer with a twist and a tap. “All done,” she said. “You can use this one again.”

Flossy clapped her hooves. “Great job!” She looked around. “We have one more thing,” she said. “Let’s do a clean-up party!” Everyone cheered.

They wiped the counters. They swept the floor. Whish, whish went the broom. They washed the folding table with warm, soapy water. Bubbles tickled Flossy’s nose. “Ah-choo!” she sneezed. Everyone laughed.

At last, the blankets were fluffy and dry. Mr. Raccoon hugged them close. “So soft,” he said. “So warm.” He looked at Flossy. “You led us with care.”

Flossy’s cheeks felt rosy. “We all helped,” she said. “Community service means we work together to help our neighbors. It keeps us safe, healthy, and happy.”

Ms. Owl added, “And we follow fair rules, so everyone gets a turn.” Ms. Beaver nodded. “That is justice.”

Baby Squirrel snuggled a small towel. “Helping feels good,” he whispered. Flossy nuzzled him gently. “Helping is good,” she said. “Helping brings wellness to our hearts and to our home.”

They put away the tools. They rolled up the towels. The room was clean and bright. The machines purred. Purr, purr, purr. The lemony smell floated like a friendly cloud.

Flossy stood by the door and waved with her hoof. “Today we learned,” she said. “When there is a problem, we can be calm, be kind, make a plan, and help together. That is community service.”

Everyone waved back. “Thank you, Flossy!” they called.

Flossy stepped into the sunshine, feeling light and happy. Her heart hummed like a washer. Hmmm. She whispered, “I can help. We can all help.”

The End.
