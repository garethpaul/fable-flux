# Pebble the Penguin’s Dinner Talk

Pebble was a cheerful penguin with shiny black feathers and a bright orange beak. Pebble loved to waddle and wiggle, hum little songs, and say kind words. Today was special. Pebble was hosting a big dinner in the dining room. The room smelled like warm bread and sweet berries. The long table had colorful napkins, clinking cups, and a bowl of fish-shaped crackers that made Pebble grin.

“Welcome, friends!” Pebble chirped, as guests waddled in and took their seats. There was Tilly the Turtle, slow and gentle. There was Bibi the Bear, soft and fuzzy. There was Niko the Nightingale, with a sweet singing voice. And there was Lolo the Lamb, fluffy and shy.

Pebble clapped flippers. “We will eat and talk together. We will listen. We will share. We will be kind.”

Everyone smiled. Pebble passed a basket. “Warm bread?” Pebble asked.

“Sí, por favor,” said Lolo the Lamb, her voice soft as a cloud.

Bibi blinked. “What did Lolo say?”

Tilly raised a slow flipper. “She said, ‘Yes, please,’ but in Spanish.”

Niko chirped, “Languages are like songs! Some words sound new, but they can still mean kind things.”

Pebble’s eyes sparkled. “We can learn words together!” Pebble turned to Lolo. “Cómo se dice ‘thank you’?”

“Gracias,” Lolo said, smiling.

Pebble tried the word. “Grah-see-ahs.” Everyone giggled happily.

They began to eat. Crunch went the crackers. Mmm went the berries. Slurp went the soup. The dining room felt warm and cozy, like a soft blanket.

But then, two flippers reached for the same shiny cup. Bibi’s big paw and Niko’s small wing touched the cup together. “I saw it first,” said Bibi.

“I need it to sip,” said Niko.

Pebble felt a little twist in the tummy. “Uh-oh,” Pebble whispered. Pebble took a deep breath, in and out, like gentle waves. “We can solve this,” Pebble said. “We can use kind words and listening ears.”

Tilly nodded slowly. “We can take turns.”

Niko’s feathers ruffled. “But I’m very thirsty.”

Bibi crossed arms. “But I am too.”

Pebble waddled closer and spoke in a calm voice. “Let’s negotiate. That means we find a way to share.” Pebble pointed to the window. “Let’s count to five for Niko’s sip, then five for Bibi’s sip.”

Niko looked at Bibi. Bibi looked at Niko. Pebble smiled. “We can do it together. I will count.”

Niko sipped while Pebble counted, “One, two, three, four, five.” Then Bibi sipped. “One, two, three, four, five.” The cup made soft clinks on the table. Niko’s eyes brightened. Bibi’s shoulders relaxed.

“That worked,” said Bibi.

“Gracias,” Niko sang to Bibi.

Bibi tried the word. “Gra… see… ahs.” Everyone cheered.

Pebble felt warm and brave. This is tolerance, Pebble thought. We are patient. We accept different needs and words.

After more munching, a tiny accident happened. Lolo dropped a spoon. Clatter! Some soup splashed onto Pebble’s napkin. Lolo’s ears drooped. “Lo siento,” she whispered, eyes shiny with tears.

Pebble dabbed the napkin and smiled gently. “It’s okay. You said ‘I’m sorry’ in Spanish. Thank you for saying sorry.”

Tilly added, “We all make little oops.”

Niko softly sang, “Oops can turn to okay.”

Bibi passed a clean napkin. “Here you go.”

Lolo sniffled and smiled. “Gracias.”

Pebble felt the room get cozy again. The cinnamon smell of warm bread floated back. The soft hum of Niko’s song filled the air like sunshine.

Pebble stood up and clapped flippers again. “Let’s play a word game! We will each teach one kind word in any language we know.”

Niko sang, “In French, merci means thank you.”

Tilly said, “In English, please is a magic word.”

Lolo said, “In Spanish, amigo means friend.”

Bibi thought hard. “In Bear, a big smile means ‘I care.’”

Everyone laughed. Pebble added, “In Penguin, a happy wiggle means ‘I’m glad you’re here.’” Pebble wiggled. The chairs creaked, and the table gently thumped. The friends wiggled too. Wiggle-wiggle-wiggle!

Then came the last plate of berries. Only one plate. Pebble looked around. Everyone wanted some.

Pebble said, “Let’s ask and listen. Who is still hungry?”

Niko raised a wing. “A little.”

Bibi raised a paw. “A little.”

Tilly blinked slowly. “A little.”

Lolo nodded shyly. “A little.”

Pebble smiled. “We can share. We can make a plan.” Pebble counted the berries. “One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight.” Pebble gently moved the berries onto small saucers. “Two for each friend.”

Everyone agreed. “Yes!” The sweet red berries popped like tiny fireworks of flavor. Pop! Pop!

As they finished, Pebble looked at each friend. “Today, we learned to be patient. We learned to take turns. We learned new words. We learned to listen when someone is different. That is called tolerance. Tolerance is being gentle and fair, even when things feel new or hard.”

Lolo smiled. “Gracias, Pebble.”

Niko sang, “Merci, Pebble.”

Bibi rumbled, “Thank you, Pebble.”

Tilly nodded. “Thank you.”

Pebble felt proud and peaceful. “You are all my amigos, my friends.”

The dining room was quiet and happy. The cups were still. The table was clean. Pebble blew out a tiny birthday candle left from dessert. Puff! A soft sweet smell drifted up like a whisper.

“Let’s remember our dinner talk,” Pebble said. “When we meet someone new, we can listen. We can share. We can learn their words. We can solve problems together. We can be kind.”

Everyone gave a gentle cheer. Pebble’s heart felt like a warm light. The dinner was done, and the friendship was strong.

The End.
