# Rosie Rabbit's Special Morning

Rosie Rabbit lived in a cozy burrow with her mama and papa. Every morning, Rosie loved to hop around and play, but there was one thing she didn't like very much - taking care of herself in the bathroom.

"Time to brush your teeth, Rosie!" called Mama Rabbit from the kitchen.

Rosie wrinkled her little pink nose. "Do I have to?" she asked, dragging her fluffy feet across the soft carpet.

When Rosie looked in the bathroom mirror, she saw her messy fur and sleepy eyes. Her teeth felt fuzzy, and her paws were dirty from yesterday's adventures in the garden.

"I don't want to brush my teeth," Rosie said to her reflection. "It takes too long!"

But then Rosie remembered something special. Today was the day of the Great Forest Storytelling Circle! All the woodland animals would gather to share their favorite tales. Rosie had been practicing her story about the magical carrot garden for weeks.

"Oh no!" Rosie gasped. "What if the other animals don't want to sit close to me if I'm not clean?"

Rosie thought about her friend Oliver Owl, who always smelled like fresh pine needles. And Bella Bear, whose fur was always so fluffy and clean. They took good care of themselves every day.

"I want to be a good friend," Rosie said honestly to herself. "Taking care of myself is important too!"

Rosie picked up her soft purple toothbrush. The minty toothpaste tasted cool and fresh on her tongue. She brushed in little circles, just like Mama had taught her. "Brush, brush, brush," she sang happily. The foamy bubbles tickled her whiskers, making her giggle.

Next, Rosie washed her face with warm, soapy water. The bubbles felt soft and slippery on her fur. She splashed gently, watching the water swirl down the drain with a happy gurgling sound.

"Now for my paws!" Rosie said, scrubbing away the dirt with a fluffy washcloth. The soap smelled like lavender flowers from Mama's garden.

Rosie combed her fur until it was smooth and shiny. She looked in the mirror again and smiled wide, showing her sparkling clean teeth.

"Wow!" she said. "I feel so fresh and happy!"

At the Storytelling Circle, all the animals gathered on the soft moss. When it was Rosie's turn, she hopped to the center confidently.

"I have a story about a magical garden," Rosie began, "but first, I want to share something important I learned today."

The animals leaned in close to listen.

"Taking care of ourselves shows we care about our friends too," Rosie said honestly. "When we're clean and fresh, we feel good, and others feel comfortable around us. It's a way of being generous with our friendship!"

Oliver Owl hooted approvingly. "That's very wise, Rosie!"

Bella Bear clapped her paws. "You're absolutely right! And you look wonderful today!"

Rosie told her magical garden story, and everyone loved it. Afterward, all the animals wanted to sit close to her during snack time.

That evening, Rosie couldn't wait to brush her teeth again.

"Taking care of myself makes me feel proud and happy," she told Mama and Papa. "And it helps me be a better friend!"

From that day on, Rosie looked forward to her bathroom time every morning. She learned that self-care wasn't just about being clean - it was about loving herself and showing kindness to others.

The End.
