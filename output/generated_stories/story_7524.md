# Tiko the Tortoise Finds Calm in the Shed

Tiko was a wise tortoise who loved the quiet garden shed. The shed smelled like fresh earth and warm wood. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. Soft dust danced in the light, like tiny stars. Tiko liked to think and breathe slowly there.

One morning, Tiko heard rattles and thumps. The rake rattled. The bucket thumped. A small mouse named Mina darted in, eyes wide. A blue bird named Bibi fluttered to the window ledge, wings shaky.

“What’s wrong?” Tiko asked in a soft, warm voice.

“My heart is bouncing,” Mina squeaked. “The wind is loud! The leaves scratch the door!”

“My feathers feel fizzy,” Bibi chirped. “I hear banging, and I feel scared.”

Tiko nodded. “Thank you for telling me. Let’s care for our bodies and our words. Wellness starts with calm.”

He took a slow breath. In… one, two, three. Out… one, two, three. “Will you breathe with me?”

Mina’s whiskers twitched. Bibi’s wings stilled. They breathed together. In… one, two, three. Out… one, two, three. The air smelled cool and leafy. The sound of the wind softened.

“I feel softer,” Bibi said, a tiny smile in her song.

“My tummy isn’t jumping,” Mina added.

Just then, the door creaked. A shadow moved. “Who’s there?” whispered Bibi, a little brave but still shaky.

A garden frog hopped into the light. He carried a shiny button, clink-clink. “Oh! I did not mean to scare you,” the frog said. “I am Fero. I lost my button in the wind. It rolled under the rake, and I bumped things. I make noise when I am busy.”

Tiko smiled kindly. “Hello, Fero. Thank you for telling us. We are practicing calm and kind words.”

Mina peeked out from behind a watering can. “I thought the noises were a storm monster,” she said.

Fero shook his head. “No monsters. Just a frog and a button.”

Bibi breathed again. “I got scared. But now I know.”

A new rattle came from the corner. A stack of pots wobbled. Ting, tong, ting! Mina squeaked and hopped backward. “Oh no! More noise!”

Tiko lifted his strong, gentle voice. “Let’s use our calm plan again. First, we pause. Then we breathe. Then we use brave, kind words. We can ask questions.”

They all paused. They breathed. In… one, two, three. Out… one, two, three.

Then Tiko called, “Hello, pots. Are you okay?”

From inside the pots came a tiny voice. “I am stuck!” It was Pip the cricket. “My leg is caught between two pots. I tried to wiggle, and they went ting, tong.”

Tiko took a small step forward. His shell brushed the floor with a soft shhh. “We will help. But we will help safely. Wellness means caring for our bodies and feelings.” He looked at his friends. “Mina, please hold the little pot so it doesn’t fall. Bibi, will you watch the window and tell us if the wind pushes the door again? Fero, keep holding your button so it doesn’t roll.”

“Yes,” Mina said, standing tall on tiny toes.

“Yes,” Bibi sang, brave and bright.

“Yes,” Fero croaked, firm and steady.

Tiko pressed his strong shell under the wobbling pots. “I will lift, slow and low.” He pushed with a small grunt. The bottom pot slid. Pip the cricket popped free. “Thank you!” piped Pip, rubbing his leg. “You were careful and kind.”

“We used peaceful words,” Tiko said. “We listened to feelings. We took slow breaths. That helps our minds and bodies.”

Bibi looked at the window. The wind made a soft hush-shush. “The wind sounds like ocean waves,” she said.

“It smells like wet leaves,” Mina added, nose wiggling.

Fero held up his button. “It shines like a tiny moon.”

Tiko’s eyes twinkled. “Words can help us feel better. We can use words to name sounds, smells, and feelings. That is language. Language can be a gentle blanket.”

Mina said, “I feel safe.”

Bibi chirped, “I feel brave.”

Fero croaked, “I feel calm.”

Pip sang a tiny note. “I feel grateful.”

They all smiled. Then the shed door banged once more. Bang!

Bibi fluffed her feathers. “That was loud. But I will try our plan.” She breathed in… one, two, three. Out… one, two, three. “Door, are you okay?” she asked.

The door squeaked a happy squeak as the wind settled. The sound was softer now. Everyone giggled.

Tiko nodded proudly. “Wellness is caring for our bodies, our minds, and our friends. We can be brave by being gentle. We can solve problems with peaceful talk. We can use kind words to share feelings. And we can take slow breaths anytime.”

The sun made gold stripes on the floor. The shed felt warm and safe. Tiko tucked in his feet and smiled at his friends.

“What did we learn today?” he asked.

Mina counted on her tiny paws. “Pause, breathe, speak kind.”

Bibi added, “Ask questions. Listen well.”

Fero said, “Work together. Move slow and safe.”

Pip chirped, “Use words to name feelings.”

Tiko nodded. “Yes. That is our wellness way.”

They sat for a quiet moment, listening to the soft wind and the tiny cricket song. Their hearts felt steady. Their bodies felt calm. Their words felt kind.

The End.
