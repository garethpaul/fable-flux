# Oliver's Fair Share Day

Oliver the otter loved playing in Papa's home office. The big wooden desk was perfect for sliding, and the soft carpet felt wonderful on his furry belly.

"Wheeee!" Oliver squeaked as he slid across the smooth desktop. His whiskers twitched with joy.

But today, something wasn't right. Oliver's little sister Luna sat crying by the bookshelf. Her tiny paws covered her eyes.

"What's wrong, Luna?" Oliver asked, waddling over on his short legs.

"Tommy Raccoon took my favorite red crayon," Luna sniffled. "He said finders keepers when I dropped it during our playdate. But that's not fair!"

Oliver's brown eyes grew wide. His fluffy tail twitched with concern. "That doesn't sound right," he said thoughtfully. "Let's think about this together."

Oliver climbed onto Papa's big office chair. It spun around slowly as he thought. "Luna, what would make this fair?" he asked.

Luna wiped her nose with her paw. "I just want my crayon back. It was Mama's special gift to me."

"Then let's go talk to Tommy," Oliver said bravely. "Sometimes friends need help understanding what's right."

The two otters waddled to the backyard where Tommy Raccoon was coloring. The red crayon was clutched tightly in his striped paws.

"Hi Tommy," Oliver said kindly but firmly. "We need to talk about Luna's crayon."

Tommy's masked face looked guilty. "But I found it on the ground," he mumbled.

Oliver sat down next to his friend. The grass felt cool and soft beneath them. "Tommy, if you dropped your favorite toy, how would you feel if someone kept it?"

Tommy's ears drooped. "I... I would feel sad," he admitted quietly.

"That's how Luna feels right now," Oliver explained gently. "Taking something that belongs to someone else, even if you find it, isn't fair. The right thing to do is give it back."

Luna stepped forward, her voice small but brave. "I'm not angry, Tommy. I just miss my special crayon."

Tommy looked at the red crayon in his paws. It felt heavy now, like it was made of sadness instead of wax. "I'm sorry, Luna," he said, holding out the crayon. "Here's your crayon back. I didn't mean to hurt your feelings."

Luna's face lit up like sunshine. "Thank you, Tommy! Would you like to color with me? We can share!"

Tommy's eyes sparkled. "Really? Even after I was mean?"

"Friends forgive each other," Luna said, taking Tommy's paw. "That's what makes friendship fair."

Oliver clapped his small paws together. "Now everyone feels good! That's what justice means - making sure everyone is treated fairly and kindly."

The three friends spent the afternoon coloring together in Papa's office. They took turns with all the crayons, making sure everyone had a chance to use their favorites.

When Papa came to check on them, he found beautiful drawings covering his desk. "What lovely artwork!" he said. "And what happy faces!"

"We learned about being fair today," Oliver said proudly. "When someone needs help standing up for what's right, good friends help them."

Papa smiled warmly. "That's a very important lesson, Oliver. Taking care of each other and doing what's right makes everyone feel safe and happy."

That night, as Oliver curled up in his cozy bed, he felt proud and peaceful. He had helped solve a problem fairly, and everyone ended up happy. His whiskers twitched with contentment as he drifted off to sleep, dreaming of red crayons and good friends.

The End.
