# Benny's Big Discovery

Benny the rabbit hopped down the creaky wooden stairs to his basement lab. His fluffy white ears twitched with excitement. The basement smelled like old books and fresh carrots. Colorful bottles lined the shelves, and a big wooden table sat in the middle.

"Today I'll make the best carrot juice ever!" Benny said, clapping his small paws together.

Benny loved mixing things in his lab. He put on his tiny safety goggles and got to work. First, he squeezed orange carrots into a big bowl. The juice was bright orange and smelled sweet.

"Perfect!" Benny smiled. But when he reached for his special sparkly powder, his elbow bumped the bowl. SPLASH! Orange juice spilled everywhere.

"Oh no!" Benny's ears drooped. His whiskers trembled. He felt angry heat rise in his chest. "This is terrible! My experiment is ruined!"

Benny wanted to stomp his feet and throw things. His heart beat fast like a drum. But then he remembered what Mama Rabbit taught him. When you feel angry, take three deep breaths.

"One... two... three," Benny breathed slowly. The angry feeling got smaller.

Just then, he heard tiny squeaking sounds. "Help! Help!" came little voices.

Benny looked down. Three baby mice were stuck in the sticky orange juice! They couldn't move their little paws or tails.

"Oh my!" Benny gasped. "Don't worry, little friends. I'll help you!"

Benny's anger disappeared completely. Now he felt worried about the mice. He gently picked up each tiny mouse with a soft cloth. The juice made them all orange and sticky.

"Thank you, Benny!" squeaked the smallest mouse. "We were so scared!"

"We came looking for food," said another mouse. "But we got stuck!"

Benny's heart felt warm and fuzzy. He wanted to help his new friends feel better. "Let me clean you up," he said kindly.

Benny filled a small bowl with warm water. He added gentle soap that smelled like lavender. Very carefully, he helped each mouse wash off the sticky juice. Their gray fur became fluffy and clean again.

"That feels so much better!" the mice cheered.

"Are you hungry?" Benny asked. His curiosity made him wonder what mice liked to eat.

"Yes!" all three mice said together.

Benny shared his lunch with them. He gave them tiny pieces of cheese and breadcrumbs. The mice's eyes sparkled with joy as they nibbled their food.

"You're so kind, Benny," said the biggest mouse. "Even though we made a mess in your lab."

Benny thought about this. "You know what? The spilled juice helped me find new friends! Sometimes accidents lead to good things."

The mice helped Benny clean up the lab. They used tiny mops and worked together. Soon everything was spotless and shiny.

"Can we come back tomorrow?" asked the smallest mouse.

"Of course!" Benny said happily. "We can do experiments together!"

As the mice scurried home, Benny felt proud. He had learned something important today. When bad things happen, taking deep breaths helps angry feelings go away. And being kind to others always makes everything better.

Benny hopped upstairs to tell Mama Rabbit about his new friends. His heart felt light and happy. Tomorrow would bring another fun adventure in his basement lab!

The End.
