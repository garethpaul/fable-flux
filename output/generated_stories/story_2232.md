# Gigi Tries Again

In a cozy bedroom with star stickers on the ceiling, a thoughtful giraffe named Gigi wiggled her long toes under a soft blue blanket. Her room smelled like clean laundry and warm cocoa. Moonlight made silver squares on the floor. On the rug lay a small foam ball and a tiny hoop that stuck to the closet door.

“I want to make a basket,” Gigi whispered to herself. “I can do it. I can try.”

Gigi sat up and hugged her favorite pillow. It was squishy and shaped like a cloud. She took the foam ball in her hoof and bounced it gently. Boing, boing, boing. The sound made her smile. She stood tall, her neck brushing the fluttery paper stars.

“Okay, ball,” she said. “Let’s go.”

Gigi aimed. She breathed in. She breathed out. She tossed the ball.

Thump. The ball hit the door and fell to the rug.

“Oh,” Gigi said softly. Her ears drooped. “I missed.”

She picked up the ball again. Boing, boing. She tried again.

Thump. Miss.

Again.

Thump. Miss.

Gigi’s cheeks felt hot. Her tail swished. Her heart made a fast beat in her chest. “This is hard,” she said. “I am mad. I am sad. I want to stomp.”

She looked at the ball on the rug. It looked back, quiet and round.

Gigi took a slow breath, just like Mama taught her. In… and out… like a sleepy balloon. She put her hoof over her heart. “I feel frustrated,” she said. “That is okay. I can calm down.”

She wiggled her ears. She shook her shoulders. She let her body feel softer, like her cloud pillow.

Knock, knock. Mama Giraffe peeked in. “Hi, Gigi,” she said in a warm voice. “I heard some thumps. Are you okay?”

Gigi nodded, then shook her head. “I am trying to play sports. I am trying to make a basket. I keep missing. I feel grumpy.”

Mama walked in and sat on the rug. The rug felt fuzzy under their hooves. “Thank you for telling me,” Mama said. “Trying new things can be hard. It is okay to feel grumpy. Do you want a hug?”

“Yes,” Gigi said. Mama gave a gentle hug. Gigi breathed in Mama’s sweet lavender smell and felt calmer.

“Do you want help?” Mama asked. “Or do you want to try again by yourself?”

Gigi thought. She was a thoughtful giraffe. She liked to understand things. “I want to try again,” she said. “But I want a tip.”

Mama smiled. “Good plan. Here is a tip: break it into little steps. Watch the hoop. Bend your knees. Use soft hands. Say, ‘I can try.’”

Gigi nodded. “Little steps,” she said. She felt a tiny spark in her chest. Not angry. Brave.

She picked up the foam ball. It felt light and a little squishy. She stood with her feet still. She looked at the hoop. She bent her knees. “I can try,” she whispered.

She tossed the ball.

Thump. It hit the rim and bounced out.

Gigi’s lip wobbled. “I almost did it,” she said. Her eyes felt wet.

Mama touched her shoulder. “Almost is good. Almost means you are close. Take a breath. Try again.”

Gigi took a balloon breath. In… out…

She looked at the hoop. “Watch the hoop,” she said. She bent her knees. “Soft hands.” She tossed.

The ball kissed the rim and fell through. Swish!

“I did it!” Gigi cheered. Her tail made happy circles. The swish sounded like a whispering star. Gigi clapped her hooves, and the foam made a soft pat-pat-pat.

Mama clapped too. “You tried. You calmed down. You used little steps. That is persistence.”

“Per-sis-tence,” Gigi said slowly. The word felt big and bright. “It means I keep going.”

“Yes,” Mama said. “You keep going, even when it is hard.”

Gigi picked up the ball again. “Again,” she said.

She missed the next one. Thump.

Gigi felt a pinch of grump. She put a hoof on her heart. “Hello, feeling,” she said. “I hear you. I can calm down.” She took a breath. She tried again.

Swish!

She missed. Thump.

She tried again.

Swish!

Gigi giggled. She was starting to understand her body and the ball. She could feel when her knees were bouncy. She could feel when her hands were soft. She could hear the gentle swish when it worked, like the hush of the night.

Soon, Gigi felt sleepy. Her eyelids were heavy. The moonlight looked like milk.

“One more,” she said. She aimed. She bent. She tossed.

Swish!

Gigi hugged her cloud pillow. “I kept going,” she said. “I can feel proud.”

Mama tucked the blanket under Gigi’s chin. “What did you learn tonight?” she asked.

Gigi yawned a big giraffe yawn. “I learned to calm my mad. I learned little steps. I learned to try and try. That is per-sis-tence.”

Mama kissed her forehead. “Yes. When things are hard, we can breathe, ask for help, and try again. That is how we grow.”

Gigi smiled. The foam ball rested by the hoop, quiet and round. The room was cozy and still.

“I can try again tomorrow,” Gigi whispered. “I can keep going.”

The stars on the ceiling glowed softly. The night felt kind. Gigi closed her eyes, proud and peaceful, and drifted to sleep.

The End.
