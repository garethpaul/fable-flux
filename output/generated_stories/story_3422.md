# Bramble's Big Hello

Bramble the rabbit sat on the peaceful front porch. The porch boards were warm from the morning sun. The air smelled like sweet clover and fresh rain. A tiny breeze tickled Bramble’s whiskers, and the rocking chair creaked, creak… creak… in a slow, comfy rhythm.

Today felt special. Bramble’s heart gave a brave thump. “Today,” Bramble said, “I will say hello to every neighbor who walks by.”

Bramble’s ears stood tall. Bramble was small, but determined. Sometimes words got stuck like pebbles. But Bramble took a big, brave breath. In… out… in… out. “I can do it,” Bramble whispered. “I can be me.”

Mama Rabbit poked her head out the blue door. She held a tray with carrot muffins. The sweet, cinnamon smell floated around the porch. “You can do it, Bramble,” Mama said, smiling. “Your hello is enough.”

Bramble nodded. “My hello is enough.”

Clip-clop, clip-clop! The first neighbor came down the sunny path. It was Miss Hen, with bright feathers and a basket of eggs. “Good morning!” she clucked.

Bramble’s tummy fluttered. The words felt shy. Bramble pressed paws on the porch boards. They felt smooth and warm. “H…hello,” Bramble said softly.

Miss Hen tilted her head. “Oh! A gentle hello. Thank you, dear.” She smiled and clucked away.

Bramble’s ears wiggled. “I did it,” Bramble whispered. The rocking chair creaked a happy tune.

Soon Mr. Turtle slid by, shell shining like a little green moon. He waved slowly. “Mornin’,” he hummed. His voice was deep and soft, like a drum.

Bramble took another big breath. “Hello, Mr. Turtle!” The words came out clear and bright.

Mr. Turtle grinned. “Strong voice, little friend. I like it.” He kept on gliding, swish, swish, through the grass.

Bramble smiled wide. The porch smelled like muffins and sunshine. The breeze hummed in the leaves. Bramble’s chest felt warm and tall.

Then a new sound came, tap-tap-ting! Tap-tap-ting! It was Pip the Woodpecker, hopping along the fence. Pip wore a shiny red cap and carried a tiny drum. “Hel-LA-LO!” Pip sang, jumping and spinning. “Do you want to sing hello too?”

Bramble’s nose twitched. “I like hello,” Bramble said. “But I don’t sing. I say it.”

Pip hopped closer. “Singing is fun!” Tap-tap-ting! “Say it loud! Say it fast! Hel-LA-LO!”

Bramble’s ears drooped for a moment. The porch felt bigger, and Bramble felt small again. “I don’t know if I can sing,” Bramble said. “My hello is small. My hello is me.”

Mama Rabbit set a muffin on a little plate. “Every hello is a good hello,” she said. “We can listen. We can learn. We can be ourselves.”

Pip blinked. “Oh,” Pip said softly. “I didn’t think about that.”

Bramble held the warm muffin. It felt safe like a tiny hug. “Pip,” Bramble said, “you can sing hello. And I can say hello. We can both be okay.”

Pip’s eyes sparkled. “Can I hear your hello?” he asked.

Bramble sat tall and felt the front porch under their paws. The rocking chair creaked a brave beat. Bramble breathed in slow, and out slow. “Hello,” Bramble said. Not loud. Not fast. Just steady, like a small bell.

Pip clapped his wings. “I like your hello,” he chirped. “It is calm. It is kind.” Pip tapped his drum softly, tap… tap… tap… and sang a gentle note to match.

Just then, a group of ducklings waddled by with Mama Duck. “Peep! Peep!” they cried. “We can’t find our leaf boat!”

Bramble stood. Determined ears pointed high. “We can help,” Bramble said. Bramble hopped down the porch steps. The grass felt cool and wet on Bramble’s paws. The air smelled leafy and bright.

By the porch, a tiny leaf boat hid under a fern. Bramble spotted it close to a shiny pebble. “Here it is,” Bramble said, lifting it carefully. “It is safe.”

“Quack-hooray!” Mama Duck smiled. “Thank you, brave rabbit.” The ducklings peeped happy peeps and paddled the leaf boat in a puddle, swish, swish.

Pip tapped a little cheer on his drum. “You were brave and calm,” he said.

Bramble felt strong inside. Helping felt warm. Saying hello felt steady. Being Bramble felt just right.

On the porch again, Bramble watched the path. The world sounded like birds and breeze and soft porch creaks. The world smelled like muffins and clover. The world felt friendly.

Pip sat on the railing. “Can I practice a quiet hello with you?” he asked.

Bramble nodded. “And I can practice a song hello with you?”

Pip grinned. “Yes!”

Together they tried. Pip took a soft breath. “Hello,” he whispered. Bramble hummed a tiny tune, “La-la… hello.” They giggled. The porch felt full of yes.

Mama Rabbit clapped. “Open hearts make big worlds,” she said.

Bramble’s whiskers bounced. “My hello is enough,” Bramble said. “And your hello is enough too.”

The sun slid higher. Neighbors waved and smiled. Bramble said hello in a calm voice. Pip sang hello in a tiny tune. Everyone was welcome on the peaceful front porch.

Bramble cuddled the warm air and the warm feeling inside. “I can do things in my way,” Bramble whispered. “I can try new things too. I am me, and that is good.”

The End.
