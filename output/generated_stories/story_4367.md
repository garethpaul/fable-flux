# Koko's Kitchen Friends

Koko the koala loved spending time in Grandma's warm kitchen. The sunshine streamed through the yellow curtains, and the air smelled like fresh-baked cookies. Koko was carefully arranging his favorite eucalyptus leaves on a plate when he heard a tiny voice.

"Excuse me," squeaked the voice. "Could I please have a crumb?"

Koko looked down and saw a little ant standing politely by his feet. The ant's name was Annie, and she had traveled all the way from Australia, just like Koko's family had long ago.

"Of course!" said Koko kindly. He broke off a small piece of cookie and placed it near Annie. "Would you like to sit with me?"

Annie's eyes sparkled. "Really? That would be wonderful!"

As they shared their snack, more friends appeared. First came Benny the butterfly from Mexico, his orange and black wings shimmering in the sunlight.

"¡Hola! Hello!" chirped Benny. "May I join you? I've been flying for so long, and this kitchen feels so cozy."

"Welcome, Benny!" said Koko, making space at the table.

Next, Penny the penguin waddled in from the cold outside. She had moved here from Antarctica and still wore her favorite blue scarf.

"Brrr! This kitchen is perfectly warm," Penny said gratefully. "Mind if I warm up with you all?"

Soon, their little group was chatting and laughing together. But when Sammy the snake slithered in from the garden, everyone suddenly went quiet.

"H-hello," hissed Sammy softly. "I don't suppose... could I join you? I promise I'm friendly."

Annie backed away nervously. "I'm not sure about snakes," she whispered.

Benny fluttered his wings anxiously. "Snakes make me scared," he admitted.

Koko noticed that Sammy's eyes looked sad. The snake started to turn away. "It's okay," Sammy said quietly. "I understand. I'll go eat alone."

"Wait!" called Koko gently. He thought about how lonely he had felt when his family first moved to this new place. "Sammy, would you like to tell us about yourself?"

Sammy's face brightened. "Well, I love sunbathing and I'm actually very gentle. I eat only fruits and vegetables. And I give the best warm hugs!"

"That sounds nice," said Koko. "But I can see my friends feel nervous. How about we start slowly? Maybe you could sit over there, and we can all talk together?"

Sammy nodded happily. "That's perfect! I don't want anyone to feel uncomfortable."

As they talked, Annie, Benny, and Penny learned that Sammy loved to tell funny jokes and knew amazing stories about different countries. He had traveled from India and knew songs in many languages.

"You're actually really funny!" giggled Annie.

"And your stories are amazing!" added Benny.

By the end of their kitchen party, Penny was brave enough to sit closer to Sammy. "Thank you for being patient with us," she said. "We learned that being different doesn't mean being scary."

Koko smiled as he looked around at all his new friends from different places around the world. "In this kitchen, everyone is welcome," he said warmly. "We can all be friends, even if we're different. And it's okay to feel nervous about new things – we just need to be kind and give each other space to feel comfortable."

As the sun set, all the friends helped clean up together, each one feeling happy and accepted.

"Same time tomorrow?" asked Sammy hopefully.

"Absolutely!" they all replied together.

The End.
