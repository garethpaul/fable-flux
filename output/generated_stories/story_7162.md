# Benny Bear's Big Art Day

In a bright, colorful art room, a little bear cub named Benny sat at a tiny table. The room smelled like crayons and paint. Rainbow pictures hung on every wall. Benny's fluffy brown fur was already spotted with purple paint.

"Today I will make the BEST picture ever!" Benny said, clapping his paws together. His black button nose twitched with excitement.

Benny grabbed red paint. Splash! He painted a big circle. Then he reached for blue. Splish! He added wiggly lines everywhere. Yellow paint went on top. Orange paint went on the sides.

"More colors! More paint!" Benny giggled. He was having so much fun that he didn't notice the mess growing bigger and bigger.

Miss Lily, the art teacher, walked over. She was a kind rabbit with gentle brown eyes. "Benny, sweetie, remember to take your time. Good art needs patience."

But Benny was too excited to listen. He grabbed green paint with both paws. SPLAT! Paint flew everywhere. It landed on his neighbor's paper.

"Oh no!" cried Maya, a little deer with spots. "My butterfly picture is ruined!" Tears filled her big brown eyes.

Benny looked at Maya's paper. Green paint covered her beautiful yellow and orange butterfly. His tummy felt heavy and sad.

"I... I'm sorry, Maya," Benny whispered. His ears drooped down. "I was going too fast."

Miss Lily hopped over with paper towels. "Accidents happen, Benny. But what can we do to fix this?"

Benny thought hard. His little bear brain worked and worked. Then his eyes lit up! "I know! Maya, can I help make your butterfly even more beautiful?"

Maya wiped her eyes. "How?"

"Green can be grass under your butterfly! And I can paint tiny flowers around it. We can work together!"

Maya's face brightened like sunshine. "Really? That sounds wonderful!"

Benny took a deep breath. This time, he moved slowly. He dipped his brush carefully in the paint. No splashing. No rushing. He painted small green blades of grass under Maya's butterfly.

"This is much better," Benny said, concentrating hard. "Slow and steady makes pretty art."

Maya painted little pink flowers next to Benny's grass. "You're right! When we take our time, everything looks nicer."

Soon, other friends came to help too. Sam the squirrel added purple dots. Zoe the zebra painted a bright sun. Everyone worked together, taking turns and sharing colors.

"Look!" Maya exclaimed. "Our picture is the most beautiful one in the whole room!"

Benny stepped back and smiled. The butterfly picture was amazing! It had green grass, pink flowers, purple dots, a yellow sun, and Maya's gorgeous butterfly in the middle.

"I learned something important today," Benny announced to his friends. "When I rush, I make mistakes. When I slow down and work with friends, everything turns out better!"

Miss Lily clapped her paws. "That's exactly right, Benny! Good art takes time and teamwork."

Benny hugged Maya. "Thank you for forgiving me. And thank you for letting me help fix my mistake."

"Thank you for saying sorry and helping me," Maya replied. "Our butterfly is perfect now!"

From that day on, Benny always remembered to slow down in art class. He learned that taking time made his pictures more beautiful. He learned that saying sorry when he made mistakes felt good. And best of all, he learned that working with friends made everything more fun.

The art room filled with happy laughter as all the friends cleaned up together, taking their time and helping each other put away the paints and brushes.

The End.
