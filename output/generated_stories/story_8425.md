# The Little List in the Treehouse

A kind mouse named Mimi lived in a cozy treehouse. The treehouse smelled like warm pine. Sunlight made golden stripes on the floor. The wind whispered, shhh, shhh, through the leaves. Mimi’s whiskers tickled as she smiled.

Mimi loved to help. She liked to sweep, stack books, and make tea. She said, “I want to make the treehouse extra nice today!” She wiggled her pink nose and clapped her tiny paws.

Her friend, Owl, hooted softly from a branch. “Hoo-hoo, Mimi. If you want to help, please follow my special list. It keeps us safe and neat.”

Mimi’s ears perked up. “A list? I can do that!”

Owl nodded and gave Mimi a small paper with four steps. The paper felt smooth and a little crinkly. It said:

1. Tie your tail with the safety ribbon.
2. Put toys in the blue box.
3. Wipe the table with the soft cloth.
4. Ask for help before using the tall ladder.

Mimi read the list out loud in a sing-song voice. “Tie tail, blue box, wipe table, ask for help.” She tapped each line with her tiny paw. “Easy-cheesy!”

Mimi started to hum. But then she saw a tower of blocks. “Oh! I will make a taller tower first!” She forgot to tie her tail. She forgot the blue box. She forgot the list.

Her tail swished and bumped the tower. CLATTER! Blocks rolled across the floor like little marbles. Mimi’s eyes went wide. “Oh no!”

She tried to fix it fast. She grabbed the tall ladder all by herself. The ladder wobbled and made a creaky sound. Mimi felt a flutter in her tummy. “This feels wobbly,” she whispered.

Owl flapped his wings and glided down. “Hoo-hoo, Mimi. Please stop. Remember the list.”

Mimi’s cheeks felt warm. “I forgot,” she said softly. “I wanted to help, but I didn’t follow the directions.”

Owl’s eyes were kind. “It’s okay to make a mistake. We can improve. Let’s use a strategy. We will read the list, do one step at a time, and then check.”

Mimi took a deep breath. In… out… She smiled a little. “One step at a time.”

She read the first line. “Tie your tail with the safety ribbon.” The ribbon was soft and red. Mimi tied a bow around her tail. It felt snug and safe. “Step one, done!”

She read the second line. “Put toys in the blue box.” Mimi picked up blocks, one-two-three. Click, clack, click. She listened to the happy sound of cleaning. “Step two, done!”

She read the third line. “Wipe the table with the soft cloth.” The cloth smelled like lemons. Swish, swish. The table shone. Mimi giggled. “Step three, done!”

She read the fourth line. “Ask for help before using the tall ladder.” Mimi looked at Owl. “Owl, can you please help me with the ladder?”

Owl nodded. “Yes, Mimi. Thank you for asking.” He held the ladder steady. Mimi climbed one, two, three steps. She reached a book on a high shelf. She put it back in its home. Tap! “All done!”

They both checked the list again. Mimi traced each line with her paw. “Tie tail? Check. Blue box? Check. Wipe table? Check. Ask for help? Check!”

The treehouse felt calm. The air was fresh and lemony. The floor was clear. The tall ladder was back in the corner. Mimi’s heart felt light and proud.

A tiny squirrel peeked in the window. “It looks so nice in here! How did you do it?”

Mimi said, “We followed directions. We used a plan. We did one step at a time. When I forgot, I stopped, listened, and tried again.”

Squirrel clapped. “That is a peaceful way!”

Mimi nodded. “It keeps us safe. It helps us improve.” She looked at Owl. “Thank you for the list. I am grateful.”

Owl smiled. “I am grateful for your kind heart, Mimi. You listened. You asked for help. That is smart.”

Mimi poured little cups of warm tea. The steam smelled sweet, like honey. They sipped together. Sip, sip. The afternoon light turned soft and pink.

Owl said, “What will you do next time you want to help?”

Mimi said, “I will read the directions. I will go step by step. If I forget, I will stop, breathe, and ask for help.”

Squirrel added, “And I will put toys in the blue box!”

They all laughed. The wind whispered, shhh, shhh, like a gentle song. The treehouse glowed with peace.

Mimi looked at the list and tucked it into a tiny frame on the wall. “Our little list,” she said, “helps us do big things.”

The End.
