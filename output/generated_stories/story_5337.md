# Kip Koala's Fair Snack Plan

Kip Koala sat quietly at the big wooden dining table. His soft gray fur felt warm in the sunny room. The dining room smelled like fresh eucalyptus leaves and toast from breakfast.

"Hmm," Kip said thoughtfully, looking around. He noticed something wasn't quite right. His little sister Penny's plate was empty, but his plate still had three yummy crackers on it.

Kip's mom was busy in the kitchen, washing dishes. *Clink, clink* went the plates as she cleaned them. Penny sat next to Kip, looking sadly at her empty plate.

"Penny, where are your crackers?" asked Kip gently.

"I dropped them on the floor," Penny whispered. "Now I'm still hungry, but I'm too scared to ask Mom for more."

Kip thought carefully. This didn't seem fair. His sister was hungry, and he had extra food. He could hear his tummy rumbling, but Penny's tummy was probably rumbling too.

"I have an idea!" said Kip, his dark eyes sparkling. "Let me think of a good plan."

Kip looked at his three crackers. He counted them slowly: "One, two, three." Then he looked at Penny's sad face. He wanted to help, but he also wanted to be fair to both of them.

"I know what to do," Kip said with a smile. He carefully broke one cracker in half. *Crack!* The sound was crisp and clean.

"Here, Penny," Kip said, giving her half a cracker. "Now we both have the same amount to eat before lunch."

Penny's face lit up like sunshine. "Thank you, Kip! But are you sure? Won't you be hungry?"

"We'll both have enough," Kip explained. "I have two and a half crackers, and you have half a cracker. If we're still hungry, we can ask Mom together for a healthy snack."

Just then, Mom walked into the dining room, drying her hands on a soft blue towel. She saw Kip and Penny sharing the crackers and smiled warmly.

"What's happening here?" Mom asked curiously.

"Penny dropped her crackers, so I shared mine," Kip explained proudly. "I made a plan so we could both have a fair snack."

Mom's eyes twinkled with pride. "Kip, that was very thoughtful of you. You saw a problem and took action to fix it. That's called taking initiative."

"What's initiative?" asked Penny, crunching her cracker.

"Initiative means seeing something that needs to be done and doing it without being asked," Mom explained. "Kip noticed you were hungry and made a plan to help you. He didn't wait for someone else to solve the problem."

Kip felt warm and happy inside, like drinking hot cocoa on a cold day. "It felt good to help," he said.

"And you made sure it was fair for everyone," Mom added. "You used your thinking skills to find the best solution."

Mom sat down at the table with them. "Since you both showed such good sharing, would you like some apple slices before lunch?"

"Yes, please!" said Kip and Penny together, giggling.

As they munched their apple slices, Kip felt proud of his choice. He had seen a problem, made a plan, and helped his sister. The dining room felt even more cozy and warm now that everyone was happy and fed.

"Taking initiative feels really good," Kip thought to himself, watching Penny smile as she ate her apple slice.

The End.
