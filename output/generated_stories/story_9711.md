# Benny the Beaver's Laundry Day

Benny the beaver loved to keep busy. He built dams, chopped wood, and helped his family every day. But today was laundry day, and Benny was feeling very frustrated.

"This washing machine is so loud!" Benny said, covering his ears with his paws. The machine went WHOOSH-WHOOSH-WHOOSH as it spun the clothes around and around.

Benny's mama called from the kitchen, "Benny, can you help me sort the clothes when they're done?"

But Benny couldn't hear her over the noisy machine. He was getting angrier and angrier. His tail started slapping the floor - THUMP, THUMP, THUMP!

"I don't like this noise!" Benny shouted. "It's too loud! I can't think!"

Just then, Grandpa Beaver shuffled into the laundry room. He had gray whiskers and kind, twinkling eyes.

"What's wrong, little builder?" Grandpa asked gently.

"The washing machine is too noisy, and I can't hear anything!" Benny said, his voice getting louder. "I'm so mad!"

Grandpa Beaver sat down on a small stool next to Benny. "You know what I do when things get noisy and I feel angry?"

Benny stopped slapping his tail. "What?"

"I listen with my heart instead of just my ears," Grandpa said with a smile. "Want me to show you?"

Benny nodded, curious.

"First, let's take three deep breaths together," Grandpa said. "In through your nose... and out through your mouth."

Benny breathed in slowly. The air smelled like fresh, clean soap. He breathed out and felt a little calmer.

"Good! Now, let's listen to the washing machine differently," Grandpa said. "Instead of thinking it's just noise, let's hear what it's really doing."

They sat quietly together. Benny closed his eyes and really listened.

"The water is swishing the dirt away," Grandpa whispered. "The machine is working hard to make our clothes clean so we can wear them again and again."

Benny opened his eyes. "It's helping us reuse our clothes instead of throwing them away?"

"Exactly!" Grandpa said. "And listen... do you hear that?"

Benny listened carefully. Between the washing machine sounds, he could hear Mama calling from the kitchen.

"Benny! The cookies are ready!"

"I can hear Mama now!" Benny said excitedly. "She was calling me before, but I wasn't really listening."

"When we're angry, it's hard to hear the good things around us," Grandpa explained. "But when we calm down and listen with our hearts, we can hear so much more."

The washing machine made a happy DING! sound.

"The clothes are clean!" Benny said. He helped Grandpa move the wet clothes to the dryer. "Grandpa, I have an idea. What if we make a game out of laundry day?"

"What kind of game?" Grandpa asked.

"We can listen for all the different sounds and guess what each one means," Benny said. "The WHOOSH means the clothes are getting clean. The DING means they're ready. And we can count how many times we reuse our favorite shirts!"

Grandpa clapped his paws together. "What a wonderful strategy! You're thinking like a true problem-solver."

From that day on, Benny looked forward to laundry day. He learned that listening carefully - even when things seemed noisy or frustrating - helped him understand the world better and stay calm.

And whenever he felt angry, he remembered Grandpa's advice: breathe deeply, listen with your heart, and look for ways to turn problems into games.

The End.
