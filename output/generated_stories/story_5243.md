# Milo the Monkey Makes a Team

Milo was a courageous monkey with bright eyes and gentle hands. One sunny morning, Milo peeked into the garden shed. It smelled like fresh soil and orange soap. Dust sparkled in the light. “Ooo-ooo! So many things,” Milo said softly.

Inside the shed were boxes and bins. One bin had paper. One bin had plastic. One bin had metal. There was also a wobbly basket of old balls. Thunk-thunk! The balls bumped together.

Milo had a big goal. “I want to make a sports day,” he said. “A kind, kind sports day for everyone.” He clapped his hands. “But I will do it with recycling!”

Milo looked at the bins. “Paper, plastic, metal,” he whispered. “We can use what we have.” He took a deep breath. He felt brave. He felt ready.

Just then, friends peeked in. Tessa the turtle slid in quietly. “Hello,” she said in a soft voice.

Buzz the bee buzzed in, humming. “Bzz-bzz! I can help!”

Pip the parrot flapped in, bright and loud. “Let’s play, let’s play!”

Milo smiled. “We will make games with old things. We will recycle. We will use kind words. We will cheer for each other. That is our goal.” He pointed to a big paper sign. On it he drew a star and wrote, “Goal: Recycle and Play!”

Pip tilted his head. “But we are all different,” he squawked.

Milo nodded. “Yes. Different is good. We will celebrate our differences. We will make games for everyone.”

They began. Milo held a plastic bottle. It felt smooth and cool. He shook it—shooka-shooka! “This can be a shaker,” he said. Buzz filled it with dry beans. Tappa-tappa! It made a happy sound.

Tessa found a stack of old paper. “Soft and thin,” she said. “We can make flags.” They colored stars and hearts. Swish, swish went the crayons. The paper smelled like books.

Pip looked at the metal cans. “Clink-clank!” he sang. “We can roll them.” Milo lined up the cans. Tessa rolled a soft ball at them. “Strike!” she giggled as the cans fell, clatter-clatter!

They worked and worked. But then—oh no! The plastic bin tipped. Plop! Bottles rolled everywhere. Buzz zipped up high. “Bzz! Too many bottles!”

Milo felt his chest thump-thump. He took a slow breath. “We can solve this,” he said calmly. “Let’s talk.”

He looked at his friends. “What do you think?” he asked.

Tessa said, “We can sort them by color. Slow and steady.”

Buzz said, “We can stack them in a crate. Bzz, neat and tidy.”

Pip said, “We can make a bottle ring toss! Fun, fun!”

Milo smiled. “Good ideas. Let’s do all three.” They sorted. They stacked. They made a ring toss with old string and round lids. The shed sounded happy—clink, swish, hum, giggle.

Soon, the games were ready:
- Bottle Ring Toss with blue and green bottles
- Can Roll with soft balls
- Paper Flag Parade with colorful flags
- Shaker Dance with the bean shakers

Milo put up another paper sign that said, “We Can Do It!” He showed his friends the plan. “Our goal is to try our best, be safe, and recycle,” he said. “We will use peaceful words. If something is hard, we will help.”

The garden sun warmed the door. Birds chirped. The air smelled like grass and lemons. It was time.

First came Bottle Ring Toss. Tessa tossed a ring gently. It missed. “Oh,” she said. Her eyes looked wet.

Milo touched her shell softly. “It’s okay, Tessa. Try again. I’m here.”

Buzz buzzed, “Bzz! You can do it!”

Tessa took a deep breath. She tried again—plop! The ring landed on a bottle. “I did it!” she cheered.

Next was Can Roll. Pip flapped with excitement. He threw the ball too fast. The cans didn’t fall. Pip frowned. “I am loud, but not strong,” he said in a small voice.

Milo smiled. “You are great at cheering,” he said. “Will you cheer for us?” Pip’s eyes shone. “Cheer, cheer!” he cried. He danced with the shaker. The shooka-shooka sound made everyone grin.

Buzz tried the Shaker Dance. “I am tiny,” he said. “I can’t dance big.”

Milo said, “Small moves can be special.” Buzz buzz-danced in a tiny zigzag. It looked like glitter in the air. “Bzz, I shine!” he said proudly.

They all marched in the Paper Flag Parade. Swish, swish went the flags. The colors fluttered like butterflies. Milo felt warm and happy. “We did it,” he said. “We met our goal.”

At the end, Milo stood on a crate. “Friends,” he said. “We reused bottles, cans, and paper. We did not throw them away. We made new games. Recycling helps our Earth. We set a goal. We talked kindly. We all are different, and that makes our team strong.”

Tessa nodded. “Slow can be good.”

Buzz buzzed, “Small can shine!”

Pip sang, “Loud can cheer!”

Milo wrapped his tail around the sign. “Let’s promise,” he said. “We will sort paper, plastic, and metal. We will reuse before we throw away. We will listen and help.”

Everyone put a hand, a wing, or a tiny foot in the middle. “Team Recycle!” they shouted.

The shed felt cozy and bright. The bins were neat. The games were clean. The friends were smiling. Milo’s heart felt big and brave.

They stepped into the soft afternoon light, flags in hand, bottles stacked, and cans ready to use again. The garden smelled sweet. The world felt cared for.

And they knew: when we recycle, set kind goals, and celebrate our differences with peaceful words, we can make fun for everyone.

The End.
