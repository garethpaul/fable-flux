# Fawn Helps in the Guest Room

In a cozy house on the edge of a quiet forest, there was a sunny guest room. The room had a soft blue rug, a big window with yellow curtains, and a fluffy quilt that smelled like clean soap and warm sunshine.

A gentle deer fawn named Dapple peeked in. Dapple’s fur was brown with tiny white spots, like little stars. Her ears twitched. Her nose wiggled. She heard footsteps and happy humming.

“Hello?” Dapple whispered. “Is anyone here?”

Grandma Maple, with kind eyes and silver hair, smiled. “Hello, little fawn,” she said softly. “We are getting the guest room ready. A friend is coming to visit. Would you like to help?”

Dapple’s heart did a tiny jump. She felt shy. Her knees felt wobbly like jelly. “I am small,” she said. “Can I really help?”

Grandma Maple nodded. “Volunteering means you offer to help. Big help or small help, it all matters. Would you like to try?”

Dapple took a brave breath. She felt the warm air smell like lemon and soap. “I will volunteer,” she said. “I want to help.”

“Thank you,” said Grandma Maple. “You are brave and kind.”

Dapple stepped into the guest room. The floor felt smooth under her hooves. Sunlight made little squares of gold on the rug. A basket of fresh towels sat by the bed. The towels were fluffy and white, like clouds.

“What can I do?” Dapple asked.

“You can fluff the pillows,” said Grandma Maple. “You can also place the towels. And you can check if the toys are ready, in case a small guest comes too.”

Dapple sniffed the pillows. They smelled like lavender. She nudged them with her nose. They puffed up like soft cupcakes. “Poof! Poof!” she giggled.

Then Dapple carried a small towel on her head. It tickled her ears. “I can do it,” she told herself. “I can help.” She placed the towel in a neat stack. Pat, pat.

Just then, a little mouse named Pippin peeked in, carrying a tiny bag. His whiskers quivered. His eyes looked watery.

“Hello,” Dapple said gently. “Are you okay?”

Pippin squeaked, “I am visiting Grandma Maple. I am scared. The room is new. It is big. I feel small.”

Dapple remembered feeling small too. She looked at Pippin with soft eyes. “I felt small when I started to help,” she said. “But volunteering made me feel strong. Would you like me to show you the room?”

Pippin nodded. His tiny feet made quiet tap-tap sounds on the rug.

Dapple showed him the window. “Look,” she said. “The trees wave hello.” The leaves rustled, whispering hush-hush in the breeze.

She showed him the quilt. “It is warm,” she said. “Touch it.” Pippin patted the quilt. It was soft and cozy. He smiled a little.

She showed him a basket of toys: a stuffed bear, a wooden car, and a bright red ball. “Would you like to pick one?” Dapple asked.

Pippin hugged the stuffed bear. “Thank you,” he said. “I feel better.”

Grandma Maple came in with a tray. There were apple slices, crackers, and a cup of warm milk with cinnamon. The sweet smell filled the room.

“You have both been helpful,” said Grandma Maple. “Dapple, you fluffed the pillows. You placed the towels. You helped Pippin feel safe. That is true volunteering. You gave your time and your heart.”

Dapple’s chest felt warm, like sunshine. “I did it,” she said softly. “I was brave.”

Pippin looked up at Dapple. “You were kind to me,” he said. “You listened. You showed me things. That made me feel brave too.”

Dapple’s ears lifted. “We can be brave together,” she said.

Just then, the red ball rolled under the bed. Pippin squeaked, “Oh no! It is dark under there.”

Dapple peered into the space. It was shadowy, like a little cave. Her legs trembled, but she remembered volunteering means trying to help. She took another brave breath. “I will get it,” she said.

She bent down, slid her nose under the bed, and nudged the ball. Roll, roll. Her whiskers brushed dust bunnies that smelled like old feathers. She pushed gently until the ball popped out. Pippin clapped. “You did it!”

Dapple smiled. “We did it. You cheered for me. That helped me be brave.”

Grandma Maple set the tray on the bedside table. “This guest room is ready,” she said. “It feels warm because you both cared.”

Dapple and Pippin shared apple slices. Crunch, crunch. The cinnamon milk was sweet and cozy.

“I learned something,” Dapple said. “Volunteering is helping, even when you feel small. Empathy is caring how others feel. Courage is trying, even when you feel wobbly. When I help, my heart grows strong.”

Pippin nodded. “And I learned I can ask for help. That makes me brave too.”

Grandma Maple smiled. “You both have big hearts,” she said. “When we help, we all shine.”

The guest room glowed with soft light. The pillows were puffy, the towels were neat, and the friends were happy.

Dapple stood tall. “I will volunteer again,” she said. “Helping makes me feel good. Helping makes others feel safe.”

Pippin hugged the bear and the red ball. “Thank you,” he whispered.

And the sunny guest room felt like a warm hug.

The End.
