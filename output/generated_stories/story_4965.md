# Tully Tortoise Shares the Colors

In a quiet study corner, with soft rugs and a little round table, lived Tully, a wise tortoise. The study corner smelled like crayons and fresh paper. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Tully loved the calm, cozy space. He loved to read, draw, and think.

One bright morning, Tully opened a box of crayons. “So many colors,” he said softly. “Red like apples, yellow like sunshine, blue like the sky.” He touched each crayon with his gentle, slow hands. The crayons felt smooth and cool.

Knock, knock. Two friends peeked in. It was Pip the puppy and Mina the mouse.

“Hi, Tully!” barked Pip. His tail thumped the rug. Thump, thump.

“Hi!” squeaked Mina, her whiskers twitching. “What are you doing?”

“I am making art,” said Tully. “Would you like to draw with me?”

“Yes!” said Pip. “I want the red crayon.”

“I want the red crayon too,” said Mina.

Both reached for the same crayon. Their paws bumped. “Ow!” said Mina.

Pip pulled the crayon close. “Mine!” he barked. Mina’s ears drooped. She looked sad.

Tully took a slow breath. He felt the room. He heard the little clock tick, tick, tick. He smelled the waxy crayons. He looked at his friends.

“Friends,” said Tully kindly, “our study corner is for sharing. Sharing makes art more fun.”

“But I need red,” said Pip.

“I need red too,” said Mina, squeaking.

Tully nodded. “We can share. We can take turns. We can be helpful to each other.”

“How?” asked Pip.

Tully smiled. “I will be a leader and make a plan. Pip, you use red for one minute. Then Mina uses red for one minute. While you wait, you can use other colors. Red is not the only bright color.”

Pip looked at the yellow crayon. It shined like a tiny sun. Mina held a blue crayon. It felt cool like water.

“Okay,” said Pip. “I can try.”

“Okay,” said Mina. “I can try too.”

Tully set a tiny sand timer on the table. Swish, swish, the sand fell like soft rain. Pip colored a big round apple. Scratch, scratch went the crayon on the paper. He smiled. “Yellow looks happy,” he said.

When the sand ran out, Tully said, “Time to share.”

Pip handed the red crayon to Mina. “Here you go,” he said. “I can wait.”

“Thank you,” Mina said. Her eyes lit up. She drew a heart. Pop, pop went her tiny strokes. “Red is warm,” she said. “It feels like a hug.”

Tully drew slowly, making green leaves and a tall blue sky. “See?” he said. “We are sharing. We are making art together.”

After drawing, Tully stretched his legs. “Let’s move our bodies,” he said. “Art and exercise are friends!”

Pip bounced. “Exercise? In the study corner?”

“Yes,” said Tully. “Gentle, quiet exercise.”

He led them in a slow stretch. “Reach up like tall trees,” said Tully. They reached up high. Their fingers and paws tingled.

“Now touch your toes,” said Tully. “Bend, bend.” They giggled. Mina’s whiskers brushed the rug. It tickled. Pip’s ears flopped over his eyes.

“Take turtle steps,” said Tully, moving slowly around the table. “Step, pause. Step, pause.” The rug felt soft under their feet and paws. The sunlight felt warm on their backs.

They sat again. Their faces were calm and happy. Their hearts beat strong and steady. Thump, thump.

Mina looked at the crayons. “We can share more,” she said. She gave Pip the blue crayon. “Your turn.”

Pip wagged. “Thanks!” He drew a big blue splash. “This looks like a pond.”

Tully nodded. “Let’s be helpful,” he said. He handed Mina a glue stick. “You can glue a leaf on your picture.” He passed Pip a little sticker star. “You can add a star.”

Mina pressed the glue. Squish. The leaf stuck with a soft tap. Pip placed the star. Peel, stick. “Shiny!” he said.

Soon, three pictures covered the table. Apples, hearts, leaves, stars, and a blue pond. The study corner looked bright and busy. It smelled like paper and glue. It sounded like quiet, happy breaths.

Tully cleared space for clean-up. “Leaders help clean,” he said. “Let’s help each other.”

They put crayons in the box. Click, click. They stacked paper. Shuffle, shuffle. They wiped the table with a soft cloth. Swish, swish.

Pip looked at Mina. “I liked taking turns,” he said. “It felt kind.”

Mina smiled. “I liked sharing. It made my heart warm.”

Tully’s eyes twinkled. “Sharing helps friends. Sharing makes art better. Sharing makes exercise fun. Today we learned to share, to help, and to lead.”

Pip and Mina nodded. They hugged Tully. His shell felt smooth and safe.

Tully said, “When we share, everyone gets a turn. When we lead kindly, everyone feels good.”

They hung their pictures on the wall. The study corner glowed with color and care.

The End.
