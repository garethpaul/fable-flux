# Jump-Ready Joey and the Bath-Time Steps

Joey was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and a springy tail. He loved to hop, hop, hop! One sunny evening, Mama Roo called from the bathroom, “Joey, it’s bath time!”

Joey bounced down the hall. The bathroom smelled like bubbly soap and fresh towels. The warm light made the tiles shine. The faucet hummed a gentle song, drip-drip-drip. Joey wiggled his ears. “Bath time! I want to splash!”

Mama Roo smiled and pointed to a little chart on the wall. It showed pictures: a towel, a toothbrush, a bath, and pajamas. “We follow our steps,” she said kindly. “Step 1: Put your towel on the hook nearby. Step 2: Brush teeth. Step 3: Take a bath. Step 4: Dry and get cozy.”

Joey looked at the chart. The pictures were bright and simple. He nodded, but his feet kept hopping. “I remember! I’ll do it fast!” he said. He zoomed to the tub and turned the faucet. “Splash time!”

“Wait, Joey,” said Mama Roo gently. “First, the towel.”

But Joey was already patting bubbles. The water was warm and tickly on his paws. “Hee-hee! Bubbles smell like oranges!” He dipped his snout. Plop! A big bubble popped and tickled his nose. He giggled.

Then he reached for the soap. It slipped—whoosh—right into the tub. His tail flicked, and water splashed onto the floor. Splish! A little puddle made the tile wet and shiny. Joey tried to hop away, but his foot slid. “Whoa!” He wobbled and grabbed the tub edge.

Mama Roo steadied him. “Are you okay, little hopper?”

Joey’s ears drooped. “I didn’t put my towel up. I didn’t brush first. I wanted to splash.”

Mama Roo nodded and pointed again to the chart. “Our steps help us be safe and clean. Let’s try again. We can fix it, together.”

Joey took a small breath. The bathroom felt warmer now, and the puddle felt cold on his toes. He liked Mama’s calm voice. “Okay, Mama. Let’s follow the steps.”

Mama Roo turned off the water. The drip-drip slowed. Together, they wiped the floor with a towel. Swish, swish. The tiles were dry again.

“Step 1,” Joey said, tapping the first picture. “Towel on the hook nearby.” He picked his soft, sky-blue towel. It smelled like sunshine. He hung it on the low hook with a little click. “Done!”

“Step 2,” said Mama Roo. “Brush teeth.”

Joey hopped to the sink. He squeezed a tiny dot of minty paste on his small toothbrush. “Minty!” he giggled. He brushed in little circles—swish, swish, swish—up, down, and all around. He watched the foamy smile in the mirror. “I’m a bubble beard!” he said.

Mama Roo laughed. “Rinse and spit.” Joey took a sip of water, swished, and spat into the sink—ptoo! He wiped his chin. “Teeth are sparkly,” he said proudly.

“Step 3,” said Mama Roo. “Take a bath.”

Joey climbed carefully into the tub. Mama Roo checked the water. “Warm like a hug,” she said. The bubbles rose like tiny clouds. Joey swished his paws, listening to the soft slosh—whoosh, wash, hush. The orange smell floated up again. He closed his eyes and took a deep breath. “Mmm.”

Mama Roo handed him a cup. “Pour water on your ears and neck.” Joey poured slowly. “Drip, drip, hello, water!” He scrubbed with a soft washcloth—arms, tummy, toes. “My toes are tickly!” he squealed. He sang a little song, “Scrub-a-dub, tub-a-dub, follow steps, rub-a-rub!”

Mama Roo smiled and nodded at the chart. “You’re doing it, Joey.”

Joey’s chest felt warm with pride. “I’m following directions!”

“Step 4,” said Mama Roo softly, “Dry and get cozy.”

Joey stood up carefully. Mama Roo held his hand. He stepped onto the bath mat—squish, soft and safe. He reached for his sky-blue towel on the hook, right where he had put it. “It’s here!” he cheered. He patted his fur—pat, pat, pat—until he was dry and fluffy. Mama Roo helped him into his pajamas. They were yellow with little stars. The fabric felt smooth and snuggly.

Joey looked at the chart from the start to the end. “We did every step,” he said. His tail gave a happy thump.

“How do you feel?” asked Mama Roo.

“Clean. Safe. Proud,” Joey said. He hugged Mama Roo. Her fur smelled like lavender. “When I tried to hurry, I slipped. When I followed directions, I stayed safe and did it right.”

Mama Roo kissed his forehead. “Following directions helps us learn. It shows understanding. And it makes things easier.”

Joey nodded. His eyes felt sleepy. “Next time, I will look at the chart first,” he said. He pointed to each picture again—towel, toothbrush, bath, pajamas. “One, two, three, four.”

They turned off the bathroom light. The faucet was quiet now. The hallway felt cool and calm. Joey hopped just once, a small, happy hop. “I can follow steps,” he whispered.

Mama Roo tucked him into bed. “Yes, you can,” she said. “And I’m proud of you.”

Joey snuggled under his blanket. He pictured the bright chart and the gentle steps. He smiled and whispered, “Follow, learn, understand.”

The End.
