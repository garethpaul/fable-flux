# Joey's Green Garden Plan

Little Joey the kangaroo bounced excitedly on Mama's balcony garden. The morning sun felt warm on his fuzzy brown fur, and the sweet smell of flowers tickled his nose.

"Mama, look at all these empty bottles!" Joey called out, pointing his tiny paw at a pile of plastic containers near the tomato plants.

Mama Kangaroo hopped over, her pouch swaying gently. "Oh my! I forgot to take those to the recycling bin yesterday," she said, scratching her head. "I was so busy watering our plants."

Joey's bright eyes sparkled with an idea. "Can I help? Please, please, please?" He bounced up and down, making his little tail wiggle.

"That's very sweet, Joey, but recycling needs careful planning," Mama explained. "We have to sort everything properly."

"I can plan!" Joey announced proudly. "I'm a big joey now!"

Mama smiled and nodded. "Alright, let's make a plan together. First, what do you see in our pile?"

Joey hopped closer and looked carefully. The plastic bottles gleamed in the sunlight, and he could hear them clink softly when the breeze moved them. "I see... um... bottles! And they're all different!"

"Very good! What else?" Mama encouraged.

"Some are clear like water, and some are green like our lettuce leaves!" Joey said, touching each bottle gently. "And look, this one still smells like orange juice!"

"Excellent observation! Now, for our plan - clear bottles go in one group, colored bottles in another. Can you help me sort them?"

Joey started picking up bottles, but then he stopped. His ears drooped a little. "Mama, I need to tell you something."

"What is it, sweetie?"

"Yesterday, I was playing with one of these bottles, and I accidentally dropped it behind the flower pots. I was scared you'd be upset, so I didn't tell you." Joey's voice was very quiet.

Mama wrapped her warm arms around Joey. "Thank you for being honest, dear one. It's always better to tell the truth. Can you show me where it is?"

Together, they found the bottle hiding behind the bright red geraniums. It smelled like earth and felt damp from the morning dew.

"Now our plan is complete!" Mama said cheerfully. "Let's sort all the bottles."

Joey carefully placed each bottle in the right group. The clear ones made a soft tinkling sound, while the colored ones clinked more deeply. "This is like a musical game!" he giggled.

"Now what's our next step?" Mama asked.

Joey thought hard, tapping his paw on his chin. "We take them to the big recycling bin downstairs!"

"Perfect planning! And do you know why we recycle?"

"Because..." Joey paused, remembering what Mama had taught him before. "Because the bottles can become new things! Like new bottles, or toys, or even park benches!"

"That's right! We're helping take care of our Earth."

Hand in hand, they carried their sorted bottles downstairs. Joey felt proud as each bottle dropped into the recycling bin with a satisfying thunk.

Back on the balcony, Joey watered the plants while Mama prepared lunch. "Mama, can we make a recycling plan for every week?" he asked.

"What a wonderful idea! Good planning helps us remember important things."

Joey bounced happily among the flowers, knowing he had helped the Earth and told the truth. The balcony garden smelled fresh and clean, and everything felt just right.

The End.
