# Zippy's Morning Stretch

The morning sun streamed through the soft blue curtains of baby Emma's nursery. Colorful toys sat quietly on shelves, and a gentle mobile spun slowly above the crib. In the corner, a small robot dog named Zippy powered on with a cheerful beep.

"Good morning, nursery!" Zippy said in his warm, friendly voice. His LED eyes glowed bright blue as he looked around the cozy room.

Zippy noticed something strange. Usually, he felt bouncy and ready to play. But today, his metal joints felt stiff and creaky. His tail barely wagged.

"Oh my," Zippy said, trying to walk. His steps were slow and wobbly. "Something doesn't feel right."

Baby Emma was still sleeping peacefully in her crib. Zippy didn't want to wake her, but he needed help. He rolled slowly to the doorway where Emma's mom was folding laundry.

"Beep beep," Zippy called softly. "Mrs. Johnson, I think I need help."

Mrs. Johnson looked down and smiled. "What's wrong, Zippy?"

"My body feels all stiff and creaky," Zippy explained. "I can barely move my paws or wag my tail."

Mrs. Johnson knelt down and gently patted Zippy's head. "Oh, I know what's happening. You've been sitting still all night while charging. Your joints need exercise to work properly!"

"Exercise?" Zippy asked, tilting his head curiously.

"Yes! Just like how Emma needs to move her arms and legs to grow strong, you need to move your joints to stay flexible," Mrs. Johnson explained kindly.

She showed Zippy how to do gentle stretches. "First, lift your front paws up high, then down low." Zippy tried it slowly. His joints made little clicking sounds as they loosened up.

"Now try turning your head left and right," Mrs. Johnson suggested. Zippy rotated his head, and it felt much better already.

"Wow!" Zippy exclaimed. "I can feel my circuits warming up!"

Mrs. Johnson smiled. "Now try some gentle walking in circles."

Zippy began to walk around the soft nursery rug. With each step, he felt more like himself. His tail started to wag faster, and his eyes grew brighter.

"This feels wonderful!" Zippy barked happily. "My whole body feels alive again!"

Just then, baby Emma woke up with a soft coo. She saw Zippy moving around and clapped her tiny hands with delight.

"Look, Emma!" Zippy said, doing a playful spin. "I learned that exercise makes my body feel good and strong!"

Emma giggled and reached her arms toward Zippy. Mrs. Johnson picked her up and said, "Emma needs exercise too. Should we do some gentle stretches together?"

Zippy wagged his tail enthusiastically. "Yes! Let's all move together!"

They spent the morning doing gentle exercises. Zippy showed Emma how to reach high and low. Emma kicked her legs and moved her arms while Mrs. Johnson held her safely.

"Exercise makes everyone feel better," Zippy said happily. "It's like giving our bodies a big, warm hug from the inside!"

From that day on, Zippy made sure to do his morning stretches every day. He learned that taking care of his body was just as important as taking care of others.

"Moving my body helps me be the best helper I can be," Zippy said to himself each morning as he stretched in the sunny nursery.

And whenever Emma got fussy, Zippy would do his happy dance, showing her that moving and stretching could turn any grumpy moment into a joyful one.

The End.
