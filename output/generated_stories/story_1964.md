# Polka the Parrot and the Rooftop Rescue

Polka was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a splash of blue on her tail. She loved to perch on the warm, sunny rooftop above her building. From up high, Polka could see the city sparkle. She could feel the soft wind and smell the sweet flowers from window boxes.

“Squawk! Good morning, sky!” Polka chirped. She flapped her wings and listened. She heard cars hum, people laugh, and little birds tweet, tweet, tweet.

Polka liked to draw with sidewalk chalk on the rooftop tiles. She drew clouds, leaves, and smiling suns. One sunny day, Polka saw something that made her tilt her head. A light, crinkly bag blew across the rooftop. It crinkled and rattled like a tiny drum. The wind pushed the bag toward a small puddle by the drain.

“Hmm,” Polka said. “That bag could go down the drain. It could get stuck and make a mess. That would be bad for the birds and the plants.”

Polka hopped closer. She felt the tiles warm under her feet. She felt the wind push her feathers. She heard the bag go crinkle-crinkle. She caught the bag with her beak and dropped it into a clean bin. “Safe and sound,” she said.

Just then, Polka heard a little peep. “Help?” A tiny sparrow stood by the puddle, shaking. One of its feet was tangled in a string from a broken kite.

Polka’s heart felt big and soft. “Oh, little friend,” she said gently. “I will help you.”

The sparrow’s eyes were wide. “I can’t fly,” it whispered.

“You will soon,” Polka said. She looked around. “We need a plan.” Polka liked plans. She liked using her creativity to solve problems. She found a loose feather, smooth and light. She found a twig, small and straight. She found a bottle cap, round like the moon.

Polka spoke softly. “I will be careful. I will be kind.” She used the feather to lift the string, so it wouldn’t scratch. She used the twig to slide the knot loose. She used the bottle cap to keep the string from rolling back. “Almost there,” she said.

The string slipped free. The sparrow wiggled its toes. “I can feel my foot!” it chirped. The sparrow fluttered its wings and hopped in a happy circle.

Polka smiled. “Let’s put this string where it can’t hurt anyone,” she said. She rolled the string into a ball and placed it in the bin with the crinkly bag. Then she picked up the bottle cap and any little bits she saw. She hummed a cheerful tune, “Pick it up, tidy up, keep our home bright!”

The sparrow peeped, “Why do you clean the rooftop?”

Polka looked at the sky and then at the tiny puddle. She dipped her toes in the cool water. “This rooftop is our outdoor home,” she said. “We share it with birds, bees, and breezes. When we care for our home, our home cares for us.”

The sparrow nodded. “I like that,” it said.

Polka had an idea. Her eyes sparkled. “Let’s make a Wind Catcher Garden!” she said. “We can use safe, clean, old things again in a new way. That is called re-use. Re-use helps the Earth.”

The sparrow tilted its head. “How?”

Polka found three empty paper rolls, a clean ribbon, and a few dry leaves. She tied the rolls together with the ribbon. She stuck the leaves in the ends, like little flags. She drew green vines on the rolls with her chalk. She hung the Wind Catcher on a hook by the flower box.

The breeze blew. Whoooooosh. The leaves rustled. The rolls spun slowly. The Wind Catcher whispered, “Swish, swish.” It made a soft, happy sound.

“It’s pretty,” said the sparrow. “And it doesn’t make trash.”

Polka nodded. “Yes! We can make new things from old things. That helps animals, plants, and people.”

More birds landed on the rooftop edge—two pigeons and a robin. “What’s that swishy thing?” asked a pigeon.

“Our Wind Catcher Garden,” Polka said proudly. “And today, we learned to help and to keep our rooftop clean.”

The robin looked at the bin. “Can we help too?”

“Yes,” Polka said. “We can all help. We can pick up loose strings. We can put bags in the bin. We can re-use safe things. We can be gentle with each other.”

The birds chirped together, happy and bright. They made a little team. The team sang a clean-up song: “Pick it up, tidy up, keep our home bright! Help a friend, be kind, make things right!”

At the end of the day, the sky turned orange and pink. The air felt cool. The rooftop smelled like fresh rain and flowers. Polka looked around. The puddle was clear. The Wind Catcher spun and whispered. The birds rested with calm, warm hearts.

The sparrow fluttered up, strong and free. “Thank you, Polka,” it said. “You helped me. You helped the rooftop too.”

Polka smiled and fluffed her feathers. “We helped together,” she said. “That’s what friends do.”

She looked at the city lights and felt proud. “When we care for our home,” Polka said softly, “we care for each other and the Earth.”

The End.
