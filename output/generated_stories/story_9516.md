# Benny Bear's Busy Day

In a cozy garden shed behind a little house lived Benny Bear, a small brown cub with fuzzy ears and a black button nose. The shed smelled like fresh wood and flowers. Sunlight streamed through the dusty windows, making everything glow.

Benny loved helping everyone. His shed was filled with tools, flower pots, and garden treasures. Every day, friends came asking for help.

"Benny! Benny!" called Rosie Rabbit, hopping through the shed door. "Can you help me plant carrots? Please, please, please?"

"Of course!" Benny said, his tail wagging. He dropped his paintbrush. He was painting a birdhouse, but helping seemed more important.

Before they finished planting, Squeaky Squirrel scampered in. "Benny! I need help finding acorns! Right now!"

"Oh! Okay!" Benny left the half-planted carrots and followed Squeaky outside.

Then Penny Pig arrived. "Benny! Help me build a mud castle!"

Benny's head felt dizzy. His paws were dirty. His fur was messy. He hadn't finished anything all day.

"I... I..." Benny's voice got small. His tummy felt tight and worried.

By evening, Benny sat in his shed feeling very tired. The birdhouse was half-painted. The carrots were half-planted. The acorns were scattered everywhere. Nothing was done.

"I wanted to help everyone," Benny sniffled. "But now everything is a mess."

His friend Oliver Owl flew to the window. Oliver had wise yellow eyes and soft gray feathers.

"What's wrong, Benny?" Oliver asked gently.

"Everyone needs help, but I can't do everything at once," Benny explained. "I feel all mixed up inside."

Oliver nodded. "Benny, it's wonderful that you want to help. But you need to take care of yourself too. It's okay to say 'not right now.'"

"But what if my friends get sad?" Benny worried.

"Real friends will understand," Oliver hooted softly. "You can help them, but one thing at a time. That's called having boundaries."

The next morning, Rosie Rabbit hopped in again. "Benny! Can you help me weed my garden?"

Benny took a deep breath. "I'd love to help you, Rosie. But first, I need to finish painting my birdhouse. Can we meet after lunch?"

Rosie's ears perked up. "Of course! I'll wait."

Benny felt proud and calm. He painted careful blue strokes on his birdhouse. The shed was quiet and peaceful.

After lunch, Benny helped Rosie pull weeds. They worked together, laughing and talking. When they finished, Benny felt happy and proud.

"This is much better," Benny thought. "I finished my work AND helped my friend."

When Squeaky came asking for help, Benny said kindly, "I can help you tomorrow morning, Squeaky. Right now, I need to rest."

Squeaky smiled. "That sounds perfect! See you tomorrow!"

That evening, Benny sat in his clean, organized shed. The birdhouse was beautifully painted. His tools were put away. He felt peaceful and proud.

Oliver flew by the window. "How do you feel, Benny?"

"Much better!" Benny grinned. "I learned that saying 'not right now' doesn't mean I don't care. It means I'm taking care of myself so I can be a better helper."

"That's exactly right," Oliver said. "Good friends respect boundaries."

Benny curled up in his cozy corner, feeling warm and happy. Tomorrow he would help Squeaky find acorns. But tonight, he would rest.

The End.
