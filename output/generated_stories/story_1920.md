# Rosie Rabbit's Art Room Adventure

Rosie Rabbit loved the colorful art room at her school. The walls were painted bright yellow like sunshine, and shelves held jars of sparkly paint, soft brushes, and stacks of paper. Today, she bounced into the room with her fluffy white tail wiggling with excitement.

"I'm going to make the most beautiful painting ever!" Rosie announced, her pink nose twitching with determination.

But when Rosie looked around, she saw a big problem. All the other little animals were already using the art supplies. Benny Bear had the red paint. Luna Lion was using the blue. Pip Pig held the only big paintbrush, and there was just one piece of large paper left.

Rosie's ears drooped down. She felt angry bubbles growing in her tummy. "This isn't fair!" she wanted to shout.

But then Rosie took a deep breath, just like her teacher had taught her. She closed her eyes and counted to three. "One... two... three." The angry bubbles started to shrink.

"Wait," thought Rosie, her ears perking up again. "Let me think about this like a detective!"

She hopped around the room, looking carefully at everything. Her brown eyes sparkled as she noticed things others had missed. There were small brushes in a cup by the window. Yellow and green paint sat unused on a high shelf. And in the corner, she spotted a roll of white paper.

"Excuse me, Mr. Owl," Rosie said politely to their teacher. "May I use that paper in the corner?"

"Of course, Rosie! I'm proud of you for asking nicely," hooted Mr. Owl, his wise eyes twinkling.

Rosie carefully unrolled the paper. It was even bigger than the piece everyone wanted! Then she climbed on a safe step-stool to reach the yellow and green paint.

As she started painting sunny flowers, Benny Bear noticed her beautiful work. "Wow, Rosie! Your flowers look amazing. But I wish I had some green for the stems."

Rosie thought for a moment. She had plenty of green paint, and Benny had been kind to compliment her work. "Would you like to share my green paint?" she offered. "And maybe I could use a tiny bit of your red for flower centers?"

"That's a great idea!" said Benny, his fuzzy face lighting up.

Soon, Luna Lion wanted to trade some blue paint for yellow to make a beautiful sky. Pip Pig offered to share her big brush when Rosie needed to paint large petals.

"Look what happened!" laughed Rosie, stepping back to admire their work. Instead of one painting, they had created four wonderful pictures, each more colorful than they could have made alone.

Mr. Owl flew over and smiled. "Rosie, you solved your problem by thinking carefully and staying calm. Instead of getting upset, you looked around, made good choices, and found a way for everyone to be happy."

Rosie's ears stood tall with pride. "When I felt angry, I stopped and thought like a detective! Then I saw there were enough supplies for everyone if we just looked harder and shared."

"That's called logical thinking," said Mr. Owl. "You used your brain to solve a problem step by step."

As Rosie cleaned her brushes in the warm, soapy water, she felt proud and happy. She had learned that when problems seem big, taking time to think and look for solutions makes everything better.

The End.
