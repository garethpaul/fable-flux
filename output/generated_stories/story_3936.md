# Koala Kai’s Little List

Koala Kai was a calm koala who lived in a cozy home with a sunny home office. The office smelled like fresh paper and warm tea. A soft rug tickled Kai’s toes. The computer hummed, “mmm,” like a sleepy bee. A small plant sat on the desk, with shiny green leaves.

“Good morning, plant,” Kai said in a gentle voice. “Today will be a good day.”

Kai liked to set goals. Kai used a bright blue notebook with a yellow star on the cover. The pages were smooth and made a soft swish sound. Kai opened the notebook and said, “Let’s make a little list.”

Kai wrote:
1. Plan our day.
2. Lead a clean-up.
3. Recycle paper.
4. Share a family tradition.

Kai smiled. “These are my goals. I can do them one by one.”

A tiny breeze came through the window and made the curtains flap like butterfly wings. Kai took a deep breath. “I feel calm. I feel ready.”

First goal: Plan our day.
Kai made simple steps. “Step one: tidy the desk. Step two: call friends. Step three: have a story time.” Kai drew little boxes to check off. Check, check, check! The desk looked neat. The pencils stood tall in a cup. The keyboard was crumb-free. The mouse felt smooth and warm under Kai’s paw.

“Good start,” Kai said. “I am leading myself with a plan.”

Second goal: Lead a clean-up.
Kai called friends on the tablet. Tap, tap. “Hello, friends!” said Kai. “Would you like to help in a clean-up? We can make our home office sparkle.”

Pip the possum popped up on the screen. “I like to clean!” Pip chirped.

Bella the bilby waved. “Let’s do it!”

“Great,” Kai said. “I will be a kind leader. We will sing while we clean.”

Kai started a song. “Pick up, sort up, one-two-three. We can do it, you and me!” The friends giggled and joined in. They could hear the swish of a broom, the clink of a jar lid, and the rustle of papers.

Third goal: Recycle paper.
Kai pointed to a stack of old papers. “These papers can be recycled. We will sort them into the blue bin.” The bin made a hollow, happy sound—thunk—each time a paper went in.

Pip asked, “Why do we recycle?”

Kai said, “We recycle to help the Earth. Old paper can become new paper. We use less, and we waste less. It is a good choice.”

Bella smiled. “I like good choices.”

Kai showed leadership by giving small jobs. “Pip, please gather the papers. Bella, please check for sticky notes. I will flatten the pages.” Pip scooped, Bella peeled, and Kai smoothed. The pile went down, down, down. The blue bin filled up with a soft papery scent.

“Teamwork feels good,” Kai said. “We are meeting our goals.”

Fourth goal: Share a family tradition.
Kai opened a little wooden box. It smelled like eucalyptus and honey. Inside was a tiny bell and a little recipe card. Kai’s eyes were warm and happy.

“This is our family tradition,” Kai said. “We ring the bell when we reach a goal, and we share a sweet tea break. My grandma taught me this.”

Kai rang the bell. Ting! The sound was clear and bright. The friends clapped.

Kai poured warm lemon tea into small cups. The steam felt soft on their noses. There were crunchy oat biscuits, too. Crunch, crunch. Everyone sat on the rug. The room felt calm and sunny.

Pip said, “I like the bell.”

Bella said, “I like the tea.”

Kai said, “I like our team.”

Kai checked the list. All four goals were complete. Kai drew a big gold star at the bottom of the page and wrote, “We did it!”

Pip asked, “How did we do it so well?”

Kai smiled. “We made a plan. We took small steps. We were kind leaders. We recycled to help the Earth. We kept a family tradition to celebrate. This is how goals get done.”

Bella nodded. “Small steps make big things.”

Kai felt proud but still calm. The computer hummed softly. The plant’s leaves shone in the light. The blue bin sat full and ready for pick-up. The home office looked neat, clean, and happy.

“Friends,” Kai said, “tomorrow we can set new goals. We can teach others, too.”

Pip and Bella cheered, “Yes!”

Kai closed the notebook with a gentle thump. “Today’s lesson,” Kai said, “is this: Make a little list. Take small steps. Lead with kindness. Care for the Earth. Celebrate with a nice tradition.”

The friends touched paws. The bell gave one last, tiny ting, like a smile you can hear.

The End.
