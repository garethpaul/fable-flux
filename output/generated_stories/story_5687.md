# Pico Parrot’s Colorful Plan

Pico was a creative parrot who lived in a cozy home office. The room was bright and warm. Sunlight made soft squares on the carpet. The printer hummed, “bzz-bzz.” The computer clicked, “tap-tap.” Paper smelled clean, like new books. Crayons smelled sweet, like fruit.

Pico loved art. He loved to paint with safe, washable paints. He loved to glue shiny shapes on paper. He loved to sing, “Squawk-a-doodle-doo!” as he worked. He loved to flap his green wings and feel the tickle of air.

But Pico had a little problem. He did not have a routine.

Every morning, Pico flew into the home office and shouted, “Art time now!” He would open all the paints. He would uncap all the markers. He would pull out all the paper. He would sprinkle all the glitter. His little heart felt excited and buzzy.

One day, Pico made a big, messy rainbow. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple—swish, swish, swish! He dotted stars. He drew a happy sun. He even made a tiny paper hat and put it on the stapler. “You are a fancy stapler,” he said.

Then the door opened. Mia came in. Mia was Pico’s person. She worked in the home office. She wore soft slippers that went “shh-shh” on the floor. She smiled at Pico. Then she looked at the desk.

“Oh, Pico,” Mia said gently. “There is paint on my papers. There is glitter on my keyboard. My work is wet and sticky.”

Pico’s feathers drooped. “I… I was making art,” he whispered. He felt a lump in his tummy.

“I know you love art,” Mia said, kneeling down. Her voice was warm. “Art is wonderful. You are very creative. But we need a plan. We need a routine. A routine will help us know when it’s art time and when it’s work time.”

Pico tilted his head. “What is a routine?”

“A routine is a set of steps we do in order,” Mia explained. “We do step one, then step two, then step three. We do it the same way each day. A routine helps our day feel calm. It helps us think carefully and make good choices.”

Pico blinked. “Like a little map?”

“Yes,” said Mia. “A little map for the day.”

Pico felt a tiny spark of hope. He wanted to fix the mess. He wanted Mia to smile again. “I’m sorry,” he said softly. “I did not think. I was excited.”

Mia smiled and hugged him. “Thank you for saying sorry. I forgive you.” The word “forgive” felt soft and warm, like a blanket. Pico’s feathers fluffed up.

“Let’s make our routine together,” Mia said. “We will use logical thinking. We will make clear steps.”

They got a big paper and a purple marker. Pico loved the squeaky sound the marker made: “skreee-skreee.”

“Step one,” said Mia. “Morning stretch. We flap and wiggle.”

Pico flapped his wings. “Flap-flap!”

“Step two,” said Mia. “Mia’s work time. Computer goes tap-tap. Pico’s quiet time. Pico can read picture books or sort crayons by color.”

Pico lined up the crayons. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple. He felt proud. It was like a rainbow parade.

“Step three,” Mia continued. “Pico’s art time! We cover the desk with paper. We put a towel on the floor. We keep a little trash bin for scraps. We use only two colors at a time.”

Pico raised a wing. “What if I want three colors?”

Mia smiled. “We can add one more when the first two are capped and put away. That keeps things tidy.”

Pico nodded. That sounded fair and smart.

“Step four,” said Mia. “Clean-up song. We wash brushes. We wipe the table. We clap while we tidy.”

Pico tapped his claws. “Clap-clap-clap!”

“Step five,” Mia finished. “Snack time and story time.”

Pico’s tummy gave a happy grumble. “Snack!” he chirped.

They drew little pictures next to each step: a sun for stretch, a laptop for work, a paint palette for art, a sponge for clean-up, and an apple for snack. The pictures were bright and cheerful.

The next morning, the sun made warm squares on the carpet again. Pico looked at the routine chart. He felt calm. “Step one,” he said. “Flap and wiggle!” He stretched his wings. Mia stretched too. They giggled.

“Step two,” said Mia. She sat at the computer. Tap-tap. Pico sorted crayons. He lined them up like little soldiers. He whispered, “Red here. Orange here.” The room felt peaceful.

“Step three,” said Mia. She laid a towel on the floor. Pico spread paper. He chose blue and yellow. He sang, “Blue and yellow, swish, swish, swish!” He painted a big sun and a tall, wavy sea. He capped the blue and yellow. Then he added white, just a little, to make foamy waves.

“Step four,” they sang together. “Clean-up, clean-up, clap-clap-clap!” Pico washed the brushes. The water went swish. The sponge was cool and squishy. The table smelled like lemon soap. The keyboard stayed clean and happy.

“Step five,” Mia said, handing Pico an apple slice. Crunch! It was sweet and juicy. They read a story about fish. Pico pointed at the pictures with a soft, happy chirp.

Days passed. The routine chart stayed on the wall. Sometimes Pico wanted to skip a step. His feathers wiggled with wild ideas. But he took a deep breath and looked at the little pictures. Step by step, he found his way.

One afternoon, Mia said, “Pico, your art is so neat and bright now. You thought carefully. You followed our plan. Thank you.”

Pico felt proud. His tummy felt warm. “Routine helps me make smart choices,” he said. “It helps my art shine.”

Mia nodded. “And when we make a mistake, we say sorry. We forgive. We try again.”

Pico opened his wings wide. “I love our routine,” he chirped. “It is my little map.”

He made a new painting: a road with steps. At each step, there was a happy bird. The last bird held a shiny star.

“This is us,” Pico said. “Step by step.”

Mia and Pico hung the painting above the chart. The home office felt bright, calm, and full of love.

The lesson was clear: a simple routine, careful thinking, and kind hearts make every day colorful, clean, and fun.

The End.
