# Squeaky’s Colorful Plan

Squeaky the squirrel had a cozy study corner under the window. Sunlight made warm stripes on the desk. The desk smelled like fresh paper and crayons. A soft chair waited, and a little clock went tick-tick-tick.

Today, Squeaky had a task. “I will make a picture chart,” he said. “I will show my day, from morning to night!” He puffed his cheeks with excitement. He loved to draw. He also wanted to finish his chart all by himself.

Squeaky opened his crayon box. “Hello, colors,” he whispered. “Red, blue, yellow, green, I have a plan for you.” He spread paper, scissors, and glue on the desk. His fluffy tail swished like a feathery brush.

Then he saw the problem. The crayons were tiny and broken. The glue cap was stuck. The scissors were too big for his small paws. Squeaky’s ears drooped. “Oh no,” he sighed. “How can I make my chart?”

Squeaky felt his heart beat fast. His tummy felt wiggly. He wanted to call for help. But he stopped. He remembered his mindful breathing. “Smell the flower,” he told himself. He breathed in. “Blow the candle,” he said softly. He breathed out. In, out. In, out. His shoulders relaxed. The wiggly feeling got smaller.

He looked around the study corner. The sunlight warmed his whiskers. The paper felt smooth. He tapped the desk. Tap-tap. “I am a resourceful squirrel,” he said. “I can try. I can make a plan.”

Squeaky made a plan out loud. “Step one: fix the glue. Step two: make crayon rubbings. Step three: draw and paste. Step four: finish my chart.”

He wrapped a warm cloth around the glue cap to make it soft. “Lefty-loosey,” he giggled, twisting slowly. Pop! The cap opened. “Yes!” He clapped his tiny paws.

Next, he looked at the broken crayons. “You can still color,” he told them. He peeled off the paper wrappers with care. He placed leaves and buttons under a paper. “I can make textures,” he said. He rubbed the side of a crayon over the paper. Scritch-scritch. A leaf shape appeared like magic. “Wow!” His eyes sparkled.

He made more rubbings: a coin, a lace ribbon, the edge of a bookmark. Each made bumpy shapes. He smiled. “This is creative. This is my way.”

The scissors were still too big. Squeaky wiggled them, but they clacked and slipped. He felt a frown coming. So he paused. “Smell the flower. Blow the candle.” He breathed. Then he had an idea. “I can tear paper with gentle paws.”

He folded the paper where he wanted to cut. Crease, crease. Then he tore slowly along the line. Rip… riiip. The edges were soft and wavy. “Curvy clouds!” he laughed. “I love the look.”

He started his chart. “Morning,” he said, drawing a sun with a warm yellow crayon. The circle glowed. He added a rubbing of the coin for breakfast cereal. “Crunchy circles,” he said. He glued the coin rubbing next to the sun. The glue smelled sweet and a little sticky.

“Afternoon,” he said, rubbing the green crayon over a leaf. A leafy shape jumped onto the paper. “This is for playtime in the yard.” His tail flicked happily.

“Evening,” he whispered, using a soft blue. He drew the window and the moon peeking in. He tore a tiny star from shiny paper. “Twinkle,” he whispered as he glued it down. He listened to the clock. Tick-tick-tick. His breathing matched the sound. He felt calm and busy.

Squeaky hummed a tune. “Color, glue, and tear, I can make it with care.” He checked his plan. “Step one, done. Step two, done. Step three, almost done.” He stood on the chair to see the whole chart. He added labels in big, simple letters: MORNING, AFTERNOON, EVENING. The letters were wobbly, but they were his.

A little problem came back. The blue crayon rolled off the desk. Plink! It fell on the floor and slid under the chair. Squeaky blinked. He breathed in and out. “I can solve this,” he said. He used a ruler like a tiny bridge and slid the crayon out. “Got you!” He grinned.

At last, he put a shiny sticker at the bottom: DONE. He patted it proudly. He cleaned up, too. He put the glue cap back on, wrapped the crayons, stacked the papers, and wiped the desk with a small cloth. The room smelled clean and papery again.

Squeaky looked at his chart. It was colorful. It was full of textures and shapes. It was his day, his way. His heart felt warm. “I did it,” he said. “I used my ideas. I stayed calm. I finished my task.”

He hung the chart on the wall in the study corner. Sunlight touched it and made the star twinkle. Squeaky’s tail swished like a happy brush.

Then he shared his lesson with a whisper: “When something is hard, breathe slow. Use your clever ideas. Try your own way. Be patient. You can make something wonderful.”

He smiled and said, “Creative expression helps me show my day, my feelings, and my ideas. I can rely on myself, and I can complete my tasks, one small step at a time.”

The End.
