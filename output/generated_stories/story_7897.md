# Kip the Kangaroo Learns to Speak Up

In a sunny backyard filled with green grass and colorful flowers, lived a little kangaroo joey named Kip. Kip loved to hop and bounce under the warm sunshine, but today something was bothering him.

"Hop, hop, hop!" went Kip's feet on the soft grass. But instead of his usual happy bouncing, his hops felt slow and sad. His tummy felt funny, like butterflies were dancing inside.

Mama Kangaroo noticed Kip wasn't bouncing as high as usual. "Good morning, Kip!" she called cheerfully. "Are you ready for our morning exercise?"

Kip just nodded quietly and gave a tiny hop. Usually, he would shout "Yes, yes, yes!" and bounce in circles with excitement.

"Let's do our stretches!" said Mama, reaching her arms up to the bright blue sky. "Touch your toes, then reach for the clouds!"

Kip tried to stretch, but his heart wasn't in it. He kept looking at the big oak tree in the corner of the yard. Yesterday, he had tried to hop all the way to that tree, but he couldn't make it. All the other animals made it look so easy!

"Now let's practice our biggest hops!" Mama said, demonstrating with a magnificent bounce that made her shadow dance on the grass.

Kip took a deep breath and tried his best hop. But when he looked at the oak tree, still so far away, his eyes filled with tears.

"Oh my," said Mama Kangaroo, hopping over to Kip. "Your face looks worried, and your shoulders look droopy. Can you tell me what's making you feel sad?"

Kip looked down at his feet. He wanted to tell Mama, but the words felt stuck in his throat like peanut butter.

"It's okay to share your feelings," Mama said gently, sitting down on the warm grass beside him. "When we talk about what's bothering us, it helps our hearts feel lighter."

Kip took a big breath that smelled like fresh morning air and flowers. "Mama," he said in a small voice, "I tried to hop to the big tree yesterday, but I couldn't do it. I'm not strong enough. I'm not a good hopper."

"Oh, sweet Kip!" Mama said, wrapping him in a soft, warm hug. "Thank you for telling me how you feel. That took courage!"

"But it's true," Kip sniffled. "I can't hop as far as the big animals."

Mama smiled. "Kip, you're still growing! Your legs are getting stronger every single day. Watch this." She pointed to a flower that was closer than the tree. "Can you hop to that yellow flower?"

"Yes!" said Kip, and he bounded over easily.

"Now try that red flower," Mama suggested, pointing to one a little farther away.

Kip hopped to it with no problem! "I did it!"

"You see? You CAN hop far! And every day you exercise and practice, you'll be able to hop even farther. Maybe tomorrow you'll reach that tree!"

Kip felt the butterflies in his tummy turn into happy bubbles. "Really?"

"Really! And Kip, I'm so proud of you for using your words to tell me how you felt. When we communicate our feelings, people who love us can help us feel better."

From that day on, whenever Kip felt worried or sad, he remembered to use his words. And sure enough, after many days of exercise and practice, Kip hopped all the way to the big oak tree!

"I did it, Mama! And I told you how I felt, and you helped me!" Kip cheered.

"That's what communication and practice can do!" Mama laughed.

The End.
