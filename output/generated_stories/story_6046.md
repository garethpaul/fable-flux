# Berry's Warm-Kitchen Song

Sunlight peeked into the warm kitchen. The room smelled like toast and cinnamon. The kettle hummed a soft shhh, shhh. A small blue bird named Berry sat on the windowsill. Berry loved to sing in the mornings.

“Good morning, spoon. Good morning, cup,” Berry chirped. “Tweet, tweet, tralala!”

Nina, a little girl, held her warm cup of cocoa. She smiled. “I love your song, Berry,” she said. “It makes the kitchen feel cozy.”

Berry puffed his feathers, proud and happy. Singing was part of Berry’s routine. He always sang after the sun peeked in and before the toast popped up. He felt calm when he followed his morning steps: stretch, sip water, sing.

As Berry lifted his beak to sing again, he heard a tiny sound. It was a rustle, like a whisper, from the corner by the recycling bin.

“Did you hear that?” Berry asked.

Nina tilted her head. “I think so. Maybe it’s the wind.”

But the warm kitchen had closed windows. Berry hopped closer. He saw a small gray mouse peeking from behind a paper bag. The little mouse’s whiskers trembled.

“Hello,” Berry sang softly. “Are you okay?”

The mouse squeaked. “I’m Mallow. I… I’m lost. I was looking for crumbs. But the floor is too bright, and my tummy is rumbly.”

Nina knelt down slowly. “Hi, Mallow. You look scared.”

Mallow nodded. “I don’t like loud sounds. And I can’t find my home.”

Berry felt a flutter in his chest. He wanted to help. But Berry also liked his calm routine. He liked clean counters and tidy crumbs. He worried that helping might make a mess. He worried his song would stop.

Berry took a gentle breath. “We can be kind,” he told himself. “Compassion means caring when someone needs help.”

Nina looked at Berry. “What should we do?”

Berry thought of a strategy. “Let’s make a quiet plan,” he said. “First, we keep the kitchen safe and clean. Second, we help Mallow find food and a way back outside. Third, we keep our routine calm.”

Nina nodded. “That sounds smart.”

Berry fluttered to the water dish by the sink. “Step one,” he chirped. “I will share a tiny sip of water.” He dipped his beak and nudged a drop toward Mallow in a little bottle cap.

“Thank you,” Mallow whispered, sipping carefully.

Nina found a few oat crumbs and a slice of apple. “Step two,” she said. “Healthy snack, small and neat.” She placed the food on a napkin so the kitchen stayed tidy.

“Yum,” squeaked Mallow, feeling braver.

Berry hopped to the window. Sun warmed his feathers. “Step three,” he sang. “We guide Mallow home.” He looked outside at the yard. There was a patch of leaves near a stone. “That looks cozy.”

Nina put on her soft slippers. “We can make an outdoor spot,” she said. “Safe for a small friend.”

Together, they worked with care. Nina folded a tiny cardboard house from an old box by the recycling bin. She placed it outside near the stone and leaves. She added a few oat crumbs in a little jar lid. She set a small cup of water. She made sure the door could stay closed so the kitchen stayed calm and clean.

Berry watched, heart light. “We are caring for our home and for the earth,” he sang. “We reduce, we reuse, and we help.”

Back inside, Mallow looked at the door. “It is bright,” he squeaked. “I feel shy.”

Berry remembered compassion again. “We can go slow,” he said. “I will sing a soft guide song.”

Berry sang a gentle tune. “Step, step, little friend. Warm hearts help you mend.” The notes floated like warm steam. Nina held the door open just a little, so the kitchen stayed warm and safe. Mallow waddled forward, sniff by sniff.

Outside, a breeze kissed their cheeks. The new little house waited. Mallow peeked in and smiled. “It’s cozy,” he squeaked. “I feel safe. Thank you.”

Nina clapped softly. “We did it!”

Berry’s feathers fluffed with joy. His routine was still here, still calm. The kitchen was warm and tidy. Mallow had a home. The earth had less waste, thanks to the reused box. Berry felt proud.

Mallow waved a tiny paw. “I will visit the window,” he said. “I will listen to your songs.”

Berry sang a happy ending song. “Kindness is strong. Kindness is bright. Helping a friend makes hearts feel light.”

Nina sipped her cocoa. “Berry, what did we learn today?”

Berry tilted his head and said, “We learned that compassion means caring for others, even when we like things just so. We learned to use a gentle plan, to keep our routine and our home clean. We learned to care for the earth by reusing and not wasting. When we help, we feel better too.”

The kettle hummed a soft shhh again. The toast popped. The warm kitchen glowed. Berry took a sip of water, stretched, and sang his morning song. Nina smiled. Outside, in his little house, Mallow listened, cozy and safe.

The End.
