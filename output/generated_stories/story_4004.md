# Ollie the Otter's Helpful Art Day

Ollie the otter wiggled his whiskers with excitement as he splashed into the bright, colorful art room. Rainbow paintbrushes stood tall in jars like colorful flowers, and sheets of white paper waited on tiny tables like fresh snow.

"Good morning, everyone!" chirped Miss Robin, the art teacher. "Today we're making friendship pictures!"

Ollie clapped his little paws together. He loved making art! But as he looked around, he noticed something that made his heart feel heavy. Little Benny Bunny sat alone at a corner table, his ears drooping sadly.

"What's wrong, Benny?" Ollie asked, sliding over with a gentle splash.

Benny's bottom lip trembled. "I can't reach the green paint," he whispered. "And I don't know how to make a tree. Everyone else is so good at art."

Ollie's brown eyes sparkled with an idea. "I can help you!" he said cheerfully. With his strong otter arms, he stretched up high and brought down the jar of green paint. "There you go!"

"Thank you," Benny said softly, but he still looked worried.

Ollie noticed Benny holding his paintbrush like a spoon. "Would you like me to show you how I hold my brush?" Ollie asked kindly. He demonstrated slowly, wrapping his paws around the brush handle. "Like this! It helps me paint better lines."

Benny tried copying Ollie. "Oh! That does feel better," he said, his ears perking up a little.

As they painted together, Ollie discovered something wonderful. When he helped Benny mix colors, Ollie learned that blue and yellow made the most beautiful green he'd ever seen! When he showed Benny how to make tree branches, Ollie figured out how to paint leaves that looked like they were dancing in the wind.

"Look, Ollie!" Benny exclaimed, pointing to his paper. "My tree has a face! It looks happy!"

"That's amazing!" Ollie cheered, doing a little spin. "You're such a creative artist, Benny!"

Soon, other friends noticed their joyful painting corner. Mia Mouse scurried over. "Can you help me make purple?" she squeaked.

"Of course!" Ollie and Benny said together. They showed Mia how red and blue swirled together like magic.

Then Freddy Frog hopped over, feeling stuck on his painting. The three new friends gathered around, sharing ideas and colors. The art room filled with happy chatter and the sweet smell of paint and creativity.

When art time ended, Miss Robin smiled at their colorful masterpieces. "What beautiful artwork! And what wonderful teamwork!"

Ollie looked at his own painting—a picture of all his friends painting together, with bright smiles and rainbow colors everywhere. It was his best artwork ever, and he had discovered something special: helping others made everything more fun and beautiful.

"Thank you for helping me, Ollie," Benny said, giving his new friend a gentle hug. "You made me feel brave to try new things."

Ollie's heart felt as warm as sunshine. "Helping friends makes me happy too," he said. "And look what we all discovered together—we're much better artists when we help each other!"

As they cleaned their brushes in the sparkly water, Ollie wiggled his whiskers with joy. He had learned that being helpful didn't just make others feel good—it helped him discover new things and make wonderful friends too.

The End.
