# Polly Paints with Kindness

In a cozy study corner, a bright green parrot named Polly loved to make things. The study corner smelled like fresh paper and orange crayons. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. There was a small table, soft pillows, and a basket of markers, glue, and shiny stickers.

“Squawk! I have an idea,” Polly chirped, hopping from the chair to the table. “I will make a kindness card for Grandma Parrot!”

Polly puffed her colorful feathers. She felt warm and happy inside. She pulled out paper, a red marker, and a glue stick. “I can do it! I will do it with care,” she said.

On the wall, there was a simple list of directions, written by Teacher Tia. It said:
1. Wash hands.
2. Sit safely.
3. Use one tool at a time.
4. Ask before using others’ things.
5. Clean up when done.

Polly read aloud, “Wash hands. Sit safely. Use one tool. Ask before using. Clean up.” She nodded. “I will follow the directions.”

Polly washed her little claws in a bowl of warm water. Splash, splash! The water felt cozy. She dried them on a soft towel. “All clean,” she said.

She sat on the small chair. It felt comfy and steady. “One tool at a time,” Polly whispered. She picked the red marker. Swish, swish went the marker on the paper. “Dear Grandma Parrot,” she wrote with careful lines. “You are sweet.”

Polly wanted to add stickers. But the sticker book was by her friend, Moon Mouse. Moon Mouse was building a paper boat. His ears wiggled as he folded. Polly took a deep breath. “Be kind. Ask before using,” she reminded herself.

“Moon Mouse,” Polly said in a gentle voice, “may I please use the star stickers?”

Moon Mouse looked up and smiled. “Yes, Polly. Thank you for asking. I am using the blue ones. You can use the gold stars.”

Polly felt proud. She was kind and also brave. “Thank you, Moon,” she said. She added two gold stars. They were shiny and smooth.

Then Polly saw a big bottle of glitter glue. It sparkled like tiny suns. “Oh! Glitter!” she chirped. Her feathers fluffed with excitement. She reached for the bottle with both wings.

Teacher Tia walked by and smiled. “Polly, what is the next direction?”

Polly paused, holding the bottle. “Use one tool at a time,” she said softly. Her wings relaxed. She put the glitter down. “I will finish with the marker first.”

“Good choice,” said Teacher Tia. “You are building skills. One step at a time.”

Polly wrote more kind words. “I like your stories. I like your songs.” She could almost hear Grandma Parrot’s soft song in her head. It sounded like warm tea and a cuddle. Polly wanted to add a special tradition: Grandma Parrot’s ribbon knot, passed down to every chick.

She found a red ribbon in the craft basket. It felt silky. But the ribbon belonged to the “Sharing Box.” A little sign said, “Ask first.”

Polly stood tall and spoke up. “Teacher Tia, may I please use the red ribbon for Grandma’s special knot? It is our family tradition.”

Teacher Tia nodded. “Yes, Polly. Thank you for asking with kind words. I can show you the knot if you like.”

Polly tilted her head. “Yes, please! I want to learn.”

Teacher Tia showed her slowly. “Over, under, loop, and pull.” Polly watched. She said the steps out loud. “Over, under, loop, and pull.” Her wings moved carefully. The ribbon made a neat little bow. Polly smiled. “I did it!”

Moon Mouse clapped. “Yay, Polly! You followed directions. You asked for help. You did the special knot.”

Polly beamed. She squeezed a tiny drop of glitter glue on the bow. Just one drop. It sparkled gently. “One tool at a time,” she whispered.

Suddenly, Baby Chick toddled over. His little feet went tap-tap-tap on the floor. He reached for Polly’s card with messy hands. “Shiny!” he peeped.

Polly put up a kind, firm wing. “Stop, please,” she said in a calm voice. “The glue is wet. You can look, but do not touch.”

Baby Chick blinked. He pulled back and nodded. “Can I help?” he asked.

Polly smiled. “Yes. You can help with a sticker. I will show you.” She handed him one gold star. “Place it here,” she said, pointing. “Gentle.”

Baby Chick pressed the star softly. “Gentle,” he echoed. He giggled. “I helped!”

“You did,” Polly said. “Thank you.”

Polly cleaned up her tools. Cap on the marker. Lid on the glue. Stickers back to Moon Mouse. Ribbon scraps in the bin. The study corner looked neat again. It smelled like paper and a little bit like sparkly glue.

She held up the card. It was bright and kind. It showed love and care and family tradition. “I made this with kindness,” Polly said.

Teacher Tia nodded. “Polly, you were kind to friends, you followed directions, and you spoke up with a strong and gentle voice. That is being assertive.”

Polly felt proud and warm. “I can make kind choices,” she said.

Later, Grandma Parrot visited. Polly gave her the card. Grandma’s eyes were shiny with happy tears. “This is the sweetest gift,” she cooed. “You followed our knot tradition. You used your skills. You were kind.”

Polly nuzzled close. “I learned that kindness helps everyone. Asking before using, taking turns, and using a strong, gentle voice keeps our study corner happy.”

Grandma Parrot hugged her. “Yes, dear. Kindness is our best craft.”

The End.
