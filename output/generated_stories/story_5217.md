# Miri Mouse and the Quiet Pantry

Miri was a kind little mouse with soft gray fur and bright, gentle eyes. She lived in a cozy corner of a big pantry. The pantry smelled like warm bread, sweet apples, and a tiny bit of cinnamon. Shelves stood tall like trees, holding jars that glowed in the soft light. Crackers crinkled in their box. Flour felt like cool, white snow under tiny paws.

Each morning, Miri woke up, stretched, and listened. She heard the hum of the fridge, the whisper of sugar in a jar when someone scooped it, and the slow tick-tick of a clock in the kitchen. Miri liked the pantry best when it was quiet and calm.

One sunny day, the pantry was not calm. It was noisy. It was busy. A jar of rice had tumbled over in the night. Little white grains shhh-shhh-shhhhed all over the wooden floor. A line of tiny ants marched in, chittering softly. A very shy moth fluttered near the flour bag, brushing the paper with papery wings. From inside a small tin, a beetle thumped, thump, thump, thump.

“Oh dear,” Miri said, patting her whiskers. “So many sounds. So many feelings.”

Her little heart beat fast. Her paws felt tingly. She wanted to hide. But Miri remembered what Nana Mouse always said. “When the world gets loud, make your inside quiet. Breathe like the pantry at night.”

Miri put a paw on her chest. “In… slow,” she whispered. She breathed in. She smelled apple and cinnamon and a tiny hint of peanut. “Out… slow.” Her belly softened. Her paws stopped tingling.

“Good,” she told herself. “Calm helps me think. Calm helps me help.”

She looked at the ants. They wiggled their tiny antennae. “Hello, friends,” Miri said in a soft voice. “There is rice for you, but we need to share and be neat. Can we make a line? We can carry some to the corner. I will sweep the rest into a little pile.”

The ants paused. One ant tapped Miri’s paw with a gentle tick. Then they turned into a straight, quiet line. Step, step, step. Miri smiled. “Thank you for listening.”

Next, Miri saw the shy moth. “Hello, Moth,” Miri whispered. The moth fluttered, bumping the flour bag. A dust cloud puffed up, soft as a whisper, smelling like toast. “I can help you,” Miri said. “Would you like the window light?”

Moth trembled. Miri remembered Nana Mouse’s story about the Night Lantern. “Nana told me once, ‘When you are scared, find a small light.’ I have a small light.” Miri opened a little jar with a glow-in-the-dark sticker on the lid. It glowed pale green, like a friendly moon.

“Follow the little light,” Miri said. She held the jar near the crack under the pantry door. Moth’s wings brushed the air—whirr, whirr—and then slipped through the crack, toward the bright kitchen window beyond. “Goodbye!” Miri called softly. The flour dust settled like quiet snow.

Thump, thump, thump. The beetle inside the tin was still bumping. “Hello, Beetle,” Miri said. “I hear you.” She tapped gently. “If I lift the lid, will you come out slowly? We can talk.”

The thumping stopped. Miri counted, “One… two… three.” She lifted the lid just a little. A shiny beetle peeked out with bright black eyes. “It is dark in there,” Beetle said, tiny voice shaky. “I felt lost.”

“I understand,” said Miri. “Sometimes I feel lost too.” She pointed her whiskers at the bottom shelf. “There is a cozy space by the tea tin. It smells like mint and honey. You can rest there.”

Beetle clicked softly. “Thank you.” He climbed out and toddled to the tea tin. He tucked in, quiet as a seed.

Now the pantry felt softer. The rice still needed tidying. Miri got her tiny broom made from a feather and a string. Swish, swish, swish. She swept the rice into a neat little hill. The ants carried some away, one grain at a time. Step, step, step.

Just then, a young mouse cousin, Tavi, peeked in. “Miri, I heard noise. Are you okay?” His ears were big. His eyes looked worried.

“I am okay,” Miri said. “I used calm. I used my voice. I used a little story.”

“A story?” Tavi asked, sitting down. He rubbed a rice grain between his paws. It felt hard and smooth.

“Yes,” Miri said. “Nana’s Night Lantern story. It helped me help the moth.” She patted the floor. “Would you like to hear it?”

“Yes, please,” said Tavi, tail curling with excitement.

Miri sat tall. Her voice was soft and slow. “Nana said, ‘Long ago, our mouse family shared stories in the pantry. When worries were big, we told a calm story. We took a big breath together. In… and out… and then we helped each other. Our story is our lantern. It helps us see.’”

Tavi breathed with Miri. In… out… His shoulders relaxed. He smiled. “I feel better,” he said.

Miri nodded. “Calm helps us speak kindly. Calm helps us listen. Calm helps us share old stories and make new ones.”

The pantry hummed again in a sweet, steady way. Honey smelled warm. Apples smelled bright. The clock went tick-tick, like a friendly heartbeat.

Tavi stood up. “Can I help sweep?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Miri. “We can do it together.” Swish, swish, swish. Their tiny brooms made soft music. The ants waved their antennae like little flags. Beetle peeked out and clicked a thanks.

When the floor was clean and the jars sat like quiet friends, Miri and Tavi sat with a crumb of cracker. They nibbled slowly. Crunch, crunch. They felt proud.

“What did we learn today?” Miri asked gently.

Tavi thought. “When things are noisy, we make our inside quiet,” he said. “We take slow breaths, we use kind words, and we tell calm stories. Then we can help.”

Miri’s eyes shone. “Yes,” she said. “Calmness, communication, and storytelling keep our pantry peaceful.”

They touched paws. The pantry glowed with hush and hope.

The End.
