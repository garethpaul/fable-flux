# Pebble the Penguin Builds a Garden Helper

Pebble was a cheerful penguin who loved his balcony garden. His little balcony was full of green leaves, bright flowers, and tiny pots that smelled like mint and lemon. In the morning sun, Pebble waddled out with a happy wiggle. He wore a small blue apron and a big smile.

“Good morning, garden!” Pebble chirped. He took a deep breath. The air smelled fresh and wet, like rain. He could hear a soft buzz from a friendly bee. He could feel the cool breeze ruffle his feathers.

Pebble checked his plants. “Hello, basil. Hello, tomato. Hello, sunflower!” he said. He touched the soil. “Uh-oh,” he whispered. “The tomato is thirsty.” The soil was dry and crumbly.

Pebble picked up his watering can. He leaned to water the tomato… and drip, drip, drip… water splashed onto his feet. “Brrr! Cold!” Pebble laughed. But then—oh no!—a strong breeze puffed across the balcony. The watering can tipped in Pebble’s flippers. Water spilled. A small pot wobbled, wibble-wobble—plop! The pot fell over and dirt scattered like cocoa powder.

Pebble blinked his big eyes. His happy wiggle paused. “This is a problem,” he said. He looked at the pots and the breeze. He felt a little worried. His heart thumped like a little drum.

Pebble put a flipper on his chest and took a slow breath. “I can solve this,” he told himself. “I can use my penguin brain. I can be calm and brave.” He stood up tall, as tall as a small penguin could.

Just then, a ladybird landed on a leaf. “Hello, little friend,” said Pebble. “I have a problem. The wind pushes my watering can. My pots fall. My tomato gets too little water.” The ladybird lifted its tiny red wings, as if listening.

Pebble nodded. “I will try a fix.” He set the pots back up. He pressed the soil gently. He cleaned the spilled dirt with a small broom. Swish, swish. He felt better already.

“Step one,” Pebble said. “I will make a plan. Plans help problems.” He got a small notepad from his apron. He drew simple pictures. A can. A pot. A wind. He hummed a tune, tap-tap-tap with his pencil.

“Step two,” he said. “I will test ideas.” He tried to hold the watering can tighter. The wind puffed. Whoosh! Pebble slipped a little. Splash! Water went on his toes again. Cold water. “That didn’t work,” he said. “It’s okay. I can try again.”

“Step three,” Pebble said. “I will build a helper.” His eyes sparkled. “A helper to water slowly. A helper that does not spill.” Pebble loved to build.

He looked around the balcony. He found a clean plastic bottle. He found some soft string. He found a tiny stick. He found a small clay bead. He smiled. “This is what I need,” he said. He spoke clearly to the breeze, “I am going to build now. Please be gentle.” The breeze tickled his cheek.

Pebble used his beak with care. Peck, peck. He made a tiny hole in the bottle cap. He pushed the little bead into the hole, snug but not tight. He tied the bottle to the tiny stick with the string. He set the stick next to the tomato pot. He filled the bottle with water. The bottle felt cool and sloshy in his flippers.

Drip… drip… drip… The water came out slowly, like raindrops. “Yes!” Pebble clapped his flippers. “A drip-drip helper!” The tomato plant’s leaves shimmered with tiny drops. They looked like little jewels.

But the wind was tricky. Whoosh! The bottle swayed. Pebble stood firm. “Stop,” he said kindly but strongly. “I need space to build safely.” He moved a wobbly pot away from the edge. He pressed it against the wall. He placed a smooth stone in the bottom of each pot to make it heavier. Clink, clink. He tested with a gentle nudge. The pots stayed steady.

Pebble tried the helper again. Drip… drip… drip… The tomato pot grew dark and damp. The smell was rich and earthy. Pebble smiled. “I did it!” he said. “I solved the problem with a plan.”

A pigeon on the next balcony cooed, “Coo-oo?” and tilted its head.

Pebble waved. “Hello, neighbor! I built a garden helper. It gives water slowly. Now the wind won’t splash it away.”

“Coo-oo!” the pigeon said, impressed.

Pebble cleaned his tools and tucked the pencil back in his apron. He patted the tomato plant gently. “How do you feel?” he asked. The leaves rustled softly, like a whisper. Pebble imagined they said, “Thank you.”

He took another deep breath. He felt proud and warm inside. He had used problem-solving. He had been calm. He had been brave. He had spoken up for what he needed. He had built something new.

Pebble sat in his tiny chair and sipped cool water from a cup. He watched the sun glow like honey on the leaves. The ladybird crawled along the tomato stem. The drip-drip helper kept dripping, like a tiny clock. Drip… drip… drip…

Pebble nodded. “Today I learned: When there is a problem, I can make a plan, try ideas, and build a solution. I can use kind, strong words to keep things safe. I can keep trying until it works.”

He smiled at his balcony garden. “Good job, team,” he said. “We did it together.”

The End.
