# Sunny’s Calm Pause in the Home Office

Sunny the squirrel lived in a cozy nook inside a bright home office. The office smelled like fresh paper and warm tea. Sunbeams made squares on the floor. The computer hummed softly, and tiny plants sipped water from a little drip tray. Sunny was a resourceful squirrel who loved tidy stacks and reusing things. He kept paper clips in a peanut shell cup and pencils in a clean jar.

“Today I fix the wobbly pencil house,” Sunny chirped, tail flicking. He had built a tiny house from scrap cardboard and rubber bands. It was a favorite place to nap.

Just then, a new sound filled the room—tap-tap-tap! It was Pip, the chipmunk neighbor, drumming on the desk with a ruler. Pip liked big noises and speedy play.

“Hi, Sunny! Let’s make a rocket!” Pip said, eyes bright.

Sunny’s ears perked. “A rocket from scraps? Fun!” He gathered cardboard bits, soft fabric, and a clean, empty paper tube. He loved turning old things into new things. It was good for the Earth.

They worked side by side. Snip-snip went the safety scissors. Crinkle-crinkle went the paper. The office smelled like glue and orange peels from Sunny’s snack.

“Let’s test it!” Pip said, bouncing. He puffed his cheeks and blew. Whoosh! The rocket zoomed across the desk—and bumped Sunny’s pencil house.

The house wobbled. It tipped. It crashed.

Sunny’s chest got tight. His cheeks felt hot. His tail puffed like a dandelion. Inside, a big storm swirled. He wanted to shout.

Pip froze. “I’m sorry! I didn’t mean—”

Sunny squeezed his paws. He remembered his calm plan on the wall. It was a little sign he made from a cereal box: Stop. Breathe. Name feelings. Use gentle words.

He whispered, “Stop.” He placed his paw on his heart.
He breathed in through his nose. In… one, two. He breathed out through his mouth. Out… one, two, three.
He named it. “I feel mad. I feel sad.”

Pip’s eyes went soft. “Are you mad at me?”

Sunny nodded. “Yes. I feel mad because my house broke. I worked hard.”

He kept his voice low and slow. “I want to talk kindly.”

Pip set the ruler down. The room got quiet, except for the soft hum of the computer and the tick-tick of the wall clock.

“I’m sorry, Sunny,” Pip said again. “I was too fast. How can I help?”

Sunny took another breath. The hot feeling cooled, like tea with milk. He twitched his whiskers. “Thank you for saying sorry. Let’s fix it together. And let’s make a safe space for flying.”

They looked around. Sunny pointed to a big empty box that held printer paper before. He had saved it for crafts. “We can reuse this box,” he said. “The rocket can fly inside. Safe and tidy. Good for the Earth.”

Pip clapped. “Yes!”

They set the box on the floor. Tap-tap went the box flaps. Rustle-rustle went the paper as they lined the inside with soft scraps. They drew a runway with a green crayon. They made a sign: Rocket Zone. No Crash!

They used gentle glue to mend the pencil house. Sunny smoothed a leaf sticker on the roof. Pip held the walls steady. The house smelled like fresh glue and pine needles from a tiny cone they used as a knob.

“Ready?” Pip asked.

Sunny smiled. “Ready.”

Whoosh! The rocket zoomed inside the box and landed on the soft scraps. No crash. Both friends cheered. “Hooray!”

Pip looked at Sunny. “Thank you for not yelling. It helps me listen.”

Sunny nodded. “Stopping and breathing helped me. Using gentle words helped us both. We solved the problem.”

They high-fived with tiny paws. Pat! Sunny’s tail relaxed and grew smooth again.

They added more safety signs. “Ask before launching,” Sunny read. “Use soft breath.” Pip added, “Keep treasures safe.” They reused old sticky notes and a ribbon from a gift. It felt good to care for their things and the planet.

After playtime, they cleaned up together. Clip-clip went paper bits into the recycle bin. Tap-tap went pencils back in the jar. The office looked neat and calm. The warm tea smell drifted again.

Pip said, “If I get upset next time, I will try your calm plan. Stop. Breathe. Name feelings. Use gentle words.”

Sunny beamed. “We can help each other remember.”

The sunbeam on the floor moved, making the room glow gold. Sunny curled up by his mended pencil house. Pip sat nearby, tail curled like a comma.

Sunny said softly, “Today I learned that feelings can be big, but I can be bigger when I stop, breathe, and speak kindly.”

Pip added, “And we can make safe plans, fix mistakes, and reuse things to help our home and Earth.”

They smiled. The office hummed. All was peaceful.

The End.
