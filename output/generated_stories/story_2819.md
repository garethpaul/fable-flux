# Kora Koala and the Fair Snack

Every morning, Kora the calm koala followed a gentle routine. She stretched her soft, fuzzy arms. She blinked her sleepy eyes. Then she padded to the pantry to help get snacks for everyone.

The pantry smelled like warm oats and sweet raisins. Paper bags rustled. Jars made tiny clink sounds. “Good morning, pantry,” Kora whispered. “I am ready.”

Kora had a job she loved. She used a small chart with pictures. The chart showed who got what snack each day. It kept things fair. It kept things kind. It was their justice chart.

“Today,” Kora said softly, tracing the pictures with her paw, “Jam for Joey, crackers for Pip, and apple slices for Lee.”

Just then, Joey the kangaroo hopped in. His tail thumped the floor. “I want extra jam,” Joey blurted. “All the jam! Please!”

Kora took a calm breath. She listened. Listening was part of her routine. “Joey, I hear you want extra jam,” she said kindly. “The chart helps us share. Everyone gets a turn.”

Joey’s ears drooped. “But I’m so, so hungry! My tummy says ‘glurp glurp!’”

Kora nodded. “Hungry tummies are important.” She tapped the chart. “Justice means we are fair to everyone, even when we really, really want more.”

Pip the possum peeked in, whiskers twitching. “What’s that sound?” Pip asked.

“It’s my tummy,” Joey sighed.

Lee the lizard slid along the shelf. “Hiss-hello,” Lee said. “Apple day!”

Kora opened a jar. Pop! The lid made a happy sound. Sweet jam smell filled the pantry—like sunshine on berries. Everyone’s nose wiggled.

Kora looked at the shelves. The jam jar was almost empty. “Oh,” she said softly. “We do not have much jam today.”

Joey’s eyes went wide. “No jam?”

Kora stayed calm. This was a problem, but she could use her resourcefulness. “Let’s be helpers,” she said. “We can find a fair way.”

She checked the chart again. She checked the baskets and the little scale for weighing snacks. She found a tiny spoon, a cutting board, and a clean cup. “We can make a plan,” Kora said.

First, Kora showed the tiny spoon. “We will share the jam equally. One little spoon for each of us who has jam on the chart today.” She peeked. “Today jam is for Joey.” She smiled. “But the jar is small. It is fair to save some for snack time later, too.”

Joey’s tail thumped again. “Only a tiny spoon?”

Kora nodded. “A tiny spoon now, and we make a tasty spread so it feels big. That keeps things fair and kind. That is justice.”

Kora opened a bag of warm oats. She poured a little into a bowl. Rustle, rustle. She added a splash of water. Drip, drip. She showed Joey the small jam spoon. “We will mix the jam into the oats. Swirl, swirl.” The pantry smelled like berries and breakfast.

Joey tasted a bite. His eyes sparkled. “Mmm! Sweet and cozy.”

Pip tugged on Kora’s sleeve. “But what about me?”

Kora smiled. “Pip, the chart says crackers for you. Let’s make them special.” She found a tiny shaker of cinnamon. “We can dust a little on top.” Sprinkle, sprinkle. Pip giggled. “It’s like snow!”

Lee blinked slowly. “Apples?”

“Apples, yes,” Kora said. She sliced crisp apple wedges. Crunch. The sound was happy and bright. She arranged them in a circle like a sun. “A sunny apple plate for you.”

Joey watched the apples. “They look fun.”

Kora nodded. “They do. And look, Joey—after your jam oats, you can have two apple slices, because apples are on the ‘extra for sharing’ shelf. That shelf is for anyone, any day. It keeps snack time fair when jars are low.”

Joey thought. He put a paw on his tummy. “My tummy says ‘thank you.’”

Pip whispered, “I like the chart. It helps me feel safe.”

Lee licked a bit of cinnamon from the air and smiled. “Fair is friendly.”

Kora gently wiped the counter. She put the tiny spoon back. She closed the jars with soft clicks. She felt warm inside. “Our routine helps us be fair,” she said. “And when a problem pops up, we use our minds and our hearts.”

Joey hopped in a small circle. “I wanted all the jam,” he said, “but I do not need all the jam. I can share.”

Kora patted his shoulder. “That is justice: making sure everyone is treated right.”

They sat on a soft mat by the pantry door. They ate together. Slurp, crunch, nibble. The sounds were happy and calm. The pantry felt peaceful again.

When the bowls were empty, Kora added a new picture to the chart: a little jar with a smile. “This means we check supplies before snack,” she explained. “We can plan ahead together.”

Pip clapped. “Resource-full!” Pip said, trying the big word.

Kora beamed. “Resourceful, yes.”

Lee raised his tail. “And I accept apple day,” he said. “Apples are great.”

Kora took a deep breath. “We listened. We shared. We were fair.” She hugged her friends. “That is our just and gentle pantry.”

The End.
