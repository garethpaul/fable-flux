# Ellie's Big Feelings

In a cozy nursery filled with soft pillows and colorful toys, lived a gentle elephant calf named Ellie. Her nursery had warm yellow walls decorated with pictures of elephants from around the world - some splashing in rivers, others painting with their trunks, and families sharing meals together.

One sunny morning, Ellie was excited to play with her favorite wooden blocks. She had big plans to build the tallest tower ever! But when she reached for her special red block, it wasn't there.

"Where is my red block?" Ellie trumpeted, her little trunk swinging back and forth. She looked under her soft blue blanket and behind her stuffed giraffe, but the red block was nowhere to be found.

Ellie's chest began to feel hot and tight. Her ears flapped faster and faster. "I NEED my red block!" she stomped her feet on the soft carpet. "My tower won't be perfect without it!"

Mama Elephant heard the commotion and gently walked into the nursery. "Oh my, Ellie. I can see you're feeling very upset. Your ears are flapping and your voice is getting loud."

"I can't find my red block!" Ellie huffed, her trunk curling up in frustration. "Everything is WRONG!"

Mama Elephant sat down on the fluffy rug beside Ellie. "When elephants feel angry, we need to take deep breaths through our trunks. Would you like to try that with me?"

Ellie sniffled. "Okay, Mama."

Together, they took three slow, deep breaths. "In through the trunk... and out," Mama said softly. Ellie felt her shoulders relax a little.

"Now," said Mama, "let's think about this problem. What are some other ways we could make your tower special?"

Ellie wiped her eyes with her trunk. "I... I don't know. I really wanted the red block."

"I understand. Red is a beautiful color. But look around your nursery - what other wonderful blocks do you have?"

Ellie looked at her collection. She had blue blocks like the ocean, green blocks like jungle leaves, and yellow blocks bright as sunshine. "Well... I do have this pretty purple block. And this orange one smells like flowers."

"What a great idea! In some countries, elephants paint with many colors to celebrate festivals. Maybe your tower could be like a rainbow celebration!"

Ellie's eyes brightened. "Really? Elephants paint with colors?"

"Oh yes! And they work together, just like we can work together to solve problems."

Ellie picked up the purple block and placed it carefully. Then the orange one, then a blue one. "Mama, will you help me negotiate with my blocks? This yellow one wants to be on top, but the green one thinks it should go there."

Mama chuckled. "Of course! Let's ask each block what would make them happy in our tower."

Together, they built the most beautiful rainbow tower Ellie had ever seen. Each block had a special place, and the tower sparkled in the sunlight streaming through the nursery window.

"You know what, Mama?" Ellie said, stepping back to admire their creation. "This tower is even better than what I first imagined. And I feel much better too."

"That's because you learned to manage your big feelings," Mama said, giving Ellie a gentle trunk hug. "When we feel angry, we can breathe deeply, think of solutions, and create something beautiful together."

Ellie smiled, her trunk swaying happily. "Next time I feel upset, I'll remember to breathe and think of new ideas!"

From that day on, whenever Ellie felt angry or frustrated, she would take deep breaths, talk about her feelings, and find creative solutions. Her nursery became filled with even more colorful creations, each one a reminder that problems can become beautiful opportunities.

The End.
