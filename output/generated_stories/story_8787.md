# Milo Mouse and the Mixed-Up Garden Tools

Milo Mouse lived in a cozy corner of Mr. Green's garden shed. The shed smelled like fresh earth and sunshine, with wooden shelves full of colorful flower pots and shiny garden tools hanging on the walls.

One bright morning, Milo woke up excited to help Mr. Green in the garden. He loved watching the flowers grow and listening to the birds chirp outside. But when he peeked out of his little home, something was very wrong.

"Oh no!" squeaked Milo. All the garden tools were scattered everywhere! The rake was upside down, the watering can was rolling around, and the flower seeds were spilled all over the floor.

Milo's whiskers twitched with worry. He felt his tiny heart beating fast, and his paws started to feel hot and shaky. "This is terrible! Mr. Green will be so upset!" he said, his voice getting louder.

The little mouse began to feel very angry. His fur stood up, and he wanted to stomp his feet. "Why did this happen? It's not fair!" he squeaked loudly.

But then Milo remembered what his mama had taught him about big feelings. She always said, "When you feel angry, little one, take three deep breaths and count to five."

Milo sat down on a soft pile of leaves. He closed his eyes and breathed in slowly through his tiny pink nose. "One... two... three," he counted. The sweet smell of flowers helped him feel calmer.

"Now I can think better," Milo said to himself. He looked around the messy shed with clear eyes. "Maybe the wind blew the door open last night. I can fix this!"

Milo started with the smallest things first. He pushed the round seeds back into their packets with his nose. They made a gentle rattling sound that made him smile. Next, he used his strong teeth to drag the watering can back to its spot.

The rake was much bigger than Milo, but he didn't give up. He pushed and pulled, taking breaks when he felt tired. "I can do this!" he told himself. "One step at a time."

When Mr. Green opened the shed door, he found everything neat and tidy. Milo was sitting proudly next to a small pile of seeds he couldn't quite reach.

"Well, hello there, little friend," said Mr. Green with a warm smile. "Did you help clean up my shed?"

Milo nodded honestly. "The tools got mixed up, and I felt very angry at first. But I took deep breaths and fixed what I could!"

Mr. Green knelt down and gently picked up the last seeds. "Thank you for being so helpful and honest, Milo. And you know what? Feeling angry is okay, but you handled it just right."

That day, Milo helped Mr. Green plant new flowers outside. The sun felt warm on his fur, and the soft dirt was fun to dig in. Every time Milo felt frustrated, he remembered to breathe deeply and keep trying.

As the day ended, Milo curled up in his cozy corner, feeling proud and happy. He had learned that angry feelings come and go, but taking deep breaths and not giving up always helps make things better.

The End.
