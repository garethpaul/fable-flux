# Sunny Song in the Warm Kitchen

In a warm kitchen full of yummy smells, a little singing bird named Sunny perched on the windowsill. The sun slipped in and made golden squares on the floor. The kettle hummed. The toast popped up with a happy click. Sunny loved to sing here. The kitchen felt cozy, like a hug.

“Tweet-a-dee! Tweet-a-doo!” sang Sunny, wiggling tiny toes on the sill.

“Good morning, Sunny,” said Mia, a child with big curious eyes. She wore a red apron with a blueberry stain. “Your songs make the kitchen feel extra warm.”

Sunny puffed up with pride. “Thank you! I sing and sing and sing!”

Mia opened a jar of peanut butter. It smelled nutty and sweet. She spread it on toast, scrape, scrape. “Do you want to listen to the kettle’s song too?” she asked.

The kettle whistled. The clock ticked. The fridge hummed. The spoon clinked. The warm kitchen had a gentle music of its own.

Sunny chirped louder to join the music. “Tweet-a-DEE! Tweet-a-DOO!”

Sunny sang and sang. Sunny sang when the pancakes sizzled. Sunny sang when the jam jar popped. Sunny sang when Mia giggled and when the cat, Marmalade, purred by the radiator.

But then something changed. Sunny’s throat felt scratchy, like a crumb stuck there. Sunny’s wings drooped a little.

“Tweet… a… dee?” Sunny tried. The sound came out squeaky and thin.

Mia looked up. “Sunny, are you okay?”

Sunny swallowed. “My song feels stuck.”

Mia put her toast down and came close. “Maybe your throat is tired.”

“I have to sing,” said Sunny, worried. “Everyone needs my song!”

Mia nodded kindly. “Your song is lovely. But you need care too. Let’s take care of you first. That is called self-care.”

Sunny blinked. “Self-care?”

“It means being kind to your body and feelings,” Mia said. “When we rest and drink water, we feel better.”

Sunny tilted her head. “But if I stop, the kitchen will be quiet.”

Marmalade purred louder and rubbed the chair leg. The kettle sighed. The pancakes sizzled softly. The kitchen still sang a little.

Mia smiled. “The kitchen can sing with you. And I can help build a little Rest Nest for your voice.”

“A Rest Nest?” Sunny’s eyes shone. Sunny was open to trying.

Mia gathered soft things. She took a small clean cloth, fluffy and warm. She folded it, pat, pat. She put it near the sunny window where the light felt gentle. She set out a tiny lid with water. She placed a small saucer with honey droplets.

“We are building a place for your body to feel better,” she said. “Step one: drink water. Step two: rest your voice. Step three: warm honey.”

Sunny looked at the little nest. It was simple and cozy, like the warm kitchen itself.

“Tweet?” Sunny asked softly.

“You can whisper,” said Mia. “Try one sip.”

Sunny dipped a tiny beak into the water. Sip. The water was cool and smooth. Sunny felt a little “ahh” inside.

“Now a teeny taste of honey,” said Mia.

Sunny touched the honey. It was warm and sweet on Sunny’s tongue. “Mmm,” Sunny whispered.

Sunny tucked into the cloth, fluff against feathers. The sunlight made Sunny feel sleepy. The sounds of the kitchen were gentle: tick, hum, purr, sizzle. Sunny closed one eye, then the other.

“I don’t want to let you down,” Sunny murmured.

“You won’t,” Mia whispered back. “Taking care of you helps your song come back. Problems can be solved when we care for ourselves first.”

Sunny rested. Sunny breathed in and out, slow and soft. The warm kitchen took care of Sunny too.

Later, Mia brought a tiny cup of warm steam. “This is just warm water near you,” she said. “Breathe in the steam.”

Sunny breathed in. Warmth tickled Sunny’s beak. It felt like a little blanket for the throat.

After a while, Sunny stretched. The scratchy feeling was smaller, like a crumb that had melted away.

“How do you feel?” asked Mia.

“Better,” Sunny said in a small, clear voice. Sunny waited, remembering the Rest Nest steps. “I will try one gentle note.”

Sunny sang a soft “tweet.” It was smooth. Sunny smiled. Mia clapped. Marmalade blinked and flicked a tail.

Sunny wanted to sing a big song, but then paused. “I will build up slowly,” Sunny said. “That is smart self-care.”

“Good plan,” said Mia. “That is problem-solving!”

Sunny hummed a little tune, like a warm cup of tea in music. The kitchen sounds joined in: the spoon went clink, the clock went tick-tock, the pancakes went sizzle-sizzle. Sunny listened, then added small notes between the sounds.

“Listen first, then sing,” Sunny said. “I can be open-minded. I can try new ways.”

Mia nodded. “You built a Rest Nest. You tried water, honey, and steam. You rested. You solved the problem with care.”

Sunny fluffed feathers and sang a gentle, happy song. It danced with the warm kitchen music but never pushed too hard. Sunny felt calm and proud.

Mia handed Sunny a tiny chart she drew with crayons. “Self-Care Plan,” it said with colorful letters.
- Drink water.
- Rest voice.
- Breathe warm steam.
- Start slow.
- Listen to your body.

Sunny read each line. “I can do that,” Sunny said. “I can care for me, and then my song can care for others.”

The warm kitchen glowed. The air smelled like pancakes and honey. Mia ate her toast, crunch, crunch. Marmalade snored. Sunny sang a sweet, soft tune that made the morning feel bright.

Sunny learned that caring for yourself is not stopping the music. It is tuning the instrument inside you. With self-care, problems can be solved, and songs can shine again.

The End.
