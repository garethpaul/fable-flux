# Ollie's Honest Art Adventure

Ollie the otter loved the creative art room. The bright colors made his whiskers twitch with excitement. Red paint, blue paint, yellow paint - so many wonderful colors to explore!

"Good morning, Ollie!" said Miss Robin, the art teacher. "Today we're making friendship pictures for our families."

Ollie clapped his little paws together. "Hooray! I love making pictures!"

The art room smelled like fresh crayons and sparkly glue. Ollie picked up a big paintbrush and dipped it in purple paint. Swish, swish, swish! He painted happy flowers on his paper.

"This is for Mama and Papa," Ollie whispered to himself.

Next to him, Luna the bunny was painting too. She had the most beautiful picture - a rainbow with puffy white clouds. Ollie's eyes grew wide.

"Wow, Luna! Your rainbow is so pretty," said Ollie.

Luna smiled. "Thank you! I worked really hard on it."

When Luna went to wash her brush, something terrible happened. Ollie accidentally knocked over his water cup! Splash! Purple water spilled all over Luna's beautiful rainbow picture.

"Oh no!" gasped Ollie. His tummy felt funny and his paws started shaking.

Luna came back and saw her ruined picture. Her bottom lip began to wobble. "My rainbow... it's all purple now."

Ollie's heart felt heavy like a rock. He could pretend it wasn't him. He could say someone else did it. But deep down, Ollie knew what was right.

"Luna," Ollie said softly, his voice barely a whisper. "I'm sorry. I spilled water on your picture. It was an accident, but it was me."

Luna's eyes filled with tears. "You did this?"

"Yes," Ollie nodded, looking down at his webbed feet. "I'm really, really sorry. I feel terrible."

Miss Robin heard them talking and came over. "What happened here?"

"I made a mistake," Ollie explained honestly. "I knocked over my water and ruined Luna's rainbow. I didn't mean to, but I did it."

Miss Robin smiled gently. "Thank you for telling the truth, Ollie. That takes courage."

Luna wiped her eyes. "You told the truth even though you were scared?"

"Yes," said Ollie. "Because being honest is important. And because you're my friend."

Luna's face softened. "I forgive you, Ollie. I know it was an accident."

"But your beautiful rainbow is ruined," Ollie said sadly.

"Maybe we can fix it together?" suggested Miss Robin.

Ollie's eyes lit up. "Yes! I want to help!"

Working side by side, Ollie and Luna discovered something magical. The purple water had made the rainbow look like it was reflected in a puddle!

"It's even more beautiful now!" laughed Luna. "It looks like a rainbow lake!"

Ollie beamed with happiness. His tummy felt light and warm again. "Really?"

"Really! Thank you for being honest with me, Ollie. That made me feel better than any picture could."

Miss Robin hung both their pictures on the wall. "These show real friendship," she said proudly.

As they cleaned up together, Ollie learned something important. Telling the truth, even when it's scary, always feels better than hiding. And real friends forgive each other when mistakes happen.

"Tomorrow, let's paint together from the start!" said Luna.

"Yes!" agreed Ollie, his tail wiggling happily. "That sounds perfect!"

The End.
