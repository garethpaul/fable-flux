# Polly's Messy Study Corner

In a cozy study corner filled with books and toys, lived a bright green parrot named Polly. She had beautiful red feathers on her head and loved to create wonderful things with her colorful supplies.

"Squawk! Time to make art!" Polly chirped happily every morning. She would flutter her wings and dive into her craft box, pulling out crayons, papers, glue sticks, and sparkly stickers.

But Polly had a big problem. Her study corner was always messy! Crayons rolled under the desk, papers scattered everywhere, and her favorite purple marker was always missing.

"Where is my purple marker?" Polly squawked, looking under piles of paper. "I need it for my rainbow picture!"

She searched and searched, tossing more things around. Soon, her study corner looked like a colorful tornado had blown through it. Books were upside down, pencils were on the floor, and her art supplies were everywhere except where they should be.

Polly's friend, Mrs. Owl, peeked into the study corner. "Oh my, Polly! What happened here?" she hooted softly.

"I can't find anything!" Polly cried, her bright eyes looking sad. "Every time I want to create something beautiful, I spend all my time looking for my supplies instead of making art!"

Mrs. Owl nodded wisely. "I think I can help you, dear. Would you like to learn about organization? It's a special skill that makes everything easier to find."

"Yes, please!" Polly chirped, perking up her red feathers.

Mrs. Owl showed Polly how to sort her supplies. "Let's put all the crayons in this red box," she explained. "And all the markers go in the blue box. See how nice and neat they look?"

Polly watched carefully. "Ooh! The colors look so pretty all lined up!"

"Now," Mrs. Owl continued, "let's put all your papers in this green folder. And your stickers can go in these small containers."

Together, they cleaned the entire study corner. Polly learned to put each thing in its special place. The crayons went in the red box with a soft *click*. The markers made a gentle *tap* as they settled in the blue box. The papers made a satisfying *whoosh* as they slid into the folder.

"This feels so good!" Polly said, fluffing her feathers with pride. "Everything has its own home!"

Mrs. Owl smiled. "Now, every time you finish using something, remember to put it back in its special place. This shows respect for your supplies and helps you find them quickly."

The next morning, Polly wanted to make a picture of a sunny day. "I need my yellow crayon!" she said. She went straight to the red box, opened it, and there was her yellow crayon, right where it belonged!

"Squawk! This is wonderful!" Polly celebrated. In just five minutes, she had all her supplies ready. She drew a beautiful sun, fluffy clouds, and green grass. She even found her purple marker easily in the blue box to add some pretty flowers.

From that day on, Polly kept her study corner organized. She learned that taking care of her things helped her create even more beautiful art. Her friends loved visiting her tidy corner, and Polly felt proud and happy.

"Organization is the best skill ever!" Polly chirped, admiring her neat and colorful study corner.

The End.
