# Bounce, Stretch, and Share!

In a sunny, playful living room, a friendly bear cub named Bibi rolled a bright red ball across a soft carpet. The carpet felt warm and fuzzy under Bibi’s paws. The curtains swished like a whisper. The room smelled like orange slices and fresh bread. It was exercise time!

“Ready to move?” Bibi giggled, bouncing the ball. “My paws are wiggly!”

Bibi’s friends came over to play. There was Nia, a kitten with shiny black fur who wore a bright green scarf. There was Javi, a kangaroo joey with gentle eyes and a blue headband. And there was Mila, a little mouse with tiny pink ears and a golden bracelet from her grandma. Each friend looked different, and each friend loved to move.

“Let’s make a fun exercise game,” said Bibi. “We can take turns and cheer for everyone.”

Nia purred, “I like to stretch long and tall.” She reached up, up, up. “Stretching feels good.”

Javi hopped in place. “I like to jump! Boing, boing, boing!”

Mila twirled slowly. “I like to balance and breathe. In… and out.” She closed her eyes for a moment and smiled.

Bibi clapped soft, fuzzy paws. “We can try them all. We’ll listen, we’ll share, and we’ll be fair. Everyone gets a turn!”

They made a sign with crayons and tape. It said, “Bounce, Stretch, and Share!” Bibi placed it on the couch. The couch felt squishy, like a giant marshmallow.

“First,” said Bibi, “let’s warm up. Shake your paws, shake your tails!” They shook gently. The floor thumped a little under Javi’s hops. Nia’s scarf swooshed. Mila’s bracelet jingled.

“Now, Nia’s turn to lead,” said Bibi.

Nia smiled. “We will stretch like tall trees. Reach up to the sun.” They felt their arms grow long. “Now bend like rainbows.” They curved. Their muscles felt tingly and awake. “Wiggle your whiskers!” Nia giggled. Bibi didn’t have whiskers, but wiggled his nose anyway. Everyone laughed.

“Javi’s turn!” Bibi said. “Ready, leader?”

Javi nodded. “We jump like kangaroos. Soft knees, soft land.” Boing! Boing! Bibi’s paws bounced on the carpet. “Count to five,” Javi said. “One, two, three, four, five.” They stopped to breathe. “Now tiny jumps.” Tip-tip-tip. “Now big jumps!” BOING! The ball bounced too and rolled under the table. They all paused.

“I want big jumps again!” Bibi said quickly. His paws buzzed.

Mila held up a tiny paw. “Fairness time. After Javi, I lead. Then you can jump again, Bibi.”

Bibi took a slow breath. He placed his paws on his belly like Mila. “In… and out.” His paws stopped buzzing. He smiled. “You are right. Let’s be fair.”

Mila’s turn came. She said, “We balance like little boats. One foot down, one paw up.” Wobble, wobble. They held their arms out like wings. Bibi wiggled but did not fall. “Now breathe. Smell the orange. Blow out the candle.” They sniffed the sweet smell and blew gently. The room felt calm and happy.

“Now my turn to lead,” Bibi said. “We can try a move from my grandpa. He learned it on a forest walk.” Bibi showed a bear crawl. “Hands down, knees down, crawl slow.” Their hands padded softly over the fuzzy carpet. They felt strong.

Nia shared a move from her auntie’s dance. “We sway side to side.” The scarf swished. Javi shared a clap rhythm from his cousins. “Clap, tap, clap.” Mila shared a gentle bow from her family. “We bow to say thank you.”

Bibi’s eyes shone. “Our moves are different. Our moves are special. Together, they make a fun exercise dance!”

They lined up to play a new game. The rule board said: “One leader, one minute. Everyone follows. Then we switch.” Bibi set a timer that beeped softly like a tiny bird.

Bibi led first. “Bear crawl to the pillow, then touch your toes.” Beep! Time to switch.

Nia led next. “Stretch like the moon, then wiggle your tail.” Beep! Switch.

Javi led. “Three hops, then freeze like statues.” Beep! Switch.

Mila led. “Balance, then breathe.” Beep! Switch.

They went round and round. They took turns. They cheered. “Go, Nia! Go, Javi! Go, Mila! Go, Bibi!” The room filled with happy sounds: thumps, swishes, jingles, and giggles.

Then the red ball rolled out again. Bibi wanted to grab it and start a fast chase. His paws tingled with excited energy. He reached out... and stopped. He looked at the rule board. “One leader, one minute. Everyone follows. Then we switch.”

Bibi took a breath. “I can wait. I can be fair.” He smiled at Mila, who was leading. “I will follow first.”

Mila nodded. “Thank you, Bibi.” She led a quiet move: “Sit tall. Stretch your arms. Shake out your paws.” The ball waited. Bibi waited too.

Beep! Switch. Now it was Bibi’s turn again. “Ball time!” he said. “We roll the ball and run to catch it. Ready?” They rolled. They ran. They laughed. Their hearts beat fast, like tiny drums. Their cheeks felt warm. Exercise felt good.

At the end, they made a cozy circle on the carpet. They sipped water from cups with bright colors. The water was cool and yummy. They shared orange slices. The room smelled sweet and sunny.

“What did we learn today?” Bibi asked.

Nia purred, “Stretching wakes up our bodies.”

Javi said, “Jumping makes us strong and happy.”

Mila said, “Breathing helps us wait and be fair.”

Bibi nodded. “And sharing turns lets everyone shine. Our different moves make our game better. Exercise is fun when we are kind, fair, and together.”

They bumped paws, hands, and tiny mouse fingers. “Bounce, stretch, and share!” they cheered.

The End.
