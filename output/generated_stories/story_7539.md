# Melody's Big Problem

The dusty attic was very quiet. Sunlight peeked through a small round window, making golden spots on the wooden floor. In the corner sat a pretty blue bird named Melody.

Melody loved to sing beautiful songs. Her voice was sweet like honey and clear like tinkling bells. But today, she felt very sad.

"Tweet, tweet, oh dear," Melody sighed. She fluffed her bright blue feathers and looked around the quiet attic.

Down below, Melody could hear the Johnson family getting ready for their big party. They were celebrating Grandma Johnson's birthday! But there was one big problem.

Mrs. Johnson climbed up the creaky attic stairs. She carried a dusty box of decorations. When she saw Melody, she stopped.

"Oh my!" Mrs. Johnson said. "Little bird, you can't stay here today. We need this attic space for our party supplies."

Melody's tiny heart felt worried. "But this is my home," she chirped softly. "I live here with my nest and my favorite singing spot by the window."

Mrs. Johnson looked kind but firm. "I'm sorry, but you'll have to find somewhere else to go. The party is very important."

Melody thought hard. She didn't want to leave her cozy home, but she also didn't want to ruin the special birthday party. Then she had a wonderful idea!

"Wait!" Melody sang in her sweetest voice. "What if we could work together? I have something special to offer!"

Mrs. Johnson tilted her head curiously. "What do you mean, little bird?"

Melody's eyes sparkled with excitement. "I can sing the most beautiful birthday songs! What if I stay in my corner quietly while you set up, and then I'll sing special music for Grandma Johnson's party?"

Mrs. Johnson smiled. "That's an interesting idea. But how do I know your singing won't be too loud or disturb our guests?"

"Let me show you!" Melody chirped. She sang a gentle, soft melody that sounded like a music box. The notes floated through the attic like magic.

"Oh my," Mrs. Johnson whispered. "That's absolutely beautiful! But what about when we need to move boxes near your nest?"

Melody thought again. "I can fly to the other side of the attic when you need my corner. And I promise to sing only when you say it's okay. We can take turns using the space!"

Mrs. Johnson clapped her hands together. "What a smart solution! You can help make Grandma's party extra special. She loves bird songs!"

"Really?" Melody's feathers practically glowed with happiness.

"Yes! And in return, you can keep your home here. We'll work together as a team."

That afternoon, the attic buzzed with activity. Melody flew back and forth, staying out of the way while Mrs. Johnson arranged decorations. When it was time for the party, Melody perched by her window and sang the sweetest birthday songs anyone had ever heard.

Grandma Johnson looked up at the ceiling with tears of joy in her eyes. "What beautiful music! It makes my birthday perfect!"

After the party, Mrs. Johnson climbed back up to the attic. "Thank you, Melody. Your singing made our celebration magical. You found such a wonderful way for us to share this space."

Melody snuggled into her cozy nest, feeling proud and happy. "When we talk together and listen to each other, we can solve any problem!" she sang softly.

From that day on, Melody and the Johnson family worked together as friends, sharing the attic and making beautiful music for every special occasion.

The End.
