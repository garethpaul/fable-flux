# Bunny Paints Together

In a cozy bedroom with soft pillows and a warm quilt, a determined rabbit named Ruby wiggled her nose. The room smelled like clean cotton and lemon soap. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. Crayons, paper, and a small set of paints sat on a low table.

“I will make a big, bright picture all by myself,” Ruby said. Her long ears stood tall. “I can do it.”

Ruby dipped a brush into blue paint. Swoosh! The brush felt smooth and wet. She made a big blue sky. She smiled. “So blue,” she whispered.

Then Ruby reached for the yellow. Drip! A little blob fell onto the quilt. “Oh no,” she said, ears drooping. She dabbed it with a cloth. The quilt felt damp and cool. “I can fix it.”

Ruby kept going. She wanted to paint a rainbow and a happy sun and a dance of flowers. She tried to hold three crayons at once. Clatter! The crayons rolled onto the floor. Tap-tap-tap. Ruby sighed. “This is hard.”

From the doorway, her friend Pip, a tiny mouse with bright eyes, peeked in. “Hi, Ruby!” Pip squeaked. “It smells like paint. What are you making?”

“A big, bright picture,” Ruby said. She picked up the crayons. “I want it to be perfect. I will do it all by myself.” Her voice was firm.

Pip stepped closer and looked at the mess—brushes, water cup, paper, and tiny drops of yellow on the quilt. Pip’s whiskers twitched. “Your sky is pretty,” he said. “Do you want help?”

Ruby felt a warm flutter in her chest. But she shook her head. “No, thank you. I am determined. I can do it.”

Pip nodded and sat on the soft rug. “Okay. I will watch,” he said kindly.

Ruby tried again. She dipped the brush in red, then orange, then green. The colors were bright and happy. But the brush slipped. Swish—splot! The orange line went squiggly. Ruby’s nose scrunched. “Not the way I want,” she murmured.

Her friend Tilly, a gentle turtle with a shiny shell, peeked around the door. “Hello,” Tilly said slowly. “I heard giggles. Is it art time?”

Ruby bit her lip. “Yes. It is art time. I want to make a rainbow, a sun, and flowers. But my lines are wiggly. My crayons roll. The quilt has spots. I am trying very hard.”

Tilly smiled. “Trying is brave,” she said. Pip nodded. “We can help if you like,” he added softly.

Ruby looked at the paint again. She listened to the tiny drip from the brush. Drip… drip… The room was quiet and warm. She felt a little tired and a little stuck. Her ears drooped, then lifted. “Maybe we can try together,” she said. “We can make one big picture.”

Pip’s eyes shone. “Yes!” Tilly clapped her slow, gentle clap. “Together,” she echoed.

“Let’s plan,” Ruby said, feeling a new spark. “I can paint the blue sky and the sun. Pip, can you draw the rainbow lines? Tilly, can you make the flowers?”

Pip puffed with pride. “I love lines,” he squeaked. “I can make them smooth.” Tilly nodded. “Flowers are my favorite,” she said. “I can press the brush like a petal.”

Ruby moved the paint water away from the quilt. “Good idea,” she said. “We need to keep things tidy.” She laid a towel under the table. Tilly held the paper steady with her gentle turtle feet. Pip passed the crayons, one by one, so they did not roll. “Red,” Pip said. “Orange,” Pip said. “Yellow,” Pip said, giggling each time.

Ruby painted the sun in little circles. Swish, swish, swish. The sun looked warm and round. “Hello, sun,” she said softly. Pip drew the rainbow in slow, careful arches. The colors hugged the sky. Tilly made flowers with soft dabs. Tap, tap, tap. The petals looked like tiny bells.

“Listen,” Ruby said. They paused. The room sounded like happy whispers: swish, tap, slide. It smelled like sweet crayons and fresh paper. Ruby’s heart felt light. “This feels good,” she said.

They shared ideas as they worked.

“Can the rainbow touch the sun?” Pip asked.

“Just a little,” Ruby said. “A sun hug!”

“May I add leaves?” Tilly asked.

“Yes! Leaves will make the flowers dance,” Ruby said.

When the picture was done, they all leaned back. The paper was full: blue sky, bright sun, a smooth rainbow, and a field of flowers. Ruby’s ears perked high. “It is even better than I thought,” she said.

Pip beamed. “We did it together,” he squeaked.

Tilly’s eyes were warm. “Cooperation makes big art,” she said.

Ruby felt proud, not just for the picture, but for the way they made it. She looked at her friends. “Thank you for your help,” she said. “I am still determined. I worked hard. But working together made it easier and more fun.”

Pip nodded. “Together means we share jobs,” he said. “And we share smiles.”

Tilly added, “Together means we listen and care.”

Ruby hung the picture above the bed. The colors glowed in the cozy bedroom. The quilt felt dry and safe. The room smelled sweet and calm.

“What did we learn today?” Ruby asked.

Pip and Tilly smiled. Ruby said, “When we cooperate, we can solve problems. We make better art. We feel happy. We help each other.”

Pip squeaked, “Teamwork!”

Tilly said, “Friendship.”

Ruby giggled. “And fun.”

They all hugged. The warm hug felt like sunshine.

The End.
