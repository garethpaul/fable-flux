# Oliver the Owl Solves the Snack Mystery

Oliver the Owl sat in his cozy home office, surrounded by colorful books and papers. His big yellow eyes sparkled as he worked at his wooden desk. The morning sun streamed through the window, making everything warm and bright.

"Hoot, hoot!" Oliver said happily as he organized his papers. But then he heard something strange from the kitchen below.

"Waaaah! I'm so hungry!" cried a little voice.

Oliver's feathery ears perked up. He heard tiny footsteps running around downstairs. "That sounds like little Benny Bunny," Oliver thought. "I wonder what's wrong?"

Being a wise owl, Oliver knew he should think carefully before acting. He put down his pen and listened closely. He heard cabinet doors opening and closing - BANG, BANG, BANG!

"There's no good food anywhere!" Benny's voice echoed up the stairs.

Oliver fluffed his soft brown feathers and thought logically. "Let me think about this step by step," he said to himself. "Benny is hungry. He's looking for food. But he says there's no good food. Maybe I can help him solve this problem!"

Even though Oliver felt a little nervous about leaving his comfortable office, he knew he needed to be brave and help his friend. He spread his wings wide and glided down to the kitchen.

There was Benny Bunny, sitting on the floor with his ears drooping sadly. The kitchen was a mess! Empty cookie boxes were scattered around, and candy wrappers lay everywhere.

"Hello, Benny," Oliver said gently. "I heard you calling. What seems to be the problem?"

Benny looked up with big, watery eyes. "Oh, Oliver! I ate all the cookies and candy for breakfast, but now I'm still hungry and my tummy feels yucky. There's no more sweet food left!"

Oliver nodded thoughtfully. "Hmm, let me think about this logically. You ate sweet foods, but you're still hungry and don't feel well. Let's look at this problem together."

Oliver opened the refrigerator with his wing. "Look, Benny! I see crispy carrots, crunchy apples, creamy yogurt, and fresh berries. These foods will give your body the energy it needs AND make your tummy feel better."

"But those aren't as yummy as cookies," Benny said, wrinkling his nose.

"Let's try an experiment," Oliver suggested wisely. "First, smell this apple." He held up a red, shiny apple.

Benny sniffed. "Mmm, it smells fresh and sweet!"

"Now take a small bite," Oliver encouraged.

CRUNCH! Benny bit into the apple. His eyes grew wide. "Wow! It's crunchy and juicy and naturally sweet!"

"You see," Oliver explained, "when we think logically about food, we learn that fruits and vegetables give us energy to play and help our bodies grow strong. Sweet treats are okay sometimes, but healthy foods make us feel our best."

Benny hopped excitedly. "Can you help me make a healthy snack?"

"Of course!" Oliver said proudly. Together, they washed colorful berries, sliced crunchy apples, and arranged them on a plate with some creamy yogurt.

As Benny munched happily, Oliver smiled. "Remember, Benny, when we have a problem, we can think through it step by step. We can be brave enough to try new solutions, and we can always ask friends for help."

"Thank you, Oliver!" Benny said with his mouth full of berries. "Logical thinking really works! And these healthy foods taste amazing!"

Oliver felt proud that he had been brave enough to help and used logical thinking to solve the problem. From that day on, Benny always thought carefully about his food choices and felt much happier and healthier.

The End.
