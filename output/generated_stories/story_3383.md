# Joey Jumps for the World

In a playful living room full of soft pillows, colorful blocks, and a round, bouncy rug, a joyful kangaroo joey named Kiki hopped from cushion to cushion. The sun poured in, warm and golden, and the room smelled like fresh oranges from snack time. Kiki’s tail swished happily.

“Boing! Boing! Good morning, world!” Kiki sang.

Mama Roo smiled from the doorway, holding a small basket. “Good morning, Kiki. It’s tidy time.”

Kiki paused mid-hop. “Tidy time now? But I want to build a big rocket tower!”

Mama Roo set the basket down. It had little pictures on the side—tiny flags, a smiling globe, and stars. “We can build a rocket after we tidy. Our home is part of our world,” Mama Roo said gently. “When we care for our home, we care for the world too.”

Kiki tilted her head. “Our home is the world?”

Mama Roo nodded. “Yes. Many people, in many places, all care for their homes. We share the same Earth. We can help by making less mess, saving water, and sharing.”

Kiki’s ears perked up. “Like sharing toys?”

“Exactly,” Mama Roo said. “And picking them up so no one trips.”

Kiki looked around. There were puzzle pieces under the couch, crayons rolling on the floor, and a soft blanket crumpled like a little mountain. The room felt busy and noisy with all the things out. Kiki rubbed her nose. “It’s a big job.”

Mama Roo knelt beside her. “We can make a plan. A little routine. We do a little each day.”

Kiki bounced once. “A routine?”

Mama Roo tapped the basket. “Yes. We can sing the Tidy Song, sort by colors, and take turns. That’s called a compromise—I help you, and you help me.”

Kiki smiled. “Okay! Compromise!”

They clapped softly. Mama Roo hummed a happy tune. “Pick up red, pick up blue, caring for the world we do.”

Kiki giggled and sang too. “Pick up green, pick up gold, we are helpers, big and bold!”

Kiki scooped up red blocks—click, clack—and dropped them into the basket. “Red goes here.” She picked up blue blocks—clink, clank—and put them in a neat pile. The sound was like tiny rain on a roof. The living room smelled like clean soap from the freshly washed floor, and the rug felt springy under Kiki’s feet.

Kiki found a crayon under the table. “Oh! A traveler!” she said. She rolled it back into the crayon box. “Safe trip, little crayon.”

Mama Roo laughed. “Good helper. Now the blanket?”

Kiki tugged the blanket. It was soft like a cloud. “I can fold!” She folded one side, and Mama folded the other. “We did it together,” Kiki said proudly.

“Teamwork,” Mama Roo said. “That’s what families and neighbors do. All over the world, people work together.”

Kiki’s eyes grew wide. “All over the world?”

“Yes,” Mama Roo said. “In sunny places and snowy places. In busy cities and quiet towns. Helping at home helps everyone. When we keep our space clean, we use things wisely. We can give extra toys to others too.”

Kiki thought for a moment. She held two yellow ducks. “I have two. Maybe one can swim to a new home.”

Mama Roo smiled softly. “That is very kind. Sharing is a big part of being a global citizen.”

Kiki felt warm inside, like sunshine in her belly. “I want to be a global sit… si—”

“Citizen,” Mama Roo said. “A person who cares about people and places, near and far.”

Kiki puffed her cheeks and then grinned. “I am Kiki the caring joey!”

They finished the song while putting away the last puzzle pieces. The room looked bright and calm. Kiki could hear the gentle tick-tock of the clock again. It felt peaceful.

“Now rocket tower?” Kiki asked.

Mama Roo nodded. “Yes. But let’s add one more part to our routine. After we play, we tidy again. We can set a timer and clean for two songs.”

Kiki thought and then bounced. “Compromise! We play, then we tidy two songs!”

They built a tall rocket tower, stacking blocks carefully. “Three, two, one… whoosh!” Kiki whispered. The tower didn’t fall, because the floor was neat and flat. Kiki clapped. “Tidy floor makes good rockets.”

Later, the timer chimed—ding-ding! Kiki took a deep breath. “Tidy time,” she said, and she began to put blocks back by color. Mama Roo joined, and they sang the Tidy Song again.

When they were done, Mama Roo poured cool water into two cups. The water splashed with a soft plip-plop. “Cheers to caring,” she said.

Kiki sipped. The water was fresh and cool. “I helped our home, and our world,” Kiki said.

“You did,” Mama Roo said, hugging Kiki. “By keeping a routine, doing your chores, and making kind choices, you help people everywhere.”

Kiki hugged back, nose to nose. “I’m small, but I can help big.”

Mama Roo nodded. “That’s the spirit of a global citizen.”

Kiki looked around the playful living room. It felt cozy and bright. “Tomorrow we can help again,” she said. “Play, tidy, share.”

Mama Roo smiled. “Play, tidy, share.”

Kiki bounced once more. “Boing for the world!”

The End.
