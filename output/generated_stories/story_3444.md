# Little Ebo on the Rooftop Garden

Ebo was a gentle elephant calf with soft gray ears and big careful feet. He lived in a bright building with a rooftop garden. On sunny mornings, Ebo liked to climb the safe, wide stairs and step onto the roof. The rooftop smelled like mint leaves and warm soil. The breeze felt cool on Ebo’s cheeks. Potted flowers made a rainbow path: red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple.

“Good morning, Rooftop,” Ebo said, wiggling his trunk.

His friends came to the garden too. Nia the sparrow swooped and chirped, “Tweet! Let’s fly high!” Jax the kitten purred and stretched. Lani the ladybug glowed like a tiny red button. Mei the mouse wore a tiny sunhat and waved.

Ebo had a big idea. “I have a goal,” he said. “I want to touch the big bell. I want to ring it one time today.”

The bell hung from a short beam near a patch of lavender. It wasn’t very high, but Ebo felt a little wobbly inside. His tummy made a fluttery feeling. He had tried yesterday and felt tired. He had tried last week and felt shaky.

Nia chirped, “You can do it! Fly, flap, ring!”

Jax said, “Pounce, pounce, ring!”

Mei squeaked, “Tiptoe, tiptoe, ring!”

Ebo smiled. “I will try. But first, I will take care of me,” he said.

He remembered what Mama Elephant taught him. “When you have a goal, care for your body and feelings. Small steps are strong steps.”

Ebo took a deep breath. He smelled the lavender. It smelled sweet and calm. “Breath in slow,” he told himself. “Breathe out slow.” The breeze rubbed his ears like soft hands. His shoulders felt less tight.

“Water break!” Ebo said. He sipped cool water from a big cup. The water felt like a fresh river in his mouth.

Nia tilted her head. “Why stop? You can go now!”

Ebo shook his head gently. “I need to listen to my body. When I rest, I grow strong.”

Mei nodded. “That is self-care!”

Ebo did a gentle warm-up. He lifted one foot and set it down. He lifted the other foot and set it down. “Step, step,” he sang softly. He wiggled his toes. He stretched his trunk to the sky. The sun kissed his trunk warm and golden.

Jax purred. “You look ready.”

Ebo looked at the bell. It was smooth and shiny. He heard bees hum in the mint. He heard a car far away. He felt brave and a little scared. “It’s okay to feel both,” he whispered. “Feelings are helpers.”

He walked to the path. A pot of basil brushed his leg and smelled like pizza. Lani the ladybug floated to his ear. “You can take tiny steps,” she said in a tiny voice. “Tiny steps make big journeys.”

Ebo set a small goal. “I will take five steps. Then I will check in,” he said.

He counted softly. “One… two… three… four… five.” He stopped. His heart beat thump-thump, not too fast. He put a cool leaf on his cheek. It felt damp and kind.

“How do you feel?” asked Mei.

“I feel okay,” Ebo said. “I will take five more.”

He took five more steps. He was near the lavender now. The purple flowers waved. A butterfly landed on his trunk. “Hello,” Ebo giggled. The giggle made the tightness in his tummy loosen.

Then he heard a shout from the stairs. “Wait for me!” It was Rudi the raccoon. He wore a bright scarf. “Sorry I’m late. I brought a snack to share!” He held up a small basket of apple slices and carrot sticks.

Ebo’s eyes lit up. “Thank you, Rudi.” He took a break and crunched a slice. Crunch, crunch. The apple was sweet and juicy.

Nia hopped. “Another break?” she chirped, confused.

Ebo nodded. “Yes. My goal is important. So is my body. I can do both.”

They all munched. The friends were all different. Some had wings, some had paws, some had tiny feet, and Ebo had big, soft feet. They liked different snacks, and different games, and different ways to rest. That was okay. Their differences made the rooftop happy.

After the snack, Ebo stood tall. “I am ready,” he said. “I will go slow and steady.”

He took five more steps. The bell was close now. He could see his round eyes in the shiny side. His tummy fluttered again. He put his trunk on his heart. “Hello, heart. I hear you. We can do this,” he whispered.

He made one last plan. “I will touch the rope gently. If I need to stop, I will stop.”

Ebo reached out. He felt the rope. It was rough and warm from the sun. He closed his eyes and breathed in lavender. He breathed out a soft whoosh.

“Ring,” he said kindly. He gave the rope a gentle pull.

Ding!

The bell sang a bright note. It sounded like a smile. The friends cheered. Nia chirped high, Jax purred loud, Mei clapped, Lani zipped in little loops, and Rudi waved his scarf.

Ebo laughed. “I did it! I took care of me. I listened. I made a plan. I used small steps.”

Nia fluttered to his shoulder. “I learned something,” she said. “Rest helps goals.”

Jax nodded. “Feelings can be friends.”

Mei added, “Different ways can still be good.”

Ebo looked at his friends. “We are all different, and we are all strong,” he said. “Self-care helped me reach my goal.”

They sat in the shade and shared the last apple slices. The rooftop breeze was cool. The lavender smelled sweet. The bell was quiet now, but Ebo’s heart rang with joy.

The End.
