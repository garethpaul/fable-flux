# Ollie Otter’s Rooftop Routine

Every morning, a playful otter named Ollie climbed the stairs to the flat, sunny rooftop. The warm tiles felt smooth under his paws. The sky smelled like fresh air and pancakes from a neighbor’s kitchen. “Good morning, rooftop!” Ollie chirped, wiggling his whiskers.

Ollie loved to play. He loved to splash in his small rooftop water tub, roll a little ball, and watch clouds drift by. But some mornings felt bumpy. His toys got tangled. His snack got lost. His time felt too short.

One sunny day, Ollie took a big breath. “I want my mornings to feel calm,” he said. “How can I make things easier?”

A gentle dove landed on the edge of the roof. “Coo, coo. Hello, Ollie,” she said. “I’m Della. I like calm mornings, too.”

Ollie smiled. “Hi, Della! My mornings get messy. I want to play and have time for snack and cloud-watching. Maybe I need a routine.”

Della tilted her head. “Routines help me. I do little steps in the same order. My feathers stay smooth, and my tummy is happy.”

“Let’s make a plan!” Ollie clapped his paws. He pulled out a small chalkboard and a blue piece of chalk. The chalk squeaked softly.

“Step one?” Della asked.

Ollie tapped his chin. “Step one: Rooftop check.” He drew a tiny sun. “I look for loose things. I make sure it’s safe.”

They walked around together. The breeze tickled Ollie’s fur. A bucket sat near the edge. “This should be far from the edge,” Ollie said. He moved it by the door. “Safe.”

“Good start,” Della cooed.

“Step two: Set up,” Ollie said, writing more. “I put out my water tub, towel, and ball.” He lined them up. Plip—water filled the tub. Thump—the towel went next to it. Roll—the ball waited by the towel.

“Step three: Snack ready,” Della added. “I like a full belly.”

Ollie giggled. “Me too!” He placed a small bowl of apple slices and a bottle of water under a shady umbrella. “Snack spot!”

“Step four: Play time,” Della said. “But with gentle, safe moves.”

Ollie nodded. “And step five: Clean up and goodbye song.” He drew a little music note.

They read the list together:
“Check.”
“Set up.”
“Snack ready.”
“Play time.”
“Clean up and goodbye song.”

“It sounds smooth,” Della said.

Ollie looked proud. “I feel ready.”

Just then, a curious raccoon named Rafi climbed from the next roof. His paws were dusty, and his eyes were bright. “Hi! Can I play?” he asked, bouncing with excitement.

Ollie felt a flutter in his tummy. He wanted to say yes, but he also wanted his calm morning. He took a gentle breath. “Hi, Rafi. I would like to play. I have a new routine. Can I tell you the steps?”

Rafi blinked. “Okay.”

Ollie pointed to the chalkboard. “We do ‘Check,’ then ‘Set up,’ then ‘Snack ready,’ then ‘Play time,’ then ‘Clean up and goodbye song.’”

Rafi shuffled his paws. “I want to splash now.”

Della fluffed her wings. “We can all feel excited. Let’s use peaceful words.”

Ollie said softly, “Rafi, I hear you want to splash now. I want to follow the steps. When we do the steps, we all feel safe. Can you help me check the roof? Then we will splash together.”

Rafi’s ears perked up. “I can help!” He picked up a tiny loose ribbon and placed it by the door. “Safe,” he said with a smile.

“Thank you,” Ollie said. “That helps.”

They finished “Check.” They did “Set up.” Rafi folded the towel, pat pat pat. Della counted the apple slices, “One, two, three, four,” her voice soft like a lullaby. Ollie poured water, plip-plip-plip.

“Snack ready!” Ollie cheered.

“Play time!” Rafi wiggled.

They took turns at the water tub. Splash! Ollie dipped his paws. Splash! Rafi swirled the water gently. Della hopped close and dipped her beak, sip sip. The sun warmed their backs. The breeze smelled like soap and flowers. Laughter bubbled up like tiny water drops.

When the ball rolled near the edge, Ollie held up a paw. “Pause.” He walked slowly, picked up the ball, and brought it back. “Our routine helps us stay safe,” he said.

Rafi nodded. “I like the steps. I know what comes next.”

After play, they sat in the shade. Crunch, crunch went the apples. Water felt cool on their tongues. Their bodies felt calm.

“Clean up?” Della asked.

“Clean up,” Ollie agreed. They emptied the tub near a thirsty rooftop plant. Swish. They put the towel in a basket. Fold. They placed the ball in a box. Click.

Ollie smiled at his friends. “Goodbye song?”

They sang softly together:
“Check and set,
Snack and play,
Clean up, friends,
A happy day.”

Their voices floated warm and gentle in the air.

Rafi waved. “Thank you for telling me the steps. It helped me wait.”

Ollie said, “Thank you for listening. Using peaceful words made it easy.”

Della cooed, “Routines are like soft paths. They guide our feet.”

Ollie looked at the chalkboard. He felt cozy and proud. His heart felt like a warm blanket. “Our routine made the morning calm and fun,” he said. “Tomorrow, we can do it again.”

He took one last deep breath. The rooftop smelled like sunshine. The tiles felt friendly under his paws. Ollie whispered, “I am ready for each step.”

The End.
