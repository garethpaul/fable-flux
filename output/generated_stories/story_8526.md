# Momo's Rooftop Adventure

High up on a tall building, a brave little monkey named Momo swung from pipe to pipe. His soft brown fur sparkled in the morning sunshine, and his bright eyes looked around curiously.

"What a beautiful day!" Momo said, stretching his arms wide. The rooftop was his favorite place to play. He could see the whole city from up here, with cars looking like tiny toys far below.

Suddenly, Momo heard a soft crying sound. "Peep, peep, peep!"

He looked around and saw a baby bird sitting in a broken nest near the edge of the rooftop. The little bird's feathers were fluffy and yellow, and it looked very scared.

"Oh no!" Momo said, swinging over quickly. "What happened, little friend?"

"My nest fell down in the wind last night," chirped the baby bird sadly. "Now I have nowhere safe to sleep, and I can't fly yet!"

Momo scratched his head, thinking hard. The broken nest was just a pile of twigs and leaves scattered on the ground. "Don't worry," he said kindly. "I'll help you!"

First, Momo tried to stack the old twigs back together. But they kept falling down. "Hmm, this isn't working," he said.

Then he had another idea. Maybe he could find something else to make a nest! Momo looked around the rooftop. He saw some old newspapers fluttering in the breeze, but they were too slippery. He found some plastic bags, but they were too crinkly and loud.

"I need to think differently," Momo said to himself. He sat down and really looked at everything around him.

That's when he spotted something perfect! Near the rooftop door, there was a small cardboard box that someone had left behind. Next to it were some soft rags that the building cleaners used.

"I have an idea!" Momo exclaimed excitedly.

He carefully carried the box to a safe corner of the rooftop, away from the wind. Then he lined it with the soft rags, making it cozy and warm. But something was still missing.

Momo looked at the scattered nest materials again. This time, instead of trying to rebuild the old nest, he picked up the softest pieces - some moss, a few small feathers, and the tiniest twigs. He arranged them gently on top of the rags.

"There!" Momo said proudly. "A brand new nest!"

The baby bird's eyes grew wide with joy. "It's perfect!" she peeped happily. She hopped into her new home and snuggled down into the soft materials. "It's even cozier than my old nest!"

Momo felt warm and happy inside. "Sometimes when something breaks, we can make something even better," he said wisely.

"Thank you, Momo!" chirped the baby bird. "You didn't give up, even when your first ideas didn't work!"

Momo smiled and swung from a nearby pipe. "That's what friends do! And you know what? Trying different ideas made this adventure even more fun!"

As the sun climbed higher in the sky, Momo and his new friend chatted happily. The baby bird was safe and cozy, and Momo had learned that the best solutions sometimes come from looking at problems in new ways.

From that day on, whenever Momo faced a tricky problem, he remembered to stay calm, try different ideas, and never give up. And the baby bird always reminded him that helping others made every day brighter.

The End.
