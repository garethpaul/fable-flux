# Penny Penguin's Porch Party Plan

Penny Penguin waddled onto her peaceful front porch, her orange feet making soft tap-tap sounds on the wooden boards. The morning sun felt warm and cozy on her black and white feathers. She stretched her flippers wide and smiled.

"What a perfect day for my birthday party!" Penny chirped happily. She had been planning this special day for weeks.

But as Penny looked around her porch, she noticed something. Her neighbor, Mrs. Rabbit, was hanging laundry on the line right next to the porch. The wet clothes flapped in the breeze - flap, flap, flap!

"Oh my," thought Penny. "The laundry will be right where my friends need to walk for the party."

Just then, Mrs. Rabbit hopped over with a basket full of more wet clothes.

"Good morning, Penny!" Mrs. Rabbit called out cheerfully. "Beautiful day, isn't it?"

"Good morning, Mrs. Rabbit," Penny replied. "Um, I was wondering... I'm having my birthday party on the porch today at two o'clock. Do you think we could talk about the laundry?"

Mrs. Rabbit's ears perked up. "Oh! I didn't know about your party. I really need to dry these clothes today though. It's the only sunny day this week."

Penny felt her tummy flutter with worry. She took a deep breath and thought carefully. "Maybe we can find a way that works for both of us?" she suggested.

"I'm listening," said Mrs. Rabbit, setting down her basket.

Penny waddled closer. "Well, what if we moved some of the laundry to the backyard line? Then you could still dry your clothes, and my friends could walk to the porch easily."

Mrs. Rabbit tapped her foot thoughtfully. "Hmm, but the backyard doesn't get as much sun. My clothes might not dry as fast."

"What if..." Penny's eyes lit up, "what if we hang the smaller things in back, and keep the big sheets here until one o'clock? Then we could move them before my party starts!"

"That's a creative idea!" Mrs. Rabbit's whiskers twitched with interest. "But what if it takes me a while to move everything?"

Penny clapped her flippers together. "I could help you move them! And maybe... would you like to stay for some birthday cake? I made extra!"

Mrs. Rabbit's eyes sparkled. "Really? You'd share your birthday cake with me?"

"Of course! Parties are more fun with more friends," Penny said warmly.

"Well then, I think we have a deal!" Mrs. Rabbit exclaimed. "Let's move the small clothes now, and I'll help you set up for your party too."

Together, they carefully moved the smaller clothes to the backyard line. The warm breeze made them dance and sway. At one o'clock, they worked as a team to move the big sheets.

"You know, Penny," said Mrs. Rabbit as they folded the last sheet, "I'm so glad you talked to me about this. I would have felt terrible if I had accidentally spoiled your party."

"And I'm glad I asked nicely instead of just worrying about it," Penny replied. "Sometimes talking about problems helps us find good solutions!"

When Penny's friends arrived at two o'clock, the porch was perfect for the party. Mrs. Rabbit joined them for cake and games, and everyone had a wonderful time.

As the sun set, Penny looked at her clean, party-ready porch and smiled. She had learned that when you prepare well, speak kindly, and stay open to new ideas, even tricky problems can have happy endings.

The End.
