# Buzzy Bear’s Busy Bedtime

In a cozy bedroom with soft blue walls and a quilt that smelled like sunshine, a friendly bear cub named Buzzy woke from a nap. The window was cracked open just a little, and a gentle breeze tickled Buzzy’s nose. He sniffed. The room smelled like clean wood, warm blankets, and a tiny bit like last night’s honey toast.

“Good evening, room!” Buzzy said, rubbing his eyes. He stretched his arms and wiggled his toes. Wiggle, wiggle. Stretch, stretch. He could hear his heart go thump-thump with excitement. It was almost bedtime, and bedtime in Buzzy’s cozy bedroom was a little daily adventure.

Mama Bear peeked in. “Hello, my busy cub,” she said with a smile. “It’s tidy-time.”

Buzzy’s ears perked up. “Tidy-time is movement time!” he cheered. He hopped off the bed and felt the soft rug under his paws. Squish-squish. The rug felt like a cloud.

On the floor, blocks were scattered like colorful confetti. Buzzy picked up a red block. “Plop!” it went into the basket. He picked up a blue block. “Plink!” Then a green block. “Ping!”

Mama Bear clapped gently. “You’re a helper. Helping feels good.”

Buzzy puffed up a little with pride. “I can do it! I am strong and kind.” He liked how those words felt in his mouth, round and warm.

“Let’s make it a dance,” Mama Bear said. “We can move our bodies while we do our chores.”

Buzzy nodded and did a little shuffle. “Shuffle, shuffle, plop!” He scooped three blocks at once. “Scoop, scoop, scoop!” He swayed his hips and spun, careful not to bump the lamp. The lampshade was smooth and cool to his paw when he steady-steady touched it.

From the shelf, Buzzy’s stuffed friends watched: a long-necked giraffe, a panda with one ear flopped down, a rainbow llama, and a soft turtle with a shiny shell. Buzzy waved. “We’re all different, and we all belong,” he told them. “Everyone helps in their own way!”

He tucked them in their row. “Good job waiting your turn,” he whispered. “Now it’s your cozy time.”

Mama Bear smiled. “That was kind, Buzzy. You noticed each friend.”

Buzzy bounced to the laundry basket. His fuzzy pajamas were half-in, half-out. “Peekaboo!” he giggled, pulling them all the way in. He felt the fabric—smooth cotton, soft fleece, and a silly sock that smelled a little like playtime. He wrinkled his nose and laughed. “Pee-yew! Sock, you need a wash.”

He stomped his feet like a tiny marching band. “March, march, march,” he sang, stepping to the closet with his shoes. Tap-tap went the shoes on the floor as he lined them up. Big paw prints and little paw prints made a quiet pattern on the rug.

“But what about my crayons?” Buzzy asked, picking up a crayon that was red like a strawberry. He held it carefully. “I drew a rainbow this morning.”

“Let’s give them a safe home,” Mama Bear suggested. “Each crayon can rest in its box, just like you rest in your bed.”

Buzzy blinked. “A home for crayons! That’s open-minded. I didn’t think of that.” He practiced a new idea. He clicked each crayon into place. Click, click, click. The sound was neat and tidy.

When he looked around, the room felt bigger and brighter. The Sun Bear nightlight glowed a warm golden color. Buzzy listened. The room made a soft hush like a whispering forest. He took a deep breath. In…and out. It smelled like calm.

Then Buzzy saw a blanket on the floor. He paused. “It’s heavy,” he said, trying to lift it. His paws slipped a little. “I need a plan.”

Mama Bear knelt beside him. “Let’s do it together. We can roll it like a log.”

“Roll like a log!” Buzzy giggled. He pressed his paws, pushed with his legs, and rolled the blanket. Roll, roll. He felt strong. The blanket made a soft swish as it slid onto the bed.

“I did it!” Buzzy cheered, his eyes bright. His chest felt warm and proud, like a candle shining.

“You did it with a smart plan and big bear muscles,” Mama Bear said. “You were open to trying a new way.”

Buzzy nodded. “I like to try new ways. I like to move. I like to help.” He tapped his paw to his heart. Thump. “I like me.”

Mama Bear kissed his forehead. “Chores help our home feel safe and cozy. They also help you feel capable.”

Buzzy looked at his room. The blocks were in the basket. The books were stacked like little houses. The crayons were snug. The stuffed friends were smiling in a row. Everything felt friendly.

He climbed into bed and tucked his chin into the soft pillow. The pillow smelled like fresh air and a tiny hint of vanilla. Mama Bear pulled up the quilt. It rustled like leaves in the wind.

“Goodnight, helpers,” Buzzy whispered to his toys. “Goodnight, cozy room.”

“Goodnight, my brave, helpful cub,” Mama Bear said. “What did you learn today?”

Buzzy thought. “Chores are like a dance. I can move my body, try new ideas, and help our home. When I help, I feel proud.”

Mama Bear nodded. “Yes. Helping every day builds your strong heart.”

Buzzy smiled, eyes fluttering closed. “Tomorrow, I can help again.”

The room was still. The nightlight glowed. Buzzy’s breath was slow and soft.

In the cozy bedroom, the friendly bear cub dreamed happy dreams—of dancing with blocks, rolling blankets like logs, and cheering for every friend, no matter how different they were.

The End.
