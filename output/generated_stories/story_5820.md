# Ollie Otter and the Garden Shed List

Ollie was a playful otter with bright, curious eyes and a whiskery smile. He loved to splash, to scoot, and to spin in circles. One sunny morning, Ollie visited Grandma Dot’s garden. The air smelled like fresh soil and sweet tomatoes. Bees hummed softly, and the wind felt warm on his fur.

Grandma Dot pointed to the garden shed. “Ollie,” she said kindly, “I need your help. We have guests for lunch at noon. The shed is messy. If we work together and follow directions, we can finish on time.”

Ollie wriggled with excitement. “I can help! I can help now!”

Grandma Dot smiled and showed him a big paper list with simple pictures. There was a drawing next to each step.

“Here are the steps,” she said. “Step one: Put gloves in the blue bin. Step two: Roll the hose and hang it on the hook. Step three: Sweep the floor. Step four: Wipe the table. Step five: Close the paint lids.”

She tapped the clock. “We have one hour. If we follow the steps and watch the time, we’ll be ready. This is fair to our guests and respectful to our neighbors, because then no tools will be in their way.”

Ollie nodded. “I will follow the list,” he said. He felt proud.

They opened the shed. It smelled like wood and lemon soap. Sunlight made dusty sparkles in the air. There were gloves, pots, a hose, and tiny jars of paint. A broom leaned in the corner.

Ollie saw a box of shiny marbles. “Ooo!” he squeaked. “Shiny!” He reached out a paw.

Grandma Dot touched his shoulder gently. “Let’s check the list first,” she said. “Step one?”

Ollie looked at the paper. He could see the picture of gloves and a blue bin. “Gloves first,” he said. “Okay!”

He picked up soft, dusty gloves and sniffed. “They smell like dirt cookies,” he giggled. He placed them in the blue bin. Plop, plop, plop. It felt good to do step one.

“Great job,” said Grandma Dot. “Step two?”

Ollie saw the wiggly hose. It made a little hiss. He started to roll it, around and around. The hose felt cool and wet on his paws. “It’s like a big green noodle,” he said, giggling. He lifted it and hung it on the hook. “Two!” he cheered.

Grandma Dot nodded. “You are following directions. That keeps us on time.” She showed him the clock again. “See, we’re moving right along.”

Ollie puffed out his chest. He liked being on time.

He looked back at the marbles. “Please?” he asked softly.

“After the list,” Grandma Dot said. “We must be fair and do the work first. That shows respect to our plan and to our guests.”

Ollie took a breath. “Okay,” he said. “Step three.”

He grabbed the broom. Sweep, sweep, sweep. The straw broom made a scratchy sound on the floor. Dust tickled his nose. “Achoo!” he sneezed. He laughed and kept going. Soon the floor looked clean and smooth.

“Step four,” he sang.

He dipped a cloth in warm, soapy water. The water felt squishy and bubbly. He wiped the table. Swish, swish. The wood looked shiny. Ollie smiled at his reflection. “Hello, shiny me,” he said.

Grandma Dot peered at the clock. “We have time for one more step,” she said. “You are doing wonderfully.”

Ollie checked the list. “Step five: Close the paint lids.” He carefully pressed each lid until it clicked. Click. Click. Click. “All closed!”

Grandma Dot clapped softly. “You followed every direction. Thank you. That is fair and safe. If paint spills, it can hurt the plants. You protected the garden.”

Just then, a little mouse peeped from behind a pot. “Squeak?” said the mouse. It looked worried. Two marbles had rolled near its tiny nest.

Ollie crouched down. “Hello, little friend,” he said gently. “I didn’t mean to scare you.”

The mouse twitched its whiskers. “Squeak,” it said again.

Grandma Dot whispered, “We must be kind and tolerant, even to tiny neighbors. Let’s move slowly and put the marbles away.”

Ollie nodded. “That’s fair,” he said. “Everyone deserves a safe space.” He rolled the marbles into a box and closed the lid softly. “All better,” he told the mouse.

The mouse darted back to its nest. The shed felt calm and tidy. The air smelled like lemon and wood again.

The clock chimed. Ding, ding, ding. Noon!

Guests arrived with cheerful voices. “What a neat shed!” they said. “And we are right on time!”

Ollie’s heart felt warm. He had followed directions. He had watched the time. He had shown respect to Grandma Dot, the guests, and the tiny mouse.

Grandma Dot handed him a small badge she made from cardboard and string. It had a picture of a list on it. “Ollie,” she said, “you are our Direction Detective.”

Ollie grinned. “I like following directions,” he said. “They help me know what to do, and they help me be fair and on time.”

Grandma Dot nodded. “Directions show a just way to share work and care for others. When we follow the steps, everyone’s needs matter.”

Ollie looked at the list again. “Can I play with the marbles now?” he asked politely.

“Yes,” said Grandma Dot. “Now is the right time. Thank you for waiting.”

Ollie rolled a marble gently across the smooth table. It made a tiny click. He tucked it back in the box when he was done. “All finished,” he said, proud and calm.

He turned to the guests. “Would you like to see the clean floor?” he asked with a laugh. He slid across it on his belly—whoosh! Everyone giggled.

Ollie waved his badge. “I followed the list,” he cheered.

And he did, every time after.

The End.
