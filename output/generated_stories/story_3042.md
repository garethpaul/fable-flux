# Koala Kipp Makes It Right

Koala Kipp was a calm koala with soft gray fur and gentle eyes. One sunny morning, Kipp padded into the bathroom. The floor was cool and smooth under his fuzzy feet. The bathroom smelled like minty soap and warm towels. Drip, drip, drip went the faucet. Plink, plink went the drops in the shiny sink.

Kipp liked quiet. He liked soft sounds and soft voices. He hummed a tiny tune, “Mmm-mmm-mmm,” and smiled at his reflection in the round mirror. “Good morning, Kipp,” he said. “Today we will brush, wash, and help.”

He squeezed toothpaste on his small, soft brush. “Squeeze, not squish,” he whispered. But the paste made a big blop and fell—splat—on the clean counter. “Oh,” said Kipp. He felt a little twist in his tummy. His ears felt warm. He looked at the white splat. He wanted to pretend he did not see it.

“Maybe it will just go away,” he thought.

Just then, his friend Poppy the parrot hopped in. Poppy’s feathers were bright like crayons—red, blue, and green. “Squawk! Good morning!” said Poppy. She fluffed and shook. Tiny water drops from her bath cup sparkled in the light. “Brush-brush time!”

Kipp hid his paws behind his back. “Good morning, Poppy,” he said in a small voice.

Poppy tilted her head. “I see a splat,” she said. “Did the sink sneeze?”

Kipp’s tummy twist got twistier. He looked at the splat. He looked at Poppy. He took a slow, calm breath in. He let it out. “I made the splat,” Kipp said softly. “I squeezed too hard. I feel worried. I don’t want the bathroom to be messy.”

Poppy blinked. “Thank you for telling me,” she said. “What can we do?”

Kipp listened to the faucet. Drip, drip, drip. He felt his heart slow down. “When I make a mess,” he said, “I can make amends. That means I can fix it and be kind.” He nodded, feeling braver. “I will wipe the splat.”

He took a soft cloth. He turned off the dripping faucet, twist-twist. The room grew quiet. Kipp wet the cloth with a tiny splash. Swish-swish went the cloth as he wiped the white splat away. He washed the cloth, wring-wring, and hung it neatly on the little hook.

Poppy smiled. “The counter looks happy,” she chirped.

Kipp felt light inside. “I feel proud,” he said. “My mess, my fix.”

Poppy hopped closer. “We can also tell others,” she said. “When we share a space, we care for the space. That is big-town kindness.”

Kipp’s eyes grew warm and bright. “Civic responsibility,” he said carefully. The words felt big in his mouth, but good. “We help the place we all use.”

They brushed their teeth, brush-brush, swish-swish. Minty bubbles tickled their tongues. They giggled and made little foam beards in the mirror. “Hello, Captain Bubble!” said Poppy. “Hello, Sir Swish!” said Kipp.

When they were done, Kipp reached for the towel. The towel slipped—plop—onto the floor. Kipp froze. He looked at Poppy. He felt a small squeeze in his chest. “I dropped the towel,” he said. “I feel a bit clumsy.”

Poppy nodded. “We can make amends again.”

Kipp picked up the towel, shook it gently, and folded it slowly. Fold, pat, smooth. He hung it straight on the bar. “There. Better,” he said. He turned to Poppy. “Thank you for being kind when I make mistakes.”

Poppy fluffed. “Everyone makes oops,” she said. “Kind words help.”

Just then, they heard a little “meep.” It was Dot, the tiny gecko, peeking from behind the soap dish. “I slipped on water,” Dot said, pointing to a small splash by the tub. Her voice was wobbly.

Kipp crouched low. “I am sorry you slipped, Dot,” he said. He felt a warm tug in his heart. “I did not see that water. We can clean it now.”

Poppy flew to the shelf to pass a dry cloth. Kipp dabbed the splash, pat-pat-pat, until the floor was safe and dry. “Are you okay?” he asked.

Dot nodded and smiled. “Thank you,” she said. “I feel safe.”

Kipp put a small sign near the tub that read, “Wipe water. Walk slow.” He said, “We can remind everyone with gentle words.”

They all took a step back and looked around. The mirror shone. The sink was neat. The towels were straight. The floor was dry. The bathroom felt cozy and calm.

Poppy sang a soft tune. Dot did a tiny tail wiggle. Kipp took a deep breath. The room smelled like mint and warm cotton. He closed his eyes for a moment and listened to the quiet. “I feel peaceful,” he said. “When I make amends, my heart feels calm. When I talk about my feelings, my friends understand. When I care for our bathroom, we all feel good.”

Poppy nodded. “Making amends, using kind words, and caring for our place,” she said, “make a happy day.”

Kipp smiled at his friends. “Next time I make a mess, I will say, ‘I did it. I’m sorry. I will fix it.’”

Dot clapped her tiny hands. “That is a good plan.”

Kipp turned to the mirror and whispered, “Good job, Kipp.” He patted his chest. Pat, pat. “We learn every day.”

They turned off the light with a soft click, and the bathroom glowed with clean, quiet pride.

The lesson was clear: When we make a mistake, we can make amends. We can use calm words, share our feelings, and take care of the places we share. That is how we help ourselves, our friends, and our home.

The End.
