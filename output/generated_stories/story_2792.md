# The Song in the Workshop

Chirp was a small, bright singing bird with a warm, golden chest. Chirp loved to sing. Chirp’s favorite place to sing was the garage workshop. The workshop smelled like fresh wood and warm oil. Sunlight made dust sparkle in the air. Tools hung on the wall like shiny silver fish.

“Tweet-tee-tee!” Chirp sang from the top of a red toolbox. The sound bounced off the shelves and made the paint cans hum.

In the workshop, friends came to build and fix things. There was Tumble the turtle with a tiny tool belt, Niko the squirrel with fast hands, and Lala the ladybug with a bright, spotted bag. They all liked to make helpful things to share with neighbors all over the world. They called it the Big Helping Plan.

One morning, Tumble set a smooth board on the table. “Let’s make a birdhouse,” Tumble said slowly. “We can send it to a faraway park where birds need homes.”

Chirp fluttered with joy. “A house for birds like me? Tweet-tee-tee! I will sing while we work!”

Niko clapped his paws. “I can saw!” he said.

Lala waved a tiny list. “And I will check our plan. We will measure, cut, and paint.”

Chirp puffed up and sang again. “Tweet-TEE-tee!” The sound filled the room.

Niko lifted the saw. “Squeeeek, saw, saw,” it sang too. Lala read the list. “Step one: measure.”

Tumble held the ruler and began to count. “One, two, thr—”

“Tweet-TEE-tee!” Chirp sang louder, happy and excited. The ruler shook. Tumble blinked. “Oh my,” he said. “I cannot hear the numbers.”

Niko tried again. “Squeeeek, saw, saw.”

“Tweet-TEE-TEE!” Chirp sang even louder. “I love to sing!”

Lala flew close. “Chirp,” she said gently, “we love your song, but we also need to hear the tools and the plan.”

Chirp’s feathers drooped. “But I want to sing now,” Chirp said softly. “Singing is my turn.”

Tumble smiled kindly. “Maybe we can take turns,” he said. “First measuring, then singing. We can plan turns like steps.”

Chirp tilted a tiny head. “Plan turns?”

Lala nodded. “Yes! We can make a Turn Plan. Everyone gets a turn. Then we share our work with friends far away. That is generous. That is kind.”

Chirp thought about birds far away. Birds who needed homes. Chirp wanted to help. Chirp took a deep breath and listened to the quiet hum of the workshop. “Okay,” Chirp said. “Let’s make a Turn Plan.”

Lala drew a simple chart on a big paper. “Step one: Tumble measures. Step two: Niko saws. Step three: Lala paints. Step four: Chirp sings while the paint dries.”

Niko wiggled with joy. “That is smart planning!”

Tumble tapped the ruler. “Ready to measure. One, two, three, four.”

Chirp folded wings and whispered, “I can wait. I can listen.” Chirp heard the soft slide of the ruler, the gentle tap-tap of Tumble’s shell on the table, and the quiet, steady breaths of friends.

Niko began to saw. “Squeeeek, saw, saw.” Chirp swayed but did not sing yet. Lala stirred paint. “Swish-swish.” The smell of fresh paint tickled Chirp’s beak.

Finally, Lala smiled. “Step four: Chirp sings while the paint dries.”

Chirp’s eyes lit up. “It’s my turn!” Chirp took a deep breath and sang a warm, soft song: “Tweet-tee-tee, tweet-tee-tee.” The song fluttered like a gentle breeze. The paint dried. Everyone smiled.

They worked like this, taking turns. The birdhouse took shape. It had a round door and a tiny porch. Lala painted it sky blue with sunny yellow dots. Niko tied a string. Tumble polished the edges smooth.

When they were done, Chirp hopped close. “It is beautiful,” Chirp whispered. Chirp pressed a small sticker on the bottom. It had a little globe on it. “This is for our friends far away,” Chirp said. “We are all neighbors in the big world.”

Lala clapped. “We worked generously. We shared time and turns.”

Niko grinned. “And our plan helped us!”

Tumble nodded. “Taking turns made the work safe and sweet.”

Just then, another friend peeked in. It was Lina, a child from next door. She had a box of seeds. “Can I help?” Lina asked shyly. “I want to send seeds to a school garden in another country.”

Chirp fluttered over. “Yes! We can make a Seed Plan too. We can take turns counting, packing, and singing.”

Lina giggled. “I like that.”

They made another Turn Plan. Lina counted seeds. Niko folded little bags. Lala wrote kind notes. Tumble stacked the bags. Chirp sang softly when it was time.

Soon, the workshop was full of good things to share: a birdhouse, seed bags, and even a small toolbox for a kid far away who liked to build.

As the sun dipped low, the workshop glowed warm and golden. Chirp looked at friends and felt full of song and pride. “Today I learned something,” Chirp said. “When we take turns, everyone is heard. When we plan, things go well. When we share, the world feels close.”

Lina smiled. “Taking turns is caring.”

Tumble added, “Planning is helping.”

Niko said, “Generosity is giving.”

Lala finished, “And together, we make a big, kind world.”

Chirp sang a gentle goodnight. “Tweet-tee-tee.” It was just the right volume, because it was time to listen too.

The End.
