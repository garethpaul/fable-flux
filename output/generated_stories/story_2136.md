# Oliver Owl's Sharing Day

High up in the big oak tree in Mrs. Miller's sunny backyard lived Oliver, a wise old owl with soft brown feathers and bright golden eyes. Every morning, Oliver would stretch his wings wide and say, "What a beautiful day to help my friends!"

The warm sunshine filtered through the green leaves as Oliver looked down at the backyard below. He could smell the sweet flowers blooming and hear the cheerful chirping of birds. It was going to be a wonderful day!

"Hoot, hoot!" called Oliver as he flew down to his favorite branch. "Good morning, everyone!"

Down on the grass, three little rabbits were hopping around in circles. They looked very sad.

"What's wrong, little ones?" asked Oliver gently.

The smallest rabbit, Rosie, wiped her eyes. "We found this bouncy red ball, but we can't agree on who gets to play with it first. We've been arguing all morning!"

Oliver tilted his head thoughtfully. "Hmm, that does sound like a problem. May I see this special ball?"

The middle rabbit, Max, pointed to a shiny red ball sitting in the grass. "It's so bouncy and fun! But there's only one ball and three of us."

Oliver's eyes twinkled with wisdom. "I have an idea! Instead of taking turns one at a time, why don't you play together? Sharing can make games even more fun!"

"But how?" asked the biggest rabbit, Ruby.

Oliver flapped his wings excitedly. "Watch this!" He picked up the ball with his talons and gently tossed it to Rosie. "Rosie, roll the ball to Max!"

Rosie giggled and pushed the ball across the soft grass to Max. The ball made a satisfying *thump* sound as it rolled.

"Now Max, bounce it to Ruby!" hooted Oliver.

Max caught the ball and bounced it high in the air. *Boing, boing!* Ruby jumped up and caught it with her paws.

"My turn to roll it back to Rosie!" laughed Ruby.

Soon all three rabbits were running, jumping, and rolling the ball to each other. Their fluffy tails wiggled with joy as they moved around the sunny yard.

"This is so much better!" panted Rosie, her nose twitching happily. "Playing together is way more fun than playing alone!"

Oliver watched from his perch, his feathers ruffling in the gentle breeze. "You see, little friends, when we share, everyone gets to have fun. And when we play together, we can create new games and make wonderful memories!"

The rabbits played their sharing game until the sun was high in the sky. They took turns being the thrower, the catcher, and the runner. Each rabbit got to enjoy the ball in different ways.

"Thank you, Oliver!" called Max. "You taught us that sharing makes everything better!"

"Every day can be a sharing day," hooted Oliver wisely. "When we share our toys, our snacks, and our time, we make friends happy and feel happy too!"

As the afternoon sun warmed the backyard, Oliver watched the three rabbit friends continue their game. They had learned that sharing wasn't about giving up something special – it was about making something special even better by including others.

From that day on, the three rabbits made sharing part of their daily routine, and their friendship grew stronger with each passing day.

The End.
