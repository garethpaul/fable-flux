# Ellie Tidies the Guest Room

Ellie was a gentle elephant calf. She had soft, gray ears like warm blankets and tiny feet that made quiet thumps. One sunny afternoon, Ellie peeked into the guest room. It smelled like fresh soap and clean sheets. The curtains were light and swishy. A little breeze tickled her trunk.

Mama Elephant said, “We have visitors tonight. The guest room needs love. Can you help, Ellie?”

Ellie’s eyes grew bright. “Yes! I can help,” she said. Her voice was small but brave.

Ellie looked around. Toys were under the bed. Books were in a crooked pile. A blanket was crumpled like a sleepy cloud. A chair was tipped a little. The room looked busy and messy.

Ellie took a deep breath. “I will try,” she whispered. She remembered a big word: persistence. It means keep trying, even when something is hard.

First, Ellie tried to pick up the crumpled blanket with her little trunk. The blanket slipped and flopped. “Oops,” said Ellie. She tried again. The blanket slid down. “Oh no,” she said softly.

Mama Elephant smiled. “You can do it, Ellie. Use small scoops. One fold at a time.”

Ellie nodded. “Small scoops.” She folded one corner. She patted it smooth. She folded the other corner. Pat, pat. The blanket felt cool and soft under her trunk. Soon it made a neat square. Ellie smiled. “I did it!”

Next, Ellie looked at the books. “Let’s make a line,” she said. She tried to stack them. The stack wobbled. Thump! The books fell like gentle rain.

Ellie’s cheeks felt warm. She wanted to stop. But she remembered persistence. “I will try again,” she told herself. “I can ask for help, too.”

She called, “Mama, how do I make them stand?”

Mama Elephant came and showed her. “We can sort by size. Big books on the bottom, little books on top. That is using our thinking. That is being responsible.”

Ellie touched the covers. Some were smooth. Some were bumpy. She sorted them big, medium, little. She stacked them slow. The tower felt steady, like a calm tree. “We did it!” Ellie giggled.

Then Ellie heard a tiny squeak under the bed. “Squeak?”

A little toy mouse was stuck behind a box. Ellie knelt down. Dust tickled her nose. Achoo! “Don’t worry, little mouse,” Ellie said. “I will help you.”

Ellie tugged the box. It was heavy. Her feet slid. She did not give up. She pushed with her shoulder. Slide, slide. The box moved a little. “Almost,” she puffed. She tried a new idea. “I will pull the corner.”

She pulled the corner, inch by inch. The box scooted out. The toy mouse popped free. “Squeak!” Ellie laughed and gave the toy a gentle hug. “You are safe now.”

Just then, Ellie saw a note on the chair. It said, “Please keep the guest room tidy. It helps everyone. Thank you!” Ellie traced the letters with her trunk.

She asked, “Mama, what does the note mean?”

Mama said, “It means we care for our shared spaces. That is civic responsibility. When we help keep rooms tidy, everyone feels welcome. Visitors feel happy. We are part of our home team.”

Ellie stood tall. “I am on the home team!”

A pillow had fallen on the floor. Ellie picked it up and fluffed it. Poof! Soft feathers made a quiet whiff. She placed the pillow on the bed. She smoothed the sheet. Swish, swish. The room began to look calm and friendly.

Suddenly, there was a tiny argument. Two toy blocks were stuck together tight. Ellie pulled and pulled. They would not come apart. She frowned. “Blocks, please let go,” she said.

The blocks stayed stuck.

Ellie stopped and took a deep breath. “I will use peaceful solutions,” she whispered. “I will be gentle. I will ask for help.”

She warmed the blocks in her hands. Rub, rub. She twisted slowly left, then right. “Slow and kind,” she said. Click! The blocks came apart. “Thank you,” Ellie smiled. She put them in the toy basket.

At last, Ellie looked around. The floor was clear. The bed was neat. The chair was straight. The books were lined up like quiet soldiers. The guest room smelled like lemon and soft blankets. The sun made a gold patch on the carpet.

Mama Elephant clapped her big, kind hands. “Ellie, you did it! You kept trying. You used your brain and your heart. You helped our home.”

Ellie’s chest felt warm and proud. “I was persistent,” she said. “I asked for help. I used peaceful ways. I took care of our shared room.”

Mama hugged her. “Yes. That is persistence. That is civic responsibility. That is a peaceful solution.”

Ellie patted the bed one more time. “Welcome, visitors,” she whispered. “This room is ready for you.”

She turned off the light. Click. The room was quiet and cozy, waiting like a happy secret.

Ellie trotted away, her small feet making soft thumps. She felt strong, kind, and brave.

The lesson was clear in her heart: Keep trying, be gentle, and help your home team. When we do that, everyone feels welcome and safe.

The End.
