# Ollie Otter and the Quiet Pause

In a cozy house on the corner, there was a basement lab. It smelled like warm soap and lemon. Little lights blinked and beeped. Soft fans hummed. On a big table sat jars of buttons, feathers, and shiny screws.

A playful otter named Ollie lived there with Dr. Dot, a kind scientist. Ollie had sleek brown fur and bright eyes. His whiskers tickled when he smiled.

“Today we try my new word machine,” said Dr. Dot. “It helps us listen to words, one by one.”

Ollie clapped. “Words! I love splashy words. Swoosh! Pop! Giggle!”

Dr. Dot laughed. “We will be careful. We will be calm.”

Ollie nodded fast. “I can be calm,” he said, but his paws tapped-tapped-tapped. He wanted to press all the buttons.

Dr. Dot showed a green button. “This one starts the machine,” she said. “We press only when we are ready. We use mindful ears. We breathe.”

Ollie watched the button glow. It looked like a juicy green grape. He wanted to tap it right now. “Can I? Can I? Can I?” he asked.

“First, a breath,” said Dr. Dot. “Smell the flower.” She held a little paper flower. “Blow the wind.” She waved her hand like a breeze.

Ollie breathed in. The air smelled like lemon and warm dust. He breathed out. His shoulders felt soft.

“Now we listen,” said Dr. Dot. She pressed the green button. The machine whirred, whirr-whirr. A tiny speaker said, “Hello.”

Ollie’s ears perked up. “Hello!” he chirped back.

The speaker said, “Thank you.” Its voice was clear and kind. Ollie smiled. He liked kind words.

Then Ollie heard a new sound. Clink! A jar of buttons wobbled near the edge of the table.

“I’ll catch it!” Ollie shouted. He leaped without looking. His tail swished. His paw bumped a red knob. Beep! The lights blinked faster. The speaker went, “B-b-b-buzz!”

“Oh no,” said Ollie. “I made a jumble.”

The jar toppled. Buttons skittered everywhere. They made a sound like tiny raindrops on the floor. Dr. Dot turned off the machine and looked at Ollie.

Ollie felt his cheeks get warm. “I was too fast,” he whispered. His belly felt wiggly and tight.

Dr. Dot knelt down. “Are you safe?”

Ollie nodded.

“Is the machine safe?” she asked.

The lights blinked slow again. “It seems okay,” said Dr. Dot. “What do we do when we feel wiggly?”

Ollie looked at the paper flower. He remembered. “We pause.”

“Let’s do our mindful pause,” said Dr. Dot gently.

Together they sat on the soft rug. The fan hummed like a bee. The lemon smell floated by.

“Smell the flower,” said Dr. Dot.

Ollie breathed in. His nose filled with calm.

“Blow the wind,” she said.

Ollie breathed out. His belly softened.

“Now put a paw on your heart,” said Dr. Dot.

Ollie felt it beat, thump-thump. “Hello, heart,” he said softly. “I hear you.”

Dr. Dot smiled. “That is self-care. You are taking care of your inside feelings.”

Ollie looked at the spilled buttons. “I need to tell the truth,” he said. “I hit the red knob. I was not careful.”

“That is integrity,” said Dr. Dot. “Thank you for being honest.”

“I want to fix it,” said Ollie.

“That is a good plan,” said Dr. Dot. “First, we clean. Then we try again, with slow paws and listening ears.”

They picked up buttons. The buttons were smooth and cool. Some were bumpy like tiny shells. They clicked as they went back into the jar. Ollie hummed, hum-hum, to keep his pace steady.

When the floor was clear, Dr. Dot said, “Let’s use our word machine to practice kind words. We will go slow.”

Ollie sat tall. He placed his paws in his lap. “I am ready,” he said.

“Green button,” said Dr. Dot.

Ollie took a breath. He pressed the green button gently. The machine whirred. The speaker said, “Please.”

Ollie liked the sound. “Please,” he echoed. He felt the shape of the word in his mouth. It was soft and round.

The speaker said, “Thank you.”

“Thank you,” said Ollie, a little bow in his head.

The speaker said, “I’m sorry.”

Ollie looked at Dr. Dot. “I’m sorry I bumped the knob,” he said.

“I forgive you,” said Dr. Dot. Her voice was warm like a blanket.

The speaker said, “I listen.”

Ollie closed his eyes. He heard the fan, the hum, his heart. He felt the stool under his paws. “I listen,” he whispered. He smiled. “Listening makes my inside feel quiet.”

They took turns making new words. “Welcome.” “Kind.” “Share.” “Rest.” Ollie loved how each word felt. Some were tiny taps, like “kind.” Some were long songs, like “wel-come.”

At last, Dr. Dot turned off the machine. “Ollie, what did you learn?”

Ollie tapped his heart. “When I feel wiggly, I pause. I breathe. I listen. Then I choose a good action. That is mindful.”

“And honest,” said Dr. Dot. “And that is care for you and for our lab.”

Ollie grinned. “And words are special. They can be soft, kind, and true.”

They washed their paws with warm water. The soap smelled like lemons again. Ollie dried his paws on a fluffy towel.

“Can we have tea?” he asked.

“Yes,” said Dr. Dot. “We will sip slow.” They sat and sipped. The tea was warm and sweet. Ollie breathed in the steam and felt calm all the way to his tail.

“I like the quiet pause,” he said.

“The quiet pause likes you,” said Dr. Dot.

The End.
