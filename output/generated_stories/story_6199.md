# Remy's Porch Promise

Remy was a determined rabbit. He liked to finish what he started. One sunny morning, he hopped onto the peaceful front porch with a little broom. The porch was warm like toast. The wooden boards smelled like fresh pine. A soft breeze tickled his whiskers. Birds sang, “Chee-chee!” from the trees.

“I will sweep the porch all by myself,” said Remy. “I can do it. I want it to be clean for everyone.”

He held the broom tight. Swish, swish, swish. Dust made tiny clouds in the sunlight. Remy’s paws went pat-pat on the boards. He hummed a small song. He felt proud.

Then Remy heard a sniffle. It was a tiny, squeaky sniffle. He looked up. On the step sat a little field mouse named Miri. Her ears drooped. Her whiskers quivered.

“Hi, Miri,” said Remy. “Are you okay?”

Miri shook her head. “I lost my red ribbon,” she whispered. “It blew away. I was going to tie it for my friend’s birthday. Now I feel sad.”

Remy blinked. He looked at his broom. He looked at Miri’s wet eyes. He felt a tug in his heart.

“I am sweeping,” he said softly. “But I see you are sad. Your feelings matter to me.”

Miri sniffled. “I don’t want to bother you.”

“You are not a bother,” said Remy. He put down the broom and sat beside her. The porch was warm under their feet. He listened. He let Miri talk.

“The ribbon was shiny red,” Miri said. “It skipped across the porch. The wind sounded whoosh, and it flew.”

Remy nodded slowly. “I hear that you are sad about the ribbon. I would feel sad too.”

He took a deep breath. He was determined to finish sweeping. He also cared about Miri. He had a problem to solve.

Remy said, “I want to help you and also clean the porch. Maybe we can do both. We can be a team.”

Miri’s ears lifted a little. “Do you mean it?”

“Yes,” said Remy. “Friends help friends. Helping is a way to be kind and responsible.”

They looked together. Remy used his ears to listen for the tiny rustle of ribbon. Miri looked under the flower pots. The porch smelled like mint from a small plant. Bees buzzed nearby. A cloud drifted like a slow sheep in the blue sky.

“I see something!” Remy said. A tiny red sparkle peeked from under the doormat. He lifted the mat. The ribbon was there, crinkled but bright.

“My ribbon!” Miri squeaked. She hugged it and hugged Remy too. “Thank you for helping. I feel better.”

Remy smiled. “I am glad. Now, would you help me sweep? We can finish faster together.”

“Yes!” Miri tied the ribbon in her fur so it would not fly away. Then she picked up the little dustpan. Remy took the broom again.

Swish, swish, swish. Scritch, scritch. They moved like a team. Remy swept the dust toward Miri. Miri held the pan steady. The dust made whispery sounds as it slid in. The breeze cooled their faces. A butterfly landed on the railing and opened its wings like tiny fans.

Just then, a friend hopped up the path. It was Gus the toad. He carried a heavy bag of books. He puffed, “Hoo! These are for story time. But they are heavy.”

Remy looked at the porch. Only one corner was left to sweep. He looked at Gus’s bag. He looked at Miri, who held the pan. He felt that same tug in his heart.

Remy said, “Gus, your bag looks heavy. How do you feel?”

“My back feels tired,” said Gus. “I could use help.”

Remy nodded. “I care about how you feel. We will help.”

Miri said, “I can hold the bag while you finish sweeping, Remy.”

Remy smiled at Miri. “Thank you. That is kind and responsible.”

Miri took the bag with a little grunt. Remy swept the last corner. Swish! The porch looked clean and bright. Sunlight made patterns on the floor like golden lace.

Remy and Miri carried the books to a safe spot by the chair. Gus sat and sighed with relief. “Thank you, friends,” he said. “You cared about me. That means a lot.”

Remy’s heart felt warm. He had finished his job. He had helped Miri find her ribbon. He had helped Gus with the books. He had listened and cared. He was still a determined rabbit, but now he was a thoughtful one too.

Gus opened a book. “Story time?” he asked.

“Yes!” said Remy and Miri together. They sat on the peaceful front porch. The wood felt smooth. The breeze was gentle. The birds sang again, “Chee-chee!” Everyone smiled.

Remy whispered, “Today I learned something big. When we listen and care, we show empathy. Helping friends is responsible. Working together makes friendship strong.”

Miri nodded. “And it feels good.”

Gus grinned. “It does.”

They read a story and shared a bowl of sweet carrot chips. The porch glowed in the afternoon sun. Remy looked around and felt proud of their clean, happy space.

The End.
