# Dippy the Dolphin Tries Something New

In a cozy study corner, by a sunny window and a shelf full of colorful books, lived a playful dolphin named Dippy. Dippy didn’t swim in water today—oh no! Dippy was a soft, bouncy toy dolphin who loved to learn. The study corner smelled like fresh crayons and warm paper. The little desk felt smooth, and the chair gave a friendly squeak when Dippy climbed up.

“Good morning, study corner!” Dippy chirped, tapping the pencil cup with a flipper. Tap-tap! The pencils wiggled like tiny trees in the wind.

Today, Dippy had a puzzle book. The cover showed shapes, numbers, and a small maze. Dippy’s eyes sparkled. “I’m curious! I want to try,” Dippy said.

On the first page was a shape puzzle. “Circle, square, triangle,” Dippy read slowly. The task said: Match the shapes. Dippy hummed a happy tune. “Easy-peasy, splashy-pleasey!”

Dippy picked up a round sticker. It was smooth and cool. “Circle goes here,” Dippy said. But when Dippy pressed the sticker, it didn’t fit. It looked a little too big. Dippy frowned. “Hmm. That didn’t work.”

Dippy felt a tiny bubble of grump rise up. “Maybe I can’t do it,” Dippy whispered.

Just then, the lamp clicked on with a warm glow. The light made the pages bright and friendly. From the shelf, a wise old bookmark with a string tassel wiggled and spoke in a soft voice. “Hello, Dippy. I’m Tassel. What’s the problem?”

“I tried, and it didn’t work,” Dippy said. “Maybe I should stop.”

Tassel swayed kindly. “Let’s try being open-minded. That means we keep our minds open to new ideas.”

“Open… like a book?” Dippy asked.

“Exactly,” Tassel said. “When we open a book, we see new pages. When we open our minds, we see new ways. Let’s use a strategy.”

Dippy perked up. “Strategy! That means a plan.”

“Yes,” said Tassel. “First, take a slow breath. In through your blowhole, out with a whoosh.”

Dippy giggled and took a slow pretend breath. “Whoooosh.”

“Now, look carefully,” Tassel continued. “What do you notice?”

Dippy touched the circle sticker again. It was shiny and smooth. “It’s big. The circle spot is small.”

“Good noticing,” said Tassel. “What could we try?”

“Maybe a smaller sticker,” Dippy said.

Dippy looked in the sticker pocket and found a tiny circle. “Hello, little circle!” Dippy whispered. The little circle fit perfectly. Snap!

“I did it!” Dippy cheered. The happy feeling inside fizzed like soda bubbles.

Next was the square. Dippy tried turning it. “Left, right, upside down,” Dippy sang. The square slid into place with a soft shoop. “Open-minded means trying new ways,” Dippy said, smiling.

On the next page was a maze. The start had a tiny boat. The finish had a cozy shell. The lines of the maze looked like twisty seaweed. Dippy held a blue crayon. It felt warm from the sun.

“Let’s go!” Dippy said. The crayon squeaked softly on the paper. Squeak, squeak. Dippy reached a dead end.

“Oh,” Dippy said. “This path is blocked.”

“What can we do?” asked Tassel.

“Be open-minded,” Dippy said. “Try a different way.” Dippy drew backwards, then tried a new path. Another block. Dippy paused.

“Strategy time!” Dippy announced. “I will use a finger first.” Dippy traced the paths with a flipper before drawing. “This way is too tight. This way loops back. Aha! This way looks good.”

Dippy drew slowly, carefully. The blue line glided along. Turn, turn, wiggle, wiggle… and out of the maze!

“I solved it!” Dippy squealed, clapping flippers. The chair squeaked again, like a cheer.

“You used your curiosity,” Tassel said. “You explored. You used strategy. You solved a problem.”

Dippy nodded. “And I kept my mind open. I tried new ideas.”

The next page had a picture of a dolphin stretching. “Self-care break,” the page said.

“What’s self-care?” Dippy asked.

“Caring for you,” said Tassel. “Let’s rest your eyes and body.”

Dippy wiggled back from the desk. The carpet felt soft on Dippy’s belly. Dippy stretched tall, flippers to the sky, and then curled small like a seashell. “Ahhh.” Dippy took a sip of cool water from a little cup. The water felt fresh, like ocean spray.

After the break, Dippy opened the book again. “I feel ready,” Dippy said. “My brain is calm and bright.”

There was one last challenge: a matching game with feelings. Happy, sad, calm, brave. Dippy looked at the faces. “This one looks happy. Big smile! This one looks calm. Soft eyes.” Dippy matched each face with gentle care.

“You did it,” Tassel said warmly.

Dippy closed the book with a soft thump. Sunlight peeked across the desk like a golden ribbon. “Today I learned to be open-minded,” Dippy said. “When one way didn’t work, I tried a new way. I used a plan. I took a break. I solved my problems!”

Tassel nodded. “That is a wonderful lesson.”

Dippy gave the pencil cup a happy tap. Tap-tap! “Tomorrow, I will try something new again. I will keep my mind open, like a book.”

The study corner felt cozy and proud. The lamp glowed softly. The chair gave one last cheerful squeak. Dippy curled up on the carpet, feeling calm, brave, and curious.

“And that,” Dippy whispered, “is how I grow.”

The End.
