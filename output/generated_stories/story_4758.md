# Toby the Tortoise's Big Goal

Toby the tortoise lived in a cozy laundry room. He loved the warm, soapy smells and the gentle hum of the washing machine. But Toby had a problem.

"I'm so slow," Toby sighed, looking at his short legs. "I want to help fold the laundry, but I can never reach the basket in time."

Every day, Toby watched the clothes tumble and spin. The dryer would *ding!* and warm, fluffy towels would come out. But by the time Toby crawled across the smooth tile floor, someone else had already folded everything.

"I wish I could be faster," Toby said sadly.

One sunny morning, Toby had an idea. "What if I make a goal? A special plan to get better!"

Toby thought hard. His wise old eyes sparkled. "I know! I'll practice moving a little bit every day. That's my goal!"

First, Toby decided to stretch. He pushed his green head up high and stretched his neck. "One, two, three!" he counted. It felt good to move his muscles.

Next, Toby practiced taking bigger steps. Instead of tiny shuffles, he tried to stretch his legs further. *Step, step, step* across the cool floor. His toenails made little clicking sounds.

"This is hard work," Toby puffed. "But I'm not giving up!"

Every day, Toby practiced. On Monday, he stretched for five minutes. On Tuesday, he walked around the laundry basket three times. On Wednesday, he tried walking a little faster.

The washing machine gurgled and bubbled, cheering Toby on. "You can do it!" it seemed to say with each *whoosh* of water.

By Thursday, something amazing happened. When the dryer went *ding!*, Toby was already halfway to the basket!

"Look at me go!" Toby called out happily. His legs felt stronger. His steps felt surer.

On Friday, Toby reached the laundry basket just as the warm clothes came out. A soft, yellow towel landed right next to him.

"I did it!" Toby cheered. "I reached my goal!"

But Toby wasn't finished. He carefully grabbed the corner of the towel with his mouth and started folding. It was tricky work for a tortoise, but Toby was determined.

Fold, fold, fold. The towel became a neat little square.

"Wow!" said a voice. It was Mrs. Chen, who owned the house. "Did you fold this towel, Toby?"

Toby nodded proudly, his shell gleaming in the laundry room light.

"That's wonderful!" Mrs. Chen smiled. "You've been working so hard. I've watched you practice every day."

Toby felt warm and happy inside, warmer than the dryer's heat.

"You know what, Toby?" Mrs. Chen said, patting his shell gently. "You taught me something important. When we set goals and practice every day, we can do amazing things!"

From that day on, Toby helped with laundry every week. He folded washcloths and matched socks. His movements were still careful and steady, but now they had purpose.

"Being slow isn't bad," Toby realized. "What matters is setting a goal and working toward it, one step at a time."

The laundry room felt even cozier now. The washing machine hummed happy songs, and Toby hummed along as he worked. He had learned that with a goal, practice, and patience, anyone can improve and help others.

And whenever young animals visited the laundry room, Toby would tell them his secret: "Set a goal, practice every day, and never give up. You'll surprise yourself with what you can do!"

The End.
