# Milo's Guest Room Adventure

Milo the monkey loved swinging from branch to branch in his backyard tree. His long, brown tail helped him balance, and his bright eyes sparkled with curiosity. Today was extra special because Grandma Banana was coming to visit!

"Milo," called Mama from inside the house. "Can you help me prepare the guest room for Grandma?"

"Yes, Mama!" Milo chirped, bouncing through the doorway. The guest room smelled like fresh lavender and sunshine streaming through the clean windows.

But oh my! The room was quite messy. Toys were scattered on the soft, blue carpet. Books lay open on the cozy reading chair. Milo's exercise balls had rolled under the bed, and his jump rope was tangled in a corner.

"Whoops," Milo said, scratching his fuzzy head. "I forgot I played in here yesterday."

Mama smiled gently. "Remember, Milo, when we have guests, we want them to feel welcome and comfortable. How do you think Grandma would feel if her room was messy?"

Milo thought carefully, tapping his finger on his chin. "She might feel sad or unwelcome," he said softly. "I want Grandma to feel happy and loved!"

"That's showing respect for others," Mama explained. "When we care about how others feel, we're being respectful."

Milo's tail swished with determination. "I want to fix this! But it's a lot of work..."

Just then, his little sister Penny peeked around the doorway. "Can I help?" she asked sweetly.

"Would you?" Milo's face lit up. "We can work together!"

First, they made a plan. "Let's pick up the toys first," suggested Penny. "Then the books, then your exercise things."

Working as a team felt wonderful! Milo gathered his colorful exercise balls while Penny carefully stacked the picture books. The room began to smell even fresher as they organized everything.

"This is actually fun!" giggled Milo as he untangled his jump rope. "And it's easier when we work together."

When they finished picking up, Milo had an idea. "Let's make the bed extra fluffy for Grandma!" He and Penny fluffed the pillows until they were as soft as clouds.

Penny found some pretty flowers from the garden. "These smell like sunshine," she said, placing them on the nightstand.

Finally, they stepped back to admire their work. The guest room looked beautiful! The bed was neat and inviting, books were organized on the shelf, and everything sparkled clean.

"We did it!" cheered Milo, giving Penny a big hug. "Thank you for helping me. I couldn't have done it without you."

When Grandma Banana arrived, her eyes twinkled with joy. "Oh my! This room is absolutely perfect. I feel so welcome and loved!"

Milo's chest puffed with pride. "We prepared it just for you, Grandma. We wanted you to feel happy here."

"You showed such respect and kindness," Grandma said, wrapping both children in a warm, banana-scented hug. "When we care about others' feelings and work together, wonderful things happen."

That night, as Milo snuggled in his own bed, he felt warm and happy inside. He had learned that respecting others means thinking about their feelings and making them feel welcome. And working together with Penny had made everything more fun and easier too!

"Tomorrow," he whispered to himself, "I'll remember to always think about others first."

The End.
