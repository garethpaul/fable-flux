# Rocco’s Music Time Deal

Rocco was a musical raccoon with a stripey tail and bright, curious eyes. He lived in a playful living room with soft pillows, colorful blocks, and a basket of shiny instruments. The rug felt fuzzy under his paws, and the air smelled like warm cocoa. Sunlight made little squares on the floor.

“Ta-tum, ta-tum!” Rocco tapped his small drum. “Ting, ting!” He shook a tiny bell. Music made his heart feel bouncy.

Mama came in with a gentle smile. She carried a folded laundry basket that smelled clean and cozy. “Rocco, my tune,” Mama said, “it’s almost tidying time.”

Rocco’s ears perked. He loved music. He did not love tidying. “But I want to play more,” he said softly, hugging his drum. His whiskers drooped.

Mama knelt so her eyes met his. “I hear you,” she said in a warm voice. “You want more music. And we also need a tidy room so we can move safely. What can we do?”

Rocco blinked. He thought. His drum felt smooth under his paws. He took a breath, just like when he played a slow song. “Maybe… we make a plan?” he asked.

“A plan sounds smart,” Mama said. “Let’s use our words and make a deal. That’s called negotiation. We both share and listen.”

Rocco nodded. “I want five songs.” He held up his little fingers. “Five!”

Mama tapped her chin. “Five songs is a lot. I need time to put away laundry. How about two songs, then tidy time?”

Rocco’s tail swished. He felt a little grumpy bubble inside. He closed his eyes and counted, “One… two…” He took a belly breath. The bubble got smaller. “Hmm,” he said. “What about three songs? And then I tidy fast, like a zoom!”

Mama’s eyes twinkled. “Three songs and a zoom-tidy? I like that. And after tidy, we can have a snack. Crunchy apple slices.”

Rocco smiled. “Deal!” He clicked his drumstick and bell together. “Click! Deal!”

“Let’s shake on it,” Mama said. They shook paws. It felt soft and safe.

Rocco began Song One. “Tippy tap, tippy tap,” went the drum. He added the bell, “Ting-ting!” He swayed and hummed. The living room sounded bright, like sunshine dancing.

For Song Two, he played the xylophone. “Plink, plonk, plink!” The notes were like rainbow drops. Rocco giggled, and his tail made a happy wiggle.

Before Song Three, Rocco paused. His eyes slid to the blocks and the stuffed giraffe. He felt a wiggle of wanting more songs. “Maybe four?” he whispered.

He looked at Mama. She was folding socks, hum-humming his tune. Rocco remembered their deal. He remembered the shake. He pressed his paw to his chest and took a slow breath. “I can do it,” he told himself. “Three songs. Then tidy.”

Self-control felt like holding a drum steady. He lifted the maracas. “Chaka-chaka, chaka-chaka!” Song Three was lively and fun. He stomped softly on the rug—pat, pat, pat—and sang, “Three songs done, tidy is next. I can do it, I can do it!”

When the last chaka faded, Rocco set the maracas in the basket. “Okay, Mama,” he said, standing tall. “Deal time. Zoom-tidy!”

Mama smiled wide. “I am proud of you for keeping our deal.”

Rocco made a tidy song. “Pick and plop, pick and plop!” He picked up blocks. Plop! Into the bin. “Fold and tuck, fold and tuck!” He placed scarves into the drawer. His paws moved quick. The room began to look calm and open. The fuzzy rug felt big again.

He saw his drum on the floor. He wanted to play it right away. The want was strong, like a big beat in his chest. “Boom-boom.” Rocco put a paw on the drum, then stopped. He took a gentle breath and sang softly, “Deal first, then drum.” He slid the drum onto the shelf. Click! It felt good to be responsible.

“All tidy!” Rocco called. He stretched his arms wide. “The floor is ready for dancing!”

Mama clapped. “You negotiated with me. You kept your promise. You used self-regulation—that means you used your calm to make a good choice.”

Rocco’s cheeks felt warm and happy. “I did it! I can make plans.”

Mama brought a plate. The apple slices were shiny and sweet. “Crunch!” Rocco bit one. The juice tasted cool and bright. They sat together on the soft couch. The living room was peaceful. The instruments rested in their basket, waiting for next time.

“Next time,” Rocco said, licking a tiny drip of juice, “maybe we make a new deal. Three songs, tidy, then one dance?”

Mama nodded. “That sounds fair. We can talk and choose together.”

Rocco’s tail curled like a music note. “Talking helps,” he said. “Deals help. I can listen. I can wait. I can tidy. Then I can play again.”

Mama kissed his forehead. “That is a wonderful lesson.”

Rocco tapped his knee softly—tap, tap—and hummed a small happy tune. The room smelled like apples, and the day felt bright and safe. He had used his words. He had made a plan. He had kept it.

The End.
