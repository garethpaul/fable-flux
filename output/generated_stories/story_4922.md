# Brave Cub Benny and the Bathroom Race

Benny was a friendly bear cub. His fur was soft like a warm towel. He loved to play, splash, and sing in the bathroom. The bathroom smelled like sweet soap and minty toothpaste. The tiles were cool under his paws.

One rainy morning, Benny lined up his rubber duck, a blue cup, and a tiny boat on the edge of the tub. “Ready, set, go!” he cheered. “It’s bath-time races!”

Benny filled the tub with warm water. Steam made little clouds on the mirror. He tapped the boat. He tapped the duck. He tapped the cup. “Float, float, float,” he sang. The toys bobbed and bumped. Splash! Swish!

Then Benny saw a big, crinkly plastic bottle from shampoo on the floor. It was empty. He held it up. Crunch! Crunch! The sound was loud. “This can be a racer too!” he said.

Benny twisted off the cap. He felt a tiny worry in his tummy. Mama Bear always said, “Caps off for recycling.” Benny looked at the bin under the sink. It had a blue circle on it. The blue circle meant recycling. The bin was almost full of clean bottles and cartons.

Benny whispered, “I should put this bottle in there.” But he wanted another racer so much. “Maybe I can use it... just for one race,” he said softly.

He placed the big bottle in the water. It bobbed and splashed. “Go, Bottle Racer!” Benny laughed. The duck bumped the bottle. The bottle bumped the boat. Water sloshed over the edge. Drip, drip, drip.

Uh-oh. A little soap slipped from the shelf. Plop! The soap slid into the tub. It made the water slippery. The boat wobbled and tipped. The bottle bumped the drain chain. Clink! The tub plug wiggled.

Benny’s heart thumped. “Oh no,” he said. “I took the bottle and made a mess.” He took a deep breath. His paws felt shaky. The bathroom felt very big and quiet, except for drip, drip, drip.

He remembered Mama Bear’s words: “Courage means doing the right thing, even when you feel wobbly inside.”

Benny looked at the mirror cloud. He drew a tiny heart with his paw. Then he nodded to himself. “I can be brave,” he said aloud. “I can fix this.”

First, he turned off the water tap. The room grew calm. Next, he gently picked up the soap and put it back on the shelf. He used a small towel to wipe the drips on the floor. Swish, pat, pat. He picked up the big bottle. “I will be honest,” Benny whispered. “I will tell Mama. And I will recycle.”

He trotted to the door. “Mama?” he called in a small voice.

Mama Bear peeked in. “Hello, Benny. I hear splashes. Are you having fun?”

Benny took a breath. “Mama, I used the empty shampoo bottle for a race,” he said. “I should have put it in the blue bin. The soap fell, and I made drips. I’m sorry.”

Mama Bear knelt and hugged him. She smelled like warm pancakes. “Thank you for telling me the truth,” she said gently. “That is honest and brave.”

Benny’s shoulders relaxed. His tummy felt lighter. “I want to fix it,” he said. He held up the bottle. “Blue bin?”

Mama smiled. “Yes, blue bin for recycling. When we recycle, old things can become new things. That helps the earth.”

They opened the bin. It made a soft whoosh. Benny dropped the bottle inside. Clunk! He grinned. “Goodbye, Bottle Racer. Hello, Helping the Earth!”

Mama looked at the tub. “You know,” she said, “we can still have bath-time races. Would you like to make a safe course?”

Benny’s ears perked up. “Yes!”

They made a little sports game in the tub. Mama placed a washcloth at one end like a finish line. Benny put the rubber duck at the start. “This is the Duck Dash,” he said. He used the blue cup to pour small, gentle water pushes. “Ready, set, go!”

The duck glided. “Quack!” Benny cheered. “Go, go, go!” The boat sailed behind. The water made tiny waves—shh, shh, shh. Benny laughed and clapped. It felt like a real race, but safe and neat.

When the race was done, Benny looked at Mama. “I was scared to tell you,” he said. “My paws were shaky.”

Mama touched his paw. “That is courage, little cub. You felt shaky and told the truth anyway. You cleaned up. You recycled. That is being strong on the inside.”

Benny stood tall on his step stool. He felt proud. “Next time,” he said, “I will put bottles in the blue bin first. And then we can race with our bath toys.”

Mama nodded. “That’s a smart plan.”

Benny wrapped himself in a big, fluffy towel. It felt like a hug. The mirror clouds started to fade. He looked at his duck and boat, lined up and ready for tomorrow’s Duck Dash.

Benny smiled. “I am brave,” he whispered. “I can do the right thing.”

The End.
