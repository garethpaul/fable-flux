# Robo Pup Helps at the Desk

Click-click! Whirr-whirr! Robo Pup’s little tail light blinked blue as he rolled into the home office. His paws were soft rubber, his nose was a tiny sensor, and his ears were shiny and silver. He made a happy beep. “Good morning!” he said in a bright, friendly voice.

Maya sat at the big desk with her dad. Papers were stacked like little towers. A plant leaned toward the sunny window. The keyboard went tap-tap-tap. The room smelled like fresh paper and warm toast. Outside, a bird sang tweet-tweet.

Maya sighed. “We have a lot to do,” she said.

Dad rubbed his eyes. “We sure do,” he said. “We need to find the red folder, water the plant, have a snack, and send a big email. It feels like too much.”

Robo Pup tilted his head. “I can help,” he said. His tail light blinked green. “Helping is my favorite.”

Maya smiled. “Thank you, Robo Pup. Let’s be a team.”

“Teamwork!” beeped Robo Pup.

They all took a deep breath in and out. “First,” Maya said, “let’s make a plan.”

Robo Pup projected a tiny light on the desk. A simple list appeared. It said: 1. Find the red folder. 2. Water the plant. 3. Snack break. 4. Send the email.

Dad nodded. “Good strategy,” he said. “A plan helps our brains.”

Robo Pup sniffed the air with his sensor nose. “Sniff-sniff. I smell paper. I hear a crinkle,” he said.

Maya looked under a stack. “Not here,” she said.

Robo Pup rolled under the chair with a soft whirr. “Excuse me, chair,” he said politely. “Beep! I see a corner of red!”

He nudged the folder out with his gentle paw. “Found it!”

Maya clapped. “You were very helpful,” she said.

Robo Pup wagged his light tail. “I like to help,” he said. “Helping makes my heart chip feel warm.”

“Next,” Maya said, “the plant.”

The plant’s leaves drooped a little. Its soil looked dry and dark. “Poor plant,” Maya whispered. “It needs a drink.”

Dad got a small cup. “Not too much, not too little,” he said.

Robo Pup scanned the plant. “Beep-beep. A small cup is just right,” he said.

Maya poured slowly. The water made a soft glug-glug. The soil turned a rich, happy brown. A tiny drop splashed on her finger. “It’s cool,” she giggled.

“Thank you,” the plant seemed to say as its leaves perked up toward the light.

Robo Pup blinked. “We helped the plant!” he said. “Helping living things is kind.”

Maya’s tummy growled. “Grrr,” it said like a tiny bear.

Robo Pup listened. “I hear a tummy,” he said. “Healthy-habits time!”

Dad smiled. “Snack break,” he agreed.

They washed hands with warm water. Splish-splash! The soap smelled like lemons. Robo Pup sang a silly song. “Wash, wash, wash your hands, get the bubbles on. Rub the tops and rub the thumbs, then the germs are gone!”

Maya laughed. “That is a good song,” she said.

They sat by the sunny window. The sun felt cozy on their shoulders. They ate crunchy apple slices and peanut butter. Crunch, crunch. “Yum,” said Maya.

Robo Pup had a charging sip from his power bowl. “Zing!” he said. “I am ready to help again.”

Back at the desk, the last job waited. The big email. The keyboard went tap-tap. Dad frowned. “We need the right words,” he said.

Maya looked at Robo Pup. “What do we do when we are stuck?” she asked.

Robo Pup’s ears twitched. “We stay open-minded,” he said. “We try new ideas. We can work as a team.”

Maya nodded. “I have an idea,” she said. “Let’s say thank you first.”

Dad smiled. “Good idea.”

Robo Pup added, “And be clear. Short and kind words help.”

Maya spoke slowly. “Hello. Thank you for reading. Here is our plan.” Dad typed. Tap-tap-tap.

Robo Pup listened for mistakes. “Beep! A missing period,” he said kindly.

“Thank you,” Dad said. He pressed the key. Click.

They read the email together. It sounded friendly and smart. “Ready?” Dad asked.

“Ready,” said Maya.

“Ready!” beeped Robo Pup.

Click! The email flew away like a little paper bird in the screen. Whoosh!

They all cheered. “We did it!” Maya said. She hugged Robo Pup. He felt warm and smooth. His tail light glowed a happy rainbow.

“You were very helpful,” she told him.

Robo Pup nuzzled her hand. “You were helpful too,” he said. “We made a plan. We took care of the plant. We took a break. We stayed open-minded. We worked as a team.”

Dad stretched and smiled. “When we help each other, hard jobs feel easy,” he said.

Maya looked around the tidy home office. The plant stood tall. The desk was neat. The room smelled like apples and lemons. The bird outside sang a bright song. It felt calm. It felt good.

Robo Pup wagged. “Helping makes the day shine,” he said.

Maya nodded. “I learned that being helpful is kind. I learned that trying new ideas helps. I learned that teamwork makes our work better.”

Robo Pup beeped softly. “And healthy habits give us energy to help.”

Maya patted his shiny ear. “Let’s help again tomorrow,” she said.

“Teamwork tomorrow,” Robo Pup agreed.

The End.
