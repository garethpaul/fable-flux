# Mango's Musical Solution

Mango the monkey swung into the guest room with a big smile. His fluffy brown tail curled with excitement. The room smelled like fresh flowers from the vase on the nightstand.

"What a perfect place to practice!" Mango said, bouncing on his toes. He pulled out his shiny red ball and started dribbling it on the wooden floor. Bounce, bounce, bounce!

But then - THUD! The ball hit the wall and knocked over a lamp.

"Oh no!" Mango gasped. His heart beat fast like a drum. The lamp was okay, but it made a loud noise. He heard footsteps coming down the hall.

Mrs. Robin, the house owner, peeked into the room. Her face looked worried. "Mango, dear, the guests next door are trying to sleep. The bouncing is too loud."

Mango's shoulders drooped. His ears felt hot with embarrassment. "I'm sorry, Mrs. Robin. I just wanted to practice my basketball moves."

"I understand," Mrs. Robin said gently. "But we need to find a quieter way."

After Mrs. Robin left, Mango sat on the soft blue bed. He looked at his ball sadly. The room felt too quiet now. Through the thin wall, he could hear someone coughing next door.

"Those guests must be sick," Mango thought. "No wonder they need rest."

Mango's eyes suddenly sparkled with an idea. He remembered his small wooden flute tucked in his backpack. His grandmother had taught him that music could make people feel better.

"Maybe I can help instead of cause trouble," he whispered.

Mango took out his flute. It was smooth and warm in his hands. He played a very soft, gentle tune. The notes floated through the air like butterflies. The melody was slow and peaceful, like a lullaby.

He heard the coughing next door stop. Someone sighed peacefully.

Then Mango had another idea. He picked up his ball, but this time he didn't bounce it. Instead, he rolled it slowly across the carpet. He pretended it was a bowling ball, rolling it toward the pillows he arranged like pins.

"Strike!" he whispered quietly, pumping his fist in the air.

The soft carpet made no noise at all. Mango could practice his aim and have fun without disturbing anyone. He rolled the ball again and again, getting better each time.

After a while, Mrs. Robin returned with a warm cup of tea. "Mango, I heard your beautiful music. And look how creative you've been with your ball game!"

Mango beamed with pride. "I wanted to help the sick guests feel better, not worse."

"That was very thoughtful," Mrs. Robin said, ruffling his soft fur. "You found a peaceful solution that made everyone happy."

Just then, a gentle knock came from the wall. The guest next door called softly, "Thank you for the lovely music, little monkey. It helped me fall asleep peacefully."

Mango's heart filled with joy. His tail wiggled happily. "You're welcome!" he called back quietly.

From that day on, whenever Mango wanted to play sports indoors, he thought of creative, quiet ways to practice. And when others needed comfort, he shared his gentle music.

Mrs. Robin smiled as she watched Mango play. "Sometimes the best solutions come from thinking about others first," she said.

Mango nodded wisely. He had learned that being courageous meant finding peaceful ways to solve problems and help others feel better.

The End.
