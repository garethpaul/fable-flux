# Momo the Monkey's Colorful Kitchen Adventure

Momo the monkey swung into Grandma's warm, cozy kitchen. The morning sun streamed through the yellow curtains, making everything glow like golden honey. The kitchen smelled wonderful - like cinnamon and fresh bread.

"Good morning, Momo!" called Grandma from the stove. "Are you ready for breakfast?"

Momo's tummy rumbled loudly. "Yes! I'm so hungry I could eat a whole banana tree!"

Grandma chuckled and opened the refrigerator. But when she looked inside, her smile turned into a worried frown. "Oh my! We only have brown bananas, wilted lettuce, and some cookies left."

Momo scratched his furry head. "That doesn't look very yummy, Grandma. What can we do?"

"Well," said Grandma thoughtfully, "we could just eat the cookies for breakfast."

Momo was about to say yes when he remembered something his teacher had told him. He took a deep breath and thought carefully. "Wait, Grandma. Cookies taste good, but they won't give my body the energy I need to play and learn today."

"You're absolutely right, little one," Grandma said proudly. "What do you think we should do?"

Momo looked around the kitchen with his bright, curious eyes. He spotted a basket of colorful fruits on the counter - red apples, orange oranges, purple grapes, and yellow lemons. Next to it sat a bag of green spinach and some white yogurt in the refrigerator.

"Look!" Momo pointed excitedly. "We have lots of rainbow foods! Can we make something healthy and delicious?"

"What a wonderful idea!" Grandma clapped her hands. "What would you like to make?"

Momo thought mindfully, closing his eyes and imagining different flavors. "How about a rainbow smoothie? We can use the colorful fruits and some spinach!"

Together, they washed the fresh fruits. Momo felt the cool water running over his paws and smelled the sweet scent of the strawberries. Grandma showed him how to carefully cut the apples into small pieces.

"The red apples will give us vitamins," Grandma explained as they worked. "The orange carrots help our eyes stay healthy. And the green spinach makes our muscles strong!"

Momo added each ingredient to the blender, watching the colors swirl together. "It looks like a beautiful rainbow!" he giggled.

When they turned on the blender, it made a funny whirring sound that made Momo dance around the kitchen. The smoothie turned a lovely purple color.

Grandma poured the smoothie into two tall glasses and added colorful straws. "Here's to making healthy choices!" she said.

Momo took a big sip. The smoothie was cold, creamy, and surprisingly delicious! "Mmm! It tastes like sunshine and happiness," he said, licking his lips.

As they sat together at the kitchen table, Momo felt proud and strong. His tummy was happy, and he had energy to play.

"Thank you for helping me think about what's best for my body, Grandma," Momo said, giving her a big hug.

"You made the smart choice all by yourself," Grandma replied. "When we take time to think about what our bodies need, we can make wonderful decisions."

Momo finished his rainbow smoothie and felt ready for a day full of adventures, knowing he had given his body exactly what it needed to grow big and strong.

The End.
