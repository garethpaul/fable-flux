# Polly's Pantry Art

Polly the parrot loved to create beautiful things. Her bright green feathers shimmered in the morning light as she flew into the kitchen pantry, looking for something special to make.

"Good morning, cans and boxes!" Polly chirped cheerfully. "What wonderful art can we make today?"

The pantry was full of interesting shapes and colors. Tall cereal boxes stood like soldiers, round cans rolled gently on the shelves, and colorful pasta packages sparkled like jewels. Polly's eyes grew wide with excitement.

"I know!" she squawked. "I'll build the most amazing tower ever!"

Polly carefully picked up a big can of tomatoes with her strong beak. *Clunk!* She placed it on the floor. Then she added a box of crackers on top. *Thud!* The tower was growing!

But when Polly tried to add a bag of rice, everything tumbled down. *Crash! Bang! Rattle!* The cans rolled across the floor, and the boxes scattered everywhere.

"Oh no!" Polly sighed, her colorful tail feathers drooping. "My tower fell down. Maybe I'm not good at making things."

Polly felt sad and wanted to give up. She tucked her head under her wing and made a small, disappointed chirping sound.

Just then, she heard a gentle voice. "Don't give up, Polly!" It was Grandma Cat, peeking into the pantry. "I saw what happened. Would you like to try again?"

"But it keeps falling down," Polly said softly. "Maybe I'm not creative enough."

Grandma Cat smiled warmly. "Being creative means trying new ways, little one. What if you tried something different?"

Polly lifted her head and looked around the pantry again. This time, she noticed how the morning sunlight made pretty shadows through the window. The round cans cast circle shadows, and the square boxes made square shadows.

"Ooh!" Polly chirped excitedly. "What if I make a shadow picture instead?"

Polly began arranging the cans and boxes in a new way. She didn't stack them high this time. Instead, she placed them carefully on the sunny floor. The round can shadows looked like suns, and the rectangular box shadows looked like houses.

"Look, Grandma Cat!" Polly called out proudly. "I made a shadow town! There's a sun shining over little houses!"

"How wonderful!" Grandma Cat purred. "You didn't give up, and you found a completely new way to be creative!"

Polly danced around her shadow creation, her feathers catching the light. She felt proud and happy. "I thought I wasn't good at making things, but I just needed to try a different way!"

"That's right," Grandma Cat said. "Creativity means there are many ways to make something beautiful. When one way doesn't work, we can always try another way."

Polly spent the rest of the morning making different shadow pictures. She created shadow flowers, shadow butterflies, and even a shadow rainbow using colorful cereal boxes.

"I love being creative!" Polly sang happily. "Even when things don't work the first time, I can keep trying new ideas!"

From that day on, whenever Polly faced a problem, she remembered her pantry adventure. She learned that being creative wasn't about doing things perfectly the first time. It was about trying, learning, and never giving up on her wonderful ideas.

And the pantry became Polly's favorite place to create new and amazing shadow art every sunny morning.

The End.
