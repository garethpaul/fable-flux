# Ellie's Rooftop Adventure

Little Ellie the elephant calf loved to explore. Her gray skin sparkled in the morning sun as she wandered through the city with her mama. Today, something very special caught her eye - a colorful rooftop garden high above the busy street.

"Mama, look!" Ellie pointed with her tiny trunk. "What a pretty place up there!"

Mama Elephant smiled. "That's Mrs. Chen's rooftop garden, dear. She grows vegetables and flowers for everyone in the neighborhood."

Ellie's big brown eyes grew wide with wonder. She could see red tomatoes, green lettuce, and bright yellow flowers dancing in the breeze. The sweet smell of herbs floated down to her sensitive trunk.

"Can we visit?" Ellie asked hopefully.

"We're too big for the stairs, little one," Mama explained gently. "But maybe you could find another way to help."

Later that day, Ellie heard loud voices from the rooftop. She looked up and saw Mrs. Chen talking with two other neighbors. They sounded upset!

"My plants need more water!" said Mr. Ahmed, his voice getting louder.

"Well, my flowers need more sunshine!" shouted Mrs. Rodriguez, stamping her foot.

Mrs. Chen looked very sad. "Please don't fight," she said quietly. "There's enough garden for everyone."

But the neighbors kept arguing. Soon, Mr. Ahmed stomped away, and Mrs. Rodriguez crossed her arms angrily. Poor Mrs. Chen sat down and began to cry.

Ellie's heart felt heavy. She wanted to help, but how? She was just a little elephant, and the rooftop was so high. Her trunk trembled with worry.

"I'm scared," Ellie whispered to herself. "But Mrs. Chen needs help."

Taking a deep breath, Ellie remembered what Mama always said: "Courage means doing the right thing, even when you feel scared."

Ellie walked to the bottom of the building and called up with her loudest, gentlest voice: "Mrs. Chen! Don't be sad!"

Mrs. Chen looked down and smiled through her tears. "Hello, little Ellie!"

"I have an idea!" Ellie trumpeted. "What if everyone shares the garden? Mr. Ahmed can have the morning sun for his plants, and Mrs. Rodriguez can have the afternoon sun for her flowers!"

Mrs. Chen's face lit up. "That's wonderful! But how do we tell them?"

Ellie thought hard. Then she had another brave idea. Using her trunk, she picked up smooth pebbles and gently tossed them at Mr. Ahmed's window, then at Mrs. Rodriguez's window.

When they both came to look, Ellie called out: "Please come back! I have a sharing plan!"

Curious, both neighbors returned to the rooftop. Ellie explained her idea about sharing the sunny spots. She also suggested they could share their vegetables and flowers with each other.

"The tomatoes would taste good with Mrs. Rodriguez's herbs," Ellie said sweetly. "And Mr. Ahmed's cucumbers would be perfect for Mrs. Chen's salad!"

The three neighbors looked at each other. Slowly, Mr. Ahmed smiled. "I'm sorry I got angry," he said.

"Me too," said Mrs. Rodriguez. "Sharing sounds much better than fighting."

Mrs. Chen clapped her hands with joy. "Thank you, brave little Ellie! You helped us remember that gardens grow better when friends work together!"

From that day on, the rooftop garden became the most beautiful place in the neighborhood. And whenever Ellie walked by, she could smell the sweet success of courage, kindness, and sharing floating down from above.

The End.
