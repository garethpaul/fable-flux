# The Guest Room Dance

In a cozy house at the edge of the woods, there was a soft, sunny guest room. The room smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap. A big window let in warm light. There was a fluffy rug that felt like a cloud.

A resourceful squirrel named Sable tip-tapped in with her little paws. “Today is dance day!” she said. Her tail twitched with joy. “I promised a dance show at two o’clock.” Sable looked at the clock on the wall. The hands pointed up and over. “I have one hour,” she whispered. “I can do this.”

Sable unpacked a shiny scarf, some tiny bells, and a small music box. She made a list on a piece of paper with a big red crayon.

“Step one: tidy the space.
Step two: practice the dance.
Step three: invite friends.
Step four: start the show at two.”

She nodded. “Plan ready!”

Sable tugged the blanket smooth and puffed the pillows. She rolled the rug flat. “All clear,” she said. The room felt neat and calm. She could hear birds singing outside and the soft tick-tick of the clock.

Then Sable heard a knock-knock-knock. “Come in!” she called.

A shy mouse peeked in. “Hello. I’m Millie. Can I watch?” Millie’s whiskers trembled. She held a button like a little drum.

Sable smiled. “Of course! You can dance too if you like. Everyone is welcome.”

Millie’s eyes sparkled. “Really?”

“Really,” Sable said. “We have thirty-five minutes. Let’s practice.”

Knock-knock. A turtle named Tino scooted in, slow and careful. “I heard music,” he said. “Can I be in the show? I dance slow.”

Sable clapped her paws. “Slow is beautiful. You are welcome.” She checked the clock. “We have thirty minutes.”

Knock-knock-knock. A tall heron, Hana, stepped in the doorway. “My legs are long,” she said softly. “Sometimes I wobble.”

Sable waved her in. “Long legs can make lovely lines. Welcome, Hana.”

Everyone smiled. The guest room felt warm and friendly. The lemon soap scent mixed with the sweet smell of Sable’s pinecone snacks.

Sable set the music box on the dresser. “Let’s make a plan so we are ready on time,” she said. “First, we will learn a simple step. Then, we will add our own special moves. Everyone is included.”

She tapped the floor. “Step, step, twirl.” The bells jingled. Millie tried. “Step, step, twirl.” Her little button drum went tap-tap. Tino moved slow. “Step… step… twirl.” Hana tried to twirl and wobbled. “Whoa!”

Sable thought hard. “We can make space for long legs.” She moved the chair aside and slid the suitcase under the bed. “More room now.” She drew a little circle on the rug with chalk. “Hana, you can twirl inside this circle. Tino, you can glide around the edge. Millie, you can keep the beat.”

Hana took a deep breath. “I’ll try again.” She twirled inside the circle. “I did it!” she chirped.

“We have twenty minutes,” Sable said, peeking at the clock. “Let’s add our special moves. Millie, what is your move?”

Millie lifted her button drum. “Tap-tap-tap!” The tiny taps sounded like raindrops on a window.

“Tino?” Sable asked.

Tino smiled. “Slow wave.” He flowed his arms like water. It was calm and kind.

“Hana?” Sable asked.

Hana stretched her wings. “Feather sweep.” She swept her long arms with a soft shhh sound.

Sable’s tail swished. “Mine is scarf swirl.” She spun the shiny scarf. It whooshed like wind.

Sable lined them up. “Order time. We go Sable, Millie, Tino, Hana, then all together.” She drew the order on her paper. “We have ten minutes. Quick review!”

They practiced. “Step, step, twirl.” Tap-tap-tap. Slow wave. Feather sweep. Scarf swirl. The room jingled and shhh-ed and clicked with tiny feet. Everyone laughed.

Then the music box stopped. “Oh no,” said Millie. “It is quiet.”

Sable took a breath. “We can solve this.” She listened. Tick-tick went the clock. Birds chirped. “We can make our own music,” Sable said. “Clap clap, tap tap, swish swish.” She clapped a pattern. Millie tapped her button. Tino hummed a gentle tune. Hana swished her sleeves. The room filled with home-made sound.

Sable checked the clock. “Two o’clock!” she said. “Show time.”

Neighbors peeked in: a chipmunk, a frog, and a ladybug. They sat on the pillows. The guest room glowed in the soft sun.

Sable stepped forward. “Welcome to our dance. Everyone belongs. We all dance in our own way.”

The show began. Sable swirled her scarf—whoosh! Millie tapped—tap-tap-tap! Tino waved—slow and smooth. Hana swept—soft and light. Then they all danced together. Step, step, twirl. Clap, tap, hum, swish. The guests smiled and clapped along.

At the end, Sable bowed. Millie squeaked with pride. Tino beamed. Hana stood tall and steady.

The frog croaked, “I liked the slow wave!” The chipmunk chirped, “I liked the button drum!” The ladybug whispered, “I liked the twirl circle!”

Sable’s heart felt warm. “Thank you for coming,” she said. “Today we learned to plan our time, solve problems, and include everyone. When we dance together, we all shine.”

Millie nodded. “I felt brave.”

Tino said, “I felt calm.”

Hana smiled. “I felt tall and strong.”

Sable wrapped her tail around them in a hug. “We are a team,” she said. “There is room for all of us.”

The guest room was quiet again, cozy and bright. The lemon scent floated in the air. The clock went tick-tick, softly and happy.

And Sable wrote on her paper, “Lesson: Everyone can join. We make space. We make time. We dance together.”

The End.
