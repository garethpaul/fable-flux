# Sprig the Squirrel’s Sparkly Clean Plan

In a cozy, comfortable nursery, morning light made soft golden squares on the rug. The room smelled like warm soap and pine blocks. Little Sprig, a resourceful squirrel with a fluffy tail and bright eyes, lived in this nursery with the children’s toys. He loved building towers, sorting storybooks, and nibbling crunchy acorn snacks.

One day, Sprig woke up and stretched. “Good morning, room!” he chirped. He rustled his whiskers and blinked. Something felt sticky. His paws were tacky. His tail had crumbs. His whiskers drooped.

“Oh acorns,” Sprig whispered. “I forgot to wash up after snack time.” He scrunched his nose. He didn’t like the sticky feeling.

From the shelf, a soft stuffed rabbit named Button wiggled her floppy ears. “Sprig, are you okay?” she asked kindly.

Sprig sighed. “I want to play, but I’m all icky. Washing takes too long,” he said, pawing at a crumb on his tail. “And the sink is so tall!”

Across the room, a shiny toy truck named Vroom rolled closer. “Rules are rules,” Vroom hummed. “Clean paws before play.”

Sprig’s cheeks grew warm. He felt a little mad at the rule and a little sad at himself. He wanted to be quick and clever, not sticky and slow. He took a deep breath. The nursery smelled like lemon polish and crayons. The smell made him feel calm.

“I am a problem-solver,” Sprig told himself. “I can make a plan.”

He looked around the nursery. He saw a step stool by the tiny sink, soft towels folded like clouds, a gentle pump soap with bubbles on the label, and a bright picture chart with happy faces washing hands. The chart showed: wet, soap, scrub, rinse, dry.

Sprig’s eyes sparkled. “I can do this! I can make it fun,” he said.

Button hopped closer. “How?”

Sprig smiled. “We’ll make a Bubble Song. Let’s try!”

He dragged the little stool with a squeak-squeak to the sink. He pressed the pump. Squish! A flower smell filled the air. Sprig wet his paws. Warm water tickled his fur. He began to sing in a small, happy voice.

“Wet, soap, scrub-a-dub,
Top and bottom, rub-rub-rub.
Fingers, thumbs, make them shine,
Rinse, rinse, dry—so fine!”

Button clapped her soft paws. Vroom tapped his wheels like a drum. Sprig scrubbed for a long moment, counting. “One, two, three... twenty!” The bubbles felt silky. The water sounded like tiny rain. He rinsed. The soap slid away. He shook his paws. Drip, drip, drip. He dried with a fluffy towel. It felt like a hug.

Sprig looked at his paws. They were clean and shiny. His whiskers perked up. “I did it!” he cheered. His chest felt warm and proud.

Just then, the nursery door creaked. A small child toddled in for quiet play time, bringing a basket of blocks. The child smiled and set them down. Sprig loved block-building. He rushed over with Button and Vroom.

But as they began to stack, a puzzle piece on the rug made a tiny crunch. Sprig looked up. Across the room, two toy bears were arguing.

“I want the blue towel!” growled Cubby Bear.

“I had it first!” squeaked Fifi Bear, clutching the towel near the sink.

Sprig took a breath. He remembered how he felt when he was sticky and upset. He wanted everyone to feel safe and calm. “Let’s solve this together,” he said gently. “We can share and still be clean.”

Cubby frowned. “But I like the blue one.”

Fifi hugged the towel. “It’s soft.”

Sprig pointed to the folded towels. “Look, there are two. The blue towel is soft. The yellow towel is soft too. We can take turns or we can match the towels to our jobs.”

“What jobs?” asked Cubby.

Sprig’s eyes shone. “Hygiene jobs! Blue towel for hand-washers. Yellow towel for face-patters. We can make a Clean Team.”

Fifi’s eyes went round. “A team?”

Sprig nodded. “First, wash. Then, dry. Then, high-five.” He sang again, and this time everyone joined: “Wet, soap, scrub-a-dub...”

Fifi giggled and washed her paws. Cubby washed, making big foamy bubbles. The sink sang with splashy sounds. The room smelled fresh and happy. Fifi dried with the yellow towel, pat-pat-pat. Cubby dried with the blue towel, rub-rub-rub. They both smiled.

“Thank you, Sprig,” said Cubby. “You helped us share.”

“Thank you,” said Fifi. “I feel clean and brave.”

Sprig’s tail fluffed with pride. “Clean means ready,” he said. “Ready for fun, ready to learn, ready to be kind.”

The child clapped. The blocks looked bright and colorful. Sprig stacked them—red, blue, yellow—as the sun made the room glow. Button hopped onto a soft cushion. Vroom parked neatly by the shelf. The nursery felt calm and cozy again.

Before nap time, Sprig checked a mirror. His whiskers were smooth. His paws were tidy. He felt strong inside. “I can take care of me,” he whispered. “I can help my friends.”

Button snuggled beside him. “What did we learn today?” she asked softly.

Sprig smiled. “Clean hands help us play. Clean faces help us feel good. Washing is quick, fun, and smart. And when we share and speak kindly, everyone can sparkle.”

He hummed the Bubble Song one more time. The water made a quiet hush, like the sea. The towels smelled like sunshine. The nursery lights dimmed. Sprig curled his fluffy tail and closed his eyes, proud of his plan, happy with his clean, cozy world.

The End.
