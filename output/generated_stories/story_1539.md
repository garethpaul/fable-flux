# Bubbles the Dolphin Finds a New Way

Bubbles was a playful dolphin with bright eyes and a big, bubbly laugh. One rainy day, Bubbles visited a quiet attic with her friend Mia. Mia was a kind girl who loved to read. The attic smelled like old paper and warm wood. Dust danced in the soft light from a tiny window. The rain made gentle pat-pat sounds on the roof.

“Wow,” Bubbles whispered. “It’s so calm up here. I can hear the rain.”

Mia smiled. “Let’s make a cozy place to play.”

Bubbles clapped her fins. “Let’s do it!”

They looked around. There were boxes, blankets, and a small trunk. The trunk had a tricky lock. Bubbles nudged it with her nose. Click. The lock stayed shut.

“I want to open it,” Bubbles said. “I want to start. I can try!”

“That’s good initiative,” Mia said. “Let’s see what we can do.”

Bubbles took a deep breath. She remembered to care for her body and mind. “First, I stretch,” she said. She wiggled and stretched her fins. “Now I drink water.” She sipped from a little cup. “Now I am ready.”

Bubbles looked closely at the lock. She listened. She felt the cool metal under her fin. “Hmm,” she hummed. “I will try a gentle tap.” Tap, tap. Nothing.

“I will try a soft twist.” Twiiiist. Nothing.

Bubbles did not pout. She blinked and smiled. “It’s okay. I can keep trying.”

Mia knelt beside her. “Maybe there’s a key.”

Bubbles nodded. “Let’s look.” She started at one corner of the attic and moved to the next. She sang a tiny song, “Look and listen, step by step,” as her fins slid over the wooden floor. She peered into a little wooden box. She sniffed a lavender sachet. It smelled soft and sweet. But no key.

Under a folded quilt, Bubbles found a notebook. She opened it carefully. Inside were drawings of a sun, a star, and a moon. “These shapes look friendly,” she said.

Mia pointed to the lock. “Look, Bubbles! The lock has tiny pictures too.”

On the lock was a sun, a star, and a moon. Bubbles’ eyes sparkled. “I can start a new idea!” she said. “Maybe the pictures are a clue.”

Bubbles gently pressed the sun on the lock. Click. She pressed the star. Click. She pressed the moon. Click. Nothing opened. She paused.

“It didn’t work,” she said, then smiled again. “I will be patient. I can try another way.”

Mia took a slow breath. “Let’s think. What if the order is different?”

Bubbles’ tail swished with a soft swish-swish on the floor. “I’m open to new ways,” she said. “I will try moon, sun, star.” Click, click, click. Still closed.

Bubbles closed her eyes for a moment. She listened to the rainy roof. “Patience,” she whispered. “I can take my time.”

She looked at the notebook again. On one page, the sun had a little number one. The star had a little number three. The moon had a little number two.

“Oh!” Bubbles chirped. “Sun is first, moon is second, star is third.”

She pressed sun. Click. She pressed moon. Click. She pressed star. Click. The lock popped open with a happy ping!

“You did it!” Mia cheered.

Bubbles giggled. “We did it because we started trying, we stayed patient, and we kept our minds open.”

They lifted the trunk lid. Inside were soft blankets, a tiny bell, and a calm, round stone. The stone was smooth and cool. Bubbles rubbed it with her fin. “It feels peaceful,” she said.

Mia rang the bell gently. Ding. The sound was bright and soft. “Let’s make a cozy corner,” she said.

They spread the blankets by the window. Bubbles placed the calm stone in the middle. “This will be our wellness stone,” she said. “When we touch it, we breathe slow and feel good.”

They sat together. Bubbles put a blanket over her shoulders. “I feel warm,” she said. “And proud.”

Mia nodded. “You showed initiative. You started, even when it was hard. You tried new ideas. You were patient.”

Bubbles smiled big. “When I see a new problem, I can start with a small try. I can take care of my body and feelings. I can be open to new ways. I can keep going.”

The rain began to fade. Sunlight peeked through the window. Dust sparkles danced like tiny stars.

Bubbles tapped the bell one more time. Ding. “Today, I learned that taking the first step is brave,” she said. “Starting helps us grow.”

Mia hugged Bubbles. “Let’s remember our cozy corner,” she said. “We can come back whenever we need calm, care, and courage.”

Bubbles nodded. “I will use my initiative every day, with patience and an open mind.”

They smiled at the quiet attic, now warm and bright. Their hearts felt light and strong.

The End.
