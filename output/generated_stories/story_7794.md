# Otter Paints the Porch

The sun was warm on the peaceful front porch. The wood boards felt smooth under little otter paws. A playful otter named Ollie wiggled his whiskers and smiled. He loved to splash, he loved to twirl, and today, he loved to make art.

“Ooh! I smell oranges,” Ollie said, sniffing the breeze. The air smelled like sweet flowers and a tiny bit like the orange slices in the kitchen. Birds sang soft songs. The porch swing creaked, creak, creak, like a friendly hello.

On the porch, there was a big piece of paper taped to the floor. There were little jars of safe, bright paint. Red like strawberries. Blue like the sky. Yellow like the sun.

Ollie clapped his paws. “I will paint with my paws! I will dance while I paint!” His eyes sparkled.

He dipped a paw into blue paint. It felt cool and tickly. “Hee hee!” he giggled. He put a blue paw print on the paper. Plop! Then he dipped a paw into yellow. Plop! Then red. Plop, plop!

“Listen, paint,” Ollie whispered. “Let’s make music too.” He tapped his paws, tap-tap, swish-swish, like a tiny drum.

Grandma came to the doorway with a gentle smile. “Hello, my little artist,” she said. “What are you making?”

“I’m painting a dance!” Ollie said. He twirled on his toes. His tail swished. His paws made swirls and dots. “I will make a sunshine swirl!”

Grandma sat on the steps and watched. The porch smelled like fresh wood and lemon soap. The breeze was soft. “I see your sunshine swirl,” Grandma said. “I hear your dance feet. Your art is full of feeling.”

Just then, two friends came by. Pip the squirrel held a tiny brush in her paws. Benny the turtle carried chalk in a small box.

“Hi, Ollie!” Pip said. Her tail flick-flicked. “Can we make art too?”

“Yes!” Ollie cheered. “We can all make art. We can make art in our own ways.” He pointed to the big paper. He pointed to the porch floor. “We have room.”

Pip dipped her brush into green. She made tiny leaves. “I like small lines,” Pip said. “They make me feel calm.” She breathed in, slow and happy.

Benny opened his chalk box. He chose purple. He drew a big round shape on the porch board. “I like slow circles,” Benny said. “They help me think. Round and round.” Scratch, scratch went the chalk. It sounded soft, like a whisper.

Ollie began to hum. He wiggled and danced in place. “I like to move while I paint,” he said. “It helps my art feel alive.” Swish, plop, pat!

Pip made tiny hop-hops with her feet. “I like to hop while I paint,” she said. “Hop, dot, hop!”

Benny swayed, left and right. “I like to sway,” he said, smiling. “Sway and draw. Sway and draw.”

Grandma clapped. “You all make art in different ways,” she said. “Different is special.”

A breeze fluttered the paper. The sun warmed their backs. A bluebird landed on the porch rail and chirped, “Chee-chee!”

Ollie looked at their picture. It had big swirls, tiny leaves, and slow circles. It looked like a garden in the sun. “Our art looks happy,” he said. “I can feel it in my tummy. Warm and happy.”

Pip nodded. “I can hear it too,” she said. “It goes hop-hop-tap.”

Benny smiled. “It smells like lemon and chalk,” he said. “It smells like a new idea.”

Ollie dipped his paw in white paint and made little twinkle dots. “Let’s add a dance,” he said. “A porch dance!”

Grandma turned on soft music from a little radio. The notes floated like bubbles. Ollie twirled. Pip hopped. Benny swayed. Their feet made gentle sounds on the wood: tip, tap, slide. Their hearts felt light.

After the dance, they looked again. The painting seemed to move. The swirls swam like water. The leaves rustled like trees. The circles rolled like clouds.

Pip tilted her head. “I used small lines. That is my style,” she said.

Benny nodded. “I used slow circles. That is my style.”

Ollie grinned. “I used dance steps and big swirls. That is my style.”

Grandma gave them each a clean cloth. “Artists also clean up,” she said kindly. They wiped their paws and claws. They put the lids on the paints. They stacked the chalk.

Ollie touched the paper with a clean paw. “We made art together,” he said. “We used different ways. We listened. We watched. We danced. We learned new skills.”

Pip smiled. “I can try a swirl now,” she said, making one tiny swirl.

Benny chuckled. “I can try a leaf,” he said, drawing one slow leaf.

Ollie tried a slow circle. “Round and round,” he said softly. “I can learn from you. You can learn from me.”

Grandma hung the painting on the porch wall with tape. The edges flapped a little. The colors shined in the sun. “This is porch art,” she said. “It shows your hearts.”

Ollie felt proud. His chest felt warm. “Art is for everyone,” he said. “We are different. That makes our art special.”

Pip and Benny said together, “Different is good!”

Ollie danced one more twirl, small and careful. “We make art. We dance. We learn. We share.”

Grandma nodded. “And you grew your skills today,” she said. “Little by little.”

Ollie hugged his friends. The porch creaked a happy creak. The bluebird sang again.

They all smiled, looking at their porch art. They felt calm, bright, and brave.

The End.
