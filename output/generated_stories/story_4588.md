# Melody in the Seatbelt

Chirp was a small, bright-blue singing bird with a golden beak and a cheerful, trilling voice. Chirp loved to sing about everything. Chirp sang about the morning sun, the crunchy seeds for breakfast, and the cozy nest in a big maple tree.

One shiny day, Chirp was invited to ride with the Feather Family in their family car. Mama Feather opened the car door and smiled. “Welcome, Chirp! We’re happy you’re coming with us to the park.”

The car smelled like clean seats and orange slices. The windows were cool to the touch, and the seat felt soft and bouncy. Chirp fluttered onto a booster seat in the middle. Little Bree, a fluffy gray chick, clapped tiny wings. “Yay! Singing time!”

Chirp puffed up proudly. “Tweet-tru-la! I will sing us all the way there!”

Mama Feather’s voice was kind and calm. “First, we follow our directions. Everyone buckles up.”

Chirp wiggled. “But I sing better when I’m free!” Chirp chirped, flapping just a little. The seatbelt clicked for everyone else. Click for Mama Feather. Click for Papa Feather. Click for Bree. But not for Chirp.

Papa Feather turned the key. Vroom. The engine hummed like a big sleepy bee. The car rolled forward. Sunlight sparkled on the windshield. Chirp began to sing, “La-la-LEAP! La-la-LOOP!”

Suddenly the car stopped at a red light. The stop made a gentle bump. Chirp slid a tiny bit on the seat and felt a jolt. Chirp’s heart went thump-thump. “Oh!” Chirp squeaked.

Mama Feather looked in the mirror. “Chirp, we have rules to keep us safe. The rule is: always wear your seatbelt in the car. Please follow our directions.”

Chirp’s feathers drooped. “But I like to sing.”

“You can still sing,” said Bree, her eyes wide and bright. “I sing with my buckle on. Click!” She tapped her shiny buckle. It felt smooth and warm in her feathers.

Papa Feather added, “Following directions shows we care for ourselves and each other. It’s a healthy habit, like washing our wings and drinking water.”

Chirp took a deep breath. Chirp could smell Bree’s orange slices. Chirp could feel the soft seat and the gentle breeze from the air vent. “Okay,” Chirp said, finding a brave voice. “I will follow the directions.”

“Would you like help?” Mama Feather asked.

Chirp sat up tall. “Yes, please.” Chirp remembered to be assertive and clear. “I need the strap to fit snug.”

Mama Feather smiled. “Thank you for telling me what you need.” She gently pulled the belt across Chirp. Click! It sounded like a tiny drum. Chirp wiggled and felt safe. The belt was snug but comfy.

Chirp tried a test note. “La!” The sound was sweet and bright. Chirp tried a longer song. “La-la, buckle up, safe and snug, warm and cozy like a hug!”

Bree giggled. “That’s my favorite!”

They drove on. The car swished down the road. Trees waved hello. The sky was blue, blue, blue. Chirp sang about the blue sky. Chirp sang about the bump-bump of the wheels on a little bridge. “Bump-bump, thump-thump, we stay safe and we don’t jump!”

Soon, Papa Feather said, “We need to drink water.” He passed a small cup with a sip top to Bree and one to Chirp in the booster holder. Chirp took a sip. Cool water slid down, and Chirp felt fresh and strong.

“Thank you, Papa,” Bree said.

Chirp copied Bree. “Thank you, Papa.” Chirp felt warm inside, like sunlight. Gratitude felt good. Saying thank you was like another song.

Then Papa Feather turned on the blinker. Click-click, click-click. “We will take the next turn,” he said.

Chirp peered out. A big, shiny sign pointed left. Another sign pointed right. Chirp chirped, “Let’s go right! The park might be right!”

Mama Feather said kindly, “The directions say left. We follow the directions, even when we feel excited.”

Chirp’s wings twitched with energy. “I really want to go right,” Chirp said, using a strong, polite voice. “But I will follow the directions.” Papa Feather turned left. The blinker stopped. The road smelled like warm grass. Chirp sat back and felt proud.

Bree smiled. “Good job, Chirp.”

Chirp sang a new verse. “Left or right, we check and see, we follow the signs for you and me.”

The car went over a little hill. Wheee! Chirp giggled. The park came into view—green grass, tall slides, and a blue pond sparkling like jewels.

“We’re here!” Papa Feather cheered.

They parked. Doors opened with soft thunks. Fresh air whooshed in. “First, we unbuckle,” Mama Feather said.

Chirp waited. Then, with careful fingers, Chirp pressed the red button. Click. The belt slipped away. Chirp stretched and fluttered. “I did it in the right order!”

Mama Feather nodded. “You followed directions. That keeps everyone safe.”

Bree handed Chirp a small orange slice. “For you.”

Chirp took a bite. Juicy, sweet, and a little tangy. “Thank you, Bree.” Gratitude sparkled like sunlight on water.

At the playground, Chirp sang a happy, gentle song that other birds and kids could enjoy. “We take turns, we drink our water, we wash our wings, we mind our chatter.” When it was time to leave, Chirp asked, “Is it time to buckle now?”

“Yes,” said Papa Feather. “Let’s do it together.”

Click. Chirp smiled. “Following directions helps me feel safe and strong. I can still sing my song.”

On the ride home, Chirp sang softly, “Buckle up, say thank you too, follow rules that care for you.”

Everyone hummed along, warm and content.

The End.
