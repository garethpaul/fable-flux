# Tilly Turtle’s Balcony Garden Day

Tilly was a shy turtle who lived in a small home with a sunny balcony garden. The balcony had pots of mint that smelled cool and fresh, bright red flowers, and tiny tomato plants that glowed like little suns. When the breeze blew, the leaves made soft shhh sounds, like they were whispering a secret.

Every morning, Tilly peeked over her shell and did her chores. She watered the plants with her tiny blue watering can. Drip, drip, drip. She swept the balcony with a little straw broom. Swish, swish. She picked old leaves and put them in the compost bucket. Plop!

One warm day, Tilly heard the happy buzz of Buzzbee, a fuzzy bee with gold and black stripes. “Hello, Tilly!” Buzzbee hummed, landing on a red flower. “Mmm, this smells sweet.”

“Hi, Buzzbee,” Tilly whispered. She was shy, but she liked Buzzbee. The sun felt warm on her shell, and the flower petals felt soft under her little feet.

Just then, a high chirp came from the railing. Cheep! Cheep! It was Pip, a small sparrow with bright eyes. Pip hopped and looked at the tomatoes. “Are those for me?” Pip asked.

Tilly blinked. “They are still green,” she said softly. “We have to wait.”

Pip didn’t like waiting. He hopped closer. “I’m hungry now,” he chirped. His tummy gave a tiny rumble. Rrmm.

Buzzbee buzzed around the flowers. “There is enough for everyone if we share,” he hummed.

Tilly thought about her chores. She wanted to take care of the garden. She wanted to be kind. But she felt a flutter in her tummy. She was shy, and she didn’t like saying no.

Just then, a rustle sounded in the mint. Rustle, rustle. A bright green caterpillar peeked out. “Hello, I am Cora,” she said. “The mint smells minty! May I have a nibble?”

Tilly’s eyes grew wide. “Oh,” she said. “I want to keep the plants safe. But I also want to be fair.” The wind tickled her nose, and she felt the warm sun again. She took a small breath. In. Out.

Pip hopped to the tomato pot. “Mine first!” he chirped, pecking at a small green tomato.

“No!” Tilly cried. Her voice shook. She hurried over, her little feet tapping on the floor. Tap, tap, tap. “Please stop, Pip. The tomatoes are not ready. If you peck now, we will have none later.”

Pip puffed his feathers. “But I’m hungry,” he said. “I don’t want to wait.”

Buzzbee buzzed by his ear. “We can solve this,” Buzzbee said. “We can use calm words. We can make a plan.”

Tilly nodded. She touched the cool railing with her toe. “Let’s make a plan,” she said. “We can share and take turns. We can do chores together so the garden grows strong.”

Pip tilted his head. “A plan?” he chirped.

“Yes,” Tilly said. “I will water the plants, and Buzzbee will visit the flowers to help make tomatoes. Cora can nibble old mint leaves that I pick for the compost. And you, Pip, can eat the tiny seeds I sweep from the floor. When the tomatoes turn red, we will share them. We will each get a sweet, juicy taste.”

Cora wiggled with joy. “I can wait for the leaves you pick,” she said. “I like old leaves best.”

Buzzbee did a happy loop. “A plan is sweet,” he hummed.

Pip pecked the floor and found a seed. Peck, peck. “I guess I can wait,” he chirped. “I can help you with chores now. I can carry little bits with my beak.”

Tilly felt the flutter in her tummy calm down. She smiled a shy, small smile. “Thank you, Pip,” she said. “Thank you, Buzzbee and Cora.”

They started their chores together. Tilly poured water. Drip, drip. Buzzbee buzzed from flower to flower. Zzz. Cora munched on old mint leaves that Tilly put in a tiny pile. Munch, munch. Pip hopped and picked up dry stems. Pick, pick.

They worked and sang. Tilly sang softly, “Water and sun help plants grow.” Pip chirped, “We wait, we share, we take it slow.” Buzzbee hummed, “Zzz, zzz, flowers to fruit.” Cora sang, “Old leaves taste cute!” They giggled at that.

Days passed. The tomatoes changed from green to yellow to bright red. The balcony smelled sweet and warm. The mint still smelled cool. The red flowers danced in the afternoon light.

On the sharing day, Tilly picked a red tomato. It felt smooth and warm in her hand. She washed it with a tiny splash. Splash! Then she cut it into four little pieces.

“Thank you for waiting and helping,” Tilly said. “Thank you for using calm words when we had a problem. We made a plan. We did our chores. We were kind.”

Pip took a piece. “Mmm! Juicy!” he chirped.

Buzzbee tasted the tomato juice on a petal. “Sweet,” he hummed.

Cora nibbled a tiny bit. “Yum,” she said.

Tilly tasted her piece. It was bright and soft and full of sun. She felt proud. She felt brave. She felt accepted and happy with her friends.

Pip fluffed his feathers. “I’m glad we waited,” he said. “I learned to listen.”

Cora nodded. “I learned to help with chores.”

Buzzbee buzzed, “I learned that calm words and plans fix problems.”

Tilly tucked into her shell and peeked out with a smile. “I learned that being shy is okay,” she said. “I can still speak up. I can share. I can make a plan.”

They sat together in the warm light, listening to the leaves whisper shhh, and the city sounds far below, beep-beep and clink-clink. The balcony garden felt cozy, green, and full of friends.

The End.
