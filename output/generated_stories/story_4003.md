# Joey's Big Heart Day

The morning sun streamed through the big window. Little Joey the kangaroo bounced into the living room. His fluffy tail wiggled with excitement. The soft carpet felt warm under his tiny paws.

"Good morning, world!" Joey chirped. He loved starting each day in the cozy living room. Colorful cushions were scattered everywhere. A big toy box sat in the corner, overflowing with treasures.

Joey's best friend Benny the bunny was already there. Benny was building a tall tower with wooden blocks. Red ones, blue ones, and yellow ones stacked high.

"Look at my castle!" Benny said proudly. His whiskers twitched with joy.

Joey bounced closer to see. His big feet went thump-thump on the carpet. But oh no! His bouncing made the whole room shake. The beautiful tower wobbled and crashed down. Blocks scattered everywhere with loud clattering sounds.

Benny's ears drooped down. His eyes filled with tears. "My castle is broken!" he cried. "You ruined it, Joey!"

Joey's heart felt heavy. He saw how sad his friend looked. "I'm sorry, Benny," Joey said softly. "I didn't mean to knock it down."

But Benny turned away. He crossed his arms and wouldn't look at Joey. "Go away! I don't want to play with you anymore!"

Joey felt tears in his own eyes. He sat down on a fluffy purple cushion. The living room felt quiet and sad now. What could he do to make things better?

Then Joey had an idea. He hopped to the toy box and pulled out all the blocks. There were so many! Round ones, square ones, triangle ones too. He started building something special.

"What are you doing?" Benny asked, peeking over.

"I'm making something for you," Joey said gently. "To say sorry and to help."

Joey carefully stacked the blocks. He made walls and towers, doors and windows. Soon, a magnificent castle appeared. It was even bigger than Benny's first one!

"Wow!" Benny's eyes grew wide. "It's beautiful!"

"Want to build it together?" Joey asked hopefully. "We could make it even more amazing!"

Benny's face lit up like sunshine. "Yes! I forgive you, Joey. You were trying to help make it better."

The two friends worked side by side. Benny added a bridge. Joey made a tall flag tower. They laughed and shared ideas. The living room filled with happy sounds again.

"You know what?" Benny said, placing the last block. "This castle is much better than my first one. When we work together, we can build anything!"

Joey's heart felt warm and full. "I learned something important today," he said. "When I make a mistake, I can say sorry and try to help fix it."

"And I learned that forgiving friends feels good," Benny added. "Plus, everything is more fun when we help each other!"

They stood back and admired their beautiful castle. It sparkled in the sunlight from the window. The living room felt cozy and peaceful again.

From that day on, whenever there was a problem, Joey and Benny remembered to use peaceful solutions. They would talk kindly, say sorry when needed, and always try to help each other.

The End.
