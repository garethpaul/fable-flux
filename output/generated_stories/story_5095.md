# Miko’s Tidy Art Adventure

Miko the kind mouse lived in a school’s creative art room. He had soft gray fur, bright whiskers, and a tiny apron with paint dots. Every morning he woke to the smell of glue, paper, and fresh crayons. Today, the sun made warm squares on the floor. The room felt busy and bright.

“Good morning, brushes,” Miko squeaked. He patted a fluffy paintbrush. “Good morning, paper. Good morning, water cups.” The cups clinked lightly, like tiny bells.

Miko loved words. He learned new words from the posters on the wall. One poster said, “Reuse. Recycle. Respect.” Miko tried the words out loud. “Re-use,” he said slowly. “Re-cy-cle. Re-spect.” They felt bouncy on his tongue, like a friendly sound puzzle.

But today, the art room was very messy. Paper scraps curled like little snowflakes on the floor. Dried glue dotted the tables like sticky stars. Paint lids were off, and colors were getting sleepy and dry.

“Oh dear,” whispered Miko. “The room needs care. This is our place. We can help!”

A crayon rolled across the table and fell with a plink. Miko caught it with his tiny paws. “I can do my chores,” he said. “Chores help everyone.”

He took a deep breath. He made a plan with simple steps.
“Step one: Sort. Step two: Save. Step three: Shine!”

He sang his plan in a little tune. “Sort, save, shine! Sort, save, shine!” His voice was soft and cheerful.

Miko pushed a small bin closer. “Paper scraps to the recycle bin,” he said. “Re-cy-cle!” He liked saying the word. It felt strong. He picked up big blue pieces, tiny pink bits, and sparkly stars. Rustle, rustle, rustle. The bin made a happy sound when he dropped them in.

Next, Miko found paint with open lids. “We will save your color,” he told them kindly. He snapped the lids on. Click! Click! Click! “Save means we do not waste,” he said. “We take care, we use again.” He liked the feel of the lids clicking snug.

He wiped the table with a damp cloth. Swish, swish. The cloth smelled clean, like lemon. “Shine,” Miko smiled. “You look bright now.”

On a shelf, he discovered a broken crayon. He felt a little droop in his whiskers. “This crayon is in two pieces,” he said. “Oh no.” He paused, then put his paw on his heart. “I can try. I can learn.” He took washi tape with tiny polka dots and softly wrapped the two parts together. “Grow with every try,” he whispered. “That is a growth mindset.” He tried the new word slowly: “Mind-set.” It felt like a brave hat on his head.

The tape held. The crayon drew a smooth, shiny line on paper. “Yes!” Miko squeaked. He hopped in a little circle. “When it is hard, we can keep trying. Try, try, try!”

At the sink, he found cloudy water cups. The water looked tired. “We will not waste,” said Miko. He poured the gray water into a plant pot by the window. The plant’s leaves were long and green. “Sip, sip,” Miko whispered. “Con-ser-va-tion.” He tried the long word with care. “That means we save water and use it well.”

He filled the cups with fresh water just a little halfway. “Only what we need,” he said proudly. He lined them up like tiny glass soldiers. Clink, clink.

“Hello?” came a voice at the door. It was Lila, the art teacher. She stepped in and looked around. Her eyes grew wide. “Oh my! The room looks wonderful!”

Miko’s ears warmed. “I did my chores,” he said softly. “I sorted, I saved, and I shined. I also learned new words. Recycle. Conservation. Mindset.”

Lila knelt so her face was close to Miko’s. “Those are strong words,” she said kindly. “You used your words and your heart. You took care of our room. That is respect.”

Miko felt big inside, even though he was small. “Chores help me help others,” he said. He pointed to the tidy table. “Now it is ready for art!”

Soon, children came in with happy feet and bright voices. “Look! It’s so clean!” they said. The brushes looked fluffy again. The crayons were easy to find. The whole room smelled like paper and lemons and ideas.

“Thank you, Miko!” the children cheered.

Miko smiled. “Let’s make art and use our words,” he said. He showed them the poster. “Recycle means paper scraps go here,” he pointed. “Conservation means save water and close lids. Mindset means we can grow when it feels hard.”

A little boy frowned at a tricky pair of scissors. “I can’t,” he whispered.

“You can,” said Miko gently. “Try slowly. Snip, snip. Grow with every try.”

The boy breathed in, snip, snip—then grinned. “I did it!”

Lila clapped softly. “Chores each day keep our room happy,” she said. “We all can help.”

Miko sang his song again, and everyone joined in. “Sort, save, shine! Sort, save, shine!”

The art room glowed with color and care. Miko curled his tail in joy. He had done his chores, saved water and supplies, learned new words, and helped friends try again.

The End.
