# Oliver Owl's Big Mistake

In a sunny backyard filled with colorful flowers and buzzing bees, there lived a wise old owl named Oliver. Oliver had soft brown feathers, big round eyes, and a gentle voice that all the garden animals loved to hear.

One bright morning, Oliver was feeling very grumpy. He hadn't slept well because the wind had blown all night. "Hoot, hoot!" he grumbled from his favorite oak tree. "I'm so tired and cranky!"

Down below, a little rabbit named Ruby was picking berries for her breakfast. She looked up and waved. "Good morning, Oliver! What a beautiful sunny day!"

But Oliver was too grumpy to be kind. "Go away, Ruby!" he hooted loudly. "Can't you see I'm trying to rest? Your berry-picking is too noisy!"

Ruby's ears drooped down. Her feelings were hurt. She quietly hopped away to the far corner of the garden, feeling sad and confused.

Oliver watched Ruby leave, and suddenly his tummy felt funny – not hungry funny, but worried funny. He realized he had been mean to his friend when she was just being cheerful.

"Oh my," Oliver said to himself, ruffling his feathers. "That wasn't very nice of me. Ruby was just saying good morning, and I was rude because I felt grumpy."

Oliver thought about what his grandmother owl had taught him: "When we make mistakes, we can always make things better by saying sorry and showing we care."

He spread his wings and flew down to where Ruby sat alone under a daisy. She looked very sad, with her little nose twitching.

"Ruby," Oliver said softly, landing nearby. "I need to talk to you."

Ruby looked up with her big brown eyes. "Did I do something wrong, Oliver?"

"Oh no, dear friend," Oliver hooted gently. "I did something wrong. I was grumpy because I was tired, but that's not a good reason to be mean to you. I'm very sorry I hurt your feelings."

Ruby's ears perked up a little. "You were just tired?"

"Yes, but being tired doesn't make it okay to be unkind," Oliver explained. "I should have said, 'Good morning, Ruby! I'm feeling grumpy today, but that's not your fault.' Instead, I was mean, and that was wrong."

Oliver had an idea. "Would you like me to help you pick berries? I know where the sweetest ones grow!"

Ruby's face brightened like sunshine. "Really? You'd help me?"

"Of course!" Oliver smiled. "Friends help each other, and I want to show you how sorry I am."

Together, they picked the juiciest red berries. Oliver used his sharp eyes to spot the best ones up high, while Ruby gathered the low ones in her little paws. They laughed and chatted, enjoying the warm sunshine on their backs.

"Thank you for saying sorry, Oliver," Ruby said, munching a sweet berry. "It made me feel much better."

"Thank you for forgiving me," Oliver replied. "I learned that when I make a mistake, I can always try to fix it. Saying sorry and doing something kind helps heal hurt feelings."

As they shared their berry breakfast under the sunny sky, Oliver felt much better – not just because he wasn't grumpy anymore, but because he had made things right with his friend.

From that day on, whenever Oliver felt grumpy, he remembered to be kind anyway, and if he did make a mistake, he always made amends.

The End.
