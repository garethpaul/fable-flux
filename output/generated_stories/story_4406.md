# Benny Beaver's Big Building Day

Benny Beaver lived in the coziest bedroom you ever did see. His bed was shaped like a log, and his walls were covered with pictures of dams and rivers. This morning, Benny woke up with the biggest, most exciting idea ever!

"Today I'm going to build the most amazing stick fort right here in my room!" Benny announced, stretching his little paws up high.

Benny gathered all his building sticks from his toy box. They were smooth and brown, just perfect for building. He started stacking them up, one by one, trying to make a tall tower.

*Crash!* Down tumbled all the sticks.

"Oh no!" Benny said, his little tail slapping the soft carpet. He felt his cheeks getting warm and his heart beating faster. "This is so hard!"

Benny tried again. He carefully placed stick after stick, building slowly. But when he reached for the very top stick... *Crash!* Down they all fell again.

"GRRR!" Benny growled, stomping his little feet. His cozy bedroom suddenly felt too small, and his favorite building sticks looked all wrong scattered on the floor. "Why won't this work? I'm the best builder ever!"

Just then, Mama Beaver peeked her head through the doorway. She could see Benny's frustrated face and the pile of sticks on the floor.

"Oh my," she said gently, sitting down on the soft rug beside him. "It looks like you're having some big feelings, little builder."

"I can't make my fort stay up!" Benny said, his voice wobbly. "Every time I try, it falls down!"

Mama Beaver nodded. "You know what? Even the best builders feel frustrated sometimes. When I feel that way, I take three deep breaths. Want to try with me?"

Together, they breathed in slowly through their noses, smelling the fresh morning air coming through the window. "One... two... three," Mama counted softly.

Benny felt his shoulders relax. "That feels better," he said, surprised.

"Now," said Mama Beaver, "what if we think of a different way to build? Maybe your fort doesn't need to be tall. What else could it be?"

Benny looked at his sticks with fresh eyes. "Maybe... maybe I could make it wide instead of tall?"

"What a smart idea!" Mama Beaver smiled. "And what if I help you? Two builders might be better than one."

Working together, they spread the sticks out on the carpet, making a big square fort. Benny's little sister Bella came in and asked, "Can I help too? I'm good at making patterns!"

"Yes!" said Benny. "You can make the walls pretty!"

Bella arranged the sticks in beautiful zigzag patterns while Mama helped hold everything steady. Soon, they had the most wonderful wide fort with patterned walls.

"We did it!" Benny cheered, crawling inside his cozy stick fort. The afternoon sunlight streamed through his bedroom window, making the brown sticks glow golden.

"You know what, Benny?" Mama Beaver said. "When you felt frustrated, you didn't give up. You took deep breaths, tried a new way, and asked for help. That's what really good builders do."

Benny beamed with pride. "And now our fort is even better because we all worked together!"

That evening, as Benny snuggled in his log-shaped bed, he looked at his beautiful wide fort and smiled. He had learned that feeling frustrated was okay, but taking deep breaths and asking for help made everything better.

The End.
