# Beat by Beat, Busy Beaver

In a playful living room, a busy beaver named Bixie wiggled her flat tail. The rug was soft under her paws. Sunlight made warm squares on the floor. Toys made a little mountain on one side. A small speaker sat on a shelf.

“I have a big plan!” Bixie said. “I will build a pillow fort and dance to music. I can do both so fast because I am very, very busy!”

She stacked two big cushions. They slid. Plop! She turned on the music. Boom-chicka. Boom-chicka. The beat made the windows hum. Bixie hopped. She twirled. She grabbed another pillow. The pillow bumped her nose. Boop!

“Ouch. I can do it,” she said. “I am strong and fast!”

She tried again. She put a cushion here, a pillow there, then spun in a circle. Spin! The fort tilted. The music got louder. Boom-chicka. Boom-chicka. Bixie wiggled her tail in time with the beat. The cushions fell. Flump!

Bixie’s shoulders drooped. “Why is this so hard?” she whispered. The room smelled like clean cotton from the pillows. Her paws tingled from hopping.

Just then, the door squeaked. Bixie’s friend, Mimi the mouse, peeped in. Mimi wore a tiny blue ribbon. “Hi, Bixie! Your music sounds fun. Are you dancing?”

“I am building and dancing,” Bixie said. “But my fort keeps falling. I want to do everything at once. I want to be the best!”

Mimi nodded. “I hear that. It feels good to be busy. But I want to understand. What do you want most right now? A tall fort or happy dancing?”

Bixie blinked. “Um… I want both.”

“That makes sense,” said Mimi. “Maybe your busy and your plan are fighting. Let’s listen to your fort and your feet. What are they telling us?”

Bixie put her paw on a cushion. It was squishy. “The fort says, ‘One at a time, please.’”

She tapped her paw to the beat. Tap, tap. “My feet say, ‘Dance now!’”

Mimi smiled. “I understand both. Maybe we can make a strategy. Strategy is a plan we can follow, step by step.”

Bixie’s ears perked up. “A step-by-step plan?”

“Yes,” said Mimi. “First, we warm up our bodies. Then, we build. Then, we dance inside the fort!”

Bixie took a big breath. In. Out. The air tasted like sweet apples from a candle. “Okay. Step one: warm up.”

Mimi clapped softly. Clap, clap. “Let’s do three moves with the music. Ready?”

Boom-chicka. Boom-chicka.

“Reach up high like a tree,” Mimi said.

Bixie reached. She felt the stretch in her back. “I can feel my muscles wake up!”

“Touch the floor like a frog,” Mimi said.

Bixie bent her knees. Thump. Her tail brushed the rug. It felt tickly.

“Step side to side,” Mimi said.

Bixie stepped left. Step. She stepped right. Step. She smiled. “This exercise feels good. My body feels calm now.”

Mimi nodded. “Great. Step two: building time. Let’s turn the music down a little.”

Bixie clicked the button. The beat became soft. Boom… chicka… boom… chicka…

“Now we build slow,” Mimi said. “We count and stack.”

They placed one big cushion flat. “One,” Bixie said.

They leaned two pillows on the sides. “Two.”

They added a blanket roof. “Three.”

Bixie pressed the corners with her paws. Squish. The fort stood tall and cozy. Light shone through the blanket and made pretty shapes. Bixie’s heart felt warm. “We did it!”

Mimi beamed. “Step three: dance! But one more tiny rule. We dance around the fort, not on the fort.”

Bixie laughed. “Good rule.”

She turned the music up a little. Boom-chicka. Boom-chicka. She and Mimi shook their hips. Shimmy-shimmy. They clapped. Clap, clap. Bixie did a gentle tail wiggle. Wiggle-wiggle. The fort stayed strong.

Bixie sang, “Beat by beat, step by step, I can learn, and I can prep!”

Mimi sang too, her voice like a tiny bell. They spun with careful toes. The rug felt warm. The room smelled like popcorn now because their dance made them think of snack time.

Bixie peeked into the fort. It was soft and quiet inside. She crawled in and took a slow breath. “I feel proud,” she said. “I understand my body and my plan. When I tried to do everything at once, I felt mixed-up. When I listened and used a strategy, I felt strong.”

Mimi nodded. “You used understanding. You understood the fort needed slow hands. You understood your feet needed warm-up and dance time. You used exercise to make your body ready. You used a step-by-step plan.”

Bixie’s eyes shone. “That helps my self-esteem. I believe I can learn new things.”

They bumped paws. Tap.

“Snack?” Mimi asked.

“Yes!” Bixie said. They ate crunchy apple slices. Crunch, crunch. They giggled. The music hummed soft.

Bixie leaned on the fort wall. “Next time, I will ask, ‘What do I need? What does my plan need?’ Then I will go step by step. That is my new strategy.”

Mimi smiled. “That is understanding.”

Bixie nodded. “And it feels good in my heart.”

The End.
