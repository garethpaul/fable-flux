# Kody the Koala Learns to Play Fair

In a cozy study corner filled with soft cushions and colorful books, lived a calm koala named Kody. His gray fur was fluffy like a cloud, and his round black nose twitched whenever he was thinking hard. Today, Kody was looking at his favorite picture book about different sports.

"Look at all these fun games!" Kody said to himself, pointing at pictures of children playing soccer, basketball, and tennis. His dark eyes sparkled with excitement. "I want to try playing sports too!"

Kody's imagination began to whir like a gentle breeze. He closed his eyes and pretended his study corner was a big playground. The soft reading cushions became soccer balls, and his wooden bookends turned into goal posts.

"I'm the best soccer player ever!" Kody declared, gently kicking a cushion across the carpeted floor. The cushion made a soft *whoosh* sound as it slid. "Goal! I win! I always win!"

Just then, his friend Penny the Penguin waddled into the study corner. Her black and white feathers gleamed under the warm reading lamp.

"Hi Kody! Can I play too?" Penny asked with a cheerful chirp.

"Okay, but I'm much better than you," Kody said, puffing out his chest. "I never lose at sports!"

They began to play their imaginary soccer game. When Penny kicked the cushion toward the goal, Kody quickly moved the bookend goalpost. "No goal!" he announced.

When it was Kody's turn, he kicked the cushion and immediately shouted, "Goal! I scored again!"

Penny's orange beak drooped sadly. "That's not fair, Kody. You moved the goal when I was about to score."

"I don't care," Kody said, his voice getting louder. "I want to win!"

Penny's eyes filled with tears that sparkled like tiny diamonds. "Playing with you isn't fun anymore," she whispered. With a sad little waddle, she left the study corner.

Kody sat alone among his cushions and books. The study corner felt bigger and quieter now. He could hear the soft tick-tock of the wall clock and smell the familiar scent of his favorite books. But something felt wrong in his tummy - like butterflies were doing somersaults.

He picked up his sports picture book again and looked carefully at the photos. In every picture, the children were smiling and laughing together. Even when someone lost, they were shaking hands and hugging.

"Oh no," Kody said softly, his ears drooping. "I wasn't playing fair. I was being mean to Penny."

Kody's imagination helped him think of a solution. He pictured himself saying sorry and playing nicely. He gathered up the soft cushions and arranged them in a perfect circle.

He waddled quickly to find Penny, who was sitting sadly by the window.

"Penny, I'm really sorry," Kody said, his voice gentle and warm. "I was being unfair and mean. Playing sports should be fun for everyone, not just me."

Penny looked up with hopeful eyes. "Do you really mean it?"

"Yes! Come back and play with me. This time, we'll take turns and play by the same rules. And if you score a goal, I'll cheer for you!"

Penny's face lit up like sunshine. "That sounds wonderful!"

Back in the study corner, they played their imaginary sports games together. They took turns being the goalkeeper, they celebrated each other's goals, and they laughed until their bellies hurt. The cushions flew gently through the air, and their giggles filled the cozy space with joy.

"You know what?" Kody said, giving Penny a warm hug. "Playing fair makes sports much more fun!"

"And being kind makes the best games of all," Penny agreed.

From that day on, Kody remembered that the most important part of any sport wasn't winning - it was playing together, being fair, and having fun with friends.

The End.
