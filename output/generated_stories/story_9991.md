# Polly Plans the Lab

Polly was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a splash of sunny yellow on her tail. She loved to say, “Squawk! Idea time!” and flap her wings when thoughts popped into her head.

Polly lived with her friend Sam in a cozy house. Down the stairs, there was a basement lab. The lab smelled like warm cocoa and clean soap. It felt cool and quiet. There were jars of buttons, boxes of beads, and stacks of paper. There were paintbrushes, glue sticks, and tiny bells that jingled when Polly walked by.

One morning, Polly had a big idea. “Squawk! Let’s make a rainbow robot!” she said.

Sam smiled. “Great idea, Polly! Where should we start?”

Polly looked around. The lab was messy. Paper was on the floor. Crayons rolled under the chair. Jars were open. Buttons spilled like little colorful stars. The sink was full of sticky cups.

Polly flapped. “We start with… um… where is the tape?”

Sam bent down. “Not here. Maybe in this box?”

They opened a box. Clatter! Marbles rolled out.

Polly looked worried. Her tummy felt twisty. “I can’t find the tape,” she said softly.

Sam nodded. “It’s hard to build when we can’t find our tools.”

Polly’s feathers drooped. “I had so many ideas. Now I feel small.”

Sam sat next to her. “It’s okay to feel that way. We can fix this.”

Polly took a deep breath. She smelled orange paint and dusty cardboard. She listened to the little hum of the basement light. She looked at the mess. Then she made a decision.

“Sam,” she said, “I think we need to get organized.”

Sam smiled. “That’s a smart plan.”

Polly puffed up. She felt brave. “Squawk! Organize first, build later.”

They made a simple plan.

Polly said, “Step one: sort.”

Sam said, “Step two: label.”

Together they said, “Step three: put things back.”

They started sorting. Polly picked up brushes. “Brushes here,” she chirped, placing them in a clean jar. Swish, swish—the bristles tickled her beak.

Sam gathered crayons. “Crayons in this box,” he said. The crayons made a soft thunk as they landed.

Polly scooped up buttons. “Buttons in the button jar.” Click, click, click went the glass lid.

Sam wiped the table. Wipe, wipe. The wood felt smooth.

They found the tape under a paper pile. “Tape!” Polly cheered. “Hello, tape!”

Polly was honest with herself. “I left the tape on the table and forgot,” she said. “Next time, I will put it back.”

Sam nodded. “That’s a good choice. We can help each other remember.”

Polly drew simple labels with big pictures. A brush picture for the brush jar. A crayon picture for the crayon box. A button picture for the button jar. She taped the labels on. Sticky, sticky, stick!

“Step three,” Polly chirped. “Put things back.” She practiced. She took a brush and put it in the brush jar. Plop. She took a button and put it in the button jar. Tink. It felt good.

“Good job,” Sam said. “You made a smart system.”

Polly’s chest felt warm. “I can do it,” she said. “I’m a good organizer.” Her self-esteem grew like a little light inside her.

Now it was time to build the rainbow robot.

Polly and Sam gathered what they needed. It was easy now.

“Glue?” asked Sam.

“In the glue bin,” said Polly.

“Red paper?”

“In the red folder,” said Polly.

“Bells?”

“In the music box,” said Polly. Jingle, jingle!

They worked together. Polly cut paper—snip, snip. Sam held the robot’s box body. They stuck on rainbow strips—red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple. They added bell hands that jingled. They made button eyes that clicked. They used tape, and then put it back in the tape spot. Polly smiled each time.

Then, a choice came. Polly found glitter. She loved glitter!

“Squawk! Glitter!” she sang. “Can we use it?”

Sam looked at the clean table. “Glitter can be messy. What should we decide?”

Polly thought. She touched the glitter bottle. It felt cool and smooth. She looked at the labels and the tidy jars. She wanted to keep the lab nice.

“I have an idea,” Polly said. “We can use a little glitter on a tray. Then we will put the glitter back.”

“That’s a good decision,” said Sam.

They shook a tiny bit—shhh—like soft rain. The robot sparkled. Polly put the glitter bottle back on the “Sparkle Shelf.” She felt proud.

At last, the rainbow robot was done. “Hello!” Polly said in a robot voice. “Beep-beep!”

Sam laughed. “It looks amazing!”

Polly flapped her wings with joy. “We did it! We worked together. We told the truth about the mess. We made a plan. We stayed organized.”

Sam nodded. “And you believed in yourself.”

Polly hugged Sam with her wings. The lab felt calm and happy. The air smelled like clean paper. The table shone. The jars sat in neat rows, ready for next time.

Polly looked at the labels and smiled. “Organization helps me find things. It helps me make good choices. It helps me feel strong.”

She tapped the robot’s bell hand. Jingle! “Squawk! Idea time,” she said. “Next time, we’ll build a rocket. And we’ll put things back!”

The End.
