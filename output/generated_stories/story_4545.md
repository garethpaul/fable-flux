# Milo Mouse and the Wonderful Waste-Not Plan

Milo Mouse lived in a cozy little hole behind the big dining room cabinet. Every morning, he would peek out with his bright black eyes and twitch his tiny pink nose. The dining room smelled like warm pancakes and fresh orange juice.

But today, something made Milo's whiskers droop sadly. Scattered all around the shiny wooden floor were scraps of food, crumpled napkins, and empty juice boxes. The beautiful dining room looked messy and wasteful.

"Oh my!" squeaked Milo, stepping carefully around a half-eaten apple. "This isn't right. All this good food is going to waste, and the room is so untidy."

Milo's little heart felt heavy. He knew it wasn't fair to waste food when so many creatures were hungry. His grandmother had always taught him, "Waste not, want not, little one."

Taking a deep breath, Milo decided to make things right. First, he needed to calm his worried feelings. He sat quietly, closed his eyes, and counted to ten slowly. "One... two... three..." This always helped him think clearly.

"I know what to do!" Milo announced to himself, feeling much better.

He scurried to his hole and returned with a tiny wagon he had built from matchsticks and bottle caps. The wheels went "click-click-click" as he pulled it across the smooth floor.

Carefully, Milo began collecting the food scraps. "This apple piece can go to the compost pile in the garden," he said, loading it into his wagon. "The birds will love these breadcrumbs!" He gathered every crumb with his tiny paws.

Next, Milo folded the napkins neatly. "These can be washed and used again," he said proudly. The juice boxes went into a special pile. "These can be recycled into new things!"

As Milo worked, he sang a happy little song: "Reduce, reuse, recycle too! That's what helpful mice can do!"

Just then, the family's little girl, Emma, walked into the dining room. She stopped and stared at the tidy piles Milo had made.

"Mommy!" Emma called. "Look! Someone organized all the messy things!"

Milo hid behind his wagon, his heart beating fast like a tiny drum.

Emma's mother came in and smiled. "What a wonderful reminder, Emma. We should always clean up after ourselves and not waste food. Let's put these scraps in the compost bin and recycle these boxes together."

"Can we make it a daily chore?" asked Emma excitedly. "I want to help take care of our Earth!"

"That's a perfect idea," said her mother. "Every day after meals, we'll clean up and sort everything properly."

Milo's whiskers twitched with joy as he watched Emma and her mother work together. They put the food scraps in the compost bin, the recyclables in the proper container, and the napkins in the washing basket.

From that day forward, the dining room stayed clean and tidy. Emma made it her special job to sort everything after meals. Sometimes, Milo would peek out and see her carefully separating items, just like he had shown her.

Late at night, when the house was quiet, Milo would sweep up any tiny crumbs with a little broom he had built from twigs. He felt proud knowing he had helped make things fair and right.

"Taking care of our world is everyone's job," Milo whispered happily. "Even the smallest mouse can make the biggest difference!"

The End.
