# Mango the Brave Helper

In a warm kitchen, where the air smelled like cinnamon toast and banana bread, lived a courageous monkey named Mango. The sun shone through the window, painting golden squares on the floor. Pots hummed, water bubbled, and a tiny fan went whirr-whirr. Mango’s tail curled with excitement. Today was chore day!

Mango clapped. “I can help! I am brave,” he said.

Mama Bear stirred a pot. “Thank you, Mango,” she said. “We all help in our own way.”

At the big table, friends gathered. There was Tia the tiger with soft stripes, Noor the rabbit with long ears, and Lolo the parrot with bright feathers that shone like rainbows. Everyone looked and sounded different, and Mango liked that. It made the kitchen feel like a friendly festival.

Mango picked up a wooden spoon. “I will stir the soup,” he said.

“Gracias,” said Tia, smiling. “That means thank you.”

Mango blinked. “Gra-see-as,” he tried. The word felt like a hop on his tongue. He giggled. Lolo squawked happily. “Muy bien!” Lolo cheered.

The soup bubbled. Plip-plop. Mango sniffed. “Mmm. Smells like carrots and hugs,” he said. He stood on a small stool to reach the pot.

Noor set plates on the table. Tap, tap, tap. “I can count,” Noor said. “One, two, three, four!”

Mango smiled. “I can count too! But I do chores first,” he said. He wiped the counter with a warm, soapy cloth. Swish, swish. Bubbles popped—pop, pop, pop!

Suddenly, Lolo flapped and cried, “Oh no! The flour bag tipped!”

Whoosh! White flour puffed into the air like a snowy cloud. It covered the floor, the stools, and Mango’s nose. He sneezed. Ah-choo! “Oh bananas,” Mango said, rubbing his nose. The kitchen went quiet.

Tia looked worried. “How do we clean this big mess?” she asked.

Noor’s ears drooped. “It is too much,” she whispered.

The warm kitchen didn’t feel warm anymore. It felt shaky and unsure. Mango took a deep breath. He put a hand on his chest and felt his brave heart beat—thump, thump. “We can do it,” he said softly. “We can help together. I am brave, and you are brave too.”

Mango looked around. “We need a plan,” he said. “We speak in our ways, but we help in one team.”

He pointed to the broom. “I broom,” he said. He pointed to the cloth. “Noor, you wipe.” He pointed to the small vacuum. “Tia, you zip-zip the flour. Lolo, you tell us ‘go!’ in many words.”

Lolo bobbed his head. “Go! ¡Vamos! Allons-y! Let’s go!” he sang.

They began. Swish went Mango’s broom. Wipe, wipe went Noor’s cloth. Zip-zip hummed Tia’s vacuum. Lolo called, “Good job! ¡Buen trabajo! Bravo!”

Mango slipped a little on the flour, but he caught himself with his tail. “I can do hard things,” he said. He swept in small circles. “Small steps,” he told himself. “Small steps make big clean.”

He listened to Lolo’s words. “¡Vamos!” sounded bouncy. “Allons-y!” felt smooth like a slide. Mango smiled. “New words are like new friends,” he said. “They help.”

As they worked, Mama Bear tasted the soup. “Mmm,” she said. “It is almost ready. You are excellent helpers.”

Mango’s cheeks felt warm with pride. He could feel the heat from the oven and the cool tickle of flour on his fur. He smelled sweet bread and heard the soft clink of plates. The kitchen felt friendly again.

They finished the floor. It shone like a clean pond. Mango hopped down from the stool. “We did it!” he cheered.

Tia lifted a paw. “High paw!”

Mango tapped it. “High tail!” He swished his tail and everyone laughed.

Noor counted the clean plates again. “One, two, three, four!” she said. “We are ready to eat.”

Lolo spread his wings. “Thank you! Merci! Gracias!” he sang.

Mango tried the words too. “Thank you. Merci. Gracias,” he said. “So many words for one happy thing!”

They sat at the table. Mama Bear ladled soup into bowls. Steam rose in curly lines. Mango blew gently—whoooo—so he wouldn’t burn his tongue. He tasted a spoonful. “Yum,” he said. “Warm and brave.”

Mama Bear looked around. “You worked together. You used kind words. You listened to each other. That is the best kind of brave.”

Mango nodded. “We are all different,” he said. “Different fur, different feathers, different words. But we can help together.”

Tia purred. “Diversity makes us strong.”

Noor smiled. “Chores are easier with friends.”

Lolo chirped. “Languages are fun!”

Mango took a deep breath and felt peaceful. The kitchen was warm and happy. He patted his broom. “Thank you, broom,” he said. “You helped too.”

He turned to his friends. “When something goes wrong, we make a plan. We try new words. We help each other. We are brave together.”

Everyone clapped. Clap, clap, clap!

And that night, as the dishes dried and the fan went whirr-whirr, Mango whispered, “Gracias. Merci. Thank you.” He curled his tail and smiled.

The End.
