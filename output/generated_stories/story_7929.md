# Sunny’s Porch Paints the World

On a peaceful front porch, a little squirrel named Sunny liked to plan. Sunny’s tail was fluffy, her eyes were bright, and her heart was big. She kept a tiny backpack by the rocking chair. Inside were acorns, a rolled-up map, shiny bottle caps, and a bundle of colored chalks.

“Today, I will make art,” Sunny chirped, tapping the wooden porch rail. Tap-tap. “Art that shows we all share one big world.”

The porch smelled like warm wood and sweet flowers. A soft breeze tickled Sunny’s whiskers. Birds sang, “Chee-chee!” The rocking chair creaked, groan-squeak, like a friendly hello.

Sunny spread out her chalk. She unrolled her map. “I prepared everything,” she said proudly. “I have a plan.”

Just then, friends arrived. A blue jay named Jaya fluttered down. “Good morning, Sunny!” Jaya called. A tiny mouse named Mali peeked from under the doormat. “Hi, hi!” he squeaked. A spotted ladybug, Dot, landed on the porch rail. “Buzz-hello!”

Sunny clapped her paws. “Friends, I want to draw a big picture. A picture of our Earth. I want to show that we are all neighbors, even if we live far away.”

“I like that!” Jaya chirped. “But what colors do we use?”

“What if we don’t have the right chalk?” Mali worried, wringing his little paws.

Dot tapped her tiny feet. “And what if some friends want different colors?”

Sunny took a deep breath. She felt the breeze. She listened to the birds. She touched the smooth chalk. “We can solve this,” she said calmly. “We will plan. We will share. We will listen.”

Sunny rolled out the map. The paper crackled. “Look,” she said, pointing with her paw. “This is the world. Land here. Water there. Many places. Many people. Many animals. All together.”

“Like us!” Dot said. “All together on this porch.”

Sunny nodded. “Yes. We will make art that welcomes everyone.”

They began. Scratch-scritch went the chalk on the wooden floor. Sunny drew a big round circle. Jaya added blue swirls for water. Dot dotted little green islands. Mali gently shaded the edges.

A breeze carried the smell of fresh-cut grass. The porch felt cool under their feet and paws. The colors felt smooth and dusty in their hands. They giggled as a puff of chalk made them sneeze. “Achoo!” “A-CHOO!” “Hee-hee!”

Soon, more friends came. A shy raccoon peered over the step. A frog hopped up, boing, boing. A girl from next door, Lina, came with her grandmother, Nani. Lina held a little basket of buttons and ribbon. Nani smiled. “What are you making?”

“A world picture,” Sunny said, standing tall. “A picture to show we care for each other. We are global citizens. That means we think about everyone, everywhere.”

Lina’s eyes sparkled. “Can we help?”

Sunny nodded. “Yes! But we have a small problem.” She pointed to the chalk. “We might not have enough colors for everyone.”

The friends looked worried. The raccoon shifted. The frog croaked softly. The porch felt quiet.

Sunny lifted her backpack. “I prepared for surprises,” she said. “We can share. We can take turns. We can use new materials too.” She opened the backpack and pulled out bottle caps, leaves, and string. Lina added buttons and ribbon. Nani pulled a small roll of tape from her pocket.

Jaya tilted her head. “We can make patterns!”

Dot buzzed. “We can make flags!”

Mali squeaked, “We can make tiny homes and friends!”

Sunny smiled. “We can listen to each other’s ideas. That is peaceful.”

They made a plan:
- We take turns with chalk.
- We use the caps and buttons for bright dots.
- We ask before we place something.
- We say please and thank you.

“Good plan,” Nani said. “Prepared and kind.”

They worked together. Scratch-scritch went the chalk again. Click-clack went the bottle caps. Snip-snip went the ribbon. The porch filled with color. The frog carefully pressed a green leaf into the picture. The raccoon lined up shiny caps like tiny stars. Lina tied a blue ribbon for a river. Jaya dotted clouds. Dot drew a tiny ladybug on a tiny island. Mali drew hands holding hands around the big round Earth.

“Look,” Sunny whispered. “Everyone has a place.”

A small voice piped up. It was a snail near the flower pot. “Do I have a place?”

Sunny knelt low. “Yes,” she said. She drew a tiny trail, silver and soft, across a warm yellow sun. “This is your shiny path.”

The snail beamed. “Thank you.”

When the picture was done, the porch glowed. The Earth in the center was huge and kind. Around it were ribbons, buttons, leaves, and many small drawings of homes, hearts, and hands. The rocking chair creaked as if it clapped.

Nani looked at the art. “What does it say?” she asked gently.

Sunny stood beside the big circle. She felt proud and calm. “It says we are connected,” she said. “We belong together. We share. We listen. We solve problems with peace. We take care of our world.”

Lina nodded. “We are global citizens.”

“And artists!” Jaya added.

“And good planners,” Mali giggled, patting the backpack.

Sunny tucked the chalk back in. She brushed the dust from her paws. She looked at her friends from near and far. “Today we made art,” she said. “Tomorrow, we can pick up litter. We can plant a flower. We can learn hello in a new language. We can keep helping.”

The breeze hummed through the porch chimes. Ting-ting. Everyone smiled. They held hands, paws, wings, and tiny feet for a moment of quiet.

Sunny whispered, “Together, we make the world bright.”

The End.
