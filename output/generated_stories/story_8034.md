# Daisy's Dancing Day

Daisy the deer fawn loved the cozy music room. Sunlight streamed through tall windows. The wooden floor felt smooth under her tiny hooves. Colorful ribbons hung from the walls, and a big mirror sparkled in the corner.

Every Tuesday, Daisy came to practice her special forest dance. Her grandmother had taught her the steps. "Tip-tap, spin around, jump high like a cloud," Daisy whispered to herself. Her spotted coat shimmered as she moved.

Today felt different. Mrs. Robin, the music teacher, had exciting news.

"Children, we need helpers for the Spring Festival," chirped Mrs. Robin. "Who wants to teach the little woodland babies how to dance?"

Daisy's heart jumped with joy. She loved dancing so much! But then she felt scared. "What if I mess up?" she thought. "What if the babies don't like my dancing?"

Three tiny bunny babies hopped into the room. Their names were Fluffy, Puffy, and Tuffy. They wiggled their pink noses and looked around with big, curious eyes.

"Hello!" squeaked Fluffy. "We want to learn dancing!"

Daisy took a deep breath. She remembered her grandmother's gentle voice: "Dancing is about sharing happiness, little one."

"Okay," said Daisy softly. "Let's start with easy steps. Watch my hooves."

Daisy began to dance. "Step, step, twirl around!" But she spun too fast. THUMP! She tumbled right into the wall. The ribbons fell down like colorful rain.

"Oops!" gasped Daisy. Her cheeks turned pink with embarrassment. The bunny babies giggled, but not in a mean way.

"That was funny!" laughed Puffy. "Can we try?"

Daisy felt better. "Yes, but let's go slower this time."

She showed them again. "Step like you're walking on flower petals. Twirl like leaves in the wind. Jump like raindrops bouncing."

The bunny babies copied her moves. Fluffy stepped carefully. Puffy twirled in tiny circles. Tuffy jumped up and down with joy.

"This is fun!" they all said together.

But then Tuffy tried to jump too high. He landed on his bottom with a soft thud. His eyes filled with tears.

"I'm sorry, Tuffy," said Daisy quickly. "I should have shown you a smaller jump first. Let me help you."

Daisy knelt down beside him. "My grandmother taught me that dancing isn't about being perfect. It's about feeling happy inside. Want to try a bunny-sized jump?"

Tuffy nodded. This time, Daisy showed him a gentle hop. "Like this - just lift your paws a little bit."

Tuffy tried again. Perfect! He smiled so big his whiskers twitched.

Soon all the bunny babies were dancing beautifully. They stepped, twirled, and hopped together. The music room filled with giggles and the soft sound of paws on wood.

"Thank you, Daisy!" said the babies. "You're the best dance teacher ever!"

Mrs. Robin clapped her wings. "Wonderful! Daisy, you shared your grandmother's tradition so kindly. And you apologized when things went wrong. That shows a caring heart."

Daisy felt warm and proud inside. She had helped others learn something special. She had shared her family's dancing tradition. And she had learned that saying sorry makes everything better.

As the sun set through the windows, Daisy danced one more time. This time, she wasn't alone. All her new friends danced with her, filling the room with joy and laughter.

The End.
