# Tully Tortoise and the Porch of Good Habits

On a peaceful front porch, a wise tortoise named Tully rocked in a gentle wooden chair. The chair creaked in a friendly way, squeak-squeak, as the morning sun warmed Tully’s smooth shell. The air smelled like fresh lemons from a nearby tree. Bees hummed softly. A little breeze tickled the wind chimes, ting-ting-ting.

Tully smiled. “Good morning, porch,” Tully said. “Time for our healthy habits!”

Tully had a little basket beside the chair. Inside were a water bottle, a soft toothbrush, a small book, and a shiny apple. Tully took a slow sip of water. “Sip, sip,” Tully said. “Water helps my body and my brain.”

Just then, a tiny rabbit named Nib hopped up the porch steps. Hop, hop, hop. Nib’s ears were floppy, and his whiskers wiggled fast. He looked a little grumpy.

“Good morning, Nib,” Tully said kindly. “You look like a storm cloud today.”

Nib stamped a foot. Thump. “I’m hungry. I’m sleepy. I don’t wanna brush my teeth,” Nib said. His tummy made a growly sound, grrrowl.

Tully nodded. “I hear your tummy. I hear your feelings too,” Tully said. “Would you like to sit and share the porch with me?”

Nib climbed into a little chair next to Tully. The chair was cool and smooth. He rubbed his paws on the wooden arm. “I don’t like mornings,” Nib said. “They feel too big.”

Tully rocked, squeak-squeak. “Mornings can feel big,” Tully said softly. “That is why we start small. We start with tiny good things.”

“What tiny good things?” Nib asked. He sniffed the lemony air. He watched the sun make golden lines on the porch floor.

Tully opened the basket and held up the toothbrush. “Brush, brush,” Tully said. “We brush to keep our smiles strong.” Tully gently brushed his beak, swish-swish. Then Tully handed Nib a little bunny brush from the basket.

Nib wrinkled his nose. “I don’t want to.”

Tully smiled kindly. “Would you listen to a story while you try?” Tully asked. “I can tell a story about a brave bunny.”

Nib’s eyes lit up. “Yes, please!”

Tully began. “Once upon a porch, there was a brave bunny who tried tiny good things. He brushed for a count of five. One… two… three… four… five.”

Nib slowly brushed, swish-swish, with Tully. “One… two… three… four… five,” Nib said. His mouth tasted minty and cool. He giggled. “That feels nice!”

“Healthy habit number one,” Tully said. “Brush our teeth.”

Nib smiled a big, clean smile. “What next?” he asked.

Tully held up the water bottle. “Sip, sip. We drink water to wake up our bodies.”

They took little sips together. The water was cool and made Nib’s tummy feel calm. “Ahhh,” Nib sighed. The hum of bees sounded happy again.

“Healthy habit number two,” Tully said. “Drink water.”

Tully opened the book. “Would you like a porch story?” Tully asked.

Nib nodded. “Yes!” His floppy ears perked up.

Tully told a gentle story about a slow, steady hero who took deep breaths. Tully said, “In… out,” like the breeze. The wind chimes answered, ting-ting-ting. Nib breathed with the story. In… out. His shoulders felt soft now, not tight.

“Healthy habit number three,” Tully said. “We take deep breaths when feelings feel big.”

Nib looked down at his paws. “Tully, I shouted at my sister this morning. She wanted a hug, and I said ‘No!’ very loud. I think I made her sad.” His eyes looked watery.

Tully reached out and patted Nib’s paw. The touch was warm and gentle. “Sometimes we forget to use soft voices,” Tully said. “A healthy heart habit is to say, ‘I’m sorry,’ and try again.”

Nib nodded slowly. “I can do that,” he said. “I will say, ‘I’m sorry,’ and give a hug.”

Tully lifted the shiny apple. “And we feed our bodies with good food,” Tully said. “Crunchy apples help us grow.”

Nib took a bite. Crunch! Sweet juice ran on his tongue. “Yum,” he said with a bright face. “Healthy habit number four: eat good food.”

The porch was quiet in a friendly way. Bees hummed. The sun felt cozy like a blanket. Nib wiggled with a happy thought. “Tully,” he said, “thank you for the story and the tiny good things.”

Tully’s eyes crinkled. “You are very welcome,” Tully said. “Gratitude is another good habit. We say, ‘Thank you,’ when someone helps us.”

Nib put his paw over his heart. “Thank you, Tully,” he said again. “I am grateful.”

Tully nodded. “Would you like to practice your apology words?” Tully asked.

Nib sat up tall. “I will tell my sister, ‘I’m sorry I shouted. I will use a soft voice next time. Would you like a hug?’”

“That is a brave and kind plan,” Tully said.

Nib hopped down from the chair. Hop, hop. He paused and looked back at the porch. “Tully, can we do tiny good things every day?”

Tully rocked and smiled. “Yes, little friend. Every day we can brush, sip water, breathe deep, eat good food, say sorry when we forget, and say thank you when we remember. Tiny good things make big strong days.”

Nib’s whiskers wiggled in a happy dance. “I will start now!” he said. He waved his paw and hopped away, light and quick.

The porch felt peaceful again. Tully took a last sip of water, listened to the wind chimes sing, and whispered, “Thank you, morning. Thank you for tiny good things.”

And somewhere down the path, a little rabbit said sorry, gave a hug, and shared an apple slice with his sister.

The lesson: Healthy habits help our bodies and hearts. We brush, drink water, breathe, eat well, say sorry, and say thank you. Tiny good things make a big, kind day.

The End.
