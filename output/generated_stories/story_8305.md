# Leo’s Snack-Smart Study Corner

Morning light peeked into the cozy study corner. A soft mat lay on the floor. A low table held crayons, big picture books, and a tiny cup for pencils. A brave little lion cub named Leo padded in on quiet paws. His fur was warm like sunshine, and his whiskers twitched with excitement.

“Today I learn big things,” Leo said, puffing his chest. He loved the study corner. It smelled like paper, clean leaves, and a little like oranges.

On the table, a snack plate waited. There were crunchy carrots, sweet apple slices, a little bowl of nutty seeds, and a cup of cool water. Beside it sat a crinkly bag of loud, salty chips.

Leo’s tummy rumbled. “Roar!” he giggled. “Snack time!”

Just then, Mama Lion stepped in, her steps soft as clouds. “Good morning, brave cub,” she said. “We will have snack-smart time first. Listening ears on?”

Leo tapped his ears. “On!” he said.

Mama smiled. “Food is our fuel. Crunchy colors help you think and play. Orange carrots help eyes. Red apples give energy. Seeds make you strong. Water helps your body work.”

Leo sniffed the apple. Sweet. He sniffed the carrots. Fresh like the garden. He sniffed the chips. Salty and loud.

“But chips are fast,” Leo said. “Chomp, chomp, done!”

Mama nodded. “Sometimes chips are okay. But not every day. Let’s make a plate that is fair.”

“Fair?” Leo asked, head tilted.

“Fair means everybody gets a turn,” Mama said. “Your body parts need a turn, too. Eyes, muscles, tummy, brain. A fair plate gives each part a helping.”

Leo looked at the plate. “We give turns!” he said. “Eyes get orange. Muscles get seeds. Brain gets apple. Tummy gets water. Chips can wait.”

He took a carrot. Crunch! It snapped like a tiny stick. He felt brave and bright. He took an apple slice. Juicy sweetness ran over his tongue. He ate a few seeds. Munch, munch, nutty and tiny. He drank the water. Cool and clear.

“Good listening, Leo,” Mama said. “How do you feel?”

Leo put a paw on his middle. “I feel steady,” he said. “Like a strong drum. Boom-boom.”

Mama patted his mane. “Now we read.”

Leo sat on the mat. He opened a bright book called Garden Friends. The pages smelled like ink and summer. “Look!” he said. “A bee!”

Buzz, buzz, went a picture bee.

Mama read, “Bees like flowers. Flowers grow when we care for the earth.”

Leo’s eyes grew wide. “We can care,” he said. He looked around the study corner. In the trash bin he saw a crumpled paper and a plastic bottle. The recycling basket was empty.

“That bottle can go to the right place,” Mama said. “We listen to our earth, too.”

Leo carried the bottle to the blue basket. Clink. He picked up the crumpled paper and smoothed it. “I can draw on the back,” he said. “No waste.” He smiled. “That is fair for the earth.”

Just then, Leo heard a soft sniffle. His cousin, Tiny Tavi, peeked in. Tavi was a shy cub with small paws and big ears. “Hi,” Tavi whispered. “Can I sit?”

Leo nodded. “Yes. All cubs welcome.” He patted the mat. “We share the study corner.”

Tavi’s tummy rumbled. “I like chips,” Tavi said, eyes on the bag.

Leo remembered listening and fairness. He spoke gently. “Chips are tasty. But my plate is a team. Carrot, apple, seeds, and water. Chips can be a sometimes friend.”

Tavi’s ears wiggled. “Can I try?” he asked.

“Yes!” Leo said. He pushed the plate closer. “We can make tiny tastes.”

Tavi crunched a carrot. “Crunchy!” He tried an apple slice. “Sweet!” He ate a seed. “Small but mighty!”

Mama smiled. “Good trying, Tavi. We accept new tastes. We listen to our bodies.”

Suddenly, a breeze tickled the curtains. A napkin slid off the table and fluttered to the floor. “Oh no!” Leo said. He picked it up and put it in the right bin. “We keep our corner clean,” he said. “That is justice for everyone who learns here.”

“Justice means fair rules for all,” Mama said. “Clean space. Kind words. Shared snacks. Listening ears.”

Leo bounced a little. “I can do that!” He looked at Tavi. “Would you like water?”

“Yes, please,” Tavi said. He sipped. “Ahh. Cool.”

They colored bees and flowers on the smoothed paper. Yellow, black, green. The crayons felt waxy and warm. The study corner smelled like apples and hope.

When the snacks were done, Leo folded the chip bag closed. “See you another day,” he said kindly. He washed his paws, splish-splash, and dried them soft.

Mama gave them each a gentle nuzzle. “What did we learn today?”

Leo stood tall. “I learned to listen. My body needs a fair plate. Colors help me grow. Water helps me go. We recycle and not waste. We share and use kind words. That is justice.”

Tavi grinned. “I learned to try new foods. I feel brave, too.”

Mama’s eyes shone. “Wonderful.”

Leo hugged Tavi. The study corner felt bright and safe. His tummy felt steady. His heart felt big.

“We are snack-smart,” Leo said.

“Yes,” Mama said. “And we are earth-smart and kind.”

The End.
