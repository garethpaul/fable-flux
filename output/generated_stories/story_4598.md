# Joey's Big Responsibility

In a cozy garden shed filled with colorful flower pots and shiny garden tools, lived a happy little kangaroo joey named Bounce. The shed smelled like fresh dirt and blooming flowers, and sunlight streamed through the dusty windows, making everything sparkle.

Bounce loved to hop around the shed, bouncing from one corner to another. *Boing, boing, boing!* His little paws would pat-pat-pat on the wooden floor as he explored every nook and cranny.

One sunny morning, Farmer Mae knocked on the shed door. "Bounce," she called in her gentle voice, "I need your help! I'm going away for three days, and I need someone responsible to take care of my special seedlings."

Bounce's ears perked up with excitement. "Me? Really?" he squeaked, his tail wiggling with joy.

"Yes, you!" Farmer Mae smiled. "But this is a very important job. These tiny plants need water every morning and evening. Can you be responsible for them?"

Bounce puffed out his little chest proudly. "I can do it! I'm big and strong!" He hopped up and down to show how capable he was.

Farmer Mae showed him the watering can - it was blue and shiny, with little holes in the spout that made the water come out like gentle rain. "Remember, just a little water. Too much will hurt the plants, and too little will make them thirsty."

The first day went perfectly. Bounce carefully filled the watering can and sprinkled water on each tiny green seedling. "Good morning, little plants!" he said cheerfully. The plants seemed to dance in the gentle breeze, their leaves shimmering with water droplets.

But on the second day, Bounce saw his friends playing outside. The sun was warm, and he could hear them laughing and having fun. "Just this once," he thought, "I'll water the plants later."

He hopped outside and played all day long. *Bounce, bounce, bounce!* He had so much fun that he forgot all about his important job.

That evening, when he finally returned to the shed, Bounce gasped. The little seedlings looked sad and droopy. Their leaves were curled up, and they looked very, very thirsty.

"Oh no!" Bounce cried, his eyes filling with tears. "I was supposed to take care of you!"

He quickly filled the watering can and gave each plant a gentle drink. But he felt terrible inside. His tummy felt heavy with worry.

The next morning, Bounce woke up extra early. He hopped straight to the shed and checked on the plants. Some looked a little better, but others still seemed sad.

"I'm so sorry," he whispered to the plants. "I made a mistake, but I promise to do better."

He watered them carefully and sat beside them, talking softly about how he would be more responsible.

When Farmer Mae returned, she found Bounce sitting quietly with the plants. "How did everything go?" she asked.

Bounce took a deep breath. "I made a mistake," he said honestly. "I forgot to water them one day because I wanted to play. But I learned that when someone trusts me with something important, I need to do my job first."

Farmer Mae smiled and patted his soft fur. "Thank you for telling me the truth, Bounce. That shows real responsibility. And look - most of your plants are growing beautifully!"

From that day on, Bounce always remembered: being responsible means keeping promises, even when something more fun comes along. And the plants? They grew into the most beautiful flowers in the whole garden!

The End.
