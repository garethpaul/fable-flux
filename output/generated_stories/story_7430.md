# Rocky Raccoon Finds His Calm Beat

Rocky was a musical raccoon who loved to make songs. He lived in a cozy home office with a big wooden desk, a soft spinning chair, and a little keyboard that went beep, boop, bop. Sunlight made warm squares on the rug. Sticky notes in bright colors lined the wall. It smelled like paper and fresh crayons.

“Today, I will make a happy song!” Rocky said, tapping his striped tail. He put on his tiny headphones and pressed a key. Plink! He shook his maraca. Shika-shika! He tapped a drum. Dum, dum, dum!

His best friends, Mia the mouse and Benji the bluebird, fluttered in through the open window. “Hi, Rocky!” chirped Benji. “Can we help?”

“Yes!” said Rocky. “Let’s make music together.”

Mia climbed onto the desk and squeaked, “I can tap the tiny drum.” Benji settled on the lamp and added sweet tweets. The room filled with sounds—beeps, tweets, shakes, and taps. It was a happy mix.

Then—oops! Mia knocked over a cup of crayons. Clatter! The crayons rolled everywhere. A red one snapped in half. Rocky’s whiskers twitched. His ears got hot. His tummy felt tight like a knot.

“My crayons!” Rocky shouted. “You broke it!”

Mia’s eyes got big and shiny. “I’m sorry. It was an accident,” she squeaked.

Benji fluttered. “We can pick them up!”

But Rocky’s face scrunched. He stomped his foot. Stomp! Stomp! “I worked hard to sort those! I’m mad!”

The room felt heavy and too loud. The happy music stopped. Benji went quiet. Mia whispered, “I didn’t mean to.”

Rocky remembered something his grandma taught him. “When big feelings roar, pause and find your calm.” He took a slow breath in. He blew it out like he was cooling hot cocoa. Ffffff. He put a paw on his chest. Pat, pat. “I feel angry,” he said, using calm words. “I need a break.”

He walked to the window and looked at the blue sky. He listened to the soft tick-tick of the clock. He took three slow breaths. In. Out. In. Out. In. Out. He counted the sticky notes: one, two, three, four, five. His tummy knot started to loosen.

Rocky turned back. “I am still a little mad,” he said honestly. “But I can use my calm tools. Mia, I know it was an accident. Thank you for saying sorry.”

Mia nodded. “I can help fix it,” she said. “I can put the crayons back. We can tape the red one.”

Benji chirped, “I will sing a soft fixing song.” He hummed a gentle tune, la-la-la, like a feather.

They cleaned together. Tap, tap went the tape on the broken crayon. Click, click went the crayon box. Rocky’s paws felt busy, and busy felt better. He noticed the colors again: bright yellow like sunshine, cool blue like the sky, green like leaves on the tree outside.

Rocky said, “Thank you for helping. I am glad you are here.” He took off his headphones and smiled. “Let’s try our song again. But this time, I want to make a Calm Song.”

“What’s a Calm Song?” Mia asked.

“It starts with breathing,” Rocky said. He counted, “One, two, three,” and they all breathed in and out together. Then he played soft keys. Ding… dong… ding. Mia tapped the tiny drum gently. Tip… tap… tip. Benji sang like a slow breeze. Oooo… oooo…

Rocky added words:

“When I feel mad,
I stop and breathe.
I say my truth,
I find my ease.”

Mia added a verse, honest and clear: “I made a mess. I said, ‘I’m sorry.’ We fix it now. No need to worry.”

Benji sang: “All friends belong. We help, we share. We listen close. We show we care.”

The home office felt warm again. The sun slid across the rug. The soft chair squeaked as Rocky swayed. Squeak-squeak. The keyboard lights blinked like tiny stars.

Rocky’s ears relaxed. He felt proud. “I learned something today,” he said. “When I feel angry, I can use calm tools. I can breathe. I can take a break. I can use kind, honest words. I can let friends help.”

Mia squeaked, “And I learned to say sorry and to be careful.”

Benji fluffed his feathers. “I learned we all belong in the song, even when we make mistakes.”

Rocky nodded. “Everyone is included in my music. We all have a beat.”

They finished the Calm Song and pressed the big red record button. Click! The music filled the room, smooth and gentle. When it ended, they all clapped. Clap, clap, clap!

Rocky laughed. “My calm beat helped my mad feelings. I feel better.” He looked at the taped red crayon. “Thank you for fixing this with me.”

Mia smiled. “Thank you for breathing instead of yelling.”

Benji chirped, “Thank you for including us!”

Rocky wrapped his striped tail around his friends. “Today’s lesson: Feelings can be big, but we can be bigger. Breathe, be honest, and include your friends. That makes the best music.”

They turned off the lamp. Click. The office grew soft and quiet. Rocky hummed the Calm Song as the evening air drifted in, cool and sweet like mint tea.

The End.
