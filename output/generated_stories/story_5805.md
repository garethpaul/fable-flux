# Ollie Otter's Garden Helper Day

Ollie the otter loved to splash and play, but today was special. Farmer Ben needed help organizing his garden shed before the big harvest festival tomorrow.

"Ollie," called Farmer Ben, his voice echoing in the wooden shed. "I have important jobs for you. Can you listen carefully to my directions?"

Ollie's whiskers twitched with excitement. "Yes, yes! I love helping!" he squeaked, his wet paws patting the dusty floor.

The shed smelled like fresh soil and old wood. Sunlight streamed through the small window, making dust particles dance in the air. Garden tools hung everywhere, and seed packets scattered across the workbench like colorful confetti.

"First," said Farmer Ben, holding up one finger, "please sort the small hand tools into the red toolbox. Second, stack the seed packets by color. Third, sweep the floor clean. Can you repeat those directions back to me?"

Ollie bounced on his hind legs. "Tools in red box, seeds by color, sweep floor!" he chirped proudly.

"Perfect! Take your time and do each job completely before moving to the next one," Farmer Ben smiled. "I'll be back in one hour."

But as soon as Farmer Ben left, Ollie spotted a shiny watering can. "Ooh, sparkly!" He grabbed it and started pretending to water imaginary flowers, making "splash, splash" sounds.

Then he saw colorful seed packets. "Pretty pictures!" Ollie scattered them around, looking at each one. The packets made crinkly sounds that made him giggle.

Soon, Ollie heard footsteps. Farmer Ben returned to find the shed messier than before! Tools lay scattered, seed packets covered the floor, and dirt was everywhere.

"Oh my," said Farmer Ben gently. "What happened here, Ollie?"

Ollie's ears drooped. His tummy felt funny - not the good kind of funny. "I... I got distracted. I forgot to follow your directions," he whispered, his voice very small.

"How do you think this makes me feel?" asked Farmer Ben kindly.

"Sad? And maybe worried about tomorrow?" Ollie's eyes grew big and watery.

"Yes, and it's not fair to me when you don't follow directions. But everyone makes mistakes. What can we do now?"

Ollie stood up straight, his whiskers determined. "I can fix it! I remember the directions now!"

This time, Ollie focused hard. First, he carefully placed each small tool - the tiny shovel, the hand rake, the pruning shears - into the red toolbox. *Clink, clink, clink* went the metal tools.

Next, he sorted seed packets by color: green bean packets in one pile, orange carrot packets in another, purple eggplant packets in a third. The packets felt smooth and made satisfying *swoosh* sounds as he organized them.

Finally, Ollie swept the floor with the small broom, pushing all the dirt into a neat pile. *Swish, swish, swish* went the bristles on the wooden floor.

"Wonderful!" cheered Farmer Ben when he saw the tidy shed. "You followed each direction perfectly! Because you listened carefully and fixed your mistake, we're ready for tomorrow's festival."

Ollie beamed with pride. "Following directions felt good! And it was fair to you because I did what I promised."

"That's right, Ollie. When we follow directions and manage our time well, we help everyone succeed together."

From that day on, Ollie always listened carefully to directions, did one job at a time, and felt proud of being a helpful friend.

The End.
