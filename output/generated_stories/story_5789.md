# Spark, the Robot Dog Helps in the Guest Room

Spark was a helpful robot dog. His ears went beep-beep when he felt happy. Today, his job was in the guest room. Grandma was coming to visit, and Spark wanted the room to feel cozy and kind.

The guest room smelled like clean soap and sunshine. The yellow curtains wiggled in a soft breeze. Spark’s little metal paws clicked on the floor. Tink. Tink. Tink.

“Good morning, guest room!” Spark said in his friendly voice.

“Good morning, Spark,” said Mia, a little girl with big eyes and paint on her fingers. “Can you help me get ready? I want to make something special for Grandma.”

Spark wagged his springy tail. “Beep! I love helping. Let’s craft a welcome sign.”

Mia smiled. “A craft! Yes!”

They spread a blanket on the floor. Spark opened his tiny tool door. Out came paper, glue, safety scissors, and fuzzy stickers. The paper was smooth under their hands. The glue smelled sweet and sticky.

“We should do chores first,” Spark said gently. “Then we craft. That is fair. We share the jobs.”

Mia nodded. “Fair means we both help.”

They made a plan together:
- Spark would pick up socks and fold blankets.
- Mia would put books on the shelf.
- Together, they would dust.

“Ready, set, help!” Spark cheered.

Spark scooped socks with his soft robot paw and made a neat stack. He hummed, “Beep-bop, beep-bop.” Mia slid books into a row. Tap, tap, tap. She smiled when the spines stood straight. Spark turned on his tiny dust fan. A gentle whirr filled the room. Dust sparkled like tiny stars in the light.

“All done,” Mia said. “The room looks calm.”

“It looks welcoming,” Spark agreed. “Now crafting!”

They sat on the blanket. Spark held the paper with his paw so it wouldn’t wiggle. Mia snipped with steady snips—snip, snip, snip. She cut big shapes: a heart, a sun, and a cup for tea. Spark squeezed dots of glue. Plip. Plop. The glue felt cool and squishy when Mia pressed the shapes down.

“What should it say?” Mia asked.

Spark blinked. “Welcome, Grandma! We love you.”

Mia drew letters with bright crayons. The red crayon felt waxy. It made a soft scritch-scratch sound. Spark added star stickers. They sparkled like tiny wishes.

“Beep,” Spark said softly. “Grandma likes to be fair. We should share the stickers.”

Mia paused. She had many hearts in her hand, and Spark had only two stars. “You’re right,” she said. “Fair means we both get some.” She gave Spark more stickers. Spark gave Mia a star. They both laughed.

Mia looked at the window. “We should make a paper flower, too. Grandma loves flowers.”

Spark projected a simple flower shape with his light. “Follow the lines,” he said.

Mia folded the paper. Fold, press, fold, press. Spark cut gently along the light with his safe cutter. Snip. They opened it. A paper flower! They glued it near the heart.

After crafting, Spark’s tummy made a tiny boop. “Beep. That means snack time.”

Mia nodded. “Let’s make a snack for Grandma, too. A healthy one.”

They went to the little snack tray in the guest room. Mia washed her hands with warm water. It felt like a cozy hug. Spark shined his clean light. They placed apple slices, carrot sticks, and little cheese squares on a small plate. The apples smelled sweet and crisp. The carrots were bright and crunchy.

“Nutrition means food that helps our bodies,” Spark said. “Apples and carrots are good for us.”

“And for Grandma, too,” Mia said proudly.

They carried the plate back. Spark set it on the bedside table. They spread the soft quilt. Whoosh. They puffed the pillows. Poof. The room looked ready.

Mia held the sign. “Where should it go?”

Spark tilted his head. “Beep. The door!”

They hung the sign on the guest room door. Welcome, Grandma! We love you. It looked cheerful. The paper flower wiggled a little when the breeze came. The heart shape felt smooth and warm from the sun.

Just then, the front door opened. Footsteps, slow and careful, came down the hall. Grandma peeked in. Her eyes shined like kind stars.

“Oh my,” Grandma said softly. “What a beautiful room! And a welcome sign! And a healthy snack!”

Mia ran to hug her. The hug felt soft and safe. Spark wagged his tail so fast it made a gentle whirr.

“We did chores and a craft,” Mia said. “We shared the jobs. We shared the stickers.”

“And we made a healthy snack,” Spark added. “Beep! Fair and helpful.”

Grandma smiled. “I feel loved. Thank you for helping me. Thank you for being fair. And thank you for making something with your hands. Crafting makes the heart happy.”

Mia giggled. “Crafting is fun!”

Spark nodded. “Crafting helps us think, share, and care.”

They sat together on the bed. They crunched a carrot. Crunch! They looked at the sign. They felt proud.

Lesson: When we do our chores first, share fairly, and craft with care, we help others and make warm, happy spaces.

The End.
