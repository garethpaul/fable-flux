# Polly's Colorful World

Polly the parrot loved to paint. Her bright green feathers sparkled as she dipped her brush into colorful paints. Every morning, she flew to the sunny bathroom window to create beautiful pictures.

"Red like roses! Blue like the sky! Yellow like sunshine!" Polly chirped happily as she painted on her special easel.

One day, Polly noticed something that made her sad. The bathroom walls were covered with her paint splatters. Drops of purple paint dotted the mirror. Green smudges marked the white tiles. Orange fingerprints covered the sink.

"Oh no!" squawked Polly. "I made such a big mess! Mama and Papa will be upset."

Polly's colorful feathers drooped. She had been so excited about painting that she forgot to be careful. The bathroom looked like a rainbow had exploded everywhere.

Just then, Polly heard voices from the hallway. Her friend Carlos was visiting from Mexico, and her friend Yuki was here from Japan. They were coming to see her art!

"Hola, Polly!" called Carlos cheerfully.

"Konnichiwa!" sang Yuki in her sweet voice.

Polly felt embarrassed. "I can't show you my bathroom studio," she said sadly. "I made a terrible mess. Paint is everywhere!"

Carlos and Yuki peeked into the bathroom. They saw the colorful splatters covering every surface.

"Wow!" said Carlos. "Look at all these beautiful colors!"

"It's like a rainbow room!" giggled Yuki.

But Polly shook her head. "It's not beautiful. It's messy. I wasn't responsible with my art supplies."

Yuki had an idea. "In Japan, we have a saying: 'Fall down seven times, stand up eight.' That means we can always fix our mistakes!"

Carlos nodded. "In Mexico, my abuela taught me that when we make art, we should take care of our space too. Art is about creating beauty everywhere!"

"You're right," said Polly, feeling hopeful. "I should clean up my mess. But how?"

"We can help!" said Yuki. "Friends from around the world help each other!"

The three friends worked together. Carlos brought warm, soapy water that smelled like lemons. Yuki found soft cloths that felt fluffy against their feathers and hands. Polly used her wings to reach the high spots.

"Scrub, scrub, scrub!" they sang together as they cleaned.

As they worked, they shared stories about art from their countries. Carlos told them about bright murals in Mexico. Yuki described delicate origami cranes. Polly learned that children everywhere love to create beautiful things.

"You know what?" said Polly as they finished cleaning. "Making art is wonderful, but taking care of our world is important too!"

The bathroom sparkled clean. The mirror shined like crystal. The white tiles gleamed like fresh snow.

"Now I have an idea!" chirped Polly excitedly. She carefully spread newspaper on the floor. She placed her easel in the center. She organized her paints in neat rows.

"From now on, I'll protect my space while I create," said Polly proudly. "That way, I can make beautiful art AND keep our world beautiful too!"

Carlos and Yuki clapped their hands. "You're being a great global citizen!" said Yuki.

"What's a global citizen?" asked Polly.

"Someone who cares for the world and helps others, no matter where they're from!" explained Carlos.

Polly painted a beautiful picture of three friends holding hands around a clean, colorful Earth. She was careful to keep every paint drop on her paper.

"Art is even more beautiful when we're responsible!" sang Polly happily.

The End.
