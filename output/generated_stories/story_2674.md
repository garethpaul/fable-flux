# Lio’s Ready Plan

In a cozy study corner with a small desk and a soft rug, a brave little lion cub named Lio wiggled his whiskers. The air smelled like warm paper and crayons. Sunlight made golden squares on the floor. On the desk sat pencils, sticky notes, a tiny bell, and a big calendar with bright stickers.

Tomorrow was a special day. Lio’s Grandma Lala would visit to share a family drum song. Lio’s class would come to hear it. Lio felt his tummy flutter. He wanted to be ready. He wanted to be brave. But right now, his paws felt busy and his mind felt fuzzy.

“Oh no,” Lio said. “So many things!” His tail flicked. The bell on the desk went ting-ting.

Mama Lion peeked in. “I hear a little bell,” she said with a smile. “What’s up, brave cub?”

“I want to be ready for Grandma’s song,” Lio said. “I want to set the drum, show the pictures, and lead the moves. But my head feels like a bouncy ball.”

Mama sat on the rug. “When our head is bouncy, we pause. We breathe. We make a plan. Preparation helps our brave grow.”

“Preparation,” Lio whispered. He liked how the word felt—soft on his tongue like a purr.

Mama touched the tiny bell. “Let’s make Lio’s Ready Plan.”

Lio nodded. “Yes, a Ready Plan!”

Step one: they made a list on a sticky note. Lio pushed the pencil. It made a soft scratch-scratch sound. He said each word slowly so his paws could write.

“Drum… pictures… movement… water… rest,” he read.

Step two: a calm breath. Mama said, “Smell the flower.” Lio breathed in through his nose. “Blow the candle.” He breathed out through his mouth. The air felt cool on his whiskers.

“Again?” Lio asked.

“Again,” Mama said.

They did three soft breaths. Lio’s shoulders melted down. The room felt bigger and kinder.

Step three: practice. Lio brought a small hand drum from the shelf. The leather top was smooth and smelled like clean rain. He tapped with gentle paws. Tum-tum. Tum. Tum-tum. The sound was round and friendly.

Grandma Lala’s words danced in his mind: “Our family drum song helps us feel our hearts together.” Lio smiled. He liked sharing family things. It felt like warm soup in winter.

He set the drum by the desk. Check!

Step four: pictures. Lio opened a little book he had made. The pages were thick. The glue smelled sweet. On the first page was a drawing of Grandma Lala with bright scarves. On the next page was Lio tapping the drum. On the last page were simple steps: tap, step, stretch, smile.

“I will show this first,” Lio said. “So friends can see.”

Check!

Step five: movement practice. “Exercise time,” Mama said. “Strong body, calm mind.”

They stood by the rug. Lio rolled his shoulders. He bent his knees. He hopped like a tiny spring. Boing! He stretched his paws toward the ceiling. He touched his toes and giggled because his nose tickled his knee.

“Now mindful paws,” Mama said softly. “Can you feel your paws on the rug?”

Lio stood still. The rug felt fuzzy-warm. He listened. Tick-tick went the clock. A bird chirped outside. His breath went in and out, like the tide in a story.

“I feel steady,” Lio said.

“Steady is ready,” Mama said.

They sipped water. It felt cool and clear. Lio put a full bottle on the desk. Check!

Just then, there was a small problem. Lio flipped open his picture book. The last page came loose. “Oh no!” he whispered. The page fell like a leaf and landed face down.

Lio’s tummy fluttered again. He wanted to cry. He looked at Mama’s eyes. They were calm.

“What does a brave cub do?” Mama asked.

“Pause,” Lio said. He touched the bell. Ting. “Breathe. Make a plan.”

He took a flower breath in and a candle breath out. “Plan: tape!” He went to the drawer, pulled out clear tape, and pressed the page back in. Smooth, smooth. He patted it. “All better,” he said. His chest felt bigger and happier.

Next, Lio laid out a soft scarf Grandma had given him. It smelled like lavender and sunshine. He placed it by the drum. “Tradition-sharing,” he said, trying the big word. “I will tell the story of this scarf.”

He practiced his words. “This scarf is from Grandma Lala. We use it when we sing our family drum song. It helps us remember. It helps us feel together.”

He looked at the calendar. Tomorrow had a bright star sticker. Lio added a tiny lion sticker too. “I am ready,” he said.

That night, Lio cuddled into bed. He did three soft breaths and felt his body rest like a sleepy leaf on a pond.

In the morning, the study corner glowed. Grandma Lala arrived with a hug that felt like a warm blanket. Lio’s classmates tiptoed in and sat in a circle on the rug.

Lio rang the bell once. Ting. “Welcome,” he said. “I made a plan. First, pictures. Then drum. Then movement. Then water.” He smiled. “Then hugs.”

The class giggled.

Lio showed the picture book. He spoke slowly. He shared the scarf story. He tapped the drum. Tum-tum. Tum. The children tapped their knees. They stood and stretched tall like trees. They took flower breaths and blew candle breaths. The room felt calm and bright, like morning sun.

When it was done, everyone clapped. Grandma Lala’s eyes shone. “You were brave and ready,” she said. “You shared our tradition with care.”

Lio felt proud. His plan had helped. Preparation made his brave grow. Exercise made his body strong. Mindful breaths made his mind steady. Sharing the drum and scarf made his heart warm.

He touched the tiny bell once more. Ting. “Steady is ready,” he whispered with a grin.

The End.
