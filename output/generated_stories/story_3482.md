# Robo-Pup’s Dancing Decorations

In a sunny guest room with a big window, a helpful robot dog named Robo-Pup wagged his shiny tail. The guest room smelled like fresh soap and lemon. The bed was soft and white, and the curtains swished like whispers. Tap-tap, went Robo-Pup’s metal paws on the wooden floor.

“Beep-boop! We have a big job,” said Robo-Pup in a friendly voice. “Grandma Mei is visiting today. We need to make the room happy and bright!”

Robo-Pup’s little light blinked green. He looked at a basket on the chair. Inside were colorful papers, string, glue, and little bells. There was also a photo of Grandma Mei smiling in a red dress.

Robo-Pup wiggled. “We will craft decorations that move! Moving helps our bodies and our brains.”

He played a cheerful chime. “Let’s start! Step, stretch, snip!”

Robo-Pup rolled out a soft mat on the floor. His friend, a small child named Coco, peeked in. Coco wore cozy socks with stars. Coco’s eyes were big and bright.

“What are you doing, Robo-Pup?” Coco asked.

“Beep! We are making dancing decorations for Grandma Mei. Will you help?”

“Yes!” Coco hopped once. “Hop! I want to help.”

Robo-Pup opened the window just a little. A sweet breeze came in and tickled Coco’s cheeks. The curtains waved hello. Far away, a bird sang, “Chee-chee-chee!”

Robo-Pup held up glittery paper. “We will make flutter fans and twirl ribbons. They will move when we move. They will move when the breeze moves too.”

Coco nodded. “How do we do it?”

“First, warm-up moves,” said Robo-Pup. “Warm bodies work better. Stretch up high like tall bamboo!”

Coco stretched up, up, up. “Ooooh!” The ceiling light looked like a sun.

“Now touch your toes like a folding fan,” Robo-Pup said.

Coco bent down. “Hee-hee, I feel a little wiggle!”

Robo-Pup wagged. “Great! Now we craft.”

They spread paper on the mat. Snip-snip went the kid-safe scissors. Coco cut big round circles. Robo-Pup cut long strips with his gentle robot cutter.

“We are making lantern circles,” Robo-Pup said. “Grandma Mei loves lanterns. They remind her of stories and songs from far away.”

Coco smiled. “I love stories!”

They glued the paper into rings. Coco slid tiny bells on the strings. The bells jingled softly, like tiny stars.

“Now let’s move to test them,” said Robo-Pup. “Swing your arms like waves.”

Coco swung left and right. Whoosh, went the paper rings. Jingle-jingle, sang the bells.

Robo-Pup clapped his metal paws. “Perfect! Movement makes magic.”

Coco giggled. “Let’s make more!”

They crafted twirl ribbons. They taped ribbons to wooden sticks. The ribbons were red, gold, and blue. The colors looked warm and bright in the sun.

“Time to learn a ribbon dance,” Robo-Pup said. “Step-step, turn, swirl!”

Coco practiced. Step, step. Turn. Swirl! The ribbons made circles in the air. They swished and whispered, shhhh-shhhh.

But then Coco looked at the bed. It was neat and quiet. The wall looked plain and a little lonely.

“Robo-Pup,” Coco said softly, “the room still looks empty. I feel worried.”

Robo-Pup’s light blinked yellow. “Problem found. We need more decoration. We need more movement. Beep! Let’s think.”

They both sat crisscross on the mat. They closed their eyes. They took slow breaths. In, and out. In, and out. The bird sang again, chee-chee-chee.

Robo-Pup beeped. “Idea! Let’s make a welcome path that moves our bodies and shows care for Grandma Mei’s culture. We can make paper footprints with kind words.”

Coco’s face lit up. “Yes! Footprints that you step on!”

Together they traced Coco’s feet on colorful paper. Snip-snip. They wrote simple words on each foot: Hello. Welcome. Love. Joy. Dance. Smile. They drew little hearts and tiny lanterns. They also drew a dragon with a friendly grin.

“Now place the footprints on the floor from the door to the bed,” Robo-Pup said. “Each foot means a move. Step-step. Hop. Spin. Bow.”

“Bow?” Coco asked.

“Grandma Mei likes a small bow to say hello,” Robo-Pup explained. “It shows respect.”

Coco nodded. “I can bow!”

They taped the footprints down in a path. Step. Step. Hop! Spin! Bow. The bells jingled as they worked. The ribbons swished. The breeze made the lantern rings flutter.

Just then, they heard soft steps in the hall. Tap-tap. Grandma Mei peeked in. She wore a bright scarf with red flowers. Her eyes sparkled.

“Oh my,” she said. “What a lovely room! And what is this path?”

“It is a welcome path,” Coco said. “You follow the moves!”

Robo-Pup pressed play. A gentle drum beat filled the room, dum-dum, dum-dum. Coco held a ribbon stick and showed Grandma Mei: step-step, hop, spin, bow.

Grandma Mei laughed, happy and warm. She followed the footprints. Step-step. Hop! Spin! Bow. The bells chimed. The lantern rings danced. The ribbons painted the air.

“This is beautiful,” Grandma Mei said. “You worked together. You moved your bodies. You made art that dances. And you remembered my culture. Thank you.”

Coco hugged her. Robo-Pup wagged and beeped. “Team success!”

Grandma Mei sat on the soft bed. “I feel very welcome,” she said. “Let us dance one more time.”

They all followed the path again, moving and smiling. Coco felt proud. Robo-Pup’s light blinked happy green.

Robo-Pup said, “Today we learned: when our bodies move, our minds learn. When we cooperate and craft, we solve problems and show love.”

Coco nodded. “Moving is fun. Working together makes it better.”

The guest room glowed with color, sound, and joy. The decorations swished and jingled, and the air felt bright and warm.

The End.
