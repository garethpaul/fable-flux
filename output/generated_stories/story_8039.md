# Penny Panda's Dancing Mistake

Penny the Panda loved music more than anything. She loved the way drums went "boom-boom-boom" and how flutes sounded like singing birds. Today, she was in the musical practice room, getting ready for the big Spring Festival show.

The practice room smelled like wood polish and had shiny mirrors on every wall. Penny could see herself from all sides as she practiced her special bamboo dance. This dance had been passed down in her family for many, many years.

"Step, step, twirl!" Penny said to herself, moving her black and white paws gracefully. "Mama taught me this dance, and her mama taught her!"

Just then, her friend Ruby the Rabbit hopped into the room. Ruby's nose twitched with curiosity. "What are you doing, Penny? That looks so pretty!"

"I'm practicing my family's bamboo dance for the festival," Penny said proudly. "Would you like to learn it?"

Ruby's eyes sparkled. "Oh yes, please!"

Penny began teaching Ruby the steps. "First, you hold your arms up like bamboo swaying in the wind. Then you step softly, like you're walking through a peaceful forest."

Ruby tried to copy Penny, but her bunny hops were very different from panda steps. She bounced and wiggled instead of swaying smoothly.

"No, no!" Penny said, feeling frustrated. "You're doing it all wrong! This is MY family's dance. You have to do it the RIGHT way!"

Ruby's ears drooped down. Her whiskers trembled. "I... I was just trying my best," she whispered. Tears filled her big brown eyes.

Penny watched as Ruby hopped sadly toward the door. The practice room suddenly felt very quiet and empty. Penny looked at herself in the mirror and didn't like what she saw.

"Oh no," Penny said softly. "I hurt my friend's feelings."

Penny thought about how patient her mama had been when teaching her the dance. Mama never said "wrong" or "bad." She always said "try again" and "you're learning!"

Penny's heart felt heavy, like a rock in her chest. She knew what she had to do.

She found Ruby sitting outside the practice room, still sniffling. Penny sat down beside her friend.

"Ruby," Penny said gently, "I'm very sorry. I was mean to you when you were trying to learn. That wasn't kind."

Ruby looked up with surprised eyes.

"The truth is," Penny continued, "my mama always told me that sharing traditions makes them more beautiful, not less special. When you dance with your bunny hops, you're not doing it wrong. You're making it YOUR way!"

Ruby's ears perked up a little. "Really?"

"Really!" Penny smiled. "Would you like to try again? This time, I'll be patient like my mama taught me."

They went back into the practice room together. This time, Penny watched as Ruby added her own special bunny bounces to the bamboo dance. It looked different, but it was beautiful in a new way.

"Look!" Ruby giggled, pointing at their reflection in the mirror. "We're both dancing the bamboo dance, but it looks like a bamboo-and-flowers dance now!"

Penny clapped her paws with joy. "It's perfect! Sharing traditions and saying sorry when we make mistakes - that's what friendship is about!"

At the Spring Festival, Penny and Ruby performed their special bamboo-and-flowers dance together. Everyone cheered and clapped. Penny felt proud, not just of the dance, but of learning to apologize and share.

The End.
