# Kiki the Calm Koala Visits the Guest Room

Kiki the calm koala loved quiet hugs and soft pillows. One sunny afternoon, Kiki tiptoed into the guest room. The room smelled like clean sheets and lemon soap. The curtains were light and swishy, and a small fan hummed softly, like a sleepy bee.

“Hello, guest room,” Kiki whispered. “I will sit and read my picture book.”

Kiki climbed onto a big, fluffy chair. The chair squeaked a little. Kiki giggled and opened the book. The pages made a soft flip-flip sound. She traced the colors with her furry finger.

Just then, she heard tiny footsteps. Tap tap, tap tap.

Mina the mouse peeked in. She had bright eyes and a tiny blue scarf. “Hi, Kiki,” said Mina in a squeaky voice. “May I paint a picture here? It’s quiet.”

Kiki smiled. “Yes, Mina. This room is for guests. We share it. What do you need?”

Mina held up a small paint set. The colors shined like little candies—red, yellow, green, and purple. “I need a table and a napkin. I don’t want to make a mess.”

“That sounds respectful,” Kiki said. “Let’s think. Where can we paint safely?”

Kiki looked around slowly. She tapped her chin. “The little desk by the window,” she decided. “We can put down a towel first.”

Mina nodded. “Good thinking.”

They laid a towel on the desk. The towel felt bumpy under Kiki’s paw. Mina dipped her brush. Swish, swish. The paint smelled a tiny bit sweet. She painted a tiny rainbow.

Tap tap, tap tap. New footsteps.

Omar the owl waddled in with round glasses. “Hello, friends,” he hooted softly. “May I practice my soft hoots in here? I am learning not to be too loud.”

Kiki took a slow breath. “Yes, Omar. This is a quiet room. We can help.”

Omar fluffed his feathers. “Hoo,” he tried, very gently. It sounded like a warm breeze. Kiki smiled.

Then the door opened wide. Sita the snail slid in, her shell shining with many colors—gold, blue, and pink. “Hi,” she said slowly. “This room is smooth. May I draw a map on paper? I like to plan my path.”

Kiki nodded. “Welcome, Sita. We all use this room with care. We respect each other and the space.”

Sita smiled. “I will be careful.” She set a piece of paper on a board so it would not stick to the table.

Now the guest room had four friends: Kiki reading, Mina painting, Omar hooting softly, and Sita drawing a map. The fan hummed. The paintbrush whispered. Paper rustled. Everything felt calm.

Suddenly, the chair squeaked again—loud this time! Kiki wiggled to make it quiet, but the chair tipped a little. “Oh!” Kiki gasped. Her book slid and bumped Mina’s paint cup. The cup wobbled.

Kiki reached out and caught the cup. But a purple drop fell on the towel. Plop!

Mina’s eyes went wide. Omar’s glasses slid down his beak. Sita paused her drawing.

Kiki took another slow breath. “I am sorry, Mina,” she said softly. “I moved too fast. Are you okay? Is your painting okay?”

Mina looked at her rainbow. “It’s okay,” she said. “Only one drop on the towel. Thank you for catching the cup.”

Kiki nodded. “Next time I will say, ‘I am moving the chair.’ That way, you know. That is how we respect each other.”

Omar lifted a wing. “We can make a plan.” He blinked. “Maybe we set gentle room rules.”

Kiki smiled. “Good idea. Let’s think together.”

They sat in a little circle on the soft rug. The rug felt warm and fuzzy on their toes, paws, claws, and slimey foot.

Kiki said, “Rule one: Use quiet voices.”

Mina added, “Rule two: Put towels or papers under messy things.”

Omar said, “Rule three: Tell friends before moving big things.”

Sita said, “Rule four: Make space for different needs. I need smooth paths. Others may need light or quiet.”

Kiki clapped softly. “These rules show respect.”

Back to work they went. Kiki whispered, “I am moving my chair now.” Mina smiled and held her paint cup. Omar adjusted his glasses. Sita slid her map board closer to the edge so it wouldn’t wrinkle.

Time passed like a gentle song. When Mina finished, she held up her picture. “It’s a rainbow over many homes,” she said. “All different, all bright.”

Omar hooted a soft tune to match the colors. “Hoo-hoo,” like a lullaby. Sita traced her map. “My path visits each home,” she said. “It is a kind path.”

Kiki felt warm in her chest. “We all come from different places,” she said. “We like different things. We can still share this room with care.”

They cleaned up together. Splash, swish, pat. Paint brushes in water. Towels folded. Papers stacked. Kiki smoothed the chair so it wouldn’t squeak.

Mina looked around. “The guest room looks happy.”

Omar nodded. “And we learned.”

Sita’s shell glimmered. “We practiced respect.”

Kiki hugged her book. “We used our thinking to make a plan. We listened. We were kind. That makes the room safe and cozy for everyone.”

They gave each other gentle high-fives—paw, wing, tiny hand, and soft slime touch—and tiptoed out, leaving the guest room neat and peaceful.

The End.
