# Leo’s Lunchtime Lesson

Leo was a brave little lion cub with bright golden fur and a soft, swishy tail. Today, he was padding around the home office where Mama worked. The room smelled like warm paper and lemon cleaner. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. The computer hummed. Tap-tap-tap went Mama’s keyboard.

“Rrrr!” Leo giggled. “I’m a helper lion!”

Mama smiled. “Hello, helper lion. It’s almost lunchtime. Let’s make a good, strong-body meal.”

Leo puffed his chest. “I want crunchy chips!”

Mama shook her head gently. “Let’s listen to our tummies and our hearts. Strong bodies need colorful foods.”

Leo’s ears perked up. He listened. His tummy made a tiny grumble. “My tummy says, ‘Food, please!’”

Mama opened a lunch box on the desk. “We have sweet orange carrots, green cucumber circles, soft brown bread, creamy hummus, and juicy, red strawberries.”

Leo wrinkled his nose. “But chips are salty and loud. Crunch, crunch!”

Mama knelt so her eyes were level with Leo’s. “We can have a happy crunch with carrots, too. Let’s try a rainbow plate. Your body needs different colors to play, run, and roar.”

Leo thought. “A rainbow plate?” He liked rainbows. Rainbows felt brave and bright.

On the windowsill sat Pip, Leo’s friend, a small gray pigeon who sometimes visited. Pip cooed, “Coo-coo! Share with me?”

Leo blinked. “Hi, Pip! You can sit and watch. We share in fair ways.”

Mama nodded. “We share and we care for the world, too. We won’t waste food. We use our napkins, and we’ll put peels in the compost to help the earth.”

Leo padded to the rolling chair and gave it a careful push. “Vroom!” He stopped and listened to Mama.

“First,” Mama said softly, “we wash our paws.”

Leo listened and obeyed. He ran to the little sink in the corner. The water felt cool like tiny raindrops. Splash, splash. The soap smelled like oranges. “All clean!” he sang.

Back in the home office, Mama set a small plate with carrot sticks, cucumber circles, a triangle sandwich with hummus, and six strawberries. Pip tilted his head and cooed again.

Leo’s whiskers twitched. “I will taste the rainbow.” He tried a carrot. Crunch! It was sweet and snappy. He smiled. “Carrots make my teeth dance.”

“Good listening,” Mama said. “Your mouth tells you what it likes, and your body says thank you.”

Leo tried a cucumber circle. It was cool and watery, like a tiny pool. “Splash in my mouth!” He giggled.

Pip fluffed his feathers. “Coo! May I have a tiny crumb?”

Leo thought about fairness and justice. “We share in safe, kind ways.” He broke a tiny crumb of bread. “Here, Pip. Just a little. Birds need bird food, too.”

Mama smiled. “That is fair and thoughtful, Leo. You cared for Pip and listened to the rules.”

Leo took a bite of the soft sandwich. Creamy hummus hugged the bread. “Mmm, it feels cozy.”

He looked at the strawberries. Their red skins shone like little hearts. He bit one. Juice kissed his chin. “Sweet!” He wiped with his napkin. “Napkins help keep the desk clean. That helps the earth.”

Mama nodded. “Yes. We keep our place tidy. We recycle and compost. That’s environmental care.”

Leo spotted a shiny bag of chips on the shelf. He reached out, then paused. He listened to his tummy again. It felt happy, not rumbly.

He said, “My tummy says, ‘I am full and strong.’ I don’t need chips now.”

Mama clapped softly. “That is brave listening.”

Pip hopped and flapped his wings. “Coo-coo! Brave lion!”

Just then, a tiny ant walked near the keyboard. Leo leaned close. The ant’s feet made no sound, but Leo imagined a whisper: step-step-step. He moved a sheet of paper and gently guided the ant away from the cords.

“We helped the ant,” Leo said. “That is kind and fair.”

Mama placed a small bowl nearby. “The compost goes in here. Carrot peels and strawberry tops will help plants grow.”

Leo dropped the green tops into the bowl. Plop, plop. “Grow, plants, grow!”

When the plate was empty, Leo put his cup and plate in the sink. He wiped the desk with a soft cloth. Swish, swish. The lemon smell floated again.

Mama hugged him. “Leo, you chose colorful foods. You listened to your body. You were fair to Pip and gentle to the ant. You cared for our earth. That is a strong lion day.”

Leo’s eyes sparkled. “I feel brave on the inside! My roar is ready.” He took a deep breath. “Rrrrr—thank you, carrots and hummus and strawberries!”

Pip cooed a happy tune. The computer hummed. Sunlight warmed Leo’s fur. The home office felt cozy and bright.

Leo whispered, “Tomorrow, I will make another rainbow plate.”

Mama nodded. “That’s a great plan.”

Leo smiled and said, “Strong food, kind heart, good listening.”

The End.
