# Roo’s Porch Postcards

Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey. Roo loved to bounce, but today Roo sat on the peaceful front porch. The wooden boards felt warm and smooth. A soft breeze smelled like sweet flowers and fresh rain. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” Somewhere far away, a dog barked, “Woof!”

On the little porch table sat a big basket of postcards. Each card had a bright picture from a different place in the world. Roo’s mom said, “These are from our friends. They live near and far.”

Roo’s eyes grew wide. “From the big world?” Roo asked.

“Yes,” said Mom with a smile. “The world is big, and we all share it.”

Roo picked up a postcard with shining blue water. “This one is from Nani by the sea,” Roo read slowly. “She says the waves sing.”

Roo closed their eyes and listened. Whoosh, whoosh, the breeze sounded like tiny waves. Roo giggled. “I can hear the sea on our porch!”

The next card showed tall trees and snow. “This is from Jun,” Mom said. “Jun wears warm hats.”

Roo touched the cool glass of the porch window. “It feels chilly, like snow,” Roo whispered.

Roo chose another card with bright kites. “From Amira,” Mom said. “They fly kites and share tea.”

Roo breathed in. The porch air smelled like mint from the little pot of leaves. “We can share tea, too,” Roo said. “We can be kind like Amira.”

Roo bounced a little. “I want to send cards back! I want to say hello to the whole wide world.”

“That is a good idea,” said Mom. “That is global citizenship. It means we care about people everywhere. We listen, we share, we help, and we say sorry when we make mistakes.”

Roo nodded. “I can do that!”

Roo got paper, crayons, and stickers. Roo drew a sun with a smile, a porch with a chair, and a big red heart. Roo said, “I will write ‘Hello’ in different ways.” Roo tried hard. “Hola. Jambo. Salaam. Namaste. Hello.”

Roo sounded out the words slowly. “Ho-la,” Roo said, tapping each letter. “I did it!”

Mom clapped softly. “You spoke up for yourself and tried. That is self-advocacy. When you need help, you can also ask.”

Roo took a deep breath. “Can you help me write ‘Kia ora’?” Roo asked.

“Of course,” Mom said, guiding Roo’s paw. “Kia ora.”

Roo smiled. “Thank you.”

Roo started to place stamps on the cards. The stamps were shiny and colorful. Roo loved the sparkle. Roo pressed too hard. Squish! A stamp bent and stuck to the table.

“Oh no,” Roo gasped. Roo’s tummy felt twisty. Roo wanted to hide, but Roo remembered: speak up.

Roo looked at Mom. “I made a mistake. I bent the stamp. I am sorry.”

Mom’s eyes were kind. “Thank you for telling me. That was a good apology. We can fix it together.”

Roo smoothed the stamp. “I will try again, gently.” Roo pressed softly this time. Press, press. Perfect!

Outside, the wind chimes sang, “Ting-ting-ting.” Roo felt calm and brave.

“Let’s learn about our friends’ homes,” Mom said. She opened a little globe on the porch shelf. Roo spun it slowly. The globe hummed on its stand. “We all share Earth,” Mom said. “We take care of it and each other.”

Roo pointed. “Here is where Jun lives. It is far and snowy.”

“Yes,” said Mom. “When we think about Jun, we can remember to dress warm and to care for people in cold places.”

Roo pointed again. “Here is Amira’s home. It is sunny.”

“Yes,” Mom said. “We can share water on hot days and plant shade trees.”

Roo hugged the globe. “We are all neighbors,” Roo whispered.

A small ant marched across the porch step, carrying a crumb. Roo watched. The crumb looked big and heavy.

Roo gently moved a leaf so the ant had a clear path. “There you go,” Roo said softly. “We help small friends, too.”

Mom smiled. “That is also global citizenship. We help our world, big and small.”

Soon the postcards were ready. Roo lined them up like a rainbow. Roo sniffed the paper. It smelled like lemon glue and crayons. Roo listened to the mailbox creak as they slipped the cards inside. Clink, clink.

Roo waved at the box. “Travel safe, little cards! Say hello for me!”

Back on the porch, Roo sipped warm mint tea. The cup was smooth and cozy in Roo’s paws. Roo felt proud.

“I used my voice when I needed help,” Roo said. “I said sorry when I made a mistake. I learned about friends far away. I can care, share, and listen.”

Mom hugged Roo. “Yes. That is being a helper to the world.”

Roo looked at the sky turning peach and gold. A bird flapped by. Roo whispered, “We are all under one sky.”

Roo bounced once, then sat still. “Tomorrow, can we pick up litter at the park?” Roo asked. “I want to help our Earth.”

Mom nodded. “Yes. Your voice matters. Your care matters.”

Roo smiled. “I am a good neighbor to the whole world.”

The porch breeze said, “Whoosh.” The wind chimes sang, “Ting-ting.” Roo’s heart felt light.

The End.
