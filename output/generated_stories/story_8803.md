# Clever Cat’s Team Talk

In a bright, playful living room, a clever cat named Coco stretched on a soft, sunny rug. Sunbeams made warm stripes on the floor. Toys were everywhere—colorful balls, tiny cones, and a basket of ribbon sticks. From the speaker, happy drum music thumped gently. Thump-thump, tap-tap!

Coco’s whiskers wiggled. “Today is sports day in the living room!” Coco purred. “We can run, jump, and dance!” Coco loved to move, but also loved to plan. A clever cat makes a clever plan.

Coco set up a little course by the couch:
- A red ball for rolling
- Soft pillows for hopping
- Blue tape on the floor for a running path
- Crinkly ribbon sticks for a finish-line dance

Paws pat, pat, pat. Tail swish-swish. “Ready!” Coco said. But just then, the door gave a tiny creak. Coco’s friends peeked in—Pip the puppy, Nia the bunny, and Dot the parakeet.

Pip wagged fast. “I want to go fast! Super fast!”

Nia twitched her nose. “I want to hop-hop-hop!”

Dot fluttered. “I want to sing and draw!”

Coco smiled. “We can do sports and art together. Sports make bodies strong. Art makes hearts bright. Let’s be a team.”

They lined up on the blue tape. “On your marks,” Coco said. “Get set…” The music beat louder. Thump-thump! “Go!”

Pip ran too fast and bumped a pillow. “Oof!” Nia hopped high and landed sideways. “Eep!” Dot flapped and dropped a ribbon stick. “Oh no!”

Coco paused and looked around. The room smelled like crayons from the art basket and fresh laundry from the couch. The pillows felt squishy under paws. Coco heard little pants and peeps. Friends felt wobbly. This was a problem.

Pip drooped. “I’m not good at this.”

Nia’s ears went down. “Maybe I can’t hop that way.”

Dot’s feathers fluffed. “I want to sing, but I feel left out.”

Coco took a deep breath. In… out… Clever cats solve problems. “Team,” Coco said gently, “let’s try again in a new way. We can speak up about what we need. That’s self-advocacy. We can tell each other, ‘This is what helps me.’”

Pip blinked. “I can say what I need?”

“Yes,” Coco said. “Your voice matters.”

Pip wagged a little. “I need a slower start.”

Nia nodded. “I need smaller hops.”

Dot chirped. “I need time to sing and draw too.”

Coco’s tail curled like a question mark. “Great! We’ll make a plan. Sports first, art next. And we’ll make the game fit us.”

Coco took the ribbon sticks and taped them into a bright, sparkly goal. “We will do three stations.”

Coco drew pictures on paper to show the plan, with big simple shapes:
- A snail for Slow Run
- A little cloud for Soft Hops
- A star for Dance-and-Draw

Coco held up the snail picture. “Station One: Slow Run. We run on the blue line like snails with smiles.” The friends giggled.

“On your marks,” Coco said. “Snail, set, go!”

They ran smooth and slow. Pip’s paws went pad-pad-pad. Pip grinned. “I can do this!” Coco clapped. Clap, clap!

Coco lifted the cloud picture. “Station Two: Soft Hops. We hop from pillow to pillow like gentle clouds.” Nia tried a small hop. Boing. Then another. Boing! “I like small hops,” Nia said, smiling wide. Pip followed with tiny hops. Dot perched and cheered, “Tweet! Soft and steady!”

Coco showed the star picture. “Station Three: Dance-and-Draw. We move to the beat, then make art about our sport.”

The music jingled lightly. Tinkle, thump, tinkle! Dot sang a sweet “La-la!” and twirled a ribbon stick. Coco swayed. Pip stepped side to side. Nia did little bunny hops. Then they sat at the low table with thick crayons and big paper.

“What will you draw?” Coco asked.

Pip chose a blue crayon. Scratch, scratch. “I draw me running slow and happy,” Pip said.

Nia used soft pink. “I draw little pillows and little hops,” she said, tap-tapping dots.

Dot used yellow. “I draw a big star for our team dance,” Dot chirped.

Coco drew a map of the living room course with arrows and smiles. “I draw our plan,” Coco said. “Plans help us play fair and safe.”

When they finished, Coco held up each picture. “Look at our art! It tells our sports story.”

Pip wagged. “I feel proud.”

Nia’s nose twitched. “I feel calm.”

Dot fluffed. “I feel bright!”

Coco purred. “I feel clever and kind. We spoke up. We listened. We made a plan. We moved our bodies. We made art. That is a great team.”

They tried the course one more time. Slow Run, Soft Hops, Dance-and-Draw. This time, no bumps, no oofs—just smiles, claps, and little cheers. The room was full of happy sounds and colors. The sun felt warm on their fur and feathers.

At the end, Coco rolled up the pictures and tied them with a ribbon. “These are our team posters,” Coco said. “They show that sports are for everyone, and speaking up helps.”

Pip raised a paw. “I like slow starts.”

Nia raised a paw. “I like small steps.”

Dot raised a wing. “I like art with sport.”

Coco raised a paw too. “I like being a helper.”

They all touched paws and wings in the middle. “Team!” they said together.

Lesson time, Coco thought. Coco turned to the friends. “Here is what we learned,” Coco said softly. “Sports make us strong. Art helps us share our story. And when we tell others what we need, we can play in a way that feels good.”

The friends nodded. The living room glowed golden. The drum music faded to a soft tap-tap.

Coco stretched again on the sunny rug. “Sports day success,” Coco purred. “And art day too.”

The End.
