# Melody's Road Trip Song

The family car bumped along the winding road. In the back seat, little Ruby pressed her nose against the cool window. "Are we there yet?" she asked for the tenth time.

"Not yet, sweetie," said Mom from the front seat. "We still have a long way to go."

Ruby's bottom lip started to wobble. She was tired of sitting. She was tired of the long car ride to Grandma's house.

Just then, a small yellow bird landed on the car's side mirror. The bird had bright orange feet and the most beautiful voice. "Tweet-tweet-hello!" sang the bird in a sweet, musical voice.

"Oh!" gasped Ruby, her eyes growing wide. "Mom, Dad, look! A singing bird!"

The bird's name was Melody, and she loved to sing. But today, she felt sad. She had been flying with her bird family when a big gust of wind blew her far away. Now she was lost and couldn't find her way home.

"Tweet-tweet-help," sang Melody softly, her little voice shaking.

Ruby put her hand on the window. "What's wrong, little bird?" she asked gently.

Dad looked in the rearview mirror. "I think she might be lost, Ruby. Birds sometimes get separated from their families during long flights."

Ruby's heart felt warm and caring. She wanted to help. "Can we help her find her family?"

"That's very kind of you to think of others," said Mom with a proud smile. "But how can we help a bird while we're driving?"

Ruby thought hard. Then her face lit up with a big idea. "Maybe we can work together! I know lots of songs about flying and finding home. Maybe if I sing with her, other birds will hear us!"

Melody tilted her tiny head, listening carefully. She liked this little girl's gentle voice.

"Tweet-tweet-yes!" sang Melody happily.

So Ruby and Melody began to sing together. Ruby sang a song about flying high in the blue sky. Melody added her beautiful bird sounds. Their voices mixed together like honey and sunshine.

"Fly, fly, little bird, up so high,
Look for family in the sky!
Sing your song both loud and clear,
So your loved ones know you're here!"

The car windows were open, and their song floated out into the warm air. Other birds in the trees heard the beautiful music.

Soon, a whole flock of yellow birds came flying toward the car. "Tweet-tweet-Melody!" they called out joyfully.

"Tweet-tweet-family!" sang Melody, her heart bursting with happiness.

Ruby clapped her hands with joy. "We did it! We worked together and helped her find her family!"

The bird family flew alongside the car for a moment. Melody sang one last beautiful song just for Ruby. "Tweet-tweet-thank-you! Tweet-tweet-friend!"

As the birds flew away to their home in the forest, Ruby felt wonderful inside. She had used her voice and her caring heart to help someone in need.

"I'm proud of you, Ruby," said Dad. "You showed real compassion today."

"And you learned that when we work together, we can solve big problems," added Mom.

Ruby smiled and waved goodbye to Melody. The rest of the car ride felt much shorter. Ruby spent the time making up new songs about helping others and working as a team.

When they finally reached Grandma's house, Ruby couldn't wait to tell her about Melody and their special song that brought a family back together.

The End.
