# Dippy Dolphin’s Balcony Garden Day

The sun was warm and soft. A gentle breeze smelled like mint and wet soil. On a small balcony garden, green leaves waved hello. Pots lined up like little drums. Cherry tomatoes glowed red. Basil leaves looked like tiny boats.

Splash! Dippy the playful dolphin peeked over the balcony wall. He lived in a big blue tank just inside the sliding door. His tail swished with joy. “Click-click! Can I play in the garden?” Dippy asked, popping his head up. Drops of water sparkled on his smooth gray back.

Mina and Leo, two friends with watering cans, looked at each other. “Hi, Dippy!” said Mina. She wore sun-yellow gloves. “We’re planting seeds today.”

“I’m curious,” Dippy chirped. “What do seeds feel like? What do they smell like? Can I help? I want to be in the garden too.”

Leo smiled. “We want you with us,” he said. “Everyone can help.” He held up a tiny packet. “These are spinach seeds. They help us grow strong. Healthy food makes healthy bodies!”

Dippy clapped his fins. “I want to join! I can splash water.”

Mina touched the soil. It was soft and cool. “Careful,” she said kindly. “Too much splash can hurt the plants. They have little roots.”

Dippy blinked. He felt a small twist in his tummy. He wanted to be included. He wanted to be part of the team. But how could a dolphin help in a balcony garden?

Just then, a butterfly landed on a purple flower. Dippy giggled. “It tickles my nose!” he said, sniffing. He loved the sweet flower smell and the earthy, muddy scent in the air.

“Let’s think,” Mina said. “We can find a safe way. We can use logic.” She tapped her head gently. “What can Dippy do without flooding the pots?”

Leo looked at Dippy’s tank. “Your tank has a slow drip hose for cleaning,” he said. “Maybe we can use slow, slow water.” He held up the tiny hose. “We can make a dolphin-sized watering straw!”

Dippy’s eyes lit up. “A straw for water drops?” he chirped.

“Problem,” Mina said, counting on her fingers. “One: Dippy wants to help. Two: Plants need gentle water. Three: We need a tool. Solution: Make a slow drip for Dippy!”

They clipped the hose to the edge of Dippy’s tank. Plip… plip… water made soft sounds. Dippy blew a teeny bubble. “I can make little puffs,” he said. “Tiny bubbles, tiny drops.”

Mina guided the end of the hose to a pot. “You can aim with your nose,” she said. “We will watch, and we will help.”

They worked together. Plip… plip… the soil grew dark and happy. The basil leaves smelled extra fresh. “Mmm,” Leo said. “Healthy habits: plants get water and sun. We get water and rest, too!”

“I drink water!” Dippy said proudly. He slurped from his tank. “Glub, glub.”

When it was seed time, Mina poured a few into a small bowl. “Seeds are tiny,” she said softly. “Like little moons.” Dippy listened and did not splash. He liked being careful. He liked being included.

“Can I hold a seed?” Dippy asked.

Leo thought. “Your fins are slippery,” he said. “But your nose is gentle. Try booping the spoon.” He held a spoon with one seed.

Dippy focused. He took a slow breath. Tap. The seed slid into the soil. “I did it!” he cheered.

“Great job,” Mina said, clapping. “That was careful and smart.”

They planted more seeds. They talked about washing hands after touching soil. They took turns. They sang a little planting song. The balcony smelled like mint, tomato, and sunshine.

Suddenly, a cloud passed over the sun. The breeze grew cooler. Leaves rustled. The watering can wobbled near the edge of the balcony.

“Oh no!” said Leo. “It might fall.”

Dippy’s eyes went wide. He swished his tail just once and made a tiny push of water. Whoosh… but soft. The can scooted back from the edge. “Safe,” Dippy chirped.

Mina smiled. “Teamwork! You used your dolphin skills in a gentle way.”

Leo nodded. “That was logical thinking. You saw the problem. You used a small push, not a big splash.”

Dippy felt warm inside. “Thank you for helping me help,” he said. “I like being part of the garden.”

Mina handed Dippy a leafy sprig of basil to sniff. “Everyone belongs here,” she said. “Different friends, different jobs. We include each other, and we keep each other safe.”

They cleaned up together. Mina washed her hands. Leo wiped the pots. Dippy swished his tank tidy. The butterfly came back and danced in the air. The sun peeked out again and kissed the leaves.

“What did we learn today?” Mina asked.

Dippy blinked happily. “Include friends,” he said. “Use gentle bodies and gentle words. Eat healthy greens, drink water, and rest. Think of smart, safe ways to help.”

Leo grinned. “And be curious! Ask questions. Try new tools. Try new jobs.”

They all looked at the garden. The soil was snug. The seeds were sleeping. The balcony felt calm and bright.

“We did it together,” Mina whispered.

Dippy chirped, “Together is best.”

The End.
