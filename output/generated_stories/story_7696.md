# Dottie the Dolphin's Ocean Helper

Dottie the dolphin loved to splash and play in the warm blue ocean. She had shiny gray skin that sparkled in the sunlight, and a big smile that made everyone happy. But today, Dottie felt worried instead of playful.

"Oh no!" Dottie squeaked as she swam near the coral reef. "What happened to my beautiful home?"

The ocean floor was covered with plastic bottles, candy wrappers, and old fishing nets. The colorful coral looked sad and gray. Fish were swimming away because they couldn't find clean places to live.

Dottie's friend Sammy the seahorse floated by, looking upset. "Dottie, I can't find anywhere safe to hide. All this trash is making our ocean sick!"

"We need help," Dottie said, thinking hard. "But who can help us clean up this big mess?"

Just then, Dottie remembered something special. She had seen humans working at their computers, solving problems and building wonderful things. Maybe they could help!

Dottie swam up, up, up to the surface of the ocean. She peeked through a window and saw a kind woman named Ms. Sarah working in her home office. The room smelled like fresh coffee and had bright yellow walls covered with pictures of sea animals.

"Excuse me!" Dottie called through the open window, making her friendliest dolphin sounds.

Ms. Sarah looked up from her computer, surprised. "Oh my! A talking dolphin! Hello there, little one."

"My name is Dottie," she said politely. "I need your help. Our ocean home is full of trash, and it's making all the sea creatures sad and sick."

Ms. Sarah's eyes grew wide with concern. "That sounds terrible! Tell me more."

Dottie explained about the plastic bottles hurting the fish and the nets tangling up the sea turtles. She described how the trash made the water dirty and the coral lose its pretty colors.

"I think I can help," Ms. Sarah said, typing quickly on her computer. "I'm going to build a special website to teach people about keeping oceans clean!"

"What's a website?" Dottie asked curiously.

"It's like a magical book that lives in computers," Ms. Sarah explained. "People all over the world can read it and learn how to take care of the ocean."

Ms. Sarah worked hard, clicking and typing. She added pictures of happy dolphins, clean beaches, and colorful fish. She wrote simple words that even little children could understand: "Don't throw trash in the water," "Use less plastic," and "Recycle to help sea friends."

"Look, Dottie!" Ms. Sarah turned her screen around. "People are already reading about ocean care. They're sharing the website with their friends and families!"

Dottie clapped her flippers with joy. "This is wonderful! But what else can we do?"

"Let's think together," Ms. Sarah said. "What if we organize beach clean-up days? People could come pick up trash and learn about protecting ocean animals."

"Yes!" Dottie squeaked excitedly. "And maybe they could use reusable water bottles instead of plastic ones!"

Over the next few weeks, amazing things happened. Families came to clean the beaches. Schools learned about ocean care. People started using less plastic and recycling more.

Soon, Dottie's ocean home became beautiful again. The coral turned bright and colorful, fish returned to play, and the water sparkled crystal clear.

"Thank you, Ms. Sarah!" Dottie called through the window. "You helped us solve our big problem!"

"We solved it together," Ms. Sarah smiled. "When we work as a team and think carefully about problems, we can make our world better for everyone."

Dottie dove back into her clean, happy ocean, knowing that caring people everywhere were helping keep her home safe and beautiful.

The End.
