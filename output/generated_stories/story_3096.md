# Pippa the Patient Parrot

Pippa was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a splash of blue on her tail. She loved the cozy study corner by the window. The study corner had a soft rug, a little table, a basket of crayons, and a shelf of books in many languages. Sunlight spilled in, warm and golden, and the air smelled like paper and lemon tea.

“Squawk! Story time!” Pippa chirped every day. She liked to make colorful posters with words and pictures. She liked to read aloud to friends, too. Today, Pippa wanted to make a fairness chart for everyone in the study corner. “We will take turns,” she said, tapping a crayon. “We will listen. We will help. We will use kind words.”

Pippa fluffed her feathers and grabbed a purple crayon. But the crayon tip snapped. Snap! The tiny piece rolled away.

“Oh no,” Pippa whispered. Her tummy felt tight. Her cheeks felt hot. “I want it to work now!”

She picked a green crayon. Scritch, scritch. The green line looked too wiggly. She tried to write “Fair Turns,” but the letters got squished. “Squawk!” she cried, louder this time. “It’s not right!”

Her friend Nia, a gentle rabbit with soft gray fur, peeked in. “Hi, Pippa,” Nia said. “I brought a basket for volunteering. We can help tidy books after story time.”

“I can’t,” Pippa huffed. “My chart is all wrong.”

Nia’s ears drooped. “It’s okay to feel mad,” she said. “Do you want a hug?”

Pippa shook her head. She felt too prickly for hugs. Her wings trembled. She knocked a blue crayon off the table by accident. Clack. Clack. It rolled under the shelf.

Just then Milo, a small turtle with a shiny shell, slowly scooted in. He wore a tiny sticker that said Helper. “Hello,” Milo said. “I can reach the crayon. I volunteer!”

Milo reached under the shelf and nudged the crayon out with his shell. “Got it,” he smiled.

“Thank you,” Pippa said, but her voice still sounded grumpy. Inside, she felt a storm. Boom-boom went her heart. Her eyes felt wet.

Nia pointed to a small poster on the wall. It had calm-down steps in big, simple pictures: Stop. Breathe. Count. Try again.

“Let’s try the steps,” Nia said softly. “We can do it together.”

Pippa looked at the poster. She wanted to make a good chart. She wanted to be fair. She wanted to be kind. But the storm was big.

She placed her wing on her chest. “Stop,” she whispered. She closed her eyes. She took a slow breath in—one, two, three. She breathed out—one, two, three. She counted, “One… two… three…” Her shoulders dropped. The storm in her tummy felt smaller.

“How do you feel?” Milo asked.

“A little better,” Pippa said. “Still bumpy, but better.”

Nia nodded. “Let’s try again. We can take turns writing. That’s fair.”

Pippa blinked. “But it’s my chart.”

“It can be our chart,” Nia said. “Fair means everyone gets a turn. And we can use words in different languages, too. We can show how we all belong.”

Pippa’s feathers smoothed. She liked that idea. “Okay,” she said.

They set the purple crayon aside and chose a sturdy black marker. Pippa drew a big happy sun at the top. She wrote “Fair Turns” carefully. Scritch-scratch. This time, the letters looked friendly.

Milo raised his hand. “May I have a turn?” he asked.

“Yes,” Pippa said. “That’s fair.” Milo drew a picture of a clock to show turn-taking.

Nia took a turn and wrote “Please” and “Thank you.”

Pippa flapped. “Let’s add words from home languages,” she chirped. She knew a few. “Gracias,” she wrote for Spanish. “Merci,” for French. “Asante,” for Swahili. Milo added “Xie xie” in Chinese. Nia wrote “Dhanyavaad” in Hindi, copying carefully from a book.

The chart looked colorful and kind. The room felt warm and sweet, like honey on toast.

A tiny squirrel named Sora peeked in. “Can I help too?” she asked in a quiet voice.

“Yes!” said Pippa. “We are volunteers today.” She pointed to the new chart. “We’re taking turns and using kind words.”

Sora smiled and drew a small heart. “In my family, we say ‘Arigato’ for thank you.”

“Arigato,” Pippa repeated, rolling the sound on her tongue. She loved how different words felt, like little songs.

Pippa picked up the blue crayon that had rolled before. She pressed gently this time. She drew four boxes: Share, Listen, Help, and Breathe. She added picture clues—a book passed hand to hand, big ears, hands holding a toy together, and a fluffy cloud for breathing.

Suddenly, two more friends arrived, Sam and Aya. “We want to write!” Sam said.

“We want to draw!” Aya said.

Pippa felt a tiny tug in her tummy again. What if there were not enough turns? She touched her chest and took a small breath. “Stop. Breathe. Count,” she whispered. She smiled. “Let’s make a line. Everyone gets a turn. Fair is fair.”

They made a little turn line on the rug. Soft paws, tiny claws, and careful feet waited. They used quiet voices. They said “please” and “thank you” in many languages. They shared crayons. They listened. They helped.

When a crayon broke, Pippa didn’t shout. She paused, took a breath, and tried again. When someone made a wiggly line, they said, “It’s okay. We can fix it.” The study corner was full of gentle sounds—scritch-scratch, soft giggles, the whisper of pages turning.

At the end, everyone looked at the bright chart. It sparkled with colors and caring words. Pippa felt calm and proud. Her storm was gone. She puffed her feathers and said, “Today, we learned to handle big feelings. We used fairness. We volunteered to help. We loved all our words.”

Nia smiled. “And we did it together.”

Pippa nodded. “When I feel frustrated, I can stop, breathe, count, and try again. That’s how I stay kind and fair.”

Everyone cheered, in many languages: “Yay! Hooray! Bravo! Ole! Hurrah!”

The study corner glowed with peace.

The End.
