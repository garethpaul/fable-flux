# Benny Beaver's Big Apology

Benny Beaver loved making things with his busy little paws. Today, he was in the bright, colorful art room at his school. Sunshine streamed through the big windows, making the paint jars sparkle like jewels.

"Look at all these wonderful supplies!" Benny said, his eyes growing wide. There were soft brushes, squishy clay, shiny scissors, and tubes of paint in every color of the rainbow.

Benny's friend Ruby Rabbit was already at the art table, carefully painting a pretty flower. "Hi, Benny!" she said with a smile. "I'm making this for my mama."

Benny felt excited and wanted to start creating right away. He reached across the table quickly to grab the red paint. But oh no! His elbow bumped into Ruby's water cup.

SPLASH! 

The dirty paint water spilled all over Ruby's beautiful flower painting. The colors ran together, making muddy brown streaks across her careful work.

"My painting!" Ruby cried, her bottom lip trembling. "It's all ruined!"

Benny's tummy felt funny and tight. He looked at the mess he had made. Ruby's eyes filled with tears, and Benny felt very sorry inside.

At first, Benny wanted to say it wasn't his fault. The words almost came out of his mouth: "It was an accident!" But then he stopped. He took a deep breath and thought about what really happened.

"I was moving too fast," Benny said quietly to himself. "I should have been more careful."

Benny looked at Ruby's sad face and felt his heart squeeze. He knew what he needed to do.

"Ruby," Benny said softly, walking around the table to her. "I'm really, really sorry. I bumped your water cup because I was rushing. I should have asked you to pass the paint instead of reaching across."

Ruby wiped her eyes with the back of her paw. "It took me a long time to paint that flower," she sniffled.

"I know," Benny said, his voice gentle and kind. "And it was really beautiful. I feel bad that I messed it up. Can I help you make a new one? We could paint it together!"

Ruby looked at Benny's sincere face. She could see that he really meant his apology. "You really want to help me?" she asked.

"Yes!" Benny nodded eagerly. "And I promise to move more slowly and carefully this time."

Together, they cleaned up the spilled water with paper towels. Then Benny helped Ruby get fresh paper and new paint. This time, Benny moved his paws slowly and asked before reaching for anything.

"Could you please pass the yellow paint?" Benny asked politely.

"Of course!" Ruby smiled, carefully sliding the paint jar to him.

They painted side by side, making an even more beautiful flower than before. Ruby painted the petals while Benny added green leaves and a sunny yellow center.

"This one is even prettier!" Ruby said happily. "Thank you for saying sorry and helping me, Benny."

"Thank you for forgiving me," Benny replied. "I learned that when I make a mistake, saying sorry and trying to fix it makes everything better."

Mrs. Owl, their teacher, smiled as she watched them work together. "You both showed such kindness today," she said warmly.

From that day on, Benny always remembered to slow down, think before acting, and say sorry when he made mistakes. And Ruby knew that real friends apologize and help each other.

The End.
