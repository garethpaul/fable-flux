# Owl Hoots in the Car

Hootley was a wise old owl with soft gray feathers and big, kind eyes. One sunny morning, Hootley perched on the back seat of a cozy family car. The seats were warm. The air smelled like oranges from a snack bag. The car hummed, “Mmmm,” as it rolled down the street.

In the car sat Mia and her little brother, Ben. Mama drove, and Papa checked the map. Hootley blinked slowly and smiled.

“Good morning,” Hootley hooted gently. “How are your bodies and hearts today?”

“My tummy is wiggly,” said Mia. “I feel jumpy.”

“My head feels loud,” said Ben, covering his ears. “Too much noise!”

The radio was buzzing. The window was whistling. The snack bag crinkled. It felt busy.

Hootley tilted his head. “Let’s take care of us first. Self-care helps our day feel good.”

Mia whispered, “What is self-care?”

“It means being kind to your body, your mind, and your heart,” Hootley said. “We listen to what we need. We help ourselves calm and strong.”

Mama nodded. “That sounds just right.”

Hootley spread his soft wings. “Step one: quiet breath. In… and out.”

Mia and Ben breathed in like smelling flowers. They breathed out like blowing bubbles. “In… out… in… out…” Their shoulders softened. The hum of the car felt softer too.

“Step two,” Hootley hooted, “body wiggles.” He tapped his talons. “Let’s do gentle squeezes.”

They squeezed their hands, then let go. They hugged their shoulders, then relaxed. The seatbelts clicked softly as they wiggled their toes inside their shoes.

Ben giggled. “My toes say hello!”

“Step three: name the feeling,” Hootley said. “Mia?”

“I feel jumpy,” Mia said. “I need a calm plan.”

“Ben?” Hootley asked.

“I feel noisy,” Ben said. “I need quiet.”

“Good listening,” said Hootley. “Self-care is fair care.” He blinked. “Justice means fair for everyone. We can make a fair plan.”

Papa turned down the radio. “Let’s keep it soft.”

Mama cracked a window just a little. A cool breeze kissed their cheeks. It smelled like cut grass. “Ahh,” sighed Mia.

Ben nodded. “Better.”

“Step four,” Hootley hooted, “use your creativity.” He blinked and smiled. “We can make a Peace Plan Song!”

Mia clapped softly. “Yes!”

Ben wiggled his fingers like piano keys. “Plink, plonk!”

Hootley tapped a slow beat on the armrest. “Tap, tap. Tap, tap.” He sang in a gentle voice.

“When I feel jumpy,
I breathe in slow.
When I feel noisy,
I let it go.”

Mia added, “I hug my shoulders,
I count to three,
I show my body
kind care for me.”

Ben added in a whisper, “I whisper please,
I whisper wait,
I ask for help,
I feel so great.”

They sang together. Their voices were soft and sweet. The car felt warm and safe. The breeze made Mia’s hair tickle her cheeks. Ben’s eyes grew bright and calm.

Just then, the snack bag crinkled again. “I want the orange!” Ben said.

“I want it too!” said Mia.

They both reached. Their hands bumped. “Ouch,” said Mia. “Hey!”

Hootley blinked. “Peaceful solution time,” he said. “Justice is fair. Let’s make a fair snack plan.”

Ben looked at the oranges. “Share?”

Mia nodded. “Yes. We take turns.”

Hootley hooted. “We can use words. Words are kind.”

Mia said, “Ben, you may have the first slice. Then I have the next.”

Ben smiled. “Thank you.” He handed her a slice too. The orange was juicy and sweet. Their fingers got sticky. They giggled and wiped with a napkin.

“Step five,” Hootley said, “make a routine. A routine is a gentle plan we do again and again.”

Papa smiled. “What is our car routine?”

Hootley lifted a wing and counted on his feathers:
- Breathe in… breathe out.
- Gentle squeezes.
- Name the feeling.
- Use creative words or songs.
- Make a fair plan.

Mia repeated, “Breathe, squeeze, name, create, fair plan.”

Ben repeated too, with a grin, “Breathe, squeeze, name, create, fair plan!”

The car hummed along. Trees whooshed by like green paint strokes. The sun made warm stripes across the seats. Mia felt calm. Ben felt cozy.

At a stoplight, a loud truck rumbled next to them. It went “GRRRR-ROOM!” Ben’s eyes went wide.

Hootley whispered, “What do we do?”

“Breathe,” said Ben. He breathed in and out.

“Squeeze,” said Mia. She hugged her shoulders.

“Name it,” Ben said. “I feel scared.”

“Create,” Mia said. “Let’s sing our Peace Plan Song.”

They sang softly. The light turned green. The truck drove away. The rumble faded. Their bodies felt safe again.

Mama smiled at Hootley in the mirror. “Your plan helps everyone.”

Hootley nodded. “Self-care helps us be kind and strong. When we care for us, we can care for others too.”

Ben leaned back and yawned. “I feel sleepy.”

Mia whispered, “Me too.”

Hootley tucked his wings like a blanket. “Rest is self-care,” he said. “Close your eyes. I will watch.”

The car hummed, “Mmmm,” like a lullaby. The breeze was cool. The orange smell was sweet. Mia and Ben’s breaths were slow and soft. Hootley’s eyes were gentle and wise.

When they reached home, Mama parked the car. “We did it,” she said. “A calm, kind ride.”

Hootley hooted proudly. “Today we learned a self-care routine. We used peaceful solutions and creative expression. That is fair. That is just. That is kind.”

Mia smiled. “Breathe, squeeze, name, create, fair plan.”

Ben whispered, “I can do it every day.”

Hootley nodded. “Yes. Every day.”

The End.
