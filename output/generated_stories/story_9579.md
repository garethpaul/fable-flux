# Daisy's Special Discovery

Daisy the deer fawn had big, curious eyes and soft brown spots on her back. Today was very special - she was staying in Grandma Rabbit's cozy guest room for the first time all by herself!

The guest room smelled like lavender and sunshine. Daisy touched the soft blue blanket with her tiny hooves. Everything felt new and exciting.

But Daisy had a problem. She wanted to brush her teeth before bed, just like Mama taught her. She looked around the pretty room with its flowery wallpaper, but she couldn't find a toothbrush anywhere.

"Oh no," Daisy whispered, her ears drooping. "How can I keep my teeth clean and shiny?"

She peeked under the bed. Just dust bunnies. She looked in the dresser drawers. Only extra pillowcases. Her little heart felt worried.

Then Daisy noticed something interesting on the windowsill - an old wooden box with tiny holes in the top. It rattled softly when she nudged it with her nose.

"What could this be?" she wondered aloud.

Carefully, Daisy opened the box. Inside were small, rough twigs that smelled minty fresh. She had never seen anything like them before.

Just then, Grandma Rabbit hopped into the room. "I see you found my special box, little one!"

"Grandma, what are these?" Daisy asked, her nose twitching with curiosity.

Grandma Rabbit smiled warmly. "Those are chew sticks, dear. Long ago, before there were toothbrushes, forest animals used these special twigs to clean their teeth. My grandmother showed me how to use them when I was young, just like you."

Daisy's eyes grew wide with wonder. "Really? How do they work?"

"Watch carefully," Grandma said, taking a twig. "You chew on the end gently until it becomes soft and frayed, like a little brush. Then you can clean your teeth naturally! The mint leaves I wrap them with keep them fresh and make your breath smell nice."

Daisy picked up a twig with her mouth. It felt bumpy and tasted like cool mint. She chewed the end carefully, just like Grandma showed her. Soon, the tip became soft and brush-like.

"I did it!" Daisy exclaimed happily. "This is like magic!"

"You're such a smart little fawn," Grandma praised. "You discovered something new and learned to take care of yourself. These twigs are special because we can use them again and again, and when we're done, they go back to the earth to help new plants grow."

Daisy felt proud and independent. She had solved her problem all by herself and learned about an old tradition too. The mint twig made her teeth feel clean and her mouth taste fresh.

"Thank you for sharing this tradition with me, Grandma," Daisy said, snuggling into the soft bed. "I love discovering new things!"

"And I love sharing old wisdom with curious young ones like you," Grandma replied, tucking the blanket around Daisy's spotted back. "Sweet dreams, little discoverer."

As Daisy drifted off to sleep, she felt happy and confident. She had learned that sometimes the best solutions come from looking around carefully, asking questions, and being open to new discoveries. The guest room didn't feel strange anymore - it felt like a place full of wonderful surprises.

The End.
