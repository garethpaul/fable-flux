# Binky Bunny Tells the Truth

Binky Bunny was a determined rabbit. His ears stood tall like little towers. His soft paws went pat-pat on the bathroom tile. The bathroom smelled like minty soap and warm towels. Sunlight made the mirror sparkle.

“Today I will brush and wash all by myself,” Binky said. He puffed his cheeks and nodded. “I can do it!”

He hopped onto his little step stool. The stool wobbled a tiny bit, wobble-wobble, but Binky held on. He turned on the water. Swish-shhhh! Cool water danced into the sink. He smelled the bubble soap. It smelled like oranges.

Binky squeezed toothpaste on his brush. Oops! A big, silly mountain of paste plopped onto the counter. Plop! It looked like a white, minty hat.

Binky blinked. “Oh no,” he whispered. His tummy felt fluttery. He looked at the door. Mama Bunny was in the hallway, humming a gentle song.

Binky thought, I could hide it. I could wipe it away and say, “Nothing happened.” He picked up a towel. It was soft and warm. His paws shook.

His heart said, Be honest, Binky. His ears drooped. He took a slow breath. In and out. He put down the towel.

“Mama?” Binky called. His voice was small. “Mama, I made a mess.”

Mama Bunny came in with kind eyes and a soft smile. “Thank you for telling me, Binky,” she said. “Tell me what happened.”

Binky pointed. “I squeezed too hard. The toothpaste went plop. I felt scared to say so.”

Mama bent down and hugged him. Her fur was cozy. “I am proud of your honesty,” she said. “Telling the truth shows a brave heart.”

Binky’s tummy felt warm now, like cocoa. “I want to fix it,” he said. “I want to try again.”

“That is a good plan,” Mama said. “We can solve this problem together.”

Binky and Mama got a washcloth. Squish-squish went the cloth as they cleaned the counter. They rinsed the sink. Swish-shhhh went the water again. The minty hat was gone. The counter shone.

Binky smiled. “I’m grateful you helped me, Mama.”

Mama smiled back. “I’m grateful you were honest. Now, let’s try with a small pea-sized dot of toothpaste.”

Binky squeezed just a tiny dot. “Like this?” he asked.

“Just right,” Mama said.

Binky brushed. Scritch-scritch went the brush. Little bubbles tickled his tongue. “It tastes minty,” he giggled. He looked in the mirror. Bubbles made a funny white mustache. He laughed. “I look like a grandpa bunny!”

Mama laughed too. “Grandpa Binky, keep brushing!”

Binky rinsed. Swish, spit, sploosh. He wiped his face with a towel that smelled like sunshine. “All clean!” he said.

Next, Binky washed his paws with orange soap. Squirt, rub, rub, rub. The bubbles felt silky. He sang a hand-washing song. “Tops and bottoms, in between!” His paws smelled like oranges and happiness.

He combed his ears gently. Smooth, smooth. He liked how soft they felt. “This is good self-care,” Mama said. “We care for our bodies with love.”

Binky nodded. “Self-care helps me feel strong and calm.”

There was a soft tap at the door. Cousin Nib, a tiny rabbit with bright eyes, peeked in. “I can’t get my brush to work,” Nib said. “I squeezed and squeezed. Now there is no paste left.”

Binky looked at the little empty tube in Nib’s paw. Nib’s nose trembled. “I tried to hide the tube,” Nib whispered. “I feel bad.”

Binky remembered his own mess. He took a slow breath. “It is okay to feel bad,” he said. “Honesty helps us feel better. You can tell Auntie. She will listen.”

Nib’s eyes went shiny. “What if she is mad?”

Mama Bunny knelt. “I will go with you,” she said. “Honesty helps people help you.”

Binky hopped to the cabinet and took out a new tube. He held it out. “Here,” he said. “You can use ours. I want to be generous.”

Nib smiled a tiny smile. “Thank you, Binky. I am grateful.” Nib hugged the tube like a treasure.

Binky showed Nib how to squeeze a pea-sized dot. “See? Just a little,” he said. Nib tried. A neat dot sat on the brush like a tiny snowball.

“You did it!” Binky cheered.

Nib brushed. Scritch-scritch. “It tastes minty!” Nib said, giggling.

Later, Auntie came to the door. Nib stood up tall. “Auntie,” Nib said, “I squeezed all the paste. I hid the tube. I am sorry.”

Auntie hugged Nib. “Thank you for being honest,” she said. “We can get more. Next time, ask for help.”

Nib nodded. “Okay.”

Binky felt proud. He felt light, like a hop. He turned to the mirror and smiled. His teeth were shiny. His paws were clean. His heart felt clean too.

Mama Bunny patted his shoulder. “What did we learn today?” she asked.

Binky held up a paw and counted. “One: Be honest, even when it’s hard. Two: Be grateful for help. Three: Take care of your body. Four: Be generous and share.”

Mama smiled. “That is a wonderful story.”

Binky grinned. “I will tell it again at bedtime,” he said. “Once upon a time, a determined rabbit told the truth…”

The End.
