# The Colorful Plan in the Shed

Pip was a curious fox kit with bright eyes and soft, russet fur. One sunny morning, Pip padded to the garden shed. The shed smelled like warm wood and dry straw. Sunlight peeked through the window and made little dust sparkles in the air.

Pip pushed the door. Creak! Inside were shelves, boxes, and a big easel with clean paper. There were jars of buttons, ribbons, and brushes. There were pots of paint with bright lids—red, blue, yellow, and green.

Pip’s whiskers twitched. “I want to make art,” Pip said. “But what will I paint?”

Pip set a goal. “I will make a big picture for our family wall. I will show our garden.” Pip said it out loud, because saying a goal out loud felt strong.

Just then, Pip heard tiny paws. Tippy-tap, tippy-tap. Pip’s friends came in. There was Mallow the mouse, Squeak the squirrel, and Dot the duckling. They liked the shed too.

“It smells like sunshine,” said Mallow, sniffing the air.

“It sounds like quiet rain,” said Squeak, hearing a gentle drip from a roof bucket.

“It feels cool,” said Dot, touching the smooth table. “What are we doing today?”

Pip’s tail swished. “I want to paint our garden! I want big red poppies, buzzing bees, and the blue birdbath. Will you help?”

“Yes!” squeaked Squeak.

“Yes!” peeped Dot.

“Yes!” nodded Mallow.

Pip smiled. “Let’s make a plan.” Pip loved plans. Plans helped big dreams become true.

Goal-setting time! Pip said, “Our goal is one big picture for the wall. We will share the space. We will take turns. We will talk and listen. Then we will smile.”

Mallow squeaked, “I can paint tiny things. I am good at tiny.”

Squeak said, “I like green. I can paint leaves.”

Dot flapped. “I can do splashes in the birdbath!”

Pip looked at the blank paper. It felt too white. Pip felt a little flutter in their tummy. “What if it gets messy?” Pip asked.

Dot said softly, “Messy can be fun.”

Squeak said, “We can try. If we don’t like it, we can talk and fix.”

Mallow held up a small brush. “We can take turns.”

Pip took a deep breath. The air smelled like paint and pine. “Okay. Let’s make a turn chart.” Pip drew four little paw prints on a scrap paper: Pip, Mallow, Squeak, Dot. “Pip starts, then Mallow, then Squeak, then Dot. We each get five swishes. After five swishes, say ‘Turn!’”

Everyone nodded. “Turn!” they practiced. It sounded like a bell. Turn!

Pip dipped a brush into blue. Swish, swish, swish! Pip painted the round birdbath. Drip. One drop slid down. Pip laughed. “Oops! Drip looks like a sparkle.” Swish, swish. Five swishes. “Turn!”

Mallow stepped close. The shed floor felt cool under tiny feet. Mallow dipped into yellow. Tap, tap. “Little bees,” Mallow said. Dot’s feathers shivered with joy. Mallow made tiny dots with tiny hums. Hummm, hummm. “Turn!”

Squeak took green and made many leaves. Swish, swish, swish-swirl. The brush made soft whisper sounds on paper. “Leaves for shade,” Squeak said. “I like shade.” “Turn!”

Dot took a wide brush and dipped into blue again. Splish! Splash! Dot made water ripples. The paint shivered like puddles after rain. “Turn!”

They kept going. Sometimes Pip wanted to swish more. Pip’s paws itched. But Pip waited and watched. Taking turns felt kind.

Then Squeak said, “I want to make a big carrot.” Dot peeped, “But we said a garden with flowers and a birdbath.” Mallow whispered, “Carrots do grow in gardens.”

Pip felt the flutter again. The plan was flowers, bees, and a birdbath. A carrot did not fit the picture in Pip’s head. Pip’s ears tipped down. “I’m not sure,” Pip said.

Squeak’s tail drooped. “I really want to paint a carrot. It is my favorite.”

Pip took another deep breath. The paint smelled sharp and sweet. Pip remembered the goal: one big picture, shared with friends. Pip remembered creative-expression: making feelings into colors and shapes. Pip said, “Let’s talk and make a new plan.”

Negotiation time! Pip put a paw on the table. “We can add one thing each that we love. I love poppies. Mallow loves bees. Dot loves water. Squeak loves carrots. If we pick a corner for favorites, we can keep our garden idea and show our hearts.”

Squeak’s eyes lit up. “A carrot corner!”

Dot peeped, “A water corner!”

Mallow squeaked, “A bee corner!”

Pip grinned. “A poppy corner!”

They drew light pencil boxes, one at each corner. “We still take turns,” Pip said. “We each get five swishes in our own corner, and five in the middle where we share.”

“Deal!” said Squeak.

“Deal!” said Dot.

“Deal!” said Mallow.

They painted again. The brushes made soft scritch-scratch sounds. The shed was filled with colors and little happy noises. Pip painted red poppies with round, warm centers. Mallow dotted bees with tiny wings that looked like sugar glass. Squeak made a bright orange carrot with green top hair. Dot made water sprinkles that looked like giggles.

In the middle, they painted a sunshine that touched everything. Yellow gleamed on the birdbath. Green shone on leaves. Red glowed on poppies. Orange smiled on the carrot. The picture looked full and friendly.

At last, Pip put the brush down. “Turn!” Pip said, and then laughed. “No more turns. We are done.”

They all stepped back. The paper smelled like fresh art. The colors felt warm and happy. Pip’s heart felt big. “We did it,” Pip said softly. “Our goal is done.”

Mallow clapped tiny paws. Squeak wiggled. Dot fluffed feathers. They carried the painting to the wall. Pip smoothed the tape. The picture hung straight.

Pip said, “We made a plan. We set a goal. We took turns. We listened. We talked. We shared. We made art that shows who we are.”

Squeak touched the carrot corner and smiled. “Creative means we can show our favorites.”

Dot nodded. “Creative means we can try and change and try again.”

Mallow added, “Creative means tiny dots can be big feelings.”

Pip’s tail swished. “Creative means our art is our voice.”

They all looked at their garden picture. It was colorful and kind.

The End.
