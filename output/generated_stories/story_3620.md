# Rosie Rabbit's Beautiful Garden Plan

Rosie Rabbit lived in a cozy apartment with a sunny balcony garden. Every morning, she would hop outside and smell the fresh air. "Mmm," she would say, twitching her pink nose. "What a perfect day for gardening!"

One bright Tuesday morning, Rosie looked at her balcony and had a wonderful idea. "I want to make my garden even more beautiful!" she said, clapping her furry paws together. "But how should I do it?"

Rosie sat down on her favorite little stool and thought carefully. She remembered what her mama always said: "The best adventures start with a good plan, little bunny."

"I need to make a plan!" Rosie announced to the morning sun. She hopped inside and found her box of colorful crayons and a big piece of white paper.

Back on the balcony, Rosie spread the paper on her small table. She picked up a green crayon and started drawing. "First, I'll draw my balcony," she said, making careful lines. The paper felt smooth under her paws, and the crayon made a satisfying scratching sound.

"Now, where should I put new flowers?" Rosie wondered aloud. She drew circles for flower pots in different spots. "Red flowers here," she said, coloring with her red crayon. "Yellow flowers there, and maybe some purple ones by the railing!"

A gentle breeze made her whiskers tickle. Rosie giggled and continued planning. She drew a small watering can, a tiny shovel, and even a little sign that said "Rosie's Garden."

"This looks wonderful!" she said, holding up her colorful drawing. "But I should think about what my flowers will need."

Rosie tapped her crayon against her chin, thinking hard. "Flowers need sunshine," she said, looking around her balcony. She drew yellow suns in the spots that got the most light. "And they need water," she added, drawing blue raindrops near her watering can drawing.

Then Rosie had another idea. "What if I make the pots pretty too?" She drew beautiful patterns on each flower pot - stripes, dots, and swirls. "I can paint these designs on real pots!"

After finishing her plan, Rosie hopped excitedly around her balcony. "Now I know exactly what to do!" she said. She could almost smell the sweet flowers and feel the soft soil between her paws.

Rosie carefully folded her plan and put it in her pocket. Then she hopped to the garden store with her mama. Using her drawing, she picked out seeds for red roses, yellow sunflowers, and purple pansies. She chose paint to decorate her pots, just like in her picture.

Back home, Rosie followed her plan step by step. She painted the pots with beautiful designs, planted the seeds in the sunny spots she had marked, and watered them gently. Everything looked just like her drawing!

"My plan worked perfectly!" Rosie said, wiping her happy, dirt-covered paws. Her balcony garden looked more beautiful than ever, with colorful painted pots waiting for flowers to grow.

That evening, as the sun set orange and pink, Rosie sat in her garden feeling proud. "Planning made everything so much easier," she told herself. "Now I can't wait to see my flowers bloom!"

From that day on, whenever Rosie wanted to try something new, she always made a plan first. And her beautiful balcony garden reminded her every day that good things happen when you think ahead.

The End.
