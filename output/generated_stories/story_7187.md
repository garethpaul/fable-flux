# Benny Bear Says "No Thank You!"

Benny Bear was brushing his teeth in the bright bathroom. The white tiles sparkled under the warm light. He loved the minty taste of his special bear toothpaste.

"Scrub, scrub, scrub," Benny sang as he moved his toothbrush up and down. His mama had taught him to brush for two whole minutes to keep his teeth strong and healthy.

Suddenly, his older cousin Max Bear peeked through the bathroom door. "Hey Benny! Want to try my grown-up toothpaste? It's way cooler than your baby stuff."

Benny looked at the big tube Max was holding. It had scary pictures and words he couldn't read. His tummy felt funny - not the good kind of funny.

"Um..." Benny said quietly. He wasn't sure what to say.

"Come on, don't be a baby," Max said, squeezing some onto Benny's toothbrush. "All the big bears use this kind."

Benny's nose wrinkled. The toothpaste smelled very strong, like the cleaning stuff Mama used. It made his eyes water a little.

"I don't think I should," Benny said softly, but Max wasn't listening.

"Just try it! What's the worst that could happen?" Max laughed.

Benny felt his heart beating fast. He remembered what Mama Bear always told him: "Your body belongs to you, Benny. If something doesn't feel right, you can always say no."

Benny took a deep breath and stood up tall. "No thank you, Max," he said in a clear, strong voice. "I like my own toothpaste better."

Max rolled his eyes. "You're being silly."

"No, I'm being smart," Benny said, feeling braver. "This toothpaste might hurt my teeth. It's made for grown-up bears, not little bears like me."

He rinsed off his toothbrush under the warm water and put his own safe toothpaste back on it. The familiar minty smell made him feel much better.

"My toothpaste has special ingredients that help my baby teeth grow strong," Benny explained. "Mama Bear and Dr. Honey Bear picked it just for me."

Max looked surprised. "Oh. I didn't know that."

"It's okay," Benny said kindly. "But I know what's best for my body. And saying 'no thank you' is always okay when something doesn't feel right."

Just then, Mama Bear appeared in the doorway. "How's tooth brushing going, my sweet cub?"

"Great, Mama!" Benny smiled, showing his clean teeth. "Max offered me his toothpaste, but I said no thank you. I told him my special toothpaste is perfect for me."

Mama Bear's eyes sparkled with pride. "That was very smart thinking, Benny. You listened to your feelings and made a good choice for your body."

Max nodded. "Sorry, Benny. You're right - you know what's best for you."

Benny finished brushing and rinsed his mouth with cool water. "It feels good to speak up for myself," he said happily.

"Speaking up takes courage," Mama Bear said, giving him a warm hug. "I'm proud of you for being assertive and keeping yourself safe."

Benny looked at himself in the mirror. His teeth were sparkling clean, and he felt proud and strong inside. He had listened to his feelings, used his thinking skills, and said "no" when he needed to.

"I'm a brave little bear," Benny said to his reflection. "And I can always trust myself to make good choices."

The End.
