# Polly's Patient Bath Time

Polly the parrot loved bright colors and making beautiful things. Her feathers were red, blue, and yellow like a rainbow! But today, Polly had a big problem in the bathroom.

"Oh no!" squawked Polly, looking at herself in the mirror. "My beautiful feathers are all messy and dirty from playing outside!"

Polly's feathers were covered in mud and leaves. She needed a bath, but she was scared of the water. The bathtub looked so big and deep!

"I don't want to get wet," Polly said sadly. "What if I can't fly with wet feathers? What if the water is too cold?"

Just then, Mama Bird came into the bathroom. "What's wrong, my colorful little one?" she asked gently.

"I need a bath, but I'm scared!" Polly chirped. "And I want to clean my feathers RIGHT NOW, but I don't know how!"

Mama Bird smiled. "Taking a bath needs patience, Polly. We'll go slowly, step by step. Being scared is okay - that just means you're being brave by trying something new!"

First, Mama Bird filled the bathtub with just a tiny bit of warm water. "Feel this," she said. Polly carefully touched the water with one toe.

"It's warm!" Polly said, surprised. "Like sunshine!"

"Now we wait for you to feel ready," Mama Bird said. "Patience means taking our time."

Polly watched the water sparkle in the light. She saw how the bubbles danced and popped. "The bubbles look like tiny clouds," she said, feeling a little braver.

Slowly, Polly put one foot in the water, then the other. "It feels nice!" she chirped happily.

"See? Your bright feathers make you special," Mama Bird said. "And being careful makes you smart. We all do things differently, and that's wonderful!"

Polly began to splash gently. The warm water felt good on her muddy feathers. She watched the dirt wash away, revealing her beautiful red, blue, and yellow colors again.

"Look, Mama! I'm being patient!" Polly said proudly. "I'm not rushing, and I'm not scared anymore!"

Mama Bird helped Polly wash behind her wings and under her tail feathers. They sang a happy song together:

"Splash, splash, in the bath,
Take your time, don't be fast,
Patience helps us do our best,
Being different is the best!"

When bath time was over, Polly looked at herself in the mirror. Her feathers were clean and bright again!

"I did it!" Polly squawked joyfully. "I was patient, and I was brave! Even though I was scared at first, I tried something new!"

"You solved your problem by being patient and asking for help," Mama Bird said, wrapping Polly in a soft, fluffy towel. "Your beautiful, colorful feathers make you special, and your brave heart makes you amazing!"

Polly felt proud and happy. She learned that being patient helped her feel less scared. She also learned that being different - like having the most colorful feathers in the whole tree - made her wonderful and unique.

From that day on, whenever Polly felt worried about trying something new, she remembered to take her time, be patient, and celebrate what made her special.

"Patience and courage help me shine!" Polly would sing, dancing in front of the bathroom mirror with her beautiful, clean feathers sparkling in the light.

The End.
