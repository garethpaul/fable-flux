# Gigi’s Porch Promise

Gigi the thoughtful giraffe loved mornings on the peaceful front porch. The porch creaked softly under her hooves. The air smelled like warm toast and sweet flowers. Birds chirped. A soft breeze tickled her ears.

“Good morning, porch,” Gigi whispered. “I will be gentle and kind today.”

Gigi had a little basket for porch time. Inside were crayons, books, and a tiny bell. She liked to ring the bell one time and say, “Ready to learn!”

Today, Gigi wanted to draw a sunny picture. She set out crayons in a neat rainbow line. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple. She smiled and hummed.

Clip-clop. Her friend Tippy the turtle came up the steps. “Hi, Gigi,” Tippy said, slow and warm. “May I sit?”

“Yes,” said Gigi. “There is space. Please wipe your feet on the mat.” She pointed to the mat that said, “Welcome.”

Tippy wiped, then sat. “Thank you for asking me kindly,” said Tippy. “That feels good.”

Gigi nodded. “Respect means we care about each other,” she said softly. “We can use kind words and gentle bodies.”

They drew together. Gigi drew a big sun. Tippy drew a tiny leaf. The crayons made soft scratchy sounds on the paper. A butterfly floated by. The porch smelled like lemon tea.

Soon, Benny the bunny bounced up. Boing, boing, boing! “Hi, friends! I want to draw too!” Benny hopped onto the porch. His paws were dusty. He bumped the crayon line. The crayons rolled everywhere—plunk, plink, plonk! One crayon tumbled under a chair.

Gigi’s neck stretched tall. Her heart felt tight. She frowned. “Benny,” she said, “please slow down. You spilled our crayons.”

Benny’s ears drooped. “Oh,” he said, “I didn’t see.” He scuffed a paw. “I’m sorry.”

Gigi took a deep breath. In, out. The breeze touched her cheeks. She put a hoof on her chest. “I feel upset,” she said. “I worked hard to make it neat. I need help to fix it.”

Tippy nodded. “We can fix it together,” he said. “Let’s use gentle paws and careful hooves.”

Benny looked up. “I can help,” he said. “What is the plan?”

Gigi’s face softened. “Thank you for saying sorry,” she said. “That is respectful. Our plan can be: first, clean paws on the mat. Then, pick up crayons. Then, we draw.”

Benny hopped down and wiped his paws on the mat. Swish, swish. He came back, slow and careful. “I am ready,” he said.

They picked up the crayons. Benny reached under the chair for the runaway blue. “Got it!” he said. “Blue is back.” Gigi lined them up again, red to purple. She smiled. “Thank you. I feel calm now.”

Tippy rang the tiny bell one time. Ding. “Ready to learn,” he said.

Gigi looked at Benny. “Would you like to draw after you ask?” she said gently.

Benny nodded. “May I please draw with you?”

“Yes,” said Gigi. “Thank you for asking with respect.”

They drew new pictures. Gigi drew a tall tree. Tippy drew a slow, winding path. Benny drew a bouncy cloud. The porch felt happy again. The sun warmed their backs. A bird sang a sweet song, tweet-tweet, tweet.

After a while, a small gust came. Whoosh! Papers fluttered. One picture flapped toward the steps.

“I’ll get it!” Benny said. He paused. He looked at the mat. He looked at his paws. “I will go slow,” he said. He tiptoed, careful and light, and caught the picture. “Safe!” he cheered.

Gigi clapped her hooves. “I see your respect,” she said. “You moved carefully. You looked. You thought. Thank you.”

Benny beamed. “I learned,” he said. “Slow and gentle on the porch.”

Gigi nodded. “And we keep our things in order,” she added. “That helps everyone.”

Tippy smiled. “Organization is a kind of respect,” he said. “It shows we care for our space and friends.”

When they finished, Gigi said, “Let’s tidy up.” They put crayons back in the basket. Snug, snug, snug. They stacked books by size. Small to tall. They folded papers and put them in a folder. The porch looked neat and calm.

Benny looked at Gigi. “I am sorry I bumped your crayons,” he said once more. “I will ask and move slow next time.”

Gigi’s eyes were warm. “Thank you for making amends,” she said. “That means you fix the hurt. You helped clean, you asked kindly, and you learned. I respect you.”

Benny’s ears stood up. “I respect you too,” he said. “And I respect the porch.”

Tippy chuckled. “We all learned today.”

Gigi took a deep breath and smiled at the quiet porch. “Today I learned that respect is using kind words, gentle bodies, and tidy habits,” she said. “Respect helps everyone feel safe and happy.”

The friends sat together, sipping cool water. The porch smelled like flowers again. The bell was still. Their hearts felt light.

Gigi whispered, “Porch promise: be kind, be careful, keep it clean.”

All three said together, “We promise.”

The End.
