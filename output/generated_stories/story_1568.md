# Joey Roo Builds Right

Joey Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and a bouncy tail. Today, Joey hopped to the study corner. The study corner was warm and cozy. A soft rug tickled Joey’s toes. Sunlight made bright squares on the floor. Crayons smelled waxy and sweet. Blocks made a gentle clack-clack in a wooden bin.

Joey wiggled. “I will build!” he said. “I will build something strong and kind.”

Mama Roo peeked in. “What will you build, Joey?”

Joey tapped his chin. “A reading fort! A place to share books. Everyone can sit inside. We can look at pictures. We can use quiet voices.”

Mama Roo smiled. “That is a good plan. Building is more than stacking. It is using your brain and your heart.”

Joey’s ears perked. “My heart?”

“Yes,” said Mama Roo. “We build with care. We build for others. That is civic responsibility. That means we help our whole home. We also tell the truth and fix our mistakes. That is integrity.”

Joey nodded. “I can do that!”

Joey poured blocks onto the rug. Clack-clack-clack! The blocks were smooth and cool. He felt the edges with his paws. He made a floor with big, flat blocks. He pressed them down. “Stay, please,” he whispered.

He started a wall. One block, two blocks, three blocks. The wall wobbled a little. Joey paused. He took a deep breath. He looked around the study corner. There were books, a little lamp, and a basket of soft pillows. “My fort needs to be safe,” he said.

Joey scooted the wall closer to the rug’s thick edge. He made the base wide. Tap, tap. “Strong and steady,” he said.

Thump, thump. Joey’s friend, Wally Wombat, waddled in. “Hi, Joey! Can I play?”

“Yes!” said Joey. “We are building a reading fort for everyone.”

Wally clapped. “I like building fast. Zip, zip!” He stacked tall skinny towers. They looked very high.

Joey looked at the towers. “Wally, they are tall, but they might fall.”

“I want tall!” Wally said. He set one more block on top. The tower wiggled. Wiggle-wobble—crash! Blocks tumbled. Thump, thump, thump!

Wally’s eyes went wide. “Oh no.”

Joey put a paw on Wally’s shoulder. “It is okay. We will fix it. We tell the truth, and we make it right.”

Wally nodded. “I knocked it down. I am sorry.”

“Thank you for telling the truth,” said Joey. “Let’s build with a strong base. Big blocks on the bottom. Little blocks on top. We can also make a door so friends can go in and out.”

They tried again. Joey showed Wally how to make corners. “See? The blocks hug each other,” Joey said. “Hug, hug.” The corners felt firm under his paws.

Their friend, Kiki Koala, peeked in. “It smells like crayons,” she said. “What are you building?”

“A reading fort for everyone,” said Joey. “Would you like to help?”

Kiki smiled. “Yes! I can bring pillows.”

Kiki carried soft pillows to the fort. They were fluffy and smelled like clean cotton. She placed them inside. “Ooh, cozy!”

Wally said, “I will make a window.” He left a square space on one wall. Sunlight came through and made a golden patch. Dust danced like tiny stars.

Joey added a roof with wide, flat blocks and a big book to cover the top. He patted the roof. “Tap, tap. Is it strong?” He listened. No wobble. No creak. Just quiet.

Mama Roo came back. “This looks safe and kind. Did you ask before using the big book on top?”

Joey’s ears twitched. He had not asked. He felt a little twist in his tummy. “I will ask now,” he said.

He hopped to the shelf. “Excuse me, shelf. May we use the big book for our roof? We will be gentle.” Then he turned to Mama Roo. “May we borrow the big book?”

Mama Roo nodded. “Thank you for asking. That is integrity. You told the truth and asked to share.”

Joey smiled. “We will take good care.” He stroked the cover. It felt smooth and cool.

Soon, the fort was ready. The door was wide. The window was bright. Pillows made soft nests inside. Joey made a little sign with a crayon. The crayon felt waxy and warm in his paw. He wrote slowly: “Reading Fort. Please Share.”

Wally read the sign aloud. “Please share.” He grinned. “That is civic responsibility, too. We made a fort for everyone.”

Kiki hugged a pillow. “Can we try it?”

“Let’s test it,” said Joey. They crawled inside. It was quiet and calm. They could hear the soft hum of the lamp and the gentle rustle of pages. Joey felt happy and proud.

Suddenly, Joey noticed a small crack in a corner. “Oh! The corner is loose.”

He crawled out and spoke kindly. “Friends, we need to fix this. Safety first.”

Wally held the wall. Kiki passed a big block. Joey moved the little block to the top and put the big block at the bottom. “Big on bottom, little on top,” he said. He pressed the corner. It felt firm. “There! Better.”

Mama Roo clapped softly. “You built with care. You told the truth. You made a space for everyone. That is building, integrity, and civic responsibility.”

Joey’s tail gave a happy thump. “We built right,” he said. “We built to share.”

They opened a book together. The pages made a gentle swish. Joey read the pictures and said, “Look, a sun! Look, a tree!” His friends giggled.

They took turns. They used quiet voices. They kept the door open for others. When they were done, Joey said, “Let’s clean up so the next friends can build, too.”

They returned extra blocks to the bin. Clack-clack. They placed the big book back on the shelf. Joey patted it and whispered, “Thank you.”

Mama Roo gave Joey a warm hug. “What did you learn today?”

Joey smiled. “Build strong. Tell the truth. Help everyone.”

“That is a wonderful lesson,” said Mama Roo.

Joey looked at his friends and the cozy study corner. His heart felt big and bright. “Tomorrow,” he said, “we can build again!”

The End.
