# Tortoise Tim and the Turn-Taking Game

In a sunny, playful living room, soft carpet hugged little toes. Pillows made colorful hills, and a tiny drum sat beside shiny blocks that clicked and clacked. A wise tortoise named Tortoise Tim moved slowly across the rug. His shell was smooth and warm like a toasted bun, and his eyes twinkled kindly.

“Good morning,” said Tortoise Tim in a calm, gentle voice. “Let’s play and practice taking turns.”

Two friends, Mia and Ben, bounced with excitement. Mia held up the drumsticks. Tap-tap! The drum made a happy thump. Ben stacked red, blue, and yellow blocks, click-click-click, as they touched.

“I want the drum first!” said Mia.

“I want it now too!” said Ben, reaching out. His fingers wiggled like little worms, eager to grab.

Tortoise Tim tucked his chin and breathed in slowly. In… and out… soft like a breeze. “Hmm,” he hummed. “I hear two wants at the same time.”

Mia’s cheeks puffed. Ben frowned. The room felt tight, like a balloon filled too full.

Tortoise Tim smiled. “Let’s try our Turn-Taking Game. It helps us share. It helps us be kind. We can use the Sand Timer.” He lifted a small, starry-blue sand timer from a basket. The sand sparkled like sugar.

“How does it work?” asked Mia, eyes wide.

“We watch the sand. When it falls, we switch,” said Tortoise Tim. “First, we breathe. Then we speak kindly.”

He showed them. “Step one: Turtle Breath. In… and out…” He breathed in through his nose, slow and steady. Mia copied. Ben tried too. Their shoulders dropped. The room felt soft again.

“Step two: Kind Words. We say, ‘You go, then I go.’”

Mia looked at Ben. “You go, then I go,” she said, tapping the drumsticks together—tap-tap.

Ben nodded. “You go, then I go,” he echoed, hands moving away from the drum.

Tortoise Tim clapped softly—pat, pat, pat. “Lovely. Mia, you can drum first. Ben, you can build while you wait. When the sand is gone, we switch.”

Mia tapped the drum—thump-thump, thumpty-thump! The sound was deep like a heartbeat. Ben stacked blocks, click, clack, click, making a tower taller than a teddy bear. He kept his eyes on the glittering sand. Down it slipped, hush… hush…

Ben felt wiggly. Waiting was hard. He pressed his hands together. “I want a turn,” he whispered.

Tortoise Tim nodded. “Waiting can feel big. Try Turtle Breath.” They breathed together: in… out… slow and low. Ben felt his belly go up and down like a small boat on gentle water.

The last grain of sand fell—plink!

“Time to switch,” said Tortoise Tim.

Mia grinned and passed the drumsticks to Ben. “Your turn,” she said.

“Thank you!” said Ben. He began to drum—boom, tap, boom-tap! Mia giggled and clapped. She picked up two blocks and added to the tower. Click! It stood even taller, wobbly but brave.

“Step three: Happy Helper Eyes,” said Tortoise Tim. “We watch our friend’s turn and cheer.”

Mia cheered, “Go, Ben!” Ben smiled so big his eyes sparkled. He cheered for Mia’s tower, “Nice blocks!”

When the sand fell again—plink!—they switched back. This time, they didn’t need a reminder. Mia handed the blocks to Ben and took the drum. Switch, smile, switch, smile. The room filled with friendly sounds: tap-tap, click-clack, and soft cheering.

Suddenly, the tower tumbled—crash! Blocks rolled like colorful apples. Mia’s eyes got wet. “Oh no!”

Ben froze, drumsticks in hand. He looked at Mia. He looked at the blocks. He wanted to keep drumming, but he remembered the game. Turtle Breath, Kind Words, Happy Helper Eyes.

Ben took a slow breath. “I can help,” he said. He set the drumsticks down gently and picked up a yellow block. “Your turn to build. I will wait.”

Mia blinked and smiled. “Thanks.” Together they clicked the blocks, steady and careful. The tower stood again, tall and proud.

Tortoise Tim beamed. “You used self-control. You were respectful. You took turns even when it was hard. That is wise.”

Mia nodded. “Taking turns feels good.”

Ben listened to his body. “Waiting felt hard, but breathing helped,” he said.

Tortoise Tim tapped the timer and made a little rhyme in his low, cozy voice:
“Breathe in slow, let worries go.
You go, I go, kind friends grow.”

They played a new game: Roll-the-Ball. The ball was soft and squishy, red like a strawberry. Squeeze—squish! Mia rolled it to Ben. Ben waited for the sand, then rolled it to Mia. The ball made a whisk-whish sound on the carpet. Their laughter bubbled like soda fizz.

At the end, they made a circle on the rug. Tortoise Tim spoke softly. “What did we learn today?”

Mia said, “We wait our turn.”

Ben said, “We use kind words: ‘You go, then I go.’”

Tortoise Tim nodded. “And we breathe when feelings get big.” He took a slow Turtle Breath. Mia and Ben joined, in… and out…

The living room felt warm and peaceful. The drum rested. The blocks stood. The ball waited. Their hearts felt light.

Mia patted Tim’s shell. “Thank you, Tortoise Tim.”

Ben added, “You are very wise.”

Tortoise Tim smiled. “You are wise too. Wise means you make good choices.”

Mia and Ben high-fived. “We can take turns tomorrow!” they said together.

“Yes,” said Tortoise Tim. “Every day is a chance to practice.”

The End.
