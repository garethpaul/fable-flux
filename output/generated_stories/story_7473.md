# Timo Turtle's Treehouse Plan

Morning mist hugged the forest. Dew drops sparkled like tiny stars. In the middle of the trees stood a wooden treehouse. It smelled like pine and warm sunshine. Inside lived Timo Turtle. Timo was shy. He liked quiet. He liked thinking.

Today was Timo’s special day. Each year, Timo’s family shared a tradition. They told stories in the treehouse about the forest and how to care for it. They called it Sharing Day.

Timo peeked out the round window. He heard soft chirps and rustling leaves. He felt his heart thump, thump. “What if I forget the order?” he whispered. “What if I say the wrong thing?” He pulled his shell up around his face.

Knock, knock, knock.

“Hi, Timo!” chirped Nia Sparrow, hopping on the ledge. Her feathers were smooth and gray. She smelled like rain.

Behind her climbed Bop the Squirrel, tail fluffy like a dandelion. “We brought pine cones for decorations!” Bop said. “And I found a shiny leaf!”

Timo smiled a tiny smile. “Sharing Day starts soon,” he said softly. “I… I am nervous.”

Nia tilted her head. “Let’s make a plan,” she said. “Plans help me feel calm.”

Bop nodded. “A plan is like nuts in a row,” he said. “Easy to find!”

Timo breathed in the treehouse smell. He liked plans. Plans felt safe.

“Step one,” Timo said, tapping a little chalkboard. “We clean.”

“Step two,” Nia chirped. “We set up seats.”

“Step three,” Bop grinned. “We share stories!”

Timo added one more. “Step four: We make a forest promise.”

They all nodded. The plan looked neat and clear on the board. Timo’s heart slowed to a gentle thump.

They began step one. Nia fluttered and dusted with her wings. Dust sparkled in the sun beams. Bop swept with a twig broom. Swish, swish. Timo gathered little sticks in a basket. Crackle, crackle.

“Listen,” Nia whispered. “Do you hear that?” A soft drip-drip-drip sounded from the roof.

Bop poked his head up. “Uh-oh,” he said. “There’s a tiny leak!”

Timo blinked. “We need to fix it,” he said. “But how?”

He looked at the chalkboard. “Let’s use our plan brain,” he whispered. “We can think step by step.”

Nia fluffed. “First we ask: What do we have?”

They set items on the floor: a dry leaf, string, tree sap in a jar, and a small wooden spoon.

Timo tapped his chin. “What do we know?” he said. “Water drips through a hole. We need to cover the hole.”

Bop peered up again. “The hole is small. Like a seed.”

Nia pointed to the sap. “Sap is sticky. It can hold.”

Timo drew a simple picture on the board: roof, hole, leaf, sap, string. “Let’s test,” he said. “We will try the leaf and sap.”

They climbed carefully. The wood was warm under their feet. Timo pressed the leaf over the hole. Nia dabbed sap, sweet and golden. Bop tied string around a tiny stick to hold the leaf in place. They waited. Drip… stopped.

“It worked!” Nia chirped.

Timo felt a glow inside. “Our plan helped,” he said. His shy smile grew.

They finished step two. They placed round cushions in a circle. The cushions were soft and squishy. The treehouse smelled like sticky sap and fresh air.

Soon, friends gathered. Mama Turtle and Grandma Turtle climbed slowly. Grandma’s shell had gentle swirls like rings on an old tree.

“Welcome,” Timo said in a small voice. He looked at the chalkboard. “We have a plan.”

Bop whispered, “You can do it.”

Timo took a breath. He felt the wood under his feet, solid and strong. “First, we clean. We did that,” he said. “Second, we sit. Please sit.” His voice was a little bigger now.

Everyone settled. Leaves rustled. Sunlight painted warm squares on the floor.

“Third,” Timo said. “We share stories.”

Grandma Turtle smiled. “I will start,” she said. Her voice was slow and kind. “When I was small, this forest was big and bright. We took only what we needed. We planted new trees when we used wood. We listened to the birds. That is our tradition.”

Nia chirped happily. “I teach my chicks to leave seeds when we snack,” she said. “Seeds grow into trees.”

Bop raised a paw. “I keep old nuts for winter and share extra with friends,” he said. “Sharing keeps the forest safe.”

Timo felt brave. “Today we fixed a roof leak,” he said. “We thought in steps. We looked at what we had. We tried a small test. It worked. We can use thinking to help the forest too.”

Mama Turtle nodded. “What promise will we make?”

Timo wrote on the board with chalk that squeaked a bit. “Step four: Forest promise,” he read. “We promise to think first. We promise to use only what we need. We promise to plant and share.”

Everyone placed a paw, wing, or flipper on the center of the floor. The wood felt warm. The treehouse hummed with happy voices.

“I promise,” they said together.

Timo’s shy heart felt large and light. He looked around. The treehouse was cozy, clean, and safe. The leak was gone. The plan had worked. The tradition was shared. The forest felt cared for.

That night, Timo made one more list before sleep:
- Notice a problem.
- Ask what we know.
- Use what we have.
- Try a small test.
- Share what we learn.

He tucked the list by the window. Stars twinkled like dew drops. Timo whispered, “Thinking helps. Sharing helps. Caring helps.” He pulled his shell snug and smiled.

The End.
