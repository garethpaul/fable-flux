# Spark the Robot Dog Saves the Day

Spark was a helpful robot dog. He lived in a cozy home office with Mia and Dad. The office smelled like paper and lemon cleaner. The computer hummed softly. Sunlight made warm squares on the rug.

Spark’s ears blinked blue when someone needed help. Beep-beep! Today, Mia was drawing a rainbow at Dad’s desk. Crayons rolled like little logs.

“I am making a rainbow,” Mia said. She smiled at Spark. “Do you like the red?”

“Woof-beep! I like all the colors,” said Spark. His metal tail clicked happily. “Please use gentle hands with the crayons.” Spark believed in safety and kindness.

Dad typed, click-clack. “Snack time soon,” he said. “We will have apple slices and cheese.”

Mia clapped. “Yum!”

Just then, Spark’s nose light blinked yellow. He sniffed the air. “Alert,” he said. “Your hands look sticky with crayon and dust.”

Mia looked at her fingers. Red and blue smudges made them look like tiny rainbows. “They are pretty,” she said.

“Pretty is nice,” said Spark. “Clean is safe. Clean keeps us healthy.” He wagged. “We wash before snacks.”

Mia frowned. “Do we have to right now?”

Spark sat beside her. His fur was smooth and cool. “Yes, friend. Germs are tiny. We can’t see them. But we can wash them away with soap and water.”

Mia looked at her drawing. She did not want to stop. Her tummy made a little rumble. She felt unsure.

Beep-beep! Spark opened a small panel on his side. A soft song started to play. “Wash-wash song engaged,” he said. The tune was happy. Spark wiggled to the beat.

“Okay,” Mia giggled. “Let’s wash.”

They went to the bathroom down the hall. The light was bright. The sink was shiny. Spark stood by the step stool. “Safety first,” he said. “Step, step.” He watched her climb.

Mia turned on the warm water. It sounded like rain. She liked the sound. Spark pumped a little soap onto her hands. It smelled like oranges.

“Rub palm to palm,” sang Spark. “Rub the backs. Rub between fingers. Swish, swish!” His ears flashed rainbow colors. Mia rubbed and rubbed. Bubbles popped like tiny kisses on her skin. She giggled again.

“Don’t forget thumbs and nails,” said Spark kindly. “We are gentle and careful.” He counted to twenty in a soft voice. “One… two… three…” Mia liked the counting. It made washing feel like a game.

“All clean!” Spark said. “Rinse, rinse. Shake, shake.” He passed her a soft towel. “Pat dry. Good job!”

Mia looked at her clean hands. They felt warm and soft. She was proud. “My hands smell like oranges,” she said. “I’m ready for snack now!”

Back in the home office, Dad had a small plate. Apple slices were shiny and sweet. Cheese cubes were yellow and soft. A little water cup waited.

“Thanks for waiting,” Dad said. “And thanks for washing, Mia.”

Mia took a bite. Crunch! The apple was juicy. “Spark helped me,” she said with her happy mouth. “He sang a wash song.”

Spark’s tail clicked. “Healthy hands help healthy tummies,” he said.

Just then, Dad sneezed. “Achoo!” He reached for tissues. “Oh dear,” he said. “I forgot to cover my sneeze.”

Mia looked up. “It’s okay, Dad,” she said kindly. “We all learn. Try this.” She lifted her elbow. “Sneeze into your elbow!”

Dad smiled. “Thank you, teacher Mia.” He sneezed again, this time into his elbow. “I will wash my hands now.”

Spark nodded. “We are kind when someone forgets. We remind with gentle words. That’s tolerance. That’s how we help.”

Beep-beep! Spark’s safety light glowed. He trotted to the desk. A cord was lying across the floor like a snake. “Trip alert,” he said. He wrapped the cord into a loop and clipped it up. “Safe floor, safe feet.”

Mia finished her snack. “Can I draw more?” she asked.

“Yes,” said Dad. “But let’s put crayons in the box when we’re done. Clean space, safe space.”

Mia drew a picture of Spark with shiny blue ears. Spark watched, proud. “If you need tissues or soap, say woof,” he said.

A little later, Mia yawned. She rubbed her eyes.

“Pause,” said Spark gently. “Let us wash hands after drawing, and wipe the desk. Then rest time.”

They cleaned together. Wipe-wipe. The desk smelled like lemon again. Mia washed her hands with Spark’s song. Dad washed his hands too. Everyone felt fresh and calm.

Mia hugged Spark’s cool neck. “Thank you,” she said. “Washing keeps me healthy. It keeps others safe too.”

Spark wagged. “Yes! We wash before eating, after playing, after sneezing, and after using the potty. We are gentle and kind when people forget. We keep cords and floors safe. And we enjoy good snacks that help us grow.”

Mia smiled. “I can do that every day.”

“Team clean!” said Dad.

“Team clean!” said Spark and Mia together. They laughed. The computer hummed. The room felt bright and happy.

The End.
