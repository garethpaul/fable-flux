# Ollie's Big Voice

Ollie the otter splashed happily in the kiddie pool in the sunny backyard. The water felt cool and refreshing on his furry brown coat. He loved diving under and popping back up with a big splash!

"This is the best pool ever!" Ollie giggled, floating on his back.

Just then, three raccoons wandered into the backyard. They saw Ollie's pool and their eyes grew wide.

"Wow! Look at that pool!" said the biggest raccoon, Rocky. "We want to swim too!"

Before Ollie could say anything, all three raccoons jumped right in. SPLASH! Water went everywhere. The pool suddenly felt very crowded.

"Um, excuse me," Ollie said quietly. But the raccoons didn't hear him. They were too busy splashing and playing loudly.

Ollie tried to swim, but there was no room. The raccoons took up all the space. One raccoon bumped into him. Another splashed water in his face.

"Excuse me," Ollie whispered again, even quieter this time. Still, nobody listened.

Ollie felt sad and squeezed. He wanted to speak up, but he felt too shy. His tummy felt all fluttery. He didn't want to seem mean, but this was his pool!

Ollie climbed out and sat on the warm grass. The sun felt nice, but he missed his pool. He watched the raccoons having fun in HIS special place.

"I should say something," Ollie thought. "But what if they get mad?"

Ollie took a deep breath. He remembered what his mama always said: "Use your voice, little one. Be kind but be brave."

Ollie stood up tall. He walked back to the pool with his head held high.

"Hey there!" Ollie said in a clear, strong voice. "I'm Ollie, and this is my pool."

The raccoons stopped splashing and looked at him.

"I love sharing and playing with friends," Ollie continued, his voice getting stronger. "But I'd like to swim in my pool too. Can we work together?"

Rocky looked surprised. "Oh! We didn't know this was your pool. We're sorry, Ollie."

"That's okay," Ollie smiled. "I have an idea! What if we take turns? You can swim for ten minutes, then I can swim for ten minutes?"

The smallest raccoon, Rosie, clapped her paws. "That sounds fair!"

"Or," said the middle raccoon, Rascal, "what if we all swim together, but we share the space? We can make sure everyone has room!"

Ollie's eyes lit up. "I like that idea even better! We can all be pool friends!"

The raccoons agreed happily. They made sure Ollie had plenty of space to dive and splash. Ollie showed them his best underwater tricks. Rocky taught Ollie how to do a perfect cannonball.

"This is so much more fun with friends!" Ollie laughed as he surfaced from a dive.

"Thanks for speaking up, Ollie," Rosie said. "We didn't mean to crowd you out."

"And thanks for sharing your pool with us," added Rascal.

As the afternoon sun sparkled on the water, all four friends played together. They took turns doing tricks and cheered for each other. Ollie felt proud and happy.

That evening, as the raccoons headed home, Rocky turned back to Ollie.

"Can we come back tomorrow?" he asked hopefully.

"Of course!" Ollie grinned. "Pool friends are the best friends!"

Ollie learned that using his voice wasn't scary at all. When he spoke up kindly and clearly, good things happened. He made new friends and still got to enjoy his pool.

From that day on, Ollie always remembered to use his big, brave voice whenever he needed to.

The End.
