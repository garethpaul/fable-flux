# Nutmeg Waits for Morning

Nutmeg was a small, fluffy squirrel with bright eyes and a bushy tail. Nutmeg lived in a cozy bedroom with soft pillows, a warm quilt, and a tiny acorn night-light that glowed like the moon. The room smelled like clean sheets and cinnamon tea. The window made a gentle hum when the night wind whispered by.

One evening, Nutmeg held a brand-new puzzle. It was a big forest puzzle with shiny pieces that clicked together. “I want to finish it now!” Nutmeg said, tail twitching with excitement.

Mama Squirrel tucked the quilt around Nutmeg’s feet. “It’s bedtime, little one,” Mama said softly. “Your eyes are yawning. The puzzle will wait.”

Nutmeg looked at the puzzle on the bedside table. The pieces were smooth and cool. Nutmeg’s paws itched to touch them. “But I want it now,” Nutmeg whispered.

Mama stroked Nutmeg’s head. “Patience is a kind friend,” she said. “Patience helps us wait safely and calmly. In the morning, you’ll be rested and ready. You’ll be brave and careful with the tiny pieces.”

Nutmeg scrunched the quilt. “Waiting is hard.”

Mama nodded. “It is. But you can do hard things. Let’s take brave, slow breaths.”

They breathed in together. In. Out. The little acorn light glowed warm and cozy. Nutmeg’s eyes felt heavy. “Okay,” Nutmeg said. “I’ll wait until morning.”

Mama smiled. “That’s courage too. Courage isn’t always loud. Sometimes courage is quiet and calm.” She kissed Nutmeg’s ear. “Good night.”

The room grew still. Outside, the crickets sang a gentle “chirp-chirp.” Nutmeg listened to the soft whoosh of the heater and the tiny creak of the wooden shelf. The quilt felt like a gentle hug. Nutmeg curled up and dreamed of big trees and click-click puzzle pieces.

Morning came with golden light slipping across the floor. The bedroom smelled like toast and apples. Nutmeg stretched, tail fanning out. “Morning!” Nutmeg chirped. “Puzzle time!”

Mama came in with a smile. “Good morning, patient squirrel. First, we eat. Then, we build safely at the table.”

Nutmeg blinked at the puzzle. “Can I bring it to the bed?”

Mama shook her head kindly. “Tiny pieces can get lost in blankets. We keep small things at the table. That keeps us safe.”

Nutmeg nodded. “Safety first.” The words felt big and strong in Nutmeg’s mouth.

After breakfast, they moved to the little table by the window. Sunbeams made dust sparkle in the air like tiny stars. Nutmeg opened the box. The pieces felt cool and smooth and smelled a little like new paper. “I will start with the corners,” Nutmeg said. “That is a good plan.”

Mama smiled. “A resourceful plan.”

Nutmeg searched. The first corner was easy. Click! The second corner was tricky. Nutmeg’s paws wiggled. “Where is it? I don’t see it!”

Nutmeg’s cheeks felt hot. The room sounded louder: tap-tap of paws, rustle-rustle of the box. “I want it now,” Nutmeg said, voice small.

Mama touched Nutmeg’s shoulder. “Pause,” she whispered. “Let’s use patience. Slow helps us see.”

Nutmeg closed eyes and took a breath. In. Out. The golden sunlight felt warm on Nutmeg’s fur. “I can wait,” Nutmeg said softly. “I can look slowly.”

Nutmeg spread the pieces in a big, flat circle, just like Mama taught before. “Edges first,” Nutmeg said. “Then the middle.” A shiny piece with a tiny leaf tip peeped from under another piece. Nutmeg giggled. “Found you!” Click!

Piece by piece, the puzzle grew. Trees with green tops. A stream with shiny blue. A fox with a gentle face. Sometimes the pieces didn’t fit. Nutmeg tried to push. “Why won’t you go?” Nutmeg huffed.

Mama held up a finger. “We don’t force pieces. Forcing can break them. We use safe, gentle hands.”

Nutmeg loosened the grip. “Gentle hands,” Nutmeg repeated. The piece slipped into the right spot with a happy click.

After a while, Nutmeg’s tummy made a little rumble. “Snack?” Mama asked.

Nutmeg looked at the unfinished puzzle. A little worry tickled. “If I stop, will I lose my place?”

“We can keep our place safe,” Mama said. She showed Nutmeg a small tray for the next pieces and a soft cloth to cover the puzzle. “This is patient planning.”

Nutmeg nodded. “Okay.” They ate apple slices that were crisp and sweet. Juice made Nutmeg’s whiskers tingle. “Yum!”

Back at the table, Nutmeg lifted the cloth. “Still here!” Nutmeg laughed. With rested paws and a calm heart, the last pieces went in more easily. Click. Click. One left.

Nutmeg held the final piece. It felt warm from Nutmeg’s paw. “I waited for morning. I took slow breaths. I used gentle hands,” Nutmeg said, smiling wide. “I was patient and brave.”

“Ready?” Mama asked.

“Ready,” said Nutmeg.

Click!

The puzzle was complete. The forest picture shone in the sun. Nutmeg’s chest felt light and proud. Mama clapped softly. “You did it. How do you feel?”

“I feel strong,” Nutmeg said. “Patience helped me. Courage helped me. Safety helped me.”

Mama nodded. “Yes. Patience helps us wait and try again. Courage helps us keep going. Safety keeps our bodies and things okay.”

Nutmeg traced the smooth puzzle with a careful paw. “Next time I feel wiggly, I will breathe and go slow.”

Mama hugged Nutmeg in the warm, cozy bedroom. The acorn night-light was off now, but the room still felt bright and kind. Nutmeg whispered, “Thank you, Patience.” The room seemed to smile back.

The End.
