# Rocky Raccoon and the Tidy Tune

Rocky was a musical raccoon who loved to play songs in a cozy basement lab. The lab smelled like warm dust and lemon soap. Little lights blinked. Buttons went click-click. Shelves held jars of shiny screws that tinkled like tiny bells. Rocky’s fluffy tail swished to the beat as he hummed, “la-la-la!”

Rocky wanted to make a new song with a tap-tap drum, a plink-plonk keyboard, and a soft whoosh shaker. He set his drum on a mat. He placed the keyboard on a bench. He put the shaker, a jar of tiny beads, on the table.

“Ready!” Rocky smiled. His whiskers twitched.

But then he looked around. The wires were in a tangle. Papers were on the floor. The shaker jar rolled and went clink, clink, clink. It rolled right under the bench.

“Oh no,” said Rocky. “My lab is a mess. I can’t find my shaker. I can’t reach my notes!”

He tried to start his song. He tapped the drum. Tap-tap. He pressed a key. Plink. Then a wire pulled loose with a zzzzip. The light went out. The room went quiet.

Rocky felt a little lump in his tummy. His ears drooped. “I want to make music,” he whispered, “but I need my things to work.”

He took a deep breath. In and out. In and out. The air smelled like crayons and wood. “I can fix this,” Rocky said. “I can be resourceful.” He said the big word slowly. “Re-sour-ce-ful means I can use what I have to solve a problem.”

He looked around. He had boxes. He had sticky notes. He had string. He had a flashlight. He had two small baskets. He had his paws and his brain.

Ping! Rocky had an idea. “I will make a plan.”

He clicked on the flashlight. A warm circle of light shone on the floor. “Step one: make a path,” he said. He pushed papers into a neat pile. Swish, swish. He rolled the wires into loops. Curl, curl. He tied each loop with a bit of string. Knot, knot. “Safe and tidy!”

“Step two: find the shaker,” Rocky said. He lay on his belly. He peered under the bench. Dust tickled his nose. “Ah-choo!” He laughed. “Hello, shaker.” He used a ruler like a gentle push stick and slid the jar back. “Got you!”

“Step three: sort my stuff,” he said. He made labels with his sticky notes. He wrote “Wires” on one. He wrote “Music Tools” on another. He wrote “Notes” on a third. He stuck the notes on boxes and baskets.

Clink! Screws went into a jar. “Small bits here.” Clip! Papers went into a folder. “Notes here.” Loops of wire went into a box. “Wires here.” Rocky’s tail made a happy swish. The lab began to look calm.

Just then, Rocky heard a gentle knock. Knock-knock. His friend, Pip the mouse, peeked in. Pip wore tiny blue overalls. “Hi, Rocky,” squeaked Pip. “It smells like lemons and music in here. Need help?”

“Yes, please,” said Rocky. “I am making it tidy so I can make a song.”

“I can help!” said Pip. “I can coil wires. I have small paws.” Pip hummed a little hum and coiled wires into pretty circles. “Round and round,” Pip sang.

Rocky smiled. “Thank you, Pip. That is cooperation. We work together.”

Pip beamed. “We are a team!”

“Step four: test the lights,” Rocky said. He gently plugged in the wire. Click. The light blinked on. Beep-beep. The buttons lit red and green. The lab felt bright and cozy.

Rocky and Pip looked at the tidy baskets and boxes. The floor was clear. The shelves were neat. The air sounded quiet and peaceful.

Rocky said, “Now I can make my song. But first, let’s make a Tidy Tune to help us remember.”

He tapped the drum. Tap-tap-tap.
Pip shook the shaker. Shhh-shhh-shhh.
Rocky plinked the keyboard. Plink-plonk-plink.

Rocky sang:
“Sort and store,
Clean the floor,
Coil the wires,
Then play more!”

Pip added:
“Work as one,
Make it fun,
Use your brain,
And you’re done!”

They giggled. The beat felt bouncy and light. Rocky felt proud. His chest felt warm and full.

Then they heard another sound. Scritch-scratch. A little robot in the corner had a loose wheel. It wobbled and squeaked. “Oh dear,” said Pip. “It can’t roll straight.”

Rocky said, “Let’s help. We can solve it peacefully.” He took a breath. “We will not fuss. We will think.”

He looked in the “Small bits” jar. Clink. He found a tiny washer. He found a small nut. He found a short screw. “We can use these,” he said. He held the wheel. Pip held the robot. Together they turned the tiny screw. Twist, twist. The wheel sat snug.

The robot rolled smooth. Whirr. It blinked a happy blue light. Beep!

Rocky clapped. Pip clapped. “We did it!” they cheered. “We used what we had. We worked together. We stayed calm.”

Rocky looked at the neat lab. He looked at his friends: Pip and the beep-y robot. He felt ready.

“Now,” Rocky said, “let’s make music.”

They played their Tidy Tune again, louder and brighter. Tap-tap! Shhh-shhh! Plink-plonk! The basement lab glowed. It smelled like lemon and joy. The music felt like sunshine in their hearts.

Rocky said, “I learned that being resourceful helps me solve problems. Being organized helps me find things. Cooperating makes work easy and fun.”

Pip nodded. “And peaceful thinking helps us fix little troubles.”

Rocky smiled. “We can do big things, one small step at a time.”

The End.
