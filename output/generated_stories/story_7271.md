# Miko’s Music Room Shuffle

In a bright musical practice room, a courageous monkey named Miko peeked in. The room smelled like lemon polish and warm wood. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. Drums, bells, and a shiny piano waited quietly. A big mirror showed Miko’s round eyes and brave smile.

“I am ready to dance,” Miko whispered. “I am brave. I can learn.”

Miko turned the small radio on. Soft music floated out—tap, tap, swish. Miko’s toes wiggled. His tail curled with excitement. He took a deep breath. In the mirror, Miko stood tall.

“Step, step, turn,” he said. “Step, step, turn!”

Miko tried the steps. Tap! Tap! Swish! He spun in a circle. His feet slid a little on the smooth floor. He giggled. “I can do this!”

But then, Miko slipped on a ribbon left on the ground. “Oof!” He sat down with a thump.

Miko rubbed his knee. It didn’t hurt much, but his brave heart felt a little wobbly. He looked around. There were ribbons on the floor, drumsticks by the door, and music sheets scattered like white leaves.

Miko took another deep breath. “I can think. I can fix this,” he said. “Good dancers make a safe space. First, chores. Then, dance.”

He made a simple plan, counting on his fingers.

“One: Pick up ribbons,” he said. He gathered the rainbow ribbons and rolled them gently. “Two: Put drumsticks in the drum basket.” He tapped the sticks together—clack, clack—and placed them inside. “Three: Stack the music sheets.” He patted each stack, neat and tidy.

The room began to look calm. The floor felt open, like a clear path. Miko smiled at the mirror. “Good job, me,” he said. “Chores help me dance.”

He turned the music up just a little. The soft beat sounded like a friendly heartbeat: bom, bom, bom. Miko tried again.

“Step, step, turn,” he said. Tap, tap, swish!

This time, he did not slip. His feet made little whisper sounds on the floor. His tail swayed to the music. He lifted his arms like tree branches in the wind. He felt light. He felt brave.

“Hmm,” Miko said. “How can I remember the steps?” He looked at the mirror and thought. “I will use pictures in my mind.”

He pointed to the corner. “Banana step,” he giggled, stepping forward like he was reaching for a banana. He pointed to the drums. “Drum step,” he tapped his toes gently in a beat. He pointed to the piano. “Piano turn,” he spun slowly like a big round note.

“Banana, drum, piano,” he chanted. “Banana, drum, piano!” He tried the three moves. Step, tap, turn! Step, tap, turn! The pattern felt good. His body remembered.

The radio switched to a faster song. Miko’s eyes grew wide. “Uh-oh,” he said. “Fast is tricky.”

He paused. He put his hand on his heart. “I can think. I can try.”

He made another plan.

“One: Start slow,” he told himself. He moved in tiny steps. “Two: Count the beat.” He counted softly, “One, two, three, four.” “Three: Breathe.” He breathed in and out, like waves—whoosh, whoosh.

Then, Miko tried the fast music. Banana, drum, piano! Step, tap, turn! He wobbled a little, then steadied his feet. He smiled. “Yes!”

A small broom leaned by the wall. Miko picked it up and swished crumbs into a pan. “Sweep, sweep,” he said. “Clean floor, happy feet.” The lemon smell grew brighter. The room felt friendly and safe.

Just then, the door opened. Miss Bell, the music teacher, peeked in. She wore a scarf with tiny notes on it. “Hello, Miko,” she said kindly. “I heard music. Are you practicing?”

“Yes!” Miko said. “I am learning a dance. I made a plan. And I did my chores!”

Miss Bell looked around. “The room looks wonderful,” she said. “And you look proud.”

Miko nodded. “I have three steps,” he said. “Banana, drum, piano.”

“Show me,” said Miss Bell.

The music played. Miko began. Step like reaching for a banana. Tap like the drums. Turn like a piano note. His feet whispered. His tail swayed. His smile shined.

Miss Bell clapped. “Beautiful! You used your brain and your body. You cleaned your space. You listened. That is smart dancing.”

Miko’s heart felt big and warm. “Thank you,” he said. “When it was messy, I slipped. So I cleaned. When it was fast, I counted and breathed. I can solve problems.”

Miss Bell nodded. “That is critical thinking,” she said. “And that makes you a safe, strong dancer.”

Miko bowed to the mirror. He bowed to Miss Bell. He turned off the radio and put it neatly back. He folded the ribbons and stacked the music again.

He patted the broom. “Thank you, helper,” he said.

As he left the musical practice room, Miko whispered, “I am brave. I can dance. I can think. I can do my chores.”

The room was quiet now, with a soft lemony glow. Miko’s steps down the hall were light, like little drum taps.

The End.
