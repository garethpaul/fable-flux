# Kiki the Koala Takes Gentle Care

Kiki the koala sat on the peaceful front porch. The porch was warm and quiet. A soft breeze tickled her fuzzy ears. The sun made golden spots on the floorboards. Birds chirped, “Cheep-cheep!” in the lemon tree.

Kiki took a deep breath in. “Ahh,” she sighed. “Today is a gentle day.”

She wiggled her toes and stretched her arms. “Good morning, body,” she said kindly. “Thank you for helping me climb and hug and play.”

On a small table sat Kiki’s blue water bottle and a bowl of crunchy apple slices. The apples smelled sweet. The water felt cool when she touched the bottle.

Kiki’s friend, Pip the possum, walked up the steps. Pip’s paws went tap-tap-tap on the wood. His tail swished like a little rope.

“Hi, Kiki!” Pip said. “Want to race down the path and back?”

Kiki smiled gently. “Good morning, Pip. I like to move my body. But first, I need my warm-up. My body needs care.”

Pip tilted his head. “Warm-up?”

Kiki nodded. “Yes. A warm-up is kind to our muscles. It’s self-care. It helps us play safely.”

Pip blinked. “Okay! Can I warm up too?”

“Of course,” Kiki said. “Let’s do it together.”

They stood side by side on the porch. The boards felt smooth under their feet.

“First, we breathe,” Kiki said softly. “In through the nose.” They breathed in. The air smelled like lemons and sunshine. “Out through the mouth,” Kiki said. They blew out like gentle wind. “Whooo.”

Pip giggled. “That feels nice.”

“Now we stretch tall like trees,” Kiki said. They reached up, up, up. Their fingers wiggled like leaves. The sky looked big and bright.

“Now touch the porch like roots,” Kiki said. They bent down and tapped the warm floor. “Hello, roots,” Pip whispered.

They rolled their shoulders. They made little circles with their arms. Their joints made tiny, happy pops. Kiki listened to her body. It felt awake and ready.

“Now a small race?” Pip asked.

“Yes,” Kiki said. “A gentle race. We run to the lemon tree and back. We keep space. We watch our steps. We respect each other and the porch.”

They lined up. “Ready?” Pip asked.

“Ready,” Kiki nodded. “Steady… go!”

They ran with small steps. The breeze lifted Kiki’s fur. Pip’s tail bobbed. Kiki listened to her feet: tap-tap-tap. She looked ahead and kept her arms by her sides. Pip giggled. His steps were quick and light.

Kiki reached the lemon tree and touched the rough bark. “Hello, tree,” she said softly. Pip touched it too. “Hi, tree!”

They jogged back to the porch. Their hearts beat thump-thump, happy and strong. Kiki slowed down. Pip slowed down too.

“Water time,” Kiki said. She took a sip. “Ahh.” The cool water felt like little raindrops inside her mouth. Pip took a sip from his own bottle. “Ahh,” he echoed.

Pip watched Kiki munch a crisp apple slice. Crunch! “Can I have one?” he asked.

“Yes,” Kiki said. “But please ask the apples nicely.” She grinned.

Pip smiled. “Apples, may I have a slice?”

Kiki giggled and gave him one. “Thank you for asking. That is respect.”

They sat on the porch step. The day felt cozy and bright. A butterfly fluttered by, orange and soft.

Pip’s ears twitched. “Kiki, can we run again? Fast, fast!”

Kiki listened to her body. Her legs felt a little tired. “I think my legs need a rest,” she said calmly. “Rest is self-care too. When we rest, we get strong.”

Pip nodded slowly. “How do you know when to rest?”

Kiki put a paw on her chest. “I listen. My heart says thump-thump, not thump-THUMP. My breath says whooo, not WHOOSH. My legs say, ‘Please, a little break.’ When my body talks, I respect it.”

Pip looked at his paws. “My paws feel tingly.” He wiggled them. “Maybe they want a rest too.”

Kiki patted the step beside her. “Let’s do a cool-down.”

They sat tall and breathed slowly. In, out. In, out. Kiki hummed a soft tune. It sounded like warm honey. Pip hummed along. Their bodies felt calm again.

A neighbor, Mrs. Wren, waved from her porch across the lane. “Hello, dears!” she called. “I like your porch exercises.”

“Thank you,” Kiki called back. “We are taking care.”

“That’s wise,” Mrs. Wren said, smiling. “Have a happy day.”

Kiki and Pip waved. Then Kiki stood and brushed a crumb from her fur. “Pip, would you like to help me sweep the porch?” she asked. “We can keep it tidy for our toes.”

Pip nodded. “Yes! That shows respect for our home.”

They held the small broom together. Swish-swish. The lemon smell grew stronger as they swept by the tree. They found a fallen leaf. It felt papery and cool. They put it gently in a little bin.

When they were done, the porch looked neat and safe. Kiki’s heart felt warm. “Thank you, Pip,” she said. “Playing, moving, resting, drinking water, eating fruit, and cleaning up—that is how we care for ourselves and our space.”

Pip beamed. “I learned a lot! I can listen to my body. I can be gentle. I can be strong too.”

Kiki nodded. “Yes. Self-care helps us be our best.”

They sat together in the sunshine. The breeze kissed their cheeks. The birds sang a soft, happy song.

“Tomorrow,” Pip said, “maybe we can stretch like stars.”

Kiki smiled. “Yes. And we will listen, breathe, and care, every day.”

The End.
