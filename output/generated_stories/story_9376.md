# Pebble Pause on the Porch

Pip was a cheerful penguin who loved to hum. “Hmm-hmm-hmm!” sang Pip, waddling to the peaceful front porch. The porch smelled like sweet tea and pine wood. A gentle breeze tickled Pip’s feathers. The porch swing creaked softly, squeak-sway, squeak-sway. Birds chirped. Sun warmed Pip’s beak.

Pip had a plan. “Today, I build a pebble tower!” Pip said. The pebbles were smooth and cool, gray and white and speckled. Pip laid them on the porch floor in a neat line. “One pebble. Two pebble. Three pebble,” Pip counted, tapping each stone with a tiny flipper.

Pip stacked the first pebble. Then the next. Tap-tap, steady and slow. The tower grew taller, a little wobbly but brave.

Just then, Pip’s friend, Tilly the turtle, clomp-clomped up the steps. “Hi, Pip!” Tilly smiled. Her shell was shiny from the sun.

“Hi, Tilly!” Pip chirped. “I’m building a tower. It’s my turn right now. You can watch, and then you can try.”

Tilly nodded. “Okay! I will watch.”

Pip placed another pebble. The tower swayed. Pip held a breath. But then—thump! A gust of wind pressed the porch swing. Squeak-sway! The swing bumped the floor. The pebbles shivered—and the tower tumbled down.

Clink-clink-clatter!

Pip’s heart felt hot. “Oh!” Pip’s flippers curled. “I worked so hard.” Pip’s face scrunched. “I feel mad.”

The mad feeling was big, like a loud drum in Pip’s chest. Boom-boom.

Pip remembered a poster by the door: When you feel big feelings, stop and do Pebble Pause. Pip whispered, “Pebble Pause.” Pip put one flipper on the belly and took a slow breath.

“In smell… out blow.” Pip sniffed like smelling a flower. Then blew out like cooling soup. In smell… out blow.

Tilly watched. “Are you okay?” she asked softly.

“I’m trying to be,” Pip said. “I feel frustrated. That means I want it to work, but it is not working yet.” A tear blinked at the corner of Pip’s eye. Pip wiped it and took another breath. In smell… out blow.

The drum in Pip’s chest got softer. Pip could hear the birds again. Cheep-cheep. Pip could feel the breeze again. Whoosh, gentle and kind.

“Thanks for waiting,” Pip said. “I need space right now. Please do not touch the pebbles yet.”

“I can give you space,” Tilly said. She stepped back with a clomp and sat on the porch step. “I will cheer quietly.”

Pip looked at the swing. Squeak-sway. “Hmm,” Pip hummed. “The swing bumped the floor. Maybe that made the tower fall.”

Pip’s eyes got bright. “I can be resourceful. I can try a different way.”

Pip slid the pebble line to a still corner of the porch, away from the swing. The wood there felt cool and dry. Pip found a flat, wide pebble. “This can be the base,” Pip said. “A strong base helps.”

Pip stacked again. “Tap-tap.” Breathe in smell… out blow. “Tap-tap.” Pip hummed to feel calm. The tower grew. It was taller than Pip’s beak now!

Then a small leaf skittered across the porch. Skit-skit! Tilly jumped up, excited, and her big shell nudged the floor. The tower wobbled—wibble-wobble—then tumbled again.

Clink-clink-clatter!

Pip’s cheeks burned. The drum feeling came back. Boom-boom. Pip put flipper to belly. “Pebble Pause,” Pip whispered. In smell… out blow. Pip looked at Tilly.

Tilly’s eyes were wide. “I’m sorry, Pip,” she said. “I got excited and forgot to be gentle. I did not mean to bump your tower.”

Pip listened. The words sounded honest. Pip took another breath. In smell… out blow. The drum softened again.

“Thank you for saying sorry,” Pip said. “I forgive you. Next time, please ask before coming close. I need my building space. That is my boundary.”

“I can do that,” Tilly said. “Can I watch from here?” She sat where the porch step was steady.

“Yes,” Pip smiled. “You can be my quiet helper.”

Tilly nodded and whispered, “Go, Pip, go.”

Pip checked the pebbles. Some were round and rolled. Some were flat and stayed. Pip chose flat ones for the bottom, and saved round ones for the top. “Flat, flat, flat,” Pip said softly, placing them just so. “Round can be a hat.”

The breeze brushed Pip’s feathers. The porch smelled like warm wood and salt air. A fly buzzed by, zzz, but Pip stayed calm. In smell… out blow.

Finally, Pip placed the round pebble hat. The tower did not wobble. It stood tall and proud.

“We did it!” Pip cheered.

Tilly clapped her little turtle feet. “Hooray!”

Pip smiled big. “I felt frustrated. I took a Pebble Pause. I used my words to share my boundary. I tried a new way. And I forgave my friend.”

Tilly smiled too. “And I listened. I gave space. I said sorry.”

Pip nodded. “That is peaceful talking. That is being kind.”

They sat on the porch swing together now, very still so it did not bump. Squeak-sway, gentle and slow. They looked at the pebble tower in the quiet corner, safe and strong.

Pip patted Tilly’s shell. “Next, it can be your turn. We can build together if we ask and take turns.”

“I like that plan,” Tilly said.

Pip hummed a happy tune. “Hmm-hmm-hmm.” The sun felt warm. The breeze felt soft. The birds sang sweet. And the pebble tower sparkled like a tiny mountain.

The lesson was clear in Pip’s heart: When I feel frustrated, I can pause, breathe, speak kindly, and try again. I can ask for space, be resourceful, and forgive.

The End.
