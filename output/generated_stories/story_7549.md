# Polly’s Paint Plan

Polly was a bright, creative parrot. Her feathers were green like leaves, with little specks of blue and yellow. She loved to paint. She loved to sing. She loved to make big, bold pictures. Today, Polly fluttered up the stairs to the quiet attic. The attic smelled like old books and warm wood. Sunlight made stripes on the floor. Dust danced in the light like tiny stars.

“Squawk! Art time!” Polly chirped. She set out paper, brushes, and little jars of paint. Tap, tap, tap went the jars as she lined them up. Swish, swish went her tail feathers as she wiggled with joy.

Polly’s friends scurried in after her. There was Miko the mouse, soft and gray. There was Tilly the turtle, slow and steady. There was Bee the bunny, hopping with happy feet.

“Let’s paint a big mural!” Polly said. “We will make a picture of our neighborhood!”

“That sounds fun,” said Miko. “Can I paint the tall trees?”

“I want to paint clouds,” said Bee, bouncing.

“I will paint the road,” said Tilly in a calm, low voice.

Polly puffed up, proud. “I am the leader today,” she said. “I brought the art things. I will show you how.” She grabbed the biggest brush. She dipped it deep into blue. Drip, drip, drip went the paint on the floor.

“Polly,” Tilly said softly, “the paint is spilling.”

Polly flapped. “Oops! I will clean it later. We need to start now!”

She swooped to the paper and painted a giant blue swoosh. “Cloud!” she cried.

Bee’s nose twitched. “But I wanted to paint clouds.”

Polly was busy. She dipped into green. Splat, splat! “Tree!” she chirped.

Miko tugged his whiskers. “Polly, I said I wanted to paint trees.”

Polly grabbed red. Swish! Swoosh! Splat! She was fast and loud. The attic was not quiet anymore.

Tilly cleared her throat. “Polly, we need to take turns,” she said. “We need to keep the attic tidy.”

Polly’s heart thumped. She felt a hot squeeze in her chest. “But I want to make it perfect!” she shouted. “I want to do it all!”

Bee’s ears drooped. Miko’s whiskers drooped too. The fun felt like it was slipping away.

Polly looked around. Paint drops were on the floor. Brushes were sticky. The big paper had messy spots. The quiet attic felt messy and loud. Polly felt a lump in her throat. She let out a small squawk.

Tilly moved closer. “It is okay to feel big feelings,” she said. “Let’s take a slow breath.”

They all breathed in. One… two… three. They breathed out. One… two… three. The attic felt softer.

Polly blinked. She listened to the gentle creak of the attic floor. She smelled the sweet wood again. She heard a little bird outside. Her chest felt calmer.

“I forgot my job,” Polly whispered. “If I am the leader, I must be responsible. I must take care of the art. I must take care of my friends’ turns.”

Bee nodded. “We can help.”

Miko smiled. “We can clean together.”

Polly puffed up, but this time in a kind way. “You are right. I am sorry,” she said. “I splashed. I rushed. I forgot to share. I will fix it.”

She flapped to the shelf and found a little cloth. “First, we clean,” she said. “Then, we plan.”

They wiped the drops. Wipe, wipe, wipe. They rinsed two brushes. Swish, swish in the water cup. They put lids on the paints they were not using. Click, click.

Polly made a chart with a crayon. “Clouds: Bee. Trees: Miko. Road: Tilly. Houses: Polly,” she read. “One turn at a time. We say ‘your turn,’ and we wait.”

Bee beamed. “My turn to paint clouds,” she said. Polly handed her the soft big brush. “Your turn,” Polly said. Bee painted fluffy white puffs. Dab, dab, dab.

“Nice clouds,” said Tilly.

“Thank you,” said Bee. She rinsed the brush. “Miko’s turn.”

Miko painted tall green trees with tiny leaves. Tap, tap, tap. He smiled. “I like going slow.”

“Great trees,” Polly said. She felt proud, not bossy.

Tilly painted the road in a steady gray line. Swish. Slow and smooth. “My turn is done,” she said. She put the brush down and nodded.

Polly took her turn last. She painted small, bright houses in red, yellow, and blue. She added tiny doors and windows. She added a small bird on a roof. Chirp, chirp, chirp, she sang softly.

They all stepped back. The mural was big and bright. The attic light made it glow. It looked like their neighborhood. It looked like their teamwork.

Polly looked at the clean floor. She looked at the neat brushes. She felt warm and happy inside. “We did it,” she said. “We made art. We took turns. We were responsible.”

Bee giggled. “And we breathed when we felt mad.”

Miko nodded. “We cleaned as we went.”

Tilly smiled. “That is good leadership, Polly. You cared for the art, the space, and your friends.”

Polly lifted her head. “Today I learned that being the leader means helping, sharing, and cleaning up. I can do big art and take good care.”

They stuck the mural to the attic wall with tape. Rip, press. It made a soft sound as it held tight. The attic felt quiet again, but the picture made it shine.

Polly fluffed her feathers. “Thank you, friends,” she said. “Next time, we can switch jobs. Everyone can try something new.”

“Your turn, my turn, our turn,” they all sang together.

The End.
