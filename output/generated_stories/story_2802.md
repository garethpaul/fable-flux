# Tilly Turtle’s Home Office Helper Day

Tilly was a shy turtle who lived with her family. She liked quiet places and slow mornings. Her shell felt warm and safe. Today, Tilly peeked into the home office. It was a small, cozy room with a big desk, a soft chair, and a humming computer.

Bzzzz, hummm, went the computer.
Tap-tap, tick-tick, went the keyboard.
Click, click, clicked the mouse.

Mama Turtle smiled. “Good morning, Tilly. Today is Home Office Helper Day.”

Tilly tucked her head a little. “Me? I am shy,” she whispered. “I don’t know how.”

Mama nodded kindly. “You can try. Start small. You can do things by yourself. I believe in you.”

Tilly took a tiny breath. She looked around. Papers sat in a little stack. Crayons rolled near a notepad. The lamp glowed like a warm sun.

“I can help,” Tilly said, very soft.

“Wonderful,” said Mama. “I’ll be right here if you need me.”

Tilly decided to make a routine, just like she did for bedtime. “First, second, third,” she told herself. “I can be brave and do one thing at a time.”

First, Tilly checked the pencil cup. “Are you cozy, pencils?” she asked. The pencils leaned and wobbled. One fell out and bounced on the floor. Plink!

Tilly blinked. “Oh no.” She wanted to hide in her shell. But she remembered: I can solve small problems.

She looked around. She saw a rubber band by the stapler. “Hmm,” she hummed. “I can use this.” Tilly wrapped the rubber band around the cup. It held the pencils snug. “There,” she said. “All cozy.”

Second, Tilly saw cords curled like little snakes behind the desk. The cords were mixed up. “You look twisty,” she told them. “You need a path.”

She searched the drawer. Rattle-rattle. She found sticky notes. She found a paper clip. She found a short piece of blue tape. “I can try this,” she said. Tilly made a gentle loop with the tape and clipped the cords together. They lined up in a neat row. “Much better,” Tilly said with a tiny smile.

Third, Tilly checked the to-do list on the notepad. The words were big and friendly.

- Drink water
- Stretch
- Send a thank-you email
- Take a deep breath

Tilly traced the words with her finger. “I can help with these,” she said. She poured water into Mama’s cup. Glug-glug. She set it near the keyboard. The cool cup felt smooth and heavy in her hands.

“Thank you, Tilly,” said Mama. “That helps me focus.”

Tilly stood tall on her short legs. “Now, stretch!” she said. She lifted one little foot and then the other. “Stretch up, stretch wide,” she sang. Mama stretched too. Their shells made soft bonk-bonk sounds when they gently bumped.

“Deep breath,” Tilly reminded. Together they breathed in slowly. In... and out... Tilly’s tummy felt calm, like a quiet pond.

“Last one,” Tilly whispered. “The email.” She looked at the keyboard. It seemed big. So many letters! “I don’t know how,” she said. Her head slid back into her shell a little.

Mama rolled closer in her chair. “Would you like to try? I can show you once. Then you can do it by yourself.”

Tilly blinked. She wanted to learn. “I will be open,” she said. “I will try.”

Mama showed her the send button. “We write kind words. Then we press here.”

Tilly practiced. Tap-tap. “Thank you for helping us,” she read slowly. Her claws made tiny tip-tip sounds on the keys. She checked the words again. “I can do it,” she said. Her heart felt warm. She pressed the button. Click.

“You did that by yourself,” Mama said. “You were careful. You were brave.”

Just then, the lamp flickered. Blink. Blink. The room looked dim.

“Oh,” said Tilly. “We need more light.” She looked at the window shade. It was closed. She reached up and pulled the cord. Swish! Sunlight filled the room. Golden light slid across the desk and warmed Tilly’s shell.

“Mm, warm,” Tilly murmured. She smiled. “I found a way.”

A soft buzz made Tilly turn. The printer blinked a little red light. “Paper,” it beeped. “I am out.” Tilly opened the cabinet. Ruffle-ruffle. She found a fresh pack of paper. But it was heavy. She bent her knees, held the pack close, and took small steps. Thump... thump... onto the chair. Slide... onto the desk. Rustle-rustle into the tray.

The printer purred and began to print. Whirr. Tilly watched the pages come out. “I did it!” she cheered in a tiny voice.

When all the jobs were done, Tilly made one more routine. She drew a little chart with crayons:

- Tidy pencils
- Tame cords
- Water and stretch
- Thank-you email
- Open shade
- Fill paper

She added simple pictures next to each word so she could remember on her own next time: a pencil cup, a looped cord, a cup with a drop, a smiling envelope, a sunny window, and a stack of paper.

Mama gave Tilly a gentle hug. “You were resourceful. You tried new ideas. You did so many things by yourself.”

Tilly felt shy, but she peeked out proudly. “I was scared at first,” she said, “but I can learn. I can try. I can help.”

She put her chart on the wall next to the desk. The sunlight made the colors glow. The home office felt neat, bright, and calm. Tilly’s shell felt lighter, as if it smiled too.

That afternoon, Tilly sat in the soft chair. She listened to the computer hum. She touched the warm desk. She breathed in the papery smell and the sweet crayon smell. She thought, This is my helper place. With my routine, I can do it by myself.

The End.
