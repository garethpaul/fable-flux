# Dolly the Dolphin's Quiet Corner

Dolly was a cheerful gray dolphin with sparkly silver spots. She loved to swim and play in her special closet hideaway. The closet was cozy and warm, with soft blankets hanging like seaweed and shoes lined up like colorful coral.

Every day, Dolly would splash happily in her imaginary ocean. She would sing bubbly songs and do flippy-flops between the hanging clothes. "La-la-la, splash-splash-splash!" she would sing, making the hangers jingle like wind chimes.

But today, something was wrong. Dolly's friend Benny the Bear had come to visit. Benny was much bigger than Dolly, and he wanted to play too.

"Move over, Dolly!" Benny said loudly, pushing into the small closet space. "I want to be a whale!" He started making loud "WHOOSH" sounds and swinging his arms wildly.

Dolly felt squished and uncomfortable. The closet suddenly felt too small and too noisy. Her sparkly spots seemed to dim. "Benny, this is my quiet space," she said softly, but Benny didn't hear her over his loud whale sounds.

Dolly's heart felt heavy like a stone. She curled up in the corner behind the winter coats, feeling sad and overwhelmed. The soft fabric smelled like lavender, but even that couldn't make her feel better.

"Why doesn't Benny understand?" Dolly whispered to herself. Her voice was shaky like ripples on water.

Then Dolly had an idea. She took three deep breaths, just like her mama had taught her. "In through my blowhole, out through my blowhole," she said calmly. The breathing made her feel stronger and braver.

Dolly swam over to Benny, who was still making loud whale noises. "Benny," she said gently, "can we talk?"

Benny stopped and looked at his friend. Dolly's usually bright eyes looked sad and tired.

"This closet is my special quiet place," Dolly explained. "When you play so loudly and take up all the space, it makes me feel scared and squished. Could we find a way to play together that feels good for both of us?"

Benny's brown eyes grew wide. "Oh no, Dolly! I didn't know I was making you feel bad. I just wanted to have fun with you."

"I want to have fun too," Dolly said with a small smile. "But maybe we could play more gently? And take turns?"

Benny nodded eagerly. "Yes! How about I be a quiet seahorse instead of a loud whale?"

Dolly's spots began to sparkle again. "That sounds perfect! And we could make soft music together instead of loud crashes."

So Benny became a gentle seahorse, moving slowly and gracefully. Dolly taught him her quiet bubble song: "Bubble-bubble-pop, gentle-gentle-stop." They took turns being the leader, swimming in slow, coordinated circles around the closet.

The closet felt peaceful again. The clothes swayed gently like underwater plants. Both friends felt happy and respected.

"Thank you for telling me how you felt, Dolly," Benny said. "I want to be a good friend."

"And thank you for listening, Benny," Dolly replied. "Now we both feel good!"

From that day on, whenever friends visited Dolly's closet hideaway, she would share her special rules: speak kindly, move gently, and always listen to each other's feelings. The closet remained a magical place where everyone felt safe and happy.

The End.
