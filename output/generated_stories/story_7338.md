# The Cozy Closet Club

Benny was a friendly bear cub with soft, cinnamon fur and bright, curious eyes. He loved warm hugs, sweet honey toast, and the little closet hideaway in his room. The closet felt safe and cozy. It smelled like cedar wood and clean laundry. A soft blanket covered the floor, and tiny string lights twinkled like sleepy stars.

One rainy afternoon, drip-drop sounds tapped on the window. Benny padded to the closet and whispered, “Closet hideaway, I’m here.” He pulled the blanket close and hugged his favorite pillow. His heart felt calm.

Knock, knock.

Benny peeked out. His friends stood in the doorway. There was Tilly, a tiny turtle with a bright red bow. There was Max, a busy chipmunk with stripey cheeks. And there was Lulu, a little owl with fluffy wings and big, gentle eyes.

“Can we come in?” asked Tilly. “The rain is loud, and your closet looks cozy.”

Benny smiled. “Yes! But please be gentle. This is my quiet space.”

They nodded and crawled in. The string lights glowed. The blanket was soft, and the air smelled sweet like soap. They all took deep breaths. Ahhh.

“I brought crayons!” Max chirped. “Let’s color the walls!”

Benny blinked. “I like clean walls,” he said softly. “The closet is special to me.”

Max wiggled. “But coloring is fun!”

Lulu hooted kindly. “Maybe we can color on paper instead.”

Max’s whiskers drooped. “Okay,” he said, and put the crayons on a little tray.

Tilly tugged the blanket into a big pile. “Let’s jump!” she said, bouncing a little.

Benny shook his head. “Please stop. Jumping makes the closet feel too wild. I want it calm.”

Tilly froze and looked at Benny’s face. His eyes were worried. “Oh,” she whispered. “I didn’t know. I want you to feel safe.”

“Thank you,” Benny said. He took a deep breath and felt better. His friends cared.

They all sat still for a moment. The rain sang on the roof. Tip-tap. Tip-tap.

Max tried to share a walnut, but he pushed it right under Lulu’s wing. Lulu fluffed up. “Max, please ask before you put things under my wing. It feels funny.”

“Sorry!” said Max. “Next time, I’ll ask.”

Benny looked around. He had a big thought growing in his heart. “Friends,” he said, “I think we need some closet rules. Rules can help us respect each other.”

They nodded.

“Rule one,” Benny said gently. “We ask before we touch someone else’s things, or someone else’s body. That shows respect.”

“Rule two,” Lulu added, “We listen when someone says yes or no. That keeps our bodies safe.”

“Rule three,” Tilly said, “We find fun that feels good to everyone. That is cooperation.”

Max squeaked, “Rule four: We celebrate how we are different! Like my zoom-zoom energy, Tilly’s careful steps, Lulu’s quiet voice, and Benny’s love for cozy.”

Benny smiled. “Yes. We can be different and still be kind.”

They made signs on paper with big, bright letters. Max colored fast. Swish-swish! Tilly made careful shapes. Tap-tap. Lulu drew stars and moons around the words. Benny wrote “Respect” at the top. He traced each letter slowly. R… e… s… p… e… c… t. The word felt strong and warm.

Just then, the rain boomed. Rumble!

Lulu trembled and tucked her head under her wing. “I feel scared,” she said in a small voice.

Benny scooted closer but stopped. “Lulu, may I give you a hug?” he asked.

“Yes, please,” Lulu said.

Benny wrapped her in a gentle bear cub hug. “You are safe,” he whispered. Tilly moved the blanket like a cape around them. Max found a small drum and tapped a steady beat. Dum… dum… dum… like a kind heartbeat.

The thunder slowly rolled away. Lulu peeked out and smiled. “Thank you for asking first, Benny. That made me feel respected.”

Benny nodded. “Thank you for telling me what you needed.”

After the storm, Max wanted to play a game. “Let’s be trains!” he said. “Choo-choo!” He lined up and started fast.

“Wait,” said Tilly. “I am small and slow. Can we go gentle?”

Max paused and looked at Tilly’s careful feet. “Yes,” he said. “I will go slow.” Choo… choo… The train moved kindly. Lulu added a soft “Woo-woo,” and Benny made track sounds, “Clack, clack,” with his paws. They laughed and chugged in a circle on the blanket.

When it was snack time, they shared honey toast and tiny apple slices. Crunch. Sweet. Sticky smiles.

Benny looked at his friends. “I learned something today,” he said. “When we listen to each other, and when we ask, we show respect. Respect helps everyone feel safe.”

“I learned that going slow can be fun too,” Max said.

“I learned that asking for a hug is okay,” Lulu hooted.

“I learned that quiet can feel cozy,” Tilly said softly.

Benny placed the “Respect” sign on the inside of the closet door with a little bit of tape. The string lights twinkled brighter, as if they understood.

“This is our Cozy Closet Club,” Benny said. “In our club, we respect, we cooperate, and we celebrate how we are different.”

They all put their paws, wing, and tiny turtle foot together. “Cozy Closet Club!” they cheered.

The rain stopped. Sunshine peeked in and made the string lights sparkle rainbow colors. Benny felt warm from nose to toes. His closet hideaway was still his safe place, and now it was a safe place for his friends too.

And that made his heart feel big and happy.

The End.
