# Leo Shares the Sunshine Stew

The warm kitchen glowed like morning sunshine. Pots hummed softly. The air smelled like sweet carrots, fresh peas, and baked bread. A brave little lion cub named Leo padded in, his paws making soft pat-pat sounds on the floor.

“Mama,” Leo said, his whiskers wiggling, “my tummy is rumble-rumble. May I help make lunch?”

Mama Lion smiled and tapped a wooden spoon on a big pot. “Yes, brave helper. Today we make Sunshine Stew. It will be warm, healthy, and kind.” She set a basket on the table. “We have carrots, peas, tomatoes, and corn. We will add kindness and patience too.”

Leo blinked. “Kindness goes in stew?”

Mama nodded. “Kindness goes in all good things.”

Leo stood on a little stool. The stove was warm, and steam puffed like tiny clouds. “Sss-sss,” went the pot. “Bubble-bubble,” sang the stew. Leo sniffed and purred. “It smells cozy.”

Knock-knock! There was a gentle tap at the door. Leo opened it and saw his friends. Tika the turtle wore a red scarf. Bibi the bunny held a small basket. Zuzu the zebra had a big smile. “Hi, Leo!” they chimed.

“Come in,” Leo said bravely. “We are cooking Sunshine Stew.”

Bibi bounced. “I brought one carrot!”

Zuzu clapped. “I brought a little corn!”

Tika said slowly, “I have… one tomato.”

Leo’s eyes shone. “Thank you, friends. We can all make the stew together.”

Mama Lion handed out little aprons. “Teamwork time,” she said. “We wash our hands first.” The water felt warm on their paws and hooves. Splash-splash. Swish-swish. They dried hands on soft towels.

Leo held a safe, small knife and looked at the carrots. His stomach went rumble-rumble again. He wanted to eat right away. But he remembered Mama’s words: patience. “We wait and work,” he whispered to himself.

Bibi asked, “Can I stir?”

“Yes,” Leo said. “But please stir slow so it won’t splash.” He was brave and kind with his words. That is called being assertive but gentle. Bibi nodded and stirred slowly. “Swirl, swirl,” went the spoon.

Tika washed the tomato. Drip, drip. Zuzu measured peas. “Plink, plink,” the peas fell into a little bowl.

Mama Lion smiled. “Teamwork makes stew taste better.”

Leo added a pinch of herbs. The kitchen filled with a green, fresh smell. His tummy called again. “I’m hungry now,” he said. A grumble-giggle rose from his belly.

Zuzu said, “Me too!”

Mama Lion tasted a tiny spoonful. “It needs a little more time,” she said. “Good food takes patience. We wait so the veggies get soft and sweet.”

Leo looked at the pot. Bubble-bubble. He took a deep breath. He looked at his friends. They were all waiting. He had an idea. “Let’s set the table!” he said. “We can share bowls and napkins.”

“Good idea, Leo,” Mama said.

They worked together. Leo placed small bowls in a circle. Tika set out spoons, one by one. Bibi folded napkins like little tents. Zuzu poured cool water. The table looked bright and happy.

Knock-knock! Another sound at the door. It was Pika the puppy from next door. She held a tiny empty bowl. “Hi,” she said softly. “I smelled something yummy. I forgot my lunch today.”

Leo felt his heart grow warm, like the stove. He wanted a big bowl for himself. But he looked at Pika’s hopeful eyes. He thought about kindness and teamwork. He stood tall and brave.

“Come sit with us, Pika,” Leo said. “We will share our Sunshine Stew.”

Pika’s tail wagged. “Really?”

“Really,” Leo nodded. “We have enough when we are generous.”

Bibi smiled. “Sharing makes more happy.”

Tika said, “Yes. More smiles, more yum.”

Finally, Mama Lion lifted the lid. “It is ready,” she said. Steam curled up like soft ribbons. The stew smelled sweet and savory. Leo helped by holding the ladle steady. “Scoop, scoop,” went the ladle. He served his friends first: a scoop for Bibi, a scoop for Zuzu, a scoop for Tika, and a gentle scoop for Pika. He saved a bowl for Mama. Then, at last, a warm bowl for himself.

He felt proud. His tummy still said rumble-rumble, but his heart said glow-glow.

They tasted the stew together. “Mmm,” said Bibi. “The carrots are soft.”

“Mmm,” said Zuzu. “The peas are sweet.”

“Mmm,” said Tika. “The tomato is cozy.”

Pika licked her lips. “This is the best lunch.”

Leo took a bite. Warm. Soft. Sweet. Healthy. It felt like sunshine in his mouth. He smiled so big his whiskers curled.

Mama Lion nodded. “What did we learn today?”

Leo wiped his mouth with his napkin. He spoke clearly and kindly. “We learned to be patient while the stew cooked. We learned to use our words kindly, like asking to stir slow. We learned that healthy veggies help our bodies. Most of all,” he looked at Pika and his friends, “we learned that sharing makes the soup and our hearts bigger. Generosity makes more for everyone.”

Everyone cheered a soft kitchen cheer. “Yay for sharing!”

They finished their bowls. Leo gave Pika a little container to take home. “For later,” he said.

Pika wagged. “Thank you, Leo. You are a brave and generous friend.”

Leo felt proud and warm. The kitchen hummed. The pot sighed. The friends stacked their bowls and washed up together. Splash-swish-sparkle. The warm kitchen was full of clean dishes, full tummies, and full hearts.

Leo looked at the empty pot and smiled. “When we share, we all have enough.”

The End.
