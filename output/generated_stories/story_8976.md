# The Joey Who Shared His Plan

Pip was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and bouncy feet. One sunny morning, Pip hopped into the garage workshop with a big grin. The workshop smelled like warm wood and a little like lemon soap. Sunlight made cozy squares on the floor, and tools hung on the wall like shiny, silver fish.

“I have a goal today,” Pip said, patting his small pouch. “I want to make a mailbox. A happy blue mailbox for our front path!”

Grandpa Roo was at the workbench, wearing big round glasses. He looked up and smiled. “A wonderful goal,” he said. “How will you do it?”

Pip wiggled with excitement. “I’ll do it fast! I’ll grab the wood, the paint, and the hammer. Then it will be done!”

Grandpa Roo chuckled gently. “Let’s practice good communication first. Tell me your plan, step by step. When we share our plan, we make it clear.”

Pip puffed up proudly. “Okay! Step one: find wood. Step two: paint it blue. Step three: put it together.”

Grandpa Roo nodded. “That’s a start. Let’s add more. Where will the nails go? What shapes do we need? How will we stay safe?”

Pip felt his ears wiggle. “I don’t know all those steps,” he admitted. “Can you help me make a clearer plan?”

“Of course,” said Grandpa Roo. He pulled out a soft pencil and a little notepad. “We talk it out. We draw it out. We listen.”

Together they made a list:
- Choose a smooth board.
- Measure with the tape.
- Mark lines with the pencil.
- Ask before using tools.
- Sand the edges.
- Paint last, after building.

Pip felt calm. “I like this plan,” he said. “It has small steps.”

Grandpa Roo handed Pip a tiny toy measuring tape. It clicked happily as Pip pulled it. “Let’s measure,” Grandpa Roo said.

The tape made a soft zzzzip sound. “Twenty hops long!” Pip announced.

Grandpa Roo laughed. “Let’s try inches instead of hops, little joey.”

They measured the board. Pip traced the line with his finger. “This is the roof of the mailbox,” he said. “It will be extra cute.”

While Grandpa Roo picked up the saw, Pip noticed a bright blue paint can. “Blue!” Pip squeaked. “I want to see the blue!” Before he told Grandpa Roo, Pip tugged the lid. Pop! The lid came off too fast. A little splash of blue paint dripped onto the workbench with a plop. A tiny dot splashed onto Grandpa Roo’s glasses!

Pip froze. His tummy felt wiggly. “Oh no,” he whispered. He wanted to hide in his pouch.

Grandpa Roo took off his glasses and blinked. He wiped them with a soft cloth. He looked at Pip’s worried face and said gently, “Accidents happen. What can we do?”

Pip remembered. Good communication. “Grandpa, I am sorry,” Pip said, eyes wide. “I opened the paint without telling you. I should have used my words. May I help clean it?”

Grandpa Roo’s face softened. “Thank you for your clear words and your apology, Pip. That helps me understand. Yes, let’s clean it together.”

They used a damp rag. Swish, swish. The blue paint made light swirls, and then the bench was clean again. Pip took a deep breath. “I feel better when I talk about it,” he said.

Grandpa Roo nodded. “Talking helps us solve problems. Next time, what will you do before opening the paint?”

Pip stood tall. “I will say, ‘Grandpa, may I open the paint now?’ I will wait for your answer.”

“Great plan,” said Grandpa Roo. “Now, back to our goal.”

They sanded the wood. Scritch, scritch. Pip liked the tickle sound. They hammered tiny nails. Tap-tap-tap! The mailbox sides stood up like little walls. Pip smiled. “It looks like a small house.”

“Time to paint?” Pip asked.

“Time to communicate,” Grandpa Roo reminded. “Tell me what you want to do.”

Pip grinned. “Grandpa, may I paint the roof blue first?”

“Yes,” Grandpa Roo said. “Thank you for asking.”

The brush slid over the wood with a whispery swish. The blue paint smelled a little sweet and a little sharp. Pip made careful strokes. “I like taking turns,” he said. “First the roof, then the door.”

Soon the mailbox shone sky-blue, like a tiny piece of the morning.

They waited for it to dry. Pip wiggled again. “I want to put a star on it,” he said. “A golden star.”

“Let’s add that to the plan,” Grandpa Roo said. “Where will the star go?”

Pip touched the front. “Right here, so the mail feels welcome.”

Grandpa Roo smiled. “A kind touch.”

When everything was dry, they set the mailbox on the workbench. Pip tapped his chin. “Grandpa, may I carry it outside?” he asked.

“Yes, with two paws,” Grandpa Roo said. “And I will carry the post.”

They hopped to the path. The sun felt warm on Pip’s ears. Birds chirped, tweet-tweet, like tiny bells. Together they set the mailbox in its place. Pip patted it proudly.

Mama Roo came to see. “What a wonderful mailbox,” she said. “How did you do it?”

Pip stood tall. “We had a goal. We made a clear plan. We talked and listened. I made a mistake. I said sorry. We fixed it together.”

Mama Roo hugged Pip. “That is the best way to work.”

Pip hugged back. His heart felt light. “I learned that when I use my words, I feel safe and strong. Talking makes big jobs small.”

Grandpa Roo nodded. “And goals are easier when we share them.”

Pip looked at the blue mailbox with its shining golden star. He whispered, “Hello, mail. You have a happy home.”

They all laughed, and the birds sang along.

The End.
