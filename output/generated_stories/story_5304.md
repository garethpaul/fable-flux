# Roo's Ready Pack

In a warm, comfortable nursery, a joyful kangaroo joey named Roo bounced on a soft rug. The rug felt fuzzy under Roo’s toes. Sunlight made tiny stars on the wall. The room smelled like clean blankets and pine blocks. Roo’s mama folded a little blue backpack by the door.

“Today is park day!” Mama said, smiling. “Swinging, running, and a snack outside.”

Roo clapped. “Yay! I love the park! Zip-zoom!” Roo hopped in a little circle.

Mama knelt down. “Before we zip-zoom, we get ready. We make a plan. That is called being prepared.”

Roo nodded. “Prepared. Like… a ready roo!”

Mama laughed softly. “Yes, my ready roo. Let’s pack your bag.”

Roo peered into the backpack. “What goes in?”

Mama pointed to a small list with pictures. “Water bottle, hat, sunscreen, a little towel, and a kind heart.”

Roo blinked. “A kind heart goes in a bag?”

Mama tapped Roo’s chest. “It goes right here. We bring kindness everywhere.”

Roo patted his chest. “I will bring it.”

They packed together. The water bottle went glug-glug as Roo tested the lid. The hat felt smooth and cool. Mama rubbed sunscreen that smelled like coconut onto Roo’s cheeks. “Rub rub! All done,” she said.

Roo twitched his nose. “I smell coconut clouds!”

Mama added a tiny bandage box. “Just in case.”

Roo zipped the bag. “I am a ready roo!”

On the way to the park, Roo bounced carefully, backpack snug. The air outside was fresh and a little chilly. “I can’t wait to swing,” Roo said.

When they reached the park, children laughed and birds chirped. The swings made a squeak-squeak sound. Grass tickled Roo’s feet. Roo put on his hat and ran to a low balance beam.

But at the edge of the walkway, Roo saw a little bunny named Bella. Bella sat very still on the bench, hugging her knees. Her eyes were wet and shiny.

Roo stopped hopping. He took a breath. His heart felt warm and wiggly. “Hello,” Roo said softly. “I’m Roo.”

Bella sniffled. “Hi.”

Roo tilted his head. “Do you want to play?”

Bella shook her head. “I can’t. I forgot my hat. The sun is bright. My ears feel hot. And I spilled my water. I am not ready.”

Roo looked at Bella’s droopy ears. He looked at his backpack. He remembered Mama’s words. Be prepared. Bring kindness.

Roo opened his bag. Zip! The sound made Bella look up.

“I have things,” Roo said. “We can share.”

Bella blinked. “Share?”

Roo took out his little towel. “This can be for your paws.” He dabbed at a drop of water near Bella’s paw. The towel felt soft and warm from the sun.

“Thank you,” Bella whispered.

Roo took out his water bottle. “We can take turns.” He held it out with both paws. “Sip-sip?”

Bella looked at Mama Bunny, who nodded. Bella took a tiny sip. “Ahh. Thank you. The water is cool.”

Roo smiled. His chest felt bigger, like a happy balloon. He looked at his hat. He remembered the sun. Roo reached into the bag again. “I also have sunscreen. It smells nice.”

Bella wrinkled her nose. “Like cookies?”

“Like coconut clouds,” Roo said. He asked Mama for help. Mama rubbed a little sunscreen on Bella’s cheeks and ears, gentle and slow. Bella closed her eyes. “It feels smooth,” she said.

Roo put the hat back on his own head and thought. His ears felt fine. Bella still needed shade. Roo looked around and spotted a small tree with spotted leaves near the sandbox.

“I have an idea,” Roo said, feeling a spark in his tummy. “Let’s play in the shade!”

Bella’s ears perked up. “We can?”

“Yes!” Roo pointed. “Look. Shade.” He hopped to the tree and patted the ground. “It is cool here.”

Bella stood and followed. The ground felt cool and a little bumpy. Leaves made whisper sounds. Mama Bunny and Roo’s mama smiled and watched from the bench.

Roo set the little towel on the ground like a picnic mat. They sat together. Roo made a tiny hill with sand. Bella made a wiggly road with her paw. “Beep beep,” Roo said. “Here comes the sand car.”

Bella giggled. “Beep beep!”

Soon, other friends came. A squirrel brought a small bucket. A duckling brought a feather. Everyone took turns. Roo poured water to make the sand smooth. Bella patted the road flat. “We are a team,” Roo said.

After some time, Roo felt his belly rumble. “Snack time!” Mama called. The smell of apple slices floated in the air. Crunch, crunch! Roo munched. Bella munched. They smiled with apple cheeks.

When it was time to go, Bella hugged Roo. “You were kind,” she said. “You shared. You had a plan. You helped me play.”

Roo’s heart glowed. “I am a ready roo. Being ready helps me be kind.”

On the walk home, Roo held Mama’s paw. “Mama,” Roo said, “I liked sharing my things. I liked playing in the shade. I liked thinking of ideas.”

Mama squeezed his paw. “That is resourcefulness,” she said. “You used what you had. You were prepared. And most of all, you were kind.”

Roo nodded. The backpack felt light, and his heart felt full. “Next time,” Roo said, “I will pack two hats. One for me. One to share.”

Mama smiled. “What a wonderful plan.”

Roo bounced softly on the rug back in the comfortable nursery. He set the backpack by the door and tucked a second hat inside. He patted his chest. “Kind heart,” he whispered. “Packed and ready.”

The End.
