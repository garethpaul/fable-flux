# Benny Beaver's Breakfast Bounce

In a bright dining room with sunny windows, a busy beaver named Benny wiggled his whiskers and looked at the big wooden table. The table smelled like warm pancakes and fresh berries. The chairs were smooth and shiny. The floor felt cool under his little webbed feet.

“I have so much to do today,” Benny said, patting his round belly. “But first, breakfast!”

Mama Beaver put a plate in front of him. “Good morning, Benny. Let’s start the day strong. After we eat, we can move our bodies.”

“Move our bodies?” Benny asked, tilting his head. “Like a game?”

“Like exercise!” Mama smiled. “Exercise makes us strong, happy, and ready to learn.”

Benny picked up a juicy blueberry. It popped sweetly in his mouth. “Mm! I like happy and strong,” he said, chewing carefully.

Papa Beaver brought in a small basket. Inside were napkins, two wooden spoons, and a soft rolled-up mat. “We can turn the dining room into our exercise space,” he said. “We will be careful and safe.”

Benny’s eyes grew wide. “Exercise right here? In the dining room?”

Mama nodded. “Yes. We make room. We make a plan. We move together.”

Benny helped push the chairs in. The chair legs whispered shhh across the floor. He placed the napkins in a neat stack and rolled the mat open. It made a whoosh sound, like a tiny wave.

“I’m ready!” Benny said. “But… what if I am not good at it?”

Mama put a gentle paw on his shoulder. “We try, we practice, we grow,” she said. “That’s a growth mindset.”

Papa added, “We say, ‘I can learn. I can get better.’”

Benny took a deep breath. He smelled maple syrup and clean wood. “Okay. I can learn. I can get better.”

They started with warm-up wiggles. Benny wiggled his whiskers and shoulders. He wiggled his tail. Swish-swish went his flat beaver tail on the mat. He giggled. “This feels silly!”

“Silly is welcome,” Mama laughed. “Now, let’s stretch.”

They stretched high, reaching for the ceiling. The ceiling light shined like a little sun. Benny felt his back grow long. “Hello, ceiling!” he said.

“Hello, strong body,” Papa said. “Next, we do chair marches.”

They stood by the smooth chairs. Benny marched in place. His feet went tap, tap, tap on the floor. “Left, right, left, right!” he sang.

After ten taps, his cheeks felt warm. “Whew!” he puffed. “This is hard.”

Papa nodded. “Hard can be good. It means we are growing.”

Benny smiled and tried again. “I can learn. I can get better.”

Next were spoon lifts. They held a wooden spoon in each paw. Up and down, up and down. The spoons made tiny whoop sounds through the air. Benny felt his arms working. “I’m strong like a log!” he cheered.

Mama clapped softly. “You are doing great. Can we try tail taps?”

Benny lay on the mat and tapped his tail gently side to side. Tap-tap, tap-tap. He felt his tummy muscles wake up like little birds. “Tweet, tweet!” he chirped.

“Now a fun one,” Papa said. “The Pancake Flip Jump!”

Benny’s eyes sparkled. “A jump? In the dining room?” He looked at the ceiling again. “Careful jumps?”

“Careful jumps,” Mama agreed. “Bend, then hop. Land soft like a pancake on a plate.”

Benny bent his knees. He hopped. Boing! He landed soft. Plop. “I did it!” he said, surprised and proud.

“Let’s try three more,” Papa said.

Benny did one. Boing, plop. He did two. Boing, plop. On the third, his legs felt wobbly. “Oh,” he said, breathing fast. “This is tough.”

Mama knelt beside him. “Let’s rest. Put a paw on your heart. Feel it beat.”

Benny felt thump-thump, thump-thump. It was strong and quick. He listened. He breathed in through his nose. He breathed out slow. The dining room smelled like syrup and soap.

“Thank you, heart,” Benny whispered. “Thank you, legs. Thank you, air.”

Mama smiled. “That is gratitude. We thank our bodies for helping us.”

Benny sat up taller. “I am thankful for my family, too.”

Papa beamed. “We are thankful for you, Benny.”

They finished with a calm stretch called the River Bend. They reached forward like a river flowing. Benny felt soft and peaceful.

After the exercise, Benny washed his paws. The water was warm and splashy. He sat back at the table. His pancakes were still cozy. He took a bite. The syrup tasted sweet and sunny.

“How do you feel?” Mama asked.

Benny grinned. “I feel strong. I feel happy. I feel proud.”

Papa nodded. “That is what exercise can do. It helps our bodies and our feelings.”

Benny thought for a moment. “Sometimes it felt hard. But when it felt hard, I tried again. I took a breath. I thanked my body.”

“That’s our growth mindset,” Mama said. “We try, we learn, we grow.”

Benny raised his cup of milk. “To exercise! To gratitude! To growing!”

They all clinked cups gently. Clink.

The sun shone brighter through the window. The dining room felt warm and full of love. Benny licked a drop of syrup from his whiskers and giggled. “Tomorrow,” he said, “can we have another breakfast bounce?”

“Yes,” Papa said. “Every day is a new chance to move.”

Benny wiggled happily. “I can learn. I can get better. I am thankful.” He took another sweet, sticky bite and felt his heart say, thump-thump, yes.

The End.
