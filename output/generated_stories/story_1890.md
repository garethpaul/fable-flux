# Fox Kit’s Big Open-Mind Day

In a sunny home office, a curious fox kit named Fenn peeked over the desk. The desk smelled like warm paper and lemon cleaner. Keys clicked on the keyboard. Tap-tap-tap! The computer screen glowed like a tiny window to the world.

“Wow,” said Fenn, his ears perking up. “So many sounds and pictures!”

Mama Fox worked at the desk, wearing soft blue headphones. “I’m writing to friends far away,” she said. “Some live across oceans.”

“Oceans?” Fenn’s eyes grew wide. “Are they really big?”

“Very big,” Mama said, smiling. “Want to help me learn about their games and snacks?”

“Yes!” Fenn bounced. His fluffy tail swished like a little broom.

On the desk sat a round globe. It was smooth and cool under Fenn’s paw. The colors were bright—blue seas, green lands, yellow deserts. A tiny paper smelled dusty and sweet when Fenn sniffed it.

Fenn spotted a small box on the shelf. “What’s this?” he asked.

“Careful,” Mama said gently. “That box has special stamps. Friends sent them from many places.”

Fenn was curious. He opened the box. Oh! The stamps were beautiful. One had a red flower. One had a snow mountain. One had a smiling sun with rays like noodles.

Fenn’s whiskers twitched. “I want to show them to my stuffed owl!”

He grabbed the stamps and ran. Some stamps fluttered like little leaves. One landed in a glass of water. Plop!

“Oh no!” Fenn’s tummy felt wiggly. “I made a mess.”

Mama took off her headphones. Her eyes looked surprised, then soft. “Fenn, are you okay?”

“I’m sorry,” Fenn whispered. “I didn’t ask. I wasn’t careful.”

Mama knelt down and hugged him. “Thank you for telling the truth,” she said. “Let’s make amends. That means we fix what we can.”

Fenn nodded. “How do we fix it?”

“First,” Mama said, “we take the stamp out of the water.” She lifted it gently with a spoon. “Then we dry it with a soft cloth.” Fenn dabbed, dab-dab, like a tiny cloud. They gathered the other stamps and placed them back in the box.

Fenn looked at the stamp with the smiling sun. “Whose stamp is this?”

“That one came from Maya,” Mama said. “She lives where the air is warm and there are mango trees.”

“Mmmm,” Fenn said. “Mango sounds sweet.”

Mama smiled. “Let’s send a note to Maya and tell her we liked her stamp. We can ask what game she plays at recess.”

Fenn sat on Mama’s lap. The chair was soft and squeaky. Squeak! Mama typed. Tap-tap. Fenn spoke slowly. “Dear Maya, thank you for the sun-stamp. What games do you play? We play hide-and-seek. From Fenn.”

They sent the note. Whoosh! Fenn grinned. “I like learning about friends far away.”

Mama nodded. “When we keep an open mind, we try new ideas, and we listen to others. We learn so much.”

Fenn blinked. “Open mind? Like opening a door?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Like a friendly door in your head and heart.”

The computer chimed. Ding! A message from Maya appeared, with a picture. Maya and her friends were playing a circle game. They clapped and laughed. Fenn could almost hear the happy claps—clap, clap, clap—and smell sweet fruit in the sunny air.

“Maya plays a circle game!” Fenn cheered. “Can we try it?”

“Let’s learn the steps,” Mama said. They watched a short video. Fenn clapped slow, then fast. He spun and giggled until his tail made a soft whoosh!

“Another message,” Mama said. “This one is from Kenji. He lives where cherry trees bloom pink in spring.”

Fenn’s nose twitched. “I bet it smells like flowers.”

The picture showed a lunch with little rice balls. “Kenji likes seaweed snacks,” Mama said.

“Seaweed?” Fenn wrinkled his nose. “That sounds yucky.”

Mama tilted her head. “Open mind, remember? We can’t know until we try.”

Fenn thought. He took a deep breath. “Okay. I will try.”

Mama opened a small packet from a drawer. The seaweed was shiny and green. It smelled salty, like the ocean on the globe. Fenn nibbled. Crunch. His eyes widened. “It’s crunchy and salty! I like it!”

He jumped up and down. “I’m glad I tried!”

Mama laughed. “That is open-mindedness.”

Fenn looked at the stamps again. He held the damp sun-stamp carefully. “Mama, I want to make amends more. Can we send a new stamp to Maya?”

“Excellent idea,” Mama said. They chose a stamp with a fox paw and a tiny star. Fenn pressed it on an envelope. Press! He also drew a picture of the circle game with crayons that smelled like wax and rainbows.

They wrote to Kenji, too. “Dear Kenji, I tried seaweed. Crunchy! What songs do you sing?” Fenn added a little music note drawing.

The home office felt cozy and busy. The keyboard clicked. The globe spun. Fenn’s heart felt warm.

“I learned something,” Fenn said. “When I rushed, I made a mess. But I told the truth and fixed it. And when I kept my mind open, I tried new things and made new friends.”

Mama hugged him. “That’s a big lesson for a small fox.”

Fenn smiled. “My mind-door is open.”

The computer chimed again. Ding! Friends from far away were smiling on the screen. Fenn waved his paw. “Hello, world!”

The End.
