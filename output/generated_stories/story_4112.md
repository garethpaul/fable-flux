# Leo's Brave Building Adventure

Little Leo the lion cub sat on his soft blue carpet in his cozy bedroom. The afternoon sun streamed through his window, making golden patches on the floor. But Leo wasn't happy. His bottom lip stuck out in a sad pout.

"My tower keeps falling down!" Leo said with a big sigh. Colorful wooden blocks were scattered all around him. He had been trying to build the tallest tower ever, but every time he got close to finishing, CRASH! Down it would tumble.

Leo's mama heard the crash from the kitchen. She peeked her fluffy head into his room. "What's wrong, my brave little cub?"

"I can't do it, Mama," Leo said, his golden eyes filling with tears. "The blocks won't stay up. I'm not good at building."

Mama Lion sat down on the soft carpet next to Leo. She smelled like warm cookies and sunshine. "Oh, my sweet Leo. Building tall towers is very tricky. Even grown-up lions have trouble sometimes."

"Really?" Leo asked, wiping his nose with his tiny paw.

"Really," Mama said with a warm smile. "But do you know what makes a lion truly brave?"

Leo shook his head.

"A brave lion tries again, even when things are hard," Mama explained. "Should we try building together?"

Leo looked at the scattered blocks. Some were red like fire trucks. Some were blue like the sky. Some were yellow like bananas. They looked friendly sitting there on his carpet.

"Okay," Leo said quietly. "But what if it falls again?"

"Then we'll build it again," Mama said. "And again. And again. That's what brave lions do."

Leo picked up a big red block. It felt smooth and solid in his small paws. "This one can be the bottom," he said, placing it carefully on the carpet.

"Perfect choice!" Mama cheered. "Strong foundations help towers stand tall."

Leo added a blue block on top. Then a yellow one. His tongue stuck out a little as he concentrated very hard. The tower was getting taller!

"Look, Mama! It's working!" Leo's tail swished with excitement.

But then... WOBBLE! The tower started to lean.

"Oh no!" Leo gasped.

"Quick thinking time," Mama said calmly. "What could we do?"

Leo looked at the wobbly tower. He thought very hard. Then he carefully moved the yellow block to make it more centered. The tower stopped wobbling!

"I fixed it!" Leo roared proudly. "I didn't give up!"

"You sure didn't," Mama said, giving him a big, warm hug. "You were brave and kept trying."

Leo added three more blocks. Then four more. His tower was now taller than his stuffed elephant! It stood straight and strong on his bedroom carpet.

"I did it, Mama! I really did it!" Leo danced around his tower, his little mane bouncing.

"Yes, you did," Mama said. "And do you know why?"

"Because I was brave and didn't give up!" Leo said, puffing out his chest proudly.

"That's right. When we keep trying, even when things are hard, we can do amazing things."

Leo looked at his beautiful tower. It was the most wonderful thing he had ever built. The blocks seemed to sparkle in the golden sunlight.

From that day on, whenever something felt too hard, Leo remembered his tower. He remembered that brave little lions never give up. They try again and again until they succeed.

And Leo felt very brave indeed.

The End.
