# Bubbles in the Garden Shed

Bubbles was a playful dolphin with bright, curious eyes and a giggle like tiny bells. One sunny day, a big wave rolled Bubbles up, up, up through a sparkling stream that ran behind a little house. With a big splash and a whoosh, Bubbles slid right into the open door of a garden shed.

“Hello?” Bubbles called, his voice soft and bubbly. The shed smelled like fresh earth and orange soap. Sunlight peeked through a dusty window. Shelves held shiny tools, soft gloves, seed packets, and a small blue watering can. A broom leaned in the corner like a tall, sleepy friend.

“Who’s there?” squeaked Moppy the Mop.

“I’m Bubbles,” said the dolphin. “I’m visiting! I love to play.”

Rake rattled gently and said, “This is a tidy place. We like it calm and neat.”

Bubbles swished his tail. “I can be neat! But can we also have fun?”

“Fun is fine,” whispered the Seed Packets, rustling like tiny paper leaves, “as long as you respect others.”

Bubbles blinked. “Respect means being kind, right?”

“And careful,” said the watering can, tipping a little. “We all share this space.”

Bubbles took a deep breath. The air tasted a bit like wet wood and lemon soap. He wanted to play a swooshy game. But he also wanted to be kind. He made a goal in his heart. “Goal one: Ask before I play with things. Goal two: Put things back. Goal three: Help the shed friends feel safe.”

“Can we play a gentle game?” Bubbles asked. “We can move like slow seaweed.”

“That sounds safe,” said the gloves. “I like gentle.”

Bubbles glided carefully over the smooth floor. “Swoosh… slow… swoosh,” he sang. The broom hummed along. Everyone felt happy.

Then, outside, a wind blew in a crinkly sound. A bit of plastic rolled to the shed door. It tumbled in and bumped the watering can. “Oh!” cried the watering can. “That hurts.”

Bubbles’ heart thumped. “I can help.” He nudged the plastic with his nose.

“Be careful!” warned Rake. “Don’t splash the seed packets.”

“I will be gentle,” said Bubbles. He pushed the plastic bit into a small bin. “Trash belongs in the bin. We care for our world.”

“Thank you,” said the watering can. “You care for us and the Earth.”

Bubbles felt warm inside, like sunshine. He wanted to play a faster game now. “Can I try a little jump?” he asked. “I will check first. Is everyone okay with that?”

“Not too fast,” said Moppy. “I get dizzy.”

“Could we make a plan?” asked the gloves. “First, a tiny jump. Then, a rest.”

“Yes,” said Bubbles. “That is fair.” He made another goal. “I will listen to ‘yes’ and ‘no.’”

Bubbles bent his tail and did a very small hop. Plop! He landed softly. “How was that?”

“Just right,” said the seed packets, rustling happily. “You asked first. That shows respect.”

A little mouse peeked from a hole in the wall. “Hi, I’m Pip. I’m small and shy.”

“Hi, Pip,” Bubbles said gently. “Do you want to play?”

“I like quiet games,” Pip whispered.

Bubbles smiled. “We can make a quiet song. Listen.” He clicked softly, like tiny raindrops. Pip’s ears twitched. The shed filled with gentle sounds—tap from the broom, tish-tish from the watering can, rustle-rustle from the seeds. It sounded like soft rain on leaves.

“Beautiful,” sighed the tools. Everyone felt calm.

Just then, a bucket rolled too close to the door and bumped Pip. “Eep!” Pip squeaked and hid.

Bubbles felt a bubble of worry. He wanted to fix it. “I’m sorry, Pip,” he said. “Are you okay?”

Pip peeked out. “I’m okay. I need space.”

Bubbles nodded. “Thank you for telling me. You can say what you need.” He turned to the bucket. “Bucket, please stay by the wall so you don’t roll.”

“I will,” the bucket clanked softly. “I didn’t mean to bump.”

Bubbles took a breath. “I will make a chart in my mind,” he said. “Ask, listen, care.” He thought about his goals again: be gentle, ask first, put back, help others, and care for Earth.

The sun moved, and golden light danced on the floor. Bubbles saw tiny crumbs by the door. “These could hurt little paws,” he said. “Let’s clean together!”

“Yes!” said Moppy. Swish-swish.

“I can sprinkle a little water,” said the watering can. “Drip, drip.”

“I can sweep,” said the broom. “Whoosh, whoosh.”

“I can sing a work song,” said Bubbles. “Swoosh-a-doo, we care for you!”

Everyone helped. The crumbs went away. The floor shined.

Pip came out, smiling. “Thank you for listening to me and cleaning up.”

“You are welcome,” said Bubbles. “We are all different. That is special.”

Rake nodded. “You are a dolphin. We are tools. Pip is small. We all matter. When we respect each other, we all feel safe.”

Bubbles made one last gentle lap around the shed. “I learned something big today,” he said. “Respect means asking, listening, and caring. It means celebrating our differences and meeting our goals with kindness.”

The wind outside was soft now. The garden smelled sweet. Bubbles heard the stream whisper. “I think the wave is ready to take me home,” he said. “Can I visit again?”

“Yes,” said everyone. “Please come back.”

Bubbles smiled. “I will. I will keep my goals and use my voice to share my needs. And I will care for our Earth.”

He waved his fin and slid out the door with a gentle splash-swish, back to the stream, back to the sea.

In the quiet shed, the friends felt safe and happy. They remembered Bubbles’ song: “Ask, listen, care.”

And they smiled, knowing respect makes every place a warm, friendly home.

The End.
