# Millie Mouse Leads the Treehouse Feast

Millie was a kind mouse with soft gray fur and bright button eyes. She lived in a cozy treehouse high in a big old oak. The treehouse smelled like pine needles and warm oatmeal. The floor was smooth, and the windows let in golden sunlight. Birds chirped. Leaves whispered. Millie smiled.

One morning, Millie peeked into her little pantry. “Oh,” she said softly. “We have crumbs, a carrot, and a very tiny piece of cheese.” Her tummy rumbled. “I am hungry, and my friends will be hungry, too.”

She looked out the window and saw her friends. Benny the bunny hopped below. Tilly the turtle stretched her neck. Pip and Dot, two little birds, fluttered on a branch. Millie felt a brave idea grow in her chest, warm as sunshine.

“I will lead a sharing feast!” Millie squeaked. “I can help us all eat well.”

She opened the door and called, “Friends! Come up, please!” Her voice was small but strong. “We will make a healthy feast together!”

Benny’s ears wiggled. “A feast?” he asked as he hopped up the ladder.

“A healthy feast,” Millie said. “We will bring food, try new bites, and care for our bodies.”

Tilly climbed slowly, tap-tap-tap. “I am not fast,” she said, “but I can help.”

Pip and Dot fluttered in, their feathers soft and bright. “Tweet! We can sing,” they chirped.

Millie set a tiny table. She put out little leaf plates and acorn cups. She washed her carrot. She placed napkins made from clean cloth squares. It looked welcoming. It smelled clean and fresh.

Benny wrinkled his nose. “I like carrots, but I want cake.”

Millie nodded kindly. “Cake is special. Today we will try foods that make us strong. Let’s each bring something from the forest. We can be brave and open-minded.”

“Open-minded?” Tilly asked.

“It means we try new things and listen,” Millie said gently. “We can learn from each other.”

Millie took a deep breath. “I will lead. We will make a plan.”

She drew a tiny map on a leaf with a berry. “Benny, you hop to the garden and see if there are peas. Tilly, please check the sunny patch for tomatoes. Pip and Dot, look for seeds and sweet berries. I will gather nuts and a few herbs.”

Benny bounced. “I can do peas!” he said.

Tilly nodded. “Tomatoes, slowly,” she agreed.

“Tweet-tweet, seeds and berries!” sang Pip and Dot.

Millie smiled. “Thank you. We will meet back here before the sun reaches the high branch.” She pointed to the bright spot in the tree. “We will wash our food. We will taste it. We will be brave.”

They all set off. Millie scurried along a root path. The ground felt cool under her paws. She found crunchy walnuts and little almonds. She sniffed green mint leaves. The mint smelled fresh, like the first morning after rain. She tucked the herbs into her basket.

When Millie came back, Benny was already there. He poured shiny peas into a bowl. “Pop! Pop!” the peas made tiny sounds.

Tilly arrived next, carrying two small tomatoes. “They are red and round,” she said proudly.

Pip and Dot fluttered in last. “Blueberries!” they chirped. The berries looked like little moons.

Millie washed everything with clean water. The berries looked brighter. The peas smelled sweet and green. The tomatoes were warm from the sun. The nuts clicked softly in the bowl.

Benny peeked. “I am still thinking about cake,” he muttered.

Millie touched his paw. “Let’s try one brave bite first,” she said. “Leaders try new things and help friends try, too. We can be strong together.”

Benny took a pea. He popped it into his mouth. “Pop!” He grinned. “It is sweet!”

Tilly bit a tomato slice. Juice dripped down her chin. “Mmm,” she hummed. “Warm and bright.”

Pip and Dot nibbled seeds. “Crunch-crunch!” they chirped happily.

Millie sprinkled mint on the peas and tomatoes. She cracked a nut and shared small pieces. “Let’s build plates with colors,” she said. “Green peas, red tomatoes, blue berries, brown nuts.” She made a rainbow on her leaf plate.

Benny chewed. “This is fun,” he said. “I feel brave.”

“You are brave,” said Millie. “You listened. You tried.”

Pip and Dot sang a cheerful tune. Tilly swayed slowly. The treehouse felt full of light and love. The wind brought the smell of mint and leaves. Everyone ate until their tummies felt just right.

Then the wind gusted. The ladder wobbled! Pip squeaked. “Oh no!”

Millie put her paw up. “It’s okay,” she said calmly. “Let’s be safe. Tilly, please hold the ladder steady. Benny, gather the plates. Pip and Dot, close the window.”

They all moved together. Tilly braced the ladder, strong and steady. Benny stacked plates. Pip and Dot pulled the tiny window closed with a soft click.

The ladder stopped shaking. The wind grew gentle again. Millie smiled. “We did it. We worked as a team.”

Benny looked at Millie. “You were a good leader,” he said.

Millie’s whiskers twitched. “A leader listens, makes a plan, and cares for others,” she said. “A leader is brave and open-minded. A leader helps friends choose foods that help us grow.”

They all sat in a cozy circle. Their bellies felt happy. Their hearts felt proud.

“Thank you, Millie,” Tilly said. “I learned to try new foods.”

“Tweet! We learned to work together,” sang Pip and Dot.

“And I learned that peas can pop,” Benny giggled.

Millie hugged them all. “We learned that leadership means helping, sharing, and being brave,” she said softly. “And good food helps our bodies and our courage.”

The sun slipped lower, painting the room gold. The treehouse seemed to hum with calm.

“We did our best today,” Millie whispered. “We can do it again tomorrow.”

The End.
