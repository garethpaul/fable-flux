# Nutkin's Big Helper Day

Nutkin the squirrel loved his home in the big oak tree next to Mrs. Chen's balcony garden. Every morning, he would peek through the green leaves and watch the colorful flowers dance in the sunshine.

"Good morning, flowers!" Nutkin chirped happily, his fluffy tail twitching with excitement.

But today was different. As Nutkin looked down at the garden, he saw something that made his little heart sad. The beautiful red tomatoes were drooping, the yellow sunflowers hung their heavy heads, and the green lettuce leaves looked very thirsty.

"Oh no!" squeaked Nutkin. "All the plants look so sad and wilted!"

Just then, he heard a tiny voice. "Help us, please! We're so thirsty!"

Nutkin looked around and saw a small ladybug sitting on a droopy daisy. Her red wings sparkled in the morning light, but she looked worried.

"I'm Rosie," said the ladybug. "Mrs. Chen went away for the weekend and forgot to water us. We need water soon, or we might not make it!"

Nutkin's whiskers twitched as he thought hard. The watering can was so big and heavy - much too big for one small squirrel to carry. But then he had an idea!

"Don't worry, Rosie! I know just what to do. But I'll need help from all my friends!"

Nutkin scampered down the tree and called out loudly, "Friends! Friends! Come quickly! The garden needs our help!"

Soon, three blue jays flew down from the sky. "What's wrong, Nutkin?" asked Jay, the biggest bird.

"The plants are thirsty, and the watering can is too heavy for me alone. Will you help?" asked Nutkin.

"Of course!" chirped all three jays together.

Next, two busy ants marched up from the ground. "We heard you need help!" said Annie Ant, her tiny voice strong and determined.

"Yes! But how can we work together?" wondered Nutkin.

Rosie the ladybug flew up and said, "I have an idea! Nutkin, you're so clever with your paws - you can turn on the water tap. Jays, you're strong fliers - you can carry the small watering cup. And Annie and her friend can help guide us to each plant!"

Everyone nodded excitedly. Working together sounded like fun!

Nutkin carefully climbed to the water tap with his nimble paws and turned it on. The jays took turns carrying a small cup back and forth, their blue feathers shining as they flew. The ants marched importantly from plant to plant, showing everyone who needed water most. Rosie flew around giving encouragement to everyone.

"This plant next!" called Annie Ant.

"Coming right up!" sang the jays as they swooped down with fresh water.

"You're all doing great!" cheered Nutkin as he watched the plants begin to perk up.

Soon, the tomatoes stood tall and red, the sunflowers lifted their bright yellow faces to the sun, and the lettuce leaves turned a happy green color.

"We did it!" squeaked Nutkin, clapping his tiny paws.

"Thank you, everyone!" whispered the flowers in the gentle breeze.

All the animal friends sat together on the balcony railing, feeling proud and happy. The garden smelled fresh and sweet, and everything looked beautiful again.

"We couldn't have done this alone," said Nutkin. "But together, we saved the whole garden!"

"That's what friends do," smiled Rosie. "We help each other!"

From that day on, whenever someone in the balcony garden needed help, all the friends worked together. They learned that the best way to solve big problems was with teamwork, kindness, and caring hearts.

The End.
