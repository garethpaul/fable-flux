# Poppy the Patient Panda on the Rooftop Garden

Poppy was a patient panda. She lived in a tall building with a sunny rooftop garden. The roof felt warm under her paws. A soft breeze tickled her whiskers. Birds chirped, “Tweet, tweet!” and tiny bees hummed, “Bzzz,” around bright flowers.

Poppy loved to visit the rooftop every morning. She would stretch her arms to the sky. “Reach, reach,” she said. She would wiggle her toes. “Wiggle, wiggle,” she giggled. Stretching made her body feel awake.

One day, Poppy had a problem. Her tummy made a funny sound. “Grrr-rrrumble,” it said. Poppy frowned and patted her round belly. “I am hungry,” she said. “But what should I eat?”

On the rooftop, there was a little garden with many pots. There were crunchy green leaves, red tomatoes, yellow bananas in a fruit basket, and purple eggplants. There was also a small basket of bamboo shoots, Poppy’s favorite snack.

Poppy looked at the bamboo. “I love bamboo,” she whispered. But then she remembered what her neighbor, Mrs. Sun, had said. “Food is like a rainbow,” Mrs. Sun had told her. “Different colors help your body in different ways.”

Poppy wanted to learn. She wanted to be strong and healthy. She wanted to play and run and dance. She took a deep breath of the warm, tomato-smelling air. She listened to the bees. She felt patient and calm.

Just then, some friends came to the rooftop.

Milo the monkey swung from the rail. “Wheee!” he laughed. His tail swished. “Hi, Poppy! What are you doing?”

“I am thinking about food,” said Poppy. “My tummy is rumbling.”

Lina the ladybug landed on a leaf. “I like leaves with tiny water drops,” she said. “Sip, sip.” She giggled.

Avery the parrot flapped her bright wings. “Squawk! Try a rainbow! Squawk!” she said.

Poppy nodded. “A rainbow of food,” she repeated. “But how?”

Mrs. Sun came up the stairs. She wore a bright scarf with many colors. She smiled kindly. “Hello, friends. What a beautiful day for a meal. What is the problem?”

“I am hungry,” Poppy said. “I want to eat bamboo, but I also want to be strong and healthy. I want to learn what to eat.”

“That is a good problem,” said Mrs. Sun. “Let’s solve it together. We will make a rainbow meal. And we will move our bodies, too.”

Poppy’s ears perked up. “A rainbow meal? Moving bodies?” she asked.

“Yes,” said Mrs. Sun. “First, let’s wash our hands.” They went to the little sink on the roof. The water went swish, swish, swish. Their paws and hands felt cool and clean.

“Now,” said Mrs. Sun, “let’s gather colors.”

Poppy picked crunchy green spinach. “Green helps you grow,” said Mrs. Sun.

Milo picked red tomatoes. “Red is good for your heart,” she said.

Avery pecked at an orange carrot. “Orange helps your eyes see,” said Mrs. Sun.

Lina sat on purple eggplant. “Purple helps your brain think,” said Mrs. Sun.

Poppy smiled. “Can I add bamboo, too?”

“Of course,” said Mrs. Sun. “Bamboo can be part of your meal. Balance is the key.”

They made little bowls. Poppy’s bowl had green spinach, red tomato slices, orange carrot sticks, purple eggplant bites, and a few bamboo shoots. Mrs. Sun added a spoon of brown rice for energy. “Whole grains help you play,” she said. She poured a tiny cup of water for everyone. “Water helps you go, go, go.”

Before eating, Mrs. Sun said, “Let’s move! Exercise helps our bodies use the good food.”

They played a game called Rainbow Moves.

“Green stretch!” said Mrs. Sun. Everyone stretched tall like a tree. “Reach, reach.”

“Red hop!” said Mrs. Sun. They hopped like bouncy tomatoes. “Boing, boing.”

“Orange twist!” They twisted side to side. “Swish, swish.”

“Purple balance!” They stood on one foot. “Wobble, wobble,” they laughed.

Poppy felt warm and happy. Her heart went thump-thump in a strong, steady beat. “I like moving,” she said. “It makes me hungry in a good way.”

They sat on a soft blanket. The food smelled fresh and sweet and a little earthy. Poppy took a bite of spinach. Crunch! She tried a tomato. Juicy! She nibbled a carrot. Snap! She tasted eggplant. Soft and smooth. She chewed a bamboo shoot. Mmm, familiar and yummy.

“This is a rainbow in my bowl,” Poppy said. Her tummy said a happy “Mmm.”

Milo smiled with red tomato on his chin. “I like the red!”

Avery flapped her wings. “Squawk! Orange and green!”

Lina tapped her tiny feet. “Purple is nice for thinking!”

Mrs. Sun nodded. “You are all different, and that is good. We can enjoy many foods together. That is diversity. Different colors. Different friends. All welcome.”

Poppy felt proud. “I learned something,” she said. “Food gives me energy. Colors help my body. Moving helps me use the food. And being patient helped me make a good choice.”

Her tummy was calm now. No more grumble. The sun was warm. The breeze was gentle. The bees hummed, “Bzzz,” like a happy song.

Poppy packed the bowls. “Thank you, Mrs. Sun. Thank you, friends,” she said.

“Anytime,” said Mrs. Sun. “Remember: eat a rainbow, drink water, and move your body. Your body will thank you.”

Poppy smiled. “I am Poppy the patient panda. I can wait. I can choose. I can grow strong.”

They all did one last “Green stretch!” and waved at the sky.

The End.
