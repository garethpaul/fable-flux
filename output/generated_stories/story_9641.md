# Leo's Rooftop Adventure

High up on a sunny rooftop, a little lion cub named Leo sat watching the other animal children play below. There was Maya the monkey swinging on the playground bars, Benny the bear bouncing a basketball, and Sara the squirrel skating on roller skates.

"I wish I could play with them," Leo sighed, his golden fur warm in the sunshine. "But I don't know how to do any of those things."

Leo's mama had always helped him with everything. She tied his shoes, carried his toys, and even helped him eat his lunch. Now, all alone on the rooftop, Leo felt scared to try new things by himself.

Just then, a colorful ball rolled across the rooftop and stopped right at Leo's paws. It was red and blue and yellow, and it smelled like fresh rubber.

"That's my ball!" called Maya the monkey from below. "Can you throw it back, Leo?"

Leo looked at the ball, then at the edge of the rooftop. It seemed so far down! "I... I don't know how," he called back nervously.

"Just try!" shouted Benny the bear. "You can do it!"

Leo picked up the ball with his small paws. It felt bumpy and round. He remembered watching his mama throw things before. She would pull her arm back and then push it forward fast.

"I can try," Leo whispered to himself. "I am brave."

Leo pulled his arm back just like he remembered. Then he threw the ball forward with all his might. But it only went a little way and bounced back to him.

"That's okay, Leo!" called Sara the squirrel. "Try again! You're learning!"

Leo felt a warm feeling in his chest. They believed in him! He picked up the ball again. This time, he stood up tall on his hind legs and threw the ball even harder.

WHOOSH! The ball flew through the air and landed right next to Maya.

"Hooray!" all the animals cheered. "Great throw, Leo!"

Leo's heart felt happy and proud. He had done it all by himself!

"Come down and play with us!" called Maya. "We can teach you basketball and skating too!"

Leo looked at the stairs leading down from the rooftop. Before, he would have waited for mama to help him. But now he felt different. He felt strong and capable.

Step by step, Leo walked down the stairs all by himself. His paws made soft tapping sounds on each step. When he reached the bottom, all his new friends gathered around him.

"I did it!" Leo roared happily. "I threw the ball and walked down the stairs all by myself!"

"That's what friends do," said Benny with a big smile. "We help each other learn and try new things."

Maya taught Leo how to dribble the basketball. Sara showed him how to balance on skates. And Leo taught them all how to roar like a brave lion.

As the sun began to set, painting the sky orange and pink, Leo realized something wonderful. When he tried things by himself, he could do more than he ever imagined. He didn't need to be afraid anymore.

"I can't wait to tell mama about my adventure," Leo said proudly. "And tomorrow, I want to try even more new things!"

From that day on, Leo always remembered that being brave and trying new things by himself made him feel strong and happy. And whenever he felt scared, he would remember his rooftop adventure and say, "I can try! I am brave!"

The End.
