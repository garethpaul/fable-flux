# Tiko’s Porch of Gentle Things

On a peaceful front porch sat Tiko, a wise tortoise with a shiny green shell. The morning was soft and warm. The porch smelled like fresh mint from a tiny pot. A little wind danced through the tall grass and made the wind chimes sing, ting-ting-ting. Tiko smiled. “Good morning, day,” he said in a slow, happy voice.

Tiko loved his porch. He had a small watering can, a broom made of twigs, and a basket for found treasures. He liked to sit, listen, and help.

A tiny ant hurried by, carrying a crumb. “Hello, little friend,” Tiko said. “That crumb looks heavy.”

“It is,” squeaked the ant. “But my family is hungry.”

Tiko lowered his head. “You are very strong. Would you like a small piece of my strawberry?” He gently sliced a tiny bit with his safe, wooden knife. The ant’s eyes shone. “Thank you!” The ant took the sweet, red piece and hurried off. Tiko felt warm in his heart. Kindness felt like sunshine.

He sipped cool water from a cup. The cup made a little clink on the porch rail. Then he heard a soft “Caw?” It was Crow, perched on the fence, looking worried.

“What troubles you, Crow?” Tiko asked.

“I dropped a shiny button,” Crow said. “It rolled into the grass. I can’t find it.”

Tiko blinked kindly. “We can look together.” He hummed a searching song. “Look left, look right, look low, look bright.” They looked near the mint pot. Rustle, rustle. They looked under the rocking chair. Creak, creak. Tiko used his gentle broom to sweep the grass. Swish, swish. The sun warmed his shell.

“There!” cried Crow. A small blue button sparkled in the light. Tiko smiled. “Found with calm and care.” Crow puffed with joy. “You were so kind, Tiko. How can I thank you?” Tiko chuckled softly. “When we are kind, the world thanks us back.”

Just then, a breeze carried a papery whisper. A leaf tumbled onto the porch. Another leaf followed. Then a paper cup, and a crinkly bag. The wind chimes went ting-ting-ting, almost surprised.

“Oh dear,” said Tiko. “The wind brought some mess.”

A little rabbit named Poppy hopped up the steps. “I’m sorry,” Poppy said. “Some trash blew from the road. I don’t like it. It makes the ground sad.”

Tiko nodded. “The earth is our friend. We can be kind to the earth, too.” He opened his treasure basket. “We can sort. We can reuse.”

They picked up the paper cup. Tiko tapped it gently. “This can be a seed cup.” They placed it on the porch. Poppy found the crinkly bag. “This can go to the bin that takes care of plastic,” Tiko said. “We don’t throw it on the ground.”

They found a long ribbon. “A ribbon!” laughed Crow, who loved bright things. “We can tie it to the flower pot to remind us to give water.” Tiko grinned. “Yes. That is resourceful.”

They swept the porch together. Swish, swish. The air smelled cleaner. The wind chimes sang, ting-ting-ting, like a thank you.

A bumblebee buzzed close, slow and round. “Bzz… I am tired,” the bee murmured. Tiko’s eyes softened. “Kindness to small ones,” he said. He poured a tiny bit of water on the porch and added a drop of sugar. “Here, little bee. Just a sip.” The bee tasted and lifted a little higher. “Bzz-thank you,” it hummed, then floated to a flower.

Poppy clapped softly. “Tiko, you are kind to everyone.”

Tiko shook his head and smiled. “We can all be kind. Kindness is a seed. We plant it, and it grows.”

He opened a small tin of seeds. They smelled like pepper and sunshine. “Would you like to plant mint and marigold?” he asked. Poppy nodded. Crow tilted his head. “Can I help?” “Yes,” said Tiko. “We can all help.”

They poked small holes in the paper cup. Poke, poke. They scooped a little soil. Soft and cool. Tiko pressed the seeds in with his careful, slow feet. Poppy patted the top. Crow brought a tiny pebble to mark the cup. “This cup is reused,” Tiko said. “That is good for our earth. When we reuse and recycle, we show love.”

A cloud drifted by and made a patch of shade. The porch felt calm. Tiko poured a sip of water. Drip, drip. “We share water. We save water,” he said. “That is kind to the earth.”

A small snail slid up the step, leaving a shiny trail. “Hello,” whispered the snail. “I am slow.” Tiko smiled wide. “I am slow too. Slow is okay. We will wait.” They watched the snail reach the mint pot. The snail felt the cool shade and rested. Everyone breathed in and out together.

The sun began to tilt. The porch was tidy and bright. The seed cup sat in a sunny spot with its ribbon reminder. The earth felt cared for. The friends felt peaceful.

Tiko rocked in his chair. Creak, creak. “Today we were kind,” he said softly. “We helped friends. We helped the earth. We used what we had.”

Poppy’s nose twitched. “Being kind feels good.”

Crow nodded. “Kindness is light.”

Tiko closed his eyes for a moment and listened to the wind chimes. Ting-ting-ting. “Here is our lesson,” he said. “Kindness is gentle help. Kindness is sharing. Kindness is caring for every small thing, even our earth. When we are kind, we make the day better for all.”

The porch glowed with evening light. Everyone smiled. The seeds were tucked in safe. The world felt soft and bright.

The End.
