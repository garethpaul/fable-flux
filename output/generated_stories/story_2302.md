# Splash's Garden Adventure

On a sunny balcony garden, Splash the dolphin lived in a big blue pool. Colorful flowers bloomed in pots all around. The sweet smell of roses filled the air. Bees buzzed happily from flower to flower.

Splash loved to jump and play in his pool. Water droplets sparkled like diamonds in the sunshine. But lately, Splash felt tired and slow.

"Why don't I feel strong anymore?" Splash wondered. He tried to leap high, but he could barely get out of the water. His usually bright gray skin looked dull.

Mrs. Robin perched on the garden fence. Her red breast glowed in the morning light. "Good morning, Splash!" she chirped. "You look sad today."

"I feel so tired, Mrs. Robin," Splash said with a small splash. "I used to jump so high and swim so fast. Now I can barely move."

Mrs. Robin tilted her tiny head. "What have you been eating, dear?"

Splash looked down at his pool. Empty chip bags and candy wrappers floated on the water. "Just snacks," he said quietly. "Sweet treats and salty chips."

"Oh my!" said Mrs. Robin. "No wonder you feel tired! Your body needs healthy food to be strong."

Just then, Benny the bee buzzed over. His fuzzy yellow body was covered in flower pollen. "Did someone say healthy food?" Benny asked excitedly.

"Splash has been eating junk food," Mrs. Robin explained. "He needs to learn about healthy eating."

Benny's tiny wings fluttered fast. "I know all about healthy food! I collect nectar from flowers every day. It gives me energy to fly!"

Splash perked up. "Really? Can you teach me?"

"Of course!" said Benny. "First, let's clean up your pool."

Together, they picked up all the wrappers. The water became crystal clear again. Splash could see his reflection perfectly.

"Now," said Mrs. Robin, "let's talk about healthy foods. Fish need fresh seaweed, small fish, and clean water."

Benny nodded. "Just like I need flower nectar! Healthy food makes us strong and happy."

Splash thought carefully. "So if I eat the right foods, I'll feel better?"

"Exactly!" chirped Mrs. Robin. "And don't forget to exercise every day too!"

From that day on, Splash made big changes. Every morning, he ate fresh kelp and small fish. He drank only clean, pure water. He swam laps around his pool and practiced jumping.

Mrs. Robin brought him different types of seaweed to try. "Variety is important," she explained. "Different foods give your body different vitamins."

Benny visited every afternoon. "Exercise is fun when you have friends!" he buzzed. They played water games together.

Soon, other garden friends joined them. Lily the ladybug brought fresh lettuce leaves to share. Carlos the caterpillar showed them stretching exercises.

"Everyone is welcome to join our healthy habits club!" Splash announced happily.

After just one week, Splash felt amazing. His skin was bright and shiny again. He could jump higher than ever before! Water sprayed everywhere as he leaped with joy.

"I feel so strong and happy!" Splash laughed. "Thank you for teaching me about healthy habits!"

Mrs. Robin smiled proudly. "You took initiative to change, Splash. That was very brave."

"And you included all of us in your journey," added Benny. "That made it even more fun!"

Now every morning, all the garden friends exercise together. They share healthy foods and encourage each other. The balcony garden rings with laughter and splashing.

Splash learned that healthy eating and exercise aren't just good for your body – they're good for making friends too!

The End.
