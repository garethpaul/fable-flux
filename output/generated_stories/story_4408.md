# Benny Beaver's Big Dream

Benny Beaver sat on his soft, blue bed in his cozy bedroom. The morning sun peeked through his yellow curtains, making warm golden spots dance on his fuzzy brown fur. But Benny didn't feel warm and happy today. He felt worried.

"I want to build something special," Benny said to his teddy bear, Mr. Chips. "But what if I'm not good enough? What if I mess it up?"

Benny's bedroom was filled with wood blocks, colorful sticks, and building toys scattered across the soft carpet. His mama had given him all these wonderful materials to practice with, but Benny was scared to try.

"Building is what beavers do best," his mama had told him yesterday. "Your daddy builds amazing dams, and your grandpa built the strongest lodge in the whole forest!"

But Benny felt his little paws shake when he picked up the first wooden block. "What if mine doesn't look as good as theirs?" he whispered.

Just then, Benny heard a gentle knock on his door. "Knock, knock! May I come in?" called Mama Beaver's sweet voice.

"Come in, Mama," Benny said softly.

Mama Beaver waddled into the cozy room, her warm brown eyes twinkling. She sat down on the fluffy rug next to Benny and gave him a big, snuggly hug that smelled like fresh pine trees.

"You look sad, little builder. What's troubling you?" Mama asked, stroking Benny's soft fur.

"I want to build something amazing, but I'm scared I won't be good at it," Benny admitted, his voice barely louder than a whisper.

Mama Beaver smiled and picked up a small wooden block. "Do you know how I learned to build my first dam?"

Benny shook his head, his round ears perking up with curiosity.

"I started with just one stick," Mama said, placing the block gently in Benny's paws. "Then I added another. And another. Each time I built something, I got a little bit better. Even if it wasn't perfect, I was proud because I tried."

Benny looked at the smooth wooden block in his paws. It felt nice and warm. "Really? You weren't perfect at first?"

"Oh my, no!" Mama laughed, a sound like tinkling bells. "My first dam had so many holes, the water went right through! But I kept practicing, and now look what I can build."

Benny felt a tiny spark of excitement in his tummy. "Maybe... maybe I could try with just one block?"

"That sounds like a wonderful start," Mama encouraged.

Benny carefully placed the first block on his bedroom floor. It made a satisfying *click* sound. Then he added another block on top. *Click!* The tower wobbled but stayed standing.

"I did it!" Benny squeaked with joy. "Look, Mama! It's my very first tower!"

"It's beautiful, Benny! I'm so proud of you for being brave and trying," Mama said, clapping her paws together.

Benny's chest puffed up with pride. The worried feeling in his tummy had turned into warm, happy butterflies. He added another block, then another, building his tower higher and higher.

"You know what, Mama?" Benny said, his eyes sparkling with newfound confidence. "I think I *am* good at building! And even if it falls down, I can always try again!"

Mama Beaver gave him another warm hug. "That's the spirit of a true builder, my brave little beaver."

From that day on, Benny knew that trying his best was what made him special.

The End.
