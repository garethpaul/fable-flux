# Sunny Bird Shares a Song

In a quiet study corner by a sunny window, there lived a small, bright bird named Sunny. Sunny was a singing bird with soft yellow feathers and a tiny blue scarf. The study corner had a little desk, a lamp that glowed warm and golden, a cozy chair, and a neat basket filled with pencils, crayons, and soft tissues. On the wall was a big clock that went tick-tock, tick-tock. It was a calm place for reading, drawing, and quiet songs.

Every morning, Sunny hopped onto the windowsill and sang. “Tweet-tee-tee, tweet-tee-tee!” Her voice was light and lovely. The room smelled like clean paper and lemon soap. Sunlight made dust sparkle like tiny stars.

One day, Sunny heard a soft sniffle. “Sniff…sniff.” It was her friend Pip, a little gray mouse who liked to read picture books. Pip climbed onto the chair and curled his tail around his feet. His whiskers trembled.

“Hi, Pip,” Sunny chirped. “Are you okay?”

Pip rubbed his nose. “I want to read and draw, but my nose is leaky. I feel tired.” His eyes looked watery, and his voice was small.

Sunny tilted her head. “I can help. Let’s make the study corner comfy and clean. It will help you feel better.”

Pip nodded. “Thank you, Sunny.”

Sunny hopped to the basket and pulled out a soft tissue. “Here you go, Pip. Use this to wipe your nose.” Pip dabbed gently. “Ah-choo!” A little sneeze came out. Sunny sang a tiny tune, “Sneeze and please, then wash with ease.”

Sunny fluttered to the little sink by the corner. She turned the water on with her beak. “Come wash your paws, Pip.”

Pip stood on a step stool. The water was warm and bubbly. “It feels nice,” he said. Sunny showed him how to rub his paws. “Front and back, between and around, clean and bright with a happy sound,” she sang. They counted to twenty together. “One, two, three…” When they finished, Pip dried his paws on a clean towel. “I feel fresh,” he smiled.

Sunny looked around the study corner. The pencils were rolling, and papers were messy. “Let’s make it tidy. A tidy place helps our bodies and minds.” She said the words slowly so Pip could hear them well.

They picked up the pencils and put them in a cup. They stacked the books in a neat pile. Sunny wiped the desk with a clean cloth that smelled like lemon. Pip swept tiny crumbs into a bin. The lamp glowed brighter, and the room felt peaceful again.

But then Sunny noticed something else: Pip’s water cup was empty. “You need water,” Sunny said. “Drinking water helps you feel strong.” Sunny poured fresh water into the cup and added a slice of cool cucumber from a little snack plate. Pip took a sip. “Ahh,” he sighed. “Thank you.”

Now they were ready to read and draw. Sunny opened a big picture book about sharing. The pages felt smooth. The pictures were bright. Pip smiled as Sunny sang the words gently. “Share a smile, share a song, sharing makes us all feel strong.”

Soon they heard a rustle by the bookshelf. It was Dot, a tiny ladybug with shiny red wings. Dot looked worried. “I lost my little pencil,” she said softly. “I need it to write my thank-you note.”

Pip glanced at his new pencil. It was his favorite. It had a shiny blue star on top. He held it tight. He loved his pencil. He wanted to keep it.

Sunny watched Pip and hummed a kind, warm tune. “When we share, we care. When we care, we share,” she sang.

Pip looked at Dot. Her eyes were round and hopeful. He looked at the neat basket. There were other pencils too, but none with a blue star. Pip took a deep breath. He felt a tiny tug in his heart. He thought about how Sunny had helped him wash, clean, and drink water. He thought about how the study corner felt safe and bright because Sunny shared her time and her care.

Pip smiled and held out the blue-star pencil. “Here, Dot. You can use my favorite pencil. I will use one from the cup.”

Dot’s wings opened in surprise. “For me? Thank you!” She hugged the pencil gently. “I will write my note now.” Dot flew to the desk and wrote, slowly and carefully: “Thank you, Sunny and Pip.”

Pip felt warm inside, like sunshine. Sunny sang a happy song that twinkled like the dust in the light. “Share your things, share your care, healthy hearts love to share.”

Dot looked up and said, “I will share too. Here is a tiny wipe for your desk.” She pulled a small wipe from her bag. “It smells like lavender.” They wiped the desk together, and the room smelled clean and sweet.

Pip’s nose was better. His paws were clean. The study corner was tidy. Dot had her pencil, and Sunny had her song. Everyone felt calm and well.

Sunny peeped softly. “What did we learn today?”

Pip said, “Sharing helps friends. It feels good inside.”

Dot added, “Clean hands and tidy spaces help us stay healthy.”

Sunny nodded. “And water helps our bodies. Generosity makes our corner bright.”

They all smiled. Sunny sang one last line, gentle and true: “When we give a little, we all get a lot.”

The End.
