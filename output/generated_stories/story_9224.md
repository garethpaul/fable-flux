# Professor Shelly's Green Discovery

Professor Shelly was a wise old tortoise who lived in a cozy basement lab. Her green shell sparkled under the bright lights. Glass bottles filled with colorful liquids lined the wooden shelves.

"What a beautiful morning to help our Earth!" Professor Shelly said, adjusting her tiny glasses.

She loved making special soaps and cleaners from plants. The basement smelled like fresh mint and lavender. Her favorite recipe used lemon peels and baking soda.

*Knock, knock, knock!*

"Professor Shelly! Help us, please!" called a squeaky voice.

Three little field mice stood at her door. Their whiskers twitched nervously. Their brown fur looked messy and dirty.

"Oh my!" said Professor Shelly. "What happened to you?"

"We played in the muddy creek," said the first mouse, Max. "Now we're all yucky!"

"And our burrow smells terrible," added the second mouse, Mia. "We don't know how to clean it."

The third mouse, Mike, wrinkled his tiny nose. "We tried using harsh chemicals from the big house. But they made us sneeze and cough!"

Professor Shelly's wise eyes grew concerned. "Oh dear! Those chemicals can hurt you AND our beautiful Earth. Let me teach you a better way."

She waddled to her shelf of natural ingredients. The glass containers clinked softly as she gathered supplies.

"First, we'll make gentle soap for your fur," she explained. "We use coconut oil, which smells sweet and creamy."

The mice watched with wide eyes as Professor Shelly mixed ingredients in a blue bowl. She added drops of peppermint oil. The fresh scent filled the air.

"This soap cleans without hurting your skin or the water when we rinse," she said kindly.

Next, she showed them how to make cleaning spray from white vinegar and water. "Vinegar comes from apples! It kills germs naturally."

"But Professor," squeaked Max, "vinegar smells funny!"

Professor Shelly chuckled. Her shell wiggled with laughter. "Add some orange peels, little one. They make everything smell like sunshine!"

The mice took turns stirring the mixture. Their tiny paws worked carefully. Soon, their cleaning spray smelled fresh and citrusy.

"Now for your burrow," said Professor Shelly. "We'll use baking soda. It absorbs bad smells like magic!"

She filled small cloth bags with the white powder. "Sprinkle this around your home. Leave it overnight, then sweep it up."

"Will this really work?" asked Mia doubtfully.

"Trust me," smiled Professor Shelly. "Nature gives us everything we need to stay clean and healthy."

The mice spent the afternoon learning. They washed their fur with the gentle soap. Their whiskers became shiny and soft. They practiced making the orange-scented cleaner.

"This feels so much better than those harsh chemicals," said Mike, his eyes bright and clear.

"And we're not hurting any fish in the creek!" added Max happily.

Professor Shelly nodded proudly. "When we take care of our Earth, our Earth takes care of us."

The mice hurried home with their new natural cleaners. They worked together, scrubbing and sprinkling baking soda. Soon their burrow smelled fresh as a spring morning.

That evening, they returned to thank Professor Shelly.

"Our home is spotless!" they cheered. "And we feel wonderful!"

Professor Shelly beamed. "You've learned something very important today. We can stay clean and healthy while protecting our beautiful planet."

"We'll teach all our friends!" promised Mia.

"And we'll always use natural cleaners," added Max.

Professor Shelly watched them scamper away happily. She felt warm inside her shell. Teaching others to care for the Earth was the best job ever.

From that day on, the mice kept their burrow sparkling clean with nature's gifts. And they never forgot Professor Shelly's important lesson about caring for their home - both their burrow and their beautiful Earth.

The End.
