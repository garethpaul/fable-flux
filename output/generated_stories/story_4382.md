# Miko’s Fair Share Pantry Parade

In a cozy pantry behind a blue kitchen door lived a kind mouse named Miko. The pantry smelled like warm bread and sweet cinnamon. Shelves stood tall like gentle giants, holding jars of jam, boxes of cereal, and bags of rice that whispered shhh when the air moved.

Miko had soft gray fur and bright button eyes. He loved to help. Every morning, he stretched his whiskers, took a deep belly breath, and said, “Good morning, pantry. I am ready to be kind.” Today, he planned to tidy the bottom shelf where crumbs often fell.

As Miko swept crumbs with his tiny broom, friends scampered in. Tika the Ant marched in a line with her ant buddies. Benny the Beetle hummed with shiny wings. Lila the Ladybug floated down like a little red leaf.

“Crumbs! Crumbs!” cheered Tika. “Let’s eat, let’s eat!”

Benny buzzed, “I am very hungry!”

Lila smiled. “Me too.”

Miko looked at the little pile of crumbs. There were not many. He felt a wiggle in his tummy. He wanted to make sure everyone got some. “Friends,” Miko said softly, “Let’s share these crumbs fairly.”

“What is fairly?” asked Tika, tilting her head.

“Fairness means everyone gets a turn and a fair share,” Miko said. He took a slow breath in, filled his belly like a balloon, and slowly let it out. “We can use calm breaths to think first. Then we can plan.”

Lila nodded. “I like calm breaths.”

Benny buzzed in a circle. “I like plans!”

Just then, a tall bag of crackers tipped and bonk! A few crackers slid out and broke into crunchy pieces. The pantry went crackle, crackle. The smell turned salty and yummy. All the little friends gasped. “More crumbs!” they cried.

Tika’s ant line dashed forward. Benny zoomed. Lila fluttered. They all moved at once, bumping softly. “Oh!” Miko squeaked. “Wait, wait. This feels pushy.”

He put his paws on his heart and noticed it beat fast. “Thump, thump.” He closed his eyes and took three belly breaths. In, out. In, out. In, out. “Mindful mouse,” he whispered. “Notice, breathe, then choose.”

He opened his eyes. “Friends, let’s stop and think. We can make this fair. We can also help the pantry stay clean.”

Tika slowed her ant line. Benny landed. Lila held still in the air like a tiny kite.

“Here is my idea,” Miko said. “We will make a crumb circle. We will take turns. First, we will sort the big crumbs and the little crumbs. Then we will count together. I will also volunteer to sweep and carry crumbs, so nobody slips.”

“What does volunteer mean?” asked Benny.

“It means I offer to help,” Miko said, smiling. “I use my time and my body to help others.”

Lila clapped her tiny feet. “That is kind!”

Miko found four tiny leaves on the floor. “Let’s use these as plates,” he said. He moved with quick little steps—step, step, sweep, sweep. “Movement can help us do our jobs,” he said cheerfully. “Let’s make it a Pantry Parade!”

Benny buzzed a beat: bzz-bzz, bzz-bzz. Tika’s ants marched: tap-tap, tap-tap. Lila twirled slowly. Miko led the way, tail swish-swish, broom swish-swish. They danced around the crumbs, moving but not pushing, keeping space between them.

“Now we sort,” Miko said. “Big crumbs here, little crumbs there. We will count to three, then take one scoop each.”

They counted together, “One, two, three.”

Miko used a tiny spoon. Scoop. He gave one scoop to Tika’s leaf plate. Scoop. One to Benny’s. Scoop. One to Lila’s. Scoop. One to his own. He looked around. “Is that fair?”

Tika checked. “We each got one scoop. That is fair.”

Benny nodded. “But I am still hungry.”

Miko’s whiskers twitched. “Let’s check our bodies. Belly check.” He put a paw on his belly. “Is it hungry? A little or a lot?”

Benny listened to his belly. “A lot.”

Tika listened. “A little.”

Lila listened. “A little.”

Miko smiled. “Thank you for being mindful. We can change our plan to be fair to how our bellies feel. If we have extra crumbs, Benny can have a little more.”

Tika nodded. “That feels fair to me.”

Lila agreed. “Me too.”

They counted the extra crumbs. Not many, but enough for two tiny scoops. “We will take turns again,” Miko said. “Benny can have one scoop now. If there is one more, someone who is still hungry can have it.”

Scoop. Benny’s belly felt better. There was one tiny scoop left. Miko checked his belly. “I feel okay.” Tika and Lila both smiled. “We feel okay too.”

Miko looked at the last scoop. He thought about fairness. “We can save this for later,” he decided. He slid it into a little jar and put a leaf label on it: Later Crumbs.

“Now,” Miko said, “let’s volunteer to clean. I will sweep. Who will carry the tiny bin?”

“I will!” said Lila.

“Who will tap-tap the beat so we move together?” Miko asked.

“I will!” buzzed Benny.

“And we ants can line up and move the little bits,” Tika said.

They moved with care. Sweep, step, lift, tap. The pantry felt happy and neat. The air smelled like crackers and cinnamon again. No one slipped. No one pushed.

When they finished, Miko gathered everyone. “Friends, today we learned about fairness. Fairness means we share, we listen to our bodies, and we take turns. We also used mindfulness. We breathed, we noticed, and we chose kindly. We volunteered to help. And we moved our bodies in a safe way.”

Tika cheered. “Fair is friendly!”

Benny smiled. “Mindful feels good.”

Lila twirled. “Helping makes my heart warm.”

Miko’s heart felt warm too. He set the Later Crumbs jar on a low shelf. “If someone is hungry later, we can share again,” he said.

The pantry glowed in the soft light. Everyone’s tummy felt just right, and everyone’s smile felt big.

The End.
