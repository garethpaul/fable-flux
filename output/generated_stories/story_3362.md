# Miko's Garden Adventure

Miko the monkey loved his balcony garden. Every morning, he would swing from his bedroom window onto the sunny balcony where colorful flowers danced in the breeze and green vegetables grew in neat rows.

"Good morning, my beautiful garden!" Miko chirped, stretching his arms wide. The morning sun felt warm on his fuzzy brown fur, and he could smell the sweet scent of blooming tomatoes.

Today was special. Miko's friends Bella the bunny and Sam the squirrel were coming to visit. He wanted to share his grandmother's tradition of making a healthy rainbow salad from garden vegetables.

"Grandmother Nana always said, 'Eat colors from the rainbow to keep your body strong and happy,'" Miko remembered, touching the red tomatoes gently.

Soon, Bella hopped onto the balcony, her white ears twitching with excitement. "Wow, Miko! Your garden smells amazing!"

Sam scampered up the railing, his bushy tail swishing. "Look at all these colors! But I only like carrots. Can we just eat carrots?"

Miko's face fell a little. He had planned to share his family's special tradition, but he wanted his friends to be happy too.

"Well," Miko said thoughtfully, "my Nana taught me that we need different colored foods to be healthy. Red tomatoes help our hearts, orange carrots help our eyes see better, and green lettuce makes our bones strong."

Bella's pink nose wiggled as she sniffed a purple eggplant. "This smells interesting, but it looks scary. What if I don't like it?"

Miko had an idea. "Let's make this fair for everyone! We can each pick one vegetable we really want to try, and one we're not sure about. Then we'll taste tiny pieces together."

"That sounds brave!" said Sam, puffing out his chest.

The three friends began harvesting. Miko picked red tomatoes and yellow peppers. Bella chose orange carrots and green lettuce. Sam selected his favorite carrots and, very carefully, a small purple eggplant.

"Now for the fun part!" Miko announced. "We wash everything with cool, clean water." The friends giggled as they splashed their vegetables clean, drops of water sparkling like tiny diamonds in the sunshine.

They sat in a circle on Miko's colorful garden mat. Miko showed them how his grandmother cut vegetables into fun shapes - stars, circles, and strips.

"In my family, we always try one bite of each color," Miko explained. "Nana says it's like painting a rainbow inside our tummies!"

Bella took a tiny bite of purple eggplant and her eyes widened. "It tastes like... sunshine and earth together! I like it!"

Sam carefully tried a piece of green lettuce. "It's crunchy and fresh! Like eating a cool breeze!"

Miko beamed with happiness. "See? When we're brave and try new things together, we discover wonderful surprises!"

The friends spent the afternoon sharing stories about their families' food traditions while munching their colorful salad. The balcony garden buzzed with bees, and butterflies danced around them.

"Thank you for sharing your tradition with us, Miko," said Bella, her tummy full and happy.

"And thank you for helping us be fair to everyone," added Sam, patting his round belly.

As the sun began to set, painting the sky in orange and pink, Miko felt proud. He had shared his family's healthy tradition while making sure everyone felt included.

"Tomorrow, let's plant new seeds together!" Miko suggested. "We can grow an even bigger rainbow!"

His friends cheered, already excited for their next garden adventure.

The End.
