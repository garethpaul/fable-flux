# Melody's Big Problem

Melody was a little yellow bird who loved to sing. She lived in the cozy study corner of a warm house. Her favorite spot was on top of the big wooden bookshelf, where she could see everything below.

Every morning, Melody would chirp happy songs. "Tweet-tweet-tra-la-la!" she sang. The sunlight streamed through the window, making her feathers sparkle like gold.

But today felt different. Melody's tummy felt fluttery and worried.

Two children, Sam and Lucy, were sitting at the study table below. They were supposed to share the box of colorful crayons, but something was wrong.

"Those are MY crayons!" Sam shouted, grabbing the whole box.

"No fair! I need the red one!" Lucy cried, trying to pull the box away.

"I had them first!" Sam yelled louder.

Melody watched as the children tugged and pulled. Crayons scattered across the floor with loud clattering sounds. Both children looked angry and sad.

"Oh no," Melody chirped softly. "This doesn't look safe. Someone might get hurt."

The little bird's heart beat fast. She wanted to help, but she was just a small bird. What could she do?

Melody thought about what her mama bird had taught her. "When friends fight, sometimes they need help remembering how to be kind."

Taking a deep breath, Melody flew down to the table. She landed right between Sam and Lucy.

"Tweet-tweet! Hello, friends!" she sang in her sweetest voice.

Both children stopped pulling and stared at the pretty yellow bird.

"Look, a birdie!" Lucy whispered.

"She's so little," Sam said, his voice getting softer.

Melody began to sing a special song: "When friends disagree, what can we do? Share and take turns, that's what's true! Red for you, blue for me, sharing makes us happy as can be!"

Sam and Lucy listened to Melody's gentle song. The angry feelings in their faces started to melt away.

"Maybe... maybe we could take turns?" Lucy said quietly.

"Yeah," Sam nodded. "You can use the red crayon first. I'll use the blue one."

"Thank you, Sam! And you can have the green one next," Lucy smiled.

Melody's heart filled with joy. She sang another happy tune: "Friends who share and friends who care, make the world bright everywhere!"

The children giggled at Melody's song. They picked up the scattered crayons together, making sure each color went back in the box.

"Thank you, little bird," Sam said gently. "You helped us remember how to be good friends."

"Your song made us feel better," Lucy added, carefully petting Melody's soft feathers.

Melody flew back to her bookshelf perch, feeling proud and happy. The study corner was peaceful again. She could hear the children talking kindly and sharing their crayons.

"Red looks pretty next to your blue," Lucy said.

"Your drawing is really good!" Sam replied.

As the warm afternoon sun filled the study corner, Melody sang one more song: "When we help our friends be kind, peace and happiness we'll find!"

From that day on, whenever the children in the study corner had a disagreement, they would look up at Melody. Her gentle songs always reminded them that talking kindly and sharing fairly made everyone feel safe and happy.

The End.
