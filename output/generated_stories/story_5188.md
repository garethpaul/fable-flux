# Ping the Panda's Rooftop Garden

High up on the sunny rooftop of the tall green building, Ping the panda sat quietly among his colorful flower pots. The warm morning sun tickled his black and white fur, and a gentle breeze made the leaves dance. Ping loved his rooftop garden more than anything.

"Good morning, little flowers," Ping whispered softly, touching each pot with his gentle paws. "Time for breakfast!"

But when Ping looked around, his happy face turned worried. His watering can was empty, and the soil in all his pots looked dry and crumbly. The pretty flowers were drooping their heads sadly.

"Oh no!" said Ping, feeling his tummy get tight with worry. "My flowers are thirsty, and I don't know what to do!"

Just then, his friend Bella the butterfly fluttered over. She could see that Ping was upset because his shoulders were hunched up and his ears were drooping.

"What's wrong, Ping?" asked Bella in her tiny, musical voice.

"My flowers need water, but my watering can is empty," Ping said, his voice shaking a little. "I feel so frustrated, and I don't want my flowers to be sad!"

Bella landed gently on Ping's nose. "Take a deep breath with me," she said kindly. "In... and out... That's better! Now, let's think together. What do you know about getting water?"

Ping closed his eyes and breathed slowly, just like Bella showed him. The tight feeling in his tummy started to go away. "Well," he said thoughtfully, "I know there's a water spigot downstairs in the building."

"That's great knowledge!" chirped Bella. "But carrying water up all those stairs might be tricky. What else do you know?"

Ping looked around his rooftop and noticed something he had forgotten. "Wait! I know something else!" he said excitedly. "I have that long green hose coiled up in the corner!"

"Perfect!" said Bella, clapping her tiny wings. "You're using your brain to solve the problem!"

Working together, Ping and Bella uncoiled the long hose. Ping carefully connected one end to the water spigot downstairs, while Bella flew down to help guide him. It took patience and teamwork, but they managed to stretch the hose all the way up to the rooftop.

"Turn it on slowly," called Bella from below.

Cool, clear water began flowing through the hose. Ping felt so proud as he gently watered each pot. The flowers seemed to perk up immediately, their petals becoming bright and cheerful again.

"Thank you for helping me think," Ping said to Bella when she flew back up. "I was getting upset, but you helped me remember what I already knew."

"You solved the problem yourself," Bella smiled. "You just needed to take a deep breath and use your knowledge!"

As the afternoon sun warmed the rooftop, Ping sat peacefully among his happy flowers. He had learned something very important: when problems seem big and scary, taking a deep breath and thinking about what you already know can help you find the answer.

From that day on, whenever Ping felt frustrated, he would remember to breathe deeply and think carefully. His rooftop garden grew more beautiful every day, and Ping grew wiser too.

The End.
