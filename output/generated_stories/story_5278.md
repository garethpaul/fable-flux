# Gigi’s Colorful Feelings Day

Gigi the thoughtful giraffe stepped into the creative art room. The room smelled like sweet glue and fresh paper. Sunlight made little squares on the floor. Paint jars smiled from a long table—red, blue, yellow, and a shiny green. Soft brushes stood like tiny trees in cups.

“Good morning,” Gigi said in a gentle voice. “I feel calm today.” Her long eyelashes fluttered. Her heart felt warm, like a soft blanket.

Her friends were already there. Poppy the panda rolled a clay ball. Tariq the turtle looked at a picture book about patterns from many countries. Lina the ladybug sorted paper scraps from the recycle bin into neat piles.

“Hello, Gigi,” said Ms. Sora, the art teacher. “Today we will make a big feelings mural. We will paint how we feel, and we will use reused paper and boxes. We take care of our Earth.”

Gigi nodded. “That is kind,” she said. “We can make beauty and not waste.” She touched the cool edge of a cardboard box. It felt bumpy and strong.

Ms. Sora put out small cards with faces on them—happy, sad, shy, brave, and more. “We all have feelings,” she said. “Feelings change, like colors. Let’s share them.”

Poppy sighed. “I feel wiggly and grumpy,” she said. “My paint lid was stuck.”

Gigi took a slow breath. She felt a tiny flutter in her tummy. She wanted to help. “Poppy,” she said softly, “your feeling is important. Would you like help or a hug?”

“Help, please,” Poppy said.

Gigi held the paint jar close to her chest and twisted carefully. Pop! The lid came off. “Thank you,” Poppy said. Her eyes looked brighter.

Tariq raised his flipper. “I feel shy,” he whispered. “My pattern is from my grandma’s scarf from far away. What if no one likes it?”

Gigi bent her long neck to see his page. “Your pattern is special because it tells a story,” she said. “I would like to learn about it.” She smiled, and Tariq smiled back.

Ms. Sora clapped softly. “Gigi, that is thoughtful leadership,” she said. “You listen, you help, and you invite others to share.”

Gigi felt proud, like a sunbeam inside. But then she looked at the paint jars and the big blank paper on the wall. Her own feelings changed. Her cheeks felt warm. “Oh,” she whispered, “now I feel unsure.”

“What do you notice in your body?” Ms. Sora asked.

“My knees feel wobbly,” said Gigi. “My breath is small.”

“Let’s try a breath like a slow brush stroke,” said Ms. Sora.

Gigi breathed in like she was smelling a flower. She breathed out like she was blowing a feather. In, out. In, out. Her knees felt steady again.

“I feel ready,” Gigi said. “I will paint my feelings.”

They began. The room filled with quiet sounds—swish, tap, swish. Poppy painted zigzags. Tariq stamped dots in rows. Lina made tiny leaf shapes from green scraps and said, “Leaves make air. That helps our Earth.”

Gigi dipped her brush in blue. The bristles were soft and cool. “Blue is my calm,” she said. She made a big round circle. Then she added a sunny yellow swirl. “Yellow is my brave.” She put small purple spots around the edge. “Purple is my unsure. It is okay to be unsure.”

Poppy looked over. “I like your purple spots,” she said. “They look like questions.”

“They are my questions,” Gigi said. “They sit with my calm and my brave.”

Ms. Sora brought a little box of fabric pieces. “These are from all around the world,” she said. “A friend shared them.” The fabrics had dots, lines, and flowers. Some were bright. Some were soft.

Tariq chose a red piece with golden stars. “This looks like my grandma’s scarf,” he said. He glued it next to his dots.

Gigi picked a soft blue cloth with tiny waves. She pressed it gently near her blue circle. “This wave cloth feels cool,” she said. “It makes me think of the ocean. We should care for the water.”

Lina nodded. “We can wash brushes in one bucket,” she said. “Then we reuse the water.” They all agreed and took turns. The water in the bucket turned the color of clouds at sunset.

Soon, a small whisper rose on the other side of the table. “I want to use the shiny green,” said Poppy. “Me too,” said Tariq. Their paws and flipper reached at the same time. The jar wobbled.

Gigi felt a tight squeeze in her chest. “I feel worried,” she said. “The jar may spill.”

She stepped closer. “Friends,” she said in a calm voice, “we can share this green. Let’s make a plan.” She placed a clean little cup between them. “We can pour some green here for Poppy. Then Tariq can use the jar. After three slow breaths, we switch.”

Poppy and Tariq looked at her. “Okay,” they said together.

They tried Gigi’s plan. One, two, three slow breaths. Switch. No spills. Just smiles.

Gigi felt light, like a kite. She had a feeling inside—warm and proud again. “We did it,” she said. “We listened to our feelings. We listened to each other.”

At last, the mural was full. There were calm blues, brave yellows, shy greens, and curious purples. There were dots, leaves, waves, and stars. It looked like a big hug made of colors.

Ms. Sora hung the mural on the wall. “What is our lesson today?” she asked.

Gigi stood tall. “Feelings tell us how we are,” she said. “We can name them. We can breathe. We can share. We can lead with kindness, and we can care for each other and our Earth.”

Everyone clapped. The sound was soft and happy. The room smelled like paint and warm paper. Gigi’s heart felt steady and bright.

“Tomorrow,” Gigi said, “we can make art from bottle caps and old boxes. We will make new things from old things.”

“Yes,” said Ms. Sora. “That is sustainability. We take care, we reuse, and we respect all cultures and all friends.”

Gigi smiled. “Today was a colorful feelings day,” she said.

The End.
