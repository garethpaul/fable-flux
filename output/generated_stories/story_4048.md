# Rooftop Song and the Sharing Breeze

On a sunny rooftop, a little singing bird named Lila fluffed her shiny blue feathers. The rooftop was warm under her feet. The wind smelled like fresh bread from the bakery below. Chimneys stood like tall friends, and a silver puddle reflected the sky.

“Tweet-a-dee! Tweet-a-doo! This is my song for you!” sang Lila, hopping along the edge. Her voice sparkled like tiny bells. She loved to sing every morning and every night.

Down on the street, people smiled up. “Listen to the sweet bird!” they said. The sound made Lila’s chest feel big and happy.

One afternoon, a small brown bird named Pip landed beside her. He had soft feathers and kind eyes. “Hi, Lila,” Pip chirped. “Your song is pretty.”

“Thank you,” Lila said, puffing with pride. “I sing the best songs!”

Pip tilted his head. “Can I sing with you? Maybe we can make a duet.”

Lila froze. Her wings twitched. In her tummy, a tight knot grew. “But it’s my rooftop song,” she said. “If you sing, will they still like my voice? Will they still listen to me?” Her heart beat fast.

Pip blinked. “I didn’t know you felt worried,” he said softly. “I only wanted to share.”

Lila looked at the silver puddle. She saw her face and her worried eyes. The worry felt like a heavy cloud. “I’m feeling scared,” she whispered. “I’m scared I’ll be less special if I share.”

Just then, a friendly breeze tickled her feathers and whispered, “Hoo-hoo.” The wind smelled like oranges from a nearby window. The breeze made the chimes on the rooftop go ting-ting-ting.

From a brick wall, a wise old pigeon named Mags cooed, “Coo, coo. I heard your words, little Lila. Thank you for naming your feeling. That is brave.”

Lila looked up. “It is?”

“Yes,” Mags said. “When we name a feeling, we can know it. When we know it, we can choose what to do.”

Pip nodded. “I can listen. How can I help?”

Lila took a deep breath. She felt the warm sun on her wings. She listened to her own heart, like a soft drum: thump, thump. “I need to try,” she said. “Maybe we can sing and take turns. Maybe we can listen to each other.”

Pip smiled. “I like taking turns.”

Mags cooed, “Listening is music too.”

They made a plan. Lila would sing a line. Pip would echo. Then they would sing together. They tapped their feet: tap, tap, tap.

Lila sang, “Tweet-a-dee!” She made her voice high like a kite in the sky.

Pip echoed, “Tweet-a-dee!” His voice was warm like cocoa.

Together they sang, “Tweet-a-dee, tweet-a-doo!” Their voices braided like ribbons. The sound floated up and up. It felt light and bright. It felt like sunshine.

Down on the street, people stopped to listen. A child pointed and giggled. A baker waved a floury hand. The chimes went ting-ting-ting. The silver puddle shivered with happy ripples.

Lila felt the tight knot in her tummy loosen, like a ribbon gently untied. She felt a new feeling—wide and cozy. “This feels good,” she said. “Sharing my song makes it bigger.”

Pip’s eyes shone. “And listening to you helps me sing better,” he said. “When I listen, I learn.”

Mags puffed with pride. “Coo! You found knowledge in your feelings. You used your worry to grow. That is smart heart and smart mind.”

They tried new parts. Pip sang a soft “doo-doo-doo” like raindrops. Lila added a bright “la-la-la” like dandelions. They listened for space and took turns. Sometimes they were quiet. The quiet sounded like a hug.

Lila said, “When you sing, I feel excited.” She touched her chest. “When we sing together, I feel calm and glad.”

Pip said, “When you let me join, I feel welcome.”

Mags said, “When we share, there is more for everyone to enjoy.”

A gray cloud slid over the sun. The rooftop cooled. A gust of wind whooshed past, and a paper hat flew up from the street. It twirled and landed near Lila.

“Oh!” Lila chirped, “The wind brought a hat!”

Pip giggled. “Let’s make it part of our show.”

Lila put the paper hat on her head. Pip bowed. They sang their duet again, softer now, to match the cloudy sky. The music felt cozy, like a blanket.

When the song ended, the cloud moved on. Warmth returned. Lila looked at her friends. “Thank you for listening to my feelings,” she said. “Thank you for sharing your voice.”

Pip chirped, “Thank you for making space for me.”

Mags cooed, “Thank you for naming, listening, and sharing.”

Lila fluttered her wings. “Today I learned that feelings are clues. When I feel worried, I can name it. I can breathe. I can listen. I can share and still be me.”

Pip added, “And when we listen to each other, our music grows.”

They sang one last gentle line together, “Tweet-a-dee, tweet-a-doo,” letting the sound drift like feathers.

On the sunny rooftop, with the breeze, the chimes, and the silver puddle, three birds smiled. Their hearts felt light, and their music felt full.

The End.
