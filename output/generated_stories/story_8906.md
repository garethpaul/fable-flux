# Nutkin's Garden Helpers

Nutkin the squirrel loved his cozy home in the big oak tree next to Mrs. Chen's balcony garden. Every morning, he would peek through the green leaves and watch the colorful flowers dance in the breeze.

"Good morning, flowers!" Nutkin chirped happily. The red roses smelled sweet. The yellow sunflowers stood tall and proud. The purple petunias felt soft like velvet.

But today, something was different. Mrs. Chen wasn't watering her plants like she always did. Nutkin waited and waited, but she didn't come outside.

"Oh my," squeaked a tiny voice below.

Nutkin looked down and saw a little mouse near the flower pots. The mouse looked worried.

"What's wrong?" Nutkin asked, scampering down to the balcony.

"I'm Pip," said the mouse. "Mrs. Chen hurt her leg yesterday. She can't come outside to water her garden. Look!"

Nutkin saw that the soil in the pots was dry and crumbly. The flower petals were starting to droop. The leaves looked thirsty.

"The flowers need water," Pip said sadly. "But I'm too small to help."

Nutkin's fluffy tail twitched as he thought. "Don't worry, Pip. I have an idea!"

Nutkin remembered seeing Mrs. Chen use a small watering can. It sat on the little table next to the door. But it was empty.

"First, we need water," Nutkin said. He looked around and spotted the rain barrel in the corner. "Perfect!"

Nutkin climbed up to the rain barrel. It was full of clean rainwater from yesterday's shower. But how could he get the water to the plants?

"I know!" Nutkin said. He found a large leaf that had fallen from his oak tree. The leaf was shaped like a little boat.

Carefully, Nutkin dipped the leaf into the rain barrel. The cool water filled the leaf-boat. Drop by drop, he carried the water to the first flower pot.

"You're so clever!" Pip squeaked, clapping his tiny paws.

But one leaf-full wasn't enough. Nutkin made trip after trip. His little arms got tired, but he didn't give up. Pip helped by showing him which plants needed water most.

"This rose is very thirsty," Pip pointed out. "And these petunias too!"

Nutkin took his time with each plant. He poured the water slowly and gently, just like he had seen Mrs. Chen do. The dry soil drank up the water happily.

After many trips, all the plants had enough water. The flowers lifted their heads. The leaves turned bright green again. Everything smelled fresh and clean.

"Thank you, Nutkin!" Pip said. "You saved Mrs. Chen's garden!"

Just then, Mrs. Chen appeared at her window. She had a bandage on her leg, but she was smiling.

"How wonderful!" she said, looking at her healthy plants. "Someone has been taking care of my garden!"

She put out a small dish of nuts and seeds on the table. "This is for my garden helpers," she said with a wink.

Nutkin and Pip shared the tasty treat together. The flowers swayed gently in the warm sunshine, and everything felt just right.

"Helping others feels good," Nutkin said, munching on a sunflower seed.

"It does," Pip agreed. "And when we work together, we can do big things!"

From that day on, Nutkin checked on the garden every morning. And whenever Mrs. Chen needed help, he was always ready to lend a paw.

The End.
