# Bubbles, Baskets, and Teamwork

Splash was a playful otter who loved to wiggle and giggle. One sunny morning, he padded into the laundry room. It smelled like clean soap and warm cotton. The washing machine hummed, “hmm-hmm,” and the dryer made a soft “whirr.” Warm air puffed out, like a tiny dragon sighing.

Splash peeked into a basket. “So many socks!” he said. “Stripy socks, dotty socks, teeny socks!” He nuzzled a fluffy towel. “Mmm, it smells like lemons.”

Just then, his friends padded in. Tippy the tiny turtle wore a blue bandanna. Buzz the bumblebee buzzed in a happy circle. Pip the puppy wagged his tail, thump-thump, on the tile.

“Hi, friends!” Splash waved a paw. “I have a big idea. Let’s help clean and sort! We can be a team.”

Tippy blinked slowly. “A plan is good,” he said. “What do we do first?”

Buzz zipped by the shelf. “We can make stations!” she buzzed. “One for dirty clothes, one for clean, and one for socks.”

Pip barked, “Woof! I can carry things!”

Splash clapped. “Yes! Teamwork time.” He drew a little map on a notepad with a purple crayon. His paws made soft scratchy sounds. “Step one: put dirty clothes in the washer. Step two: move clean clothes to the dryer. Step three: sort and fold. Step four: match socks. Step five: put baskets away. Everyone picks a job. Fair and fun!”

They all nodded. “Fair and fun!” they said together.

“Who wants what?” Splash asked.

“I am small and slow,” said Tippy, “but I can watch the timer.” He pointed to the round washer light. “Beep means done!”

“I can fly,” said Buzz. “I will find socks hiding under things.”

“I am strong,” woofed Pip. “I will carry baskets.”

“I can pour soap,” said Splash. “I will also fold.”

They started. Pip pushed the laundry basket with his nose. It slid over the floor with a gentle shhh. Splash opened the washer door. Cool, damp air puffed out. He poured a little cup of soap. It was blue and slippery. “Glug-glug,” it went.

“Ready?” Splash asked.

“Ready!” said the team.

Tippy pressed the button. Beep! The washer swished. Water sloshed, swish-swash, like a tiny river. They waited and hummed. Buzz did a little dance in the air. Pip wagged. Splash tapped the beat on the basket rim.

When the washer stopped, Tippy said, “Beep! Step two!”

Pip lifted the wet clothes, plop-plop, into the dryer. The clothes were heavy and cool. “Woof, chilly!” he said with a wiggle. Splash closed the door and turned the knob. The dryer went warm-whirr.

As they waited, Buzz flew low. “I spy a sock!” She pointed to a pink sock under the shelf. “And another in the corner!” She scooped them up gently. “Got you, little socks.”

Splash made a sock mountain. He lined them up: red with red, blue with blue. “We can match by color and size,” he said. “That is our plan.”

The dryer beeped. Warm, sweet air filled the room. “Fresh,” said Tippy, smiling.

They moved to step three. Splash showed how to fold. “Fold in half, smooth-smooth,” he said, paws pressing softly. Pip tried to fold a towel but it flopped over his nose. “Oops,” he giggled.

“It’s okay,” Splash said kindly. “Try again. We can help.” He held one corner. Pip held the other. Together they folded. Smooth-smooth. “Teamwork!” they cheered.

Buzz landed on the sock pile. “Two green dots, two blue stripes,” she sang. “Match-match!” She found a tiny sock with a moon and another with a star. “These don’t match,” she said.

Tippy nodded. “We save the lonelies for later. That is fair.”

They worked and worked. Then the sock mountain wobbled. Socks fell like soft snow. “Oh no!” Pip barked. He looked sad. “I made a mess.”

Splash put a gentle paw on Pip’s shoulder. “It’s okay. We clean up together.” He smiled. “We follow the plan. We can fix it.”

Buzz had an idea. “Innovation time!” she buzzed. She pulled a clean laundry basket close. “Let’s make sock lanes. Long socks in this lane, short socks in that lane.”

Tippy used a ruler to make two straight rows. “Left is long. Right is short.”

Pip wagged again. “I can sort by length!”

They tried the new plan. It worked! The socks matched faster. Pip’s tail thumped happily. “We did it!”

At last, all the clothes were folded. The towels made neat stacks, soft and warm. The shirts smelled like sunshine. The socks were in pairs, cozy and snug.

“Step five,” said Splash. “We put baskets away. Everyone carries one.”

“I will take the light one,” said Tippy.

“I will take the middle one,” said Buzz.

“I will take the big one,” said Pip.

Splash nodded. “Fair and fair.” He picked up a small basket. They walked like a little parade down the hall, clip-clop, tip-tap, buzz-buzz, and soft otter steps.

Back in the laundry room, they cheered. “Teamwork!” Their voices echoed softly. Splash felt warm and proud.

“We made a plan,” he said. “We shared jobs. We helped each other. When there was a problem, we made a new idea. That is teamwork.”

Tippy smiled. “Teamwork makes big jobs small.”

Buzz twirled. “Cooperation feels good.”

Pip nuzzled his friends. “And it is fair when everyone helps.”

Splash wiggled with joy. “Let’s remember our steps for next time. Plan, share, help, and try new ideas.”

They touched paws, wings, and flipper. “Teamwork!” they said again, happy and bright.

The End.
