# Ollie's Attic Adventure

Ollie the otter loved to explore. His whiskers twitched with excitement as he climbed the creaky wooden stairs to Grandma Rabbit's attic. The dusty air smelled like old books and forgotten treasures.

"Wow!" Ollie whispered, his dark eyes sparkling. Sunbeams danced through a small window, making everything glow golden. He saw boxes stacked high, old furniture covered in white sheets, and something that made his heart skip with joy.

In the corner sat a beautiful music box with a tiny ballerina on top. Ollie's paws reached out eagerly. "I just want to see how it works," he said to himself.

*Click!* The music box opened, and the sweetest melody filled the quiet attic. The little ballerina spun around and around. Ollie clapped his paws together, giggling with delight.

But as he reached to touch the spinning dancer, his elbow bumped into the music box. *CRASH!* It tumbled to the floor. The music stopped. The ballerina's arm broke off.

Ollie's tummy felt heavy and cold. "Oh no, oh no, oh no!" he whispered, his whiskers drooping. He picked up the broken piece with trembling paws. The attic suddenly felt very quiet and sad.

He heard Grandma Rabbit's gentle footsteps on the stairs. "Ollie? Are you up there, dear?"

Ollie's first thought was to hide the music box behind a big box. His heart beat fast like a drum. But then he remembered what Mama Otter always said: "Being honest makes your heart feel light and happy."

"Grandma Rabbit," Ollie called softly, his voice shaky. "I need to tell you something."

Grandma Rabbit's kind face appeared at the top of the stairs. Her nose twitched as she sniffed the dusty air. "What is it, little one?"

Ollie held up the broken music box and the tiny arm. Tears made his eyes sparkle. "I was looking at your pretty music box, and I accidentally broke it. I'm so, so sorry. I didn't mean to hurt it."

Grandma Rabbit's eyes grew soft and warm. She sat down on an old trunk and patted her lap. "Come here, Ollie."

Ollie climbed onto her cozy lap, still holding the broken pieces. "Are you angry with me?" he asked in a small voice.

"Oh, my dear otter," Grandma Rabbit said, stroking his soft fur. "I'm not angry. I'm proud of you for telling the truth. That takes courage."

"But I broke your special music box," Ollie sniffled.

Grandma Rabbit smiled. "This music box belonged to my grandmother. She always said the most beautiful music comes from kind hearts, not from boxes. Your honest heart makes the most beautiful music of all."

"Can we fix it together?" Ollie asked hopefully.

"Of course we can," Grandma Rabbit nodded. "And while we work, you can tell me all about what you discovered up here."

As they carefully glued the ballerina's arm back on, Ollie felt the heavy feeling in his tummy float away like a bubble. The music box played its sweet song again, and this time it sounded even more beautiful.

"Thank you for helping me make it better," Ollie said, hugging Grandma Rabbit tight.

"Thank you for being brave and honest," she replied. "That's how we make everything better."

From that day on, whenever Ollie made a mistake, he remembered how good it felt to tell the truth and make things right.

The End.
