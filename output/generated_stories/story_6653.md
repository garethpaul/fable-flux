# Rocco’s Treehouse Band

Rocco was a musical raccoon who loved to play shiny spoons. His tail would swish-swish, and his spoons went ting-ting! He lived in a cozy treehouse with windows that smelled like pine and sunshine. The treehouse had a soft rug, a round drum, a tiny bell, and a basket of vegetables that smelled fresh and sweet.

One bright morning, Rocco looked out the window and smiled. “Today, I want to make music with my friends,” he said. He took a big, calm breath in and out. “I will ask with kind words.”

He climbed down and called to the forest. “Friends, would you like to come to my treehouse for music time?”

Soon, his friends arrived. Bree the bluebird flapped her soft wings. “Tweet! I brought my whistle.”

Milo the mouse squeaked and held a little drum. “I want to play now!”

Tess the turtle came slowly with a tiny triangle. “I can ting-ting,” she said, smiling.

They all went up into the treehouse. It smelled like warm wood. Sunlight danced on the floor. Rocco’s heart felt happy and a little bouncy. “Let’s make a band,” he said. “We can play together.”

Right away, Milo banged the drum. BOOM-BOOM! Bree blew her whistle. FWEEEE! Rocco tapped his spoons. TING-TING! Tess lifted her triangle, but she stopped. She looked worried.

“It’s too loud,” Tess said softly, covering her ears. “I cannot hear my ting.”

Milo frowned. “But I want to play first!” he said. His whiskers twitched.

Bree puffed up her feathers. “I want to play my song now,” she chirped.

Rocco felt the room getting noisy. His tummy wiggled with a little knot. He remembered his calm breath. In… and out. He put his spoons in his lap and spoke gently. “Friends, let’s talk. We can use peaceful words.”

Everyone got quiet. The sunlight made warm stripes on the floor.

Rocco said, “I hear Tess. It feels too loud. I hear Milo. He wants to play first. I hear Bree. She wants to share her song. We can find a plan. We can take turns.”

Bree tilted her head. “How?” she asked.

Rocco smiled. “We can use a turn stick.” He picked up a smooth twig from the windowsill. It smelled sweet like sap. “When you hold the stick, it is your turn to lead. The rest of us listen and play softly. Then we pass the stick.”

Tess’s eyes lit up. “That is a good plan,” she said.

Milo looked at the drum and the stick. He took a small breath. “I can try.”

“First, let’s make our bodies calm,” Rocco said. “Put a paw or wing or flipper on your belly. Breathe in like you smell a flower. Breathe out like you blow a feather.” They all breathed together. In… and out. The room felt soft and quiet again.

Rocco held the turn stick. “I will start and model,” he said kindly. He tapped the spoons very softly. Ting… ting… ting… “Now I pass the stick to Milo.”

Milo held the stick and sat up tall. “I feel excited,” he said. “My plan is slow, then fast.” He tapped the drum slow. Boom… boom… Then a little faster. Boom-boom, boom-boom! Rocco listened and played soft spoon sounds. Bree whispered a tiny whistle. Tess tinged her triangle like a raindrop. Ting… The music felt like a friendly heartbeat.

Milo smiled. “I’m done.” He passed the stick to Bree.

Bree took a breath. “I feel proud,” she chirped. “My plan is a windy song.” She blew a gentle, breezy note. Fweee… fweee… The air felt cool and smooth. Rocco’s spoons made light taps like falling leaves. Tess added a bright ting at the end of each breath. Ting!

Bree passed the stick to Tess. Tess held it carefully. “I feel shy,” she said. “My plan is tiny sounds.” She lifted the triangle and made one soft ting. The sound sparkled in the sunny air. Everyone waited, then played just a whisper. Ting… tap… fwee… “Thank you for listening,” Tess said, smiling big.

Now the stick came back to Rocco. He looked at his friends. “We did it,” he said. “We used peaceful talking. We made a plan. We took turns. How do we feel?”

“Happy!” said Milo.

“Calm,” said Tess.

“Together,” chirped Bree.

Rocco nodded. “Let’s make one more song with our plan. We will play in this order: Milo, Bree, Tess, then me. We will keep our bodies calm and our ears open.”

They played their last treehouse song. Boom-boom… fweee… ting… ting-ting! The music sounded like sunshine through leaves. The wood floor hummed. The treehouse felt warm and safe. When they finished, they giggled and clapped for one another.

Rocco brought out the basket of vegetables. “Carrot sticks for everyone!” he said. Crunch-crunch! The carrots were sweet and crunchy, and the friends felt full and peaceful.

Before they left, Rocco said, “Let’s share our words.” He pointed to a small sign he had made. It said: “We speak kindly. We make a plan. We take turns.”

Milo touched the sign. “Kind words help my ears,” he said.

Bree nodded. “Plans help my wings.”

Tess patted her shell. “Taking turns helps my heart.”

Rocco smiled. “When we listen and share, our music is beautiful. When we breathe and wait, our feelings feel better.”

The friends hugged. The tree rustled softly. Rocco’s tail swished, and his spoons went ting-ting, like a little thank-you.

The End.
