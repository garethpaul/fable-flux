# Owl Hoots Please and Thank You

In a playful living room full of soft pillows and bright blocks, a wise old owl named Olive sat on the back of a squishy couch. The couch felt bouncy like a gentle trampoline, and the sun made warm golden patches on the soft, fuzzy rug. Toys clicked and clacked. A tiny fan hummed. It smelled a little like crayons and a little like cookies.

Olive’s big round eyes blinked kindly. “Hoo-hoo,” she said in a gentle voice. “Words are like feathers. Soft, light, and special.”

Milo and Hana were playing with a rainbow puzzle on the floor. “I want the red piece,” Milo said, reaching fast.

Hana held the red piece close. “I’m using it,” she said, hugging it to her chest.

Milo grabbed. Hana pulled back. The puzzle pieces made a clatter. Milo’s face scrunched. Hana’s lips wobbled.

Olive spread her soft gray wings. “Pause, little friends,” she hooted, calm and warm. “Let’s use gentle words. Gentle words help gentle hands.”

Milo looked up. “But I want it now.”

Hana frowned, holding the piece tight. “I don’t like grabbing.”

Olive nodded. “That is a good feeling to share, Hana. It is a boundary. A boundary says, ‘This is my space, and this is my choice.’ We can use words to keep it safe.”

Hana took a breath. “Please don’t grab,” she said quietly.

Milo blinked. He touched his own chest and felt his heart thump-thump. He thought about Olive’s words. He let his hands fall to his lap. “Okay,” he whispered. “I won’t grab. Can I please have a turn after you?”

Olive smiled and her feathers rustled softly. “Hoo-hoo! Those are helpful words.”

Hana smiled back. “Yes. After I put it in.”

They waited together. Click! The red piece slid into the puzzle, smooth and snug. It made a happy sound, like a tiny tap.

Milo clapped. “Your turn was good,” he said. “Now it’s my turn?”

Hana nodded and handed him a blue piece. “Here.”

Milo’s eyes shone. “Thank you.”

Olive tilted her head. “Did you feel that? ‘Please’ and ‘thank you’ are friendly feathers. They make the room soft and kind.”

Milo giggled. “Feathery words!”

Hana spread her arms like wings and said, “Hoo-hoo!” They all laughed.

Soon, they built a tall tower with blocks. The blocks were smooth and cool under their fingers. The tower wobbled a little. Milo leaned close to blow a soft puff. “It’s standing,” he whispered.

Then a small car zoomed—vrroom!—and bumped the tower. The tower shook and tumbled. Clack-clack-clatter! Blocks slid across the rug.

Hana felt a tear and wiped her cheek. “I worked hard.”

Milo held up a block. “I can help,” he said. “May I fix the bottom first?”

Hana sniffled, thinking. She nodded. “Yes. Please be gentle.”

Milo spoke slowly. “I will be gentle.” He placed the block with care. Hana placed one too. Together they built a stronger base.

Olive watched with bright, happy eyes. “You used words to share your plan. That is peaceful communication. Words can help hands work together.”

They kept playing. They found a soft plush bear under the couch. “Hello, Bear,” said Hana, giving it a hug. The bear’s fur felt fuzzy and warm. Milo reached for the bear too.

Hana hugged Bear closer. “I need a cuddle,” she said. “Please wait.”

Milo pulled his hand back. “Okay. When you’re ready, can I have a turn?”

Hana nodded. “Yes. In two cuddles.” She squeezed Bear twice—squeeze, squeeze—then passed Bear to Milo.

Milo beamed. “Thank you for sharing.” He squeezed Bear gently once. “One cuddle,” he said, and then gave Bear back.

Olive made a soft hoot. “You are using words to set boundaries and to be helpful. That makes the room feel safe.”

Milo puffed up proudly. “Words can be cozy.”

Hana giggled. “Words can be kind.”

Olive fluffed her feathers. “Words can be songs, too.” She sang a little rhyme:

“Please and thank you, soft and bright,
Help our hearts feel warm and light.
If you’re not ready, say it clear,
I will listen. I am near.”

Milo and Hana repeated the rhyme. Their voices were small and sweet, like bells. They liked how the words felt in their mouths—pl, th, k—light little sounds.

Soon it was snack time. The room smelled like apple slices and cinnamon. Crunch, crunch! Milo chewed and said, “May I have more apple, please?”

Hana poured water slowly. “Here you go,” she said. “Sip-sip.” She looked at Olive. “Did we do good words?”

Olive nodded. “Hoo-hoo, yes. You used words to ask kindly, to share feelings, to set boundaries, and to help each other.”

Milo patted the rug. “Our room feels happy.”

Hana leaned on Olive’s soft wing. “Our words made it so.”

Olive smiled. “Remember: Your words are your feathers. They help you fly through feelings and play. Use them gently, and they will carry you and your friends.”

Milo and Hana looked at each other. “Please,” said Milo.

“Thank you,” said Hana.

They both whispered, “I will listen,” and hugged.

The End.
