# Benny Bear’s Task Train

Sunlight peeked through the curtains of the comfortable nursery. Soft rugs felt squishy under paws, and the air smelled like clean blankets and warm oatmeal. Toys rested in baskets, and picture books waited on a low shelf. In the middle of it all stood Benny, a friendly bear cub with bright eyes and a round belly.

“Good morning, Nursery!” Benny giggled, stretching his little arms. His fur felt cozy and warm. He heard birds chirp outside, “cheep-cheep!” and the clock on the wall went, “tick-tock, tick-tock.”

Today, Benny had a list. It was a small list, with three picture tasks drawn by his teacher: a tiny shirt, a little toothbrush, and a toy basket. The list meant: get dressed, brush teeth, clean up toys.

Benny’s teacher, Ms. Maple, smiled. “Good morning, Benny Bear. We will have a fun day. Let’s practice doing one thing at a time.”

Benny nodded. “One thing at a time,” he repeated, patting his list. “I can do it!”

Task One was getting dressed. Benny looked at his soft blue shirt and fuzzy socks. He felt a wiggle in his legs. He wanted to play with blocks right now! The big red block tower called, “Build me, Benny!”

Benny took a deep breath. He remembered Ms. Maple’s words: “When you want to play, but there’s a job to do, pause, breathe, and talk to you.”

Benny put a paw on his belly. He took a slow breath in… and out. “Pause, breathe, and talk to me,” he whispered. “First shirt, then play.”

He slipped one paw into the sleeve. Swoosh! Then the other paw. Wiggle, wiggle. “I did the sleeves!” Benny cheered.

“Listen to that zipper,” Ms. Maple said softly. “Zip-zip!”

“Zip-zip!” Benny laughed, pulling his little hoodie up. The zipper made a happy sound. He pulled on his socks. Soft! Warm! His toes felt snug like marshmallows.

Task One: done!

Ms. Maple clapped gently. “Great work, Benny. What comes next?”

Benny looked at the list. “Toothbrush!” he said. The little bathroom smelled minty and fresh. The water in the sink went, “drip-drop,” and the toothpaste was a tiny pea on his brush.

Benny felt a bounce inside. The mirror was shiny! He wanted to make silly faces. He wanted to roar like a lion. But he remembered: pause, breathe, and talk to me.

He took a slow breath. “First brush, then roar,” he told himself.

“Scrub-scrub on top,” sang Ms. Maple. “Scrub-scrub below.”

Benny sang too. “Scrub-scrub, nice and slow!” The bubbles tasted minty-cool. He felt the tickle of the brush on his little bear teeth. Swish, swish! Spit! Giggle!

Task Two: done!

Benny looked at the last picture. The toy basket. He saw blocks on the floor, crayons by the books, and a small train resting on the rug. He heard the train whisper, “Choo-choo, let’s go!”

Benny’s paws twitched. He really wanted to play train. He could almost hear the train whistle. He could almost feel the wheels rolling. But he looked at his list and patted his belly again.

“Pause, breathe, and talk to me,” he said. He closed his eyes for a moment. In… and out. His tummy rose and fell like a tiny boat on a gentle lake.

“First clean, then choo-choo,” he decided.

Ms. Maple nodded. “Would a little plan help?”

“Yes, please,” said Benny.

“Let’s make a Task Train,” Ms. Maple said, laying three small basket cars in a row. She put a block basket first, then a crayon cup, then a book bin. “We’ll go car by car.”

Benny’s eyes sparkled. “Task Train! Choo-choo!”

He picked up the big red block. It felt smooth and heavy. “Clunk!” it landed in the first basket. The blue block went “plunk!” The yellow block went “bonk!” Benny liked the sounds. He counted softly, “One, two, three.”

Next was the crayon car. “Tap-tap-tap,” went each crayon as he put them in. The green crayon left a tiny squeak on his paw. “Oops,” he giggled, wiping it with a warm cloth.

Last was the book bin. Benny smoothed the covers. The pages smelled like paper and stories. “Good night, little books,” he whispered as he set them in the bin.

He picked up the toy train and set it on the shelf. He paused. “Is the floor clear?” he asked, scanning with big bear eyes.

Ms. Maple smiled. “Check and cheer.”

Benny looked around. The rug was tidy. The baskets were full. His chest felt light, like a balloon. He clapped his paws. “Task Three: done!”

Ms. Maple gave him a little high-five. “You did it, Benny Bear. One thing at a time. How do you feel?”

Benny puffed with pride. “I feel proud! I finished my tasks. Now I can play.”

“Yes,” said Ms. Maple. “First we start, then we stick with it, then we finish. That’s completing tasks.”

Benny set the toy train on the rug. “Choo-choo,” he whispered. The wheels rolled smoothly. The nursery felt peaceful and happy. The sun made warm squares on the floor. Benny’s heart felt calm.

He smiled and said, “Pause, breathe, and talk to me. First we do, then we play. I can finish my jobs today!”

Ms. Maple nodded. “You learned self-control. You practiced and you grew. That’s how we learn.”

Benny’s train circled the rug. Choo-choo! He waved. “Task Train, all aboard!”

The End.
