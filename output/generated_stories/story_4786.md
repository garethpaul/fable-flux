# Ellie Shares the Sunshine

In a sunny backyard, a gentle elephant calf named Ellie woke up to warm light on her ears. The grass smelled sweet. Bees hummed softly. Ellie stretched her wiggly trunk and said, “Good morning, sunshine!”

Every morning, Ellie had a routine. She watered the little garden, picked up fallen leaves, and filled a small birdbath with clean water. She liked to help. It made her heart feel big and warm.

But today, something was different. The air felt extra hot. The puddle by the fence was dry. Ellie’s favorite flower, a red zinnia, bent its head and looked sad.

“Oh no,” said Ellie. “You look thirsty.”

Ellie went to the garden hose. She turned the knob with her trunk. The hose sputtered, then only a tiny dribble came out. “Drip, drip,” it went. No water.

Ellie’s ears drooped. “How can I help my friends if there is no water?” she wondered.

Just then, her friends came by. Pip the sparrow fluttered down. “Chirp! My bath is empty,” he said.

“Milo the mouse needs a sip,” squeaked Milo, peeking from behind a flowerpot.

Lumi the ladybug landed on Ellie’s nose. “It is so hot,” she said, fanning her tiny wings.

Ellie took a deep breath. She wanted to be generous. She wanted to share and care. But what could she do?

She listened. She heard a drip-drip from the roof. She smelled damp dirt near the gutters. She felt a cool breeze on her cheeks.

“Maybe we can save the drips,” Ellie said. “We can be gentle with water. We can share.”

“How?” asked Pip.

Ellie looked around the sunny backyard. She saw an old bucket, a big leaf, a wide shell, and a clean jam jar.

“I have an idea,” Ellie said, her eyes bright. “Let’s make a water saver. We’ll catch the little drips!”

That was Ellie’s innovation, a new way to solve the problem. She set the bucket under the roof edge, right where the drips fell from the gutter. “Plip, plop,” went the drops.

She leaned the big leaf like a slide from the tarp to the bucket. “Whee!” said Lumi, riding the leaf for a second before flying off.

She lined up the shell and the jam jar next to the bucket to catch more drips. Milo wiped the jar with a tiny cloth. “Clean and ready,” he squeaked.

Pip flitted to a branch and called, “Chirp! Teamwork!”

They waited. They listened to the soft music of water. “Plip, plop, plip,” it went.

The bucket filled a little. The shell shimmered with a shiny pool. The jar held a small, clear sip.

Ellie smiled. “We did it. We saved some water!”

Ellie used her trunk to pour a gentle splash into the birdbath. “For you, Pip,” she said.

Pip hopped in. “Splash-splash!” He shook his feathers and sang, “Thank you, Ellie!”

Ellie gave Milo a tiny sip from the jar. “For you, Milo.”

Milo licked his whiskers. “So cool! Thank you!”

Ellie carried a careful sprinkle to the garden. “For you, flowers,” she said. She gave just enough, not too much. The red zinnia lifted its head a little.

Lumi sat on Ellie’s ear and whispered, “You are very kind.”

Ellie felt proud, but she also felt calm. “We shared the water. We did not waste. That is generous and gentle,” she said.

The sun moved across the sky. In the afternoon, the roof dripped again after a short, soft sprinkle. Ellie and her friends followed their routine: check the bucket, save the drops, share them fairly.

They added small stones to the birdbath so tiny bugs could stand safely. They put a leaf over the jar to keep it clean. They set a timer made of a shadow stick to remind them to check the water. It was simple and fun.

Each time someone needed water, Ellie said, “We can share.” And each time, her friends said, “Thank you.”

Later, a thirsty butterfly fluttered by. “May I have a sip?” it asked.

Ellie nodded. “Of course.” She tipped the shell so the butterfly could drink. The butterfly’s wings glowed in the sunshine.

“Your heart is big,” said Milo.

“It feels big when I give,” said Ellie. She felt the warm glow again, brighter than the sun.

As the day ended, the sky turned pink. The backyard smelled like wet grass and flowers. The bees hummed goodnight. Ellie and her friends sat together, calm and happy.

Pip said, “Your idea saved the day.”

Lumi added, “And you shared with everyone.”

Ellie smiled. “We were kind. We tried something new. We took care of our home. When we are generous, we all feel better.”

Pip chirped, “Sharing is sunshine!”

Ellie nodded. “Yes. Sharing is sunshine.”

The red zinnia stood tall. The birdbath sparkled. The bucket, the leaf slide, the shell, and the jar were ready for tomorrow. Ellie felt peaceful. She knew their gentle routine would help again.

The lesson Ellie learned was clear: Being generous means sharing what we have, using smart ideas, and caring for others and our world. Even small drops can help, when we share them with kindness.

The End.
