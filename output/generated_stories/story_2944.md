# Leo's Story Circle in the Garden

Leo was a brave little lion cub with a golden mane that sparkled in the morning sun. He loved spending time in Grandma Lion's beautiful balcony garden, where colorful flowers danced in the breeze and butterflies fluttered from bloom to bloom.

Today was extra special because all of Leo's friends were coming to visit the garden. There was Ruby the rabbit, Sam the squirrel, and Bella the bluebird. They loved playing together among the sweet-smelling roses and tall sunflowers.

"I have the BEST story to tell!" Leo roared excitedly as his friends arrived. "It's about a super brave lion who saved a whole forest!"

"Wait, wait!" chirped Bella, flapping her bright blue wings. "I want to tell my story first! It's about flying through rainbow clouds!"

"No, me first!" squeaked Sam, his fluffy tail twitching. "My story has magic acorns!"

Ruby's ears drooped sadly. "I have a story too," she whispered softly.

Soon everyone was talking at once, and no one was listening. The beautiful garden filled with loud voices instead of the peaceful sounds of buzzing bees and rustling leaves.

Grandma Lion stepped onto the balcony, her wise eyes twinkling. "My dear cubs," she said gently, "I can smell the confusion in the air. What's happening in my garden?"

"We all want to tell our stories!" Leo explained, his little paws gesturing wildly.

Grandma Lion smiled and sat down on the soft grass between the lavender plants. "When I was a young lioness, my family had a special tradition. We called it a Story Circle. Would you like to learn how it works?"

All four friends nodded eagerly, their ears perked up with curiosity.

"First," Grandma Lion explained, picking up a smooth, round stone from the garden path, "we choose a Story Stone. Whoever holds the stone gets to tell their story while everyone else listens with their ears, eyes, and hearts."

She handed the warm stone to Ruby. "The youngest friend goes first. That's our tradition."

Ruby's eyes grew wide with surprise and joy. She held the stone carefully in her soft paws. "Really? I get to go first?"

"Tell us your story, Ruby," Leo said kindly, settling down on the fragrant mint leaves.

Ruby's voice grew stronger as she began. "Once upon a time, there was a little rabbit who found a garden where vegetables grew in rainbow colors..." Her friends listened carefully, imagining purple carrots and blue lettuce.

When Ruby finished, she passed the stone to Sam, then to Bella, and finally to Leo. Each friend told their wonderful story while the others listened, asked questions, and shared in the magic.

"Your tradition is amazing, Grandma!" Leo purred, nuzzling against her warm fur. "Everyone got to share, and everyone got to listen."

"The best part," added Bella, "is that when we listen to each other's stories, we learn new things and feel closer together."

Sam nodded, munching on a mint leaf. "And it's more fun when we take turns instead of talking all at once!"

As the sun began to set, painting the garden in golden light, the friends made a promise. Every week, they would meet in Grandma Lion's garden for Story Circle, taking turns sharing tales and listening with open hearts.

Leo learned that being brave sometimes means waiting your turn and listening to others. The sweetest stories bloom when everyone gets a chance to share.

The End.
