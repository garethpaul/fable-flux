# Hoot the Owl and the Busy Bathroom

Hoot was a wise old owl with soft gray feathers and big, bright eyes. He lived in a cozy house with a shiny, white bathroom. In the morning, the bathroom smelled like mint toothpaste and warm soap bubbles. The mirror was cool and smooth, and the tiles felt a little cold under Hoot’s feet.

“Good morning, bathroom,” Hoot hooted gently. “Today is a good day to take care of my body and my manners.”

Hoot liked to flap his wings for exercise. He called it “wing wiggles.” He would flap slowly, then fast, and then stretch them out wide. “Flap, flap, stretch!” he sang.

Just as Hoot started his wing wiggles, he heard little feet patter. Tap-tap-tap! His friend, Squeak the mouse, peeked around the door. Squeak’s whiskers twitched, and he held a tiny toothbrush.

“Hoot,” Squeak squeaked, “may I brush my teeth? I have crumbs in my mouth from my breakfast cracker.”

Hoot smiled. “Yes, Squeak. Thank you for asking. We can share the bathroom.”

Squeak hopped onto a step stool. He turned on the water. Splash, splash! The water was cool and clear. Hoot stepped to the side, giving Squeak space. He took a deep breath and made a good choice.

“I will wait my turn and be respectful,” Hoot told himself. “I can do my wing wiggles a little farther from the sink.”

Hoot moved to the mat near the tub. The bath mat felt soft and squishy under his feet. He flapped his wings quietly. “Flap, flap, stretch,” he whispered. He counted, “One, two, three,” and smiled.

Soon, Squeak spat into the sink. “Ptooey!” He rinsed carefully. “All done!” he said. He wiped a tiny droplet of water and looked at Hoot. “Thank you for giving me space.”

“You are welcome,” Hoot said. “Thank you for using kind words.”

Just then, another friend waddled in. It was Quack the duckling. Quack’s feathers were fluffy and yellow. He held a little towel in his beak.

“Quack?” Quack asked softly. “Can I wash my wings? I got sticky syrup on them.”

Hoot looked around. The sink was open now. Hoot wanted to brush his beak, too. He had a choice to make.

He thought, I can go first, or I can let Quack go first. He felt the stickiness on Quack’s wings. He heard Quack’s quiet voice. He decided, “I will respect my friend. I will let Quack go first.”

“Quack, you may go first,” Hoot said. “I can do more wing wiggles while I wait.”

Quack’s eyes shone. “Thank you!” he quacked. He turned on the water. The stream went shhhhh. He washed his wings. Swish, swish. The soap smelled like lemons. Quack giggled. “It tickles!”

Hoot stood on one foot for balance practice. “Stand tall, breathe in, breathe out,” he whispered. He lifted his other foot and counted to five. “One, two, three, four, five!” He switched feet. “This is good exercise,” he said. “My body is strong, and my heart is kind.”

Squeak clapped his tiny paws. “You are patient, Hoot!”

Hoot nodded. “We can all be patient. We can all be respectful.”

Quack finished and dried his wings. “All clean!” he cheered. He stepped away from the sink. “Your turn, Hoot.”

Hoot walked to the sink. He looked into the mirror. His big eyes twinkled. He picked up his toothbrush. The toothpaste smelled minty and fresh. Squish! He squeezed a tiny dot. He brushed in little circles. “Swish-swish, up and down,” he sang. He spit. “Ptooey!” He rinsed. The water felt cool on his beak.

Then Hoot saw water drops on the counter. He felt the floor. It was a little wet. He thought, If we leave water here, someone might slip. He made another good choice.

“Friends,” Hoot said kindly, “let’s clean up for others who will use the bathroom. That is respectful.”

Quack nodded. “I will wipe the counter.” He used his towel. Wipe, wipe.

Squeak nodded. “I will roll up the bath mat straight.” He tugged and smoothed. Tug, smooth.

Hoot closed the toothpaste cap and put the toothbrushes in their cups. Click! He hung the towels neatly. The bathroom looked tidy and calm. It smelled like lemons and mint.

All three friends took a deep breath. They listened to the quiet drip, drip of the faucet. Hoot turned it off. No more drips. The quiet felt peaceful.

“I feel proud,” Squeak said, his whiskers twitching happily.

“I feel light and clean,” Quack quacked softly.

“I feel strong and wise,” Hoot hooted. “We used our bodies, we used our words, and we used our kind hearts.”

Hoot looked at his friends. “What did we learn?”

Squeak raised a paw. “Wait your turn.”

Quack lifted a wing. “Share the space.”

Hoot nodded. “Make good choices that help others. That is respect.”

They all smiled. The bathroom door was open. Sunshine shone on the tiles, warm and bright.

“Wing wiggles in the hallway?” Quack asked.

“Yes!” Hoot said. “Let’s move our bodies and keep being kind.”

They flapped, waddled, and scampered out together, happy and clean, ready for a new day.

The End.
