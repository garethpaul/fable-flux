# Bramble Bunny Finds Brave Feelings

In a quiet study corner, with a small desk, a soft lamp, and the smell of fresh paper, lived Bramble, a determined rabbit. Bramble had long ears, a fluffy tail, and a little blue pencil tucked behind one ear. The study corner was cozy. The chair made a tiny squeak. The lamp made a warm glow. The window let in a whisper of wind.

Today, Bramble had a big job. Bramble’s teacher, Owl, said, “Please draw three shapes and put them in a row: circle, square, triangle.” Owl’s voice was calm. “Follow the directions, step by step.”

“I can do it,” Bramble said, tapping the pencil. “I am determined!”

Bramble sat up tall. The paper felt smooth. The pencil felt pointy. “First, circle,” Bramble whispered. The circle came out wobbly, like a bumpy cookie. “That’s okay,” Bramble said, taking a deep breath. “Wobble is fine.”

“Next, square,” Bramble said. The pencil scratched softly: scritch, scritch. The square had one side too long. Bramble’s nose twitched.

“Last, triangle,” Bramble said. The triangle looked a bit sleepy, like it wanted to lean.

Bramble looked at the paper. The shapes did not sit in a neat row. They bumped into each other. The brave feeling inside Bramble grew quiet. Another feeling crept in: a tight twist in the tummy, a warm push in the cheeks.

“I feel… funny,” Bramble whispered.

From the bookshelf, the ruler leaned and clinked. “Hello,” said the ruler in a tiny voice. “I’m straight and steady. I can help with lines.”

Bramble’s ears drooped. “I think I feel frustrated,” Bramble said, naming the feeling. The word felt big, but it fit. “My tummy is tight. My face is hot. My shapes are messy.”

The lamp hummed kindly. The chair gave a small squeak, like a little cheer. The air smelled like crayons.

Bramble remembered Owl’s other words: “When big feelings come, pause. Breathe. Name your feeling. Ask for help if you need it.”

Bramble placed a paw on the chest and took a slow breath in. In… and out. In… and out. The breath was like warm tea in winter. The tight twist softened. The hot cheeks cooled.

“I am Bramble,” the rabbit said softly. “I am determined. I am brave. I can follow directions.”

Bramble spoke to the feeling. “Hello, frustration. You are telling me this is hard. Thank you. I will slow down.”

Bramble read the directions again, pointing with a paw. “Circle. Square. Triangle.” The rabbit smiled. “Step by step.”

“Ruler, may I use you?” Bramble asked.

“Yes,” said the ruler. “Hold me gently.”

Bramble lined the paper’s edge with the ruler and drew a gentle line for the shapes to sit on, like a tiny train track. “First, circle,” Bramble said, and traced around a little cup from the desk. The pencil made a soft round whisper. “Next, square,” Bramble said, using the ruler for straight sides. Scritch, scritch. “Last, triangle,” Bramble said, counting three sides, one, two, three.

The shapes sat in a neat row now. The circle looked like the moon. The square looked like a window. The triangle looked like a mountain.

Bramble’s chest felt light. “I feel proud,” Bramble said. “My tummy is calm. My cheeks are cool.”

Just then, a new page fluttered to the floor with a papery swish. A second set of directions was written there: “Color the circle blue, the square red, the triangle yellow.”

Bramble blinked. “Oh!” The feelings wiggled again. “What if I make a mistake?”

The colored pencils lay in their cup. Blue smelled like rain. Red smelled like strawberries. Yellow smelled like sunshine.

“I can be brave,” Bramble told the feelings. “I can follow directions.”

Bramble read the words again, pointing slowly. “Circle blue.” The blue pencil slid across the moon shape. Swish, swish. “Square red.” The red pencil warmed the window. Swish, swish. “Triangle yellow.” The triangle shone like a little sun on a mountain. Swish!

Bramble held up the page. The lamp glowed. The chair squeaked a happy squeak. The window wind said, “Whoosh!” like clapping.

Owl peeked in. “I see a circle, a square, and a triangle, all in a row,” Owl said. “And the colors match the directions. How did you do it?”

Bramble touched a paw to the heart. “I felt frustrated. I named it. I breathed. I asked for help. I went slow. I followed directions. That was brave.”

Owl nodded. “That is emotional-awareness,” Owl said gently. “You noticed your feelings. You took care of them. You chose your next step.”

Bramble smiled, ears tall and bright. “When feelings get big, I can pause, breathe, and ask for help. I can be brave and follow directions, one step at a time.”

The study corner felt extra cozy now. The paper was smooth and colorful. The pencils smelled sweet. The ruler rested, proud and straight.

Bramble tucked the blue pencil back behind one ear and whispered, “Thank you, feelings, for talking to me. I am listening.”

The End.
