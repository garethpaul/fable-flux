# Panda Poppy’s Car Trip

“Seat belts, everyone,” said Mama Panda with a warm smile. The family car smelled like clean seats and sweet oranges. The morning sun made little sparkles on the dashboard.

Poppy, a patient panda with soft black-and-white fur, climbed into her booster seat. She patted the smooth strap. “Click!” The buckle snapped shut.

“I can wait,” Poppy said, nodding. “Waiting is safe.”

Papa Panda passed a small water bottle to Poppy. “Sip, sip,” he said. “Water helps our bodies.”

Poppy took a small drink. The cool water felt fresh on her tongue. “Mmm,” she hummed. “My tummy says thank you.”

Baby brother Bean wiggled in his seat and kicked his little feet. “Go now?” he chirped.

“We will go when everyone is ready,” Mama said. Her voice was calm like a slow song. “We respect each other’s needs.”

Poppy looked at Bean. “I can help,” she said. She reached into her bag and pulled out a soft panda sticker. “Here’s a sticker for Bean.” Bean clapped. His tiny paws made happy pats.

The car engine purred. The road hummed. Trees whooshed by, green and bright. Poppy watched the clouds float like fluffy rice cakes.

“May we hear music?” Poppy asked.

“Let’s ask kindly,” Papa said. “We share and take turns.”

Poppy took a patient breath. “Mama, Papa, may we please play the quiet song?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Thank you for asking with respect.”

A gentle tune filled the car—twinkle, twinkle, soft and sweet. Bean swayed. Poppy tapped one paw on her knee, slow and steady.

After a while, Poppy’s tummy made a tiny grumble. “Grum, grum.” She felt it, like a little drum. “Mama, my tummy says it is snack time,” Poppy said.

Mama nodded. “Thank you for telling me with calm words.” She passed a small snack box to Poppy. Inside were apple slices and crunchy carrot sticks.

Poppy sniffed the apple. “It smells like a fresh morning.” She took one slice. “Crunch!” The sound was bright. She chewed slowly. “I will save some for Bean later,” she said.

Papa looked into the mirror. “That is thoughtful, Poppy. But we also keep Bean’s food safe for Bean’s tummy. We are fair. Everyone gets their own snack.”

Poppy smiled. “That is fair. Respect means we follow the rules and keep bodies safe.”

The car slowed. “Red light,” Papa said. He stopped gently. “We wait for our turn.”

Poppy watched other cars go—vroom, vroom. “We wait. They go. Then we go. That’s fair,” she said. She wiggled happily in her seat.

Bean dropped his toy. It rolled under his seat. “Oh,” he said, lip wobbly.

Poppy felt a squeeze in her chest. She wanted to unbuckle and help fast. But she took a slow, deep breath. In through her nose. Out through her mouth. “I will be patient,” she told herself. “I sit safe in my seat.”

“Mama,” Poppy said softly, “Bean dropped his toy. Can we help when the car stops, please?”

“Yes,” Mama said. “Thank you for waiting and using gentle words.”

At the next stop, Mama reached back, picked up the toy, and placed it in Bean’s paws. Bean giggled. “Thank you, Poppy.”

“You’re welcome,” Poppy said. Her heart felt warm and light.

As they drove on, a bright sign showed a picture of a carrot. “Farm Market,” Poppy read slowly. “Can we stop for more fruit?”

Papa smiled. “We can stop. We will use our listening ears, walking feet, and kind voices.”

At the market, Poppy held Mama’s paw. The air smelled like oranges and earth. The ground felt bumpy under her shoes. A friendly farmer waved. “Hello, Panda family!”

“Hello,” Poppy said. “We are using our respectful voices.” She pointed to a basket. “May I choose two apples? One for me and one for later?”

“That’s a good plan,” Mama said. “Two apples is fair.”

Poppy chose a red apple and a green one. She held them like treasures. “We take turns, we share space, we use kind words,” she whispered, like a little song.

Back in the car, Bean yawned. His eyes grew sleepy. Poppy tucked a small blanket near his chin. “Soft, soft,” she said.

Papa turned down the music. The car was calm and cozy. The sky turned the color of peach tea.

“Poppy,” Mama asked, “what did you learn today?”

Poppy thought. She felt the gentle rumble of the car and the smooth strap across her chest. “Respect means I wait my turn, I use kind words, and I keep my body and others safe,” she said. “Fairness means everyone gets a turn. Healthy habits are seat belts, water, and crunchy snacks.”

Mama squeezed her paw. “That is a wise lesson.”

Poppy smiled. “I am a patient panda,” she said proudly. “Patient pandas show respect.”

The car turned onto their street. The engine made a soft shhh as it stopped. Poppy unbuckled when Mama said, “All done.” Click! She climbed out, holding her apples.

“Today felt good,” Poppy said. “Respect makes the world gentle.”

The End.
