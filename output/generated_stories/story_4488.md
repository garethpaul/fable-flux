# Tiko’s Balcony Art Day

The morning sun warmed the leaves in the balcony garden. Basil smelled sweet. Mint felt cool. Tiny tomatoes glowed red like little buttons. On a sunny stone sat Tiko, a wise tortoise with gentle eyes and a smooth, dark shell.

Tiko loved to think and to share knowledge. Today, he wanted to make art. “Art helps us see with our hearts,” he said softly. “Art can be brave.”

He took a deep breath. He had paints in a small tray, a cup of water, and a clean brush. He also had a big sheet of paper, clipped so the wind would not blow it away. Tiko had prepared his space. He even put a little mat under the paper to keep the balcony clean.

Squeak! A sparrow landed on the railing. “What are you doing, Tiko?”

“I am painting the garden,” Tiko said. “Would you like to watch?”

The sparrow tilted her head. “I would!”

Tiko dipped his brush in blue. Drip. Drop. He made a sky. He dipped in green to paint leaves. Swish, swish, swish. The brush whispered on the paper. The mint smelled fresh and bright. He felt calm.

Just then, Buzz the bee zoomed by. “Bzz! Is that paint? I like yellow!”

“Yes,” said Tiko. “You are yellow and black. You are beautiful. Would you like me to paint you?”

Buzz did a tiny dance. “Bzz-yes!”

Tiko lifted his brush. Then his tummy felt tight. His shell felt heavy. “What if I make a mistake?” he whispered. “What if Buzz looks wrong?”

Sparrow hopped closer. “That sounds like fear,” she chirped. “Maybe be brave.”

Tiko nodded. “Bravery is not no fear. Bravery is trying even when afraid.” He took another deep breath. He looked at Buzz carefully. He used his knowledge. “Buzz has small wings. Buzz has big, kind eyes.”

Tiko painted a soft oval for the bee’s body. Tap, tap. He added stripes. He added clear wings with a tiny glimmer of white. He smiled. “Hello, Buzz.”

Buzz buzzed in circles. “That looks like me! Bzz thank you!”

A gust of wind whooshed through the balcony garden. The curtain flapped like a white bird. Tiko’s cup of water wobbled—tip! Splash! A blue wave of paint water rushed over the paper. The bee, the sky, the leaves—blurrrr. Colors ran together.

“Oh no,” gasped Sparrow.

Buzz hovered, worried. “Bzz-oh!”

Tiko’s heart thumped. His neck pulled into his shell a little. He felt like hiding. “My art,” he said softly. “It is all messy now.”

He stayed still for a moment. He listened to the wind. He smelled the mint. He felt the warm stone under his feet. Then he slowly peeked out of his shell. “I can be brave again,” he said. “I can fix this. I can learn.”

First, Tiko made amends to the garden. “I am sorry for the spill,” he said kindly. He took a cloth and gently wiped the droplets on the stone. “I will be more careful.”

He thought about preparation. “What could help?” He placed a small smooth rock in the cup to make it heavy. He moved the cup away from the edge. He used clips to hold the paper tighter. “Prepared,” he said with a little grin.

Sparrow chirped, “Smart plan!”

Tiko looked at the wet, swirly paper. It looked like a soft, dreamy cloud. “Maybe,” he said slowly, “this is art too. It is a new start.”

He dipped his brush in yellow. He used the blurry blue as a sky. He used the soft green as faraway leaves. On top, with small careful strokes, he painted Buzz again. He painted Sparrow on the railing with tiny eyes that sparkled. He painted the basil and mint, adding little lines for veins on the leaves. He pressed his nose near the basil and smiled. “It still smells sweet.”

“Bzz-wow,” said Buzz. “I like the soft colors.”

Sparrow fluffed her feathers. “I like my tiny eyes!”

Tiko added a small red tomato and a clay pot to stand on. He signed his name with a little T. “Art can grow from mistakes,” he said. “Art can be brave and new.”

A neighbor child peeked over the balcony rail. “Hi, Tiko! What are you painting?”

Tiko turned the paper gently so the child could see. “I made a picture of our balcony garden. The wind made a mess, and I felt sad. But I prepared better. I tried again. I used my knowledge to look closely. I made amends for the spill. Now the picture shows what I learned.”

The child smiled. “It’s beautiful. The colors look like a dream!”

Tiko’s eyes shone. “Thank you. Art helps us share feelings. It helps us practice bravery. It helps us plan, and learn, and be kind.”

The sun moved higher. The paint dried. The mint leaves rustled softly. Buzz hummed a happy tune. Sparrow sang a light song. Tiko rested on his warm stone, feeling proud and peaceful.

He whispered, “Today I learned: prepare your space, be brave when there’s a splash, use knowledge to look closely, and make amends when you can. Art is for everyone, every day.”

The End.
