# The Little Songbird's Art Show

In a comfortable nursery, sunlight made warm squares on a soft rug. The room smelled like clean cotton and crayons. A tiny blue singing bird named Lulla lived in a cozy nest near the window. Lulla loved to sing. Her song sounded like tinkly bells and gentle wind. “La-la, tweet-tweet!” she sang every morning.

One day, the teacher, Miss Star, clapped her hands softly. “Friends,” she said, “today we will make an art show. We will paint, glue, and sing. Everyone will help.”

Lulla fluffed her feathers. “I will sing for the show!” she chirped. “I can sing all by myself.”

Across the rug, the children gathered art supplies. Soft brushes whispered across paper. Glue smelled sweet and sticky. Snip-snip went the small scissors. “Can we help with the song too?” asked Mina, a child with bright eyes.

Lulla tilted her head. “Hmm. I am the singing bird,” she said. “Maybe I should sing alone.”

Mina looked down. “Oh,” she whispered. “Okay.”

Miss Star placed a basket of colorful scarves on the table. “We also need posters,” she said. “And someone to invite everyone.”

Lulla hopped to a chair. She took a shiny golden bell. “I will ring the bell,” she said. “I will tell everyone to come. I will do it all.”

Lulla tried to hold the bell and a crayon and a big paper sign. The bell slipped. The crayon rolled. The paper bent. Clang. Roll. Crinkle.

“Oh no,” said Lulla. Her feathers felt hot. Her tummy felt twisty. “This is hard.”

Mina knelt down. “We can help,” she said kindly. “We can make the sign. You can sing.”

Lulla puffed up. “But I want it to be perfect,” she said. “What if it is not perfect if others help?”

Miss Star smiled. “Perfect means everyone is welcome,” she said. “Inclusion means we let friends join. We share jobs. We listen. We make space.”

Lulla blinked. “In-clu-sion,” she repeated slowly. The word felt soft and caring.

A small child named Theo wiggled his fingers. “I can draw stars!” he said.

Jaya waved a paintbrush. “I can paint rainbows!”

Mina picked up a marker. “I can write letters.”

Lulla looked at the bent paper. She took a deep breath. Her song softened. “Maybe my friends can help. I can still sing. We can all do art.”

She hopped to the table. “I will be responsible,” she chirped. “I will share jobs. I will also practice my song.”

Miss Star nodded. “That shows responsibility.”

Together, they began. Theo drew bright yellow stars that sparkled like tiny suns. Jaya brushed rainbow arcs that smelled faintly like wet paper and fruit markers. Mina wrote big letters: ART SHOW TODAY!

Lulla practiced slow, gentle notes. “La-la, tweet-tweet,” she sang, watching the others. She listened to their ideas too.

“The sign needs glitter,” said Theo.

“Glitter makes a mess,” said Lulla. “How can we use it safely?”

Mina thought. “We can put a tray under the paper,” she said. “We can shake just a little.”

“That is smart,” said Lulla. “That is critical thinking.”

They sprinkled just a tiny bit. The tray caught the extra glitter. The letters sparkled.

Next, they planned the stage area. “Where should the chairs go?” asked Jaya.

Lulla looked around. The rug felt soft under her feet. The sunlit spot felt warm on her feathers. She hummed and thought. “Chairs in a half-circle,” she said. “Everyone can see. No one is behind.”

Miss Star set chairs. “Great thinking,” she said.

A small drum sat lonely on a shelf. Mina touched it. “Can I play a soft beat while you sing?” she asked.

Lulla paused. Her heart fluttered. “It is my song,” she said, then stopped. She remembered inclusion. She remembered how good it felt when friends helped.

“Yes,” she chirped. “Please play with me. Soft taps. Tap-tap, like little raindrops.”

They practiced. Lulla sang. Mina tapped. Theo shook a tiny shaker like leaves in the wind. Jaya twirled a scarf like a rainbow ribbon. Lulla felt her song grow bigger and kinder.

At show time, the nursery looked beautiful. The sign shone with glitter. The chairs made a friendly half-circle. The air smelled like paper and orange peels from snack time. Friends and toys lined up to “watch.”

Miss Star whispered, “Are you ready?”

Lulla’s tummy fluttered again, but this time it felt like excitement. “We are ready,” she said.

Mina tapped soft beats. Tap-tap. Theo shook-shake-shook. Jaya swish-swish-swooshed the scarf. Lulla sang her sweet melody. “La-la, tweet-tweet, we work as a team. La-la, tweet-tweet, together we dream.”

Everyone clapped. Clap-clap-clap! Lulla bowed. Her feathers glowed.

After the show, Lulla flew to her friends. “Thank you,” she chirped. “You made the art show better. I learned that inclusion means everyone belongs. When we share jobs, we are responsible. When we think of safe, smart ways, we use critical thinking. And when we practice together, we grow.”

Mina smiled. “We did it together.”

Lulla sang one more soft note, like a hug. “La,” she whispered. “Together is the best way.”

The End.
