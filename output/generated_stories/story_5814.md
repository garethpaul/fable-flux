# Ollie Otter and the Garden Shed Plan

Ollie was a playful otter with soft whiskers and bright, curious eyes. One sunny morning, he peeked into Nana’s garden shed. The shed smelled like fresh wood and sweet soil. There were shiny tools, colorful pots, and a big paper sign that said, “Seed Box.”

“Ooh!” said Ollie, wiggling his tail. “I want to help Nana plant seeds!”

Nana smiled and handed him a small list. “You can help, Ollie,” she said gently. “But we must follow directions. Step by step. That keeps us safe and makes the work go well.”

Ollie nodded. “I can do it!”

Nana pointed to the list. “Step 1: Put on gloves. Step 2: Get the small blue watering can. Step 3: Fill it halfway. Step 4: Find the seed tray with little cups. Step 5: Sprinkle water. Then we’ll plant.”

Ollie’s eyes sparkled. He loved helping. He loved going fast, too. He bounced into the shed. The floor felt cool under his paws, and dust danced in the sunbeams.

He looked at the list. He looked at the shiny things. “I’ll be super quick!” he said.

He skipped Step 1 and grabbed the big red watering can instead of the small blue one. “This one can water more!” he giggled.

Nana called, “Remember the steps, Ollie!”

“I remember!” Ollie chirped, but he was already turning the spout. Water whooshed out too fast. Splash! Splash! The dirt got soggy. The seed tray was still on the shelf, and now there was a puddle on the floor.

“Oh no,” Ollie said softly. His paws felt wet and slippery. His heart made a little thump. He looked at the list again. “I didn’t follow the directions.”

Nana walked over and knelt beside him. She spoke calmly. “It’s okay to make a mistake. What can we do now?”

Ollie took a breath. He looked at the red watering can. He looked at the floor. He made a decision. “We can clean up. And start again. Slow.”

“That’s a good plan,” said Nana. “Let’s use time wisely. First, gloves. Then towel. Then the right can.”

Ollie slid on the soft garden gloves. They felt snug and safe. He and Nana pressed a towel on the puddle. Squeeze, squish. Ollie liked the sound. He smiled a little.

“Ready,” he said.

Nana tapped the list. “Step 1?”

“Gloves,” Ollie said proudly, wiggling his paws.

“Step 2?”

Ollie looked around. He saw a tall shelf. On top was the small blue watering can. It was light and sunny like the sky. “That one!” he said. He waited and pointed. “Nana, it’s high. Can you help?”

“That was a careful choice,” Nana said. “You asked for help. That is smart.”

Nana lifted the blue can down. It felt cool and smooth in Ollie’s paws.

“Step 3?” Nana asked.

“Fill halfway,” Ollie said. He watched the water line rise. He stopped at the middle. “Half!” he cheered.

“Step 4?” Nana smiled.

“Find the seed tray with little cups.” Ollie looked. He saw many trays. Some were big. Some were tiny. He paused. “I will check the labels.” He read the small tags slowly. “Cups… cups… here!” He found the tray with cute little pockets, like tiny nests.

“Step 5?” Nana asked.

“Sprinkle water,” Ollie said. He tilted the can gently. Drip, drip, drip. The sound was soft, like a little rain. The soil turned dark and damp, just right. Not soggy. Not dry.

Ollie’s chest felt warm and proud. He had been patient. He had followed the steps.

Nana opened the seed box. It smelled like warm grain. She poured seeds into Ollie’s glove. They felt smooth and small, like tiny buttons.

“Can I plant now?” Ollie asked.

“Yes,” Nana said. “Same way. One step at a time.”

Ollie made a plan. “First, a tiny hole. Then one seed. Then a cozy cover of dirt. Then a little pat. Then a little wait.”

He worked slowly. Tap, drop, cover, pat. Tap, drop, cover, pat. The shed was quiet. Ollie could hear birds outside and the tick-tick of the old clock on the wall. Time moved gently. He did not rush. He did not skip.

“I’m doing it,” he whispered. “I am using my time well.”

When all the seeds were tucked in, Ollie gave the tray one last soft sprinkle. Drip, drip. He wiped his gloves on the towel and looked at Nana.

“You followed the directions,” Nana said, giving him a hug. “You were patient. You made good choices.”

Ollie’s whiskers twitched with joy. “I felt better when I went slow,” he said. “I did not trip. I did not spill. I finished!”

They set the tray by the sunny window in the shed. Light warmed the little cups. Ollie pressed his nose to the glass. “Grow, little seeds,” he whispered.

“Seeds need time,” Nana said. “We will check them each day.”

Ollie nodded. “I can wait,” he said. “I can follow steps. Step by step.”

Outside, the breeze was soft, and the garden smelled sweet. Ollie held the list like a treasure. It was his map.

He smiled big. “Next time, I will read the list first,” he said. “Following directions helps me do my best.”

The End.
