# Polly Leads the Paint Parade

Polly was a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a soft yellow tummy. She loved colors. She loved smells of crayons and paints. She loved the cozy creative art room where she and her friends made pictures every afternoon. The room smelled like fruit paint and fresh paper. Sunlight danced on the jars. Brushes rested like sleepy cats in cups.

One day, Ms. Lila the teacher clapped her hands softly. “Friends,” she said, “today is Art Share Day! We will make a big mural together. Our mural will show how people celebrate around the world.”

Polly’s eyes sparkled. “Squawk! Colors, sounds, happy dance!” she chirped, flapping with joy.

Her friends gathered around the long paper on the wall. There was Timo the turtle, Mia the mouse, Zuzu the zebra, and Hana the hedgehog. Jars of red, blue, yellow, and green lined up like a rainbow train. The room felt busy and bright.

Ms. Lila smiled. “Remember: everyone shares, we listen, and we try our best.”

Polly puffed her feathers. She wanted to help. She wanted the mural to be beautiful. She wanted everyone to feel proud.

“Let’s paint fast!” Polly chirped. She swooped to the paper and dipped a brush in red. Swoosh! She painted big swirls.

“Wait,” Timo said softly. “I am slow. I need time.”

“I want to add my drum,” Zuzu said. “We dance with drums where my Nana lives.”

“I want to draw lanterns,” Mia said. “Red lanterns for a happy celebration.”

“I will make flowers,” Hana hummed. “Flowers with tiny dots.”

Polly’s feathers drooped a little. There were many ideas. Everyone talked at once. Paints dripped. Brushes clacked. Some colors mixed into brown puddles. The paper started to wrinkle.

“Oh no,” Polly whispered. The room felt noisy. The mural looked messy, not merry.

Ms. Lila leaned down. “What could help?” she asked gently.

Polly took a deep breath. She listened to the tap of dripping paint. She smelled the sweet fruit paints again. She felt her wings settle. Her heart said, Be brave. Be kind. Try again.

“I can help us take turns,” Polly said. “We can make a plan.” She stood tall on the paint table, careful with her claws. “Friends,” she called in a calm voice. “Let’s make a Paint Parade! One at a time, we will share our idea. We will listen. We will practice small, then add it big.”

Timo blinked. “A plan sounds good,” he said.

“Squawk! I will go last,” Polly added. “I will help each friend.”

Ms. Lila nodded. “That is leadership, Polly. You are guiding with care.”

Polly set out little sample papers. “Practice spots,” she said. “Try your idea small first.”

Mia stepped up. “I want red lanterns,” she said. She painted a round red circle on the practice spot, then added a little gold tassel. Swish, dot, dot. “Like this!”

Polly smiled. “Mia’s lanterns are bright and happy,” she said. “They hang in the sky at festivals. We will give them space.” Polly taped a clean spot on the big mural. “Now, lanterns here.” Mia painted slowly. The lanterns glowed.

Next, Zuzu clacked her hooves with joy. “Drums!” She practiced a drum on the little paper—two brown circles with a zigzag string. “We dance, stomp, and sing!” she said. “Boom-boom!”

Polly nodded. “Zuzu’s drums make music we can feel.” She pointed. “We will put drums next to the lanterns, so the light and music go together.” Zuzu painted, humming “boom-boom” softly.

Hana patted her brush. “Tiny flowers,” she whispered. She practiced pink and blue petals with tiny yellow dots. “We wear flower crowns in spring.”

Polly cooed, “They smell sweet and look gentle.” She found a spot at the bottom of the mural, like a soft meadow. “Flowers here, to hold the party.”

Timo moved slowly, steady and sure. “I want to draw a long table,” he said, “with rice and bread and juice.” He practiced little bowls and loaves. “We share food.”

Polly felt warm inside. “Food brings friends together,” she said. “Let’s place the table in the middle, so everyone can reach.” Timo smiled and painted carefully.

Polly helped wash brushes between turns. Swish in the water cup. Tap-tap on the towel. She reminded friends, “Your turn. Now we listen. Now we cheer.” The room became calm and happy again. Colors stayed bright. Shapes kept their space.

Finally, it was Polly’s turn. She looked at the mural. Lanterns glowed. Drums sang. Flowers smiled. The table waited. “I will paint a big sky,” she said, “with birds from many places, all flying together.” She practiced a small bird—blue wings, gold beak, a soft white eye. Then she floated up and painted them across the top. The birds looked like songs in the sky.

Everyone stepped back. The mural was full and friendly. It showed a party of many cultures—lanterns, drums, flowers, foods, and birds all together. It looked like a hug.

Ms. Lila clapped. “Beautiful teamwork,” she said. “Polly, you led with listening, planning, and practice.”

Polly felt proud, but also calm. “We did it together,” she said. “We took turns. We respected each other. We practiced. That is how leaders help.”

The friends smiled. The art room smelled like paint and paper and happiness. The sunlight felt warm on their faces. They all whispered, “Boom-boom, swish-swish, glow-glow,” and giggled.

Polly tilted her head. “Our mural shows that our differences make one big celebration,” she said. “When we listen and lead with care, everyone shines.”

The End.
