# Bella Bear and the Fair Share Guest Room

Bella was a friendly bear cub with soft brown fur and bright eyes. She wiggled her round nose and sniffed the cozy guest room in her family’s den. The guest room smelled like fresh pine and warm blankets. The window let in a soft, golden light. On the rug were toys, books, and a big basket of crayons.

“Today we share,” Mama Bear said, smoothing the quilt. “Cousin Leo is visiting. He comes from a far forest, across the river. He is our guest.”

Bella clapped her paws. “Yay! I love to share.” But when she looked at her favorite puzzle on the table, her tummy felt tight. “What if Leo takes my puzzle?” Bella whispered.

Mama Bear hugged her. “We can make fair rules. Fair means everyone is treated right. That is justice.”

Bella nodded. Justice. Fair rules. She could try.

Soon, the door creaked. “Hello!” roared a small, cheerful voice. It was Cousin Leo the bear cub. He wore a little scarf with tiny world shapes on it. It was bright blue and soft. He dragged a small suitcase that bumped over the rug. Thump, thump.

“Hi, Leo!” Bella said, waving. “Welcome to our guest room.”

Leo sniffed. “Mmm, it smells like cinnamon and pine!” He grinned. “Can we color?”

“Yes,” Bella said, pointing to the basket. “We have crayons for everyone.”

They sat on the rug. The crayons felt smooth and warm in Bella’s paw. The paper made soft scratch-scratch sounds. Leo reached for the biggest red crayon. Bella reached too. Their paws bumped.

“I had it first,” Bella said, her cheeks getting hot.

“But I need red for my world flag,” Leo said. “I want to draw our planet with friends holding hands.”

Bella’s tummy tightened again. She wanted the red for her picture of a big strawberry. She felt a little storm of frustration in her chest. Her paws pressed into the rug. She wanted to grab the crayon.

Mama Bear peeked in. “I see big feelings,” she said softly. “What can we do when we feel frustrated?”

Bella took a deep breath. In, out. “Smell the flowers, blow the candles,” she whispered to herself. She breathed in like smelling a flower. She breathed out like blowing candles. The tight feeling loosened.

“We can make a fair plan,” Bella said. “A plan is justice. We both get a turn.”

Leo tilted his head. “How?”

“We can use the timer,” Bella said, pointing to the small sand timer on the shelf. The sand was sparkly and purple. “Two minutes for you. Two minutes for me. We say ‘stop’ and switch when the sand runs out.”

Leo looked at the sand. “Okay,” he said. “That is fair.”

They turned the timer. The sand was like tiny stars falling. Leo used the red crayon first. He drew a round Earth, blue and green. He hummed a happy tune. Bella watched. She felt a little tug inside. She wanted the red. She looked at her strawberry outline and squeezed her paws. Then she sniffed the cozy blanket. She listened to the sand’s soft shhh. She waited.

“Switch,” Leo said when the sand was gone. He smiled and passed the crayon. “Thank you for waiting, Bella.”

“Thank you for sharing,” Bella said. The red crayon felt just right in her paw. She colored her strawberry bright and sweet. She could almost taste it—juicy and fresh.

They kept going. They used the timer for the puzzle too. Click, click, the pieces fit. When Leo took a puzzle corner Bella wanted, she felt the little storm again. She breathed in and out. “Let’s make a rule,” she said. “When someone is using a piece, we wait. We can ask, ‘Can I have it next?’”

Leo nodded. “Can I have it next?” he asked when Bella held a blue piece.

“Yes. After I snap it in,” Bella said. “Then it’s your turn.”

They worked together, turn by turn. The puzzle showed animals from many lands—elephants, penguins, pandas, and bears. “We all live on the same Earth,” Leo said, pointing at the animals. “We are global friends.”

Bella smiled. “Global friends share and use fair rules.”

When the puzzle was done, Mama Bear brought warm honey milk. The mugs felt smooth and hot. The honey smelled sweet. They sipped. “How does your body feel now?” Mama asked.

“Calm,” Bella said. “My tummy is soft.”

“Happy,” Leo said. “I got a turn. Bella got a turn. That is justice.”

Mama Bear nodded. “Justice means fair treatment. It helps everyone feel safe and heard. You made fair rules together. You solved the problem.”

Bella’s eyes shone. “When I felt frustrated, I breathed. I used words. I made a plan.” She tapped the timer. “This helped.”

Leo held up his drawing. It showed children and animals around the Earth, holding hands. He added a tiny red strawberry in the corner. “For you,” he said.

Bella giggled and added a blue scarf to her strawberry. “For you.”

They hung the art on the guest room wall, right by the window where the golden light made the colors glow. The room felt even cozier now, like a hug.

Bella patted the timer and the crayon basket. “These are our justice tools,” she said.

“And we can use them with anyone,” Leo added. “Guests, friends, and neighbors all around the world.”

Bella nodded. “When we feel big feelings, we can breathe, use fair rules, and share. That helps everyone.”

Mama Bear smiled. “That is the lesson for today.”

Bella and Leo clinked their mugs. “Fair and kind,” they said together.

The End.
