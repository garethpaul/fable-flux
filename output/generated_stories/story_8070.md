# Ollie Otter's Car Seat Cheer

Ollie was a playful otter with bright eyes and a bouncy tail. He loved to wiggle, giggle, and splash. Today, Ollie did not splash. He buckled into the family car. The seat was warm from the sun. The air smelled like orange slices and clean seats. The engine purred, “vrrr-vrrr,” like a sleepy cat.

“We are going to the park,” Mama said. “Otter League day!”

Ollie clapped his little paws. “Sports day! I love to play!” he chirped.

In the back seat, a small bag of balls rolled softly. Tap-tap-tap. There was a tiny soccer ball, a bouncy blue tennis ball, and a soft foam basketball. Ollie leaned toward them and whispered, “Hi, balls. Are you ready?”

The car rolled forward. Trees whooshed by. Ollie bounced in his seat, but the seatbelt hugged him snug. “I will score goals! I will throw high! I will be the best!” Ollie said, puffing his chest.

Mama’s eyes smiled in the mirror. “Sports are for fun,” she said. “We try our best, we practice, and we cheer for friends.”

Ollie nodded, but he still wanted to be the best.

At the red light, Papa turned and handed Ollie a little whistle on a ribbon. “You can be our car coach until we arrive,” Papa said.

Ollie grinned. “Beep-beep! Team family, ready!” he squeaked. He blew a tiny toot. Tweet!

“Warm-up time,” Mama said. “Wiggle your paws.”

Ollie wiggled and felt his body wake up. He could hear the soft swish of Mama’s jacket and smell Papa’s mint gum. He felt brave.

Soon, the car turned into the park lot. Ollie saw kids and critters with bright jerseys: a rabbit with long ears, a turtle with shiny shell pads, and a beaver with big teeth. The grass looked like a green blanket. A light breeze smelled like fresh dirt and cut grass.

But then Ollie saw something that made his whiskers droop. Next to the field, a tall otter named Max spun a soccer ball on one finger. “Look at me!” Max shouted. The ball went zzzzip! Everyone clapped.

Ollie’s heart sank. “I want to do that,” he whispered. “But I can’t.”

They parked. Papa unbuckled Ollie. “Deep breath,” Papa said. “In… and out.” Ollie did. His tummy felt softer. The breeze kissed his nose.

On the field, Coach Bear clapped big paws. “Welcome! Today we play mini-soccer, mini-basketball, and relay runs. We learn to share, to try, and to cheer!”

Ollie joined Team Splash with Turtle Tessa and Rabbit Rio. Max was on the other team. The first game was mini-soccer. The whistle blew. Tweet!

Ollie ran. His feet went pat-pat-pat on the grass. The ball rolled toward him, but Max zipped in and kicked it away. Zoom! “Mine!” Max said.

Ollie’s cheeks grew hot. “That’s not fair,” he said. He wanted to shout. He wanted to quit. He felt small.

Turtle Tessa touched his paw. “We can try again,” she said softly. “I am slow, but I see good passes.”

Rabbit Rio bounced. “I am fast! I can help.”

Ollie blinked. He looked at his team. They were different. They were special. He took a breath. “Okay,” he said. “Pass to me and I pass to you.”

The ball rolled back. Tessa tapped it to Ollie. Tap. Ollie tapped it to Rio. Tap. Rio zipped forward. Zoom! “Shoot!” Ollie called.

Rio kicked. The ball slid into the tiny net. Swish!

“Goal!” Ollie cheered. He threw his paws up. His chest felt warm and proud. “We did it together!”

Max frowned. “I want to score all the goals,” he said.

Coach Bear nodded kindly. “Max, sharing the ball helps everyone play. Sports are a team song.”

Max looked at his shoes. “Oh,” he said.

Next came mini-basketball. The hoop was low. The ball was soft and orange. Ollie tried to throw. His first throw went bonk off the rim. The second went swoosh! “Yes!” he squeaked.

Max rushed over. “Show me your throw,” he asked.

Ollie smiled. “Bend knees, look at the hoop, and push up.” He showed slowly. Max tried. Bonk. Ollie clapped. “Good try! Try again.” Max tried again. Swoosh.

Max’s face brightened. “Thanks,” he said. “You are good at helping.”

Ollie felt tall inside. “You are good at spinning the ball,” he said. “Can you teach me later?”

“Deal,” said Max.

For the relay, Coach Bear gave each team a ribbon baton. It felt smooth and silky in Ollie’s paw. “Run to the cone and back,” Coach said. “Cheer for your friends!”

Tessa went first. “Go, Tessa!” Ollie and Rio shouted. Tessa was slow but steady. The wind went whoosh in her ears. She passed to Rio. Rio zipped so fast the ribbon fluttered like a flag. He passed to Ollie.

Ollie ran. His paws pounded soft-soft on the grass. He felt the sun on his whiskers and the cool air in his fur. He heard his team cheer, “Go, Ollie, go!” He smiled and ran his best. He passed the ribbon with a happy gasp.

When the games ended, Coach Bear gathered everyone. “What did we learn?” he asked.

Ollie raised his paw. “Sports are fun when we share,” he said. “We can try, practice, and cheer. Being different helps the team.”

Max raised his paw too. “I learned to pass and to say thank you,” he said. He handed Ollie a bright sticker with a star. “For being a kind coach,” Max added.

Ollie blinked. “For you,” he said, giving Max the blue tennis ball from his bag. “You can practice spins.” Max hugged it. “Thank you!”

Back in the family car, Ollie buckled in. The seat was cool now. The engine purred again. Mama looked in the mirror. “How do you feel?”

Ollie’s smile was big and soft. “I feel strong,” he said. “I did my best. I helped a friend. We played as a team.”

Papa nodded. “That’s the spirit.”

Ollie held his little whistle and gave a tiny tweet. “Team family,” he whispered. “We cheer, we share, we play.”

The End.
