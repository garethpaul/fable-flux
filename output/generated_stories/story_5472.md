# Panda Poppy Builds a Ride-Ready Plan

Poppy the panda sat in her family car with a soft click of her seat belt. “Click!” went the buckle. She wiggled her furry toes and smiled. The car smelled like warm sunshine, orange peels, and a tiny bit like crayons. Dad Panda sat in the front. Mom Panda checked the map. Baby Bamboo Bear held a toy block and made little “tap-tap” sounds.

Today was a building day at the park. There would be blocks, ramps, and little wheels to make cars go fast. Poppy loved to build, but she knew building takes time. Poppy was a patient panda.

“Snacks first,” said Mom Panda, passing back a crunchy apple slice. “Healthy builders need healthy bites.”

“Crunch,” went the apple. Sweet juice tickled Poppy’s tongue. “I’m fueling up,” she said. “Like a car!”

Dad Panda laughed. “Vroom, vroom.”

The engine hummed, soft like a lullaby. The road made a gentle “whoosh.” Trees waved hello. Poppy held her little travel box of blocks. The box rattled happily. “Clack, clack.”

“We will build a car ramp,” Poppy whispered to Baby Bamboo Bear. “We will test. We will fix. We will try again.”

Baby Bamboo Bear clapped. “Try, try!”

But then the car made a tiny “beep” sound. The dashboard light blinked. Dad Panda pulled over to a quiet spot under a big shade tree. The air smelled like pine and cool dirt.

“Oh dear,” said Mom Panda. “The tire feels soft.”

Poppy felt her tummy flutter. “Will we miss building time?” she asked.

Dad Panda touched her paw. “We will try. We will stay calm. We will think. We will be patient.”

Poppy took a deep breath. In, two, three. Out, two, three. The air was fresh. The breeze kissed her cheeks. “I can help,” she said.

Mom Panda nodded. “Let’s make a plan. Plans help us keep trying.”

Poppy loved plans. She pulled out her little notebook. The pages smelled like paper and lemons. She drew three simple steps with crayon:

1. Look.
2. Think.
3. Try again.

“Step one: Look,” Poppy said, peeking out the window. The tire was sleepy and squishy.

“Step two: Think,” she said. “We could use the spare tire. We could call for help. We could do both!”

Dad Panda smiled. “That’s open-minded thinking. Let’s try the spare, and if it’s tricky, we call.”

“Step three: Try again,” Poppy whispered.

Dad Panda took out the spare tire and the jack. They all watched. The metal felt cool and shiny. The jack went “crank, crank.” The car lifted just a little. Baby Bamboo Bear made the same “crank” sound and giggled.

Poppy held the flashlight steady. The light was warm in her paw. She kept her arm strong. “I can hold it,” she said. “I can wait.” Her arm got tired, but she breathed and switched hands. “I am a patient panda.”

The wind rustled the leaves. A bird sang, “Chee-chee!” Mom Panda handed Poppy a sip of water. “Builders take care of their bodies,” Mom said. “Sip, sip.”

“Sip,” went Poppy. The cool water felt good in her mouth. She offered a tiny sip to her stuffed bamboo toy, then giggled. “Stuffies don’t drink.”

Dad Panda loosened the bolts. “Turn, turn,” he said. “Slow and safe.”

Poppy watched closely. “Lefty loosey, righty tighty,” she said. “That is logical thinking.”

“Exactly,” said Dad Panda.

One bolt rolled away. “Oh!” Poppy reached and caught it before it slipped into the grass. Her paw pads felt the smooth, cold circle. “Got you!”

“That’s persistence,” Mom said. “You kept your eyes on the job.”

The new tire went on. “Thump.” Dad Panda tightened the bolts. “Righty tighty.” The jack went “crank, crank” down.

Poppy waited, calm and steady. She wanted to hop and wiggle, but she kept her light shining. “I can wait. I can help.”

At last, Dad Panda wiped his paws. “We did it together.”

Poppy clapped. “We didn’t give up!”

Back in the car, Poppy ate another apple slice. “Crunch.” She felt strong and ready. The road hummed. The sun peeked from a cloud. Poppy’s heart felt warm like hot cocoa.

When they reached the park, the building table was still there. Blocks in bright colors, smooth and cool, waited for busy paws. Wheels in a basket whispered, “Spin, spin.” A small ramp stood, but it was wobbly.

Poppy set down her travel box. “We will build a steady ramp,” she said. “Look, think, try again.”

She lined up wide blocks at the bottom for a strong base. “These are like the car’s wheels,” she told Baby Bamboo Bear. “They need to be firm.”

She tested the ramp. A little wooden car rolled, “rattle-rattle,” and fell off the side. “Oops.”

Poppy took a breath. “We do not give up. We fix.” She added side rails. She tried again. The car rolled, “rrrrrr,” and stopped too soon.

“What else can we try?” Mom asked.

Poppy tapped her chin. “More slope.” She added taller blocks. She tested. “Zoom!” The car zipped and landed in the soft basket. Baby Bamboo Bear squealed, “Yay!”

Poppy shared the ramp with a new friend, a shy little fox cub. “Would you like to try?” Poppy asked.

The fox cub nodded. “I’m nervous.”

“It’s okay,” Poppy said kindly. “We can try together. If it doesn’t work, we try again.”

They rolled the car. “Zoom!” Success! The fox cub smiled, tail swishing. “Thank you.”

Poppy’s heart felt big. She patted the ramp. “Building takes time. We look. We think. We try again. We take care of our bodies. We help.”

On the ride home, the sky turned cotton-candy pink. The car hummed. Poppy rested her head on the soft seat. “Today I was patient,” she whispered. “Today I kept trying.”

Mom Panda squeezed her paw. “And that is how we grow.”

The End.
