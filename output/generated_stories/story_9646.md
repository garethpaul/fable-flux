# Roary’s Rolling Practice

The family car hummed softly like a bee. The seats were warm and cozy. Sunlight danced in wiggly lines on the windows. In the back seat sat Roary, a brave little lion cub with bright eyes and soft, golden fur. His tail swished with little flicks. He wore tiny sneakers with blue stripes.

“Today is team day,” said Mama Lion, turning the steering wheel gently. “We will meet your new cub team at the field.”

Roary’s tummy fluttered. “But what if I can’t kick the ball?” he whispered, pressing a paw to his belly. He could smell the sweet, fruity snack Mama packed and hear the soft whirr of the air from the vents.

Papa Lion looked back with a kind smile. “Sports are for learning,” Papa said. “We try. We practice. We have fun.”

Roary nodded, but his ears still wiggled with worry.

Big sister Lina sat beside him with a water bottle that went slosh-slosh. “Let’s warm up,” she said. “We can practice in the car!”

Roary blinked. “In the car?”

“Little practice,” Mama said with a giggle. “Safe practice.”

Papa tapped the screen and a gentle song began. The beat was soft and bouncy. Roary could feel it thump-thump through the seat.

“First, breathing,” Papa said. “Sports and wellness go together. Deep breath in... and out.”

Roary breathed in through his nose. He smelled the clean car smell and a hint of orange peel. He breathed out through his mouth. It felt slow and calm. “In... out,” he said. His tummy settled a little.

“Now, kindness warm-up,” Lina said. “We say kind words to our team. I’ll start. Roary, you are brave.”

Roary’s whiskers lifted. “Thank you,” he said softly. “Lina, you are helpful.”

Mama smiled. “I like this team already.”

“Next, tiny kicks,” Lina said, holding a soft, squishy ball the size of an orange. “Only toe taps.” She placed it on the floor by Roary’s feet. The ball felt cool and rubbery when his paw touched it.

“Tap-tap,” Roary said, tapping gently with his sneaker. The ball made a little squeak. Tap-tap-tap. He liked the sound. It felt like a small drum. His tail swished in time.

“Good rhythm,” Papa said. “Sports use our bodies and our minds. Tap, breathe, smile.”

Roary breathed, tapped, and smiled. The car rolled along. Trees slid by like green paint strokes. The snack bag rustled. Roary’s tummy made a tiny rumble. He giggled.

Then the car slowed. Rain began to patter on the roof. Pitter-patter. Pitter-PAT. The sky turned gray. “Oh dear,” Mama said. “The field is muddy. The coach says we must wait.”

Roary’s ears drooped. “No team day?”

“We can still practice,” Lina said. “We can do car stretches.”

Roary sat up. “I want to be ready. I want to be strong and kind.”

“Great goals,” Papa said. “Let’s make a plan. Problem: we can’t go to the field. Solution: we make the car our mini gym.”

They all laughed.

“Check your body,” Mama said. “Wellness first. Sip of water.”

Roary took a sip. The water felt cool and fresh. He felt it slide down, like a tiny stream.

“Stretch arms,” Lina said, reaching up toward the soft fabric roof. “Reach for the raindrops!”

Roary reached too. He felt his shoulders wake up. “Hello, shoulders,” he said.

“Now ankle circles,” Papa guided. Roary moved his paw in a gentle round-and-round. His sneaker made a quiet shuff-shuff on the mat.

“Kindness time again,” Lina said. “We cheer. Roary, you can say, ‘You can do it!’ to yourself.”

Roary took a breath. “Roary, you can do it,” he said. His voice sounded steadier. His chest felt warm, like a hug.

They played “Pass the Squeeze.” Lina handed Roary the squishy ball. It felt bouncy and cool in his paws.

“Light toss to me,” Papa said, holding out his big paw between the seats. Roary tossed gently. The ball made a soft boop against Papa’s paw. “Nice control,” Papa cheered.

“Kindness to the ball,” Mama joked. “Soft hands.”

Roary giggled. “Soft hands, soft heart.”

They practiced toe taps again. Tap-tap-tap. They counted. One, two, three, four. Roary kept a steady beat. He felt proud. The rain slowed to a drip-drop. The gray turned to silver, then to soft blue.

A message buzzed on Mama’s phone. “The field is open!” she said.

They parked by the grass. Other cubs waved. The ground smelled like wet earth. The air felt cool and clean. Roary stepped out. His sneakers squished. Squish-squish. He liked the funny sound.

Coach Bear smiled. “Welcome, team! Let’s warm up.” Roary knew just what to do. He breathed in and out. He stretched his arms to “reach for raindrops.” He did ankle circles. He tapped the ball. Tap-tap-tap.

A shy cub stood alone, ears low. Roary walked over with a kind smile. “Hi, I’m Roary. You can do it,” he said, handing the cub the soft ball. “Soft hands, soft heart.”

The cub smiled. “Thank you,” the cub said.

They practiced passing. Boop, boop, boop. Roary moved with care. He listened to the coach. He drank water when he needed. He helped a friend up when the friend slipped in the mud. “Are you okay?” Roary asked.

“I’m okay,” the friend said, grinning.

At the end, Coach Bear clapped. “Great job, team. Sports are about trying, caring, and being healthy.”

Roary felt tall inside. He snuggled into his car seat on the way home. The seat was warm. The car hummed. He held the squishy ball and smiled.

“I was brave,” Roary said.

“You were kind,” Lina said.

“You practiced and stayed well,” Papa said.

“And you had fun,” Mama added.

Roary nodded. “Sports help my body and my heart,” he said. “I can do it, and I can help others do it too.”

The End.
