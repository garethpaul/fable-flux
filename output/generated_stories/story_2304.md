# Dori the Dolphin Visits the Balcony Garden

Dori was a playful dolphin who loved to learn new things. One sunny morning, she splashed out of the blue sea with a happy squeak. “Eee-eee! I wonder what is up there,” she said, pointing her fin toward a tall building by the shore. On the building, there was a little balcony with a tiny garden. It was bright with flowers and green leaves. It smelled like mint and rain.

Dori wiggled and waved her tail. “I am curious,” she whispered. “I want to discover what grows up there.” She took a big bounce with a sparkling splash. With a gentle whoosh, a friendly wave lifted Dori, like a soft elevator made of water. It set her down in a big blue bucket on the balcony. Plop!

“Hello?” Dori called, peeking over the bucket. “Is this a garden?”

“Hello!” chirped a ladybug. “Welcome to our balcony garden. I’m Dot.”

“Hi, Dot!” Dori giggled. “I am Dori. I am a dolphin, and I want to learn.”

A warm breeze tickled the leaves. The air smelled like sweet strawberries and peppery basil. Somewhere, a tiny bell rang. Tink, tink.

“Come and see,” said Dot. “We have many plants.” She pointed with a tiny red wing.

Dori slid from the bucket into a big pan of cool rainwater. “Ahh,” she sighed. “This water feels nice.” Her smooth skin shivered with happy bubbles.

A snail peeked from under a pot. “I am Slow Sam,” he said, smiling. “Be careful. The soil is soft.”

“I will be gentle,” said Dori. She flapped her fin very softly. She made a little breeze that smelled like wet dirt.

Dori looked around with round, shiny eyes. She saw yellow sunflowers. She saw red tomatoes. She saw a little lemon tree with tiny green lemons. “Ooo,” she breathed. “So many shapes and colors!”

Dot the ladybug flew close to Dori’s nose. “What do you want to discover today?”

“I want to know how a garden grows,” Dori said. “I want to help. But I am a dolphin. I do not have hands.”

“That is okay,” said Slow Sam. “We can include you. Everyone can help in a different way.”

Just then, a small problem came. The strawberry plant looked droopy. Its leaves were floppy. The soil felt dry and dusty. A bee buzzed by and frowned. “Bzz… this plant is thirsty,” said Bee Bea.

“Oh no!” Dori gasped. “How can we fix it?”

Dot tapped her chin. “We need water, but not too much.”

Dori looked at the rainwater pan. She looked at her tail. “I can splash,” she said. “But I must be careful. Gentle splashes.”

“Good thinking,” said Bee Bea. “Discovery time! Try a small splash first.”

Dori took a slow breath. She wiggled just a little. Swish. A soft wave slid over the dry soil. It made a quiet sound like shhhh. The strawberry leaves lifted a tiny bit.

“Again?” asked Dori.

“Again,” said Slow Sam.

Swish. Swish. Two more tiny waves kissed the soil. The air smelled fresh, like a cloud after rain. The strawberry plant perked up. The little white flowers opened. A red berry glowed in the sun.

“We did it!” Dori cheered. “The plant is happy.”

“You discovered the right amount,” said Bee Bea. “Not too much. Not too little.”

Dori grinned. “I learned by trying and watching.”

Dot clapped her tiny wings. “Curiosity helped! You asked, you tested, and you saw what happened.”

Dori swam in a small circle in the pan, making soft ripples. She felt proud and calm. Then she noticed another problem. The tomato stems were leaning to one side. A gentle wind pushed them. They wobbled.

“These tomatoes need support,” said Slow Sam. “We must keep them safe.”

“How can I help?” asked Dori.

Dot pointed to some soft cloth ties. “We can tie the stems to the stick. But the knot is tricky.”

Dori thought and thought. “I can hold the stems steady with my nose,” she said. “You can tie.”

“Great idea!” said Bee Bea.

Dori pressed her cool nose against the tomato stem. She held very still. Dot and Bee Bea worked together. The cloth felt soft and smooth. The knot was snug but gentle. The tomatoes stood tall, like little soldiers in red hats.

“Teamwork!” said Dot.

“Inclusion!” buzzed Bee Bea.

“Discovery!” sang Dori. “I discovered I can help in my own dolphin way.”

Just then, a child stepped onto the balcony. The child wore a blue shirt and kind eyes. “Wow,” the child said. “A dolphin in our garden!”

“Hello!” Dori squeaked. “We are caring for the plants.”

The child knelt and smiled. “Thank you. Everyone is welcome here.” The child sprinkled a little water and patted the soil. The sun felt warm on Dori’s back. The lemon tree leaves smelled bright and sunny.

Dori looked around. The garden whispered with bee buzz, leaf rustle, and happy plant sighs. She felt safe and included. She felt smart and kind.

“I must go back to the sea,” Dori said. “But I will visit again.”

“Come back soon,” said Dot.

“We will discover more,” said Slow Sam.

“And learn more,” buzzed Bee Bea.

Dori gave one last gentle splash. “I learned that being curious helps me find answers. I learned that I can help, even if I am different. And I learned that we grow best when we learn together.”

She smiled and slid back into the soft water wave. With a whoosh and a sparkle, she swam toward the open sea, heart full and happy.

The garden stood tall and bright, ready for the next curious day.

The End.
