# Pippa Parrot’s Big Basement Day

Pippa Parrot lived in a busy basement. The basement hummed and whirred. The washer swished. The dryer rumbled. A tiny fan buzzed like a bee. Shelves held boxes of buttons, ribbons, paper, and paints. The floor was cool and smooth. It smelled like soap and warm towels.

Pippa was a creative parrot. Her feathers were green with a splash of sunny yellow. She loved to make things. She loved to try new ideas.

“Today, I will make a show!” Pippa chirped. “A bright, brave, beautiful show!”

She flapped to a table and opened a box. “Oooh,” she said, “soft ribbons.” She rubbed the ribbons on her cheek. They felt silky. She sniffed the paints. “They smell like lemons.” She tapped a drum with her claw. “Boom-boom!” The sound bounced around the room.

Pippa laid out paper and crayons. She drew a big blue sky. She glued little stars that sparkled. She made a tiny stage from a shoebox. She taped a curtain made of purple fabric.

“I can do this,” she told herself. Her heart felt warm and proud. “I am good at making.”

Just then, down the stairs came Sammy Squirrel and Tilly Tortoise. Sammy scampered fast. Tilly moved slow and steady. Their feet made little sounds—pitter-patter and soft scrape.

“What are you doing, Pippa?” asked Sammy, twitching his tail.

“A show!” Pippa said. “I will sing. I will dance. I will make art.”

Sammy sniffed. “Are you sure you can? Shows are hard,” he said.

Tilly blinked kindly. “I like your purple curtain,” she said.

Pippa’s tummy fluttered. Was a show too hard? The washer swished louder. The fan buzzed in her ear. “Whirr, whirr,” it sang. Pippa looked at her stage. She felt small.

“I think I can,” she whispered. “I will try.”

Sammy bounced. “I can help! I can play the drum,” he said, tapping, “boom-boom-boom!” It was very loud.

Tilly smiled. “I can hold the stars,” she said softly.

Pippa took a deep breath. She fluffed her feathers. “Friends,” she said, “I like help. But I need the drum to be soft, not loud. I need the stars to shine slow, not all at once.”

Sammy froze. “Soft?” he asked.

“Yes, please,” Pippa said. “Boom… pause… boom,” she hummed. “Like a heartbeat.” She patted her chest. “I feel brave when the drum is calm.”

Sammy nodded. “I can do soft,” he said. He tapped gently. “Boom… pause… boom.”

Tilly lifted one star at a time. The silver points twinkled. “Slow and shiny,” she said.

Pippa smiled. Her shoulders relaxed. She felt tall inside. She cleared her throat. “Ahem.”

She stepped onto the shoebox stage. The curtain swished. The dryer rumbled like far thunder. The room smelled lemon-sweet. The lights made the ribbons glow.

“Hello,” Pippa chirped. “Welcome to my Big Basement Day!” Her voice was bright. “Today I will show my ideas.”

She sang a little song about colors. “Red is warm, blue is wide, yellow is happy by my side.” Her notes were clear and sweet. Sammy’s soft drum kept time. Tilly’s stars winked like tiny smiles.

Pippa danced a tiny dance. She hopped and twirled. Her feathers brushed the air. “Swish, swish,” they said. She held up her drawing of the blue sky and the shining stars.

Then, Pippa tried a new trick. She balanced a ribbon on her beak. The ribbon wiggled and slipped. “Oops!” Pippa giggled. The ribbon fell to the floor.

Sammy gasped. Tilly leaned in. Pippa felt a pinch in her tummy. “It’s okay,” she told herself. “I am still me. I can try again.”

She spoke out loud, strong and kind. “I made a mistake, and that is okay. I am still proud. I will try a new way.”

She made a loop with the ribbon and slipped it over her wing. It stayed! She fluttered and the ribbon danced like a friendly snake.

“Yay, Pippa!” Sammy cheered.

“Beautiful,” Tilly said.

Pippa bowed. “Thank you,” she said. Her cheeks felt warm with joy. “I believed in me, and I asked for what I needed. That helped a lot.”

After the show, Sammy looked shy. “I talked too loud at first,” he said. “Sorry.”

Pippa patted his paw with her wing. “Thank you for helping,” she said. “You listened when I asked for soft. That was kind.”

Tilly nodded. “You were very brave, Pippa. You told us your needs.”

Pippa chirped. “That is called being assertive,” she said carefully. “It means using your brave voice to say what helps you.”

They cleaned up together. The ribbons went in a box. The stars went in a jar. The paint brushes swished in soapy water. Swish-swish. The basement hummed and glowed.

Pippa looked at her little stage. She felt strong inside. “I can make. I can try. I can ask. I can be kind.”

Sammy smiled. “And we can listen.”

Tilly smiled too. “And we can care.”

Pippa flapped her wings and laughed. “Together, we shine,” she said.

The End.
