# Joey Roo’s Bubbly Bathroom Routine

Joey Roo was a joyful kangaroo joey with bright eyes and a bouncy tail. Every morning, he hopped to the bathroom. The floor felt cool on his toes. The mirror smelled like lemon spray. The faucet made a gentle, shiny drip… drip… drip.

“Good morning, toothbrush!” Joey chirped. He loved bubbles and splashes and silly songs.

Mama Roo peeped in with a warm smile. “Time for your bathroom routine, little hopper. We brush, we wash, we flush, and we tidy. That helps the earth and helps us feel good.”

Joey clapped his tiny paws. “I can do it!”

He turned on the water. Whoosh! The sink sang. “Swish-swish!” Joey scrubbed his toothbrush in the stream. Minty smell tickled his nose.

Mama Roo tapped the faucet. “Joey, we keep the water off while we brush. That saves water for fish, flowers, and friends.”

Joey blinked. “For fish?” He pictured tiny fish waving their fins. He giggled. “Okay!” He turned the water off. The bathroom felt quiet and calm.

“Brush-brush, top and bottom,” Joey sang. “Little circles, no big splashes.” He felt the soft bristles and tasted cool mint. He hummed his brushing song: “One, two, three… smile for me!” When he was done, he turned the water on for a little rinse, then clicked it off again. “All done!”

“Great routine,” Mama said. “You remembered to save water.”

Joey puffed up with pride. He reached for the soap. “Squish!” The soap was slippery and smelled like oranges. He made bubbly gloves and washed between his fingers. “Bubbles, bubbles, pop-pop-pop!”

But then Joey saw something on the floor. “Uh-oh.” His towel lay in a soggy puddle. He had dropped it last night. The floor was wet and icky. Joey’s ears drooped. “I made a mess.”

Mama Roo knelt beside him. “We all make mistakes. What can we do to make amends?”

Joey thought. He put a paw on his heart. “I can fix it.” He picked up the towel carefully. Drip! The water splashed his toes. “Cold!” He giggled, then wrung the towel over the tub. “Twist-twist.” He hung the towel neatly on the hook. “There. Tidy!”

Mama Roo nodded. “That’s making amends. We fix what we can. We learn for next time.”

Joey looked around. The toilet paper roll was rolling, rolling… across the floor! “Oops.” Last night he had pulled too much. The paper felt soft like clouds.

“How much do we need?” Mama asked softly.

“Just a little,” Joey said. He rolled the extra paper back up. “Round and round.” He patted it gently. “All set.”

He flushed the toilet: “Whoooosh!” Then he closed the lid and listened to the quiet. He smiled. “Good-bye, whoosh.”

Joey hopped to the light switch. The bathroom lamp shone bright and warm. “Lights on for looking,” he said. “Lights off when we’re done.” He clicked it off and on, off and on.

Mama Roo chuckled. “One click is enough, little hopper. Turning off lights saves energy.”

Joey nodded. “Save water. Save paper. Save light.” He tapped his nose. “That’s my routine!”

Just then, he noticed something else. The bin was full of empty bottles and boxes. “Trash mountain,” he whispered.

Mama Roo opened a new bin with a blue sticker. “This one is for recycling. Bottles and boxes go here. We can give them a new life.”

Joey’s eyes sparkled. “New life? Like a caterpillar to a butterfly?” He picked up an empty shampoo bottle and plopped it into the blue bin. “Plink!” He folded the toothpaste box with his small paws. “Fold-fold.” He placed it in. “Plonk!”

He took a deep breath. The bathroom smelled clean and citrusy. The floor felt dry now, warm from the morning sun. He heard a bird chirp outside the window. “Cheep-cheep!”

“Joey,” Mama said, “what’s your bathroom routine?”

Joey stood tall and recited, tapping each paw on the mat:
- Turn on water, wet brush, then turn water off.
- Brush-brush, little circles.
- Rinse quick, turn water off.
- Wash hands, bubbles pop, water off.
- Use just enough toilet paper.
- Flush once, lid down.
- Towels on hooks, toys in the basket.
- Lights off when we go.
- Recycle bottles and boxes.
- If I make a mess, I make amends.

Mama Roo clapped. “That is a helpful, earth-friendly routine.”

Joey beamed. “I can help the earth every day! I can help our home, too.”

Before leaving, Joey looked at the sink. A tiny drip hung on the faucet. He reached up and gave it a careful twist. The drip stopped. “Good-night, little drip,” he whispered, even though it was morning. He giggled.

Mama Roo held his paw. “Kind hands, kind heart, kind home.”

Joey hopped to the door. “Routine helps me remember. Routine helps me care.” He clicked off the light. The bathroom grew softly dim. He felt proud and cozy inside.

As they left, Joey turned back and waved. “Thank you, bathroom! See you after playtime.”

He bounced down the hall, humming his brushing song. He felt light as a bubble and strong as a big kangaroo. He knew he could fix mistakes, try again, and help the earth with his little choices, every single day.

The End.
