# Nutkin's Big Kitchen Adventure

Nutkin the squirrel had a very rumbly tummy. He pressed his tiny paws against his fluffy gray belly and listened. "Grumble, grumble, grumble!" it said loudly.

"Oh my!" squeaked Nutkin. "I forgot to eat breakfast again!" He had been so busy playing chase with the butterflies that he hadn't taken care of himself.

Nutkin's nose twitched as he smelled something wonderful. Sweet, warm smells were floating from the big house nearby. His little black eyes grew wide with curiosity. "What could smell so yummy?" he wondered.

Being a very curious squirrel, Nutkin scampered toward the house. He climbed up the ivy and peeked through a window. Inside was the coziest kitchen! Sunlight streamed through yellow curtains, and steam rose from a pot on the stove. The whole room felt warm and safe.

But then Nutkin saw something that made his heart beat fast. A big orange cat was sleeping on a cushion near the stove!

"Oh no!" whispered Nutkin. "Cats chase squirrels! But I'm so hungry, and that kitchen smells like fresh bread and honey."

Nutkin's tummy grumbled again, even louder this time. He knew he needed to eat to take care of himself, but he was scared of the cat.

"Maybe I should just go find some acorns," he thought. But then he remembered what his mama always said: "Sometimes being brave means doing what's right, even when you're scared."

Nutkin took three deep breaths. "I can be brave," he told himself. "I'll be very quiet and careful."

Slowly, Nutkin squeezed through a crack in the window. The kitchen was even warmer inside! The wooden floor felt smooth under his paws, and the air smelled like cinnamon and vanilla.

He tiptoed past the sleeping cat, his fluffy tail twitching nervously. On the counter, he found exactly what his nose had promised – a plate of fresh bread with golden honey dripping down the sides.

"Just a tiny piece," whispered Nutkin. But as he reached for the bread, his paw knocked over a small spoon. CLANG!

The orange cat opened one green eye, then both eyes! "Well, hello there, little squirrel," purred the cat in a gentle voice.

Nutkin froze, his heart pounding. But the cat didn't chase him. Instead, she stretched and yawned.

"You look hungry," said the cat kindly. "I'm Marmalade. Don't be afraid – I only chase mice, not squirrels."

"I'm Nutkin," he squeaked. "I'm sorry I came into your kitchen. I was just so hungry, and I forgot to eat breakfast."

Marmalade smiled. "It's important to take care of yourself and eat when you're hungry. Would you like to share some bread with me?"

Nutkin couldn't believe it! His curiosity and courage had led him to a new friend.

They sat together in the sunny kitchen, sharing warm bread and honey. Nutkin learned that being brave doesn't mean not being scared – it means doing the right thing even when you are scared.

"Thank you for being so kind," said Nutkin. "Tomorrow I'll remember to eat breakfast, and maybe I can bring you some acorns to share!"

From that day on, Nutkin always took care of himself by eating regular meals, and he visited his new friend Marmalade every week. He had learned that courage and curiosity can lead to wonderful friendships.

The End.
