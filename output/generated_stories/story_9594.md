# Penny the Fawn Finds Her Voice

In a quiet attic above an old farmhouse lived a gentle deer fawn named Penny. She had soft brown fur with white spots like tiny stars. Her big dark eyes sparkled with kindness.

Penny loved the peaceful attic. Sunbeams danced through the dusty window. Old boxes and forgotten treasures filled every corner. The wooden floor creaked softly under her small hooves.

But today, Penny heard something that made her ears perk up. Tiny voices were crying from behind a big trunk.

"Help us! We're lost!" squeaked the voices.

Penny's heart beat faster. She felt scared, but she knew someone needed help. Taking a deep breath, she walked toward the sounds.

Behind the trunk, she found three baby mice. They were very small and very frightened. Their gray fur was dusty, and their whiskers trembled.

"We can't find our way home," cried the smallest mouse. "We've been lost all day!"

Penny felt sorry for the little mice. She wanted to help, but she had never been a leader before. What if she made a mistake?

"Don't worry," Penny said gently. "I'll help you find your family."

The mice looked up at her with hopeful eyes. "Really? You'll help us?"

"Yes," said Penny, feeling braver. "But we need to work together."

Penny thought carefully. She sniffed the air with her sensitive nose. She could smell different scents - old wood, dust, and something else.

"I smell cheese," Penny said. "That might lead us to your home!"

She led the mice slowly across the attic floor. Her hooves made soft tapping sounds. The mice followed close behind, their tiny paws pattering.

"This way," Penny said confidently. She felt proud of herself for being brave.

They walked past old picture frames and dusty books. The cheese smell grew stronger near a small hole in the wall.

"Our home!" squeaked the mice excitedly. "You found it!"

But there was a problem. The hole was up high, and the mice were too small to climb up.

Penny looked around the attic. She saw an old wooden stool. Working together, they pushed it near the wall. It was hard work, but Penny encouraged everyone.

"We can do this if we help each other," she said.

One by one, Penny helped the baby mice climb onto the stool. Then she gently lifted them with her nose to reach their home.

"Thank you, Penny!" called the mice. "You're the best leader ever!"

As the mice disappeared into their cozy hole, Penny felt warm and happy inside. She had been scared at first, but she chose to be brave. She had helped others and discovered something wonderful about herself.

"Being a leader means caring about others," Penny said to herself. "Even when you feel scared, you can still be brave and help."

The attic felt even more peaceful now. Penny had learned that leadership wasn't about being the biggest or strongest. It was about having a kind heart and the courage to help when others needed it.

From that day on, whenever anyone in the attic needed help, they knew they could count on Penny the Fawn. She had found her voice and discovered that being a leader felt wonderful.

The End.
