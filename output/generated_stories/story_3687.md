# Polly's Words Around the Room

Sunlight peeked through soft curtains in the comfortable nursery. The rug felt fluffy like a cloud. Blocks clicked, books smelled like fresh paper, and tiny hands clapped with joy. In the middle of a circle of toddlers sat Polly, a creative parrot with bright green feathers and a rainbow tail.

“Good morning!” Polly chirped, flapping gently. “My feathers feel tickly today!”

The children giggled. Mia tugged her sweater. “Polly, can we sing?”

“Yes,” Polly said, bobbing her head. “But first, let’s try some hello words. Words are like colors. They make our world beautiful!”

Polly puffed up with pride. “In English we say, ‘Hello!’ Can you say hello?”

“Hello!” the children shouted.

Polly tapped her beak on a drum. “In Spanish we say, ‘Hola!’”

“Hola!” the room echoed. The sound was warm and bright, like sunshine on toast.

“In Swahili we say, ‘Jambo!’” Polly chirped.

“Jambo!” The word bounced like a happy ball.

“In Mandarin we say, ‘Nǐ hǎo!’” Polly said slowly.

“Nee how!” the children tried. Their tongues tickled. They smiled and wiggled.

Polly fluffed her feathers. “Every language is a treasure. We can share and learn. It helps us be kind.”

Milo held up a blue block. “Polly, can you say block?”

“Yes! In English, ‘block.’ In French, ‘bloc.’ In Hindi, ‘ईंट,’ ‘eent,’” Polly said gently, making the sounds slow and simple. “Listen to the soft ee.”

Milo hugged the block. “Eent,” he whispered, proud.

On the other side, Noor lined up plush animals. “My lion is sleepy,” she said.

Polly blinked. “In Arabic, lion is ‘as-sad.’ It rumbles low,” Polly said, making a soft growl, “rrrr.” The children giggled at the silly rumble.

Then something wiggled inside Polly. She wanted to show more. She wanted everyone to listen. When some children started tapping the window, Polly felt a hot spark. “Wait! Listen to me!” she squawked a little too loud.

Timmy covered his ears. “Too loud,” he said, frowning.

Polly froze. The hot spark grew, like a tiny fire. She felt her chest puff and her wings twitch. She wanted to shout because she loved words so much.

Miss Hana, the teacher, knelt next to Polly. Her voice was calm like a soft blanket. “Polly, take a breath with me. In… and out.”

Polly breathed in. She smelled crayons, sweet soap, and orange slices. She breathed out. The tiny fire cooled. Her wings relaxed. “Thank you,” Polly whispered. “I felt mad. I wanted everyone to hear. I forgot to be gentle.”

Miss Hana smiled. “When we love something, we can still be kind.”

Polly nodded. She turned to the children. “I’m sorry for shouting. I will use a calm voice.”

“Okay,” said Timmy. He patted Polly’s wing. “We can take turns.”

Polly clacked her beak softly. “Let’s make a word rainbow. Everyone shares a word from any language. We will listen. We will respect.”

Mia raised her hand. “My grandma says ‘Cześć’ to say hi.” She tried the sound carefully. “Cheshch.”

Polly beamed. “Beautiful! Thank you, Mia. That word is special.”

Noor hugged her lion. “My baba says ‘Shukran’ to say thank you.”

“Shukran,” Polly repeated, warm and gentle. “Thank you, Noor.”

Milo held a toy car. “Vroom,” he said, then giggled. “In Japanese, my aunt says ‘kuruma’ for car.”

“Kuruma,” Polly echoed, feeling the round k sound. The word rolled like wheels.

Timmy whispered, “In American Sign Language, my mom says ‘thank you’ like this.” He opened his hand by his chin and moved it forward.

Polly watched with bright, happy eyes. “That is beautiful. Words can be hands too.”

The room felt cozy and full, like a bowl of warm soup. The children’s voices were soft and sweet. The words danced, swirled, and glowed. Polly’s heart felt like a drum, steady and glad.

“Now,” Polly said gently, “let’s put our words into a song.”

She tapped the drum: tap, tap, tap.

“Hello, hola, jambo, nǐ hǎo,
Cześć and shukran, let’s say it slow.
Bloc and eent, kuruma too,
Thank you in hands, I hear you!”

The children swayed. Miss Hana hummed. The curtains waved. The scent of orange slices made the air bright. The song felt like a hug.

When the song ended, Polly looked around. “What did we learn today?”

Mia raised her hand. “Words are treasures.”

Milo added, “We can share and listen.”

Noor said, “We can be kind to every language.”

Timmy nodded. “If we get mad, we breathe and use gentle voices.”

Polly smiled. “Yes. Every language is a way to love. When we listen, we show respect. When we learn, we see the big, big world. We can be calm and kind.”

Miss Hana clapped softly. “Snack time,” she said.

They passed orange slices. “Shukran,” Noor said.

“Gracias,” Mia said.

“Thank you,” Timmy said.

“Merci,” Milo added.

Polly tasted a tiny drop of juice on her beak. It was sweet and sunny. “Thank you, everyone,” she said. “You made a rainbow of words.”

They all smiled. The nursery felt safe and bright, and the word rainbow stayed in their hearts.

The End.
