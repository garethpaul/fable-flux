# Buzzy Beaver Builds a Better Basket

Buzzy was a busy beaver with bright teeth and a big heart. He liked to build. He liked to fix. He liked to help. Today, Buzzy padded into the laundry room with a squeak, squeak, squeak of his little wet feet.

The laundry room smelled warm and soapy. The washing machine hummed, hum, hum, hum, like a sleepy bee. The dryer spun, whirr, whirr, whirr, like a little wind. Piles of soft clothes sat on the floor like colorful clouds.

“Oh no,” said Buzzy. “The laundry basket broke!”

A big basket lay on its side. A crack ran down the edge. Socks peeked out and whispered, “Help!” A tiny button rolled across the tile, click, click.

“I can build a better basket,” said Buzzy. His tail thumped, thump, thump, with joy. “I will make it strong. I will make it safe. I will make it for everyone.”

Buzzy gathered things. He picked up clothespins that clicked, click, click. He found a soft scarf that felt like a hug. He found a strong belt that smelled like clean leather. He found a bright ribbon that shone like a rainbow.

His friends came to see. Dot the duck waddled in. “Quack, quack! I love bubbles,” Dot said, peeking at the soap. Tiko the tiny turtle slid slowly across the floor. “Hello,” Tiko said. “I like quiet places.” Zuzu the zebra zipped in with zigzag socks. “Let’s help!” Zuzu said. “I can hold things with my teeth.”

Buzzy smiled. “We will build together,” he said. “We are different, and that helps us in big ways.”

Buzzy began. “Clothespins make the frame,” he said. Click! He clipped them in a circle. “The scarf can line the inside,” he said. Swish! He smoothed the soft cloth. “The belt can be the handle,” he said. Snap! He buckled it tight.

“Can the ribbon be just for me?” Zuzu asked, tapping a hoof. “I like the rainbow.”

Buzzy shook his head kindly. “The ribbon is for everyone,” he said. “We share. That is fair.”

Dot dipped her bill in a cup of water and dripped drops onto the scarf. Drip, drop. “This makes it smooth,” she said. Tiko tugged a clothespin into place. Tug, tug. “This makes it even,” he said. Zuzu stretched the ribbon. “I can tie a bow,” she said, “for all of us.”

The washing machine sang, bing! The room smelled like fresh rain. Buzzy felt proud. He lifted the basket with both paws. Lift. Lift. But—oh no! The handle slipped. The belt was too loose. The basket wobbled and tumbled. Flop!

Socks popped out like popcorn. Pop! Pop! A little sock slid under the dryer. “Eep!” squeaked the sock.

Buzzy’s ears drooped. “I wanted it to be perfect,” he said softly. “I wanted it to be strong.”

Dot waddled close. “Quack, it’s okay,” she said. “We can try again.”

Zuzu nodded. “We keep going,” she said. “That is persistence.”

Tiko tapped his shell. Tap, tap. “We can slow down and look,” he said. “We can make it better.”

Buzzy took a deep breath. He felt the warm air from the dryer. He smelled the lemon soap. He listened to the soft hum. “You are right,” he said. “We do not give up.”

Buzzy went to the sock under the dryer. He could not reach. “I need help,” he said. “I am sorry I tried to do it too fast alone.” He looked at his friends. “I want to do what is right.”

“That is integrity,” Tiko said kindly. “You tell the truth and fix it.”

Dot splashed a little water to slide the sock out. Swish. Tiko used his small, steady legs to nudge the sock. Push. Zuzu used her teeth to pull. Tug. The sock came out with a happy pop! “We did it together,” Zuzu cheered.

Buzzy tightened the belt handle with a new loop. He asked, “Tiko, is this even?” Tiko peered closely. “Yes,” he said. “It is snug.” Buzzy asked, “Dot, is the scarf smooth?” Dot patted it with her soft wing. “Yes,” she said. “No bumps.” Buzzy asked, “Zuzu, can you tie the ribbon in a bow we can all hold?” Zuzu grinned. “Yes!” she said. She tied a big rainbow bow with two loops, one on each side.

Buzzy lifted the basket again. Lift. Strong. He set it down. Set. Steady. He put in shirts. He put in pants. He put in socks and soft towels. The basket held them all.

“Ta-da!” said Buzzy. His tail thumped with joy. “We made a basket for everyone.”

He looked at his friends. “We are all different,” he said. “Dot likes water. Tiko is slow and careful. Zuzu is fast and strong. I like to build. Our differences help us build better.”

Dot flapped. “We are a team,” she said. “Quack!”

Tiko smiled. “We listened,” he said. “We were patient.”

Zuzu nuzzled the ribbon. “We shared,” she said. “We cared.”

Buzzy hugged them with his paws. The laundry room felt warm and bright. The dryer hummed a happy tune. The clean clothes smelled like sunshine and soap.

Buzzy said, “Today I learned that being different is good. When we work together and do what is right, we make strong things. We keep trying. We say sorry when we need to. We share. We include everyone.”

They carried the basket together. Step, step, step. The bow bounced, boing, boing, boing, like a rainbow smile.

The End.
