# Story story_634

**The Shy Horse in the Closet**

Pepper was a small toy horse. She lived in Emma's bedroom closet. The closet was dark and cozy. Pepper liked it there most days.

But today was different. Pepper woke up feeling scared. The closet door was stuck tight! She pushed with her tiny hooves. The door would not open.

"Oh no," whispered Pepper. Her voice was very quiet. She always spoke softly. Pepper was the shyest toy in the whole house.

Outside the closet, she could hear Emma playing. Emma's laugh sounded happy and warm. Pepper wanted to join her friend. But the door stayed closed.

Pepper felt her heart beating fast. She needed help. But asking for help felt scary. What if Emma was too busy? What if she said no?

The little horse decided to try alone first. She backed up to the far wall. Then she ran forward as fast as she could. Her hooves made tiny clip-clop sounds on the closet floor.

THUMP! Pepper bumped into the door. It didn't budge. She rubbed her nose with her hoof. That hurt a little bit.

"Maybe I'm not strong enough," Pepper said sadly. She sat down in the corner. The closet felt smaller now. The darkness felt heavier.

Pepper heard Emma's footsteps in the hallway. They were getting closer! This was her chance. But her throat felt tight. The words wouldn't come out.

"I can't do it," Pepper whispered to herself. "I'm too shy." She curled up behind a pile of shoes. The shoes smelled like grass and sunshine from outside.

Hours passed slowly. Pepper grew more worried. What if she stayed trapped forever? What if Emma forgot about her?

Then Pepper remembered something important. Last week, Emma had told her a story. It was about a brave little mouse. The mouse was scared too. But she asked for help anyway. And everything turned out wonderful.

"Maybe I can be brave like that mouse," Pepper thought. Her voice was still shaky. But she felt a tiny spark of courage growing inside her chest.

Pepper took a deep breath. The air tasted dusty but familiar. She walked to the door again. This time, she wouldn't try to open it alone.

"Emma!" Pepper called out. Her voice was soft but clear. "Emma, can you help me please?"

She waited. Her heart was beating like a tiny drum.

Footsteps came running down the hall. "Pepper? Is that you?" Emma's voice sounded worried and kind.

"Yes! I'm stuck in the closet," Pepper said. Speaking up felt scary but good. Like jumping into a warm bath.

"Don't worry, Pepper. I'll get you out!" Emma said. Pepper heard her friend trying the door handle. It rattled and creaked.

"The door is really stuck," Emma said. "Let me get my dad to help."

Soon, Pepper heard two sets of footsteps. Emma's dad had tools that jingled and clanked. After a few minutes, the door opened with a loud CREAK!

Bright, golden sunlight poured into the closet. Pepper blinked her eyes. Emma's smiling face appeared in the doorway.

"There you are!" Emma said happily. She scooped Pepper up in a gentle hug. Pepper felt safe and warm in her friend's arms.

"I was so scared to ask for help," Pepper admitted. Her voice was getting stronger now.

"But you did ask," Emma said proudly. "That was very brave. Asking for help is always okay. I'm your friend. I want to help you."

Pepper felt her worries melting away like ice cream in summer. "Thank you for helping me," she said.

"Always," Emma promised. She carried Pepper to the sunny window. They spent the afternoon playing together in the warm light.

From that day on, Pepper felt a little braver. She learned that friends want to help each other. Speaking up might feel scary at first. But good things happen when you try.

And whenever Pepper felt shy again, she remembered the stuck closet door. She remembered how asking for help had set her free.

The end.
