# Story story_1457

**Daisy's Garden Adventure**

The morning sun painted the sky bright yellow. Farmer Mae rang the big brass bell. "Garden time!" she called cheerfully.

All the farm animals loved garden time. The chickens clucked with excitement. The pigs oinked happily. But Daisy the cow just stood still.

"I don't want to go," Daisy said softly. Her black and white spots seemed to droop. "Gardens are boring."

Farmer Mae walked over to Daisy. She patted her warm, fuzzy head. "Come on, sweet girl. Just try today."

Daisy's hooves felt heavy as rocks. She walked very slowly to the garden. The other animals were already busy. The chickens pecked at tiny bugs. The pigs rolled in the soft dirt.

"What should I do?" Daisy wondered aloud. She looked at the rows of green plants. Nothing seemed interesting at all.

Farmer Mae handed Daisy a big watering can. "Help me water the vegetables, please." The can was bright red and shiny.

Daisy took the handle in her mouth. The water inside made sloshing sounds. She walked to the first row of plants. Green tomatoes hung like little balls.

As Daisy poured the water, something amazing happened. The plants seemed to dance! Their leaves wiggled in the gentle breeze. Tiny water drops sparkled like diamonds in the sunshine.

"Oh my!" gasped Daisy. "They're so pretty!"

A small butterfly landed on a yellow flower. Its wings were orange and black. Daisy had never seen anything so beautiful. She stood very still and watched.

The butterfly fluttered to another flower. Then another. Daisy followed it with her big brown eyes. Soon she was walking all around the garden.

"Look at the carrots!" Daisy called to her friends. Orange tops peeked out from the dark soil. "They look like tiny sunsets!"

A fat bumblebee buzzed past Daisy's ear. It landed on a purple eggplant flower. The bee was fuzzy and round. It made a happy humming sound.

Daisy giggled. The sound was like tiny bells ringing. She had never noticed how many friends lived in the garden.

Farmer Mae showed Daisy the strawberry patch. Red berries hid under green leaves. "These smell so sweet," said Farmer Mae. She picked one for Daisy to try.

The strawberry was juicy and delicious. It tasted like sunshine and summer rain. "Mmmm!" said Daisy. "This is the best thing ever!"

Next, they visited the herb garden. Farmer Mae let Daisy smell the mint leaves. They smelled cool and fresh. The basil smelled like pizza. The lavender smelled like Grandma's soap.

Daisy's nose twitched with each new smell. "The garden is full of surprises!" she said happily.

A tiny green frog hopped onto a lily pad. Splash! It jumped into the small garden pond. Daisy laughed and clapped her hooves together.

"I want to help plant seeds too!" Daisy said. Farmer Mae gave her a packet of sunflower seeds. They were big and black and white.

Daisy used her hoof to make small holes. She dropped in the seeds very carefully. "Grow big and tall," she whispered to each one.

The warm dirt felt good under her hooves. The sun felt nice on her back. Birds sang pretty songs in the nearby trees.

Soon it was time to go back to the barn. Daisy didn't want to leave! "Can we stay longer?" she asked.

"We'll come back tomorrow," promised Farmer Mae. "The garden will be here waiting for us."

That night, Daisy dreamed about dancing plants and colorful butterflies. She dreamed about sweet strawberries and friendly frogs.

When morning came, Daisy woke up early. She looked toward the garden and smiled. The sun was just peeking over the hills.

"I can't wait for garden time!" Daisy said to herself. She had learned something wonderful. Gardens weren't boring at all. They were full of magic and beauty and friends.

Farmer Mae rang the brass bell. "Garden time!" she called.

This time, Daisy ran to the garden as fast as she could go.

The end.
