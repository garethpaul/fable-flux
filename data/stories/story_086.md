# Penny Puppet's Park Day

Penny Puppet sat on her toy shelf. She watched the morning sun peek through the window. Today was park day! But Penny felt worried.

"I don't want to go," Penny whispered. Her soft fabric arms hung down. "The park is so big. There are so many new things."

Mama picked up Penny gently. "Come on, little one. Park time is fun time!" But Penny's button eyes looked sad.

At the park, Mama set Penny on a bench. Children ran everywhere. Swings creaked back and forth. Dogs barked happily. Penny felt very small.

"Too loud," Penny mumbled. She pulled her little hat down. "Too busy."

A red ball rolled near Penny's feet. She looked at it carefully. The ball was bright and round. It sparkled in the sunshine.

"Hello there," said a gentle voice. A little girl sat next to Penny. "I'm Lucy. What's your name?"

"I'm Penny," the puppet said quietly. "I don't like the park much."

Lucy smiled. "Why not? There's so much to see!" She pointed around. "Look at those fluffy clouds. See the dancing leaves?"

Penny looked up slowly. The clouds did look fluffy. Like cotton candy! The leaves danced and twirled. They made soft whooshing sounds.

"Want to explore with me?" Lucy asked. She picked up the red ball. "This ball is magic. It shows us fun things!"

Penny felt curious now. "Magic? Really?"

Lucy rolled the ball toward the sandbox. It sparkled as it moved. "Let's follow it!"

In the sandbox, the ball stopped. Penny saw children building castles. Their hands moved sand into tall towers. The sand felt warm and soft.

"We could build too," Lucy said. She helped Penny sit in the sand. Together they made a tiny castle. Penny used her small hands to pat the sand.

"This is nice," Penny said. Her voice sounded happier.

The magic ball rolled toward the duck pond. Penny and Lucy followed it again. At the pond, ducks swam in circles. They quacked softly. The water rippled and shone.

"The ducks are saying hello," Lucy laughed. She waved at them. Penny waved too with her fabric hand.

One little duckling swam close. It looked right at Penny. The duckling quacked once, very gently. Penny giggled for the first time.

"He likes you!" Lucy said.

The ball rolled once more. This time to the flower garden. Penny gasped. Flowers bloomed everywhere! Red roses, yellow daisies, purple violets. Bees buzzed from flower to flower.

"So pretty," Penny whispered. She touched a soft petal. It felt like silk.

A butterfly landed on Penny's hat. Its wings were orange and black. It stayed there for a moment. Then it fluttered away gracefully.

"The butterfly chose you," Lucy said. "That's very special."

Penny felt warm inside. Her button eyes sparkled. The park wasn't scary anymore. It was wonderful!

As the sun moved across the sky, Penny played more. She went down the small slide. She swung gently on the baby swing. She collected pretty leaves.

Everything felt magical now. The sounds weren't too loud. They were like music! The busy children weren't scary. They were having fun!

When Mama came to get her, Penny felt sad to leave. "Can we stay longer?" she asked.

"We'll come back tomorrow," Mama promised. She picked up Penny carefully.

As they walked home, Penny waved goodbye. She waved to the ducks. She waved to the flowers. She waved to her new friend Lucy.

"Thank you for showing me the magic," Penny called out.

That night, Penny sat on her shelf again. But now she felt different. She felt excited! Tomorrow would bring another park day.

"I can't wait," Penny said to her teddy bear friend. "The park is full of wonders. I want to see them all!"

She closed her button eyes happily. In her dreams, she danced with butterflies. She built sand castles with friends. She discovered new magic everywhere.

Park time was the best time! And tomorrow couldn't come fast enough.

The end.
