# Story story_834

Benny was a fluffy brown dog. He lived near a sunny meadow. Every day he played there alone.

The meadow had soft green grass. Pretty flowers grew everywhere. Benny could smell their sweet scent. But playing by himself made him sad.

"I wish I had a friend," Benny said. He threw his stick up high. It came down with a thump. No one was there to catch it.

Near the old oak tree sat Ruby. She was a small cloth doll. Her red dress was faded but clean. Her button eyes watched Benny play.

Ruby wanted to join him so badly. But she was just a doll. Could she really play with a real dog?

Benny picked up his stick again. He tossed it toward the tree. The stick landed right next to Ruby!

"Oh!" said Ruby softly. The stick had knocked her over. Now she lay on her side.

Benny ran over to get his stick. He saw the little doll there. Her kind button eyes looked up at him.

"Hello," Benny said gently. "I'm sorry I knocked you down."

Ruby felt so happy. The dog was talking to her! "That's okay," she whispered. "I'm Ruby."

"I'm Benny," he said. His tail wagged slowly. "Do you live here in the meadow?"

"Yes," Ruby said. "I watch you play every day. It looks like so much fun."

Benny's ears perked up. "You want to play with me?"

Ruby nodded her soft head. "More than anything!"

Benny gently picked up Ruby in his mouth. He was very careful with his teeth. He set her down on a smooth rock.

"What should we play?" Benny asked.

Ruby thought hard. "Can we play hide and seek?"

"Yes!" Benny barked happily. "You hide first!"

Ruby looked around the meadow. She saw tall grass and colorful flowers. Behind a big yellow sunflower looked perfect.

"Count to ten," Ruby called out. She hurried to her hiding spot. The sunflower's big petals hid her well.

Benny closed his eyes tight. "One, two, three..." He counted slowly. "Ten! Ready or not!"

Benny sniffed around the meadow. His nose twitched left and right. He could smell Ruby's cotton stuffing. It smelled clean and warm.

"Found you!" Benny said. He peeked behind the sunflower. Ruby giggled with joy.

"Your turn to hide," Ruby said.

Benny was much bigger than Ruby. Where could a dog hide? He spotted some tall purple flowers. He crouched down low behind them.

Ruby looked everywhere. She checked behind rocks and trees. Finally she saw a brown tail sticking out.

"I found you, Benny!" she laughed.

They played tag next. Benny would run in big circles. Ruby would tumble through the soft grass. They chased butterflies together. The butterflies were orange and black.

When they got tired, they rested. Benny lay down in the warm sunshine. Ruby sat on his soft back. She could feel his gentle breathing.

"This is the best day ever," Ruby said.

"I agree," Benny said. "I was so lonely before."

"Me too," Ruby admitted. "I watched you every day. I wished we could be friends."

A gentle breeze blew through the meadow. It made the flowers dance. Ruby's red dress fluttered in the wind.

"We are friends now," Benny said. "Best friends forever."

Ruby hugged Benny's warm fur. "Forever and always."

From that day on, they played together. Every morning Benny would find Ruby. They would run and laugh in their special meadow.

The other animals noticed their friendship. The rabbits smiled when they saw them. The birds sang happy songs above them.

Ruby learned that being different didn't matter. Benny learned that friends come in all shapes. Together they were never lonely again.

As the sun set each day, they would sit together. The sky turned pink and orange. Stars began to twinkle above them.

"Thank you for being my friend," Ruby would say.

"Thank you for playing with me," Benny would answer.

Their meadow was filled with joy and laughter. Two very different friends had found each other. And that made everything perfect.

The end.
