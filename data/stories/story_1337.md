# Story story_1337

**Grandpa's Magic Bedtime**

The sun was setting behind the trees. Orange and pink colors painted the sky. Little Emma yawned and rubbed her sleepy eyes.

"Time for bed, sweetheart," called Mama from the kitchen.

But tonight was special. Grandpa Joe was staying over. He sat in his favorite blue chair. His gray mustache wiggled when he smiled.

"I don't want bedtime yet," Emma said softly. She looked at her grandpa with sad eyes. "Can we stay up longer?"

Grandpa's shoulders drooped a little. He felt tired too. His old bones ached from the long day. Playing in the garden had worn him out.

"Well," Grandpa said slowly. "Bedtime does sound nice."

Emma's bottom lip stuck out. "But bedtime is boring, Grandpa."

Grandpa looked at his granddaughter's pouty face. Then something twinkled in his kind brown eyes. He had an idea.

"What if bedtime could be magical?" he asked.

Emma tilted her head. "Magical how?"

Grandpa stood up with a little groan. "Follow me, little one."

They walked to Emma's bedroom together. Her pink walls were covered with butterfly stickers. Her stuffed animals sat on the bed.

"First," said Grandpa, "we need the magic pajamas."

He pulled out Emma's favorite pajamas. They were soft and purple with tiny stars. "These aren't just any pajamas," he whispered. "These are dream pajamas."

Emma giggled. "They look the same, Grandpa."

"Ah, but they feel different," he said. "Put them on and see."

Emma changed into her pajamas. They felt extra cozy tonight. The soft fabric hugged her like a warm hug.

"Now for the magic toothbrush," Grandpa announced.

In the bathroom, he made brushing teeth fun. "This toothbrush fights sugar bugs," he said in a silly voice. "Brush them away! Swoosh, swoosh!"

Emma laughed and brushed extra well. Her teeth felt squeaky clean.

Back in her room, Grandpa sat on the bed. "Time for the most magical part," he said.

"What's that?" Emma asked, climbing under her covers.

"The bedtime story spell," Grandpa whispered.

He opened an old book. The pages were yellow and soft. "This book has been in our family for years," he said. "It holds sleepy magic."

Grandpa began to read. His voice was warm like honey. He told stories about brave little mice. He talked about friendly dragons who loved to nap.

Emma's eyelids grew heavy. But she didn't feel sad anymore. She felt cozy and loved.

"Grandpa," she mumbled sleepily. "This is the best bedtime ever."

Grandpa smiled and patted her head. Her hair smelled like strawberry shampoo. "Sweet dreams, little star," he whispered.

He kissed her forehead gently. Then he tucked her blanket up to her chin. Emma's room felt warm and safe.

"Will you do magic bedtime tomorrow too?" Emma asked.

"Every night I'm here," Grandpa promised.

Emma closed her eyes with a big smile. She dreamed of flying with friendly dragons. She dreamed of dancing with mice in tiny tutus.

The next morning, Emma woke up excited. She ran to find Grandpa in the kitchen. He was making pancakes that smelled like vanilla.

"Grandpa! Grandpa!" she called. "Last night was amazing!"

Grandpa flipped a golden pancake. "Did you have good dreams?"

"The best dreams ever," Emma said. "Can we have magic bedtime again tonight?"

Grandpa's eyes twinkled. "Of course, sweetheart."

Emma hugged his legs tight. She learned something wonderful. Bedtime wasn't boring at all. With someone you love, bedtime could be the most magical time of day.

That evening, Emma couldn't wait for bedtime. She put on her dream pajamas early. She brushed her teeth extra well. She picked out three books for Grandpa to read.

"Ready for more magic?" Grandpa asked.

"Always!" Emma giggled.

And from that night on, bedtime became Emma's favorite time. Because with Grandpa's love and imagination, even sleepy time could be an adventure.

The end.
