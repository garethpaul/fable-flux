# Story story_794

**Splish Splash Building Fun**

Emma loved her colorful blocks. She had red ones and blue ones. Yellow ones and green ones too. Every day she built tall towers. She made castles and bridges.

"Bath time, Emma!" called Mama.

Emma looked at her blocks sadly. She was building the best castle ever. The towers were so tall. The walls were so strong.

"Do I have to?" Emma asked.

"Yes, sweetie. Time to get clean," Mama said gently.

Emma walked slowly to the bathroom. The warm water filled the tub. Bubbles danced on top. But Emma still felt sad about leaving her blocks.

She climbed into the warm water. It felt nice on her skin. The bubbles tickled her arms.

"I wish I had my blocks," Emma sighed.

Mama smiled. "Wait right here," she said.

Mama left the bathroom. Emma heard her footsteps in the hallway. What was Mama doing?

Soon Mama came back. She carried a special basket. Inside were blocks Emma had never seen before.

"Bath blocks!" Mama said happily.

Emma's eyes grew wide. These blocks were different. They were soft and squishy. They had holes in them. Water could go through the holes.

"They float!" Emma gasped.

She picked up a red block. Water dripped through the tiny holes. It looked like rain. The drops made soft splashing sounds.

Emma stacked two blocks together. Water flowed from the top block to the bottom one. Then it splashed into the tub.

"Look, Mama! A waterfall!"

Emma built a tower in the water. The blocks stuck together when they were wet. Her tower wobbled and swayed. But it didn't fall down.

She made a boat with three blocks. It floated around the tub. Emma pushed it gently with her finger. The boat sailed through the bubbles.

"Chug, chug, chug," Emma said. She was the boat captain now.

Next, Emma tried something new. She held a block up high. Water poured out like a shower. It felt tickly on her head.

"Rain, rain, go away," Emma sang. But she didn't want the rain to go away. This rain was fun!

Emma discovered more magic. When she squeezed the blocks, water squirted out. She made fountains and streams. She made waterfalls and rivers.

The yellow block became a duck. Emma made it swim and dive. The blue block became a fish. It swam under the bubbles.

"Glub, glub, glub," said the fish block.

Emma built a castle in the water. The walls were wobbly but wonderful. Water flowed through every room. It was the most amazing castle ever.

"Look, Mama! A water castle!"

Mama clapped her hands. "It's beautiful, Emma!"

Emma played until her fingers got wrinkly. She made boats and towers. She made bridges and houses. Every creation was special.

The warm water felt so good. The blocks were so much fun. Emma didn't want bath time to end.

"Time to get out, sweetie," Mama said softly.

Emma looked at her bath blocks. "Can we do this again tomorrow?"

"Of course," Mama promised.

Emma climbed out of the tub. Mama wrapped her in a fluffy towel. It was warm and soft. Emma felt clean and happy.

"Thank you for the bath blocks, Mama."

"You're welcome, sweetie. I'm glad you had fun."

Emma brushed her teeth. She put on her cozy pajamas. But she kept thinking about tomorrow's bath.

She could hardly wait to play again. Maybe she would build a boat big enough for all the blocks. Maybe she would make a water city.

Emma climbed into bed. Mama tucked her in tight. She kissed Emma's forehead.

"Sweet dreams, my little builder," Mama whispered.

Emma smiled sleepily. Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough. Bath time wasn't scary anymore. It was the best time of day.

She closed her eyes and dreamed of floating castles. She dreamed of water slides and bubble boats. In her dreams, bath time lasted forever.

And every day was a new building adventure.

The end.
