# Story story_697

**Benny Boat's Lost Toy Car**

Benny was a bright blue toy boat. He lived in the pretty park pond. Every morning, children came to play with him.

Benny loved floating on the warm water. The sun made little sparkles dance around him. But his favorite thing was his tiny red toy car.

The red car was very special. Benny kept it safe on his deck. He loved how it rolled back and forth as he bobbed in the water.

One sunny morning, Benny woke up early. Birds were singing sweet songs. The water felt nice and cool.

But something was wrong. Very wrong.

"Where is my red car?" Benny said out loud.

He looked left. He looked right. He looked all around his deck. The little red car was gone!

Benny's heart felt heavy and sad. His deck looked so empty without the bright red car. Where could it be?

"Maybe it fell in the water," Benny thought.

He decided to look for it right away. Benny took a deep breath. Then he dove down under the water.

The pond was darker down below. Green plants swayed back and forth. Little fish swam by with silver scales that sparkled.

Benny searched everywhere. He looked behind rocks. He looked under lily pads. He even asked a friendly frog.

"Have you seen my red car?" Benny asked.

"Ribbit! No, I haven't," said the frog. "But keep looking!"

Benny searched and searched. His eyes got tired from looking. But he could not find his red car anywhere underwater.

Feeling sad, Benny floated back up. The warm sunshine felt good on his blue paint. But he still felt worried about his lost car.

"What if I never find it?" Benny thought.

Just then, he heard children laughing. Three kids were running toward the pond. They had big smiles and sandy hands.

"Look! There's Benny!" said a girl with curly hair.

"Let's play with him!" said a boy in a yellow shirt.

The children waded into the shallow water. They picked up Benny gently. He felt their warm hands holding him.

"Wait," said the third child. "What's that red thing over there?"

Benny's heart jumped with hope! He looked where the child was pointing.

There, sitting on a big gray rock near the shore, was his little red car! It was safe and sound. The morning sun made it shine like a tiny fire truck.

"That must belong to the boat!" said the curly-haired girl.

She waded over to the rock. Carefully, she picked up the small red car. Then she placed it gently on Benny's deck.

"There you go, little boat," she said softly.

Benny felt so happy! His red car was back where it belonged. It must have rolled off during the night when the wind was blowing.

The children played with Benny for a long time. They made gentle waves with their hands. They watched the red car roll back and forth on his deck.

"The boat looks so happy now," said the boy in yellow.

Benny really was happy. He had learned something important today. Sometimes when you lose something special, you have to keep trying different ways to find it.

Looking underwater didn't work. But asking for help did work! The children had eyes that could see places Benny couldn't reach.

As the sun got higher in the sky, the children said goodbye. They placed Benny back in his favorite spot on the pond.

"See you tomorrow, Benny!" they called.

Benny floated peacefully on the gentle water. His red car sat safely on his deck. The little car caught the sunlight and gleamed like a ruby.

"Tomorrow I'll be more careful," Benny promised himself. "But if I lose something again, I won't give up. I'll keep trying until I find it!"

A gentle breeze made tiny waves on the water. Benny bobbed happily up and down. His red car rolled softly back and forth.

Everything was perfect in the peaceful park pond.

The end.
