# Story story_572

**Benny's Big Cleanup Adventure**

Benny the little blue airplane sat in the corner of the playroom. His propeller drooped down low. Toys were scattered everywhere around him.

"Cleanup time!" called Miss Sarah.

All the children started putting toys away. Blocks went into bins. Books went on shelves. But Benny stayed very still.

"I don't want to be put away," Benny whispered. "It's dark in the toy box."

His friend Emma picked him up gently. "Come on, Benny. Time to go home."

Benny's wings felt heavy. He made a sad humming sound. "Hmmmm."

Emma heard his little hum. She looked at Benny's droopy propeller. "Are you sad, little plane?"

Benny nodded his nose up and down. Just a tiny bit.

"I have an idea," Emma said softly. "What if cleanup time was flying time?"

She held Benny up high. "Zoom! You can fly around and help!"

Suddenly, Benny felt lighter. His propeller started to spin. "Vroom, vroom!"

"Look!" Emma giggled. "Benny wants to help clean up!"

Emma made Benny swoop down low. He picked up a red crayon in his landing gear. "Whoosh!" They flew to the art table.

Benny dropped the crayon in the right cup. "Perfect landing!" Emma cheered.

Other children watched with big smiles. "Can Benny help us too?" asked Jake.

"Of course!" Emma said. "Pilot Benny is ready for action!"

Benny zoomed over to Jake. His propeller spun faster and faster. "Whirrrr!"

Jake giggled and loaded a puzzle piece onto Benny's wing. They flew in loops around the room. "Banking left!" Jake announced.

Benny felt the wind rushing past his wings. This was the best feeling ever! His engine hummed happily. "Purrrr."

They landed at the puzzle table. The piece fit perfectly. "Mission complete!" Jake said proudly.

More children wanted Benny's help. He carried blocks, crayons, and toy cars. Each trip felt like a real adventure.

"Emergency delivery!" called Mia. She loaded three bouncy balls onto Benny's back.

Benny flew in zigzags across the room. The balls bounced gently as they soared. "Boing, boing, boing!"

They landed safely at the ball bin. All three balls rolled in perfectly. "Another successful flight!" Mia clapped.

Soon, Benny had helped clean up everything. The room looked neat and tidy. All the toys were in their special places.

But Benny's favorite part was still coming. Emma carried him toward his home.

"Wait," she said. "Your hangar needs to be special too."

Emma lined Benny's box with a soft blue blanket. She drew clouds on the sides with white chalk. She even made a little runway with tape.

"Now it looks like the sky!" Emma said.

Benny's propeller spun with joy. His box wasn't dark anymore. It looked like flying through fluffy white clouds.

"Thank you, Emma," Benny hummed softly.

Emma tucked him in gently. "Sweet dreams, Pilot Benny. Tomorrow we'll have more cleanup adventures."

Benny closed his eyes and smiled. His wings felt warm and cozy. The soft blanket smelled like lavender soap.

He thought about all the fun they had. Zooming around the room. Helping his friends. Making cleanup time into flying time.

"Tomorrow," Benny whispered to himself, "I can't wait for cleanup time!"

His propeller gave one last happy spin. Then he drifted off to sleep.

In his dreams, Benny soared through cotton candy clouds. He delivered toys to children all around the world. Every landing was perfect. Every takeoff was smooth.

When morning came, Benny woke up excited. His propeller was already spinning. "Vroom, vroom!" he hummed cheerfully.

He couldn't wait to help clean up again. Because now he knew the secret. Cleanup time was really flying time in disguise.

And flying time was the very best time of all.

The end.
