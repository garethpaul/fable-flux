# Woolly's New Friend

Woolly the sheep sat in the long school hallway. Her fluffy white wool bounced as she rolled a red ball back and forth. The ball made a soft *thump, thump* sound on the shiny floor.

"This is fun," Woolly said quietly. But her voice echoed in the empty hallway. She wished someone would play with her.

Woolly pushed the ball harder. It rolled faster down the hallway. She chased after it, her hooves making gentle *clip-clop* sounds. When she caught the ball, she felt a little sad. Playing alone wasn't as exciting as she hoped.

Just then, Ben walked around the corner. He was carrying his lunch box and humming a happy tune. Ben saw Woolly sitting by herself with the red ball.

"Hi there!" Ben said with a big smile. "I'm Ben. What's your name?"

"I'm Woolly," she replied, looking up hopefully. "I'm playing ball, but it's kind of lonely."

Ben's eyes lit up. "Can I play too? I love ball games!"

Woolly's tail wagged with excitement. "Yes, please! That would be wonderful!"

Ben set down his lunch box carefully. "What game should we play?"

"We could roll the ball to each other," Woolly suggested.

"Great idea!" Ben said. He sat down across from Woolly in the hallway.

Woolly gently pushed the red ball toward Ben. It rolled smoothly across the floor. Ben caught it and rolled it back to her. The ball made happy bouncing sounds as it traveled between them.

"This is much more fun!" Woolly giggled. Her wool seemed fluffier and brighter.

"I think so too," Ben agreed. "Hey, want to try something new?"

Ben stood up and held the ball high. "What if we bounce it and catch it together?"

Woolly jumped up excitedly. "I've never tried that before!"

Ben tossed the ball gently in the air. Woolly watched it carefully. When it came down, she caught it in her soft, woolly arms.

"You did it!" Ben cheered. "That was amazing!"

Woolly felt proud and happy. She tossed the ball to Ben. He caught it with both hands.

"Your turn was perfect too!" Woolly said, clapping her hooves together.

They took turns bouncing and catching the ball. Sometimes they missed, but they just laughed and tried again. The hallway filled with their giggles and the *bounce, bounce* of the red ball.

"I have an idea," Ben said. "What if we count how many times we can pass the ball without dropping it?"

"Yes! I love counting!" Woolly said, her eyes sparkling.

Ben rolled the ball to Woolly. "One!" they said together.

Woolly rolled it back. "Two!" they counted.

Back and forth the ball went. "Three! Four! Five!" Their voices grew more excited with each number.

When they reached ten, they both cheered loudly. "We did it! Ten passes!"

"Let's try for twenty!" Woolly suggested, bouncing on her hooves.

"You're such a good teammate," Ben said warmly.

They played their counting game again and again. Each time, they got better at working together. When one of them missed the ball, the other would say, "Good try! Let's go again!"

Soon, other children walked by the hallway. They saw Ben and Woolly having so much fun together.

"Can we play too?" asked a little girl.

"Of course!" Ben and Woolly said at the same time. They looked at each other and laughed.

As more friends joined their game, Woolly felt her heart grow warm and full. She wasn't lonely anymore. She had found a wonderful friend in Ben.

"Thank you for playing with me," Woolly whispered to Ben.

"Thank you for sharing your ball," Ben whispered back. "Friends make everything better!"

They hugged, and Woolly's soft wool tickled Ben's arms. Both children giggled happily.

From that day on, Ben and Woolly played together every day. They learned that cooperation made every game more exciting, more fun, and more special.

The red ball became their friendship ball, and the hallway became their favorite place to play together.

The end.
