# Story story_983

**Kite's Musical Day**

The big clock on the wall said three o'clock. Miss Sarah clapped her hands together. "Music time, everyone!" she called out cheerfully.

All the children ran to the colorful carpet. They sat in a big circle. Tommy grabbed the shiny tambourine. Lucy picked up the wooden blocks. Maya chose the jingling bells.

But Kite stayed by the window. She watched the real kites dancing in the sky outside. Their long tails swished in the wind. She wished she could fly with them instead.

"Come join us, Kite," Miss Sarah said gently. "We're going to make beautiful music together."

Kite shook her head. "I don't want to," she whispered. "Music is too loud. I don't know the songs."

The other children started singing. Their voices filled the room like warm sunshine. But Kite felt scared. What if she sang the wrong words? What if her voice sounded funny?

Miss Sarah sat down next to Kite. "Would you like to just listen today?" she asked softly.

Kite nodded. She curled up small like a little ball.

The children began with a song about raindrops. They tapped their instruments gently. Tap, tap, tap went the drums. Ting, ting, ting went the triangle. The sounds were soft and peaceful.

Kite's ears perked up. The music sounded like real raindrops on her bedroom window. She loved that sound when she was cozy in bed.

Next came a song about birds flying high. The children moved their arms like wings. They made their voices go up and down. Up and down, just like birds soaring through clouds.

Kite watched their arms move. It looked like the kites outside her window. Her own arms started to move just a tiny bit.

Then something magical happened. Miss Sarah started a new song. It was about kites dancing in the wind!

"Red kites, blue kites, flying so free," sang the children. "Dancing and spinning for you and me!"

Kite's eyes grew big and round. This song was about her! Well, about kites like her name.

"Yellow kites, green kites, up in the sky," the children continued. "Twirling and swirling way up high!"

Kite couldn't help herself. Her arms began to sway. Left and right, just like kites in the breeze. Her feet started to move too. She felt light and bouncy.

"Purple kites, orange kites, what a sight to see," everyone sang together. "Come and dance along with me!"

Before she knew it, Kite was standing up. She was moving her whole body like a kite in the wind. Her arms were the kite's wings. Her body swayed back and forth.

The other children smiled at her. "Look at Kite dancing!" Tommy said happily.

Kite felt warm and happy inside. The music wasn't scary anymore. It was fun! It made her feel like she really was flying.

Miss Sarah handed Kite some colorful ribbons. "These can be your kite tails," she said with a kind smile.

Kite held the ribbons high. She spun around slowly. The ribbons fluttered and danced behind her. Red, blue, and yellow ribbons flowing like real kite tails.

All the children danced together. They were all different kites in the sky. Some spun fast. Some moved slow. Some jumped high. Some stayed low.

The music made Kite feel brave and strong. She wasn't scared of her voice anymore. She started to hum along quietly. Then she sang a few words. Her voice was sweet and clear.

When music time ended, Kite felt sad it was over. "Can we do the kite song again tomorrow?" she asked Miss Sarah.

"Of course we can," Miss Sarah replied. "We can sing it every day if you want."

Kite hugged her colorful ribbons tight. She looked out the window at the real kites flying outside. Now she understood. She could fly too, right here in the classroom.

That night, Kite went to sleep humming the kite song. She dreamed of dancing with ribbons and singing with friends.

Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough. She was ready for music time!

The end.
