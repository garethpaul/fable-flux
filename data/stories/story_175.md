# Freddy Frog's Missing Blocks

Freddy Frog loved the playground. He hopped there every sunny morning. His big green feet made soft *thump-thump* sounds on the warm sand.

Today, Freddy carried his special bag. Inside were his favorite wooden blocks. Red ones, blue ones, and yellow ones too! He loved building tall towers. He loved making tiny houses. The blocks felt smooth in his webbed hands.

"Time to build!" Freddy said happily.

He found his favorite spot. It was under the big oak tree. The leaves made dancing shadows. Freddy opened his bag wide.

But wait! Something was wrong.

"Oh no!" Freddy croaked. "Where are my blocks?"

The bag was empty! Freddy's heart felt sad. His blocks were gone! He looked inside again. Still nothing.

Freddy's eyes filled with tears. "I can't build without my blocks," he whispered.

Then Freddy remembered something. Mama Frog always said, "When you lose something, look carefully. Don't give up!"

Freddy wiped his eyes. "I won't give up!" he said.

First, Freddy looked under the slide. The metal felt cool and smooth. He crawled on his belly. No blocks there. Just some acorns and leaves.

Next, he searched by the swings. The chains made soft *clink-clink* sounds. Freddy looked in the mulch. His hands got dirty. Still no blocks.

"Hmm," said Freddy. "Where else?"

He hopped to the sandbox. The sand felt warm between his toes. Freddy dug and dug. Sand flew everywhere! His green skin got dusty. But no blocks appeared.

Freddy sat down. He felt tired. His shoulders drooped low.

Just then, Bella Bunny hopped over. "Hi, Freddy! You look sad."

"I lost my blocks," Freddy sighed. "I looked everywhere!"

"Maybe I can help," said Bella kindly. "Four eyes are better than two!"

Bella's nose twitched. She sniffed around. "Tell me about yesterday," she said.

Freddy thought hard. "Yesterday... I built a castle. Right here by the tree!"

"Then what?" asked Bella.

"Then it rained!" Freddy remembered. "Big, heavy drops! I ran home fast!"

Bella's ears perked up. "You ran fast in the rain?"

"Yes! I was getting soaked!"

Bella smiled. "I think I know something. Come with me!"

She led Freddy to the lost-and-found box. It sat near the playground gate. The box was big and blue.

"Look inside," Bella said gently.

Freddy lifted the lid. His heart jumped! There were his blocks! All red, blue, and yellow. They sparkled clean and bright.

"My blocks!" Freddy croaked with joy. "They're here!"

Mrs. Robin, who watched the playground, flew down. "Oh yes! I found those blocks yesterday. They were getting wet in the rain. I put them somewhere safe."

Freddy's face lit up. "Thank you! Thank you so much!"

He hugged his blocks tight. They felt wonderful in his arms.

"You didn't give up," said Bella proudly. "You kept looking!"

"And you asked for help," added Mrs. Robin. "That was very smart."

Freddy felt warm and happy inside. He had learned something important. When you lose something, don't stop trying. Ask friends to help. Look in new places.

Soon, Freddy was building again. *Click-click* went the blocks. He made a tall, tall tower. Red on bottom, blue in middle, yellow on top!

"Look!" he called to Bella. "It's a rainbow tower!"

Bella clapped her paws. "Beautiful!"

Other playground friends came to see. They all helped build. Together, they made the biggest block city ever!

The sun felt warm on Freddy's back. His heart felt even warmer. He had his blocks back. He had good friends too.

"I'm so happy," Freddy said, grinning wide.

Sometimes things get lost. But if you don't give up, if you ask for help, and if you keep trying, good things happen.

Freddy learned that problems have solutions. You just have to be brave enough to find them.
