# Lily's Garden Magic

Lily heard Mama calling from the kitchen. "Garden time, sweet pea!" But Lily didn't want to go outside today. She was building a tall tower. Her blocks were almost touching the ceiling!

"Do I have to?" Lily asked. The garden seemed boring today. Just dirt and plants. Nothing exciting ever happened there.

Mama smiled and took Lily's hand. "Come on, little gardener. Let's see what's growing."

Lily dragged her feet. Step by step. The morning sun felt warm. But she still wanted to play inside.

In the garden, Mama handed Lily the small watering can. It was bright yellow like sunshine. "The plants are thirsty," Mama said softly.

Lily sighed. She poured water on the tomatoes. Drip, drip, drip. Then something caught her eye. A tiny green sprout! Right where she planted her sunflower seed!

"Mama, look!" Lily squealed with delight. "My baby sunflower is here!"

The little sprout was no bigger than Lily's thumb. Two perfect green leaves reached toward the sky. Lily had never seen anything so wonderful.

"You did that, sweetheart," Mama said proudly. "You planted the seed. You watered it every day."

Lily's heart felt bubbly with joy. She had grown something! All by herself! Well, with a little help from Mama.

Next, they checked the strawberry patch. Three red berries hung like tiny lanterns. "Can we pick them?" Lily asked hopefully.

"Of course! You helped plant these too."

The strawberries were warm from the sun. Sweet and juicy. Lily ate one immediately. The taste exploded on her tongue. Better than any store berry!

Then Lily heard a funny buzzing sound. A fat bumblebee danced from flower to flower. His fuzzy body was covered in yellow dust.

"He's helping our garden grow," Mama explained. "The bee takes pollen to other flowers."

Lily watched in wonder. The bee worked so hard! Just like she did. They were garden partners!

Near the fence, Lily discovered something amazing. Purple morning glories had opened their trumpet flowers. They twisted around the wooden posts like nature's decorations.

"They only open in the morning," Mama said. "Just for early birds like us."

Lily felt special. These flowers were her secret morning surprise!

She helped Mama pull three small weeds. The roots came up easily. Dark, rich soil clung to them. Lily liked how the earth felt cool and crumbly.

"Good job, Lily! The vegetables have more room now."

A red butterfly landed on the marigolds. Its wings opened and closed slowly. Like it was saying hello to Lily.

"Hi, butterfly," Lily whispered. She stood very still. The butterfly stayed for a whole minute!

Mama showed Lily the cucumber vines. Tiny white flowers dotted the green leaves. "Soon these will become cucumbers," she promised.

Lily could hardly believe it. Those little flowers would become crunchy vegetables? Garden magic was everywhere!

They filled the bird bath with fresh water. Almost immediately, two sparrows splashed and chirped. They flicked water droplets with their wings.

Lily giggled at their bath time dance.

As they walked back inside, Lily's hands were dirty. Her knees had grass stains. But her heart felt light and happy.

"Mama, can we come back tomorrow?" she asked eagerly.

"Of course, little gardener. Every morning."

Lily could hardly wait! Tomorrow she would check her sunflower. Maybe it would be taller! And the strawberries might have new berries.

The butterfly might visit again too.

At lunch, Lily told Daddy everything. About her sunflower baby. The dancing bee. The magic morning flowers.

"Gardens are full of surprises," Daddy agreed.

That night, Lily dreamed about her garden. In her dream, her sunflower grew as tall as the house. The butterfly gave her rides on its wings.

When morning came, Lily jumped out of bed. "Is it garden time yet?" she called to Mama.

Garden time wasn't boring anymore. It was the best part of every day! Full of growing things and flying friends and sweet surprises.

Lily couldn't wait to see what magic would happen today.

The end.
