# Zara the Zebra's Lost Doll

Zara the zebra loved bath time. The warm water felt so nice. Bubbles danced on her black stripes. She always brought her favorite doll, Rosie.

Rosie had a pink dress. She had curly yellow hair. Zara loved Rosie very much.

"Time to wash up, Rosie!" said Zara. She splashed gently in the tub. The bubbles smelled like strawberries.

After her bath, Zara dried off. She wrapped herself in a fluffy towel. It was soft and warm.

"Where is Rosie?" asked Zara. She looked around the bathroom. Rosie was gone!

Zara felt very sad. Her tummy felt funny. Where could Rosie be?

"I must find her," said Zara. She looked behind the toilet. No Rosie there.

She looked under the sink. Just some old towels. No pretty doll.

Zara started to cry. Little tears rolled down her striped face.

"Don't give up," she told herself. "Keep looking, Zara."

She checked the medicine cabinet. Too high for Rosie. She looked in the laundry basket. Just dirty clothes.

Zara sat on the bathroom floor. She felt very upset. Where could her doll be?

Then she had an idea. "Maybe Rosie fell somewhere," she said.

Zara got on her hands and knees. She looked under the bathtub. It was dark under there.

"Rosie? Are you there?" called Zara. Her voice echoed a little.

She reached her hoof under the tub. She felt something soft! It was fuzzy and small.

"That's just a dust bunny," said Zara. She kept reaching around.

Her hoof touched something hard. Something with tiny arms and legs!

"Rosie!" shouted Zara. She was so happy! Her heart felt like dancing.

Carefully, Zara pulled out her doll. Rosie was dusty but safe. Her pink dress was a little gray.

"Oh, Rosie!" said Zara. She hugged her doll tight. "I missed you so much!"

Zara's mommy heard the noise. She came into the bathroom.

"What happened, sweetie?" asked Mommy.

"I lost Rosie!" said Zara. "But I found her under the tub!"

Mommy smiled. "You didn't give up," she said. "That was very smart."

"I looked everywhere," said Zara proudly. "I kept trying and trying."

"Let's clean Rosie up," said Mommy. She turned on the sink water. It was nice and warm.

Zara washed Rosie's face gently. She cleaned her yellow hair. She wiped her pink dress.

"There," said Zara. "Good as new!"

Rosie looked beautiful again. Her dress was bright pink. Her hair was fluffy and clean.

"Thank you for finding me," said Zara in Rosie's voice. She made the doll dance a little.

Zara giggled. She felt so happy inside. Her chest felt warm and good.

"I learned something today," said Zara to Mommy.

"What did you learn?" asked Mommy.

"When I lose something important, I shouldn't give up," said Zara. "I should keep looking until I find it."

Mommy gave Zara a big hug. "That's right," she said. "You were very persistent."

"What does persistent mean?" asked Zara.

"It means you keep trying," said Mommy. "Even when things are hard."

Zara nodded. She understood now. Persistent was a good thing to be.

That night, Zara put Rosie on her nightstand. She could see her doll from her bed.

"Good night, Rosie," whispered Zara. "I'll never lose you again."

But if she did, Zara knew what to do. She would look everywhere. She would keep trying. She would be persistent.

Zara closed her eyes and smiled. Tomorrow would be another fun day. Maybe they would play in the garden. Or read books together.

But tonight, she was just happy. Rosie was safe. And Zara had learned something wonderful.

She had learned to never give up.
