# Story story_783

**Mama and Max on the Farm**

Mama sat by the old oak tree. She picked dandelions from the soft grass. The yellow flowers felt smooth in her hands. But Mama felt sad and lonely.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," she said softly.

The warm sun shone on her face. Birds chirped happy songs above her head. But Mama still felt alone on the big farm.

Behind the red barn, Max the little pig peeked out. His pink nose wiggled as he sniffed the air. Max saw Mama sitting all by herself.

"She looks sad," Max thought. "Maybe I can help."

Max trotted over on his short legs. His hooves made soft sounds on the dirt path. He stopped near the oak tree.

"Hello," said Max in his squeaky voice. "Can I play with you?"

Mama looked up with surprise. Her eyes grew wide and bright.

"You want to play with me?" asked Mama.

"Yes!" said Max. His curly tail wiggled with joy. "Playing is more fun with friends."

Mama smiled for the first time that day. "What should we play?"

Max thought hard. His little ears twitched as he listened to the farm sounds.

"Let's make mud pies!" said Max. "I know the best mud spot."

Mama clapped her hands. "That sounds wonderful!"

Max led Mama to a puddle by the chicken coop. The mud was brown and squishy. It felt cool between their fingers.

They scooped up handfuls of mud. They patted it into round shapes. Soon they had ten mud pies lined up on a flat rock.

"These look delicious," laughed Mama. The mud felt funny and wet on her hands.

"Now let's play hide and seek," said Max. His eyes sparkled with excitement.

Mama covered her eyes and counted. "One, two, three, four, five!"

Max ran to hide behind the water trough. But his pink bottom stuck out. Mama giggled when she saw it.

"I found you!" she called.

Max squealed with delight. "Your turn to hide!"

Mama ran to the hay bales. She squeezed between two golden stacks. The hay smelled sweet and fresh. It tickled her nose.

Max looked everywhere. He checked the chicken coop. He looked under the apple tree. Finally, he spotted Mama's shoes.

"Found you!" Max shouted happily.

They played until the sun began to set. The sky turned orange and pink. Cool evening air brushed their faces.

"I'm getting hungry," said Max. His tummy made a little rumble.

"Me too," said Mama. "Should we pick some apples?"

They walked to the apple tree together. Red apples hung from the branches. They looked shiny and sweet.

Max jumped up but couldn't reach. Mama stood on her tiptoes. She picked two perfect apples.

They sat under the tree to eat. The apples were crisp and juicy. Sweet juice ran down their chins.

"This is the best day ever," said Mama. Her heart felt warm and happy.

"I'm glad we're friends," said Max. He leaned against Mama's shoulder.

As stars began to twinkle, they heard Mama's mother calling.

"Time to come inside, Mama!"

Mama felt sad to leave her new friend. "Will you play with me tomorrow?" she asked.

"Every day!" promised Max. "Friends always play together."

They hugged goodbye. Max's fur felt soft and warm. Mama's hug felt gentle and kind.

Mama walked home with a big smile. Her lonely feeling was completely gone. She had found the best friend ever.

Max watched until Mama disappeared into her house. Then he trotted back to the barn. His heart felt full of joy.

The next morning, Mama ran outside early. Max was already waiting by the oak tree.

"Ready to play?" called Max.

"Always!" laughed Mama.

From that day on, they played together every single day. The farm felt full of friendship and fun. Mama never felt lonely again.

And Max had found the perfect playmate. Their friendship grew stronger with each passing day on the happy farm.

The end.
