# Story story_707

**Teddy's Magical Art Time**

The big clock on the wall went tick-tock. Miss Emma clapped her hands together. "Art time, everyone!" she called out cheerfully.

All the children jumped up from the reading circle. They ran to the art table with big smiles. But Teddy stayed sitting on the soft blue carpet. His shoulders drooped down low.

"Come on, Teddy!" called his friend Maya. She waved a fat red crayon in the air. "Let's make pictures!"

Teddy shook his head slowly. "I don't like art time," he whispered. His voice was very quiet. "I'm not good at drawing."

Miss Emma walked over to Teddy. She sat down next to him on the carpet. "What makes you feel that way?" she asked gently.

"My pictures never look right," Teddy said. He pointed to the wall where other kids' artwork hung. "Look at Sarah's butterfly. It's so pretty. My animals just look like messy scribbles."

Miss Emma smiled warmly. "Would you like to try something different today?" she asked. "Something special?"

Teddy looked up with curious eyes. "What do you mean?"

Miss Emma stood up and reached for a big box. The box was covered in sparkly silver paper. "This is my magic art box," she said with a wink. "Want to see what's inside?"

Teddy nodded slowly. He felt a tiny flutter of excitement in his tummy.

Miss Emma opened the box carefully. Inside were sponges cut into fun shapes. There were star sponges and heart sponges. Circle sponges and triangle sponges too. Next to them sat shallow dishes filled with bright, thick paint.

"Sponge painting!" Miss Emma announced. "You dip the sponge in paint. Then you press it on paper. Like magic stamps!"

Teddy's eyes grew wide. The paint looked smooth and creamy. The colors were so bright and cheerful. Red like fire trucks. Blue like the sky. Yellow like sunshine.

"Can I try?" Teddy asked. His voice was a little louder now.

"Of course!" Miss Emma said. She helped Teddy to the art table. She gave him a big sheet of white paper.

Teddy picked up a star-shaped sponge. He dipped it into the yellow paint. The sponge felt soft and squishy in his hand. He pressed it down on his paper.

Pop! A perfect yellow star appeared.

"Wow!" Teddy gasped. His eyes sparkled with surprise. "It really worked!"

He tried a heart sponge with red paint. Pop! A beautiful red heart. Then a blue circle. Pop! Then a green triangle. Pop! Pop! Pop!

Soon Teddy's paper was covered in colorful shapes. They made a cheerful pattern all across the page. The colors were bright and happy.

"This is so fun!" Teddy laughed. His whole face lit up with joy. "Look, Miss Emma! I made a rainbow garden!"

Other children gathered around Teddy's table. "That's so cool!" said Maya. "Can I try the sponges too?"

"Your picture is awesome, Teddy!" said Sarah.

Teddy felt warm and proud inside. His cheeks turned pink with happiness. He had never felt this good about art before.

The paint smelled fresh and clean. The sponges made soft squishing sounds. Teddy's fingers got a little messy, but he didn't mind. The mess felt fun and creative.

When art time was over, Teddy held up his colorful picture. The shapes looked perfect and bright. Each one was exactly where he wanted it to be.

"Can we do sponge painting again tomorrow?" Teddy asked Miss Emma hopefully.

"We can try finger painting tomorrow," Miss Emma said with a smile. "And next week, we'll use cotton balls to make fluffy clouds."

Teddy's heart felt light and happy. He couldn't wait to try more new art activities. Maybe he wasn't bad at art after all. Maybe he just needed to find the right way to create.

As Teddy walked to his cubby, he whispered to himself, "I can't wait for art time tomorrow!"

His rainbow garden picture would go right on his bedroom wall at home.

The end.
