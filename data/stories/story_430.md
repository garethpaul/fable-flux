# Tommy's Big Bath Adventure

Tommy stood in the long hallway. His bare feet felt cold on the wooden floor. Mom called from the bathroom down the hall.

"Bath time, Tommy!"

Tommy crossed his arms tight. He shook his head hard. "No bath!" he said loudly. "I don't want to!"

The bathroom door was open wide. Steam floated out like fluffy clouds. Tommy could hear water splashing. But he didn't move his feet.

"Baths are yucky," Tommy whispered to himself. He sat down on the soft hallway rug. The rug was blue like the ocean.

Mom peeked her head out. Her smile was warm and kind. "What's wrong, sweetheart?"

"I don't like baths," Tommy said. His voice was very quiet. "The water is too hot. The soap gets in my eyes."

Mom sat down next to Tommy. The hallway felt cozy now. "I understand," she said gently. "Let's think together. How can we make bath time fun?"

Tommy thought hard. His thinking face was very serious. "Maybe... maybe we could make it different?"

"What would make it better?" Mom asked.

Tommy's eyes got bright. "Toys! Bath toys would help!"

They walked to Tommy's room together. Tommy picked up his yellow rubber duck. He grabbed his red boat too. The boat had tiny white sails.

Back in the hallway, Tommy felt braver. But he still worried about the water. "What if it's too hot?" he asked.

"Let's test it first," Mom said wisely.

In the bathroom, Tommy touched the water. It felt just right! Not too hot. Not too cold. Perfect like his porridge.

"Now what about soap?" Mom asked kindly.

Tommy remembered his problem. "It stings my eyes!"

Mom showed him special tear-free soap. The bottle was green and friendly. "This soap won't hurt," she promised.

Tommy felt much better now. But he had one more worry. "Baths take too long," he said.

Mom had another good idea. "What if we sing songs? Songs make time go fast!"

Tommy nodded eagerly. He loved singing! His favorite song was about five little ducks.

Slowly, Tommy put one toe in. The water felt nice and warm. He put his whole foot in. Then both feet!

"This isn't scary!" Tommy said happily.

His duck bobbed on the water. Bob, bob, bob! His red boat sailed around. Tommy made whooshing sounds like the wind.

Mom helped wash his hair gently. No soap in his eyes! Tommy felt proud and clean.

They sang the duck song together. "Five little ducks went swimming one day!" Their voices echoed happily in the bathroom.

Tommy played with bubbles too. They were white and fluffy. Some bubbles popped on his nose. Pop! Pop! Pop! He giggled at the silly sound.

"I'm having fun!" Tommy announced loudly. The water splashed when he moved. Splash, splash, splash!

Soon it was time to get out. Tommy felt sad to leave his toys. But he also felt very proud. "I did it!" he said with a big smile.

Mom wrapped him in a soft towel. The towel was warm from the dryer. It felt like a cozy hug.

"You solved your problem," Mom said proudly. "You thought of good ideas. You tried new things. You were very brave!"

Tommy felt happy inside his chest. The scared feeling was all gone. "Next time will be easier," he said confidently.

They walked back down the hallway together. Tommy's feet felt warm now. His hair smelled like sweet strawberries.

"Want to pick toys for tomorrow's bath?" Mom asked.

"Yes!" Tommy shouted excitedly. He was already thinking of new ideas. Maybe his green frog would like swimming too!

That night, Tommy felt proud. He had faced his fear. He had found solutions. Bath time wasn't scary anymore. It was actually fun!

From his bed, Tommy could see the hallway. Tomorrow he would walk down it happily. No more hiding from bath time. He was a brave problem-solver now!

Tommy smiled as he fell asleep. Dreams of rubber ducks and bubble mountains filled his head. Bath time was his friend now.

The end.
