# Story story_1364

**Papa's Reading Adventure**

Every evening at seven o'clock, Mama called out, "Reading time!" Little Emma would run to get her favorite books. She would pile them high on the soft blue couch. But Papa always seemed busy.

"I have dishes to wash," Papa would say. Or "I need to check my phone." Papa loved Emma very much. But reading felt hard after his long day at work.

Tonight was different. Mama had to help Grandma. "Papa, will you read with me?" Emma asked. Her big brown eyes looked hopeful. Papa's shoulders felt heavy and tired.

"I suppose so," Papa said slowly. He sat down on the couch. Emma climbed up next to him. She smelled like strawberry shampoo and sunshine. Her small hand felt warm in his.

Emma picked a book about a brave little mouse. The pages were bright yellow and green. Papa opened to the first page. The words looked blurry. He rubbed his eyes.

"Once upon a time," Papa began. His voice sounded scratchy. Emma snuggled closer. Her head rested on his arm. Papa felt his heart slow down.

As Papa read about the mouse's adventure, something magical started happening. Emma giggled at the funny parts. She gasped when the mouse got scared. Her excitement made Papa smile.

"Look, Papa!" Emma pointed to a picture. "The mouse found cheese!" Her finger traced the bright orange cheese. Papa noticed how carefully she looked at each drawing.

Papa's voice grew stronger. The words came easier now. Emma asked questions about the pictures. "Why is the mouse happy?" she wondered. Papa thought about it. "Because he was brave," Papa said.

They read another book about a sleepy bear. Emma made snoring sounds when the bear went to sleep. Papa laughed out loud. The sound surprised him. When did he last laugh like that?

Emma picked a third book about dancing frogs. Papa used silly voices for each frog character. Emma clapped her hands with joy. "More voices, Papa!" she said. Papa made the frogs sound squeaky and deep.

The room felt cozy and warm. The lamp cast a golden glow over their books. Outside, crickets chirped their evening song. But Papa only heard Emma's happy breathing.

"One more book?" Emma asked sweetly. Papa looked at the clock. It was already past bedtime. But Emma's smile melted his heart. "Just one more," he said.

They read about a little girl who loved stars. Emma pointed to the twinkling stars in the pictures. "I want to catch stars too," she whispered. Papa hugged her tight. "Maybe we can look for real stars tomorrow," he said.

When the last book closed, Emma yawned. Her eyelids looked heavy. "That was the best reading time ever," she said. Papa felt warmth spread through his chest.

"Thank you for reading with me, Papa," Emma said. She gave him a big hug. Her arms squeezed tight around his neck. Papa felt tears in his eyes. Happy tears.

As Papa carried Emma to bed, she said something special. "Papa, your voices made the stories come alive." Papa tucked her under her soft pink blanket. He kissed her forehead gently.

Walking back to the living room, Papa saw the pile of books. Tomorrow's reading time didn't feel scary anymore. It felt like a gift waiting to be opened.

Papa picked up one of the books. He smiled at the colorful pictures. Maybe he could practice the voices. Maybe he could find new books at the library.

The next evening, when Mama called "Reading time!" Papa was ready. He had already chosen three new books. Emma ran to the couch with excitement.

"Are you ready for another adventure?" Papa asked. His voice sounded happy and strong. Emma nodded with a huge smile.

From that day on, reading time became Papa's favorite time too. Every evening brought new stories, new voices, and new memories with his little girl.

The end.
