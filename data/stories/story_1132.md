# Story story_1132

**Zara the Zebra and the Big Apple Tree**

Zara the zebra loved her sunny field. Green grass tickled her hooves. Colorful flowers danced in the breeze. But the best thing in her field was the giant apple tree.

The tree was very, very tall. Its thick brown trunk reached up to the clouds. Sweet red apples hung from every branch. They smelled like sunshine and sugar.

Every morning, Zara looked up at those apples. Her tummy rumbled with hunger. "Those apples look so yummy," she said. "I wish I could reach them."

Other animals came to visit the tree. Monkey swung up easily. His long arms grabbed apple after apple. "Mmm, so sweet!" he called down to Zara.

Squirrel scampered up the rough bark. Her tiny claws helped her climb fast. She stuffed her cheeks with juicy apple pieces.

Even Bear could reach the low branches. He stood on his back legs and stretched his big paws up high.

But Zara just stood there. Her legs were too straight. Her hooves couldn't grip the bark. Her neck was long, but not long enough.

"I can't climb like them," Zara sighed. A tear rolled down her striped face. "I'll never taste those wonderful apples."

The next morning, Zara had an idea. "I know! I'll run really fast. Then I'll jump as high as I can!"

She backed up to the far side of the field. The morning sun warmed her black and white stripes. She took a deep breath.

"Here I go!" Zara galloped across the field. Her hooves pounded the soft earth. Faster and faster she ran. The wind whooshed past her ears.

When she reached the tree, Zara jumped with all her might. Up, up, up she soared! But the branches were still too high. She landed back on the ground with a soft thud.

"Oh no," she said sadly. "That didn't work either."

Zara sat in the shade feeling disappointed. The apples smelled even sweeter now. Her stomach growled louder than before.

Then she noticed something. The wind was blowing hard today. Some apples swayed back and forth on their branches. A few small ones looked loose.

"Wait a minute," Zara said. Her eyes grew bright. "Maybe I don't need to climb the tree!"

Zara positioned herself under the lowest branch. She lifted her long neck as high as possible. Then she began to shake her whole body.

Her zebra dance made the ground vibrate. The tree trunk started to wiggle. The branches began to sway more and more.

"Come on, apples!" Zara called out. She shook harder and harder.

Suddenly, a beautiful red apple broke free! It tumbled down through the leaves. Zara caught it perfectly in her mouth.

"Oh my!" she said, crunching the sweet fruit. "This is the most delicious apple ever!"

The apple was crispy and juicy. Sweet juice dripped down her chin. It tasted like sunshine and happiness.

More apples began to fall. Plop! Plop! Plop! They landed softly in the grass around Zara.

"Hooray!" she cheered. "I found a way!"

Soon, Monkey swung down to see what was happening. "Wow, Zara! How did you get those apples?"

"I couldn't climb up," Zara explained proudly. "So I brought the apples down to me!"

Squirrel chittered with excitement. "That's so smart!"

Bear nodded his big furry head. "You never gave up, Zara. That's wonderful!"

From that day on, Zara enjoyed apples whenever she wanted them. She learned that there's always more than one way to solve a problem.

And the best part? Zara discovered that her special zebra dance worked on other fruit trees too!

Every morning, she would dance under the apple tree. The sweet fruit would rain down like gifts from above. Zara's field became the happiest place for everyone to share delicious treats together.

"Sometimes the best solution," Zara would tell her friends, "is the one you create yourself!"

The end.
