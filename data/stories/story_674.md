# Story story_674

**Bella and the Magic Reading Time**

Every day after lunch, Mama called out, "Reading time!" 

Bella loved many things. She loved her soft pink doll named Rosie. She loved building blocks. She loved running in the yard. But reading time? Bella wrinkled her nose.

"Do I have to?" Bella asked. She hugged Rosie tight.

"Yes, sweetie," Mama said gently. "Reading helps your brain grow big and strong."

Bella dragged her feet to the cozy reading corner. Soft blue pillows waited on the fluffy rug. Sunlight streamed through the window. But Bella felt grumpy.

She plopped down with a big sigh. Rosie sat in her lap. The doll's button eyes seemed sad too.

"I don't want to read," Bella whispered to Rosie. "Books are boring."

Mama picked up a colorful book. "Let's try this one about a brave little mouse."

Bella crossed her arms. She looked at the ceiling. She counted her fingers. She wiggled her toes. Anything but look at the book.

Then something strange happened. Rosie felt warm in Bella's arms. The doll's soft fabric seemed to shimmer in the sunlight.

"Once upon a time," Mama began reading, "a tiny mouse lived in a big castle."

Suddenly, Rosie whispered, "I love castles!" 

Bella's eyes grew wide. Did her doll just talk? She looked down at Rosie. The doll's button eyes seemed to sparkle.

"The mouse was very small," Mama continued. "But she was also very brave."

"Just like me!" Rosie whispered again. "I'm brave too!"

Bella giggled. This was getting interesting! She snuggled closer to Mama. The book felt warm in Mama's hands.

As Mama read, amazing things happened. Bella could smell the castle's stone walls. She could hear the mouse's tiny footsteps. She could see the golden cheese the mouse was trying to reach.

"Go, little mouse!" Rosie cheered quietly. "You can do it!"

Bella found herself cheering too. "Climb higher!" she called out.

The mouse in the story climbed and climbed. She never gave up. Finally, she reached the delicious cheese.

"Hooray!" Bella and Rosie shouted together.

Mama smiled. "Would you like to hear another story?"

"Yes, please!" Bella said. She couldn't believe it. Reading was actually fun!

They read about a singing bird. Rosie hummed along with the bird's pretty song. They read about a dancing frog. Rosie wiggled in Bella's arms like she was dancing too.

Each story felt like a magical adventure. Bella could taste the sweet berries the bird found. She could feel the cool pond water where the frog lived.

"One more story?" Bella asked hopefully.

Mama laughed. "Tomorrow, sweetheart. Reading time is over for today."

"Aww," Bella said. But she wasn't really sad. She felt excited and happy.

That night, Bella tucked Rosie into bed beside her. "Thank you for making reading fun," she whispered.

Rosie's button eyes seemed to wink in the moonlight.

The next day after lunch, Bella heard the familiar call. "Reading time!"

This time, Bella jumped up with joy. "Come on, Rosie!" she said. "Let's go on another adventure!"

She raced to the reading corner. The soft pillows looked so inviting. The warm sunlight felt perfect.

Bella sat down and hugged Rosie close. "What story will we read today?" she asked Mama.

Mama's eyes twinkled. "How about a story about a little girl who loves to read?"

"Perfect!" Bella said. She opened the book eagerly.

As Mama began to read, Bella smiled big. Reading time wasn't boring anymore. It was magical! Every book was a new world to explore. Every story was a new friend to meet.

And with Rosie by her side, every adventure felt extra special.

"I love reading time," Bella whispered to Rosie.

And somehow, Bella knew that Rosie loved it too.

The end.
