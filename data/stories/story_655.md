# Story story_655

**Penny the Puppet's Big Problem**

Penny the puppet lived on Emma's toy shelf. She had soft yellow yarn hair. Her dress was bright red with tiny white dots. Penny loved watching Emma play every day.

One morning, Penny felt something wrong. Her arm was loose! It wiggled and wobbled when she moved. The string inside was coming apart.

"Oh no," whispered Penny. "What if I fall apart?"

Emma was getting ready for school. She grabbed her backpack and ran downstairs. Penny wanted to call out for help. But she was too shy to speak up.

All day, Penny sat very still. She didn't want her arm to get worse. The other toys played and laughed around her. Penny just watched sadly.

"Why don't you play with us?" asked Teddy Bear.

"I can't," said Penny quietly. "My arm is broken."

"Just tell Emma," said Teddy. "She always fixes us."

Penny shook her head. "I'm too scared. What if she doesn't want a broken puppet?"

That night, Emma came home tired. She did her homework at her desk. Penny watched Emma's gentle hands write her letters. Those same hands could fix her arm. But Penny stayed silent.

The next morning was Saturday. Emma slept late. Penny's arm felt even looser now. She had to do something!

Penny took a deep breath. She wiggled to the edge of the shelf. Maybe she could fix herself. She tried to reach her loose arm with her good hand. Stretch, stretch, stretch! But she couldn't reach far enough.

"Careful, Penny!" called Teddy Bear.

Penny wobbled too far. She tumbled off the shelf! She landed with a soft thump on the carpet. Her loose arm twisted underneath her.

"Ow," she whispered. Now her arm hurt even more.

Emma heard the sound. She opened her sleepy eyes. "What was that noise?"

Emma sat up in bed. She looked around her room. Then she saw Penny on the floor.

"Oh, Penny!" Emma jumped out of bed. She picked up the little puppet carefully. "What happened to you?"

Emma noticed the loose arm right away. "Your poor arm is all wobbly. Does it hurt?"

Penny felt tears in her button eyes. But Emma's voice was so kind and worried. Maybe she could be brave.

"Yes," Penny said in her tiniest voice. "It hurts a little."

Emma's eyes grew wide with surprise. Then she smiled the biggest smile.

"You can talk! I always hoped you could. Don't worry, Penny. I'll fix you right up."

Emma carried Penny to her craft table. She got out her sewing box. The needle gleamed silver in the morning light. The thread was strong and white.

"This might tickle," Emma said gently.

Emma worked very carefully. She pushed the needle through Penny's arm. In and out, in and out. The thread made tiny whisking sounds. Soon Penny's arm felt strong again.

"How does that feel?" asked Emma.

Penny moved her arm up and down. It felt perfect! "Much better! Thank you, Emma."

"I'm so glad you told me what was wrong," said Emma. "I want to help you always."

Penny felt warm and happy inside. "I was scared you wouldn't want a broken puppet."

Emma hugged Penny close. "I love you no matter what. Broken toys just need extra care."

From that day on, Penny never stayed quiet about problems. When her dress got a tiny tear, she told Emma right away. When her yarn hair got tangled, she asked for help.

Emma always listened. She always helped. And Penny learned that asking for help made everything better.

The other toys were proud of brave Penny. Teddy Bear gave her extra hugs. The toy cars honked happy songs for her.

Every night, Penny sat on her shelf feeling grateful. Her arm was strong. Her heart was full. And she knew that Emma would always be there to help.

"Good night, Emma," Penny whispered.

"Good night, my brave little puppet," Emma whispered back.

And they both slept peacefully, knowing they had each other.

The end.
