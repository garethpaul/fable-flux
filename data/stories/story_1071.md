# Story story_1071

**The Hallway Friends**

Little Blue Bird sat alone in the long hallway. The walls were painted sunny yellow. Soft carpet covered the floor. Blue Bird had a small red ball. She rolled it back and forth. Back and forth. But playing alone felt sad.

"I wish I had a friend," chirped Blue Bird softly. Her tiny voice echoed in the empty hallway. The ball rolled slowly toward the wall. Blue Bird sighed a little sigh.

Around the corner came Tiger. His orange stripes were bright and bold. His paws were big and fluffy. Tiger saw Blue Bird with her red ball. He wanted to play too.

"Hello," said Tiger in his gentle voice. "Can I play with you?"

Blue Bird looked up. Tiger was much bigger than her. His teeth looked sharp. Blue Bird felt a little scared.

"You're so big," whispered Blue Bird. "What if you step on me?"

Tiger sat down carefully. He made himself as small as possible. "I'll be very gentle," he promised. "I just want to be your friend."

Blue Bird thought for a moment. Tiger's eyes looked kind. His voice sounded friendly. Maybe big didn't mean scary.

"Okay," said Blue Bird. "Let's play together."

Tiger smiled a big, happy smile. Blue Bird pushed the red ball toward him. Tiger used just one paw. He tapped it very softly. The ball rolled back to Blue Bird.

"This is fun!" chirped Blue Bird. She felt excited now.

They rolled the ball back and forth. Tiger was careful with his big paws. Blue Bird flew up and dropped the ball from above. It bounced between them.

"Catch it!" laughed Blue Bird.

Tiger gently caught the ball. He was so careful not to hurt it. Then he rolled it back slowly. Blue Bird hopped on top of the rolling ball. She rode it like a tiny red wagon.

"Wheee!" she sang happily.

The hallway filled with their laughter. Tiger's deep chuckles mixed with Blue Bird's sweet chirps. The yellow walls seemed brighter. The soft carpet felt warmer under their play.

"Let's try something new," suggested Tiger. He lay down flat on his belly. "You can ride on my back!"

Blue Bird felt brave now. She landed gently on Tiger's soft fur. Tiger crawled very slowly down the hallway. Blue Bird spread her wings wide. She felt like she was flying and walking at the same time.

"This is amazing!" she chirped with joy.

Tiger purred softly. He was happy too. "I've never had such a wonderful friend," he said.

They played hide and seek next. Blue Bird hid behind Tiger's big paws. Tiger pretended he couldn't find her. "Where could Blue Bird be?" he wondered out loud.

"Here I am!" giggled Blue Bird, popping out.

Tiger acted very surprised. "There you are! You're the best hider ever!"

As the sun came through the hallway window, they sat together. Blue Bird perched on Tiger's head. Tiger's fur was warm and cozy.

"I'm so glad you wanted to play," said Blue Bird.

"Me too," purred Tiger. "You're the best friend I could ask for."

They decided to meet in the hallway every day. Blue Bird would bring her red ball. Tiger would bring his gentle heart. Together they would fill the yellow hallway with friendship and fun.

From that day on, the hallway was never lonely. Two friends had found each other. One small, one big. One who could fly, one who could prowl. But both with hearts full of kindness.

Blue Bird learned that being different sizes didn't matter. Tiger learned that being gentle made him strong. Together they learned that friendship comes in all shapes and sizes.

The red ball sat between them as they planned tomorrow's games. The yellow hallway glowed with happiness. Two friends had turned a lonely place into the most wonderful playground ever.

And they lived happily, playing together every single day.

The end.
