# Foxy's Sharing Discovery

In the cozy attic above Grandma's house, a little red fox named Foxy had made his special hideaway. Dust particles danced in the golden sunbeams that streamed through the round window. Old wooden boxes and soft quilts created the perfect fort for playing.

Foxy loved his attic treasures. He had shiny marbles that rolled across the creaky floor. He had colorful picture books with soft, worn pages. Best of all, he had a collection of stuffed animals that sat in a neat row along the wall.

One sunny morning, Foxy heard footsteps on the wooden stairs. *Thump, thump, thump.* His cousin Bella, a fluffy gray squirrel, poked her head through the attic door.

"Hi, Foxy!" Bella chirped. "Can I play with you?"

Foxy's ears twitched nervously. He looked at his precious toys. "Um... these are mine," he said, gathering his marbles closer. "I don't know how to share them."

Bella's tail drooped a little. "Oh. Can I just look at your picture books?"

"No," Foxy said quickly, stacking the books higher. "They might get messy."

Bella sat quietly in the corner. She watched Foxy play alone with his marbles. *Click, click, roll.* The sound echoed in the big attic. But something felt wrong. Playing alone wasn't as fun as Foxy remembered.

Foxy noticed Bella looking sad. Her whiskers drooped down. This made Foxy's tummy feel funny and tight.

"Maybe..." Foxy said slowly, "maybe you could hold just one marble?"

He handed Bella his smallest, plainest marble. Bella smiled a tiny smile. "Thank you, Foxy."

But Foxy watched her like a hawk. When Bella rolled the marble gently, Foxy quickly snatched it back. "That's enough," he said.

Bella's ears flattened against her head. Playing with one marble for one minute wasn't very fun.

Foxy tried again. "You can look at one page of one book," he offered.

Bella opened the book carefully. The page showed dancing rabbits in a meadow. "Oh, this is beautiful!" she gasped.

But before Bella could really see the picture, Foxy closed the book. "Time's up," he said.

Now Bella looked ready to cry. A single tear sparkled in her eye.

Foxy's heart felt heavy like a stone. The attic felt too quiet and too lonely, even with Bella there. He looked around at all his toys. They seemed less shiny somehow.

Then Foxy remembered something. Last week, when he had visited Bella's tree house, she had shared everything. Her acorn collection, her leaf pile, even her favorite swing. They had laughed and played until sunset painted the sky orange and pink.

"Wait," Foxy said softly. "I think I'm doing this wrong."

He took a deep breath. "Bella, would you like to play marbles together? We could make a big circle and take turns."

Bella's eyes brightened like morning stars. "Really?"

"Really," Foxy nodded. "And after that, we could read books together. You could turn the pages."

They spread out on the soft quilt. Foxy showed Bella how to flick marbles into the circle. *Click, click, roll!* When Bella's marble knocked out two of Foxy's, they both cheered.

"This is so much more fun!" Bella laughed, clapping her tiny paws.

Foxy discovered something wonderful. Sharing didn't make his toys disappear. Instead, it made everything twice as exciting! Bella's giggles filled the attic with joy. Her questions about the picture books made Foxy notice new details he'd never seen before.

As the afternoon sun painted everything golden, the two friends built a fort together using quilts and pillows. They shared stories and snacks that Bella had brought in her tiny backpack.

"Thank you for teaching me how to share," Foxy said, his heart feeling warm and full.

"You figured it out all by yourself," Bella smiled. "You just needed to practice."

From that day on, Foxy's attic became the most popular playroom in the whole house. And Foxy learned that the very best treasures of all were the friends who came to share them.
