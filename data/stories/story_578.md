# Story story_578

**Sleepy Meadow Nap Time**

The sun climbed high in the bright blue sky. Farmer Mae rang her little silver bell. "Nap time, everyone!" she called sweetly.

All the farm animals knew this sound. The cows walked slowly to their cool barn. The pigs rolled in their muddy pen. The chickens fluffed their soft feathers.

But Woolly the sheep did not want to nap. Her fluffy white wool felt warm in the sunshine. The green grass smelled so fresh and good. Butterflies danced around the yellow flowers.

"I'm not sleepy," Woolly said to her friend Baa-Baa. "Naps are boring. I want to play more."

Baa-Baa yawned a big yawn. "But naps feel so nice," she said. "Come lie down with me."

Woolly shook her curly head. "No thank you. I'll stay awake."

All the other sheep found cozy spots. They curled up under the shady apple tree. Soon they were breathing softly. Their eyes closed tight.

Woolly felt lonely now. The meadow seemed too quiet. Even the buzzing bees had found flowers to rest on.

She walked around the empty field. Her little hooves made soft sounds on the grass. The warm sun made her feel drowsy. But she still didn't want to sleep.

Then something magical happened. Woolly saw tiny sparkles in the air. They looked like golden dust dancing in the sunlight. The sparkles swirled around her wool.

"What are you?" Woolly whispered.

A gentle voice answered. "We are Dream Sprites. We bring happy dreams to sleepy animals."

The sparkles grew brighter. They made soft tinkling sounds like tiny bells. Woolly watched them spin and twirl.

"We can show you wonderful places," said the Dream Sprites. "But only if you close your eyes."

Woolly felt curious now. "What kind of places?"

"Meadows made of cotton candy. Rivers that taste like sweet milk. Mountains covered in the softest clouds."

This sounded amazing to Woolly. She looked at her sleeping friends. They all had little smiles on their faces.

"Are they seeing these magical places too?" Woolly asked.

"Yes," the Dream Sprites sang. "Every nap is an adventure."

Woolly's eyes felt heavy now. The warm sunshine felt like a cozy blanket. The gentle breeze smelled like lavender flowers.

"Maybe I could try a little nap," she said softly.

Woolly found a perfect spot next to Baa-Baa. The grass felt like a fluffy pillow. She curled up in a little ball.

The Dream Sprites sprinkled their golden magic around her. "Sweet dreams, little sheep," they whispered.

Woolly closed her eyes. Right away, she began to dream. She saw a rainbow made of wool. She bounced on clouds that felt like trampolines. She met a friendly dragon who gave rides through the sky.

In her dream, Woolly laughed and played. She had the most wonderful adventures. Everything felt magical and safe.

When Woolly woke up, the sun was lower in the sky. All her friends were stretching and yawning. They looked happy and rested.

"How was your nap?" asked Baa-Baa.

"It was incredible!" Woolly said. "I went to the most amazing places. I can't wait to tell you about them."

Farmer Mae rang her bell again. "Time to wake up, sleepy heads!"

Woolly felt so much better now. Her legs felt strong. Her mind felt clear. The afternoon seemed full of possibilities.

"Tomorrow I'll nap right away," Woolly told Baa-Baa. "I want to see what other magical places the Dream Sprites will show me."

That evening, Woolly helped the other sheep with their chores. She had so much energy. She felt happy and peaceful.

When bedtime came, Woolly settled down easily. She thought about her wonderful nap adventure. She smiled as she drifted off to sleep.

The Dream Sprites watched over all the animals. They knew Woolly had learned something special. Sometimes the best adventures happen when you close your eyes.

The end.
