# Story story_580

Lily loved playing in her backyard garden. The flowers smelled sweet like candy. The grass felt soft under her bare feet. Best of all, she had her favorite red ball.

The ball was bright and shiny. It bounced high into the blue sky. Lily threw it up and caught it again. Up and down, up and down. She giggled with joy.

"I love my red ball!" Lily said happily.

She threw the ball extra high. It went up, up, up into the air. The ball flew over the tall sunflowers. It disappeared behind the green bushes.

"Oh no!" Lily cried. "Where did my ball go?"

She looked everywhere she could see. The ball was gone. Lily felt sad and worried. Her bottom lip started to shake.

"I need to find my ball," she said.

Lily walked to the sunflowers first. They were very tall. Much taller than her. She stood on her tippy-toes. She stretched her arms up high.

"I can't see anything," Lily said sadly.

She tried to push through the thick flowers. The stems were too strong. The leaves tickled her face. But she couldn't get through.

Lily sat down on the grass. A tear rolled down her cheek. She wiped it away with her small hand.

"Maybe I should ask for help," she thought.

Lily ran to the house. Her mom was watering plants by the window.

"Mom! Mom!" Lily called. "I lost my red ball!"

Mom put down her watering can. She gave Lily a warm hug.

"Where did you see it last?" Mom asked gently.

"It went over the sunflowers," Lily explained. "I can't reach it."

Mom smiled kindly. "Let's look together. Two people can solve problems better than one."

They walked back to the garden. Mom was much taller than Lily. She could see over the sunflowers easily.

"I don't see it here," Mom said. "Let's check behind the bushes."

They walked around the big green bushes. The leaves rustled in the warm breeze. Birds chirped happy songs above them.

"There it is!" Mom pointed.

Lily looked where Mom was pointing. Her red ball sat in a patch of soft dirt. It was behind the rose bushes. The ball looked a little dirty but still bright red.

"My ball!" Lily shouted with joy.

But the ball was stuck between thorny rose stems. Lily reached for it carefully. The thorns were sharp and scratchy.

"Ouch!" she said, pulling her hand back.

"Let me help you," Mom said. "I have garden gloves."

Mom put on her thick gloves. She carefully moved the rose branches apart. The thorns couldn't hurt her hands now.

"Here you go, sweetie," Mom said. She handed Lily the red ball.

Lily hugged her ball tight. It felt smooth and familiar in her arms. She was so happy to have it back.

"Thank you, Mom!" Lily said. "You helped me solve my problem!"

"You did great too," Mom said proudly. "You didn't give up. You asked for help when you needed it."

Lily bounced her ball gently. She didn't throw it too high this time. She wanted to keep it safe and close.

"I learned something today," Lily said thoughtfully.

"What did you learn?" Mom asked.

"When something is hard, I shouldn't give up. I can ask for help. Two people working together is better than one person alone."

Mom nodded with a big smile. "That's exactly right, my smart girl."

Lily played with her ball in a smaller part of the garden. She threw it just high enough to catch it easily. The afternoon sun felt warm on her face.

She was happy to have her favorite red ball back. She was even happier that she learned something new. Problems could be solved with patience and help from people who love you.

"This was a good day," Lily said to herself. She bounced her ball and smiled in the beautiful garden.

The end.
