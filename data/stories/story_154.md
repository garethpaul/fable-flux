# Lily's Lost Book

Lily the sheep loved playing in the big sandbox at Sunny Park. The warm sand felt soft between her hooves. She wiggled her toes and giggled.

Every day, Lily brought her special book. It had bright pictures of flowers. The pages were smooth and shiny. She loved reading under the apple tree.

Today felt perfect for reading. Lily set her book down carefully. Then she built a tall sand castle. She made roads and bridges too.

"Time for my book!" Lily said happily.

But her book was gone!

Lily looked left. She looked right. No book anywhere!

"Oh no!" Lily cried. Her eyes filled with tears. "Where is my special book?"

She searched near the swings. Nothing there. She checked by the slide. Still nothing.

Lily sat down and sniffled. "Maybe I should give up," she whispered.

Then she remembered something important. Mama always said, "Keep trying, little one. Good things happen when we don't give up."

Lily stood up tall. "I won't give up!" she declared.

She decided to search more carefully. First, she looked in the sandbox again. She moved sand with her hooves. Scoop, scoop, scoop went the sand.

"Not here," Lily said. But she didn't stop.

Next, she searched under the apple tree. She pushed aside fallen leaves. Crunch, crunch, crunch went the leaves. Red and yellow leaves danced in the breeze.

"Not here either," Lily said. Her voice stayed strong.

Then Lily had a smart idea. She would ask for help!

"Excuse me," she called to Robin in the tree. "Have you seen my flower book?"

Robin chirped sweetly. "I saw something colorful by the pond!"

Lily's heart jumped with hope. She trotted to the small pond. Her hooves made soft pat-pat sounds on the grass.

There by the water sat Oliver the frog. He was reading something colorful!

"Oliver!" Lily called. "Is that my book?"

Oliver looked up and smiled. "Oh! Is this yours? I found it by the sandbox. The pictures are so pretty!"

Lily's face lit up like sunshine. "Yes! That's my special flower book!"

Oliver hopped over with the book. "I'm sorry I took it. I should have asked first."

"That's okay," Lily said kindly. "Would you like to read it together?"

Oliver's eyes sparkled. "Really? That would be wonderful!"

The two friends sat under the apple tree. They looked at every colorful page. They saw red roses and yellow daisies. They saw purple violets and orange marigolds.

"This page smells like real flowers," Oliver said, sniffing gently.

Lily giggled. "That's my favorite part too!"

They read about bees buzzing from flower to flower. They learned about butterflies dancing in gardens. The afternoon sun felt warm on their backs.

When they finished reading, Lily felt proud and happy. She had found her book! She had made a new friend too!

"Thank you for helping me find my book," Lily told Robin.

"Thank you for sharing," she told Oliver.

Oliver smiled. "Can we read together tomorrow?"

"Yes!" Lily said. "Friends always share good books!"

As the sun began to set, Lily carefully put her book in her special bag. She waved goodbye to her friends.

Walking home, Lily felt very proud. She had felt sad when her book was lost. She had wanted to give up. But she remembered to keep trying.

"I'm so glad I didn't give up," Lily said to herself. "I found my book AND made a new friend!"

At home, Lily told Mama about her adventure. Mama hugged her tight.

"You were very brave and smart," Mama said. "You kept trying even when things seemed hard."

That night, Lily fell asleep with her special book nearby. She dreamed of colorful flowers and kind friends. Tomorrow would bring another wonderful day at the sandbox.

And she would remember: good things happen when we keep trying and ask friends for help.
