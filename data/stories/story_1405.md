# Story story_1405

**Lily's Lost Red Ball**

Baby Lily loved her bright red ball. It was shiny and smooth. It bounced high in the air. She carried it everywhere in the backyard.

On this sunny morning, Lily rolled her ball across the soft grass. The ball sparkled in the warm sunlight. Birds chirped happy songs in the tall oak tree.

"Ball, ball!" Lily giggled as she crawled after it.

The red ball rolled faster and faster. It bounced over the flower bed. It rolled under the big green bushes. Then it disappeared!

Lily looked left. She looked right. Where was her special ball?

"Ball? Ball?" she called out. Her voice sounded worried.

Lily crawled to the flower bed. Pretty yellow flowers swayed in the breeze. She pushed the soft petals aside. She dug her tiny fingers in the cool dirt. No red ball anywhere.

Tears filled Lily's brown eyes. Her bottom lip trembled. She sat on the grass and sniffled.

"Oh no," she whispered sadly.

Mommy came outside with a glass of cold water. She saw Lily's sad face.

"What's wrong, sweet pea?" Mommy asked gently.

"Ball gone," Lily said. She pointed to the bushes.

Mommy knelt down beside her. "Let's look together," she said with a smile.

They crawled to the green bushes. The leaves rustled softly. Mommy pushed branches aside. Lily peeked underneath.

"I don't see it here," Mommy said. "Where else could it be?"

Lily thought hard. She put her finger on her chin. Then she remembered something important.

The ball had bounced very high. Maybe it went over the bushes!

"Up!" Lily pointed to the sky.

Mommy understood. "Good thinking! Let's check the other side."

They walked around the big bushes together. Lily's heart beat fast with hope. On the other side was the vegetable garden.

There, between the green tomato plants, sat the bright red ball! It looked like a big red tomato hiding in the leaves.

"Ball!" Lily squealed with joy.

She crawled quickly through the soft dirt. Her hands got muddy. Her knees got dirty. But she didn't care at all.

Lily grabbed her precious red ball. It felt smooth and perfect in her small hands. She hugged it tight against her chest.

"Found it!" she announced proudly.

The ball was still shiny and beautiful. It had waited patiently for her to find it.

Mommy clapped her hands. "You didn't give up, Lily! You kept looking until you found it."

Lily bounced the ball gently. It made a soft thump on the ground. She smiled so wide her cheeks hurt.

"Ball back!" she said happily.

They walked to the grassy area together. Lily rolled her ball carefully this time. She didn't want it to get lost again.

The warm sun felt good on her face. The grass tickled her toes. Everything felt perfect now.

"You're very smart," Mommy said. "When something seems lost, we can always try looking in new places."

Lily nodded. She had learned something important today. When things go wrong, don't cry for too long. Think of new ideas. Keep trying different ways.

She rolled the ball to Mommy. Mommy rolled it back gently. The red ball sparkled in the sunshine like a happy smile.

"I love my ball," Lily said.

"And your ball loves you too," Mommy replied. "That's why it waited for you to find it."

Lily giggled at that idea. Maybe her ball really did wait for her!

As the day continued, Lily played carefully with her red ball. She rolled it on the smooth patio. She bounced it on the soft grass. She kept it close and safe.

When Daddy came home, Lily ran to show him her ball. "Found it!" she told him proudly.

That night, Lily placed her red ball next to her bed. Tomorrow would bring another fun day of playing together.

She had learned that losing something doesn't mean it's gone forever. Sometimes you just need to look harder and think of new places to search.

With a happy heart, Lily fell asleep dreaming of bouncing balls and sunny days.

The end.
