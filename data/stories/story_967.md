# Story story_967

**Lily's Lost Doll**

Lily loved playing with her toy train in the big sandbox. The train was bright red with yellow wheels. It made soft chugging sounds as she pushed it through the warm sand.

"Choo-choo!" Lily said happily. She had her favorite doll, Rosie, sitting in the train car. Rosie had curly brown hair and a pink dress with tiny flowers.

The sun felt warm on Lily's face. The sand was soft between her fingers. Everything was perfect for playing.

Suddenly, the train hit a big bump in the sand. It tipped over sideways! Sand flew everywhere like tiny sparkles in the sunlight.

"Oh no!" Lily cried. She quickly turned the train right-side up. But something was wrong. Very wrong.

Rosie was gone!

Lily looked in the train car. Empty. She looked around the sandbox. No Rosie anywhere.

"Where did you go, Rosie?" Lily whispered. Her eyes filled with tears. Rosie was her very best doll friend.

Lily decided to search everywhere. She would find Rosie!

First, she looked under the sandbox edge. Maybe Rosie fell out when the train tipped over. Lily got on her hands and knees. She peered under the wooden boards.

She saw a red ball. She saw some old leaves. She saw a plastic spoon. But no Rosie.

"Not here," Lily said sadly. She brushed dirt off her knees.

Next, she looked behind the big oak tree. Maybe Rosie rolled that far. Lily walked around the thick trunk. She moved some fallen branches with her hands.

She found a shiny rock. She found a feather from a blue jay. She found an acorn with its little cap. But still no Rosie.

Lily sat down in the grass. A tear rolled down her cheek. Where could her precious doll be?

Then Lily had another idea. Maybe Rosie was still in the sandbox! Maybe she was buried under all that sand.

Lily ran back to the sandbox. She knelt down where the train had tipped over. The sand felt cool and grainy in her palms.

She started digging with both hands. Scoop, scoop, scoop. Sand flew behind her like a little fountain.

"I won't give up, Rosie," Lily said out loud. "I'll keep looking for you."

She dug deeper. Her hands moved faster. More sand, more digging. Her arms were getting tired, but she didn't stop.

Suddenly, her fingers touched something soft. Something that felt like fabric!

"Rosie!" Lily shouted with joy.

She dug more carefully now. First, she saw a tiny pink shoe. Then a leg. Then Rosie's whole body appeared!

Lily lifted her doll out of the sand. Rosie's curly hair was full of tiny grains. Her pink dress was dusty. But she was safe!

"Oh, Rosie! I found you!" Lily hugged her doll tight. The doll felt warm from the sun and soft like always.

Lily gently brushed the sand from Rosie's hair. She shook out the pretty pink dress. Soon Rosie looked perfect again.

"You must have been scared in all that dark sand," Lily said softly. She kissed Rosie's forehead.

Lily placed Rosie carefully back in the red train. This time, she made sure the doll was sitting safely in the middle.

"Let's go for another ride," Lily said. "But slower this time!"

She pushed the train gently through the smooth sand. The yellow wheels turned slowly. No bumps, no tips, no spills.

"Choo-choo," Lily said quietly. Rosie rode safely in her train car.

Lily smiled as big as the bright sun above. She had worked hard to find her special doll. She didn't give up, even when it seemed impossible.

"I'm so glad I kept trying, Rosie," Lily whispered. "Good things happen when we don't give up."

The train rolled peacefully through the warm sand. Lily and Rosie were together again, ready for many more happy adventures.

The end.
