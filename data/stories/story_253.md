# Grandma's Wiggly Laces

Grandma Rose sat in her cozy living room. Soft morning sunlight streamed through the flowery curtains. Her favorite rocking chair creaked gently. Today felt like a perfect day for a walk.

"Time to put on my walking shoes!" Grandma said cheerfully.

She reached for her bright blue sneakers. They had bouncy white laces and squeaky clean soles. Grandma slipped her feet inside. The shoes felt snug and comfortable.

But when Grandma bent down to tie her laces, something felt different. Her fingers felt stiff and wobbly. The laces seemed extra slippery today.

"Oh my," Grandma whispered. "These laces won't cooperate!"

She tried again. Loop, twist, pull. The laces came undone immediately. They flopped around like wiggly worms.

Grandma's cheeks turned pink with frustration. "Silly laces," she mumbled. "I've tied shoes for seventy years!"

She took a deep breath. The air smelled like her morning coffee. Grandma decided to try a different way.

This time, she sat up very straight. She held one lace in each hand. Slowly, carefully, she made the first loop.

"There!" she said proudly.

But when she reached for the second lace, the first loop disappeared. The laces fell flat again.

Grandma's shoulders drooped a little. Walking outside seemed impossible now. She loved her daily walks around the neighborhood. The fresh air always made her smile.

"I won't give up," Grandma declared firmly.

She looked around her sunny living room. Her eyes spotted something helpful. On the coffee table sat her reading glasses with the pretty purple frames.

"Maybe I need to see better," she thought.

Grandma put on her glasses. Now the laces looked much clearer. She could see exactly where to grab them.

She tried again, moving extra slowly. Loop the right lace. Hold it tight. Wrap the left lace around. Pull it through the hole.

"Almost there," she whispered encouragingly.

But her fingers still felt clumsy. The loops kept getting tangled up.

Grandma noticed her favorite footstool nearby. It was covered in soft, fuzzy fabric. She had an idea!

"My feet need to be higher," she realized.

Grandma placed both feet on the footstool. Now her shoes were much closer to her hands. She didn't have to bend down so far.

With her glasses on and feet up high, Grandma tried once more. Her movements felt steadier now.

Right lace over left lace. Make a loop with the right side. Wrap the left lace around the loop. Push it through the space. Pull both loops tight.

"I did it!" Grandma exclaimed happily.

The first shoe was perfectly tied! The bow looked neat and snug. Grandma wiggled her toes with joy.

She tied the second shoe even faster. Her confidence grew with each twist and pull. Soon both shoes were ready for walking.

Grandma stood up and took a few test steps. Her shoes felt secure and comfortable. The laces stayed tied perfectly.

"Sometimes we need to try different ways," she told herself proudly. "Glasses helped me see. The footstool helped me reach. Taking my time helped me succeed."

She walked to her front door, feeling accomplished. The morning sun beckoned her outside. Birds chirped cheerful songs in the trees.

As Grandma stepped onto her porch, she felt grateful. Her daily walk could happen after all! The neighborhood looked especially beautiful today.

"Every problem has a solution," she said with a warm smile. "We just need to keep trying and thinking creatively."

Grandma Rose walked down her front steps confidently. Her blue shoes carried her forward on another wonderful adventure. The laces stayed tied tight, just like her determination.

Behind her, the living room sat peacefully in the morning light. The rocking chair, footstool, and reading glasses had all helped solve today's puzzle.

Sometimes the best solutions come from looking around and trying new approaches. Grandma had learned that lesson perfectly.

The end.
