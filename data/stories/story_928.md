# Story story_928

**The Little Zebra Who Couldn't See**

Zara was a small zebra who lived on Sunny Farm. Her black and white stripes sparkled in the morning sun. She had the softest mane and the gentlest brown eyes.

Every day, Zara heard wonderful sounds from beyond the tall wooden fence. She heard children laughing and playing. She heard music from a carousel. She heard the cheerful sounds of a county fair.

"What's happening over there?" Zara wondered aloud.

She walked to the fence and stretched her neck up high. But Zara was too short. The fence was much taller than she was. All she could see were the brown wooden boards.

Zara felt sad. Her ears drooped down. "I wish I could see what makes those happy sounds," she sighed.

The other farm animals noticed Zara looking upset. Rosie the pig trotted over with mud on her snout.

"What's wrong, Zara?" Rosie asked kindly.

"I can't see over the fence," Zara explained. "I want to know what's making those wonderful sounds."

Clucky the chicken flapped her wings. "We can help you!" she clucked excitedly.

Benny the goat nodded his head. "Yes! We're your friends. Friends help each other."

Zara's heart felt warmer. Her ears perked up a little.

"I have an idea," said Rosie. "You can stand on my back!"

Zara carefully climbed onto Rosie's round, pink back. Rosie was strong, but she started to wobble. Her little legs shook under Zara's weight.

"This is too hard," puffed Rosie. "You're too heavy for me."

Zara slipped off gently. She felt disappointed again. Her tail swished sadly in the dirt.

"Don't give up," said Benny. "I have a better idea!"

Benny trotted to the old wooden crates near the barn. The crates smelled like sweet hay and fresh apples. He pushed one crate with his strong horns.

"Help me stack these," Benny called.

All the animals worked together. Rosie pushed with her snout. Clucky flapped and encouraged everyone. Even the farm cat, Whiskers, helped by not knocking anything over.

They stacked the crates against the fence. The wood felt rough under Zara's hooves as she climbed up step by step.

"Higher, higher!" cheered Clucky.

Zara climbed to the top crate. Now she was tall enough! She could finally see over the fence.

"Oh my!" Zara gasped with delight.

Beyond the fence was the most magical sight. Colorful tents dotted a green field. A bright red and yellow carousel spun slowly. Children rode painted horses that went up and down. Cotton candy vendors sold fluffy pink treats that looked like sweet clouds.

Families walked hand in hand, laughing and smiling. Balloons in every color floated in the gentle breeze. The air smelled like popcorn and funnel cake.

"It's a carnival!" Zara exclaimed. Her voice was full of wonder and joy.

She watched clowns making balloon animals. She saw people playing games and winning prizes. Everything sparkled and shined in the afternoon sun.

"What do you see?" asked Rosie, jumping up and down.

Zara described every wonderful detail to her friends. They all listened with wide eyes and big smiles.

"Thank you for helping me," Zara said gratefully. "I couldn't have done this alone."

"That's what friends are for," said Benny warmly.

From that day on, whenever Zara heard the carnival sounds, she climbed her special crate tower. Sometimes her friends climbed up too, and they all watched the magic together.

Zara learned that when something seems impossible, trying different ideas can help. She also learned that friends make everything better.

Every evening, as the sun set orange and pink, Zara felt grateful. She was small, but she was clever. She had good friends, and she never gave up.

The little zebra with the sparkling stripes had found her way to see the world beyond the fence.

The end.
