# Ollie the Owl's Sleepover Game

Ollie the owl perched on the windowsill. His big round eyes watched the bedroom. Tonight was Emma's sleepover party. Three friends sat on the soft carpet. They giggled and whispered together.

"Let's play hide and seek!" said Emma. She clapped her hands with joy. The other girls jumped up excitedly. Their socks made soft sounds on the floor.

Ollie fluffed his brown feathers. He wanted to play too. But the girls didn't notice him. They were too busy having fun.

"I'll count first!" Emma announced. She covered her eyes tight. "One, two, three..." Her voice echoed in the cozy room.

The other girls scurried away quickly. One hid behind the pink curtains. Another squeezed under the bed. The third friend crouched in the closet.

Ollie's heart felt heavy and sad. He was right there watching. But nobody asked him to play. His feathers drooped down low.

"Ready or not, here I come!" Emma called out. She opened her bright eyes. She found her friends one by one. They all giggled when they were caught.

"My turn to count!" said Sarah. She spun around three times. Then she covered her eyes tight.

Ollie had an idea suddenly. Maybe he could join the game! He spread his soft wings wide. He glided down from the windowsill. His talons gripped the carpet gently.

Ollie hopped behind the toy chest. He tucked his head down low. His heart beat fast with excitement. Maybe the girls would find him!

"Ready or not, here I come!" Sarah sang out. She looked behind the curtains first. She checked under the fluffy bed. She opened the closet door wide.

But Sarah walked right past Ollie. She didn't see him there. Ollie's shoulders slumped down sadly. His plan didn't work at all.

The girls played three more rounds. Each time, Ollie tried hiding somewhere. Behind the lamp. Under the desk. Next to the bookshelf tall.

But nobody ever found him. Nobody even looked his way. Ollie felt invisible and forgotten. His eyes filled with tears.

Then Ollie heard Emma say something. "I wish we had more players. Three people isn't very many."

"Yeah," agreed Sarah with a sigh. "Four would be much better."

Ollie's ears perked up hopefully. Maybe this was his chance! He took a deep breath. He stepped out from his hiding spot.

"Hoot! Hoot!" Ollie called out softly. He waved one brown wing up high.

All three girls turned around quickly. Their eyes grew wide with surprise.

"Oh my goodness!" Emma gasped loudly. "A real owl in my room!"

"He's so cute and fluffy!" said Sarah. She smiled from ear to ear.

"Look at his big eyes!" the third girl exclaimed. She pointed at Ollie's face.

Ollie's heart jumped with pure joy. Finally, they noticed him there! He hooted again, even louder this time.

"I think he wants to play!" Emma said with excitement. She clapped her hands together happily.

"Can owls play hide and seek?" asked Sarah. She tilted her head curiously.

"Let's find out!" Emma replied cheerfully. "Would you like to play, owl?"

Ollie nodded his head up and down. His whole body wiggled with happiness. His feathers practically glowed with excitement.

"Okay, you count first!" Emma told him. She pointed to the corner.

Ollie flew to the counting spot. He closed his big eyes tight. "Hoot... hoot... hoot..." he counted slowly. Each hoot was one number.

The girls giggled as they hid. They loved his owl counting sounds. It was different and special.

When Ollie finished counting, he opened his eyes. His excellent owl vision helped him. He could see in dark corners. He found all three girls quickly!

"You're really good at this!" Emma laughed. She hugged Ollie's soft feathers gently.

They played until very late. Ollie felt warm and included. He had tried many different ways. Finally, he found the courage. He spoke up for himself.

As the moon shone bright outside, Ollie learned something important. Sometimes you have to be brave. Sometimes you have to ask. Good friends will always make room. There's always space for one more.

The end.
