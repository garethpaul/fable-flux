# Story story_740

Emma's alarm clock buzzed softly. The sun peeked through her pink curtains. It was morning time. Time for breakfast.

"Emma, breakfast is ready!" called Mom from downstairs.

Emma pulled her blanket over her head. She did not want to get up. Her tummy felt funny. Not hungry at all.

"I don't want breakfast," Emma said quietly.

Mom came upstairs with a gentle smile. "What's wrong, sweetie?"

"My tummy doesn't want food," Emma said. "Breakfast feels yucky today."

Mom sat on Emma's bed. The mattress bounced a little. "Sometimes our tummies feel shy in the morning," Mom said. "Maybe we can wake it up together."

Emma peeked out from her blanket. "How do we wake up a tummy?"

"Come see," Mom said with twinkling eyes.

Emma slowly got out of bed. Her bare feet touched the cool wooden floor. She followed Mom downstairs to the kitchen.

The kitchen smelled warm and sweet. Emma saw something new on the counter. A big mixing bowl. Colorful ingredients everywhere.

"We're making rainbow pancakes!" Mom announced.

Emma's eyes grew wide. "Rainbow pancakes?"

"Yes! You can help me make them," Mom said. "First, we mix the batter."

Mom cracked two eggs into the bowl. Crack, crack! Emma watched the yellow yolks plop in. Mom poured white milk. It made a splashing sound.

"Your turn," Mom said. "Stir very slowly."

Emma took the big wooden spoon. She stirred the mixture round and round. The batter became smooth and creamy.

"Now for the magic part," Mom whispered.

Mom brought out small bowls. Each bowl had a different color. Red, blue, yellow, green, and purple food coloring.

"Pick your favorite color," Mom said.

Emma pointed to the purple bowl. "This one!"

Mom added three drops of purple to some batter. The white mixture turned into beautiful purple. Like Emma's favorite flowers.

"Wow!" Emma gasped. "It's magic!"

They made more colored batter. Red like strawberries. Blue like the sky. Yellow like sunshine. Green like grass.

Mom heated the pan. It sizzled when she added butter. The kitchen filled with a yummy smell.

"Pour the purple batter," Mom said.

Emma carefully poured the purple batter into the pan. It bubbled and danced. The pancake looked like a purple cloud.

"My tummy is starting to wake up," Emma said. She felt a little rumble inside.

They made more pancakes. Each one a different color. Emma flipped the yellow one all by herself. It landed perfectly in the pan.

"I did it!" Emma cheered.

Soon they had a stack of rainbow pancakes. Purple, red, blue, yellow, and green. They looked like a edible rainbow on the plate.

Mom put butter on top. It melted into little golden rivers. She drizzled honey over everything. The honey sparkled in the morning light.

Emma sat at the table. The pancakes smelled amazing. Sweet and warm and inviting.

She took a small bite of the purple pancake. It tasted fluffy and sweet. Her tummy rumbled happily.

"These are delicious!" Emma said.

She tried the red pancake next. Then the blue one. Each color tasted wonderful. Her tummy felt warm and happy now.

"Thank you for helping me make breakfast fun," Emma said to Mom.

"You're very welcome," Mom said. "Cooking together makes everything taste better."

Emma ate three whole pancakes. Her tummy felt perfectly full and content.

"Can we make rainbow pancakes again tomorrow?" Emma asked.

"We can make something new and fun every day," Mom promised.

Emma hugged Mom tight. "I love breakfast time with you."

That night, Emma went to bed excited. She couldn't wait for morning to come. Breakfast would be another fun adventure.

Her tummy felt happy too. Ready for whatever delicious surprise tomorrow would bring.

Emma drifted off to sleep with a big smile. Dreams of rainbow pancakes danced in her head.

The end.
