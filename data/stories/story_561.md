# Story story_561

Teddy sat in the sunny field. The grass tickled his fuzzy brown fur. Yellow flowers danced in the warm breeze. But Teddy felt sad and lonely.

He picked up a smooth round stone. He tossed it high into the blue sky. The stone landed with a soft thud. Teddy sighed a big sigh.

"Playing alone is no fun," he said quietly.

Behind a tall sunflower, someone was watching. It was Turtle! Her green shell sparkled in the sunshine. She wanted to play too.

Turtle felt shy and nervous. Her little heart beat fast. She took one slow step forward. Then another step.

"Hello," Turtle said in a tiny voice.

Teddy's brown eyes grew wide with surprise. He had never seen Turtle before. She looked kind and friendly.

"Hi there," Teddy said with a gentle smile. "I'm Teddy."

"I'm Turtle," she replied softly. "Can I play with you?"

Teddy's heart filled with happiness. His sad feelings melted away like ice cream in summer.

"Yes, please!" Teddy said, jumping up. "I was feeling lonely."

Turtle smiled her biggest smile. Her eyes twinkled like stars.

"What should we play?" asked Turtle.

Teddy looked around the pretty field. He saw the colorful flowers. He saw the green grass. He saw fluffy white clouds.

"Let's play hide and seek!" Teddy said excitedly.

Turtle clapped her little hands. "I love that game!"

Teddy covered his eyes with his paws. He counted slowly. "One, two, three, four, five!"

Turtle looked for the perfect hiding spot. She crawled behind a big red poppy. The flower's petals felt soft and silky.

"Ready or not, here I come!" called Teddy.

Teddy searched high and low. He looked behind rocks. He looked under leaves. He could not find Turtle anywhere.

Then he heard a tiny giggle. The sound came from the red poppy. Teddy tiptoed closer.

"Found you!" he said happily.

Turtle popped out, laughing with joy. Her shell was covered in yellow pollen. She looked like a walking sunshine.

"Your turn to hide," Turtle said.

Teddy ran to a cluster of purple violets. He curled up in a fuzzy ball. The flowers smelled sweet like honey.

Turtle counted very slowly. "One, two, three, four, five!"

She moved carefully through the field. Her little legs carried her past butterflies and buzzing bees. She spotted something brown and furry.

"Found you, Teddy!" Turtle called out.

They played hide and seek for hours. The sun warmed their backs. Birds sang cheerful songs above them. Butterflies danced around their heads.

Soon they felt tired and hungry. They sat together on a soft patch of clover. The green leaves felt cool and comfortable.

"I'm so happy you wanted to play," Teddy said.

"Me too," Turtle replied. "I was watching you for a long time."

"Why didn't you come sooner?" asked Teddy.

Turtle looked down at her feet. "I felt scared and shy."

Teddy patted Turtle's shell gently. "You don't need to feel scared with me."

"I know that now," Turtle said with a big grin.

They shared some sweet berries they found. The purple juice stained their lips. They laughed at how silly they looked.

"Will you be my friend?" asked Teddy hopefully.

"Yes!" Turtle said, nodding her head fast. "Best friends forever!"

They made a special friendship promise. They would play together every sunny day. They would help each other when feeling sad.

As the orange sun began to set, they hugged goodbye. Teddy's fur was soft and warm. Turtle's shell was smooth and cool.

"See you tomorrow!" called Teddy.

"Can't wait!" Turtle called back.

Teddy walked home with a happy heart. He was no longer lonely. He had found a wonderful friend.

Turtle smiled all the way to her cozy burrow. She had been brave and made a friend.

The field was quiet now. But tomorrow it would ring with laughter again. Two best friends would play together under the bright sun.

The end.
