# Story story_545

**Lily's Magical Bath Time**

The sun was setting outside Lily's window. Orange and pink colors painted the sky. Mama walked into Lily's room with a warm smile.

"Bath time, little one," Mama said gently.

Lily looked up from her toy blocks. She made a pouty face. "Do I have to?" she asked.

Bath time felt boring to Lily. The water was always too hot or too cold. The soap got in her eyes sometimes. She would rather keep playing with her blocks.

"Come on, sweetie," Mama said. "Let's make it fun tonight."

Lily dragged her feet to the bathroom. The white bathtub looked big and empty. Mama turned on the faucet. Water splashed and gurgled as it filled the tub.

"Can we add bubbles?" Lily asked quietly.

"Of course!" Mama squeezed lavender bubble bath into the water. Sweet purple bubbles started growing everywhere.

Lily dipped her toe in the water. It felt just right - warm like a cozy blanket. She climbed in slowly. The bubbles tickled her arms and legs.

"Look, Mama!" Lily scooped up a handful of bubbles. "They're like little clouds!"

Mama smiled and sat beside the tub. "What else do you see in the bubbles?"

Lily looked closer. The bubbles sparkled in the bathroom light. Some were big like balloons. Others were tiny like pearls. She blew gently on them. They danced in the air like floating snowflakes.

"I see a castle!" Lily pointed to a tall pile of bubbles. "And there's a princess inside!"

"Where?" Mama asked, playing along.

"Right there!" Lily carefully shaped the bubbles with her hands. "She has a crown made of bubbles too!"

The warm water felt wonderful on Lily's skin. She sank deeper into the tub. The bubbles came up to her chin like a fluffy white beard.

"Ho ho ho!" Lily laughed. "I'm Santa Claus!"

Mama giggled. "The bubbliest Santa ever!"

Lily discovered she could make bubble hats. She piled them high on her head. She made bubble gloves for her hands. Soon she was covered in sweet-smelling bubbles from head to toe.

"I'm a bubble monster!" she roared playfully.

"The friendliest bubble monster in the world," Mama said.

Next, Lily tried catching bubbles in a small cup. Pop! Pop! Pop! They burst when she touched them. Each pop made a tiny sound like a whisper.

"They're talking to me!" Lily said with wonder.

"What are they saying?" Mama asked.

"They say... 'Lily is having fun!'" she announced proudly.

Mama brought out Lily's favorite rubber duck. The yellow duck floated between the bubbles like it was swimming in a cloud. Lily made waves with her hands. The duck bobbed up and down on the bubbly water.

"Quack, quack!" Lily made the duck say. "This is the best bath ever!"

When it was time to wash her hair, Mama was extra gentle. She used a special cup to pour water slowly. No soap got in Lily's eyes. The warm water felt like rain in a summer garden.

"All clean!" Mama said, wrapping Lily in a soft, fluffy towel.

The towel was warm from the dryer. It smelled like sunshine and fresh flowers. Lily snuggled into its softness.

"That was magical, Mama," Lily said sleepily.

"Bath time can be an adventure," Mama replied, brushing Lily's damp hair.

Lily put on her cozy pajamas. They were soft and warm against her clean skin. She felt fresh and sleepy and happy.

As Mama tucked her into bed, Lily smiled. "Can we have bubble bath again tomorrow?"

"Every night if you want," Mama promised.

"I love bath time now," Lily whispered.

She closed her eyes and dreamed of bubble castles and floating ducks. In her dreams, she was a bubble princess in a kingdom made of warm water and sweet-smelling clouds.

From that night on, Lily never complained about bath time again. She looked forward to her magical bubble adventures every single evening.

The end.
