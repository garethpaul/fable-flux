# Story story_627

**The Fish in the Attic**

High up in Anna's house was a special attic. Sunlight danced through the round window. Dust sparkles floated like tiny stars.

In the corner sat a big glass bowl. Inside lived Bubbles the goldfish. He swam in slow, sad circles. His orange fins drooped down.

"I wish I had a friend," Bubbles sighed. His voice made tiny bubbles pop at the surface.

Every day, Bubbles played alone. He swam through his little castle. He pushed his tiny treasure chest around. But playing alone felt empty.

Anna loved exploring the attic. She climbed the creaky wooden stairs. Her footsteps went thump, thump, thump.

One sunny morning, Anna discovered Bubbles. "Oh my!" she gasped. "A fish lives here!"

Bubbles saw Anna's kind brown eyes. His fins perked up with hope. Maybe this girl could be his friend.

Anna pressed her nose against the cool glass. "Hello, little fish. You look lonely."

Bubbles swam to the glass. He wiggled his tail in greeting. Anna giggled at his funny dance.

"I want to play with you," Anna said softly. "But how can we play together?"

Anna thought and thought. She tapped her finger on her chin. Then her eyes lit up bright.

"I know!" Anna ran downstairs. Her feet pattered fast on the steps.

She returned with colorful scarves. The silk felt smooth in her hands. She also brought her toy piano.

Anna draped the scarves around Bubbles' bowl. Red, blue, and yellow silk swayed gently. The bowl looked like a magical underwater kingdom.

"Now we can dance together!" Anna announced.

She pressed the piano keys. Soft music filled the attic. The notes floated through the air like butterflies.

Bubbles began to swim in graceful loops. His fins spread wide like tiny wings. Anna twirled around the attic floor.

They danced together for the first time. Anna spun in circles. Bubbles glided in figure-eights. The scarves moved like ocean waves.

"This is wonderful!" Anna laughed. Her voice echoed in the attic.

Bubbles felt warm happiness fill his heart. Finally, he had found a friend.

The next day, Anna brought picture books. She sat cross-legged by Bubbles' bowl. The pages crinkled as she turned them.

"Once upon a time," Anna read aloud. Her voice was gentle and sweet.

Bubbles pressed against the glass. He loved hearing Anna's stories. The words painted pictures in his mind.

Anna brought crayons and paper too. She drew pictures of their adventures. Bubbles swimming through rainbow clouds. Anna riding on a giant fish.

"Look, Bubbles! It's us!" Anna held up her drawing.

Bubbles did a happy flip. His scales sparkled in the sunlight.

Soon, Anna visited every single day. She sang songs to Bubbles. She told him about her dreams. She shared her snacks by the bowl.

Bubbles showed Anna his best swimming tricks. He made heart shapes with his tail. He played peek-a-boo behind his castle.

One afternoon, Anna felt sad. She had argued with her sister. Tears rolled down her cheeks.

Bubbles saw Anna's sadness. He swam very slowly and gently. His movements were like a warm hug.

"Thank you, Bubbles," Anna whispered. "You always make me feel better."

Their friendship grew stronger each day. Anna learned that friends don't have to be the same. Bubbles learned that friendship feels wonderful.

The attic became their special place. Sunlight still danced through the window. But now it felt warm and full of love.

Anna brought a small sign. She had written "Bubbles and Anna's Clubhouse" in purple crayon.

She taped it to Bubbles' bowl. "Now everyone knows we're best friends!"

Bubbles swam in the happiest circles ever. His fins stood up proud and tall.

From that day on, they were never lonely. They had found something precious. They had found each other.

The attic filled with laughter and joy. Two friends, so different but so close. Playing together in their magical space above the world.

The end.
