# Story story_895

**Ellie's Missing Blocks**

Ellie the elephant loved playing at Sunshine Playground. Her favorite spot was the big sandbox. She kept her special blocks there every day.

The blocks were bright red and yellow. They felt smooth in her trunk. Ellie loved stacking them high into towers.

But today something was wrong. Ellie looked in the sandbox. No blocks anywhere! She dug with her trunk. Still no blocks.

"Oh no!" said Ellie. Her voice sounded sad. "Where are my blocks?"

Ellie's friend Benny Bear heard her. He ran over on his little legs.

"What's wrong, Ellie?" asked Benny.

"My blocks are gone," said Ellie. A tiny tear rolled down her gray cheek. "I can't build my tower today."

Benny patted Ellie's trunk gently. "Don't worry. We'll find them together."

Ellie thought hard. Maybe the wind blew them away. The playground was big and grassy.

"Let's look by the swings," said Ellie.

They walked to the swing set. Ellie used her trunk to search. She looked under each swing. She checked behind the tall poles.

No red blocks. No yellow blocks either.

"Maybe they're by the slide," said Benny.

They hurried to the curvy slide. It was tall and shiny. Ellie looked all around the bottom. She checked in the mulch chips.

Still no blocks anywhere.

Ellie sat down with a thump. The ground felt hard under her bottom. She felt very disappointed.

"This isn't working," said Ellie. Her trunk drooped sadly.

Benny sat beside his friend. "Don't give up yet, Ellie."

Then Benny had a good idea. "Let's ask other friends for help."

Ellie lifted her head. "That's smart thinking, Benny!"

They walked around the playground together. They saw Rosie Rabbit by the monkey bars.

"Hi Rosie," called Ellie. "Have you seen my red and yellow blocks?"

Rosie's nose twitched as she thought. "I saw Maya Monkey playing with blocks this morning."

Ellie's heart felt lighter. "Where is Maya now?"

"She's in the big oak tree," said Rosie.

Ellie and Benny looked up. The tree was very tall. Its leaves rustled in the breeze.

"Maya!" called Ellie loudly. "Are you up there?"

A little brown head peeked down. "Hi Ellie! Hi Benny!"

Maya swung down on a branch. She landed softly on the grass.

"Maya," said Ellie hopefully. "Do you have my blocks?"

Maya's face turned red. She looked at her feet. "I borrowed them this morning. I'm sorry, Ellie."

"Where are they now?" asked Benny kindly.

"In my tree house," said Maya quietly. "I forgot to bring them back."

Maya climbed up quickly. Soon she tossed down the blocks. They landed in the soft grass with gentle thuds.

Ellie picked up each block carefully. They felt wonderful in her trunk again. The red ones were warm from the sun. The yellow ones smelled like fresh leaves.

"Thank you for returning them," said Ellie.

"I'm really sorry I took them," said Maya. "Next time I'll ask first."

"That's okay," said Ellie kindly. "Friends make mistakes sometimes."

All three friends walked back to the sandbox. Ellie began building her tower. She stacked each block carefully.

"This is fun," said Benny. "Can I help?"

"Me too?" asked Maya hopefully.

"Of course!" said Ellie happily. "We can build together."

They built the tallest tower ever. It had red blocks and yellow blocks. It sparkled in the afternoon sunshine.

Ellie felt proud and happy. She had found her blocks by not giving up. Asking friends for help was a good idea.

"I learned something today," said Ellie.

"What's that?" asked her friends.

"When something is hard, don't give up. Ask friends to help. Together we can solve any problem."

The three friends played until sunset. Their tower stood tall and strong. Just like their friendship.

From that day on, they always shared their toys. The playground felt even more special when they played together.

Ellie never worried about lost toys again. She knew her good friends would always help her find them.

The end.
