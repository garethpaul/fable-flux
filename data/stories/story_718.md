# Story story_718

**Daisy Duck's Messy Attic**

Daisy Duck lived in a cozy attic above Farmer Brown's red barn. Sunlight streamed through the round window. Dust particles danced in the golden beams.

But Daisy's attic was very messy. Toy blocks covered the wooden floor. Stuffed animals sat everywhere. Picture books lay open and scattered. Puzzle pieces hid under her soft blue blanket.

"Oh my!" said Daisy, looking around. "I can't even see my favorite rug!"

Mama Duck called from downstairs. "Daisy! Your cousin Danny is coming to play!"

Daisy's orange feet went tap-tap-tap on the floor. She was excited to see Danny. But her room was too messy for a friend to visit.

"I need to clean up," Daisy said. "But there are so many toys!"

She looked at the big pile of blocks. She looked at the scattered books. She looked at all the puzzle pieces.

"This is too hard," Daisy sighed. "Maybe I can just hide everything!"

Daisy grabbed her biggest blanket. It was soft and yellow like sunshine. She threw it over the pile of blocks. Then she threw another blanket over the books.

"Perfect!" she said. "Now everything looks clean!"

But when Daisy walked across the room, she tripped. THUMP! She fell right over the hidden blocks.

"Ouch!" said Daisy, rubbing her wing. "That didn't work at all!"

The blankets slipped off. All the toys showed again. The attic looked messier than before.

Daisy sat down and felt sad. A tear rolled down her yellow feek. "I'll never get this clean in time," she whispered.

Then Daisy remembered something important. Mama Duck always said, "Big jobs are easier when you take small steps."

"Maybe I can clean one thing at a time," Daisy said.

She stood up and brushed off her feathers. "I'll start with just the blocks."

Daisy picked up one red block. Then one blue block. Then one green block. She stacked them carefully in their special box.

"That's better!" she said, smiling.

Next, Daisy gathered all the puzzle pieces. She found them under the blanket, behind her pillow, and near the window. Each piece went into its own puzzle box.

"I'm getting good at this!" Daisy quacked happily.

The picture books came next. Daisy put the animal books on one shelf. The fairy tale books went on another shelf. The counting books stood together too.

Finally, Daisy arranged her stuffed animals. The teddy bear sat by her bed. The toy rabbit went on the windowsill. The little elephant found a spot on her pillow.

Daisy looked around her clean attic. The wooden floor sparkled in the sunlight. Her favorite rug showed its pretty flower pattern. Everything had its special place.

"I did it!" Daisy said, flapping her wings with joy.

Just then, she heard footsteps on the ladder. Danny Duck's head appeared through the attic opening.

"Wow, Daisy!" said Danny. "Your room looks wonderful!"

"Thank you," said Daisy proudly. "I cleaned it all by myself!"

The two ducks played with blocks. They read stories together. They worked on puzzles. They had tea parties with the stuffed animals.

When it was time for Danny to go home, he hugged Daisy tight.

"This was the best playdate ever," Danny said. "Your clean room made everything more fun!"

After Danny left, Daisy looked around her tidy attic. She felt proud and happy inside.

"Mama was right," Daisy said to herself. "Big jobs really are easier when you take small steps!"

From that day on, Daisy cleaned up her toys every night before bed. She put away one thing at a time. Her attic stayed neat and cozy.

And whenever friends came to visit, Daisy's room was always ready for fun and games.

The golden sunlight still danced through her window. But now it shined on a clean, happy space where a little duck had learned that she could do hard things, one small step at a time.

The end.
