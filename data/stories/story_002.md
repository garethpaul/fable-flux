# Emma's Magical Bath Time

It was time for bath time, and little Emma was not happy about it at all. She sat on her bedroom floor playing with her colorful blocks, building a tall tower that reached almost to her knees. When Mama called from the bathroom, "Emma, sweetie, bath time!" Emma's face scrunched up like a raisin.

"I don't want to take a bath," Emma grumbled, crossing her arms tightly across her chest. "I want to keep playing with my blocks. Baths are boring and wet and I don't like getting my hair washed." She stomped her little feet on the soft carpet and shook her head so hard that her curly brown hair bounced everywhere.

Mama came into the room with a gentle smile. "I know you'd rather play, Emma, but we need to get clean before bedtime. Come on, let's go see what's waiting in the bathtub today." Mama held out her warm hand, and even though Emma didn't want to, she slowly took it and walked toward the bathroom.

The bathroom was filled with warm, steamy air that felt cozy on Emma's skin. The bathtub was full of perfectly warm water that sparkled under the bright lights. But Emma still felt grumpy about stopping her fun game to get clean.

"I still don't want to take a bath," Emma said with a big frown, standing next to the tub in her pajamas. "Can't I just play for five more minutes? Or maybe ten more minutes? Or maybe forever more minutes?"

Mama chuckled softly and reached for Emma's favorite soap - the special soap that smelled like strawberries and came in a pretty pink bottle. "Let's just start washing your hands," Mama said, squirting a little bit of the soap onto Emma's palms.

But then something absolutely wonderful and amazing happened! As soon as Emma rubbed her hands together with the soap, instead of regular white bubbles, the most beautiful rainbow bubbles began to appear! Red bubbles and orange bubbles and yellow bubbles floated up toward the ceiling like tiny flying rainbows.

"Whoa!" gasped Emma, her eyes growing as wide as saucers. "Mama, look! The bubbles are rainbow colors! How did they do that? Are they magic?" She held up her soapy hands and watched in wonder as more colorful bubbles danced through the air.

"They must be very special bubbles today," said Mama with a mysterious smile. "What should we do with them?"

Emma forgot all about being grumpy. She clapped her hands together and laughed as purple and green bubbles floated around her head like a bubble crown. "I want to pop them! I want to catch them! I want to make more!" she squealed with delight.

Emma quickly took off her pajamas and climbed into the warm bathtub. The water felt perfect - not too hot and not too cold, but just right for a little girl. She squirted more of the magical soap into the water and watched as hundreds of rainbow bubbles filled the entire bathroom.

"Look, Mama! I'm a bubble princess!" Emma announced, piling bubbles on top of her head like a fluffy, colorful hat. Blue bubbles stuck to her arms, yellow bubbles covered her knees, and pink bubbles floated all around her like she was sitting in a rainbow cloud.

Emma played with the magical bubbles for a long time. She blew on them gently and watched them float away. She caught them in her cupped hands and giggled when they popped with tiny "pop!" sounds. She even tried to count them, but there were too many! "One, two, three, four, five... oh my, there are millions!" she laughed.

When it was time to wash her hair, Emma didn't mind at all because the shampoo made even more rainbow bubbles. She pretended she was a mermaid swimming in a sea of colors, and Mama helped her rinse off with warm water that felt like a gentle rain shower.

After bath time was over and Emma was wrapped in her soft, fluffy towel, she looked at Mama with sparkling eyes. "That was the most fun bath ever!" she said, bouncing on her toes. "The rainbow bubbles were so pretty and magical! Can we have rainbow bubbles again tomorrow?"

"We'll have to wait and see what kind of bubbles tomorrow brings," said Mama, drying Emma's hair gently.

Bath time became Emma's favorite part of the day! From that night on, she would run to the bathroom when Mama called, excited to see what wonderful surprises might be waiting in the bathtub. Sometimes there were rainbow bubbles, sometimes there were bubbles that smelled like flowers, and sometimes there were bubbles that glittered like stars.

Emma can't wait for bath time tomorrow. She wonders what magical things will happen next, and she knows that every day holds new surprises when you keep your heart open to wonder and joy.

The end.
