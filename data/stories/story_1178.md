# Story story_1178

**Penny Pig's Magic Nap**

The sun was high in the sky. It was nap time at the farm. All the animals were getting sleepy. Cows lay down in the soft grass. Chickens tucked their heads under their wings. Even the busy bees stopped buzzing.

But Penny Pig did not want to nap.

"I'm not tired," Penny said to Mama Pig. She stomped her little hooves. "Nap time is boring!"

Mama Pig smiled. "Every little pig needs rest," she said gently. "Come to your cozy bed."

Penny's bed was in the barn. It had soft, yellow straw. It smelled like sunshine and flowers. But Penny still didn't want to sleep.

"I want to play," Penny whined. She rolled in the mud instead. The cool mud felt good on her pink skin. But she still felt grumpy.

Mama Pig led Penny to the barn. "Just try to rest," she said. "Close your eyes."

Penny flopped on her straw bed. She squeezed her eyes shut tight. But sleep would not come. She wiggled her tail. She wiggled her nose. She wiggled her ears.

Then something amazing happened.

A tiny, sparkly butterfly flew into the barn. It had wings like rainbows. Purple, blue, and gold colors danced in the sunlight.

"Hello, little pig," the butterfly whispered. Its voice sounded like tiny bells. "I'm Luna, the Dream Butterfly."

Penny's eyes grew wide. "A talking butterfly!"

Luna landed on Penny's snout. Her wings tickled. "I bring special dreams to sleepy animals," Luna said. "But only if they close their eyes."

"What kind of dreams?" Penny asked. She was very curious now.

"Magic dreams," Luna said. "Dreams where you can fly. Dreams where you can swim with dolphins. Dreams where you eat clouds that taste like cotton candy."

Penny's tummy rumbled. Cotton candy clouds sounded wonderful.

"Will you show me?" Penny asked softly.

Luna smiled. "Close your eyes. Take three deep breaths. Think happy thoughts."

Penny closed her eyes. She breathed in slowly. The straw smelled sweet. She breathed out slowly. Her body felt warm and cozy.

Luna's wings made soft, musical sounds. Like a gentle lullaby. The butterfly sprinkled magic dream dust around Penny.

Soon, Penny was flying! She soared over green fields. She danced with other flying pigs in the clouds. The clouds really did taste like cotton candy! Pink and fluffy and sweet.

Then she was swimming in a crystal blue ocean. Friendly dolphins played with her. They splashed and laughed together. The water felt like silk.

Penny dreamed she was a brave explorer. She found a treasure chest full of the juiciest apples. She shared them with all her farm friends.

When Penny woke up, the sun was lower. Golden light filled the barn. She felt wonderful. Her body was rested. Her mind was happy.

Luna was still there. "Did you like your dreams?" the butterfly asked.

"They were amazing!" Penny said. She stretched her legs. "I felt like I went on the best adventure ever."

"Dreams make nap time special," Luna said. "Rest helps your body grow strong. Dreams help your imagination grow too."

Penny nodded. She understood now. "Thank you, Luna."

The butterfly fluttered her rainbow wings. "Sweet dreams, little pig." Then she flew away through the barn window.

Mama Pig came to check on Penny. "How was your nap, sweetie?"

"It was magical!" Penny said. She told Mama about her flying dreams and cotton candy clouds.

Mama Pig hugged her tight. "Dreams are wonderful gifts," she said.

That evening, Penny played happily with her friends. She told them about Luna and her amazing dreams. But when bedtime came, she was ready.

"I can't wait for tomorrow's nap," Penny said to Mama. "I wonder what dreams Luna will bring next."

Mama Pig tucked her in with extra straw. "Sweet dreams, my little dreamer."

Penny closed her eyes with a big smile. Nap time wasn't boring anymore. It was the most magical time of all.

The end.
