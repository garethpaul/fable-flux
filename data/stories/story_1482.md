# Story story_1482

**Ellie and the Hallway Game**

Ellie the elephant walked slowly down the long hallway. Her big feet made soft thumping sounds on the wooden floor. The hallway was quiet and empty.

Ellie felt lonely. She wanted someone to play with. The hallway seemed so long when she was all alone.

She picked up her favorite red ball. Ellie rolled it down the hallway. The ball bounced off the wall and rolled back to her. She caught it with her trunk.

"This is fun," Ellie said softly. But she wished someone else was there too.

Ellie rolled the ball again. This time it went faster. It bounced twice before coming back. She giggled a little bit.

From around the corner came her little brother Eddie. Eddie was smaller than Ellie. His ears were not as big yet. His trunk was shorter too.

"What are you doing, Ellie?" Eddie asked. His voice was curious and excited.

"I'm playing ball in the hallway," Ellie said. "But it's more fun with friends."

Eddie's eyes grew wide and bright. "Can I play too? Please?"

Ellie looked at her little brother. She smiled a big elephant smile. "Of course you can play!"

Eddie clapped his little hands together. "Yay! What do we do?"

"First, we roll the ball," Ellie explained. "Then we catch it when it comes back."

Ellie showed Eddie how to hold the ball with his trunk. Eddie's trunk was wiggly. The ball almost fell.

"Try again," Ellie said gently. "You can do it."

Eddie wrapped his trunk around the ball more carefully. This time he held it tight.

"Good job!" Ellie cheered.

Eddie rolled the ball down the hallway. It went crooked and bumped the wall. Both elephants laughed.

"My turn!" Ellie said. She rolled the ball straight and smooth.

The ball came back to them. Eddie caught it! Well, almost. The ball bounced off his trunk, but he grabbed it quickly.

"I caught it!" Eddie squealed with joy.

"You did!" Ellie said proudly.

They took turns rolling the ball. Sometimes it went straight. Sometimes it went sideways. They didn't care. They were having fun together.

"Let's try something new," Ellie suggested. "Let's both roll the ball at the same time."

They each got a ball. Ellie had her red ball. Eddie found a blue ball in the corner.

"Ready?" Ellie asked.

"Ready!" Eddie replied.

They rolled their balls together. The balls crashed into each other in the middle of the hallway. Both balls bounced in different directions.

Ellie and Eddie giggled so hard their trunks shook. They ran after the bouncing balls.

"That was silly!" Eddie laughed.

"Let's do it again!" Ellie said.

They played the crashing ball game over and over. Each time was funnier than the last.

Soon they were both tired. They sat down in the hallway together. The warm sunlight came through the window. It made pretty patterns on the floor.

"This was the best day ever," Eddie said. He leaned against his big sister.

"Playing together is much better than playing alone," Ellie agreed. She put her trunk around Eddie gently.

"Can we play again tomorrow?" Eddie asked hopefully.

"We can play every day," Ellie promised. "Brothers and sisters make the best playmates."

Eddie smiled up at Ellie. "I love playing with you."

"I love playing with you too," Ellie said warmly.

They picked up their balls together. The hallway didn't seem long anymore. It seemed just right for two elephants to play in.

As they walked back to their room, their feet made happy thumping sounds. The hallway echoed with their laughter and chatter.

From that day on, Ellie was never lonely in the hallway again. She had her brother Eddie to play with. And Eddie had learned that big sisters make wonderful friends.

The hallway became their special place. A place where an elephant and her brother could play, laugh, and be together. And that made everything perfect.

The end.
