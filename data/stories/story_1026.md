# Story story_1026

**Lily and the Tall New Friend**

Lily sat on the warm sand. The beach was big and sunny. She built a small sandcastle all by herself. The waves made soft whooshing sounds nearby.

"I wish I had someone to play with," Lily said quietly. She patted her sandcastle with her little shovel. The sand felt cool and bumpy under her hands.

Behind the tall palm trees, something moved. A very long neck poked out. Then a friendly face appeared. It was a young giraffe with brown spots!

The giraffe stepped onto the beach carefully. His long legs made big footprints in the sand. He looked at Lily with kind, dark eyes.

"Hello," said the giraffe softly. "I'm Patches. Can I play too?"

Lily looked up, up, up at the tall giraffe. "You're so big! I'm just little."

Patches felt sad. His ears drooped down. "I know I'm different. But I really want to be friends."

Lily saw how sad Patches looked. She stood up and brushed sand off her hands. "What games do you like?"

Patches smiled. His whole face lit up. "I love building things! And I can reach really high places."

"That sounds fun!" Lily said. "Let's build the biggest sandcastle ever!"

They started working together. Lily dug holes in the wet sand. Patches used his long neck to gather seaweed from far away. The seaweed smelled salty and felt slippery.

"This can be our flag!" Patches said. He placed the green seaweed on top of their castle.

Lily clapped her hands. "Perfect! What else should we add?"

Patches looked around the beach. He spotted pretty pink shells near the water. "I'll get those shells. They're too far for you."

While Patches collected shells, Lily made windows in their castle. She used her finger to draw shapes in the sand. The castle was getting bigger and better.

"Here are the shells," Patches said. He gently dropped them near Lily. The shells were smooth and shiny. They made tiny clicking sounds.

Lily arranged the shells around their castle. "Now it looks like a real palace!"

The sun felt warm on their backs. A gentle breeze made the palm trees whisper. Seagulls called from high above.

"Want to play in the water?" Lily asked.

Patches nodded excitedly. They ran to the shallow waves together. Well, Lily ran and Patches took big, careful steps.

The water was cool and tickly on their feet. Little waves splashed against their legs. Lily giggled as the foam bubbled around her toes.

Patches used his long neck like a periscope. "I can see fish swimming down there!"

"Really?" Lily tried to look into the clear water.

Patches bent down low. "Climb on my back! Then you can see too."

Lily climbed up carefully. From up high, she could see everything! Tiny silver fish swam in circles. Colorful shells dotted the sandy bottom.

"This is amazing!" Lily laughed. "Thank you, Patches!"

They played in the water until the sun started getting lower. Then they walked back to their sandcastle.

"I'm so glad you wanted to play," Lily said. "You're the best friend ever."

Patches felt warm and happy inside. "Really? Even though I'm so tall and different?"

"That's what makes you special!" Lily said. "You can reach high things and see far away. And you're really kind."

They sat together by their beautiful castle. The sunset painted the sky pink and orange. The waves sang their gentle song.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" Lily asked.

"Of course!" Patches said. "Friends always come back."

They watched the stars appear in the darkening sky. Their sandcastle stood proudly beside them. The beach felt magical with friendship filling the air.

"Best day ever," whispered Lily.

"Best day ever," agreed Patches.

And from that day on, they met at the beach every morning. The tall giraffe and the little girl became the very best of friends. Their laughter could be heard mixing with the ocean waves, creating the happiest sound in the whole wide world.

The end.
