# Tommy the Little Blue Train Learns to Clean Up

Tommy was a bright blue toy train with shiny red wheels. He lived in the bathroom with lots of other toys. There were rubber ducks, colorful boats, and floating sea animals. Tommy loved bath time adventures!

Every evening, Tommy zoomed around the bathtub. "Choo-choo!" he called happily. The rubber ducks quacked along. The boats bobbed up and down. Splash, splash, splash went the water everywhere.

But when bath time ended, something happened. All the toys stayed scattered around. Wet ducks sat on the floor. Boats floated in puddles. Sea animals hid behind the toilet.

"Time to clean up, Tommy!" called Mama from the hallway.

Tommy's wheels stopped moving. His bright smile turned into a frown. "I don't want to clean up," he whispered. "It's too much work. It's too hard."

Tommy looked around the messy bathroom. There were so many toys! His engine felt tired just thinking about it. "Maybe Mama will do it," he thought hopefully.

But Mama was busy. The toys stayed scattered everywhere. Tommy felt sad looking at the mess.

The next morning, something terrible happened. Tommy couldn't find his favorite friend, Ducky! "Where is Ducky?" Tommy cried. He looked and looked. Ducky was lost in the big mess!

Tommy's red wheels started turning slowly. "I need to find Ducky," he said. "But how?"

First, Tommy tried pushing all the toys together. Push, push, push! But they just made a bigger pile. "This isn't working," Tommy sighed.

Next, Tommy tried asking for help. "Boats, can you help me?" The boats just floated quietly. "Sea animals, will you help?" They didn't answer either.

Tommy felt frustrated. His blue paint seemed less shiny. Then he had an idea. "What if I clean up one toy at a time?"

Tommy picked up one rubber duck. He carried it carefully to the toy basket. Plop! In it went. "That wasn't so hard," Tommy said, surprised.

He picked up another duck. Plop! Into the basket. Then another. Plop! "This is actually fun!" Tommy discovered.

One by one, Tommy cleaned up each toy. His wheels moved faster now. His engine hummed a happy tune. "Choo-choo! Clean-up time!" he sang.

As Tommy worked, something wonderful happened. The bathroom looked better and better. The floor became clear. The puddles dried up. Everything found its proper place.

And then... "Ducky!" Tommy shouted with joy. There was his yellow friend, hiding behind the towels! "I found you!"

Ducky quacked happily. "Thank you for cleaning up, Tommy! Now we can play together again!"

Tommy felt proud and happy. His blue paint sparkled in the bathroom light. "Cleaning up wasn't so hard after all," he realized. "I just needed to try one step at a time."

That evening, when bath time ended again, Tommy knew what to do. "Come on, friends!" he called to all the toys. "Let's clean up together!"

This time, Tommy started right away. One toy, then another, then another. His wheels moved confidently. His whistle tooted cheerfully.

Soon, all the other toys joined in. The ducks waddled to their basket. The boats sailed to their shelf. The sea animals swam to their special box.

"Look how beautiful our bathroom is!" Tommy said proudly. Everything was clean and organized. Everything was in its place.

Mama peeked into the bathroom. "Wow, Tommy! You did such a good job cleaning up!" she said with a big smile.

Tommy's engine puffed with pride. "I learned something important today," he told Ducky. "Big jobs aren't scary when you do them one small step at a time."

From that day on, Tommy always cleaned up after bath time. He discovered that taking care of his space made him feel happy and proud. And best of all, he never lost his friends in the mess again!

"Choo-choo!" Tommy whistled contentedly. "Clean-up time is the best time!"

The end.
