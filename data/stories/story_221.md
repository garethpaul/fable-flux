# Mama's Magical Bath Time

It was seven o'clock. Time for bath time. Mama looked at the bathtub. She felt very tired today.

"Bath time again," Mama sighed softly. She turned on the warm water. The faucet made a gentle whooshing sound.

Mama had given so many baths. Bath time for baby. Bath time for the dog. But Mama's own bath time? That felt different somehow.

"I'm too tired for this," she whispered. The warm water kept flowing. Steam began to rise up.

Mama reached for her favorite soap. It smelled like lavender flowers. She poured some bubble bath too. Just a little bit.

Suddenly, something wonderful happened. Tiny bubbles started dancing! They sparkled in the bathroom light. They looked like little stars.

"Oh my!" Mama said with surprise. More bubbles appeared. Big ones and small ones. They floated everywhere!

One bubble landed on Mama's nose. It tickled! She giggled like a little girl. Another bubble popped near her ear. 

*Pop! Pop! Pop!*

The bubbles made happy sounds. Mama smiled her first real smile. The bathroom felt magical now.

She stepped into the warm water. "Ahhhh," Mama said peacefully. The water hugged her tired body. Her shoulders felt better already.

The bubbles kept dancing around her. Some bubbles were purple. Some were pink. Others looked like tiny rainbows.

"You're beautiful," Mama told the bubbles. They seemed to dance even more. Like they were happy too!

Mama picked up her soft washcloth. She dipped it in the bubbly water. The cloth felt like a cloud. Warm and gentle.

She washed her arms slowly. She washed her legs carefully. Each bubble that popped made her smile. The warm water made her feel peaceful.

"This is wonderful," Mama said out loud. Her voice echoed softly. The bathroom felt like a special place. A place just for her.

More bubbles appeared from nowhere. They swirled around like tiny dancers. Some bubbles stuck together. They made funny shapes!

"That one looks like a heart," Mama laughed. "And that one looks like a star!" The bubbles seemed to hear her. They made even sillier shapes.

Mama closed her eyes. She listened to the gentle sounds. Water lapping softly. Bubbles popping quietly. Her own peaceful breathing.

"I forgot how nice this feels," she whispered. The warm water melted her worries away. Her tired muscles relaxed completely.

She opened her eyes again. The bubbles were still there. Still dancing their magical dance. The bathroom glowed with soft light.

Mama washed her hair gently. The shampoo smelled like flowers. Bubbles covered her head like a funny hat. She laughed again.

"I look silly!" she said happily. But feeling silly felt wonderful. When did she stop being silly?

The magical bubbles began to fade. But Mama's smile stayed bright. She felt clean and fresh. Most importantly, she felt happy.

She stepped out slowly. The fluffy towel felt amazing. Soft and warm against her skin. Like a gentle hug.

Mama looked in the mirror. Her eyes sparkled now. Her cheeks looked rosy. She looked like herself again. Her best self.

"That was magical," she said softly. The last few bubbles popped goodbye. *Pop! Pop! Pop!*

Mama dried off carefully. She put on her coziest pajamas. They felt extra soft tonight. Everything felt better now.

She drained the bathtub slowly. The water swirled down happily. Like it was dancing too. The bubbles waved goodbye.

"Thank you," Mama whispered to her bathroom. It felt like saying thank you to an old friend.

As Mama brushed her teeth, she smiled. Her whole body felt relaxed. Her heart felt light and happy.

She turned off the bathroom light. But the magical feeling stayed. It followed her to bed.

"Tomorrow is bath time again," Mama thought sleepily. But now she felt excited! Would the dancing bubbles come back? Would new magic happen?

"I can't wait," she whispered into her pillow. And for the first time ever, Mama truly meant it. Bath time had become her favorite time.

Tomorrow would bring another magical bath. Another chance to dance with bubbles. Another moment just for her.

Mama fell asleep smiling. Dreams of dancing bubbles filled her night.

The end.
