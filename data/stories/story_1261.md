# Story story_1261

**Penny the Puppet's Big Problem**

Penny was a cheerful little puppet. She lived in a bright playroom. The playroom had soft blue walls. Colorful toys filled every corner.

Penny loved to help. She wanted to make the playroom tidy. Every morning, she would pick up toys. She would put them away carefully.

One sunny morning, Penny looked around. Toys were everywhere! Red blocks sat on the floor. A yellow truck was under the table. Three stuffed bears were by the window. A purple ball rolled near the door.

"Oh my!" said Penny. "So many toys to clean up!"

Penny tried to pick up everything at once. She grabbed the blocks with her tiny puppet hands. She tucked the truck under her arm. She squeezed the bears against her chest. She tried to balance the ball on top.

But Penny's hands were very small. The toys were heavy for a little puppet. The blocks slipped through her fingers. CRASH! They fell to the floor. The truck tumbled down. THUMP! The bears dropped with soft thuds. The ball bounced away. BOING, BOING, BOING!

Penny felt sad. Her button eyes looked down. "I can't carry them all," she whispered. "My hands are too little."

Penny sat on the soft carpet. She felt like giving up. The toys seemed so big. She felt so small.

Then Penny remembered something important. Her friend Max the teddy bear always said, "Try again, but try differently."

Penny stood up straight. She brushed off her colorful dress. "I will find another way," she said with determination.

Penny looked around the playroom. She spotted something useful. A bright red wagon sat in the corner! It had four round wheels. It had a long handle to pull.

"The wagon can help me!" Penny said excitedly.

Penny pulled the wagon over. It made a happy rolling sound. The wheels turned smoothly on the carpet.

First, she picked up one red block. She placed it gently in the wagon. Then another block. And another. The blocks made soft clicking sounds as they touched.

Next, Penny lifted the yellow truck. It was heavy, but she could manage one toy at a time. Into the wagon it went. The truck fit perfectly next to the blocks.

The three stuffed bears were next. Penny picked up the brown bear first. It was soft and squishy. She placed it carefully in the wagon. Then the white bear. Finally, the golden bear. They all snuggled together nicely.

Last was the purple ball. Penny caught it as it rolled by. She placed it on top of everything else. It sat like a perfect purple crown.

"I did it!" Penny cheered. Her voice echoed happily in the playroom.

Penny pulled the wagon across the room. The wheels rolled smoothly. The toys stayed safe inside. She felt proud and strong.

She put each toy in its special place. The blocks went in their wooden box. The truck went on the toy shelf. The bears went to their cozy corner. The ball went in the sports bin.

The playroom looked beautiful and neat. Sunlight streamed through the windows. Everything sparkled and shone.

Penny smiled her biggest puppet smile. "I couldn't carry everything at once," she said. "But I found a better way!"

Just then, Max the teddy bear walked over. "You did wonderful work, Penny," he said warmly. "You didn't give up when things got hard."

"The wagon helped me," Penny said. "And thinking of a new way helped too."

Max nodded wisely. "Sometimes the best solution isn't the first one we try."

Penny felt happy and proud. She had learned something important. When one way doesn't work, try another way. Keep trying until you succeed.

The playroom was clean and bright. All the toys were in their homes. And Penny had discovered that being small didn't mean she couldn't do big things.

From that day on, Penny always used her wagon. She helped keep the playroom tidy every single day. And whenever other toys had big problems, they came to Penny. She always reminded them: "Try again, but try differently!"

The end.
