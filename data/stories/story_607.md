# Story story_607

**Moo-Moo's Shoe Problem**

Moo-Moo the cow loved the playground. She loved the swings that went whoosh in the wind. She loved the slide that was smooth and shiny. Most of all, she loved running with her friends.

Today was sunny and warm. Moo-Moo put on her favorite red shoes. She looked down at the long white laces. They were supposed to be tied in bows. Instead, they hung loose like wiggly worms.

"Oh no," said Moo-Moo. "My laces are untied again!"

She tried to run to the swings. Trip! Her hoof caught in a lace. She stumbled and sat down hard on the soft grass.

"Ouch," she said, rubbing her bottom.

Bunny hopped over. "What's wrong, Moo-Moo?"

"I can't tie my shoes," Moo-Moo said sadly. "Every time I try to run, I trip."

"Don't worry," said Bunny. "I'll help you!"

Bunny sat next to Moo-Moo. She picked up the white laces. "First, you make an X," Bunny explained. "Then you pull one lace under."

Moo-Moo watched carefully. Bunny's small paws moved quickly. The laces twisted and turned.

"Now you make bunny ears," said Bunny. "See? Like this!"

But Moo-Moo's hooves were big and round. When she tried to hold the laces, they slipped through her hooves like water.

"I can't do it," Moo-Moo moaned. "My hooves are too big."

She tried again. The laces fell apart. She tried once more. The laces got tangled in a messy knot.

"This is too hard," Moo-Moo said. Tears filled her brown eyes.

Wise old Owl hooted from the oak tree. "Moo-Moo, come here please."

Moo-Moo walked slowly to the tree. Her untied laces dragged on the ground.

"I heard about your problem," said Owl kindly. "Sometimes we need to find our own special way."

"What do you mean?" asked Moo-Moo.

"Your hooves are different from Bunny's paws," Owl explained. "Maybe you need a different way to tie shoes."

Owl flew down with a thick piece of rope. "Let's practice with this first. It's bigger and easier to hold."

Moo-Moo sat on the wooden bench. Owl showed her a new way. "Hold one end with your left hoof. Hold the other end with your right hoof."

Moo-Moo could grip the thick rope easily.

"Now cross them over," said Owl. "Good! Push one end through the hole."

Moo-Moo pushed the rope through. It worked!

"Pull tight," said Owl. "Perfect! Now make two loops."

Moo-Moo made two big loops with the rope. Her hooves could hold them well.

"Cross the loops," said Owl. "Push one through. Pull tight!"

Moo-Moo did exactly what Owl said. She made a beautiful bow with the rope!

"I did it!" Moo-Moo cheered. "I really did it!"

"Now try with your shoe laces," said Owl.

Moo-Moo looked at her white laces. They seemed less scary now. She held one lace in each hoof. Cross over. Push through. Pull tight.

Then she made two loops. Her hooves gripped them carefully. Cross the loops. Push through. Pull tight!

A perfect bow appeared on her red shoe!

"Hooray!" shouted all her friends.

Moo-Moo tied her other shoe the same way. Both bows looked neat and tight.

She stood up and took a careful step. Then another step. The laces stayed tied!

"I can run now!" Moo-Moo said happily.

She ran to the swings. Her shoes stayed on perfectly. She ran to the slide. No tripping at all!

"Thank you, Owl," Moo-Moo called. "You helped me find my special way."

"You did the hard work," Owl hooted back. "You didn't give up."

Moo-Moo played all afternoon. Her red shoes with perfect bows carried her everywhere. She felt proud and happy.

When it was time to go home, Moo-Moo looked at her shoes. The bows were still tight and neat.

"Tomorrow I'll teach other friends," she decided. "Everyone can learn their own special way."

Moo-Moo walked home with a big smile. Her shoes were tied, and her heart was full of joy.

The end.
