# Story story_1494

**Woolly's New Friend**

Woolly the sheep lived in a big green field. The grass was soft under her hooves. She loved to run and jump all day. But Woolly played alone. She felt lonely sometimes.

Every morning, Woolly would bounce around the field. She would roll down the little hill. The sun felt warm on her fluffy white wool. Birds sang pretty songs in the trees. But Woolly wished she had a friend to play with.

One sunny day, Woolly saw something new. A little brown rabbit sat by the fence. The rabbit had long ears and a twitchy nose. His name was Benny.

"Hello," said Benny softly. "I'm new here. Can I play with you?"

Woolly felt excited but also shy. "I don't know how to play with a rabbit," she said. "I'm just a sheep."

Benny hopped closer. "We can learn together," he said with a smile.

First, Woolly tried to hop like Benny. Boing, boing, boing! But her legs were too short. She tumbled in the grass. "This is too hard," Woolly sighed.

"Let me try something you like," said Benny. He tried to run fast like Woolly. But his little legs couldn't keep up. He fell behind, panting hard.

Both friends sat in the shade feeling sad. "Maybe we're too different," said Woolly.

Benny's ears drooped down. "Maybe you're right," he whispered.

Then Woolly had an idea. "What if we don't copy each other? What if we play our own way?"

Benny's ears perked up. "What do you mean?"

"You can hop," said Woolly. "I can run. Let's play tag!"

Benny's eyes sparkled. "Yes! Let's try!"

Woolly started running around the field. Her hooves made soft sounds on the grass. Benny hopped after her, bouncing high and low. Soon they were both laughing.

"Tag! You're it!" called Benny as he touched Woolly's wool.

Now Woolly chased Benny. She ran in big circles. Benny hopped in zigzags. They played until they were both tired and happy.

Next, they found a muddy puddle from yesterday's rain. Woolly loved splashing in water. Benny liked getting his paws wet too. Splash, splash, splash! They made big muddy prints everywhere.

"This is so much fun!" laughed Woolly. Water drops sparkled on her wool.

"The best!" agreed Benny. His brown fur had muddy spots now.

When they got thirsty, they shared cool water from the stream. The water tasted fresh and clean. They sat together in the soft grass.

"I was wrong," said Woolly. "Being different makes playing more fun."

Benny nodded. "We don't have to be the same to be friends."

As the sun got lower, they played hide and seek. Benny hid behind small rocks. Woolly hid behind big trees. They took turns finding each other.

"Found you!" Woolly would call out happily.

"Your turn to hide!" Benny would giggle.

The other animals in the field watched them play. The cows mooed approval. The chickens clucked with joy. Everyone loved seeing the new friendship.

When evening came, Woolly and Benny were very tired. They lay down together under a shady tree. The grass smelled sweet around them.

"Will you play with me tomorrow?" asked Woolly.

"Every day," promised Benny. "Best friends always play together."

Woolly felt so happy. Her wool felt extra fluffy. Her heart felt warm and full. She wasn't lonely anymore.

From that day on, Woolly and Benny played together every single day. They ran and hopped. They splashed and explored. They shared snacks and told stories.

All the animals in the field smiled when they saw them. The best friendships happen when you accept each other just as you are.

Woolly learned that being different was actually wonderful. Benny learned that friendship means caring, not copying.

And they both learned that the very best games happen when friends bring their own special gifts to play.

The field was never quiet again. It was filled with laughter, friendship, and joy every single day.

The end.
