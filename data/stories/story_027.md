# Miko and the Little Blue Boat

Deep in the green forest, a little monkey named Miko sat on a mossy rock. He picked up smooth stones. He tossed them into the babbling creek. *Plop, plop, plop* went the stones.

But Miko felt sad inside. Playing alone wasn't very fun. He wished he had a friend.

"I want someone to play with," Miko sighed. His fuzzy brown tail drooped down. The forest felt too quiet.

Just then, something bright caught his eye. A cheerful blue boat came floating down the creek. The boat had a white sail that fluttered in the breeze. Two happy eyes were painted on its front.

"Hello there!" called the little boat. "I'm Blu! What's your name?"

Miko's eyes grew wide. "You can talk? I'm Miko!"

"Of course I can talk!" Blu giggled. The sound was like tiny bells ringing. "I've been floating all alone. It gets lonely on the water."

Miko's heart jumped with excitement. "Really? I've been playing alone too!"

Blu bobbed up and down in the water. "Would you like to be friends? We could play together!"

"Yes, yes, yes!" Miko clapped his hands. He had never met a talking boat before.

But then Miko looked puzzled. "How can we play? You're in the water. I'm on land."

Blu thought for a moment. His little sail swished back and forth. "I have an idea! You could ride on me!"

Miko felt nervous. "I don't know how to sail."

"That's okay," Blu said kindly. "I know how to float. You know how to balance. Together we can do it!"

Carefully, Miko stepped onto Blu's deck. He wobbled a little bit. Blu stayed very still to help.

"You're doing great!" Blu encouraged. "Now sit down and hold my mast."

Miko sat cross-legged on the warm deck. He wrapped his long arms around the tall mast. It felt smooth and strong.

"Ready for an adventure?" Blu asked.

"Ready!" Miko grinned.

Off they went down the winding creek. The water gurgled beneath them. Green leaves hung down from the trees above. Colorful fish swam alongside them.

"Look at those pretty fish!" Miko pointed.

"Let's count them together!" Blu suggested.

"One, two, three, four, five!" they counted. The fish seemed to dance in the clear water.

Miko spotted low-hanging branches ahead. "Duck!" he called out.

Blu steered carefully around them. "Thanks for watching out!"

"Thanks for the fun ride!" Miko replied.

They worked together perfectly. When the creek got narrow, Miko pushed off rocks with his hands. When the current got strong, Blu used his sail to stay steady.

"This is so much better than playing alone," Miko said happily.

"I agree!" Blu laughed. "Everything is more fun with a friend."

They sailed past a family of ducks. The ducks quacked hello. Miko and Blu waved back with joy.

Soon they reached a quiet pond. Lily pads floated like green plates. Dragonflies buzzed around with shimmery wings.

"Let's play a game!" Miko suggested. "I'll toss pebbles. You try to catch them!"

"What a great idea!" Blu spun in a happy circle.

Miko threw small pebbles gently. Blu moved left and right to catch them. *Splash!* went the pebbles when they missed. They both giggled every time.

When they caught three in a row, they cheered together. "Hooray for teamwork!"

As the sun began to set, they floated quietly. The sky turned pink and orange. Everything felt peaceful and warm.

"Miko," Blu said softly, "I'm so glad we met."

"Me too, Blu," Miko smiled. "You're the best friend ever."

"Want to play again tomorrow?" Blu asked hopefully.

"Every day!" Miko promised. "We make a great team!"

They sailed back home as the first stars appeared. Miko felt his heart full of happiness. He wasn't lonely anymore.

The forest didn't feel too quiet now. It felt full of friendship and fun. And tomorrow would bring another wonderful adventure with his new best friend, Blu the boat.

From that day on, Miko and Blu played together every single day. And they lived happily ever after.

The end.
