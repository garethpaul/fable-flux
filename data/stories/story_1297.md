# Story story_1297

**The Lost Red Ball**

Tommy loved his little sister Lily very much. Every morning, he walked her to the nursery classroom. Today felt different though.

Lily sat on the colorful carpet. Her bottom lip stuck out. Big tears rolled down her cheeks.

"What's wrong, Lily?" Tommy asked softly.

"My red ball is gone!" Lily sniffled. "I can't find it anywhere!"

Tommy knew how special that ball was. Grandma gave it to Lily for her birthday. It bounced so high and felt smooth in her hands. The bright red color sparkled like a fire truck.

"Don't worry," Tommy said. "I'll help you look."

Tommy searched under the toy box first. He moved aside wooden blocks and stuffed animals. Dust tickles made him sneeze. But no red ball.

He looked behind the bookshelf next. Picture books tumbled down. The pages made soft whooshing sounds. Still no red ball.

Tommy even checked inside the dress-up box. Sparkly princess dresses and pirate hats spilled out. The box smelled like lavender soap. The red ball wasn't there either.

"It's really gone," Lily whispered. More tears fell.

Tommy felt sad seeing his sister cry. But he didn't give up. Good big brothers never give up.

"Let's think harder," Tommy said. "Where did you play with it last?"

Lily wiped her nose with her sleeve. "I remember! We played catch outside yesterday."

Tommy's eyes grew wide. "The playground! Maybe it rolled outside."

They asked Mrs. Chen, their teacher, for help. She smiled kindly and nodded. "Let's all go look together."

Outside, the morning sun felt warm on their faces. Birds chirped happy songs in the tall oak trees. The grass smelled fresh from last night's rain.

Tommy looked under the slide first. Only wet leaves and a wiggly worm lived there. The worm said hello by stretching long and thin.

Lily checked near the swings. The chains clinked softly in the breeze. But no red ball swayed there.

Mrs. Chen searched by the fence. Purple flowers grew wild and tall. Bees buzzed busily from bloom to bloom. The red ball wasn't hiding there.

Tommy felt worried. Maybe the ball was really lost forever. But then he remembered something important. Sometimes the best ideas come when you stop and think.

He closed his eyes tight. He remembered yesterday clearly now. Lily threw the ball really high. It bounced once, twice, then rolled fast down the small hill.

"The hill!" Tommy shouted. "It rolled down the hill!"

They all ran to the grassy slope. At the very bottom sat a storm drain. The dark opening looked like a sleepy giant's mouth.

Tommy got on his hands and knees. He peered into the shadowy drain. His voice echoed when he called out.

"I see something red!" he yelled excitedly.

Mrs. Chen had a long net for cleaning the sandbox. Tommy held one end carefully. Mrs. Chen guided the other end into the drain.

Scrape, scrape, scrape went the net against the concrete. Then something round rolled toward them.

"My ball!" Lily squealed with joy.

The red ball looked a little dirty but perfectly fine. Lily hugged it tight against her chest. It still felt smooth and wonderful.

"Thank you, Tommy!" Lily said. "You're the best big brother ever!"

Tommy felt proud and happy inside. His chest puffed up like a balloon. Helping others always felt better than anything else.

"I didn't give up," Tommy said with a big smile. "When something important is lost, we keep looking until we find it."

Lily bounced her ball three times. Bounce, bounce, bounce. Each bounce sounded like a tiny drum.

"Want to play catch?" she asked Tommy.

"Always," Tommy replied.

They played together in the sunshine. The red ball flew back and forth between them. Their laughter mixed with the singing birds and buzzing bees.

Sometimes problems seem really big and scary. But with patience, thinking, and help from people who love us, we can solve almost anything. Tommy learned that being a good big brother meant never giving up on the people you love.

The red ball bounced happily ever after.

The end.
