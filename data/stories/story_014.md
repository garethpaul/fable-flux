# Mimi's Magical Story Time

Mimi Mouse yawned and stretched her tiny paws. The afternoon sun streamed through the window. It made golden squares on the living room rug.

"Story time, Mimi!" called Mama Mouse from the cozy reading corner. She patted the soft blue cushions beside her.

Mimi's whiskers twitched. She looked at her toy blocks. She looked at her puzzle pieces. Story time happened every day at three o'clock. But today, Mimi wanted to keep playing.

"Do we have to read today?" Mimi asked. Her voice was small and uncertain. "I want to build my castle higher."

Mama Mouse smiled warmly. "Stories are waiting for us, little one. Come sit with me."

Mimi's tiny feet dragged across the carpet. She climbed onto the soft cushions. The reading corner smelled like lavender and cookies. Mama's warm arm wrapped around her.

"Which book today?" Mama asked. She pointed to the colorful books on the low shelf.

Mimi shrugged her small shoulders. "I don't know. They're all the same."

Mama picked up a red book. It had a golden dragon on the cover. "Let's try this one," she said gently.

As Mama opened the book, something wonderful happened. The pages seemed to shimmer. The pictures looked brighter than before. Mimi blinked her dark eyes.

"Once upon a time," Mama began, "a brave little dragon lived in a crystal cave."

Suddenly, Mimi could hear something. Soft whooshing sounds filled the air. It sounded like dragon wings! She looked around the room. Everything looked normal. But the sounds continued.

Mama turned the page. The dragon was flying over a purple mountain. Mimi gasped. She could feel a gentle breeze! It ruffled her fur softly.

"The dragon searched for the magic flower," Mama continued. Her voice was musical and warm.

Mimi leaned forward. Her heart beat faster. She could smell something sweet. It smelled like honey and sunshine. The scent seemed to come from the book itself.

"There!" Mimi pointed at the page. "I see sparkles around the flower!"

Mama smiled mysteriously. "Do you really see them?"

Mimi nodded eagerly. Tiny golden sparkles danced around the illustrated flower. They twinkled like real stars. The sparkles moved when she blinked.

As the story continued, more magic happened. When the dragon flew through clouds, Mimi felt soft mist on her cheeks. When he landed in a forest, she heard birds singing beautiful songs.

"The dragon found the magic flower," Mama read. "It granted one special wish."

"What did he wish for?" Mimi asked breathlessly. She was sitting on the edge of the cushion now.

"He wished for a friend," Mama said softly.

Suddenly, a tiny light appeared on the page. It grew bigger and brighter. Mimi watched in wonder as a small, friendly dragon stepped out of the book!

The little dragon was no bigger than Mimi's paw. His scales shimmered like rainbows. He smiled at Mimi with kind eyes.

"Hello, Mimi," the dragon whispered. His voice sounded like tinkling bells. "Thank you for believing in magic."

Mimi's mouth opened wide. "You're real!"

The dragon nodded. "Stories make everything real. When you listen with your heart, magic happens."

The tiny dragon flew in a circle around Mimi. He left a trail of golden dust. Then he flew back into the book with a gentle pop.

Mama closed the book slowly. "Did you enjoy our story today?"

Mimi hugged the book tight. Her eyes sparkled with excitement. "That was amazing! The dragon was really here!"

"Stories have special magic," Mama said. "But only for those who truly listen."

Mimi looked at all the books on the shelf. Each one seemed to glow softly. She wondered what magic lived inside them.

"Mama," Mimi said, bouncing on the cushions. "Can we read another story tomorrow?"

"Of course, sweetheart," Mama laughed. "Stories are waiting for us every day."

Mimi snuggled closer to Mama. Story time wasn't boring anymore. It was the most magical part of her day. She couldn't wait to discover what adventures tomorrow would bring.

The reading corner felt warm and perfect. Mimi closed her eyes and dreamed of dragons, magic flowers, and all the wonderful stories yet to come.

The end.
