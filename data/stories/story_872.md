# Story story_872

Jack looked at the kitchen clock. The big hand pointed to twelve. It was lunch time.

"Jack, come eat your lunch!" called Mom from the kitchen.

Jack dragged his feet slowly. He did not want to eat lunch. His sandwich looked boring. The carrots seemed too crunchy. Even his favorite apple juice did not look good.

"I'm not hungry," Jack said with a frown.

Mom smiled gently. "Your body needs food to grow big and strong."

Jack sat at the table. He poked his sandwich with one finger. The bread felt soft and squishy. He made a sad face.

"What if we made lunch more fun?" asked Mom.

Jack looked up. His eyes were curious now.

Mom opened the kitchen drawer. She pulled out cookie cutters shaped like stars and hearts. They were shiny and silver.

"We can cut your sandwich into fun shapes," Mom said.

Jack's eyes grew wide. "Really?"

"Really," Mom nodded.

Jack picked up the star cookie cutter. He pressed it into his sandwich. The bread made a soft squishing sound. When he lifted the cutter, a perfect star appeared.

"Wow!" Jack giggled. "It looks magical!"

He cut another star. Then a heart. Soon his plate had five sandwich stars and three sandwich hearts.

"Now the carrots," said Mom. She gave Jack a small cup of ranch dressing. "You can dip them like french fries."

Jack picked up an orange carrot stick. He dipped it in the creamy white sauce. Crunch! It tasted sweet and fresh.

"These taste like candy carrots!" Jack laughed.

Mom brought over a special plate. It had different sections like a puzzle. "Let's make a rainbow lunch," she said.

Jack put his orange carrots in one section. His red apple slices went in another. The yellow cheese from his sandwich made the plate even more colorful.

"Look, Mom! It's like a rainbow!" Jack clapped his hands.

He took a bite of his star sandwich. The peanut butter was creamy and smooth. The jelly was sweet like summer berries. It tasted much better now.

"Yum!" Jack said with his mouth full.

He crunched on more carrots. He sipped his apple juice slowly. The cold liquid felt refreshing on his tongue.

"This is the best lunch ever," Jack said happily.

Mom sat down next to him. "What made it so special?"

Jack thought for a moment. "The shapes made it fun. The colors made it pretty. And you helped me."

"Food tastes better when we're happy," Mom said.

Jack finished all his star sandwiches. He ate every carrot stick. He even drank all his apple juice.

"My tummy feels good now," Jack said, patting his belly.

"That's because you gave your body good fuel," Mom explained.

Jack helped Mom clean up the dishes. The warm soapy water felt nice on his hands. He dried each plate carefully.

"Mom, can we make shapes tomorrow too?" Jack asked hopefully.

"Of course! What shapes should we try?"

"Maybe circles and triangles!" Jack bounced excitedly.

"That sounds perfect," Mom agreed.

Jack hugged Mom tight. She smelled like vanilla and sunshine.

"Thank you for making lunch magical," he whispered.

The next morning, Jack woke up early. He ran to the kitchen in his pajamas.

"Is it lunch time yet?" he asked Mom.

Mom laughed. "Not yet, sweetie. But after breakfast and playtime."

Jack could hardly wait. He kept looking at the clock. He helped Mom make breakfast. He played with his toys. But he kept thinking about lunch.

When the big hand pointed to twelve again, Jack cheered.

"Lunch time! Lunch time!" he sang happily.

He raced to the kitchen table. Today felt like another magical adventure was about to begin.

"What shapes shall we make today?" Mom asked, holding up the cookie cutters.

Jack grinned from ear to ear. Lunch had become his favorite time of day.

The end.
