# Story story_811

**The Little Red Truck and the Big Swing Set**

Little Red Truck loved playing in the backyard. He rolled around the soft green grass. He pushed toy cars with his bumper. But today, something was very wrong.

Red Truck had gotten stuck. His front wheels were tangled in the swing set chains. The metal chains wrapped around and around his tires. He couldn't move forward. He couldn't move backward.

"Oh no," whispered Red Truck. His engine made worried puttering sounds.

The swing set was much bigger than him. It had tall metal poles and long chains. The swings hung down like giant arms. Red Truck felt very small underneath it.

He could hear children playing in the front yard. Their happy voices drifted over the fence. But Red Truck was too shy to call for help.

"I can do this myself," he said quietly.

Red Truck tried his first solution. He revved his engine really loud. VROOM! VROOM! He pushed forward with all his might. The chains got tighter around his wheels. They squeezed like a hug that was too tight.

"Ow, ow, ow!" cried Red Truck. The chains pinched his rubber tires. Now he was stuck even worse than before.

Red Truck's headlights drooped sadly. A tear rolled down his shiny red hood. The sun felt hot on his roof. The grass smelled sweet around him. But he couldn't enjoy anything while he was trapped.

He heard footsteps coming closer. It was Emma, his favorite little girl. She had curly brown hair and wore a yellow dress with flowers.

Red Truck wanted to ask for help. But the words got stuck in his throat. What if Emma thought he was silly? What if she laughed at him?

Emma walked right past the swing set. She didn't see Red Truck hiding underneath. His heart felt heavy like a rock.

"I have to try again," Red Truck told himself.

This time, he tried something different. Instead of pushing forward, he turned his wheels left and right. Slowly, carefully, he wiggled back and forth. The chains started to loosen just a tiny bit.

"It's working!" he whispered excitedly.

Red Truck kept wiggling. Left, right, left, right. The chains made soft clinking sounds as they moved. A gentle breeze blew through the backyard. It smelled like fresh flowers and cut grass.

But then Emma came back. She was looking for her jump rope. Red Truck froze. His engine stopped puttering.

Emma looked under the swing set. Her eyes grew wide when she saw Red Truck.

"Oh my goodness!" she said softly. "You're all tangled up!"

Red Truck's engine made a tiny peep. He was so embarrassed. But Emma's voice was kind and gentle.

"Don't worry, little truck," Emma said. "I'll help you."

She knelt down in the soft grass. Her small fingers worked carefully. She untangled one chain, then another. The metal felt cool in her hands.

"There you go," Emma said as the last chain came free.

Red Truck rolled backward slowly. His wheels turned smoothly again. He felt so much better!

"Thank you, Emma," Red Truck said in his quietest voice.

Emma smiled big. "You're welcome! Next time you need help, just honk your horn. I'll always come running."

Red Truck's headlights brightened. His engine purred happily. He had learned something important today.

Sometimes trying hard by yourself is good. But sometimes asking for help is even better. Friends want to help each other.

Red Truck honked his horn twice. Beep! Beep! It was his way of saying "I love you" to Emma.

From that day on, Red Truck wasn't shy about asking for help. He knew that being brave enough to ask was just as important as being brave enough to try.

And Emma always listened for his little horn. Because that's what friends do.

Red Truck rolled happily through the grass. The sun warmed his red paint. The breeze felt wonderful. And best of all, he wasn't stuck anymore.

The end.
