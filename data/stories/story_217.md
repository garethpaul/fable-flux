# Penny's Perfect Game

In a cozy nursery filled with soft toys and colorful books, lived a little blue bird named Penny. She had bright yellow feet and the tiniest chirp. Penny loved watching the children play every day.

The nursery was always busy and fun. Red blocks sat in tall towers. Soft teddy bears lined the windowsill. Rainbow books filled the wooden shelves. But Penny's favorite thing was watching the games.

One sunny morning, three children started playing. Emma had curly brown hair. Sam wore a striped shirt. Little Maya giggled as she clapped her hands.

"Let's play the clapping game!" said Emma.

The children sat in a circle. They clapped their hands together. *Clap, clap, clap!* They sang a happy song. They laughed and smiled together.

Penny watched from her perch. Her little heart felt sad. She wanted to join the fun game. But how could a tiny bird play?

"I want to play too," chirped Penny softly.

The children didn't hear her small voice. They kept clapping and singing. Penny felt left out and lonely.

Penny thought hard about the problem. Maybe she could clap too! She spread her little wings wide. She flapped them together. *Flap, flap, flap!*

But her wings made no clapping sound. The children still didn't notice her. Penny's wings felt tired and droopy.

"That didn't work," sighed Penny.

Next, Penny tried to sing louder. She puffed up her tiny chest. She opened her small beak wide. "CHIRP! CHIRP! CHIRP!"

Her chirps were still very quiet. The children kept playing their game. They didn't hear Penny's little song.

Penny felt even sadder now. Her throat felt scratchy from chirping. Maybe she would never join in.

Then Penny had another idea. Maybe she could dance! She hopped from foot to foot. Her yellow feet tapped on the perch. *Tap, tap, tap!*

The children were too busy laughing. They didn't see Penny's dance. Penny stopped hopping and hung her head.

"Nothing works," whispered Penny sadly.

Just then, Maya looked around the room. Her eyes sparkled with joy. She pointed up at Penny's perch.

"Look! A pretty blue bird!" said Maya.

Emma and Sam looked up too. They saw Penny sitting quietly. Her blue feathers looked very sad.

"She looks lonely," said Emma gently.

"Maybe she wants to play," said Sam.

Maya had a wonderful idea. She smiled at Penny. "Would you like to play with us?"

Penny's heart jumped with happiness. She chirped a tiny "Yes!"

"I know!" said Emma excitedly. "Let's make music together!"

The children started clapping again. *Clap, clap, clap!* This time, Penny flapped her wings. *Flap, flap, flap!*

"Your wings make pretty sounds!" said Maya.

Sam tapped his feet on the floor. *Stomp, stomp, stomp!* Penny tapped her yellow feet too. *Tap, tap, tap!*

"We're all making music!" laughed Emma.

Then something magical happened. The children sang their song. Penny chirped along with her sweetest voice. All the sounds mixed together beautifully.

The clapping, flapping, stomping, and tapping made perfect music. Penny's tiny chirps added the sweetest notes. Everyone was part of the same wonderful game.

"This is the best game ever!" said Maya.

"Penny makes it extra special," agreed Sam.

Penny's heart felt warm and full. She had found her own way to play. Her wings, feet, and voice were perfect for music-making.

From that day on, Penny always joined the games. Sometimes she flapped. Sometimes she tapped. Sometimes she chirped sweet songs.

The children loved having Penny as their friend. She learned that being different made her special. Her unique sounds made their games even better.

Penny never felt left out again. She had discovered that trying different ways leads to wonderful surprises. The nursery was filled with friendship, music, and joy.

Every game was more fun with everyone included. Penny's little heart sang with happiness. She had found her perfect place in the perfect game.
