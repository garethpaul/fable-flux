# Story story_1368

**The Little Blue Boat**

Tommy sat on the kitchen floor. His little blue boat floated in a big mixing bowl. The water was warm and bubbly. Tommy pushed the boat around and around.

"Sail away, little boat," Tommy whispered. But playing alone felt quiet. The kitchen seemed too big. Tommy wished someone would play with him.

His big brother Sam walked into the kitchen. Sam was seven years old. He saw Tommy sitting by himself. The little blue boat bobbed in the soapy water.

"What are you doing?" Sam asked. He sat down next to Tommy. The kitchen tiles felt cool under his knees.

"Playing boats," Tommy said softly. "But it's not very fun alone." Tommy's voice sounded sad. He looked at his boat floating by itself.

Sam watched the little blue boat. It had white sails and a red stripe. The boat looked lonely too. It sailed in circles with no friends.

"Can I play?" Sam asked. His eyes lit up with an idea. "I could make another boat!"

Tommy's face brightened. "Really? You want to play with me?" Tommy had been hoping Sam would join him. Big brothers were usually too busy.

Sam nodded and smiled. He looked around the kitchen. "I'll find something to float." Sam opened a drawer. He pulled out a plastic container. It was yellow and shaped like a banana.

"This can be my boat!" Sam said. He filled another bowl with warm water. The water smelled like soap bubbles. Sam placed his yellow boat next to Tommy's blue one.

"Now we both have boats," Tommy giggled. He clapped his hands together. The sound echoed in the sunny kitchen.

The brothers began to play. They made wave sounds with their mouths. "Whoosh! Splash!" they said together. Their boats sailed side by side.

"My boat is going to the island," Sam announced. He pushed his yellow boat across the water. It made tiny ripples that tickled the bowl's edges.

"Mine too!" Tommy said excitedly. He guided his blue boat next to Sam's. "They can be boat friends!" The two boats bumped gently together.

Sam had another idea. "Let's make a storm!" He wiggled his fingers in the water. The boats rocked back and forth. Water splashed softly on the counter.

"Oh no!" Tommy laughed. "The boats need to help each other!" He moved his blue boat closer to Sam's yellow one. "Friends stick together in storms."

The brothers played for a long time. They created islands with kitchen sponges. They made harbors with measuring cups. The boats sailed everywhere together.

Their mom walked into the kitchen. She saw the boys sitting close together. Water puddles sparkled on the floor. Two boats floated peacefully in their bowls.

"You two look happy," Mom said warmly. She smiled at her sons. The kitchen felt full of joy and laughter.

"We're boat captains!" Tommy announced proudly. He held up his little blue boat. Water dripped from its tiny hull.

"Best boat captains ever," Sam added. He put his arm around Tommy's shoulders. "We make a great team."

Tommy felt warm inside. Playing with Sam was much better than playing alone. The kitchen didn't seem big anymore. It felt cozy and perfect for two boat captains.

"Can we play boats again tomorrow?" Tommy asked hopefully. He looked at Sam with bright, happy eyes.

"Every day if you want," Sam promised. He meant it too. Playing with his little brother was more fun than he expected.

The two boats sat side by side in their bowls. Just like Tommy and Sam sat side by side on the floor. The brothers had discovered something wonderful. Playing together was twice as fun as playing alone.

From that day on, the kitchen became their special place. Two boats, two brothers, and endless adventures waiting to happen. The little blue boat was never lonely again.

The end.
