# Story story_851

**Zara's Garden Adventure**

The morning sun peeked through Zara's window. "Time for garden time!" called Mama Zebra cheerfully. Zara pulled her striped blanket over her head. She did not want to get up.

"I don't like garden time," Zara mumbled. Her hooves felt heavy. The dirt was always messy. The sun was too bright. And pulling weeds was hard work.

Mama Zebra gently nudged her little zebra. "Come on, sweetie. The garden needs us today." Zara slowly got out of bed. She dragged her hooves across the floor. Everything felt too difficult.

Outside, the garden waited. Rows of green plants stretched across the yard. Zara wrinkled her nose. "It smells funny out here," she complained. The earthy smell of soil filled the air.

"Let's start with watering," said Mama. She handed Zara a small red watering can. The can felt cold in Zara's hooves. Water sloshed inside as she walked.

Zara poured water on the tomato plants. Drip, drip, splash! Some water landed on her hooves. It felt cool and refreshing. She giggled a tiny bit.

"Now let's check the carrots," Mama suggested. They walked to the orange rows. Green tops poked up from the brown dirt. Zara touched one gently. The leaves felt soft and feathery.

Suddenly, something moved in the soil! A small green worm wiggled near the carrots. "Look, Mama!" Zara called excitedly. "A tiny friend!"

The worm curled and uncurled. It made little tunnels in the dirt. "Worms help our garden grow," Mama explained. "They make the soil healthy and strong."

Zara watched the worm with wonder. She had never noticed garden creatures before. "Are there more friends here?" she asked. Her voice sounded curious now.

They looked around together. A fuzzy bee buzzed near the sunflowers. Its wings made a gentle humming sound. Colorful butterflies danced between the bean plants. Their wings sparkled in the sunshine.

"The garden is full of helpers," Mama said softly. A ladybug crawled across a lettuce leaf. Its red shell had tiny black spots. Zara counted them carefully. "One, two, three, four spots!"

Next, they picked ripe strawberries. The red berries felt warm from the sun. Zara popped one in her mouth. Sweet juice burst across her tongue. "Mmm! This tastes like summer!" she exclaimed.

Her hooves were dirty now. But Zara didn't mind anymore. The soil felt soft between her toes. She even liked the earthy smell. It reminded her of rain and growing things.

"Can we plant something new?" Zara asked hopefully. Mama smiled widely. "Of course! What would you like to grow?"

They chose purple flower seeds. Zara dug small holes with her hooves. She dropped seeds into each hole carefully. Then she covered them with gentle pats of soil.

"How long until they grow?" Zara wondered. She sprinkled water on the planted seeds. The drops made tiny dark circles in the dirt.

"A few weeks," Mama answered. "Gardens teach us to be patient. Good things take time to grow."

Zara looked around at their work. The plants looked happy and healthy. Water droplets sparkled on green leaves. The whole garden seemed to shimmer and dance.

A butterfly landed on Zara's nose! She stayed very still. Its wings tickled softly. She felt connected to all the growing things around her.

"I like garden time now," Zara admitted. Her heart felt warm and full. "It's like taking care of magical friends."

As they walked inside, Zara's hooves left muddy prints. Usually this worried her. Today, she felt proud of her dirty feet. They showed she had worked hard.

"Can we do garden time again tomorrow?" Zara asked eagerly. She was already planning what to check first. Maybe the worm would be back. Maybe new butterflies would visit.

"Every morning," Mama promised. "Our garden friends will be waiting."

That night, Zara dreamed of growing flowers. She couldn't wait for tomorrow's garden adventure. Her heart felt as bright as sunshine.

The end.
