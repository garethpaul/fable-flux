# Story story_1434

Lily sat on her bedroom floor. She felt very lonely today. Mama was busy downstairs. Papa was at work. Her toys lay scattered around her. But nothing felt fun.

She picked up her favorite doll. "Hello, Emma," she whispered. But Emma just stared back quietly. Lily sighed and put the doll down.

The closet door stood open wide. Inside hung her pretty dresses. Her shoes sat in neat rows. But something else was there too.

A soft white sheep peeked out! It had fluffy wool and kind black eyes. The sheep looked at Lily hopefully.

"Oh!" Lily gasped. "A sheep in my closet!"

The sheep stepped forward carefully. Its hooves made tiny clicking sounds. "Baa," it said gently. "I'm Woolly. Can I play?"

Lily's eyes grew wide with wonder. "You can talk!"

"Only to special children," Woolly said. "Children who need a friend."

Lily felt her heart warm up. "I do need a friend. I feel so lonely."

Woolly's tail wagged like a happy puppy. "Then let's play together!"

First, they had a tea party. Lily set out her tiny cups. Woolly sat very carefully on a cushion. His wool was so soft and white.

"More tea, Woolly?" Lily asked politely.

"Yes, please," Woolly said. He pretended to sip carefully. "This is delicious!"

Lily giggled. The lonely feeling started going away.

Next, they played dress-up. Lily put a pink hat on Woolly's head. She tied a purple scarf around his neck. Woolly looked very fancy!

"I feel beautiful," Woolly said proudly.

"You look wonderful!" Lily clapped her hands.

They danced around the room together. Woolly's hooves tapped the floor. Lily spun in circles. Her dress twirled like a flower.

"This is so much fun!" Lily laughed.

Then they built a fort. They used pillows and blankets. Woolly helped carry the soft pillows. His mouth was gentle and careful.

Inside their cozy fort, they told stories. Woolly told tales about cloud jumping. Lily shared stories about her dreams.

"I dreamed I could fly," Lily said.

"Maybe we can fly together," Woolly suggested.

They pretended to soar around the room. Woolly's wool felt like touching clouds. Lily felt light as a feather.

When they got tired, they cuddled up. Woolly's wool was warm and fluffy. Lily felt safe and happy.

"Woolly," she said softly. "Will you be my best friend?"

"Forever and always," Woolly promised. "Best friends stick together."

Lily hugged Woolly tight. His wool smelled like fresh meadows. His heart beat steady and calm.

"I'm not lonely anymore," Lily whispered.

"Me neither," Woolly said. "Friends are never lonely."

They heard Mama calling for dinner. "Come eat, Lily!"

"I have to go," Lily said sadly.

"I'll wait right here," Woolly promised. "In our special closet."

Lily gave him one more hug. "Thank you for playing with me."

"Thank you for being my friend," Woolly said.

At dinner, Lily smiled brightly. Mama noticed right away.

"You look happy, sweetie," Mama said.

"I made a new friend today," Lily announced.

"How wonderful!" Mama said. "Friends make everything better."

After dinner, Lily ran upstairs. Woolly was waiting in the closet. His eyes sparkled with joy.

"Ready for more adventures?" he asked.

"Always!" Lily said.

They played until bedtime. When Lily fell asleep, Woolly watched over her. He kept her dreams safe and sweet.

From that day on, Lily was never lonely. Woolly lived in her closet always. They shared secrets and laughter. They had tea parties and adventures.

Best friends had found each other. The closet became their magical place. Love and friendship filled Lily's room.

And they all lived happily ever after.

The end.
