# Max's Magical Garden Morning

The sun peeked through Max's bedroom window. Golden rays danced across his pillow. "Time for garden time!" called Mom from downstairs.

Max pulled his blanket over his head. Garden time meant dirt under his fingernails. It meant bugs crawling everywhere. It meant hot sun making him sweaty.

"Do I have to?" Max asked, dragging his feet to the kitchen.

Mom smiled and handed him his little watering can. "The plants are waiting for you, sweetie."

Max shuffled outside to the backyard garden. The tomato plants looked droopy and sad. The sunflowers hung their heavy heads low. Even the cheerful marigolds seemed tired.

"Everything looks thirsty," Max mumbled. He filled his watering can at the spigot. The water felt cool against his fingers.

Max started with the tomatoes. Splash, splash, splash went the water. Suddenly, something amazing happened. The tomato leaves began to perk up! They seemed to dance in the morning breeze.

"Wow!" Max whispered. He moved to the sunflowers next.

As the water touched their roots, the giant flowers slowly lifted their faces. They turned toward the warm sunshine. Max giggled as one sunflower seemed to wink at him.

The marigolds were next. Orange and yellow petals sparkled with water drops. They looked like tiny suns scattered across the garden bed.

"You're all waking up!" Max laughed.

A butterfly landed on his shoulder. Its wings were orange and black stripes. "Hello, little friend," Max said softly. The butterfly stayed for three whole seconds!

Max noticed something moving near the lettuce. A tiny green frog sat on a big leaf. It croaked a happy morning song. "Ribbit, ribbit!"

"Good morning, Mr. Frog," Max said. The frog seemed to nod before hopping away.

As Max watered the herb garden, wonderful smells filled the air. The mint smelled fresh and cool. The basil smelled like pizza sauce. The lavender smelled like Mom's bubble bath.

Max touched a fuzzy lamb's ear plant. It felt soft like his teddy bear. The rosemary felt prickly but smelled amazing.

A red ladybug climbed up a bean plant. Max counted seven black spots on its back. "Lucky seven!" he said, remembering what Grandpa taught him.

The garden was coming alive everywhere Max looked. Bees hummed happy songs as they visited flowers. A blue jay chirped from the apple tree. Even the earthworms wiggled with joy in the damp soil.

Max discovered something special behind the cucumber plants. Three tiny green cucumbers were growing! Yesterday they weren't there at all.

"Mom! Come see!" Max called excitedly. "The cucumbers grew overnight!"

Mom hurried outside with her coffee mug. "Oh my goodness! They're perfect, Max. You've been taking such good care of them."

Max beamed with pride. His chest felt warm and happy. "The whole garden is waking up because of the water!"

"And because of your gentle care," Mom added. She ruffled his hair lovingly.

Max looked around at his beautiful garden. The colors seemed brighter now. The green leaves, yellow sunflowers, orange marigolds, and red tomatoes created a rainbow of joy.

A gentle breeze rustled through the plants. It sounded like they were whispering "thank you" to Max.

"Can I plant some carrots today?" Max asked eagerly. "And maybe some radishes too?"

"Of course!" Mom said. "We can go to the garden center after lunch."

Max spent the rest of the morning discovering garden treasures. He found a perfect smooth stone for his collection. He watched ants marching in a straight line. He even helped a lost beetle find its way home.

When lunchtime came, Max didn't want to go inside. "Just five more minutes?" he asked.

"The garden will be here after lunch," Mom promised.

That evening, Max drew pictures of all his garden friends. The butterfly, the frog, the ladybug, and the busy bees. He hung the pictures on his bedroom wall.

As he snuggled into bed, Max smiled. Tomorrow he would visit his garden again. He would water the plants and check on the growing cucumbers. Maybe he would meet new garden friends too.

"I love garden time," Max whispered sleepily. Outside his window, the garden rested peacefully under the twinkling stars.

The end.
