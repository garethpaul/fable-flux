# Story story_1210

**Nutkin's Big Problem**

Nutkin the squirrel loved his cozy tree by the pond. Every morning, he would gather shiny acorns. He kept them all in his special hollow.

"Mine, mine, mine!" Nutkin would chatter happily. His fluffy tail twitched with joy. The acorns made soft clicking sounds in his paws.

One sunny day, Nutkin saw Bella Bunny hopping by. Her tummy made a loud rumbling sound. She looked very hungry.

"Hello, Nutkin," Bella said softly. "Do you have any food? I can't find carrots anywhere."

Nutkin hugged his acorn bag tight. "These are MY acorns," he squeaked. "I worked hard to find them!"

Bella's ears drooped down sadly. She hopped away slowly.

Soon, Freddy Frog came splashing up. His green skin looked pale. "I'm so hungry," Freddy croaked weakly. "Could you share one tiny acorn?"

"No way!" Nutkin chattered loudly. "Find your own food!" He scurried up his tree quickly.

Freddy sank down on a lily pad. He looked very sad.

That night, Nutkin felt funny inside. His tummy felt heavy and strange. It wasn't hunger making him feel bad. Something else was wrong.

**First Try**

The next morning, Nutkin had an idea. "I'll hide my acorns better," he decided. "Then no one can ask for them."

He dug holes everywhere around the pond. Under rocks, behind flowers, near the water's edge. He buried all his precious acorns.

"Perfect!" Nutkin squeaked with pride. "Now they're safe from everyone!"

But when lunchtime came, Nutkin felt hungry. He ran to his first hiding spot. Empty! He checked the second hole. Nothing there either!

"Oh no!" Nutkin cried out loudly. "Where are my acorns?"

He dug and dug everywhere. The soft dirt got under his claws. His fur became messy and brown. But he found nothing at all.

Nutkin sat down and started crying. Big tears rolled down his furry cheeks. His plan had failed completely.

**Second Try**

Wise Old Owl hooted softly from above. "What's wrong, little squirrel?"

"I lost all my acorns!" Nutkin sobbed. "I tried to hide them. Now they're gone forever!"

Old Owl's yellow eyes twinkled kindly. "Maybe the problem isn't losing acorns. Maybe it's not sharing them."

Nutkin wiped his wet eyes. "I don't understand," he sniffled.

"When you share with friends," Old Owl explained gently, "they help you too. Try being kind instead."

Nutkin thought hard about this. His little heart felt lighter already.

The next day, Nutkin gathered fresh acorns. This time felt different somehow. The morning air smelled sweet and fresh.

He found Bella Bunny looking sad. "Here, Bella," Nutkin said warmly. "Please take some acorns. You need food too."

Bella's eyes grew big and bright. "Thank you so much, Nutkin!" She smiled happily.

"I know where carrots grow!" Bella said excitedly. "I'll show you tomorrow!"

Then Nutkin saw Freddy Frog. "Freddy, want to share lunch?" Nutkin called out cheerfully.

Freddy's face lit up immediately. "Really? You'd share with me?"

"Of course!" Nutkin grinned widely. "Friends help each other always."

**Happy Ending**

Soon, all the pond animals gathered together. They shared a wonderful feast. Bella brought sweet orange carrots. Freddy found juicy water berries. Other friends brought nuts and seeds.

"This is the best meal ever!" Nutkin laughed joyfully. His tummy felt full and happy. But his heart felt even better.

"Sharing makes everything taste better," Bella agreed. She munched her acorn contentedly.

"And we have more friends now!" Freddy added with a big smile.

From that day on, Nutkin shared everything gladly. He learned that giving to others made him happier than keeping everything alone.

The pond became filled with laughter. Every day brought new adventures with friends. And Nutkin's heart stayed warm and full forever.

"Sharing is the best treasure of all," Nutkin would tell everyone proudly.

The end.
