# Ducky's Brave Night Adventure

Ducky loved playing in Willowpond Park. The sunny pond sparkled like diamonds. Green grass tickled her orange feet. Colorful flowers smelled so sweet.

Every day, Ducky splashed happily. She paddled with her friends. She found shiny pebbles underwater. She nibbled tender water plants.

But when evening came, Ducky worried. The sun went down slowly. Shadows grew long and dark. Soon it would be nighttime.

"I'm scared of the dark," Ducky whispered. Her heart beat fast. Her feathers felt all ruffled.

When darkness came, Ducky hid. She tucked her head down. She squeezed her eyes shut tight. "Dark is scary," she said sadly.

Mama Duck waddled over gently. "What's wrong, little one?" she asked. Her voice was warm and kind.

"I don't like the dark," Ducky said. "It makes me feel scared inside."

Mama Duck nodded understanding. "Let's try something together," she said. "Maybe we can help you."

First, they tried counting stars. "One bright star," said Mama Duck. "Two bright stars," counted Ducky. Soon they counted ten twinkling stars. The stars looked like tiny lights. But Ducky still felt scared.

Next, they tried singing songs. "Twinkle, twinkle, little star," they sang. Their voices echoed softly. The melody was pretty and calm. But Ducky's heart still raced.

Then Mama Duck had another idea. "Let's take tiny steps," she said. "We'll walk together very slowly."

They started walking around the pond. Mama Duck stayed close beside Ducky. Step by step, they moved forward. Ducky's feet felt the cool grass.

"Listen," said Mama Duck softly. "What do you hear now?"

Ducky listened very carefully. She heard gentle water sounds. Splash, splash went little waves. Crickets sang their nighttime songs. Frogs croaked deep, happy sounds.

"The dark has music!" Ducky said. Her eyes grew wide with wonder.

They kept walking together slowly. Ducky noticed more night sounds. Owls hooted soft, low calls. Leaves rustled in gentle breezes. Her own feet made soft sounds.

"Look up," whispered Mama Duck gently.

Ducky looked at the sky. The moon glowed silver-white. It made everything look magical. Moonbeams danced on the water. Everything sparkled and shimmered beautifully.

"The dark is pretty!" Ducky said. She felt less scared now. Her breathing became more calm.

They walked further around the pond. Ducky saw fireflies blinking yellow. They looked like flying stars. Blink, blink, blink they went. Like tiny magic lights dancing.

"I want to follow them!" Ducky said excitedly. She forgot about feeling scared. The fireflies were so pretty.

Ducky and Mama Duck followed slowly. The fireflies led them everywhere. They flew over tall grass. They danced near purple flowers. They sparkled above the water.

Soon Ducky was having fun. She laughed at the fireflies. She splashed in moonlit water. She listened to night sounds.

"I'm not scared anymore!" Ducky said. She felt proud and happy. Her chest puffed out with confidence.

"You were very brave," said Mama Duck. "You tried new things tonight."

Ducky smiled a big smile. "The dark isn't scary," she said. "It's different from daytime. But it's beautiful too!"

From that night on, Ducky loved evenings. She looked forward to darkness. She listened for cricket songs. She watched for dancing fireflies. She counted twinkling stars above.

When other young ducks felt scared, Ducky helped them. "Let's walk together," she said kindly. "I'll show you night magic."

Ducky learned something very important. Sometimes scary things aren't really scary. They just need time and trying. With help from someone loving, brave feelings can grow strong.

Now Ducky enjoys both day and night. Sunny mornings are still wonderful. But dark evenings are magical too. Every night brings new adventures.

Ducky sleeps peacefully now. She dreams of fireflies dancing. She dreams of moonbeams sparkling. She dreams of being brave.
