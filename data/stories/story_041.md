# Danny Drum's Reading Time Discovery

Danny Drum lived on the sunny windowsill in Emma's bedroom. He was round and cheerful with a bright red top. Every morning, he watched Emma get ready for school. Every afternoon, he waited for her return.

"Reading time!" called Emma's mom from downstairs.

Emma bounced up from her toys. "Coming, Mama!" She grabbed her favorite book about dancing elephants.

Danny Drum felt left behind. Reading time meant sitting quietly. No tapping. No music. No fun drum sounds. He watched Emma skip toward the living room.

"I wish I could join reading time," Danny sighed. "But drums are too noisy for books."

Danny rolled closer to the bedroom door. He could hear Emma's mom reading in her gentle voice. The story was about a little mouse who loved cheese. Emma giggled at the funny parts.

"Maybe I could listen quietly," Danny whispered to himself.

Very carefully, Danny rolled down the hallway. Bump, bump, bump went his round sides. He stopped outside the living room door.

Emma and her mom sat together on the big blue couch. Sunlight streamed through the windows. Emma's mom pointed to pictures while she read.

"The mouse tiptoed through the kitchen," read Mama. "Tip, tip, tip went his tiny feet."

Something magical happened then. Danny felt a gentle rhythm in the words. Tip, tip, tip. Like a quiet drum beat! His red top tingled with excitement.

"The mouse's heart went thump, thump, thump," continued the story.

Danny's whole body vibrated softly. Thump, thump, thump! The story had music inside it! He had never noticed before.

Emma looked up from her book. "Mama, do you hear that gentle tapping?"

"I think our house is singing along," smiled Mama.

Danny couldn't help himself. Very, very softly, he tapped along with the story's rhythm. Pat, pat, pat with the raindrops. Boom, boom, boom with the thunder. Tick, tick, tick with the clock.

Emma clapped her hands. "The story sounds like music!"

"Stories often do," said Mama. "Every book has its own special beat."

They read three more books together. Danny discovered each story had different rhythms. Adventure books went fast and exciting. Sleepy books went slow and gentle. Funny books bounced and giggled.

Danny felt wonderful. Reading time wasn't quiet at all! It was full of hidden music and secret rhythms. He belonged here after all.

"Tomorrow we'll read about singing birds," said Emma's mom.

"Can we invite Danny Drum?" asked Emma. "I think he likes stories too."

Danny's red top glowed with happiness. Emma had noticed him! She wanted him to join reading time officially.

"What a lovely idea," said Mama. "Drums and books make beautiful music together."

That evening, Danny rolled back to his windowsill spot. But everything felt different now. He wasn't just waiting for tomorrow. He was excited! Reading time would be his special time too.

He practiced very quiet rhythms. Soft pats for gentle stories. Tiny taps for mystery books. Silent vibrations for sleepy tales.

The stars twinkled outside Emma's window. Danny dreamed about all the stories waiting to be discovered. Stories about brave knights and magical forests. Stories about friendly animals and far-away places.

Each story would have its own special music. And Danny would help bring that music to life! Very quietly, very gently, very perfectly.

"Tomorrow will be wonderful," Danny whispered to the moon.

The next morning, Emma placed Danny right beside her on the reading couch. "Ready for story music?" she asked.

Danny's red top sparkled in the sunlight. "Ready!" he seemed to say.

Reading time had become Danny's favorite time of day. Not because it was quiet, but because it was full of the most beautiful, gentle music in the whole world.

And every day brought new songs hidden inside new stories, just waiting to be discovered together.

The end.
