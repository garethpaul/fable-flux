# Penny Pig's Perfect Dinner Party

Penny Pig sat alone in her cozy dining room. The big wooden table looked so empty. Her pretty plates sat quietly on the shelves. The colorful napkins stayed folded and unused.

"I wish I had someone to play with," Penny sighed. She looked at her favorite tea set. The tiny cups sparkled in the sunlight. But playing tea party alone wasn't very fun.

Penny picked up a small blue cup. She pretended to sip imaginary tea. "More tea, please?" she asked the empty chair. But no one answered back. The dining room felt very quiet and sad.

Just then, she heard a gentle knock. Tap, tap, tap! "Hello, Penny!" called a cheerful voice. It was Ruby Rabbit from next door. Ruby's long ears twitched with excitement.

"Hi, Ruby," Penny said softly. She felt a little shy. "I was just playing tea party. But it's not much fun alone."

Ruby's eyes grew bright and sparkly. "Oh, I love tea parties! May I join you, please?" Ruby's nose wiggled with happiness.

Penny's heart felt warm and fluttery. "Really? You want to play with me?" Her curly tail wiggled with joy.

"Of course!" Ruby hopped into the dining room. She looked around with wonder. "Your dining room is so beautiful! These dishes are amazing!"

Penny felt proud and happy. "Thank you! My grandma gave me this tea set. Would you like to help me set the table?"

"Yes, please!" Ruby clapped her paws together. "I know how to fold napkins fancy!"

The two friends got busy right away. Penny carefully placed the delicate cups on saucers. Ruby folded the napkins into pretty triangles. They worked together like a perfect team.

"Let's put flowers in the middle," suggested Ruby. She pointed to Penny's garden outside the window.

"Great idea!" Penny squealed with delight. They ran outside together. They picked yellow daisies and purple violets. The flowers smelled sweet and fresh.

Back in the dining room, they arranged everything perfectly. The table looked magical and special. Much better than when Penny worked alone.

"Now we need pretend food," giggled Penny. She opened her play kitchen cabinet. Inside were tiny plastic cookies and cakes.

"Ooh! And I can make mud pies!" Ruby said excitedly. "Well, pretend mud pies. From my imagination!"

They sat down at the beautiful table. Penny poured imaginary tea very carefully. Ruby served her special pretend cookies. They both used their fanciest manners.

"This tea is delicious," Ruby said politely. She held her pinky finger up high.

"Your cookies are the best ever," Penny replied. She took a tiny pretend bite. "They taste like sunshine and rainbows!"

Both friends giggled until their tummies hurt. Playing together was so much more fun. Everything felt magical and special now.

"Let's have tea parties every week," suggested Ruby. Her ears bounced with excitement.

"Yes! And we can take turns hosting," Penny agreed. "Next time at your house!"

They cleaned up the dishes together. Ruby washed while Penny dried. Working as a team made the job quick and easy. Soon everything sparkled clean again.

"Thank you for playing with me," Penny said warmly. She gave Ruby a big, gentle hug.

"Thank you for sharing your beautiful tea set," Ruby replied. She hugged Penny back tightly.

As Ruby hopped home, Penny looked around her dining room. It looked exactly the same as before. But somehow it felt completely different now. It felt full of friendship and joy.

Penny touched her heart and smiled big. "Everything is better with a friend," she whispered happily.

The next morning, Penny woke up excited. She couldn't wait to see Ruby again. Maybe today they would have a breakfast party! Or perhaps they would set up a picnic in the garden.

Penny had learned something wonderful and important. Playing alone could be okay sometimes. But playing with a friend was absolutely magical. Cooperation made everything more fun, more beautiful, and more special.

From that day on, Penny's dining room was never lonely again. It was filled with laughter, friendship, and the most wonderful tea parties ever.

The end.
