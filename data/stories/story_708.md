# Story story_708

**Monkey and Puppet**

Max the monkey sat in the big closet. He played with his red ball alone. The closet was dark and quiet. Sunlight peeked through the crack under the door.

Max rolled his ball back and forth. He caught it with his fuzzy hands. But playing alone felt sad. Max wished he had a friend to play with.

"I want someone to play with me," Max said softly.

High up on a shelf sat Penny the puppet. She had bright yellow hair and a purple dress. Penny watched Max play every day. She wanted to be his friend so badly.

Penny's button eyes sparkled in the dim light. Her soft fabric arms wiggled a little. She tried to speak but only made tiny squeaking sounds.

"Squeak, squeak," went Penny quietly.

Max looked up at the shelf. He saw Penny moving slightly. Her yellow hair swayed back and forth. Max tilted his furry head to one side.

"Did you make that sound?" Max asked.

Penny nodded her cloth head up and down. Her purple dress rustled softly. Max's brown eyes grew wide with surprise.

"You want to play with me?" Max asked excitedly.

Penny nodded again. This time she squeaked louder. "Squeak, squeak, squeak!"

Max climbed up the closet shelves carefully. His small hands gripped each wooden board. His feet found safe places to step. Soon he reached Penny's shelf.

"Hi there," Max said with a big smile. "I'm Max."

Penny's button eyes seemed to twinkle. She felt so happy inside her soft body. Finally, she had found a friend!

Max gently picked up Penny. She was light and squishy. Her yellow yarn hair felt smooth between his fingers. Her purple dress was made of velvet.

"You're very soft," Max said kindly. "Do you like to play ball?"

Penny squeaked yes. Max carefully climbed down with his new friend. He set Penny on the closet floor gently.

Max rolled the red ball to Penny. The ball bumped into her softly. Penny couldn't catch it with her floppy arms. But she could push it back with her whole body.

"Good job!" Max cheered happily.

They played catch in their own special way. Max threw the ball gently. Penny used her body to stop it. Then she pushed it back to Max.

The closet filled with giggles and squeaks. Max laughed when Penny fell over trying to catch the ball. Penny squeaked with joy when she successfully pushed it back.

"This is so much fun!" Max said, clapping his hands.

Next, they played hide and seek. Max hid behind winter coats. The coats smelled like cedar and mothballs. Penny hid under a pile of soft scarves.

When Max found Penny, he tickled her gently. Penny squeaked and wiggled with happiness. Her button eyes seemed to smile.

They built a fort with shoe boxes. Max stacked them carefully. Penny helped by leaning against them. Their fort was cozy and perfect for two friends.

"We make a great team," Max said proudly.

As the day grew darker, they sat together. Max held Penny close to his warm, fuzzy chest. Penny felt safe and loved in her friend's arms.

"I was lonely before," Max whispered. "But now I have you."

Penny squeaked softly. It sounded like "me too" to Max's ears.

The closet didn't feel dark anymore. It felt like home. It was their special place to play and laugh together.

Max made a bed from old towels. The towels were blue and fluffy. He tucked Penny in carefully next to him.

"Tomorrow we'll play more games," Max promised.

Penny squeaked happily. She dreamed of rolling balls and building forts. She dreamed of her wonderful new friend.

From that day on, Max and Penny were never lonely. They had each other. The closet became filled with laughter and friendship.

Sometimes the best friends are found in the most unexpected places. And sometimes friendship squeaks instead of talks. But love always finds a way to bring friends together.

Max and Penny lived happily ever after in their cozy closet home.

The end.
