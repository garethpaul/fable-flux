# Story story_683

**Puppet's Park Adventure**

Lily held her favorite puppet, Mr. Buttons, close to her chest. "Time for the park!" called Mama from the kitchen.

Mr. Buttons had soft red fur and two shiny black buttons for eyes. Lily made him shake his tiny head. "I don't want to go," she whispered to him.

The puppet felt heavy in her hands. His arms flopped down sadly. Lily made Mr. Buttons hide behind her back.

"Parks are too big," Lily said in Mr. Buttons' squeaky voice. "Too many new things. Too many people."

Mama smiled and knelt down. "Maybe Mr. Buttons will find something special today."

Lily tucked the puppet into her jacket pocket. Only his button eyes peeked out. They walked to Sunshine Park together.

The grass smelled fresh and green. Birds chirped happy songs in tall oak trees. Other children laughed on bright swings.

Lily sat on a wooden bench. Mr. Buttons stayed hidden in her pocket. She watched kids play tag and climb monkey bars.

A little boy with curly hair walked over. He carried a purple dragon puppet on his hand.

"Hi!" said the boy. "I'm Sam. This is Sparkle."

The dragon puppet had glittery wings. It bobbed up and down on Sam's hand.

"Hello," said Sparkle in Sam's voice. "Who's hiding in your pocket?"

Lily slowly pulled out Mr. Buttons. His red fur caught the warm sunlight.

"This is Mr. Buttons," she said quietly.

Sparkle the dragon puppet flew closer. "Want to play puppet adventure?"

Mr. Buttons wiggled his arms. Lily made him nod yes.

Sam pointed to the sandbox. "Sparkle lives in a magic castle there."

The sand felt cool and soft under their knees. Lily moved Mr. Buttons across tiny sand hills.

"Look!" said Sam. "Sparkle found treasure!"

He held up a smooth, round pebble. It sparkled like a real gem in the sunshine.

Mr. Buttons clapped his small hands. "So pretty!" said Lily in his voice.

More children noticed their puppet show. A girl with pigtails brought her sock monkey. Twin brothers shared a fuzzy bear puppet.

Soon five puppets danced together in the sandbox. They built sand castles and hunted for treasure rocks.

Mr. Buttons felt brave now. He climbed sand mountains and slid down smooth slopes. His button eyes gleamed with joy.

"The puppets are having a tea party!" announced the girl with pigtails.

They found bottle caps for tiny cups. Smooth leaves became pretend cookies. The puppets sat in a circle on the warm sand.

"Yummy!" said Mr. Buttons, pretending to sip tea.

Lily giggled. Her puppet felt light and happy in her hands.

The sun painted everything golden yellow. A gentle breeze made the tree leaves dance and whisper.

"Time to go home," called Mama from the bench.

Lily felt sad to leave. Mr. Buttons waved goodbye to all his new puppet friends.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" asked Sparkle the dragon.

"Yes!" said Mr. Buttons excitedly. "We'll bring more treasure hunting tools!"

On the walk home, Mr. Buttons rode on Lily's shoulder instead of hiding in her pocket. His red fur felt warm from sunshine and fun.

"The park was magical," Lily told Mama. "Mr. Buttons made lots of friends."

The puppet nodded happily. His button eyes sparkled like the treasure pebble they found.

At bedtime, Lily tucked Mr. Buttons under her blanket. "Tomorrow we'll build an even bigger castle," she whispered.

Mr. Buttons seemed to smile in the soft lamplight. His arms hugged Lily's hand.

"Park time is the best time," said Lily sleepily.

Outside her window, the moon shone down on Sunshine Park. The sandbox waited quietly for tomorrow's puppet adventures.

Lily dreamed of dragons and tea parties and treasure hunts. Mr. Buttons dreamed too, snuggled safely in her arms.

Morning couldn't come soon enough for their next magical park day.

The end.
