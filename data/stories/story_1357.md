# Story story_1357

**Bessie's Big Day**

Bessie the cow lived in a sunny meadow near the forest. Her black and white spots sparkled in the morning light. Today was her first day at Forest School.

"Moo," said Bessie softly. Her tummy felt funny and wiggly. She had never been to school before. What if the other animals didn't like her? What if she couldn't find her classroom?

Bessie's mama gave her a gentle nuzzle. "You'll do great, sweet Bessie," Mama said. "Just be yourself."

Bessie walked slowly toward the big oak tree school. Her hooves made soft sounds on the leafy path. She could hear chattering and laughing from other animals. A red squirrel raced past her. Two rabbits hopped by together.

At the school gate, Bessie stopped. Her heart beat fast like a drum. She peeked around the corner and saw all the animals playing. They looked so happy and confident.

"I'm too nervous," Bessie whispered. "Maybe I should go home."

She turned around and started walking back to the meadow. But then she remembered Mama's words. Bessie took a deep breath of the fresh forest air.

"I can try," she said to herself.

Bessie walked back to school. This time, she went straight to the playground. She stood by a tall pine tree and watched the other animals. A fox was painting at an easel. Three mice were building with blocks. Everyone seemed to know exactly what to do.

Bessie felt her nervousness growing bigger. She decided to find a quiet spot to hide. She walked behind the school building where no one could see her.

But hiding didn't feel good either. Bessie could hear the teacher calling everyone inside. She was missing everything! Her ears drooped down sadly.

"This isn't working," Bessie said. "Hiding makes me feel worse."

Just then, a friendly voice called out. "Hello there! Are you okay?"

Bessie looked up and saw a kind brown bear walking toward her. The bear had warm, gentle eyes and a big smile.

"I'm Teacher Bruno," the bear said. "You must be Bessie! We've been waiting for you."

"I feel nervous," Bessie admitted. Her voice was very quiet.

Teacher Bruno nodded. "First days can feel scary. Would you like to walk to class with me?"

Bessie thought about it. Walking with Teacher Bruno felt much better than walking alone. She nodded her head.

"Yes, please," she said.

Teacher Bruno walked slowly beside Bessie. He pointed out pretty flowers along the path. He told her about the fun things they would do in class. Bessie started to feel a little better.

When they reached the classroom, Teacher Bruno introduced Bessie to everyone. "This is our new friend, Bessie!"

All the animals waved and said hello. A little rabbit named Rosie patted the seat next to her. "Sit here, Bessie!"

Bessie sat down carefully. Rosie smiled at her. "I was nervous on my first day too," Rosie whispered. "But everyone here is really nice."

During story time, Bessie forgot all about being nervous. Teacher Bruno read about a brave little turtle. At art time, Bessie painted a beautiful picture of her meadow home. The fox complimented her pretty colors.

When it was time for snack, Bessie shared her sweet clover with Rosie. Rosie shared her carrots with Bessie. They both giggled as they munched.

"This is fun!" Bessie said happily.

At the end of the day, Bessie felt proud and excited. She had made new friends. She had learned new things. She couldn't wait to come back tomorrow.

Walking home through the forest, Bessie thought about her day. Hiding hadn't helped at all. But asking for help from Teacher Bruno had made everything better.

When she reached the meadow, Mama was waiting with a warm smile.

"How was school, dear?" Mama asked.

"It was wonderful!" Bessie said. "I was nervous at first. But I asked for help, and that made everything okay."

Mama gave Bessie a big hug. "I'm so proud of you for being brave."

That night, Bessie fell asleep dreaming about tomorrow's adventures at Forest School.

The end.
