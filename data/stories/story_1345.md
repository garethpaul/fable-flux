# Story story_1345

**Benny's Big Block Hunt**

Benny was a bright red toy truck. He lived in Emma's bedroom closet. Every morning, he loved to play with his colorful wooden blocks.

But today was different. Benny looked all around his cozy corner. His blocks were gone! The red ones, the blue ones, the yellow ones too. Where could they be?

"Oh no!" said Benny. His little horn beeped sadly. "I can't build my tower without my blocks!"

Benny's wheels started to shake. He always built a tall tower every morning. It made him feel proud and strong. But now his blocks had disappeared.

The closet felt very quiet. Benny could hear Emma playing in her room. He could smell her mom making pancakes downstairs. The sweet smell made his metal belly rumble.

"I must find my blocks," Benny said bravely. "I won't give up!"

First, Benny decided to look under everything. He rolled under Emma's winter coats. The coats were soft and tickled his roof. But no blocks there.

He pushed under a pile of old shoes. The shoes smelled like grass and mud. Still no blocks anywhere.

Benny rolled behind Emma's dress-up box. The box was heavy and wooden. He could barely squeeze behind it. But his blocks weren't hiding there either.

"This isn't working," Benny sighed. His red paint felt dusty from all the searching.

Benny sat still for a moment. He thought very hard. Where else could blocks hide in a closet?

Then Benny had a new idea. Maybe he should look up high instead of down low!

Benny rolled to the back wall. There was a tall shelf above him. The shelf was very high up. It looked dark and scary up there.

"I'm just a little truck," Benny worried. "How can I reach so high?"

But Benny remembered what Emma always said. "Try your best and don't give up!" So Benny took a deep breath.

He spotted a small stool nearby. The stool was wooden and wobbled a bit. Benny carefully pushed it with his bumper. Push, push, push! The stool made a scraping sound on the floor.

Benny backed up and rolled fast. Up, up, up the stool he went! His wheels gripped tight. His engine worked very hard.

At the top, Benny could see the high shelf. And there they were! All his beautiful blocks sat in a neat pile. They sparkled in the dim light.

"Hooray!" Benny beeped happily. "I found them!"

But now Benny had another problem. How could he get the blocks down safely?

Benny thought again. He used his strong bumper to gently push one block. It slid to the edge of the shelf. Then it dropped down onto his truck bed with a soft thunk.

One by one, Benny pushed each block. Red block, thunk! Blue block, thunk! Yellow block, thunk! Soon all his blocks were safely in his truck bed.

Carefully, Benny rolled back down the stool. His wheels moved slowly and carefully. He didn't want to drop his precious blocks.

Back on the closet floor, Benny felt wonderful. He had all his blocks again! They felt smooth and warm in his truck bed.

Benny began building his morning tower. Stack, stack, stack! Red on blue, yellow on red. Higher and higher it grew.

"I did it!" Benny said proudly. "I didn't give up, and I found my blocks!"

Just then, Emma opened the closet door. Bright sunlight poured in. Emma smiled when she saw Benny's beautiful tower.

"Good job, Benny!" Emma said sweetly. "I moved your blocks yesterday to clean. I'm sorry I forgot to tell you!"

Benny's horn beeped with joy. He had learned something important today. When something seems lost, don't give up. Think of new ways to solve the problem.

From that day on, Benny felt braver and smarter. He knew that with patience and good thinking, he could solve any problem that came his way.

And his block towers grew taller and more beautiful than ever before.

The end.
