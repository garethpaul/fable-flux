# Kite's Magical Snack Time

It was three o'clock. Time for snack time! Mama called from the kitchen. "Kite, come eat your snack!"

Kite dragged his feet slowly. He didn't want snack time today. His tummy felt grumbly. But snacks seemed boring. The same old crackers. The same old apple slices.

"Do I have to?" Kite asked sadly.

"Yes, sweetie," Mama smiled gently. "Your body needs good fuel."

Kite slumped into his chair. His plate sat there waiting. Round crackers in neat rows. Apple slices fanned out perfectly. A small cup of milk.

Everything looked so ordinary. So plain.

Kite picked up one cracker. He held it up high. The afternoon sunlight streamed through. It made the cracker glow golden!

"Look, Mama!" Kite whispered excitedly. "It's like a tiny sun!"

Mama looked closer too. "It really is beautiful!"

Kite took a small bite. Crunch! The sound echoed happily. He imagined he was crunching through clouds. Fluffy, tasty clouds in the sky.

Next, he picked up an apple slice. The juice sparkled like diamonds. Kite held it carefully. He could see tiny rainbows dancing.

"This apple is magical!" he announced proudly.

"Tell me more," Mama said warmly.

"It's a crescent moon!" Kite explained. "From the fairy kingdom!"

He bit into the apple moon. Sweet juice dripped down. It tasted like sunshine and laughter. Like the best summer day ever.

The milk looked different now too. Kite swirled it gently around. Tiny white whirlpools formed and disappeared. Like clouds dancing in wind.

"I'm drinking liquid clouds!" Kite giggled.

Each sip felt cool and smooth. It tasted like comfort hugs. Like cozy bedtime stories. Like everything good and safe.

Kite ate another golden sun cracker. Then another fairy moon apple. Each bite brought new magic. New wonder and surprise.

"Mama, look at this!" Kite pointed excitedly.

Cracker crumbs made tiny star patterns. Apple juice drops sparkled like jewels. The empty milk cup reflected rainbows.

"You've made snack time beautiful," Mama said softly.

Kite beamed with pride and joy. His tummy felt happy now. Full of magical sunshine and moonbeams. His heart felt warm too.

"Can we have snack time again?" he asked hopefully.

"Every day," Mama promised lovingly.

Kite helped clean his special plate. He washed the magical cup carefully. Everything felt important now. Everything felt special and wonderful.

"What will tomorrow's snack be?" Kite wondered aloud.

"Cheese squares and orange slices," Mama said.

Kite's eyes grew wide with excitement. "Golden treasure blocks! And sunset wheels!"

Mama laughed her musical laugh. "I love your imagination, Kite."

That evening, Kite told Daddy. "Snack time is the best! I ate sunshine and moonbeams!"

Daddy smiled big and bright. "That sounds absolutely delicious, buddy."

Before bed, Kite thought happily. Tomorrow would bring new magic. New treasures to discover. Cheese treasure blocks would taste amazing. Orange sunset wheels would glow.

Maybe he'd find more rainbows. Maybe more dancing cloud swirls. Maybe crackers would become sailing ships. Maybe apples would become magic wands.

Kite drifted off to sleep. He dreamed of magical snacks. Of golden treasures and fairy moons. Of sunshine in his tummy. Of love in every bite.

The next morning, Kite woke up. He thought about snack time. His heart fluttered with excitement. His tummy rumbled with anticipation.

"Good morning, sunshine," Mama said gently.

"Good morning!" Kite bounced happily. "Is it snack time yet?"

Mama hugged him tight and warm. "Not yet, sweet boy. But soon."

Kite smiled the biggest smile. Every day held magic now. Every snack held wonder. Every bite held love.

And snack time would never be boring again. Because Kite had learned something wonderful. Magic lives everywhere. Even in simple crackers. Even in ordinary apple slices.

You just have to look. You just have to believe. You just have to see.

The end.
