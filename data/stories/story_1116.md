# Story story_1116

Finny the Fish Lives in a Big Tank

Finny the little goldfish lived in a big glass tank. The tank sat on a wooden shelf in the Sunny Days Nursery. Finny had pretty orange scales that sparkled in the light. He had big round eyes that looked everywhere.

Every day, Finny swam in circles. Round and round he went. Up to the top, down to the bottom. The water felt cool and smooth. Tiny bubbles tickled his fins.

But Finny felt sad. He watched the children play together. They laughed and ran around. They shared toys and told stories. Finny wished he had a friend too.

"I want someone to play with," Finny said softly. His voice made little bubbles in the water. The bubbles floated up and popped.

Across the room sat Bella the bike. She was bright red with shiny silver wheels. Her bell went "ring-ring" when touched. Her seat was soft and blue.

Bella watched Finny swim alone every day. She felt sorry for the little fish. She wanted to help him feel happy.

"Hello, Finny!" called Bella. Her voice was gentle and kind.

Finny stopped swimming. He looked around the room. "Who said that?" he asked.

"It's me, Bella the bike!" she said. "I'm over here by the window."

Finny swam to the side of his tank. He could see Bella's red frame gleaming. Her wheels looked so round and smooth.

"You look sad," said Bella. "Do you want to be friends?"

Finny's fins wiggled with excitement. "Yes! But how can we play? You're big and I'm small. You have wheels and I have fins."

Bella thought hard. Her bell jingled softly as she moved a little. "We can still have fun together!" she said.

"How?" asked Finny. He pressed his face against the glass.

"I can roll close to your tank," said Bella. "Then we can talk and play games."

Bella rolled herself closer. Her wheels made soft sounds on the floor. Now she was right next to Finny's tank.

"Let's play follow the leader!" said Bella. "I'll move my wheel. You swim the same way!"

Bella turned her front wheel to the left. Finny swam left in his tank. Bella turned right. Finny swam right too. They both giggled with joy.

"This is fun!" said Finny. His tail swished happily in the water.

Next, they played counting games. Bella would ring her bell. Finny would blow that many bubbles. Ring-ring! Two bubbles floated up. Ring-ring-ring! Three bubbles danced in the water.

The children in the nursery noticed them playing. "Look!" said little Emma. "The fish and bike are friends!"

"They're playing together!" said Sam with a big smile.

All day long, Finny and Bella played their games. When Finny felt tired, Bella would tell him stories. When Bella felt lonely, Finny would do funny tricks in his tank.

"You're the best friend ever," said Finny. He did a happy flip in the water.

"You're my best friend too," said Bella. Her bell gave a cheerful ring.

As the sun set through the nursery windows, both friends felt warm and happy. The golden light made Finny's scales glow. It made Bella's red paint look extra bright.

"I'm not lonely anymore," said Finny. He swam in a happy circle.

"Neither am I," said Bella. "Friends don't need to be the same to have fun together."

From that day on, Finny and Bella were the best of friends. Every morning, Bella would roll over to greet Finny. Every evening, they would say goodnight to each other.

The children loved watching their friendship. It showed them that being different was okay. Friends could be big or small, fast or slow.

Finny never felt lonely again. He had found his special friend. And Bella was happy too. Together, they filled the nursery with joy and laughter every single day.
