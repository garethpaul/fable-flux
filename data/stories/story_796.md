# Story story_796

**Penny the Puzzle and the Dark Night**

Penny was a colorful jigsaw puzzle. She lived in a sunny meadow. Her pieces sparkled red, blue, and yellow. During the day, Penny loved to shine. The warm sun made her pieces glow.

But when night came, everything changed. The meadow grew dark and quiet. Penny's bright colors disappeared. She could not see her beautiful patterns. The darkness felt scary and cold.

"I don't like the dark," Penny whispered. Her pieces trembled together. "I can't see anything. What if I lose my pieces?"

Every evening, Penny worried. She tried to stay awake. She wanted the sun to come back. But the darkness always came anyway.

One night, Penny heard soft footsteps. A little rabbit hopped nearby. The rabbit looked lost and scared too.

"Hello," said the rabbit quietly. "I can't find my way home. Everything looks different in the dark."

Penny wanted to help. But how could she? She was just a puzzle. She couldn't even see herself.

"Maybe we can help each other," Penny said. She thought hard about the problem. "I have an idea!"

Penny tried her first plan. She asked the moon for help. "Dear moon," she called. "Can you shine brighter? We need more light down here."

The moon smiled kindly. But clouds covered her silver face. The meadow stayed dark. Penny's first idea didn't work.

The little rabbit sat down sadly. "I'll never find my home," he sighed.

Penny felt determined. She wouldn't give up. Good friends don't quit when things get hard.

"Wait," Penny said. "I have another idea!"

Penny thought about her puzzle pieces. Each piece was special. Each piece had its own shape. Even in the dark, she knew where they belonged.

"Little rabbit," Penny said. "Feel my pieces with your paws. Each one is different. They can make a path!"

The rabbit touched Penny's pieces gently. The red pieces felt smooth. The blue pieces had bumpy edges. The yellow pieces were perfectly round.

"Follow the smooth red pieces," Penny said. "They lead to the old oak tree. Then follow the bumpy blue pieces. They go to the stream. The round yellow pieces will take you home!"

The rabbit felt each piece carefully. He followed the red path. Then the blue path. Finally, the yellow path. Soon he could hear familiar sounds. He smelled sweet clover from his home.

"It worked!" the rabbit cheered. "Thank you, Penny!"

As the rabbit hopped away happily, something wonderful happened. Penny felt proud and brave. She realized something important. The dark wasn't so scary anymore.

"I can still help others," Penny said. "I can still be useful. My pieces are special even when I can't see them."

From that night on, Penny wasn't afraid. When darkness came, she remembered her lesson. She had special gifts that worked in any light. Her different pieces could guide lost animals. Her kind heart could comfort scared friends.

Many animals visited Penny at night. Mice used her pieces as stepping stones. Owls perched on her edges to rest. Fireflies danced around her colorful patterns.

"The dark is just different," Penny learned. "It's not something to fear."

Now Penny loves both day and night. During the day, her colors shine bright. At night, her pieces help friends find their way. She discovered that being helpful feels even better than being beautiful.

The little rabbit visits Penny often. They tell stories under the stars. Penny's pieces twinkle in the moonlight. She feels happy and brave.

"Thank you for teaching me," Penny tells her rabbit friend. "Problems have solutions when we try different ideas."

Penny learned that persistence pays off. When one idea doesn't work, try another. Good friends never give up on each other. And sometimes the dark holds its own special magic.

Every night now, Penny helps someone new. She is the helpful puzzle in the meadow. The one who turns scary darkness into friendly adventures.

The end.
