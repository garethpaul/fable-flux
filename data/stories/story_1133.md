# Story story_1133

**Penny the Puppet's Story Time**

Every day at three o'clock, Miss Sarah rang the little silver bell. "Story time!" she called to all the children. The boys and girls would run to the reading corner. They sat on the soft, colorful rug.

But Penny the puppet stayed hidden in her special box. She was small and red with yellow yarn hair. Her tiny button eyes peeked out from the dark box. Penny felt scared about story time.

"What if the children don't like me?" Penny whispered. "What if I forget my words?" Her little puppet hands shook. She heard the children laughing and talking. They sounded so big and loud.

Miss Sarah opened Penny's box. "Come on, little friend," she said softly. "The children are waiting." Miss Sarah's hand was warm and gentle. She slipped Penny onto her arm.

Penny felt herself moving toward the children. Her heart beat fast like a tiny drum. The children's eyes were big and bright. They looked excited to see her.

"Hello," Penny said in a squeaky voice. The children giggled. It was a happy sound, not scary at all. One little girl with curly hair waved at Penny. A boy with freckles smiled big.

"Today we're reading about a brave little mouse," Miss Sarah said. Penny helped turn the pages. The pictures were colorful and fun. There was a tiny gray mouse wearing a red cape.

As Miss Sarah read, Penny acted out the story. When the mouse climbed a tall mountain, Penny moved up high. When the mouse felt scared, Penny hid behind the book. The children laughed and clapped.

"Look! The mouse is just like Penny!" said the curly-haired girl. "They're both small and brave!" Penny felt warm and happy inside. She wasn't scary to the children. They thought she was brave!

During the story, something magical began to happen. Penny forgot about being scared. She was having too much fun! She danced when the mouse danced. She sang when the mouse sang a little song.

The children sang along with Penny. Their voices mixed together like a happy choir. The room felt cozy and safe. Sunlight streamed through the windows. Everything felt just right.

When the mouse in the story made new friends, Penny realized something wonderful. She was making new friends too! The children weren't scary at all. They were kind and fun.

"Can Penny help with the next part?" asked the boy with freckles. Miss Sarah nodded. Penny helped the mouse character talk to a friendly rabbit. She used different voices for each animal.

The children listened carefully to every word. Their eyes sparkled with wonder. When Penny made the mouse laugh, the children laughed too. When the mouse felt proud, Penny stood up tall and proud.

As the story ended, the mouse learned he was braver than he thought. Penny learned the same thing about herself! She had been brave enough to try something new.

"That was the best story time ever!" said a little girl in a pink dress. All the children clapped for Penny and Miss Sarah. Penny felt proud and happy.

"Thank you for helping me tell the story," Miss Sarah whispered to Penny. "You made it extra special." Penny's button eyes twinkled with joy.

As Miss Sarah put Penny back in her box, Penny didn't feel scared anymore. The box didn't seem dark and lonely. It felt cozy, like a little bedroom.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" asked the curly-haired girl. Penny nodded her little puppet head. "Yes! I can't wait!" she said.

That night, Penny dreamed about all the stories they would tell. She dreamed about making the children laugh and smile. She dreamed about being brave and having fun.

The next morning, Penny woke up excited. She couldn't wait to hear Miss Sarah's silver bell ring. She couldn't wait for story time to begin again.

Story time had become her favorite part of every day.

The end.
