# Story story_1339

**Benny and the Colorful Garden**

Little Benny sat in the sunny field behind his house. His mama had packed a special lunch. She opened the basket and smiled.

"Look, Benny! Fresh carrots from our garden," Mama said. The orange carrots were bright and crunchy. "And green peas too!"

Benny wrinkled his tiny nose. "No thank you, Mama." He pushed the vegetables away. "They look yucky."

Mama felt worried. Benny only wanted crackers and milk. "Vegetables help you grow big and strong," she said softly.

But Benny shook his head. He crawled away from the basket. The vegetables stayed untouched in the warm sunshine.

A friendly rabbit hopped over. "Hello, little one," said Rabbit. "Why do you look sad?"

"Benny won't eat his vegetables," Mama explained. "I don't know what to do."

Rabbit's whiskers twitched. "I have an idea! Let me show him something special."

Rabbit picked up a bright carrot. He bit it with a loud CRUNCH. "Mmm! This tastes like sunshine," he said. "Want to try?"

Benny watched with big eyes. The carrot did look pretty. Maybe it wasn't so scary.

Benny reached for the carrot. He took a tiny bite. But it felt strange in his mouth. He made a funny face and spit it out.

"That's okay," said Rabbit kindly. "Sometimes new things feel different."

Mama picked up Benny. "We'll try again later," she said. But she still felt worried.

The next day, Mama had a new idea. She brought Benny back to the field. This time, she brought something special.

"Look what I have," Mama said. She pulled out colorful plates and cups. "We're having a rainbow picnic!"

She arranged the vegetables on the pretty plates. Red tomatoes. Orange carrots. Yellow corn. Green peas. Purple cabbage.

"Wow!" said Benny. The colors looked like a beautiful rainbow.

"Each color has a different taste," Mama explained. "Red tastes sweet. Orange tastes crunchy. Green tastes fresh."

Benny touched a red tomato. It felt smooth and warm. He smelled it. It smelled like summer.

"This one smells nice," he said.

"Would you like to paint with it?" Mama asked. She showed Benny how to dip the tomato in water. Then she helped him press it on paper.

It made a pretty red circle! Benny giggled and clapped his hands.

"Now try the carrot," Mama said. Benny dipped the carrot and made orange lines. The corn made yellow dots.

Soon Benny's paper was full of colorful shapes. His fingers were messy and happy.

"I made art!" Benny said proudly.

"You did! And look," Mama said. "Your fingers taste like vegetables now."

Benny licked his orange finger. It tasted sweet and earthy. He licked his red finger. It tasted juicy and fresh.

"They taste like colors!" Benny said with wonder.

Mama cut the vegetables into tiny pieces. She put them on Benny's favorite blue plate. "Want to taste the rainbow?" she asked.

Benny nodded eagerly. He picked up a small piece of carrot. This time, he chewed it slowly. The crunch sounded happy in his ears.

"It tastes like orange!" he said. He tried a pea next. "This tastes like green!"

Soon Benny was eating all the colorful vegetables. Each bite was a new adventure. His tummy felt happy and full.

"I like rainbow food," Benny told Mama. "Can we have it every day?"

Mama hugged him tight. "Of course, my sweet boy. Vegetables are yummy when we try them in fun ways."

From that day on, Benny loved eating vegetables. He learned that trying new things could be fun. Sometimes the second try was better than the first.

And whenever Benny felt scared of something new, he remembered his rainbow picnic. He remembered that good things happen when you keep trying.
