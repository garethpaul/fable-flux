# Story story_741

**Sister and Max**

Sister sat by the big window in the nursery. Sunlight made pretty patterns on the colorful carpet. She held her favorite teddy bear close to her chest.

"I wish I had someone to play with," Sister whispered to Teddy. Her voice sounded small in the quiet room.

Sister looked around the nursery. Bright toys filled the shelves. A wooden train set sat on the low table. Building blocks made a tall tower in the corner. But playing alone felt boring.

Sister sighed and hugged Teddy tighter. The soft brown fur felt warm against her cheek. She watched dust dance in the sunny air.

Suddenly, the nursery door opened with a gentle creak. A little boy peeked inside. He had curly hair and big brown eyes. His name was Max.

"Can I come in?" Max asked quietly. He held a small red car in his hand.

Sister looked up with surprise. "Yes!" she said, her face brightening like sunshine.

Max stepped into the room. His sneakers made soft sounds on the carpet. He looked around at all the wonderful toys.

"What are you playing?" Max asked. He sat down near Sister but not too close.

"I was just sitting with Teddy," Sister said. "But it's not very fun alone."

Max nodded. He understood feeling lonely. "I like your teddy bear. He looks very soft."

Sister smiled. "His name is Teddy. He's my best friend." She paused. "Well, my only friend."

"I could be your friend too," Max said hopefully. His eyes sparkled with excitement.

Sister's heart felt warm and happy. "Really? You want to be my friend?"

"Yes!" Max said. "Friends make everything more fun."

Sister stood up and brushed off her dress. "What should we play first?"

Max looked at the train set. "How about trains? We could build a long track together."

"That sounds wonderful!" Sister said. She carefully placed Teddy on a soft cushion.

The two children sat by the train table. Sister picked up blue track pieces. Max chose red ones. Together they connected the pieces.

"Click, click, click," went the tracks as they snapped together.

"Look how long our track is!" Max said with wonder.

Sister clapped her hands. "It goes all around the table!"

They placed the little wooden train on the tracks. Sister pushed it gently. The train rolled smoothly around the curves.

"Choo-choo!" Max called out, making train sounds.

Sister giggled. The sound filled the quiet nursery with joy. "Your turn to drive the train!"

Max carefully pushed the train. It went over a little bridge they built. "This is the best train ride ever!"

After trains, they played with building blocks. Sister stacked them high. Max made them into a castle. They worked together like a perfect team.

"Our castle needs a flag," Sister said.

Max found a small yellow cloth. They put it on top of their castle. It looked magnificent.

Next, they played house with the dolls. Sister was the mommy. Max was the daddy. They made pretend dinner and set the tiny table.

"This soup is delicious," Max said, pretending to eat.

Sister pretended to taste it too. "Thank you! I made it with love."

The afternoon flew by like a happy dream. They laughed and played until their cheeks were rosy.

When it was time for Max to go home, they felt sad. But it was a good kind of sad.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" Sister asked hopefully.

"Of course!" Max said. "We're friends now!"

Sister hugged Teddy tight. "Did you hear that, Teddy? We have a new friend!"

Max waved goodbye from the doorway. "See you tomorrow, Sister!"

Sister waved back with her whole arm. The nursery felt different now. It felt full of friendship and joy.

She looked around at their toys. The train track still made a perfect circle. Their castle stood tall and proud. Everything looked the same, but everything felt better.

"Tomorrow will be another fun day," Sister told Teddy.

The nursery filled with golden sunset light. Sister felt warm and happy inside. She had learned something wonderful today. Playing with friends made everything more special.

Sister smiled and hugged Teddy close. She couldn't wait for tomorrow.

The end.
