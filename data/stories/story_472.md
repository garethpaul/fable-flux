# Thomas the Little Train and the Missing Teddy Bear

Thomas was a bright red train. He had shiny wheels and a cheerful whistle. Every day, Thomas loved to play in Sunshine Park with his best friend, Mr. Buttons the teddy bear.

Mr. Buttons was soft and brown. He had a blue bow tie. Thomas carried him everywhere in his special cargo car. They played together under the big oak tree. They rolled down the grassy hills. They always had so much fun!

One sunny morning, Thomas woke up excited. "Time to play!" he whistled happily. But when he looked in his cargo car, something was wrong. Mr. Buttons was gone!

"Oh no!" cried Thomas. "Where is my teddy bear?" His whistle made a sad, worried sound. Thomas felt scared and lonely without his best friend.

Thomas puffed slowly into the park. His wheels clicked softly on the path. "I must find Mr. Buttons," he said. "I won't give up!"

First, Thomas looked under the big oak tree. He moved his bright headlight back and forth. The light made dancing shadows on the grass. "Mr. Buttons? Are you here?" called Thomas. But he only found acorns and leaves.

Next, Thomas rolled to the playground. His wheels squeaked as he searched. He looked under the slide. He checked behind the swings. The metal felt cold and smooth. "Mr. Buttons!" he called again. But his teddy bear wasn't there.

Thomas felt sad, but he didn't stop looking. "I'll keep trying," he puffed bravely.

Then Thomas remembered the flower garden. Maybe Mr. Buttons was there! Thomas rolled carefully between the colorful flowers. The petals smelled sweet like honey. Red roses tickled his sides. Yellow daisies brushed his windows.

"Still no Mr. Buttons," sighed Thomas. His steam made little white clouds.

Thomas was getting tired. His wheels felt heavy. But then he heard something. "Chirp, chirp, chirp!" It was a little blue bird.

"Hello, little bird," said Thomas politely. "Have you seen my teddy bear? He's brown and soft with a blue bow tie."

The bird tilted her tiny head. "Chirp! Yes! I saw him by the duck pond!" she sang sweetly.

"Thank you!" whistled Thomas excitedly. His wheels spun faster now. Hope filled his engine with warm, happy feelings.

Thomas hurried to the duck pond. The water sparkled like diamonds in the sunshine. Ducks paddled around, making gentle splashing sounds. Their feathers were white and gray and pretty.

And there, sitting on a wooden bench, was Mr. Buttons!

"Mr. Buttons!" called Thomas joyfully. His whistle played a happy tune. "I found you!"

The teddy bear looked soft and cuddly. His blue bow tie was a little crooked. Thomas carefully picked him up with his special crane arm. He placed Mr. Buttons gently in his cargo car.

"I missed you so much," said Thomas, nuzzling his friend. Mr. Buttons felt warm and fuzzy against Thomas's metal nose.

"I must have dropped you yesterday," realized Thomas. "When we were watching the ducks swim."

Thomas felt proud and happy. He had searched everywhere. He hadn't given up, even when he felt tired and sad. And now he had his best friend back!

The little blue bird flew down. "I'm glad you found him!" she chirped cheerfully.

"Thank you for helping me," said Thomas. "You're very kind."

Thomas and Mr. Buttons spent the whole day playing in the park. They rolled through the soft grass. They watched fluffy white clouds drift by. They listened to the gentle wind in the trees.

As the sun began to set, Thomas felt grateful and content. "I learned something important today," he told Mr. Buttons. "When something is really important to you, you should never give up looking. If you keep trying, you can solve any problem!"

Mr. Buttons seemed to smile in the golden sunlight.

Thomas whistled a happy tune as they headed home. His wheels clicked merrily on the path. He felt brave and proud. Most importantly, he felt the warm, wonderful feeling of having his best friend safe beside him.

And from that day on, Thomas always checked his cargo car twice before leaving the park. Because some things are too precious to lose!

The end.
