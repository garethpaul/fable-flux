# Story story_1144

Emma loved her new preschool. The colorful walls made her smile. The toys sparkled in the sunlight. But today, something was wrong.

Emma stood in the bathroom. She had washed her hands carefully. Now she wanted to dry them. But the paper towel holder was too high up. Way too high for a little girl like Emma.

She stretched her arms up high. Her fingers wiggled in the air. But she couldn't reach the towels. Not even close.

Emma looked around the empty bathroom. She could hear her friends playing outside. Their happy voices drifted through the window. She wanted to join them so badly.

"I can do this myself," Emma whispered. She didn't want to bother anyone. Asking for help felt scary. What if the teacher was busy? What if her friends laughed?

Emma tried jumping. Hop, hop, hop! Her wet hands dripped water drops. The drops made tiny splashing sounds on the floor. But she still couldn't reach the towels.

She tried again. Jump, jump, jump! Her shoes squeaked on the wet floor. Her arms stretched as high as they could go. Still no luck.

Emma's hands felt cold and drippy. She wiped them on her dress. But that made her dress damp and uncomfortable. The wet fabric stuck to her legs.

"Maybe I can climb up," she thought. Emma looked at the sink. It seemed very tall and slippery. That didn't feel safe at all.

She heard footsteps in the hallway. Someone was coming! Emma's heart beat faster. Should she ask for help? Her cheeks felt warm and pink.

Mrs. Garcia, her teacher, peeked into the bathroom. "Emma? Are you okay in here?"

Emma nodded quickly. "I'm fine," she said in a tiny voice. But her hands were still wet and cold.

Mrs. Garcia noticed Emma's damp dress. She saw the water drops on the floor. Her kind eyes looked up at the paper towel holder.

"Oh my," said Mrs. Garcia gently. "That's quite high up, isn't it?"

Emma looked down at her shoes. "I tried to reach it," she admitted softly.

"You know what?" said Mrs. Garcia. "I think you're very smart for trying. But sometimes it's okay to ask for help."

Mrs. Garcia reached up easily. She pulled down several soft paper towels. "Here you go, sweetie."

Emma took the towels gratefully. They felt fluffy and dry against her skin. She patted her hands until they were completely dry. Then she wiped the water drops from her dress.

"Thank you," Emma said. Her voice was still quiet, but she smiled.

"You're very welcome," said Mrs. Garcia. "Emma, can I tell you a secret?"

Emma nodded, her eyes wide with curiosity.

"Even grown-ups ask for help sometimes. I asked the janitor to help me move a heavy box yesterday. Asking for help shows you're brave and smart."

Emma thought about this. Maybe asking for help wasn't scary after all. Maybe it was just another way to solve problems.

"Next time I can't reach something, I'll ask," Emma promised.

"That sounds perfect," said Mrs. Garcia. "Now, shall we go play with your friends?"

Emma's face lit up with a big smile. "Yes, please!"

They walked outside together. Emma's hands were clean and dry. Her dress was comfortable again. Best of all, she felt proud and happy.

At the playground, Emma helped her friend Jake reach a ball that rolled under the slide. When Jake said "thank you," Emma remembered how good it felt to help others.

"Sometimes we all need help," Emma told Jake. "And that's perfectly okay."

From that day on, Emma wasn't afraid to ask for help when she needed it. She learned that asking for help was just another way to be brave and smart. And she always remembered to help others too.

The bathroom paper towels never seemed quite so high again.

The end.
