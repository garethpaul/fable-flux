# Story story_1129

**The Little Brother and the Big Fence**

Tommy lived in a cozy house with his big sister Emma. Their backyard had a tall wooden fence. Emma could peek over the fence easily. She was much taller than Tommy.

One sunny morning, Tommy heard happy sounds. Laughter came from the other side of the fence. Barking too. And splashing water sounds.

"What's happening over there?" Tommy asked Emma.

Emma stood on her tippy-toes. She looked over the fence. "The new neighbors have a puppy!" she said. "He's playing in a kiddie pool. He's so fluffy and brown!"

Tommy jumped up and down. He stretched his arms high. But the fence was too tall. He could only see the blue sky above.

"I want to see the puppy too!" Tommy said sadly.

Tommy felt left out. His shoulders drooped down. Why was he so short? It wasn't fair.

The puppy barked again. Tommy's heart felt heavy. He wanted to see the cute puppy so badly.

"I have an idea!" Tommy said. He ran to the garage. Dad kept a small step stool there. Tommy dragged it across the grass. The stool made scraping sounds.

Tommy climbed onto the step stool. He stood as tall as he could. He looked toward the fence. But he still couldn't see over it. The fence was still too high.

"Oh no," Tommy whispered. His eyes felt watery. The step stool wasn't tall enough.

Emma saw Tommy's sad face. "Don't give up, little brother," she said kindly. "Let's think of another way."

Tommy wiped his eyes. He looked around the yard. Then he saw something. Dad's old wooden crate sat by the shed. It was bigger than the step stool.

"Emma! Help me move that crate!" Tommy called out.

Together, they pushed the heavy crate. It made a loud scraping noise on the ground. Tommy's arms felt tired. But he didn't stop trying.

They placed the crate next to the fence. Tommy climbed up carefully. The crate was much taller than the step stool.

Tommy stood up slowly. He held onto the fence for balance. Then he looked over the top.

"I can see!" Tommy shouted with joy.

There was the neighbor's backyard! A small brown puppy splashed in a blue kiddie pool. Water droplets sparkled in the sunshine. The puppy's tail wagged fast. His wet fur looked so soft and fluffy.

"Hello, little puppy!" Tommy called out.

The puppy looked up. He barked a happy bark. His pink tongue hung out. He ran toward the fence. Water dripped from his paws.

"He likes you!" Emma said, smiling big.

The neighbor lady came outside. She had kind eyes and gray hair. "Hello there!" she waved. "I see you found our new puppy. His name is Cocoa."

"Hi Cocoa!" Tommy waved back. "I'm Tommy!"

Cocoa barked again. He jumped up toward the fence. His little paws scratched the wood.

"Would you like to meet Cocoa properly?" the neighbor asked. "You can come through our front gate."

Tommy's face lit up like sunshine. "Yes please!"

Tommy climbed down from the crate. He felt so proud and happy. He had solved his problem all by himself. Well, with Emma's help too.

They walked around to the front yard. The neighbor opened her gate. Cocoa ran to Tommy right away. The puppy's fur felt soft and damp. He licked Tommy's hands with his warm, wet tongue.

"You're so cute, Cocoa!" Tommy giggled.

That afternoon, Tommy played with Cocoa for a long time. They splashed in the kiddie pool together. Tommy's clothes got all wet. But he didn't care one bit.

When it was time to go home, Tommy felt wonderful. He had wanted to see over the fence. The first try didn't work. But he didn't give up. He tried again with a better idea.

"I'm glad I kept trying," Tommy told Emma.

"Me too," Emma said. "Now we have a new friend next door!"

Tommy smiled. Sometimes being short was hard. But with good thinking and trying again, he could solve problems. That made him feel very tall inside.

The end.
