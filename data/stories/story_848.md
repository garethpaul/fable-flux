# Story story_848

**Music Time Magic**

Every morning after breakfast, Mama smiled big. "Time for music time, baby Lily!" she would say. The colorful music mat waited on the living room floor. Bright red drums sat next to yellow maracas. A tiny blue piano sparkled in the sunlight.

But today, baby Lily shook her head. "No music," she whispered. Her bottom lip stuck out. Music time felt too loud. Too busy. Too much.

Mama sat down on the soft carpet. "That's okay, little one," she said gently. "Would you like to just sit with me?" Lily crawled into Mama's warm lap. She felt safe there.

"Listen," Mama whispered. "Do you hear that?" Lily tilted her tiny ear up. Tweet, tweet, tweet! Birds sang outside the window. Their voices sounded sweet and high.

"The birds are making music," Mama said softly. Lily's eyes grew wide. She had never thought about bird songs as music before.

Tap, tap, tap. Rain drops danced on the roof. "The rain is drumming," Mama pointed up. Lily giggled. The gentle sounds made her feel cozy.

Whoosh! The wind blew through the trees. Leaves rustled and whispered. "Even the wind sings," Mama smiled. Lily listened harder. Everything around them made beautiful sounds.

"Music is everywhere," Mama said. "In our house too." She pointed to the kitchen. Tick, tick, tick went the clock. Hum, hum, hum went the refrigerator. Even their cat Mittens purred a soft song.

Lily looked at the music mat again. The instruments didn't look scary anymore. They looked friendly. Like they wanted to join the nature concert.

"Can I try?" Lily asked in her small voice. Mama nodded and helped her crawl to the mat. Lily picked up one yellow maraca. She shook it very gently. Shake, shake, shake. It sounded like rain on leaves.

The sound made her smile. She picked up the other maraca. Now both hands were shaking. Shake, shake, shake, shake! The rhythm felt good in her little arms.

Mama clapped softly. "Beautiful music, Lily!" The clapping sounded like gentle thunder. Lily giggled and shook her maracas faster.

Next, she touched the red drum with one finger. Tap! The sound was deeper than the maracas. She tapped again. Tap, tap! It reminded her of woodpeckers in the big oak tree.

"I'm making bird music!" Lily laughed. She tapped the drum with both hands now. The beats mixed with her maraca shaking. Her whole body wiggled with the sounds.

Mama picked up the tiny piano. She pressed one key. Ting! The note floated through the air like a butterfly. Lily stopped drumming to listen. Mama pressed another key. Ting, ting! Two musical butterflies danced together.

"My turn!" Lily crawled to the piano. Her small finger found a white key. Ting! She found a black key. Tong! Each note sounded different. Special. Magic.

Soon Lily was pressing lots of keys. The piano sang a happy, jumbled song. She drummed with one hand and played piano with the other. The maracas waited patiently for their turn.

"This is fun!" Lily shouted over her music. Her cheeks were pink and her eyes sparkled. The scared feeling was completely gone. Music time felt like playing in a sound garden.

Mama and Lily made music together for a long time. They drummed like rain. They shook like wind in trees. They played piano like singing birds. The whole room filled with their joyful noise.

When music time ended, Lily hugged each instrument. "Thank you for the songs," she whispered to them. She couldn't wait to play again.

That night, Lily dreamed about musical birds and drumming rain. When she woke up the next morning, she ran to Mama.

"Is it music time yet?" she asked with bright eyes. "I want to make more magic!"

Mama laughed and gave her the biggest hug. "Yes, my little musician. Let's make beautiful music together."

The end.
