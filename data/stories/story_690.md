# Story story_690

Max sat on the bathroom floor. He felt sad and lonely. His toy cars were scattered around him. But playing alone was not fun.

Max sighed. The bathroom was quiet. Too quiet. He picked up a red car. Then he put it down again.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," Max whispered.

Just then, something sparkled in the corner. Max looked closer. Behind the big white bathtub was a tiny blue bicycle. It was the prettiest bike Max had ever seen.

The little bike had shiny silver wheels. Its bell was golden and bright. The handlebars gleamed like stars.

"Hello," said a small, sweet voice.

Max's eyes grew wide. "Did you just talk?"

The blue bike giggled. "Yes! I'm Penny the bike. I've been watching you play. You look lonely."

Max smiled for the first time all day. "I am lonely. Do you want to be my friend?"

"Oh yes!" Penny said. Her bell jingled with joy. "I've been hoping for a friend too."

Max gently picked up Penny. She was just the right size for his hands. Her wheels spun smoothly. Her blue paint felt smooth and cool.

"What should we play?" Max asked.

Penny's bell rang happily. "Let's race around the bathroom!"

Max set Penny on the smooth tile floor. She zoomed around the toilet. She spun in circles by the sink. Max laughed and clapped his hands.

"My turn!" Max said. He crawled on his hands and knees. He chased Penny around the bathtub. They played tag together.

"This is so much fun!" Max giggled.

Penny agreed. "Playing with friends is the best!"

Next, they played hide and seek. Penny hid behind the toilet brush. Max found her quickly. Then Max covered his eyes. Penny rolled under the sink cabinet.

"Where are you, Penny?" Max called out.

"Here I am!" Penny's bell chimed. She rolled out with a big smile.

They played for a long time. Max forgot all about feeling lonely. The bathroom filled with their happy laughter.

"Let's build a race track," Penny suggested.

Max gathered his toy cars. He made a long line across the floor. Penny rolled alongside the cars. They pretended to race to the finish line.

"Beep beep!" Max made car sounds.

"Ring ring!" Penny's bell sang out.

They zoomed and spun together. The bathroom became their special playground.

Soon, Max's tummy rumbled. "I'm getting hungry," he said.

"That's okay," Penny said kindly. "We can play again tomorrow."

Max hugged Penny gently. "Thank you for being my friend. I was so sad before."

"I was lonely too," Penny admitted. "But now we have each other."

Max's mom called from the kitchen. "Max! Lunch time!"

"I have to go eat," Max told Penny. "But I'll come back soon."

"I'll wait right here," Penny promised. "Friends always wait for each other."

Max carefully placed Penny by the bathtub. He gave her one more gentle pat. Her blue paint sparkled in the bathroom light.

"See you soon, best friend," Max whispered.

Penny's bell gave a soft, happy ring. "See you soon, Max."

Max skipped to the kitchen. He felt warm and happy inside. His heart was full of joy. He had found something wonderful.

He had found a true friend.

At lunch, Max told his mom about his day. "I played in the bathroom. I had so much fun!"

His mom smiled. "That's wonderful, sweetie. Playing makes everything better."

Max nodded eagerly. He couldn't wait to play with Penny again. Having a friend made everything special.

The bathroom wasn't quiet anymore. It was filled with friendship and laughter. Max and Penny had found exactly what they needed.

They had found each other.

From that day on, Max was never lonely. Whenever he felt sad, he knew Penny was waiting. Their friendship grew stronger every day.

And they lived happily ever after.

The end.
