# Story story_1197

**The Book and the Pig**

In a sunny playground, a little book sat alone on a wooden bench. The book had bright red covers and golden pages that sparkled in the morning light. Birds chirped in the tall oak trees above.

The book watched children run and laugh. They zoomed down the twisty slide. They swung high on the creaky swings. The book felt very lonely.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," whispered the book sadly.

Just then, a small pink pig waddled into the playground. The pig had rosy cheeks and a curly tail. Her hooves made soft clicking sounds on the path.

The pig saw the book sitting all alone. She noticed the book's droopy pages. The pig's kind heart felt warm and fuzzy.

"Hello there," said the pig gently. "I'm Penny the pig. What's your name?"

The book perked up his pages. "I'm Benny the book. I was watching everyone play."

"Would you like to play with me?" asked Penny with a big smile.

Benny's pages fluttered with excitement. "Yes! But how can a book play?"

Penny thought for a moment. She tapped her hoof on the ground. "I have an idea! Follow me."

The pig led Benny to the sandbox. The sand felt warm and soft between Penny's hooves. Benny carefully placed his corner in the sand.

"We can build a castle together," said Penny happily.

Penny pushed sand with her snout. Benny used his pages like little shovels. They worked side by side in the sunshine.

The sand castle grew bigger and bigger. It had tall towers and deep moats. Benny felt proud of their teamwork.

"This is so much fun!" laughed Benny.

Next, they went to the see-saw. Penny climbed on one end. Benny opened his pages wide and sat on the other end.

Up and down they went! The see-saw creaked and groaned. Penny squealed with joy. Benny's pages flapped in the breeze.

"Wheee!" they shouted together.

After the see-saw, they played hide and seek. Penny closed her eyes tight. She counted to ten very slowly.

Benny hid behind the big slide. His red covers blended with the red paint. Penny searched everywhere.

"Where could Benny be?" wondered Penny out loud.

She looked under the monkey bars. She checked behind the swings. Finally, she spotted red covers peeking out.

"Found you!" giggled Penny.

Now it was Benny's turn to seek. Penny was tricky to find. She squeezed behind a tree trunk. Only her curly tail showed.

The two friends played until lunch time. The sun climbed high in the blue sky. Fluffy white clouds drifted overhead.

They sat together on the bench. Penny shared her apple slices. The apples were crisp and sweet. Benny told funny stories from his pages.

"You're the best friend ever," said Benny warmly.

"You're my best friend too," replied Penny with a happy oink.

Other children began to notice them. A little girl with pigtails came over. A boy with freckles followed behind her.

"Can we play too?" asked the little girl.

"Of course!" said Penny and Benny together.

Soon, more children joined their group. They all played together happily. The playground filled with laughter and joy.

Benny read exciting stories to everyone. His pages turned smoothly in the gentle breeze. The children sat in a circle around him.

Penny taught everyone pig games. They learned to roll in the soft grass. They practiced making pig sounds.

As the day ended, the sunset painted the sky orange and pink. The playground grew quiet and peaceful.

"Thank you for being my friend," said Benny to Penny.

"Thank you for sharing your stories," replied Penny sweetly.

They hugged goodbye with promises to play again tomorrow. Benny felt warm and happy inside. Penny skipped home with a smile.

From that day forward, Benny was never lonely. He had found his very best friend. The playground became their special place.

Every morning, they met by the big oak tree. Every day brought new adventures and fun.

Their friendship grew stronger with each passing day. The book and the pig were the happiest friends in the whole wide world.

The end.
