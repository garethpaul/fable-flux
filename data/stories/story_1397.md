# Story story_1397

Woolly's Bedtime Adventure

The sun was setting behind the green hills. Orange and pink colors painted the sky. It was bedtime on Farmer Mae's farm.

"Time for bed, little sheep!" called Farmer Mae. She rang her silver bell. Ding, ding, ding!

All the sheep walked slowly to the cozy barn. Their hooves made soft sounds on the dirt path. But one little sheep named Woolly stayed in the field.

Woolly loved playing outside. She loved rolling in the soft grass. She loved looking at the pretty flowers. She did not want to go to sleep.

"I'm not tired," Woolly said to herself. She sat down and crossed her fluffy white legs. "Sleep is boring."

Farmer Mae walked over to Woolly. Her boots crunched on the dried leaves. "What's wrong, little one?" she asked gently.

"I don't want to go to bed," Woolly said. Her voice was quiet and sad. "I want to keep playing."

Farmer Mae smiled. She had kind eyes that sparkled. "I have something special to show you," she said. "But we need to go to the barn first."

Woolly was curious. What could be special about bedtime? She stood up and followed Farmer Mae. Her woolly coat bounced as she walked.

Inside the barn, something magical was happening. Tiny lights twinkled everywhere! They looked like stars that had fallen from the sky. The lights were warm and golden.

"Wow!" Woolly gasped. Her eyes grew big and round. "What are those pretty lights?"

"These are sleepy-time stars," Farmer Mae explained. "They only come out when it's time for bed. They help little sheep have wonderful dreams."

The other sheep were already lying on their soft hay beds. The twinkling lights danced above them. Some sheep were already closing their eyes. They looked peaceful and happy.

Woolly touched one of the lights with her nose. It felt warm and tickly. The light made a tiny, musical sound. Like a lullaby just for her.

"Each light carries a special dream," Farmer Mae whispered. "Some have dreams about flying through clouds. Others have dreams about fields of rainbow flowers."

Woolly found her own hay bed. It was fluffy and smelled like sunshine. She lay down carefully. The hay felt soft against her woolly coat.

More lights floated down around her. They glowed gently like tiny suns. One light landed on her nose. Woolly giggled softly.

"This light has a dream about dancing," Farmer Mae said. "And that one has a dream about finding treasure."

Woolly's eyes began to feel heavy. The lights swayed back and forth. They moved like a gentle dance. The barn felt warm and safe.

"Will the lights be here tomorrow night?" Woolly asked with a yawn.

"Every single night," Farmer Mae promised. "They wait all day just for bedtime."

Woolly snuggled deeper into her hay bed. She watched the magical lights float around her friends. Some lights were blue like the sky. Others were pink like flower petals.

"I think I like bedtime now," Woolly whispered. Her voice was getting sleepy. "The lights are so beautiful."

"Sweet dreams, little Woolly," Farmer Mae said softly. She patted Woolly's head with her gentle hand.

As Woolly closed her eyes, the lights seemed to sing a quiet song. The song was about peaceful meadows and butterfly friends. About warm sunshine and cool streams.

Woolly drifted off to sleep with a smile. She dreamed about flying with the magical lights. In her dream, she soared over the farm and waved to all her friends below.

When morning came, Woolly woke up feeling happy and rested. She couldn't wait to play all day. But she also looked forward to something else.

"Tonight I get to see the sleepy-time stars again!" she said excitedly.

From that day on, Woolly never minded bedtime. She knew that magical things happened when little sheep went to sleep. And the most wonderful dreams were waiting just for her.

The end.
