# Story story_807

Zoe sat at the big dining room table. The wooden surface felt smooth under her hands. Sunlight streamed through the tall windows. It made pretty patterns on the floor.

She had her favorite puzzle spread out. The pieces showed a colorful garden. Zoe loved the bright flowers and butterflies. But something felt missing today.

"I wish I had someone to play with," Zoe whispered.

The dining room felt so quiet. Too quiet. Even the tick-tock of the clock sounded loud.

Just then, footsteps came down the hall. Max peeked around the corner. His curly hair bounced as he walked. He carried his toy cars in both hands.

"Hi Zoe," Max said softly. "What are you doing?"

"Working on my puzzle," Zoe replied. She didn't look up. "It's really hard."

Max took a step closer. The wooden floor creaked under his feet. "Can I help you?"

Zoe looked at Max's toy cars. Then she looked at her puzzle. "You probably want to play cars. Not puzzles."

"I like puzzles too," Max said. His voice was hopeful. "Please?"

Zoe thought for a moment. The dining room chairs were big enough for two. Maybe having help would be nice.

"Okay," she said. "You can sit here."

Max climbed onto the chair next to Zoe. His cars made a small clattering sound as he set them down. The smell of his strawberry shampoo was sweet and familiar.

"Where should I start?" Max asked.

Zoe pointed to the corner pieces. "These go on the edges first. See the straight sides?"

Max picked up a blue piece. It had fluffy white clouds on it. "This looks like sky!"

"Yes!" Zoe smiled. "Good job, Max."

They worked together quietly. Their fingers moved the pieces around. Sometimes they bumped hands reaching for the same spot. They giggled when that happened.

"This flower piece is tricky," Max said. He turned it three different ways.

Zoe leaned over to look. "Try it here. Next to the red tulip."

Click! The piece fit perfectly.

"We did it!" Max cheered. His eyes sparkled with joy.

As they worked, the dining room felt warmer. More cheerful. The afternoon sun made their puzzle glow like real flowers.

"Look at all the butterflies," Zoe said. She pointed to the yellow and orange pieces.

"They're beautiful," Max agreed. "Just like real ones in the garden."

Piece by piece, their puzzle grew. The empty spots got smaller. Their teamwork got better too.

"We make a good team," Zoe said. She felt happy inside. Much happier than when she played alone.

"The best team!" Max agreed. He high-fived Zoe with his small hand.

Finally, only one piece remained. It was right in the middle. A bright red rose with green leaves.

"You put it in," Zoe told Max. "We found it together."

Max carefully placed the last piece. The puzzle was complete! The garden looked perfect and whole.

"We finished it!" they shouted together.

The dining room filled with their laughter. Even the afternoon light seemed brighter. Their voices echoed off the tall ceiling.

"Want to do another puzzle?" Max asked hopefully.

"Yes!" Zoe said. "But first, let's play with your cars."

Max's face lit up like sunshine. "Really? You want to play cars?"

"We can drive them around the puzzle garden," Zoe suggested. "Like they're visiting the flowers."

They spent the rest of the afternoon playing together. Cars zoomed around puzzle flowers. Laughter filled every corner of the dining room.

When dinner time came, they didn't want to stop.

"Can we play again tomorrow?" Max asked.

"Every day," Zoe promised. "Friends always play together."

The dining room had felt lonely before. Now it felt full of friendship and joy. Zoe learned that playing together was much more fun than playing alone.

And Max learned that trying new games could lead to wonderful friendships.

From that day on, the dining room became their special place. A place where two friends shared puzzles, cars, and lots of happy memories.

The end.
