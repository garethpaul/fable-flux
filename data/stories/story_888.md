# Story story_888

**The Sandbox Friends**

Maya sat alone in the big sandbox. The warm sand felt soft between her fingers. She made little hills and valleys. But something was missing.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," Maya said softly.

The sandbox felt too quiet. Too empty. Maya's shoulders drooped as she patted another sand mountain.

Just then, she heard gentle footsteps. Clip-clop, clip-clop. A beautiful brown horse walked over to the fence.

The horse had kind, dark eyes. His mane was shiny and black. He stretched his long neck toward Maya.

"Hello there," Maya whispered. "Do you want to play?"

The horse nodded his big head. He made a soft whinnying sound. It sounded like "yes" to Maya.

But there was one problem. The horse was too big for the sandbox. His hooves wouldn't fit inside.

Maya thought hard. How could they play together?

"I have an idea!" she said brightly.

Maya scooped up handfuls of sand. She carried them to the fence. The sand felt cool and grainy in her palms.

She made a small pile near the horse's feet. The horse sniffed the sand with his soft nose. It tickled Maya and made her giggle.

"Let's build something together," Maya said.

Maya patted the sand into a little castle. The horse watched with interest. His ears pointed forward.

Then the horse did something amazing. He gently pushed sand with his nose. He helped make the castle bigger!

"You're so smart!" Maya laughed.

They worked side by side. Maya used her hands. The horse used his nose. Together they built the most wonderful sand castle.

Maya added towers and walls. The horse smoothed the sides. The castle grew taller and prettier.

"This is the best castle ever," Maya said happily.

The horse whinnied in agreement. His tail swished back and forth.

Maya noticed the horse looking at the sandbox. His eyes seemed sad. Maybe he wanted to try the soft sand too.

"Wait right here," Maya told her new friend.

Maya ran to get her bucket. It was bright red and shiny. She filled it with the finest, softest sand.

She brought the bucket to the horse. "Here," she said. "This sand is just for you."

The horse sniffed the bucket. Then he gently touched the sand with his nose. His eyes sparkled with joy.

Maya giggled as the horse played. He made little holes in the sand. He tossed some sand in the air with his nose.

"You like it!" Maya clapped her hands.

For the rest of the afternoon, they played together. Maya built roads in her sandbox. The horse made patterns in his bucket of sand.

They shared stories without words. Maya would show the horse her creations. The horse would nicker softly to show he liked them.

When Maya felt thirsty, she shared her water. The horse drank from her cupped hands. The water felt cool and refreshing.

When the horse felt playful, he would toss sand gently. Maya would laugh and toss some back. They had their own special game.

As the sun began to set, Maya felt warm and happy. The lonely feeling was completely gone.

"Thank you for being my friend," Maya told the horse.

The horse lowered his head. Maya reached through the fence and patted his soft neck. His fur felt warm and smooth.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" Maya asked hopefully.

The horse nodded again. He made his gentle whinnying sound.

Maya smiled the biggest smile. She had found the most wonderful friend. A friend who loved to play in the sand just like her.

From that day on, Maya was never lonely in the sandbox. Every afternoon, her horse friend would visit. They would build castles and play games.

Maya learned something important. Friendship can happen anywhere. Even between a little girl and a gentle horse. Even in a simple sandbox.

The best friendships are built together, one grain of sand at a time.

The end.
