# Story story_744

**The Sandbox Friends**

Drum sat alone in the big sandbox. He had shiny red skin and made soft thumping sounds when touched. The warm sand felt good under him, but he felt sad. No one wanted to play with him today.

"Thump, thump," he said quietly. His voice echoed in the empty playground. The sun was bright and warm, but Drum wished for a friend.

Little Lily walked by with her bucket and shovel. She had curly brown hair and wore a yellow dress with flowers. She heard the soft thumping sounds and stopped.

"Hello," said Lily. "What are you doing?"

"I'm just sitting here," said Drum. "I want to play, but I don't know how to build castles."

Lily's eyes sparkled. She loved making new friends. "I can teach you! Building castles is fun."

Drum felt excited but worried. "But I'm round. I can't hold a shovel like you."

"That's okay," said Lily with a big smile. "We can find other ways to play together."

Lily sat down in the soft, warm sand. She filled her blue bucket with sand. The sand made a nice whooshing sound as it fell in.

"You can help me pat the sand," said Lily. "Your round shape is perfect for making the castle smooth."

Drum rolled closer. He gently pressed against the sand castle. The sand felt cool and grainy against his skin. "Like this?" he asked.

"Perfect!" said Lily. She clapped her hands with joy. "You're really good at this!"

They worked together for a long time. Lily dug moats around their castle. Drum rolled back and forth to make the walls smooth and round. The sand stuck to their hands and made them giggle.

"This is the best castle ever," said Drum. He felt warm and happy inside. Making friends was easier than he thought.

"Let's make music too," said Lily. She patted Drum's side gently. He made a beautiful, deep sound that echoed across the playground.

"Boom, boom, pat, pat," they sang together. Other children heard their happy sounds and came over to watch.

"Can we play too?" asked a little boy with freckles.

"Yes!" said Lily and Drum together. Soon the sandbox was full of laughing children. They all took turns patting Drum and building castles.

Drum had never felt so happy. His thumping sounds mixed with the children's giggles. The sand flew everywhere as they played. Their hands got dirty, but nobody cared.

As the sun began to set, the other children went home. Lily and Drum sat together in their sandbox. Their beautiful castle stood tall and proud.

"Thank you for playing with me," said Drum. "I was lonely before you came."

"Thank you for being my friend," said Lily. "You make the best music, and you're really good at building castles."

They watched the orange and pink sunset together. The sandbox felt cozy and warm around them. Tomorrow they would play again.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" asked Drum hopefully.

"Of course!" said Lily. "Best friends always play together."

Drum made one last happy thump. The sound echoed across the quiet playground like a promise. He wasn't lonely anymore.

Lily gave Drum a gentle hug before going home. "See you tomorrow, friend," she whispered.

The stars began to twinkle in the dark sky. Drum stayed in the sandbox, surrounded by their castle. He felt proud and happy. Making friends was the best adventure of all.

The next morning, Lily came running back to the sandbox. She had new ideas for games they could play. Drum's happy thumping filled the air as she arrived.

From that day on, they were the very best of friends. They played in the sandbox every day, making music, building castles, and sharing lots of laughs. Their friendship grew stronger with each passing day, and the sandbox became their special place where anything was possible.

The end.
