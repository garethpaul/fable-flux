# Story story_830

**Ben's Magical Snack Time**

The big clock on the kitchen wall said three o'clock. "Snack time, Ben!" called Mom from the kitchen. She had a cheerful voice that usually made Ben smile.

But today, Ben didn't feel like smiling. He sat on his bedroom floor with his toy cars. His tummy felt a little rumbly, but he didn't want to stop playing. "Do I have to?" Ben asked, dragging his feet down the hallway.

Mom was setting out apple slices on a blue plate. She had put peanut butter in a small bowl next to them. The apples looked shiny and crisp. The peanut butter smelled nutty and sweet. But Ben just stared at his snack with a frown.

"I'm not hungry," Ben said, even though his tummy made a tiny growling sound. He poked at an apple slice with his finger. It felt cool and smooth.

Mom sat down next to Ben at the little table. "Sometimes snacks taste better when we make them special," she said with a wink. She picked up an apple slice and dipped it in peanut butter. "What if this apple slice could talk? What do you think it would say?"

Ben looked at the apple slice more carefully. It had tiny brown seeds that looked like little eyes. The peanut butter made it look like it had a creamy mustache. "Maybe it would say hello?" Ben whispered.

"Hello there, Ben!" Mom said in a funny, squeaky voice, pretending the apple was talking. "I'm Apple Annie! I've been waiting all day to meet you!"

Ben giggled. The apple slice did look friendly with its peanut butter smile. "Hi, Apple Annie," Ben said softly.

Mom picked up another slice. "And I'm Captain Crunch!" she said in a deeper voice. "I sailed here from the apple tree to be your friend!"

Ben's eyes got wide with wonder. He reached for an apple slice and dipped it carefully in the peanut butter. The creamy spread felt smooth on the apple. "What's your name?" Ben asked his snack.

"I'm Super Apple!" Ben announced in his own special voice. "I give kids energy to play and grow big and strong!"

Ben took a small bite. The apple crunched between his teeth with a satisfying sound. The sweet juice mixed with the salty peanut butter taste. It was delicious! His tummy felt happy and warm.

"Super Apple tastes really good," Ben said with his mouth a little full. He took another bigger bite. Crunch, crunch, crunch.

Mom smiled and picked up her own apple slice. "I'm Princess Peanut Butter," she said in a fancy voice. "I make everything taste extra yummy!"

Ben laughed so hard that he almost snorted. Soon, he and Mom were having a whole conversation with their apple slices. They made up silly voices and funny stories. Ben ate slice after slice without even thinking about it.

The kitchen filled with their giggles and crunching sounds. Sunlight streamed through the window, making the apple slices sparkle. Ben's tummy felt perfectly full and satisfied.

"That was the best snack ever!" Ben said, licking the last bit of peanut butter from his fingers. It tasted salty and sweet.

Mom gave Ben a warm hug that smelled like vanilla lotion. "When we use our imagination, everything becomes more fun," she said.

Ben helped Mom wash the dishes in the warm, soapy water. The bubbles felt slippery and tickly on his hands. "Mom, can we have talking snacks again tomorrow?" he asked hopefully.

"Of course we can," Mom said. "What kind of voices do you think the crackers and cheese will have?"

Ben's eyes sparkled with excitement. He was already thinking about tomorrow's snack adventure. Maybe the crackers would be brave knights and the cheese could be a friendly dragon!

From that day on, Ben always looked forward to snack time. Because he learned that with a little imagination, even ordinary things could become magical and wonderful.

The end.
