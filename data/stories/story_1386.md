# Story story_1386

Ben sat on his back porch steps. He felt very lonely today. The sun was warm on his face. But his heart felt sad.

"I wish I had someone to play with," Ben said softly.

He looked around his big backyard. There were pretty flowers everywhere. Red roses smelled sweet. Yellow daisies danced in the breeze. But Ben had no one to share them with.

Ben picked up his red ball. He threw it high in the air. It bounced on the soft grass. He caught it and threw it again. Playing alone was not very fun.

Behind the tall oak tree, something moved. A small brown bunny peeked out. The bunny had long ears and a fluffy white tail. His nose twitched as he sniffed the air.

The bunny watched Ben play. He wanted to join in too. But he felt scared. What if Ben didn't want to play with a bunny?

Ben threw his ball again. This time it rolled toward the oak tree. The bunny hopped closer to look. The ball was bright red and very bouncy.

"Oh!" said Ben. "A bunny!"

The bunny froze. His little heart beat fast. He wanted to run away. But Ben's voice sounded kind.

"Don't be scared," Ben said gently. "I won't hurt you."

Ben sat down on the grass. He made himself very small. The bunny took one tiny hop forward. Then another hop. Soon he was close to Ben.

"Do you want to play with me?" Ben asked.

The bunny wiggled his nose. That meant yes in bunny language.

Ben rolled the ball slowly toward the bunny. The bunny sniffed it with his pink nose. It smelled like rubber and sunshine. He pushed it back with his little paws.

"You're good at this!" Ben laughed.

They rolled the ball back and forth. The bunny's ears bounced up and down. Ben giggled at how cute he looked.

Next, they played hide and seek. Ben closed his eyes and counted. "One, two, three, four, five!"

The bunny hid behind a big sunflower. His brown fur blended with the dirt. But his white tail stuck out.

"I found you!" Ben said with a smile.

Then it was Ben's turn to hide. He crouched behind the garden shed. The bunny hopped around looking for him. When he found Ben, he did a happy little dance.

They played tag next. Ben ran on his legs. The bunny hopped on his strong back feet. Around the yard they went. Past the rose bushes. Under the apple tree. Through the tall grass.

Both friends were getting tired. They sat under the shady oak tree. Ben's cheeks were pink from running. The bunny's fur was warm from the sun.

"I'm so happy you came to play," Ben said.

He gently petted the bunny's soft ears. The bunny closed his eyes. He felt safe and loved.

Ben's mom came outside with two cups. One had cold water for Ben. The other had fresh carrots for the bunny.

"You made a new friend!" Mom said with a big smile.

"This is my best bunny friend," Ben said proudly.

The bunny munched his orange carrots. They were crisp and sweet. Ben drank his cool water. It tasted refreshing after all their games.

As the sun began to set, the sky turned orange and pink. The bunny knew it was time to go home. But he didn't want to leave his new friend.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" Ben asked hopefully.

The bunny nodded his little head. He would definitely return.

Ben hugged his bunny friend very gently. The bunny's fur felt like silk. His small body was warm and cozy.

"See you tomorrow!" Ben called as the bunny hopped away.

Ben waved until his friend disappeared into the woods. His heart felt full of joy now. He was no longer lonely.

The next morning, Ben ran to his backyard. There by the oak tree sat his bunny friend. They had many more adventures waiting for them.

From that day on, Ben and the bunny played together every single day. They were the very best of friends forever and always.

The end.
