# Story story_1184

**Grandpa's Magic Stories**

Every evening at seven o'clock, the big clock chimed. "Story time!" called Mama from the kitchen. Little Emma ran to find Grandpa Joe sitting in his favorite blue chair. The soft cushions made a gentle whoosh sound when he sat down.

But tonight, Grandpa Joe looked tired. His shoulders drooped like a sleepy cat. "Oh, Emma," he sighed softly. "Grandpa doesn't feel like telling stories today. My voice is scratchy. My head feels heavy."

Emma's smile turned into a worried frown. She loved story time more than cookies. More than cartoons. Even more than playing in puddles! "Please, Grandpa?" she whispered. Her big brown eyes looked sad.

Grandpa Joe rubbed his gray beard. It made a scratchy sound like sandpaper. "Well," he said slowly. "Maybe just one tiny story. But it might not be very good tonight."

Emma clapped her small hands together. They made a happy pat-pat sound. She climbed onto Grandpa's warm lap. His sweater smelled like peppermint and old books.

"Once upon a time," Grandpa began in his tired voice. But then something wonderful happened. As he spoke, his voice grew stronger. "There was a little mouse who lived in a cheese castle."

Emma giggled. "A cheese castle? That's silly, Grandpa!"

Grandpa's eyes began to twinkle like stars. "This mouse wore tiny purple boots that squeaked when he walked. Squeak, squeak, squeak!" Grandpa made the funny sound with his mouth.

Emma laughed louder. Her giggles sounded like tiny bells ringing. "What happened next?"

Grandpa sat up straighter. His voice became deeper and more exciting. "The mouse met a friendly dragon who breathed bubbles instead of fire! Pop, pop, pop went the bubbles everywhere!"

Emma bounced on his lap. "More, more!"

Now Grandpa was having fun too. He used different voices for each character. The mouse sounded squeaky and small. The dragon's voice was big and booming. His hands moved through the air like he was painting pictures with words.

"The dragon and mouse became best friends," Grandpa continued. "They rode on rainbow slides and ate cotton candy clouds that tasted like strawberries."

Emma could almost taste the sweet, fluffy clouds. She licked her lips and smiled wide.

Grandpa's tiredness melted away like ice cream in sunshine. His cheeks turned rosy pink. His eyes sparkled brighter than Christmas lights. "And they lived happily ever after in their magical kingdom!"

"That was the best story ever!" Emma cheered. She hugged Grandpa tight around his neck. He smelled warm and safe.

Grandpa hugged her back. "You know what, little one? I feel much better now. Stories are like magic medicine. They make everything brighter."

Emma nodded seriously. "Your voice wasn't scratchy anymore, Grandpa. It sounded like music!"

Grandpa chuckled. The sound rumbled in his chest like a happy motor. "That's because you helped me remember how much fun stories can be. When we share stories, they become twice as magical."

Emma yawned a tiny yawn. Her eyelids felt heavy like soft blankets. "Will you tell me another story tomorrow, Grandpa?"

"Of course, sweetheart," Grandpa said gently. He tucked a strand of her hair behind her ear. "Tomorrow we'll visit the singing flowers and the dancing trees."

Emma's eyes grew wide with excitement. "Really?"

"Really," Grandpa promised. "And maybe the purple-booted mouse will join us for another adventure."

As Mama came to take Emma to bed, Grandpa was already thinking about tomorrow's story. His heart felt light and happy. He realized that story time wasn't just for Emma. It was magical for him too.

The next evening, when the big clock chimed seven, Grandpa Joe was ready and waiting. His eyes twinkled with new stories to tell. And Emma ran faster than ever to climb onto his lap for another magical adventure together.

The end.
