# Story story_1472

**Moo-nificent Art Time**

The big yellow sun smiled down on Sunny Meadow Farm. All the animals gathered in the red barn. It was art time!

"Come on, Bessie!" called Teacher Hen. "Time to make beautiful pictures!"

Bessie the cow stood by the fence. Her black and white spots seemed to droop. She watched the other animals rush inside.

"I don't want to do art," Bessie said softly. "My hooves are too big. I can't hold a paintbrush."

Pig was already painting pink flowers. Sheep drew fluffy white clouds. Duck made yellow sunshine with her orange beak.

Bessie felt sad. She wanted to create something beautiful too.

Teacher Hen noticed Bessie outside. She walked over with gentle steps. "What's wrong, dear Bessie?"

"My hooves won't work," Bessie sighed. "Everyone else has paws or beaks. I can't make art like them."

Teacher Hen's eyes twinkled. "Let me show you something special."

She led Bessie to a big white paper. It covered the whole barn floor. Next to it sat shallow pans. They held red, blue, and yellow paint.

"Step right in," Teacher Hen said with a smile.

Bessie looked confused. "In the paint?"

"Yes! Your hooves are perfect for this."

Bessie carefully stepped into the blue paint. It felt cool and squishy between her toes. The paint squished up around her hooves.

"Now walk on the paper," Teacher Hen encouraged.

Bessie took one step. Then another. Blue hoof prints appeared on the white paper. They looked like beautiful flowers!

"Oh my!" Bessie gasped. "Look what I made!"

She stepped in red paint next. Her hooves made lovely red circles. Then she tried yellow paint. Golden shapes bloomed across the paper.

Soon Bessie was dancing! She made big steps and little steps. She walked in circles and straight lines. The paint felt wonderful and warm.

"This is amazing!" Bessie laughed. Her tail swished with joy.

The other animals gathered around. They watched Bessie create her masterpiece.

"Those are the most beautiful flowers I've ever seen," said Pig.

"Your hoof prints look like sunshine," added Duck.

Sheep clapped her hooves together. "You're a real artist, Bessie!"

Bessie's heart felt as warm as summer sunshine. She had never felt so proud. Her hooves weren't too big at all. They were just right for making art!

She added purple paint to her creation. Then orange and green. Soon the paper looked like a magical garden. Every color mixed and swirled together.

"I love how the paint feels," Bessie said. "It's smooth and cool. And look at all these pretty colors!"

Teacher Hen smiled proudly. "Every animal has their own special way to make art. Yours is wonderful, Bessie."

When art time ended, everyone admired Bessie's painting. It covered the whole barn floor. Bright colors danced everywhere like a rainbow.

"Can we hang it up?" asked Pig.

"It's too big for the wall," Teacher Hen laughed. "But we can keep it on the floor. Everyone can walk on this beautiful art."

Bessie felt so happy. Her chest puffed out with pride. She had made something truly special.

That evening, Bessie told her mama all about art time. "I painted with my hooves! It was the best day ever!"

Mama Cow nuzzled Bessie's head. "I'm so proud of you for trying something new."

As the stars came out, Bessie looked toward the barn. Tomorrow there would be art time again. She could hardly wait!

"I wonder what I'll paint tomorrow," she said to herself. "Maybe I'll make a whole field of flowers. Or a big blue sky!"

Bessie closed her eyes and dreamed of colors. In her dreams, she danced through paint. She made beautiful pictures with every step.

Art time had become her favorite time of all.

The next morning, Bessie woke up early. She couldn't wait to get to the barn. Her hooves were ready to create more magic!

The end.
