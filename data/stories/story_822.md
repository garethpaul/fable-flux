# Story story_822

Ben sat in the big sandbox. The sand felt warm on his hands. He made little hills and valleys. But something was missing.

Ben felt lonely. He wished he had a friend to play with. The sandbox was so big for just one little boy.

"I wish someone would come play," Ben said softly.

Just then, he heard soft footsteps. Clip-clop, clip-clop. Ben looked up and gasped.

A small zebra stood by the sandbox! The zebra had black and white stripes. Her mane was soft and fuzzy. Her eyes were big and kind.

"Hello," said the zebra in a sweet voice. "I'm Zara. Can I play too?"

Ben's eyes grew wide. A talking zebra! This was amazing.

"Yes!" Ben said with a big smile. "I'm Ben. I was feeling lonely."

Zara stepped into the sandbox. Her hooves made little prints in the sand. The sand was cool between her toes.

"What should we build?" asked Zara.

Ben thought hard. "How about a castle?"

"I love castles!" Zara said happily.

They started digging together. Ben used his hands. Zara used her hooves. The sand flew everywhere.

Ben made the castle walls. Zara made the towers tall. They worked side by side.

"This is fun!" Ben laughed.

"Much better than playing alone," Zara agreed.

But then their castle fell down. Plop! The sand scattered.

Ben felt sad. "Oh no! Our castle broke."

Zara nuzzled Ben's shoulder. "That's okay. We can try again."

"Really?" Ben asked.

"Friends help each other," Zara said. "Let's make it stronger this time."

They started building again. This time, Ben packed the sand tight. Zara stamped it down with her hooves.

"Press hard," Ben said.

"Like this?" Zara asked, stamping carefully.

"Perfect!" Ben clapped.

Their new castle was bigger. It had four tall towers. They made a moat around it too.

"We need a bridge," said Ben.

Zara found a stick. "How about this?"

Ben placed the stick across the moat. "Now the king can cross!"

They made little sand people. Ben made a king and queen. Zara made horses for them to ride.

"The horses look like me!" Zara giggled.

Ben laughed too. "But smaller!"

The sun felt warm on their backs. A gentle breeze blew. The sand sparkled like tiny diamonds.

"I'm thirsty," said Ben.

"Me too," said Zara.

Ben's mom brought them cold water. She smiled at the zebra.

"What a lovely friend you have, Ben," she said.

Ben beamed with pride. "This is Zara. She's the best!"

After their drink, they kept playing. They built roads in the sand. They made mountains and valleys. They dug tunnels and caves.

"Look!" Zara pointed with her nose. "A sand river!"

Ben traced the wiggly line. "It goes to the ocean!"

They made boats from leaves. The boats sailed down their sand river.

"This is the best day ever," Ben said.

"I think so too," Zara replied.

As the sun started to set, they sat together. The sandbox was full of their creations. Castle, roads, rivers, and mountains.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" Ben asked hopefully.

Zara's eyes twinkled. "Of course! Friends always come back."

Ben hugged Zara's neck. Her fur was soft and warm.

"Thank you for playing with me," Ben whispered.

"Thank you for sharing your sandbox," Zara whispered back.

Ben's mom called from the house. "Time to come in, Ben!"

Ben waved goodbye to Zara. "See you tomorrow!"

Zara trotted away, her stripes glowing in the sunset.

Ben looked at their sandbox kingdom. Tomorrow they would build even more amazing things together.

He wasn't lonely anymore. He had found a true friend.

Ben skipped to the house, his heart full of joy. The best adventures happen when friends play together.

And in the sandbox, their castle stood strong under the stars.

The end.
