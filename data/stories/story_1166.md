# Story story_1166

**Captain Splash's Sleepy Adventure**

The sun was setting over the harbor. Golden light danced on the water. All the boats were getting ready for bed.

Captain Splash was a little red tugboat. He had bright yellow stripes on his sides. His horn made a cheerful "toot-toot" sound.

"Time for bed, Captain Splash," called Harbor Master Ben. Ben wore a cozy blue sweater. He waved from the dock.

Captain Splash puffed out his smokestack. "But I'm not sleepy yet!" he said. "I want to keep sailing around the harbor."

The other boats were already settling down. Big Bella the ferry had her lights dimmed. Speedy Sam the sailboat had folded his sails neatly. Even Old Pete the fishing boat was quiet.

"Please, just five more minutes?" asked Captain Splash. His engine made soft rumbling sounds.

Harbor Master Ben smiled kindly. "Even brave tugboats need their rest," he said. "Tomorrow will bring new adventures."

Captain Splash slowly chugged to his sleeping spot. The water felt cool and gentle underneath him. But he still didn't want to close his eyes.

Suddenly, something magical began to happen. Tiny lights started twinkling in the water. They looked like underwater stars!

"Wow!" gasped Captain Splash. "What are those pretty lights?"

"Those are sea fireflies," whispered Harbor Master Ben. "They only come out when little boats are getting ready for sleep."

The glowing creatures swirled around Captain Splash. They tickled his hull with their soft light. The water sparkled like liquid diamonds.

More sea fireflies appeared. They made swirling patterns in the dark water. Some were blue like sapphires. Others were green like emeralds.

"They're so beautiful," said Captain Splash softly. His voice was getting sleepy now.

The sea fireflies began to dance in circles. Round and round they went. Their gentle light made Captain Splash feel warm and cozy.

"They're dancing just for you," said Harbor Master Ben. "It's their special bedtime show."

Captain Splash watched the magical display. The sea fireflies moved like a underwater ballet. Their light grew softer and softer.

Soon, Captain Splash's eyes felt heavy. The rocking motion of the water was soothing. The sea fireflies' gentle glow was peaceful.

"This is the most wonderful bedtime ever," yawned Captain Splash. His smokestack puffed out one last sleepy cloud.

Harbor Master Ben tucked a soft rope around Captain Splash. "Sweet dreams, little tugboat," he whispered.

The sea fireflies slowly faded away. But their magic stayed in the water. Captain Splash felt safe and happy in his harbor home.

His red paint gleamed in the moonlight. His yellow stripes looked like golden ribbons. The gentle waves rocked him like a lullaby.

"Will the sea fireflies come back tomorrow night?" asked Captain Splash sleepily.

"Every night when you're ready for bed," promised Harbor Master Ben. "They love dancing for good little boats."

Captain Splash smiled with his eyes closed. "Then I can't wait for bedtime tomorrow," he said.

The harbor grew quiet and peaceful. All the boats were sleeping soundly. The water lapped gently against the dock.

Captain Splash dreamed of sailing through seas of starlight. He dreamed of dancing with the sea fireflies. He dreamed of all the adventures waiting for tomorrow.

When morning came, Captain Splash woke up refreshed. His engine hummed with energy. His horn tooted happily.

"Good morning, Harbor Master Ben!" he called out cheerfully.

"Good morning, Captain Splash," said Ben. "Ready for today's adventures?"

"Yes!" said Captain Splash. "And I'm already excited for tonight too!"

From that day on, Captain Splash never complained about bedtime. He knew something magical was waiting for him. The sea fireflies had made bedtime the best part of his day.

Every evening, he would sail to his sleeping spot eagerly. He would watch for the first twinkle of underwater light. And he would drift off to sleep surrounded by dancing magic.

Bedtime had become Captain Splash's favorite adventure of all.

The end.
