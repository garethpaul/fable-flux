# Story story_646

**Tilly the Tiger's Big Day**

Tilly the Tiger sat on her soft blue bed. Her orange and black stripes glowed in the morning sun. Today was her first day at Forest School. Her tummy felt funny and fluttery.

"I don't want to go," Tilly whispered. She pulled her fuzzy blanket up to her chin. The blanket smelled like her mama's warm hugs.

Mama Tiger peeked into the cozy bedroom. "What's wrong, little one?" she asked gently.

"School feels too scary," Tilly said. Her golden eyes looked worried. "What if the other animals don't like me? What if I can't find the bathroom?"

Mama Tiger sat on the bed. It creaked softly under her weight. "Let's think of ways to feel better," she said.

Tilly had an idea. "I'll hide under my blanket all day!" She pulled the soft fabric over her head. Under the blanket, everything felt dark and safe.

But soon it got very hot. Tilly couldn't breathe well. The blanket stuck to her fur. This wasn't working at all.

Tilly poked her head out. Her fur was messy and damp. "That didn't help," she sighed.

"What else could you try?" Mama asked kindly.

Tilly thought hard. She tapped her furry paw on her chin. Then her face lit up like sunshine.

"I could take something special with me!" Tilly jumped off the bed. Her paws made soft sounds on the wooden floor.

She looked around her room carefully. On her shelf sat Mr. Buttons, her favorite teddy bear. He was brown and squishy. One of his button eyes was loose.

"Mr. Buttons always makes me brave," Tilly said. She hugged him tight. He smelled like vanilla cookies and felt warm and soft.

Tilly put Mr. Buttons in her new school backpack. The backpack was bright red with yellow stars. "Now I won't be alone," she said happily.

At breakfast, Tilly ate her favorite honey toast. The sweet honey dripped down her chin. She felt much better now.

"Are you ready for school?" Papa Tiger asked. His deep voice was gentle and kind.

"Yes!" Tilly said. She patted her backpack. "Mr. Buttons is coming too."

At Forest School, Tilly met Ruby Rabbit and Sam Squirrel. They had kind faces and friendly smiles.

"What's in your backpack?" Ruby asked. Her pink nose twitched with curiosity.

Tilly carefully took out Mr. Buttons. "This is my special friend," she said proudly.

"He's wonderful!" Sam said. His bushy tail wiggled with excitement. "I brought my lucky acorn."

Ruby showed them her smooth worry stone. "My grandma gave this to me," she explained.

The three new friends sat together at snack time. They shared apple slices and crackers. The apples were crisp and sweet. The crackers crunched loudly when they bit them.

During art time, Tilly painted a picture of her family. The paint felt cool and smooth on her paws. She made bright orange stripes just like hers.

"You're a great painter!" their teacher, Mrs. Owl, hooted kindly.

At the end of the school day, Tilly felt proud and happy. Her nervousness had melted away like ice cream in the sun.

"How was school?" Mama Tiger asked when Tilly got home.

"It was amazing!" Tilly exclaimed. She danced around the living room. "I made two new friends. We all brought special things to help us feel brave."

That night, Tilly put Mr. Buttons back on her shelf. "Thank you for helping me," she whispered to her teddy bear.

As she snuggled under her cozy blanket, Tilly smiled. Tomorrow she would go to school again. She felt excited instead of scared.

"When something feels hard, I can find ways to help myself," Tilly said softly.

Outside her window, the stars twinkled like tiny diamonds. Tilly closed her eyes and dreamed happy dreams about her new school friends.

Sometimes being brave means bringing a little help along the way.

The end.
