# Story story_1138

Anna loved her backyard. The green grass felt soft under her bare feet. Colorful flowers smelled sweet in the warm sunshine.

Today was special. Anna had new sidewalk chalk. She had twelve bright colors in a shiny box. Pink, purple, blue, yellow, and more!

"I'm going to make the most beautiful picture ever," Anna said.

She sat on the smooth concrete. She drew a big rainbow. She added fluffy white clouds. She drew her house with red bricks.

"Wow! That's amazing!" said a voice.

Anna looked up. Her neighbor Lucy stood by the fence. Lucy's brown eyes sparkled with excitement.

"Can I help you draw?" Lucy asked. "Please?"

Anna hugged her chalk box tight. "No. This is mine. Go away."

Lucy's smile disappeared. Her shoulders drooped. She walked back to her yard slowly.

Anna kept drawing. She made purple butterflies. She added orange flowers. But something felt wrong. The colors didn't look as bright. The picture didn't feel as fun.

Anna looked at Lucy's yard. Lucy sat alone on her porch steps. She looked sad and bored.

"Maybe I should share," Anna thought. "But what if Lucy uses all my chalk?"

Anna had an idea. She picked up one old, tiny piece of white chalk. It was almost too small to hold.

"Lucy!" Anna called. "You can use this one."

Lucy came over. She looked at the tiny chalk piece. "Thank you," she said quietly.

Lucy tried to draw with the small chalk. It broke into even tinier pieces. She could barely make any marks.

Anna watched Lucy struggle. The white marks were hard to see. Lucy's flower looked like a small, pale blob.

"This isn't working," Anna thought. "Lucy can't really draw with that tiny piece."

Anna felt sorry. Lucy looked frustrated and disappointed. Anna remembered when her big brother wouldn't share his toys. It made her feel left out and sad.

"Wait, Lucy," Anna said. "I have a better idea."

Anna opened her chalk box wide. All twelve colors sat in neat rows. They looked like a rainbow in a box.

"Let's draw together," Anna said. "You can pick any colors you want."

Lucy's face lit up like sunshine. "Really? Any colors?"

"Yes! Let's make the biggest, most colorful picture ever!"

Lucy picked bright red chalk. Anna chose sunny yellow. They drew side by side on the concrete.

"Let's make a garden," Lucy suggested.

"Yes! With lots of flowers," Anna agreed.

They drew roses, daisies, and tulips. Lucy made red ladybugs. Anna added yellow bees. The chalk made soft scraping sounds on the concrete.

"Your bees look so real," Lucy said.

"Your ladybugs are perfect," Anna replied.

They worked together for a long time. The sun felt warm on their backs. Birds chirped happy songs in the trees.

Soon their picture covered the whole sidewalk. It had flowers, bugs, trees, and even a bright blue pond.

"This is the best picture ever!" Lucy exclaimed.

Anna looked at their creation. It was much bigger and more beautiful than her first picture. The colors seemed brighter when they worked together.

"You're right," Anna said. "It's amazing what we can make together."

Anna's mom came outside. "Wow! What a wonderful picture, girls!"

"We made it together," Anna said proudly. "Sharing made it better."

Lucy smiled big. "Thank you for sharing, Anna. You're a great friend."

Anna felt warm and happy inside. Sharing her chalk didn't make her have less fun. It made everything more fun!

"Want to draw more tomorrow?" Anna asked.

"Yes!" Lucy said. "I'll bring my colored pencils to share too."

Anna learned something important that day. Sharing didn't take away from her happiness. It made her happiness grow bigger. Just like their beautiful sidewalk picture, good things became even better when shared with friends.

The two girls admired their colorful creation as the sun began to set. Tomorrow would bring new adventures and more sharing.

The end.
