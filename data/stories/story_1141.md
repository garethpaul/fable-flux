# Story story_1141

**Freddy Fox's Missing Blocks**

Freddy Fox loved playing in his sunny garden. The grass felt soft under his orange paws. Pretty flowers smelled sweet in the morning air.

Every day, Freddy built tall towers with his colorful blocks. He had red blocks and blue blocks. He had yellow blocks and green blocks too. Building made him very happy.

But today was different. Freddy looked everywhere for his blocks. He checked under the big oak tree. No blocks there. He looked behind the rose bushes. Still no blocks.

"Where are my blocks?" Freddy asked sadly. His fluffy tail drooped down. His ears felt heavy.

Freddy's mom heard him from the kitchen window. "What's wrong, sweetie?" she called out.

"I can't find my blocks anywhere!" Freddy said. His voice sounded worried.

"Keep looking, dear," Mom said kindly. "They must be somewhere in the garden."

Freddy decided to search more carefully. He would look in every corner. He would check every hiding spot.

First, he ran to the vegetable patch. Maybe the blocks were hiding with the carrots. Freddy dug around the orange carrots. The dirt felt cool and crumbly. But no blocks were there.

Next, he checked the flower bed. The purple petals tickled his nose. The yellow daisies smelled like sunshine. But still no blocks.

Freddy felt frustrated. Where could they be? He sat down on the grass. The green blades felt prickly against his fur.

"Think, Freddy, think," he said to himself. "Where did I play yesterday?"

Then Freddy remembered something important. Yesterday was very windy. The leaves had danced in the air. The wind had blown very hard.

"Maybe the wind moved my blocks!" Freddy said excitedly.

He jumped up and started looking again. This time, he searched where the wind might blow things. Behind the garden shed, he found one red block. Under the picnic table, he discovered a blue block.

"I'm getting closer!" Freddy said happily.

He followed the path the wind might have taken. Near the fence, he found the yellow block. It was hiding under some fallen leaves. The leaves crackled when he moved them.

But where were the rest? Freddy looked up at the big oak tree. Its branches swayed gently in the breeze.

Then he had another idea. What if some blocks got stuck up high? Freddy was good at climbing. His sharp claws helped him grip the bark.

Up, up, up he climbed. The bark felt rough under his paws. Higher and higher he went. The garden looked smaller from up there.

In a fork between two branches, Freddy saw something green. It was his green block! It had gotten caught when the wind blew it up.

Carefully, Freddy grabbed the block with his teeth. Then he climbed back down. The bark scratched his belly a little, but he didn't mind.

"I found four blocks," Freddy counted. "But I had five blocks yesterday."

He sat down to think again. Where could the last block be? Freddy closed his eyes and remembered playing yesterday. He had built a castle. Then the wind started blowing.

Suddenly, Freddy remembered! He had put one block in his pocket. He reached into his fuzzy pocket and felt something square and hard.

"My purple block!" he shouted with joy.

Now Freddy had all five blocks again. He arranged them in a neat pile. The colors looked bright in the sunshine. Red, blue, yellow, green, and purple.

Freddy built the tallest tower ever. It reached almost to his chin. He felt proud and happy.

"I found them all!" he called to his mom.

Mom Fox came outside and hugged him. "You didn't give up," she said proudly. "You kept trying different ways to solve your problem."

"The wind had blown them everywhere," Freddy explained. "But I thought carefully and found them all."

"That's what good problem-solvers do," Mom said. "They think and try again."

Freddy smiled big. His tower stood tall and colorful in the garden. Tomorrow he would remember to put his blocks away safely. But today, he would just enjoy building and playing.

The sun felt warm on his fur. The flowers smelled sweet. And best of all, Freddy had learned something important about never giving up.

The end.
