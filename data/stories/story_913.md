# Story story_913

**Jack's Big Game Adventure**

Jack sat on the basement steps. His big sister Emma and her friends were playing upstairs. He could hear them laughing and running around.

"I want to play too," Jack said softly.

The basement felt cold and quiet. Jack's toys were scattered on the soft carpet. His teddy bear sat in the corner looking lonely too.

Jack climbed the wooden steps. Each step creaked under his feet. He peeked around the corner into the living room.

Emma and her three friends were playing a jumping game. They hopped from pillow to pillow across the floor.

"Can I play?" Jack asked in his small voice.

"Sorry Jack," Emma said. "This game is for big kids only. You might get hurt."

Jack's shoulders drooped. His eyes felt watery. He walked back down to the basement with heavy feet.

"It's not fair," he whispered to his teddy bear.

Jack looked around the basement. The walls were painted bright blue. Colorful bins held his blocks and cars. A small table sat near the window.

Then Jack had an idea. His eyes grew wide and bright.

"I'll make my own game!" he said.

Jack pulled out all his soft blocks. He stacked them high like a tower. Then he tried to jump from block to block like Emma's game.

But the blocks were too wobbly. They tumbled down with a loud crash. Jack fell on his bottom with a thump.

"Ouch," he said, rubbing his knee.

The blocks scattered everywhere. Jack felt sad again. His first plan didn't work.

Jack sat quietly for a moment. He could still hear the laughter upstairs. The sound made his heart feel heavy.

But then Jack remembered what Daddy always said. "Try again, little buddy. Good ideas take time."

Jack looked around the basement again. This time he noticed the couch cushions. They were big and squishy and safe.

"Those are perfect!" Jack said with a smile.

Jack pulled the cushions off the old couch. He arranged them in a line across the basement floor. The cushions were soft and steady.

Jack stepped onto the first cushion. It felt bouncy under his feet. He took a careful step to the next one.

"One, two, three!" he counted as he hopped.

The cushions didn't move or fall down. Jack jumped from cushion to cushion across the whole basement.

"I did it!" he cheered, throwing his hands up high.

Just then, Emma appeared at the top of the steps.

"What are you doing down there?" she asked.

"Playing my jumping game!" Jack said proudly. "Want to see?"

Emma walked down the steps. Her friends followed behind her.

"Wow, Jack!" Emma said. "This looks really fun!"

"Can we try?" asked Emma's friend Sarah.

Jack beamed with happiness. "Yes! Everyone can play!"

Soon all the kids were taking turns on Jack's cushion game. They laughed and cheered for each other.

"This is even better than our game upstairs," said Emma's friend Tom.

"Jack, you're really smart," Emma said, giving him a big hug.

Jack felt warm and proud inside. His chest puffed out like a superhero.

"I tried two times," Jack told them. "The first time didn't work. But I didn't give up!"

"That's what makes you special," Emma said with a smile.

The kids played Jack's game for a long time. The basement filled with happy sounds and giggles.

When it was time for Emma's friends to go home, they all thanked Jack.

"Best game ever!" they said.

That night at dinner, Jack told Mommy and Daddy about his adventure.

"I felt left out," Jack said. "But I made my own fun instead!"

"We're so proud of you," Mommy said, kissing his forehead.

"You learned something very important today," Daddy added. "When things don't work out, you can always try again."

Jack smiled big. He learned that being creative and not giving up could turn a sad day into the best day ever.

From that day on, Jack's basement became the favorite place to play.

The end.
