# Story story_638

Emma's Magical Nap Time

The big clock on the wall said two o'clock. Mommy smiled and clapped her hands together. "Nap time, Emma!" she called from the kitchen.

Emma was playing with her colorful blocks on the soft carpet. She had built a tall tower that reached almost to her knee. Red block, blue block, yellow block, green block. It was perfect.

"But Mommy, I'm not sleepy," Emma said. She rubbed her eyes with her small fists. "My tower needs a door."

Mommy walked over and sat down beside Emma. She smelled like the cookies they had baked that morning. "Your tower is beautiful, sweetie. But your body needs rest to grow big and strong."

Emma's bottom lip stuck out. She didn't want to stop playing. The afternoon sun made golden squares on the floor through the window. Birds were singing happy songs outside.

"Come on, little one," Mommy said gently. She took Emma's hand. It felt warm and safe.

They walked to Emma's bedroom together. The room was cozy and quiet. Emma's favorite stuffed elephant, Peanut, sat on her pillow. His gray fur was soft from many hugs.

Mommy pulled back the covers. The sheets were cool and smooth. "Climb in, Emma."

Emma crawled into bed slowly. She pulled Peanut close to her chest. His trunk tickled her chin. "I still don't feel sleepy, Mommy."

"Close your eyes anyway," Mommy whispered. She sat on the edge of the bed. "Sometimes magic happens when we rest."

Emma squeezed her eyes shut tight. Mommy began to rub her back in gentle circles. Her hand was warm through Emma's soft pajamas.

"Breathe in slowly," Mommy said in a quiet voice. "Breathe out slowly."

Emma took a deep breath. The air smelled like lavender from the little purple flowers on her windowsill. She felt her shoulders relax.

Suddenly, something wonderful happened. Behind her closed eyes, Emma saw colors dancing. Swirls of pink and purple floated like cotton candy. Golden sparkles twinkled like tiny stars.

"Mommy, I see pretty colors!" Emma whispered.

"That's the magic beginning," Mommy said softly. "Keep watching."

The colors began to form shapes. Emma saw fluffy white clouds that looked like animals. A bunny hopped across a rainbow bridge. A friendly dragon breathed flowers instead of fire.

Emma's breathing became slower and deeper. Her body felt heavy and warm in the cozy bed. Peanut's fur was like a soft cloud against her cheek.

In her magical vision, Emma was flying on the back of a gentle butterfly. They soared over a land made of pillows and blankets. Everything was soft and peaceful.

The butterfly landed in a field of sleepy flowers. Each flower played a quiet lullaby. Emma could hear the sweet music in her dreams.

When Emma woke up, the room was filled with orange sunset light. She felt refreshed and happy. Her body was full of energy again.

Mommy peeked through the door. "How was your nap, sweetheart?"

"It was magical!" Emma said, sitting up and stretching her arms high. "I saw colors and clouds and a singing flower garden!"

"That sounds wonderful," Mommy said, giving Emma a warm hug. "Did you know that rest helps your brain create those beautiful dreams?"

Emma nodded, her eyes bright with excitement. "Can I tell Daddy about my flying butterfly?"

"Of course," Mommy said. "But first, let's have a snack. Nap time made you grow, so you might be hungry."

As they walked to the kitchen together, Emma thought about her magical adventure. She looked forward to tomorrow's nap time. Maybe she would see the singing flowers again. Or perhaps she would meet a dancing rainbow.

"Mommy," Emma said, tugging on her mother's shirt. "Tomorrow can we have nap time again?"

Mommy smiled and kissed the top of Emma's head. "Every day, my little dreamer. Every single day."

Emma grinned and hugged Peanut tight. Nap time wasn't so bad after all. In fact, it was the most magical part of her day.

The end.
