# Story story_1467

Ben sat on his bedroom floor. He pushed his toy cars around sadly. The house felt very quiet today.

"Vroom, vroom," Ben whispered softly. But playing alone wasn't much fun. He wished he had a friend to play with.

Suddenly, Ben heard a small sound. It came from his closet. The door was open just a crack.

"Hello?" Ben called out quietly.

A fluffy orange paw poked out slowly. Then a black striped tail wiggled. Ben's eyes grew very wide.

"Don't be scared," said a gentle voice. "I'm Tiger. I live in your closet."

Tiger stepped out carefully. He was small and soft looking. His orange fur had beautiful black stripes. His green eyes sparkled kindly.

"You're a real tiger!" Ben gasped.

"Yes, but I'm friendly," Tiger said with a smile. "I've been watching you play. You look lonely."

Ben nodded his head slowly. "I am lonely. My friends are all busy today."

Tiger's whiskers twitched happily. "I'm not busy! Can we be friends?"

Ben's face lit up like sunshine. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" Tiger purred. "What should we play first?"

Ben looked at his toy cars. "Do you like cars?"

Tiger's tail swished excitedly. "I love cars! But I've never played with them before."

Ben picked up a red race car. He showed Tiger how to push it. The car rolled across the smooth floor.

"Wow!" Tiger said. His paw batted at the moving car. "It's so fast!"

Soon they were racing cars together. Tiger was very gentle with his big paws. The cars zoomed around the room. Ben and Tiger made loud engine sounds.

"Vroom! Beep beep!" they called out together.

After cars, they played with building blocks. Tiger helped stack them very high. His steady paws were perfect for building.

"Look how tall our tower is!" Ben cheered.

The tower reached almost to Ben's waist. It had red, blue, and yellow blocks. Tiger carefully added one more green block on top.

"We make a great team," Tiger purred proudly.

Next, they played hide and seek. Tiger was very good at hiding. His stripes helped him blend behind the curtains. Ben giggled when he found Tiger's tail sticking out.

"I see you!" Ben laughed.

Tiger pretended to be surprised. "Oh no! You found me!"

When it was Ben's turn to hide, Tiger always found him quickly. But Tiger pretended it was hard. He looked in silly places first.

"Are you under this tiny book?" Tiger asked, winking.

Ben giggled from behind the chair. "No! I'm too big!"

As the afternoon went by, they played many games. They had tea parties with Ben's stuffed animals. Tiger sipped pretend tea very politely.

They drew pictures with crayons. Tiger held the crayon carefully in his mouth. He drew orange and black stripes everywhere.

"This is my family," Tiger explained proudly.

Ben drew a picture of himself and Tiger playing. "This is us being best friends," Ben said.

When dinner time came, Tiger's ears drooped sadly. "I should go back to my closet now."

Ben felt sad too. "Will you come play again tomorrow?"

Tiger's eyes sparkled with joy. "Every day! Best friends always play together."

Ben hugged Tiger gently. Tiger's fur was so soft and warm. He smelled like cinnamon and sunshine.

"Thank you for being my friend," Ben whispered.

"Thank you for letting me play," Tiger purred back.

Tiger padded quietly back to the closet. He turned and waved with his paw. "See you tomorrow, Ben!"

Ben waved back happily. "See you tomorrow, Tiger!"

From that day on, Ben was never lonely. Every morning, Tiger came out to play. They built amazing things together. They went on pretend adventures around the room.

Ben learned that friends can come from the most surprising places. Even from a closet! And Tiger learned that sharing games makes them twice as fun.

The two best friends played together every single day. Ben's room was always filled with laughter and joy. The house never felt quiet anymore.

The end.
