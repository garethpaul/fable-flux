# Story story_883

**Gigi the Giraffe and the Sandy Problem**

Gigi the giraffe loved the beach. She loved the warm sand between her hooves. She loved the salty sea breeze. She loved watching the waves dance and splash.

Every morning, Gigi walked along the shore. The soft sand felt so good on her feet. She would stretch her long neck high to see the boats far away. The seagulls called "hello" from above.

But there was one big problem. Gigi did not like baths. Not even a little bit.

"Time to wash off that sand!" called Mama Giraffe every evening. "You're covered from head to toe!"

Gigi would shake her spotted head. "No thank you, Mama. I like being sandy."

The sand stuck to her fur. It made her feel itchy and scratchy. But still, Gigi said no to baths.

One sunny Tuesday, something terrible happened. Gigi rolled in the warm sand as usual. She wiggled and giggled with joy. But when she tried to stand up, she felt very heavy.

"Oh my!" gasped Gigi. Sand covered every inch of her body. Her beautiful yellow spots were hidden. Sand filled her ears and made everything sound funny. Sand stuck between her eyelashes.

Gigi tried to shake it off. She wiggled her head left and right. She stomped her hooves. She swished her tail back and forth. But the sand would not come off.

"Help!" called Gigi. Her voice sounded muffled and strange.

Mama Giraffe came running. "Oh Gigi! You look like a sand castle with legs!"

"I can't see very well," said Gigi sadly. "And I feel so heavy. The sand is everywhere!"

Mama Giraffe thought carefully. "Let's try brushing it off first."

They found a big, soft brush. Mama Giraffe brushed Gigi's back very gently. Some sand fell off, but most stayed stuck. The brush felt scratchy on Gigi's sensitive skin.

"Ouch," said Gigi. "That doesn't feel good, Mama."

"You're right, sweetie. This isn't working well."

Gigi felt worried. How would she ever get clean? She wanted to see her pretty spots again. She wanted the sand out of her ears.

Then Mama Giraffe had another idea. "What if we make bath time fun? We could play in the shallow water by the shore."

Gigi's ears perked up. Well, the ears that weren't full of sand. "Play in the water?"

"Yes! We could splash gently. The warm ocean water will wash the sand away. And you can still be at your favorite beach."

Gigi thought about this. She did love the beach. And the water did look sparkly and inviting.

"Okay, Mama. Let's try."

They walked to where the waves met the sand. The water felt warm and tingly on Gigi's hooves. Little waves lapped at her legs.

"This feels nice," said Gigi with surprise.

Mama Giraffe splashed very gently. The water swirled around Gigi's legs. Slowly, the sand began to wash away.

"Look!" shouted Gigi happily. "I can see my spots again!"

They played in the shallow water for a long time. Gigi discovered that the waves gave her a gentle massage. The salt water made her skin feel smooth and clean.

Soon, all the sand was gone. Gigi's beautiful yellow fur sparkled in the sunshine. Her spots looked bright and cheerful.

"I feel so much better!" said Gigi. "And that was actually fun!"

From that day on, Gigi looked forward to her beach baths. She learned that trying new things could be wonderful. Sometimes the thing you think you won't like turns out to be the best part of your day.

Every evening, Gigi and Mama Giraffe played in the gentle waves. The beach became even more special because now Gigi could enjoy being both sandy and clean.

"Thank you for helping me try something new, Mama," said Gigi.

"Thank you for being brave and giving it a chance," said Mama Giraffe.

And they splashed happily in the warm ocean water as the sun painted the sky orange and pink.

The end.
