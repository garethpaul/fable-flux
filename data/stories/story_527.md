# Lily's Magical Story Time

It was three o'clock in the afternoon. Time for story time! 

Mama walked into the living room. She carried a big basket of books. "Story time, Lily!" she called softly.

Baby Lily sat on her soft blue blanket. She was playing with her colorful blocks. Red block. Yellow block. Green block. Stack, stack, stack!

"Come here, little one," Mama said gently. She patted the cozy reading chair.

But Lily shook her head. "No story," she said quietly. She hugged her favorite teddy bear tight. The books looked big and scary today.

Mama sat down in the reading chair anyway. She opened the basket slowly. "Let's see what adventures are waiting," she whispered.

Lily peeked over her blocks. She was curious but still unsure.

Mama pulled out a book with a bright purple cover. On the front was a little bunny. The bunny had floppy ears and a twitchy nose.

"This bunny looks lonely," Mama said softly. "I wonder if he needs a friend."

Lily's eyes grew wide. She loved bunnies! Slowly, she crawled closer to Mama's chair.

Mama opened the book carefully. The pages made a gentle whooshing sound. "Once upon a time," she began.

Suddenly, something amazing happened! As Mama read, the pictures seemed to move. The bunny's ears wiggled! His nose twitched! He hopped right off the page!

"Mama!" Lily giggled. "Bunny is dancing!"

The little bunny bounced around the room. He landed softly on Lily's lap. She could feel his warm, fuzzy fur. He smelled like sunshine and flowers.

"Hello, Lily," the bunny whispered. "Will you be my friend?"

Lily clapped her hands with joy. "Yes, yes, yes!"

Mama continued reading. More characters came alive! A wise old owl hooted from the bookshelf. A friendly mouse squeaked from behind the couch. A colorful butterfly fluttered near the window.

"We love story time!" they all sang together.

Lily laughed and laughed. She had never seen anything so wonderful. The living room became a magical forest. Sunbeams danced through the trees. Birds chirped happy songs.

The bunny showed Lily how to hop. Hop, hop, hop around the room! The owl taught her how to hoot. "Hoo, hoo, hoo!" The mouse showed her how to wiggle her nose.

As Mama read the last page, something special happened. All the characters gathered around Lily. They gave her the biggest, warmest hug.

"Thank you for being our friend," the bunny said. "We'll see you tomorrow!"

One by one, the magical friends waved goodbye. They jumped back into their book. The pages glowed softly, then became still.

Lily looked up at Mama with sparkling eyes. "More stories, please?"

Mama smiled and hugged her close. "Tomorrow, little one. Story time happens every day."

Lily snuggled into Mama's arms. She felt warm and happy inside. The living room looked normal again. But she knew the magic was still there, waiting in the books.

"Will bunny come back?" Lily asked sleepily.

"Every single day," Mama promised. "And he'll bring new friends too."

Lily yawned and rubbed her eyes. She looked at the basket of books. They didn't look scary anymore. They looked like treasure chests full of adventures.

"I love story time, Mama," she whispered.

Mama kissed the top of her head. "And story time loves you too, sweet girl."

That night, Lily dreamed of magical bunnies and wise owls. She dreamed of dancing butterflies and squeaky mice. In her dreams, she was the hero of every story.

When she woke up the next morning, Lily ran to the living room. She sat by the reading chair and waited. The basket of books seemed to shimmer in the sunlight.

"Is it story time yet?" she asked hopefully.

From that day on, Lily could hardly wait for three o'clock. Story time became her favorite part of every day. Because she knew that inside every book, magical friends were waiting to play.

And they always would be.

The end.
