# Story story_871

**Tiny Turtle's Big View**

Tiny Turtle lived in a beautiful garden. Green grass tickled his feet. Colorful flowers smelled sweet. He loved his garden home very much.

Every morning, Tiny Turtle heard wonderful sounds. Children laughed and played beyond the tall fence. Dogs barked happily. Balls bounced on the ground. The sounds made him very curious.

"I want to see what's happening," said Tiny Turtle. He walked to the wooden fence. It was brown and very tall. Tiny Turtle stretched his neck up high. He stood on his tippy toes. But he was too short to see over the fence.

"Oh no," sighed Tiny Turtle. "I'm too little to see anything."

The sounds continued every day. Children giggled. Dogs woofed. Tiny Turtle felt sad. He wanted to see the fun too.

One sunny morning, Tiny Turtle had an idea. "I'll climb up something tall!" he said excitedly. He looked around the garden. Near the fence stood a big rock. It was gray and bumpy.

Tiny Turtle crawled onto the rock. His little legs worked hard. The rock felt rough under his belly. He climbed to the very top. Then he stretched his neck as high as possible.

"Almost there!" he puffed. But the fence was still too tall. Tiny Turtle could only see the very top boards. He couldn't see what was beyond them.

"This didn't work," said Tiny Turtle sadly. He climbed down from the rock. His shell felt heavy with disappointment.

Tiny Turtle sat quietly in the soft grass. He listened to the happy sounds again. A ball bounced. Children cheered. His heart felt lonely.

Then Rabbit hopped by. She had fluffy white fur and pink ears. "Why do you look sad?" asked Rabbit kindly.

"I want to see over the fence," explained Tiny Turtle. "But I'm too short. Even with the rock, I can't see."

Rabbit's nose twitched thoughtfully. "Maybe you need help from a friend," she suggested. "I have an idea!"

Rabbit hopped to the fence. She sat down next to the big rock. "Climb on my back," said Rabbit. "Then climb on the rock. You'll be much taller!"

Tiny Turtle's eyes sparkled with hope. "Really? You'd help me?"

"Of course!" smiled Rabbit. "Friends help each other."

Carefully, Tiny Turtle climbed onto Rabbit's soft back. Her fur felt warm and cozy. Then he climbed from Rabbit onto the rock. Now he was much higher than before!

Tiny Turtle stretched his neck up tall. This time, his head popped over the fence! "I can see!" he shouted with joy.

Beyond the fence was a wonderful playground. Children ran and laughed on green grass. They kicked a red ball back and forth. A golden dog chased butterflies. Swings moved back and forth in the breeze.

"It's beautiful!" gasped Tiny Turtle. The children's faces were bright and happy. The dog's tail wagged fast. Everything looked so fun and cheerful.

"Thank you, Rabbit!" called Tiny Turtle. "This is amazing!"

From that day on, Rabbit visited every morning. She helped Tiny Turtle see over the fence. They became the very best of friends.

Sometimes other garden friends joined them. Squirrel brought acorns to share. Bird sang pretty songs. They all watched the playground together.

Tiny Turtle learned something important. When something seems impossible, don't give up. Try different ways to solve the problem. Ask friends for help when you need it.

"I'm still a small turtle," said Tiny Turtle happily. "But with good friends and creative thinking, I can see big things!"

Every morning brought new adventures to watch. Tiny Turtle's heart felt full of joy. His garden was perfect, and now he could see the wonderful world beyond it too.

The fence didn't seem so tall anymore. With friendship and persistence, any problem could be solved. Tiny Turtle smiled as he watched the children play, grateful for his helpful friends and his beautiful view.

The end.
