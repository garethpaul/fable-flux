# Story story_892

**Lily's First Day**

Lily was a little baby with big brown eyes. She had soft curly hair and tiny pink fingers. Today was her first day at the nursery.

Mama carried Lily into the bright, colorful room. There were toys everywhere. Soft blocks sat on the carpet. Picture books filled the shelves. Other babies played with rattles and stuffed animals.

"I'll be back soon, sweet Lily," Mama said softly. She gave Lily a warm hug and a gentle kiss. Then Mama walked toward the door.

Lily watched Mama leave. Her bottom lip started to wobble. Big tears rolled down her cheeks. She wanted Mama to stay! The nursery felt too big without her.

Miss Sarah, the kind teacher, picked up Lily. "Oh, little one," she said in a gentle voice. "I know you miss Mama."

Lily cried louder. She reached her little arms toward the door. Where did Mama go? When would she come back?

Miss Sarah tried the first solution. She showed Lily a bright red ball. It had yellow stars all over it. "Look at this pretty ball, Lily," she said. She rolled it on the soft carpet.

But Lily didn't want the ball. She pushed it away with her tiny hand. The tears kept coming. Her nose felt stuffy from crying.

Miss Sarah tried again. She brought out a music box. It played a sweet, tinkling song. A little dancer spun around and around inside.

Lily stopped crying for just a moment. She watched the tiny dancer. But then she remembered Mama was gone. The tears started again, even bigger than before.

Miss Sarah thought hard. What would help little Lily feel better? Then she had a wonderful idea.

Miss Sarah went to Lily's special cubby. Inside was Lily's soft blue blanket. It smelled just like home. It smelled like Mama's perfume and warm hugs.

"Look what I found," Miss Sarah said sweetly. She wrapped the cozy blanket around Lily's shoulders.

Lily felt the familiar softness. She grabbed the blanket with both hands. She buried her face in the gentle fabric. It felt like Mama's warm arms around her.

The crying started to slow down. Lily's breathing became calmer. The blanket made everything feel safer.

Miss Sarah sat in the big rocking chair. She held Lily close with the special blanket. They rocked back and forth, back and forth. The motion felt soothing and peaceful.

"Mama loves you very much," Miss Sarah whispered. "She'll come back after snack time. The blanket will keep you cozy until then."

Lily snuggled deeper into her blanket. She felt Mama's love wrapped around her. Even though Mama wasn't here, part of her was still close by.

Soon, Lily noticed other things in the nursery. A mobile with colorful butterflies hung above her head. Soft music played from somewhere nearby. Another baby was giggling at peek-a-boo.

Miss Sarah showed Lily a book with big, bright pictures. There were fluffy puppies and smiling babies. Lily reached out to touch the smooth pages.

When snack time came, Lily was playing with soft blocks. She still held her special blanket, but she wasn't crying anymore. She felt brave and curious about her new place.

Then the door opened. "Lily!" called a familiar voice. It was Mama!

Lily's face lit up with the biggest smile. She crawled quickly across the carpet, dragging her blanket behind her. Mama scooped her up in a wonderful, warm hug.

"Did you have a good morning, my brave girl?" Mama asked.

Lily babbled happily. She showed Mama her special blanket and pointed at Miss Sarah.

"Thank you for taking such good care of her," Mama told Miss Sarah.

As they got ready to leave, Lily waved bye-bye to Miss Sarah. Tomorrow, she would come back to the nursery. She would bring her special blanket again. She knew that even when Mama had to go, love would always stay close by.

Sometimes feeling sad is okay. But with something special to comfort us, we can be brave and try new things.

The end.
