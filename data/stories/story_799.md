# Story story_799

**Lily's Big Toy Problem**

Baby Lily loved playing in the garden. The warm sun kissed her chubby cheeks. Soft grass tickled her tiny toes. Pretty flowers danced in the gentle breeze.

Today, Lily brought all her favorite toys outside. She had her red ball. She had her yellow truck. She had her purple teddy bear. She had her green blocks too.

"So many toys!" Lily giggled. She clapped her little hands together.

Lily rolled the red ball across the grass. It bounced and tumbled. She pushed the yellow truck through the dirt. "Beep beep!" she said happily.

Purple teddy got a big hug. The green blocks made a wobbly tower. Lily was having the best day ever.

Then Mommy called from the house. "Lily! Time for lunch, sweetie!"

Lily looked at all her toys. They were scattered everywhere in the garden. The ball was by the roses. The truck was near the fence. Teddy sat under the apple tree. The blocks were spread around the flower bed.

"Oh no," Lily said. Her bottom lip trembled. "Too many toys!"

She tried to pick them all up. First, she grabbed the red ball. Then she reached for the yellow truck. But the ball slipped from her tiny hands. It rolled away again.

"Mommy!" Lily called. Her voice was shaky.

Mommy peeked out the door. "What's wrong, little one?"

"Can't carry all my toys," Lily said. Tears filled her big brown eyes.

Mommy smiled kindly. "Try again, honey. You can do it!"

Lily wiped her eyes. She took a deep breath. The sweet smell of flowers filled her nose. A butterfly landed on her shoulder.

"Okay," Lily said bravely. "I try again."

This time, Lily had a different idea. She picked up just the red ball. She carried it carefully to the garden gate. She set it down gently.

"One toy safe!" she said proudly.

Next, she got the yellow truck. She pushed it slowly to the gate. The wheels made happy clicking sounds on the stone path.

"Two toys safe!" Lily cheered.

Then she carried purple teddy. She gave him an extra squeeze. His fur was so soft and warm. She placed him next to the ball and truck.

"Three toys safe!" she said with a big smile.

Finally, she gathered the green blocks. She could only carry two at a time. Her little hands were so small. But she didn't give up.

Trip after trip, she moved the blocks. One by one, they joined the other toys. The pile grew bigger and bigger.

"All toys safe!" Lily shouted happily. She did a little dance.

Mommy came outside. She saw all the toys by the gate. She saw Lily's proud, beaming face.

"Wow, Lily! You did it!" Mommy said. "You solved the problem all by yourself!"

"I carry one toy at a time," Lily explained. "Not all together."

"That's very smart thinking," Mommy said. She scooped Lily up in a warm hug. "Sometimes the best way is the slow way."

Lily giggled. She felt so proud and happy. The sun felt warmer now. The flowers seemed brighter. Even the grass felt softer under her feet.

"I did it!" Lily said again. "All by myself!"

Mommy helped carry the toys inside. They put them in Lily's toy box. Each toy had its special place.

During lunch, Lily told Daddy about her adventure. "I couldn't carry all my toys," she said. "But I tried a new way. One toy at a time!"

"That's my clever girl," Daddy said. He ruffled her soft hair.

That night, Lily felt extra proud. She learned something important. Big problems have simple answers. Sometimes you just need to try a different way.

When things seem too hard, take them one step at a time. Just like carrying toys from the garden. Slow and steady works best.

Lily fell asleep with a big smile. Tomorrow she would play in the garden again. And she knew exactly how to bring her toys inside.

One by one, nice and slow.

The end.
