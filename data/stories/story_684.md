# Story story_684

**Grandma's Garden Friends**

Grandma Rose sat on her favorite wooden chair. She was in the sunny nursery room. Green plants grew everywhere around her. The room smelled like fresh dirt and flowers.

But Grandma Rose felt lonely today. She watered her tiny tomato plants. She touched the soft leaves gently. Still, something was missing.

"I wish I had someone to share my garden with," she whispered.

Little Emma peeked through the doorway. Emma was only four years old. She had curly brown hair and bright eyes. Emma loved her grandma very much.

"Grandma, what are you doing?" Emma asked softly.

Grandma Rose looked up. Her face lit up with a warm smile. "I'm taking care of my baby plants, sweetie."

Emma stepped closer. The floor felt cool under her bare feet. She could smell the earthy scents. Everything looked so green and alive.

"Can I help you?" Emma asked. Her voice was hopeful.

"Of course, my dear," Grandma said. "Come sit with me."

Emma climbed onto a small stool. It was just her size. Grandma handed her a tiny watering can. The metal felt smooth and warm.

"Pour just a little water," Grandma showed her. "The plants get thirsty like we do."

Emma poured carefully. Water dripped onto the dark soil. The plants seemed to dance happily. Emma giggled at the tickling drops.

"Look, Grandma! The plants are drinking!"

"Yes, they are," Grandma laughed. "You're a wonderful helper."

Next, they planted new seeds together. Grandma gave Emma three small seeds. They felt bumpy in her tiny palm.

"What will grow from these?" Emma wondered.

"Sunflowers," Grandma said. "Big, yellow, happy flowers."

Emma pushed each seed into the soft dirt. Her fingers got muddy. But she didn't mind at all. This was fun!

"Now we pat the soil gently," Grandma showed her.

Emma patted with her small hands. The dirt felt squishy and cool. She imagined the seeds sleeping underground.

"Will they grow tall like me?" Emma asked.

"Even taller," Grandma smiled. "Much taller than both of us."

They worked together for a long time. Emma learned to pinch dead leaves. Grandma taught her to feel the soil. If it felt dry, the plants needed water.

"You have gentle hands," Grandma said proudly. "Plants love gentle touches."

Emma beamed with happiness. She felt important helping Grandma. The nursery felt magical with both of them there.

Soon they heard Emma's mom calling. "Emma, time for lunch!"

Emma's face fell. She didn't want to leave. "Can I come back tomorrow, Grandma?"

"Every day if you want," Grandma said. "We're garden partners now."

Emma hugged Grandma tight. She smelled like flowers and sunshine. "I love being your partner."

"I love it too, sweet pea."

The next morning, Emma ran to the nursery. Grandma was already there, waiting with two small shovels.

"Good morning, partner," Grandma said cheerfully.

"Good morning, Grandma!" Emma replied.

They watered plants together. They sang silly songs to help them grow. Emma made up a song about dancing carrots. Grandma laughed until her eyes sparkled.

Every day became special. Emma learned about different plants. She discovered which ones liked shade. Others loved bright sunshine.

"Plants are like people," Grandma explained. "They all need different things to be happy."

Emma nodded wisely. She understood perfectly.

One morning, something amazing happened. Tiny green shoots poked through the soil. Their sunflower seeds were growing!

"Look, Grandma! Our babies are waking up!"

Grandma clapped her hands with joy. "We did this together, Emma."

Emma danced around the nursery. Her bare feet padded on the cool floor. Everything smelled fresh and alive.

"We're the best garden team ever!" Emma announced.

Grandma agreed completely. She wasn't lonely anymore. Emma had filled her heart with sunshine. Together, they had created something beautiful.

The nursery became their special place. Every plant they grew together was a symbol of their love. Grandma and Emma had found the perfect friendship, growing stronger every single day.

The end.
