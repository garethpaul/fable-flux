# Story story_1013

**Kira's Flying Lunch**

The lunch bell rang at Sunny Hill School. All the children ran inside. Their teacher, Miss Rose, smiled at them. "Time for lunch, everyone!"

Kira sat at her desk. She looked at her lunch box. It had bright yellow stars on it. But Kira felt sad. She didn't want to eat lunch. She wanted to keep playing outside with her red kite.

"Come on, Kira," said her friend Ben. "Aren't you hungry?"

Kira shook her head. "I want to fly my kite more. Lunch is boring."

Miss Rose heard Kira. She walked over with a gentle smile. "What's wrong, sweetie?"

"I don't want lunch," Kira said quietly. "I want to play with my kite. It flies so high and pretty."

Miss Rose looked out the window. The wind made the trees dance. "I have an idea," she said. "What if we eat lunch outside today?"

Kira's eyes got bigger. "Really? We can do that?"

"Of course! Let's have a picnic lunch."

All the children cheered. They grabbed their lunch boxes. They followed Miss Rose outside to the grassy field. The warm sun felt nice on their faces.

Kira opened her lunch box. Inside was a peanut butter sandwich. She also had apple slices and crackers. But something amazing happened next.

A gentle breeze picked up her napkin. It floated up like a tiny white kite! Kira giggled and reached for it.

"Look!" shouted Ben. "My wrapper is flying too!"

Soon all the children were laughing. Their napkins and wrappers danced in the wind. They looked like little paper kites!

"This is magical!" said Kira. She took a big bite of her sandwich. It tasted so much better outside. The apple slices were crisp and sweet. The crackers crunched loudly.

Miss Rose pulled out a real kite from her bag. It was blue with silver ribbons. "Who wants to fly this while we eat?"

"Me! Me!" all the children called out.

They took turns holding the kite string. They ate their lunches and watched the kite dance. It swooped and soared high above them. The ribbons sparkled in the sunshine.

Kira felt the soft grass under her legs. She heard birds singing in the trees. The wind smelled like flowers. Her sandwich tasted wonderful. Everything felt perfect.

"This is the best lunch ever!" Kira said happily.

Ben nodded. "The wind makes everything taste better!"

A butterfly landed on Kira's apple slice. Its wings were orange and black. Kira sat very still. The butterfly stayed for a whole minute. Then it flew away like a tiny living kite.

"Did you see that?" Kira whispered. "The butterfly visited my lunch!"

Miss Rose smiled. "Nature loves picnics too."

After lunch, the children flew Kira's red kite together. They shared the string. Everyone got a turn. The kite soared higher than ever before.

"Look how happy it looks up there," said Kira. "Just like how happy I feel!"

The wind carried their laughter across the field. Other classes looked out their windows. They wished they were having a picnic too.

When it was time to go inside, Kira felt full and happy. Her tummy was warm from good food. Her heart was warm from fun with friends.

"Thank you, Miss Rose," Kira said. "Lunch was magical today."

"You're welcome, dear. Sometimes we just need to try things in a new way."

That night, Kira told her mom about the flying lunch. "We ate outside! My napkin flew like a kite! A butterfly came to visit!"

Her mom hugged her tight. "What a wonderful day!"

As Kira got ready for bed, she looked out her window. The stars twinkled like tiny kites in the dark sky.

"Tomorrow I can't wait for lunch," she whispered. "Maybe we'll have another picnic. Maybe more butterflies will come. Maybe the wind will make everything magical again."

Kira fell asleep dreaming of flying sandwiches and dancing napkins. She couldn't wait to see what tomorrow's lunch would bring.

The end.
