# Story story_1207

**The Little Fox and the Big Fence**

Rusty was a little red fox. He lived on Farmer May's farm. The farm had green grass and tall trees. It had chickens and cows too.

But there was one big problem. A tall wooden fence went around the farm. Rusty was too short to see over it. All the other animals could peek over the fence. The horses could see. The cows could see. Even the goats could see.

"What's on the other side?" Rusty asked Bella the cow.

"Beautiful flowers," said Bella. "Red roses and yellow sunflowers. They smell so sweet."

Rusty's nose twitched. He loved flowers. He wanted to see them too. But when he stood on his back legs, he still couldn't reach. The fence was just too tall.

"I wish I could see," Rusty sighed.

That morning, Rusty had an idea. He saw a big pile of hay near the fence. The hay was golden and smelled like sunshine.

"I'll climb the hay!" Rusty said.

He ran to the hay pile. Up, up, up he climbed. His little paws grabbed the scratchy hay. Higher and higher he went.

But the hay was loose and wobbly. Suddenly, Rusty tumbled down. Hay flew everywhere. It stuck to his red fur. It tickled his nose.

"Achoo!" Rusty sneezed.

Farmer May's dog, Buster, came running over.

"What happened here?" Buster barked, wagging his tail.

"I wanted to see over the fence," Rusty said sadly. He brushed hay from his whiskers.

"The hay pile won't work," said Buster kindly. "But don't give up. Think of another way."

Rusty sat down and thought hard. His tail swished back and forth. Then he saw something near the apple tree. Old wooden boxes were stacked by the barn.

"Those boxes!" Rusty said. His eyes sparkled with hope.

Rusty ran to the boxes. They were heavy but sturdy. He pushed one box with his nose. It was hard work. The box scraped against the ground.

"Almost there," Rusty puffed.

He pushed the box right next to the fence. Then he went back for another box. Push, push, push. His paws got tired, but he didn't stop.

Rusty stacked the second box on top of the first. It wobbled a little, but it stayed put. The boxes made perfect steps.

"Here I go," Rusty whispered.

Up he climbed onto the first box. Then up to the second box. Now he was tall enough!

Rusty peeked over the fence. His mouth opened wide with wonder.

The most beautiful garden spread before him. Red roses danced in the breeze. Yellow sunflowers turned their faces to the sun. Purple lavender made the air smell like heaven.

Butterflies danced from flower to flower. Their wings were orange and black. Bees hummed happy songs as they worked.

"It's amazing!" Rusty called to his friends.

All the farm animals came to see. They watched Rusty enjoy his view.

"You did it!" mooed Bella. "You found a way!"

"I'm so proud of you," barked Buster.

Rusty's chest puffed up with pride. His tail wagged with joy.

From that day on, Rusty climbed his special boxes every morning. He watched the sunrise paint the flowers gold. He saw the butterflies wake up and stretch their wings.

Sometimes the farm animals joined him. They took turns looking over the fence. Everyone shared the beautiful view.

"Thank you for building the steps," said Clara the chicken.

"You're welcome," said Rusty. "I learned something important."

"What's that?" asked Bella.

"When something seems too hard, don't give up," Rusty said. "Try again in a new way. There's always a solution if you keep trying."

The sun set behind the flowers. The sky turned pink and orange. Rusty climbed down from his boxes, tired but happy.

He had solved his problem all by himself. And now he could see the beautiful world beyond the fence whenever he wanted.

That night, Rusty dreamed of flowers and butterflies. He dreamed of tomorrow's sunrise. And he dreamed of his wonderful boxes that made big dreams come true.

The end.
