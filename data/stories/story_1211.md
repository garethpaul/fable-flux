# Story story_1211

**Danny's Magic Morning Beat**

Danny loved his shiny red drum. He played it every day after lunch. But this morning was different.

"Time for breakfast, Danny!" called Mom from the kitchen.

Danny sat on his bed. He looked at his drum. His tummy felt grumbly, but he didn't want to eat. Breakfast seemed boring today.

"I don't want breakfast," Danny said softly. He tapped his drum once. *Tap.*

Mom came to his room. She had kind eyes and a warm smile. "What's wrong, little drummer?"

"Breakfast is not fun," Danny said. He made a sad face. "I want to play my drum instead."

Mom sat next to Danny. She thought for a moment. Then her eyes sparkled with an idea.

"What if we bring your drum to breakfast?" Mom asked.

Danny's eyes grew wide. "Really? To the kitchen?"

"Yes! Maybe we can make breakfast musical," Mom said.

Danny picked up his drum. He followed Mom to the bright kitchen. Sunlight danced through the windows. The kitchen smelled like warm toast and sweet jam.

Mom put Danny's breakfast on the table. There were fluffy pancakes, yellow butter, and red strawberries. Everything looked colorful and yummy.

"Now," said Mom, "let's make breakfast music!"

Danny held his drumsticks. He wasn't sure what to do.

Mom cracked an egg into a bowl. *Crack!* "Can you make that sound on your drum?"

Danny tapped his drum. *Tap!* It sounded just like the egg cracking!

"Perfect!" Mom clapped her hands.

Mom stirred the pancake batter. *Swish, swish, swish.* Danny made the same sound with soft drum rolls. His drumsticks moved in circles.

The butter sizzled in the pan. *Sizzle, pop!* Danny made quick little taps. The kitchen filled with cooking sounds and drum beats.

"This is fun!" Danny giggled. His tummy stopped feeling grumbly.

Mom flipped a pancake high in the air. Danny played a big *BOOM* when it landed. They both laughed together.

Soon the pancakes were ready. They were golden brown and smelled amazing. Mom put three on Danny's plate.

"Can I still play while I eat?" Danny asked.

"Of course," Mom said. "But use one hand for drumming and one for eating."

Danny took a bite of pancake. It was warm and fluffy. Sweet syrup dripped down his chin. He giggled and played a happy beat with one hand.

*Tap-tap-tap* went his drum. *Munch-munch-munch* went his mouth.

"The strawberries are like little red drums," Danny said. He popped one in his mouth. Then he played a tiny *tap* on his drum.

Mom poured orange juice into Danny's cup. The juice made a *glub-glub* sound. Danny copied it with his drumsticks.

"You're making breakfast into a song!" Mom said proudly.

Danny felt warm and happy inside. His tummy was getting full. His drum made everything more fun.

"Can we do this every morning?" Danny asked. His eyes sparkled with excitement.

"We can try," Mom said. "What other breakfast sounds can we make music with?"

Danny thought hard. "The toaster! And the coffee maker! And when you pour cereal!"

"Those are wonderful ideas," Mom said.

Danny finished his last bite of pancake. He played a gentle drum roll to celebrate. His tummy felt happy and full.

"Thank you, Mom," Danny said. He gave her a syrup-sticky hug. "Breakfast is fun now!"

Mom hugged him back. "Music makes everything better, doesn't it?"

Danny nodded. He looked at his drum with new excitement. Tomorrow morning would bring new breakfast sounds to copy. Maybe he could make music with his spoon in his cereal bowl!

"I can't wait for breakfast tomorrow!" Danny said.

He carefully carried his drum back to his room. But he was already thinking about morning. What sounds would breakfast make tomorrow? What new beats could he create?

Danny smiled big. Breakfast wasn't boring anymore. It was musical!

From now on, every morning would start with delicious food and happy drum beats.

The end.
