# Story story_809

**Freddy Frog's Special Snack Time**

The big clock on the wall went tick-tock. Miss Lily clapped her hands. "Snack time, everyone!" she called out cheerfully.

All the little animals in the forest classroom jumped up. Bunny hopped to her cubby. Squirrel scampered to get his acorns. Bear lumbered over to find his honey crackers.

But Freddy Frog stayed in his chair. His green skin felt cold and bumpy. His big eyes looked down at his webbed feet.

"Come on, Freddy," said Miss Lily gently. "Time for snacks."

Freddy shook his head slowly. "I don't want to," he croaked quietly. His tummy made a little rumble, but he didn't move.

Freddy always felt different at snack time. The other animals had such yummy treats. Bunny had crunchy carrots. Squirrel had nutty acorns. Bear had sweet honey crackers.

But Freddy's snack was always the same. Flies. Just plain old flies in a jar.

"Flies are gross," Freddy whispered to himself. "I wish I had something special too."

Miss Lily walked over to Freddy's desk. She knelt down beside him. "What's wrong, little one?" she asked softly.

"My snack isn't fun like everyone else's," Freddy said sadly. His voice was barely a whisper.

Miss Lily smiled warmly. "Would you like to try something magical today?"

Freddy's big eyes grew even bigger. "Magical?" he croaked with curiosity.

Miss Lily reached into her special teacher bag. She pulled out a tiny, sparkly jar. The jar shimmered like rainbow bubbles in the sunlight.

"These are rainbow flies," Miss Lily explained. "They taste like different fruits when you catch them with your tongue."

Freddy couldn't believe his eyes. Inside the jar, colorful flies buzzed around happily. Some were red like strawberries. Others were orange like peaches. Yellow ones looked like lemons. Purple ones seemed to taste like grapes.

"Really?" Freddy asked, his voice getting excited.

"Really," Miss Lily nodded. "But you have to catch them the frog way."

She opened the magical jar carefully. Out flew the rainbow flies, dancing through the air like tiny, flying jewels.

Freddy's long tongue shot out - SNAP! He caught a red one. His eyes went wide with amazement. "It tastes like strawberries!" he croaked happily.

SNAP! He caught an orange one. "Peaches!" he giggled, bouncing in his chair.

Soon Freddy was hopping around the classroom. His tongue zipped out to catch the colorful flies. Each one tasted like a different delicious fruit.

The other animals gathered around to watch. "Wow, Freddy!" said Bunny. "That looks so fun!"

"Can you teach us?" asked Squirrel excitedly.

Freddy felt proud and special. His unique frog tongue wasn't different - it was amazing! He showed his friends how to spot the fastest flies. He demonstrated his best tongue-catching techniques.

"Being a frog is pretty cool," Bear said admiringly.

Freddy's chest puffed out with pride. His green skin felt warm and happy now. "It really is!" he agreed with a big smile.

As snack time ended, Miss Lily helped Freddy catch the last few rainbow flies. "How do you feel now?" she asked.

"Happy!" Freddy croaked loudly. "My tongue is special. My snacks are magical!"

The other animals clapped for Freddy. He took a little bow, his webbed feet dancing with joy.

"Same time tomorrow?" Miss Lily asked with a wink.

"Yes, please!" Freddy said eagerly. "I can't wait for snack time tomorrow!"

As the animals cleaned up their snack area, Freddy helped put away the magical jar. He felt different now - not sad different, but wonderfully special different.

Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough. Freddy was already dreaming about what new flavors the rainbow flies might taste like. Maybe banana! Maybe cherry! Maybe something he'd never tasted before!

Freddy Frog had learned something important. Being different wasn't something to hide from. It was something to celebrate. And snack time? Well, that was going to be his favorite time of day from now on.

The end.
