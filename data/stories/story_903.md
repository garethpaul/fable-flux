# Story story_903

Zoe sat in the sunny meadow. The grass felt soft under her legs. She picked yellow dandelions one by one. The flowers smelled sweet and fresh.

"I wish I had a friend," Zoe said softly. Her voice sounded small in the big meadow. She made a chain with the dandelions. But playing alone felt sad.

Behind the tall oak tree, a little fox watched. His orange fur sparkled in the sunlight. His black nose twitched with curiosity. The fox wanted to play too.

The fox stepped closer. His paws made no sound on the grass. Zoe looked up and gasped. "Oh! A fox!"

The fox's tail wagged like a happy dog. His brown eyes looked kind and friendly. He tilted his head to one side.

"Hello, little fox," Zoe whispered. She held out her hand slowly. The fox sniffed her fingers gently. His nose felt cool and wet.

The fox picked up a stick in his mouth. He dropped it near Zoe's feet. His tail wagged faster now.

"Do you want to play?" Zoe asked. The fox yipped softly. It sounded like a tiny bark.

Zoe threw the stick across the meadow. The fox ran after it quickly. His legs moved like lightning. He brought the stick back to Zoe.

"Good catch!" Zoe laughed. Her voice rang out happy and clear. The fox seemed to smile with his whole face.

Next, they played hide and seek. Zoe closed her eyes tight. She counted to ten slowly. "Ready or not, here I come!"

The fox hid behind a big rock. Only his fluffy tail showed. Zoe pretended not to see it. "Where could Fox be?" she wondered aloud.

The fox giggled in fox language. It sounded like little chirps. Zoe found him easily. "There you are, silly fox!"

Now it was Fox's turn. He covered his eyes with his paws. Zoe hid behind the oak tree. The bark felt rough on her back.

Fox found her right away. His nose was very good at smelling. "You're so smart," Zoe said proudly.

They rolled down the grassy hill together. The world spun around them in green and blue. They laughed as they tumbled. The sun warmed their faces.

At the bottom, they lay on their backs. Fluffy white clouds floated overhead. "That cloud looks like a rabbit," Zoe pointed up.

Fox yipped and pointed with his nose. He saw a cloud that looked like a fish.

"We see the same things," Zoe realized. "We think alike, Fox."

They picked more dandelions together. Fox was very gentle with his teeth. He helped Zoe make the longest flower chain ever.

"This is for you," Zoe said. She put the chain around Fox's neck. It looked like a beautiful yellow necklace.

Fox found a pretty blue feather. He gave it to Zoe carefully. She tucked it behind her ear.

"Thank you, dear friend," Zoe said warmly. Fox nuzzled her hand with his soft head.

The sun began to set slowly. The sky turned pink and orange. It was time to go home.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" Zoe asked hopefully. Fox nodded his head up and down.

"I won't be lonely anymore," Zoe said happily. Fox yipped in agreement.

They hugged goodbye gently. Fox's fur felt warm and silky. He smelled like pine trees and sunshine.

Zoe walked home with light steps. She wore her blue feather proudly. The dandelion chain had made her first real friend.

Fox watched her go safely. Then he ran to his den. He wore his yellow necklace with pride.

The next morning, Zoe ran to the meadow. Fox was already there waiting. He had brought her a smooth, pretty stone.

"Best friends forever," Zoe declared joyfully. Fox barked his happy agreement.

From that day on, they played together every single day. The meadow became their special place. Two friends who found each other when they needed friendship most.

The end.
