# The Drum and the Frog

On a sunny farm morning, a bright red drum sat alone by the old oak tree. The drum loved to make music. *Boom, boom, boom!* went the drum. But something felt missing.

"I wish I had a friend," sighed the drum. "Music is fun, but it's lonely by myself."

The drum played a sad, slow beat. *Thump... thump... thump...* The sound drifted across the green farm fields.

Near the sparkling pond, a little green frog heard the music. The frog loved sounds too! Every day, the frog sang beautiful songs. *Ribbit, ribbit, ribbit!*

"What a wonderful sound!" said the frog. "Someone is making music!"

The curious frog hopped toward the oak tree. Hop, hop, hop! The frog's webbed feet made soft sounds on the grass.

"Hello!" called the friendly frog. "I heard your music. It sounds lovely!"

The drum felt excited. "Really? You like my drumming?"

"Oh yes!" said the frog. "I love music too. I sing songs by the pond."

The drum's surface gleamed with happiness. "Would you like to make music together?"

"That sounds wonderful!" croaked the frog with joy.

The frog hopped onto a smooth rock. The drum began a gentle rhythm. *Boom, boom, boom!*

Then the frog joined in. *Ribbit, ribbit, ribbit!*

"This is amazing!" laughed the drum. "Our sounds go together perfectly!"

The frog smiled wide. "Your drumbeats make my singing sound even better!"

They played a happy song together. The drum kept steady time while the frog sang a cheerful melody. The music floated across the entire farm.

Soon, other farm animals came to listen. The cows mooed softly. The chickens clucked along. Even the farmer stopped working to enjoy their beautiful music.

"Look!" said the frog. "Everyone loves our music!"

"Playing together is so much better than playing alone," said the drum happily.

The frog nodded. "We make a great team! Your strong beats help me sing my best."

"And your pretty voice makes my drumming sound magical," replied the drum.

They discovered they both loved morning sunshine. They both enjoyed making others happy with music. They both liked the peaceful sounds of the farm.

"We have so much in common!" said the frog.

"I'm so glad you heard my music," said the drum. "I was feeling lonely."

"Now we're both happy!" chirped the frog. "Friends make everything more fun."

Every day after that, the drum and frog met by the oak tree. They created new songs together. Sometimes they played fast, exciting music. Other times they made gentle, sleepy lullabies.

The other animals always gathered to listen. The farm became filled with joy and music every single day.

"You're my very best friend," said the drum one sunny afternoon.

"And you're mine too!" replied the frog. "Making music together is the best thing ever."

They played their favorite song as the sun set over the farm. The golden light made everything glow warmly. Their music mixed perfectly together, just like their friendship.

The drum learned that sharing made everything better. The frog learned that cooperation created beautiful things. Together, they discovered that friendship was the most wonderful music of all.

From that day forward, the red drum was never lonely again. The green frog had found the perfect musical partner. Their friendship grew stronger with every song they played together.

And whenever you visit a farm and hear beautiful music floating on the breeze, you might just be hearing the drum and the frog, playing their happy songs of friendship under the old oak tree.

*Boom, boom, boom! Ribbit, ribbit, ribbit!*

The end.
