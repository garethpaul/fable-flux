# Story story_1134

**Duck and Lily's Bedroom Adventure**

Duck sat on the soft blue carpet in Lily's bedroom. The morning sun made golden squares on the floor. Duck had fuzzy yellow feathers and bright orange feet. He felt very lonely.

Duck pushed his red toy car around in circles. The wheels made quiet rolling sounds. But playing alone was not much fun. Duck sighed a small, sad quack.

Lily peeked through her bedroom door. She had curly brown hair and wore pink pajamas with stars. Lily saw Duck sitting by himself. Her kind heart felt worried.

"Hi, Duck," Lily said softly. "Can I play too?"

Duck looked up with his shiny black eyes. No one had ever asked to play with him before. He felt shy and nervous.

"I don't know how to share," Duck whispered. His voice sounded like a tiny bell.

Lily smiled her warmest smile. She sat down on the carpet next to Duck. The carpet felt fluffy under her hands.

"Sharing is easy," Lily said gently. "We can take turns."

Duck thought about this. Maybe Lily was right. Maybe sharing could be fun.

"Okay," Duck said. "You can push my car."

Lily carefully took the little red car. She pushed it slowly around Duck's feet. The car's wheels sparkled in the sunlight.

"Vroom, vroom!" Lily said happily. "Where should the car go?"

Duck's eyes grew bright and excited. "To the pillow mountain!" he quacked.

Lily looked at the big fluffy pillows on the bed. They did look like snowy mountains. She pushed the car toward the pillows.

"The car is climbing up, up, up!" Lily said.

Duck clapped his small wings together. This was much more fun than playing alone. His lonely feeling started to disappear.

"My turn now," Duck said politely.

Lily handed him the car. Duck pushed it down the pillow mountain. The car tumbled and rolled. Both friends giggled with joy.

"Let's build a road," Lily suggested.

They used Lily's wooden blocks to make a long, winding road. The blocks clicked together like puzzle pieces. Duck and Lily worked side by side.

"This block goes here," Duck said, placing a blue block carefully.

"And this yellow one goes next," Lily added.

Their road stretched from the bed to the window. It curved around Lily's stuffed animals and under her small table.

Duck and Lily took turns pushing the car on their special road. Sometimes the car got stuck. They helped each other fix the problem.

"Friends help friends," Lily said, moving a block.

Duck nodded his small head. His heart felt warm and happy. He had never had a real friend before.

Next, they built a garage for the car. They used a shoebox and colored it with crayons. Lily drew flowers on the sides. Duck added wavy lines that looked like water.

"Our car needs a home," Duck said proudly.

The garage smelled like fresh crayons. It had a rainbow roof and green grass drawn around it.

When playtime was almost over, Duck felt worried. Would Lily still want to be his friend tomorrow?

"Lily," Duck said quietly. "Will you play with me again?"

Lily hugged Duck gently. His feathers felt soft and warm.

"You're my best friend now," Lily said. "We'll play together every day."

Duck's heart filled with happiness. He wasn't lonely anymore. He had found something wonderful – a true friend.

They put the car in its new garage. They stacked the blocks in a neat pile. Working together made cleaning up fun too.

"Thank you for teaching me to share," Duck said.

"Thank you for being my friend," Lily replied.

As the sun climbed higher in the sky, Duck and Lily planned their next adventure. Maybe they would build a castle. Or create a race track. With friendship, anything was possible.

Duck learned that sharing made everything better. Playing with a friend was much more fun than playing alone. And Lily learned that being kind to others brings the greatest joy.

From that day on, Duck was never lonely again. He had found the most precious thing of all – a friend who cared.

The end.
