# Story story_602

Penny the pig yawned big. The sun was high and warm. It was nap time at the farm.

"Time to rest, Penny," called Farmer Mae. "All good pigs need their sleep."

Penny's pink ears drooped down. She did not want to nap. The other animals were sleeping already. The cows lay under the big oak tree. The chickens tucked their heads under soft wings. Even the busy bees rested quietly.

But Penny wanted to play more. She wanted to splash in her mud puddle. She wanted to roll in the cool dirt. Nap time seemed so boring.

"I'm not sleepy," Penny said to herself. She walked slowly to her cozy pen. Her straw bed looked fluffy and nice. But Penny just stood there.

She tried closing her eyes. She opened them right back up. She tried lying down. She sat up again. The warm sun made her feel drowsy. But she fought the sleepy feeling.

Penny heard a soft sound. "Whooo... whooo..." 

She looked up. A wise old owl sat on her fence post. The owl had big golden eyes and fluffy gray feathers.

"Hello, little pig," said the owl gently. "I'm Oliver the dream owl. I help animals have wonderful naps."

Penny tilted her head. "Dream owl? What's that?"

Oliver smiled with his eyes. "I bring magical dreams to sleeping animals. But only to those who rest peacefully."

"What kind of dreams?" asked Penny. Her voice was curious now.

"Flying dreams," whispered Oliver. "Dreams where you soar high above the clouds. Dreams where you dance with butterflies. Dreams where you swim in lakes made of chocolate pudding."

Penny's eyes grew wide. "Really? That sounds amazing!"

"Would you like to try?" asked Oliver softly.

Penny nodded her head fast. She lay down in her straw bed. The straw smelled sweet and fresh. It felt soft under her round belly.

"Close your eyes," said Oliver. "Listen to my gentle hooting. Let the sleepy feelings come."

Penny closed her eyes tight. Oliver's soft hooting filled the air. "Whooo... whooo... whooo..."

The sound was like a lullaby. Penny felt her body getting heavy. Her breathing slowed down. The warm sun kissed her pink skin.

Soon Penny was fast asleep. And then the magic began.

In her dream, Penny had beautiful wings. They sparkled like diamonds in the sunlight. She flew high above the farm. The fields looked like a green quilt below.

She danced with orange butterflies. They twirled and spun together. Their wings made pretty patterns in the sky.

Then Penny dove into a lake of chocolate pudding. It was smooth and sweet. She swam and splashed happily. Marshmallows floated like little clouds.

When Penny woke up, she felt wonderful. Her body felt rested and strong. Her mind felt clear and happy.

"Did you enjoy your magical nap?" asked Oliver. He was still sitting on the fence post.

"Oh yes!" said Penny with a big smile. "That was the best dream ever! I flew and danced and swam!"

"Dreams make nap time special," said Oliver. "Every time you rest, new adventures wait for you."

Penny stood up and stretched. She felt full of energy. But she also felt grateful for her peaceful sleep.

"Thank you, Oliver," said Penny. "I never knew naps could be so much fun!"

The other animals were waking up too. They all looked happy and refreshed. The chickens clucked softly. The cows mooed in gentle voices.

That evening, Penny played joyfully. She splashed in her mud puddle. She rolled in the cool dirt. But she didn't worry about tomorrow's nap time.

When bedtime came, Penny was excited. She couldn't wait to see Oliver again. She couldn't wait for new magical dreams.

"Tomorrow I might dream about flying to the moon," she thought happily.

Penny snuggled into her straw bed. She closed her eyes with a smile. Soon Oliver's gentle hooting filled the air again.

And Penny drifted off to sleep, ready for another wonderful adventure in dreamland.

The end.
