# Story story_1108

**The Horse in the Basement**

Honey the horse lived in a cozy basement. Her home had soft hay beds. It had warm yellow lights. It smelled like sweet oats and fresh straw.

Every day, Honey heard sounds upstairs. She heard children laughing and playing. Their feet went thump, thump, thump on the floor above.

"What are they doing up there?" Honey wondered.

One sunny morning, Honey heard new sounds. The children were singing a happy song. They were clapping their hands together.

"We're playing Musical Chairs!" shouted a boy named Sam.

"When the music stops, find a chair!" called a girl named Maya.

Honey's ears perked up tall. Musical Chairs sounded like so much fun! She wanted to play too.

But there was one big problem. Honey was stuck in the basement. The stairs were too narrow for a horse. The door was too small.

Honey felt sad and left out. A tear rolled down her fuzzy cheek.

"I wish I could play with my friends," she whispered.

Then Honey had an idea. Maybe she could make her own Musical Chairs game! She looked around her basement home.

Honey found three old wooden boxes. She pushed them with her nose. She made them into chairs in a circle.

Next, Honey found an old radio. She turned the knob with her teeth. Beautiful music filled the basement.

"Perfect!" neighed Honey happily.

But when Honey tried to play alone, it wasn't fun. She walked around the boxes. The music played loudly. When it stopped, she sat on a box.

"This is boring," Honey sighed. "Musical Chairs needs more than one player."

Honey's first idea didn't work. She felt disappointed. But she didn't give up.

Honey listened to the sounds upstairs again. She heard the children having so much fun. She really wanted to join them.

Then Honey had a better idea. A wonderful idea!

She walked to the bottom of the stairs. She took a very deep breath. Then Honey began to stomp her hooves.

STOMP! STOMP! STOMP!

The sound echoed through the whole house.

Upstairs, the children stopped playing.

"What's that noise?" asked Sam.

"It sounds like music!" said Maya.

STOMP! STOMP! STOMP! went Honey's hooves.

She made a steady, happy beat. It sounded like drums playing.

The children ran to the basement door. They opened it carefully.

"Honey!" they called down the stairs. "Is that you making music?"

"Yes!" neighed Honey proudly. "I want to help with Musical Chairs!"

Sam clapped his hands with joy. "Honey can be our drummer!"

"What a great idea!" agreed Maya.

The children moved their game to the top of the basement stairs. They put their chairs in the hallway. Now Honey could see them play.

When Honey stomped her hooves, the children danced around the chairs. When Honey stopped stomping, they quickly found seats.

"This is the best Musical Chairs ever!" laughed Sam.

"Honey makes the perfect music!" giggled Maya.

Honey felt so happy and proud. Her heart felt warm and full. She wasn't left out anymore. She was the most important part of the game!

All afternoon, they played together. Honey's hoofbeats made wonderful music. The children danced and laughed above her.

When the game ended, the children came to the basement door.

"Thank you, Honey!" they called down. "You're the best drummer ever!"

"You made our game so much more fun!" added Maya.

Honey smiled her biggest horse smile. She had learned something important that day.

Sometimes when you can't do something one way, you can find another way. Sometimes the best solution is different than you first imagined.

From that day on, Honey was always part of the children's games. She became their special music maker. She never felt left out again.

And every time they played, Honey remembered her lesson. When one idea doesn't work, try another idea. Never give up on having fun with friends.

The basement filled with happy sounds every day. And Honey's heart stayed full of joy.

The end.
