# Story story_1017

**The Blocks and the Friendly Cow**

In a sunny garden, three colorful blocks sat alone. There was Red Block, Blue Block, and Yellow Block. They lived under a big apple tree.

Red Block sighed. "I wish we had a friend to play with."

Blue Block nodded sadly. "We stack ourselves up every day. But it gets lonely."

Yellow Block looked around the empty garden. "Maybe someone will come play with us today."

The blocks tried to have fun by themselves. They made a tall tower. They made a bridge. They rolled around in the soft grass. But something was missing.

"Playing alone isn't as fun," said Red Block.

Just then, they heard a gentle "Moo!" A friendly brown cow walked into the garden. Her name was Daisy. She had big brown eyes and a pink nose.

Daisy saw the three blocks under the apple tree. "Hello there! What are you doing?"

"We're just playing," said Blue Block quietly. "But we don't have any friends."

Daisy's eyes sparkled with kindness. "I would love to be your friend! Can I play with you?"

The blocks looked at each other with surprise. They had never played with a cow before.

"How can we play together?" asked Yellow Block. "You're so big and we're so small."

Daisy thought for a moment. She lowered her head gently. "I have an idea! You could stack on my back. I'll be very still."

Red Block felt excited. "That sounds fun! But will it be safe?"

"I promise to be very careful," said Daisy softly. "I love making new friends."

The blocks decided to try. Red Block climbed onto Daisy's back first. The cow's fur felt warm and soft. Blue Block stacked on top of Red Block. Finally, Yellow Block climbed to the very top.

"This is amazing!" laughed Yellow Block. "We're so high up!"

Daisy walked slowly around the garden. The blocks could see everything from up high. They saw butterflies dancing. They smelled sweet flowers blooming. They felt the warm sunshine on their faces.

"Look at those red roses!" pointed Blue Block.

"I can see the whole garden!" said Red Block happily.

After their ride, the blocks slid down gently. They landed in the soft grass with giggles.

"That was the best game ever!" said Yellow Block.

Daisy smiled widely. "I had fun too! What else can we play?"

They played hide and seek next. The blocks hid behind flowers while Daisy counted. Her deep voice echoed in the garden. "One, two, three..."

Then they played a new game. The blocks made patterns in the dirt. Daisy used her hoof to trace around them. They made circles and squares together.

"You're so creative!" said Red Block to Daisy.

"You taught me new games!" replied Daisy happily.

As the sun began to set, they sat together under the apple tree. The blocks leaned against Daisy's warm side. They felt safe and happy.

"I'm so glad you came to our garden," said Blue Block.

"Me too," said Daisy. "I was lonely walking around by myself."

Yellow Block had an idea. "Will you come back tomorrow?"

"Every day!" promised Daisy. "We're friends now."

Red Block felt warm inside. "We'll never be lonely again."

The four friends watched the sunset paint the sky pink and orange. They made plans for tomorrow's games. They would build block castles. They would explore the garden together.

"Friendship makes everything more fun," said Daisy wisely.

The blocks agreed completely. They had learned something wonderful today. Playing alone was okay, but playing with friends was magical.

As stars began to twinkle, Daisy said goodnight. "Sweet dreams, my block friends."

"Goodnight, Daisy!" called all three blocks together.

The blocks snuggled close under their apple tree. They fell asleep with big smiles. Tomorrow would bring new adventures with their dear friend Daisy.

In the quiet garden, friendship had bloomed like the prettiest flower of all.

The end.
