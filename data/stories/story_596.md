# Story story_596

**Zoe's Magical Snack Time**

The big clock on the kitchen wall said three o'clock. Mama smiled and clapped her hands together. "Snack time, Zoe!" she called cheerfully.

Zoe sat on the living room carpet. She was building a tall tower with her colorful blocks. Red ones, blue ones, yellow ones stacked up high. She didn't want to stop playing.

"Do I have to?" Zoe asked. Her bottom lip stuck out a little. "My tower isn't finished yet."

Mama walked over and sat down beside her. "Your tower is beautiful, sweetie. But your tummy needs food to grow big and strong."

Zoe's tummy did feel a bit rumbly. But snack time seemed so boring. Just sitting at the table eating crackers or apple slices. She wanted to keep building and playing.

"I'm not hungry," Zoe said quietly. She added another blue block to her tower.

Mama thought for a moment. Then her eyes sparkled with an idea. "What if we made snack time special today? Come see what's in the kitchen."

Zoe followed Mama slowly. Her bare feet made soft patting sounds on the cool tile floor. The kitchen smelled like cinnamon and sunshine from the open window.

On the counter sat a big wooden cutting board. Next to it were small bowls filled with different foods. Orange carrot sticks stood up like tiny trees. Round crackers looked like golden coins. Purple grapes sparkled like little jewels.

"We're going to build something together," Mama said with a wink. "But not with blocks this time."

Zoe's eyes grew wide. "Build with food?"

"Yes! We can make faces, animals, or anything you want. Food can be our building blocks today."

Zoe climbed onto her special step stool. Now she could reach the counter perfectly. The wooden board felt smooth under her small hands.

"Can I make a butterfly?" Zoe asked. Her voice sounded excited now.

"Of course! What would make good butterfly wings?"

Zoe picked up two round crackers. She placed them side by side on the board. "These can be the wings!"

"Perfect! What about the body?"

A long carrot stick became the butterfly's body. Zoe placed it right down the middle. Two thin pretzel sticks made wonderful antennae. She stuck them at the top.

"It needs spots on its wings," Zoe decided. She carefully placed purple grapes on each cracker wing. The butterfly looked beautiful and delicious.

Mama made her own creation. She used apple slices for a flower's petals. A round cracker became the center. Green cucumber pieces made the stem and leaves.

"This is so much fun!" Zoe giggled. Her hands were busy arranging and rearranging her food art.

They made a whole garden on the cutting board. Zoe's butterfly sat next to Mama's flower. Cherry tomatoes became ladybugs with pretzel stick legs. Cheese cubes turned into sunny houses.

"Now comes the best part," Mama said. "We get to eat our artwork!"

Zoe bit into her butterfly's wing. The cracker crunched between her teeth. It tasted salty and good. The sweet grapes burst with juice in her mouth.

"Mmm! My butterfly tastes like sunshine!" Zoe said happily.

She ate every piece of her creation. The carrots were crisp and fresh. The pretzels were perfectly salty. Even the vegetables tasted better when they were part of her butterfly.

"Can we make food art again tomorrow?" Zoe asked. Cracker crumbs dotted her chin.

"We can make something new every day," Mama promised. "What would you like to build tomorrow?"

Zoe thought hard while she chewed. "Maybe a rainbow! Or a castle! Or a funny face!"

"All of those sound wonderful," Mama said. She wiped Zoe's chin with a soft napkin.

After snack time, Zoe went back to her block tower. But now she was thinking about tomorrow's food adventure. Maybe she could make a block castle AND a food castle.

Her tummy felt happy and full. Snack time wasn't boring anymore. It was magical! She couldn't wait to see what delicious creations they would build together tomorrow.

The kitchen clock ticked softly as Zoe played, already dreaming of her next edible masterpiece.

The end.
