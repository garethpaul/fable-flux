# Story story_1200

**The Lonely Blocks and Mama's Love**

In a sunny green field lived three little blocks. There was Red Block, Blue Block, and Yellow Block. They sat all alone under the big oak tree.

The warm sun shone on their bright colors. The soft grass tickled their square sides. But the blocks felt very lonely.

"I wish we had someone to play with," sighed Red Block.

"Me too," said Blue Block sadly. "Playing alone is no fun."

Yellow Block rolled closer to his friends. "Maybe someone will come today."

Just then, they heard gentle footsteps. Mama was walking through the field. She had kind eyes and a warm smile. Her dress swayed in the breeze.

"Hello there, little blocks," Mama said softly. "What are you doing all by yourselves?"

The blocks looked up at her hopefully. "We don't have anyone to play with," Red Block explained.

"We've been sitting here all morning," added Blue Block.

Mama knelt down in the soft grass. "Well, I have an idea. May I play with you?"

The blocks wiggled with excitement. "Really? You want to play with us?"

"Of course!" Mama laughed. Her laugh sounded like music. "What shall we build together?"

Yellow Block bounced up and down. "Let's make something tall!"

"A tower!" shouted Blue Block.

Mama gently picked up Red Block first. She placed him carefully on the ground. Then she stacked Blue Block on top. Finally, Yellow Block went on the very top.

"Look how high we are!" called Yellow Block from way up high.

The tower swayed gently in the warm breeze. The blocks giggled as they felt the wind tickle them.

"This is wonderful!" said Red Block. "But I want to try something else."

Mama carefully helped them down. "What would you like to build now?"

"A train!" suggested Blue Block.

Mama lined up all three blocks in a row. She made train sounds. "Choo-choo! All aboard the block train!"

The blocks laughed and laughed. They felt so happy playing with Mama.

Next, they built a bridge. Mama used Red and Blue as the base. Yellow Block became the bridge across the top.

"I can see the whole field from here," Yellow Block called out.

They played for hours in the warm sunshine. Mama showed them how to make a house. She helped them roll down the small hill. They even played hide-and-seek behind the oak tree.

"You're the best playmate ever," Red Block told Mama.

"You make everything more fun," agreed Blue Block.

Yellow Block rolled close to Mama's hand. "Will you come back tomorrow?"

Mama smiled her warm smile. "I would love to play with you every day."

As the sun began to set, Mama gathered the blocks together. She made them a cozy circle under the oak tree.

"Thank you for playing with us," said Red Block.

"We're not lonely anymore," said Blue Block happily.

"Now we have a friend," added Yellow Block.

Mama patted each block gently. "And I have three wonderful new friends too."

The blocks felt warm and happy inside. They had learned something special today. Playing together was much better than playing alone.

"Good night, my dear block friends," Mama whispered.

"Good night, Mama," the blocks said together.

The stars began to twinkle above them. A gentle breeze rustled the oak tree leaves. The blocks snuggled close together under their tree.

They dreamed of all the fun games they would play tomorrow. They dreamed of building castles and rockets. They dreamed of rolling races and block parades.

But most of all, they dreamed of playing with their new friend Mama.

From that day on, Mama came to the field every morning. She and the blocks became the very best of friends. They built amazing things together. They laughed and played until sunset.

The blocks were never lonely again. They had learned that friendship makes everything better. And Mama had learned that playing with friends brings the greatest joy of all.

The field was now filled with laughter, love, and the happiest blocks in the whole world.

The end.
