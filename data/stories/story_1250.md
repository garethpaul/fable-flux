# Story story_1250

**The Magic of Park Day**

"Time for the park!" called Mom.

Tommy looked up from his toy cars. He made a sad face.

"I don't want to go," he said. "I want to stay home."

His big brother Jake put on his sneakers. "Come on, Tommy. It will be fun!"

Tommy shook his head. The park seemed boring today. He wanted to play with his cars instead.

Mom smiled gently. "We can bring one car with you."

Tommy picked his favorite red car. He put it in his pocket. His feet felt heavy as they walked outside.

The sun felt warm on Tommy's face. Birds sang in the tall trees. But Tommy still felt grumpy.

At the park, Jake ran to the swings. "Push me, Mom!" he called.

Tommy sat on the grass. He took out his red car. He rolled it back and forth. Back and forth.

The car rolled toward a small hill. Tommy followed it. The car rolled faster down the hill.

"Vroom!" said Tommy softly.

The car rolled into some leaves. Tommy heard a rustling sound. What was that?

He pushed the leaves away. Underneath was a tiny path. The path was made of smooth stones.

"Jake! Mom! Come see!" Tommy called.

Jake ran over. "Wow! What did you find?"

"A tiny road," said Tommy. His eyes got big and round.

Mom came to look too. "What a wonderful discovery!"

Tommy put his car on the stone path. It rolled perfectly. The stones made a gentle clicking sound.

"It's like a real road," whispered Jake.

Tommy pushed his car along the path. The car went around a big tree root. It went past some pretty flowers.

"Where does it go?" asked Tommy.

They followed the path together. It curved and twisted. It led to a small pond.

"Look at the water," said Jake. "It sparkles like diamonds!"

Tommy knelt down by the pond. He saw his reflection in the water. His car reflected too.

"The car looks like it's swimming," giggled Tommy.

A friendly duck swam over. It quacked hello. Tommy quacked back.

"Can we make the path longer?" asked Tommy.

"Great idea!" said Jake.

They found more smooth stones. Tommy and Jake worked together. They made the path go around the pond.

Mom helped too. She found some pretty pebbles. The pebbles were white and shiny.

"These can be road markers," she said.

Tommy's car zoomed along the new path. It went fast and smooth. The wheels made happy spinning sounds.

"This is the best road ever!" said Tommy.

Jake found a stick. He made bridges over small puddles. Tommy's car could drive right over them.

"Beep beep!" said Tommy, making car sounds.

Other children came to watch. They thought the path was amazing too.

"Can we help?" asked a little girl.

"Yes!" said Tommy. "We need more stones!"

Soon five children were building the path. They laughed and worked together. The path grew longer and longer.

Tommy's car had so many places to explore. It could go fast or slow. It could take different routes.

"Time to go home," called Mom.

"Already?" asked Tommy. He felt surprised. The time went by so fast.

"Can we come back tomorrow?" he asked.

"Of course," said Mom. "The path will be waiting."

Tommy carefully put his car in his pocket. He patted it gently.

Jake smiled at his little brother. "That was really fun, Tommy."

"The best park day ever," agreed Tommy.

As they walked home, Tommy skipped. His feet felt light and happy now.

"Tomorrow I want to bring my blue car too," said Tommy.

"And maybe we can build a parking lot," suggested Jake.

"And a gas station!" added Tommy excitedly.

Mom listened to their plans. She smiled warmly.

That night, Tommy dreamed about his stone path. In his dream, all his cars could drive on it. They had races and adventures.

When he woke up, Tommy ran to the window. The sun was shining brightly.

"Is it time for the park yet?" he called to Mom.

Tommy couldn't wait for another magical day outside.

The end.
