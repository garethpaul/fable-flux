# Story story_948

**Emma and the Meadow Friend**

In a sunny meadow filled with yellow flowers, a little rabbit named Benny sat all alone. He picked dandelions one by one. The soft petals felt smooth in his tiny paws. Benny sighed a big sigh. Playing by himself wasn't very fun.

The warm sun made the grass smell sweet. Butterflies danced around purple clover. But Benny felt sad inside. He wished he had someone to play with. He threw a dandelion into the air. It floated down slowly like a tiny parachute.

"I wish I had a friend," Benny whispered.

Just then, footsteps came through the tall grass. A little girl with curly brown hair peeked out. She wore a bright red dress and yellow shoes. Her name was Emma.

Emma saw Benny sitting alone. Her heart felt warm and caring. She wanted to help the lonely rabbit feel better.

"Hello," Emma said softly. "I'm Emma. What's your name?"

Benny looked up with surprise. His brown eyes grew wide. "I'm Benny," he said in a quiet voice. "I was just picking flowers."

Emma smiled her biggest smile. "Can I pick flowers too? Playing together sounds like fun!"

Benny's ears perked up. Nobody had ever wanted to play with him before. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" Emma said. She sat down in the soft grass. The ground felt cool and comfortable.

Emma picked a bright yellow dandelion. She showed Benny how to make a flower crown. They wove the stems together carefully. Their fingers worked side by side.

"Look, Benny! We made something beautiful together!"

Benny felt happy bubbles in his tummy. The flower crown was perfect. They had made it as a team.

Next, they played hide and seek. Emma counted to ten while Benny hid behind a big oak tree. The bark felt rough against his soft fur. When Emma found him, they both giggled.

Then Benny showed Emma how to hop like a rabbit. Emma tried her best. She hopped and hopped until she was out of breath. They laughed together as Emma tumbled in the grass.

"Your turn to teach me something," Benny said.

Emma taught Benny how to make daisy chains. They picked white daisies with sunny yellow centers. The flowers smelled fresh and clean. Soon they had long, pretty chains.

"We can wear them like necklaces!" Emma said.

They put the daisy chains around their necks. Benny felt so special wearing his flower necklace. Emma clapped her hands with joy.

The afternoon sun felt warm on their faces. A gentle breeze made the grass dance. Birds sang sweet songs in the trees above.

"Benny, you're my very best friend," Emma said. Her voice was full of kindness.

Benny's heart felt so full of happiness. "You're my best friend too, Emma. Thank you for playing with me."

They decided to have a friendship picnic. Emma shared her apple slices. Benny found sweet clover for them to taste. The apple was crispy and juicy. The clover tasted like honey.

As the sun began to set, they lay on their backs. They watched fluffy white clouds float by. One cloud looked like a bunny. Another looked like a heart.

"I was so lonely before," Benny said. "Now I feel happy inside."

"Me too," Emma said. "Friends make everything better."

They promised to meet in the meadow every day. They would pick flowers and play games. They would share snacks and tell stories.

The meadow felt magical now. It wasn't just a place with flowers anymore. It was where their friendship began.

Benny and Emma walked home together. Their flower crowns sat perfectly on their heads. Their daisy chain necklaces swayed as they walked.

From that day on, the meadow was never quiet. It was filled with laughter and joy. Two best friends played there every single day.

And they lived happily ever after, together.

The end.
