# The Little Red Bike and Ben

In the corner of the sunny backyard sat a little red bike named Ruby. She had shiny silver handlebars and a cheerful bell. But today, Ruby felt very lonely.

"Ring, ring," went her bell sadly. Ruby watched the empty sandbox nearby. The golden sand sparkled in the morning sun. She wished she had someone to play with.

Ruby rolled herself closer to the sandbox. Her wheels made soft crunching sounds on the grass. She looked at all that beautiful sand. "I wish I could play in there," she sighed.

Just then, a little boy named Ben came outside. He had curly brown hair and wore a bright blue shirt. Ben saw Ruby sitting by the sandbox all alone.

"Hello there, little bike," Ben said kindly. "You look sad. What's wrong?"

Ruby's bell jingled softly. "I want to play in the sandbox. But I don't know how. My wheels might get stuck."

Ben smiled and patted Ruby's red seat. "Maybe we can figure it out together! I love playing in sand too."

Ben looked at the sandbox carefully. Then he looked at Ruby's wheels. "I have an idea!" he said excitedly.

Ben ran to the garage. He came back with two wooden boards. "These can be ramps!" he explained. "You can roll into the sandbox safely."

Together, they placed the boards carefully. Ben held them steady while Ruby rolled forward slowly. Her wheels touched the sand with a soft whoosh.

"It worked!" Ruby chimed happily. Her bell rang with joy.

Ben climbed into the sandbox too. "What should we build?" he asked.

"How about roads for me to ride on?" suggested Ruby.

"Yes! And I can make mountains too!" Ben said.

Ben used his hands to smooth the sand. He made winding paths for Ruby to follow. Ruby rolled along the sandy roads, leaving pretty wheel tracks behind.

"This is so much fun!" Ruby laughed. Her bell jingled merrily.

Ben built tall sand mountains. Ruby rode around them in circles. The sand felt cool and soft under her wheels.

"Let's make a tunnel!" Ben suggested. He dug carefully through one mountain. Ruby rode through it slowly.

"Wheee!" Ruby called out. "I feel like I'm on an adventure!"

Ben giggled and clapped his hands. "You're the bravest bike ever!"

They played for hours in the warm sunshine. Ben made bridges from sticks. Ruby rode over them carefully. They built a whole sand city together.

"You know what?" Ben said, sitting back to admire their work. "This is much more fun than playing alone."

Ruby's bell rang in agreement. "Everything is better with a friend! I couldn't have done this without you."

Ben hugged Ruby's handlebars gently. "And I couldn't have built such cool roads without your help. Your wheels make the best tracks!"

As the sun grew warmer, other children came to see what they had built. Everyone admired their amazing sand city.

"Wow!" said Ben's sister Emma. "How did you make such perfect roads?"

"Ruby and I worked together," Ben explained proudly. "She's my new friend."

Ruby's bell chimed happily. She wasn't lonely anymore. She had found a wonderful friend who understood her.

Ben carefully helped Ruby roll out of the sandbox. He brushed the sand off her wheels gently.

"Thank you for playing with me," Ruby said. "You made me feel special."

"Thank you for sharing your ideas," Ben replied. "Tomorrow, let's build a sand castle with a bike path around it!"

Ruby's bell sang out joyfully. "That sounds perfect!"

As the day ended, Ruby didn't feel lonely anymore. She had learned that the best adventures happen when friends work together. Ben had learned that everyone has special talents to share.

From that day on, Ruby and Ben played together every afternoon. They discovered that cooperation made everything more fun. Whether building sand cities or racing through the yard, they were always better together.

Ruby's bell rang happily in the sunset. She had found her very best friend, and their friendship would last forever.
