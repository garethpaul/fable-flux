# Story story_929

**Truck's Art Adventure**

The big yellow school bell rang loudly. "Art time!" called Miss Emma cheerfully. All the children jumped up from their seats. They clapped their hands with excitement.

But Truck sat still in his chair. He looked down at his sneakers. Art time made his tummy feel funny. His hands always got messy. The paintbrush felt too big. The crayons broke when he pressed too hard.

"Come on, Truck!" said his friend Lily. "We're making rainbow pictures today!" Lily skipped to the art table. Her ponytail bounced up and down.

Truck walked very slowly to his spot. The art table was covered with bright paper. There were cups of water that sparkled. Red, blue, and yellow paint sat in little containers. The brushes looked soft and fluffy.

Miss Emma smiled at Truck. "What colors make you happy?" she asked gently. Her voice was warm like hot chocolate.

Truck shrugged his shoulders. "I don't know," he whispered. "I'm not good at art."

Miss Emma sat down next to him. "Everyone is good at art," she said. "Art is about having fun. Not about being perfect."

Truck picked up a fat purple crayon. It felt smooth in his fingers. He made one small circle on his paper. The purple looked pretty against the white.

"That's a beautiful start!" said Miss Emma. "What could that purple circle be?"

Truck looked at his circle. It reminded him of something. "Maybe... a wheel?" he said quietly.

"A wheel! What has wheels?" asked Miss Emma.

Truck's eyes got bigger. "Trucks have wheels!" He loved trucks more than anything. Big trucks, little trucks, fire trucks, dump trucks.

Suddenly, Truck felt excited. He grabbed a blue crayon. He drew a big rectangle above the purple wheel. Then he added another purple wheel. His truck was taking shape!

"Wow!" said Lily from across the table. "That's an awesome truck!"

Truck smiled for the first time. He picked up an orange crayon. He drew flames on the side of his truck. The orange felt waxy and smooth. It smelled like vanilla.

Next, he tried the paint. The brush tickled his fingers. He dipped it in yellow paint. The color was bright like sunshine. He painted windows on his truck. The paint felt cool and wet.

"My truck needs a driver," Truck said to himself. He used a pink crayon to draw a little person. The person had a big smile and curly hair.

Miss Emma walked by his table. "Tell me about your truck," she said.

"It's a super fast truck!" Truck explained proudly. "It can fly through rainbows. The driver is me! I'm going on adventures."

Truck kept adding to his picture. He drew green grass at the bottom. He made fluffy white clouds in the sky. He even added some birds flying nearby.

His hands were covered in paint and crayon marks. But it felt good. It felt like he was creating magic on paper.

"Five more minutes!" called Miss Emma.

Truck looked at his artwork. It was colorful and messy and wonderful. The truck looked ready to drive right off the paper. He had never made anything so special before.

When art time ended, Truck held up his picture. "Look what I made!" he called out happily.

All his friends gathered around. "That's so cool!" said Ben. "I love the flames!" said Sarah. "Can you teach me to draw trucks?" asked Maya.

Truck felt warm and proud inside. His cheeks turned pink with happiness.

Miss Emma helped him hang his picture on the wall. "This deserves a special spot," she said. The picture looked important hanging there.

As Truck packed up his backpack, he felt different. Art time wasn't scary anymore. It was fun! He couldn't wait to tell his mom about his flying truck.

"What are we making tomorrow?" Truck asked Miss Emma.

"Clay animals," she replied with a wink.

Truck grinned from ear to ear. "I can't wait!" he said. "Maybe I'll make a clay truck too!"

He skipped out of the classroom, already dreaming about tomorrow's art adventure.

The end.
