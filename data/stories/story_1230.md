# Story story_1230

Anna sat on the wooden fence. The farm was very quiet today. Her boots swung back and forth. She watched the chickens peck at seeds.

"I wish I had someone to play with," Anna said softly. The wind rustled through the tall corn stalks. It made a gentle whooshing sound.

High up in the old oak tree, Ollie the owl was watching. His big round eyes blinked slowly. Ollie wanted to play too. But he was shy. He had never played with a little girl before.

Anna picked up a smooth stone. She tossed it into the pond. Splash! The water made tiny ripples. A frog croaked from somewhere in the reeds.

Ollie fluffed his soft brown feathers. He wanted to say hello. But what if Anna didn't like owls? What if she ran away?

Anna found a stick and drew pictures in the dirt. She drew a house. She drew a flower. She drew a sad face.

"Hoo, hoo," came a gentle sound from above.

Anna looked up. She saw two bright yellow eyes looking down at her. The owl sat on a thick branch. His feathers were the color of chocolate milk.

"Hello there," Anna said sweetly. "Are you lonely too?"

Ollie's heart fluttered with excitement. She wasn't scared! He spread his wings a little bit. Then he glided down to a lower branch.

"Hoo, hoo," he said again. This time it sounded friendlier.

Anna stood up on the fence. Now she was closer to Ollie. "Do you want to play with me?" she asked. Her voice was kind and warm.

Ollie bobbed his head up and down. Yes, yes, yes! He wanted to play very much.

Anna climbed down from the fence. She picked up her stick again. "Want to see me draw?" she asked.

Ollie flew down to the ground. His talons made soft scratching sounds in the dirt. He watched Anna draw a big circle.

"This is the sun," Anna explained. She added lines around it. "These are the sun's rays."

Ollie was amazed. He had never seen anyone draw before. He picked up a small twig in his beak. He tried to make a line too.

"Good job!" Anna clapped her hands. "You're drawing too!"

They drew together for a while. Anna made flowers and trees. Ollie made squiggly lines and dots. The dirt became their canvas.

"Let's play hide and seek," Anna suggested. Her eyes sparkled with joy.

Ollie tilted his head. He didn't know this game.

"I'll count to ten," Anna said. "You hide. Then I'll try to find you."

Anna covered her eyes with her hands. "One, two, three..." she counted slowly.

Ollie looked around quickly. Where could he hide? He flew to the haystack. His brown feathers blended with the golden hay perfectly.

"Ten! Ready or not, here I come!" Anna called out.

She looked behind the water trough. No owl there. She checked near the chicken coop. Still no Ollie. She walked toward the haystack.

"Hoo!" came a happy sound from the hay.

"Found you!" Anna giggled. The sound was like tiny silver bells.

Now it was Ollie's turn to seek. Anna hid behind the big red barn. Ollie found her quickly with his sharp eyes.

They played until the sun began to set. The sky turned pink and orange. It looked like cotton candy.

"I'm so happy we're friends," Anna said. She gently petted Ollie's soft feathers.

Ollie nuzzled against Anna's hand. He felt warm and happy inside. He wasn't lonely anymore.

"Will you play with me tomorrow?" Anna asked.

"Hoo, hoo!" Ollie answered. That meant yes in owl language.

Anna's grandmother called from the farmhouse. "Anna! Time for dinner!"

"I have to go now," Anna said. "But I'll see you tomorrow, Ollie."

Ollie watched Anna skip toward the house. Her ponytail bounced with each step. He felt grateful for his new friend.

From that day on, Anna and Ollie played together every afternoon. The farm was never quiet anymore. It was filled with laughter, hooting, and the sounds of friendship.

Anna learned that friends can be found in the most surprising places. Sometimes they have feathers and say "hoo" instead of "hello."

And that was perfectly wonderful.

The end.
