# Grandma's Brave Morning

Grandma Ruby stood in her cozy bathroom. She looked at herself in the mirror. Her silver hair was neat and tidy. Her favorite purple dress was ready for the day.

But Grandma Ruby felt butterflies dancing in her tummy. Today was very special. Today, she was going back to school!

"Oh my," she whispered to her reflection. "I haven't been to school in fifty years!"

Grandma Ruby was taking a computer class. She wanted to learn how to video chat with her grandchildren. But thinking about sitting in a classroom made her hands feel shaky.

She turned on the warm water. The gentle sound usually made her feel calm. But today, even the bubbling water couldn't chase away her nervous feelings.

"What if I'm too old?" she wondered aloud. "What if the other students are much younger?"

Grandma Ruby tried taking deep breaths. In through her nose. Out through her mouth. The steam from the warm water felt soft on her cheeks.

"Breathe, Ruby," she told herself. "Just breathe."

But her heart still beat fast like a little bird's wings.

Next, she tried splashing cool water on her face. The droplets felt refreshing and bright. She patted her cheeks dry with her fluffy yellow towel.

"There," she said, looking in the mirror again. "That's better."

But the nervous butterflies were still dancing around.

Then Grandma Ruby remembered something special. She opened her medicine cabinet carefully. Inside was a small, sparkly pin. It was shaped like a star.

Her granddaughter Emma had given it to her. "This is for being brave, Grandma," Emma had said.

Grandma Ruby pinned the star to her purple dress. She touched it gently with her finger. It felt smooth and warm.

"Emma believes I'm brave," she said softly. "Maybe I am brave."

She thought about all the brave things she had done. She had raised three children. She had learned to drive when she was forty. She had even tried sushi last week!

"I can do hard things," she said to the mirror. Her voice sounded stronger now.

Grandma Ruby brushed her teeth extra carefully. The minty toothpaste tasted fresh and clean. She hummed a little song while she brushed.

Then she had another wonderful idea. She reached for her special lipstick. It was bright red and made her feel confident. She put it on slowly, making her lips look pretty and bold.

"There!" she said, smiling at herself. "Now I look ready for anything!"

She practiced introducing herself to the mirror. "Hello, I'm Ruby. I'm excited to learn about computers."

Her voice sounded cheerful and friendly. The nervous butterflies were getting quieter.

Grandma Ruby picked up her new notebook and pencil. They smelled like fresh paper and wood. She had covered the notebook with flower stickers.

"Learning new things keeps my brain healthy," she reminded herself. "This is good for me."

She took one last look in the bathroom mirror. Her eyes were bright and twinkling. Her star pin caught the morning light.

"I might be nervous," she said to her reflection, "but I'm also excited. New adventures are waiting for me!"

Grandma Ruby walked out of the bathroom with her head held high. Her purple dress swished softly as she moved. The star pin sparkled on her chest.

At school, she discovered something wonderful. Many other grandmas and grandpas were in her class! They all felt nervous too. But they were all brave enough to try something new.

By lunchtime, Grandma Ruby was laughing with her new friends. She had learned how to turn on a computer. She had even typed her name!

That evening, she looked in her bathroom mirror again. "I did it!" she said proudly. "I was nervous, but I was brave too."

The star pin twinkled back at her. Tomorrow, she would go to school again. And she couldn't wait!

Sometimes feeling nervous just means something exciting is about to happen.

The end.
