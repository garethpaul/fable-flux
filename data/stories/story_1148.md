# Story story_1148

**Papa's Magical Bedtime**

The clock on the wall said eight o'clock. Mama looked at Papa with a smile. "Time for bed, Papa," she said softly.

Papa was sitting on his favorite blue chair. He was reading a book about dragons. The pictures were so colorful and exciting. Papa did not want to stop reading.

"But Mama," Papa said with a frown. "I want to see what happens next. The dragon is about to find the treasure!"

Mama walked over to Papa. She smelled like vanilla cookies. Her voice was gentle and warm. "The dragon will wait for you tomorrow, Papa. Your bed is calling."

Papa dragged his feet across the soft carpet. His pajamas felt cozy but he still felt grumpy. Brushing his teeth seemed to take forever. The toothpaste tasted like mint. The water was cool on his face.

Papa climbed into his bed slowly. The sheets were smooth and smelled like sunshine. But Papa crossed his arms. He stared at the ceiling with big, sad eyes.

"I don't want to sleep yet," Papa whispered.

Suddenly, something amazing happened. Tiny sparkles of light began dancing on his bedroom wall. They twinkled like little stars. Papa sat up quickly. His eyes grew wide with wonder.

"What is that?" Papa asked, pointing at the lights.

The sparkles began to move in patterns. They made shapes of animals and flowers. A sparkling bunny hopped across the wall. A glittery butterfly fluttered near the window.

Papa giggled with delight. "They're so pretty!"

Mama came into the room. She saw the magical lights too. "Oh my," she said with surprise. "The Dream Fairies are here!"

"Dream Fairies?" Papa asked, bouncing a little on his bed.

"Yes," Mama explained, sitting beside him. "They only come when children are ready for wonderful dreams. They bring the most amazing adventures."

Papa watched as the sparkles formed a castle. Then they made a rocket ship. The lights danced and swirled in beautiful colors. Purple, gold, and silver twinkled everywhere.

"Can I talk to them?" Papa whispered.

"Close your eyes and make a wish," Mama said softly. "Tell them what kind of dream you want."

Papa closed his eyes tight. He thought about flying with dragons. He imagined sailing on pirate ships. He dreamed of exploring magical forests with talking animals.

When Papa opened his eyes, the sparkles were forming letters. They spelled "SWEET DREAMS" in glowing letters across his wall.

Papa clapped his hands together. "This is the best bedtime ever!"

The sparkles slowly faded away. But Papa didn't feel sad. He felt excited and sleepy at the same time. His pillow felt extra fluffy. His blanket was perfectly warm.

"Will the Dream Fairies come back?" Papa asked, yawning.

"Every night when you go to bed happily," Mama said. She tucked the blanket under Papa's chin. "They love children who are ready for sleep."

Papa snuggled deep into his cozy bed. His eyelids felt heavy and peaceful. "Mama, tomorrow can I tell you about my dreams?"

"Of course, Papa," Mama said, kissing his forehead. "I can't wait to hear about your adventures."

Papa smiled as his eyes closed. He could almost see the Dream Fairies preparing magical stories just for him. Maybe he would ride a friendly dragon. Maybe he would discover a treasure chest full of toys.

"Good night, Dream Fairies," Papa whispered softly.

The room became quiet and peaceful. Papa's breathing grew slow and steady. He drifted off to sleep with a happy smile on his face.

In the morning, Papa woke up excited. He had dreamed of the most wonderful adventures. He couldn't wait to tell Mama all about them. And he couldn't wait for bedtime to come again.

From that night on, Papa never felt grumpy about bedtime. He knew the Dream Fairies were waiting to take him on amazing journeys. Bedtime became the most magical time of his whole day.

The end.
