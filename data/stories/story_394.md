# Tilly Train's Lost Treasure

In a cozy attic filled with dusty boxes and forgotten treasures, lived a cheerful little train named Tilly. Her bright red paint sparkled even in the dim light. Tilly had shiny silver wheels and a happy whistle that went "Toot-toot!"

Tilly loved her special home in the attic. Sunbeams danced through the small window. Old toys sat quietly on wooden shelves. Best of all, Tilly had her very own pretty doll named Rosie.

Rosie had curly yellow hair and a pink dress. Her tiny shoes were as blue as the sky. Tilly carried Rosie everywhere in her little cargo car.

One sunny morning, Tilly woke up excited. "Time to play with Rosie!" she said cheerfully. But when she looked in her cargo car, it was empty!

"Oh no!" cried Tilly. "Where is my pretty doll?"

Tilly's wheels began to shake with worry. Rosie was her best friend. They played tea party together. They watched the sunset through the window. Now Rosie was gone!

"I must find her," Tilly said bravely. "I won't give up!"

First, Tilly rolled to the big cardboard boxes. She pushed her nose against each one. "Rosie? Are you hiding in here?" The boxes rustled and creaked. But no Rosie appeared.

Next, Tilly chugged over to the old rocking horse. His painted mane was faded and soft. "Have you seen my doll?" asked Tilly politely. The rocking horse just smiled his painted smile.

Tilly's wheels felt tired, but she kept searching. She rolled under the dusty table. Cobwebs tickled her smokestack. "Achoo!" she sneezed. Still no Rosie.

The little train checked behind the mirror. She looked inside the toy chest. She even peeked under the quilts. Everywhere she went, she called, "Rosie! Where are you?"

As the sun moved across the sky, Tilly felt very sad. Her bright red paint seemed less shiny. Her whistle made only soft, worried sounds.

"Maybe I'll never find her," Tilly whispered.

Just then, she heard something. A tiny, muffled voice called, "Help! Help me!"

Tilly's headlight lit up with hope. "Rosie? Is that you?"

"Yes!" came the small voice. "I'm stuck!"

Tilly followed the sound carefully. It came from near the window. There, wedged between two heavy books, was Rosie! Her pink dress was wrinkled. Her curly hair was messy. But she was safe!

"Oh, Rosie!" cried Tilly joyfully. "I found you!"

"I fell off while you were sleeping," explained Rosie. "I got stuck and couldn't get out."

Tilly pushed against the books with all her might. Her wheels spun and her engine worked hard. Slowly, the books moved apart. Pop! Out came Rosie!

Tilly gently scooped up her precious doll. Rosie's blue shoes still sparkled. Her smile was as sweet as ever.

"I was so scared," said Rosie, hugging Tilly's warm side.

"I searched everywhere for you," said Tilly. "I didn't stop until I found you."

"Thank you for not giving up," whispered Rosie.

Tilly carefully placed Rosie back in the cargo car. This time, she made sure her friend was extra secure. "There! Now you're safe and sound."

As the golden sunset filled the attic, Tilly and Rosie played their favorite tea party game. The toy cups clinked softly. The pretend cookies smelled like vanilla dreams.

"I'm so happy we're together again," said Tilly.

"Me too," agreed Rosie. "You're the best friend ever."

That night, Tilly kept Rosie extra close. She had learned something important: when someone you love needs help, you never give up. You keep trying until you find a way.

The attic grew quiet and peaceful. Moonlight painted everything silver. Tilly's whistle gave one soft, happy "Toot-toot!" And both friends drifted off to sleep, dreaming of tomorrow's adventures.

From that day on, Tilly always remembered: with persistence and love, even the biggest problems can be solved.

The end.
