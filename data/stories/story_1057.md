# Story story_1057

**Benny Bear and the Dark Bathroom**

Benny Bear loved his cozy house. He loved his soft bed. He loved his warm kitchen. But there was one place Benny didn't like at all. The bathroom was very dark at night.

Every evening, Benny needed to brush his teeth. The bathroom light was broken. Mama Bear said they would fix it tomorrow. But tonight felt scary and dark.

Benny stood outside the bathroom door. His heart went thump-thump-thump. The room looked like a big black cave. He could hear the faucet drip. Drip, drip, drip.

"I can't go in there," whispered Benny. "It's too dark and scary."

Benny's teeth felt fuzzy. They needed brushing. But the dark bathroom made his paws shake. He took one tiny step inside. Then he ran right back out.

"Maybe I can brush my teeth in the kitchen," thought Benny. He got his blue toothbrush. He squeezed minty toothpaste on it. The kitchen sink was very tall. Benny stood on his tippy-toes. He could barely reach the faucet.

Splash! Water went everywhere. It dripped down his furry face. It soaked his pajamas. Toothpaste foam fell on the floor. What a mess!

"This isn't working," sighed Benny. He felt cold and wet. His teeth still weren't clean. The kitchen sink was just too big for a little bear.

Benny sat on the floor. He felt sad and frustrated. His teeth really needed brushing. But the dark bathroom still scared him. What could he do?

Then Benny had a bright idea. "Maybe I need some light!" he said. He remembered his special flashlight. It was red and shiny. Papa Bear gave it to him for camping trips.

Benny ran to his bedroom. He found the flashlight under his pillow. He pressed the button. Click! A warm yellow light filled his paw. The light felt friendly and safe.

"Now I can try again," said brave Benny. He walked slowly to the bathroom. This time, he held his flashlight high. The yellow light danced on the walls. It made everything look normal and nice.

The bathroom didn't look scary anymore. Benny could see his sink. He could see his mirror. He could see his favorite towel with fish on it. Everything looked the same as always.

Benny set his flashlight on the counter. The light made a cozy glow. He picked up his toothbrush. He squeezed fresh toothpaste on it. The toothpaste smelled like strawberries.

Brush, brush, brush went Benny's toothbrush. Up and down, back and forth. His teeth felt clean and smooth. The minty taste made his mouth feel fresh and happy.

"I did it!" cheered Benny. "I brushed my teeth all by myself!" He felt proud and brave. The dark bathroom wasn't scary with his helpful flashlight.

Benny rinsed his mouth with cool water. He wiped his face with his soft towel. He smiled at himself in the mirror. His teeth sparkled white and clean.

From that night on, Benny kept his flashlight in the bathroom. When it was dark, he always had his bright helper. The yellow light made everything feel safe and warm.

A few days later, Papa Bear fixed the bathroom light. But Benny still liked using his flashlight sometimes. It reminded him of being brave and smart.

"You solved your problem all by yourself," said Mama Bear. She gave Benny a big, warm hug. "You didn't give up when things felt hard."

Benny smiled proudly. He learned something important. When something feels scary, you can find ways to make it better. Sometimes you just need to try a different idea.

Now Benny brushes his teeth every night. He's not scared of the dark bathroom anymore. His flashlight helped him be brave. And his teeth are always clean and healthy.

Benny felt happy and proud. He solved his problem by thinking and trying again. That made him feel like the bravest little bear in the whole wide world.

The end.
