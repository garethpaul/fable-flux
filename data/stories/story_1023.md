# Story story_1023

**The Bouncy Ball and Baby Emma**

In a sunny backyard, a bright red ball sat all alone. The ball was round and shiny. It loved to bounce up and down. But today, the ball felt sad.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," said the ball. It rolled around the soft green grass. The ball bounced against the wooden fence. *Thump, thump, thump.* But no one came to play.

The warm sun made the ball feel sleepy. Birds chirped in the tall oak tree. A gentle breeze made the flowers dance. Still, the ball felt lonely.

Suddenly, the back door opened with a *creak*. Out crawled Baby Emma! She had curly brown hair and bright blue eyes. Emma wore a yellow dress with tiny daisies on it.

"Ba-ba-ba!" said Baby Emma happily. She saw the red ball right away. Her eyes grew big and round with wonder.

The ball felt excited. "Maybe she wants to play!" thought the ball hopefully.

Baby Emma crawled closer on her hands and knees. The soft grass tickled her little fingers. She reached out toward the shiny red ball.

But when Emma touched the ball, something unexpected happened. The ball rolled away quickly! Emma's face looked confused and sad.

"Oh no!" thought the ball. "She thinks I don't want to play!" The ball felt terrible. It wanted to be Emma's friend so badly.

Emma tried again. She crawled after the ball with determination. Her little hands patted the ground as she moved. But again, the ball rolled away when she touched it.

Emma sat down and made a sad little sound. "Mmm-mmm," she whimpered. A tiny tear rolled down her rosy cheek.

The ball's heart felt heavy. "I need to show her I want to play," thought the ball. "But how?"

Then the ball had a wonderful idea! Instead of rolling away, it would roll toward Emma. The ball took a deep breath and rolled slowly to Emma's feet.

Emma looked surprised. She clapped her tiny hands together. *Clap, clap, clap!* The sound made the ball feel warm and happy inside.

Very gently, Emma picked up the ball. She held it close to her chest. The ball felt Emma's heartbeat. *Thump-thump, thump-thump.* It was the most beautiful sound ever.

"Ball!" said Emma proudly. It was one of her first words! The ball felt so special and loved.

Emma threw the ball softly onto the grass. The ball bounced once, twice, three times. Then it rolled back to Emma's feet. Emma giggled with pure joy.

"Again! Again!" Emma seemed to say with her happy sounds. She clapped and bounced up and down while sitting.

They played their new game over and over. Emma would gently toss the ball. The ball would bounce and roll back to her. Each time, Emma laughed louder and louder.

The afternoon sun felt warm on their faces. Butterflies danced around the colorful flowers. Everything smelled like sweet grass and sunshine.

Emma's mommy came outside to watch. "Look at you playing so nicely!" she said with a big smile. "You found a special friend."

As the day grew later, Emma yawned. She was getting sleepy from all the fun. She picked up her red ball friend one more time.

Emma gave the ball the biggest, gentlest hug. The ball felt wrapped in love and warmth. "Best friends forever," thought the ball happily.

Emma's mommy carried her inside for naptime. Emma waved bye-bye to her ball. "Ba-ba, ball!" she said sweetly.

The ball settled contentedly on the soft grass. It wasn't lonely anymore. Tomorrow would bring another day of wonderful play with Emma.

The ball watched the twinkling stars appear in the purple sky. It felt grateful for finding such a perfect friend. Sometimes the best friendships happen when you least expect them.

"Thank you for choosing me," whispered the ball to the gentle evening breeze. And somewhere inside the house, Baby Emma dreamed of bouncing and playing with her very best ball friend.

The end.
