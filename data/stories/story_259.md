# Grandma's Storm Day

Grandma Rose lived on a cozy farm. She had fluffy white chickens. She had a big red barn. She had a garden full of flowers.

Every morning, Grandma Rose fed her animals. The chickens said "cluck, cluck, cluck!" The cows said "moo!" The little pig said "oink, oink!"

One gray morning, Grandma Rose looked up. Dark clouds covered the sky. The wind made the trees dance. "Oh my," said Grandma Rose. "A big storm is coming!"

Grandma Rose felt worried in her tummy. What about her chickens? What about her flowers? What about her vegetables?

"I must keep everyone safe," she said.

First, Grandma Rose went to her chickens. "Come here, little ones," she called. The chickens were scared too. They flapped their wings. "Bawk, bawk, bawk!"

Grandma Rose opened the chicken coop door. "In you go," she said gently. One by one, the chickens went inside. Safe and warm!

Next, Grandma Rose looked at her flower garden. Her pretty sunflowers were so tall. The wind might knock them down!

Grandma Rose got her wooden stakes. She got her soft rope. Carefully, she tied each sunflower. "There you go, my beauties," she whispered.

Then Grandma Rose saw her tomato plants. The little green tomatoes might fall off! What could she do?

Grandma Rose thought and thought. Then she smiled! She got her big laundry baskets. She picked all the tomatoes. Green ones, red ones, yellow ones too!

"You'll be safe in my kitchen," she told them.

The wind blew harder now. "Whoooosh!" it said. Rain started to fall. "Pitter-patter, pitter-patter!"

But Grandma Rose wasn't finished yet. She remembered her cow, Bessie. And her pig, Wilbur.

"Come on, Bessie!" called Grandma Rose. The big brown cow walked slowly to the barn. Her hooves went "clip-clop, clip-clop" on the ground.

"Your turn, Wilbur!" said Grandma Rose. The little pink pig trotted to his cozy pen. His curly tail wiggled with happiness.

Now the rain fell harder. Lightning flashed across the sky. "Crack!" went the thunder. But Grandma Rose felt better now.

All her animals were safe and dry. Her flowers were protected. Her vegetables were saved.

Grandma Rose went inside her warm house. She made hot cocoa with marshmallows. She sat by her window and watched the storm.

The rain drummed on her roof. "Tap, tap, tap!" The wind sang through the trees. But everything was peaceful inside.

"I was so worried," Grandma Rose said to herself. "But I didn't give up. I found a way to help everyone."

She looked at her green tomatoes on the counter. She looked at her chickens through the window. They were all safe in their coop.

The storm lasted all day. But Grandma Rose felt proud. She had worked hard. She had been brave. She had taken care of everyone.

When the sun came out again, something wonderful happened. Grandma Rose walked outside. Everything sparkled with raindrops! Her flowers stood tall and strong. Her vegetables were growing bigger.

"Hello, my friends!" she called to her animals. The chickens came running. "Cluck, cluck!" they said happily. Bessie the cow mooed a thank-you. Wilbur the pig oinked with joy.

That evening, Grandma Rose sat on her porch. She watched the sunset paint the sky pink and orange. A little bird landed near her feet.

"You know what?" Grandma Rose said to the bird. "I learned something today. When I feel worried, I can do something about it. I can make a plan. I can work hard. I can keep everyone safe."

The bird chirped a sweet song. It sounded like "thank you!"

From that day on, when storm clouds came, Grandma Rose didn't feel scared. She felt ready. She knew she could take care of her farm family.

And they all lived happily ever after.

**The End**

*Remember: When you feel worried, you can make a plan and work hard to solve your problems, just like Grandma Rose!*
