# Anna's Magical Bath Time

Anna heard Mama calling from upstairs. "Bath time, sweetie!" Mama's voice was warm and gentle. But Anna didn't want to stop playing. Her toy blocks were stacked up high. Her stuffed bunny sat nearby, watching. Anna sighed and looked at her toys.

"Do I have to?" Anna asked softly. Bath time meant leaving her fun behind. It meant getting wet and soapy. It meant the day was almost over. Anna's shoulders drooped as she walked upstairs.

The bathroom felt big and echoey. Steam rose from the warm water. Mama had filled the tub perfectly. Little bubbles floated on top like tiny clouds. But Anna still felt grumpy about bath time.

"I don't want to get in," Anna said. She crossed her arms tight. Her bottom lip stuck out in a pout. The water looked warm and inviting. But Anna wanted to keep playing instead.

Mama smiled and sat on the edge. "What if we made it special tonight?" she asked. Mama reached into the cabinet. She pulled out a small bottle. It sparkled purple and blue in the light.

"What's that?" Anna asked curiously. Her grumpiness started to fade away. The bottle looked mysterious and exciting. Mama unscrewed the shiny silver cap.

"Magic bubble bath," Mama whispered secretly. "But it only works for brave girls." She poured three drops into the water. The drops shimmered as they fell. They made tiny rainbow circles on top.

Suddenly, the water began to glow! Soft pink and purple lights danced. The bubbles grew bigger and fluffier. They looked like cotton candy clouds. Anna's eyes grew wide with wonder.

"Wow!" Anna gasped with delight. She forgot all about being reluctant. The water looked like a fairy tale. Anna quickly took off her clothes. She stepped carefully into the magical tub.

The water felt perfectly warm and silky. The bubbles tickled her toes gently. They popped with tiny musical sounds. Pink sparkles floated around Anna's hands. She giggled as they tickled her fingers.

"Look, Mama!" Anna scooped up bubbles. They formed a tall, wobbly tower. The tower shimmered with rainbow colors. Anna blew gently on the bubbles. They floated up like tiny balloons.

Mama handed Anna a special washcloth. It was soft and fluffy yellow. "This helps the magic work better," Mama explained with a wink. Anna rubbed the washcloth on her arms. Glittery soap suds appeared everywhere.

Anna pretended to be a mermaid princess. She swished her hair in the water. The bubbles made a beautiful crown. She sang a happy little song. Her voice echoed prettily in the bathroom.

"I'm the bubble queen!" Anna announced proudly. She scooped up handfuls of sparkly foam. The bubbles felt like silk between her fingers. They smelled like sweet vanilla and lavender.

Mama helped wash Anna's hair gently. The shampoo made even more bubbles. They piled up like whipped cream. Anna shaped them into funny hats. She made bubble beards and mustaches.

"This is the best bath ever!" Anna laughed loudly. She splashed carefully in the magical water. Rainbow droplets flew through the air. They caught the light like tiny diamonds.

When bath time was almost over, Anna felt sad. She didn't want the magic to end. The glowing water was still warm. The bubbles were still dancing prettily.

"Can we do this again tomorrow?" Anna asked hopefully. Her eyes sparkled with excitement. She loved how special bath time felt. It was like having her own fairy tale.

"Magic bubble bath is for every night," Mama promised sweetly. She wrapped Anna in a fluffy towel. The towel felt warm and cozy. Anna snuggled into Mama's loving arms.

As Anna got ready for bed, she smiled. Tomorrow seemed very far away. But she knew something wonderful waited. Bath time wasn't boring anymore. It was magical and special and fun.

Anna drifted off to sleep peacefully. She dreamed of rainbow bubbles dancing. In her dreams, she was always the bubble queen. And bath time was always an adventure.

The end.
