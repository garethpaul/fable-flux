# The Best Day at the Swing Set

Lily sat on the big red swing. She pushed herself back and forth slowly. The playground was very quiet today. All her friends were busy with their families.

"I wish someone would play with me," Lily sighed. She looked around the empty playground. The slide was lonely too. The seesaw sat still and sad.

Suddenly, she heard a cheerful sound. "Choo-choo! Choo-choo!" A bright blue toy train rolled up to the swing set. The little train had shiny wheels and a happy face painted on front.

"Hello there!" called the train. "I'm Thomas the Train. You look sad. What's wrong?"

Lily stopped swinging. "Hi, I'm Lily. I'm lonely today. No one is here to play with me."

Thomas rolled closer. His wheels sparkled in the sunshine. "I'm lonely too! I've been rolling around the park all by myself. Can I play with you?"

Lily smiled for the first time today. "Yes! But how can a train use a swing?"

Thomas thought for a moment. His little bell jingled. "Maybe we can find a way together!"

Lily jumped off her swing. She looked at Thomas carefully. "You have a long rope on your back. What's that for?"

"That's for pulling train cars," Thomas explained. "But I don't have any cars today."

Lily's eyes lit up. "I have an idea! Can you pull me while I swing?"

Thomas wiggled with excitement. "That sounds fun! Let's try it!"

Lily tied Thomas's rope to her swing. She sat down and held on tight. "Ready, Thomas?"

"All aboard!" Thomas called out. He began to roll forward slowly. The swing moved in a big, gentle circle around the swing set.

"Wheee!" Lily laughed. "This is amazing! I feel like I'm flying!"

Thomas beeped his horn happily. "This is the best game ever! I love helping you fly!"

They played their new game for a long time. Thomas pulled Lily in big circles. Then they tried small circles. Sometimes Thomas went fast. Sometimes he went slow.

"Let's try something else," Lily said. She untied the rope and got off the swing. "Can you teach me about trains?"

Thomas's eyes sparkled. "Oh yes! I know all about trains! We carry people and things. We go fast on tracks. We say choo-choo and ring our bells!"

Lily clapped her hands. "That sounds wonderful! Can I be a train too?"

"Of course!" Thomas said. "Hold onto my rope. You can be my train car!"

Lily held the rope behind Thomas. They rolled around the playground together. "Choo-choo!" Lily called out. "Ding-ding!" She pretended to ring a bell.

"You're a great train car!" Thomas laughed. "Now I'm not pulling an empty rope. I'm pulling my best friend!"

They chugged around the slide. They rolled past the seesaw. They even went around the monkey bars. Other children began to notice their fun game.

"Look at that!" said a little boy. "A girl and a train playing together!"

"Can we play too?" asked a little girl.

Lily and Thomas looked at each other. "The more friends, the more fun!" they said together.

Soon, five children were holding Thomas's rope. They made the longest train ever! Thomas pulled them all around the playground. Everyone was laughing and making train sounds.

"Choo-choo-choo!" they all called out together.

When it was time to rest, everyone sat in a circle. Thomas was in the middle, beaming with joy.

"Thank you, Thomas," Lily said. "You turned my lonely day into the best day ever!"

"Thank you, Lily," Thomas replied. "You showed me that playing together is much better than playing alone."

The other children agreed. "Cooperation makes everything more fun!" they cheered.

As the sun began to set, Lily gave Thomas a gentle hug. "Will you come back tomorrow?"

"Every day!" Thomas promised. "Best friends always come back!"

Lily waved goodbye as Thomas rolled away. She couldn't wait to see her new friend again. The swing set didn't look lonely anymore. It looked ready for another day of friendship and fun.

From that day on, Lily and Thomas played together every afternoon. They learned that the best adventures happen when friends work together!

The end.
