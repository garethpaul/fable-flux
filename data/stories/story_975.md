# Story story_975

Teddy sat in the corner of the big playroom. His soft brown fur felt warm in the sunny spot. The room smelled like crayons and playdough. But Teddy felt sad and lonely.

He had been playing by himself all morning. His toy cars sat in a neat line. His building blocks made a tall tower. Everything looked perfect, but something was missing.

Teddy's button eyes looked around the empty room. He wished he had a friend to play with. He hugged his fuzzy arms around his round belly. Maybe no one wanted to play with a teddy bear.

Just then, the playroom door creaked open. A little girl with curly hair peeked inside. Her name was Anna. She had bright blue eyes and wore a yellow dress with tiny flowers.

Anna saw Teddy sitting alone. She stepped into the room quietly. Her shoes made soft tapping sounds on the wooden floor.

"Hello," Anna said in a gentle voice. "Are you playing by yourself?"

Teddy nodded his furry head. He felt too shy to speak at first.

Anna walked closer. She sat down on the soft rug next to Teddy. The rug felt bumpy under her legs.

"I'm Anna," she said with a warm smile. "What's your name?"

"I'm Teddy," he said in a small voice. "I don't have anyone to play with."

Anna's face lit up like sunshine. "I would love to play with you! What games do you like?"

Teddy's ears perked up. His sad feeling started to fade away. "I like building things," he said. "And racing cars too."

"Those sound like fun games!" Anna clapped her hands together. The sound echoed in the playroom.

Anna picked up some colorful blocks. They felt smooth and cool in her hands. "Let's build a castle together," she suggested.

Teddy's eyes sparkled with excitement. He grabbed some blocks too. His paws felt clumsy at first, but Anna helped him.

They stacked red blocks and blue blocks. They added yellow ones and green ones. The castle grew taller and taller. It had towers and walls and a big front door.

"This is the best castle ever!" Teddy said happily.

Anna giggled. The sound was like tiny bells ringing. "Now let's make the cars race around it!"

They pushed the little cars along the floor. The cars made zoom-zoom sounds as they drove. Anna made her red car beep its horn. Teddy made his blue car do loops around the castle.

The two friends played for hours. They built bridges with blocks. They had tea parties with tiny cups. They drew pictures with chunky crayons that smelled waxy and new.

Anna drew a picture of her and Teddy playing together. She used every color in the crayon box. Teddy drew wobbly hearts all around the edges.

"You're my best friend," Anna told Teddy. She gave him a gentle hug. His fur felt soft against her cheek.

"You're my best friend too," Teddy said. His voice was warm and happy now. The lonely feeling was completely gone.

As the afternoon sun streamed through the window, they sat together on the rug. Anna brushed Teddy's fur with a tiny brush. It felt nice and tickly.

"Will you play with me again tomorrow?" Teddy asked hopefully.

"Every day!" Anna promised. "We can build new things and have new adventures."

Teddy's heart felt full of joy. He had found the best friend in the whole world. Anna felt happy too. She loved having a special teddy bear friend.

They cleaned up their toys together. Anna put the blocks in their box. Teddy lined up the cars on the shelf. Everything had its place.

When it was time to go, Anna gave Teddy one more big hug. "See you tomorrow, best friend," she whispered.

Teddy waved goodbye with his fuzzy paw. The playroom didn't feel empty anymore. It felt full of friendship and love.

From that day on, Teddy and Anna played together every single day. They shared toys and stories and lots of giggles. The playroom became their special place where friendship grew stronger each day.

The end.
