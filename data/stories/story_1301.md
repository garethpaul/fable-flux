# Story story_1301

Bessie the cow looked at the bright morning sun. Today was park day! All the farm animals were going to Meadow Park.

"Come on, Bessie!" called Farmer Mae. "Time for park time!"

But Bessie felt worried. She had never been to a park before. What if the other animals were faster? What if she couldn't play the games?

Bessie's hooves felt heavy as she walked. Her tummy had butterflies dancing inside. The grass felt soft under her feet, but she barely noticed.

"I don't want to go," Bessie whispered to her friend Penny the pig.

Penny's pink nose wiggled. "Parks are fun, Bessie. You'll see!"

At Meadow Park, colorful flowers bloomed everywhere. The air smelled sweet like honey. Birds sang happy songs in the tall oak trees.

Rabbit was already bouncing on the seesaw. Sheep was rolling down the grassy hill. Duck splashed in the sparkly pond.

Bessie stood by the fence. She watched everyone play. Her ears drooped down sadly.

"Why aren't you playing?" asked a gentle voice.

Bessie turned around. A beautiful butterfly sat on a daisy. The butterfly's wings shimmered like rainbows.

"I'm scared," Bessie said softly. "Everyone is so good at playing."

The butterfly fluttered closer. "What makes you special, Bessie?"

Bessie thought hard. "I'm big and strong. I can moo really loud."

"Show me!" said the butterfly with a twinkle.

Bessie took a deep breath. "MOOOOO!" she called out.

Her moo echoed across the park. All the animals stopped playing. They looked around with big smiles.

"Bessie!" called Rabbit. "We need you!"

Sheep ran over, fluffy and excited. "We're playing hide and seek. Your loud moo can call everyone when time is up!"

Bessie's eyes grew wide. "Really? I can help?"

Duck waddled up with wet feathers. "And we need someone tall to get the ball from the tree!"

Bessie looked up. A red ball was stuck in the branches. She stretched her long neck up, up, up. Her tongue reached out and pushed the ball free!

"Hooray!" cheered all the animals.

The butterfly winked and flew away on a warm breeze.

Soon Bessie was having the best time ever. She counted to ten for hide and seek. Her loud moo made everyone giggle and come running.

When Pig got stuck in the mud, Bessie used her strong legs to pull him out. When Chicken's chicks got scared of the slide, Bessie's gentle moo calmed them down.

"You're the best park helper!" said Rabbit, hopping in circles.

Bessie felt warm and happy inside. The sun felt perfect on her black and white spots. The grass tickled her hooves in a good way.

They played tag around the big trees. Bessie's hooves made happy clip-clop sounds. They had races across the soft meadow. Bessie cheered for everyone who ran.

For lunch, they shared apples and carrots. The food tasted extra yummy in the fresh air. Bessie made new friends with the park squirrels.

As the sun began to set, Farmer Mae called out, "Time to go home!"

All the animals groaned. "Already?"

Bessie couldn't believe it. The day had gone by so fast! Her heart felt full of joy.

Walking home, Penny smiled at Bessie. "Did you have fun?"

"The best fun ever!" Bessie said, skipping a little. "I was scared at first. But everyone needed my help!"

That night, Bessie dreamed of rainbow butterflies and happy games. She thought about her new friends and all the fun they had.

When morning came, Bessie jumped up quickly. The sun felt warm and welcoming.

"Is it park day again?" she asked hopefully.

Farmer Mae laughed. "Tomorrow, Bessie. Park day is tomorrow!"

Bessie could hardly wait. She spent the whole day practicing her loudest moo. Tomorrow would be another magical day at Meadow Park with all her friends.

Being different made her special. Being helpful made her happy. And being brave made everything possible.

The end.
