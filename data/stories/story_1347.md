# Story story_1347

Papa sat on the green grass. He rolled his red ball back and forth. The sun felt warm on his face. But Papa felt sad inside.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," Papa said softly. He looked around the big park. Other children played with their friends. Papa played alone.

The red ball rolled away from Papa. It bumped into something gray and big. Papa looked up. A gentle elephant stood there!

"Oh!" said Papa. "That's my ball."

The elephant had kind brown eyes. She picked up the ball with her long trunk. She held it out to Papa.

"Thank you," Papa said. He took the ball carefully.

The elephant made a soft trumpet sound. She pointed to the ball with her trunk. Then she pointed to herself.

"Do you want to play?" Papa asked.

The elephant nodded her big head up and down.

Papa's heart felt happy. "Yes! Let's play together!"

Papa rolled the red ball to the elephant. She caught it with her trunk. The elephant was very gentle. She rolled the ball back to Papa.

"You're good at this!" Papa laughed.

They rolled the ball back and forth. The elephant trumpeted happily. Papa clapped his hands. The soft grass tickled their feet.

"What else can we play?" Papa wondered.

The elephant pointed to the tall slide. Papa looked up at it. It was very high.

"I'm scared of the big slide," Papa said quietly.

The elephant walked to the slide. She put her trunk on the ladder. She looked at Papa with encouraging eyes.

"Will you help me?" Papa asked.

The elephant nodded. She stood next to the ladder. Papa climbed up slowly. The elephant's trunk stayed near him. Papa felt safe.

At the top, Papa felt proud. "I did it!" he called down.

The elephant trumpeted a happy sound. Papa slid down the smooth slide. The wind felt cool on his face.

"That was fun!" Papa said. "Thank you for helping me."

Next, they played hide and seek. The elephant was too big to hide well. Her gray body stuck out from behind trees. Papa giggled when he found her.

When Papa hid, the elephant pretended not to see him. She looked left and right. Papa giggled behind a small bush.

"Here I am!" Papa jumped out.

The elephant acted surprised. She trumpeted with joy.

They played on the swings together. Papa sat on a small swing. The elephant pushed him gently with her trunk. Papa flew high in the air.

"Higher! Higher!" Papa laughed.

The sweet smell of flowers filled the air. Birds sang pretty songs in the trees. Papa felt so happy.

After swinging, they sat under a shady tree. Papa shared his apple with the elephant. She ate it with her trunk. The apple crunched loudly.

"You're the best friend ever," Papa told the elephant.

The elephant curled her trunk around Papa gently. It felt like a warm hug.

"Will you play with me tomorrow?" Papa asked.

The elephant nodded yes. She made happy trumpet sounds.

Papa's mom called from across the park. "Time to go home, Papa!"

Papa felt sad to leave. But he felt happy too. He had made a wonderful friend.

"Goodbye, Elephant," Papa said. "See you tomorrow!"

The elephant waved her trunk. She trumpeted a goodbye song.

Papa ran to his mom. His face glowed with happiness.

"I made a new friend today!" Papa told her.

"That's wonderful," Mom said. "Tell me all about it."

As they walked home, Papa talked about his elephant friend. He couldn't wait for tomorrow. He would bring more apples to share.

The park would never feel lonely again. Papa had learned something special. Friends can be found in the most surprising places.

The elephant watched them leave. She felt happy too. Tomorrow would bring more fun and friendship.

The sun set over the park. It painted the sky pink and orange. It had been a perfect day for making friends.

The end.
