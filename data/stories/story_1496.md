# Story story_1496

**Maya's Magic Lunch**

The school bell rang loud and clear. "Time for lunch!" called Mrs. Chen. All the children jumped up from their desks. They grabbed their colorful lunch boxes. Everyone was excited except Maya.

Maya walked slowly to the lunch table. Her new lunch box felt heavy in her hands. She sat down next to her friend Sam. Sam had a big smile on his face.

"What did you bring today?" asked Sam. He opened his lunch box with a happy pop. Inside was a peanut butter sandwich cut into triangles. He had apple slices that smelled sweet and fresh.

Maya looked at her own lunch box. She didn't want to open it. "I don't know if I like my lunch," she said quietly. Her mom had packed something new today. Maya felt worried in her tummy.

"Maybe it's something yummy!" said Sam. His eyes sparkled with excitement. "Let's open it together!"

Maya slowly lifted the lid. Inside was a sandwich she had never seen before. It had green things sticking out. There were orange carrots cut like flowers. A small container held something purple and bumpy.

"Wow!" said Sam. "Your lunch looks like a rainbow!"

Maya picked up the sandwich. It smelled like the garden behind her house. The bread was soft and warm. "My mom said it has avocado," Maya whispered. "I've never tried it."

Sam took a bite of his apple. "Want to try mine first?" he asked. "Then you can try yours!"

Maya smiled a little. She took a small bite of Sam's apple. It was crispy and sweet. The juice ran down her chin. "That's good!" she said.

Now Maya felt a little braver. She took a tiny bite of her sandwich. The avocado was smooth and creamy. It tasted like butter made from plants. "It's not scary at all!" she said with surprise.

Something magical started to happen. The more Maya ate, the more excited she felt. The carrots crunched between her teeth. They tasted sweet and earthy. The purple things were grapes that popped in her mouth.

"This is like eating a garden!" Maya laughed. She took another big bite. The flavors danced on her tongue.

Sam giggled too. "Want to trade a carrot for an apple slice?" he asked.

"Yes!" said Maya. She felt warm and happy inside. Sharing food with her friend made everything taste even better.

Other children came to sit with them. Emma had cheese crackers that looked like stars. Jake brought soup that steamed in the cool air. The smell made everyone hungry.

"Let's all share a little bit!" suggested Maya. She felt proud of her colorful lunch now. The children passed around small bites. They tasted new foods together.

Maya tried Emma's star crackers. They were salty and made her smile. Jake let her sip his warm soup. It tasted like home and hugs.

The lunch table became full of laughter. Children pointed at their favorite foods. They made happy sounds when they found something delicious. Maya felt like she was at a wonderful party.

When lunch time ended, Maya had eaten everything in her box. Her tummy felt full and satisfied. She had tried five new foods today!

"That was the best lunch ever!" said Maya. She hugged Sam tight. "Thank you for helping me be brave."

"Friends help friends try new things," said Sam. He hugged her back.

Maya packed up her empty lunch box. She couldn't wait to tell her mom about the avocado. Maybe tomorrow she would try something else new.

As they walked back to class, Maya felt excited. "I wonder what lunch will be tomorrow!" she said to Sam.

"Whatever it is, we'll try it together!" Sam replied.

Maya skipped all the way back to her desk. Lunch time was magical when you had good friends to share it with.

The end.
