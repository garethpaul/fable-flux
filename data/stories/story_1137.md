# Story story_1137

**The Drum and the Truck**

In a cozy bedroom sat a shiny red drum. The drum had silver rims that sparkled. Every day, the drum played happy beats. *Boom, boom, tap!* went the drumsticks.

But the drum felt lonely. No one played with him. The drum sighed a quiet *whoosh* sound.

Across the room lived a bright yellow truck. The truck had big black wheels. His horn went *beep, beep!* The truck loved to roll around the soft carpet.

The truck heard the drum's pretty music. *Boom, boom, tap!* The beats made the truck feel happy inside. The truck wanted to dance!

"Can I play with you?" asked the truck. His voice was gentle and kind.

The drum stopped playing. "You want to play with me?" The drum sounded surprised.

"Yes!" said the truck. "Your music makes me want to move!"

The truck rolled closer. His wheels made a soft *whoosh* on the carpet. The drum felt excited. Maybe he wouldn't be lonely anymore!

"How can we play together?" asked the drum.

The truck thought hard. His engine made a quiet *purr* sound when he thought.

"I can drive in circles!" said the truck. "You can play beats for me!"

The drum felt happy. His surface gleamed in the sunlight. "Let's try!"

The drum started playing. *Boom, boom, tap!* The beats were slow and steady.

The truck began to roll. Around and around he went. His wheels turned smoothly on the soft rug.

"This is fun!" laughed the truck. His horn beeped with joy.

"I love having a friend!" said the drum. His beats got faster. *Boom-tap, boom-tap!*

The truck spun in happy circles. His yellow paint shone brightly. The room filled with music and laughter.

But then the truck got dizzy. He wobbled left and right. "Oh no!" he said.

The truck bumped into the toy box. *Crash!* Some blocks fell out.

The drum stopped playing. "Are you okay?" he asked with worry.

The truck felt sad. "I'm sorry. I made a mess."

"It's okay," said the drum kindly. "We can clean up together."

The truck felt better. Friends help each other, he thought.

They worked as a team. The truck pushed blocks with his bumper. The drum rolled them with his sticks. Soon the toys were back in the box.

"We make a good team," said the drum.

"The best team!" agreed the truck.

They started playing again. This time, the truck drove more slowly. The drum played gentle, easy beats. *Tap, tap, boom!*

The truck drove in figure-eights. He drove in squares and triangles. The drum's music guided him perfectly.

"You're getting really good at this!" said the drum.

The truck beamed with pride. "You're the best music teacher!"

As the sun set, the room grew cozy and warm. Golden light streamed through the window.

"Thank you for being my friend," said the drum.

"Thank you for sharing your music," said the truck.

They decided to have a concert. The drum played his favorite song. *Boom-tap-tap, boom-tap-tap!* The truck danced beautifully to the rhythm.

Other toys gathered to watch. The teddy bear clapped his paws. The doll swayed to the music. Everyone loved their performance!

"Hooray!" cheered all the toys.

The drum and truck felt so happy. They had found something special - friendship.

From that day on, they played together every day. The drum was never lonely again. The truck had a best friend who made beautiful music.

Sometimes they played fast songs. Sometimes they played slow songs. But they always played together.

At bedtime, they sat side by side. The drum hummed soft lullabies. The truck's engine purred quietly. They dreamed of tomorrow's adventures.

The bedroom was full of friendship and joy. The drum and truck had learned something wonderful. Playing together is much more fun than playing alone.

And they lived happily ever after, making music and memories together.

*The End*
