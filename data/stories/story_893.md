# Story story_893

**Turtle's Art Adventure**

The morning sun peeked through the classroom windows. Miss Robin clapped her hands together. "Art time!" she called cheerfully.

All the little animals hurried to the art table. Bunny hopped over quickly. Fox trotted with his bushy tail swishing. Even sleepy Bear waddled over with a smile.

But Turtle moved very, very slowly. His green shell felt heavy today. Art time made his tummy feel funny.

"I'm not good at art," Turtle whispered to himself. He watched his friends grab paintbrushes and crayons. Their eyes sparkled with excitement.

Turtle finally reached the art table. Miss Robin had set out bright paints in red, blue, and yellow. Soft brushes lay next to white paper. The paints smelled sweet like flowers.

"Today we're painting our favorite things," Miss Robin said gently. She noticed Turtle's worried face. "What makes you happy, Turtle?"

Turtle thought hard. "I like the pond," he said quietly. "The water feels nice and cool."

"That sounds wonderful to paint," Miss Robin smiled. She handed Turtle a brush. "Try dipping it in the blue paint."

Turtle dipped his brush slowly. A big blue drop fell onto his paper. "Oh no!" he said. "I made a mess."

Fox looked over. "That looks like water to me!" he said kindly.

Turtle looked at the blue drop again. Maybe it did look like water. He dipped his brush once more. This time he made a wavy line. Then another wavy line.

Something magical began to happen. The blue paint flowed like real water on his paper. Turtle's eyes grew wide with wonder.

"I can make waves!" Turtle said, his voice getting excited. He painted more blue lines. The brush felt good in his flipper.

Miss Robin brought over green paint. "What else is at your pond?" she asked.

"Lily pads!" Turtle said. He dipped his brush in green. Slowly, he painted round shapes on his blue water. Each lily pad looked different. Some were big. Some were small.

Bunny hopped over to see. "Your pond looks so peaceful," she said. "I want to swim there!"

Turtle felt warm and happy inside. He added yellow paint for the sun. The yellow mixed with blue and made light green. "Look!" he called out. "New colors!"

Bear shuffled over too. "Wow, Turtle! You're really good at this."

Turtle's shell didn't feel heavy anymore. He painted tiny fish swimming in his pond. He added brown cattails growing tall. He even painted himself sitting on a lily pad.

The brush danced in his flipper. Paint squished between his toes. Everything smelled like creativity and joy.

"Time to clean up," Miss Robin announced.

"Already?" Turtle asked. He looked at his painting. The pond seemed to shimmer and shine. He had made something beautiful all by himself.

Turtle carefully carried his artwork to the drying rack. His painting would hang there until home time.

"I love your pond," said Miss Robin. "You painted it with such care."

Turtle beamed with pride. His chest felt warm and full. "I painted how the pond feels," he said. "Cool and quiet and safe."

Fox patted Turtle's shell. "You're a real artist!"

As the friends washed their brushes, Turtle helped clean the paint pots. The water swirled with rainbow colors. It reminded him of his pond painting.

"Miss Robin," Turtle said as they finished cleaning. "What will we paint tomorrow?"

Miss Robin's eyes twinkled. "What would you like to paint?"

Turtle thought carefully. "Maybe my family? Or the flowers by the pond? Or the clouds that look like ice cream?"

"Those all sound wonderful," Miss Robin said. "I can't wait to see what you create."

That afternoon, Turtle carried his pond painting home proudly. His mom hung it on the refrigerator right away.

"It's beautiful," she said, giving him a gentle hug.

Turtle smiled big. Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough. He had so many more pictures living inside his imagination, just waiting to come alive with paint and brushes.

Art time was going to be the best part of every day.

The end.
