# Story story_1406

**Melody's Music Time**

Every morning at Sunny Meadow Farm, the animals heard a special bell. *Ding-a-ling-ling!* It was music time!

The cows mooed with excitement. The pigs oinked happy songs. The chickens clucked cheerful tunes. But Melody the sheep felt different.

"I don't want to sing today," Melody whispered. She hid behind the big oak tree. Her fluffy white wool felt heavy with worry.

Farmer Sue walked into the meadow. She carried her shiny guitar. "Come along, everyone! Time for music!"

All the animals gathered in a circle. They sat on the soft green grass. Melody stayed behind her tree. She peeked out with one brown eye.

"Where's Melody?" asked Farmer Sue. She looked around the meadow.

"She's hiding," said Bessie the cow. "She feels shy about singing."

Farmer Sue smiled gently. She walked to the oak tree. "Hello, Melody. Would you like to join us?"

Melody shook her woolly head. "I can't sing pretty like the others. My voice sounds funny."

"Every voice is special," said Farmer Sue. "Music isn't about being perfect. It's about having fun."

Melody's ears twitched. She listened to the sounds around her. Birds chirped in the trees. Wind whispered through the grass. Bees hummed near the flowers.

"Those are all music sounds," said Farmer Sue. "Nature makes beautiful music every day."

Farmer Sue began to strum her guitar. The strings made soft, golden sounds. They floated through the air like butterflies.

The animals started to sing. Each one sounded different. Bessie's moo was deep and rich. Penny Pig's oink was bouncy and fun. Henrietta Hen's cluck was quick and bright.

Melody listened carefully. Nobody sounded the same. But together, they made something wonderful.

"Maybe I could try," Melody thought. She took a tiny step forward.

Farmer Sue saw her moving. She played a gentle tune. "This song is about friendship," she said. "It goes like this: 'Friends come in every size, friends with different colored eyes.'"

The animals sang along. Their voices mixed like colors in a rainbow.

Melody felt something magical happening. The music seemed to dance around her. It tickled her ears and warmed her heart.

She took another step. Then another. Soon she stood at the edge of the circle.

"Friends who hop and friends who fly," sang the animals. "Friends who moo and friends who cry."

Melody opened her mouth. A soft "baa" came out. It was quiet but sweet.

"Beautiful!" said Farmer Sue. "Sing it again, Melody."

This time Melody sang a little louder. "Baa-aa-aa!" Her voice was gentle like morning mist.

The other animals smiled. They made room for Melody in their circle. She sat on the warm grass between Bessie and Penny Pig.

"Friends who sing and friends who play," they all sang together. "Friends who brighten every day!"

Melody's voice grew stronger. She discovered she could make different sounds. High "baas" and low "baas." Long "baas" and short "baas."

Farmer Sue taught them a clapping song. Melody's hooves made soft tapping sounds on the ground. *Pat-pat-pat!*

They sang about sunshine and rainbows. They sang about growing vegetables in the garden. They sang about friendship and kindness.

Melody felt the music flowing through her whole body. Her tail wiggled with joy. Her wool seemed to sparkle in the sunlight.

When music time ended, Melody felt different. She felt proud and happy. She felt like she belonged.

"Thank you for helping me try," Melody told Farmer Sue.

"You helped yourself," said Farmer Sue. "You were brave enough to join us."

That evening, Melody practiced her singing. She "baaed" softly to the moon and stars. The night sounds joined her - crickets chirping and owls hooting.

The next morning, Melody heard the music bell. *Ding-a-ling-ling!*

This time, she didn't hide. She ran to the circle with her friends. Her hooves danced on the grass.

"I can't wait for music time!" Melody said happily.

And from that day on, Melody's sweet voice was always part of the beautiful farm music. She learned that being different made the songs even more special.

The end.
