# Ben and Mango's Big Adventure

Ben sat on his backyard swing. The sun was warm and bright. But Ben felt sad and lonely.

"I wish I had someone to play with," Ben sighed. He pushed the swing gently back and forth.

The big oak tree rustled above him. Its green leaves danced in the breeze. Ben looked up and saw two bright brown eyes peeking through the branches.

"Hello there!" called a cheerful voice.

A small monkey swung down from the tree. She had golden fur and a long, curly tail. Her name was Mango.

"Hi," said Ben quietly. "I'm Ben."

"I'm Mango!" she chattered excitedly. "You look sad. Can I play with you?"

Ben's face lit up a little. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" Mango did a little flip. "Playing is my favorite thing!"

Ben smiled for the first time that day. "What should we play?"

Mango looked around the backyard. She spotted Ben's red ball near the flower bed.

"Let's play catch!" she suggested.

Ben picked up the ball. He threw it gently to Mango. She caught it with her tiny hands and giggled.

"Your turn!" Mango tossed the ball back.

They played catch for a while. The ball went back and forth. Back and forth. Both friends laughed and smiled.

"This is fun!" said Ben. "But I wish we could do something bigger."

Mango's eyes sparkled with an idea. "I know! Let's build a fort!"

Ben looked around the yard. There were sticks and leaves everywhere. "But how do we build one?"

"Together!" said Mango. "You're tall and I'm good at climbing. We make a perfect team!"

Ben gathered long sticks from the ground. Mango scampered up the oak tree. She picked the biggest, softest leaves she could find.

"These will make great walls!" Ben said, arranging the sticks.

"And these leaves will be our roof!" Mango called from above.

Working together felt wonderful. Ben placed each stick carefully. Mango dropped leaves down like green confetti. Soon they had built a cozy fort.

"We did it!" cheered Ben.

"Hooray!" Mango clapped her hands.

They crawled inside their fort. It was cool and shady. Sunlight peeked through the leaf roof in pretty patterns.

"This is the best fort ever," said Ben.

"It's perfect because we built it together," Mango agreed.

Ben's tummy rumbled loudly. "Oh! I'm getting hungry."

"Me too!" said Mango. "I love bananas!"

"I have bananas in my kitchen!" Ben said excitedly. "And peanut butter sandwiches!"

They shared lunch in their fort. Ben had never tasted anything so delicious. Everything was better with a friend.

After lunch, they played more games. They raced around the yard. Mango was fast, but Ben had longer legs. They were perfectly matched!

They played hide and seek. Ben hid behind the big rose bush. Mango hid high in the oak tree. Finding each other was half the fun.

As the sun began to set, they sat together on the swing. Ben pushed gently with his feet. Mango held on tight.

"This was the best day ever," said Ben.

"I agree!" said Mango. "Want to be best friends?"

"Yes!" Ben said happily. "Best friends forever!"

Mango reached into the oak tree. She picked a perfect golden leaf. "This is for you, Ben. To remember our friendship."

Ben carefully took the leaf. "Thank you, Mango. You're the best friend ever."

They hugged tight. Ben wasn't lonely anymore. He had found something wonderful - a true friend.

"Can you come back tomorrow?" asked Ben hopefully.

"Wild horses couldn't keep me away!" Mango giggled.

Ben laughed at her silly expression. "See you tomorrow, best friend!"

Mango swung back up to her tree. She waved goodbye with her curly tail.

Ben walked inside, holding his special leaf. His heart felt warm and full of joy.

Playing alone was okay. But playing with a friend was magical. Together, they could build anything and have amazing adventures.

Ben couldn't wait for tomorrow.

The end.
