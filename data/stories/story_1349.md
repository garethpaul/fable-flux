# Story story_1349

**The Magic Reading Puzzle**

Every day at three o'clock, Mrs. Chen rang the little silver bell. "Reading time!" she called to her kindergarten class. All the children knew what that meant. Time to sit on the soft rainbow carpet. Time to open books and discover new worlds.

But today, little Puzzle felt different. She dragged her feet across the classroom floor. Her favorite book about dancing cats sat on the shelf, but it looked boring today. The cozy reading corner with its fluffy pillows seemed too far away.

"I don't want to read," Puzzle whispered to her friend Maya. "My brain feels tired and fuzzy."

Maya patted Puzzle's shoulder. "Maybe you just need to try," she said softly.

Puzzle picked up her cat book with a big sigh. The letters looked like tiny black ants marching across the white pages. She couldn't remember what sounds they made together. Her eyes felt heavy and sleepy.

Mrs. Chen noticed Puzzle's frown. She sat down beside her on the carpet. "What's wrong, sweetie?" she asked in her gentle voice.

"Reading is too hard today," Puzzle said. "The words don't make sense anymore."

Mrs. Chen smiled and pulled out a special box from behind her desk. "Let me show you something magical," she whispered. Inside the box were colorful letter tiles that sparkled in the afternoon sunlight.

"These are magic reading tiles," Mrs. Chen explained. "They help make words come alive."

Puzzle's eyes grew wide with wonder. She touched one of the shiny tiles. It felt smooth and warm under her fingers.

"Let's spell your name first," Mrs. Chen suggested. Together, they found the letters P-U-Z-Z-L-E. Each tile made a soft clicking sound as they placed it down.

Something amazing happened. As Puzzle touched each letter, she could hear its sound clearly in her mind. The P made a popping sound like bubbles. The U hummed like a happy bee.

"Now let's try a word from your book," Mrs. Chen said. They spelled C-A-T using the sparkly tiles. Suddenly, Puzzle could see the dancing cat in her mind, twirling and spinning with joy.

Other children gathered around to watch. Soon, everyone wanted to use the magic tiles. They spelled words together, making stories come alive on the carpet.

Puzzle felt excitement bubbling up in her chest like fizzy soda. She opened her cat book again. This time, the words seemed to dance off the page just like the cats in the story. She could read about the orange tabby who loved to waltz and the black kitten who could tap dance.

"Look!" Puzzle called out happily. "I can read about the purple cat who does ballet!"

The other children clapped and cheered. Maya hugged Puzzle tight. "You did it!" she said with a big smile.

Mrs. Chen gathered all the children in a circle. "Sometimes our brains need a little extra help," she explained. "The magic isn't really in the tiles. The magic is inside each of you."

Puzzle felt proud and warm inside, like sunshine was glowing in her heart. She had discovered that reading could be fun again, even on difficult days.

As the reading time ended, Puzzle carefully put the magic tiles back in their special box. She hugged her cat book close to her chest.

"Mrs. Chen," she said, her voice full of excitement, "can we use the magic tiles again tomorrow?"

"Of course," Mrs. Chen replied with a twinkle in her eye. "They'll be here whenever you need them."

That night, Puzzle told her mom all about the magic reading tiles and how they helped words come alive. She couldn't wait for tomorrow's reading time.

"Reading is like solving puzzles," she said, giggling at her own name. "And I love puzzles!"

As she drifted off to sleep, Puzzle dreamed of dancing cats and sparkling letters that sang beautiful songs. Tomorrow would bring another wonderful reading adventure.

The end.
