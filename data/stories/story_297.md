# Mia and the Flying Friend

Mia sat on the bathroom floor. She felt sad and lonely. Her big brother was at school. Mama was busy cooking lunch. The house felt very quiet.

She looked at her toy boats. They floated in the bathtub water. "Sail away, little boats," she whispered. But playing alone wasn't very fun.

Suddenly, something colorful fluttered by the window. It was bright red and yellow! It danced in the breeze outside.

"What's that?" Mia wondered aloud. She climbed on her step stool. She pressed her nose against the glass.

A beautiful kite bobbed up and down! It had a long, wiggly tail. The tail sparkled in the sunshine.

The kite seemed to notice her too. It swooped closer to the window. It wiggled its tail like saying hello!

"Hi there!" Mia giggled. She waved her hand. The kite danced happily in response.

But then the kite looked sad. It drooped lower and lower. Its bright colors seemed duller now.

"What's wrong, little kite?" Mia asked softly. She opened the bathroom window carefully.

The kite fluttered inside! It landed gently on the tile floor. Its tail curled around like a rainbow.

"Are you lonely too?" Mia whispered. The kite's fabric rustled quietly. It sounded like a gentle "yes."

Mia had an idea! "Want to play boats?" she asked. The kite perked up immediately. Its colors grew bright again.

Together, they played in the bathtub. Mia pushed her boats around. The kite used its tail to make waves. Splash, splash, splash!

"This is so much better!" Mia laughed. The kite spun around happily. Water droplets sparkled on its surface.

They discovered they both loved adventures. Mia told stories about sailing far away. The kite showed her flying tricks. It looped and twirled gracefully.

"You're a wonderful friend," Mia said. The kite wrapped its soft tail around her wrist. It felt like a gentle hug.

They played flying games next. Mia held the kite's string carefully. She helped it soar around the bathroom. The kite swooped between the towels. It danced around the mirror.

"Wheee!" Mia giggled as they played. The kite seemed to giggle too. Its fabric fluttered with joy.

When the boats got boring, they invented new games. They played hide-and-seek behind the shower curtain. They had tea parties with Mia's cups.

The kite was very helpful. It used its tail to reach high shelves. It helped Mia organize her bath toys. Working together was so much fun!

"We make a great team!" Mia declared happily. The kite bobbed up and down. It agreed completely.

Soon, Mama called for lunch. "Coming, Mama!" Mia replied. But she felt worried now.

"Will you stay?" she asked her new friend. The kite nuzzled against her cheek. Its fabric felt soft and warm.

The kite pointed to the window. It would wait outside until later. Then they could play again!

"Promise?" Mia asked hopefully. The kite did three happy loop-de-loops. That meant "yes" for sure!

Mia carefully helped her friend outside. She watched it dance in the breeze. The kite waved its colorful tail goodbye.

At lunch, Mia told Mama everything. "I made a flying friend!" she announced proudly. Mama smiled and hugged her tight.

After lunch, Mia rushed back upstairs. Her kite friend was waiting! It spun excitedly when it saw her.

They spent the whole afternoon together. They played boats and flying games. They told jokes and shared secrets.

As the sun set, Mia felt happy. She wasn't lonely anymore at all. She had found a wonderful friend.

"Thank you for playing with me," she whispered. The kite wrapped its tail around her hand. It squeezed gently with friendship.

From that day on, they were best friends. Whenever Mia felt lonely, her kite friend appeared. Together, they could make any day magical.

Playing together was always better than playing alone. Friendship made everything more fun and special!

The bathroom became their favorite place. It was where their beautiful friendship first began.

The end.
