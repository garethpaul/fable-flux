# Story story_562

Ben sat in the warm sandbox. The sand felt soft between her fingers. She loved playing here every day.

Ben reached for her favorite doll. The doll had long yellow hair. She wore a pretty pink dress. But the doll was gone!

"Where is Rosie?" Ben asked herself. She looked around the sandbox. No doll anywhere.

Ben's eyes filled with tears. "Mommy!" she called. "I lost Rosie!"

Mommy came running over. "What happened, sweetie?"

"Rosie is gone," Ben sniffled. "I can't find her anywhere."

Mommy sat down beside Ben. "Let's think about this together. Where did you play with Rosie today?"

Ben wiped her nose. "We built a castle. We had a tea party. We dug holes."

"Maybe Rosie fell in the sand," Mommy suggested. "Let's look carefully."

Ben nodded. She would find her doll. She had to try.

Ben used her small red shovel. She dug in one corner. Sand flew everywhere. She found a toy car. She found a seashell. But no Rosie.

"Not here," Ben said sadly. The sand was getting in her shoes. It felt gritty and rough.

Ben moved to another spot. She dug deeper this time. The sand was cooler down below. She found a buried spoon. She found some pebbles. Still no Rosie.

"This isn't working," Ben sighed. Her arms were getting tired. Sweat dripped down her face.

Mommy patted Ben's shoulder. "Sometimes we need to try a different way."

Ben sat back on her heels. She closed her eyes. She thought really hard.

"What if I remember everything I did?" Ben said. "Maybe that will help."

Ben stood up slowly. "First, I built the castle right here." She pointed to a lumpy sand pile.

"Then we had our tea party." Ben walked to the edge of the sandbox. "We sat here on this side."

Ben's eyes grew wide. "Wait! After the tea party, what did we do?"

"You tell me," Mommy said gently.

"We played hide and seek!" Ben remembered. "I hid Rosie somewhere safe!"

Ben looked around with new eyes. Where would she hide a doll? It had to be somewhere special.

Ben walked to the big oak tree. Its branches made cool shadows. She looked behind the thick trunk.

Nothing there.

Ben checked under the park bench. Just some old leaves. They crackled under her hands.

Then Ben saw the flower bed. Pretty yellow flowers grew there. They smelled sweet like honey.

"I remember now!" Ben shouted. "I hid Rosie with the flowers!"

Ben ran to the flower bed. She looked between the bright blooms. There, lying on soft dirt, was Rosie!

"Found her!" Ben cheered. She picked up her doll carefully. Rosie's pink dress was a little dirty. But she was safe.

Ben hugged Rosie tight. The doll felt warm from the sun. Her yellow hair was soft and silky.

"I was so worried," Ben whispered to Rosie. "But I didn't give up."

Mommy smiled big. "You were very smart, Ben. You tried one way. When that didn't work, you tried another way."

"I remembered everything step by step," Ben said proudly. "That helped me find Rosie."

"Sometimes the best solution is to think carefully," Mommy said. "You showed great problem-solving today."

Ben brushed dirt off Rosie's dress. "Next time I'll remember where I put you," she told her doll.

Ben and Rosie went back to the sandbox. But now Ben was more careful. She kept Rosie right beside her.

They built a new sand castle together. They had another tea party. And when it was time to go home, Ben held Rosie's hand.

"I learned something today," Ben told Mommy as they walked. "When something seems lost, I shouldn't give up. I should think and try different ways."

"That's exactly right," Mommy said. "You're growing up to be very wise."

Ben smiled at Rosie. It had been a good day after all. She had her best friend back. And she had learned that she could solve problems all by herself.

The sun was setting as they headed home. Ben felt proud and happy. Tomorrow would bring new adventures in the sandbox.

The end.
