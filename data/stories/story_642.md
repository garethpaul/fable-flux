# Story story_642

**Finny and the Meadow Friend**

In a sparkling blue pond lived a little fish named Finny. The pond sat in the middle of a sunny meadow filled with soft grass and pretty flowers. Finny had shiny silver scales that caught the sunlight. He loved to swim in circles and blow tiny bubbles.

But Finny felt lonely. He swam by himself every day. The other fish lived in the deep river far away. "I wish I had a friend to play with," Finny said sadly. His voice made little ripples on the water.

One warm morning, a small brown mouse named Pip ran through the tall grass. Pip had soft fur and bright black eyes. He stopped at the edge of the pond to drink the cool water. Pip saw Finny swimming alone.

"Hello there!" called Pip in his squeaky voice. "I'm Pip. What's your name?"

"I'm Finny," said the little fish. He swam closer to where Pip sat. "I live in this pond all by myself."

Pip's whiskers twitched with excitement. "Would you like to play with me?" he asked. "I love to play games!"

Finny's fins wiggled with joy. "Yes! But how can we play? You live on land and I live in water."

Pip thought hard. His little nose sniffed the sweet meadow air. "I have an idea!" he said. "Watch this!"

Pip ran in a big circle around the pond. His tiny paws made soft patting sounds on the grass. Finny swam in a circle too, following Pip from inside the water. They went round and round together.

"This is fun!" laughed Finny. The water splashed gently as he swam.

"Let's play hide and seek!" said Pip. He ran behind a tall yellow flower. Finny dove under a lily pad in his pond. They took turns hiding and finding each other.

Next, they played follow the leader. Pip would hop on his back legs, and Finny would jump out of the water. When Pip rolled on the soft grass, Finny would spin in the water. They giggled and laughed together.

The warm sun made everything feel cozy. Butterflies danced around the colorful flowers. Bees hummed happy songs. The meadow smelled like fresh grass and sweet clover.

"I know another game!" said Finny. "I can splash water drops, and you can try to catch them!"

Finny used his tail to make tiny water drops fly through the air. Pip jumped and reached with his little paws. The cool drops felt nice on his fur. Some drops made tiny rainbows in the sunshine.

"You're so good at jumping!" said Finny.

"And you're amazing at swimming!" said Pip. "I wish I could swim like you."

"And I wish I could run in the grass like you," said Finny.

They played until the sun started to set. The sky turned pink and orange. Pip sat on a smooth rock by the water's edge. Finny floated nearby, his silver scales glowing in the soft light.

"This was the best day ever," said Finny. "I'm so happy we're friends."

"Me too," said Pip. "Even though we're different, we can still have fun together."

"Will you come back tomorrow?" asked Finny hopefully.

"Every day!" promised Pip. "We can make up new games to play."

From that day on, Finny and Pip met every morning in the sunny meadow. They played their special games and shared stories. Finny told Pip about life underwater. Pip shared tales of his adventures in the grass.

The other animals in the meadow loved watching them play. Soon, Finny and Pip had many friends who came to visit the pond.

Finny never felt lonely again. He learned that friendship can happen anywhere, even between a fish and a mouse. Every morning, he would swim to the surface and look for his best friend Pip.

And Pip would always be there, waiting by the pond with a big smile and a new game to play.

The end.
