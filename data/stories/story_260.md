# Mama's Magical Snack Time

The kitchen clock chimed three times. "Snack time!" called little Emma from the living room.

Mama sighed softly in her chair. She felt so tired today. Her shoulders drooped like wilted flowers. Making snacks felt like climbing a mountain.

"Coming, sweetie," Mama called back. She slowly stood up. Her feet felt heavy on the wooden floor.

Emma bounced into the kitchen. Her eyes sparkled with excitement. "What snack today, Mama?"

Mama opened the pantry door. Boxes and jars stared back at her. Everything looked boring and plain. "Maybe some crackers?" she said quietly.

"Can we make something special?" Emma asked. She tugged gently on Mama's apron.

Mama looked down at her daughter. Emma's hopeful smile made something flutter in Mama's chest. "What did you have in mind?"

"Rainbow snacks!" Emma clapped her hands together.

Mama tilted her head. "Rainbow snacks?"

Emma nodded eagerly. "With all the colors!"

Something warm began to grow in Mama's heart. She opened the refrigerator door. Red strawberries winked at her. Orange carrots smiled up from their drawer.

"I see red," Mama said softly.

"And orange!" Emma pointed excitedly.

Mama's lips curved into a small smile. She reached for yellow cheese. Green grapes rolled playfully in their container. Blue... blue was tricky.

"Blueberries!" Emma shouted with joy.

Mama laughed. The sound surprised her. It felt like sunshine breaking through clouds. "And purple grapes!"

They laid everything on the counter. The colors looked like a beautiful painting. Mama felt her tiredness melting away like ice cream in summer.

"Let's make patterns!" Emma suggested.

They arranged the foods in rainbow rows. Red strawberry, orange carrot, yellow cheese. The pattern repeated across their plates. Each color seemed to glow brighter.

"It's like magic," Mama whispered.

Emma giggled. "You're the magic, Mama!"

Mama's heart swelled with warmth. She hadn't felt this happy all day. The kitchen suddenly felt cozy and bright. Sunlight danced through the window.

They sat together at the small table. Each bite tasted like pure joy. The strawberries were sweet summer rain. The carrots crunched like autumn leaves. The cheese melted smooth as silk.

"This is the best snack ever," Emma said. Juice from the grapes dripped down her chin.

Mama reached over and wiped it gently. "It really is special."

They talked and laughed between bites. Emma told funny stories about her toys. Mama shared memories of her own childhood snacks. Time moved slowly and sweetly.

"The colors taste like happiness," Emma said seriously.

Mama nodded. She understood exactly what Emma meant. Each color brought its own special feeling. Together they made something wonderful.

When they finished eating, they washed dishes together. Emma stood on her little stool. Soap bubbles floated around them like tiny clouds. Even cleaning felt magical now.

"Can we make rainbow snacks tomorrow?" Emma asked hopefully.

Mama's eyes twinkled with excitement. "What about rainbow pancakes for breakfast too?"

Emma squealed with delight. She threw her arms around Mama's waist. "You're the best mama ever!"

Mama hugged her daughter close. The tiredness was completely gone now. In its place lived joy and anticipation. She felt light as a feather.

That evening, Mama planned tomorrow's rainbow snacks. She drew colorful pictures in her notebook. Purple cabbage for new colors. Maybe some orange peppers too.

As she tucked Emma into bed, Mama smiled. "Sweet dreams about rainbow snacks."

"Will you dream about them too?" Emma asked sleepily.

"I definitely will," Mama promised.

The next morning, Mama woke up excited. She couldn't wait for snack time. The kitchen called to her like an old friend. Today would bring new colors and new magic.

Sometimes the most ordinary moments hold the most extraordinary joy. Mama had learned this wonderful secret. Every snack time could be an adventure when shared with love.

The end.
