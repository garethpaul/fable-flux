# Story story_560

**Tiger's Magical Snack Time**

The big clock on the wall went tick-tock. "Snack time!" called Mom from the kitchen. Tiger heard her voice from his room. He was building a tall tower with blocks.

Tiger's tummy felt a little hungry. But he didn't want to stop playing. His tower was almost finished. Just one more block on top.

"Tiger, come eat your snack," Mom said again. Her voice sounded happy and warm.

Tiger's shoulders drooped down. He walked slowly to the kitchen. His feet felt heavy on the floor. The blocks could wait, but snack time couldn't.

Mom had set out a small blue plate. On it sat apple slices and crackers. A cup of milk waited next to the plate. Everything looked nice and neat.

"I don't feel like eating," Tiger said softly. He climbed onto his chair. The wooden seat felt cool under his legs.

Mom sat down next to him. Her eyes were kind and understanding. "What if we made snack time special today?" she asked.

Tiger looked up at her. "How?" he wondered.

Mom smiled her biggest smile. "Let's pretend the apple slices are boats. The crackers can be islands." She pointed to the white milk. "And this milk is a calm ocean."

Tiger's eyes grew wide and bright. That sounded like fun! He picked up an apple slice. It was smooth and cool in his fingers.

"Sail away, little boat," Tiger said in a quiet voice. He dipped the apple into the milk. Tiny white drops splashed around the cup.

The apple slice tasted sweet and crisp. The milk was cold and creamy. Tiger's tummy felt happy with each bite.

"Now the boat needs to visit an island," Mom suggested. She pointed to a round cracker.

Tiger picked up the cracker carefully. It was golden brown and bumpy. He took a small bite. It crunched between his teeth. The salty taste mixed with the sweet apple.

"The island has treasure," Tiger said with excitement. His voice got louder and more cheerful. "Golden treasure that tastes good!"

Mom clapped her hands together. "What a wonderful adventure!" she said.

Tiger sailed more apple boats across his milk ocean. Each cracker island had different treasures. Some were salty. Some were crunchy. All of them tasted delicious.

Soon his plate was empty. His cup had only a few drops left. Tiger's tummy felt full and satisfied.

"That was the best snack time ever," Tiger said. He hugged Mom tight. She smelled like vanilla and sunshine.

"You made it special with your imagination," Mom told him. Her hug felt warm and safe.

Tiger helped Mom wash his plate and cup. The warm soapy water felt good on his hands. They dried everything with a soft yellow towel.

"Can we have another adventure tomorrow?" Tiger asked hopefully. His eyes sparkled with excitement.

"Of course," Mom said with a gentle smile. "What kind of adventure do you want?"

Tiger thought for a moment. His finger tapped his chin. "Maybe the crackers can be flying carpets next time!"

Mom laughed a musical laugh. "That sounds perfect," she agreed.

Tiger ran back to his room. His block tower was still waiting for him. But now he felt happy and full. His tummy wasn't hungry anymore.

He placed the last block on top. The tower stood tall and proud. Just like Tiger felt inside.

Later that day, Tiger told Dad about his snack adventure. Dad's eyes lit up with interest. "Tomorrow I want to hear about flying carpets," Dad said.

That night at bedtime, Tiger smiled into his pillow. Snack time wasn't boring anymore. It was magical and fun. He could hardly wait for tomorrow's adventure.

His eyes closed sleepily. He dreamed of apple boats sailing on milk oceans. And flying carpet crackers soaring through the sky.

Tiger learned something important that day. Even ordinary things can become extraordinary. All you need is imagination and someone to share it with.

The end.
