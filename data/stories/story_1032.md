# Story story_1032

**The Big Friend in the Little Kitchen**

Baby Emma sat on the kitchen floor. She played with her colorful blocks. The sun came through the window. It made pretty patterns on the tiles.

Emma stacked her blocks up high. They fell down with a crash. She giggled and tried again. But after a while, she felt lonely. Playing alone wasn't as much fun.

Outside the window, a gentle elephant named Ellie was walking by. Ellie had soft gray skin and kind brown eyes. She heard Emma's blocks falling down. She peeked through the kitchen window.

"Hello, little one," Ellie said in her sweet voice. "May I come play with you?"

Emma looked up with big, surprised eyes. She had never seen an elephant before! But Ellie looked so friendly and warm.

"Yes, please!" Emma said, clapping her hands.

Ellie was very big for the kitchen. She squeezed through the door very carefully. Her trunk wiggled as she tried not to bump anything. She moved slowly and gently.

"What are you building?" Ellie asked, sitting down carefully.

"A tower!" Emma said proudly. "But it keeps falling down."

Ellie's trunk reached down. She picked up a red block with the tip. "May I help you?" she asked.

Emma nodded happily. Together, they started building. Ellie used her trunk like a gentle hand. She placed blocks exactly where Emma pointed.

"This one goes here," Emma said, pointing to a spot.

"Perfect choice," Ellie said, setting down a blue block.

Their tower grew taller and taller. It had red blocks and blue blocks. It had yellow blocks and green blocks too. The colors looked like a rainbow.

Emma's tummy rumbled loudly. "I'm hungry," she said.

"I know about food!" Ellie said with a happy trumpet sound. "What do you like to eat?"

Emma pointed to the refrigerator. Ellie opened it carefully with her trunk. Inside were apples, bananas, and crackers.

"Let's make a snack together," Ellie suggested.

Ellie peeled a banana with her trunk. Emma broke crackers into small pieces. They arranged everything on a pretty plate. The banana smelled sweet and fresh. The crackers crunched between Emma's teeth.

"This tastes so good!" Emma said, sharing her crackers with Ellie.

Ellie munched happily. "Everything tastes better with a friend," she said.

After their snack, they played more games. They played peek-a-boo with a kitchen towel. Ellie hid behind the towel. Emma giggled when Ellie's trunk poked out.

They made music with pots and spoons. Emma banged the pots. Ellie tapped them gently with her trunk. Their music filled the whole kitchen with happy sounds.

"I was lonely before," Emma told Ellie. "But now I feel happy."

"I was lonely too," Ellie said. "I didn't have anyone to play with either."

They hugged each other tight. Ellie's trunk wrapped around Emma like a warm, soft hug. Emma felt safe and loved.

"Will you be my friend forever?" Emma asked.

"Forever and always," Ellie promised. "Friends stick together."

Just then, Emma's mom came into the kitchen. She saw Emma and Ellie playing together. She smiled the biggest smile ever.

"What wonderful friends you are," Mom said. "Ellie, would you like to stay for dinner?"

"I would love that," Ellie said happily.

They set the table together. Emma put out the plates. Ellie arranged the napkins with her trunk. Mom cooked their favorite foods.

As they ate dinner, Emma felt warm inside. She wasn't lonely anymore. She had found the best friend ever. Ellie felt happy too. She had found someone who liked to play and laugh with her.

"Thank you for being my friend," Emma whispered to Ellie.

"Thank you for letting me play," Ellie whispered back.

From that day on, Emma and Ellie played together every day. The kitchen became their special place. It was where their friendship began. It was where they learned that the best friends come in all sizes.

And they were never lonely again.

The end.
