# Story story_1475

**Puzzle Time Magic**

Lily looked at the big clock. The little hand pointed to three. "Snack time!" called Mrs. Chen from the kitchen.

All the children ran to wash their hands. But Lily walked very slowly. After snack time came puzzle time. Lily did not like puzzles. The pieces were too small. They never fit right. Other kids finished their puzzles fast. Lily always felt stuck.

"Come eat your apple slices, Lily," said Mrs. Chen. The apple pieces were sweet and crunchy. Lily ate them one by one. Crunch, crunch, crunch. The juice dripped down her chin.

Soon snack time was over. "Time for puzzles!" said Mrs. Chen. The other children cheered. They ran to the puzzle table. Lily's tummy felt funny. She wanted to hide.

"I don't want to do puzzles," Lily whispered. Her voice was very quiet.

Mrs. Chen knelt down beside her. "What's wrong, sweetie?"

"Puzzles are too hard," said Lily. "I can't make the pieces fit."

Mrs. Chen smiled. "Let's try something special today." She took Lily's hand. It felt warm and soft.

They walked to the puzzle table together. Mrs. Chen picked up a new box. On the front was a picture of a sunny garden. Big yellow flowers smiled at them.

"This puzzle is different," said Mrs. Chen. "The pieces are bigger. And look!" She opened the box. Each puzzle piece felt thick and smooth. They were twice as big as regular pieces.

"Can you find a yellow piece?" asked Mrs. Chen.

Lily looked in the box. She found a bright yellow piece. It felt smooth under her fingers. "Here's one!"

"Perfect! Now let's find its friend."

Lily picked up another piece. This one had green on it. She tried to put them together. Click! They fit perfectly.

"It worked!" Lily said. Her eyes grew wide.

"You did it!" said Mrs. Chen. "Keep going!"

Lily found more pieces. A red flower piece. A blue sky piece. Each time they clicked together, Lily smiled bigger. The sound was like a tiny celebration.

Soon other children came to watch. "Wow, Lily!" said Sam. "Your puzzle looks amazing!"

"The flowers are so pretty!" said Emma.

Lily felt proud and happy. Her cheeks felt warm. She kept adding pieces. Click, click, click. The garden picture grew bigger and bigger.

After a while, Lily had finished half the puzzle. The yellow flowers looked like they were dancing. The green leaves seemed to wave hello.

"This is fun!" said Lily. She could not stop smiling.

"You're doing great work," said Mrs. Chen. "See how you kept trying?"

Lily nodded. "The big pieces are easier. And the picture is so beautiful!"

She added three more pieces. The puzzle was almost done. Just the blue sky was left. Lily found the last few pieces. They fit together like old friends.

"I finished it!" Lily shouted. "I really finished it!"

All the children clapped. Lily felt like she was glowing inside. The finished puzzle showed a garden full of happy flowers. Bees buzzed around purple lavender. A red ladybug sat on a green leaf.

"You should feel proud," said Mrs. Chen. "You didn't give up."

Lily touched the smooth puzzle pieces. "It was hard at first. But then it got fun!"

"That's how learning works," said Mrs. Chen. "Hard things can become easy with practice."

Lily looked at the puzzle shelf. So many boxes waited there. Some had puppies on them. Others showed ocean scenes. A few had colorful butterflies.

"Can I try another puzzle tomorrow?" asked Lily.

"Of course!" said Mrs. Chen. "Which one looks interesting?"

Lily pointed to a box with a rainbow on it. "That one has lots of colors!"

"Perfect choice," said Mrs. Chen.

Lily felt excited about tomorrow. Puzzle time didn't seem scary anymore. It felt like an adventure waiting to happen. She couldn't wait to see what picture she would build next.

The end.
