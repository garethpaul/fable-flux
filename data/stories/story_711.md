# Story story_711

Max sat on the bathroom floor. He felt very lonely today. His toy blocks were scattered around him. The tiles felt cold under his legs.

"I wish I had a friend," Max whispered. His voice echoed in the small room. The bathroom was quiet except for dripping water.

Max picked up a red block. It felt smooth in his hands. He stacked it on a blue one. Then he added a yellow block on top.

Suddenly, the blocks started to wiggle! Max's eyes grew wide. The red block hopped off the stack. It landed with a soft thud.

"Hello, Max!" said the red block. Its voice was tiny and cheerful. "I'm Ruby!"

Max rubbed his eyes. "Blocks can't talk," he said.

The blue block jumped up too. "I'm Barry!" it chirped. "We want to play with you!"

The yellow block bounced excitedly. "I'm Sunny!" it sang. "Playing alone looks boring!"

Max felt his heart skip with joy. Real friends! Right here in the bathroom!

"But what can we play?" Max asked. He looked around the small space.

Ruby rolled closer. "Let's build a castle!" she suggested.

Barry wiggled happily. "Yes! A big, tall castle!"

Sunny spun in a circle. "The best castle ever!"

Max smiled for the first time today. He gathered his blocks carefully. His hands felt warm now, not cold anymore.

"I'll make the base," Max said. He placed blocks in a square. The blocks felt heavier somehow, more real.

Ruby hopped onto the base. "I'll be a tower!" she declared. She stood up straight and proud.

Barry rolled over quickly. "I'll be the wall!" He lined up next to other blocks. They all stood together like soldiers.

Sunny bounced up high. "I'll be the flag!" She perched on top of Ruby. She sparkled in the bathroom light.

Max clapped his hands together. The sound bounced off the walls. "It's beautiful!" he exclaimed.

But then the castle wobbled. The blocks started to fall! Max reached out to catch them.

"Don't worry!" Ruby called out. "We'll try again!"

"Falling down is fun too!" Barry laughed.

"Let's make it stronger!" Sunny cheered.

Max felt better already. His new friends weren't giving up. They wanted to keep playing!

This time, Max built more carefully. He pressed each block down firmly. The blocks helped by staying very still.

"Perfect!" Ruby said as she took her place.

"Super strong!" Barry added proudly.

"The most wonderful castle!" Sunny declared.

Max stepped back to look. The castle was small but perfect. It fit just right in the bathroom corner.

"We did it together," Max said softly. His chest felt warm and happy.

The blocks all cheered. Their tiny voices filled the room with joy. Even the bathroom seemed brighter now.

"Can we play again tomorrow?" Max asked hopefully.

"Every day!" Ruby promised.

"Forever and always!" Barry agreed.

"Best friends forever!" Sunny sang.

Max hugged his blocks gently. They felt warm in his arms. He wasn't lonely anymore.

From that day on, Max never felt alone. His blocks were always ready to play. They built towers, bridges, and trains together.

Sometimes Mom found Max giggling in the bathroom. She would smile and shake her head. "That boy and his imagination," she would say.

But Max knew the truth. His blocks were real friends. They made every day special.

When bedtime came, Max tucked his blocks into their box. "Good night, friends," he whispered.

"Good night, Max!" they whispered back.

Max fell asleep with a big smile. Tomorrow would bring more adventures with his very best friends.

The bathroom had become their special place. A place where friendship bloomed and imagination grew. A place where no one ever felt lonely again.

Max learned that friends can be found anywhere. Even in the most unexpected places. And the best friendships start with an open heart and a willingness to play.

The end.
