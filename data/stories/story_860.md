# Story story_860

Teddy sat on his soft blue blanket. The big clock on the wall said seven o'clock. Mama walked into his cozy bedroom with a smile.

"Story time, Teddy!" she said cheerfully.

Teddy pulled his knees up to his chest. He wrapped his arms around his legs. Story time felt too long tonight. He wanted to keep playing with his toy cars instead.

"I don't want stories," Teddy mumbled quietly. "Stories are boring."

Mama sat down beside him on the fluffy carpet. She opened the big red book with golden letters. The pages smelled like vanilla and old paper.

"Let's try just one story," Mama said gently. "This book looks very special."

Teddy peeked at the colorful pictures. A little dragon smiled from the first page. The dragon had sparkly purple scales and kind green eyes.

"Once upon a time," Mama began reading, "there lived a young dragon named Pip."

Suddenly, something amazing happened. The pages began to glow softly. Tiny golden sparkles danced around the words. Teddy's eyes grew wide with wonder.

"Mama, look!" Teddy whispered excitedly. "The book is glowing!"

The sparkles grew brighter and brighter. They swirled around Teddy and Mama like magical fairy dust. The bedroom walls seemed to disappear completely.

Now Teddy found himself in a beautiful meadow. Soft green grass tickled his bare feet. Sweet-smelling flowers bloomed everywhere he looked. A gentle breeze made the tall trees whisper.

"Welcome to my home!" said a friendly voice.

Teddy turned around quickly. There stood Pip the dragon! He was small and purple, just like in the book. His scales shimmered like tiny rainbows.

"You're real!" Teddy laughed with pure joy.

Pip nodded his scaly head happily. "Stories make everything real, Teddy. Come play with me!"

They ran through the sunny meadow together. Pip showed Teddy how to catch floating bubbles. The bubbles popped with tiny musical sounds. They tasted like strawberries and sunshine.

Next, they climbed a hill covered with soft moss. At the top grew a magnificent tree. Its branches held books instead of leaves. Each book glowed with different colored light.

"Every book holds a new adventure," Pip explained proudly. "Red books have brave knights. Blue books have sailing ships. Yellow books have friendly animals."

Teddy reached for a green book. It felt warm in his small hands. When he opened it, butterflies flew out. They danced around his head singing sweet songs.

"This is incredible!" Teddy shouted with excitement.

Pip smiled widely, showing tiny white teeth. "Reading opens doors to everywhere, Teddy. You can visit any place you imagine."

They spent hours exploring together. They met talking rabbits who wore tiny hats. They sailed on a boat made of flower petals. They even flew on Pip's back above fluffy white clouds.

Finally, the golden sparkles returned. They swirled around Teddy again, lifting him gently. The magical meadow slowly faded away.

Teddy found himself back in his bedroom. Mama still held the big red book. But now it looked ordinary again.

"Did you like the story?" Mama asked softly.

Teddy's heart felt full of happiness. His cheeks were pink with excitement. "That was the best story ever! Pip was my friend!"

Mama hugged him tight and warm. "Stories can take us anywhere, sweetheart. They help our imagination grow strong."

Teddy snuggled into his cozy bed. His head sank into the fluffy pillow. "Can we read another story tomorrow?"

"Of course," Mama said, kissing his forehead. "We'll have many more adventures together."

She turned off the lamp. Moonlight streamed through the window. Teddy closed his eyes peacefully.

He dreamed about Pip all night long. They played in meadows filled with singing flowers. When morning came, Teddy woke up smiling.

"I can't wait for story time tonight!" he told Mama at breakfast.

From that day on, Teddy loved stories. Each book became a doorway to magic. Reading time became his favorite time.

And sometimes, late at night, he swore he could still hear Pip's happy laughter echoing in his dreams.

The end.
