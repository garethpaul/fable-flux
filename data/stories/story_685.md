# Story story_685

**Ziggy the Zebra's Bright Idea**

Ziggy the zebra loved helping in the kitchen. He wore a bright red apron. His black and white stripes looked cheerful in the morning sun.

Every day, Ziggy helped make breakfast. He stirred the pancake batter. He set the colorful plates on the table. The kitchen smelled like sweet syrup and warm butter.

But when nighttime came, everything changed. The kitchen became very dark. The shadows looked scary to Ziggy. The refrigerator made strange humming sounds. The faucet went drip, drip, drip.

"I don't like the dark kitchen," Ziggy whispered to himself. His knees felt wobbly. His heart beat fast like a drum.

One evening, Mama asked Ziggy to get milk from the kitchen. "The milk is in the refrigerator," she said with a smile.

Ziggy's tummy felt fluttery. The kitchen was so dark and spooky. But he wanted to help Mama.

He tiptoed to the kitchen door. The room was black as night. Ziggy could not see anything. The refrigerator hummed loudly. A chair creaked in the corner.

"Maybe I can be brave," Ziggy said softly. He took one tiny step forward. Then another small step.

Suddenly, he heard a strange noise. THUMP! Something fell in the dark kitchen. Ziggy's eyes grew wide. He ran back to Mama as fast as his hooves could carry him.

"I couldn't get the milk," Ziggy said sadly. "The kitchen is too scary in the dark."

Mama hugged Ziggy tight. "Being scared is okay," she said gently. "Let's think of a way to help you feel better."

Ziggy thought and thought. His ears twitched as he concentrated. "What if I bring my flashlight?" he asked hopefully.

"That's a wonderful idea!" Mama said. She clapped her hooves together.

Ziggy got his small yellow flashlight. It felt warm and smooth in his hoof. He clicked it on. A bright circle of light appeared on the wall.

"Now I can see!" Ziggy said with excitement. He felt much braver with his flashlight.

He walked slowly to the kitchen. The flashlight made a cozy glow. Ziggy could see the friendly refrigerator. He could see the clean counters. The scary shadows were just ordinary things.

The refrigerator still hummed, but now it sounded like a happy song. The faucet still dripped, but it sounded like gentle rain.

Ziggy opened the refrigerator door. The inside light was bright and cheerful. He found the cold milk bottle. It felt smooth and cool in his hooves.

"I did it!" Ziggy said proudly. He carried the milk back to Mama. His chest felt warm and happy.

"You were very brave," Mama said. She gave Ziggy another big hug. "You found a smart solution to your problem."

From that night on, Ziggy always used his flashlight in the dark kitchen. Soon, he knew exactly where everything was. He could find the milk quickly. He could get cookies from the jar. He could even wash dishes in the sink.

The kitchen didn't seem scary anymore. It felt like a friendly place, even in the dark. Ziggy's flashlight made everything bright and safe.

One evening, Ziggy's little sister Zara felt scared of the dark kitchen too. Her bottom lip trembled. Her eyes looked worried.

"Don't worry," Ziggy said kindly. "I have an idea that will help you." He showed Zara his flashlight. "We can use this together."

Zara smiled a big smile. "Thank you, Ziggy!" she said happily.

Now both zebras help in the kitchen, day and night. They learned that being scared is normal. They learned that thinking of solutions makes problems easier. Most importantly, they learned that helping each other makes everything better.

Ziggy still wears his red apron. But now he also carries his yellow flashlight. The kitchen is always bright and cheerful when Ziggy is there, no matter how dark it gets outside.

The end.
