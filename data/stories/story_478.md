# Teddy's Big Day Practice

Teddy sat on the soft grass in his backyard. The warm sun tickled his fuzzy brown fur. Tomorrow was his first day at the new school. His tummy felt all twisty inside.

"I'm scared," Teddy whispered to himself. He pulled his knees close to his chest. The big oak tree swayed gently above him. Birds chirped happy songs, but Teddy didn't feel happy at all.

His mama bear came outside with a basket. "What's wrong, little one?" she asked softly.

"School starts tomorrow," Teddy said. His voice was very quiet. "What if the other cubs don't like me? What if I can't find the bathroom? What if I forget my lunch?"

Mama sat down beside him. The grass crunched under her paws. "Those are big worries," she said. "But maybe we can practice here first."

Teddy looked up. His brown eyes were wide. "Practice school? In our backyard?"

"Why not?" Mama smiled. "We have everything we need right here."

First, they practiced walking to school. Teddy marched around the apple tree three times. "Left, right, left, right," he said with each step. His paws made soft sounds on the dirt path.

Next, they practiced meeting new friends. Mama pretended to be a shy little rabbit. "Hi, I'm Rosie," she said in a squeaky voice. 

Teddy giggled. "Hi Rosie! I'm Teddy. Do you want to play?"

They practiced sharing toys too. Teddy brought out his favorite red ball. He rolled it to Mama-Rosie. "Here, you can play with this," he said proudly.

Then they practiced asking for help. Teddy walked up to the garden gate. "Excuse me, Teacher Sunflower," he said to the tall yellow flower. "Can you help me find the art room?"

Mama made her voice sound like a teacher. "Of course, Teddy! It's right past the sandbox."

But Teddy still felt nervous. His ears drooped down. "What if I spill my juice?" he worried.

"Let's practice that too," Mama said. She brought out a small cup of apple juice. Teddy held it with both paws. He walked very slowly across the yard.

Oops! A few drops spilled on his shirt. "Oh no!" Teddy cried.

"That's okay," Mama said gently. "Accidents happen. Watch this." She dabbed the spots with a napkin. "All clean!"

Teddy smiled a little. The juice spots were gone.

Next, they practiced sitting quietly for story time. Teddy sat criss-cross under the shady tree. Mama read him a book about a brave little mouse. The pages rustled in the gentle breeze.

"I can sit still," Teddy said proudly. "Just like at real school!"

As the afternoon went on, Teddy felt braver. They practiced washing paws at the outdoor spigot. The cool water splashed and sparkled. They practiced hanging up backpacks on the fence post. They even practiced raising paws to answer questions.

"What color is the sky?" Mama asked.

Teddy's paw shot up high. "Blue! Blue like my favorite crayon!"

The sun began to set. Pretty orange and pink colors painted the sky. Teddy looked around their backyard classroom. He had practiced so many things!

"I think I'm ready now," Teddy said. His voice sounded stronger. The twisty feeling in his tummy was almost gone.

Mama hugged him tight. "You practiced so hard today. You tried every solution we could think of."

"And if I get scared tomorrow," Teddy said, "I'll remember our practice time."

That night, Teddy slept peacefully. He dreamed about making new friends and learning new things. In his dream, he was brave and confident.

The next morning, Teddy bounded out of bed. "I'm ready for school!" he announced. His backpack was packed. His fur was brushed neat and clean.

As they walked to school together, Teddy felt proud. When he got nervous, he remembered practicing in the backyard. He had worked hard to solve his worry problem.

And you know what? School turned out to be wonderful. Teddy made three new friends, found the bathroom easily, and only spilled his juice once.

"Practice makes everything better," Teddy told Mama that afternoon. And he was absolutely right.

The end.
