# Story story_726

**Bounce and the Little Fish**

Bounce was a bright red ball. He lived on the sandy beach. Every day, he rolled around by himself.

The warm sand felt bumpy under him. The salty sea breeze made him wobble. But Bounce felt lonely.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," Bounce sighed. He bounced softly on the hot sand.

The waves crashed nearby. They made a swooshing sound. Seagulls called from above. But no one wanted to play.

Down in the clear blue water lived Splash. She was a small yellow fish. She had bright orange fins. Her scales sparkled in the sunlight.

Splash swam near the shore every day. She watched the red ball bounce alone. "That ball looks sad," she thought.

One sunny morning, Splash swam closer to the beach. The water felt cool and refreshing. Little waves tickled her fins.

"Hello!" called Splash from the shallow water. "I'm Splash! What's your name?"

Bounce stopped rolling. His red surface grew warm with excitement. "I'm Bounce! Are you talking to me?"

"Yes!" giggled Splash. "You look lonely. Do you want to play?"

Bounce's whole body seemed to glow. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" said Splash. "But how can we play together? You're on land. I'm in water."

Bounce thought hard. Then he had an idea. "I can roll to the water's edge!"

Bounce rolled toward the foamy waves. The wet sand felt cool and squishy. The water touched his bottom gently.

Splash swam right up to him. "This is perfect!" she said. "Now we can play!"

First, they played catch. Splash would splash water at Bounce. Bounce would roll back and forth. The water drops felt like tiny kisses on his surface.

"This is so much fun!" laughed Bounce. The sun made the water drops sparkle like diamonds.

Then Splash had another idea. "Let's play follow the wave!"

When a wave came in, Bounce would roll with it. Splash would swim alongside him. When the wave went out, they'd go back together.

"Wheee!" shouted Bounce as a big wave pushed him. The cool water swirled around him. It felt amazing!

"You're getting good at this!" cheered Splash. Her fins flapped with joy.

They played for hours. The sun felt warm on Bounce's red surface. The salty water made Splash's scales shine brighter.

Sometimes they raced. Bounce would roll fast along the wet sand. Splash would swim quickly in the shallow water. They always finished at the same time.

"We're both winners!" they would say together.

Other times they just talked. Bounce told Splash about life on the beach. He described how the sand felt warm and grainy.

Splash told Bounce about underwater life. She described the colorful coral and dancing seaweed.

"Your world sounds beautiful," said Bounce.

"Yours does too," replied Splash.

As the day grew late, the sky turned orange and pink. The water became calmer. Gentle waves lapped softly.

"I had the best day ever," said Bounce. His red color seemed brighter somehow.

"Me too," agreed Splash. "Will you be here tomorrow?"

"Every day!" promised Bounce. "Will you come play again?"

"Always!" said Splash. "We're best friends now!"

From that day on, Bounce was never lonely. Every morning, he'd roll to the water's edge. Splash would be waiting for him.

They invented new games together. They shared stories and dreams. They laughed until their sides hurt.

The other sea creatures began to notice. Soon, a crab joined their games. Then a starfish wanted to play too.

But Bounce and Splash always stayed best friends. They had found something special together.

"Thank you for being my friend," Bounce would say.

"Thank you for teaching me new games," Splash would reply.

Every sunset, they'd watch the sky together. The ball on the sand, the fish in the water. Two different worlds, one beautiful friendship.

And they lived happily ever after, playing by the sea.

The end.
