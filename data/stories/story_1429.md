# Story story_1429

**The Lonely Brother in the Woods**

Tommy lived in a small house by the forest. His sister Emma was at camp for two weeks. His mom and dad worked in their garden all day. Tommy felt very lonely.

"I have no one to play with," Tommy said sadly. He looked out his window at the tall green trees. Birds chirped in the branches. Squirrels ran up and down the tree trunks.

Tommy walked into the forest behind his house. His feet crunched on dried leaves. The air smelled like pine needles and fresh dirt. Sunlight made pretty patterns on the ground.

"Hello!" Tommy called out loudly. "Is anyone here to play?"

Only his echo came back. "Hello... hello... hello..."

Tommy sat on a big rock. He felt tears in his eyes. The forest was so quiet. Even the birds had stopped singing.

Then Tommy had an idea. "I will build something fun!" he said. "Maybe other kids will come play!"

Tommy gathered long sticks and brown leaves. He tried to build a fort. But the sticks kept falling down. The leaves blew away in the wind. After an hour, Tommy had only a small pile of sticks.

"This is not working," Tommy sighed. His hands were dirty and scratched. His fort looked like a messy pile.

Tommy sat down again. He watched a red squirrel jump from tree to tree. The squirrel chattered and flicked its fluffy tail. It seemed happy and playful.

"I wish I could talk to animals," Tommy said softly.

Then Tommy remembered something. His grandpa once told him that animals like gentle, quiet people. "Be still and patient," Grandpa had said. "Animals will come to you."

Tommy decided to try a different way. He sat very still on his rock. He breathed quietly. He listened to all the forest sounds.

A blue jay landed on a branch near Tommy. It tilted its head and looked at him. Tommy smiled but did not move.

Soon, a brown rabbit hopped closer. Its pink nose twitched. Its ears stood up tall and alert.

Tommy whispered very softly, "Hello, little rabbit."

The rabbit did not run away. It sat near Tommy's feet. Tommy felt so happy and excited inside.

More animals came closer. Two squirrels ran down a tree trunk. A tiny chipmunk peeked out from behind a rock. Even a gentle deer stepped into the clearing.

Tommy felt like Snow White in his favorite story. He was surrounded by forest friends. The animals seemed curious about this quiet boy.

"You are all so beautiful," Tommy whispered. His heart felt warm and full.

The rabbit hopped in a circle. The squirrels chased each other around the trees. The chipmunk gathered acorns. The deer munched on green grass.

Tommy laughed softly. He felt like he was watching the best show ever. The animals were playing just for him.

Tommy spent the whole afternoon with his new friends. He learned that the blue jay liked shiny things. The rabbit loved clover flowers. The squirrels were very good at hide and seek.

When the sun began to set, Tommy stood up slowly. "Thank you for playing with me," he said. "I will come back tomorrow."

The animals scattered back to their homes. But Tommy knew they would remember him.

Tommy walked home with a big smile. His mom was watering her tomato plants.

"Did you have fun today?" she asked.

"Yes!" Tommy said. "I made lots of new friends in the forest!"

"That is wonderful," his mom said. "How did you do it?"

"I tried building a fort first," Tommy explained. "But that did not work. Then I remembered to be patient and gentle. The animals came to me!"

His mom hugged him tight. "You learned something very important today. Sometimes the best way to make friends is to be quiet and kind."

That night, Tommy fell asleep dreaming about his forest friends. He could not wait to visit them again tomorrow. Being patient had worked much better than being loud.

Tommy was not lonely anymore.

The end.
