# Story story_556

**Charlie's Missing Teddy**

Charlie the horse loved playing at Sunny Park playground. His favorite thing was his soft brown teddy bear named Cocoa. Charlie took Cocoa everywhere. They went down slides together. They swung on swings together. They were best friends.

One bright morning, Charlie galloped to the playground. He set Cocoa on a bench. "Wait here, Cocoa," Charlie said. "I want to try the monkey bars."

Charlie stretched his long legs. He reached for the first bar. His hooves felt cold on the metal. He swung from bar to bar. "Look at me, Cocoa!" he called.

But when Charlie looked back, the bench was empty. Cocoa was gone!

"Oh no!" Charlie whinnied. His heart felt heavy. Where could his best friend be?

Charlie's eyes filled with tears. He trotted around the playground. "Cocoa! Cocoa!" he called. His voice echoed off the slides.

First, Charlie decided to look in all the obvious places. He checked under the slide. The sand felt cool under his hooves. No Cocoa. He looked behind the swings. The chains creaked in the wind. Still no Cocoa.

Charlie peeked inside the tunnel. It was dark and echoey. "Cocoa, are you in here?" His voice bounced back. Empty.

He checked the sandbox. Other toys were buried in the sand. A red bucket. A yellow shovel. But no brown teddy bear.

Charlie felt very sad. His ears drooped down. Maybe Cocoa was lost forever.

Then Charlie had an idea. Maybe someone found Cocoa! He decided to ask for help.

Charlie trotted to the park entrance. A kind woman was walking her dog. "Excuse me," Charlie said politely. "Have you seen a brown teddy bear?"

The woman smiled. "I'm sorry, sweetie. I haven't seen one."

Charlie asked a jogger. "No teddy bear here," the man said.

Charlie asked a family having a picnic. The little girl shook her head. "We didn't see any teddy bears."

Charlie felt even sadder. His second plan wasn't working either. He sat on the empty bench. The wood felt warm from the sun.

Just then, Charlie heard a small voice. "Help! Help me!"

Charlie's ears perked up. That sounded like Cocoa! But where was the voice coming from?

"I'm stuck!" the voice called again.

Charlie followed the sound. It led him to the big oak tree. He looked up. Way up high, a brown shape was caught in the branches.

"Cocoa!" Charlie neighed happily. "How did you get up there?"

"The wind blew me up here," Cocoa said. "I can't get down!"

Charlie tried jumping. But he couldn't reach that high. He tried climbing. But horses aren't good at climbing trees.

Then Charlie saw the park worker, Mr. Sam. Mr. Sam had a long ladder on his truck.

"Mr. Sam!" Charlie called. "Can you help me? My teddy bear is stuck in the tree!"

Mr. Sam looked up. He saw little Cocoa hanging from a branch. "Of course I can help," he said kindly.

Mr. Sam got his ladder. He climbed up carefully. The ladder made soft creaking sounds. He gently freed Cocoa from the branch.

"Here you go," Mr. Sam said. He handed Cocoa to Charlie.

Charlie hugged Cocoa tight. Cocoa felt soft and warm. He smelled like fresh air and leaves.

"Thank you so much!" Charlie said to Mr. Sam.

"You're welcome," Mr. Sam smiled. "Next time, maybe keep your teddy in a safe spot."

Charlie nodded. "I will!"

Charlie and Cocoa played together all day. They went down the slide. They swung on the swings. But this time, Charlie held Cocoa tight.

As the sun set, Charlie felt very happy. He had learned something important. When you have a problem, don't give up. Try different solutions. Ask for help when you need it.

"I'm so glad you're safe, Cocoa," Charlie whispered.

"Me too," Cocoa said. "Tomorrow let's play again!"

Charlie smiled. Tomorrow would be another wonderful day at the playground. And this time, he would keep his best friend close and safe.

The end.
