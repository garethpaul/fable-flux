# Zoe's Big Game

Zoe stood by the tall wooden fence in her backyard. The warm sun tickled her cheeks. She could hear her big brother Max laughing with his friends next door.

"Catch it, Sam!" Max shouted loudly.

Zoe peeked through the fence slats. The older kids were playing catch with a bright red ball. It bounced high in the air. Then it plopped down with a soft thud.

"Can I play too?" Zoe called out sweetly.

"You're too little," Max said quickly. "This game is for big kids only."

Zoe's shoulders drooped down low. Her bottom lip started to wobble. She felt very left out and sad.

She walked slowly to her sandbox. The sand felt cool between her fingers. But playing alone wasn't very fun today.

"I want to play catch too," Zoe whispered softly.

Then Zoe had a wonderful idea! She ran to the garage quickly. She found her own bouncy blue ball. It was smaller than Max's ball. But it was perfect for her hands.

"I'll make my own game!" Zoe said proudly.

She threw the ball against the fence. THUNK! It bounced back to her hands. She caught it with a big smile.

"This is fun!" Zoe giggled happily.

She threw the ball again and again. Each time, she caught it perfectly. The ball made a nice rhythm. THUNK, catch. THUNK, catch.

Soon, Zoe heard footsteps on the grass.

"What are you doing?" asked a gentle voice.

Zoe turned around quickly. Her neighbor Lucy was watching through the fence. Lucy was seven years old. She had curly brown hair and kind eyes.

"I'm playing my own catch game," Zoe explained proudly. "Want to see?"

Zoe threw her blue ball high. It bounced off the fence perfectly. She caught it with both hands.

"Wow! That looks really fun!" Lucy said excitedly. "Can you teach me how?"

Zoe's face lit up like sunshine. "Yes! Come to my yard!"

Lucy ran around the fence quickly. Soon she was standing in Zoe's backyard.

"First, you throw the ball here," Zoe pointed to a spot on the fence. "Then you catch it when it comes back."

Lucy tried throwing the ball. It bounced crooked and rolled away. Both girls giggled and chased after it.

"Try again!" Zoe said encouragingly.

Lucy threw the ball once more. This time it bounced straight back! She caught it with a happy squeal.

"I did it! I did it!" Lucy danced around excitedly.

More footsteps came across the grass. Max and his friend Sam appeared.

"What's all that laughing about?" Max asked curiously.

"We're playing fence ball!" Zoe announced proudly. "It's my new game!"

"Can we try?" Sam asked politely.

Zoe smiled her biggest smile ever. "Of course! Everyone can play fence ball!"

Soon all four children were taking turns. They threw the blue ball against the fence. They cheered when someone made a good catch. They helped each other when the ball rolled away.

"This is even better than regular catch!" Max said happily. "Good thinking, little sister!"

Zoe felt warm and proud inside. Her heart was full of joy. She had solved her problem all by herself!

As the sun started setting orange and pink, the children sat on the soft grass. They were tired but very happy.

"Tomorrow can we play fence ball again?" Lucy asked hopefully.

"Yes!" Zoe said with a bright grin. "And maybe we can make up more new games too!"

Max gave Zoe a gentle hug. "You're pretty smart for a little kid," he said kindly.

Zoe hugged him back tightly. She felt proud and happy. Sometimes the best games come from your own creative ideas. And sometimes being left out leads to something even better!

That night, Zoe fell asleep with a smile. She couldn't wait to play fence ball again tomorrow. Her backyard felt like the most magical place in the whole world.

The end.
