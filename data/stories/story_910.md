# Story story_910

**Rusty the Fox and the Big Bath Day**

Rusty the fox lived on Sunny Farm. He had bright orange fur and a fluffy tail. Rusty loved rolling in the dirt. He loved splashing in mud puddles. He loved digging holes under the apple trees.

But Rusty did NOT love taking baths.

One sunny morning, Farmer Mae walked to the barn. She saw Rusty covered in mud from nose to tail. His orange fur looked brown and messy.

"Oh my!" said Farmer Mae. "Rusty, you need a bath right now!"

Rusty's ears drooped down. His tail stopped wagging. A bath meant soap and water. A bath meant standing still. A bath meant getting all wet!

"No thank you," said Rusty softly. He tried to hide behind the big red barn.

But Farmer Mae had already filled a tub with warm water. She added sweet-smelling soap that made bubbles. The bubbles floated in the air like tiny clouds.

"Come here, little fox," called Farmer Mae. "The water is nice and warm."

Rusty shook his head. He ran to the chicken coop. Maybe he could hide there! The chickens clucked and flapped their wings. They did not want a muddy fox in their clean home.

"Shoo! Shoo!" clucked Henrietta the hen. "You are too dirty!"

Rusty felt sad. Even the chickens did not want him around. He walked slowly back to Farmer Mae.

"I have an idea," said Farmer Mae with a kind smile. "What if we make bath time fun?"

She brought out Rusty's favorite toy duck. The yellow duck squeaked when squeezed. Farmer Mae put the duck in the soapy water.

"Look! Ducky wants to swim!" said Farmer Mae.

Rusty watched the duck float in the bubbles. It did look fun. But he still felt scared.

"I don't like getting wet," whispered Rusty.

Farmer Mae nodded. "I understand. Let's try something different."

She got a soft, warm washcloth. She dipped it in the soapy water. Then she gently cleaned just one of Rusty's paws.

"How does that feel?" asked Farmer Mae.

Rusty looked surprised. "It feels... nice! The water is warm like sunshine."

Farmer Mae smiled. "Would you like to try putting just one more paw in the water?"

Rusty thought about it. The warm washcloth felt good. Maybe the water would feel good too. He carefully put one paw in the tub.

The water was warm and cozy! The bubbles tickled his fur. His paw felt clean and fresh.

"This is not so bad," said Rusty. He put his other front paw in the water.

Soon, Rusty was standing in the tub. The warm water felt wonderful on his muddy fur. The bubbles made him giggle. His toy duck bobbed up and down beside him.

"Quack! Quack!" squeaked the duck.

Rusty laughed. He splashed gently with his paws. The soap smelled like flowers and sunshine. Farmer Mae used a soft brush to clean his fur. It felt like getting a gentle hug.

"You are doing so well!" said Farmer Mae. "Look how clean and beautiful you are!"

Rusty looked at his reflection in the water. His fur was bright orange again! It was soft and fluffy. He looked like a completely different fox.

When bath time was over, Farmer Mae wrapped Rusty in a fluffy towel. She dried his fur until it was warm and cozy. Rusty felt fresh and clean and happy.

"Thank you, Farmer Mae," said Rusty. "I was scared, but you helped me try something new."

"Sometimes scary things turn out to be wonderful," said Farmer Mae. "You were very brave today."

Rusty wagged his tail. His clean fur sparkled in the sunlight. The chickens came over to say hello.

"You look wonderful!" clucked Henrietta. "And you smell like flowers!"

From that day on, Rusty still loved playing in the dirt. But he also loved bath time with his rubber duck and warm, bubbly water. He learned that trying new things could be fun, especially with a friend to help.

And every bath day became a happy adventure on Sunny Farm.

The end.
