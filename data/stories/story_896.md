# Story story_896

**The Magic Playtime**

Maya loved playtime at school. Every day after lunch, the children ran outside to play. They climbed on swings and slid down slides. They laughed and shouted with joy.

But Maya's little sister Emma was different. Emma was only three years old. She started at the big kid school this week. When playtime came, Emma sat on a bench. She watched the other children play. Her bottom lip stuck out in a sad frown.

"Come play with me, Emma!" Maya called out. She waved her hands in the air. "The sandbox is so much fun!"

Emma shook her head. "Too scary," she whispered. Her voice was tiny like a mouse. The playground looked huge to her small eyes. The swings moved too fast. The slide seemed too tall.

Maya felt sad for her sister. She wanted Emma to have fun too. Maya walked over to the quiet bench. She sat down next to Emma. The wooden seat felt warm from the sunshine.

"What if we start small?" Maya asked gently. She pointed to a patch of soft grass. "We could sit on the ground first."

Emma's eyes grew wide. She looked at the green grass. It looked safe and soft. She nodded her head slowly.

The two sisters walked to the grassy spot. They sat down together. The grass felt cool and tickly under their legs. Emma giggled a tiny bit.

"Feel this," Maya said. She picked up a dandelion. The yellow flower was bright like the sun. Emma touched the soft petals. They felt like velvet on her fingers.

"It's pretty," Emma said. Her voice was getting braver.

Maya blew on the dandelion. White fluffy seeds floated in the air. They danced like tiny fairies in the breeze. Emma's eyes sparkled with wonder.

"I want to try!" Emma said. Maya found another dandelion. Emma took a deep breath. She blew as hard as she could. More white seeds flew everywhere.

The sisters laughed together. The sound was like music in the air.

"Look what I found!" Maya said. She picked up a smooth, round stone. It was gray with white spots. "It looks like a cookie!"

Emma found a stick shaped like a letter Y. "This looks like a tree!" she said proudly.

Soon they were collecting treasures everywhere. Red leaves that crackled in their hands. Acorns that rolled like tiny balls. A feather that was soft and light.

"We can make a fairy house!" Maya suggested. Emma's face lit up like a Christmas tree.

They built a tiny house with their treasures. The stick made the walls. Leaves became the roof. The smooth stone was a table. The feather was a bed.

"The fairies will love it," Emma said. She was smiling now. Her cheeks were pink from the fresh air.

Other children came to see their creation. "That's so cool!" said Jack. "Can we help make another one?" asked Lily.

Soon five children were building fairy houses together. Emma was right in the middle, laughing and talking. She wasn't scared anymore. The playground felt friendly now.

"Time to go inside!" called their teacher.

Emma jumped up. "Can we play again tomorrow?" she asked Maya. Her eyes were bright and happy.

"Of course!" Maya said. She hugged her little sister tight. Emma smelled like sunshine and fresh air.

As they walked inside, Emma skipped a little. "I love playtime now," she said. "It's the best part of school!"

Maya felt warm and happy inside. She had helped her sister discover something magical. Sometimes the best games were the ones you made yourself.

That night at dinner, Emma told Mama and Papa all about the fairy houses. She talked about the dandelion seeds and the smooth stones. Her words tumbled out like water from a fountain.

"Tomorrow I'm going to find a purple leaf," Emma announced. "And maybe a shell if there is one!"

Maya smiled. She couldn't wait for tomorrow's playtime either. Playing with Emma made everything more fun. Sisters made the best playmates of all.

The end.
