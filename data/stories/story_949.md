# Story story_949

**Teddy and the Tall Fence**

Teddy was a small brown bear. He lived in a cozy cottage near the woods. Every morning, Teddy liked to explore.

Today felt special. The sun was warm on his fuzzy fur. Birds sang sweet songs in the trees. Teddy walked deeper into the forest than ever before.

Soon he heard wonderful sounds. Children were laughing and playing. Their voices sounded so happy! Teddy wanted to see what fun they were having.

He followed the joyful sounds. They led him to a tall wooden fence. The fence was much taller than Teddy. He could only see the brown wooden boards.

Teddy jumped up and down. He still couldn't see over the fence. The sounds of fun grew louder. Children were giggling and shouting with glee.

"I wonder what they're doing," Teddy said softly. His little ears twitched with curiosity.

He pressed his nose against the fence. It smelled like fresh pine wood. Through a tiny crack, he could see colorful movement. But he couldn't see the whole picture.

Teddy felt sad. His shoulders drooped down. "I'm too short," he sighed. "I'll never see what's happening."

Then Teddy had an idea. He looked around the forest floor. There were rocks scattered everywhere. Some were big and round.

"I'll stack rocks to make myself taller!" Teddy said excitedly.

He rolled a large gray rock to the fence. It was heavy work. Teddy pushed with all his might. His paws got dirty from the rough stone.

Next, he found a smaller rock. He tried to lift it onto the big rock. But his arms were too short. The rock kept slipping and falling.

Teddy tried again and again. Each time, the rocks tumbled down. They made loud crashing sounds. His fur was now covered in dust.

"This isn't working," Teddy said sadly. He sat down and felt like giving up.

Just then, he noticed something else. A fallen tree lay nearby. Its trunk was long and smooth. Thick branches stuck out like steps.

"Maybe I can climb this tree!" Teddy thought.

He dragged the fallen tree closer to the fence. It was hard work, but Teddy didn't give up. The bark felt rough under his paws.

Carefully, Teddy climbed onto the tree trunk. He stepped from branch to branch. Each step took him higher and higher.

His heart beat fast with excitement. "Almost there!" he whispered to himself.

Finally, Teddy reached the top branch. Now he was tall enough! He could see over the wooden fence.

What a wonderful sight met his eyes! Children were playing in a beautiful playground. They swung on swings that flew high in the air. They went down curvy slides that sparkled in the sunlight.

Some children played on a merry-go-round. It spun around and around with bright colors. Others climbed on jungle gyms that looked like castles.

Everyone was having so much fun! Their faces glowed with happiness. Teddy felt warm and joyful watching them play.

"How wonderful!" Teddy said with a big smile.

A little girl noticed Teddy peeking over the fence. She waved at him with both hands. "Hello, teddy bear!" she called out sweetly.

Other children saw him too. They all waved and smiled. "Come play with us!" they shouted together.

Teddy waved back with his furry paw. His heart felt full of happiness. Even though he couldn't join them today, seeing their joy made him feel wonderful.

He climbed down from his tree branch carefully. The walk home felt different now. Teddy felt proud and happy.

"I didn't give up," he said to himself. "When the rocks didn't work, I tried something else."

Back at his cottage, Teddy looked in the mirror. His fur was messy and dusty. But his eyes sparkled with pride.

That night, Teddy dreamed of swings and slides. He dreamed of making new friends. Most importantly, he dreamed of never giving up when things seemed too hard.

Tomorrow would bring new adventures. And Teddy would be ready for them all.

The end.
