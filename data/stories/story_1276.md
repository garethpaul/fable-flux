# Story story_1276

**Nutkin's Big Problem**

Nutkin the squirrel loved her new red shoes. They were shiny and bright. They made soft sounds on the park path. But there was one big problem. Nutkin could not tie her shoes.

Every morning, Nutkin's mom tied them tight. "There you go, sweetie," Mom would say. The laces stayed neat all day. But this morning was different. Mom had to leave early. "You're a big squirrel now," she said. "Try tying them yourself."

Nutkin looked down at her shoes. The laces were long and white. They flopped around like wiggly worms. "How hard can it be?" she said.

She grabbed one lace in each paw. She crossed them over. Then she pulled them tight. So far, so good! Next came the tricky part. She tried to make a loop. The lace slipped through her tiny claws. It fell in a messy pile.

"Oh no!" Nutkin said. She tried again. And again. Each time, the laces got more tangled. Soon they looked like a bird's nest. Nutkin's whiskers twitched with worry.

Her friend Pip the chipmunk scurried over. "What's wrong, Nutkin?" he asked. His cheeks were full of acorns. They made his voice sound funny.

"I can't tie my shoes," Nutkin said. Her tail drooped sadly. "The laces won't listen to me."

Pip looked at the messy knot. "Maybe you need help," he said. "Let's ask Mrs. Robin. She's very wise."

They found Mrs. Robin on her favorite branch. She was preening her blue feathers. They sparkled in the morning sun. "Good morning, little ones," she chirped.

"Mrs. Robin," Nutkin said, "can you teach me to tie my shoes? I keep making mistakes."

Mrs. Robin tilted her head. Her black eyes were kind. "Of course, dear. But first, let's untangle this mess."

Together, they loosened the knots. The laces were free again. Mrs. Robin flew down to the ground. "Now, watch carefully," she said.

"First, cross the laces like an X," Mrs. Robin explained. Nutkin did it perfectly. "Good! Now tuck one under and pull tight."

Nutkin's paws moved slowly. She tucked the lace under. She pulled it tight. The first part was done!

"Next comes the bunny ears," Mrs. Robin said. "Make one loop with this lace. Hold it tight with your left paw."

Nutkin made a small loop. It looked like a tiny ear. She held it carefully. Her paw was shaking a little.

"Now wrap the other lace around the loop," Mrs. Robin continued. "Push it through the hole you made."

This part was hard. Nutkin's tongue stuck out as she concentrated. The lace went around the loop. She found the small hole. Slowly, she pushed the lace through.

"Pull both loops tight!" Mrs. Robin said excitedly.

Nutkin pulled gently. The loops grew smaller. The knot got tighter. Suddenly, she had done it! A perfect bow sat on her shoe.

"I did it!" Nutkin squeaked. She jumped up and down. Her tied shoe stayed snug and neat. "I really did it!"

Pip clapped his tiny paws. "You're so smart, Nutkin!"

Mrs. Robin smiled proudly. "You didn't give up," she said. "That's the most important thing. When something is hard, we keep trying."

Nutkin practiced on her other shoe. This time was easier. Her paws remembered what to do. Soon both shoes were tied perfectly.

She ran around the park to test them. The shoes felt wonderful. They stayed tight and comfortable. The red leather gleamed in the sunshine.

"Thank you, Mrs. Robin," Nutkin said. "And thank you, Pip, for helping me find her."

"Remember," Mrs. Robin said, "asking for help is very brave. And practice makes everything easier."

From that day on, Nutkin tied her own shoes. Sometimes the bows were crooked. Sometimes they came loose. But she never gave up trying. And soon, she was the best shoe-tier in the whole park.

Every morning, Nutkin smiled at her red shoes. She remembered that with patience and practice, she could learn anything at all.

The end.
