# Story story_724

**Freddy's Missing Car**

Freddy the frog loved his bright red toy car. Every morning, he zoomed it across the smooth dining room floor. The little wheels made a soft rolling sound. Vroom, vroom!

But this morning was different. Freddy hopped into the dining room. He looked left. He looked right. Where was his special car?

"Oh no!" croaked Freddy. His big green eyes filled with tears. "My car is gone!"

The dining room felt too quiet without his car's happy sounds. Freddy's webbed feet felt cold on the wooden floor. He sniffed the air. It smelled like Mom's pancakes, but that didn't make him feel better.

Freddy decided to look under the big dining table. Maybe his car rolled there during yesterday's play. He hopped on his belly. He crawled under the dark space.

"Car, where are you?" he called softly.

Under the table, Freddy found dust bunnies. He found a lost sock. He found three cheerios. But no red car anywhere.

Freddy wiggled back out. His tummy felt sad and worried. He sat on the floor and thought hard. Where else could his car be?

Then Freddy remembered something important. Yesterday, he played a hiding game! Maybe he hid his car and forgot where.

"I need to check everywhere," said Freddy bravely.

First, he hopped to the tall wooden chairs. He climbed up each one with his sticky frog feet. The seats felt smooth and cool. He looked behind every cushion. No car.

Next, Freddy checked the big china cabinet. The glass doors reflected his green face. Inside, fancy plates sparkled in neat rows. But his car wasn't with the dishes.

Freddy's heart still felt heavy. But he didn't give up. Good problem-solvers keep trying, he told himself.

Then Freddy had a new idea. What if his car wasn't lost at all? What if it was just in a very good hiding spot?

He hopped to the corner where the tall plant lived. The green leaves hung down like a curtain. Freddy pushed them aside gently. The leaves felt soft and tickly on his face.

"Hello, plant," whispered Freddy. "Have you seen my car?"

Behind the biggest leaf, something red caught his eye. Freddy's heart jumped with excitement. There it was! His bright red car sat quietly behind the plant.

"My car!" shouted Freddy happily. He grabbed it with both hands. The metal felt cool and familiar. The wheels turned perfectly.

Freddy remembered now. Yesterday, he pretended his car was camping in the jungle. The plant was the perfect jungle hideout!

"I found you!" Freddy told his car. He gave it a gentle hug. The smooth paint felt wonderful against his green skin.

Freddy placed his car on the floor. He gave it a little push. Vroom! It rolled straight and fast, just like always. The wheels made their happy rolling sound again.

The dining room felt cheerful once more. Sunlight streamed through the windows. The wooden floor felt warm under Freddy's feet. Everything seemed brighter now.

"I'm so glad I didn't give up," said Freddy proudly. His chest puffed out with confidence.

Mom Frog hopped into the room. "Good morning, Freddy! I heard you calling for your car."

"I found it, Mom!" said Freddy excitedly. "It was hiding behind the plant. I looked everywhere until I found it!"

Mom smiled her biggest frog smile. "You're such a good problem-solver, Freddy. You didn't give up, even when it felt hard."

Freddy nodded happily. He learned something important today. When things seem lost, keep looking. Try different places. Think of new ideas. Never give up!

"Now let's play cars together," said Mom.

Freddy and Mom spent the whole morning racing cars around the dining room. Their laughter filled the sunny space. The red car rolled perfectly, making its happy vroom sounds.

Freddy felt proud and joyful. He solved his problem all by himself. What a wonderful morning it turned out to be!

The end.
