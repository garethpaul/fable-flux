# Story story_859

Anna sat on the soft carpet. She had many toys around her. There were colorful blocks and stuffed animals. A pretty doll with yellow hair smiled at her.

Her little brother Max crawled over. He reached for Anna's favorite red truck. "No!" Anna said loudly. She pulled the truck away. "Mine!"

Max's bottom lip started to shake. His eyes filled with tears. "Truck," he said softly.

"No sharing!" Anna hugged all her toys. She made a big circle around them with her arms.

Mom walked into the living room. She saw Max crying. She saw Anna holding tight to her toys. "What's wrong?" Mom asked gently.

"Anna won't share," Max sniffled.

Anna looked down at her toys. She felt funny in her tummy. Not a good funny. A worried funny.

"Sharing is hard sometimes," Mom said. She sat down on the floor. "But it can be fun too."

Anna shook her head. "My toys might break. Max might lose them."

Mom nodded. "I understand. Let's try something."

Anna thought for a moment. Maybe she could share just one toy. She picked up a small blue ball. It wasn't her favorite. She rolled it to Max.

Max smiled big. He rolled it back to Anna. But then he crawled toward her other toys again. Anna quickly grabbed them all.

"Mine!" she said again. The blue ball rolled under the couch.

Max started crying harder. Anna felt that funny feeling again. This time it felt worse.

Mom picked up Max. "Anna, how do you feel when someone shares with you?"

Anna remembered yesterday. Her friend Emma shared cookies. Anna felt happy and warm inside. She felt special.

"Good," Anna said quietly.

"How do you think Max feels right now?" Mom asked.

Anna looked at her brother's sad face. She looked at all her toys. She had so many. Max had none right now.

Anna had an idea. A better idea.

She picked up her second-favorite toy. It was a soft brown teddy bear. "Max," she called. "Want to play together?"

Max stopped crying. He looked at the teddy bear.

Anna placed it between them. "We can share Teddy. You hug him. Then I hug him."

Max crawled over carefully. He gently touched the bear's fuzzy ear. Anna helped him hold it.

"Soft," Max said. He smiled.

After a minute, Anna said, "My turn now." Max gave her the bear. Anna hugged it tight. Then she gave it back to Max.

They played this way for a while. Taking turns felt good. It felt fair.

Then Anna had another idea. She got her blocks. "Want to build?" she asked Max.

They built a tower together. Anna put on red blocks. Max put on blue ones. When it fell down, they both laughed.

The sound filled the whole living room. It was a happy sound.

"Look!" Anna called to Mom. "We're sharing!"

Mom clapped her hands. "You found a wonderful way to play together."

Anna felt proud and warm inside. Sharing wasn't scary after all. It was actually more fun than playing alone.

Max giggled as they built another tower. This one was even taller. Anna helped Max reach the top.

"Thank you, Anna," Max said.

"Thank you too," Anna replied. She meant it.

They played until dinnertime. They shared the blocks, the teddy bear, and even the red truck. Nothing broke. Nothing got lost.

When Dad came home, he found them playing happily. "What are you building?" he asked.

"Our castle!" Anna and Max said together.

That night at bedtime, Anna felt different. Happy different. Sharing made everything better. It made playing more fun. It made Max smile. It made her feel good inside.

"Tomorrow we can share again," Anna told Max.

Max clapped his little hands. "Share!" he said.

Anna smiled as she fell asleep. She couldn't wait for tomorrow. Sharing was the best game of all.

The end.
