# Story story_567

Tommy sat in the big green field behind his house. The soft grass tickled his bare feet. He threw his red ball up high and caught it. Up and down, up and down.

"I wish I had someone to play with," Tommy said sadly. His big sister was at school. His mom was busy inside. The field felt very quiet and empty.

A small blue bird watched Tommy from a nearby oak tree. The bird's bright yellow beak opened and closed. It wanted to chirp hello. The bird had been watching the lonely boy for days.

"Tweet, tweet!" called the little bird.

Tommy looked up. "Oh, hello there," he said softly. The bird's feathers sparkled in the warm sunshine. Its tiny black eyes looked friendly and curious.

The bird flew down closer. It landed on a fence post near Tommy. The wooden post felt rough under its small orange feet.

"Do you want to play?" Tommy asked hopefully. He rolled his red ball toward the bird. The ball made a soft whooshing sound through the grass.

The bird hopped down to the ground. It pecked gently at the ball with its beak. The ball rolled a little bit. Tommy giggled with delight.

"You do want to play!" Tommy clapped his hands together. The sound echoed across the quiet field.

Tommy rolled the ball again. This time, the bird pushed it back with its wing. The ball moved slowly through the green grass. Tiny daisies bent as it passed by.

"This is fun!" Tommy laughed. His lonely feeling started to melt away like ice cream in summer.

The bird chirped happily. It flapped its blue wings and flew in a circle. Tommy tried to run in a circle too. He stumbled and fell on the soft grass. Both friends laughed together.

Next, Tommy found a long stick. He drew pictures in the dirt. The bird watched carefully. It picked up small pebbles in its beak. The bird made its own patterns next to Tommy's drawings.

"You're very smart," Tommy told his new friend. The bird puffed up its chest proudly.

The warm sun moved across the sky. Fluffy white clouds drifted by slowly. A gentle breeze made the grass dance around them.

Tommy lay down on his back. The cool earth felt good against his skin. The bird perched on Tommy's tummy. Its feet felt light as feathers.

"I was so lonely before," Tommy whispered. "Now I have you."

The bird sang a sweet song. The melody floated through the air like magic. Tommy hummed along softly.

"Let's be best friends forever," Tommy said. He held out his finger. The bird stepped onto it carefully. Its tiny claws gripped gently.

Tommy's mom called from the house. "Tommy! Time for lunch!"

"I have to go eat," Tommy told the bird. "Will you come back tomorrow?"

The bird nodded its small head up and down. It flew to the oak tree and chirped goodbye.

Tommy ran toward his house. His heart felt full and happy. He wasn't lonely anymore. He had found a wonderful friend.

The next morning, Tommy rushed outside. The bird was waiting in the oak tree. It sang a cheerful good morning song.

"You came back!" Tommy shouted joyfully. He brought crackers to share. The bird pecked at the crumbs happily.

From that day on, Tommy and the little blue bird played together every day. They rolled the ball back and forth. They drew pictures in the dirt. They sang songs together.

The empty field became full of laughter and friendship. Tommy learned that friends can come in all shapes and sizes. Even a tiny bird can fill a big lonely heart.

The bird learned that humans can be kind and gentle. Not all big creatures are scary.

Together, they discovered that the best friendships happen when you open your heart. The green field became their special place. A place where a boy and a bird learned that nobody has to be lonely when friendship is near.

Every sunset, they promised to meet again tomorrow.

The end.
