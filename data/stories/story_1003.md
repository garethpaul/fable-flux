# Story story_1003

**Zelda's Lost Doll**

Zelda the zebra lived in a cozy attic room. She had black and white stripes that sparkled in the sunlight. Her favorite thing was her pretty pink doll named Rosie.

Every morning, Zelda brushed Rosie's soft hair. She dressed her in tiny clothes. They had tea parties together. Rosie made Zelda feel happy and safe.

One sunny Tuesday morning, Zelda woke up excited. "Time for breakfast, Rosie!" she called out cheerfully. But when she looked around her bed, Rosie was gone.

Zelda's heart felt heavy. Her eyes filled with tears. "Where is my Rosie?" she whispered sadly.

The attic felt too quiet without her special doll. Dust particles danced in the morning light streaming through the small window. Old boxes and trunks sat silently in the corners.

Zelda wiped her eyes with her striped hoof. "I must find Rosie," she said bravely. "She needs me."

First, Zelda looked under her soft blue blanket. She moved her pillow carefully. She checked behind her little wooden chair. No Rosie anywhere.

"Maybe she fell down," Zelda thought. She got on her knees and looked under the bed. It was dark and dusty down there. A spider scurried away quickly. But no pink doll.

Zelda felt worried. Her tummy had butterflies. But she didn't give up.

"I'll look in my toy box," she decided. Zelda opened the big wooden chest. Inside were blocks, crayons, and puzzle pieces. She moved everything around gently. Still no Rosie.

The morning sun climbed higher. The attic grew warmer. Zelda's stripes felt damp with worry-sweat.

"Think, Zelda, think," she said to herself. "Where did I play with Rosie yesterday?"

Suddenly, Zelda remembered something important. Yesterday evening, she had played hide-and-seek with Rosie. They had giggled and run around the attic together.

"The old trunk!" Zelda gasped. "We hid behind the big brown trunk!"

Zelda trotted quickly to the far corner. The old trunk was heavy and tall. It cast a long shadow on the wooden floor.

Zelda walked around the trunk slowly. Her hooves made soft clip-clop sounds. She looked left. She looked right.

Then she saw something wonderful. A tiny bit of pink fabric peeked out from behind the trunk.

"Rosie!" Zelda called out joyfully.

There was her precious doll, sitting quietly in the corner. Rosie's pink dress was a little dusty. Her brown hair was slightly messy. But she was perfectly safe.

Zelda scooped up Rosie in her hooves. She hugged her tight against her striped chest. "I found you!" she whispered happily.

Rosie felt warm and soft. She smelled like lavender soap from yesterday's bath time. Zelda's heart felt full of sunshine again.

"I was so worried about you," Zelda told her doll. "But I didn't stop looking. I kept trying until I found you."

Zelda carried Rosie to the sunny window. She brushed the dust from Rosie's pretty dress. She combed her soft hair with tiny fingers.

"You must have been scared in that dark corner," Zelda said gently. "But everything is okay now."

The attic felt cozy and bright again. Sunbeams made rainbow patterns on the walls. Birds chirped happy songs outside the window.

Zelda and Rosie had their morning tea party as always. They shared pretend cookies and laughed together. The scary morning felt far away now.

"I learned something important today," Zelda told Rosie. "When something is lost, I shouldn't give up. I should keep looking and trying new ideas."

Rosie seemed to smile in the warm sunlight.

That night, Zelda put Rosie safely on the nightstand. "Tomorrow we'll play together again," she promised. "But tonight, you'll stay right where I can see you."

Zelda fell asleep feeling proud and happy. She had solved her problem all by herself. Her precious Rosie was safe and sound.

The attic was peaceful once more.

The end.
