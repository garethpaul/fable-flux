# Story story_1083

Mama Bear walked slowly through the quiet woods. The tall trees swayed gently above her head. Sunlight danced through the green leaves. But Mama felt sad and lonely.

She picked up a smooth, round stone. "I wish I had someone to play with," she said softly. Mama threw the stone into the sparkling stream. Splash! The water made pretty circles.

Mama found a stick and drew pictures in the soft dirt. She drew a heart. She drew a flower. But playing alone wasn't very fun.

Little Lily Rabbit hopped nearby. Her fluffy white tail bounced with each hop. Lily's pink nose twitched as she smelled the sweet forest air. She heard Mama's quiet voice.

"Can I play too?" asked Lily in her tiny, cheerful voice.

Mama looked up with surprise. Her brown eyes grew bright and happy. "Oh yes! I would love that very much!"

Lily hopped closer. Her soft paws made no sound on the mossy ground. "What are you drawing?" she asked.

"Pictures in the dirt," said Mama. "Would you like to try?"

Lily nodded her small head up and down. Mama gave her the stick. Lily drew a wobbly circle. Then she drew three dots inside it.

"That's a happy face!" said Mama, clapping her big paws together.

The two new friends giggled and drew more pictures. They drew trees and clouds and butterflies. The warm sun felt good on their backs.

"Let's play hide and seek!" said Lily, bouncing excitedly.

Mama covered her eyes with her paws. "One, two, three..." she counted slowly.

Lily hopped behind a thick oak tree. Her heart beat fast with excitement. This was so much fun!

"Ready or not, here I come!" called Mama.

Mama looked behind rocks and bushes. She sniffed the air with her black nose. "Where could Lily be?" she wondered aloud.

A tiny giggle came from behind the oak tree. Mama smiled and pretended not to hear it.

"I wonder if she's behind this big tree," said Mama, walking very slowly.

"Found me!" squeaked Lily, hopping out with joy.

Now it was Mama's turn to hide. But where could a big bear hide? Lily looked everywhere. She checked under fallen logs. She peeked behind berry bushes.

Finally, Lily saw two brown ears sticking up behind a large rock. "I found you, Mama!" she called happily.

The friends played together all afternoon. They splashed in the cool stream. The water felt refreshing on their paws. They picked sweet, juicy berries and shared them. Purple juice stained their mouths.

"These taste like sunshine," said Lily, licking her lips.

They rolled down a grassy hill together. The soft grass tickled their bellies. They laughed until their sides hurt.

As the sun began to set, it painted the sky orange and pink. The forest grew quiet and peaceful.

"Thank you for playing with me," said Mama. "I was feeling very lonely today."

"I'm glad I found you," said Lily. "Playing together is much better than playing alone."

Mama gently patted Lily's soft head. "Would you like to be my friend?" she asked hopefully.

"Yes!" said Lily, her eyes sparkling like stars. "Best friends forever!"

The two friends hugged gently. Mama's warm, fuzzy arms wrapped around tiny Lily. It felt safe and cozy.

"Can we play again tomorrow?" asked Lily.

"Every day if you want to," said Mama with a big smile.

As they walked home through the darkening woods, fireflies began to twinkle around them. The forest felt magical and full of friendship.

Mama was no longer lonely. She had found something wonderful in the woods today. She had found a best friend.

Lily hopped alongside her new friend, her heart full of happiness. Tomorrow would bring another day of fun and friendship in their special woods.

The moon smiled down on the two friends as they said goodnight. Their friendship would last forever and ever.

The end.
