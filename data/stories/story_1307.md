# Story story_1307

Lily sat on her bed. Her shoes were by the door. Mom called from downstairs.

"Time for the park, Lily!"

Lily made a sad face. She did not want to go. The park seemed boring today. She wanted to stay home with her toys.

"I don't want to go," Lily said softly.

Mom came upstairs. She sat next to Lily. Her voice was gentle and kind.

"The fresh air will feel nice," Mom said. "The sun is shining bright today."

Lily dragged her feet. She put on her pink shoes slowly. Each step felt heavy and tired.

Outside, the warm sun touched her face. Birds sang happy songs overhead. The grass smelled fresh and clean.

But Lily still felt grumpy. She walked behind Mom. Her head hung down low.

At the park, other kids were playing. They laughed and ran around. The swings moved back and forth.

Lily sat on a bench. She watched a butterfly flutter by. Its wings were orange and black.

"Pretty butterfly," she whispered to herself.

The butterfly landed on a flower. Then it flew to another flower. And another one too.

Lily stood up slowly. She followed the butterfly carefully. It led her to the garden.

There were so many flowers! Red roses smelled sweet. Yellow daisies danced in the breeze. Purple violets grew near the fence.

The butterfly landed on Lily's hand. Its tiny feet tickled her skin. Lily giggled with pure delight.

"Hello, little friend," she said softly.

More butterflies came to visit. They flew around Lily's head. She felt like a flower princess.

A little boy walked over. His name was Sam. He had curly brown hair.

"Wow! The butterflies like you," Sam said.

"They're so pretty," Lily replied happily.

Sam and Lily watched together. They counted seven different butterflies. Each one had special colors.

"Want to play butterfly tag?" Sam asked.

Lily nodded with a big smile. They spread their arms wide. They pretended to be butterflies.

They fluttered around the garden. They landed on pretend flowers. They made buzzing and humming sounds.

Other kids joined their game. Soon five children were playing. They laughed and spun in circles.

Lily felt light and happy. The grumpy feeling was gone. Her heart felt warm inside.

They played for a long time. They found pretty rocks too. Lily put three in her pocket.

Mom called from the bench. "Five more minutes, Lily!"

Lily ran to the swings. The wind blew through her hair. She pumped her legs higher.

"Look how high I am!" she called.

The world looked different up high. She could see the whole park. Everything seemed magical and new.

Sam pushed her swing gently. "This is so fun!" he said.

When it was time to leave, Lily felt sad. But it was happy sad. She had made a friend.

"Can we come back tomorrow?" Lily asked.

Mom smiled and nodded. "Of course we can, sweetie."

Lily waved goodbye to Sam. She waved to the butterflies too. They seemed to wave back.

Walking home, Lily skipped happily. Her pocket rocks made soft sounds. The sun felt warm and nice.

"Thank you for taking me," Lily said.

She hugged Mom's hand tight. Mom squeezed back with love.

"The park was magical today," Lily said.

That night, Lily dreamed of butterflies. They danced in her sleep. She dreamed of swings too.

In the morning, Lily woke up early. She put on her shoes fast.

"Can we go to the park?" she asked.

Mom laughed at Lily's excitement. "After breakfast," she said kindly.

Lily ate her cereal quickly. She could hardly wait. The park was calling her name.

Today would be another adventure. Maybe the butterflies would come back. Maybe Sam would be there.

Lily smiled as she finished eating. Sometimes the best days start unexpectedly. Sometimes magic happens when you're open.

The park was waiting for her. And she was ready to play.

The end.
