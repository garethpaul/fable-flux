# Zoe and the Woodland Friend

Zoe sat on a mossy log in the quiet woods. She picked up fallen leaves one by one. Red leaves. Yellow leaves. Brown leaves too. She made a small pile beside her.

"I wish someone would play with me," Zoe sighed. Her voice echoed softly through the tall trees. The woods felt very big and empty.

Suddenly, a small brown bunny hopped out from behind a tree. The bunny had soft fur and twitching whiskers. Its little pink nose sniffed the air.

"Hello," said the bunny in a gentle voice. "What are you doing?"

Zoe looked up with surprise. "I'm collecting pretty leaves. But it's not very fun alone."

The bunny's ears perked up with excitement. "I love leaves too! Can I help you?"

Zoe smiled for the first time that day. "Yes, please! That would be wonderful."

The bunny hopped around quickly, gathering leaves. "Look at this orange one!" the bunny called out. "It looks like a tiny sun!"

Zoe giggled and picked up a yellow leaf. "This one looks like a butterfly!"

Together, they collected leaves of every color. Their pile grew bigger and bigger. Red, orange, yellow, and brown leaves covered the ground.

"What should we do with them?" asked Zoe.

The bunny's eyes sparkled with an idea. "Let's make leaf pictures! We can arrange them into shapes."

"That sounds amazing!" Zoe clapped her hands together.

They worked side by side on the forest floor. Zoe arranged red leaves into a heart shape. The bunny used yellow leaves to make a smiling sun.

"Your heart is beautiful," said the bunny.

"Your sun makes me feel warm and happy," Zoe replied.

Then they had another wonderful idea. Together, they made one big picture using all their leaves. Orange leaves became flower petals. Brown leaves formed a tree trunk. Yellow leaves made cheerful flowers.

"It's the most beautiful leaf picture ever!" Zoe exclaimed.

The bunny nodded happily. "We make a great team!"

Next, they decided to play hide-and-seek. The bunny was very good at hiding behind trees. Zoe was excellent at finding creative hiding spots.

"Ready or not, here I come!" called Zoe.

The bunny giggled from behind a big oak tree. When Zoe found the bunny, they both laughed with joy.

Then it was Zoe's turn to hide. She crouched behind some colorful bushes. The bunny found her quickly because Zoe was giggling too much.

"Playing together is so much more fun!" said Zoe.

"Everything is better with a friend," agreed the bunny.

As the afternoon sun began to set, they sat together on the mossy log. Their leaf picture lay spread out before them like a colorful carpet.

"I was feeling lonely before you came," Zoe admitted.

The bunny nodded. "I was looking for a friend too. I'm so glad I found you!"

They watched the golden sunlight dance through the tree branches. The woods didn't feel big and empty anymore. They felt cozy and full of friendship.

"Can we play again tomorrow?" asked Zoe hopefully.

"Yes!" said the bunny, ears wiggling with excitement. "We can build a fort with sticks!"

"And maybe collect acorns too!" added Zoe.

They hugged goodbye as the first stars appeared in the sky. Zoe felt warm and happy inside. The bunny hopped away with a joyful spring in each step.

As Zoe walked home through the woods, she looked back at their beautiful leaf picture. It reminded her of their wonderful day together.

"Tomorrow will be another great adventure," she whispered to herself.

The woods seemed to whisper back in agreement. The trees rustled gently in the evening breeze, as if they were happy too.

Zoe had learned something special that day. Playing alone could be nice, but playing with a friend was magical. Together, they could create beautiful things and have amazing adventures.

She couldn't wait to see her new bunny friend again. The woods would never feel lonely anymore.

The end.
