# Story story_1102

**Moobert's Big Problem**

Moobert the cow loved the library. Every Tuesday, she walked to town. Her black and white spots sparkled in the sunshine. She carried her favorite purple backpack.

But today was different. Moobert looked down at her feet. Her shoelaces were loose and floppy. They dragged on the ground like wiggly worms.

"Oh no!" said Moobert. "My shoes are untied!"

She tried to walk carefully. Step, step, step. But the long laces got tangled. They wrapped around her hooves like spaghetti.

THUMP! Moobert tripped and tumbled. Her backpack flew off. Books scattered everywhere. A red apple rolled across the sidewalk.

"Moo!" she said sadly. "I can't walk with messy laces."

Mrs. Robin, the librarian, heard the noise. She peeked out the big glass door. Her kind eyes sparkled behind round glasses.

"What happened, dear Moobert?" asked Mrs. Robin.

"My shoelaces won't stay tied," Moobert explained. "I keep tripping and falling."

Mrs. Robin smiled warmly. "Let's solve this together. Come inside where it's cozy."

The library smelled like old books and fresh cookies. Soft carpet felt good under Moobert's hooves. Colorful picture books lined the shelves like rainbow stripes.

Moobert sat in the reading corner. The big cushions were soft and squishy. She looked at her troublesome shoes. The laces seemed extra long and wiggly.

"I have an idea," said Moobert. "I'll tie them really, really tight!"

She grabbed the laces with her hooves. She pulled and tugged and twisted. The laces made a big, bumpy knot. It looked like a pretzel.

Moobert tried to stand up. But the knot was too tight! Her hooves felt squeezed and uncomfortable.

"Ow, ow, ow!" she said. "This hurts my feet!"

She sat back down with a heavy sigh. The tight knot made her toes tingle. This wasn't working at all.

Mrs. Robin noticed Moobert's sad face. "That knot looks very tight, sweetie."

"Too tight," agreed Moobert. "My hooves feel squished."

"Sometimes the first try doesn't work," said Mrs. Robin gently. "But that's okay. We can try again."

Mrs. Robin helped untie the tight, bumpy knot. Moobert's hooves felt much better. They wiggled her toes happily.

"Let me show you something special," said Mrs. Robin.

She pulled out a colorful picture book. The cover showed a little bunny learning to tie shoes. Inside were step-by-step pictures with simple words.

"Look here," said Mrs. Robin. "First, make two loops like bunny ears."

Moobert studied the pictures carefully. She made two small loops with her laces. They looked like floppy ears on a sleepy puppy.

"Now wrap one ear around the other," continued Mrs. Robin.

Moobert followed along slowly. Her tongue stuck out as she concentrated. The loops twisted together nicely.

"Pull both ears tight, but not too tight," said Mrs. Robin.

Moobert pulled gently. The laces made a perfect bow! It looked like a pretty butterfly sitting on her shoe.

"I did it!" mooed Moobert happily. "Look at my beautiful bow!"

She tied the other shoe the same way. Two perfect bows sat on her feet. They were snug but comfortable.

Moobert stood up carefully. She took one step, then another. The bows stayed tied perfectly. No more dragging laces!

"Thank you, Mrs. Robin!" said Moobert. "You helped me learn something new."

"You never gave up," said Mrs. Robin proudly. "That's what made you succeed."

Moobert walked around the library. Her steps were confident and steady. She picked out three picture books about farm animals.

As she left the library, Moobert felt proud and happy. Her shoes stayed tied all the way home. The afternoon sun felt warm on her spotted coat.

From that day on, Moobert tied her shoes every morning. Sometimes it took a few tries. But she always remembered Mrs. Robin's wise words.

When something seems hard, just try again. With practice and patience, you can learn anything!

And Moobert never tripped on her shoelaces again.

The end.
