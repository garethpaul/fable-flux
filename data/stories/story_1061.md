# Story story_1061

**Benny's Musical Flight**

Every day at three o'clock, Ms. Rosa rang the silver bell. "Music time!" she called to all the toys in the classroom. The teddy bears wiggled with joy. The dolls clapped their tiny hands. Even the toy cars honked their little horns.

But Benny the airplane sat quietly in the corner. His bright red wings drooped down low. His propeller turned very slowly. Benny did not like music time.

"Come join us, Benny!" called Ms. Rosa. Her voice was warm like sunshine. "We're going to sing and dance today."

Benny's engine made a soft puttering sound. "I don't know how to make music," he whispered. "Airplanes just fly. We don't sing or dance."

Ms. Rosa walked over to Benny. She knelt down beside him. "Every toy can make music in their own special way," she said gently.

The other toys gathered in a circle on the soft blue carpet. Bella the bear had maracas that went shake-shake-shake. Tommy the truck had a drum that went boom-boom-boom. The twin dolls, Lucy and Lily, had bells that went ting-a-ling-ting.

Benny watched from his corner. The music sounded happy and bright. But he still felt left out.

"I wish I could join them," Benny sighed. A small puff of air came from his propeller.

Suddenly, Ms. Rosa had an idea. Her eyes sparkled like stars. "Benny, what sound does your propeller make?"

Benny thought for a moment. "It goes whirrrr-whirrrr-whirrrr," he said, spinning his propeller faster.

"That's beautiful!" Ms. Rosa exclaimed. "And what about your engine?"

"It goes putt-putt-putt-putt," Benny said, his voice getting stronger.

Ms. Rosa clapped her hands together. "Those are wonderful musical sounds! Come join our band, Benny."

Benny felt nervous butterflies in his fuel tank. But he rolled slowly toward the circle. The other toys made room for him with big smiles.

"Let's all play together," said Ms. Rosa. She started counting. "One, two, three!"

Bella shook her maracas. Shake-shake-shake! Tommy beat his drum. Boom-boom-boom! The dolls rang their bells. Ting-a-ling-ting!

Benny took a deep breath. He spun his propeller and rumbled his engine. "Whirrrr-putt-putt-whirrrr!"

Something magical happened. All the sounds mixed together like colors in a rainbow. The music filled the room with joy. It bounced off the walls and danced in the air.

"You sound amazing, Benny!" cheered Bella.

"Your airplane music is so cool!" said Tommy.

Benny felt warm and happy inside. His red paint seemed to shine brighter. His propeller spun with excitement.

Ms. Rosa started a marching song. All the toys moved around the room together. Benny rolled in loops and figure-eights. His whirring and putting added the perfect airplane rhythm to their parade.

They played three more songs. Each time, Benny felt more confident. His sounds got louder and prouder. The other toys cheered for him.

When music time ended, Benny didn't want to stop. "That was the best music time ever!" he said, his propeller still spinning happily.

"Every toy brings something special to music," said Ms. Rosa. "Your airplane sounds made our band complete."

That evening, Benny could hardly sleep. He kept thinking about tomorrow's music time. He practiced his whirring and putting softly so he wouldn't wake the other toys.

The next day, when Ms. Rosa rang the silver bell, Benny was the first toy to roll to the carpet. "Music time!" he called out joyfully.

From that day on, Benny never missed music time. He learned that being different made him special, not left out. His airplane sounds became everyone's favorite part of their daily concerts.

And whenever a new toy felt shy about joining music time, Benny would roll over with a friendly smile. "Everyone can make music," he would say. "Even airplanes like me!"

The end.
