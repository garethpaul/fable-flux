# Woolly's Wonderful Nap Time

Woolly the sheep lived in a cozy blue house. Her bed had soft pink pillows. A warm yellow blanket covered her mattress.

"Nap time, Woolly!" called Mama Sheep.

Woolly's ears drooped down sadly. She didn't want to nap. The sun was still shining bright. Birds were singing outside her window. Her toys looked lonely on the floor.

"But Mama, I'm not sleepy yet," Woolly said. She rubbed her fluffy white wool. "Can I play just five more minutes?"

Mama Sheep smiled gently. "Nap time helps you grow strong. Your body needs rest to play later."

Woolly walked slowly to her bedroom. Her hooves made soft clicking sounds. She climbed onto her bed reluctantly. The pillow felt too warm. The blanket felt too heavy.

She tossed and turned restlessly. Left side, right side, on her back. Nothing felt comfortable at all. Woolly sighed a big, long sigh.

Then something magical started happening. As Woolly closed her eyes, she heard a gentle whooshing sound. The wind outside began singing softly. It sounded like a lullaby.

"Whoosh, whoosh, sleep little sheep," the wind whispered through her window.

Woolly's ears perked up curiously. She listened more carefully now. The birds weren't just chirping randomly. They were singing her a song! 

"Tweet, tweet, dream sweet dreams," sang the blue jay on her windowsill.

Even her toys seemed different. Her teddy bear looked peaceful. Her blocks were stacked like sleepy towers. Her dollhouse family was tucked in bed.

Woolly pulled her yellow blanket closer. It felt warm and snuggly now. Like a gentle hug from Mama. The pillow cradled her head perfectly.

She noticed sunbeams dancing on her wall. They made pretty golden patterns. Like butterflies made of light. The shadows swayed back and forth gently.

"This is actually quite nice," Woolly whispered to herself.

Her breathing became slow and steady. In and out, in and out. Her woolly body felt heavy. But it was a good heavy. Like sinking into a cloud.

Woolly began to dream beautiful dreams. She dreamed of running through meadows. Green grass tickled her hooves. She dreamed of playing with friends. They laughed and danced together happily.

In her dream, she was the fastest sheep ever. She could jump over rainbows. She could fly through cotton candy clouds. Everything was possible in dreamland.

When Woolly woke up, she felt wonderful. Her eyes sparkled with energy. Her wool felt extra fluffy. Her legs felt strong and ready.

"Mama!" she called excitedly. "I had the best nap ever!"

Mama Sheep peeked into the room. "You look so refreshed, little one."

"I dreamed about flying and jumping," Woolly said, bouncing on her bed. "And the wind sang to me. The birds sang too!"

"Nap time is magical, isn't it?" Mama said, giving Woolly a hug.

Woolly nodded enthusiastically. "The sunbeams danced on my wall. My blanket felt like a hug. Everything was so peaceful and pretty."

They walked to the kitchen together. Woolly felt ready for anything now. She had energy for playing. She had energy for helping Mama. She had energy for exploring outside.

"Can we read a story now?" Woolly asked, feeling bright and happy.

"Of course, my well-rested lamb," Mama replied.

As bedtime approached that evening, Woolly thought about tomorrow. She was already excited for nap time! She couldn't wait to hear the wind's lullaby again. She wanted to see more dancing sunbeams.

"Mama," Woolly said as Mama tucked her in. "Tomorrow's nap time will be wonderful too, won't it?"

"Every nap time can be magical," Mama said, kissing Woolly's forehead. "When you rest with a peaceful heart."

Woolly closed her eyes happily. She had discovered something special today. Nap time wasn't boring at all. It was a daily adventure! A time for dreams and rest. A time for her body to grow strong.

Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough. Woolly drifted off to sleep, already dreaming of her next magical nap time adventure.

The end.
