# Story story_784

**Grandpa's Big Problem**

Grandpa sat in his favorite chair at the big dining room table. The morning sun made everything bright and warm. He loved eating breakfast here every day.

But today was different. Today there was a problem.

The salt shaker was empty. Grandpa shook it and shook it. Not one tiny grain came out. His scrambled eggs needed salt. They tasted so plain without it.

Grandpa looked around the dining room. The walls were painted soft yellow like butter. Family pictures smiled down at him. The wooden table felt smooth under his hands.

He could hear Grandma humming in the kitchen. She was washing dishes. The water made splashing sounds. Pots and pans clinked together.

Grandpa wanted to ask for help. But his cheeks felt hot and red. His tummy felt funny inside. He was too shy to call out.

"I can fix this myself," he whispered.

Grandpa stood up slowly. His knees made little creaking sounds. He walked to the kitchen doorway and peeked around the corner.

Grandma was busy at the sink. Soap bubbles floated in the air. They looked like tiny rainbows in the sunlight.

Grandpa opened his mouth to speak. But no words came out. His throat felt tight. He tiptoed back to his chair.

"Maybe I can find salt somewhere else," he said to himself.

Grandpa searched the dining room. He opened the pretty wooden cabinet. Inside were fancy plates and cups for special days. But no salt.

He looked in all the drawers. One had soft cloth napkins that smelled like lavender. Another had silver spoons that sparkled. Still no salt.

His eggs were getting cold. They looked sad and lonely on his blue plate.

Grandpa sat back down. He felt worried and frustrated. His shoulders drooped down low.

Then he heard footsteps. Grandma walked into the dining room. She carried a steaming cup of coffee. The smell was rich and warm.

"Good morning, dear," she said with a big smile. "How are your eggs?"

Grandpa's face turned pink. He pointed at the empty salt shaker. His voice came out very quiet.

"The salt is all gone," he whispered.

Grandma's eyes grew wide. "Oh my! Let me help you right away!"

She hurried to the kitchen. Her slippers made soft patting sounds on the floor. Soon she returned with a full salt shaker.

"Here you go," she said gently. She sprinkled salt on his eggs. "Is that better?"

Grandpa took a big bite. The eggs tasted perfect now. Warm and fluffy and just right.

"Thank you," he said. His voice was stronger now. "I was too shy to ask."

Grandma sat down beside him. She patted his hand with her soft fingers.

"You never have to be shy with me," she said. "I love helping you. That's what families do."

Grandpa smiled. His whole face felt warm and happy. The worried feeling in his tummy went away.

"Next time I'll ask right away," he promised.

Grandma laughed. It sounded like silver bells ringing. "Good! And I'll make sure we always have plenty of salt."

They ate breakfast together. The dining room felt cozy and perfect. Sunlight danced on the table. Birds sang pretty songs outside the window.

Grandpa learned something important that day. Asking for help isn't scary. The people who love you want to help. They feel happy when they can make things better.

From that day on, Grandpa never felt too shy to ask Grandma for help. When he needed anything at all, he would speak up with a clear, strong voice.

And Grandma always had a smile ready. She loved taking care of her dear Grandpa.

The dining room became an even happier place. It was filled with love, laughter, and perfectly salted eggs every single morning.

The end.
