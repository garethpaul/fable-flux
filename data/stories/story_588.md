# Story story_588

Jack sat on the soft green hill. The warm sun touched his face. He rolled his red ball down the slope. It bounced and tumbled in the grass.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," Jack said softly. His voice sounded small in the big open space.

The hill was quiet except for chirping birds. Jack picked up his ball and rolled it again. He watched it roll all the way to the bottom. Then he walked down to get it.

Behind a big oak tree, something moved. Jack stopped walking. His heart beat fast.

A golden lion stepped out slowly. The lion had a fluffy mane and kind brown eyes. Jack had never seen a real lion before.

"Hello," said the lion in a gentle voice. "I'm Leo. I live on this hill."

Jack felt scared at first. But Leo's voice was warm like hot chocolate. "I'm Jack," he whispered.

"What are you playing?" Leo asked. His tail swished back and forth in the tall grass.

"Just rolling my ball," Jack said. "But it's not very fun alone."

Leo's eyes lit up. "Can I play too? I love to play!"

Jack thought for a moment. Leo seemed very nice. "Okay," Jack said with a small smile.

Leo bounded over to the ball. His big paws were soft on the ground. "Let's roll it together!"

Jack and Leo pushed the red ball up the hill. It was much easier with two friends working together. At the top, they let it go.

The ball rolled fast down the slope. It spun and bounced over rocks and flowers. Jack and Leo laughed as they chased it.

"This is so much fun!" Jack giggled. His lonely feeling was melting away like ice cream in sunshine.

They played catch next. Leo was very gentle. He used his soft mouth to pick up the ball. Then he tossed it carefully to Jack.

"You're good at this!" Jack said. Leo's tail wagged like a happy dog.

The sun felt warm on their backs. The grass smelled sweet and fresh. Butterflies danced around them in the breeze.

"Let's play hide and seek," Leo suggested. His whiskers twitched with excitement.

Jack hid behind the oak tree first. Leo counted to ten with his eyes closed tight. "Ready or not, here I come!"

Leo found Jack quickly. Then it was Leo's turn to hide. But hiding was hard for a big lion! Jack could see Leo's golden tail sticking out from behind a small bush.

"I found you!" Jack laughed. Leo pretended to be surprised.

They rolled down the hill together. The soft grass tickled their skin. They tumbled and laughed until they reached the bottom.

"I was lonely before," Jack said as they lay in the grass. "I didn't have any friends to play with."

"I was lonely too," Leo admitted. "I watched other animals play together. But they were afraid of me."

Jack looked at Leo's kind face. "You're not scary at all. You're the best friend ever!"

Leo's whole face smiled. "You're my best friend too, Jack!"

They spent the whole afternoon playing. They raced up and down the hill. They made flower crowns from dandelions. They shared Jack's apple snack.

As the sun began to set, it painted the sky orange and pink. The hill looked magical in the golden light.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" Leo asked hopefully.

"Yes!" Jack said right away. "We can play every day!"

They hugged goodbye. Leo's fur was warm and soft. Jack felt happy all the way down to his toes.

Walking home, Jack couldn't stop smiling. He had found the best friend in the whole world. The hill didn't feel lonely anymore.

Tomorrow would bring new games and adventures. Jack and Leo would laugh and play together again.

Sometimes the best friendships come from the most unexpected places. And sometimes, all you need is someone who wants to play too.

Jack skipped all the way home, already planning tomorrow's fun with his new friend Leo.

The end.
