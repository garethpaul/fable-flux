# Story story_689

**Tommy Truck's Art Adventure**

The morning sun peeked through the classroom windows. Miss Rosa clapped her hands together. "Art time, everyone!" she called cheerfully.

All the toy vehicles rolled to the art table. Red Race Car zoomed over first. Blue Bus rolled up with a happy honk. Yellow Bulldozer pushed forward eagerly.

Tommy Truck stayed in the corner. His big wheels felt heavy. His dump bed drooped low.

"Come on, Tommy!" called Red Race Car. "Art time is starting!"

Tommy's engine made a quiet rumble. "I don't think I'm good at art," he whispered. "My wheels are too big. My body is too bumpy."

Miss Rosa walked over to Tommy. She knelt down beside him. "What's wrong, little truck?" she asked gently.

"Art is for pretty things," Tommy said sadly. "I'm just a big, dirty truck."

Miss Rosa smiled warmly. "Oh, Tommy. Art is for everyone. Would you like to try something special today?"

Tommy looked up with curious headlights. "What kind of special?"

Miss Rosa brought out a large sheet of paper. She placed it on the floor. Then she squeezed bright paint into shallow trays.

"Today we're making tire track art!" she announced.

Tommy's headlights flickered with interest. "Tire track art?"

"Roll your wheels through the paint," Miss Rosa explained. "Then drive across the paper. Make beautiful patterns!"

Red Race Car went first. His thin tires made delicate lines. Blue Bus created wide, smooth stripes. Yellow Bulldozer left thick, strong marks.

Tommy watched carefully. The colors looked so bright. The patterns looked so pretty.

"Your turn, Tommy," Miss Rosa said kindly.

Tommy rolled forward slowly. He dipped his front wheels in blue paint. The paint felt cool and smooth.

He rolled onto the paper. His wheels left two perfect blue lines. Tommy's engine hummed with surprise.

"Look at that!" exclaimed Blue Bus. "Those lines are so straight and strong!"

Tommy felt a warm feeling in his engine. He rolled to the red paint next. Now he had red tracks following his blue ones.

"Try making curves!" suggested Red Race Car.

Tommy turned his wheels left and right. Beautiful curved lines appeared on the paper. Red and blue swirled together.

"This is fun!" Tommy honked happily.

He rolled through yellow paint. Then green paint. Then purple paint. His big wheels left wonderful tracks everywhere.

The other vehicles cheered. "Amazing patterns, Tommy!" they called.

Tommy backed up to see his artwork. Colorful tire tracks crisscrossed the paper. Some lines were straight. Some were curvy. Some made circles and loops.

"It looks like a rainbow road!" gasped Yellow Bulldozer.

Miss Rosa clapped her hands. "Tommy, this is beautiful! Your tracks tell a story of adventure."

Tommy's headlights glowed brightly. His dump bed lifted high with pride. "I made real art!" he said joyfully.

"You sure did," said Miss Rosa. "Your wheels create something no one else can make."

Red Race Car rolled over to Tommy. "Your art is the best kind," he said. "It's strong and colorful, just like you!"

Tommy beamed. His engine purred happily. "I love my tire tracks!"

The friends spent the rest of art time creating together. They made a giant rainbow road across three big papers. Tommy's tracks were the foundation for everyone else's designs.

When art time ended, Tommy didn't want to stop. "Can we do more tomorrow?" he asked hopefully.

"Of course!" laughed Miss Rosa. "We can try finger painting next."

Tommy's eyes widened. "But I don't have fingers!"

"You have a dump bed," Miss Rosa winked. "We can fill it with paint and tip it gently."

Tommy honked with excitement. "I can't wait!"

As the toys cleaned up, Tommy looked at his colorful wheels. Paint still decorated his tires like pretty socks.

"Tomorrow will be another great art day," he said happily.

Tommy had learned something wonderful. Art wasn't about being perfect or pretty. Art was about being yourself and having fun.

And Tommy Truck was very good at being himself.

The end.
