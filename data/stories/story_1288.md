# Story story_1288

Zoe sat on the red park bench. She swung her legs back and forth. Her mom opened the lunch box.

"Time to eat, sweetie," Mom said with a smile.

Zoe peeked inside the box. She saw her favorite peanut butter sandwich. She saw crunchy apple slices. She saw orange crackers shaped like fish.

Then she saw the green broccoli trees.

"Yuck!" Zoe said. She wrinkled her nose. "I don't like green things."

The broccoli looked like tiny trees. They smelled funny. Zoe pushed them to one side of her box.

"Vegetables help you grow big and strong," Mom explained. "They taste good too."

Zoe shook her head. "No thank you."

She ate her sandwich happily. The peanut butter was smooth and sweet. She crunched the apple slices. They were juicy and cold.

But the broccoli sat there. Green and unwanted.

A squirrel ran up to their bench. It had fluffy gray fur and a bushy tail. The squirrel looked at Zoe with bright black eyes.

"Look, Mom!" Zoe pointed. "A squirrel friend!"

The squirrel sniffed the air. It came closer to the lunch box.

Zoe had an idea. "Maybe my squirrel friend wants the broccoli."

She picked up a green piece. She held it out to the squirrel.

The squirrel grabbed the broccoli quickly. Crunch, crunch, crunch! It ate the whole piece.

"See?" Zoe said proudly. "The squirrel likes vegetables!"

But Mom shook her head gently. "That's nice, Zoe. But you still need to eat your vegetables too."

Zoe frowned. Her first plan didn't work.

She watched the squirrel run back to a big oak tree. It climbed up the rough brown bark. High in the branches, Zoe saw something special.

A bird's nest sat on a thick branch. Baby birds poked their heads up. They opened their tiny beaks wide.

"Chirp! Chirp! Chirp!" they called.

The mama bird flew down to the grass. She picked up seeds and small bugs. She flew back up to feed her babies.

Zoe watched with wonder. "Look how the mama bird feeds her babies."

"Yes," Mom said. "She gives them good food to help them grow."

Zoe thought about this. The baby birds ate what their mama gave them. They trusted her to pick good food.

Zoe looked at her mom's kind face. Mom always took care of her. Mom always chose good things for her.

"Mom," Zoe said slowly. "Do you really think vegetables taste good?"

"I do," Mom nodded. "Want to try a tiny bite with me?"

Zoe felt nervous. But she trusted her mom.

"Okay," she whispered. "Just a tiny bite."

Mom picked up a small broccoli piece. She took a bite first. "Mmm, crunchy!"

Zoe took the tiniest bite ever. She chewed slowly. The broccoli was crunchy like Mom said. It tasted fresh and green. It wasn't scary at all.

"It's not yucky!" Zoe said with surprise.

She took another small bite. Then another. Soon she had eaten three whole pieces.

"I'm proud of you for trying," Mom said. She gave Zoe a warm hug.

Zoe felt proud too. She had been brave. She had tried something new.

The squirrel came back to their bench. Zoe shared one more piece with her furry friend.

"Now we both eat vegetables!" she told the squirrel.

As they packed up lunch, Zoe felt happy. Her tummy was full of good food. She had made a squirrel friend. She had been brave like the baby birds.

"Can we bring carrots next time?" Zoe asked.

Mom smiled big. "Of course we can."

Zoe skipped to the playground. She felt strong and ready to play. Maybe vegetables weren't so bad after all.

Sometimes trying new things was scary. But sometimes scary things turned out to be wonderful.

Zoe couldn't wait for tomorrow's lunch.

The end.
