# Jack and the Missing Doll

Jack sat on her soft pink bed. She looked around her cozy bedroom. The morning sun made everything bright and cheerful. But Jack felt sad.

"Where is my pretty doll?" Jack whispered. Her favorite doll, Rosie, was missing. Rosie had curly yellow hair. She wore a sparkly blue dress. Jack loved Rosie very much.

Jack's eyes filled with tears. "Mama!" she called. "I can't find Rosie!"

Mama came into the room. She gave Jack a warm hug. "Don't worry, sweetie," Mama said gently. "Let's look for Rosie together."

"But where could she be?" Jack sniffled.

"Sometimes things hide in funny places," Mama smiled. "Let's be detectives!"

Jack wiped her eyes. She felt a little better. "Okay, Mama. Let's find Rosie!"

First, Jack looked under her bed. She got down on her hands and knees. She pushed aside her slippers. She moved her toy box. No Rosie there.

"Hmm," said Jack. "Maybe she's hiding somewhere else."

Next, Jack checked her toy chest. She lifted out her blocks. She moved her stuffed bear. She found her red ball. But no pretty doll.

Jack's lip started to wobble again. "She's really gone, Mama."

"Don't give up yet," Mama encouraged. "Good detectives keep looking."

Jack took a deep breath. She walked to her closet. She pushed aside her dresses. She looked behind her shoes. Still no Rosie.

"This is hard work," Jack sighed. But she didn't stop looking.

Jack remembered playing yesterday. She had taken Rosie to the living room. Maybe Rosie was still there!

"Come on, Mama!" Jack said excitedly. They walked to the living room together.

Jack looked behind the big couch cushions. She checked under the coffee table. She even looked in the magazine basket. No luck.

Jack's shoulders drooped. "I miss Rosie so much."

"I know, honey," Mama said softly. "But you're being very brave. Let's try one more place."

They went back to Jack's bedroom. Mama pointed to the window seat. "Have you checked there?"

Jack shook her head. She walked over slowly. She lifted up the fluffy cushion.

"ROSIE!" Jack shouted with joy.

There was her beautiful doll! Rosie's yellow curls sparkled in the sunlight. Her blue dress looked perfect. Jack scooped up Rosie and hugged her tight.

"I found you!" Jack laughed. "I missed you so much!"

Jack spun around with Rosie. She felt so happy and proud. "Mama, I found her! I didn't give up!"

"You did such a good job," Mama praised. "You kept trying even when it was hard."

Jack sat on her bed with Rosie. She smoothed the doll's pretty dress. "I was scared you were lost forever," she whispered to Rosie.

"Sometimes things seem lost," Mama explained. "But if we keep looking and don't give up, we usually find them."

Jack nodded wisely. "Like being a detective!"

"Exactly like being a detective," Mama agreed.

Jack gave Rosie another big squeeze. "I won't lose you again," she promised.

Mama tucked both Jack and Rosie into bed. "You learned something important today," she said.

"What did I learn?" Jack asked curiously.

"You learned that trying again and again helps us solve problems," Mama explained. "Even when things seem impossible."

Jack smiled brightly. She felt proud and strong. "I'm a good problem-solver!"

"The very best," Mama agreed. She kissed Jack's forehead.

Jack closed her eyes. She held Rosie close. The doll felt warm and safe in her arms. Tomorrow would bring new adventures. But tonight, Jack felt happy and confident.

She had solved her big problem. She had found her precious doll. Most importantly, she had learned to never give up.

"Good night, Rosie," Jack whispered sleepily. "Sweet dreams."

The room grew quiet and peaceful. Jack drifted off to sleep, smiling. Her pretty doll was safe beside her. Everything was perfect once again.

The end.
