# Story story_1497

**Bouncy and Max**

In the sunny playground, a bright red ball sat all alone. His name was Bouncy. He rolled back and forth on the soft grass. The warm sun made his shiny surface sparkle.

Bouncy watched children play on the swings. He saw them laugh on the slide. But nobody wanted to play with him today. All the children had gone home for lunch.

"I wish I had a friend," Bouncy said sadly. He bounced once, very softly. The sound echoed in the empty playground.

High up in the big oak tree, a little monkey was watching. His name was Max. Max had fuzzy brown fur and bright, curious eyes. He swung from branch to branch with his long tail.

Max saw the lonely ball below. The ball looked so sad sitting there by himself. Max's kind heart felt sorry for Bouncy.

"Hello down there!" called Max from his tree branch. His voice was cheerful and friendly.

Bouncy looked up in surprise. "Oh! Hello, monkey! I'm Bouncy."

"I'm Max," said the monkey with a big smile. "You look lonely. Would you like to play?"

Bouncy's red surface seemed to glow brighter. "Really? You want to play with me?"

Max swung down from the tree. His feet landed softly on the playground grass. "Of course! Playing is my favorite thing to do."

"But I can only bounce and roll," said Bouncy. "Is that fun enough?"

Max clapped his small hands together. "That sounds perfect! I love to catch and throw."

Max gently picked up Bouncy. The ball felt smooth and warm in his furry hands. Max tossed Bouncy high into the air.

"Wheee!" laughed Bouncy as he flew through the blue sky. The wind tickled his round sides.

Bouncy bounced back down. Max caught him perfectly. They both giggled with joy.

"Again! Again!" called Bouncy happily.

Max threw the ball higher this time. Bouncy spun and twirled in the sunshine. His red color looked beautiful against the white clouds.

They played catch for a long time. Max would throw Bouncy to the slide. Bouncy would bounce off and roll back. Then Max would scoop him up and throw him again.

"Let's try something new," said Max. He rolled Bouncy toward the sandbox.

Bouncy rolled fast across the smooth playground. The feeling of rolling made him dizzy with happiness. Max ran alongside him, laughing.

At the sandbox, they invented a new game. Max would bury his hands in the cool sand. Then Bouncy would roll over and knock the sand away.

"This is the best day ever!" said Bouncy. His voice was full of joy.

The warm afternoon sun began to set. Golden light painted the playground orange and pink. Both friends were tired but very happy.

"Max, thank you for playing with me," said Bouncy. "I was so lonely before."

Max sat down next to his new friend. He patted Bouncy's smooth surface gently. "I was lonely too. Now we both have a friend."

"Will you come back tomorrow?" asked Bouncy hopefully.

"Every day!" promised Max. "We can play catch and roll and make up new games."

Bouncy bounced with excitement. "We'll have so much fun together!"

As the stars began to twinkle, Max climbed back up his tree. "Good night, Bouncy. Sweet dreams!"

"Good night, Max. Thank you for being my friend!"

Bouncy settled into the soft grass under the tree. He wasn't lonely anymore. Tomorrow would bring another day of fun with his best friend Max.

The playground was quiet now. But it was a happy quiet. Two friends had found each other. And that made everything perfect.

From that day on, Bouncy and Max played together every single day. Their friendship grew stronger with each game they shared. The playground was never lonely again.

The end.
