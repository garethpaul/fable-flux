# Stella the Sheep and the Missing Doll

High on a grassy green hill lived Stella the sheep. She had soft, fluffy white wool and kind brown eyes. Every morning, Stella loved to play with her favorite toy. It was a pretty pink doll named Rosie.

"Good morning, Rosie!" Stella would say each day. She would brush the doll's silky hair. She would fix the tiny blue dress. Then they would roll down the hill together, laughing and spinning in the warm sunshine.

But one windy morning, something terrible happened. Stella woke up and looked around her cozy spot. She looked under the big oak tree. She looked behind the smooth gray rocks. Rosie was nowhere to be found!

"Oh no!" cried Stella. "Where is my pretty doll?" Her fluffy tail wiggled with worry. Her heart felt heavy and sad.

The wind had blown very hard all night long. It whistled through the trees. It made the grass dance and sway. Maybe the wind had taken Rosie away!

Stella decided to search everywhere. She would not give up. She loved Rosie too much to stop looking.

First, Stella walked to the bottom of the hill. Her hooves made soft *tap-tap-tap* sounds on the dirt path. She looked behind every purple flower. She peeked under every fallen leaf. But Rosie was not there.

"I must keep trying," Stella said to herself. She felt determined and brave.

Next, Stella climbed to the very top of the hill. The wind was stronger up there. It made her wool flutter like tiny white clouds. She looked in the tall, swaying grass. She checked near the old wooden fence. Still no Rosie.

Stella's eyes filled with tears. But she took a deep breath. "I won't give up," she whispered.

Then Stella had a new idea. Maybe Rosie had rolled somewhere during the night. Stella began to follow a winding path around the hill. She looked carefully at every spot.

*Rustle, rustle* went the leaves as she walked. *Chirp, chirp* sang the happy birds above. The sounds made Stella feel a little better.

Suddenly, Stella heard a tiny voice. "Help me! Help me!" it seemed to say.

Stella's ears perked up. She followed the sound to a thick, green bush. The bush had gotten very tangled in the strong wind. Its branches were twisted together like a puzzle.

"Rosie? Is that you?" called Stella.

"Yes! I'm stuck!" came the small voice.

Stella's heart jumped with joy! She had found her beloved doll! But now she needed to get Rosie out of the tangled bush.

Very gently, Stella used her teeth to move the branches. She was careful not to tear Rosie's pretty dress. Slowly, carefully, she worked and worked.

"Almost there!" Stella said, breathing hard.

Finally, *pop!* Rosie came free! Her pink dress was a little wrinkled. Her silky hair was messy. But she was safe and sound.

"Oh, Rosie!" Stella cried with happiness. She hugged her doll tight. "I missed you so much!"

"Thank you for not giving up," said Rosie. "You were so brave and smart!"

Stella felt proud and happy. She had solved the problem all by herself. Even when it seemed hard, she kept trying different ways to find Rosie.

Together, they walked back to their favorite spot under the oak tree. The sun felt warm on Stella's wool. The grass smelled sweet and fresh. Everything was perfect again.

"I learned something important today," Stella told Rosie as they settled down. "When something seems lost, I should keep looking. I should try different places and different ways. I should never give up on something I love."

Rosie smiled her tiny doll smile. "And I learned that you're the best friend ever!"

As the sun set behind the hill, Stella and Rosie snuggled together. Tomorrow would bring new adventures. But tonight, they were just happy to be together again.

Stella closed her eyes and felt grateful. She had been persistent and brave. She had solved her problem. And most importantly, she had her dear friend back safe and sound.
