# Story story_865

**Jack's Big Problem**

Jack loved the library. It smelled like old books and crayons. Today was special toy day. Children could borrow toys just like books.

Jack's eyes grew wide. So many wonderful toys! There were soft teddy bears and colorful blocks. Shiny cars sat next to puzzle boxes. A red fire truck caught his eye.

"I want them all!" Jack whispered.

He picked up the fire truck. It was heavy and cool in his hands. Then he grabbed a fuzzy brown bear. The bear was soft and squishy.

Jack saw wooden blocks in bright colors. He needed those too! He tucked them under his arm. A puzzle with farm animals looked perfect. He balanced it on top.

More toys called to him. A green dinosaur. A book about cats. Markers in every color. Jack kept picking up more and more.

His arms started to shake. The toys felt heavier and heavier. Jack tried to walk to the checkout desk.

CRASH!

Everything tumbled to the floor. The fire truck rolled away. Blocks scattered everywhere. The puzzle pieces spilled out of their box.

"Oh no!" Jack said. His face felt hot and red.

Mrs. Chen, the librarian, hurried over. She had kind eyes behind her glasses.

"What happened here?" she asked gently.

"I wanted all the toys," Jack said sadly. "But I dropped them."

Mrs. Chen helped Jack pick everything up. "That's a lot of toys for two small arms," she said.

Jack tried again. This time he held the toys very tight. He squeezed his arms around them. He walked very slowly.

But his hands started to slip. The toys began to slide. Jack walked faster, trying to catch them.

CRASH!

Down they went again! The fire truck's siren made a sad beep. Jack felt tears in his eyes.

"This is too hard," he sniffled.

Mrs. Chen sat down next to Jack. "Sometimes we need to think of new ways to solve problems," she said.

Jack wiped his nose. "How?"

Mrs. Chen pointed to a corner. "What do you see over there?"

Jack looked. "A red wagon!"

"Library wagons help carry heavy things," Mrs. Chen explained. "Would you like to try?"

Jack's face lit up. "Yes, please!"

He pulled the little red wagon over. It had four round wheels and a long handle. The wagon was just his size.

Jack carefully placed the fire truck in the wagon. It fit perfectly! He added the teddy bear. Then the blocks and puzzle.

"Look!" Jack said happily. "They all fit!"

He pulled the wagon slowly. The wheels rolled smoothly on the carpet. Nothing fell out. Nothing crashed down.

Jack felt proud and strong. He could carry all his favorite toys now!

At the checkout desk, Mrs. Chen stamped each toy. "These are yours for one whole week," she said.

"Thank you for helping me," Jack said. "The wagon was a great idea."

"You didn't give up," Mrs. Chen smiled. "That's the most important part."

Jack pulled his wagon to the car. His mom was waiting outside.

"Wow, you got so many toys!" Mom said. "How did you carry them all?"

"I had a problem," Jack explained. "First I tried using just my arms. That didn't work. Then I tried holding them tighter. That didn't work either."

Jack pointed to his wagon proudly. "But Mrs. Chen helped me think of a new way. The wagon worked perfectly!"

Mom gave Jack a big hug. "You kept trying until you found a solution. That's very smart!"

Jack loaded his toys into the car. He couldn't wait to play with them at home. But first, he wanted to return the library wagon.

"Thank you, wagon," he whispered, patting its red side.

Next week, Jack would remember this lesson. When problems seemed too big, he would try different solutions. He would ask for help when he needed it.

Most importantly, he would never give up.

Jack waved goodbye to Mrs. Chen through the library window. She waved back, smiling. The library would always be his favorite place to learn and grow.

The end.
