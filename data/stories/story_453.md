# Daisy Duck and Bruno Bear

In a sunny green field dotted with yellow flowers, a little duck named Daisy waddled slowly by herself. The grass tickled her orange feet. The warm sun felt nice on her white feathers. But Daisy's heart felt heavy and sad.

"I wish I had someone to play with," Daisy sighed. She picked up a smooth pebble and tossed it gently. It landed with a soft *plop* in the nearby stream. "Playing alone isn't very fun."

Daisy tried to play hide-and-seek by herself. She hid behind a big oak tree and counted to ten. "One, two, three..." But when she finished counting, nobody was there to find her. She felt even more lonely than before.

Just then, a friendly brown bear came walking through the field. His name was Bruno, and he had kind chocolate eyes. Bruno saw Daisy sitting sadly by the stream.

"Hello there," Bruno said in his warm, rumbly voice. "I'm Bruno Bear. You look a little sad. Would you like to play together?"

Daisy's yellow beak turned up in a small smile. "I'm Daisy Duck. I was feeling lonely playing by myself. But I don't know if we can play the same games."

"Let's try!" Bruno said cheerfully. "What do you like to do?"

"I love to splash in the water," Daisy said. "But you're so big and furry. Won't you get cold?"

Bruno laughed, a sound like gentle thunder. "I love water too! Watch this!" He carefully stepped into the shallow stream. *Splash!* The cool water felt wonderful on his paws.

Daisy's eyes grew wide with delight. She paddled over to Bruno. Together, they splashed and played. Daisy showed Bruno how to make ripples with his paws. Bruno showed Daisy how to find pretty stones underwater.

"This is so much more fun together!" Daisy quacked happily.

After their water play, they discovered more things they both enjoyed. Bruno loved rolling down the grassy hill. At first, Daisy thought she couldn't roll like a bear. But Bruno had an idea.

"Try flapping your wings while you roll!" Bruno suggested.

Daisy tried it. Down the hill she tumbled, wings flapping, giggling with joy. "Wheee! This is amazing!"

They took turns rolling and laughing. Then they played a new game together. Bruno would gently toss flower petals high in the air. Daisy would fly up and catch them in her beak. Pink petals, white petals, yellow petals – they caught them all!

"You're so good at flying!" Bruno said admiringly.

"And you're so good at throwing!" Daisy replied. "We make a great team!"

As the afternoon sun began to set, painting the sky soft orange and pink, the two new friends sat together by the stream. Daisy nestled comfortably next to Bruno's warm, fluffy side.

"I was so sad this morning," Daisy said softly. "Playing alone made everything feel boring and empty."

"I know exactly how you feel," Bruno nodded. "I was looking for a friend too. Everything is better when you share it with someone special."

Daisy looked up at her new friend. "Bruno, playing with you made every game more exciting. When we splashed together, the water felt more refreshing. When we rolled together, the hill felt more fun. When we caught petals together, the flowers seemed more beautiful."

"That's what friendship does," Bruno said wisely. "It makes ordinary things feel magical."

As the first stars began to twinkle in the purple sky, Daisy and Bruno made a special promise. They would meet in their field every day to play together.

"Tomorrow, let's try building a fort with sticks!" Bruno suggested.

"And we can decorate it with flowers!" Daisy added excitedly.

They hugged goodbye – a gentle bear hug and a soft wing hug. As they walked home in different directions, both friends felt their hearts full of joy and excitement.

Daisy wasn't lonely anymore. Bruno had found his perfect playmate. Together, they had discovered that friendship and cooperation made everything more wonderful, more fun, and more magical than they ever imagined possible.

The moon smiled down on the peaceful field, where tomorrow would bring new adventures for two very best friends.

The end.
