# Story story_619

**Bruno the Bear and the High-Up Cookies**

Bruno the Bear loved visiting the library. He loved the soft carpet under his big paws. He loved the quiet whisper sounds people made. Most of all, he loved story time with Miss Lucy.

Today was special cookie day at the library. Miss Lucy always put out yummy cookies after story time. Bruno could smell them from across the room. They smelled like warm vanilla and sweet chocolate chips.

Bruno finished listening to a story about brave little mice. His tummy rumbled loudly. "Time for cookies!" he said to himself.

He walked over to the snack table. His nose twitched with excitement. The cookies smelled so good! But when Bruno looked up, his fuzzy face fell.

The cookies sat on a very tall shelf. Way, way up high. Bruno stretched his arms as far as he could. His paws wiggled in the air. But he couldn't reach the cookies at all.

"Oh no," Bruno said sadly. "The cookies are too high up."

Little Mouse scurried by with a cookie in her tiny paws. "Don't give up, Bruno!" she squeaked. "You can figure this out!"

Bruno thought hard. His brown eyes looked around the library. Then he spotted something useful. A big, sturdy chair sat near the reading corner.

"I know!" Bruno said. "I'll use that chair!"

Bruno pushed the chair across the carpet. It made a soft swooshing sound. He pushed and pushed until it was right under the cookie shelf.

Bruno climbed onto the chair carefully. His big paws gripped the seat. He stood up tall and reached for the cookies again.

But the shelf was still too high! Bruno's paws could only touch the bottom edge. The cookies sat just out of reach.

"This isn't working," Bruno sighed. He climbed down from the chair. His shoulders drooped with disappointment.

Miss Lucy walked over with her kind smile. "Having trouble, Bruno?" she asked gently.

"The cookies are too high," Bruno explained. "Even with the chair, I can't reach them."

Miss Lucy nodded thoughtfully. "Sometimes we need to ask for help," she said. "That's not giving up. That's being smart."

Bruno's ears perked up. He had an idea! He looked around and saw Giraffe reading a book by the window. Giraffe had the longest neck in the whole library.

"Giraffe!" Bruno called softly. "Could you please help me?"

Giraffe looked up from her book. "Of course, Bruno! What do you need?"

Bruno explained about the high-up cookies. Giraffe smiled and walked over to the snack table. She stretched her long, spotted neck up high. With ease, she reached the cookie jar on the top shelf.

"How many cookies would you like?" Giraffe asked kindly.

"Just two, please," Bruno said politely. "One for me and one for you! Thank you for helping me."

Giraffe carefully took down two cookies. She handed one to Bruno with her gentle mouth. The cookie felt warm and crumbly in his paws.

Bruno took a big bite. It tasted sweet and chocolatey. "Mmm!" he said happily. "This is the best cookie ever!"

"It tastes extra good because we shared it," Giraffe said with a smile.

Miss Lucy clapped her hands softly. "You both did wonderful problem-solving today," she said. "Bruno, you tried different ideas. And you both learned that asking for help is very brave."

Bruno felt proud and happy. His tummy was full of delicious cookie. His heart was full of warm feelings.

"Thank you, Giraffe," Bruno said. "Next time I can't reach something, I'll remember to ask for help."

"And I'll always be happy to help my friends," Giraffe replied.

From that day on, Bruno and Giraffe became the best of friends. They shared many more cookies together. And Bruno learned that the best solutions sometimes come from working together.

The library felt even more special now. It wasn't just a place for books and stories. It was a place where friends helped each other reach their goals.

The end.
