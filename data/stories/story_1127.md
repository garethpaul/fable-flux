# Story story_1127

**Finn's Special Story Time**

Every day at three o'clock, the bell rang. "Story time!" called Mrs. Coral from the reading corner. All the little sea creatures swam over quickly. They loved story time.

But Finn the fish always stayed behind. He hid under the big pink coral. His orange scales shimmered as he watched the others gather. "I don't like stories," Finn whispered to himself.

"Come on, Finn!" called his friend Shelly the seahorse. "Mrs. Coral has a new book today!"

Finn shook his head. The words in books looked too squiggly. The pictures seemed too busy. Stories made his tummy feel funny and worried.

Mrs. Coral noticed Finn hiding. She swam over slowly. Her gentle voice was soft like bubbles. "Would you like to sit with me today, Finn?"

"Stories are too hard," Finn said sadly. His fins drooped down.

"What if I told you a secret?" Mrs. Coral smiled. "Stories can be magical. Close your eyes and listen."

Finn closed his eyes tight. Mrs. Coral began to read about a brave little turtle. Her voice was warm and musical. It flowed like the ocean current.

Something wonderful started happening. Finn could see the turtle in his mind! The turtle had a shiny green shell. It sparkled in the sunlight. Finn could almost feel the warm sand under the turtle's flippers.

"The turtle swam through crystal blue water," Mrs. Coral read. Finn felt like he was swimming too. The water felt cool and fresh on his scales.

The story turtle met a friendly dolphin. They played together in the waves. Finn giggled softly. He could hear their happy splashing sounds.

When the turtle felt scared, Finn's heart beat faster. When the turtle was brave, Finn felt proud and strong. The story filled his whole body with feelings.

"The turtle found a treasure chest," Mrs. Coral continued. Finn opened his eyes wide. He could picture the golden chest perfectly! It was covered in pretty seashells and pearls.

Other fish had gathered around them. They were all listening with big, wondering eyes. Finn didn't feel worried anymore. He felt excited and happy.

The story turtle shared his treasure with all his friends. Everyone celebrated together. Finn clapped his fins quietly. What a beautiful ending!

"That was amazing!" Finn said when the story finished. "I could see everything! The turtle felt so real!"

Mrs. Coral's eyes twinkled. "That's the magic of stories, Finn. They help us imagine wonderful places. We can feel what the characters feel."

"Can we read another one?" Finn asked hopefully.

"Tomorrow," Mrs. Coral promised. "We'll have a new adventure then."

Finn swam home with a big smile. His mom noticed right away. "You look very happy, little one."

"I discovered something magical today," Finn said proudly. "Stories aren't just words on pages. They're like movies in your mind! You can see and feel everything!"

That night, Finn dreamed about story characters. He swam with dolphins and explored coral castles. He met singing whales and dancing starfish.

The next morning, Finn woke up early. He couldn't wait for three o'clock. He wanted to hear another magical story.

At story time, Finn swam to the front. He sat right next to Mrs. Coral. His eyes sparkled with excitement.

"Today's story is about a little fish who learns to be brave," Mrs. Coral announced.

Finn wiggled his tail happily. He closed his eyes and let the magic begin. The words painted pictures in his mind. His imagination soared through the ocean.

From that day on, Finn never missed story time. He learned that stories were doorways to amazing worlds. Each book was a new adventure waiting to happen.

And every day at three o'clock, when the bell rang, Finn was always the first one there.

The end.
