# Story story_644

**Splish Splash Car**

Little Red Car loved to zoom around the backyard. He rolled through mud puddles. He raced through tall grass. He played in the sandbox all day long.

"Beep beep!" Red Car honked happily.

But when the sun started to set, Mama called out. "Red Car, it's bath time!"

Red Car's wheels stopped spinning. His headlights dimmed a little. He did not want a bath.

"I'm not dirty," Red Car said quietly.

But his red paint was covered in brown mud. His wheels were full of sand. His windows were dusty and gray.

"Come on, little one," Mama said gently. "Bath time can be fun."

Red Car rolled very slowly toward the big blue tub. The water looked deep and scary. Bubbles floated on top like tiny clouds.

"I don't like getting wet," Red Car whispered.

Mama filled a soft yellow sponge with warm, soapy water. She squeezed it gently over Red Car's roof.

The warm water felt nice on his paint. It tickled a little bit. Red Car giggled softly.

"That feels good," he said with surprise.

Mama scrubbed his muddy wheels with a small brush. The bristles felt like tiny fingers giving him a massage. More giggles bubbled up from Red Car's engine.

"My wheels feel tingly!" he laughed.

Then something magical happened. The soap bubbles began to dance! They floated up into the air like sparkly balloons. Some bubbles landed on Red Car's windshield.

"Pop!" went one bubble.

"Pop! Pop!" went two more.

Red Car's eyes grew wide with wonder. The bubbles made rainbow colors in the evening light. Purple, green, yellow, and pink swirled together.

"Look at all the pretty bubbles!" Red Car exclaimed.

Mama smiled and made even more bubbles. She swirled the soapy water with her hands. Hundreds of bubbles filled the air around them.

Red Car tried to catch bubbles with his side mirrors. "Got one! Got another one!"

The bubbles popped with soft little sounds. Each pop made Red Car laugh harder.

Mama washed his windows with a squeaky cloth. "Squeak, squeak, squeak!" The sound made Red Car giggle uncontrollably.

"I sound like a happy mouse!" he said between giggles.

The warm water splashed gently against his doors. It sounded like a tiny waterfall. Red Car closed his eyes and listened to the peaceful sound.

His red paint began to shine bright and clean. His chrome bumpers sparkled like stars. His wheels looked brand new again.

"Wow!" Red Car said, looking at his reflection in a clean puddle. "I look amazing!"

Mama rinsed him with fresh, warm water. It felt like a gentle summer rain. All the soap and bubbles washed away.

"Time to dry off," Mama said.

She wrapped Red Car in a big, fluffy towel. The towel was soft and warm from sitting in the sunshine all day. It smelled like fresh flowers.

Red Car felt cozy and clean. His paint gleamed in the moonlight. His wheels were spotless and shiny.

"I love being clean!" Red Car announced proudly.

He rolled in a happy circle around the yard. His clean wheels made no dust clouds. His shiny paint reflected the twinkling stars above.

"Bath time was the best!" he said to Mama.

Mama tucked him into his garage for the night. She gave him a gentle pat on his hood.

"Sweet dreams, my clean little car," she whispered.

Red Car yawned sleepily. He thought about the dancing bubbles and the warm, tingly water. He thought about how shiny and bright he looked now.

"Mama?" Red Car said softly.

"Yes, dear?"

"Can we have another bubble bath tomorrow?"

Mama laughed her warm, happy laugh. "Of course we can, little one."

Red Car smiled as he drifted off to sleep. He dreamed of rainbow bubbles dancing in the air. He dreamed of warm water and squeaky sounds.

Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough. Bath time was going to be wonderful again.

"Beep beep," he whispered happily in his dreams.

The end.
