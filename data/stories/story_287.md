# Daisy Duck's Colorful Discovery

The morning sun peeked through the kitchen window. Mama Duck fluffed her yellow feathers. "Art time, Daisy!" she called cheerfully.

Daisy Duck waddled slowly to the table. Her orange feet dragged on the floor. The art supplies sat waiting for her. Crayons, paper, and paintbrushes lined up neatly.

"Do I have to?" Daisy sighed softly. "Art time feels boring today."

Yesterday's painting still sat on the counter. It looked messy and wrong to her. The colors had run together strangely. The sun looked like a muddy circle.

Mama Duck sat down beside her daughter. "Sometimes art feels tricky," she said gently. "But maybe today will surprise you."

Daisy picked up a blue crayon reluctantly. She drew a wobbly line across her paper. The line looked lonely by itself there.

"What if I add another line?" she wondered aloud.

She drew a second blue line below the first. Then something funny happened inside her tummy. The lines looked like they were dancing together!

"Oh!" Daisy quacked with surprise. "They look happy together!"

She reached for a red crayon next. Red lines joined the blue ones playfully. Soon yellow lines wiggled across the paper too.

The colors seemed to sing to each other. Blue whispered to red about sky and flowers. Yellow giggled with both about sunshine and warmth.

Daisy's tail feathers wiggled with growing excitement. Her webbed feet tapped against her chair. "Look, Mama! The colors are talking!"

"I can hear them!" Mama Duck smiled warmly.

Green joined the colorful conversation on paper. Purple danced in between all the others. Orange bounced around the edges like laughter.

Each crayon felt different in Daisy's wing. Some felt smooth and cool. Others felt warm and bumpy. The paper made soft scratching sounds underneath.

"This one tickles!" Daisy giggled, using pink. The pink crayon seemed extra wiggly today.

She drew swirls and circles and zigzags. Each mark felt like a tiny celebration. The paper filled up with joy and movement.

"What's happening here?" asked Papa Duck, waddling over. His eyes grew wide and bright. "This looks like a happiness storm!"

"It IS a happiness storm!" Daisy quacked proudly. "All the colors are playing together!"

She added dots with her paintbrush next. Tiny purple dots. Medium green dots. Big yellow dots that looked like suns.

The dots seemed to bounce off the paper. They made her think of raindrops and bubbles. Of polka dots and birthday confetti.

"Each dot has its own little dance," Daisy discovered.

Her artwork grew bigger and more wonderful. Colors layered on top of each other. They created new colors where they met.

"Purple and yellow make brown!" she observed excitedly. "Like my favorite mud puddles!"

Time seemed to fly by like butterflies. Before Daisy knew it, her paper overflowed with color and life.

She held up her finished masterpiece proudly. It looked like a rainbow had come to play. Like music you could see instead of hear.

"I made this whole world!" Daisy announced happily. "And tomorrow I'll make another one!"

Mama Duck hugged her tight and warm. "Your art makes our home more beautiful."

Papa Duck hung the artwork on the refrigerator. "Now everyone can see Daisy's happiness storm!"

That evening, Daisy looked at her art again. The colors still seemed to dance and sing. They made her heart feel light and fluttery.

"Tomorrow I want to try making purple flowers," she told her parents at dinner. "And maybe orange butterflies too!"

"Art time will be here before you know it," Mama Duck said lovingly.

Daisy snuggled into her cozy bed that night. She dreamed about colors dancing together. About brushes that painted by themselves. About papers that sang gentle lullabies.

When morning came again, Daisy woke up smiling. Her feet practically danced to the breakfast table.

"Is it art time yet?" she asked hopefully.

And somewhere in the kitchen, all her crayons waited patiently. Ready for another day of colorful adventures. Ready to help Daisy discover new worlds of wonder.

Art time had become the most magical part of every single day.

The end.
