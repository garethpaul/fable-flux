# Story story_817

**Grandma's Shoe Day**

Grandma Rose loved her morning walks. Every day, she put on her bright red sneakers. She walked to Sunny Park to feed the ducks.

Today felt different. Grandma sat on her favorite bench. She looked down at her feet. Oh no! Her shoelaces were loose and floppy.

"I need to tie my shoes," said Grandma Rose. She bent down to reach her laces. Her back felt stiff and sore. Her fingers felt wobbly. The laces slipped away from her hands.

"This is tricky," she said softly. Grandma tried again. She leaned forward slowly. Her glasses slid down her nose. The shoelaces looked blurry and far away.

A little girl named Emma was playing nearby. She had curly brown hair and a sunny yellow dress. Emma noticed Grandma looking worried.

"Are you okay?" asked Emma sweetly. She skipped over to the bench. Her own pink shoes had neat, tight bows.

"My shoelaces won't cooperate today," said Grandma Rose. "My hands feel shaky. I can't reach very well."

Emma tilted her head thoughtfully. "Maybe I can help you think of something!"

Grandma Rose smiled warmly. "That would be wonderful, dear."

First, Grandma tried a different way. She lifted her foot up onto the bench. This felt much better! Her shoe was closer to her hands now. She could see the laces clearly.

Grandma grabbed the left lace carefully. She grabbed the right lace too. She tried to make the first loop. But her fingers still felt wobbly and weak. The laces kept slipping and sliding away.

"Almost!" said Emma cheerfully. "You're getting closer!"

Grandma Rose felt frustrated. Her shoelaces hung loose and messy. "I used to tie shoes easily," she sighed. "Now it feels so hard."

"Don't give up," said Emma gently. "My daddy says trying again makes us stronger."

Emma looked around the park thoughtfully. She spotted something helpful nearby. "Look! There's a picnic table over there!"

They walked slowly to the wooden table. Grandma sat down on the bench part. The table was the perfect height! She could rest her foot on the seat.

"This feels much better," said Grandma Rose. Her foot stayed still and steady. Her hands could reach the laces easily now.

Emma sat beside her quietly. "You can do it, Grandma Rose!"

Grandma took a deep breath. The morning air smelled fresh and clean. Birds chirped happily in the trees above. She felt calm and ready.

She picked up the left shoelace. She picked up the right shoelace. This time, her hands felt steady and sure. She made the first loop carefully.

"Perfect!" whispered Emma excitedly.

Grandma wrapped the other lace around. She pulled it through the hole. She made a beautiful, tight bow! The red laces looked neat and pretty.

"I did it!" said Grandma Rose proudly. "Thank you for helping me think, Emma."

"You did all the hard work," said Emma with a big grin.

Grandma tied her other shoe perfectly too. Both feet felt snug and comfortable now. She was ready for her duck-feeding adventure!

"Would you like to come with me?" asked Grandma Rose. "I have extra bread crumbs to share."

"Yes, please!" said Emma happily.

They walked together toward the sparkling pond. Grandma's shoes felt perfect and secure. The ducks quacked loudly when they saw them coming.

Emma and Grandma Rose scattered bread crumbs on the water. The ducks splashed and played joyfully. The sun felt warm on their faces.

"Thank you for not giving up with me," said Grandma Rose. "Sometimes we need to try different ways."

"And sometimes we need good friends too," added Emma wisely.

Grandma Rose hugged Emma gently. She felt happy and proud. Her morning walk was even better with a new friend beside her.

From that day on, Grandma Rose always used the picnic table. Her shoes stayed tied perfectly. And Emma visited every morning to help feed the grateful ducks together.

The end.
