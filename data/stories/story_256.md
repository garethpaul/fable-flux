# Puzzle's Pretty Doll

Puzzle the frog lived by a beautiful pond. The water sparkled like tiny diamonds. Lily pads floated like green boats. Puzzle loved her cozy home.

Every morning, Puzzle played with her favorite toy. It was a pretty pink doll. The doll had golden hair. She wore a blue dress. Puzzle named her Rosie.

"Good morning, Rosie!" Puzzle said happily. She hugged the soft doll tight. They would play tea party together. They would sing songs by the water.

One sunny day, Puzzle hopped to the pond's edge. She wanted to show Rosie the fish. "Look at the pretty fish!" she said. Puzzle pointed at the orange fish swimming.

But then... SPLASH! 

Rosie slipped from Puzzle's hands. The pretty doll fell into the pond. Down, down, down she sank. The water was deep and dark.

"Oh no!" cried Puzzle. "Rosie! Come back!" 

Puzzle felt very sad. Her eyes filled with tears. Where was her special doll? How could she get Rosie back?

Puzzle took a deep breath. "I must find Rosie," she said. "I won't give up!"

First, Puzzle tried reaching into the water. She stretched her green arm down. The water was too deep. She couldn't reach the bottom.

"Hmm," thought Puzzle. "What else can I try?"

Next, Puzzle looked for a long stick. She found a brown branch. She poked it in the water. She moved it around and around. But she couldn't find Rosie.

Puzzle sat on her lily pad. She felt worried. But she didn't give up. Smart frogs always find solutions!

"Maybe my friends can help," Puzzle said.

She called to Benny the turtle. "Benny! Can you help me? My doll is in the pond!"

Benny swam over slowly. "I'll try to find her," he said. Down he dove with a splash. Benny looked under rocks. He searched near the reeds. But Rosie wasn't there.

"Don't worry," said Benny kindly. "We'll keep looking."

Then Puzzle had another idea. She called to Bella the duck. "Bella! Please help me find Rosie!"

Bella paddled over quickly. Her yellow feet kicked fast. "I'm good at diving," she said. Down she went with a splash. Bella looked by the muddy bottom. She searched near the cattails. Still no Rosie.

Puzzle felt sad again. Two friends tried to help. But Rosie was still lost. What could she do now?

Just then, old Mr. Crane walked by. His long legs stepped carefully. His neck was very, very long.

"What's wrong, little Puzzle?" he asked gently.

"My pretty doll fell in the pond," Puzzle explained. "We can't find her anywhere."

Mr. Crane smiled kindly. "My neck is very long. My beak is very sharp. Maybe I can help."

Mr. Crane stepped into the shallow water. He stretched his long neck down. Down, down, down it went. His beak searched the pond bottom.

"I feel something soft!" he called out.

Up came his head. In his beak was something pink. It was Rosie! The pretty doll was found!

"Rosie!" shouted Puzzle with joy. She hopped up and down. "You found her! Thank you, Mr. Crane!"

Mr. Crane placed Rosie gently on the lily pad. The doll was wet but safe. Her golden hair dripped water. Her blue dress was soggy.

Puzzle hugged Rosie tight. "I missed you so much!" she said. She was so happy. Her heart felt warm and full.

"You didn't give up," said Mr. Crane proudly. "You kept trying different ways. You asked friends for help. That's very smart!"

Puzzle smiled big. "When something is important, you don't give up. You keep trying until you find a way!"

From that day on, Puzzle was more careful. She held Rosie tight near the water. And she remembered: when problems seem big, keep trying. Ask friends for help. There's always a solution!

Puzzle and Rosie played happily ever after. The pond sparkled in the sunshine. And Puzzle knew she could solve any problem with patience and help from friends.

The end.
