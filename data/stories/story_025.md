# Pip the Plane and the Missing Teddy Bear

Pip was a bright red toy plane. He loved flying around Sunny Beach. The warm sand felt good under his wheels. The ocean waves made gentle swooshing sounds.

Every day, Pip carried his best friend everywhere. His friend was a small brown teddy bear named Cocoa. Cocoa had soft fuzzy fur and a red ribbon. They went on adventures together.

But this morning was different. Pip woke up in the beach house. He looked left. He looked right. Where was Cocoa?

"Oh no!" said Pip. "I can't find Cocoa anywhere!"

Pip's propeller spun with worry. His bright red paint seemed less shiny. He felt sad and scared without his teddy bear.

"I must find Cocoa," Pip said bravely. "He needs me, and I need him!"

First, Pip rolled around the beach house. He checked under the yellow beach chair. No Cocoa. He looked behind the blue umbrella. Still no Cocoa. He searched near the striped towels. Nothing there either.

"Hmm," thought Pip. "Maybe Cocoa is outside."

Pip rolled his wheels through the soft sand. The sand felt warm and tickly. He flew low over the beach. His shadow made a plane shape on the ground.

He looked near the sandcastles. Children had built tall towers and deep moats. But no brown teddy bear was there.

Next, Pip searched by the tide pools. The water was clear and sparkly. Little crabs scuttled sideways. Colorful shells dotted the wet sand. Still no sign of Cocoa.

"Where could he be?" Pip wondered aloud.

A friendly seagull named Gus heard Pip's worried voice. "What's wrong, little plane?" asked Gus.

"I lost my teddy bear, Cocoa," said Pip sadly. "I've looked everywhere!"

"Don't give up," said Gus kindly. "Sometimes we need to look in new places. Where did you play yesterday?"

Pip thought hard. His propeller spun slowly as he remembered.

"We played by the big rocks!" Pip exclaimed. "Maybe Cocoa fell there!"

Pip zoomed toward the rocky area. The rocks were smooth and gray. They felt cool in the morning shade. Waves splashed against them with a gentle splash-splash sound.

Pip flew between the rocks carefully. He looked in every crack and crevice. His red paint gleamed in the sunlight.

Suddenly, Pip heard a tiny voice. "Help! Help me!"

It was Cocoa! The little teddy bear was stuck between two rocks. His brown fur was damp from ocean spray. His red ribbon was tangled on a piece of seaweed.

"Cocoa!" cried Pip happily. "I found you!"

But how could Pip rescue his friend? The space was too small for Pip to fit through.

Pip thought and thought. Then he had an idea!

"Hold on, Cocoa!" called Pip. "I'll get help!"

Pip flew back to find Gus the seagull. "Please help me!" Pip asked. "Cocoa is stuck between the rocks!"

Gus flapped his white wings. "Of course I'll help! That's what friends do!"

Together, they flew back to the rocks. Gus used his orange beak to gently untangle the red ribbon. Then he carefully picked up Cocoa and placed him safely on a flat rock.

"Thank you, Gus!" said Pip and Cocoa together.

Pip scooped up Cocoa with his wing. The teddy bear felt soft and warm again. His brown fur was fluffy after drying in the sun.

"I was so scared without you," said Pip.

"I knew you'd find me," said Cocoa with a smile. "You never give up!"

From that day on, Pip learned something important. When problems seem big and scary, don't stop trying. Ask friends for help. Look in new places. Keep going even when things feel hard.

Now Pip and Cocoa play safely together every day. They build sandcastles, splash in shallow waves, and watch beautiful sunsets. And Pip always makes sure Cocoa is safe and close by.

The beach is full of their happy laughter. Their friendship grew even stronger after their adventure.

Sometimes the best treasures are found when we don't give up looking!

The end.
