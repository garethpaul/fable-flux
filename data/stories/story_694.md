# Story story_694

Jack sat on the soft carpet in the living room. Sunlight streamed through the big window. He held a shiny glass jar in his small hands.

The jar had a bright red lid. Inside were his favorite cookies. Grandma had given him the jar yesterday. She said the cookies were extra special.

Jack twisted the lid. It would not move. He tried again. The lid stayed tight.

"I want my cookies," Jack said softly. His tummy rumbled. The cookies looked so yummy through the glass.

Jack stood up on the fluffy rug. He held the jar tight with both hands. He twisted as hard as he could. His face turned red from trying.

The lid still would not budge. Jack felt frustrated. He sat back down with a small sigh.

"Maybe I need help," Jack thought. He looked around the cozy living room. Mom was in the kitchen. Dad was upstairs working.

Jack had an idea. He would try something different first.

He walked to the big brown couch. Jack climbed up on the soft cushions. He put the jar between his knees. This way he could use both hands on the lid.

Jack gripped the red lid tightly. He turned it slowly. Then he turned it faster. The jar slipped a little between his knees.

"Come on, lid," Jack whispered. He tried once more. The lid moved just a tiny bit. Jack's eyes grew wide with hope.

He twisted again. The lid moved a little more. Jack could hear a soft sound. It was the lid starting to loosen.

"I can do this," Jack said with determination. He took a deep breath. The air smelled like Mom's fresh flowers nearby.

Jack turned the lid one more time. Pop! The lid came off completely. Jack nearly fell backward on the couch cushions.

"I did it!" Jack shouted happily. He held up the open jar. The sweet smell of chocolate chip cookies filled the air.

The cookies looked perfect. They were golden brown and had lots of chocolate chips. Jack's mouth watered as he looked at them.

He reached in and took out one cookie. It felt warm and soft in his fingers. Jack took a big bite. The cookie was delicious and sweet.

"Mmm," Jack said as he chewed. The cookie tasted like vanilla and chocolate. It was the best cookie ever.

Mom heard Jack's happy shout. She came into the living room. Her face lit up with a big smile.

"You opened the jar all by yourself," Mom said proudly. She sat down next to Jack on the couch.

"It was really hard at first," Jack explained. "But I kept trying. I used my knees to help hold it."

Mom gave Jack a warm hug. "You were very clever," she said. "You didn't give up when it was difficult."

Jack felt proud and happy. His chest felt warm inside. He offered Mom a cookie from the jar.

"Thank you, sweetie," Mom said. She took a small bite. "Grandma makes the best cookies."

Jack nodded and smiled. He learned something important today. When things are hard, he should keep trying. Sometimes he just needs to think of a new way.

The living room felt extra cozy now. Jack and Mom sat together eating cookies. The sunshine made everything bright and cheerful.

"Next time I have a tricky problem, I'll remember today," Jack said. He took another yummy bite of his cookie.

Mom smiled and ruffled his hair gently. "That's my smart boy," she said.

Jack felt happy and proud. The cookies tasted even better because he had opened the jar himself. He had learned that trying different ways can help solve problems.

The jar sat open on the couch between them. There were still plenty of cookies left to share. Jack couldn't wait to tell Dad about his success when he came downstairs.

The end.
