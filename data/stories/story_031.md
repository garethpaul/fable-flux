# Zoe's Meadow Friends

Zoe skipped into the sunny meadow. Her red shoes made soft sounds on the grass. The meadow was big and green. Pretty flowers danced in the breeze.

But Zoe felt sad. She had no one to play with.

"I wish I had a friend," Zoe said. She looked around the empty meadow. Only tall grass and colorful flowers were there.

Zoe sat on a smooth rock. She kicked her feet back and forth. The wind made a whooshing sound through the trees.

Then Zoe had an idea. "Maybe I can find friends here!"

She stood up and called out loudly. "Hello! Is anyone here to play?"

A tiny cricket chirped back. But crickets were too small to play with.

Zoe walked to the flower patch. She bent down low. "Hello, pretty flowers! Do you want to play?"

The flowers just swayed quietly. They smelled sweet like honey. But flowers couldn't run or laugh.

Zoe felt disappointed. She almost wanted to go home.

But then she remembered what Mama always said. "Keep trying, little one. Good things come to those who don't give up."

Zoe took a deep breath. She would try again.

She walked to a big oak tree. "Hello, tree! Want to play hide and seek?"

The tree was quiet. Its leaves rustled softly. But trees couldn't hide or seek.

Zoe sat down again. She picked up some smooth pebbles. They felt cool in her hands.

"Maybe I can make my own fun," she thought.

Zoe began building a little tower with the pebbles. Stack, stack, stack. The tower grew taller.

Suddenly, she heard a small voice. "That looks fun!"

Zoe looked around. A little bunny hopped closer. Its nose twitched. Its fur was soft and brown.

"You want to help?" Zoe asked excitedly.

The bunny nodded. It pushed a pebble with its nose. The pebble rolled to Zoe's tower.

"Thank you!" Zoe giggled.

Soon, a red squirrel scampered down the tree. "Can I play too?" it chattered.

"Yes!" Zoe clapped her hands.

The squirrel gathered acorns. It dropped them gently near the tower.

Then a yellow butterfly fluttered over. It landed on Zoe's shoulder. Its wings felt like silk.

"We're building together!" Zoe said happily.

More friends came to help. A friendly frog hopped over with a shiny leaf. Two busy ants carried tiny twigs.

Everyone worked together. They made the most beautiful tower ever. It had pebbles, acorns, leaves, and twigs.

"This is amazing!" Zoe laughed.

The bunny hopped in circles. The squirrel chittered with joy. The butterfly danced in the air.

"Thank you for being my friends," Zoe said.

The bunny nuzzled her hand. The squirrel patted her arm with its tiny paw.

Zoe realized something wonderful. She had been looking for friends the wrong way. She had asked others to play with her. But she hadn't offered to play with them.

When she started building something fun, friends came to join her.

"I know what to do next time," Zoe said to herself.

She spent the whole afternoon playing with her new friends. They played hide and seek around the flowers. They raced through the tall grass. They shared stories under the big tree.

When the sun began to set, Zoe waved goodbye. "See you tomorrow!" she called.

All her friends waved back in their own special ways.

Zoe skipped home with a huge smile. Her heart felt warm and happy.

At dinner, Mama asked, "How was your day?"

"I learned something important," Zoe said. "When you want friends, start by being friendly. Do something fun, and friends will come!"

Mama hugged her tight. "That's very wise, my little one."

That night, Zoe dreamed about her meadow friends. She couldn't wait to play with them again tomorrow.

And she never felt lonely in the meadow again.

*The End*

---

**Lesson learned:** When we start by being kind and doing fun things, friends naturally want to join us. Sometimes the best way to find friendship is to create something wonderful that others want to be part of.
