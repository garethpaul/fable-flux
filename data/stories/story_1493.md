# Story story_1493

Ben heard Mom calling from the kitchen. "Dinner time, Ben!" she said in her cheerful voice. Ben was building a tall tower with his blocks. He didn't want to stop playing.

"Do I have to?" Ben asked. His tower was almost as tall as him. The red blocks balanced carefully on the blue ones. He wanted to add more blocks to make it even taller.

"Yes, sweetie," Mom said gently. "Your food is getting cold." Ben sighed and looked at his tower one more time. He walked slowly to the kitchen, dragging his feet on the soft carpet.

The kitchen smelled like warm bread and soup. Steam rose from his bowl like tiny clouds. Ben climbed into his chair and stared at his dinner. There were orange carrots, green peas, and brown chicken. Everything looked ordinary and boring.

"I'm not hungry," Ben said quietly. He pushed a pea around his plate with his fork. The pea rolled in a little circle. Ben pushed it again and watched it spin.

Mom sat down next to him. "What if we make dinner more fun?" she asked with a twinkle in her eye. Ben looked up curiously. Mom had a special smile that meant something exciting was coming.

"Watch this," Mom whispered. She picked up a carrot stick and held it like a tiny orange wand. "Abracadabra, carrots are crunchy!" She took a big bite. The carrot made a loud CRUNCH sound.

Ben giggled. The sound was funny and loud. "Can I try the magic too?" he asked. Mom nodded and handed him a carrot stick. Ben waved it in the air like Mom did.

"Hocus pocus, carrots are yummy!" Ben said in his best wizard voice. He bit down hard. CRUNCH! The carrot was sweet and made his teeth feel strong. "It worked!" Ben laughed.

Next, Mom picked up three peas on her spoon. "These are three little green balls," she said. "Let's see if they can jump into my mouth!" She lifted the spoon high and carefully aimed. The peas landed perfectly on her tongue.

Ben tried the same game. He balanced four peas on his spoon. "Ready, peas? One, two, three, jump!" The peas tumbled into his mouth. They were soft and tasted like tiny green clouds. "They're good jumpers!" Ben said happily.

For the chicken, Mom made it into a fun story. "This chicken used to live on a happy farm," she said. "It ate corn and played in the sunshine all day." Ben imagined the chicken running around and feeling free. The chicken tasted warm and made his tummy feel cozy.

Ben discovered that eating could be like playing a game. He made train sounds as his fork carried food to his mouth. "Choo-choo! Here comes the dinner train!" The fork was the engine and his mouth was the station.

Soon Ben's plate was almost empty. His tummy felt full and happy. The kitchen felt warm and safe. Mom's eyes sparkled with pride as she watched Ben finish his last bite.

"That was the best dinner ever!" Ben said, wiping his mouth with his napkin. He felt proud of himself for trying all his food. His body felt strong and ready for more playing.

"You were such a good eater tonight," Mom said, giving him a gentle hug. Her arms felt warm and loving around him. "You tried everything on your plate."

Ben helped Mom clear the dishes. The empty plate felt light in his small hands. He liked helping Mom in the kitchen. It made him feel like a big boy.

As Mom tucked him into bed later, Ben smiled sleepily. "Mom, can we play the dinner games again tomorrow?" he asked. His soft pillow felt perfect under his head.

"Of course we can," Mom said, kissing his forehead. "We can try new games too." Ben closed his eyes and dreamed about magical carrots and jumping peas. Tomorrow's dinner would be another fun adventure.

The end.
