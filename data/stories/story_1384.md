# Story story_1384

**The Missing Blocks**

Baby Sam loved his red blocks. He carried them everywhere in his little blue bucket. Today, Mama took him to Sunny Park to play.

The grass felt soft under Sam's bare feet. Birds sang sweet songs in the tall trees. Other children laughed and played on the swings. Sam found a perfect spot under a shady oak tree.

He dumped his blocks on the ground. Red squares scattered on the green grass. Sam clapped his hands with joy. He started building a tall tower.

Stack, stack, stack went the blocks. Higher and higher Sam built. The tower wobbled a little. Sam giggled and kept building.

A fluffy white dog ran by. It wagged its tail fast. The dog's paws hit Sam's tower. CRASH! All the blocks fell down.

The blocks rolled everywhere. Some went behind the tree. Others rolled toward the pond. One block disappeared under a big bush.

Sam looked around. Where were his blocks? His bottom lip started to shake. His eyes filled with tears.

"Blocks gone!" Sam cried. "All gone!"

Mama heard Sam crying. She hurried over and knelt down. "What's wrong, sweetie?" she asked softly.

"My blocks! Can't find them!" Sam sniffled.

Mama hugged Sam tight. "Let's look together," she said. "I bet we can find them."

Sam wiped his nose with his sleeve. He wanted his blocks back so much. Maybe if he looked really hard, he could find them.

Sam crawled on his hands and knees. He looked under leaves. He peeked behind rocks. He found a pretty yellow flower. He found a smooth gray stone. But no red blocks.

Sam sat down hard. He felt very sad. His favorite blocks were lost forever. More tears rolled down his cheeks.

"This is too hard," Sam whispered.

But then Sam remembered something. Mama always said, "Try again, Sam. You can do it." Maybe he should try a different way.

Sam stood up and brushed grass off his knees. This time, he would look more carefully. He would check every single spot.

First, Sam looked behind the big oak tree. He moved the brown leaves with his hands. There! Two red blocks hiding under the leaves. Sam squealed with happiness.

Next, Sam walked slowly toward the pond. He looked left and right. Near the water, three more blocks sat in the mud. Sam picked them up carefully.

Now Sam needed one more block. Where could it be? He remembered the fluffy dog running toward the playground. Maybe the block rolled that way too.

Sam walked to the big green bush. He got on his belly and crawled underneath. The leaves tickled his back. It was dark and cool under there.

Something red caught his eye. His last block! Sam reached out and grabbed it. Now he had all six blocks again.

"Mama! Mama! I found them!" Sam called out. He ran to show her his bucket full of blocks.

Mama smiled big. "You didn't give up, Sam. You kept trying until you found them all."

Sam felt proud and happy. His chest puffed out like a little bird. He had solved the problem all by himself.

Sam built his tower again. This time, he built it far from where dogs might run. He made it strong and steady. The red blocks made a perfect castle.

Other children came to see Sam's tower. "Wow!" said a girl with pigtails. "That's so tall!"

Sam beamed with pride. He had found his blocks and built something wonderful.

When it was time to go home, Sam carefully put each block in his bucket. He hugged the bucket tight.

"Good job today, Sam," Mama said. "You learned something important."

"What did I learn?" Sam asked.

"When something is hard, you don't give up. You try again in a new way."

Sam nodded. He felt very grown-up. Tomorrow he would bring his blocks to the park again. And if they got lost, he knew exactly what to do.

He would look carefully and never give up. Because that's what big boys do.

The end.
