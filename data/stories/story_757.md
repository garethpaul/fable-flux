# Story story_757

**Pip the Puppet and the Big Storm**

Pip was a small wooden puppet. He lived in a cozy tree house. The tree house sat high in the old oak tree. Pip loved his forest home very much.

One gray morning, Pip woke up early. The wind was howling loudly. Dark clouds filled the sky. Rain drops began to fall. "Oh no!" said Pip. "A big storm is coming!"

Pip looked out his window. The wind shook the tree branches. Leaves flew everywhere. His friend Bunny was hopping around below. Bunny looked scared and wet.

"I must help Bunny!" Pip said. He wanted to keep his friend safe. But how could a small puppet help?

Pip had an idea. He would build a shelter! He gathered some big leaves. The leaves were green and waxy. Pip tried to make a roof with them. He placed the leaves over a low branch.

But the wind was too strong. WHOOSH! The leaves blew away quickly. They scattered all over the forest floor. Pip felt sad and worried.

"That didn't work," Pip sighed. He watched Bunny shiver in the rain. The poor bunny was getting wetter and wetter.

Pip thought harder. What else could he try? Then he remembered something special. His tree house had extra space inside! It was warm and dry in there.

"Bunny! Come up here!" Pip called down. His voice was kind and gentle. "You can stay with me!"

But Bunny couldn't climb the tall tree. The trunk was too smooth and slippery. Bunny tried to hop up. She slipped back down each time.

Pip needed a better plan. He looked around his tree house. There was his old jump rope! It was long and strong. The rope was bright red and soft.

Pip tied one end to his window. He threw the other end down to Bunny. "Hold on tight!" Pip called. "I'll pull you up!"

Bunny grabbed the rope with her paws. Pip pulled and pulled. His wooden arms worked hard. Slowly, Bunny came up higher and higher.

"You're doing great!" Pip cheered. He could smell the fresh rain in the air. Thunder rumbled in the distance.

Finally, Bunny reached the window! She hopped inside the warm tree house. Her fur was wet but she was safe now.

"Thank you, Pip!" Bunny said happily. She shook the water from her ears. "You saved me from the storm!"

Pip felt proud and happy. He had helped his friend! They sat by the window together. The tree house stayed dry and cozy.

Outside, the storm grew stronger. Rain pattered on the roof. Lightning flashed across the sky. But inside, Pip and Bunny felt safe and warm.

"I was so scared," Bunny said softly. "The thunder is very loud."

"Don't worry," Pip said gently. He patted Bunny's paw. "We're safe here together. Storms always end."

They shared some sweet berries for breakfast. The berries were purple and juicy. They told funny stories to pass the time. Pip's wooden face smiled brightly.

As the day went on, the storm got weaker. The thunder became quieter. The rain turned into tiny sprinkles. Finally, the sun peeked through the clouds.

"Look!" Bunny pointed outside. A beautiful rainbow stretched across the sky. It had all the colors they could imagine.

"The storm is over," Pip said happily. "And we made it through together!"

Bunny hugged her puppet friend. "You didn't give up on me," she said. "Even when the first plan didn't work."

Pip felt warm inside his wooden chest. "That's what friends do," he said. "We keep trying until we find a way."

From that day on, Pip knew something important. When problems seem too big, don't give up. Try one solution, then try another. With patience and kindness, there's always a way to help.

And whenever storms came to the forest, Bunny knew exactly where to go. She would hop straight to Pip's tree house. There, they would wait together until the sun came out again.

The forest was peaceful once more. Pip and Bunny were the very best of friends.

The end.
