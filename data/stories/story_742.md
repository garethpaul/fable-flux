# Story story_742

**Mama's Bedtime Helper**

Mama loved working at Sunny Days Nursery. She helped little children play and learn all day. But when bedtime came, Mama felt scared.

"Time for nap," said Mama softly. Her hands shook a little. The children lay on their colorful mats. Some giggled. Some wiggled. Some cried for their mommies.

Mama's heart beat fast. What if the children wouldn't sleep? What if they kept crying? What if she couldn't help them feel safe?

"I don't know how to do this," Mama whispered to herself.

Little Emma sat up on her purple mat. "Mama, I can't sleep," she said. "It's too bright in here."

Mama's cheeks turned pink. She walked to the window. Her feet felt heavy. She pulled the curtains closed. The room became darker.

"There," said Mama. "Now try to sleep."

But Emma still sat up. So did Tommy and Grace and Sam. The room felt quiet but not sleepy. The children looked around with wide eyes.

"This isn't working," thought Mama. She felt like giving up.

Then Mama remembered something. Her own mama used to sing to her at bedtime. Maybe that would help!

Mama took a deep breath. She sat in the rocking chair. The chair creaked softly as she rocked back and forth.

"Let me try something new," said Mama. Her voice was gentle now.

She began to hum a quiet song. The sound was warm and sweet. Like honey on toast. Like rain on flowers.

Emma lay down on her mat. She smiled at Mama. Tommy closed his eyes. Grace pulled her soft blanket up to her chin.

But some children still wiggled. Sam kicked his legs. Maya played with her hair. The humming wasn't quite enough.

Mama thought harder. What else did children need at bedtime?

"I have another idea," whispered Mama.

She stood up from the rocking chair. She walked around the room very slowly. Her shoes made soft padding sounds on the carpet.

Mama gently patted each child's back. Pat, pat, pat. Like a mama bird caring for her babies. Her touch was warm and kind.

"Close your eyes, little ones," Mama said softly. "You are safe here."

She hummed her quiet song and patted backs at the same time. The room filled with peaceful sounds. Soft humming. Gentle patting. Slow breathing.

Emma's eyes closed first. Then Tommy's. Grace snuggled deeper into her blanket. One by one, all the children grew sleepy.

Even Sam stopped kicking. Maya stopped playing with her hair. Their little bodies relaxed on their mats.

Mama kept humming and patting. She moved like a gentle breeze around the room. Her fear melted away like ice cream in sunshine.

Soon all the children were sleeping. Their faces looked peaceful and happy. Soft snores filled the air like tiny purrs.

Mama sat back in her rocking chair. She felt proud and calm. Her heart didn't beat fast anymore.

"I did it," she whispered to herself. "I helped them feel safe."

When the children woke up from their nap, they smiled at Mama. Emma gave her a big hug. Tommy said, "That was the best nap ever!"

The next day at bedtime, Mama wasn't scared. She knew what to do. She hummed her gentle song. She patted little backs. She helped the children feel loved and safe.

"You're the best bedtime helper," said Grace.

Mama's heart felt warm and full. She had learned something important. When things seem hard, you don't give up. You try one way. If that doesn't work, you try another way.

Sometimes the best solutions come when you keep trying. And sometimes helping others helps you feel brave too.

From that day on, Mama loved bedtime at the nursery. The children slept peacefully. And Mama felt proud of herself for not giving up.

Every day brought new chances to help and learn. And that made Mama very happy indeed.

The end.
