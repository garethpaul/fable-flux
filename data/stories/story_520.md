# Benny's Big Search

Benny was a little boy who loved two things most in the whole world. He loved his favorite teddy bear, Mr. Snuggles, and he loved the big red swing set in his backyard.

Every sunny morning, Benny would run outside. He would carry Mr. Snuggles in his arms. Together, they would swing high into the blue sky. "Whee!" Benny would giggle. "Look how high we go, Mr. Snuggles!"

But this morning was different. Very different indeed.

Benny woke up and looked around his room. He looked under his pillow. He looked behind his toy box. He looked in his closet. Where was Mr. Snuggles?

"Mom!" called Benny. "I can't find Mr. Snuggles anywhere!"

Mom came into his room. She gave him a warm hug. "Let's think about this together," she said gently. "Where did you play with him yesterday?"

Benny's eyes grew wide. "The swing set! We were swinging outside!"

Benny ran to the backyard as fast as his little legs could carry him. The morning grass felt cool and wet under his bare feet. The red swing set stood tall and bright in the sunshine.

But Mr. Snuggles was nowhere to be seen.

Benny climbed onto his favorite swing. He looked left. He looked right. No teddy bear. He felt sad and worried. How could he swing without his best friend?

"I have to find him," Benny said out loud. His voice sounded very determined.

First, Benny looked under the swing set. He crawled on his hands and knees. The grass tickled his arms. He found three shiny rocks and a red button. But no Mr. Snuggles.

Next, Benny searched behind the big oak tree. The bark felt rough under his small hands. He found a pretty blue feather and two acorns. But still no Mr. Snuggles.

Then Benny had another idea. Maybe Mr. Snuggles fell into the flower garden! He walked carefully between the colorful flowers. The petals felt soft like velvet. He smelled the sweet roses. But Mr. Snuggles wasn't there either.

Benny sat down on his swing. He felt like crying. Where could his teddy bear be?

"Don't give up," he whispered to himself. "Think, think, think."

Suddenly, Benny remembered something important. Yesterday had been very windy! The wind had made the swings move back and forth. Maybe the wind had blown Mr. Snuggles somewhere new!

Benny looked up at the big oak tree again. This time, he looked way up high. There, sitting on a thick brown branch, was something brown and fuzzy.

"Mr. Snuggles!" Benny shouted with joy.

But how could he reach so high? Benny was too little to climb the big tree. He thought and thought.

Then he had the best idea ever! Benny ran to get his dad's long rake from the garage. The wooden handle felt smooth in his hands. Very carefully, he used the rake to gently nudge Mr. Snuggles.

Down, down, down came the teddy bear! Benny caught him in his arms and hugged him tight. Mr. Snuggles smelled like fresh air and leaves.

"I found you!" Benny laughed. "I never stopped looking!"

Benny climbed onto his swing with Mr. Snuggles safe in his lap. They swung high into the bright blue sky together. The wind felt wonderful in Benny's hair.

"You know what, Mr. Snuggles?" Benny said happily. "When something is really important, you should never give up. You just keep trying until you find the answer!"

Mr. Snuggles seemed to nod in agreement.

From that day on, whenever Benny faced a problem, he remembered his big search. He learned that being persistent and thinking of new ideas could solve almost anything. And he always made sure to hold Mr. Snuggles extra tight during windy days!

The two best friends swung together until lunchtime, both feeling proud and happy. Sometimes the best solutions come to those who never stop trying.

The end.
