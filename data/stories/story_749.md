# Story story_749

**Whiskers and the Magic Paintbrush**

It was art time at Rainbow Daycare. All the children sat at little tables. Bright papers and colorful paints waited for them.

"Come on, Whiskers!" called Miss Lucy. "Time to paint!"

Whiskers the gray kitten sat in the corner. His ears drooped down. His tail curled around his paws.

"I don't want to paint," Whiskers whispered. "I'm not good at art."

The other animals were already busy. Bunny painted big purple flowers. Duck made yellow sunshine. Bear drew green trees.

Whiskers watched them laugh and smile. He felt left out. But his paws felt too clumsy for painting.

Miss Lucy sat down beside him. She had kind brown eyes. "What's wrong, little one?"

"My paws are too big," Whiskers said sadly. "I'll make a mess."

Miss Lucy smiled. She handed him a special paintbrush. It had sparkly silver bristles. The handle felt warm in his paw.

"This is a magic paintbrush," she whispered. "It helps nervous artists feel brave."

Whiskers looked at the brush. It seemed to shimmer and glow. Maybe it really was magic.

He dipped it in blue paint. The brush felt light as a feather. He touched it to the white paper.

Something wonderful happened. The blue paint swirled and danced. It made perfect little waves. Like the ocean on a sunny day.

"Wow!" gasped Whiskers. His green eyes grew wide.

He tried red paint next. The brush made beautiful roses. Their petals looked soft and real. Whiskers could almost smell their sweet scent.

"This is amazing!" he purred loudly.

Duck waddled over to look. "Your painting is so pretty, Whiskers!"

Bunny hopped closer too. "Those flowers look real enough to pick!"

Bear lumbered over with his painting. "Can you teach me how to make roses?"

Whiskers felt warm and happy inside. His chest puffed out with pride. He showed Bear how to move the magic brush.

Soon all the animals gathered around. They watched Whiskers paint purple butterflies. The butterflies seemed to flutter on the paper.

"How do you do that?" asked Duck.

Whiskers thought carefully. "I think the magic is in believing," he said. "And in trying your best."

He painted yellow stars that twinkled. Green grass that looked soft. Orange carrots that made Bunny's tummy rumble.

The more he painted, the better he felt. His paws didn't feel clumsy anymore. They felt just right for making art.

Miss Lucy watched with a big smile. "You're doing wonderful work, Whiskers."

"The brush really is magic!" Whiskers said excitedly.

Miss Lucy winked. "The real magic was inside you all along. You just needed to believe in yourself."

Whiskers looked at his beautiful painting. It showed a sunny meadow. Flowers danced in the breeze. Butterflies played among the trees.

"I made this," he whispered in wonder. "I really made this."

When art time ended, Whiskers didn't want to stop. His painting was still wet. The colors seemed to glow in the afternoon sun.

"Can we have art time again tomorrow?" he asked hopefully.

"Of course!" laughed Miss Lucy. "We have art time every day."

Whiskers carefully hung his painting to dry. He couldn't wait to show his mama. She would be so proud.

The other animals gathered their paintings too. Everyone had made something special. The room smelled like paint and creativity.

As they cleaned their brushes, Whiskers felt different. His shoulders stood straighter. His whiskers twitched with excitement.

"Tomorrow I want to paint a rainbow," he announced. "A big, bright, beautiful rainbow!"

Duck clapped her wings. "That sounds wonderful!"

Bunny nodded eagerly. "I want to paint with you!"

Bear gave Whiskers a gentle hug. "You're a real artist now."

Whiskers purred so loudly the whole room could hear. Art time had become his favorite time. He couldn't wait for tomorrow.

The magic paintbrush sat ready for the next day. But Whiskers knew the truth now. The real magic lived in his heart. And it would be there whenever he needed it.

The end.
