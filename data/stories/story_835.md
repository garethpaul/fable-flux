# Story story_835

**Toby's Big Problem**

Toby was a small blue train. He lived in a sunny meadow. Wild flowers grew everywhere. Bees buzzed from flower to flower. Birds sang happy songs in the trees.

Every morning, Toby rolled through the meadow. He loved the smell of fresh grass. He loved watching butterflies dance. The warm sun made his blue paint shine bright.

But today was different. Toby felt worried. His engine made funny sounds. "Chug-a-sputter-chug," went his motor. That wasn't right at all.

Toby stopped near a patch of daisies. He tried to start again. "Chug-a-sputter-STOP!" His engine quit completely. Black smoke puffed from his smokestack.

"Oh no," whispered Toby. "I'm broken."

A red robin landed on his roof. "What's wrong, little train?" she chirped.

Toby felt his wheels get wobbly. "My engine won't work," he said quietly. "But I'm too shy to ask for help."

The robin tilted her head. "Why are you shy?"

"What if they laugh at me?" Toby worried. "What if I sound silly?"

The robin sang a cheerful tune. "Friends want to help friends," she said. Then she flew away.

Toby sat alone in the meadow. The grass tickled his wheels. A gentle breeze cooled his hot engine. He watched clouds float by like fluffy cotton balls.

"Maybe I can fix myself," thought Toby. He tried very hard to start his engine. He pushed and strained. His face turned red from trying. But nothing happened.

A family of rabbits hopped nearby. They were picking clover for lunch. The mama rabbit had soft gray fur. The baby rabbits had twitching pink noses.

"Hello there," called the mama rabbit. "You look sad."

Toby wanted to ask for help. The words felt stuck in his throat. "I'm... I'm okay," he whispered. But he wasn't okay at all.

The rabbits kept picking clover. Soon they hopped away. Toby felt more alone than ever. A tear rolled down his blue side.

The sun climbed higher in the sky. Toby grew very hot. His metal sides felt warm as toast. He wished he could roll to the shade. But he still couldn't move.

Then he heard a friendly voice. "Need some help?"

It was Oliver the old farm truck. Oliver had kind eyes. His red paint was faded but cheerful. He always helped others in the meadow.

Toby's heart beat fast. This was his chance. He took a deep breath. The sweet smell of honeysuckle filled the air.

"Yes," said Toby in a tiny voice. "My engine is broken. I tried to fix it myself. But I can't do it alone."

Oliver smiled warmly. "That's what friends are for," he said. "Let me take a look."

Oliver checked under Toby's hood. "I see the problem," he said. "Your spark plug came loose. That's easy to fix."

Oliver tightened the spark plug with his tools. His big hands worked carefully. "Try starting now," he said.

Toby turned his key. "Chug-chug-chug!" His engine purred like a happy cat. No more sputtering sounds!

"It works!" cheered Toby. "Thank you so much!"

"You're very welcome," said Oliver. "But remember something important. Asking for help shows you're brave, not silly."

Toby felt warm inside. Not from the sun, but from happiness. "I was scared you'd laugh at me," he admitted.

"Real friends never laugh at problems," Oliver explained. "They help solve them together."

From that day on, Toby wasn't shy anymore. When he had problems, he asked for help right away. He learned that friends love to help each other.

And whenever Toby saw someone else who needed help, he always stopped to lend a wheel. The meadow became an even happier place because everyone helped everyone.

Toby rolled through the flowers with joy. The bees still buzzed. The birds still sang. But now Toby sang along too, knowing he had wonderful friends all around him.

The end.
