# Story story_723

Finny the fish lived in a big blue pond. But today, Finny was not in the water. Today, Finny was playing in the sandy playground next to his pond.

Finny had special wheels on his fish bowl. He could roll around and play with all the land animals. The warm sand felt bumpy under his rolling bowl. The sun made sparkly patterns on the water in his bowl.

But Finny felt sad today. He was building a sand castle all by himself. His little fins pressed the sand into towers. The sand was soft and cool between his fins. "I wish I had a friend to play with," Finny said quietly.

High above the sandbox, Gigi the giraffe watched Finny play. Her long neck stretched way up to the treetops. She could see everything from up there. Gigi saw how carefully Finny was building his castle. She saw how lonely he looked.

"That looks like fun," Gigi said softly. But she was scared to ask if she could play. Gigi was very tall. Sometimes the other animals said she was too big to play their games.

Gigi took a deep breath. She walked slowly to the sandbox. Her long legs made big steps in the sand. Each step made a soft thump sound.

"Hello," Gigi said in her gentlest voice. "I'm Gigi. Can I help you build your castle?"

Finny looked up, up, up at the tall giraffe. Her spots looked like pretty polka dots. Her kind eyes sparkled in the sunshine. "Hi! I'm Finny. I would love help with my castle!"

Gigi smiled a big, happy smile. She carefully folded her long legs. Down, down, down she went until she was sitting in the sandbox. Now she was closer to Finny's size.

"What should we build?" Gigi asked.

"How about a really tall tower?" Finny suggested. "You can reach way up high!"

Gigi's eyes lit up. "Yes! And you can make the bottom strong and beautiful!"

They started working together. Finny used his fins to pack sand at the bottom. The sand felt cool and squishy. Gigi used her long neck to stack sand way up high. She was very gentle and careful.

"This is the best tower ever!" Finny giggled.

"We make a great team," Gigi said happily.

Next, they built a moat around their castle. Finny rolled his bowl around the castle. His wheels made perfect tracks in the sand. Gigi used her hooves to dig the moat deeper.

"Now we need water for the moat," Finny said.

Gigi had an idea. She stretched her long neck to the water fountain. She filled her mouth with cool water. Then she gently poured it into their moat. The water sparkled like diamonds in the sun.

"Perfect!" Finny clapped his fins together.

They built bridges and walls and towers. They made sand cookies in Finny's bucket. Gigi's spots got sandy, and Finny's bowl got sandy too. They laughed and played until the sun started to set.

"This was the best day ever," Finny said. His eyes were bright and happy.

"I was scared to ask if I could play," Gigi admitted. "I thought I might be too big."

"You're the perfect size for building tall towers," Finny said. "And I was scared I'd have to play alone forever."

"We don't have to be scared anymore," Gigi said. "We have each other now."

They looked at their beautiful sand castle. It had tall towers and deep moats. It had bridges and walls and even a flag made from a leaf.

"Friends forever?" Finny asked.

"Friends forever," Gigi promised.

From that day on, Finny and Gigi played together every sunny afternoon. The tall giraffe and the little fish were the very best of friends. They learned that being different made them an even better team.

And their sand castles were always the most amazing castles in the whole playground.

The end.
