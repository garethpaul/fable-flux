# Story story_1247

**Splish Splash Magic**

"Bath time, Lily!" called Mama from the bathroom.

Lily was playing with her toy cars. She loved making them zoom around the carpet. The little red car was her favorite.

"Do I have to?" Lily asked. She made her cars drive in circles.

Mama smiled and nodded. "Yes, sweetie. You played outside today. You have dirt on your hands and grass in your hair."

Lily looked at her hands. They were brown with mud. Her knees had dirt too. But baths seemed so boring.

"I don't want to stop playing," Lily said softly.

Mama sat down next to her. "What if we make bath time special tonight?"

Lily looked up with curious eyes. "How?"

"Come see," Mama said with a wink.

They walked to the bathroom together. Mama turned on the water. Steam rose from the warm bath.

But something was different tonight.

The water sparkled! Tiny bubbles danced on top. They smelled like strawberries.

"Wow!" Lily gasped. "The water is shiny!"

Mama laughed. "I added special bubble bath. Watch this."

She squeezed more soap into the water. Mountains of fluffy bubbles appeared. They grew higher and higher.

Lily clapped her hands. "It looks like snow!"

"Want to touch them?" Mama asked.

Lily reached out carefully. The bubbles felt soft and tickly. They popped with tiny sounds. Pop! Pop! Pop!

"This is fun!" Lily giggled.

She climbed into the warm water. It felt cozy like a hug. The bubbles covered her arms and legs.

"Look, Mama! I'm a bubble monster!" Lily put bubbles on her chin like a beard.

Mama laughed. "You're the silliest bubble monster ever!"

Lily scooped up more bubbles. She blew them into the air. They floated around the bathroom like tiny balloons.

"Can I make bubble hats?" Lily asked.

"Of course!" Mama said.

Lily piled bubbles on her head. She made bubble mittens on her hands. She even put bubbles on her nose.

Mama took pictures with her phone. "You look magical!"

The warm water made Lily feel sleepy and happy. Mama gently washed her hair with sweet-smelling shampoo.

"Close your eyes, sweetie," Mama said softly.

Lily closed her eyes tight. Mama poured warm water over her hair. It felt like gentle rain.

"All clean!" Mama said.

But Lily didn't want to get out yet.

"Can I play a little more?" she asked.

"Five more minutes," Mama agreed.

Lily made bubble soup in her hands. She pretended to feed her rubber duck. The duck bobbed in the sparkly water.

"Quack, quack! Thank you for the soup!" Lily made the duck say.

Soon the water started to cool down. The bubbles began to disappear.

"Time to get out now," Mama said gently.

She held up a big, fluffy towel. It was warm from the dryer.

Lily stood up in the tub. Mama wrapped her in the cozy towel. It felt like being hugged by a cloud.

"That was the best bath ever!" Lily said.

Mama dried Lily's hair with the soft towel. She helped her put on clean pajamas. They were purple with little stars.

"I smell good now," Lily said, sniffing her arm.

"You smell like strawberries," Mama agreed.

They brushed Lily's teeth together. Her toothbrush was blue with sparkles.

"Mama?" Lily said as they walked to her bedroom.

"Yes, sweetie?"

"Can we have bubble bath again tomorrow?"

Mama smiled and tucked Lily into bed. "We can have special bath time whenever you want."

Lily hugged her favorite stuffed bunny. "I love bath time now."

"I love you," Mama said, kissing her forehead.

"I love you too, Mama. Good night."

As Lily drifted off to sleep, she dreamed of floating bubbles and warm, sparkly water. Tomorrow's bath time couldn't come soon enough.

The next evening, Lily ran to the bathroom all by herself. "Is it bubble time yet?" she called happily.

Bath time had become the best part of her day.

The end.
