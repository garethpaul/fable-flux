# Rosie and the Lost Pretty Doll

Rosie was a soft, cuddly doll with bright red yarn hair and a sunny yellow dress. She lived in a cozy dollhouse at the edge of Maple Grove Playground. Every morning, Rosie would peek out her tiny window and watch children play on the swings and slides.

But Rosie had a special secret. Hidden under her little bed was her most treasured possession—a tiny pretty doll with sparkly blue dress and golden curls. Rosie called her "Little Belle," and she loved her more than anything in the whole world.

One breezy Tuesday morning, Rosie decided to take Little Belle on an adventure around the playground. "Come on, Little Belle!" Rosie whispered excitedly. "Let's explore together!"

They rolled down the grassy hill, giggling softly. They hid behind the big oak tree, playing peek-a-boo with the dancing shadows. They even had a tea party on the picnic table using tiny acorn caps as cups.

But when the afternoon sun began to set, something terrible happened. Rosie reached into her pocket for Little Belle, but it was empty!

"Oh no!" Rosie gasped, her button eyes growing wide. "Little Belle is gone!" Her heart felt heavy like a rock in her chest. Where could her precious doll be?

Rosie took a deep breath. "I won't give up," she said firmly. "I'll find you, Little Belle!"

First, Rosie searched the grassy hill where they had rolled. She pushed aside dandelions and clover, looking carefully. The grass tickled her fabric hands, but no Little Belle appeared.

"Hmm," Rosie thought. "Maybe she's by the oak tree."

Rosie hurried to the big tree and searched behind its bumpy bark. She found three shiny beetles and a smooth pebble, but still no Little Belle. The tree's leaves rustled overhead as if whispering, "Keep looking, Rosie!"

Next, Rosie climbed onto the picnic table where they'd had their tea party. She checked under napkins and behind salt shakers. A gentle breeze made the tablecloth flutter, but Little Belle wasn't there either.

Rosie felt tears prick her button eyes. "Where are you, Little Belle?" she called softly.

Then Rosie remembered something important. "I need to think like Little Belle," she said. "Where would a tiny doll go?"

Suddenly, Rosie heard a faint, musical tinkling sound. *Tinkle, tinkle, tinkle.* It was coming from the sandbox! Little Belle's dress had tiny silver bells sewn on it.

Rosie's heart jumped with hope. She rushed to the sandbox as fast as her little legs could carry her. The sand felt cool and grainy under her feet.

There, half-buried in the soft sand near the sandbox edge, was a flash of sparkly blue!

"Little Belle!" Rosie cried joyfully.

She carefully brushed away the sand with gentle pats. Little Belle's golden curls were a bit dusty, but her sweet smile was just the same. The tiny silver bells on her dress chimed softly in the breeze.

"I found you!" Rosie hugged Little Belle tight against her yellow dress. "I was so worried!"

Rosie dusted off Little Belle's sparkly dress and smoothed her golden curls. "You must have fallen out when we were playing near the sandbox," Rosie said. "But I never stopped looking for you."

As the stars began to twinkle overhead, Rosie and Little Belle walked hand in hand back to their dollhouse. Rosie felt proud and happy. She had solved the problem all by herself!

"Tomorrow we'll have another adventure," Rosie whispered to Little Belle as she tucked her safely under the tiny bed. "But this time, I'll hold your hand extra tight."

Little Belle's silver bells chimed softly, as if to say, "Thank you for never giving up on me."

Rosie smiled as she drifted off to sleep. She had learned something very important: when you don't give up and keep trying different ways to solve a problem, you can find what you're looking for. Sometimes the answer is closer than you think—you just have to listen carefully and never stop believing.

The playground was peaceful and quiet, ready for tomorrow's new adventures.

The end.
