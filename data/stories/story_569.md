# Story story_569

**Benny's Musical Day**

The morning sun peeked through the classroom windows. Miss Robin clapped her hands together. "Time for music!" she announced cheerfully.

All the little animals gathered on the colorful carpet. They sat in a big circle. Emma the elephant swayed excitedly. Carlos the cat tapped his paws. Sophie the squirrel bounced up and down.

But Benny the bunny stayed in his chair. His long ears drooped down. His nose twitched nervously.

"Come join us, Benny," called Miss Robin. Her voice was soft and kind.

Benny shook his head. "I don't like music time," he whispered. His voice was very quiet.

The other animals looked at him. Their eyes were full of questions. Why didn't Benny want to play?

"Music is too loud," Benny said. He covered his ears with his paws. "And I can't sing good."

Miss Robin smiled warmly. She walked over to Benny's chair. "Would you like to try something special?" she asked.

Benny peeked through his paws. "What kind of special?"

Miss Robin brought out a small wooden box. It had tiny holes on top. She wound a little key on the side. The most beautiful sound came out.

Ting-a-ling, ting-a-ling. The music box played a gentle song. It sounded like tiny raindrops on flower petals.

Benny's ears perked up. The sound was soft and sweet. Not loud at all.

"This is a music box," Miss Robin explained. "It makes quiet music. Just like whispers."

Benny touched the smooth wooden box. It felt warm in his paws. The melody made him feel calm and happy.

"Can I hold it?" Benny asked shyly.

"Of course!" Miss Robin placed it carefully in his paws.

The other animals gathered around. They listened to the gentle tune. Emma swayed slowly. Carlos purred softly. Sophie smiled big.

"It's like magic," whispered Benny. His eyes sparkled with wonder.

Miss Robin brought out more quiet instruments. She had wooden blocks that clicked softly. She had bells that chimed like wind in trees. She had a small drum that made gentle thumps.

"These make soft sounds," she said. "Perfect for sensitive bunny ears."

Benny tried the wooden blocks first. Click-click, click-click. They made a nice rhythm. Not scary at all.

Then he rang the tiny bell. It made a sweet, clear sound. Like a bird singing good morning.

"I like this!" Benny said. His tail wiggled with excitement.

Soon all the animals were making music together. Emma played big, soft drums. Carlos shook quiet maracas. Sophie tapped triangle chimes.

And Benny? He played his music box and wooden blocks. He even hummed along very quietly.

The classroom filled with gentle sounds. It was like a forest full of friendly animals. Everyone played at their own special volume.

"Listen," said Miss Robin. "We're making beautiful music together."

Benny listened carefully. All the different sounds blended together. They made something wonderful and new.

"We sound like a real band!" Benny exclaimed. He couldn't believe it.

For the rest of music time, Benny played happily. He discovered he loved quiet music. He loved making rhythms with his friends.

When music time ended, Benny felt proud. He had tried something new. He had found his own special way to enjoy music.

"Thank you, Miss Robin," Benny said. He hugged the music box gently.

"You're very welcome," she replied. "Music is for everyone. We just need to find the right kind."

As the animals cleaned up, Benny helped put away instruments. He was careful with each one.

"Will we have music time tomorrow?" Benny asked hopefully.

"Every day," Miss Robin promised.

Benny's ears stood up tall. His nose twitched with happiness. He couldn't wait for tomorrow's music time.

That night, Benny dreamed of gentle melodies. He dreamed of wooden blocks and tiny bells. He dreamed of making beautiful music with all his friends.

Music time had become his favorite time of day.

The end.
