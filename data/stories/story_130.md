# The Brave Little Brother

Tommy was a little brother with soft brown hair and gentle eyes. He loved playing in the sunny meadow behind his house. The grass felt tickly under his bare feet. Colorful flowers danced in the warm breeze.

Today, Tommy had a big problem. He wanted to build a fort. A super special fort with tall walls and a cozy inside. But the sticks were too heavy. The rocks were too big. Tommy's little hands couldn't move them alone.

"I need help," Tommy whispered to himself. But his tummy felt fluttery. His cheeks turned pink. Tommy was too shy to ask anyone.

He watched his big sister Emma playing nearby. She was strong and clever. She could help him build the best fort ever! But Tommy's voice felt stuck in his throat.

"Maybe I can do it myself," Tommy said. He pushed and pulled at a big branch. It wouldn't budge. The branch was three times longer than Tommy!

Tommy tried rolling a round rock. It was heavy as a bowling ball. The rock barely moved an inch. Tommy sat down with a big sigh.

Next, Tommy tried dragging smaller sticks. He made a tiny pile. But it looked like a mouse house, not a fort. Tommy's eyes filled with tears.

"This is too hard," he sniffled.

Just then, his neighbor Mrs. Chen walked by. She had kind eyes and a warm smile. Tommy wanted to ask for help. But his words stayed hidden inside.

Mrs. Chen noticed Tommy's sad face. "What are you building, sweetie?" she asked gently.

Tommy pointed at his little stick pile. "A fort," he whispered very quietly.

"That sounds wonderful!" Mrs. Chen said. "Forts are tricky to build alone."

Tommy nodded. His heart beat fast like a little drum.

Mrs. Chen sat down in the grass. "You know, asking for help takes courage. It makes you brave, not weak."

Tommy looked up with surprise. "Really?"

"Really," Mrs. Chen smiled. "Even grown-ups ask for help."

Tommy took a deep breath. The words felt scary in his mouth. But he tried anyway.

"Could you... help me?" Tommy asked in a tiny voice.

"I would love to help!" Mrs. Chen said cheerfully.

Soon, Emma heard them talking. She ran over with a big grin. "Can I help build the fort too?"

Tommy's face lit up like sunshine. "Yes, please!"

Working together felt magical. Mrs. Chen lifted the heavy branches. Emma arranged the rocks in a circle. Tommy gathered soft leaves for the floor.

"This stick goes here," Tommy said, pointing confidently.

"Great idea!" Emma replied.

They stacked and built and laughed together. The fort grew bigger and stronger. It had tall walls and a doorway. Soft leaves made a comfy carpet inside.

"We did it!" Tommy cheered, clapping his hands.

Inside the fort, everything felt cozy and safe. Sunlight peeked through the stick walls. Birds chirped happy songs overhead.

"Thank you for helping me," Tommy said proudly.

"Thank you for asking," Mrs. Chen replied. "That took real courage."

Emma gave Tommy a big hug. "You're braver than you think, little brother."

Tommy's chest felt warm and full. Asking for help wasn't scary anymore. It felt good! It felt brave!

From that day on, Tommy remembered the important lesson. When problems feel too big to solve alone, asking for help is the bravest thing to do.

And whenever Tommy played in his beautiful fort, he smiled. He remembered how good it felt to be brave. How wonderful it was to work with friends.

The meadow fort became Tommy's favorite place. A place where he learned that being brave doesn't mean doing everything alone. Being brave means reaching out when you need a helping hand.

Tommy was no longer too shy to ask for help. He was a brave little brother who knew that together, anything was possible.
