# Story story_614

**Freddy's Reading Time**

The big clock on the wall went tick-tock-tick. Miss Lily clapped her hands softly. "Reading time, everyone!" she called to her class.

All the little animals found their cozy spots. Bunny picked up a book about carrots. Mouse chose a story about cheese. But Freddy the frog just sat on his lily pad. He looked at the books with big, worried eyes.

"I don't like reading time," Freddy whispered to himself. The letters looked all squiggly. The words seemed too hard. His green fingers felt clumsy holding the books.

Miss Lily noticed Freddy sitting alone. She hopped over with a gentle smile. "What's wrong, Freddy?" she asked in her sweet voice.

"Reading is too hard," Freddy said sadly. His shoulders drooped down. "The letters jump around like flies. I can't catch them with my eyes."

Miss Lily sat beside him on the soft grass. "Would you like to try something special?" she asked. Her eyes twinkled like stars.

She handed Freddy a bright green book. The cover felt smooth and cool. "This book is about a little frog," Miss Lily said. "Just like you!"

Freddy touched the cover carefully. It showed a small frog sitting by a pond. The frog looked friendly and brave. "He looks nice," Freddy said quietly.

Miss Lily opened the book slowly. "Let's read together," she said. "You can help me with the sounds."

The first page had big, clear letters. Miss Lily pointed to each word. "Once upon a time," she read. Her voice was warm like sunshine.

Freddy looked closer. The letters didn't seem so scary now. "Can I try?" he asked. His voice was very small.

"Of course!" Miss Lily smiled. She pointed to a simple word. "This says 'frog.'"

Freddy looked at the letters. F-R-O-G. "Frog!" he said proudly. "That spells frog!" His eyes grew wide with surprise.

Something magical happened then. The letters on the page began to glow softly. They sparkled like dewdrops in the morning sun. Freddy rubbed his eyes and looked again.

The picture of the little frog seemed to move! It waved at Freddy from the page. Freddy giggled with delight. "Did you see that?" he whispered.

Miss Lily winked. "Books are full of magic," she said. "When you read the words, the magic comes alive."

Freddy turned the page carefully. The story frog was jumping into a clear blue pond. Freddy could almost feel the cool water. He could hear the gentle splash.

"The frog swims fast," Freddy read slowly. Each word felt special in his mouth. The magical frog in the book did a happy flip.

Page after page, Freddy read about the brave little frog. The frog made friends with fish. He helped a lost turtle find home. He sang songs under the moon.

Freddy's voice grew stronger with each word. His green fingers turned pages with care. The worried feeling in his tummy went away. Now he felt warm and happy inside.

Other animals gathered around to listen. Bunny and Mouse smiled at Freddy's reading. Even shy Squirrel came closer to hear the story.

When the story ended, Freddy closed the book gently. He hugged it tight against his chest. "That was the best story ever!" he said.

Miss Lily patted his head softly. "You're a wonderful reader, Freddy," she said. "The magic was inside you all along."

Freddy looked at all the other books on the shelf. They didn't look scary anymore. They looked like treasure chests full of adventures. His heart felt light and bouncy.

"Miss Lily," Freddy said with a big smile. "Can we have reading time again tomorrow?"

"Every day," Miss Lily promised. "Books are always here when you're ready for magic."

Freddy hopped home that evening feeling proud and excited. He couldn't wait to tell his family about the magical reading time. Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough!

The end.
