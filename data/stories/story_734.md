# Story story_734

**Benny Bear's Magical Nap**

The afternoon sun shone bright and warm. Mama Bear looked at the big clock. "Nap time, Benny!" she called softly.

Benny Bear was playing with his wooden blocks. He had built a tall tower. Red blocks, blue blocks, yellow blocks stacked high. "But Mama, I'm not sleepy," Benny said. His brown eyes looked sad.

"Come along, little bear," Mama said gently. "Your body needs rest to grow big and strong."

Benny's furry shoulders drooped down. He walked slowly to his cozy bedroom. His soft blue blanket waited on the bed. His favorite teddy sat on the pillow.

"I don't want to nap," Benny whispered. He climbed into bed anyway. The sheets felt cool and smooth. But his eyes stayed wide open.

Mama Bear tucked him in. She kissed his fuzzy forehead. "Sweet dreams, my little cub," she said. Then she tiptoed out quietly.

Benny stared at the ceiling. He counted his toes. One, two, three, four, five. He wiggled them under the blanket. Still not sleepy.

The room felt very quiet. Too quiet. Benny sighed a big sigh.

Suddenly, something sparkled near the window. Tiny golden lights danced in the air. They twirled and spun like little stars. "Oh my!" Benny gasped softly.

The lights grew brighter and bigger. They made gentle tinkling sounds. Like tiny silver bells ringing. The sounds were soft and musical.

One golden light floated closer to Benny. It glowed warm and friendly. "Hello, little bear," it whispered. "We are the Dream Sprites."

"Dream Sprites?" Benny asked. His eyes grew round with wonder.

"We help children have magical naps," the sprite said. "Would you like to come with us?"

Benny nodded his head yes. The golden light touched his paw. Suddenly, he felt light as a feather. He floated up from his bed.

The Dream Sprites led him through fluffy white clouds. The clouds felt soft like cotton candy. They smelled sweet like vanilla cookies. Benny giggled with joy.

They flew over a rainbow bridge. The colors were bright and beautiful. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple. Each color hummed a different tune.

Below the bridge was a magical meadow. Butterflies danced among purple flowers. A gentle stream bubbled and laughed. The water sparkled like diamonds.

"This is the Sleepy Meadow," the sprite explained. "Here, rest feels wonderful."

Benny landed on the soft grass. It felt like the coziest carpet ever. Friendly rabbits hopped by and waved. Sleepy owls sang lullabies from tall trees.

A warm breeze blew gently. It smelled like fresh flowers and honey. Benny's eyelids started to feel heavy. His body felt relaxed and peaceful.

"Napping feels nice here," Benny said with a yawn.

"Rest helps you grow and learn," the sprite said kindly. "When you wake up, you'll feel happy and strong."

Benny curled up on the magical grass. The Dream Sprites sang a soft song. Their voices sounded like wind chimes. Soon, Benny's eyes closed tight.

He dreamed of flying with friendly birds. He dreamed of playing in sunny gardens. He dreamed of building the tallest block tower ever.

When Benny woke up, he was back in his own bed. Afternoon sunlight streamed through his window. He felt wonderful and refreshed.

"Mama!" he called happily. "I had the best nap ever!"

Mama Bear came into his room. She smiled warmly. "You look bright and cheerful, little cub."

"I met Dream Sprites!" Benny said excitedly. "They showed me a magical place. Napping was really fun!"

Mama Bear hugged him tight. "I'm so glad you enjoyed your rest."

Benny jumped out of bed. He felt full of energy. His body felt strong and ready to play. "Can I take another nap tomorrow?" he asked.

"Of course," Mama laughed. "Nap time comes every day."

Benny grinned a big bear grin. Now he couldn't wait for tomorrow's nap. Maybe the Dream Sprites would take him somewhere new. Rest time had become his favorite time.

From that day on, Benny Bear loved nap time. And he always slept peacefully.

The end.
