# Lily's Big Search

Lily loved visiting Grandpa Joe's farm. The red barn stood tall and proud. Chickens clucked in their pen. Cows mooed softly in the green pasture.

But today, something was very wrong.

"Oh no!" Lily cried. Her favorite red ball was gone! She had been playing catch with Grandpa Joe. The bright red ball bounced so high. It sparkled in the sunny sky.

Now it was nowhere to be seen.

Lily's eyes filled with tears. "Grandpa Joe, I can't find my ball!" she said. Her voice was shaky and sad.

Grandpa Joe knelt down beside her. His kind eyes crinkled at the corners. "Don't worry, little one," he said gently. "We'll find it together. Sometimes the best treasures are just hiding."

Lily wiped her nose with her sleeve. She took a deep breath. "Okay," she said. "Let's look everywhere!"

First, they searched the vegetable garden. Lily pushed aside the big green leaves. She looked under the orange pumpkins. She checked behind the tall corn stalks.

No red ball anywhere.

"Maybe it's in the chicken coop," said Lily. She walked carefully around the fluffy white chickens. They pecked at the ground and made soft clucking sounds.

Still no ball.

Next, Lily looked in the pig pen. The pink pigs rolled in the cool mud. They snorted and oinked happily. Lily giggled at their muddy snouts.

But her ball wasn't there either.

Lily felt tired and worried. Her shoulders drooped down. "Where could it be?" she wondered aloud.

"Let's try the hay barn," suggested Grandpa Joe. "Sometimes balls like to hide in cozy places."

Inside the barn, everything smelled like sweet, dry grass. Golden hay bales reached up to the ceiling. Dust particles danced in the sunbeams.

Lily climbed carefully on the hay bales. She looked in every corner. She moved aside loose hay with her hands.

"I still can't find it," she said sadly.

Grandpa Joe sat on a hay bale. He patted his knee. "Come here, Lily," he said softly.

Lily climbed onto his lap. She felt warm and safe.

"Sometimes," said Grandpa Joe, "we need to think like detectives. Where did you last see your ball?"

Lily closed her eyes tight. She thought very hard. "We were playing catch by the apple tree," she remembered.

"And which way did the ball go?" asked Grandpa Joe.

"It went really, really high!" said Lily. She pointed up with her finger. "Then it came down fast!"

Grandpa Joe smiled. "Let's check the apple tree again. But this time, let's look up!"

They walked to the big apple tree. Its branches spread wide like giant arms. Red apples hung heavy on the green branches.

Lily looked up, up, up into the tree. At first, she saw only leaves and apples.

Then she saw something else.

"There!" she shouted with joy. "My red ball!"

High in the apple tree, stuck between two branches, was her bright red ball. It looked like a big red apple!

"How will we get it down?" asked Lily.

Grandpa Joe found a long wooden stick. He reached up carefully. He gently pushed the ball.

The red ball tumbled down, down, down. Lily caught it in her arms. She hugged it tight.

"We found it! We found it!" she sang happily.

The ball felt smooth and bouncy in her hands. It was just as bright and beautiful as before.

"You never gave up," said Grandpa Joe proudly. "That's what good problem-solvers do."

Lily beamed with pride. "I looked everywhere," she said. "And I kept trying!"

"That's right," said Grandpa Joe. "When something is important to us, we don't stop looking."

Lily bounced her red ball on the soft grass. It made a gentle thump-thump sound. The chickens clucked nearby. The sun felt warm on her face.

She had learned something special today. Problems can be solved if you keep trying. Sometimes you just need to look at things differently.

"Want to play catch again?" asked Grandpa Joe.

"Yes!" said Lily. "But this time, I'll watch where it goes!"

They played until the sun began to set. Lily never lost her ball again.

The end.
