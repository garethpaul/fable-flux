# Story story_599

**Bella's Bath Time Adventure**

Bella loved her favorite book. It had bright pictures of animals. Every day, she carried it everywhere.

"Bath time, Bella!" called Mama.

Bella hugged her book tight. She did not want to leave it. The book felt warm in her hands. It smelled like vanilla cookies.

"Can my book come too?" asked Bella.

Mama smiled. "Books don't like water, sweetie. They might get hurt."

Bella's bottom lip stuck out. She put the book on her bed. It looked lonely there.

The bathtub was full of warm water. Bubbles danced on top. They smelled like strawberries. But Bella felt sad without her book.

"I miss my animal friends," she said.

Mama had an idea. She reached under the sink. Out came a special bag.

"What's that?" asked Bella.

"Bath books!" said Mama. "These books love water."

Bella's eyes grew wide. The books were soft and squishy. They had bright colors just like her bedroom book.

Mama dropped one in the water. It floated like a boat! The pages showed a happy duck.

"It's swimming!" laughed Bella.

She climbed into the warm tub. The water felt like a cozy hug. Bubbles tickled her toes.

Bella picked up the floating book. Water dripped from its pages. But the duck still smiled at her.

"Hello, duck," whispered Bella.

She turned the page. A frog appeared. It was green and shiny.

"Ribbit!" said Bella. She made the frog hop in the water.

Mama added more bath books. Soon five books floated around Bella. Each one had different animals.

There was a splashing fish. A swimming turtle. A diving penguin. A floating seahorse.

"Look, Mama! They're having a party!"

Bella made the books dance in the bubbles. The turtle raced the fish. The penguin played with the seahorse.

The water stayed warm and cozy. Bubbles popped softly. They sounded like tiny kisses.

"This book is drinking water!" giggled Bella. She squeezed it gently. Water came out like rain.

Mama washed Bella's hair with sweet-smelling shampoo. Bella kept reading her floating books.

"The duck says quack-quack," read Bella. "The frog says ribbit-ribbit."

She practiced all the animal sounds. Her voice echoed in the bathroom. It sounded funny and magical.

"These books are brave," said Bella. "They're not scared of water."

"Just like you," said Mama. "You're brave too."

Bella felt proud. She was brave like the swimming animals. She could read in the bathtub!

The penguin book showed the animal sliding on ice. Bella made it slide across the bubbles.

"Wheee!" she called.

Time passed quickly. The water stayed perfectly warm. Bella's fingers looked like tiny raisins.

"Five more minutes?" asked Bella.

"Okay," said Mama. "Five more minutes with your water friends."

Bella hugged each book goodbye. The soft pages felt different from her bedroom book. But they were just as special.

She climbed out of the tub. Mama wrapped her in a fluffy towel. It was warm from the dryer.

"Where do the bath books sleep?" asked Bella.

"In their special basket," said Mama. "They need to dry off too."

Bella helped put each book away. She patted them gently.

"Thank you for swimming with me," she whispered.

In her pajamas, Bella felt clean and happy. Her skin smelled like strawberry bubbles. Her hair was soft and shiny.

Mama read her bedroom book before sleep. It was dry and cozy. The animals looked ready for dreams.

"Mama?" said Bella. "Can we have another book party tomorrow?"

"In the bathtub?" asked Mama.

"Yes! The books want to swim again."

Mama kissed Bella's forehead. "Sweet dreams, my little reader."

Bella closed her eyes. She dreamed of books that could swim. And ducks that could read.

Tomorrow would bring another magical bath time adventure.

The end.
