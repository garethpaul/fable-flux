# Story story_1105

**The Tiger and the High-Up Cookies**

Tiger loved to play at Sunny Park. The playground had swings that squeaked. It had slides that sparkled in the sun. Best of all, it had a big climbing tree.

Today, Tiger's nose twitched. Something smelled wonderful. Sweet and warm and yummy. Tiger followed his nose across the soft grass.

There! High up in the oak tree sat a basket. The basket was full of golden cookies. They smelled like honey and oats. Tiger's tummy rumbled loudly.

"Those cookies look so good," Tiger said. His orange and black stripes seemed to wiggle with excitement.

Tiger stretched his paws up high. He reached and reached. But the basket was too far away. Even on his tippy-toes, Tiger couldn't touch it.

"I really want those cookies," Tiger sighed. His whiskers drooped sadly.

Tiger had an idea. He would jump! Tiger crouched down low. His muscles felt tight and ready. He sprang up with all his might.

Up, up, up Tiger flew! But not high enough. The basket stayed out of reach. Tiger landed with a soft thump on the grass.

"Jumping didn't work," Tiger said. He sat down and thought hard. His tail swished back and forth.

Then Tiger noticed something. The climbing tree had thick, sturdy branches. They made a ladder up to the cookies!

"I can climb!" Tiger said. His eyes sparkled with hope.

Tiger walked to the tree trunk. The bark felt rough under his paws. He dug his claws in gently. Step by step, Tiger climbed up.

First branch. Tiger felt proud. Second branch. His heart beat fast with excitement. Third branch. Almost there!

The basket sat on the fourth branch. Tiger climbed carefully. The branch was strong and wide. Perfect for a tiger to sit on.

"I did it!" Tiger cheered. The cookies smelled even better up close. They were round and golden. Little oats sparkled on top.

Tiger took one cookie. It tasted sweet and crunchy. Honey flavor danced on his tongue. It was the best cookie ever!

But then Tiger looked down. Other animals played below. Rabbit hopped by the swings. Squirrel chased his tail near the slide. Mouse picked flowers by the fence.

Tiger's tummy felt full from one cookie. But his heart felt empty. The cookies would taste better with friends.

"Hey everyone!" Tiger called down. "Want to share some cookies?"

Rabbit's ears perked up. "Cookies? Yes please!"

Squirrel stopped spinning. "We love cookies!"

Mouse dropped her flowers. "How wonderful!"

Tiger carefully climbed down. He brought the whole basket. The cookies made soft clinking sounds as he moved.

All the animals sat in a circle on the warm grass. Tiger passed the basket around. Everyone took one perfect cookie.

"These are delicious!" said Rabbit. Cookie crumbs dotted her whiskers.

"So sweet!" said Squirrel. His fluffy tail swished happily.

"Thank you for sharing," said Mouse. Her tiny paws held the cookie carefully.

Tiger felt warm inside. The sun shone on his stripes. His friends smiled at him. The cookies tasted even better when shared.

"I couldn't reach the cookies at first," Tiger said. "But I kept trying. Climbing worked better than jumping."

"You didn't give up," said Rabbit proudly.

"And you shared with us," added Squirrel.

"That makes you a very good friend," finished Mouse.

Tiger's chest puffed out with pride. He had solved his problem. He had shared his treats. Best of all, he had wonderful friends.

The animals finished their cookies. They played together until sunset. The playground filled with laughter and joy.

That night, Tiger went home happy. His tummy was full of cookies. His heart was full of friendship. He had learned something important.

When something seems too hard, try a different way. And good things are always better when shared with friends.

Tiger fell asleep with a big smile on his striped face.

The end.
