# Emma and the Big Book Adventure

Emma skipped into the bright, cozy library. Tall shelves reached up to the ceiling. Colorful books lined every wall. The carpet felt soft under her feet.

"Welcome back, Emma!" said Mrs. Chen, the librarian. Her voice was warm like hot chocolate.

Emma loved story time at the library. She could hardly wait! But first, she wanted to find her favorite book. The one with the purple dragon.

Emma searched the picture book section. Her eyes scanned shelf after shelf. There it was! The purple dragon book sat on the middle shelf.

Emma reached up high. She grabbed the special book. She hugged it tight against her chest.

Just then, a little boy walked over. His name was Sam. Sam had curly hair and big brown eyes.

"That's my favorite book too," Sam said quietly. His voice sounded sad.

Emma looked at Sam. Then she looked at her book. She held it even tighter.

"I found it first," Emma said. She turned away from Sam.

Sam's bottom lip started to shake. A tear rolled down his cheek. Emma felt funny in her tummy. Not the good kind of funny.

Emma sat in the reading corner. She opened the purple dragon book. But the pictures didn't look as bright. The story didn't feel as magical.

She peeked over at Sam. He sat alone by the window. He looked at other books. But his shoulders drooped down low.

Emma tried to read her book again. The words seemed jumbled up. She couldn't focus on the dragon's adventure.

"Maybe I could..." Emma whispered to herself. She closed the book. She thought hard.

Emma had an idea! She walked over to Sam. Her heart beat fast like a drum.

"Sam?" Emma said softly. "Do you want to read this together?"

Sam looked up. His eyes grew wide with surprise.

"Really?" Sam asked. "You would share?"

Emma nodded. "We could take turns. I'll read one page. Then you read one page."

Sam's face lit up like sunshine. "That sounds perfect!"

Emma and Sam found a cozy spot. They sat on the big, squishy bean bag. Emma opened the book to page one.

"Once upon a time," Emma read clearly, "there lived a brave purple dragon."

Sam listened carefully. His eyes followed every word. When Emma finished the page, she passed the book to Sam.

"The dragon loved to fly," Sam read with excitement. "She soared high above the clouds."

They took turns reading each page. When Sam struggled with a big word, Emma helped him. When Emma forgot what happened next, Sam reminded her.

The purple dragon's adventure felt more exciting than ever! Emma discovered something wonderful. Sharing made the story twice as fun.

"Look!" Sam pointed to a picture. "The dragon found a friend!"

"Just like us!" Emma giggled. She felt warm and happy inside.

Soon, Mrs. Chen called for story time. Emma and Sam walked to the circle together. They sat side by side on the colorful rug.

"Did you enjoy your books?" Mrs. Chen asked the children.

"We shared!" Emma said proudly. "It was the best reading ever!"

Sam nodded eagerly. "Emma taught me new words. And I helped her remember the story!"

Mrs. Chen smiled big. "Sharing books makes reading extra special. You both did something wonderful today."

After story time, Emma and Sam walked to the book return. They placed the purple dragon book in the slot together.

"Same time next week?" Sam asked hopefully.

"Yes!" Emma said. "We can find another book to share!"

Emma waved goodbye to Sam. She skipped out of the library with a huge smile. Her heart felt full of joy.

Emma had learned something important today. Sharing didn't mean getting less. Sharing meant getting more! More fun, more friendship, and more happiness.

She couldn't wait to come back to the library. Next time, she would look for Sam first. Then they could pick out a book together.

Emma hummed a happy tune all the way home. The library had given her the best gift of all. Not just a story, but a new friend.

And that was better than any book on the shelf.

The end.
