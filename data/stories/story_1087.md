# Story story_1087

**The Brave Little Owl**

Ollie the owl lived in a cozy closet. His home was warm and dark. He loved his soft blanket nest. The closet smelled like lavender soap.

One evening, Ollie heard scary sounds. BOOM! CRASH! Thunder shook the walls. Lightning flashed under the door. Rain pounded on the roof. The storm was very loud.

Ollie's feathers trembled. His big yellow eyes grew wide. "Oh no!" he hooted softly. "The storm sounds so scary!"

The thunder got louder. BOOM! BOOM! BOOM! Ollie pulled his blanket over his head. But he could still hear everything. His heart beat fast like a drum.

"I need to make the noise stop," Ollie said. He looked around his closet home. Maybe something could help him feel safe.

Ollie saw cotton balls on a shelf. "These are soft and fluffy," he thought. "They might block the scary sounds." He took the cotton balls. He stuffed them in his ears.

BOOM! The thunder still came through. The cotton balls were too small. They fell right out. Ollie sighed. "That didn't work," he said sadly.

The storm kept going. Rain hit the windows. Wind howled outside. Ollie felt scared again. But he didn't give up.

"I'll try something else," Ollie said bravely. He looked around again. His eyes spotted a big winter coat. It was thick and puffy. The coat hung on a hook.

"This coat is much bigger," Ollie thought. "Maybe it can help me." He flew up to the coat. He snuggled deep inside the soft pockets. The coat felt warm and safe.

Ollie pulled the coat around himself. It was like a cozy cave. The thick fabric made the thunder sound far away. The storm seemed much quieter now.

"This is better!" Ollie hooted happily. He felt snug in his coat cave. The scary sounds became gentle whispers. The thunder sounded like a distant drum.

Ollie closed his eyes. He listened to the soft rain. It sounded like a lullaby now. The wind became a gentle song. Even the thunder seemed friendly.

"The storm isn't so scary anymore," Ollie said. He felt proud of himself. "I didn't give up. I kept trying until I found something that worked."

Soon, Ollie felt sleepy. The coat was so warm and comfortable. The storm sounds became peaceful music. His eyes grew heavy. He yawned a big owl yawn.

The thunder got softer. The rain became gentle drops. The storm was moving away. But Ollie felt safe and calm now. He wasn't worried anymore.

"Tomorrow I'll tell my friends," Ollie thought sleepily. "I'll tell them how I was brave. I'll share what I learned about trying again."

Ollie snuggled deeper into the coat. His feathers felt warm and dry. The closet smelled like home. Everything felt just right.

As morning came, the storm ended. Sunshine peeked under the door. Birds sang happy songs outside. The scary night was over.

Ollie stretched his wings. He felt different now. He felt braver and stronger. "I solved my problem all by myself," he said proudly.

He flew back to his blanket nest. But now he knew something special. When problems seem too big, he could try different solutions. If one way doesn't work, another way might.

"Storms come and go," Ollie said wisely. "But I can always find ways to feel safe and brave."

From that day on, Ollie wasn't afraid of storms. He kept the big coat nearby. He remembered that trying again often leads to success. And he felt proud of being a brave, smart little owl.

The closet felt more like home than ever. Ollie had learned that with patience and creativity, even scary problems can be solved.

The end.
