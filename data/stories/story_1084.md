# Story story_1084

**Jack and the Veggie Swing**

Jack loved his red swing set. He swung high every day after lunch. Up and down, back and forth. The swing made him feel like he was flying.

But Jack had a big problem. He did not like vegetables. Carrots were too crunchy. Broccoli looked like tiny trees. Green beans were too squishy. Peas rolled around his plate.

"Eat your vegetables, Jack," said Mom every day. "They help you grow big and strong."

Jack would frown. He would push the vegetables around his plate. Then he would run outside to his swing.

One sunny morning, Jack sat on his swing. He pushed off with his feet. But something felt wrong. The swing moved very slowly. Jack felt tired and weak.

"Why can't I swing high today?" Jack wondered. His legs felt heavy. His arms felt wobbly.

Jack tried again. He pushed harder with his feet. The swing barely moved. Jack felt sad and confused.

Mom came outside. She saw Jack sitting still on his swing.

"What's wrong, sweetie?" Mom asked gently.

"I can't swing high anymore," Jack said. His voice was quiet. "I feel too tired."

Mom sat on the grass next to the swing. "Maybe your body needs more energy," she said. "Vegetables give us energy to play and swing."

Jack shook his head. "But vegetables taste yucky."

Mom smiled. "Let's try something fun. What if we make vegetables taste better?"

For his first try, Mom put cheese on the broccoli. The cheese smelled good. It was warm and melty. But Jack still wrinkled his nose. The broccoli still looked like little green trees. He took one tiny bite and made a face.

"Still yucky," Jack said sadly. He went to his swing again. But he still felt weak and tired.

The next day, Mom had a new idea. "Let's make vegetables into fun shapes," she said.

Mom cut carrots into star shapes. She made faces on cherry tomatoes with tiny dots. She arranged green beans like a rainbow on Jack's plate. The vegetables looked like colorful art.

"Wow!" said Jack. His eyes grew wide. "The carrots look like stars in the sky!"

Jack picked up a star carrot. It crunched between his teeth. It tasted sweet and fresh. "This is good!" he said with surprise.

He tried a smiley tomato. It was juicy and burst with flavor. The green bean rainbow made him giggle. Each bean was crisp and tasty.

"More please!" Jack said happily. He ate all the star carrots. He ate every smiley tomato. He finished the whole green bean rainbow.

After lunch, Jack ran to his swing set. He felt different. His legs felt strong. His arms felt powerful.

Jack pushed off hard with his feet. The swing soared high into the air. Higher and higher he went. The wind whooshed through his hair. His tummy felt fluttery with excitement.

"Look, Mom!" Jack called out. "I'm flying again!"

Mom clapped her hands. "You have energy from eating vegetables!"

Jack swung back and forth with joy. He felt amazing. His body felt strong and happy.

From that day on, Jack ate vegetables every day. Mom made them into fun shapes. Sometimes they were dinosaurs. Sometimes they were flowers. Sometimes they were silly faces.

Jack learned that vegetables could taste good. They gave him energy to swing high. They helped him run fast and play hard.

Every afternoon, Jack would finish his colorful vegetables. Then he would rush to his red swing set. He would soar through the air with a big smile.

"Thank you, vegetables!" Jack would shout as he swung. "You help me fly!"

Jack discovered that trying new things could be fun. Sometimes the second try works better than the first. Now Jack loved vegetables almost as much as he loved swinging.

And his swing set carried him higher than ever before.

The end.
