# Story story_987

**Grandma's Hallway Game**

Grandma sat in the long hallway. She rolled a red ball back and forth. The ball made soft sounds on the wooden floor. Thump, thump, thump.

Grandma sighed. The house felt very quiet. She wished someone would play with her. The hallway seemed so empty and long.

Little Emma peeked around the corner. She saw Grandma sitting alone. Emma's eyes grew wide with curiosity.

"What are you doing, Grandma?" Emma asked softly.

Grandma looked up and smiled. "I'm playing ball, sweetie. But it's not much fun alone."

Emma's face lit up like sunshine. "Can I play too? Please?"

"Of course!" Grandma said. Her voice sounded happy again. "Come sit with me."

Emma ran to Grandma. Her little feet pattered on the floor. She plopped down across from Grandma. The hallway didn't feel empty anymore.

Grandma rolled the red ball to Emma. It traveled slowly across the smooth wood. Emma caught it with both hands.

"I got it!" Emma giggled. She rolled the ball back to Grandma.

They took turns rolling the ball. Back and forth it went. The soft thumping filled the quiet hallway.

"This is fun!" Emma said. Her cheeks turned pink with excitement.

Grandma's eyes twinkled. "Let's try something new. Can you roll it very slowly?"

Emma nodded. She pushed the ball gently. It moved like a sleepy turtle. Grandma clapped her hands.

"Now try rolling it fast!" Grandma suggested.

Emma gave the ball a big push. It zoomed across the floor. Grandma had to reach quickly to catch it.

"Whee!" Emma laughed. "That was super fast!"

They played for a long time. Sometimes the ball went straight. Sometimes it bumped into the wall. They didn't mind at all.

"Let's count how many times we roll it," Grandma said.

"One!" Emma called out as she rolled.

"Two!" Grandma counted as she rolled back.

"Three!" Emma shouted with joy.

They counted all the way to ten. Emma felt very proud. She knew her numbers well.

"I have another idea," Grandma said. Her eyes sparkled with mischief. "Let's sing while we play."

Grandma started singing a simple song. "Roll the ball, roll the ball, back and forth we go!"

Emma joined in. Her sweet voice mixed with Grandma's warm voice. The hallway filled with music and laughter.

The ball rolled to the beat of their song. Their voices echoed softly off the walls. The house didn't feel quiet anymore.

"This is the best game ever!" Emma declared. She clapped her hands together.

Grandma felt her heart grow warm. "It's better because you're here with me."

Emma crawled over to Grandma. She gave her the biggest hug. Grandma hugged her back tightly.

"I love playing with you, Grandma," Emma whispered.

"I love playing with you too, my dear," Grandma whispered back.

They sat together in the hallway. The red ball rested between them. Sunlight streamed through the window. It made pretty patterns on the floor.

"Can we play again tomorrow?" Emma asked hopefully.

"Every day if you want," Grandma promised. "This can be our special hallway game."

Emma bounced with excitement. "Yes! Our very own game!"

From that day on, they played together often. The hallway became their favorite place. It wasn't long and empty anymore. It was full of love and laughter.

Grandma never felt lonely again. Emma had a new best friend. They discovered that the simplest games are the most fun. Especially when you share them with someone you love.

The red ball sat waiting in the hallway. It was ready for their next adventure together. And there would be many more to come.

The end.
