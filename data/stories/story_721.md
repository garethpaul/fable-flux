# Story story_721

**Mama's Big Day**

Mama looked around the bright nursery room. Colorful toys filled the shelves. Soft rugs covered the floor. Little chairs sat in a circle.

"Today is my first day," Mama whispered. Her tummy felt funny. Like butterflies were dancing inside.

Mama was starting her new job. She would help take care of children. But she felt very nervous.

The other teachers smiled at her. "Welcome, Mama!" said Miss Jenny. Her voice was warm and kind.

Children began arriving. They ran and played. They laughed and sang. But Mama stayed by the wall.

"What if I make mistakes?" Mama thought. "What if the children don't like me?"

A little boy named Sam started crying. His mama had just left. Tears rolled down his cheeks.

"I want my mama!" Sam sobbed.

Mama wanted to help. But her hands shook. Her heart beat fast. She didn't know what to do.

Miss Jenny picked up Sam. She rocked him gently. Soon Sam stopped crying.

"I should have helped," Mama thought sadly.

Later, the children sat for story time. Miss Jenny asked Mama to read. The book felt heavy in Mama's hands.

Mama opened the book. But her voice came out tiny. Like a mouse squeaking.

"Once upon a time..." she whispered.

The children couldn't hear her. They started talking to each other. Some got up and walked away.

Mama's cheeks turned red. She felt like hiding. This was too hard.

"Maybe I'm not good at this," Mama said to herself.

At lunch time, Mama sat alone. She watched the other teachers. They made it look so easy.

Miss Jenny sat beside her. "How are you feeling?" she asked.

"Scared," Mama said honestly. "I don't think I can do this."

Miss Jenny smiled. "I felt the same way once. Do you know what helped me?"

Mama shook her head.

"I remembered why I wanted this job. I love children. That's what matters most."

Mama thought about this. She did love children. That's why she came here.

After lunch, little Emma fell down. She scraped her knee. Emma cried loudly.

This time, Mama didn't think. She just moved. Her feet carried her quickly to Emma.

"Oh sweetie," Mama said softly. She knelt down beside Emma. "Let me see."

Mama's voice was gentle. Her hands were steady now. She cleaned Emma's knee carefully.

"It stings," Emma sniffled.

"I know," Mama said. "But you're very brave. Would you like a special bandage?"

Emma nodded. Mama put on a bandage with stars. Emma smiled through her tears.

"Thank you, Mama," Emma said. She hugged Mama tight.

Mama's heart felt warm. The nervous feeling was going away.

Later, it was story time again. Mama picked up a different book. This time, she thought about Emma's smile.

"Who wants to hear about a little bunny?" Mama asked.

All the children raised their hands. "Me! Me!" they called out.

Mama began reading. Her voice was clear and strong. She made funny voices for each character. The children giggled and clapped.

"More! More!" they shouted when the story ended.

Mama felt proud. She could do this after all.

At the end of the day, Sam ran to Mama. "Will you be here tomorrow?" he asked.

"Yes," Mama said with a big smile. "I'll be here."

Miss Jenny patted Mama's shoulder. "You did wonderful today. The children love you already."

Mama looked around the nursery. It felt different now. It felt like home.

Walking to her car, Mama thought about her day. She had been scared. She had made mistakes. But she didn't give up.

"Tomorrow will be even better," Mama said to herself.

The butterflies in her tummy were gone. Now she felt excited. She couldn't wait to see the children again.

Sometimes new things feel scary. But when we keep trying, we can do amazing things. Mama learned that being nervous is okay. What matters is not giving up.

And the children? They thought Mama was perfect just the way she was.

The end.
