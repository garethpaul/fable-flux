# Story story_605

**Reading Time Magic**

The big clock on the wall went tick-tock, tick-tock. "Reading time!" called Miss Jenny to all the children. Everyone sat on the soft, colorful carpet. Everyone except one little mouse.

Pip the mouse hid behind a stack of books. His tiny whiskers twitched nervously. Reading time felt too long and too quiet. The letters on the pages looked jumbled and confusing. Other children seemed to love story time, but Pip just wanted to play with blocks instead.

"Come sit with us, Pip," Miss Jenny said gently. Her voice was warm like honey. Pip's little paws clutched his favorite toy car. He shook his head and stayed hidden.

Miss Jenny picked up a big book with a shiny cover. "Today we're reading about a brave little train," she announced. The other children wiggled with excitement. Pip peeked out just a tiny bit.

As Miss Jenny opened the book, something magical happened. The pages began to glow with a soft, golden light. Pip rubbed his eyes with his small paws. Was he dreaming?

"I think I can, I think I can," Miss Jenny read in a cheerful voice. Suddenly, Pip heard a real train whistle! Choo-choo! The sound came right from the book.

Pip crept closer. His nose twitched at the smell of fresh mountain air. The book's pictures seemed to move and dance. He could see the little blue train puffing up a steep hill.

"The train needs help," whispered Sarah, a kind girl with curly hair. "Maybe Pip can help us cheer for the train." She patted the carpet next to her.

Pip's ears perked up. Help the train? That sounded important. He scurried over and sat down. The carpet felt soft and cozy under his tiny bottom.

Miss Jenny continued reading. "I think I can, I think I can." All the children joined in, saying the words together. Pip's small voice squeaked along too.

The magical book showed the train working very hard. Pip could almost feel the train's determination. Just like when he tried to reach the high shelf for his favorite book about cheese.

"The train is just like us," Pip whispered to Sarah. "Sometimes things are hard, but we keep trying." Sarah smiled and nodded.

As the story continued, Pip smelled the sweet scent of flowers from the book's garden scenes. He heard birds chirping and felt a gentle breeze. The words didn't seem scary anymore. They painted beautiful pictures in his mind.

When the little train finally reached the top of the hill, all the children cheered. "Hooray!" squeaked Pip, jumping up and down. His tail wiggled with joy.

"I did it! I did it!" read Miss Jenny as the train celebrated. Pip felt proud too. He had listened to the whole story. The letters and words had told him about a brave friend.

Miss Jenny closed the book gently. The golden glow faded, but the warm feeling stayed. "What did you think, Pip?" she asked.

Pip's whiskers twitched happily. "The train was brave," he said. "And the words made pictures in my head. Pretty pictures with colors and sounds."

"That's the magic of reading," Miss Jenny explained. "Books take us on wonderful adventures. They help us meet new friends and learn new things."

Pip looked around at all the books on the shelves. Each one held a different adventure. Maybe tomorrow's story would have a mouse hero, or a magical forest, or a flying bicycle.

"Will you read another train story tomorrow?" Pip asked hopefully. His small paws were already reaching for another book.

"We can explore any story you'd like," Miss Jenny promised. "Reading time is adventure time."

That night, Pip dreamed of little trains and magical books. He couldn't wait for tomorrow's reading time. Maybe he would even help Miss Jenny turn the pages.

From that day on, Pip was always first on the reading carpet. He discovered that books weren't just words on pages. They were doorways to amazing worlds, just waiting for him to step inside.

The end.
