# Story story_930

**The Baby and the Lion at the Swing Set**

Little Maya sat on the red swing. She pushed her tiny feet back and forth. The swing moved just a little bit. Maya felt sad and lonely.

The playground was very quiet. No other children were there. Maya's mommy sat on a bench nearby. She was reading a book.

Maya looked at the empty swings. She wished someone would play with her. The sun felt warm on her face. Birds chirped in the tall trees.

Suddenly, Maya heard soft footsteps. A young lion walked slowly toward the swings. His golden fur shined in the sunlight. His eyes were kind and gentle.

The lion stopped near Maya's swing. He tilted his head and looked at her. Maya stopped swinging. She stared at the lion with wide eyes.

"Hello, little one," said the lion in a soft voice. "My name is Leo. What's your name?"

Maya felt a little scared but also curious. "I'm Maya," she whispered. Her voice was very quiet.

Leo smiled a big, friendly smile. "Would you like to play together, Maya? I love swings too."

Maya nodded slowly. She pointed to the blue swing next to hers. "You can use that one, Leo."

Leo walked carefully to the blue swing. He sat down gently. The swing creaked a little under his weight. Leo pushed off with his strong paws.

Maya started swinging again too. Soon they were swinging side by side. The wind blew through Maya's hair. Leo's mane flowed in the breeze.

"This is fun!" Maya giggled. Her feet kicked happily in the air. She felt much better now.

Leo laughed too. His laugh sounded like a gentle rumble. "I love swinging high! Do you want to go higher, Maya?"

"Yes!" Maya said with excitement. She pumped her legs harder. Leo showed her how to lean back and forth.

They swung higher and higher together. Maya felt like she was flying. The playground looked different from up high. Everything seemed magical.

"Look at the clouds, Leo!" Maya pointed up at the sky. "That one looks like a butterfly!"

Leo looked up too. "I see it! And that one looks like a big ice cream cone!"

They played a cloud game while swinging. Maya forgot all about feeling lonely. Leo was the best friend she could imagine.

After a while, they slowed down their swings. Maya's cheeks were pink from the fresh air. Leo's golden fur sparkled in the sunshine.

"Thank you for playing with me, Leo," Maya said sweetly. "I was feeling sad before you came."

Leo's eyes were warm and caring. "I was lonely too, Maya. I'm so happy we found each other."

Maya's mommy looked up from her book. She smiled when she saw Maya laughing. Maya waved at her mommy excitedly.

"Leo, will you come back tomorrow?" Maya asked hopefully. She didn't want their fun to end.

"Of course!" Leo said with joy. "We can swing every day if you want. We can be best friends."

Maya clapped her hands together. "Yes! Best friends forever!"

They got off their swings carefully. Leo helped Maya down from her swing. His paw was gentle and warm.

Maya gave Leo a big hug. His fur was soft like her favorite teddy bear. Leo hugged her back very gently.

"See you tomorrow, Maya!" Leo waved goodbye. He walked away with a happy bounce in his step.

Maya ran to her mommy. "Mommy! I made a new friend! His name is Leo!"

Her mommy hugged her tight. "That's wonderful, sweetheart. You both looked so happy together."

Maya looked back at the swings. She couldn't wait for tomorrow. She had learned something special today. When you're kind to others, you can make amazing friends.

The playground didn't feel lonely anymore. It felt full of friendship and joy. Maya smiled all the way home.

The end.
