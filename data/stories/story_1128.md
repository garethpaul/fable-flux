# Story story_1128

**Penny Pig's New Friend**

Penny Pig sat alone in the sunny nursery. She had pink, soft skin and tiny, wiggly ears. The nursery smelled like fresh hay and sweet milk. Penny pushed her red ball around the room.

"Roll, roll, roll," she said softly. The ball bumped into the wall. Penny sighed. Playing alone was not very fun.

She looked out the big window. Other animals played together in the yard. Lambs jumped and ran. Chickens pecked and clucked. Penny felt sad and lonely.

"I wish I had a friend," Penny whispered. Her little heart felt heavy.

Just then, she heard clip-clop sounds. Hooves walked across the wooden floor. Penny looked up. A young horse stood in the doorway.

The horse had a shiny brown coat. His mane was soft and black. His eyes were kind and bright.

"Hello," said the horse. "I'm Henry Horse. Can I play with you?"

Penny's ears perked up. Her curly tail wiggled with excitement. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Yes!" Henry smiled. "Playing alone is no fun. Playing together is much better."

Penny felt warm and happy inside. "I'm Penny Pig. I would love to play!"

Henry walked into the nursery. His hooves made gentle tap-tap sounds. "What should we play first?"

Penny pointed to her red ball. "We could roll the ball back and forth."

"Great idea!" Henry said.

Penny pushed the ball to Henry. It rolled across the smooth floor. Henry used his nose to push it back. The ball felt cool and bouncy.

"This is fun!" Penny giggled. Her laugh sounded like tiny bells.

They rolled the ball many times. Back and forth, back and forth. Both friends smiled and laughed.

"What else can we do?" Henry asked.

Penny looked around the nursery. She saw building blocks in bright colors. Red, blue, yellow, and green blocks sat in a big pile.

"Let's build something together!" Penny said.

They worked side by side. Penny used her snout to move blocks. Henry used his mouth to lift them up high. The blocks felt smooth and warm.

"Let's make a castle!" Henry suggested.

"Yes! A friendship castle!" Penny added.

They stacked blocks carefully. Red block on blue block. Yellow block on green block. Higher and higher their castle grew.

"Look how tall it is!" Penny said proudly.

"We make a great team," Henry replied.

Their castle had four tall towers. It looked strong and beautiful. Both friends felt proud of their work.

Then Penny had another idea. "Let's play hide and seek!"

"I'll count first," Henry offered. He closed his eyes tight. "One, two, three, four, five!"

Penny looked for a hiding spot. She squeezed behind a big, soft cushion. The cushion smelled like lavender flowers.

"Ready or not, here I come!" Henry called.

Henry looked under the table. No Penny there. He looked behind the toy box. No Penny there either.

Then he saw a pink ear peeking out. "Found you!" Henry laughed.

Penny came out giggling. "Your turn to hide!"

They played hide and seek many times. Each game was more fun than the last.

As the sun began to set, both friends felt tired but happy. They sat together on the soft rug. The rug felt fuzzy under them.

"This was the best day ever," Penny said. Her eyes sparkled with joy.

"I'm so glad we're friends," Henry replied.

"Best friends forever?" Penny asked hopefully.

"Best friends forever!" Henry promised.

They decided to have a friendship celebration. Penny found some apple slices. Henry found fresh carrots. They shared their snacks together.

The apples tasted sweet and crunchy. The carrots were crisp and orange. Everything tasted better when shared with a friend.

From that day on, Penny and Henry played together every day. They never felt lonely again. The nursery became filled with laughter and joy.

Penny learned that friendship makes everything more fun. Henry learned that being kind brings happiness.

Their friendship grew stronger each day. And they lived happily ever after in their cozy nursery home.

The end.
