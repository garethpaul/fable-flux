# Story story_680

**The Magic of Playtime**

The morning sun peeked through Tommy's window. Birds chirped happy songs outside. Mom's voice floated up the stairs.

"Tommy! Time to play with your little brother!"

Tommy groaned and pulled his blanket over his head. He loved his brother Jake, but Jake was only three. Tommy was five and wanted to build big castles with his blocks.

"Coming, Mom," Tommy called softly.

Downstairs, Jake sat on the colorful play mat. His chubby fingers held a red toy truck. His eyes sparkled when he saw Tommy.

"Tommy! Play trucks!" Jake giggled and rolled the truck across the soft carpet.

Tommy flopped down with a big sigh. "Okay, Jake. But just for a little while."

Jake handed Tommy a blue truck. "Vroom, vroom!" Jake made loud engine sounds. The truck bounced over imaginary hills.

Tommy pushed his truck slowly. It felt boring. He watched Jake zoom around the room. Jake's truck climbed over pillows and under chairs.

"Look, Tommy! My truck is flying!" Jake held the red truck high in the air. He made whooshing sounds like wind.

Something strange happened then. Tommy's blue truck felt warmer in his hands. It seemed to hum quietly, like it had a real engine inside.

"Beep, beep!" Tommy said, surprising himself. His truck rolled faster across the carpet.

Jake clapped his hands. "Your truck is talking to mine!"

Tommy looked closer at his truck. The tiny wheels sparkled in the sunlight. He could almost see little people inside the cab.

"Where should we drive our trucks?" Tommy asked.

Jake pointed to the kitchen. "To the cookie mountain!"

They crawled on their hands and knees, pushing trucks ahead of them. The wooden floor felt smooth and cool under Tommy's palms. Their trucks made rumbling sounds as they rolled.

In the kitchen, Mom had left a plate of round cookies on the low table. The cookies smelled like vanilla and sugar.

"Look! The cookie mountain!" Jake whispered like they had discovered treasure.

Tommy's truck climbed up a chair leg. It was harder than he expected. "My truck needs help getting up there."

"My truck will push!" Jake's red truck bumped gently against Tommy's blue one.

Together, the trucks reached the table top. The cookies looked enormous from down on the floor.

"The cookie giants live here," Tommy said in a mysterious voice.

Jake's eyes grew wide. "Are they friendly giants?"

"Very friendly. They share cookies with brave truck drivers."

Mom smiled and gave them each a warm cookie. The boys sat cross-legged on the kitchen tiles, munching happily.

"Our trucks are tired now," Jake said. "They need to rest."

Back in the living room, they built a garage from couch cushions. The cushions smelled like Mom's lavender soap. Inside the soft garage, it was dim and cozy.

"Tomorrow our trucks can go to the park," Tommy suggested.

"And the library!" Jake added excitedly.

"And maybe to space," Tommy said with a grin.

Jake bounced up and down. "Space trucks! With rocket boosters!"

They made whooshing rocket sounds together. Their voices echoed in the cushion garage.

As they played, Tommy forgot about his big block castle. Jake's imagination made everything more exciting. The living room had become a whole world of adventures.

When Dad came home, he found them building a ramp from books.

"What are you boys doing?" Dad asked.

"Teaching our trucks to jump!" Tommy explained proudly.

Jake nodded seriously. "It's very important truck training."

At bedtime, Tommy tucked his blue truck under his pillow. Through the thin wall, he could hear Jake talking to his red truck.

"Tomorrow we'll have more adventures," Jake whispered to his toy.

Tommy smiled in the darkness. Playing with Jake wasn't boring at all. His little brother saw magic everywhere. Jake helped Tommy see it too.

"Good night, Jake," Tommy called softly.

"Good night, Tommy. Good night, trucks."

Tommy drifted off to sleep, already planning tomorrow's truck adventures. Maybe they would visit the moon this time.

The end.
