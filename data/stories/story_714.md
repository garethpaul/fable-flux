# Story story_714

**The Best Playground Friend**

Tommy sat on the big red swing. He pushed himself back and forth slowly. The playground was full of children laughing and playing. But Tommy felt all alone.

His sneakers dragged in the sandy dirt below. The swing creaked softly in the warm sunshine. Tommy watched other kids climb the tall slide. He watched them spin on the merry-go-round. Everyone seemed to have a friend to play with.

"I wish I had someone to play with," Tommy said quietly.

Near the monkey bars stood a girl with curly brown hair. She wore a bright yellow dress and sparkly shoes. The girl looked around the playground. She looked a little shy too.

The girl walked over to Tommy's swing. "Hi," she said with a small smile. "I'm Emma. Can I swing next to you?"

Tommy looked up. Emma's eyes were kind and friendly. "Sure," he said. "I'm Tommy."

Emma climbed onto the swing beside him. She pushed off with her feet. Soon both children were swinging together. Their swings moved back and forth like a dance.

"This is fun!" Emma called out. The wind blew through her curly hair.

"Want to try the slide?" Tommy asked. He felt braver now.

"Yes!" Emma jumped off her swing.

They ran to the tall blue slide together. Their feet made soft thumping sounds on the rubber playground mat. Tommy climbed up first. Emma followed right behind him.

"Wheee!" Tommy slid down fast. His hands felt the smooth, warm plastic.

"My turn!" Emma slid down after him. She giggled all the way down.

At the bottom, they both laughed. "That was awesome!" Emma said.

Tommy pointed to the sandbox. "Want to build something?"

In the sandbox, they found red buckets and yellow shovels. The sand felt cool and grainy in their hands. Tommy started digging. Emma helped him scoop.

"Let's make a castle," Emma suggested.

"With a big tower!" Tommy added.

They worked together carefully. Tommy made the walls. Emma shaped the tower. They used their hands to pat the sand smooth. The castle grew bigger and bigger.

"We need a flag," Emma said. She found a small stick and a leaf. She stuck them on top of their tower.

"Perfect!" Tommy clapped his hands.

Other children came to look at their castle. "Wow, that's really good!" said a boy with red hair.

Tommy and Emma smiled proudly. They had built something wonderful together.

"Want to play tag?" Emma asked.

"You're it!" Tommy tapped Emma's shoulder gently.

They ran around the playground laughing. Emma chased Tommy past the swings. Tommy ran around the slide. Their feet pounded on the soft ground.

"Got you!" Emma tagged Tommy by the monkey bars.

"Now I'm it!" Tommy chased Emma back to the swings.

They played until they were both tired and happy. They sat on the grass under a shady tree. The grass felt soft and cool beneath them.

"This was the best day ever," Tommy said. He was breathing hard but smiling big.

"I'm so glad I asked to swing with you," Emma said. "You're really nice."

"You're nice too," Tommy replied. "Want to be best friends?"

"Yes!" Emma said. "Best friends forever!"

They shook hands like grown-ups. Then they both started giggling.

Tommy's mom called from across the playground. "Time to go, Tommy!"

"Will you be here tomorrow?" Tommy asked Emma.

"I'll be here," Emma promised. "We can build an even bigger castle!"

"And go down the slide a hundred times!" Tommy added.

They waved goodbye as Tommy walked to his mom. Tommy felt warm and happy inside. He wasn't lonely anymore.

The playground didn't feel big and scary now. It felt like a magical place where best friends could meet.

Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough. Tommy had found his very first playground friend. And Emma had found hers too.

The sun was setting, painting the sky orange and pink. It had been a perfect day for making friends.

The end.
