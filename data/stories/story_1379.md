# Story story_1379

**Squirrel's Colorful Discovery**

The morning sun peeked through the classroom windows. All the forest friends sat at their little tables. Today was art time at Woodland School.

"Good morning, everyone!" said Mrs. Owl. "Today we will paint pictures of our favorite things."

Squirrel looked at the paintbrushes and colorful paints. His fluffy tail twitched nervously. He did not like art time very much.

"I'm not good at painting," Squirrel whispered to his friend Rabbit. "My pictures never look right."

Rabbit smiled kindly. "Maybe today will be different," she said.

Mrs. Owl placed a big white paper in front of each student. She put out cups of water and soft paper towels. The paints smelled fresh and clean.

"What should I paint?" asked Bear, dipping his brush in blue paint.

"Paint something that makes you happy," said Mrs. Owl.

Squirrel picked up his brush slowly. It felt heavy in his small paws. He looked at the red paint, then the yellow, then the green. Everything looked so bright and scary.

"I don't know what to paint," Squirrel said sadly.

Mrs. Owl sat down next to him. "Close your eyes, Squirrel. What do you love most?"

Squirrel closed his brown eyes tight. He thought about climbing trees. He thought about collecting acorns. He thought about his cozy nest high in the oak tree.

"I love my tree," Squirrel said softly.

"Then paint your tree," said Mrs. Owl with a gentle hoot.

Squirrel opened his eyes. He dipped his brush in brown paint. Very carefully, he made a long line down his paper. The paint felt smooth and wet on the brush.

Something wonderful happened then. The brown line looked just like a tree trunk! Squirrel's eyes grew wide with surprise.

"Look!" he said excitedly. "It really looks like a tree!"

He dipped his brush in green paint. He made little dots and swirls at the top. Green leaves appeared like magic on his paper tree.

"This is fun!" Squirrel said, his tail twitching with joy now.

He added more green leaves. Then he painted yellow acorns hanging from the branches. The yellow paint sparkled in the sunlight from the window.

"What a beautiful tree!" said Rabbit, looking over at his painting.

Squirrel felt warm and happy inside. He painted a blue sky behind his tree. He added white fluffy clouds that looked like cotton balls.

"I want to add more," Squirrel said. He painted himself climbing up the tree trunk. He made his fur brown and his tail big and bushy.

The painting grew more and more beautiful. Squirrel painted red flowers at the bottom of the tree. He added a bright yellow sun in the corner.

"Time to clean up," announced Mrs. Owl.

Squirrel looked at his finished painting. It was colorful and bright and happy. It looked just like his favorite place in the whole world.

"I love my painting," Squirrel said proudly.

Mrs. Owl hung all the paintings on the wall to dry. Squirrel's tree painting looked wonderful next to all his friends' artwork.

"Your tree is amazing," said Bear. "I can almost see you climbing it!"

Squirrel's chest puffed up with pride. He had made something beautiful with his own paws. The colors were bright and cheerful. The tree looked real and magical at the same time.

As the friends cleaned their brushes, Squirrel helped put away the paints. He touched each color gently. Red like apples. Blue like the sky. Green like summer leaves.

"Mrs. Owl," said Squirrel as they finished cleaning up. "What will we paint tomorrow?"

Mrs. Owl smiled her wise smile. "What would you like to paint, Squirrel?"

Squirrel thought for a moment. His whiskers twitched with excitement. "Maybe I could paint my family! Or my favorite acorn! Or the sunset!"

"Those all sound wonderful," said Mrs. Owl.

Squirrel skipped home that afternoon, his painting tucked safely under his arm. He could hardly wait for tomorrow's art time. Painting was magical after all.

The end.
