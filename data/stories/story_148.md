# Ruby the Robin's New Friends

Ruby the robin sat on her favorite branch in the big oak tree. The morning sun warmed her bright red chest. She fluffed her soft gray feathers. The backyard looked so pretty today.

"What a beautiful day to play!" Ruby chirped happily.

She looked around the quiet backyard. Pretty flowers swayed in the breeze. A butterfly danced near the roses. But Ruby felt lonely. She had no one to play with.

"I wish I had a friend," Ruby sighed. Her little voice sounded sad in the empty yard.

Ruby flew down to the green grass. Maybe she could find someone to play hide-and-seek. She hopped around the garden. She looked behind the tall sunflowers. She peeked under the leafy bushes. But no other birds were there.

"Hello? Anyone want to play?" Ruby called out. Only the wind answered back.

Ruby didn't give up. She had an idea! Maybe she could make some noise. Then other birds would hear her. Ruby flew to the bird bath. She splashed the cool water with her wings.

Splash! Splash! Splash!

The water droplets sparkled like tiny diamonds. Ruby giggled at the pretty sight. But still, no birds came to play.

Ruby tried again. She flew to the fence post. She sang her loudest, sweetest song.

"Tweet-tweet-twitter! Come and play! Tweet-tweet-twitter! It's a lovely day!"

Her beautiful voice echoed through the yard. Ruby waited and listened. She heard a car honk far away. She heard leaves rustling. But no bird songs answered back.

Ruby felt disappointed. Her tiny heart felt heavy. She almost flew back to her tree to be alone.

But then Ruby remembered something important. Mama always said, "Keep trying, little one. Good things come to those who don't give up."

Ruby took a deep breath. She would try one more time! This time, she would be extra creative.

Ruby gathered small twigs in her beak. She arranged them in a circle on the grass. Then she found colorful flower petals. She made a beautiful pattern inside the twig circle.

"I'll make the most fun playground ever!" Ruby said with determination.

She rolled a small pebble to the center. She found a shiny bottle cap for decoration. Ruby worked very hard. Soon she had created something wonderful.

Just then, Ruby heard a sound. "Chirp-chirp!"

A little blue jay landed nearby. His name was Benny.

"Wow!" said Benny. "What did you make?"

"It's a playground!" Ruby said excitedly. "Want to play with me?"

"Yes!" Benny hopped into the circle.

Soon, more birds arrived. Penny the sparrow flew down from the maple tree. Carlos the cardinal heard the happy sounds. Even old Mr. Crow came to see what was happening.

"This looks like fun!" said Penny.

"Can we all play together?" asked Carlos.

"Of course!" Ruby beamed with joy. Her heart felt warm and full.

The birds played wonderful games. They played hopscotch with the pebbles. They raced around the twig circle. They made up silly songs together. They laughed until their bellies hurt.

"Ruby, you're so clever!" said Benny. "You made the best playground!"

"And you brought us all together!" added Penny.

Ruby felt so proud and happy. She had solved her problem all by herself! By working hard and not giving up, she had found friends.

As the sun began to set, the birds gathered in a circle.

"Same time tomorrow?" asked Carlos.

"Yes!" they all chirped together.

Ruby flew back to her branch in the oak tree. She looked down at her beautiful playground. She looked at her new friends saying goodbye to each other.

"Mama was right," Ruby whispered to herself. "Good things really do come to those who don't give up."

Ruby tucked her head under her wing. She felt so grateful for her wonderful day. Tomorrow would bring even more fun with her new friends.

The backyard wasn't lonely anymore. It was full of friendship and joy. And it all started because one little robin decided to keep trying.

Ruby smiled as she drifted off to sleep, dreaming of tomorrow's adventures.

The end.
