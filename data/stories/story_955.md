# Story story_955

**Bruno's Missing Teddy**

Bruno the bear lived in a cozy attic. Sunlight streamed through the round window. Dust sparkles danced in the air. Old boxes sat everywhere like wooden mountains.

Every morning, Bruno hugged his teddy bear, Patches. Patches was soft and brown. He had one blue eye and one green eye. Bruno loved Patches very much.

But this morning was different. Bruno reached for Patches. His paw touched only air. "Where is Patches?" Bruno asked out loud.

Bruno looked left. He looked right. No teddy bear anywhere. His heart felt heavy like a stone. "Patches! Where are you?" Bruno called.

The attic stayed quiet. Only the wind whispered outside.

Bruno started to search. He pushed aside dusty books. He moved old picture frames. He looked behind a rocking chair. Still no Patches.

"I must find him," Bruno said. His voice shook a little.

Bruno decided to search every box. That was his first plan. He opened the red box first. Inside were Christmas ornaments. Shiny balls rolled around. No teddy bear.

The blue box held winter clothes. Scarves and mittens spilled out. They smelled like cedar wood. But no Patches.

Box after box, Bruno searched. His paws got tired. His fur collected dust. Some boxes were too heavy. Bruno pushed and pushed. They barely moved.

"This is too hard," Bruno sighed. He sat down feeling sad. Maybe Patches was gone forever.

Bruno's tummy rumbled. He was hungry and tired. But then he remembered something. Mama Bear always said, "Try a different way, little one."

Bruno stood up straight. He had a new idea. Instead of moving boxes, he would follow his nose. Bears have excellent noses. Maybe he could smell Patches.

Bruno closed his eyes. He took a deep sniff. The attic smelled like old wood and dust. He smelled something else too. Something familiar and sweet.

Bruno followed the scent. Step by step across the wooden floor. The smell grew stronger near the window. It smelled like honey and sunshine. Just like Patches!

Bruno looked up high. On top of the tallest bookshelf sat Patches! His teddy bear looked down with button eyes. How did he get up there?

"There you are!" Bruno called happily.

But the shelf was very tall. Bruno stretched his paws up high. He could not reach. He tried jumping. Still too high.

Bruno looked around the attic. He spotted some books. Big thick books. Bruno had another idea.

Carefully, Bruno stacked the books. One book. Two books. Three books high. The pile wobbled a little. Bruno climbed up slowly.

Now he could reach! Bruno grabbed Patches gently. "I found you!" he said. His heart felt light as a feather.

Bruno climbed down with Patches. He hugged his teddy bear tight. Patches felt warm and soft. He still smelled like honey.

"How did you get up there?" Bruno asked Patches. Of course, teddy bears cannot talk. But Bruno remembered now. Last night he was playing airplane with Patches. He must have thrown him too high!

Bruno giggled. "Silly me," he said. "I forgot about our airplane game."

Bruno sat in his favorite sunny spot. He held Patches close. The morning sun felt warm on his fur. Dust sparkles still danced around them.

"I was worried I lost you forever," Bruno told Patches. "But I did not give up. I tried two different ways."

Bruno felt proud of himself. When the first way did not work, he tried again. Using his nose was much smarter than moving heavy boxes.

"Mama Bear was right," Bruno said. "There is always another way to solve problems."

Bruno and Patches spent the rest of the morning playing quietly. They built block towers. They read picture books. They took a cozy nap together.

From that day on, Bruno always remembered. When something seems too hard, try a different way. Never give up on the things you love.

And Bruno never threw Patches too high again!

The end.
