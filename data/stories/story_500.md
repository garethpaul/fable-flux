# Ben's Colorful Discovery

The morning sun peeked through Ben's bedroom window. "Art time!" called Mom from downstairs. Ben heard her cheerful voice echo up the stairs.

Ben sat on his bed. He looked at his rumpled pajamas. Art time happened every day after breakfast. But today felt different somehow.

"I don't want to paint today," Ben mumbled. He dragged his feet down the stairs. The kitchen smelled like warm toast and jam.

Mom had already set up the art table. Red paint, blue paint, yellow paint sat in tiny cups. Clean brushes waited in a glass jar. White paper lay flat and ready.

"What's wrong, sweetie?" Mom asked gently. She ruffled Ben's messy hair with her warm hand.

"Art time is boring," Ben said quietly. He slumped into his chair. The wooden seat felt cold against his legs.

Mom smiled her understanding smile. "Sometimes we all feel that way. But maybe today will surprise you."

Ben picked up a brush. It felt light in his small fingers. He dipped it in red paint. One tiny drop fell onto the paper.

The red drop looked lonely. Ben stared at it for a long moment. Then something wonderful happened.

The red drop seemed to wiggle! Ben blinked his eyes twice. Was he seeing things? He leaned closer to look.

Ben added a blue drop next to the red one. The colors touched each other softly. Purple appeared where they met! Like magic happening right before his eyes.

"Wow!" Ben whispered in amazement. His heart started beating faster with excitement.

He tried yellow and red together. Bright orange bloomed on his paper! Ben giggled with pure delight. The sound filled the quiet kitchen.

"Look, Mom! The colors are dancing!" Ben pointed at his paper with his free hand.

Mom came over to see. "They really are dancing, aren't they? What beautiful color magic you're making!"

Ben's brush moved faster now. Green appeared when blue met yellow. Purple grew deeper and richer. Orange glowed like a tiny sunset.

Soon Ben's paper looked like a rainbow garden. Swirls and dots and happy lines covered every inch. His fingers had paint on them too.

"This is the most beautiful painting ever!" Ben declared proudly. He held up his masterpiece for Mom to see.

"It really is magnificent," Mom agreed warmly. "You discovered something special today, didn't you?"

Ben nodded enthusiastically. "I discovered that colors are alive! They want to play and dance together!"

He looked at his paint-covered hands. Even his fingernails had tiny rainbows under them. Everything felt magical and new.

"Can we hang it on the refrigerator?" Ben asked hopefully. His eyes sparkled with joy and pride.

"Of course we can," Mom said. She helped Ben carry his painting to the refrigerator. Together they found the perfect spot.

Ben stepped back to admire his work. The colors seemed to glow in the kitchen light. Red danced with blue. Yellow sang with green.

"Tomorrow I want to make an even bigger rainbow!" Ben announced excitedly. He was already planning his next masterpiece.

"That sounds wonderful," Mom said. "Art time will be here again tomorrow."

But now those words sounded different to Ben. Art time wasn't just something that happened every day. It was a chance for magic!

Ben hugged Mom tightly. "Thank you for letting the colors teach me their secrets," he whispered against her shirt.

That night, Ben fell asleep thinking about colors. He dreamed of purple elephants and orange butterflies. Green grass that sang songs and blue skies that giggled.

When morning came again, Ben jumped out of bed. "Is it art time yet?" he called downstairs eagerly.

He couldn't wait to see what new magic the colors would show him today. Every day was full of wonderful surprises, just waiting to be discovered.

Ben had learned something important. Sometimes the most ordinary things hold the most extraordinary magic. You just have to look with curious, open eyes.

And art time would never be boring again.

The end.
