# Story story_1393

**The Little Blue Book and the Cookie Jar**

In a sunny school hallway lived a little blue book named Benny. Benny had bright yellow pages and a cheerful smile drawn on his cover. Every day, children carried him to class and back.

Benny loved living in the hallway. He could watch kids run and play. He heard their happy voices echo off the walls. The hallway smelled like fresh crayons and clean floors.

One afternoon, Benny noticed something wonderful. High up on a shelf sat a big glass jar. Inside were the most amazing chocolate chip cookies he had ever seen. They looked golden brown and perfectly round. The sweet smell of vanilla drifted down to him.

"Oh my!" said Benny. "Those cookies smell so good. I wish I could have just one tiny bite."

But there was a big problem. The shelf was very, very high. Benny was just a small book sitting on the floor. He stretched his cover as tall as he could. He reached and reached with his corner. But he was much too short.

"I need to get up there somehow," Benny said to himself.

Benny had his first idea. He would bounce! Books could bounce a little bit, right? He rocked back and forth on his spine. Then he pushed off the ground with all his might.

Bounce! Benny jumped up about two inches. That was not nearly high enough. The cookie jar was still far, far away. He tried bouncing again and again. Each time, he only went up a tiny bit.

"Bouncing is not working," puffed Benny. He felt tired and sad. The cookies still smelled wonderful, but they seemed impossible to reach.

Just then, Benny heard footsteps. Down the hallway walked Mrs. Chen, the nice librarian. She had kind eyes and always spoke softly to the books.

"Hello there, little blue book," said Mrs. Chen. "You look worried. What's wrong?"

Benny's pages fluttered with excitement. This was his second idea! Maybe Mrs. Chen could help him.

"Mrs. Chen," said Benny in his small book voice. "I can smell those delicious cookies up there. But I'm too little to reach them. Could you please help me get just one?"

Mrs. Chen looked up at the cookie jar. Then she looked down at Benny with a warm smile. "Those cookies do smell wonderful," she said. "But you know what, Benny? Those cookies are for the teachers' meeting today."

Benny's pages drooped a little. But Mrs. Chen wasn't finished talking.

"However," she continued, "I happen to have something even better." She reached into her pocket and pulled out a small, soft cookie shaped like a star. It was perfectly book-sized.

"This is a special reading cookie," said Mrs. Chen. "I made it just for helpful books like you. Would you like to try it?"

Benny's cover practically glowed with happiness. "Yes, please! Thank you so much!"

Mrs. Chen gently placed the star cookie on Benny's pages. It smelled like cinnamon and honey. Benny took a tiny bite. It was the most delicious thing he had ever tasted! It was sweet and warm and made him feel cozy inside.

"This is even better than those cookies," said Benny. "Thank you for being so kind to me."

"You're very welcome," said Mrs. Chen. "You know, Benny, sometimes when we can't reach something we want, something even better comes along. You just have to be patient and ask for help when you need it."

Benny learned two important things that day. First, it's okay to ask for help when you have a problem. Second, sometimes what you get is even better than what you wanted in the first place.

From that day on, whenever Benny smelled something delicious he couldn't reach, he remembered Mrs. Chen's kindness. He learned that good things come to those who are patient and brave enough to ask for help.

And every Friday, Mrs. Chen brought Benny a special reading cookie. They became the very best of friends, sharing stories and treats in the sunny school hallway.

The end.
