# Story story_1340

**Splish Splash Magic**

Mama looked at the clock. "Bath time, Emma!" she called cheerfully.

Little Emma was playing with her blocks. She built a tall tower. Red block, blue block, yellow block.

"Not yet, Mama," Emma said softly. "My tower needs more blocks."

Mama smiled and walked over. "Your tower is beautiful, sweetie. But look at your hands."

Emma looked down. Her fingers were sticky from lunch. Her knees had dirt from playing outside. Her hair felt messy too.

"I don't want a bath," Emma whispered. "The water might be too cold. Or too hot."

Mama knelt down beside her. "What if we make bath time special tonight?"

Emma tilted her head. "How?"

"Come see," Mama said, taking Emma's small hand.

They walked to the bathroom together. Mama turned on the faucet. Warm water flowed into the big white tub.

"Feel this," Mama said, letting Emma touch the water.

The water felt just right. Not cold. Not hot. Perfect and warm.

But Emma still felt worried. "What if I slip?"

Mama showed her the rubber mat. "This will keep you safe. See the little bumps?"

Emma touched the mat. It felt bumpy and soft.

Then Mama did something magical. She reached under the sink. Out came a bottle of bubble bath.

"Purple bubbles?" Emma asked, eyes growing wide.

"Purple bubbles," Mama confirmed. She squeezed the bottle. Sweet lavender smell filled the air.

The water turned cloudy. Then fluffy bubbles appeared. More and more bubbles! They grew tall like Emma's block tower.

"Wow!" Emma gasped. "They're everywhere!"

Mama helped Emma into the tub. The warm water hugged her toes. The bubbles tickled her arms.

"Look, Mama!" Emma scooped up bubbles. "I can make a beard!"

She put bubbles on her chin. Mama laughed. "You look so silly!"

Emma giggled too. The sound echoed in the bathroom.

"Can I make bubble mountains?" Emma asked.

"Yes!" Mama said. "Make as many as you want."

Emma pushed bubbles together. She made bubble castles. She made bubble hats. She even made a bubble crown.

"I'm the bubble princess!" she announced proudly.

Mama handed her a soft washcloth. "Even princesses need to get clean."

The washcloth felt gentle on Emma's skin. Mama helped wash her hair with sweet-smelling shampoo.

"Close your eyes," Mama said softly.

Warm water rinsed the soap away. Emma felt fresh and clean.

"Can we sing the splashing song?" Emma asked.

Mama nodded. They sang together: "Splish splash, in the bath. Bubbles floating, bubbles dancing. Clean and happy, that's our path!"

Emma splashed gently with her hands. The water made happy sounds. Plop, plop, splash!

"Look at this!" Emma discovered she could blow the bubbles. They floated up like tiny balloons.

She blew softly. One bubble. Two bubbles. Three bubbles! They danced in the air.

"Magic bubbles," Emma whispered in wonder.

After washing, Mama lifted Emma out. She wrapped her in a big fluffy towel. The towel felt like a warm hug.

"All clean!" Mama said, drying Emma's hair.

Emma looked at herself in the mirror. Her cheeks were pink and rosy. Her hair smelled like flowers. She felt cozy and fresh.

"That was the best bath ever," Emma said, hugging Mama tight.

Mama kissed her forehead. "I'm so proud of you for trying something new."

They walked to Emma's room. Mama helped her put on soft pajamas. The pajamas felt smooth against her clean skin.

"Mama?" Emma said as Mama tucked her into bed.

"Yes, sweetie?"

"Can we have purple bubbles again tomorrow?"

Mama smiled. "We can have any color bubbles you want."

Emma snuggled into her pillow. "Maybe rainbow bubbles next time."

"Rainbow bubbles sound perfect," Mama whispered.

Emma closed her eyes. She dreamed of floating bubbles and warm water. Bath time wasn't scary anymore. It was magical.

Tomorrow's bath would be another adventure. Maybe she would be a mermaid. Or a ship captain. Or maybe just a happy little girl who loved splashing in magical bubbles.

The end.

The end.
