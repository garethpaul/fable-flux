# The Lonely Puzzle in the Hallway

On the soft blue carpet of Grandma's hallway sat a colorful jigsaw puzzle named Pip. Pip had bright red edges and pretty pictures of farm animals on each piece. His box was worn and cozy from many years of love.

But today, Pip felt very sad.

"I wish someone would play with me," Pip whispered to himself. The hallway was quiet and empty. Sunlight streamed through the window, making rainbow patterns on the wall, but Pip couldn't enjoy them.

All around him, other toys were having fun. The teddy bears were having tea parties in the living room. The blocks were building tall towers in the playroom. Even the crayons were drawing beautiful pictures together.

But Pip sat alone in the hallway.

"Maybe I can find someone to play with," Pip said bravely. He wiggled and jiggled until one of his corner pieces fell out. The piece rolled down the hallway, making a soft *tap-tap-tap* sound on the wooden floor.

A little mouse peeked out from behind the baseboard. "What's that noise?" squeaked the mouse.

"Hello!" called Pip excitedly. "Would you like to put my pieces together? We could make a beautiful picture!"

The mouse looked at Pip's many pieces. "Oh my," said the mouse. "I'm too small for such a big puzzle. Sorry, Pip!" And the mouse scurried away.

Pip felt disappointed, but he didn't give up.

Next, Pip noticed Grandma's cat, Whiskers, walking by. Whiskers had soft gray fur and green eyes that sparkled in the sunlight.

"Whiskers!" called Pip hopefully. "Would you help me put my pieces together?"

Whiskers stopped and sniffed Pip's box. "Meow," said Whiskers thoughtfully. "I would love to help, but my paws are too big. I might lose your tiny pieces." Whiskers padded away quietly.

Pip's pieces rattled sadly in his box. Two friends had said no. Maybe nobody wanted to play with him.

But then Pip remembered what Grandma always said: "When something is hard, try one more time."

Just then, Pip heard small footsteps coming down the hallway. *Pat-pat-pat* went the little feet on the carpet.

"What's in this box?" asked a sweet voice.

Pip looked up to see Emma, Grandma's granddaughter. Emma had curly brown hair and wore a yellow dress with tiny flowers. She was six years old and loved solving problems.

"I'm a puzzle!" said Pip excitedly. "But I'm all mixed up in pieces. Would you help me become whole again?"

Emma's eyes lit up like stars. "I LOVE puzzles!" she said, sitting down cross-legged on the soft carpet. "Let's see what picture you make!"

Emma gently poured Pip's pieces onto the floor. There were pieces with red barns, green grass, fluffy white sheep, and a bright yellow sun.

"First, let's find all your edge pieces," Emma said, carefully sorting through the pieces. Her fingers were gentle and patient.

Together, Emma and Pip worked on the puzzle. Emma would pick up a piece and say, "Where do you think this goes, Pip?" And somehow, Pip would know exactly where it belonged.

*Click!* went the pieces as they fit together perfectly.

Slowly, beautifully, Pip's picture began to appear. The red barn grew bigger. The green grass spread across the bottom. The fluffy sheep found their places in the meadow.

"We did it!" Emma cheered as she placed the very last piece. Pip was complete! He showed a beautiful farm scene with animals playing happily together.

Pip felt warm and wonderful. He wasn't just scattered pieces anymore. He was whole, and he had made a new friend.

"Thank you for not giving up on me," Pip said gratefully.

Emma smiled and gently ran her finger along Pip's smooth, connected pieces. "Thank you for teaching me that the best things take time and patience," she said.

From that day on, Emma and Pip played together every time she visited Grandma. And Pip learned that sometimes the best friends are worth waiting for.

The hallway wasn't lonely anymore. It was filled with laughter, teamwork, and the happy sound of puzzle pieces clicking into place.
