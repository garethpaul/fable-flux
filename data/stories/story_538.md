# Milo's Missing Teddy

Milo the monkey loved the beach. The warm sand felt soft between his toes. The blue waves made happy splashing sounds. Best of all, he could play here with Teddy Brown, his favorite stuffed bear.

"Come on, Teddy!" Milo said happily. "Let's build a big sandcastle!"

Milo and Teddy worked together all morning. They dug holes with their hands. They filled buckets with wet sand. The sun felt warm on their backs.

When lunch time came, Milo was very hungry. "Stay right here, Teddy," he said. He patted his furry friend's soft head. "I'll be back soon!"

Milo ran to his family's beach blanket. He ate his banana sandwich quickly. He drank cold water from his cup. The food tasted so good!

"Time to play again!" Milo said excitedly.

But when he ran back to their spot... Teddy Brown was gone!

"Teddy? Teddy!" Milo called out loudly.

He looked left and right. He looked up and down the beach. His brown eyes filled with worried tears.

"Where are you, Teddy?" Milo whispered sadly.

Milo decided to search everywhere. First, he looked near the big rocks. Maybe Teddy rolled behind them? Milo moved small stones carefully. He checked every dark shadow. No Teddy.

Next, Milo searched by the water. The waves were bigger now. They made louder whooshing sounds. Maybe Teddy fell in the ocean? Milo walked along the wet sand. His feet made squishy sounds. Still no Teddy.

Milo felt very sad. His bottom lip started to shake. But then he remembered something important. Mama always said, "Don't give up, little one. Keep trying!"

"I won't give up!" Milo said bravely.

He looked under beach umbrellas. He checked behind coolers and chairs. He asked other families nicely. "Have you seen my teddy bear?"

Everyone shook their heads no. But they all smiled kindly at Milo.

The sun was getting lower now. It painted the sky pretty orange colors. Milo sat down on the sand. He felt tired and worried.

"Think, Milo, think," he said to himself.

Then he had a new idea! Maybe someone moved Teddy to keep him safe? Milo ran to the lifeguard tower. His feet kicked up little clouds of sand.

"Excuse me," Milo said politely. "Did you find a brown teddy bear?"

The friendly lifeguard smiled big. "Are you looking for this little guy?" She held up Teddy Brown!

"Teddy!" Milo shouted with joy.

"The wind blew him toward the water," the lifeguard explained. "I kept him safe up here."

Milo hugged Teddy tight. The soft fur felt perfect against his cheek. Teddy smelled like sunshine and sea air.

"Thank you so much!" Milo said gratefully.

He ran back to his family. His heart felt light and happy. "Look! I found Teddy!"

"You never gave up," Mama said proudly. "You kept looking and asking for help."

"That's what good problem-solvers do!" Papa added.

Milo hugged Teddy even tighter. "I was scared," he told his stuffed friend. "But I didn't stop looking for you."

As the sun set over the ocean, Milo and Teddy sat together. The waves made gentle lapping sounds. Seagulls called softly overhead.

"Tomorrow we'll play again," Milo promised. "But I'll hold your paw the whole time!"

Teddy's button eyes seemed to twinkle. Milo felt proud and happy. He had learned something important today. When things go wrong, don't give up. Keep trying different ways. Ask for help when you need it.

The beach was beautiful at sunset. But it was even more beautiful with his best friend beside him.

"I love you, Teddy Brown," Milo whispered.

The gentle ocean breeze seemed to whisper back, "Well done, brave little monkey. Well done."

The end.
