# Story story_1401

**Turtle's New Friend**

In the big dining room, Turtle sat alone. The wooden table stretched high above him. Sunlight streamed through tall windows. Dust sparkles danced in the warm air.

Turtle pushed his favorite red ball. It rolled across the smooth floor. The ball made a soft bouncing sound. Turtle sighed. Playing alone wasn't much fun.

His shell felt heavy today. His little legs moved slowly. "I wish I had a friend," Turtle whispered. His voice echoed in the quiet room.

The dining room chairs looked like giant trees. The table legs were like brown pillars. Everything seemed so big. Turtle felt very small.

Just then, footsteps pattered down the hallway. A little girl peeked around the corner. She had curly brown hair and bright blue eyes.

"Hello," said the girl softly. "I'm Zoe."

Turtle looked up. His eyes grew wide. "Hi," he said shyly. "I'm Turtle."

Zoe stepped into the dining room. Her sneakers squeaked on the floor. She wore a yellow dress with tiny flowers. It swished when she walked.

"What are you doing?" Zoe asked. She knelt down beside Turtle. Her knees touched the cool floor.

"Playing ball," Turtle said. "But it's not very fun alone."

Zoe's face lit up with a big smile. "Can I play too?" she asked. Her voice was gentle and kind.

Turtle's heart jumped with joy. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" Zoe laughed. The sound was like tiny bells ringing.

Zoe sat cross-legged on the floor. She was now eye-level with Turtle. The red ball sat between them.

"Let's roll it back and forth," Zoe suggested. She gave the ball a gentle push.

The ball rolled to Turtle. He pushed it back with his nose. It felt warm from the sunshine. The ball made a soft thud against Zoe's hands.

"This is fun!" Turtle giggled. His shell didn't feel heavy anymore.

They rolled the ball faster. Back and forth it went. Sometimes it bounced off chair legs. Sometimes it spun in circles.

"Let's try something new," Zoe said. She stood up and walked to the other side of the room.

"Roll it to me from there," she called out.

Turtle pushed hard with his nose. The ball rolled across the long floor. It went straight to Zoe's feet.

"Great job!" Zoe cheered. She clapped her hands together. The sound echoed happily.

Next, they played hide and seek. Turtle hid under the table. His green shell blended with the shadows. Zoe pretended she couldn't find him.

"Where could Turtle be?" she wondered aloud. She looked behind chairs and under napkins.

Turtle giggled from his hiding spot. The sound gave him away.

"Found you!" Zoe laughed. She gently tickled Turtle's shell.

Then Zoe hid behind the big wooden cabinet. Turtle searched everywhere. His little legs carried him around the room. Finally, he spotted her yellow dress.

"There you are!" Turtle called out happily.

As the afternoon passed, they invented new games. They raced around the table legs. They sang silly songs together. They shared stories about their favorite things.

The dining room felt different now. It wasn't big and empty anymore. It was filled with laughter and friendship. The sunlight seemed brighter. The air felt warmer.

"I'm so glad we're friends," Turtle said. His eyes sparkled with happiness.

"Me too," Zoe agreed. She gently patted Turtle's shell. "Friends make everything more fun."

They decided to have a friendship party. Zoe brought crackers from the kitchen. Turtle shared his favorite sunny spot by the window. They had a picnic on the dining room floor.

The crackers tasted extra delicious. The sunshine felt extra warm. Everything was better when shared with a friend.

As the day ended, Turtle wasn't lonely anymore. His heart felt full and happy. He had found something wonderful – a true friend.

"Can we play again tomorrow?" Turtle asked hopefully.

"Every day," Zoe promised with a warm hug.

The dining room would never feel empty again. It was now their special place. A place where friendship bloomed and joy lived forever.

The end.
