# Story story_1142

**The Magic Cleanup**

Tommy heard Mama's voice from downstairs. "Cleanup time!" she called cheerfully. Tommy looked around his messy room. Toys covered the soft blue carpet. Books lay open everywhere. His red fire truck sat upside down by the window.

Tommy flopped onto his bed with a big sigh. "I don't want to clean up," he mumbled into his pillow. The pillow smelled like his favorite lavender soap. Cleanup time felt like work. Playing was much more fun.

He sat up slowly and picked up one small block. It was smooth and cool in his hand. Tommy tossed it toward the toy box. It landed with a soft thud on the carpet instead.

"This is too hard," Tommy said sadly. He looked at all the toys again. There were so many! His shoulders felt heavy and tired.

Just then, something sparkled near his closet. A tiny golden light danced in the air. Tommy rubbed his eyes and looked again. The light was still there!

"Hello, Tommy," whispered a sweet, tinkling voice. The golden light grew bigger and brighter. "I'm Sparkle, the Cleanup Fairy!"

Tommy's eyes grew wide. "A real fairy?" he asked softly.

Sparkle giggled like tiny silver bells. "I help children make cleanup time magical! Would you like to try?"

Tommy nodded eagerly. "Yes, please!"

"Touch each toy and make a wish," Sparkle said. "Watch what happens!"

Tommy picked up his red fire truck. "I wish you would drive home," he whispered. Suddenly, the truck's wheels began to spin. It rolled across the carpet by itself! The truck drove straight into the toy box with a happy beep-beep sound.

"Wow!" Tommy laughed with delight. His heart felt light and bouncy.

He grabbed three blocks next. "I wish you would stack yourselves," he said. The blocks wiggled and danced. They jumped on top of each other, making a perfect tower. Then they hopped into their container.

Tommy clapped his hands together. This was amazing! He picked up a stuffed elephant. "I wish you would march to bed," he giggled. The gray elephant swayed back and forth. It walked slowly to Tommy's bed and settled on the pillow.

Book after book flew through the air. They landed neatly on the bookshelf. Crayons rolled into their box like colorful caterpillars. Puzzle pieces clicked together and slid into place.

Tommy spun around happily. The room sparkled and shined. Everything had a special place. The carpet felt soft under his bare feet. Sunlight streamed through the clean window.

"You did wonderful work," Sparkle said proudly. "How do you feel?"

Tommy looked around his tidy room. "I feel happy!" he said with a big smile. "My room looks beautiful now."

"The magic was inside you all along," Sparkle explained gently. "You just needed to believe in yourself."

Tommy hugged his stuffed elephant tight. "Will you come back tomorrow?" he asked hopefully.

Sparkle's light twinkled extra bright. "Magic happens when you try your best," she said. "Remember that, and I'll always be near."

The golden light faded slowly. Tommy waved goodbye until Sparkle disappeared completely.

Mama peeked into his room. "Oh my!" she said with surprise. "Your room looks perfect!"

"I had help from magic," Tommy said proudly. "But I did the work myself."

Mama gave him a warm, squeezy hug. "You should feel very proud," she said. "Hard work makes everything better."

That night, Tommy fell asleep quickly. His clean room felt peaceful and calm. He dreamed about dancing toys and golden lights.

The next morning, Tommy woke up excited. He couldn't wait for cleanup time again. Maybe Sparkle would visit. Maybe he would find new magic.

But Tommy already knew the best secret. The real magic lived inside his own helpful hands and caring heart.

He smiled and stretched in his cozy bed. Today would be another wonderful day.

The end.
