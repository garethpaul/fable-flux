# Story story_1435

**Grandpa's Missing Book**

Grandpa sat in his big brown chair. The soft cushions felt warm and cozy. Sunlight streamed through the window. It made golden patterns on the carpet.

Grandpa reached for his special book. It was his favorite story about pirates and treasure. But the small table beside his chair was empty.

"Oh my!" said Grandpa. His eyes grew wide. "Where is my book?"

He looked at the empty spot again. The book was always there. Every afternoon, Grandpa read his pirate story. The pages were soft and yellow. The cover had a picture of a big ship.

Grandpa stood up slowly. His knees made little creaking sounds. He looked under his chair. No book there. He looked behind the lamp. Still no book.

"This is very strange," Grandpa said to himself. He scratched his gray beard. It felt scratchy and tickly.

Grandpa decided to search the whole living room. He would look everywhere!

First, he checked the big sofa. The blue cushions were puffy and soft. Grandpa lifted each one. He found three pennies and a button. But no book.

He looked under the coffee table. It was dark and dusty underneath. A spider scurried away quickly. Still no book.

Grandpa checked behind the tall bookshelf. Many books stood in neat rows. But his special pirate book was not there.

"Hmm," said Grandpa. He felt worried. The book was very important to him. His own grandfather had given it to him long ago.

Grandpa sat down in his chair again. He closed his eyes and thought hard. Where could the book be?

Then he remembered something. Yesterday, his little granddaughter Emma had visited. She loved hearing stories. They had read together on the sofa.

Grandpa smiled. Maybe Emma had moved the book!

He walked to the sofa again. This time, he looked more carefully. He moved the soft pillows one by one. He checked between the cushions.

His fingers felt something hard and flat. It was stuck way down in the crack!

"Aha!" said Grandpa happily.

He pulled gently. Out came his special book! The cover was a little wrinkled. But the pirate ship still looked brave and strong.

Grandpa hugged the book to his chest. It smelled like old paper and adventure. The familiar weight felt good in his hands.

He opened to his favorite page. There was the pirate captain with his black hat. The words were just as exciting as always.

"I found you!" Grandpa said to the book. "You were hiding from me!"

Grandpa settled back into his brown chair. The cushions welcomed him with a soft whoosh. He opened the book to the first page.

The afternoon sun made everything golden and warm. Grandpa began to read about pirates sailing on blue seas. The words danced off the page like magic.

As he read, Grandpa thought about his search. First, he had looked in the wrong places. That didn't work. But he didn't give up. He thought harder and tried again.

The second time, he remembered Emma's visit. He looked more carefully in the right place. And he found his treasure!

"Just like the pirates in my book," Grandpa chuckled. "Sometimes you have to search twice to find the treasure."

The pirate story was even better now. Grandpa had gone on his own little adventure. He had solved a mystery in his very own living room.

When Emma visited next time, Grandpa would tell her about his search. Maybe they would read the pirate book together again. But this time, they would put it back on the table when they finished.

Grandpa turned another page. The pirates were finding their treasure chest. Golden coins sparkled in the pictures.

"We both found our treasure today," Grandpa said softly.

The warm sun, the cozy chair, and his special book made everything perfect. Sometimes the best adventures happen right at home.

Grandpa smiled and kept reading. The living room was quiet and peaceful. Everything was exactly where it should be.

The end.
