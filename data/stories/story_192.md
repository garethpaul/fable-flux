# Nutkin and Grandma Rose

On a sunny green hill, a little squirrel named Nutkin sat alone. He had a pile of acorns. He was trying to build something. But his acorns kept falling down.

"This is too hard," Nutkin sighed. His fluffy tail drooped sadly. "I wish I had help."

The hill was quiet except for birds singing. Nutkin felt very lonely. He wanted to build the tallest acorn tower ever. But every time he stacked three acorns, they tumbled down.

*Thump, thump, thump!*

Just then, someone walked up the hill. It was Grandma Rose! She had silver hair and kind eyes. She carried a walking stick with flowers on it.

"Hello there, little one," Grandma Rose said gently. "What are you building?"

Nutkin looked up shyly. "I'm trying to make an acorn tower. But it keeps falling down. I'm not very good at it."

Grandma Rose smiled warmly. "May I help you? I love building things too!"

Nutkin's eyes grew wide. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" Grandma Rose sat down carefully on the soft grass. "Building together is much more fun."

Nutkin felt excited butterflies in his tummy. "Okay! But I should warn you. I'm not very good at this."

"That's perfectly fine," Grandma Rose said. "I have an idea. What if we work as a team?"

She picked up an acorn gently. "You choose where each acorn goes. I'll help hold them steady."

Nutkin's whiskers twitched with excitement. "That's a great idea!"

They started building together. Nutkin carefully placed each brown acorn. Grandma Rose held them with her gentle hands. The tower grew taller and taller.

"Look!" Nutkin squeaked happily. "It's working!"

"You're doing wonderfully," Grandma Rose said. "Your little paws are perfect for this."

As they built, they talked and laughed. Nutkin learned that Grandma Rose loved collecting pretty stones. Grandma Rose learned that Nutkin loved telling jokes.

"Why don't acorns ever get lost?" Nutkin asked, giggling.

"I don't know," Grandma Rose chuckled. "Why?"

"Because they're always oak-ay!" Nutkin burst into giggles.

Grandma Rose laughed so hard her eyes sparkled. "That's the silliest joke I've heard all day!"

Soon their acorn tower was twelve acorns tall! It was the most beautiful tower Nutkin had ever seen. The brown acorns caught the golden sunlight.

"We did it!" Nutkin cheered. He did a happy little dance.

"We make a wonderful team," Grandma Rose said proudly. "Your patience and my steady hands worked perfectly together."

Nutkin felt warm and happy inside. "I was so lonely before. But now I have a friend!"

"And I have a friend too," Grandma Rose said. "Would you like to be building buddies?"

"Yes, yes, yes!" Nutkin jumped up and down. His bushy tail swished with joy.

They admired their tower together. It stood tall and proud on the sunny hill. The wind made the leaves dance around them.

"Tomorrow, let's build a castle!" Nutkin suggested.

"With stone walls and acorn towers!" Grandma Rose added.

They high-fived with Nutkin's tiny paw and Grandma Rose's gentle hand. The sound echoed across the peaceful hill.

As the sun began to set, they carefully took apart their tower. They saved every acorn for tomorrow's adventure.

"Thank you for helping me," Nutkin said softly. "Everything is more fun with a friend."

"Thank you for letting me join you," Grandma Rose replied. "You made my day so much brighter."

They hugged goodbye. Nutkin scampered up his oak tree. Grandma Rose walked down the hill, humming happily.

From his tree branch, Nutkin waved with his fluffy tail. "See you tomorrow, Grandma Rose!"

"See you tomorrow, dear Nutkin!" she called back.

The hill grew quiet again. But now it felt different. It felt full of friendship and joy. Tomorrow would bring new adventures for two very special friends.

Nutkin curled up in his cozy nest. He dreamed of castles and towers. Most of all, he dreamed of his wonderful new friend.

The end.
