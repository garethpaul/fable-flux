# Story story_1100

**Penny's Magical Snack Time**

The big clock on the kitchen wall went tick-tock. Mama Pig looked at the clock. "Snack time, Penny!" she called.

Little Penny Pig was playing with her blocks. She built a tall tower. Red blocks, blue blocks, yellow blocks too. "Do I have to?" Penny asked. She made a sad face.

Penny did not want snack time. She wanted to keep playing. Snacks seemed boring today. Her tummy felt hungry but she didn't care.

Mama Pig smiled. "Come see what we have today," she said. Mama's voice sounded happy and excited.

Penny walked slowly to the kitchen. Her little hooves went tap-tap on the floor. She dragged her feet. Everything felt slow and heavy.

On the table sat a big blue bowl. Steam came up from the bowl. It smelled warm and sweet. "What is it?" Penny asked. Her nose twitched.

"Apple slices with cinnamon," Mama said. "But these are special apples." Mama winked one eye.

Penny climbed onto her chair. The apples looked normal. They were white and shiny. She picked up one slice. It felt cool and smooth.

When Penny took a bite, something amazing happened! The apple slice sparkled in her mouth. Tiny golden lights danced on her tongue. The cinnamon tasted like warm sunshine.

"Wow!" Penny said. Her eyes grew big and round. "It's magic!"

Mama Pig laughed. "Magic apples make everything better," she said. "But only when we try them."

Penny took another bite. This time she heard soft music. The music sounded like wind chimes. Sweet and gentle notes filled her ears. The apple crunched perfectly between her teeth.

"I can hear music!" Penny squealed. She clapped her hooves together. The sound made her giggle.

With the third bite, Penny saw colors. Beautiful rainbow colors swirled around the kitchen. Pink, purple, green, and orange. They danced in the air like butterflies.

"The colors are so pretty!" Penny said. She reached out to touch them. Her fingers went right through the rainbow swirls.

Mama Pig sat down next to Penny. "Magic happens when we open our hearts," she said. "Even to simple things like snack time."

Penny ate more apple slices. Each bite brought new surprises. Sometimes she tasted chocolate. Sometimes vanilla ice cream. Sometimes fresh strawberries from the garden.

"How do they change flavors?" Penny asked. She licked her lips. Everything tasted wonderful.

"The magic comes from trying new things," Mama explained. "And from being grateful for what we have."

Penny finished all her apple slices. Her tummy felt happy and full. The magical lights slowly faded away. The music got softer and softer. The colors gently disappeared.

But Penny still felt the magic inside her heart. It felt warm and glowing. Like a tiny sun in her chest.

"That was the best snack ever!" Penny said. She hugged Mama Pig tight. Mama smelled like flour and love.

"Tomorrow we'll have pear slices," Mama said. "Do you think they might be magical too?"

Penny's eyes sparkled with excitement. "Can we find out together?" she asked. She bounced up and down in her chair.

"Of course," Mama said. "Magic is always better when shared."

Penny helped clean up the kitchen. She put her bowl in the sink. She wiped the table with a soft cloth. Everything felt easy and fun now.

When bedtime came, Penny couldn't wait. She wanted tomorrow to come quickly. She wanted to discover new magic. Maybe the pears would sing songs. Maybe they would tell stories.

"Thank you for the magic apples," Penny whispered to Mama. She yawned a big yawn.

"Thank you for trying them," Mama whispered back.

Penny fell asleep dreaming of magical snacks. In her dreams, carrots danced and crackers painted pictures. Everything was possible when you opened your heart to magic.

And somewhere in the kitchen, tomorrow's pears waited quietly. Ready to share their own special magic with a little pig who learned to love snack time.

The end.
