# Story story_739

**Pepper the Puppet's Big Day**

Pepper the puppet lived in a colorful beach house. Her wooden arms moved when the wind blew. Her painted smile was always bright and cheerful.

Today was different though. Pepper felt wobbly inside her tummy. School started tomorrow. She had never been to school before.

"What if the other kids don't like me?" Pepper worried. "What if I can't do the work?"

The warm sand tickled her wooden feet. Seagulls called overhead. The ocean waves crashed softly nearby. But Pepper couldn't enjoy these happy sounds.

She sat on a piece of driftwood. Her strings felt all tangled up inside. Even the sunshine couldn't make her feel better.

"I need to practice being brave," Pepper decided. She looked around the beach for help.

First, she saw a big hermit crab. The crab was moving into a new shell. "Excuse me, Mr. Crab," Pepper called. "How do you feel brave in new places?"

The crab clicked his claws. "I practice walking around first. Then I feel ready for anything!"

Pepper tried walking around the beach. She marched in circles. She waved her arms up and down. She even did jumping jacks in the sand.

But her tummy still felt nervous. Walking around didn't help much.

"Maybe I need a different idea," Pepper sighed.

Next, she noticed a small sandpiper bird. The bird was learning to catch fish in the shallow waves. Sometimes the bird missed. But it kept trying anyway.

"Little bird," Pepper asked, "how do you keep going when things feel scary?"

The sandpiper chirped sweetly. "I practice the things I need to know. Practice makes everything easier!"

Pepper's wooden eyes lit up. "That's it! I can practice school things!"

She found a smooth piece of beach glass. She pretended it was a pencil. Pepper drew letters in the wet sand. A for apple. B for ball. C for cat.

The cool sand felt good under her fingers. Writing letters was actually fun!

Then Pepper gathered some pretty shells. She counted them out loud. "One, two, three, four, five!" Her voice got stronger with each number.

She found colorful seaweed and made patterns. Red, green, red, green. The seaweed felt soft and slippery.

A friendly dolphin swam close to shore. "What are you doing?" the dolphin asked.

"I'm practicing for school tomorrow," Pepper explained proudly. "I was feeling nervous. But practicing makes me feel better!"

The dolphin smiled with his whole face. "That's very smart! You're going to do great."

Pepper felt the warm sun on her painted cheeks. The ocean breeze made her strings dance. Her nervous tummy was starting to feel calm.

She practiced saying "Hello, my name is Pepper." She practiced raising her hand. She even practiced sitting quietly and listening.

A little sea turtle crawled by. "You look ready for anything now," the turtle said kindly.

Pepper realized the turtle was right. Her wooden body felt steady again. Her painted smile was real now, not just pretend.

"I am ready!" Pepper announced to the whole beach. "I practiced lots of things. I know my letters and numbers. I can be friendly and kind."

The waves seemed to clap for her. The seagulls cheered overhead. Even the sand crabs waved their claws.

That evening, Pepper packed her school bag. She put in a special shell to remember her beach friends. She wasn't nervous anymore.

The next morning, Pepper walked to school with confident steps. When the teacher asked everyone to write their names, Pepper remembered practicing in the sand.

When they counted pencils, Pepper thought of her shells. When they made patterns with blocks, she remembered her seaweed designs.

At recess, Pepper told her new friends about her beach home. They thought living by the ocean sounded wonderful.

"I was nervous about school," Pepper shared. "But practicing helped me feel brave."

Her new friend Maya nodded. "I was nervous too! But now school feels fun."

Pepper learned something important that day. When something feels scary, practicing helps. Trying different solutions helps too. And friends make everything better.

The beach had taught her well.

The end.
