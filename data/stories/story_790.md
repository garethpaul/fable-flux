# Story story_790

**Daisy Duck's Big Day**

Daisy Duck lived in a beautiful garden. Red roses grew by the pond. Yellow sunflowers reached toward the sky. The grass felt soft under her orange feet.

Every morning, Daisy splashed in the cool water. She ate crunchy seeds from the bird feeder. She napped under the shady apple tree. Life in the garden was perfect.

One sunny day, Mama Duck waddled over. "Daisy, tomorrow you start school," she said gently. "You'll learn new things and make friends."

Daisy's tummy felt funny. Her heart beat fast like a drum. "But I like our garden," she whispered. "What if the other ducks don't like me? What if I can't find the bathroom?"

That night, Daisy couldn't sleep. She tossed and turned on her soft nest. The stars twinkled above, but they couldn't calm her worried mind.

Morning came too soon. "Time for school!" called Mama Duck cheerfully.

Daisy had an idea. She would hide! She squeezed behind the big sunflower. The yellow petals tickled her head. "Mama can't see me here," she thought.

"Daisy? Where are you?" Mama Duck called. Her voice sounded worried now.

Daisy peeked out. Mama Duck looked sad. Her feathers drooped down. Daisy felt bad for making Mama worry.

"I'm here," Daisy said softly. She waddled out slowly.

"Are you scared about school?" asked Mama Duck. Her voice was warm like sunshine.

Daisy nodded. A tiny tear rolled down her yellow cheek.

Mama Duck hugged her tight. "Let's try something," she said. "We'll practice being at school right here in our garden."

This was Daisy's second idea, but better! They could pretend together.

Mama Duck gathered some smooth stones. She arranged them in a circle by the pond. "This is your classroom," she explained.

A friendly frog hopped over. "Hello! I'm Teacher Frog," Mama Duck said in a funny voice. Daisy giggled despite feeling nervous.

"Good morning, students," said Teacher Frog. "Today we'll learn about colors."

Daisy looked around the garden. "The roses are red!" she said proudly.

"Very good! What else?" asked Teacher Frog.

"The grass is green. The sun is yellow. The sky is blue!" Daisy felt excited now.

They practiced eating lunch together. Mama Duck pretended to be different duck friends. "Hi, I'm Penny Duck. Want to share my crackers?" she quacked.

"Yes, please!" said Daisy. "I have some seeds to share too."

They practiced raising wings to ask questions. They practiced walking in a line. They even practiced bathroom breaks behind the rose bush.

"This is fun!" said Daisy. Her nervous feeling started melting away like ice in warm water.

The next morning felt different. Daisy still felt butterflies in her tummy. But now she also felt brave and ready.

At the real school, everything looked familiar. There was a circle for sitting, just like in the garden. The teacher was kind, just like Teacher Frog. The other ducklings smiled and shared their snacks.

"Hi, I'm Benny," said a little duck with brown spots. "Want to be friends?"

"Yes!" said Daisy happily. "I'm Daisy. I live in a garden with beautiful flowers."

During art time, Daisy painted her garden. She used red for roses, yellow for sunflowers, and blue for the pond. Her painting looked wonderful.

"Tell us about your picture," said the real teacher.

"This is my home," said Daisy proudly. "It's where I learned to be brave."

After school, Daisy raced home to the garden. "Mama! School was amazing!" she quacked loudly.

"What happened?" asked Mama Duck with a big smile.

"I made a friend named Benny. I painted our garden. I answered questions. And I wasn't scared anymore!"

Mama Duck hugged her tight. "You were very brave, little one."

That evening, Daisy splashed happily in the pond. Tomorrow she would go to school again. She felt excited instead of scared.

Sometimes being brave means trying again when the first idea doesn't work. Sometimes the best solutions come from practicing and preparing together.

Daisy fell asleep under the twinkling stars, dreaming of new adventures at school.

The end.
