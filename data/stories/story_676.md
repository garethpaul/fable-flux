# Story story_676

**Teddy's Big Search**

Teddy the brown bear lived in a cozy playroom. She had soft, fuzzy fur and kind brown eyes. Every morning, Teddy played with her best friend. Her best friend was a pretty doll named Rosie.

Rosie had golden hair that sparkled. She wore a blue dress with tiny flowers. Teddy loved Rosie very much. They had tea parties together. They read books and sang songs.

One sunny morning, Teddy woke up. She stretched her fuzzy arms wide. "Good morning, Rosie!" she called out. But Rosie was not on her special pillow. Teddy looked left. She looked right. Where was her pretty doll?

"Oh no!" Teddy gasped. Her heart felt heavy and sad. "Rosie is gone! I must find her!"

Teddy's first idea was simple. She would look under things. Maybe Rosie fell somewhere dark. Teddy got down on her fuzzy belly. She crawled under the toy box. Dust tickled her nose. She found three marbles and a red crayon. But no Rosie.

Next, Teddy looked under the rocking chair. The wood felt smooth and cool. She found a puzzle piece shaped like a star. Still no Rosie. Under the bookshelf, Teddy discovered a squeaky mouse toy. It made her jump! But Rosie was not there either.

Teddy sat down and sighed. Her shoulders felt droopy. Looking under things did not work. What could she try next?

Then Teddy had a new idea. She would look inside things! Maybe Rosie was hiding somewhere cozy. Teddy opened the toy chest with both paws. Inside were blocks and balls. She moved them carefully with her fuzzy hands. The blocks felt smooth. The balls were bouncy and round. But no pretty doll.

Teddy checked inside the dollhouse. She peeked in every tiny room. The little furniture was so cute! But the rooms were empty. No Rosie anywhere.

Last, Teddy looked inside the big basket. This basket held dress-up clothes. Teddy pushed aside a sparkly crown. She moved a soft, silky scarf. The fabric felt smooth against her paw.

Then something caught her eye. Way at the bottom, she saw something blue. Something with tiny flowers! Teddy's heart jumped with joy.

"Rosie!" she cried happily. There was her pretty doll! Rosie was wrapped in a cozy blanket. Her golden hair still sparkled. Her blue dress was not wrinkled at all.

Teddy picked up Rosie gently. She hugged her tight against her fuzzy chest. Rosie felt warm and perfect. "I missed you so much!" Teddy whispered. "I looked and looked for you!"

Teddy remembered now. Yesterday, she played dress-up. She wrapped Rosie in the blanket to keep her warm. Then she forgot! The blanket made Rosie hard to see.

"I will never lose you again," Teddy promised. She gave Rosie another squeeze. "Next time, I will put you on your special pillow."

Teddy felt proud and happy. She did not give up when looking under things failed. She tried a different way. She kept searching until she found her friend.

The two friends sat by the sunny window. Warm light made everything glow. Teddy brushed Rosie's golden hair with a tiny brush. The hair felt silky and smooth.

"Should we have a tea party?" Teddy asked. She set up the little table. She arranged two small cups. The cups clinked softly together.

Teddy poured pretend tea. Steam seemed to rise from the cups. "Here is your tea, Rosie," she said politely. "I am so glad you are safe."

The playroom felt perfect again. Teddy learned something important that day. When one way does not work, try another way. Never give up on something special.

As the sun set, Teddy yawned. She placed Rosie on her special pillow. "Good night, dear friend," she whispered. "Sweet dreams."

Teddy curled up nearby. She felt warm and content. Tomorrow would bring new adventures. But tonight, she was happy. Her pretty doll was safe and sound.

Both friends slept peacefully in their cozy playroom.

The end.
