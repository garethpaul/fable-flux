# Story story_1099

**Lily's Lost Doll**

Baby Lily sat in the long hallway. She looked left and right. Her pretty pink doll was gone! 

"Where is Rosie?" Lily whispered. Her bottom lip started to wobble. Rosie had soft yellow hair and a sparkly dress. Lily loved her so much.

The hallway was quiet and dim. Sunlight came through the window at the end. Dust danced in the warm light. Lily's bare feet felt cold on the smooth floor.

"Mama!" Lily called. But Mama was busy in the kitchen. The smell of cookies floated down the hall. Lily's tummy rumbled, but she didn't want cookies. She wanted Rosie.

Lily remembered playing with Rosie this morning. They had a tea party by the big plant. Then they played peek-a-boo near the coat closet. After that, everything was fuzzy.

"I will find you, Rosie," Lily said bravely.

First, Lily crawled to the coat closet. It was dark inside. She pushed aside the heavy coats. They smelled like Daddy's soap and Mama's flowers. 

"Rosie? Are you hiding?" Lily called softly.

She felt around with her little hands. She found Daddy's shoes and Mama's purse. She found her brother's baseball glove. But no Rosie.

Lily sat back on her heels. A tear rolled down her cheek. "Where are you?" she sniffled.

The hallway seemed longer now. The walls were tall and white. Family pictures smiled down at her. But Lily felt very small and sad.

Then Lily heard something. A tiny sound from the living room. It sounded like... crying?

Lily crawled faster than she ever had before. Her knees made soft patting sounds on the floor. Her heart beat fast with hope.

She peeked around the corner. There was Rosie! But something was wrong. Rosie was stuck under the heavy coffee table. Only her sparkly dress showed.

"Oh no!" Lily gasped. "Poor Rosie!"

Lily tried to reach under the table. Her arm was too short. She stretched and stretched until her fingers hurt. She could just touch Rosie's dress, but she couldn't grab her.

Lily sat back and thought hard. She looked around the room. She saw the big couch pillows. They were soft and squishy.

"I have an idea!" Lily said.

She pushed a pillow to the coffee table. It was almost as big as she was! The pillow felt fluffy under her hands. It smelled like home and hugs.

Lily climbed on top of the pillow. Now she was higher up! She could reach further under the table.

"Come here, Rosie," Lily said gently.

She stretched her arm under the table again. This time, her fingers wrapped around Rosie's soft body. Slowly, carefully, Lily pulled her doll free.

"Rosie!" Lily squealed with joy.

She hugged her doll tight. Rosie's yellow hair tickled Lily's nose. Her sparkly dress felt smooth and pretty. Lily spun around and around, holding Rosie close.

"I missed you so much," Lily whispered to her doll.

Just then, Mama came into the room. She was wiping her hands on her apron. She smelled like warm cookies and love.

"There you are, little one," Mama said with a smile. "I see you found Rosie."

"She was stuck, Mama," Lily explained. "But I saved her!"

"You did such a good job," Mama said proudly. "You didn't give up."

Lily beamed. She felt proud and happy. Her chest felt warm and full.

"I used my thinking brain," Lily said, pointing to her head.

"Yes, you did," Mama agreed. "And you were very brave."

Lily sat on the soft rug with Rosie in her lap. She smoothed down Rosie's yellow hair. The sparkly dress caught the light from the window.

"Next time we play hide-and-seek," Lily told Rosie, "don't hide so well!"

Mama laughed. The sound was like music in the sunny room.

Lily had learned something important today. When something seems too hard, she could try a different way. She could use her thinking brain and never give up.

"Come on, Rosie," Lily said, standing up. "Let's have those cookies with Mama."

Hand in hand with her pretty doll, Lily walked to the kitchen. The hallway didn't seem scary anymore. It was just the path to her next adventure.

The end.
