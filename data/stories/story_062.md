# Benny's Wonderful Walk Time

Benny the bunny sat by the big window. He watched raindrops race down the glass. His mama called from the kitchen, "Benny! It's walk time!"

Benny's ears drooped down low. "But Mama," he said softly. "It's raining outside today. My paws will get all muddy. My fur will get all wet."

Mama Bunny hopped over slowly. She sat beside her little one. "Sometimes the best adventures happen," she said gently, "when we try new things."

Benny looked at his yellow boots. They sat by the front door. "Do I have to go?" he asked with a tiny sigh.

"Yes, little one," Mama said kindly. "But I have a surprise today. We're going somewhere very special."

Benny put on his bright boots. He pulled on his red raincoat. The hood made funny crinkly sounds. Mama opened the big front door.

Step, step, splash! Benny's boots hit the wet ground. The rain felt cool and gentle. It made soft pattering sounds everywhere.

"Listen, Benny," Mama whispered softly. "Do you hear that music?"

Benny stopped and listened carefully. Pitter-patter, pitter-patter went the rain. Drip-drop, drip-drop sang the leaves. Splash-splash went his little boots.

"It's a rainy day song!" Benny said with growing excitement.

They walked down the garden path. Everything looked different and magical. The flowers wore sparkling water crowns. The grass looked extra green. Spider webs held tiny silver beads.

"Look there!" Mama pointed ahead.

A big puddle reflected the sky. It looked like a mirror. Benny saw fluffy white clouds. He saw his own happy face.

"Can I jump in?" Benny asked hopefully.

"Of course!" Mama laughed with joy.

Splash! Benny jumped right in. Water flew everywhere in circles. He giggled and jumped again. Splash, splash, splash!

"I'm making water fireworks!" Benny shouted with pure delight.

They walked to the little park. The swings were empty and quiet. But the slide was perfect. Water made it super slippery.

"Wheee!" Benny slid down fast. Water sprayed behind him playfully. He climbed up and slid again. And again and again!

Under the big oak tree, they found something wonderful. Dozens of earthworms wiggled freely. They were having their own party.

"Hello, little worms!" Benny said politely.

He watched them dance and wiggle. They seemed so happy together. The rain was their favorite weather.

"They love rainy days," Mama explained gently. "Just like we do now."

Benny picked up fallen leaves. He made a colorful leaf boat. He put it carefully in a stream. The water carried it away.

"Goodbye, little boat!" he called out cheerfully.

They found a snail shell. They discovered smooth, wet rocks. They saw a robin splashing happily. Everything felt fresh and new.

"Mama," Benny said with wonder, "rain makes everything so beautiful."

"Yes, it does," Mama agreed warmly. "And you discovered that yourself."

As they walked home together, Benny felt different inside. His paws were muddy, yes. His fur was damp, true. But his heart felt light.

"Tomorrow is walk time again," Mama said at the door.

"Will it rain tomorrow too?" Benny asked with bright hope.

"Maybe," Mama said with twinkling eyes. "But sunny days are magical too."

Benny thought about that carefully. "Can we find puddles when it's sunny?" he wondered out loud.

"We can look for them," Mama promised with a smile.

That night, Benny dreamed sweetly. He dreamed of splashing puddles. He dreamed of water fireworks. He dreamed of leaf boats sailing.

When morning came bright and early, Benny hopped to the window. The sun was shining brilliantly. But he wasn't disappointed at all.

"Mama!" he called out excitedly. "It's walk time soon! What will we discover today?"

Mama Bunny smiled her biggest smile. Her little Benny had learned something wonderful. Every walk time held magic. You just had to look.

And from that day forward, Benny never missed walk time. Rain or shine, adventure waited.

The end.
