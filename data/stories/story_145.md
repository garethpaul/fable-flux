# Woolly's Big Block Hunt

Woolly the sheep loved playing in the big sandbox at Sunny Meadow Park. His soft, fluffy wool bounced as he hopped over the wooden edge. The warm sand felt good between his hooves.

"Time to build!" Woolly said happily. He looked around for his favorite red blocks. But wait—where were they?

Woolly's eyes grew wide. His blocks were gone! Yesterday he had built a tall tower. Now he only saw empty sand everywhere.

"Oh no," Woolly whispered. His bottom lip wobbled. "Where are my blocks?"

The sandbox felt very big and empty. Other toys were scattered around. There was Maya's yellow shovel. Tom's blue bucket sat by the corner. But no red blocks anywhere.

Woolly took a deep breath. "I won't give up," he said firmly. "I'll find them!"

First, Woolly walked slowly around the sandbox edges. Step, step, step went his little hooves. He looked behind the wooden walls. He checked under the bench. No blocks there.

Next, Woolly began digging gently in the sand. His hooves made soft scratching sounds. Scritch, scratch, scritch. The sand was cool and smooth. But still no blocks.

"Maybe they're buried deeper," Woolly thought. He dug a little hole. Then another hole. Sand flew everywhere, making tiny clouds. His wool got dusty, but he kept digging.

Scritch, scratch, scritch.

A glint of red caught his eye! Woolly's heart jumped. He dug faster. But it was only Maya's red hair ribbon, lost from yesterday.

Woolly sat down in the sand. His shoulders drooped. This was harder than he thought. The sun felt warm on his woolly back. A gentle breeze rustled the nearby trees.

"Don't give up, Woolly!" called his friend Bella the bunny from across the playground. "You can do it!"

Woolly smiled. Bella believed in him. He stood up and brushed sand from his wool. Time to try something different.

Woolly closed his eyes and thought carefully. Where was the last place he played yesterday? He remembered building near the big oak tree's shadow. The shadow had moved as the sun traveled across the sky.

"The tree!" Woolly exclaimed. He trotted to where the shadow fell now. The sand here looked different—a little bumpy.

Woolly began digging again. Scritch, scratch, scritch. His hooves hit something hard. Something square!

"A block!" he shouted joyfully. Woolly dug faster. One red block appeared. Then two! Then three! Soon he found all six of his precious building blocks, buried in a neat pile.

"I found them! I found them!" Woolly danced around in circles. His wool bounced up and down. Sand sparkled in the sunlight like tiny diamonds.

Bella hopped over to see. "You did it, Woolly! You never gave up!"

"It was hard," Woolly panted, catching his breath. "But I kept trying different ways. First I looked around the edges. Then I dug in the middle. Finally I remembered where I played yesterday!"

Woolly carefully arranged his blocks in the sand. He built a beautiful castle with tall towers. Each red block fit perfectly on top of the next. The castle stood strong and proud.

"My blocks are even more special now," Woolly said, patting his creation gently. "I worked so hard to find them."

As the afternoon sun warmed the sandbox, Woolly felt proud and happy. His wool was sandy and his hooves were tired, but his heart felt light as a feather.

Other children came to admire his castle. "Wow, Woolly! How did you build that?" they asked.

"With patience and persistence," Woolly said wisely. "And by never giving up, even when things got hard."

The sandbox was full of laughter and play once again. Woolly's red blocks gleamed in the sunshine, and his castle stood tall—a reminder that good things come to those who keep trying.

That night, Woolly fell asleep with a big smile on his fuzzy face, dreaming of tomorrow's new adventures in the sandbox.

The end.
