# Story story_897

**Tiger and the Dancing Kite**

Tiger sat alone in the big dining room. The wooden floor felt cool under his orange paws. Sunlight streamed through tall windows, making golden patches on the carpet. Tiger's striped tail swished back and forth. He felt very lonely.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," Tiger sighed. His voice echoed in the quiet room.

High up near the ceiling, a bright red kite heard Tiger's sad words. The kite had been hanging there all morning. Her long ribbon tail sparkled in the sunlight. She wanted to help the lonely tiger.

"Hello down there!" called the kite in a cheerful voice.

Tiger looked up, surprised. "Who said that?"

"It's me! I'm Kite!" She wiggled her colorful paper body. "I've been watching you. You look sad."

Tiger's amber eyes grew wide. "You can talk?"

"Of course I can! Would you like to be friends?" Kite asked hopefully.

Tiger's whiskers twitched with excitement. "Yes! But how can we play? You're way up there."

Kite thought for a moment. Then she had an idea. "Watch this!" She began to spin slowly. Her ribbon tail danced through the air like a rainbow snake.

Tiger laughed. The sound filled the dining room with joy. "That's amazing! Can you do more tricks?"

"I can glide!" Kite pushed herself gently forward. She floated across the room like a graceful bird. Her paper crinkled softly as she moved.

Tiger clapped his big paws together. "Wow! I want to try something too!" He stood on his back legs and spun in a circle. His orange and black stripes blurred together.

"Beautiful spinning!" Kite cheered. "Now let's play together!"

Kite swooped down lower. Tiger jumped up high, trying to touch her ribbon tail. They played this game for several minutes. Tiger's pounces made soft thumping sounds on the wooden floor.

"Let's play hide and seek!" suggested Tiger.

Kite floated behind the big dining table. "Find me!" she called.

Tiger prowled around the room on quiet paws. He sniffed the air with his pink nose. "I smell... paper and... happiness!" he announced.

"You found me!" Kite giggled, floating out from her hiding spot.

Next, they played follow the leader. Kite glided in figure-eights around the dining room. Tiger followed below, weaving between chair legs. His paws padded softly on the smooth floor.

"This is the most fun I've had all week!" Tiger purred loudly.

"Me too!" agreed Kite. "I was getting tired of just hanging still."

They played until the afternoon sun began to fade. Tiger's tummy rumbled with hunger. Kite noticed right away.

"Are you hungry, friend?" she asked kindly.

"A little," Tiger admitted. "But I don't want to stop playing."

Kite had another wonderful idea. "What if I keep you company while you eat? I can tell you stories!"

Tiger's eyes sparkled. "That sounds perfect!"

Tiger padded to his food bowl in the corner. Kite floated nearby, her ribbon tail curling prettily. As Tiger crunched his kibble, Kite told him about flying high above the clouds and dancing with the wind.

"Tomorrow, can we play again?" Tiger asked hopefully.

"Every day!" promised Kite. "Friends stick together."

Tiger's purr rumbled through the dining room. He felt warm and happy inside. The lonely feeling had completely disappeared.

As evening came, Kite settled into her favorite spot near the window. Tiger curled up in a sunny patch on the carpet below. Even though they were resting, they were together.

"Thank you for being my friend," Tiger whispered.

"Thank you for asking me to play," Kite whispered back.

From that day forward, Tiger was never lonely in the dining room. He had found something wonderful – a true friend who loved to play and laugh together. Every morning brought new games and adventures.

The dining room filled with joy and laughter every single day. Tiger and Kite had learned that friendship makes everything better. They were the happiest pair in the whole house.

The end.
