# Story story_1344

**Baby Luna and Whiskers the Cat**

Baby Luna sat on the soft grass. The farm was big and green. She played with her red ball alone. The sun felt warm on her face.

Luna rolled the ball back and forth. She giggled a little. But something felt missing. Playing alone wasn't as much fun.

"I wish I had a friend," Luna said softly.

Near the red barn lived Whiskers the cat. Whiskers had orange and white fur. His whiskers were long and tickly. He watched Luna from behind a hay bale.

Whiskers wanted to play too. He felt shy though. Would the baby like him?

The orange cat took tiny steps closer. His paws made no sound on the grass. Luna didn't see him yet.

Whiskers reached out one paw. He gently touched the red ball. The ball rolled toward Luna.

"Oh!" Luna clapped her hands. "Where did you come from?"

Whiskers meowed softly. His green eyes sparkled. His tail swished back and forth.

Luna smiled big. "Do you want to play with me?"

The cat purred loudly. It sounded like a tiny motor. Luna giggled at the funny sound.

She rolled the ball to Whiskers. The cat batted it with his paw. The ball rolled back to Luna.

"This is fun!" Luna said. She clapped again.

They played catch with the red ball. Back and forth it went. Whiskers jumped and pounced. Luna laughed and squealed.

Then Whiskers had an idea. He ran to the flower garden. Pretty yellow flowers grew there. The cat picked one with his mouth.

Whiskers brought the flower to Luna. He dropped it in her lap. The flower smelled sweet like honey.

"For me?" Luna asked. She touched the soft petals.

Whiskers rubbed against her arm. His fur felt warm and fluffy. Luna pet his head gently.

"You're so soft," she whispered.

The cat climbed into Luna's lap. He curled up in a ball. His purring got louder and louder.

Luna hugged Whiskers carefully. She didn't want to squeeze too tight. The cat's whiskers tickled her cheek.

"I have a friend now," Luna said happily.

They played more games together. Whiskers chased butterflies. Luna crawled after him on her hands and knees. The grass felt cool and bumpy.

When Whiskers climbed a small tree, Luna watched from below. She pointed up at him.

"You're so high!" she called.

Whiskers meowed down at her. Then he jumped into a pile of soft leaves. Luna laughed as leaves flew everywhere.

The baby tried to catch the falling leaves. Whiskers helped by batting them toward her. Soon they were both covered in brown and yellow leaves.

Luna's mama came outside. She carried a plate of crackers and milk.

"I see you found a friend," Mama said with a smile.

"This is Whiskers," Luna said proudly. "He's my best friend."

Mama pet the cat's head. Whiskers purred and rubbed against her hand.

"Would Whiskers like some milk?" Mama asked.

She poured milk into a small bowl. Whiskers lapped it up with his pink tongue. Luna ate her crackers and watched.

As the sun got lower, Luna felt sleepy. She yawned big. Whiskers yawned too, showing his tiny white teeth.

"Time for a nap," Mama said.

Luna lay down on a soft blanket under the apple tree. Whiskers curled up next to her. His warm body felt cozy.

"Will you be here when I wake up?" Luna asked.

Whiskers meowed softly. He licked her hand with his rough tongue. It felt like sandpaper but gentle.

Luna closed her eyes. She listened to Whiskers purring. The sound made her feel safe and happy.

When she woke up, Whiskers was still there. He touched her nose with his cold, wet nose.

"You stayed!" Luna said, hugging him tight.

From that day on, Luna and Whiskers played together every day. They were the very best of friends. The farm felt full of joy and laughter.

Luna was never lonely again. She had found the perfect friend in Whiskers the cat.

The end.
