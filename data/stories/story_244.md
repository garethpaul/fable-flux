# Grandma's Cookie Adventure

Grandma Rose loved her sunny garden. She wore her favorite purple hat and bright yellow gloves. The flowers danced in the warm breeze. Birds sang happy songs in the trees.

"What a beautiful day for gardening!" said Grandma Rose. She smiled at her colorful flowers. Red roses. Pink tulips. Orange marigolds everywhere.

Grandma Rose worked hard all morning. She pulled weeds from the soft dirt. She watered her thirsty plants. The sun made her feel warm and happy.

Soon, Grandma Rose felt very hungry. Her tummy made a little rumble sound. "Oh my," she said. "Time for my favorite cookies!"

She looked up at her kitchen window. There on the windowsill sat a plate. A plate full of chocolate chip cookies! They looked so yummy and sweet.

But there was a big problem. The window was very, very high. Grandma Rose reached up with her arms. She stretched and stretched. But she couldn't reach the cookies at all.

"Oh dear," said Grandma Rose. "How will I get my cookies?"

First, she tried jumping. Hop! Hop! Hop! But Grandma Rose was too short. The cookies stayed far away.

Next, she looked around her garden. "Maybe something can help me," she said. She saw her small watering can. Grandma Rose stood on the watering can. She reached up again. Still too short!

"Hmm," said Grandma Rose. "I need to think harder." She tapped her finger on her chin. Think, think, think.

Then she had an idea! Grandma Rose found her sturdy garden stool. It was green and strong. She pushed it close to the window. Step by step, she climbed up carefully.

She reached up with both arms. Almost there! But still not quite high enough. The cookies looked so close but felt so far.

Grandma Rose didn't give up. She looked around some more. Then she spotted her long garden rake. "Perfect!" she said with a big smile.

Very carefully, she held the rake handle. She stretched it up toward the window. The rake was just long enough! Gently, slowly, she pulled the cookie plate closer.

"Come here, little cookies," she said softly. The plate slid across the windowsill. Closer and closer it came.

Finally, the plate reached the edge. Grandma Rose caught it safely in her hands. "Hooray!" she cheered. "I did it!"

She climbed down from her stool carefully. The cookies smelled wonderful. Sweet chocolate and warm vanilla filled the air.

Grandma Rose sat on her favorite garden bench. She took a big, delicious bite. "Mmm," she said. "These taste extra special today."

A little blue bird landed nearby. "Hello, little friend," said Grandma Rose. "Would you like some cookie crumbs?" She scattered tiny pieces on the ground.

The bird chirped happily and ate the crumbs. More birds came to join the feast. Soon, Grandma Rose had many feathered friends around her.

"You know," she said to the birds, "sometimes problems seem too big. But if we keep trying, we can solve them." The birds tilted their heads and listened.

Grandma Rose finished her cookies slowly. She felt proud and happy. The warm sun felt good on her face. Her garden smelled like roses and fresh earth.

"I couldn't reach high enough," she told the birds. "But I didn't stop trying. I used my stool. I used my rake. I found a way!"

The birds chirped as if they understood. They seemed to say, "Good job, Grandma Rose!"

As the afternoon sun began to set, Grandma Rose felt wonderful. Her tummy was full of yummy cookies. Her heart was full of happy feelings.

She had learned something important today. Big problems need creative solutions. When one idea doesn't work, try another idea. And another. Never give up!

"Tomorrow I'll put my cookies somewhere easier to reach," laughed Grandma Rose. "But today, I proved I can solve any problem!"

The birds sang their evening songs. Grandma Rose hummed along happily. Her garden adventure had ended perfectly.

The end.
