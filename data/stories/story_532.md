# Zara and the Stormy Night

Zara the zebra loved her cozy living room. Soft purple cushions covered the big couch. Warm yellow light glowed from the pretty lamp. Her favorite books sat stacked on the wooden table.

But tonight felt different. Very different.

*RUMBLE!* Thunder rolled across the sky. *FLASH!* Lightning lit up the windows. Rain went *pitter-patter-pitter-patter* on the roof.

Zara's black and white stripes trembled. Her ears drooped down. "Oh no," she whispered. "The storm sounds so scary!"

She pulled her soft blue blanket over her head. But the thunder still went *BOOM!* She could still hear everything.

"Maybe I can hide," Zara thought. She trotted behind the big brown chair. She curled up very small. But *CRASH!* went the thunder again.

"That didn't work," Zara said sadly.

Next, she tried covering her ears. She pressed her hooves tight against them. "La-la-la-la-la!" she sang loudly. But she could still hear the storm rumbling.

Zara felt tears in her big brown eyes. "Nothing helps! I'm still scared!"

Then she remembered something special. Mama always said, "When you feel worried, try something new."

Zara looked around the living room. She saw her music box on the shelf. "Maybe music will help," she thought.

She wound up the little silver key. *Tinkle-tinkle-tinkle!* The gentle music played. It sounded like tiny bells dancing.

The pretty music made Zara feel a little better. But *RUMBLE!* The thunder came again.

"I need more help," Zara decided.

She looked at her books. The red one had pictures of rainbows. The green one showed sunny meadows. "Maybe pretty pictures will help too!"

Zara opened the rainbow book. She saw pink clouds and golden sunshine. She saw purple flowers and blue butterflies.

*FLASH!* Lightning lit the room. But Zara kept looking at the beautiful pictures. They made her think of happy things.

"This helps a little more," she said. "But I still feel worried."

Then Zara had her best idea yet. "What if I do something really special?"

She gathered all her favorite things. Her soft blanket. Her music box. Her rainbow book. She even got her stuffed elephant, Peanut.

Zara made a cozy nest on the couch. She wrapped the blanket around herself and Peanut. She played the tinkling music. She looked at the pretty pictures.

*RUMBLE!* went the thunder.

But this time, Zara did something new. She took a deep breath. "The storm is just rain and wind," she said out loud. "It can't hurt me in here. I'm safe and warm."

She hugged Peanut tight. "See, Peanut? We have everything we need. Soft blankets. Pretty music. Beautiful pictures. And each other."

*Pitter-patter-pitter-patter.* The rain sounded gentler now. Like tiny feet dancing on the roof.

*FLASH!* The lightning looked like nature's flashlight. Showing the world for just a moment.

*RUMBLE!* The thunder sounded like a big drum. Playing music in the sky.

"You know what, Peanut?" Zara smiled. "The storm isn't scary. It's just different. It's like the sky is putting on a show!"

She listened to her music box. She looked at her rainbow pictures. She felt her soft blanket. Everything warm and good was right here with her.

Soon, Zara's eyes grew heavy. The storm sounds became a lullaby. *Pitter-patter, rumble, pitter-patter.* Like a gentle song.

When morning came, golden sunshine filled the living room. The storm was gone. Everything sparkled clean and fresh.

Zara stretched her striped legs. She felt proud and happy. "I did it!" she said. "I felt scared, but I didn't give up. I tried and tried until I found what helped."

She hugged Peanut tight. "When something feels scary, we can always find ways to feel better. We just have to keep trying!"

From that day on, whenever storms came, Zara knew exactly what to do. She would make her cozy nest, play her music, and remember: she was brave, she was safe, and she could handle anything.
