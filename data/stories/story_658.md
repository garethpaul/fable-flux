# Story story_658

**Max and the Lost Doll**

Max loved playing in the sunny meadow behind her house. The grass felt soft under her bare feet. Pretty wildflowers danced in the warm breeze. She always brought her favorite doll, Rosie, to play with her.

Rosie had curly yellow hair and a bright red dress. Max loved her so much. They would have tea parties under the big oak tree. They would pick dandelions together. Rosie was Max's very best friend.

One afternoon, Max was running through the tall grass. She was chasing a beautiful blue butterfly. The butterfly fluttered here and there. Max giggled as she followed it deeper into the meadow.

When the butterfly finally flew away, Max looked around. Something was wrong. Very wrong.

"Rosie!" Max called out. "Where are you, Rosie?"

Max looked down at her empty hands. Her heart felt heavy and sad. She had dropped her precious doll somewhere in the meadow. The grass was so tall and thick. Where could Rosie be?

Max started to cry. Big tears rolled down her cheeks. "I lost my best friend," she whispered.

But Max was a brave little girl. She wiped her tears away. "I will find you, Rosie," she said firmly. "I won't give up."

Max decided to look near the oak tree first. That was their favorite spot. She pushed through the green grass. She looked behind every flower. She checked under every leaf.

"Rosie? Are you here?" Max called softly.

She found a pretty pink flower. She found a smooth gray rock. She found a fuzzy caterpillar crawling on a stick. But no Rosie.

Max felt worried. The sun was getting lower in the sky. Soon it would be dinner time. Her mom would call her inside.

"Think, Max, think," she said to herself. She closed her eyes. She tried to remember exactly where she had been playing.

Then Max had an idea. Maybe she should follow the same path the butterfly took. She remembered chasing it near the little stream. The water made a gentle bubbling sound.

Max walked carefully toward the stream. The grass was even taller here. It tickled her arms as she pushed through it. She could hear the water getting closer.

"Please be here, Rosie," Max whispered hopefully.

She looked all around the mossy rocks by the stream. She checked behind the cattails that grew in the water. The stream sparkled in the afternoon sunlight.

Then Max saw something red near a fallen log. Her heart jumped with excitement. She ran over as fast as she could.

There was Rosie! Her doll was sitting against the old log. Her red dress was a little dusty. Some grass was stuck in her yellow curls. But she was safe and sound.

"Rosie!" Max shouted with joy. She scooped up her doll and hugged her tight. "I found you! I was so worried about you."

Max brushed the grass out of Rosie's hair. She smoothed down her red dress. "You look beautiful again," Max said with a big smile.

The two friends sat by the babbling stream for a few minutes. Max felt so happy and relieved. The warm sun felt good on her face. A gentle breeze made the water ripple.

"I almost gave up looking for you," Max told Rosie. "But I kept trying. I'm so glad I didn't stop."

Max learned something important that day. When something is really important to you, you should never give up. Even when things seem hard or scary, you can solve problems if you keep trying.

As the sun began to set, Max heard her mom calling from the house. "Max! Time for dinner!"

"Coming, Mom!" Max called back. She stood up and held Rosie's hand.

Together, they walked home through the meadow. The grass swayed gently around them. Max felt proud and happy. She had solved her problem all by herself.

From that day on, Max always held Rosie's hand extra tight when they played in the meadow. And she never forgot the lesson she learned about being brave and never giving up.

The end.
