# Story story_1370

**Tiger's Special Walk**

Tiger stretched his orange paws. The sun was bright outside. "Walk time!" called Mama Tiger.

Tiger's ears drooped down. He did not want to go. Walking seemed boring today. His cozy bed felt so warm.

"Do I have to?" Tiger asked softly. His tail swished slowly back and forth.

Mama Tiger smiled. "Fresh air will feel good. We can go slowly today."

Tiger sighed a big sigh. He put on his tiny red shoes. They felt heavy on his paws. The door seemed very far away.

Outside, the grass tickled Tiger's nose. Birds chirped hello from tall trees. Tiger still felt grumpy about walking.

"My legs feel tired already," Tiger said. He sat down on the soft ground.

Mama Tiger sat beside him. "What do you see?" she asked gently.

Tiger looked around. A yellow butterfly danced near his ear. Its wings made tiny whooshing sounds. Tiger's eyes grew wide.

"Pretty butterfly," Tiger whispered. He stood up slowly. The butterfly flew three steps ahead.

Tiger took three small steps. The butterfly waited for him. Then it flew three more steps.

"It wants to play!" Tiger said. His tail began to wiggle. The butterfly's wings sparkled like tiny jewels.

They walked past Mrs. Rabbit's garden. Red tomatoes hung like ornaments. Purple flowers smelled like candy. Tiger sniffed deeply.

"Everything smells so good," Tiger said. His nose twitched happily.

The butterfly led them to the duck pond. Baby ducks paddled in circles. They made soft peeping sounds. Their yellow feathers looked fluffy.

"Quack, quack!" called Mother Duck. "Hello, Tiger!"

Tiger waved his paw. "Hello, ducks!" The baby ducks splashed tiny water drops. Tiger giggled at the sparkles.

Next, they found the berry bushes. Blueberries hung like tiny blue balloons. Mama Tiger picked some for Tiger.

"Sweet!" Tiger said. Juice dripped down his chin. The berries tasted like summer sunshine.

The butterfly flew to a big oak tree. Tiger followed underneath. Leaves rustled like whispered secrets. Acorns crunched under his paws.

"Listen," said Mama Tiger. "The tree is talking."

Tiger pressed his ear close. Wind sang through the branches. It sounded like a gentle lullaby.

They walked up a small hill. Tiger's legs felt stronger now. At the top, he could see everything. His house looked tiny below.

"I'm up so high!" Tiger called out. The butterfly circled around his head three times.

On the way down, Tiger found a smooth rock. It felt warm from the sun. He put it in his pocket.

"A treasure," Tiger said proudly. The rock would remind him of today.

They passed the flower shop. Roses smelled like his grandmother's hugs. Sunflowers stood tall like yellow giants. Tiger touched their soft petals.

Near home, Tiger saw his friend Bunny. "Tiger! Want to play tomorrow?" Bunny asked.

"Yes!" Tiger said. "We can follow butterflies!"

Back in their yard, Tiger felt proud. His legs had carried him far. His heart felt happy and full.

"Walk time was fun," Tiger told Mama. "I saw so many things."

Mama Tiger hugged him tight. "You were very brave today."

Tiger looked at his red shoes. They didn't feel heavy anymore. His legs felt strong and ready.

"Can we walk again tomorrow?" Tiger asked. His eyes sparkled with excitement.

"Of course," Mama Tiger said. "There are always new things to discover."

That night, Tiger held his warm rock. He thought about the dancing butterfly. He remembered the sweet berries and singing trees.

"Tomorrow I'll find more treasures," Tiger whispered. He closed his eyes and smiled.

Outside his window, the butterfly rested on a flower. It would wait for Tiger's next adventure.

Tiger fell asleep dreaming of sunny walks. His tail curled up happily. Walk time wasn't boring at all. It was magical.

The end.
