# Story story_1387

**Papa's Lost Doll**

Papa sat at the big wooden table. She was eating her breakfast cereal. The spoon made tiny clink sounds in her bowl.

"Where is my pretty doll?" Papa asked herself. She looked around the dining room. Her eyes searched everywhere.

The doll had long yellow hair. It wore a pink dress with tiny flowers. Papa loved that doll so much. She always kept it close by.

Papa's tummy felt worried. Her heart beat fast. "I need to find my doll," she whispered.

The dining room had many places to look. There were chairs with soft cushions. A tall cabinet stood against the wall. Sunlight came through the big window.

Papa pushed back her chair. It made a scraping sound on the floor. She got down on her hands and knees. The carpet felt soft under her fingers.

"Maybe my doll is under the table," Papa thought. She crawled around looking everywhere. She found a crayon and some crumbs. But no doll.

Papa looked under each chair. She moved the cushions around. Still no pretty doll anywhere.

Her eyes started to feel watery. "This is too hard," Papa said sadly. She sat on the floor and sighed.

Then Papa remembered something important. Mama always said, "Try again when things seem hard."

Papa wiped her eyes with her sleeve. She stood up tall. "I will look one more time," she said bravely.

This time Papa looked more carefully. She opened the cabinet doors slowly. They creaked as they opened. Inside were pretty dishes and glasses.

No doll there either.

Papa looked behind the long curtains. They felt smooth and silky. She checked near the window sill. A warm breeze tickled her face.

Then Papa had a new idea. "What if my doll fell down?" she wondered.

She looked more carefully under the big table. Way in the back corner, something pink caught her eye.

"There you are!" Papa called out happily.

She reached her arm way back. Her fingers touched something soft. It was her doll's pink dress!

Papa pulled gently. Out came her beautiful doll. The yellow hair was a little messy. But the doll was perfectly fine.

Papa hugged her doll tight. The doll smelled like lavender soap. Papa spun around the dining room with joy.

"I found you, pretty doll!" Papa laughed. Her voice echoed in the big room. "I was so worried about you."

Papa sat back down at the table. She placed her doll carefully on the chair next to her. The doll's blue eyes seemed to sparkle.

"I almost gave up looking," Papa told her doll. "But I tried again. That's how I found you."

Papa finished her cereal happily. Every few bites, she smiled at her doll. The morning sun made everything warm and bright.

"Next time I will be more careful," Papa promised. "I will always put you in a safe place."

The doll seemed to nod in agreement. Papa giggled at the silly thought.

After breakfast, Papa brushed her doll's yellow hair. She straightened the pretty pink dress. Everything looked perfect again.

Papa carried her doll to the living room. They would play together all morning. Maybe they would have a tea party. Or read books together.

"I'm so glad I didn't give up," Papa said to herself. She felt proud and happy inside.

The dining room was quiet now. But it felt like a happy place. This was where Papa learned something important.

When things seem hard, try again. Look more carefully. Think of new ideas. Good things happen when you don't give up.

Papa and her pretty doll played all day long. They were the very best of friends. And Papa never forgot the lesson she learned that morning.

Sometimes the best treasures are hiding close by. You just need to keep looking with your heart full of hope.

The end.
