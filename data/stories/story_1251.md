# Story story_1251

**Duck and Owl's Bathroom Adventure**

Little Duck loved bath time. Every evening, she waddled into the big white bathroom. The warm water felt so good on her yellow feathers.

Duck had many bath toys. She had a red boat. She had blue bubbles. She had a squeaky fish too. But Duck played all alone. Sometimes she felt lonely.

"Splash, splash," went Duck in the tub. The water made tiny waves. Duck pushed her boat around the bubbles. But playing alone wasn't very fun.

One evening, Owl peeked into the bathroom. His big round eyes looked curious. Owl had soft brown feathers. His head could turn all around.

"What are you doing, Duck?" asked Owl softly.

Duck looked up. Water dripped from her orange beak. "I'm taking a bath," she said. "But it's not much fun alone."

Owl hopped closer to the tub. His talons clicked on the tile floor. "Can I play too?" he asked hopefully.

Duck's eyes lit up. "Yes! Come in the water!"

But Owl looked worried. "I don't like getting wet," he said. "My feathers get too heavy."

Duck thought for a moment. The warm water felt so nice. How could she share bath time with Owl?

"I have an idea!" Duck said excitedly. She splashed gently with her wing. "You can sit on the edge!"

Owl's eyes grew even bigger. "Really? I can help you play?"

"Of course!" Duck quacked happily.

Owl carefully climbed onto the bathtub edge. His claws gripped the smooth rim. He felt wobbly at first. But Duck smiled at him warmly.

"Here's my boat," Duck said. She pushed the red boat toward Owl. "You can be the captain!"

Owl used his wing tip carefully. He pushed the boat back to Duck. The boat sailed through the soapy bubbles. It made a tiny wake in the water.

"This is fun!" hooted Owl softly.

Duck giggled. "Watch this!" She dove under the bubbles. Only her tail feathers showed above water. Owl laughed at the silly sight.

When Duck popped up, bubbles sat on her head. She looked like she wore a white hat. Owl hooted with joy.

"Your turn to be silly!" Duck said.

Owl puffed out his chest feathers. He made his eyes extra big. He turned his head upside down. Duck laughed so hard she snorted.

The two friends played together happily. Owl pushed the boat in circles. Duck made bubble mountains. They took turns with the squeaky fish.

"Squeak, squeak!" went the fish.

"Hoot, hoot!" went Owl.

"Quack, quack!" went Duck.

The bathroom filled with happy sounds. Steam made everything warm and cozy. The mirror got foggy from their giggles.

Soon Duck's feathers were clean and fluffy. The water started getting cooler. Bath time was almost over.

"I don't want you to go," Duck said sadly.

Owl's heart felt warm. "I don't want to leave either," he said. "You're my best friend now."

Duck's orange beak smiled wide. "Best friends forever?"

"Best friends forever," Owl promised.

Duck climbed out of the tub. She shook her feathers dry. Owl helped by flapping his wings gently. The air felt nice and warm.

"Can we play again tomorrow?" Duck asked hopefully.

"Every day!" Owl said happily. "Bath time is much better with friends."

Duck wrapped herself in a soft yellow towel. Owl preened his brown feathers. They both felt happy and clean.

From that day on, Duck and Owl shared bath time. Duck splashed in the warm water. Owl sat safely on the edge. They played with boats and bubbles together.

The bathroom became their special place. A place where two different friends could play happily. Duck learned that sharing made everything more fun. Owl learned that being different was okay.

Every evening, you could hear them laughing. "Quack, quack!" and "Hoot, hoot!" echoed off the bathroom walls.

Duck was never lonely again. Owl had found his first best friend. Together, they made bath time the best time of day.

And they lived happily ever after, splashing and hooting with joy.

The end.
