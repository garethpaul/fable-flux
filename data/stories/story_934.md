# Story story_934

**Moo and the Messy Sandbox**

Moo the cow loved to play. She had big brown eyes and soft white spots. Every morning, she ran to her favorite sandbox.

The sandbox sat under a shady apple tree. It had yellow sand that felt warm and soft. Moo kept all her toys there. She had red buckets, blue shovels, and green trucks.

Today, Moo wanted to build a castle. She dug her hooves into the sand. It felt cool and smooth. She filled her buckets with sand. She dumped them out to make towers.

"This is fun!" Moo said happily.

She used her shovels to dig roads. She pushed her trucks around the castle. The sand made soft whooshing sounds. Soon, toys covered the whole sandbox.

When the sun got hot, Moo felt tired. She wanted to go inside for lunch. But then she saw the big mess. Toys were everywhere! Sand stuck to the buckets. The shovels lay scattered about.

"Oh no," Moo sighed. "I should clean up."

But Moo did not want to clean. It looked like too much work. The mess seemed very big. She felt worried and sad.

"Maybe I can just leave it," she thought.

Moo started to walk away. But then she stopped. Her mama always said to clean up toys. Other animals might want to play here too.

Moo looked at the messy sandbox again. She had an idea!

"I'll clean up super fast!" she said.

Moo rushed around the sandbox. She grabbed toys with her hooves. She tried to pick up three buckets at once. But they were too heavy! The buckets fell down with loud clanks. Sand spilled everywhere.

"Oops!" Moo said. Now the mess was even bigger!

She tried to scoop sand with her hooves. But the sand just slipped through. It scattered all over the ground. Moo felt frustrated.

"This isn't working," she moaned.

Moo sat down in the sand. She felt like giving up. The mess looked worse than before. Her hooves were covered in sand.

Then Moo remembered something. Her friend Pip the pig always cleaned up slowly. Pip said going slow helped her do better work.

"Maybe I should try that," Moo thought.

This time, Moo moved slowly and carefully. She picked up one toy at a time. First, she gathered all the red buckets. She brushed the sand off each one. The sand felt gritty under her hooves.

Next, she collected the blue shovels. She shook each one clean. The sand made tiny tinkling sounds as it fell.

Then she found all the green trucks. She wiped them with her soft tail. They looked shiny and new again.

Moo made neat piles of each toy. Red buckets in one corner. Blue shovels in another. Green trucks lined up in a row.

Finally, she smoothed the sand with her hooves. She made it flat and even. The sandbox looked perfect again!

"I did it!" Moo cheered.

Just then, her friends arrived. Pip the pig came running over. Ben the bunny hopped behind her.

"Wow!" said Pip. "Your sandbox looks amazing!"

"Can we play with you?" asked Ben.

"Of course!" Moo smiled big. She felt proud and happy.

The three friends played together all afternoon. They built castles and roads. They shared all the clean toys. The sand felt wonderful between their toes.

When it was time to go home, they cleaned up together. It was much easier with friends helping!

That night, Moo felt very proud. She had learned something important. Big jobs seemed scary at first. But taking them slowly made them easier.

"Tomorrow I'll clean up right away," Moo promised herself.

She fell asleep dreaming of playing in her clean sandbox. In her dreams, the sand sparkled like tiny stars. And all her toys were lined up perfectly, waiting for another day of fun.

From that day on, Moo always cleaned up her toys. She learned that taking care of things made playing even more special.

The end.
