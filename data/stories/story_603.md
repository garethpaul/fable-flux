# Story story_603

**Bessie and Shelly's Big Day**

Bessie the cow stood in the sunny pasture. Her black and white spots looked pretty in the morning light. But Bessie felt sad and lonely.

"Moo," she said softly. "I wish I had a friend to play with."

Bessie kicked at the soft grass with her hoof. She walked around the big oak tree. She sniffed the sweet clover flowers. Nothing made her feel better.

Near the old wooden fence lived Shelly the turtle. Shelly had a shiny green shell with yellow stripes. She loved watching Bessie from her cozy spot.

"That cow looks lonely," Shelly thought. "Maybe she wants to be friends."

Shelly took a deep breath. She slowly walked toward Bessie. Her little feet made soft sounds on the dirt.

"Hello," said Shelly in a quiet voice.

Bessie looked down. She saw the small turtle with kind eyes.

"Oh! Hello there," said Bessie. "I'm Bessie."

"I'm Shelly," said the turtle. "You look sad. Are you okay?"

Bessie's tail swished back and forth. "I'm lonely. I don't have anyone to play with."

Shelly smiled. "I don't have friends either. Want to play together?"

Bessie's eyes grew bright. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Yes!" said Shelly. "What games do you like?"

Bessie thought for a moment. "I like to run in the grass. But you're very small."

Shelly nodded. "I move slowly. But I have good ideas!"

"What kind of ideas?" asked Bessie.

Shelly pointed to some colorful flowers. "We could make pretty patterns with those."

Bessie bent down to smell the flowers. They smelled like honey and sunshine.

"That sounds fun," said Bessie. "But how?"

Shelly showed Bessie how to arrange the flowers. They made circles and lines. The red and yellow flowers looked beautiful together.

"This is nice," said Bessie. "What else can we do?"

Shelly looked around the farm. She saw a small pond with cool water.

"Let's go to the water," said Shelly.

They walked to the pond together. Bessie took big steps. Shelly took tiny steps. But they stayed together.

At the pond, Bessie drank the fresh water. Shelly splashed gently with her feet.

"The water feels good," said Shelly.

"It tastes good too," said Bessie.

A butterfly landed on Bessie's nose. It had orange and black wings.

"Don't move," whispered Shelly. "It's beautiful."

Bessie stood very still. The butterfly stayed for a long moment. Then it flew away toward the apple trees.

"That was amazing," said Bessie.

"I see butterflies every day," said Shelly. "Now I can share them with you."

They played hide and seek next. Shelly hid under big leaves. Bessie hid behind the barn. They giggled when they found each other.

The sun climbed higher in the sky. It felt warm on their backs.

"I'm getting hungry," said Bessie.

"Me too," said Shelly.

Bessie ate sweet grass and clover. Shelly munched on tender lettuce leaves from the garden.

"This food tastes better with a friend," said Bessie.

After lunch, they rested under the oak tree. The shade felt cool and nice.

"Shelly," said Bessie. "Thank you for being my friend."

"Thank you for playing with me," said Shelly. "I was lonely too."

"Really?" asked Bessie.

"Yes. But now I'm happy," said Shelly.

They watched white clouds float across the blue sky. Some clouds looked like animals. Some looked like ice cream.

"Look," said Shelly. "That cloud looks like a heart."

Bessie looked up. The fluffy white cloud did look like a heart.

"Just like our friendship," said Bessie.

As the day ended, they made plans for tomorrow. They would explore the cornfield. They would count the chickens. They would watch the sunset together.

"Best friends forever?" asked Shelly.

"Best friends forever," said Bessie.

The two friends smiled at each other. They had found something special. They had found friendship on the farm.

From that day on, Bessie and Shelly were never lonely again.

The end.
