# Story story_820

**Grandpa's Lost Book**

Grandpa Joe loved the beach. Every morning, he walked on the warm sand. He carried his special red book everywhere. The book had stories about pirates and treasure.

Today felt different. Grandpa sat on his favorite blue chair. He reached into his beach bag. His fingers felt around and around. No book! His special red book was gone.

"Oh no!" said Grandpa Joe. His eyes looked everywhere. The book was not in his bag. It was not on his chair. Where could it be?

The sun felt hot on his face. The waves made splashing sounds. But Grandpa felt sad. He loved reading his pirate stories by the ocean.

Little Emma ran over. She had curly hair and a yellow swimsuit. "What's wrong, Grandpa?" she asked.

"I lost my special book," said Grandpa. "It has my favorite stories inside."

Emma's eyes grew big. "Let's find it together!"

Grandpa smiled a little. "Okay, Emma. Let's try."

First, they looked near the water. The wet sand felt cool under their feet. Seagulls flew overhead making loud calls. Small waves washed over their toes.

"Maybe the book fell here," said Emma. She picked up a piece of seaweed. "Nope, just slimy plants!"

They dug in the sand with their hands. The sand felt grainy and rough. They found three shells and a smooth rock. But no red book.

"This isn't working," said Grandpa. He felt tired and worried.

Emma patted his arm. "Don't give up! Let's think harder."

She closed her eyes tight. "Where did you go yesterday?"

Grandpa thought and thought. "I walked to the snack stand. Then I sat under the big umbrella. After that, I went to the bathroom."

"Let's check those places!" said Emma.

They walked to the snack stand first. The smell of french fries filled the air. Hot dogs sizzled on the grill. But the man behind the counter shook his head. No red book here.

Next, they checked under the big striped umbrella. A family sat there now. The mom was reading a magazine. The dad was building a sandcastle. Two kids were eating crackers.

"Excuse me," said Emma politely. "Did you see a red book?"

The mom smiled. "Sorry, sweetie. We just got here."

Grandpa's shoulders drooped down. "Maybe it's gone forever."

"One more place!" said Emma. She grabbed his hand. "The bathroom!"

They walked to the small beach building. It smelled like soap and salt water. Emma waited outside while Grandpa went in.

"EMMA!" Grandpa called loudly. "COME HERE!"

Emma ran inside. There on the sink counter sat the red book! It looked perfect and clean.

"You found it!" Emma jumped up and down. "How did it get there?"

Grandpa laughed. "I must have put it down when I washed my hands. I was thinking about lunch and forgot!"

He picked up the book carefully. The red cover felt smooth and familiar. He opened it and saw his favorite pirate picture.

"Thank you for helping me," said Grandpa. He gave Emma a big warm hug. "I wanted to give up. But you kept trying."

"That's what friends do," said Emma. "We help each other solve problems."

They walked back to the beach chairs together. The sun felt warm and happy now. The ocean sparkled like diamonds. Seagulls danced in the blue sky.

Grandpa opened his book. "Would you like to hear a pirate story?"

Emma sat close beside him. "Yes, please!"

Grandpa began to read. His voice was gentle and excited. The story was about a brave pirate who never gave up searching for treasure.

"Just like us!" said Emma. "We never gave up searching for your book!"

Grandpa smiled big. "You're right, Emma. Sometimes the best treasures are the ones we work hard to find."

The waves whispered softly. The warm breeze felt perfect. And Grandpa's special book was safe in his hands again.

The end.
