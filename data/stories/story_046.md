# Tiger's Attic Adventure

In a cozy house on Maple Street, a little orange tiger named Toby lived in the attic. The attic was his special place. Dusty sunbeams danced through the window. Old toys sat in wooden boxes. Toby loved his attic home.

But Toby had a big problem. He did not like vegetables. Not one bit!

Every day, Mama Tiger climbed the creaky stairs. She brought Toby his meals on a blue tray. "Time for lunch, Toby!" she called.

Toby looked at his plate. There were bright green peas. Orange carrots cut into circles. Yellow corn kernels that sparkled. But Toby turned his striped head away.

"Yuck!" said Toby. "I only want fish and milk!"

Mama Tiger sighed softly. "Vegetables help you grow big and strong," she said. "They give you energy to play."

But Toby pushed the vegetables around his plate. He made faces at the broccoli. He hid the carrots under his napkin.

Days passed. Toby felt tired during playtime. His tiger stripes seemed less bright. He couldn't climb the attic ladder very well.

One morning, Toby heard strange sounds. Scratch, scratch, scratch. Someone was in his attic!

Toby peeked around a dusty trunk. A little gray mouse was there! The mouse was eating something. Crunch, crunch, crunch.

"What are you eating?" asked Toby.

The mouse looked up. She had bright, sparkly eyes. Her gray fur was shiny and soft.

"I'm eating carrots!" said the mouse. "They make my eyes strong. I can see everything in the dark attic!"

Toby watched the mouse. She looked very healthy. Very happy.

"Do carrots really help?" asked Toby.

"Oh yes!" said the mouse. "And green peas give me energy. I can run so fast! Watch me!"

The little mouse zipped around the attic. She jumped over boxes. She climbed up ropes. She was amazing!

"Wow!" said Toby. "You're so fast!"

"It's the vegetables," said the mouse. "They're like magic food!"

Toby thought about this. Maybe vegetables weren't so bad. Maybe they were actually good.

"Will you eat lunch with me?" asked Toby.

"I would love to!" said the mouse.

When Mama Tiger brought lunch, Toby was ready. The mouse sat beside him on the wooden floor.

"Let's try one pea," said Toby. He picked up a small green pea. It was round and smooth. He put it in his mouth.

Pop! The pea was sweet and fresh.

"That's good!" said Toby, surprised.

Next, he tried a carrot circle. It was crunchy and orange. It tasted like sunshine.

"Mmm!" said Toby. "This is yummy too!"

The mouse cheered. "Try the corn!"

Toby ate three yellow kernels. They were sweet and juicy. They made little popping sounds when he chewed.

Soon, Toby had eaten all his vegetables. His tummy felt happy and full.

"How do you feel?" asked the mouse.

Toby stretched his paws. He felt different. Better! Energy flowed through his orange fur.

"I feel strong!" said Toby. He jumped up high. He did a little tiger dance.

Every day after that, Toby ate his vegetables. Green ones, orange ones, yellow ones, red ones too! The mouse became his best friend. They shared meals together in the sunny attic.

Toby's stripes grew brighter. His eyes sparkled like stars. He could climb the attic ladder easily now. He could play all day without getting tired.

"Thank you for teaching me," Toby told his mouse friend.

"Thank you for being brave," said the mouse. "Trying new things takes courage!"

Mama Tiger was so proud. "Look how strong you've grown!" she said.

Toby smiled his biggest tiger smile. He had learned something wonderful. Vegetables weren't scary at all. They were colorful, tasty, and magical!

Now Toby loved his vegetables. And he loved sharing them with friends. The attic was filled with happy crunching sounds every single day.

Sometimes the best surprises come in small, colorful packages!

The end.
