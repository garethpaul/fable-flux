# Story story_624

Lily sat in the sunny meadow. The grass felt soft under her legs. Yellow flowers danced in the warm breeze. But Lily felt sad and lonely.

She picked a daisy. "I wish I had a friend," she whispered. The white petals felt smooth between her fingers. Birds chirped high above her head.

Behind a big oak tree, someone was watching. A small brown bear peeked out. His fuzzy ears twitched with curiosity. The bear wanted to play too.

The bear took one tiny step forward. Then another step. His big paws made soft sounds on the grass. But he felt scared and shy.

Lily heard a small noise. She looked up from her flower. "Hello?" she called softly. "Is someone there?"

The bear's heart beat fast. He wanted to hide. But he also wanted a friend. He took a deep breath. Then he stepped out from behind the tree.

"Oh!" said Lily. Her eyes grew wide. "A bear!"

The bear sat down quickly. "Please don't run away," he said. His voice was gentle and kind. "I just want to play."

Lily looked at the bear's friendly brown eyes. He seemed nice and safe. "What's your name?" she asked.

"I'm Bruno," said the bear. "What's your name?"

"I'm Lily," she said with a small smile. "Do you really want to play with me?"

Bruno nodded his fuzzy head. "Yes! I've been watching you. You looked lonely. I felt lonely too."

Lily stood up and brushed grass off her dress. "What should we play?"

Bruno's eyes sparkled with joy. "Do you like to roll down hills?"

"I love rolling down hills!" Lily laughed. The sound made Bruno happy.

They climbed to the top of a grassy hill. "Ready?" asked Bruno. "One, two, three!"

Down they rolled together. The world spun around them. Green grass and blue sky mixed together. They laughed and giggled all the way down.

At the bottom, they lay on their backs. They looked up at fluffy white clouds. "That cloud looks like a butterfly," said Lily.

"And that one looks like honey," said Bruno. His tummy rumbled softly.

They played hide and seek next. Bruno hid behind the oak tree. But his big brown bottom stuck out. Lily found him right away. They both giggled.

When Lily hid behind a flower bush, Bruno pretended he couldn't see her. "Where could Lily be?" he wondered out loud. This made Lily laugh even more.

They picked flowers together. Lily made a crown of daisies. She put it on Bruno's head. "You look like a flower king," she said.

Bruno felt special and proud. He picked purple flowers for Lily. She tucked them behind her ears. "Now I'm a flower princess," she said.

The sun began to set. Orange and pink colors painted the sky. Their shadows grew long on the grass.

"I had the best day ever," said Lily. She hugged Bruno's soft, warm fur. He smelled like sunshine and honey.

"Me too," said Bruno. He hugged her back gently. "Will you be my friend forever?"

"Forever and always," promised Lily. "Best friends stick together."

They sat in the meadow as stars appeared. Fireflies began to twinkle around them. The air smelled sweet like flowers.

"Tomorrow we can play again," said Bruno.

"We can build a fort," said Lily.

"And catch butterflies," added Bruno.

"And have a picnic," said Lily.

They made plans until the moon rose high. Then Lily's mother called from far away. It was time to go home.

"See you tomorrow, Bruno," said Lily.

"See you tomorrow, best friend," said Bruno.

Lily skipped home through the meadow. Her heart felt full of happiness. She had found the best friend ever.

Bruno watched her go. Then he curled up under his oak tree. He dreamed of tomorrow's adventures.

The meadow was quiet now. But it was filled with the magic of new friendship. And tomorrow would bring more joy.

The end.
