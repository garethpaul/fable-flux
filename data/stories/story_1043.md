# Story story_1043

**Ruby's Reading Adventure**

The big clock on the wall said three o'clock. "Reading time!" called Miss Emma to all the children. Everyone sat on the soft, colorful carpet. They picked up their favorite books.

Ruby the robin watched from her perch by the window. She lived in the classroom with the children. Ruby loved many things. She loved singing songs. She loved playing with blocks. But reading time made her feel worried.

"I don't know how to read," Ruby chirped sadly. "Books have too many words. The letters look like tiny black squiggles."

Ruby fluffed her red feathers. She turned away from the children. Reading looked hard and scary.

"Come sit with us, Ruby," said Emma gently. "You can look at the pictures."

Ruby shook her little head. "Pictures aren't enough," she whispered. "I want to understand the words too."

Emma smiled warmly. She picked up a special book. The cover sparkled with gold letters. "This book is about birds," Emma said. "Would you like to try?"

Ruby felt curious but still nervous. She hopped closer. The book felt smooth under her tiny feet.

Emma opened to the first page. There was a picture of a red robin. It looked just like Ruby! "This bird's name is Robin," Emma read slowly. "She loves to sing beautiful songs."

Ruby's eyes grew wide. "That's me!" she chirped excitedly. "I love singing too!"

Emma pointed to each word as she read. "Robin... loves... to... sing." The words seemed to dance on the page.

Something magical began to happen. Ruby started to see shapes in the letters. The letter 'R' looked like a tiny tree branch. The letter 'O' looked like a perfect round nest.

"Can you find the letter 'R'?" asked Emma.

Ruby looked carefully. She spotted it right away! "There!" she chirped, pointing with her wing. "It's at the beginning of Robin!"

Emma clapped her hands. "You found it! You're reading!"

Ruby felt warm and happy inside. Her heart fluttered like butterfly wings. Reading wasn't scary anymore. It was like solving a fun puzzle!

They turned the page together. Ruby saw more words she could try. "Bird," she said proudly. "Tree. Nest. Sing."

The other children gathered around to watch. "Ruby's reading!" said Tommy with a big smile.

"She's so smart!" added Sarah, clapping her hands.

Ruby puffed out her chest with pride. Her red feathers gleamed in the afternoon sunlight. Reading felt wonderful now.

Emma showed Ruby how letters made sounds. 'B' made a buzzing sound like a bee. 'S' made a hissing sound like wind through leaves. Ruby practiced each sound carefully.

"Every letter has its own special voice," Emma explained. "Just like every bird has its own special song."

Ruby understood perfectly. Letters were like musical notes. Words were like songs. Stories were like beautiful concerts!

They read about robin families. They read about building nests. They read about flying south for winter. Each page brought new discoveries.

Ruby's fear melted away completely. Reading became as natural as singing her morning songs.

When reading time ended, Ruby felt sad. "Can we read more tomorrow?" she asked hopefully.

"Of course!" said Emma. "We'll read together every day."

Ruby hopped excitedly on her perch. She couldn't wait for tomorrow's reading time. She had so many books to explore!

That night, Ruby dreamed about letters and words. She dreamed about flying through storybooks. She dreamed about all the adventures waiting in books.

The next morning, Ruby woke up singing. But this time, she sang the alphabet song. "A, B, C, D, E, F, G!" she chirped happily.

When three o'clock came, Ruby was ready. She hopped right to the reading carpet. She picked out her own book about birds.

"I love reading time!" Ruby announced to everyone.

Reading had become Ruby's favorite time of day. She learned that trying new things could be scary at first. But with patience and help from friends, scary things could become wonderful adventures.

From that day on, Ruby never missed reading time. She had discovered the magic of stories.

The end.
