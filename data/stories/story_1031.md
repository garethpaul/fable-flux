# Story story_1031

Anna's pajamas felt soft and cozy. They had tiny pink stars all over them. Mama smiled at Anna from the doorway.

"Time for bed, sweet pea," Mama said gently.

Anna looked at her toys scattered on the floor. Her teddy bear sat by the toy box. Her coloring books lay open with bright crayons nearby.

"But I'm not sleepy yet," Anna said. She picked up her favorite doll. "Lucy and I want to play more."

Mama walked over and sat on Anna's bed. The mattress bounced a little. "I know you're having fun," she said. "But your body needs rest to grow big and strong."

Anna dragged her feet to the bathroom. The toothbrush felt scratchy on her teeth. The minty toothpaste tasted cool and fresh. She made funny faces in the mirror while brushing.

Back in her room, Anna climbed into bed slowly. The sheets felt cold at first. She pulled them up to her chin and frowned.

"I don't want to sleep," Anna whispered to Lucy. "I want to stay awake forever."

Mama tucked the blankets around Anna's shoulders. "Would you like me to read a story?" she asked.

"Okay," Anna said with a small sigh.

Mama picked up Anna's favorite book. It had a shiny blue cover with a smiling moon. As Mama opened the book, something magical happened.

A soft golden light began to glow from the pages. The light danced and swirled around the room. Anna's eyes grew wide with wonder.

"Look, Mama!" Anna gasped. "The story is coming alive!"

Tiny sparkling stars floated out of the book. They twinkled like real stars in the night sky. The stars danced around Anna's ceiling in gentle circles.

A friendly moon face appeared above Anna's bed. It had kind eyes and a warm smile. "Hello, Anna," the moon said in a voice like honey. "I'm here to help you have sweet dreams."

Anna giggled with delight. "You're so pretty, Mr. Moon!"

The moon winked at her. "Thank you, little one. Would you like to see where dreams come from?"

Anna nodded eagerly. The sparkling stars began to tell stories with their light. They showed pictures of Anna playing in sunny meadows. They painted images of her riding on fluffy white clouds.

"Dreams are like magical movies," the moon explained softly. "They only play when you close your eyes and rest."

Anna watched the beautiful light show above her bed. The stars smelled like vanilla cookies. Their glow felt warm and cozy on her face.

"This is amazing," Anna whispered. She snuggled deeper into her pillows.

The moon sang a gentle lullaby. His voice was soft like a summer breeze. The stars swayed back and forth to the music.

Anna's eyelids began to feel heavy. But now she wasn't worried about sleeping. She was excited to see what dreams would come.

"Will you visit me again tomorrow night?" Anna asked the moon.

"Every single night," the moon promised. "Dreams are waiting for you whenever you're ready to rest."

The stars slowly faded back into the storybook. The golden light grew dimmer and dimmer. Soon everything looked normal again.

But Anna still felt the magic in her heart. She hugged Lucy tight and smiled at Mama.

"I think I'm ready to sleep now," Anna said. "I want to see my dreams."

Mama kissed Anna's forehead. "Sweet dreams, my brave girl," she whispered.

Anna closed her eyes and took a deep breath. Her room smelled like lavender and love. The soft sheets felt like a warm hug around her body.

As she drifted off to sleep, Anna smiled. She couldn't wait to see what magical dreams were waiting for her.

Tomorrow night, she would brush her teeth extra fast. She would put on her pajamas quickly. She would climb into bed with excitement.

Because now Anna knew that bedtime wasn't the end of fun. It was the beginning of the most wonderful adventures of all.

Anna fell asleep with a happy heart, ready for tomorrow's magic.

The end.
