# Story story_1185

Ellie the elephant stood in the big kitchen. She felt very lonely today. The kitchen was quiet and empty.

Ellie picked up a wooden spoon. She stirred the air like making soup. "Stir, stir, stir," she said softly.

But playing alone was not fun. Ellie's trunk drooped down sadly. She wished she had a friend to play with.

Ben peeked around the corner. He was a little boy with curly hair. Ben watched Ellie play by herself.

"That looks lonely," Ben thought. He wanted to help Ellie feel better.

Ben stepped into the kitchen. His bare feet made soft sounds on the cool floor. "Hi, Ellie," he said in a gentle voice.

Ellie looked up. Her big brown eyes sparkled with hope. "Hi, Ben," she said. "Do you want to play?"

Ben smiled wide. "Yes! What are we making?"

"Pretend soup!" Ellie said happily. Her trunk swayed back and forth.

Ben climbed onto a sturdy chair. Now he could reach the counter too. The wooden spoon felt smooth in his small hands.

"What goes in our soup?" Ben asked.

Ellie thought hard. "Carrots!" she said. "I love crunchy carrots."

Ben pretended to chop carrots. "Chop, chop, chop!" The imaginary pieces fell into their pretend pot.

"Now we need something sweet," Ben said.

"Apples!" Ellie trumpeted softly. She pretended to wash red apples in the sink.

The water made splashing sounds. Ben giggled at the fun noise. Ellie's trunk was perfect for washing fruit.

"Splash, splash!" Ben said. He helped rinse the pretend apples.

They worked together happily. Ben stirred while Ellie added ingredients. The kitchen felt warm and cozy now.

"Our soup needs something special," Ellie said.

Ben's eyes lit up. "Magic sprinkles!"

They both reached high into the air. Their fingers grabbed invisible sprinkles. Down, down into the pot the magic went.

"Now we stir together," Ben said.

Ben held one end of the spoon. Ellie held the other end with her trunk. Round and round they stirred.

"It smells so good!" Ellie said. She sniffed with her long trunk.

Ben sniffed too. "Like sunshine and flowers!"

The pretend soup bubbled and popped. They both made silly bubble sounds. "Pop! Bubble! Pop!"

"I think it's ready," Ben announced.

They each got a pretend bowl. The soup looked golden and warm. Steam rose up in curly wisps.

"Careful, it's hot," Ellie warned.

They blew on their soup together. "Whoosh!" Their breath made cooling sounds.

Ben took a pretend sip. "Mmm! This is the best soup ever!"

Ellie sipped too. "It tastes like friendship," she said happily.

Ben looked at Ellie's kind face. "We make a good team," he said.

"The best team!" Ellie agreed. Her trunk reached over and gently patted Ben's shoulder.

They sat together at the small kitchen table. The afternoon sun shone through the window. Everything felt perfect and warm.

"Can we cook together tomorrow?" Ben asked.

"Every day!" Ellie said. "We can make pretend pancakes next time."

Ben clapped his hands. "With pretend syrup!"

"And pretend berries!" Ellie added.

They both laughed together. The kitchen filled with happy sounds. Their friendship was just beginning.

From that day on, Ben and Ellie cooked together often. They made pretend pizza and imaginary cookies. The kitchen became their special place.

Sometimes they made real snacks too. Ben would wash fruit while Ellie helped carry things. They always shared everything they made.

The lonely feeling was gone forever. Ben and Ellie had found the best ingredient of all. They had found friendship.

Every cooking adventure was better with a friend. The kitchen was never quiet again. It was full of laughter, joy, and love.

Ben and Ellie learned that playing together was much more fun than playing alone. Their friendship made everything taste sweeter.

And they lived happily ever after, cooking up fun every single day.

The end.
