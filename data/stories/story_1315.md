# Story story_1315

**Gigi the Tall Giraffe**

Gigi was a very tall giraffe. She lived in a green forest. Her neck reached high into the trees. She could see far, far away.

Every morning, Gigi found the sweetest leaves. They grew at the very top. The other animals looked up sadly. They were too short to reach them.

"Please share some leaves," said Benny the bunny. His tummy rumbled loudly.

"These are mine," said Gigi. She munched the crispy leaves. They tasted like honey and sunshine.

Rosie the raccoon climbed up high. But she still couldn't reach. "Could I have just one leaf?" she asked.

"No," said Gigi. "I found them first."

Soon, all the animals walked away. They looked for food somewhere else. Gigi felt happy to have all the leaves.

But something felt wrong. The forest seemed very quiet. Gigi's heart felt heavy and cold.

She ate lunch all by herself. The leaves didn't taste as sweet. She ate dinner alone too. The forest felt empty and sad.

"Why doesn't anyone play with me?" Gigi wondered aloud.

The next morning, Gigi had an idea. She would give each animal one tiny leaf. That would make them happy. Then they would be her friends again.

Gigi picked the smallest leaves she could find. She gave them to Benny bunny. The leaves were brown and dry.

"Thank you," said Benny quietly. But he didn't smile. The leaves tasted bitter and old.

Gigi gave tiny leaves to Rosie raccoon too. Rosie nibbled them politely. But they were too small for her hungry tummy.

"Why aren't you happy?" asked Gigi. She felt confused and worried.

"These leaves are not very good," said Benny softly. "And they're so tiny."

Gigi's feelings hurt. She tried to be nice. But it didn't work. She sat under her favorite tree. Big tears rolled down her long neck.

Oliver the wise owl hooted from above. "What's wrong, Gigi?"

"I shared my leaves," sniffled Gigi. "But nobody wants to be my friend."

Oliver's yellow eyes twinkled kindly. "Sharing means giving your best things. Not your worst things."

Gigi thought about this. She wiped her tears away. "You mean the good leaves?"

"Yes," said Oliver. "Real friends share the sweet, juicy leaves. Not the old, dry ones."

The next morning, Gigi climbed high again. She picked the most beautiful leaves. They were bright green and fresh. They smelled like morning dew.

She found Benny by the stream. "Would you like some sweet leaves?" asked Gigi. She offered him the very best ones.

Benny's eyes grew wide. "These look wonderful! Thank you, Gigi!"

The leaves were crispy and delicious. Benny's tummy felt full and happy.

Gigi shared good leaves with Rosie too. And with Max the mouse. And Sara the squirrel. Everyone got the sweet, tasty ones.

"These are amazing!" said Rosie. She hugged Gigi's long leg.

"You're so kind," said Max, his tiny voice squeaking with joy.

Soon all the animals gathered around Gigi. They laughed and played together. They told funny stories. They sang happy songs.

Gigi's heart felt warm and full. Sharing the good leaves made her feel wonderful. Much better than eating alone.

"Thank you for sharing," said all her friends.

From that day on, Gigi always shared her best leaves. She learned that giving good things to friends made everything better. The leaves even tasted sweeter when everyone ate together.

Every morning, the forest filled with laughter. Gigi was never lonely again. She had learned the best secret of all. Sharing good things with others made her heart happy too.

And whenever new animals came to the forest, Gigi always offered them the sweetest leaves from the very top of the trees.

The end.
