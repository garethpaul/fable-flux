# Story story_1045

**Bella's Lost Doll**

Bella the sheep loved her dining room. The big wooden table gleamed in the sunlight. Pretty flowers sat in a blue vase. Everything looked perfect and tidy.

But today, something was very wrong.

"Where is Rosie?" Bella cried. She looked around the dining room with worried eyes. Rosie was her favorite doll. The doll had soft yellow hair and a pink dress. Bella took Rosie everywhere.

Yesterday, Bella and Rosie had tea parties at the big table. They pretended to eat cookies and drink milk. Rosie sat in the chair next to Bella. They giggled and talked about fun things.

Now Rosie was gone.

Bella's fluffy white wool felt all prickly with worry. Her heart beat fast like a drum. "Rosie! Where are you?" she called out loudly.

First, Bella looked under the table. She got down on her knees. The floor felt cold and smooth. She crawled around, pushing chairs aside. Dust tickled her nose. She found a red crayon and a button. But no Rosie.

"Maybe she fell behind the big cabinet," Bella thought. She squeezed herself into the narrow space. The wall felt rough against her wool. Spider webs stuck to her ears. She pushed boxes around. They made scraping sounds on the floor.

Still no Rosie.

Bella sat down and felt very sad. Tears made her eyes feel hot and watery. "I'll never find her," she whispered. Her voice sounded small and shaky.

But then Bella remembered something important. Mama always said, "When you feel stuck, try thinking differently."

Bella wiped her eyes with her hoof. She took three deep breaths. The air smelled like the lavender flowers on the table. "Think, Bella, think," she said to herself.

She closed her eyes tight. She remembered yesterday's tea party. Rosie sat in the chair. They pretended to eat. Then they cleaned up together.

"Wait!" Bella's eyes popped open wide. "We put the pretend dishes away!"

Bella hurried to the wooden hutch. This was where Mama kept the real dishes. The hutch had many shelves and small doors. Bella opened the bottom door carefully.

Inside, behind a stack of blue plates, something pink caught her eye.

"Rosie!" Bella shouted with joy.

There was her precious doll, sitting quietly behind the dishes. Rosie's yellow hair looked a little messy. Her pink dress had a tiny wrinkle. But she was safe and sound.

Bella hugged Rosie tight against her fluffy chest. The doll felt soft and familiar. She smelled like the lavender soap Bella used for bath time.

"I was so worried about you," Bella whispered to Rosie. "I looked everywhere!"

Bella remembered now. Yesterday, she had pretended Rosie was helping put dishes away. She must have left Rosie in the hutch by mistake.

"I'm sorry I forgot where I put you," Bella said gently. She smoothed Rosie's yellow hair with her hoof.

Bella carried Rosie to the dining room table. Sunlight made everything look warm and golden. She sat Rosie in her favorite chair.

"Let's have another tea party," Bella said happily. "But this time, I'll remember where everything goes."

Bella set out pretend cups and plates. She and Rosie had a wonderful tea party. They talked about their adventure.

"I learned something today, Rosie," Bella said. "When I feel worried, I should stop and think carefully. And I should never give up looking for what's important to me."

Rosie seemed to smile in the warm sunlight.

From that day on, Bella always remembered to put things back where they belonged. She kept Rosie close by her side. And whenever she lost something, she would take deep breaths and think carefully about where it might be.

The dining room stayed beautiful and tidy. And Bella and Rosie had many more happy tea parties together, making wonderful memories that would last forever.

The end.
