# Story story_1303

**The Little Frog Who Wouldn't Eat His Greens**

Little Benny was a bright green frog. He lived in a sparkly blue pond. The pond had lily pads everywhere. Water bugs danced on the surface.

Benny's mama made yummy meals every day. She picked fresh water plants. She found crunchy algae. She gathered sweet pond weeds.

"Dinner time!" called Mama Frog.

Benny hopped to the dinner rock. His plate was full of green things. There were slimy water plants. There was stringy algae. There were mushy pond weeds.

"Yuck!" said Benny. "I don't want vegetables!"

He pushed his plate away. The green food splashed into the water.

"But Benny," said Mama. "Vegetables help you grow big and strong."

Benny shook his little head. "I only want flies and bugs!"

Day after day, Benny ate only insects. He caught buzzing flies. He snapped up water beetles. He munched on mosquitoes.

Soon, Benny felt very tired. His bright green skin looked pale. His strong legs felt wobbly. He couldn't hop very far.

"What's wrong with me?" Benny asked his big brother Freddy.

Freddy was a big, healthy frog. His skin was bright and shiny. His legs were strong and powerful.

"You need to eat vegetables," said Freddy. "They give you energy. They make your skin glow. They help your muscles grow."

Benny frowned. "But vegetables taste yucky!"

Freddy thought hard. He wanted to help his little brother.

"Let's try something fun," said Freddy. "Close your eyes tight."

Benny closed his eyes. Freddy brought a small piece of sweet water lily. He held it near Benny's nose.

"What do you smell?" asked Freddy.

Benny sniffed. "It smells like... sunshine and rain!"

"Now take a tiny bite," said Freddy.

Benny opened his mouth. He took the smallest bite ever. His face scrunched up.

"Still yucky!" he said. He spit it out.

Freddy didn't give up. He had another idea.

"Let's play restaurant!" said Freddy. "You can be the chef!"

Freddy gathered different pond plants. He found purple water flowers. He picked yellow algae strands. He collected tiny green water berries.

"Chef Benny," said Freddy. "Can you make me a beautiful salad?"

Benny's eyes lit up. He loved playing games!

Benny arranged the plants on a big lily pad. He made pretty patterns with the colors. Purple flowers made a circle. Yellow algae made squiggly lines. Green berries dotted the edges.

"This looks amazing!" said Freddy. "A real chef always tastes his food."

Benny felt proud of his creation. It did look pretty. The colors were so bright and cheerful.

He picked up one tiny green berry. It was round and smooth. He popped it in his mouth.

"Hey!" said Benny. "This tastes sweet!"

He tried a purple flower petal. It tasted like honey. He nibbled some yellow algae. It was crunchy and fresh.

"These aren't yucky at all!" said Benny.

From that day on, Benny helped make dinner. He arranged colorful vegetables on lily pads. He made rainbow salads. He created veggie art.

Soon, Benny's skin was bright green again. His legs grew strong and powerful. He could hop all around the pond.

"Thank you, Freddy," said Benny. "You helped me discover something wonderful."

"What's that?" asked Freddy.

"Vegetables can be fun and tasty!" said Benny. "And trying new things isn't scary when someone helps you."

Mama Frog smiled as she watched her boys. They were making dinner together. They were laughing and playing. The pond sparkled in the sunset.

Benny learned that sometimes good things come in packages that look different. With creativity and help from family, even challenging things can become enjoyable. The little frog never went hungry again.

Every evening, the pond filled with happy croaking sounds. Two brothers shared delicious, colorful meals. And Benny always remembered to try new things with an open heart.

The end.
