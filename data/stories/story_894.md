# Story story_894

**Bessie and the Tea Party**

Bessie the cow sat alone in the big dining room. The wooden floor felt cool under her hooves. She had set up her favorite tea party with tiny cups and saucers.

"One cup for me," Bessie said softly. "One cup for Teddy Bear."

She poured pretend tea into the little pink cups. The afternoon sun streamed through the tall windows. It made golden patches on the floor.

Bessie sighed. "I wish someone real would join us, Teddy."

Her sister Mila peeked around the corner. Mila had curly brown hair and bright eyes. She watched Bessie talking to her stuffed bear.

"Can I play too?" Mila asked quietly.

Bessie looked up. Her big brown eyes sparkled with hope. "Really? You want to have tea with us?"

"Yes, please!" Mila clapped her hands together.

Bessie's tail swished with excitement. "Sit right here!" She patted the soft rug with her hoof.

Mila sat down carefully. The rug felt fuzzy and warm. Bessie poured pretend tea into another tiny cup.

"This is Earl Grey tea," Bessie announced proudly. "It's very fancy."

Mila lifted the cup to her lips. She made a loud sipping sound. "Mmm! It tastes like sunshine and flowers."

Bessie giggled. The sound filled the quiet dining room. "Would you like a cookie, Miss Mila?"

"Oh yes, please!" Mila pretended to take a bite. "These are the best cookies ever!"

The sisters played for a long time. They served tea to all the stuffed animals. They talked about their day in silly, fancy voices.

"More tea, Lady Bessie?" Mila asked in her fanciest voice.

"Why certainly, Princess Mila!" Bessie replied with a curtsy.

They laughed until their bellies hurt. The dining room felt warm and cozy now. It wasn't lonely anymore.

Suddenly, Bessie had an idea. "Let's make real cookies for our tea party!"

"Can we really?" Mila's eyes grew wide.

"Mama keeps cookie mix in the kitchen," Bessie said. "We can ask her to help."

The sisters ran to find their mama. Their footsteps echoed in the hallway. Mama smiled when she heard their plan.

"What a wonderful idea!" Mama said. "Let's bake together."

Soon the kitchen smelled like vanilla and sugar. The warm oven made the whole house cozy. Bessie and Mila took turns stirring the dough.

"These will be perfect for our tea party," Mila said.

"The best cookies ever!" Bessie agreed.

When the cookies finished baking, they were golden brown. They smelled like a warm hug. The sisters carried them carefully to the dining room.

They set the real cookies on their tiny plates. Steam rose from the warm treats. The sweet smell filled the air.

"Now our tea party is perfect," Bessie said happily.

Mila nodded. "Playing together is so much more fun!"

They shared the warm cookies with their stuffed animals. Crumbs scattered on the soft rug. The pretend tea tasted even better with real cookies.

"Bessie?" Mila said softly.


"Yes, Mila?"

"Can we have tea parties every day?"

Bessie's heart felt full and happy. "Every single day!" she promised.

The sisters hugged tight. Their tea party had become something magical. The dining room felt like the coziest place in the world.

As the sun began to set, golden light danced on the walls. The sisters cleaned up their tea party together. They put each cup away carefully.

"Thank you for playing with me," Bessie whispered.

"Thank you for sharing your tea party," Mila whispered back.

From that day on, the dining room was never lonely. Every afternoon, the sisters met for their special tea party. They shared stories, cookies, and lots of giggles.

Their mama would peek in and smile. The sound of their laughter was the sweetest music in the house.

Bessie learned that sharing made everything more fun. Mila learned that sisters make the very best friends.

And their tea parties? They were absolutely perfect.

The end.
