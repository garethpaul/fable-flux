# Story story_867

**The Little Plane and the Friendly Fox**

In a big green field, a small red plane sat alone. The plane's name was Pip. He had shiny wings and a bright yellow propeller. Pip loved to zoom around the field. But today, he felt sad.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," Pip said softly. His propeller spun slowly in the warm breeze. The sun made his red paint sparkle. But sparkles couldn't make him happy.

Pip started his engine. Vroom, vroom! He rolled across the bumpy grass. He lifted into the air. Up, up, up he went! The wind tickled his wings. The clouds looked like fluffy cotton balls.

But flying alone wasn't fun anymore. Pip landed with a gentle thump. He sighed a big, whooshy sigh.

Behind a tall oak tree, someone was watching. It was Ruby, a small orange fox. She had pointy ears and a fluffy tail. Ruby loved to run and play. She watched Pip fly every day.

"That plane looks lonely," Ruby thought. Her heart felt warm and fuzzy. "Maybe I can be his friend."

Ruby stepped out from behind the tree. Her paws made soft sounds on the grass. Pip saw her coming. His propeller stopped spinning.

"Hello," said Ruby in her sweetest voice. "I'm Ruby. What's your name?"

"I'm Pip," the plane replied quietly. "Are you going to chase me away?"

Ruby's ears drooped. "Oh no! I want to play with you. Flying looks so wonderful!"

Pip's engine made a happy purring sound. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Yes!" Ruby's tail wagged fast. "Can you teach me about flying?"

Pip felt excited for the first time all day. "I can't teach you to fly. But we can play flying games!"

Ruby's eyes sparkled like morning dew. "What kind of games?"

"Follow me!" Pip started rolling across the field. His wheels made tracks in the soft dirt. Ruby ran beside him. Her paws patted the ground in rhythm.

"This is like flying on the ground!" Ruby laughed. The sound was like tiny silver bells.

Pip's propeller spun faster. "Now watch this!" He zoomed in a big circle. Ruby ran in the circle too. Around and around they went!

"Wheee!" Ruby called out. "This is amazing!"

They played airplane tag next. Pip would fly low over Ruby's head. Ruby would jump up and try to touch his wheels. When she touched them, it was her turn to be "it."

"Got you!" Ruby giggled when she tagged Pip's landing gear.

The warm sun made everything golden. Butterflies danced around them. Bees hummed happy songs. The sweet smell of wildflowers filled the air.

"Let's play follow the leader," suggested Ruby. She ran in zigzag patterns. Pip flew above her, copying every turn. Left, right, loop-de-loop!

"You're a great pilot, Ruby!" Pip called down.

"You're a wonderful teacher, Pip!" Ruby called back.

As the sun got lower, they found a shady spot. Pip parked under the oak tree. Ruby curled up next to his wing. They were both tired but happy.

"I had the best day ever," Ruby said. Her fur was warm from running.

"Me too," Pip replied. "I'm so glad you wanted to be my friend."

Ruby's whiskers twitched with joy. "Can we play again tomorrow?"

"Every day!" Pip promised. His engine made a contented humming sound.

The evening stars began to twinkle. Fireflies blinked like tiny lanterns. The field smelled like fresh grass and summer flowers.

"Thank you for playing with me," Pip whispered.

"Thank you for teaching me about flying," Ruby whispered back.

From that day on, Pip and Ruby played together every single day. The little red plane was never lonely again. The orange fox had found her best friend.

And in their special field, you could always hear the sounds of joy. A propeller spinning happily. Little paws patting the ground. And the sweetest sound of all - two friends laughing together under the big blue sky.

The end.
