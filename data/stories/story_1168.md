# Story story_1168

Sam sat on the big red swing. His feet dangled in the air. The swing moved back and forth slowly.

The playground was very quiet. Sam looked around with sad eyes. All the other swings were empty. The slide had no children on it. The monkey bars were still and silent.

"I wish someone would play with me," Sam said softly. His voice sounded lonely in the empty playground.

Sam pushed his feet against the ground. The swing went higher. The wind blew through his brown hair. But swinging alone wasn't very fun.

He watched the gate, hoping to see another child. A red bird flew by and landed on the fence. The bird chirped a happy song.

"Hello, little bird," Sam called out. "Do you want to swing with me?"

The bird tilted its tiny head. Then it flew away to find some seeds.

Sam's shoulders drooped down. He felt very alone. The sun was warm on his face, but his heart felt cold.

Then Sam had an idea. His eyes grew bright and wide.

"I know!" he said, jumping off the swing. "I'll find someone to play with me!"

Sam ran to the sandbox near the swings. He picked up a small shovel. He dug in the soft, cool sand.

"Maybe I can build a friend," Sam said to himself.

He piled the sand up high. He made arms and legs. He used a stick for the nose. Two smooth rocks became the eyes.

"There!" Sam said proudly. "Now I have a friend!"

Sam ran back to the swings. He tried to lift his sand friend onto the swing next to him.

But the sand friend fell apart. The arms crumbled. The legs broke into pieces. Soon there was just a pile of sand on the ground.

Sam's bottom lip trembled. A tiny tear rolled down his cheek.

"That didn't work," he whispered sadly.

Sam sat back on his swing. He felt worse than before. The playground seemed even more quiet now.

But Sam was a brave little boy. He wiped his eyes with his sleeve. He took a deep breath of the fresh air.

"I won't give up," Sam said with determination. "I'll try something else!"

Sam looked around the playground again. This time, he noticed something different. Near the big oak tree, he saw a small white dog. The dog was tied to the tree with a long rope.

The dog looked just as lonely as Sam felt.

Sam walked over slowly. The dog wagged its fluffy tail. Its pink tongue hung out happily.

"Hello there," Sam said gently. "Are you lonely too?"

The dog barked once. It sounded friendly and excited.

Sam looked around for the dog's owner. He saw a woman sitting on a bench. She was reading a thick book.

Sam walked over to the woman. His heart beat fast with hope.

"Excuse me," Sam said politely. "Is that your dog?"

The woman looked up and smiled. She had kind, warm eyes.

"Yes, that's Fluffy," she said. "I'm waiting for my daughter. She's at dance class."

"Can Fluffy play with me?" Sam asked. "I don't have anyone to swing with."

The woman's smile grew bigger. "What a wonderful idea! Fluffy loves to play."

Sam and the woman walked back to Fluffy. The woman untied the rope.

"Go play, Fluffy!" she said.

Fluffy ran straight to Sam. The dog jumped up and licked Sam's hands. Sam giggled with pure joy.

Sam ran to the swings with Fluffy right behind him. Sam sat on his swing and pushed off. Fluffy ran in circles around the swing set. The dog barked happily.

"This is so much fun!" Sam laughed.

Soon, other children arrived at the playground. They saw Sam playing with Fluffy. They wanted to join in too.

Before long, Sam had many new friends. The playground filled with laughter and happy voices.

Sam learned something important that day. When something doesn't work the first time, you try again. Good things happen when you don't give up.

And sometimes, the best friends come when you least expect them.

The end.
