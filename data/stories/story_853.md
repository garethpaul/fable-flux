# Story story_853

**Penny's Lost Book**

Penny the seaplane loved her cozy home on Crystal Pond. Every morning, she would float gently on the sparkling water. The sun felt warm on her bright red wings. Birds chirped happy songs in the tall pine trees.

Penny had one very special treasure. It was her favorite storybook about flying adventures. She kept it safe in her cockpit every day. The book had colorful pictures of clouds and rainbows. She loved to read it before bedtime.

One windy Tuesday morning, Penny woke up excited. She wanted to read her special book. But when she looked in her cockpit, the book was gone!

"Oh no!" Penny gasped. "Where is my book?"

She looked all around her cockpit. She checked under her seats. She searched behind her controls. The book was nowhere to be found.

Penny felt very sad. Her bright red paint seemed less shiny. The morning didn't feel as cheerful anymore.

"Maybe the wind blew it away," she thought sadly.

Then Penny had an idea. She would fly high above the pond to look for her book. Maybe she could spot it from up in the sky.

Penny started her engine. The propeller spun round and round. She took off from the water with a gentle splash. Up, up, up she flew into the blue sky.

From high above, Penny could see everything. She saw the green trees swaying in the breeze. She saw the pond shimmering like a mirror. She looked very carefully for her special book.

But she couldn't see it anywhere. The book wasn't floating on the water. It wasn't caught in the tree branches. It wasn't lying on the grassy shore.

Penny felt disappointed. Her first plan didn't work. She flew back down and landed on the pond with a soft splash.

"What should I try next?" Penny wondered aloud.

Just then, her friend Ollie the otter popped his head above the water. His whiskers were dripping with pond water.

"Hi Penny!" Ollie said cheerfully. "You look worried. What's wrong?"

"I lost my favorite book," Penny explained. "I looked everywhere from the sky. But I couldn't find it."

Ollie's dark eyes sparkled with an idea. "Maybe your book fell into the pond! I'm a great swimmer. I can dive down and look for you."

Penny felt hopeful again. "Would you really help me?"

"Of course!" Ollie said with a big smile. "That's what friends are for!"

Ollie took a deep breath. He dove down into the cool, clear water. Down, down, down he swam. He looked near the lily pads. He checked around the smooth rocks. He searched by the cattails.

After a few minutes, Ollie popped back up. In his paws, he held something very special.

"Is this your book?" Ollie asked proudly.

Penny's heart filled with joy. "Yes! That's my special book! Thank you so much, Ollie!"

The book was a little wet, but it wasn't damaged. Ollie had found it resting on a soft bed of pond weeds. It must have fallen out during yesterday's strong winds.

Penny carefully placed the book in her cockpit to dry. The colorful pictures were still bright and beautiful. Soon she would be able to read it again.

"I'm so grateful for your help," Penny told Ollie. "I almost gave up looking."

"I'm glad I could help," Ollie replied. "Sometimes we need to try different ways to solve problems."

Penny nodded wisely. "You're right! Flying high didn't work. But asking for help did work."

That evening, Penny read her favorite book as the sun set over the pond. The pages were completely dry now. The story seemed even more wonderful than before.

Penny learned something important that day. When one solution doesn't work, it's good to try another way. And asking friends for help makes everything better.

From that day on, Penny always kept her book in a special waterproof box. She never lost it again. And whenever other friends needed help, Penny was always ready to lend a wing.

The pond felt peaceful and happy. Penny drifted off to sleep, dreaming of tomorrow's new adventures.

The end.
