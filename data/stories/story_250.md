# Rosie's Big Voice

In a cozy playroom filled with sunshine, lived a soft pink doll named Rosie. She had curly yellow hair and a polka-dot dress. Rosie loved her special spot on the toy shelf. From there, she could watch all the fun.

Every day, children came to play. They built tall towers with blocks. They zoomed cars around the carpet. They had tea parties with the other toys. But Rosie always stayed on her shelf.

One morning, something terrible happened. Rosie's favorite button popped off her dress! The shiny red button rolled across the floor. It stopped under the big toy box.

"Oh no!" whispered Rosie. She felt very sad. Her dress looked funny with a missing button. But the button was too far away. She couldn't reach it from her shelf.

Rosie watched the other toys playing. The teddy bear was building with blocks. The toy train was chugging around. The puzzle pieces were scattered everywhere. Everyone looked so busy.

"Maybe someone could help me," thought Rosie. But her tummy felt all fluttery. What if they said no? What if they were too busy? Rosie had never asked for help before.

She took a tiny breath. "Um... excuse me?" she said very quietly. Her voice was softer than a whisper. Nobody heard her.

The teddy bear kept building. The train kept chugging. The puzzle pieces kept chattering.

Rosie tried again. "Hello?" she said, a little louder. Still, nobody looked up. Her voice got lost in all the playroom sounds.

Rosie felt her cheeks get warm. This was so hard! But she really needed that button. She loved her polka-dot dress.

She watched a little girl pick up the teddy bear. "You're so soft and cuddly!" the girl said. The teddy bear looked so happy.

"I want to feel happy too," thought Rosie. "I need to be brave."

Rosie took three deep breaths. She thought about her pretty button. She thought about how much she loved her dress. She thought about how good it would feel to be whole again.

"TEDDY BEAR!" she called out. Her voice was stronger now. It traveled across the playroom like a little bell.

The fuzzy brown teddy bear looked up. "Did someone call me?"

"Yes! It's me, Rosie!" She waved from her shelf. "I need help, please!"

The teddy bear smiled. "Of course! What's wrong?"

"My button fell off," Rosie explained. "It rolled under the toy box. I can't reach it."

"Don't worry," said Teddy Bear kindly. "I'll help you right away!"

Teddy Bear walked over to the toy box. He got down on his furry tummy. He reached his paw way underneath. "I can see it!" he said. "It's shiny and red!"

With a gentle push, Teddy Bear rolled the button out. It sparkled in the sunlight.

"Thank you so much!" said Rosie. She felt warm and happy inside.

"You're very welcome," said Teddy Bear. "But how will we put it back on?"

Just then, the toy train chugged over. "Did someone need help?" he asked.

"Rosie needs her button sewn back on," explained Teddy Bear.

"The sewing box is right here!" said Train. "We can work together!"

Soon, all the toys were helping. They found thread and a needle. They worked very carefully. The puzzle pieces cheered them on.

When they finished, Rosie's dress looked perfect again. The red button sparkled like a tiny jewel.

"I'm so happy!" said Rosie. "Thank you, everyone!"

"We're happy to help," said Teddy Bear. "But next time, just ask right away! We love helping friends."

Rosie giggled. "I will! I learned that asking for help isn't scary. It's how we take care of each other."

From that day on, Rosie used her voice whenever she needed help. She also helped other toys when they needed it. The playroom became an even happier place.

And Rosie never felt shy about speaking up again. Her voice was strong, kind, and important. Just like her.

The end.
