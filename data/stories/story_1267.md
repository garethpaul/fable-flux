# Story story_1267

**Papa and the Tall Fence**

Papa loved visiting the library with his little daughter Emma. Every Tuesday, they walked hand in hand down the sunny street. The library had big windows and colorful books inside.

But Papa had a problem. Behind the library was a beautiful garden. All the other grown-ups could peek over the wooden fence. They smiled at the pretty flowers and chirping birds.

Papa was too short. He could only see the brown fence boards. This made him feel sad.

"I want to see the garden too," Papa whispered to Emma.

Emma patted his hand. "We can find a way, Papa!"

Papa looked at the tall fence. It was much taller than him. The wood smelled like pine trees. He could hear bees buzzing on the other side.

"Maybe I can jump," Papa said.

He took three big steps back. Then he ran toward the fence. Papa jumped as high as he could. His feet left the ground for just a second.

But it wasn't high enough. Papa landed with a soft thud. He could still only see brown wood.

"That didn't work," Papa said with a sigh.

Emma giggled. "Try again, Papa! You're very smart."

Papa brushed dirt off his pants. He looked around the library yard. There were benches, trees, and flower pots everywhere.

Then Papa had an idea. His eyes sparkled.

"Emma, look at those flower pots!"

Three large clay pots sat near the fence. They were empty and waiting for spring flowers. Papa smiled big.

"Can you help me move these?" Papa asked.

Emma nodded. "Yes! I'm a good helper!"

Together, they pushed the first pot. It scraped along the ground with a rough sound. The pot was heavy, but they worked as a team.

"One, two, three, push!" Emma counted.

They moved the second pot next to the first one. Then they added the third pot. Now Papa had three steps leading to the fence.

"This might work," Papa said hopefully.

Papa climbed onto the first pot. It felt sturdy under his feet. Then he stepped onto the second pot. He was getting taller!

Finally, Papa stepped onto the third pot. Now he was high enough!

Papa looked over the fence for the first time. His mouth opened wide with wonder.

The garden was amazing! Red roses bloomed everywhere. Yellow sunflowers reached toward the sky. Purple butterflies danced from flower to flower.

A small pond sparkled in the sunshine. Goldfish swam in circles, making tiny ripples. White daisies lined the walking paths.

"It's beautiful!" Papa called down to Emma.

"What do you see?" Emma asked excitedly.

"Roses and butterflies! Fish and flowers! Come up and look!"

Emma climbed up the pots too. Papa held her hand to keep her safe. Together, they gazed at the magical garden.

"The flowers smell like honey," Emma said, sniffing the air.

"And look at those orange marigolds," Papa pointed. "They're as bright as the sun!"

A friendly robin landed on the fence. It chirped hello to Papa and Emma. They waved at the little bird.

"We did it, Papa!" Emma clapped her hands. "You didn't give up!"

Papa hugged his daughter. "You're right, Emma. When something seems too hard, we just need to think of new ways to try."

They climbed down from their flower pot steps. Papa felt proud and happy. The problem that seemed so big now felt small.

"Next Tuesday, we can look at the garden again," Papa said.

"And maybe we'll see new flowers!" Emma added.

They walked into the library together. Papa's heart felt warm and full. He had learned something important today.

Sometimes being different isn't a problem. Sometimes it just means you need to be creative.

Papa and Emma found their favorite picture books. But Papa kept thinking about the beautiful garden. And about how good it felt to never give up.

From now on, Papa would remember: there's always another way to try.

The end.
