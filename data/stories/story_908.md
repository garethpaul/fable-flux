# Story story_908

**Freddy Fox and Music Time**

The big clock on the wall went tick-tock. Miss Luna clapped her hands twice. "Time for music!" she called out cheerfully.

All the little animals in the forest school jumped up. Bunny hopped to get her tambourine. Squirrel scampered to find the wooden blocks. Bear waddled over to the big drum.

But Freddy Fox stayed in his corner. He wrapped his fluffy red tail around himself. His ears drooped down low.

"Come on, Freddy!" called Bunny. "Music time is starting!"

Freddy shook his head. "I don't want to," he whispered. His voice was very quiet. "I'm not good at music."

Miss Luna walked over to Freddy. Her paws were soft and warm. "What's wrong, little fox?" she asked gently.

"The sounds are too loud," Freddy said. "I don't know which instrument to pick. Everyone else is better than me."

Miss Luna smiled. Her eyes twinkled like stars. "Would you like to try something special today?"

Freddy peeked up with one orange eye. "What kind of special?"

Miss Luna reached into her magical music box. She pulled out something that sparkled silver. It was small and smooth. "This is a rain stick," she said. "Listen."

She turned it over slowly. Tiny beads inside made gentle sounds. They sounded like raindrops on leaves. They sounded like a babbling brook. The noise was soft and peaceful.

Freddy's ears perked up. "It sounds like the forest," he whispered.

"Would you like to hold it?" Miss Luna asked.

Freddy nodded. He took the rain stick carefully. It felt cool in his paws. He turned it over. The gentle sound made him smile.

"Now close your eyes," Miss Luna said. "What do you hear?"

Freddy closed his eyes tight. He listened to his rain stick. Around him, the other animals played their instruments. But now the sounds felt different.

Bunny's tambourine sounded like wind in the trees. Squirrel's blocks clicked like acorns falling. Bear's drum boomed like thunder far away.

"It's like a forest storm!" Freddy said with wonder.

"Exactly!" Miss Luna laughed. "You're making music with your friends."

Freddy opened his eyes. He saw all his friends smiling at him. They kept playing their instruments. Freddy turned his rain stick again and again.

The music grew louder and softer. Sometimes it was fast like a summer storm. Sometimes it was slow like morning mist.

Freddy began to sway back and forth. His tail swished happily. He felt the rhythm in his whole body.

"This is fun!" he called out. His voice was strong now.

Bunny hopped closer. "You sound wonderful, Freddy!"

Squirrel chittered with joy. "Your rain stick makes everything magical!"

Bear nodded his big head. "You make our music better!"

They played together until the music time was over. When Miss Luna said "All done," Freddy felt sad.

"Can I play the rain stick again tomorrow?" he asked hopefully.

"Of course," Miss Luna said. "But maybe tomorrow you'd like to try something else too?"

Freddy thought about this. "Maybe I could try the tambourine with Bunny?"

"That sounds perfect," Miss Luna said.

As the animals put away their instruments, Freddy helped too. He placed his rain stick gently in the music box.

"Thank you for the special music," he told Miss Luna.

She patted his head softly. "The magic was inside you all along, Freddy. You just needed to find it."

That night, Freddy dreamed of forest sounds. He dreamed of making music with all his friends. When he woke up, he couldn't wait for school.

"Today is music day again!" he told his mama.

His tail wagged with excitement. He ran all the way to school. He couldn't wait to make more magical music with his friends.

Music time wasn't scary anymore. It was the best part of his whole day.

The end.
