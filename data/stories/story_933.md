# Story story_933

Emma climbed the creaky wooden stairs. The old attic smelled like dusty books. Sunlight streamed through a small round window.

Emma loved the attic. It was her special place. She had soft blankets spread on the floor. Her favorite dolls sat in a neat row.

But today felt different. Emma sighed as she picked up her doll. "I wish I had someone to play with," she whispered.

Emma made her doll dance. She gave it a tiny voice. "Hello, Emma! Want to have tea?"

Emma pretended to sip from a plastic cup. The pretend tea tasted like nothing. Playing alone wasn't very fun anymore.

Downstairs, little footsteps pattered on the floor. Emma heard her baby sister Zoe calling. "Emma! Emma! Where are you?"

Zoe was only four years old. She always wanted to follow Emma everywhere. Emma usually said no. The attic was Emma's special space.

"I'm busy, Zoe," Emma called down. "Go play with Mom."

But Zoe's footsteps came closer. Soon her curly head peeked up the stairs. Her big brown eyes looked hopeful.

"Can I play too?" Zoe asked softly. She held her stuffed bunny tight.

Emma looked around her quiet attic. Her dolls stared back with painted smiles. The tea party felt lonely again.

"I don't know," Emma said. "You might mess up my things."

Zoe's face fell. Her bottom lip trembled. "I'll be careful. I promise."

Emma saw how sad Zoe looked. Her sister's eyes were shiny with tears. Emma's heart felt heavy too.

"Okay," Emma said slowly. "But you have to be gentle."

Zoe's face lit up like sunshine. She climbed the stairs carefully. Her bunny bounced with each step.

"Wow!" Zoe gasped. "Your attic is so pretty!"

Emma felt proud. She had worked hard to make it special. "That's my tea table," she explained. "And those are my favorite dolls."

Zoe sat down gently. She placed her bunny next to Emma's dolls. "Can Bunny have tea too?"

Emma nodded. She poured pretend tea for everyone. Zoe giggled as she sipped from her cup.

"This tea tastes like strawberries!" Zoe said.

Emma smiled. "Mine tastes like chocolate!"

They played house next. Zoe was the baby. Emma was the mommy. They made dinner with wooden blocks.

"More soup, please!" Zoe said in a silly baby voice.

Emma laughed. Zoe was actually fun to play with. Her imagination was wonderful.

Then they built a fort. They used blankets and pillows. The fort had secret windows and a cozy inside.

"This is our castle!" Zoe declared. Her eyes sparkled with joy.

"We're princess sisters," Emma added. She felt warm and happy inside.

They told stories in their castle. Zoe's stories were silly and sweet. Emma's stories were brave and exciting.

The afternoon flew by like magic. Soon Mom called for dinner. Emma felt surprised. Had they really played that long?

"I had so much fun," Zoe said. She hugged her bunny tight.

Emma looked around the attic. It felt different now. It felt warmer and more alive. Playing with Zoe had made everything better.

"Me too," Emma said. She meant it completely.

"Can we play again tomorrow?" Zoe asked hopefully.

Emma grinned. "Of course! This can be our special place now."

Zoe clapped her hands. "Our sister place!"

They cleaned up together. Emma showed Zoe where everything belonged. Zoe helped carefully and gently.

As they walked downstairs, Emma felt different too. She wasn't lonely anymore. She had her sister.

"Thank you for letting me play," Zoe said quietly.

Emma hugged her little sister tight. "Thank you for making it fun."

From that day on, the attic belonged to both sisters. They had tea parties and built forts. They told stories and shared dreams.

Emma learned something important. Playing together was much better than playing alone. Sisters made the very best friends.

And in their special attic, two sisters discovered the magic of sharing and caring.

The end.
