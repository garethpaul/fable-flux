# Story story_1234

**Nutkin's Red Ball**

Nutkin the squirrel loved her bright red ball. She carried it everywhere in the forest. The ball was shiny and smooth. It felt perfect in her tiny paws.

Every morning, Nutkin played catch with her ball. She would throw it high into the air. Then she would catch it with a happy giggle. The red ball bounced perfectly every time.

One sunny day, Nutkin was playing near the pond. The water sparkled like diamonds in the sunshine. Nutkin threw her ball extra high. She wanted to see how far it would go.

"Wheee!" squeaked Nutkin as the ball flew through the air.

But oh no! The ball sailed right over her head. It landed in the middle of the pond with a big splash. Nutkin watched her favorite red ball float away from the shore.

"My ball!" cried Nutkin. "Come back, little red ball!"

The ball bobbed on the gentle waves. It drifted further and further away. Nutkin felt tears fill her brown eyes. Her whiskers trembled with worry.

Nutkin had to get her ball back. She looked around for something to help. A long stick lay on the ground nearby. Maybe she could reach the ball with the stick.

Nutkin picked up the heavy stick. She stretched as far as she could. The stick was almost long enough. Almost, but not quite. The red ball floated just out of reach.

"Come on, stick," huffed Nutkin. "Please be longer!"

She leaned forward more and more. Suddenly, her paws slipped on the muddy bank. Splash! Nutkin tumbled right into the shallow water.

The cold water made her fur all wet and soggy. Nutkin climbed back onto the shore. She shook herself off, sending water drops flying everywhere. But her ball was still floating away.

Nutkin sat down and thought hard. Her tail twitched as she made a new plan. Maybe she needed help from a friend.

"Oliver!" called Nutkin. "Oliver the duck!"

Oliver swam over from across the pond. His orange feet paddled quickly through the water. His white feathers gleamed in the sunlight.

"What's wrong, Nutkin?" asked Oliver kindly.

"My red ball fell in the water," sniffled Nutkin. "Can you help me get it back?"

Oliver looked at the floating ball. It had drifted quite far from shore. But Oliver was an excellent swimmer.

"Of course I can help," quacked Oliver. "That's what friends are for!"

Oliver dove under the water with a splash. His webbed feet kicked strongly. He swam underwater like a feathery fish. Soon he popped up right next to the red ball.

Oliver grabbed the ball gently in his orange beak. He swam back to shore as fast as he could. The ball looked even brighter red against his white feathers.

"Here you go," said Oliver, dropping the ball at Nutkin's feet.

Nutkin hugged her precious red ball tight. It felt wonderful to hold it again. The ball was a little wet, but it was still perfectly round and bouncy.

"Thank you so much, Oliver!" squeaked Nutkin happily. "You're the best friend ever!"

"I'm glad I could help," smiled Oliver. "But maybe we should play further from the water next time."

Nutkin nodded eagerly. That was a very good idea. She had learned something important today. When one way doesn't work, try thinking of another way. And asking friends for help is always okay.

From that day on, Nutkin played with her red ball in the safe, grassy meadow. Sometimes Oliver would join her for a game of catch. The ball never fell in the water again.

Nutkin kept her red ball for many years. Every time she looked at it, she remembered Oliver's kindness. She also remembered that giving up is never the answer. There's always another way to solve a problem.

And whenever other forest friends needed help, Nutkin was always ready to lend a paw. Just like Oliver had done for her.

The end.
