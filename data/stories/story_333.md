# The Puzzle in the Attic

Up in the dusty attic, Puzzle sat alone. He was a beautiful jigsaw puzzle with bright blue pieces. His picture showed a happy farm scene. But Puzzle felt sad and lonely.

"I wish someone would play with me," Puzzle sighed. His pieces were scattered everywhere. Some pieces hid under old boxes. Others lay near the window.

Puzzle had been in the attic for months. No one ever came to visit. The mice were too small to help. The spiders were too busy spinning webs.

"Maybe I'll never be whole again," Puzzle whispered sadly.

Just then, footsteps creaked on the wooden stairs. A little boy peeked into the attic. His name was Sam. He was looking for his brother Jake.

"Jake, are you up here?" Sam called out.

Sam's eyes grew wide when he saw Puzzle. "Wow! A puzzle!" he said excitedly. Sam loved puzzles very much.

Sam sat down on the dusty floor. He started picking up Puzzle's pieces. "Let me help you," Sam said gently.

Puzzle felt a warm, happy feeling. Finally, someone wanted to play! Sam found four corner pieces first. He placed them carefully on the floor.

"This is hard work," Sam said. He wiped sweat from his forehead. There were so many pieces to find!

More footsteps came up the stairs. Jake appeared at the top. He was Sam's older brother.

"There you are, Sam!" Jake said. "What are you doing up here?"

"I found this puzzle," Sam explained. "But it's really big. I need help putting it together."

Jake looked at all the scattered pieces. "That does look tricky," he agreed. Jake sat down next to Sam.

"Can we work together?" Sam asked hopefully.

"Of course!" Jake smiled. "Two heads are better than one!"

The brothers started sorting Puzzle's pieces. Jake found all the edge pieces. Sam looked for pieces with the same colors. They made neat little piles.

"Look! This piece has a red barn!" Sam pointed excitedly.

"And this one has a brown cow!" Jake added.

Puzzle felt wonderful. Both boys were being so gentle. They talked and laughed while they worked. It felt like a warm hug.

Sam found two pieces that fit together. "They match!" he cheered. Jake clapped his hands proudly.

"You're really good at this," Jake told Sam. Sam's face lit up with joy.

Jake spotted a tricky corner piece. "This one goes here," he said. The piece fit perfectly!

"Great job!" Sam said. "We make a good team!"

Hour by hour, Puzzle came together. The boys took turns finding pieces. Sometimes they helped each other. They never got frustrated or gave up.

"The barn is almost done!" Sam announced.

"And look at this pretty blue sky!" Jake added.

When they got stuck, they worked together. Four eyes could see better than two. Four hands could sort pieces faster.

"I found the missing piece!" Sam shouted. It was the cow's tail!

"Perfect!" Jake laughed. "Our cow is complete now!"

Finally, only one piece remained. It was a small piece of green grass. Both boys reached for it together.

"You put it in," Jake said kindly.

"No, you do it," Sam replied sweetly.

They decided to place it together. Their hands touched as the last piece clicked into place.

"We did it!" both boys cheered loudly.

Puzzle felt absolutely amazing. He was whole and beautiful again! His farm scene was complete and colorful.

"That was so much fun!" Sam said happily.

"It was better with two people," Jake agreed. "We should do puzzles together more often!"

The brothers high-fived each other. They admired their beautiful work. Puzzle's picture showed a sunny farm with red barns, brown cows, and green grass.

"Thanks for helping me," Sam said.

"Thanks for sharing," Jake replied.

Puzzle smiled his biggest smile. He had helped two brothers become even better friends. Working together had made everything more fun and easier.

From that day on, Sam and Jake visited the attic often. They did puzzles together every weekend. And Puzzle was never lonely again.

The attic became filled with laughter and love. The best things happen when friends work together!

The end.
