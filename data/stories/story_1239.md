# Story story_1239

**The Puzzle and the Little Bird**

In a sunny nursery room, a colorful puzzle sat on the soft carpet. The puzzle had bright red apples, yellow suns, and blue butterflies on each piece. But the puzzle felt very lonely.

"I wish someone would play with me," sighed the puzzle. Its pieces were scattered all around. Some pieces hid under the toy box. Others lay near the window where morning light made them sparkle.

The puzzle tried to put itself together. It wiggled and jiggled its pieces. But puzzles need gentle hands to help them. The puzzle felt sad and incomplete.

Just then, a small yellow bird flew through the open window. The bird had tiny black eyes and soft feathers. It chirped a sweet morning song.

"Tweet, tweet! What a pretty puzzle!" sang the little bird. It landed on the carpet with tiny feet that went tap, tap, tap.

The puzzle's pieces seemed to shimmer with hope. "Hello, little bird! Would you like to play with me?"

The bird tilted its head. "I would love to! But I don't know how puzzles work."

"It's easy!" said the puzzle happily. "You put pieces together to make a picture. Each piece has a special place."

The little bird hopped closer. It picked up a piece with its small beak. The piece had part of a red apple on it.

"Where does this go?" asked the bird.

"Look for the other apple pieces," said the puzzle gently. "Colors and shapes help you know."

The bird searched with bright, curious eyes. It found another apple piece nearby. The bird carefully placed them together.

"They fit!" chirped the bird with joy. "This is fun!"

Together, they worked on the puzzle. The bird used its sharp eyes to spot matching colors. The puzzle helped by wiggling when pieces were close to the right spot.

"Try the yellow piece by the book," suggested the puzzle.

The bird picked it up. "It has sunshine on it!"

"Yes! That goes in the sky part," said the puzzle warmly.

Piece by piece, they built the picture together. The bird's tiny feet danced as it worked. Its happy chirps filled the nursery with music.

Sometimes pieces didn't fit right away. "Don't worry," said the puzzle kindly. "We can try again."

The bird never gave up. It turned pieces around and tried new spots. "Practice makes it easier!" the bird discovered.

As they worked, they talked and laughed together. The puzzle shared stories about all the children who had played with it. The bird told tales of flying high above the trees.

"You're a wonderful friend," said the puzzle.

"You are too!" chirped the bird. "I love solving puzzles with you."

Finally, they placed the very last piece. The complete picture showed a beautiful garden with flowers, trees, and a bright blue sky.

"We did it together!" they cheered.

The bird spread its wings wide with pride. The puzzle's pieces gleamed in the sunlight streaming through the window.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" asked the puzzle hopefully.

"Every morning!" promised the little bird. "We make a great team."

From that day on, the bird visited the nursery every morning. They solved puzzles together, shared stories, and became the very best of friends.

The other toys in the nursery watched with smiles. They loved seeing their friends so happy together.

When children came to play, they found the puzzle already complete. "Look how beautiful!" the children would say. They didn't know about the special friendship that made it possible.

The puzzle was never lonely again. It had found a true friend who visited every single day. The little bird had found a special place where it belonged.

Together, they learned that friendship makes everything more fun. Working together helps solve any problem. And the best adventures happen when you share them with someone you care about.

Every morning, their friendship grew stronger, just like the beautiful pictures they created together, piece by piece.

The end.
