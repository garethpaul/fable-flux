# Story story_1270

**Blocks and the Missing Red Ball**

Blocks loved to play in the long hallway. She was a cheerful little girl with curly brown hair. Every day after breakfast, she would roll her favorite red ball back and forth.

The red ball was special. It was bright like a fire truck. It bounced with a happy *thump, thump, thump* sound. Blocks had played with it every single day for two whole months.

One sunny morning, Blocks skipped to the hallway. She looked left. She looked right. She looked under the small table. Her red ball was nowhere to be found!

"Oh no!" Blocks said out loud. Her voice echoed in the empty hallway. "Where is my red ball?"

Blocks felt worried. Her tummy felt funny and tight. She really wanted to find her special ball. The hallway seemed too quiet without the bouncing sounds.

"I will look everywhere," Blocks said with determination. She started her search right away.

First, Blocks looked in all the usual places. She checked under the coat rack. She peeked behind the tall plant. She even looked inside the umbrella stand. The red ball was not there.

Blocks sat down on the soft carpet. She felt sad and confused. "Maybe someone took my ball outside," she thought.

So Blocks ran to the big window. She pressed her nose against the cool glass. Outside, she could see the green grass and colorful flowers. She searched with her eyes for anything red.

There! Under the big oak tree was something red! Blocks felt excited. Her heart beat faster.

"That must be my ball!" she shouted happily.

Blocks rushed outside. The warm sun felt nice on her face. The grass tickled her bare feet. She ran all the way to the oak tree.

But when she got closer, she saw it was just a red flower. Not her ball at all.

Blocks felt disappointed. Her shoulders drooped down. "This is not working," she said softly.

She walked slowly back inside. The hallway still felt empty and quiet. But Blocks did not give up. She was a brave girl who tried hard.

"I need to think more carefully," Blocks said to herself. She sat down and closed her eyes. She tried to remember the last time she played with her ball.

Yesterday, she had bounced it really, really high. It went *bounce, bounce, bounce* all the way down the hallway. Then it rolled and rolled until it stopped.

"Wait!" Blocks said suddenly. Her eyes opened wide. "Maybe it rolled too far!"

She had never looked at the very end of the hallway. There was a dark corner behind the big bookshelf. Blocks had never played there before.

Blocks walked carefully to the dark corner. She got down on her hands and knees. The carpet felt soft under her fingers. She reached her arm way back behind the bookshelf.

Her fingers touched something round and smooth!

"I found it!" Blocks shouted with joy. She pulled out her bright red ball. It was a little dusty but perfectly fine.

Blocks hugged the ball tight against her chest. It felt warm and familiar. She was so happy she did a little dance right there in the hallway.

"You were just hiding," she told the ball with a big smile. "I missed you so much!"

Blocks learned something important that day. Sometimes when things seem lost, they are just in places you have not looked yet. You have to keep trying and thinking of new ideas.

She also learned that giving up too quickly does not help. Being patient and trying different ways is much better.

From that day on, Blocks always checked the dark corners when she cleaned up. She never lost her red ball again.

Now when she played in the hallway, the happy *thump, thump, thump* sounds filled the air once more. And Blocks felt proud that she had solved the mystery all by herself.

The red ball bounced higher than ever, as if it was happy to be found too.

The end.
