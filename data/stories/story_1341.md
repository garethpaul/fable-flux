# Story story_1341

**Baby and the Gentle Giant**

Little Maya sat on a soft blanket in the sunny forest clearing. She had pretty blocks to stack. She had a red ball to roll. But Maya felt lonely playing all by herself.

The trees swayed gently above her. Birds chirped sweet songs. Butterflies danced in the warm air. But Maya wanted someone to play with.

"I wish I had a friend," Maya said softly.

Behind the tall oak trees, a baby elephant named Kesi heard Maya's voice. Kesi had been watching the little girl play. The elephant's big brown eyes were kind and curious.

Kesi wanted to play too. But she was worried. "Am I too big?" Kesi wondered. "Will the little girl be scared of me?"

Kesi took one small step forward. A twig snapped under her foot. CRACK!

Maya looked up. She saw a gray trunk peeking around the tree. Then she saw one big, gentle eye looking at her.

"Oh!" Maya gasped. But she wasn't scared. The eye looked friendly.

Kesi stepped out slowly. She was much bigger than Maya. Her skin was wrinkled and gray. Her ears flapped like big fans.

Maya smiled. "Hello," she said in her tiny voice.

Kesi's trunk reached down. She touched Maya's hand very gently. The trunk felt soft and warm.

"You want to play?" Maya asked.

Kesi nodded her big head. Her trunk picked up Maya's red ball. She rolled it back to Maya with her nose.

Maya giggled. The sound made Kesi's ears flap happily.

Maya rolled the ball to Kesi. Kesi caught it with her trunk. She rolled it back. They played catch together.

"This is fun!" Maya clapped her hands.

Next, Maya showed Kesi her blocks. The blocks were too small for Kesi's trunk. But Kesi had an idea.

Kesi gathered smooth stones with her trunk. Big ones and little ones. She stacked them carefully.

"You're building too!" Maya said. She stacked her colorful blocks next to Kesi's stone tower.

The two friends built side by side. Maya's tower was small and bright. Kesi's tower was tall and gray. Both towers were beautiful.

When Maya got tired, Kesi sat down. Maya leaned against Kesi's warm, soft side. The elephant's breathing was slow and peaceful.

"You're like a big, cozy chair," Maya said.

Kesi's trunk gently touched Maya's hair. It felt like the softest blanket.

Maya picked yellow flowers from the grass. She made a pretty chain. Kesi lowered her head. Maya put the flower chain around Kesi's neck.

"Now you look fancy," Maya giggled.

Kesi found purple berries on a low bush. She picked them carefully with her trunk. She shared them with Maya. The berries were sweet and juicy.

"Yummy!" Maya said. "Thank you, Kesi."

As the sun got lower, Maya's mama called from far away. "Maya! Time to come home!"

Maya felt sad. She didn't want to leave her new friend.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" Maya asked.

Kesi nodded. She touched Maya's cheek with her trunk. It was like a gentle kiss.

Maya hugged Kesi's trunk. It was the best hug ever.

"I'm so happy we're friends," Maya said.

Maya picked up her blanket and toys. Kesi walked with her to the edge of the forest. Maya's mama was waiting there.

"Mama, this is Kesi!" Maya said proudly. "She's my best friend!"

Maya's mama smiled. "Hello, Kesi. Thank you for playing with Maya."

Kesi bowed her head politely.

The next day, Maya came back to the forest. Kesi was waiting by the oak tree. She had gathered pretty shells and smooth rocks for them to play with.

Maya ran to her friend. "Kesi! You came back!"

From that day on, Maya and Kesi played together every afternoon. Maya was never lonely again. She had found the most wonderful friend in the whole forest.

Sometimes the best friends come in the biggest packages.

The end.
