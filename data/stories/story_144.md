# Woolly and Sam's Hill Adventure

On top of a grassy green hill lived a fluffy white sheep named Woolly. Every morning, Woolly would roll in the soft grass. She would chase butterflies around the daisies. She would bounce on her springy legs.

But something was missing. Woolly felt lonely.

"I wish I had a friend," she sighed. Her woolly coat felt heavy with sadness. The hill seemed too big for just one sheep.

Woolly tried to have fun alone. She rolled down the hill. *Bump, bump, bump!* But rolling alone wasn't very exciting. She tried playing hide-and-seek behind the oak tree. But there was nobody to find her.

The morning sun felt warm on her back. Birds chirped happy songs in the trees. But Woolly still felt alone.

Then she heard footsteps. *Crunch, crunch, crunch* through the grass.

A boy with curly brown hair climbed up the hill. He carried a red ball under his arm. His name was Sam.

"Hello there, sheep!" Sam called out cheerfully. "What's your name?"

"I'm Woolly," she replied shyly. "What are you doing here?"

"I came to play," Sam said. "But I don't have anyone to play with. May I play with you?"

Woolly's heart jumped with excitement. "Yes, please!" she bleated happily.

Sam rolled his red ball toward Woolly. She pushed it back with her nose. *Boing!* The ball bounced between them.

"This is fun!" laughed Sam.

"Much better than playing alone!" agreed Woolly.

They discovered they both loved rolling down the hill. Sam would lie on his back. Woolly would curl up in a ball. Together they rolled down the grassy slope.

"Wheee!" they shouted together. Their laughter echoed across the hill.

At the bottom, they were both dizzy and giggling. Grass stuck in Sam's hair. Woolly's wool was full of dandelion seeds.

"Let's try something else!" suggested Sam.

They played a new game together. Sam would hide behind the big oak tree. Woolly would close her eyes and count. "One, two, three... ready or not!"

When Woolly found Sam, they switched places. Now Sam counted while Woolly hid behind a boulder.

"Found you!" Sam called out. They both burst into giggles.

Next, they worked together to build something special. Sam gathered smooth stones. Woolly pushed them into place with her nose. Together, they made a perfect circle.

"It's our friendship ring!" declared Sam.

"A place just for us!" added Woolly proudly.

They sat inside their stone circle. Sam shared his apple slices with Woolly. She shared the sweet clover she had found.

"You know what?" said Sam, patting Woolly's soft wool. "Everything is more fun with a friend."

"I was so lonely before," admitted Woolly. "But now I feel happy and warm inside."

"Me too," smiled Sam. "Should we be best friends?"

"Yes!" bleated Woolly excitedly. "Best friends forever!"

They decided to meet on the hill every day. Sam would bring new games to try. Woolly would show him the best spots for rolling and jumping.

As the sun climbed higher, they played one more game. They took turns being the leader. When Sam led, they marched around like soldiers. When Woolly led, they hopped like bunnies.

"This was the best day ever!" declared Sam.

"The very, very best!" agreed Woolly.

They hugged goodbye at the bottom of the hill. Sam promised to return tomorrow with his kite. Woolly promised to find the juiciest grass to share.

As Sam walked home, Woolly climbed back up the hill. But now the hill didn't feel too big anymore. It felt just right for two best friends.

She curled up in their stone circle. The warm sun made her drowsy. But her heart felt light and happy.

Tomorrow would bring another day of friendship and fun. Woolly couldn't wait to play with Sam again. Having a friend made everything better.

The hill was still the same hill. But now it was filled with laughter, friendship, and joy.
