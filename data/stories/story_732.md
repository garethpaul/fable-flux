# Story story_732

**The Little Mouse and the Garden Doll**

Pip the mouse lived under the big oak tree. Every morning, he poked his tiny nose out. The backyard smelled like fresh grass and flowers.

Pip loved to play alone. He would chase dandelion seeds in the wind. He would climb on smooth garden stones. But sometimes, Pip felt lonely.

"I wish I had a friend," Pip squeaked softly.

Near the flower bed sat a beautiful doll. Her name was Rosie. She had curly yellow hair and a pink dress. Rosie had been sitting there for many days.

Rosie watched Pip play every morning. She wanted to join him so badly. But dolls cannot move by themselves. This made Rosie very sad.

One sunny day, the wind blew extra hard. It pushed Rosie off her rock. She tumbled onto the soft grass with a gentle thump.

Pip heard the sound. He scurried over to look. There was Rosie, lying on her side in the clover.

"Oh my!" said Pip. "Are you hurt?"

Rosie's button eyes sparkled in the sunlight. Even though she could not talk, Pip felt something special. He could tell she was friendly.

"You look lonely too," Pip whispered.

Pip pushed Rosie upright with his tiny paws. Her dress felt smooth like flower petals. She smelled like sunshine and soap.

"Would you like to play with me?" asked Pip.

The wind seemed to nod Rosie's head yes. Pip's whiskers twitched with excitement.

First, they played hide and seek. Pip hid behind Rosie's dress. Then he hid under her yellow curls. Rosie was the perfect hiding spot!

Next, they had a tea party. Pip found acorn caps for cups. He filled them with sweet morning dew. They pretended to sip tea together.

"This tastes wonderful!" Pip said, making tiny sipping sounds.

The warm sun made everything feel magical. Butterflies danced around them. Bees hummed happy songs in the flowers.

Then Pip had his best idea yet. He climbed onto Rosie's lap. Her dress made a cozy nest. Together, they watched fluffy white clouds float by.

"That cloud looks like cheese," Pip giggled.

A gentle breeze made Rosie's hair tickle Pip's ears. It felt like she was laughing too.

As the day grew warmer, they played more games. Pip showed Rosie how to watch ants march in lines. He taught her about the different bird songs.

"That's a robin," Pip explained. "And that's a blue jay."

Rosie listened to every word. Her button eyes never looked away from her new friend.

When afternoon came, Pip felt tired. He curled up against Rosie's soft dress. Her fabric felt warm from the sun.

"Thank you for being my friend," Pip whispered.

Just then, a little girl ran into the backyard. It was Emma, Rosie's owner.

"There you are, Rosie!" Emma said. "I've been looking everywhere!"

Emma gently picked up Rosie. She noticed Pip sleeping peacefully nearby.

"Oh, how sweet," Emma said softly. "You made a little friend."

Emma carefully placed Rosie back on her rock. She made sure Rosie was facing Pip's tree.

From that day on, Pip and Rosie played together every morning. Emma would always put Rosie where Pip could reach her.

Sometimes Emma would even join their games. She would have tea parties with both her doll and the little mouse.

The backyard became filled with laughter and joy. Pip never felt lonely again. He had found the best friend ever.

Every evening, Pip would whisper goodnight to Rosie. Every morning, he would greet her with excitement.

"Good morning, best friend!" Pip would squeak happily.

And though Rosie could not speak, Pip always knew she was saying good morning back. Her sparkling button eyes told him everything.

The oak tree, the flowers, and even the busy ants seemed happier too. The whole backyard celebrated their special friendship.

Pip learned that the very best friends can be found in the most surprising places. And sometimes, the best friendships happen when you open your heart to someone different from you.

The end.
