# Story story_914

**Milly's Big Park Day**

Milly Mouse lived in a cozy hole under the big oak tree. Every morning, her mama would say, "Time for park time, Milly!" But today, Milly felt different. She pulled her soft pink blanket over her tiny head.

"I don't want to go to the park," Milly whispered. Her whiskers twitched nervously. "What if the slide is too tall? What if I fall down?"

Mama Mouse sat on Milly's little bed. "The park has so many fun things to do," she said gently. "We can take it slow today."

Milly peeked out from under her blanket. Her mama's warm brown eyes looked kind and patient. "Will you stay with me?" Milly asked.

"Of course, little one," Mama said. "Let's pack some cheese crackers for a snack."

They walked paw-in-paw down the winding path. The morning sun felt warm on Milly's fur. Birds chirped happy songs in the trees above. The grass smelled fresh and green.

At the park, Milly saw the big playground. The slide looked very tall. The swings moved back and forth in the breeze. Other little animals played and laughed together.

"I want to go home," Milly said quietly.

Just then, something sparkly caught her eye. Near the flower bed, tiny dewdrops covered a spider's web. The drops looked like diamonds in the sunshine. They twinkled and danced when the web moved.

"Oh my!" Milly gasped. "Look at the pretty sparkles!"

A friendly butterfly landed on the web. Its wings were orange and black with white spots. "Hello, little mouse," the butterfly said. "I'm Luna. Do you like my friend's artwork?"

Milly nodded. She had never seen anything so beautiful. "It's like magic," she whispered.

"Magic is everywhere in the park," Luna said. "Would you like me to show you more?"

Milly looked at her mama. Mama smiled and nodded. "Let's follow Luna," she said.

Luna fluttered to the sandbox. "Watch this," she said. She sprinkled golden sand into the air. The tiny grains caught the sunlight and sparkled like fairy dust.

"Wow!" Milly giggled. She scooped up some sand. It felt cool and smooth in her paws. She made little sand castles and drew pictures with her finger.

Next, Luna led them to the pond. "Listen carefully," she said. The water made gentle splashing sounds. Frogs sang deep, rumbly songs. A duck family paddled by, leaving ripples behind them.

"It sounds like music," Milly said. She sat by the water's edge and listened to the peaceful sounds.

"Now for the best part," Luna said. She flew to the merry-go-round. "Climb on, Milly!"

Milly felt nervous again. But the merry-go-round was small and moved very slowly. Mama Mouse climbed on too. As they spun gently around, Milly saw the whole park from a new view.

She saw children playing on swings. She saw squirrels chasing each other up trees. She saw flowers dancing in the breeze. Everything looked friendly and fun.

"I can see everything from up here!" Milly squeaked with joy.

When the merry-go-round stopped, Luna smiled. "The magic was inside you all along," she said. "You just needed to look with wonder."

Milly spent the rest of the morning exploring. She went down the small slide three times. She collected smooth pebbles and pretty leaves. She made friends with a shy rabbit named Benny.

During snack time, she and Mama shared their cheese crackers under the oak tree. "Thank you for bringing me to the park," Milly said. "I found so much magic today."

"I'm proud of you for being brave," Mama said, giving Milly a warm hug.

As they walked home, Milly's heart felt light and happy. She had discovered that the park wasn't scary at all. It was full of wonderful surprises and new friends.

"Mama," Milly said, skipping along the path. "Can we come back tomorrow? I want to show Luna my new drawing skills!"

Mama Mouse smiled. "Of course, sweet Milly. Tomorrow will bring even more magic."

That night, Milly fell asleep dreaming of butterfly friends and sparkling dewdrops, excited for another day of park adventures.

The end.
