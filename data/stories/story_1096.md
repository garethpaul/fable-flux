# Story story_1096

**Rosie the Fox Learns to Share**

In a sunny forest lived a little red fox named Rosie. She had bright orange fur and a fluffy white tail. Rosie loved to collect shiny acorns and pretty stones.

Every morning, Rosie would hunt for treasures. She found smooth pebbles by the stream. She gathered golden acorns under the oak trees. Her cozy den was full of beautiful things.

One day, Rosie sat counting her acorns. "One, two, three, four, five!" she said happily. Her tummy rumbled with hunger.

Just then, she heard a soft crying sound. "Sniff, sniff, sob."

Rosie peeked outside her den. A small gray squirrel sat by the big oak tree. Tears rolled down his furry cheeks.

"Why are you crying?" asked Rosie gently.

"I'm so hungry," said the squirrel. "I can't find any acorns anywhere. Winter is coming soon."

Rosie looked at her pile of acorns. Then she looked at the sad squirrel. Her heart felt funny inside.

"I have acorns," she said slowly. "But... but they're mine."

The squirrel nodded sadly. "I understand." He walked away with droopy shoulders.

Rosie felt worse and worse. Her tummy still rumbled. But now her heart hurt too.

"Maybe I should share," she thought. "But what if I give away too many? What if I get hungry later?"

Rosie had an idea. She would share just one tiny acorn. That wouldn't hurt anything.

She ran after the squirrel. "Wait!" she called. "Here's an acorn for you."

She gave him the smallest acorn from her pile. It was barely bigger than a pea.

The squirrel smiled a little. "Thank you, Rosie." But he still looked very hungry.

Rosie watched him nibble the tiny acorn. It disappeared in two small bites. The squirrel's tummy made a loud growling sound.

Rosie's heart sank. One tiny acorn wasn't enough. The squirrel was still hungry. She felt silly and mean.

"That didn't work," she whispered to herself. "I need to try again."

Rosie thought hard. What would make her feel better if she was hungry? She would want enough food to fill her tummy.

She ran back to her den. This time, she filled her paws with acorns. Big ones, medium ones, and small ones too.

"Squirrel!" she called. "Come back! I have more acorns!"

The squirrel turned around. His eyes grew wide and bright.

"Here," said Rosie. "Take all of these. Your tummy needs to feel full and happy."

The squirrel's face lit up like sunshine. "Really? For me?"

"Yes!" said Rosie. "I have plenty. We can share them."

They sat together under the oak tree. The squirrel ate acorn after acorn. His cheeks got nice and round.

"Mmm, these taste so good," he said. "Thank you, Rosie. You're very kind."

Rosie felt warm and glowy inside. It felt much better than keeping everything to herself.

"I'm sorry I only gave you one tiny acorn before," said Rosie. "That wasn't really sharing."

"It's okay," said the squirrel. "You learned how to share better. That's what matters."

Soon other forest friends came by. A rabbit needed pretty stones for her burrow. A hedgehog wanted smooth pebbles for his path.

Rosie shared with everyone. Her den got emptier, but her heart got fuller.

That night, Rosie curled up in her cozy den. She had fewer treasures now. But she had something much better. She had friends.

The next morning, Rosie found surprises outside her den. The squirrel left her sweet berries. The rabbit brought colorful flowers. The hedgehog gave her interesting leaves.

"Sharing feels wonderful," Rosie said happily. "When I share with others, they share with me too."

From that day on, Rosie always shared her treasures. The forest became a happier place for everyone.

And Rosie learned that the best treasures of all were friendship and kindness.

The end.
