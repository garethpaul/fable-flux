# Story story_1279

**Milo's Veggie Adventure**

Milo the monkey loved the playground. He swung on the monkey bars every day. He slid down the big yellow slide. He climbed the rope ladder up and down.

But Milo had a big problem. He did not like vegetables. Not carrots. Not broccoli. Not green beans. "Yuck!" he would say. "Vegetables taste bad!"

Every day, Mama Monkey packed his lunch. She put in bananas and nuts. She also put in fresh carrots and green peas. Milo always ate the bananas first. Then he ate the crunchy nuts. But the vegetables? He threw them in the trash.

"Milo needs energy to play," said Mama Monkey. "Vegetables help monkeys grow strong."

One sunny morning, Milo felt very tired. His arms felt weak on the monkey bars. His legs felt wobbly on the slide. "Why am I so tired?" he wondered.

His friend Bella the bunny hopped over. "Hi Milo! Want to race to the swings?"

"I'm too tired," said Milo sadly. He sat on a bench. His tummy rumbled loudly.

Bella looked at Milo's lunch box. "Where are your vegetables?" she asked.

"I threw them away," said Milo. "They taste yucky."

"But vegetables give you energy!" said Bella. "They help you play longer. They make you feel strong!"

Milo thought about this. Maybe Bella was right. Maybe he needed vegetables to feel better.

**First Try**

Milo decided to try eating vegetables. He opened his lunch box. Inside were bright orange carrots. He picked one up. It felt smooth and cold. He took a tiny bite.

"Blech!" The carrot tasted bitter and hard. Milo made a funny face. He spit it out right away. "This is terrible!" he said.

Bella giggled. "Try something else," she said kindly.

Milo looked at the green peas. They were small and round. He put one in his mouth. It was mushy and strange. "Even worse!" he cried. He spit out the pea too.

"I can't do this," said Milo. "Vegetables are just too yucky." He felt sad and frustrated.

**Second Try**

The next day, Milo still felt tired. He watched other animals play. They had lots of energy. They ran and jumped and laughed.

"I want to play like that," thought Milo.

Then he had an idea. "Maybe I can make vegetables taste better!"

Milo asked Mama Monkey for help. "Can we try cooking vegetables differently?" he asked.

Mama Monkey smiled. "Of course! Let's experiment!"

They went to the kitchen together. Mama Monkey cut carrots into fun star shapes. She roasted them with a little honey. The kitchen smelled sweet and warm.

"These smell good!" said Milo. His nose twitched with excitement.

Mama Monkey also steamed broccoli until it was bright green. She added a tiny bit of cheese. The cheese melted and looked creamy.

"Want to try now?" asked Mama Monkey.

Milo felt nervous but excited. He picked up a star-shaped carrot. It felt warm in his fingers. He took a small bite.

"Mmm!" The carrot was sweet and soft. "This tastes like candy!" he said happily.

Next, he tried the cheesy broccoli. It was creamy and mild. "This is actually good!" he said with surprise.

**Happy Ending**

The next day at the playground, Milo felt amazing. His arms were strong on the monkey bars. His legs were steady on the slide. He had energy to play all morning!

"Race you to the swings!" called Bella.

"You're on!" laughed Milo. He ran fast and felt wonderful.

At lunch time, Milo opened his box eagerly. Inside were honey carrots and cheesy broccoli. He ate every single bite.

"How do you feel?" asked Bella.

"Strong and happy!" said Milo. "Vegetables aren't scary when they taste good!"

From that day on, Milo tried new vegetables every week. Sometimes he liked them. Sometimes he didn't. But he kept trying different ways to cook them.

"I learned something important," Milo told Mama Monkey. "If something seems hard, I can find a new way to try it!"

Mama Monkey hugged him tight. "You're my brave little monkey," she said proudly.

And Milo played happily ever after, strong and full of energy!

The end.
