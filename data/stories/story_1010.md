# Story story_1010

**Luna and the Sleepy Star**

Luna loved playing with her best friend Maya. They built tall block towers. They drew pictures with bright crayons. They giggled and ran around the yard.

But when the sun went down, Luna felt sad. "I don't want bedtime," she told Maya. "Bedtime means you have to go home."

Maya's mom came to pick her up. "Time to go, sweetie," she called.

Luna's bottom lip wobbled. "Can't Maya stay longer? Please?"

"Tomorrow is another day for playing," said Luna's mom gently. "But now it's time to get ready for bed."

Maya gave Luna a big hug. "I don't want to leave either," she whispered.

Luna dragged her feet to her bedroom. She put on her soft pajamas slowly. She brushed her teeth with tiny scrubs. Everything felt heavy and sad.

"I wish Maya could stay forever," Luna sighed.

Mom tucked Luna into her cozy bed. The sheets smelled like lavender. "Close your eyes, little one," Mom said softly.

Luna squeezed her eyes shut. But she couldn't fall asleep. She missed Maya too much.

Suddenly, something twinkled outside her window. A tiny golden light danced in the air. It sparkled like glitter.

"What's that?" Luna whispered.

The light grew bigger and brighter. It floated through her open window. The light felt warm and friendly.

"Hello, Luna," said a tiny voice. "I'm a Sleepy Star."

Luna sat up in amazement. The star was no bigger than her thumb. It glowed soft yellow like butter.

"Why are you sad?" asked the Sleepy Star.

"My friend had to go home," Luna explained. "I miss her already."

The Sleepy Star smiled. "Would you like to see something special?"

Luna nodded eagerly.

The star touched Luna's forehead gently. Suddenly, Luna could see through her bedroom walls. She saw Maya in her own bed across town.

Maya was holding a stuffed elephant. She was smiling in her sleep.

"Maya is dreaming about playing with you," whispered the Sleepy Star. "Look closer."

In Maya's dream, Luna could see herself! Dream-Maya and Dream-Luna were having a tea party. They were laughing and sharing cookies.

"Even when you're apart, you're still together," said the Sleepy Star. "Friends connect through dreams."

Luna felt warm and happy inside. "Really?"

"Every night, friends visit each other in dreams," the star explained. "You can play together while you sleep."

The Sleepy Star sprinkled golden dust around Luna's room. "Now you can dream with Maya too."

Luna felt her eyelids getting heavy. But this time, she wasn't sad. She was excited.

"Will I see Maya in my dreams?" she asked sleepily.

"Close your eyes and find out," said the Sleepy Star.

Luna snuggled deep into her pillow. The golden dust made her feel cozy and safe. She drifted off to sleep with a smile.

In her dream, Luna found herself in a magical playground. Maya was there, sliding down a rainbow slide. They played on swings that touched the clouds. They picked flowers that sang little songs.

"This is the best!" Dream-Luna laughed.

"We can play here every night!" Dream-Maya giggled.

When Luna woke up in the morning, she felt wonderful. She remembered every bit of her magical dream.

At breakfast, the phone rang. It was Maya!

"Luna! I had the most amazing dream," Maya said excitedly. "We played in a magical playground together!"

Luna gasped. "I had the same dream! We went down a rainbow slide!"

"And picked singing flowers!" Maya added.

Both girls laughed with joy.

"Can you come over today?" Luna asked.

"Yes! And tonight we can meet in our dreams again," Maya said happily.

Luna couldn't wait for bedtime anymore. She knew that sleep meant adventure. It meant visiting her best friend in magical places.

That evening, Luna put on her pajamas with excitement. She brushed her teeth quickly. She hopped into bed with a big smile.

"Ready for another dream adventure?" Mom asked.

"Yes!" Luna said. "Maya and I are going to explore a castle made of clouds!"

Luna closed her eyes eagerly. She couldn't wait to see what wonderful dreams would bring.

The end.
