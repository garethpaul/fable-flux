# Story story_672

**The Little Squirrel and the Big Elephant**

Nutkin the squirrel sat on a tall oak tree. He watched the colorful flowers dance in the breeze. The garden smelled like sweet roses and fresh grass. But Nutkin felt sad and lonely.

Every morning, Nutkin played by himself. He would chase his fluffy tail in circles. He would jump from branch to branch. He would collect shiny acorns in neat little piles.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," Nutkin sighed softly.

One sunny morning, the ground began to shake. Thump, thump, thump! Heavy footsteps came closer and closer. Nutkin's whiskers twitched with worry.

A gentle gray elephant appeared through the tall sunflowers. Her name was Ellie. She had kind brown eyes and a long, wiggly trunk. Ellie looked up at the little squirrel.

"Hello there!" Ellie called in a warm voice. "I'm new to this garden. Would you like to be friends?"

Nutkin's bushy tail trembled with excitement. But then he looked down at Ellie's huge size. He looked at his own tiny paws.

"We're so different," Nutkin said quietly. "You're big and I'm small. How can we play together?"

Ellie's trunk curled into a gentle smile. "Different can be wonderful! Let's try playing together."

First, they tried playing hide-and-seek. Ellie closed her eyes and counted to ten. Nutkin scampered behind a daisy flower. But his fluffy tail stuck out like a gray feather.

"Found you!" Ellie giggled, making a happy trumpet sound.

When it was Nutkin's turn to seek, Ellie tried hiding behind a tiny rosebush. Her big gray body showed from every side. Nutkin burst into giggles.

"I can see you, Ellie!" he chattered happily.

Next, they tried having a race. Ellie's big feet went thump, thump, thump on the soft dirt. Nutkin's little paws went pitter-patter, pitter-patter on the ground.

Ellie was much faster with her long legs. But she slowed down so Nutkin could keep up. They crossed the finish line together, both breathing hard and laughing.

"That was fun!" Nutkin squeaked with joy.

Then Ellie had a wonderful idea. "I can help you reach the highest branches!" she said. "Climb on my back!"

Nutkin carefully climbed onto Ellie's warm, gray back. She lifted him high with her strong trunk. Up, up, up he went! Nutkin could see the whole beautiful garden from way up there.

"Look at all the pretty butterflies!" Nutkin pointed with his tiny paw.

Orange and yellow butterflies danced around them. The flowers below looked like a rainbow carpet. The view was magical and amazing.

Nutkin helped Ellie too. He used his quick paws to pick juicy apples from the high branches. He dropped them down to his big friend below.

"Thank you, Nutkin!" Ellie munched the sweet, crunchy apples happily.

As the golden sun began to set, they sat together under the oak tree. Ellie's trunk gently patted Nutkin's soft fur. Nutkin shared his favorite acorns with his new friend.

"You know what?" Nutkin said with a big smile. "Being different makes us a perfect team!"

Ellie nodded her wise head. "I can reach high places, and you can fit in small spaces. Together, we can do anything!"

The fireflies began to twinkle like tiny stars. The garden smelled like evening jasmine. Both friends felt warm and happy inside.

From that day on, Nutkin and Ellie played together every single day. They had tea parties under the roses. They splashed in the garden pond. They told each other funny stories and shared delicious snacks.

The little squirrel never felt lonely again. The big elephant had found her very best friend. Their friendship filled the whole garden with joy and laughter.

"Best friends come in all sizes," they would tell the other garden animals. And everyone agreed that Nutkin and Ellie were the happiest pair in the whole wide world.

The end.
