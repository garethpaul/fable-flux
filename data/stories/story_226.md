# Jack's Big Day

Jack sat on the soft blue couch in his living room. His teddy bear, Mr. Buttons, was tucked under his arm. Tomorrow was his first day at a new school.

"My tummy feels funny," Jack whispered to Mr. Buttons. He wiggled his toes in his fuzzy socks. The feeling in his belly was like butterflies dancing around.

Jack's mom sat down beside him. "What's wrong, sweetie?" she asked gently.

"I'm nervous about school," Jack said quietly. "What if the kids don't like me? What if I get lost?"

Mom gave Jack a warm hug. "Those are big feelings," she said. "Let's think of ways to help."

Jack thought hard. He had an idea! "Maybe I can practice," he said. He jumped up from the couch.

First, Jack pretended the hallway was the school hallway. He walked back and forth, counting his steps. "One, two, three, four," he said out loud. Walking felt good. His legs felt strong.

Next, Jack practiced introducing himself. He stood in front of the big mirror by the door. "Hi, I'm Jack," he said to his reflection. "I like dinosaurs and building blocks."

The boy in the mirror smiled back at him. That made Jack giggle.

But his tummy still felt wiggly. Jack tried something else. He got his favorite book about a brave little mouse. Maybe reading would help him feel better.

Jack curled up on the carpet with Mr. Buttons. The soft rug felt cozy under his legs. He read about the mouse who was scared to explore a new garden. The mouse tried and tried until he made friends with a friendly frog.

"Just like me!" Jack said to Mr. Buttons. "I can try too."

Jack had another idea. He ran to his toy box. Inside were his wooden blocks and toy cars. He built a pretend school on the living room floor.

"This is the classroom," Jack said, making a square with red blocks. "This is the playground," he added, using green blocks for grass.

Jack drove his little blue car around his pretend school. "Beep beep! Jack is here!" he said in a silly voice. Mr. Buttons watched from the couch.

Playing made Jack feel braver. But he still had butterflies in his tummy.

Dad came home from work. "How's my boy?" he asked, seeing Jack's block school.

"I'm nervous about tomorrow," Jack explained. "My tummy feels funny."

Dad sat on the floor next to Jack's creation. "You know what helps me when I'm nervous?" Dad asked.

"What?" Jack wondered.

"I think of three good things that might happen," Dad said. "Want to try?"

Jack nodded. He thought carefully. "Maybe I'll meet a new friend," he said slowly.

"That's one!" Dad encouraged.

"Maybe my teacher will be nice," Jack added.

"That's two! One more."

Jack smiled. "Maybe I'll learn something really cool, like about space or animals!"

"That's three!" Dad clapped his hands. "See? Good things can happen too."

Jack felt the butterflies in his tummy slow down. They weren't dancing as much anymore.

Mom came back with Jack's favorite snack - apple slices with peanut butter. The three of them sat together on the living room floor, eating and talking.

"You know what, Jack?" Mom said. "Being nervous is okay. It means you care about doing well."

"Really?" Jack asked, crunching his apple.

"Really," Dad agreed. "Even grown-ups get nervous sometimes. The brave thing is trying anyway."

Jack hugged Mr. Buttons tight. "Will you come with me tomorrow?" he asked his teddy bear.

"Mr. Buttons can wait in your backpack," Mom suggested. "He'll be right there if you need him."

That night, Jack put his school clothes next to his bed. He packed his backpack with Mr. Buttons inside. The butterflies in his tummy were much quieter now.

"I practiced walking. I practiced talking. I thought of good things," Jack whispered to himself. "I'm ready to try."

Jack fell asleep feeling proud. He had worked hard to feel braver. Tomorrow would be an adventure, and he was ready for it.

Sometimes the best way to feel better is to try different solutions until you find what works. Jack learned that being nervous was normal, but being brave meant trying anyway.

The end.
