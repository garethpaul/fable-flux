# The Brother and the Wise Little Owl

Tommy sat on the warm sand. He watched the waves roll in. The beach was so big and quiet. He felt very lonely today.

Tommy picked up some shells. He tried to build a castle. But the sand kept falling down. "This is too hard," he sighed.

Just then, a small brown owl landed nearby. The owl had big golden eyes. His feathers looked soft and fluffy.

"Hoot! Hello there," said the owl. "I'm Oliver. You look sad."

Tommy looked up in surprise. "Animals can talk here?"

Oliver nodded and smiled. "Beach animals are special! What's wrong?"

"I'm trying to build a castle," Tommy said. "But I can't do it alone. The sand won't stay up."

Oliver hopped closer. "May I help you? I love building things!"

Tommy's eyes lit up. "Really? You want to help me?"

"Of course!" Oliver flapped his wings. "Two friends work better than one!"

Tommy showed Oliver his pile of shells. "I want to make towers," he explained.

Oliver had a great idea. "I can fly high! I'll drop wet sand from above. You pack it down below!"

They started working together. Oliver flew up with his tiny claws full of wet sand. He dropped it carefully where Tommy pointed.

Tommy packed the sand tight. "This is working!" he cheered.

Soon they had one tall tower. Then another! Their castle grew bigger and bigger.

"Let's add a moat!" suggested Oliver.

Tommy dug a circle around the castle. Oliver brought water in a big shell. They filled the moat together.

"We need a bridge," said Tommy.

They found a long piece of driftwood. Oliver held one end steady. Tommy placed the other end carefully.

"Perfect!" they both said at once.

Then they started decorating. Tommy arranged colorful shells on the walls. Oliver found smooth pebbles for the path.

"Look what we made!" Tommy said proudly.

Their castle was beautiful! It had three tall towers. Pretty shells sparkled in the sun. The moat shimmered blue.

"I couldn't have done this alone," Tommy admitted.

"Me neither," said Oliver. "We make a great team!"

They sat down to admire their work. The waves made gentle sounds. Seagulls called overhead.

"Want to play another game?" asked Oliver.

"Yes! What should we do?"

Oliver pointed to some flat stones. "Let's skip rocks! I can show you how."

Tommy had never skipped rocks before. Oliver demonstrated first. He picked up a smooth, flat stone.

"Throw it low and fast," Oliver explained. "Make it spin!"

Tommy tried. His first rock just plopped in the water. But Oliver encouraged him.

"Try again! You're learning!"

On his third try, Tommy's rock bounced twice! "I did it!" he shouted.

Oliver clapped his wings. "Wonderful! Now try for three bounces!"

They took turns skipping stones. Each throw got better. They cheered for each other.

The sun began to set. The sky turned orange and pink. Their shadows grew long on the sand.

"This was the best day ever," said Tommy.

"I agree," said Oliver. "I was lonely too. Flying alone isn't much fun."

Tommy smiled at his new friend. "Will you come back tomorrow?"

"Of course! We can build an even bigger castle!"

They looked at their beautiful creation one more time. The castle stood proud and tall. Waves gently touched the moat.

"Thank you for helping me," said Tommy.

"Thank you for being my friend," said Oliver.

They hugged goodbye. Oliver flew to his tree. Tommy waved until he couldn't see him anymore.

Walking home, Tommy felt so happy. His heart was warm and full. He had learned something important today.

Working together made everything better! Playing with a friend was much more fun than playing alone.

Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough. Tommy already had ideas for their next castle. Maybe they could build a whole village!

He looked back at the beach one more time. Their castle stood strong against the sunset. It was proof of their new friendship.

Tommy smiled all the way home. He wasn't lonely anymore.

The end.
