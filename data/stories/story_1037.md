# Story story_1037

**Danny's Drum Adventure**

The big clock on the wall said three o'clock. Music time was starting soon. All the instruments sat quietly on their shelf. The shiny trumpet gleamed in the sunlight. The wooden flute lay still and straight. But Danny the drum felt worried.

"I don't want to make noise today," Danny whispered. His red and gold surface felt tight. "What if I'm too loud? What if the children don't like my sound?"

The other instruments looked at Danny with kind eyes. "Music time is fun," said Bella the bell. Her silver voice tinkled softly. "The children always smile when we play together."

But Danny rolled backward on his stand. He wanted to hide behind the big piano. His drumsticks felt heavy in their holder. "I'm scared," he said in a small voice.

Just then, little Emma walked into the music room. Her eyes were red from crying. She had scraped her knee at recess. The bandage was bright pink with purple stars.

Emma sat down in front of Danny's shelf. She sniffled quietly. A tear rolled down her cheek and dropped onto the floor.

Danny felt something warm in his middle. Emma looked so sad. Maybe he could help somehow. But how could a drum make someone feel better?

"Try me," Danny whispered, though Emma couldn't hear his words.

Emma reached up slowly. Her small fingers touched Danny's smooth surface. Tap, tap, tap. The sound was gentle and soft.

Something magical happened then. Danny felt a tingle spread across his drum skin. The taps sounded like raindrops on a roof. They sounded like a mama's heartbeat. They sounded like comfort.

Emma's eyes opened wide. She tapped again, a little harder this time. Boom, boom, boom. The sound was warm and strong.

"That sounds like my daddy's footsteps," Emma said. She smiled for the first time all day. "Coming home from work."

Danny felt joy bubble up inside him. His sound was helping! He wasn't too loud or scary. He was perfect just the way he was.

More children came into the music room. They heard Danny's cheerful beats. Soon, little hands were clapping along. Feet were tapping on the carpet.

"Let's all play together!" called Mrs. Rose, the music teacher. She had curly hair and a bright yellow dress.

The children picked up shakers and bells. They grabbed triangles and wooden blocks. Danny led them all with his steady beat.

Boom-tap-boom-tap! The rhythm filled the room. The children giggled and danced. Emma's sad face turned into a big, bright smile.

"This is the best music time ever!" shouted Tommy, shaking his maracas high in the air.

Danny felt proud and happy. His beats were strong and sure now. He wasn't scared anymore. Making music with friends was wonderful.

The children sang along to Danny's rhythm. Their voices were sweet and clear. Some were high like little birds. Some were low like friendly bears.

When music time ended, Emma gave Danny a gentle pat. "Thank you for making me feel better," she whispered. "Your music sounds like a hug."

Danny's heart felt full and warm. He had discovered something amazing. His drum voice wasn't something to hide. It was a gift to share.

As the children put away their instruments, Danny felt excited. Tomorrow there would be more music time. More children to comfort. More songs to play.

"See you tomorrow, Danny!" called Emma as she skipped out the door. Her scraped knee didn't hurt anymore.

Danny settled happily on his shelf. The afternoon sun made his red and gold surface shine like fire. He couldn't wait for tomorrow's music time.

"Making music is the best job in the world," Danny said to his instrument friends.

And all the instruments agreed with happy, musical sighs.

The room grew quiet, but it was a good quiet. It was full of tomorrow's music, waiting to begin.

The end.
