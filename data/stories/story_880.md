# Story story_880

**Muddy Moo's Bath Day**

On Sunny Hill Farm, there lived a little cow named Moo. Moo loved rolling in the mud. She loved splashing in puddles. She loved playing in the dirt all day long.

But Moo did not love baths.

One bright morning, Farmer Ben looked at Moo. "Oh my!" he said. "You are very muddy, Moo. Bath time!"

Moo's eyes grew wide. She shook her head. "No bath!" she mooed loudly. "No, no, no!"

Moo ran behind the big red barn. She hid behind the apple tree. She even tried hiding under the hay pile. But Farmer Ben always found her.

"Come on, Moo," said Farmer Ben gently. "The water is nice and warm."

But Moo just ran away again.

Lucy the pig watched from her pen. Lucy was Moo's best friend. She saw how scared Moo looked. Lucy wanted to help.

"I have an idea!" Lucy said to herself.

Lucy waddled over to the water trough. She jumped in with a big splash! "Look, Moo!" Lucy called. "Water is fun! Come play with me!"

The cool water felt wonderful on Lucy's pink skin. She splashed and giggled. She blew bubbles in the water. She made silly faces at Moo.

Moo watched from far away. The water did look fun. But she was still scared. "Water is too cold," Moo said. "Water is too scary."

Lucy climbed out of the trough. Her first idea didn't work. But Lucy didn't give up. Good friends never give up.

Lucy thought and thought. Then she had another idea!

"Wait here, Moo," Lucy said. She ran to the farmhouse as fast as her little legs could go.

Lucy found Farmer Ben's daughter, Emma. Emma was only five years old. She loved all the farm animals.

"Emma!" Lucy oinked. "Moo needs help!"

Emma couldn't understand pig language. But she saw Lucy pointing toward the barn. Emma followed Lucy outside.

Emma saw Moo hiding behind the fence. Moo looked very dirty and very sad.

"Poor Moo," Emma said softly. "Are you scared of your bath?"

Emma had an idea too. She ran to get her favorite yellow rubber duck. She also brought her bubble soap that smelled like strawberries.

"Look what I brought, Moo!" Emma said. She showed Moo the rubber duck. "This is Ducky. He loves baths!"

Emma put sweet-smelling bubbles in the water. She placed Ducky in the trough. The bubbles sparkled in the sunshine. They smelled like fresh strawberries.

"See?" Emma said. "Ducky is having fun!"

Moo stepped closer. The bubbles looked pretty. They smelled wonderful. Ducky looked very happy floating around.

"The water is warm like sunshine," Emma said. She put her hand in the water. "Feel it, Moo."

Moo touched the water with one hoof. It was warm! It felt nice on her muddy foot.

Lucy jumped back in the water. "Come on, Moo! It's wonderful!"

Slowly, very slowly, Moo stepped into the trough. The warm water felt good on her legs. The strawberry bubbles smelled amazing.

"This is nice," Moo said with surprise.

Emma gently poured water over Moo's back. The dirt washed away. Moo's black and white spots appeared clean and shiny.

Lucy splashed playfully next to her friend. Emma sang a happy song. Even Ducky seemed to dance in the bubbles.

"I like baths!" Moo mooed happily. "This is fun!"

When bath time was over, Moo sparkled in the sun. She felt fresh and clean and wonderful.

"Thank you, Lucy," Moo said. "You're the best friend ever."

"Thank you, Emma," Lucy oinked.

Emma hugged both animals. "You both tried so hard. That's what friends do!"

From that day on, Moo looked forward to bath time. She learned that trying new things can be fun. She learned that good friends help each other. And she learned that being clean felt just as good as being muddy.

Every bath day, Lucy and Emma helped make it special. Sometimes scary things become wonderful when you have friends who care.

The end.
