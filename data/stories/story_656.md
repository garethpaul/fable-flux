# Story story_656

**Anna and the Magic Music Time**

The big clock on the wall said three o'clock. Miss Sarah clapped her hands together. "Time for music!" she called out cheerfully.

All the children jumped up from their play mats. They ran to the colorful circle rug. Red drums sat in a neat row. Yellow tambourines sparkled in the sunlight. Blue maracas waited to be shaken.

But Anna stayed put. She sat very still by the toy kitchen. Her arms were crossed tight. Music time felt too loud. Too busy. Too much.

"Come join us, Anna," Miss Sarah said gently. Her voice was soft like a warm hug.

Anna shook her head. "I don't want to," she whispered. The drums looked scary. The other kids seemed so excited. What if she did it wrong?

Miss Sarah walked over slowly. She sat down next to Anna. "Would you like to watch first?" she asked.

Anna nodded just a tiny bit.

The music started. It was a gentle song about raindrops. Tommy tapped his drum softly. Tap, tap, tap. Like rain on the roof. Emma shook her tambourine. Jingle, jingle, jingle. Like wind chimes dancing.

Anna's foot started moving. Just a little wiggle. The music felt nice in her ears.

"The raindrops are getting bigger," Miss Sarah sang. The children played a bit louder. Anna watched their happy faces. They were smiling so wide.

Something magical began to happen. Anna felt the music in her chest. It made her heart feel warm and bouncy. Her shoulders started swaying back and forth.

"Would you like to try?" Miss Sarah held out small wooden blocks. They were smooth and painted bright green.

Anna looked at the blocks. They seemed friendly. Not scary at all. She reached out and took them.

Click, click, click. Anna tapped them together softly. The sound was crisp and clear. Like tiny footsteps on a sidewalk.

"Beautiful!" Miss Sarah smiled. "You're making rain sounds too."

The song changed. Now it was about sunshine after the rain. Anna clicked her blocks faster. Click-click-click! It felt like dancing with her hands.

Maya moved over to sit next to Anna. "I like your blocks," she said. "They sound happy."

Anna grinned. Her blocks did sound happy! She clicked them high above her head. Then down low by her knees. The music swirled all around her.

The children played together. Drums and tambourines. Maracas and blocks. Triangle bells that went ting-a-ling-ling. Anna closed her eyes and listened. Every sound fit together like puzzle pieces.

When the song ended, everyone was quiet. Anna felt peaceful inside. Like after a warm bath or a good story.

"How did that feel, Anna?" Miss Sarah asked.

"It felt like magic," Anna said. Her cheeks were pink and glowing. "The music lived in my body."

Miss Sarah nodded. "Music does that. It finds a home inside us."

Anna looked at her green blocks. "Can I use these again tomorrow?"

"Of course!" Miss Sarah said. "They'll be waiting for you."

As the children put away the instruments, Anna helped too. She placed her blocks gently in their special box. She patted them goodnight.

"See you tomorrow," she whispered to the blocks.

At snack time, Anna sat with Maya. "Music time was fun," Anna said. She took a bite of her apple. It crunched loudly.

"That sounds like music too," Maya giggled.

Anna listened to her crunching apple. Maya was right! Music was everywhere. In footsteps and water drops. In birds singing and leaves rustling.

When Mama came to pick her up, Anna ran to the door. "Mama! I played music today! I made rain sounds and sunshine sounds!"

"How wonderful," Mama said. "Tell me all about it."

As they walked to the car, Anna hummed the raindrop song. She couldn't wait for tomorrow. Music time would come again. And she would be ready.

Her green blocks would be waiting. The magic would happen again. Anna smiled all the way home.

Music lived inside her now.

The end.
