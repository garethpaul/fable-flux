# Story story_1214

**Zara's Walk Time**

"Walk time, Zara!" called Mama Zebra.

Zara the little zebra was playing with her toy blocks. She loved making tall towers. She did not want to stop playing.

"Do I have to?" Zara asked. Her black and white stripes drooped sadly.

"Yes, sweetie," said Mama. "Fresh air is good for you."

Zara sighed a big sigh. She put down her blocks. Walk time was so boring. All they did was walk around and around.

Mama and Zara stepped outside. The warm sun touched Zara's nose. Birds sang happy songs in the trees. But Zara still felt grumpy.

"My legs feel tired already," Zara said.

"We just started," laughed Mama gently.

They walked down the dusty path. Zara's hooves made soft sounds. Clip-clop, clip-clop. She dragged her feet slowly.

Then something sparkled in the grass. Zara stopped walking. She looked closer.

"Mama, what is that?" she asked.

A tiny fairy sat on a flower. The fairy had rainbow wings. She glowed like a little star.

"Hello, Zara!" said the fairy in a voice like tinkling bells.

Zara's eyes grew wide. "You know my name?"

"I know all the animals who walk here," smiled the fairy. "I'm Rosie. I make walks magical."

"Really?" asked Zara. Her ears perked up.

"Follow me!" said Rosie.

The fairy flew ahead. Her wings left sparkly dust in the air. Zara trotted faster to keep up.

"Look down at your hooves," said Rosie.

Zara looked down. Her hooves made colorful prints! Red, blue, yellow, green. Each step was a different color.

"Wow!" gasped Zara. She started prancing. Her hooves made a rainbow trail behind her.

"Now listen to the wind," said Rosie.

Zara stopped and listened. The wind whispered secrets. It told her about the clouds. It shared stories from faraway places.

"The wind says there are butterflies ahead," said Zara excitedly.

"Let's find them!" said Mama, smiling at her daughter's joy.

They walked faster now. Zara felt light and happy. Her legs didn't feel tired anymore.

Around the bend, hundreds of butterflies danced. Orange ones, purple ones, spotted ones too. They fluttered all around Zara.

"They like your stripes!" giggled Rosie.

One butterfly landed on Zara's nose. It tickled. Zara laughed out loud.

"This is the best walk ever!" she said.

They walked through a field of soft grass. The grass smelled sweet like honey. Zara rolled in it. The green blades felt cool on her back.

"Smell the flowers," suggested Rosie.

Zara sniffed the wildflowers. They smelled like sunshine and rain mixed together. Some were soft like velvet. Others had bumpy centers.

"I never noticed flowers before," said Zara.

They reached a babbling brook. The water sang a gentle song. Zara dipped her hooves in. The water felt cool and refreshing.

"The brook is happy to see you," said Rosie.

Fish swam up to say hello. They were silver and shiny. Zara watched them dance in the clear water.

"Walk time is almost over," said Mama.

"Already?" asked Zara. "But we just started!"

Rosie winked. "Time flies when you pay attention."

They walked back home. Zara noticed everything now. Buzzing bees, chirping crickets, rustling leaves. The world was full of wonders.

"Thank you, Rosie," said Zara.

"The magic was always here," said Rosie. "You just needed to look."

The fairy disappeared in a shower of sparkles.

At home, Zara felt happy and peaceful. Her body felt strong from walking. Her mind felt calm from all the beautiful things she saw.

"Mama," said Zara. "Can we have walk time again tomorrow?"

"Of course!" said Mama. "Same time, same path."

"I can't wait!" said Zara. She hugged Mama tight.

That night, Zara dreamed of rainbow hoofprints and butterfly friends. She couldn't wait for tomorrow's magical walk time.

The end.
