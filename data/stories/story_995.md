# Story story_995

**The Magic Dinner Blocks**

Lily loved her colorful blocks. She had red ones and blue ones. Yellow ones and green ones too. Every day after lunch, she built tall towers. She made castles and bridges. Playing with blocks was her favorite thing.

"Lily, time for dinner!" called Mama from the kitchen.

Lily looked at her beautiful block tower. It was taller than her teddy bear. She had worked so hard on it. The blocks fit together perfectly.

"Do I have to come now?" Lily asked. Her voice sounded sad.

"Yes, sweetie. Dinner is ready," Mama said gently.

Lily's shoulders drooped. She didn't want to leave her blocks. What if her tower fell down? What if someone moved her blocks?

She walked slowly to the kitchen. Her feet dragged on the floor. The kitchen smelled like warm soup and fresh bread. Usually, those smells made her happy. Today, she felt grumpy.

"I don't want dinner," Lily said. She crossed her arms.

Mama smiled and pointed to the table. "Look what I made for you."

Lily looked up. Her eyes grew wide. On her plate sat something amazing. Mama had cut her sandwich into squares. The squares looked just like blocks!

"Block sandwiches!" Lily gasped.

"That's right," Mama said. "And look at your cheese."

The cheese was cut into tiny cubes. They looked like yellow blocks too. Even her carrots were cut into small rectangles.

"Can I build with my food?" Lily asked.

"Of course," Mama laughed. "But remember to eat it too."

Lily picked up a cheese cube. She placed it on top of a sandwich square. Then she added a carrot block. Her dinner tower grew taller and taller.

"Look, Mama! A food castle!" Lily said.

She built walls with her sandwich blocks. She made windows with her carrots. The cheese cubes became tiny yellow doors.

"This is the best dinner ever," Lily said. She took a big bite of her sandwich block. It tasted like peanut butter and jelly. Her favorite!

Papa came home from work. He saw Lily's food creation. "Wow! What a wonderful block dinner," he said.

"I'm an architect," Lily said proudly. "I build with food blocks."

She ate her carrot windows. Crunch, crunch, crunch. They were sweet and crispy. The cheese doors were creamy and smooth. Everything tasted delicious.

"Can you teach me to build?" Papa asked.

Lily nodded eagerly. She showed Papa how to stack the sandwich squares. She taught him about making strong walls. They built a food city together.

"Building with food is fun," Papa said. "But playing with real blocks is fun too."

After dinner, Lily ran back to her block tower. It was still standing tall and proud. She felt happy that she had eaten dinner. She felt happy that her blocks waited for her.

"Tomorrow, can we have block breakfast?" Lily asked Mama.

"What a great idea," Mama said. "We could cut your toast into squares."

"And my fruit into cubes?" Lily asked hopefully.

"Yes, and your fruit into cubes," Mama agreed.

That night, Lily dreamed about blocks. She dreamed about food blocks and toy blocks. She dreamed about building the tallest tower in the world.

When morning came, Lily jumped out of bed. She ran to the kitchen. "Is it time for block breakfast?" she asked.

Mama was already cutting toast into perfect squares. The kitchen smelled like cinnamon and butter. Lily's stomach rumbled with hunger.

"I love block meals," Lily said. She hugged Mama tight.

From that day on, Lily never minded leaving her blocks for meals. She knew that eating could be just as fun as playing. She knew her blocks would always wait for her.

And every meal became a new building adventure.

The end.
