# Story story_1201

**Anna and the Messy Library Corner**

Anna loved the children's corner at the library. Soft blue carpet covered the floor. Colorful books lined the low shelves. Best of all, there were toys everywhere!

Anna pulled out wooden blocks. She built a tall tower. Then she found puzzles with big pieces. She dumped them on the carpet. Next came the stuffed animals. A brown bear. A yellow duck. A spotted dog.

"I'm having the best day ever!" Anna said.

She played and played. The carpet disappeared under toys. Blocks scattered everywhere. Puzzle pieces mixed together. Books lay open on the floor.

Mrs. Chen, the librarian, walked over. She had kind eyes and a gentle smile. "Anna, it's almost closing time," she said softly. "Time to clean up, please."

Anna looked around. Toys covered everything! Her stomach felt funny. Her hands got sweaty.

"There are too many toys," Anna whispered. "I can't clean all this up!"

She sat down hard on the carpet. Her bottom lip stuck out. Tears filled her eyes.

"It's too much work," Anna said. "I don't want to do it."

Mrs. Chen sat down beside her. "I understand. Big messes can feel scary."

Anna nodded. The mess looked like a toy mountain. Where should she even start?

"Maybe I could just leave it," Anna said hopefully.

Mrs. Chen shook her head gently. "Other children come tomorrow. They need a clean space to play."

Anna sighed. She had to try something.

**First Try**

Anna stood up. She grabbed armfuls of toys. Blocks clinked together. Puzzle pieces scattered more. The stuffed bear fell on her foot.

"Ow!" Anna cried. She dropped everything. The mess got bigger!

Anna's face turned red. Her eyes filled with tears again. "See? I told you it was too hard!"

She plopped back down. The carpet felt soft under her. But the mess still surrounded her like a colorful tornado.

Mrs. Chen watched quietly. She didn't look angry. She looked patient and understanding.

"That was a good try," Mrs. Chen said. "But maybe there's another way."

**Second Try**

Mrs. Chen pointed to a red basket. "What if we start with just one type of toy?"

Anna wiped her nose. "Just one type?"

"Yes. Pick your favorite toy. Put all of those away first."

Anna looked around. She spotted the wooden blocks. They were smooth and colorful. She loved how they felt in her hands.

"Blocks!" Anna said.

She crawled around the carpet. One block at a time. Red block in the basket. Blue block in the basket. Yellow block in the basket.

Soon all the blocks were away! Anna smiled. The mess looked smaller already.

"What's next?" Anna asked.

"You choose," Mrs. Chen said.

Anna picked puzzle pieces. She found every piece from the farm puzzle. Then every piece from the ocean puzzle. Her fingers felt each smooth edge.

The carpet started showing again! Anna could see the pretty blue color.

Next came the stuffed animals. The bear smelled like vanilla. The duck felt silky soft. The dog had floppy ears that tickled her palm.

"Look!" Anna pointed. "I can see the whole carpet now!"

Finally, Anna stacked the books carefully. She loved their colorful covers. Some felt smooth. Others had bumpy letters she could trace.

**Happy Ending**

The children's corner looked perfect again. Clean carpet. Organized shelves. Everything in its place.

Anna stood with her hands on her hips. She felt proud and strong.

"I did it!" she announced. "I cleaned everything up!"

Mrs. Chen clapped her hands. "You certainly did! You didn't give up."

Anna beamed. Her cheeks felt warm with happiness. "It wasn't too hard after all."

"Big jobs feel easier when we break them into small pieces," Mrs. Chen explained.

Anna nodded wisely. "Like putting away one toy type at a time!"

"Exactly right."

As they walked to the door, Anna turned back. The children's corner looked beautiful and ready for tomorrow's visitors.

"I can't wait to play here again," Anna said. "And I know how to clean up now!"

Mrs. Chen smiled. "You learned something very important today, Anna. When things seem too big, we can make them smaller. One step at a time."

Anna skipped toward the exit, already planning her next library adventure.

The end.
