# Story story_1456

**Lily's Missing Blocks**

Lily loved playing in the basement. It was her special place. The basement had soft carpet and warm lights. Her favorite toys lived down there.

Today, Lily wanted to build a castle. She needed her colorful blocks. The blocks were red, blue, yellow, and green. They made the best towers ever.

Lily looked on the toy shelf. No blocks there. She checked the toy box. Still no blocks. She looked under the couch. Nothing but dust bunnies.

"Where are my blocks?" Lily said out loud. Her voice echoed in the quiet basement. She felt worried. How could she build without her blocks?

Lily's tummy felt funny. She really wanted those blocks. Building castles was her favorite thing to do. The basement felt too quiet without the clicking sounds of blocks.

She decided to look harder. Maybe the blocks were hiding. Lily got on her hands and knees. She crawled around the basement floor.

Under the stairs was dark and dusty. Lily used her phone's flashlight. The bright light showed old boxes and spider webs. No blocks anywhere.

"This isn't working," Lily said sadly. She sat on the bottom step. Her chin rested in her hands. The basement felt bigger and emptier now.

Then Lily had a new idea. Maybe Mom knew where the blocks were. Mom always helped when things were lost.

"Mom!" Lily called up the stairs. "Can you help me please?"

Mom's footsteps came down the wooden stairs. Each step made a creaking sound. Mom's face appeared with a warm smile.

"What's wrong, sweetie?" Mom asked gently.

"I can't find my blocks anywhere," Lily said. "I looked and looked. They're gone!"

Mom sat down next to Lily. The step felt warm and safe. Mom smelled like cookies and soap.

"Let's think together," Mom said. "When did you last play with them?"

Lily closed her eyes to remember. "Yesterday! I built a big tower. It was taller than me!"

"Where did you build it?" Mom asked.

"Right here on the carpet," Lily pointed to the soft area.

Mom looked around carefully. Her eyes were like a detective's. Then she smiled a knowing smile.

"Did you clean up yesterday?" Mom asked.

Lily nodded. "I put everything away like you taught me."

"Show me where you put things away," Mom said.

Lily walked to the storage closet. She opened the white door. Inside were shelves full of games and toys. Everything was neat and organized.

"I put the blocks in here," Lily said. She pointed to a high shelf. "But I can't reach that shelf!"

Mom laughed a gentle laugh. "Mystery solved! You were so good at cleaning up. You put them up high and safe."

Mom reached up to the tall shelf. Her arms stretched way up high. She pulled down the familiar box of blocks.

"Here they are!" Mom said proudly.

Lily's face lit up like sunshine. "My blocks! They're back!" She hugged the box tight against her chest.

The blocks rattled and clinked inside. That was Lily's favorite sound. She could hardly wait to start building.

"Thank you, Mom!" Lily said. "I was so worried."

"You did great looking by yourself first," Mom said. "But sometimes we all need help. That's okay."

Mom went back upstairs with a wave. Lily sat on the soft carpet. She opened her box of wonderful blocks.

The blocks tumbled out in bright colors. Red ones, blue ones, yellow ones, and green ones. They felt smooth and cool in her hands.

Lily started building her castle. First the walls, then the towers. The blocks clicked together perfectly. Soon she had the most beautiful castle ever.

"I found my blocks!" Lily said happily. "And I learned something new."

She learned that looking hard was good. But asking for help was good too. Sometimes problems need two people to solve them.

Lily's castle grew taller and taller. The basement filled with happy building sounds. Everything was perfect again in her special basement place.

The end.
