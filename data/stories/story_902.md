# Story story_902

Emma heard Mama's voice from upstairs. "Bath time, Emma!" Mama called sweetly. Emma was playing with her toy cars on the living room rug. The red car was her favorite. She made zoom-zoom sounds as it raced around.

"Five more minutes?" Emma asked hopefully. She didn't want to stop playing. Bath time meant putting away her cars. Bath time meant getting wet and soapy. Emma wrinkled her nose. She liked being clean, but getting clean felt like work.

"Come on, little one," Mama said gently. "The water is nice and warm." Emma sighed a big sigh. She put down her red car carefully. Her feet felt heavy as she walked upstairs.

The bathroom smelled like lavender bubbles. Steam danced in the air. The bathtub looked very big and very wet. Emma touched the water with one finger. It was warm, just like Mama promised. But Emma still felt unsure.

"I don't want to get my hair wet," Emma said softly. Mama smiled and kissed Emma's forehead. "We'll be gentle," Mama promised. "Just like washing a precious doll."

Emma stepped into the tub slowly. The warm water felt nice on her toes. Then something wonderful happened. Mama squeezed the bubble bottle. Mountains of fluffy bubbles appeared! They sparkled like tiny rainbows. They smelled like flowers in spring.

"Wow!" Emma gasped. She had never seen so many bubbles before. They felt soft and tickly on her hands. She scooped up a handful. The bubbles looked like whipped cream. Emma giggled as they popped between her fingers.

"Look, Emma," Mama said with a smile. She shaped the bubbles into a funny hat. The hat sat perfectly on Emma's head. Emma laughed and laughed. She felt like a bubble princess in a fairy tale.

Emma started making her own bubble creations. She built a bubble castle on her knee. She made bubble mountains on her arms. Each bubble caught the light and shimmered. The bathroom felt magical and dreamy.

"Can we make bubble animals?" Emma asked excitedly. Mama's eyes twinkled with joy. Together they made a bubble elephant. They made a bubble butterfly. Emma clapped her hands with delight. Water splashed gently around her.

When it was time to wash her hair, Emma wasn't scared anymore. The warm water felt like a gentle rain. Mama's fingers were soft and careful. The shampoo smelled like sweet strawberries. Emma closed her eyes and pretended she was in a magical garden.

"You're such a brave girl," Mama said proudly. Emma felt warm and happy inside. Being brave felt good. Taking care of herself felt good too. She was growing up to be a big girl.

After the washing came the best part. Emma played with her rubber duck. The yellow duck bobbed and floated in the bubbly water. Emma made the duck swim through bubble islands. She quacked like a duck and made Mama laugh.

The bubbles slowly disappeared down the drain. But Emma's smile stayed bright and big. Her skin felt soft and clean. Her hair smelled like strawberries and sunshine. She felt fresh and cozy.

Mama wrapped Emma in a fluffy towel. The towel was warm from the dryer. It felt like a soft hug all around her body. Emma snuggled close to Mama. Bath time had been an adventure after all.

"That was fun!" Emma said happily. She couldn't believe how much she had enjoyed herself. The bubbles had made everything special and magical. Playing in the water had been like swimming in a cloud.

As Mama helped Emma into her pajamas, Emma asked an exciting question. "Can we have more bubbles tomorrow?" Mama smiled and nodded. "Of course, my little bubble princess."

Emma hugged Mama tight. She was already looking forward to tomorrow's bath time. Maybe they could make bubble dragons next time. Or bubble flowers. The possibilities felt endless and wonderful.

That night, Emma dreamed of dancing bubbles and magical baths. Bath time wasn't scary anymore. It was the most fun part of her day.

The end.
