# Story story_1106

**Lily's First Park Adventure**

"Time for the park, Lily!" called Mama.

Baby Lily sat on her soft blanket. She held her favorite teddy bear tight. The park sounded big and scary.

"I don't want to go," Lily whispered.

Mama smiled and picked her up. "We'll have fun together," she said gently.

Lily buried her face in Mama's warm shoulder. She could smell Mama's sweet perfume. It made her feel a little better.

They walked outside into the bright sunshine. Lily peeked over Mama's shoulder. The world looked so big and noisy.

At the park, Lily saw tall trees swaying. She heard children laughing and playing. Birds chirped high above in the branches.

"Look at all the fun things," Mama said softly.

Lily shook her head. She wanted to go home to her cozy room.

Mama sat on a bench under a shady tree. She kept Lily close on her lap. "We can just watch for now," Mama said.

Lily felt the warm breeze on her cheeks. She watched a red ball roll by. A little boy chased after it, giggling.

Then something magical happened.

A beautiful yellow butterfly landed right on Lily's tiny hand. Its wings felt soft like flower petals. The butterfly was bright as sunshine.

"Oh my!" whispered Mama. "It likes you, Lily."

Lily's eyes grew wide with wonder. The butterfly stayed very still. She could see pretty patterns on its delicate wings.

"Hello, little butterfly," Lily said in her small voice.

The butterfly fluttered its wings gently. Then it flew in a circle around Lily's head. She giggled for the first time all day.

"It's dancing with you!" Mama laughed.

The butterfly led them to a patch of colorful flowers. Pink roses smelled sweet and lovely. Purple violets felt soft under Lily's fingers.

More butterflies joined their new friend. Orange ones and white ones danced together. They made the flowers look like a rainbow.

Lily clapped her hands with joy. The butterflies seemed to dance faster. She laughed and laughed.

"This is magical," she told Mama.

A gentle duck waddled over from the pond. It had bright orange feet and soft brown feathers. The duck quacked hello to Lily.

"Quack, quack!" Lily tried to copy the sound.

The duck seemed to understand. It flapped its wings happily.

Mama helped Lily stand on wobbly legs. Together they walked to the duck pond. The water sparkled like diamonds in the sun.

Little fish swam near the surface. They made tiny ripples in the clear water. Lily pointed at each one she saw.

"Fish! Fish!" she said excitedly.

A family of ducks paddled by. The baby ducklings were fluffy and yellow. They followed their mama in a perfect line.

Lily waved at the ducklings. They seemed to wave back with their little wings.

The afternoon sun felt warm on Lily's face. She could hear happy sounds everywhere. Children playing, birds singing, leaves rustling.

She wasn't scared anymore.

Mama spread their blanket under the big oak tree. They shared crackers and apple juice. Everything tasted extra good outside.

The yellow butterfly came back to visit. It sat on Lily's knee like an old friend.

"Thank you for showing me the park," Lily whispered to it.

As the sun began to set, the sky turned pretty pink and orange. Lily felt sleepy in Mama's arms.

"Did you have fun today?" Mama asked.

Lily nodded with a big smile. "The butterfly was my friend," she said.

"The park has many friends waiting to meet you," Mama said.

They walked home slowly. Lily looked back at the park. She could still see the flowers dancing in the breeze.

"Can we come back tomorrow?" Lily asked hopefully.

"Of course we can," Mama promised.

That night, Lily dreamed of yellow butterflies and friendly ducks. She couldn't wait for another magical day at the park.

The park wasn't scary at all. It was full of wonderful surprises and new friends.

Tomorrow would bring another beautiful adventure.

The end.
