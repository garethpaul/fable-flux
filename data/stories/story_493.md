# Nutkin Learns to Share

Nutkin the squirrel loved the big blue pond. He sat on his favorite rock. The rock was warm and smooth. Nutkin watched fish swim below. He listened to birds sing above.

Every morning, Nutkin brought acorns to eat. He had a special hiding spot. Under the old oak tree, he kept his treasure. Nutkin had collected many, many acorns. His fluffy tail twitched with pride.

One sunny day, three little rabbits hopped over. Their names were Pip, Pop, and Penny. They looked very hungry. Their tummies made soft rumbling sounds.

"Hello, Nutkin," said Pip softly. "We smell something yummy. Do you have food?"

Nutkin's paws wrapped around his acorn bag. He pulled it closer to his chest. "These are MINE," he said quickly. "I worked very hard for them."

The rabbits' ears drooped down sadly. They hopped away without saying goodbye. Nutkin felt a funny feeling in his tummy. It wasn't hunger. It was something else.

Soon, two young ducks waddled over. They quacked hopefully at Nutkin. "We're so hungry," they said together. "Could you share just one acorn?"

Nutkin shook his head fast. "No, no, no! I need all my acorns. Winter is coming soon."

The ducks paddled away across the pond. Their heads hung low with disappointment. Nutkin watched them go. The funny feeling in his tummy grew bigger.

Nutkin tried to enjoy his lunch. He cracked open an acorn. *Crack!* But it didn't taste as good. He tried another one. *Crack!* Still not delicious like before.

A wise old turtle swam to shore. "Hello, young squirrel," she said kindly. "You look troubled today."

"I don't understand," Nutkin said sadly. "My acorns don't taste good anymore. And my tummy feels strange."

The turtle smiled gently. "Sometimes our hearts feel heavy. This happens when we keep everything to ourselves. Have you tried sharing today?"

"But what if I run out?" Nutkin worried. His tail twitched nervously back and forth.

"Let's try something small," suggested the turtle. "Share just one acorn. See how it feels."

Nutkin thought and thought. He looked at his big bag. It was very full of acorns. Maybe he could spare just one.

The hungry ducks were still nearby. Nutkin took a deep breath. He walked over slowly. "Would you like this acorn?" he asked quietly.

The ducks' eyes sparkled with joy! "Thank you, Nutkin!" they quacked happily. They shared the acorn together. *Munch, munch, munch!*

Something wonderful happened inside Nutkin's chest. The strange feeling disappeared completely! Instead, warm happiness bubbled up. It felt like sunshine in his heart.

The three little rabbits returned to the pond. They still looked very hungry. This time, Nutkin didn't hesitate. "Here are some acorns for you," he said cheerfully.

"Really?" gasped Pip with surprise.

"Really!" laughed Nutkin. He gave each rabbit two acorns.

The rabbits munched happily. "These are delicious!" said Pop. "Thank you for sharing, Nutkin!"

More animals gathered around the pond. Nutkin shared acorns with everyone. A chipmunk, two mice, and a friendly frog. Each time he shared, his heart felt lighter.

When the sun began to set, Nutkin counted his acorns. He still had plenty left! More than enough for winter. But the best part was different.

All his new friends stayed to play. They told funny stories and sang songs. The pond echoed with happy laughter. Nutkin had never felt so joyful.

"Thank you for teaching me," Nutkin told the wise turtle. "Sharing makes everything taste better!"

The turtle winked. "And it makes friends taste sweeter too."

From that day forward, Nutkin shared his acorns gladly. The pond became the happiest place in the forest. Every animal knew they could count on Nutkin's kindness.

And Nutkin learned the most wonderful secret of all: the more you share, the more happiness grows in your heart.
