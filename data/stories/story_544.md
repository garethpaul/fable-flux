# Story story_544

**Lucy's Big Move**

Lucy sat on the basement steps. Her eyes felt wet with tears. Tomorrow, her family would move far away. She would miss Mama so much.

The basement smelled like old books and dust. Lucy touched the cold stone wall. This was her favorite hiding spot. She came here when she felt scared or sad.

"I don't want to leave Mama," Lucy whispered. Her voice echoed in the quiet space.

Mama lived next door in the big yellow house. She had soft gray hair and warm hugs. Every day, Lucy visited Mama's garden. They picked tomatoes and fed the birds together.

Lucy wiped her nose with her sleeve. She had to find a way to stay close to Mama. There must be something she could do.

Lucy ran upstairs to find her mom. "Mom, can I live with Mama instead?" she asked.

Mom knelt down and hugged Lucy tight. "Oh sweetie, Mama is moving too. She's going to live near us in our new town."

Lucy's heart sank. "But what if I forget her?"

"You won't forget Mama," Mom said gently. "She loves you very much."

But Lucy still felt worried. She went back to the basement to think. The wooden stairs creaked under her feet. She sat in her corner and hugged her knees.

"There has to be something," Lucy said to herself.

Then Lucy had an idea. She would make Mama a special book! She could draw pictures of all their favorite memories. That way, Mama would never forget their fun times together.

Lucy found her art box. She got out colorful crayons and blank paper. She drew a picture of Mama's garden. She drew the red tomatoes and yellow flowers. But when she tried to draw Mama's face, it looked all wrong.

Lucy crumpled up the paper. "This is terrible!" she cried.

She tried again. This time, the drawing looked even worse. Lucy threw her crayons across the basement floor. They scattered with tiny clicking sounds.

Lucy put her head in her hands. Nothing was working. She felt more sad than before.

Then she heard footsteps on the stairs. "Lucy? Are you down there?" It was Mama's voice!

Mama came down slowly. Her shoes made soft tapping sounds. She sat next to Lucy on the cold floor.

"Mom told me you were upset," Mama said. She smelled like flowers and cookies.

"I wanted to make you something special," Lucy sniffled. "But I can't draw good pictures."

Mama looked at Lucy's crumpled drawings. She smoothed them out carefully. "These are beautiful, Lucy."

"Really?" Lucy looked up with surprised eyes.

"Really. But I have an even better idea." Mama's eyes twinkled. "What if we make something together?"

Mama pulled out a small camera from her pocket. "We can take pictures of our favorite places. Then we'll make a photo book together."

Lucy's face lit up like sunshine. "That's perfect!"

They went outside to Mama's garden. Click! They took a picture by the tomato plants. Click! They took a picture feeding the birds. Click! They took a silly picture making funny faces.

Back in the basement, they looked at all the photos. Lucy picked her favorites. Mama helped her glue them into a small book.

"Now we each get a copy," Mama said. "When you miss me, you can look at our book."

Lucy hugged the photo book tight. "And when you miss me, you can look at yours!"

"That's right, sweet girl." Mama kissed Lucy's forehead.

The next morning, Lucy didn't feel as sad about moving. She had her special book in her suitcase. She knew Mama would always be close in her heart.

As the moving truck drove away, Lucy waved at the yellow house. She couldn't wait to make new memories with Mama in their new town.

Sometimes the best solutions come when we ask for help. Lucy learned that working together makes everything better.

The end.
