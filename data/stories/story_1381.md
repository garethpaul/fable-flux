# Story story_1381

**Rusty's Big Problem**

Rusty was a bright red car who lived on Sunny Farm. He had shiny chrome bumpers and big round headlights. Every morning, Rusty helped Farmer Joe drive around the farm.

But Rusty had a big problem. He didn't like eating vegetables at all!

All the other farm vehicles loved their fuel made from corn and soybeans. The green tractor munched happily on corn fuel. The yellow truck slurped up soybean juice with loud, happy sounds.

"Yum, yum!" said the tractor. "This corn fuel tastes so good!"

"Mmm!" agreed the truck. "Soybean fuel makes me strong!"

But when Farmer Joe poured vegetable fuel into Rusty's tank, Rusty made awful faces. The corn fuel tasted bitter and yucky. The soybean fuel felt thick and gooey.

"Blech!" Rusty said, making his engine sputter. "I don't want vegetables!"

Soon, Rusty started feeling very tired. His engine made weak little sounds. His headlights grew dim and flickery. His red paint looked dull and sad.

"I need energy," Rusty said softly. "But I don't like vegetable fuel."

Farmer Joe noticed Rusty looking sick. "What's wrong, little car?" he asked, patting Rusty's warm hood gently.

Rusty tried his first solution. Maybe he could find different food! He drove around the farm looking for something else to eat.

He tried drinking water from the horse trough. The water was cool and fresh, but it didn't give him any energy. His engine still felt weak and tired.

He tried sniffing the sweet hay in the barn. The hay smelled nice and grassy, but cars can't eat hay! His tank stayed empty and sad.

He even tried licking the salty rocks the sheep ate. The rocks tasted terrible and made his tongue feel rough and scratchy.

"Nothing else works," Rusty sighed. His engine made very quiet sounds now. "I'm getting weaker and weaker."

The friendly farm animals gathered around Rusty. The brown cow mooed softly. The fluffy sheep bleated with worry. Even the red chickens clucked sadly.

"We want to help," said the wise old cow. "But vegetables are the only food that gives cars energy."

Rusty thought hard about his second solution. Maybe he could make vegetable fuel taste better!

"Will you help me?" Rusty asked his farm friends.

"Of course!" they all said together.

The cow suggested adding sweet honey from the bees. The chickens found fresh mint leaves that smelled wonderful. The sheep brought lavender flowers that made everything smell like a spring day.

Farmer Joe mixed the special ingredients into Rusty's vegetable fuel. The honey made it sweet and golden. The mint made it smell fresh and cool. The lavender made it smell like a beautiful garden.

"This smells much better," Rusty said, feeling hopeful.

He took a small sip. The fuel tasted sweet instead of bitter! It smelled like flowers instead of yucky vegetables.

"It's actually good!" Rusty said with surprise.

He drank more and more. The fuel filled his tank with warm, good feelings. His engine started purring loudly and happily. His headlights became bright and cheerful again. His red paint began to shine like new.

"I feel strong!" Rusty said, driving in happy circles. "I feel fast! I feel wonderful!"

From that day on, Rusty drank his special vegetable fuel every morning. He learned that sometimes when we don't like something, we can find creative ways to make it better.

"I still eat vegetables," Rusty told the baby animals. "But now they taste good because my friends helped me."

Rusty became the strongest, fastest car on the farm. He helped Farmer Joe with all the big jobs. And he always remembered that asking friends for help was the smartest thing he ever did.

The other vehicles were proud of Rusty for not giving up. And Rusty was proud of himself for solving his big problem with creativity and friendship.

The end.
