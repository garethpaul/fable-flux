# Story story_1255

**Maya's Missing Doll**

Maya the monkey loved her swing set. She swung high every morning. The red swing was her favorite spot. Today felt extra special because she brought Rosie.

Rosie was Maya's pretty doll. She had soft yellow hair. Her blue dress sparkled in the sun. Maya held Rosie tight as she swung.

"Wheee!" Maya laughed. "We're flying, Rosie!"

The wind felt cool on Maya's face. Birds sang happy songs above. Maya pushed her feet to swing higher. She wanted to touch the fluffy white clouds.

Suddenly, Maya's hands slipped. Rosie flew out of her arms! The pretty doll sailed through the air. She landed far below in the tall grass.

"Oh no!" Maya cried. "Rosie!"

Maya jumped off her swing. She ran to where Rosie fell. But the grass was so tall! It reached Maya's tummy. She could not see her doll anywhere.

Maya got down on her hands and knees. She crawled through the green grass. The blades tickled her nose. They made her sneeze.

"Rosie? Where are you?" Maya called softly.

She pushed the grass apart with her hands. She looked left and right. She looked behind every rock. But Rosie was nowhere to be found.

Maya sat down and felt very sad. Tears rolled down her furry cheeks. She sniffled and wiped her nose.

"I lost my best doll," she whispered.

Just then, Papa Monkey came outside. He saw Maya sitting in the tall grass. Her shoulders were shaking as she cried.

"What's wrong, little one?" Papa asked gently.

"I dropped Rosie from my swing," Maya said. "Now she's lost forever!"

Papa sat down next to Maya. He put his warm arm around her. "Let's think about this together," he said.

Maya looked up at Papa with hopeful eyes. "How can we find her?"

"Well," Papa said, "sometimes we need to look from a different place."

Maya thought hard. She looked at her swing set. Then she looked at the tall grass. An idea popped into her head!

"Papa! What if I climb up high?" Maya asked excitedly.

"That's smart thinking!" Papa smiled.

Maya scrambled up the swing set pole. Her strong monkey hands gripped tight. She climbed higher and higher. Soon she was at the very top.

From up high, Maya could see everything! She could see over all the tall grass. The green blades looked like a soft carpet below.

Maya looked carefully in every direction. She shaded her eyes with her hand. The morning sun was bright and warm.

Then she saw something blue! It was tiny but sparkly. Right by the big oak tree!

"There she is!" Maya shouted. "I can see Rosie!"

Maya climbed down quickly but safely. She ran through the grass straight to the oak tree. There was Rosie! Her blue dress was a little dusty. But she was perfectly fine.

Maya picked up her doll and hugged her tight. "I found you, Rosie! I missed you so much!"

She brushed the dust off Rosie's pretty dress. The doll's yellow hair still felt soft and silky.

Papa Monkey walked over with a big smile. "You didn't give up," he said proudly. "You tried a new way to solve your problem."

Maya beamed with happiness. "I climbed high to see far!"

"That's right," Papa said. "Sometimes when we're stuck, we need to try looking from somewhere new."

Maya carried Rosie back to the swing set. This time, she held her doll extra carefully. She sat gently on her red swing.

"We're safe now, Rosie," Maya whispered to her doll.

As Maya swung slowly back and forth, she felt proud. She had solved a big problem all by herself! Well, with a little help from Papa.

The sun felt warm on her face. The birds still sang their happy songs. And Maya had learned something important today.

When things seem impossible, don't give up. Try looking at the problem in a new way. Sometimes the answer is right there waiting!

Maya smiled and hugged Rosie close. It was going to be a wonderful day.

The end.
