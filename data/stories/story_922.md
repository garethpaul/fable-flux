# Story story_922

**The Lonely Puzzle in the Basement**

In a quiet basement, a colorful jigsaw puzzle sat on a dusty shelf. His name was Patches. He had one thousand pieces that made a beautiful rainbow when put together. But Patches had a big problem. No one ever came downstairs to play with him.

The basement was dark and cold. Patches could hear footsteps upstairs. Children laughed and played above his head. "I wish someone would find me," Patches whispered to himself. His pieces rattled softly when he sighed.

Patches looked around the basement. Old boxes stacked up high. A washing machine hummed in the corner. Cobwebs hung from the ceiling like gray curtains. "This place needs some color," Patches thought. "I could help with that!"

Patches decided to solve his problem. He would make himself impossible to miss. During the night, he scattered his pieces across the basement floor. Bright red pieces here. Sunny yellow pieces there. Purple pieces by the stairs. Blue pieces near the washing machine.

"Now someone will surely notice me!" Patches said happily.

The next morning, footsteps came down the stairs. It was Mom doing laundry. She stepped carefully around the colorful pieces. "What a mess," she muttered. Mom gathered all the pieces quickly. She put Patches back on his dusty shelf. "Someone must have dropped this puzzle," she said.

Patches felt very sad. His first plan did not work. Mom just thought he was messy. She did not want to play with him. Patches sat quietly for three whole days. He watched dust motes dance in the dim light.

Then Patches had another idea. A better idea. He would not scatter himself around. Instead, he would put himself together bit by bit. He would make himself so beautiful that no one could ignore him.

Late at night, Patches began his work. One piece clicked into another. Then another. And another. It was hard work doing it alone. His pieces moved slowly across the shelf. Click. Click. Click. The sound echoed softly in the quiet basement.

By morning, Patches had made a small rainbow corner. The colors glowed even in the dim basement light. The next night, he added more pieces. Yellow sunshine appeared. Then green grass. Then purple flowers.

Each night, Patches worked harder. His rainbow grew bigger and brighter. Soon, he was almost complete. Just ten pieces left to go. Then five pieces. Then three. Then one.

On Saturday morning, little Emma came downstairs with her dad. They were looking for Christmas decorations. Emma's eyes grew wide when she saw Patches on the shelf.

"Daddy, look!" Emma pointed with excitement. "A beautiful rainbow puzzle! It's almost finished!"

Dad smiled. "Wow! Someone did amazing work on this puzzle. Should we finish it together?"

Emma clapped her hands. "Yes! Yes! Please!"

Dad lifted Patches down from the shelf. Emma found the last missing piece. It was a bright red piece that completed the rainbow's arc. Click! The puzzle was perfect.

"This rainbow is so pretty," Emma said. She ran her small fingers over the smooth pieces. "Can we frame it for my room?"

"Of course," Dad said. "This puzzle deserves to be displayed."

Patches felt warm and happy inside. His plan had worked! He was no longer lonely. Emma loved his beautiful colors. Soon he would hang on her bedroom wall where he could see her every day.

From that day on, Patches lived in Emma's sunny room. She showed him to all her friends. "This is my rainbow puzzle," she would say proudly. "We finished it together."

Patches learned something important. Sometimes the best way to make friends is to show your very best self. When you work hard and never give up, wonderful things can happen. And sometimes, the most beautiful solutions come from believing in yourself.

Every night, Patches' rainbow colors glowed softly in the moonlight. He was never lonely again.

The end.
