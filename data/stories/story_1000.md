# Story story_1000

**Ellie's Vegetable Adventure**

Ellie the elephant lived by a big blue pond. She loved splashing in the cool water. She loved rolling in the soft mud. But Ellie did not like eating vegetables.

Every morning, Mama Elephant brought fresh green leaves. "Eat your breakfast, Ellie," Mama said sweetly. Ellie wrinkled her long nose. The leaves smelled bitter. They looked too green. They felt rough on her tongue.

"Yuck!" said Ellie. She pushed the leaves away with her trunk. Her tummy rumbled loudly. She felt tired and weak. Other baby elephants played and ran around the pond. Ellie could only watch. She had no energy to join them.

Mama Elephant looked worried. "You need vegetables to grow big and strong," she said. "Like the tall trees around our pond."

Ellie's friend Benny the bird landed on her head. "Why do you look so sad?" chirped Benny. His bright yellow feathers tickled Ellie's ears.

"I don't like vegetables," Ellie sighed. "But I'm too tired to play."

Benny thought for a moment. "Maybe we can make eating fun!" he said. "Let's try something new."

Ellie felt a tiny spark of hope. She decided to try Benny's idea.

First, they tried making the vegetables pretty. Benny helped Ellie arrange the green leaves in a circle. They made flower shapes with the carrots. They stacked the corn like little towers.

"Now try eating," said Benny cheerfully.

Ellie took a small bite. The carrot still tasted too crunchy. The leaves still felt too rough. She made a face and stopped eating.

"This doesn't work," Ellie said sadly. Her tummy rumbled again. She felt even more tired than before.

But Benny didn't give up. "Let's try one more thing," he said. "What if we make it taste different?"

Ellie tilted her head. "How?"

Benny flew to the pond. He came back with fresh, clean water in his beak. "Dip the vegetables in the pond water first," he suggested.

Ellie felt curious. She dipped a green leaf in the cool pond water. Tiny water drops sparkled like diamonds. She took a small bite.

"Oh!" Ellie's eyes grew wide. The water made the leaf taste fresh and clean. It didn't taste bitter anymore. The water helped the rough feeling go away.

She tried a carrot next. Dip, bite, crunch. It tasted sweet and cool! The corn was juicy and delicious after its water bath.

"This is wonderful!" Ellie trumpeted happily. Her trunk swayed back and forth with joy. She ate all her vegetables. Every single one!

Soon, Ellie felt energy flowing through her body. Her legs felt strong again. Her trunk felt powerful. She splashed into the pond with a big SPLASH!

"Come play with us!" called her elephant friends.

Ellie ran and played all day. She could keep up with everyone now. She felt happy and strong.

That evening, Mama Elephant smiled proudly. "You found a way to eat your vegetables!" she said. "How did you do it?"

"I didn't give up," said Ellie. "Benny helped me try different ways. The pond water made everything taste better!"

Benny puffed up his yellow chest. "Sometimes we need to try more than once," he chirped wisely.

From that day on, Ellie always dipped her vegetables in the fresh pond water. She grew bigger and stronger every day. She could play longer with her friends. She could help Mama carry heavy branches.

Ellie learned something very important. When something seems too hard, don't give up. Try a different way. Ask friends for help. Keep trying until you find what works.

Now Ellie loved eating vegetables almost as much as she loved splashing in her beautiful blue pond. And she was the strongest, happiest little elephant in the whole forest.
