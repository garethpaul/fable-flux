# Story story_841

**The Cookie Jar Problem**

Little Fox loved going to Rainbow Nursery. The walls were painted bright yellow. Colorful toys filled every corner. But Fox's favorite thing was snack time.

Every day, Mrs. Rabbit put fresh cookies in a big glass jar. The jar sat high on a wooden shelf. It was way above Fox's head. The cookies smelled like warm vanilla and chocolate chips.

One Tuesday morning, Fox arrived early. His tummy rumbled loudly. The other animals hadn't come yet. Fox looked up at the cookie jar. His mouth watered.

"I want a cookie," Fox said to himself. "Just one little cookie."

Fox stood on his tippy-toes. He stretched his orange paws high above his head. He jumped up and down. But the shelf was too high. Fox couldn't reach the cookies.

"This is not fair," Fox huffed. He sat down and thought hard.

Then Fox had an idea. He saw a small blue chair nearby. Fox pushed the chair across the room. It made a scraping sound on the floor. He climbed up on the chair.

Fox reached up again. His paws wiggled in the air. He was closer now! But the jar was still too far away. Fox stretched and stretched. He almost fell off the chair.

"Oh no," Fox sighed. "This isn't working either."

Fox climbed down from the chair. He felt frustrated. His ears drooped down. His fluffy tail dragged on the floor.

Just then, Bunny hopped into the nursery. She was Fox's best friend. Bunny had long ears and soft white fur.

"Hi Fox!" Bunny said cheerfully. "Why do you look sad?"

Fox pointed up at the cookie jar. "I want a cookie. But I can't reach it. I tried standing tall. I tried using a chair. Nothing works."

Bunny tilted her head. She thought for a moment. "Maybe we can work together," she said.

"How?" asked Fox.

"You're strong," Bunny explained. "I'm tall when I hop. What if you help me reach higher?"

Fox's eyes lit up. His tail started wagging. "That's a great idea!"

Fox got back on the blue chair. Bunny hopped up next to him. Fox held Bunny's paws steady. Bunny stood on her back legs and stretched up high.

"I can almost touch it!" Bunny said excitedly.

Fox held on tight. Bunny reached higher and higher. Her paw touched the cookie jar. She carefully lifted the lid.

"I got it!" Bunny cheered.

She took out two cookies. One for Fox and one for herself. The cookies were round and golden brown. They smelled delicious.

Fox and Bunny climbed down carefully. They sat together on the soft carpet. Fox bit into his cookie. It was sweet and crunchy. Chocolate chips melted on his tongue.

"Thank you, Bunny," Fox said happily. "You helped me solve my problem."

"We solved it together," Bunny reminded him. "Sometimes two friends can do what one friend cannot."

Fox nodded. "Next time I have a problem, I'll ask for help."

Mrs. Rabbit came into the room. She saw the two friends sharing cookies. She smiled warmly.

"Good morning, Fox and Bunny," Mrs. Rabbit said. "I see you found the cookies."

"We worked as a team," Fox explained proudly.

"That's wonderful," Mrs. Rabbit said. "Teamwork makes everything better."

Soon, more animals arrived at nursery. Fox told everyone about his morning adventure. He learned that asking friends for help was smart, not silly.

From that day on, Fox never felt bad about needing help. He knew his friends would always be there for him. And he would always help them too.

The cookie jar stayed on the high shelf. But Fox wasn't worried anymore. He had learned something important. Some problems are easier to solve with friends.

Fox finished his cookie and smiled. It was going to be a wonderful day at Rainbow Nursery.

The end.
