# Story story_827

**Pip the Puppet's Story Time**

It was three o'clock. Story time was starting soon. All the children sat on the soft, colorful rug. They wiggled with excitement.

But Pip the puppet stayed hidden. He lived in a wooden box. The box sat on Teacher Anna's desk. Pip could hear the children talking. Their voices sounded happy and loud.

"I don't want to come out," Pip whispered. His red felt body trembled a little. "What if I forget my words? What if the children don't like me?"

Teacher Anna opened the box gently. "Hello, Pip," she said softly. "The children are waiting for you."

Pip peeked out with one button eye. He saw twenty smiling faces. Some children had missing teeth. Others had braids and ponytails. They all looked so big to little Pip.

"I'm scared," Pip told Teacher Anna. His voice was very quiet.

"That's okay," she whispered back. "We can be brave together."

Teacher Anna picked up Pip carefully. She put him on her hand. Pip's purple hat wobbled on his head. His tiny bells jingled softly.

"Everyone, meet Pip!" Teacher Anna said.

"Hi, Pip!" the children called out. Their voices were warm and friendly.

But Pip still felt nervous. He hid behind Teacher Anna's arm. The children giggled. It was a nice sound, like bubbles popping.

"Pip is feeling shy today," Teacher Anna explained. "Should we tell him a story to help him feel better?"

"Yes!" the children shouted together.

Little Emma raised her hand. "I know a story about a brave mouse!"

Pip listened as Emma told her story. The mouse was small like him. But the mouse went on big adventures. He helped his friends. He wasn't afraid of anything.

"That mouse sounds wonderful," Pip said quietly. His voice got a tiny bit louder.

Then Marcus shared a story about a singing frog. The frog started out scared to sing. But when he tried, his voice was beautiful. Everyone loved his songs.

Pip's button eyes grew wide. "The frog was scared too?"

"Yes!" Marcus nodded. "But he got brave!"

More children told stories. They talked about shy rabbits who made friends. They shared tales of little birds learning to fly. Each story made Pip feel warmer inside.

Something magical started to happen. Pip began to feel less scared. The children's stories filled him with courage. Their kind faces made him smile.

"I want to tell a story too," Pip said suddenly. His voice was clear and strong now.

The children clapped their hands. The sound was like gentle rain on leaves.

Pip told them about a little puppet who lived in a dark box. The puppet felt lonely and afraid. But then he met wonderful children who shared their stories. The children's kindness made the puppet feel brave and happy.

"That puppet sounds just like you!" laughed Sofia.

"It is me!" Pip giggled. His bells jingled merrily.

The children loved Pip's story. They asked him questions. They told him he was funny and smart. Pip felt his heart growing bigger and warmer.

Teacher Anna brought out a picture book. "Should Pip help me read this story?"

"Yes!" everyone cheered.

Pip helped turn the pages with his small puppet hands. He made silly voices for the characters. The children laughed and gasped at all the right parts. Pip felt proud and happy.

When story time ended, Pip didn't want to go back to his box.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" asked Maya.

"Of course!" Pip said with a big smile. "I love story time now!"

The children waved goodbye as Pip went into his box. But now the box didn't feel dark or scary. It felt cozy and safe.

"Thank you for helping me be brave," Pip told Teacher Anna.

"You were brave all by yourself," she replied. "You just needed to discover it."

That night, Pip dreamed of all the stories they would share tomorrow. He couldn't wait for three o'clock to come again. Story time had become his favorite time of day.

The end.
