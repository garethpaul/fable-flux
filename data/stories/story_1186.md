# Story story_1186

**Woolly's Big Bedtime Adventure**

Woolly the sheep lived in a cozy living room. She had soft white wool and kind brown eyes. The living room was her favorite place. It had a warm fireplace and fluffy rugs.

Every day, Woolly played happily. She rolled on the soft carpet. She watched birds through the big window. She snuggled by the warm fire.

But when nighttime came, Woolly felt scared. The living room looked different in the dark. Shadows danced on the walls. Strange sounds came from outside. Her heart beat fast like a drum.

"I don't want to sleep," Woolly whispered. "The dark is too scary."

One evening, Woolly discovered something important. She was very tired. Her eyes felt heavy. Her legs felt wobbly. But she was still afraid of bedtime.

"I need to fix this," Woolly said bravely.

Woolly tried her first solution. She gathered all the pillows from the couch. She built a tall pillow fort. The pillows smelled like lavender soap.

"Now the scary shadows can't reach me," she said.

Woolly crawled inside her pillow fort. It felt safe and cozy. But then she heard a loud HOOT from outside. An owl was calling in the night.

Woolly's heart jumped. She peeked out from the pillows. The shadows still looked scary. Her fort felt too small and dark.

"This isn't working," Woolly sighed sadly.

The next night, Woolly had a new idea. She remembered something special. Her grandmother had given her a small night light. It looked like a bright yellow star.

Woolly found the night light in a drawer. She plugged it into the wall. The star began to glow softly. It made gentle yellow light dance around the room.

"Maybe this will help," Woolly hoped.

The night light changed everything magical. The scary shadows became friendly shapes. One shadow looked like a dancing rabbit. Another looked like a flying bird.

The room felt warm and peaceful. Woolly could see her favorite toys clearly. Her teddy bear smiled at her. Her favorite blanket waited on the couch.

Woolly walked around the room slowly. She touched her soft blanket. It felt like a warm hug. She picked up her teddy bear. It smelled like sunshine and home.

"The room isn't scary anymore," Woolly said happily.

She made a cozy bed on the rug. She spread out her favorite blanket. It was blue with white clouds. She placed her teddy bear beside her.

The night light kept glowing gently. It made everything look safe and friendly. Woolly felt her eyes getting heavy. Her body felt relaxed and calm.

"I'm not afraid anymore," she whispered softly.

Outside, the owl hooted again. But now it sounded like a lullaby. The wind made gentle whooshing sounds. Even the shadows looked like old friends.

Woolly closed her eyes peacefully. She felt proud and brave. She had solved her problem all by herself.

"Tomorrow I'll try again," she thought sleepily. "I can be brave every night."

The yellow star light watched over Woolly. It kept her safe and warm. Her teddy bear snuggled close beside her.

Soon, Woolly was fast asleep. She dreamed of sunny meadows. She dreamed of playing with other sheep. She dreamed of rolling in soft green grass.

When morning came, Woolly woke up happy. The sun streamed through the window. Birds sang cheerful songs outside.

"I did it!" Woolly said proudly. "I wasn't afraid."

That night, Woolly felt excited for bedtime. She turned on her star light. She got her cozy blanket ready. She hugged her teddy bear tight.

"Bedtime is fun now," Woolly smiled.

From that day on, Woolly loved bedtime. She learned that trying different solutions helps. She learned that being brave takes practice. Most importantly, she learned that nighttime could be peaceful and safe.

Every night, the little star light glowed softly. It reminded Woolly that she was brave and smart. She had turned her fear into something wonderful.

And Woolly slept happily ever after in her cozy living room home.

The end.
