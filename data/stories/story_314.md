# Toby's Magical Story Time

Toby the little red train lived in a cozy train station that looked just like home. Every evening at seven o'clock, the station bell would ring. "Ding-dong! Ding-dong!" That meant story time was starting.

But tonight, Toby didn't want to stop playing. He was having so much fun rolling his wheels back and forth. "I don't want story time," Toby puffed softly. "I want to keep playing!"

Papa Engine called from the reading corner. "Come on, Toby! Time for our story!" The reading corner had soft cushions everywhere. There were books on every shelf. A warm lamp made everything glow yellow and cozy.

Toby rolled over very, very slowly. His wheels made quiet clicking sounds on the wooden floor. "Click-clack, click-clack." He wasn't excited at all. Story time felt boring tonight.

Papa Engine had picked out a big, colorful book. "Tonight we're reading about brave trains who travel the world," Papa said with a smile. Toby settled onto his favorite blue cushion. It was soft and squishy.

Papa Engine opened the book. The first page showed a magnificent silver train. It was racing through snowy mountains! "Whoooosh!" Papa made the train sound. Toby's eyes grew a little wider.

On the next page, a green train chugged through a jungle. Monkeys were swinging from tree to tree! "Chug-a-chug-a-choo!" Papa called out. Toby started wiggling with excitement.

Then something magical happened. As Papa read about the brave purple train crossing a big desert, Toby could almost feel hot sand between his wheels. The story felt so real! He could hear the wind whistling. "Whooooo!"

"The purple train was scared," Papa read. "But she was also very brave." Toby thought about times when he felt scared but tried to be brave too. Like when he had to go up the really steep hill.

Papa turned the page. Now there was an orange train sailing across the ocean on a special boat! Toby had never seen anything like it. "Wow!" he whispered. His wheels started spinning with excitement.

"The orange train met dolphins who jumped and played," Papa continued. "Splash! Splash!" Papa made water sounds. Toby giggled and made little train whistles. "Toot-toot!"

As the story went on, Toby forgot all about wanting to play. He was too busy imagining adventures! He pictured himself as each brave train. Racing through snow! Chugging through jungles! Sailing across oceans!

The best part came when Papa read about the little red train. Just like Toby! "The little red train was the bravest of all," Papa said proudly. "Even though he was small, his heart was huge."

Toby puffed up with pride. His red paint seemed to shine brighter. He felt warm and happy inside. Story time was making him feel wonderful!

When Papa closed the book, Toby didn't want it to end. "Can we read another one?" he asked hopefully. His wheels were wiggling with excitement.

Papa Engine smiled. "Tomorrow night, little engineer. We'll have another adventure then." Papa gave Toby a gentle pat. "Did you enjoy story time tonight?"

"Yes!" Toby exclaimed. "I felt like I was really traveling! I saw mountains and jungles and oceans!" His whistle tooted softly with joy. "Toot-toot!"

As Papa Engine tucked him into his sleeping spot, Toby's mind was still full of adventures. He dreamed about being brave like the trains in the story. He dreamed about helping other trains and seeing amazing places.

The next evening, when the station bell rang "Ding-dong! Ding-dong!" Toby zoomed straight to the reading corner. His wheels clicked happily. "Click-clack, click-clack!"

"I'm ready for story time!" Toby announced cheerfully. He snuggled into his blue cushion and waited eagerly. Tonight felt like the start of another wonderful adventure.

Papa Engine picked up a new book. "Tonight, let's read about trains who help their friends," he said.

Toby's eyes sparkled with excitement. Story time had become his favorite part of every day!

The end.
