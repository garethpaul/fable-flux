# Freddy Frog's Garden Magic

The morning sun peeked through the kitchen window. Mama Frog smiled at little Freddy. "Time for garden work!" she said cheerfully.

Freddy's shoulders drooped down low. His big green eyes looked sad. "Do I have to?" he asked softly. "Garden work is boring and dirty."

Every day was the same thing. Pull weeds. Water plants. Pick vegetables. Freddy wanted to play instead. He wanted to jump on lily pads. He wanted to catch flies.

"Come on, little one," Mama said gently. "The garden needs our help today."

Freddy hopped slowly outside. His feet felt heavy. The sun felt too warm. The dirt looked too messy.

Mama handed him small gardening gloves. They were bright green like his skin. "Let's start with the tomatoes," she said.

Freddy sighed a big sigh. He grabbed the tiny watering can. It was just his size. He filled it with cool water.

As he walked to the tomato plants, something caught his eye. A tiny spider sat on a leaf. It had built a beautiful web. Water drops sparkled like diamonds on the silky threads.

"Wow!" Freddy whispered. "Look at that!"

The spider waved one tiny leg. It seemed to say hello. Freddy giggled and waved back.

He poured water gently around the plants. The dry soil drank it up quickly. It made soft bubbling sounds. Like tiny streams flowing underground.

"The plants are thirsty," Freddy said. "Just like me after hopping!"

Next, they moved to the carrot patch. Mama showed Freddy how to pull weeds. "Weeds steal food from our vegetables," she explained.

Freddy tugged on a big dandelion. It came up with a satisfying *pop*! Dirt scattered everywhere. A fat earthworm wiggled in the hole.

"Hello, wormy!" Freddy laughed. The worm disappeared back underground. It was playing hide and seek!

As Freddy worked, he noticed more garden friends. Busy bees hummed happy songs. Colorful butterflies danced from flower to flower. A red ladybug walked across his hand.

"The garden is full of friends!" Freddy exclaimed.

Mama smiled warmly. "They all help our garden grow."

In the bean patch, Freddy discovered something amazing. The bean plants had grown tall overnight! They climbed up wooden poles like green ladders.

"They're reaching for the sky!" Freddy said. He stretched his arms up high. He pretended to be a growing bean plant too.

Mama laughed at his silly dance. "Plants love to grow," she said. "Just like little frogs."

They picked ripe tomatoes together. The red ones felt warm from sunshine. They smelled sweet and earthy. Freddy's tummy rumbled with hunger.

"Can we eat one now?" he asked hopefully.

Mama cut a slice with her garden knife. The tomato juice ran down Freddy's chin. It tasted like summer sunshine. Like happiness in his mouth.

"This is the best tomato ever!" Freddy declared.

They harvested crispy lettuce leaves. They pulled up orange carrots. They gathered purple beans. Freddy's basket filled with colorful treasures.

"We grew all this food ourselves!" Freddy said proudly. His chest puffed out with pride.

As they worked, Freddy felt different inside. The garden wasn't boring anymore. It was magical! Every plant had a story. Every bug had a purpose.

He noticed how the flowers turned toward the sun. How the leaves rustled in the breeze. How everything worked together perfectly.

"Mama," Freddy said thoughtfully. "The garden is like a big outdoor home."

"Yes," Mama agreed. "And we're the caretakers."

When garden time ended, Freddy felt surprised. The morning had flown by quickly. His hands were dirty but happy. His heart felt full and warm.

That evening, they ate their garden vegetables for dinner. Every bite tasted extra special. Freddy had helped grow this food!

As bedtime approached, Freddy looked out his window. The garden rested peacefully under starlight. Tomorrow's adventures waited in the soil.

"Mama," Freddy said sleepily. "Can we work in the garden again tomorrow?"

Mama tucked him in gently. "Of course, little gardener."

Freddy smiled as he drifted off to sleep. He dreamed of growing plants and garden friends. Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough!

The end.
