# Story story_1140

**Teddy and Sister at the Library**

Teddy sat alone in the big library. The soft carpet felt fuzzy under his paws. Tall shelves reached up to the ceiling. Books filled every space. Teddy hugged his favorite red book tight.

"I wish I had someone to read with," Teddy whispered. His voice was very quiet. The library was so big and quiet too. Teddy felt small and lonely.

Other children played together near the story corner. They laughed and shared picture books. Teddy watched them with sad eyes. He wanted a friend to play with.

Sister Bear walked into the library. Her pink bow bounced as she moved. She carried a purple backpack full of books. Sister saw Teddy sitting by himself.

"Hi Teddy," Sister said softly. "What are you reading?"

Teddy looked up. His brown eyes were a little sad. "It's a book about trains," he said. "But reading alone isn't very fun."

Sister's face lit up with a big smile. "I love trains too! Can I sit with you?"

Teddy's ears perked up. "Really? You want to read with me?"

"Of course!" Sister plopped down on the soft carpet. The cushions made a gentle whoosh sound. "Reading is always better with a friend."

Teddy opened his red book. The pages smelled like vanilla and old paper. Sister scooted closer. Her warm shoulder touched Teddy's arm.

"Look at this blue train," Teddy pointed to the picture. "It goes choo-choo through the mountains."

"Wow!" Sister's eyes grew wide. "The mountains look so tall and snowy. I can almost hear the train whistle."

They turned the page together. Their fingers touched the smooth paper. A yellow train appeared in the picture.

"This train carries apples," Sister read out loud. "Red apples and green apples. They smell sweet and fresh."

Teddy giggled. "My tummy is rumbling now. Those apples look so yummy."

Sister laughed too. Her laugh sounded like tiny bells ringing. "We should ask Mama for apples later."

They read three more pages together. Each picture showed different colored trains. Purple trains. Orange trains. Pink trains with polka dots.

"I have an idea," Sister said. She reached into her purple backpack. "Let's build our own train!"

Sister pulled out wooden blocks. They were smooth and colorful. Red blocks. Blue blocks. Yellow blocks that smelled like cedar wood.

"Great idea!" Teddy clapped his paws together. The sound echoed softly in the quiet library.

They built their train on the carpet. Click, click, click went the blocks. They stacked them carefully. Their train grew longer and longer.

"All aboard!" Teddy whispered. He didn't want to be too loud.

"Choo-choo!" Sister whispered back. They pretended to ride their block train.

The librarian walked over. Her shoes made soft tap-tap sounds. She smiled at Teddy and Sister.

"What a wonderful train," she said quietly. "You two work well together."

Teddy and Sister beamed with pride. Their cheeks felt warm and happy.

"We're train engineers," Teddy explained. "Sister helped me build it."

"And Teddy shared his train book with me," Sister added. "We make a good team."

The librarian nodded. "The best adventures happen when friends share them."

Teddy and Sister spent the whole afternoon together. They read four more books. They built a block castle. They drew pictures with crayons that smelled waxy and bright.

When it was time to leave, Teddy felt different. His heart felt full and warm. The lonely feeling was completely gone.

"Thank you for playing with me," Teddy said to Sister. "You made today special."

Sister hugged Teddy tight. "Thank you for sharing your books. You're the best reading buddy ever."

They walked out of the library together. Their hands swung back and forth. The afternoon sun felt warm on their faces.

"Same time tomorrow?" Sister asked hopefully.

"Yes!" Teddy said with a huge grin. "Reading together is the best."

From that day on, Teddy and Sister met at the library every week. They shared stories and built amazing things. Most importantly, they shared the joy of friendship.

The library never felt too big or too quiet again.

The end.
