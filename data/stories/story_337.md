# Nutkin's Missing Blocks

Nutkin the squirrel loved building towers. Every morning, he would scurry down the long hallway to his favorite corner. His fluffy tail would twitch with excitement. Today would be perfect for building!

But when Nutkin reached his corner, something was wrong. His colorful blocks were gone! The red ones, the blue ones, even his special yellow star block. All missing!

"Oh no!" squeaked Nutkin. His whiskers drooped sadly. "Where are my blocks?"

The hallway felt empty without his toys. Nutkin's tiny paws patted the soft carpet. Maybe the blocks were hiding underneath? He lifted the edge and peeked. Nothing there but dust bunnies.

"I must find them," Nutkin said firmly. His brown eyes sparkled with determination.

First, Nutkin checked behind the big potted plant. Green leaves tickled his nose. He pushed them aside carefully. No blocks there, just dark soil and a sleepy earthworm.

Next, he scampered to the coat closet. The door creaked open slowly. Inside smelled like rain boots and winter mittens. Nutkin climbed up, up, up to the top shelf. Still no blocks anywhere.

"Hmm," thought Nutkin, scratching his furry head. "Where else could they be?"

He remembered playing with his friend Pip yesterday. Maybe she knew something! Nutkin's claws clicked on the wooden floor as he hurried down the hallway.

"Pip! Pip!" he called softly.

A little mouse poked her pink nose out from under the stairs. "Hi, Nutkin! You sound worried."

"I can't find my blocks anywhere," Nutkin explained. "Have you seen them?"

Pip's whiskers twitched thoughtfully. "I saw your mom carrying something colorful this morning. Maybe she moved them?"

Nutkin's heart jumped with hope. "Thank you, Pip!" He gave her a gentle hug.

Down the hallway Nutkin raced, his bushy tail streaming behind him. He found Mama Squirrel in the kitchen, humming a happy tune.

"Mama," panted Nutkin, "do you know where my blocks went?"

Mama Squirrel smiled warmly. "Oh yes, little one. I moved them to keep them safe."

"Safe from what?" asked Nutkin, tilting his head.

"The cleaning lady comes today," Mama explained. "I put your blocks in your bedroom so they wouldn't get lost."

Nutkin felt silly but grateful. "Thank you, Mama!"

He scurried to his cozy bedroom. There, on his soft blue blanket, sat his beloved blocks! The red ones gleamed like apples. The blue ones sparkled like the sky. And his special yellow star block sat right on top, waiting for him.

"Hooray!" cheered Nutkin, doing a little dance.

He carefully carried his blocks back to the hallway corner. One by one, he stacked them high. Red block, blue block, green block too. The tower grew taller and taller.

"Perfect!" Nutkin said proudly.

Just then, Pip scurried over. "Wow, Nutkin! Your tower is beautiful!"

"Want to help me build another one?" asked Nutkin.

"Yes, please!" squeaked Pip happily.

Together, they built towers and castles and bridges. The hallway filled with their joyful laughter. The afternoon sun streamed through the window, making their blocks glow like jewels.

As they played, Nutkin felt proud of himself. He hadn't given up when his blocks went missing. He had asked for help when he needed it. Most importantly, he had kept trying until he found the answer.

"I'm glad I didn't stop looking," Nutkin told Pip.

"Me too," she replied. "Now we can play together!"

When evening came, Nutkin carefully stacked his blocks in a neat pile. Tomorrow would bring another day of building and fun. He had learned something important: when things seem lost, keep looking and ask for help. Good things happen to those who don't give up.

Nutkin curled up in his warm bed, his tail wrapped around him like a cozy blanket. Outside, the stars twinkled just like his favorite yellow block. Tomorrow would be another wonderful day for building dreams.

The end.
