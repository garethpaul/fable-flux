# Story story_1109

**The Magic of Story Snacks**

Emma loved snack time at school. Every day at three o'clock, Mrs. Garcia would ring the little silver bell. "Snack time, everyone!" she would call out cheerfully.

All the children would gather on the big rainbow carpet. They sat in a circle with their crackers and apple slices. But today was different. Today was the first "Book and Snack Time."

"We're going to read while we eat," Mrs. Garcia announced. She held up a thick picture book with a bright blue cover.

Emma's tummy felt funny. She loved books. She loved snacks too. But together? That seemed hard. How could she hold her crackers and turn pages? What if she got crumbs on the pretty pictures?

"I don't want to," Emma whispered to her friend Sam. "Books might get messy."

Sam nodded. "My hands get sticky from apple juice."

Mrs. Garcia sat down with them. The book was called "The Little Mouse's Big Adventure." On the cover, a tiny gray mouse wore a red cape.

"Let me try something special," Mrs. Garcia said with a smile. She opened the book to the first page. The mouse was in a cozy kitchen, just like their snack time.

Emma took a small bite of her cracker. It was salty and crunchy. Mrs. Garcia began to read in a soft, magical voice.

"Little Mouse was hungry for an adventure," she read. "And maybe a snack too."

Emma giggled. The mouse looked just like her, sitting with food and wondering what to do next.

As Mrs. Garcia turned the page, something wonderful happened. The mouse in the story found a piece of cheese. Emma was eating cheese crackers! It felt like the mouse was having snack time with her.

"Look!" Sam pointed to the picture. "The mouse has apple slices too!"

Emma's eyes grew wide. The mouse really did have apple slices, just like theirs. The story felt real and magical.

Mrs. Garcia kept reading. The mouse climbed a tall mountain made of books. Emma imagined climbing her own stack of favorite stories. The mouse sailed across a milk river. Emma sipped her milk and pretended to sail too.

Every page brought new surprises. When the mouse felt scared in a dark cave, Emma held her cracker tight. When the mouse found treasure, Emma's apple slice tasted extra sweet.

"The food makes the story better," Emma whispered to Sam.

"The story makes the food better too," Sam whispered back.

Mrs. Garcia heard them and smiled. "That's the magic of story snacks," she said. "Everything becomes more special together."

As the story continued, Emma forgot all about messy hands. She forgot about crumbs on pages. She was too busy living the adventure with the brave little mouse.

The mouse met friendly butterflies who shared their nectar. Emma's juice box tasted like sunshine. The mouse found a garden full of vegetables. Emma's carrot sticks became magical orange treasures.

Finally, the little mouse returned home, tired but happy. The mouse curled up with a warm cookie and a good book. Emma felt warm and happy too.

"The end," Mrs. Garcia said softly. She closed the book with a gentle thud.

Emma looked around the circle. All her friends had big smiles. Their snacks were gone, but nobody seemed to notice. They were all thinking about the mouse's adventure.

"That was the best snack time ever!" Emma said.

"Can we do it again tomorrow?" asked Sam.

Mrs. Garcia laughed. "Of course! Tomorrow we'll read about a little bear who loves honey."

Emma clapped her hands. "I hope we have honey crackers!"

As they cleaned up their napkins and cups, Emma felt excited. She couldn't wait for tomorrow's story snack. Maybe the bear would have the same snacks as them. Maybe they would go on another magical adventure.

Emma learned something wonderful that day. Books and snacks really were better together. Stories could make food taste more delicious. And food could make stories feel more real.

She skipped to her backpack, already dreaming about tomorrow's book and snack time.

The end.
