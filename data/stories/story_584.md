# Story story_584

**Benny's Bath Time Adventure**

Benny was a bright red toy plane. He lived on Tommy's bedroom shelf. Every night, Tommy played with Benny before bed.

"Bath time, Tommy!" called Mom from downstairs.

Tommy picked up Benny. "Come on, Benny. Bath time!"

Benny's propeller drooped. He did not like bath time. Water made him feel heavy. Soap bubbles got in his eyes. The bathtub seemed so big and scary.

Tommy carried Benny to the bathroom. The water was warm and bubbly. Steam rose from the tub like fluffy clouds.

"I don't want to get wet," whispered Benny to himself.

Tommy placed Benny on the edge of the tub. Benny looked at the soapy water. It sparkled under the bright lights.

"Zoom!" said Tommy. He moved Benny through the air. "You're flying, Benny!"

Benny still felt worried. What if he sank? What if the water was too deep?

Tommy slowly dipped Benny's wheels in the water. The warm bubbles tickled! They felt soft and gentle.

"The water feels nice," Benny thought. "Like warm summer rain."

Tommy moved Benny deeper. Bubbles covered his red wings. They looked like puffy white clouds!

"Look, Benny!" Tommy giggled. "You're flying through clouds!"

Something magical began to happen. The bubbles made Benny feel light. He felt like he was really flying! The bathroom became his sky.

Tommy swooshed Benny left and right. Bubbles flew everywhere! They popped with tiny sounds. Pop! Pop! Pop!

"This is fun!" Benny realized. "I'm not heavy at all!"

The warm water felt like sunshine on his wings. The bubbles smelled like sweet flowers. Benny's red paint gleamed in the light.

"Whoosh!" Tommy made airplane sounds. "Benny is the fastest plane ever!"

Benny zoomed over Tommy's knees. They looked like mountains below. He flew around Tommy's arms like tall trees.

The soap made everything slippery and smooth. Benny glided through the water easily. He felt graceful and free.

"I can do loops!" Benny thought happily.

Tommy lifted Benny high. Water dripped from his wings like rain. The drops made pretty splashing sounds in the tub.

"You're so clean and shiny now," Tommy said. "You look brand new!"

Benny looked at his reflection in the water. His red paint sparkled. His propeller gleamed silver. He had never looked so bright!

Tommy gently washed Benny's wings. The soft washcloth felt like gentle clouds. Warm water rinsed away all the soap.

"Flying through water is different than air," Benny discovered. "But it's wonderful too!"

The bubbles began to disappear. Tommy lifted Benny from the tub. Mom wrapped them both in fluffy towels.

"All clean!" Mom smiled. "What good boys!"

Tommy dried Benny carefully. He patted each wing gently. The towel was soft and warm.

"Tomorrow we'll fly again," Tommy whispered to Benny.

Benny's propeller spun with excitement. Bath time wasn't scary anymore! It was magical and fun.

Tommy placed Benny on his nightstand. Benny felt fresh and clean. His paint gleamed in the moonlight.

"Bath time makes me sparkle," Benny thought proudly. "I look like a real airplane now!"

As Tommy fell asleep, Benny dreamed of flying. He dreamed of warm water and fluffy bubbles. He dreamed of tomorrow's adventures.

"I can't wait for bath time again," Benny whispered to the stars. "Flying through bubbles is the best!"

The next evening, when Mom called for bath time, Benny's propeller spun happily. He was ready for another magical flight through the bubbly clouds.

Bath time had become his favorite time to fly.

The end.
