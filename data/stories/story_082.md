# Bella the Brave Little Cow

In a cozy farmhouse dining room, a small spotted cow named Bella lived with the Miller family. She had soft brown eyes and a gentle "moo" that made everyone smile. Bella loved sitting by the big window during breakfast time.

Every morning, Bella watched the children eat their cereal. She listened to their happy chatter. But there was one thing that made Bella very sad.

"I wish I could see over the fence," Bella sighed. "All the other animals talk about the beautiful flower garden. But I'm too short to see it!"

The wooden fence was very tall. Even when Bella stood on her tippy-hooves, she could only see the top boards. She heard bees buzzing happily. She smelled sweet flowers blooming. But she couldn't see the pretty colors everyone described.

"There must be a way," Bella said firmly. "I won't give up!"

First, Bella tried jumping. She bent her knees down low. Then she sprang up high! But even her biggest jump wasn't tall enough. Her hooves landed with a soft thump on the grass.

"Jumping didn't work," Bella said. "But I'll try something else!"

Next, Bella looked for something to stand on. She found a small wooden box near the barn. She pushed it with her nose. The box made scraping sounds on the ground. When she climbed on top, she was a little taller. But still not tall enough to see over the fence.

"This box helps a little," Bella said. "But I need to be even taller!"

Then Bella had another idea. She found a bigger rock near the garden gate. She rolled it next to the fence with her strong nose. The rock was heavy and bumpy. She climbed on top very carefully. Now she was taller than before! But the fence was still too high.

Bella felt frustrated. Her ears drooped down sadly. "Maybe I'll never see the flower garden," she whispered.

Just then, Mrs. Miller came outside. She saw Bella standing on the rock, stretching her neck toward the fence.

"Oh, sweet Bella!" Mrs. Miller said kindly. "Are you trying to see the flowers?"

Bella nodded and gave a soft, hopeful "moo."

Mrs. Miller smiled warmly. "I have an idea that might help you!"

She went to the tool shed. She came back carrying a sturdy wooden step stool. It had three wide steps that looked very strong.

"Let's try this together," Mrs. Miller said gently.

She placed the step stool right next to the fence. Bella climbed up carefully. One step. Two steps. Three steps! Now she was much taller than before.

Bella stretched her neck up high. Her eyes grew wide with wonder and joy!

"Oh my!" Bella gasped happily.

Over the fence was the most beautiful garden she had ever seen! Red roses danced in the breeze. Yellow sunflowers stood tall and proud. Purple lavender swayed gently. Orange marigolds sparkled in the sunshine. Butterflies fluttered from flower to flower like flying rainbows.

"It's even more beautiful than I imagined!" Bella said with excitement.

Mrs. Miller patted Bella's soft head. "Sometimes we need help to reach our goals," she said. "And that's perfectly okay!"

From that day on, Bella used her special step stool every morning. She would climb up and admire the colorful garden. The sweet flower smells made her feel peaceful and happy.

Bella learned something very important. When something seems impossible, don't give up! Try different ideas. Ask for help when you need it. Keep being brave and creative.

"I'm so glad I kept trying," Bella said to herself proudly.

Now Bella starts each day by enjoying the beautiful view. She watches the flowers grow taller. She sees new buds opening into blooms. Sometimes she even spots baby birds learning to fly!

Bella's persistence had paid off. She felt proud, happy, and grateful. The dining room window wasn't the only place with a wonderful view anymore!

Every morning, Bella climbs her step stool and whispers, "Good morning, beautiful flowers!" And the garden seems to whisper back, "Good morning, brave little cow!"

The end.
