# Story story_662

**Tiger's Reading Adventure**

The big clock on the wall went tick-tock. Miss Sarah clapped her hands softly. "Reading time, everyone!" she called to her class.

All the little animals found their cozy spots. Bunny grabbed her favorite book about carrots. Mouse picked up a story about cheese. Even sleepy Bear found a book about honey.

But Tiger stayed in his chair. His orange and black stripes seemed duller today. Reading time felt hard and boring.

"I don't want to read," Tiger whispered. His ears drooped down low. "The words are too squiggly. They jump around like bugs."

Miss Sarah sat down next to Tiger. Her voice was gentle and warm. "Would you like to try together?" she asked.

Tiger shook his big furry head. "Reading is not fun," he said sadly.

Miss Sarah smiled and picked up a special book. The cover sparkled with golden letters. It had a picture of a jungle with tall green trees.

"This book is different," she said. "It's about a brave tiger just like you."

Tiger's ears perked up a tiny bit. He looked at the shiny cover with curious yellow eyes.

Miss Sarah opened the book slowly. Something amazing happened! The pages began to glow with soft, warm light. The pictures seemed to move and dance.

"Once upon a time," Miss Sarah read, "there lived a tiger in a magical jungle."

As she read the words, Tiger could smell sweet flowers. He could hear birds singing beautiful songs. The classroom felt like it was turning into a real jungle!

"The tiger loved to explore," Miss Sarah continued. "He splashed in cool rivers and climbed tall trees."

Tiger's nose twitched. He could almost feel the cool water on his paws. The story felt so real and wonderful.

"Can I try reading some words?" Tiger asked. His voice was excited now.

Miss Sarah pointed to simple words on the page. "The tiger RAN through the trees," she helped him read.

Tiger sounded out each letter carefully. "R-A-N... RAN!" he said proudly. His tail swished with happiness.

As Tiger read more words, the magical pictures grew brighter. He saw himself running through the jungle in the book. His stripes gleamed in the golden sunlight.

"The tiger found a hidden waterfall," Tiger read all by himself. The words didn't look squiggly anymore. They looked like friends waiting to tell him stories.

Other animals gathered around to listen. Bunny's eyes were wide with wonder. Mouse clapped his tiny paws. Bear smiled sleepily but stayed awake.

Tiger kept reading about his jungle adventure. He learned about colorful parrots and playful monkeys. He discovered a secret cave filled with sparkling gems.

The magical book showed Tiger that he was brave and smart. Reading made him feel strong and happy inside.

When the story ended, the glowing light faded gently. But Tiger's smile stayed bright and big.

"That was the best story ever!" Tiger said. His orange stripes seemed to shine brighter now. "Reading is actually super fun!"

Miss Sarah hugged Tiger softly. "You did a wonderful job," she said. "Reading opens doors to amazing adventures."

Tiger looked around at all the books on the shelves. Each one looked like a new adventure waiting for him. Some had dragons, some had pirates, and some had friendly dinosaurs.

"Can we read another story tomorrow?" Tiger asked hopefully. His tail swished back and forth with excitement.

"Of course!" Miss Sarah said. "There are so many magical stories waiting for you."

The other animals cheered for Tiger. They were proud of their friend for being so brave.

Tiger hugged the special book close to his heart. He couldn't wait for tomorrow's reading time. Now he knew that books were full of magic and wonder.

As the children put their books away, Tiger whispered, "Thank you, magical book. You helped me love reading."

From that day on, Tiger was always the first one ready for reading time. He discovered that the real magic wasn't in the glowing pages. The real magic was in his own imagination and the joy of learning new things.

The end.
