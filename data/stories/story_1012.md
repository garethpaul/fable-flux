# Story story_1012

**Grandma's Lost Book**

Grandma Rose loved the big library. She visited every Tuesday morning. The library smelled like old paper and fresh coffee. Sunlight danced through tall windows.

Today felt different though. Grandma Rose looked worried. She checked her purple purse three times. She patted her coat pockets. Her special book was missing!

"Oh my," she whispered. "Where is my recipe book?"

The recipe book was very special. It had belonged to her mother. The pages were yellow and soft. It held secrets for the best cookies ever. Grandma Rose needed it for her granddaughter's birthday party tomorrow.

She walked to the front desk. Mrs. Chen, the librarian, smiled warmly.

"Can you help me?" asked Grandma Rose. "I lost my recipe book. It has a red cover."

Mrs. Chen nodded kindly. "Let's check the lost and found box."

They looked through the big box together. There were mittens and scarves. A toy car sat next to reading glasses. But no red recipe book.

"I'm sorry," said Mrs. Chen. "No recipe book today."

Grandma Rose felt her heart sink. She sat in her favorite chair by the window. The soft cushion usually made her happy. Today it didn't help.

"What will I do?" she thought. "Emma loves my special cookies."

Then Grandma Rose remembered something. Yesterday she had sat in the children's section. She was reading to little Tommy while his mom picked books.

Grandma Rose walked to the colorful children's area. Bright pictures covered the walls. Small chairs sat in neat rows. She looked under tables and behind bookshelves.

Still no recipe book.

She checked between picture books. She moved soft pillows on the reading carpet. Her back started to ache from bending down.

"This isn't working," she sighed.

Grandma Rose sat on a tiny chair. It creaked under her weight. She closed her eyes and thought hard.

"Where else did I go yesterday?" she wondered.

Suddenly she remembered! After reading to Tommy, she had helped Mrs. Chen put books away. They worked in the cooking section together.

Grandma Rose hurried to the cooking books. Her feet made soft tapping sounds on the floor. The cooking section smelled like vanilla candles.

She looked at every shelf carefully. Cookbooks stood in neat rows. Some were thick and heavy. Others were thin and colorful.

Then she saw something red! Her heart jumped with joy.

There it was! Her special recipe book sat between two large cookbooks. It looked so small and lonely.

"Found you!" she whispered happily.

Grandma Rose hugged the book tight. The cover felt smooth and familiar. She opened it carefully. Her mother's handwriting still looked beautiful on every page.

She hurried back to Mrs. Chen's desk.

"I found it!" Grandma Rose said with a big smile. "It was with the cooking books."

Mrs. Chen clapped her hands together. "Wonderful! You didn't give up."

"I almost did," admitted Grandma Rose. "But then I remembered where I helped you yesterday."

"Sometimes we need to think step by step," said Mrs. Chen. "You were very smart to remember."

Grandma Rose felt proud and happy. She tucked the recipe book safely in her purse. Tomorrow she would make Emma's favorite chocolate chip cookies.

"Thank you for helping me," she told Mrs. Chen.

"You helped yourself," smiled Mrs. Chen. "You kept trying until you found it."

Grandma Rose walked home with light steps. The sun felt warm on her face. Birds sang in the trees above.

That evening, she opened her recipe book. She read her mother's cookie recipe twice. Tomorrow would be perfect.

The next day, Emma's birthday party was wonderful. The cookies were sweet and chewy. Everyone asked for the recipe.

"The secret ingredient is never giving up," laughed Grandma Rose.

Emma hugged her tight. "These are the best cookies ever, Grandma!"

Grandma Rose smiled. Sometimes the best solutions come when you keep trying and think carefully about your steps.

The end.
