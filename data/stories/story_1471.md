# Story story_1471

**Benny's Big Splash**

Benny the bright red bike lived by Willow Pond. He loved rolling through the soft grass. He loved feeling the warm sunshine on his shiny handlebars.

But Benny had a big problem. He was very, very dirty.

Mud covered his wheels. Leaves stuck to his seat. His red paint looked brown and yucky. Even the little ducks by the pond wrinkled their beaks when they saw him.

"Benny needs a bath," said Mama Duck to her babies.

Benny heard her words. His bell jingled with worry. "I don't want a bath!" he said to himself. "Water is scary and cold!"

The pond water looked deep and dark. Benny rolled backward when he saw it. "No way!" he squeaked. "I'll stay dirty forever!"

But being dirty made Benny sad. Children walked right past him. They didn't want to ride a messy bike. Benny's bell drooped down low.

"I need to get clean," Benny sighed. "But how?"

First, Benny tried rolling in the dry grass. He spun his wheels fast. He rubbed against the tall weeds. The grass tickled his spokes.

"This will work!" Benny said hopefully.

But the mud stayed stuck. The leaves wouldn't come off. Benny looked just as dirty as before. Maybe even dirtier!

"Oh no," Benny groaned. "That didn't work at all."

Benny felt very discouraged. His handlebars drooped. His pedals felt heavy and sad.

Then he heard gentle splashing sounds. The baby ducks were playing in the shallow water. They looked so happy and clean!

"The water doesn't look scary there," Benny noticed. "It's not deep at all."

The shallow part of the pond was warm and sunny. Little fish swam in circles. The water sparkled like diamonds.

Benny took a deep breath. "I can try again," he said bravely.

Very slowly, Benny rolled toward the water. His front wheel touched the pond. The water felt warm and nice!

"This isn't scary," Benny whispered. "It feels good!"

He rolled in just a little bit. The water reached his pedals. It felt like a gentle hug.

Splash! Splash! The baby ducks swam over to help. They used their little beaks to pull off the stuck leaves. The fish nibbled away the stubborn mud.

"Thank you, friends!" Benny laughed. His bell rang with joy.

The warm water washed over his wheels. It cleaned his muddy spokes. It made his red paint shine bright again.

Benny rolled back and forth in the shallow water. The sun made rainbow drops on his handlebars. He felt fresh and clean and wonderful.

"I love my bath!" Benny called out happily.

A little girl named Lucy walked by the pond. She saw Benny's shiny red paint gleaming in the sunshine.

"What a beautiful bike!" Lucy said with delight. "May I ride you?"

Benny's bell rang the happiest song ever. "Yes, yes, yes!" he seemed to say.

Lucy climbed on Benny's clean seat. They rode around the pond together. The wind felt cool and fresh. Benny's wheels turned smoothly on the path.

"You're the best bike ever," Lucy giggled.

Benny felt proud and happy. His red paint sparkled. His chrome handlebars gleamed like silver.

From that day on, Benny wasn't afraid of baths anymore. Every week, he rolled into the shallow pond. The ducks always helped him get clean.

Benny learned something very important. Sometimes things that seem scary aren't scary at all. Sometimes you just need to try a different way.

And sometimes, when you don't give up, everything works out perfectly.

Now Benny was the cleanest, happiest bike by Willow Pond. Children loved to ride him. The ducks loved to splash with him.

And Benny loved his weekly baths in the warm, sunny water.

"Being clean feels wonderful!" Benny would ring to all his friends.

And it really, truly did.

The end.
