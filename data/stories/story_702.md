# Story story_702

**The Library Friends**

Benny was a small brown dog. He lived in the town library. Every day, he played alone between the tall bookshelves. His tail wagged slowly as he walked around.

The library was quiet and cozy. Sunlight came through big windows. It made warm spots on the soft carpet. Benny liked to lie in the sunny places. But sometimes he felt lonely.

"I wish I had a friend," Benny said softly. His brown ears drooped down. He sniffed the air. It smelled like old books and fresh flowers.

On a high shelf sat Puzzle. Puzzle was a colorful jigsaw puzzle in a red box. She had one thousand pieces inside. Her box showed a picture of a rainbow garden. Puzzle watched Benny every day.

"That little dog looks sad," Puzzle thought. "Maybe I can help him feel better."

Puzzle wiggled and wiggled. She pushed herself to the edge of the shelf. Down, down, down she tumbled! Her box opened up. Colorful pieces scattered everywhere.

"Oh my!" said Benny. He ran over quickly. "Are you hurt?"

"I'm okay," said Puzzle cheerfully. "I wanted to meet you. You looked lonely."

Benny's tail started wagging faster. "You wanted to be my friend?"

"Yes!" said Puzzle. "Let's play together!"

Benny looked at all the puzzle pieces. They were scattered on the carpet. Some pieces were blue like the sky. Others were green like grass. Many were bright yellow and pink.

"How do we play?" asked Benny.

"Help me put my pieces together," said Puzzle. "We can make the rainbow garden!"

Benny picked up a blue piece in his mouth. It felt smooth and cool. He placed it gently on the carpet. Puzzle guided him with her voice.

"That piece goes here," she said kindly. "Try the yellow one next."

Benny worked carefully. His pink tongue stuck out a little. He was concentrating hard. Piece by piece, the garden began to grow.

"You're doing great!" said Puzzle happily.

The two friends worked all morning. Benny's tail never stopped wagging. He loved having someone to play with. Puzzle felt happy too. She had never had a helper before.

Slowly, the rainbow garden came together. First the blue sky appeared. Then green grass and tall trees. Beautiful flowers bloomed across the picture. Red roses, yellow daisies, and purple violets.

"We did it!" barked Benny excitedly. He danced around the completed puzzle. His paws made soft tapping sounds on the carpet.

"We make a great team," said Puzzle proudly. The finished garden sparkled in the sunlight.

Mrs. Chen, the librarian, found them together. She smiled when she saw the beautiful puzzle. "What wonderful teamwork," she said gently. She petted Benny's soft fur.

From that day on, Benny and Puzzle played together every day. Sometimes they built the rainbow garden puzzle. Other times they made different pictures. A farm with red barns. A beach with blue waves. A forest with tall green trees.

Benny was never lonely anymore. He had found his best friend. Puzzle was happy too. She loved helping Benny learn new things.

The other library visitors enjoyed watching them play. Children would sit nearby and help sometimes. The library felt warmer and friendlier with Benny and Puzzle there.

Every evening, they would rest together. Benny would curl up next to Puzzle's box. The sunset made everything glow orange and pink.

"Thank you for being my friend," Benny would whisper.

"Thank you for playing with me," Puzzle would whisper back.

The library was quiet and peaceful. But it was never lonely anymore. Two friends had found each other. They had learned that the best games are the ones you play together.

Benny's tail wagged softly as he fell asleep. Tomorrow would bring another day of fun with his dear friend Puzzle. The rainbow garden would bloom again.

The end.
