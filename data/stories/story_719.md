# Story story_719

Max sat at the kitchen table. His stomach made a little growling sound. Mom smiled and opened the refrigerator door.

"Time for lunch, Max!" she said cheerfully.

Max looked at his empty plate. He felt tired and not very hungry. Sometimes lunch felt like work. He had to sit still. He had to use his fork properly. He had to try new foods.

"I don't want lunch," Max said quietly. He put his chin in his hands.

Mom sat down next to him. "What if we made lunch special today?" she asked.

Max shrugged his shoulders. He watched Mom take out bread, cheese, and apples. She placed them on the counter with a soft thud.

"Close your eyes, Max," Mom said with a twinkle in her voice.

Max squeezed his eyes shut tight. He heard Mom moving around the kitchen. Pots clinked softly. The refrigerator hummed. Something smelled good and buttery.

"Now open them!" Mom announced.

Max opened his eyes wide. On his plate sat a sandwich cut into the shape of a star! Golden cheese melted at the edges. Apple slices formed a rainbow around the star. A small cup held ranch dressing that looked like fluffy white clouds.

"Wow!" Max gasped. His eyes grew big and round.

"This is a magic star sandwich," Mom explained. "It gives you energy to fly like a superhero."

Max picked up the star sandwich carefully. It felt warm in his small hands. He took a tiny bite. The bread was soft and the cheese was creamy. It tasted like sunshine.

"I can feel the magic!" Max said with his mouth full.

Mom laughed her musical laugh. "What kind of superhero are you today?"

Max thought hard while he chewed. "I'm Super Max! I help animals and fly really fast."

"Super Max needs good fuel," Mom said. She made flying sounds as Max ate his apple rainbow. "Whoosh! There goes Super Max!"

Max giggled and ate another apple slice. The fruit was crisp and sweet. Juice dripped down his chin. Mom wiped it with a soft napkin.

"Look! The ranch clouds make everything taste better," Max discovered. He dipped his star sandwich and took a big bite.

"Super Max is getting stronger," Mom observed. She flexed her arm muscles.

Max flexed too. "I can feel my muscles growing!" He ate faster now, enjoying each bite.

The kitchen felt warm and cozy. Sunlight streamed through the window. Max's plate became emptier and emptier. His tummy felt happy and full.

"All gone!" Max announced proudly. He held up his empty plate.

"Super Max finished his magic lunch," Mom cheered. She clapped her hands together.

Max stood up and pretended to fly around the kitchen. "Zoom! I have so much energy now!"

Mom caught him in a big hug. "You did such a good job eating lunch today."

Max felt proud and satisfied. His body felt strong and ready for afternoon adventures. Lunch hadn't been work at all. It had been fun and magical.

"Mom, can we have magic lunch again tomorrow?" Max asked hopefully.

"What kind of magic would you like?" Mom wondered aloud.

Max thought carefully. "Maybe rocket ship sandwiches? Or dinosaur fruit?"

"Those sound perfect for Super Max," Mom agreed. She kissed the top of his head.

Max felt excited about tomorrow's lunch already. His tummy was happy. His heart was happy too. Magic could happen anywhere, even at the lunch table.

"I love magic lunches," Max declared. He hugged Mom tight.

"I love making them for you," Mom replied softly.

Max skipped to his room, ready for his afternoon nap. Dreams of flying and star sandwiches filled his head. Tomorrow would bring new lunch magic. He could hardly wait.

Outside, the sun shone brightly on their little house. Inside, Max drifted off to sleep with a smile on his face.

The end.
