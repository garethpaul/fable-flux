# Moo and the Storm Clouds

Moo the cow loved her special sandbox. Every morning, she would waddle over. Her hooves made soft *thump-thump* sounds. The warm sand felt wonderful between her toes.

Moo was building a tall castle today. She patted the sand carefully. *Pat, pat, pat.* The tower grew higher and higher. "This is my best castle ever!" she said happily.

Suddenly, dark clouds rolled across the sky. They looked big and scary. The wind began to blow. *Whoooosh!* Moo's ears flapped in the breeze.

"Oh no!" said Moo. "A storm is coming! What if it ruins my beautiful castle?" Her brown eyes grew wide with worry. The clouds looked darker and darker.

Thunder rumbled far away. *Rumble, rumble.* Moo's tail swished nervously. "I need to protect my castle," she said. "But how?"

First, Moo tried covering her castle. She found some big leaves. She placed them gently on top. But the wind blew them away. *Whoosh!* The leaves danced through the air.

"That didn't work," sighed Moo. She tapped her hoof thoughtfully. *Tap, tap, tap.*

Next, Moo tried building walls around her castle. She scooped up handfuls of sand. She packed them into little hills. But the sand was too dry. The walls crumbled down. *Plop, plop, plop.*

"Hmm," said Moo. "I need to think harder." She sat down in her sandbox. The sand felt cool and soft.

Then Moo had another idea. "Maybe I need water!" she said. She ran to get her watering can. The can was red and shiny. Water sloshed inside. *Slosh, slosh.*

Moo sprinkled water on her sand walls. The sand became darker and stickier. She pressed it together firmly. The walls stayed up this time!

But the storm clouds were getting closer. Big raindrops started to fall. *Pitter, patter, pitter, patter.* They made tiny holes in the sand.

Moo felt worried again. Her castle still wasn't safe enough. She needed something stronger.

Then she spotted her beach umbrella. It was bright yellow like sunshine. "Perfect!" said Moo. She opened the umbrella wide. *Pop!*

Moo pushed the umbrella into the sand. It stood up straight and tall. The yellow fabric flapped gently. Now her castle had a roof!

More raindrops fell. *Pitter, patter, pitter, patter.* But they bounced off the umbrella. Her castle stayed completely dry underneath.

"It's working!" cheered Moo. She clapped her hooves together. *Clap, clap, clap.*

The storm arrived with lots of rain. *Pitter, patter, PITTER, PATTER.* Wind blew the trees. But Moo's castle was safe and dry. The umbrella kept every raindrop away.

Moo sat under her umbrella too. She watched the rain fall all around. It made pretty patterns in the sand. The air smelled fresh and clean.

"I'm so glad I didn't give up," said Moo. "Even when my first ideas didn't work." She patted her castle gently. The sand felt cool and damp.

After a while, the storm passed by. The sun came out again. It made the wet sand sparkle. Everything looked shiny and new.

Moo's friend Benny Bunny hopped over. "Wow!" said Benny. "Your castle looks amazing! How did you keep it safe?"

"I tried many different ideas," said Moo proudly. "Some didn't work at first. But I kept trying new things. Then I found the perfect solution!"

Benny's ears perked up. "You're so smart, Moo!"

Moo smiled a big, happy smile. "When problems seem big and scary, we just need to keep trying. There's always a way to solve them!"

The two friends played in the sandbox together. They built more castles and roads. The sand felt perfect for building now. Not too wet, not too dry.

As the sun set, Moo looked at her special castle. It stood tall and proud. The yellow umbrella still protected it.

"Tomorrow I'll try building something new," said Moo. "And if problems come again, I know I can solve them!"

She felt proud, happy, and ready for anything.

The end.
