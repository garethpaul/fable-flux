# Benny's Big Block Hunt

In a sunny green field, there lived a bright red bike named Benny. Benny had shiny wheels and a cheerful bell. He loved to ride around the soft grass. Most of all, Benny loved playing with his colorful blocks.

Every morning, Benny would stack his blocks. He made tall towers and funny shapes. The blue blocks felt smooth and cool. The yellow blocks were warm from the sun. When he finished playing, Benny always put his blocks under the big oak tree.

But this morning was different. Benny rolled to his special spot. He looked left. He looked right. His blocks were gone!

"Oh no!" said Benny. His bell jingled sadly. "Where are my blocks?"

Benny felt worried. His favorite blocks had disappeared! He took a deep breath. "I won't give up," he said. "I'll find them!"

First, Benny searched near the oak tree. He rolled around slowly. He looked behind every root. He checked under fallen leaves. The leaves crunched and crackled. But no blocks appeared.

"Maybe they rolled away," thought Benny. He pedaled to the flower patch. Pretty pink flowers swayed in the breeze. Benny looked between the stems. He found a busy bee and three ladybugs. But still no blocks.

Benny's wheels felt tired. His bell drooped down. "This is hard work," he sighed. But then he remembered something important. "Good things happen when I keep trying!"

Next, Benny rolled to the pond. The water sparkled like diamonds. Little fish swam in circles. Frogs went "ribbit, ribbit!" Benny looked all around the muddy edge. He found smooth stones and cattails. But where were his blocks?

Benny sat still for a moment. He listened carefully. The wind whispered through the grass. Birds chirped happy songs. Then he heard something new. A soft giggling sound!

"That's strange," said Benny. He followed the sound. It came from behind a big rock. Benny rolled closer and closer. The giggling got louder!

Behind the rock, Benny found something wonderful. Three little field mice were playing! They had built a tiny castle with his blocks. The red blocks made walls. The blue blocks made towers. The yellow blocks made a perfect door.

"Those are my blocks!" said Benny. But he wasn't angry. The mice looked so happy. Their whiskers twitched with joy. Their tiny paws clapped together.

"We're sorry," squeaked the smallest mouse. "We found these pretty blocks. We wanted to build something special."

Benny's bell jingled thoughtfully. These little mice had worked so hard. Their castle was beautiful! The blocks fit together perfectly. Benny felt proud of his blocks. But he also felt something else. He felt kind.

"Your castle is amazing!" said Benny. "Would you like to play together?"

The mice jumped up and down. "Really?" they squeaked. "You'll share with us?"

"Of course!" said Benny. "Playing is more fun with friends!"

So Benny and the mice played together. They built the tallest tower ever. They made a bridge across a tiny stream. They created a rainbow with all the colors. Red, blue, yellow, green!

When the sun began to set, they carefully put the blocks away. The mice helped carry them back to the oak tree. They stacked them in a neat pile.

"Thank you for sharing," said the mice. "Can we play again tomorrow?"

"Yes!" said Benny. His bell rang with happiness. "Meet me here when the sun comes up!"

As the mice scurried home, Benny felt wonderful. He had solved his problem! He found his blocks by not giving up. Even better, he made new friends.

Benny rolled in happy circles around the field. The stars began to twinkle above. "I'm so glad I kept looking," he said. "Good things really do happen when you don't give up!"

That night, Benny dreamed of building castles and towers. He dreamed of his new mouse friends. And he smiled, knowing that tomorrow would bring another day of fun and friendship in his favorite field.

The End.
