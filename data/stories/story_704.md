# Story story_704

**Toby's Snack Time Adventure**

The big clock in the train yard said three o'clock. All the trains knew what that meant. Snack time!

"Choo-choo!" called Engineer Emma. "Time for fuel, Toby!"

Toby the little blue train puffed slowly. He didn't want snack time today. The other trains always ate so fast. They made loud slurping sounds. Toby felt shy about eating with everyone.

"I'm not hungry," Toby whispered. His wheels barely moved.

Emma climbed into Toby's cab. She patted his dashboard gently. "What's wrong, little friend?"

"The other trains eat so quickly," Toby said. "They make big noises. I eat slowly. They might laugh at me."

Emma smiled warmly. "Would you like to try something special today?"

Toby's headlight flickered with curiosity. "What do you mean?"

Emma pulled out a shiny whistle. It sparkled like a rainbow in the sunshine. "This is my magic snack whistle. When I blow it, something wonderful happens."

She put the whistle to her lips. *Toot-toot-toodle-oo!*

Suddenly, colorful butterflies appeared! They danced around Toby's smokestack. Sweet music filled the air. The butterflies spelled out words in the sky: "SLOW AND STEADY!"

"Wow!" gasped Toby. His wheels wiggled with excitement.

"The butterflies want to watch you eat," Emma said. "They love trains who take their time."

Toby rolled slowly to the fuel station. The butterflies followed him. They fluttered around his coal car like tiny friends.

Thomas the Tank Engine was there. So was Rosie the Pink Engine. They were eating their coal quickly as always.

"Hi, Toby!" called Thomas. "Want to eat with us?"

Toby felt nervous. But the butterflies tickled his smokestack softly. Their wings felt like gentle feathers. This made him giggle.

"Okay," Toby said quietly.

Emma poured fresh, sweet-smelling coal into Toby's tender. The coal pieces looked like little black diamonds. They clinked softly as they fell.

Toby took one small piece. He chewed it slowly. The coal tasted rich and warm. It made his engine purr happily.

The butterflies clapped their tiny wings. They seemed to cheer for Toby!

"You're a good eater," said Rosie kindly. "I like how carefully you chew."

Thomas nodded. "My grandpa always said slow eating helps engines run better."

Toby felt warm inside. Not just from the coal. The other trains weren't laughing. They were being nice!

He took another piece of coal. This time he wasn't scared. The butterflies danced around him. Their colorful wings made pretty shadows.

"This coal tastes like sunshine," Toby said happily.

Emma laughed. "That's because you're taking time to really taste it."

Toby ate three more pieces. Each bite tasted better than the last. His boiler felt cozy and full. Steam puffed gently from his funnel.

The other trains finished eating. But they didn't leave. They stayed to talk with Toby.

"Want to play after snack time?" asked Thomas.

"We could race to the bridge," suggested Rosie.

Toby's whistle tooted with joy. "Yes, please!"

The butterflies began to fade away. But they left sparkles in the air. Emma winked at Toby.

"See you tomorrow at snack time?" she asked.

Toby's headlight beamed brightly. "Yes! I can't wait!"

The next day at three o'clock, Toby rolled quickly to the fuel station. He wasn't scared anymore. He knew snack time could be fun.

The other trains welcomed him warmly. Toby ate slowly and happily. He tasted every bite. He talked with his friends.

Emma watched from nearby. She smiled as Toby laughed with the other trains.

"Magic happens," she whispered, "when we're brave enough to try."

Toby's engine hummed contentedly. Snack time had become his favorite time of day. He learned that being different wasn't bad. It made him special.

From that day on, Toby looked forward to three o'clock. He knew good friends and tasty fuel were waiting for him.

The end.
