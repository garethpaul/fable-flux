# Nutkin's Big Adventure

In a tall oak tree lived a little squirrel named Nutkin. His fur was soft and brown. His tail was big and fluffy. Nutkin loved his cozy nest high up in the branches.

Every morning, Mama Squirrel would brush Nutkin's fur. She would sing sweet songs to him. "Good morning, my little acorn," she would say. Nutkin felt safe and happy.

But today was different. Today was a very special day. Nutkin was old enough to find his own acorns! All young squirrels must learn this important job.

"Time to go, sweet Nutkin," Mama said gently. "You must practice finding food by yourself."

Nutkin's little heart felt heavy. His whiskers drooped down sadly. "But Mama, I don't want to leave you," he whispered.

Mama gave him a warm, fuzzy hug. "I will be right here in our tree. You are brave and smart. I believe in you."

Nutkin climbed down the rough bark slowly. Each step felt scary. The forest floor seemed so far from Mama. His tiny paws shook a little.

"I miss Mama already," Nutkin said to himself. A tear rolled down his furry cheek.

First, Nutkin tried looking under some leaves. Crunch, crunch, crunch went his paws. But he only found old twigs. No acorns anywhere.

"This is too hard," Nutkin sighed. He wanted to run back to Mama. But he remembered her words: "You are brave and smart."

Next, Nutkin tried digging near a big rock. Scratch, scratch, scratch went his claws. The dirt felt cool and damp. Still no acorns to be found.

Nutkin sat down and sniffled. "Maybe I'm not good at this," he thought sadly.

Then he heard a gentle voice. "Hello, little friend!" It was Rosie Rabbit, hopping by with her fluffy white tail bouncing.

"I'm trying to find acorns," Nutkin explained. "But I miss my mama. Everything feels too hard."

Rosie's pink nose wiggled kindly. "I understand feeling sad. When I first left my burrow, I felt scared too. But you know what helped me?"

"What?" asked Nutkin, wiping his eyes.

"I remembered that my mama's love stays with me everywhere I go. Even when she's not right beside me."

Nutkin thought about this. He could almost feel Mama's warm hug in his heart.

"And," Rosie continued, "acorns like to hide under the biggest, oldest trees. Try looking there!"

Nutkin's ears perked up. He felt a little spark of hope. "Thank you, Rosie!"

Nutkin scampered to the biggest oak tree he could find. Its trunk was wide and strong. He looked up and saw acorns hanging high above.

Nutkin climbed up, up, up. His sharp claws gripped the bark perfectly. Higher and higher he went. The wind rustled through the green leaves.

There they were! Beautiful brown acorns, round and smooth. Nutkin carefully picked three perfect ones.

"I did it!" Nutkin cheered. "I really did it!"

As he climbed down with his acorns, Nutkin realized something wonderful. He didn't feel sad anymore. He felt proud and strong. Mama's love was still with him, warm in his heart.

Nutkin raced back to his tree as fast as his little legs could carry him. "Mama! Mama! Look what I found!"

Mama Squirrel's eyes sparkled with joy. "Oh, Nutkin! You found such beautiful acorns! I'm so proud of you!"

"I was sad to leave you, Mama," Nutkin said. "But then I remembered your love stays with me always. And I made a new friend who helped me!"

Mama hugged him tight. "That's exactly right, my brave little squirrel. Love travels with us wherever we go."

That night, Nutkin snuggled in his cozy nest. He looked at his three perfect acorns. Tomorrow, he would go out again. He wasn't scared anymore.

Nutkin had learned that missing someone you love is okay. But love makes you strong enough for any adventure.

And with that happy thought, Nutkin fell fast asleep, dreaming of all the wonderful things tomorrow might bring.

The end.
