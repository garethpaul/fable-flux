# Sam's Missing Red Car

Sam loved playing on Grandpa's farm. The big red barn smelled like hay. The chickens clucked in their pen. The cows mooed softly in the field.

But today, Sam felt sad. His favorite red toy car was missing! He had played with it yesterday. Now he couldn't find it anywhere.

"Grandpa, I can't find my car!" Sam said. His voice sounded wobbly.

Grandpa smiled kindly. "Let's look together, Sam. Where did you play yesterday?"

Sam thought hard. "I played near the chicken coop first."

They walked to the chicken coop. Sam got down on his knees. He looked under the wooden fence. He checked behind the water dish. The chickens clucked curiously at him.

"Not here," Sam sighed.

"Where else did you play?" Grandpa asked gently.

"By the apple tree!" Sam remembered.

They walked to the big apple tree. Sam looked all around the thick trunk. He searched through the fallen leaves. They crackled under his feet. A red apple fell down, but it wasn't his car.

"Still not here," Sam said quietly.

Sam felt like crying. But Grandpa patted his shoulder. "Don't give up, Sam. Think some more."

Sam closed his eyes tight. He remembered something else. "I played in the barn too!"

Inside the barn, it was cool and dim. Dust sparkled in the sunlight. Sam looked behind hay bales. He checked near the farm tools. He even looked in the empty horse stall.

Nothing.

Sam's eyes filled with tears. "My car is gone forever!"

"Wait," Grandpa said softly. "Did you play anywhere else?"

Sam wiped his nose. He thought very hard. "Oh! I played by the pig pen too!"

They walked to the muddy pig pen. The pigs were sleeping in the sunshine. Sam looked carefully around the fence. He checked near their food trough.

Then he saw something red! But it was just a red bucket.

Sam sat down on the ground. He felt very tired. "I looked everywhere, Grandpa."

Grandpa sat beside him. "You worked very hard, Sam. You didn't give up. That's what good problem-solvers do."

Just then, they heard a funny sound. "Oink, oink, CLANK!"

One pig was awake now. It was pushing something with its nose. Something small and red!

"My car!" Sam shouted happily.

The pig had been playing with it! Sam's red car was covered in mud, but it was safe. The pig nudged it toward the fence.

"Thank you, pig!" Sam laughed.

Grandpa helped Sam reach through the fence. Sam picked up his muddy car. It felt wonderful in his hands again.

"Let's clean it up," Grandpa said.

At the water pump, Sam washed his car carefully. The red paint shined bright again. The little wheels spun perfectly.

"It looks brand new!" Sam said proudly.

"You found it because you kept trying," Grandpa said. "You looked in lots of places. You didn't give up when it was hard."

Sam nodded. "I was sad, but I kept looking."

"That's called persistence, Sam. It means trying even when things are difficult."

Sam drove his clean car in the dirt. It made perfect little tracks. "I'll remember to put it in my pocket next time."

"Good thinking!" Grandpa chuckled.

That evening, Sam told Grandma the whole story. He acted out looking everywhere. He made pig noises when he got to the best part.

"I was persistent!" Sam said proudly.

Grandma hugged him tight. "You sure were, sweetheart."

Before bed, Sam put his red car on the nightstand. Right where he could see it. He smiled sleepily.

Tomorrow he would play with it again. And he would remember: when something seems lost, keep looking. Try different places. Ask for help. Never give up.

Problems have solutions. You just have to be persistent to find them.

Sam closed his eyes, feeling proud and happy. His red car was safe beside him, ready for tomorrow's adventures on the farm.

The end.
