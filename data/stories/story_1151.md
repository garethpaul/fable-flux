# Story story_1151

**The Sleepy Block Tower**

Lily loved her colorful blocks. Red ones, blue ones, yellow ones too. She built tall towers every day. But now Mama said something scary.

"Time for bed, Lily."

Lily shook her head. "No! Want to play more!"

Her blocks sat scattered on the soft carpet. The red block looked sad. The blue block seemed tired. Even the bright yellow block looked sleepy.

"But I'm not done building," Lily said. Her bottom lip stuck out.

Mama sat down beside her. "What if we make bedtime special?"

Lily picked up her favorite purple block. It felt smooth and warm in her hands. "How?"

"Let's build one last tower together," Mama said. "A sleepy-time tower."

Lily's eyes grew wide. "What's that?"

Mama smiled. "A magical tower that helps everyone sleep."

They started with the biggest red block. Lily placed it carefully on the floor. It made a soft thunk sound.

"This block is tired from holding up towers all day," Mama whispered.

Next came the blue block. Lily set it gently on top. "Is this block sleepy too?"

"Very sleepy," Mama said. "It's been working hard."

The yellow block went on third. Then the green one. Each block looked ready for rest.

"Now the purple block," Mama said softly.

Lily held her special purple block. She gave it a tiny kiss. "Good night, purple block."

The block fit perfectly on top. The tower stood tall and proud.

"Now what?" Lily asked.

"Now we sing the blocks to sleep," Mama said.

They hummed a gentle lullaby. "La la la, sleepy blocks. La la la, time to rest."

Something wonderful happened. The blocks seemed to glow in the dim light. They looked cozy and peaceful.

"They're really sleeping!" Lily whispered.

"Just like you need to," Mama said. "Blocks need rest to be strong tomorrow."

Lily yawned. Suddenly she felt sleepy too. "Will they wait for me?"

"Of course," Mama said. "They'll dream about all the fun towers you'll build."

Lily carefully covered the blocks with her soft baby blanket. "Sleep tight, blocks."

She brushed her teeth with the minty toothpaste. It tasted fresh and clean. Then she put on her favorite pajamas with the little stars.

In her cozy bed, Lily snuggled under her warm covers. The room felt peaceful and safe.

"Mama," she said softly. "The blocks looked happy."

"They were happy," Mama said. "You took good care of them."

"Will they really dream?" Lily asked.

Mama tucked the blanket under Lily's chin. "I think they'll dream about rainbow towers and castle walls."

Lily smiled. "And rocket ships too?"

"Especially rocket ships," Mama said.

Through her window, Lily could see the moon. It looked like a big white block in the dark sky. The stars twinkled like tiny yellow blocks.

"Tomorrow I'll build a moon tower," Lily said sleepily.

"That sounds wonderful," Mama whispered.

Lily closed her eyes. She could almost hear her blocks sleeping peacefully. They made tiny, happy sighs.

"Good night, Mama," Lily said.

"Good night, my little builder," Mama said.

As Lily drifted off to sleep, she dreamed about her blocks. In her dream, they danced and played. They built themselves into amazing shapes.

The red block became a fire truck. The blue block turned into an ocean wave. The yellow block shined like the sun.

And her special purple block? It became a magic carpet that carried her through the clouds.

When morning came, Lily woke up excited. She ran to check on her blocks. They sat under the blanket, ready for a new day.

"Good morning, blocks!" she said cheerfully.

The blocks seemed to sparkle in the sunshine. They were rested and ready to play.

Lily couldn't wait to build that moon tower. Bedtime had made everything better.

Now she knew the secret. Taking care of her blocks meant taking care of herself too.

"Tonight we'll build a star tower," she told them happily.

And the blocks, if they could talk, would have said they couldn't wait.

The end.
