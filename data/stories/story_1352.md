# Story story_1352

**Penny's First Flight**

The morning sun painted the toy room golden. All the toys stretched and yawned. It was playtime!

The teddy bears rolled over happily. The toy cars zoomed across the carpet. The dolls brushed their hair with tiny combs.

But Penny the little plane stayed quiet in her corner. She watched the other toys play together. Her red wings felt heavy. Her propeller drooped down.

"Come play with us, Penny!" called Zoom the race car. His wheels spun excitedly on the soft rug.

Penny shook her nose sadly. "I don't know how to be fun," she whispered. "I just sit here all day."

The other toys had tried before. They rolled balls to her. They sang songs near her. But Penny always felt too scared to join in.

Today felt different though. A warm breeze drifted through the open window. It smelled like flowers and fresh grass. The breeze tickled Penny's wings gently.

"What if I could really fly?" Penny thought. She wiggled her propeller just a tiny bit.

Something magical happened. Her propeller began to spin! Slowly at first, then faster and faster. Whirr, whirr, whirr!

"Look!" gasped Bella the ballerina doll. "Penny's propeller is moving!"

The spinning felt wonderful. Penny's whole body started to vibrate with excitement. The breeze from the window grew stronger. It lifted her just a little bit off the shelf.

"I'm floating!" Penny squealed with joy. "I'm really floating!"

She bobbed gently in the air like a butterfly. The other toys cheered and clapped their hands. Their happy sounds filled the room with music.

Penny discovered she could steer herself. She glided over to visit Zoom the race car. "Want to race?" she asked, hovering beside him.

"Yes!" Zoom revved his engine. They raced around the toy box together. Penny flew above while Zoom rolled below.

Next, Penny floated over to the tea party corner. The dolls were having pretend cookies and juice. "May I join?" Penny asked politely.

"Of course!" said Bella. She poured pretend tea into a tiny cup. Penny hovered at the perfect height to sip from it.

The building blocks needed help too. Penny could fly high and place blocks on top of tall towers. The blocks felt smooth and cool in her grip.

"This is amazing!" laughed Tommy the teddy bear. "You can reach places we can't!"

Penny felt warm and happy inside. Her red paint seemed to shine brighter. Her propeller hummed a cheerful tune.

She gave airplane rides to the small toys. The toy soldiers marched onto her back. She flew them around the room on patrol missions. They felt safe and brave up high with her.

The toy mice squeaked with delight as Penny carried them over the dollhouse. They could see everything from up there! The tiny furniture looked perfect from above.

Even the shy puzzle pieces wanted rides. Penny gently picked them up one by one. She helped them find their correct spots in the big puzzle.

As the sun moved across the sky, Penny felt tired but wonderful. Her propeller slowed down gradually. She settled softly onto her shelf.

"That was the best playtime ever!" she sighed happily. Her wings felt light and proud now.

All the toys gathered around her shelf. "Will you play with us again tomorrow?" asked Zoom hopefully.

Penny's propeller gave one more happy spin. "Yes! I can't wait for tomorrow's adventures!"

She looked out the window at the real airplanes flying high in the clouds. "Maybe someday I'll fly that high too," she whispered.

But for now, she was perfectly happy. She had discovered something wonderful. Playing with friends made everything more fun. Being helpful made her feel important.

The toy room grew quiet as evening came. Penny closed her eyes with a big smile. Tomorrow would bring new games, new friends, and new ways to fly.

Her propeller twitched once more in her dreams. In those dreams, she soared through cotton candy clouds with all her toy friends riding safely on her wings.

The end.
