# Story story_1466

**Finny's First Walk**

Finny the goldfish lived in a big glass bowl. The bowl sat on a table by the window. Every day, Finny watched the world outside.

"Time for walk time!" said Mom to her little boy Sam.

Sam always took his toy boat to the park. But today, he had a new idea.

"Can Finny come too?" Sam asked.

Mom smiled. "Fish can't walk, sweetie."

"But Finny looks sad," Sam said. "He wants to see the park."

Finny pressed his nose against the glass. He did want to see more than just the window view.

Mom thought for a moment. "We could carry his bowl very carefully."

Finny's fins drooped. He felt scared. What if the bowl tipped? What if the sun was too bright? What if other animals were mean?

"I don't want to go," Finny bubbled quietly.

But Sam was already getting ready. He put on his red shoes. He found his favorite hat.

"Don't worry, Finny," Sam whispered. "I'll keep you safe."

Mom wrapped a soft towel around Finny's bowl. Sam carried it like precious treasure. Each step was slow and careful.

Outside, Finny blinked in the bright sunlight. Everything looked so different! The sky was huge and blue. Fluffy white clouds drifted by.

"Wow," Finny whispered.

At the park, Sam set the bowl on a picnic table. Finny could see everything from up high.

Children ran and played. Dogs wagged their tails. Birds sang in the trees.

"Look, Finny!" Sam pointed. "There's the pond!"

The pond was bigger than anything Finny had ever seen. Ducks swam in circles. Water sparkled like diamonds.

"I wish I could swim there," Finny sighed.

Just then, a gentle breeze blew. It made tiny waves in Finny's bowl. The waves caught the sunlight and made rainbow colors.

"Oh!" Finny gasped with joy.

A friendly duck waddled over. "Hello, little fish," she quacked softly. "Welcome to our park."

"You can talk to me?" Finny asked, amazed.

"All water friends can talk to each other," the duck smiled. "We've been waiting to meet you."

A butterfly landed on Finny's bowl. Her wings were orange and black. "The flowers told us you were coming," she said sweetly.

Finny couldn't believe it. He had friends everywhere!

Sam played on the swings nearby. He checked on Finny every few minutes. "Are you having fun?" he asked.

"The best fun ever!" Finny bubbled happily.

A squirrel scampered down from a tree. "Want to hear about my acorn collection?" he chattered.

Finny listened to wonderful stories. The duck told him about flying south. The butterfly shared secrets about flower nectar. The squirrel described his cozy tree home.

The sun felt warm on Finny's fins. Fresh air smelled like grass and flowers. He could hear children laughing and birds singing.

"This is magical," Finny thought.

When it was time to go home, Finny felt sad to leave. But he also felt excited.

"Thank you for bringing me," he told Sam.

"Did you really have fun?" Sam asked.

"The most wonderful time!" Finny said.

Back home, Finny's bowl felt different. It still sat by the window. But now he knew what was beyond the glass.

He could smell the memory of fresh air. He could feel the warmth of sunshine. He could hear the echo of new friends' voices.

That night, Finny dreamed of ducks and butterflies. He dreamed of sparkling water and rainbow waves.

In the morning, Sam ran to the bowl. "Good morning, Finny!"

"Good morning!" Finny replied cheerfully.

"Want to go to the park again today?" Sam asked.

Finny's fins wiggled with excitement. "Yes, please! I can't wait!"

He pressed his nose against the glass again. But this time, he wasn't sad. He was looking forward to another adventure.

"Walk time is the best time," Finny bubbled happily.

And from that day on, Finny and Sam went to the park every sunny afternoon. Together, they discovered that the world was full of magic and friendship.

The end.
