# Story story_770

**Robin's Colorful Discovery**

"Art time!" called Miss Lily cheerfully. She clapped her hands together. The other children rushed to the art table. They grabbed crayons and paper with big smiles.

Robin the little bird sat on her perch. She watched from across the room. Her bright yellow feathers looked dull today. Robin did not want to join art time.

"I can't hold crayons," Robin whispered sadly. "My wings are too small. My beak is too pointy." She tucked her head under her wing.

Miss Lily noticed Robin sitting alone. She walked over with gentle steps. "Would you like to try something special?" Miss Lily asked softly.

Robin peeked one black eye out. "I don't know how to make art," she chirped quietly.

"Let me show you," Miss Lily said. She brought over a shallow dish. The dish held bright blue paint. It looked like a tiny lake. "Dip your feet in here."

Robin tilted her head. "My feet?" she asked. The paint felt cool and smooth. It tickled between her tiny toes. The blue color was beautiful like the morning sky.

"Now step on this white paper," Miss Lily said gently. Robin took one careful step. Then another. Tiny blue footprints appeared on the paper. They looked like little flowers blooming.

"Oh my!" Robin chirped with surprise. "I made those!" Her tail feathers wiggled with excitement.

Miss Lily smiled warmly. "Your turn to choose colors." She brought more paint dishes. Red like strawberries. Yellow like sunshine. Green like fresh grass.

Robin hopped into the yellow paint. Squish, squish went her feet. The paint felt warm and gooey. She made a trail of sunny footprints. They danced across the paper like happy thoughts.

"This is fun!" Robin sang. Her voice sounded like tiny bells. She tried the red paint next. The color was bright and cheerful. It reminded her of her favorite berries.

Soon Robin was dancing on the paper. Hop, hop, step, step. Her feet made beautiful patterns. Swirls and dots and zigzag lines. The colors mixed together like a rainbow.

"Look what I created!" Robin called to her friends. The other children gathered around. Their eyes grew wide with wonder.

"Those are the prettiest footprints ever!" said Tommy. He was painting with a big brush. Green paint covered his fingers.

"You're a real artist, Robin!" said Emma. She was drawing with purple crayons. Her picture showed a smiling sun.

Robin felt her chest puff up with pride. Her yellow feathers seemed brighter now. She had never felt so happy and proud.

Miss Lily brought clean water in a small bowl. Robin splashed her feet clean. The water felt refreshing and cool. She flapped her wings to dry them.

"Can I take my art home?" Robin asked hopefully. Her painting was full of colorful footprints. It looked like a magical garden.

"Of course," Miss Lily said. "Every artist keeps their special work." She carefully rolled up Robin's painting. The paper made a soft crinkling sound.

Robin carried her artwork proudly. She showed it to everyone she met. "I made this with my feet!" she would chirp happily.

That evening, Robin hung her painting on her favorite branch. The colors looked beautiful in the sunset light. Red and yellow and blue sparkled like jewels.

"Tomorrow is art time again," Robin whispered to herself. She could hardly wait. Her feet were already tingling with excitement.

Robin closed her eyes and dreamed. She dreamed of purple paint and orange paint. She dreamed of making more beautiful footprint pictures. Maybe she would try painting with her tail feathers too.

Art time had become Robin's favorite time. She learned that being different made her art special. Her tiny feet created magic that no one else could make.

Robin fell asleep with a smile. Tomorrow would bring new colors and new adventures. She couldn't wait to dance on paper again.

The end.
