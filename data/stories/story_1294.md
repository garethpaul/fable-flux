# Story story_1294

**Tommy and the Green Things**

Tommy sat at the big wooden table. His plate had chicken, rice, and green things. The green things were broccoli. Tommy made a yucky face.

"I don't like green things," Tommy said. He pushed the broccoli around his plate. It looked like tiny green trees. Tommy didn't want to eat trees.

Mom smiled at Tommy. "Broccoli helps you grow big and strong," she said. "Try just one bite."

Tommy shook his head. "No thank you, Mom. Green things taste bad."

Dad looked at Tommy's plate. "You need to eat your vegetables, buddy," Dad said. "No dessert until you finish them."

Tommy's eyes got big. He loved dessert! Tonight Mom made chocolate cookies. They smelled so good. The sweet smell floated from the kitchen.

Tommy picked up his fork. He poked the broccoli. It felt bumpy. He brought it close to his nose. It smelled like... nothing much.

"I have an idea!" Tommy said. He closed his eyes tight. Maybe if he couldn't see the broccoli, it would taste better.

Tommy took a big bite with his eyes closed. "Yuck!" he said. The broccoli still tasted green and mushy. Tommy spit it into his napkin.

"That didn't work," Tommy said sadly. He looked at his plate. The broccoli was still there. The cookies were still in the kitchen.

Mom sat down next to Tommy. "What's wrong with the broccoli?" she asked.

"It tastes like grass," Tommy said. "And it's mushy. I don't like mushy things."

Mom thought for a moment. "What if we try something different?" she asked. "What do you like to eat?"

"I like chicken," Tommy said. "And cheese. And ranch dressing!"

Mom's face lit up. "I have another idea! Wait here."

Mom went to the kitchen. She came back with a small bowl. The bowl had creamy white ranch dressing. It smelled tangy and good.

"Try dipping the broccoli in ranch," Mom said. "It might taste better."

Tommy looked at the ranch dressing. He loved ranch on everything. Maybe it would help the broccoli too.

Tommy picked up a piece of broccoli. He dipped it in the white dressing. The ranch covered the green bumps. Now it looked like a tiny tree with snow.

Tommy took a small bite. His eyes got wide. "It tastes good!" he said. The ranch made the broccoli creamy and tangy. The broccoli was still a little crunchy, but not mushy anymore.

"I like it with ranch!" Tommy said. He took another bite. Then another. Soon all the broccoli was gone.

Dad clapped his hands. "Great job, Tommy!" he said. "You found a way to like vegetables."

Tommy felt proud. His tummy felt full and happy. "Can I have my cookie now?" he asked.

Mom laughed. "Yes, you can have your cookie. You earned it!"

Tommy bit into his warm chocolate cookie. It was sweet and gooey. But he felt even better about eating his vegetables.

The next day, Tommy sat at the table again. His plate had carrots this time. They were orange and long. Tommy remembered the ranch dressing.

"Mom, can I have ranch for my carrots?" Tommy asked.

"Of course!" Mom said. She brought the ranch bowl.

Tommy dipped his carrot. He took a bite. "Yummy!" he said. The carrot was sweet and crunchy with the creamy ranch.

Tommy learned something important. Sometimes when things seem hard, you just need to try a different way. The vegetables weren't really bad. They just needed the right helper.

Now Tommy looked forward to dinner. He wondered what vegetable would come next. Maybe green beans? Maybe corn? Whatever it was, Tommy knew he could make it taste good.

Tommy smiled at Mom and Dad. "I like vegetables now," he said. "Especially with ranch!"

Mom and Dad smiled back. They were proud of Tommy for not giving up. Tommy felt proud too. He was a big boy who ate all his dinner.

The end.
