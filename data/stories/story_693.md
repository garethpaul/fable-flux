# Story story_693

Lily sat on the soft green hill. The warm sun touched her face. She picked yellow flowers and made a tiny pile. But something felt missing.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," Lily said quietly.

The hill was so pretty. Butterflies danced in the air. Birds sang sweet songs. But Lily felt alone. She sighed and looked at her small flower pile.

Suddenly, she heard a sound. Woof! Woof!

A fluffy brown dog ran up the hill. His tail wagged fast. His pink tongue hung out. He looked very friendly.

"Hello, doggy," Lily said softly.

The dog tilted his head. His brown eyes sparkled. He sniffed the flowers Lily had picked. Then he wagged his tail even more.

"Do you want to play?" Lily asked.

The dog barked once. That meant yes! He picked up a stick with his mouth. He dropped it near Lily's feet.

Lily smiled big. "You want to play fetch!"

She threw the stick down the hill. The dog ran fast. His ears bounced as he ran. He brought the stick back to Lily.

"Good boy!" Lily laughed.

They played fetch three more times. The dog was so happy. Lily felt happy too. Her lonely feeling started to go away.

"What else should we do?" Lily wondered.

The dog saw her flower pile. He gently picked up one yellow flower. He brought it to Lily and dropped it in her lap.

"You like my flowers!" Lily giggled.

Together, they picked more flowers. Lily picked the yellow ones. The dog found white ones with his nose. Soon they had a big, colorful pile.

"We make a good team," Lily said.

The dog licked her hand. His tongue felt warm and wet. Lily petted his soft fur. It felt like a fluffy blanket.

Then the dog started digging in the dirt. His paws moved fast. Dirt flew everywhere. Lily watched and laughed.

"Are you making a hole?" she asked.

The dog barked happily. He kept digging. Soon he made a perfect little hole. He picked up their flower pile. He put all the flowers in the hole.

"Oh! You made a flower garden!" Lily clapped her hands.

The dog wagged his tail proudly. The flowers looked beautiful in their new home. Red and yellow and white flowers all together.

Lily and the dog sat next to their garden. The afternoon sun felt warm on their backs. A gentle breeze made the flowers dance.

"I'm not lonely anymore," Lily whispered.

The dog put his head on Lily's lap. She scratched behind his ears. He made a happy sound, almost like purring.

"Will you be my friend forever?" Lily asked.

The dog looked up at her. His tail thumped on the ground. Thump, thump, thump. That definitely meant yes.

They rolled down the hill together. Lily laughed as she tumbled. The dog barked with joy. The grass felt cool and tickly. They reached the bottom covered in green grass bits.

"That was so fun!" Lily said, brushing grass from her hair.

The dog shook his whole body. Grass flew everywhere. Lily giggled and hugged him tight.

As the sun began to set, they climbed back up the hill. Their flower garden waited for them at the top. The flowers seemed to smile in the golden light.

"This is our special place," Lily said.

The dog curled up next to her. Together they watched the pretty sunset. The sky turned pink and orange. Stars started to twinkle.

"Thank you for being my friend," Lily whispered.

The dog's tail wagged softly. He licked her cheek one more time.

Lily felt so happy and warm inside. She had found the best friend ever. The lonely feeling was completely gone. Tomorrow they would play on their hill again.

The hill wasn't just Lily's anymore. Now it belonged to both of them. Their friendship had made everything more wonderful.

As they walked home together, Lily smiled. She couldn't wait for tomorrow's adventures with her new best friend.

The end.
