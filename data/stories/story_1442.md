# Story story_1442

**Garden Magic**

"Time for garden time!" called Mama from the kitchen.

Lily groaned and flopped on the couch. Her big sister Emma was already putting on her sun hat. Lily did not want to go outside. The dirt was yucky. The sun was too hot. Garden time was boring.

"Come on, Lily," said Emma gently. "It will be fun."

Lily shook her head. "Gardens are muddy and gross."

Emma sat down next to her little sister. "What if I show you something special today?"

Lily peeked up with one eye. "What kind of special?"

"You have to come see," Emma smiled.

Slowly, Lily put on her tiny garden gloves. They walked outside together. The morning air smelled fresh and sweet. Birds chirped happy songs in the apple tree.

Emma led Lily to the vegetable patch. "Look here," she whispered.

Lily saw nothing but green leaves. "I don't see anything special."

"Look closer," Emma said softly.

Lily bent down. She pushed aside a big cucumber leaf. There, hiding underneath, was the tiniest baby cucumber she had ever seen! It was no bigger than her thumb.

"Wow!" gasped Lily. "It's so little!"

"We planted that seed just three weeks ago," Emma explained. "Now it's growing into food we can eat."

Lily touched the baby cucumber very gently. It felt smooth and cool. "Can I find more?"

Emma nodded. "Let's go exploring."

They walked to the tomato plants. Emma showed Lily how to look for tiny green tomatoes. They found seven little ones hiding among the leaves. Some were round like marbles. Others were still just tiny bumps.

"This one looks like a button!" Lily giggled.

Next, they checked the sunflower seeds they had planted. Green shoots poked up from the dark soil like tiny umbrellas.

"They're saying hello!" said Lily, waving at the baby plants.

Emma laughed. "Maybe they are!"

They watered the thirsty plants together. Lily held the watering can while Emma helped guide it. The water made a gentle splashing sound. The wet earth smelled rich and earthy.

A bright orange butterfly landed on the marigold flowers. It fluttered its wings slowly. Lily watched with wide eyes.

"It's beautiful," she whispered.

"Butterflies help our garden grow," Emma explained. "They move pollen from flower to flower."

The butterfly danced from bloom to bloom. Lily followed it quietly, trying not to scare it away.

Then Emma showed Lily the herb garden. She picked a small mint leaf and held it out. "Smell this."

Lily sniffed. Her nose tingled with the fresh, cool scent. "It smells like toothpaste!"

They smelled the basil next. It was warm and spicy. The lavender smelled like Grandma's soap.

"Gardens smell like magic," said Lily.

Emma smiled. "They really do."

They picked some lettuce leaves for lunch. Lily carefully chose the prettiest ones. She put them gently in their basket.

A fat bumblebee buzzed around the bean flowers. It was fuzzy and round. Lily watched it work, going from flower to flower.

"The bee is helping too," she said.

"That's right," said Emma. "Gardens need lots of helpers."

They found three ripe strawberries hiding under the leaves. Emma washed them with the hose. They ate them right there in the garden. The berries were warm from the sun and incredibly sweet.

"These taste like summer," said Lily, berry juice on her chin.

When Mama called them for lunch, Lily felt sad to leave.

"Can we come back after lunch?" she asked Emma.

"Of course," Emma said. "We need to check on our baby cucumbers."

Lily skipped toward the house. Her hands were dirty but she didn't mind. Dirt meant growing things. Dirt meant garden magic.

"Emma," she said, tugging on her sister's shirt. "Can we plant more seeds tomorrow?"

Emma hugged her little sister tight. "I would love that."

That night, Lily dreamed of dancing butterflies and tiny green sprouts reaching toward the sun. She couldn't wait for tomorrow's garden time.

The end.
