# Ruby the Library Doll

In the sunny children's corner of Willowbrook Library, Ruby the cloth doll sat quietly on a soft blue cushion. She had bright button eyes and yarn hair the color of autumn leaves. Ruby loved living at the library because children visited her every day.

"Good morning, Ruby!" said Mrs. Chen, the librarian. "The children will be here soon for story time."

Ruby smiled her sewn-on smile. She couldn't wait to play!

Soon, giggly voices filled the library. Children scattered colorful blocks everywhere. They pulled out puzzles and picture books. Toy cars zoomed across the carpet. The reading corner became a wonderful, messy playground.

"Zoom! Beep beep!" called Tommy, racing a red fire truck.

"Look at my tall tower!" shouted Emma, stacking blocks high.

Ruby watched happily as toys flew in all directions. But then Mrs. Chen rang her little silver bell.

"Clean-up time, everyone!" she announced cheerfully.

The children groaned. "Aww, do we have to?"

"Yes, we must put our toys away," said Mrs. Chen. "Ruby needs help too!"

Ruby's button eyes grew wide. Clean up? She looked around at the scattered toys. Books lay open on tables. Crayons rolled under chairs. Puzzle pieces hid behind cushions.

"I don't want to clean up," Ruby thought. "It looks too hard!"

She stayed very still on her cushion. Maybe if she didn't move, nobody would notice her.

But the children started picking up toys. "Come on, Ruby!" called Emma. "Help us!"

Ruby felt her stuffing sink a little. She really didn't want to clean. The mess seemed enormous!

First, Ruby tried closing her button eyes tight. "If I can't see the mess, maybe it will go away," she hoped.

She squeezed her eyes shut for a whole minute. When she opened them, toys still covered the floor.

"That didn't work," Ruby sighed.

Next, Ruby tried rolling behind a big picture book. "Maybe I can hide until someone else cleans up," she thought.

She rolled and rolled until she bumped into the bookshelf. But she could still hear children working.

"Ruby, where are you?" called Tommy.

"Hiding doesn't help either," Ruby realized.

Then Ruby tried something new. She looked at just one small corner of the mess. There sat three wooden blocks, all by themselves.

"Maybe I could move just those three blocks," she thought.

Ruby rolled slowly toward the blocks. She pushed the first red block with her soft arm. It slid across the carpet toward the toy bin.

"I did it!" Ruby felt proud.

She pushed the blue block next. Then the yellow one. All three blocks reached the bin!

"Look!" shouted Emma. "Ruby is helping!"

Ruby felt warm and happy inside. The corner looked much neater now.

"If I can clean one corner, maybe I can clean another," Ruby thought.

She rolled to where crayons had scattered. One by one, she nudged them toward their box. Red crayon. Blue crayon. Green crayon.

"Great job, Ruby!" cheered Tommy.

Other children joined her. Together, they gathered puzzle pieces. They stacked books neatly. They lined up toy cars in perfect rows.

Ruby discovered something wonderful. When she focused on one small job at a time, cleaning felt much easier. And working with friends made it fun!

Soon the library corner sparkled again. Every toy had found its home.

"Thank you for helping, Ruby," said Mrs. Chen, giving the doll a gentle pat. "You showed us that big jobs become smaller when we work together."

Ruby beamed with pride. She had learned something important. Even when jobs seemed too big, she could start with one small step. And asking friends for help made everything better.

As the children waved goodbye, Ruby settled back on her blue cushion. She felt proud and happy. Tomorrow, when clean-up time came, she would remember: start small, work together, and never give up.

The library grew quiet and peaceful. Ruby closed her button eyes, dreaming of tomorrow's adventures and the friends who would help her face any challenge.

Sometimes the best solutions come from taking tiny steps forward.

The end.
