# Story story_1097

**Finley and the Magic Story Time**

Every day at three o'clock, the big bell rang. "Story time!" called Miss Coral from the reading circle. All the little sea creatures swam over quickly. They loved story time.

But Finley the fish always hid behind the tall seaweed. He watched his friends swim to the soft sand circle. They sat with big smiles on their faces. Finley wanted to join them. But he felt too scared.

"Stories are for smart fish," Finley whispered to himself. "I'm just a little fish. I won't understand the big words."

Miss Coral opened her sparkly blue book. "Today we'll read about a brave little fish," she said. Her voice was warm like sunshine through the water.

Finley peeked out from the seaweed. A brave little fish? That sounded interesting. He swam a tiny bit closer. The water felt cool against his orange fins.

"Once upon a time," Miss Coral began, "there lived a small fish named Splash." 

Finley's eyes grew wide. Splash looked just like him! Same orange fins. Same worried expression. Finley swam even closer to hear better.

In the story, Splash was afraid to explore the coral reef. He thought he was too small. He thought he wasn't brave enough. But then something magical happened.

As Miss Coral read, the words seemed to glow. Tiny bubbles danced around the book. The pictures moved like real fish swimming! Finley gasped with wonder.

"Look!" whispered Shelly the seahorse. "The story is coming alive!"

Splash swam right out of the book! He floated in the water next to the reading circle. His scales shimmered like real fish scales. His fins moved like real fins.

"Hello, everyone," said Splash. His voice was gentle and kind. "I was scared of stories too. But then I learned something special."

Finley forgot to hide. He swam right up to the circle. "What did you learn?" he asked.

Splash smiled at Finley. "Stories aren't about being smart. They're about feeling brave. They're about having fun. They're about making friends."

Miss Coral kept reading. In the story, Splash found a treasure chest. It was full of golden shells and pretty pearls. But the real treasure was something else.

"The real treasure," said Splash, "was believing in myself."

Finley felt warm bubbles in his tummy. Good bubbles. Happy bubbles. He understood every word of the story. The words weren't too big at all.

When story time ended, Splash winked at Finley. Then he swam back into the book. The magical bubbles slowly faded away.

"Did you like the story, Finley?" asked Miss Coral.

Finley nodded his head up and down. "I loved it! Splash was brave. Just like I want to be."

"You were brave today," said Miss Coral. "You joined our story circle."

Finley looked around at his friends. They were all smiling at him. Tommy the turtle patted his shell. Bella the blowfish gave him a gentle hug.

"Will you sit with us tomorrow?" asked Shelly.

"Yes!" said Finley. "I want to hear more stories. I want to meet more book friends."

That night, Finley dreamed about magical books. He dreamed about brave fish and exciting adventures. He dreamed about story time.

The next day, Finley was the first one at the reading circle. He saved spots for all his friends. When the bell rang at three o'clock, he was ready.

"Today's story is about a little octopus," said Miss Coral.

Finley wiggled his fins with excitement. He couldn't wait to see what magic would happen. He couldn't wait to meet the octopus. Most of all, he couldn't wait to feel brave again.

Story time had become his favorite time of day. And tomorrow, there would be another story. Another adventure. Another chance to believe in magic.

Finley smiled his biggest smile. Being part of story time was the best treasure of all.

The end.
