# Penny the Pony's Big Problem

Penny was a little brown pony who loved to play. She had soft, fuzzy ears and a white spot on her nose. Every morning, Penny galloped to her favorite sandbox in the meadow.

The sandbox was special. It had golden sand that felt warm between her hooves. Penny loved to dig and build little hills. She made paths for beetles to walk on. The sand made a nice *swoosh* sound when she moved it around.

But Penny had a big problem. Right next to her sandbox stood a tall wooden fence. All the other animals could see over it, but Penny was too short. She could hear wonderful sounds from the other side - children laughing, dogs barking, and birds singing. 

"I wish I could see what's over there," Penny sighed. She stretched her neck as high as it would go. She stood on her tippy-hooves. But the fence was still too tall.

Her friend Bella the bunny hopped by. "What's wrong, Penny?" Bella asked.

"I can't see over the fence," Penny said sadly. "I'm too short."

"Maybe you could jump!" suggested Bella.

Penny tried jumping. *Boing! Boing! Boing!* She jumped as high as she could. But she still couldn't see over the fence.

"Hmm," thought Penny. "Maybe I need to be taller."

Penny had an idea. She began pushing sand into a big pile. *Scoop, scoop, scoop.* The sand felt cool on her hooves. She pushed and pushed until she made a small hill.

Penny climbed on top of her sand hill. She was a little bit taller now! But the fence was still too high.

"I need more sand," Penny said to herself.

She worked very hard. *Scoop, push, pat.* She made her hill bigger and bigger. Sweat dripped from her forehead, but she didn't give up.

Penny climbed up again. She was taller than before! But she still couldn't quite see over the fence.

"Don't give up, Penny!" called Bella from below.

Penny thought and thought. Then she had another idea. She found some smooth rocks near the sandbox. She rolled them over with her nose. *Clunk, clunk, clunk.* The rocks were heavy, but Penny was determined.

She placed the rocks on top of her sand hill. Now she had made a rock-and-sand mountain!

Carefully, Penny climbed up her mountain. Step by step, she went higher. The rocks felt bumpy under her hooves, but she was very careful.

Finally, Penny reached the top. She stretched her neck up, up, up. And then...

"I can see! I can see!" Penny whinnied with joy.

Over the fence was the most beautiful sight. There was a playground where children were swinging and sliding. Colorful flowers danced in the breeze. A family of ducks swam in a sparkling pond.

"It's wonderful!" Penny called down to Bella.

From her high perch, Penny could see everything. She watched the children play tag. She saw butterflies landing on yellow flowers. She even spotted a red balloon floating in the sky.

"You did it, Penny!" cheered Bella. "You never gave up!"

Penny felt so proud. Her heart was full of happiness. She had solved her problem all by herself. Even though she was still a little pony, she had found a way to see the big wide world.

Every day after that, Penny climbed her special mountain. She would sit on top and watch all the wonderful things happening beyond the fence. Sometimes she would tell Bella about everything she saw.

"Being small doesn't mean you can't do big things," Penny told her friend. "You just have to keep trying and use your brain."

Penny learned that when you have a problem, you shouldn't give up. You can try different ideas until you find one that works. And the best part was, she had built something amazing with her own hooves.

As the sun set each evening, Penny would slide down her mountain with a big smile. Tomorrow would bring another day of wonderful sights to see from her very own lookout post.

And Penny lived happily ever after, proving that little ponies can solve big problems with patience, hard work, and clever thinking.

The end.
