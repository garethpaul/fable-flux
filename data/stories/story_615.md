# Story story_615

**Dolly and Papa in the Sunny Field**

Dolly sat alone in the big green field. The soft grass tickled her tiny hands. Yellow flowers danced in the warm breeze. But Dolly felt sad and lonely.

She picked a daisy and sighed. "I wish I had someone to play with," she whispered. The sun felt warm on her face. Birds sang happy songs above her head. But Dolly still felt alone.

Papa Bear lived near the field. He was big and fuzzy and kind. Papa Bear saw little Dolly sitting by herself. His heart felt heavy seeing her so sad.

"Hello there, little one," Papa Bear called softly. His voice was deep and gentle like rumbling thunder.

Dolly looked up with big, round eyes. "Oh! Hello, Papa Bear," she said quietly.

Papa Bear walked closer on his big, soft paws. "May I sit with you?" he asked politely.

Dolly nodded and smiled just a little. Papa Bear sat down carefully in the grass. The ground shook gently under his weight.

"What are you doing here alone?" Papa Bear asked kindly.

"I'm looking for someone to play with," Dolly said. "But everyone is busy today."

Papa Bear's brown eyes twinkled. "I love to play! May I be your friend?"

Dolly's face lit up like sunshine. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" Papa Bear said with a warm laugh. "What should we play first?"

Dolly thought for a moment. "Can we pick flowers together?"

"What a wonderful idea!" Papa Bear said happily.

They walked through the field hand in paw. Dolly picked small white daisies. Papa Bear picked big yellow sunflowers. The flowers smelled sweet like honey.

"Look at my pretty bouquet!" Dolly giggled. She held up her small bunch of daisies.

"Beautiful!" Papa Bear said. "And look at mine!" He showed his giant sunflowers.

They made flower crowns for each other. Dolly's crown was too small for Papa Bear's big head. They both laughed until their bellies hurt.

"Let's play hide and seek!" Dolly suggested excitedly.

Papa Bear covered his eyes and counted slowly. "One... two... three..."

Dolly ran and hid behind a small rock. But her bright dress stuck out like a rainbow.

"Ready or not, here I come!" Papa Bear called.

He pretended to look everywhere else first. "Hmm, where could Dolly be?" he wondered out loud.

Dolly giggled from behind the rock. The sound made Papa Bear smile.

"I found you!" Papa Bear said, pointing to the rock.

Dolly jumped out laughing. "Your turn to hide!"

Papa Bear looked around the field. There was nowhere big enough for him to hide! He crouched behind a tiny flower.

Dolly opened her eyes and saw Papa Bear right away. His big brown bottom stuck up high in the air.

"I see you, Papa Bear!" she laughed.

"Oh no! You found me too fast!" Papa Bear chuckled.

They played until the sun started to set. The sky turned pink and orange like cotton candy. Cool evening air made them feel sleepy.

"This was the best day ever," Dolly said with a big yawn.

Papa Bear picked her up gently. "Should I carry you home, little friend?"

Dolly nodded and snuggled into Papa Bear's soft, warm fur. She felt safe and happy.

As they walked home, Dolly said, "Papa Bear, will you be my friend forever?"

"Forever and always," Papa Bear promised. "And tomorrow we can play in the field again."

Dolly smiled and closed her eyes. She wasn't lonely anymore. She had found the best friend in the whole wide world.

The stars began to twinkle above them. Papa Bear hummed a gentle song as they walked. The field would be waiting for them tomorrow, ready for new adventures.

From that day on, Dolly and Papa Bear played together every single day. They were the very best of friends, and they were never lonely again.

The end.
