# Ben's Wonderful Walk Time

Every day at three o'clock, Mom called out, "Ben! It's walk time!" 

Today, Ben sat on his soft blue rug. He was building a tall tower with blocks. Red blocks, yellow blocks, green blocks too. His tower was almost done!

"Do I have to go?" Ben asked. "My tower isn't finished yet."

Mom smiled her warm smile. "We can finish it later, sweetie. Fresh air will feel good."

Ben sighed a big sigh. He put on his sneakers slowly. First the left one. Then the right one. He tied the laces in wobbly loops.

Outside, the sun felt warm on Ben's face. But he still missed his blocks. His feet moved slowly down the sidewalk.

"Look, Ben," Mom said softly. "What do you see?"

Ben looked around with curious eyes. The big oak tree had new green leaves. They danced in the gentle breeze. A squirrel ran up the bumpy bark. Its fluffy tail twitched back and forth.

"The squirrel is playing!" Ben said.

They walked a little further. Ben's steps got a bit faster. A yellow butterfly fluttered near the purple flowers. It landed on a bright pink rose.

"Hello, butterfly," Ben whispered. The butterfly's wings sparkled in the sunshine.

Around the corner, they found something special. Mrs. Garcia was walking her new puppy. The puppy had soft brown fur and floppy ears.

"Can I pet him?" Ben asked politely.

"Of course!" Mrs. Garcia said. "His name is Cocoa."

Ben gently touched Cocoa's silky fur. The puppy licked Ben's hand with his tiny pink tongue. It felt tickly and warm.

"He likes you!" Mrs. Garcia laughed.

Ben giggled as Cocoa wagged his whole body. His tail went swish, swish, swish!

They said goodbye to Cocoa and Mrs. Garcia. Ben skipped ahead a few steps. The sidewalk had interesting cracks that looked like puzzle pieces.

"I can hop over this one!" Ben said. He jumped over a big crack. Then another one. And another!

Mom joined the hopping game. "This is fun!" she said.

At the park, Ben discovered something amazing. The old slide had been painted bright blue. The swings had new yellow seats. Everything looked fresh and cheerful.

"Can we swing for a minute?" Ben asked.

"Absolutely," Mom said.

Ben climbed onto a swing. Mom gave him gentle pushes. Higher and higher he went. The wind felt cool on his cheeks. He could see the whole neighborhood from up there!

"I can see our house!" Ben called out. "And the ice cream truck!"

The ice cream truck played its happy song. Ding-a-ling, ding-a-ling! Other children ran toward the music.

"Maybe we can get ice cream tomorrow," Mom said. "After our walk."

Ben's eyes got big and sparkly. "Really? That sounds perfect!"

On the way home, Ben noticed more wonderful things. A red cardinal singing in the maple tree. Pink flowers blooming in window boxes. A friendly dog barking hello from behind a white fence.

"Look at all these pretty things!" Ben said. "I didn't see them before."

"They were always here," Mom explained. "Sometimes we just need to look carefully."

Ben nodded wisely. "Walking helps us see better."

Back home, Ben ran to his blocks. But first, he gave Mom a big hug.

"Thank you for walk time," he said. "It was really fun today."

"I'm so glad," Mom said. "You made it special by paying attention."

Ben built his tower even taller than before. Each block reminded him of something from their walk. The red block was like the cardinal. The yellow block was like the butterfly. The green block was like the dancing leaves.

That night at dinner, Ben told Dad all about their adventure. The friendly puppy, the colorful playground, the singing bird, and the hopping game.

"Tomorrow can we walk a different way?" Ben asked excitedly. "Maybe we'll find new things!"

"That's a wonderful idea," Mom said.

Ben went to bed with happy thoughts. He dreamed about butterflies and puppies and ice cream trucks. In his dreams, every day was full of magical surprises.

The next afternoon, when Mom called "Ben! It's walk time!" Ben jumped up immediately.

"Coming!" he shouted joyfully. "I can't wait to see what we'll discover today!"

The end.
