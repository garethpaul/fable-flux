# Story story_1015

**Nutkin's Red Ball**

Nutkin the squirrel loved her bright red ball. It was round and bouncy and perfect. She played with it every morning in Maple Park.

The red ball was shiny like a cherry. It smelled like new rubber. When Nutkin pushed it, it rolled fast across the soft grass.

One sunny morning, Nutkin woke up early. She stretched her fluffy tail. She wiggled her little pink nose. Time to play with her favorite ball!

But where was it?

Nutkin looked under the big oak tree. No red ball there. She looked behind the park bench. No red ball there either.

"Oh no!" said Nutkin. Her whiskers drooped sadly. "Where is my special ball?"

She searched near the pond. She searched by the flower garden. She even looked in the trash can. Still no red ball anywhere.

Nutkin's tummy felt worried and upset. Her tail wasn't fluffy anymore. It dragged on the ground like a sad gray rope.

Then she remembered yesterday. She had been playing catch with Benny the rabbit. They threw the ball back and forth. It was so much fun!

"Maybe Benny knows where it went," thought Nutkin.

She scampered across the park quickly. Her little paws made soft patting sounds on the path. She found Benny eating clover near the playground.

"Benny! Benny!" called Nutkin. "Have you seen my red ball?"

Benny looked up. Green clover stuck to his white whiskers. "Hi Nutkin! I remember your ball. We threw it really high yesterday."

"Yes! Where did it go?" asked Nutkin hopefully.

"I think it went over there." Benny pointed with his paw toward the tall maple trees.

Nutkin hurried to the maple trees. These trees were very, very tall. Their branches reached up to the clouds. Their leaves whispered in the gentle breeze.

She looked up and up and up. Way high in the branches, something red caught her eye.

"There it is!" squeaked Nutkin excitedly.

Her beautiful red ball was stuck between two thick branches. It was so high up that it looked tiny like a red dot.

Nutkin tried to climb the tree. She grabbed the rough bark with her sharp claws. Up, up, up she went. But the branches were too far apart. She couldn't reach the ball.

She tried jumping from a nearby tree. She leaped with all her might. But the ball was still too far away. Nutkin landed safely on a soft pile of leaves.

"This is too hard," sighed Nutkin. She sat down and felt very sad.

Then she heard a friendly voice. "Need some help, little squirrel?"

Nutkin looked up. Oliver the owl was perched on a low branch. His big round eyes were kind and wise.

"My ball is stuck way up there," explained Nutkin. "I can't reach it."

"I can fly up there," said Oliver gently. "Would you like me to help?"

"Oh yes, please!" said Nutkin. Her tail got fluffy again with hope.

Oliver spread his strong brown wings. He flew up, up, up to the very top of the tree. His wings made soft whooshing sounds in the air.

Carefully, Oliver used his sharp beak to push the ball. It wiggled loose from the branches. Down, down, down it bounced through the leaves.

Nutkin caught her precious red ball in her tiny paws. It felt smooth and warm from the sunshine. It still smelled like rubber and adventure.

"Thank you, Oliver!" chirped Nutkin happily. "You're the best friend ever!"

"You're welcome," hooted Oliver. "Sometimes we all need help. That's what friends are for."

Nutkin hugged her red ball tight. She learned something important that day. When something seems too hard, it's okay to ask for help.

She bounced her ball on the soft grass. It felt wonderful to play again. The morning sunshine made everything bright and cheerful.

From that day on, Nutkin always remembered Oliver's kindness. And she always offered to help her friends too.

The red ball bounced happily in the warm sunshine. Nutkin's tail was fluffy and proud. She had her favorite toy back, and she had learned about friendship.

What a perfect day in Maple Park!

The end.
