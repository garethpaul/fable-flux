# Story story_722

**Foxy's Magical Art Time**

The big clock on the wall went tick-tock. Miss Rabbit clapped her paws together. "Art time, everyone!" she called out cheerfully.

All the little animals in the classroom got excited. Bunny hopped to the art table. Squirrel scampered over with a big smile. Bear waddled quickly to get his favorite spot.

But Foxy stayed in his chair. His fluffy red tail curled around his feet. Art time made his tummy feel funny. What if he made a mess? What if his picture looked silly?

"Come on, Foxy," said Miss Rabbit gently. "We're painting today."

Foxy's ears drooped down. He walked very slowly to the art table. The other animals were already dipping their brushes in bright paint. Red paint. Blue paint. Yellow paint like sunshine.

Foxy picked up a brush. It felt heavy in his small paw. He looked at the white paper in front of him. It was so clean and perfect. He didn't want to ruin it.

"I don't know what to paint," Foxy whispered.

Miss Rabbit sat down next to him. "What makes you happy, Foxy?"

Foxy thought for a moment. "Playing in the forest," he said quietly.

"Then paint the forest," Miss Rabbit said with a warm smile.

Foxy dipped his brush in green paint. He made one tiny dot on the paper. The paint felt cool and smooth. It smelled fresh like spring leaves.

Something amazing happened next. The green dot seemed to shimmer and dance. Foxy blinked his amber eyes. The dot looked like a real leaf!

He painted another dot. Then another. Soon he had painted a whole tree. The tree looked so real he could almost hear wind rustling through its branches.

"Wow!" said Bunny, looking over. "Your tree is beautiful!"

Foxy felt a warm feeling in his chest. He dipped his brush in brown paint. He painted a tree trunk. The brown paint smelled like rich earth after rain.

Then Foxy tried blue paint. He painted a stream flowing past his tree. The blue paint sparkled like real water. He could almost hear it babbling and gurgling.

"This is fun!" Foxy said, his tail beginning to wag.

He painted yellow flowers that seemed to glow like tiny suns. He painted purple butterflies that looked ready to fly away. He painted red berries that looked good enough to eat.

Squirrel gasped. "Your painting is magical, Foxy!"

Bear nodded his big head. "It looks so real!"

Foxy stepped back to look at his painting. His forest scene filled the whole paper. Birds seemed to chirp from painted branches. Flowers seemed to sway in a painted breeze.

"I did this?" Foxy asked in wonder.

"You certainly did," said Miss Rabbit proudly. "You painted with your heart."

Foxy's chest puffed out with pride. His painting wasn't perfect, but it was his. It showed his favorite place in the whole world. Every brushstroke told a story about what he loved.

When art time ended, Foxy carefully carried his painting to the drying rack. He couldn't stop looking at it. The colors seemed to dance in the afternoon sunlight streaming through the window.

"Can we have art time again tomorrow?" Foxy asked hopefully.

Miss Rabbit laughed her tinkly laugh. "We have art time every day, Foxy."

Foxy's eyes grew wide with excitement. "Really? Every day?"

That night, Foxy told his mama all about his magical painting. He described every color, every brushstroke, every wonderful moment.

"I can't wait for art time tomorrow," Foxy said as Mama tucked him into bed. "I want to paint the meadow where we pick berries."

Mama kissed his furry forehead. "Sweet dreams, little artist."

Foxy closed his eyes and dreamed of paintbrushes and colors. In his dreams, his paintings came alive with chirping birds and dancing flowers.

Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough. Art time was the best time of all.

The end.
