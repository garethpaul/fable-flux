# Story story_781

**The Lost Red Ball**

Tommy loved his bright red ball. It was round and shiny. It bounced high in the air. Today, he brought it to the beach with his family.

The sand felt warm under Tommy's feet. Seagulls called out above him. The ocean waves made a whooshing sound. Tommy threw his red ball up and caught it. Up and down, up and down.

"Be careful near the water," called Mom from her beach chair.

Tommy nodded. He threw the ball again. This time, a big gust of wind caught it. The red ball flew toward the ocean!

"My ball!" Tommy cried.

He ran after it. The ball bobbed in the shallow water. Then a wave pulled it further out. Tommy's eyes filled with tears.

"My favorite ball is gone," he said sadly.

Dad came over and hugged Tommy. "Let's try to get it back," Dad said gently.

Tommy wiped his eyes. He had an idea. "I'll wade in and grab it!"

Tommy rolled up his shorts. He stepped into the cool water. The waves tickled his ankles. He reached for the red ball. But another wave pushed it away from him.

"It's too far now," Tommy said. His voice was shaky.

The ball floated further from shore. Tommy felt his heart sink. He walked back to the sand with droopy shoulders.

"I'll never see my ball again," Tommy whispered.

Mom sat down next to him. "Sometimes we need to think of new ways to solve problems," she said softly.

Tommy looked around the beach. He saw kids building sandcastles. He saw people walking with metal detectors. Then he spotted something interesting.

An older boy was playing with a long fishing net. The net had a big scoop at the end. Tommy's eyes grew wide.

"Maybe that boy can help!" Tommy said excitedly.

Tommy ran over to the boy. "Hi! I'm Tommy. My red ball is floating in the water. Can your net reach it?"

The boy smiled. "I'm Jake. Let me see where your ball is."

Tommy pointed to the red dot bobbing in the waves. Jake squinted his eyes and nodded.

"I think I can help," Jake said kindly.

Jake waded into the water with his long net. The waves splashed around his legs. He stretched the net toward the red ball. The first try missed.

"Almost got it," Jake called out.

Tommy held his breath. His hands were pressed together. Jake tried again. This time, the net scooped under the ball perfectly!

"Got it!" Jake shouted happily.

Tommy jumped up and down on the sand. "You did it! You got my ball!"

Jake walked back to shore. Water dripped from the net. He handed Tommy the wet red ball.

"Thank you so much!" Tommy said. His smile was huge.

"You're welcome," Jake replied. "I'm glad I could help."

Tommy hugged his ball tight. It felt cold and sandy, but he didn't care. His favorite toy was safe.

Dad patted Tommy's back. "You didn't give up," Dad said proudly. "You found another way to solve the problem."

Tommy nodded. "When one idea doesn't work, I can try a different one!"

Mom smiled warmly. "And you were brave enough to ask for help."

"Jake was really nice," Tommy said. He waved at Jake, who waved back.

For the rest of the day, Tommy played carefully with his red ball. He stayed far from the water's edge. When the wind blew, he held the ball tighter.

As the sun began to set, the family packed up their beach things. Tommy's red ball was safely tucked in the beach bag.

"Today I learned something important," Tommy told his parents.

"What's that?" asked Mom.

"If one way doesn't work, I can find another way. And asking for help is okay too."

Dad ruffled Tommy's sandy hair. "That's a very wise lesson."

Tommy felt proud and happy. His favorite red ball was safe, and he had made a new friend. Sometimes the best solutions come from working together.

The end.
