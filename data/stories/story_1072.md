# Story story_1072

**Ellie and the Yucky Vegetables**

Ellie the elephant lived in a beautiful garden. The garden had bright red roses. It had tall green trees. It had a sparkly blue pond.

But Ellie had a big problem. She did not like vegetables. Not one bit.

Every morning, Mama Elephant would say, "Ellie, eat your carrots." Ellie would wrinkle her long nose. "Yuck! They taste like dirt!"

"Eat your green beans," Papa Elephant would say. Ellie would shake her big gray head. "No way! They're too crunchy!"

Even the sweet corn made Ellie frown. "It gets stuck in my teeth," she complained.

But elephants need vegetables to grow big and strong. Ellie's tummy rumbled all day. She felt tired and grumpy. Her friends wanted to play, but Ellie had no energy.

One sunny day, Ellie had an idea. "I know!" she trumpeted loudly. "I'll hide the vegetables!"

Ellie used her trunk to grab the carrots. She buried them under the rose bushes. She tossed the green beans into the pond. Splash! The corn went behind the big oak tree.

"All done!" Ellie said proudly.

But at dinner time, Mama brought more vegetables. "Where did the other vegetables go?" Mama asked.

Ellie's ears drooped. Her plan didn't work. She still had to face the yucky vegetables. And now she was even hungrier than before.

The next morning, Ellie met Benny the bunny. Benny was munching on a bright orange carrot. Crunch, crunch, crunch!

"Benny, how do you eat those awful things?" Ellie asked.

Benny's whiskers twitched. "Awful? Carrots are sweet and crunchy! They taste like candy to me."

"Candy?" Ellie's eyes grew wide. "Really?"

"Try this," Benny said. He showed Ellie how to nibble just the tip. "Start small. Take tiny bites."

Ellie touched the carrot with her trunk. It smelled earthy and fresh. She took the tiniest bite ever. It was... not terrible!

"Now try dipping it in the pond water," Benny suggested. "It makes it extra crisp and cold."

Ellie dipped the carrot. The cold water dripped down. She took another small bite. This time, she tasted something sweet! Her eyes sparkled with surprise.

"It's actually good!" Ellie trumpeted.

Benny hopped with joy. "Now try the green beans!"

Ellie picked up a green bean. She dipped it in the cool water. Crunch! It tasted fresh and snappy. Like eating a piece of spring morning.

"These are crunchy in a good way!" Ellie laughed.

Then she tried the corn. The yellow kernels were sweet like sunshine. They popped between her teeth in a fun way.

"I can't believe it!" Ellie danced around the garden. "Vegetables are actually yummy!"

From that day on, Ellie loved eating vegetables. She discovered that carrots made her eyes bright. Green beans made her trunk strong. Corn gave her energy to play all day.

She even found new vegetables to try. Purple eggplant tasted rich and creamy. Red tomatoes burst with juicy flavor. Yellow peppers were sweet and crunchy.

"Thank you, Benny," Ellie said. "You taught me to try new things."

"Sometimes we just need to try things differently," Benny replied wisely.

Mama and Papa Elephant were so proud. "Look how big and strong you're growing!" they said.

Ellie's friends noticed too. "You have so much energy now!" they cheered.

Every morning, Ellie woke up excited for breakfast. She would pick fresh vegetables from the garden. The sun warmed her back as she ate. The vegetables tasted like rainbow flavors.

Ellie learned that trying new things takes courage. Sometimes the first try doesn't work. But if you keep trying and ask for help, you might discover something wonderful.

Now Ellie was the happiest, strongest elephant in the whole garden. And vegetables were her very favorite food of all.

The end.
