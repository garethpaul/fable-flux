# Kiko the Kite and the Missing Blocks

Kiko was a bright red kite. He had yellow ribbons for a tail. Today, Kiko lived in the cozy bathroom. The tiles were cool and white. The bathtub sparkled like a lake.

Kiko loved to play with blocks. He had ten colorful blocks. Red ones, blue ones, green ones too. Every morning, he built tall towers. Every afternoon, he made long trains.

But this morning was different. Kiko looked around the bathroom. "Where are my blocks?" he wondered. The bathroom felt too quiet. Too empty.

Kiko's ribbons drooped sadly. His bright colors seemed duller. "I need my blocks to play," he said. His voice echoed off the tiles.

First, Kiko looked behind the toilet. He fluttered his paper body carefully. Nothing there but dust bunnies. They tickled his ribbons softly.

"Maybe under the sink?" Kiko thought. He swooped down low. The space was dark and cool. He saw a bottle of soap. He saw a spare towel. But no blocks anywhere.

Kiko felt worried now. His tail ribbons twisted together. "Where could they be?" he whispered.

Next, Kiko checked the bathtub. The white surface was smooth. It made a hollow sound. Echo, echo, echo. Still no blocks to find.

Kiko tried the medicine cabinet. The mirror showed his reflection. Two red kites stared back. But the shelves held only bottles. Tall ones, short ones, clear ones.

"This is hard work," Kiko puffed. His paper crinkled with effort. But he didn't give up. Kites are brave and strong.

Then Kiko had an idea. "Maybe they're in the laundry basket!" The wicker basket sat quietly. It smelled like clean clothes.

Kiko dove inside the basket. Soft towels surrounded him everywhere. They felt warm and fluffy. Like floating on clouds. But still no blocks.

Kiko sat on the bathroom floor. His ribbons spread around him. "I've looked everywhere," he sighed. A tear drop fell. It made a tiny splash.

Just then, something caught his eye. Behind the door! A corner he'd forgotten. Kiko had never looked there.

He flew over quickly. His heart beat faster. Could this be it?

Yes! There they were! All ten blocks sat quietly. They had been waiting patiently. Red blocks, blue blocks, green blocks too.

"My blocks!" Kiko shouted with joy. His ribbons danced in the air. The bathroom filled with happiness.

But how did they get there? Kiko remembered yesterday's bath time. The little girl had moved them. She'd put them somewhere safe. Behind the door, away from water.

Kiko gathered his blocks carefully. One by one, he moved them. Back to his favorite playing spot. Near the warm radiator.

"Now I can build again!" Kiko said. His colors seemed brighter now. His ribbons wiggled with excitement.

Kiko built a magnificent tower. Ten blocks high! It reached toward the ceiling. Red on bottom, blue in middle. Green blocks crowned the top.

"I'm so glad I kept looking," Kiko said. He admired his beautiful tower. Sunlight streamed through the window. It made rainbow patterns everywhere.

The bathroom felt cheerful again. Full of color and joy. Kiko's ribbons swayed gently back and forth.

"Sometimes things seem lost," Kiko learned. "But if you keep trying..." He looked at his wonderful tower. "You'll find what you need."

From that day forward, Kiko remembered. When something seems impossible, don't stop. Keep looking, keep trying, keep hoping. Good things come to those who persist.

The blocks sparkled in the sunlight. Kiko's ribbons danced with pride. The bathroom was filled with happiness. And Kiko felt proud and strong.

He had solved his problem. All by himself! What a wonderful feeling. Tomorrow would bring new adventures. But today, Kiko was perfectly content.
