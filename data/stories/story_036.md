# Grandma Rose and Ziggy's Kitchen Adventure

Grandma Rose stood in her sunny yellow kitchen. She mixed cookie dough all by herself. The wooden spoon went round and round. But something felt missing today.

"I wish I had someone to bake with," she sighed. The kitchen felt too quiet. Too empty for such a happy task.

*Knock, knock, knock!*

Grandma Rose wiped her floury hands. She opened the back door. A friendly zebra peeked inside!

"Hello there," said the zebra politely. "I'm Ziggy. I smelled something wonderful. May I come in?"

Grandma Rose smiled wide. "Of course, dear Ziggy! I'm making chocolate chip cookies. But I'm all alone."

Ziggy's ears perked up happily. "I love baking! May I help you?"

"Really?" asked Grandma Rose. "You want to bake with me?"

"Yes, please!" Ziggy stepped inside carefully. His hooves clicked on the tile floor.

Grandma Rose felt excitement bubble up inside. "Let's be baking partners!"

Ziggy washed his hooves in the sink. Grandma Rose tied an apron around him. The apron had dancing cupcakes on it.

"What should I do first?" asked Ziggy.

"Can you crack these eggs?" Grandma Rose pointed to a blue bowl.

Ziggy tried to crack an egg. *SPLAT!* Pieces of shell fell everywhere. They both started giggling.

"Let me show you," said Grandma Rose gently. She cracked an egg perfectly. "Tap, tap, pull apart."

Ziggy tried again. This time it worked! "I did it!" he cheered.

"Wonderful!" said Grandma Rose. "You're a natural baker!"

They worked together beautifully. Grandma Rose measured the flour. Ziggy poured it slowly. White flour puffed like tiny clouds.

"Now we mix," said Grandma Rose. She handed Ziggy the big wooden spoon.

Ziggy stirred and stirred. The dough got thick and sticky. "This is hard work!" he puffed.

"Let's take turns," suggested Grandma Rose. "Teamwork makes everything easier!"

They passed the spoon back and forth. Stir, stir, stir. The dough became smooth and perfect.

"Time for chocolate chips!" announced Grandma Rose.

Ziggy's eyes grew big. "My favorite part!"

They sprinkled chocolate chips together. Some fell on the counter. They giggled and ate them up.

"You know what?" said Grandma Rose. "This is much more fun together."

"I think so too," agreed Ziggy. "Everything feels more special with a friend."

They rolled the dough into little balls. Grandma Rose showed Ziggy how to space them out. The cookie sheet filled up quickly.

"Into the oven they go!" said Grandma Rose.

While the cookies baked, they cleaned up. Ziggy washed dishes with his careful hooves. Grandma Rose dried them with a soft towel.

"We make a great team," said Ziggy proudly.

The kitchen filled with amazing smells. Sweet chocolate and warm vanilla danced in the air.

*DING!* The timer rang cheerfully.

"They're ready!" both friends said together.

Grandma Rose pulled out golden brown cookies. They looked absolutely perfect. Steam rose up like little happy clouds.

"We did this together," said Grandma Rose. "They're our cookies."

Ziggy beamed with pride. "Our very first batch!"

They sat at the kitchen table. Each took a warm cookie. The chocolate chips were melty and delicious.

"Mmm," said Grandma Rose. "These taste like friendship."

"The best flavor ever," agreed Ziggy.

From that day on, every Saturday morning, Ziggy knocked on Grandma Rose's door. They baked together in the sunny yellow kitchen. Cookies, cakes, and sweet breads filled their days.

"Thank you for being my baking buddy," said Grandma Rose.

"Thank you for teaching me," said Ziggy. "Friends make everything better!"

They hugged with floury hands and happy hearts. The kitchen was never lonely again. It was filled with laughter, delicious smells, and the very best ingredient of all – friendship.

And every cookie they made together tasted sweeter because they made it with love.
