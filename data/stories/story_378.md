# Grandpa and Penny's Dinner Party

Grandpa sat alone in his big dining room. The long wooden table looked so empty. He had twelve chairs but no one to sit with him.

"I wish I had a friend," Grandpa sighed. He looked at his fancy plates and cups. They were pretty but lonely.

Grandpa decided to set the table anyway. He put out his best dishes. Red plates, blue cups, and shiny spoons. Maybe setting a nice table would cheer him up.

*Knock, knock, knock!*

Someone was at the door! Grandpa hurried to answer it.

"Hello!" said a cheerful voice. A small pink pig stood on his doorstep. She wore a purple bow and smiled big.

"I'm Penny the pig," she said. "I smelled something yummy. Can I come in?"

Grandpa's eyes lit up. "Of course! I'm Grandpa Joe. I was just setting my table."

Penny peeked into the dining room. "Wow! So many pretty dishes! But why so many?"

"I was hoping for company," Grandpa said softly. "But no one ever comes to visit."

Penny's heart felt warm. "I love dinner parties! Can we have one together?"

Grandpa clapped his hands. "What a wonderful idea! But I only made soup for one."

"I can help!" Penny squealed. "I make the best corn bread ever!"

They hurried to the kitchen together. Grandpa stirred his vegetable soup. It smelled like carrots and herbs. Penny mixed flour and corn meal. She hummed while she worked.

"You're a good helper," Grandpa said. "And you make cooking fun!"

"You're nice too!" Penny replied. "I love your soup recipe."

Soon the corn bread was baking. The kitchen filled with sweet, warm smells. Grandpa and Penny worked side by side.

"Let's make this extra special," Grandpa said. He found colorful napkins in his drawer. Penny helped fold them into pretty shapes.

"Ooh! Can we pick flowers too?" Penny asked.

They went to Grandpa's garden together. Penny found yellow daisies. Grandpa picked purple violets. They made a beautiful bouquet.

Back inside, they finished setting the table. Two places instead of twelve. But it looked perfect.

"This is the prettiest table ever!" Penny said. She did a little happy dance.

Grandpa smiled bigger than he had in months. "It's beautiful because we made it together."

They sat down to eat. The soup was creamy and delicious. The corn bread was warm and sweet. But best of all was the company.

"Tell me about yourself, Penny," Grandpa said.

"I love to cook and garden," Penny shared. "What about you?"

"Those are my favorite things too!" Grandpa exclaimed. "Plus reading stories and playing checkers."

"I love stories and games!" Penny squealed. "We like the same things!"

After dinner, they played checkers. Penny was good at jumping pieces. Grandpa was good at planning moves. They helped each other learn new tricks.

"You're getting better!" Grandpa cheered when Penny won a game.

"You taught me well!" Penny laughed.

As the sun set, they sat on the porch. Grandpa read Penny a story about friendship. Penny's eyes sparkled with joy.

"This was the best day ever," Penny said. "Thank you for sharing dinner with me."

"Thank you for making it special," Grandpa replied. "My dining room felt so empty before."

"Can we have dinner parties every week?" Penny asked hopefully.

"I would love that!" Grandpa said. "We make a great team."

They hugged goodbye. Penny promised to come back soon. Grandpa waved until she disappeared down the road.

That night, Grandpa looked at his dining room. The dishes were clean. The table was empty again. But it didn't feel lonely anymore.

He had a friend now. Someone who loved cooking and gardening too. Someone who made everything more fun.

Grandpa smiled and started planning next week's menu. He couldn't wait to cook with Penny again. Having a friend made everything better.

The dining room would never feel empty again.

The end.
