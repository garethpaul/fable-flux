# Rosie's Big Cleanup Day

In a cozy nursery with sunshine yellow walls, there lived a special doll named Rosie. She had curly brown hair tied with a bright red ribbon. Her blue dress sparkled with tiny silver buttons. Rosie loved playing with all the toys in the nursery.

Every morning, Rosie would tumble out of her toy basket. She would roll colorful balls across the soft carpet. She would stack wooden blocks into tall towers. She would dress up her teddy bear friends in funny hats and scarves.

But when playtime ended, Rosie had a big problem. She did NOT want to clean up the toys.

"Clean up time, Rosie!" called out Emma, the little girl who owned the nursery. Emma always helped put toys away before dinner.

Rosie looked around the messy room. Blocks were scattered everywhere. Balls had rolled under the rocking chair. Teddy bears sat in a confused pile. The toy basket looked so far away.

"I don't want to clean up," Rosie whispered to herself. "It's too much work. It's too hard."

Rosie tried hiding behind the big stuffed elephant. Maybe if she stayed very still, the mess would disappear. She squeezed her eyes shut tight. When she opened them, the toys were still everywhere.

"That didn't work," sighed Rosie.

Next, Rosie tried asking for help. "Please, Mr. Elephant, can you clean up for me?" But the stuffed elephant just smiled his stitched smile. He couldn't move by himself.

Rosie flopped down on the carpet. She felt frustrated and tired. The room looked like a tornado had spun through it.

Then Rosie had an idea. "What if I make cleaning fun?" she wondered aloud.

She picked up one red ball. "This ball wants to go home to the toy basket," she said in a silly voice. She rolled the ball across the floor. It bumped gently into the basket with a soft *thunk*.

"That was fun!" Rosie giggled.

She picked up a blue block. "All aboard the cleanup train!" she announced. She carried the block like a train conductor. "Choo-choo!" The block found its place in the block box.

Rosie's cleaning game grew bigger. She pretended the teddy bears were sleepy and needed to go to bed in their special corner. She tucked each one in carefully.

"Good night, bears," she whispered softly.

The wooden puzzle pieces became lost puppies looking for their home. Rosie helped each piece find its spot in the puzzle frame. "There you go, little puppy!"

Soon, Rosie was dancing around the room. She spun and twirled as she picked up crayons. She marched like a soldier returning toy cars to their garage box.

With each toy she put away, the room looked better. The carpet appeared bigger and fluffier. The yellow walls seemed brighter and happier.

Emma watched from the doorway. "Wow, Rosie! You're doing such a great job!"

Rosie beamed with pride. Her cheeks felt warm and rosy. Her heart felt light and bouncy.

Finally, only one toy remained. It was Rosie's favorite purple crayon. She had used it to draw beautiful flowers that morning.

"You're the last one, little crayon," Rosie said gently. She placed it carefully in the crayon box. "Sweet dreams."

Rosie looked around the clean nursery. Everything sparkled and shone. The toy basket sat neatly in the corner. The books stood straight on their shelf. Even the rocking chair looked happy and peaceful.

"I did it!" Rosie exclaimed. She spun around with her arms stretched wide. "I cleaned up everything!"

Emma scooped Rosie up in a warm hug. "You were so persistent, Rosie. Even when it seemed hard, you kept trying until you found a way."

Rosie snuggled into Emma's arms. She felt proud and accomplished. The clean nursery looked beautiful and welcoming.

"Tomorrow I'll clean up right away," Rosie promised. "Because I know how to make it fun!"

That night, Rosie slept peacefully in her toy basket. She dreamed of dancing toys and cleanup games. She had learned that even big jobs become easier when you try different ways and never give up.

And the nursery stayed tidy and bright, ready for another day of wonderful play.

The end.
