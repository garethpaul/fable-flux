# Story story_1431

Jack sat on the big red swing. His feet barely touched the ground. The swing moved back and forth slowly. Back and forth. Back and forth.

The playground was very quiet today. No other children were here. Jack pushed himself with his toes. The swing creaked softly. He looked around the empty park. The slide was empty. The monkey bars were empty too.

Jack felt lonely. Playing alone was not very fun. He wished someone would come play with him. The wind blew gently through his hair. It made a whooshing sound in the trees.

"Jack!" called a familiar voice.

Jack looked up. Mama was walking toward him. She had a big smile on her face. Her shoes made soft sounds on the wood chips. Jack's heart felt happy to see her.

"Can I swing too?" Mama asked sweetly.

Jack nodded fast. "Yes, yes, yes!" he said.

Mama sat on the swing next to Jack. Her swing was blue. The chains jingled when she sat down. Mama's feet touched the ground easily. She was much taller than Jack.

"Let's swing together," Mama said.

Jack pushed off with his toes. Mama pushed off too. Their swings moved at the same time. Up and down. Back and forth. Side by side.

"This is fun!" Jack giggled.

"I love swinging with you," Mama said.

They swung higher and higher. Jack felt the wind on his face. It felt cool and nice. Mama's hair flew behind her like a flag. She laughed a musical laugh.

"Look how high we can go!" Jack called out.

"We're flying like birds!" Mama called back.

Jack pretended he was a bird. He spread his arms wide. Mama did the same thing. They soared through the air together. The swings made happy squeaking sounds.

"I'm a blue jay!" said Mama.

"I'm a red cardinal!" said Jack.

They flew higher and higher. The trees looked smaller below them. The clouds looked closer above them. Jack felt like he could touch the sky.

After a while, they slowed down. Their swings moved gently again. Jack's cheeks were pink from the wind. Mama's eyes sparkled with joy.

"That was the best flying ever," Jack said.

"The very best," Mama agreed.

They sat quietly for a moment. Jack listened to the birds singing. He heard the leaves rustling. Everything sounded peaceful and happy.

"Mama?" Jack said softly.

"Yes, sweetie?"

"I was lonely before you came."

Mama's face looked kind and caring. "I'm sorry you felt lonely."

"But now I'm happy," Jack said. "Swinging with you is better than swinging alone."

"Everything is better when we're together," Mama said.

Jack reached over and held Mama's hand. Her hand felt warm and soft. It made him feel safe and loved.

"Can we swing together every day?" Jack asked.

"We can swing together whenever you want," Mama promised.

Jack smiled the biggest smile. His whole face felt bright and happy. Mama squeezed his hand gently.

"Should we swing some more?" Mama asked.

"Yes!" Jack said excitedly.

They started swinging again. This time they sang a song together. Their voices mixed with the wind. The melody floated through the air like magic.

Other children began arriving at the playground. They heard Jack and Mama laughing. The playground filled with happy sounds. But Jack didn't feel lonely anymore.

He had his mama right beside him. They were the best swinging team ever. Jack felt lucky to have such a wonderful mama. She always knew how to make everything better.

As the sun began to set, they slowed their swings down. Jack's heart felt full of love and happiness. Today had been perfect.

"Thank you for swinging with me, Mama," Jack said.

"Thank you for sharing your swing time with me," Mama replied.

Hand in hand, they walked home together. Jack couldn't wait for tomorrow. Tomorrow they would swing together again. And the day after that too.

Jack had learned something important today. The best adventures happen when people you love are right beside you.

The end.
