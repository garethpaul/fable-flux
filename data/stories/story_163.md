# The Great Car Hunt

Tommy sat at the big wooden dining table. His feet swung back and forth. The morning sun made golden squares on the floor.

"Where is my red car?" Tommy asked himself.

His favorite toy car was missing. The shiny red one with yellow wheels. Tommy loved that car more than anything.

Mom was making breakfast in the kitchen. "Tommy, come eat your pancakes!" she called.

But Tommy couldn't think about food. His special car was gone!

"I need to find it," Tommy said. He slipped down from his chair.

First, Tommy looked under the table. He crawled on his hands and knees. The carpet felt soft and bumpy. But no red car.

"Maybe it's behind the curtains," Tommy thought.

He pushed the long blue curtains aside. Dust tickles danced in the sunlight. Still no car.

Tommy's bottom lip started to wobble. Where could his car be?

"Don't give up," he told himself. That's what Daddy always said.

Next, Tommy checked the big china cabinet. He stood on his tippy-toes. The dishes sparkled like jewels. But the shelves were too high to see.

"Mom!" Tommy called. "Can you help me look up high?"

Mom wiped her hands on her apron. She had flour in her brown hair. "What are we looking for, sweetie?"

"My red car. It's lost forever!" Tommy's eyes filled with tears.

Mom gave him a warm hug. "Let's look together. Two sets of eyes are better than one."

They searched the dining room carefully. Mom moved the heavy chairs. Tommy looked in every corner. They even checked inside the old wooden buffet.

No red car anywhere.

Tommy sat on the floor. A big tear rolled down his cheek. "I'll never find it," he sniffled.

"Wait," Mom said. "When did you last play with your car?"

Tommy wiped his nose. "Yesterday. After dinner. I was driving it around the table."

"Show me exactly where," Mom said gently.

Tommy crawled under the table again. He pretended to drive his car. "Vroom, vroom! I went this way. Then that way. Then..."

Tommy stopped. He remembered something important.

"I drove it to the corner! By the plant!"

Tommy and Mom hurried to the big green plant. Its leaves hung down like an umbrella.

"Look carefully," Mom said.

Tommy pushed aside the thick leaves. They felt cool and smooth. Behind the plant was a basket of magazines.

And there, tucked between two colorful books, was something red!

"My car!" Tommy shouted. "I found it!"

He pulled out his shiny red toy. The yellow wheels still spun perfectly. Tommy hugged it tight.

"You didn't give up," Mom said proudly. "You kept trying until you found it."

Tommy beamed. His smile was bigger than the morning sun.

"I remembered where I played," Tommy said. "That helped me think."

Mom ruffled his hair. "Sometimes the best way to find something is to remember your steps."

Tommy drove his car across the dining room floor. "Vroom, vroom!" The wheels made happy clicking sounds on the wood.

"Now," Mom said with a smile, "how about those pancakes?"

Tommy's tummy rumbled loudly. All that searching made him hungry!

He parked his red car safely on the dining table. Right where he could see it.

"I won't lose you again," Tommy promised his car.

As Tommy ate his fluffy pancakes, he felt proud and happy. He had solved his problem all by himself. Well, with a little help from Mom.

The syrup tasted extra sweet. The sun felt extra warm. And his red car looked extra shiny sitting nearby.

Tommy learned something important that morning. When you lose something special, don't give up. Think carefully. Remember your steps. Keep looking.

And sometimes, the thing you're looking for is closer than you think.

Tommy smiled and took another big bite of pancake. It was going to be a wonderful day.

The end.
