# Story story_564

Anna sat on the soft carpet. Her toys were spread around her. She had dolls and blocks and crayons. But Anna felt sad and lonely.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," she whispered.

The living room felt very quiet. Sunlight came through the big window. It made warm yellow patches on the floor. Anna picked up her favorite doll. She brushed its yellow hair.

Suddenly, Anna heard a soft sound. It was like a gentle rumble. She looked up from her doll. Her eyes grew very wide.

A big, golden lion sat by the couch! His mane was fluffy and soft-looking. His eyes were kind and brown. The lion looked friendly, not scary at all.

"Hello, little Anna," said the lion. His voice was warm like honey. "I am Leo. I live in your imagination."

Anna blinked twice. She rubbed her eyes. The lion was still there!

"You're really here?" Anna asked. Her voice was very small.

"Yes, I am here," Leo smiled. "I heard you were lonely. I came to play with you."

Anna felt excited but nervous too. She had never played with a lion before.

"What should we play?" she asked.

Leo's tail swished happily. "What do you like to do?"

Anna thought for a moment. "I like to build towers with blocks."

"That sounds wonderful!" Leo said. "May I help you?"

Anna nodded. She felt braver now. Leo seemed very nice.

They started building together. Anna stacked the red blocks. Leo carefully placed the blue ones with his big paws. His paws were surprisingly gentle.

"Look how tall it is!" Anna giggled.

The tower reached up to the coffee table. It had red and blue stripes. It looked like a castle tower.

"We make a good team," Leo said proudly.

Next, they played with Anna's dolls. Leo pretended to be a gentle giant. He spoke in a funny, deep voice. Anna laughed until her tummy hurt.

"Your dolls are having a tea party," Leo announced. He pretended to sip from a tiny cup. "This tea is delicious!"

Anna clapped her hands. "You're so silly, Leo!"

Then they played hide and seek. Leo was very big. It was hard for him to hide! His tail stuck out from behind the chair. Anna found him right away.

"I see your tail!" Anna called out.

Leo pretended to be surprised. "Oh no! You found me!"

Anna giggled and ran to tag him. Leo's fur was soft and warm. It felt like the fluffiest blanket ever.

They colored pictures together. Anna drew a little girl. Leo drew a big lion. In both pictures, they were holding hands and smiling.

"We look happy," Anna said.

"That's because we are happy," Leo replied.

As the afternoon went on, Anna felt different. The lonely feeling was gone. Her heart felt full and warm. Playing with Leo was the best fun ever.

They built a fort with couch cushions. They pretended to be brave explorers. They sang silly songs together. Leo's voice was rumbly but sweet.

When the sun started to set, Anna felt tired. She yawned a big yawn.

"Will you stay with me, Leo?" she asked.

"I will always be here when you need a friend," Leo promised. "Just close your eyes and think of me."

Anna hugged Leo tight. His mane tickled her face. He smelled like sunshine and adventure.

"Thank you for playing with me," Anna whispered.

"Thank you for being my friend," Leo whispered back.

Anna learned something important that day. Even when she felt lonely, friendship was always possible. Sometimes friends come in surprising ways. Sometimes they come from your imagination. But they are always real in your heart.

From that day on, Anna was never truly lonely. Whenever she needed a friend, Leo would appear. They would play and laugh together. Their friendship filled the living room with joy and love.

Anna smiled as she fell asleep. Tomorrow would bring new adventures with her dear friend Leo.

The end.
