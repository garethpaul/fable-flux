# Papa and Anna's Special Day

Papa sat on the soft carpet in the living room. He had his favorite puzzle spread out. The pieces showed a bright rainbow. Papa picked up each piece carefully. He tried to fit them together.

"This is tricky," Papa said quietly. He looked around the empty room. The house felt very quiet today. Papa wished someone would play with him.

Just then, little Anna peeked around the corner. Her curly hair bounced as she walked. She saw Papa sitting with his puzzle.

"What are you doing, Papa?" Anna asked. Her eyes grew wide with curiosity.

Papa looked up and smiled. "I'm making a rainbow puzzle. But it's harder than I thought."

Anna's face lit up like sunshine. "Can I help you? Please, Papa?"

Papa patted the carpet beside him. "Of course! Come sit here with me."

Anna plopped down next to Papa. She picked up a blue puzzle piece. "This looks like the sky!"

"You're right!" Papa said happily. "Let's find where it goes."

Together, they looked at the puzzle box. The rainbow had seven beautiful colors. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple, and pink.

"I see red pieces!" Anna pointed excitedly.

"And I found orange ones!" Papa added.

They worked side by side. Anna found pieces that fit perfectly. Papa showed her how to look for straight edges. Soon, they had the border done.

"We make a great team," Papa said. He gave Anna a gentle hug.

Anna giggled. "This is so much fun! Can we do the middle now?"

They took turns picking up pieces. Anna would hand Papa the colors he needed. Papa would help Anna turn pieces the right way.

"Look, Papa! The yellow fits here!" Anna clapped her hands.

"Perfect! You have good eyes," Papa said proudly.

As they worked, they talked and laughed. Anna told Papa about her favorite colors. Papa shared stories about rainbows after rainstorms.

"I love green," Anna said. "Like grass and trees!"

"Green is wonderful," Papa agreed. "My favorite is blue, like the ocean."

Piece by piece, their rainbow grew bigger. The living room filled with happy sounds. Anna hummed a little song. Papa tapped his fingers on the carpet.

"We're almost done!" Anna bounced excitedly.

Papa held up the very last piece. It was purple and sparkly. "Would you like to put in the final piece?"

Anna's eyes sparkled too. "Can we do it together?"

They both held the purple piece. Carefully, they placed it in the rainbow. Click! It fit perfectly.

"We did it!" they shouted together.

The rainbow puzzle was complete and beautiful. All seven colors shined brightly on the carpet.

Anna jumped up and down. "Papa, we're the best puzzle team ever!"

Papa stood up and spun Anna around. "You made this so much more fun, Anna. I was lonely before you came."

"I was looking for something fun to do," Anna said. "I'm so glad I found you!"

They both looked at their finished rainbow. It was even prettier than the picture on the box.

"Should we do another puzzle tomorrow?" Papa asked hopefully.

"Yes! And maybe we can build blocks too!" Anna suggested.

"And read stories!" Papa added.

They gave each other the biggest hug. The living room felt warm and cozy now. It was full of love and laughter.

Papa realized something special. Playing alone was okay sometimes. But playing with Anna was much more wonderful. They helped each other and shared ideas.

Anna learned something too. Papa was the best playing partner ever. He was patient and kind. Together, they could do anything!

They decided to leave the rainbow puzzle out. It would remind them of their special day together.

"I love you, Papa," Anna said sweetly.

"I love you too, Anna. Thank you for being my friend."

They sat together on the couch. They looked at their beautiful rainbow. Outside, the sun was setting. But inside, their friendship glowed brighter than any rainbow.

From that day on, Papa and Anna played together every afternoon. They discovered that everything was more fun when shared with someone special.
