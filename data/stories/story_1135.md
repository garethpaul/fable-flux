# Story story_1135

Anna loved her swing set. It sat under the big oak tree in her backyard. The red seat was smooth and warm from the sunshine.

Today felt extra special. Mama had baked chocolate chip cookies. The sweet smell floated through the kitchen window. Anna could smell the warm chocolate and butter. Her tummy rumbled with hunger.

"The cookies are cooling on the counter," Mama called. "You can have one after lunch."

Anna swung back and forth. She pumped her little legs. Higher and higher she went. The wind tickled her face. Her brown hair flew behind her.

Through the kitchen window, Anna could see the cookies. They sat on a white plate. The chocolate chips looked dark and yummy. The cookies were golden brown. They looked perfectly round.

Anna's mouth watered. She really wanted a cookie. But there was one big problem. The counter was too high. Anna was too short to reach.

Anna stopped swinging. She ran to the kitchen. Mama was outside watering flowers. Anna stood on her tippy-toes. She stretched her arms up high. Her fingers wiggled in the air. But the cookies were still too far away.

"I need to get taller," Anna said to herself.

Anna had an idea. She would use her swing to help! Anna ran back outside. She pumped her legs hard. The swing went up and up. When she swung toward the window, Anna reached out her hand.

Whoosh! She swung past the window. Her fingers barely touched the glass. The cookies were still too far inside. Anna tried again and again. Each time she missed. The swing moved too fast. Anna felt frustrated.

"This isn't working," Anna sighed. She dragged her feet to stop the swing.

Anna sat very still. She thought hard. There had to be another way. She looked around the yard. Her eyes stopped at the garden. Mama's step stool sat by the tomato plants!

Anna's face lit up with a big smile. "That's it!" she said.

Anna ran to get the step stool. It was blue and had two steps. Anna carried it carefully to the kitchen. The step stool was a little heavy. But Anna was strong.

Anna placed the step stool by the counter. She climbed up slowly. One step. Two steps. Now Anna was much taller! She could see the whole kitchen counter.

The cookies were right there. Anna could smell them even better now. They smelled like vanilla and chocolate. Her tummy rumbled again.

Anna reached out carefully. Her small hand touched the warm cookie. It felt soft and a little crumbly. Anna picked up one cookie. She held it like a treasure.

"I did it!" Anna said happily. She felt very proud of herself.

Just then, Mama came inside. She saw Anna on the step stool. Anna held the cookie in her hand. Anna's cheeks turned pink.

"I'm sorry, Mama," Anna said quietly. "I really wanted a cookie."

Mama smiled warmly. "I can see that, sweetie. But lunch is in ten minutes."

Anna's shoulders dropped. She started to put the cookie back.

"Wait," Mama said. "You worked very hard to solve your problem. You didn't give up when the swing didn't work. You found another way."

Anna looked up hopefully.

"You may have that cookie now," Mama said. "But just one before lunch."

Anna's smile was as big as the sun. She took a tiny bite. The cookie was sweet and warm. The chocolate melted on her tongue. It was the most delicious cookie ever.

"Thank you, Mama!" Anna said with her mouth full.

"You're welcome," Mama laughed. "Next time, just ask for help reaching high places."

Anna nodded. She climbed down from the step stool. She finished her cookie outside on the swing. The chocolate made her fingers sticky. But Anna didn't mind.

She had learned something important today. When something seems impossible, don't give up. Think of new ways to solve the problem. Sometimes the answer is right there waiting. You just have to look around and keep trying.

Anna swung gently under the oak tree. She felt happy and proud. And the cookie tasted extra sweet.

The end.
