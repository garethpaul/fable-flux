# Story story_832

**Grandpa's Missing Blocks**

Grandpa Joe loved visiting the nursery school. He had fuzzy gray hair and kind brown eyes. Today he wore his favorite blue sweater with tiny elephants on it.

"Good morning, children!" Grandpa Joe said with a big smile. The little ones giggled and waved. They loved story time with Grandpa Joe.

Grandpa Joe looked around the colorful room. Red cushions sat in a circle. Yellow paint covered one wall. Green plants hung from the ceiling. Everything looked perfect for story time.

But something was wrong. Grandpa Joe scratched his head. "Oh my," he said softly. "Where are my special blocks?"

Every week, Grandpa Joe brought wooden alphabet blocks. The blocks were smooth and warm. They smelled like cedar wood. Each block had bright letters painted on all sides.

"I always tell stories with my blocks," Grandpa Joe said. His voice sounded worried. "The children love to touch the letters."

Little Emma tugged on his sweater. "Don't be sad, Grandpa Joe," she said. Her small hand felt warm on his arm.

Grandpa Joe smiled down at Emma. "You're right, sweetie. Let me think hard."

Grandpa Joe closed his eyes tight. He tried to remember. Where did he put those blocks? His forehead wrinkled up like a raisin.

"I remember now!" Grandpa Joe said suddenly. His eyes popped open wide. "I left them in my car!"

Grandpa Joe walked outside quickly. His shoes made soft tapping sounds on the sidewalk. The morning air felt cool and fresh. Birds chirped happy songs in the tall oak trees.

He opened his car door. The inside smelled like coffee and peppermint candy. Grandpa Joe looked under the front seat. Nothing there. He checked the back seat. Still nothing.

"Oh dear," Grandpa Joe sighed. His shoulders drooped down low. "They're not here either."

Grandpa Joe walked back inside slowly. The children sat waiting on their red cushions. Their faces looked hopeful and excited.

"I'm sorry, friends," Grandpa Joe said sadly. "I can't find my blocks anywhere."

But then Grandpa Joe had another idea. His eyes sparkled like stars. "Wait just one minute!"

Grandpa Joe remembered something important. Yesterday he visited his daughter's house. He played with his grandson Tommy. They built tall towers together.

"I think I left them at Sarah's house!" Grandpa Joe said. He clapped his hands together. The sound echoed in the quiet room.

Grandpa Joe pulled out his phone. It was big and black with large numbers. He pressed the buttons carefully. Ring, ring, ring went the phone.

"Hello, Sarah?" Grandpa Joe said. "Do you see my blocks anywhere?"

Sarah's voice sounded cheerful through the phone. "Yes, Dad! Tommy was playing with them this morning. They're right here on the kitchen table."

"Wonderful!" Grandpa Joe said. His whole face lit up with joy. "Can you bring them to the nursery?"

"I'll be right there," Sarah promised.

Ten minutes later, Sarah arrived. She carried a wooden box that rattled softly. Inside were all twenty-six alphabet blocks. They gleamed like tiny treasures.

"Hooray!" shouted all the children. They clapped their small hands together. The room filled with happy sounds.

Grandpa Joe hugged the box tight. "Thank you for being so patient," he told the children. "Sometimes we lose things. But we should never give up looking."

Emma raised her tiny hand. "You didn't give up, Grandpa Joe!"

"That's exactly right," Grandpa Joe said proudly. "When something seems lost, we try again. We think of new places to look."

Grandpa Joe opened the wooden box. The blocks smelled wonderful and felt smooth. He handed one to each child. Their faces glowed with happiness.

"Now," said Grandpa Joe with a twinkle in his eye, "let's build the best story ever!"

The children giggled and held their blocks tight. Story time would be perfect after all. Sometimes the best adventures happen when things don't go as planned.

The end.
