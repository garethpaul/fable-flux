# Story story_1371

**The Tall Friend on the Hill**

Nutkin the squirrel sat on a big rock. The rock was on top of Green Hill. He looked down at the pretty valley below.

The sun felt warm on his fluffy tail. Birds sang sweet songs in the trees. Flowers smelled like honey and sunshine.

But Nutkin felt sad inside. He had no friends to play with. All the other squirrels lived far away.

"I wish someone would play with me," Nutkin said softly.

Just then, he heard heavy footsteps. THUMP. THUMP. THUMP.

A very tall giraffe walked up the hill. Her spots were golden brown. Her neck reached up to the clouds.

"Hello, little squirrel," said the giraffe. "I'm Rosie. What's your name?"

"I'm Nutkin," he said quietly. He felt very small next to Rosie.

"You look lonely," Rosie said kindly. "May I play with you?"

Nutkin's eyes lit up like tiny stars. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" Rosie smiled. "Friends come in all sizes."

But how could they play together? Nutkin was tiny. Rosie was enormous.

"Let's play hide and seek," suggested Nutkin.

Rosie tried to hide behind a small tree. Her long neck stuck out everywhere. Her legs showed on both sides.

Nutkin giggled. "I can see you, Rosie!"

"Your turn to hide," Rosie laughed.

Nutkin scurried behind a pebble. But he was too big for such a tiny hiding spot.

"Found you!" Rosie called right away.

They both started laughing. The sound echoed across the hill.

"Let's try something else," said Rosie.

"How about tag?" asked Nutkin.

Rosie took one giant step. She was already far ahead. Nutkin ran as fast as his little legs could go. But he couldn't catch up.

"This isn't working either," panted Nutkin.

Rosie bent her long neck down low. "Don't give up, friend. We'll find something fun."

Nutkin thought hard. He tapped his tiny paw on his chin.

"I have an idea!" he squeaked excitedly.

Nutkin climbed up Rosie's long neck. It felt like climbing a warm, spotted ladder. Her fur was soft like velvet.

"What a wonderful view!" Nutkin called from the top.

From Rosie's head, Nutkin could see everything. The whole valley spread out below them. Rivers looked like silver ribbons. Trees looked like tiny green dots.

"Now I'll be your lookout," said Nutkin proudly.

"And I'll be your tall ride," said Rosie happily.

They walked around the hill together. Nutkin pointed out interesting things from up high. Rosie shared what she saw down low.

"Look! A family of rabbits!" Nutkin called.

"I see a patch of sweet clover," Rosie said.

They played this way all afternoon. The sun painted the sky orange and pink.

"This is the best day ever," said Nutkin.

"I think so too," agreed Rosie.

As the stars began to twinkle, Nutkin climbed down. He felt warm and happy inside.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" he asked hopefully.

"Every day," promised Rosie. "That's what friends do."

They hugged goodnight. Well, Rosie nuzzled Nutkin gently with her big nose.

The next morning, Nutkin woke up excited. He wasn't lonely anymore. He had found a special friend.

When Rosie arrived, she brought a surprise. Three other animals followed behind her.

"I told everyone about my wonderful friend," Rosie explained.

Soon the hill was full of laughter and games. All the animals played together happily.

Nutkin learned something important that day. Friends don't have to be the same size. They just need kind hearts.

And the best friendships can start with someone brave enough to say hello.

From that day on, Green Hill became known as Friendship Hill. Animals came from everywhere to play together.

Nutkin was never lonely again. He had learned that the best adventures happen when friends help each other.

Every sunset, Nutkin and Rosie sat together on their special rock. They watched the stars come out and planned tomorrow's fun.

"Thank you for being my friend," Nutkin would say.

"Thank you for teaching me that friendship has no size," Rosie would answer.

And they lived happily ever after on their beautiful hill.

The end.
