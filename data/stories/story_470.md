# Grandma's Magical Bath Time

Grandma Rose sat in her cozy chair. The clock said six o'clock. "Time for my bath," she whispered. But Grandma Rose didn't want to move. Her old bones felt tired today.

"Bath time feels like work," she sighed. The bathroom seemed so far away. Her fuzzy slippers felt heavy. "Maybe I'll skip it tonight."

But Grandma Rose knew better. She stood up slowly. Her knees made little creaky sounds. Step by step, she walked down the hall. The bathroom tiles felt cold under her feet.

Grandma turned on the water. Splash, splash, splash! The tub began to fill. She added her lavender soap. Purple bubbles started to dance. The smell reminded her of something special.

"Oh my," Grandma smiled softly. "This smells like Mama's garden." She remembered picking lavender flowers. Her mama had long, gentle hands. They would make soap together. The kitchen always smelled like heaven.

Grandma Rose dipped her toe in. The water felt perfectly warm. She stepped in carefully. Down, down, down she went. The bubbles tickled her chin. They made her giggle like a little girl.

"Bubble beard!" she laughed out loud. She scooped up white, fluffy bubbles. She put them on her chin. In the mirror, she looked silly. Her eyes sparkled with joy.

The warm water hugged her body. Her tired muscles began to relax. "Ahhhh," she said happily. The water felt like a warm hug. All her worries floated away.

Grandma Rose found her yellow rubber duck. His name was Sunny. She hadn't played with him for weeks. "Hello, old friend," she whispered. Sunny bobbed up and down. Splash! He made tiny waves.

She remembered giving Sunny baths too. Her granddaughter Emma loved Sunny. They would play together every visit. "Quack, quack!" Emma would say. They would laugh and laugh.

Grandma closed her eyes peacefully. She listened to gentle water sounds. Drip, drop, drip, drop. The bubbles popped softly around her. Pop, pop, pop! Each sound felt magical.

Her washcloth was soft and warm. She rubbed it on her arms. The lavender smell grew stronger. It filled the whole bathroom. Everything felt calm and beautiful.

"I feel like a queen," Grandma said. The bathtub was her royal throne. The bubbles were her sparkling jewels. The warm water was her silk robe. She felt fancy and special.

Grandma Rose sang her favorite song. "You are my sunshine, my only sunshine." Her voice echoed softly. The bathroom became her concert hall. Even Sunny seemed to listen.

Twenty minutes passed like magic. Grandma felt completely new. Her skin was soft and smooth. Her hair smelled like flowers. Her heart felt light and happy.

She stood up slowly. Water dripped down gently. She wrapped herself in her fluffy towel. It felt like a cloud. Warm and soft and perfect.

"That was wonderful!" she said aloud. Grandma Rose looked in the mirror. Her cheeks were rosy pink. Her eyes were bright and shiny. She looked beautiful and peaceful.

She drained the magical water. Glub, glub, glub! The bubbles swirled away. But the happiness stayed inside her. It filled her whole body.

Grandma Rose put on her nightgown. It was soft and cozy. She brushed her clean hair. Everything felt fresh and new. She felt ready for sweet dreams.

Walking back to her chair, she smiled. Her body felt relaxed and comfortable. Her mind felt calm and happy. Bath time had been magical.

"Tomorrow I'll add rose petals," she planned. "Maybe I'll bring my favorite book." She could read while soaking. That would be even more special.

Grandma Rose settled into her chair. She closed her eyes peacefully. Tomorrow's bath time couldn't come soon enough. She would make it magical again.

"Thank you, Mama," she whispered softly. "For teaching me about lavender magic." Some daily routines hold special surprises. We just need to look for them.

Bath time had become her favorite time. Every day would bring new magic.

The end.
