# Penny Plane's Colorful Discovery

Penny Plane sat on the kitchen table. Her silver wings gleamed in the morning light. Today was art time at home.

"Art time, Penny!" called Mom. "Let's make something beautiful together."

Penny's propeller drooped a little. She looked at the art supplies. Crayons, paper, and paint brushes covered the table. Everything looked so messy and complicated.

"I don't know how to make art," Penny whispered. "My wings are too big. My propeller gets in the way."

Mom smiled warmly. "Every artist starts somewhere, little plane. Let's try together."

Penny rolled closer to the paper. She picked up a blue crayon with her wing tip. The crayon felt heavy and strange. She tried to draw a line.

Wobble, wobble went the crayon. The line looked bumpy and crooked.

"This is too hard," Penny sighed. Her engine made a sad puttering sound.

"What if we try something different?" Mom suggested. She dipped a paint brush in bright yellow paint. "Watch this, Penny."

Mom showed Penny how to hold the brush. "Dip, dab, and swish!" Mom said cheerfully.

Penny tried again. She dipped her wing in yellow paint. Then she touched the paper gently. A beautiful yellow dot appeared!

"I did it!" Penny's propeller spun with excitement. "Look, Mom! I made a sun!"

"That's wonderful!" Mom clapped her hands. "What else can you paint?"

Penny dipped her other wing in blue paint. She made swooshy lines across the paper. "These are clouds!" she announced proudly.

The blue paint mixed with yellow. Something magical happened. Green appeared on the paper!

"Oh my!" Penny gasped. "The colors are dancing together!"

Her propeller spun faster. She dipped her tail in red paint. Red swirls joined the blue and yellow. The colors blended and swirled like a rainbow.

"This is amazing!" Penny zoomed around the table. Paint drops flew from her wings. They landed on the paper like colorful rain.

Mom laughed. "You're a natural artist, Penny!"

Penny felt warm and happy inside. Her engine hummed a cheerful tune. She painted puffy white clouds with her propeller. She made green grass with her landing gear.

"Look!" Penny pointed with her wing. "I painted a whole sky!"

The paper was full of beautiful colors. Swirls of blue, yellow, and green danced together. Red dots looked like flowers. White clouds floated peacefully.

"It's the most beautiful painting ever," Mom said. "You should be very proud."

Penny's chest puffed out with pride. Her silver body sparkled in the sunlight. She had never felt so accomplished.

"Can we hang it up?" Penny asked hopefully.

"Of course!" Mom taped the painting to the refrigerator. "Now everyone can see your beautiful art."

Penny stepped back to admire her work. The colors seemed to glow under the kitchen lights. Her bumpy lines and wobbly dots looked perfect now.

"I love how the yellow became green," Penny marveled. "And how the red made everything bright!"

Mom cleaned Penny's wings gently. "Art is about expressing yourself, sweetie. There's no wrong way to create."

Penny's propeller spun thoughtfully. "I thought art would be scary. But it was fun!"

"The best activities often surprise us," Mom said wisely.

As evening came, Penny looked at her painting again. She felt proud and excited. Her wings tingled with creative energy.

"Mom," Penny said sleepily. "Can we do art time again tomorrow?"

"Of course!" Mom smiled. "What would you like to paint next?"

Penny's eyes sparkled with ideas. "Maybe a rainbow! Or flowers! Or other planes flying!"

She snuggled close to Mom. Her engine purred contentedly. Art time had become her favorite part of the day.

"Tomorrow will be another colorful adventure," Penny whispered happily.

Outside, stars twinkled like paint dots in the dark sky. Penny dreamed of all the beautiful things she would create. Art time wasn't scary anymore. It was magical.

And Penny Plane couldn't wait for tomorrow's creative fun to begin.

The end.
