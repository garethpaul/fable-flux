# Story story_927

**Giraffe's New Friend**

In a sunny backyard, a tall giraffe stood alone. His name was Spots. He had brown patches all over his yellow body. Spots was very tall. His head reached the apple tree branches.

Spots felt sad and lonely. He wanted someone to play with. The birds were too small. The squirrels ran too fast. Nobody wanted to be his friend.

Spots stretched his long neck down. He picked up a red ball with his mouth. He tried to bounce it. The ball rolled away under the fence. Spots sighed a big, sad sigh.

From the kitchen window, a little girl watched. Her name was Anna. She had curly brown hair and bright green eyes. Anna saw the lonely giraffe. Her heart felt warm and caring.

Anna opened the back door. She stepped into the soft grass. The sun felt warm on her face. She walked slowly toward Spots.

"Hello, tall friend," Anna said softly. Her voice was gentle and kind.

Spots looked down. He saw a small girl smiling up at him. His brown eyes grew wide with surprise. Nobody had ever talked to him before.

"I'm Anna," she said. "What's your name?"

"I'm Spots," the giraffe said quietly. His voice was deep but friendly.

Anna noticed the red ball by the fence. She ran to get it. Her shoes made soft sounds on the grass. She picked up the ball and held it high.

"Want to play catch?" Anna asked.

Spots felt excited. His tail swished back and forth. "Yes, please!" he said.

Anna threw the ball gently. Spots caught it with his mouth. The ball felt smooth and round. He tossed it back down to Anna. She caught it with both hands.

"Good catch!" Anna laughed. Her laugh sounded like tiny bells.

They played catch for a long time. The ball went up and down. Up and down. Anna giggled when Spots made funny faces. Spots smiled when Anna did little jumps.

"Let's play hide and seek," Anna suggested.

Spots looked worried. "But I'm too tall to hide," he said.

Anna thought hard. She tapped her finger on her chin. "You can hide your head behind the tree," she said. "I'll close my eyes."

Anna counted to ten. "One, two, three..." Her voice got quieter as she counted.

Spots bent his long neck. He put his head behind the big apple tree. His body still showed, but he felt hidden.

"Ready or not, here I come!" Anna called.

She pretended to look everywhere. Under the garden chair. Behind the flower pots. She even looked in the sandbox.

"Where could Spots be?" Anna wondered out loud.

Spots giggled softly. The sound made Anna smile.

"Found you!" Anna shouted. She pointed at the tree.

Spots lifted his head up high. "That was fun!" he said happily.

Now it was Anna's turn to hide. She curled up behind a big sunflower. The yellow petals tickled her nose. She tried not to sneeze.

Spots looked down with his keen eyes. He found Anna quickly. But he waited a moment. He wanted the game to last longer.

"I wonder where Anna went," Spots said loudly.

Anna giggled behind the sunflower. The sound gave her away.

"There you are!" Spots said gently.

They played until the sun began to set. The sky turned orange and pink. The air smelled like fresh flowers and grass.

Anna's mother called from the house. "Anna, time for dinner!"

Anna felt sad to stop playing. Spots felt sad too. His ears drooped down.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" Spots asked hopefully.

"Yes!" Anna said. "We're friends now. Friends always come back."

She gave Spots a gentle hug around his front leg. His fur felt soft and warm. Spots nuzzled Anna's hair with his nose.

"Thank you for playing with me," Spots said.

"Thank you for being my friend," Anna replied.

Anna waved goodbye. She skipped toward the house. Her heart felt happy and full.

Spots watched her go. He wasn't lonely anymore. Tomorrow would bring more fun and friendship. He had found his very first friend.

The stars began to twinkle. Spots fell asleep with a big smile. Dreams of friendship danced in his head.

The end.
