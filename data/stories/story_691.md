# Story story_691

**Grandpa's Big Problem**

Grandpa Joe loved his new house. It had a cozy kitchen. It had a sunny living room. Best of all, it had a big attic where he could work on puzzles.

One morning, Grandpa climbed the creaky wooden stairs. Step by step, he went up to his attic. The morning sun made everything bright and cheerful.

Grandpa walked to the small attic window. He wanted to see his beautiful garden outside. But when he looked out, all he could see was the tall wooden fence.

"Oh my!" said Grandpa. "I cannot see over this fence at all!"

Grandpa stood on his tippy-toes. He stretched his neck like a giraffe. He could only see the brown fence boards. No flowers. No trees. No pretty garden.

Grandpa felt sad. He loved looking at his red roses. He loved watching the yellow butterflies dance. He loved seeing the green grass sparkle with morning dew.

"I must find a way to see my garden," Grandpa said to himself.

Grandpa looked around his attic. He saw his old wooden chair. It had soft blue cushions on it.

"This chair will help me!" Grandpa said with a smile.

He pushed the heavy chair across the floor. The chair legs made scraping sounds on the wood. Grandpa huffed and puffed as he moved it.

Finally, the chair sat by the window. Grandpa climbed up carefully. He held onto the window frame. His hands felt the smooth, cool glass.

Grandpa looked out again. He could see a little bit more now. But the fence was still too tall! He could only see the very tops of his sunflowers.

"This is not working," Grandpa sighed. He climbed down from the chair. His shoulders drooped with disappointment.

But Grandpa did not give up. He was a smart man who solved problems.

Grandpa looked around his attic again. In the corner, he saw old books stacked up high. The books had colorful covers and thick pages.

"I have an idea!" Grandpa said. His eyes twinkled with excitement.

Grandpa carried the heavy books one by one. His arms worked hard. The books smelled like old paper and memories. He stacked them on the chair seat.

The pile grew taller and taller. Grandpa made sure each book sat straight and steady. He tested the pile with his hand. It felt strong and safe.

"Now let me try again," Grandpa said hopefully.

Very carefully, Grandpa climbed onto the chair. Then he stepped up onto the books. He held the window frame tightly with both hands.

Grandpa looked out the window again. This time, something wonderful happened!

He could see over the fence! His whole garden spread out below him like a beautiful painting.

Grandpa saw his red roses blooming in the sunshine. He saw orange marigolds dancing in the gentle breeze. He watched a blue jay splash in his little bird bath. The water made tiny ripples that caught the light.

"How beautiful!" Grandpa said with joy. "I can see everything now!"

A yellow butterfly landed on a purple flower. Two squirrels chased each other around the big oak tree. The morning sun made all the colors bright and happy.

Grandpa smiled the biggest smile. His heart felt warm and full of happiness.

From that day on, Grandpa climbed up to his special lookout every morning. He watched his garden wake up with the sun. He saw which flowers opened first. He counted how many birds came to visit.

Sometimes his granddaughter Emma came to visit. Grandpa would lift her up to see the garden too.

"Grandpa, how did you get so smart?" Emma would ask.

"When we have a problem, we keep trying," Grandpa would say. "We think of new ideas. We never give up. That is how we solve things."

Grandpa learned that being short was not really a problem at all. It just meant he needed to be creative and try harder.

Now Grandpa's attic was his favorite room in the whole house. Every day brought new surprises to see in his wonderful garden below.

The end.
