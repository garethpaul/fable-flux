# Story story_647

**Ben and the Magic Story Time**

The big clock on the wall said three o'clock. "Story time!" called Miss Sarah from the reading corner. She patted the soft, colorful carpet. All the children ran over with big smiles.

All the children except Ben.

Ben sat at the art table. He kept coloring his dinosaur picture with a green crayon. The crayon made scratchy sounds on the paper. "I don't want to," Ben whispered to himself.

"Come on, Ben!" said his friend Emma. She waved her hand. "Miss Sarah has a new book today!"

Ben shook his head. His tummy felt funny and tight. Story time meant sitting still. Story time meant listening for a long time. Ben liked to move around. He liked to build blocks and run and jump.

Miss Sarah walked over to Ben. Her shoes made soft sounds on the floor. She smelled like vanilla cookies. "Would you like to help me today, Ben?" she asked gently.

Ben looked up. His eyes were wide. "Help?"

"Yes," said Miss Sarah. "This story needs a special helper."

Ben felt curious now. He put down his green crayon. Slowly, he walked to the reading corner. The carpet felt bumpy under his feet. He sat down next to Emma.

Miss Sarah held up a big book. The cover showed a sleepy dragon with purple scales. "This is 'The Dragon Who Couldn't Roar,'" she said.

Ben's eyes got bigger. Dragons were cool!

Miss Sarah opened the book. "Once upon a time," she began, "there lived a little dragon named Puff."

As Miss Sarah read, something amazing happened. Ben could see Puff in his mind. The dragon had sparkly purple scales. His wings were like soft blankets. But poor Puff could only whisper.

"Ben," said Miss Sarah, "can you help Puff try to roar?"

Ben nodded fast. He stood up. He took a deep breath. "ROARRR!" he said as loud as he could.

All the children giggled. Ben felt happy and proud.

Miss Sarah kept reading. In the story, Puff met other animals. He met a wise old owl who hooted. She met a friendly lion who roared. Each time, Miss Sarah asked Ben to make the sounds.

Ben hooted like the owl. "Hoo, hoo!"

Ben roared like the lion. "ROARRR!"

Ben even chirped like the little birds. "Tweet, tweet!"

The other children clapped. They laughed and smiled. Ben felt warm inside, like sunshine.

As the story went on, Puff learned something important. He didn't need to roar loud like the lion. His gentle whisper was special too. When baby animals were scared, Puff's soft voice made them feel safe.

"Just like Ben's helpful sounds made our story special," said Miss Sarah. She smiled at Ben.

Ben felt his cheeks get warm. He felt proud and happy.

At the end of the story, Puff found his perfect roar. It wasn't the loudest roar. But it was his very own roar. All the forest animals loved it.

Miss Sarah closed the book. The pages made a soft whoosh sound. "What did you think, everyone?"

"I loved it!" said Emma.

"The dragon was cool!" said another boy.

Ben raised his hand high. "Can I help with tomorrow's story too?"

Miss Sarah's eyes twinkled. "Of course, Ben! Tomorrow we're reading about a dancing bear."

Ben bounced a little bit. Dancing sounded fun! Maybe he could show the bear's dance moves.

As the children got ready for snack time, Ben felt different. His tummy didn't feel tight anymore. Instead, it felt fluttery with excitement.

Story time wasn't just about sitting still. It was about going on adventures. It was about helping characters. It was about using his imagination.

Ben couldn't wait for tomorrow's story. Maybe the dancing bear would need help learning new moves. Ben was already thinking of dance steps.

He skipped to the snack table, humming happily. Story time was magical after all.

The end.
