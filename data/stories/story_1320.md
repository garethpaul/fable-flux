# Story story_1320

**Tiger and Zoe**

Tiger sat alone in the sunny nursery corner. His orange and black stripes looked bright. But Tiger felt sad inside. He pushed his toy car back and forth. The wheels made soft rolling sounds on the carpet.

"Vroom, vroom," Tiger whispered quietly. He wished someone would play with him. The other children were busy with puzzles and blocks. Tiger's ears drooped down.

Across the room, Zoe watched Tiger. She had curly brown hair and kind eyes. Zoe saw Tiger playing by himself. She wanted to help him feel better.

Zoe walked over slowly. Her shoes made tiny tapping sounds. "Hi Tiger," she said with a warm smile. "Can I play cars too?"

Tiger looked up. His eyes grew wide and hopeful. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Yes!" Zoe said. She sat down next to Tiger. The soft carpet felt cozy under her legs. "I love cars. What color is yours?"

"It's red and shiny," Tiger said. He held up his favorite toy car. "See how the wheels spin?"

Zoe clapped her hands together. "Wow! It's beautiful. I have a blue truck. Should we make a road?"

Tiger's tail began to swish happily. "Yes! We can build a big road together."

The two friends used wooden blocks. They made a long, winding path across the carpet. Tiger placed each block carefully. Zoe helped make the road smooth and straight.

"Beep beep!" Zoe said as her blue truck drove along. "I'm going to the store."

"Wait for me!" Tiger laughed. His red car zoomed behind her truck. "We can go shopping together."

They drove their cars over the block road. The toys made clicking sounds on the wooden blocks. Tiger and Zoe giggled as they played. Their cars went up hills and around corners.

"Let's pretend there's a car wash," Zoe suggested. She found a soft blue cloth. "Our cars are getting dusty."

Tiger loved this idea. "Yes! My car needs to be clean and sparkly."

They gently wiped their toy cars. The cloth felt smooth and silky. Tiger's car gleamed in the sunlight from the window. Zoe's truck looked bright and new.

"Now they're perfect," Tiger said proudly. His whiskers twitched with joy.

Miss Sarah, their teacher, walked over. She smiled at the happy friends. "You two are playing so nicely together," she said.

"Tiger is really fun," Zoe told Miss Sarah. "He builds the best roads."

Tiger's chest puffed up with pride. "Zoe is the best friend ever. She shares everything."

Miss Sarah's eyes twinkled. "Would you like to show the other children your road?"

Tiger and Zoe looked at each other. They nodded eagerly. Soon, more children came over. Everyone wanted to drive cars on their special road.

"This is amazing!" said Ben, rolling his green car.

"Can you teach us to build roads too?" asked Emma.

Tiger felt warm and happy inside. He wasn't lonely anymore. Zoe had helped him make new friends. Together, they taught everyone how to build the perfect car road.

At snack time, Tiger and Zoe sat together. They shared apple slices and crackers. The fruit tasted sweet and crunchy.

"Thank you for playing with me," Tiger said softly.

"Thank you for sharing your cars," Zoe replied. "Friends always share."

Tiger's heart felt full of joy. He had learned something wonderful today. When you're kind to others, they're kind back. Playing together is much more fun than playing alone.

After snack, all the children played cars together. The nursery filled with happy laughter and zooming sounds. Tiger and Zoe smiled at each other. They had started something beautiful.

From that day on, Tiger and Zoe were best friends. They played together every day. Tiger never felt lonely again. He had learned that friendship begins with one simple word: "Hi."

And whenever a new child looked lonely, Tiger and Zoe always said, "Come play with us!" Because the best part of friendship is sharing it with everyone.

The end.
