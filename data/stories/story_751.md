# Story story_751

**Puzzle the Crab and the Green Problem**

Puzzle was a little orange crab who lived by the sunny beach. He had bright button eyes and tiny claws that clicked when he walked. Every morning, Puzzle scuttled across the warm sand looking for breakfast.

"Click, click, click," went his little feet on the shells.

Puzzle loved to eat many things. He munched on sweet berries. He nibbled salty seaweed chips. He even enjoyed crunchy fish crackers that washed up on shore. But there was one big problem.

Puzzle did not like vegetables at all.

"Yuck!" he would say when he saw green kelp. "No thank you!" he would cry when someone offered him sea lettuce. Even the pretty purple sea grapes made him scrunch up his nose.

One sunny day, Puzzle's tummy started to hurt. He felt tired and grumpy. His bright orange shell looked a little dull. His friend Shelly the seahorse swam over to visit.

"What's wrong, Puzzle?" asked Shelly in her gentle voice.

"I don't feel good," Puzzle said sadly. "My tummy hurts and I'm so tired."

Shelly looked at Puzzle with her kind eyes. "When did you last eat vegetables?" she asked.

Puzzle's claws drooped. "I never eat vegetables. They taste yucky."

"Oh dear," said Shelly. "Your body needs vegetables to stay healthy and strong. Let's find a way to help you."

Puzzle didn't want to feel sick anymore. He decided to try eating vegetables, even though he didn't like them.

First, Puzzle tried to eat plain sea lettuce. He picked up a green piece with his tiny claw. It smelled like the ocean. He took a small bite and made a face.

"Blech!" he said, spitting it out. "It tastes like slimy seaweed soup!"

The sea lettuce was too bitter and salty. Puzzle's first try didn't work at all. He felt even more frustrated.

"Don't give up," said Shelly kindly. "Let's try something different."

Shelly had a wonderful idea. "What if we mix the vegetables with foods you already love?" she suggested.

Puzzle's eyes got bigger. "Really? We can do that?"

"Of course!" said Shelly with a smile.

Together, they gathered sweet berries and crunchy nuts. Shelly helped Puzzle chop up small pieces of sea lettuce and kelp. They mixed everything together in a big shell bowl.

The mixture looked colorful and pretty. It had purple berries, brown nuts, and green vegetables all mixed up. It smelled sweet and nutty instead of just fishy.

Puzzle took a tiny bite. His eyes grew wide with surprise.

"This tastes good!" he said happily. "The berries are sweet and the nuts are crunchy. I can barely taste the vegetables!"

Puzzle ate the whole bowl. The vegetables didn't taste yucky anymore. They tasted like a delicious fruit salad with a little ocean flavor.

Every day after that, Puzzle made his special mixture. Sometimes he added different berries. Sometimes he used different nuts. He always included some vegetables.

Soon, Puzzle started feeling much better. His tummy stopped hurting. He had lots of energy to play. His orange shell became bright and shiny again.

"Look how healthy I am!" Puzzle said to Shelly one morning. He did a happy little dance on the sand.

"You figured out the perfect solution," said Shelly proudly. "You didn't give up when the first try didn't work."

Puzzle learned that sometimes solving problems takes more than one try. When something seems impossible, there might be a creative way to make it work. He also learned that taking care of his body was important.

Now Puzzle loves his colorful vegetable mixtures. He even tries new vegetables sometimes. And best of all, he feels happy and healthy every single day.

The warm sun shone down on Puzzle as he clicked across the beach, looking for his next adventure.

The end.
