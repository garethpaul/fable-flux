# The Forest Friends

In a sunny green forest, a little rabbit named Ruby sat alone by a babbling brook. She had found the perfect smooth stones for skipping. But playing by herself felt quiet and empty.

Ruby picked up a flat gray stone. She threw it across the water. *Plop!* It sank right away. She tried again with a rounder stone. *Splash!* That one sank too.

"This is harder than I thought," Ruby sighed. Her ears drooped down sadly. The forest felt very big when she was all alone.

Just then, footsteps crunched through the fallen leaves. A cheerful fox named Lily appeared through the trees. Her orange fur sparkled in the dappled sunlight.

"Hi there!" Lily called out. "What are you doing?"

Ruby looked up with hopeful eyes. "I'm trying to skip stones. But they keep sinking."

Lily's tail swished with excitement. "Oh, I love skipping stones! May I try with you?"

Ruby nodded eagerly. "Yes, please! I could use some help."

Lily picked up a thin, flat stone. She showed Ruby how to hold it. "See? Hold it like this. Then throw it sideways, not down."

Ruby watched carefully. Lily threw her stone. *Skip, skip, skip!* It bounced three times across the water.

"Wow!" Ruby clapped her paws. "Show me again!"

Lily found another perfect stone. This time, she guided Ruby's paw. "Feel how flat it is? Now throw it low and fast."

Ruby tried again. *Skip!* Her stone bounced once before sinking. "I did it!" she squealed with joy.

"You're getting it!" Lily cheered. "Let's find more good stones together."

The two friends searched along the rocky shore. Ruby found smooth ones. Lily found flat ones. They made a pile of perfect skipping stones.

"Look at all these!" Ruby said. "We found so many together."

"Want to have a contest?" Lily asked. "Let's see who can skip theirs the farthest."

Ruby's eyes sparkled. "Yes! That sounds fun!"

They took turns throwing stones. Ruby's went *skip, skip!* Lily's went *skip, skip, skip, skip!* They laughed and cheered for each other.

"You're so good at this," Ruby said. "I'm glad you came by."

"Me too," Lily replied. "Playing together is much more fun!"

Soon they discovered something else they both loved. Pretty wildflowers grew everywhere in the forest. Purple violets. Yellow buttercups. White daisies with sunny centers.

"Want to make flower crowns?" Lily suggested.

"I've never made one before," Ruby admitted.

"I'll teach you!" Lily said kindly. "It's easy when we work together."

Lily showed Ruby how to weave the stems. Ruby was good at finding the prettiest flowers. Lily was good at the tricky weaving part.

"You pick the colors," Lily said. "You have such a good eye!"

"And you make them stay together," Ruby added. "Your paws are so clever!"

They helped each other make beautiful crowns. Ruby's had purple and white flowers. Lily's had yellow and pink ones. They placed the crowns on their heads.

"We look like forest princesses!" Ruby giggled.

"The most beautiful ones ever!" Lily agreed.

As the sun began to set, the friends sat by the brook. Their flower crowns glowed in the golden light. The water sparkled like tiny diamonds.

"This was the best day," Ruby said softly. "I was so lonely before you came."

"I was walking alone too," Lily shared. "But now I have a wonderful friend."

Ruby's heart felt warm and full. "Want to meet here again tomorrow?"

"Yes!" Lily said. "We can skip more stones and make more flower crowns!"

The two friends hugged goodbye. Ruby hopped home through the trees. Lily trotted away with her bushy tail high.

Both friends fell asleep that night with big smiles. They dreamed of their magical forest and all the fun adventures waiting for them.

The next morning, they both woke up excited. Today they would play together again in their special forest home.

From that day on, Ruby and Lily were the very best of friends.

The end.
