# Story story_1085

**Papa's Magic Lunch**

The big clock on the kitchen wall said twelve. "Lunch time!" called Mama from the kitchen. But Papa was still in his chair. He was reading his newspaper. He did not want to stop.

"Papa, come eat!" said little Emma. She tugged on his shirt sleeve. Papa looked tired. His shoulders felt heavy. Making lunch seemed like too much work today.

"Maybe later," Papa said softly. He turned another page. The newspaper crinkled in his hands. Emma's face looked sad. She really wanted to eat lunch with Papa.

Emma had a big idea. She ran to the kitchen. She whispered something to Mama. Mama smiled and nodded. They began to work together.

First, Emma got the special red plates. The ones with dancing bears on them. She set them on the table very carefully. Each plate made a tiny clink sound.

Next, Mama helped Emma make sandwiches. They cut them into fun triangle shapes. The peanut butter smelled sweet and nutty. The jelly was bright purple grape.

Emma found the yellow napkins. She folded each one like a little tent. She put a napkin by every plate. The table looked so pretty now.

"Papa!" Emma called again. "We have a surprise!"

Papa looked up from his newspaper. Something smelled really good. His tummy made a little rumble sound. Maybe he was hungrier than he thought.

Papa walked slowly to the kitchen. His eyes grew wide. The table looked magical! The dancing bear plates sparkled in the sunlight. The triangle sandwiches looked like tiny flags.

"Wow!" said Papa. "This looks amazing!"

Emma giggled and clapped her hands. "Sit here, Papa!" She patted the chair next to hers. Papa sat down with a big smile.

Mama brought cold milk in tall glasses. The milk was white and creamy. She also brought orange slices. They looked like little sunshine pieces.

"Thank you for making lunch special," Papa said. He took a big bite of his sandwich. "Mmm! This tastes wonderful!"

Emma took a bite too. Crunch! The sandwich was perfect. Not too much peanut butter. Just the right amount of jelly.

They all ate together at the pretty table. Papa told funny jokes. Emma laughed so hard she almost spilled her milk. Mama smiled and ate her orange slices.

"The oranges taste like summer," said Papa. The juice was sweet on his tongue. Emma tried an orange slice too. She made a happy face.

After lunch, they all helped clean up. Papa washed the dancing bear plates. Emma dried them with a soft towel. Mama put everything away.

"That was the best lunch ever," said Papa. He gave Emma a big hug. She smelled like peanut butter and sunshine.

"Can we make lunch special again tomorrow?" asked Emma. Her eyes sparkled with hope.

"Yes!" said Papa. "But tomorrow, I want to help make it special too." He felt much better now. His shoulders did not feel heavy anymore.

That night, Papa thought about lunch time. He remembered Emma's happy face. He remembered the dancing bear plates. He remembered how good everything tasted when they ate together.

Papa smiled in the dark. Tomorrow he would not read his newspaper at lunch time. Tomorrow he would help make the table pretty. Maybe they could use the plates with the singing cats!

Emma was already planning too. She thought about what shapes to cut the sandwiches. Maybe stars! Or maybe hearts! Lunch time was going to be so much fun.

The next morning, Papa woke up early. He was excited about lunch time. He could not wait to see what magical things they would do together.

From that day on, lunch time became Papa's favorite time. Every day brought new surprises. Every day brought more smiles. And every day, Papa remembered that the best magic happens when families eat together.
