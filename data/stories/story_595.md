# Story story_595

**Luna and the Lost Red Ball**

Luna was a fluffy golden dog with floppy ears. She loved playing in the backyard every sunny day. Her favorite toy was a bright red rubber ball. It bounced high and squeaked when she bit it.

One morning, Luna ran outside to play. She looked left and right for her red ball. Where could it be? She sniffed around the grass with her wet black nose. She checked under the big oak tree. No red ball anywhere.

Luna felt sad and worried. Her tail drooped down low. She whimpered softly, "Woof, woof, where is my ball?"

Then Luna heard a familiar squeak from above. She looked up at the tall swing set. There it was! Her precious red ball was stuck high up in the metal bars. The wind must have blown it there yesterday.

Luna's tail started wagging again. She had found her ball! But how could she get it down? The swing set was much taller than Luna.

Luna tried her first plan. She jumped as high as she could. Her four paws left the ground. She stretched her neck up, up, up. But she was not tall enough. The red ball stayed stuck in the metal bars.

Luna landed back on the soft grass. She panted hard from jumping. Her pink tongue hung out of her mouth. This was harder than she thought.

Luna sat down and looked around the yard. She needed to think of something else. Her brown eyes spotted the wooden picnic table nearby. That gave her a bright idea!

Luna ran to the picnic table with quick steps. Her paws made soft tapping sounds on the wood. She climbed up onto the smooth wooden bench. Then she carefully stepped onto the table top.

From up there, Luna could see the whole backyard. She felt proud of herself for climbing so high. The red ball looked much closer now. But it was still too far away to reach.

Luna needed to be even higher. She looked at the swing set again. One of the swings hung down near the table. The thick rope looked strong enough to hold her.

Very carefully, Luna stepped from the table onto the swing seat. The swing moved back and forth gently. Luna felt a little scared, but she wanted her ball back.

She stood up on her back legs on the swing. Now she was tall enough! Luna stretched her front paws toward the red ball. She could almost touch it with her nose.

The ball was wedged tight between two metal bars. Luna pushed it gently with her paw. Push, push, push. The ball wiggled loose!

Down came the red ball, bouncing on the grass below. Luna barked with joy, "Woof, woof, hooray!" She carefully climbed down from the swing to the table, then jumped to the grass.

Luna picked up her beloved red ball in her mouth. It felt perfect between her teeth. She squeezed it gently, and it made that wonderful squeaking sound. She had missed that happy noise.

Luna's family came outside to see what the barking was about. "Good girl, Luna!" said her little girl owner. "Did you solve a problem all by yourself?"

Luna wagged her whole body with pride. She had not given up when jumping didn't work. She had tried a different way. Sometimes the first idea doesn't work, but that's okay.

Luna spent the rest of the morning playing with her red ball. She threw it up in the air and caught it. She rolled it around the yard with her nose. But she made sure to keep it away from the swing set!

As the sun got warmer, Luna lay down in the cool shade. Her red ball rested safely between her front paws. She felt happy and proud.

Luna had learned something important that day. When something seems too hard, don't give up. Try a different way. Think of new ideas. With patience and creativity, problems can be solved.

Luna closed her eyes for a nap, dreaming of bouncing red balls and successful adventures.

The end.
