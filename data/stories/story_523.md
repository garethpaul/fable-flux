# Emma and the Missing Red Ball

Emma loved playing in the big grassy field behind her house. The field was wide and green. Soft dandelions dotted the grass like tiny yellow suns. Emma's favorite thing was her bright red ball. It was round and bouncy and perfect for throwing.

"Catch, ball!" Emma called out happily. She threw her red ball high into the blue sky. The ball sailed up, up, up. Then it came down with a soft *thump* in the tall grass.

Emma ran to find her ball. She looked left. She looked right. But the red ball was nowhere to be seen!

"Oh no!" Emma said. Her bottom lip started to wobble. "Where did my ball go?"

The grass was much taller than Emma had noticed. It swayed gently in the warm breeze. Some grass was as tall as Emma's waist! Her precious red ball could be hiding anywhere.

Emma felt worried. But she took a deep breath. "I can find my ball," she told herself. "I just need to look carefully."

First, Emma got down on her hands and knees. She crawled through the grass like a little explorer. The grass tickled her arms. It smelled fresh and earthy. She pushed aside green blades with her small hands.

"Ball? Are you here?" she called softly. But she only found a fuzzy caterpillar and three smooth pebbles.

Emma stood up and brushed off her knees. She had another idea. Maybe she could see better from up high! Emma found a small hill nearby. She climbed to the top on her tippy-toes.

From up there, Emma could see the whole field. She shaded her eyes with her hand. She looked for anything red. She saw yellow dandelions. She saw green grass. She saw a brown rabbit hopping. But no red ball.

"Hmm," Emma said, tapping her chin. "What else can I try?"

Emma remembered something important. When she threw the ball, it had bounced. Maybe it bounced far away! She walked in a big circle around where she first threw it. Step by step, she made her circle bigger.

The sun felt warm on Emma's face. A gentle breeze made the grass dance. Birds chirped cheerful songs in the nearby trees. Emma kept walking and looking.

Suddenly, Emma heard a sound. *Boing, boing, boing.* It was a bouncing sound! Emma's heart jumped with excitement.

"That sounds like my ball!" she said.

Emma followed the bouncing sound. It led her to a small dip in the field. There, in the middle of some very tall grass, was her bright red ball! It was slowly bouncing all by itself in the wind.

"There you are!" Emma laughed with joy. She ran to her ball and scooped it up. The ball felt smooth and warm from the sun. Emma hugged it tight.

"I found you!" she said to her ball. "I didn't give up, and I found you!"

Emma felt proud of herself. Finding the ball had been hard work. But she had tried different ways to solve her problem. She had crawled low. She had climbed high. She had walked in circles. And most importantly, she had kept trying.

The little girl threw her red ball gently in the air. This time, she watched exactly where it landed. The ball bounced twice and rolled to a stop near her feet.

"Much better," Emma giggled.

As Emma played with her ball, she felt happy and confident. She had learned something important today. When something goes wrong, there are always different ways to fix it. You just have to keep trying and not give up.

The sun began to set, painting the sky soft orange and pink. Emma tucked her red ball safely under her arm. She walked home through the field, smiling all the way.

"Tomorrow," Emma said to herself, "I'll be even more careful. But if I lose my ball again, I know I can find it. I'm a good problem solver!"

And with that happy thought, Emma skipped home, ready for whatever adventures tomorrow might bring.

The end.
