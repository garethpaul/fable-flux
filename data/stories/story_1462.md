# Story story_1462

**Freddy Fox's Big Day**

Freddy Fox sat on his soft blue bed. Tomorrow was his first day at Forest School. His tummy felt funny and twisty. His orange ears drooped down low.

"I don't want to go," Freddy whispered to his teddy bear. The morning sun peeked through his window. Birds chirped happy songs outside. But Freddy felt scared.

Mama Fox knocked on his door. "Time for breakfast, sweetie," she called. Her voice was warm and gentle.

"I'm not hungry," Freddy said. He pulled his fluffy tail over his face. "My tummy hurts."

Mama Fox came in and sat beside him. The bed squeaked softly. "What's wrong, little one?"

"School is scary," Freddy said. "What if no one likes me? What if I can't find the bathroom? What if I forget my lunch?"

Mama Fox hugged him tight. She smelled like fresh pancakes and love. "Those are big worries for a little fox."

Freddy had an idea. "Maybe I can practice being sick!" He coughed loudly. "Cough, cough! See? I'm too sick for school."

He flopped back on his pillow. He made his voice sound weak. "I feel terrible, Mama."

Mama Fox smiled. "Nice try, Freddy. But your nose is cold and wet. That means you're healthy."

Freddy's first plan didn't work. He felt even more worried now.

After breakfast, Freddy tried again. He ran to his closet and hid behind his clothes. The hangers clinked together. His winter coat felt soft against his fur.

"If I hide here all day, I can't go to school," he thought.

Papa Fox found him twenty minutes later. "What are you doing in there, buddy?"

"I live here now," Freddy said. "Foxes belong in dark places."

Papa Fox chuckled. "Forest School has foxes, rabbits, and squirrels. You'll fit right in."

Freddy's second plan failed too. He felt silly hiding in his closet.

Mama Fox sat down with Freddy again. "Let's try something different," she said. "Tell me what scares you most."

Freddy thought hard. "Making friends is scary."

"What do you like to do?" Mama asked.

"I like to build with blocks. I like to draw pictures. I like to run fast."

Mama Fox's eyes twinkled. "Those sound like fun things to share with new friends."

She brought Freddy's art box to his bed. The crayons rattled inside. "Let's draw a picture of your perfect school day."

Freddy picked up a red crayon. He drew himself smiling big. He added a rabbit friend and a squirrel friend. They were building a tall block tower together.

"That looks wonderful," Mama said. "What else?"

Freddy drew a playground with swings. He drew a lunch table with yummy food. He drew a cozy reading corner with soft pillows.

As he colored, Freddy's tummy felt better. The twisty feeling went away. His ears perked up a little.

"Maybe school could be fun," he said quietly.

That evening, Freddy packed his backpack carefully. He put in his favorite pencil. He added a small toy car to share. He packed the picture he drew.

"I'm still a little nervous," Freddy told his parents.

"That's okay," Papa Fox said. "Being nervous means you care about doing well."

The next morning, Freddy's tummy had tiny butterflies. But he felt brave too. He wore his favorite red shirt. He ate his pancakes slowly.

At school, Freddy met Ruby Rabbit. She loved to build with blocks too. He met Sam Squirrel, who drew amazing pictures.

During lunch, Freddy showed his drawing. "This is what I hoped school would be like," he said.

"It's perfect!" said Ruby. "Want to build that tower after we eat?"

Freddy's tail wagged happily. His nervous feelings had turned into excited feelings.

That afternoon, Freddy ran to his parents. His eyes sparkled with joy.

"How was school?" Mama asked.

"Amazing! I made two new friends. We built the tallest block tower ever. And tomorrow we're going to paint pictures together!"

Freddy learned that trying new things feels scary at first. But when you're brave and keep trying, wonderful things can happen. Sometimes the things that scare us turn into the things we love most.

The end.
