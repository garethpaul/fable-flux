# Story story_706

**Grandma's Big Problem**

Grandma Rose loved playing in her backyard playroom. The sunny room had colorful toys everywhere. Soft blocks sat in neat piles. Puzzle pieces sparkled on the table. 

One morning, Grandma heard happy sounds. Children were laughing next door. Their voices floated over the tall wooden fence.

"I want to see them play," said Grandma Rose. She walked to the fence. She stood on her tippy toes. She stretched her neck up high.

But Grandma was too short. The fence was too tall. She could only see the brown wooden boards. She could hear the children, but she couldn't see them.

"Oh my," sighed Grandma Rose. "This fence is much too high for me."

The children's laughter made her smile. She heard them running around. She heard a ball bouncing. She heard them counting together.

"One, two, three, four, five!" they shouted.

Grandma Rose wanted to watch them have fun. She had an idea.

"I know what to do," she said. She went to her playroom. She picked up a small wooden stool. The stool was red with yellow flowers painted on top.

Grandma carried the stool outside. She placed it by the fence. She climbed up carefully. She held the fence with both hands.

Now she was a little taller. But the fence was still too high. She could see the tops of some trees. She could see a few clouds in the blue sky. But she still couldn't see the children playing.

"This stool is not tall enough," said Grandma Rose. She climbed down slowly. She sat on the stool and thought hard.

The children were playing a clapping game now. Their hands made soft slapping sounds. They were singing a happy song about butterflies.

Grandma Rose looked around her yard. She saw her garden. She saw pretty flowers and green plants. Then she noticed something special.

"My stepping stones!" she said with excitement.

Grandma had flat, round stones in her garden. She used them to walk between her flower beds. The stones were smooth and strong.

Grandma picked up two stepping stones. They felt cool and heavy in her hands. She carried them to the fence. She stacked one stone on top of the other.

Then she put her red stool on top of the stones. Now she had made herself much taller.

"This should work," said Grandma Rose.

She climbed up very carefully. First onto the stones. Then onto the stool. She held the fence tight with both hands.

Now Grandma Rose could see over the fence! Three children were playing in the yard next door. A little girl with curly hair was throwing a bright red ball. Two boys were trying to catch it.

"Hello there!" called Grandma Rose.

The children looked up and waved. "Hi, Grandma!" they called back.

"We're playing catch," said the little girl. "Do you want to play with us?"

"I would love to watch you play," said Grandma Rose. "You look like you're having so much fun!"

The children smiled big smiles. They threw the ball higher so Grandma could see it better. They did silly dances that made Grandma laugh.

Grandma Rose felt so happy. Her heart felt warm and full. She had solved her problem by trying different things.

After watching for a while, Grandma climbed down carefully. She put her stones back in the garden. She carried her stool to the playroom.

"Tomorrow I'll watch them play again," she said to herself.

That night, Grandma Rose felt proud. She had been too short to see over the fence. But she didn't give up. She tried one way, then another way. Finally, she found the perfect solution.

"When something seems too hard," said Grandma Rose, "I just need to keep trying new ideas."

From that day on, Grandma Rose watched the children play every morning. She always remembered that big problems sometimes need creative solutions. And the best part was making new friends who waved at her every single day.

The end.
