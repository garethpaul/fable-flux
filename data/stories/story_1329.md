# Story story_1329

**Leo the Lion and Tommy the Train**

Leo the Lion sat in the big bathroom. He felt very lonely today. The white tiles were cold under his paws. The bathroom was quiet and still.

Leo had his favorite yellow ball. He rolled it back and forth. The ball made soft sounds on the floor. But playing alone was not much fun.

"I wish I had a friend," Leo said sadly. His voice echoed in the empty room.

Just then, Leo heard a happy sound. "Choo-choo! Choo-choo!" 

Around the corner came Tommy the Train. Tommy was bright red and blue. His wheels clicked on the bathroom tiles. Steam puffed from his little smokestack.

"Hello there!" said Tommy with a big smile. "I saw you playing. Can I join you?"

Leo's ears perked up. His tail began to wag. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" Tommy said. "Playing together is always better!"

Leo picked up his yellow ball. "We can roll this back and forth!"

Tommy's whistle tooted with joy. "That sounds wonderful!"

Leo gently pushed the ball to Tommy. Tommy used his front bumper to push it back. The ball rolled smoothly across the clean floor.

"This is fun!" Leo laughed. His golden mane bounced as he moved.

"Choo-choo! I love this game!" Tommy puffed happily.

They played catch with the ball. Leo would bat it with his big paws. Tommy would push it with his nose. The bathroom filled with their happy sounds.

"Let's try something new," said Leo. "Want to play hide and seek?"

Tommy's bell rang with excitement. "Yes! I'll hide first!"

Tommy rolled behind the big white bathtub. Only his red caboose showed. Leo covered his eyes and counted.

"One, two, three! Ready or not!"

Leo pretended he couldn't see Tommy. He looked under the sink. He checked behind the toilet. Tommy giggled from his hiding spot.

"Where could Tommy be?" Leo wondered out loud.

Finally, Leo "found" Tommy behind the tub. "There you are!"

Tommy tooted his horn. "Your turn to hide!"

Leo squeezed behind the tall cabinet. His fluffy tail stuck out just a little. Tommy chugged around looking for him.

"Hmm, where is my friend Leo?" Tommy said playfully.

When Tommy found Leo, they both laughed together. The bathroom felt warm and happy now.

"I have an idea," said Tommy. "Let's make a parade!"

Leo's eyes sparkled. "How do we do that?"

"You walk in front. I'll follow behind. We'll march around the bathroom!"

Leo stood tall and proud. He walked slowly around the room. Tommy followed, puffing steam and ringing his bell. They marched past the mirror, around the bathtub, and by the sink.

"We make a great team!" Leo roared softly.

"The best team ever!" Tommy agreed.

They played until the sun started to set. The bathroom glowed with orange light from the window.

"This was the best day," Leo said. He felt warm and happy inside.

"I'm so glad we're friends," Tommy replied. His steam made little heart shapes in the air.

"Will you play with me again tomorrow?" Leo asked hopefully.

"Every day!" Tommy promised. "Friends always play together!"

Leo gave Tommy a gentle hug. Tommy's warm engine hummed with happiness.

From that day on, Leo was never lonely. Every morning, he would hear Tommy's cheerful "Choo-choo!" The bathroom became their special playground.

They played ball and hide and seek. They had parades and told stories. Sometimes they just sat together, enjoying each other's company.

Leo learned that friendship makes everything better. Playing alone was okay, but playing with a friend was magical.

And Tommy learned that the best adventures happen when you reach out to someone new.

The bathroom was no longer quiet and cold. It was filled with laughter, friendship, and joy. Leo and Tommy proved that friends can be found in the most unexpected places.

Their friendship grew stronger every single day.

The end.
