# Story story_1039

**Benny the Brave Bear**

In the dusty attic above Emma's bedroom lived a small brown teddy bear named Benny. Soft golden sunlight streamed through the round window. It made dancing dust sparkles in the air. Benny had been in the attic for many months. He missed playing with children very much.

One morning, Benny heard happy sounds from below. Emma was laughing and playing with her toys. "I wish I could play too," whispered Benny. He looked around the quiet attic. Old boxes sat everywhere. A rocking horse stood in the corner. Benny felt very lonely.

Benny walked to the attic door. It was closed tight. He pushed with his fuzzy paws. The door would not move. He pushed harder. Still nothing happened. "Oh no," said Benny sadly. "I am stuck up here."

Benny was very shy. He did not like to bother anyone. "Maybe I can fix this myself," he thought. Benny looked around for another way out. He saw the small round window. Bright sunshine poured through the glass.

Benny climbed onto an old wooden chair. He reached up to the window. His little paws could barely touch the glass. He pushed and pushed. The window was stuck tight. Benny's arms got very tired. He sat down on the dusty floor.

"This is too hard," Benny sighed. A tear rolled down his brown fuzzy cheek. He felt scared and alone. The attic seemed bigger and darker now. Benny curled up in a ball. He closed his eyes tight.

Then Benny heard Emma's voice again. She was singing a happy song. "Maybe Emma could help me," thought Benny. But his tummy felt nervous. "What if she thinks I am silly?" he worried.

Benny took a deep breath. He walked back to the attic door. This time he did something different. Instead of just pushing, Benny knocked on the door. Tap, tap, tap went his small paw. The sound was very quiet.

Benny knocked a little louder. Tap, tap, TAP! He waited and listened. Nothing happened. Emma was still singing downstairs. She could not hear the soft knocking.

Benny felt brave inside his fuzzy chest. He knocked much louder this time. THUMP, THUMP, THUMP! The door made a big noise. Benny knocked again and again. THUMP, THUMP, THUMP!

"What is that sound?" called Emma from downstairs. Her footsteps came closer. They went thump, thump, thump on the stairs. Emma was coming to the attic!

"Hello?" called Emma. She sounded curious and kind. "Is someone up there?"

Benny's heart beat fast. He was still shy. But he was also very excited. "Yes!" called Benny in his small voice. "I am Benny! I live in your attic!"

Emma opened the door slowly. Sunlight from the hallway made the attic brighter. Emma peeked inside with big surprised eyes. She saw Benny sitting by the door. His brown fur was covered in dust. His black button eyes looked hopeful.

"Oh my!" said Emma with a big smile. "A talking teddy bear! You must have been lonely up here." She bent down and picked up Benny gently. Her hands felt warm and soft.

"I was scared to ask for help," said Benny quietly. "I thought I could get out by myself."

Emma hugged Benny close to her heart. "It is always okay to ask for help," she said sweetly. "That is what friends are for."

Emma carried Benny down to her sunny bedroom. She gave him a warm bath in the sink. She brushed his fur until it was fluffy again. Benny felt clean and happy.

"Now you can play with me every day," said Emma. She set Benny on her bed with her other toys. "You will never be alone again."

Benny smiled his biggest smile. He learned something very important that day. Asking for help is not scary. It is brave. And sometimes the best adventures happen when you find the courage to speak up.

From that day on, Benny and Emma were the very best of friends.

The end.
