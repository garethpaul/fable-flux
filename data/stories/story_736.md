# Story story_736

**Thomas the Little Farm Train**

Thomas was a bright red train. He lived on Sunny Meadow Farm. Every day, he carried fresh vegetables. Carrots, corn, and green beans filled his cars.

But Thomas had a big problem. He didn't like eating vegetables at all! When Farmer Joe gave him carrot fuel, Thomas made yucky faces. "Blech!" he said. "I don't want carrots!"

The green bean fuel made him scrunch his nose. "Eww, no thank you!" Thomas puffed. The corn fuel made him shake his smokestack. "That's gross!" he cried.

Poor Thomas felt tired and slow. His engine made weak puff-puff sounds. His bright red paint looked dull. He could barely pull one vegetable car.

"Thomas, you need vegetable fuel to be strong," said Farmer Joe kindly. "Vegetables help trains grow big and powerful."

Thomas shook his head. "I only want cookie fuel!" he declared. "Cookies taste much better than vegetables!"

Farmer Joe scratched his head. He wanted to help Thomas feel better.

The next morning, Farmer Joe tried his first idea. He brought Thomas a big plate of chocolate chip cookies. "Here you go, Thomas," he said with a smile.

Thomas's eyes lit up like headlights. "Yay! Cookies!" he cheered. He gobbled up every single cookie. They tasted sweet and crunchy and wonderful.

Thomas felt happy for a little while. But soon, something felt wrong. His tummy started to hurt. His engine made funny gurgling sounds. "Glub, glub, glub," went his belly.

"Oh no!" Thomas groaned. "My tummy feels sick!" He tried to pull his vegetable cars. But he felt even weaker than before. His wheels moved very, very slowly.

Thomas could only travel halfway around the farm. Then he had to stop and rest. "I don't feel good," he whimpered to Farmer Joe.

Farmer Joe patted Thomas's side gently. "Cookies taste good," he said. "But they don't give trains the right energy. Let me try something else."

The next day, Farmer Joe had a new idea. He brought Thomas a small bowl. Inside were tiny pieces of carrots mixed with apple slices. The orange and red colors looked pretty together.

"Just try one bite," Farmer Joe said softly. "The apples will make the carrots taste sweeter."

Thomas looked at the bowl carefully. The apple pieces smelled fresh and fruity. Maybe this wouldn't be so bad. He took a tiny, careful bite.

"Hey!" Thomas said, surprised. "This tastes good!" The sweet apple flavor mixed with the carrot taste. It wasn't yucky at all!

Thomas ate another bite, then another. Soon, he finished the whole bowl. His engine started purring happily. "Purr, purr, purr," it hummed.

Something amazing happened next. Thomas felt energy flowing through his wheels. His red paint began to shine again. His smokestack puffed strong, white clouds.

"I feel fantastic!" Thomas exclaimed. He pulled all six vegetable cars easily. He zoomed around the farm three whole times without getting tired.

From that day on, Thomas ate his vegetable fuel happily. Farmer Joe always mixed in apple pieces to make everything taste better. Sometimes he added sweet corn or cherry tomatoes too.

Thomas learned that trying new foods was an adventure. He discovered that vegetables helped him be the best train he could be. His engine stayed strong, and his paint stayed bright and shiny.

Now Thomas loves helping on the farm. He carries vegetables to the market with pride. "Look at all these healthy foods!" he tells everyone. "They help me be strong and fast!"

Thomas still enjoys cookies sometimes as a special treat. But he knows that vegetables are the best fuel for growing trains. And mixing them with sweet fruits makes everything taste amazing.

"I'm so glad I kept trying," Thomas puffs happily as he chugs around the farm. "Now I'm the strongest little train in the whole wide world!"

The end.
