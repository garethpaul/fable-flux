# Story story_1076

Jack sat on his bedroom floor. He played with his toy cars. "Jack, it's garden time!" called Mom from downstairs.

Jack made a grumpy face. Garden time meant dirt. Garden time meant bugs. Garden time meant getting his hands messy. "Do I have to?" Jack called back.

"Yes, sweetie. Come help me plant the new seeds," Mom said. Her voice sounded happy and excited.

Jack walked slowly down the stairs. His feet felt heavy like rocks. He dragged his sneakers on each step. Thump, thump, thump.

Outside, the sun felt warm on Jack's face. The grass tickled his ankles. Mom knelt by a patch of dark, rich soil. She held a small packet of seeds.

"What are those?" Jack asked. He stayed far away from the dirt.

"Sunflower seeds," Mom said. "They grow into tall, beautiful flowers. Want to see?"

Jack took one tiny step closer. The seeds looked like little black teardrops. They felt smooth in his palm. "They're so small," he whispered.

"Small things can become big and amazing," Mom said. She smiled at Jack. "Will you help me plant them?"

Jack wrinkled his nose. "But my hands will get dirty."

"We can wash them after," Mom said gently. "Sometimes the best adventures start with dirty hands."

Jack knelt down beside Mom. The soil felt cool and crumbly. It smelled like rain and earth. Mom showed him how to make a small hole with his finger.

"Drop the seed in," Mom said. "Then cover it up like tucking it into bed."

Jack dropped his first seed. The tiny plop made him giggle. He covered it with soft dirt. His hands were getting brown and muddy.

"This one's mine," Jack said, patting the soil. "Grow big and strong, little seed."

As they planted more seeds, something wonderful happened. Jack heard a tiny chirping sound. A baby bird sat on the fence post. It had fluffy gray feathers and bright black eyes.

"Look, Mom!" Jack pointed. "A bird friend!"

The little bird tilted its head. It seemed to watch Jack plant each seed. When Jack moved to a new spot, the bird hopped along the fence. It chirped soft, sweet songs.

"I think it likes our garden," Mom said. "Birds know where good things grow."

Jack planted seed after seed. His hands were completely dirty now. But he didn't mind anymore. The bird kept singing. The sun warmed his back. The soil felt like soft, cool cake mix.

"Can I plant this whole row?" Jack asked. His eyes sparkled with excitement.

"Of course," Mom said. "You're becoming a real gardener."

Jack worked carefully. He made each hole the perfect size. He tucked each seed in gently. The bird watched and chirped encouragement.

When they finished, Jack looked at their work. The garden bed looked neat and tidy. Little markers showed where each seed slept underground.

"When will they grow?" Jack asked.

"In a few weeks," Mom said. "We'll water them every day. We'll watch for tiny green shoots."

Jack clapped his muddy hands together. Dirt flew everywhere. He laughed at the little brown clouds. "This was fun! Can we plant more tomorrow?"

Mom hugged Jack tight. "We can plant vegetables tomorrow. Maybe carrots and beans."

The baby bird chirped one more time. Then it flew to a nearby tree. Jack waved goodbye to his feathered friend.

Inside, Jack washed his hands with warm, soapy water. The dirt swirled down the drain. But his excitement stayed bright and strong.

At dinner, Jack told Dad about the seeds. He told him about the bird friend. He told him about tomorrow's planting plans.

"I love garden time now," Jack said. "Tomorrow I want to plant the biggest garden ever!"

Mom winked at Dad. "I think we created a little gardener today."

Jack smiled big. He couldn't wait for tomorrow. He couldn't wait to see what would grow. Garden time was the best time of all.

The end.
