# Story story_1021

**Freddy the Frog's Big Problem**

Freddy the frog lived in a cozy attic. He had soft blankets and warm sunlight. But Freddy had one big problem. He did not like baths at all.

Every morning, Mama Frog called up the stairs. "Freddy! Time for your bath!" Freddy would hide under his favorite blue blanket. He would cover his green head completely.

"I don't want a bath!" Freddy would croak loudly. "Baths are cold and scary!"

One sunny Tuesday morning, something terrible happened. Freddy looked in his little mirror. His beautiful green skin was covered in dust. Gray dust was everywhere on him. Even his bright yellow belly was dirty.

"Oh no!" Freddy gasped. He tried to brush the dust away. But it stuck to his skin like glue. The dust made him feel itchy and uncomfortable.

Freddy hopped to his window. He saw his friends playing outside. They looked so clean and shiny. Their skin sparkled in the warm sunshine.

"I need to get clean," Freddy said sadly. "But I really don't want a bath."

Freddy had his first big idea. He found an old feather duster. The feather was soft and tickly. Maybe he could dust himself clean!

Freddy rubbed the feather all over himself. He dusted his arms and legs. He even dusted his back carefully. The feathers felt nice and fluffy.

But when Freddy looked in the mirror again, he was still dusty. The feather just moved the dust around. Now the dust was in different places.

"That didn't work at all," Freddy sighed heavily.

Then Freddy heard his friend Lily outside. She was laughing and splashing happily. Freddy peeked out his small window.

Lily was playing in a beautiful pond. The water sparkled like tiny diamonds. She was jumping and diving gracefully. Her green skin looked so bright and clean.

"The water looks fun," Freddy whispered to himself. "Maybe baths aren't so scary after all."

Freddy took three deep breaths. He hopped slowly down the stairs. Each step felt very important.

"Mama," Freddy called in a small voice. "I think I'm ready now."

Mama Frog smiled her biggest smile. "Ready for what, my little one?"

"Ready for my bath," Freddy said bravely.

Mama filled the bathtub with warm water. She added bubbles that smelled like fresh flowers. The bubbles looked like fluffy white clouds.

Freddy touched the water with one toe. It felt warm and nice. He put his whole foot in next. The water felt like a cozy hug.

Slowly, Freddy slipped into the bathtub. The warm water covered his dusty skin. It felt wonderful and soothing.

"This is actually really nice!" Freddy laughed happily.

Mama helped him wash with gentle soap. The dust washed away completely. Freddy's skin became bright green again. His yellow belly sparkled like sunshine.

Freddy splashed and played in the bubbles. He made bubble hats and bubble beards. The bathroom filled with his happy laughter.

When bath time was over, Freddy felt amazing. His skin was soft and clean. He felt fresh and comfortable again.

"Thank you, Mama," Freddy said, giving her a big hug. "Baths are actually pretty wonderful."

From that day on, Freddy loved bath time. He learned that trying new things can be scary. But sometimes scary things turn out great.

Every morning, when Mama called for bath time, Freddy hopped right down. He would sing happy songs in the bubbles. His friends always complimented his shiny, clean skin.

Freddy learned an important lesson that day. Sometimes we have to try something twice. The first way might not work perfectly. But if we keep trying, we find better solutions.

Now Freddy was the cleanest, happiest frog in the whole attic. And he never hid from bath time again.

The end.
