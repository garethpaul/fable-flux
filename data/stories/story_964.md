# Story story_964

**Benny's Big Mess**

Benny was a fluffy golden dog. He lived in a sunny meadow. Green grass grew everywhere. Pretty flowers danced in the breeze.

Benny loved to play. He had red balls and blue ropes. He had squeaky toys and soft bears. Every day he scattered them around.

One morning, Benny woke up early. The sun felt warm on his fur. Birds sang sweet songs. He stretched and yawned.

"Time to play!" Benny barked happily.

But when he looked around, his eyes grew wide. Toys were everywhere! Red balls hid in tall grass. Blue ropes tangled around flower stems. His favorite squeaky bone sat in a muddy puddle.

"Oh no," Benny whimpered. "What a big mess!"

His friend Rabbit hopped over. Her white fur sparkled in the sunlight.

"Want to play fetch?" Rabbit asked cheerfully.

Benny's tail drooped low. "I can't find my good ball. Everything is so messy."

Rabbit's pink nose twitched. "Maybe you should clean up?"

Benny shook his head fast. "Cleaning is hard work. I don't want to."

But as the day went on, playing became impossible. Benny stepped on his squeaky mouse. It made a sad, broken sound. His rope toy was too dirty to carry. The mess made him feel grumpy.

"This isn't fun anymore," Benny sighed.

He decided to try something. Maybe the wind would help! Benny ran in big circles. He flapped his ears really hard. He hoped the wind would blow his toys together.

Whoosh! The wind picked up leaves. It scattered flower petals. But the toys stayed exactly where they were. Some moved even farther apart!

"That didn't work," Benny panted. His tongue hung out. He felt tired and frustrated.

Rabbit hopped back over. "How's the cleaning going?"

"It's not," Benny grumbled. "The wind made things worse!"

Rabbit's whiskers twitched thoughtfully. "Maybe try a different way?"

Benny sat down hard. He thought and thought. His tail swished in the soft grass. Then his eyes lit up bright.

"I have another idea!" he barked.

This time, Benny made it a game. He pretended to be a treasure hunter. Each toy was special treasure to find.

"I spy a red treasure!" Benny called out. He bounded toward his red ball. He picked it up gently in his mouth.

"Now for blue treasure!" He grabbed his rope toy. It felt good between his teeth.

One by one, Benny collected his toys. He made a neat pile under his favorite shady tree. The pile grew bigger and bigger.

"Look at all my treasure!" Benny wagged his tail proudly.

The squeaky bone needed washing. Benny carried it to the clear stream. He dipped it in the cool water. He shook off the mud. Soon it squeaked perfectly again.

As Benny worked, he discovered something wonderful. Cleaning could be fun! Finding each toy felt like an adventure. Making the pile neat felt good.

Finally, every single toy sat in the pile. They looked clean and bright. The meadow looked beautiful again. Green grass waved freely. Flowers stood tall and proud.

"All done!" Benny barked happily.

Rabbit hopped over with big eyes. "Wow! Everything looks amazing!"

Benny's chest puffed out with pride. "I thought cleaning would be awful. But it was actually fun!"

"Now we can really play," Rabbit said with a smile.

Benny picked up his clean red ball. He threw it high in the air. It sailed over the pretty flowers. Rabbit chased after it, giggling.

They played all afternoon. The toys stayed neat and clean. Playing felt so much better now.

As the sun began to set, Benny looked around his tidy meadow. Orange light painted everything golden. His toys sat in their special spot.

"Tomorrow I'll put toys away after playing," Benny decided. "That way cleanup will be easy!"

He curled up under his favorite tree. The grass felt soft and cool. Stars began to twinkle overhead.

Benny had learned something important. Hard jobs could become fun with the right attitude. And keeping things tidy made everything better.

With a happy sigh, Benny closed his eyes and dreamed of tomorrow's adventures.

The end.
