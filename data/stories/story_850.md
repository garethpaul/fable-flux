# Story story_850

**Lily's Big Toy Problem**

Lily lived in a cozy house on Sunshine Hill. The hill was steep and green. Pretty flowers grew everywhere. Lily loved her house, but she had one big problem.

Every morning, Lily wanted to play outside. She had so many wonderful toys! There were bouncy balls and soft dolls. She had colorful blocks and toy cars. She had jump ropes and stuffed animals too.

Lily would pile all her toys in her arms. She would try to carry them down the hill. But the toys were heavy and wiggly. 

"Oh no!" Lily would say. The toys would tumble and roll. They bounced down the hill without her. Some toys got stuck in bushes. Others rolled into the tall grass.

Lily felt sad and frustrated. Her bottom lip started to wobble. "I just want to play with all my toys," she whispered.

One sunny Tuesday morning, Lily had an idea. "I know! I'll stuff them in my backpack!"

She opened her small purple backpack. She pushed in her teddy bear. She squeezed in three balls. She tried to fit her toy truck too.

The backpack bulged and stretched. The zipper made angry sounds. "Come on, zipper!" Lily said. She pulled and tugged.

RIIIIIP! The backpack tore open. All the toys spilled out again. They scattered across her bedroom floor.

Lily sat down with a big sigh. A tear rolled down her cheek. "This is too hard," she said.

Her big sister Emma heard the noise. Emma peeked into the room. "What's wrong, Lily?"

"I can't carry all my toys outside," Lily sniffled. "They always fall down the hill."

Emma sat beside her little sister. "Hmm," she said thoughtfully. "What if we think of a different way?"

Emma looked around the room. She spotted Lily's red wagon in the corner. The wagon had four strong wheels. It had tall wooden sides too.

"Look, Lily!" Emma pointed. "What about your wagon?"

Lily's eyes grew wide and bright. "My wagon! I forgot about my wagon!"

The sisters worked together. They carefully placed each toy in the wagon. The teddy bear sat safely inside. The balls couldn't roll away. Even the biggest toys fit perfectly.

"Now you can pull your wagon down the hill," Emma said with a smile.

Lily grabbed the wagon handle. It felt smooth and strong in her hands. She pulled gently. The wagon rolled easily on its wheels.

Down the hill they went. The wagon bumped softly on the grass. All the toys stayed safe inside. Not one toy fell out!

"It's working!" Lily giggled with joy. "All my toys are coming with me!"

At the bottom of the hill, Lily found the perfect play spot. She carefully took out each toy. The sun felt warm on her face. The grass smelled fresh and sweet.

Lily played happily all morning. She built block towers. She had tea parties with her dolls. She threw balls and drove toy cars.

When playtime was over, Lily loaded everything back up. The wagon made it easy to bring everything home. Up the hill they went, all together.

"Thank you for helping me, Emma," Lily said. She gave her sister a big, squeezy hug.

"You didn't give up," Emma said proudly. "You kept trying until you found the answer."

That night, Lily felt proud and happy. She had learned something important. When one way doesn't work, try another way. There's always a solution if you keep thinking.

From that day on, Lily and her red wagon were best friends. Every morning, they would roll down Sunshine Hill together. All of Lily's toys came along for the adventure.

And Lily never had to leave a single toy behind again.

The end.
