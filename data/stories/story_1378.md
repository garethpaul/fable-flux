# Story story_1378

**Benny's Big Problem**

Benny was a bright red bike. He lived in the sunny sandbox. Every day, children played with him there.

Benny loved to help carry toys. He had a small basket on his front. The basket was yellow like sunshine.

One morning, Benny looked around the sandbox. There were so many toys everywhere! A blue shovel lay in the corner. Three colorful buckets sat by the fence. Five toy cars were scattered about. A purple ball rolled near the swings.

"Oh my!" said Benny. "Look at all these toys!"

The children had left them out overnight. Now they needed to go back to the toy box. The toy box sat far across the playground.

Benny wanted to help. He rolled over to the blue shovel first. He picked it up with his handlebars. Then he tried to grab a bucket too.

CLANG! The shovel fell into the sand.

"This is harder than I thought," Benny said sadly.

He tried again. This time, he put the shovel in his little basket. Then he pushed a bucket with his front wheel. The bucket rolled and bumped along.

But when Benny tried to pick up a toy car, everything fell down! The shovel tumbled out. The bucket tipped over. Sand flew everywhere.

"I can't carry all these toys," Benny sighed. His red paint felt hot from trying so hard.

A gentle breeze blew through the sandbox. It made a soft whooshing sound. Benny felt the cool air on his handlebars.

"Maybe I need to think differently," he said to himself.

Benny looked at all the toys again. He counted them slowly. "One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine toys total."

Then Benny had an idea! His wheels started to spin with excitement.

"I don't have to carry them all at once!" he said happily.

Benny made his first trip. He put the blue shovel in his basket. He pushed one bucket with his front wheel. Slowly and carefully, he rolled across the playground.

The morning sun warmed his back. Birds chirped in the trees above. His wheels made a pleasant crunching sound on the gravel path.

He reached the toy box and gently placed the toys inside.

"One trip down!" Benny said proudly.

Back to the sandbox he went. This time, he took two more buckets. He balanced them carefully. One went in his basket. He pushed the other with his wheel.

The second trip was just as successful!

On his third trip, Benny carried three toy cars. They fit perfectly in his basket. They clinked together softly as he rode.

For his fourth trip, he took the remaining cars and the purple ball. The ball was squishy and fun to push. It bounced gently as they traveled together.

Finally, all the toys were safe in the toy box!

Benny felt wonderful. His red paint seemed to shine brighter. His yellow basket looked cheerful in the sunlight.

"I did it!" he called out happily. "I carried all the toys!"

Just then, the children arrived for morning playtime.

"Look!" said Emma, a girl with curly hair. "Someone put all our toys away!"

"The playground looks so neat and tidy," said Sam, her little brother.

The children began to play. They rode Benny around the sandbox. They filled his basket with sand and flowers. Everyone was happy.

That evening, as the sun set orange and pink, Benny thought about his day.

"I learned something important today," he said to himself. "Big problems can be solved with small steps. I don't have to do everything at once."

A wise old owl hooted softly from a nearby tree. The stars began to twinkle overhead. The sandbox felt peaceful and calm.

Benny was proud of himself. He had worked hard and never given up. Tomorrow would bring new adventures and new ways to help.

"Good night, playground," whispered Benny. "Tomorrow I'll be ready for anything!"

And with that happy thought, the little red bike settled in for a peaceful night's rest, dreaming of all the wonderful ways he could help his friends.

The end.
