# Bounce the Brave Little Ball

In the middle of Sunny Park Playground sat a bright red ball named Bounce. He was perfectly round and shiny. His red color sparkled in the morning sun.

Bounce loved to roll and bounce and play. But today, something was wrong. The playground was very quiet. No children were running around. No laughter filled the air.

"Where is everyone?" Bounce wondered sadly. He looked left and right. He looked up and down. The swings were empty. The slide was lonely too.

Bounce felt very sad. His bright red color seemed less shiny. "I have no one to play with," he sighed. A tear rolled down his round face.

But Bounce was a brave little ball. He didn't want to give up. "I will find someone to play with!" he said with determination.

First, Bounce rolled over to the sandbox. Maybe the sandbox toys wanted to play! He bounced up and down excitedly.

"Hello, bucket!" Bounce called out. "Want to play catch?"

The yellow bucket just sat there. It couldn't move or talk. Bounce felt disappointed but didn't give up.

Next, Bounce rolled to the monkey bars. The metal bars gleamed in the sunshine. "Hello, monkey bars!" Bounce said hopefully. "Want to play bounce-ball?"

The monkey bars stayed perfectly still. They couldn't play either. Bounce's shoulders drooped a little.

Then Bounce spotted the seesaw. It could move up and down! "Perfect!" Bounce thought. He rolled over quickly.

"Hello, seesaw!" Bounce called out cheerfully. "Let's play together!"

Bounce climbed onto one end. The seesaw tipped down with a loud THUNK. But it wasn't fun alone. Bounce needed a friend on the other side.

Bounce was starting to feel really sad. He had tried so hard! But nobody could play with him.

Just then, Bounce heard something wonderful. Footsteps! Happy voices! Children were coming to the playground!

"Look!" shouted a little girl with curly hair. "A red ball!"

"Can we play with it?" asked a boy with a big smile.

Bounce's heart jumped with joy. His red color became bright and shiny again. Finally! Friends to play with!

The children picked up Bounce gently. "Let's play catch!" said the girl named Emma.

"Yes!" agreed the boy named Sam. "Then we can play kickball!"

Bounce felt so happy he could burst! The children threw him back and forth. He sailed through the air like he was flying. The wind tickled his round surface.

Then they played kickball. Bounce rolled fast across the grass. The soft green blades brushed against him. He loved the feeling!

Next, they played bounce-catch. Bounce went up, up, up into the blue sky. Then down, down, down into waiting hands. Each bounce made him giggle inside.

More children came to play. Soon, Bounce had five new friends! They laughed and played together all morning long.

"You're the best ball ever!" Emma said, hugging Bounce tight.

"We love playing with you!" added Sam.

Bounce felt warm and happy inside. His red color glowed brighter than ever before.

As the sun climbed higher, the children had to go home for lunch. But they promised to come back.

"We'll play again tomorrow!" they called out, waving goodbye.

Bounce sat in his favorite spot on the playground. He wasn't sad anymore. He felt proud and happy.

"I didn't give up," Bounce said to himself. "I kept trying until I found friends!"

The afternoon sun warmed Bounce's red surface. He thought about his wonderful morning. Even when things seemed impossible, he had stayed hopeful.

Bounce learned something very important that day. Sometimes you have to wait for good things to happen. Sometimes you have to keep trying different ways. But if you don't give up, wonderful surprises can come your way!

As Bounce settled down for a little nap, he smiled. Tomorrow would bring more friends and more fun. He couldn't wait to bounce and play again!

The playground was quiet now, but Bounce wasn't lonely anymore. He knew his friends would return. And he would be ready to play!

The end.
