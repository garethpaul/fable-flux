# Story story_1227

Anna sat on the big red swing. Her feet dangled in the air. The swing moved back and forth slowly.

The playground was quiet today. Other children played far away on the slides. Anna wished she had a friend to play with.

She pushed her feet against the ground. The swing went higher. The wind touched her face gently. It felt cool and nice.

"Whoosh!" went the swing as Anna moved. Her long hair flew behind her like a flag.

But swinging alone wasn't much fun. Anna looked around the empty playground. She sighed softly.

Just then, a boy walked over. He had curly brown hair and a bright blue shirt. His shoes made soft sounds on the mulch.

"Hi," said the boy. "I'm Max. Can I swing too?"

Anna smiled. "Yes! I'm Anna. This swing is empty." She pointed to the yellow swing next to hers.

Max climbed onto the yellow swing. His feet barely touched the ground. "I love swings," he said happily.

"Me too!" said Anna. "Let's see who can swing higher!"

Both children pushed off with their feet. Up and down they went. The chains made creaky sounds above them.

"Look how high I am!" called Max. His swing went up toward the clouds.

"I'm even higher!" laughed Anna. Her tummy felt funny as she swung up and down.

The two swings moved like dancers. Sometimes Anna was high while Max was low. Sometimes they swung up together.

"Let's count!" suggested Max. "One, two, three!"

"Four, five, six!" Anna counted back. They giggled as they swung.

The sun felt warm on their faces. Birds chirped in the nearby trees. The playground smelled like fresh grass and flowers.

"Want to try something fun?" asked Anna. "We can swing and sing!"

Max nodded excitedly. "What song?"

"Twinkle, Twinkle, Little Star!" said Anna.

Together they sang as they swung. "Twinkle, twinkle, little star!" Their voices mixed with the wind.

Other children heard the singing. They came over to watch. Soon a small crowd gathered around the swings.

"You two look like you're having fun," said a girl with pigtails.

"We are!" said Max. "Swinging with a friend is the best!"

Anna felt warm and happy inside. Making a new friend felt wonderful. She had been lonely, but now she felt joyful.

"Want to play a game?" asked Anna. "We can see who can jump the farthest!"

"Great idea!" said Max. "On the count of three?"

They slowed their swings down a little. "One... two... three!" they shouted together.

Both children jumped from their swings. They landed in the soft mulch with quiet thuds. Anna's jump was a little longer.

"Good jump!" said Max. "You're really good at this!"

"Thanks!" said Anna. "You jumped far too!"

They brushed the mulch off their clothes. Their faces were red from swinging and laughing.

"I'm thirsty," said Max. "Want to get some water?"

"Yes!" said Anna. "Then we can come back and swing more!"

They walked to the water fountain together. The cool water tasted refreshing after all their playing.

"This is the best day ever," said Anna. "I was sad before because I was alone."

"Now you're not alone," said Max kindly. "We can be swing partners!"

"Swing partners!" repeated Anna. "I like that name!"

They walked back to their swings hand in hand. The red and yellow swings waited for them like old friends.

"Same time tomorrow?" asked Max hopefully.

"Yes!" said Anna. "I'll save the red swing for you if you're late."

"And I'll save the yellow one for you!" promised Max.

They gave each other a high five. Their hands made a happy clapping sound.

Anna wasn't lonely anymore. She had found a wonderful friend. Tomorrow would bring more swinging, more laughing, and more fun together.

The two new friends swung side by side as the afternoon sun shone down on them. Their friendship had begun with a simple swing set, but it would last much longer than just one day.

The end.
