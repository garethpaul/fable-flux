# The Drum and the Little Blue Bird

By the quiet pond, a small red drum sat all alone. The drum loved to make music. *Boom, boom, tap!* went the drum. But nobody was around to hear the beautiful sounds.

The drum felt very lonely. "I wish I had a friend to play with," sighed the drum. The water in the pond rippled softly. The tall grass swayed in the breeze. But still, the drum was all alone.

Just then, a little blue bird flew down from the sky. The bird had bright yellow feet and sparkly black eyes. She landed on a rock near the water's edge.

"What a lovely sound!" chirped the bird. "Are you making music?"

The drum perked up with excitement. "Yes! I love to play music. But it's not much fun alone."

The little bird tilted her head. "May I try making music with you?"

"Really?" asked the drum hopefully. "How would you make music?"

The bird hopped closer. "I can tap my feet on your top! And I can sing beautiful songs!"

The drum's face lit up with joy. "That sounds wonderful! Let's try it together!"

The bird flew up and landed gently on the drum. *Tap, tap, tap* went her tiny yellow feet. The drum joined in with a deep *boom, boom!*

"This is amazing!" laughed the bird. She began to sing a sweet melody. "La, la, la, tweet, tweet!"

The drum kept the beat steady and strong. *Boom, tap, boom, tap!* The music echoed across the peaceful pond.

A family of frogs heard the music. They began to croak along. *Ribbit, ribbit!* Even the fish in the pond splashed their tails to the rhythm.

"Look!" said the bird excitedly. "Everyone loves our music!"

The drum beamed with happiness. "We make such a good team! Your singing makes my drumming sound even better."

"And your steady beat helps me sing my best songs!" replied the bird.

They played song after song together. Sometimes the music was fast and bouncy. Sometimes it was slow and gentle. Each song was more beautiful than the last.

As the sun began to set, painting the sky pink and orange, the bird and drum took a rest.

"I was so lonely before you came," said the drum. "Making music alone wasn't nearly as fun."

The bird nodded her small head. "I love singing by myself, but singing with you is much more special!"

"Will you come back tomorrow?" asked the drum hopefully.

"Of course!" chirped the bird. "We're friends now! Friends always play together."

The drum felt warm and happy inside. "I've never had a friend before. This is the best day ever!"

The bird spread one wing and gave the drum a gentle hug. "You're my first drum friend too! We can make beautiful music every day."

As the stars began to twinkle in the darkening sky, the bird sang one last soft lullaby. The drum provided a quiet, gentle rhythm. The pond grew peaceful and still.

"Good night, my friend," whispered the bird.

"Good night," replied the drum softly. "Thank you for making today so special."

The bird tucked her head under her wing to sleep on a nearby branch. The drum sat contentedly by the pond, no longer feeling lonely.

The next morning, the bird woke up early. She flew down to greet her friend with a cheerful song. The drum joined in immediately with happy beats.

Their music filled the air around the pond. Other animals came to listen and dance. The frog family clapped their webbed feet. A butterfly danced in circles above them.

"Making music together is the most wonderful thing in the world!" sang the bird.

"Yes!" agreed the drum joyfully. "Everything is better when you have a good friend!"

And from that day on, the drum and the little blue bird made beautiful music together every single day. They had learned that cooperation and friendship make everything more fun and special.

Their happy songs could be heard across the pond, bringing joy to all the creatures who lived nearby.

The end.
