# Finley's Lost Doll

Finley the little fish loved visiting the park. Today she wore her favorite sparkly blue scales. She carried her pretty doll, Rosie, everywhere she went.

"Come on, Rosie!" Finley giggled. "Let's go play!"

The park pond sparkled in the sunshine. Green lily pads floated like little islands. Colorful flowers grew all around the water's edge.

Finley swam to her favorite spot. She placed Rosie carefully on a smooth rock. "Wait here, Rosie. I'll be right back!"

Finley dove down deep. She played with the wiggly tadpoles. She chased bubbles through the water. The cool water felt wonderful on her fins.

When Finley swam back up, her heart sank. The rock was empty! Rosie was gone!

"Oh no!" Finley cried. "Where is my doll?"

Tears made tiny ripples in the water. Finley looked left and right. She looked up and down. No Rosie anywhere!

"I have to find her," Finley said bravely. "Rosie needs me!"

First, Finley searched under the lily pads. She pushed each green leaf aside. Water drops rolled off like tiny pearls. But no Rosie hiding there.

Next, Finley swam to the reedy grass. The tall green stalks swayed gently. She looked between each one carefully. Still no Rosie anywhere.

Finley felt worried. Her little heart beat fast. But she didn't give up.

"Maybe Rosie fell deeper," she thought.

Down, down, down Finley swam. The water grew darker and cooler. She searched behind every rock. She looked in every shadowy corner.

"Rosie! Rosie!" she called softly.

But only her echo answered back.

Finley swam back up feeling sad. Then she had another idea!

"Maybe someone else saw Rosie!"

Finley found Mrs. Turtle sunning herself. "Have you seen my pretty doll?" Finley asked politely.

Mrs. Turtle blinked her wise eyes. "No, dear. But keep looking!"

Finley asked the jumping frogs. "Have you seen Rosie?"

"Ribbit! No, sorry!" they croaked together.

She asked the busy water bugs. They skated across the surface. "We haven't seen any dolls today!"

Finley felt like crying again. But she took a deep breath. "I won't give up," she whispered.

Then Finley heard a tiny voice. "Help! Help me!"

The voice was very, very quiet. Finley listened carefully. There it was again!

"Help! I'm stuck!"

Finley followed the small voice. It led her to the water fountain. Pretty water sprayed up and down. Behind the fountain, something pink was trapped!

"Rosie!" Finley gasped with joy.

Her pretty doll was caught in some floating leaves. The water current had carried her there. Poor Rosie bobbed up and down.

"Don't worry, Rosie! I'm coming!"

Finley swam fast to the fountain. The water pushed and pulled strongly. But Finley was determined. She pushed through the swirling water.

Carefully, Finley freed Rosie from the leaves. She hugged her doll tight. "I found you! I found you!"

Rosie's pink dress was a little wet. But she was perfectly safe and sound.

"Thank you for not giving up!" Rosie seemed to say.

Finley swam back to their special rock. This time, she held Rosie close. The warm sunshine made them both feel cozy.

"I was so scared," Finley told her doll. "But I kept trying and trying!"

Mrs. Turtle swam over slowly. "You did wonderfully, Finley. You never stopped looking!"

The frogs hopped closer too. "Ribbit! You were so brave!"

All the pond friends gathered around. They were so proud of little Finley.

"When something important is lost," Mrs. Turtle said wisely, "we must keep searching. Good things happen to those who don't give up!"

Finley smiled the biggest smile ever. She had learned something special today. When problems seem big and scary, trying different ways helps. Being brave and persistent pays off.

As the sun set, Finley and Rosie headed home. The water glowed golden and warm. Finley felt proud and happy.

"Tomorrow we'll play again," she told Rosie. "And I'll take extra good care of you!"

The little fish had discovered something wonderful. With courage and persistence, lost things can be found. And the joy of finding them feels absolutely magical.

The end.
