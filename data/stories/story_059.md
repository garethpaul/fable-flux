# Zoe's Magical Snack Time

The kitchen clock chimed three times. "Snack time, Zoe!" called Mama from downstairs.

Zoe sighed from her bedroom floor. She was building a tall tower with blocks. Red ones, blue ones, yellow ones too. The tower was almost done!

"Do I have to?" Zoe asked. She didn't want to stop playing. Snack time seemed so ordinary. So boring.

Mama appeared in the doorway. She wore her sunny yellow apron. "Your tummy needs fuel for more building," she said with a smile.

Zoe's tummy did feel a little rumbly. She left her blocks and trudged downstairs. Her feet felt heavy on each step.

In the kitchen, Mama had set out a small plate. It was Zoe's favorite blue one with dancing stars. But today, something looked different.

"What's that?" Zoe asked, pointing at her plate.

Instead of regular crackers, there were tiny squares. Golden squares that sparkled in the sunlight. Next to them sat a small bowl of bright orange cheese. The cheese looked like sunshine in a bowl.

"Magic crackers," Mama whispered. "They only appear for special girls."

Zoe giggled. "Magic crackers aren't real, Mama."

"Are you sure?" Mama asked. She picked up one cracker. "Listen carefully."

Zoe leaned closer. *Crunch, crunch, crunch.* The cracker made tiny music! Like little bells ringing.

"My turn!" Zoe said. She bit into a golden square. *Crunch, crunch, crunch.* It did sound like music!

"Now try the sunshine cheese," Mama suggested.

Zoe dipped her cracker carefully. The cheese was warm and creamy. It tasted like happiness on her tongue. Sweet and salty together.

"It's like eating a cloud!" Zoe exclaimed.

Mama sat down beside her. "What else do you notice?"

Zoe looked around the kitchen. Sunbeams danced through the window. They made rainbow patterns on the wall. The flowers on the table smelled like spring. Everything seemed brighter somehow.

"The whole kitchen is magical," Zoe whispered.

"It was always magical," Mama said softly. "You just noticed it today."

Zoe ate another cracker. *Crunch, crunch, crunch.* She watched the sunbeams dance. She smelled the sweet flowers. She felt the smooth cheese on her fingers.

"Mama, why does everything feel special now?"

"Because you're paying attention," Mama explained. "Magic happens when we really look and listen."

Zoe nodded. She understood now. She took tiny bites to make it last. Each crunch sounded like a celebration. Each taste felt like a small party.

"Look!" Zoe pointed outside the window. A red cardinal sat on the bird feeder. His feathers gleamed like jewels.

"He's having snack time too," Mama observed.

Zoe giggled. "Bird crackers must taste different."

They watched the cardinal eat. He looked so happy pecking at seeds. His little head bobbed up and down.

"Mama, can we have magic crackers tomorrow?"

"Every snack can be magical," Mama said. "If you bring your noticing eyes."

Zoe finished her last cracker. Her tummy felt happy and full. But more than that, her heart felt warm. Snack time wasn't boring at all. It was wonderful!

"I want to go tell my blocks about magic crackers," Zoe announced.

"Good idea," Mama agreed. "They've been waiting patiently."

Zoe hugged Mama tight. "Thank you for showing me the magic."

"Thank you for being ready to see it," Mama replied.

Zoe skipped back upstairs. Her feet felt light now. Like she was floating on air. Her tower of blocks looked even more beautiful. The colors seemed brighter than before.

As she added the final block, Zoe smiled. Tomorrow's snack time would be another adventure. Maybe she'd notice different things. Maybe she'd hear new sounds.

"I can't wait," she whispered to her blocks.

The tower stood tall and proud. Just like Zoe felt inside. Magic was everywhere when you knew how to look.

And the best part? Tomorrow there would be more magic waiting. Right there in the kitchen. Right there at snack time.

Zoe couldn't wait to discover what tomorrow would bring.

The end.
