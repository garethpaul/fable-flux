# Story story_997

Grandpa's Lost Red Ball

Grandpa Joe loved his garden. Every morning, he watered his tomatoes. He picked weeds from his flower beds. The sun felt warm on his face.

Today was special. His granddaughter Emma was coming to visit. She always brought her favorite red ball. They would play catch together.

Grandpa looked around his garden. Where was Emma's ball? Yesterday, they had played until sunset. The ball was bright red and bouncy. Emma loved that ball more than anything.

"Oh no!" said Grandpa Joe. He scratched his gray head. The ball was nowhere to be seen.

He looked under the rose bushes. The thorns pricked his fingers. He moved aside the big sunflower leaves. Still no red ball.

Grandpa checked behind the garden shed. He found an old watering can. He found his muddy boots. But no red ball anywhere.

The morning sun climbed higher. Emma would arrive soon. Grandpa's heart felt heavy. How could he tell Emma her ball was lost?

He decided to try his first plan. Grandpa got his long garden rake. Maybe the ball rolled under the vegetable patch. The rake was wooden and smooth in his hands.

He pushed the rake under the tomato plants. Scritch, scratch went the metal teeth. He felt around carefully. The dirt was cool and damp.

"Come on, little ball," whispered Grandpa. "Where are you hiding?"

The rake hit something round and hard. Grandpa's eyes lit up! He pulled with the rake. Out rolled a big brown potato. Not the red ball.

Grandpa sighed. His first plan didn't work. But he wouldn't give up. Emma needed her ball back.

He looked up at the tall oak tree. Its branches stretched over the garden. Maybe the ball was stuck up there. Yesterday had been windy.

Grandpa got his sturdy ladder. It was blue and felt cold under his hands. Step by step, he climbed up high. The leaves rustled in the breeze.

He pushed apart the green branches. A bird chirped at him. "Sorry, little bird," said Grandpa softly. "I'm looking for something special."

Higher and higher he looked. The leaves tickled his face. He could smell the fresh morning air. Then, wedged between two thick branches, he saw it!

The bright red ball sat perfectly still. It looked like a giant cherry in the tree. Grandpa smiled so wide his cheeks hurt.

"There you are!" he said happily.

Carefully, Grandpa reached for the ball. His fingers touched the smooth rubber. He pulled gently. The ball popped free!

Down the ladder he climbed. The red ball felt perfect in his hands. It was just as bouncy as before.

Just then, he heard a car door slam. Emma was here! Her footsteps ran up the garden path.

"Grandpa! Grandpa!" called Emma. Her voice sounded worried. "I can't find my ball anywhere!"

Grandpa Joe grinned. He held the red ball behind his back. Emma's face looked sad and scared.

"Don't worry, sweet pea," said Grandpa. "Sometimes things get lost. But we never stop looking."

He brought the ball out from behind his back. Emma's eyes grew as big as saucers. Her mouth made a perfect O shape.

"My ball!" she squealed with joy. She hugged Grandpa tight. He smelled like sunshine and dirt.

"How did you find it?" asked Emma.

"I tried looking low first," said Grandpa. "That didn't work. Then I tried looking high. Sometimes we have to try different ways."

Emma bounced her ball on the garden path. Thump, thump, thump it went. The sound made them both smile.

"Want to play catch now?" asked Emma.

"I thought you'd never ask!" laughed Grandpa.

They played catch until lunchtime. The red ball flew back and forth. Their laughter filled the sunny garden.

Emma learned something important that day. When something seems impossible, you just try again. Grandpa Joe never gave up. That's what made him the best grandpa ever.

The red ball bounced happily in the warm summer sun.

The end.
