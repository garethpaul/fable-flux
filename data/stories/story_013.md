# Leo's Lost Red Car

Leo the lion loved playing by the big blue pond. Every sunny morning, he brought his favorite red toy car. The shiny car had yellow wheels that spun round and round. Leo made zoom-zoom sounds as he drove it through the soft grass.

"Vroom, vroom!" Leo said happily. He pushed his car along the muddy pond edge. The morning sun felt warm on his golden fur.

But then something terrible happened. Leo's paw slipped on a wet rock. His precious red car flew through the air. SPLASH! It landed right in the deep pond water.

"Oh no!" Leo cried. "My car! My special car!"

Leo stared at the rippling water. His car had sunk down, down, down. He couldn't see it anymore. The pond looked so deep and dark.

Leo's eyes filled with big tears. "How will I get it back?" he sniffled.

First, Leo tried reaching with his paw. He stretched and stretched as far as he could. The water felt cold and slippery. But his paw was too short. The car was too far down.

"This isn't working," Leo said sadly.

Next, Leo found a long stick. He poked it into the pond water. Poke, poke, poke went the stick. Leo felt around the muddy bottom. He touched rocks and squishy plants. But no toy car.

"Where could it be?" Leo wondered.

Then Leo had another idea. Maybe he could ask for help! He called to his friend Bella the bunny.

"Bella! Bella! Can you help me?" Leo shouted.

Bella hopped over on her soft white paws. "What's wrong, Leo?" she asked kindly.

"My red car fell in the pond," Leo explained. "I can't reach it. I tried a stick too."

Bella's pink nose twitched as she thought. "Let's look together," she said. "Two friends are better than one!"

Bella found a fishing net by the pond. It had tiny holes and a long handle. "Let's try this!" she said excitedly.

Leo held one side of the net. Bella held the other side. Together, they dipped it into the cool water. They moved it slowly back and forth.

"I feel something!" Leo said. His heart jumped with hope.

They pulled up the net carefully. Inside was a muddy boot, some pond weeds, and... nothing else.

"Don't give up," Bella said gently. "Let's try again."

Leo felt tired and sad. But Bella's kind words made him feel stronger. "Okay," he said. "One more try."

This time, they moved the net to a different spot. The water was clearer here. Sunshine made pretty sparkles on the surface.

"Look!" Bella pointed with her paw. "Something red down there!"

Leo's golden eyes grew wide. Deep in the water, he saw a tiny flash of red. "That's it! That's my car!"

Very carefully, they lowered the net. Leo's paws shook with excitement. The net went down, down, down. Then Leo felt something bump against it.

"Got it!" he roared happily.

They pulled up the net together. There, covered in pond weeds and mud, was Leo's beloved red car! The yellow wheels were still attached. It looked a little dirty but perfectly fine.

"My car!" Leo laughed with joy. "You helped me find it!"

Leo hugged Bella tight. "Thank you for not giving up," he said. "I couldn't have done it alone."

They washed the car in the shallow water. Soon it looked shiny and new again. The red paint gleamed in the sunshine.

"Vroom, vroom!" Leo said, rolling his car through the grass. It worked perfectly!

From that day on, Leo was more careful near the pond. But he also remembered something important. When problems seem too big, asking friends for help makes everything better. And if you keep trying different ways, you can solve almost anything.

Leo and Bella played together by the pond until the sun went down. The red car zoomed happily through the soft grass, and Leo's heart felt full of joy.

The end.
