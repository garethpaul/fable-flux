# Story story_1007

**Danny the Drum and Cleanup Time**

Danny the Drum sat quietly in the corner. The music room was very messy today. Toys were everywhere. Books lay on the floor. Crayons rolled under tables.

"Cleanup time!" called Miss Sarah cheerfully. "Everyone help put things away!"

All the children jumped up. They started picking up toys. They put books back on shelves. But Danny just sat there.

Danny did not want to help. He was tired from making music all day. His drum skin felt sore. His wooden sides ached a little.

"Danny looks sad," said little Emma. She walked over to him. "Don't you want to help us clean?"

Danny made a very soft thump. It meant "no thank you."

Emma patted his rim gently. "Cleaning can be fun," she said. "Watch this!"

Emma picked up some blocks. She dropped them into a basket. Clink, clink, clink! The blocks made a rhythm.

Danny's ears perked up. That sounded like music!

Tommy heard the rhythm too. He started putting away crayons. He dropped them in the box. Tap, tap, tap! Another beat joined Emma's rhythm.

Soon Sarah was stacking books. Thud, thud, thud! The books made a deep sound.

Danny began to feel excited. All these cleanup sounds made music together! Maybe he could help make cleanup music too.

Danny rolled forward a little bit. His wheels squeaked softly. Squeak, squeak! That was another sound for their cleanup song.

"Look!" shouted Emma. "Danny wants to help!"

Danny rolled to where the tambourines lay scattered. He bumped them gently with his side. Jingle, jangle! The tambourines sang as they slid together.

"Great job, Danny!" said Miss Sarah. She clapped her hands. Clap, clap, clap!

Now everyone was making music while they cleaned. Emma's blocks went clink, clink. Tommy's crayons went tap, tap. Sarah's books went thud, thud. Danny's wheels went squeak, squeak. The tambourines went jingle, jangle.

Danny felt his energy coming back. Making cleanup music was actually fun! He rolled to the triangle area. He nudged the triangles into their holder. Ting, ting, ting! Beautiful bell sounds joined their song.

Next, Danny helped with the maracas. He pushed them gently with his rim. Shake, shake, shake! The maracas rattled happily into their basket.

The rhythm got faster and faster. Everyone moved to the beat. Clean up became a dancing game!

"This is the best cleanup ever!" laughed Emma. She spun around while putting away blocks.

Tommy marched like a soldier. His feet went stomp, stomp as he organized the crayons by color.

Miss Sarah swayed back and forth. She hummed along with all their cleanup sounds.

Danny rolled everywhere, helping with every instrument. His drum heart felt warm and happy. Working together felt wonderful!

Soon the music room sparkled clean. Every toy had a home. Every book stood straight on its shelf. Every instrument waited in its special place.

"We did it!" cheered Miss Sarah. "And Danny helped make it musical!"

Danny felt very proud. His drum skin glowed in the afternoon sunlight. He had learned something important today.

Helping others felt good. Making work into play felt even better! When everyone worked together, hard jobs became easy and fun.

"Thank you, Danny," said Emma. She gave his rim one more gentle pat. "You made cleanup time special."

Danny made a happy little boom sound. He couldn't wait for tomorrow.

Tomorrow there would be more music to make. There would be more messes to clean up. But most importantly, there would be more chances to help his friends.

Danny rolled back to his corner spot. But now he wasn't hiding. He was resting up for another day of music and friendship.

As the children left the room, Danny heard them singing. They sang the cleanup song they had made together. Their voices echoed down the hallway like the sweetest music ever.

Danny closed his eyes and smiled. Tomorrow would be another wonderful day.

The end.
