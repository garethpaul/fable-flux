# Story story_1215

Sam sat on the warm sand. The beach was big and quiet. Only the waves made soft whooshing sounds.

Sam built a small sand castle. He patted it with his hands. The sand felt cool and bumpy. But playing alone was not very fun.

Sam looked around the empty beach. He wished he had a friend to play with. The sun felt hot on his back. The salty air tickled his nose.

Just then, something colorful caught his eye. A little red boat bobbed in the shallow water. It had a white sail and a friendly face painted on its side.

The boat seemed to be looking right at Sam. It bounced gently on the small waves. Sam stood up and brushed sand off his shorts.

"Hello there, little boat," Sam said softly.

The boat bobbed up and down. It looked like it was nodding! Sam giggled and walked closer to the water.

The cool waves tickled Sam's toes. The boat floated closer and closer. Soon it was right next to Sam's feet.

Sam reached down carefully. He picked up the little boat. It felt smooth and warm from the sun.

"Do you want to play with me?" Sam asked.

The boat's painted smile seemed to grow bigger. Sam was sure it wanted to be his friend.

Sam carried the boat back to his sand castle. He set it down gently next to the sandy walls.

"This can be your harbor," Sam said. He dug a small channel in the sand. The water from the waves flowed into it.

The boat floated perfectly in the little harbor. Sam clapped his hands with joy. Now his castle had a real boat!

Sam and the boat played together all morning. Sam made roads in the sand. He pretended the boat was visiting different islands.

"Let's go on an adventure," Sam told his new friend.

Sam carried the boat to deeper water. He let it sail on the gentle waves. The boat danced on the water like it was happy.

The white sail caught the warm breeze. The boat glided smoothly through the blue water. Sam walked alongside it in the shallow waves.

"You're such a good sailor," Sam said proudly.

When the boat sailed too far, Sam would guide it back. They played this game over and over. Sam's laughter mixed with the sound of the waves.

The sun climbed higher in the bright sky. Sam's tummy started to rumble. It was almost lunch time.

But Sam didn't want to stop playing. He had finally found a perfect friend. The boat seemed to understand everything Sam said.

Sam picked up the boat again. He hugged it gently against his chest. The boat felt like it was hugging him back.

"Will you be my special friend?" Sam whispered.

The boat bobbed in his arms. Sam knew that meant yes.

Sam carried his friend back to the sand castle. He made a special spot for the boat to rest. He used soft seaweed to make a cozy bed.

"You can live here," Sam said. "We'll play together every day."

The boat looked happy in its new home. The painted face seemed to smile even brighter.

Sam's mom called from far down the beach. "Sam! Time for lunch!"

Sam waved to his mom. Then he looked at his boat friend.

"I have to go eat now," Sam explained. "But I'll come back soon. I promise."

Sam gave the boat one more gentle pat. He covered it with soft sand to keep it safe.

As Sam ran toward his mom, he felt wonderful. The warm sand squished between his toes. The salty breeze blew through his hair.

He wasn't lonely anymore. He had found the best friend ever. Tomorrow they would have even more adventures together.

Sam looked back at his little boat. It bobbed peacefully in its sandy harbor. Sam knew it would be waiting for him.

The beach didn't feel big and empty anymore. It felt like the perfect place for two best friends to play.

Sam smiled all the way to lunch.

The end.
