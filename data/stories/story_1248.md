# Story story_1248

**Duck and Lily's Special Day**

In the sunny Meadowbrook Nursery, a little yellow duck named Pip sat by the blue water table. Pip loved the cool water on his orange feet. He loved the way it splashed and sparkled. But today, Pip felt sad and lonely.

All around him, children played together. They laughed and shared toys. They built towers with colorful blocks. Pip wished he had a friend to play with too.

Pip dipped his beak into the water. He made tiny waves with his wings. "I wish someone would play with me," he whispered softly.

Near the reading corner sat a little girl with curly brown hair. Her name was Lily. She had been watching Pip all morning. Lily loved animals, especially ducks. She thought Pip looked as lonely as she felt.

Lily was new to the nursery. She missed her old friends. Making new friends felt scary and hard.

Mrs. Rose, their kind teacher, noticed them both. She had warm eyes and a gentle smile. "Lily," she said softly, "would you like to meet Pip? He's our special duck friend."

Lily's eyes grew wide with excitement. "Can I really play with him?" she asked.

"Of course you can," Mrs. Rose said. "Just move slowly and speak quietly."

Lily walked carefully to the water table. Her heart beat fast with excitement. "Hello, Pip," she whispered. "I'm Lily. Would you like to be friends?"

Pip looked up at Lily's kind face. Her voice sounded sweet and gentle. He flapped his little wings happily. "Quack, quack!" he said, which meant "Yes, please!"

Lily giggled at Pip's happy sound. She sat down beside the water table. Very gently, she touched the warm water with her finger.

Pip swam closer to Lily. He liked how carefully she moved. He liked her quiet voice too. Pip splashed playfully, sending tiny drops of water toward Lily.

"That tickles!" Lily laughed. The cool water drops felt refreshing on her arms. She cupped some water in her small hands. She let it trickle back into the table.

Pip thought this was wonderful. He dove under the water and popped back up. Water dripped from his yellow feathers. Lily clapped her hands with joy.

"You're so clever, Pip!" she said. Her eyes sparkled with happiness.

Soon, other children came to watch. They saw how much fun Lily and Pip were having. "Can we play too?" asked Sam, a boy with freckles.

"Of course!" Lily said. She felt proud to share her new friend.

The children took turns gently petting Pip's soft feathers. They watched him swim in circles. They gave him tiny pieces of lettuce for snacks. Pip's orange beak felt smooth and warm.

Lily helped the other children learn how to be gentle. "Speak softly," she told them. "Move slowly so Pip isn't scared."

Pip felt so happy with all his new friends. But Lily was extra special. She had been the first to be kind to him.

At snack time, Mrs. Rose brought crackers and apple juice. Lily sat next to Pip's water table. She shared stories about her family while Pip listened quietly.

"I was scared to come to nursery," Lily whispered to Pip. "But now I have you as my friend."

Pip nuzzled against Lily's hand gently. His feathers felt soft and downy. Lily's heart felt warm and full.

When it was time for afternoon play, Lily and Pip played hide-and-seek. Pip would hide behind the toy castle. Lily would pretend she couldn't find him. Then Pip would pop out with a happy "Quack!"

As the day ended, Lily hugged Pip very gently. "Thank you for being my friend," she whispered.

Mrs. Rose smiled as she watched them. "You both found something special today," she said. "You found friendship."

The next morning, Lily ran straight to Pip. Pip swam quickly to greet her. They were no longer lonely. They had each other, and that made every day at nursery wonderful and bright.

From that day on, Lily and Pip were the very best of friends.

The end.
