# Story story_944

**Nutkin's Garden Adventure**

The morning sun peeked through the oak tree. Birds chirped their happy songs. It was time for garden work!

"Come on, Nutkin!" called Mama Squirrel. "Let's tend our garden today."

Nutkin's fluffy tail drooped down. He didn't want to leave his cozy nest. Garden work seemed hard and boring.

"Do I have to?" Nutkin asked softly.

"Gardens need love and care," Mama said. "Just like you do."

Nutkin climbed down slowly. His tiny paws felt heavy. The dirt looked messy and cold.

Mama handed him a small watering can. The metal felt cool in his paws. Water sloshed inside as he walked.

"I don't know how," Nutkin whispered.

"We'll learn together," Mama smiled.

They walked to their little garden patch. Tiny green shoots poked through the soil. Some looked droopy and sad.

Nutkin poured water on the first plant. Drip, drip, drip. The water made dark circles in the dirt.

"Look!" Mama pointed excitedly.

A small flower bud was opening! Bright yellow petals unfurled like tiny fingers. The sweet smell drifted up to Nutkin's nose.

"Did I do that?" Nutkin's eyes grew wide.

"You helped it grow," Mama nodded.

Nutkin watered the next plant more carefully. He watched for magic to happen. The leaves seemed to stand up straighter.

A fat bumblebee buzzed over to visit. It landed on the yellow flower. Its fuzzy body wiggled as it gathered nectar.

"Hello, bee!" Nutkin giggled.

The bee hummed a happy tune. Then it flew to another flower. More bees came to join the party.

Nutkin moved to the carrot patch. Orange tops peeked through the soil. He pulled gently on one. Up came a bright orange carrot!

"Look how big it grew!" Nutkin cheered.

The carrot felt smooth and firm. It smelled earthy and fresh. Nutkin's mouth watered thinking about lunch.

They found ripe red tomatoes next. Nutkin picked them carefully. The skin felt warm from the sunshine. Red juice dripped down his paw.

"Can we eat one now?" he asked.

Mama laughed. "Of course!"

The tomato burst with sweet flavor. Juice ran down Nutkin's chin. It tasted like sunshine and rain mixed together.

A butterfly landed on Nutkin's ear. Its wings felt soft as flower petals. Orange and black patterns decorated each wing.

"It tickles!" Nutkin giggled.

The butterfly danced from plant to plant. Nutkin followed it around the garden. Everything looked more colorful now.

They pulled weeds from around the lettuce. The green leaves felt crisp and cool. Nutkin made a game of finding the biggest weeds.

"I'm the weed-pulling champion!" he announced.

Mama clapped her paws together. "You certainly are!"

A robin hopped nearby. It pulled a fat worm from the soil. The bird chirped thank you to Nutkin.

"Even the birds like our garden," Nutkin observed.

The sun climbed higher in the sky. Nutkin's fur felt warm and toasty. His paws were dirty but happy.

They watered the bean plants last. Tiny white flowers covered the green vines. Soon there would be crunchy beans to eat.

"Gardens are like magic," Nutkin said thoughtfully.

"They are," Mama agreed. "We plant seeds with love. Nature does the rest."

Nutkin looked around their beautiful garden. Flowers bloomed in every color. Vegetables grew fat and healthy. Bees and butterflies danced everywhere.

His tummy rumbled loudly. All this work made him hungry!

They gathered vegetables for lunch. Nutkin carried the basket proudly. It felt heavy with good food.

"Tomorrow can we garden again?" Nutkin asked eagerly.

Mama's whiskers twitched with joy. "Every day if you want."

"I want to plant more flowers," Nutkin decided. "For the bees and butterflies."

That night, Nutkin dreamed of growing things. In his dreams, giant sunflowers reached the clouds. Rainbow carrots grew in perfect rows.

When morning came, Nutkin jumped from his nest. The garden was calling his name. Today would bring new adventures and more magic.

Garden time had become his favorite time of all.

The end.
