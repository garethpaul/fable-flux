# Story story_1018

**The Stubborn Jar**

Lily skipped through the sunny meadow. Her picnic basket bounced on her arm. The grass tickled her bare feet. Butterflies danced around purple flowers.

"What a perfect day!" Lily said.

She found a soft spot under an oak tree. Lily spread out her red blanket. She unpacked her lunch carefully.

A sandwich with peanut butter. An apple that was red and shiny. And her favorite treat - strawberry jam in a glass jar.

Lily picked up the jar. She twisted the lid. It wouldn't move. She tried again. The lid stayed stuck tight.

"Oh no!" Lily said. "I can't open my jam!"

Her bottom lip began to wobble. The jam looked so yummy through the glass. She could see the bright red berries inside.

Lily wiped her eyes. She would not give up. She had to think of something.

"Maybe I need to be stronger," she said.

Lily stood up tall. She held the jar with both hands. She squeezed very tight. Her face turned red. She twisted and twisted.

The lid still wouldn't budge. Not even a little bit.

Lily sat down hard on her blanket. A tear rolled down her cheek. The jam jar sat there, still closed tight.

"This is too hard," she whispered.

Just then, a fuzzy bee buzzed by her ear. It landed on a daisy near her blanket. Lily watched the little bee work.

The bee pushed its tiny body deep into the flower. It wiggled and moved around. Soon it flew to another flower.

"The bee doesn't give up," Lily said. "Maybe I shouldn't either."

Lily picked up the jar again. This time she looked at it carefully. The lid had little bumps on the sides. Maybe those bumps were there to help.

She wrapped her shirt around the jar. The cloth made it easier to hold. Then she placed her fingers on the bumps.

"Come on, jar," she said softly. "Please open for me."

Lily took a deep breath. She turned the lid slowly and gently. She felt something move! Just a tiny bit.

"It's working!" she said.

She kept turning the lid bit by bit. Soon she heard a small pop. The lid came free!

"I did it! I did it!" Lily cheered.

She jumped up and danced around her blanket. The sweet smell of strawberries filled the air. Her mouth watered.

Lily dipped her finger into the jam. It tasted like summer sunshine. Sweet and perfect.

She spread the jam on her sandwich. Each bite was delicious. The jam made everything taste better.

As she ate, Lily thought about the stubborn jar. First, she tried to use force. That didn't work. Then she looked more carefully. She found a better way.

"Sometimes the best way isn't the hardest way," she said to herself.

A small rabbit hopped near her blanket. Its nose twitched at the jam smell.

"Hello, little rabbit," Lily said. "Would you like some jam?"

She put a tiny bit on her finger. The rabbit licked it carefully. Its whiskers wiggled with joy.

"See? Good things happen when we don't give up," Lily told the rabbit.

The sun felt warm on her face. Birds sang in the trees above. The meadow smelled like flowers and fresh grass.

Lily finished her lunch slowly. She wanted to enjoy every bite. The jam jar sat open beside her, empty now.

When it was time to go home, Lily packed everything up. She held the empty jar carefully.

"Thank you for teaching me something today," she said to the jar.

Lily skipped back through the meadow. Her heart felt light and happy. She had solved a problem all by herself.

At home, she told her mom about the stubborn jar.

"You were very smart to try a different way," Mom said. "Sometimes we have to think instead of just push harder."

Lily smiled big. She couldn't wait for her next picnic. And next time, she would remember what she learned.

Problems might seem big at first. But there's always another way to try.

The end.
