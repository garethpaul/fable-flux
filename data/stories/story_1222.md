# Story story_1222

**Benny the Brave Bike**

Benny was a shiny red bike. He lived in a sunny meadow. Green grass tickled his wheels every day. Colorful flowers danced around him in the breeze.

Benny loved his meadow home. Butterflies landed on his handlebars. Bees buzzed hello as they flew by. The warm sun made his red paint sparkle.

One morning, Benny felt worried. Dark clouds filled the sky. The wind blew harder and harder. "A big storm is coming!" Benny said to himself.

Thunder rumbled far away. Lightning flashed in the distance. Benny's bell jingled nervously in the wind. He had never been in a storm before.

"I need to find shelter," Benny thought. "But where can a bike hide?"

Rain drops started to fall. They made tiny pitter-patter sounds on his seat. Benny looked around the meadow. He needed to move fast.

First, Benny tried rolling under a small bush. He pushed through the leaves with his front wheel. The branches scratched his shiny paint. His handlebars got stuck in the twigs.

"This won't work," Benny said sadly. The bush was too small. Rain was already dripping through the leaves. He wiggled backward out of the bush.

The storm was getting closer now. Thunder boomed louder. The wind made Benny wobble back and forth. His kickstand kept slipping in the wet grass.

Then Benny saw something wonderful. A big oak tree stood at the edge of the meadow. Its thick trunk was very wide. Its branches spread out like a giant umbrella.

"That tree looks strong," Benny said hopefully. "Maybe it can keep me dry."

Benny rolled as fast as he could. His wheels splashed through puddles. Raindrops bounced off his handlebars. The wind pushed him from side to side.

He reached the oak tree just in time. The rain started pouring down hard. But under the tree, Benny stayed completely dry. The thick leaves caught all the raindrops.

"This is perfect!" Benny said with joy. He leaned against the rough bark. The tree felt solid and safe. Its roots went deep into the ground.

Lightning flashed bright white. Thunder crashed very loud. But Benny felt brave now. The strong tree protected him from everything.

A little rabbit hopped under the tree too. "Hello," said the rabbit. "Can I share your shelter?"

"Of course!" Benny said kindly. "There's room for both of us."

Soon a family of squirrels joined them. Then a hedgehog waddled over. They all huddled together under the big tree. Everyone felt safe and warm.

The storm lasted for one whole hour. Rain pounded on the leaves above. Wind howled through the meadow. But under the oak tree, it was peaceful.

The animals told funny stories. They made Benny laugh and forget his worries. Time passed quickly with new friends around him.

Finally, the storm clouds moved away. Sunshine broke through the sky. The rain stopped completely. Everything in the meadow sparkled with water drops.

"Thank you for sharing," said the rabbit. "You're very kind, Benny."

The squirrels chittered goodbye happily. The hedgehog waved with his tiny paw. All the animals scampered back to their homes.

Benny rolled back to his favorite spot. The grass smelled fresh and clean. Flowers lifted their heads toward the sun. The meadow looked more beautiful than ever.

"I was scared of the storm," Benny thought. "But I found a good solution. The oak tree kept me safe."

From that day on, Benny wasn't afraid anymore. When storm clouds appeared, he knew exactly what to do. He would roll to his friend the oak tree.

Benny learned something important that day. Every problem has a solution if you keep trying. Sometimes the first idea doesn't work. But if you don't give up, you'll find the right answer.

Now Benny felt proud and confident. He was ready for any adventure in his beautiful meadow home.

The end.
