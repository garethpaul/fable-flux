# Story story_659

**Tommy and the Magic Dinner**

"Time for dinner!" called Mom from the kitchen.

Tommy heard her voice from his room. He was building a tall tower with his blocks. The tower had red blocks and blue blocks. It looked perfect.

"I don't want dinner," Tommy said quietly. He wanted to keep playing. Dinner meant stopping his fun game.

Dad peeked into Tommy's room. "Come on, buddy. We made something special tonight."

Tommy's shoulders dropped down. He walked slowly to the kitchen. His feet felt heavy like rocks.

The kitchen smelled warm and good. Mom had made chicken and rice. There were green beans too. Tommy liked chicken, but he still felt grumpy.

"I want to play more," Tommy said. He sat in his chair with a frown.

Mom smiled at him. "I know you love building, Tommy. But your body needs food to grow strong."

Tommy picked up his fork. He poked at a green bean. It rolled around his plate like a tiny green ball.

"Watch this," said Dad. He picked up a green bean with his fork. "This green bean is a magic wand!"

Dad waved the green bean in the air. "Abracadabra! I turn this rice into... snow!"

Tommy giggled a little bit. The white rice did look like tiny snow.

"My turn!" said Tommy. He picked up some rice with his spoon. "I make the chicken into... a castle!"

Mom clapped her hands. "What a wonderful castle! Can I visit it?"

"Yes!" said Tommy. He took a big bite of his chicken castle. It tasted warm and yummy.

Dad made his green beans into little soldiers. They marched across his plate. Tommy laughed and made his green beans dance.

"Look at my dancing beans!" Tommy said. He ate three dancing beans. They were crunchy and good.

Mom turned her chicken into a boat. "Sail away, little boat!" she said. The chicken boat sailed into her mouth.

Tommy's eyes got bright and happy. "I want to make a chicken boat too!"

He carefully cut his chicken with his fork. The piece looked just like a tiny boat. "Whoosh!" he said as he ate it.

"This is the best dinner game ever," Tommy said. His plate was getting empty fast.

Dad made funny voices for his food. The rice talked in a squeaky voice. The green beans spoke in deep, silly voices.

Tommy copied Dad's funny voices. Soon the whole family was laughing. The kitchen filled with giggles and joy.

"My food is having a party in my tummy," said Tommy. He rubbed his belly and smiled big.

Mom gave Tommy a warm hug. "You ate everything! Your body will grow strong tonight."

Tommy felt proud and happy. His tummy felt full and cozy. Playing with food made dinner so much fun.

"Can we play the dinner game tomorrow too?" asked Tommy.

"Of course!" said Dad. "We can make up new games every night."

Tommy helped clear his plate. He put his fork and spoon in the sink. He felt like a big helper.

"Thank you for the magic dinner," Tommy told Mom and Dad.

Mom kissed the top of his head. "Thank you for trying new things, sweetie."

Tommy ran back to his room. His block tower was still standing tall. But now he felt excited about tomorrow's dinner too.

He thought about what games they could play next time. Maybe his food could be racing cars. Or maybe it could be singing birds.

Tommy smiled as he added one more block to his tower. Dinner time wasn't so bad after all. It was actually really fun when his family played together.

He couldn't wait to see what magical dinner games they would play tomorrow night.

The end.
