# Story story_1278

Anna sat in the big sandbox. The sand felt warm under her hands. She made little hills and valleys. But she felt lonely.

The playground was quiet today. Other children were at home. Anna sighed and patted the sand.

"I wish I had someone to play with," she said softly.

Just then, she heard a gentle snort. Anna looked up. A zebra stood by the sandbox! The zebra had black and white stripes. His mane was soft and fuzzy.

"Hello," said the zebra. "I'm Zeke. Can I play too?"

Anna's eyes grew wide. "You can talk!"

"Of course I can," Zeke smiled. "I love to play in sand."

Anna felt excited. "Yes! Please come play with me!"

Zeke stepped into the sandbox. His hooves made soft sounds in the sand. He was careful not to step on Anna's hills.

"What should we build?" asked Zeke.

Anna thought hard. "How about a castle?"

"I love castles!" said Zeke.

They started digging together. Anna used her small hands. Zeke used his hooves very gently. The sand felt cool and soft.

Anna made the walls. Zeke helped pile sand high. They worked as a team.

"This is fun!" laughed Anna.

"Much better than playing alone," agreed Zeke.

They built towers next. Anna rolled sand into balls. Zeke helped stack them up. The towers grew tall and proud.

"Look how high they are!" said Anna.

The sun felt warm on their backs. A gentle breeze blew. It made Zeke's mane flutter.

"Let's make a moat," suggested Zeke.

"What's a moat?" asked Anna.

"Water around the castle," explained Zeke.

They dug a circle around their castle. Anna pretended to pour water in it. Zeke neighed with joy.

"Now our castle is safe," said Anna.

They added more details. Anna found small stones for windows. Zeke used a stick to draw doors. Their castle looked magical.

"We make a great team," said Zeke.

Anna nodded happily. "The best team ever!"

They played for a long time. They built roads in the sand. They made tiny sand animals. Everything was more fun together.

Anna's tummy started to rumble. "I'm getting hungry," she said.

"Me too," said Zeke. "I should go find some grass."

Anna felt sad. "Will you come back tomorrow?"

Zeke nuzzled her shoulder gently. "Of course! Friends always come back."

"Really? We're friends?" asked Anna.

"The very best of friends," said Zeke warmly.

Anna hugged Zeke's neck. He smelled like sunshine and adventure. His stripes felt smooth under her arms.

"Thank you for playing with me," whispered Anna.

"Thank you for sharing your sandbox," said Zeke.

They looked at their beautiful castle. It stood tall and strong. Just like their new friendship.

"Tomorrow we can build a whole city," said Anna.

"With bridges and parks!" added Zeke excitedly.

Anna clapped her hands. "And maybe a zoo!"

"I know some animals who would love that," winked Zeke.

As the sun started to set, Zeke prepared to leave. His hooves made soft prints in the sand.

"See you tomorrow, Anna," he called.

"See you tomorrow, Zeke!" Anna waved.

Anna watched her new friend trot away. His stripes looked beautiful in the golden light. She felt warm and happy inside.

She looked back at their castle. It reminded her of their wonderful day. Playing alone had been lonely. But playing with a friend was magical.

Anna's mom called from across the playground. "Time to go home, sweetie!"

"Coming!" Anna called back.

She gave their castle one last pat. Tomorrow would bring another adventure. Tomorrow would bring Zeke back.

Anna skipped toward her mom. Her heart felt light and joyful. She had found something special today. She had found a true friend.

"Did you have fun?" asked her mom.

"The best day ever!" said Anna, smiling bright.

And it really was.

The end.
