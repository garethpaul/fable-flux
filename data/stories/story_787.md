# Story story_787

**Luna's Sleepy Garden**

Luna loved playing in her backyard. The grass felt soft under her bare feet. Colorful flowers danced in the warm breeze. But when the sun started to set, Luna felt scared.

"I don't want bedtime to come," Luna whispered to her teddy bear, Mr. Buttons. She hugged him tight as the sky turned orange and pink.

Every evening was the same. Luna's tummy felt funny when darkness crept across the yard. The shadows looked like monsters. Strange sounds came from the bushes. Even the familiar oak tree seemed spooky in the dim light.

"Luna, time to come inside!" called Mama from the back door.

Luna's heart beat fast. "Five more minutes, please!" she called back. But she knew bedtime was coming soon.

That night, Luna had an idea. She would make the backyard less scary before dark. The next day, she worked hard on her plan.

Luna picked all the prettiest flowers. She made a circle of daisies around the oak tree. She arranged pink roses by the fence. She scattered yellow sunflower petals on the grass.

"There!" Luna said proudly. "Now the yard is too pretty to be scary."

But when evening came, the flowers couldn't help. The pretty petals looked gray in the shadows. The daisy circle seemed like a mysterious ring. Luna still felt afraid when Mama called her inside.

Luna felt sad. Her flower plan didn't work. She sat on the back steps with Mr. Buttons. Tears rolled down her cheeks.

"What's wrong, little star?" asked Mama, sitting beside her.

"I'm scared of the backyard at night," Luna sniffled. "I tried to make it prettier, but it's still scary."

Mama hugged Luna close. "What if we made the yard brighter instead of prettier?"

Luna wiped her eyes. "How do we do that?"

The next day, Luna and Mama went to the store. They bought strings of tiny white lights. They bought solar lanterns that looked like fireflies. They bought glow-in-the-dark stepping stones.

Luna helped Mama wrap the lights around the oak tree. The branches sparkled like stars. They hung the firefly lanterns from the fence posts. The soft yellow glow made everything warm and cozy.

They pressed the special stepping stones into the grass. The stones would glow green when darkness came. They made a safe, bright path to the back door.

"This feels magical," Luna said, clapping her hands.

That evening, Luna waited excitedly for sunset. When the sky grew dark, something wonderful happened. The oak tree twinkled with tiny lights. The firefly lanterns glowed softly in the breeze. The stepping stones made a glowing path through the grass.

The backyard looked like a fairy garden. Luna felt brave and happy instead of scared.

"Wow!" Luna gasped. "It's beautiful!"

She danced along the glowing path. She touched the twinkling tree trunk. She watched the gentle firefly lights sway back and forth.

When Mama called for bedtime, Luna didn't feel afraid. She skipped along the bright stepping stones. She waved goodbye to her magical backyard.

"Thank you for helping me," Luna told Mama at the back door.

"You didn't give up," Mama smiled. "When your first idea didn't work, you tried again. That's what brave people do."

Luna hugged Mr. Buttons tight. She learned something important that day. Sometimes the first try doesn't work. But trying again can lead to something even better.

Now Luna loves bedtime. She knows her magical backyard will be waiting for her tomorrow. The twinkling lights remind her that darkness can be beautiful too.

Every night, Luna looks out her bedroom window. She sees the gentle glow from her fairy garden. It makes her feel safe and happy.

"Good night, magical backyard," Luna whispers before closing her eyes.

And in the morning, Luna can't wait to play outside again.

The end.
