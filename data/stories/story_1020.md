# Story story_1020

Teddy sat in the sunny meadow. The soft grass tickled his brown fur. Yellow flowers danced in the breeze. But Teddy felt sad and lonely.

He picked up a smooth stone. He threw it far across the meadow. The stone landed with a soft thud. Teddy sighed a big sigh.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," Teddy said to himself.

Behind a tall daisy, a little gray mouse was watching. Mouse had big round ears and a twitchy pink nose. She wanted to play too. But she felt scared.

"What if Teddy doesn't like me?" Mouse whispered. "I am so small."

Mouse took a tiny step forward. Then another step. Her little heart went thump-thump-thump.

Teddy picked up another stone. This time he saw movement in the flowers. A small gray nose peeked out.

"Hello there," Teddy said gently. His voice was warm like honey.

Mouse's whiskers twitched nervously. "Hi," she squeaked in her tiny voice.

"Would you like to play with me?" Teddy asked. He smiled his biggest smile.

Mouse's eyes grew wide with surprise. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" Teddy said. "Playing is more fun with friends."

Mouse scurried out from behind the daisy. Her tail swished with excitement. The warm sun felt good on her gray fur.

"What should we play?" Mouse asked.

Teddy thought for a moment. "Let's play catch!"

He rolled a small pebble to Mouse. Mouse pushed it back with her tiny paws. The pebble rolled slowly through the green grass.

"This is fun!" Mouse giggled. Her laugh sounded like tiny silver bells.

They rolled the pebble back and forth. The grass smelled fresh and sweet. Butterflies fluttered around them like flying rainbows.

"My turn to pick a game," Mouse said. "Let's play hide and seek!"

Teddy covered his eyes with his big paws. "One, two, three," he counted slowly.

Mouse ran behind a purple flower. She tried to make herself very small. Her heart beat fast with excitement.

"Ready or not, here I come!" Teddy called.

He looked left and right. He saw the purple flower wiggle. A tiny gray tail stuck out.

"Found you!" Teddy laughed.

Mouse popped up with a big grin. "Your turn to hide!"

Teddy looked around the meadow. Where could a big teddy bear hide? He spotted a pile of golden leaves.

Mouse counted with her eyes closed tight. "Ten! Ready or not!"

She searched high and low. Finally she saw brown fur under the leaves.

"There you are!" Mouse squeaked happily.

They played until the sun climbed high overhead. The warm breeze carried the sweet smell of flowers. Bees buzzed softly in the distance.

"I'm getting hungry," Teddy said. His tummy rumbled like gentle thunder.

"Me too," Mouse agreed. "I know where the best berries grow."

Mouse led Teddy to a patch of wild strawberries. The berries were red and juicy. They smelled like summer sunshine.

Teddy picked the berries carefully with his big paws. Mouse gathered tiny ones perfect for her size. They sat together in the soft grass.

"These berries taste like candy," Teddy said. Sweet juice dripped down his chin.

"The red ones are my favorite," Mouse said. She nibbled delicately with her small teeth.

After their snack, they lay on their backs. Fluffy white clouds drifted overhead. The clouds looked like animals and ice cream cones.

"Thank you for playing with me," Mouse said softly.

"Thank you for being my friend," Teddy replied. His voice was full of happiness.

"Will you play with me again tomorrow?" Mouse asked hopefully.

"Every day!" Teddy promised. "Friends always play together."

As the afternoon sun warmed their faces, Teddy and Mouse felt perfectly happy. The meadow seemed brighter with friendship blooming like the colorful flowers around them.

From that day on, they met in the meadow every morning. They played games, shared snacks, and told stories. The lonely feeling was gone forever.

True friendship had made their world complete.

The end.
