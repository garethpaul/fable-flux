# Story story_993

**The Little Boat and the Friendly Zebra**

In the sunny park, a small blue boat sat by the pond. His name was Bobber. The water sparkled like tiny diamonds. Bobber watched the ducks swim by. He felt very lonely.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," Bobber said softly. His voice made little ripples in the water. The morning sun felt warm on his wooden sides.

Behind the tall oak tree, Zara the zebra was eating sweet grass. She heard a sad voice by the water. Her black and white stripes shimmered in the sunlight. Zara had kind brown eyes.

"Someone sounds lonely," Zara whispered to herself. She walked slowly toward the pond. Her hooves made soft sounds on the green grass.

When Zara saw Bobber, she smiled. "Hello there, little boat. I'm Zara. What's your name?"

Bobber looked up with surprise. "I'm Bobber! Are you talking to me?"

"Yes, I am!" Zara laughed. Her laugh sounded like tiny bells. "You looked sad. Do you want to play together?"

Bobber's blue paint seemed to glow brighter. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" Zara stepped carefully into the shallow water. The cool pond felt nice on her feet. "What games do you like?"

Bobber thought for a moment. "I love to sail around the pond. But it's more fun with friends."

Zara had an idea. "I could walk beside you! We could explore together."

So Bobber began to sail slowly around the pond. Zara walked in the shallow water next to him. The water made gentle splashing sounds around her legs.

"Look at those pretty lily pads!" Zara pointed with her nose. The lily pads were bright green and round.

"And see those colorful fish!" Bobber said excitedly. Orange and silver fish swam beneath them. Their scales caught the sunlight.

They played hide and seek behind the cattails. The tall plants swayed in the gentle breeze. They smelled like fresh rain and earth.

Zara would hide first. Bobber would sail around looking for her black and white stripes. "I see you behind the big rock!" he would call out.

Then Bobber would hide under the wooden bridge. Zara would search with her eyes. "There you are, you silly boat!" she would giggle.

They raced from one side of the pond to the other. Zara's hooves splashed happily in the water. Bobber's little sail caught the wind perfectly.

"This is the best day ever!" Bobber said. His voice was full of joy.

"I think so too!" Zara agreed. She felt warm and happy inside.

As the sun got higher, they sat together by the water's edge. Bobber floated peacefully. Zara stood in the cool shade.

"Bobber, you're a wonderful friend," Zara said. Her voice was gentle and kind.

"You're the best friend I've ever had," Bobber replied. "I was so lonely before. Now I feel happy and special."

"Friends make everything better," Zara said wisely. "Even the sun seems brighter today."

They watched the clouds float by like fluffy white boats. The birds sang sweet songs in the trees. Everything felt perfect and peaceful.

"Can we play again tomorrow?" Bobber asked hopefully.

"Every day!" Zara promised. "We can have new adventures together."

From that day on, Bobber and Zara met at the pond every morning. They played games and told stories. They shared the beautiful park together.

Bobber was never lonely again. He had found a true friend. Zara was happy too. She loved having someone special to play with.

The other animals in the park smiled when they saw them. A boat and a zebra were unusual friends. But their friendship was real and strong.

As the stars came out that first night, both friends felt grateful. They had learned that friendship can happen anywhere. Even a boat and a zebra can be best friends.

And they lived happily ever after in their beautiful park.

The end.
