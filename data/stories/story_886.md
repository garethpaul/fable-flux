# Story story_886

**Tommy's Missing Blocks**

Tommy sat on the soft carpet in the dining room. The morning sun made everything bright and warm. He looked around for his favorite wooden blocks.

"Where are my blocks?" Tommy wondered out loud.

Yesterday, he had built a tall tower right here. The blocks were red, blue, yellow, and green. They made a clicking sound when he stacked them up high.

Tommy crawled around the dining room floor. He looked under the big wooden table. No blocks there. He peeked behind Mama's chair. Still no blocks.

"Mama!" Tommy called. "My blocks are gone!"

Mama came into the dining room. She had flour on her hands from making breakfast. "Let's look together, sweetie," she said with a smile.

Tommy decided to search by himself first. He was a big boy now. He could find his own toys.

Tommy crawled to the corner by the window. He moved the curtains aside. The fabric felt smooth and cool. But no blocks hid behind them.

He looked inside the toy basket. He found his stuffed elephant and his red ball. But the blocks were not there either.

Tommy felt sad. His bottom lip started to wobble. "I can't find them anywhere!" he said.

Then Tommy had an idea. Maybe the blocks rolled somewhere far away. He crawled under the dining room table.

Under the table was dark and cozy. Tommy felt around with his hands. He touched something smooth and round. It was just Mama's shoe.

He crawled further under the table. His little hands patted the carpet. He felt something hard and square!

"A block!" Tommy said happily.

But when he pulled it out, it was just a napkin that had fallen. Tommy felt disappointed again.

He sat under the table and thought hard. Where could blocks go? They were too heavy to fly away. They were too big to disappear.

Then Tommy remembered something. Yesterday, baby sister Lucy was playing nearby. She liked to put things in her mouth. Maybe she moved his blocks!

Tommy crawled out from under the table. He went to look for Lucy.

Lucy was in her high chair in the kitchen. She was eating mashed bananas. Yellow banana was all over her face and hands.

"Lucy," Tommy said. "Did you see my blocks?"

Lucy giggled and pointed toward the dining room. She pointed down low, near the floor.

Tommy crawled back to the dining room. This time, he looked very carefully. He got down on his belly like a snake.

Under the long curtains, he saw something colorful! Tommy pushed the curtain aside.

There were all his blocks! They had rolled under the curtains by the window. The morning sun made them sparkle like jewels.

"I found them!" Tommy shouted with joy.

Mama came running. "Where were they, Tommy?"

"Under the curtains!" Tommy said proudly. "I looked and looked. Then I found them all by myself!"

Tommy gathered his blocks in his arms. They felt smooth and solid. He loved the weight of them in his hands.

He sat on the carpet and started building. First, he put the red block down. Then the blue one on top. The yellow block came next.

Click, click, click went the blocks as he stacked them.

"I'm building the tallest tower ever!" Tommy announced.

Lucy clapped her sticky hands from her high chair. Mama smiled and gave Tommy a big hug.

"You didn't give up, Tommy," Mama said. "You kept looking until you found them."

Tommy felt proud and happy. His tower grew taller and taller. The blocks felt perfect in his small hands.

"Next time I lose something, I'll keep looking," Tommy said. "I won't give up!"

The sun shone through the window. Tommy's colorful tower stood tall and strong. He had solved the mystery of his missing blocks all by himself.

Tommy smiled as he added the last green block to the very top. Sometimes the best things are worth searching for.

The end.
