# Story story_635

**Luna and the Sleepy Star**

The clock showed eight o'clock. "Time for bed, Luna," called Mama from downstairs.

Luna sat on her bedroom floor. Her toys were scattered everywhere. She held her favorite stuffed elephant tight.

"I don't want to go to sleep," Luna said softly. "Sleep is boring."

Her big sister Maya walked into the room. Maya had sparkly pajamas with moons on them. She sat down next to Luna on the fuzzy carpet.

"What's wrong, little sister?" Maya asked gently.

"Bedtime is no fun," Luna said. "I want to keep playing."

Maya smiled and looked out the window. The sky was getting dark. Little stars were starting to twinkle.

"I have an idea," Maya said. "Want to see something magical?"

Luna's eyes grew wide. "What kind of magic?"

Maya turned off the big light. She turned on Luna's special night light. It made tiny stars dance on the ceiling.

"Look up," Maya whispered.

Luna looked up at the dancing stars. They moved slowly across the ceiling. Some were blue. Some were yellow. They twinkled like real stars.

"Wow," Luna breathed. "They're so pretty."

"Each star has a special bedtime job," Maya said. "The blue ones bring sweet dreams. The yellow ones keep you safe all night."

Luna pointed at a spinning star. "What does that one do?"

"That one helps you have the best sleep ever," Maya said. "But you have to be in bed first."

Luna felt excited now. Bedtime seemed different somehow. "Can we brush my teeth under the stars?"

"Of course," Maya said.

They walked to the bathroom together. Luna brushed her teeth while watching the hallway night light glow. The toothpaste tasted like bubble gum. Maya helped her rinse with the special cup.

Back in Luna's room, Maya helped her put on soft pajamas. They were pink with little cats on them. The fabric felt smooth and cozy.

"Now we pick a bedtime story," Maya said.

They chose a book about a sleepy bunny. Maya read in a gentle voice. Luna snuggled under her warm blanket. The dancing stars kept moving above them.

"The bunny looks so comfortable," Luna said with a yawn.

"Just like you," Maya said. "Your pillow is fluffy. Your blanket is soft. Everything is perfect for sleeping."

Luna's eyes felt heavy. The stars seemed to slow their dance. Maya's voice was soothing and calm.

"Maya?" Luna said sleepily.


"Yes, little sister?"

"Will the stars be here tomorrow night too?"

"Every single night," Maya promised. "They're always here to help you sleep."

Luna smiled and closed her eyes. The room felt peaceful and safe. She could hear Maya's quiet breathing beside her.

"Sweet dreams, Luna," Maya whispered.

"Sweet dreams," Luna whispered back.

Maya gave her sister a gentle kiss on the forehead. She tiptoed quietly out of the room. The dancing stars kept glowing softly.

Luna felt warm and happy under her covers. Her stuffed elephant was tucked beside her. The stars made everything feel magical and special.

She thought about tomorrow. Maybe they could read another story. Maybe Maya would show her more star magic. Maybe bedtime could be fun every night.

Luna's breathing became slow and peaceful. The blue stars brought her wonderful dreams. She dreamed about flying through clouds and playing with friendly animals.

In the morning, Luna woke up feeling great. The sun was shining through her window. She had slept all night long.

"Maya!" she called happily. "I can't wait for bedtime tonight!"

Maya peeked into the room with a big smile. "Really? You want bedtime to come?"

"Yes!" Luna said, bouncing on her bed. "I want to see the dancing stars again. I want to pick another story. Bedtime is the best!"

Maya hugged her little sister tight. "I'm so glad you think so."

From that night on, Luna always looked forward to bedtime. The dancing stars became their special tradition. And every night felt like a magical adventure.

The end.
