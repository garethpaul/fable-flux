# Story story_1064

**Sam's Magical Snack Time**

The big clock on the kitchen wall said three o'clock. "Snack time, Sam!" called Mom from the kitchen. She was cutting up fresh apple slices. The sweet smell filled the air.

Sam was building a tall tower with his blocks. Red blocks, blue blocks, yellow blocks stacked high. "Do I have to?" Sam asked. His tower was almost done. Just one more block to go.

"Yes, sweetie," Mom said gently. "Your body needs fuel to grow big and strong." She arranged the apple slices on Sam's favorite blue plate. The one with tiny stars around the edge.

Sam dragged his feet to the kitchen table. He slumped in his chair. The apple slices looked boring. Just plain white pieces sitting there. Sam poked one with his finger. It felt cold and smooth.

"I'm not really hungry," Sam said quietly. He looked back at his unfinished tower. The blocks seemed to call to him from the living room.

Mom sat down next to Sam. "What if we made snack time special?" she asked. Her eyes twinkled like the stars on Sam's plate. "What if we used our imagination?"

Sam tilted his head. "How?"

"Close your eyes," Mom whispered. "Take a bite and tell me what you taste."

Sam picked up an apple slice. He closed his eyes tight. Crunch! The apple was crisp and juicy. Sweet juice ran down his chin. "It tastes... sunny," Sam said, opening his eyes wide.

"Sunny?" Mom smiled. "Tell me more."

Sam took another bite. This time he chewed slowly. "It tastes like sunshine and... and summer days!" The apple suddenly tasted different. Better. More exciting.

"What else do you notice?" Mom asked.

Sam held the apple slice up to the light. "It looks like a tiny moon!" he said with wonder. "A delicious moon I can eat!"

Mom clapped her hands. "What a magical discovery! What about the sound?"

Sam bit down again. Crunch, crunch, crunch. "It sounds like walking on fall leaves!" He giggled and took another bite. "Or like a tiny drum!"

"And how does it feel?" Mom asked.

Sam touched the smooth skin of the apple. "Smooth like a river rock. But inside it's soft and wet." He took a bigger bite. Juice dripped onto his plate.

"You know what?" Sam said, sitting up straighter. "This apple is like a treasure chest. The outside looks simple. But inside there's sweetness and crunch and juice!"

Mom nodded. "Every snack can be an adventure. What other treasures might we find?"

Sam looked around the kitchen with new eyes. "Can we try crackers tomorrow? They might sound like ocean waves!" He crunched his apple excitedly.

"And cheese could be like sunshine squares," Mom added.

Sam finished his last apple slice. His tummy felt happy and full. "Mom, can we always eat our snacks like explorers?" he asked. "Finding new tastes and sounds and feelings?"

"Of course we can," Mom said, hugging Sam tight. "Every snack time can be magical if we use our senses and imagination."

Sam hopped down from his chair. He felt energized and ready to play. "I'm going to finish my tower now. But tomorrow at snack time, I want to be an explorer again!"

"It's a deal," Mom said, cleaning up the empty plate. "What should we explore tomorrow?"

Sam thought for a moment. "Carrots! I bet they sound different than apples. And maybe they taste like... like orange sunshine!"

Mom laughed. "I can't wait to find out."

Sam skipped back to his blocks. His tower waited patiently for him. But now he was also excited about tomorrow's snack time. Who knew eating could be such an adventure?

The kitchen clock ticked softly. Three-fifteen now. Sam carefully placed the last yellow block on top of his tower. Perfect! Just like his magical snack time had been.

Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough. Sam was already wondering what treasures carrots might hold. What sounds would they make? What adventures waited in every crunchy bite?

Snack time would never be boring again.

The end.
