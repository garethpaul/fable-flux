# Story story_978

Leo the lion sat on the big red swing. His golden fur sparkled in the warm sunshine. But Leo felt sad and lonely.

The playground was full of happy sounds. Children laughed on the slide. Birds chirped in the tall trees. But Leo had no one to play with.

Leo pushed himself gently on the swing. Back and forth, back and forth. His big paws held the chains tight. He watched other animals play together.

"I wish I had a friend," Leo said softly.

Just then, Zara the zebra walked into the playground. Her black and white stripes looked beautiful in the sun. She carried a bright red ball under her arm.

Zara saw Leo on the swing. He looked very sad. His head hung down low. His tail drooped to the ground.

"Hello," said Zara in a kind voice. "I'm Zara. What's your name?"

Leo looked up with surprise. His golden eyes grew wide. "I'm Leo," he said quietly.

"Would you like to play with me?" asked Zara. She held up her red ball. "We could bounce it together."

Leo's face lit up like sunshine. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" said Zara with a big smile. "Playing is more fun with friends."

Leo jumped off the swing. His paws made a soft thud on the ground. He felt excited and happy.

Zara rolled the red ball to Leo. It bounced gently across the soft grass. Leo caught it with his big paws.

"Good catch!" said Zara, clapping her hooves.

Leo rolled the ball back to Zara. She stopped it with her front hoof. The ball felt warm from the sun.

"This is fun!" said Leo. His tail began to wag.

They played catch for a while. The ball went back and forth. Sometimes it bounced high. Sometimes it rolled low.

"Let's try the slide!" suggested Zara.

They ran to the tall, blue slide. Their feet made happy sounds on the playground ground. Leo climbed up first. His claws gripped the ladder well.

"Wheee!" Leo slid down fast. The slide felt smooth and cool. He landed with a soft bump.

Zara climbed up next. Her hooves clicked on each step. She slid down even faster than Leo.

"That was amazing!" she laughed.

They took turns on the slide. Up and down, up and down. Each time felt more fun than the last.

Next, they played on the seesaw. Leo sat on one side. Zara sat on the other. Up and down they went.

"Higher!" giggled Zara.

"Hold on tight!" laughed Leo.

The seesaw made gentle creaking sounds. The warm breeze tickled their faces. They felt so happy together.

After the seesaw, they played hide and seek. Leo hid behind the big oak tree. His golden fur was hard to see in the shadows.

"Found you!" called Zara. She had looked everywhere.

Then Zara hid behind the sandbox. Her stripes blended with the fence shadows. Leo searched and searched.

"Where are you?" he called.

"Here I am!" Zara jumped out with a big smile.

As the sun began to set, they sat together on a bench. The sky turned pretty colors. Pink and orange filled the air.

"Thank you for playing with me," said Leo. "I was so lonely before."

"Thank you for being my friend," said Zara. "You're very kind and fun."

Leo's chest felt warm and happy. "Will you play with me tomorrow?"

"Every day!" said Zara. "Friends always play together."

They hugged each other tight. Leo's soft fur felt cozy. Zara's smooth coat felt nice.

From that day on, Leo and Zara played together every afternoon. They shared the swings and slide. They rolled the red ball back and forth.

Leo was never lonely again. He had found his best friend. And Zara was so happy too.

The playground became their special place. A place where a lion and zebra became the very best of friends.

"Friendship makes everything better," they both agreed.

And they lived happily ever after.

The end.
