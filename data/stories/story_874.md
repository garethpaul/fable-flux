# Story story_874

**The Brave Little Puzzle**

On the soft living room carpet lived a colorful jigsaw puzzle named Patches. She had one thousand tiny pieces that fit together perfectly. Her picture showed a beautiful rainbow over a sunny meadow. Patches loved how her bright colors made the whole room cheerful.

One gray afternoon, Patches noticed something scary. Dark clouds gathered outside the big window. The wind began to howl loudly. Rain started tapping against the glass like tiny fingers. Lightning flashed bright white across the sky.

"Oh no!" whispered Patches. "A big storm is coming!"

Thunder rumbled so loud it made the house shake. Patches felt her pieces wiggle and jiggle. Some of her corner pieces started to slide apart. Her beautiful rainbow picture began to break up.

"I'm falling apart!" she cried. "The storm is too scary and loud!"

Patches knew she had to do something fast. She tried her first idea. She squeezed all her pieces together as tight as she could. She held her breath and made herself very stiff.

BOOM! Another loud thunder crash shook the room. The scary sound made Patches jump. When she jumped, her pieces scattered everywhere! Her rainbow broke into hundreds of tiny bits across the carpet.

"That didn't work at all," Patches sniffled. She felt even more worried than before.

The storm grew stronger outside. Rain poured down the windows like little rivers. The wind whistled through the trees. But Patches noticed something new. She could hear gentle voices from the kitchen.

"Don't worry, sweetie," said Mom's warm voice. "Storms can be loud, but we're safe inside."

"The thunder sounds scary, but it can't hurt us," added Dad's calm voice.

Patches listened carefully to the kind words. They made her feel a little bit braver. She had a new idea for staying together during the storm.

Instead of holding tight and staying stiff, Patches tried something different. She let her pieces stay loose and flexible. When the thunder boomed, she let herself sway gently like a tree in the wind. She didn't fight the scary sounds anymore.

"I can bend without breaking," she said to herself. "I can be flexible and strong."

CRASH! The biggest thunder yet shook the whole house. But this time, Patches swayed softly with the sound. Her pieces moved together like a gentle wave. She stayed connected but not too tight.

Lightning flashed again and again. Each time, Patches swayed and stayed together. She discovered that being flexible helped her much more than being stiff and scared.

As the storm continued, Patches began to notice beautiful things too. The rain made pretty patterns on the window. The lightning lit up her rainbow colors in amazing ways. The thunder sounded like a big drum playing music.

"This storm is actually quite wonderful," she realized. "It's loud and strong, but so am I!"

Slowly, the storm began to move away. The thunder grew quieter. The rain became a gentle patter. Sunshine started peeking through the gray clouds.

Patches looked at herself with pride. Every single piece was still connected. Her rainbow picture was complete and beautiful. She had stayed together through the whole scary storm.

"I did it!" she cheered. "I was brave and flexible!"

The living room filled with warm golden sunlight. Patches' rainbow colors sparkled brighter than ever before. She felt proud and strong and very happy.

From that day on, whenever storms came, Patches remembered her lesson. She knew that being flexible and brave was much better than being stiff and scared. She learned that she could handle difficult things by staying calm and trying new ways to solve problems.

The little puzzle never worried about storms again. Instead, she looked forward to showing how strong and bendable she could be. Patches had discovered that she was much braver than she ever knew.

And in the cozy living room, surrounded by sunshine and love, Patches smiled her rainbow smile every single day.

The end.
