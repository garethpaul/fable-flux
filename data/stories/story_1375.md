# Story story_1375

**The Little Garden Friend**

Benny the bunny loved his garden home. Green leaves danced in the breeze. Colorful flowers smelled sweet and fresh. But Benny felt lonely every day.

He hopped around the empty garden. "I wish I had someone to play with," Benny sighed. The only sounds were buzzing bees and chirping birds high above.

Benny's ears drooped down sadly. He had no friends at all. The garden felt too quiet and still.

One sunny morning, Benny had an idea. "I'll make a friend!" he said excitedly. He gathered soft brown mud from near the watering can.

Benny shaped the mud carefully with his paws. He made a round head and a chubby body. "This will be my new friend," he whispered happily.

But when the sun came out, something terrible happened. The mud friend began to crack and crumble. Pieces fell off onto the ground.

"Oh no!" cried Benny. His mud friend was breaking apart. Soon there was only a pile of dried dirt left.

Benny felt sadder than before. He sat under a big sunflower and started to cry. Warm tears rolled down his fuzzy cheeks.

"Why are you crying, little bunny?" asked a gentle voice.

Benny looked up with surprise. A small girl with curly hair was peeking over the garden fence. She had kind brown eyes and a friendly smile.

"I made a friend from mud," Benny sniffled. "But it broke into pieces. Now I'm all alone again."

The girl climbed carefully over the fence. "My name is Maya," she said softly. "I live next door. I see you playing here every day."

"You do?" asked Benny, wiping his eyes.

"Yes! I always wanted to meet you. But I was too shy to say hello."

Benny's ears perked up with hope. "Really? You wanted to be my friend?"

Maya nodded and sat down beside him. "I love gardens too. They smell like sunshine and flowers."

Benny felt his heart fill with joy. "Would you like to play with me?"

"I would love that!" Maya said with a big grin.

They spent the whole morning playing together. Maya showed Benny how to make flower crowns from daisies. Benny taught Maya how to hop like a bunny between the vegetable rows.

They played hide-and-seek behind the tall corn stalks. The green leaves rustled as they giggled and ran around. They found smooth pebbles and made patterns in the dirt.

"This is the best day ever!" laughed Benny.

Maya picked a bright yellow dandelion. "This is for you, my new friend."

Benny's whiskers twitched with happiness. "Thank you! I have something for you too."

He found the juiciest red strawberry in the garden. Maya took a small bite and smiled. The sweet berry tasted like summer sunshine.

As the day grew warm, they sat in the shade together. Maya gently petted Benny's soft fur. Benny felt peaceful and content.

"I was trying so hard to make a friend," said Benny thoughtfully. "But the best friend was right next door!"

"Sometimes the answer is closer than we think," Maya agreed. "I'm so glad I was brave enough to talk to you."

From that day on, Benny and Maya played in the garden every morning. They watered plants together and watched butterflies dance on the flowers.

Benny learned that making friends takes patience and courage. Sometimes you have to try different ways. But the most important thing is to keep your heart open.

The garden was never quiet again. It was filled with laughter, friendship, and joy. Benny had found exactly what he was looking for - a true friend who loved gardens just as much as he did.

And they lived happily ever after, playing together in their magical garden home.

The end.
