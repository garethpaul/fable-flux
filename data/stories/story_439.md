# Mila's Library Adventure

Mila the little mouse peeked out from behind a thick book. The library was quiet and peaceful. Tall shelves reached up like gentle giants. Soft sunlight danced through the windows.

But Mila's tiny heart felt heavy. She missed her mama so much.

This morning, Mama had brought Mila to the library. "I need to run errands," Mama had said. "Mrs. Chen will watch you here. You'll have fun exploring!"

Now Mila sat alone on a soft reading cushion. Her whiskers drooped sadly. The library felt too big without Mama.

"I want to go home," Mila whispered.

Mrs. Chen, the kind librarian, noticed Mila's sad face. "What's wrong, little one?" she asked gently.

"I miss my mama," Mila said. A tiny tear rolled down her fuzzy cheek.

Mrs. Chen smiled warmly. "Missing someone you love is normal. Let's find ways to feel better!"

Mila wiped her eyes. Maybe she could try something new.

First, Mila climbed up to the picture books. She found a story about a mama rabbit and her baby. But looking at the pictures made her miss Mama even more.

"That didn't work," Mila sighed.

Next, she tried the puzzle corner. Colorful pieces scattered across a small table. Mila pushed them around with her tiny paws. The puzzle showed a happy family of bears.

"Still thinking about Mama," Mila mumbled.

Then Mila spotted the art station. Bright crayons sat in neat rows. Clean paper waited for someone to draw on it.

Mila had an idea! She grabbed a red crayon with both paws.

Carefully, she drew a big heart. Inside the heart, she drew two mice - one big, one small. They were holding paws and smiling.

"That's me and Mama!" Mila said proudly.

Drawing made her feel a little better. But she still felt lonely.

Mrs. Chen walked over. "What a beautiful picture, Mila!"

"It's me and Mama," Mila explained. "But I still miss her."

"Would you like to hear a story?" Mrs. Chen asked. "Sometimes stories help when we're sad."

Mila nodded. Mrs. Chen picked up a book with a soft blue cover.

"This story is about a little fox who missed his mama too," Mrs. Chen said.

As Mrs. Chen read, Mila listened carefully. The little fox in the story felt exactly like Mila did! The fox learned that even when Mama wasn't there, her love was always in his heart.

"The fox's mama loved him even when they were apart," Mrs. Chen explained. "Just like your mama loves you right now."

Mila touched her chest. "Mama's love is in my heart?"

"Always," Mrs. Chen smiled.

Suddenly, Mila felt warmer inside. She looked at her drawing again. The two mice looked so happy together.

"I can keep Mama close by thinking about her!" Mila realized.

Just then, she heard familiar footsteps. Tiny claws clicked on the floor.

"Mila!" called a sweet voice.

Mama Mouse appeared around the corner! Her eyes sparkled with joy.

"Mama!" Mila squeaked. She ran as fast as her little legs could carry her.

Mama scooped Mila up in the biggest, warmest hug.

"I missed you so much!" Mila said.

"I missed you too, sweetie," Mama replied. "How was your library time?"

Mila showed Mama her heart drawing. "I drew us! And Mrs. Chen taught me that your love stays in my heart always."

Mama's eyes grew misty. "That's exactly right, my brave little mouse."

"I was sad at first," Mila explained. "But I kept trying new things. Drawing helped the most!"

"You were very persistent," Mama said proudly. "You didn't give up when you felt sad."

As they walked home together, Mila felt proud and happy. She had learned something important today.

Even when people we love aren't with us, their love stays in our hearts. And when we feel sad, we can try different things until we feel better.

"Can we go back to the library tomorrow?" Mila asked.

Mama smiled. "Of course, my persistent little artist!"

Mila skipped all the way home, her heart full of love and newfound confidence.

The end.
