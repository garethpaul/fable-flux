# Penny Plane's Musical Day

Penny Plane sat quietly on the windowsill. The morning sun painted her silver wings gold. Today was music time at home. But Penny felt a little worried.

"Music time, Penny!" called Mama from downstairs. Her voice was warm and cheerful. Penny's propeller drooped just a tiny bit.

You see, Penny was very good at flying. She could zoom and swoosh beautifully. But music? That seemed different. That seemed hard.

Penny slowly rolled down the hallway. Her wheels made soft clicking sounds. Click, click, click. She didn't notice how nice it sounded.

In the living room, colorful instruments waited. There were shiny bells and smooth wooden blocks. A big, round drum sat in the corner. Everything looked so bright and friendly.

"What would you like to try first?" asked Mama. She held up two wooden sticks. They had red ribbons tied around them.

Penny rolled closer to the drum. It was bigger than she expected. The top looked like a big, brown circle. She touched it gently with her wing.

BOOM!

The sound surprised her. It was deep and rich. It made her whole body tingle. Penny tried again.

BOOM! BOOM!

"That's wonderful!" said Mama. "You're making music!"

Penny felt a little spark of excitement. She tried tapping faster. BOOM-boom-BOOM! The rhythm made her want to move.

Next, Mama showed her the silver bells. They were small and shiny. When Penny brushed them with her wing, they sang. Ting-a-ling-a-ling!

The sound was like tiny stars dancing. Penny tried making different patterns. Ting-ting-TING! Ling-a-ling-a-LING!

"You're creating your own song!" said Mama. Her eyes sparkled with joy.

Something magical began to happen. Penny started moving to her music. Her wheels rolled in time with the beats. Her propeller spun gently to the rhythm.

She discovered the wooden blocks next. When she tapped them together, they made happy clicking sounds. Just like her wheels in the hallway! Click-click-tap! Click-click-tap!

Penny began mixing all the sounds together. BOOM went the drum. Ting-a-ling sang the bells. Click-tap replied the blocks.

Her whole body was dancing now. She rolled in circles around the instruments. Her wings swayed back and forth. Her propeller hummed along.

"Listen, Penny," whispered Mama. "You're flying through music!"

And it was true! Penny felt like she was soaring. Not through the sky, but through beautiful sounds. Each note lifted her higher. Each rhythm carried her forward.

She tried the triangle next. DING! It rang clear and bright. Like a bell in the clouds. Then the rain stick, which sounded like gentle raindrops. Pitter-patter-pitter-pat!

Penny created a whole song. First the drum: BOOM-boom-BOOM! Then the bells: Ting-a-ling! The blocks joined in: Click-tap-click! Finally, the triangle: DING!

She played her song three times. Each time felt better than the last. Mama clapped and danced along.

"That was beautiful music, Penny!" said Mama. "You found your own special way to fly!"

Penny's propeller spun with happiness. Music wasn't scary at all. It was like flying, but with sounds instead of wind.

As they put the instruments away, Penny helped. She gently rolled each one to its special place. The bells tinkled softly. The drum hummed quietly.

"Will we have music time again tomorrow?" asked Penny. Her voice was full of hope.

"Every day, if you'd like," said Mama. "Music is always here waiting for us."

That night, Penny dreamed of flying through clouds made of music notes. She heard drums like thunder and bells like raindrops. In her dream, she conducted a whole orchestra of airplanes.

When morning came, Penny was already excited. She rolled to the window and watched the sunrise. Even the birds seemed to be singing just for her.

Today would bring another music time. Another chance to fly through beautiful sounds. Another magical adventure at home.

Penny's propeller hummed a happy tune. She couldn't wait to make music again.

Music time had become her favorite time of day.

The end.
