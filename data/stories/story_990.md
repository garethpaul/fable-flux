# Story story_990

**Lily and the Little Squirrel**

Lily sat in the sunny field behind her house. The grass felt soft under her legs. She picked yellow dandelions one by one. The flowers smelled sweet and fresh.

"I wish I had someone to play with," Lily said softly. She made a chain with the dandelions. But playing alone felt quiet and sad.

The warm sun made her sleepy. Birds chirped in the tall oak tree nearby. Lily sighed and looked around the empty field.

Suddenly, something moved in the grass. A small gray squirrel poked his head up. His black eyes sparkled with curiosity. His fluffy tail twitched back and forth.

The squirrel crept closer to Lily. He tilted his tiny head to one side. "Chitter, chitter," he said in his squeaky voice.

"Hello, little squirrel," Lily whispered. She stayed very still. She didn't want to scare him away.

The squirrel took three small hops toward her. His whiskers twitched as he sniffed the air. He seemed interested in her dandelion chain.

Lily smiled and held out a bright yellow flower. "Do you want to play with me?"

The squirrel's eyes grew wide with excitement. He grabbed the dandelion gently in his tiny paws. Then he did a happy little flip in the grass.

"You're so funny!" Lily giggled. The sound made the squirrel chatter with joy.

Lily picked more dandelions and tossed them in the air. The squirrel jumped and caught them as they fell. His acrobatic moves made Lily clap her hands.

"Let's play hide and seek," Lily suggested. She covered her eyes and counted to ten. "One, two, three..."

When she opened her eyes, the squirrel had vanished. Lily looked behind the oak tree. No squirrel there.

She checked under the big rocks. Still no squirrel.

Then she heard a soft giggle from above. The squirrel sat on a low branch, his tail hanging down like a furry rope.

"There you are!" Lily laughed. "You're very good at hiding."

The squirrel chittered proudly and scampered down the tree. Now it was Lily's turn to hide.

She crouched behind a cluster of tall sunflowers. The squirrel searched everywhere. He looked under leaves and around tree stumps.

Finally, he found her behind the golden flowers. They both burst into happy laughter.

Next, they played a rolling game. Lily rolled a small acorn across the grass. The squirrel chased after it, tumbling and spinning. His gray fur got covered in green grass bits.

"You look silly," Lily said, brushing the grass from his coat. The squirrel seemed to smile as she petted him gently.

They spent the whole afternoon together. They made dandelion crowns and raced through the field. The squirrel showed Lily how to climb the lower branches of the oak tree.

As the sun began to set, it painted the sky orange and pink. Lily felt so happy and full of joy.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" she asked her new friend.

The squirrel nodded and chittered excitedly. He gave her a tiny acorn as a special gift.

Lily carefully put the acorn in her pocket. "This will remind me of our fun day," she said.

The squirrel touched her hand with his small paw. It felt warm and friendly.

"I'm so glad you wanted to play with me," Lily told him. "I was lonely before, but now I have a wonderful friend."

The squirrel did one last happy flip before scampering up his tree. He waved goodbye with his bushy tail.

Lily walked home with a huge smile on her face. The dandelion chain bounced around her neck. The little acorn felt smooth in her pocket.

She couldn't wait to tell her mom about her new squirrel friend. Tomorrow would bring another day of adventure and fun.

From that day on, Lily never felt lonely in the field again. She had learned that friendship can come from the most unexpected places. Sometimes the best friends have fluffy tails and twitchy whiskers.

The field had become a magical place full of laughter and friendship.

The end.
