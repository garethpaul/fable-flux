# Story story_1089

Anna sat on the big red swing. Her feet dangled in the air. The playground was quiet today.

"I wish I had someone to play with," Anna said softly. She pushed herself back and forth. The swing creaked gently.

Above her head, something sparkled in the blue sky. A small silver airplane was flying in circles. It had bright yellow wings that glinted in the sunshine.

The plane swooped down lower. It made a soft whooshing sound. Anna looked up and smiled.

"Hello there, little plane," she called out.

To her surprise, the plane dipped its wings. It was saying hello back! The plane circled around Anna three times.

"Do you want to play?" Anna asked.

The plane bobbed up and down in the air. That meant yes! Anna jumped off the swing. Her sneakers hit the soft sand with a thud.

The plane flew to the slide. It zoomed down the smooth yellow surface. Anna giggled and clapped her hands.

"That looks fun!" she said. "But I'm too big for you to carry."

The plane thought for a moment. It flew to the sandbox and drew a circle in the sand with its propeller. Then it drew another circle. And another.

"Oh, I see!" Anna said. "You want me to make circles too!"

Anna ran to the merry-go-round. She gave it a big push. Round and round it went. The plane flew alongside it, spinning in the same direction.

"Wheee!" Anna laughed. The wind blew through her brown hair. The plane's engine hummed a happy tune.

Next, the plane flew to the monkey bars. It wove between each bar like a snake. Anna watched carefully.

"I can do that too!" she said.

Anna grabbed the first bar. Hand over hand, she swung across. The plane flew right beside her, matching her speed perfectly.

"We make a good team!" Anna puffed as she reached the end.

The plane did a little loop in the air. It was very excited.

Then Anna had an idea. She ran to the seesaw. But how could a plane use a seesaw?

The plane landed gently on one end. Anna sat on the other end. She was much heavier than the plane.

"This won't work," Anna said sadly.

But the plane had another idea. It flew up and down, up and down. Anna copied the movement. She stood up and sat down, up and down.

"We're still seesawing!" Anna giggled. "Just in a different way!"

The sun was getting lower in the sky. It painted everything orange and pink. Anna's mom would come soon.

"I had so much fun today," Anna told the plane. "Will you come back tomorrow?"

The plane flew close to Anna's face. Its propeller barely whispered. Anna felt the gentle breeze on her cheeks.

The plane drew a heart shape in the air. Anna's eyes grew wide with joy.

"That means you'll be my friend forever!" she said.

Anna drew a heart in the sand with her finger. The plane flew down and traced the same heart with its wing tip.

"Now we both have the same heart," Anna said. "We'll always be connected."

Just then, Anna heard her mom calling. "Anna! Time to go home!"

"I have to leave now," Anna told the plane. "But I'll see you tomorrow. We can play hide and seek!"

The plane flew up high and did three perfect loops. That was its way of saying goodbye.

Anna waved until the plane was just a tiny speck in the sky. She skipped all the way to her mom.

"Did you have fun at the playground?" her mom asked.

"Yes!" Anna said. "I made a new friend today. The best friend ever!"

Anna looked back at the playground. Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough. She had so many games to play with her airplane friend.

The playground wasn't lonely anymore. It was full of friendship and joy.

Anna smiled all the way home.

The end.
