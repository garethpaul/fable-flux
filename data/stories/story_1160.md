# Story story_1160

**Gigi's Reading Time**

The big clock on the wall said three o'clock. Miss Lily clapped her hands together. "Reading time, everyone!" she called out cheerfully.

All the animal friends in the classroom started moving. Bear grabbed his favorite book about honey. Rabbit hopped over to find a story about carrots. Mouse scurried to the cozy reading corner.

But Gigi the giraffe stayed at her desk. She looked down at her long legs. She looked at the small chairs in the reading corner. Her tummy felt funny and twisted.

"I don't want to read today," Gigi whispered to herself.

Miss Lily walked over with gentle steps. "What's wrong, Gigi?" she asked softly.

Gigi's cheeks turned pink. "The chairs are too small. My neck is too long. I can't fit anywhere."

Miss Lily smiled warmly. "Let's try something different today."

She led Gigi to a sunny spot by the tall window. There sat a pile of soft, colorful cushions. "These are just for you," Miss Lily said.

Gigi carefully folded her long legs. She settled down on the cushions. But she still felt worried. "I'm not good at reading," she said quietly.

Miss Lily sat down beside her. She opened a big, bright book. On the cover was a picture of tall, green trees reaching up to fluffy white clouds.

"This book is about a magical forest," Miss Lily said. "Would you like to read it together?"

Gigi nodded slowly. Miss Lily opened the first page. The words were big and clear. There were beautiful pictures of trees, flowers, and a winding path.

"Once upon a time," Gigi read carefully, "there was a forest that touched the sky."

As Gigi read, something wonderful happened. The words seemed to dance off the page. She could smell the fresh forest air. She could hear birds singing in the trees. She could feel soft grass under her feet.

"The trees were so tall," Gigi continued, "that only special animals could see their tops."

In the story, a young giraffe just like Gigi walked through the forest. The giraffe could see things no other animal could see. Beautiful birds' nests high in the branches. Ripe, sweet fruit growing at the very top of trees.

Gigi's eyes grew wide with excitement. "The giraffe is the hero!" she said happily.

"Just like you could be," Miss Lily said with a warm smile.

Gigi read faster now. Her voice grew stronger and clearer. The other animals gathered around to listen. They loved hearing about the brave giraffe in the story.

"She saved the day because she was tall!" Gigi read proudly.

Bear clapped his paws. "You read so well, Gigi!"

Rabbit nodded eagerly. "Your voice makes the story come alive!"

Mouse squeaked with joy. "Read more, please!"

Gigi felt warm and happy inside. Her heart beat fast with excitement, not worry. She loved how the words flowed from her mouth. She loved how her friends listened carefully.

When the story ended, everyone cheered. Gigi stood up tall and proud. Her long neck didn't feel awkward anymore. It felt special and wonderful.

"Thank you, Miss Lily," Gigi said. "That was the best reading time ever!"

Miss Lily hugged Gigi gently. "You're a wonderful reader. Tomorrow we have more books about tall, brave animals."

Gigi's eyes sparkled with happiness. "Really? Can I read to everyone again?"

"Of course," Miss Lily said. "You make reading magical."

As the animals cleaned up their books, Gigi helped put cushions away. She couldn't stop smiling. Reading wasn't scary anymore. It was an adventure.

"Tomorrow I want to read about tall elephants," Bear said.

"And tall horses!" added Rabbit.

"And tall giraffes!" squeaked Mouse.

Gigi laughed with pure joy. She couldn't wait for tomorrow's reading time. She had discovered something amazing. Being different made her special. Her height was her superpower.

That night, Gigi dreamed of magical stories and brave, tall heroes. She dreamed of reading adventures yet to come.

The end.
