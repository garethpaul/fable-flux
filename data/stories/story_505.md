# Lily and the Big Oak Tree

Lily loved the sparkling pond near her house. She visited every sunny day. The water was cool and clear. Little fish swam in gentle circles. Dragonflies danced above the surface.

But there was something special by the pond. A big, tall oak tree stood proudly. Its branches reached high into the sky. Lily watched squirrels scamper up easily. Birds nested in the leafy top.

"I want to climb up there too," Lily said. She looked at the thick, brown trunk. It seemed so very tall.

Lily wrapped her small arms around the tree. She tried to pull herself up. Her feet slipped on the smooth bark. Down she slid with a soft thump.

"Ouch," she said, brushing off her knees. But Lily didn't give up.

She tried again. This time, she jumped higher. Her fingers barely reached the first branch. She dangled for just a moment. Then down she came again.

Lily sat by the pond feeling sad. A friendly frog hopped nearby.

"Why the long face?" asked the frog.

"I can't climb the big tree," Lily sighed. "I've tried and tried."

The wise old frog smiled. "Maybe you need a different plan."

Lily wiped her eyes. "What do you mean?"

"Look around," said the frog. "What else do you see?"

Lily looked carefully. Near the tree base, she spotted something. Small rocks were scattered everywhere. They were different sizes and shapes.

"Rocks!" Lily said excitedly.

She gathered the biggest rocks first. Lily stacked them carefully by the tree. One rock. Then another on top. The pile grew taller and taller.

But the rocks kept tumbling down. They made a loud clatter. Lily felt frustrated again.

"This isn't working either," she said.

The frog hopped closer. "What about those fallen branches?"

Lily looked where he pointed. Several sturdy branches lay nearby. They had fallen during last week's storm.

"I could make steps!" Lily realized.

She dragged the first branch over. It was heavy but not too heavy. Lily leaned it against the tree trunk. It made a perfect ramp.

She found another branch. This one was shorter. Lily placed it higher up. Now she had two steps.

"This might work," she said hopefully.

Lily stepped onto the first branch. It held her weight perfectly. She climbed to the second branch. Her heart beat with excitement.

From there, she could reach the lowest tree branch. Lily stretched up carefully. Her fingers wrapped around the rough bark.

"I did it!" she called down.

The frog clapped his little webbed hands. "You found a way!"

Lily pulled herself up slowly. Soon she sat on the first real branch. The pond looked beautiful from up here. She could see fish swimming below.

"This is amazing!" Lily laughed with joy.

She climbed a little higher. Each branch felt safer now. The leaves rustled gently around her. A butterfly landed on her shoulder.

After a while, Lily climbed back down. She used her branch-steps carefully. Soon her feet touched the ground.

"Thank you, frog friend," she said. "You helped me think differently."

"You did the hard work," the frog replied. "You didn't give up."

Lily looked at her branch-ladder proudly. Tomorrow she would climb again. Maybe she'd go even higher.

The sun began to set. Pretty colors painted the sky. Lily waved goodbye to the pond. She skipped home happily.

At dinner, Lily told her family. "I climbed the big oak tree today!"

"How wonderful!" said her mom. "How did you do it?"

"I tried many ways," Lily explained. "When one way didn't work, I tried another. I used branches to make steps."

Her dad smiled proudly. "That's very clever thinking."

That night, Lily dreamed of climbing. In her dreams, she reached the very top. She could see the whole world below.

The next morning, Lily hurried to the pond. Her branch-ladder waited by the tree. The frog waved from his lily pad.

"Ready for another adventure?" he asked.

"Always!" Lily grinned, starting her climb.

Sometimes the best solutions come from trying again. And again. And again.

The end.
