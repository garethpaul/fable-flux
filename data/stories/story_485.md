# Gigi's Garden Discovery

The morning sun painted golden stripes across the kitchen window. Mama Giraffe stretched her long neck and smiled. "Good morning, Gigi! It's garden time!"

Gigi the little giraffe yawned big. Her spots were still sleepy. Her legs felt wobbly. "Do we have to?" she asked. "The dirt is muddy. The bugs are buzzy. Garden time is boring."

Mama Giraffe's eyes twinkled like stars. "Come see what's waiting outside," she said gently.

Gigi dragged her hooves slowly. Step by step by step. The back door creaked open. Cool morning air tickled her nose. But Gigi still felt grumpy about garden time.

"What should we do first?" asked Mama.

Gigi shrugged her spotted shoulders. "I don't know. I don't care."

Mama handed Gigi a tiny watering can. It was bright yellow like sunshine. "The flowers look thirsty," Mama said. "Could you help them drink?"

Gigi took the little can. It felt smooth in her hooves. She walked to the flower bed. Drip, drop, splash! Water sparkled on the red roses.

Suddenly, something amazing happened. A butterfly landed on Gigi's nose! It had wings like orange velvet. Its tiny feet tickled.

"Oh!" gasped Gigi. "Hello, little butterfly!"

The butterfly danced from flower to flower. Gigi followed with her watering can. Drip, drop, splash! Each flower seemed to giggle with joy.

"Look, Mama!" called Gigi. "The butterfly likes our garden!"

Next, they planted tiny seeds. Gigi dug small holes with her hooves. The soil felt cool and soft. She dropped seeds gently. Pat, pat, pat! She covered them with dirt.

"What will grow?" asked Gigi.

"Carrots!" said Mama. "Orange and crunchy."

Gigi's tummy rumbled with excitement. She loved carrots!

Then they picked ripe tomatoes. Red ones, yellow ones, purple ones too! Gigi reached high with her long neck. She could touch tomatoes nobody else could reach.

"I'm the best tomato picker!" laughed Gigi.

A friendly bee buzzed hello. Gigi wasn't scared. The bee was working hard too. It flew from sunflower to sunflower. Buzz, buzz, buzz!

"We're both garden helpers," Gigi told the bee.

They pulled weeds next. Gigi made it a game. She pretended weeds were tickly green monsters. Pull, tug, pop! Out they came.

"Got you, silly weed!" she giggled.

The garden smelled wonderful now. Like roses and mint and fresh earth. Gigi took deep, happy breaths.

A robin landed nearby. It cocked its head at Gigi. "Tweet, tweet!" it sang.

"Tweet, tweet!" Gigi sang back.

The robin found a juicy worm. It flew to feed its babies. Gigi felt proud. Their garden helped the robin family too.

As they worked, Gigi discovered more magic. Ladybugs wore polka-dot coats. Earthworms wiggled like tiny dancers. Leaves whispered secrets in the breeze.

"Garden time is treasure hunting!" said Gigi.

They watered the vegetable patch together. Gigi sprinkled water on lettuce leaves. They sparkled like green jewels. The cucumber vines reached toward the sun.

"Everything is growing," marveled Gigi.

Mama smiled. "Just like you, sweet girl."

Finally, they sat on the garden bench. Gigi's spots were dusty but happy. Her hooves were muddy but proud. She had worked hard.

A hummingbird zipped past. Its wings moved faster than blinking. It sipped nectar from the purple petunias.

"Thank you, flowers," chirped the hummingbird.

Gigi felt warm inside. She had helped make this beautiful place.

"Mama," said Gigi softly. "Can we do garden time again tomorrow?"

Mama hugged her tight. "Every single day, if you'd like."

Gigi looked around their magical garden. Butterflies danced. Bees hummed. Flowers nodded in the gentle breeze. Everything felt alive and happy.

"I love garden time," whispered Gigi.

That night, Gigi dreamed of tomorrow's garden adventures. She couldn't wait to see what new magic would bloom.

Garden time wasn't boring at all. It was the most wonderful part of every day.

The end.
