# Story story_763

**Thomas the Little Train and the Missing Teddy Bear**

Thomas was a bright red toy train. He lived on the kitchen counter. Every morning, he played with his best friend. His best friend was a soft brown teddy bear named Cocoa.

Today felt different. Thomas woke up early. The sun peeked through the kitchen window. Birds sang happy songs outside. But something was wrong.

"Where is Cocoa?" Thomas asked himself. He looked left. He looked right. No teddy bear anywhere!

Thomas felt worried. His wheels began to shake. Cocoa always slept right next to him. They did everything together. They raced around the sugar bowl. They played hide-and-seek behind the coffee pot. They watched the family cook breakfast.

"Cocoa! Where are you?" Thomas called out. His voice echoed in the big kitchen. The refrigerator hummed softly. The clock tick-tocked on the wall. But no answer came.

Thomas decided to search everywhere. He would find his best friend!

First, Thomas rolled toward the fruit bowl. Maybe Cocoa was hiding behind the red apples. The apples smelled sweet and fresh. Thomas pushed his nose between them. No teddy bear there.

Next, he checked near the toaster. The metal felt cool and smooth. Thomas looked in every corner. Still no Cocoa.

"This is harder than I thought," Thomas said. He felt sad. His bright red paint seemed less shiny.

Thomas rolled to the edge of the counter. He looked down at the floor below. It seemed very far away. The kitchen tiles looked cold and hard.

"Maybe Cocoa fell down there," Thomas thought. But how could he get down? He was just a little train. He couldn't fly or jump.

Thomas felt like giving up. Tears formed in his painted eyes. "I miss my friend so much," he whispered.

Just then, he heard a tiny sound. "Mmmph! Mmmph!"

Thomas stopped crying. He listened carefully. The sound came again. "Mmmph! Mmmph!"

"That sounds like Cocoa!" Thomas said. His wheels started spinning with excitement.

Thomas followed the sound. It led him toward the bread box. The wooden box sat in the corner. It had a heavy lid on top.

"Mmmph! Mmmph!" The sound was louder now.

"Cocoa! Are you in there?" Thomas called.

"Yes! Help me!" came a muffled voice.

Thomas felt so happy. But now he had a new problem. How could he open the heavy lid?

Thomas thought hard. He remembered watching the family use the bread box. They always lifted from the front corner.

Thomas backed up. He took a deep breath. Then he rolled forward as fast as he could. He pushed his front against the lid corner.

The lid wiggled a little. But it didn't open.

"Don't give up, Thomas!" Cocoa called from inside.

Thomas tried again. This time, he pushed harder. His wheels spun on the smooth counter. The lid moved more.

"Almost there!" Cocoa said.

Thomas tried one more time. He used all his strength. He pushed and pushed. Suddenly, the lid popped open!

Out tumbled Cocoa the teddy bear. His brown fur was covered in bread crumbs. But he was smiling big.

"Thomas! You found me!" Cocoa said. He hugged his train friend tight.

"How did you get in there?" Thomas asked.

"I was playing last night," Cocoa explained. "I climbed in to hide. Then the lid closed. I couldn't get out!"

Thomas felt so relieved. His best friend was safe. "I was so worried about you," he said.

"You never gave up looking," Cocoa said. "That's what best friends do."

Thomas learned something important that day. When problems seem big, don't give up. Keep trying different ways. Ask for help when you need it. Most importantly, never stop caring about your friends.

From that day on, Thomas and Cocoa always told each other where they were going. They stayed closer together. And they had many more wonderful adventures in their cozy kitchen home.

The sun shone brighter. The kitchen felt warm and happy. Thomas and Cocoa were together again, just like it should be.

The end.
