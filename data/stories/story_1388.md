# Story story_1388

**Toby's Magical Cleanup Time**

The big clock on the wall went tick-tock. It was cleanup time at the train yard. All the toy trains had to put away their things.

Toby the little blue train looked around. His coal cars were scattered everywhere. His track pieces lay in messy piles. His whistle sat forgotten under a bench.

"Time to clean up, Toby!" called Conductor Sam.

Toby's wheels felt heavy. Cleanup time was not fun. It was hard work. He wanted to keep playing instead.

"Do I have to?" Toby puffed sadly. His steam came out in small, tired clouds.

"Yes, little engine," said Sam gently. "Everything needs its special place."

Toby rolled slowly toward his coal cars. They were so heavy! He pushed one with his nose. It barely moved. Toby sighed a big, steamy sigh.

Then something wonderful happened. A tiny sparkle danced in the air. Then another sparkle appeared. Soon, golden sparkles swirled all around Toby!

"What's happening?" Toby whispered.

The sparkles landed on his coal cars. Suddenly, the cars felt light as feathers! They rolled easily behind Toby. Click-clack, click-clack went their wheels. It sounded like a happy song.

"This is amazing!" Toby chuffed with excitement.

He pushed the cars to their parking spot. The sparkles made everything glow softly. Even the dark corner looked bright and cheerful.

Next, Toby went to gather his track pieces. The magical sparkles followed him. They made the metal tracks shine like silver ribbons. Toby picked up each piece with his crane arm. They felt warm and tingly.

"I'm getting stronger!" Toby realized. His engine hummed with pride.

He arranged the tracks in neat rows. They looked like a beautiful rainbow made of silver. The sparkles danced above them, making tiny chiming sounds.

Now for his whistle under the bench. Toby stretched his crane arm way down. The sparkles tickled his metal sides. He giggled as he reached for the shiny whistle.

"Got it!" Toby cheered.

He polished the whistle with a soft cloth. It gleamed like a golden star. When he hung it in its special spot, it sang a sweet little tune.

Toby looked around the train yard. Everything was clean and tidy. His coal cars sat in perfect rows. His tracks sparkled in neat stacks. His whistle hung proudly on its hook.

The magical sparkles began to fade. But Toby felt warm and happy inside. His engine purred with satisfaction.

"You did wonderful work, Toby," said Conductor Sam. His voice was full of pride.

"It was fun!" Toby said, surprised. "The sparkles helped me."

Sam smiled. "Those sparkles come when you try your best. They appear for trains who care about their things."

Toby's headlight glowed brightly. He had never seen the sparkles before. Maybe he had never tried his very best at cleanup time.

"Will they come back tomorrow?" Toby asked hopefully.

"Try your best and see," Sam winked.

Toby rolled to his sleeping spot. The train yard looked beautiful in the moonlight. Everything had its proper place. The air smelled fresh and clean.

As Toby closed his eyes, he thought about tomorrow. There would be more playing with friends. There would be new adventures on the tracks. And yes, there would be cleanup time again.

But now Toby knew a secret. When you try your best, magic can happen. Hard work becomes fun. Heavy things feel light. Messy places become beautiful.

"I can't wait for cleanup time tomorrow," Toby whispered sleepily.

The stars twinkled above like tiny sparkles. They seemed to wink at the little blue train. Toby smiled as he drifted off to sleep, dreaming of golden sparkles and shining tracks.

In the morning, he would be ready for another magical cleanup adventure.

The end.
