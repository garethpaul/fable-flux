# Story story_877

**Benny Bear's Big Day**

Benny Bear lived in a cozy cave on Maple Hill. The cave smelled like honey and warm fur. Benny loved his home very much.

One sunny morning, Mama Bear shook Benny gently. "Wake up, little bear," she said softly. "Today is your first day of school."

Benny's tummy felt funny. It felt like butterflies were dancing inside. His paws felt sweaty and cold.

"I don't want to go to school," Benny whispered. He pulled his soft blanket over his head. "What if the other animals don't like me? What if I can't find the bathroom?"

Mama Bear sat on his bed. The mattress squeaked under her weight. "School will be fun," she said. "You'll make new friends and learn new things."

But Benny's heart beat fast like a drum. He felt very nervous.

Benny had an idea. Maybe if he hid, he wouldn't have to go to school. He crawled under his bed and curled up tight. The floor felt cold and dusty.

"Benny?" called Mama Bear. "Where are you?"

Benny stayed very quiet. He closed his eyes tight.

After a few minutes, his back hurt from the hard floor. Dust made his nose tickle. This hiding spot wasn't working very well.

Mama Bear found him easily. "Come out, sweet bear," she said. "Hiding won't make your worries go away."

Benny crawled out slowly. His fur was covered in dust bunnies. He sneezed three times.

"I'm still scared," Benny said. His voice was very small.

Mama Bear hugged him tight. She smelled like pancakes and love. "Let's try something different," she said. "What if we practice being at school?"

Benny tilted his head. "How?"

Mama Bear smiled. "I'll be your teacher. You can be the student."

They set up chairs in the living room. Mama Bear wore Papa's reading glasses. She looked very teacherly.

"Good morning, Benny," said Mama Bear in a gentle voice. "Welcome to our classroom."

"Good morning, Teacher Mama," said Benny. He felt a little silly but also curious.

They practiced raising paws to ask questions. They practiced walking in a line. They even practiced eating lunch together.

"This is actually fun," said Benny. His butterflies felt smaller now.

Mama Bear taught him a special breathing trick. "When you feel nervous, take three deep breaths," she said. "Like this."

They breathed together slowly. In and out, in and out. Benny's shoulders felt more relaxed.

"What if I forget your breathing trick?" asked Benny.

Mama Bear had another idea. She gave him a small, smooth stone. It fit perfectly in his paw.

"This is your brave stone," she said. "When you feel scared, hold it tight. Remember that you are brave and loved."

The stone felt warm and comforting. Benny squeezed it gently.

Soon it was time for real school. Benny walked down the hill with Mama Bear. His backpack bounced on his shoulders.

At the school door, Benny's tummy felt funny again. He reached into his pocket and held his brave stone. He took three deep breaths.

"I can do this," he whispered to himself.

Inside, he met Mrs. Owl, his teacher. She had kind eyes and a soft voice. "Hello, Benny," she said. "We're so happy you're here."

Benny saw other young animals. There was Rosie Rabbit and Sam Squirrel. They looked a little nervous too.

"Hi," said Benny bravely. "I'm Benny Bear."

"Hi, Benny!" said Rosie. "I like your backpack."

Sam smiled too. "Want to sit with us?"

Benny felt warm inside. These animals seemed very nice.

The day was full of fun activities. They sang songs, painted pictures, and heard a wonderful story. Benny only needed his brave stone twice.

When Mama Bear picked him up, Benny was smiling big. "School was great!" he said. "I made two new friends!"

That night, Benny put his brave stone on his nightstand. Tomorrow was another school day, and he felt ready.

Sometimes the best way to feel brave is to practice being brave.

The end.
