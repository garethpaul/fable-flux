# Story story_1042

**Finny's Red Ball**

Finny the fish lived in a sparkly blue pond. The pond sat right in the middle of a sunny meadow. Tall grass danced around the water. Pretty flowers bloomed everywhere.

Finny loved her special red ball. It was round and bouncy. It was bright like a cherry. Every morning, Finny played with her ball. She would push it with her nose. She would chase it around the pond. The red ball made her very happy.

One windy morning, Finny woke up early. She looked for her favorite red ball. She swam to the left. She swam to the right. She looked under the lily pads. She looked behind the rocks. The red ball was gone!

"Oh no!" said Finny. Her fins felt wobbly. Her heart felt sad. "Where is my red ball?"

Finny swam to the top of the pond. She poked her head above the water. The wind was blowing hard. It made the grass bend and sway. Maybe the wind took her ball away.

Finny saw something red in the tall grass. It was far from the pond. "There it is!" she said. "I found my ball!"

Finny tried to jump out of the water. She wiggled her tail very hard. She pushed with all her might. Splash! She fell back into the pond. Fish cannot live outside of water. Finny felt very frustrated.

"I need help," said Finny. She swam around looking for friends.

Soon she saw Benny the frog. Benny sat on a big green lily pad. He was catching flies with his long tongue.

"Benny!" called Finny. "Can you help me? My red ball is in the grass. I cannot reach it."

Benny's eyes grew big. "I can help!" he said. "I can hop on land and in water."

Benny jumped off his lily pad. Hop, hop, hop! He went toward the tall grass. The grass was very thick. Benny pushed through the green blades. He looked left and right.

"I see something red!" called Benny. He hopped closer. "Oh," he said sadly. "This is just a red flower. Not your ball."

Benny came back to the pond. His green skin looked tired. "Sorry, Finny. I could not find your ball."

Finny felt sad again. But she did not give up. She kept thinking hard. Then she had a new idea.

"Maybe I should ask more friends," said Finny. "More eyes can see better."

Finny called to the meadow animals. "Help! Help! I lost my red ball!"

Soon many friends came to help. Rosie the rabbit hopped over. Charlie the chipmunk scurried down from his tree. Bella the butterfly fluttered near the water.

"We will all look together," said Rosie. Her soft ears twitched with excitement.

The friends spread out across the meadow. They looked behind rocks. They looked under bushes. They searched through the tall grass. Everyone worked as a team.

"Found it!" squeaked Charlie. He was by an old oak tree. "The red ball is stuck in these roots!"

The wind had blown the ball very far. It rolled into the twisted tree roots. The ball was stuck tight.

Working together, the friends freed the red ball. Rosie pulled with her strong paws. Charlie pushed with his tiny hands. Benny used his sticky tongue to grab it. Bella cheered them on.

Pop! The red ball came loose.

Benny carried the ball back to Finny. He gently dropped it in the water. The red ball bobbed up and down. It looked so bright and cheerful.

"Thank you, friends!" said Finny happily. Her fins wiggled with joy. "You are the best helpers ever!"

Finny pushed her red ball with her nose. It felt smooth and perfect. She was so glad to have it back.

"I learned something today," said Finny. "When I have a big problem, I should ask for help. Friends make everything better."

All the meadow friends smiled. They watched Finny play with her red ball. The pond sparkled in the sunshine. Everyone felt warm and happy.

From that day on, Finny always remembered to ask friends for help. And she kept her red ball safe from the wind.

The end.
