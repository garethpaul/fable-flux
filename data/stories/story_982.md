# Story story_982

**The Shy Tiger's Big Problem**

Tiger lived in a cozy living room. He had orange fur with black stripes. His whiskers were long and white. Tiger loved his soft blue cushion by the window.

One sunny morning, Tiger woke up hungry. His tummy made a loud rumble. "Grumble, grumble!" it said. Tiger looked around for his food bowl. 

Oh no! His bowl was stuck under the heavy coffee table. Tiger could see it way back there. The space was too small for him to fit. His striped paws couldn't reach it either.

Tiger felt worried. His tummy rumbled again. He was very hungry but also very shy. The family was eating breakfast in the kitchen. Tiger could hear them talking and laughing.

"I should ask for help," Tiger thought. But his paws felt sweaty. His heart beat fast. What if they were too busy? What if they said no?

Tiger decided to try fixing it himself first. He pushed against the coffee table with his nose. "Oof!" The table was too heavy. It didn't move at all. Tiger's nose felt sore now.

Next, he tried lying on his belly. He stretched his paw as far as possible. His claws barely touched the bowl's edge. "Almost there!" he whispered. But the bowl slipped away even further.

Tiger sat back on his cushion. He felt sad and frustrated. His tummy rumbled louder than before. The morning sun felt warm on his fur, but he couldn't enjoy it.

Then Tiger heard little footsteps. Emma, the youngest family member, walked into the living room. She had curly brown hair and kind eyes. She was carrying her favorite doll.

Emma noticed Tiger sitting sadly by his cushion. "What's wrong, Tiger?" she asked softly. Her voice sounded gentle and caring.

Tiger's ears perked up. Maybe Emma could help! But he still felt nervous. He looked at the coffee table, then back at Emma. He made a small "meow" sound.

Emma followed Tiger's gaze. She saw the food bowl stuck under the table. "Oh! Your breakfast is stuck!" she said. "That's a big problem for a hungry tiger."

Tiger nodded his head. His whiskers twitched hopefully. Emma seemed to understand exactly what he needed.

"Don't worry, Tiger. I'll help you!" Emma said cheerfully. She got down on her hands and knees. Her small hands could fit under the table easily.

"Here it comes!" Emma said. She pulled out Tiger's blue food bowl. It was empty but clean. "Mom!" she called toward the kitchen. "Tiger needs his breakfast!"

Soon, Mom came with Tiger's favorite food. The kibble made pleasant clicking sounds as it filled the bowl. Tiger's mouth watered at the delicious smell.

"Good job asking for help, Tiger," Mom said. She petted his soft fur gently. "Emma is always happy to help her friends."

Tiger purred loudly. The sound rumbled deep in his chest. He felt warm and grateful. Emma had been so kind and helpful.

Tiger ate his breakfast happily. Each bite tasted wonderful. His tummy stopped rumbling. The morning sun felt perfect on his striped fur.

After eating, Tiger rubbed against Emma's legs. This was his way of saying "thank you." Emma giggled and scratched behind his ears.

"You're welcome, Tiger," she said. "Next time you need help, just ask right away. I love helping my friends."

Tiger learned something important that day. Asking for help wasn't scary at all. His family loved him and wanted to help. Being shy had made his problem bigger than it needed to be.

From that day on, Tiger felt braver. When he needed help, he would walk right up to his family. He would meow politely and show them what he needed.

Tiger never went hungry again. His food bowl never stayed stuck under furniture. And Emma became his very best helper and friend.

The living room felt even more like home now. Tiger had learned that asking for help made everything better.

The end.
