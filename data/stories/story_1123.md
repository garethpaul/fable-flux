# Story story_1123

**Mama Bear's Big Problem**

Mama Bear lived in a cozy cave. She had soft brown fur. She loved her three little cubs. Every morning, Mama Bear would sniff the air. She would listen to the birds sing.

But today was different. Mama Bear wrinkled her nose. "Oh my!" she said. "Something smells very bad." She sniffed again. The bad smell was coming from her!

Mama Bear looked at her fur. It was covered in mud. Sticky tree sap stuck to her paws. Her fur felt yucky and gross. "I need a bath," said Mama Bear. "But I don't want to take one!"

The three little cubs came running. "Mama, you smell funny," said the smallest cub. "Like old fish and dirty socks," giggled the middle cub. The biggest cub held his nose. "Pee-yew!"

Mama Bear felt embarrassed. She wanted to be clean. But she really, really didn't want to get wet. The water would be cold. Her fur would get heavy. Taking baths was no fun at all.

"I have an idea," said Mama Bear. She found some sweet-smelling flowers. She rubbed them all over her fur. The purple flowers smelled like candy. "There!" she said. "Now I smell good."

But the flowers didn't work. The bad smell came right through. The cubs still held their noses. "Mama, you still smell yucky," they said. The flowers fell off her dirty fur. They landed on the ground with little plops.

Mama Bear felt sad. She sat down on a log. "What can I do?" she wondered. She really needed that bath. But she was still scared of the cold water.

Then the smallest cub had an idea. "Mama, what if we help you?" The three cubs ran to the stream. They brought back smooth, warm rocks. They had been sitting in the sunshine all morning.

"We can put these in the water," said the middle cub. "They will make it warm!" The biggest cub clapped his paws. "And we can splash with you!"

Mama Bear's eyes lit up. "That sounds wonderful!" she said. The cubs dropped the warm rocks into the stream. Steam rose up like tiny clouds. The water felt nice and toasty.

"Here we go!" said Mama Bear. She stepped into the warm water. It felt good on her paws. The cubs splashed beside her. They giggled and played. They helped scrub Mama's fur with soft leaves.

The mud washed away in brown swirls. The sticky sap melted off her paws. Mama Bear's fur became fluffy and clean. She smelled like fresh pine trees and morning air.

"This is actually fun!" laughed Mama Bear. She splashed water at her cubs. They squealed with joy. The water sparkled in the sunlight. Little fish swam around their feet.

After the bath, Mama Bear felt amazing. Her fur was soft and shiny. She smelled wonderful. The cubs hugged her tight. "Now you smell like flowers and sunshine," they said.

"Thank you for helping me," said Mama Bear. "I was scared of taking a bath. But you made it special." She nuzzled each little cub. "Sometimes scary things become fun when we try together."

From that day on, bath time was family time. Mama Bear and her cubs would gather warm rocks. They would splash and play in the stream. They would help each other stay clean and happy.

Mama Bear learned something important. Problems seem big when we face them alone. But with help and creativity, we can solve anything. Even taking a bath can become an adventure.

The forest was peaceful again. Mama Bear smelled fresh and clean. Her cubs were proud of their smart idea. And they all lived happily ever after in their cozy cave, taking warm, fun baths together every week.
