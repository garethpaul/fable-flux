# Story story_1153

**Bella's Missing Book**

Bella Bear lived in a cozy basement den. She had soft brown fur and kind eyes. Her favorite thing was reading books.

Every night, Bella read her special book. It had pictures of faraway places. Mountains with snow. Oceans with whales. Forests with tall trees. The book made her feel happy and sleepy.

But tonight was different. Bella looked on her reading chair. No book. She looked on her bed. No book there either.

"Oh no!" said Bella. "Where is my special book?"

Bella's tummy felt funny. Her eyes felt watery. She needed her book to fall asleep. Without it, bedtime felt wrong.

The basement was dark and quiet. Bella turned on her small lamp. Yellow light filled the room. She could see her toys and blankets. But still no book.

"I must find it," Bella said to herself. "I cannot sleep without my special book."

Bella decided to look everywhere. She would be like a detective. Detectives always find what they are looking for.

First, Bella checked under her bed. She got down on her hands and knees. The floor felt cold on her paws. She pushed aside her toy box. She moved her winter boots. Dust tickled her nose. She found three marbles and an old sock. But no book.

"Maybe it fell behind something big," thought Bella.

Next, she looked behind her bookshelf. Bella pushed and pushed. The shelf was heavy. Her arms felt tired. Finally, it moved a little bit. She squeezed behind it. Spider webs stuck to her fur. The space smelled musty and old. She found a lost crayon and some paper clips. Still no special book.

Bella sat on her bed. She felt sad and worried. One tear rolled down her furry cheek.

"What if I never find it?" she whispered.

Then Bella remembered something important. Her mama always said, "When you feel stuck, try thinking differently."

Bella closed her eyes. She thought about the last time she read her book. She was sitting in her chair. She felt sleepy. She put the book down and...

"Wait!" said Bella out loud. "I remember now!"

Yesterday, she was very sleepy. She walked to bed with heavy eyes. She was carrying her book. But she was so tired, she might have dropped it.

Bella looked at the path from her chair to her bed. She walked slowly. She looked down at the soft rug. There, between her chair and bed, was a small bump under her fuzzy blanket.

Bella lifted the blanket carefully. Underneath was her special book! The cover showed a beautiful rainbow. The pages smelled like vanilla. Bella hugged the book tight.

"There you are!" she said happily. "I missed you so much!"

Bella climbed into her warm bed. She opened her book to her favorite page. It showed a mama bear and baby bear watching stars. The picture made her smile.

As she read, Bella felt peaceful again. Her worried feelings melted away. The words on the page sounded like a gentle song.

When she finished reading, Bella yawned. She felt ready for sleep now. But this time, she put her book on her bedside table. Right where she could see it.

"Tomorrow I will remember to put you in your safe place," she told her book. "Good night, special book. Thank you for helping me sleep."

Bella turned off her lamp. Moonlight came through her small basement window. It made everything look silver and magical.

As she drifted off to sleep, Bella smiled. She had learned something important tonight. When things seem lost, it helps to stop and think. Sometimes the answer is closer than you think.

And sometimes, the best treasures are hiding right under your nose. Or in Bella's case, right under her favorite blanket.

Bella slept peacefully all night long. She dreamed of all the wonderful places in her special book. And when morning came, her book was right where she left it, waiting for another adventure.

The end.
