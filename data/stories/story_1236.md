# Story story_1236

Ben sat on the bathroom floor. He played with his toy boats. The white tiles felt cool under his legs. Water dripped from the faucet. Drip, drip, drip.

"Sail away, little boat," Ben whispered. He pushed the red boat across the floor. It slid on the smooth tiles. Ben made whooshing sounds with his mouth.

But Ben felt sad inside. Playing alone was not much fun. He wished he had a friend to play with. His shoulders drooped down low.

From behind the big towel came a soft sound. "Grrrr?" 

Ben looked up. A fuzzy brown bear peeked out. The bear had kind black eyes. His fur looked soft and fluffy. The bear was small, just right for Ben.

"Hello," said Ben quietly. "Are you real?"

The bear nodded his furry head. "I'm Benny Bear. Can I play too?"

Ben's eyes grew wide with surprise. "You can talk!"

"Of course I can," said Benny Bear. He stepped out from behind the towel. His paws made tiny tapping sounds on the tiles. "I love boats. Can we sail together?"

Ben's face lit up with joy. "Yes! Come sit with me."

Benny Bear sat down next to Ben. His warm fur tickled Ben's arm. The bear smelled like honey and sunshine. Ben giggled at the tickly feeling.

"This is my red boat," Ben said. He handed it to Benny Bear. "And this is my blue one."

Benny Bear held the red boat carefully. His paws were gentle and soft. "What a beautiful boat! Where should we sail?"

Ben pointed to the bathtub. "Let's sail to Treasure Island!"

They climbed into the empty tub. The white surface was smooth and slippery. Ben turned on just a little water. It made a happy bubbling sound.

"Look out for pirates!" called Benny Bear. He sailed his red boat through the shallow water. Splash, splash went the tiny waves.

"I see one!" Ben shouted with excitement. He sailed his blue boat fast. "Quick! To the secret cove!"

They sailed their boats all around the tub. The water felt warm on their hands. Steam rose up like little clouds. Ben and Benny Bear laughed together.

"This is the best adventure ever," said Ben. His cheeks were pink with happiness.

"I think so too," agreed Benny Bear. "Playing with a friend is wonderful."

After their boat adventure, they played other games. They built a fort with fluffy towels. The towels smelled fresh and clean. They pretended to be brave explorers.

Ben taught Benny Bear how to brush teeth. "Up and down," Ben showed him. "Like this!"

Benny Bear tried with his paws. "This is tricky!" he said. They both giggled at his funny faces in the mirror.

Then they played hide and seek. Benny Bear hid behind the shower curtain. Ben found him right away. The bear's fuzzy ears stuck out too far!

"My turn to hide," said Ben. He squeezed behind the toilet. Benny Bear pretended he could not find him. "Where could Ben be?" the bear wondered aloud.

Ben popped out laughing. "Here I am!"

As the sun began to set, golden light came through the window. It made everything glow warm and cozy. Ben and Benny Bear sat together on the soft bath mat.

"Will you be my best friend?" asked Ben hopefully. His heart felt full of joy.

"Forever and always," promised Benny Bear. He gave Ben a gentle, furry hug. The hug felt safe and warm.

Ben hugged him back tightly. "I'm so happy you came to play."

"Me too," said Benny Bear. "Friends make everything more fun."

They cleaned up their toys together. Working as a team felt good. Ben was not lonely anymore. His heart felt light and happy.

From that day on, Ben and Benny Bear played together every day. They had wonderful adventures in the bathroom and everywhere else. Ben learned that friendship makes ordinary places magical.

The bathroom became their special place. A place where a boy and a bear became the very best of friends.

The end.
