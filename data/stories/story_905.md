# Story story_905

**Nutkin's Magical Snack Time**

The big clock in the kitchen went "tick-tock, tick-tock." It was three o'clock. Time for afternoon snack!

"Nutkin!" called Mama Squirrel from downstairs. "Snack time, little one!"

Nutkin sat on his bed, swishing his fluffy tail. He didn't want snack time today. His tummy felt rumbly, but he wanted to keep playing with his acorn collection.

"I don't want snacks," Nutkin said quietly. He lined up his acorns by size. Big ones, medium ones, tiny ones.

Mama Squirrel climbed up to his room. Her whiskers twitched with a smile. "What's wrong, sweetie?"

"Snack time is boring," Nutkin sighed. "I just sit and eat. I want to play more."

Mama's eyes sparkled like morning dewdrops. "What if we made snack time special today?"

Nutkin's ears perked up. "Special how?"

"Come see," Mama said mysteriously.

Nutkin followed Mama downstairs. The kitchen smelled like warm honey and fresh berries. On the table sat a big wicker basket.

"What's in there?" Nutkin asked, his nose twitching.

"Magic," Mama whispered.

She opened the basket slowly. Inside were small cloth bags tied with colorful ribbons. Each bag had a different smell. Nutkin sniffed carefully.

"This one smells like sunshine!" he said, pointing to a yellow bag.

"And this one smells like rain!" He touched a blue bag.

Mama giggled. "Each bag has a different snack inside. But here's the magic part."

She pulled out a shiny spinner with pictures on it. There was a tree, a flower, a cloud, and a rainbow.

"Spin this," Mama said. "Whatever it lands on tells us where to eat our snack!"

Nutkin's eyes grew wide. He spun the colorful wheel. Round and round it went. It stopped on the tree picture.

"The big oak tree!" Mama clapped her paws.

They grabbed the basket and scampered outside. The oak tree's branches swayed gently in the breeze. Nutkin could hear birds chirping happy songs.

Mama spread a soft blanket under the tree. "Now pick a mystery bag!"

Nutkin chose the yellow bag. Inside were golden honey crackers shaped like leaves. They crunched sweetly in his mouth.

"These taste like sunshine!" Nutkin laughed.

A butterfly landed on their blanket. Its wings were orange and black. Nutkin broke off a tiny cracker piece.

"For you, butterfly friend," he whispered.

The butterfly seemed to dance before flying away. Nutkin felt warm and happy inside.

"Can we spin again?" he asked excitedly.

Mama nodded. This time the spinner landed on the flower. They moved their blanket next to Mama's garden. Purple flowers nodded in the breeze.

Nutkin picked the blue bag. Inside were blueberries that popped sweetly in his mouth. The juice was cool and refreshing.

"I can taste the rain in these!" Nutkin said, his cheeks full.

A friendly bee buzzed nearby. Nutkin giggled and waved hello. The bee seemed to wave back with its tiny wings.

They spun two more times. Under the cloud picture, they found a shady spot by the pond. Nutkin chose green grapes that were crisp and juicy. Under the rainbow picture, they sat by the colorful flower patch. Nutkin discovered strawberries that were sweet and soft.

As the sun began to set, they packed up their magical basket. Nutkin's tummy felt happy and full. His heart felt even fuller.

"Mama, that was the best snack time ever!" Nutkin said, hugging her tight.

"What made it special?" Mama asked.

Nutkin thought carefully. "We went on an adventure. We made friends with the butterfly and bee. And everything tasted better outside!"

Mama smiled proudly. "Tomorrow we can have another snack adventure."

Nutkin jumped up and down. "Really? Can we try the red bag tomorrow?"

"Of course, little one."

That night, Nutkin dreamed of magical snack adventures. He couldn't wait for three o'clock tomorrow. Snack time wasn't boring anymore. It was the most exciting part of his day!

The next morning, Nutkin woke up early. He looked at the clock hopefully. Only eight o'clock. Seven more hours until snack time magic!

The end.
