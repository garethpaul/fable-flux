# Teddy's Big Adventure

Teddy sat on the soft blue couch in the living room. His fuzzy brown fur felt warm in the morning sunlight. The room smelled like mama's lavender soap. But Teddy's button eyes looked very sad.

"I don't want to go," Teddy whispered. His voice was small and worried.

Today was a big day. Teddy was going to spend the whole afternoon at Grandma's house. Mama needed to go to work. But Teddy had never been away from mama for so long before.

His little paws trembled. His tummy felt funny and twisty. "What if I miss mama too much?" he wondered.

Mama sat down beside Teddy. She gently stroked his soft ears. "I know you're scared, little one," she said kindly. "But I have an idea. What if we solve this problem together?"

Teddy looked up with hopeful eyes. "How, Mama?"

"Let's think of ways to feel close to me," Mama suggested. "Even when we're apart."

Teddy's ears perked up a little. He loved solving problems! "Okay," he said bravely.

First, they tried the photo idea. Mama gave Teddy a small picture of them hugging. Teddy held it tight in his paws. The photo was smooth and shiny. It made him smile a tiny bit.

"This helps," Teddy said. "But I still feel sad."

"Let's try something else," Mama encouraged.

Next, Mama sprayed her special perfume on Teddy's favorite blanket. It smelled just like her - sweet and flowery. Teddy buried his nose in the soft fabric. He breathed in deeply.

"Mmm," Teddy sighed. "It smells like hugs."

But his tummy still felt twisty.

"One more idea," Mama said with a gentle smile.

She gave Teddy her old cell phone. "You can call me anytime," she explained. "Just press the big red button. My voice will come right through!"

Teddy's eyes grew wide. "Really? Your voice will travel through this tiny box?"

"Really," Mama laughed. Her laugh sounded like tinkling bells.

But Teddy still felt nervous. His paws were still shaky.

Then Teddy had his own idea. His little face lit up like sunshine. "Mama! What if I take a piece of you with me?"

"What do you mean, sweetie?"

Teddy pointed to his chest. "What if I put your love right here? Inside my heart? Then it can't get lost!"

Mama's eyes sparkled with happy tears. "Oh, Teddy! That's the most wonderful idea ever!"

She wrapped her arms around him tight. "I'm putting extra love in your heart right now," she whispered. "Can you feel it?"

Teddy closed his eyes. He felt warm and tingly inside. "Yes! It feels like sunshine and cookies and bedtime stories!"

"That love will stay with you always," Mama promised. "Even when I'm at work. Even when you're at Grandma's house."

Teddy stood up tall. His chest puffed out proudly. "I can do this!" he declared.

When it was time to go, Teddy felt different. He still missed Mama, but the sad feeling was much smaller now. The love in his heart felt bigger and stronger.

At Grandma's house, Teddy had a wonderful time. He played with blocks. He ate yummy cookies. He even helped Grandma water her flowers.

When Mama picked him up, Teddy ran into her arms. "Mama! I did it! I kept your love safe in my heart the whole time!"

"I'm so proud of you," Mama said, spinning him around. "You solved our problem perfectly!"

That night, as Teddy snuggled in his bed, he felt very proud. He had learned something important. Love doesn't disappear when someone goes away. It stays right inside your heart, warm and safe.

"Tomorrow I can be brave again," Teddy whispered happily. "Because love always comes with me."

He drifted off to sleep with a big smile on his fuzzy face. The love in his heart glowed like a cozy nightlight, keeping him warm all night long.

The end.
