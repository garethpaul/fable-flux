# Story story_1034

**Tommy Truck's Garden Adventure**

Tommy Truck sat in the sandbox. The sun was warm on his red paint. His friend Emma walked over with a big smile.

"Time for garden time, Tommy!" Emma said cheerfully.

Tommy's engine made a quiet rumble. He did not want to go. Gardens were for digging and dirt. Trucks were for racing and zooming.

"I don't like gardens," Tommy said softly. "They're too quiet. Too slow."

Emma patted his bumper gently. "Come on, Tommy. Let's try something new today."

Tommy rolled slowly behind Emma. His wheels made soft sounds on the grass. They walked to the big garden behind the house.

The garden smelled like fresh earth. Green plants grew in neat rows. Tiny flowers peeked out everywhere. A butterfly landed on Tommy's hood.

"What do I do here?" Tommy asked.

Emma picked up a small shovel. "We help the plants grow. We water them. We pull weeds."

Tommy watched Emma dig small holes. She dropped seeds inside. Then she covered them with soft dirt.

"This is boring," Tommy thought.

Suddenly, something tickled his tire. Tommy looked down. A little earthworm wiggled hello.

"Hi there, little friend," Tommy whispered.

The worm disappeared into the soil. Tommy felt curious now. What else lived in this garden?

Emma showed Tommy the watering can. "Want to help water the tomatoes?"

Tommy used his strong bumper to push the watering can. Water sprinkled onto the green plants. The leaves sparkled with tiny drops.

"The plants look happy," Tommy noticed.

A robin hopped nearby. It chirped a sweet song. Tommy chirped back with his horn. The bird tilted its head and chirped again.

"We're talking!" Tommy laughed.

Emma giggled. "Animals love gardens, Tommy."

They moved to the flower section. Bees buzzed around colorful blooms. Their fuzzy bodies were covered in yellow dust.

"What are they doing?" Tommy asked.

"Making honey," Emma explained. "They take pollen from flowers."

Tommy watched the busy bees. They worked so hard. Just like trucks work hard on construction sites.

"Bees are like tiny trucks," Tommy realized.

Emma nodded. "They carry important things from place to place."

Tommy felt proud. He was like the bees. He could help carry things too.

Emma filled a basket with ripe strawberries. The red berries looked like tiny jewels. She gave one to Tommy to taste.

The strawberry was sweet and juicy. Tommy had never tasted anything so fresh.

"This grew right here?" Tommy asked amazed.

"Yes! We planted these seeds last spring."

Tommy looked around the garden with new eyes. Everything here had a purpose. The plants fed the bees. The bees helped plants grow. The worms made the soil healthy.

"I want to help too," Tommy said excitedly.

Emma showed him how to pull weeds. Tommy used his front bumper to push out dandelions. The good plants had more room to grow.

A family of ladybugs crawled onto Tommy's side. Their red shells had black polka dots. They tickled as they walked.

"They eat bad bugs," Emma explained. "They protect our plants."

Tommy felt important. He was helping create a safe home for all these creatures.

The afternoon sun felt warm and peaceful. Tommy listened to all the garden sounds. Bees humming. Birds singing. Leaves rustling in the breeze.

"This is actually nice," Tommy admitted.

Emma smiled. "Gardens are full of surprises."

They watered the last row of vegetables. Tommy's red paint was dusty now. But he felt proud of their work.

"Same time tomorrow?" Emma asked.

Tommy's engine revved happily. "Yes! I want to see if our new seeds sprouted."

As they walked home, Tommy thought about his day. Gardens weren't boring at all. They were full of life and friendship.

"Thank you for showing me," Tommy told Emma.

"Thank you for helping," she replied.

Tommy rolled into the garage feeling content. Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough. He had a garden to tend and friends to visit.

His last thought before falling asleep was about the little earthworm. He hoped to see his wiggly friend again soon.

The end.
