# Story story_1483

**The Friend in the Attic**

Emma heard soft crying sounds above her bedroom. The sounds came from the attic. She climbed the creaky wooden stairs. Each step made a gentle squeak.

In the dusty attic, Emma found Oliver. Oliver was a small, fuzzy creature with big round eyes. He had soft gray fur and tiny paws. Oliver lived in an old shoebox lined with colorful fabric scraps.

"Why are you crying, Oliver?" Emma asked softly.

Thunder rumbled outside. Lightning flashed through the small attic window. Rain pounded on the roof above them.

"The storm is so loud!" Oliver whispered. "The thunder scares me. The lightning is too bright. I can't sleep when it storms."

Emma's heart felt sad for her friend. She wanted to help Oliver feel better. The storm was getting stronger. More thunder boomed overhead.

"Don't worry," Emma said. "I have an idea!"

Emma ran downstairs to her bedroom. She grabbed her favorite blue blanket. The blanket was soft and warm. It smelled like lavender soap.

Emma brought the blanket to Oliver. She wrapped it around his small body. "This will keep you cozy," she said with a smile.

But the thunder still boomed loudly. Oliver still shook with fear. The blanket helped a little bit. But Oliver was still scared of the storm sounds.

"The thunder is still too loud," Oliver said sadly. His ears drooped down. His whiskers trembled.

Emma thought harder. She needed a better solution. Her friend needed more help.

"Wait here," Emma said. "I have another idea!"

Emma hurried back downstairs. She searched through her toy box. She found her small music box. The music box played gentle, sleepy songs.

Emma also grabbed her stuffed elephant. The elephant was gray and squishy. It had a red ribbon around its neck.

Emma climbed back up to the attic. She wound up the music box. Soft, peaceful music began to play. The melody was sweet and calming.

"Listen to this beautiful music," Emma said. She placed the music box near Oliver's shoebox.

Emma also gave Oliver her stuffed elephant. "You can hug Mr. Peanuts," she said. "He always makes me feel brave."

Oliver hugged the soft elephant tight. The music played its gentle tune. When thunder rumbled, the music was still there. When lightning flashed, Oliver squeezed Mr. Peanuts closer.

"The music helps," Oliver said. His voice sounded calmer. "Mr. Peanuts feels so soft and warm."

Emma sat next to Oliver's box. She hummed along with the music box. Her voice was sweet and quiet.

"You know what?" Emma said. "Storms can be scary. But they don't last forever. The rain helps flowers grow. Thunder is just clouds bumping together."

Oliver listened to Emma's words. He felt the soft elephant in his arms. The peaceful music played on and on.

Soon, Oliver's eyes grew heavy. His breathing became slow and steady. The thunder seemed quieter now. The lightning seemed less scary.

"Thank you, Emma," Oliver whispered sleepily. "You are such a good friend. The music and Mr. Peanuts help so much."

Emma smiled warmly. She felt happy inside. Helping her friend made her heart feel full.

The storm continued outside. But now Oliver felt safe and calm. The music box played its lullaby. Mr. Peanuts stayed cozy in Oliver's arms.

"Sweet dreams, Oliver," Emma whispered.

Oliver closed his eyes. He fell asleep peacefully. The storm didn't scare him anymore.

Emma learned something important that night. Sometimes the first idea doesn't work perfectly. But trying again can lead to better solutions. Good friends never give up on each other.

The next morning, sunshine streamed through the attic window. Oliver woke up feeling brave and happy. The storm had passed. Beautiful rainbow colors painted the sky outside.

"Thank you for not giving up on me," Oliver told Emma.

Emma hugged her special attic friend. "That's what friends do," she said with a big smile.

From that day on, Oliver kept the music box and Mr. Peanuts in his shoebox. He never felt scared during storms again.

The end.
