# Story story_1369

**The Missing Blocks**

Tommy loved to play at the big swing set in his backyard. The red swings moved back and forth in the gentle breeze. The yellow slide sparkled in the morning sun. But today, something was very wrong.

"Where are my blocks?" Tommy asked out loud. He looked under the swing set. He looked behind the slide. His favorite wooden blocks were nowhere to be found.

Tommy's blocks were special. They were smooth and colorful. Some were red like fire trucks. Some were blue like the sky. Some were green like grass. He built tall towers with them every day after swinging.

"Mom!" Tommy called. "I can't find my blocks anywhere!"

Mom came outside. Her shoes made soft sounds on the grass. "When did you last see them, sweetie?"

Tommy scratched his head. "Yesterday. I made a castle right here." He pointed to an empty spot near the swings.

"Let's look together," Mom said with a smile.

First, they searched everywhere around the swing set. Tommy crawled under the wooden platform. Dirt stuck to his knees. Mom moved the garden chairs. They lifted up the colorful play mat.

"Not here," Tommy sighed. His shoulders felt heavy.

They looked in the sandbox next. Tommy dug with his small hands. The sand felt cool and grainy. Mom used a little shovel. They found a toy car and two marbles. But no blocks.

"Still not here," Tommy said. His voice sounded sad.

Tommy sat on his favorite swing. It creaked softly as he moved. "Maybe the blocks are gone forever," he whispered.

Mom sat on the swing next to him. "Don't give up yet. Let's think harder. Where else could they be?"

Tommy closed his eyes. He tried to remember yesterday. The sun had been warm. The birds were singing. He was building and building. Then what happened?

"Wait!" Tommy jumped up. "It rained last night! Big drops of water!"

"That's right," Mom said. "What did you do when the rain started?"

Tommy's eyes got wide. "I ran inside super fast! But I forgot something!"

He ran to the back door. There, in a neat pile, sat all his blocks. They were dry and safe on the covered porch.

"I put them here so they wouldn't get wet!" Tommy laughed. "I forgot because I was so excited about the rain!"

Tommy carefully carried his blocks back to the swing set. The wooden pieces felt familiar in his hands. He smelled the sweet scent of the wood.

"Now I can build again!" he said happily.

Tommy started with the red blocks. He made a strong base. Then he added blue blocks for walls. Green blocks became windows. Yellow blocks made a bright roof.

"Look, Mom! It's a house for the swing set!"

Mom clapped her hands. "Beautiful! You worked so hard to find those blocks."

"I almost gave up," Tommy admitted. "But thinking helped me remember."

"That's what good problem solvers do," Mom said. "They keep trying new ideas."

Tommy built tower after tower. Each one was different. Each one was special. The blocks clicked together with satisfying sounds.

As the sun moved across the sky, Tommy played happily. His blocks were safe. His castle was strong. His problem was solved.

"Tomorrow I'll remember to bring them inside if it rains," Tommy said.

"That's very smart thinking," Mom agreed.

Tommy gave his mom a big hug. Then he went back to building. The swing set creaked in the breeze. The blocks made colorful patterns in the grass. Everything was perfect again.

That night, Tommy carefully carried each block inside. He counted them one by one. All twenty-four blocks were safe and sound.

"Good night, blocks," he whispered. "See you tomorrow for more building fun!"

Tommy learned that giving up is never the answer. Sometimes the best solution comes from taking time to think. And sometimes, what seems lost is just waiting to be remembered.

The next day, Tommy built the biggest castle ever. Right next to his favorite swing set, under the warm sunshine.

The end.
