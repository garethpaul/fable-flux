# Daisy Duck and Moo's Library Adventure

Daisy Duck waddled quietly through the big library. Her orange feet made soft *pat-pat* sounds on the smooth floor. She looked at all the tall bookshelves filled with colorful books.

"I wish I had a friend," Daisy whispered sadly. She found a cozy reading corner with soft purple cushions. Daisy picked up a picture book about farm animals and sat down alone.

The book was heavy in her small wings. She tried to hold it up, but it kept slipping. *Thump!* The book fell to the floor. Daisy sighed and picked it up again.

Just then, she heard gentle *clip-clop* sounds coming closer. A friendly cow with black and white spots peeked around the bookshelf.

"Hi there!" said the cow cheerfully. "I'm Moo. Are you reading by yourself?"

Daisy looked up with bright eyes. "Yes, but this book is too heavy for me. I can't hold it up."

Moo smiled warmly. "Maybe I can help! I love books too. Can we read together?"

"Really?" Daisy's voice got excited. "That would be wonderful!"

Moo sat down carefully beside Daisy. The cow was much bigger, so she could hold the book easily with her hooves. Daisy snuggled close to see the pictures.

"Look!" Daisy pointed with her wing. "This duck looks just like me!"

"And that cow has spots like mine!" Moo laughed. Her laugh sounded like gentle bells ringing.

They turned each page slowly together. Daisy made *quack-quack* sounds when they saw ducks. Moo made soft *moo* sounds when they found cows. Soon they were both giggling happily.

"This is so much more fun than reading alone," Daisy said, flapping her wings with joy.

When they finished the first book, Moo had an idea. "Let's find more books together! I can reach the high shelves."

"And I can look at the low shelves!" Daisy added excitedly.

They walked through the library together. Moo stretched her long neck to see books up high. Daisy waddled low to check books near the floor. Soon they had a wonderful pile of books to share.

They found books about friendship, adventures, and silly songs. Each book was better because they read it together. When words were hard, they helped each other sound them out.

"Moo, you're such a good friend," Daisy said warmly. "Reading is much better with you here."

"I think so too, Daisy," Moo replied. "We make a great team!"

They discovered they both loved books about adventures. They both liked stories with happy endings. They both enjoyed making funny voices for different characters.

As the afternoon sun shone through the big library windows, Daisy and Moo built a fort with soft cushions. They made it just right for both of them - low enough for Daisy, wide enough for Moo.

Inside their cozy fort, they read their favorite story three times. They took turns reading different parts. Daisy did all the duck voices. Moo did all the cow voices. Together, they did all the other animal sounds.

Other children in the library smiled when they heard the happy laughter coming from the reading corner.

"Tomorrow, let's come back and read more books together," Moo suggested as they cleaned up their cushion fort.

"Yes!" Daisy quacked happily. "And maybe we can act out our favorite stories!"

"That sounds perfect," Moo agreed.

They carefully put all their books back where they belonged. Working together made cleanup quick and easy.

As they walked toward the library door together, Daisy felt warm and happy inside. She wasn't lonely anymore. She had found a wonderful friend who loved books just as much as she did.

"Thank you for sharing the books with me, Moo," Daisy said gratefully.

"Thank you for making reading so much fun, Daisy," Moo replied with a gentle smile.

They promised to meet at the library again tomorrow. Both friends went home with hearts full of joy, already excited about their next reading adventure together.

From that day on, Daisy and Moo were the very best of library friends.

The end.
