# Story story_611

**Blocks and the Magic Walk**

It was time for walk time at Little Sprouts Daycare. Miss Sarah clapped her hands together. "Everyone line up!" she called cheerfully. "Time to go outside!"

All the children jumped up from their activities. Everyone except Blocks. Blocks was a little boy who loved building towers. He had shiny brown hair and bright green eyes. Right now, those eyes looked very sad.

Blocks sat on the carpet. His tower of red and blue blocks reached almost to his chin. "I don't want to go," he whispered. "My tower isn't finished yet."

Miss Sarah knelt down beside him. Her voice was soft and kind. "I know you love building, Blocks. But fresh air will help you think better."

Blocks sighed a big sigh. He carefully put his blocks away. His shoulders drooped as he walked to the line. The other children were chatting and giggling. Blocks just stared at his sneakers.

Outside, the sun felt warm on everyone's faces. A gentle breeze made the trees dance. Birds chirped happy songs in the branches above.

Miss Sarah led the group down the sidewalk. "Look around," she said. "What do you see?"

Emma pointed to a yellow flower. "Pretty!" she said.

Jake found a smooth rock. "Shiny!" he announced.

But Blocks kept looking down. He kicked a small pebble with his toe. Walking felt boring. He missed his blocks already.

Then something caught his eye. There, next to the sidewalk, sat a pile of smooth stones. They were different sizes and colors. Gray ones, brown ones, and even some with white stripes.

Blocks stopped walking. He picked up one stone. It felt cool and smooth in his hand. Then he picked up another. This one was perfectly round.

"Miss Sarah," Blocks called out. "Can I collect these?"

Miss Sarah smiled. "Of course! But we need to keep walking. You can carry them with us."

Blocks filled his pockets with the special stones. As the group walked further, he found more treasures. A pinecone that looked like a tiny tower. A stick that was perfectly straight. Some acorns that rolled like little balls.

"Look what I found!" Blocks showed Emma his collection. "I can build with these too!"

Emma's eyes grew wide. "Wow! Can you teach me?"

Soon other children gathered around Blocks. They all wanted to find building treasures too. The walk became a treasure hunt. Everyone searched for interesting shapes and sizes.

"This stick looks like a bridge!" said Jake.

"These leaves could be roofs!" added Emma.

Blocks felt excitement bubbling in his chest. His pockets bulged with natural building supplies. He had never thought about building with things from outside before.

When they reached the park, Miss Sarah spread out a big blanket. "Free play time!" she announced.

Blocks dumped his treasures on the blanket. The other children brought their finds too. Soon they had a wonderful collection of natural building materials.

Blocks started stacking stones carefully. He balanced a pinecone on top. Then he added the straight stick as a flag pole. "It's a castle!" he said proudly.

Emma helped him build a wall with small rocks. Jake added a bridge made from a curved stick. Other children joined in too. They built an entire village together.

The sun warmed their backs as they worked. Bees buzzed nearby flowers. The grass felt soft under their knees. Everything smelled fresh and clean.

"Time to go back," Miss Sarah called eventually.

Blocks looked at their amazing outdoor creation. His heart felt full and happy. "Can we take some treasures back?" he asked.

"Absolutely," said Miss Sarah. "You can use them inside too."

As they walked back to daycare, Blocks chatted excitedly with his friends. They planned what to build tomorrow. Maybe a bridge! Or a tall tower!

Back in the classroom, Blocks carefully arranged his nature treasures next to his regular blocks. He couldn't wait to mix them together.

When his mom came to pick him up, Blocks ran to her. "Mom! I found the best building supplies ever! And tomorrow we get to go on another treasure walk!"

His mom smiled at his glowing face. "That sounds wonderful, sweetie."

Blocks hugged his nature collection close. Tomorrow's walk time couldn't come soon enough.

The end.
