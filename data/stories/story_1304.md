# Story story_1304

Emma heard Mommy's gentle voice. "Time for bed, sweetie."

Emma was playing with her toy horses. The brown one was jumping over blocks. The white one was eating pretend grass. She didn't want to stop playing.

"Five more minutes?" Emma asked. Her bottom lip stuck out a little.

"The horses need sleep too," Mommy said softly. "They've had a big day."

Emma looked at her toys. Maybe the horses were tired. She picked up the brown one. Its mane felt silky between her fingers.

"Come on, little horse," she whispered. "Let's go upstairs."

In the bathroom, Emma brushed her teeth. The mint toothpaste tasted cool and fresh. She made funny faces in the mirror. The toothbrush tickled her gums.

"Don't forget behind your back teeth," Mommy reminded her.

Emma giggled and brushed extra hard. Foam bubbled around her mouth like a white beard.

Next came pajamas. Emma chose her favorite ones. They had tiny stars all over them. The fabric felt soft and cozy against her skin.

"These stars glow in the dark," Emma told her horse. She held it close to her chest.

In her bedroom, Mommy pulled back the covers. The sheets smelled like lavender from the wash. Emma's pillow was fluffy and cool.

"I'm not sleepy yet," Emma said. She sat on the edge of her bed.

"Let's read a story," Mommy suggested. "Maybe about magical horses."

Emma's eyes lit up. "Really?"

Mommy picked up a book from the shelf. The cover showed a silver horse with a flowing mane. Sparkles danced around its hooves.

"Once upon a time," Mommy began, "there lived a special horse named Starlight."

Emma snuggled closer. The book's pages felt smooth under her fingers. The pictures were full of bright colors.

"Starlight could fly through the night sky," Mommy read. "She visited children's dreams."

"Like me?" Emma asked.

"Just like you," Mommy smiled.

As the story continued, something magical happened. Emma's toy horse seemed to shimmer in the lamplight. Its plastic eyes looked almost real.

"Look, Mommy!" Emma pointed. "My horse is glowing!"

The tiny stars on Emma's pajamas began to glow too. They twinkled like real stars in the sky. The whole room felt magical and peaceful.

"Starlight is here," Mommy whispered. "She's making everything special for bedtime."

Emma felt sleepy now. Her eyelids were getting heavy. The soft blanket wrapped around her like a warm hug.

"Will Starlight visit my dreams?" Emma yawned.

"I think she will," Mommy said. "Close your eyes and listen."

Emma closed her eyes. She could almost hear gentle hoofbeats. They sounded like a lullaby in the quiet room.

Mommy sang softly. Her voice was warm and soothing. "Twinkle, twinkle, little star..."

Emma's breathing became slow and deep. The toy horse rested beside her pillow. Its mane tickled her cheek gently.

"Sweet dreams, my little star," Mommy whispered. She kissed Emma's forehead. The kiss felt warm and safe.

"Tomorrow can we play horses again?" Emma mumbled sleepily.

"Of course we can," Mommy promised. "Starlight will be waiting."

Emma smiled in the darkness. Tomorrow would bring new adventures. Maybe they could build a stable for the horses. Or create a magical forest with pillows and blankets.

The glowing stars on her pajamas faded slowly. But Emma felt their magic all around her. Bedtime wasn't scary anymore. It was the beginning of wonderful dreams.

Outside her window, real stars twinkled in the sky. They seemed to wink at Emma. Even the moon smiled down at her.

Emma drifted off to sleep. In her dreams, she rode Starlight through clouds of silver and gold. They galloped over rainbow bridges. They danced with other magical horses in a field of flowers.

When morning came, Emma would wake up happy. She would remember her magical dream. And she would look forward to bedtime again.

Because bedtime meant dreams. And dreams meant magic. And magic meant anything was possible.

The end.
