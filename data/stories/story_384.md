# The Library Friends

Benny the bunny sat alone in the quiet library. His soft brown ears drooped down sadly. He pushed a small wooden train around the carpet. The train made quiet "choo-choo" sounds as it moved.

"This isn't very fun," Benny whispered to himself. The library felt big and empty. Other children played in different corners. But Benny stayed by himself.

He looked at the colorful picture books on the low shelves. He saw the cozy reading corner with fluffy pillows. Everything looked nice, but playing alone made him feel lonely.

Anna walked by carrying a stack of books. She had curly red hair and bright green shoes. She noticed Benny sitting quietly with his train.

"Hi," Anna said softly. "Can I play with you?"

Benny's ears perked up a little. "You want to play trains?"

"Yes! I love trains," Anna smiled. She sat down on the soft carpet next to Benny. "What's your train's name?"

"His name is Whistle," Benny said. His voice got a bit louder. "He likes to go fast."

Anna clapped her hands together. "That's a perfect name! Can Whistle take me on a trip?"

Benny's eyes lit up. "Where do you want to go?"

"To the moon!" Anna giggled. "Do you think Whistle can fly?"

"Of course he can!" Benny pushed the train high in the air. "Zoom! Off to the moon we go!"

Anna made rocket sounds. "Whoosh! Look at all the stars!"

They took turns pushing Whistle around the carpet. Anna made the train visit a magical castle. Benny made it swim through an ocean of books.

"Let's build a train station," Anna suggested. She gathered some blocks from a nearby shelf.

"Great idea!" Benny hopped excitedly. "We need a big station for Whistle."

Together, they stacked red and blue blocks. Anna made the walls tall and strong. Benny added a special platform for passengers. They worked side by side, sharing ideas and blocks.

"Your station needs a roof," Anna said. She carefully placed a flat block on top.

"And yours needs a door," Benny added. He found a perfect yellow block for the entrance.

When they finished, their train station looked amazing. It had colorful walls, a sturdy roof, and two platforms. Whistle could visit both sides.

"Wow!" said Benny. "It's so much better than I could make alone."

"Building together is more fun," Anna agreed. "And faster too!"

They played with their station for a long time. Whistle picked up pretend passengers. Anna made ticket-taking sounds. Benny announced train departures.

"All aboard the friendship express!" Benny called out happily.

"Next stop, the land of fun!" Anna announced with a big smile.

Other children in the library started watching their game. Soon, two more kids asked to join. The train station grew bigger and bigger.

Mrs. Chen, the librarian, smiled as she watched them play. "What wonderful cooperation," she whispered to herself.

When it was time to clean up, Anna and Benny worked together again. They carefully put each block back in its place. They made sure Whistle was safe in the toy box.

"Thank you for playing with me," Benny said. His ears stood up tall and proud. "I was feeling lonely before you came."

"Thank you for sharing Whistle with me," Anna replied. "Playing together made everything more exciting!"

They sat together in the reading corner. Anna picked out a book about trains. Benny found one about friendship.

"Can we play again tomorrow?" Anna asked hopefully.

"Yes!" Benny nodded eagerly. "We can build an even bigger station!"

They gave each other a gentle hug. Anna's books felt lighter in her arms. Benny's heart felt warm and happy.

As they walked toward the library door, both friends were already planning their next adventure. The library didn't feel big and empty anymore. It felt like a magical place where friendships begin.

"See you tomorrow, friend," Anna waved goodbye.

"See you tomorrow!" Benny waved back, his ears bouncing with joy.

Both friends skipped home, excited about their new friendship and all the fun they would have together.

The end.
