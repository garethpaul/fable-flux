# Story story_1146

**Tiger and Frog at the Beach**

Tiger sat on the warm sand. The sun made his orange stripes glow. He built a tall sandcastle all by himself. The waves went whoosh, whoosh on the shore.

Tiger felt sad. He had no friends to play with. His castle looked pretty, but it was lonely work. He wished someone would come play.

The sand felt hot under his paws. Seagulls called overhead. Tiger sighed a big sigh.

Behind a rock sat Frog. Frog was small and green. He watched Tiger build the castle. It looked like so much fun!

Frog wanted to help. But Tiger was big. Frog was tiny. Would Tiger want to be friends?

Frog took a deep breath. He hopped closer to Tiger. Hop, hop, hop across the sand.

"Hello, Tiger," said Frog in his small voice.

Tiger looked down. He saw the little green frog. Frog's eyes were big and kind.

"Hi there," said Tiger. "I'm building a castle."

"It looks wonderful," said Frog. "Can I help you?"

Tiger's heart felt warm. Someone wanted to play with him!

"Yes, please!" said Tiger. "I would love help."

Frog smiled his biggest smile. His webbed feet were perfect for patting sand. Tiger used his big paws to pile sand high.

They worked together. Frog made small towers. Tiger made big walls. The castle grew and grew.

"This is the best castle ever!" said Frog.

Tiger nodded. "It's better with two friends building it."

The warm sun shone on them. Cool ocean breezes felt nice. Their castle had tall towers and deep moats.

But then a big wave came. Splash! It knocked down one wall.

"Oh no!" cried Frog.

"Don't worry," said Tiger. "We can fix it together."

They rebuilt the wall. It was even better than before. Tiger and Frog high-fived with paw and webbed foot.

"Let's make a moat," said Frog.

"Great idea!" said Tiger.

Frog dug with his little hands. Tiger scooped with his big paws. Soon water filled their moat. It sparkled in the sunshine.

They found pretty shells. Pink ones and white ones. They decorated their castle walls. The shells made patterns in the sand.

"Look what I found!" called Frog.

He held up a shiny piece of sea glass. It was blue like the ocean. Tiger found a smooth white stone.

They added treasures to their castle. It looked magical now.

"I'm getting hungry," said Tiger.

"Me too," said Frog.

Tiger had packed a lunch. He had fish sandwiches and fruit. Frog had brought flies and lily pad snacks.

"Want to share?" asked Tiger.

"Yes, please!" said Frog.

They sat in the shade. The sand felt cool here. They shared their food and told stories. Tiger told funny jokes. Frog sang sweet songs.

After lunch, they played in the waves. Tiger splashed with his big paws. Frog dove under the water. The ocean felt refreshing and fun.

They chased seagulls together. They collected more shells. They drew pictures in the wet sand.

As the sun got lower, it painted the sky orange and pink. The colors matched Tiger's stripes.

"This was the best day," said Frog.

"I agree," said Tiger. "I was lonely before you came."

"I was scared to ask to play," said Frog. "But I'm glad I did."

Tiger smiled. "Friends come in all sizes."

"And friends make everything better," said Frog.

They watched the sunset together. The sky turned purple and gold. Stars started to twinkle.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" asked Tiger.

"Of course!" said Frog. "We're beach buddies now."

They hugged goodbye. Tiger's hug was big and warm. Frog's hug was small but full of love.

Tiger walked home happy. His heart felt full. He had found a wonderful friend.

Frog hopped home singing. Tomorrow would bring another adventure with Tiger.

The waves whispered goodnight. The beach held their castle safe. And two new friends dreamed of sunny days ahead.

From that day on, Tiger and Frog played together every day. The beach was never lonely again.

The end.
