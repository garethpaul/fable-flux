# Ducky's Musical Morning

The morning sun peeked through the curtains. Mama Duck fluffed her feathers cheerfully. "Ducky, it's music time!" she called.

Ducky sat by his toy box. He looked at his little drums. He looked at his shiny triangle. Music time happened every morning. But today felt different somehow.

"I don't want to play music," Ducky said softly. His orange feet shuffled on the wooden floor. "It's the same songs every day."

Mama Duck waddled over gently. She sat beside her little duckling. "Sometimes new things happen," she said. "Even during music time."

Ducky picked up his drumsticks slowly. They felt heavy in his wings. He tapped once on the drum. *Tap.* The sound was quiet and sad.

"Try tapping like raindrops," Mama suggested. Her eyes twinkled with a secret smile.

Ducky tapped again. *Tap, tap, tap.* It did sound like rain! He tapped faster. *Tap-tap-tap-tap-tap!* Now it sounded like a spring shower.

"Listen," whispered Mama Duck. She cupped her wing around her ear.

From outside came a gentle sound. *Pitter-patter, pitter-patter.* Real raindrops were dancing on the roof! They matched Ducky's drumming perfectly.

Ducky's eyes grew wide and bright. "The rain is playing with me!" he quacked excitedly.

He grabbed his triangle next. *Ding!* The clear, sweet sound rang out. From the garden came an answering sound. *Tweet-tweet!* A little robin was singing back!

"The birds want to join our band!" Ducky giggled. His tail feathers wiggled with joy.

Mama Duck picked up her wooden flute. She played a gentle, flowing melody. It sounded like wind through the trees. And sure enough, the wind outside began to whistle softly through the leaves.

Ducky clapped his wings together. *Clap, clap, clap!* Thunder rumbled far away. *Rumble, rumble, rumble!* It was like nature's own drum solo.

"We're making music with the whole world!" Ducky exclaimed. He hopped from foot to foot.

The rain grew stronger. The birds sang louder. The wind played its leafy song. And in their cozy living room, Ducky and Mama Duck played along.

Ducky tried his maracas. *Shake, shake, shake!* They sounded like leaves rustling. He rang his small bell. *Ring-a-ling!* It sounded like dewdrops falling.

Every instrument had a friend outside. Every sound found an echo in nature. The house filled with the most beautiful music Ducky had ever heard.

"This is magical!" Ducky laughed. He spun in a circle, holding his triangle high. The whole world was his orchestra!

As the rain began to slow, so did their music. The birds sang softer lullabies. The wind whispered gentle goodbyes. Ducky played quieter and quieter until only peaceful silence remained.

"That was the best music time ever," Ducky sighed happily. He hugged his drum tight. "I never knew the world wanted to play with us."

Mama Duck smiled warmly. "Music is everywhere," she said. "In raindrops and birdsongs. In wind and thunder. Even in quiet moments."

Ducky carefully put away his instruments. But he left them where he could see them. Each one now felt like a special friend.

That night, Ducky dreamed of symphonies. He dreamed of conducting clouds and dancing with dewdrops. He dreamed of music floating on every breeze.

When morning came, Ducky woke up early. He looked at his instruments with bright, eager eyes. He wondered what nature sounds would join him today.

Maybe the bees would hum along. Maybe the creek would babble a rhythm. Maybe even the flowers would sway to his melodies.

"Mama!" Ducky called excitedly. "Is it music time yet?"

Mama Duck chuckled softly. "Almost, little one."

Ducky couldn't wait to discover what magical music the new day would bring. Every morning was now full of musical possibilities.

Music time would never be ordinary again.

The end.
