# Story story_727

**Woolly's Big School Day**

Woolly the sheep loved playing in the sandbox. The warm sand felt soft between her hooves. She built tall castles and deep tunnels every day.

But tomorrow was different. Tomorrow was her first day of school.

"I don't want to go," Woolly whispered to herself. Her fluffy white wool trembled. "What if the other animals don't like me? What if I can't find my classroom?"

Woolly's tummy felt all twisty inside. She pushed the sand around with her hoof. The sandbox felt safe and cozy. School felt big and scary.

Her friend Benny the bunny hopped over. "Why do you look sad?" he asked.

"I'm nervous about school tomorrow," Woolly said. Her voice was very quiet. "I wish I could stay in the sandbox forever."

Benny's ears wiggled. "Maybe you need practice being brave," he said. "Let's pretend the sandbox is school!"

Woolly liked this idea. She built a sand school with her hooves. She made classrooms and hallways. She even made tiny sand desks.

"Now walk to your classroom," Benny said.

Woolly took one step. Then another step. But her legs felt wobbly. Her heart beat very fast. She sat down in the sand with a thump.

"This isn't working," she said. "I still feel scared."

The warm sun shone on Woolly's wool. Birds chirped in the nearby trees. The sandbox smelled like summer and adventure.

"Let's try something else," said Benny. His nose twitched thoughtfully. "What makes you feel brave and happy?"

Woolly thought hard. "Playing in the sand makes me happy. Building things makes me feel strong."

"Perfect!" Benny clapped his paws together. "Let's make a special brave-sheep charm!"

Together, they gathered the smoothest pebbles from the sandbox. Woolly picked her three favorites. One was white like her wool. One was brown like rich soil. One was gray like storm clouds.

"These pebbles will remind you that you're brave," Benny explained. "The white one is for your kind heart. The brown one is for staying grounded. The gray one is for being strong during storms."

Woolly held the pebbles tight in her hoof. They felt warm and smooth. "Tell me more," she said.

"When you feel nervous tomorrow, just touch your pebbles," Benny said. "Remember how you build amazing sand castles. Remember how you solve problems. Remember how many friends love you."

Woolly practiced walking around the sandbox. She held her pebbles in her pocket. With each step, she felt a little braver. The pebbles reminded her of all the wonderful things she could do.

"I built the tallest sand tower yesterday," she said proudly. "And I helped fix the broken sandbox gate last week."

"Exactly!" Benny cheered. "You're already brave and helpful."

That evening, Woolly put her special pebbles in her school bag. She felt excited instead of scared. The pebbles were her secret helpers.

The next morning came bright and sunny. Woolly walked to school with her head held high. Her wool was fluffy and clean. Her pebbles jingled softly in her bag.

At school, she met a shy little lamb named Luna. Luna looked nervous and alone.

"Hi," Woolly said gently. "I'm Woolly. Want to be friends?"

Luna's eyes lit up. "Yes, please! I was feeling scared."

Woolly reached into her bag. She gave Luna the brown pebble. "This will help you feel brave," she said. "It worked for me."

During art time, Woolly and Luna built a sand table together. They laughed and shared stories. Soon, other animals joined them.

By lunchtime, Woolly had three new friends. She felt proud and happy. The scary feelings were all gone.

"School is actually fun," she told Benny later. "I was worried for nothing."

"You were brave when you tried again," Benny said. "That's what made the difference."

Woolly smiled wide. Tomorrow, she couldn't wait to go back to school. She still loved her sandbox, but now she loved learning too.

Sometimes the scariest things turn into the most wonderful adventures.

The end.
