# Grandma Joins the Fun

Grandma Rose sat on the green park bench. She watched her grandson Tommy play. The playground buzzed with happy children. They ran and laughed together.

"Tag, you're it!" shouted a girl with pigtails.

The children raced around the swings. They climbed up the slide. Tommy giggled as he chased his friends.

Grandma Rose smiled. But inside, she felt a little sad. The children were having so much fun. She wanted to play too.

"I wish I could join them," she whispered.

But the children didn't notice her. They were too busy playing their game. Grandma Rose felt left out.

She watched Tommy tag a boy. The boy laughed and ran away. All the children were part of something special. Grandma Rose was just sitting alone.

"Maybe I'm too old to play," she thought.

But Grandma Rose didn't give up. She had an idea.

She stood up slowly. Her knees made a little creak. She walked closer to the children.

"Excuse me," she said softly.

The children kept running. They didn't hear her quiet voice. The wind carried her words away.

Grandma Rose tried again. This time she spoke louder.

"Hello, children!"

A few kids looked over. But they quickly went back to their game. They didn't know what to say to the grandma.

Grandma Rose felt her cheeks turn pink. Maybe this wasn't working. But she remembered something important. Tommy's mom always said, "Keep trying, and good things will happen."

So Grandma Rose thought harder. What could she do differently?

She noticed the children were getting tired. They were breathing hard from all the running. Some sat down to rest.

That's when Grandma Rose had her best idea yet.

She walked to her big purse. Inside were juice boxes and crackers. She always brought snacks for Tommy.

"Who wants a snack break?" she called out cheerfully.

The children's eyes lit up. Running made them thirsty and hungry.

"Me! Me!" they shouted.

Soon, all the children gathered around Grandma Rose. She handed out juice boxes and crackers. The apple juice was cold and sweet. The crackers were crunchy and salty.

"Thank you!" said the girl with pigtails.

"You're the best grandma ever!" said Tommy.

While they ate, Grandma Rose told a funny story. It was about a squirrel who forgot where he hid his nuts. The children giggled at all the silly parts.

"Tell us another one!" they begged.

After snack time, something wonderful happened. The children asked Grandma Rose to play with them.

"Will you be the snack monster?" asked Tommy.

"The snack monster?" Grandma Rose asked.

"Yes! You chase us, and when you catch someone, you give them a hug and a snack!"

Grandma Rose's heart felt warm and happy. "I would love to be the snack monster!"

She put her hands up like claws. "Roar! I'm coming to get you!"

The children squealed with delight. They ran around the playground. Grandma Rose chased them slowly. Her gray hair bounced as she moved.

When she caught the girl with pigtails, she gave her a gentle hug. Then she handed her a small cookie.

"The snack monster got me!" the girl laughed.

All afternoon, they played together. Grandma Rose felt included and happy. The children loved having her in their game.

As the sun began to set, parents came to pick up their children.

"Thank you for playing with us, Grandma Rose!" they called out.

"Thank you for letting me join your fun," she replied.

Tommy hugged his grandma tight. "You're the best snack monster ever!"

Walking home, Grandma Rose felt proud. She had felt left out at first. But she didn't give up. She kept trying new ways to connect.

Sometimes the first try doesn't work. Sometimes the second try doesn't work either. But if you keep trying and use your heart, you'll find a way.

Grandma Rose learned that being included isn't about age. It's about being kind, creative, and caring.

And sometimes, the best way to join in is to bring something special to share.

The end.
