# Nutkin's Garden Discovery

The morning sun peeked through the oak tree. Nutkin the squirrel stretched his fluffy tail. "Garden time!" called Mama Squirrel cheerfully.

Nutkin's ears drooped down. Every day was the same. Dig holes. Plant seeds. Water plants. Pull weeds. "Do I have to?" he sighed.

"Gardens need care every day," Mama said gently. "Just like you need breakfast every day."

Nutkin dragged his tiny paws slowly. The garden looked boring and brown. Dirt everywhere. Nothing exciting at all.

"Let's start with watering," Mama suggested. She handed Nutkin a small watering can. It was painted bright yellow like sunshine.

Nutkin poured water on the tomato plants. Drip, drip, drip went the water. Suddenly, a tiny green shoot wiggled. "Mama! Look!" Nutkin squeaked excitedly.

"The seeds you planted yesterday are growing!" Mama smiled. "You helped make that happen."

Nutkin's eyes grew wide with wonder. He watered more plants carefully. Each drop made the leaves dance. The garden smelled fresh and earthy.

"Now let's check the berry bushes," Mama said. They walked to the corner together.

Hidden under green leaves were tiny red strawberries! "I remember planting these!" Nutkin chattered. "They were just little seeds then."

"You've been watering them for weeks," Mama nodded. "Your daily care helped them grow."

Nutkin picked one perfect strawberry carefully. It was sweet and juicy. "This tastes like sunshine!" he giggled.

Next, they pulled weeds from the carrot patch. Nutkin found it was like a treasure hunt. Each weed he pulled revealed orange carrot tops. "The carrots are growing bigger!" he discovered.

A butterfly landed on the sunflower nearby. Its wings were orange and black. "Hello, butterfly!" Nutkin whispered softly. The butterfly seemed to wave hello.

"Gardens are full of surprises," Mama said. "But you have to visit every day to see them."

Nutkin understood now. Each day brought something new. Yesterday's seeds became today's green shoots. Last week's buds became today's flowers.

They planted new seeds together. Nutkin made perfect little holes. He covered each seed gently. "Grow big and strong," he whispered to them.

A robin hopped over to investigate. "Tweet, tweet!" it sang happily. "I think she's saying thank you," Mama laughed. "Birds love gardens too."

Nutkin scattered birdseed near the flowers. Three more birds flew down quickly. Soon the garden was full of cheerful chirping.

"Gardens bring friends together," Nutkin realized. He felt proud and happy inside.

They finished by organizing the garden tools. Nutkin wiped dirt off the small shovel. Everything had its special place.

"Tomorrow the seeds will grow more," Mama said. "The strawberries will get redder. The carrots will get bigger."

Nutkin's tail twitched with excitement now. "And maybe new butterflies will visit! And the robin might bring friends!"

"Every garden day is different," Mama agreed. "Even though we do the same things."

That evening, Nutkin drew pictures of everything. The dancing water drops. The surprise strawberries. The friendly butterfly. The singing birds.

"I can't wait for tomorrow!" Nutkin told Papa Squirrel. "I want to see what happens next in our garden."

Papa smiled warmly. "Sounds like someone discovered garden magic today."

"It's not boring anymore," Nutkin said. "It's like... like reading a new page in a story book every day!"

Nutkin fell asleep dreaming happily. He dreamed of growing plants. Singing birds. Sweet strawberries. Colorful butterflies dancing everywhere.

The next morning, Nutkin woke up early. Sunlight was just beginning to shine. "Is it garden time yet?" he called out excitedly.

Mama peeked into his room. "Someone's eager today!" she laughed. "Yes, it's garden time!"

Nutkin raced outside with his yellow watering can. The garden was waiting for him. Full of growing surprises and daily magic.

Every ordinary day had become an extraordinary adventure.

The end.
