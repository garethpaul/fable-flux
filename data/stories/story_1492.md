# Story story_1492

**Teddy's Brave Night**

Teddy Bear loved his swing set. Every day, he swung high in the sunshine. The warm sun tickled his fuzzy brown fur. Birds sang happy songs in the trees above.

But when night came, everything changed. The swing set looked scary in the dark. Long shadows danced around the yard. Strange sounds came from the bushes. Teddy's button eyes grew wide with worry.

"I don't like the dark," Teddy whispered. He hugged his arms tight around himself. The moon hid behind thick clouds. The swing chains creaked in the wind.

Teddy wanted to swing at night too. The cool air felt nice on his fur. But the darkness made his stuffing feel all jumpy inside. What if something was hiding in the shadows?

"I need to be brave," Teddy said to himself. He had an idea. Maybe if he closed his eyes, the dark wouldn't seem so scary.

Teddy climbed onto his favorite swing. He squeezed his button eyes shut tight. Then he pushed off with his fuzzy paws. Back and forth he went, eyes closed.

But this didn't work at all! With his eyes closed, Teddy felt even more scared. He couldn't see where he was going. The swing wobbled and shook. Teddy's ears flapped in the wind.

"This is worse!" Teddy cried. He opened his eyes quickly. The swing slowed down and stopped. Teddy felt sad and disappointed.

He sat on the swing for a long time. His paws dragged in the soft dirt below. Maybe he would never swing at night. Maybe the dark would always be too scary.

Then Teddy noticed something special. A tiny firefly blinked near his nose. Its little light glowed yellow and warm. Soon another firefly appeared. Then another!

"Hello, little lights," Teddy said softly. The fireflies danced around his head. Their gentle glow made everything look magical.

Teddy had a new idea. "Will you help me swing?" he asked the fireflies. They seemed to understand. More fireflies came from the garden. Soon dozens of tiny lights surrounded the swing set.

The fireflies made the darkness beautiful. They twinkled like tiny stars. Their soft light showed Teddy that nothing scary was hiding. Just friendly flowers and sleepy trees.

Teddy started to swing again. This time felt different. The fireflies flew alongside him. Up and down they danced together. The night air smelled like sweet jasmine flowers.

"This is wonderful!" Teddy laughed. He wasn't scared anymore. The fireflies were his new friends. They made the dark feel cozy and safe.

Higher and higher Teddy swung. The fireflies sparkled all around him. Their light painted pretty patterns in the air. Teddy's fur felt cool and fresh in the night breeze.

A wise old owl hooted from the oak tree. "Who's having fun?" the owl asked kindly.

"I am!" Teddy called back. "I learned that darkness isn't scary. It's just different from daytime."

The fireflies blinked in agreement. They seemed happy that Teddy was brave now. The moon peeked out from behind the clouds. It smiled down at Teddy with a silver glow.

From that night on, Teddy loved swinging in the dark. The fireflies visited him every evening. They played together until bedtime. Teddy discovered that nighttime had its own special magic.

Sometimes being brave means trying again. When Teddy's first idea didn't work, he didn't give up. He looked around and found a better way. The fireflies helped him see that darkness could be beautiful too.

Now Teddy swings both day and night. In sunshine, he feels warm and bright. In darkness, he feels peaceful and calm. His firefly friends taught him that every time has its own special gifts.

Teddy learned that being scared is okay. Everyone feels scared sometimes. But with a little help and trying again, scary things can become wonderful adventures.

The swing set became Teddy's favorite place. Day or night, rain or shine, it was always perfect for flying high.

The end.
