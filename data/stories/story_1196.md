# Story story_1196

**Maya's Magic Lunch**

The kitchen smelled like warm soup. Mom called from downstairs. "Maya! Lunch time!"

Maya sat on her bed. She didn't want lunch. Her tummy felt funny. Not hungry funny. Worried funny.

"I don't like lunch," Maya whispered. She hugged her teddy bear tight.

Her big sister Emma peeked in. "What's wrong, Maya?"

"Lunch is scary," Maya said. "The soup is too hot. The sandwich is too big. Everything tastes yucky."

Emma sat down beside her. "I have an idea. Want to make lunch magic?"

Maya's eyes got wide. "Magic?"

Emma smiled. "Come see."

They walked to the kitchen together. Mom had soup bowls ready. Steam danced up like tiny clouds.

"First," Emma said, "we need magic words." She whispered, "Abracadabra, soup be cool!"

Maya giggled. She blew on her spoon. "Cool down, soup!"

The soup felt just right. Not too hot. Not too cold. Perfect.

"It worked!" Maya said. Her spoon made tiny ripples. The soup tasted like sunshine and carrots.

Emma picked up Maya's sandwich. "Now for sandwich magic." She waved her hands. "Shrinking spell!"

She broke the sandwich into small pieces. Each piece looked like a puzzle.

"Wow," Maya said. "Little bites are better." She took a nibble. The bread was soft. The cheese was creamy. The tomato was juicy.

"What about the apple?" Maya asked.

Emma cut it into star shapes. "Star power!" she announced.

Maya crunched a star piece. Sweet juice ran down her chin. She laughed and wiped it away.

"The stars are crunchy," Maya said. "And sweet like candy."

Mom watched from the counter. She smiled big. "You girls are wonderful helpers."

Emma poured milk into Maya's special cup. The one with dancing bears. "Magic milk for growing strong!"

Maya drank the cold, creamy milk. A white mustache appeared above her lip.

"You look funny," Emma laughed.

Maya looked in the shiny toaster. She giggled too. "Milk mustache magic!"

They ate their lunch together. Maya tried everything. The soup warmed her tummy. The sandwich pieces disappeared one by one. The apple stars crunched between her teeth.

"I ate it all!" Maya announced. She felt proud and full.

Emma clapped her hands. "You did it! You're a lunch champion!"

Maya's tummy felt happy now. Not worried anymore. Just satisfied and warm.

"Emma," Maya said, "will you do lunch magic tomorrow too?"

"Every day," Emma promised. "We can try new spells."

Maya hugged her sister tight. "You're the best magic helper ever."

Mom came over and kissed both girls. "What lovely magic you made together."

After lunch, they cleaned up. Maya helped put dishes in the sink. The warm soapy water felt nice on her hands.

"Tomorrow," Emma said, "we can make rainbow vegetables."

"Rainbow vegetables?" Maya asked.

"Red tomatoes, orange carrots, yellow corn," Emma explained. "All the colors!"

Maya clapped her hands. "That sounds beautiful!"

They went outside to play. Maya's tummy felt light and happy. No more lunch worries. Just excitement for tomorrow.

"Emma," Maya said as they swung on the swings, "lunch isn't scary anymore."

"That's because you were brave," Emma said. "You tried new things."

Maya pumped her legs higher. The wind felt cool on her face. "And because you helped me."

"Sisters always help sisters," Emma said.

As the sun shone down, Maya thought about tomorrow's lunch. Maybe they would make soup that looked like a rainbow. Maybe sandwiches shaped like butterflies.

"I can't wait for lunch tomorrow," Maya said.

Emma smiled. "Me too, little sister. Me too."

Maya had learned something important. Scary things become fun with the right helper. And lunch could be the best part of the day.

The magic wasn't really in the words. It was in sharing meals with people who love you.

The end.
