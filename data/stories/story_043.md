# Freddy Frog's Big Toy Problem

Freddy Frog loved playing in the cozy living room. The soft carpet felt nice under his webbed feet. Colorful toys were scattered everywhere like a rainbow.

"Time to clean up!" called Mama Frog from the kitchen. "Put all your toys away, please!"

Freddy looked around the room. There were so many toys! Red blocks sat by the window. Blue cars hid under the coffee table. Yellow stuffed animals rested on the couch. His favorite green ball rolled near the fireplace.

"I can do this!" Freddy said proudly.

He hopped over to the red blocks first. Freddy picked up one block with his little hands. Then he tried to pick up another. And another. Soon his arms were very full.

But there were still more blocks! Freddy tried to grab them all. The blocks tumbled down with a loud clatter.

"Oh no!" Freddy sighed.

Next, he hopped to the blue cars. Freddy scooped up three cars in his arms. He tried to pick up more cars. But they slipped through his fingers and rolled across the floor.

"This is too hard!" Freddy croaked sadly.

He sat on the soft carpet. All the toys were still scattered around. The living room looked messy. Freddy felt frustrated.

"Maybe I need help," he thought.

But then Freddy remembered something. Last week, Mama Frog had used a big basket to carry groceries. Maybe he could use something like that!

Freddy looked around the room carefully. There, by the bookshelf, sat his toy wagon! It was red with yellow wheels that squeaked softly.

"Perfect!" Freddy exclaimed happily.

He pulled the wagon across the carpet. It made a gentle rumbling sound. First, Freddy gathered all the red blocks. He placed them carefully in the wagon. They fit perfectly!

Freddy pulled the wagon to the toy box. He lifted each block out gently. Into the toy box they went, one by one.

"This works great!" Freddy cheered.

Next, he filled the wagon with blue cars. The wagon rolled smoothly across the carpet. Freddy hummed a happy tune as he worked.

Then came the yellow stuffed animals. They were soft and fluffy in his hands. The wagon carried them all easily to their special shelf.

Finally, Freddy picked up his green ball. It was bouncy and smooth. He didn't need the wagon for just one toy. He carried it proudly to the toy box.

The living room looked wonderful now! The carpet was clear and clean. Sunlight streamed through the window, making everything sparkle.

"All done!" Freddy announced proudly.

Mama Frog peeked into the living room. Her eyes grew wide with surprise and joy.

"Freddy, you cleaned up everything!" she said warmly. "How did you carry all those toys?"

"I used my wagon!" Freddy explained excitedly. "When my hands couldn't carry everything, I found a better way!"

Mama Frog gave Freddy a big, warm hug. "You were very smart to think of that solution. I'm so proud of you!"

Freddy felt happy and proud inside. His heart felt as warm as sunshine.

"Sometimes problems seem too big," Mama Frog said gently. "But if we keep trying and think creatively, we can find good solutions."

Freddy nodded eagerly. "Next time I have a big job, I'll remember to use helpful tools!"

That evening, Freddy played quietly in the clean living room. He rolled his green ball gently across the soft carpet. Everything was in its proper place.

When bedtime came, Freddy felt tired but happy. He had learned something important today. Big problems could be solved with good thinking and the right tools.

"Good night, wagon," Freddy whispered to his helpful red wagon. "Thank you for helping me today."

As Freddy drifted off to sleep, he smiled peacefully. Tomorrow would bring new adventures and maybe new problems to solve. But now he knew he could handle them with creativity and persistence.

The living room stayed neat and tidy, ready for another day of fun and learning.

The end.
