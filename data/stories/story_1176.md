# Story story_1176

**Giraffe and Anna's Special Day**

On Sunny Meadow Farm, a tall giraffe named Patches stood by the big oak tree. His yellow coat had brown spots like puzzle pieces. Patches felt very lonely today. All the other animals were busy with their families.

The cows mooed to their calves. The sheep bleated with their lambs. The chickens clucked around their chicks. But Patches had no one to play with.

He stretched his long neck high into the tree. The green leaves tasted sweet and crunchy. But eating alone wasn't much fun. Patches sighed a big, sad sigh.

"I wish I had a friend," he whispered to the wind.

Just then, a little girl with curly red hair skipped into the pasture. Her name was Anna. She wore bright blue overalls and yellow rain boots. Anna loved visiting the farm with her grandmother.

Anna saw the tall giraffe looking sad. His big brown eyes seemed lonely. She wanted to help him feel better.

"Hello, Mr. Giraffe!" Anna called out cheerfully. "My name is Anna. What's your name?"

Patches looked down at the small girl. His heart felt a little warmer. "I'm Patches," he said in his gentle voice. "Nice to meet you, Anna."

"Would you like to play with me?" Anna asked with a big smile. "I brought my red ball!"

Patches had never played with a human child before. He felt nervous but excited too. "I would love to play," he said. "But I'm very tall. How can we play together?"

Anna thought for a moment. She tapped her finger on her chin. "I have an idea!" she said. "You can use your long neck!"

Anna rolled the red ball toward Patches. He bent his long neck down low. His soft nose pushed the ball back to Anna. The ball bounced on the soft grass.

"This is fun!" laughed Anna. She clapped her hands with joy.

They rolled the ball back and forth. Patches felt happy for the first time all day. His tail swished with excitement.

"Let's try something else!" Anna said. She found some yellow flowers growing nearby. "Can you reach the flowers up high?"

Patches stretched his neck up, up, up. He picked the prettiest flowers from the tall branches. Then he bent down and gave them to Anna.

"Thank you, Patches!" Anna said. The flowers smelled sweet like honey. "You're the best friend ever!"

Next, they played hide and seek. Anna hid behind the water trough. Patches pretended he couldn't see her. His spots helped him hide behind the dappled tree shadows.

"Found you!" Anna giggled when she spotted his long neck.

The warm sun made them both feel sleepy. They sat together under the shady oak tree. Anna leaned against Patches' warm, fuzzy leg. She felt safe and happy.

"I was so lonely today," Patches told Anna softly. "Thank you for playing with me."

"I was looking for a special friend too," Anna said. "Now I found you!"

They watched the white clouds float across the blue sky. Some clouds looked like animals. Some looked like ice cream cones.

"Look!" Anna pointed up. "That cloud looks like a giraffe!"

Patches laughed his deep, rumbly laugh. "And that one looks like a little girl with curly hair!"

As the day grew later, Anna's grandmother called her name. It was time to go home. Anna felt sad to leave her new friend.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" Patches asked hopefully.

"Yes!" Anna promised. "We can play every day!"

She gave Patches a gentle hug around his front leg. He nuzzled her curly hair with his soft nose.

From that day on, Anna visited Patches every morning. They played ball and picked flowers. They told each other stories and shared snacks.

Patches was never lonely again. He had found his very best friend. And Anna had found the kindest, tallest friend in the whole world.

The other farm animals smiled when they saw them together. Friendship comes in all shapes and sizes. Sometimes the best friends are the most unexpected ones.

The end.
