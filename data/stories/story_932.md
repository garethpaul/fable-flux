# Story story_932

**Maya and the Magic Playtime**

Maya heard Mom calling from downstairs. "Playtime with Emma!" Mom said cheerfully. Maya looked at her books. She wanted to keep reading. Her little sister Emma was only four. Emma always wanted to play baby games.

Maya walked slowly to the playroom. Emma was already there. She had blocks everywhere. Red blocks. Blue blocks. Yellow blocks too. "Maya! Maya! Come play!" Emma called out. Her voice was so excited.

"I don't want to play blocks," Maya said. She sat in the corner chair. Emma's face looked sad. Her brown eyes got watery. Maya felt bad inside her tummy.

Emma picked up a sparkly purple block. "This one is magic," she whispered. "It talks to me." Maya rolled her eyes. But then something strange happened. The purple block seemed to shimmer. It caught the sunlight from the window.

"What does it say?" Maya asked quietly. She moved a little closer. Emma held the block near her ear. "It says we need to build a castle. For the fairy princess who lives inside."

Maya looked at the purple block again. It really did look special. The sparkles danced in the light. "A fairy princess?" Maya asked. Emma nodded very seriously. "She's been waiting all day."

Maya sat down on the soft carpet. She touched the purple block. It felt warm and smooth. "Where should we build her castle?" Maya asked. Emma's whole face lit up like sunshine.

"By the window!" Emma said. "So she can see the garden." The sisters started building together. Maya found the biggest blocks for the bottom. Emma chose the prettiest ones for the walls. Pink ones. Silver ones. Green ones too.

As they built, Maya started having fun. Emma had good ideas. "We need a tower," Emma said. "For her to see far away." Maya carefully stacked blocks high. Her hands were gentle and steady.

"And a garden!" Maya added. She used small green blocks for trees. Emma clapped her hands. The sound was happy and musical. "The fairy princess will love it!"

They worked together for a long time. The castle grew taller and prettier. Maya forgot about her books upstairs. This was actually really fun. Emma was creative and smart.

"Now we need to invite her out," Emma said. She held the purple block carefully. Maya watched as Emma whispered to it. "Please come out, Princess. Your castle is ready."

Maya played along. "I think I see her!" she said. Emma gasped with joy. "Where? Where?" They both looked at the sparkly castle. The afternoon sun made it glow like magic.

"She's dancing on the tower," Maya said softly. Emma smiled so big. Her teeth were tiny and white. "She loves our castle!" Emma said. "She says thank you!"

The sisters played with the fairy princess all afternoon. They made up stories. They built more rooms. They even made a stable for her horse. Maya's imagination felt alive and buzzing.

When Mom called for dinner, Maya felt surprised. Time had gone so fast. "Can we play again tomorrow?" Emma asked. Her voice was hopeful and sweet.

Maya looked at their beautiful castle. She looked at Emma's happy face. "Yes," Maya said. "But next time, let's build a whole fairy kingdom." Emma jumped up and down. Her curls bounced everywhere.

"With a school for baby fairies!" Emma added. Maya giggled. "And a library too," she said. Emma hugged Maya tight. She smelled like strawberry shampoo and sunshine.

As they cleaned up the blocks, Maya felt warm inside. Emma wasn't just her little sister. She was her friend too. Playing together was actually really wonderful.

"Tomorrow will be the best day ever," Emma said. Maya smiled and nodded. She couldn't wait for playtime tomorrow. Maybe they would discover even more magic together.

The purple block sparkled one last time in the evening light. Maya was sure the fairy princess was smiling too.

The end.
