# Story story_1268

Ben looked at the art table. Colorful paints sat in small cups. Brushes waited in a jar. Paper lay ready and white.

"Art time!" called Miss Sarah.

Ben's tummy felt funny. He did not like art time. His hands always got messy. His pictures never looked right.

Other children ran to the table. They grabbed brushes and smiled big smiles. Ben walked slowly. His feet felt heavy.

"I don't want to paint," Ben whispered.

Miss Sarah knelt down beside him. "What would you like to make today?" she asked softly.

Ben shrugged his shoulders. "Nothing looks good when I make it."

The red paint sparkled in the sunlight. The blue paint looked like the sky. Yellow paint was bright like sunshine.

"Let me show you something special," Miss Sarah said. She dipped a big brush in water. "Close your eyes, Ben."

Ben squeezed his eyes shut tight. He felt the wet brush touch his paper. It made soft swooshing sounds.

"Now open them!"

Ben opened his eyes wide. The wet brush had made the paper shine. Colors would spread and dance on the wet spots.

"Try it," Miss Sarah said with a warm smile.

Ben picked up a brush. It felt good in his small hand. He dipped it in water first. The paper grew shiny and wet.

Then he touched blue paint. Just a tiny bit. He put the brush on the wet paper.

Something magical happened! The blue paint spread out like a cloud. It made soft, pretty shapes. Ben gasped with surprise.

"Wow!" he said.

He tried yellow next. The yellow paint danced into the blue. It made green where they met. Ben had never seen anything so beautiful.

His friend Emma looked over. "That's so pretty, Ben!" she said.

Ben felt warm and happy inside. He dipped his brush in red paint. The red swirled with the other colors. It looked like a sunset.

"I'm making a sky!" Ben said proudly.

The paint felt smooth under his brush. It smelled fresh and clean. Ben's paper filled with dancing colors.

He added more water. The colors spread even more. They looked like magic clouds floating together.

"Look what I made!" Ben called to Miss Sarah.

Miss Sarah came over quickly. Her eyes grew big and bright. "Ben, this is wonderful! The colors are so beautiful together."

Ben beamed with pride. His chest felt warm and happy. He had made something beautiful all by himself.

Other children gathered around his table. "How did you do that?" asked his friend Max.

"You put water first," Ben explained. "Then the paint dances!"

Ben looked at his hands. They were covered in pretty colors. Red, blue, and yellow paint decorated his fingers. He did not mind at all.

He made another painting. This one had purple and orange. The colors swirled like a rainbow. Each brush stroke felt exciting.

"Art time is almost over," Miss Sarah announced.

"Already?" Ben asked. He felt surprised. Time had gone so fast.

Ben held up both his paintings. They were bright and cheerful. The colors seemed to glow in the light.

"I made two pictures!" he told his mom when she came.

"They're absolutely beautiful, sweetheart," Mom said. She gave him a big hug.

Ben carefully carried his artwork to the car. He could not wait to show Dad and his little sister.

That night at dinner, Ben talked about art time. "Tomorrow I want to try green paint," he said excitedly.

"And maybe I'll paint a flower garden!"

Mom and Dad smiled at each other. They were so happy to see Ben excited about art.

Ben went to bed thinking about colors. He dreamed of paint dancing on paper. In his dreams, he made the most beautiful pictures in the world.

The next morning, Ben woke up early. "Is it art time yet?" he asked Mom.

Ben had discovered something wonderful. Art was not about making perfect pictures. It was about having fun with colors. It was about trying new things.

He could hardly wait for art time to come again.

The end.
