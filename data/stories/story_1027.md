# Story story_1027

**Maya's Big School Day**

Maya sat on the red swing at the playground. Her feet dragged in the soft sand below. She pushed herself back and forth very slowly.

"Hi Maya!" called her friend Ben. He ran over with a big smile. "Want to play on the slide?"

Maya shook her head. "No thank you," she said quietly.

Ben sat on the swing next to her. "What's wrong? You look sad."

Maya's voice was very small. "Tomorrow is my first day at the big school. I feel scared in my tummy."

Ben nodded. He remembered feeling nervous too. "What makes you feel scared?"

"Everything!" Maya said. "What if I get lost? What if the other kids don't like me? What if I can't find the bathroom?"

Maya's hands felt sweaty. Her heart beat fast like a little bird.

Ben thought hard. He wanted to help his friend feel better.

"I have an idea!" Ben said. "Let's practice being at school right here!"

Ben pointed to the big climbing structure. "That can be your classroom. The slide is the hallway. The sandbox is the cafeteria!"

Maya looked around. Maybe this could work.

They walked to the climbing structure together. Ben pretended to be the teacher.

"Good morning, class!" Ben said in a grown-up voice. "Please sit down."

Maya giggled a little. Ben sounded funny trying to be a teacher.

But then Maya frowned. "This doesn't feel like real school," she said. "I'm still worried about tomorrow."

Ben scratched his head. His first idea didn't work. But he didn't give up.

"Wait!" Ben said. "I know what we can do. Let's ask my mom!"

Ben's mom was sitting on a bench nearby. She was reading a book and watching them play.

"Mom," Ben called. "Maya feels nervous about school tomorrow. Can you help?"

Ben's mom smiled warmly. She walked over to them.

"Oh sweetie," she said to Maya. "It's okay to feel nervous. That means you care about doing well."

Maya looked up at Ben's mom. "Really?"

"Really," she said. "Would you like to know a secret that helps with big feelings?"

Maya nodded eagerly.

"When you feel scared, take three deep breaths," Ben's mom said. "Breathe in slowly through your nose. Smell the flowers."

Maya breathed in deep. She could smell the sweet flowers planted near the playground.

"Now breathe out slowly through your mouth," Ben's mom continued. "Blow out the candles."

Maya breathed out. She felt a little calmer already.

"And remember," Ben's mom said gently, "your teacher will help you. Other kids feel nervous too. You can make new friends."

Ben jumped up and down. "And I'll be there too! We can eat lunch together!"

Maya smiled for the first time that day. "Really? You'll help me find my way?"

"Of course!" Ben said. "That's what friends do."

Maya practiced her deep breathing three more times. Her tummy felt less jumpy now.

"Let's practice walking to school," Maya said. She felt braver now.

They marched around the playground together. Ben showed Maya how to introduce herself to new friends.

"Hi, I'm Maya," she practiced saying. "Want to play with me?"

Her voice got stronger each time she said it.

The sun felt warm on Maya's face. The birds chirped happy songs in the trees. Everything seemed brighter now.

"Thank you for helping me," Maya told Ben. "I still feel a little nervous, but I'm excited too."

"You're going to do great," Ben said. "And tomorrow after school, we can play here again. You can tell me all about your day!"

Maya hugged her friend tight. Tomorrow would be a big adventure. She felt ready now.

With good friends and deep breaths, Maya knew she could handle anything. Even big new schools didn't seem so scary anymore.

"Race you to the swings!" Maya called out, running ahead with a huge smile.

The end.
