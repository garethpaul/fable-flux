# Story story_1389

Emma sat under the big oak tree. She felt very lonely today. Her friends were all busy. The woods around her were quiet and still.

Emma picked up a small stick. She drew circles in the dirt. The leaves rustled softly above her head. A gentle breeze touched her cheek.

"I wish I had someone to play with," Emma said sadly.

Just then, something rolled toward her feet. It was a bright red ball. The ball was shiny and smooth. It felt warm in the sunshine.

"Where did you come from?" Emma asked the ball.

The ball seemed to bounce a little. It rolled closer to Emma. She picked it up and held it tight.

"Are you lonely too?" Emma whispered.

The ball felt happy in her hands. Emma stood up and bounced it gently. The ball came right back to her. It made a soft thump sound on the ground.

"You want to play!" Emma said with a smile.

Emma threw the ball against the oak tree. It bounced back to her perfectly. She caught it with both hands.

"Good catch!" she told herself.

The ball felt excited to play. Emma rolled it down a small hill. She ran after it, laughing. Her feet crunched on the dry leaves.

The ball waited for her at the bottom. Emma picked it up and hugged it close. The ball felt soft and warm.

"Let's play hide and seek," Emma said.

She hid the ball behind a big rock. Then she counted to ten. "Ready or not, here I come!"

Emma found the ball right away. It seemed to be giggling silently. She picked it up and spun around.

"My turn to hide," Emma said.

She put the ball down gently. Emma hid behind a tree trunk. The ball stayed very still. It waited patiently for Emma.

"Here I am!" Emma called out.

She ran back to the ball. It bounced once with joy. Emma clapped her hands together.

"You're such a good friend," she said.

Emma and the ball played catch. They rolled down the grassy hill together. Emma's hair flew in the wind. She felt happy and free.

The ball loved playing too. It bounced high when Emma threw it up. It rolled fast when she pushed it along.

"Let's make a game," Emma said.

She found some smooth stones. Emma made a circle with them. She and the ball took turns rolling into the circle.

"You win!" Emma cheered when the ball stopped perfectly inside.

The sun began to set. Golden light filled the woods. Emma sat down with the ball in her lap.

"Thank you for playing with me," she said softly.

The ball felt warm and content. Emma stroked its smooth surface. She could feel how happy it was.

"Will you be my friend forever?" Emma asked.

The ball seemed to nod. Emma smiled the biggest smile. She wasn't lonely anymore.

Emma heard her mom calling from far away. "Time to come home, Emma!"

"I have to go now," Emma told the ball.

She picked it up carefully. Emma walked slowly through the woods. She didn't want to leave her new friend.

"You can come home with me," Emma decided.

The ball felt so excited. Emma carried it all the way home. She showed it to her mom.

"I made a new friend today," Emma said proudly.

Her mom smiled. "What a beautiful ball. Where did you find it?"

"It found me," Emma said. "We played together all afternoon."

That night, Emma put the ball on her bedside table. She could see it in the moonlight. The ball looked happy and peaceful.

"Goodnight, friend," Emma whispered.

The next morning, Emma woke up smiling. Her red ball was waiting for her. They would have many more adventures together.

Emma was never lonely again. She had found the best friend ever. Sometimes the best friends come when you need them most.

The ball had found its perfect home too. Together, Emma and her ball would play in the woods forever.

The end.
