# Story story_717

**Woolly and the Swing Set Friend**

Woolly the sheep stood by the big swing set. The morning sun felt warm on her fluffy white coat. She pushed the empty swing back and forth with her nose. The chains made a soft creaking sound.

"I wish I had someone to play with," Woolly said sadly. Her voice was quiet like a whisper. The playground was so big and empty. All the other animals were busy doing other things.

Woolly climbed onto the swing. Her hooves gripped the seat carefully. She pushed with her back legs to make herself go higher. The wind tickled her ears as she swung back and forth. But swinging alone wasn't very fun.

Near the slide, a small rag doll sat watching. Her name was Rosie. She had bright red yarn hair and a cheerful blue dress. Rosie's button eyes sparkled as she watched Woolly swing.

"That looks like so much fun," Rosie thought to herself. She stood up on her soft cloth legs. They felt a little wobbly, but she was brave. Slowly, she walked toward the swing set.

"Hello," Rosie called out in her tiny, sweet voice. "Can I play too?"

Woolly stopped swinging and looked down. She saw the little doll with the friendly smile. Woolly's heart felt suddenly lighter and happier.

"Yes, yes!" Woolly bleated excitedly. "I would love to play with you!"

Woolly jumped down from her swing. The grass felt cool and soft under her hooves. She helped Rosie climb onto the swing next to hers. Rosie's small hands gripped the chains tightly.

"Push me, please?" Rosie asked politely.

Woolly gave Rosie a gentle push with her nose. The doll giggled as she swung forward. Her red hair flew behind her like streamers. The blue dress fluttered in the breeze.

"This is wonderful!" Rosie laughed. Her voice sounded like tiny silver bells.

Soon both friends were swinging side by side. They pumped their legs to go higher and higher. Woolly's wool bounced with each swing. Rosie's dress danced in the wind.

"Let's see who can swing the highest!" Woolly suggested.

They swung back and forth, laughing together. The playground filled with the happy sounds of their giggles. Birds chirped in the nearby trees. Butterflies danced around the swing set.

After swinging, they tried the seesaw. Woolly sat on one end. Rosie sat on the other. They bounced up and down gently. Each time Rosie went up, she squealed with delight.

"You're the best friend ever!" Rosie said as she came down.

"You are too!" Woolly replied, her eyes twinkling with joy.

Next, they played on the slide together. Woolly helped Rosie climb the ladder. Then they slid down side by side. Rosie's dress made a swooshing sound. Woolly's wool felt smooth against the slide.

At the bottom, they tumbled into a giggling heap. Soft grass cushioned their landing. They rolled around laughing until their tummies hurt.

"I was so lonely before," Woolly admitted. "Playing alone isn't nearly as fun."

"I was lonely too," Rosie said. "But I was scared to ask if I could play."

"I'm so glad you were brave," Woolly said warmly.

As the sun climbed higher in the sky, the two friends sat together on the grass. They made plans for tomorrow's play. Woolly would bring her favorite ball. Rosie would bring her tea set for a picnic.

"We can be playground friends forever," Rosie said happily.

"Forever and ever," Woolly agreed.

The sheep and the doll hugged gently. Woolly's soft wool felt cozy against Rosie's cloth body. The playground didn't feel big and empty anymore. It felt perfect for two best friends.

From that day on, Woolly and Rosie played together every morning. The swing set became their special meeting place. And neither friend ever felt lonely again.

Sometimes the best friendships begin with just one brave "hello."

The end.
