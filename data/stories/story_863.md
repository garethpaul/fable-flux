# Story story_863

**Lily's Special Snack Time**

The kitchen clock chimed three times. "Snack time, Lily!" called Mama from the kitchen. Yummy smells floated through the house. Lily could smell fresh apples and warm crackers.

But Lily didn't want snack time today. She was having too much fun playing. Her favorite doll, Rosie, sat on the living room floor. Rosie had curly brown hair and a pretty pink dress. They were having a tea party with tiny cups and plates.

"I don't want snacks yet," Lily said softly. She picked up Rosie and hugged her tight. "We're not hungry, are we, Rosie?"

Mama walked into the living room. She had kind eyes and a gentle smile. "What if Rosie comes to snack time too?" she asked. "Maybe she's hungry."

Lily looked at Rosie's painted face. The doll's blue eyes seemed to sparkle. "Are you hungry, Rosie?" Lily whispered. She put her ear close to the doll. "Oh! She says yes!"

Hand in hand with Rosie, Lily walked to the kitchen. The table looked so pretty. Mama had set out apple slices on a blue plate. Golden crackers sat next to a small cup of milk. Everything looked delicious.

"Where should Rosie sit?" asked Mama.

Lily looked around the kitchen. The chairs seemed too big for her doll. "She needs her own special place," Lily said.

Mama smiled and brought a small box from the pantry. She covered it with a soft yellow cloth. "How about this?" she asked.

Lily's eyes grew wide. "Perfect!" She placed Rosie carefully on the little seat. The doll looked just right at the table.

But something amazing happened next. As Lily bit into a crispy apple slice, she heard a tiny sound. "Crunch!" It sounded like Rosie was eating too!

Lily giggled and looked at her doll. "Did you hear that, Mama?"

Mama winked. "I think Rosie really likes apples."

Lily took another bite. "Crunch!" came the sound again. This time, Lily was sure it came from Rosie's direction. The doll's smile seemed bigger somehow.

"Try a cracker, Rosie," Lily said. She pretended to give the doll a small piece. When Lily ate her own cracker, she heard a soft "munch" sound.

"She loves crackers too!" Lily laughed.

The milk was cold and creamy. When Lily took a sip, she heard a quiet "gulp" from Rosie's spot. Lily giggled so hard that milk almost came out her nose.

"This is the best snack time ever!" Lily said. She felt warm and happy inside. Her tummy felt good too. The apple was sweet and crunchy. The crackers were salty and perfect.

Mama sat down with her own cup of tea. "Tell me about your tea party," she said.

Lily's words tumbled out fast. She told Mama about the tiny cups and how Rosie liked sugar in her pretend tea. She talked about the invisible cookies they shared. Mama listened to every word.

When snack time was over, Lily felt full and content. Her cheeks were rosy and her eyes sparkled. "Thank you for the special seat, Mama," she said.

"Thank you for sharing snack time with us, Rosie," Mama said to the doll.

Lily hugged Rosie tight. "Did you have fun?" she whispered. The doll felt warm in her arms.

As they cleaned up together, Lily helped put the plates away. She carefully folded Rosie's special yellow cloth. "We can use this again tomorrow," she said.

That night, Lily tucked Rosie into bed next to her. "Tomorrow we'll have snack time again," she whispered. "And maybe we'll hear you eating again too."

Lily closed her eyes and smiled. She was already excited for tomorrow's snack time. Maybe they would try strawberries next time. Or maybe cheese and crackers. Whatever it was, she knew it would be magical with Rosie there.

The house grew quiet and peaceful. Tomorrow would bring another wonderful snack time adventure.

The end.
