# Teddy Learns to Share

In a cozy nursery filled with soft sunlight, there lived a brown teddy bear named Teddy. His fur was fluffy and warm. His button eyes sparkled like stars. Teddy loved his special corner by the window.

Every morning, children came to play. They laughed and giggled together. But Teddy had a big problem. He didn't know how to share.

When little Emma reached for his favorite red ball, Teddy hugged it tight. "Mine!" he thought, pulling it close. When Sam wanted to play with the wooden blocks, Teddy gathered them all up. He made a big pile around himself.

The other toys watched sadly. Bunny's ears drooped down. Dolly sighed softly. Even the toy train looked lonely on its track.

"Why won't anyone play with me?" Teddy wondered. He sat alone in his corner. His heart felt heavy and sad.

Mrs. Chen, the kind teacher, noticed Teddy sitting by himself. She picked him up gently. Her hands were warm and soft.

"Oh, Teddy," she whispered. "Friends play together. Sharing makes everyone happy."

But Teddy still didn't understand. How could giving things away make him happy?

The next day, Teddy tried something new. He watched the children carefully. Emma and Sam were building a tall tower together. They passed blocks back and forth.

"Here, Emma!" Sam said with a big smile.

"Thank you!" Emma giggled. "Now you try!"

They both looked so happy. Their faces glowed with joy.

Teddy's button eyes grew wide. Maybe sharing wasn't about losing things. Maybe it was about something else.

When playtime started again, Teddy felt nervous. His tummy fluttered like butterflies. But he wanted to try.

Slowly, Teddy rolled his red ball toward Emma. It bounced softly across the carpet. Emma's face lit up like sunshine.

"Thank you, Teddy!" she squealed with delight.

Emma rolled the ball back to Teddy. Then Teddy rolled it to Sam. Soon, all three friends were playing together. The ball rolled back and forth, back and forth.

Teddy's heart felt warm and tingly. This was fun! Much more fun than playing alone.

Next, Teddy tried sharing his blocks. He gave three to Bunny. He gave two to Dolly. Together, they built a amazing castle. It was bigger and better than anything Teddy could make alone.

"Look what we made!" Bunny hopped excitedly.

"It's beautiful!" Dolly clapped her hands.

Teddy felt proud and happy. His chest puffed out with joy.

At snack time, Teddy shared his pretend cookies. Everyone sat in a circle. They munched and crunched together. The sounds were happy and cheerful.

"Sharing is caring," sang little Emma.

"And caring is fun!" added Sam.

By afternoon, Teddy had many friends. They all wanted to play with the kind teddy bear. The nursery filled with laughter and giggles. Even the sunshine seemed brighter.

When the children went home, Teddy sat with his toy friends. They were all smiling and content.

"I was wrong," Teddy said softly. "Sharing doesn't take things away. It brings friends closer."

Bunny nodded her fuzzy head. "You figured it out, Teddy!"

"And you didn't give up," added Dolly. "You kept trying until you learned."

That night, Teddy curled up in his corner. But now it didn't feel lonely. It felt peaceful and happy. Tomorrow would bring more friends and more sharing.

Teddy closed his button eyes. His heart was full of love. He had learned something wonderful. Sharing wasn't scary or hard. It was the best way to make friends.

"Tomorrow I'll share even more," Teddy whispered to himself.

And with that happy thought, the little brown teddy bear drifted off to sleep. He dreamed of red balls rolling, blocks stacking high, and friends laughing together.

In the morning, Teddy would wake up ready to share again. Because now he knew the secret. The more you share, the more happiness grows.

The end.
