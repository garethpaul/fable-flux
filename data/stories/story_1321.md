# Story story_1321

**Penny the Shy Pig**

Penny was a little pink pig who lived in a cozy backyard. She had a soft mud puddle for rolling. She had a shady apple tree for napping. She had everything she needed to be happy.

But Penny was very, very shy.

One sunny morning, Penny woke up hungry. She walked to her food bowl with tiny steps. Oh no! The bowl was empty. Her tummy made a loud rumbling sound.

Penny looked around the yard. She could see Farmer Joe working in his garden. He was pulling weeds and humming a happy song. Penny wanted to ask for breakfast. But her little hooves stayed glued to the ground.

"I'm too shy," whispered Penny to herself. "What if he's too busy? What if I bother him?"

Penny's tummy rumbled again. She was getting hungrier and hungrier. The warm sun made her feel sleepy, but she couldn't nap without food.

Penny decided to find food by herself. She sniffed around the yard with her pink snout. Maybe she could find some fallen apples. She walked to the big apple tree and looked up. All the apples hung high on the branches. Way too high for a little pig to reach.

Penny tried jumping. Hop! Hop! Hop! Her little legs bounced up and down. But she couldn't jump high enough. The apples stayed far above her head.

"This isn't working," panted Penny. She felt tired from all that jumping.

Next, Penny tried to climb the tree. She put her front hooves on the rough bark. She tried to pull herself up. But pigs aren't very good at climbing trees. Penny slipped down with a soft thud.

"Ouch," said Penny, rubbing her snout. Now she felt hungry AND sore.

Penny sat under the apple tree feeling sad. Her tummy rumbled louder than ever. She watched Farmer Joe watering his tomato plants. He looked so kind and gentle.

"Maybe I should try asking," thought Penny. "But what if my voice is too quiet?"

Penny took a deep breath. She walked slowly toward Farmer Joe. Her heart beat fast like a little drum. Step by step, she got closer.

When she reached the garden, Penny opened her mouth. But only a tiny squeak came out. Farmer Joe didn't hear her. He kept watering his plants.

Penny felt disappointed. But then she remembered how hungry she was. She thought about her empty food bowl. She needed to be braver.

Penny took an even bigger breath. She puffed out her chest. This time, she made a proper pig sound.

"Oink! Oink!" called Penny.

Farmer Joe turned around. His face lit up with a big smile.

"Well, hello there, Penny!" said Farmer Joe. "What can I do for you, little friend?"

Penny pointed her snout toward her empty food bowl. Then she looked up at Farmer Joe with hopeful eyes.

"Oh my!" laughed Farmer Joe. "I forgot your breakfast! You must be so hungry. Thank you for reminding me."

Farmer Joe walked to the barn. He came back with a bucket full of delicious pig food. The food smelled like corn and oats and apples. Penny's mouth watered.

Farmer Joe poured the food into Penny's bowl. "There you go, sweet girl. I'm sorry I was late with breakfast."

Penny oink-ed a happy thank you. She gobbled up her food with joy. It tasted even better because she had been brave enough to ask for help.

After eating, Penny felt proud and full. She had learned something important. Sometimes being shy was okay. But when she really needed something, asking for help was the right thing to do.

From that day on, Penny still felt shy sometimes. But she remembered that Farmer Joe was kind and caring. Whenever she needed help, she found her brave voice.

And Farmer Joe never forgot her breakfast again.

Penny rolled happily in her mud puddle. Being brave had made everything better.

The end.
