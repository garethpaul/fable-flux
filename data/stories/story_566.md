# Story story_566

**Drum's Walking Adventure**

Every morning after breakfast, Mama called out the same words. "Time for walk time!" she would say with a big smile.

Little Drum heard Mama's voice from the living room. He was playing with his favorite toy cars. The red one was his best car. It made vrooming sounds when he pushed it.

"I don't want to walk," Drum said softly. His cars were having such a good race. The blue car was winning today.

Mama came over and sat beside him. "Walking helps our bodies grow strong," she said gently. "Just like how your cars need wheels to move."

Drum looked at his sneakers by the door. They were bright green with sparkly laces. But he still felt sleepy and cozy inside.

"Can we walk tomorrow instead?" Drum asked. He picked up his yellow car and rolled it slowly.

"Let's try walking for just a little bit," Mama suggested. "We can come back soon."

Drum sighed a tiny sigh. He put his cars in their special box. Then he walked slowly to get his shoes.

Outside, the morning air felt cool and fresh. Drum could smell flowers blooming in the garden. Birds were singing their happy songs in the tall trees.

"My feet feel heavy," Drum said as they started down the sidewalk.

"That's okay," Mama said. "Sometimes walking starts slow. Like when you first wake up."

They walked past Mrs. Chen's house. Her cat, Whiskers, was sitting in the window. Whiskers waved his fluffy tail at Drum.

"Hi, Whiskers!" Drum called out. The cat meowed back softly.

As they kept walking, something sparkly caught Drum's eye. There on the ground was a smooth, round stone. It was gray with white stripes. It looked like a tiny zebra egg.

"Look, Mama!" Drum said, bending down. "A magic stone!"

He picked it up carefully. The stone felt smooth and warm in his small hand.

"That's a beautiful find," Mama said. "What makes it magic?"

Drum thought for a moment. "It has zebra stripes! Maybe it will help zebras find their way home."

They continued walking. Drum held his magic stone tight. Soon he spotted another treasure. A bright red leaf had fallen from an oak tree.

"Another magic thing!" Drum exclaimed. His feet started moving faster. Walking was becoming more exciting.

They found a purple flower petal next. Then a shiny acorn. Each treasure went into Drum's pocket.

"My pocket is getting full of magic," Drum told Mama. He was practically skipping now.

They walked past the playground. Other children were swinging and laughing. Drum waved at his friend Sophie. She was going down the big slide.

"Walking helps us see so many wonderful things," Mama observed.

Drum nodded eagerly. "I found five magic treasures! And I saw Whiskers and Sophie too!"

His legs felt strong and happy now. The sleepy feeling was completely gone. His heart was beating like a cheerful drum.

They walked around the whole block. Drum pointed out a buzzing bee, a colorful butterfly, and a dog wearing a red bandana.

"That dog looks fancy," Drum giggled.

When they arrived back home, Drum's cheeks were rosy pink. His eyes were bright and sparkly.

"How do you feel now?" Mama asked as they took off their shoes.

"I feel like I can run really fast!" Drum said. "My body feels awake and strong!"

He carefully placed his magic treasures on his bedroom windowsill. The zebra stone looked perfect next to the red leaf.

"Can we go walking again tomorrow?" Drum asked. "I want to find more magic treasures!"

"Of course we can," Mama said, giving him a warm hug. "Walking time is always waiting for us."

That night, Drum dreamed about walking adventures. In his dreams, he found rainbow stones and golden leaves. He couldn't wait for tomorrow's walk time to begin.

From that day on, when Mama called "Time for walk time!" Drum would jump up with excitement. Walking had become his favorite treasure hunt.

The end.
