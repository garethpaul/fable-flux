# Story story_1170

**The Hallway Friends**

Little Mouse sat in the long hallway. The floor was cool and smooth. Sunlight made yellow squares on the walls. Mouse rolled his tiny red ball back and forth. Back and forth. Back and forth.

"This is not very fun," Mouse said softly. His whiskers twitched sadly. The hallway felt big and empty. Mouse wished he had someone to play with.

The red ball rolled away from him. It bumped against the white wall. Mouse scurried after it on his little pink feet. His tail made a soft swoosh sound on the floor.

Just then, Frog hopped around the corner. Frog's green skin sparkled in the sunlight. His big round eyes saw Mouse playing alone.

"Hello there!" Frog said in his deep, friendly voice. "What are you doing?"

Mouse looked up. His black eyes were wide with surprise. "Oh! I am playing with my ball. But it is not much fun by myself."

Frog's mouth curved into a big smile. "May I play too? I love to play!"

Mouse's ears perked up. His tail wiggled with excitement. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" Frog said. He gave a happy hop. "Friends always play together!"

Mouse pushed the red ball toward Frog. Frog caught it with his sticky green hands. Then he rolled it back to Mouse. The ball made a gentle rolling sound on the smooth floor.

"This is fun!" Mouse squeaked. His voice echoed in the hallway. He felt warm and happy inside.

Frog had an idea. "Let's make up a new game!" he said. "We can roll the ball and chase it together!"

Mouse's eyes sparkled. "Yes! That sounds wonderful!"

They took turns rolling the ball down the long hallway. Mouse ran on his quick little feet. Frog hopped with big bounces. Their sounds mixed together - pitter-patter and boing-boing.

The red ball rolled fast. It bounced off the walls. Mouse and Frog laughed as they chased it. The hallway filled with their happy sounds.

"Catch it, Mouse!" Frog called out.

"I got it!" Mouse squeaked back.

They played until they were tired. Both friends sat down on the cool floor. They breathed hard and smiled at each other.

"That was the best game ever," Mouse said. His small chest went up and down quickly.

Frog nodded. "Playing with a friend is always better than playing alone."

Mouse looked at Frog's kind green face. "Will you be my friend forever?"

"Forever and always," Frog said. He reached out his wet hand. Mouse touched it with his tiny paw.

The sunlight moved across the walls. It made new patterns of light and shadow. But Mouse did not feel lonely anymore. His heart felt full and warm.

"What should we play tomorrow?" Mouse asked.

Frog thought for a moment. "We could play hide and seek! Or we could race our ball!"

"Or both!" Mouse said with a giggle.

They picked up the red ball together. Frog held one side. Mouse held the other. They walked down the hallway side by side.

"Thank you for playing with me," Mouse said quietly.

"Thank you for sharing your ball," Frog replied.

As they reached the end of the hallway, both friends felt happy. The big empty space did not feel scary anymore. It felt like their special place to play.

Mouse looked up at his new friend. "Same time tomorrow?"

"Same time tomorrow," Frog promised.

They waved goodbye with big smiles. Mouse scurried home with his ball. Frog hopped away with joy in his heart.

The hallway was quiet again. But it was not empty anymore. It was full of friendship and fun memories.

Mouse could hardly wait for tomorrow. He had found something better than a toy to play with. He had found a true friend.

And in the morning, they would play together again.

The end.
