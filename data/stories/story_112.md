# Maya's Big View

Maya stood on her tippy-toes in the sunny kitchen. She pressed her nose against the cool window glass. "I can't see!" she said with a big sigh.

The kitchen window looked out to the backyard. Beyond their green grass was a tall wooden fence. Maya knew something wonderful was happening on the other side. She could hear happy barking sounds. She could hear children laughing and playing.

"What's over there?" Maya asked her mom.

"The new neighbors are having a puppy party," Mom said with a smile. "All the neighborhood dogs are playing together."

Maya's eyes grew wide. She loved puppies! Their fluffy fur and waggy tails always made her giggle. But the fence was much too tall. Maya was much too short.

"I want to see the puppies!" Maya said. She jumped up and down. Jump, jump, jump! But she still couldn't see over the fence.

Maya looked around the bright kitchen. The yellow walls seemed to sparkle in the sunlight. She spotted a small wooden stool by the counter. "That might work!" she said.

Maya pushed the stool to the window. The wooden legs made a soft scraping sound on the tile floor. She climbed up carefully. One foot, then the other. Now she was taller!

But it still wasn't enough. The fence was still too high. Maya could only see the very tops of the trees beyond.

"Hmm," Maya said, tapping her finger on her chin. She climbed down and looked around again.

In the corner sat a stack of thick cookbooks. They had colorful covers with pictures of yummy food. "Maybe these will help!" Maya said.

She carried the heavy books one by one. Thump, thump, thump across the kitchen floor. Maya stacked them on top of the wooden stool. The pile wobbled a little bit.

"Be careful, sweetie," Mom said, watching from nearby.

Maya climbed up slowly. The books felt bumpy under her feet. She was much taller now! But the fence was still a tiny bit too high.

Maya felt frustrated. Her cheeks turned pink. She really, really wanted to see those puppies!

Then Maya noticed something. The kitchen had a special breakfast bar with tall stools. These stools were much higher than her little wooden one.

"Mom, may I try the breakfast bar?" Maya asked politely.

"What a smart idea!" Mom said. "Let me help you."

Together, they moved one tall stool to the window. The metal legs clinked softly on the floor. Mom held Maya's hand as she climbed up.

Maya's feet dangled in the air. She felt very tall and important! She looked out the window with excitement.

"I can see!" Maya shouted with joy.

Over the fence, she saw the most wonderful sight. Five fluffy puppies were running in circles! There was a golden puppy with floppy ears. A tiny black puppy was chasing its own tail. A spotted puppy was rolling in the soft grass.

The puppies barked happy little barks. "Woof, woof!" Their tails wagged like fuzzy windshield wipers. Some children were throwing colorful balls for the puppies to catch.

"They're so cute!" Maya giggled. She clapped her hands together. The sound echoed in the warm kitchen.

Maya watched the puppy party for a long time. She saw the puppies play tug-of-war with a rope toy. She saw them drink water from bright blue bowls. She saw them take sleepy naps in the sunshine.

"Thank you for helping me, Mom," Maya said. She gave her mom a big, squeezy hug.

"You didn't give up," Mom said proudly. "You kept trying new ideas until you found one that worked."

Maya smiled a huge smile. She felt proud and happy inside. Even though she was small, she had solved her big problem! She learned that being persistent means trying again and again.

From her tall perch, Maya waved at the puppies. She couldn't wait to meet them someday. But for now, she was perfectly happy watching their joyful games from her special spot in the kitchen.

The afternoon sun filled the room with golden light. Maya felt warm and content. Sometimes the best view comes after you keep trying!

The end.
