# Story story_613

**Penny the Pig and the Tall Fence**

Penny was a little pink pig. She lived in a cozy bedroom. The bedroom had soft blue walls. It had a warm, fuzzy rug too.

Penny loved her bedroom very much. But she had one big problem. There was a tall wooden fence outside her window. The fence was very, very tall. Penny was very, very short.

Every morning, Penny heard wonderful sounds. She heard birds singing pretty songs. She heard children laughing and playing. She heard dogs barking happily. The sounds came from behind the tall fence.

"I want to see what's back there," said Penny. She pressed her little pink nose against the window. She could only see the brown wooden fence. She could not see over it at all.

Penny felt sad. Her curly tail drooped down. Her little ears felt heavy. "I wish I could see," she whispered.

One sunny morning, Penny had an idea. "I will jump!" she said. She took three big steps back. She ran as fast as she could. She jumped up high in the air.

Penny looked over the fence for just one second. She saw green grass. She saw colorful flowers. She saw a big playground with swings. Then she fell down with a soft thump.

"Ouch," said Penny. She rubbed her bottom. "That hurt a little bit."

Penny tried jumping again. And again. And again. Each time, she only saw the wonderful place for one quick second. Then she fell down hard.

"Jumping is too hard," said Penny. She sat on her soft rug. She felt tired and disappointed. Her little legs were sore from jumping.

But Penny did not give up. She was a smart little pig. She looked around her cozy bedroom. She saw her red toy box. She saw her stack of picture books. She saw her fluffy yellow pillow.

"Wait!" said Penny. "I have another idea!"

Penny pushed her red toy box to the window. It made a scraping sound on the floor. The toy box was heavy, but Penny was strong. She pushed and pushed until it was just right.

Next, Penny carried her picture books. She stacked them on top of the toy box. The books smelled like vanilla and adventure. She made a neat, tall pile.

Finally, Penny placed her fluffy pillow on top. The pillow was soft and squishy. It felt like a cloud under her hands.

"Perfect!" said Penny. She climbed up carefully. First onto the toy box. Then onto the books. Then onto the pillow.

Now Penny was tall enough! She could see over the fence easily. She saw the most beautiful sight ever.

There was a lovely garden with red roses. There were yellow daisies dancing in the breeze. Three little rabbits were hopping around. Two squirrels were chasing each other up a tree. A family of ducks was swimming in a small pond.

Children were playing on swings and slides. They were laughing and having fun. The sun was shining bright and warm.

"Oh my!" said Penny. "It's so beautiful!" She clapped her little hooves together. Her heart felt happy and full.

Every day after that, Penny climbed her special tower. She watched the garden change with the seasons. She saw new flowers bloom. She watched baby animals grow bigger.

Penny learned something very important. When something seems too hard, don't give up. Think of a new way to try. Use what you have around you. Be creative and keep trying.

"I'm so glad I didn't stop trying," said Penny. She smiled as she watched a butterfly land on a purple flower. "Sometimes the best solutions are right in your own room."

Penny felt proud of herself. She had solved her problem all by herself. Now she could see the wonderful world beyond the fence whenever she wanted.

And from that day on, Penny was the happiest little pig in the whole wide world.

The end.
