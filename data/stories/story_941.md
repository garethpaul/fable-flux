# Story story_941

**Rosie's Dinner Adventure**

Rosie sat on her bedroom floor. Her favorite doll, Bella, was in her arms. Mama called from downstairs.

"Rosie! Time for dinner!"

Rosie frowned. She didn't want to eat dinner. She wanted to keep playing with Bella. The doll had soft yellow hair and a pretty blue dress.

"I don't want to go," Rosie whispered to Bella. "Dinner is boring."

Bella's painted smile seemed to understand. Rosie hugged her tight. The doll smelled like vanilla from yesterday's tea party.

"Rosie!" Mama called again. "Come wash your hands!"

Rosie stood up slowly. She carried Bella downstairs. The kitchen smelled warm and good. Steam rose from a big pot on the stove.

"What's for dinner?" Rosie asked. She still held Bella close.

"Chicken soup and fresh bread," Mama said. "Your favorite!"

But Rosie shook her head. "I'm not hungry."

Mama smiled gently. "Why don't you sit down? Bella can sit too."

Rosie climbed onto her chair. She put Bella in the chair next to her. The doll's blue dress spread out nicely.

Mama ladled soup into Rosie's bowl. The golden broth sparkled. Chunks of chicken and carrots floated inside. The bread was warm and crusty.

"It looks yummy," Rosie said quietly. But she didn't pick up her spoon.

Then something amazing happened. Bella seemed to turn her head slightly. Her painted eyes looked at the soup.

"Bella wants to try some," Rosie said, surprised.

"She does?" Mama asked with a twinkle in her eye.

Rosie nodded. She picked up her spoon. "I'll help her taste it first."

The soup was delicious. It warmed Rosie's tummy. The carrots were sweet and soft. The chicken was tender.

"Mmm," Rosie said. "Bella likes it!"

She pretended to give Bella tiny sips. The doll's smile seemed bigger now. Rosie giggled and took another spoonful herself.

"The bread smells like sunshine," Rosie told Bella. She tore off a small piece. The crust crunched between her teeth. The inside was fluffy and warm.

Mama sat down with her own bowl. "How does it taste?"

"Perfect!" Rosie said. "Bella thinks so too!"

As they ate, Rosie told Bella about the soup. "See the green herbs? They make it special. And the carrots are like little orange jewels!"

Bella listened quietly. Her painted face looked happy. Rosie felt happy too. Dinner wasn't boring anymore. It was fun to share with her best friend.

"Can Bella have some bread?" Rosie asked.

"Of course," Mama said. "But you'll have to eat it for her."

Rosie giggled. She took another bite of the crusty bread. "Bella says thank you!"

The kitchen felt cozy and warm. Candlelight danced on the table. Outside, the sun was setting. Everything felt peaceful and nice.

Rosie finished her whole bowl of soup. She ate two pieces of bread too. Her tummy felt full and happy.

"That was the best dinner ever," she told Mama.

"Why was it so special?" Mama asked.

Rosie looked at Bella. The doll's blue dress had a tiny drop of soup on it. Her yellow hair caught the candlelight.

"Because Bella was here," Rosie said. "Everything is more fun with friends."

Mama smiled and wiped Bella's dress clean. "Friends make everything better."

After dinner, Rosie helped clear the table. She carried Bella carefully. They went upstairs to get ready for bed.

"Tomorrow we'll have breakfast together," Rosie told Bella. "And lunch too!"

The doll's painted smile seemed to agree. Rosie brushed Bella's soft hair. She tucked her into the toy bed.

"Thank you for making dinner fun," Rosie whispered.

As she got into her own bed, Rosie felt excited. Tomorrow would bring new meals and new adventures. With Bella by her side, everything would be wonderful.

She couldn't wait for breakfast to begin their next delicious adventure together.

The end.
