# Story story_947

**Freddy and the Magic Garden**

The sun peeked through Freddy's bedroom window. Birds sang their morning songs. "Time for garden time!" called Mama Frog from downstairs.

Freddy pulled his soft green blanket over his head. He didn't want to get up. Garden time meant digging in the dirt. It meant pulling weeds. It meant getting his hands all muddy and gross.

"I don't like garden time," Freddy whispered to his teddy bear. "It's too hard work."

Mama Frog hopped up the stairs. Her footsteps went thump, thump, thump. "Come on, little one," she said gently. "The garden is waiting for us."

Freddy slowly got dressed. He put on his old green overalls. He pulled on his tiny rain boots. They squeaked when he walked.

Outside, the morning air felt cool and fresh. Dewdrops sparkled on the grass like tiny diamonds. The garden smelled like sweet flowers and rich earth.

"What should we plant today?" asked Mama Frog. She handed Freddy a small shovel.

"I don't know," Freddy said with a big sigh. "This looks boring."

Mama Frog smiled. "Let's start with these sunflower seeds." She showed him the little black seeds in her palm. "They're very special seeds."

Freddy took one seed. It felt smooth and small. "What makes them special?" he asked.

"You'll see," said Mama Frog with a wink.

Freddy dug a small hole. The dirt felt soft between his fingers. It wasn't as yucky as he remembered. He dropped the seed in the hole.

"Now cover it gently," said Mama Frog.

Freddy pushed dirt over the seed. Then something amazing happened. The ground began to glow with a soft golden light!

"Wow!" gasped Freddy. "What's happening?"

"The seed is happy," explained Mama Frog. "It knows you care about it."

Freddy planted another seed. Again, the ground glowed! This time, he heard a tiny whisper: "Thank you, Freddy!"

"Did the seed just talk to me?" Freddy's eyes grew wide.

"Seeds always talk," said Mama Frog. "You just have to listen with your heart."

Freddy planted seed after seed. Each one glowed and whispered "thank you." The garden began to shimmer like magic. Butterflies danced around them. Bees hummed happy songs.

"This is the best day ever!" laughed Freddy. He dug faster and faster. His hands were muddy, but he didn't care anymore.

They planted carrots next. The seeds felt tiny and round. "We'll grow big and orange!" they whispered. Freddy giggled at their squeaky voices.

Then they planted tomatoes. These seeds felt flat and smooth. "We'll be red and juicy!" they promised in deeper voices.

Freddy wiped his muddy hands on his overalls. "Can we plant more tomorrow?" he asked.

"Of course," said Mama Frog. "Gardens need love every day."

They watered all the seeds with a big watering can. The water made soft splashing sounds. It smelled clean and fresh.

"The seeds are drinking!" said Freddy. He could almost hear them gulping happily.

As they worked, Freddy felt proud and strong. His arms got tired, but it felt like good tired. Like after playing all day tired.

"I love getting dirty now," Freddy announced. He had mud on his nose and dirt under his fingernails.

Mama Frog laughed. "Gardens teach us that messy can be beautiful."

When they finished, Freddy looked at their work. The garden looked neat and ready. Tiny golden sparkles still danced in the soil.

"Will the magic happen again tomorrow?" asked Freddy hopefully.

"Every day brings new magic," said Mama Frog. "When we take care of growing things, they take care of us too."

That night, Freddy dreamed of talking seeds and glowing gardens. He dreamed of tall sunflowers waving hello. He dreamed of fat red tomatoes saying his name.

When morning came, Freddy jumped out of bed. "Is it garden time yet?" he called to Mama Frog.

From now on, garden time would be his favorite time of day.

The end.
