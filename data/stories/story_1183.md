# Story story_1183

**Bella Bear's Lost Doll**

Bella Bear loved her playroom. It had soft blue walls. There were toys everywhere. Her favorite was Rosie the doll.

Rosie had golden hair. She wore a pink dress. Bella carried Rosie everywhere. They had tea parties together. They read books together. Rosie was Bella's best friend.

One sunny morning, Bella woke up. She reached for Rosie. But Rosie wasn't there! Bella looked at her bed. No Rosie. She looked under her pillow. Still no Rosie.

"Where is Rosie?" Bella said sadly. Her brown eyes filled with tears. Her fuzzy ears drooped down.

Bella walked to her playroom. Maybe Rosie was there. The room smelled like crayons. It smelled like play dough too. Bella loved those smells. But today she felt worried.

"Rosie!" called Bella. "Where are you?" No answer came back.

Bella decided to look everywhere. She would find Rosie! First, she looked in the toy box. She moved the blocks. She moved the cars. She moved the stuffed animals. No Rosie anywhere.

Next, she looked under the table. It was dark under there. She saw dust bunnies. She saw a lost crayon. But no Rosie.

Bella felt more worried. Her tummy felt funny. "I need help," she said.

Bella ran to find Mama Bear. Mama was in the kitchen. She was making honey toast. The kitchen smelled warm and sweet.

"Mama!" said Bella. "I can't find Rosie!"

Mama Bear hugged Bella tight. "Let's look together," Mama said gently. "Two bears are better than one."

They went back to the playroom. Mama looked high up. Bella looked low down. They looked behind the bookshelf. They looked in the dress-up box. Still no Rosie.

"This is hard work," said Mama. "Let's think carefully. Where did you play yesterday?"

Bella rubbed her furry chin. "We had a picnic!" she said. "Under the big blanket fort!"

Bella had forgotten about the fort. Yesterday, she built it with pillows. She used chairs too. The blanket made a cozy roof.

Bella crawled under the blanket. It was dim inside. She felt around with her paws. The carpet was soft and fuzzy.

"Rosie?" she whispered hopefully.

Then her paw touched something smooth. It felt like hair! Golden hair!

"Rosie!" Bella shouted with joy. She pulled her doll out carefully. Rosie's pink dress was a little wrinkled. But she was perfect.

Bella hugged Rosie tight. The doll felt warm and familiar. Bella's heart felt happy again.

"I found her, Mama!" Bella called out.

Mama Bear smiled big. "You didn't give up," she said proudly. "You kept trying different places."

Bella nodded. "First I looked alone. That didn't work. Then you helped me think. That worked better!"

"Sometimes we need to try new ways," said Mama. "And sometimes we need help from friends."

Bella sat in the blanket fort. She held Rosie on her lap. "I'm sorry I lost you," she told her doll. "I'll be more careful next time."

The fort felt cozy and safe. Sunlight peeked through the blanket. It made pretty patterns on the floor.

"Want to have a tea party?" Bella asked Rosie. Of course, Rosie couldn't answer. But Bella knew she would say yes.

Bella set up her tiny tea set. She poured pretend tea. She gave Rosie a cup too. They sat together in their fort.

"This is the best tea party ever," said Bella happily.

Mama Bear peeked into the fort. "Room for one more?" she asked.

"Yes!" said Bella. "Rosie says yes too!"

The three of them had tea together. Bella felt grateful. She had her doll back. She learned something important too. When things get hard, don't give up. Try different ways. Ask for help when you need it.

Bella hugged Rosie extra tight. Tomorrow would bring new adventures. But for now, everything was just right in their cozy fort.

The end.
