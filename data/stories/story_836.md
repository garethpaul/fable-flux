# Story story_836

**Mama's Magic Reading Time**

Every evening at seven o'clock, the big clock chimed. "Ding dong, ding dong!" it sang. This meant one thing. Reading time!

Little Emma bounced on her toes. "Mama, it's reading time!" she called. Her voice was happy and excited.

But Mama sat on the soft couch. She felt very tired. Her shoulders drooped down low. "Oh sweetie," Mama said with a big yawn. "Mama is so sleepy tonight."

Emma's smile turned into a frown. She loved reading time with Mama. It was the best part of every day.

"Please, Mama?" Emma asked softly. She held up her favorite book. The cover was bright red with a smiling dragon.

Mama looked at Emma's hopeful face. She couldn't say no to those big brown eyes. "Okay, little one," Mama said. She patted the cozy spot next to her.

Emma climbed up on the couch. She snuggled close to Mama's warm side. The couch cushions felt soft and squishy.

Mama opened the book slowly. Her voice sounded tired and flat. "Once upon a time," she began. The words came out like a whisper.

But something strange started to happen. As Mama read about the brave little dragon, her voice grew stronger. The dragon was trying to learn how to fly.

"The dragon flapped his tiny wings," Mama read. She moved her arms like wings. Emma giggled at the silly sight.

The dragon in the story felt scared. He didn't think he could fly high. "I'm too little," the dragon said sadly.

"Just like me sometimes," Emma whispered. She understood how the dragon felt.

Mama's voice became more excited. She made her voice deep for the wise old owl. She made it squeaky for the friendly mouse. Each character sounded different and fun.

Emma's eyes grew wide with wonder. The living room felt magical now. It was like they were really in the dragon's forest.

"The dragon took a deep breath," Mama read with feeling. "He ran fast and jumped high!" Mama made whooshing sounds like wind.

Emma clapped her hands together. "He's flying, Mama! He's really flying!"

Mama smiled a real, big smile. She felt happy and awake now. Reading with Emma was filling her heart with joy.

They read about the dragon soaring over tall trees. They read about him making new friends in the clouds. The dragon learned he was braver than he thought.

"The end," Mama said as she closed the book gently. The room felt warm and peaceful.

Emma hugged Mama tight. "That was the best story ever!" she said. Her voice was full of love.

Mama hugged Emma back. "You know what?" she said. "I feel much better now. Reading with you makes me happy."

"Really?" Emma asked. Her eyes sparkled like little stars.

"Really," Mama said. "Your smile gives me energy. Your excitement makes me excited too."

Emma yawned a tiny yawn. She felt sleepy and content. "Can we read again tomorrow?" she asked hopefully.

"Of course we can," Mama promised. "I can't wait for tomorrow's reading time."

Mama tucked Emma into her soft bed. She pulled the blanket up to Emma's chin. "Sweet dreams, little one," she whispered.

Emma closed her eyes with a smile. She dreamed about flying dragons and story time with Mama.

The next evening, when the clock chimed seven, something was different. Mama was already sitting on the couch. She had three books ready to read.

"Reading time!" Emma called out happily.

"Reading time!" Mama called back with just as much joy.

They snuggled together on their special couch. Outside, the stars began to twinkle. Inside, Mama and Emma shared the magic of stories together.

From that night on, reading time was the favorite part of both their days.

The end.
