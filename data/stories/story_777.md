# Story story_777

**Ellie and the Little Doll**

Ellie the elephant sat by the big oak tree. She picked up fallen leaves with her trunk. The leaves were yellow and orange. They crinkled when she touched them.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," Ellie said softly.

The park was quiet today. Other animals were taking naps. Ellie felt very lonely. She made little piles of leaves. But playing alone wasn't much fun.

Near the playground, a small rag doll watched Ellie. The doll had button eyes and yarn hair. Her name was Rosie. She wore a pretty blue dress with tiny flowers.

Rosie had been sitting on the bench all morning. A little girl had forgotten her there yesterday. Now Rosie felt lonely too.

"That elephant looks sad," Rosie whispered to herself. "Maybe she needs a friend."

Rosie wanted to go say hello. But she was just a doll. Could she really walk and talk? She had never tried before.

Rosie took a deep breath. She wiggled her soft arms. She moved her little legs. Slowly, she stood up on the bench.

"I can do this," Rosie said bravely.

Step by step, Rosie walked toward Ellie. Her feet made tiny sounds on the grass. Ellie heard the soft footsteps. She turned around.

"Oh!" Ellie gasped. "A walking doll!"

"Hello," Rosie said shyly. "I'm Rosie. You looked lonely. I'm lonely too."

Ellie's eyes grew wide with wonder. "You can talk! That's amazing!"

"Would you like to play together?" Rosie asked hopefully.

Ellie's trunk curled up in a smile. "Yes! I would love that!"

Rosie climbed onto Ellie's back. It felt warm and safe. Ellie was very gentle. She walked slowly so Rosie wouldn't fall.

"What should we play first?" Ellie asked.

"Let's make a leaf castle!" Rosie suggested.

They gathered colorful leaves together. Ellie used her trunk to pile them high. Rosie arranged the prettiest ones on top. The castle sparkled in the sunshine.

"It's beautiful!" Ellie trumpeted happily.

Next, they played hide and seek. Rosie hid behind a flower pot. Ellie pretended she couldn't see her. They both giggled when Ellie "found" her.

Then Ellie gave Rosie rides around the park. They went past the duck pond. The water sparkled like diamonds. They smelled the sweet roses in the garden.

"This is the best day ever!" Rosie laughed.

"I'm so happy we're friends," Ellie said warmly.

They sat under the oak tree to rest. Rosie leaned against Ellie's soft gray side. The sun felt warm on their faces.

"Ellie, will you be my best friend?" Rosie asked.

"Of course!" Ellie said. "Best friends forever!"

Just then, a little girl ran into the park. She had curly brown hair and wore a yellow dress.

"Rosie!" the girl called out. "There you are! I've been looking everywhere!"

The girl was named Emma. She had lost Rosie yesterday. Emma was so worried about her special doll.

But when Emma saw Rosie with Ellie, she stopped. She watched them playing together. Emma smiled a big, warm smile.

"Rosie, you made a friend!" Emma said gently.

Emma wasn't sad anymore. She could see how happy Rosie was. And Ellie looked happy too.

"Can we all play together?" Emma asked.

"Yes!" Ellie and Rosie said at the same time.

From that day on, they met at the park every afternoon. Emma would bring Rosie to visit Ellie. They played games and told stories. They shared snacks and watched clouds.

Ellie was never lonely again. Rosie had found her best friend. And Emma loved watching them play together.

The three friends learned something special. Friendship can happen anywhere. It can happen between anyone. Even an elephant and a little doll.

Every sunset, they sat under their oak tree. They watched the sky turn pink and orange. They felt grateful for their wonderful friendship.

"Tomorrow we'll have more adventures," Ellie would say.

"I can't wait!" Rosie would answer.

And Emma would hug them both, feeling lucky to have such magical friends.

The end.
