# Story story_677

**Freddy Frog's Magical Bath Time**

Freddy Frog lived by a sparkling pond. His green skin was muddy from playing all day. Mama Frog called from their lily pad home.

"Bath time, Freddy!"

Freddy's big eyes grew wide. He did not want a bath. Baths meant soap bubbles. Baths meant scrubbing. Freddy loved being muddy and messy.

"Do I have to?" Freddy asked sadly.

"Yes, little one," Mama said gently. "Clean frogs are healthy frogs."

Freddy hopped slowly to the bathroom. The white bathtub looked enormous. Steam rose from the warm water. It smelled like lavender flowers.

Mama poured special frog soap into the tub. Tiny bubbles started forming. Freddy dipped one webbed toe in. The water felt nice and cozy.

"It's too scary," Freddy whispered.

Mama smiled warmly. "What if we make it fun?"

She sprinkled glittery bath powder into the water. Suddenly, something amazing happened! The bubbles began to glow. They sparkled like tiny stars. Purple and blue lights danced on the water.

"Wow!" Freddy gasped. His eyes grew huge with wonder.

The magical bubbles tickled his toes. They felt soft and fluffy. Freddy giggled as more bubbles appeared. Some bubbles were big. Some bubbles were small. All of them shimmered beautifully.

Freddy jumped into the tub. Splash! Water droplets flew everywhere. The glowing bubbles surrounded him like a fairy tale. He felt like a prince in an enchanted pond.

"This is wonderful!" Freddy laughed.

He scooped up handfuls of sparkly bubbles. They felt like cotton candy clouds. The bubbles popped with tiny musical sounds. Pop! Pop! Pop! It sounded like a gentle song.

Mama gave Freddy a soft yellow sponge. "Let's wash behind your ears," she said kindly.

Freddy scrubbed happily. The sponge felt squishy and fun. Soap suds made a beard on his chin. He looked in the mirror and giggled. He looked like a silly bubble monster.

The warm water made Freddy feel relaxed. His muscles felt loose and happy. The lavender smell made him sleepy. But the magical bubbles kept him excited.

"Look, Mama!" Freddy pointed up.

Rainbow bubbles floated toward the ceiling. They moved like slow-motion fireworks. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple lights swirled together. The bathroom looked like a magical cave.

Freddy played with his rubber duck. The duck squeaked cheerfully. Squeak! Squeak! The sound echoed in the bubbly water. Even his duck seemed to glow.

"Time to rinse off," Mama said sweetly.

She poured clean water over Freddy's head. The water felt like a gentle waterfall. All the soap washed away. Freddy's skin felt smooth and clean. He smelled like flowers now.

Mama wrapped him in a fluffy green towel. The towel was warm from the dryer. It felt like a cozy hug. Freddy snuggled into the soft fabric.

"That was the best bath ever!" Freddy declared proudly.

His skin felt fresh and tingly. He looked at himself in the mirror. His green skin sparkled. His eyes looked bright and happy. He felt wonderful.

Mama tucked him into his lily pad bed. The sheets smelled clean and fresh. Freddy's body felt relaxed and comfortable. He was ready for sweet dreams.

"Can we use the magic bubbles tomorrow?" Freddy asked hopefully.

"Of course," Mama smiled. "Good frogs deserve magical baths."

Freddy closed his eyes happily. He dreamed about glowing bubbles and rainbow lights. He dreamed about squeaky ducks and fluffy towels. Bath time was no longer scary.

The next evening, Freddy hopped quickly to the bathroom. He couldn't wait for another magical adventure. Bath time had become his favorite part of the day.

"I love being clean!" Freddy sang cheerfully.

From that day on, Freddy looked forward to every single bath. Magic bubbles made everything better. And clean little frogs always have the sweetest dreams.

The end.
