# Maya's Garden Discovery

Maya pressed her nose against the kitchen window. Outside, the garden looked sleepy and quiet. Her mom was putting on her gardening gloves.

"Garden time, Maya!" Mom called cheerfully.

Maya sighed softly. Garden time meant dirt. Garden time meant bugs. Garden time meant getting messy. She liked staying clean and tidy.

"Do I have to come?" Maya asked.

"The plants need our help," Mom said gently. "They're waiting for us."

Maya dragged her feet outside. The morning sun felt warm. The grass tickled her bare toes. But she still wasn't excited.

Mom handed Maya tiny gardening gloves. They were purple with yellow flowers. Maya's favorite colors! She wiggled her fingers inside.

"What do we do first?" Maya asked quietly.

"Let's check our seedlings," Mom suggested.

They walked to the small raised bed. Maya gasped with surprise. The tiny seeds they planted last week had grown! Little green shoots poked through the dark soil.

"They're so small!" Maya whispered.

"But growing bigger every day," Mom smiled.

Maya knelt down carefully. She touched one tiny leaf. It felt soft and delicate. Like a baby's skin.

"Can I water them?" Maya asked.

Mom gave her a small watering can. It was red with white polka dots. Maya sprinkled water gently. The droplets sparkled like diamonds.

The plants seemed to dance happily. Maya giggled at their wiggly movements.

"Look over here," Mom called.

Maya skipped to the flower bed. Bright yellow sunflowers towered above her head. Their faces followed the morning sun.

"They're like giant suns!" Maya exclaimed.

A fuzzy bumblebee buzzed from flower to flower. Maya watched it work busily. The bee's wings hummed a gentle song.

"The bee is having breakfast," Mom explained.

Maya spotted something moving near the roses. A tiny green caterpillar inched along a leaf. Its body stretched and scrunched rhythmically.

"Hello, little caterpillar," Maya whispered.

The caterpillar seemed to wave with its head. Maya waved back with one finger.

Mom showed Maya how to deadhead flowers. They picked off the old blooms together. Maya's pile of faded petals grew bigger.

"Why do we take them off?" Maya asked.

"So new flowers can grow," Mom said.

Maya understood. Making room for new things. Like making room for toys.

They moved to the vegetable patch. Cherry tomatoes hung like red jewels. Maya picked one carefully. It felt warm from sunshine.

"Can I taste it?" she asked.

Mom nodded. Maya bit the tiny tomato. Sweet juice burst in her mouth. It tasted like summer itself.

"It's delicious!" Maya laughed.

They filled a small basket. Red tomatoes, purple eggplant, green beans. Maya counted each vegetable carefully.

A butterfly landed on Maya's shoulder. Its wings were orange and black. Maya held perfectly still. The butterfly rested quietly.

"It likes you," Mom whispered.

Maya felt special and chosen. The butterfly finally flew away. Maya watched it dance among flowers.

They planted new seeds together. Maya made small holes. Mom dropped in seeds. Maya covered them gently.

"What will grow here?" Maya asked.

"Carrots," Mom said. "Orange and crunchy."

Maya patted the soil lovingly. "Grow big and strong," she told the seeds.

By lunchtime, Maya's hands were dirty. Her knees had grass stains. But she felt wonderful inside.

"Garden time was fun!" Maya announced.

Mom hugged her tight. "You were a great helper."

That afternoon, Maya drew pictures. She drew bees and butterflies. She drew tiny green shoots. She drew herself smiling in the garden.

At bedtime, Maya looked out her window. The garden looked peaceful. Moonlight made everything silver and magical.

"Mom," Maya called. "Can we do garden time tomorrow?"

"Of course, sweetie," Mom replied.

Maya snuggled under her covers. She dreamed of growing plants. She dreamed of friendly bees. She dreamed of tomorrow's garden adventures.

Garden time wasn't just about dirt anymore. It was about watching life grow. It was about helping tiny seeds. It was about being part of something wonderful.

Maya couldn't wait for tomorrow's garden time.

The end.
