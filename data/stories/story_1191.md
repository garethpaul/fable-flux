# Story story_1191

Emma sat on the red swing. Her feet barely touched the ground. The swing moved back and forth slowly.

The playground was quiet today. No other children were here. Emma pushed with her toes. The swing went a little higher.

"I wish I had someone to play with," Emma said softly.

The sun felt warm on her face. Birds chirped in the tall oak tree. But Emma still felt lonely.

Suddenly, she heard a soft snort. Emma looked around. Where did that sound come from?

"Oink, oink!"

Behind the slide stood a small pink pig. The pig had a curly tail and kind brown eyes. Mud covered his tiny hooves.

"Hello there," Emma said. "What are you doing here?"

The pig trotted closer. He looked at the empty swing next to Emma. Then he looked at her with hopeful eyes.

"Do you want to swing too?" Emma asked.

The pig nodded his head up and down. His ears flopped as he moved.

Emma giggled. "But pigs don't swing on playgrounds!"

The pig sat down. His tail stopped wagging. He looked very sad.

Emma felt sorry for the little pig. "Wait," she said. "Maybe we can find a way."

She jumped off her swing. The chains made a jingling sound. Emma walked over to the pig.

"You're too small for the big swing," she said. "But maybe..."

Emma looked around the playground. She saw the baby swings. They had bucket seats and safety bars.

"Perfect!" Emma clapped her hands.

She took the pig's hoof gently. "Come with me."

They walked to the baby swing together. The pig's hooves made soft clicking sounds on the ground.

"Let me help you up," Emma said.

She carefully lifted the pig. He was heavier than she thought! The pig wiggled a little but stayed calm.

Emma placed him in the bucket swing. His bottom fit perfectly. His legs dangled over the sides.

"Hold on tight," Emma said.

She gave the swing a gentle push. The pig went forward slowly. His eyes grew wide with surprise.

"Wheee!" squealed the pig.

Emma laughed. The pig was having fun! She pushed him again, a little higher this time.

The pig's ears flapped in the breeze. His curly tail bounced up and down. He made happy snorting sounds.

"This is wonderful!" the pig said.

Emma was surprised. "You can talk!"

"Of course I can talk," said the pig. "I'm Pip!"

"I'm Emma," she said, pushing Pip higher.

They played together for a long time. Emma pushed Pip on the swing. Then Pip watched Emma go down the slide.

They played hide and seek behind the trees. Pip was very good at hiding. His small size helped him squeeze into tiny spaces.

"You're the best friend ever," Emma told Pip.

"You're my best friend too," Pip said.

The sun began to set. Orange and pink colors filled the sky. It was time to go home.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" Emma asked.

"Every day," promised Pip. "We can swing together again."

Emma hugged Pip gently. He smelled like sunshine and fresh grass. His fur felt soft and warm.

"I was lonely before," Emma said. "But now I have you."

"Friends make everything better," Pip said wisely.

They walked to the playground gate together. Emma's mom was waiting in the car.

"Who's your new friend?" Mom asked.

"This is Pip," Emma said proudly. "He's my swinging buddy."

Mom smiled. "He looks like a very special pig."

"The most special," Emma agreed.

Pip waved goodbye with his hoof. "See you tomorrow, Emma!"

"See you tomorrow, Pip!"

Emma skipped to the car. She wasn't lonely anymore. Tomorrow would bring another day of swinging and playing with her new best friend.

The playground would never feel empty again. Emma and Pip would fill it with laughter, friendship, and joy.

Sometimes the best friends come when you need them most. And sometimes they're exactly who you never expected them to be.

The end.
