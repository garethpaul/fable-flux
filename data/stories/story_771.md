# Story story_771

**Lily and the Lonely Sheep**

In a cozy living room with soft blue walls, a little white sheep sat all alone. Her name was Snowball. She had fluffy wool that felt like cotton clouds. Her pink nose twitched sadly.

Snowball pushed a red ball across the carpet. The ball rolled under the big brown couch. She sighed a tiny sheep sigh. Playing by herself was no fun at all.

"Baa," she said quietly. "I wish I had a friend."

The living room felt very big and empty. Sunlight streamed through the tall windows. It made golden squares on the soft rug. But even the pretty light couldn't make Snowball feel better.

She tried building blocks next. Stack, stack, stack went the colorful towers. But when they fell down, no one was there to clap. No one giggled with her. Snowball's ears drooped down.

Just then, footsteps pattered on the wooden floor. A little girl with curly brown hair peeked around the corner. Her name was Lily. She wore a yellow dress with tiny daisies on it.

"Oh!" said Lily softly. "A sheep in our living room!"

Snowball looked up with big, hopeful eyes. Her tail wiggled just a little bit.

"You look sad," Lily said. She sat down on the fluffy carpet. "Are you lonely?"

"Baa," nodded Snowball. Her voice was small and shy.

Lily's face lit up like sunshine. "Would you like to play with me?"

Snowball jumped up on her four little hooves. "Baa, baa!" she said happily. That meant yes in sheep talk.

"What should we play first?" asked Lily. She looked around the room thoughtfully.

Snowball nudged the red ball with her nose. It rolled toward Lily's feet.

"Ball!" said Lily. "Great idea!"

They rolled the ball back and forth. Lily giggled when Snowball bumped it with her fluffy head. The ball bounced off the couch and spun in circles. Both friends laughed together.

"Your turn to pick," said Lily.

Snowball trotted to the toy box. She pulled out soft stuffed animals with her teeth. A brown bear, a yellow duck, and a striped cat tumbled onto the rug.

"Tea party!" clapped Lily.

They arranged the animals in a circle. Lily brought pretend cups and plates from her play kitchen. Snowball carefully carried a tiny teapot in her mouth.

"Would you like some tea, Miss Snowball?" asked Lily politely.

"Baa, please!" said Snowball. She tried to hold her cup with her hooves.

Lily helped her friend drink the pretend tea. It tasted like friendship and giggles. The stuffed animals were very good guests. They sat quietly and listened to the friends chat.

Next, they built the tallest block tower ever. Lily stacked the big blocks. Snowball pushed the small ones into place with her nose. When their tower reached the coffee table, they both cheered.

"We make a great team!" said Lily.

"Baa!" agreed Snowball. Her wool bounced as she nodded.

The afternoon flew by like a happy dream. They played hide and seek behind the curtains. They danced to music from Lily's toy radio. Snowball's hooves clicked on the floor. Lily twirled in her daisy dress.

When the sun started setting, they snuggled together on the big couch. Lily's mom brought them warm milk and cookies. The cookies were shaped like stars and smelled like vanilla.

"I'm so glad I found you," whispered Lily. She gently petted Snowball's soft wool.

"Baa," whispered Snowball back. She nuzzled Lily's cheek with her pink nose.

From that day on, Snowball was never lonely again. Every morning, she would wait by the living room window. Soon she would see Lily running down the hallway, ready to play.

They became the very best of friends. The living room became their special place. It was filled with laughter, games, and the warm feeling of friendship.

And whenever other toys felt lonely, Lily and Snowball would invite them to play too. Because they learned that friendship grows bigger when you share it with others.

The end.
