# Story story_540

**Splash and Friends**

Hazel the horse loved the big blue pond. Every sunny morning, she would gallop down the green hill. Her hooves made happy clip-clop sounds on the soft grass.

The pond water felt cool and wonderful. Hazel would splash with her front legs. She would dip her nose in the water. Sometimes she would wade in up to her belly.

But playing alone made Hazel feel sad. She wished she had a friend to share the fun.

One bright morning, Hazel heard soft footsteps. Moo-moo sounds came from behind the tall oak tree. A gentle brown cow walked slowly toward the pond.

"Hello," said the cow in a sweet voice. "I'm Bessie. I watch you play here every day."

Hazel's ears perked up. "You do?"

"Yes," said Bessie. "The water looks so nice and cool. But I'm scared to go in."

Hazel's heart felt warm. "Would you like to try together?"

Bessie's big brown eyes looked worried. "I don't know how to play in water."

"I can show you," said Hazel kindly. "We can start very slowly."

Hazel walked to the pond's edge. The water lapped gently at the muddy shore. She put one hoof in the shallow water.

"See? It's just like a big puddle," Hazel said.

Bessie took tiny steps forward. Her black hooves touched the cool water. "Oh! It feels nice!"

"Now try both front feet," encouraged Hazel.

Bessie stepped in with both hooves. The water made little ripples around her legs. "This is fun!"

Hazel smiled big. "Want to try splashing?"

The two friends stood side by side. Hazel showed Bessie how to lift her leg. Together they made small splashes. Water droplets sparkled in the sunshine.

"Look at the pretty drops!" laughed Bessie.

The friends splashed bigger and bigger. Soon they were both giggling. Water flew everywhere in shiny arcs.

Bessie tried to copy Hazel's movements. She lifted her leg high and made a huge splash. Water soaked both animals from head to tail.

"That was amazing!" cheered Hazel.

They played follow the leader in the shallow water. Hazel would take three steps left. Bessie would copy her. Then Bessie would bob her head up and down. Hazel would do the same thing.

The warm sun dried their wet fur. But they kept playing and splashing together.

"I love having a friend," said Bessie happily.

"Me too," said Hazel. "Playing together is much better than playing alone."

They discovered new games. They would see who could make the biggest splash. They would walk in circles and make whirlpools. They even learned to splash in rhythm together.

When the sun got very hot, they would stand in the deeper water. The cool pond felt wonderful on their warm bodies.

"Thank you for teaching me," said Bessie. "I was scared before. But you helped me be brave."

"Thank you for being my friend," said Hazel. "You made my lonely heart feel happy again."

As the day ended, they walked out of the pond together. Their fur dripped with cool water. They felt tired but very happy.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" asked Hazel hopefully.

"Every day!" promised Bessie. "We can be pond friends forever."

From that day on, Hazel and Bessie met at the pond every morning. They splashed and played together. They shared stories and jokes. They helped each other try new things.

Other animals would stop to watch their fun. The pond became a place full of laughter and friendship.

Hazel never felt lonely again. She had learned that the best adventures happen when you share them with a good friend.

And Bessie learned that trying new things isn't scary when you have someone kind to help you.

Every evening, they would say goodbye at the pond's edge. Both friends would smile, knowing tomorrow would bring another day of fun together.

The pond sparkled in the sunset, waiting for two best friends to return.

The end.
