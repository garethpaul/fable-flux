# Teddy's Garden Discovery

The morning sun peeked through Teddy's bedroom window. Golden light danced across his fuzzy brown fur. 

"Time for garden time!" called Mama from downstairs.

Teddy pulled his soft blanket over his head. Garden time meant dirt under his paws. Garden time meant bugs crawling everywhere. Garden time meant getting his fur messy.

"I don't want to go outside," Teddy mumbled into his pillow.

But Mama's gentle footsteps came up the stairs. She sat on his bed and stroked his ears.

"The garden is waiting for us, little bear," she said softly. "Just for a few minutes?"

Teddy's tummy rumbled. Maybe he could find some berries outside. He slowly climbed out of bed and followed Mama to the back door.

The garden looked big and wild today. Green leaves rustled in the breeze. Colorful flowers swayed back and forth. Teddy wrinkled his nose at the earthy smell.

"What should we do first?" asked Mama, putting on her yellow gardening gloves.

"I don't know," Teddy said quietly. He sat on the wooden steps. His paws stayed clean and safe.

Mama began pulling weeds near the tomato plants. She hummed a cheerful tune. The melody floated through the air like butterfly wings.

Suddenly, something caught Teddy's eye. A tiny green sprout pushed through the dark soil. It was so small and brave. Teddy had never noticed it before.

"Mama, look!" Teddy pointed with his paw. "A baby plant!"

"Oh my!" Mama smiled. "That's our sunflower seed from last week. It's growing!"

Teddy crept closer. The little sprout had two perfect leaves. They were bright green and shiny. Teddy touched one gently with his soft paw.

"It's so smooth," he whispered.

"Would you like to water it?" Mama asked.

Teddy nodded eagerly. Mama brought him a small blue watering can. It was just the right size for bear paws. Cool water splashed softly onto the soil around the sprout.

"Drink up, little sunflower," Teddy said.

As he watered, Teddy noticed more amazing things. Red ladybugs with black spots climbed up green stems. Purple butterflies visited yellow marigolds. Busy bees buzzed from flower to flower.

"The garden is full of friends!" Teddy exclaimed.

He found three fat earthworms wiggling in the dirt. They weren't scary at all. They were helping the plants grow strong.

"Can I dig a little hole?" Teddy asked.

"Of course," Mama said. "Here's a small shovel."

Teddy dug carefully in the soft earth. The soil felt cool and crumbly between his claws. He planted new carrot seeds in neat little rows.

"One, two, three, four," he counted as he dropped each seed.

A robin landed nearby and tilted its head at Teddy. Its red breast was bright as a cherry. It chirped a sweet hello song.

"Hi there, Robin," Teddy said. "I'm planting carrots."

The robin chirped again and flew to a nearby branch. It seemed to say, "Good job, Teddy!"

Soon Teddy's paws were muddy and brown. His fur had bits of leaves stuck in it. But he didn't mind at all. He felt proud of his hard work.

"Look at our beautiful garden," Mama said, putting her arm around Teddy.

Teddy looked around with new eyes. The tomatoes were growing tall and green. Pink roses climbed up the wooden fence. His tiny sunflower sprout stood bravely in its spot.

"I helped make it beautiful," Teddy said with a big smile.

"You certainly did," Mama agreed. "You're a wonderful gardener."

As they walked back to the house, Teddy's heart felt warm and happy. His paws were dirty, but they had done important work. He had fed plants and made friends with bugs and birds.

"Mama," Teddy said as they reached the door. "Can we do garden time again tomorrow?"

"Of course, my little gardener," Mama said, kissing the top of his fuzzy head.

That night, Teddy dreamed of sunflowers growing tall as trees. In his dreams, he danced with butterflies and sang with robins. Garden time wasn't scary anymore.

It was magical.

The end.
