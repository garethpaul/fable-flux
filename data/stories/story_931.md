# Story story_931

**Mama Mouse and the Big School Day**

Mama Mouse lived in a cozy burrow under the big oak tree. She had soft brown fur and kind, twinkling eyes. Today was special. Today, her little mouse children would start school for the very first time.

"Time to wake up, my darlings!" Mama called softly. She peeked into their tiny beds. Little Pip and Squeaky were still sleeping. Their whiskers twitched as they dreamed.

Mama's tummy felt funny. Her paws felt shaky. She had never walked her babies to school before. What if she got lost? What if the other mamas knew more than her? What if she said the wrong thing?

"I feel nervous," Mama whispered to herself. Her heart went thump-thump-thump like a woodpecker on the oak tree.

Pip opened one sleepy eye. "Mama, are you okay?" he asked. His voice was small and worried.

"Yes, sweet one," Mama said. But her voice wobbled like jelly. "Let's get ready for your big day!"

Mama helped Pip and Squeaky wash their faces. She packed their lunch boxes with cheese crumbs and sunflower seeds. The food smelled nutty and delicious. But Mama's nervous feeling grew bigger and bigger.

"Maybe I should stay home," Mama thought. "Maybe Daddy Mouse can take them instead."

Mama tried her first idea. She called to Daddy Mouse. "Could you walk the children to school?" she asked hopefully.

"Oh my," said Daddy Mouse. "I have to gather acorns today. The winter is coming soon." He looked very busy with his brown sack.

Mama's heart sank like a pebble in the pond. She would have to be brave. Her little ones needed her.

"It's okay, Mama," squeaked little Squeaky. "We can walk together. You know lots of things!"

Mama looked at her children's bright, trusting faces. Their eyes sparkled like morning dewdrops. They believed in her. Maybe she could believe in herself too.

Mama tried her second idea. She took three deep breaths. The air smelled like fresh grass and wildflowers. She counted to ten slowly. She reminded herself of all the things she did know.

"I know how to make the best cheese soup," she said out loud. "I know how to tell bedtime stories. I know how to give the warmest hugs."

Pip clapped his tiny paws. "You know everything, Mama!"

Mama felt a little spark of courage in her chest. It grew warmer and brighter. She stood up straight and smoothed her fur.

"Let's go on an adventure," Mama said with a smile.

Hand in hand, they walked through the meadow. The morning sun felt warm on their backs. Butterflies danced around purple flowers. Birds sang happy songs in the trees above.

At school, Mama met other animal parents. Mrs. Rabbit looked nervous too. Her ears twitched with worry. Mr. Squirrel's tail was all fluffy with excitement.

"Is this your first time too?" asked Mrs. Rabbit kindly.

"Yes," said Mama Mouse. "I was feeling scared. But we made it here together."

"Me too!" said Mrs. Rabbit. "But look how happy our children are."

Mama looked around. Pip and Squeaky were already making friends. They were laughing and playing with other little animals. Their nervous mama had brought them safely to this wonderful place.

The school bell rang like a gentle chime. Teacher Owl smiled warmly at all the families. "Welcome to our school," she hooted softly. "Every parent here is doing their very best."

Mama's nervous feeling melted away like snow in spring. She had done it! She had been brave when her children needed her most.

"See you after school, my loves," Mama called. She waved goodbye with confidence.

Walking home, Mama felt proud and happy. The meadow looked more beautiful than ever. She had learned something important today. Being nervous was okay. Trying even when scared was what made her a good mama.

Sometimes the bravest thing is taking the first step forward.

The end.
