# Freddy the Muddy Frog

On Farmer Mae's sunny farm lived a little green frog named Freddy. He loved hopping through the tall corn. He loved chasing buzzing bees. Most of all, he loved rolling in the soft, squishy mud.

Every morning, Freddy would hop to his favorite mud puddle. Splish! Splash! Squish! He would roll and tumble until he was covered from head to toe. The brown mud felt cool and wonderful on his skin.

"Time for your bath, Freddy!" called Mama Frog from the clean pond.

Freddy's big eyes went wide. "No thank you!" he croaked loudly. "I like being muddy!"

Mama Frog hopped closer. Her green skin sparkled in the sunshine. "But Freddy, you need to wash off that mud. You're so dirty I can barely see your beautiful green spots!"

Freddy looked down at himself. He was completely brown with mud. But he didn't care one bit.

"I don't want a bath!" Freddy said. He hopped away as fast as his little legs could carry him.

First, Freddy tried hiding behind the big red barn. He squeezed himself into a tiny corner. But Mama Frog found him right away.

"Come out, little one," she said gently. "The water is nice and warm."

"No!" croaked Freddy. He hopped away again.

Next, Freddy tried hiding under the farmer's old wheelbarrow. He made himself very small and very quiet. But his muddy footprints led right to his hiding spot.

"I can see those muddy prints," laughed Mama Frog. "Bath time, Freddy!"

"Never!" said Freddy. Off he hopped once more.

Then Freddy had a clever idea. He would hide in his mud puddle! Mama would never think to look there. He jumped right in and sank down low. Only his eyes peeked above the surface.

But something felt different. The mud was getting thicker and stickier. It felt heavy on his skin. Freddy tried to hop out, but his legs felt slow and tired.

"Help!" he called. "I'm stuck!"

Mama Frog heard his little voice. She hopped over quickly. "Oh my! You're covered in too much mud, sweetie. I can't even grab you!"

Freddy felt scared. The thick mud was making it hard to move. "What can we do?" he asked.

"We need to wash some of this mud off," said Mama Frog. "Then I can help pull you out."

Farmer Mae heard the commotion. She brought her gentle garden hose. "Let's help little Freddy," she said kindly.

The cool water sprayed softly on Freddy's back. Slowly, the heavy mud began to wash away. His green skin started to show through.

"That feels good!" Freddy said, surprised. The water was refreshing and clean.

More mud washed off. Freddy felt lighter and lighter. Soon he could move his legs again! Mama Frog reached in and helped him hop out safely.

"Thank you!" croaked Freddy. He looked at his reflection in the clean pond water. His green skin was bright and shiny. His yellow spots sparkled like tiny suns.

"You look wonderful!" said Mama Frog, giving him a big hug.

Freddy felt amazing. He was clean and fresh and could hop so much better! "Being clean feels really nice," he admitted.

"You can still play in the mud," said Mama Frog. "But taking baths helps keep you healthy and strong."

From that day on, Freddy still loved playing in the mud. But every evening, he would hop to the pond for his bath. He learned that being clean felt just as good as being muddy.

"Bath time!" Mama Frog would call.

"Coming!" Freddy would answer happily.

And he would splash and play in the clean water, watching his green skin sparkle in the sunset. Sometimes the best solutions come when we really need them most.
