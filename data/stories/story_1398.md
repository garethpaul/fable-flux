# Story story_1398

**Pinky and Grandma's Garden Day**

Pinky the pig sat alone in the big garden. The sun felt warm on her pink nose. She rolled in the soft dirt. She sniffed the pretty flowers. But something felt missing.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," Pinky said sadly. She looked at the empty garden. No one was there to share the fun.

Pinky tried to play by herself. She rolled down the grassy hill. It was fun for a minute. Then she felt lonely again. She dug little holes with her hooves. The dirt felt cool and squishy. But it wasn't the same without a friend.

Just then, Grandma Rose walked into the garden. She had gray hair and kind eyes. Her hands were gentle and wrinkled. She carried a big watering can.

"Hello there, little pig," Grandma Rose said sweetly. "You look sad. What's wrong?"

"I'm lonely," Pinky said. "I want someone to play with me."

Grandma Rose smiled. "Well, I love gardens too. May I play with you?"

Pinky's tail wiggled with joy. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" Grandma Rose laughed. Her laugh sounded like tiny bells.

First, they watered the flowers together. Grandma Rose held the heavy can. Pinky helped aim the water. The flowers sparkled with water drops. They smelled like sunshine and sweetness.

"This is fun!" Pinky squealed happily.

Next, they planted new seeds. Grandma Rose showed Pinky how to dig small holes. Pinky used her snout to make perfect holes. They dropped tiny seeds inside. Then they covered them with soft soil.

"These will grow into beautiful carrots," Grandma Rose explained. "We can share them when they're ready."

Pinky loved learning new things. Her heart felt warm and happy.

Then they played hide and seek. Pinky hid behind the big sunflowers. The yellow petals tickled her ears. Grandma Rose pretended she couldn't find her.

"Where could Pinky be?" Grandma Rose wondered out loud. "Is she behind the roses? No! Is she by the tomatoes? No!"

Pinky giggled and giggled. Her giggles sounded like little snorts.

"Found you!" Grandma Rose said. She gave Pinky a gentle hug.

Now it was Grandma Rose's turn to hide. She crouched behind the apple tree. Pinky found her right away. But they both laughed anyway.

After that, they had a picnic on the soft grass. Grandma Rose brought apple slices and crackers. Pinky munched happily. The apples tasted sweet and crunchy.

"Thank you for playing with me," Pinky said. "I was so lonely before."

"Thank you for letting me join you," Grandma Rose replied. "I was lonely too. My grandchildren live far away."

They sat together in comfortable silence. Birds chirped in the trees above them. A gentle breeze made the leaves dance. Everything felt peaceful and right.

"Can we be garden friends?" Pinky asked hopefully.

"I would love that very much," Grandma Rose said. "We can meet here every day."

Pinky's heart felt full of joy. She had found the perfect friend. Grandma Rose felt happy too. She had found someone special to share her garden with.

From that day on, Pinky and Grandma Rose met in the garden every morning. They watered plants together. They planted new flowers. They shared snacks and stories.

Sometimes they just sat quietly. They watched butterflies dance around the flowers. They listened to bees buzzing busily. They enjoyed the warm sunshine together.

The garden became even more beautiful. It was filled with love and laughter. Other animals started visiting too. But Pinky and Grandma Rose remained the very best of friends.

Every evening, they said goodbye with big hugs. Pinky would roll in her favorite dirt spot. Grandma Rose would wave from her porch.

"See you tomorrow, dear friend," Grandma Rose would call.

"See you tomorrow!" Pinky would call back.

The garden had brought them together. Now they would never be lonely again. They had each other, and that made everything perfect.

Their friendship grew stronger every day, just like the beautiful flowers in their special garden.

The end.
