# Story story_1212

Max sat on the big red swing. His feet barely touched the soft grass below. The swing moved back and forth slowly. Back and forth, back and forth.

The playground was quiet today. No other children were here. Max felt lonely. He wished he had a friend to play with.

The sun felt warm on his face. Birds chirped in the tall oak tree. Max pushed himself higher on the swing. The chains creaked softly. Squeak, squeak, squeak.

"I wish someone would play with me," Max said out loud.

Suddenly, something green caught his eye. A small frog sat near the swing set. The frog had bright yellow spots. His eyes were big and round.

"Ribbit!" said the frog. "Ribbit, ribbit!"

Max stopped swinging. He looked down at the little frog. "Hello there, little guy," Max said softly.

The frog hopped closer. Hop, hop, hop. He sat right under Max's swing.

"Do you want to play?" Max asked.

The frog tilted his head. His throat puffed out. "Ribbit!" he said again.

Max climbed off his swing carefully. He sat in the cool grass next to the frog. The grass felt tickly on his legs.

"My name is Max," he said. "What's your name?"

The frog just looked at him. His eyes blinked slowly.

"I'll call you Freddy," Max decided. "Do you like that name?"

Freddy the frog hopped in a little circle. Max giggled. The sound made him feel happy inside.

"Want to swing with me, Freddy?" Max asked.

But Freddy was too small for the big swing. Max thought hard. How could they play together?

"I have an idea!" Max said. He cupped his hands gently. "Would you like a ride?"

Freddy hopped right into Max's hands. His skin felt cool and smooth. Max could feel Freddy's tiny heart beating fast.

Max stood up slowly. He held Freddy close to his chest. Then Max climbed back onto the swing.

"Hold on tight, Freddy!" Max said.

Max pushed off with his feet. The swing moved gently. Freddy sat safely in Max's lap. His big eyes looked all around.

"Ribbit!" Freddy said. He sounded excited.

Higher and higher they swung. The wind blew through Max's hair. Freddy's spots seemed to sparkle in the sunlight.

"This is fun!" Max laughed. "Do you like swinging, Freddy?"

"Ribbit, ribbit!" Freddy answered happily.

They swung together for a long time. Max felt the warm sun on his back. He smelled the sweet flowers nearby. Everything felt perfect.

After a while, Max slowed the swing down. "Want to try the slide next?" he asked.

Freddy hopped up and down in Max's hands. That meant yes!

Max carried Freddy to the yellow slide. He set Freddy at the top gently. "Ready?" Max asked.

Freddy slid down the smooth slide. "Wheeee!" Max said for both of them. Freddy landed softly in the sand below.

"Ribbit, ribbit, ribbit!" Freddy said. He hopped back up to Max.

Max and Freddy played on everything. The seesaw. The monkey bars. The spinning wheel. They had so much fun together.

When it was time to go, Max felt sad. "Will I see you again, Freddy?" he asked.

Freddy hopped to the edge of the playground. He looked back at Max. Then Freddy hopped to a small pond nearby.

"Oh!" Max said. "You live right here!"

Max visited the playground every day after that. Freddy was always waiting by the swings. They became the very best of friends.

Sometimes other children came to play too. Max would introduce them to Freddy. Soon everyone loved the friendly little frog.

Max learned something special that day. Friends can come in all shapes and sizes. Even small green frogs with yellow spots can be wonderful friends.

The playground was never quiet again. It was filled with laughter and joy. Max and Freddy made sure of that.

And they all lived happily ever after.

The end.
