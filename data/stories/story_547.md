# Story story_547

**Bella's Big Bath Problem**

Little Bella Duck lived in a sparkly blue pond. She loved to splash and play all day long. Her yellow feathers gleamed in the warm sunshine.

One morning, Mama Duck called out sweetly. "Bath time, Bella! Come get clean."

Bella's eyes grew wide. She looked down at herself. Her fluffy feathers were covered in green pond slime. Bits of leaves stuck to her wings. She smelled like muddy water.

"No bath!" Bella quacked loudly. "I don't want to!"

She paddled away as fast as her little webbed feet could go. The water made soft splashing sounds around her.

Mama Duck swam closer. "Bella, you need a bath. You're very dirty."

"I like being dirty!" Bella said. She dove under the water to hide.

But when she came back up, she felt itchy. The dried mud made her skin feel tight and scratchy. She tried to preen her feathers with her orange beak, but they felt sticky and gross.

Bella had an idea. Maybe she could clean herself without taking a real bath.

She found a big lily pad floating nearby. Bella climbed on top and started rolling around. The smooth, wet leaf felt cool against her feathers.

"This will work!" she quacked happily.

She rolled left. She rolled right. She wiggled and squirmed all over the lily pad.

But when she looked at herself in the water, she gasped. She was even dirtier than before! Green lily pad slime covered her from head to tail. Now she smelled like pond weeds too.

"Oh no!" Bella cried. Her bottom lip started to tremble.

Just then, her friend Freddy Frog hopped over. His green skin sparkled clean in the sunlight.

"Hi Bella!" Freddy croaked cheerfully. "Want to play?"

Bella felt embarrassed. "I'm too dirty to play. I tried to clean myself, but I made it worse."

Freddy's big eyes blinked kindly. "Why don't you ask your mama for help?"

Bella thought about this. Maybe Mama Duck would know what to do.

She swam back to where Mama was waiting patiently. The warm water felt good on her tired little body.

"Mama," Bella said in a small voice. "I tried to clean myself. But I got even messier."

Mama Duck smiled gently. "That's okay, sweetie. Sometimes we need help. Would you like me to show you how to take a proper bath?"

Bella nodded slowly. "Yes, please."

Mama Duck led her to a shallow, sunny spot. The water here was crystal clear and warm.

"First, we dip our heads underwater," Mama said. She showed Bella how to duck down and come back up.

Bella copied her mama. The clean water felt refreshing on her face.

"Now we flap our wings gently," Mama demonstrated. "This helps the water reach everywhere."

Bella flapped her little wings. Clean water splashed all around her. It felt wonderful!

"Finally, we shake our tail feathers," Mama laughed.

Bella wiggled her bottom. Clean droplets flew everywhere, making tiny rainbows in the sunshine.

After a few minutes, Bella looked at herself in the clear water. Her feathers were bright yellow again. She smelled fresh and clean like morning air.

"I did it!" Bella quacked proudly. "I'm all clean!"

"You certainly are," Mama said, nuzzling her daughter gently. "How do you feel?"

Bella stretched her clean wings wide. "I feel amazing! Light and fluffy and fresh!"

Freddy Frog hopped over again. "Wow, Bella! You look great! Want to play now?"

"Yes!" Bella said excitedly. "And Freddy? Thank you for telling me to ask for help."

From that day on, Bella never minded bath time. She learned that asking for help wasn't giving up. It was being smart and brave.

And every morning, she and Mama Duck would take their bath together in the sunny shallow water, splashing and laughing with joy.

The pond sparkled around them, and Bella's heart felt as clean and bright as her beautiful yellow feathers.

The end.
