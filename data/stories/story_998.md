# Story story_998

**Kira's Colorful Discovery**

The big clock on the wall said three o'clock. Miss Emma clapped her hands together. "Art time, everyone!" she called out cheerfully.

All the children jumped up from their reading mats. They ran to the art table with big smiles. But Kira stayed sitting on her mat. She looked at her shoes and frowned.

"Come on, Kira!" called her friend Sam. "We're making pictures today!"

Kira shook her head slowly. "I don't like art time," she whispered. "My pictures never look right. They're always messy."

Miss Emma walked over to Kira. She sat down on the soft mat. "What's wrong, sweetie?" she asked gently.

"I'm not good at making art," Kira said sadly. "My crayons always go outside the lines. My colors get all mixed up."

Miss Emma smiled warmly. "Would you like to try something different today?" she asked. "Something special?"

Kira looked up with curious eyes. "What do you mean?"

Miss Emma stood up and held out her hand. "Come see," she said with a twinkle in her eye.

Kira took Miss Emma's soft hand. They walked to a corner of the room. There sat a big white paper on an easel. Next to it were bottles of bright paint.

"This is finger painting," Miss Emma explained. "You use your fingers instead of brushes. There are no lines to stay inside."

Kira touched the smooth paper gently. "Can I really use my fingers?" she asked.

"Of course!" Miss Emma said. "Watch this." She dipped her finger in red paint. She made a big circle on the paper. "See? It's like magic!"

Kira giggled softly. The red circle looked like a happy sun. She dipped her finger in yellow paint. She made little dots around the red circle.

"Those look like sunshine rays!" Sam said, walking over to watch.

Kira smiled bigger now. She tried blue paint next. She made wavy lines across the paper. "It's like water!" she said excitedly.

The blue paint felt cool and smooth on her finger. It made a whooshing sound as she moved it. The paper felt bumpy under her hand.

"Can I add green?" Kira asked, bouncing a little.

"Add whatever you want," Miss Emma said. "This is your picture."

Kira dipped two fingers in green paint. She made tall lines that looked like grass. Then she added purple flowers with tiny dots.

Other children came to watch. "Wow, Kira!" said Maya. "Your picture is so pretty!"

"I love the colors!" added Ben.

Kira felt warm and happy inside. Her picture was getting bigger and more colorful. She added orange butterflies and pink clouds.

The paint squished between her fingers. It made her laugh out loud. She wasn't worried about staying in lines anymore. There were no lines to worry about!

"This feels so good!" Kira said, adding more blue to her water. "The paint is so squishy and fun!"

Miss Emma nodded. "Art is about having fun and being creative," she said. "There's no wrong way to make art."

Kira stepped back to look at her picture. It was full of bright colors and happy shapes. The sun smiled down at the grass and flowers. Butterflies danced in the sky.

"I made this!" Kira said proudly. "I really made this beautiful picture!"

"You sure did," Miss Emma said, giving Kira a gentle hug. "And look at your hands. They're covered in rainbow colors!"

Kira looked at her painted fingers. They were purple and yellow and green. She wiggled them and laughed. "I'm a rainbow!" she said.

When art time was over, Kira carefully carried her picture to the drying rack. She couldn't stop smiling at her colorful creation.

"Miss Emma," Kira said, tugging on her teacher's shirt. "What are we making tomorrow?"

Miss Emma's eyes sparkled. "It's a surprise," she said. "But I think you'll love it."

Kira clapped her painted hands together. "I can't wait for art time tomorrow!" she said happily.

From that day on, Kira was always first in line for art time. She learned that making art wasn't about being perfect. It was about having fun and letting her imagination fly free like a colorful kite in the sky.

The end.
