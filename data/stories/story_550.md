# Story story_550

**Freddy Fox and the Scary Hallway**

Freddy Fox lived in a cozy house with his mama and papa. He loved playing with his toy cars. He loved eating crunchy apples. But there was one thing Freddy did not love. He did not love bedtime.

Every night, Freddy had to walk down the long, dark hallway. The hallway led to his bedroom. But the hallway was very scary. It made strange creaking sounds. Shadows danced on the walls. The floorboards went "squeak, squeak, squeak" under his paws.

"Time for bed, Freddy," called Mama Fox.

Freddy's tummy felt all twisty. His ears drooped down low. "Do I have to go?" he whispered.

"Yes, little one," said Papa Fox kindly. "Sleep helps you grow big and strong."

Freddy stood at the start of the hallway. It looked so long and dark. The shadows seemed to wiggle and move. His heart went thump-thump-thump in his chest.

"I can do this," Freddy said to himself. He took one tiny step. Then another. But then he heard a loud CREAK! Freddy's fur stood up straight. He ran back to the living room as fast as his legs could carry him.

"Mama! Papa!" cried Freddy. "The hallway is too scary!"

Mama Fox gave him a warm hug. Her fur smelled like lavender and cookies. "What makes it scary, sweetie?"

"It's dark and creaky," said Freddy. "And there are scary shadows everywhere."

Papa Fox scratched his chin thoughtfully. "Maybe we can make it less scary. What if you take your flashlight?"

Freddy's eyes lit up. "Yes! My red flashlight!"

He grabbed his bright red flashlight from the toy box. The beam was strong and yellow. Freddy felt much braver now.

"Here I go," said Freddy. He clicked on the flashlight. The yellow light made a circle on the wall. Freddy walked into the hallway. His flashlight pushed away some of the darkness.

But then the batteries started to get weak. The light got dimmer and dimmer. Soon it was just a tiny orange glow. The shadows came creeping back. The hallway still went "creak, creak, creak."

Freddy felt scared again. He hurried back to his parents.

"The flashlight got too dim," said Freddy sadly. His whiskers drooped.

Mama Fox hugged him tight. "You were very brave to try," she said. "What else might help?"

Freddy thought hard. He tapped his paw against his nose. "Maybe... maybe I could sing?"

"That's a wonderful idea!" said Papa Fox. "Singing makes everything better."

Freddy took a deep breath. He thought of his favorite song about dancing bunnies. He started to sing in a clear, sweet voice:

"Hop, hop, hop go the bunnies, dancing in the sun so bright. Skip, skip, skip go the bunnies, everything will be alright!"

His voice echoed softly in the hallway. The sound was friendly and warm. Freddy kept singing as he walked. His paws made a happy rhythm on the floor. Step-step, step-step, in time with his song.

The shadows were still there. The floor still creaked. But now they seemed friendly. Like they were dancing along to his song. The creaks sounded like music. The shadows looked like they were swaying.

Before Freddy knew it, he was at his bedroom door. His cozy bed was waiting for him. His stuffed rabbit sat on his pillow.

"I did it!" Freddy called happily. "I walked the whole hallway!"

Mama and Papa Fox came to tuck him in. They were so proud of their brave little fox.

"You didn't give up," said Mama Fox, kissing his forehead.

"You tried a new way when the first way didn't work," said Papa Fox.

Freddy snuggled under his soft blue blanket. "Tomorrow I'll sing again," he said sleepily. "The hallway isn't scary when I sing."

From that night on, Freddy sang his bunny song every evening. Soon, bedtime became his favorite time of day. He learned that being brave doesn't mean not feeling scared. Being brave means trying again when something doesn't work.

And every night, the hallway filled with Freddy's happy song.

The end.
