# Teddy's Big Adventure

Teddy was a soft, brown bear who loved playing in the forest. She had fuzzy ears and a pink nose. Her favorite thing was her bright red ball. It bounced high and rolled fast. Teddy took it everywhere she went.

One sunny morning, Teddy skipped into the forest. The green leaves rustled softly overhead. Birds chirped happy songs. Flowers smelled sweet and fresh. Teddy threw her red ball up high. It sparkled in the sunshine.

"Catch me if you can!" Teddy giggled. She tossed the ball again and again. It bounced off trees with a soft *thump*. It rolled over mossy rocks. Teddy chased it everywhere.

Then something terrible happened. Teddy threw the ball extra high. It flew up, up, up into the trees. She watched it disappear behind the thick green leaves.

"Oh no!" Teddy cried. "Where did my ball go?"

She looked all around. No red ball anywhere. Teddy's eyes filled with tears. Her favorite ball was gone. What would she do without it?

"I have to find my ball," Teddy said bravely. She wiped her eyes with her paw. "I won't give up."

First, Teddy looked under every bush. She pushed aside the scratchy branches. She crawled on her hands and knees. The dirt felt cool under her paws. But no red ball.

"Maybe it's by the stream," Teddy thought. She walked to the babbling water. It sounded like a gentle song. Teddy looked behind every rock. She checked under the wooden bridge. Still no ball.

Teddy felt sad, but she didn't stop looking. She was determined to find her treasure.

Next, she climbed up a small hill. Her legs felt tired, but she kept going. At the top, she could see far across the forest. She shaded her eyes with her paw.

"Red ball, where are you?" she called out loud. Her voice echoed through the trees.

Then Teddy heard a tiny voice. "Help! Help!"

She followed the sound down the other side of the hill. There, stuck in a thorny bush, was a little blue bird.

"Oh dear," said Teddy. "You're all tangled up!"

The bird looked scared. "I can't get free," he chirped sadly.

Teddy forgot about her ball for a moment. She carefully untangled the bird's wing. The thorns pricked her paws, but she was very gentle.

"Thank you so much!" said the bird, flapping his wings. "You're so kind. What's your name?"

"I'm Teddy," she said. "I'm looking for my red ball. Have you seen it?"

The bird's eyes lit up. "A red ball? Yes! I saw it roll into the old oak tree. There's a hollow at the bottom."

Teddy's heart jumped with joy. "Really? Will you show me?"

The grateful bird led Teddy to the biggest tree in the forest. Its trunk was wide and brown. At the bottom was a small, dark hole.

Teddy got on her tummy. She reached her paw deep into the hollow. She felt around carefully. Her paw touched something round and smooth.

"Got it!" Teddy shouted happily.

She pulled out her beautiful red ball. It was a little dusty but perfectly fine. Teddy hugged it tight against her chest.

"My ball! My wonderful ball!" she laughed.

The blue bird danced in the air. "I'm so happy you found it!"

Teddy bounced her ball gently. "Thank you for helping me," she told her new friend. "I was so worried I'd never see it again."

"You never gave up," said the bird. "That's what made you find it."

Teddy smiled big. She had learned something important today. When things seem lost or hard, don't give up. Keep trying different ways. Ask for help. Help others too.

"Want to play ball with me?" Teddy asked the bird.

"Yes!" chirped her friend.

They played together until the sun began to set. Teddy's ball bounced happily between them. The forest felt warm and friendly. Teddy's heart felt full of joy.

She had found more than just her ball today. She had found a new friend and learned that she was braver than she thought.
