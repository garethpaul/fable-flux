# Tommy and the Magic Jar

Tommy loved exploring the dusty old attic. Sunbeams danced through the small window. They made golden patterns on the wooden floor.

"What treasures will I find today?" Tommy whispered.

The attic smelled like old books and memories. Boxes sat stacked in tall towers. Blankets covered mysterious shapes like sleeping giants.

Tommy tiptoed between the boxes carefully. His footsteps made soft creaking sounds. Then something sparkled in the corner.

"Oh wow!" Tommy gasped.

There sat a beautiful glass jar. It was deep blue like the ocean. Silver sparkles swirled inside like tiny stars. The jar had a tight metal lid.

Tommy picked up the jar gently. It felt smooth and cool in his hands. The sparkles danced when he moved it.

"I wonder what's inside," he said softly.

Tommy tried to twist the lid. It wouldn't budge. He wrapped his small fingers around it. He twisted harder. Still nothing.

"Come on, please open," Tommy pleaded.

The lid stayed stuck tight. Tommy sat down on an old trunk. He looked at the jar sadly.

"Maybe I'm not strong enough," he sighed.

But Tommy didn't give up. He stood up tall. He took a deep breath.

"I can do this," he told himself.

Tommy tried turning the lid the other way. Left, then right, then left again. The lid stayed stuck.

Next, Tommy tapped the lid gently. Tap, tap, tap. He tried opening it again. Still stuck.

"Hmm," Tommy thought out loud. "What else can I try?"

Tommy remembered helping Mama in the kitchen. Sometimes she ran warm water over stuck lids. But there was no water in the attic.

Tommy rubbed the lid with his shirt. Maybe it needed cleaning. He polished it until it shined. Then he tried again. Still stuck.

"Don't give up," Tommy whispered to himself.

Tommy looked around the attic for help. He saw an old rubber mat near the window. Mama used one like it in the kitchen!

"That might help me grip better," Tommy said.

Tommy placed the rubber mat over the lid. Now his hands wouldn't slip. He took another deep breath.

"One, two, three!" Tommy counted.

He twisted with all his might. The mat helped his hands grip tight. Slowly, the lid began to turn!

"It's working!" Tommy cheered quietly.

Twist, twist, twist. The lid turned more and more. Finally, it came off completely!

Tommy looked inside the jar carefully. It was filled with smooth, colorful marbles! They clinked together like tiny bells. Red ones, blue ones, green ones too!

"Beautiful marbles!" Tommy said with wonder.

He poured a few into his palm. They felt cool and smooth. The sunlight made them glow like jewels.

Tommy found a note at the bottom. In wobbly letters, it said: "For a special grandchild who never gives up. Love, Grandpa Joe."

Tommy's eyes grew wide with surprise. Grandpa Joe had left this just for him! But Grandpa Joe lived far away now.

"He knew I would find it," Tommy whispered.

Tommy carefully put the marbles back. He screwed the lid on gently. This was his special treasure now.

He hugged the jar close to his chest. It felt warm from the sunlight and his happiness.

"Thank you for not giving up," Tommy told himself.

Tommy climbed down from the attic carefully. He carried his treasure jar like precious gold.

In the kitchen, Mama smiled at him. "What did you find, sweetie?"

"Grandpa Joe's marble jar," Tommy said proudly. "I opened it all by myself!"

"How wonderful!" Mama said. "What helped you succeed?"

Tommy thought for a moment. "I tried different ways. I didn't give up. And I used the rubber mat!"

"That's smart problem-solving," Mama said warmly.

Tommy beamed with pride. He had learned something important today. When things seem impossible, keep trying new ways. The answer is always there, waiting to be found.

That night, Tommy placed the jar by his bed. The marbles caught the moonlight and sparkled softly. They reminded him that he was brave, smart, and strong.

"Tomorrow I'll try new things too," Tommy whispered sleepily.

The magic jar had taught him the most important lesson of all: never give up on yourself.

The end.
