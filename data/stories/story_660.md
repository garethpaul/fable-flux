# Story story_660

**Puzzle and the Swing Set Friend**

Puzzle sat on the big red swing. She moved back and forth slowly. The playground was quiet and empty. 

Puzzle was a little girl with curly brown hair. She loved to solve puzzles at home. But today she felt lonely. All her friends were busy with their families.

The swing creaked softly in the breeze. Puzzle's shoes dragged in the sandy dirt below. She watched the clouds float by like cotton balls.

"I wish I had someone to play with," Puzzle said quietly.

Just then, she heard a soft snorting sound. A small pink pig waddled toward the swing set. The pig had a curly tail and bright black eyes.

"Oink! Hello there," said the pig cheerfully. "I'm Penny the Pig. You look sad."

Puzzle stopped swinging. She had never met a talking pig before! "Hi, I'm Puzzle. I am a little sad. I'm playing all by myself today."

Penny's ears perked up. "I love to play! Can I swing with you? I've never tried a swing before."

Puzzle smiled for the first time that day. "Yes! But swings might be tricky for pigs."

Penny trotted to the swing next to Puzzle. She tried to climb up. Her little hooves slipped on the smooth seat. She tumbled backward into the soft sand.

"Oops!" Penny giggled. "That was harder than it looked!"

Puzzle hopped off her swing. "Let me help you, Penny."

Puzzle held the swing steady. Penny tried again. This time she managed to sit on the seat. But her short legs couldn't reach the ground to push off.

"I can push you!" Puzzle offered kindly.

Puzzle gave Penny a gentle push. The pig squealed with delight as she soared forward. Her curly tail wiggled with excitement.

"This is amazing!" Penny called out. "I feel like I'm flying!"

Puzzle pushed Penny higher and higher. The pig's happy oinks filled the air. Soon other children heard the joyful sounds. They came running to see what was happening.

"Wow! A pig on a swing!" shouted a boy with red hair.

"That's so cool!" said a girl with pigtails.

More children gathered around. They all wanted to meet Penny. But Penny only had eyes for her new friend Puzzle.

"Puzzle taught me how to swing," Penny told everyone proudly. "She's the best friend ever!"

Puzzle's cheeks turned pink with happiness. She had never felt so special before.

The children took turns pushing both Puzzle and Penny. The playground filled with laughter and squeals of joy. But Puzzle and Penny stayed side by side on their swings.

"Let's play a game!" Puzzle suggested. "I'll call out colors. Everyone wearing that color has to swing extra high!"

"Blue!" Puzzle shouted. Two children in blue shirts pumped their legs hard.

"Pink!" called Penny. She giggled as she swung higher, since she was pink all over.

The game continued until the sun began to set. The orange sky looked like a giant puzzle piece above them.

One by one, the other children went home for dinner. Soon only Puzzle and Penny remained at the swing set.

"Thank you for being my friend today," Puzzle said softly. "I was so lonely before you came."

Penny's tail curled with happiness. "Thank you for teaching me to swing! And for being so kind to me."

They swung gently as the first stars appeared. The evening air smelled like flowers and fresh grass.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" Puzzle asked hopefully.

"Every day!" Penny promised. "We can solve puzzles together too. I love puzzles almost as much as swinging!"

Puzzle clapped her hands with joy. She had found a true friend who shared her interests.

As they walked home together, Puzzle realized something important. The best friendships happen when you're kind to others. And sometimes the most wonderful friends come in the most surprising packages.

From that day on, Puzzle and Penny were inseparable. The swing set became their special meeting place, where their beautiful friendship had begun.

The end.
