# Benny's Colorful Discovery

It was three o'clock on Tuesday afternoon. Time for art time! Mama called from the kitchen. "Benny, come get your art supplies!"

Benny the golden retriever sighed deeply. His fluffy tail dragged on the floor. Art time happened every day after lunch. Every single day. Benny thought it was boring.

"Do I have to?" Benny whimpered softly. He loved running and playing fetch. He loved digging holes in the garden. But sitting still with crayons? That felt hard.

Mama smiled and patted his head. "Art time helps us be creative, Benny. Come try something new today."

Benny walked slowly to his art corner. The small table had paper waiting. Colorful crayons sat in a bright cup. Everything looked the same as always.

Benny picked up a red crayon. He drew a wobbly circle. It looked like nothing special. He sighed again.

Then something wonderful happened.

A tiny drop of water fell. Plip! It landed right on his red circle. The water made the crayon mark spread. It looked like a beautiful flower blooming!

"Oh!" Benny barked with surprise.

He looked up. Sunlight streamed through the window. It made tiny rainbows dance on his paper. The colors sparkled like magic gems.

Benny's tail started wagging slowly.

He picked up a blue crayon. This time he drew with excitement. He made swirling lines across the page. They looked like ocean waves!

The sunlight made the blue shimmer. It looked like real water moving. Benny could almost hear waves crashing.

"Woof!" he barked happily.

Next, Benny grabbed the yellow crayon. He drew a big, bright sun. The real sunlight made it glow. His paper sun looked warm and cheerful.

Benny's tail wagged faster now.

He discovered something amazing. When he held crayons in different ways, they made different marks. Sideways made thick, soft lines. The tip made thin, sharp lines.

Benny drew green grass with sideways strokes. He drew purple flowers with tiny dots. He drew orange butterflies with careful wings.

Each color seemed to dance together. The red flower, blue waves, yellow sun, green grass, purple flowers, and orange butterflies. They made a beautiful picture together.

"This is like magic!" Benny woofed excitedly.

Mama peeked over his shoulder. "Benny, your picture is wonderful! Look how creative you are!"

Benny felt proud and happy. His chest puffed up with joy. Art time wasn't boring anymore. It was like having a rainbow right in his paws!

He drew more pictures. A brown tree with green leaves. A pink house with a red door. A family of stick dogs playing together.

Time flew by like a happy dream.

Before Benny knew it, art time was over. He had made five beautiful pictures. Each one told a different story.

"Can I hang them up?" Benny asked hopefully.

"Of course!" Mama laughed. "Let's put them on the refrigerator."

They hung up all of Benny's artwork. The kitchen looked bright and cheerful. Benny felt like a real artist.

That night at dinner, Benny showed Papa his pictures. Papa's eyes grew wide with amazement.

"These are incredible, Benny! You're very talented."

Benny's tail wagged so hard it knocked over his water bowl. Everyone laughed together.

As bedtime approached, Benny thought about tomorrow. Usually he worried about boring things. But tonight felt different.

"Mama," Benny said sleepily. "What time is art time tomorrow?"

Mama tucked him into his cozy bed. "Three o'clock, just like always."

Benny smiled a big, happy smile. "I can't wait! I want to draw a castle tomorrow. Maybe with dragons and knights!"

"That sounds wonderful, sweetie," Mama whispered.

Benny closed his eyes peacefully. He dreamed of magical colors dancing together. Red dragons breathed blue fire. Yellow knights rode purple horses across green meadows.

Art time had become his favorite time. Every day would bring new colors, new pictures, and new adventures. Benny had discovered that magic was hiding in everyday moments.

He just had to look with creative eyes.

Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough!

The end.
