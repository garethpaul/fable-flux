# Anna and the Storm Clouds

Anna loved the beach more than anywhere. She loved the warm sand between her toes. She loved the sound of waves splashing. She loved building tall sandcastles with her red bucket.

Today, Anna sat on her favorite beach blanket. Her mom was reading nearby. The sun felt warm on Anna's face. Everything was perfect.

But then Anna looked up at the sky. Dark gray clouds were moving closer. They looked big and scary. Anna's tummy felt funny.

"Mom," Anna said quietly. "Those clouds look angry."

Mom looked up from her book. "Those are storm clouds, sweetie. They might bring rain."

Anna's eyes grew wide. "Will the storm hurt us? Will it wash away my sandcastle?"

The clouds rumbled softly in the distance. Anna hugged her knees tight. She had worked so hard on her castle. It had four towers and a deep moat.

"I need to save my castle," Anna said. She stood up and brushed sand off her legs.

First, Anna tried covering her castle. She found a big piece of seaweed. She draped it over the towers carefully. But the seaweed was too small. The wind blew it away.

"That didn't work," Anna said. She looked around for something else.

Next, Anna tried building walls around her castle. She scooped wet sand with her hands. She made a circle around the castle. But the sand walls were too low. They wouldn't stop big raindrops.

Anna felt worried again. The clouds were getting darker. She could hear thunder far away. "Rumble, rumble," it said.

"I won't give up," Anna told herself. "There has to be a way."

Then Anna had a new idea. She ran to her mom's beach bag. Inside, she found a clear plastic container. It was big enough to fit over her whole castle!

"Mom, can I use this?" Anna asked.

"That's very clever thinking," Mom said with a smile. "Yes, you can use it."

Anna carefully placed the container over her sandcastle. It fit perfectly! The castle was safe and dry inside. Anna could still see it through the clear plastic.

Just then, the first raindrops began to fall. "Pitter-patter, pitter-patter," they said on the container. Anna's castle stayed completely dry underneath.

"I did it!" Anna cheered. "My castle is safe!"

The rain fell harder now. Other families packed up their things. But Anna and her mom stayed under their beach umbrella. They watched the rain bounce off Anna's castle protector.

"You didn't give up," Mom said proudly. "You kept trying until you found the answer."

Anna felt warm and happy inside. The storm had seemed so scary at first. But she had solved the problem all by herself. She had saved her special castle.

The rain only lasted twenty minutes. Soon, the sun peeked through the clouds again. Anna carefully lifted the container off her castle. Every tower was perfect. Every wall was still standing.

"My castle made it through the storm!" Anna laughed. She did a little happy dance in the sand.

Other children came over to see Anna's castle. "How did you keep it safe?" they asked.

Anna showed them the container. "I tried different ways," she explained. "This one worked the best."

The children thought Anna was very smart. They asked if she could help them build storm-proof castles too.

Anna spent the rest of the day helping her new friends. She showed them how to find containers and shells for protection. Together, they built the most amazing beach town.

As the sun began to set, Anna looked at all the castles. She felt proud and brave. The storm had seemed scary, but she hadn't let it stop her.

"I learned something today," Anna told her mom as they packed up.

"What did you learn?" Mom asked.

"When something seems hard or scary, I can try different ways to fix it. I don't have to give up."

Mom hugged Anna tight. "That's a very important lesson. You're becoming such a good problem-solver."

Anna smiled as they walked to the car. Tomorrow, she would come back to the beach. She wasn't worried about storms anymore. She knew she could handle whatever came her way.

The waves said goodbye with gentle splashes. Anna waved back, already excited for her next beach adventure.

The end.
