# Benny's Brave Bedtime

In a cozy house with yellow walls, a fluffy golden dog named Benny sat in the long hallway. His tail drooped low. His ears hung down sadly. 

Every night was the same. When the sun went down, Benny felt scared. The hallway grew dark and quiet. Shadows danced on the walls. His cozy dog bed waited at the end of the hall, but Benny was too frightened to walk there alone.

"Woof," Benny whimpered softly. "I don't like bedtime."

Mom heard his sad little bark. She knelt down and petted his soft fur. "What's wrong, sweet Benny?"

"The hallway is too dark," Benny said with a tiny whine. "The shadows look scary. I can't walk to my bed."

Mom's eyes twinkled with understanding. "Let's think of ways to make bedtime better," she said gently.

Benny's first idea was simple. He would run very, very fast! Maybe he could zoom past the scary shadows before they noticed him.

Benny took three big steps back. He wiggled his bottom. Then he ran as fast as his little paws could carry him!

But halfway down the hall, a shadow moved. Benny stopped suddenly. His heart beat fast like a drum. "That didn't work," he panted.

"What else could you try?" Mom asked kindly.

Benny thought hard. His second idea was to close his eyes tight! If he couldn't see the shadows, maybe they couldn't see him either.

Benny squeezed his eyes shut like tiny buttons. He took one careful step. Then another. But walking with closed eyes was tricky! He bumped into the wall with a soft "thump."

"Ouch," Benny said, opening his eyes. "That didn't work either."

Mom sat down on the soft carpet. "You're being very brave, Benny. What else might help?"

Benny's tail wiggled just a little. His third idea was the best yet! He would sing a happy song to chase the scary feelings away.

"Woof, woof, I'm brave and strong," Benny sang in his sweet puppy voice. "Woof, woof, nothing can go wrong!"

His song echoed in the hallway. The sound made him feel bigger and braver. But when he stopped singing, the quiet returned. The shadows still looked dark and mysterious.

Benny sat down with a heavy sigh. "I tried three different ways, but I'm still scared."

"You've been so creative and persistent," Mom said proudly. "But maybe you don't have to solve this problem all by yourself."

Mom stood up and held out her hand. "Would you like me to walk with you tonight?"

Benny's tail began to wag slowly. "You would do that?"

"Of course," Mom smiled. "And tomorrow night, maybe we can leave a small night light on. Would that help?"

Benny's whole body wiggled with happiness. "Yes! A tiny light would make the shadows friendly!"

Together, Mom and Benny walked down the hallway. Their footsteps made a gentle rhythm on the wooden floor. The shadows didn't seem scary anymore with Mom beside him.

When they reached his cozy bed, Benny spun in a happy circle. The soft blanket smelled like lavender. His favorite squeaky toy waited on his pillow.

"Thank you for helping me," Benny said, nuzzling Mom's hand with his wet nose.

"Sometimes the best solution is asking for help," Mom said, tucking his blanket around him. "And you were so brave to keep trying different ideas."

The next night, a small butterfly night light glowed softly in the hallway. It cast gentle, dancing lights on the walls instead of scary shadows. Benny walked confidently to his bed, his tail wagging high.

"I'm not afraid anymore," Benny said proudly. "I learned that trying different solutions and asking for help makes me brave!"

From that night on, Benny loved bedtime. The hallway became his favorite place to practice his brave puppy walk. And every night, the butterfly light reminded him that even big problems can have simple, wonderful solutions.

"Good night, brave Benny," Mom would say. And Benny would drift off to sleep with a smile on his furry face, dreaming of all the problems he could solve tomorrow.

The end.
