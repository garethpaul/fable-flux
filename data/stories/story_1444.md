# Story story_1444

**Benny Bear and the Big Storm**

Benny Bear lived in a cozy bedroom. His bed had soft blue sheets. His favorite teddy sat on the pillow.

One dark evening, Benny heard loud sounds outside. CRASH! BOOM! Lightning flashed through his window. Rain tapped hard on the glass.

"Oh no!" said Benny. "The storm sounds so scary!"

The thunder made him jump. The wind howled like a monster. Benny's furry ears drooped down. His brown eyes grew wide with worry.

"I don't like this storm," Benny whispered. "It's too loud and bright!"

Benny tried to sleep. He pulled his blanket over his head. But the thunder still boomed. The lightning still flashed. The rain still pattered loudly.

His heart beat fast. His paws felt sweaty. Sleep would not come.

"I need to make the storm quiet," Benny said.

Benny had an idea. He would close the curtains tight. Maybe that would help block the scary storm.

He climbed out of bed. His bare paws touched the cold floor. Benny walked to the big window. He grabbed the heavy curtains.

Benny pulled the curtains closed. The room got darker. But the thunder still rumbled loud. The lightning still flickered through the fabric.

"That didn't work," Benny sighed. "The storm is still scary."

Benny felt sad. His first plan had failed. But he didn't give up. Good bears keep trying!

"Maybe I need something else," Benny thought hard.

Then Benny remembered something special. His grandmother had given him a music box. It played gentle, happy songs.

"Music might help!" Benny said with hope.

Benny found his special music box. It was small and silver. Pretty flowers were painted on top. He wound the tiny key carefully.

Beautiful music began to play. The melody was soft and sweet. It sounded like a lullaby his mama used to sing.

Benny climbed back into bed. He held his teddy tight. The music box sat on his nightstand. The pretty song filled the room.

The gentle music was louder than the rain. It was prettier than the thunder. Benny listened to the peaceful melody.

His worried feelings started to fade away. His breathing became slow and calm. His eyelids felt heavy and sleepy.

"The music makes me feel safe," Benny smiled.

The storm still raged outside. But inside, Benny felt warm and cozy. The music wrapped around him like a hug.

Soon, Benny's eyes closed. His breathing was deep and steady. The music box played its gentle song. Benny drifted off to sleep.

In the morning, sunshine streamed through the curtains. The storm had passed completely. Birds chirped happy songs outside.

Benny woke up feeling wonderful. He stretched his arms wide. He yawned a big, sleepy yawn.

"I did it!" Benny cheered. "I found a way to feel better!"

The music box sat quietly on his nightstand. Benny picked it up carefully. He hugged it close to his chest.

"Thank you, music box," he whispered. "You helped me be brave."

Benny learned something important that night. When one idea doesn't work, try another idea. Keep trying until you find what helps.

He also learned that storms don't last forever. They always pass by morning. And there are many ways to feel safe and calm.

From that day on, Benny wasn't scared of storms. When thunder boomed, he played his music box. When lightning flashed, he listened to pretty songs.

Benny became known as the bravest bear on his street. Other animals came to him for advice. He always told them the same thing.

"If something scares you, don't give up," Benny would say. "Try one way, then try another way. You'll find something that helps you feel better."

And Benny was always right. Because the best solutions come to those who keep trying.

The music box still sits by Benny's bed. Ready to help whenever storms come calling.

The end.
