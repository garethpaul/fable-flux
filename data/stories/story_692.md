# Story story_692

**Daisy's Magical Playtime**

The morning sun painted the pond golden. All the little ducks were splashing happily. "Time for playtime!" called Mama Duck. The other ducklings raced to the water. They flapped their tiny wings with joy.

But Daisy Duck stayed on the grassy bank. She tucked her yellow head under her wing. "I don't want to play today," she whispered. Her fluffy feathers felt heavy and sad.

"Come on, Daisy!" quacked her brother Danny. "The water feels so warm and nice!" Daisy just shook her head. Playing seemed too hard today. She watched the other ducks dive and splash. They looked so happy together.

Mama Duck waddled over to Daisy. Her orange feet made soft sounds on the mud. "What's wrong, little one?" she asked gently. Daisy's dark eyes looked up sadly. "I'm too tired to play," she said. "My wings feel funny today."

"Sometimes we all feel that way," said Mama Duck. She sat down next to Daisy. "But maybe we can try something different." Mama Duck pointed her wing toward the cattails. "See those tall plants dancing in the breeze?"

Daisy looked where Mama pointed. The brown cattails swayed back and forth. They looked like fuzzy hot dogs on sticks. "They're moving like magic wands," said Daisy softly.

"Let's go see them up close," suggested Mama Duck. Daisy felt a tiny spark of wonder. She stood up slowly on her webbed feet. Together, they walked toward the dancing plants.

As they got closer, something amazing happened. Tiny white seeds floated from the cattails. They danced in the air like snow. "Oh!" gasped Daisy. "They're flying everywhere!" The fluffy seeds tickled her orange beak.

One seed landed on Daisy's head. She giggled for the first time all day. "It feels like a tiny feather hat!" she said. More seeds swirled around them. The air sparkled with floating fluff.

"I want to catch them!" said Daisy suddenly. She forgot all about feeling tired. Her little legs started moving. She chased the dancing seeds around the cattails. Each step felt lighter than before.

Danny and the other ducklings saw the magical seeds too. They came running from the pond. "Wow!" they all quacked together. "This is the best game ever!" They all tried to catch the floating treasures.

Daisy found herself right in the middle of the fun. She jumped and stretched her neck high. The seeds made her laugh every time they touched her. "This is better than swimming!" she called out happily.

Soon all the ducklings were playing together. They made up new games with the seeds. They pretended to be seed-catching dragons. They built tiny seed mountains. They even had seed-blowing contests.

"Look what I can do!" said Daisy proudly. She spun in circles as seeds swirled around her. The other ducklings clapped their wings. "You're the best seed dancer, Daisy!" they cheered.

As the wind slowed down, fewer seeds floated by. But Daisy's heart felt light and happy. "That was the most magical playtime ever!" she said to Mama Duck. Her tired feeling had completely disappeared.

"Sometimes the best play happens when we least expect it," said Mama Duck wisely. She nuzzled Daisy's soft head. "You just needed to find your special kind of fun."

The sun was getting higher in the sky. The pond sparkled like diamonds. "Can we come back tomorrow?" asked Daisy eagerly. "I want to see if the cattails dance again!"

"Of course," smiled Mama Duck. "And maybe we'll discover something new and wonderful." Daisy's eyes twinkled with excitement. She couldn't wait for tomorrow's adventure.

As they walked home together, Daisy felt grateful. Sometimes the most magical moments come when you're brave enough to try something new. She had learned that playtime could surprise you in the most wonderful ways.

The end.
