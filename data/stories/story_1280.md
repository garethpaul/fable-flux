# Story story_1280

**Rosie Fox and the Sleepy Stars**

The sun was setting behind the tall trees. Orange and pink colors painted the sky. Mama Fox called from their cozy den.

"Rosie, it's bedtime!"

Little Rosie Fox poked her red nose out. Her fluffy tail twitched with worry. She did not want to go to sleep.

"But Mama, I'm not tired yet," Rosie said. Her amber eyes looked very wide. "Can I play just a little more?"

Mama Fox smiled gently. She had soft, warm fur. "Come inside, little one. Let's get ready together."

Rosie dragged her paws slowly. The grass felt cool under her feet. She could smell the sweet night flowers. Everything outside seemed so interesting.

Inside the den, Mama Fox filled a basin. The water was nice and warm. "Time to wash your face and paws," she said.

Rosie splashed the water halfheartedly. "I don't like bedtime," she whispered. "It's too dark and quiet."

Mama Fox dried Rosie's paws with a soft cloth. "What if I told you something special?" she asked. "Something magical happens at bedtime."

Rosie's ears perked up. "Really? What kind of magic?"

"Come see," Mama Fox said mysteriously.

They walked to Rosie's sleeping corner. Mama Fox had made a bed of soft moss. It smelled like the forest after rain. Cozy blankets lay folded nearby.

"Look up," Mama Fox whispered.

Rosie looked at the den ceiling. At first, she saw nothing special. Then something amazing happened. Tiny lights began to twinkle. They looked just like stars!

"Oh!" Rosie gasped. "How did they get there?"

Mama Fox winked. "These are sleepy stars. They only come out at bedtime. They watch over little foxes while they dream."

The lights grew brighter and brighter. Some were blue like sapphires. Others were golden like honey. They danced and shimmered softly.

"They're so pretty!" Rosie said. She felt much better now. The den didn't seem dark anymore.

Mama Fox tucked Rosie into her mossy bed. The blankets felt warm and snuggly. "The sleepy stars have a special job," she explained. "They collect all the fun from today. Then they turn it into wonderful dreams."

Rosie wiggled deeper into her blankets. "What kind of dreams?"

"Dreams about chasing butterflies," Mama Fox said softly. "Dreams about splashing in puddles. Dreams about finding the sweetest berries."

The sleepy stars twinkled even brighter. Rosie watched them dance overhead. They made her feel safe and happy.

"Will they be here tomorrow night too?" Rosie asked. Her eyes were getting heavy.

"Every single night," Mama Fox promised. "They're always waiting for bedtime."

Rosie yawned a big fox yawn. "I think I like bedtime now," she said sleepily. "The stars are like tiny friends."

Mama Fox kissed Rosie's forehead. It felt warm and loving. "Sweet dreams, my little fox. The sleepy stars will keep you safe."

As Rosie closed her eyes, the stars seemed to sing. Their light was gentle and soothing. She felt herself drifting away like a leaf on water.

In her dreams, Rosie danced with the sleepy stars. They showed her magical places in the forest. She met friendly rabbits and singing birds. Everything was wonderful and bright.

When morning came, sunlight streamed into the den. Rosie opened her eyes slowly. The sleepy stars had gone away. But she remembered their beautiful light.

"Mama!" she called excitedly. "I had the most amazing dreams!"

Mama Fox smiled as she prepared breakfast. "The sleepy stars worked their magic," she said.

Rosie ate her berries happily. She couldn't wait to tell her friends about the sleepy stars. And she couldn't wait for bedtime to come again.

"Tonight I'll say hello to each star," Rosie decided. "Maybe they'll give me even better dreams!"

From that day on, Rosie Fox loved bedtime. She knew something magical was always waiting for her. The sleepy stars became her favorite part of every day.

The end.
