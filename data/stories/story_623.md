# Story story_623

**Benny Bear's Special Snack Time**

The big clock on the kitchen wall went tick-tock, tick-tock. Mama Bear looked at the time. "Benny, it's snack time!" she called cheerfully.

Benny Bear was playing with his toy blocks. He had built a tall, wobbly tower. Red blocks, blue blocks, yellow blocks stacked high. "Do I have to?" Benny asked with a big sigh. "I'm not hungry yet."

Benny dragged his furry paws to the kitchen. His tummy felt empty, but he didn't want to stop playing. Snack time seemed boring today. The same old apple slices. The same old crackers. Nothing felt exciting.

Mama Bear smiled at her little cub. "What if we make snack time special today?" she asked. Her eyes twinkled like stars. "Close your eyes, Benny."

Benny squeezed his brown eyes shut tight. He heard Mama Bear opening cupboards. He heard her humming a happy tune. Something smelled sweet and fruity. Something smelled warm and nutty.

"You can open your eyes now," Mama Bear said softly.

Benny opened his eyes wide. The kitchen table looked magical! Colorful fruits were arranged like a rainbow. Red strawberries, orange carrots, yellow cheese cubes, green grapes, and purple blueberries. Everything sparkled in the sunny window light.

"Wow!" Benny gasped. "It looks like a rainbow!"

"We're going to make rainbow kabobs," Mama Bear explained. She handed Benny a wooden stick. "Pick your favorite colors first."

Benny's paws felt excited now. He picked up a juicy red strawberry. It felt soft and bumpy. He pushed it onto his stick carefully. Next came an orange carrot piece. It was crunchy and bright. Then a cube of yellow cheese that smelled creamy and rich.

"This is fun!" Benny giggled. His tail wiggled with joy.

Green grapes came next. They were round and smooth. Pop! One grape fell and rolled across the table. Benny and Mama Bear laughed together. Finally, Benny added purple blueberries. They were tiny and sweet.

"Look at my beautiful rainbow!" Benny held up his colorful kabob. It looked like art you could eat.

Mama Bear made her own kabob too. "Now for the best part," she said. "Eating our rainbows!"

Benny took his first bite. The strawberry was sweet and juicy. Red juice dripped down his chin. The carrot was crunchy between his teeth. Crunch, crunch, crunch! The cheese was smooth and creamy. It melted on his tongue.

"Mmm," Benny said happily. "This tastes like sunshine!"

The grapes burst with flavor. Sweet juice filled his mouth. The blueberries were tiny explosions of yumminess. Each bite was a new surprise.

Benny and Mama Bear sat together at the sunny table. They munched their rainbow kabobs slowly. The kitchen felt warm and cozy. Birds chirped outside the window. The smell of fresh fruit filled the air.

"I love making food together," Benny said. His tummy felt happy and full. "It's like playing, but with snacks!"

Mama Bear hugged her little cub. "Food tastes better when we make it with love," she said. Her fur was soft and warm. "And when we use our imagination."

Benny licked his sticky paws clean. "Can we make something special tomorrow too?" he asked hopefully. His eyes were bright with excitement.

"Of course," Mama Bear promised. "What would you like to try?"

Benny thought hard. His little brain was full of ideas. "Maybe we could make flower sandwiches? Or butterfly crackers?"

"Those sound wonderful," Mama Bear said. "We'll plan our next food adventure tonight."

Benny cleaned up his plate happily. Snack time wasn't boring anymore. It was magical! Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough. He was already dreaming of butterfly crackers and flower sandwiches.

From that day on, Benny never dragged his paws to snack time. Every day brought new food adventures. And every bite tasted like love and imagination mixed together.

The kitchen clock went tick-tock, tick-tock. But now that sound meant something wonderful was about to begin.

The end.
