# Story story_701

**Charlie Car's Art Adventure**

The sun peeked through the garage window. It was morning time. "Art time!" called Miss Rosa cheerfully. All the little cars lined up in the sunny classroom.

Charlie Car rolled slowly to his spot. His red paint gleamed in the light. But Charlie felt worried inside. "I don't like art time," he whispered to his friend Bella Bus.

"Why not?" asked Bella. Her yellow paint sparkled.

"My wheels are too big," Charlie said sadly. "I can't hold the brushes right. My pictures always look messy."

Miss Rosa smiled at all her students. "Today we're painting rainbows!" she announced. She placed big sheets of white paper on each table. The paper felt smooth and cool.

Charlie looked at his paintbrush. It felt heavy in his small wheel. The other cars started painting right away. Their brushes made soft swooshing sounds on the paper.

"I can't do it," Charlie mumbled. He tried to dip his brush in the red paint. The brush wobbled. Red paint dripped everywhere.

"Oops," said Charlie. The paint made little puddles on his table. They looked like tiny red ponds.

Miss Rosa rolled over to Charlie. "What's wrong, little one?" she asked gently.

"My wheels are too clumsy," Charlie explained. "I can't paint pretty pictures like everyone else."

Miss Rosa's headlights twinkled. "Let me show you something special," she said. She rolled to the art supply closet. Inside were sponges shaped like cars.

"These are wheel stamps," Miss Rosa explained. "You dip your wheels right in the paint. Then you roll across the paper."

Charlie's engine hummed with curiosity. "Really?"

"Try it," Miss Rosa encouraged.

Charlie rolled his front wheel into the blue paint. It felt cool and squishy. Then he rolled across his paper. A beautiful blue line appeared! It curved like a smile.

"Wow!" gasped Charlie. His engine purred with excitement.

He dipped his other wheel in yellow paint. Roll, roll, roll across the paper. A sunny yellow stripe appeared next to the blue one.

"This is fun!" Charlie laughed. The paint tickled his wheels. He could smell the fresh, clean paint smell.

Soon Charlie was rolling all over his paper. He made red stripes and green ones too. Purple paint and orange paint. His rainbow grew bigger and brighter.

"Look at my rainbow!" Charlie called to Bella.

"It's beautiful!" Bella honked happily. "Those wheel prints look like rolling hills."

Charlie stepped back to see his artwork. His rainbow wasn't like the others. It had thick, bumpy stripes from his big wheels. But it was colorful and cheerful and uniquely his.

"I love your wheel-print rainbow," said Miss Rosa. "It looks like a road through the sky."

Charlie beamed with pride. His headlights glowed bright. "It does look like a road! A magical rainbow road!"

The other cars gathered around Charlie's painting. "How did you make those cool thick lines?" asked Tommy Truck.

"With my wheels!" Charlie explained proudly. "Miss Rosa showed me how."

Soon all the cars were trying wheel painting. The classroom filled with happy honking and purring sounds. Paint squished under their wheels. The air smelled like creativity and joy.

When art time ended, Charlie didn't want to stop. "Can we do more tomorrow?" he asked Miss Rosa.

"Of course," she smiled. "Tomorrow we're making wheel-print flowers."

Charlie carefully hung his rainbow on the wall. It dried in the warm sunlight. The colors looked even brighter now.

As the cars rolled home, Charlie felt different inside. His engine hummed a happy tune. "I can't wait for art time tomorrow," he told Bella.

"What changed your mind?" Bella asked.

Charlie thought for a moment. "I learned that being different makes art special," he said. "My big wheels aren't clumsy. They make the best rainbow roads ever!"

That night, Charlie dreamed of painting wheel-print flowers. In his dreams, his big wheels created the most beautiful garden in the whole world.

Art time had become his favorite time of day.

The end.
