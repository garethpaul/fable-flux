# Story story_661

**Ducky's Lost Teddy Bear**

Ducky waddled into his cozy bedroom. The morning sun made everything glow bright yellow. He stretched his orange webbed feet and yawned.

"Time to wake up, Mr. Buttons!" Ducky called happily.

But his soft brown teddy bear was not there. Ducky looked at his rumpled blue blanket. No teddy bear. He looked at his fluffy white pillow. Still no Mr. Buttons.

"Oh no!" Ducky quacked loudly. "Where is Mr. Buttons?"

Ducky's heart felt heavy and worried. He could not start his day without his special teddy bear. Mr. Buttons had been his best friend since he was a tiny duckling. They did everything together.

Ducky decided to search everywhere. He would find Mr. Buttons!

First, Ducky looked under his bed. He got down on his yellow belly. The space was dark and dusty. He saw one red sock. He saw three toy cars. He saw a forgotten cookie that was now very hard.

"Mr. Buttons? Are you hiding here?" Ducky called softly.

But Mr. Buttons was not under the bed. Ducky felt his eyes getting watery. He sat down on the soft carpet and thought hard.

"Maybe Mr. Buttons fell behind my toy box," Ducky said to himself.

He waddled over to the big wooden toy box. It was heavy and hard to move. Ducky pushed with all his might. His little webbed feet slipped on the smooth floor.

"Oof!" Ducky grunted as he pushed harder.

The toy box moved just a little bit. Ducky squeezed behind it. The space was narrow and cramped. He found a bouncy ball and two puzzle pieces. But no Mr. Buttons.

Ducky felt very sad now. His bottom lip started to quiver. Where could his beloved teddy bear be?

"I will not give up," Ducky said bravely. "Mr. Buttons needs me to find him."

Ducky took three deep breaths. He thought about all the places he had played yesterday. Suddenly, his eyes grew wide and bright.

"The laundry basket!" Ducky quacked excitedly.

He remembered playing hide-and-seek with Mr. Buttons yesterday. They had hidden in the big wicker laundry basket in the hallway. Maybe Mr. Buttons was still there!

Ducky waddled quickly down the hall. His webbed feet made soft patting sounds on the wooden floor. The laundry basket sat in the corner. It was full of clean, fluffy towels that smelled like fresh flowers.

Ducky carefully moved the warm towels aside. They felt soft and cozy under his feathers. He dug deeper into the basket.

"Mr. Buttons!" Ducky shouted with joy.

There was his precious brown teddy bear! Mr. Buttons was snuggled deep in a big white towel. He looked very sleepy and comfortable.

Ducky scooped up Mr. Buttons and hugged him tight. The teddy bear was warm and smelled like sunshine and soap. Ducky's heart felt happy and full again.

"I missed you so much!" Ducky whispered into Mr. Buttons' fuzzy ear.

Ducky carried his teddy bear back to his bedroom. He felt proud and excited. He had not given up, even when he felt sad and worried.

"I am a good problem solver," Ducky said with a big smile.

He tucked Mr. Buttons safely under his blue blanket. Then he patted the teddy bear's head gently.

"Next time we play hide-and-seek, I will remember where you are hiding," Ducky promised.

Ducky learned something important that morning. When something seems lost or hard to find, he should not give up. He should think carefully and try different ideas. Sometimes the answer comes when he keeps trying.

From that day on, Ducky always put Mr. Buttons in the same special spot before bedtime. But he also knew that if his teddy bear ever got lost again, he could find him by being patient and trying his best.

Ducky and Mr. Buttons spent the whole day playing together. They were both very happy to be reunited. And Ducky felt proud of himself for never giving up on finding his best friend.

The end.
