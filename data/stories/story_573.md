# Story story_573

**Bunny and Owl at the Pond**

Benny the bunny sat by the shiny pond. The water sparkled in the warm sunshine. He picked up smooth stones and tossed them in. Splash! The water made pretty circles.

But Benny felt sad and lonely. No one was there to play with him. He sighed a big sigh. "I wish I had a friend," he whispered.

High up in the old oak tree, Olivia the owl heard him. She had been watching Benny all morning. Her big yellow eyes looked down kindly. Olivia wanted to play too, but she felt shy.

"Maybe he won't like me," Olivia thought. "I'm awake during the day. Most owls sleep now."

Benny threw another stone. Plop! It sank to the bottom. A little fish swam away quickly. Benny's ears drooped down sadly.

Olivia took a deep breath. She spread her soft brown wings. Down, down, down she flew. She landed gently on a log near Benny.

"Hello," said Olivia in her gentle voice. "I'm Olivia. Can I play with you?"

Benny's ears perked up right away. His pink nose twitched with excitement. "Yes! I'm Benny. I was feeling so lonely."

"What are you doing?" asked Olivia. She tilted her head to one side.

"I'm throwing stones in the water," said Benny. "Want to try?"

Olivia had never thrown stones before. She picked one up with her sharp talons. She tossed it carefully. Splash! The stone made bigger circles than Benny's.

"Wow!" said Benny. "You're really good at this!"

They took turns throwing stones. The pond made happy splashing sounds. Little ripples danced across the water. The sun felt warm on their backs.

"Let's try something else," said Benny. He hopped to the pond's edge. "We can look for tadpoles!"

Olivia flew over and perched nearby. They both looked into the clear water. Tiny black tadpoles wiggled their little tails. Green water plants swayed back and forth.

"Look at that one!" pointed Benny. "It has tiny legs growing!"

"Amazing!" hooted Olivia softly. "I've never seen tadpoles up close before."

A gentle breeze blew across the pond. It smelled like fresh grass and flowers. Dragonflies zipped around with their shiny wings. Benny and Olivia watched them dance.

"I have an idea," said Olivia. "Want to play hide and seek?"

Benny clapped his paws together. "Yes! That sounds fun!"

Olivia covered her big eyes with her wings. "One, two, three..." she counted slowly.

Benny hopped behind a big mushroom. His fluffy white tail stuck out just a little. He giggled quietly.

"Ready or not, here I come!" called Olivia.

She looked around carefully. She spotted the white tail right away. But she pretended not to see it. She looked under leaves and behind rocks.

"Hmm, where could Benny be?" she wondered out loud.

Benny giggled louder. His whole body shook with laughter.

"Found you!" laughed Olivia. She gently tapped his tail with her wing.

They played and played until the sun hung low. The sky turned pretty pink and orange. Fireflies began to twinkle like tiny stars.

"This was the best day ever," said Benny. His eyes sparkled with joy.

"I had so much fun too," said Olivia. "Can we be friends forever?"

"Yes!" shouted Benny happily. "Best friends forever!"

They hugged gently by the peaceful pond. The water reflected their happy faces. Night sounds began all around them. Crickets chirped their evening songs.

"Let's meet here tomorrow," said Olivia. "We can play new games!"

"I can't wait!" said Benny. He hopped in excited circles.

From that day on, Benny and Olivia met at the pond every day. They never felt lonely again. They had found something very special - a true friendship that would last forever.

The pond became their favorite place. A place where a bunny and an owl learned that being different made their friendship even more wonderful.

The end.
