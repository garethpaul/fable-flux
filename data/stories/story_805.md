# Story story_805

**Bella's Lost Book**

Bella Bear loved her sandbox. Every sunny day, she played there with her toys. Her favorite thing was reading her special red book under the big oak tree.

The book had pictures of honey bees and flowers. Bella's mama had given it to her for her birthday. It smelled like vanilla and felt soft in her paws.

One Tuesday morning, Bella ran to her sandbox. She looked left. She looked right. Her red book was gone!

"Oh no!" Bella cried. Her bottom lip wobbled. "Where is my special book?"

Bella looked under her yellow bucket. No book. She looked behind her green shovel. No book there either.

"Maybe the wind blew it away," Bella thought. She walked around the yard. She checked under bushes. She looked behind the garden fence. Still no red book.

Bella sat on the sandbox edge. A tear rolled down her furry cheek. "I'll never find my book," she sniffled.

Then Bella remembered something. Yesterday was very windy. The leaves danced in circles. Her sandbox toys had blown around too.

"Maybe my book is buried!" Bella said. She wiped her eyes with her paw.

Bella grabbed her green shovel. She started digging in one corner. Scoop, scoop, scoop. The sand felt cool and gritty. She found her red ball. But no book.

She dug in another corner. Scoop, scoop, scoop. She found her blue truck. But no book.

Bella's arms felt tired. The sun was getting hot. She wanted to give up.

"Wait," Bella said to herself. "Mama always says to keep trying. Good things happen when we don't give up."

Bella took a deep breath. She smelled the sweet roses nearby. A friendly robin chirped in the oak tree. This made her feel better.

She looked at her sandbox again. There was one corner left. Right under the oak tree where she always read.

Bella dug carefully with her paws this time. The sand was softer here. It felt different. Cooler and damper.

"Something is here!" Bella said excitedly.

She dug faster. Her heart beat like a drum. Soon she felt something smooth and flat.

"My book!" Bella shouted with joy.

She pulled out her special red book. It was covered in sand but not hurt. Bella brushed it off gently. The vanilla smell was still there.

"You're safe!" Bella hugged the book tight.

Just then, Mama Bear walked over. "What happened, sweetie?" she asked.

"I lost my special book," Bella explained. "First I looked everywhere in the yard. That didn't work. Then I dug in the sandbox. I found it buried under the oak tree!"

Mama Bear smiled proudly. "You didn't give up. That was very smart thinking."

"The wind must have blown sand over it yesterday," Bella said. "I almost stopped looking. But I remembered what you taught me."

"What's that?" Mama asked.

"Good things happen when we keep trying," Bella said with a big grin.

Mama Bear gave Bella a warm hug. "That's exactly right. You solved the problem all by yourself."

Bella opened her book to the first page. The pictures looked bright and cheerful. She could hardly wait to read about the honey bees again.

"Can we read together under our tree?" Bella asked.

"Of course," Mama said. "That sounds perfect."

They sat in the cool shade. Bella leaned against Mama's soft fur. The leaves rustled gently above them. A butterfly landed on the book's red cover.

"This is the best day ever," Bella said happily.

As they read together, Bella felt proud and content. She had learned something important. When problems seem big, taking time to think helps. Trying different solutions works too. And never giving up is the most important thing of all.

From that day on, Bella always put her special book in a safe place after reading. But she never forgot the lesson about persistence. It helped her solve many more problems as she grew up.

The red book stayed her favorite forever.

The end.
