# Story story_632

Max heard the lunch bell ring. All the children lined up at the door. Max walked very slowly behind his friends.

"I don't want lunch," Max whispered to himself. His tummy felt funny and tight. The lunch room seemed so big and loud.

Max's teacher, Mrs. Apple, smiled at him. "Come along, Max," she said gently. "Let's see what yummy food we have today."

The lunch room smelled like warm soup and fresh bread. Max could hear spoons clinking and children laughing. But his feet felt heavy like rocks.

Max sat at the long blue table. His lunch tray had a sandwich, orange slices, and milk. The sandwich looked lonely on the big plate. Max poked it with his finger.

"Not hungry?" asked his friend Lily. She sat next to him with her purple lunch box.

Max shook his head. The sandwich just looked boring. The orange slices seemed too wet. Even the milk looked too white.

Suddenly, something amazing happened. The sandwich started to wiggle! Max rubbed his eyes and looked again.

"Hello there!" said a tiny voice. It came from the sandwich! Two little olive eyes peeked out from the bread.

Max giggled. "Did you just talk?"

"Of course I did!" said the sandwich cheerfully. "I'm Sandy the Sandwich. I've been waiting all morning to meet you!"

The orange slices started bouncing on the plate. "We're the Orange Orchestra!" they sang together. "We make music when you bite us. Crunch, crunch, crunch!"

The milk in the glass began to swirl and dance. "I'm Milky the Magnificent!" it announced. "I make your bones strong like a superhero!"

Max's eyes grew wide with wonder. This was the most magical lunch ever!

"But I'm not very hungry," Max told his new food friends.

Sandy the Sandwich winked. "That's okay! We can play first. Watch this!" Sandy did a little flip on the plate.

The Orange Orchestra played a happy tune. "Squeeze us gently," they sang. "We smell like sunshine!"

Max squeezed an orange slice. Sweet, citrus smell filled the air. It reminded him of summer and playing outside.

"That smells wonderful!" Max said, smiling.

Milky the Magnificent swirled faster. "Dip Sandy in me! We love to dance together!"

Max dipped his sandwich in the milk. It made a tiny splash. Sandy giggled and tasted creamy and delicious.

"This is fun!" Max laughed. He took a small bite. Sandy tasted like turkey and cheese and friendship.

The Orange Orchestra cheered when Max tried them next. They were sweet and juicy. The juice ran down Max's chin and made him giggle more.

Milky the Magnificent was cool and smooth. "See how strong you're getting?" Milky said proudly.

Max ate and played with his magical lunch friends. Soon his plate was empty and his tummy felt happy and full.

"Thank you for playing with me," Max said to his food friends.

"We had fun too!" said Sandy. "Will you come back tomorrow?"

"Yes!" Max said excitedly. "What will we play then?"

"It's a surprise!" sang the Orange Orchestra.

Mrs. Apple walked by Max's table. "You ate all your lunch, Max! How wonderful!"

Max beamed with pride. His tummy felt warm and satisfied. The lunch room didn't seem scary anymore. It felt friendly and magical.

Lily smiled at Max. "You look happy now!"

"I am!" Max said. "Lunch is actually really fun!"

As Max walked back to class, he felt excited. His food friends made lunch the best part of the day. Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough.

Max skipped down the hallway. He couldn't wait to tell his mom about Sandy, the Orange Orchestra, and Milky the Magnificent.

"Tomorrow I'll have lunch again," Max thought happily. "And maybe I'll make even more food friends!"

The lunch bell would ring again tomorrow. But this time, Max would be the first one in line.

The end.
