# Story story_1238

**Kira's Flying Lunch**

The lunch bell rang at school. Ring, ring, ring! All the children ran to get their lunch boxes. Kira walked slowly to her cubby. She did not want to eat lunch today.

Kira opened her bright red lunch box. Inside was a peanut butter sandwich. There were apple slices too. Her mom packed crackers that looked like little fish. But Kira felt sad. She wanted to keep playing outside.

"I don't want lunch," Kira said softly. She looked out the big window. The wind was blowing the trees. Leaves danced in the air like tiny dancers.

Mrs. Chen, her teacher, sat down next to Kira. "What's wrong, sweetie?" she asked in a gentle voice.

"I want to fly my kite," Kira said. "The wind looks perfect outside." She pointed to the window. A red balloon floated by in the bright blue sky.

Mrs. Chen smiled. "What if we make lunch more fun?" she asked. "Close your eyes and make a wish."

Kira closed her eyes tight. She wished and wished. When she opened them, something amazing happened. Her sandwich looked like a kite! The peanut butter was spread in a diamond shape. Two pretzel sticks made the kite's tail.

"Wow!" Kira said. Her eyes grew big and round. "My sandwich is a kite!"

Mrs. Chen winked. "Magic happens when we use our imagination," she said.

Kira picked up her kite sandwich. She made whooshing sounds like wind. "My kite is flying high," she said. She took a small bite. The peanut butter tasted creamy and sweet.

Next, Kira looked at her apple slices. They were cut in half-moon shapes. "These look like clouds!" she said. She held them up to the light. The apple pieces were pale yellow like fluffy clouds.

"My kite is flying through the clouds," Kira said happily. She ate an apple slice. It was crisp and juicy. The sweet juice ran down her chin.

The fish crackers became birds in the sky. Kira made them fly around her kite sandwich. "Tweet, tweet," she chirped. "The birds want to play with my kite."

She crunched the crackers. They tasted salty and good. Crunch, crunch, crunch went her teeth.

Other children noticed Kira's fun lunch game. "Can we play too?" asked Sam. He had a turkey sandwich.

"Yes!" said Kira. "What does your lunch look like?"

Sam looked at his sandwich. "It looks like a big fluffy cloud," he said. Soon Sam was flying his cloud sandwich around Kira's kite.

Emma joined in too. Her round grapes became bouncing balls in the sky. "Boing, boing," she said as she ate them. The grapes popped in her mouth. They tasted sweet and purple.

More children gathered around. The lunch room filled with happy sounds. Everyone was flying their food in an imaginary sky. Laughter echoed off the walls.

Kira finished her flying lunch. Her tummy felt full and happy. "That was the best lunch ever," she told Mrs. Chen.

"See?" said Mrs. Chen. "Sometimes the best adventures happen right where we are."

After lunch, the children went outside. The real wind was still blowing. Kira ran around the playground with her arms spread wide. She pretended to be a kite flying in the wind.

"I'm soaring high," she called to her friends. The warm sun touched her face. The grass felt soft under her feet.

When school ended, Kira ran to her mom. "Mom, mom!" she said. "I had a flying lunch today!"

Her mom smiled. "Tell me all about it," she said.

That night, Kira helped pack her lunch for tomorrow. She was excited to see what new adventure her food would become. Maybe her sandwich would be a boat. Maybe her carrots would be rockets.

"I can't wait for lunch tomorrow," Kira said as she hugged her mom goodnight.

Outside her window, the stars twinkled like tiny kites dancing in the dark sky.

The end.
