# Story story_641

**Woolly's Bedtime Adventure**

The sun was setting behind the hills. The sky turned pink and purple. It was bedtime on Farmer Mae's farm.

"Time for bed, little sheep," called Farmer Mae. She rang her silver bell. Ding, ding, ding!

All the sheep walked to the cozy barn. All except Woolly. Woolly was the smallest sheep. She had the fluffiest white wool.

"I don't want to go to bed," Woolly said. She stomped her tiny hooves. "I want to play more!"

Woolly hid behind the apple tree. The grass felt cool under her feet. She could smell the sweet flowers nearby.

"Woolly, where are you?" called Farmer Mae. Her voice was kind and gentle.

Woolly peeked out from the tree. She saw something amazing. Tiny lights were dancing in the air! They sparkled like little stars.

"Oh my!" whispered Woolly. "What are those?"

The lights came closer. They were fireflies! They glowed yellow and green. They made the whole farm look magical.

One firefly landed on Woolly's nose. It tickled! Woolly giggled softly.

"Hello, little sheep," said the firefly. "I'm Luna. We only come out at bedtime."

"Really?" asked Woolly. Her eyes grew wide.

"Yes! We light up the night," Luna said. "We help everyone feel safe and sleepy."

More fireflies joined them. They danced around Woolly's fluffy wool. She felt warm and happy.

"Will you show me more?" asked Woolly.

"Follow us!" said Luna.

The fireflies led Woolly to the barn. Inside, it was dark and quiet. The other sheep were already sleeping. They looked so peaceful.

The fireflies made the barn glow softly. It looked like a fairy tale castle. Woolly had never seen anything so beautiful.

"This is amazing," whispered Woolly. "Bedtime is magical!"

Farmer Mae appeared with a warm blanket. It smelled like lavender. "There you are, Woolly," she said softly.

"Look at the fireflies!" Woolly said excitedly.

Farmer Mae smiled. "They come every night. But only sleepy sheep get to see them."

Woolly yawned. The fireflies' gentle light made her feel drowsy. Her eyelids felt heavy.

"I think I'm ready for bed now," said Woolly.

Farmer Mae tucked the soft blanket around Woolly. The hay beneath her felt like a cloud. It smelled sweet and fresh.

"Thank you, Luna," Woolly whispered to her firefly friend.

"Sweet dreams, little sheep," Luna replied. "We'll be here every night."

Woolly closed her eyes. She listened to the gentle sounds around her. The other sheep were breathing softly. An owl hooted in the distance. The wind rustled the leaves outside.

She felt safe and cozy in her bed. The barn was the perfect place to sleep.

"Tomorrow will be wonderful," Woolly thought. "I can play all day. Then I can see the fireflies again!"

She smiled as she drifted off to sleep. Her dreams were full of dancing lights and happy adventures.

In the morning, Woolly woke up feeling great. The sun streamed through the barn windows. Birds were singing outside.

"Good morning, everyone!" she called cheerfully.

The other sheep looked surprised. Woolly was usually grumpy in the morning.

"You seem happy today," said her friend Patch.

"I am!" said Woolly. "I can't wait for bedtime tonight!"

All day long, Woolly played happily. She ran through the meadow. She ate sweet clover. She rolled in the soft grass.

But the best part was knowing what came next. When the sun set, she would see her firefly friends again.

That evening, Woolly was the first sheep to head to the barn. She couldn't wait to see Luna and the other fireflies.

"Ready for bed?" asked Farmer Mae.

"Yes!" said Woolly with a big smile. "I love bedtime now!"

And from that night on, Woolly always looked forward to bedtime. Because she knew that the most magical adventures happen when little sheep are ready to sleep.

The end.
