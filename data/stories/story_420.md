# The Drum and Grandma's Forest Song

Deep in the green forest, a little red drum sat alone on a mossy rock. The drum loved to make music. *Boom, boom, tap!* went the drum's cheerful beat.

But today, the drum felt sad. "I wish someone would play with me," sighed the drum. "Music is more fun with friends."

The drum played a slow, lonely song. *Boom... tap... boom...* The sound echoed through the tall trees.

Just then, Grandma walked down the forest path. She carried a wicker basket full of berries. Her silver hair sparkled in the sunlight.

Grandma heard the drum's music. She stopped and smiled. "What a lovely sound!" she said.

The drum looked up hopefully. "Hello, Grandma. I'm making music all by myself. But it feels lonely."

Grandma set down her basket. "May I join you?" she asked kindly. "I love music too!"

The drum's face lit up. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" Grandma clapped her hands together. *Clap, clap, clap!* "I can make rhythm with my hands."

The drum began to play again. *Boom, boom, tap!* Grandma clapped along. *Clap, clap, clap!*

"This sounds wonderful!" laughed the drum. The music was twice as beautiful now.

Grandma picked up two smooth sticks. She tapped them on a hollow log. *Click, click, tap!* The drum played louder. *BOOM, BOOM, TAP!*

A squirrel peeked down from his tree. A rabbit hopped closer to listen. Even the birds began to chirp along.

"Look!" said Grandma. "Our music is bringing everyone together!"

The drum spun around with joy. "I never knew music could sound this good!"

Grandma smiled warmly. "Everything is better with a friend."

They played a bouncy song together. *Boom-clap-click-tap!* The forest filled with happy sounds.

"Let's try something new," suggested Grandma. She hummed a gentle tune. The drum played softly underneath. *Tap, tap, boom.*

"That's beautiful," whispered the drum. "You make the prettiest melodies."

"And you make the strongest beat," said Grandma. "We make a perfect team!"

They discovered they both loved morning songs. They both enjoyed dancing rhythms. They both wanted to make others happy.

"I have an idea," said the drum excitedly. "Let's have a forest concert!"

Grandma's eyes twinkled. "What a wonderful plan!"

They practiced their favorite songs. The drum played the beat. Grandma added claps and taps and hums.

Soon, all the forest animals gathered around. The squirrels chittered. The rabbits thumped their feet. The birds sang along.

"One, two, three!" called Grandma.

Together, they played the most joyful song ever. *BOOM-CLAP-CLICK-CHIRP-THUMP!* The whole forest danced and sang.

When the song ended, everyone cheered. "More! More!" called the animals.

The drum beamed with happiness. "Thank you, Grandma. You're the best friend ever!"

Grandma hugged the little drum gently. "Thank you for sharing your music with me. Friends make everything more special."

As the sun began to set, they played one last quiet song. The forest grew peaceful and calm.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" asked the drum hopefully.

"Every day," promised Grandma. "We'll make beautiful music together."

The drum felt warm and happy inside. No more lonely songs. No more playing alone.

From that day on, the drum and Grandma met in the forest every morning. They made music that filled the woods with joy.

Sometimes other friends joined them too. But the drum's favorite times were with Grandma. She had taught him that sharing made everything better.

"Music and friendship," said the drum contentedly. "The two most beautiful things in the world."

And deep in the forest, their happy songs could be heard every single day. *Boom, clap, tap!* The sound of perfect friendship.

The End.
