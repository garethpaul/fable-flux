# Story story_568

**Buddy Learns to Share**

Buddy was a fluffy golden dog. He loved playing in Sunny Park. The grass felt soft under his paws. Birds chirped happy songs in the trees.

Today, Buddy brought his favorite red ball. It was round and bouncy. He squeezed it in his mouth. It made a fun squeaky sound.

"This is my special ball," Buddy said proudly. He tossed it high in the air. The ball flew up, up, up. Then it came down with a soft thud.

Soon, other dogs came to the park. There was Rosie, a small brown puppy. Max was a big black dog with floppy ears. Luna had spots all over her white fur.

"What a pretty ball!" said Rosie. Her tail wagged fast. "Can we play with you?"

Buddy hugged his ball tight. "No," he said. "This is mine. Go find your own toys."

The other dogs looked sad. Their ears drooped down. They walked away slowly.

Buddy played alone with his red ball. He threw it and chased it. But something felt wrong. The park seemed quiet and lonely.

He watched the other dogs. They sat under a shady tree. Nobody was playing. Nobody was having fun.

"Maybe I should try to play with them," Buddy thought. But he didn't want to give up his ball.

Buddy had an idea. He picked up his ball and walked over. "You can look at my ball," he said. "But don't touch it."

He held the ball high above his head. The other dogs gathered around. They could only stare at it.

"It's very nice," said Max politely. But his voice sounded sad.

"Can we please play catch?" asked Luna. Her brown eyes looked hopeful.

"No," said Buddy firmly. "You might lose it. Or break it."

The dogs sighed and walked away again. Buddy felt even more lonely than before. His idea didn't work at all.

Buddy sat on the soft grass. He rolled his ball between his paws. The sun felt warm on his fur. But his heart felt cold and empty.

He looked at the other dogs again. They were trying to play tag. But they kept bumping into each other. They looked bored and unhappy.

"This isn't fun for anyone," Buddy realized. His ears drooped down too.

Then Buddy remembered something. Last week, he saw children sharing their toys. They laughed and played together. They looked so happy and excited.

"Maybe sharing makes playing more fun," Buddy thought.

He picked up his red ball. His paws felt shaky. His heart beat fast. But he walked over to the other dogs.

"Would you like to play catch?" Buddy asked. His voice was quiet but friendly.

All the dogs jumped up. Their tails wagged like crazy. "Really?" asked Rosie. "You want to share?"

Buddy nodded. "Yes. Playing together sounds better than playing alone."

He gently tossed the ball to Max. Max caught it in his big mouth. Then he threw it to Luna. Luna bounced it to Rosie with her nose.

"Catch, Buddy!" called Rosie. She tossed the ball back to him.

Buddy caught his red ball. But now it felt different. It felt lighter and more special. The squeaky sound seemed happier too.

All four dogs ran around the park. They chased the ball together. The grass tickled their paws. The sunshine warmed their backs. Their happy barks filled the air.

"This is the best game ever!" panted Max.

"Sharing makes everything more fun!" agreed Luna.

Buddy's tail wagged so hard his whole body wiggled. "I'm so glad I shared my ball," he said. "Playing with friends is much better than playing alone."

As the sun began to set, the dogs were tired but happy. They had played for hours together.

"Thank you for sharing, Buddy," said Rosie. She licked his furry cheek.

Buddy smiled a big doggy smile. "Thank you for being my friends," he said.

From that day on, Buddy always shared his toys. He learned that sharing didn't mean losing something special. It meant making everything twice as fun. And that made his heart feel full and warm every single day.

The end.
