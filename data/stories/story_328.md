# Toby Tiger's Big Dream

In a sunny green forest, there lived a little tiger named Toby. Toby had bright orange fur with bold black stripes. His whiskers twitched with curiosity every single day.

Toby loved exploring the forest. He would hop over logs. He would splash in puddles. He would chase colorful butterflies through the trees.

But there was one special place Toby couldn't see. At the edge of the forest stood a tall wooden fence. Behind that fence was the most wonderful garden. Toby could hear birds singing sweetly. He could smell pretty flowers blooming. Sometimes he heard children laughing and playing.

"I wish I could see over that fence!" Toby said sadly. He stood on his tippy-toes. He stretched his neck as high as possible. But Toby was still too short.

The fence was much taller than little Toby.

Every morning, Toby would walk to the fence. He would try to peek through the wooden boards. But the spaces were too small. He could only see tiny glimpses of green grass.

"There must be a way," Toby thought. His orange tail swished with determination.

First, Toby tried jumping. He took three big steps back. Then he ran toward the fence as fast as he could. "One, two, three... JUMP!" he called out. Toby leaped high into the air. But he still couldn't see over the top.

He landed softly on his paws. "Hmm," he said, scratching his furry chin.

Next, Toby looked for something to stand on. He found a smooth gray rock. He rolled it over to the fence. The rock made a scraping sound across the ground. Toby climbed on top. Now he was a little taller! But the fence was still too high.

"I need something bigger," Toby decided.

Toby spotted a fallen tree trunk nearby. It was brown and bumpy. He pushed and pushed with all his might. The trunk was very heavy. "Oof! This is hard work!" Toby panted. His paws got muddy from pushing.

Slowly, the trunk rolled toward the fence. Toby wiped his forehead with his paw. He climbed onto the trunk. Then he climbed onto the rock. Now he was much taller than before!

Toby stretched his neck up, up, up. His whiskers trembled with excitement. But he still needed to be just a little bit higher.

"What else can I try?" Toby wondered aloud.

Just then, his friend Bella the bunny hopped by. Her white fur was fluffy and soft. "What are you doing, Toby?" she asked.

"I'm trying to see over this fence," Toby explained. "But I'm still too short."

Bella's pink nose twitched thoughtfully. "Maybe I can help! I'm good at hopping high."

Bella bounced onto the rock. Then she hopped onto the trunk. "Climb on my back, Toby!" she offered kindly.

Toby carefully climbed onto Bella's back. She was sturdy and strong. Together, they were finally tall enough!

Toby's eyes grew wide with wonder. Over the fence was the most beautiful garden he had ever seen. Red roses bloomed everywhere. Yellow sunflowers reached toward the sky. A crystal blue pond sparkled in the sunshine. Butterflies danced from flower to flower.

Children were playing on a tire swing. They were laughing and having fun. A friendly dog was chasing a bright red ball.

"It's even more wonderful than I imagined!" Toby whispered happily.

"Sometimes we need friends to help us reach our dreams," Bella said with a smile.

From that day on, Toby and Bella visited the fence together. They took turns helping each other see the beautiful garden. Toby learned that trying different ideas was important. He also learned that asking friends for help made everything better.

Every time Toby saw something he couldn't reach, he remembered his adventure. He would try new ways to solve problems. And he would always remember to ask his friends for help when he needed it.

Toby's heart felt warm and happy. The forest was full of wonderful friends and amazing discoveries. There was always something new to explore when you never gave up trying.

The end.
