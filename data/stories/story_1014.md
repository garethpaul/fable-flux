# Story story_1014

Zoe sat on the wooden fence. She swung her little legs back and forth. The morning sun felt warm on her face. But Zoe felt sad inside.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," she whispered.

Zoe lived on a farm with her grandpa. She loved the farm. But sometimes she felt lonely. All the animals had friends. The chickens clucked together. The cows stood in groups. Even the pigs rolled in mud together.

Zoe jumped down from the fence. She picked up a stick. She drew circles in the dirt. The circles looked like sad faces.

"Baa! Baa!"

Zoe looked up. A fluffy white sheep stood nearby. The sheep had the softest wool Zoe had ever seen. Its eyes were big and kind.

"Hello, sheep," said Zoe. "Do you want to play?"

The sheep stepped closer. It sniffed Zoe's hand with its wet nose. Zoe giggled. The nose felt cold and tickly.

"Baa!" said the sheep. It sounded happy.

Zoe smiled for the first time that day. "I think that means yes!"

Zoe ran to the apple tree. The sheep followed her. Its hooves made soft sounds on the grass. Zoe picked up a red ball from under the tree.

"Want to play catch?" asked Zoe.

She rolled the ball gently. The sheep pushed it back with its nose. The ball rolled right to Zoe's feet.

"Good job!" cheered Zoe.

They played catch for a long time. The sheep was very good at pushing the ball. Sometimes it would run around the ball first. That made Zoe laugh out loud.

Next, Zoe found a jump rope. "Can you jump?" she asked.

The sheep tilted its head. It looked confused.

Zoe showed the sheep how to jump. She hopped up and down. "Like this!"

The sheep tried to jump. It bounced a little. Not very high, but it tried hard.

"You're learning!" said Zoe. "Let's try something else."

Zoe ran to the flower garden. The sheep trotted beside her. The flowers smelled sweet like honey. Bees buzzed softly around the yellow sunflowers.

"We can pick flowers for Grandpa," said Zoe.

Zoe picked purple flowers. The sheep picked flowers too. But it ate them instead. Zoe laughed until her tummy hurt.

"Silly sheep! Those are for Grandpa!"

The sheep looked embarrassed. It made a quiet "baa" sound.

"It's okay," said Zoe. "You can help me carry them."

Zoe put flowers behind the sheep's ears. The purple flowers looked pretty against the white wool. The sheep stood very still and proud.

"You look beautiful!" said Zoe.

They walked to the farmhouse together. Grandpa sat on the porch. He was drinking coffee from his favorite blue cup.

"Look, Grandpa!" called Zoe. "I made a friend!"

Grandpa smiled big. "What a wonderful friend you found!"

The sheep walked up to Grandpa. It let him pet its soft wool. Grandpa's eyes twinkled with happiness.

"This sheep has been lonely too," said Grandpa. "She just moved to our farm. I think she needed a friend."

Zoe's heart felt warm and full. "Really?"

"Really," said Grandpa. "Animals need friends just like people do."

Zoe hugged the sheep gently. The wool felt like a soft cloud. The sheep nuzzled Zoe's cheek with its nose.

"What should we name her?" asked Zoe.

"How about Fluffy?" suggested Grandpa.

"Perfect!" said Zoe. "Hi, Fluffy!"

"Baa!" said Fluffy happily.

From that day on, Zoe and Fluffy played together every morning. They ran through the tall grass. They splashed in the pond. They napped under the shady oak tree.

Zoe never felt lonely again. She had found her best friend. And Fluffy had found hers too.

Every evening, they sat on the fence together. They watched the sunset paint the sky orange and pink. Zoe would tell Fluffy about her day. Fluffy would listen with her kind eyes.

"Thank you for being my friend," Zoe would whisper.

And Fluffy would always answer, "Baa!"

Which meant, "Thank you for being mine too."

The end.
