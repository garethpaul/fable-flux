# Story story_1088

**Jack's Garden Adventure**

"Time for garden time!" called Mom from the kitchen.

Jack was playing with his toy cars. He made vrooming sounds as they raced around the rug. The little red car was his favorite.

"Do I have to?" Jack asked. He looked out the window. The garden looked big and dirty.

Mom smiled. "Yes, sweetie. We need to plant our seeds today."

Jack dragged his feet to the back door. He put on his old sneakers. They were already muddy from yesterday.

Outside, the sun felt warm on his face. But Jack still didn't want to garden. Dirt was messy. Worms were yucky.

Mom handed him a small shovel. "Let's dig holes for the sunflower seeds," she said.

Jack poked at the dirt. It was soft and crumbly. Some stuck to his shovel. He wrinkled his nose.

"I don't like getting dirty," Jack said.

Mom knelt down beside him. "Look closer at the soil," she whispered.

Jack leaned in. Something moved in the dirt. A tiny green shoot was pushing up!

"What's that?" Jack asked. His eyes grew wide.

"That's a bean we planted last week," Mom explained. "It's growing into a plant."

Jack touched the little green shoot gently. It felt smooth and cool. "It's so small," he said.

"All big plants start small," Mom said. "Just like you did."

Jack giggled. He imagined himself as a tiny baby plant.

Mom showed him how to make holes with his finger. "Press down like this," she said. "Not too deep."

Jack tried it. The dirt felt squishy between his fingers. It was kind of fun. Like playing with clay.

"Now drop in a seed," Mom said.

Jack held a sunflower seed. It was black and white striped. He dropped it carefully into the hole.

"Cover it up gently," Mom said.

Jack pushed dirt over the seed. He patted it down softly. "Sleep tight, little seed," he whispered.

Mom smiled. "That's perfect, Jack."

They planted more seeds together. Jack found a wiggly earthworm. At first he jumped back. But Mom showed him how the worm helped the garden.

"Worms make the soil healthy," she explained. "They're garden helpers."

Jack watched the worm wiggle. "Hello, garden helper," he said.

The worm disappeared into the dirt. Jack laughed.

Next, they watered the planted seeds. Jack held the watering can. The water sparkled in the sunshine. It made tiny rainbows in the spray.

"The seeds are getting a drink," Jack said. He pretended to be thirsty like a seed. "Glub, glub, glub!"

Mom laughed. "You're silly, Jack."

A butterfly landed on a nearby flower. It was orange and black. Its wings moved slowly up and down.

"Look, Mom!" Jack pointed. "A butterfly visitor!"

The butterfly flew around Jack's head. He stood very still. He could see its tiny face and long tongue.

"Gardens attract beautiful creatures," Mom said softly.

Jack felt something tickle his arm. A ladybug was crawling up his sleeve. It was bright red with black spots.

"I'm a garden too!" Jack giggled.

The ladybug flew away. Jack watched it land on a leaf.

They finished planting all the seeds. Jack's hands were brown with dirt. His knees had grass stains. But he didn't mind anymore.

"When will they grow?" Jack asked.

"In a few days, you'll see tiny green shoots," Mom said. "Just like the bean plant."

Jack looked at all their planted rows. He imagined tall sunflowers reaching toward the sky. He pictured yellow petals bright as the sun.

"Can we check on them tomorrow?" Jack asked.

"Of course," Mom said. "Gardens need daily care."

Jack ran to the house to wash his hands. The soap felt bubbly and warm. He could hardly wait for tomorrow.

At dinner, Jack told Dad about the butterfly and ladybug. He described the wiggly worm and tiny green shoot.

"I can't wait to see my sunflowers grow," Jack said. "Tomorrow I'll water them again."

Mom winked at Dad. "I think someone loves garden time now."

Jack smiled big. "Garden time is the best time!"

That night, Jack dreamed of giant sunflowers and friendly garden creatures. Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough.

The end.
