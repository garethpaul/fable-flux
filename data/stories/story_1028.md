# Story story_1028

Emma's mommy looked at the clock. "Time for nap, little one," she said softly.

Emma shook her head. "No nap today, Mommy. I want to play more." She hugged her teddy bear tight.

The afternoon sun made warm stripes on Emma's bedroom floor. Her bed looked cozy with its purple blanket. But Emma did not want to sleep.

"I'm not tired," Emma said. She rubbed her eyes.

Mommy smiled. "Let's try lying down first. Close your eyes and listen."

Emma climbed into bed. She pulled the soft blanket up to her chin. The pillow felt fluffy under her head.

"I still don't want to nap," Emma whispered.

"That's okay," Mommy said. "Just rest your eyes."

Emma squeezed her eyes shut. She heard birds singing outside. She heard the clock tick-tock on the wall.

Then something wonderful happened.

Emma heard tiny footsteps. Pitter-patter, pitter-patter. She opened one eye.

A small bunny sat on her windowsill. The bunny had sparkly white fur. Its nose twitched like a little pink button.

"Hello, Emma," said the bunny. "I'm Sleepy."

Emma sat up. "A talking bunny!"

Sleepy hopped onto Emma's bed. "I help children have happy naps. Want to come on an adventure?"

Emma nodded. This was much better than sleeping.

Sleepy touched Emma's hand with his soft paw. Suddenly, Emma felt like she was floating.

They drifted through her bedroom window. They soared over fluffy white clouds. The clouds felt like cotton candy.

"Where are we going?" Emma asked.

"To the Dream Garden," Sleepy said.

They landed in a magical place. Flowers sang lullabies. Trees had leaves shaped like pillows. A gentle stream bubbled sleepy songs.

"This is beautiful," Emma said. She yawned without meaning to.

Sleepy showed her rainbow butterflies. They danced in slow, peaceful circles. Their wings made soft whooshing sounds.

Emma and Sleepy walked on grass that felt like velvet. They found a hill covered in dandelions. Each dandelion held a tiny dream.

"Pick one," Sleepy said.

Emma chose a dandelion that glowed golden. She blew on it gently. The seeds floated away like little stars.

"What did you wish for?" Sleepy asked.

"Sweet dreams," Emma said. She felt very sleepy now.

Sleepy smiled. "Dreams come true in the Dream Garden."

They lay down on the soft grass. Emma watched dream seeds float in the sky. They twinkled and danced above her.

The warm sun kissed Emma's cheeks. A gentle breeze hummed a quiet song. Emma's eyelids felt heavy.

"Sleepy?" Emma whispered.

"Yes?"

"Nap time is nice."

Sleepy patted Emma's hand. "Rest now, little one."

Emma closed her eyes. She dreamed of singing flowers and rainbow butterflies. She dreamed of floating on clouds soft as marshmallows.

When Emma woke up, afternoon light filled her room. Mommy sat in the rocking chair, reading a book.

"Did you have a good nap?" Mommy asked.

Emma stretched and smiled. "The best nap ever! I went to the Dream Garden."

"That sounds wonderful," Mommy said. She gave Emma a warm hug.

Emma looked at her windowsill. She saw a small white feather. It sparkled in the sunlight.

"Mommy, look!" Emma pointed to the feather.

"How pretty," Mommy said. "Maybe it's from your dream."

Emma picked up the feather. It felt soft and magical. She put it on her nightstand.

"Mommy?" Emma said.

"Yes, sweetie?"

"Can I take another nap tomorrow?"

Mommy laughed. "Of course you can."

Emma hugged her teddy bear. She couldn't wait for tomorrow's nap time. Maybe Sleepy would come back. Maybe they would visit the Dream Garden again.

That night, Emma went to bed early. She held the sparkly feather close. She dreamed of magical adventures and cozy naps.

From that day on, Emma loved nap time. She knew that wonderful dreams were waiting for her. All she had to do was close her eyes and listen.

The end.
