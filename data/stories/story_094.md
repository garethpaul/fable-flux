# Maya's Field Day Fun

Maya skipped across the sunny green field. Her red shoes made soft thumping sounds on the grass. The warm sun tickled her face. She could hear children laughing nearby.

"This will be so much fun!" Maya said to herself.

A group of kids was playing a circle game. They held hands and spun around. They sang a happy song together. Maya watched from behind a big oak tree.

"Ring around the rosie!" they sang loudly. "We all fall down!"

The children giggled as they tumbled to the soft grass. Maya's heart felt heavy. She wanted to play too. But the circle looked so full already.

Maya took a small step forward. Then she stopped. Her tummy felt wobbly. What if they said no? What if they didn't want her?

She watched them play three more rounds. Each time, Maya felt more left out. The other children seemed so happy together.

"I wish I could join them," Maya whispered.

Maya decided to try something. She walked closer to the group. Her hands felt sweaty. Her heart beat fast.

"Um, excuse me," Maya said quietly.

But the children were singing loudly. They didn't hear her small voice. They kept spinning and laughing. Maya's cheeks turned pink.

She tried again, speaking a little louder. "Can I play too?"

Still, no one heard her. The game was too noisy. Maya felt tears in her eyes. She turned to walk away.

Then Maya had another idea. She took a deep breath. She made her voice strong and clear.

"Hi everyone!" Maya called out cheerfully. "Your game looks really fun!"

This time, the children stopped spinning. They looked at Maya with friendly smiles.

"Hi!" said a boy with curly hair. "I'm Sam!"

"I'm Maya," she replied. "Can I play with you?"

"Of course!" said a girl with braids. "I'm Emma. We need more friends!"

"Yes!" said Sam. "The more friends, the better!"

Maya's heart felt light and happy. She joined the circle between Emma and Sam. Their hands felt warm in hers.

"Ring around the rosie!" they all sang together.

Maya's voice joined the others. She spun and laughed with her new friends. When they all fell down, Maya giggled the loudest.

"That was awesome!" Maya said, sitting up in the grass.

"Want to play again?" asked Emma.

"Yes, please!" Maya said.

They played the circle game five more times. Maya felt so happy and included. Her worries melted away like ice cream in sunshine.

After the game, Sam had an idea. "Let's play tag now!"

"Maya can be 'it' first!" suggested Emma.

Maya beamed with joy. "Really? Thank you!"

She counted to ten while everyone scattered. "Ready or not, here I come!"

Maya ran across the field, chasing her new friends. The grass felt springy under her feet. The wind blew through her hair. She felt free and happy.

When it was time to go home, Maya hugged Emma and Sam.

"Thank you for letting me play," Maya said.

"Thank you for asking!" said Emma. "You made our games more fun!"

"See you tomorrow?" asked Sam.

"Definitely!" Maya replied.

Maya walked home with a big smile. She had learned something important today. Sometimes you have to be brave and speak up. Sometimes you have to try more than once.

"I'm so glad I didn't give up," Maya told her mom.

"What happened, sweetie?" Mom asked.

Maya told her about the field and the circle game. She explained how scared she felt at first.

"But I kept trying," Maya said proudly. "And I made new friends!"

"I'm proud of you for being brave," Mom said. "Good friends are always worth the effort."

That night, Maya dreamed about playing in the sunny field. She couldn't wait for tomorrow's adventures with Emma and Sam.

Maya had learned that speaking up can lead to wonderful friendships. Sometimes the best things happen when you try again.

The end.
