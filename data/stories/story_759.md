# Story story_759

Emma sat on the soft blue carpet in the playroom. Colorful toys filled every corner. Red blocks stacked high like towers. Yellow cars lined up in neat rows. Purple dolls sat quietly on tiny chairs.

But Emma felt sad and lonely. She picked up a green crayon. She drew circles on white paper. Round and round went the crayon. The waxy smell filled her nose.

"I wish I had someone to play with," Emma whispered. Her voice sounded small in the big room.

Max peeked around the doorway. His brown hair stuck up funny. He held his favorite teddy bear tight. The bear had soft fur and one missing button eye.

"Can I play too?" Max asked quietly. His feet shuffled on the wooden floor.

Emma looked up. Her eyes felt wet with tears. "I don't know," she said. "I'm not very good at sharing."

Max stepped closer. His socks made squeaky sounds. "I can share my teddy bear," he offered. He held up the fuzzy brown toy.

Emma touched the bear's soft ear. It felt warm and cuddly. "He's very nice," she said with a small smile.

"His name is Buttons," Max explained. "Because he lost a button."

Emma giggled. The sound made Max smile too. "Would you like to color with me?" Emma asked. She pointed to her box of crayons.

Max nodded fast. His whole body wiggled with excitement. They sat down together on the carpet. The soft fabric felt good under their legs.

Emma picked up a red crayon. Max chose blue. They drew on the same big paper. Emma made flowers with round petals. Max drew the sky with fluffy clouds.

"Look!" Emma pointed. "Your clouds look like cotton candy!"

Max sniffed the air. "I can almost smell the sweetness," he laughed.

They colored and colored. Their hands moved across the paper. The crayons made scratchy sounds. Soon their picture filled with bright colors.

"Let's build something," Emma suggested. She pointed to the wooden blocks in the corner.

They stacked blocks together. Emma put red ones on the bottom. Max added blue ones on top. The tower grew taller and taller.

"It's wobbly!" Max giggled as the blocks swayed.

Emma held the tower steady. "We make a good team," she said.

The blocks tumbled down with a loud crash. Both children laughed until their bellies hurt. The sound echoed around the room.

"Again! Again!" they both shouted together.

They built towers and knocked them down. They drove toy cars around the room. The wheels rolled smoothly on the floor. They had tea parties with Emma's dolls. The plastic cups clinked together softly.

Max shared his crackers from lunch. They tasted salty and crunchy. Emma shared her apple slices. The sweet juice dripped down their chins.

"This is the best day ever," Emma said. Her cheeks felt warm and happy.

"I'm glad we're friends," Max replied. He hugged his teddy bear tight.

They played until the sun started setting. Golden light came through the big window. It made everything glow warm and cozy.

Emma's mom came to the playroom. "Time to clean up," she said with a smile. She saw the children's happy faces.

Emma and Max put toys away together. They made it into a fun game. Blocks went in one box. Crayons went in another. Everything had a special place.

"Will you play with me tomorrow?" Emma asked hopefully.

"Yes!" Max said. "We can build an even bigger tower!"

They gave each other a big hug. Emma felt warm and happy inside. Her lonely feeling was completely gone.

From that day on, Emma and Max played together every day. They shared toys and snacks and stories. They learned that friendship made everything more fun.

The playroom became their special place. A place full of laughter and joy. A place where two friends could imagine anything together.

And they lived happily, playing and laughing, every single day.

The end.
