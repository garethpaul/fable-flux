# Story story_1180

**Milo's Missing Car**

Milo the monkey loved the library. He swung from shelf to shelf. Books made him happy. Stories made him smile.

Every Tuesday, Milo brought his red toy car. The car had shiny wheels. It made soft rolling sounds on the carpet. Milo would zoom it between the book stacks while Mama read.

"Time for story hour," said Mrs. Chen, the librarian. Her voice was gentle and warm.

Milo put his car on the puzzle table. He scampered to the reading circle. The other children sat crisscross on the soft rug. Mrs. Chen opened a big picture book.

The story was about brave pirates. Milo listened carefully. His tail swished with excitement. When story time ended, Milo clapped his little hands.

"Great job listening, Milo," Mrs. Chen said with a smile.

Milo hurried back to get his car. But the puzzle table was empty! His red car was gone. Milo's eyes grew wide. His heart beat fast.

"Mama!" Milo called. "My car is missing!"

Mama Monkey looked around the table. She moved puzzle pieces. She checked under chairs. No red car anywhere.

"Don't worry, sweetie," Mama said. "We'll find it together."

Milo had an idea. He would look in the toy bin! The big blue bin sat in the corner. It was full of library toys. Maybe someone put his car there by mistake.

Milo dug through blocks and dolls. He found green trucks and yellow airplanes. His hands touched soft teddy bears. But no red car with shiny wheels.

"It's not here," Milo said sadly. His voice was quiet. His shoulders drooped down.

Other children played nearby. They built tall towers with blocks. They giggled and had fun. But Milo felt worried and sad.

"Let's try something else," Mama suggested. "Where did you play with your car today?"

Milo thought hard. He touched his fuzzy chin. "I rolled it by the picture books," he remembered.

Together, they walked to the picture book section. The shelves were tall and colorful. Hundreds of books filled every space.

Milo got on his hands and knees. He crawled along the bottom shelf. Mama looked behind the books. They searched carefully and quietly.

"I see something red!" Milo squeaked with excitement.

Behind a book about dinosaurs, something red peeked out. Milo reached his small hand into the space. His fingers touched smooth plastic. It felt familiar and wonderful.

"My car!" Milo shouted happily.

He pulled out his shiny red toy car. The wheels still sparkled. It was perfect and safe. Milo hugged the car tight against his chest.

"You found it!" Mama said proudly. "You didn't give up."

Mrs. Chen walked over to see what happened. "Great detective work, Milo," she said. "The car must have rolled behind the books."

Milo nodded wisely. "I looked in two places," he said. "The toy bin and the book shelf."

"That's smart thinking," Mrs. Chen replied. "Sometimes we have to try different ideas."

Milo rolled his car on the carpet. It made that same soft sound he loved. The wheels turned smoothly. Everything was right again.

"I was worried," Milo told Mama. "But I kept looking."

"That's what brave monkeys do," Mama said. "They don't give up when things get hard."

Milo smiled his biggest smile. He learned something important today. When you lose something special, you keep trying. You look in different places. You ask for help. You don't stop until you find it.

As they walked to the library door, Milo waved goodbye to Mrs. Chen. He held his red car safely in his other hand.

"See you next Tuesday, Milo," Mrs. Chen called.

"I'll bring my car again," Milo promised. "And I'll remember where I put it."

Outside, the sun felt warm on Milo's fur. He was happy and proud. His red car was safe in his hands. Next week would be another fun library day.

Sometimes problems seem scary at first. But with patience and trying different ideas, you can solve almost anything. Milo knew that now. And he couldn't wait to tell his friends about his big adventure.

The end.
