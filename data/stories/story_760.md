# Story story_760

**Toby the Brave Little Train**

Toby was a cheerful red train. He loved riding along the sandy beach tracks. The warm sun made his paint shine bright. The salty ocean breeze felt cool and nice.

Every morning, Toby carried happy families to the beach. Children waved from his windows. "Choo choo!" Toby whistled back with joy.

But one sunny Tuesday morning, something felt wrong. Toby's wheels made a strange grinding sound. "Screech, screech, screech!" His wheels were full of sticky, wet sand.

Toby tried to move forward. His engine worked extra hard. "Puff, puff, puff!" But he could only move very slowly. The sand made everything difficult and bumpy.

"Oh no," whispered Toby quietly. "I need help cleaning my wheels. But I'm too shy to ask anyone."

Toby saw his friend Clara the Crane nearby. Clara was tall and strong. She could easily help clean the sand away. But Toby felt nervous and scared.

"What if Clara thinks I'm silly?" worried Toby. "What if she's too busy to help me?"

So Toby decided to fix the problem himself. He rolled back and forth very fast. Maybe the sand would shake loose from his wheels.

"Whoosh, whoosh!" Toby rocked side to side. He tried spinning his wheels really quickly. Sand flew everywhere like tiny brown clouds.

But the sticky sand stayed stuck tight. Now Toby felt even more frustrated and tired. His engine was getting very hot from working so hard.

More families were waiting at the station. They wanted their fun beach ride. Toby knew he had to try something different and better.

Toby took three deep breaths. The ocean air smelled fresh and salty. He watched the gentle waves splash against the shore. This made him feel a little braver.

"I can do this," Toby said to himself. "Asking for help isn't silly. Friends help each other all the time."

Toby rolled slowly toward Clara the Crane. His wheels still made that awful grinding sound. But he kept going anyway.

"Hi Clara," said Toby in a small, quiet voice. "Could you please help me? My wheels are stuck with sand."

Clara smiled her biggest, warmest smile. "Of course I'll help you, Toby! That's what friends do for each other."

Clara gently lifted Toby up high. She used her strong crane arm carefully. Then she sprayed clean, cool water on his wheels. The sticky sand washed away completely.

"There you go!" said Clara happily. "Your wheels look shiny and new again."

Toby's wheels felt smooth and perfect. He rolled forward and backward easily. No more grinding or screeching sounds at all.

"Thank you so much, Clara!" said Toby. "You're the best friend ever. I was scared to ask for help."

"Being shy is okay," said Clara kindly. "But friends always want to help each other. You can ask me anything, anytime."

Toby felt warm and happy inside. He had learned something very important today. Asking for help wasn't scary or silly. It was actually very smart and brave.

Now Toby could carry all the excited families. Children pressed their faces against his clean windows. They laughed and pointed at the beautiful blue ocean.

"Choo choo!" Toby whistled loudly and proudly. His whistle sounded clear and strong. The ocean breeze felt wonderful rushing past his shiny red sides.

From that day on, Toby never felt too shy. Whenever he had a problem, he asked for help right away. Clara was always happy to help him.

And Toby helped other friends too. He learned that helping each other made everyone feel good and happy.

The beach train rides were the best ever. Families smiled and waved every single day. Toby whistled cheerfully as he rolled along the sandy tracks.

He was no longer the shy little train. Now he was Toby the Brave Little Train who knew that asking for help was always the right thing to do.

The end.
