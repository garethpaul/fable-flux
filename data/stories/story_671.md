# Story story_671

**Benny's Magic Lunch**

Benny the golden puppy heard Mom call from the kitchen. "Lunch time, Benny!" she said in her cheerful voice. But Benny didn't feel hungry at all. He was having too much fun playing with his red ball in the sunny backyard.

"I don't want lunch," Benny barked softly. He rolled his ball under the big oak tree. The grass felt cool and tickly under his paws. A butterfly danced around the yellow flowers nearby.

Mom came outside with Benny's blue food bowl. It smelled like chicken and rice. Usually Benny loved that smell. But today he just wanted to keep playing. The warm sun felt so good on his fuzzy coat.

"Come on, little one," Mom said gently. "You need to eat to grow big and strong." She set the bowl down on the wooden deck. The kibble made tiny clicking sounds as it settled.

Benny walked slowly to his bowl. He sniffed the food with his wet black nose. It did smell good. But he still felt more like playing than eating. His tail drooped down low.

Just then, something amazing happened. A tiny blue bird landed right next to Benny's bowl! The bird had bright yellow feet and sparkly dark eyes. It chirped a happy little song.

"Hello, Benny," the bird said in a tiny, musical voice. "I'm Pip the lunch bird. I help puppies enjoy their meals."

Benny's eyes grew wide with wonder. "You can talk?" he whispered.

"Of course!" Pip chirped. "I make lunch time magical for special puppies like you." The little bird hopped around the bowl. "Watch this!"

Pip began to sing a silly lunch song. "Crunch and munch, time for lunch! Yummy food makes puppies strong!" As Pip sang, sparkly golden dust floated from his wings. The dust made Benny's food shimmer and glow.

Benny giggled. The bird's song made him feel happy and hungry. He took a small bite of his food. It tasted extra delicious! The chicken was tender and warm. The rice was fluffy and perfect.

"This tastes like magic!" Benny barked with joy. His tail started wagging fast.

Pip laughed with a sound like tiny bells. "That's because you're eating with a happy heart," the bird explained. "Food always tastes better when you're grateful and joyful."

Benny ate another bite, then another. Each mouthful tasted wonderful. The warm food filled his belly and made him feel cozy inside. Pip danced around the bowl, singing cheerful songs about friendship and growing strong.

Mom watched from the doorway with a big smile. She couldn't see Pip, but she could see how happy Benny looked. His tail wagged the whole time he ate.

When Benny finished every last bite, he felt full and satisfied. His energy was coming back already. Pip landed on his head for just a moment.

"Remember, Benny," Pip whispered. "I'll visit whenever you need help enjoying your meals. Just think happy thoughts about growing big and strong."

"Thank you, Pip!" Benny said. But when he looked up, the magical bird had disappeared. Only a single blue feather remained by his empty bowl.

Mom came over and patted Benny's head. "Good boy! You ate everything." She picked up his bowl and gave him fresh water. The water was cool and refreshing.

Benny felt proud and happy. His tummy was full, and he had energy to play again. He picked up his red ball and tossed it in the air. Playing felt even more fun now that he wasn't hungry.

That evening, as Mom prepared dinner, Benny's tail wagged with excitement. He couldn't wait for tomorrow's lunch. Maybe Pip would visit again with more magical songs and sparkly dust.

Benny curled up in his soft bed that night, dreaming about blue birds and golden dust. He learned that eating good food was just as important as playing. Tomorrow would bring another wonderful lunch adventure.

From that day on, Benny always came running when Mom called for meals.

The end.
