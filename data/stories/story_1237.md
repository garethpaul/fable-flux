# Story story_1237

**Moo's Missing Blocks**

Moo the cow lived in Farmer Jane's backyard. He had soft brown spots and kind eyes. Every morning, Moo liked to play with his colorful blocks.

The blocks were red, blue, yellow, and green. Moo could stack them high. He could make towers and bridges. Playing with blocks made Moo very happy.

One sunny morning, Moo woke up early. Birds were singing in the apple tree. The grass felt cool under his hooves. Moo walked to his favorite spot by the big oak tree.

But something was wrong. His blocks were gone!

"Where are my blocks?" Moo said sadly. He looked left and right. He looked under the apple tree. He looked behind the water trough. No blocks anywhere.

Moo's eyes filled with tears. "I can't play without my blocks," he mooed softly.

Just then, Farmer Jane came outside. She carried a bucket of fresh water. Her boots made squishing sounds in the mud.

"Good morning, Moo," she said cheerfully. "Why do you look so sad?"

"My blocks are missing," Moo explained. "I can't find them anywhere."

Farmer Jane set down her bucket. She patted Moo's head gently. "Don't worry," she said. "We can look together."

Moo felt a little better. He wasn't alone anymore.

"Let's think," said Farmer Jane. "Where did you play yesterday?"

Moo remembered. "I played by the fence. Then I played near the barn."

"Let's check the fence first," Farmer Jane suggested.

They walked to the wooden fence together. Farmer Jane's boots crunched on dry leaves. Moo's hooves made soft thuds on the ground.

They looked behind every fence post. They looked in the tall grass. They even looked in the flower garden. But they found no blocks.

"This isn't working," Moo sighed. His tail swished sadly.

"Don't give up," said Farmer Jane. "Let's try looking by the barn."

They walked to the red barn. It smelled like hay and apples. Chickens clucked nearby. A gentle breeze rustled the corn stalks.

Farmer Jane opened the big barn door. It creaked loudly. Sunlight streamed inside, making everything golden.

"Let's look carefully," she said.

They searched behind hay bales. They looked in empty stalls. They checked near the tool shed. Still no blocks.

Moo felt very discouraged. "Maybe they're gone forever," he mooed sadly.

"Wait," said Farmer Jane. "What's that sound?"

They both listened carefully. Click, clack, click. The sound came from above.

Farmer Jane looked up at the hay loft. "I see something colorful up there!"

She climbed the wooden ladder. Her boots made hollow sounds on each rung. At the top, she gasped.

"Moo! I found them!"

Three baby barn cats were playing with the blocks! The kittens batted them with tiny paws. The blocks rolled and tumbled in the soft hay.

"Meow, meow," said the kittens. They looked very sorry.

Farmer Jane carefully gathered all the blocks. She climbed down the ladder slowly. The blocks clinked together in her arms.

"Here they are," she said, smiling. "The kittens must have carried them up there to play."

Moo felt so happy! His blocks were safe. They weren't lost forever.

"Thank you for helping me," Moo said gratefully. "I couldn't have found them alone."

"That's what friends do," said Farmer Jane. "When something seems lost, we keep looking. We don't give up."

Moo carried his blocks back to the oak tree. The sun felt warm on his back. The grass smelled sweet and fresh.

He stacked the red block on the blue block. Then the yellow on top. Finally, the green block went on the very top.

"Perfect!" Moo said happily.

From that day on, Moo always remembered Farmer Jane's words. When things seemed lost or hard, he would keep trying. He learned that asking for help made problems easier to solve.

And sometimes, the baby cats would come down from the loft. They would play blocks with Moo in the sunshine. Everyone was happy together.

The end.
