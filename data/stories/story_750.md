# Story story_750

**Maya and the Butterfly Field**

Maya sat in the sunny field behind her house. The grass tickled her bare feet. Yellow flowers danced in the warm breeze. But Maya felt sad and lonely.

Her big sister Emma was at camp. Her mom was busy working. Maya had no one to play with today.

"I wish I had a friend," Maya whispered. She picked a dandelion and blew the fluffy seeds. They floated away like tiny parachutes.

Just then, Maya heard footsteps. A girl with curly red hair walked toward her. The girl looked about Maya's age. She carried a small purple backpack.

"Hi," said the girl shyly. "I'm Lily. My family just moved next door."

Maya looked up. "I'm Maya. I live in the yellow house."

Lily pointed to the white house nearby. "That's my new home. I saw you from my window. You looked lonely."

Maya nodded. "I am lonely. My sister is away."

"Can I play with you?" Lily asked hopefully. Her green eyes sparkled.

Maya smiled for the first time all day. "Yes! I would like that."

The girls sat together in the soft grass. Lily opened her backpack. She pulled out a small jar with holes in the lid.

"I collect pretty things," Lily explained. "Want to help me find treasures?"

Maya clapped her hands. "That sounds fun!"

The girls walked through the field together. Maya found a smooth white stone. It felt cool in her palm. Lily discovered a bright red ladybug on a leaf.

"Look at its tiny spots!" Maya giggled. The ladybug crawled onto her finger. Its little legs tickled.

Lily gently placed the ladybug in her jar. "We'll let it go later," she promised.

They found more treasures. A blue feather from a jay. A shiny acorn. Three perfect clover flowers. Each discovery made them laugh with joy.

Then something magical happened. Dozens of orange butterflies appeared. They fluttered all around the girls like flying flowers.

"Wow!" Maya gasped. "They're beautiful!"

The butterflies landed on their hair and arms. Their wings felt soft as silk. Maya and Lily stood very still. They didn't want to scare them away.

"This is the best day ever," Lily whispered.

Maya agreed. "I'm so happy you moved here."

The butterflies flew away after a few minutes. But the girls kept playing. They made daisy chains for their wrists. They played hide and seek behind the big oak tree.

When the sun got lower, both girls felt hungry. Maya had an idea.

"Want to have a picnic?" she asked.

"Yes!" Lily said excitedly.

Maya ran to her house. She came back with apple slices and crackers. Lily brought juice boxes from her backpack.

They spread Maya's blanket under the oak tree. The shade felt nice and cool. They shared their snacks and talked about everything.

"I was scared to move," Lily admitted. "I missed my old friends."

"Now you have a new friend," Maya said warmly. "Me!"

Lily smiled big. "And you're not lonely anymore."

"Nope! Not one bit," Maya laughed.

As the sky turned pink and orange, the girls' moms called them home. But they didn't feel sad.

"Can we play tomorrow?" Lily asked.

"Every day!" Maya promised. "We can explore more of the field."

They hugged goodbye. Maya felt warm and happy inside. The lonely feeling was completely gone.

That night, Maya told her mom about her wonderful day. She couldn't wait to tell Emma about her new friend when she came home from camp.

Before bed, Maya looked out her window. She could see Lily's house next door. A light glowed in Lily's bedroom window.

Maya waved, and Lily waved back.

The field looked peaceful in the moonlight. Tomorrow it would be full of adventures again. But this time, Maya wouldn't be alone.

She had found something better than treasures in that field. She had found a true friend.

Maya fell asleep with a big smile on her face, dreaming of butterflies and friendship.

The end.
