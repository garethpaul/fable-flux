# Story story_577

**Milo's Missing Blocks**

Milo the monkey loved playing in Sunny Park. Every morning, he swung from tree to tree. His favorite spot was the big oak tree. Under its shady branches, he kept his special wooden blocks.

The blocks were bright red, blue, and yellow. Milo built tall towers with them. He made bridges and castles too. Playing with blocks made him very happy.

One sunny Tuesday morning, Milo swung to his oak tree. He looked down at his usual spot. His eyes grew wide. His mouth fell open.

"Oh no!" Milo cried. "My blocks are gone!"

He scrambled down the tree trunk. His little paws searched everywhere. He looked behind rocks. He checked under leaves. He even peeked in the flower bushes.

No blocks anywhere.

Milo's tail drooped sadly. His bottom lip trembled. Where could his precious blocks be?

"I need to find them," Milo said to himself. "But where should I look?"

Milo scratched his fuzzy head. Then he had an idea. Maybe the wind blew them away! Yesterday was very windy indeed.

He swung from branch to branch. He searched high and low. Milo looked in every tree around the playground. He checked the monkey bars. He searched the slide.

Still no blocks.

Milo sat on a park bench. He felt very sad. A tear rolled down his furry cheek.

"Why are you crying?" asked a gentle voice.

Milo looked up. Mrs. Robin sat on the bench beside him. Her red breast was bright in the sunshine.

"I lost my blocks," Milo sniffled. "I looked everywhere for them."

Mrs. Robin tilted her little head. "Did you ask for help?" she chirped softly.

Milo wiped his eyes. "Ask for help?"

"Sometimes friends see things we miss," Mrs. Robin said kindly.

Milo's ears perked up. That was a good idea! He jumped off the bench.

"Will you help me look?" Milo asked hopefully.

"Of course!" chirped Mrs. Robin.

Soon, other park friends joined them. Benny the squirrel scampered over. Rosie the rabbit hopped up too. Even old Chester the turtle came to help.

"Let's spread out," suggested Benny. "We'll cover more ground that way."

The friends searched together. Benny checked the tall grass. Rosie looked under the picnic tables. Chester searched slowly by the pond.

Mrs. Robin flew high above the trees. Her sharp eyes scanned the whole park.

"I see something colorful!" she called down. "Behind the maintenance shed!"

All the friends hurried over. There, scattered behind the small building, were Milo's blocks! They were a bit dirty but perfectly fine.

"My blocks!" Milo cheered. He gathered them up carefully. "But how did they get here?"

Chester smiled his wise, slow smile. "The park workers probably moved them," he said. "They clean up every Monday night."

Milo felt so relieved. His blocks were safe! He hugged each one tightly.

"Thank you, everyone!" Milo said happily. "I couldn't have found them alone."

"That's what friends are for," said Rosie, wiggling her pink nose.

Milo learned something important that day. Sometimes the best solution is asking for help. Friends can see things we cannot see ourselves.

He built a wonderful castle with his blocks. All his friends helped. Benny found the perfect sticks for flags. Rosie picked pretty flowers for decoration. Mrs. Robin sang beautiful songs while they worked.

Even Chester helped by giving good advice about making the walls strong.

As the sun set over Sunny Park, Milo felt grateful. He had his blocks back. But more importantly, he had amazing friends.

"Tomorrow I'll ask the park workers about a safe place for my blocks," Milo said.

His friends agreed that was very smart thinking.

From that day on, Milo always remembered: when you have a problem, don't give up. Try different solutions. And never be afraid to ask friends for help.

The best adventures happen when friends work together.

The end.
