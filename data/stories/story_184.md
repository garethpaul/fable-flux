# Tommy the Brave Little Truck

Down by the sparkling blue pond lived Tommy the truck. Tommy was bright red with shiny chrome bumpers. He had big, round wheels that loved to roll.

Every morning, Tommy watched the pond. Ducks splashed in the water. Frogs hopped on lily pads. Fish jumped up and down. "What a beautiful place," Tommy thought.

But Tommy had a big problem. He was stuck! His back wheels were deep in the muddy pond edge. The soft, squishy mud held him tight. Tommy could not move forward. He could not move backward.

"Oh no," Tommy whispered softly. "I need help."

Near the pond, other vehicles played together. Sara the school bus honked her horn. Mike the motorcycle zoomed around trees. Bella the bicycle rang her silver bell.

Tommy wanted to call for help. But his engine felt nervous. His horn felt quiet. "What if they are too busy?" Tommy worried. "What if they say no?"

So Tommy tried to help himself first.

He pushed with his front wheels. "Rrrrrr," went his engine. The mud made squishy, gloppy sounds. But Tommy stayed stuck.

He rocked back and forth. "Vroom, vroom," Tommy tried harder. The pond water splashed gently. Mud bubbled around his tires. Still stuck.

Tommy tried spinning his wheels fast. "Whirrrrrr!" Round and round they went. The mud flew everywhere! It made splat sounds on nearby rocks. Tommy was still very stuck.

"Maybe I should ask for help," Tommy thought. His red paint felt warm in the sunshine. But asking felt scary.

Tommy watched Sara the bus drive by. "Hi Sara!" he wanted to shout. But only a tiny "beep" came out. Sara did not hear him.

Mike the motorcycle zoomed past next. "Help me, Mike!" Tommy tried to call. But his voice was too quiet again. Mike kept riding.

Tommy felt sad. His chrome bumpers did not shine as bright. "I will never get out," he sighed.

Just then, little Bella the bicycle rode close to the pond. She was picking pretty yellow flowers.

Tommy took a deep breath. His engine rumbled with courage. "Bella!" he called out loud. "Can you help me please?"

Bella looked up with surprise. "Tommy! You are stuck!" She dropped her flowers and pedaled quickly.

"I was too shy to ask before," Tommy said. His voice shook a little.

"That is okay," Bella said kindly. "Everyone feels shy sometimes. Let me get the others!"

Bella rang her bell loudly. "Ding, ding, ding!" Sara and Mike heard the sound. They rushed over to help.

"We can pull you out!" Sara said with her cheerful voice.

Mike brought a strong, thick rope. Sara tied it to Tommy's front bumper. All three friends worked together.

"One, two, three, PULL!" they shouted.

Tommy's engine roared with hope. "Vroom!" His wheels turned and turned. The mud made loud, squelchy sounds.

Slowly, Tommy felt himself moving! His back wheels came free from the sticky mud. "Pop!" went the sound as he rolled onto dry ground.

"I am free! I am free!" Tommy honked his horn happily. "Beep, beep, beep!"

All his friends cheered loudly. "Hooray for Tommy!"

Tommy's red paint gleamed in the warm sunlight. His chrome bumpers sparkled like stars. He felt proud and brave.

"Thank you for helping me," Tommy said. "I learned something important today."

"What did you learn?" asked Bella sweetly.

"I learned that asking for help is not scary," Tommy said. "Friends want to help each other. I just needed to be brave enough to ask."

From that day on, Tommy was never too shy. When he needed help, he asked his friends. And when his friends needed help, Tommy was always ready.

The pond sparkled more beautiful than ever. And Tommy the truck felt happy, brave, and loved.
