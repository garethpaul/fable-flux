# Ellie and Captain Splash

On Farmer Joe's big farm, there lived a gentle elephant named Ellie. She had soft gray skin and kind brown eyes. Every morning, Ellie played by the pond alone.

Ellie loved to splash in the water. She picked up mud with her trunk. She made little mud pies. But something was missing. Ellie felt lonely.

"I wish I had a friend," Ellie sighed. She looked at her reflection sadly. The water rippled around her big feet.

One sunny morning, something new appeared. A bright red boat floated on the pond. The boat had a cheerful yellow sail. His name was Captain Splash.

"Hello there!" called Captain Splash. "You look sad. What's wrong?"

Ellie lifted her trunk shyly. "I'm just playing alone. It's not very fun."

Captain Splash bobbed gently. "May I play with you? I love pond games!"

Ellie's eyes lit up. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" Captain Splash smiled. "What do you like to do?"

"I love making mud pies," Ellie said. "And splashing in the water."

Captain Splash bounced excitedly. "I love water games too! Let's play together!"

Ellie waded deeper into the pond. She scooped up water with her trunk. She sprayed it high in the air. The water sparkled like diamonds.

"That's amazing!" cheered Captain Splash. "Can you spray water on my sail?"

Ellie giggled. She aimed carefully. Cool water splashed over Captain Splash's red sides. He spun around happily.

"Your turn!" said Ellie. "Show me what you can do!"

Captain Splash sailed in perfect circles. He made gentle waves. The waves tickled Ellie's feet. She laughed and clapped her trunk.

"Let's make mud pies together!" suggested Ellie.

"How can I help?" asked Captain Splash.

Ellie thought carefully. "You can carry the mud! I'll scoop it up."

Ellie filled Captain Splash's boat with soft mud. He sailed slowly to the shore. Together, they made the most beautiful mud pies ever.

"Look at our creations!" said Captain Splash proudly.

They had made five perfect mud pies. Some were big. Some were small. All were wonderful.

"This is so much better than playing alone," said Ellie.

"Everything is more fun with friends," agreed Captain Splash.

They played hide and seek next. Ellie hid behind the big oak tree. Captain Splash hid among the lily pads. They took turns finding each other.

"Found you!" giggled Ellie.

"Your turn to hide!" laughed Captain Splash.

The sun climbed higher in the sky. Farmer Joe's rooster crowed loudly. But Ellie and Captain Splash kept playing.

They raced across the pond. Ellie ran through the shallow water. Captain Splash sailed as fast as he could. They always finished at exactly the same time.

"We're both winners!" they cheered together.

As afternoon came, they felt tired but happy. They had played for hours. Neither felt lonely anymore.

"Thank you for being my friend," said Ellie softly.

"Thank you for sharing your pond," said Captain Splash.

They sat together peacefully. Ellie's trunk touched Captain Splash's sail gently. The pond sparkled in the sunshine.

"Should we play again tomorrow?" asked Captain Splash hopefully.

"Yes!" trumpeted Ellie joyfully. "Every day!"

From that day forward, Ellie and Captain Splash played together daily. They built magnificent mud castles. They had splashing contests. They told each other funny stories.

Other farm animals came to watch. The ducks quacked with delight. The pigs oinked happily. Everyone loved seeing the unlikely friends.

Farmer Joe smiled when he saw them. "True friendship comes in all shapes and sizes," he said.

Ellie learned that cooperation made everything better. Captain Splash discovered that different friends bring special gifts. Together, they could do amazing things.

Every evening, they watched the sunset together. The sky turned pink and orange. The pond reflected the beautiful colors.

"Friends forever?" asked Captain Splash.

"Friends forever!" promised Ellie.

And they were. The elephant and the boat became the very best of friends. Their laughter echoed across Farmer Joe's farm every single day.

The end.
