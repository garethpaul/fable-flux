# Woolly's Big Sharing Day

Woolly was a fluffy white sheep who loved to play. Her favorite place was the sunny playroom. It had soft blue carpet. It had colorful toys everywhere. Woolly bounced on her little hooves. Today would be a wonderful day!

The playroom door opened with a creak. In walked Bunny, Mouse, and Duck. They were Woolly's friends from next door.

"Hi, Woolly!" said Bunny with a wiggle of her nose.

"Can we play?" squeaked Mouse in his tiny voice.

Woolly looked at her special toys. She had red blocks that clicked together. She had a purple ball that bounced high. She had yellow crayons that smelled waxy and new.

"These are MY toys," Woolly said loudly. She hugged the red blocks tight. "You can't have them!"

Bunny's ears drooped down sadly. Mouse's whiskers twitched. Duck's orange feet shuffled on the carpet.

"But Woolly," said Duck softly. "We just want to play together."

"No!" Woolly stamped her hoof. "Mine, mine, mine!"

Her friends sat in the corner. They looked very sad. The playroom felt quiet and empty. Even the sunshine seemed dimmer.

Woolly played with her blocks alone. Click, click, stack. But it wasn't very fun. She bounced her purple ball. Bounce, bounce, catch. But nobody laughed with her. She drew with her yellow crayons. Scribble, scribble, color. But nobody said "Pretty picture!"

Woolly's tummy felt funny and twisted. Her heart felt heavy like a rock. Playing alone was no fun at all.

She looked at her sad friends again. Bunny was staring at her paws. Mouse was curled up small. Duck had her head tucked down.

"Maybe..." Woolly whispered to herself. "Maybe I could try something different."

Woolly picked up one red block. She walked slowly to Bunny. Her hooves made soft tapping sounds.

"Bunny?" Woolly's voice was quiet. "Would you like to build with me?"

Bunny's ears perked up a little. "Really?"

"Yes," said Woolly. She handed Bunny the block. "We could make a tall tower together."

Bunny smiled. Her pink nose wiggled happily. "I would love that!"

They started building. Click, stack, click, stack. The tower grew taller and taller.

"Mouse!" called Woolly. "Come help us! Your tiny paws are perfect for the small blocks."

Mouse scurried over quickly. His tail swished with joy. Soon all three friends were building together.

"Duck," said Woolly with a big smile. "Want to color our tower when we're done?"

Duck flapped her wings excitedly. "Oh yes! I love yellow!"

They worked together happily. The playroom filled with giggles and chatter. Click went the blocks. Swish went the crayons. Tap, tap went their feet dancing around.

When they finished, they had built the most amazing rainbow tower ever. It was red and blue and yellow and green. It sparkled in the sunshine.

"Wow!" said all the friends together.

Woolly's heart felt warm and light. Like it was filled with bubbles. Her tummy felt happy and fluttery.

"This is much more fun than playing alone," Woolly said. She looked at her friends' smiling faces.

"Sharing makes everything better!" agreed Bunny.

"More friends means more fun!" squeaked Mouse.

"And more colors!" quacked Duck happily.

They all hugged in a big, fuzzy group hug. Woolly's soft wool tickled everyone. They giggled until their sides hurt.

From that day on, Woolly always shared her toys. The playroom became the happiest place ever. Friends came to play every single day. They built towers together. They drew pictures together. They bounced balls together.

Woolly learned that sharing wasn't about giving things away. It was about making joy bigger. When she shared her toys, the fun grew twice as big. When she shared with friends, her happiness grew three times as big.

And every night, as Woolly snuggled into her cozy bed, she smiled. Tomorrow would bring another wonderful day of sharing and playing with friends.

The end.
