# Story story_1054

**The Little Red Bike in the Library**

In the corner of the sunny library sat a little red bike. Her name was Ruby. She had shiny handlebars and a cheerful bell. Ruby loved to watch the children read books.

But Ruby felt very lonely. The books had each other on the shelves. The chairs sat together in circles. The computers hummed happily side by side. Ruby sat all by herself.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," Ruby sighed. Her bell made a tiny, sad ding.

Every day, children came to the library. They read stories about dragons and princesses. They giggled at funny pictures. They whispered secrets to their friends. But nobody played with Ruby.

Ruby watched a little boy named Sam. He picked up book after book. Sam looked sad too. He sat alone at a small table. Other children played together, but Sam stayed by himself.

Ruby had an idea! She would ring her bell to get Sam's attention. Maybe he would want to be her friend.

"Ding! Ding! Ding!" Ruby rang her bell as loud as she could.

Sam looked up from his book. He saw Ruby in the corner. But then Ms. Chen, the librarian, walked over.

"Shh," Ms. Chen said gently. "Libraries are quiet places, little bike."

Ruby's plan didn't work. She felt even sadder than before. Her red paint seemed less bright. Her handlebars drooped down.

The next day, Ruby tried again. This time, she had a different idea. She would roll closer to the children. Maybe they would notice her then.

Ruby wiggled and wobbled. She pushed with all her might. Slowly, she rolled toward the reading area. Her wheels squeaked softly on the carpet.

But Ruby rolled too far! She bumped into a bookshelf. Three books tumbled down with a thump, thump, thump.

"Oh my!" said Ms. Chen. She picked up the books. She rolled Ruby back to her corner. "You need to stay here, little bike."

Ruby felt terrible. Now she had made a mess. Her bell didn't even want to ring anymore.

That afternoon, Ruby noticed Sam again. He was reading a book about bicycles. The book had pictures of kids riding bikes in the park. They looked so happy and free.

Sam sighed. He touched the pictures gently. "I wish I could ride a bike," he whispered.

Ruby's handlebars perked up. She had a wonderful new idea! Instead of trying to get attention, she would help Sam. That's what friends do for each other.

Ruby thought very hard. How could she help Sam learn about bikes? Then she remembered something special. She had pedals that moved when pushed. She had handlebars that turned left and right. She could teach Sam without even moving!

Ruby waited patiently. When Sam walked by her corner, she made her pedals sparkle in the sunlight. Sam stopped and looked.

"Wow," Sam said quietly. "A real bike."

Sam came closer. He touched Ruby's smooth handlebars. He turned them gently left and right. Ruby felt so happy! This was much better than ringing her bell loudly.

"Would you like to practice?" whispered Ms. Chen. She had been watching with a smile.

Sam nodded eagerly. Ms. Chen helped him sit on Ruby's seat. Sam put his hands on the handlebars. He pushed the pedals with his feet. Ruby stayed perfectly still, but she felt proud and useful.

"This is fun!" Sam said softly. "Thank you, bike."

From that day on, Sam visited Ruby every time he came to the library. First, he would read his books. Then, he would practice with Ruby. Other children saw how much fun Sam was having. Soon, Ruby had many friends who wanted to practice too.

Ruby learned something important. The best way to make friends isn't by being loud or pushy. The best way is by being helpful and kind.

Now Ruby's bell rings with joy every day. She has Sam and all her new friends. The library corner doesn't feel lonely anymore. It feels full of friendship and learning.

Ruby discovered that when you help others, you help yourself too. And that made her the happiest little red bike in the whole library.

The end.
