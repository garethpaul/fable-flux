# Lily and the Missing Red Ball

Lily loved her bright red ball more than any toy in her playroom. It was shiny and smooth, and it bounced perfectly every time. The red ball was round like the sun and made a happy *boing* sound when it hit the floor.

"Time to play!" Lily said cheerfully. She looked around her colorful playroom. Soft teddy bears sat on shelves. Building blocks made tall towers. Puzzle pieces scattered across the carpet like tiny rainbows.

But where was her special red ball?

Lily's smile turned into a worried frown. "Where is my ball?" she whispered. Her voice sounded small and sad. The playroom suddenly felt too quiet without the happy bouncing sounds.

She looked under her wooden toy chest. No red ball. Just dust bunnies that tickled her nose. "Achoo!"

Next, Lily checked behind the big blue bookshelf. She found three crayons and a missing sock. But no bouncy red ball anywhere.

"Maybe it's in the toy basket," Lily said hopefully. She dug through soft stuffed animals and plastic cars. Her hands felt fuzzy bears and smooth race cars. Still no red ball.

Lily sat down on the soft carpet. Her eyes felt watery. "I really miss my ball," she said quietly.

But then Lily remembered something important. Mama always said, "When something is hard, try again!" 

Lily stood up tall. She wiped her eyes with her small hands. "I won't give up," she said bravely.

This time, Lily looked more carefully. She got down on her hands and knees. She crawled around like a puppy, checking every corner.

Under the art table, she found colored pencils. Behind the dollhouse, she discovered a tiny plastic spoon. In the dress-up box, she saw sparkly hats and funny glasses.

Then Lily had a smart idea. "What if my ball rolled somewhere high?" she wondered aloud.

She dragged her step stool across the room. The wooden stool made a *scritch-scratch* sound on the floor. Lily climbed up carefully, holding tight to be safe.

She looked on top of the tall bookshelf. No ball there either.

Lily felt tired, but she didn't want to stop looking. She thought harder. "Where did I play with my ball yesterday?"

Suddenly, she remembered! Yesterday, she had been bouncing her ball near the window. The afternoon sun had made the red ball sparkle like a ruby.

Lily hurried to the big window. Long, flowery curtains hung down to the floor. The curtains swayed gently in the breeze.

"Could my ball be hiding?" Lily asked herself.

She carefully moved the soft curtains aside. The fabric felt smooth and cool against her fingers.

And there it was! Her beautiful red ball sat quietly behind the curtains, waiting for her.

"I found you!" Lily shouted happily. She picked up her ball and hugged it tight. The ball felt perfectly round and wonderfully smooth in her arms.

*Boing! Boing! Boing!* The ball bounced happily as Lily played. The sound filled the playroom with joy again.

"I'm so glad I didn't give up," Lily said, smiling big. Her cheeks felt warm and happy. "Looking everywhere was hard work, but I found my special ball!"

She bounced the ball gently, watching it go up and down, up and down. The red color looked bright and cheerful in the sunlight.

Lily learned something important that day. When something seems lost or hard to find, trying different ways and not giving up can help solve the problem.

"Tomorrow I'll remember to put my ball in the toy basket when I'm done playing," Lily said wisely.

She spent the rest of the afternoon bouncing her favorite red ball, feeling proud and happy. The playroom was filled with joyful *boing* sounds once again.

And every time her ball bounced, Lily remembered: she was brave, smart, and never gave up!

The end.
