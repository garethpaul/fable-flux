# Story story_582

Mama sat at the kitchen table. She looked very sad. The kitchen felt quiet and empty.

Mama picked up her favorite book. She tried to read. But reading alone was not fun. She put the book down with a soft thump.

Mama looked around the sunny kitchen. Yellow sunlight danced on the floor. The smell of fresh cookies filled the air. But Mama still felt lonely.

On the counter sat a colorful puzzle box. The puzzle had been there for many days. Nobody had played with it. The puzzle felt forgotten too.

The puzzle box wiggled a little bit. "Hello, Mama," said a tiny voice. "I am your puzzle friend. I see you are sad."

Mama looked surprised. "Oh! You can talk, little puzzle?"

"Yes!" said the puzzle happily. "I have been waiting to play. Will you be my friend?"

Mama smiled for the first time today. "I would love a friend. But I have never done a puzzle before."

"That's okay," said the puzzle gently. "I will help you learn. We can figure it out together."

Mama opened the colorful box. Hundreds of puzzle pieces spilled out. They made a soft rustling sound. The pieces were bright red, blue, yellow, and green.

"Oh my!" said Mama. "There are so many pieces. This looks very hard."

The puzzle giggled. "Don't worry, Mama. We start with the corners first. Look for the pieces with flat edges."

Mama picked up different pieces. She felt their smooth surfaces. Some pieces had bumpy edges. Some pieces had flat, straight sides.

"Here's a corner piece!" Mama said proudly. The piece was bright yellow like sunshine.

"Perfect!" cheered the puzzle. "Now find three more corners."

Mama searched carefully through all the pieces. She found a red corner. Then a blue corner. Finally, a green corner piece.

"Wonderful!" said the puzzle. "Now we make the border. Look for pieces with one flat edge."

Mama and the puzzle worked together slowly. They talked and laughed. The kitchen felt warm and happy now.

"This piece goes here," said Mama. Click! The piece fit perfectly.

"And this piece goes there," said the puzzle. Click! Another perfect fit.

Soon they had built the whole border. It looked like a picture frame. Inside was empty space waiting to be filled.

"Now comes the fun part," said the puzzle excitedly. "We fill in the middle! Look at the colors and shapes."

Mama studied the picture on the box. It showed a beautiful garden. There were red roses, yellow sunflowers, and green grass.

"I see where this flower piece goes," said Mama. She placed it carefully. Click!

"And I know where this grass piece belongs," said the puzzle. Click!

They worked together for a long time. Piece by piece, the garden picture grew. Mama's fingers felt the smooth puzzle pieces. She smelled the sweet cookie scent still in the air.

Sometimes pieces didn't fit. "That's okay," said the puzzle kindly. "We just try again. Puzzles teach us to be patient."

Mama nodded. "You're right, puzzle friend. I'm learning so much."

As the afternoon went on, more pieces found their homes. The roses bloomed red. The sunflowers turned bright yellow. The grass grew green and thick.

"We're almost done!" said Mama happily. Only one piece was left. It was a small piece shaped like a butterfly.

"Where does this go?" asked Mama.

"Right there in the sky," said the puzzle. "You can do it!"

Mama placed the last piece. Click! The puzzle was complete. The beautiful garden picture was finished.

"We did it!" shouted Mama. "We finished the whole puzzle!"

"Yes!" cheered the puzzle. "We make a great team!"

Mama clapped her hands with joy. The kitchen didn't feel lonely anymore. It felt full of friendship and happiness.

"Thank you, puzzle friend," said Mama. "You taught me something wonderful today."

"What did you learn?" asked the puzzle.

"I learned that doing things together is much more fun than doing them alone," said Mama.

The puzzle smiled warmly. "And I learned that the best part of any puzzle is sharing it with a good friend."

From that day on, Mama and the puzzle played together every afternoon. The kitchen was never lonely again.

The end.
