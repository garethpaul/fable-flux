# Story story_1287

Mama sat on the soft hallway rug. She felt very lonely today. The house was quiet and still.

She picked up her favorite book. But reading alone wasn't fun. Mama sighed and put the book down.

"I wish I had someone to play with," she whispered.

High up on the hallway shelf sat a colorful jigsaw puzzle. The puzzle had been watching Mama all morning. Its bright pieces sparkled in the sunlight.

The puzzle felt sad seeing Mama alone. "I want to help," thought the puzzle. "Maybe we could play together!"

The puzzle wiggled and jiggled on the shelf. One corner hung over the edge. Then another corner slipped down.

THUMP! The puzzle box tumbled to the floor. Puzzle pieces scattered everywhere like rainbow confetti.

Mama looked up with surprise. "Oh my!" she said softly. "What happened here?"

She saw the empty puzzle box. Colorful pieces covered the hallway floor. Some pieces had flowers. Others showed a sunny garden scene.

"This looks like a beautiful puzzle," Mama said. She picked up a bright yellow piece. It felt smooth and warm in her hand.

Mama sat down on the rug again. She began sorting the puzzle pieces. Blue pieces went in one pile. Green pieces in another.

"This edge piece has pretty roses," she said aloud. Her voice echoed gently in the hallway.

Slowly, Mama started connecting pieces together. Click! Two pieces fit perfectly. The sound made her smile.

She found another matching piece. Click! Then another. Click, click!

"Look at this lovely garden growing!" Mama laughed. Her lonely feeling started melting away.

The puzzle felt happy too. Its pieces were coming together. Mama's gentle hands moved each piece carefully.

"You have such beautiful colors," Mama told the puzzle. "Thank you for falling down to play with me."

If puzzles could giggle, this one would have laughed with joy.

Mama worked quietly for a while. She hummed a soft tune. The hallway felt cozy and warm now.

"Almost finished," she said, looking for the last few pieces. "Just need this corner here."

She found a piece with a bright red butterfly. It fit perfectly in the top corner. Click!

"There!" Mama clapped her hands together. "You're all complete now, beautiful puzzle!"

The finished puzzle showed a magical garden. Butterflies danced over colorful flowers. A little pond sparkled in the corner.

Mama traced the picture with her finger. "This is the most wonderful puzzle ever," she said. "Playing with you made me feel so happy."

She carefully picked up each piece. One by one, they went back in the box. But Mama didn't put the puzzle back on the high shelf.

Instead, she placed it on the low table nearby. "I want you close to me," she said. "We're friends now."

From that day on, Mama and the puzzle played together often. Sometimes they built the garden picture. Sometimes they just sorted the pretty pieces.

The hallway became their special place. Sunshine streamed through the window. It made the puzzle pieces glow like tiny jewels.

Other toys noticed how happy they looked together. The teddy bear smiled from his chair. The picture books seemed to glow brighter.

"Playing together is much better than playing alone," Mama said one sunny afternoon.

She patted the puzzle box gently. "Thank you for being brave enough to fall down and find me."

The puzzle felt proud and loved. It had found the perfect friend.

Now the hallway was never quiet or lonely. It was filled with laughter and joy. Mama and her puzzle friend played together every single day.

And they lived happily ever after, creating beautiful pictures and sharing wonderful times in their cozy hallway home.

The sun smiled down on them both. Their friendship made everything brighter and more beautiful than ever before.

The end.
