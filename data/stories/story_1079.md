# Story story_1079

**Sunny's Special Lunch**

The big yellow sun shone bright in the sky. It was lunchtime at Meadow Farm. All the animals hurried to their food bowls. All except Sunny the horse.

Sunny stood in the corner of her paddock. Her ears drooped down. Her tail swished slowly. She looked at her hay pile with sad eyes.

"Come eat, Sunny!" called Farmer Mae. "Your lunch is ready!"

But Sunny didn't move. The hay looked boring. It was the same brown hay every day. Sunny wanted something different. Something exciting.

She took one tiny bite. The hay tasted dry and plain. Sunny sighed a big horse sigh. Her tummy rumbled, but she didn't feel hungry anymore.

A little blue butterfly landed on Sunny's nose. "Why so sad?" asked the butterfly in a tiny voice.

Sunny's eyes grew wide. "You can talk!"

"Of course I can talk," giggled the butterfly. "I'm Bella. Why aren't you eating your lunch?"

"It's boring," said Sunny. "Same hay every day. I wish lunch could be fun."

Bella's wings sparkled in the sunlight. "Maybe it can be! Close your eyes and take another bite."

Sunny felt silly. But she closed her eyes anyway. She took a big bite of hay. Suddenly, something amazing happened!

The hay tasted like sweet apples! Sunny opened her eyes wide. The hay still looked the same. But it tasted wonderful!

"How did you do that?" asked Sunny.

"I sprinkled some butterfly magic," said Bella. "But the real magic is using your imagination!"

Sunny took another bite. This time she imagined carrots. The hay tasted orange and crunchy! She giggled with her mouth full.

"Try again!" said Bella, clapping her tiny hands.

Sunny closed her eyes tight. She thought about fresh grass after rain. The hay tasted green and sweet. She could almost feel dewdrops on her tongue!

"This is amazing!" said Sunny. She took bigger bites now. Each bite was a new adventure. Sometimes it tasted like sugar cubes. Sometimes like fresh oats. Sometimes like juicy pears.

Other animals came to watch. Molly the cow mooed with excitement. "What's happening, Sunny?"

"I'm having a magic lunch!" said Sunny. "Want to try?"

Soon all the animals were using their imagination. Molly pretended her grain was chocolate cake. The chickens imagined their seeds were tiny cookies. Even grumpy old Goat Gordon smiled as he ate.

Farmer Mae watched from the fence. She smiled big. She didn't know about the magic. But she could see something wonderful was happening.

Sunny finished every bit of her hay. Her tummy felt full and happy. Her heart felt warm too.

"Thank you, Bella," said Sunny. "You made lunch special."

"You made it special," said Bella. "I just reminded you how."

The little butterfly flew up high. Her wings caught the sunlight like tiny mirrors. "Remember, Sunny. Magic is always inside you. You just have to believe!"

Bella flew away over the farm. But her words stayed with Sunny.

That evening, Sunny felt excited. Tomorrow there would be another lunch. Another chance for magic. Another chance to use her imagination.

She looked at the stars twinkling above. Each star looked like a tiny Bella. Sunny made a wish on the brightest star.

"Thank you for teaching me," she whispered. "Tomorrow I'll help other animals find their magic too."

The next morning came quickly. Sunny woke up happy. She couldn't wait for lunchtime. She had so many new flavors to imagine. So many friends to share the magic with.

From that day on, lunch was never boring again. Sunny learned that the best ingredient wasn't in her food bowl. It was in her heart. And imagination made everything taste better.

The other animals learned too. Soon the whole farm was full of magical lunches. And magical friends. And one very happy horse named Sunny.

The end.
