# Story story_622

Anna loved her pretty doll named Rosie. Rosie had curly yellow hair and a pink dress. Anna took Rosie everywhere she went.

One sunny morning, Anna and Mommy walked into the green woods. The tall trees made cool shadows on the path. Birds sang sweet songs above them. Anna carried Rosie in her arms.

"Look at the pretty flowers," said Mommy. Purple and white flowers grew by the path. Anna set Rosie down on a soft log. She picked three purple flowers for Mommy.

"Thank you, Anna," said Mommy. "These are beautiful."

They walked deeper into the woods. Anna loved the smell of pine trees. She listened to leaves rustling in the wind. A fuzzy squirrel ran up a big oak tree.

After a long walk, they sat on a wooden bench. Anna reached for Rosie to show her the squirrel. But Rosie was not there!

"Mommy!" Anna cried. "Where is Rosie? I lost my doll!"

Anna's eyes filled with tears. Her tummy felt upset. She looked all around the bench. No Rosie anywhere.

"Don't worry," said Mommy gently. "We will find her. Let's think about where we went."

Anna wiped her eyes. She wanted to find Rosie so much. They started walking back on the path.

Anna looked behind every tree. She looked under green bushes. She looked by the babbling creek. Still no Rosie.

"Maybe someone took her," Anna said sadly. Her voice was very quiet.

"Keep looking," said Mommy. "Don't give up yet."

They walked and walked. Anna's legs felt tired. But she kept searching for her special doll.

Then Anna remembered something important. "Mommy! I put Rosie on a log when I picked flowers!"

"Good thinking," said Mommy. "Let's go back to that spot."

They hurried to where the purple flowers grew. Anna looked at every log and rock. She pushed aside tall grass with her hands.

"I don't see her," Anna said. Her bottom lip started to shake.

"Try looking more carefully," said Mommy. "Sometimes things hide in surprising places."

Anna took a deep breath. She would not give up. She looked behind a fallen log. She checked under some big leaves. Then she saw something pink behind a thorny bush!

"Rosie!" Anna shouted with joy. "I found you!"

Anna carefully reached through the bush. She pulled out her pretty doll. Rosie's pink dress had some leaves on it. But she was perfectly safe.

Anna hugged Rosie tight against her chest. "I missed you so much," she whispered to her doll.

"You did it!" said Mommy proudly. "You didn't give up. You kept trying until you found her."

Anna smiled the biggest smile. Her heart felt warm and happy. She brushed the leaves off Rosie's dress. She smoothed Rosie's curly yellow hair.

"Next time I will hold you tight," Anna told Rosie. "I won't put you down anymore."

They walked home through the sunny woods. Anna carried Rosie safely in her arms. The birds still sang their pretty songs. The flowers still smelled sweet.

"I'm proud of you," said Mommy. "When something was hard, you kept trying. That's how we solve problems."

Anna nodded. She felt very proud too. She had been scared when Rosie was lost. But she didn't give up. She thought hard and kept looking.

At home, Anna gave Rosie a pretend tea party. She told Rosie all about their adventure in the woods. Rosie seemed happy to be home too.

That night, Anna put Rosie safely on her pillow. "Tomorrow we can play again," she said softly. "But I will always keep you close."

Anna learned something important that day. When things seem hard or scary, she should keep trying. Good things happen when you don't give up. Anna fell asleep holding her pretty doll, dreaming of their next adventure together.

The end.
