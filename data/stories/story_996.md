# Story story_996

Sam sat on his bedroom floor. He felt very lonely today. His toys were scattered around him. But playing alone wasn't fun anymore.

The closet door creaked softly. Sam looked up with curious eyes. A small brown monkey peeked out. The monkey had bright golden eyes. His fur looked soft and fuzzy.

"Hello there," Sam whispered gently. The monkey tilted his little head. He stepped out slowly. His tiny feet made no sound.

Sam had never seen this monkey before. "Where did you come from?" he asked. The monkey pointed to the dark closet. Then he looked at Sam hopefully.

The monkey saw Sam's scattered toys. Building blocks were everywhere. Toy cars sat in a messy pile. The monkey's eyes grew wide with excitement.

"Do you want to play?" Sam asked. The monkey nodded his head quickly. He clapped his small hands together. Sam smiled for the first time today.

The monkey picked up a red block. He handed it to Sam carefully. Sam took the block and smiled bigger. "Thank you, little friend," he said softly.

Together they started building a tower. Sam placed blocks on top. The monkey helped steady each piece. The tower grew taller and taller.

"This is the best tower ever!" Sam laughed. The monkey chattered happily in response. His tail swished back and forth.

The tower wobbled a little bit. Both friends held their breath. But it stayed standing tall. They cheered together with joy.

Next, they played with the toy cars. The monkey made funny driving sounds. "Vroom, vroom!" he chattered loudly. Sam giggled at the silly noises.

They raced cars across the carpet. The monkey's car was bright blue. Sam's car was shiny green. The cars zoomed everywhere together.

"Your car is super fast!" Sam said. The monkey puffed out his chest proudly. He made his car do loops.

Then they played hide and seek. The monkey hid behind the curtains. His tail stuck out just slightly. Sam pretended not to see him.

"Where could my friend be?" Sam wondered. The monkey giggled from his spot. Sam walked around looking everywhere.

"Found you!" Sam called out happily. The monkey jumped out with glee. They both laughed until their bellies hurt.

Now it was Sam's turn. He hid under his soft blanket. The monkey searched high and low. He looked in every corner.

The monkey found Sam quickly too. "You're really good at this!" Sam said. The monkey did a little dance.

As the sun began to set, they sat together. The room felt warm and cozy. Sam's loneliness had completely disappeared now.

"I'm so glad you came out," Sam told his friend. The monkey snuggled close to Sam. His fur felt incredibly soft.

"Will you stay and play tomorrow?" Sam asked hopefully. The monkey nodded his head eagerly. He pointed to the closet door.

"You can live there anytime," Sam said. The monkey clapped his hands again. His golden eyes sparkled with happiness.

They cleaned up all the toys. Working together made it easy. Every block found its place. Every car was put away.

The monkey helped fold Sam's blanket. They made Sam's bed together too. Everything looked neat and tidy.

"Thank you for being my friend," Sam whispered. The monkey hugged Sam's arm gently. It felt like the best hug.

Sam wasn't lonely anymore at all. He had found a wonderful friend. The monkey had found someone too.

They said goodnight to each other. The monkey went to the closet. Sam climbed into his warm bed.

"See you tomorrow, buddy," Sam called. The monkey waved from the doorway. Then he disappeared inside quietly.

Sam fell asleep with a smile. He dreamed of more adventures ahead. Tomorrow would bring new games. Tomorrow would bring more laughter.

The closet door stayed slightly open. Just in case his friend needed anything. Sam had learned something very important. The best friendships can come unexpectedly. Sometimes they're hiding right nearby.

The end.
