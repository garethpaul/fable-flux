# Zuri's Missing Blocks

Zuri the zebra loved playing in his garden. The soft green grass tickled his hooves. Colorful flowers danced in the warm breeze. Birds chirped happy songs in the tall trees.

Every morning, Zuri played with his special blocks. Red ones. Blue ones. Yellow ones too. He built tall towers and long bridges. He made castles and cars.

But this morning was different. Zuri looked under the big sunflower. No blocks there. He peeked behind the rose bush. Nothing at all.

"Where are my blocks?" Zuri asked sadly. His striped ears drooped down. His tail swished back and forth.

Zuri's mama heard him calling. She trotted over on gentle hooves. "What's wrong, little one?" she asked softly.

"I can't find my blocks anywhere!" Zuri sniffled. "I looked and looked. They're gone!"

Mama nuzzled Zuri's neck. "Let's think together," she said. "Where did you play yesterday?"

Zuri's eyes grew wide. "By the pond!" he remembered. "I built a boat there!"

Off they went together. Their hooves made soft sounds on the dirt path. Zuri's heart beat fast with hope.

At the pond, they looked everywhere. Under the lily pads. Behind the smooth rocks. Near the splashing water. But no blocks appeared.

"They're not here either," Zuri sighed. His shoulders slumped down low.

"Don't give up," Mama said gently. "Good detectives keep looking. Where else did you play?"

Zuri thought hard. He tapped his hoof. "The vegetable garden!" he shouted. "I made a fence for the carrots!"

They hurried to the vegetable patch. Green leaves rustled in the wind. Fat tomatoes hung like red balloons. But still no blocks anywhere.

Zuri sat down heavily. "I'll never find them," he whispered. Tears made his eyes shiny.

"Wait," Mama said. "I hear something." She tilted her head. "Do you hear that sound?"

Zuri listened carefully. Tap, tap, tap. Something was making noise. The sound came from the apple tree.

"Let's look up there," Mama suggested.

They walked to the big tree. Its branches reached high into the sky. Green apples hung like ornaments. And there, in a cozy nest, sat Rosie Robin.

Tap, tap, tap. Rosie was building something special. She used twigs and leaves. And Zuri's colorful blocks too!

"Rosie!" Zuri called up. "Those are my blocks!"

Rosie peeked over the nest. "Oh my!" she chirped. "I found these pretty things. I thought they were gifts!"

"I need them for playing," Zuri explained kindly. "But your nest looks beautiful."

Rosie thought for a moment. "I can use twigs instead," she decided. "Twigs work better anyway."

Carefully, Rosie dropped each block down. Zuri caught them in his hooves. Red block. Blue block. Yellow block. All safe and sound.

"Thank you, Rosie!" Zuri called happily. His tail swished with joy. His stripes seemed to sparkle.

"You're welcome!" Rosie sang. "Thanks for being so nice!"

Zuri and Mama walked back home. Zuri carried his blocks carefully. The garden smelled like flowers and sunshine.

"I'm proud of you," Mama said. "You didn't give up. You kept looking until you found them."

Zuri beamed with pride. "I was a good detective!" he said. "I thought hard. I looked everywhere. I asked for help too."

Back in their favorite spot, Zuri began to build. First, a tall tower. Then, a bridge for ants to cross. His blocks felt wonderful in his hooves.

From the apple tree came Rosie's cheerful song. She had finished her nest with twigs and soft moss. Everyone was happy.

Zuri learned something important that day. When you lose something special, don't give up. Think carefully. Look everywhere. Ask friends for help. Keep trying, and you might find exactly what you need.

The sun shone warmly on Zuri's stripes. His blocks sparkled in the light. The garden was full of joy and friendship. It was the perfect day for building and playing.

And Zuri never forgot where he put his blocks again.

The end.
