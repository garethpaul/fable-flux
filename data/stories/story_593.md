# Story story_593

**Truck's Art Time Adventure**

The big yellow school bell rang loudly. "Art time!" called Miss Luna with a bright smile. All the toy trucks lined up by the art table. Red truck zoomed forward first. Blue truck followed close behind.

But Big Truck stayed in the corner. His wheels felt heavy and slow. "I don't like art time," he rumbled quietly. "My wheels are too big. My body is too bumpy. I can't make pretty pictures."

Miss Luna walked over to Big Truck. Her shoes made soft tapping sounds on the floor. "What's wrong, Big Truck?" she asked gently. Her voice was warm like sunshine.

"Art is too hard for me," Big Truck said sadly. "My friends make beautiful drawings. I just make messy marks." He looked at the colorful paints and brushes. They seemed scary and strange.

"Let's try something new today," Miss Luna said. She picked up a big, soft sponge. "This might be perfect for you." The sponge felt bouncy in her hands. It smelled clean and fresh.

Big Truck rolled slowly to the art table. The other trucks were already painting. Red truck made neat little dots. Blue truck painted smooth, straight lines. Their pictures looked perfect and tidy.

Miss Luna dipped the sponge in purple paint. "Roll your wheels through this," she said. The paint felt cool and squishy. Big Truck's big wheels picked up the purple paint easily.

"Now roll across the paper," Miss Luna encouraged. Big Truck rolled forward carefully. His wheels left thick, purple tracks on the white paper. The tracks looked strong and bold.

"That's interesting!" said Red truck, looking over. "Those tracks are really cool!" Blue truck nodded and smiled. "They look like real roads!"

Big Truck felt a tiny spark of excitement. Maybe this wasn't so scary after all. Miss Luna dipped the sponge in yellow paint. "Try this color too," she suggested.

Big Truck rolled through the yellow paint. It made a happy, sunny sound as it squished. Then he rolled across his paper again. The yellow tracks crossed over the purple ones. They made a beautiful pattern.

"It looks like sunshine roads!" giggled a small toy car nearby. "Can you make more?" Big Truck's engine hummed with happiness. He was starting to have fun.

Miss Luna brought out blue paint next. Then green paint. Then orange paint. Big Truck rolled through each color. His paper filled with colorful wheel tracks. They twisted and turned like a rainbow highway.

"This is amazing!" said Blue truck. "It looks like a magical road map!" All the other trucks gathered around to look. Their wheels made excited clicking sounds on the floor.

Big Truck stepped back to look at his artwork. The wheel tracks swirled and danced across the paper. Some were thick and bold. Some were thin and delicate. All the colors mixed together beautifully.

"I made this?" Big Truck asked in wonder. His voice was full of surprise and joy. The painting was unlike anything he had ever seen. It was uniquely his own creation.

"You certainly did," Miss Luna said proudly. "Your wheels make the most wonderful paintbrushes." She hung Big Truck's colorful track painting on the wall next to all the others.

Big Truck looked at his artwork hanging up high. The colors seemed to glow in the classroom light. Other trucks stopped to admire his wheel-track masterpiece. He felt proud and happy inside.

"That was really fun," Big Truck said to Miss Luna. "I didn't know my wheels could make such pretty pictures." His whole body felt lighter and more cheerful.

As cleanup time began, Big Truck helped put away the art supplies. The sponges felt soft in his scoop. The paint containers made gentle clinking sounds as he moved them carefully.

"Will we have art time again tomorrow?" Big Truck asked hopefully. His wheels were already excited about trying new colors and patterns.

"Every day," Miss Luna promised with a warm smile. "And I can't wait to see what beautiful roads you'll create next."

Big Truck rolled home that day feeling very happy. Tomorrow's art time couldn't come soon enough.

The end.
