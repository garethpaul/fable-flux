# Story story_1342

Ben loved going to the library. She loved the smell of books. She loved the soft carpet in the reading corner. Today, Ben wore her new sneakers with long white laces.

Ben walked through the tall glass doors. The library was quiet and cozy. Sunlight streamed through the big windows. Ben headed straight to the picture book section.

But something felt funny. Ben looked down at her feet. Oh no! Her shoelaces were untied. They dragged on the floor like wiggly snakes.

"I need to tie my shoes," Ben whispered to herself.

Ben sat down on the soft blue carpet. She picked up one lace in each hand. The laces felt smooth and slippery. Ben tried to remember what Mama showed her.

"Loop, around, and through," Ben said quietly.

She made a loop with one lace. She wrapped the other lace around it. But when she tried to pull it through, everything came apart. The laces fell loose again.

Ben frowned. Her cheeks felt warm. Other kids walked by with perfectly tied shoes. Ben felt embarrassed.

Mrs. Chen, the librarian, noticed Ben sitting on the floor. She had kind eyes and a gentle smile.

"Are you okay, sweetheart?" Mrs. Chen asked softly.

"My shoes won't stay tied," Ben said. Her voice was very quiet.

Mrs. Chen sat down next to Ben. "Learning to tie shoes is tricky. Would you like some help?"

Ben nodded. She really wanted to learn.

"Let's try the bunny ear way," Mrs. Chen said. "It's easier than loops."

Mrs. Chen showed Ben how to make two loops. "These are bunny ears," she explained. "Now we tie the bunny ears together."

Ben watched carefully. Mrs. Chen's hands moved slowly. Ben could see every step.

"Now you try," Mrs. Chen said encouragingly.

Ben picked up her laces again. She made one bunny ear. Then she made another bunny ear. The loops looked a little wonky, but they stayed.

"Good job!" Mrs. Chen whispered. "Now cross the bunny ears."

Ben crossed the loops. She felt more confident now.

"Pull one ear through the hole," Mrs. Chen guided.

Ben carefully pushed one loop through. She pulled both ears tight. The bow looked perfect!

"I did it!" Ben said, but not too loudly. She remembered they were in the library.

"You sure did," Mrs. Chen smiled. "Now try the other shoe."

Ben's second shoe was even easier. Her fingers remembered what to do. Loop, cross, through, and pull. Another perfect bow!

Ben stood up and looked at her feet. Both shoes were tied tight. The white laces made neat little bows. Ben felt proud and happy.

"Thank you, Mrs. Chen," Ben said. "You're a great teacher."

"You're a great learner," Mrs. Chen replied. "You didn't give up."

Ben walked to the picture book section. Her steps felt different now. Her shoes stayed on tight. She didn't trip over loose laces.

Ben picked out three books about animals. She sat in the cozy reading corner. The afternoon sun made the pages glow. Ben read about elephants and butterflies and friendly bears.

When it was time to leave, Ben looked at her shoes. The bows were still perfect. She had tied them all by herself.

Ben walked to the checkout desk. Mrs. Chen stamped her books.

"See you next week," Mrs. Chen said with a warm smile.

"See you next week," Ben replied. "And my shoes will be tied!"

Ben walked through the glass doors. The warm sunshine felt good on her face. She had learned something new today. She had asked for help when she needed it. She had kept trying until she succeeded.

Ben skipped down the sidewalk. Her tied shoes made soft tapping sounds. She couldn't wait to show Mama her perfect bows.

Some things are hard at first. But with practice and help, Ben could do anything. Even tie her shoes at the library.

The end.
