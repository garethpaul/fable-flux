# Story story_977

**Bella Bird's Magical Bedtime**

The sun was setting behind the tall oak tree. Orange and pink colors painted the sky. Mama Robin called from the cozy nest.

"Bella, it's bedtime now!"

Little Bella Bird puffed up her fluffy feathers. She did not want to sleep. The evening air felt so nice and cool. Fireflies were starting to twinkle below.

"But Mama, I'm not tired yet," Bella chirped softly. "Can I fly just a little more?"

Mama Robin smiled with her bright eyes. "Come here, my little one. Let me tuck you in."

Bella slowly hopped to the nest. Her tiny claws gripped the soft twigs. The nest smelled like fresh leaves and morning dew. But she still felt wide awake.

"I don't want to close my eyes," Bella whispered. "What if I miss something fun?"

Mama Robin gently smoothed Bella's downy feathers. "Sometimes the most magical things happen when we rest," she said warmly.

Bella curled up in the nest. She pulled a soft dandelion fluff over herself like a blanket. The fluffy seeds tickled her beak. She giggled quietly.

"Close your eyes, little bird," Mama said. "Listen to the night sounds."

Bella tried to close her eyes. But then something wonderful happened. Tiny sparkles of light began dancing around the tree. They looked like floating stars!

"Mama, look!" Bella chirped excitedly. "What are those pretty lights?"

"Those are dream sprites," Mama explained. "They only come out when little birds are ready for sleep."

The dream sprites twirled and spun. They made soft tinkling sounds like tiny bells. Their light was gentle and warm. Some sprites were blue like the morning sky. Others glowed yellow like sunshine.

One little sprite floated close to Bella. It had wings like butterfly petals. The sprite whispered in a voice like wind chimes.

"Hello, Bella Bird. We bring sweet dreams to sleepy birds."

Bella's eyes grew wide with wonder. "You know my name?"

"We know all the good little birds," the sprite giggled. "Would you like to see a dream preview?"

Bella nodded eagerly. The sprite touched her forehead with a tiny, glowing finger. Suddenly, Bella could see amazing pictures in her mind.

She saw herself flying through cotton candy clouds. She was racing with friendly butterflies. She found a tree made entirely of seeds and berries. Everything looked so colorful and fun!

"That's what you'll dream about tonight," the sprite said kindly. "But only if you close your eyes and sleep."

Bella felt her eyelids getting heavy. The dream pictures looked so exciting. She wanted to visit that magical place right away.

"Will the dreams really come true?" she asked sleepily.

"Dreams always come true when you believe," Mama Robin said softly. "And tomorrow you'll have new adventures when you wake up."

The dream sprites began their gentle lullaby. It sounded like a babbling brook mixed with soft rain. Bella felt warm and safe in her cozy nest.

Her eyes fluttered closed. The last thing she saw was the sprites waving goodbye. They promised to guard her dreams all night long.

"Good night, little Bella," Mama whispered. "Sweet dreams, my precious bird."

Bella snuggled deeper into her nest. The night air felt perfect now. Cool and calm. She could hear crickets singing their sleepy songs. An owl hooted gently in the distance.

As she drifted off to sleep, Bella smiled. She couldn't wait to fly through those cotton candy clouds. Tomorrow would bring new adventures too. Maybe she would learn a new song. Or discover a field full of juicy worms.

But right now, sleep felt wonderful. The dream sprites kept their promise. Bella flew through the most amazing dreamland ever. She played with rainbow-colored fish in sky puddles. She raced shooting stars across the night.

When morning came, Bella woke up happy and excited. She had loved her magical dreams. And she was ready for another day of real adventures.

"Good morning, Mama!" she chirped cheerfully. "I can't wait for bedtime tonight!"

The end.
