# Story story_912

**The Lonely Ball and Anna**

In a sunny backyard sandbox sat a bright red ball. The ball was perfectly round and shiny. But the ball felt very sad and lonely.

Every day, the ball watched children play nearby. They ran and laughed together. They shared toys and told stories. The ball wished someone would play with him too.

"I want a friend," whispered the ball softly. The warm sand felt good beneath him. But playing alone was no fun at all.

The ball tried rolling around the sandbox. He made little trails in the soft sand. The sand tickled his smooth surface. Still, he felt lonely without a friend to share the fun.

One morning, a little girl named Anna walked into the backyard. She had curly brown hair and bright blue eyes. Anna wore her favorite yellow dress with tiny flowers on it.

Anna saw the red ball sitting alone. The ball looked so sad and still. Anna's kind heart felt sorry for the lonely ball.

"Hello there, little ball," Anna said gently. Her voice was sweet and caring. "You look like you need a friend."

The ball's heart jumped with joy! Someone finally noticed him. Someone wanted to talk to him. Maybe this was his chance for friendship.

Anna sat down in the warm sand. She picked up the ball carefully. The ball felt Anna's gentle hands. Her touch was soft and kind.

"Would you like to play with me?" asked Anna. She smiled her biggest, brightest smile. The ball felt warm happiness spreading through him.

Anna rolled the ball across the sandbox. The ball loved moving through the soft sand. Tiny grains of sand stuck to his red surface. It felt like getting a gentle massage.

"This is so much fun!" laughed Anna. Her giggles sounded like tiny silver bells. The ball bounced with excitement and joy.

They played catch together. Anna tossed the ball up high. The ball flew through the cool morning air. Then Anna caught him safely in her arms.

"You're a wonderful ball," Anna told him. The ball glowed with pride and happiness. No one had ever called him wonderful before.

Next, they played rolling games. Anna pushed the ball around the sandbox. The ball made curvy paths in the sand. Together they drew pictures and shapes.

Anna built small sand hills. The ball rolled over them carefully. The sand felt bumpy and fun under him. Anna clapped her hands with delight.

"We make a great team!" said Anna happily. The ball agreed completely. Playing together was much better than playing alone.

They created a sand castle together. Anna shaped the walls with her hands. The ball helped pack the sand down tight. The castle looked beautiful in the sunshine.

Anna decorated their castle with leaves and flowers. The ball stayed close by her side. He loved being part of their special project.

As the sun grew warmer, they rested together. Anna held the ball in her lap. She told him stories about faraway places. The ball listened to every word.

"I'm so glad I found you," Anna whispered. She hugged the ball gently. The ball felt the warmest, happiest feeling ever.

From that day on, Anna and the ball were best friends. Every morning, Anna came to the sandbox. The ball waited excitedly for her arrival.

They played new games every day. Sometimes they rolled around together. Other times they built amazing sand creations. They always had fun together.

The ball never felt lonely anymore. Anna had given him the greatest gift of all. She had given him friendship and love.

Anna learned something special too. She discovered that being kind to others felt wonderful. Helping someone who was lonely made her heart happy.

Now the sandbox was filled with laughter every day. Anna and her red ball friend played until sunset. Their friendship grew stronger with each passing day.

The ball finally had what he had always wanted. He had a true friend who cared about him. And Anna had found a loyal friend too.

Together, they proved that friendship makes everything better. The lonely ball was lonely no more.

The end.
