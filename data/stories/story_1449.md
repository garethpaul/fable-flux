# Story story_1449

Max sat in the sunny meadow. The grass felt soft under his feet. He picked up a stick and threw it far away. But no one was there to play with him.

Max sighed and looked around. The meadow was big and green. Pretty flowers grew everywhere. Bees buzzed from flower to flower. But Max felt lonely.

"I wish I had a friend," Max said softly.

Just then, he heard a sound. Oink! Oink! A small pink pig walked out from behind a tree. The pig had a curly tail and bright eyes.

The pig looked at Max. Max looked at the pig. They stared at each other for a long moment.

"Hello," said Max. "I'm Max."

"Oink!" said the pig happily. The pig wagged his curly tail.

Max smiled. "Do you want to play with me?"

The pig nodded his head up and down. He ran in a little circle. His hooves made soft sounds on the grass.

"What should we play?" asked Max.

The pig picked up Max's stick with his mouth. He brought it back to Max. His tail wiggled with joy.

"Oh! You want to play fetch!" said Max.

Max threw the stick again. The pig ran fast across the meadow. His little legs moved quickly. He brought the stick back to Max.

They played fetch many times. Max laughed as the pig ran. The pig made happy oinking sounds.

"You're very fast!" said Max. "And very smart!"

The pig rolled on his back in the soft grass. Max giggled and sat down next to him. The warm sun felt good on their faces.

"I know another game," said Max. "Follow the leader!"

Max stood up and marched around the meadow. The pig followed behind him. Step, step, step went Max. Oink, oink, oink went the pig.

Max hopped on one foot. The pig tried to hop too. He looked funny hopping on his little legs. Max laughed and clapped.

Then the pig wanted to be the leader. He walked to a mud puddle. The pig rolled in the cool, squishy mud. It felt good on his pink skin.

"Should I roll in the mud too?" asked Max.

The pig nodded. Max sat down carefully in the mud. It was cool and soft. It squished between his fingers.

"This feels nice!" said Max. "But I'm getting very dirty!"

The pig showed Max a small stream nearby. The water was clear and cold. They washed off the mud together. The water felt refreshing.

Max splashed gently in the stream. The pig drank some water. Then they both lay down in the warm grass to dry off.

"I was lonely before," said Max. "But now I'm happy."

The pig curled up next to Max. His warm body felt cozy. Max petted the pig's soft ears.

"Will you be my friend?" asked Max.

"Oink!" said the pig. He licked Max's hand with his wet tongue.

Max giggled. "I think that means yes!"

They heard Max's mom calling from far away. "Max! Time for lunch!"

"I have to go home now," said Max sadly. "But will you come back tomorrow?"

The pig nodded his head. He touched Max's hand with his snout.

"I'll bring some apples," said Max. "Do you like apples?"

The pig's eyes got very bright. He ooinked loudly and jumped up and down.

Max hugged his new friend goodbye. The pig's fur was warm and soft. He smelled like grass and sunshine.

"See you tomorrow, friend!" said Max.

Max ran home across the meadow. He felt so happy inside. The sun was shining. The flowers smelled sweet. And best of all, he had made a wonderful friend.

The pig watched Max go. Then he rolled happily in the grass. Tomorrow would be another fun day in the meadow.

Max told his mom all about his new pig friend. She smiled and helped him pick out the best apples to share.

"Friends make everything better," said Mom.

Max nodded and smiled big. He couldn't wait for tomorrow.

The end.
