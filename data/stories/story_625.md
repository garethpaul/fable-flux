# Story story_625

**Finn's Bright Idea**

Finn was a little goldfish who lived in a big glass bowl. His bowl sat on a wooden table in Lucy's bedroom. During the day, sunshine came through the window. The water sparkled like tiny diamonds. Finn loved swimming in the bright, warm light.

But when night came, everything changed. The room became very dark. Finn's bowl felt cold and scary. He could not see his favorite plastic castle. He could not see his colorful pebbles. The darkness made him shake his little fins.

"I don't like the dark," Finn whispered to himself. "It makes me feel so scared."

Every night, Finn would hide behind his castle. He would close his eyes tight. But he still felt frightened. The shadows on the wall looked like big monsters. Strange sounds came from outside the window. Finn's heart beat very fast.

One morning, Lucy noticed something was wrong. "Why do you look so tired, Finn?" she asked. "Are you feeling okay?"

Finn swam in slow, sad circles. He wanted to tell Lucy about his fear. But fish cannot talk to people. So he just floated near the bottom of his bowl.

That night, Finn decided to be brave. "I will try to fix this problem," he said. He had an idea. Maybe if he swam very fast, he could make his own light. Finn swam around and around his bowl. He moved his fins as quickly as possible. He hoped the water would glow.

But nothing happened. The bowl stayed dark. Finn got very tired from all that swimming. His fins hurt. His idea did not work. He felt even more scared than before.

"That didn't help at all," Finn sighed. He sank to the bottom of his bowl. A tear rolled down his little fish face.

The next day, Finn watched Lucy very carefully. She turned on a small lamp when it got dark. The lamp made a soft, yellow glow. It was not too bright. It was not too dim. It was just right.

"That's it!" Finn thought. "I need my own gentle light."

Finn looked around his bowl. He saw his favorite toy - a small, clear marble. Lucy had put it there last week. The marble was smooth and round. When sunlight hit it during the day, it made pretty rainbow colors.

That evening, Finn had a new plan. As the room grew dark, he pushed the marble with his nose. He moved it to the very top of his bowl. The marble caught the tiny bit of light from the hallway. It began to glow softly.

The gentle light spread through the water. Finn could see his castle again! He could see his colorful pebbles. The scary shadows were gone. Everything looked peaceful and safe.

"This is wonderful!" Finn said happily. He swam a little victory dance around his bowl. The marble light made everything look magical. The water shimmered with soft, silvery sparkles.

From that night on, Finn was never scared of the dark. He would push his special marble to the top of his bowl every evening. The gentle glow helped him feel safe and calm. He could sleep peacefully all night long.

Lucy noticed that Finn looked much happier. "You seem to love that marble," she said with a smile. "It makes your bowl look so pretty at night."

Finn did a happy flip in the water. He had learned something very important. When you have a problem, don't give up after one try. Keep thinking of new ideas. Keep trying different solutions. Sometimes the best answer is right in front of you.

Now Finn loved both day and night. The sunshine was bright and warm. The marble light was soft and cozy. His bedroom home was perfect all the time.

"I'm not scared anymore," Finn whispered as he drifted off to sleep. "I found my own special light."

And in his dreams, Finn swam through waters that sparkled like stars.

The end.
