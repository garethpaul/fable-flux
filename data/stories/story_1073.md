# Story story_1073

**Tommy and the Magic Breakfast**

The sun peeked through Tommy's bedroom window. Birds chirped their morning songs outside. Mom's voice floated up the stairs.

"Tommy! Time for breakfast!"

Tommy pulled his blanket over his head. He did not want to get up. Breakfast time was boring. He always ate the same things. Toast with jam. A glass of milk. Sometimes cereal.

"Tommy, your brother Jake is already eating," Mom called again.

Tommy sighed. He rolled out of bed slowly. His feet touched the cold floor. He shuffled down the hallway in his pajamas.

In the kitchen, Jake sat at the table. He was eating pancakes with syrup. The sweet smell filled the air. Tommy's tummy rumbled a little.

"Good morning, sleepyhead," Jake said with a grin.

Tommy slumped into his chair. Mom placed a plate in front of him. Two plain pancakes sat there. They looked so ordinary.

"I don't want breakfast," Tommy mumbled. "It's always the same."

Jake looked at his little brother. "Watch this," he whispered. Jake picked up the syrup bottle. He squeezed it in a wiggly line. The golden syrup made a funny snake shape.

"Look! A syrup snake!" Jake said.

Tommy's eyes got bigger. The syrup did look like a snake. Jake added two blueberries for eyes. Now it really looked alive!

"Can I try?" Tommy asked quietly.

"Of course!" Jake said. He handed Tommy the syrup bottle.

Tommy squeezed carefully. The syrup came out in a circle. Then he added lines coming out. "It's a sun!" he said with excitement.

Jake clapped his hands. "That's perfect! Now let's make more."

The boys took turns with the syrup. Jake made a smiley face. Tommy drew a flower. The pancakes became their art canvas.

"What else can we use?" Tommy wondered aloud.

Mom smiled from the counter. She brought over strawberries and bananas. "Try these," she said warmly.

Jake sliced a banana into circles. He made buttons on his pancake person. Tommy used strawberry pieces for flower petals. The red berries looked so pretty.

"This is the best breakfast ever!" Tommy laughed.

The boys ate their creations happily. The pancakes tasted extra delicious. Maybe because they made them special. Maybe because they worked together.

"Tomorrow can we make animals?" Tommy asked between bites.

"We can make anything you want," Jake promised.

Mom watched her boys eating and creating. Her heart felt warm and happy. "You two make a great team," she said.

Tommy finished every bite on his plate. He even drank all his milk. His tummy felt full and satisfied.

"Can we clean up together too?" Tommy asked Jake.

"Sure thing, little brother," Jake replied.

They carried their plates to the sink. Tommy wiped the table with a damp cloth. Jake put the syrup bottle away. Working together made cleaning fun too.

"Thank you for making breakfast special," Tommy told Jake.

Jake gave his brother a big hug. "That's what big brothers do," he said proudly.

Mom hugged both boys tight. "You both made my morning wonderful," she said softly.

That night, Tommy went to bed early. He could hardly wait for morning. His mind was full of breakfast ideas. Maybe they could make a butterfly. Or a rainbow. Or even a castle!

"Jake?" Tommy called across their shared room.

"Yes, Tommy?"

"I love breakfast time now."

Jake smiled in the darkness. "Me too, buddy. Me too."

The next morning, Tommy jumped out of bed. He ran to the kitchen before Mom even called. Jake was already there, waiting with a big smile.

"Ready for another breakfast adventure?" Jake asked.

"Ready!" Tommy said with joy.

From that day on, Tommy never complained about breakfast. Every morning brought new possibilities. Every meal became a chance to create something magical with his brother.

And Mom always made sure there was plenty of syrup for their breakfast art.

The end.
