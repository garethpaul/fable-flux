# Story story_816

Ben sat on the soft blue carpet in the nursery. His toy trucks were lined up in a neat row. The room smelled like crayons and apple juice. But Ben felt lonely.

The other children played together in small groups. They laughed and shared their toys. Ben watched them from his quiet corner. He wished he had a friend to play with too.

Suddenly, a fluffy golden dog trotted into the room. The dog had a red collar with a tiny bell. The bell made soft jingling sounds as he walked. His tail wagged back and forth like a happy flag.

The dog spotted Ben sitting alone. He tilted his furry head to one side. His brown eyes sparkled with kindness. The dog took small steps toward Ben.

"Hello," Ben whispered softly. "Are you lonely too?"

The dog sat down next to Ben. His warm body felt cozy against Ben's arm. The dog's fur was as soft as a cloud. Ben gently petted the dog's head.

"Do you want to play trucks?" Ben asked.

The dog barked once. It sounded like "yes" to Ben. Ben smiled for the first time that day. He picked up his favorite red truck.

"This one goes beep beep," Ben said. He rolled the truck across the carpet. The dog watched with bright, interested eyes.

The dog nudged a blue truck with his nose. The truck rolled toward Ben's feet. Ben giggled at the silly sight.

"You're good at this game," Ben said.

They played together for a long time. Ben made truck sounds while the dog pushed toys around. The dog's tail never stopped wagging. Ben's giggles filled the quiet corner.

Miss Sarah, the nursery teacher, walked over to them. "What a wonderful friendship," she said with a warm smile. "You two make a great team."

Ben felt his cheeks turn pink with happiness. "This is my new friend," he told Miss Sarah. "He likes trucks too."

The dog licked Ben's hand with his pink tongue. It felt warm and tickly. Ben laughed out loud. The sound made other children look over curiously.

"Can we play too?" asked Emma, a girl with pigtails.

Ben looked at his new dog friend. The dog wagged his tail even faster. "Yes," Ben said proudly. "We can all play together."

Soon, four children sat in a circle with the friendly dog. They rolled trucks back and forth. The dog chased the trucks with playful barks. Everyone laughed and clapped their hands.

Ben felt his heart grow warm and full. He wasn't lonely anymore. The dog had helped him make new friends. The nursery room felt brighter and more cheerful.

When snack time came, Ben saved a small piece of his graham cracker. He gave it to the dog as a special thank you. The dog ate it gently from Ben's small hand.

"You're the best friend ever," Ben whispered in the dog's soft ear.

The dog rested his head on Ben's lap. His breathing was slow and peaceful. Ben stroked the dog's golden fur. He felt grateful and happy.

At the end of the day, Ben's mom came to pick him up. "Did you have fun today?" she asked.

"Yes!" Ben said excitedly. "I made a new friend. He's furry and kind and loves trucks."

Ben hugged the dog goodbye. "See you tomorrow," he promised.

The dog's tail wagged one more time. His eyes seemed to say "see you tomorrow too."

Ben skipped to the door holding his mom's hand. He couldn't wait to come back to nursery. He couldn't wait to play with his special friend again.

The nursery felt like the happiest place in the world. Ben had learned that friendship can come from the most wonderful surprises. Sometimes all you need is an open heart and a wagging tail.

The end.
