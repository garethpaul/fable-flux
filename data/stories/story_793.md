# Story story_793

**Zoe and the Too-Many Toys**

Zoe loved library day. She skipped through the big glass doors. Her shoes made soft squeaking sounds on the shiny floor.

"Good morning, Zoe!" called Mrs. Chen, the librarian. Her voice was warm like hot chocolate.

Zoe waved and ran to her favorite spot. The children's corner had bright yellow walls. Soft cushions sat everywhere like fluffy clouds.

But today, something special waited for her. A big wooden box sat in the middle of the reading circle. The box was painted red with blue flowers. It had a sign that said "New Toys!"

Zoe's eyes grew wide. She lifted the heavy lid with both hands. Inside were the most wonderful toys she had ever seen!

There were wooden blocks that smelled like pine trees. Soft stuffed animals with button eyes winked at her. Colorful puzzles showed pictures of butterflies and rainbows. A toy train had wheels that really rolled.

"Oh my!" Zoe whispered. She wanted to play with everything at once.

Zoe reached into the box. She grabbed a purple teddy bear. Then she picked up some blocks. She tried to hold a puzzle too. The train looked so fun that she grabbed it with her pinky finger.

But her arms were getting full. Very full.

"I can carry them all," Zoe said to herself. "I'm a big girl now."

She took one more step toward the reading corner. Suddenly, everything started to slip!

The blocks tumbled down like rain. Clunk! Clunk! Clunk! The teddy bear fell on her toe. The puzzle pieces scattered across the floor like fallen leaves.

"Oh no!" Zoe cried. Her bottom lip started to wobble.

Mrs. Chen heard the noise. She hurried over with gentle footsteps.

"What happened, sweetie?" Mrs. Chen asked. She knelt down next to Zoe.

"I wanted all the toys," Zoe sniffled. "But I dropped them. My arms aren't big enough."

Mrs. Chen smiled kindly. "That's a tricky problem. What do you think we could do?"

Zoe wiped her nose with her sleeve. She looked at all the toys on the floor. Her brain started thinking hard.

"Maybe I need bigger arms?" Zoe said.

Mrs. Chen giggled softly. "Your arms are perfect just the way they are. Can you think of another way?"

Zoe tapped her finger on her chin. She always did that when she was thinking super hard.

Then her face lit up like sunshine. "I know! I can make trips!"

"What a smart idea!" Mrs. Chen clapped her hands.

Zoe picked up just the purple teddy bear and three blocks. Her arms felt much better now. She walked carefully to the reading corner. No dropping this time!

She placed the toys gently on a soft blue cushion. Then she walked back to get more.

This time she chose the puzzle and the toy train. Step by step, she carried them safely to her spot.

"One more trip," Zoe said happily.

She gathered the last few toys. A yellow duck that squeaked when she squeezed it. Some crayons that smelled waxy and new. A book about friendly dragons.

Soon all the toys sat in a neat pile on the cushions. Zoe felt proud and happy. Her chest puffed up like a balloon.

"You solved your problem all by yourself!" Mrs. Chen said. "You didn't give up when it got hard."

Zoe nodded. "Sometimes big problems need little steps."

She sat down in the middle of her toys. First, she hugged the purple teddy bear. Then she started building a tower with the blocks. The train chugged around her cushion mountain.

Other children came to play too. They all shared the wonderful new toys. The reading corner filled with giggles and happy voices.

When it was time to go, Zoe helped put everything back. She made three trips to the toy box, just like before.

"See you next week, Zoe," Mrs. Chen called.

Zoe skipped toward the door. She had learned something important today. Big dreams just need small steps to make them come true.

And that made her smile all the way home.

The end.
