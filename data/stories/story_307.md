# Bessie's Lost Book

On Sunny Meadow Farm, there lived a gentle cow named Bessie. She had soft brown spots and kind, warm eyes. Every morning, Bessie loved to sit under her favorite apple tree. She would read her special storybook about faraway places.

The book had bright pictures inside. There were tall mountains and deep blue oceans. There were colorful birds and dancing butterflies. Bessie loved every single page.

One cloudy morning, Bessie walked to her apple tree. She looked left. She looked right. She looked all around. But her special book was nowhere to be found!

"Oh no!" mooed Bessie sadly. "Where could my book be?"

Her friend Rosie the pig came waddling over. "What's wrong, Bessie?" asked Rosie.

"I can't find my special book," said Bessie. "I always keep it right here under the apple tree."

"Don't worry," said Rosie kindly. "We'll help you find it!"

Bessie decided to search everywhere. First, she looked in the red barn. She moved the hay bales carefully. She checked behind the milk buckets. She even looked in the horse stalls. But no book anywhere.

"Maybe it's in the vegetable garden," thought Bessie.

She walked slowly to the garden. The carrots were orange and crunchy. The lettuce was green and leafy. Bessie looked between each row. She searched under the tomato plants. She checked near the pumpkin patch. Still no book.

Charlie the rooster came strutting by. "Cock-a-doodle-doo! What are you looking for, Bessie?"

"My special storybook," said Bessie. "Have you seen it anywhere?"

Charlie flapped his colorful feathers. "I saw some papers flying around yesterday. The wind was very strong!"

Bessie's heart jumped with hope. "Which way did they go?"

Charlie pointed with his wing. "Toward the duck pond!"

Bessie hurried to the pond. The water was calm and peaceful. Ducks were swimming in circles. Bessie walked around the muddy edge. She looked behind the cattails. She checked near the old wooden bridge.

Then she heard a soft quacking sound. It was Dottie the duck.

"Are you looking for something, Bessie?" asked Dottie.

"Yes! My special book with colorful pictures," said Bessie hopefully.

Dottie's eyes lit up. "Oh! I know where that is! Follow me!"

Dottie waddled to some tall grass. There, caught on a low branch, was Bessie's book! The wind had blown it here yesterday. The pages were a little damp but not torn.

"My book!" mooed Bessie joyfully. "You found it!"

Bessie gently took her book down. She wiped the pages with soft grass. The pictures were still bright and beautiful. The mountains still looked tall. The ocean still looked blue.

"Thank you so much, Dottie!" said Bessie.

"I'm so happy we found it," said Dottie. "It looks like a wonderful book."

Bessie carried her book back to the apple tree. Rosie and Charlie were waiting there. They all sat down together in the cool shade.

"Would you like me to read to everyone?" asked Bessie.

"Yes, please!" said all her friends.

Bessie opened her special book. She read about the tall mountains. She read about the deep blue ocean. She read about the colorful birds and dancing butterflies. Her friends listened with big smiles.

"That was beautiful," said Rosie.

"I love the pictures," said Charlie.

"Thank you for sharing," said Dottie.

Bessie felt warm and happy inside. She had found her special book. She had wonderful friends who helped her. She learned that when something seems lost, you should never give up. You should keep looking. You should ask friends for help.

From that day on, Bessie always put her book in a safe place. She kept it in the barn when it was windy. But she still read under the apple tree every sunny morning. And sometimes, her friends joined her for story time.
