# Lily's Magical Art Time

The morning sun peeked through Lily's bedroom window. "Art time!" called Mama from downstairs. Lily heard the familiar words. She pulled her blanket over her head.

"I don't want to," Lily whispered. Yesterday's painting looked messy. The colors had mixed into brown. Her flowers looked like blobs. Art time felt hard today.

Lily walked slowly to the kitchen table. Mama had set out fresh paper. Bright paints waited in little cups. Red, blue, yellow, and green. A clean brush sat beside them.

"What will you make today?" asked Mama. Her voice was gentle and warm.

Lily shrugged her shoulders. "I don't know. My art looks silly."

Mama sat down beside her. "Sometimes the best art starts with just one dot."

Lily picked up the brush. It felt light in her small hand. She dipped it in yellow paint. One tiny dot appeared on the white paper.

"Look," whispered Mama. "Your dot looks like sunshine."

Lily tilted her head. The yellow dot did sparkle. She added another dot. Then another. Soon she had made a line of dancing yellow dots.

"They look happy," Lily said softly. A small smile appeared on her face.

The blue paint called to her next. Lily made gentle swooshes around the yellow dots. The blue looked like sky. Like water. Like her favorite dress.

Something wonderful was happening. The colors seemed to sing together. Yellow and blue swirled and mixed. They made the most beautiful green.

"Magic!" Lily gasped. Her eyes grew wide with wonder.

She tried mixing red and yellow. Orange appeared! Like autumn leaves. Like her cat's fur. Like the sunset.

Lily's brush danced across the paper. Swish, swirl, dot, dash. Each movement felt like flying. The colors bloomed like flowers in spring.

"I'm making a garden!" Lily announced. Her voice bubbled with excitement.

Red roses grew beside blue streams. Yellow butterflies fluttered among green grass. Orange carrots peeked from brown soil.

Mama watched with twinkling eyes. "Your garden is beautiful, Lily."

Lily painted purple violets. Pink cherry blossoms. White clouds floating overhead. Her paper filled with life and color.

The kitchen smelled like paint and creativity. Lily's fingers were rainbow-colored. Her apron had happy splatters. Even her nose had a tiny blue smudge.

"Can I paint another one?" Lily asked. Her heart felt light and bouncy.

"Of course," said Mama. "Art time can last as long as you want."

Lily started a new picture. This time she painted her family. Mama had yellow hair like sunshine. Papa had a big red smile. Their dog had brown spots that looked like cookies.

Time flew by like a magical bird. Lily painted and painted. Each brushstroke felt like a tiny celebration. The colors danced together in new ways.

When lunch time came, Lily looked at her artwork. Five beautiful paintings covered the table. Each one told a different story. Each one sparkled with joy.

"I love art time," Lily said, hugging her paintbrush. "The colors are my friends."

Mama helped clean up the paints. But they left the pictures out to dry. They would hang them on the refrigerator later.

"Tomorrow is another art time," said Mama. "What will you create then?"

Lily's eyes sparkled with ideas. "Maybe a castle! Or a dragon! Or a rainbow pizza!"

She could hardly wait for tomorrow. Art time wasn't scary anymore. It was magical. It was wonderful. It was her favorite time of day.

That night, Lily dreamed in colors. Yellow happiness. Blue peace. Red excitement. Green hope. All the colors swirled together in her dreams.

When morning came, Lily jumped out of bed. "Is it art time yet?" she called to Mama.

Art time had become the best part of her day. And tomorrow would bring even more magic. More colors. More joy. More wonderful surprises waiting in each brushstroke.

Lily smiled as she got dressed. Today would be another beautiful day to create something special.

The end.
