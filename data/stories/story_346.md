# Tilly Tiger's Bath Time Adventure

In a cozy nursery with soft yellow walls and dancing butterfly stickers, lived a little tiger named Tilly. Her orange and black stripes gleamed in the morning sunlight. Tilly loved playing with her toy blocks. She loved reading picture books. She loved snuggling with her favorite teddy bear.

But there was one thing Tilly did NOT love. Bath time.

"Tilly, it's time for your bath!" called Mama Tiger from the bathroom. The sound of running water echoed down the hall. Splash, splash, splash went the warm water into the big white tub.

Tilly's ears drooped. Her tail stopped swishing. "No bath!" she said firmly. "I don't want to get wet!"

Tilly had an idea. She would hide! Quick as lightning, she crawled under her crib. The wooden slats made cool shadows on her striped fur. "Mama won't find me here," she whispered.

"Tilly? Where are you?" Mama's voice came closer. Step, step, step went her paws on the soft carpet.

Mama peeked under the crib. "There you are, little tiger!" she said with a gentle smile. "Why are you hiding?"

"Bath water is too splashy," Tilly said. "And too bubbly. And too... wet!"

Mama Tiger sat down on the nursery floor. "I understand," she said softly. "Sometimes new things feel scary. But I have an idea. What if we make bath time fun?"

Tilly poked her head out. "Fun? How?"

"Come see," Mama said, reaching out her paw.

Together, they walked to the bathroom. The tub was full of warm, sudsy water. White bubbles danced on top like tiny clouds.

"First," said Mama, "let's test the water." She dipped her paw in. "Feel this, Tilly. Is it too hot or too cold?"

Tilly touched the water with one tiny toe. "It's... warm," she said, surprised. "Like my milk!"

"That's right! Now, what if we brought some friends to join you?"

Mama gathered Tilly's rubber duck, her floating boat, and her favorite washcloth with stars on it. One by one, they placed them in the tub.

"Look!" said Mama. "Duck wants to swim. Boat wants to sail. And Star Washcloth wants to play peek-a-boo in the bubbles!"

Tilly giggled. The yellow duck bobbed up and down. The red boat rocked gently. The washcloth disappeared under white, fluffy bubbles.

"Can I touch the bubbles?" Tilly asked.

"Of course!" Mama scooped up a handful. The bubbles felt soft and tickly on Tilly's paw. Pop! Pop! Pop! Some bubbles burst, making tiny snapping sounds.

"They tickle!" Tilly laughed. "Like butterfly kisses!"

Slowly, carefully, Tilly stepped one paw into the warm water. Then another paw. The water felt cozy and comfortable, like a warm hug.

"This isn't scary," Tilly said, settling into the tub. "It's nice!"

Mama poured water gently over Tilly's back. "See? Bath time can be peaceful and fun."

Tilly played with her duck. She sailed her boat through bubble islands. She made the star washcloth dance underwater. The warm water made her muscles feel relaxed and sleepy.

"Mama," Tilly said as soap bubbles crowned her head like a funny hat, "I like bath time now!"

"What changed your mind?" Mama asked, scrubbing behind Tilly's ears.

"I was brave," Tilly said proudly. "I tried something new. And you helped me feel safe."

When bath time was over, Mama wrapped Tilly in a fluffy orange towel. Tilly felt clean and fresh and happy.

"Tomorrow, can we have another bubble bath?" Tilly asked, yawning.

Mama Tiger smiled and kissed Tilly's forehead. "Of course, my brave little tiger. When we face our worries together, we can solve any problem."

That night, Tilly dreamed of sailing boats through bubble oceans, knowing that being brave and trying new things could lead to wonderful adventures.

And from that day forward, bath time became Tilly's favorite time of day.

The end.
