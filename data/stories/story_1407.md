# Story story_1407

Little Duck splashed in the big blue pond. The water felt cool on her yellow feathers. She paddled her orange feet back and forth. Splash, splash, splash went the water.

But Little Duck felt sad. She was all alone. No one was there to play with her. The pond felt very quiet and empty.

"I wish I had a friend," said Little Duck. She looked around the pond. She saw tall green grass. She saw pretty pink flowers. But she did not see any friends.

Little Duck tried to have fun by herself. She dove under the water. Down, down, down she went. The water was dark and cool. She came back up with a pop! Water dripped from her beak.

Then she tried swimming in circles. Round and round she went. The water made little waves. But it was not very fun alone.

From across the pond, Papa Duck was watching. He saw Little Duck playing by herself. He saw her sad face. Papa Duck's heart felt warm and caring.

"Little Duck looks lonely," said Papa Duck. "Maybe I can help her have fun."

Papa Duck swam over slowly. His big feet made gentle splashes. Swoosh, swoosh, swoosh went the water. Little Duck heard the sound and looked up.

"Hello, Little Duck," said Papa Duck with a kind smile. "May I play with you?"

Little Duck's eyes grew bright and happy. "Yes, please!" she said. "I was feeling lonely."

"What would you like to play?" asked Papa Duck.

"Let's play follow the leader!" said Little Duck. She was so excited her tail feathers wiggled.

Little Duck swam to the left. Papa Duck followed behind her. Then she swam to the right. Papa Duck followed again. They made a line of ripples in the water.

"Now I will be the leader," said Papa Duck. He swam under a low tree branch. Little Duck followed. The leaves tickled their backs. It felt funny and nice.

Next, they played hide and seek. Little Duck hid behind the tall cattails. The green plants smelled fresh and earthy. Papa Duck pretended he could not find her.

"Where could Little Duck be?" said Papa Duck loudly. He looked left and right. Little Duck giggled behind the plants.

"Here I am!" she called out. She popped out from the cattails. Papa Duck acted very surprised.

"There you are!" he said. "You are very good at hiding!"

Then Papa Duck hid behind a big rock. The rock was gray and smooth. Little Duck found him right away. They both laughed together. Their happy sounds echoed across the pond.

"Let's race to the other side," said Papa Duck. 

They lined up next to each other. "Ready, set, go!" called Little Duck.

They paddled as fast as they could. Their feet moved like little motors. The water splashed everywhere. Papa Duck let Little Duck win the race.

"You are so fast!" said Papa Duck.

Little Duck felt proud and happy. "That was fun!" she said.

As the sun got lower in the sky, they floated together. The water was calm and peaceful. Little Duck felt warm and safe next to Papa Duck.

"Thank you for playing with me," said Little Duck. "I was sad before you came."

"I love playing with you," said Papa Duck. "Friends make everything more fun."

"Will you be my friend?" asked Little Duck.

"I would love to be your friend," said Papa Duck. "We can play together every day."

Little Duck and Papa Duck smiled at each other. The pond did not feel empty anymore. It felt full of friendship and joy.

They watched the sunset together. The sky turned orange and pink. The water sparkled like tiny diamonds. Everything felt perfect and happy.

From that day on, Little Duck and Papa Duck played together every single day. They never felt lonely again. The pond became their special place of friendship.

And they lived happily ever after.

The end.
