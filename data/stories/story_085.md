# Whiskers Learns to Share

Whiskers was a fluffy orange cat with bright green eyes. He loved playing in Sunny Park every morning. The grass felt soft under his paws. The warm sun made his fur sparkle.

Today, Whiskers brought his favorite red ball. It bounced perfectly and made a happy *boing* sound. He rolled it with his nose. He batted it with his paws. "This is the best ball ever!" he purred.

Soon, three little mice came running over. Their tiny feet pattered on the ground. "Can we play too?" asked Luna, the smallest mouse. Her whiskers twitched with excitement.

Whiskers hugged his ball tight. "No way! This is MY ball." He turned his back to the mice. His tail swished back and forth.

The mice looked sad. Their ears drooped down. They walked away slowly, making tiny squeaking sounds.

Whiskers kept playing alone. But something felt wrong. The ball didn't seem as fun anymore. His purrs got quieter and quieter.

He watched the mice sitting under a big oak tree. They were trying to play with an old pinecone. It kept breaking apart. The pieces scattered everywhere.

"Hmm," thought Whiskers. "Maybe I could try something different."

First, he rolled his ball halfway to the mice. Then he stopped. "What if they break it?" he worried. He rolled it back to himself.

Next, he tried throwing the ball very high. The mice watched it bounce. Their eyes grew wide with wonder. But Whiskers caught it quickly. "Mine!" he called out.

The mice sighed. They went back to their broken pinecone.

Whiskers felt a funny feeling in his tummy. It wasn't hunger. It was something else. Something sad and empty.

He looked at his shiny red ball. Then he looked at the lonely mice. The ball felt heavy in his paws.

"Wait!" Whiskers called out. His voice echoed across the park. The mice turned around with hopeful faces.

Whiskers took a deep breath. "Would you like to play catch?" His heart beat fast like a tiny drum.

Luna's eyes lit up like stars. "Really? You mean it?"

"Yes," said Whiskers. "But we have to be gentle." He rolled the ball softly toward them.

The mice cheered! They made happy squeaking sounds. Luna caught the ball with her tiny paws. It was almost as big as she was!

"My turn!" squeaked Max, the middle mouse. Luna rolled it to him carefully.

Then Benny, the biggest mouse, rolled it back to Whiskers. "Thank you for sharing!" he said.

They played catch in a circle. The ball bounced from paws to paws. *Boing, boing, boing!* Everyone giggled and laughed.

"This is so much fun!" purred Whiskers. His green eyes sparkled brighter than before.

They played different games too. They rolled the ball through tunnels made of leaves. They played gentle soccer on the soft grass. They even played hide-and-seek with the ball.

When the sun got higher, they all sat in the shade. The cool grass felt nice and soft. Whiskers' ball sat in the middle of their circle.

"You're a good friend, Whiskers," said Luna. She patted his paw with her tiny one.

Whiskers felt warm and happy inside. Much happier than when he played alone. "You're all good friends too," he purred loudly.

"Can we play again tomorrow?" asked Max.

"Of course!" said Whiskers. "Sharing makes everything more fun!"

The mice did little happy dances. Their tails wiggled with joy.

As they said goodbye, Whiskers realized something important. His ball was still perfect and red and bouncy. But now it was also magical. It had helped him make three wonderful friends.

Walking home through the park, Whiskers purred a happy song. The birds chirped along from the trees. His paws felt light on the warm path.

Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough. He had friends to play with and a ball to share. And that made him the luckiest cat in the whole wide world.

Sharing, Whiskers learned, was the best game of all.

The end.
