# Story story_1354

**Grandma's Garden Problem**

Grandma Rose loved her big garden. She had rows of green beans. She had orange carrots. She had red tomatoes too. Every morning, she walked through her field. She checked on all her plants.

But Grandma Rose had a secret. She did not like eating vegetables! She loved growing them. She loved watching them get big. But when dinnertime came, she made faces. "Yuck," she would say. "These taste terrible."

One sunny morning, Grandma Rose walked to her garden. She saw something scary. Little green bugs were eating her bean plants! The bugs had tiny holes in all the leaves. Her beautiful plants looked sick.

"Oh no!" said Grandma Rose. "My poor plants need help!"

She ran to get her bug spray. She sprayed and sprayed. The bugs flew away. But the next day, they came back! There were even more bugs now. They were eating her carrots too.

Grandma Rose felt very sad. She sat on her old wooden chair. She put her head in her hands. "What can I do?" she asked.

Her neighbor, Mrs. Chen, walked over. Mrs. Chen had a beautiful garden too. Her plants never had bugs.

"Hello, Rose," said Mrs. Chen. "You look worried."

"The bugs are eating everything!" said Grandma Rose. "I tried bug spray. But they keep coming back."

Mrs. Chen smiled. She had kind, wrinkled eyes. "I know the secret," she said. "Come see my garden."

They walked to Mrs. Chen's field together. Grandma Rose saw something amazing. There were no bugs anywhere! The plants looked perfect and green.

"How do you do it?" asked Grandma Rose.

"I eat my vegetables every day," said Mrs. Chen. "When you love your food, you take better care of it. You notice things faster. You water more carefully. You pull weeds right away."

Grandma Rose thought about this. "But vegetables taste so bland," she said.

Mrs. Chen laughed. Her laugh sounded like tiny bells. "Come to my kitchen," she said. "Let me show you something special."

In Mrs. Chen's kitchen, wonderful smells filled the air. She was cooking fresh vegetables from her garden. She added butter to the green beans. She put honey on the carrots. She sprinkled salt on the tomatoes.

"Try this," said Mrs. Chen.

Grandma Rose took a small bite. Her eyes grew wide! The green beans tasted sweet and buttery. The carrots were like candy. The tomatoes burst with flavor.

"This is delicious!" said Grandma Rose. "How did you make them taste so good?"

"Fresh vegetables taste better," said Mrs. Chen. "And a little seasoning helps too. When you care about your food, you learn how to make it tasty."

Grandma Rose hurried home. She picked fresh vegetables from her garden. She washed them carefully. She cooked them just like Mrs. Chen showed her. She added butter and herbs and love.

The vegetables tasted amazing! They were crisp and sweet. They were nothing like the old, bitter vegetables she used to eat.

The next morning, Grandma Rose walked through her garden differently. She looked closely at each plant. She touched the leaves gently. She watered them with care. She pulled every tiny weed.

When she saw three little bugs, she picked them off right away. She checked under every leaf. She made sure her plants were healthy and happy.

Days passed. Grandma Rose's garden grew more beautiful. She ate fresh vegetables every single day. She learned to cook them in new ways. Sometimes she made soup. Sometimes she made salad. Everything tasted wonderful.

The bugs never came back. Her plants grew big and strong. Other neighbors came to ask for her secret.

"Love your food," Grandma Rose would say. "Take good care of it. And always try new ways to make it tasty!"

Now Grandma Rose's garden was the most beautiful in the whole neighborhood. And she was the happiest gardener too. She learned that when you love something, you take better care of it. And when you take good care of something, it takes good care of you too.

The end.
