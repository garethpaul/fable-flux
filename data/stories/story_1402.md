# Story story_1402

**Nutkin's Veggie Adventure**

Nutkin the squirrel loved nursery school. He loved story time. He loved playing with blocks. He loved painting pictures. But there was one thing Nutkin did not love at all.

Vegetables.

Every day at lunch time, Miss Rabbit would put a small bowl of colorful vegetables on each table. There were bright orange carrots. There were green peas that rolled around. There were yellow corn kernels that looked like tiny suns.

"Yuck!" Nutkin would say. He would push his bowl away. "I don't like vegetables. They taste funny."

All the other animals ate their vegetables happily. Benny Bunny crunched his carrots loudly. Penny Pig popped peas into her mouth. Even tiny Molly Mouse nibbled on corn.

But not Nutkin. He would just eat his acorn bread and drink his milk.

One morning, Nutkin's tummy felt rumbly and grumbly. He felt tired during circle time. When they played outside, he couldn't run as fast as his friends.

"What's wrong, Nutkin?" asked Miss Rabbit gently.

"I feel sleepy," Nutkin said. "And my tummy feels empty."

Miss Rabbit nodded wisely. "Your body needs good food to grow strong. Vegetables help you have energy to play."

At lunch time, Nutkin stared at his bowl again. The vegetables looked the same as always. Orange carrots. Green peas. Yellow corn.

"I want to try," Nutkin said quietly. "But they look scary."

Nutkin had an idea for his first solution. He would close his eyes tight and eat the vegetables super fast. Maybe he wouldn't taste them at all!

He squeezed his eyes shut. He grabbed a handful of peas. He shoved them in his mouth quickly.

"Blech!" The peas felt mushy and strange. Nutkin made a face and spit them out. "That didn't work!"

Benny Bunny saw what happened. "Are you okay, Nutkin?"

"I want to eat vegetables," Nutkin said sadly. "But I don't know how to like them."

Penny Pig had an idea. "Maybe you need a different way to try them!"

"What do you mean?" Nutkin asked.

"Watch me!" Penny said. She picked up one tiny piece of corn. She smelled it first. "Mmm, it smells sweet." Then she took a small bite. She chewed slowly. "It tastes like sunshine!"

Nutkin thought about this. Maybe eating slowly was better than eating fast.

For his second solution, Nutkin decided to be very brave and very slow.

He picked up one small carrot stick. First, he smelled it. It smelled fresh and earthy, like the garden outside. Then he took the tiniest bite possible.

He chewed very slowly. The carrot was crunchy and a little bit sweet. It wasn't scary at all!

"Hey!" Nutkin said with surprise. "This tastes good!"

Next, he tried one pea. It was soft and had a gentle, green taste. Finally, he tried a kernel of corn. Penny was right - it did taste like sunshine!

"I did it!" Nutkin squeaked happily. "I ate vegetables!"

All his friends cheered. Miss Rabbit smiled proudly.

From that day on, Nutkin ate his vegetables every day. He discovered that carrots helped him see better during hide-and-seek. Peas made his muscles strong for climbing trees. Corn gave him energy to run and play.

Soon, Nutkin felt much better. His tummy stopped feeling rumbly. He had lots of energy to play with his friends. He could run fast and climb high.

"I'm so glad I learned to like vegetables," Nutkin told his mom that evening. "They make me feel strong and happy."

"You were very brave to keep trying," his mom said, giving him a warm hug.

Nutkin smiled. He had learned something important. Sometimes when things seem hard or scary, you just need to find the right way to try. And if the first way doesn't work, you can always try again.

The next day at nursery, Nutkin was the first one to finish all his vegetables. And he couldn't wait to try something new!

The end.
