# Story story_844

**The Brave Little Book**

In the quiet corner of Sunshine Library lived a small blue book named Benny. Benny had shiny pages filled with pictures of rainbows and butterflies. He sat proudly on the children's shelf next to his friends.

One dark afternoon, Benny heard rumbling sounds outside. Thunder rolled across the sky like bowling balls. Lightning flashed through the tall windows. Rain tapped hard against the glass.

"Oh no!" whispered Benny. "The storm sounds so scary!"

The wind howled louder and louder. It made the windows rattle and shake. Benny's pages trembled with worry. He had never heard such frightening noises before.

"What if the storm comes inside?" Benny thought. "What if it gets my pages all wet?"

Benny looked around the library for help. Mrs. Chen, the librarian, was helping children find books. She couldn't hear his tiny worried voice. The other books seemed calm and quiet.

Benny decided he needed to hide somewhere safe. He wiggled and squirmed on the shelf. Maybe he could squeeze behind the bigger books. He pushed and pushed with all his might.

But Benny was stuck tight between two thick dictionaries. His blue cover got scratched from trying so hard. His pages felt more crumpled than before.

"This isn't working," sighed Benny sadly.

The storm grew stronger outside. Rain pounded on the roof like tiny hammers. More thunder boomed across the sky. Benny felt even more frightened than before.

Then Benny had a new idea. Instead of hiding from the storm, maybe he could learn about it. He opened his pages and looked at his own pictures. There were images of rain helping flowers grow. There were pictures of puddles where ducks liked to swim.

"Maybe storms aren't so bad," Benny thought hopefully.

Benny called softly to his friend Rosa, a red book about weather. "Rosa, can you tell me about storms?"

Rosa's pages rustled gently. "Storms bring water to thirsty plants," she said kindly. "They wash the air clean and fresh. Thunder is just clouds bumping together."

"Really?" asked Benny, feeling a bit braver.

"Yes," said Rosa. "And lightning is like nature's flashlight in the sky."

Benny listened to Rosa's wise words. He thought about all the good things storms could do. The thunder didn't sound quite so scary anymore. It sounded more like nature's drums playing a song.

Just then, little Emma walked over to their shelf. She had been reading quietly during the storm. Emma's small fingers reached for Benny's blue cover.

"This looks like a happy book," Emma said with a smile.

Emma carried Benny to a cozy reading chair by the window. She opened his pages carefully and looked at all his colorful pictures. As she read, Emma pointed to each rainbow and butterfly.

"Look at the pretty colors," Emma whispered.

Through the window, Benny could see the storm was ending. The rain became gentler, like soft music. Sunlight began peeking through the gray clouds. A real rainbow appeared in the sky, just like the ones in Benny's pages.

"You helped me feel better about the storm," Emma told Benny as she hugged him close.

Benny felt warm and proud inside. He had been so worried about the storm hurting him. But instead, the storm brought him a new friend. Emma needed a cheerful book during the scary weather, and Benny was perfect for the job.

When Emma returned Benny to his shelf, he felt completely different. The storm was over, and everything was peaceful again. Benny realized he had been brave all along.

"Thank you for teaching me about storms," Benny said to Rosa.

"You're welcome," Rosa replied. "Sometimes the best way to feel better is to learn something new."

From that day on, Benny never worried about storms again. He knew that even scary things could bring good surprises. And whenever thunder rumbled, Benny remembered that he was brave enough to help other children feel better too.

The library grew quiet and calm as the sun set. Benny closed his pages contentedly, ready for tomorrow's adventures.

The end.
