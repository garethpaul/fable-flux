# Story story_720

**The Ball and the Fish**

Bounce, bounce, bounce! A bright red ball rolled around the quiet library. The ball felt very lonely. All the children had gone home for the day.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," sighed the ball. He rolled past tall bookshelves filled with colorful stories. The library smelled like old books and fresh flowers.

In the corner sat a big glass tank. Inside lived Goldie, a cheerful orange fish. Goldie watched the ball bounce around. Her fins wiggled with excitement.

"Hello there!" called Goldie from her tank. "You look sad. What's wrong?"

The ball stopped rolling. He looked up at the fish tank. "Oh! You can talk! I'm lonely. I want to play, but I have no friends."

Goldie swam in happy circles. "I want to play too! But I'm stuck in this tank. I can only swim around and around."

The ball thought hard. "Maybe we can still be friends. Maybe we can find a way to play together!"

"But how?" asked Goldie. "You bounce and roll. I swim and splash."

The ball had an idea. He rolled closer to the tank. "Watch this!" He bounced up and down next to the glass. Bounce, bounce, bounce!

Goldie giggled. The water made tiny bubbles around her. "That looks fun! My turn!" She swam up and down, following the ball's bounces.

"This is great!" laughed the ball. "We're dancing together!"

They played their bouncing-swimming dance for a long time. The ball would bounce high. Goldie would swim up. The ball would bounce low. Goldie would swim down.

"I have another idea," said the ball. He rolled to get a small book from a low shelf. The book had pictures of the ocean.

"Look, Goldie! I can show you pictures while you tell me about swimming!"

Goldie's eyes sparkled. "Yes! And you can tell me about rolling and bouncing! I've always wondered what it feels like."

The ball opened the book with his round body. "Swimming looks so peaceful and smooth."

"Bouncing looks so exciting and free!" said Goldie. "I wish I could try it."

The ball thought again. "Wait here!" He rolled away quickly. Soon he came back with a small rubber duck from the children's play area.

"This duck can float in your water and bounce a little too!" The ball gently pushed the duck to the edge of the tank.

Goldie was so happy! She nudged the duck with her nose. The duck bobbed up and down in the water. "It bounces in the water! Just like you bounce on the floor!"

"And it floats like you swim!" said the ball. "Now we both have something that does what the other does!"

They played with the rubber duck together. The ball would tap the glass, and Goldie would push the duck. The duck would bob and float between them.

"You're the best friend ever," said Goldie. Her fins sparkled in the library lights.

"You're my best friend too," said the ball. He felt warm and happy inside.

Just then, Mrs. Chen the librarian came back. She saw the ball by the fish tank and the rubber duck floating in the water.

"Well, look at this!" she smiled. "It looks like Goldie has made a new friend."

Mrs. Chen decided to leave the ball near the fish tank. "You two seem perfect together," she said softly.

From that day on, the ball and Goldie played together every day. They told each other stories. They shared their different ways of moving. They laughed and played until the library closed.

The ball never felt lonely again. Goldie was never bored in her tank. They learned that friendship doesn't need to be the same. Different can be wonderful too.

Every morning, the ball would roll over to say, "Good morning, Goldie!"

And Goldie would swim up to say, "Good morning, Ball! Ready to play?"

The library was filled with their happy friendship every single day.

The end.
