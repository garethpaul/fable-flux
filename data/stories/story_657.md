# Story story_657

**Grandma and the Little Red Truck**

Grandma Rose sat on her porch swing. The old wooden swing creaked softly. She watched the chickens peck in the yard. The farm felt very quiet today.

"I wish I had someone to play with," she sighed. Her gray hair blew gently in the warm breeze. The sun felt nice on her face.

Near the red barn sat a little truck. His paint was bright red like apples. His wheels were round and black. The truck had been sitting there all morning.

The little truck watched Grandma Rose. He saw her sitting all alone. His engine made a soft rumbling sound. He wanted to help somehow.

"Vroom, vroom," whispered the truck quietly. He rolled forward just a little bit. His wheels crunched on the gravel path.

Grandma Rose looked up. She saw the truck moving toward her. Her eyes grew wide with surprise.

"Hello there, little truck," she said softly. "Did you come to visit me?"

The truck's headlights seemed to twinkle. He rolled closer to the porch. His red paint sparkled in the sunshine.

"I think you want to play," said Grandma Rose. She stood up slowly. Her knees creaked like the old swing.

The truck honked his horn once. It was a happy, friendly sound. Grandma Rose clapped her hands together.

"What shall we play?" she asked. She walked down the porch steps. Her shoes made soft tapping sounds.

The truck rolled to the vegetable garden. He stopped next to the tomato plants. Red tomatoes hung heavy on the vines.

"Oh, you want to help me pick tomatoes!" said Grandma Rose. She understood perfectly now.

Grandma Rose climbed into the truck's bed. She felt a little wobbly at first. But the truck stayed very still for her.

Together they rolled through the garden. Grandma Rose picked ripe tomatoes. She put them gently in baskets. The truck carried her safely between the rows.

"These tomatoes smell so fresh," said Grandma Rose. She sniffed the green leaves. "Thank you for helping me, little truck."

The truck beeped softly. He was happy to help. His engine purred like a contented cat.

Next, they went to collect eggs. The chickens clucked and flapped their wings. Grandma Rose gathered warm eggs carefully. The truck waited patiently by the chicken coop.

"You are such a good friend," she told him. She patted his shiny red hood gently.

After their work was done, they played games. The truck gave Grandma Rose rides around the farm. She laughed as they bumped over small hills.

"This is the most fun I've had all week!" she called out. The wind made her hair dance. Her cheeks turned pink with joy.

They played hide-and-seek behind the barn. The truck tried to hide his bright red color. Grandma Rose pretended she couldn't see him. They both giggled together.

As the sun began to set, they sat together. Grandma Rose leaned against the truck's warm side. The metal felt smooth and comforting.

"I was lonely today," she said quietly. "But you made everything better."

The truck's engine hummed a gentle tune. It sounded almost like a lullaby. Stars began to twinkle in the purple sky.

"Would you like to be my special friend?" asked Grandma Rose. "We could play together every day."

The truck flashed his headlights twice. That meant yes in truck language. Grandma Rose understood perfectly.

From that day on, they were best friends. Every morning, the little red truck waited by the porch. Grandma Rose would climb aboard with a big smile.

They worked together in the garden. They collected eggs from happy chickens. They took long rides around the beautiful farm.

The farm never felt lonely anymore. It was filled with laughter and friendship. Grandma Rose and her little red truck had found something special together.

"Friends make everything more fun," said Grandma Rose. The truck beeped in agreement. And they lived happily on their wonderful farm.

The end.
