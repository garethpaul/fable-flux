# Story story_815

**Finley's Snack Time Adventure**

The big clock on the wall showed three o'clock. Mrs. Turtle clapped her flippers together. "Snack time, everyone!" she called out cheerfully.

All the little fish in the underwater classroom swam to their seats. Finley the goldfish stayed at his desk. He looked at his lunch box with worried eyes. His tummy felt funny and rumbly.

"Come on, Finley," said his friend Bella the angelfish. "Aren't you hungry?"

Finley shook his head slowly. "I don't want snacks today," he whispered. "My tummy feels too wiggly."

The other fish opened their colorful lunch boxes. Crunchy kelp chips spilled out. Sweet sea berry juice boxes made happy popping sounds. The classroom filled with yummy smells.

But Finley just stared at his blue lunch box. It had sparkly fish stickers on top. Inside were his favorite foods. Mom had packed crispy seaweed crackers. She added creamy coral dip too. There were even tiny rainbow fish cookies.

"Sometimes tummies feel nervous," said Mrs. Turtle gently. She swam over to Finley's desk. "Would you like to sit with me?"

Finley nodded and followed Mrs. Turtle to the reading corner. The soft cushions felt cozy against his fins. Mrs. Turtle opened a magical picture book.

"This is about a little fish like you," she said with a warm smile.

As Mrs. Turtle read, something amazing happened. The pictures began to glow and shimmer. A tiny fish swam right out of the book! It was silver and sparkly like a shooting star.

"Hello, Finley!" said the magical fish. "I'm Shimmer. I used to feel scared at snack time too."

Finley's eyes grew wide with wonder. "Really? You did?"

"Oh yes," Shimmer giggled. Her scales twinkled like tiny diamonds. "But then I learned a secret. Want to know what it is?"

Finley nodded eagerly. His nervous feeling started to fade away.

"Eating with friends makes everything taste better," Shimmer whispered. "And taking tiny bites helps your tummy feel brave."

Shimmer swam in happy circles around Finley. Sparkly bubbles floated everywhere. They smelled like cotton candy and sunshine.

"Would you like to try?" asked Mrs. Turtle kindly.

Finley thought about Shimmer's words. He looked at his friends eating together. They were laughing and sharing their snacks. It looked really fun.

"Okay," said Finley softly. "But just a tiny bite."

Mrs. Turtle helped Finley get his lunch box. He sat between Bella and his friend Nemo. They made room for him with big smiles.

Finley opened his lunch box slowly. The seaweed crackers looked golden and crispy. He took one small bite. It tasted salty and good. His tummy didn't feel wiggly anymore.

"These are really yummy!" said Bella, trying Finley's crackers.

"Want to try my kelp chips?" offered Nemo.

Soon all the friends were sharing and giggling. Finley tried new foods he had never tasted before. Sweet sea berries burst with flavor. Crunchy kelp chips tasted like the ocean breeze.

Shimmer the magical fish danced around their table. She made their food sparkle with happiness. But only Finley could see her special magic.

"See?" whispered Shimmer. "Snack time is the best time!"

When snack time ended, Finley felt proud and full. His tummy was happy and warm. He had tried three new foods today.

"Thank you for helping me," Finley told Mrs. Turtle.

"You were very brave," she replied with a proud smile.

As they cleaned up, Finley looked at tomorrow's lunch menu. It had fish stick Friday written in colorful letters. He felt excited instead of worried.

"I can't wait for snack time tomorrow," Finley told Bella.

Shimmer winked at Finley before swimming back into the magical book. "See you soon, brave little fish," she whispered.

Finley smiled the biggest smile ever. Snack time wasn't scary anymore. It was the most magical part of his day.

The end.
