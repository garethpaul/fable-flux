# Tessa Tiger's Magical Story Time

It was three o'clock on Sunday afternoon. The big clock in the living room went *tick-tock, tick-tock*. Mama Tiger called from the cozy reading corner, "Tessa! Story time!"

Tessa Tiger peeked around the kitchen doorway. She had orange and black stripes that shimmered in the sunlight. Her little pink nose twitched. "Do I have to, Mama?" she asked softly.

Tessa loved playing with her toy cars. She loved building tall block towers. But story time felt... boring. She had to sit still for so long. The books had too many words. Her paws always felt wiggly.

"Come sit with me, little tiger," Mama said gently. She patted the soft blue cushion beside her. "I picked a special book today."

Tessa walked slowly to the reading corner. Her striped tail dragged behind her. She climbed onto the cushion with a small sigh. The afternoon sun made warm golden squares on the carpet.

Mama opened a bright red book. On the cover was a jungle filled with green leaves and colorful flowers. "This story is about a brave tiger," Mama said with a smile.

Tessa's ears perked up a tiny bit. "A tiger like me?"

"Just like you," Mama nodded.

As Mama began reading, something wonderful started happening. The words seemed to dance off the pages. Tessa could almost hear jungle birds singing *tweet-tweet-chirp*. She could almost smell the sweet tropical flowers.

"The little tiger crept through the tall grass," Mama read in a whisper. "*Rustle, rustle, rustle.*"

Tessa found herself leaning forward. Her eyes grew wide. In her mind, she could see the brave tiger moving through the jungle. The tiger looked just like her!

"Suddenly, the tiger heard a tiny cry for help," Mama continued. Her voice became worried and excited.

"What happened next?" Tessa asked quickly. She forgot all about feeling wiggly.

Mama turned the page slowly. The picture showed the tiger finding a small, lost monkey. "The kind tiger helped the little monkey find his family," she read.

Tessa's heart felt warm and happy. "The tiger was so nice!" she said, clapping her paws together.

As the story continued, Tessa felt like she was right there in the jungle. She could feel the soft dirt under her paws. She could hear the gentle *whoosh* of wind through the trees. When the tiger in the story felt scared, Tessa felt scared too. When the tiger felt brave, Tessa sat up tall and proud.

"And they all lived happily in the beautiful jungle," Mama finished. She closed the book with a soft *thump*.

"That was amazing!" Tessa bounced on her cushion. "Can we read another one? Please, please?"

Mama laughed, a sound like gentle bells. "Of course, sweet tiger. Which book would you like?"

Tessa looked at the tall bookshelf. For the first time, all those colorful books looked exciting instead of boring. "The one with the purple cover! No, wait... the yellow one! Actually, can you pick? I want to be surprised!"

They read three more stories together. Tessa traveled to a magical castle. She met a friendly dragon who loved to bake cookies. She sailed across a sparkling blue ocean with a crew of singing seals.

When Papa Tiger came home, Tessa ran to meet him. "Papa! Papa! We read the most wonderful stories! There was a tiger just like me, and a dragon, and singing seals!"

Papa Tiger scooped her up in a big, warm hug. "It sounds like you had quite an adventure!"

"I did! And tomorrow at story time, Mama said we can read about space bears!" Tessa's eyes sparkled with excitement.

That night, as Mama tucked her into bed, Tessa asked, "Mama, how do books make everything feel so real?"

Mama kissed her forehead gently. "That's the magic of stories, little one. They help our imaginations grow big and strong."

Tessa curled up under her soft blanket. She couldn't wait for tomorrow's story time. Maybe she would travel to the moon with those space bears!

As she drifted off to sleep, Tessa smiled. Story time had become her favorite part of every day.

The end.
