# The Little Red Ball's Big Adventure

In a long, bright hallway lived a little red ball named Ruby. She was round and bouncy with a cheerful red color. Ruby loved to roll and spin. She made soft whooshing sounds on the smooth floor.

But today, Ruby felt very sad. She had rolled far from her mama. Mama was a big, beautiful basketball. She lived in the toy room. Ruby missed her mama's warm hugs. She missed their fun games together.

"I want to go home," Ruby whispered. A tiny tear rolled down her round side. The hallway felt so big and empty. Ruby felt so small and alone.

Ruby looked around the long hallway. The walls were tall and white. Sunlight came through a window. It made pretty patterns on the floor. But Ruby just wanted her mama.

"I will find my way back!" Ruby said bravely. She took a deep breath. Then she started to roll. Her little body moved slowly at first. Roll, roll, roll down the hallway.

Ruby rolled to the left. She bumped into a wall. "Ouch!" she said. That wasn't the right way. Ruby rolled to the right. She hit another wall. "Ow!" That wasn't right either.

Ruby sat still for a moment. She felt tired and confused. The hallway had so many doors. Which one led to mama? Ruby started to cry again.

Then Ruby heard something wonderful. It was a bounce, bounce, bounce sound. It came from behind a blue door. Ruby's heart jumped with joy. "That sounds like mama!" she said.

Ruby rolled quickly to the blue door. She pushed against it hard. Push, push, push! But the door wouldn't open. Ruby tried again. She rolled back and forth. She spun in circles. But nothing worked.

Ruby felt sad again. She could hear mama bouncing. But she couldn't reach her. "How can I open this door?" Ruby wondered.

Then Ruby had an idea. She rolled back very far. She took a big breath. "I can do this!" she said loudly. Ruby rolled as fast as she could. She zoomed toward the door like a rocket.

BUMP! Ruby hit the door hard. The door opened just a little bit. "It worked!" Ruby cheered. She tried again. This time she rolled even faster. BUMP! The door opened more.

One more time, Ruby rolled back. She gathered all her courage. "Here I go!" she shouted. Ruby rolled super fast. Her red color blurred as she moved. BUMP! The door swung wide open.

"Ruby!" called a deep, happy voice. There was mama! The big basketball was bouncing in the toy room. She looked so happy to see Ruby.

"Mama!" Ruby cried with joy. She rolled straight into mama's arms. Mama hugged her tight. Ruby felt warm and safe again.

"I missed you so much," Ruby said.

"I missed you too, little one," mama said gently. "But look how brave you were! You found your way back all by yourself."

Ruby felt proud and happy. She had been scared. She had felt sad. But she didn't give up. She kept trying new things. She solved her problem!

"I was persistent," Ruby said proudly. "I didn't stop trying."

"That's right," mama said with a smile. "When something is hard, we keep trying. We think of new ways to solve problems."

Ruby bounced happily next to mama. The toy room felt cozy and bright. All the other toys welcomed Ruby home. They had missed her too.

"Tell us about your adventure!" said a stuffed bear.

Ruby told everyone her story. She told them about feeling sad. She told them about rolling and bumping. She told them about opening the door. All the toys cheered for Ruby.

That night, Ruby snuggled close to mama. She felt happy and proud. She had learned something important. When problems seem big, she could break them into small steps. She could try different solutions. She could be persistent.

"I love you, mama," Ruby whispered.

"I love you too, brave little ball," mama whispered back.

Ruby closed her eyes and smiled. Tomorrow would bring new adventures. But now she knew she could handle anything. She was persistent. She was a problem-solver. She was Ruby, the brave little red ball.

The end.
