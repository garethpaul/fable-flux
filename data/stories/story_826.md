# Story story_826

**Dusty the Drum's Big Problem**

High up in Grammy's attic lived Dusty the Drum. He was round and shiny red. Well, he used to be shiny. Now Dusty was covered in thick, gray dust.

"Achoo!" sneezed Dusty every morning. The dust tickled his nose. It made his beautiful red color look dull and sad.

One sunny day, Grammy climbed up to the attic. "Oh my!" she said. "Poor little drum. You need a good bath!"

Dusty's eyes grew wide. A bath? With water? "No, no, no!" he whispered. "Drums don't like water. Water makes my skin all soggy!"

Grammy picked up Dusty gently. She carried him downstairs to the kitchen sink. The water sparkled in the sunlight. It looked cold and scary.

"Please don't!" Dusty begged. But Grammy couldn't hear his tiny voice.

She turned on the faucet. Splash! The water hit Dusty's dusty top. "Help!" cried Dusty. The water felt cold and wet. His drum skin started to feel funny.

Grammy scrubbed with a soft cloth. The dust came off, but Dusty felt awful. His drum skin got loose and floppy. His beautiful sound became dull and quiet.

"Oh dear," said Grammy. "You don't sound right anymore." She dried Dusty with a fluffy towel. Then she put him on the sunny windowsill.

Dusty felt terrible. His skin was all wrinkled. When Grammy tapped him, he made a sad, muffled sound. "Thump, thump," instead of his usual happy "BOOM, BOOM!"

"I told you water was bad," Dusty said sadly. He watched the dust settle on him again. Soon he would be gray and dusty, but at least he would sound good.

Three days passed. Dusty was dusty again, but his skin was still loose. He couldn't make his proud, loud sounds anymore.

Then little Emma came to visit Grammy. Emma loved music. She saw Dusty on the windowsill.

"Grammy, why does your drum sound so quiet?" Emma asked.

"I washed him, but now he's not the same," Grammy said with a frown.

Emma touched Dusty's loose skin. "My music teacher says drums need tight skin to sound good. Maybe we can fix him!"

Emma ran to get Grammy's hair dryer. "Heat makes things tight," she said. "Let's try warming him up!"

Grammy plugged in the hair dryer. Warm air blew over Dusty's damp skin. "Ooh," said Dusty. "That feels nice and cozy."

The warm air made Dusty's skin start to tighten. Slowly, slowly, it pulled tight like it used to be.

"Tap me now!" Dusty wanted to shout.

Emma picked up two wooden spoons. She tapped Dusty gently. "Boom!" went Dusty. Then "BOOM, BOOM!"

"He sounds perfect!" Emma clapped her hands.

Grammy smiled. "You're so smart, Emma. The heat fixed his skin!"

Now Dusty was clean AND he sounded wonderful. His red color shone bright in the sunlight. No more dust tickled his nose.

Emma played a happy song on Dusty. "Boom, boom, tap, tap, BOOM!" The music filled the whole house with joy.

"I was wrong about baths," Dusty realized. "Getting clean felt scary at first. But Emma didn't give up on me. She found a way to make everything work!"

From that day on, Dusty got a bath once a month. Grammy would wash him gently, then Emma would warm him with the hair dryer. Soon Dusty's skin would be tight and perfect again.

Dusty learned that trying new things can be scary. Sometimes the first try doesn't work. But with help from friends and a little creativity, problems can be solved.

Now Dusty sits proudly on Grammy's shelf. He's shiny red and sounds beautiful. When children visit, he makes the most wonderful music.

"BOOM, BOOM, BOOM!" sings Dusty happily. "I'm clean, I'm bright, and I sound just right!"

And Grammy and Emma smile every time they hear his joyful song.

The end.
