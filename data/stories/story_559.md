# Story story_559

**Maya's Missing Book**

Maya the monkey loved her special book. It had bright pictures of bananas. It had fun stories about jungle friends. She carried it everywhere in her little backpack.

One sunny morning, Maya played in the big grassy field. She swung from tree to tree. She rolled down the soft hill. She picked pretty yellow flowers.

When lunch time came, Maya felt hungry. She reached into her backpack for her book. She always read while eating bananas.

But her backpack felt empty. Maya looked inside. No book! She turned the backpack upside down. Still no book!

"Oh no!" Maya cried. "Where is my special book?"

Maya's tummy felt funny. Her eyes got watery. She loved that book so much. It made her feel happy and safe.

Maya looked around the big field. Tall grass waved in the breeze. Colorful flowers dotted the green space. But where could her book be?

"I will find it," Maya said. She wiped her eyes with her furry paw. "I must look everywhere."

First, Maya climbed the tallest oak tree. She looked down at the field below. The grass looked like a green carpet. She could see the whole area from up high.

Maya spotted something red near the pond. "That must be my book!" she shouted. She swung down quickly from branch to branch.

Maya ran through the tall grass. Her feet made soft sounds on the earth. The red thing got bigger as she got closer.

But it was not her book. It was just a red leaf. Maya's shoulders dropped. She felt sad again.

"Maybe the wind blew my book away," Maya thought. She decided to search in a different way.

Maya got down on her hands and knees. She crawled slowly through the grass. The green blades tickled her nose. She smelled the sweet earth underneath.

She looked behind every rock. She checked under every flower. She searched near the bubbling stream.

Then Maya heard a soft sound. "Ribbit, ribbit." A little green frog sat on a lily pad.

"Hello, Frog," Maya said. "Have you seen my special book? It has banana pictures."

"Yes!" croaked the frog. "A book fell in the tall grass. Over by the big sunflower patch."

Maya's heart jumped with joy. "Thank you, Frog! You are so helpful!"

Maya hurried to the sunflower patch. The tall yellow flowers towered above her head. Their faces followed the warm sun.

Maya pushed through the thick grass. She moved the green stems carefully. Then she saw something colorful.

There it was! Her special book lay nestled in the soft grass. The cover still looked bright and new. Maya picked it up and hugged it tight.

"My book!" Maya laughed. "I found my special book!"

She opened it carefully. All the banana pictures smiled back at her. The pages felt smooth under her fingers. Everything was perfect.

Maya sat in the sunny field. She read her favorite story about brave jungle animals. The warm sun felt good on her fur. Birds sang happy songs above her.

"I am so glad I kept looking," Maya said to herself. "Even when I felt sad, I did not give up."

Maya learned something important that day. Sometimes things seem lost forever. But if you keep trying, you might find them. Being patient and looking carefully really helps.

From that day on, Maya always zipped her backpack tight. She checked it twice before playing. But she was not worried anymore. She knew that if something got lost, she could find it again.

Maya finished reading her book. She put it safely in her backpack. Then she picked some flowers for her mom. The field felt like the most wonderful place in the world.

As Maya walked home, she smiled big. Her special book was safe. Her heart felt warm and happy. Tomorrow would bring another fun adventure.

The end.
