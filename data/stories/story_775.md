# Story story_775

**Sam and the High-Up Cookies**

Sam wiggled his toes in the warm sand. The beach was perfect today. Blue waves splashed nearby. Seagulls called overhead.

"Time for our picnic!" called Mom from their beach blanket.

Sam ran over, his feet making little puffs in the soft sand. Mom had set up their lunch under a big beach umbrella. Sandwiches sat on paper plates. Cold juice boxes waited in the cooler.

But something made Sam's eyes grow wide.

High up on the picnic table sat a jar of his favorite cookies. Chocolate chip cookies! The jar sparkled in the sunshine. Sam could almost taste the sweet, chewy treats.

Sam reached up with both arms. He stretched as tall as he could. His fingers wiggled in the air. But the cookie jar was too high up.

"I can't reach the cookies," Sam said sadly.

The jar seemed so far away. Sam felt disappointed. His tummy rumbled for those yummy cookies.

"Maybe I can help myself," thought Sam.

Sam looked around the beach. He spotted a small piece of driftwood. The smooth wood felt warm from the sun. Sam carried it over to the picnic table.

He placed the driftwood on the sand. Then Sam stepped up onto it. He reached up again. His arms stretched high above his head.

But the cookies were still too far away.

"This isn't working," Sam sighed.

The driftwood wasn't tall enough. Sam stepped down. He felt frustrated. Those cookies looked so good up there.

Sam sat in the sand for a moment. He watched the waves roll in and out. The salty breeze tickled his face. Then Sam had another idea.

"Maybe I need something taller," he thought.

Sam noticed their beach cooler nearby. It was bigger than the driftwood. Much more sturdy too. The cooler had a flat top that looked perfect for standing.

"Mom, can I move the cooler?" asked Sam.

"Of course, sweetie," said Mom with a smile.

Sam pushed the cooler next to the picnic table. It made a scraping sound in the sand. The cooler felt solid and safe.

Carefully, Sam climbed up onto the cooler. He held the table edge for balance. Now he was much higher than before.

Sam reached up once more. This time his fingers touched the cookie jar! The glass felt smooth and cool. Sam carefully lifted the jar down.

"I did it!" Sam cheered.

He hugged the cookie jar close. Success felt wonderful! Sam had solved his problem all by himself.

"Good thinking, Sam!" said Mom proudly. "You didn't give up."

Sam climbed down from the cooler. He unscrewed the jar lid. The sweet smell of chocolate chips filled the air. Sam took out two cookies and offered one to Mom.

"Thank you for being patient with me," said Sam.

The cookie tasted even better than Sam had imagined. Chocolate melted on his tongue. The cookie was soft and perfect.

Sam and Mom sat together on their blanket. They munched cookies and listened to the ocean sounds. Waves whooshed onto the shore. Seagulls laughed in the distance.

"I'm proud of you," said Mom. "You tried two different ways."

"The first way didn't work," said Sam. "But I kept trying."

"That's what problem-solving is all about," said Mom.

Sam smiled big. He had learned something important today. When something seems too hard, you don't give up. You try a different way.

The beach day continued perfectly. Sam built sandcastles with his cookie energy. He splashed in the gentle waves. The sun warmed his shoulders.

Every time Sam looked at the picnic table, he felt proud. He had reached those high-up cookies all by himself. It just took some creative thinking.

As the sun began to set, Sam helped pack up their beach things. He carefully put the cookie jar back in the basket.

"Today was the best beach day ever," said Sam.

"Every day is better when we don't give up," said Mom.

Sam nodded happily. He couldn't wait for their next adventure.

The end.
