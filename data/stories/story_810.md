# Story story_810

**The Little Blue Boat and Terry the Turtle**

In a big white bathtub lived a little blue boat. His name was Splash. Every day, Splash floated alone in the warm, bubbly water.

Splash had a bright red sail. He had a shiny yellow anchor too. But Splash felt very lonely.

"I wish I had a friend," Splash said sadly. His voice echoed in the empty bathroom.

The water felt warm on his hull. The bubbles tickled his sides. But playing alone wasn't much fun.

One sunny morning, something moved near the drain. A small green head poked up from the water.

"Hello!" said a cheerful voice.

Splash looked around. "Who said that?"

"Down here!" called the voice.

Splash saw a little green turtle. The turtle had kind brown eyes. His shell was smooth and round.

"I'm Terry the Turtle," he said with a big smile. "I live under the bathtub. I heard you feeling sad."

Splash's sail fluttered with excitement. "You did? I'm Splash the Boat!"

Terry paddled closer. His little flippers moved through the water. "Would you like to play together?"

"Yes, please!" Splash said happily.

Terry climbed onto Splash's deck. His wet flippers made tiny puddles. "What should we do first?"

"Let's sail around the tub!" Splash suggested.

They sailed in big circles. The water made gentle whooshing sounds. Terry laughed as they spun around.

"This is so much fun!" Terry giggled.

Next, they played hide-and-seek. Terry hid behind the soap bottle. Splash found him right away.

"Your green shell is showing!" Splash laughed.

Then Splash hid behind the rubber duck. Terry spotted his red sail quickly.

"I see you!" Terry called out.

They played catch with a small sponge. Terry threw it with his flippers. Splash caught it with his sail.

The sponge felt soft and squishy. It made a tiny splash each time.

"You're really good at this!" Splash said.

"So are you!" Terry replied.

They made up a racing game. Terry swam as fast as he could. Splash sailed with all his might.

The water rippled around them. Their laughter filled the bathroom.

"Let's race to the faucet!" Terry suggested.

"Ready, set, go!" Splash shouted.

They zoomed across the tub together. Terry's flippers kicked quickly. Splash's sail caught the air.

They reached the faucet at the same time.

"It's a tie!" they both said together.

After racing, they floated quietly. The water was calm and peaceful.

"I've never had so much fun," Splash said softly.

"Me neither," Terry agreed. "I'm so glad I met you."

They watched the bubbles pop around them. Each bubble made a tiny sound.

"Would you like to be best friends?" Splash asked hopefully.

"I would love that!" Terry said with joy.

They decided to play every day. Terry would visit each morning. They would have adventures together.

"We could explore the whole bathtub!" Terry said excitedly.

"We could pretend to sail to far-away lands!" Splash added.

They made plans for tomorrow. They would build a bubble castle. They would have underwater tea parties too.

As the sun set, Terry yawned. "I should go home now."

"Will you come back tomorrow?" Splash asked.

"Of course! Best friends always come back," Terry promised.

Terry slipped into the water. He waved goodbye with his flipper.

"See you tomorrow, Splash!"

"See you tomorrow, Terry!"

Splash felt warm and happy inside. He wasn't lonely anymore. He had found his very best friend.

The bathroom felt cozy and bright. The water sparkled in the evening light.

Splash closed his eyes and smiled. Tomorrow would bring more fun adventures. Tomorrow would bring more laughter and games.

But best of all, tomorrow would bring his dear friend Terry.

From that day on, Splash and Terry played together every single day. They became the very best of friends.

And neither of them ever felt lonely again.

The little blue boat and the little green turtle had found something wonderful. They had found friendship.

The end.
