# Story story_994

**Puzzle's Missing Book**

Puzzle the teddy bear lived on Emma's bedroom shelf. She had soft brown fur and a red ribbon. Every night, Puzzle read her favorite book. It had pictures of rainbows and butterflies.

But this morning, something was wrong. Puzzle looked left and right. She looked up and down. Her special book was gone!

"Oh no!" said Puzzle. Her button eyes grew wide. "Where is my rainbow book?"

Puzzle climbed down from the shelf. Her paws made tiny sounds on the wooden floor. She felt worried and sad. Reading her book made her happy every day.

First, Puzzle looked under Emma's bed. It was dark and dusty there. She saw a lost sock and some crayons. But no rainbow book anywhere.

"Maybe it fell behind the dresser," thought Puzzle. She squeezed into the narrow space. Dust tickles made her nose twitch. She found a bouncy ball and three puzzle pieces. Still no book.

Puzzle sat on the soft carpet. She felt like crying. The morning sun streamed through the window. It made golden squares on the floor.

"I need help," whispered Puzzle. She looked around the room. Maybe her friends could help her think.

Puzzle walked to the toy chest. She knocked three times with her paw. "Hello, friends! Can you help me?"

Out popped Buttons the clown. His red nose was bright and shiny. "What's wrong, Puzzle?" he asked kindly.

"My rainbow book is missing," said Puzzle. "I looked everywhere already."

Buttons thought hard. His colorful hat jingled softly. "Did you check the bookshelf?" he asked.

Puzzle's eyes lit up. "No! I didn't look there!" The bookshelf was tall and full of Emma's books. Maybe her book was hiding there.

Puzzle and Buttons climbed up together. They moved big books and little books. They checked behind thick books and thin books. The books smelled like vanilla and adventure.

"Here it is!" shouted Buttons suddenly. The rainbow book was tucked between two tall books. It had slipped down and gotten stuck.

Puzzle hugged her precious book tight. The cover felt smooth and familiar. She could see the beautiful rainbow on the front. The colors were red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple.

"Thank you, Buttons!" said Puzzle happily. "You helped me think of new places to look."

"That's what friends do," smiled Buttons. His painted smile was extra wide.

That evening, Emma picked up Puzzle for bedtime. "Would you like me to read to you?" asked Emma sweetly.

Puzzle nodded her fuzzy head. Emma opened the rainbow book carefully. The pages made soft rustling sounds. Emma's voice was gentle and warm.

They read about rainbow bridges and butterfly gardens. They learned about colors mixing in the sky. Puzzle felt cozy and content in Emma's arms.

"I'm glad we found your book," whispered Emma. She gave Puzzle a soft kiss on her furry head.

Puzzle snuggled into her spot on the shelf. She held her rainbow book close. Tomorrow she would read it again by herself.

"I learned something important today," thought Puzzle. "When I have a problem, I shouldn't give up. I can try different ways to solve it."

She also learned that asking friends for help was smart. Buttons helped her think of the bookshelf. She never would have found her book alone.

The moon peeked through Emma's window. Its silver light made everything peaceful and calm. Puzzle yawned a tiny teddy bear yawn.

"Good night, rainbow book," she whispered softly. "See you in the morning."

Puzzle closed her button eyes and smiled. She felt proud of herself for not giving up. She felt grateful for good friends like Buttons. And she felt happy to have her favorite book back.

The bedroom grew quiet and still. All the toys settled in for the night. Puzzle dreamed of colorful rainbows dancing in butterfly gardens. It was the most wonderful dream ever.

Tomorrow would bring new adventures. But tonight, everything was just right.

The end.
