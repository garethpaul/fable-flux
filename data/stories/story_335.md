# Grandpa's Special Breakfast

Every morning, the sun peeked through Grandpa Joe's kitchen window. The golden light danced on his wooden table. It was time for breakfast again.

Grandpa Joe sat in his favorite chair. He looked at his empty bowl. He looked at his quiet kitchen. "Another breakfast all by myself," he sighed softly.

Yesterday's breakfast was just toast. The day before was just cereal. Grandpa Joe felt a little lonely. He missed the busy breakfasts when his family visited.

"Maybe I'll just have coffee today," Grandpa Joe mumbled. He shuffled to the kitchen counter. His slippers made soft whisper sounds on the floor.

But then something wonderful happened. A tiny red bird landed on his windowsill. The bird tapped its beak on the glass. Tap, tap, tap!

"Well, hello there, little friend," Grandpa Joe smiled. The bird tilted its head and chirped a sweet song.

Grandpa Joe opened his window just a crack. "Are you hungry too?" he asked the bird. The bird chirped again, as if saying yes!

Suddenly, Grandpa Joe had an idea. "Let's have breakfast together!" He felt excited for the first time in days.

Grandpa Joe took out his special blue bowl. He added creamy oatmeal with cinnamon. The kitchen smelled like warm cookies. He sprinkled brown sugar on top. It sparkled like tiny diamonds.

For his new friend, he crumbled some bread. He placed the crumbs on the windowsill. The red bird hopped closer and began to eat.

"This oatmeal tastes better with company," Grandpa Joe said. The bird chirped happily between bites. They ate breakfast together in comfortable silence.

Then more birds arrived! A yellow finch landed next. Then a gray dove joined them. Soon, five colorful birds shared the windowsill.

Grandpa Joe laughed with delight. "What a breakfast party!" The birds seemed to sing just for him. Their songs filled his quiet kitchen with joy.

He added fresh blueberries to his oatmeal. The berries were plump and sweet. He made more bread crumbs for his feathered friends. Everyone was happy and full.

After breakfast, the birds flew away. But the red bird stayed a moment longer. It looked right at Grandpa Joe and chirped twice. Then it flew to the big oak tree outside.

Grandpa Joe waved goodbye through the window. "Thank you for breakfast!" he called. His heart felt warm and light.

He cleaned his blue bowl carefully. He swept up the bread crumbs with a smile. The kitchen didn't feel quiet anymore. It felt peaceful and friendly.

Grandpa Joe looked at his calendar. Tomorrow was Tuesday. "I wonder what my bird friends like for Tuesday breakfast," he thought happily.

He made a shopping list on paper. Sunflower seeds for the finch. Oat flakes for the dove. Maybe some fresh strawberries for himself. His pencil moved quickly across the page.

That evening, Grandpa Joe set his alarm clock. He wanted to wake up early. He wanted to prepare a special breakfast for everyone. He could hardly wait!

He placed the blue bowl on the counter. He put bread near the window. Everything was ready for tomorrow's breakfast party.

Grandpa Joe went to bed with a big smile. He dreamed of colorful birds and warm oatmeal. He dreamed of songs and friendship.

The next morning, Grandpa Joe jumped out of bed. The sun was just rising. He hurried to the kitchen in his cozy slippers.

"Good morning, friends!" he called to the window. Sure enough, the red bird was already there. It tapped the glass cheerfully. Tap, tap, tap!

Grandpa Joe opened the window wide. "Ready for another breakfast adventure?" The bird chirped yes!

From that day on, breakfast became Grandpa Joe's favorite time. Every morning brought new bird friends. Every meal was a small celebration.

He learned that ordinary moments could become magical. All it took was an open heart and a friend to share them with.

Breakfast was never lonely again.

The end.
