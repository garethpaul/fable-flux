# Story story_1359

**Grandma's Bathroom Friend**

Grandma Ruby sat on her soft blue bathroom rug. She had her favorite toy boats floating in the big white bathtub. The water was warm and bubbly. Little soap bubbles popped on her wrinkled hands.

"Sail away, little boat," she whispered softly. But her voice sounded lonely in the quiet room. The yellow duck soap smelled like lemons. The fluffy towels hung perfectly on their hooks.

Grandma Ruby sighed. Playing alone wasn't very fun. She wished someone would play with her. The bathroom felt too big and empty.

Just then, she heard a gentle knock. Tap, tap, tap.

"Who could that be?" Grandma Ruby wondered.

A big brown bear peeked around the door. His fur looked soft and fuzzy. His black nose twitched with curiosity.

"Hello," said Bear in a rumbly voice. "I heard splashing sounds. Can I play too?"

Grandma Ruby's eyes lit up like sunshine. "Oh yes! Please come in, Bear!"

Bear stepped carefully into the bathroom. His big paws made soft padding sounds on the tile floor. He sniffed the lemony soap smell.

"What are you playing?" Bear asked politely.

"Boat sailing," said Grandma Ruby. "But it's more fun with friends."

Bear's brown eyes sparkled with joy. "I love boats! May I try?"

Grandma Ruby handed him a red toy boat. Bear's big paws held it very gently. He placed it in the warm, bubbly water.

"Look! It floats!" Bear said happily.

The two friends watched their boats sail around the tub. The red boat bumped into the yellow boat. Both friends giggled at the silly sight.

"Let's make waves," suggested Grandma Ruby.

They swished their hands in the water. Gentle waves carried their boats back and forth. The bathroom filled with happy splashing sounds.

Bear found a green washcloth. "This can be an island!"

He placed it in the middle of the tub. Now their boats had somewhere special to visit.

"What a wonderful idea!" said Grandma Ruby.

They sailed their boats to the washcloth island. They made up stories about brave sea captains. The warm steam made everything feel cozy and magical.

Bear's tummy rumbled loudly. Both friends started laughing. The sound echoed off the bathroom walls.

"Even sea captains get hungry," giggled Grandma Ruby.

"The boats need food too," said Bear seriously.

Grandma Ruby found tiny soap pieces. "Boat food!" she announced.

They pretended to feed their hungry boats. Bear made funny eating sounds. "Chomp, chomp, chomp!"

Grandma Ruby clapped her hands with delight. Playing with Bear was the most fun ever.

After a while, the water grew cool. Time to end their boat adventure.

"Thank you for playing with me," said Grandma Ruby. Her heart felt warm and happy.

"Thank you for sharing your boats," said Bear. His fuzzy face showed a big smile.

They carefully lifted their boats from the water. Bear helped Grandma Ruby dry them with a soft towel. Everything smelled clean and fresh.

"Will you play again tomorrow?" asked Grandma Ruby hopefully.

"Every day!" promised Bear. "Friends always play together."

They hugged gently. Bear's fur felt warm and comforting. Grandma Ruby's arms felt safe and loving.

From that day on, Grandma Ruby was never lonely. Every morning, Bear knocked on the bathroom door. They sailed boats in bubbly water. They told stories and shared giggles.

The bathroom became their special friendship place. It smelled like lemon soap and happiness. It sounded like splashing and laughter.

Sometimes they invited rubber duckies to join their games. Sometimes they built soap bubble mountains. But always, they played together.

Grandma Ruby learned that sharing games makes them twice as fun. Bear learned that gentle friends make the best adventures.

Their friendship grew stronger every single day. The bathroom walls heard countless happy stories. Two friends had found each other in the most wonderful way.

And they lived happily ever after, sailing toy boats and sharing endless giggles in their cozy bathroom kingdom.

The end.
