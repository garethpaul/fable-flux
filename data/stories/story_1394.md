# Story story_1394

**Woolly's Dinner Adventure**

The sun was setting behind the big red barn. Farmer Mae rang the dinner bell. "Ding-a-ling! Ding-a-ling!" All the animals knew what that meant. It was time to eat!

The cows mooed happily. The pigs oinked with joy. The chickens clucked and flapped their wings. But Woolly the sheep just stood in the corner of the pasture. Her fluffy white wool felt heavy. Her tummy felt funny.

"Come on, Woolly!" called her friend Baa-baa. "Farmer Mae has fresh hay for us!"

Woolly shook her head. "I don't want dinner," she said softly. "My tummy feels all twisty. I'm not hungry."

Baa-baa walked over to Woolly. "But dinner is the best part of the day! The hay smells so sweet. The water is cool and fresh."

Woolly looked at the ground. Her hooves made little marks in the dirt. "I just don't feel like eating," she whispered.

Farmer Mae noticed Woolly standing alone. She walked over with gentle steps. "What's wrong, little one?" she asked. Her voice was warm like honey.

"I don't want dinner," Woolly said. "My tummy feels strange."

Farmer Mae smiled kindly. She reached into her pocket. Out came something special. It was a small, shiny bell! The bell was purple and sparkled in the evening light.

"This is a magic dinner bell," Farmer Mae said. "It helps animals feel better about eating. Would you like to try it?"

Woolly's eyes grew wide. Magic? She had never seen a magic bell before! The bell tinkled softly in Farmer Mae's hand. It sounded like tiny raindrops on leaves.

"How does it work?" Woolly asked. Her ears perked up with curiosity.

"You hold it while you eat," Farmer Mae explained. "It makes everything taste extra special. And it helps nervous tummies feel calm."

Woolly looked at the pretty bell. It caught the last rays of sunlight. The purple color reminded her of spring flowers. Maybe dinner wouldn't be so bad with a magic bell.

"Okay," Woolly said quietly. "I'll try."

Farmer Mae tied the bell around Woolly's neck with a soft ribbon. The bell made gentle music as Woolly walked. Tinkle, tinkle, tinkle! It sounded happy and peaceful.

Woolly walked slowly to the feeding area. The other sheep smiled at her. "Your bell is so pretty!" said Baa-baa.

Woolly took a small bite of hay. The bell jingled softly. Suddenly, the hay tasted wonderful! It was sweet and crunchy. Her tummy began to feel better.

"This hay is delicious!" Woolly said with surprise. She took another bite. The bell sang its tiny song.

The cool water tasted perfect too. It made Woolly feel refreshed and happy. Her nervous feeling melted away like ice cream in sunshine.

Soon Woolly was eating with all her friends. The bell made beautiful music with every movement. The other animals gathered around to listen. Even the fireflies came to dance to the bell's sweet sound.

"I feel so much better!" Woolly said. Her tummy was full and content. The twisty feeling was completely gone.

Farmer Mae patted Woolly's soft wool. "The real magic wasn't in the bell," she said with a wink. "The magic was in trying something new. Sometimes we feel nervous about things. But when we're brave and try anyway, we often find they're wonderful!"

Woolly looked down at her special bell. It twinkled in the moonlight. She understood now. The bell helped her feel brave enough to try.

As the stars came out, all the animals settled down for the night. Woolly's bell made one last gentle tinkle.

"I can't wait for dinner tomorrow!" Woolly said to Baa-baa. "Maybe I'll help ring the dinner bell for everyone!"

Baa-baa smiled. "That sounds perfect, Woolly."

And under the starry sky, Woolly drifted off to sleep. She dreamed of purple bells, sweet hay, and all the wonderful dinners waiting for her tomorrow.

The end.
