# Story story_1067

**Blocks and Walk Time**

Every day at three o'clock, Miss Sarah rang the little bell. "Walk time!" she called to all the children. The playground filled with happy voices and running feet.

But Blocks sat quietly in the corner. He loved building tall towers with his wooden blocks. Red ones, blue ones, yellow ones too. Stack, stack, stack until they touched the ceiling! 

"Come on, Blocks," said his friend Emma. "Time to go outside!"

Blocks shook his head. "I want to build more towers. Walking is boring."

Miss Sarah walked over with a gentle smile. "The fresh air will feel good, Blocks. Your towers will wait for you."

Blocks sighed a big sigh. He put down his favorite red block. His feet felt heavy as he walked to the door.

Outside, the other children ran around the playground. They climbed on swings and went down slides. They laughed and played tag.

Blocks walked slowly behind Miss Sarah. The sun felt warm on his face. A gentle breeze tickled his nose. But he still missed his blocks.

"I wish I could build something out here," Blocks mumbled.

Miss Sarah heard him. "What did you say, sweetie?"

"I want to build towers. But there are no blocks outside."

Miss Sarah's eyes twinkled. "Look around, Blocks. What do you see?"

Blocks looked left and right. He saw grass and trees. He saw flowers and rocks. Then something caught his eye.

"Pinecones!" he said, pointing to the ground.

"That's right! What else?"

Blocks bent down and picked up smooth stones. "Rocks! Pretty rocks!"

"And over there?" Miss Sarah pointed to a pile of sticks.

"Sticks! Long ones and short ones!" Blocks felt excited now.

Miss Sarah smiled. "What could you build with those?"

Blocks' eyes grew wide. He had never thought about this before. He picked up three pinecones. Then he found some flat rocks.

"I can make a tower!" he said.

Blocks carefully placed a flat rock on the ground. Then he put a pinecone on top. Another rock, another pinecone. His outdoor tower grew taller and taller.

"Wow!" said Emma, running over. "That's beautiful, Blocks!"

Soon other children came to see. "Can we help?" asked Tommy.

"Yes!" said Blocks. "Find more rocks and pinecones!"

The children scattered around the playground. They brought back treasures from everywhere. Smooth pebbles, bumpy pinecones, and straight sticks.

Together, they built the most amazing tower ever. It had pinecone walls and stick bridges. Rock steps led to the very top.

"This is the best tower I ever made," said Blocks, clapping his hands.

A squirrel ran up to look at their creation. It sniffed the pinecones and chattered happily. A butterfly landed on the tallest stick.

"Even the animals like our tower!" laughed Emma.

Miss Sarah rang the little bell again. "Time to go inside now."

"Already?" said Blocks. "But we just started building!"

The other children groaned too. Nobody wanted walk time to end.

"Don't worry," said Miss Sarah. "Your outdoor tower will be here tomorrow. You can build more then."

Blocks looked at his wonderful creation one more time. The afternoon sun made the rocks sparkle. The pinecones smelled like Christmas trees.

"I can't wait for walk time tomorrow," said Blocks, skipping toward the door.

Inside, Blocks picked up his favorite red block. But now he had a new idea. Maybe he could build a tower that looked like the one outside!

He started stacking blocks in a new way. Red, then brown like pinecones. Blue like the sky above his outdoor tower.

"What are you building?" asked Emma.

"A memory tower," said Blocks proudly. "It reminds me of outside."

That night, Blocks dreamed about pinecones and rocks. He dreamed about sunshine and fresh air. He dreamed about building towers that reached the clouds.

The next day, when Miss Sarah rang the bell, Blocks was the first one at the door.

"Walk time!" he cheered, running outside to find more building treasures.

From that day on, Blocks loved both kinds of building. Inside blocks and outside blocks. Both made him happy in their own special way.

The end.
