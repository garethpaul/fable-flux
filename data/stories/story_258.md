# Lily and Baby Sam's Garden Adventure

In the sunny backyard garden, baby Sam sat alone on his soft blue blanket. The flowers were pretty. The grass was green. But Sam felt lonely.

He picked up his red toy truck. "Vroom, vroom," he said quietly. The truck rolled across the blanket. Sam sighed a little sigh.

Big sister Lily was reading inside. She heard Sam's quiet playing. She looked out the window. Sam looked so small and lonely.

"Maybe Sam wants company," Lily thought. She put down her book. She walked outside to the garden.

"Hi, baby Sam!" Lily called sweetly. "Can I play with you?"

Sam looked up. His eyes got bright. He clapped his tiny hands. "Li-li!" he babbled happily.

Lily sat down on the blanket. "What are you playing?" she asked.

Sam held up his red truck. "Vroom!" he said proudly.

"Oh, trucks!" said Lily. "I love trucks too! Should we build something?"

Sam nodded his head up and down. His wispy hair bounced.

Lily looked around the garden. She saw some smooth stones. She saw fallen twigs. She saw colorful leaves.

"Let's make a road!" Lily suggested. "We can work together!"

Sam watched as Lily gathered stones. She made a winding path. Sam crawled over to help. He picked up a small stone. It was smooth and warm.

"Good job, Sam!" Lily cheered. "You're helping!"

Sam giggled. He felt important and happy. Together, they placed more stones. The road grew longer and longer.

"Now we need bridges," said Lily. She showed Sam the twigs. "These can go across!"

Sam grabbed a twig. It was bumpy and brown. He put it over the stone road. Lily helped him balance it.

"Perfect bridge, Sam!" Lily said. "You're so smart!"

Sam beamed with pride. His chubby cheeks turned pink.

Next, they added leaves for decoration. Red leaves. Yellow leaves. Orange leaves. The road looked beautiful and colorful.

"Ready to drive?" asked Lily.

Sam picked up his red truck. "Vroom, vroom!" he said loudly.

Lily found a small toy car. "I'll follow you!" she said.

Sam's truck rolled down the stone road. Over the twig bridge. Around the leaf corners. Lily's car followed behind.

"Beep, beep!" called Lily. "This is fun!"

Sam laughed and laughed. His whole body wiggled with joy. Playing together was much better than playing alone.

They drove their vehicles everywhere. Under the rose bush. Around the bird bath. Through the vegetable garden.

"Let's make a parking lot!" suggested Lily.

They found a flat spot. They arranged leaves in squares. Each car got its own space.

"Park here, Sam," said Lily gently.

Sam carefully placed his truck. Right in the middle of the leaf square.

"You did it!" Lily clapped. "We make a great team!"

Sam clapped too. His little hands made soft sounds.

The afternoon sun felt warm. A gentle breeze rustled the flowers. Birds sang happy songs above them.

"I love playing with you," said Lily. "You have wonderful ideas!"

Sam crawled over to Lily. He gave her a big hug. His arms were short but strong.

"Li-li," he said softly. It was his special word for love.

"I love you too, Sam," said Lily. She hugged him back.

They sat together on the blanket. The garden felt peaceful and perfect. Their stone road sparkled in the sunlight.

"Tomorrow we can build more!" said Lily.

Sam nodded eagerly. He couldn't wait.

As the day ended, they packed up together. Lily helped Sam carry his truck. Sam helped Lily gather the stones.

Working together made everything easier. And much more fun.

That night, Sam fell asleep smiling. He dreamed of stone roads and twig bridges. And his wonderful big sister who made everything better.

Lily fell asleep happy too. Tomorrow would bring another garden adventure. Another day of cooperation and love.

The garden held their friendship like a precious flower. Growing stronger and more beautiful each day.

The end.
