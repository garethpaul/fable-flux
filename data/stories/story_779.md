# Story story_779

**Penny's Park Adventure**

Penny the little plane sat in her hangar. The sun was shining bright outside. Her pilot, Captain Sam, walked over with a big smile.

"Time for park time, Penny!" he said cheerfully.

Penny's propeller drooped down low. She did not want to go. The park was full of loud children. They always pointed and stared at her. Sometimes they even tried to climb on her wings.

"Do we have to go?" Penny asked sadly.

Captain Sam patted her shiny red nose. "The children are waiting for us. They love seeing planes up close."

Penny sighed with a whoosh sound. Her engine made a grumpy rumble. But she knew Captain Sam was right. They always visited the park on sunny days.

Captain Sam climbed into her cockpit. Penny's engine started with a reluctant sputter. She rolled slowly out of the hangar. The bright sunlight made her silver wings sparkle.

Up, up, up they went into the blue sky. White fluffy clouds danced around them. The wind felt cool and fresh. Penny started to feel a tiny bit better.

Down below, the park looked like a green carpet. Penny could see the playground and picnic tables. Lots of families were having fun together.

"There's our landing spot," Captain Sam pointed.

Penny circled the park three times. She landed gently on the soft grass. Her wheels made quiet rolling sounds.

Right away, children came running over. Their eyes were wide with wonder. Their faces were full of excitement.

"Wow! A real airplane!" shouted a little boy.

"She's so pretty!" said a girl with pigtails.

Penny felt nervous butterflies in her fuel tank. The children formed a circle around her. But something felt different today.

A tiny girl with curly hair stepped forward. She was very quiet and shy. Her big brown eyes looked scared.

"Can I touch the plane?" she whispered.

Captain Sam smiled kindly. "Of course you can, sweetie."

The little girl reached out slowly. Her small hand touched Penny's wing gently. It felt warm and soft, like a butterfly landing.

"She feels smooth," the girl said with wonder.

More children came closer. They all touched Penny's wings carefully. Their hands were gentle and kind. They whispered nice things to her.

"You're beautiful," said one boy.

"I wish I could fly like you," said another girl.

Penny's heart began to warm up. These children weren't scary at all. They were just curious and excited. They loved airplanes just like she loved flying.

Captain Sam told stories about their adventures. The children listened with sparkling eyes. They asked lots of questions about clouds and wind.

"Do you like flying?" asked the shy girl.

Penny's propeller spun happily. "I love flying more than anything!"

The children giggled at her spinning propeller. They clapped their hands with joy. Some of them pretended to be airplanes too. They spread their arms wide and made zooming sounds.

Penny watched them play and laugh. Their happiness made her feel warm inside. She realized something important. Sharing her love of flying made it even more special.

When it was time to leave, the children waved goodbye. The shy little girl blew Penny a kiss. Penny's engine purred with happiness.

"Thank you for coming!" the children called out.

As they flew home, Penny felt light as a feather. The sunset painted the sky orange and pink. Everything looked magical and beautiful.

"That was wonderful," Penny told Captain Sam.

"I knew you'd have fun," he replied.

Back in the hangar, Penny thought about the day. The children's smiling faces filled her heart with joy. She remembered their gentle touches and kind words.

"Captain Sam," Penny said sleepily. "Can we go back tomorrow?"

Captain Sam chuckled softly. "Of course we can, little plane."

Penny closed her eyes with a happy sigh. She dreamed of flying and children's laughter. Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough.

Sometimes the best adventures happen when we're brave enough to try something new.

The end.
