# Story story_597

**Lily and the Lonely Sheep**

In a cozy yellow kitchen, a fluffy white sheep sat alone. Her name was Snowball. She had soft, curly wool and kind brown eyes. The kitchen smelled like warm cookies and fresh bread.

Snowball pushed a red ball across the smooth floor. It rolled under the big wooden table. She walked slowly to get it. Her hooves made tiny clicking sounds on the tiles.

"This is no fun," Snowball said softly. She sighed and looked around the empty kitchen. The ball felt heavy in her mouth.

Just then, little Lily peeked around the corner. Lily had bouncy brown curls and bright green eyes. She wore a sunny yellow dress with tiny flowers on it.

"Hello," Lily whispered. She stepped into the warm kitchen. Her bare feet felt cool on the floor.

Snowball looked up with surprise. "Oh! Hello there." She dropped the red ball. It bounced twice and stopped.

Lily's eyes grew wide with wonder. "You can talk!" She clapped her small hands together. The sound echoed in the kitchen.

"Yes, I can," Snowball said with a shy smile. "But I have no one to play with. I feel very lonely."

Lily's face lit up like sunshine. "I love to play! Can I play with you?" She took a small step forward.

Snowball's tail wiggled with excitement. "Really? You want to play with me?" Her voice was full of hope.

"Yes!" Lily said. She ran over and gently petted Snowball's soft wool. It felt like touching a fluffy cloud.

Lily picked up the red ball. "What games do you like?" She tossed it gently to Snowball.

Snowball caught it and giggled. "I like rolling the ball. And hide and seek too!" She pushed the ball back with her nose.

They played catch for a while. The ball bounced back and forth between them. Lily laughed when Snowball's wool bounced as she moved.

"Let's play hide and seek now," Lily suggested. Her cheeks were pink from playing.

Snowball nodded eagerly. "I'll count first!" She closed her eyes tight. "One, two, three..."

Lily looked around the kitchen quickly. She spotted a big cabinet with open doors. She crawled inside and giggled quietly.

"Ten! Ready or not, here I come!" Snowball opened her eyes. She looked under the table first. Then behind the chairs.

She heard a tiny giggle from the cabinet. Snowball smiled and walked over slowly. "I wonder where Lily could be," she said loudly.

More giggles came from the cabinet. Snowball gently opened the door wider. "Found you!" she said happily.

Lily tumbled out, laughing. "Your turn to hide!" She covered her eyes with her hands.

Snowball looked around. She squeezed behind the big refrigerator. Her fluffy wool barely fit in the space.

"Coming to find you!" Lily called. She looked in all the places Snowball had looked before. Then she saw white wool peeking out from behind the fridge.

"There you are!" Lily said. She helped pull Snowball out gently.

They played many more games together. They rolled the ball in circles. They danced around the kitchen table. They even made up a song about friendship.

As the sun began to set, golden light filled the kitchen. Both friends were tired but very happy.

"I'm so glad you came to play," Snowball said. She nuzzled Lily's hand with her soft nose.

"Me too," Lily said. She hugged Snowball's woolly neck. "You're my best friend now."

"And you're mine," Snowball replied. Her eyes sparkled with joy.

From that day on, Lily visited the kitchen every afternoon. She and Snowball played together until bedtime. They were never lonely again.

The kitchen was always filled with laughter, games, and the warm feeling of true friendship. And whenever other children visited, Lily and Snowball welcomed them to join their fun.

Their friendship grew stronger every day, like flowers growing in a sunny garden.

The end.
