# Ellie Learns to Share

In a bright, cheerful nursery filled with colorful toys, lived a little elephant named Ellie. Her trunk was soft and gray. Her eyes sparkled like stars. The nursery had red blocks, yellow balls, and purple puzzles scattered everywhere.

Ellie loved playing with toys. She loved them so much that she wanted to keep them all to herself. When other animals came to play, Ellie would wrap her trunk around the toys and pull them close.

"Mine!" Ellie would trumpet loudly. "All mine!"

One sunny morning, Bunny hopped over to play. "Can I play with the red blocks?" asked Bunny sweetly.

Ellie shook her head. "No! My blocks!" She hugged all the blocks with her trunk.

Bunny's ears drooped down sadly. She hopped away to find something else to do.

Next, Mouse scurried over. "May I bounce the yellow ball?" squeaked Mouse hopefully.

"No way!" trumpeted Ellie. She rolled the ball under her big, round belly. "My ball!"

Mouse's whiskers twitched with disappointment. He scurried away with tiny, sad steps.

Then Monkey swung down from the ceiling. "Could we do the purple puzzle together?" chatted Monkey cheerfully.

"Never!" Ellie stomped her feet. "My puzzle!" She spread her body over all the puzzle pieces.

Monkey's tail drooped. He swung away slowly.

Soon, Ellie sat alone in the nursery. She had all the toys around her. But something felt wrong. The nursery was too quiet. Too lonely.

Ellie looked at her blocks. They weren't as fun without friends to build with. She bounced her ball. It felt boring all by herself. She tried the puzzle. The pieces seemed harder without help.

"This isn't fun anymore," Ellie said softly. Her voice echoed in the empty room.

Ellie thought and thought. She wiggled her trunk. She flapped her ears. She remembered how happy Bunny looked when asking about blocks. She remembered Mouse's excited squeak about the ball. She remembered Monkey's friendly chatter about puzzles.

"Maybe," Ellie whispered, "playing together is better than playing alone."

Ellie made a big decision. She picked up some red blocks with her trunk. She walked to where Bunny sat sadly in the corner.

"Bunny," called Ellie gently. "Would you like to build something with me?"

Bunny's ears perked up. "Really? You want to share?"

"Yes!" trumpeted Ellie happily. "Let's build a castle together!"

They stacked blocks high and tall. Red blocks, blue blocks, green blocks too! Their castle had towers and walls. It was the most beautiful castle ever!

"This is wonderful!" giggled Bunny. "Thank you for sharing, Ellie!"

Next, Ellie found Mouse. "Mouse, want to play ball with me?"

Mouse's eyes grew bright. "Oh yes, please!"

They rolled the yellow ball back and forth. They bounced it high. They caught it low. Mouse squeaked with joy. Ellie trumpeted with laughter.

"Sharing makes everything more fun!" squeaked Mouse happily.

Finally, Ellie called to Monkey. "Monkey, let's solve this puzzle together!"

Monkey swung down excitedly. "What a great idea!"

They worked side by side. Ellie found corner pieces. Monkey found middle pieces. Together, they completed a beautiful picture of a rainbow.

"We make a great team!" chatted Monkey proudly.

As the sun set through the nursery windows, all four friends sat together. They had built amazing things. They had played wonderful games. They had solved tricky puzzles.

"I learned something important today," said Ellie with a big, happy smile. "Sharing toys makes everything better. Playing with friends is the most fun of all!"

Bunny, Mouse, and Monkey all agreed. They hugged Ellie with their arms, paws, and wings.

From that day on, Ellie always shared her toys. The nursery filled with laughter, friendship, and joy. Ellie discovered that the best games happen when everyone plays together.

And every night, Ellie fell asleep with a warm, happy heart, dreaming of all the fun tomorrow would bring with her dear friends.

The End.
