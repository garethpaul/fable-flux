# Plane's Big Block Hunt

Little Plane loved playing at the playground every morning. His bright red wings sparkled in the sunshine. His propeller spun happily as he zoomed around.

"Time to build!" Plane said with excitement. He always brought his special blocks to the playground. The blocks were blue and yellow and green. They made the most wonderful clicking sounds when they fit together.

But today something was wrong. Very wrong.

Plane looked left. He looked right. He spun his propeller and looked again. "Where are my blocks?" he wondered aloud. His wings drooped a little bit.

The playground felt too quiet without his blocks. No clicking sounds. No colorful towers to build. Just empty sandbox space where his blocks should be.

"I must find them!" Plane declared. His propeller started spinning faster. "They have to be here somewhere!"

First, Plane zoomed over to the big slide. Maybe his blocks rolled down there? He looked under the curvy yellow slide. He found three acorns and a red leaf. But no blocks.

"Hmm," Plane hummed thoughtfully. His engine made soft puttering sounds.

Next, Plane flew to the swing set. The swings creaked gently in the breeze. Maybe his blocks were hiding behind the tall metal poles? Plane circled around each swing. He found a bouncy ball and two pretty feathers. Still no blocks.

Plane's propeller slowed down a little. This was harder than he thought!

"Don't give up!" he told himself. "Keep looking!"

Plane remembered something important. Yesterday, he had played near the sandbox with his friend Bunny. Maybe Bunny knew where the blocks went!

Plane zoomed across the playground. His red wings caught the morning light. There was Bunny, hopping near the monkey bars.

"Bunny! Bunny!" called Plane. "Have you seen my blocks?"

Bunny's soft ears perked up. "Your colorful building blocks?" she asked. "The ones that make clicking sounds?"

"Yes! Those ones!" Plane's propeller spun hopefully.

Bunny thought for a moment. She tapped her fuzzy paw on her chin. "I remember! Yesterday you were building near the big oak tree. You made a tall tower there!"

Plane's wings fluttered with excitement. "The oak tree! I forgot about the oak tree!"

Together, Plane and Bunny hurried to the shady oak tree. Its big green leaves rustled softly above them. The grass felt cool and tickly.

Plane looked around the tree trunk. Behind the thick brown bark, something colorful peeked out. Blue! Yellow! Green!

"My blocks!" Plane shouted joyfully. "They're here!"

The blocks had tumbled behind the tree when the wind blew yesterday. They were safe and sound, just waiting to be found.

Plane carefully gathered each block. Click, click, click! They made their happy sounds again. His propeller spun with pure joy.

"Thank you for helping me, Bunny!" said Plane. "I couldn't have found them without you!"

"That's what friends are for," smiled Bunny. Her whiskers twitched happily.

Plane and Bunny spent the morning building together. They made a castle with blue walls. They built a bridge with yellow blocks. They created a tower with green pieces on top.

Click, click, click! The blocks sang their building song.

As the sun climbed higher, Plane felt proud and happy. He had been worried about his lost blocks. But he didn't give up! He kept looking and looking. He asked for help when he needed it. And he found his blocks after all!

"Sometimes things seem lost," Plane said to Bunny. "But if we keep trying and ask friends for help, we can solve any problem!"

Bunny nodded wisely. "And it's more fun to solve problems together!"

Plane's propeller spun one more happy spin. Tomorrow he would remember to put his blocks in a safe place. But today, he would just enjoy building with his good friend.

The playground filled with the cheerful sounds of clicking blocks and laughing friends. It was turning out to be a wonderful day after all!

The end.
