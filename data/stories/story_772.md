# Story story_772

**Tilly Tiger and the Bedtime Pond**

Tilly Tiger lived by a pretty pond. The water sparkled blue in the sunshine. Lily pads floated like green boats. Tilly loved splashing and playing all day long.

But when the sun went down, Tilly had a big problem. She was scared of bedtime. The pond looked dark and spooky. Strange sounds came from the water. Plop! Splash! Croak!

"I don't want to sleep," Tilly whispered. Her orange fur stood up. Her black stripes seemed to wiggle. The moon made scary shadows on the water.

Every night, Tilly's mama called, "Time for bed, little tiger!" But Tilly would hide behind the tall grass. She would peek out with worried eyes.

One evening, Tilly decided to be brave. "I will fix this problem," she said. She had an idea for her first try.

Tilly found her favorite red blanket. She pulled it over her head. "Now I can't see the scary pond," she said. She curled up in a fuzzy ball.

But under the blanket, everything felt hot. Tilly couldn't breathe well. The night sounds seemed even louder. Hoot! Hoot! went an owl. Ribbit! Ribbit! sang the frogs.

"This isn't working," Tilly sighed. She threw off the blanket. Her fur felt all sweaty. She was still scared of the dark pond.

The next night, Tilly tried again. This time, she had a different idea. She remembered something important. Mama always said, "When you're scared, look for something good."

Tilly sat by the pond's edge. Her paws touched the cool water. Instead of hiding, she looked carefully. She listened with her pointed ears.

The moon made silver circles on the water. They danced and twinkled like stars. "How pretty," Tilly whispered. The scary shadows were really just tree branches swaying in the breeze.

Plop! A friendly frog jumped on a lily pad. "Hello, Tilly," croaked the frog. "I'm not scary. I'm just saying goodnight to my family."

Splash! A fish jumped up to catch a bug. The ripples spread out in perfect circles. "The fish are having dinner," Tilly giggled.

Hoot! Hoot! The owl called from a tall tree. "Goodnight, everyone," the owl seemed to say. "Sleep well and have sweet dreams."

Tilly's fear started melting away like ice cream in sunshine. The pond wasn't scary at all. It was full of friendly night creatures. They were all getting ready for bed, just like her.

"The pond is beautiful at night," Tilly said. She felt proud and brave. Her heart wasn't beating fast anymore. Her fur lay flat and smooth.

Mama Tiger found Tilly sitting peacefully by the water. "You look calm, little one," Mama said. Her voice was warm and gentle.

"I was scared of bedtime," Tilly explained. "But I tried two different ways to feel better. The second way worked perfectly."

"What did you learn?" asked Mama.

"When something scares me, I shouldn't hide," Tilly said. "I should look closer and find the good things. The pond has nice friends who say goodnight too."

Mama nuzzled Tilly's soft head. "You were very brave to keep trying. You solved your problem all by yourself."

From that night on, Tilly loved bedtime. She would sit by the pond and watch the moon dance on the water. She would wave to the frogs and fish. She would listen to the owl's gentle hoots.

"Goodnight, pond friends," Tilly would whisper. Then she would curl up in her cozy den. Her dreams were full of silver moonbeams and dancing water.

Tilly learned that being scared was okay. But trying different solutions was even better. When the first idea didn't work, she didn't give up. She tried again with a new plan.

Now Tilly tells other young animals, "If something scares you, don't hide. Look for the beautiful things. Keep trying until you find what works. You might discover something wonderful."

And every night, the pond sparkled peacefully under the stars.

The end.
