# Story story_1499

**Benny's Drum Walk**

Benny loved his shiny red drum. He played it every morning after breakfast. Tap-tap-tap went his drumsticks on the smooth surface.

"Time for walk time!" called Mama from the kitchen.

Benny's shoulders dropped. He looked at his drum sadly. "Do I have to go, Mama?"

"Yes, sweetie. Fresh air is good for you."

Benny dragged his feet to the door. He didn't want to leave his drum behind. Walking seemed so boring compared to making music.

Mama helped him put on his blue sneakers. "Maybe bring your drumsticks?" she suggested with a smile.

Benny's eyes lit up a little. He grabbed his wooden drumsticks and stuffed them in his pocket.

Outside, the sun felt warm on Benny's face. Birds chirped in the tall oak trees. But Benny still wished he was inside drumming.

They walked down the sidewalk slowly. Benny kicked a small pebble. It made a tiny clicking sound on the concrete.

"That sounded like music," Mama said.

Benny stopped walking. He listened carefully. The pebble did make a nice sound! He kicked it again. Click-click-click.

He pulled out one drumstick. He tapped it on the metal fence they were passing. Ting-ting-ting! The sound was bright and cheerful.

"What a lovely song!" said Mrs. Garcia from her garden.

Benny smiled. He tapped the fence again, making a rhythm. Ting-ting-tap, ting-ting-tap.

They kept walking. Benny discovered new sounds everywhere. He drummed on a wooden mailbox. Thump-thump-thump! He tapped a street sign. Ping-ping-ping!

"Listen, Mama!" Benny said excitedly. He drummed on different things as they walked. The mailbox, the fence, a trash can, a fire hydrant.

Each thing made its own special sound. The trash can was deep and hollow. The fire hydrant rang like a bell. The wooden fence post sounded warm and soft.

"You're making the whole neighborhood musical!" Mama laughed.

A squirrel chattered from a tree branch. Benny tried to copy the sound with his drumsticks on a park bench. Tap-tap-chatter, tap-tap-chatter.

The squirrel seemed to answer back! It chattered again, louder this time.

"I think he likes your music," Mama said.

They reached the park. Benny saw a big hollow log near the playground. His eyes grew wide. He ran over and drummed on it with both sticks.

BOOM-BOOM-BOOM! The sound was deep and rich. It echoed through the trees.

Other children heard the drumming. They came running over.

"Can we try?" asked a girl with curly hair.

"Can we make music too?" asked a boy with glasses.

Benny nodded happily. He showed them how to find good drumming spots. They used sticks and rocks to make rhythms on the log, the bench, and the jungle gym.

Soon the whole park filled with joyful sounds. The children laughed and drummed together. They made up songs about flowers and clouds and ice cream.

"This is the best walk ever!" Benny told Mama.

They drummed until it was time to go home. Benny felt sad to leave his new friends. But he felt excited too.

"Mama, can we take the same walk tomorrow?" he asked.

"Of course, my little musician," Mama said. She ruffled his hair gently.

On the way home, Benny noticed even more drumming spots. A metal gate, a wooden bridge, some clay pots on a porch. The whole world was like a giant drum set!

"I never knew walking could be so musical," Benny said.

"Everything can be musical if you listen carefully," Mama replied.

That night, Benny could hardly wait for tomorrow's walk time. He put his drumsticks by the front door so he wouldn't forget them.

As he drifted off to sleep, he dreamed of all the new sounds he would discover. Maybe he would find a puddle to splash in rhythm. Maybe the wind would help him make music in the leaves.

Walk time wasn't boring anymore. It was the best part of his day!

The end.
