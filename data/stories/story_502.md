# Tommy the Little Blue Train

In the cozy living room, Tommy the little blue train sat quietly on the soft carpet. His bright red wheels gleamed in the morning sunlight. Tommy loved to play with his colorful wooden blocks every day.

"Time to build!" Tommy said cheerfully. He looked around the living room. "Where are my blocks?"

Tommy rolled forward slowly. His wheels made a gentle *click-clack* sound on the hardwood floor. He searched under the big, fluffy sofa. No blocks there.

"Hmm," Tommy said, scratching his tiny chimney. "They must be somewhere!"

Tommy chugged over to the bookshelf. He peeked behind the tall books. Still no blocks. He checked inside the toy basket. Empty! Tommy's bright smile began to fade.

"Oh no," Tommy whispered. "Where could they be?"

Tommy felt sad and confused. He really wanted to build a tall tower today. Without his blocks, he couldn't play his favorite game.

But Tommy was a brave little train. "I won't give up!" he declared. "I'll keep looking everywhere!"

Tommy rolled to the kitchen doorway. *Click-clack, click-clack* went his wheels. He looked under the dining table. No blocks hiding there either.

"Maybe they're upstairs?" Tommy wondered aloud.

Tommy couldn't go upstairs by himself. He was too small. So he rolled back to the living room. He felt worried and tired.

"Think, Tommy, think!" he said to himself. "Where did I play yesterday?"

Tommy closed his eyes tight. He remembered building a castle yesterday afternoon. He remembered putting his blocks away. But where?

Suddenly, Tommy heard a soft *thump* from the coat closet. His blue eyes opened wide. "What was that sound?"

Tommy rolled over to the closet door. It was slightly open. He pushed it with his nose. *Creak* went the door.

Inside the dark closet, Tommy saw something colorful. "My blocks!" he shouted happily.

But the blocks were on a high shelf. Tommy couldn't reach them. He felt disappointed again.

"I need help," Tommy realized. "That's okay!"

Tommy rolled to find Mama. She was in the garden, watering her pretty flowers.

"Mama!" Tommy called. "I found my blocks! But they're too high up!"

Mama smiled warmly. "Good job looking everywhere, Tommy! I'll help you get them down."

Together, they went to the closet. Mama carefully lifted down the wooden box of blocks. The blocks made a cheerful *clatter* as they moved.

"Here you go, sweet train," Mama said. She placed the box on the carpet.

Tommy's face lit up with joy. "Thank you, Mama! I looked and looked!"

"You never gave up," Mama said proudly. "That's what good problem-solvers do!"

Tommy opened his box of blocks. Red ones, blue ones, yellow ones, and green ones! They felt smooth and cool in his hands.

"Now I can build!" Tommy said excitedly.

Tommy started stacking his blocks carefully. One on top of another. The tower grew taller and taller. *Click* went each block as he placed it.

"Look, Mama! I'm building a rainbow tower!"

Mama clapped her hands. "Beautiful, Tommy! You worked so hard to find your blocks."

Tommy felt proud and happy. His tower reached almost to his chimney! The bright colors made him smile big.

"I'm glad I didn't give up," Tommy said. "Even when I felt sad, I kept trying!"

Tommy rolled around his magnificent tower. The afternoon sun made his blue paint sparkle. His blocks made wonderful shadows on the carpet.

That evening, Tommy carefully put his blocks back in their box. This time, he placed them on the low shelf where he could reach them.

"Tomorrow I'll build a bridge!" Tommy said sleepily.

As the stars came out, Tommy felt grateful. He had learned something important today. When problems seem big, keep trying different solutions. Ask for help when you need it. Never give up on what makes you happy.

Tommy closed his eyes peacefully. His blocks were safe and ready for tomorrow's adventures. And Tommy was ready too, feeling proud of his persistence and problem-solving skills.
