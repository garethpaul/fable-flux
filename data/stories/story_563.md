# Story story_563

**Benny's Magic Dinner**

The sun was setting behind the tall oak trees. Mama Bunny called from the kitchen. "Benny! Time for dinner!"

Benny Bunny sat under his favorite bush. He wiggled his pink nose. He didn't want to eat dinner. His tummy felt funny and twisty.

"I'm not hungry," Benny said softly. He touched his fluffy white belly. It made quiet rumbling sounds.

Mama Bunny hopped outside. Her brown ears twitched gently. "What's wrong, little one?"

"Dinner is boring," Benny sighed. "I don't like green things. They taste yucky."

Mama smiled with her warm eyes. "Come inside, sweetie. Let's see what we can do."

Benny followed Mama slowly. His little paws dragged on the soft grass. The kitchen smelled like fresh carrots and herbs.

On the table sat a big wooden bowl. It was filled with orange carrots and green lettuce. Everything looked very plain to Benny.

"Sit down, my dear," Mama said. She patted the small chair with a soft cushion.

Benny climbed up. He stared at his plate. The carrots looked like orange sticks. The lettuce looked like boring green leaves.

Suddenly, something sparkled in the bowl. Benny blinked his dark eyes. A tiny fairy appeared! She had silver wings and a blue dress.

"Hello, Benny!" the fairy giggled. Her voice sounded like tiny bells. "I'm Luna, the Dinner Fairy!"

Benny's mouth opened wide. "A real fairy? In our kitchen?"

Luna danced around the carrots. Where she touched them, they began to glow. "These aren't just carrots," she whispered. "They're magic wands!"

The carrots started changing colors. Some turned purple and blue. Others became yellow and pink. They sparkled like rainbow jewels.

"And this lettuce," Luna said, spinning around the green leaves. "These are flying carpets for tiny adventures!"

The lettuce leaves lifted up gently. They floated around the bowl like soft green clouds. Benny giggled with delight.

"Can I touch them?" Benny asked. His eyes grew bright and curious.

"Of course!" Luna laughed. "But first, you must take a magical bite!"

Benny picked up a glowing purple carrot. It felt cool and smooth. He took a small nibble. It tasted sweet and crunchy! Much better than he remembered.

"Wow!" Benny said. "It tastes like sunshine!"

He grabbed a floating lettuce leaf. It was crisp and fresh. It tasted like morning dew and garden rain.

Luna clapped her tiny hands. "The magic works when you try new things!"

Benny ate another carrot. Then another lettuce leaf. Each bite tasted better than the last. His tummy felt happy and warm.

"The magic is inside you," Luna explained. "When you're brave and try new foods, everything tastes wonderful!"

Mama Bunny watched from the doorway. She smiled but didn't see the fairy. Only Benny could see Luna's special magic.

Soon, Benny's plate was empty. His belly felt full and satisfied. The twisty feeling was completely gone.

Luna gave Benny a tiny hug. "Remember, little bunny. Every meal can be an adventure!"

She disappeared in a shower of golden sparkles. The kitchen looked normal again. But Benny felt different inside.

"Mama!" Benny called excitedly. "Dinner was amazing! The carrots tasted like sunshine! The lettuce was like flying clouds!"

Mama Bunny hugged him tight. "I'm so proud of you for trying everything!"

Benny helped clear the dishes. He hummed a happy song. His paws felt light and bouncy.

At bedtime, Benny snuggled in his cozy burrow. He thought about Luna and the magical dinner. Maybe the magic wasn't really from the fairy. Maybe it came from being brave.

"Mama," Benny whispered. "What's for dinner tomorrow?"

Mama kissed his forehead. "Fresh broccoli and sweet corn."

Benny smiled sleepily. "I can't wait! Tomorrow will be another magical adventure!"

He closed his eyes and dreamed of rainbow vegetables and flying food. His tummy felt perfectly happy. Dinner time would never be boring again.

The end.
