# Story story_821

**Benny's Art Adventure**

It was Tuesday morning at Sunny Hill School. Miss Clara clapped her hands together. "Time for art class, everyone!" she said with a big smile.

All the children jumped up from the reading circle. They ran to the art table with bright eyes. Crayons, paint brushes, and colorful paper covered the long wooden table.

But Benny the beagle puppy stayed on his soft blue mat. His tail drooped down low. His ears hung sadly by his face.

"Come on, Benny!" called his friend Emma. "Art time is starting!"

Benny shook his furry head. "I don't like art," he whispered. "My paws are too big. I can't hold the brushes right."

Miss Clara walked over to Benny's mat. She knelt down beside him. "What's wrong, sweet pup?" she asked gently.

"Art is too hard for me," Benny said. His brown eyes looked worried. "Everyone else makes pretty pictures. Mine always look messy."

Miss Clara smiled warmly. "Would you like to try something special today?" she asked. "I have a new idea just for you."

Benny's ears perked up a tiny bit. "What kind of idea?"

Miss Clara brought over a big white paper. She set it on the floor next to Benny. Then she got small dishes of paint. Red, blue, yellow, and green paint sparkled in the dishes.

"Today we're making paw print art," Miss Clara explained. "You can dip your paws in the paint. Then step on the paper!"

Benny sniffed the paint dishes. The colors smelled fresh and clean. "Will it feel funny?" he asked.

"Let's find out together," Miss Clara said.

Benny put one paw in the blue paint. It felt cool and squishy between his toes. He giggled. "It tickles!"

He stepped on the white paper. His blue paw print appeared like magic. "Look!" Benny barked happily. "I made a mark!"

Emma came over to watch. "That's so cool, Benny!" she said.

Benny tried the red paint next. Then the yellow. Soon his paper had colorful paw prints all over it. Each print was different and special.

"I want to make a rainbow!" Benny said excitedly.

He dipped his paws in different colors. Step, step, step across the paper. Red prints, then orange, then yellow. The colors mixed and swirled together beautifully.

Other children gathered around Benny's artwork. "Wow!" they said. "It looks like flowers!" "I see butterflies!" "Those look like happy suns!"

Benny's tail wagged faster and faster. His artwork really was beautiful. The colors danced across the white paper like a party.

"Can I try the green now?" Benny asked Miss Clara.

"Of course!" she said. "This is your special art time."

Benny made green paw prints around the edges. They looked like leaves on a tree. In the middle, his red and yellow prints looked like bright flowers blooming.

"I love my painting!" Benny barked joyfully.

Miss Clara helped Benny wash his paws in warm soapy water. The water felt nice and bubbly. "You're a wonderful artist, Benny," she said.

When art time ended, Miss Clara hung Benny's painting on the wall. Right next to Emma's drawing and Sam's clay pot.

Benny sat back and looked at his colorful creation. His chest felt warm and proud. "I really am an artist," he said to himself.

Emma patted Benny's head. "Your painting is my favorite," she said. "The colors are so happy!"

As the children cleaned up, Benny helped put away the paint brushes. His tail wagged the whole time.

"Miss Clara," Benny said, "what will we make tomorrow?"

Miss Clara laughed. "Maybe we'll try finger painting," she said. "Or clay sculptures!"

Benny's eyes sparkled with excitement. "I can't wait!" he said. "Art time is the best time!"

That night, Benny dreamed of all the beautiful things he would create. Colorful paintings, fun sculptures, and magical artwork filled his happy dreams.

Art time wasn't scary anymore. It was wonderful.

The end.
