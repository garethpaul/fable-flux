# Teddy and the Stubborn Jar

Teddy the bear loved playing in Grandma's garden. The flowers smelled sweet like honey. The grass felt soft under his fuzzy paws. Today, the sun made everything sparkle and shine.

"What a perfect day for adventure!" Teddy said happily.

He wandered between the tall sunflowers. Their yellow faces smiled down at him. Near the tomato plants, something caught his eye. A shiny glass jar sat on the wooden garden table.

Teddy climbed onto the bench. Inside the jar were colorful flower seeds. They looked like tiny rainbows waiting to grow. A note was taped to the jar: "Special seeds for Teddy - Love, Grandma."

"Oh wow!" Teddy gasped with excitement. "I can plant my own flowers!"

He wrapped his paws around the jar lid. He twisted and turned with all his might. The lid wouldn't budge. Not even a little bit.

"Come on, jar," Teddy huffed. "Please open!"

He tried again, pushing and pulling. His face turned red from trying so hard. The jar stayed firmly closed.

Teddy sat down feeling frustrated. "This is too hard," he sighed. A butterfly landed on his nose, tickling him gently.

"Maybe I need a different way," Teddy thought aloud.

He looked around the garden. The wheelbarrow was too big to help. The watering can was too heavy to lift. Then he spotted Grandma's gardening gloves hanging on a hook.

"Those might give me a better grip!"

Teddy pulled on the soft green gloves. They were much too big for his small paws. But the bumpy rubber dots felt rough and grippy.

He grabbed the jar again. This time, he could hold on better. He twisted the lid slowly and steadily. It moved just a tiny bit!

"I felt it move!" Teddy cheered.

He kept turning, little by little. The lid made small squeaking sounds. Squeak, squeak, squeak. Each turn was easier than the last.

But then the lid got stuck again. Teddy's shoulders drooped with disappointment.

"I almost had it," he whispered sadly.

Just then, Mrs. Robin landed on the table. She tilted her red head at Teddy.

"Sometimes," she chirped sweetly, "asking for help is the smartest thing to do."

Teddy thought about her words. He looked toward the house where Grandma was baking cookies. The warm smell drifted through the garden.

"Grandma!" Teddy called, waving his paws. "Could you help me, please?"

Grandma came outside, wiping flour from her hands. Her eyes twinkled when she saw Teddy with the jar.

"Having trouble with that lid, sweetie?" she asked kindly.

"I tried really, really hard," Teddy explained. "I even used your gloves! But it's still stuck."

"You did so well trying different ways," Grandma said proudly. "Sometimes jars need two sets of hands."

Together, they held the jar. Grandma's strong hands helped guide Teddy's small paws. With one final twist, the lid popped off with a satisfying *pop*!

"We did it!" Teddy jumped up and down with joy.

The seeds sparkled in the afternoon sunlight. Red ones, blue ones, purple ones, and yellow ones. They looked like tiny jewels in Teddy's paws.

"Thank you for helping me, Grandma," Teddy said, giving her a big, fuzzy hug.

"You never gave up, Teddy," Grandma replied warmly. "You tried different ways and asked for help when you needed it. That's what smart problem-solvers do."

Together, they planted the seeds in a special corner of the garden. Teddy patted the soft dirt gently with his paws. He watered them with the small watering can.

"Soon these will grow into beautiful flowers," Grandma said.

Teddy smiled proudly at his new garden patch. He had learned that some problems need patience, creativity, and sometimes a helping hand. But most importantly, he should never give up trying.

As the sun set, Teddy felt warm and happy inside. Tomorrow, he would check on his seeds and maybe learn something new in the garden.

"I can't wait to see what grows!" he said with a big, contented smile.

The end.
