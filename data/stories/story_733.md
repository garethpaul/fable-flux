# Story story_733

**The Muddy Block Adventure**

Benny the Block loved playing outside. He was bright red and perfectly square. Every day, he rolled around the grassy field with his friends.

Today was extra special. It had rained last night. The field was full of wonderful, squishy mud puddles.

"Wheee!" shouted Benny as he rolled through a big brown puddle. Splash! Mud covered his shiny red sides.

His friend Bella the Blue Block giggled. "Look at me!" she called. She jumped into an even bigger puddle. Splat! Now she was brown all over too.

Green Block Gary rolled by fast. "This is the best day ever!" he laughed. Soon he was covered in thick, gooey mud from top to bottom.

The three blocks played all morning. They rolled through puddles. They slid down muddy hills. They had mud fights and made mud pies.

When the sun got high and hot, Mama Block called from the house. "Time to come in for lunch, little blocks!"

The three friends looked at each other. They were covered in mud from head to toe. Their bright colors were hidden under layers of brown goop.

"Oh no," whispered Benny. "Mama will want us to take a bath!"

"I don't want a bath," said Bella. Her voice was small and worried.

"Baths are scary," agreed Gary. "The water is too wet and soapy."

They slowly rolled toward the house. Their muddy sides left brown trails in the grass.

Mama Block saw them coming. Her eyes grew wide. "My goodness! You three need a good bath right away."

"No, no, no!" cried Benny. "We don't want a bath!"

"Please, Mama," begged Bella. "Can't we stay muddy?"

Mama Block smiled gently. "I understand you're nervous. But look at yourselves. You can't even see your beautiful colors anymore."

The blocks looked down. Mama was right. They couldn't see any red, blue, or green at all.

"I have an idea," said Mama. "What if we make bath time fun? You can bring your favorite rubber ducky."

"But baths are still scary," said Gary softly.

Mama thought for a moment. "How about this? What if I run the water very slowly? And we use the gentlest soap that smells like strawberries?"

The blocks looked at each other. That sounded a little better.

"And," Mama continued, "you can all take a bath together. Friends make everything less scary."

Benny felt a tiny bit braver. "Will you stay with us the whole time?"

"Of course," promised Mama. "I'll be right there."

They went to the bathroom together. Mama filled the big tub with warm, shallow water. She added just a little strawberry soap. Sweet bubbles danced on top.

"It smells like summer," said Bella, sniffing the air.

Gary touched the water with one corner. "It's nice and warm," he said, surprised.

Benny was the first to roll in. The warm water felt gentle on his muddy sides. "This isn't scary at all!"

Soon all three blocks were splashing happily. The brown mud swirled away in pretty patterns. Their bright colors appeared again like magic.

"Look!" laughed Bella. "I'm blue again!"

"And I'm green!" shouted Gary.

"My red is so shiny now," said Benny proudly.

They played with rubber duckies and made bubble beards. The strawberry soap made everything smell wonderful.

When bath time was over, they were clean and sparkling. Their colors were brighter than ever.

"That was actually fun," admitted Gary.

"I'm not scared of baths anymore," said Bella.

"Can we take another bath tomorrow?" asked Benny.

Mama Block hugged them all. "See? Sometimes things that seem scary are actually quite nice. You were very brave to try."

The three friends rolled outside to play again. But this time, they didn't mind getting dirty. They knew that bath time could be fun too.

And from that day on, they never worried about getting muddy. Because they knew that getting clean could be just as much fun as getting dirty.

The end.
