# Zara the Zebra Finds Her Voice

Zara the zebra loved books very much. She visited the big, bright library every week. The library smelled like old paper and new adventures. Sunlight streamed through tall windows. It made rainbow patterns on the floor.

But Zara had a big problem. She was very, very shy. When she needed help, her voice felt stuck. Her black and white stripes seemed to tremble. Her heart went *thump-thump-thump* so loudly!

Today, Zara wanted to find a special book. It was about zebras who could fly. She had seen it once before. But now she couldn't remember where it was.

Zara wandered through the tall shelves. She looked up high and down low. She checked the picture book section twice. She peeked behind the reading chairs. No flying zebra book anywhere!

The kind librarian, Mrs. Owl, sat at her desk. She had fluffy gray feathers and gentle eyes. Zara knew Mrs. Owl could help her. But asking felt so scary!

Zara's hooves made soft *clip-clop* sounds. She walked toward Mrs. Owl's desk slowly. When she got close, her courage disappeared. She turned around and walked away quickly.

"Maybe I can find it myself," Zara whispered.

She tried looking again. She searched every single shelf. She checked under cushions and behind plants. Still no flying zebra book! Her shoulders drooped with disappointment.

Zara sat in a cozy reading corner. She felt sad and frustrated. A little tear rolled down her striped face.

Just then, a friendly mouse appeared. His name was Max. He had read many books too.

"Why do you look so sad?" Max asked gently.

Zara took a deep breath. Talking to Max felt easier somehow. "I can't find my favorite book. It's about zebras who fly. I want to ask Mrs. Owl for help. But I feel too shy."

Max's whiskers twitched thoughtfully. "Being shy is okay," he said. "But asking for help is brave. Mrs. Owl loves helping everyone find books."

"What if she's too busy?" Zara worried.

"Mrs. Owl is never too busy for book lovers," Max smiled. "Want me to come with you?"

Zara nodded gratefully. Having a friend helped her feel stronger.

Together, they walked to Mrs. Owl's desk. Zara's heart still went *thump-thump-thump*. But Max gave her an encouraging smile.

"Excuse me, Mrs. Owl," Zara said quietly. Her voice was soft as a whisper.

Mrs. Owl looked up with twinkling eyes. "Hello, dear Zara! How can I help you today?"

"I'm looking for a book," Zara said. Her voice got a little stronger. "It's about zebras who can fly."

"Oh, what a wonderful choice!" Mrs. Owl hooted happily. "That book is in our special new arrivals section. Follow me!"

Mrs. Owl led them to a bright display. There was the flying zebra book! It had a beautiful cover with zebras soaring through puffy white clouds.

"Thank you so much!" Zara said. Her stripes practically glowed with happiness.

"You're very welcome," Mrs. Owl said warmly. "Never hesitate to ask for help. That's what I'm here for!"

Zara hugged the book close. She felt proud and relieved. Asking for help hadn't been scary at all!

She found a sunny spot to read. Max curled up beside her. The book was even more wonderful than she remembered. The zebras flew over mountains and oceans. They had amazing adventures together.

When it was time to leave, Zara felt different. She felt braver and more confident. She had learned something important today.

"Thank you for helping me be brave," she told Max.

"You were brave all by yourself," Max replied. "You just needed to discover it."

Zara walked home with her book. Her hooves made happy *clip-clop* sounds. She couldn't wait to return next week. And next time, she would ask for help right away!

Sometimes being brave means asking for help. And that's perfectly wonderful.
