# Story story_1104

**The Big Friend and the Little Friend**

Ellie the elephant walked slowly through the sunny field. Her big gray feet made soft *thump, thump* sounds on the grass. She picked up a stick with her long trunk and tossed it high in the air.

*Whoosh!* The stick flew up and came back down. Ellie caught it and smiled a little. But playing alone wasn't very fun.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," Ellie said sadly. Her voice was deep and rumbly like thunder far away.

High up in the blue sky, Pip the little bird heard Ellie's words. Pip had bright yellow feathers that sparkled in the sunshine. His tiny heart felt sorry for the lonely elephant.

"Maybe I could be her friend," Pip chirped to himself. He flapped his small wings and flew down, down, down to the green field.

But when Pip landed near Ellie, he felt very small. Ellie was SO big! Her foot was bigger than Pip's whole body. Her trunk was longer than ten birds standing in a row.

"Um, hello," Pip said in his tiny voice. "Can I play with you?"

Ellie looked around with her kind brown eyes. "Who said that?" she asked.

"Down here!" Pip called out, jumping up and down. "I'm Pip the bird!"

Ellie bent her huge head down low. There was the smallest, brightest bird she had ever seen!

"Oh my," Ellie said gently. "You want to play with me? But I'm so big and you're so little."

Pip's chest puffed out with pride. "Size doesn't matter for friends!" he chirped bravely.

Ellie thought about this. She picked up her stick again. "Want to play catch?" she asked.

Pip tried to catch the big stick, but it was too heavy. *Bonk!* It knocked him right over into the soft grass.

"Oops! Sorry!" Ellie said quickly. "Maybe that stick is too big."

Pip brushed off his feathers and smiled. "Let's find something better!"

They looked around the field together. Pip spotted something perfect - a small, round pebble that sparkled like a tiny star.

"How about this?" Pip asked, picking up the pebble in his orange beak.

Ellie gently took the pebble with the tip of her trunk. It was just the right size! She tossed it softly to Pip.

Pip caught it and threw it back. "This is fun!" he sang.

Soon they were laughing and playing catch perfectly. The pebble sailed back and forth between the big elephant and the little bird.

"Your turn to pick a game," Ellie said, setting the pebble down carefully.

Pip thought hard. "Let's play hide and seek!"

Ellie covered her eyes with her big ears. "One, two, three..." she counted in her rumbly voice.

Pip flew to hide behind a yellow dandelion. The flower was just his size - perfect for hiding!

"Ready or not, here I come!" Ellie called.

She looked behind rocks and trees. She checked under leaves and bushes. Finally, she spotted a tiny bit of yellow feather peeking out from the dandelion.

"Found you!" Ellie laughed.

Pip giggled and flew up to sit on Ellie's head. "Now you hide!"

But where could a big elephant hide in an open field?

Ellie tried hiding behind a small tree. Her bottom stuck out. She tried hiding behind a rock. Her trunk hung over the top.

Pip pretended he couldn't see her. "Where could Ellie be?" he wondered out loud, flying in circles.

Ellie giggled. Her whole body shook when she laughed.

"There you are!" Pip said, landing on her trunk. "You're the silliest hider ever!"

They played until the sun started to set. The sky turned pink and orange like a beautiful painting.

"This was the best day," Ellie said happily. "I'm so glad you wanted to be my friend."

Pip snuggled into the warm, soft spot behind Ellie's ear. "Me too. We make a great team!"

From that day on, Ellie and Pip played in the field every single day. The big elephant and the little bird were the very best of friends.

And they learned that friendship comes in all sizes - big and small, just like them.

The end.
