# Story story_1103

**Benny's Bedtime Adventure**

The sun was setting behind the trees. Orange and pink colors painted the sky. It was time for little Benny to go to bed.

"Come inside, Benny!" called Mom from the porch. "Time to put your bike away."

Benny looked at his shiny red bike. He had been riding all day. The bike's bell still sparkled in the evening light. Benny's bike was his best friend.

"I don't want to go to bed," Benny said softly. He patted his bike's handlebars. "My bike doesn't want to sleep either."

Benny slowly walked his bike to the garage. The garage felt dark and quiet. He didn't like leaving his bike alone.

"Good night, bike," Benny whispered. He gave the seat a gentle pat.

As Benny turned to leave, something magical happened. His bike began to glow! Soft blue light came from the spokes. The light was warm and friendly.

"Don't be sad, Benny," said a tiny voice. It was coming from his bike! "I have something special to show you."

Benny's eyes grew wide with wonder. His bike was talking to him!

"While you sleep, I go on adventures," said the bike. "Would you like to see?"

The garage filled with twinkling stars. They weren't scary stars. They were gentle and bright. The stars danced around Benny and his bike.

"Where do you go?" asked Benny.

"I ride through dream clouds," said the bike. "I visit sleeping animals in the forest. I help the moon light up the night sky."

Benny watched as pictures appeared in the starlight. He saw his bike rolling through fluffy white clouds. Sleepy rabbits waved from their burrows. Owls hooted hello from tall trees.

"The night is full of friends," said the bike. "While you dream, I make sure everyone is safe and happy."

"That sounds wonderful," said Benny. His eyes felt heavy and sleepy.

"When you rest, I get strong for tomorrow," explained the bike. "Sleep helps us both have energy for new adventures."

Benny yawned a big yawn. The magical stars began to fade. But he wasn't sad anymore. He felt excited and peaceful.

"Will you tell me about your night adventures tomorrow?" asked Benny.

"Of course," said the bike. "Sweet dreams, my friend."

Benny walked back to the house. His feet felt light and happy. Mom was waiting with his favorite pajamas. They had little bicycles printed on them.

"Did you say good night to your bike?" asked Mom.

"Yes," said Benny with a big smile. "And my bike told me about its night job!"

Mom smiled and helped Benny brush his teeth. The toothpaste tasted like mint. It made his mouth feel fresh and clean.

In his cozy bed, Benny pulled up his soft blanket. It smelled like sunshine from hanging outside. His pillow felt fluffy under his head.

"Mom, did you know bikes have adventures at night?" asked Benny.

"That sounds magical," said Mom. She kissed his forehead. "What kind of adventures?"

"My bike helps take care of everyone while we sleep," said Benny proudly. "It rides through clouds and visits forest friends."

"Your bike sounds very special," said Mom. "Just like you."

Benny closed his eyes. He could almost hear his bike's wheels turning softly in the garage. The sound was like a gentle lullaby.

He dreamed of riding through starlit clouds. In his dream, he and his bike visited sleeping deer and rabbits. They helped the moon shine bright silver light.

When morning came, Benny jumped out of bed. He ran to the garage in his pajamas. His bike was waiting, shiny and ready.

"Good morning, bike!" said Benny. "Thank you for your night work!"

The bike seemed to sparkle extra bright in the morning sun.

Benny couldn't wait for another day of riding. And tonight, he would go to bed happily. He knew his bike would have wonderful adventures while he slept.

Bedtime wasn't scary anymore. It was the start of magical dreams.

The end.
