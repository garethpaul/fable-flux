# The Best Day at the Swing Set

Lily sat on the tall swing. Her legs dangled down below. The swing moved back and forth slowly. Back and forth, back and forth.

The playground was very quiet today. Most children were inside having lunch. Lily pushed herself gently with her toes. The swing squeaked softly in the warm breeze.

"I wish someone would play with me," Lily whispered. She looked around the empty playground. The slide stood silent. The monkey bars waited for little hands. Even the merry-go-round sat still.

Lily sighed and dragged her feet. The swing stopped moving completely.

Just then, footsteps crunched on the wood chips. A girl with curly brown hair walked over. She wore a bright yellow dress with tiny flowers.

"Hi!" the girl said with a big smile. "I'm Emma. Can I swing too?"

Lily's face lit up immediately. "Yes! I'm Lily. I was hoping someone would come!"

Emma climbed onto the swing next to Lily. She gripped the chains tightly. "Want to see who can swing higher?" Emma asked.

"That sounds fun!" Lily replied. She pushed off hard with her feet.

Both girls began pumping their legs. Up and down, up and down. Their swings went higher and higher. The wind rushed through their hair.

"Look how high I am!" Emma called out, laughing.

"Me too!" Lily shouted back. "This is amazing!"

They swung back and forth together. Sometimes they were high at the same time. Sometimes one was up while the other was down. They looked like they were dancing in the air.

"Let's try something else," Emma suggested. She slowed her swing down carefully. "Want to swing at exactly the same time?"

"How do we do that?" Lily asked.

"We count together," Emma explained. "On three, we both push off. One, two, three!"

Both girls pushed off together. Their swings moved in perfect rhythm. Back and forth, back and forth, exactly together.

"We're like twin swings!" Lily giggled.

"This is even more fun!" Emma agreed.

They practiced their synchronized swinging. Sometimes they missed the timing. But they kept trying together. Soon they were swinging perfectly in sync.

"I have another idea," Lily said excitedly. "Want to make music while we swing?"

"Music?" Emma asked curiously.

"Listen," Lily said. She began humming as she swung. "La la la, la la la."

Emma joined in immediately. "La la la, la la la."

Their voices blended beautifully together. The rhythm matched their swinging perfectly. Other children began gathering around to listen.

"That's so pretty!" called a boy from the slide.

"Can you teach us?" asked a girl by the monkey bars.

Soon, five children were swinging and singing together. The whole playground filled with happy music. Everyone was laughing and having fun.

"This is the best day ever," Emma told Lily.

"It's so much better with friends," Lily agreed.

When it was time to go inside, both girls were sad to stop. But they had made plans for tomorrow.

"Want to meet here again?" Emma asked hopefully.

"Yes!" Lily said. "Maybe we can teach more children our swing song."

"And we can try new games together," Emma added.

They walked toward the school building side by side. Both girls were smiling from ear to ear.

"Thank you for playing with me," Lily said.

"Thank you for sharing your swing," Emma replied.

The next day, Lily arrived at the playground early. She saved the swing next to hers. When Emma arrived, her face broke into a huge grin.

"Ready for another adventure?" Emma asked.

"With my best friend? Always!" Lily answered.

They climbed onto their swings together. Soon their laughter filled the air once again. The playground was alive with friendship and joy.

From that day on, Lily and Emma met at the swings every day. They discovered that everything was more fun when shared with a friend. The lonely swing set had become their special place of friendship.

And whenever other children looked lonely, Lily and Emma always invited them to join their swinging adventures.

The end.
