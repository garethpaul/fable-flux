# Story story_590

**Benny's Big Adventure**

Benny loved his cozy bookshelf. He sat between his best friends every day. The fairy tale book sparkled on his left. The picture book giggled on his right.

"Time for park time!" called Miss Sarah.

All the books wiggled with excitement. They loved going to the park with the children. But Benny felt worried.

"I don't want to go," Benny whispered. "What if the children don't pick me?"

Benny was a book about numbers. He thought he was boring. The fairy tale book had princesses. The picture book had funny animals. What did Benny have? Just numbers.

Miss Sarah picked up all the books carefully. She put them in her big blue bag. Benny bounced around as they walked to the park.

The sun felt warm and bright. Birds sang happy songs in the trees. Children ran and played on the soft green grass.

Miss Sarah spread out a rainbow blanket. She placed all the books in a neat row. Children came running over with big smiles.

"Ooh, a princess story!" said Emma. She grabbed the fairy tale book.

"Look at the silly monkey!" laughed Jake. He picked up the picture book.

Benny sat alone on the blanket. His pages felt heavy and sad. No one wanted a number book at the park.

Then a little girl with curly hair walked over. Her name was Lily. She looked at Benny with curious eyes.

"What's this book about?" Lily asked Miss Sarah.

"That's Benny," said Miss Sarah. "He teaches about numbers in fun ways."

Lily picked up Benny gently. She opened his first page. Benny felt excited tingles.

"One red butterfly!" Lily read out loud.

Suddenly, a real red butterfly landed on Lily's nose! She giggled with delight. The butterfly danced around them both.

"Two blue birds!" Lily read the next page.

Two bright blue birds flew down from a tree. They chirped a sweet song just for Lily and Benny.

"This is magic!" Lily whispered.

She kept reading. "Three yellow flowers!"

Three sunny daisies popped up in the grass nearby. Their petals glowed like tiny suns.

"Four buzzing bees!"

Four friendly bees hummed hello. They made a circle around Lily and Benny.

Other children heard the excitement. They came running over to see.

"How did you do that?" asked Emma.

"It's the magic number book!" said Lily.

Soon all the children sat in a big circle. Lily read Benny's pages out loud. Every time she said a number, something wonderful happened.

Five orange leaves danced down from trees. Six purple butterflies did loop-de-loops. Seven green crickets played music together.

The children clapped and cheered. They counted along with every page. Benny felt warm and happy inside.

"Numbers are everywhere!" said Jake.

"They make the world magical!" added Emma.

The children spent the whole afternoon with Benny. They counted flowers and birds. They counted their fingers and toes. They even counted clouds in the sky.

When it was time to go home, Lily hugged Benny tight.

"Thank you for the best day ever," she whispered.

Benny felt proud and happy. His pages practically glowed with joy.

Back on the bookshelf, all the books chattered excitedly.

"You were amazing today!" said the fairy tale book.

"The children loved you!" giggled the picture book.

Benny smiled from cover to cover. He learned something important today. Every book has special magic inside. His magic was helping children see numbers everywhere.

"I can't wait for tomorrow," Benny said happily.

He looked out the window at the twinkling stars. One star, two stars, three stars. Even the night sky was full of numbers!

Benny closed his eyes and dreamed of tomorrow's adventure. Maybe the children would count raindrops. Maybe they would count ladybugs. Whatever they counted, Benny would be ready.

Numbers really were magical. And Benny was the most magical number book of all.

The end.
