# Story story_825

**Momo the Monkey and Pip the Mouse**

Momo the monkey sat on a big red barn. His long tail hung down sadly. The sun was warm on his brown fur. But Momo felt lonely.

"I wish I had a friend," Momo said softly.

He watched the chickens peck at corn. He saw the pigs roll in mud. But no one wanted to play with him.

Momo picked up a stick. He threw it high in the air. Then he caught it with his quick hands. The stick made a soft whoosh sound.

"That looks fun!" said a tiny voice.

Momo looked around. Where did that voice come from?

"Down here!" squeaked the voice.

Momo looked down. A small gray mouse sat by the barn door. The mouse had bright black eyes. His whiskers twitched with excitement.

"Hi! I'm Pip," said the mouse. "Can I play too?"

Momo's heart felt warm and happy. "Yes! I'm Momo. Do you want to catch sticks?"

Pip's tail wiggled. "I'm too small for big sticks. But I have an idea!"

Pip ran to a pile of hay. He found tiny twigs and acorns. "We can play catch with these!"

Momo climbed down from the barn. His feet made soft thumps on the ground. The smell of fresh hay filled the air.

"Great idea!" said Momo.

Pip threw a small twig. Momo caught it gently in his big hands. Then Momo tossed an acorn very softly. Pip caught it in his tiny paws.

"This is so much fun!" laughed Pip.

They played catch for a long time. The warm sun made them happy. Birds chirped sweet songs above them.

"What else can we play?" asked Momo.

Pip's eyes sparkled. "Let's play hide and seek!"

Momo covered his eyes with his hands. "One, two, three, four, five! Ready or not!"

Pip had squeezed into a small crack in the barn wall. Only his pink nose showed.

"I found you!" giggled Momo.

Now it was Pip's turn to count. Momo climbed up the apple tree. His long arms helped him swing from branch to branch. The leaves rustled softly.

"Found you too!" squeaked Pip from below.

They played until their tummies rumbled with hunger.

"I know where the farmer keeps carrots," whispered Pip.

They found orange carrots in the garden. The carrots crunched between their teeth. Sweet juice dripped down their chins.

"These taste like sunshine," said Momo.

After lunch, they explored the farm together. Pip rode on Momo's shoulder. The wind felt cool on their faces.

They saw fluffy white sheep in the meadow. They watched red ladybugs crawl on yellow flowers. They heard the gentle moo of brown cows.

"Being with you makes everything more fun," said Pip.

Momo's chest felt warm and full. "I was so lonely before. Now I'm happy!"

As the sun began to set, they sat together on the fence. The sky turned pink and orange. Stars started to twinkle like tiny diamonds.

"Will you be my best friend?" asked Momo.

"Yes!" said Pip. "Best friends forever!"

They made a special promise. Every day they would meet by the red barn. They would play games and share snacks. They would explore and have adventures.

The next morning, Momo woke up excited. He wasn't lonely anymore! He had a wonderful friend.

Pip was already waiting by the barn. His tail wagged with joy.

"Good morning, best friend!" called Pip.

"Good morning!" Momo called back.

They ran toward each other. Momo was careful not to step on tiny Pip. Pip climbed onto Momo's hand.

"What should we play today?" asked Momo.

"Everything!" laughed Pip.

And they did. They played until the stars came out again. The farm was full of their happy laughter.

From that day on, Momo and Pip were never lonely. They had each other. And that made every day special.

The other farm animals smiled when they saw the two friends. A big monkey and a tiny mouse. Different sizes, but the very best of friends.

The end.
