# Story story_1208

**Pancakes with Penny**

The sun peeked through Maya's bedroom window. Birds sang their morning songs. Maya stretched and yawned.

"Time for breakfast!" called Mom from downstairs.

Maya loved breakfast time. She loved the smell of pancakes. She loved the sound of sizzling butter. But today felt different.

Her best friend Penny was sleeping over. Penny sat on the bed looking sad. Her tummy made quiet rumbling sounds.

"I'm not hungry," Penny whispered. She pulled the blanket over her head.

Maya felt confused. Breakfast was the best part of morning. Why didn't Penny want to eat?

"My tummy feels funny," Penny said softly. "I miss my mom's breakfast."

Maya understood now. Penny felt homesick. Sometimes new places made tummies feel wobbly.

"Come with me," Maya said gently. "I have an idea."

They walked downstairs together. The kitchen smelled like vanilla and cinnamon. Mom was flipping golden pancakes.

"Good morning, girls!" Mom smiled warmly.

"Penny's tummy feels nervous," Maya explained.

Mom nodded knowingly. "I have something special for nervous tummies."

She opened the cupboard. Inside were colorful bottles and jars. She pulled out a small blue bottle.

"This is magic breakfast dust," Mom said with a wink.

Penny's eyes grew wide. "Real magic?"

"The most real kind," Mom replied.

She sprinkled sparkly sugar on two pancakes. The sugar glittered like tiny stars. It smelled like strawberries and sunshine.

"Make a breakfast wish," Mom suggested.

Penny closed her eyes tight. She whispered something secret. When she opened her eyes, she smiled a little.

Maya sprinkled more magic dust. "I wish for the yummiest breakfast ever!"

The pancakes seemed to glow. Steam rose like little clouds. Penny giggled for the first time that morning.

"They look like flying carpets," Penny said.

"Let's pretend we're flying!" Maya suggested.

They cut their pancakes into airplane shapes. They made whooshing sounds as they ate. The magic dust made everything taste extra sweet.

"These are the best flying pancakes," Penny laughed.

Her nervous tummy felt much better now. The magic dust was working perfectly.

Mom brought warm syrup in a tiny pitcher. "Special flying fuel," she announced.

The girls drizzled golden syrup on their pancake planes. It looked like sunset clouds. They took big, happy bites.

"My mom makes pancakes too," Penny shared. "But these taste like adventure."

Maya felt proud. Her friend was smiling again. Her tummy had stopped feeling wobbly.

They pretended to fly to different countries. They visited pancake mountains and syrup rivers. They met friendly butter butterflies.

"Look!" Penny pointed to her plate. "My pancake is shaped like a heart now."

"That means it's full of friendship," Maya said wisely.

They finished every single bite. Their tummies felt warm and happy. The magic dust had worked perfectly.

"Can we make breakfast tomorrow too?" Penny asked hopefully.

"Every day can be magic breakfast day," Mom promised.

The girls helped clear the dishes. They washed their sticky fingers. They planned tomorrow's breakfast adventure.

"Maybe we'll make pancake rockets," Maya suggested.

"Or pancake rainbows," Penny added excitedly.

They hugged each other tight. Penny's homesick feelings had melted away like butter on warm pancakes.

"Thank you for sharing magic breakfast," Penny whispered.

"That's what best friends do," Maya replied.

They ran outside to play. Their tummies were full and happy. The morning sun felt extra warm and bright.

Mom watched them through the window. She smiled knowing that sometimes the best magic is simply sharing a meal with someone you love.

That night, Penny didn't feel sad anymore. She dreamed about flying pancakes and magic dust. She couldn't wait for tomorrow's breakfast adventure.

And when morning came again, both girls woke up excited. Because they knew that breakfast with a best friend is always the most magical meal of all.

The end.
