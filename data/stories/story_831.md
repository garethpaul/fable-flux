# Story story_831

Little Duck sat on the soft blue rug. The living room felt very big and quiet. Sunlight came through the tall windows. It made golden squares on the floor.

Duck had yellow feathers that felt smooth. Her orange feet were small and webbed. She looked at her toy box sadly. Playing alone was not very fun.

"I wish someone would play with me," Duck whispered. Her voice was soft like a gentle breeze.

Duck pulled out her favorite red ball. She rolled it across the room. The ball made a soft bouncing sound on the wood floor. But catching it by herself felt lonely.

Then Duck heard footsteps. Mama walked into the living room. Mama had kind brown eyes and a warm smile. She wore her cozy green sweater.

"Hello, little one," Mama said gently. "What are you doing?"

"Playing by myself," Duck said with a small sigh. "But it's not much fun."

Mama sat down on the soft rug. The cushions made a whooshing sound. "May I play with you?" she asked.

Duck's eyes grew bright like stars. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" Mama said. "Playing together is always better."

Duck picked up her red ball. "Want to roll it back and forth?"

"That sounds wonderful," Mama said.

Duck rolled the ball to Mama. It made a gentle rolling sound across the smooth floor. Mama caught it with both hands. Then she rolled it back to Duck.

"This is fun!" Duck giggled. Her laugh sounded like tiny bells ringing.

They rolled the ball faster and faster. Sometimes it bounced off the soft couch. Sometimes it rolled under the coffee table. They both laughed when they had to crawl to get it.

"What else should we play?" Mama asked.

Duck thought for a moment. "Let's build a fort!"

They pulled cushions off the big brown couch. The cushions were fluffy and smelled like lavender. They stacked them up high. Then they draped a soft blanket over the top.

"Our fort is perfect!" Duck said proudly.

They crawled inside their cozy fort. The blanket made everything dim and peaceful. They could hear each other breathing softly.

"Tell me a story," Duck asked.

Mama told a story about brave little ducks. Duck listened with wide eyes. The story made her feel warm and safe.

After story time, they played hide and seek. Duck hid behind the tall green plant. Mama pretended she couldn't find her. Duck giggled from her hiding spot.

"Where could Duck be?" Mama wondered aloud. "I hear giggling somewhere!"

Duck jumped out. "Surprise! Here I am!"

Mama scooped Duck up in a big hug. Duck felt safe and loved in Mama's warm arms. She smelled like cookies and sunshine.

"You're the best hiding duck ever," Mama said.

They played until the sun started to set. The room filled with orange and pink light. Everything looked magical and warm.

"Thank you for playing with me," Duck said. "Today was the best day ever."

"I loved playing with you too," Mama replied. "We make a great team."

Duck snuggled close to Mama on the couch. The soft cushions felt like clouds. "Can we play together again tomorrow?"

"Every day," Mama promised. "Playing with you makes me happy."

Duck felt her heart fill with joy. She wasn't lonely anymore. Having Mama as her play friend was better than anything.

They sat together watching the sunset. The living room felt cozy and full of love. Duck knew that tomorrow would bring more fun and games.

"I love you, Mama," Duck whispered.

"I love you too, sweet Duck," Mama whispered back.

The room grew quiet again. But this time, the quiet felt peaceful and happy. Duck had learned something important. Playing alone could be fun, but playing with someone who loves you was magical.

Duck closed her eyes and smiled. Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough.

The end.
