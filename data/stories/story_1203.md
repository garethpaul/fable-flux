# Story story_1203

**Nutkin and Big Red**

Nutkin the squirrel sat alone on the old oak tree. His fluffy gray tail drooped sadly. The farm was quiet today. All the other animals were busy with their families.

Nutkin's tiny paws held an acorn. He tossed it up and caught it. Up and down, up and down. But playing alone wasn't much fun. His little heart felt heavy and lonely.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," Nutkin whispered to himself.

Near the red barn sat Big Red, a shiny farm truck. His paint sparkled in the morning sun. Big Red had been watching Nutkin play by himself. The truck's engine made a gentle rumbling sound.

"Beep beep!" Big Red called softly. "Hello there, little squirrel!"

Nutkin's ears perked up. He looked around with his bright black eyes. "Did someone say hello to me?"

"Down here!" Big Red's headlights blinked like friendly eyes. "I'm Big Red. Want to play together?"

Nutkin scampered down the bumpy tree bark. His tiny claws clicked against the wood. He stopped in front of the big truck. Big Red seemed so large and strong.

"You want to play with me?" Nutkin asked. His voice was small and shy. "But you're so big, and I'm so little."

Big Red's engine purred warmly. "Size doesn't matter for friendship! We can have lots of fun together."

Nutkin's tail began to twitch with excitement. "What should we play?"

"How about hide and seek?" Big Red suggested. His horn tooted cheerfully.

Nutkin clapped his tiny paws. "Yes! I'll hide first!"

The little squirrel scurried around the farmyard. His nose twitched as he smelled fresh hay and sweet corn. He found the perfect spot behind a big pumpkin. The orange pumpkin was round and smooth.

"Ready or not, here I come!" Big Red called. His wheels crunched slowly over the gravel path.

Big Red looked behind the chicken coop. No Nutkin there. He checked near the pig pen. Still no squirrel. Finally, he spotted a gray tail peeking out from behind the pumpkin.

"Found you!" Big Red laughed. His engine made happy rumbling sounds.

Nutkin giggled and jumped up. "Your turn to hide!"

Big Red thought hard. Where could a big truck hide? He backed up behind the tall corn stalks. The green leaves rustled in the gentle breeze.

Nutkin counted to ten on his tiny fingers. Then he began searching. He climbed up fence posts to look around. His whiskers twitched as he sniffed the air.

"Where could Big Red be?" Nutkin wondered aloud.

He heard a quiet beep from the cornfield. Nutkin scampered through the rows of corn. The tall stalks tickled his fur. There was Big Red, trying to hide behind the green plants!

"I found you!" Nutkin squeaked with joy.

They played chase around the barnyard next. Nutkin ran in circles while Big Red drove slowly behind him. The truck was careful not to go too fast. Nutkin's little legs moved quickly across the soft dirt.

The warm sun climbed higher in the sky. Nutkin and Big Red were having the best day ever. They raced leaves in the wind. They counted clouds in the blue sky.

"This is so much fun!" Nutkin said, breathing hard from all their games. He climbed onto Big Red's hood to rest.

"I'm so happy we're friends," Big Red said. His engine hummed contentedly.

Farmer Mae came out of the house. She smiled when she saw them playing together. "Well, look at that! You two make quite a team."

As the sun began to set, Nutkin yawned. His belly was full of acorns Big Red had helped him find. The truck's warm hood felt cozy under his fur.

"Thank you for being my friend," Nutkin said sleepily.

"Thank you for playing with me," Big Red replied. "Friends make everything better."

From that day on, Nutkin and Big Red played together every day. The little squirrel never felt lonely again. And Big Red had found the best friend a truck could ask for.

Their friendship filled the farm with laughter and joy.

The end.
