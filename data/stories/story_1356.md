# Story story_1356

Daisy the duck waddled down the long school hallway. Her orange feet made soft pat-pat sounds on the shiny floor. She carried her favorite red ball under one wing.

The hallway was very quiet. All the other animals were in their classrooms. Daisy felt lonely. She wished she had a friend to play with.

Daisy bounced her red ball against the wall. Bounce, catch. Bounce, catch. The sound echoed in the empty hallway. She sighed a little duck sigh.

"That looks fun!" said a cheerful voice.

Daisy turned around. A small pink pig stood behind her. He had kind brown eyes and a wiggly tail.

"Hi," said Daisy shyly. "I'm Daisy."

"I'm Pete the pig," he said with a big smile. "Can I play with you?"

Daisy's heart felt warm and happy. "Yes, please!" she said.

Pete trotted over on his little hooves. Click-click-click on the floor. Daisy rolled the red ball to him. Pete caught it with his snout.

"Good catch!" said Daisy, clapping her wings.

Pete rolled the ball back. It was wobbly and funny. Both friends giggled. The sound filled the quiet hallway with joy.

"Let's try something new," said Daisy. She threw the ball up high. It bounced off the ceiling with a soft thump.

"Wow!" said Pete. "You're really good at that!"

They took turns throwing the ball up. Sometimes they missed. Sometimes they caught it. They laughed every time.

"I have an idea," said Pete. "Let's roll the ball really far!"

Daisy nodded. They went to one end of the long hallway. Pete pushed the ball with his snout. It rolled and rolled and rolled.

"Chase it!" yelled Daisy.

They ran after the red ball together. Daisy's feet went pat-pat-pat. Pete's hooves went click-click-click. The sounds mixed together like music.

The ball stopped at the other end. They were both breathing hard. But they were smiling big smiles.

"That was the best!" said Pete.

"Let's do it again!" said Daisy.

They played their rolling game five more times. Up and down the hallway they ran. Their laughter echoed off the walls.

Then Daisy had another idea. "Want to play follow the leader?"

"Yes!" said Pete, jumping up and down.

Daisy went first. She waddled in a zigzag line. Pete followed, trying to step where she stepped. His hooves made the same pattern as her feet.

Then Pete was the leader. He did little hops and spins. Daisy copied him perfectly. She flapped her wings when he wiggled his tail.

"You're a great follower!" said Pete.

"You're a great leader!" said Daisy.

They played until the school bell rang. Ding-dong, ding-dong. It was time for lunch.

"I had so much fun," said Daisy. Her voice was warm and happy.

"Me too," said Pete. "Will you be my friend?"

"Yes!" said Daisy. "Best friends!"

They hugged right there in the hallway. Daisy's soft feathers tickled Pete's nose. Pete's warm snout felt cozy against Daisy's wing.

"Let's play together every day," said Pete.

"Every single day," agreed Daisy.

They walked to the cafeteria together. Daisy carried her red ball. Pete walked beside her. Their different footsteps made a happy rhythm.

Other animals saw them coming. "Look at the new friends!" said a bunny.

"They look so happy," said a squirrel.

Daisy and Pete smiled at each other. They had found something wonderful in that quiet hallway. They had found friendship.

From that day on, they were never lonely. The hallway became their special place. It was where they first played together. It was where their friendship began.

Every morning, they would meet in the hallway. They would play with the red ball and laugh together. The empty hallway was never empty anymore. It was full of friendship and joy.

And that made everything perfect.

The end.
