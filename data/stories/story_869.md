# Story story_869

**Rosie's Garden Adventure**

"Time for garden time!" called Mama from the kitchen window.

Rosie the little brown horse looked up from her favorite shady spot. She wrinkled her nose. Garden time meant getting dirty. It meant pulling weeds. It meant hard work under the hot sun.

"Do I have to?" Rosie asked, swishing her tail.

"Yes, sweetie," Mama said gently. "The vegetables need our help to grow big and strong."

Rosie walked slowly to the garden. Her hooves made soft sounds in the dirt. She didn't want to leave her cool, comfortable spot under the apple tree.

The garden looked very big today. Rows and rows of green plants stretched across the yard. Weeds poked up everywhere between the carrots and lettuce. The sun felt warm on Rosie's back.

"Where should I start?" Rosie wondered aloud.

She began pulling tiny weeds near the tomato plants. The dirt felt cool between her hooves. Pull, pull, pull. One weed. Two weeds. Three weeds.

Suddenly, something sparkled in the soil.

"What's that?" Rosie said, bending down closer.

A small, shiny button peeked out from the earth. Rosie brushed the dirt away with her nose. The button was silver and round, with tiny flowers carved on top.

"How pretty!" she whispered.

As Rosie kept working, she found more treasures. A smooth blue stone. A small red bead. An old coin with interesting marks.

"Look what I found!" Rosie called to Mama.

Mama trotted over to see. "My goodness! Those are beautiful treasures, Rosie."

"The garden is like a treasure hunt!" Rosie said, her eyes sparkling.

She started pulling weeds faster now. Each handful of dirt might hide something special. Under the bean plants, she discovered a tiny toy soldier. Near the corn, she found a marble that looked like a rainbow.

The work didn't feel hard anymore. Rosie forgot about the warm sun. She forgot about getting dirty. She was having an adventure!

"I wonder what's under these weeds," Rosie said, digging near the pumpkin patch.

She found an old key, rusty but still interesting. She found a small whistle that made a soft sound when she blew through it.

As Rosie worked, something else magical happened. The garden began to smell wonderful. The tomato plants had a fresh, green smell. The herbs smelled like pizza and cookies. The flowers smelled sweet like candy.

"The garden smells so good!" Rosie told Mama.

"That's because you're taking such good care of it," Mama said proudly.

Rosie noticed other things too. Busy bees buzzed from flower to flower. Butterflies danced in the warm air. A friendly robin hopped nearby, looking for worms.

"Hello, little bird," Rosie whispered softly.

The robin chirped back as if saying hello.

By afternoon, Rosie had cleared three whole rows. Her treasure collection grew bigger and bigger. She had buttons, stones, beads, coins, and many other interesting things.

"Look how much you've done!" Mama said, looking at the neat, clean rows.

The vegetables looked happy in their weed-free beds. The carrots stood up straighter. The lettuce leaves looked greener. Even the tiny seedlings seemed to wave in the gentle breeze.

"I can see the baby vegetables better now," Rosie noticed.

"They'll grow much better without weeds taking their food and water," Mama explained.

Rosie felt proud looking at her work. The garden looked beautiful and organized. Her treasure box was full of wonderful finds. Her hooves were dirty, but she didn't mind anymore.

"Can we do garden time again tomorrow?" Rosie asked hopefully.

Mama smiled. "Of course! There are more rows that need help."

"Maybe I'll find even more treasures," Rosie said excitedly.

That evening, Rosie washed her hooves and arranged her treasures in a special box. She could hardly wait for tomorrow's garden adventure.

As she fell asleep under her favorite apple tree, Rosie dreamed about shiny buttons and friendly butterflies. Garden time wasn't work anymore. It was the best treasure hunt ever.

The end.
