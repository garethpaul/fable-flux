# Story story_687

**Nutkin's New Friend**

Nutkin the squirrel sat on a big oak tree. His fluffy gray tail twitched sadly. The garden below looked so pretty. Colorful flowers danced in the warm breeze. But Nutkin felt lonely.

"I wish I had someone to play with," he sighed. His small paws held an acorn. He rolled it back and forth. The smooth shell felt cool against his fur.

Nutkin jumped down to the soft grass. He started to dig small holes. Maybe he could bury his acorns here. The dirt smelled fresh and earthy. His tiny claws made scratching sounds.

Behind the rose bushes, something moved. Nutkin's ears perked up. He heard a soft rustling noise. His heart beat faster.

"Hello?" called a gentle voice. "Can I play too?"

A small brown rabbit hopped out. She had the softest fur Nutkin had ever seen. Her pink nose wiggled as she sniffed the air. Her name was Rosie.

"I'm Nutkin," said the squirrel shyly. "I was just burying acorns."

Rosie's eyes sparkled with excitement. "That looks like fun! I love digging too."

Nutkin felt his tail puff up with happiness. "Really? You want to help me?"

"Yes, please!" Rosie bounced on her strong back legs.

The two friends started digging together. Nutkin showed Rosie the best spots. Under the sunflowers was perfect. The soil was loose and dark. They made little holes in neat rows.

"This one is deep enough," said Rosie. She pointed with her small paw.

Nutkin dropped in a shiny acorn. They covered it with soft dirt. Then they patted it down gently. The earth felt warm from the morning sun.

"What should we do next?" asked Rosie.

Nutkin's whiskers twitched with an idea. "Want to race to the apple tree?"

"Yes!" Rosie giggled. Her laugh sounded like tiny bells.

They lined up next to the garden gate. Nutkin counted down. "Three, two, one, go!"

Nutkin scampered quickly on four paws. His bushy tail helped him balance. Rosie hopped in big bounces. Her ears flopped up and down. They ran through the sweet-smelling flower beds.

Nutkin reached the tree first. But he waited for Rosie. Friends should finish together, he thought.

"That was amazing!" panted Rosie. "You're so fast!"

"You're a great hopper!" said Nutkin. "Your jumps are huge!"

Under the apple tree, they found fallen fruit. The apples smelled crisp and sweet. Red ones and yellow ones lay on the grass. Some had small brown spots.

"These look yummy," said Rosie. She took a tiny bite. The juice was perfect.

Nutkin nibbled an apple slice. The fruit was crunchy and fresh. Sweet juice dripped down his chin. They shared the snack happily.

After eating, they played hide and seek. Rosie hid behind the big sunflowers. Nutkin found her quickly. Her brown ears stuck up too high. They both laughed together.

Then Nutkin hid in the oak tree. Rosie looked everywhere. She checked under bushes and behind rocks. Finally, she spotted his gray tail.

"Found you!" she called up.

As the sun got higher, they felt tired. They sat in the cool shade together. The grass felt soft under them. A gentle breeze rustled the leaves above.

"This was the best day ever," said Nutkin. His heart felt warm and full.

"I'm so glad we're friends," said Rosie. She leaned against the tree trunk.

"Me too," said Nutkin. "Want to play again tomorrow?"

"Absolutely!" Rosie's nose wiggled with joy.

They made plans to meet by the rose bushes. They would plant more acorns together. Maybe they could build a fort too. The garden had so many perfect spots.

As Rosie hopped home, Nutkin climbed his tree. He wasn't lonely anymore. He had found something wonderful today. A true friend who loved to play. His tail felt extra fluffy with happiness.

The garden looked even more beautiful now. Everything seemed brighter when you had a friend to share it with.

The end.
