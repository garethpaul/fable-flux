# Story story_1351

**The Blocks Who Didn't Want to Clean Up**

In a sunny playroom with yellow walls, there lived a family of colorful blocks. There was Big Blue Block, Little Red Block, and Wiggly Green Block. They loved to build tall towers and make pretend castles all day long.

Every evening, their friend Emma would say, "Time to clean up!" But the blocks had a secret. They didn't want to go back in their toy box. The box was dark and cramped. It smelled like old cardboard.

"I don't like cleaning up," whispered Little Red Block. "It's scary in there."

"Me too," said Wiggly Green Block. "I want to stay out and play."

Big Blue Block nodded. "Let's hide when Emma comes!"

One Tuesday afternoon, Emma started picking up toys. "Where are my blocks?" she asked, looking around the room.

The three blocks had rolled under the cozy reading chair. They giggled quietly. Emma's footsteps made soft sounds on the carpet as she searched.

"I can't find them anywhere," Emma said sadly. She sat on the floor with a big sigh.

The blocks felt their first tummy wobbles. Emma looked upset. Her shoulders drooped down low.

"Maybe we should come out," whispered Little Red Block.

"Not yet," said Big Blue Block. "She'll put us away."

The next day, Emma couldn't build anything. She had puzzles and dolls, but no blocks for making towers. She sat quietly, drawing pictures of sad, empty castles.

"I miss my blocks," Emma said to her mom. "They help me build the best things."

From under the chair, the blocks heard every word. Their tummy wobbles got bigger and stronger.

"Emma sounds really sad," said Wiggly Green Block.

"She needs us," added Little Red Block.

Big Blue Block felt warm tears on his corners. "But what about the dark toy box?"

That night, Little Red Block had a bright idea. "What if we ask Emma for help?"

"How?" asked the others.

"We could tell her about the scary box!"

The next morning, the three blocks rolled out from their hiding spot. Emma's face lit up like sunshine.

"My blocks! You're back!" she cheered.

"Emma," said Big Blue Block in his tiny voice. "We need to tell you something."

Emma bent down close to listen.

"The toy box is dark and scary," said Little Red Block. "That's why we hide."

"We want to help you build," added Wiggly Green Block. "But we don't like going away."

Emma's eyes grew wide and understanding. "Oh! I never knew you felt scared."

She thought for a moment, tapping her finger on her chin.

"I have an idea!" Emma said excitedly.

She brought a soft, fluffy blanket from her bed. It smelled like lavender and felt like clouds. Emma placed it gently in the toy box.

"Now it's cozy!" she said.

Then she added a tiny flashlight that made pretty star patterns on the walls.

"And not dark anymore!"

The blocks looked at each other with big, happy smiles.

"It looks wonderful!" said Big Blue Block.

"Like a magical cave!" added Little Red Block.

"Can we try it?" asked Wiggly Green Block.

Emma carefully placed each block in their new cozy home. The blanket felt warm and soft. The star lights twinkled like a gentle night sky.

"This is actually nice," said Big Blue Block, surprised.

"It smells good too," said Little Red Block, snuggling into the blanket.

"I like the pretty lights," said Wiggly Green Block.

From that day on, cleaning up became the blocks' favorite time. They would race to their cozy box, excited to rest in their comfortable home.

Emma learned that listening helps solve problems. The blocks learned that speaking up about feelings makes everything better.

Every evening, they would all say together, "Time to clean up!" And everyone would smile.

The playroom stayed tidy, Emma could always find her blocks, and the three friends felt safe and happy in their special starlit home.

Sometimes the best solutions come from working together and sharing how we really feel.

The end.
