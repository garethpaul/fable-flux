# Story story_1175

Sam's Magical Morning

The sun peeked through Sam's bedroom window. Golden light danced on the walls. Birds chirped their happy morning song.

"Sam, breakfast time!" called Mom from the kitchen.

Sam pulled the blanket over his head. He didn't want to get up. His bed felt so warm and cozy. The pillow was soft under his cheek.

"Five more minutes," Sam mumbled.

Mom's footsteps came closer. She sat on the edge of his bed. Her hand felt gentle on his back.

"I made your favorite pancakes," she said softly.

Sam peeked out from under the blanket. He could smell something wonderful. It smelled sweet and buttery. His tummy made a little growling sound.

But Sam still felt grumpy. He didn't want to leave his cozy bed. He didn't want to brush his teeth. He didn't want to get dressed.

"I don't want breakfast," Sam said.

Mom smiled. "What if breakfast wants you?"

Sam looked confused. "Breakfast can't want me. It's just food."

Mom winked. "Come see."

Sam slowly got out of bed. His bare feet touched the cold floor. He shuffled to the kitchen behind Mom.

On the table sat a plate. But this wasn't just any plate. The pancakes looked like a smiling face! Two round pancakes made the eyes. A banana slice made the nose. Strawberry pieces made a big happy smile.

"Hello, Sam!" Mom said in a funny voice. She pretended the pancake face was talking. "I've been waiting for you!"

Sam giggled. The pancake face did look like it was talking.

"I'm delicious!" Mom continued in the silly voice. "I taste like sunshine and sweetness!"

Sam sat down at the table. He picked up his fork. The first bite was warm and fluffy. It tasted like vanilla and maple syrup. His tongue felt happy.

"Mmm," Sam said. "You do taste good."

Mom sat down with her own breakfast. She had made her pancakes look like a cat. Two triangle pieces made ears. Blueberries made the eyes and nose.

"Meow!" Mom said, making her pancake talk too. "I'm a breakfast cat!"

Sam laughed out loud. This was the silliest breakfast ever. He took another bite. The pancake felt soft in his mouth. The strawberries tasted sweet and juicy.

"Can my pancake talk to your pancake?" Sam asked.

"Of course!" Mom said.

Sam made his pancake face say hello. Mom made her cat pancake meow back. They had a whole conversation with their breakfast faces.

Sam ate and laughed. He forgot all about being grumpy. His tummy felt warm and full. The kitchen smelled like happiness.

"This is the best breakfast ever," Sam said.

He carefully ate around the smile first. Then he ate the banana nose. The pancake eyes were last. Each bite tasted better than the one before.

Mom poured orange juice into Sam's special cup. The one with the rocket ship on it. The juice was cold and tangy. It woke up his whole mouth.

"Tomorrow is Saturday," Mom said. "What kind of breakfast face should we make?"

Sam thought hard. His eyes got bright and excited.

"A dinosaur!" he said. "A friendly dinosaur with a big smile!"

"That sounds perfect," Mom said. "We can use different fruits for the dinosaur spikes."

Sam finished his last bite. His plate was completely clean. His tummy felt happy and satisfied.

"Can we make breakfast faces every day?" Sam asked.

Mom hugged him tight. She smelled like pancakes and love.

"We can make breakfast special whenever you want," she said.

Sam helped clear the table. He wasn't grumpy anymore. His whole body felt energetic and ready for the day.

That night, Sam went to bed excited. He couldn't wait for morning. He couldn't wait for breakfast. He dreamed about dinosaur pancakes and talking fruit.

When the sun came up, Sam jumped out of bed. He ran to the kitchen with a big smile.

"Time for dinosaur breakfast!" he called happily.

And it was the most delicious dinosaur he ever ate.

The end.
