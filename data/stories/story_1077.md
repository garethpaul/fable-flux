# Story story_1077

Mama sat on the soft green grass in the backyard. The warm sun touched her face. She had her favorite red ball. But she felt sad and lonely.

"I wish someone would play with me," Mama said quietly. She rolled the ball back and forth. The ball made a soft bouncing sound on the ground.

Mama looked at the pretty flowers. She smelled their sweet smell. Yellow butterflies danced around the roses. But playing alone was not fun.

Inside the house, Sister heard Mama talking. Sister peeked out the window. She saw Mama sitting all by herself.

"Mama looks sad," Sister said. "Maybe she wants a friend to play with."

Sister opened the back door. The door made a squeaky sound. She walked outside on her little bare feet. The grass felt cool and tickly.

"Hi Mama," Sister said with a big smile. "Can I play with you?"

Mama's eyes got bright and happy. "Yes! I would love that very much!"

Sister sat down next to Mama on the soft grass. "What should we play?" Sister asked.

"We could roll the ball," Mama said. She pushed the red ball to Sister. Sister giggled and pushed it back.

The ball rolled between them. Back and forth, back and forth. They both started laughing. Their happy sounds filled the quiet backyard.

"This is fun!" Sister said. "But I have another idea."

Sister jumped up and ran to the flower garden. She picked some yellow dandelions. The flowers felt soft in her small hands.

"We can make flower crowns," Sister said. She showed Mama how to twist the stems together.

Mama and Sister worked together. Their fingers moved carefully. They made two beautiful yellow crowns. The flowers smelled like sunshine.

"You look like a princess," Mama told Sister.

"You look like a queen," Sister told Mama.

They put their flower crowns on their heads. Then they danced around the yard. Their feet made soft sounds on the grass. They spun in circles until they felt dizzy.

"Let's play hide and seek," Mama said.

Sister hid behind the big oak tree. Mama counted to ten with her eyes closed. "Ready or not, here I come!"

Mama found Sister giggling behind the tree. Then it was Mama's turn to hide. She crouched behind the garden fence. Sister found her right away.

They played and played until their tummies rumbled. The sun was getting lower in the sky. Pink and orange colors painted the clouds.

"I'm hungry," Sister said.

"Me too," said Mama. "Should we have a picnic?"

They went inside together. Mama made peanut butter sandwiches. Sister got two juice boxes from the fridge. They carried everything outside on a red blanket.

They spread the blanket under the big tree. The shade felt cool and nice. They ate their sandwiches and drank their juice. Everything tasted extra good outside.

"This is the best day ever," Sister said. She had peanut butter on her nose.

Mama wiped Sister's nose with a napkin. "Playing with you makes me so happy," Mama said.

"Playing with you makes me happy too," Sister said. "Can we play together every day?"

"Yes," Mama said. "We can be best friends forever."

They hugged tight under the tree. The birds sang sweet songs above them. A gentle breeze made the leaves dance.

As the sun went down, they cleaned up their picnic. They carried the blanket inside together. Their hands were dirty from playing. Their hearts were full of joy.

"Thank you for playing with me," Mama said.

"Thank you for letting me join you," Sister said.

They looked out the window at their backyard. Tomorrow they would play there again. They would have more adventures together.

Mama and Sister smiled at each other. They had found something wonderful today. They had found the joy of playing together. They had found the happiness of being best friends.

The backyard waited for them, ready for tomorrow's fun.

The end.
