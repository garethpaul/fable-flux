# Daisy Duck's Reading Adventure

The afternoon sun streamed through the kitchen window. Mama Duck dried her feathers with a soft towel. "Reading time, Daisy!" she called cheerfully.

Daisy Duck peeked around the corner. Her yellow feathers drooped a little. Reading time meant sitting still. Reading time meant quiet voices. Daisy liked splashing and quacking loudly.

"Do I have to?" Daisy asked softly. She wiggled her orange feet. She flapped her wings twice.

"Books are wonderful adventures," Mama said gently. "Come choose one with me."

Daisy waddled slowly to the cozy reading corner. Soft blue cushions made a perfect nest. A small lamp glowed warmly. Colorful books lined the wooden shelf.

"I don't know which one," Daisy sighed. She looked at all the books. So many pictures! So many words!

Mama Duck smiled. "Close your eyes, Daisy. Pick with your heart."

Daisy closed her bright eyes tight. She reached out one wing. Her feathers touched a book cover. It felt smooth and cool.

"This one," she whispered.

Daisy opened her eyes. The book sparkled! Golden letters danced across the cover. "The Magic Pond" glowed in beautiful script.

"Ooh!" Daisy gasped. "It's shiny!"

Mama Duck looked surprised. "I've never seen it sparkle before!"

They snuggled together on the cushions. Daisy felt warm and safe. Mama opened the book carefully.

The first page shimmered like water. A little duck just like Daisy smiled back. The duck in the book waved!

"She moved!" Daisy squeaked excitedly.

As Mama read aloud, something magical happened. The words floated off the page! They swirled around Daisy like tiny butterflies. Each word glowed softly.

"Once upon a time," Mama read. The words "once upon a time" danced in golden circles.

Daisy giggled. She tried to catch the floating words. They tickled her feathers gently.

The story duck jumped from the page. She landed right beside Daisy! "Want to explore my pond?" the story duck asked.

"Yes!" Daisy quacked happily.

Together, they dove into the book's pages. Splash! Cool water surrounded them. Lily pads bobbed nearby. Friendly frogs said hello.

"This is amazing!" Daisy laughed. She swam through liquid words. She played with alphabet fish. She slid down a rainbow waterfall.

The story duck showed Daisy secret caves. Inside, treasure chests held more stories. Each book glowed with its own magic.

"Every book has adventures inside," the story duck explained. "You just have to believe."

They swam and played and explored. Time felt like floating bubbles. Daisy never wanted to leave.

But slowly, the magic pond faded. The story duck waved goodbye. "See you in another book!"

Daisy found herself back on the cushions. Mama was closing the book gently. The afternoon light had grown softer.

"That was the best reading time ever!" Daisy exclaimed. Her eyes sparkled brightly. "The duck talked to me! We swam together!"

Mama Duck hugged her close. "Books can take us anywhere, sweetie."

Daisy looked at all the other books. Each one seemed to shimmer slightly. What adventures waited inside them?

"Can we read another one?" Daisy asked hopefully.

"Tomorrow," Mama promised. "Reading time comes every day."

Daisy's heart felt full and happy. She couldn't wait for tomorrow's reading time. Maybe she'd visit a jungle. Or fly to the moon. Or meet a friendly dragon.

"I love reading time now," Daisy whispered. She hugged the magic book tight.

That night, Daisy dreamed of floating words. She dreamed of story friends. She dreamed of all the adventures waiting.

When morning came, Daisy was already excited. Only a few hours until reading time! She helped Mama with breakfast. She played quietly.

But mostly, she thought about books. Beautiful, magical, wonderful books.

Reading time wasn't just sitting still anymore. It was the most exciting part of her day. And tomorrow, there would be new adventures waiting.

Daisy Duck had discovered the greatest magic of all. The magic of stories.

The end.
