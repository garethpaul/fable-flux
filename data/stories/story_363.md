# The Hill Where Friends Meet

Tommy sat on the grassy hill behind his house. He rolled his red ball down the slope. It bounced and tumbled to the bottom. Tommy walked down to get it. Then he climbed back up. He did this again and again.

"This is fun," Tommy said to himself. But deep down, he felt a little lonely. Playing alone wasn't quite the same. He wished someone would play with him.

Just then, a small brown bunny hopped out from behind a big oak tree. The bunny had soft, fluffy fur and bright black eyes. Its nose twitched as it sniffed the air.

"Hello!" called Tommy, waving his hand. "I'm Tommy. What's your name?"

The bunny hopped closer. "I'm Benny," he said in a gentle voice. "What are you doing up here?"

"I'm playing with my ball," Tommy explained. "I roll it down the hill. Then I chase it. Want to try?"

Benny's ears perked up with excitement. "That sounds wonderful! I love rolling and tumbling."

Tommy placed the red ball at the top of the hill. He gave it a gentle push. The ball rolled down, down, down. Both friends raced after it, laughing as they ran.

"My turn!" said Benny. He nudged the ball with his nose. It rolled even faster this time. Tommy and Benny giggled as they chased it together.

"This is much more fun with a friend," Tommy said, catching his breath.

"I agree!" Benny hopped in a happy circle. "Let's try something new!"

Benny showed Tommy how to do bunny hops. Tommy tried his best, jumping with both feet together. It felt silly and wonderful at the same time.

"Now you teach me something," Benny said.

Tommy thought for a moment. "Can you do a somersault?" he asked.

"I'm not sure," Benny replied. "Will you show me?"

Tommy demonstrated a perfect somersault on the soft grass. Benny watched carefully. Then he tried it himself. He rolled into a fuzzy ball and tumbled gently down the hill.

"I did it!" Benny cheered. "That was amazing!"

The two friends spent the afternoon creating new games together. They made up a rolling race. Tommy would somersault while Benny hopped beside him. They laughed until their bellies hurt.

"Let's build something," Tommy suggested. He started gathering small sticks and colorful leaves.

"Great idea!" Benny used his teeth to carry twigs. His strong legs helped him dig small holes for their creation.

Working together, they built a tiny fort. Tommy arranged the bigger sticks. Benny filled in gaps with leaves and grass. Their fort had walls, a roof, and even a little door.

"We make a good team," Tommy said, admiring their work.

"The best team!" Benny agreed, his tail wiggling with joy.

As the sun began to set, painting the sky orange and pink, the friends sat in their fort. They shared Tommy's apple slices that he had brought for his snack.

"I was feeling lonely before you came," Tommy admitted. "Playing alone isn't as fun."

"I was looking for a friend too," Benny said softly. "I'm so glad I found you."

"Everything is better when we work together," Tommy observed. "The games are more exciting. Building is easier. Even eating is more fun!"

Benny nodded enthusiastically. "When we cooperate, we can do amazing things!"

Tommy had an idea. "Would you like to meet here tomorrow? We could explore the other side of the hill."

"Yes, yes, yes!" Benny hopped up and down. "I can't wait!"

As they said goodbye, both friends felt warm and happy inside. Tommy waved as Benny hopped toward the woods. The hill didn't feel lonely anymore.

The next morning, Tommy raced to the hill with his ball and a picnic lunch. Benny was already there, waiting with a big smile.

"Ready for another adventure?" Benny asked.

"Ready!" Tommy replied.

Hand in paw, the best friends climbed to the very top of their special hill. Together, they could do anything. The world felt full of possibilities and joy.

From that day on, the hill became known as the place where the very best friendships begin.

The end.
