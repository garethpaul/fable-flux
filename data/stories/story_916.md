# Story story_916

**The Missing Red Car**

Tommy loved playing in the big playroom. Colorful toys filled every corner. Soft blocks sat in neat piles. Puzzles covered the round table. But Tommy's favorite toy was his shiny red car.

"Vroom, vroom!" Tommy said every morning. He would push the little car around the room. The wheels made a happy rolling sound on the wooden floor.

But today was different. Tommy looked on his toy shelf. No red car. He checked under the cozy reading chair. Still no car.

"Where is my car?" Tommy asked out loud. His voice sounded worried.

Tommy's friend Lily heard him. She had curly brown hair and wore a yellow dress with tiny flowers.

"What's wrong, Tommy?" Lily asked. She put down her purple crayon.

"I can't find my red car anywhere," Tommy said. His eyes looked sad. "I always play with it first thing."

Lily smiled kindly. "Let's look together! Two friends can find it faster."

Tommy felt a little better. Having help made problems seem smaller.

"Where did you play with it yesterday?" Lily asked.

Tommy thought hard. He put his finger on his chin. "I think I drove it to the book corner."

They walked to the soft book corner. Fluffy pillows made it cozy. Picture books lined the low shelves. The carpet felt squishy under their feet.

"Let's move these pillows," Lily suggested.

They lifted each pillow carefully. The first pillow was light blue. Underneath sat a forgotten cookie crumb. The second pillow was striped. They found a lost sock beneath it.

"No car here," Tommy sighed. His shoulders drooped down.

But Lily didn't give up. "That's okay! Let's try somewhere else."

She tapped her finger thoughtfully. "Where else did you play yesterday?"

Tommy's face brightened a little. "The block area! I built a garage there."

They hurried to the block corner. Wooden blocks of every size created tall towers. Some blocks were smooth and warm. Others felt bumpy and cool.

"Maybe your car is still in the garage," Lily said hopefully.

They began moving blocks one by one. The blocks made gentle clicking sounds as they touched. Some blocks were heavy. Others were light as feathers.

Behind a wall of green blocks, they found something red!

"There it is!" Tommy shouted with joy.

His little red car sat perfectly in the block garage. The shiny paint caught the morning sunlight. The tiny wheels looked ready to roll again.

"You left it parked in your garage," Lily laughed. "Just like a real car!"

Tommy picked up his precious toy. It felt smooth and familiar in his hands. "I remember now! I was being so careful with it."

"Vroom, vroom!" Tommy said happily. He pushed the car in a big circle. The wheels rolled smoothly across the floor.

"Thank you for helping me, Lily," Tommy said. His smile was as bright as sunshine.

"That's what friends do," Lily replied. "We help each other solve problems."

Tommy nodded wisely. "And we don't give up when things get hard."

"Even when the first try doesn't work," Lily added.

They spent the morning playing together. Tommy drove his red car through Lily's colorful drawings. Lily built new roads with the wooden blocks.

The playroom filled with happy sounds. Wheels rolling, blocks clicking, and friends laughing. The warm sunlight made everything glow.

When snack time came, Tommy carefully parked his red car on his toy shelf. Right where it belonged.

"Now I'll remember where to find you tomorrow," he told the little car.

Lily giggled. "And if you forget again, I'll help you look."

Tommy learned something important that day. Problems feel smaller when friends help. Trying again after failing makes you stronger. And the best treasures are worth searching for.

The red car sat safely on its shelf, ready for tomorrow's adventures. And Tommy felt proud that he never gave up looking for his favorite toy.

The end.
