# Story story_1306

**Ellie's Big Cleanup**

Ellie the elephant loved her backyard playground. She had a big red swing that creaked happily when she swayed back and forth. Every morning, she would climb onto her special swing and look around her yard.

Today felt different. Ellie pumped her legs and swung high into the air. The wind tickled her ears. She could see everything from up there - the sandbox, the slide, and... oh no!

Toys were scattered everywhere. Her bouncy balls rolled in the dirt. Colorful blocks sat in the flower bed. Her favorite teddy bear lay upside down by the fence. The yard looked very messy.

"I should clean up," Ellie said to herself. But the swing felt so cozy. The sun warmed her gray skin. She didn't want to stop swinging.

"Maybe the toys will clean themselves up," she thought hopefully. She closed her eyes and counted to ten. When she opened them, the toys were still there. They hadn't moved at all.

Ellie's tummy felt funny. She knew Mama would be upset about the messy yard. But cleaning up seemed like such hard work.

"I have an idea!" Ellie said out loud. She would try to clean up without leaving her swing.

Ellie stretched her long trunk toward a red ball. She reached and reached. Her trunk wiggled in the air. Almost... almost... but the ball was too far away. She tried reaching for a yellow block. Still too far.

Ellie huffed and puffed. This wasn't working. She felt frustrated. Her trunk felt tired from all that stretching.

"This is impossible," she sighed. The swing squeaked sadly as she slowed down.

Just then, Mama Elephant walked outside. She saw the messy yard and Ellie on the swing.

"Oh my," Mama said gently. "What happened here?"

Ellie's cheeks felt hot. "I made a mess yesterday. I tried to clean up from my swing, but I can't reach anything."

Mama smiled kindly. "Some jobs need us to get up and move around. Would you like help?"

Ellie thought about this. Maybe she needed to try a different way.

"I'll try again," Ellie said bravely. She took a deep breath and climbed down from her swing. Her feet touched the soft grass.

First, Ellie picked up her teddy bear. It felt dusty but huggable. She brushed off the dirt and placed it in the toy box. One toy done!

Next, she gathered the colorful blocks. They felt smooth and cool in her trunk. Red ones, blue ones, yellow ones - she sorted them carefully. The blocks made a satisfying clink as they dropped into their container.

The bouncy balls were trickier. They rolled away when she tried to grab them. But Ellie had an idea. She used her big feet to gently trap each ball, then picked them up with her trunk. Success!

As Ellie worked, something wonderful happened. Cleaning up felt good! Each toy she picked up made the yard look better. Her trunk got stronger with each reach and lift.

Soon, all the toys were in their proper places. The yard looked beautiful again. Ellie felt proud and happy inside.

"Well done!" Mama said, giving Ellie a big hug. "You didn't give up when the first way didn't work."

Ellie beamed. "I learned something important. Some problems need me to get up and try harder."

She climbed back onto her swing, but now it felt even better. The yard sparkled clean around her. The swing creaked its happy song. The sun felt warm and wonderful.

"Tomorrow I'll put my toys away right after playing," Ellie promised herself.

As she swung gently back and forth, Ellie felt grateful. She had learned that trying different ways to solve problems was worth it. Sometimes the best feeling comes after working hard.

The swing carried her high into the bright blue sky, and Ellie smiled her biggest elephant smile.

The end.
