# Story story_937

**Bella and the Messy Playground**

Baby Bella loved her bright red swing. She pumped her chubby legs back and forth. The swing creaked softly in the warm sunshine.

"Wheee!" Bella giggled as she swayed gently.

From her swing, Bella could see the whole playground. There were colorful blocks scattered everywhere. Toy cars lay upside down in the sandbox. Dolls sat in puddles from yesterday's rain.

"Oh no," Bella said softly. "Everything is so messy."

Mama walked over with a big smile. "Time to clean up, sweet pea," she said. "Then we can have our snack."

Bella's bottom lip stuck out. She did not want to stop swinging. She did not want to clean up toys. Cleaning looked hard and boring.

"I don't want to," Bella said, crossing her tiny arms.

Mama sat on the bench nearby. "The toys are sad when they're all mixed up," she explained gently. "They want to go home to their special places."

Bella looked at the messy toys again. A teddy bear lay face-down in the dirt. A puzzle piece sat all alone by the slide. Maybe Mama was right.

But cleaning still seemed too hard.

Bella had an idea. She would clean from her swing! She stretched her little arms as far as they could reach. She tried to grab a block near her feet.

The swing moved away from the toy. Bella stretched harder. The swing swayed back and forth. She could not reach anything.

"This isn't working," Bella huffed.

She tried leaning way over to one side. The swing tilted. Bella felt wobbly and scared. She quickly sat up straight.

"Cleaning from the swing is too tricky," she admitted to Mama.

Mama nodded. "Some jobs need us to use our feet," she said with a warm smile.

Bella looked at all the scattered toys again. The teddy bear still looked sad in the dirt. The toy cars needed help getting right-side up.

"Maybe I can try a different way," Bella said thoughtfully.

She took a deep breath. Then she carefully climbed down from her beloved swing. Her bare feet touched the soft grass. It felt cool and squishy between her toes.

Bella walked to the teddy bear first. She picked him up and brushed off the dirt. "There you go, Teddy," she said sweetly. She placed him gently in the toy box.

Next, she found three red blocks. They felt smooth and warm from the sun. She stacked them carefully in their special corner.

"This is actually fun!" Bella discovered.

She flipped over the toy cars in the sandbox. The sand felt grainy on her fingers. She lined up the cars in a neat row.

Mama helped her find the puzzle pieces. Together they put each piece in the puzzle box. The pieces made soft clicking sounds as they dropped in.

"Look at this pretty doll," Bella said, picking up a doll with yellow hair. She dried off the doll with a soft towel. Then she placed her gently in the dollhouse.

Step by step, toy by toy, the playground became neat and tidy. Bella felt proud as she worked. Her cheeks were pink from the exercise.

"We did it!" Bella clapped her hands together.

The playground looked beautiful now. Every toy had found its home. The teddy bear smiled from the toy box. The cars sat ready for the next adventure.

"You worked so hard," Mama said, giving Bella a big hug. "How do you feel?"

"Happy!" Bella giggled. "The toys look happy too."

Mama unpacked their snack. Apple slices and crackers tasted extra delicious after all that work.

Then Bella climbed back onto her favorite swing. She looked around at the clean playground. Everything sparkled in the afternoon sunshine.

"I like cleaning up," Bella told Mama. "It makes everything pretty."

From her swing, Bella could see how nice the playground looked. She felt proud and happy inside. Tomorrow she would clean up right away.

But for now, she would swing and enjoy the beautiful, tidy playground she had helped create.

"Wheee!" Bella laughed, swinging higher than ever before.

The end.
