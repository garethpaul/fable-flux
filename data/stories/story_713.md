# Story story_713

Emma looked at the colorful art supplies on the table. Paintbrushes sat in tall jars. Bright paints waited in little cups. Paper lay ready and white.

"It's art time!" called Miss Sarah cheerfully.

Emma's tummy felt funny. She didn't move from her chair. Art time made her worried. What if her painting looked messy? What if the other kids laughed?

"I don't want to paint today," Emma whispered.

Miss Sarah sat down beside her. "That's okay, Emma. Would you like to watch first?"

Emma nodded slowly. She watched Ben dip his brush in blue paint. The brush made swooshy sounds on paper. Lily mixed red and yellow together. The colors swirled like a sunset.

"The paint smells like flowers," said Lily, giggling.

Emma's nose twitched. The paint did smell nice. Sweet and fresh, like her mom's garden.

"Look!" shouted Ben. "My blue looks like the ocean!"

Emma leaned forward. Ben's painting had wavy lines. They really did look like water moving. Maybe painting wasn't so scary after all.

"Would you like to try just one color?" asked Miss Sarah gently.

Emma thought about it. One color didn't seem too hard. She picked up a small brush. It felt soft in her fingers.

"I'll try yellow," Emma said quietly.

Miss Sarah helped Emma dip the brush in sunny yellow paint. The paint felt smooth and cool. Emma touched the brush to her paper very carefully.

A tiny yellow dot appeared. Then another. And another.

"Those look like little suns!" said Ben, peeking over.

Emma smiled a tiny smile. She made more dots. Some big, some small. The brush felt friendly now, like a magic wand.

"Can I try orange too?" Emma asked.

"Of course!" Miss Sarah said with a big smile.

Emma dipped her brush in orange paint. The color was warm and bright. She painted little circles around her yellow dots. They looked like flowers blooming in sunshine.

"Beautiful!" whispered Lily. "They're so happy!"

Emma's heart felt warm and bubbly. Her painting was making other kids smile. She tried red next. Then purple. Each color felt different on the brush.

The red was thick and rich. The purple was smooth like silk. Emma painted swirls and lines. She painted hearts and stars.

"This is fun!" Emma said, laughing.

Her paper filled with colors. Yellow flowers danced with purple swirls. Red hearts floated near blue clouds. Orange butterflies seemed to flutter across the page.

Emma's hands were covered in paint. Her apron had rainbow spots. She didn't mind at all. The mess felt like part of the magic.

"Time to clean up," announced Miss Sarah.

"Already?" Emma asked, surprised.

She looked at her colorful painting. It was bright and cheerful. It made her feel proud and happy inside.

At the sink, Emma washed her hands. The warm water felt good. She watched the colors swirl down the drain like tiny rainbows.

"Emma, your painting is wonderful," said Miss Sarah.

Emma beamed. "It was scary at first. But then it got really fun!"

"Sometimes the best things start a little scary," Miss Sarah said wisely.

Emma carefully hung her painting on the drying rack. The colors seemed to glow under the classroom lights. Other kids gathered around to look.

"I love your flowers!" said Lily.

"The butterflies look real!" added Ben.

Emma felt proud and excited. Art time hadn't been scary after all. It had been magical and wonderful.

As Emma packed her backpack, she thought about tomorrow. Would there be more paint? Maybe crayons or markers? Her fingers tingled thinking about all the colors waiting to be discovered.

"Miss Sarah," Emma called out. "Will we have art time again tomorrow?"

"We sure will," Miss Sarah replied with a warm smile.

Emma practically skipped to the door. She couldn't wait to tell her mom about her beautiful painting. And she definitely couldn't wait for tomorrow's art time.

Sometimes trying something new was the best adventure of all.

The end.
