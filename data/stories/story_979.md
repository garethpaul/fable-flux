# Story story_979

**The Turtle Who Wanted to Swing**

Shelly the turtle loved playing in the sunny park. Every morning, she walked slowly to her favorite spot. The big swing set stood tall under the oak tree.

Three little rabbits were already there. They hopped onto the swings with ease. "Wheee!" they called out as they soared high. Their fluffy tails bounced in the warm breeze.

Shelly watched with her big brown eyes. The swings looked so fun! She wanted to fly through the air too. She wanted to feel the wind on her green shell.

"Can I swing with you?" Shelly asked politely.

The rabbits stopped swinging. They looked down at Shelly's short legs. "You're too small to reach," said the first rabbit. "And too slow to jump up," added the second rabbit.

Shelly felt sad. Her shell felt heavy on her back. She tucked her head inside for a moment.

But Shelly was brave. She would not give up! She had an idea.

Shelly found a small log nearby. She pushed it with her nose. The log was rough and smelled like pine. It rolled slowly across the soft grass.

"This will help me reach!" she said to herself.

Shelly climbed onto the log. She stretched her little legs up high. She reached for the swing seat with her front feet.

Almost there! But the log rolled away. Shelly tumbled onto the grass with a soft thud. The rabbits giggled as they kept swinging.

Shelly's shell was dusty now. She felt disappointed. But she did not cry. Instead, she thought harder.

"There must be another way," she whispered.

Shelly looked around the park again. She spotted something perfect! A pile of smooth rocks sat near the flower bed. They smelled like rain from yesterday.

One by one, Shelly carried the rocks. She stacked them carefully under the swing set. The rocks felt cool under her feet. They made a perfect little staircase!

"Look what I built!" Shelly called to the rabbits.

Step by step, Shelly climbed her rock stairs. Her claws clicked softly on each stone. Finally, she reached the swing seat!

Shelly held on tight with all four feet. "Push me, please!" she asked kindly.

The rabbits stopped their own swinging. They had been watching Shelly work so hard. "We want to help!" said the third rabbit.

Together, the three rabbits gave Shelly a gentle push. Up she went! The swing carried her higher and higher. The wind tickled her neck. The sun warmed her shell.

"This is amazing!" Shelly laughed with joy.

She could see the whole park from up high. The flowers looked like tiny rainbows below. The trees waved their branches at her. Everything sparkled in the golden sunlight.

"You're really smart, Shelly!" said the first rabbit. "We're sorry we didn't help before," said the second rabbit. "Your rock stairs are brilliant!" added the third rabbit.

From that day on, all four friends swung together. Shelly's rock stairs helped other small animals too. A tiny mouse used them the next day. Then a young squirrel climbed up to swing.

Shelly felt proud and happy. She had turned a problem into a solution. She had made the playground better for everyone.

Every evening, Shelly would clean her rock stairs. She wanted them ready for tomorrow's fun. The rocks stayed sturdy and safe.

"I'm glad I didn't give up," Shelly said to herself. She watched the sunset paint the sky orange and pink. "Good things happen when you keep trying."

The rabbits agreed. They had learned something important too. Everyone deserves a chance to play and have fun.

As the stars began to twinkle, Shelly smiled. Tomorrow would bring another wonderful day of swinging with her friends. Her rock stairs would be waiting, and so would her new rabbit friends.

Sometimes the best solutions come from thinking differently. And sometimes helping others makes everything more fun.

The end.
