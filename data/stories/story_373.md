# Grandma's Field Friends

Grandma Rose loved her big green field. Every morning, she walked outside with her walking stick. The grass tickled her toes. The sun warmed her face.

But today felt different. Grandma Rose looked around. The field was very quiet. No children were visiting. No neighbors were nearby.

"I wish I had someone to play with," said Grandma Rose. She sat on her old wooden bench. The wind made lonely sounds through the trees.

Grandma Rose was not ready to give up. She had an idea! She walked to her garden shed. Inside, she found a bright red ball.

"Maybe I can play catch," she said. Grandma Rose threw the ball high into the air. Up, up, up it went! Down, down, down it came. She caught it with both hands.

"That was fun," she said. "But playing alone is not the same."

Then Grandma Rose heard a sound. "Chirp, chirp, chirp!" A little blue bird sat on the fence post. It tilted its head and looked at her.

"Hello, little bird," said Grandma Rose. "Do you want to play?" She rolled the ball gently toward the bird. The bird hopped closer. It pecked at the red ball with its tiny beak.

"You like my ball!" laughed Grandma Rose. The bird chirped happily.

Soon, another sound came from the field. "Buzz, buzz, buzz!" A fat bumblebee flew in circles around Grandma Rose. The bee seemed to dance in the warm air.

"Hello, buzzy bee," said Grandma Rose. She hummed a little tune. The bee buzzed along with her music. Together they made a sweet song.

Next, Grandma Rose noticed something moving in the tall grass. A brown rabbit hopped out. Its nose twitched. Its ears stood up tall.

"Come here, little rabbit," called Grandma Rose softly. She sat very still. The rabbit came closer and closer. Soon it was right next to her feet.

Grandma Rose reached into her pocket. She found some lettuce from lunch. The rabbit nibbled the green leaves. Its whiskers tickled her fingers.

"This is wonderful," said Grandma Rose. "But I want to play games!"

She had another idea. Grandma Rose walked to different spots in the field. She made a circle with small white stones. In the middle, she put her red ball.

"Let's play a game," she called to her new friends. The bird flew down. The bee buzzed lower. The rabbit hopped closer.

Grandma Rose clapped her hands. "We can take turns moving the ball!" She pushed the ball with her walking stick. It rolled to the bird.

The bird pushed the ball with its wing. The ball rolled to the bee. The bee bumped the ball with its fuzzy body. The ball rolled to the rabbit.

The rabbit pushed the ball with its nose. Around and around the ball went! Everyone was playing together.

"What a perfect game!" said Grandma Rose. She clapped and laughed. The bird chirped joyfully. The bee buzzed with happiness. The rabbit wiggled its tail.

More friends came to join the fun. A butterfly with orange wings danced above them. Two squirrels ran down from the oak tree. A friendly cricket jumped into the circle.

Soon, Grandma Rose's field was full of happy sounds. Chirping, buzzing, squeaking, and laughing filled the air. The sun seemed to shine brighter. The grass seemed greener.

"I thought I had no one to play with," said Grandma Rose. "But friends were all around me! I just needed to look and listen."

As the sun began to set, all the animals gathered close to Grandma Rose. She felt so happy and warm inside. Her field was not empty anymore. It was full of wonderful friends.

"Thank you for playing with me," she whispered. "Tomorrow we can play again!"

The bird chirped goodnight. The bee buzzed softly. The rabbit touched her hand with its nose. All her new friends said goodbye in their own special ways.

Grandma Rose walked back to her house. She was not lonely anymore. She had learned something important: friends can be found everywhere if you keep trying and keep your heart open.

The field was quiet again, but it was a happy quiet. Tomorrow would bring another day of friendship and fun.

The end.
