# Story story_663

**The Puzzle and the Tiger**

Puzzle sat alone on the bright red swing. Back and forth, back and forth she went. The metal chains squeaked softly in the warm afternoon breeze.

Puzzle was a small, colorful piece with bumpy edges. She had pretty blue and yellow swirls on her surface. But today, she felt sad and lonely.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," Puzzle sighed. She dragged her corners in the sandy ground below. The playground was quiet except for chirping birds.

From behind the tall oak tree, Tiger watched. Tiger had bright orange stripes and kind golden eyes. He wanted to play too, but he felt nervous.

"What if she doesn't like me?" Tiger whispered to himself. His black stripes trembled a little. He took a deep breath that smelled like fresh grass.

Tiger stepped out slowly. His paws made soft sounds on the wood chips. "Hello," he said in his gentlest voice. "Can I swing too?"

Puzzle looked up with surprise. Her edges sparkled in the sunlight. "You want to play with me?" she asked.

"Yes, please," Tiger said. His tail swished hopefully. "I've been watching you. You look like fun."

Puzzle's sadness melted away like ice cream in summer. "Yes! Come sit on the yellow swing next to me!"

Tiger climbed onto the sunny yellow swing. It creaked happily under his weight. Soon they were swinging side by side.

"This is wonderful!" Puzzle laughed. Her voice tinkled like little bells. The wind made her feel like she was flying.

"I love how high we can go!" Tiger roared softly with joy. His orange fur rippled in the breeze.

They swung faster and higher. Their swings moved like a dance in the sky. Puzzle's blue and yellow colors caught the light. Tiger's stripes looked like flowing ribbons.

"Let's try something new," Puzzle suggested. "Can you twist your swing around?"

Tiger spun his swing in circles. Around and around he went until the chains were all twisted up. Then he lifted his paws and spun back down super fast.

"Wheee!" Tiger shouted with delight. His golden eyes sparkled with happiness.

Puzzle tried it too. She spun and spun until she was dizzy with giggles. When she unwound, the world looked like a colorful blur.

"That was amazing!" Puzzle cheered. "You're the best friend ever!"

"You are too!" Tiger purred loudly. The sound was warm and rumbly like a cozy blanket.

They played more games on the swings. They pretended to be birds flying through clouds. They sang silly songs about cheese and trees. They told jokes that made them laugh until their bellies hurt.

As the sun began to set, painting the sky pink and orange, they slowed their swings down.

"I was so lonely before," Puzzle admitted. "But now I'm the happiest I've ever been."

Tiger nodded, his whiskers twitching with a smile. "Me too. I was scared to ask if I could play. But I'm so glad I did."

"Friends should always ask to play together," Puzzle said wisely. "That's how the best friendships start."

They sat quietly for a moment, gently swinging back and forth. The evening air smelled sweet like honeysuckle flowers.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" Puzzle asked hopefully.

"Every day!" Tiger promised. "We can try the monkey bars next time!"

Puzzle clapped her corners together with excitement. "And the slide! And the seesaw!"

As the first stars appeared in the purple sky, Puzzle and Tiger said goodbye. But they weren't sad anymore. Tomorrow would bring new adventures with their new best friend.

The swing set stood peacefully in the moonlight, waiting for two friends to return and fill it with laughter again.

From that day on, Puzzle and Tiger played together every afternoon. They learned that friendship makes everything more fun, and being brave enough to say hello can lead to the most wonderful surprises.

The end.
