# Story story_710

**The Shy Little Drum**

Maya loved playtime at school. Every day after lunch, all the children gathered on the soft blue carpet. Today, their teacher Miss Sarah brought out the music box.

"Time to make music together!" Miss Sarah said with a big smile.

The music box was full of wonderful instruments. There were shiny bells that jingled. There were wooden sticks that clicked. There were maracas that shook with a happy sound.

At the bottom of the box sat a small red drum. The drum had a smooth, round top. It had a wooden rim that felt warm to touch. But the little drum felt very shy.

"Pick me! Pick me!" called the bells.

"Choose us!" sang the maracas.

The drum stayed quiet in the corner. It watched as children picked up other instruments. The drum felt too nervous to make any sound.

Maya looked into the music box. She saw the little red drum hiding behind a tambourine. Maya had never played a drum before. She felt a little nervous too.

"Hello, little drum," Maya whispered softly.

The drum's surface seemed to shimmer slightly. Maya gently picked it up. The drum felt smooth and cool in her hands.

Miss Sarah started playing gentle music on her guitar. All around Maya, children began to play their instruments. The bells made tiny silver sounds. The maracas made soft swishing noises.

Maya held the drum carefully. She wasn't sure what to do. The drum felt heavy and important in her small hands.

"Just tap gently," Miss Sarah said kindly. "Let the drum tell you what it wants to do."

Maya touched the drum's surface with one finger. It made a soft, deep sound. The sound felt warm and friendly. Maya smiled and tried again.

This time she used her whole hand. The drum made a bigger, rounder sound. The sound seemed to bounce around the room like a happy ball.

Something magical began to happen. When Maya played the drum, she could feel the music in her chest. The drum's beat matched her heartbeat. Thump-thump, thump-thump.

The other children heard the drum's steady rhythm. They began to play along with Maya's beat. The bells jingled in time. The maracas shook on the beat. Everyone was making music together.

Maya felt the drum getting warmer under her hands. The drum seemed happy to be making music. It wasn't shy anymore. Maya wasn't nervous anymore either.

"Listen to that beautiful rhythm!" Miss Sarah said. "Maya and her drum are helping everyone play together."

Maya played louder and stronger. The drum's voice filled the room with rich, deep sounds. Other children began to clap along. Some children started to dance.

The music room filled with joyful sounds. Maya felt proud and happy. The little drum felt proud too. Together, they were making something wonderful.

When the music ended, Maya hugged the drum gently. "Thank you for playing with me," she whispered.

The drum seemed to glow with happiness. It had found its voice. It had found a friend.

"That was beautiful music," Miss Sarah said. "Maya, would you like to play the drum again tomorrow?"

Maya nodded eagerly. "Yes, please! Can I play with the same drum?"

"Of course," Miss Sarah smiled. "I think this drum likes playing with you."

As Maya carefully placed the drum back in the music box, she patted its surface one more time. The drum made one last gentle sound, like it was saying goodbye.

The other instruments welcomed the drum back. "You sounded wonderful!" they seemed to say.

Maya couldn't wait for tomorrow's playtime. She had made a new friend. The shy little drum had found its courage. Together, they would make more beautiful music.

That night, Maya dreamed of drums and music. In her dreams, she and her drum friend played songs that made everyone dance and smile.

Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough.

The end.
