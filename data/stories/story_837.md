# Story story_837

**The Little Red Plane and Buzzy the Duck**

On Tommy's bedroom shelf sat a little red plane. His name was Zoom. Zoom had shiny silver wings. He had a bright yellow propeller too.

Every morning, Zoom watched the sun peek through the window. The warm light made his red paint sparkle. But Zoom felt sad inside his metal heart.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," Zoom whispered. His voice was tiny and soft.

Zoom spent his days alone on the high shelf. He watched dust dance in the sunbeams. He listened to birds chirping outside. Sometimes he pretended to fly around the room.

Down below on the toy chest lived Buzzy. Buzzy was a bright yellow rubber duck. She had an orange beak and kind black eyes. Buzzy squeaked when Tommy squeezed her.

Every day, Buzzy looked up at Zoom. She wanted to be his friend. But the shelf was so very high. Buzzy was too small to climb up there.

"Hello up there!" Buzzy called one sunny Tuesday. Her voice squeaked a little.

Zoom's propeller spun with excitement. "Hello down there!" he called back. "I'm Zoom!"

"I'm Buzzy!" she squeaked happily. "Want to play together?"

Zoom's wings drooped sadly. "I can't get down. You can't get up."

Buzzy thought and thought. Her rubber head felt warm from thinking so hard. Then she had a wonderful idea.

"Wait here!" Buzzy squeaked.

Buzzy waddled across the soft carpet. The blue fibers tickled her orange feet. She pushed Tommy's building blocks with her beak. The wooden blocks made clicking sounds.

Buzzy stacked one block. Then another block on top. The tower grew taller and taller. Soon she had built steps to the shelf.

"I'm coming up!" Buzzy called.

Step by step, Buzzy climbed her block tower. Her little webbed feet gripped each block carefully. Up, up, up she went.

At last, Buzzy reached the high shelf. "Hi Zoom!" she puffed. She was tired but happy.

"Buzzy! You made it!" Zoom's propeller spun with joy.

The two friends looked at each other. Zoom's red paint gleamed in the sunlight. Buzzy's yellow feathers felt soft and warm.

"What shall we play?" asked Buzzy.

"Let's fly around the room!" said Zoom.

But Buzzy looked worried. "I can't fly. I'm just a duck."

Zoom thought for a moment. His kind heart had another idea.

"Hop on my back!" Zoom said. "We'll fly together!"

Buzzy carefully climbed onto Zoom's smooth red back. She held tight to his silver wings. Zoom's propeller began to spin faster and faster.

"Ready?" asked Zoom.

"Ready!" squeaked Buzzy.

Off they flew around Tommy's bedroom! They soared over the soft blue bed. They zoomed past the tall brown dresser. They flew circles around the spinning ceiling fan.

The wind felt cool on their faces. Buzzy's feathers fluffed in the breeze. Zoom's engine hummed a happy tune.

"This is amazing!" Buzzy squeaked with delight.

"Flying is better with friends!" Zoom called back.

They landed gently on the window sill. Outside, real birds were flying in the bright blue sky. Zoom and Buzzy watched them together.

"Look!" said Buzzy. "Those birds are friends too!"

"Just like us," agreed Zoom.

From that day on, Zoom and Buzzy played together every day. Sometimes they flew around the room. Sometimes they told stories on the sunny shelf. Sometimes they just sat quietly and watched the world outside.

Tommy noticed his toys had moved. He smiled when he saw them sitting together. "My plane and duck are best friends," he said happily.

Zoom's red paint seemed brighter now. Buzzy's yellow feathers felt softer. Their hearts were full of friendship and joy.

Every night, they watched the stars twinkle outside the window. Every morning, they greeted the sunshine together.

"I'm so glad you climbed up here," Zoom said one evening.

"I'm so glad you let me fly," Buzzy replied.

The two friends settled down for a peaceful sleep. Tomorrow would bring new adventures. But tonight, they were simply happy to be together.

Neither toy would ever feel lonely again.

The end.
