# Story story_1098

**The Hallway Friends**

Little Blue Bird sat alone in the long hallway. Her tiny feet tapped on the smooth floor. The hallway was quiet and empty. Blue Bird felt very lonely.

She had a small red ball. Blue Bird pushed it with her beak. The ball rolled down the hallway. It made a soft bouncing sound. Blue Bird flew after it slowly.

"This is not much fun," she chirped sadly. "Playing alone is boring."

From around the corner came big footsteps. THUMP, THUMP, THUMP. Blue Bird looked up. A friendly orange tiger walked into the hallway.

Tiger had bright yellow eyes. His orange fur had black stripes. He was much bigger than Blue Bird. But his face looked kind and gentle.

"Hello, little bird," said Tiger softly. "What are you doing?"

Blue Bird felt shy. "I am playing with my ball. But it is not very fun alone."

Tiger's ears perked up. His long tail swished back and forth. "Can I play too?" he asked hopefully. "I love to play games."

Blue Bird looked worried. "You are so big. I am so small. How can we play together?"

Tiger sat down on the floor. Now he was not so tall. "We can find a way," he said with a warm smile.

Blue Bird thought for a moment. Then she had an idea. "Maybe you can roll the ball to me. Then I can fly it back to you."

"That sounds perfect!" said Tiger excitedly.

Tiger gently pushed the red ball with his big paw. The ball rolled slowly toward Blue Bird. She flapped her wings and caught it in her tiny feet.

"I got it!" chirped Blue Bird happily.

She flew the ball back to Tiger. He caught it carefully with his soft paws. Tiger was very gentle with the small ball.

"This is fun!" said Tiger. "You are good at this game."

They played back and forth. The ball rolled and flew around the hallway. Blue Bird's wings made soft fluttering sounds. Tiger's paws made quiet padding noises on the floor.

Soon they tried new games. Tiger would hide behind a corner. Blue Bird would fly around looking for him. When she found him, they both laughed together.

"Found you!" Blue Bird would sing.

"You are so good at finding me!" Tiger would say.

Then Blue Bird had another idea. "Let's race down the hallway!"

Tiger looked puzzled. "But you can fly. That is not fair to me."

Blue Bird thought hard. "I will hop like you do. No flying allowed!"

So Blue Bird hopped on her tiny feet. Tiger walked slowly beside her. They moved down the long hallway together. Their different footsteps made a happy rhythm.

"We both win!" said Blue Bird.

"Yes! We are both fast!" agreed Tiger.

As they played, Blue Bird felt warm and happy inside. Tiger felt joyful too. The empty hallway was now full of laughter and friendship.

"I was so lonely before," said Blue Bird. "Now I feel happy."

Tiger nodded his big head. "I was looking for a friend. I am glad I found you."

The afternoon sun shone through the hallway windows. It made pretty patterns on the floor. Blue Bird and Tiger sat together in the warm sunlight.

"Will you be my friend?" asked Blue Bird.

"Yes!" said Tiger. "Best friends forever!"

They decided to meet in the hallway every day. They would play their special games together. Blue Bird would bring her red ball. Tiger would bring his gentle smile.

From that day on, the hallway was never quiet. It was filled with the sounds of friendship. Little footsteps and big footsteps. Chirping and soft roars of laughter.

Blue Bird learned that friends come in all sizes. Tiger learned that small friends can have big hearts.

Every evening, they would say goodbye. "See you tomorrow!" they would call to each other.

The hallway had become a magical place. It was where a tiny bird and a big tiger found the best thing of all - true friendship.

And they lived happily ever after, playing together every single day.

The end.
