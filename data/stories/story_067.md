# Milo's Big Bath Adventure

Milo the monkey loved his cozy living room. He had soft blue cushions to bounce on. He had colorful books to look at. He had his favorite red ball to roll around. The living room was perfect for playing!

"Milo!" called Mama Monkey from the kitchen. "Bath time!"

Milo's ears drooped down. His tail stopped swishing. He did NOT want to take a bath. The water felt too splashy. The soap made too many bubbles. Baths were just too... wet!

"I don't want a bath," Milo said softly. He curled up behind the big green chair. Maybe Mama wouldn't find him there.

"Where's my little monkey?" Mama called. Her footsteps came closer. Tap, tap, tap across the wooden floor.

Milo tried to make himself very small. He tucked his tail under his body. He closed his eyes tight. If he couldn't see Mama, maybe she couldn't see him!

But Mama found him anyway. "There you are, silly monkey! Come on, the warm water is waiting."

"No thank you," Milo said. He shook his head back and forth. "I like being dirty."

Mama sat down on the soft carpet. "Tell me what's wrong, Milo."

Milo's bottom lip wiggled. "Baths are scary. The water goes splash! The bubbles tickle my nose. And... and... I might slip!"

Mama's eyes grew warm and kind. "Oh, my brave little monkey. What if we made bath time more fun?"

Milo peeked up with one eye. "How?"

"Well," Mama said, tapping her chin. "What if we brought some friends to help?"

Milo sat up straight. "Friends?"

Together, they walked to Milo's toy box. It was painted bright yellow like sunshine. Milo picked out his rubber duck. Squeak, squeak! He chose his small blue boat too. It had tiny white sails.

"These can come with me?" Milo asked.

"Of course! They love water," Mama said with a smile.

In the bathroom, Mama filled the tub. The water made gentle splashing sounds. It looked warm and inviting. Steam danced up like little clouds.

"Can I test it first?" Milo asked.

"Great idea!" Mama said.

Milo dipped one finger in the water. It felt nice and warm. Like soup, but for playing! He dipped his whole hand in. The water swirled around his fingers.

"It's not scary," Milo whispered.

He put his rubber duck in first. It bobbed up and down happily. The little boat sailed in circles. Milo giggled as bubbles popped around them.

"My turn!" Milo said.

Step by step, Milo climbed into the tub. The warm water hugged his toes. It felt like a cozy blanket, but wet! He sat down slowly. The water came up to his tummy.

"This is actually fun!" Milo laughed.

He sailed his boat around his knees. He made the duck swim in circles. The bubbles felt soft and fluffy on his arms. They smelled like sweet flowers.

Mama helped wash Milo's fur. She was very gentle. She sang a silly song about monkeys and bubbles. Milo sang along, making up funny words.

When bath time was over, Mama wrapped Milo in a big, fluffy towel. It was warm from the dryer and felt like a hug.

"I did it!" Milo said proudly. "I took a whole bath!"

"You were so brave," Mama said. "You tried something new, even when it felt scary."

Milo's chest puffed out with pride. His fur was clean and soft. He smelled like flowers and felt fresh all over.

Back in the living room, Milo bounced on his blue cushions. But now he felt different. He felt proud and brave and clean!

"Mama," Milo said, "can I take another bath tomorrow?"

Mama laughed and gave him a big squeeze. "Of course, my brave little monkey!"

That night, Milo dreamed of sailing boats and floating ducks. He dreamed of warm water and fluffy bubbles. And he couldn't wait for his next bath adventure!

Sometimes scary things become fun when we try them with help from people who love us.

The end.
