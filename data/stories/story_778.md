# Story story_778

**Penny the Puppet's Lost Doll**

Penny the puppet lived in a cozy tree house. Her wooden arms moved up and down. Her painted smile was always bright. She wore a red dress with tiny white dots.

Penny loved her special doll named Rosie. Rosie had soft yellow hair. Her blue dress sparkled in the sunlight. Penny took Rosie everywhere she went.

One sunny morning, Penny woke up early. She reached for Rosie on her little bed. But Rosie was not there! Penny looked under her pillow. She looked in her toy box. Rosie was nowhere to find.

"Oh no!" said Penny. "Where is my dear Rosie?"

Penny remembered playing outside yesterday. She had taken Rosie to pick berries. They had walked deep into the green woods. The tall trees made dancing shadows. Colorful flowers smelled so sweet.

"Rosie must be in the woods!" Penny said.

Penny hurried outside to look. The morning air felt cool and fresh. Birds sang happy songs in the trees. Penny's wooden feet made soft sounds on the forest path.

She looked behind every big tree trunk. She searched under leafy bushes. She checked near the babbling creek. But Rosie was not anywhere.

Penny felt very sad. Tears rolled down her painted cheeks. "I will never find my Rosie," she whispered.

Then Penny had an idea. "I will ask my friends for help!"

She found Benny the bear eating honey. "Have you seen my doll Rosie?" asked Penny.

"No," said Benny. "But I will help you look!"

They searched together by the old oak tree. They looked in the flower meadow. They checked near the rocky hill. Still no Rosie anywhere.

"Don't give up," said Benny kindly. "Keep trying, Penny."

Next, Penny found Lucy the rabbit. Lucy was nibbling fresh clover. "Will you help me find Rosie?" asked Penny.

"Of course!" said Lucy. Her fluffy tail wiggled.

The three friends looked everywhere together. They searched high and low. They moved aside fallen branches. They looked under colorful leaves.

Penny felt tired and worried. "Maybe Rosie is lost forever," she sighed.

"Wait!" said Lucy. "Let's think carefully. Where exactly did you play yesterday?"

Penny closed her eyes tight. She remembered the sweet berry patch. She remembered the sunny clearing. She remembered the old fallen log.

"The hollow log!" Penny shouted. "We played hide and seek there!"

The three friends ran to the fallen log. It was big and brown. Moss grew on its bumpy bark. The inside was dark and quiet.

Penny knelt down carefully. She reached inside the hollow space. Her wooden fingers felt something soft and familiar.

"Rosie!" Penny cried with joy.

She pulled out her precious doll. Rosie's yellow hair was a bit messy. Some leaves stuck to her blue dress. But she was perfectly safe.

Penny hugged Rosie tight against her chest. "I missed you so much!" she said.

Benny and Lucy clapped their paws together. "Hooray!" they cheered happily.

"Thank you for helping me," said Penny. "I could not have found Rosie without you."

"Friends always help friends," said Lucy.

"And never give up when something is important," added Benny.

Penny nodded her wooden head. "I learned something today. When I have a big problem, I should keep trying. I should ask friends for help too."

The three friends walked home together. The warm sun shone through the green leaves. Pretty butterflies danced around them. Everything felt perfect again.

That night, Penny tucked Rosie safely in bed. She made sure her doll was right beside her. "Tomorrow we will play again," Penny whispered. "But I will be more careful with you."

Penny closed her painted eyes. She felt happy and proud. She had not given up. She had asked for help. She had found her precious Rosie.

The moon smiled down on the little tree house. All was well in the peaceful woods.

The end.
