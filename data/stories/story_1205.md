# Story story_1205

**The Magic Story Time**

Every evening at seven o'clock, Mama called out sweetly. "Story time, girls!" Her voice floated up the stairs. Emma always ran down right away. She loved curling up with books. But her little sister Lily usually hid.

Tonight was no different. Emma bounced on the soft blue couch. She picked out three colorful books. Mama smiled and patted the cushion next to her. "Where's Lily?" she asked gently.

Upstairs, Lily sat under her bed. She didn't like story time. The words looked too squiggly. The pictures seemed too busy. Reading felt hard and confusing. "I don't want to!" she called down.

Emma climbed the creaky wooden stairs. She found Lily's bare feet sticking out. "Come on, Lily," Emma said softly. "Stories are fun!" But Lily shook her head. Her curly hair bounced against the floor.

"The letters dance around," Lily whispered. "I can't catch them." Her voice sounded sad and small. Emma's heart felt heavy. She wanted her sister to love books too.

Emma had a wonderful idea. She grabbed her favorite stuffed elephant. His name was Peanut. "What if Peanut reads with us?" Emma suggested. Lily peeked out from under the bed. Peanut's gray trunk was soft and comforting.

Downstairs, Mama waited patiently. She held a book about a brave little mouse. When the girls appeared, Lily clutched Peanut tightly. "Peanut wants to hear the story," Lily announced quietly.

Mama opened the colorful book. The pages smelled like vanilla and adventure. As she began reading, something magical started happening. Mama used different voices for each character. The mouse squeaked high and tiny. The cat meowed low and rumbly.

Lily giggled when Mama made funny faces. Emma joined in with silly sound effects. "Whoosh!" went the wind. "Splash!" went the puddle. Soon Lily was making sounds too. "Squeak, squeak!" she said for the little mouse.

The pictures seemed to come alive. Lily could see the brave mouse's whiskers twitching. She could almost smell the cheese he was seeking. The scary cat didn't seem so frightening anymore. He was just part of the adventure.

"What happens next?" Lily asked excitedly. Her eyes sparkled with curiosity. Emma grinned widely. Mama's voice grew more dramatic. The mouse found his courage and saved the day.

When the story ended, Lily clapped her small hands. "Another one!" she pleaded. Peanut seemed to nod in agreement. Mama picked up a book about dancing bears. This time, Lily helped turn the pages carefully.

The second story was even more fun. Lily stood up and danced like the bears. Emma twirled around the living room. Mama clapped along to their rhythm. The house filled with laughter and joy.

As the bears found their perfect dance, Lily snuggled closer to Mama. The couch felt warm and safe. Emma's shoulder pressed against hers. Peanut sat in Lily's lap, listening carefully.

"I like the pictures," Lily said softly. "They tell stories too." Mama nodded and smiled. "Pictures and words work together," she explained gently. "Like you and Emma do."

For the third book, Lily chose one herself. It had a shiny cover with a friendly dragon. The dragon looked kind, not scary. His scales shimmered green and gold. As Mama read, Lily traced the letters with her finger.

The words didn't dance around anymore. They sat still and patient. Lily began to recognize some letters. "That's an 'L' like my name!" she said proudly. Emma cheered for her sister.

When story time ended, both girls felt sleepy and happy. Lily hugged Peanut tight. "Will you read tomorrow?" she asked Mama hopefully. Her voice was full of excitement now.

"Every night," Mama promised. She kissed both girls goodnight. Their cheeks felt warm and smooth.

As they climbed the stairs together, Lily whispered to Emma. "Story time is magical." Emma agreed completely. Tomorrow couldn't come soon enough.

That night, Lily dreamed of dancing bears and brave mice. In her dreams, she could read every single word.

The end.
