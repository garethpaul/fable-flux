# Story story_854

**Tommy Tiger's Special Snack Time**

The big clock on the kitchen wall went tick-tock, tick-tock. It was three o'clock. Time for afternoon snack!

"Tommy Tiger, come eat your snack," called Mama Tiger from the kitchen. She had set out a pretty blue plate on the table.

Tommy Tiger was playing with his toy cars in the living room. Zoom, zoom! He made the red car go fast around the track.

"I don't want a snack," Tommy said. His tummy felt full from lunch. The apple slices on his plate looked boring.

Mama Tiger sat down next to Tommy. "Your body needs good food to grow big and strong," she said gently. "Just like your toy cars need fuel to go zoom."

Tommy looked at his plate again. The apple slices were cut like little moons. There were also orange carrot sticks and some crackers shaped like fish.

"I'm not hungry," Tommy said with a big sigh.

Just then, something amazing happened. Tommy blinked his eyes. The apple slices started to glow with a soft, golden light!

"Wow!" Tommy whispered. The carrot sticks began to shimmer like tiny orange jewels. The fish crackers wiggled on the plate like they were swimming in an invisible ocean.

"What's happening, Mama?" Tommy asked, his eyes growing wide.

Mama Tiger smiled. "When you eat with a happy heart, food becomes magical," she said. "Try taking one small bite."

Tommy picked up a glowing apple slice. It felt cool and smooth in his paw. He took a tiny bite. Crunch! The apple tasted sweet like sunshine and made his mouth feel fresh.

Suddenly, Tommy heard a little voice. "Thank you for eating me!" squeaked the apple slice. "I'm giving you energy to run and play!"

Tommy giggled. He picked up an orange carrot stick. Crunch, crunch! It tasted earthy and sweet. "I'm making your eyes sparkle!" whispered the carrot.

The fish crackers made tiny splashing sounds as Tommy ate them. "We're swimming to your tummy to make you strong!" they sang in squeaky voices.

With each bite, Tommy felt more energetic. The colors in the kitchen looked brighter. The warm afternoon sun streaming through the window felt cozy on his striped fur.

"This is the best snack ever!" Tommy said, crunching happily. His tail swished back and forth with joy.

Mama Tiger clapped her paws together. "When we eat slowly and think about our food, we can taste all the wonderful flavors," she said.

Tommy ate every last crumb on his plate. His tummy felt perfectly full and happy. The magical glow faded, but Tommy still felt special inside.

"Mama, why did my food talk to me?" Tommy asked, licking his whiskers clean.

"Because you opened your heart to trying something new," Mama explained. "When we're grateful for our food, everything tastes better."

Tommy helped Mama Tiger wash his blue plate in the warm, soapy water. The bubbles tickled his paws and smelled like lemons.

"Can we have magical snack time again tomorrow?" Tommy asked hopefully.

Mama Tiger gave him a warm hug. "Every snack time can be magical if you eat with joy and thankfulness," she said.

Tommy thought about all the good foods that helped his body grow. Fruits gave him vitamins. Vegetables made him strong. Even water helped his body work properly.

The next afternoon, when the clock went tick-tock at three o'clock, Tommy ran to the kitchen. "I'm ready for snack time!" he announced cheerfully.

On his plate were strawberry slices, cheese cubes, and whole grain crackers. Tommy sat down with a big smile. He closed his eyes and took a deep breath, smelling the sweet strawberries.

"Thank you for this yummy snack," he said quietly. Then he took his first bite, ready for another magical adventure.

From that day on, Tommy Tiger always looked forward to snack time. He learned that the real magic wasn't in talking food, but in being grateful and trying new things with an open heart.

The end.
