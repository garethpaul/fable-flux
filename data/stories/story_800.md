# Story story_800

**Muddy Mike's Bath Time Adventure**

Mike was a bright red dump truck. He loved playing in the dirt all day. His wheels were covered in thick, brown mud. His bumper had grass stains. His windows were so dirty he could barely see.

"Bath time, Mike!" called Sarah, his little girl. She held a big, fluffy sponge. Warm water filled the blue plastic tub outside.

Mike's engine made a worried sound. "Rumble, rumble, no!" He didn't want a bath. Baths meant no more fun dirt. Baths meant sitting still. Mike liked moving and playing.

"I know you don't want to," Sarah said gently. "But you'll feel so much better." She patted his muddy hood with her small hand.

Mike rolled backward slowly. His wheels squeaked. Maybe he could hide behind the big oak tree. But Sarah followed him with the soapy water.

"Please, Mike?" Sarah asked. Her voice was kind and patient. "The water is nice and warm."

Mike touched one wheel to the water. It felt good! The warm water tickled his tire. It wasn't scary at all. It felt like a cozy hug.

Sarah dipped her sponge in the bubbly water. She started washing Mike's front bumper. The soap smelled like fresh lemons. Sweet and clean and bright.

Something amazing happened then. As Sarah scrubbed, Mike's red paint began to shine. It sparkled like a ruby in the sunshine. He had forgotten how bright and beautiful he was!

"Look at that pretty red!" Sarah laughed. Her eyes twinkled with joy. "You're so handsome, Mike!"

Mike felt proud. He liked seeing his shiny paint. The mud had hidden his beautiful color. Now he remembered why he loved being red.

Sarah washed his windows next. The glass became clear and sparkling. Mike could see everything perfectly now! The green grass looked greener. The blue sky looked bluer. The yellow flowers looked brighter.

"I can see so much better!" Mike beeped happily. His horn sounded cheerful and loud.

The warm, soapy water felt wonderful on his wheels. Sarah scrubbed each tire carefully. She hummed a happy song while she worked. Mike began to hum along with her.

Bubbles floated all around them. Some bubbles were big and round. Some were tiny and quick. Mike tried to catch them with his front bumper. Pop! Pop! Pop! They made him giggle.

Sarah rinsed him with clean, warm water. It flowed down his sides like a gentle waterfall. All the dirt and mud swirled away. Mike felt light and fresh and new.

"There!" Sarah said, stepping back to look. "You're the most beautiful truck in the whole world!"

Mike's red paint gleamed in the sunlight. His chrome bumper sparkled like silver. His windows were crystal clear. He felt amazing!

"Thank you, Sarah," Mike said. His voice was happy and grateful. "I feel wonderful!"

Sarah dried him with a soft, fluffy towel. It felt like clouds against his paint. She polished his windows until they shined perfectly.

Mike looked at himself in the garage mirror. He couldn't believe how good he looked! His red paint was brilliant and bright. Every part of him was clean and shiny.

"I love being clean!" Mike announced proudly. He honked his horn three times. Beep! Beep! Beep!

Sarah hugged his side gently. "I love you, clean or dirty," she said. "But doesn't this feel nice?"

Mike had to agree. Being clean felt wonderful. He could see clearly. His paint looked beautiful. The fresh, lemony smell made him happy.

That night, Mike slept peacefully in the garage. He dreamed of playing in the dirt tomorrow. But he also dreamed of bath time afterward. The warm water and bubbles and Sarah's gentle hands.

When morning came, Mike was excited. He would play hard and get dirty. Then he would have another wonderful bath. Both parts sounded like fun!

Mike had learned something important. Getting dirty was fun. Getting clean was fun too. The best days had both adventures!

The end.
