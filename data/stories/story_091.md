# Rusty Learns to Share

In a sunny garden filled with colorful flowers, there lived a bright red truck named Rusty. Every morning, Rusty would rumble through the garden paths. His big wheels rolled over soft dirt. His shiny bumper caught the warm sunlight.

Rusty loved his garden home. He had his favorite parking spot. It was under a big apple tree. The spot had the softest grass. It had the coolest shade. Rusty thought it was perfect.

One day, a little blue truck arrived. Her name was Bella. She looked tired from traveling. Her wheels were dusty. Her paint was faded from the hot sun.

"Hello," said Bella softly. "I'm looking for a place to rest. This garden looks so nice."

Rusty's engine grumbled. He didn't want to share his special spot. "This is MY garden," he said loudly. "Find somewhere else!"

Bella's headlights drooped sadly. She drove to a corner. There were no trees there. The sun was very hot. The ground was bumpy and hard.

Rusty watched Bella struggle. She tried to get comfortable. But the rocks hurt her tires. The bright sun made her very warm. She looked unhappy.

"Maybe I should help," thought Rusty. But then he shook his bumper. "No! My spot is too special."

The next morning, Rusty heard strange sounds. *Drip, drip, drip.* Rain was falling! The drops hit his roof. They rolled down his windows.

Rusty was dry under his tree. But poor Bella had no shelter. The rain soaked her seats. It made her engine sputter. She shivered in the cold water.

"Achoo!" sneezed Bella. "This rain is so cold!"

Rusty felt something funny in his gas tank. It was a warm, tingly feeling. He wanted to help Bella. But he still didn't want to share.

"I could move over just a little," he thought. "But what if there's not enough room?"

Rusty tried backing up closer to the tree. Now there was a tiny space. But it was very small. Too small for Bella.

"That won't work," sighed Rusty.

Then Rusty had another idea. "Maybe we could take turns!" he called to Bella. "You can have the spot in the morning. I'll take it in the afternoon."

Bella drove over hopefully. But when morning came, Rusty didn't want to leave. When afternoon came, he didn't want to share either.

"This isn't working," said Bella sadly.

Rusty felt terrible. His engine made sad puttering sounds. Sharing was harder than he thought. But seeing Bella so unhappy made him feel worse.

That evening, Rusty had his best idea yet. "What if we share the spot together?" he called out. "Side by side!"

"But is there enough room?" asked Bella.

"Let's try!" said Rusty excitedly.

Rusty moved to one side of the tree. Bella drove up beside him. They fit perfectly! Both trucks had shade. Both trucks had soft grass. Both trucks had a wonderful view.

"This is even better!" laughed Bella. Her headlights sparkled with joy.

"You're right!" agreed Rusty. "It's more fun together!"

The two trucks discovered many wonderful things. They could talk about the pretty flowers. They could watch butterflies dance. They could listen to birds singing together.

When it rained, they both stayed dry. When the sun was hot, they both had shade. When the wind blew, they kept each other company.

"Thank you for sharing," said Bella one evening. "You're such a good friend."

Rusty's chrome bumper gleamed with pride. "Thank you for teaching me how," he said. "Sharing makes everything better!"

From that day on, Rusty and Bella shared everything. They shared the best parking spot. They shared stories and songs. They shared the beautiful garden.

Rusty learned that sharing didn't mean having less. It meant having more! More friendship, more fun, more happiness.

Every morning, the two trucks would wake up together. They would watch the sunrise paint the sky. They would smell the sweet flowers. And they would feel grateful for their friendship.

Rusty had found something better than the perfect spot. He had found the perfect friend.

And they lived happily ever after in their shared garden home.

The end.
