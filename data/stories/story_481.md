# Benny's Garden Surprise

Benny was a fluffy golden dog. He loved playing in his family's garden. The garden had bright red flowers. It had tall green trees. It smelled like sunshine and fresh dirt.

Every morning, Benny ran outside. He wagged his tail happily. "Woof! Time to play!" he barked.

The garden was Benny's special place. He had his favorite sunny spot. He had his red rubber ball. He had his squeaky bone toy. Everything in the garden was his.

One sunny day, something changed. A little gray kitten appeared. The kitten was sitting in Benny's sunny spot. She was batting at Benny's ball.

"Hey!" Benny barked loudly. "That's MY spot! That's MY ball!"

The kitten looked up. Her green eyes were big. "Meow," she said softly. "I'm Luna. Can I play?"

Benny's tail stopped wagging. He didn't want to share. The garden was his place. His toys were his toys.

"No!" Benny said firmly. "Go find your own garden!"

Luna's ears drooped down. She looked very sad. But she didn't leave.

Benny tried to make Luna go away. First, he picked up his ball. He carried it to the other side. "Now you can't play with it," he thought.

But Luna followed him. She sat down nearby. She watched him play alone.

Next, Benny lay down on his sunny spot. He spread out very wide. "No room for you," he huffed.

But the sun was big enough. Luna found another warm patch. She curled up quietly.

Then Benny buried his bone toy. He dug a deep hole. He covered it with dirt. "She'll never find it now," he thought.

But Luna didn't try to find it. She just sat there. She looked lonely.

Benny played by himself all morning. He threw his ball up high. He caught it in his mouth. But something felt different. Playing alone wasn't as fun.

He looked at Luna. She was watching butterflies. Her eyes followed them up and down. She looked like she wanted to chase them. But she stayed in her spot.

Benny's heart felt funny. Luna seemed nice. She wasn't being mean. She just wanted to play too.

"Maybe," Benny thought slowly, "sharing could be okay."

He picked up his red ball. He walked over to Luna. His paws felt heavy. This was hard to do.

"Luna," he said quietly. "Want to play catch?"

Luna's eyes got bright. "Really?" she meowed. "You'll share with me?"

Benny nodded. He dropped the ball. Luna batted it with her paw. It rolled toward Benny. He pushed it back with his nose.

"This is fun!" Luna purred.

They played catch together. The ball rolled back and forth. Back and forth. They both laughed and played.

Then Benny had an idea. "Want to see my bone?" he asked.

He dug up his squeaky toy. Luna sniffed it carefully. "It smells like you," she said. "It's special."

"You can squeak it once," Benny said. He was learning to share.

Luna pressed the bone gently. SQUEAK! She giggled. "Thank you, Benny!"

As the sun got warmer, they shared the sunny spot. Benny lay on one side. Luna curled up on the other. There was plenty of room.

"Sharing is nice," Benny realized. "I have a friend now."

Luna purred softly. "You're the best dog ever, Benny."

From that day on, they played together. They chased butterflies. They rolled the ball. They napped in the sunshine.

Benny learned something important. Sharing didn't mean losing his things. It meant having more fun. It meant having a friend.

The garden was still special. But now it was even better. It was a place where friends played together.

Every morning, Benny and Luna ran outside. "Woof! Meow!" they called. "Time to play together!"

And they did.

**The End**

*Sometimes the best things happen when we learn to share.*
