# Story story_628

**Rusty the Little Red Bike**

Rusty was a little red bike who lived on Sunshine Hill. He loved rolling down the bumpy dirt paths. He loved splashing through muddy puddles. Most of all, he loved getting wonderfully, perfectly dirty.

Every day, Rusty would zoom around the hill. His red paint got covered in brown mud. His silver spokes collected leaves and twigs. His handlebars grew thick with dust. Rusty thought he looked magnificent!

But one sunny morning, Rusty's owner Emma came outside. She looked at her bike and gasped.

"Oh my! Rusty, you need a bath right away!"

Rusty's wheels started shaking. A bath? He did NOT want a bath! Baths meant soap and water. Baths meant scrubbing away all his beautiful dirt. Baths meant looking boring and shiny again.

"No, no, no!" Rusty whispered to himself. "I must hide!"

Rusty quickly rolled behind the big oak tree. He tucked himself between the thick roots. Emma walked around the yard, calling his name.

"Rusty? Where are you, little bike?"

Rusty held very still. A squirrel ran across his seat. A butterfly landed on his bell. He tried not to jingle.

But soon, Emma found him. "There you are, silly bike!"

She wheeled Rusty toward the garden hose. The water sparkled in the sunshine. Rusty felt very worried.

"I have an idea," Emma said gently. "What if we make bath time fun?"

Emma filled a bucket with warm, soapy water. The bubbles smelled like sweet lavender. She added rubber ducks that squeaked. She brought out soft, colorful sponges.

"Look, Rusty! The yellow sponge is like sunshine. The blue one feels like clouds."

Rusty watched the bubbles float and pop. They made tiny rainbow colors. The rubber ducks bobbed up and down. One duck had a sailor hat. Another wore tiny sunglasses.

Emma started washing Rusty very gently. The warm water felt nice on his frame. The soft sponge tickled his spokes. The soap smelled like a flower garden.

"See? This isn't scary at all," Emma said sweetly.

As Emma scrubbed, something amazing happened. Rusty's beautiful red paint began to shine. His silver parts sparkled like stars. His bell gleamed bright and golden.

"Wow!" said Emma. "Look how handsome you are!"

Rusty looked down at himself. His reflection showed in a nearby puddle. He looked strong and proud. His red paint was brilliant as a fire truck. His chrome parts twinkled in the sunlight.

"I do look rather wonderful," Rusty thought.

Emma dried him with a fluffy towel. She polished his bell until it sang beautifully. She oiled his chain so it moved smoothly.

"Now you're ready for our bike ride!" Emma said happily.

Rusty felt light and fast. His clean parts moved perfectly. When Emma rang his bell, it made the clearest, prettiest sound ever.

They rode down Sunshine Hill together. Rusty rolled faster than before. The wind felt fresh against his shiny frame. Other bikes stopped to admire him.

"You look fantastic!" called Bella the blue bike.

"So bright and cheerful!" added Max the mountain bike.

Rusty felt proud and happy. He realized something important. Being clean felt just as good as being dirty. Both were wonderful in different ways.

When they got home, Rusty thought about his day. He had been scared of trying something new. But Emma was patient and kind. She made the scary thing fun and gentle.

"Thank you for the nice bath," Rusty whispered to Emma.

Emma patted his seat lovingly. "You're welcome, brave little bike."

From that day on, Rusty still loved getting dirty on adventures. But he also looked forward to bath time. He knew that being clean helped him roll better. It made his colors bright and beautiful.

Most importantly, Rusty learned that trying new things wasn't so scary. Especially with someone kind to help you.

And whenever other bikes felt worried about baths, Rusty would tell them his story. "Don't worry," he would say. "Sometimes the things that seem scary turn out to be wonderful!"

The end.
