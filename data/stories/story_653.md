# Story story_653

**Teddy's Musical Day**

The big clock on the wall said three o'clock. Miss Anna clapped her hands together. "Time for music!" she called out cheerfully.

All the children jumped up from their play mats. They ran to the big colorful rug. Emma grabbed her favorite spot by the window. Sam plopped down next to the toy box. But Teddy stayed right where he was.

Teddy loved his quiet corner. He had his soft blocks there. He had his picture books too. Music time meant singing loud songs. It meant clapping and dancing around. Teddy's tummy felt funny just thinking about it.

"Come on, Teddy!" called Miss Anna. She held out her hand with a warm smile. "We're going to make beautiful music today."

Teddy shook his head. His cheeks turned pink. "Too loud," he whispered. "Too much moving around."

Miss Anna sat down beside him. "What if we start very quiet?" she asked gently. "Like a whisper?"

Teddy looked at his blocks. Then he looked at Miss Anna's kind eyes. He took a tiny step toward the music rug.

Miss Anna picked up her guitar. The other children sat in a circle. Teddy sat at the very edge. He was ready to run back to his corner.

"Let's begin with the softest sound," Miss Anna said. She touched one guitar string very lightly. It made a gentle hum. Like a bee flying far away.

Teddy's ears perked up. That wasn't scary at all.

"Now let's hum like the guitar," Miss Anna whispered. The children hummed so quietly. It sounded like a gentle breeze through trees.

Teddy found himself humming too. The sound tickled his throat nicely.

Miss Anna played another soft note. "This sounds like raindrops," she said. "Pitter patter, pitter patter."

Emma tapped her fingers on her knees. Tap tap tap. Like tiny raindrops on a roof. Sam joined in. Tap tap tap.

Teddy watched their fingers dance. He tried tapping too. His fingers made the softest little sounds.

"Listen," said Miss Anna. "Do you hear that?" She cupped her ear with her hand.

Everyone got very still. They heard birds singing outside the window. Tweet tweet tweet.

"The birds want to join our music!" laughed Miss Anna. She played notes that sounded just like the birds.

Teddy giggled. The guitar really did sound like singing birds!

"What other sounds can we make?" asked Miss Anna. She showed them how to rub their hands together. It sounded like wind in the leaves. Whoosh, whoosh.

Teddy rubbed his hands together. The sound made him think of cozy blankets.

Next, Miss Anna showed them foot tapping. But not loud stomping. Just gentle taps like a horse walking. Clip clop, clip clop.

Teddy tapped his feet. He liked how it felt. Like a happy dance while sitting down.

"Now let's put it all together," said Miss Anna. "Humming, finger tapping, hand rubbing, and foot tapping."

All the sounds mixed together. It made the most beautiful gentle music. Like a forest full of friendly sounds.

Teddy found himself moving closer to the center. The music felt warm and safe. Like a musical hug.

"You're making magic, everyone!" said Miss Anna. "Teddy, you helped make this beautiful song."

Teddy's face lit up with a big smile. He had made music! Real music with his friends!

They played their gentle forest song three more times. Each time felt more magical. Teddy's heart felt light and happy.

When music time ended, Teddy didn't want to stop. "Can we do the quiet music again tomorrow?" he asked Miss Anna.

"Of course!" she said. "And maybe we can learn about ocean sounds too."

Teddy's eyes sparkled. Ocean sounds! That sounded wonderful.

As the children cleaned up, Teddy hummed their forest song. His fingers tapped on his leg. Music wasn't scary at all. It was like having a gentle adventure.

That night, Teddy told his mom all about music time. "Tomorrow we might make ocean sounds!" he said excitedly.

Teddy couldn't wait for three o'clock to come again.

The end.
