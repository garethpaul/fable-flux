# Ducky's Big Game

Little Ducky lived by a sparkling blue pond. The water felt cool on her tiny orange feet. Every morning, she waddled to the water's edge. The sun made the pond shimmer like diamonds.

Ducky loved watching the big ducks play. They splashed and dove deep underwater. They played tag in the tall green reeds. Their loud quacks echoed across the pond.

"Can I play too?" Ducky asked one sunny day.

The big ducks looked down at her. "You're too little," said Drake, the biggest duck. "This game is for grown-ups only."

Ducky's heart felt heavy and sad. Her small wings drooped by her sides. She watched the ducks swim in circles. They dove down and popped back up. It looked like so much fun.

"I want to play," Ducky whispered softly.

She tried to follow the big ducks. Her little legs paddled as fast as possible. But the big ducks swam too quickly. Ducky got left far behind.

She tried to dive underwater too. Down, down she went with a tiny splash. But she could only stay under for three seconds. The big ducks stayed under much longer.

Ducky felt tears in her bright black eyes. She swam to a quiet corner alone. The water lapped gently against her fluffy feathers.

"Maybe I'll never be big enough," she sighed.

Just then, Mama Duck swam over gracefully. Her warm wing wrapped around little Ducky. "What's wrong, my sweet baby?" she asked.

"The big ducks won't let me play. I'm too small for their games." Ducky's voice was very quiet and sad.

Mama Duck smiled with her bright orange beak. "Being small doesn't mean you can't play. You just need to find your own special way."

"But how?" Ducky asked, looking up hopefully.

"Watch and learn, little one," Mama said gently.

Ducky thought hard about the diving game. The big ducks disappeared underwater completely. But maybe she could play differently.

She had a wonderful new idea!

Ducky swam back to the playing area. The big ducks were still diving deep. She took a big breath of fresh air.

Instead of diving straight down like them, Ducky did something special. She put just her head underwater. Her fluffy bottom stuck up high. Her orange feet wiggled in the air.

"Look at me!" she called out cheerfully. "I'm playing the upside-down diving game!"

The big ducks stopped swimming around. They stared at Ducky's silly position. Her tail feathers wiggled like a flag.

Drake started to giggle at the sight. "That looks really fun!" he quacked loudly.

Soon all the ducks were laughing. They tried Ducky's upside-down diving game too. Their big bottoms stuck up everywhere. The pond looked like a forest of duck tails.

"This is the best game ever!" laughed Drake. "You're so clever, little Ducky!"

All the ducks splashed and played together. They took turns doing upside-down dives. Ducky was the very best at it. Her small size made it easy.

"Will you teach us more games?" asked another big duck named Splash.

Ducky's chest puffed out with pride and joy. "Of course! I know lots of fun games!"

They played until the sun turned orange. The water reflected the beautiful sunset colors. Everyone quacked with happiness and laughter.

When it was time to go home, Drake swam over to Ducky. "I'm sorry I said you were too little. You're just the right size for amazing games."

Ducky smiled her biggest, brightest smile ever. Her heart felt warm and full. "Thank you for playing with me today."

That night, Ducky fell asleep peacefully. She dreamed of all the fun games they would play tomorrow. Being small wasn't a problem at all. It just meant she could think of creative new ways to have fun.

From that day on, all the ducks played together happily. Ducky learned that being different made her special. Sometimes the best solutions come from trying something completely new.

The pond was filled with laughter every single day.

The end.
