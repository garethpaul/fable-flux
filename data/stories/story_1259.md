# Story story_1259

Ben sat on his bedroom floor. His toy cars were lined up perfectly. Red car, blue car, green car, yellow car.

"Time for the park!" called Mom from downstairs.

Ben's shoulders drooped. He loved his cars. The park meant leaving them behind. What if someone moved them? What if he forgot how they were arranged?

"Ben, let's go!" Mom called again.

Ben slowly walked downstairs. His feet felt heavy like rocks. Mom was waiting by the door with a big smile.

"I don't want to go," Ben said quietly.

Mom knelt down beside him. "Why not, sweetie?"

"I want to stay with my cars. The park is too loud. Too many kids."

Mom nodded. "I understand. But fresh air feels good. And I packed your favorite crackers."

Ben thought about the crackers. They were shaped like fish and tasted salty. Maybe the park wouldn't be so bad.

They walked to the park together. Ben held Mom's warm hand. The sun felt nice on his face. Birds chirped in the tall trees. The grass smelled fresh and green.

At the park, Ben saw the big slide. Kids were laughing and running everywhere. It was very loud. Ben stayed close to Mom.

"Let's sit on this bench," Mom said. "We can watch the other children play."

Ben sat down. He watched a girl with pigtails swing very high. A boy with a red shirt climbed the monkey bars. They looked happy, but Ben felt nervous.

Then something caught his eye. Near the sandbox, something sparkled in the dirt. Ben walked closer to look.

It was a small, shiny rock. But not just any rock. This one had tiny crystals that caught the sunlight. It looked like it held tiny rainbows inside.

"Wow," Ben whispered. He picked up the rock carefully.

A little girl with curly hair came over. "That's pretty! I'm Sarah. What's your name?"

"I'm Ben," he said, still looking at the rock.

"I love rocks too," Sarah said. "Want to find more treasure?"

Ben looked at Mom. She smiled and nodded. "Okay," Ben said.

Sarah showed Ben the best places to dig. Under the big oak tree. Near the flower bed. By the water fountain.

They found smooth white stones. Rough gray pebbles. A piece of pink quartz. Each rock was special in its own way.

"Look at this one!" Sarah held up a black rock with white stripes.

Ben's eyes grew wide. "It looks like a zebra!"

They both giggled. Ben forgot about being nervous. He forgot about the noise. Finding rocks was like a treasure hunt.

More children came to see their collection. A boy named Jake found a rock shaped like a heart. A girl named Emma discovered one that looked like a tiny egg.

Soon they had a whole pile of beautiful rocks. Ben felt proud and happy. His pockets were full of treasures.

"Time to go home," Mom said gently.

Ben looked at his new friends. "Can I take some rocks home?"

"Of course," Mom said. "But maybe leave a few for other children to find."

Ben carefully chose his three favorite rocks. The sparkly one he found first. The zebra-striped one. And a smooth blue one that felt good in his hand.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" asked Sarah.

Ben looked around the park. The swings looked fun now. The slide didn't seem so scary. The sandbox was full of possibilities.

"Yes," Ben said with a big smile. "I want to find more treasure."

On the walk home, Ben showed Mom each rock. He told her about Sarah and Jake and Emma. His voice was excited and happy.

"I'm glad you had fun," Mom said.

"Me too," Ben said. "Can we come back after lunch tomorrow?"

Mom laughed. "We'll see."

That night, Ben put his special rocks on his dresser. Right next to his toy cars. Both made him happy in different ways.

He couldn't wait for tomorrow's adventure at the park.

The end.
