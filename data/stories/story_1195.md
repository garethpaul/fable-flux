# Story story_1195

Emma lived in a cozy house on Sunshine Hill. She had curly brown hair and bright green eyes. Emma loved playing with her toys during the day. But when the sun went down, Emma felt scared.

Every night, Mama said, "Time for bed, Emma!" Emma's tummy would feel funny. Her hands would shake a little. The dark hallway looked scary. Her bedroom seemed too quiet and strange.

"I don't want to go to bed," Emma whispered. She hid behind the big blue couch. The soft cushions felt safe and warm.

Papa sat down next to her. "What's wrong, little one?" His voice was gentle and kind.

"Bedtime is scary," Emma said. "My room feels too dark. I hear strange noises. What if monsters come?"

Papa gave her a warm hug. "Let's try something special tonight," he said. "We'll make bedtime fun instead of scary."

First, Papa brought Emma's favorite stuffed elephant, Peanut. Peanut was soft and gray with floppy ears. "Peanut will keep you safe," Papa said. He tucked Peanut under Emma's arm.

They walked to Emma's room together. Papa turned on the small pink nightlight. It made pretty star shapes on the wall. The room looked a little better.

"See? Not so scary," Papa said. He kissed Emma's forehead. "Sweet dreams, my brave girl."

But when Papa left, Emma still felt afraid. The house made creaky sounds. The wind whooshed outside her window. Even with Peanut and the nightlight, her heart beat fast.

Emma couldn't sleep. She tossed and turned in her soft bed. The pillow felt lumpy. The blanket felt too hot, then too cold.

She got up and ran to Mama and Papa's room. "I'm still scared," she said with tears in her eyes.

Mama sat up and hugged her tight. "Oh, sweetie. Let's try something else."

The next night, Mama had a new idea. "We'll make a special bedtime routine," she said. "Something just for you."

First, they took a warm bubble bath. The bubbles smelled like strawberries. Emma giggled as she played with her rubber duck. The warm water made her feel sleepy and calm.

Then they brushed Emma's teeth together. They made funny faces in the bathroom mirror. Emma laughed at their silly expressions.

Next, they read three stories in Emma's bed. Mama's voice was soft and soothing. The stories were about brave little animals who weren't afraid of anything.

"Now we'll sing your special song," Mama said. She sang a gentle lullaby about stars and moonbeams. Emma felt her eyes getting heavy.

"Can you sing it with me?" Mama asked. Emma's small voice joined in. The song made her feel peaceful and loved.

"Last step," Mama whispered. "We'll say goodnight to everything in your room." They said goodnight to the teddy bears on the shelf. They said goodnight to the books and the rocking chair. They even said goodnight to the friendly moon outside.

Emma felt much better. Her room didn't seem scary anymore. It felt like home.

Mama tucked her in with the soft purple blanket. "You were so brave to try again," she said. "Sometimes the best solutions take time to find."

Emma smiled sleepily. "I like our bedtime routine," she said. "It makes me feel safe and happy."

From that night on, Emma looked forward to bedtime. She knew exactly what would happen. The routine made her feel secure and loved.

Soon, Emma was helping other kids on Sunshine Hill who were afraid of bedtime too. She shared her special routine with them. "Don't give up," she told them. "If one thing doesn't work, try something else."

Emma learned that being afraid was okay. The important thing was to keep trying new solutions. With love, patience, and creativity, even scary things could become wonderful.

Now Emma sleeps peacefully every night on Sunshine Hill. Her dreams are full of adventures and joy. And she's never afraid of bedtime anymore.

The end.
