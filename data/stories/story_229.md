# Teddy's Big Discovery

In the quiet attic above Emma's bedroom lived a soft, brown teddy bear named Teddy. Dust sparkles danced in the sunbeams that peeked through the small window. Old boxes sat stacked like towers around him. Teddy loved his cozy attic home.

But Teddy had a big problem. He was stuck.

Yesterday, while exploring behind a tall box, Teddy had wiggled into a tight space. Now his fuzzy arm was caught under a heavy trunk. He pulled and tugged, but he couldn't get free.

"Oh no," whispered Teddy softly. His button eyes looked worried.

Teddy could hear Emma playing downstairs. Her happy voice floated up through the floorboards. "Come help me, Mommy!" Emma called out cheerfully.

Teddy's heart felt warm hearing that. Emma always asked for help when she needed it. But Teddy felt too shy to call out. What if Emma was too busy? What if he bothered her?

So Teddy tried to solve his problem alone.

First, he pushed with his free paw. "Ungh!" he grunted quietly. The trunk didn't budge. It was much too heavy.

Next, Teddy tried twisting his stuck arm. He turned left, then right. "Ow, ow," he whispered. That made his arm hurt even more.

Then Teddy tried rocking back and forth. Maybe he could wiggle loose! Rock, rock, rock. But his arm stayed trapped tight.

Hours passed slowly. The sunbeam moved across the dusty floor. Teddy felt tired and sad. His tummy rumbled with hunger.

"I wish I could ask for help," Teddy sighed. "But I'm too scared."

Just then, he heard Emma's footsteps on the attic stairs. Creak, creak, creak. She was coming up to get her winter clothes!

Teddy's heart beat fast like a little drum. This was his chance! But the shy feelings bubbled up inside him.

Emma's head popped through the attic door. Her curly hair bounced as she looked around.

Teddy opened his mouth, then closed it. The words felt stuck, just like his arm.

Emma walked closer, humming a happy tune. She was looking right at the box near Teddy!

"Help," Teddy whispered so quietly, even he could barely hear it.

Emma kept humming. She didn't hear him.

Teddy took a deep breath. He remembered how brave Emma was when she asked for help. Maybe he could be brave too.

"Help me, please!" Teddy called out, louder this time.

Emma stopped humming. She looked around with surprise.

"Teddy?" Emma said, her eyes growing wide. "Where are you?"

"I'm stuck behind this box!" Teddy called back. His voice felt stronger now.

Emma hurried over and gasped. "Oh my! Your poor arm is trapped!"

Gently, Emma lifted the heavy trunk. It was easy for her big, strong hands. Teddy pulled his arm free with a happy "Pop!"

"Thank you, Emma!" Teddy said, hugging his rescued arm. "I was scared to ask for help."

Emma scooped Teddy up in a warm hug. "Asking for help is always okay, Teddy. That's what friends do!"

She carried him downstairs to her sunny bedroom. Emma set Teddy on her soft pillow and brought him a tiny cup of pretend tea.

"You were very brave to call out," Emma said, patting his fuzzy head. "Sometimes asking for help is the bravest thing we can do."

Teddy's button eyes sparkled with happiness. His arm felt much better, and his heart felt warm and full.

From that day on, whenever Teddy had a problem he couldn't solve alone, he remembered Emma's kind words. Asking for help wasn't something to be shy about. It was something friends did for each other.

And Emma always, always helped her dear friend Teddy, just like he knew she would.

That night, as moonbeams danced through Emma's window, Teddy snuggled close and smiled. He had learned that being brave sometimes meant asking for help. And that made him feel like the luckiest teddy bear in the whole wide world.

The end.
