# Story story_1330

**Lily's Missing Blocks**

Lily loved the library. She went there every Tuesday with Mama. The children's corner had soft blue carpets. It smelled like vanilla cookies and old books.

Today, Lily wanted to build a castle. She looked at the toy shelf. Her favorite red blocks were gone!

"Where are my blocks?" Lily asked out loud. Her bottom lip started to shake. The blocks were always on the bottom shelf. Now there was just an empty space.

Lily looked under the reading table. She saw dust bunnies and a lost crayon. No blocks. She checked behind the puppet theater. Just spider webs and a forgotten sock.

"Mrs. Chen!" Lily called to the librarian. "My blocks are missing!"

Mrs. Chen had kind eyes behind purple glasses. "Oh dear," she said gently. "When did you see them last?"

"Last Tuesday," Lily said. "They were right here." She pointed to the empty shelf with her small finger.

Mrs. Chen nodded. "Let's think like detectives. Where else might blocks live?"

Lily's first idea was to check the lost and found box. She and Mrs. Chen walked to the front desk. The box was big and brown. It smelled musty inside.

Lily dug through winter mittens. She found three library books and someone's lunch box. She pulled out a stuffed elephant and two hair clips. But no red blocks anywhere.

"Not here," Lily sighed. Her shoulders drooped down. Building a castle seemed impossible now.

"Don't give up yet," Mrs. Chen said softly. "Good detectives keep looking."

Lily wiped her nose with her sleeve. Maybe the blocks were in the craft room. She and Mrs. Chen walked down the quiet hallway. Their footsteps echoed on the shiny floor.

The craft room door was closed. Mrs. Chen turned the silver handle. Inside, Lily heard familiar sounds. Click, click, stack!

"My blocks!" Lily shouted with joy.

Three children sat around a small table. Tommy, Sarah, and Ben were building with Lily's red blocks. They had made a tall tower and a bridge.

Lily's happy feeling turned sad again. "Those are mine," she whispered.

Mrs. Chen knelt down beside Lily. "The blocks belong to everyone at the library," she explained. "But I have an idea."

Mrs. Chen walked to Tommy's group. "Lily would like to build too. Could she join your game?"

Tommy looked up. His face was friendly. "We're making a whole city! Want to help?"

Sarah smiled big. "You can build the fire station!"

Ben nodded. "We need more builders!"

Lily felt butterflies in her tummy. Playing alone was easier. But the city looked amazing. She took a tiny step forward.

"I like fire stations," Lily said quietly.

Soon, Lily was sitting with the group. The carpet felt soft under her knees. The blocks felt smooth and warm in her hands. She stacked them carefully, one by one.

"Your fire station is perfect!" Sarah said. "It matches our hospital!"

Tommy added a red block to Lily's building. "Now the fire truck can fit inside!"

Lily giggled. Building together was more fun than building alone. The city grew bigger and bigger. They made houses and shops and even a park.

When Mama came to pick up Lily, she found four children laughing together. Their block city covered the whole table.

"Look what we made!" Lily said proudly. "I found my blocks, but I found new friends too!"

Mrs. Chen winked at Mama. "Sometimes the best treasures are the ones we share."

Lily hugged Tommy, Sarah, and Ben goodbye. "Can we build again next Tuesday?" she asked hopefully.

"Yes!" they all said together.

Walking to the car, Lily held Mama's hand tight. "I thought my blocks were lost forever," she said. "But they were just waiting for friends."

Mama squeezed Lily's hand. "Sometimes when we lose something, we find something even better."

Lily smiled up at the sunny sky. Next Tuesday couldn't come fast enough. She had a city to finish and friends to see.

The library doors closed softly behind them with a gentle whoosh.

The end.
