# Story story_1161

**The Friend in the Attic**

Maya climbed the creaky wooden stairs. Step by step, she went up to the attic. The old house was very quiet today.

Her mom was busy downstairs. Her dad was at work. Maya felt lonely and sad.

The attic smelled like old books and dust. Sunlight came through a small round window. It made golden spots on the floor.

Maya sat on a soft, old blanket. She had her favorite doll with her. But playing alone wasn't much fun.

"I wish I had a friend," Maya said quietly.

Suddenly, she heard a small sound. Scratch, scratch, scratch.

Maya looked around the dusty attic. Where was that noise coming from?

Behind a big brown box, two bright eyes appeared. They were golden like honey.

A small red fox stepped out slowly. His fur was soft and fluffy. His tail was big and bushy.

"Oh!" Maya said. "A fox!"

The fox tilted his head. He looked at Maya with curious eyes. His ears twitched back and forth.

"Are you lonely too?" Maya asked softly.

The fox took a tiny step closer. Then another step. And another.

Maya sat very still. She didn't want to scare him away.

The fox sniffed the air. He could smell Maya's apple juice. He could hear her gentle voice.

"Would you like to play with me?" Maya whispered.

The fox's tail wagged just a little bit. He came closer to the blanket.

Maya smiled. "I'm Maya. What's your name?"

The fox couldn't talk. But he made a soft, happy sound. It sounded like "Rusty."

"Rusty!" Maya said. "I like that name."

Rusty sat on the blanket next to Maya. His fur felt warm and soft.

Maya showed Rusty her doll. "This is Emma. She likes to have tea parties."

Rusty sniffed the doll gently. He was very careful with his nose.

Maya had an idea. "Let's have a tea party together!"

She pretended to pour tea from a tiny cup. She offered some to Rusty.

Rusty pretended to sip the tea. He licked his lips and wagged his tail.

Maya giggled. "You're funny, Rusty!"

They played house together. Maya was the mom. Rusty was the pet. Emma was the baby.

Rusty curled up in a pretend bed. Maya covered him with a small cloth.

"Good night, Rusty," she said sweetly.

Rusty closed his eyes. He pretended to sleep. Then he opened one eye and winked.

Maya laughed and laughed. Her lonely feeling went away.

They played hide and seek next. Rusty was very good at hiding. His red fur blended with the old boxes.

"Where are you, Rusty?" Maya called.

Rusty poked his nose out. Maya found him quickly.

"My turn to hide!" Maya said.

She hid behind a tall mirror. Rusty found her right away. His nose was very smart.

The sun moved across the sky. The golden light changed colors.

Maya's tummy started to rumble. "I'm getting hungry," she said.

Rusty's ears perked up. He was hungry too.

"Would you like to come downstairs?" Maya asked. "We can have lunch together."

Rusty looked worried. What if Maya's mom didn't like foxes?

Maya saw his worried face. "Don't worry. My mom loves animals. She'll love you too."

Rusty felt brave with his new friend. He nodded his fuzzy head.

They walked down the stairs together. Maya went first. Rusty followed quietly.

"Mom!" Maya called. "I made a new friend!"

Maya's mom saw the gentle fox. She smiled big. "Hello there, little one."

Rusty wagged his tail. Maya's mom was kind and nice.

They all had lunch together. Rusty ate some berries. Maya had a sandwich.

"Can Rusty stay and play?" Maya asked.

"Of course," her mom said. "Friends are always welcome here."

Maya hugged Rusty gently. "We'll never be lonely again."

Rusty purred like a happy cat. He had found his best friend.

From that day on, Maya and Rusty played together every day. The attic became their special place.

They were the very best of friends.

The end.
