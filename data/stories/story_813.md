# Story story_813

Anna sat on a soft log in the quiet woods. The trees were tall and green around her. She picked up small stones and tossed them gently. 

"I wish I had someone to play with," Anna said softly.

The birds chirped above her head. Squirrels ran up and down the tree trunks. But Anna felt lonely sitting all by herself.

She heard a rustling sound behind the big oak tree. Anna turned her head to look. A fuzzy brown bear peeked out shyly.

The bear had kind black eyes. His nose twitched as he sniffed the air. He looked at Anna with a hopeful expression.

"Hello there," Anna said in her gentlest voice.

The bear took one small step forward. Then another step. He sat down a little distance away from Anna.

Anna smiled at the friendly bear. "Do you want to play with me?"

The bear nodded his furry head up and down. His ears wiggled with excitement.

"What should we play first?" Anna asked.

The bear pointed with his paw toward a pile of colorful leaves. The leaves were red, yellow, and orange. They looked like a rainbow on the ground.

Anna jumped up from her log. "Let's play in the leaves!"

She ran to the leaf pile. The bear followed right behind her. Anna scooped up handfuls of crunchy leaves. She tossed them high in the air.

The leaves floated down like confetti. They landed on Anna's hair and shoulders. Some leaves landed on the bear's nose. He sneezed a tiny sneeze.

"Achoo!" said the bear.

Anna giggled at the silly sound. The bear started giggling too. His laugh sounded like gentle rumbling.

They took turns throwing leaves at each other. The bear was very careful. He threw the leaves softly so they wouldn't hurt Anna.

"This is so much fun!" Anna said happily.

Next, they played hide and seek. Anna counted to ten while covering her eyes. The bear hid behind different trees.

"Ready or not, here I come!" Anna called out.

She found the bear hiding behind a skinny birch tree. His big body stuck out on both sides. Anna pretended not to see him at first.

"Where could that bear be?" she wondered out loud.

The bear tried not to giggle. His shoulders shook with quiet laughter.

"Found you!" Anna said with a big smile.

Now it was the bear's turn to count. He covered his eyes with his paws. Anna found a good hiding spot behind a fallen log.

The bear looked and looked. He sniffed around with his nose. Finally, he found Anna curled up small.

"You're a good hider," the bear said in his rumbly voice.

They played tag next. The bear ran slowly so Anna could keep up. When Anna was "it," the bear let her catch him easily.

The sun began to set through the trees. Golden light filtered through the leaves. Anna and the bear sat together on the soft grass.

"I'm so happy we're friends," Anna said.

She reached over and gently patted the bear's furry arm. The bear's fur felt warm and soft.

"Me too," said the bear. "I was lonely before I met you."

"Really?" Anna asked. "I was lonely too!"

They smiled at each other. Anna picked some pretty wildflowers nearby. She made a flower crown for the bear.

The bear found smooth, round stones. He arranged them in a heart shape for Anna.

"These gifts show our friendship," Anna said.

The bear nodded happily. His flower crown sat perfectly on his head.

As the stars began to twinkle above, Anna and the bear made plans. They would meet in the woods again tomorrow. They would bring snacks to share.

"Friends make everything better," Anna said.

The bear agreed completely. He gave Anna a gentle, warm hug.

Anna walked home through the woods. She felt happy and excited. Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough.

The bear waved goodbye until Anna disappeared. He touched his flower crown gently. He had found the best friend ever.

In the woods, two lonely hearts had found each other. Now they would never feel alone again.

The end.
