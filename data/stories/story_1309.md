# Story story_1309

**Ben and the Big Hill**

Ben lived on a tall green hill. His house sat right at the very top. Every morning, Ben could see the whole world below. He loved his cozy home with Mama.

But today was different. Today was moving day.

"We have to go to our new house," Mama said gently. She packed their things in brown boxes. "It will be a wonderful adventure, Ben."

Ben's bottom lip wobbled. His eyes filled with tears. "I don't want to leave our hill, Mama. This is our special place."

The moving truck came up the bumpy road. It made loud rumbling sounds. Ben watched the big men carry away his toy box. They took his little bed too.

"Can we stay just one more day?" Ben asked. He hugged Mama's leg tight.

"I know it's hard, sweetheart," Mama said. She bent down and kissed his forehead. "But we'll make new memories in our new home."

Ben had an idea. Maybe if he hid, they couldn't leave! He ran to his favorite hiding spot behind the old oak tree. The tree had rough bark that felt bumpy under his hands. Ben squeezed his eyes shut.

"Ben? Where are you?" Mama called. Her voice sounded worried.

Ben stayed very still. A squirrel chattered above him. The wind rustled the green leaves. But soon Ben felt lonely behind the tree. Hiding didn't feel good at all.

"I'm here, Mama," Ben said sadly. He came out from behind the oak tree.

Mama smiled and took his hand. "I have something special to show you," she said.

Ben had another idea. Maybe he could take the whole hill with them! He found his little red shovel in the garage. Ben dug and dug in the soft dirt. He filled his bucket with hill soil.

"This dirt can come with us," Ben said proudly. Sweat dripped down his face. His hands were muddy and brown.

But the bucket was so heavy! Ben could barely lift it. The dirt spilled everywhere. Some got in his shoes. It felt gritty between his toes.

"Oh my," Mama laughed. "That's a lot of dirt, Ben."

Ben's shoulders drooped. His second plan didn't work either. A tear rolled down his cheek.

"Come here, my brave boy," Mama said softly. She wiped the dirt from his hands with a warm cloth. "I want to show you something very special."

Mama led Ben to their car. In the back seat was a small potted plant. It had tiny green leaves and smelled like fresh earth.

"This is a piece of our hill," Mama explained. "I dug up this little tree yesterday. It can grow in our new yard."

Ben's eyes grew wide. "Really? Our hill can come with us?"

"The best parts always do," Mama said. She pointed to Ben's heart. "Our memories live right here. And this little tree will help us remember."

Ben gently touched the soft leaves. They felt smooth and cool. The plant smelled like home. Like morning walks and afternoon picnics.

"Can I help it grow?" Ben asked.

"Of course! We'll plant it together," Mama promised.

Ben carefully held the pot on his lap. The car started with a gentle purr. As they drove down the hill, Ben waved goodbye to the oak tree.

"Goodbye, hill," Ben called out the window. "Thank you for being my home."

The little tree bounced gently on Ben's lap. Soon they arrived at their new house. It had a big yard and a friendly porch.

Ben and Mama planted the tree together. They dug a perfect hole in the soft new soil. Ben patted the dirt around the roots.

"Now our hill has a new home too," Ben said happily.

That night, Ben looked out his new bedroom window. He could see the little tree in the moonlight. It looked happy and strong.

"I love you, Mama," Ben whispered.

"I love you too, my sweet boy," Mama said. "Home isn't just a place. Home is wherever we're together."

Ben smiled and closed his eyes. Tomorrow would bring new adventures.

The end.
