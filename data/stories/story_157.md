# Ben and the Pond Game

Ben sat by the quiet pond. The water sparkled in the sunshine. Green lily pads floated like tiny boats. Ben watched the other children play.

"Splash! Splash!" went the water. The children were playing a jumping game. They hopped from rock to rock. Each rock was a different color. Red, blue, yellow, green.

"Jump to the red rock!" called Maya.

"Now the blue one!" shouted Sam.

Ben wanted to play too. He walked closer to the pond. The children kept jumping and laughing.

"Can I play?" Ben asked quietly.

The children didn't hear him. They were too busy jumping. Ben felt his tummy get tight. His shoulders drooped down low.

"Maybe they don't want me," Ben thought. He sat on a smooth gray stone. He picked up a small pebble. Plop! It went into the water.

Ben watched the ripples spread out. Round and round they went. Like magic circles in the pond.

"I could try asking again," Ben said. He took a deep breath. The air smelled like flowers and water.

Ben stood up tall. He walked to the edge. "Hi everyone! Can I join your game?"

This time Maya heard him. "Oh! Hi Ben! We're playing Rock Hopper!"

"How do you play?" Ben asked. His voice was stronger now.

"You jump from rock to rock," Sam explained. "But you can't fall in!"

Ben looked at the rocks. They seemed far apart. "What if I can't jump that far?"

"That's okay!" Maya smiled. "We can help you!"

Ben felt better. The tight feeling left his tummy. He stepped onto the first rock. It was warm from the sun.

"Good job!" the children cheered.

Ben tried to jump to the red rock. His foot slipped! Splash! His shoe got wet.

"Oops!" Ben laughed. The water felt cool. "Can I try again?"

"Of course!" said Sam. "I fell in three times!"

Ben climbed back onto the first rock. This time he bent his knees. He swung his arms back and forth. Jump! He landed on the red rock!

"Hooray!" everyone clapped.

Ben felt proud and happy. His chest felt warm inside. He jumped to the blue rock next. Then the yellow one!

"You're getting good at this!" Maya said.

Ben grinned big. "This is fun! What's next?"

"The green rock is tricky," Sam warned. "It's wobbly."

Ben looked at the green rock. It was bigger but it moved. He watched it rock back and forth. Back and forth.

"I'll go slow," Ben decided.

He took tiny steps. The rock wiggled under his feet. Ben spread his arms wide. Like a bird flying! He stayed balanced.

"Wow!" the children cheered. "You did it!"

Ben felt so happy. His whole body felt light. Like he could float on air!

"Let's play again!" Maya said. "Ben, you pick the next path!"

Ben looked around the pond. He saw more rocks. Some were tiny. Some were big. "Let's try those purple ones!"

The children played for a long time. They jumped and laughed together. Sometimes they fell in. Sometimes they made it across. They always helped each other.

When the sun got lower, Ben sat down. His clothes were damp but he felt wonderful.

"Thanks for letting me play," Ben said.

"Thanks for asking!" Maya replied. "You made the game more fun!"

Ben smiled. "I was scared to ask at first."

"That's okay," Sam said. "Being brave is hard sometimes."

Ben nodded. "But good things happen when you try!"

The friends packed up their things. Ben waved goodbye at the pond. The water was calm now. The lily pads bobbed gently.

Walking home, Ben felt proud. He had asked to play. He had tried hard. He had made new friends.

"Tomorrow I'll come back," Ben decided. "Maybe I'll ask someone new to play!"

Ben skipped all the way home. His wet shoes made squishy sounds. But his heart felt light and happy.

Sometimes asking takes courage. But wonderful things happen when you try!

The end.
