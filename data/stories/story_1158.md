# Story story_1158

Emma sat in the dusty attic. Sunlight peeked through the small window. She felt very lonely today.

Her mom was busy downstairs. Her dad was at work. Emma had no one to play with.

She looked around the quiet attic. Old boxes sat everywhere. Cobwebs hung in the corners. Dust danced in the sunny air.

Emma sighed. "I wish I had a friend," she said softly.

Suddenly, she heard a sound. Quack! Quack!

Emma looked up. A little yellow duck sat on a wooden beam. The duck had bright orange feet. Its feathers looked soft and fluffy.

"Hello there," Emma whispered. "Are you real?"

The duck tilted its head. It looked at Emma with kind black eyes. Quack! it said again.

Emma smiled for the first time today. "Do you want to play with me?"

The duck flapped its tiny wings. It seemed to nod yes.

But how could they play together? The duck was way up high. Emma was down on the floor.

Emma thought hard. She looked around the attic. There were old pillows everywhere. She had an idea!

Emma stacked the pillows high. She climbed up carefully. Now she was closer to her new friend.

"Hi, little duck," she said gently. "My name is Emma."

The duck waddled closer on the beam. It made soft, happy sounds.

Emma reached out slowly. The duck was not scared. It let Emma pet its silky feathers.

"You're so soft!" Emma giggled. "You feel like my favorite blanket."

The duck seemed to smile. It rubbed against Emma's hand.

Emma noticed the duck looked hungry. "Are you thirsty?" she asked.

She climbed down and found an old cup. Emma filled it with water from a leaky pipe. The drops made tiny plink sounds.

The duck drank the cool water gratefully. Quack! Quack! it said. Emma knew that meant "thank you."

Now they could really play together! Emma found an old scarf in a box. She waved it gently in the air.

The duck watched the colorful scarf dance. It flapped its wings with joy. The duck tried to catch the scarf with its beak.

Emma laughed. The sound echoed in the quiet attic. She felt warm and happy inside.

They played hide and seek next. Emma hid behind big boxes. The duck would find her and quack loudly.

Then the duck would hide behind old picture frames. Emma would peek around and say, "Found you!"

The duck's feathers ruffled with excitement. Its orange feet tapped on the wooden beams.

Emma's heart felt full of joy. She was not lonely anymore. She had found a wonderful friend.

"I'm so happy you're here," Emma told the duck. "You make everything more fun."

The duck nestled close to Emma. It felt safe and loved.

As the afternoon sun grew warmer, they rested together. Emma sat on the soft pillows. The duck curled up beside her.

Emma gently stroked the duck's yellow feathers. They were warm from the sunshine. The duck made sleepy, content sounds.

"Will you be my best friend?" Emma asked quietly.

The duck looked up at her with bright, loving eyes. Quack! it said softly.

Emma knew that meant yes.

From that day on, Emma was never lonely. Every morning, she climbed to the attic. Her duck friend was always waiting.

They played games and told stories. Well, Emma told stories. The duck listened with its head tilted.

Sometimes they just sat quietly together. They watched dust sparkles dance in the sunbeams. They listened to rain drops on the roof.

Emma learned that friendship can come from anywhere. Even a dusty old attic can hold wonderful surprises.

The little duck taught Emma something special. The best friends are the ones who choose to stay. They make ordinary days feel magical.

Emma smiled every time she climbed those attic stairs. She knew her feathered friend would be there. Waiting, happy, and ready to play.

Their friendship filled the attic with laughter and love. Emma was never lonely again.

The end.
