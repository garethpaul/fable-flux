# Story story_761

**The Puzzle Who Didn't Want Lunch**

Maya loved puzzles more than anything. Every morning, she would sit on her soft blue rug. She would dump out her favorite puzzle pieces. The pieces made a gentle *clink-clink* sound on the floor.

Today, Maya was working on a big farm puzzle. She had found all the red barn pieces. She had connected the fluffy white sheep. Only the green grass pieces were left.

"Maya, time for lunch!" called Mommy from the kitchen.

Maya looked at her puzzle. It was almost finished! Just a few more pieces needed homes.

"Five more minutes, please?" Maya asked.

"Your sandwich is getting soggy," Mommy said gently. "Come eat now."

Maya's shoulders drooped. She didn't want to leave her puzzle. What if the pieces got mixed up? What if someone moved them?

She walked slowly to the kitchen. Her feet felt heavy like elephant feet. The puzzle pieces seemed to whisper, "Don't leave us!"

Maya sat at the small yellow table. Mommy had made her favorite peanut butter sandwich. There were crunchy apple slices too. And a cup of cold, white milk.

But Maya just stared at her food. She kept thinking about her puzzle. The green grass pieces were waiting for her. They needed to find their special spots.

"What's wrong, sweetie?" asked Mommy. She sat down next to Maya.

"My puzzle isn't finished," Maya said sadly. "I want to work on it."

Mommy smiled her warm smile. "I have an idea," she said. "What if we make lunch into a puzzle game?"

Maya looked up. Her eyes grew curious and bright.

Mommy pointed to the sandwich. "See how it's cut in triangles? Each triangle is like a puzzle piece!"

Maya picked up one triangle. She looked at it carefully. "It does look like a puzzle piece!" she said.

"And look at your apple slices," said Mommy. "They fit together in a circle. Just like puzzle pieces!"

Maya giggled. She started arranging her apple slices. First, she made them into a flower shape. Then she made them into a sun. The apple pieces clicked together perfectly.

"This is fun!" Maya said. She took a big bite of her sandwich triangle. It tasted sweet and creamy.

Mommy brought over some cheese squares. "These can be puzzle pieces too," she said.

Maya arranged the cheese into a yellow castle. She made towers and walls. Each piece had its perfect place.

"I'm making a cheese puzzle!" Maya laughed. Her worry about the floor puzzle started to melt away.

She ate each cheese square one by one. They tasted salty and smooth. The milk washed everything down with cool, creamy goodness.

"Look, Mommy!" Maya arranged her remaining apple slices. "I made a smiley face!"

The apple slices curved into a happy smile. Maya felt happy too. Her tummy felt full and satisfied.

"Lunch puzzles are the best," said Maya. She finished her milk with a satisfied *ahh* sound.

After lunch, Maya ran back to her floor puzzle. But something wonderful had happened. While she was eating, she had been thinking about puzzle shapes. Now the green grass pieces seemed easier to place.

"This piece goes here!" she said happily. *Click!* It fit perfectly.

"And this one goes there!" *Click!* Another perfect fit.

In just a few minutes, Maya finished her farm puzzle. The green grass covered the bottom perfectly. All the animals stood in their right places.

"I did it!" Maya clapped her hands together.

That night, Maya told Daddy about her puzzle day. "I learned that lunch can be a puzzle too," she said proudly.

"What a smart discovery," said Daddy. He gave her a warm hug.

Maya looked at her completed farm puzzle one more time. Tomorrow, she would start a new puzzle. And when lunch time came, she would remember the fun puzzle game.

Maybe she could make letter puzzles with her crackers. Or number puzzles with her grapes. The possibilities felt endless and exciting.

Maya smiled as she got ready for bed. Tomorrow would bring new puzzles and new lunch adventures. She couldn't wait to see what shapes her food would make.

The end.
