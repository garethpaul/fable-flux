# Mittens and the High-Up Cookies

On a sunny green hill lived a fluffy orange cat named Mittens. She had white paws that looked like tiny mittens. That's how she got her name! Mittens loved to explore the hill every day. She would chase butterflies and roll in the soft grass.

One morning, Mittens smelled something wonderful. *Sniff, sniff.* It was the sweet smell of cookies! She followed her pink nose up the hill. The smell grew stronger and stronger.

At the top of the hill stood a tall oak tree. High up in the branches was a picnic basket. The yummy cookie smell was coming from inside! A family had left their basket there after lunch yesterday.

Mittens sat down and looked up. The basket was so very high. Her green eyes grew wide. "Those cookies smell like vanilla and chocolate," she purred. "I really want to try one!"

She jumped as high as she could. *Boing!* But she only reached the lowest branch. The basket was still far above her head. Mittens tried again. *Boing, boing!* Still too high.

"Meow," sighed Mittens. "I can't reach those cookies. They're too far up." She sat down feeling sad. Her fluffy tail drooped to the ground.

But Mittens didn't give up. She was a smart cat! She looked around the hill for ideas. Near the tree, she spotted some smooth rocks. "Maybe I can stack them up!" she thought.

Mittens pushed the first rock with her paws. It was heavy! She pushed and pushed until it reached the tree. *Grunt, grunt.* Then she found another rock. She rolled it on top of the first one. Now she had two rocks stacked up!

She climbed onto her rock tower. It was higher than before, but still not high enough. The basket seemed to wink at her from above. "I need to think of something else," Mittens said.

She noticed long, bendy branches hanging down. "I could climb up the branches!" she decided. Mittens was good at climbing. She put her sharp claws into the bark. Up, up, up she went!

The branch started to bend under her weight. *Creak, creak.* It bounced gently up and down. Mittens held on tight with all four paws. She was getting closer to the cookies!

But the branch was too bendy. It wouldn't let her reach the basket. Every time she got close, the branch would bounce her back down. "This isn't working either," she meowed.

Mittens climbed back down to think. She sat on her rock pile and tapped her paw on her chin. "What else can I try?" she wondered.

Then she had a wonderful idea! She could use both the rocks AND the bendy branch together! Mittens pushed her rock tower right under the hanging branch. Now she could climb the rocks first, then grab the branch.

Up onto the rocks she went. Then she reached for the branch. This time, she was much closer to the basket! The branch still bounced, but not as much. Mittens stretched her paw as far as she could.

Her white paw touched the basket handle! She pulled it gently toward her. The basket tipped slightly. Out tumbled two golden cookies! They fell right onto her rock pile below.

"Hooray!" purred Mittens happily. She climbed down carefully and sniffed the cookies. They smelled even better up close! She took a tiny bite. It was sweet and crunchy and perfect.

As Mittens enjoyed her treats, she felt very proud. "I didn't give up," she said to herself. "I tried different ways until I found one that worked!"

The warm sun shone on her orange fur. A gentle breeze rustled the leaves above. Mittens saved one cookie to share with her friend Robin later. Sharing made everything taste even better.

From that day on, whenever Mittens faced a hard problem, she remembered the cookie adventure. She would try one way, then another, then another. She learned that with patience and creativity, she could solve almost anything!

And that's how Mittens became the cleverest cat on the whole green hill.
