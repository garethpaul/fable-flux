# Story story_1292

Sam sat on his bed. He looked at his shoes. Mom called from downstairs.

"Sam! Time for the park!"

Sam's shoulders dropped down. He did not want to go. The park seemed boring today. All the swings looked the same. The slides felt too hot from the sun.

"Coming, Mom," Sam said quietly.

He put on his red sneakers slowly. Each lace took forever to tie. Maybe if he went really slow, Mom would change her mind.

Mom waited by the front door. She had a big smile on her face. Her keys jingled in her hand.

"Ready for some fresh air?" Mom asked.

Sam nodded but his feet felt heavy. They walked to Sunny Park together. The grass smelled sweet and green. Birds chirped happy songs in the tall oak trees.

Other kids ran around the playground. They laughed and shouted with joy. But Sam just stood by the gate. Everything looked ordinary and plain.

"What would you like to do first?" Mom asked.

Sam shrugged his shoulders. "I don't know."

Just then, something sparkled near the big slide. A tiny rainbow danced in the air. Sam blinked his eyes twice. Was he seeing things?

He walked closer to look. Water droplets floated like magic bubbles. They came from the drinking fountain nearby. The sun hit each drop perfectly. Tiny rainbows appeared and disappeared quickly.

"Wow," Sam whispered.

He reached out his hand carefully. A water drop landed on his palm. It felt cool and refreshing. More drops created a light, misty shower.

Sam giggled as the water tickled his face. He opened his mouth to catch some drops. They tasted fresh and clean.

"Look, Mom! Rainbow water!" Sam called out.

Mom hurried over to see. Her eyes grew wide with wonder. "How beautiful!" she said.

Sam discovered something amazing then. When he stood in just the right spot, rainbows appeared everywhere. The monkey bars had tiny rainbows too. Even the sandbox sparkled with colorful light.

"It's like the whole park is magical," Sam said.

He ran to the swings next. As he pumped his legs higher, the wind felt wonderful. It whooshed through his hair softly. From up high, he could see more rainbow spots around the park.

A little girl with curly hair swung beside him. "I see rainbows too!" she said happily.

"They're everywhere!" Sam replied with excitement.

Together they counted rainbow patches. One by the seesaw. Two near the picnic tables. Three more by the walking path.

Sam tried the slide after swinging. The metal felt warm but not too hot. As he slid down fast, he saw rainbow reflections in a nearby puddle. The colors danced and swirled like paint.

"This is the best slide ever!" Sam shouted.

He climbed up and slid down again. And again. Each time felt like a new adventure.

Mom sat on a bench nearby. She watched Sam play with his new friend. Both children chased rainbow spots around the playground. Their laughter filled the air sweetly.

Time passed quickly like a fun dream. Soon Mom called out gently.

"Five more minutes, Sam!"

Sam's heart felt sad for a moment. He did not want to leave yet. The park had become a magical rainbow kingdom.

He said goodbye to his new friend. "Will you be here tomorrow?" he asked hopefully.

"Yes! Let's look for more rainbows!" she replied.

Sam waved goodbye and skipped to Mom. His sneakers felt light and bouncy now. The walk home seemed much shorter.

"Did you have fun?" Mom asked.

"The best time ever!" Sam said with a huge grin.

That night at dinner, Sam told Dad everything. He described each rainbow in detail. He drew pictures of the magical water drops.

Before bed, Sam looked out his window. Tomorrow's sunshine would make new rainbows at the park. He could hardly wait to discover them.

"Good night, rainbow park," Sam whispered softly.

He fell asleep dreaming of colorful light dancing everywhere. Tomorrow would bring another magical adventure.

The end.
