# Penny Plane's Sleepy Sky Adventure

High up on Tommy's bedroom shelf, Penny Plane stretched her silver wings. The sun was setting outside. Orange and pink colors painted the sky.

"Time for bed, everyone," called Tommy's mom.

Penny watched Tommy brush his teeth. She saw him put on pajamas. Soon the room would be dark. Soon it would be quiet.

Penny's propeller drooped down sadly. "I don't want bedtime," she whispered. "Bedtime is boring. I want to keep flying!"

During the day, Penny zoomed around. Tommy made whooshing sounds. They flew over mountains of pillows. They soared through valleys of blankets. Flying was the best!

But nighttime meant sitting still. Nighttime meant no adventures. Penny's wings felt heavy with sadness.

Tommy climbed into bed. His mom turned off the lights. "Sweet dreams," she said softly.

The room grew dark and quiet. Penny sat alone on her shelf. She stared out the window sadly.

Then something wonderful happened.

A gentle breeze floated through the window. It tickled Penny's wings. The breeze felt warm and magical.

"Come fly with us," whispered the wind.

Penny looked outside. Her propeller spun with excitement! In the starry sky, she saw other toy planes. They glowed softly in the moonlight.

"We're the Dream Flight Squadron," called a red plane. "We fly every night!"

Penny's wings tingled with joy. "Can I really fly?"

"Of course!" laughed a blue plane. "Night flying is the most magical!"

Suddenly, Penny felt herself lifting up. The magical breeze carried her gently. Out the window she floated. Into the dreamy night sky!

The air felt soft like cotton. Stars twinkled like tiny diamonds. The moon smiled down warmly.

"Follow us!" called the other planes.

Together they flew over sleeping houses. They swooped past fluffy white clouds. They glided through streams of starlight.

"This is amazing!" Penny laughed. Her propeller spun happily.

Below, children were sleeping peacefully. Some smiled in their dreams. Penny realized something special. Maybe she was helping them dream!

"We bring good dreams," explained the red plane. "Every night we fly. We sprinkle sleepy magic."

Penny felt proud and important. This was better than day flying! This was magical flying!

They flew over Tommy's house again. Penny saw him sleeping soundly. He was smiling in his dreams.

"I'm helping Tommy dream," Penny whispered happily.

The squadron flew in gentle loops. They traced patterns in the sky. They wrote messages in starlight.

"Sleep brings the best adventures," said the blue plane wisely.

Penny nodded. She understood now. Bedtime wasn't boring. Bedtime was magical! Bedtime meant special flying time.

As dawn approached, the planes returned. The magical breeze carried Penny back. Gently, she landed on her shelf.

"See you tomorrow night!" called her new friends.

Penny's wings felt warm and happy. Her propeller hummed a sleepy song. She had discovered something wonderful.

The sun came up slowly. Tommy woke up stretching.

"I had the best dream!" Tommy said. "I dreamed about flying planes!"

Penny's propeller wiggled with joy. She had helped! She had brought good dreams!

All day, Penny flew with Tommy. They had wonderful adventures together. But now Penny had a secret. She knew something special.

When evening came again, Penny felt excited. Her wings fluttered happily. She couldn't wait for bedtime!

"Time for bed, Tommy," called his mom.

Tommy put on his pajamas. He brushed his teeth carefully. He climbed into his cozy bed.

Penny sat on her shelf smiling. Soon the lights would go out. Soon the magical breeze would come. Soon she would fly with her friends!

"Goodnight, Penny," whispered Tommy sleepily.

"Goodnight, Tommy," Penny whispered back. "Sweet dreams!"

As the room grew dark, Penny's propeller spun softly. She looked out at the starry sky. Her new friends were waiting!

Bedtime was the best time. Bedtime meant magical adventures. Bedtime meant helping children dream.

Penny Plane had found the joy in bedtime. And she couldn't wait for tomorrow night's flight!

The end.
