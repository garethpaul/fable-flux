# Story story_686

**Art Time Magic**

"Time for art!" called Mom from the kitchen. She set out papers and crayons on the big wooden table.

Tommy looked up from his toy cars. His little brother Sam was already running to the table. Sam loved art time. He always made colorful pictures with big, happy smiles.

Tommy walked slowly to his chair. He sat down with a big sigh. "I don't want to draw today," he said quietly.

"Why not?" asked Sam. His eyes were bright and excited.

Tommy shrugged. "My pictures never look good. Yours are always better."

Sam picked up a red crayon. "But drawing is fun! Look, I'm making a fire truck!"

Tommy watched Sam scribble red lines across his paper. The crayon made a soft scratching sound. Sam hummed a little tune while he drew.

Mom sat down next to Tommy. "What would you like to draw?" she asked gently.

"I don't know," Tommy mumbled. He picked up a blue crayon but didn't use it.

Sam finished his fire truck. It had wobbly wheels and a crooked ladder. "Look, Tommy! It's going to save the day!"

Tommy smiled a tiny bit. "That's nice, Sam."

"Draw with me!" Sam said. "We can make a whole fire station!"

Tommy looked at the blank paper in front of him. Maybe he could try. He pressed the blue crayon to the paper. A thin line appeared.

"What's that?" Sam asked, leaning over.

"Maybe... a building?" Tommy said softly.

"Yes! The fire station!" Sam clapped his hands. "Make it big and tall!"

Tommy drew another line. Then another. The blue crayon felt smooth in his fingers. The paper smelled clean and fresh.

"I'll add windows!" Sam said. He grabbed a yellow crayon. He drew bright squares on Tommy's building.

Tommy felt something warm in his chest. "Can I add a door?" he asked.

"Of course!" Sam giggled. "Fire trucks need doors!"

Tommy drew a big rectangle. Inside, he added a smaller rectangle for the handle. The crayon made gentle swooshing sounds.

"Now we need firefighters!" Sam announced. He started drawing stick figures with orange crayon.

Tommy picked up a brown crayon. He drew a firefighter too. His had a big hat and boots. The firefighter looked friendly.

"He looks brave!" Sam said, pointing at Tommy's drawing.

Tommy's face lit up. "Really?"

"Really! Draw more!"

Tommy reached for a green crayon. He added grass around the fire station. Then he used yellow for the sun. The sun had a smiling face.

Sam added a red fire truck next to the building. "Wee-ooo, wee-ooo!" he made siren sounds.

Tommy laughed. He drew a ladder on the truck. "The firefighters can climb up high!"

"To rescue cats in trees!" Sam added, drawing a tiny cat.

The brothers colored together. Their hands moved across the paper. Crayons rolled and clicked on the table. They shared colors back and forth.

"Purple flowers!" Sam announced, adding dots everywhere.

"And a rainbow!" Tommy said, making colorful stripes in the sky.

Soon their paper was full of colors. The fire station had become a whole neighborhood. There were houses, trees, flowers, and even a playground.

Mom came over to look. "Wow! What a beautiful picture!"

Tommy beamed. His chest felt full of sunshine. "We made it together!"

"It's the best picture ever!" Sam said, hugging Tommy's arm.

Tommy looked at their colorful creation. Every crayon mark told part of their story. The paper was covered with their imagination.

"Can we hang it on the fridge?" Tommy asked.

"Of course!" Mom said. "It deserves the best spot."

As they cleaned up the crayons, Tommy felt excited. His fingers were a little waxy from the crayons. The smell of paper and art supplies made him happy.

"Tommy?" Sam said, putting the last crayon in the box.

"Yeah?"

"Will you draw with me again tomorrow?"

Tommy grinned big. "Yes! Maybe we can draw a spaceship next time!"

Sam's eyes sparkled. "With aliens!"

"Friendly aliens," Tommy added.

Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough. Art time was going to be amazing.

The end.
