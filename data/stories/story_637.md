# Story story_637

**The Lost Red Ball**

Tommy loved his bright red ball. It bounced high and rolled fast. He took it everywhere he went.

One sunny morning, Tommy was playing in the bathroom. He tossed his red ball up and down. Up, down, up, down. The ball felt smooth in his hands.

"Wheee!" Tommy said as he threw it higher.

But then something bad happened. The ball bounced too high. It hit the ceiling with a soft thump. Then it rolled behind the big white bathtub.

"Oh no!" Tommy cried. His ball was gone!

Tommy got down on his hands and knees. He looked under the bathtub. It was very dark back there. He could see something red, but it was too far away.

"My ball!" he said sadly. "How can I get it back?"

Tommy tried to reach with his arm. He stretched and stretched. His fingers wiggled in the air. But his arm was too short. The ball stayed stuck.

"This isn't working," Tommy said with a frown.

Tommy sat on the cool bathroom floor. He felt worried. What if he never got his ball back? What if it stayed there forever?

Then Tommy had an idea. He ran to his bedroom. He grabbed his long wooden ruler from his desk. It was yellow and had numbers on it.

"This will work!" Tommy said with a smile.

He hurried back to the bathroom. Tommy lay down on the floor again. The tiles felt cold on his tummy. He pushed the ruler under the bathtub.

He moved it left and right. The ruler made scraping sounds on the floor. Tommy tried to push his ball out.

But the ruler was too thin. It went right past the ball. The ball didn't move at all.

"Come on, ball," Tommy said. "Please come out!"

He tried again and again. His face got red from trying so hard. But the ball stayed where it was.

Tommy felt like crying. Nothing was working. His favorite ball was still lost.

Just then, his big sister Maya walked by. She saw Tommy on the floor looking sad.

"What's wrong, Tommy?" Maya asked in a kind voice.

"My red ball is stuck," Tommy said. "I can't get it out."

Maya knelt down beside him. She looked under the bathtub too. Her long brown hair touched the floor.

"I have an idea," Maya said. "Wait here."

Maya came back with a long wooden spoon from the kitchen. It had a big flat end.

"This might work better," she said. "The spoon is wider than your ruler."

Tommy's eyes got bright. "Can I try?" he asked.

"Of course!" Maya said.

Tommy took the wooden spoon. It felt heavy in his hands. He pushed it under the bathtub very carefully.

This time, the wide spoon touched the ball. Tommy pushed gently. The ball started to roll!

"It's moving!" Tommy shouted with joy.

He pushed the spoon more. The red ball rolled closer and closer. Finally, it came out from under the bathtub.

"My ball!" Tommy said happily. He picked up his smooth red ball and hugged it tight.

"You did it!" Maya said with a big smile. "You didn't give up."

Tommy bounced his ball on the floor. Bounce, bounce, bounce. It made a happy sound.

"Thank you for helping me," Tommy told his sister.

"You solved the problem," Maya said. "You tried two different ways. That's called being persistent."

Tommy learned something important that day. When something is hard, you don't give up. You try different ways. You ask for help when you need it.

From then on, Tommy was more careful when he played with his ball in the bathroom. But he wasn't afraid of problems anymore. He knew he could solve them.

That night, Tommy put his red ball safely on his bedroom shelf. He felt proud and happy. Tomorrow would bring new adventures, and he was ready for anything.

The end.
