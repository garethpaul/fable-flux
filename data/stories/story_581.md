# Story story_581

**Benny's Garden Adventure**

The morning sun peeked through Benny Bunny's bedroom window. Golden light danced across his fluffy white fur. Mama Bunny knocked gently on his door.

"Time for garden work, little one," she called sweetly.

Benny pulled his soft blanket over his head. His ears drooped down low. Garden time meant digging in the dirt. It meant pulling weeds under the hot sun. His paws would get all muddy and gross.

"Do I have to, Mama?" Benny asked with a big sigh.

"The vegetables need our help to grow," Mama said. "Come eat your breakfast first."

Benny hopped slowly to the kitchen. His carrot pancakes smelled wonderful. The maple syrup was warm and sticky-sweet. But his tummy felt full of worry butterflies.

After breakfast, Mama handed Benny his tiny red shovel. They walked outside together. The morning air felt cool and fresh. Birds sang happy songs in the apple trees.

The garden looked very big to little Benny. Rows and rows of green plants stretched everywhere. Some had tiny orange carrots peeking out. Others had fat red tomatoes hanging down.

"Where do we start?" Benny asked quietly.

"Right here by the lettuce," Mama said. "We need to pull these weeds."

Benny knelt down in the soft brown earth. He grabbed a green weed with both paws. It felt rough and scratchy. He pulled and pulled until... POP! The weed came out with long white roots.

"Good job, sweetie," Mama said with a warm smile.

As Benny worked, something wonderful began to happen. He found a shiny black beetle walking slowly across a leaf. The little bug had six tiny legs that tickled Benny's paw.

"Hello there, little friend," Benny whispered.

Then he spotted something amazing. A bright yellow butterfly landed right on his nose! Its wings felt soft like flower petals. Benny giggled as it tickled his whiskers.

"Look, Mama!" Benny called excitedly. "A butterfly chose me!"

Mama smiled and kept working nearby. "The garden is full of surprises," she said.

Benny discovered more magical things. Earthworms wiggled through the dark soil. They helped make the dirt healthy for plants. A tiny green frog hopped between the cucumber vines. Its skin looked wet and bumpy.

The best surprise came when Benny dug near the carrot patch. His shovel hit something hard and orange. He brushed away the dirt with his paws. A perfect baby carrot appeared!

"I found treasure!" Benny shouted with joy.

The carrot was small and sweet. Orange dirt still clung to its sides. Benny rubbed it clean on his fur. When he took a bite, it crunched between his teeth. The taste was fresh and peppery.

"This carrot is delicious," Benny said happily. "Much better than store carrots!"

Mama nodded and wiped sweat from her forehead. "Food always tastes better when you grow it yourself."

Benny worked harder now. His paws moved quickly through the soil. He found three more baby carrots and two tiny radishes. Each one tasted like sunshine and rain mixed together.

A gentle breeze made the corn stalks whisper secrets. Bees buzzed from flower to flower. Their fuzzy bodies were covered in yellow pollen dust. The whole garden smelled like earth and growing things.

When the sun climbed high overhead, Mama called for lunch break. Benny looked at his muddy paws and dirty overalls. But now he felt proud instead of yucky.

"Can we work more after lunch?" Benny asked eagerly.

"Of course, little gardener," Mama laughed.

They sat in the shade eating sandwiches. Cool lemonade tasted perfect after working hard. Benny watched butterflies dance around the marigold flowers.

"Tomorrow can we plant more carrots?" Benny asked with bright eyes.

"We sure can," Mama said. "And maybe some lettuce too."

Benny's tail wiggled with excitement. Garden time wasn't scary anymore. It was full of wonderful surprises and tasty treasures. He couldn't wait to discover what tomorrow would bring in their magical garden.

The afternoon sun felt warm on his back as they returned to work together.

The end.
