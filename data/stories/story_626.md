# Story story_626

**Max and the Sleepy Stars**

The clock on Max's wall showed eight o'clock. Mama smiled at Max from the doorway. "Time for bed, little one," she said softly.

Max was playing with his toy cars. The red car was his favorite. He made zoom sounds as it raced around the carpet. "But I'm not sleepy," Max said. He yawned a big yawn.

"Come on, Max," said Mama. "Let's brush your teeth first." Max's feet felt heavy. Walking to the bathroom seemed so far away. The toothbrush felt cold in his mouth. The mint toothpaste tasted fresh and tingly.

Max climbed into his cozy bed. The sheets were soft and warm. But his eyes stayed wide open. "I don't want to sleep," he whispered. "Sleep is boring."

Mama tucked the blanket around Max's chin. She kissed his forehead gently. "Close your eyes and count to ten," she said. "Maybe something special will happen."

Max squeezed his eyes shut tight. One, two, three, four, five. His room felt quiet and still. Six, seven, eight, nine, ten. He opened his eyes slowly.

Something sparkled on his ceiling! Tiny golden lights danced above his head. They twinkled like real stars. Max sat up in amazement. "Wow," he breathed.

The sparkles began to move. They swirled in gentle circles. Some were big and bright. Others were small and soft. They made a whooshing sound like wind through trees.

One star floated down toward Max. It glowed with warm yellow light. "Hello, Max," the star whispered. Its voice sounded like tinkling bells. "I'm Luna. Would you like to fly with us?"

Max's eyes grew round with wonder. "Can I really fly?" he asked. Luna bobbed up and down like a nod. "Hold my light gently," she said.

Max reached out his hand. Luna felt warm and smooth. Suddenly, Max felt light as a feather. He floated up from his bed! The other stars gathered around him. They giggled like happy children.

"Where are we going?" Max asked. Luna spun in a happy circle. "To visit the Dream Garden," she said. "It's where good dreams grow."

They floated through Max's window. The night air felt cool on his face. Below, his house looked tiny and peaceful. Above, the real stars winked hello.

The Dream Garden was beautiful. Flowers glowed in rainbow colors. Purple tulips hummed lullabies. Blue roses smelled like vanilla cookies. Orange daisies sparkled like jewels.

"Pick a dream flower," said Luna. "It will give you wonderful dreams tonight." Max chose a silver flower. It felt soft like his favorite stuffed animal. It smelled like Mama's hugs.

"This is the most fun ever," Max said. He felt sleepy now but in a good way. His eyes felt heavy and comfortable. Luna smiled warmly.

"Time to go home," she said gently. "But we'll visit again tomorrow night." The stars carried Max back through his window. They tucked him into bed with invisible hands.

Max held the dream flower close. It made him feel safe and happy. "Thank you, Luna," he whispered. "Thank you for the magic."

Luna and her star friends faded slowly. Soon Max's ceiling looked normal again. But he could still feel the magic in his heart. The dream flower glowed softly in his hands.

Max's eyes drifted closed. He felt warm and peaceful. Sleep didn't seem boring anymore. It felt like the start of an adventure.

In his dreams, Max flew with Luna again. They visited candy clouds and rainbow waterfalls. They played hide and seek with friendly moon rabbits.

When morning came, Max woke up smiling. The dream flower had turned into his regular pillow. But Max remembered everything. He couldn't wait to tell Mama about his adventure.

"Tonight will be bedtime again," Max thought happily. His eyes sparkled with excitement. Maybe Luna would come back. Maybe they would visit somewhere new.

Max jumped out of bed ready for his day. But deep down, he was already looking forward to bedtime. Sleep was magical after all.

The end.
