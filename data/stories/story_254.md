# Zoe's Magical Bath Time

The sun was setting outside. Golden light danced through the bathroom window. "Bath time, Zoe!" called Mama from downstairs.

Zoe sighed deeply. She was building the most amazing tower. Her blocks reached almost to her knee! "Do I have to?" she called back. Bath time seemed so boring. The water was always too hot. Or too cold. And soap got in her eyes.

"Yes, sweetie. Come on up!"

Zoe's shoulders drooped. She climbed the stairs slowly. Each step felt heavy. In the bathroom, Mama was filling the big white tub. Steam rose like tiny clouds.

"I don't want to take a bath," Zoe mumbled. She sat on the fuzzy rug. Her chin rested in her hands.

Mama smiled gently. "What if we made it special tonight?" She reached under the sink. Out came a small bottle. It sparkled purple and blue.

"What's that?" Zoe asked. Her eyes grew wide.

"Magic bubble bath," Mama whispered. "But it only works for brave girls."

Zoe stood up straight. "I'm brave!"

Mama poured three drops into the water. Nothing happened at first. Then tiny bubbles appeared. They grew bigger and bigger. Soon the whole tub was full of fluffy white bubbles!

"Wow!" Zoe gasped. The bubbles looked like snow. Or whipped cream. Or fluffy sheep!

She dipped her toe in the water. It felt just right. Warm and cozy. Like a hug from Grandma. Zoe slipped into the tub. The bubbles tickled her chin.

"Look, Mama!" Zoe scooped up bubbles. She blew them gently. They floated up like tiny balloons. Some landed on her nose. She giggled.

Mama handed her a special cup. It had holes in the bottom. Zoe filled it with water. Then she lifted it high. Water sprinkled down like rain. "I'm making a storm!" she laughed.

The bubbles changed colors in the light. Pink, blue, yellow, green. They shimmered and sparkled. Zoe felt like a princess in a cloud castle.

She made a bubble beard. Then bubble hair. Mama laughed too. "You look silly!" she said. Zoe made funny faces in the mirror.

"Can I wash my hair?" Zoe asked. Usually she hated hair washing. But tonight felt different. Special.

Mama used the gentlest shampoo. It smelled like strawberries. Zoe closed her eyes tight. The warm water felt like a gentle massage. No soap got in her eyes at all.

"You're doing great," Mama said softly. Her voice was proud and loving.

Zoe splashed happily. She sailed her rubber duck through bubble islands. She made mountains of foam. The bathroom filled with her giggles.

Soon her fingers looked like raisins. "Time to get out," Mama said. She held up the fluffiest towel. It was warm from the dryer.

Zoe stood up slowly. Bubbles slid off her skin. They popped with tiny sounds. Like quiet fireworks. Mama wrapped her in the cozy towel.

"That was the best bath ever!" Zoe declared. She felt clean and fresh. Her skin was soft and smooth. She smelled like flowers.

Mama helped her into warm pajamas. They were soft and purple. Zoe's favorite color. She felt sleepy and happy.

"Can we use magic bubbles tomorrow?" Zoe asked. She yawned widely. Her eyes were getting heavy.

"Of course," Mama said. She tucked Zoe into bed. "Bath time is always better together."

Zoe smiled. Tomorrow seemed far away. But she was already excited. Maybe they could try different colors. Or make even bigger bubbles!

She closed her eyes. In her dreams, she floated on bubble clouds. She sailed bubble boats. Bath time wasn't boring anymore. It was magical.

The next evening, Zoe heard those special words. "Bath time, Zoe!" This time, she jumped up quickly. Her blocks could wait. Adventure was calling from upstairs.

She raced to the bathroom. Ready for more bubble magic. Ready for more giggles. Ready for the best part of her day.

Bath time had become her favorite time.

The end.
