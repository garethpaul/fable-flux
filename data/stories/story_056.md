# Maya's Magical Art Time

Maya looked at the art supplies on the kitchen table. Crayons lay scattered like colorful logs. Paint brushes stood in a jar. Paper waited, blank and white.

"Art time, Maya!" called Mom from the kitchen.

Maya sighed and shuffled her feet. "Do I have to?" she asked. Her shoulders drooped down low.

Every day after lunch was art time. Maya used to love it. But lately, everything she drew looked wrong. Her cats looked like blobs. Her houses looked wobbly. Her flowers looked like scribbles.

"I'm not good at art," Maya whispered.

Mom sat down beside her. "What would you like to try today?" she asked gently.

Maya shrugged. "I don't know. Nothing looks right."

She picked up a blue crayon. It felt smooth and waxy. She made a small mark on the paper. Just a tiny blue dot.

The dot looked lonely on the big white page.

Maya added another dot. Then another. Soon she had five blue dots dancing across the paper.

"Those look like raindrops," said Mom softly.

Maya tilted her head. They did look like raindrops! She picked up a gray crayon. She drew fluffy clouds above the dots.

The clouds looked friendly and round. Maya smiled just a little bit.

She reached for the yellow crayon. A bright sun peeked out from behind the clouds. The sun had a happy face with rosy cheeks.

"Oh!" said Maya. "The sun is saying hello!"

Her hand moved faster now. Green grass sprouted at the bottom. Purple flowers popped up like tiny surprises. A red butterfly danced near the flowers.

Maya's eyes grew wide. Her picture was coming alive! Each crayon felt like a magic wand. Every color had its own special job.

She added orange carrots in a garden. Pink roses climbing up a fence. A brown puppy playing in the yard.

"Maya, this is wonderful!" said Mom. "Look how your raindrops grew into a whole world!"

Maya looked at her picture. It wasn't perfect. The puppy's ears were different sizes. The flowers were wobbly circles. But somehow, that made it even better.

"The puppy looks happy," said Maya. "Even with funny ears!"

She picked up the black crayon. Carefully, she drew herself in the picture. A little girl with curly hair, standing in the magical garden.

In the picture, Maya was smiling big. Her arms were stretched wide, like she was hugging the whole world.

"I'm in my own art!" Maya giggled. The sound bubbled up from her toes.

She added more details. Bees buzzing around flowers. Clouds that looked like cotton candy. A rainbow arcing across the sky.

Each mark felt important. Each color had a story. The picture wasn't just lines and shapes anymore. It was Maya's own special world.

When she finished, Maya held up her artwork. The colors seemed to glow in the afternoon light.

"I made this," she said proudly. "I really made this!"

Mom hugged her tight. "You certainly did. And look how happy it makes you feel!"

Maya nodded. Her chest felt warm and fuzzy. Like she had sunshine inside her heart.

She carefully placed her picture on the drying rack. Tomorrow's blank paper sat waiting on the table.

"What will you draw tomorrow?" asked Mom.

Maya's eyes sparkled with ideas. "Maybe a castle! Or a rocket ship! Or a family of dancing elephants!"

She could hardly wait. Art time wasn't scary anymore. It was like having a box full of magic. Every crayon was a key to a new adventure.

"Can we do art time again tomorrow?" Maya asked.

"Of course," said Mom. "Same time, same place."

Maya grinned and gave her crayons a little pat. "See you tomorrow, magic friends," she whispered.

As she skipped away from the table, Maya felt light as a feather. Art time had become her favorite time. Not because her pictures were perfect, but because they were perfectly hers.

Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough.

The end.
