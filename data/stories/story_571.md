# Story story_571

**Toby the Brave Little Train**

Toby was a bright red train. He lived by the sunny beach. Every day, he carried happy children to the seaside. The warm sand felt good under his wheels. The salty air smelled fresh and clean.

One morning, Toby woke up excited. Today was the big Beach Day Festival! Hundreds of children would want rides. Toby loved making children smile. He puffed his chest out proudly.

But when Toby tried to start his engine, something was wrong. "Chug... sputter... cough!" His engine made funny sounds. Black smoke came out instead of white. Toby's wheels wouldn't turn properly.

"Oh no!" Toby whispered to himself. "The children will be here soon. They're counting on me!"

Toby looked around the train yard. He saw his friend Emma the Engine. She was big and strong. Emma could definitely help fix his problem. But Toby felt his cheeks get hot. He was too shy to ask.

"What if Emma thinks I'm silly?" Toby worried. "What if she's too busy?"

So Toby decided to fix himself. He tried pushing really hard. "Chug, chug, PUSH!" The wheels moved a tiny bit. Then they stopped again. Toby tried blowing out the black smoke. "Puff, puff, BLOW!" But more black smoke came out.

Toby felt very frustrated. His red paint seemed less shiny. The morning sun was getting higher. Soon the children would arrive for their special day.

Then Toby heard happy voices. "Where's our train ride?" called a little girl. "We want to go to the beach!" shouted a boy. More and more children gathered at the platform.

Toby's heart felt heavy. These children had been waiting all week. Their parents had promised them a magical train ride. Now he was letting everyone down.

But then Toby remembered something important. His grandfather had told him, "Being brave isn't about not feeling scared. Being brave is asking for help when you need it."

Toby took a deep breath. The salty air filled his engine. He rolled slowly toward Emma. His wheels squeaked nervously.

"Emma?" Toby said quietly. "I need help, please."

Emma turned around with a warm smile. "Of course, Toby! What's wrong?"

"My engine won't work right. The children are waiting. I feel embarrassed."

Emma's eyes were kind and understanding. "Everyone needs help sometimes, little friend. Even me!"

Emma looked at Toby's engine carefully. She listened to the strange sounds. "I know this problem!" she said cheerfully. "You just need fresh water and clean oil."

Together, they filled Toby with sparkling clean water. Emma helped pour in golden oil. She showed Toby how to check these things himself.

"Try now!" Emma encouraged.

Toby's engine purred perfectly. "Chug-a-chug-a-choo!" White steam puffed happily from his smokestack. His wheels rolled smoothly on the shiny tracks.

"Thank you, Emma!" Toby beamed. "You saved the day!"

"You saved the day by being brave enough to ask," Emma replied.

Toby hurried to the platform. The children cheered when they saw him coming. "Our train is here!" they shouted with joy.

All day long, Toby carried laughing children to the beach. They built sandcastles and splashed in the waves. The sun warmed everyone's faces. The ocean breeze felt wonderful.

At sunset, Toby returned to the train yard. He felt proud and happy. Not just because he had given great rides. But because he had learned something important.

"Asking for help isn't scary," Toby thought. "It's brave!"

From that day on, whenever Toby had a problem, he remembered Emma's kindness. He wasn't shy about asking questions. He helped other trains too.

And every Beach Day Festival after that, Toby's engine ran perfectly. The children always had their magical train rides. Toby smiled knowing that being brave and asking for help had made it all possible.

The beach waves whispered, "Well done, brave little train."

The end.
