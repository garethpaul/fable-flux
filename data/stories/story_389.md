# Woolly's Wonderful Playtime

The sun peeked through the fluffy white clouds. It was almost time for playtime! But Woolly the sheep felt tired today. Her soft wool felt heavy. Her little hooves dragged slowly.

"Playtime, Woolly!" called Mama Sheep from the cozy barn. "Come play in the yard!"

Woolly sighed a big, whooshy sigh. "I don't want to play today, Mama. Playing feels too hard."

Every day was the same. Wake up, eat breakfast, then playtime. Woolly loved her family. She loved her home. But today, playtime felt... boring.

Woolly walked slowly to the grassy yard. Her favorite red ball sat waiting. The rope swing hung quietly. Even the colorful flower garden looked plain today.

"Maybe I'll just sit here," Woolly mumbled. She plopped down with a soft thump.

But then something sparkly caught her eye. A tiny dewdrop sat on her red ball. It sparkled like a diamond! The morning sun made it dance with rainbow colors.

"Oh!" Woolly gasped. "How pretty!"

She rolled the ball gently. The dewdrop rolled too! It made a tiny, magical trail. More dewdrops appeared on the grass. They twinkled like stars.

Woolly's eyes grew wide with wonder. She had never noticed dewdrops before! She rolled the ball again. This time, she giggled.

The ball bumped into the rope swing. *Creak, creak!* The swing moved back and forth. Woolly watched it dance in the breeze.

"The swing looks lonely," Woolly said. "Maybe it wants to play too!"

She climbed onto the swing carefully. Up and down, back and forth she went. The wind tickled her wool. It felt cool and fresh.

"Wheee!" Woolly laughed out loud. "This feels like flying!"

From up high, she could see everything differently. The flower garden looked like a rainbow carpet. The dewdrops sparkled everywhere. Even the old oak tree seemed to wave hello.

Woolly jumped off the swing. Her hooves landed with a happy *thud* in the soft grass. She felt bouncy now! Light as a cloud!

She noticed something else magical. Her hoofprints made perfect little shapes in the dewy grass. Each step created a tiny pattern.

"I'm making art!" Woolly bleated excitedly.

She pranced around the yard. Step, step, hop! Her hoofprints made circles and zigzags. Soon the whole yard was covered with her special designs.

A butterfly landed on her nose. *Flutter, flutter!* Its wings were orange and black. It tickled!

"Hello, butterfly friend!" Woolly whispered. "Do you like my art?"

The butterfly danced around her hoofprint patterns. It seemed to say yes! More butterflies came to visit. They turned playtime into a magical butterfly party.

Woolly chased the butterflies gently. She leaped over her own hoofprint art. She spun in circles until she felt dizzy and happy.

"Mama, look!" Woolly called. "Everything is magical today!"

Mama Sheep smiled warmly. "Playtime is always magical, sweet Woolly. Sometimes we just need to look closer."

Woolly realized Mama was right. The dewdrops were always there. The swing always moved in the breeze. The butterflies visited every sunny day.

But today, she had really *seen* them. Really *noticed* them. That made everything feel brand new!

As the sun climbed higher, the dewdrops began to disappear. But Woolly's joy stayed bright. She had discovered something wonderful.

Every ordinary thing could be extraordinary. Every regular day could be magical. She just needed to pay attention with her heart.

"Tomorrow is playtime again!" Woolly said happily. "I wonder what magic I'll find then?"

She gave her red ball one more gentle push. It rolled through the grass, leaving a trail of sparkles in her imagination.

That night, Woolly fell asleep smiling. She dreamed of dancing dewdrops and butterfly friends. She dreamed of tomorrow's magical playtime adventure.

And when morning came again, Woolly woke up excited. Because now she knew the secret. Magic was everywhere, waiting to be discovered. Even in everyday playtime at home.
