# Story story_1038

**The Kitchen Friends**

Bounce, bounce, bounce! A bright red ball rolled around the sunny kitchen. The ball loved to play, but today felt different. Today felt quiet and sad.

"I wish I had a friend," sighed the ball. He rolled past the tall refrigerator. He bounced near the warm stove. But no one wanted to play.

The kitchen smelled like fresh bread. Sunlight danced through the window. But the ball felt lonely rolling all by himself.

Suddenly, he heard soft footsteps. Tap, tap, tap. A little yellow duck waddled into the kitchen. Her feathers were fluffy and bright. Her orange feet made tiny sounds on the floor.

"Hello there," said the duck sweetly. "I'm Dottie. What's your name?"

The ball stopped bouncing. "I'm Bouncer," he said quietly. "I was just playing alone."

Dottie tilted her small head. "Playing alone isn't very fun. Can I play with you?"

Bouncer's red surface seemed to glow brighter. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" quacked Dottie happily. "Friends always play together!"

Bouncer bounced with excitement. "What should we play first?"

Dottie looked around the big kitchen. Pots hung from hooks above. Colorful dishes sat on shelves. The floor was smooth and perfect for games.

"Let's play catch!" suggested Dottie.

Bouncer rolled toward her eagerly. Dottie pushed him gently with her wing. Bouncer rolled across the kitchen floor. He bumped softly against the cabinet.

"Wheee!" laughed Bouncer. "That was fun! Now you try!"

Dottie waddled over quickly. Bouncer used all his energy. He bounced high toward his new friend. Dottie caught him carefully in her soft wings.

"This is wonderful!" giggled Dottie. "You're so bouncy and fun!"

They played catch for a long time. Back and forth across the kitchen. The warm afternoon sun watched them play. Their laughter filled the quiet room.

"I have another idea," said Bouncer breathlessly. "Let's play hide and seek!"

Dottie clapped her wings together. "Yes! I'll hide first!"

Bouncer closed his eyes tight. He counted slowly to ten. "One, two, three..."

Dottie looked around the kitchen quickly. Where could a little duck hide? Behind the big flour jar? Under the wooden table?

She spotted the perfect place. A cozy corner behind the bread box. Dottie squeezed herself into the small space. Her yellow feathers barely showed.

"Ready or not, here I come!" called Bouncer.

Bouncer rolled around the kitchen carefully. He checked behind the fruit bowl. He looked near the cookie jar. Where was his friend hiding?

Then he heard a tiny giggle. It came from the corner! Bouncer rolled over quickly.

"Found you!" he shouted happily.

Dottie popped out laughing. "You're good at this game! Now it's your turn!"

Bouncer thought hard about hiding spots. The kitchen seemed huge to a small ball. Finally, he rolled under the kitchen table. The shadows hid his red color perfectly.

Dottie searched everywhere. She waddled past the sink. She checked behind the tall chairs. She even looked in the fruit basket.

"This is tricky," she said thoughtfully.

Then she noticed something red under the table. "There you are, Bouncer!"

They both laughed loudly. Playing together was so much better than playing alone.

"I'm getting hungry," said Dottie. "All this playing made me tired."

"Me too," agreed Bouncer. "But I don't want to stop being friends."

Dottie smiled warmly. "Silly Bouncer! Friends don't stop being friends. We can play again tomorrow!"

"And the day after that?" asked Bouncer hopefully.

"And every day!" promised Dottie.

They found some breadcrumbs on the floor. Dottie shared them with Bouncer. The kitchen felt cozy and warm around them.

As the sun began to set, they sat together quietly. The golden light made everything glow softly.

"Today was the best day ever," whispered Bouncer.

"Having a friend makes everything better," agreed Dottie.

From that day on, the kitchen was never quiet again. Two best friends filled it with laughter, games, and joy. Bouncer learned that sharing fun with others makes everything twice as wonderful.

And they lived happily ever after, playing together every single day.

The end.
