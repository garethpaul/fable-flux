# Story story_1447

**Pip's Lost Car**

Pip the pig loved to play by the big pond. He had soft pink skin and a curly tail. Every morning, he brought his favorite red toy car to the water.

The car was small and shiny. It had four black wheels that spun around. Pip liked to zoom it through the mud near the pond's edge.

"Vroom, vroom!" Pip said happily. He pushed the car around rocks and sticks.

The morning sun felt warm on his back. Birds chirped in the tall trees. Everything was perfect.

Then something terrible happened. Pip pushed his car too hard. It rolled down the muddy bank. Splash! The little red car fell right into the pond.

"Oh no!" cried Pip. His car was gone! He could see it sinking down, down, down into the dark water.

Pip felt very sad. His tummy felt heavy and tight. Big tears rolled down his cheeks.

"I need to get my car back," he said to himself. But how could he reach it? The water looked so deep and scary.

Pip thought hard. Then he had an idea! He found a long stick near the trees. The stick was brown and bumpy.

"I can fish out my car," Pip said. He lay down on his belly at the pond's edge. The mud felt cool and squishy under him.

Pip poked the stick into the water. He moved it around and around. He could feel leaves and rocks on the bottom. But where was his car?

He tried for a long time. His arms got tired. The stick kept getting stuck in the mud. Pip pulled and pulled, but no car came up.

"This isn't working," Pip sighed. He felt like giving up. Maybe his car was lost forever.

Just then, Pip heard a gentle voice. "What's wrong, little pig?"

Pip looked up. A friendly duck was swimming nearby. She had white feathers and orange feet.

"I dropped my toy car in the pond," Pip explained. "I can't reach it with this stick."

The duck nodded kindly. "I'm Della. I live in this pond. Maybe I can help you."

"Really?" Pip's eyes grew wide with hope.

"I can dive underwater," Della said. "Ducks are very good swimmers. Can you show me where your car fell in?"

Pip pointed to the spot. "Right there, by that big rock."

Della took a deep breath. Down she went with a small splash. Her orange feet disappeared under the water.

Pip waited and waited. The water was very still. Where was Della?

Suddenly, Della's head popped up. In her yellow beak was the little red car! Water dripped off its shiny surface.

"You found it!" Pip squealed with joy. He clapped his hooves together.

Della swam to the edge and gave Pip his car. It was wet but not broken. The wheels still spun perfectly.

"Thank you so much!" Pip said. He hugged his car tight. It felt wonderful to have it back.

"You're very welcome," Della smiled. "But next time, be more careful near the water."

Pip nodded. "I will! I learned something important today."

"What's that?" asked Della.

"When one way doesn't work, I should try another way," Pip said proudly. "And it's okay to ask friends for help."

Della quacked happily. "That's very wise, little pig."

From that day on, Pip played more carefully by the pond. He kept his car away from the water's edge. And whenever he saw Della swimming by, he waved and said hello.

Pip never forgot how good it felt to solve his problem. He learned that giving up was not the answer. There was always another way to try.

Sometimes the best solutions came from unexpected places. Like a kind duck who lived in the pond.

Pip smiled as he zoomed his car through the safe, dry mud. "Vroom, vroom!" he said happily. His favorite toy was home where it belonged.

The end.
