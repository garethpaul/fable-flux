# Story story_764

**Baby's Magical Dinner**

"Dinner time, sweet baby!" called Mama from the kitchen.

Baby sat in her high chair. The yellow bib felt scratchy around her neck. Green peas rolled around her plate. The carrots looked orange and mushy. Baby pushed the spoon away with her tiny hand.

"No, no, no," Baby said, shaking her head.

Mama smiled softly. "I know dinner feels hard sometimes. But your body needs good food to grow big and strong."

Baby crossed her little arms. The peas looked like tiny green balls. The carrots smelled sweet but felt squishy. She wanted to play with her blocks instead.

"What if we make dinner fun?" asked Mama. She sat down next to Baby's chair. "Let's pretend these peas are little green marbles."

Baby looked at the peas again. They did look round like marbles. She picked one up with her fingers. It felt smooth and cool.

"Pop!" said Mama as Baby put the pea in her mouth. "You caught a marble!"

Baby giggled. The pea tasted fresh and a little sweet. She reached for another one. "Pop!" she said, copying Mama.

"Now the carrots," said Mama. "These are tiny orange fish swimming on your plate."

Baby's eyes grew wide. She loved fish at the aquarium. The carrots did look like little fish pieces. She picked up her spoon this time.

"Splash!" said Baby as she scooped up a carrot. The soft orange piece tasted sweet and warm. It felt smooth on her tongue.

"The fish are swimming to your tummy!" laughed Mama.

Baby clapped her hands. Orange carrot juice made her fingers sticky. She didn't mind at all. She scooped up more fish with her spoon.

"What about the rice?" asked Mama. "What could that be?"

Baby looked at the white rice on her plate. The tiny grains looked like something she knew. She thought hard.

"Snow!" Baby said, pointing at the rice.

"Perfect!" said Mama. "Tiny snowflakes for you to catch."

Baby used her spoon to gather the rice snowflakes. They felt soft and warm in her mouth. The rice tasted mild and comforting. She made little "whoosh" sounds like wind blowing snow.

Soon Baby's plate looked almost empty. Her tummy felt full and happy. The bib had colorful spots from her magical dinner adventure.

"You ate so much good food!" said Mama, wiping Baby's face with a warm cloth. The cloth felt soft against her cheeks.

Baby smiled big. Her teeth looked tiny and white. "More fish?" she asked, pointing to the empty carrot spot.

"Tomorrow we can have more fish," promised Mama. "And maybe some other fun foods too."

Baby clapped her sticky hands together. "What food tomorrow?"

"Maybe we'll have yellow corn kernels," said Mama. "They could be tiny suns."

"Suns!" repeated Baby, bouncing in her chair.

"Or red strawberries that look like little hearts," added Mama.

Baby's eyes sparkled. "Hearts! I like hearts!"

Mama lifted Baby from her high chair. Baby's legs felt wobbly from sitting. She hugged Mama tight around the neck.

"Thank you for making dinner magical," whispered Mama.

Baby patted Mama's cheek with her small hand. "Magic dinner fun!"

They walked to the window together. Outside, the real sun was setting. The sky looked orange like the carrots Baby ate.

"Tomorrow is another day for magical food," said Mama.

Baby nodded eagerly. Her tummy felt satisfied and warm. She already wondered what magical foods tomorrow would bring.

"Magic dinner tomorrow too?" asked Baby.

"Every dinner can be magical when we use our imagination," said Mama.

Baby smiled and rested her head on Mama's shoulder. She felt happy and loved. Dinner wasn't scary anymore. It was an adventure waiting to happen.

From now on, Baby would look forward to dinner time. Every meal could become a magical journey with Mama by her side.

The end.
