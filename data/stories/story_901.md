# Story story_901

**Tiger's Big Bath Day**

Tiger loved his cozy living room. He had soft cushions everywhere. His favorite red blanket lay on the sofa. Sunlight streamed through the big window.

But today, Tiger had a problem. A very smelly problem.

"Pee-yew!" said Mama, walking into the room. "Tiger, you need a bath right now!"

Tiger's orange fur was muddy. His paws were dirty black. He smelled like old fish and wet leaves.

"No bath!" Tiger said, shaking his head. "Baths are scary and cold!"

Tiger ran behind the big blue chair. He squeezed his eyes shut tight. Maybe if he couldn't see Mama, she couldn't see him.

"I can still see your striped tail," Mama laughed gently.

Tiger's tail was sticking out. It swished back and forth nervously.

"Come on, little one," Mama said. "Let's try the bathroom."

Tiger peeked out. The bathroom looked dark and scary. The white bathtub seemed huge and deep.

"Too big!" Tiger whimpered. "Too scary!"

He ran back to his living room. Tiger dove under his red blanket. The soft fabric felt safe and warm.

Mama sat down on the sofa. She thought and thought. How could she help Tiger feel better?

"I have an idea," Mama said softly. "What if we bring the bath here?"

Tiger's ears perked up. "Here? In my living room?"

"Yes," Mama smiled. "Your special place can be our bath place too."

Mama brought a small pink basin. It was just Tiger's size. She filled it with warm, bubbly water.

The bubbles smelled like sweet strawberries. They sparkled in the sunlight. Some bubbles floated up to the ceiling.

"Pretty!" Tiger said, reaching out one paw.

Mama placed the basin on Tiger's favorite soft rug. The colorful rug made everything feel cozy.

"The water is just right," Mama said. "Not too hot, not too cold."

Tiger touched the water gently. It felt warm like sunshine. The bubbles tickled his paw.

"It feels nice," Tiger admitted quietly.

Mama brought fluffy yellow towels. She placed them on the sofa. Tiger's red blanket was nearby too.

"Your warm things are right here," Mama promised.

Tiger took a deep breath. The strawberry bubbles smelled wonderful. His living room still felt safe and happy.

Slowly, Tiger stepped into the basin. The warm water felt good on his dirty paws.

"Not scary," Tiger said, surprised.

Mama gently poured water over Tiger's back. She used a soft purple sponge. Tiger's orange fur became bright and clean.

"This feels good," Tiger purred loudly.

The muddy water turned brown. Tiger watched his dirt wash away. Clean orange stripes appeared on his fur.

Mama rubbed Tiger's ears carefully. She cleaned between his toes. Tiger giggled when the bubbles tickled his nose.

"All done!" Mama said cheerfully.

She wrapped Tiger in the fluffy yellow towel. Tiger felt warm and cozy. He smelled like sweet strawberries now.

Tiger rolled on his red blanket. His clean fur was soft and fluffy. He felt fresh and happy.

"I'm clean!" Tiger announced proudly.

He looked at his reflection in the window. His orange stripes gleamed brightly. His white chest was snowy clean.

"You smell wonderful," Mama said, giving Tiger a gentle hug.

Tiger curled up on his favorite cushions. Everything felt perfect in his living room. The sunlight made his clean fur glow.

"Mama," Tiger said thoughtfully. "Bath time wasn't scary after all."

"Sometimes new things seem scary," Mama explained. "But trying them can be wonderful."

Tiger nodded wisely. "Next time, I'll try being brave first."

He stretched happily on his soft blanket. His living room smelled like strawberries now. Tiger felt proud and clean.

"I like being a clean tiger," he purred softly.

As the sun set, Tiger dozed peacefully. He dreamed of warm bubbles and gentle hugs. Tomorrow, he might even ask for another bath.

Being brave had made everything better.

The end.
