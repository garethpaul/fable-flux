# Ben's Forest Friends

Ben loved playing in the green forest behind his house. The tall trees made cool shadows. Colorful birds sang happy songs. Ben carried his favorite red backpack everywhere he went.

Today, Ben had something very special inside his backpack. His grandmother had given him a big bag of golden crackers. They smelled like honey and sunshine. Ben's tummy rumbled just thinking about them.

"These are all mine," Ben said to himself. He hugged his backpack tight.

As Ben walked deeper into the forest, he heard soft crying sounds. Behind a mossy rock, he found a small gray squirrel. The squirrel's fluffy tail drooped sadly.

"What's wrong?" Ben asked gently.

"I'm so hungry," squeaked the squirrel. "I can't find any nuts anywhere. The other animals are hungry too."

Ben looked around. A brown rabbit sat under a fern. Its ears hung down sadly. A tiny chipmunk peeked out from behind a tree trunk. Even the bright blue birds looked tired.

Ben's hand touched his backpack. Inside were those wonderful crackers. His tummy rumbled again. But then he looked at all the sad animal faces.

"I could share," Ben thought. "But what if I don't have enough for me?"

Ben tried to walk away. But the crying sounds followed him. His heart felt heavy like a stone.

He sat down on a fallen log. "Maybe I could give away just one cracker," Ben whispered.

Ben opened his backpack carefully. He took out one golden cracker. It sparkled in the sunlight. He broke it into tiny pieces.

"Here, little squirrel," Ben said softly.

The squirrel's eyes grew big and bright. "Thank you, Ben!" The squirrel nibbled the cracker piece. "Mmm, this tastes like happiness!"

But the rabbit and chipmunk still looked hungry. Ben's heart felt funny. Not bad funny. Different funny.

"Maybe one more cracker," Ben said.

He broke another cracker into small pieces. The rabbit's nose twitched with joy. The chipmunk's cheeks puffed out happily.

"You're so kind!" chirped the birds.

Ben watched his new friends eat. Their tails wagged. Their eyes sparkled. They made happy little sounds.

Something amazing happened. Ben's tummy still felt hungry. But his heart felt warm and full. Sharing made him feel bigger inside.

"I have more crackers," Ben announced. "Let's have a forest picnic!"

Ben spread out all his crackers on a flat rock. The animals gathered around in a circle. Everyone took turns taking small bites.

The squirrel told funny jokes. The rabbit showed everyone how to hop on one foot. The chipmunk sang a squeaky song. The birds taught Ben how to whistle.

Ben laughed and laughed. His crackers tasted even better when shared with friends. The honey flavor seemed sweeter. The sunshine felt warmer.

"Sharing is like magic," Ben realized. "When I give some away, everything gets better!"

Soon, all the crackers were gone. But nobody felt sad. Everyone felt happy and full.

"Thank you for sharing, Ben," said the squirrel. "You made our day bright!"

"You made my day bright too," Ben replied. His smile stretched from ear to ear.

The animals gave Ben a group hug. Their soft fur tickled his arms. Ben giggled with pure joy.

As the sun began to set, Ben walked home through the forest. His backpack felt lighter now. But his heart felt heavier in the best way possible.

"Tomorrow I'll bring more crackers," Ben promised himself. "Sharing makes everything more fun!"

Ben skipped all the way home. He couldn't wait to tell his grandmother about his wonderful discovery. He had learned that sharing wasn't about having less. Sharing was about having more friends, more fun, and more happiness.

The forest seemed to sing Ben's name as he walked. Every bird, every rustling leaf, every gentle breeze whispered the same message: "Sharing makes the world brighter for everyone."

Ben smiled up at the twinkling stars. Tomorrow would bring another day of forest adventures and new friends to share with.

The end.
