# Story story_1058

**Ellie's Magical Lunch**

The lunch bell rang at Sunny Grove School. All the little animals hurried to the cafeteria. Their feet made happy patting sounds on the floor.

Ellie the elephant walked very slowly. Her big ears drooped down sadly. She dragged her trunk along the ground.

"Come on, Ellie!" called her friend Benny the bear. "Lunch time is fun time!"

But Ellie shook her head. "I don't want lunch," she whispered. "The tables are too small. The chairs are too tiny. Everyone stares at me."

Ellie's tummy rumbled loudly. She was very hungry. But she felt too big and clumsy for the little lunch room.

Mrs. Robin, the teacher, noticed Ellie standing alone. Her kind eyes sparkled. "Ellie, would you like to eat outside today?" she asked gently.

Ellie nodded slowly. Maybe outside would be better.

Mrs. Robin led Ellie to the school garden. Colorful flowers danced in the warm breeze. Butterflies fluttered around like tiny rainbows.

"Sit anywhere you like," said Mrs. Robin with a smile.

Ellie sat on the soft grass. It felt perfect under her bottom. She unpacked her lunch bag carefully.

Suddenly, something amazing happened. A little blue butterfly landed on her peanut butter sandwich. Then another butterfly came. And another!

"Oh my!" gasped Ellie. The butterflies had sparkly wings. They shimmered in the sunshine like magic.

More friends appeared. A friendly squirrel peeked from behind a tree. Two rabbits hopped closer. A family of birds chirped hello from above.

"They want to eat with me!" Ellie said happily.

She broke her sandwich into tiny pieces. The animals gathered around her trunk. They ate together like old friends.

The squirrel chattered funny stories. The rabbits wiggled their noses. The birds sang sweet songs. Ellie giggled and trumpeted with joy.

"This is the best lunch ever!" Ellie announced.

Her apple tasted extra crispy. Her milk tasted extra creamy. Everything felt magical in the beautiful garden.

More school friends found them outside. Benny the bear came running. "Wow, Ellie! This looks amazing!" he said.

Soon, many animals joined their garden picnic. They all sat in a big, happy circle. Everyone shared their food and stories.

"You're so good at making friends, Ellie," said Lucy the lamb. "All the garden animals love you!"

Ellie's heart felt warm and full. She wasn't too big here. She was just right. Her trunk helped her share food easily. Her big size made everyone feel safe and cozy.

The sunshine felt perfect on her gray skin. The grass smelled fresh and green. Happy sounds filled the air around them.

"I love eating outside," said Ellie. "Everything tastes better here!"

Mrs. Robin watched from nearby. Her smile was as bright as the sun. "You found the perfect lunch spot, Ellie," she said proudly.

When lunch time ended, Ellie felt sad to go inside. But she also felt excited and happy. She had discovered something wonderful.

"Can we eat outside again tomorrow?" Ellie asked hopefully.

"Of course!" said Mrs. Robin. "The garden will always be here for you."

Ellie trumpeted with joy. Her trunk swayed back and forth happily. All her new garden friends waved goodbye.

That afternoon, Ellie could hardly wait. She drew pictures of butterflies and flowers. She planned what to bring for tomorrow's garden lunch.

"I'm going to bring extra crackers," she told Benny. "The squirrels really liked them!"

Benny grinned. "Can I eat outside with you tomorrow too?"

"Yes!" said Ellie. "Everyone can come!"

The next morning, Ellie woke up excited. She packed her lunch with extra care. She even added some birdseed for her feathered friends.

At school, she couldn't stop smiling. Her tail swished happily. Her ears flapped with excitement.

When the lunch bell rang, Ellie was ready. She marched proudly toward the garden. Her friends followed behind her.

Lunch time had become the best part of Ellie's day. She learned that being different made her special. The garden was her perfect place to shine.

And every day after that, the magical garden lunch got even better.

The end.
