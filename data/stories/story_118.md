# Finley's First Day

In a sparkling pond deep in the green woods lived a little fish named Finley. His scales shimmered like tiny rainbows. The water felt cool and safe around him.

But today was different. Today was Finley's first day at Forest School.

"I don't want to go," Finley whispered. His tummy felt all twisty inside. "What if the other animals don't like me? What if I can't keep up?"

Mama Fish swam close. "Every fish feels nervous sometimes," she said gently. "But you are brave and kind, Finley."

Finley's fins trembled a little. He had never left his pond before. The woods seemed so big and scary.

"I have an idea," said Finley. "Maybe I can practice being brave first."

He swam to the shallow part of the pond. The water was warm here from the morning sun. Finley took a deep breath of water through his gills.

"Hello, I'm Finley!" he practiced saying. His voice came out as a tiny squeak.

A friendly frog hopped onto a lily pad. "Hello there, little fish! Are you okay?"

"I'm practicing for school," Finley explained. "But I feel so nervous. My scales are all tingly."

"Oh, I remember my first day!" croaked the frog. "I was so scared, I hid under a rock. But you know what helped me?"

"What?" asked Finley, swimming closer.

"I made one friend first. Just one. Then everything felt easier."

Finley nodded. That sounded like a good plan.

When it was time for school, Finley's papa helped him into a special water-filled backpack. "This will keep you comfortable," Papa said. "And remember, if you need anything, just ask for help."

The forest school was amazing! There were pools for the fish, cozy dens for the rabbits, and tall perches for the birds. Everything smelled like pine needles and fresh earth.

But Finley still felt nervous. His heart went thump-thump-thump.

He spotted a shy little turtle sitting alone by a small stream. The turtle looked just as worried as Finley felt.

"Maybe I can help someone else feel better," thought Finley.

He swam over slowly. "Hi, I'm Finley. Are you nervous too?"

The turtle's eyes brightened. "Yes! I'm Shelly. I don't know anyone here."

"Me neither," said Finley. "Want to be nervous together?"

Shelly giggled. "That sounds much better than being nervous alone!"

They explored the classroom together. Finley showed Shelly the colorful counting rocks. Shelly pointed out the soft reading corner with fluffy cushions.

Soon, a cheerful rabbit named Mrs. Bunny called everyone together. "Welcome to Forest School! Let's go around and share our names."

When it was Finley's turn, his voice was still small, but it didn't squeak. "I'm Finley, and this is my new friend Shelly."

The other animals smiled and waved. A little bird chirped hello. A young deer nodded shyly.

During snack time, Finley and Shelly sat with the bird and deer. They shared stories about their favorite games. Everyone laughed when the bird did a funny dance.

"You know what?" Finley whispered to Shelly. "I'm still a little nervous, but I'm excited too!"

"Me too!" agreed Shelly. "And tomorrow will be even easier."

When school ended, Finley couldn't wait to tell his parents everything. His scales sparkled extra bright with happiness.

"I was nervous," he told them. "But I tried being brave anyway. And I made friends!"

Mama Fish hugged him with her fins. "That's what courage is, Finley. Feeling scared but trying anyway."

That night, as Finley drifted off to sleep in his cozy pond, he thought about tomorrow. He might still feel a little nervous, but now he knew something important.

Sometimes the best way to feel better is to help someone else feel better too. And being nervous doesn't mean you can't be brave.

Finley smiled in his sleep, already excited for day two at Forest School.

The end.
