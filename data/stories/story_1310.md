# Story story_1310

**Snack Time Magic**

The big clock on the wall said three o'clock. "Snack time!" called Mrs. Chen with a big smile. All the children ran to the colorful carpet. They sat in a happy circle.

Everyone was excited except Maya. She stayed by the window. Her tummy felt funny and tight. Maya was new at school. She didn't know anyone yet.

"Come sit with us, Maya," said Mrs. Chen gently. Maya walked slowly to the circle. She sat down but looked at her shoes. The other children were already munching crackers. They were giggling and talking.

Maya opened her lunch box. Inside was her favorite sandwich. Peanut butter and strawberry jam on soft white bread. But her tummy still felt nervous. She took a tiny bite.

A boy with curly hair sat next to her. "Hi! I'm Sam," he said with a friendly grin. "What's in your sandwich? It smells really good!"

"Peanut butter and jam," Maya whispered. Her voice was quiet like a mouse.

"That's my favorite too!" Sam said. He opened his own lunch box. "Want to trade a bite? I have cheese and crackers."

Maya looked at Sam's crackers. They were shaped like little fish. Golden and crunchy looking. She nodded and broke off a piece of sandwich.

Something wonderful happened when they traded food. Maya smiled for the first time all day. The cheese crackers tasted salty and delicious. They made a happy crunch sound.

"These are amazing!" Maya said. Her voice was louder now.

A girl with braids leaned over. "I'm Zoe," she said sweetly. "Can I try some too? I have apple slices that are super crispy."

Soon the whole circle was sharing snacks. Maya tried sweet apple slices that went crunch-crunch. She tasted smooth yogurt with tiny blueberries. Someone shared soft banana bread that smelled like cinnamon.

The children passed food around the circle. Everyone was laughing and talking. Maya's nervous tummy feeling went away. Now it felt warm and happy.

"This tastes like sunshine!" Maya said, biting into an orange slice. The juice was sweet and tangy. It made her lips tingle.

Sam giggled. "And these pretzels taste like clouds!" He held up a fluffy pretzel twist.

All the children started making silly food descriptions. "My grapes taste like purple rainbows!" said Zoe. "My cookies taste like happy dreams!" laughed another friend.

Mrs. Chen clapped her hands. "What a wonderful snack time," she said. "You're all being such good friends."

Maya looked around the circle. Everyone was smiling at her. She felt warm inside, like drinking hot chocolate. Her cheeks felt rosy and happy.

"Thank you for sharing with me," Maya said to all her new friends. "This was the best snack time ever!"

The children finished eating together. They cleaned up their lunch boxes. Maya helped Sam pick up dropped crackers. Zoe shared her napkin when Maya's got messy.

As they put their lunch boxes away, Sam turned to Maya. "Will you sit with us again tomorrow?" he asked hopefully.

"Yes!" Maya said with a big grin. "And I'll bring extra sandwich to share!"

"I'll bring more fish crackers," Sam promised.

"And I'll bring apple slices for everyone," added Zoe.

Mrs. Chen smiled as she watched the children. Maya was no longer standing alone. She was surrounded by new friends. Her nervous feelings had turned into excited ones.

The next morning, Maya couldn't wait for school. She helped her mom pack extra sandwiches. She put them carefully in her lunch box.

"I can't wait for snack time!" Maya told her mom. "I have the best friends now. We share everything!"

When three o'clock came again, Maya was the first one to the carpet. She saved spots for Sam and Zoe. Her lunch box was full of treats to share.

Snack time had become magical. Not because of special food, but because of special friends. Maya learned that sharing makes everything taste better. And tomorrow would bring another wonderful snack time adventure.

The end.
