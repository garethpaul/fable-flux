# Story story_670

**Lily's Big Day**

Lily sat on her soft pink bed. Tomorrow was her first day of school. Her tummy felt funny and twisty. She hugged her teddy bear tight.

"I don't want to go to school," Lily whispered. Her room felt safe and cozy. The walls were painted yellow like sunshine. Her toys sat quietly on the shelves.

Mama peeked through the door. "What's wrong, sweetie?"

"School feels scary," Lily said. Her voice was very small. "What if I don't make friends? What if I get lost?"

Mama sat on the bed. It creaked softly. "Let's think of ways to feel better," she said gently.

Lily had an idea. "Maybe I can stay home forever!" She pulled her blanket over her head. The blanket smelled like lavender soap. Under the blanket felt dark and warm.

"Staying home won't help you learn," Mama said kindly. "School has fun things to discover."

Lily peeked out from her blanket. "Like what?"

"Books with colorful pictures. Paint for making art. New friends to play with."

But Lily still felt worried. Her heart beat fast like a little bird. She tried hiding under her bed next. The carpet felt scratchy on her knees. Dust bunnies tickled her nose.

"Lily, where did you go?" Mama called.

"I'm hiding," Lily said from under the bed. "If I hide, school can't find me."

Mama's face appeared upside down. She smiled. "Hiding won't make school go away. But I have another idea."

Mama helped Lily crawl out. They sat together on the soft rug. Mama's hands felt warm and gentle.

"Let's practice being at school," Mama said. "We can pretend right here."

Lily's eyes grew wide. "How do we pretend school?"

Mama gathered Lily's stuffed animals. There was Brown Bear, Purple Elephant, and Spotted Dog. She lined them up in a row.

"These are your new classmates," Mama explained. "You can be the teacher."

Lily giggled. Her nervous feeling started to shrink. She stood up tall like a real teacher.

"Good morning, class!" Lily said in her biggest voice. Brown Bear seemed to smile. Purple Elephant's trunk wiggled softly.

"What should we learn today?" Lily asked her animal friends. She pretended Spotted Dog raised his paw.

"Math!" Lily announced. She counted her fingers. "One, two, three, four, five!"

The stuffed animals listened quietly. Lily felt proud and happy. Being a teacher was fun!

Next, Lily taught art class. She drew pictures with her crayons. The colors looked bright and cheerful. Red like apples. Blue like the sky. Green like grass.

"Look at my beautiful flower!" Lily showed her drawing to Purple Elephant. She imagined he said it was wonderful.

Then came snack time. Lily shared pretend cookies with everyone. She poured pretend milk into tiny cups. Her tea set clinked softly.

"School is actually fun," Lily realized. Her tummy stopped feeling twisty. Instead, it felt excited and bubbly.

Mama clapped her hands. "You're such a good teacher! Tomorrow you'll be a good student too."

Lily hugged all her animal friends. "Thank you for helping me practice," she whispered. Their fur felt soft against her cheek.

That night, Lily picked out her favorite dress. It was purple with tiny stars. She set her new backpack by the door. Inside was a notebook and colorful pencils.

"I'm ready for school now," Lily told Mama. She felt brave and excited. "I know how to count and draw. I can make new friends too."

Mama tucked Lily into bed. The sheets smelled fresh and clean. "Sweet dreams, my brave girl."

Lily closed her eyes. She dreamed about her new classroom. In her dream, she made lots of friends. They painted pictures together and sang happy songs.

When morning came, Lily jumped out of bed. "Today is school day!" she announced cheerfully.

Sometimes feeling scared is okay. But trying new things can be wonderful. Lily learned that practice makes everything better. She couldn't wait to start her big adventure at school.

The end.
