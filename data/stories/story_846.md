# Story story_846

**The Little Train and Sister Mae**

Sister Mae sat in the sunny field behind her house. The grass felt soft under her legs. She picked yellow dandelions and made a small pile. But Mae felt lonely today.

"I wish I had someone to play with," she said quietly.

The warm breeze tickled her face. Birds chirped in the tall oak tree. But Mae wanted a friend to share her games.

Just then, she heard a gentle sound. "Choo-choo! Choo-choo!"

Mae looked up. A bright red toy train was rolling through the grass toward her. The train had a happy face painted on its front. Its wheels clicked softly on the ground.

"Hello there!" called the little train. "I'm Tommy Train. You look sad."

Mae's eyes grew wide. "You can talk?"

"Of course I can!" Tommy smiled. "I live in the old barn. I saw you sitting alone. Can I play with you?"

Mae felt excited. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Yes!" Tommy's whistle tooted happily. "What games do you like?"

Mae thought for a moment. "I like to pick flowers. And I like to have tea parties."

Tommy's bell rang with joy. "I love adventures! Maybe we can do both."

Mae clapped her hands. "Let's pick flowers first!"

They rolled and walked through the field together. Mae picked purple violets. Tommy used his tiny crane arm to lift white daisies. The flowers smelled sweet and fresh.

"These are beautiful!" Mae said. She put the flowers in Tommy's cargo car.

"Now what?" asked Tommy.

"Tea party time!" Mae announced.

Mae spread her pink blanket on the grass. She set out her tiny cups and plates. Tommy parked beside the blanket. His steam puffed gently in the air.

"Would you like some pretend tea?" Mae asked politely.

"Yes, please!" Tommy said. "I'll bring the cookies!"

Tommy opened his cargo car. Out rolled tiny wooden cookies he had made in his workshop. They looked delicious.

Mae poured invisible tea into the cups. "This is the best tea party ever!"

Tommy's headlight blinked happily. "I think so too! The tea tastes like sunshine."

They giggled together. Mae felt warm inside. She had found a wonderful friend.

"Tommy," Mae said, "will you be my friend forever?"

"Of course!" Tommy's whistle sang. "Best friends forever and always!"

After their tea party, they played more games. Tommy gave Mae rides around the field. Mae felt like she was flying. The wind blew through her hair.

They played hide and seek behind the big rocks. They raced to see who could reach the fence first. Mae ran fast, but Tommy's wheels were faster.

"You win!" Mae laughed. She wasn't sad about losing. Playing was more fun than winning.

As the sun began to set, it painted the sky orange and pink. Mae and Tommy sat together watching the pretty colors.

"Thank you for playing with me," Mae said softly.

"Thank you for being my friend," Tommy replied. "I was lonely too."

Mae hugged Tommy gently. His metal felt warm from the sunshine. "We'll never be lonely again!"

Tommy's bell chimed sweetly. "Never ever!"

Mae's mother called from the house. "Mae! Time for dinner!"

"I have to go," Mae said. "But I'll see you tomorrow?"

"I'll be right here waiting!" Tommy promised. "We can have more adventures!"

Mae waved goodbye as she walked home. She felt happy and excited. Tomorrow would bring new games with her special friend.

Tommy watched her go, his headlight glowing softly. He had found the best friend in the whole world.

From that day on, Mae and Tommy played together every afternoon. They had tea parties and flower picking adventures. They raced and explored and laughed together.

Mae was never lonely again. And neither was Tommy Train. They had each other, and that made everything perfect.

The field became their special place. A place where friendship bloomed like the flowers they picked together.

The end.
