# Story story_1479

Anna sat on a soft green hill. The forest was quiet around her. She picked up small stones and tossed them gently. The birds chirped in the tall trees above.

"I wish I had someone to play with," Anna said softly. She drew circles in the dirt with her finger. The warm sun felt nice on her face.

A rustling sound came from the bushes nearby. Anna looked up with curious eyes. A fluffy white sheep peeked out from behind a big oak tree. The sheep had kind brown eyes and a pink nose.

"Hello there," Anna whispered. She didn't want to scare the sheep away. The sheep took one small step closer. Then another step.

The sheep made a gentle "baa" sound. Anna smiled wide. "Do you want to be my friend?" she asked. The sheep nodded its woolly head up and down.

Anna stood up slowly. She brushed the dirt off her blue dress. "What should we play?" she wondered out loud. The sheep looked around the forest clearing.

The sheep trotted over to a pile of colorful autumn leaves. It jumped right into the middle with a happy "baa!" Leaves flew everywhere like confetti. Anna giggled at the silly sight.

"That looks fun!" Anna said. She ran to the leaf pile too. She jumped in next to her new friend. The leaves crunched under her feet. They smelled like cinnamon and earth.

The sheep rolled around in the leaves. Anna rolled too. Soon they were both covered in orange and red leaves. Anna laughed until her tummy hurt. The sheep made happy bleating sounds.

"Let's play hide and seek," Anna suggested. She covered her eyes with her hands. "One, two, three, four, five!" she counted. "Ready or not, here I come!"

Anna opened her eyes and looked around. Where was the sheep? She checked behind the big rocks. No sheep there. She looked up in the low tree branches. Still no sheep.

Then Anna heard a quiet giggle. Wait, sheep don't giggle! She followed the sound to a thick bush. The sheep's fluffy tail was sticking out. Anna could see it wiggling.

"Found you!" Anna called out cheerfully. The sheep popped out of the bush with leaves stuck to its wool. They both laughed together.

Now it was the sheep's turn to count. The sheep closed its eyes tight. "Baa, baa, baa, baa, baa!" it counted in sheep language. Anna quickly hid behind a wide tree trunk.

The sheep opened its eyes and started looking. It checked under fallen logs. It looked in tall grass patches. Finally, it found Anna behind her tree. "Baa!" the sheep said happily.

As the sun got lower in the sky, Anna felt tired. She sat down on the soft grass. The sheep curled up right next to her. Anna gently petted the sheep's warm, fluffy wool.

"You're the best friend ever," Anna told the sheep. The sheep nuzzled Anna's hand with its soft nose. Anna felt so happy and warm inside.

They watched the sunset paint the sky orange and pink. The forest grew quiet except for gentle evening sounds. Crickets started their nighttime songs.

"Will you come play again tomorrow?" Anna asked hopefully. The sheep nodded yes with enthusiasm. It gave Anna a gentle sheep hug.

Anna hugged the sheep back. The wool felt like the softest blanket. "I'm so glad we found each other," she whispered. The sheep made a content "baa" sound.

When it was time to go home, Anna waved goodbye. The sheep waved back with its hoof. Anna skipped all the way home with a big smile.

She wasn't lonely anymore. She had made a wonderful new friend. Tomorrow would bring another day of fun and games in their special forest clearing.

Anna fell asleep that night dreaming of leaf piles and hide and seek. She couldn't wait for morning to come. Having a friend made everything better and brighter.

The end.
