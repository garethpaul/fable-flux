# Mama and the Little Blue Bird

Mama climbed the creaky wooden stairs. Step by step, up to the attic. Dust danced in the sunny beams. Old boxes sat everywhere.

"I'll organize these old things," Mama said. But the attic felt so quiet. So empty. Mama sighed as she opened a dusty box.

She pulled out photo albums. Wedding pictures made her smile. Baby clothes felt soft and warm. But working alone felt lonely. Very, very lonely.

*Tap, tap, tap.*

What was that sound? Mama looked around the attic. Nothing moved. She went back to sorting.

*Tap, tap, tap.*

There it was again! Mama followed the sound. It came from the small attic window.

A little blue bird sat outside. Its bright feathers sparkled in the sunlight. The bird tapped the glass with its tiny beak.

"Hello there, little one," Mama said softly. She opened the window carefully. The bird tilted its head and chirped.

"Tweet, tweet! Can I come in?" the bird seemed to say.

"Of course you can," Mama smiled. The bird hopped inside. It looked around with curious black eyes.

The bird saw the photo albums. It fluttered over and perched nearby. Mama sat down beside her new friend.

"These are family pictures," Mama explained. The bird chirped happily. It seemed very interested.

Mama turned the pages slowly. "This is my wedding day." The bird cooed softly. "These are my children when they were babies." The bird bobbed its head.

The little bird flew to another box. It pecked at the cardboard edge. "You want to help?" Mama asked. The bird chirped yes!

Together, they opened the box. Inside were colorful Christmas ornaments. Red balls and silver stars. Golden bells and green garland.

The bird picked up a small silver bell. It rang with a gentle *ting*. The bird chirped with delight. Mama laughed at the sweet sound.

"Let's sort these together," Mama suggested. She made three piles on the floor. Red ornaments here. Silver ones there. Gold ones over there.

The bird understood perfectly! It picked up ornaments carefully. One by one, it placed them in the right piles. Red with red. Silver with silver. Gold with gold.

"You're such a good helper!" Mama said. The bird puffed up its chest proudly.

They worked side by side. Mama hummed softly. The bird chirped along. The attic filled with gentle music.

Next, they found a box of books. Old storybooks with colorful covers. Mama opened one about forest animals. The bird loved the pictures!

"This bunny looks like you," Mama pointed. The bird giggled in chirps. "And this wise owl!" The bird flapped its wings excitedly.

They read three whole books together. The bird turned pages with its beak. Mama read in different voices. A deep voice for bears. A squeaky voice for mice.

Time flew by quickly. The attic looked much better now. Organized boxes sat in neat rows. Photo albums stacked carefully. Christmas ornaments sorted by color.

"We make a great team," Mama said. The bird chirped in agreement. Working together felt wonderful. Much better than working alone.

The bird found one more surprise. A small music box in the corner. Mama wound it up carefully. Gentle music filled the attic.

The bird danced on Mama's shoulder. Mama swayed to the pretty tune. They moved together like old friends.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" Mama asked. The bird nodded enthusiastically. It chirped a happy yes!

The bird flew to the window. It turned back and chirped goodbye. "Thank you for helping me," Mama called. "Thank you for being my friend!"

The little blue bird flew away. But Mama didn't feel lonely anymore. She had a new friend. Someone to share her work. Someone to share her stories.

The next day, Mama heard familiar tapping. *Tap, tap, tap.* She smiled and opened the window. Her little blue friend had returned!

From that day on, they met in the attic. Working together, playing together, reading together. The best of friends.

Because everything is better when shared with a friend.
