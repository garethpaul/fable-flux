# Story story_1308

**Ben and the Bouncy Ball**

In the sunny yellow kitchen, a small red ball sat by the window. The ball was perfectly round and shiny. But the ball felt very lonely.

"Bounce, bounce," went the ball softly. It rolled from the counter to the floor. Then it bounced once, twice, three times. The kitchen was quiet except for the gentle bouncing sounds.

The ball wished it had a friend to play with. It rolled under the big wooden table. The floor felt cold and smooth. The ball sighed a tiny sigh.

Just then, footsteps came down the hallway. Tap, tap, tap went little feet on the tiles. A boy named Ben peeked into the kitchen. He had curly brown hair and bright green eyes.

"Oh!" said Ben when he saw the red ball. "What a pretty ball!"

The ball felt excited. Maybe this boy wanted to play! The ball rolled toward Ben's feet. It made a soft rolling sound on the kitchen floor.

Ben smiled big. "Do you want to play with me?" he asked the ball sweetly.

The ball bounced up and down. Yes, yes, yes! it seemed to say.

Ben picked up the ball. It felt warm and smooth in his hands. "You're so bouncy!" Ben laughed. His laugh sounded like tiny bells ringing.

Ben gently threw the ball across the kitchen. The ball sailed through the air. It bounced off the soft yellow wall. Then it rolled back to Ben.

"Good catch!" said Ben, even though he threw it. The ball felt happy to be noticed.

They played catch for a while. Ben threw the ball high. The ball touched the ceiling with a soft tap. Then it bounced down into Ben's waiting hands.

"Let's try something new," said Ben. He rolled the ball under the kitchen chairs. The ball loved weaving in and out. It felt like a fun maze game.

The ball rolled around the chair legs. First the blue chair, then the green chair. Ben clapped his hands with joy. The clapping echoed in the cozy kitchen.

"You're so good at rolling!" Ben told the ball. The ball felt proud and warm inside.

Ben sat on the floor. He rolled the ball in a big circle around himself. The ball enjoyed going round and round. The kitchen tiles felt smooth and cool.

"We make a great team," said Ben softly. He patted the ball gently. The ball had never felt so happy.

Then Ben had another idea. "Let's bounce together!" he said. Ben started hopping on one foot. Hop, hop, hop went Ben. Bounce, bounce, bounce went the ball.

They made a rhythm together. Hop-bounce, hop-bounce, hop-bounce. It sounded like music in the kitchen. Even the sunshine seemed to dance on the walls.

Ben's mom came into the kitchen. She smiled when she saw them playing. "What wonderful friends you two are!" she said warmly.

Ben hugged the ball close. "This is my new best friend," he told his mom proudly.

The ball felt so warm and loved. It had found the perfect friend. Ben was kind and gentle and fun to play with.

"Can we play again tomorrow?" Ben asked the ball. The ball bounced once for yes. Ben giggled at the bouncy answer.

Ben's mom made them snacks. Ben shared his crackers with the ball. He pretended the ball was eating too. They sat together by the sunny window.

The kitchen felt cozy and full of friendship. The ball was no longer lonely. Ben was no longer bored. They had each other now.

As the sun began to set, Ben yawned. "Time for bed soon," he said. But he kept the ball close beside him.

"Tomorrow we'll play even more games," Ben promised. The ball rolled happily in his lap.

The ball had learned something wonderful. The best games happen when you have a good friend. And Ben had learned that friendship can come in many shapes and sizes.

They were the perfect team. A bouncy red ball and a kind little boy. Together in the warm, sunny kitchen. Together forever as the very best of friends.

The end.
