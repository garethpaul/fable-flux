# Story story_636

**Bella's Big Splash**

Bella was a bright red bike. She lived by a sparkly pond. Every day, she rolled around the pond all by herself. 

The water made soft splashing sounds. Birds chirped happy songs in the trees. But Bella felt lonely.

"I wish I had a friend," Bella sighed. Her bell made a tiny ring sound.

Other bikes had friends to play with. They raced together on the path. They laughed and had fun. But Bella was always alone.

One sunny morning, Grandma Rose walked to the pond. She had fluffy white hair and kind eyes. She carried a small fishing pole.

Grandma Rose saw Bella by the water. "Hello there, little bike," she said sweetly. "You look sad today."

Bella's handlebars drooped down. "I am sad. I have no friends to play with."

Grandma Rose smiled warmly. "Would you like to be my friend? We could play together."

Bella's bell jingled with excitement. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" said Grandma Rose. "What games do you like?"

Bella thought hard. "I like to roll fast. I like to splash in puddles. But I don't know pond games."

Grandma Rose's eyes twinkled. "I know lots of pond games! Let me show you."

First, they played rock skipping. Grandma Rose threw smooth stones. The stones bounced across the water. Splash, skip, splash! Bella counted each bounce.

"One, two, three!" Bella cheered. Her spokes sparkled in the sunlight.

Next, they fed the ducks. Grandma Rose brought bread crumbs. The ducks quacked happily. They swam in circles around the bread.

"Look how they paddle!" said Bella. "Their feet move like my pedals!"

Grandma Rose laughed. "You're right! They do look similar."

Then they played shadow tag. Bella rolled around the pond. Her shadow danced on the grass. Grandma Rose chased the shadow with her own.

"Catch me if you can!" giggled Bella. She felt so happy inside.

The warm sun made everything glow. The pond water shimmered like diamonds. Dragonflies buzzed around the cattails.

After lunch, they tried a new game. Grandma Rose called it "pond treasure hunt."

"Look for something red," said Grandma Rose.

Bella found a red leaf floating. "Found one!" she rang proudly.

"Now find something that flies," said Grandma Rose.

A blue butterfly landed on Bella's seat. "This butterfly picked me!" Bella whispered softly.

They played until the sun got low. The sky turned orange and pink. Fireflies started to blink.

"This was the best day ever," said Bella. Her chrome parts glowed in the sunset.

Grandma Rose patted Bella's handlebar gently. "I had wonderful fun too, dear friend."

"Will you come back tomorrow?" asked Bella hopefully.

"Every day," promised Grandma Rose. "We're pond buddies now!"

Bella's heart felt full of joy. She wasn't lonely anymore. She had found her very best friend.

The next morning, Bella waited eagerly. She polished her red paint until it shined. She practiced ringing her bell.

When Grandma Rose arrived, Bella was ready. "What should we play today?" Bella asked excitedly.

"Let's try pond boat races!" said Grandma Rose. She had brought paper boats.

They spent the whole morning racing boats. The little paper boats sailed across the pond. Bella cheered for each one.

From that day on, Bella and Grandma Rose met every morning. They shared stories and games. They watched clouds and counted stars.

Other bikes started joining them too. Soon the pond became a happy meeting place. Everyone wanted to play with the friendly bike and kind grandma.

Bella learned something important. The best friendships can happen anywhere. All you need is an open heart and someone who cares.

Now Bella was never lonely. She had Grandma Rose and many new friends. The pond rang with laughter every single day.

"Thank you for being my friend," Bella told Grandma Rose.

"Thank you for teaching me new games," said Grandma Rose.

They smiled at each other. Their friendship would last forever.

The end.
