# Story story_792

**Ollie and Luna's Library Adventure**

Ollie the owl sat alone in the big library. The tall shelves reached up to the ceiling. Colorful books filled every space. Ollie loved books very much.

But today felt different. Ollie felt lonely. He pulled out his favorite book about flying. The pages felt smooth under his feathers. But reading alone wasn't fun anymore.

"I wish someone would play with me," Ollie whispered. His voice echoed in the quiet library.

Luna walked by with her arms full of books. She was Ollie's little sister. Her eyes were bright and curious. She loved stories too.

"What are you doing, Ollie?" Luna asked softly. She set her books down carefully. They made a small thump on the wooden table.

"Just reading," Ollie said sadly. He closed his book with a sigh. "But it's not very fun today."

Luna tilted her head. "Can I read with you?" she asked. Her voice was hopeful and sweet.

Ollie looked surprised. "You want to read with me?" he asked. "But you're still learning your letters."

"I can try," Luna said. She climbed up on the big chair. The cushion was soft and squishy. "Please, Ollie?"

Ollie smiled for the first time today. "Okay," he said. "Let's read together."

They opened the flying book. The pictures were bright and colorful. Ollie read the words out loud. Luna pointed at the pictures with her tiny finger.

"Look at that bird!" Luna said excitedly. "It's flying so high!"

"That's an eagle," Ollie explained. "They have very strong wings."

Luna's eyes grew wide. "Can we pretend to fly?" she asked. She spread her arms like wings.

Ollie grinned. "That sounds fun!" he said. They stood up carefully. The library was their sky now.

They flapped their arms gently. They swooped between the tall shelves. The carpet felt soft under their feet. Other children giggled quietly as they flew past.

"I'm flying to the clouds!" Luna whispered. She tiptoed on her toes. Her face glowed with joy.

"I'm flying to the moon!" Ollie whispered back. He felt so happy inside. Playing with Luna was wonderful.

They flew back to their table. Both owlets were breathing hard. Their cheeks were pink and warm.

"That was amazing!" Luna said. She hugged Ollie tight. "Can we read more books?"

"Of course!" Ollie said. He felt proud to be her big brother. "What kind of story do you want?"

Luna thought carefully. "Something about friends," she said. "Like us."

They found a book about two rabbits. The rabbits were best friends. They went on adventures together. They helped each other every day.

"Just like us," Luna said happily. She snuggled closer to Ollie. The chair felt cozy with both of them.

Ollie read every word slowly. Luna asked lots of questions. Her curiosity made the story more fun. Time passed quickly in their little corner.

"Ollie?" Luna said softly. "I'm glad you're my brother."

Ollie's heart felt warm and full. "I'm glad you're my sister too," he said. "You make everything better."

The librarian walked over to them. She had a kind smile. "You two are wonderful readers," she said quietly. "The library closes soon."

They packed up their books carefully. Luna held Ollie's wing as they walked. The setting sun made the windows glow orange.

"Can we come back tomorrow?" Luna asked. Her eyes sparkled with excitement.

"Every day," Ollie promised. "We'll read lots of stories together."

They walked home under the starry sky. Ollie wasn't lonely anymore. He had the best reading partner ever. Luna skipped beside him happily.

From that day on, they visited the library together. They read about dragons and princesses. They discovered new worlds in every book. But best of all, they discovered how wonderful it was to share stories with someone special.

The library became their magical place. And their friendship grew stronger with every page they turned together.

The end.
