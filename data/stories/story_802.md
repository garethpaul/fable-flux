# Story story_802

**Sandy's New Friends**

Sandy the squirrel sat on the warm beach sand. The sun felt nice on her fluffy tail. Salty air tickled her nose. She watched the blue waves splash and foam.

"This looks like fun," Sandy said. She had never seen a beach before. The sand was soft between her tiny paws. Seagulls flew high above her head.

But something felt wrong. Sandy looked around the big beach. She saw crabs scuttling in the sand. She saw fish jumping in the water. She saw children building sandcastles far away.

"I have no one to play with," Sandy sighed. Her ears drooped down sadly. "Everyone looks busy. No one wants to play with a squirrel."

Sandy's tummy felt empty and sad. She missed her forest friends. At home, rabbits would hop with her. Birds would sing songs together. Here, she felt very alone.

"I need to find friends," Sandy decided. She brushed sand off her paws. "I will try my best."

Sandy saw some hermit crabs near the water. They were carrying pretty shells on their backs. Their shells were pink and orange and white.

"Hello!" Sandy called out cheerfully. "Want to play with me?" She ran toward the crabs with a big smile.

But the crabs got scared. They quickly hid inside their shells. Sandy could only see their little eyes peeking out.

"We don't know you," said one crab. "Squirrels don't belong on beaches." The crabs scurried away fast. Their shells made scratching sounds on the sand.

Sandy felt tears in her eyes. Her first try didn't work. She sat down and watched the waves again. The water made whooshing sounds that felt lonely.

"Maybe I should go home," Sandy whispered. But then she saw something interesting. A group of seagulls was playing a game. They were dropping shells into the water and diving to catch them.

"That looks really fun," Sandy said. She wiped her eyes with her paw. "I won't give up yet."

Sandy picked up a pretty shell. It was smooth and shiny. She walked closer to the seagulls. Her heart beat fast with hope.

"Can I play too?" Sandy asked politely. She held up her shell. "I found this one."

The biggest seagull tilted his head. "You're not a bird," he said. "How can you dive for shells?"

"I can't fly or dive," Sandy admitted. "But I have other skills." She thought hard about what she could do. "I can climb and I can throw really well."

Sandy threw her shell high into the air. It spun and sparkled in the sunlight. Then she quickly climbed up a piece of driftwood. She caught the shell perfectly as it came down.

"Wow!" said a young seagull. "That was amazing!" The other birds flapped their wings excitedly.

"I have an idea," said the biggest seagull. "You throw shells up high. We'll try to catch them in the air. Then we'll drop shells for you to catch."

Sandy's tail twitched with happiness. "That sounds perfect!"

They played their new game for hours. Sandy threw shells that sparkled like jewels. The seagulls swooped and caught them gracefully. When they dropped shells for Sandy, she climbed and jumped to catch them.

"This is the best game ever," laughed Sandy. Her fur felt warm from running and playing. The salty breeze felt wonderful now.

As the sun began to set, it painted the sky orange and pink. Sandy's new friends gathered around her.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" asked the young seagull.

"Yes!" Sandy said happily. "I'll bring more shells to throw."

Sandy learned something important that day. Sometimes making friends takes time. Sometimes the first try doesn't work. But if you keep trying and stay kind, you can find wonderful friends anywhere.

Even a squirrel can belong at the beach. Even different animals can play together. Sandy fell asleep that night with sand between her toes and joy in her heart.

She couldn't wait for tomorrow's adventures.

The end.
