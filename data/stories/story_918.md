# Story story_918

Mama sat on the swing at the playground. She moved back and forth slowly. The chains made a soft creaking sound. Other children played nearby, but Mama felt lonely.

She watched the slide. Children went down laughing. She saw the monkey bars. Kids swung from bar to bar. But Mama had no one to play with.

The sun felt warm on her face. Birds chirped in the big oak tree. The playground smelled like fresh grass and flowers. But Mama still felt sad.

Then she heard footsteps on the wood chips. A little boy walked toward her. He had curly brown hair and bright blue eyes. He wore a red shirt with a rocket on it.

"Hi," said the boy. "I'm Jack. Can I play with you?"

Mama looked up. Her eyes were a little watery. "I'm Mama," she said softly. "I don't have anyone to play with."

Jack smiled big. "Now you do! Want to go on the seesaw?"

Mama's face lit up. "Yes, please!"

They ran to the bright yellow seesaw. Jack sat on one end. Mama sat on the other. Up and down they went. The seesaw squeaked happily.

"This is fun!" Mama giggled.

"Let's try the slide next!" Jack said.

They climbed up the tall blue slide. The metal steps felt warm from the sun. At the top, they could see the whole playground.

"Ready?" asked Jack.

"Ready!" said Mama.

Down they went together. The slide was smooth and fast. They landed in the soft sand at the bottom. Both children laughed out loud.

"That was amazing!" Mama said. Her cheeks were pink with excitement.

"Want to swing together?" Jack asked.

They found two swings side by side. Jack pushed Mama first. She flew high into the air. The wind blew through her hair. She felt like she was flying.

Then Mama pushed Jack. He pumped his legs hard. Soon he was swinging very high too.

"Look how high I am!" Jack called out.

"You're almost touching the clouds!" Mama laughed.

Next, they played on the monkey bars. Jack went first. He swung from bar to bar like a real monkey. Mama clapped for him.

"Your turn," Jack said.

Mama tried the monkey bars. Her hands felt sweaty on the cold metal. She made it halfway across.

"I can't do it," she said.

"Try again," Jack said kindly. "I'll help you."

Jack stood under the bars. "If you fall, I'll catch you," he promised.

Mama felt brave with Jack there. She tried again. This time she made it all the way across!

"I did it!" she cheered.

"You were awesome!" Jack said.

They found a shady spot under the oak tree. The grass felt cool and soft. They sat down to rest.

"I was lonely before," Mama said. "Thank you for playing with me."

"I was lonely too," Jack said. "My family just moved here. I don't know anyone yet."

"Now we know each other," Mama smiled.

"Want to be best friends?" Jack asked.

"Yes!" Mama said happily.

They made a pinky promise. Their little fingers locked together tight.

"Best friends forever," they said together.

A butterfly landed on a nearby flower. It had orange and black wings. The children watched it quietly.

"Tomorrow, let's play pirates," Jack suggested.

"And the day after, let's play space explorers," Mama added.

They planned many fun games. The afternoon sun began to set. The sky turned pink and orange.

Soon their parents called them. Time to go home.

"See you tomorrow?" Jack asked.

"See you tomorrow!" Mama promised.

They waved goodbye. Both children skipped home happily. Mama wasn't lonely anymore. Jack had a new friend too.

The playground was quiet now. But tomorrow it would be full of laughter again. Two best friends would play together. The swings would creak with joy. The slide would echo with giggles.

Friendship had bloomed like the flowers around the oak tree. And it would grow stronger every day.

The end.
