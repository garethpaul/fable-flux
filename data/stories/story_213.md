# **The Best of Friends**

Rumble the red truck sat alone in the sunny farm yard. He rolled back and forth by himself. The chickens clucked nearby. The cows mooed in the distance. But Rumble felt lonely.

"I wish I had a friend," sighed Rumble. His bright red paint sparkled in the morning sun. His big wheels made soft sounds on the dirt.

Just then, something blue caught his eye. Down by the farm pond, a little boat was floating. The boat had a cheerful white sail. Her name was Splash.

Splash saw Rumble too. She bobbed up and down in the water. "Hello there!" she called out. "I'm Splash! What's your name?"

"I'm Rumble," said the truck shyly. "I was just playing by myself."

"Playing alone isn't much fun," said Splash kindly. "Would you like to play together?"

Rumble's engine hummed with excitement. But then he looked worried. "I can't go in the water," he said sadly. "And you can't come on land."

Splash thought for a moment. Her sail fluttered in the breeze. "Maybe we can find a way!" she said cheerfully.

Rumble rolled closer to the pond's edge. The water lapped gently at the shore. "What do you like to do?" he asked.

"I love to carry things!" said Splash proudly. "I can float heavy loads across the pond."

Rumble's headlights lit up with joy. "I love carrying things too! I haul hay and corn all around the farm!"

"We're the same!" laughed Splash. The water sparkled around her blue hull.

Rumble had an idea. "Look! There are apples by the big tree. The farmer wants them by the barn."

"But the tree is on my side of the pond," said Splash. "And the barn is on your side."

"We could work together!" said Rumble excitedly. His horn beeped twice.

Splash clapped her tiny anchor. "Yes! I'll collect the apples. You can take them to the barn!"

The two friends got to work. Splash sailed to the apple tree. She carefully gathered the red apples in her hull. The apples made soft plunking sounds as they landed.

"Ready!" called Splash. She sailed back to the shore.

Rumble backed up to the water's edge. Splash gently passed the apples to his truck bed. "One, two, three, four, five!" counted Rumble happily.

"Now it's your turn!" said Splash with a smile.

Rumble rumbled across the farm yard. His wheels crunched on the fallen leaves. He delivered the apples to the red barn. The farmer waved and smiled.

"Thank you, Rumble!" called the farmer.

Rumble hurried back to the pond. "The farmer was so happy!" he told Splash.

"We make a great team!" said Splash. She did a little spin in the water.

From that day on, Rumble and Splash played together every day. Sometimes they raced along the pond's edge. Rumble would roll fast on land. Splash would zoom through the water.

Sometimes they played hide and seek. Rumble would hide behind the big oak tree. Splash would hide under the wooden dock.

But their favorite game was helping the farmer together. They moved pumpkins, corn, and hay. Working as a team made everything easier. And much more fun!

"I'm so glad you wanted to be my friend," said Rumble one sunny afternoon.

"Me too," said Splash happily. "Friends make everything better!"

The farm animals gathered around to watch. The chickens clucked approval. The cows mooed cheerfully. Even the old farm dog wagged his tail.

As the sun set over the farm, Rumble and Splash knew they would be best friends forever. They had learned that being different didn't matter. What mattered was being kind and helpful.

"Same time tomorrow?" asked Splash.

"Wouldn't miss it!" beeped Rumble.

And they never played alone again.

**The End**

*Sometimes the best friendships happen when we work together and help each other, even when we're different!*
