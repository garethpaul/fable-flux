# Story story_1172

**Teddy's Magical Park Day**

"Time for the park, Teddy!" called Mama.

Teddy looked up from his blocks. He made a sad face. The park seemed too big. Too loud. Too scary.

"I don't want to go," Teddy whispered.

Mama sat down beside him. "What if we bring your favorite truck?"

Teddy shook his head. His little hands held tight to his blocks.

"The swings are fun," said Mama gently.

"Too high," mumbled Teddy.

Mama smiled. "We can go slow. Just you and me."

Teddy thought about it. Mama's hand felt warm. Maybe the park wouldn't be so scary.

"Okay," he said in a tiny voice.

At the park, Teddy stayed close to Mama. The playground looked enormous. Children ran everywhere. They laughed and shouted.

Teddy hid behind Mama's legs.

"Let's sit on this bench first," said Mama.

They sat together. Teddy watched the other kids. A girl with pigtails went down the slide. She giggled the whole way down.

"That looks fun," said Mama.

Teddy wasn't sure. The slide looked very tall.

Then something caught his eye. Near the sandbox, something sparkled. It twinkled like a tiny star.

"What's that?" asked Teddy.

He pointed to the sparkling spot. Mama looked too.

"I'm not sure. Should we look?"

Teddy nodded. His curiosity was bigger than his fear now.

They walked to the sandbox together. In the sand sat a small, shiny rock. It was smooth and round. When the sun hit it, it sparkled like magic.

"It's beautiful," whispered Teddy.

He picked up the rock. It felt warm in his palm. The sparkles danced in the sunlight.

"Maybe it's a wishing rock," said Mama.

Teddy's eyes grew wide. "Really?"

"Make a wish and see."

Teddy closed his eyes tight. He held the rock close. "I wish the park could be fun," he whispered.

When he opened his eyes, a butterfly landed nearby. It had orange and black wings. It fluttered around the sandbox.

"Look!" said Teddy.

The butterfly seemed to dance just for him. Teddy followed it with his eyes. It flew to the swings.

"The butterfly wants to swing," giggled Teddy.

Mama smiled. "Should we swing with it?"

Teddy looked at the swings. They didn't seem so scary now. The butterfly made everything feel magical.

"Yes!" said Teddy.

Mama helped him onto a small swing. She pushed very gently. Teddy went up just a little bit. Then down. Up and down.

"This is nice," said Teddy.

The butterfly flew around them. Other children came to watch. They thought the butterfly was wonderful too.

"Hi," said the girl with pigtails. "I'm Lucy."

"I'm Teddy," he said shyly.

"Want to see my sand castle?" asked Lucy.

Teddy looked at Mama. She nodded with a smile.

At the sandbox, Lucy had built a beautiful castle. Teddy showed her his sparkling rock.

"Wow!" said Lucy. "It's like treasure!"

They decided to bury the treasure near the castle. They dug together. They built roads in the sand. They made a whole kingdom.

More children joined them. Everyone wanted to help. They built bridges and towers. They found sticks for flags.

Teddy forgot all about being scared. His hands were sandy. His cheeks were pink from sunshine. His heart felt happy and light.

"Time to go home," called Mama.

Teddy looked surprised. Had they been playing that long?

"Can we come back tomorrow?" asked Teddy.

"Of course," said Mama.

Teddy waved goodbye to Lucy. He waved to the other children. He even waved to the butterfly.

Walking home, Teddy held Mama's hand. The sparkling rock was safe in his pocket.

"The park is fun," said Teddy.

"Yes, it is," agreed Mama.

"Tomorrow I want to try the slide."

Mama squeezed his hand. "That sounds perfect."

Teddy skipped a little. He couldn't wait for tomorrow. The park wasn't scary anymore. It was magical. Full of friends and butterflies and sparkling treasures.

And best of all, Mama would be there too.

The end.
