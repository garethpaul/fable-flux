# Story story_1331

**The Magic Reading Puzzle**

Every afternoon at three o'clock, Mrs. Chen rang the special bell. "Reading time!" she called to her class. All the children put away their toys. They walked to the cozy corner with soft pillows.

But today was different. Today, Mrs. Chen brought a big wooden box. Inside were colorful puzzle pieces. Each piece had letters and pictures on it.

"Today we'll do puzzle reading time," Mrs. Chen said with a smile. The children sat in a circle on the fluffy blue carpet.

Emma looked at the puzzle pieces. They seemed too hard. The letters looked mixed up and confusing. She crossed her arms and frowned.

"I don't want to do puzzles," Emma whispered. "Reading is easier with books."

Her friend Jake picked up a red piece. It had the letter "C" and a picture of a cat. "Look, Emma! This piece is pretty."

Emma shook her head. "Puzzles are too tricky for me."

Mrs. Chen sat down next to Emma. "Would you like to try just one piece?" she asked gently.

Emma picked up a yellow piece. It felt smooth and warm in her hands. The piece had the letter "S" and a picture of a smiling sun.

"Sun starts with S," Emma said quietly.

"That's right!" Mrs. Chen clapped her hands. "You're so smart, Emma."

Emma looked for another piece. She found a blue one with the letter "K" and a picture of a kite. The kite had long, colorful ribbons.

"Kite starts with K," Emma said louder this time.

Something wonderful started to happen. As Emma put the pieces together, they began to glow softly. The pictures seemed to move and dance.

The cat on the "C" piece stretched and yawned. The sun on the "S" piece winked at Emma. The kite on the "K" piece fluttered its ribbons.

"Wow!" Emma gasped. Her eyes grew wide with wonder.

Other children noticed too. "My dog piece is wagging its tail!" Jake laughed.

"My bird is singing!" called out Mia.

Soon all the children were excited. They worked together to find matching pieces. The letters spelled out simple words. The pictures told little stories.

Emma found pieces that spelled "CAT." The orange cat jumped out of the puzzle and rubbed against her hand. It felt soft and fuzzy. Emma giggled with delight.

"This is amazing!" Emma said. She reached for more pieces.

The children worked for thirty minutes. They made words like "DOG," "FISH," and "TREE." Each word came alive with magic.

The puzzle dog barked happily. The puzzle fish swam in circles. The puzzle tree swayed in an invisible breeze.

Emma's heart felt warm and happy. Reading with puzzles was fun! She could touch the letters. She could see the stories move.

"I love puzzle reading time," Emma told Mrs. Chen.

"I'm so proud of you for trying," Mrs. Chen replied. "You discovered something wonderful today."

As reading time ended, the magic slowly faded. The pictures became still again. But Emma's excitement stayed bright.

She carefully put the puzzle pieces back in the wooden box. Her fingers traced each letter lovingly.

"Will we do puzzle reading tomorrow?" Emma asked hopefully.

"Of course!" Mrs. Chen smiled. "We have many more magical words to discover."

Emma skipped back to her desk. She couldn't wait for tomorrow's reading time. Puzzles weren't scary anymore. They were full of surprises and magic.

That night, Emma dreamed of dancing letters and moving pictures. She dreamed of cats that purred and birds that sang. She dreamed of all the wonderful words waiting to be discovered.

The next morning, Emma was the first one ready for reading time. She sat by the wooden box, waiting patiently.

"I'm ready for more magic," she whispered to herself.

And when three o'clock came, Emma's face lit up with the biggest smile. Reading time was her favorite time now.

The end.
