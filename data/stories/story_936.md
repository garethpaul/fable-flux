# Story story_936

**Tommy and the Magic Ball**

Tommy sat in the big sandbox. The warm sand felt soft under his hands. He made little hills and valleys. But something felt missing.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," Tommy said sadly.

Near the fence sat a bright red ball. The ball had been watching Tommy all morning. It wanted to roll over and play.

"Maybe he would like to be my friend," thought the ball.

The ball took a tiny bounce. Then another bounce. It rolled closer to the sandbox.

Tommy looked up. "Oh! A ball!"

The red ball rolled right up to Tommy's feet. It seemed to wiggle with excitement.

"Do you want to play?" Tommy asked the ball.

The ball bounced once. That meant yes!

Tommy picked up the ball. It felt smooth and warm from the sunshine. "What should we play first?"

Tommy gently rolled the ball across the sand. The ball made a soft whooshing sound. It rolled in a perfect circle around Tommy.

"Wow! You're really good at rolling!" Tommy laughed.

Next, Tommy tried bouncing the ball. Bounce, bounce, bounce! The ball went up and down happily. Each bounce made a soft thump in the sand.

"Let's build something together," Tommy said.

Tommy started making a sand castle. The ball rolled around the castle walls. It helped pack the sand down tight.

"You're helping me build!" Tommy smiled big.

The ball bounced with joy. It had never helped build anything before.

Tommy made a tunnel through the sand. The ball rolled through it perfectly. In and out, in and out it went.

"This is so much fun!" Tommy giggled.

The warm sun shone down on them. A gentle breeze made the leaves whisper softly. Birds chirped happy songs in the trees.

Tommy and the ball played catch next. Tommy threw the ball up high. The ball loved flying through the air. It came down right into Tommy's waiting hands.

"Good catch, Ball!" Tommy said proudly.

The ball felt so happy. It had found the best friend ever.

They played hide and seek in the sandbox. Tommy covered his eyes and counted. "One, two, three..."

The ball rolled behind a sand hill. When Tommy looked, he pretended he couldn't find it.

"Where could Ball be?" Tommy wondered out loud.

The ball giggled silently behind the sand hill.

"Found you!" Tommy called out happily.

The ball bounced out from its hiding spot. They both felt so joyful.

As the afternoon went on, they invented new games. They made sand soup together. The ball helped stir by rolling in circles.

They built roads in the sand. The ball rolled down each road like a little car.

"Beep beep!" Tommy made car sounds.

The ball rolled faster and faster down the sandy roads.

When Tommy felt thirsty, the ball rolled over to get his water bottle. What a helpful friend!

"Thank you, Ball," Tommy said. "You take such good care of me."

The ball felt warm and proud inside.

Soon, Tommy's mom called from the house. "Tommy! Time to come in!"

Tommy felt sad. He didn't want to stop playing.

"Will you be here tomorrow?" Tommy asked his new friend.

The ball bounced twice. That meant "yes, yes!"

Tommy carefully placed the ball in a safe spot by the sandbox.

"Good night, Ball," Tommy whispered. "Thank you for being my friend."

The ball settled down contentedly. It had found something wonderful today.

The next morning, Tommy ran outside. The red ball was waiting right where he left it.

"Ball! You came back!" Tommy shouted with glee.

The ball bounced excitedly. Of course it came back!

From that day on, Tommy and the ball played together every day. They had sandbox adventures, rolling games, and building contests.

Tommy never felt lonely again. He had found the most special friend right in his own backyard.

And the ball? It had never been happier. Sometimes the best friendships happen when you least expect them.

Every day brought new games and more laughter. Tommy and his ball were the very best of friends.

The end.
