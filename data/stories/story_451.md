# Lily's Special Book

Lily loved going to nursery every day. She had curly brown hair that bounced when she walked. Her favorite purple dress had tiny flowers on it. Today felt extra special because she brought her most treasured book.

"This is my special book," Lily told her friend Sam. The book had a soft, fuzzy cover. It was bright red like a strawberry. Inside were pictures of dancing animals. Her grandma gave it to her last week.

Lily carried the book everywhere at nursery. She read it during snack time. She looked at the pictures during quiet time. The fuzzy cover felt so nice under her fingers.

But after lunch, something terrible happened. Lily reached for her book. It wasn't there! She looked left and right. No book anywhere.

"Oh no!" Lily said. Her eyes filled with tears. "My special book is gone!"

Mrs. Chen, their kind teacher, came over. "What's wrong, sweet Lily?" she asked gently.

"I can't find my book," Lily sniffled. "The red fuzzy one from Grandma."

"Let's look together," Mrs. Chen said warmly. "Where did you have it last?"

Lily thought hard. "I had it at story time," she remembered.

They walked to the reading corner. Lily moved the big blue cushions. She looked under the wooden bookshelf. She checked behind the toy box. No red fuzzy book anywhere.

"Maybe it's by the art table," Lily said hopefully.

They searched near the crayons and paper. Lily looked in every basket. She checked under every chair. Still no book.

Lily's bottom lip trembled. "It's really gone," she whispered.

Sam came over to help. "I'll look too," he said kindly.

More friends joined the search. Emma checked the dress-up corner. Marcus looked by the blocks. Everyone wanted to help Lily.

"Let's try the playground area," suggested Mrs. Chen.

Outside, Lily searched everywhere. She looked under the slide. She checked the sandbox. She even looked in the flower garden. The book was nowhere to be found.

Lily sat on a bench. She felt very sad. "I'll never see my book again," she sighed.

Mrs. Chen sat beside her. "Sometimes when we lose things, we find them in surprising places," she said softly.

"Like where?" Lily asked.

"Well, let's think. What did you do today?"

Lily closed her eyes. She remembered reading her book. She remembered showing it to friends. Then she remembered something else.

"I took my sweater off!" Lily said excitedly. "When it got warm outside!"

They hurried to the coat hooks inside. Lily's yellow sweater hung on her special hook. Mrs. Chen helped her check the pockets.

In the left pocket... nothing. In the right pocket... Lily's fingers touched something soft and fuzzy!

"My book!" Lily shouted with joy. She pulled out her special red book. The fuzzy cover felt wonderful in her hands.

"You found it!" Sam cheered. All her friends clapped and smiled.

Lily hugged her book tight. "I put it in my pocket to keep it safe," she remembered. "Then I forgot!"

Mrs. Chen smiled proudly. "You didn't give up, Lily. You kept trying different places."

"And my friends helped me," Lily added happily.

At the end of the day, Lily felt very proud. She had solved her big problem. She learned that when something seems lost, it helps to think carefully. It helps to look in many places. And it helps to ask friends for help.

Lily's mom came to pick her up. "How was your day?" she asked.

"I lost my special book," Lily said. "But I found it again! I looked and looked until I remembered where it was."

"You're such a good problem-solver," Mom said, giving her a warm hug.

That night, Lily put her special book safely on her bedside table. She felt happy and proud. Tomorrow she would bring it to nursery again. But this time, she would remember to keep track of where she put it.

Sometimes losing things feels scary. But Lily learned that with patience, thinking, and good friends, problems can be solved. That made her feel very grown-up indeed.

The end.
