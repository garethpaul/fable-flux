# Story story_1118

**Buddy's Colorful Day**

The big clock on the wall went tick-tock. Miss Sarah clapped her hands together. "Art time, everyone!" she called out cheerfully.

All the children jumped up from their seats. They ran to the art table with big smiles. Red paint pots sat ready. Blue brushes waited in cups. Yellow paper lay flat and clean.

But Buddy the classroom dog stayed under his favorite desk. His brown ears drooped down low. His tail tucked between his legs. Buddy did not like art time.

"Come on, Buddy," called Emma. She had paint on her fingers already. "Art time is fun!"

Buddy shook his furry head. No, no, no. Art time was messy. Art time was loud. Paint smelled funny. Brushes felt weird.

Miss Sarah walked over to Buddy's hiding spot. She bent down low. "What's wrong, sweet boy?" she asked softly.

Buddy whimpered a tiny whimper. He pointed his nose at the paint table. Everything looked so messy already.

"I have an idea," Miss Sarah said. Her eyes twinkled. "Would you like to try something special?"

She brought over a big, shallow pan. She poured thick, colorful paint inside. Purple and orange swirled together. Green and pink mixed in spirals.

"You can paint with your paws," Miss Sarah explained. "Just like the children paint with brushes."

Buddy tilted his head. Paint with paws? That sounded different. Maybe not so scary.

Miss Sarah dipped her hand in the paint. She pressed it on white paper. Her handprint appeared like magic! Purple and orange and beautiful.

"See?" she said. "It feels squishy and warm."

Buddy sniffed the paint. It smelled like berries and flowers. Not scary at all. He touched one toe to the purple paint.

Squish! It felt cool and smooth. Like mud after rain. Like pudding at snack time.

Buddy put his whole paw in the paint. Squish, squash, squish! The paint squeezed between his toes. It tickled in the best way.

He stepped on the white paper. His paw print appeared! A perfect purple paw with four little toe marks.

"Beautiful!" cheered Emma. "Look at Buddy's art!"

Buddy wagged his tail. Just a little wag. He tried his other paw. This time in orange paint. Squish, squash, step! Another perfect paw print.

Soon Buddy was stepping all around the paper. Purple paws and orange paws everywhere. He added green paws and pink paws too.

The children gathered around to watch. "Buddy is an artist!" laughed Jake.

"His painting is so pretty!" said Lily.

Buddy's tail wagged faster and faster. He felt proud and happy. The paint felt wonderful on his paws. The colors looked bright and cheerful.

He made paw prints in rows. He made paw prints in circles. He even tried walking backwards to make a special pattern.

"You're creating a masterpiece," Miss Sarah said. She scratched behind Buddy's ears.

When art time ended, Buddy's painting was full of colorful paw prints. It looked like a rainbow had danced across the paper.

Miss Sarah hung Buddy's artwork on the wall. Right next to all the children's paintings. "Every artist deserves to show their work," she said.

Buddy sat back and looked at his creation. His chest puffed out with pride. The paint on his paws was already dry. It didn't feel scary anymore. It felt like magic.

The children washed their hands at the sink. Buddy got his paws cleaned too. The warm water felt nice. Miss Sarah dried his paws with a soft towel.

As the day ended, Buddy curled up for his nap. But he kept looking at his colorful painting on the wall. His very own artwork.

The next morning, Buddy heard the familiar tick-tock of the clock. Miss Sarah clapped her hands. "Art time, everyone!"

This time, Buddy jumped up first. His tail wagged excitedly. He ran straight to the paint table.

Art time wasn't scary anymore. Art time was magical. Art time was fun.

And Buddy couldn't wait to make more colorful paw prints.

The end.
