# Woolly's Cookie Adventure

Woolly the sheep loved to explore the long, sunny hallway in Farmer Mae's house. The wooden floors creaked softly under her hooves. Sunlight danced through the tall windows. Pictures of farm animals smiled down from the walls.

Today, something wonderful caught Woolly's nose. She sniffed the air. *Sniff, sniff.* "Mmm!" she said. "I smell cookies!"

Woolly followed the sweet smell. It led her to a high kitchen counter. There, in a bright red jar, sat the most delicious-looking cookies. They were golden brown and sparkled with sugar. Woolly's tummy rumbled.

"I want those cookies," Woolly said. She stood on her back legs. She stretched her neck as high as she could. But the counter was too tall. Her hooves couldn't even touch it.

Woolly tried jumping. *Boing, boing!* Up and down she went. But she still couldn't reach the cookies. She felt disappointed.

"Maybe I need to be taller," Woolly thought. She looked around the hallway. She spotted a small wooden stool. Perfect!

Woolly pushed the stool with her nose. *Scrape, scrape* across the floor. She positioned it near the counter. Then she climbed up carefully. Her hooves wobbled on the smooth wood.

Now she was higher! But the cookies were still too far away. Woolly stretched and stretched. She could almost touch the jar. Almost... but not quite.

"Hmm," said Woolly. "I need to think harder."

She climbed down from the stool. She walked around the hallway, thinking. Her hooves made soft *clip-clop* sounds on the floor.

Then Woolly saw it. A tall plant stand with a leafy green plant. It was just the right height! But how could she move it?

Woolly had an idea. She gently nudged the plant stand with her shoulder. *Bump, bump.* Slowly, carefully, she pushed it toward the kitchen. The plant swayed but didn't fall.

Next, Woolly moved her wooden stool. She placed it right next to the plant stand. Now she had two things to help her!

First, Woolly climbed onto the stool. Then, very carefully, she stepped onto the plant stand. She felt wobbly but brave. The leaves tickled her woolly sides.

"Almost there!" Woolly whispered to herself.

From her new height, Woolly could see into the cookie jar. The cookies looked even more delicious up close. She reached out with her hoof. Closer... closer...

*Clink!* Her hoof touched the jar. She carefully tipped it toward herself. One golden cookie tumbled out and landed softly on the counter.

"I did it!" Woolly cheered. But then she realized something. How would she get down safely with her cookie?

Woolly thought again. She gently pushed the cookie to the edge of the counter. Then she climbed down, step by step. When she reached the floor, she positioned herself right under the cookie.

"Here goes!" Woolly said. She gave the counter a little bump with her head. The cookie fell down and landed right on her soft, woolly back!

Woolly wiggled with joy. The cookie rolled into her mouth. It was sweet and crunchy and perfect. *Crunch, crunch, crunch.*

Just then, Farmer Mae walked into the hallway. She saw the stool and plant stand by the counter. She saw cookie crumbs on Woolly's face. Farmer Mae smiled.

"You clever little sheep," she said, patting Woolly's head. "You really wanted that cookie, didn't you?"

Woolly nodded happily. Her tail wagged.

"You know what?" said Farmer Mae. "Because you tried so hard and didn't give up, you can have another cookie. But next time, just ask me for help!"

Farmer Mae reached up and got another cookie for Woolly. This one had chocolate chips!

As Woolly munched her second cookie, she felt proud and happy. She had faced a big problem. She had tried many different solutions. She had never given up. And in the end, she had succeeded!

From that day on, whenever Woolly faced a challenge, she remembered her cookie adventure. She learned that with creativity, persistence, and careful thinking, she could solve almost any problem.

And the hallway? It became her favorite place to practice being brave.
