# Story story_1245

**The Library Friends**

In the quiet town library, a small red drum sat on a wooden shelf. His name was Danny. Every day, Danny watched children come and go. They picked up books about animals and adventures. But nobody ever picked up Danny.

"Tap, tap, tap," Danny played softly to himself. The sound echoed in the empty corner. Danny felt very lonely. He wished he had a friend to play with.

One sunny morning, a gentle cow named Bella walked into the library. She wore a purple bow around her neck. Bella loved to read stories about music and dancing. Her hooves clicked softly on the floor.

"Click, click, click," went Bella's hooves as she walked. Danny heard the pretty sound. It reminded him of music!

"Hello there," said Bella kindly. She noticed Danny sitting alone. "You look sad, little drum."

"I am sad," Danny replied. "Nobody wants to play music with me. I practice every day, but I'm always alone."

Bella's brown eyes sparkled with an idea. "I love music too! May I try playing with you?"

Danny's drumsticks wiggled with excitement. "Really? You want to make music together?"

"Yes!" said Bella. "Let me try." She tapped her front hoof gently on Danny's surface. "Tap, tap, tap!"

The sound was beautiful! Danny played along with his drumsticks. "Rat-a-tat-tat!" Together they made a wonderful rhythm.

But their music was too loud for the quiet library. "Shhhh!" said Mrs. Chen, the librarian. "Please play more softly."

Danny and Bella looked at each other sadly. How could they make music together without being too noisy?

"I have an idea," whispered Bella. "Follow me." She picked up Danny carefully in her mouth. They walked to a special room in the back of the library.

The room had soft carpet and thick walls. "This is the story room," said Bella. "We can practice here!"

Danny felt so happy. Finally, he had found a friend and a place to play! Bella set him down gently on a small table.

"Let's try again," said Bella. She tapped her hooves on the soft carpet. "Thump, thump, thump!" The sound was much quieter now.

Danny played his drumsticks softly too. "Pit-a-pat, pit-a-pat!" Their music blended together perfectly. It sounded like raindrops dancing on leaves.

Soon, other library visitors heard their gentle music. A family of mice peeked around the corner. Two rabbits hopped closer to listen. Even Mrs. Chen came to hear their beautiful song.

"How lovely!" said Mrs. Chen. "Would you play for our story time tomorrow?"

Danny and Bella looked at each other with big smiles. "Yes!" they said together.

The next day, children filled the story room. Danny and Bella played their special music while Mrs. Chen read a story about friendship. The children clapped and swayed to the rhythm.

"More! More!" called the children when the story ended.

Danny felt his heart fill with joy. He wasn't lonely anymore. He had found his best friend and discovered that sharing music made it even more beautiful.

After the children left, Bella and Danny sat together quietly. "Thank you for being my friend," said Danny. "I was so sad before I met you."

"Thank you too," said Bella. "Music is much more fun when you share it with someone special."

From that day on, Danny and Bella played together every afternoon in the library. They learned new rhythms and made up silly songs. Sometimes they played loud and bouncy. Sometimes they played soft and gentle.

The library became their special place. Children would come just to hear their music. Danny never felt lonely again because he had found something wonderful – a true friend who loved music just as much as he did.

And every evening, as the library grew quiet, Danny and Bella would play one last gentle song together, grateful for their beautiful friendship.

The end.
