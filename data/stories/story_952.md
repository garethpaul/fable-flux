# Story story_952

**Moo-Moo Learns to Share**

Moo-Moo the cow loved playing in the big, sunny playroom. The room smelled like crayons and had soft, squishy carpet under her hooves. Colorful toys filled every corner.

But Moo-Moo had a big problem. She didn't know how to share.

When Bunny wanted the red ball, Moo-Moo hugged it tight. "Mine!" she mooed loudly. When Piggy reached for the blocks, Moo-Moo pulled them away. "No! Mine!" she said again.

Soon, all the animals sat in the corner. They looked very sad. Bear had tears in his fuzzy eyes. Duck's orange beak pointed down. Even happy Monkey wasn't smiling.

Moo-Moo sat alone with all the toys. The red ball felt heavy in her hooves. The blocks didn't seem fun anymore. The playroom felt quiet and lonely.

"Why won't anyone play with me?" Moo-Moo wondered out loud.

Mrs. Hen walked into the playroom. Her feathers were white and fluffy. She wore tiny glasses on her beak. "What's wrong, little ones?" she asked gently.

"Moo-Moo won't share," whispered Bunny. "She keeps all the toys."

Mrs. Hen looked at Moo-Moo. "Sharing is caring, dear," she said kindly. "When we share, everyone has fun."

Moo-Moo's black and white spots wiggled as she thought. She wanted friends to play with. She wanted everyone to be happy.

"I'll try," Moo-Moo said softly.

First, Moo-Moo tried to share everything at once. She grabbed all the toys in her arms. Blocks fell everywhere with loud crashes. Crayons rolled across the floor. The puzzle pieces scattered like confetti.

"Here! Take them!" Moo-Moo mooed. But the mess was too big. The animals didn't know what to do. They looked confused and overwhelmed.

This didn't work at all.

Moo-Moo felt frustrated. Her tail swished back and forth. "Sharing is too hard!" she huffed.

Mrs. Hen pecked up some fallen blocks. "Try sharing one toy at a time," she suggested. "Start small, dear one."

Moo-Moo took a deep breath. She picked up the red ball. It was smooth and bouncy in her hooves. She walked slowly to Bunny.

"Would you like to play ball?" Moo-Moo asked quietly.

Bunny's ears perked up. "Really? You'll share?"

"Yes," Moo-Moo nodded. "We can roll it back and forth."

They sat on the soft carpet. Moo-Moo gently rolled the red ball to Bunny. Bunny giggled and rolled it back. The ball made a soft thump sound each time.

"This is fun!" Bunny said, clapping her paws.

Next, Moo-Moo invited Piggy to build with blocks. They made a tall, wobbly tower together. When it fell down, they both laughed. The blocks clattered happily on the floor.

Duck joined them with the crayons. They drew pictures on big white paper. Duck drew yellow suns. Moo-Moo drew green grass. The crayons smelled waxy and new.

Soon, Monkey and Bear came over too. Everyone was playing together. The playroom filled with giggles and happy sounds.

"Look!" said Bear, pointing to their colorful drawing. "We made something beautiful together!"

Moo-Moo felt warm and fuzzy inside. Her heart felt bigger than before. Playing with friends was much more fun than playing alone.

"Sharing makes everything better," Moo-Moo realized. "When I share, I get something even better back."

Mrs. Hen smiled proudly. "You learned something very important today, Moo-Moo."

As the sun set through the playroom windows, all the animals cleaned up together. They put toys in bins and crayons in boxes. Working together made the job quick and easy.

"Can we play again tomorrow?" asked Bunny.

"Yes!" mooed Moo-Moo happily. "And I'll remember to share!"

From that day on, Moo-Moo became the best sharing friend in the whole playroom. She learned that the more you give to others, the more joy comes back to you.

And they all played happily ever after.

The end.
