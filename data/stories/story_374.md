# Bubbles and Wheels

Bella the bike lived in the cozy garage next to the house. Her bright red frame sparkled in the morning sun. Her silver bell jingled softly in the breeze.

Every day, Bella loved rolling down sidewalks. She loved feeling the wind whoosh past her spokes. She loved hearing children laugh as they rode her.

But there was one thing Bella didn't love. Bath time.

"Time for your bath, Bella!" called Papa from the driveway. He rolled out the big blue bucket. He turned on the garden hose. Water sprayed everywhere, making tiny rainbows.

Bella's wheels felt heavy. Her handlebars drooped down low. "I don't want a bath," she whispered. "Water makes me all wet and drippy."

Papa filled the bucket with warm, soapy water. Bubbles danced on top like tiny clouds. "Come on, Bella. You'll feel so much better."

Bella rolled forward very, very slowly. Her tires squeaked on the concrete. "Baths are no fun," she sighed.

Papa dipped a soft yellow sponge in the bubbly water. He gently wiped Bella's red frame. The warm water felt... nice? Bella blinked in surprise.

"See? Not so bad," Papa said kindly.

As Papa cleaned her spokes, something magical happened. The soap bubbles began to shimmer and glow. They floated up, up, up into the sky. Each bubble caught the sunlight like a tiny rainbow ball.

"Oh my!" gasped Bella. "They're beautiful!"

More bubbles appeared as Papa scrubbed. Some bubbles were big as balloons. Some bubbles were tiny as pearls. They all danced around Bella like floating fairy lights.

One enormous bubble landed on Bella's bell. *Ding!* It popped with a gentle sound. Bella giggled, a sound like wind chimes.

"The bubbles are magical!" she laughed.

Papa smiled and made even more bubbles. He showed Bella how to blow them with her bell. *Ding, puff!* Bubbles flew everywhere, sparkling in the sunshine.

Bella's frame began to shine. Her silver parts gleamed like mirrors. Her red paint looked bright as strawberries. She felt fresh and clean and wonderful.

"I can see myself in my spokes!" Bella marveled. Everything looked crystal clear and shiny new.

Papa rinsed her with the gentle spray. The water felt like a warm summer rain. Bella stretched her handlebars wide, enjoying every drop.

"Now for the best part," Papa said mysteriously.

He brought out a fluffy blue towel. It was soft as a cloud. Papa dried Bella carefully, making sure every part was cozy and warm.

Then Papa polished her with special bike wax. Bella's frame gleamed like a ruby. Her spokes sparkled like diamonds. She looked absolutely magnificent.

"I feel like a brand new bike!" Bella exclaimed.

Papa stepped back to admire his work. "You look beautiful, Bella. Ready for tomorrow's adventure?"

Bella rang her bell happily. *Ding-a-ling!* The sound was clearer and prettier than ever before.

That evening, as stars began to twinkle, Bella thought about her magical bath. She remembered the dancing bubbles. She remembered feeling fresh and sparkly clean.

"Papa," she called softly. "Will there be more magic bubbles tomorrow?"

Papa chuckled from the kitchen window. "Every bath can be magical, Bella. When we take care of the things we love."

Bella's spokes caught the moonlight, glittering like silver stars. She felt proud and beautiful and loved.

As she drifted off to sleep, Bella dreamed of tomorrow. She dreamed of rolling down sunny sidewalks with her shiny red frame. She dreamed of children smiling at her sparkly spokes.

But most of all, she dreamed of bath time. She couldn't wait to see what magical bubbles would dance around her tomorrow.

"Bath time is the best time," Bella whispered happily.

Her bell gave one last gentle *ding* as she fell asleep, already excited for another day of adventures and another magical bath.

The end.
