# Story story_1033

**Finn's Messy Hallway**

Finn the little fish swam down the long hallway. His orange fins sparkled in the morning light. The hallway was full of water, just perfect for swimming.

But something was wrong. Toys were everywhere! Building blocks floated near the ceiling. Toy cars sat on the sandy bottom. Rubber balls bobbed up and down in the water.

"Oh no!" said Finn. "What a big mess!"

Finn's mom swam up behind him. "The hallway needs to be clean," she said gently. "Can you help put the toys away?"

Finn looked at all the toys. There were so many! Red blocks, blue cars, yellow balls, and green puzzle pieces. They were scattered from one end of the hallway to the other.

"I don't want to," said Finn. "It's too much work."

But Mom had already swum away to make breakfast. Finn was alone with the messy hallway.

He tried to swim around the toys. A big block bumped his nose. "Ouch!" he said. A toy car rolled into his tail. The rubber balls kept getting in his way.

Finn couldn't swim properly. The hallway was too crowded.

"Maybe I should try," thought Finn.

**First Try**

Finn had an idea. He would push all the toys to one corner. That would be fast and easy!

He pushed a red block with his nose. It moved a little bit. He pushed harder. The block moved more.

But then something bad happened. When Finn pushed one toy, three more toys floated into its place. The yellow balls rolled everywhere. The blue cars scattered across the sandy bottom.

"This isn't working!" said Finn. He felt frustrated. His fins were tired from all the pushing.

The hallway looked even messier than before. Toys swirled around in the water. Some bumped into the walls. Others sank to the bottom in a big pile.

Finn sat on the sandy floor. He put his fins over his eyes. "I made it worse," he said sadly.

**Second Try**

Finn's big sister Coral swam by. She saw him looking upset.

"What's wrong, little brother?" she asked.

"The toys are everywhere," said Finn. "I tried to clean up, but I made a bigger mess."

Coral smiled. "I have an idea. Let's sort the toys first."

"Sort them?" asked Finn.

"Yes! Put all the same toys together. Blocks with blocks. Cars with cars. Balls with balls."

This sounded like a good plan. Finn felt hopeful again.

First, they gathered all the red blocks. Finn carried them in his fins. Coral pushed them with her tail. They put all the blocks in the toy box marked "Blocks."

Next, they collected the blue cars. The cars were heavy, but working together made it easier. They rolled them to the "Cars" box.

Then came the yellow balls. These were tricky because they kept floating away. But Finn and Coral chased them down. They caught every single ball.

The green puzzle pieces were last. They were tiny and hard to see. Finn used his good eyes to spot them. Coral helped him pick them up.

**Success!**

Soon, the hallway was clean! The water was clear and sparkly. Finn could swim from one end to the other without bumping into anything.

"We did it!" said Finn happily. He did a little flip in the water.

"You did most of the work," said Coral. "I just helped you get started."

Finn felt proud. His orange fins glowed with happiness. The hallway looked beautiful and neat.

Mom swam back and saw the clean hallway. "Wonderful job, Finn!" she said. "You didn't give up."

"It was hard at first," said Finn. "But sorting made it easier. And asking for help was smart."

"That's right," said Mom. "Big jobs are easier when you break them into small pieces."

Finn smiled. He had learned something important. When things seem too hard, try a different way. And it's okay to ask for help.

Now Finn could swim freely in his clean hallway. The water felt smooth and cool. Everything was in its right place.

"Tomorrow I'll put my toys away right after playing," said Finn.

And he did.

The end.
