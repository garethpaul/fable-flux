# Story story_601

**Penny's Red Ball**

Penny the duck loved bath time. She splashed in the warm water. She played with her toys. But her favorite toy was special. It was a bright red ball.

The red ball bounced high. It floated on the water. It made Penny giggle when she pushed it around. Every day, Penny played with her red ball in the big white bathtub.

One morning, Penny waddled into the bathroom. She looked around for her red ball. Where was it? She checked the soap dish. Not there. She looked behind the toilet. Not there either.

"Oh no!" said Penny. "Where is my red ball?"

Penny felt sad. Her favorite toy was gone. The bathroom felt empty without it. She sat on the cold tile floor. A tear rolled down her yellow cheek.

"I must find my red ball," said Penny. She stood up tall. "I won't give up!"

Penny had an idea. Maybe the ball rolled under something. She got down low. She looked under the sink. Dust bunnies tickled her nose. She sneezed loudly. "Achoo!" But no red ball.

She crawled to the bathtub. She peeked underneath. It was dark and dusty. She saw a old washcloth. She saw a bottle cap. But no red ball anywhere.

Penny felt worried. Her first plan didn't work. But she remembered what Mama always said. "Try again, little duck. Good things come to those who keep trying."

Penny thought harder. Where else could the ball be? She looked up at the ceiling. Could it be up high somewhere?

Then Penny noticed something. The bathroom window was open. A gentle breeze blew in. The white curtains moved back and forth. They danced in the wind.

"Maybe my ball went outside," thought Penny.

Penny climbed onto the toilet seat. She was still too short. She needed to get higher. She saw a small stool by the sink. Mama used it to reach high shelves.

Carefully, Penny pushed the stool over. She climbed up step by step. Now she could see out the window. She looked down at the garden below.

There it was! Her bright red ball sat in the flower bed. It was nestled between yellow daisies and purple petunias. The flowers smelled sweet in the morning air.

"My ball!" cried Penny happily.

But how could she get it? The window was too high to jump from. Penny thought and thought. Then she remembered something important. She could ask for help.

"Mama! Papa!" called Penny loudly.

Mama Duck hurried into the bathroom. "What's wrong, sweetie?"

"My red ball is outside," said Penny. She pointed out the window. "It's in the flowers. Can you help me get it?"

Mama smiled. "Of course I can help you." She gave Penny a warm hug. "You did a good job looking for it yourself first."

Together, they went outside. The grass felt cool under Penny's webbed feet. The morning sun warmed her feathers. Mama reached into the flower bed and picked up the red ball.

"Here you go," said Mama. She handed the ball to Penny.

Penny hugged her red ball tight. It felt smooth and perfect in her wings. "Thank you, Mama!"

"You're welcome," said Mama. "I'm proud of you for not giving up. You kept trying until you found your ball."

Back in the bathroom, Penny filled the tub with warm water. She dropped her red ball in with a splash. It bobbed up and down on the surface. The water rippled around it.

Penny climbed into the tub. The warm water felt wonderful. She pushed her red ball around and around. It made her smile big.

"I found you," she whispered to her ball. "I never stopped looking."

From that day on, Penny always checked that the window was closed before bath time. She learned that problems can be solved. Sometimes you need to try different ways. Sometimes you need to ask for help. But you should never give up.

And Penny and her red ball lived happily ever after.

The end.
