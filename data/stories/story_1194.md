# Story story_1194

Anna sat on the big red swing. Her feet barely touched the ground. The playground was sunny and warm. But Anna felt sad inside.

Other children played together everywhere. They laughed and ran around. Anna watched them with big, lonely eyes. She pushed herself gently on the swing.

The swing made a soft creaking sound. Back and forth, back and forth. Anna's brown hair moved in the breeze. She wished she had a friend to play with.

Near the slide stood a boy with curly hair. His name was Jack. He had kind eyes and a shy smile. Jack wanted to play too. But he felt nervous about asking.

Jack watched Anna swing by herself. She looked sad and alone. He took a deep breath. His heart beat fast. Jack walked slowly toward the swings.

"Hi," said Jack quietly. "Can I swing next to you?"

Anna looked up with surprise. Her eyes got brighter. "Yes!" she said with a big smile. "I would like that very much."

Jack climbed onto the blue swing. It was right next to Anna's red one. He started swinging gently. The chains made happy jingling sounds.

"I'm Anna," she said. "What's your name?"

"I'm Jack," he replied. "I like your pretty dress. It's as yellow as sunshine."

Anna giggled softly. "Thank you! I like your green shirt. It's like grass in summer."

They swung higher and higher together. The wind felt cool on their faces. Their hair flew behind them like flags. Both children started laughing with joy.

"Let's count how high we go!" said Anna.

"One, two, three!" they counted together. Their voices mixed in the air. They swung so high their feet pointed at the clouds.

After swinging, Jack had an idea. "Want to play on the seesaw?" he asked.

"Yes!" Anna jumped off her swing. They ran to the yellow seesaw together. Their feet made soft sounds on the rubber ground.

Anna sat on one end. Jack sat on the other end. Up and down they went. When Anna went up, she could see the whole playground. When Jack went up, he felt like he was flying.

"This is so much fun!" laughed Anna. Her cheeks were pink and happy.

"Much better than playing alone," agreed Jack. He grinned from ear to ear.

Next they tried the monkey bars. Jack went first. His hands gripped the cold metal bars. He moved carefully from bar to bar. Anna cheered for him loudly.

"Go, Jack, go!" she shouted. "You're doing great!"

Then Anna tried. Her small hands held tight. Jack stood below her. He encouraged her with kind words.

"You can do it, Anna!" he called. "I believe in you!"

Anna made it across all the bars. She felt so proud and strong. Jack clapped his hands for her.

They played hide and seek around the big oak tree. Its thick trunk had rough, brown bark. Green leaves made cool shadows everywhere. Anna hid behind the tree first. Jack found her quickly.

"Found you!" he giggled. Anna pretended to be surprised. They both laughed together.

When Jack hid, Anna looked everywhere. She found him crouched behind a bench. His curly hair peeked out like a fluffy cloud.

The sun started getting lower in the sky. Orange and pink colors painted the clouds. But Anna and Jack didn't want to stop playing.

"Will you be my friend?" asked Anna. She looked at Jack with hopeful eyes.

"Yes!" said Jack happily. "I would love to be your friend. You're really nice and fun."

They sat together on the warm grass. Tiny daisies grew all around them. The flowers smelled sweet and fresh.

"Friends forever?" asked Anna.

"Friends forever," promised Jack.

They made plans to play again tomorrow. Both children felt warm and happy inside. The playground didn't seem lonely anymore.

Anna's mom called from across the park. It was time to go home. Anna and Jack waved goodbye to each other.

"See you tomorrow, friend!" called Anna.

"See you tomorrow!" Jack called back.

Anna skipped all the way to her mom. She couldn't wait to tell her about Jack. Today started lonely but ended with joy. She had found a wonderful new friend.

The playground would never feel empty again.

The end.
