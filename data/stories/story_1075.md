# Story story_1075

Anna sat in the big sandbox. The warm sand felt soft between her fingers. She looked around the playground. Other children played together everywhere.

Two girls shared the swings. Three boys kicked a soccer ball. A group built a fort near the slide. Anna sighed. She had no one to play with.

The sand was perfect for building. Anna loved making castles and roads. But playing alone felt lonely. She watched the other children laugh and talk. Their voices sounded happy and fun.

Anna picked up her red bucket. She filled it with sand. The grains slipped through her fingers like tiny waterfalls. Maybe someone would want to help her build.

She started making a small castle. Anna patted the sand carefully. She made walls and towers. The castle looked pretty good. But still, no one came over.

Anna had an idea. She would build something amazing. Something so cool that other kids would notice. Then they would want to play with her.

She dumped out her bucket. Anna started digging a huge hole. Sand flew everywhere. Her hands got dirty and dusty. The hole grew bigger and bigger.

"Look at my giant hole!" Anna called out. She hoped someone would hear her. A few children glanced over. But they went back to their own games. Anna's shoulders drooped. Her first plan didn't work.

Anna sat in her big hole. The sand felt cool against her legs. She felt sad and disappointed. Maybe she would never find someone to play with.

Then Anna heard crying nearby. A little boy sat alone on a bench. His face looked upset. Tears rolled down his cheeks.

Anna climbed out of her hole. She walked over to the boy. Her sneakers crunched on the playground gravel.

"Hi," Anna said gently. "I'm Anna. What's your name?"

"I'm Ben," the boy sniffled. "I lost my toy truck. It was my favorite red truck."

Anna's eyes lit up. "I can help you find it! I'm really good at looking for things."

Ben wiped his nose. "You would help me?"

"Of course!" Anna smiled. "Let's search together."

They walked around the playground. Anna checked under the slide. Ben looked behind the swings. They searched near the monkey bars. No truck anywhere.

Then Anna remembered something. "Wait! Let's check my sandbox hole. Maybe your truck rolled in there."

They hurried to the sandbox. Anna pointed to her big hole. "I dug this really deep hole. Let's look inside."

Ben peered down. His face broke into a huge smile. "There it is! My red truck!"

The truck sat at the bottom of Anna's hole. Ben reached down and grabbed it. He hugged the toy tight.

"Thank you so much, Anna!" Ben said. "You saved my truck!"

Anna felt warm and happy inside. "You're welcome! Want to play in the sandbox with me?"

"Yes!" Ben jumped up and down. "Can we build something together?"

Anna grinned. "I know how to make awesome castles. You can help me!"

They sat in the soft sand. Anna showed Ben how to pack the bucket tight. Ben learned to make strong walls. Together they built the most amazing castle ever.

Other children started watching. Soon, two more kids asked to join. Then three more came over. The sandbox filled with new friends.

"This is the best castle!" said a girl named Maya.

"Anna taught us how to build it," Ben explained proudly.

Anna felt so happy. Her hole had seemed like a failure. But it helped her find Ben's truck. And helping Ben led to finding friends.

The sun shone warm on their faces. Sand covered their hands and clothes. But nobody cared. They were having too much fun.

Anna learned something important that day. Sometimes the best way to make friends is to help others. When you're kind and caring, good things happen.

The children played until their parents called them home. Anna waved goodbye to all her new friends. Tomorrow, she couldn't wait to play in the sandbox again.

The end.
