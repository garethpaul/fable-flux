# Story story_824

**Bella's Colorful Day**

"Art time!" called Miss Sarah. She clapped her hands together. All the children ran to the big table. Bright papers and crayons waited for them.

Bella the little blue jay stayed on her perch. She tucked her head under her wing. "I don't want to," she chirped softly.

Miss Sarah smiled at Bella. "Come join us, sweet bird. We're making rainbow pictures today."

Bella peeked out with one black eye. The other children were already drawing. Red crayon marks danced across white paper. Yellow circles became sunny suns. Green lines grew into tall trees.

"My wings are too small," Bella said. "I can't hold crayons like everyone else."

Tommy looked up from his picture. "That's okay, Bella. You can try anyway."

Bella flew down slowly. She landed near a purple crayon. It was bigger than her tiny wing. She tried to pick it up. The crayon rolled away across the table.

"See?" Bella sighed. "I told you so."

Miss Sarah had an idea. She brought over a small cup of water. Then she squeezed drops of blue paint into it. The water turned the prettiest sky blue.

"Dip your wing tip in here," Miss Sarah said gently.

Bella looked curious. She touched her wing to the blue water. Cool drops clung to her feathers. They felt nice and wet.

"Now touch the paper," Miss Sarah suggested.

Bella pressed her wing against the white paper. Something magical happened! A beautiful blue feather print appeared. It looked like a tiny cloud.

"Wow!" gasped Emma. "That's so pretty!"

Bella's eyes grew wide. She dipped her wing again. This time she made three more prints. They looked like blue flowers dancing on the page.

Miss Sarah brought more colors. Red paint made her wing prints look like roses. Yellow paint created sunshine spots. Green paint became little leaves.

"I'm making art!" Bella sang happily. She hopped from color to color. Her wing prints filled the whole paper. Each print was different and special.

The other children watched in wonder. "Can you teach us?" asked Sophie.

Bella felt proud and excited. She showed Sophie how to dip just the tip. "Not too much paint," Bella chirped. "Just a little bit."

Soon everyone was making wing prints with their fingers. The table became a rainbow of colors. Paint drops sparkled like tiny jewels. The room smelled like fresh art supplies.

Bella's paper was covered in beautiful prints. Blue ones looked like sky. Red ones looked like flowers. Yellow ones looked like happy sunshine. All together, they made the most wonderful picture.

"This is my best art ever!" Bella declared. She did a little dance on the table. Paint drops flew from her wings like colorful rain.

Everyone laughed and clapped. Miss Sarah hung Bella's picture on the special wall. It looked beautiful next to all the other artwork.

"I didn't know I could make art," Bella said to Tommy. "My wings work perfectly for painting."

Tommy smiled. "Everyone can make art. We just do it differently."

As art time ended, the children cleaned up. Bella helped by flying the paintbrushes to the sink. Her wings still had tiny drops of paint. They made her look extra colorful and special.

"Will we have art time tomorrow?" Bella asked hopefully.

Miss Sarah nodded. "Every day, sweet bird. What would you like to try next?"

Bella thought carefully. "Maybe I can make prints with my feet too! Or draw with my beak!"

The other children got excited. "We want to try that too!" they called out.

That night, Bella looked at her beautiful picture. It hung on the wall where everyone could see it. She felt warm and happy inside.

"Tomorrow will be another wonderful art day," she whispered to herself. Then she tucked her colorful wings close and fell asleep. She dreamed of rainbows and paint drops and all the beautiful things she would create.

Art time had become her favorite time of all.

The end.
