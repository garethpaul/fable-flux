# Story story_667

**Luna the Lion Learns to Tie**

Luna the lion lived in the sunny woods. She had a golden mane that sparkled in the light. Luna loved to run and play with her friends.

One morning, Luna looked down at her feet. She wore bright red shoes with long white laces. The laces were all tangled up. They looked like messy spaghetti.

"Oh no!" said Luna. "My laces are so loose. I might trip and fall."

Luna tried to walk carefully. But the laces dragged on the ground. They caught on sticks and leaves. Luna stumbled over a small rock.

"I need to tie my shoes," Luna said. "But I don't know how."

She sat on a smooth log. Luna picked up one lace in each paw. She tried to remember what her mama had shown her. She made two loops with the laces.

"Around and around," Luna whispered. She twisted the loops together. But they came apart right away. The laces fell loose again.

Luna felt frustrated. Her ears drooped down. "This is too hard," she sighed.

Just then, her friend Benny the bear walked by. Benny's brown shoes were tied perfectly. His laces made neat little bows.

"Hi Luna!" said Benny. "Why do you look sad?"

"I can't tie my shoes," Luna said. "I tried really hard. But the laces won't stay."

Benny smiled kindly. "I can help you," he said. "My mama taught me a song. It makes tying shoes easy."

Benny sat next to Luna on the log. The morning sun felt warm on their fur. Birds chirped softly in the trees above.

"First, make an X with your laces," Benny said. He showed Luna with his own shoes. "Then pull one lace under and through."

Luna watched carefully. She made an X with her red laces. She pulled the right lace under the left one. Then she pulled it through the hole.

"Good job!" said Benny. "Now pull both ends tight."

Luna pulled gently. The laces made a neat knot. "I did it!" she said. Her tail swished with excitement.

"Now comes the fun part," said Benny. "Make one loop. This is the bunny ear."

Luna made a loop with her right lace. It looked just like a floppy bunny ear. She giggled at the silly shape.

"Wrap the other lace around the bunny ear," Benny sang. "Then push it through the hole. Pull both loops tight!"

Luna followed each step slowly. She wrapped the left lace around her bunny ear. She found the little hole behind it. She pushed the lace through carefully.

"Now pull!" said Benny.

Luna pulled both loops at the same time. The laces formed a perfect bow. It looked like a pretty butterfly sitting on her shoe.

"I did it! I really did it!" Luna roared with joy. Her voice echoed through the woods. Squirrels poked their heads out to see what was happening.

Luna tied her other shoe all by herself. She sang Benny's song quietly. Both shoes now had beautiful bows.

"Thank you, Benny," said Luna. "You're such a good friend."

"You did the hard work," said Benny. "You didn't give up. That's what made you succeed."

Luna stood up and took a few steps. Her shoes felt snug and comfortable. The laces stayed tied perfectly. No more tripping or stumbling.

Luna and Benny ran through the woods together. They jumped over fallen logs. They splashed through a babbling creek. Luna's shoes stayed tied the whole time.

That evening, Luna showed her mama her tied shoes. Mama lion's eyes sparkled with pride.

"I'm so proud of you," Mama said. "You kept trying even when it was hard."

Luna smiled big. "Benny helped me learn. And I practiced until I got it right."

From that day on, Luna tied her shoes every morning. She sang Benny's song and made perfect bows. She even taught other young animals in the woods.

Luna learned that asking for help was brave. She learned that practice makes things easier. Most importantly, she learned to never give up on herself.

The end.
