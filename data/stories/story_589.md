# Story story_589

**Freddy's Messy Basement**

Freddy the frog lived in a cozy basement. The walls were cool and damp. Perfect for a little green frog! Freddy loved his underground home.

But Freddy had one big problem. His basement was very, very messy. Toy cars were scattered everywhere. Building blocks covered the floor. Puzzle pieces hid under his lily pad bed.

"Oh no," said Freddy one morning. He hopped around his room. *Squish, squash* went his webbed feet on the wet floor. Every step landed on a toy.

His mom called from upstairs. "Freddy! Time to clean your room!"

Freddy's big eyes looked around. There were so many toys! Red trucks, blue balls, yellow blocks. They were everywhere he looked.

"I don't want to clean up," Freddy said sadly. "There are too many toys. It will take forever!"

He sat on his favorite rock and sighed. The basement felt cold and gloomy. Freddy wished the mess would just disappear.

Then Freddy had an idea. "I know!" he said. "I'll hide all the toys!"

Freddy grabbed his biggest blanket. It was soft and green like his skin. He threw it over all the toys in one corner.

"Perfect!" said Freddy. The toys were hidden under the bumpy blanket.

But when Freddy hopped across his room, he tripped. *CRASH!* The blanket fell off. All the toys tumbled out again.

"That didn't work," said Freddy. He felt frustrated. His little heart beat fast.

Freddy tried again. This time he pushed all the toys under his bed. He used his strong frog legs to shove them underneath.

"There!" said Freddy, wiping his forehead. "All clean!"

But the toys didn't fit. They stuck out everywhere. Red wheels poked out here. Blue blocks stuck out there. It looked even messier than before.

Freddy felt like crying. "This is too hard," he said. His voice was small and sad.

Just then, his big sister Lily hopped down the stairs. She was older and wiser than Freddy.

"Why do you look so sad?" asked Lily. Her voice was kind and gentle.

"I can't clean my room," said Freddy. "There are too many toys. I tried hiding them. I tried pushing them away. Nothing works!"

Lily looked around the messy basement. Then she smiled at her little brother.

"Freddy," said Lily, "you're trying to clean everything at once. That's too much for anyone!"

"What should I do?" asked Freddy.

"Let's clean one type of toy at a time," said Lily. "Start small. Pick just the red cars first."

Freddy's eyes lit up. "Just the cars?"

"Just the cars," said Lily.

Freddy hopped around his room. He found every red car. There were five of them. He put them in their special box.

"That was easy!" said Freddy. He felt proud and happy.

"Now try the blue balls," said Lily.

Freddy found all the blue balls. There were three of them. Into their basket they went.

"This is fun!" said Freddy. His webbed feet did a little dance.

One by one, Freddy cleaned up each type of toy. Yellow blocks went on the shelf. Green puzzles went in their drawer. Purple crayons went in their cup.

Soon, Freddy's basement was clean and tidy. The floor was clear. Everything had its place. The room felt bright and cheerful.

"I did it!" said Freddy. He jumped up and down with joy. "I cleaned my whole room!"

"You sure did," said Lily. "You learned something important today."

"What's that?" asked Freddy.

"Big jobs are easier when you break them into small pieces," said Lily.

Freddy nodded. "And it's okay to ask for help!"

"That's right," said Lily. She gave Freddy a big hug.

From that day on, Freddy kept his basement tidy. When toys got messy, he remembered Lily's advice. One type of toy at a time. Small steps make big jobs easy.

Freddy learned that cleaning up could actually be fun. Especially when you have a good plan and a helpful sister!

The end.
