# Story story_551

**Grandpa's Magic Snack Time**

Every day at three o'clock, the big clock chimed. "Ding dong, ding dong!" it sang. Lily clapped her hands. "Snack time, Grandpa!" she called.

Grandpa sat in his big blue chair. He looked at his book. He looked at the clock. "Oh my," he sighed softly. "I suppose it's time."

Grandpa felt tired today. His back was sore. His feet hurt a little. Making snacks seemed like so much work. "Maybe just crackers," he mumbled.

But Lily bounced on her toes. Her eyes sparkled bright. "Can we make something special, Grandpa? Please?" she asked sweetly.

Grandpa looked at her happy face. He couldn't say no. "Alright, little one. Let's see what we can find."

They walked to the kitchen together. Lily's small hand held Grandpa's big one. The kitchen smelled like warm bread. Sunlight danced through the window.

Grandpa opened the cupboard slowly. "Hmm," he said. "We have apples. We have peanut butter. We have crackers too."

"Ooh!" Lily squeaked. "Can we make apple smiles?"

Grandpa smiled a little. "Apple smiles? What are those?"

Lily giggled. "You cut the apple. Then you put peanut butter on it. Then you add tiny marshmallows for teeth!"

Something wonderful happened then. Grandpa's eyes began to twinkle. "That sounds magical," he said. His voice grew stronger.

They washed their hands together. The water felt cool and fresh. Grandpa found his special knife. It had a red handle that Lily loved.

"First, we slice the apple," Grandpa said. His hands moved carefully. The knife made soft crunching sounds. Sweet apple smell filled the air.

Lily watched with wide eyes. "It looks like a moon!" she said. The apple slice was pale and curved.

"Now the peanut butter," Grandpa said. He spread it thick and smooth. The golden color looked warm and yummy.

"My turn!" Lily said. She picked up tiny white marshmallows. Her little fingers placed them carefully. One, two, three, four teeth!

"Look, Grandpa! It's smiling at us!" Lily laughed. The apple slice really did look happy.

Grandpa laughed too. His laugh was deep and warm. "It is smiling! Let's make more friends for it."

They made five apple smiles together. Each one looked different. Some had big grins. Some had shy smiles. One had a crooked tooth that made them both giggle.

"This one looks like you, Grandpa," Lily said. She pointed to an apple with lots of teeth.

"And this one looks like you," Grandpa said. He showed her one with a big happy grin.

They sat at the little round table. The apple smiles looked up at them. "Thank you for the snack," Lily whispered to her apple.

Grandpa whispered to his apple too. "Thank you for making me smile."

The apples tasted crisp and sweet. The peanut butter was creamy and rich. The marshmallows were soft and fluffy. Everything tasted better when they made it together.

"Grandpa," Lily said with her mouth full. "This is the best snack ever."

Grandpa felt warm inside. His back didn't hurt anymore. His feet felt fine. "You know what, Lily? You're right."

"Can we make something new tomorrow?" Lily asked. Her eyes were bright with hope.

Grandpa's heart felt light and happy. "Of course we can. What should we make?"

"Banana boats!" Lily said. "With peanut butter sails!"

Grandpa chuckled. "That sounds perfect."

As they cleaned up together, Grandpa felt excited. Tomorrow's snack time would be another adventure. Making food with Lily wasn't work at all. It was pure magic.

The kitchen sparkled clean. The dishes were put away. But the best part stayed with them. The joy of creating something special together.

"Same time tomorrow?" Grandpa asked.

"Same time tomorrow," Lily agreed.

And Grandpa couldn't wait.

The end.
