# Story story_664

Ben loved playing in his sandbox. The warm sand felt soft between his fingers. He built tall castles and deep tunnels every day.

But Ben had a big problem. He did not like vegetables. Green beans looked yucky. Carrots tasted too crunchy. Broccoli smelled funny. Every dinner time was hard.

"Ben, please eat your peas," said Mom. The little green balls sat on his plate. They looked like tiny marbles.

"No thank you," Ben said. He pushed the peas around with his fork. They rolled across his plate.

Mom looked sad. "Vegetables help you grow big and strong," she said.

Ben frowned. He wanted to be big and strong. But vegetables tasted so bad. He wished there was another way.

The next morning, Ben played in his sandbox. He dug deep holes with his red shovel. The sand was cool and damp underneath.

"I have an idea!" Ben said out loud. He ran to the kitchen. Mom was making lunch.

"Mom, can I plant vegetables in my sandbox?" Ben asked. His eyes were bright with excitement.

Mom smiled. "That sounds fun, Ben. But sandbox sand won't help vegetables grow."

Ben's face fell. His first idea didn't work. He felt disappointed. But he didn't give up.

Ben thought hard while he ate his sandwich. The peanut butter was creamy and sweet. Maybe there was another way.

After lunch, Ben had a new idea. He ran outside to his sandbox again. This time, he brought his toy vegetables from his play kitchen.

Ben planted the plastic carrots in the sand. He buried the fake broccoli too. The rubber corn went in a corner.

"Now I'm a vegetable farmer!" Ben said proudly. He watered his pretend garden with his watering can.

But the toy vegetables didn't taste any better. They still weren't real food. Ben sighed. His second idea didn't work either.

Ben sat in his sandbox feeling sad. The afternoon sun warmed his back. He really wanted to like vegetables. But how?

Just then, his neighbor Mrs. Rose came over. She had dirt on her hands and a big smile.

"Hello, Ben!" she said. "I'm working in my vegetable garden. Would you like to help?"

Ben looked curious. "What do you do in a vegetable garden?"

"Come see!" Mrs. Rose said kindly.

Ben followed her to the backyard next door. Rows of green plants grew in the dark soil. Red tomatoes hung like Christmas ornaments. Orange carrots peeked out of the ground.

"Wow!" Ben said. His eyes grew wide. The garden smelled fresh and earthy.

Mrs. Rose handed Ben a small carrot. "I just picked this. Would you like to try it?"

The carrot was still warm from the sun. It had bits of soil on it. Ben brushed it clean.

"It's so orange," Ben said. The carrot was brighter than his toy ones.

Ben took a tiny bite. The carrot was sweet and crunchy. It tasted like sunshine. It was nothing like the carrots at dinner!

"This is good!" Ben said, surprised. He took another bite.

Mrs. Rose laughed. "Fresh vegetables taste the best. They're full of sunshine and rain."

Ben helped Mrs. Rose pick more vegetables. He tried a cherry tomato. It burst with flavor in his mouth. The lettuce leaves were crisp and fresh.

That evening, Ben ran home with a basket of vegetables. "Mom! Mom! Look what I picked!"

At dinner, Ben ate every vegetable on his plate. The fresh carrots were sweet. The tomatoes were juicy. Even the lettuce tasted good.

"These are delicious!" Ben said happily.

Mom smiled big. "I'm so proud of you, Ben!"

Ben learned something important that day. Sometimes the first idea doesn't work. Sometimes the second idea doesn't work either. But if you keep trying, you might find the perfect solution.

From then on, Ben helped Mrs. Rose in her garden every week. He learned to love vegetables. And he learned that trying new things can lead to wonderful surprises.

Ben still loved his sandbox. But now he loved fresh vegetables too. The best part was sharing them with his family every night.

The end.
