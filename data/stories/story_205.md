# Penny Pig and the Big Storm

On Farmer Mae's cozy farm, a little pink pig named Penny lived in the happiest barn. Penny loved sunny days best. She liked rolling in cool mud. She enjoyed eating sweet corn. Most of all, she loved playing with her friends.

One gray morning, Penny woke up worried. Dark clouds filled the sky. The wind made whooshing sounds. "A big storm is coming!" said Penny. Her curly tail wiggled with worry.

Thunder rumbled like a hungry giant. "BOOM! BOOM!" Lightning flashed bright white. Penny's little pink ears trembled. "I'm scared," she whispered softly.

Penny ran to her friend Rosie. Rosie was a red hen. "The storm sounds so loud!" said Penny. "What if it's too scary?"

"Let's find somewhere safe," clucked Rosie kindly. They looked around the big barn. But everywhere seemed too open. Too noisy.

First, Penny tried hiding under hay. The golden hay felt soft and warm. But she could still hear thunder. "CRACK!" went the lightning. Penny's heart beat fast like tiny drums.

"This isn't working," said Penny sadly. She peeked out from the hay. Rain started pattering on the roof. "Pitter-patter, pitter-patter." The sound grew louder and louder.

Next, Penny tried the corner. She curled up very small. She closed her eyes tight. But the storm sounds seemed even bigger. Wind howled through the barn walls. "WHOOOOSH!"

"I need a better plan," said Penny. She thought very hard. Her little pig brain worked and worked.

Then Penny had an idea! "What if I make the barn cozier?" She gathered soft blankets from Farmer Mae. She found fluffy pillows too. One by one, she carried them carefully.

Penny built a special fort. She used wooden boxes for walls. She draped blankets over the top. Inside, everything felt warm and snug. "This is much better!" said Penny.

But she still felt a little worried. The storm sounds continued outside. Rain drummed harder on the roof. "What else can I do?" wondered Penny.

Penny remembered something important. When she felt scared before, singing helped. She took a deep breath. In her sweetest voice, she sang:

"Rain, rain, go away,
Come again another day.
Little Penny wants to play,
Rain, rain, go away!"

Her voice sounded beautiful inside the fort. The walls made it echo nicely. Soon, Rosie joined in singing. Then Charlie the cow sang too. Even old Buster the dog howled along.

All the farm animals gathered together. They sang song after song. They told funny stories. They shared warm carrots and apples. The cozy fort felt full of friendship.

Slowly, Penny forgot about being scared. The thunder didn't sound so frightening. The lightning seemed less bright. With her friends nearby, everything felt safe.

"You know what?" said Penny happily. "The storm isn't so bad anymore. We made it better together!"

The animals kept singing and laughing. Outside, rain continued falling. But inside their special fort, everything felt perfect. Warm and dry and full of love.

After a while, the storm grew quieter. Sunbeams peeked through the clouds. The rain became gentle drops. "Look!" said Penny excitedly. "The storm is ending!"

When they stepped outside, everything sparkled. Raindrops hung like tiny diamonds. Puddles reflected the blue sky. The air smelled fresh and clean. "It's beautiful!" gasped Penny.

Farmer Mae found them playing outside. "You brave animals!" she said proudly. "You took such good care of each other during the storm."

Penny felt very proud indeed. She had been scared at first. But she didn't give up. She tried different solutions. She asked friends for help. Most importantly, she made the scary time better.

"I learned something today," said Penny to Rosie. "When problems seem too big, I can find ways to make them smaller. And friends always help everything feel better."

From that day on, Penny wasn't afraid of storms. She knew exactly what to do. Build a cozy fort. Gather good friends. Sing happy songs. Turn scary times into special times.

And whenever storm clouds gathered, all the farm animals would smile. Because they knew Penny Pig had the best ideas for staying brave and happy together.

The end.
