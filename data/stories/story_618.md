# Story story_618

**Woolly's New Friend**

Little Emma sat on the soft carpet. Her blocks were scattered around her. She built a tall tower with red and blue pieces. But something felt wrong. The living room was very quiet. Emma looked around the empty space. She felt lonely.

"I wish I had someone to play with," Emma said softly.

Outside the big window, a fluffy white sheep named Woolly was eating grass. Woolly had wandered away from the farm next door. The sheep saw Emma through the glass. Emma looked sad and alone. Woolly's heart felt warm and caring.

Woolly walked to the front door. The door was open just a crack. The curious sheep pushed it with her nose. The door opened wider. Woolly stepped inside on her soft hooves.

Emma heard gentle footsteps. She turned around and gasped. A real sheep stood in her living room! Woolly had the fluffiest white wool Emma had ever seen. The sheep's dark eyes sparkled with kindness.

"Hello there," Emma whispered. She was surprised but not scared.

Woolly made a soft "baa" sound. The sheep walked closer to Emma. Her wool looked like fluffy clouds. Emma reached out her small hand. She touched Woolly's side. The wool felt warm and bumpy.

"You're so soft!" Emma giggled.

Woolly sniffed Emma's blocks with her wet nose. The sheep picked up a yellow block with her mouth. She dropped it next to Emma's tower. Emma clapped her hands with joy.

"Do you want to build with me?" Emma asked.

Woolly nodded her head up and down. Emma couldn't believe it. This sheep understood her! Emma picked up a green block. She placed it on top of her tower. Woolly found a purple block. She gently put it next to the green one.

They worked together building the tallest tower ever. Emma would place a block. Then Woolly would add another one. The tower grew higher and higher. Emma laughed when Woolly's nose tickled her arm.

"We make a great team!" Emma said happily.

After building, they played a fun game. Emma would hide behind the big couch. Woolly would count to ten by stamping her hooves. Then Woolly would find Emma. When the sheep found her, Emma would giggle loudly.

"Found you!" Emma would say, hugging Woolly's neck.

Next, they played with Emma's soft ball. Emma rolled the red ball across the carpet. Woolly would chase it with her hooves clicking on the floor. The sheep would push the ball back to Emma with her nose. Back and forth the ball went.

The afternoon sun made everything golden and warm. Emma and Woolly sat together on the fluffy rug. Emma leaned against Woolly's side. The sheep's wool smelled like fresh grass and sunshine.

"I was lonely before," Emma told her new friend. "But now I'm so happy."

Woolly made a gentle "baa" sound. She nuzzled Emma's cheek with her soft nose. Emma felt loved and special.

Emma's mom came home from the store. She saw Emma and Woolly cuddled together. The blocks were built into an amazing castle. The room was full of laughter and joy.

"What a wonderful friend you've found," Mom said with a smile.

Emma looked up at her mom. "Woolly came to play with me. We built things together. We played games too!"

Mom talked to the farmer next door. The kind farmer said Woolly could visit Emma every day. Emma jumped up and down with excitement.

"Really? Every day?" Emma asked.

"Every day," Mom promised.

Emma hugged Woolly tight. "We'll never be lonely again," she whispered.

Woolly's tail wagged happily. The sheep had found her best friend too. From that day on, Emma and Woolly played together every afternoon. They built block towers. They played hide and seek. They shared quiet cuddles.

The living room was never quiet again. It was filled with friendship, laughter, and love. Emma learned that the best friends can come in the most surprising ways. Sometimes they even have four legs and fluffy wool!

The end.
