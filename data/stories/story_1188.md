# Story story_1188

Papa sat in the backyard. The grass felt soft under his feet. He bounced his red ball up and down. Bounce, bounce, bounce. But playing alone felt quiet and sad.

Papa looked around the empty yard. The sun was warm on his face. Birds sang in the tall oak tree. But something was missing. Papa wanted a friend to play with.

Suddenly, the ground shook a little. Thump, thump, thump. Papa looked up and gasped. A big gray elephant walked into his backyard! The elephant had kind brown eyes and a long swishy trunk.

"Hello there," said Papa softly. He had never seen an elephant so close before.

The elephant made a gentle rumbling sound. It sounded like a happy purr. The elephant's trunk reached toward Papa's red ball. Its trunk was wrinkled and warm-looking.

"Do you want to play?" asked Papa. His heart beat fast with excitement.

The elephant nodded its big head. Its ears flapped like gray blankets in the breeze. Papa smiled the biggest smile ever.

Papa rolled the ball to the elephant. The elephant picked it up with its trunk. It was very careful and gentle. Then the elephant tossed the ball back to Papa.

"You're good at this!" said Papa, clapping his hands.

They played catch for a while. The ball went back and forth, back and forth. Papa giggled when the elephant's trunk tickled the air. The elephant made happy trumpet sounds.

Next, Papa had an idea. "Let's play hide and seek!" he said.

Papa counted to ten. "One, two, three..." The elephant tried to hide behind the small apple tree. But elephants are very big! Papa could see the elephant's gray bottom sticking out.

"I found you!" Papa laughed. The elephant's trunk wiggled with joy.

Now it was the elephant's turn to count. The elephant covered its eyes with its big ears. Papa hid behind the garden shed. He made himself very small and quiet.

The elephant looked around the yard. It sniffed with its trunk near the flowers. It checked behind the fence. Finally, it found Papa and trumpeted happily.

"You found me!" said Papa. "You're really good at this game!"

The elephant gently wrapped its trunk around Papa. It felt like getting a warm, soft hug. Papa patted the elephant's rough, gray skin. It felt like touching a friendly rock.

"I'm thirsty from all this playing," said Papa. "Are you thirsty too?"

The elephant nodded. Papa ran inside and brought back a big bucket of cool water. The elephant sucked up the water with its trunk. Then it sprayed some on its back to cool off. A few drops splashed on Papa.

"That feels nice!" Papa giggled. The water was cool and refreshing.

They sat together under the shady oak tree. Papa leaned against the elephant's warm side. The elephant's breathing was slow and peaceful. Papa felt safe and happy.

"I was lonely before," Papa told his new friend. "But now I'm not lonely anymore."

The elephant made a soft rumbling sound. It seemed to understand every word Papa said.

As the sun began to set, the sky turned orange and pink. Papa's mom called from the house. "Papa! Time for dinner!"

"Will you come back tomorrow?" Papa asked the elephant.

The elephant touched Papa's cheek gently with its trunk. Then it nodded yes. Papa's heart filled with joy.

The elephant walked slowly toward the back fence. It turned around once to wave its trunk goodbye. Papa waved back with both hands.

"See you tomorrow, friend!" Papa called out.

That night at dinner, Papa told his mom about his amazing day. He could hardly eat because he was so excited. His mom smiled and listened to every word.

Before bed, Papa looked out his window at the backyard. Tomorrow would bring another day of fun with his new elephant friend. Papa fell asleep with the biggest smile on his face.

He was never lonely again.

The end.
