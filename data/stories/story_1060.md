# Story story_1060

**The Little Boat's Lost Car**

Captain Splash was a bright blue toy boat. He lived on the kitchen counter. His best friend was a tiny red car named Zoom.

Every morning, Captain Splash and Zoom played together. They raced around the big white sink. They sailed through sudsy water. They had the best adventures!

But this morning was different. Captain Splash woke up from his spot by the salt shaker. He looked left. He looked right. Where was Zoom?

"Zoom! Where are you?" called Captain Splash. His voice echoed in the quiet kitchen.

No answer came back. Captain Splash felt worried. His little boat heart started beating faster. Zoom was always right beside him when he woke up.

Captain Splash decided to search everywhere. He rocked back and forth on his hull. Maybe he could see better from up high.

The kitchen looked so big from the counter. There were so many places to hide. Captain Splash saw the tall refrigerator humming softly. He saw the shiny toaster reflecting morning light. But no red car anywhere.

"I need to get down there," Captain Splash said to himself. "Zoom might be on the floor."

Captain Splash had an idea. He would slide down the dish towel! The blue and white towel hung from the counter like a rope.

Captain Splash pushed himself forward. He slid onto the soft towel. Down, down, down he went! But halfway down, the towel twisted. Captain Splash spun around and around.

Thump! He landed upside down on the cold tile floor. His mast pointed toward the ground. His sail was all crumpled.

"Ow," said Captain Splash. He felt dizzy from spinning. This plan did not work at all.

Captain Splash flipped himself right side up. The kitchen floor felt huge and scary. Everything looked giant from down here. The chair legs looked like tall trees. The table seemed as big as a mountain.

But Captain Splash did not give up. He was determined to find his friend. He pushed himself forward with his little rudder. Across the smooth tiles he went.

Under the big wooden table, Captain Splash spotted something. A flash of red! His heart jumped with excitement.

"Zoom! Is that you?" he called out hopefully.

Captain Splash pushed harder. He slid under the dark table. There, behind the table leg, sat a sad little red car.

"Zoom!" Captain Splash cried with joy. "I found you!"

Zoom's tiny wheels were dusty. His shiny red paint looked dull in the shadows. "I rolled off the counter last night," Zoom said quietly. "I've been stuck here all alone. I was too scared to call for help."

"Don't worry," said Captain Splash kindly. "We'll get out together."

Captain Splash thought carefully. They needed to get back to the counter. But how?

Then he remembered something. Every morning, Mom put away clean dishes. She always used the step stool by the counter.

"Follow me!" said Captain Splash. Together, they pushed toward the yellow step stool.

The step stool had three levels. Like tiny stairs! Captain Splash pushed Zoom up the first step. Then he pushed himself up too.

Step by step, they climbed higher. Captain Splash's hull scraped against the plastic. Zoom's wheels clicked on each level. But they kept going.

Finally, they reached the top step. From there, they could slide onto the counter. Home at last!

Captain Splash and Zoom snuggled together by the salt shaker. The morning sun made their colors bright and cheerful again.

"Thank you for finding me," said Zoom gratefully. "I was so scared down there."

"That's what friends do," said Captain Splash. "We never give up on each other."

From that day on, they always stayed close together. And Captain Splash learned something important. When you don't give up, you can solve any problem. Even the biggest ones.

The kitchen felt warm and happy again. Captain Splash and Zoom were ready for another day of wonderful adventures.

The end.
