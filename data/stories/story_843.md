# Story story_843

**Tommy and Grandma's Field Adventure**

Tommy kicked a small rock across the grassy field. The sun felt warm on his face. Birds chirped in the tall oak tree. But Tommy felt sad and alone.

His big sister was at soccer practice. His mom was busy with baby Lucy. His dad was fixing the fence. Tommy had nobody to play with.

He picked up a dandelion and blew the fluffy seeds. They danced in the gentle breeze. Tommy watched them float away like tiny parachutes.

"What are you doing out here, sweetie?" asked a kind voice.

Tommy turned around. Grandma Rose walked toward him slowly. Her silver hair sparkled in the sunlight. She wore her favorite purple sweater.

"Just playing by myself," Tommy said quietly. His shoulders drooped down.

Grandma's eyes twinkled. "Playing alone can be fun. But playing together is even better!"

She sat down in the soft grass. Her knees made a little creaking sound. "What should we play?"

Tommy's face lit up like a bright light. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" Grandma clapped her hands together. "I love playing games!"

Tommy thought for a moment. "We could play hide and seek!"

"Wonderful idea!" Grandma said. "You hide first. I'll count to ten."

Grandma covered her eyes with her hands. "One, two, three..."

Tommy giggled and ran behind the big oak tree. The bark felt rough against his back. He tried to make himself very small.

"Ten! Ready or not, here I come!"

Grandma looked left and right. She checked behind a bush. She peeked under a low branch. "Hmm, where could Tommy be?"

Tommy tried not to giggle. His tummy felt bubbly with excitement.

"I wonder if he's behind this tree?" Grandma said loudly.

"Found me!" Tommy jumped out, laughing.

"Your turn to find me!" Grandma said. She moved surprisingly fast for someone with gray hair.

Tommy counted carefully. "Eight, nine, ten!"

He looked everywhere. Behind trees, near the fence, by the flower patch. Where was Grandma?

Then he heard a soft giggle. It came from the tall grass near the pond. Tommy walked closer. He saw purple fabric peeking out.

"Found you, Grandma!"

Grandma popped up like a jack-in-the-box. Grass stuck to her sweater. Her cheeks were pink from laughing.

"Let's play something else!" Tommy said, bouncing on his toes.

"How about we make flower crowns?" Grandma suggested.

They picked yellow buttercups and white daisies. The flowers smelled sweet and fresh. Grandma showed Tommy how to link the stems together.

"Like this, sweetie. Twist them gently."

Tommy's little fingers worked carefully. His crown looked a bit wobbly. But Grandma said it was perfect.

"You're the best flower crown maker!" she said.

They placed the crowns on their heads. Tommy felt like a king. Grandma looked like a fairy queen.

Next, they played tag around the field. Grandma ran slowly, but she laughed loudly. Her laughter sounded like music to Tommy's ears.

They rolled down the small hill together. The world spun around them. Green grass and blue sky mixed together. They landed at the bottom, giggling.

"I'm dizzy!" Tommy said, still lying on his back.

"Me too!" Grandma laughed. "But the good kind of dizzy!"

They watched white clouds float by. One looked like a bunny. Another looked like a boat.

"Grandma?" Tommy said softly.


"Yes, sweetie?"

"This is the best day ever."

Grandma reached over and squeezed his hand. Her hand felt warm and soft.

"Playing with you makes me happy too," she said. "We make a great team."

Tommy smiled the biggest smile. He wasn't lonely anymore. He had found the perfect playmate.

As the sun began to set, they walked home together. Tommy held Grandma's hand tightly. The field had given them something special.

"Can we play in the field again tomorrow?" Tommy asked.

"Every day if you want," Grandma promised.

Tommy's heart felt full of sunshine. Sometimes the best adventures happen right in your own backyard. And sometimes the best friends are the ones who love you most.

The end.
