# Story story_973

**Moo and the Tall Fence**

Moo was a little brown cow. She lived in a sunny meadow. Green grass tickled her hooves. Birds sang sweet songs above her.

One morning, Moo walked to the edge of her meadow. A tall wooden fence stood there. It was very, very tall. Moo stretched her neck up high. She could not see over it.

"What is on the other side?" Moo wondered. She heard happy sounds. Children were laughing and playing. Dogs were barking with joy. 

Moo tried to peek through the fence. The wooden boards were too close together. She could only see tiny slivers of green. Her big brown eyes looked sad.

"I want to see what makes those happy sounds," Moo said softly. She pushed her nose against the fence. It felt rough and warm from the sun.

Moo had an idea. She would jump! She took three big steps back. Her hooves made soft thuds in the grass. She ran toward the fence as fast as she could.

Moo jumped with all her might. Up, up, up she went! But she was still too short. She landed with a gentle thump. Dirt puffed up around her hooves.

"Oh no," Moo sighed. Her tail swished back and forth. She felt disappointed but not ready to give up.

Moo looked around her meadow. She spotted a small hill nearby. The hill was covered in yellow dandelions. They smelled sweet and fresh.

"Maybe the hill will help me!" Moo said. Her voice sounded hopeful again. She walked over to the hill. Her hooves crunched on fallen leaves.

Moo climbed to the top of the little hill. Now she was higher than before. The wind felt cool on her face. She walked to the edge closest to the fence.

From the hill, Moo could see over the tall fence! Her heart felt happy and excited. On the other side was a beautiful playground. Children were swinging high in the air. A golden dog was catching a red ball. 

"Wow!" Moo said with wonder. Her eyes grew wide and bright. The playground had colorful slides and spinning wheels. Flowers grew in pretty gardens all around.

A little girl with curly hair noticed Moo. The girl waved her small hand. "Hello, cow!" she called out sweetly.

Moo felt so proud and happy. She had solved her problem! "Moo!" she called back. Her voice was loud and cheerful.

More children saw Moo on the hill. They all waved and smiled. Some pointed at her with excitement. "Look at the pretty cow!" they said.

Moo felt warm inside her heart. She had found a way to see the playground. The hill was the perfect solution. She did not give up when jumping did not work.

Every day after that, Moo climbed her special hill. She watched the children play. They watched her eat grass and swish her tail. Everyone was happy.

Sometimes the children brought apples to share. They tossed them over the fence. Moo caught them with her mouth. The apples were crunchy and sweet.

Moo learned something important that day. When one idea does not work, try another idea. Keep trying until you find the answer. Problems can always be solved with thinking and trying.

Now Moo loved her meadow even more. She had her soft grass and pretty flowers. She also had her hill and her view of the playground. Best of all, she had new friends to wave to.

As the sun set orange and pink, Moo walked down from her hill. She felt proud of herself. Tomorrow she would climb up again. Tomorrow she would see her friends and wave hello.

Moo fell asleep in the soft grass. She dreamed happy dreams about hills and fences and friendly children. Her problem had become her favorite part of each day.

The end.
