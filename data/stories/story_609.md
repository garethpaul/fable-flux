# Story story_609

Leo the lion sat in the big sandbox. The warm sand felt soft under his paws. He pushed sand into little hills. Then he knocked them down.

"This is fun," Leo said to himself. But his voice sounded quiet. The playground was empty except for him.

Leo made a sand castle. It had tall towers and deep moats. He worked very hard on it. When he finished, he looked around. No one was there to see his beautiful castle.

"I wish someone would play with me," Leo sighed. His golden mane drooped a little. The sun felt hot on his back.

High up in the oak tree, Bella the bluebird watched Leo. Her bright blue feathers sparkled in the sunlight. She wanted to play too. But she felt nervous.

"What if he doesn't want to be friends?" Bella chirped softly. She flapped her small wings. "Lions are so big. I am so little."

Bella took a deep breath. She flew down from her branch. Her tiny feet landed on the edge of the sandbox.

"Hello," she said in her sweet voice. "Can I play with you?"

Leo looked up. His amber eyes grew wide with surprise. Then they filled with joy.

"Yes!" Leo roared happily. "I would love a friend to play with!"

Bella smiled. Her worries flew away like dandelion seeds. "What should we build?" she asked.

"How about a sand city?" Leo suggested. "You can make the small parts. I can make the big parts."

They started working together right away. Leo used his big paws to make roads and buildings. The sand crunched and shifted as he worked.

Bella used her tiny beak to make details. She carved windows in the sand houses. She made little gardens with stick trees.

"This looks amazing!" Bella chirped. She hopped from house to house. Her claws left tiny prints in the sand.

Leo grinned. His whiskers twitched with happiness. "We make a great team!"

They built bridges over sand rivers. Leo carried big sticks in his mouth. Bella arranged small pebbles in pretty patterns.

"Look what I found!" Bella called out. She had discovered a shiny bottle cap. It looked like a tiny swimming pool.

"Perfect!" Leo laughed. "Our city needs a pool!"

The sun climbed higher in the sky. Their sand city grew bigger and better. They added a park with leaf trees. They made a school with acorn students.

"I'm getting thirsty," Leo said. His pink tongue hung out a little.

"Me too," agreed Bella. "Should we take a break?"

They sat in the shade of the oak tree. Leo's mom had packed him apple slices. Bella's mom had given her sunflower seeds.

"Want to share?" Leo asked kindly.

"Yes, please!" Bella chirped.

They munched their snacks together. The apple was sweet and crunchy. The seeds were salty and good.

"This is the best day ever," Bella said. She tucked her head under her wing shyly.

"I think so too," Leo agreed. "I was lonely before you came."

"Really?" Bella asked. "But you're so big and brave!"

"Big doesn't mean not lonely," Leo explained. "Everyone needs friends."

Bella nodded. She understood now. "I was scared to ask to play. But I'm so glad I did!"

They finished their snacks and went back to building. Their city now had a library and a bakery. It even had a tiny airport for visiting birds.

Other children started arriving at the playground. They gathered around the amazing sand city.

"Wow!" said a little girl. "Did you build this together?"

"Yes!" Leo and Bella said at the same time. They looked at each other and giggled.

As the day ended, they carefully saved part of their city. Tomorrow they would build even more.

"Will you play with me again?" Bella asked hopefully.

"Every day!" Leo promised. "Best friends always play together."

They hugged goodbye. Leo's warm fur felt soft against Bella's feathers. From that day on, they were never lonely again.

The sandbox became their special place. Where a lion and a bird proved that friendship comes in all sizes.

The end.
