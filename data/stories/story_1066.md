# Story story_1066

**Puzzle's Missing Teddy Bear**

Puzzle was a happy little boy who loved his kitchen. He had tiny pots and pans. He had wooden spoons that clicked together. He had a small red table where he cooked pretend soup.

But today, Puzzle felt very sad. His favorite teddy bear was missing! Brown Bear always sat on the kitchen counter. Brown Bear watched Puzzle cook every day. Now Brown Bear was gone.

"Where are you, Brown Bear?" called Puzzle. His voice sounded wobbly. He looked at the empty counter. The shiny surface felt cold under his fingers.

Puzzle's mom heard him calling. She walked into the kitchen. Her soft slippers made quiet sounds on the floor.

"What's wrong, sweetie?" Mom asked. She knelt down next to Puzzle. Her warm hand touched his shoulder.

"I can't find Brown Bear!" said Puzzle. "He's not on the counter. He's not anywhere!"

Puzzle's eyes filled with tears. Brown Bear was his best friend. They cooked together every morning. They shared pretend cookies every afternoon.

"Let's look together," said Mom. Her voice was gentle and calm. "Where did you play with Brown Bear yesterday?"

Puzzle thought hard. He rubbed his chin with his small finger. "We made pretend pancakes," he said. "We stirred the bowl. We flipped them high!"

"Good thinking," said Mom. "Let's check the cabinets first."

Puzzle opened the low cabinet doors. They creaked softly. Inside, he saw his plastic bowls. He saw his measuring cups. But no Brown Bear.

He opened another cabinet. This one smelled like vanilla cookies. Inside were Mom's big pots. Still no Brown Bear.

Puzzle felt more worried. His tummy felt tight and twisty. "Maybe Brown Bear is lost forever," he whispered.

"Don't give up," said Mom. "Sometimes we need to look in surprising places. Where else did you and Brown Bear go yesterday?"

Puzzle closed his eyes. He tried to remember. "We played restaurant!" he said suddenly. "Brown Bear was the customer. I served him soup under the table!"

Puzzle's heart beat faster. He dropped to his knees. The kitchen floor felt smooth and cool. He crawled under the small red table.

There, in the dark corner, sat Brown Bear!

"Brown Bear!" shouted Puzzle. He grabbed his fuzzy friend tight. Brown Bear felt soft and warm. He smelled like Puzzle's favorite blanket.

Puzzle crawled out from under the table. He hugged Brown Bear close to his chest. The teddy bear's button nose tickled Puzzle's cheek.

"You found him!" said Mom. She clapped her hands together. The sound was happy and bright.

"I didn't give up," said Puzzle proudly. "I kept looking and looking. I remembered our restaurant game."

"That's right," said Mom. "You used your brain. You thought about where you played. That was very smart."

Puzzle sat Brown Bear on the counter again. The teddy bear looked happy to be home. His black button eyes seemed to sparkle.

"Now we can cook together," Puzzle told Brown Bear. "Want to make pretend soup?"

Puzzle got his wooden spoon. It felt smooth in his hand. He stirred an empty pot. The spoon made soft tapping sounds.

"This soup is for you, Brown Bear," said Puzzle. "It has carrots and noodles. It tastes like sunshine."

Brown Bear sat quietly on the counter. He watched Puzzle cook with his friendly button eyes.

Mom smiled as she watched them play. "I'm proud of you, Puzzle," she said. "You didn't give up when things seemed hard. You kept trying until you found Brown Bear."

Puzzle felt warm and happy inside. His kitchen felt perfect again. Brown Bear was safe on the counter. The sun shone through the window, making everything bright and cheerful.

"I love you, Brown Bear," whispered Puzzle. "Tomorrow we'll cook pancakes together. Real ones with Mom!"

Brown Bear seemed to smile back. Everything was just right in Puzzle's kitchen once again.

The end.
