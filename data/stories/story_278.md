# Tommy's Garden Discovery

The morning sun peeked through Tommy's bedroom window. "Garden time!" called Mom from downstairs. Tommy pulled his blanket over his head. He didn't want to go outside today.

Yesterday, garden time felt boring. All that digging and weeding. His hands got dirty. The sun felt too hot. Tommy wanted to stay inside with his toys instead.

"Coming, Tommy!" Mom called again. Tommy sighed deeply. He put on his sneakers slowly. Maybe today would be different. But probably not.

Tommy walked to the backyard. Mom was already there. She wore her big sun hat. Her gardening gloves looked ready for work. "Good morning, sunshine!" she said cheerfully.

Tommy looked at the garden beds. Green plants everywhere. Some were tall. Some were short. Nothing looked very exciting to him. "Do I have to help?" he asked quietly.

"Let's try something new today," Mom said. She handed Tommy a small watering can. It was bright red. Just his size. "The plants are thirsty. Want to give them drinks?"

Tommy took the watering can. It felt lighter than expected. Mom showed him the hose. Cool water filled the can quickly. Tommy liked the splashing sound it made.

He walked to the first plant. A tomato plant with tiny green balls. Tommy poured water gently. The soil made happy bubbling sounds. The water disappeared like magic. "The plant is drinking!" Tommy said with surprise.

"That's right!" Mom smiled. "Plants get thirsty just like you do."

Tommy moved to the next plant. Carrots with feathery green tops. He watered them carefully. Then the lettuce. Then the peppers. Each plant seemed to dance when the water touched them.

Suddenly, Tommy noticed something amazing. A tiny yellow butterfly landed on a sunflower. It opened and closed its wings slowly. Tommy watched, barely breathing. The butterfly looked like a flying flower.

"Look, Mom!" Tommy whispered. The butterfly stayed for a long moment. Then it fluttered to another flower. Tommy followed it with his eyes. More butterflies appeared. Orange ones. White ones. The garden was full of flying colors.

"Wow," Tommy said softly. He had never noticed the butterflies before. They made the garden feel magical. Like a fairy tale come to life.

Mom pointed to a strawberry plant. "Look here, Tommy." Three red strawberries hung from green stems. They looked like tiny hearts. "These weren't ready yesterday," Mom said. "But today they're perfect."

Tommy touched one strawberry gently. It felt soft and warm. "Can I pick it?" he asked. Mom nodded. Tommy carefully twisted the strawberry. It came off easily. He smelled it. Sweet and wonderful.

"You can eat it," Mom said. Tommy bit into the strawberry. Juice ran down his chin. It tasted like sunshine and happiness. The sweetest thing ever. "I grew this?" Tommy asked amazed.

"You helped water it every day," Mom said proudly. "You helped it grow big and strong."

Tommy looked around the garden with new eyes. He had helped make all of this. The tall tomato plants. The colorful flowers. The buzzing bees. Everything was alive and growing because he helped.

A fat bumblebee landed on a purple flower. It wiggled inside, gathering pollen. Tommy giggled at its fuzzy bottom sticking out. The bee flew to another flower. Then another. Like it was shopping for the best treats.

Tommy found a ladybug on a leaf. Seven black spots on red wings. It walked across his finger tickling gently. "Hello, little friend," Tommy whispered. The ladybug stayed for a moment. Then flew away to explore.

The morning passed quickly. Tommy watered every plant. He picked ripe vegetables with Mom. He counted butterflies. He watched busy ants marching in lines. The garden was full of wonderful surprises.

When lunch time came, Tommy felt sad to go inside. "Can we come back after lunch?" he asked hopefully. Mom hugged him tight. "Of course we can."

That night, Tommy looked out his window. The garden looked peaceful in moonlight. Tomorrow there might be new strawberries. New butterflies. New adventures waiting. Tommy smiled sleepily. He couldn't wait for garden time tomorrow.

The garden wasn't boring at all. It was the most magical place in the whole world.

The end.
