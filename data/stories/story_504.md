# Anna and the Swing Set Friend

Anna sat on the big red swing. Her feet barely touched the ground. She pushed herself back and forth. Back and forth. The chains made a soft creaking sound.

"This is boring," Anna said quietly. She looked around the empty playground. No other children were playing today. The slide looked lonely too. Even the sandbox sat empty.

Anna stopped swinging. She dragged her shoes in the dirt. Little puffs of dust swirled around her feet. She wished someone would come play.

Just then, a fluffy golden dog trotted over. His tail wagged like a happy flag. His tongue hung out in a big doggy smile. He had kind brown eyes that sparkled.

"Woof!" said the dog softly. He sat down near Anna's swing. His head tilted to one side.

"Hello, doggy," Anna said. "Are you lonely too?"

The dog's tail wagged faster. He stood up and walked closer. He sniffed Anna's hand gently. His nose felt cold and wet.

"Do you want to play?" Anna asked.

The dog barked once. It sounded like "Yes!" Anna giggled. She had an idea.

"I can push you on the swing!" Anna said. She patted the swing next to hers. The dog looked confused. He wagged his tail but didn't move.

Anna tried a different idea. She got off her swing. "Maybe we can take turns," she said. "I'll push you first!"

The dog sniffed the swing seat. He put his front paws on it. Anna gently helped him climb up. The dog wobbled a little. Anna held the swing steady.

"Ready?" Anna asked. She gave the swing a tiny push. The dog's ears flapped in the breeze. His tail wagged with excitement.

"Woof! Woof!" the dog barked happily. Anna pushed him higher. Not too high, just right for a dog. The swing went back and forth. Back and forth. The dog loved it!

After a few minutes, Anna stopped pushing. "My turn now!" she said. The dog jumped down. His paws made soft thuds in the dirt.

Anna climbed back on her swing. The dog watched carefully. He seemed to understand. Anna started swinging slowly.

The dog ran behind her. He gave her swing a gentle push with his nose! Anna laughed with delight.

"You're helping me!" she said. "We're a team!"

The dog pushed again. And again. Anna swung higher than before. The wind blew through her hair. She felt like she was flying!

They took turns for a long time. Anna pushed the dog. The dog pushed Anna. Sometimes they both tried to swing at the same time. That made them both giggle and bark.

"This is so much better!" Anna said. "Playing together is more fun!"

The dog agreed with a happy "Woof!"

Then Anna had another idea. "Let's play a game!" she said. She got off her swing. "I'll count to three. Then we both run to the slide!"

"One... two... three!" Anna shouted. She and the dog raced across the playground. The dog's legs were longer, but Anna was fast too. They reached the slide together.

"Tie!" Anna laughed. The dog wagged his whole body.

They played more games. They ran around the swings. They dug in the sandbox together. Anna made a castle. The dog helped by bringing her sticks and leaves.

The sun felt warm on their faces. Birds chirped happy songs in the trees. The playground didn't feel empty anymore. It felt full of friendship and fun.

"I'm so glad you came to play," Anna told the dog. She scratched behind his soft ears. The dog leaned against her leg. His tail never stopped wagging.

From that day on, Anna and the dog met at the swing set. They were the very best of friends. Anna learned that everything was more fun with a friend. The dog learned that too.

And whenever other children came to play, Anna and the dog welcomed them. Soon the playground was full of laughter every day.

The swing set became their special place. A place where friendship began. A place where being together made everything better.
