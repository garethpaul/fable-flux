# Story story_926

**Sam's Magical Dinner Adventure**

"Dinner time, Sam!" called Mom from the kitchen.

Sam was building a tall tower with blocks. The blocks were red and blue and yellow. He did not want to stop playing.

"Five more minutes?" Sam asked.

"Come now, sweetie," Mom said with a smile.

Sam walked slowly to the kitchen. His feet felt heavy like rocks. The dining room smelled like chicken and vegetables. Sam wrinkled his nose.

"I'm not hungry," Sam said quietly.

Mom put a plate in front of him. There were green beans and carrots and pieces of chicken. Everything looked boring and plain.

Sam poked the green beans with his fork. They rolled around the white plate. He pushed the orange carrots into a little pile.

"Try one bite," Mom said gently.

Sam shook his head. He wanted pizza or cookies instead.

Then something strange happened. A green bean wiggled on his plate! Sam blinked his eyes. Did he really see that?

Another green bean moved. It stood up like a tiny green soldier!

"Hello, Sam!" squeaked the green bean in a tiny voice.

Sam's mouth fell open. He looked around. Mom was washing dishes. She did not hear the tiny voice.

"We're the Veggie Squad!" said a bright orange carrot. "We're here to help you grow big and strong!"

The chicken pieces started talking too. "We give you energy to run and play!" they said in cheerful voices.

Sam giggled softly. This was amazing!

"Want to play a game?" asked the green bean captain.

"What kind of game?" Sam whispered.

"The Growing Game!" said all the vegetables together. "Every bite helps you grow bigger!"

Sam picked up his fork. He carefully took a small bite of green bean. It tasted fresh and crispy.

"Hooray!" cheered the vegetables. "You're growing already!"

Sam felt a warm, happy feeling in his tummy. He took another bite. The carrot was sweet and crunchy.

"Look how strong you're getting!" said the carrot pieces.

Sam flexed his little arm. He did feel stronger! He took a bite of chicken. It was tender and tasty.

"Now you can run faster!" squeaked the chicken.

Sam pretended to run in his chair. His legs felt powerful and quick.

Bite by bite, Sam ate his dinner. Each bite made the vegetables cheer louder. The green beans did little dances. The carrots sang happy songs.

"We're helping you grow!" they sang. "Bite by bite, day by day!"

Sam's plate got emptier and emptier. His tummy felt full and satisfied. The warm feeling spread through his whole body.

"You did it!" shouted all the food friends. "You ate a whole healthy dinner!"

Sam smiled proudly. His plate was completely clean. Not one green bean was left.

"Great job, Sam!" said Mom, sitting down beside him. "You ate everything!"

"It was fun!" Sam said, his eyes sparkling.

The vegetables winked at Sam before becoming quiet again. Only he could see their magic.

"Can I have vegetables tomorrow too?" Sam asked.

Mom looked surprised and happy. "Of course! What would you like?"

"Green beans and carrots," Sam said quickly. "And maybe some broccoli trees?"

"Broccoli trees sound perfect," Mom laughed.

Sam hugged his mom tight. His tummy felt good and full. He felt strong and healthy.

That night, Sam dreamed about his veggie friends. They played games and sang songs. They taught him about growing big and strong.

When Sam woke up the next morning, he felt excited. He wondered what vegetables would talk to him today. Maybe the broccoli trees would tell stories!

"What's for dinner tonight?" Sam asked at breakfast.

Mom smiled. "More veggie friends," she said with a wink.

Sam clapped his hands. He could hardly wait for dinner time. Eating healthy food was the best adventure ever!

From that day on, Sam always looked forward to dinner. He knew his magical veggie friends would be waiting to help him grow.

The end.
