# Story story_747

Ducky sat alone in the big dining room. The wooden table was shiny and smooth. Tall chairs stood all around like quiet giants. Ducky's yellow feathers looked bright in the sunny room.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," Ducky said softly. Her orange feet made tiny tap-tap sounds on the floor. The dining room felt so big and empty.

Ducky picked up a red ball from under the table. She rolled it back and forth with her bill. The ball made a soft whoosh sound on the carpet. But playing alone wasn't very fun.

Just then, heavy footsteps came down the hall. THUMP, THUMP, THUMP. The sound got louder and louder.

A big gray elephant peeked around the corner. His trunk swayed gently from side to side. His kind brown eyes looked at Ducky.

"Hello there," said Elephant in his deep, rumbly voice. "I'm Eddie. What's your name?"

"I'm Ducky," she said shyly. She tucked her head down a little bit.

Eddie's trunk touched the red ball. "That looks like a fun ball. Can I play too?"

Ducky's eyes lit up like tiny stars. "Yes! Yes! I would love that!"

Eddie used his long trunk to push the ball to Ducky. The ball rolled smoothly across the shiny floor. Ducky caught it with her orange feet and giggled.

"My turn!" Ducky said. She pushed the ball back with her bill. The ball bumped gently into Eddie's big foot.

"This is wonderful!" Eddie trumpeted happily. His ears flapped like gray flags.

They rolled the ball back and forth many times. The dining room filled with happy sounds. Ducky's giggles mixed with Eddie's gentle trumpets.

"Let's try something new," said Eddie. He picked up the ball with his trunk and held it high. "Can you catch it?"

Ducky spread her yellow wings wide. "I'm ready!"

Eddie gently tossed the ball. It sailed through the air like a red cloud. Ducky caught it perfectly in her bill.

"Hooray!" they both cheered together.

The warm sunlight streamed through the big window. It made everything golden and cozy. The dining room didn't feel empty anymore.

"You know what?" said Ducky. "I was feeling lonely before you came."

Eddie's trunk reached over and gently patted Ducky's soft feathers. "I was lonely too. I heard you playing and hoped we could be friends."

"We ARE friends now!" Ducky said with a big smile.

They played hide-and-seek around the table legs. Eddie's trunk helped him find Ducky behind the chairs. Ducky found Eddie behind the big wooden cabinet.

The sweet smell of flowers came through the open window. A gentle breeze made the curtains dance. Everything felt perfect and peaceful.

"I have an idea," said Eddie. He used his trunk to move two chairs together. "Let's make a special friendship spot."

Ducky waddled over quickly. They sat together between the chairs. Eddie's gray skin felt warm and safe. Ducky's yellow feathers were soft and cheerful.

"This is our friendship corner," Ducky said proudly.

"The very best corner in the whole dining room," Eddie agreed.

They talked about their favorite foods and colors. Ducky loved seeds and the color blue. Eddie loved peanuts and the color green. They both loved playing games.

As the afternoon sun grew softer, they made plans for tomorrow. They would play ball again. Maybe they would explore the garden together.

"Thank you for being my friend," Ducky whispered.

"Thank you for sharing your ball," Eddie whispered back.

The dining room was full of friendship now. The chairs seemed to smile down at them. The table gleamed happily in the golden light.

Ducky and Eddie sat close together in their special corner. They watched the pretty shadows dance on the walls. Their hearts felt warm and full.

"Friends forever?" asked Ducky.

"Friends forever," promised Eddie.

And in that cozy dining room, two lonely hearts had found each other. The best kind of magic had happened. The magic of friendship.

The end.
