# Story story_788

**Penny's Sleepy Adventure**

The sun hung high in the bright blue sky. All the forest animals were getting ready for afternoon nap time. Mama Robin called softly to her little bird, Penny.

"Time for nap, sweet Penny," Mama said. Her voice was gentle like a warm breeze.

Penny fluffed her bright yellow feathers. She did not want to nap. The day felt too exciting to sleep. Butterflies danced in the sunshine. Flowers smelled so sweet. Everything looked wonderful and fun.

"But Mama, I'm not tired," Penny chirped. She hopped from branch to branch. Her tiny feet made soft tapping sounds on the bark.

"I want to play more. I want to fly around the big oak tree. I want to sing with the other birds."

Mama Robin smiled with her kind eyes. "Close your eyes first, little one. Just try for a few minutes."

Penny sighed a tiny bird sigh. She tucked her head under her wing. Her nest felt soft and cozy. The afternoon air was warm and gentle.

But something magical began to happen. As Penny closed her eyes, she heard beautiful sounds. The wind whispered secrets through the leaves. It sounded like a lullaby just for her.

A cricket played music like a tiny violin. Somewhere far away, a stream bubbled and gurgled. The sounds mixed together like a sleepy song.

Penny felt herself getting drowsy. Her breathing became slow and peaceful. In her dreams, something wonderful started to happen.

She was flying through puffy white clouds. The clouds felt soft like cotton balls. They smelled fresh like rain. Penny could taste the sweet morning dew on her beak.

In her dream, she met a wise old owl. The owl had sparkly silver feathers. His eyes twinkled like stars.

"Hello, little Penny," the owl hooted softly. "I have something special to show you."

The owl led Penny to a magical garden. Rainbow flowers grew everywhere. They sang tiny songs when the wind touched them. The whole garden smelled like honey and sunshine.

"This garden only appears during nap time," the owl explained. "Only sleeping birds can visit this special place."

Penny gasped with wonder. Purple and pink flowers chimed like little bells. Orange flowers hummed happy tunes. Blue flowers whispered gentle poems.

"Every time you nap, you can come back here," the owl said kindly. "This magical garden waits just for you."

Penny danced among the singing flowers. She felt so happy and peaceful. The dream felt more real than being awake.

When Penny woke up, the sun was lower in the sky. Golden light filtered through the leaves. She felt refreshed and full of energy.

"Mama!" Penny chirped excitedly. "I had the most amazing dream! There was a magical garden with singing flowers!"

Mama Robin's eyes sparkled. "Dreams during nap time are always the most special," she said.

Penny stretched her wings wide. She felt wonderful after her rest. Her body felt strong and ready to play. Her mind felt clear and happy.

"The wise owl said I can visit the garden every nap time," Penny shared. "Is that really true, Mama?"

"Dreams are full of magic, sweet one," Mama replied. "When we rest, our minds can travel to beautiful places."

That evening, Penny played happily until bedtime. She sang with her bird friends. She practiced flying in loops around the oak tree. She felt energetic and joyful.

When nighttime came, Penny was ready for sleep. She snuggled into her cozy nest. The stars twinkled above like tiny diamonds.

"Mama," Penny whispered sleepily. "Tomorrow can we have nap time again? I want to visit the singing flower garden."

Mama Robin tucked Penny in with her wing. "Of course, little one. Sweet dreams."

Penny closed her eyes with a happy heart. She couldn't wait for tomorrow's nap time adventure. Maybe she would meet new magical friends. Maybe she would discover new wonderful places.

Nap time wasn't boring anymore. It was the most exciting part of her day.

The end.
