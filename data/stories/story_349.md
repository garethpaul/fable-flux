# Penny the Puppet and the Lost Doll

Penny the puppet lived by a sparkling blue pond. She had soft yellow yarn hair. Her red dress swished when she walked. Every morning, she loved to play by the water's edge.

Penny carried her favorite doll everywhere. The doll had a pink dress. She had tiny pearl buttons. Her name was Rosie. Penny loved Rosie very much.

One sunny morning, Penny skipped to the pond. She wanted to show Rosie the lily pads. The water sparkled like diamonds. Dragonflies danced in the air.

"Look, Rosie!" Penny said happily. "The fish are swimming!"

Penny leaned over to see better. Her hands slipped. Oh no! Rosie fell into the pond with a tiny splash.

"Rosie!" Penny cried. "Come back!"

The doll floated away slowly. The gentle waves carried her further. Penny's button eyes filled with tears.

"I must get Rosie back," Penny said. She wiped her yarn cheeks. "I won't give up."

First, Penny tried reaching with her arms. She stretched and stretched. The water was too deep. Rosie floated further away.

"That didn't work," Penny sighed. "I need another plan."

Next, Penny found a long stick. She poked it toward Rosie. The stick was too short. It barely touched the water.

"Hmm," Penny thought. "Maybe something longer?"

Penny looked around the pond. She saw tall cattails growing. She picked the longest one. It swayed in her hands.

She reached out again. The cattail was longer! But Rosie was still too far. The doll bobbed near some lily pads.

"Don't worry, Rosie!" Penny called. "I'm still trying!"

A friendly frog hopped over. His name was Frederick. He had bright green skin.

"What's wrong, Penny?" Frederick asked kindly.

"My doll fell in," Penny sniffled. "I can't reach her."

Frederick's eyes sparkled with an idea. "I can help! I'm a good swimmer."

"Really?" Penny's face brightened. "You would do that?"

"Of course!" Frederick smiled. "Friends help friends."

Frederick dove into the cool water. Splash! He swam with strong kicks. His webbed feet pushed him forward.

Penny watched hopefully from the shore. Frederick reached the lily pads. He grabbed Rosie gently in his mouth.

"You found her!" Penny cheered. "Thank you, Frederick!"

Frederick swam back carefully. He didn't want to drop Rosie. The water dripped from his shiny skin.

He placed Rosie on the muddy bank. Penny scooped up her precious doll. Rosie was wet but safe.

"Oh, Rosie!" Penny hugged her tight. "I missed you so much!"

Penny dried Rosie with soft grass. She smoothed the doll's tiny dress. The pearl buttons still sparkled.

"Thank you, Frederick," Penny said gratefully. "You're the best friend ever!"

"I was happy to help," Frederick grinned. "But you didn't give up. That was very brave."

Penny felt proud and warm inside. She had kept trying. She had asked for help. Everything worked out perfectly.

From that day on, Penny was more careful. She held Rosie tightly near water. But she wasn't afraid to play.

Penny and Frederick became best friends. They played by the pond together. Sometimes they helped other animals too.

When problems seemed too big, Penny remembered something important. She could try different solutions. She could ask friends for help. She should never give up.

The pond sparkled in the afternoon sun. Penny, Rosie, and Frederick sat together happily. The lily pads swayed gently. The dragonflies still danced.

"I love our pond," Penny said softly.

"Me too," Frederick agreed. "Especially with good friends."

Penny smiled her biggest smile. She had learned that persistence pays off. With determination and friendship, any problem could be solved.

The three friends watched the sunset paint the sky pink and orange. It was the perfect end to a challenging but wonderful day.

And Penny never forgot: when you don't give up and friends help each other, everything turns out just right.

The end.
