# Story story_1224

**Ellie and Grandma Rose**

Ellie the elephant sat in the big playroom. Her gray trunk dragged on the soft carpet. Colorful toys filled every corner of the room.

Red blocks stood in tall towers. Yellow cars lined up near the window. A purple ball sat by her feet. But Ellie felt very lonely.

"I wish I had someone to play with," she whispered. Her big ears drooped down sadly.

Ellie picked up a green crayon. She drew circles on white paper. The crayon felt waxy in her trunk. But drawing alone was not much fun.

She built a castle with wooden blocks. The blocks felt smooth and cool. Her castle grew taller and taller. Then it tumbled down with a loud crash.

"Oh no," Ellie sighed. Playing by herself was hard work.

Just then, the door opened with a gentle creak. Grandma Rose peeked inside. Her silver hair sparkled in the sunlight. She wore a cozy blue sweater.

"Hello there, little one," Grandma Rose said softly. Her voice sounded like warm honey.

Ellie looked up with surprise. "Hi," she said quietly.

Grandma Rose walked slowly into the room. Her shoes made soft tapping sounds. "May I play with you?" she asked kindly.

Ellie's trunk curled up with excitement. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course, dear," Grandma Rose smiled. Her eyes twinkled like stars. "What shall we play first?"

Ellie pointed to her fallen blocks. "Maybe we could build together?"

Grandma Rose sat down carefully on the carpet. "What a wonderful idea," she said.

They started building a new castle. Ellie stacked the big blocks. Grandma Rose placed the small ones on top. Their castle grew higher than before.

"Look how tall it is!" Ellie trumpeted happily.

"We make a great team," Grandma Rose laughed. Her laugh sounded like tinkling bells.

Next, they colored pictures together. Ellie drew big purple flowers. Grandma Rose added tiny yellow bees. The crayons smelled sweet and waxy.

"Your flowers are beautiful," Grandma Rose said.

"Your bees look so real!" Ellie giggled.

Then they played with the toy cars. Ellie pushed the red race car. Grandma Rose drove the blue truck. They made engine sounds together.

"Vroom, vroom!" they both called out.

The cars zoomed around the carpet roads. Ellie's trunk wiggled with joy. Grandma Rose clapped her hands.

After that, they rolled the purple ball back and forth. The ball felt bouncy and smooth. It made soft thudding sounds on the carpet.

"Catch, Ellie!" Grandma Rose called.

Ellie caught the ball with her trunk. "Got it!" she cheered.

They played catch until they felt tired. Both friends sat down to rest.

"Thank you for playing with me," Ellie said. She curled her trunk around Grandma Rose's hand.

"Thank you for letting me join you," Grandma Rose replied. She patted Ellie's trunk gently.

"I was feeling so lonely before," Ellie admitted.

"I was feeling lonely too," Grandma Rose said. "But now we have each other."

Ellie's eyes grew wide. "You were lonely too?"

"Sometimes everyone feels lonely," Grandma Rose explained. "That's why friends are so special."

Ellie trumpeted with happiness. "Will you be my friend?"

"I would love to be your friend," Grandma Rose said warmly.

They hugged each other tight. Ellie's trunk wrapped gently around Grandma Rose. The hug felt warm and safe.

"Should we have a friendship party?" Ellie asked.

"What a perfect idea!" Grandma Rose agreed.

They gathered all their favorite toys. The blocks became party decorations. The crayons made colorful streamers on paper. The cars formed a parade.

"This is the best party ever!" Ellie trumpeted.

"The very best," Grandma Rose agreed. "Because we're celebrating our new friendship."

As the sun set outside the window, Ellie and Grandma Rose sat together. The playroom glowed with warm orange light.

Ellie was not lonely anymore. She had found a wonderful friend. Grandma Rose smiled peacefully. She had found a special friend too.

"Tomorrow we can play again," Ellie said hopefully.

"I can't wait," Grandma Rose replied.

And they both knew their friendship would last forever.

The end.
