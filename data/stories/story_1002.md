# Story story_1002

Anna stood on her tippy-toes in the kitchen. She reached for the big wooden spoon. The counter felt cold under her hands.

"I want to cook something special," Anna said to herself. She felt a little sad. Playing alone was not very fun.

Anna opened the cupboard doors. She found a big red bowl. She found some flour in a white bag. The flour felt soft like snow.

She poured the flour into the bowl. Puff! A white cloud filled the air. Anna giggled as flour landed on her nose.

"What are you doing?" asked a small voice.

Anna turned around. Her neighbor Lily stood in the doorway. Lily had curly brown hair and a bright smile.

"I'm making pretend cookies," Anna said. She wiped flour from her cheek. "But it's not much fun by myself."

Lily's eyes grew wide with excitement. "Can I help you cook?" she asked. "I love making pretend food!"

Anna felt her heart jump with happiness. "Yes! Come help me!"

Lily ran to the sink. She washed her hands with bubbly soap. The soap smelled like lemons. Anna washed her hands too.

"What should we make?" Lily asked.

"How about birthday cake?" Anna suggested. "We can pretend it's for a princess!"

"Yes!" Lily clapped her hands. "I love princesses!"

The two girls worked together. Anna measured pretend sugar. Lily stirred the bowl with the big spoon. They took turns adding water.

"Mix, mix, mix!" they sang together. The spoon made soft sounds in the bowl.

Lily found some cinnamon in a small jar. "This smells like cookies," she said. She sprinkled some into their mix.

Anna discovered vanilla in a tiny bottle. "This smells sweet!" She added three drops.

They stirred and stirred. Their pretend cake batter looked perfect. It was thick and creamy white.

"Now we need to bake it," Anna said. She opened the pretend oven. It was really just a low cupboard.

Lily helped Anna put the bowl inside. "How long should we bake it?" Lily asked.

"Five minutes," Anna decided. "We can count together!"

They sat on the kitchen floor. They counted slowly from one to sixty. Five times! Their voices echoed in the warm kitchen.

"Ding! Our cake is ready!" Anna announced.

They carefully took out their pretend cake. It looked beautiful in the red bowl.

"We need frosting," Lily said. She found another bowl. She pretended to mix pink frosting.

Anna found blue food coloring. "Let's make it rainbow frosting!" she said.

They mixed pretend colors together. Red and blue and yellow. Their frosting looked magical.

They spread the pretend frosting on their cake. They used rubber spatulas. The smooth tools felt good in their small hands.

"Our cake needs decorations," Anna said.

Lily found colorful buttons in a drawer. "These can be flowers!" she said.

Anna found some ribbons. "These can be frosting swirls!"

They decorated their beautiful cake. It had button flowers and ribbon swirls. It was the prettiest cake ever.

"Let's have a tea party!" Lily suggested.

Anna found two small cups. Lily filled them with pretend tea. The cups felt smooth and cool.

They sat at the little table. They pretended to eat their delicious cake. They sipped their pretend tea.

"This is the best cake ever!" Anna said.

"You're the best cooking friend ever!" Lily replied.

Anna felt warm and happy inside. Cooking with a friend was so much better than cooking alone.

"Can we cook together again tomorrow?" Anna asked.

"Yes!" Lily said. "We can make pretend pizza!"

They cleaned up their kitchen together. They put away all the bowls and spoons. Everything sparkled clean.

Anna hugged Lily tight. "Thank you for cooking with me," she said.

"Thank you for sharing your kitchen!" Lily hugged back.

From that day on, Anna and Lily cooked together every week. They made pretend soup and pretend bread. They made pretend everything!

Anna learned something important that day. Playing with friends makes everything more fun. Especially cooking in the kitchen!

The two girls smiled at each other. They had found the best recipe of all. The recipe for friendship.

The end.
