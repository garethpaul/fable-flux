# Story story_1383

Tommy sat on the big red swing. He pushed his feet against the soft grass. Back and forth he went. Back and forth.

The swing set stood tall in his backyard. It had two swings side by side. Tommy always swung alone. His big sister was at school. Mom was busy inside.

Tommy felt lonely. The empty swing moved in the gentle wind. It creaked softly. He wished he had someone to play with.

"I want a friend," Tommy said quietly.

Just then, he heard a soft sound. Clip-clop, clip-clop. Something was coming closer. Tommy stopped swinging and looked around.

A beautiful brown horse walked into the yard. The horse had a long black mane. Her coat shone in the warm sunlight. She had kind, dark eyes.

"Hello there," Tommy whispered. He had never seen a horse so close.

The horse walked slowly to the swing set. She sniffed the empty swing with her soft nose. The swing moved back and forth.

"Do you want to swing too?" Tommy asked.

The horse looked at him. She made a gentle neighing sound. It sounded like "yes" to Tommy.

Tommy got off his swing carefully. He walked to the horse. She was much bigger than him. But she seemed friendly.

"I'm Tommy," he said. "What's your name?"

The horse nuzzled his hand gently. Her nose felt soft and warm.

"I'll call you Star," Tommy decided. "You have a white spot on your nose. It looks like a star."

Star seemed to like her new name. She tossed her head happily.

Tommy had an idea. "Let's try to swing together!"

But the swing was too small for Star. And Star was too big for the swing seat.

Tommy thought hard. How could they play together?

"I know!" Tommy said. "You can push me!"

Tommy climbed back on his swing. Star walked behind him. She gently pushed the swing with her nose.

"Wheee!" Tommy laughed as he flew higher.

Star pushed again. Tommy soared through the air. The wind tickled his face. His hair blew back.

"This is so fun!" Tommy giggled.

Star made happy horse sounds. She seemed to enjoy the game too.

They played for a long time. Star would push. Tommy would swing high. Then Tommy would jump off and pet Star's soft neck.

"Now let's try something else," Tommy said.

He found a long rope in the garage. Dad had taught him to tie knots. Tommy tied one end to the swing set.

"Hold this end, Star," he said.

Star took the rope gently in her teeth. Tommy held the middle. They played tug-of-war. Star was stronger, but she was gentle.

They pulled back and forth. Both of them were having fun. Tommy laughed. Star whinnied with joy.

Next, they played hide and seek. Tommy would hide behind the big oak tree. Star would find him easily. Her nose always knew where he was.

Then Star would close her eyes. Tommy would "hide" right next to her. When Star opened her eyes, she pretended to be surprised.

The sun was getting lower in the sky. It painted everything golden. Tommy's mom called from the house.

"Tommy! Time to come in!"

Tommy felt sad. He didn't want Star to leave.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" he asked.

Star nuzzled his cheek softly. It felt like a promise.

"I had the best day ever," Tommy told her. "You're my very best friend."

Star made a gentle sound. She seemed to understand.

Tommy hugged Star's neck. She smelled like sunshine and grass. Her mane was silky soft.

"Thank you for playing with me," Tommy whispered.

Star walked slowly toward the fence. She turned back once. Tommy waved goodbye.

The next morning, Tommy ran outside. Star was waiting by the swing set. She was ready to play again.

From that day on, Tommy was never lonely. He had found his special friend. Every day brought new adventures. The swing set became their favorite place.

Tommy learned that friendship can come from anywhere. Even from a gentle horse who loved to play. Together, they made every day magical.

The end.
