# Story story_898

**The Little Blue Train and the Toy Mountain**

Choo-choo-choo! The little blue train puffed up the steep hill. His name was Benny. He had bright red wheels and a shiny yellow bell.

Every day, Benny carried toys to children. Today felt different. At the bottom of the hill sat the biggest pile of toys he had ever seen!

There were soft teddy bears. Colorful blocks tumbled everywhere. Red fire trucks and pink dolls covered the ground. A mountain of toys waited for him.

"Oh my!" said Benny. His bell jingled with worry. "How will I carry all these toys?"

The toy store owner, Mrs. Chen, smiled kindly. "The children are waiting, Benny. Can you help?"

Benny wanted to help. He always helped. But this pile looked so big and heavy.

"I can do it!" Benny said bravely. His whistle tooted with determination.

Mrs. Chen and her helpers loaded the toys. They filled every car. Teddy bears squeezed together. Blocks stacked high like towers. Dolls sat in neat rows.

The train cars looked ready to burst! Toys peeked out from every corner.

"Ready, Benny?" called Mrs. Chen.

Benny took a deep breath. His engine rumbled. Steam puffed from his smokestack. "Choo-choo-choo!"

He started up the hill. Push, push, push! His wheels turned slowly. The heavy load made everything harder.

Halfway up, Benny's engine began to wheeze. "Huff-puff-huff-puff!" His red wheels slipped on the tracks.

Suddenly, toys started falling! A teddy bear tumbled out. Then blocks scattered everywhere. Fire trucks rolled down the hill.

"Oh no!" cried Benny. He stopped before more toys fell. His bell drooped sadly.

Mrs. Chen ran to help. "Don't worry, Benny. We'll figure this out."

Benny felt terrible. The children needed their toys. But the load was too heavy for one little train.

"Maybe I'm not strong enough," Benny whispered. His whistle made a sad, quiet sound.

Mrs. Chen patted his side gently. "You are strong, Benny. Sometimes we just need to think differently."

She looked at all the toys. Then she looked at Benny's cars. Her face lit up with an idea.

"What if we make two trips?" she suggested.

Benny's headlight brightened. "Two trips?"

"Yes! Half the toys now. Half the toys later. You can do it twice!"

This sounded much better! Benny's bell jingled happily.

Mrs. Chen and her helpers took out half the toys. Now Benny's cars weren't so full. The teddy bears had room to sit. The blocks fit perfectly. The dolls could stretch out.

"This feels just right!" said Benny. His engine hummed with confidence.

Up the hill he went. Chug-chug-chug! His wheels gripped the tracks firmly. Steam puffed steadily from his smokestack.

This time, Benny made it to the top! The children cheered when they saw him coming. "Hooray for Benny!"

He delivered the first half of toys. The children hugged their new teddy bears. They built towers with the colorful blocks. Everyone smiled and laughed.

"I'll be right back!" Benny called cheerfully.

Down the hill he zoomed. Back to Mrs. Chen and the remaining toys. The second load went on easily.

Up the hill again! Chug-chug-chug! Benny felt proud and strong. His bell rang out a happy tune.

Soon, all the toys were delivered. Every child had something special to play with. The playground filled with joyful sounds.

"Thank you, Benny!" called the children. "You're the best train ever!"

Mrs. Chen smiled proudly. "You solved the problem, Benny. Sometimes the best solution is the simplest one."

Benny's whistle tooted with joy. He had learned something important today. Big problems could have simple answers. When something seemed too hard, he could break it into smaller pieces.

As the sun set, Benny rolled home happily. His red wheels sparkled in the golden light. Tomorrow would bring new adventures. But today, he had made lots of children very happy.

"Choo-choo-choo!" sang Benny. "I can do anything if I think it through!"

The end.
