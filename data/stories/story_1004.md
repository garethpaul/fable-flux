# Story story_1004

**Freddy's Magical Bath Time**

Freddy the frog sat on his lily pad. The sun was setting over the pond. Mama Frog called from their cozy home.

"Freddy! Bath time!"

Freddy's big green eyes grew wide. He did not like bath time. The water felt too warm. The soap made bubbles that tickled his nose.

"Do I have to, Mama?" Freddy asked.

"Yes, little one," Mama said gently. "You played in the mud all day. Your green skin needs a good wash."

Freddy hopped slowly to the bathroom. His webbed feet made soft plop sounds on the floor. The bathtub looked very big and scary.

Mama filled the tub with warm water. Steam rose up like tiny clouds. She added some special soap that smelled like water lilies.

"I don't want to get in," Freddy said. He backed away from the tub.

"What if we make it fun?" Mama asked. She reached into a basket. Out came three rubber ducks, a small boat, and a magic washcloth.

Freddy looked curious. "What makes the washcloth magic?"

"You'll see," Mama smiled. "But first, let's get you in the water."

Freddy took a tiny step forward. Then another. The warm water touched his toes. It felt nice and cozy.

"It's not so bad," he whispered.

Mama helped him into the tub. The water came up to his round belly. Freddy giggled as the warmth spread over his skin.

"Now for the magic washcloth," Mama said. She dipped it in the soapy water. "This washcloth can make rainbow bubbles!"

She squeezed the washcloth gently. Dozens of colorful bubbles floated up! Red bubbles, blue bubbles, yellow bubbles. They sparkled in the bathroom light.

"Wow!" Freddy gasped. He reached out to touch a purple bubble. Pop! It disappeared with a tiny splash.

"My turn!" Freddy said. He grabbed the magic washcloth. He squeezed it just like Mama did. More rainbow bubbles filled the air!

Freddy laughed and clapped his hands. The rubber ducks bobbed in the bubbly water. He made them swim in circles around his legs.

"Look, Mama! The ducks are having a race!"

The yellow duck bumped into Freddy's knee. The red duck spun around three times. The blue duck dove under a big soap bubble.

Freddy picked up the little boat. He blew gently on its white sail. The boat glided across the water like it was sailing on a real lake.

"I'm the captain!" Freddy announced. "We're sailing to Bubble Island!"

Mama laughed. "What do you find on Bubble Island?"

"Lots of friendly bubble creatures!" Freddy said. "They love to play and splash!"

The warm water felt wonderful on Freddy's skin. The sweet smell of water lily soap filled his nose. Rainbow bubbles danced all around him.

Mama washed behind Freddy's ears with the magic washcloth. More bubbles appeared! She gently scrubbed his back and arms. Freddy didn't mind at all.

"This is the best bath ever!" Freddy said.

He scooped up handfuls of bubbles. He blew them toward the ceiling. They floated like tiny balloons before popping.

When it was time to get out, Freddy felt sad. "Can I stay a little longer?"

"Tomorrow we'll have another magical bath," Mama promised. She wrapped Freddy in a soft, fluffy towel. It felt like a warm hug.

Freddy's skin was clean and shiny. He smelled like beautiful flowers. His green color looked bright and fresh.

"I love bath time now," Freddy said. He hugged Mama tight.

That night, Freddy dreamed about rainbow bubbles and sailing boats. He couldn't wait for tomorrow's bath time.

When morning came, Freddy played extra hard in the mud. He rolled and jumped and splashed. He got very, very dirty.

"Bath time tonight!" he told his friend Lily.

"Don't you hate baths?" Lily asked.

Freddy grinned his biggest smile. "Not anymore! Bath time is magical!"

He hopped home happily, already thinking about rainbow bubbles and floating boats. Bath time was now his favorite time of day.

The end.
