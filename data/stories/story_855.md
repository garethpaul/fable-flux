# Story story_855

Little Sam walked through the green meadow. His shoes made soft sounds in the grass. The sun felt warm on his face.

Sam kicked a small rock. It rolled away slowly. He picked up a stick and threw it far. Nobody was there to catch it.

"I wish I had someone to play with," Sam said quietly.

Behind the old wooden fence, a brown horse watched Sam. The horse's name was Buttercup. She had a white spot on her nose. Her tail swished back and forth.

Buttercup saw Sam sitting alone on a big rock. The little boy looked sad. His shoulders were down. His head hung low.

The gentle horse walked closer to the fence. Her hooves made clip-clop sounds on the ground. She made a soft neighing sound.

"Hello there," she whinnied sweetly.

Sam looked up with surprise. His eyes grew wide. He had never talked to a horse before.

"Did you just talk to me?" Sam asked.

"Yes, I did!" Buttercup said with a friendly smile. "You look lonely. Would you like to play?"

Sam jumped up from the rock. His face lit up with joy. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" Buttercup said. "I love to play games."

Sam ran to the fence. Buttercup lowered her big head down. Sam gently petted her soft nose. It felt like warm velvet.

"What games do you like?" Sam asked.

"I love to run and jump," Buttercup said. "Do you like those games too?"

"Yes!" Sam said excitedly. "But how can we play together?"

Buttercup thought for a moment. Her ears twitched as she had an idea.

"You can run along the fence," she said. "I will run with you on my side!"

Sam clapped his hands with glee. "That sounds wonderful!"

They lined up at one end of the fence. Sam counted to three. "One, two, three, go!"

Sam ran as fast as his little legs could go. Buttercup galloped beside him. Her mane flowed like silk in the wind. Sam laughed with pure happiness.

They reached the other end together. Both were breathing hard but smiling big.

"That was so much fun!" Sam giggled.

"Let's play hide and seek now," Buttercup suggested.

Sam covered his eyes and counted to ten. Buttercup hid behind a large apple tree. Her brown coat blended with the tree bark.

"Ready or not, here I come!" Sam called out.

He looked left and right. He checked behind rocks and bushes. Finally, he spotted Buttercup's white tail peeking out.

"Found you!" Sam shouted happily.

They played for hours in the sunny meadow. They played tag along the fence. They made up songs together. Buttercup taught Sam how horses say hello. Sam showed Buttercup how to play patty-cake with hooves.

The afternoon sun began to set. It painted the sky orange and pink. Sam's tummy started to rumble.

"I should go home for dinner," Sam said sadly.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" Buttercup asked hopefully.

"Every day!" Sam promised. "You're my best friend now."

Buttercup nuzzled Sam's cheek gently. "You're my best friend too, little Sam."

Sam gave Buttercup one last pat. He picked some sweet clover for her to eat. Buttercup munched it gratefully.

"Thank you for playing with me," Sam said. "I was so lonely before."

"Friends should never be lonely," Buttercup said wisely. "True friends always find each other."

Sam skipped home through the meadow. His heart felt light and happy. The grass seemed greener now. The flowers smelled sweeter.

The next morning, Sam ran straight to the meadow. Buttercup was waiting by the fence. She whinnied with joy when she saw him.

From that day on, Sam and Buttercup played together every day. They shared stories and dreams. They helped each other feel brave and strong.

The meadow became their special place. A place where a lonely boy found his very best friend. A place where friendship bloomed like wildflowers in spring.

And they lived happily ever after, playing in their beautiful meadow home.

The end.
