# Benny Bear's Big Bath Problem

Benny Bear sat at the big wooden table in the dining room. His brown fur was messy from playing outside all day. Mud covered his paws. Leaves stuck to his ears. 

"Time for your bath, Benny!" called Mama Bear from the kitchen.

Benny's eyes grew wide. He did not want a bath. Not today. Not ever! 

"No thank you, Mama," Benny said softly. He slid down in his chair. Maybe if he hid under the table, Mama would forget about bath time.

Mama Bear walked into the dining room. She smiled when she saw Benny's dirty paws on the clean table. "Oh my! Someone had fun in the garden today."

Benny peeked up from under the table. "I don't need a bath, Mama. Bears don't take baths in the forest."

"That's true," said Mama Bear. "But we live in a house. And dirty paws make everything messy."

Benny looked at the table. Brown paw prints covered the white tablecloth. He touched his face. His fur felt gritty and rough.

"But baths are cold and scary," Benny whispered.

Mama Bear sat down next to the table. "What if we made bath time more fun? What would help you feel better about it?"

Benny thought hard. His little bear brain worked on the problem. "Maybe... maybe if the water was warm like sunshine?"

"We can do that!" said Mama Bear. "What else?"

Benny's ears perked up. "Could I bring my yellow rubber duck? The one that squeaks?"

"Of course! Ducky loves baths."

Benny crawled out from under the table. He was starting to feel curious instead of scared. "Can we put bubbles in the water? Lots and lots of bubbles?"

Mama Bear clapped her paws together. "Bubbles make everything better! Should we make the bubbles smell like strawberries?"

"Yes!" Benny jumped up and down. "And can I wash my own paws? I'm a big bear now."

"You are very big," agreed Mama Bear. "You can wash your paws all by yourself."

Benny looked at his muddy fur again. Maybe taking a bath wouldn't be so bad after all. "Will you stay with me, Mama? In case I need help?"

"I'll stay right there the whole time," promised Mama Bear.

Hand in hand, they walked to the bathroom. Mama Bear turned on the warm water. It sounded like a gentle waterfall. She added strawberry bubble bath. Sweet-smelling bubbles grew tall and fluffy.

Benny dipped one paw in the water. "It feels like a warm hug!"

He climbed into the tub slowly. The warm water felt wonderful on his tired muscles. The bubbles tickled his nose and made him giggle.

"Squeak! Squeak!" went his rubber duck as it bobbed in the bubbles.

Benny splashed gently. He washed his paws with a soft cloth. The mud swirled away in brown spirals. His fur became clean and shiny again.

"Look, Mama! I can see my paws again!" Benny held up his clean, pink paw pads.

"You did such a good job washing yourself," said Mama Bear proudly.

Benny played with his duck for a few more minutes. The warm water made him feel sleepy and happy. When he was ready, Mama Bear wrapped him in a big, fluffy towel.

"That was the best bath ever!" said Benny as Mama Bear dried his fur. "Can we use strawberry bubbles tomorrow too?"

Mama Bear hugged her clean little bear. "Of course we can. You were very brave today, Benny. You tried something that scared you."

Benny snuggled into the soft towel. His fur smelled sweet and felt so soft. "I was scared at first. But talking about it helped me feel better."

"That's what we do when we have problems," said Mama Bear. "We talk about them and find solutions together."

As they walked back to the dining room, Benny felt proud of himself. He had been scared of bath time, but he didn't give up. He found ways to make it fun instead.

"Tomorrow I want to try green bubbles!" said Benny with a big, happy smile.

The end.
