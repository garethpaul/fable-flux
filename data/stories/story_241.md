# Benny's Big Cookie Problem

Benny was a fluffy brown dog with floppy ears and a wagging tail. He loved playing in the big sandbox at Sunny Park. The sand felt cool and soft between his paws.

"Woof! What a perfect day to dig!" Benny barked happily.

He was making the biggest sand castle ever. Scoop, pat, scoop, pat went his paws in the warm sand. The sun made everything sparkle like tiny diamonds.

Then Benny smelled something wonderful. *Sniff, sniff.* It was sweet and yummy. He looked up and saw a plate of chocolate chip cookies sitting on the picnic table.

"Oh my! Those cookies smell so good!" Benny said, licking his lips.

But there was a big problem. The picnic table was very, very tall. Benny was not very tall at all. He jumped up on his back legs. *Boing, boing!* But his nose barely reached the edge.

"I can't reach the cookies," Benny whimpered sadly. His tail drooped down like a sad flower.

But Benny was a smart dog. He wouldn't give up that easily.

"I know! I'll dig a big pile of sand!" he said. His tail started wagging again.

Benny dug and dug with his strong paws. *Swoosh, swoosh* went the sand. Soon he had a tall sand mountain. He climbed to the top and reached up. Still too short!

"Hmm," Benny thought, scratching behind his ear. "What else can I try?"

He spotted a red bucket near the sandbox. *Clank, clank* went the bucket as he pushed it with his nose. He flipped it upside down and jumped on top.

*Thump!* Up he went! But the bucket was wobbly. *Wobble, wobble* and down he tumbled into the soft sand.

"Oof!" Benny shook the sand from his fur. But he didn't feel sad. He felt determined.

"I won't give up!" he barked bravely.

Then Benny had his best idea yet. He would build a sand ramp! Like the ones at the dog park, but made of sand.

*Scoop, pat, scoop, pat.* Benny worked hard. The sand felt warm under the bright sun. Sweat dripped from his pink tongue, but he kept going.

He made the ramp higher and higher. Then he packed it down tight with his paws. *Pat, pat, pat.*

"This has to work!" Benny said, taking a deep breath.

Up the ramp he ran. His paws made soft *pitter-patter* sounds. Higher and higher he climbed. The cookies smelled even better now!

At the top of his sand ramp, Benny stretched his neck as far as he could. His nose touched the edge of the plate!

*Sniff, sniff.* So close! He stretched just a tiny bit more and... *GOTCHA!*

One cookie fell right into his mouth! It was warm and gooey and perfect. The chocolate chips melted on his tongue.

"Woof! I did it! I did it!" Benny danced happily on his ramp.

Just then, Sarah, his favorite girl, came running over.

"Oh Benny! You're so clever!" she laughed, clapping her hands. "You built the best ramp ever!"

Sarah picked up the plate and sat in the sand next to Benny. She broke a cookie in half and shared it with him.

"Good dogs who work hard deserve treats," she said, scratching behind his ears.

Benny's tail wagged so fast it made the sand fly. The cookie tasted even better because he had worked so hard for it.

As the sun began to set, Benny looked at his amazing sand ramp. He had learned something very important today.

"When something seems too hard, I just need to keep trying," he thought happily. "There's always a way if I don't give up!"

Benny curled up next to his sand castle, his belly full of cookies and his heart full of pride. Tomorrow he would build even bigger and better things in his favorite sandbox.

The end.
