# Leo the Brave Little Lion

In a sunny backyard filled with tall grass and colorful flowers, lived a little lion named Leo. His golden fur sparkled in the sunlight. His tiny mane was fluffy and soft. Leo loved playing outside all day long.

Leo would chase butterflies through the garden. He would pounce on fallen leaves. The crunchy sound made him giggle. He would roll in the warm dirt. Playing felt wonderful and safe.

But when the sun started to set, Leo felt worried. His tummy would flutter like butterflies. His paws would shake a little bit. Bedtime was coming, and Leo was scared.

"I don't want to go to sleep," Leo whispered. He hid behind the big oak tree. The shadows looked dark and scary. "What if I have bad dreams?"

Every evening, Leo's mama called from the house. "Leo, time for bed, sweetie!" But Leo would curl up in a tiny ball. He squeezed his eyes shut tight.

One evening, Leo decided to be brave. "I will solve this problem myself," he said. His voice was small but determined. Leo thought very hard about what to do.

First, Leo tried counting sheep. "One sheep, two sheep, three sheep," he whispered. But the sheep in his mind looked scary. They had big teeth and mean eyes. This idea didn't work at all.

Next, Leo tried singing a happy song. "La la la, I'm a brave lion!" But his voice sounded shaky and scared. The song made him feel worse. Leo's ears drooped down sadly.

Then Leo tried making his sleeping spot perfect. He gathered soft flower petals for his bed. He arranged them in a circle. The petals smelled sweet like honey. But he still felt frightened and alone.

Leo sat down and thought harder. "What makes me feel safe and happy?" he wondered. He remembered playing with his mama. He remembered her warm, rumbly purr. Her gentle voice always made him smile.

"Maybe I need to talk to Mama," Leo said. His whiskers twitched with a new idea. Leo walked slowly toward the house. His paws felt heavy with worry.

"Mama," Leo called softly. "I'm scared of bedtime. The dark feels too big. I don't know what to do."

Mama Lion came outside right away. Her eyes were kind and understanding. "Oh, my brave little cub," she said gently. "Being scared is okay sometimes."

Mama sat down beside Leo. She wrapped her warm, strong arms around him. "Let's make bedtime feel safer together," she suggested. Her voice was soft and soothing.

"What if we bring your favorite toy outside?" Mama asked. Leo's eyes lit up with excitement. His stuffed elephant was very special. It always made him feel better.

They brought Leo's elephant to the garden. Mama helped him make a cozy nest. They used soft blankets and fluffy pillows. The nest felt warm and secure.

"What if I stay with you?" Mama offered. "I can tell you a gentle story. We can look at the pretty stars." Leo's whole face brightened with happiness.

That night, Leo lay in his outdoor nest. Mama told him about brave lions. She pointed to twinkling stars above. The stars looked like tiny night lights. They made the darkness feel friendly.

Leo's elephant was soft against his cheek. Mama's voice was calm and peaceful. The night sounds became a gentle lullaby. Crickets chirped their sleepy songs.

"I'm not scared anymore," Leo whispered happily. His eyelids felt heavy and comfortable. "Bedtime can be nice and safe."

From that night on, Leo loved bedtime. He learned that asking for help was very brave. Solving problems together felt much better. His mama was always there when needed.

Leo discovered that persistence really worked. He tried many different solutions. He never gave up on feeling better. Now bedtime was his favorite time.

Every evening, Leo would smile big. He would gather his elephant and blanket. The backyard became his special sleeping place. Dreams came easily now, filled with sunshine and adventures.

Leo had become the bravest little lion ever.

The end.
