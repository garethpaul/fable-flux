# The Little Ball and Grandma Rose

In the back of a cozy closet, behind old coats and fuzzy slippers, sat a small red ball. The ball's name was Bounce, and he felt very lonely.

"I wish I had someone to play with," Bounce sighed sadly. He rolled back and forth by himself. The closet felt quiet and empty.

Bounce tried bouncing against the wall. *Thump, thump, thump.* But playing alone wasn't much fun. He rolled in circles until he felt dizzy. Still, no one came to play.

"Maybe I'll just stay here forever," Bounce whispered.

Just then, the closet door creaked open. Warm light spilled inside. A kind lady with silver hair peeked in.

"Hello there, little ball," said Grandma Rose gently. "What are you doing all alone?"

Bounce felt shy at first. "I'm just... rolling around," he said quietly.

Grandma Rose smiled warmly. "That looks like fun! May I join you?"

Bounce's eyes grew wide with surprise. "You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" Grandma Rose said cheerfully. "I love to play!"

She sat down carefully on the closet floor. Her eyes twinkled like stars.

"What games do you like?" asked Grandma Rose.

"I love to bounce and roll," Bounce said, feeling braver. "But it's more fun with friends."

"I have an idea!" Grandma Rose clapped her hands softly. "Let's play catch!"

She gently tossed Bounce into the air. He felt so happy! Up, up, up he went, then safely back to her hands.

"Wheee!" laughed Bounce. "This is wonderful!"

They played catch for a while. Bounce felt lighter than air. Grandma Rose giggled like a little girl.

"Your turn to choose a game," said Grandma Rose.

"Let's play roll-and-chase!" suggested Bounce excitedly.

Bounce rolled slowly across the closet floor. Grandma Rose crawled after him, making funny sounds. "I'm coming to get you!" she said playfully.

They both started laughing. The closet filled with joy and giggles.

"This is the best day ever!" said Bounce happily.

"Mine too," agreed Grandma Rose. "Playing together is much better than playing alone."

They discovered they both loved gentle games. They both enjoyed quiet, cozy spaces. And they both loved making new friends.

"Let's play hide-and-seek!" said Bounce.

Grandma Rose covered her eyes. "One, two, three..."

Bounce rolled behind a winter boot. He tried not to giggle.

"Ready or not, here I come!" called Grandma Rose.

She looked behind the coats. She peeked under a hat. Finally, she found Bounce behind the boot.

"Found you!" she said with delight.

"Now you hide!" said Bounce excitedly.

Grandma Rose squeezed behind a big coat. Bounce rolled around looking for her. When he found her, they both laughed and hugged.

"We make a great team," said Grandma Rose warmly.

"The very best team!" agreed Bounce.

As the afternoon sun grew golden, they played more games together. They rolled race cars down imaginary hills. They pretended to be explorers in the closet jungle.

Everything was more fun when they worked together. When Bounce got stuck behind a shoe, Grandma Rose helped him out. When Grandma Rose dropped her glasses, Bounce rolled them back to her.

"You're my very best friend," said Bounce happily.

"And you're mine," said Grandma Rose, giving him a gentle hug.

They decided to meet in the closet every day. It would be their special friendship place.

"Thank you for playing with me," said Bounce.

"Thank you for sharing your games," said Grandma Rose.

From that day on, the closet was never quiet again. It was filled with laughter, games, and the warm glow of friendship.

Bounce learned that cooperation made everything better. Playing together was much more fun than playing alone. And Grandma Rose learned that new friends can be found in the most unexpected places.

They lived happily ever after, proving that friendship makes every day brighter and every game more wonderful.
