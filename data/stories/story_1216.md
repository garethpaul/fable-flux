# Story story_1216

**The Muddy Baby**

Little Lily loved playing in Grandma's garden. The dirt felt soft under her tiny hands. The flowers smelled so sweet. The green leaves tickled her nose.

Today was extra fun. It had rained last night. The garden was full of wonderful mud puddles. Lily crawled from puddle to puddle. She splashed with her hands. She wiggled her toes in the squishy mud.

"Look at me!" Lily giggled. Brown mud covered her arms. Her face had dirty streaks. Her yellow dress was now brown and green.

Grandma came outside. "Oh my! What a muddy little one!" she said with a smile. "Time for a nice warm bath."

Lily shook her head. "No bath! No bath!" She loved being muddy. The dirt felt nice and cool. Why would she want to wash it off?

"But sweetie, you need to get clean," Grandma said gently.

Lily crawled away fast. She hid behind the big tomato plants. "No bath!" she called out.

Grandma had an idea. She brought out a small bucket of warm water. "Look, Lily! I brought the bath to you!"

Lily peeked out from the plants. The bucket looked scary and big. "No!" she said. She crawled deeper into the garden.

Grandma sat down on her garden stool. She thought and thought. How could she help Lily want to get clean?

Then Grandma smiled. She had a wonderful idea!

She brought out Lily's favorite bath toys. There was Rubber Ducky, yellow and squeaky. There was the purple boat that floated so well. There was the red cup for pouring water.

"Oh look!" Grandma said in a happy voice. "Rubber Ducky wants to play! But he can only play in clean water."

Lily's eyes got big. She loved Rubber Ducky so much.

Grandma filled a big, shallow basin with warm water. She put it right on the grass. The water sparkled in the sunshine. It looked inviting and fun.

"Rubber Ducky is lonely," Grandma said. She made the duck swim in circles. "Quack, quack! Where is Lily?" she made the duck say.

Lily crawled closer. The water looked pretty in the sunlight. Rubber Ducky looked so happy swimming around.

"The water is nice and warm," Grandma said. She dipped her hand in. "Just right for little ones."

Lily reached out one muddy finger. She touched the water. It felt good! Warm and gentle.

"More?" she asked softly.

"Of course!" Grandma said. She helped Lily sit in the shallow basin. The warm water felt wonderful on her skin.

Rubber Ducky swam over to say hello. "Quack! Hi Lily!" Grandma made him say.

Lily giggled. She splashed gently with her hands. The mud started washing away. She could see her clean pink skin underneath.

The purple boat floated by. Lily pushed it around the basin. The red cup was perfect for pouring water on her arms.

"This is fun!" Lily said. The warm water felt so nice. Her toys bobbed and floated around her.

Grandma used a soft cloth to gently wash Lily's face. "There's my beautiful granddaughter!" she said.

Soon Lily was all clean and pink. Her hair was soft and fluffy. She smelled like lavender soap instead of dirt.

"All done!" Grandma said. She wrapped Lily in a big, fluffy towel. It was warm from sitting in the sunshine.

Lily felt fresh and cozy. "Bath fun!" she said with a big smile.

"Yes, baths can be very fun," Grandma agreed. She gave Lily a gentle hug.

They sat together in the garden. Lily was clean and happy. She had learned something new today. Sometimes things that seem scary can actually be fun. You just need to try them in a new way.

"Tomorrow more garden?" Lily asked.

"Of course!" Grandma laughed. "And tomorrow more baths too!"

Lily clapped her hands. She couldn't wait for both.

The end.
