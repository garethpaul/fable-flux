# Penny the Plane's Lost Book

Penny was a bright red toy plane who lived on Tommy's bedroom shelf. She had shiny silver wings and a propeller that spun when the wind blew. Every night, Penny loved to read her special book about flying adventures.

"Where is my book?" Penny whispered one morning. She looked all around her spot on the shelf. Her favorite blue book with fluffy white clouds on the cover was nowhere to be seen!

Penny's propeller drooped sadly. That book told stories about brave planes flying over mountains and oceans. It helped her dream of wonderful adventures while Tommy slept.

"I must find my book," Penny said with determination. She carefully rolled to the edge of the shelf and looked down at the bedroom floor below.

First, Penny checked under Tommy's bed. She zoomed down and peeked in the dark, dusty space. She saw a red sock, three toy cars, and a crayon. But no blue book with clouds.

"Not here," she sighed, backing out with a little cough from the dust.

Next, Penny searched Tommy's toy box. She flew over the colorful pile of blocks, stuffed animals, and puzzle pieces. She moved teddy bears and looked behind building blocks. Still no special book.

"Where could it be?" Penny wondered, feeling worried. Her silver wings trembled a little.

Then Penny remembered Tommy's desk. Maybe her book fell behind it! She zoomed over and squeezed into the narrow space between the desk and the wall. It was dark and cramped.

"Ouch!" Penny bumped her wing on the desk leg. But she kept looking. She found pencils, paper clips, and an old eraser. No book anywhere.

Penny flew back to her shelf feeling very sad. Her propeller barely spun at all now. She had looked everywhere she could think of.

"Maybe I'll never find my book," she whispered, a tiny tear rolling down her red nose.

Just then, Tommy's little sister Emma toddled into the room. She was carrying something blue and fluffy-looking.

"Pwane book!" Emma giggled, holding up Penny's special book. "Emma wead!"

Penny's propeller started spinning with excitement. "That's my book!" she wanted to shout, but toy planes can't talk to children.

Emma climbed onto Tommy's bed and opened the book. She pointed at the pictures of planes flying through puffy white clouds. "Zoom, zoom!" she said happily.

When Emma finished looking at the pictures, she carefully placed the book back on Tommy's shelf, right next to Penny.

"Thank you, Emma!" Penny whispered gratefully. Her propeller spun so fast it made a happy humming sound.

That evening, as moonlight streamed through the bedroom window, Penny snuggled up with her precious book. She opened to her favorite page showing a red plane just like her flying over a beautiful rainbow.

"I never gave up looking," Penny said proudly to herself. "Even when I felt sad and worried, I kept trying new places to search."

She thought about how she had checked under the bed, in the toy box, and behind the desk. Each place taught her something new about being brave and not giving up.

"Sometimes the answer comes when you least expect it," Penny realized. "Emma found my book and brought it back to me!"

As Penny read her favorite flying stories, her silver wings gleamed in the moonlight. She felt warm and happy inside. Tomorrow she would dream of soaring through cotton candy clouds and dancing with rainbow butterflies.

"I learned that when something important is lost, I should keep looking and never give up hope," Penny whispered sleepily. "Good things happen to planes who don't quit!"

With her special book safe beside her, Penny closed her eyes and dreamed of the most wonderful flying adventures. Her propeller gave one last gentle spin before she drifted off to sleep, feeling proud of her persistence and grateful for unexpected help.

The bedroom was peaceful and quiet, and Penny the plane slept soundly, knowing that tomorrow would bring new adventures and that her special book would always be there to inspire her dreams.

The end.
