# Story story_552

**The Best of Friends**

Pip the mouse lived in a tiny hole near the big oak tree. Every morning, she would peek out and look around the sunny backyard. The grass felt soft under her small paws. The flowers smelled sweet and pretty.

But Pip always played alone. She would chase dandelion seeds in the wind. She would climb on smooth rocks. She would nibble on fallen berries. Playing by herself made her feel sad and lonely.

"I wish I had a friend," Pip whispered softly.

One bright morning, Pip heard heavy footsteps. THUMP, THUMP, THUMP! The ground shook a little bit. A big gray elephant walked into the backyard. His name was Eddie.

Eddie had kind brown eyes. His trunk swayed gently as he walked. He looked around the pretty garden. Then he saw tiny Pip sitting on a rock.

"Hello there, little mouse," Eddie said in a deep, warm voice. "I'm Eddie. What's your name?"

Pip felt scared at first. Eddie was so very big! But his voice sounded nice and friendly.

"I'm Pip," she squeaked quietly. "You're really, really big!"

Eddie laughed softly. The sound rumbled like gentle thunder. "Yes, I am big. But I'm also very gentle. Would you like to play with me?"

Pip's whiskers twitched with excitement. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" Eddie smiled. "Friends come in all sizes."

But Pip worried. "How can we play together? You're huge and I'm tiny."

Eddie thought for a moment. His big ears flapped slowly. "Let's try different games. We'll find something fun!"

First, they tried playing catch. Eddie tossed a small pebble with his trunk. But it was too heavy for Pip to throw back. The pebble made a loud THUNK when it hit the ground.

"That didn't work," Pip sighed.

"Don't give up," Eddie said kindly. "Let's try something else."

Next, they tried hide and seek. Pip hid behind a tiny flower. Eddie closed his eyes and counted to ten. But when he looked, he couldn't find her anywhere!

"Where are you, Pip?" Eddie called out.

"I'm right here!" Pip giggled from behind the yellow daisy.

Eddie squinted his big eyes. "I still can't see you!"

Pip realized Eddie was too big to see such small hiding spots.

"This is hard too," Pip said sadly.

But Eddie had another idea. "What if we play follow the leader?"

Eddie walked very slowly around the garden. His big feet made soft thumps on the grass. Pip ran alongside him. Her tiny paws made no sound at all.

They walked around the rose bushes. The roses smelled like sweet perfume. They walked past the bird bath. The water sparkled in the sunshine. They walked under the apple tree. The leaves rustled softly above them.

"This is fun!" Pip squeaked happily.

Then Eddie had the best idea yet. "Climb on my back, Pip! I'll give you a ride."

Pip scampered up Eddie's trunk. She held on tight to his big ears. Eddie walked around the whole backyard. Pip could see everything from up high!

She saw the neighbor's cat sleeping. She saw butterflies dancing on flowers. She saw birds building nests in the trees.

"Wow!" Pip laughed. "I can see the whole world!"

Eddie trumpeted happily. The sound was loud but joyful.

From that day on, Pip and Eddie played together every morning. Eddie would give Pip rides on his back. Pip would tell Eddie about all the tiny things she discovered in the grass.

They shared sweet berries for snacks. Eddie would pick high fruit with his trunk. Pip would find the juiciest berries hiding under leaves.

"Thank you for being my friend," Pip said one sunny afternoon.

"Thank you for being mine too," Eddie replied. "You're the best friend ever."

Pip snuggled against Eddie's warm, gray side. She wasn't lonely anymore. She had found the most wonderful friend in the whole wide world.

Sometimes the best friendships happen between the most different friends. And that made their friendship extra special.

The end.
