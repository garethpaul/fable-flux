# Story story_1408

Jack loved her bright red ball. It bounced high and felt smooth in her hands. Every day at the playground, she threw it up to the sky. She caught it with a big smile.

Today was sunny and warm. Jack ran to the swings first. She put her red ball down carefully. The soft grass tickled her feet. Birds sang happy songs in the tall trees.

"Push me higher!" Jack called to her mom. Up and down she went. The wind blew through her hair. She felt like she was flying.

After swinging, Jack wanted her ball. She looked at the spot where she left it. The ball was gone! Jack's tummy felt funny. Her eyes got big and round.

"Mom! My red ball is missing!" Jack said. Her voice was shaky. She looked under the swing. No ball there.

Mom came over quickly. "Don't worry, sweetie. Let's look together." Mom's voice was calm and gentle.

Jack searched behind the slide. She moved her hands through the cool sand. Still no red ball. She checked near the monkey bars. Nothing there either.

"Maybe it rolled away," Mom said softly. Jack felt tears in her eyes. That ball was her very favorite thing.

Then Jack had an idea. "I'll climb the big slide!" she said. "I can see everything from up there."

Jack climbed the ladder slowly. Step by step she went up. The metal felt warm from the sun. At the top, she looked all around the playground.

She saw the swings moving gently. She saw kids playing tag. She saw the sandbox full of toys. But no red ball anywhere.

Jack slid down with a whoosh. The slide was fast and fun. But she still felt sad inside. Where could her ball be?

"Let's try something else," Mom said. "What if we ask other kids? Maybe someone saw your ball."

Jack felt a little scared. Talking to new kids was hard sometimes. But she really wanted her ball back.

They walked to the sandbox together. Three kids were building a tall castle. Sand stuck to their fingers.

"Excuse me," Jack said quietly. "Did you see a red ball? It's round and bouncy."

A girl with curly hair looked up. "Yes! I saw it roll down the hill." She pointed toward the trees. "It went that way fast."

Jack's heart jumped with hope. "Thank you!" she said with a big smile.

Jack and Mom walked down the grassy hill. The ground felt bumpy under their feet. Leaves crunched as they stepped.

Near a big oak tree, something red caught Jack's eye. There it was! Her special ball sat between two roots.

"My ball!" Jack shouted with joy. She ran over and picked it up. The ball felt perfect in her hands again. Warm and smooth and wonderful.

Jack hugged her ball tight. "I found you!" she whispered to it. She felt happy all over.

"You didn't give up," Mom said proudly. "You kept trying different ways to solve the problem."

Jack bounced her ball on the soft grass. Bounce, bounce, bounce. It sounded just right.

"First I looked by myself," Jack said. "Then I climbed up high to see better. Then I asked for help."

"That's called persistence," Mom explained. "It means you keep trying even when things are hard."

Jack threw her ball up toward the blue sky. She caught it perfectly. The sun felt warm on her face.

"Can we play catch now?" Jack asked. Her smile was bigger than ever.

"Of course!" Mom said. They tossed the red ball back and forth. It flew through the air like a happy bird.

Jack learned something important that day. When you lose something special, don't give up. Try looking in new places. Ask friends for help. Keep trying until you find what you're looking for.

The red ball bounced and bounced. Jack's laughter filled the playground. Everything was perfect again.

The end.
