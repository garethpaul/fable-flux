# Story story_1409

**The Magic of Art Time**

The big clock on the wall said three o'clock. "Art time!" called Miss Sarah with a bright smile. All the children clapped their hands. They loved art time at Rainbow Preschool.

But Blocks felt different today. Blocks was a little boy who loved building towers. He loved stacking things up high. He loved making bridges and castles. But art time meant painting and drawing. Blocks wrinkled his nose.

"I don't want to paint today," Blocks whispered to his friend Maya. "I want to play with my blocks instead."

Maya patted his arm gently. "Maybe it will be fun," she said. "You never know!"

Miss Sarah put out big sheets of white paper. She opened jars of bright paint. Red like apples. Blue like the sky. Yellow like sunshine. The sweet smell of paint filled the air.

"Today we're painting our favorite things," Miss Sarah announced. "What makes you happy?"

Blocks sat at the art table. He picked up a fat paintbrush. It felt strange in his small fingers. Not like his smooth wooden blocks at all. The brush was soft and fuzzy.

He dipped it in red paint. Drip, drop. A blob landed on his paper. "Oops," he said quietly.

Then something magical happened. The red blob looked just like a block! Blocks smiled a tiny smile. He dipped his brush in blue paint. He made another block shape next to the red one.

"Look!" he said to Maya. "I'm painting blocks!"

His paintbrush danced across the paper. Blue blocks. Yellow blocks. Green blocks too. Soon his paper was full of colorful block towers. They looked just like the ones he built every day.

"This is amazing, Blocks!" said Miss Sarah. She knelt down beside him. "Tell me about your painting."

"These are my favorite blocks," Blocks explained proudly. "This red one goes on top. The blue ones make a strong base. And look! I painted my castle too!"

Other children gathered around his table. "Wow!" said Emma. "Those look so real!"

"Can you teach me to paint blocks?" asked Sam.

Blocks felt warm and happy inside. His cheeks turned pink with pride. "Sure!" he said. "First you make a square like this."

He showed Sam how to paint block shapes. They worked together, laughing and sharing colors. The paintbrushes made soft swooshing sounds on the paper.

Soon Blocks had painted a whole block city. There were tall towers reaching up to the sky. There were bridges connecting different buildings. There were even tiny block people walking around.

"I love how you used art to show your favorite things," Miss Sarah said. "You turned your love of blocks into beautiful art."

Blocks looked at his colorful painting. It was covered in every color of the rainbow. His hands were messy with paint. But he felt so proud and happy.

"I never knew painting could be like building," he said. "But with colors instead of real blocks!"

When art time ended, Blocks carefully carried his painting to the drying rack. He couldn't wait to show his mom and dad. He couldn't wait to hang it on the refrigerator at home.

"That was the best art time ever," he told Maya as they washed their hands. The warm soapy water felt good on his paint-covered fingers.

"See? I told you it might be fun," Maya giggled.

As they cleaned up the art supplies, Blocks felt excited inside. His tummy had happy butterflies. Tomorrow was another day at school. Tomorrow meant another art time.

"Miss Sarah," Blocks called out. "What are we painting tomorrow?"

"It's a surprise," she said with a wink. "But I think you'll love it."

Blocks grinned from ear to ear. He couldn't wait to see what magical things would happen with paint and brushes tomorrow. Maybe he would paint his block friends. Maybe he would paint a rainbow castle.

Art time wasn't scary anymore. It was another way to build and create. And that made Blocks very, very happy.

The end.
