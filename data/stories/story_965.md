# Story story_965

**Woolly's Garden Adventure**

The morning sun painted the sky yellow and pink. Farmer Mae rang the garden bell. "Garden time!" she called cheerfully.

All the farm animals loved garden time. The chickens clucked with excitement. The rabbits hopped in circles. But Woolly the sheep just sighed.

"I don't want to help today," Woolly mumbled. His fluffy white wool felt heavy. His legs felt tired already.

Garden work seemed hard and boring. Pulling weeds made his hooves dirty. Carrying water buckets was heavy work. The sun made him hot and sleepy.

"Come on, Woolly!" called his friend Buttercup the cow. "The vegetables need our help!"

Woolly slowly walked to the garden. He dragged his hooves in the dirt. Maybe he could hide behind the big tomato plants.

Farmer Mae smiled at Woolly. "Would you like to water the carrots today?" she asked kindly.

Woolly shook his head. "Watering is too hard," he said softly.

"How about picking strawberries?" suggested Farmer Mae.

"My wool might get stuck," Woolly worried.

Farmer Mae thought for a moment. Then she had a wonderful idea. "Woolly, would you help me plant these special seeds?"

She showed him a small packet. The seeds sparkled like tiny stars. They felt warm in Woolly's hoof.

"What makes them special?" Woolly asked curiously.

"These are rainbow flower seeds," whispered Farmer Mae. "They only grow when planted with kindness."

Woolly's eyes grew wide. Rainbow flowers sounded magical! Maybe garden work wasn't so boring after all.

He carefully dug small holes in the soft earth. The dirt felt cool and crumbly. It smelled like rain and growing things.

Woolly placed each seed gently in its hole. He whispered kind words to them. "Grow big and strong, little seeds."

Then something amazing happened! Tiny green shoots popped up immediately. The shoots grew taller and taller. Colorful buds appeared like magic.

"Wow!" gasped Woolly. His wool bounced with excitement.

The buds opened into beautiful rainbow flowers. Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple petals danced in the breeze. They smelled like honey and sunshine.

"They're gorgeous!" Woolly exclaimed happily.

Butterflies came to visit the rainbow flowers. Bees hummed cheerful songs. The whole garden felt more alive and joyful.

Woolly felt proud and happy. He had helped create something beautiful! Garden work wasn't boring at all.

"Can I water the carrots now?" Woolly asked eagerly.

Farmer Mae handed him a small watering can. The water sparkled as it fell on the green carrot tops. The plants seemed to dance with joy.

Next, Woolly helped pick ripe strawberries. They were red and sweet and perfect. He only ate two or three. Maybe four.

He pulled weeds carefully around the lettuce. The baby plants had more room to grow. Everything looked neat and tidy.

Woolly even helped carry water buckets. They weren't too heavy when he took his time. The cool water splashed gently.

By lunch time, Woolly felt wonderful. His wool was a little dirty but his heart was full. The garden looked beautiful and healthy.

"Thank you for your help, Woolly," said Farmer Mae. "The plants are so happy!"

Woolly looked at his rainbow flowers. They swayed in the gentle breeze like they were saying hello.

That evening, Woolly told all his friends about the magical day. "Garden time is actually fun!" he said with surprise.

The next morning, Woolly woke up early. He heard Farmer Mae ring the garden bell. This time, Woolly jumped up quickly.

"Garden time!" he called out happily.

He ran to the garden with bouncing steps. His rainbow flowers had grown even bigger overnight. They seemed to smile at him.

Woolly couldn't wait to plant more seeds. Maybe he could grow rainbow vegetables too! Or singing sunflowers! Or dancing daisies!

Garden time had become his favorite time of day. Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough.

The end.
