# Penny's Colorful Sky

Penny the paper plane sat on the kitchen table. She watched the morning sun peek through the window. "Art time!" called Mom from across the room.

Penny's wings drooped a little. Art time meant sitting still. Art time meant no flying around. She loved to zoom and swoop. She loved to dance in the air.

"Come on, Penny," Mom said gently. "Let's make something beautiful together."

Penny floated slowly to the art corner. Crayons lay scattered like tiny rainbows. Paper waited, white and empty. Paintbrushes stood ready in their jar.

"I don't know what to make," Penny sighed. Her paper felt crinkled with worry.

Mom smiled and picked up a blue crayon. "What do you see when you fly?" she asked.

Penny thought about her favorite flight. "I see clouds," she whispered. "Fluffy white clouds."

"Show me," Mom said softly.

Penny picked up the white crayon. She made small, puffy circles. The clouds looked like cotton balls. They looked like her grandmother's soft hair.

"What else do you see?" Mom asked.

"Birds!" Penny said, feeling warmer. She drew tiny V-shapes flying between clouds. The birds looked happy and free.

Something magical began to happen. The white paper became Penny's sky. Her sky from way up high. The crayons felt smooth in her grip.

"And the sun?" Mom suggested.

Penny grabbed the yellow crayon. She made a big, bright circle. She added lines all around it. The sun seemed to shine right off the paper.

"Oh!" Penny gasped. "I can see my whole world!"

Her excitement grew like a bubble. She reached for the green crayon. She drew the treetops she saw below. Tiny green circles dotted her picture.

The blue crayon made a winding river. The brown crayon made little houses. The red crayon made flowers in tiny gardens.

"This is where I fly every day!" Penny laughed. Her whole body wiggled with joy.

Mom picked up a purple crayon. "May I add something?" she asked.

Penny nodded eagerly. Mom drew a small paper plane. Right in the middle of Penny's sky. Right where Penny loved to fly most.

"That's me!" Penny squealed. "That's really me!"

She looked at her picture again. Every crayon mark told a story. Every color showed a memory. Her daily flights lived right there on paper.

Penny added pink clouds for sunset. She added orange leaves falling down. She added silver raindrops for stormy days.

"Art time lets me keep my adventures," Penny realized. "Even when I'm not flying."

She drew tiny ants on the ground. She drew her friend Robin the bird. She drew the wind with swirly lines.

The paper was full of color now. Full of life and movement. Full of all the things that made Penny happy.

"Tomorrow we could paint," Mom suggested. "Paint makes colors blend together."

Penny's wings fluttered with excitement. "Could we paint my sunset flight? When the sky turns pink and orange?"

"Of course," Mom said. "We could paint any flight you want."

Penny looked at her finished picture. She had captured her whole world. Her flying world lived in crayon colors now.

"I love art time," Penny said, surprising herself. "It's like flying, but different."

"How is it like flying?" Mom asked.

Penny thought carefully. "When I fly, I feel free. When I make art, my thoughts feel free."

Mom hugged Penny gently. "That's beautiful, sweetie."

Penny carefully placed her picture on the refrigerator. Every time she walked by, she could see her sky. She could remember every wonderful flight.

"Can we do art time again tomorrow?" Penny asked.

"Every day if you want," Mom promised.

Penny smiled as big as her paper wings allowed. Tomorrow she would paint. She would paint her sunset sky. She would paint the clouds turning purple and gold.

Art time wasn't about sitting still anymore. Art time was about catching magic. About keeping adventures safe on paper.

Penny couldn't wait for tomorrow's art time. She had so many skies to create.

The end.
