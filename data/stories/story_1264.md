# Story story_1264

**The Little Red Truck and the Farm Games**

Big Red was a little red truck who lived on Sunny Meadow Farm. Every morning, he helped Farmer Joe carry hay bales to the barn. Big Red loved his shiny red paint and his strong, bumpy tires.

One sunny afternoon, Big Red heard happy sounds coming from the pasture. He rolled over to see what was happening. The farm animals were playing a game called "Follow the Leader."

Moo the cow was in front. Behind her walked Oink the pig, then Baa the sheep, and Cluck the chicken. They marched around the apple tree, through the tall grass, and over the small wooden bridge.

"That looks so fun!" Big Red said to himself. His engine made a hopeful purring sound.

Big Red rolled closer to the animals. "Can I play Follow the Leader too?" he asked with his friendliest beep-beep.

Moo looked at Big Red's big wheels. "Oh dear," she said gently. "You're too heavy for our wooden bridge. It might break."

Oink nodded his pink snout. "And you're too wide to fit through our favorite hiding spot by the fence."

Big Red's headlights dimmed with sadness. His red paint seemed less shiny now. He watched the animals continue their game without him.

"I really want to play," Big Red whispered. A tear of motor oil rolled down his bumper.

Big Red had an idea for his first try. "Maybe I can make myself smaller!" he thought. He tried to scrunch down low. He pulled in his side mirrors. He made his engine very quiet.

"Now can I play?" Big Red asked hopefully.

But he was still too big and heavy. When he tried to cross the wooden bridge, it creaked and groaned. The animals looked worried.

"Sorry, Big Red," said Baa kindly. "It's still not safe."

Big Red felt even sadder. His wipers drooped down like sad eyebrows. But then he remembered what Farmer Joe always said: "When one way doesn't work, try another way!"

Big Red thought harder. His engine hummed with concentration. Then his headlights brightened with a wonderful new idea.

"What if I don't follow you?" Big Red called out. "What if I make my own path and you follow me instead?"

The animals looked curious. Cluck tilted her feathered head. "What do you mean?"

Big Red's engine rumbled with excitement. "I can make a new game! I'll drive on the dirt road around the pasture. You can follow me there! The road is strong enough for everyone."

Moo's eyes sparkled. "That sounds wonderful!"

Big Red led the way on the smooth dirt road. His wheels crunched happily on the gravel. Behind him, the animals walked in a line. Moo's hooves clip-clopped. Oink's feet made soft patting sounds. Baa's wool bounced as she trotted. Cluck's claws scratched the ground in a cheerful rhythm.

They passed the red barn with its sweet smell of hay. They rolled by the vegetable garden where tomatoes grew fat and round. They went around the duck pond where the water sparkled like diamonds.

"This is the best Follow the Leader game ever!" squealed Oink.

"We can see the whole farm from here!" added Baa.

Big Red's horn tooted with joy. His red paint gleamed in the sunshine. He felt proud and happy.

When the game ended, all the animals gathered around Big Red.

"Thank you for finding a way we could all play together," said Moo.

"You didn't give up," said Cluck. "You kept trying until you found the perfect solution."

Big Red's engine purred with happiness. "I learned something important today. When something doesn't work the first way, I can always try a different way."

From that day on, Big Red led the Follow the Leader game every afternoon. All the farm animals joined in, walking safely on the dirt road while Big Red showed them new and exciting paths around Sunny Meadow Farm.

And Big Red never felt left out again.

The end.
