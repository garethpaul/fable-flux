# Story story_1009

**Penny the Puppet's Lost Doll**

Penny the puppet lived in a cozy nursery. She had soft yarn hair and a bright red dress. Every day, she played with her special doll named Rosie.

Rosie had golden curls and a pink dress. Penny loved Rosie very much. They had tea parties together. They sang songs and danced.

One sunny morning, Penny woke up. She looked around her toy box. "Where is Rosie?" she asked. Penny's button eyes grew wide.

She searched under her blanket. No Rosie. She looked behind the books. Still no Rosie. Penny felt sad and worried.

"Oh no!" Penny cried. "My pretty doll is gone!" A tear rolled down her fabric cheek. The nursery felt empty without Rosie.

Penny thought hard. "Maybe Rosie fell somewhere," she said. She decided to look everywhere. Penny was determined to find her friend.

First, Penny climbed onto the rocking horse. She could see the whole room from up high. The wooden horse creaked gently as she looked around.

Penny spotted something pink under the big chair. "That must be Rosie!" she said excitedly. She slid down and crawled under the chair.

But it was only a pink sock. Penny felt disappointed. The sock was soft but it wasn't her doll. She sighed and kept looking.

Next, Penny checked the toy chest. She moved teddy bears and blocks. She pushed aside toy cars and puzzles. Everything smelled like cedar wood.

"Rosie? Are you in here?" Penny called softly. She heard her voice echo in the chest. But no answer came back.

Penny climbed out of the toy chest. She felt tired and sad. Her yarn hair was messy now. Maybe she would never find Rosie.

Then Penny had another idea. "What if Rosie is hiding?" she wondered. Sometimes they played hide-and-seek together.

Penny closed her eyes and thought. Where would Rosie like to hide? She remembered their favorite game. Rosie always hid in cozy, warm places.

"The dollhouse!" Penny said suddenly. Her button eyes sparkled with hope. The little dollhouse sat in the corner. It had tiny rooms and furniture.

Penny hurried over to the dollhouse. She carefully lifted the roof. Inside, she could see the miniature kitchen and bedroom.

And there, sleeping in the tiny bed, was Rosie! Her golden curls spread across the small pillow. Her pink dress looked perfect.

"Rosie!" Penny called happily. "I found you!" She gently picked up her doll. Rosie felt warm and soft in her arms.

Rosie's painted eyes seemed to smile. "I was taking a nap," Rosie seemed to say. "The little bed was so comfortable."

Penny hugged Rosie tight. She spun around the nursery with joy. The morning sunlight made everything look bright and cheerful.

"I was so worried," Penny told her doll. "But I didn't give up. I kept looking and looking." She felt proud of herself.

Penny learned something important that day. When something goes wrong, you shouldn't stop trying. If one idea doesn't work, try another idea.

"Next time, tell me if you want to nap," Penny said to Rosie. They both giggled together. The sound filled the happy nursery.

Penny and Rosie had their tea party after all. They used tiny cups and pretend cookies. Everything tasted wonderful because they were together again.

As the day ended, Penny tucked Rosie into her own toy box. "Sleep here tonight," she said gently. "So I can find you easily tomorrow."

The two friends settled down for the night. The nursery was peaceful and quiet. Stars twinkled outside the window.

Penny felt happy and sleepy. She had solved her problem all by herself. Tomorrow would bring new adventures with Rosie.

And they all lived happily in their cozy nursery, playing together every single day.

The end.
