# Story story_1249

**Too Many Toys**

Maya loved playing at the big playground. The swings creaked in the wind. The slide sparkled in the sunshine. Her favorite spot was the sandbox.

Today, Maya brought her special toy bag. Inside were her red truck, blue shovel, yellow bucket, and green dinosaur. She also had her purple ball and orange blocks.

"I want to play with everything!" Maya said happily.

She dumped all her toys in the sand. The red truck rolled away. The purple ball bounced toward the swings. The orange blocks scattered everywhere.

Maya tried to pick them up. She grabbed the truck in one hand. She held the shovel in the other hand. She tucked the bucket under her arm.

"Oh no!" she cried.

The dinosaur fell from her fingers. The ball rolled away again. The blocks tumbled down.

Maya's face felt hot and frustrated. Her arms were too small. Her hands couldn't hold everything at once.

"This is too hard," Maya whispered.

She sat down in the warm sand. Tears made her cheeks wet. The toys were spread all around her.

Then Maya had an idea. Her eyes lit up like stars.

"Maybe I can use my bucket!" she said.

Maya picked up the yellow bucket. She put the green dinosaur inside. Then she added the orange blocks. The purple ball fit too.

She lifted the bucket with both hands. It was very heavy. Maya took one step. Then another step.

CRASH!

The bucket tipped over. All the toys spilled out again. The dinosaur landed on her toe.

"Ow!" Maya said. She felt like crying again.

Maya looked around the playground. Other kids were playing happily. The sun felt warm on her back. Birds were singing in the trees.

"I won't give up," Maya said bravely.

She thought very hard. Her forehead wrinkled with concentration. Then she smiled a big, bright smile.

"I know what to do!" she exclaimed.

Maya made two trips instead of one. First, she put some toys in the bucket. The dinosaur, the ball, and two blocks fit perfectly.

She carried the bucket to her favorite sandbox corner. It felt just right in her hands. Not too heavy at all.

"One trip done!" Maya said proudly.

Then she went back for the other toys. The truck, shovel, and remaining blocks were easy to carry. Her arms felt strong and confident.

Soon all her toys were together in one spot. Maya clapped her hands with joy. The sound echoed across the playground.

"I did it! I did it!" she sang.

Maya played for hours in the sunshine. She built sand castles with her shovel. Her truck carried loads of sand. The dinosaur guarded the castle walls.

When it was time to go home, Maya packed up easily. She made two trips to her mom's car. Each trip felt simple and fun.

"How did you carry all those toys?" asked her mom.

"I made two trips!" Maya said proudly. "Sometimes the best way is the smart way."

Her mom gave her a warm hug. "You're such a good problem solver."

Maya smiled as they drove home. The car windows were down. Cool air blew through her hair. She felt happy and proud inside.

At dinner, Maya told her dad about her day. "First I tried to carry everything at once," she explained. "That didn't work. Then I tried my bucket, but it was too heavy. Finally, I made two trips. That worked perfectly!"

"You learned something important today," said Dad. "Sometimes we need to try different ways to solve problems."

Maya nodded wisely. "And it's okay if the first way doesn't work. I can always try again."

That night, Maya dreamed about the playground. In her dream, she solved puzzles and helped other kids with their problems. She felt brave and smart and happy.

The next morning, Maya couldn't wait to go back to the playground. She had learned that with patience and clever thinking, she could handle any challenge.

"Two trips are better than one messy trip," Maya said, packing her toy bag carefully.

The end.
