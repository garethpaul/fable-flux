# Story story_631

**The Lost Blocks**

Bouncy the red ball lived in a cozy attic. Dust danced in the warm sunlight. Old boxes sat everywhere like tiny houses.

Bouncy loved to play with his wooden blocks. Every morning, he would roll over to find them. Today felt different.

"Where are my blocks?" Bouncy wondered aloud.

He rolled left. No blocks there. He rolled right. Still no blocks. His favorite toys were missing!

Bouncy felt worried. His smooth surface grew warm. Playing with blocks made him so happy. He loved stacking them high. He loved knocking them down with a gentle bump.

"I must find them," Bouncy said firmly.

First, Bouncy decided to look under things. He rolled under the old rocking chair. Dust tickles made him sneeze. "Achoo!" But no blocks were there.

He squeezed under a heavy trunk. It was dark and musty. A spider said hello, but Bouncy only found old newspapers. The papers crinkled loudly under his weight.

Bouncy rolled under every piece of furniture. Under the dresser. Under the small table. Under the toy chest. No blocks anywhere.

"This isn't working," Bouncy sighed sadly.

His red color seemed less bright. He felt tired from all the rolling. Maybe the blocks were gone forever.

Then Bouncy had a new idea. Maybe the blocks were up high instead of down low!

"I need to look up," he said with new hope.

But how could a ball look up high? Bouncy thought hard. Then he remembered something important. Balls can bounce!

Bouncy rolled to the middle of the attic. He took a deep breath. Then he bounced as high as he could. Boing! Up he went!

From up high, he could see the top of the bookshelf. Nothing there. He bounced again. Boing! This time he saw the top of the wardrobe. Still nothing.

Bouncy kept bouncing. Each bounce made a happy sound on the wooden floor. Boing! Boing! Boing!

On his biggest bounce yet, Bouncy saw something wonderful. Behind the tall mirror, something colorful peeked out. Could it be?

"My blocks!" Bouncy shouted with joy.

But the blocks were stuck behind the heavy mirror. How could he reach them?

Bouncy thought carefully. He rolled to the wall. Then he bounced toward the mirror. Not too hard – he didn't want to break anything.

Bounce! The mirror wiggled just a little. Bounce! It wiggled more. Bounce! The mirror slid sideways.

There they were! All twelve wooden blocks sat in a neat pile. Red ones, blue ones, yellow ones, and green ones. They looked perfect in the dusty sunlight.

"I found you!" Bouncy laughed happily.

The blocks seemed to smile back at him. "We missed you too," they seemed to say.

Bouncy gently pushed the blocks into the open space. Now they could play together again! He rolled around them in a happy circle.

First, he helped stack them into a tall tower. The blocks felt smooth and warm. They smelled like old wood and sunshine. When the tower was finished, Bouncy backed up.

Then – bump! Down came the blocks with a cheerful clatter. They scattered across the floor like colorful confetti.

"That was fun!" Bouncy giggled.

He helped stack them again. This time they built a long wall. Then a small house. Then a bridge for Bouncy to roll under.

As the sun began to set, Bouncy felt very proud. He had solved his problem all by himself. When the first way didn't work, he tried another way.

"I didn't give up," Bouncy said happily.

The blocks agreed. They were proud of their friend too.

That night, Bouncy made sure to keep his blocks close by. He would never lose them again. And if he did, he knew what to do. Try one way. If that doesn't work, try another way.

In the quiet attic, Bouncy dreamed of tomorrow's games. There would be more stacking. More bouncing. More fun with his dear block friends.

Sometimes the best solutions come when you look at problems in a new way.

The end.
