# Story story_1258

**The Brave Little Puzzle**

In a cozy house on Maple Street, a colorful jigsaw puzzle lived on a small table in the hallway. The puzzle had bright pieces showing a sunny meadow with dancing butterflies and blooming flowers. His name was Patches, and he loved making people smile when they walked by.

One dark afternoon, thick gray clouds gathered outside the windows. The wind began to howl loudly through the trees. Rain started tapping against the glass like tiny fingers. Thunder rumbled in the distance, getting closer and closer.

Patches felt his pieces shake with worry. "Oh no!" he whispered to himself. "What if the storm is too strong? What if the wind blows through the house and scatters my pieces everywhere?"

The thunder grew louder. BOOM! Lightning flashed bright white through the hallway. Patches trembled so much that two of his corner pieces wiggled loose.

"I need to stay together," Patches said bravely. "But how can I keep safe from this scary storm?"

Patches looked around the hallway for help. He spotted a soft blue blanket folded on a nearby chair. "Maybe that blanket can protect me," he thought.

Using all his puzzle magic, Patches called out to the blanket. "Please help me, soft blanket! Cover me so the storm wind cannot blow my pieces away!"

The blanket heard his worried voice. It unfolded itself and gently floated down to cover Patches completely. Under the cozy blanket, Patches felt warmer and safer.

But then another loud CRACK of thunder shook the whole house. The sound was so strong that it made the blanket slip right off the table! Now Patches was uncovered again, and he felt more scared than before.

"That didn't work," Patches sighed sadly. "I need to try something else."

Patches noticed the family cat, Whiskers, sleeping peacefully on a sunny windowsill nearby. Even with all the storm noise, Whiskers looked calm and comfortable.

"Whiskers knows how to stay brave during storms," Patches realized. "Maybe I can learn from her."

Patches called softly to the sleeping cat. "Whiskers, how do you stay so calm when thunder is so loud and scary?"

Whiskers opened one sleepy eye and stretched her paws. "Oh, little puzzle," she purred gently. "I learned that storms always pass. I focus on good thoughts instead of scary sounds."

"What kind of good thoughts?" asked Patches.

"Think about your beautiful picture," Whiskers suggested. "Remember how happy people feel when they see your sunny meadow and pretty butterflies."

Patches closed his puzzle eyes and thought hard about his colorful picture. He imagined children laughing as they put his pieces together. He remembered families working on him during cozy winter evenings. He thought about all the joy he brought to people.

As Patches focused on these happy memories, something wonderful happened. The storm sounds seemed quieter. His pieces stopped shaking. Even when lightning flashed, he felt steady and strong.

Soon, the thunder moved farther away. The rain became a gentle patter instead of angry drumming. Soft sunlight began peeking through the clouds.

"You did it!" purred Whiskers proudly. "You stayed brave by thinking good thoughts."

Patches felt so proud of himself. His pieces fit together perfectly, showing his beautiful meadow picture even brighter than before. When the family came home, they smiled extra wide seeing their favorite puzzle looking so cheerful.

"I learned something important today," Patches said to Whiskers. "When I feel scared, I can choose to think about good things instead."

From that day on, whenever storms came, Patches would remember his lesson. He would think about sunshine, laughter, and all the happiness he shared with others. And he was never afraid of storms again.

The puzzle learned that being brave doesn't mean never feeling scared. Being brave means finding ways to feel better when scary things happen. Sometimes the best solution comes from changing how we think, not changing what's around us.

And every time children worked on Patches after that, his sunny meadow seemed to glow extra bright with courage and joy.

The end.
