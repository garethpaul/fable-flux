# Baby Luna and the Stormy Night

Baby Luna sat in her cozy living room. She wore her favorite yellow pajamas. The soft rug felt nice under her tiny hands.

Outside, dark clouds filled the sky. Rain began to fall. *Pitter-patter, pitter-patter* on the windows.

Luna's eyes grew wide. The storm sounds were so loud! Thunder rumbled like a big bear. *BOOM!* Lightning flashed bright white.

"Oh no," Luna whispered softly. Her bottom lip started to tremble. The storm seemed so big and scary.

Luna crawled to her toy box. Maybe her teddy bear could help! She hugged Mr. Fuzzy tight against her chest. His brown fur felt warm and soft.

But the thunder boomed again. *CRASH!* Luna jumped and squeezed her eyes shut.

"That didn't work," she said sadly.

Luna crawled to the big window. She pressed her nose against the cool glass. Maybe she could see the storm better.

The raindrops raced down the window. They looked like little rivers. But another flash of lightning made Luna gasp.

She crawled away quickly. "Still too scary," she mumbled.

Next, Luna found her music box. She wound it up carefully. The pretty ballerina spun around and around. Gentle music played a sweet song.

*Twinkle, twinkle, little star* filled the room. Luna smiled a tiny bit. The music was nice and peaceful.

But then thunder crashed again! The loud noise covered up her pretty song.

Luna's chin wobbled. What could she do? The storm kept being loud and bright.

Just then, Mama walked into the room. She had been folding laundry upstairs.

"Oh, my sweet baby," Mama said gently. "Are you worried about the storm?"

Luna nodded her head up and down. She reached her little arms toward Mama.

Mama scooped Luna up in a warm hug. "Let's try something special together," she said.

Mama sat on the soft couch. She placed Luna on her lap. "Close your eyes and listen," Mama whispered.

Luna closed her eyes tight. She felt safe in Mama's arms.

"Can you hear the rain?" Mama asked. "It's watering all the flowers outside."

Luna listened carefully. The rain did sound gentler now. *Tap, tap, tap* on the roof.

"The thunder is just the sky saying hello," Mama said. "Like this." She clapped her hands softly. "Hello, little Luna!"

Luna giggled. Mama's claps were much nicer than scary thunder.

"And lightning is like the sky taking pictures," Mama explained. "Flash! It wants to remember this special night."

Luna opened her eyes. She looked at Mama's kind face. "Pictures?" she asked.

"Yes, baby. The storm is just nature playing," Mama said. "It's not trying to scare you."

Together, they listened to the storm. Mama hummed a gentle song. Luna felt her body relax.

When thunder rumbled, Mama said, "Hello, sky!" When lightning flashed, she said, "Smile for the picture!"

Luna started copying Mama. "Hello!" she said to the thunder. She waved at the lightning.

The storm didn't seem scary anymore. It seemed like a friend saying hello.

Soon, Luna's eyelids felt heavy. The rain sounded like a lullaby now. *Pitter-patter, sleepy-time, pitter-patter.*

"See?" Mama whispered. "You were very brave tonight. You kept trying until you found a way to feel better."

Luna yawned a big yawn. She snuggled closer to Mama.

"When something seems scary," Mama said, "we can always try different ways to feel brave."

Luna smiled sleepily. She had tried her teddy bear. She had tried looking outside. She had tried music. And finally, she tried asking for help.

"I'm brave," Luna whispered proudly.

The storm continued outside, but Luna felt peaceful now. She had learned that scary things could become friendly. She just needed to keep trying new ways to understand them.

As Mama carried her to bed, Luna waved goodbye to the storm. "Goodnight, sky friend," she said.

The rain seemed to whisper back, "Goodnight, brave little Luna."

And Luna fell asleep with a happy smile, knowing she could face any storm.

The end.
