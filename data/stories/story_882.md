# Story story_882

**The Drum and the Blocks**

In a sunny backyard, there lived a big red drum. The drum sat under a tall swing set. Every day, the drum played music all by himself.

BOOM! BOOM! BOOM! went the drum.

The drum loved making music. But sometimes he felt very lonely. He watched the empty swings move in the breeze. He wished he had a friend to play with.

"I wish someone would play music with me," sighed the drum.

Near the swing set, there was a pile of colorful wooden blocks. The blocks heard the drum's sad voice. They wanted to help.

"We could play music too!" said the red block.

"Yes! Yes!" said the blue block and yellow block together.

The blocks rolled and tumbled toward the drum. They made soft clicking sounds as they moved. CLICK! CLICK! CLICK!

"Hello, drum!" called the green block. "Can we make music with you?"

The drum's surface sparkled in the sunlight. He felt excited and happy.

"Really? You want to play with me?" asked the drum.

"Of course!" said all the blocks together.

The red block climbed up first. He tapped gently on the drum's surface. TAP! TAP! TAP!

The blue block rolled up next. She knocked against the drum's side. KNOCK! KNOCK!

Soon all the blocks were making music with the drum. Some blocks tapped on top. Other blocks bumped the sides. The smallest blocks made tiny ticking sounds.

The drum played his deep, booming beats. BOOM! BOOM! BOOM!

The blocks played their light, happy sounds. TAP! CLICK! KNOCK!

Together, they made the most beautiful music. The sound filled the whole backyard. Birds stopped to listen. Butterflies danced in the air.

"This is wonderful!" laughed the drum.

"We sound amazing together!" giggled the blocks.

They played fast songs that made them wiggle. They played slow songs that made them sway. The swing set creaked happily above them.

The warm sun made the drum's red surface glow. The blocks' bright colors looked like a rainbow around him.

"Let's play every day!" said the yellow block.

"Yes! We can be the backyard band!" said the drum.

From that day on, the drum was never lonely. Every morning, the blocks would roll over to greet him. They would play music until the sun went down.

Sometimes they played marching songs. The blocks would line up and tap in order. TAP! TAP! TAP! TAP!

Sometimes they played gentle lullabies. The drum would boom softly while the blocks whispered tiny sounds.

The other toys in the yard loved listening. The jump rope would wiggle to the beat. The soccer ball would bounce along with the music.

"You two make the best team," said the swing set above them.

The drum and blocks learned they were different but special together. The drum had deep, strong sounds. The blocks had light, cheerful sounds. But when they played together, their music was perfect.

One evening, as the stars began to twinkle, the drum and blocks played their favorite song. It was slow and peaceful. The sound floated through the air like a gentle hug.

"Thank you for being my friends," whispered the drum.

"Thank you for sharing your music," whispered the blocks back.

They played until the moon was bright and full. Then they settled down for the night. The blocks cuddled close to their friend the drum.

The drum felt warm and happy. His loneliness was gone forever. He had found the best friends in the whole world.

The next morning, they would wake up and make beautiful music again. But tonight, they dreamed of all the songs they would play together.

The backyard was quiet and peaceful. Only the gentle breeze made any sound. It seemed to whisper, "Friendship makes the most beautiful music of all."

And in their dreams, the drum and blocks played the happiest songs ever created.

The end.
