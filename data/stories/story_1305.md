# Story story_1305

**Ducky and the Little Puppet**

Ducky sat on the big red swing. Her yellow feathers felt warm in the sunshine. The playground was full of children laughing and playing. But Ducky felt lonely.

She pushed herself back and forth slowly. The swing made a soft creaking sound. "I wish I had a friend to play with," Ducky whispered to herself.

Near the sandbox, something colorful caught her eye. A little puppet sat propped against a tree. He had bright blue hair and a striped shirt. His button eyes looked sad too.

The puppet's name was Pip. He watched all the children play. He wanted to join them so badly. But puppets can't move by themselves. Pip felt left out and forgotten.

A gentle breeze blew through the playground. It pushed Pip over onto his side. Now he could see Ducky on the swing better.

"Hello there," Ducky called out softly. She hopped off the swing and waddled closer. "You look lonely too."

Pip couldn't talk back. But his button eyes seemed to sparkle when Ducky spoke to him.

Ducky picked up Pip carefully. His fabric felt soft and squishy. "Would you like to play with me?" she asked.

She put Pip on the swing next to hers. Then she gave him a gentle push. Pip swayed back and forth in the warm breeze. If puppets could giggle, Pip would be giggling now.

"This is fun!" Ducky quacked happily. She pushed Pip higher and higher. His striped shirt fluttered in the wind.

Next, they went down the curvy slide together. Ducky held Pip tight as they whooshed down. The slide felt smooth and cool under her feathers.

At the bottom, they tumbled into a pile of soft sand. Ducky brushed the sand off Pip's blue hair. "Are you okay, friend?" she asked.

Pip's button eyes twinkled. Ducky was sure he was smiling inside.

They played in the sandbox next. Ducky used her orange bill to dig holes. She helped Pip build a castle with towers and walls. The sand felt grainy between her webbed toes.

"Look what we made together!" Ducky said proudly. Their castle was the best one in the whole sandbox.

Other children started to notice them playing. A little girl came over to watch. "What a cute duck and puppet!" she said.

Soon more children gathered around. They all wanted to help build the sand castle. Everyone took turns holding Pip and helping him play.

Ducky felt so happy. Her heart was warm and full. She had found a special friend. And now they had lots of friends to play with.

As the sun began to set, the playground grew quieter. Most children went home for dinner. Ducky and Pip sat together under their favorite tree.

"Thank you for being my friend," Ducky whispered to Pip. She smoothed down his striped shirt with her wing.

The evening breeze was cool and sweet. It smelled like flowers and fresh grass. Ducky could hear crickets starting to chirp.

A little boy ran over to them. "There's my puppet!" he said happily. "I was looking everywhere for Pip."

The boy picked up Pip and hugged him tight. "Did you have fun today, Pip?" he asked.

Ducky quacked softly. She knew Pip had the best day ever.

"Can Pip come back tomorrow?" the boy asked Ducky. "You two make great friends."

Ducky flapped her wings with joy. "Yes, please!" she quacked.

The boy smiled and waved goodbye. He carried Pip toward the playground gate.

Ducky felt excited for tomorrow. She had learned something wonderful today. Sometimes the best friendships start when you reach out to someone who looks lonely too.

She waddled to the pond nearby. The water felt cool on her feet. As she settled down for the night, Ducky smiled. Tomorrow would bring another day of friendship and fun.

The stars began to twinkle overhead. Ducky closed her eyes and dreamed of all the adventures she and Pip would share.

The end.
