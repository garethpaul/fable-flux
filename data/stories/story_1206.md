# Story story_1206

**Lily and the Little Green Friend**

Lily sat on her back porch steps. The sun felt warm on her face. Birds chirped in the tall oak tree. But Lily felt lonely.

"I wish I had someone to play with," she said softly.

Lily walked into the backyard. The grass tickled her bare feet. She picked up her red bouncy ball. She threw it against the fence. Catch. Throw. Catch. Throw.

"This is no fun alone," Lily sighed.

Near the garden, something moved. A small green frog sat by the flower bed. He had bright golden eyes. His skin looked wet and shiny.

The little frog watched Lily play. He hopped closer. Hop, hop, hop. His tiny feet made soft sounds on the dirt.

"Hello there, little frog," Lily said gently.

The frog tilted his head. He seemed to smile. Lily rolled her ball toward him. The frog jumped back, startled.

"Don't be scared," Lily whispered. "Do you want to play?"

The frog hopped closer to the red ball. He touched it with his nose. The ball was bigger than he was! The frog pushed the ball with his front feet. It rolled just a tiny bit.

Lily clapped her hands. "You did it! You moved the ball!"

The frog made a happy croaking sound. "Ribbit! Ribbit!"

Lily sat down in the grass. Now she was closer to frog size. "What should we call you?" she asked.

The frog hopped in a circle. Then he jumped toward the sprinkler. Water drops sparkled like diamonds in the sunshine.

"I know! Your name is Splash!" Lily said.

Splash seemed to like his new name. He croaked louder. "RIBBIT!"

Lily turned on the sprinkler. Cool water sprayed everywhere. Splash jumped right into the water. He spun and splashed. Water drops flew all around.

"That looks fun!" Lily said.

She ran through the sprinkler too. The cold water made her giggle. Splash hopped beside her. They ran in circles together. Water splashed on Lily's face and arms.

"This is the best!" Lily laughed.

After the water play, they sat in the shade. Lily's hair dripped. Splash's skin gleamed in the dappled sunlight.

"Are you hungry, Splash?" Lily asked.

She remembered that frogs eat bugs. In the garden, tiny gnats buzzed around the tomato plants. Splash's tongue shot out quick as lightning. Zap! He caught a gnat.

"Wow! You're so fast!" Lily said.

For Lily's snack, Mom brought apple slices and crackers. Lily shared her crackers with the ants. She knew Splash preferred his bug snacks.

The afternoon sun made them sleepy. Lily lay on the soft grass. Splash hopped onto a flat rock nearby. The warm stone felt good on his belly.

"I'm so happy we're friends," Lily said.

Splash closed his golden eyes. He made soft, content croaking sounds.

When evening came, purple shadows grew longer. Fireflies began to twinkle. Splash caught one gently in his mouth. Then he let it go. The firefly blinked away into the twilight.

"Will you come back tomorrow?" Lily asked.

Splash hopped to the edge of the small garden pond. He looked back at Lily. Then he dove into the water with a tiny splash.

But the next morning, Lily found Splash waiting by the porch steps. A dewdrop sat on his head like a tiny crown.

"You came back!" Lily cheered.

Every day after that, Lily and Splash played together. They rolled the ball. They splashed in the sprinkler. They rested in the shade.

Lily was never lonely in her backyard again. She had found the very best kind of friend. A friend who loved to play. A friend who made her laugh. A friend who always came back.

Sometimes the best friendships hop into your life when you least expect them.

And sometimes, they're green and say "Ribbit!"

The end.
