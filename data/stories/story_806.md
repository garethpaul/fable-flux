# Story story_806

**Sunny's Garden Adventure**

The morning sun peeked through the trees. Birds sang happy songs outside. "Time for garden time!" called Mama Squirrel from downstairs.

Sunny Squirrel pulled his fluffy tail over his head. He did not want to get up. Garden time meant work. It meant getting his paws dirty. It meant pulling weeds and watering plants.

"I don't like garden time," Sunny mumbled. He rolled over in his cozy nest. The soft leaves felt so warm and nice.

"Come on, little one," said Mama gently. "The garden needs our help today." She climbed up to his branch. Her brown eyes sparkled with kindness.

Sunny dragged his feet down the oak tree. His bushy tail drooped behind him. The morning air smelled fresh and sweet. Dewdrops sparkled on the grass like tiny diamonds.

In the garden, Mama handed him a small watering can. It was bright red and just his size. "Let's start with the carrot patch," she said.

Sunny sighed loudly. He poured water on the green carrot tops. The water made soft splashing sounds. Some drops tickled his nose.

Suddenly, something moved under the soil. Sunny jumped back. "Mama! Something's down there!"

A tiny green shoot poked through the dirt. Then another one appeared. And another! The baby carrots were growing right before his eyes.

"Wow!" Sunny gasped. His eyes grew wide with wonder. "They're like magic!"

Mama smiled. "Your watering helped them grow. You gave them just what they needed."

Sunny felt a warm feeling in his chest. He had helped something grow! He watered more spots carefully. More little green shoots appeared.

Next, they moved to the tomato plants. Sunny noticed tiny yellow flowers on the vines. "What are these pretty flowers for?" he asked.

"Those will become red tomatoes," Mama explained. "But first, we need to help them."

She showed Sunny how to gently tie the vines to wooden sticks. His small paws worked carefully. The green vines felt smooth and cool.

A fat bumblebee buzzed over to visit the flowers. It was fuzzy and yellow. "Hello, bee!" Sunny called out. The bee seemed to wave with its tiny wing.

"The bee is helping too," said Mama. "It carries pollen from flower to flower."

Sunny watched the busy bee work. "We're all helping the garden together!"

They pulled some weeds next. Sunny discovered this was like a treasure hunt. Under each weed, the soil felt soft and rich. It smelled earthy and good.

A pink earthworm wiggled in the dirt. "Don't worry, little worm," Sunny said softly. "You help the garden too." He gently moved it to safety.

Time passed quickly. Sunny's paws were dirty but he didn't mind. The sun felt warm on his fur. A gentle breeze rustled the leaves above.

"Look what we found!" Mama pointed to a patch of strawberries. Three red berries hid under green leaves. They looked like tiny red hearts.

"Can we eat them?" Sunny asked hopefully.

"Of course! You helped them grow."

The strawberries tasted sweet and juicy. The flavor burst in Sunny's mouth. Red juice dripped down his chin. He giggled with delight.

As the morning ended, Sunny looked around the garden. Everything looked happy and healthy. The plants seemed to dance in the breeze.

"The garden is beautiful," he said proudly. "We made it beautiful together."

Mama hugged him close. "You were such a good helper today."

Sunny's chest puffed out with pride. His tail twitched with happiness. The dirty paws didn't bother him anymore. They showed how hard he had worked.

"Mama," Sunny said as they walked home. "Can we do garden time again tomorrow?"

Mama's eyes twinkled. "I thought you didn't like garden time."

"I love it now!" Sunny bounced excitedly. "I want to see what grows next!"

That night, Sunny dreamed of dancing carrots and singing tomatoes. He couldn't wait for tomorrow's garden adventure. Garden time had become his favorite time of all.

The end.
