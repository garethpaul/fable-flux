# Story story_1095

Emma sat in her garden feeling sad. The sun was warm on her face. Birds chirped in the tall oak tree. But Emma felt very lonely today.

She picked up her favorite red ball. She bounced it once, twice, three times. Then she sighed and put it down. Playing alone was not much fun.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," Emma said softly.

Just then, she heard a gentle humming sound. Emma looked up at the bright blue sky. A small yellow airplane was flying in circles above her garden.

The plane dipped down low. It made a soft whooshing sound as it flew past Emma's ear. The plane smelled like fresh morning air and adventure.

"Hello there," called a friendly voice from the plane. "I'm Pippy the Plane. You look sad. What's wrong?"

Emma's eyes grew wide with wonder. "You can talk!" she said.

"Of course I can talk," laughed Pippy. "I saw you sitting all alone. Would you like to play with me?"

Emma clapped her hands together. "Yes, please! But how can we play? You fly and I walk."

Pippy thought for a moment. His propeller spun slowly as he hovered near the ground. "I have an idea! Can you run really fast?"

"I can run super fast," said Emma. She jumped up from the soft grass.

"Perfect!" said Pippy. "Let's play follow the leader. You run and I'll fly right beside you."

Emma started running around her garden. Her feet made soft thumping sounds on the earth. Pippy flew right next to her, his engine purring like a happy cat.

They ran and flew around the flower beds. The red roses smelled sweet. The yellow daisies danced in the breeze. Emma giggled as Pippy did loop-de-loops above her head.

"This is so much fun!" Emma called out as she ran.

"Let's try something new," said Pippy. "Can you hop like a bunny?"

Emma began hopping on both feet. Boing, boing, boing! Pippy flew up and down to match her bouncing. They hopped all the way to the vegetable garden.

"Now let's skip!" suggested Emma.

They skipped past the tall corn stalks. They skipped around the pumpkin patch. Emma's ponytail bounced as she moved. Pippy's wings tilted left and right as he followed her path.

After a while, Emma felt tired. She sat down under the apple tree. The shade felt cool and nice. Pippy landed gently on the grass beside her.

"That was the best game ever," said Emma, breathing hard.

"I had so much fun too," said Pippy. "You're a wonderful friend, Emma."

Emma smiled her biggest smile. "You're my wonderful friend too, Pippy. Will you come back tomorrow?"

"Every day if you want me to," promised Pippy. "We can play new games. We can explore the garden together."

Emma reached out and gently touched Pippy's smooth yellow wing. It felt warm from the sunshine. "I was so lonely before you came. Now I feel happy inside."

"Friends make everything better," said Pippy wisely. "Even the flowers look prettier when you have someone to share them with."

Emma looked around her garden with new eyes. The butterflies seemed more colorful. The grass felt softer. The whole world seemed brighter and more beautiful.

"Let's have a friendship party," said Emma excitedly.

She picked some daisies and made a flower crown. She placed it carefully on Pippy's nose. Pippy did three happy loops in the air.

"Now we're official best friends," declared Emma.

As the sun began to set, painting the sky pink and orange, Emma and Pippy sat together under the apple tree. They watched the stars appear one by one.

"Thank you for being my friend," whispered Emma.

"Thank you for playing with me," whispered Pippy back.

From that day on, Emma was never lonely in her garden again. She had found the very best friend a girl could ask for. And every morning, she would run outside to play with Pippy the Plane, her heart full of joy and friendship.

The end.
