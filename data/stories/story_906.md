# Story story_906

**Fluffy's New Friend**

In a sunny green meadow, a little white sheep named Fluffy played all by herself. She rolled in the soft grass. She chased butterflies with orange wings. She jumped over small rocks.

But Fluffy felt sad inside. Playing alone was not very fun. She wanted someone to play with her.

"I wish I had a friend," Fluffy said softly. Her woolly ears drooped down. The warm sun felt nice on her back. But her heart felt empty.

Across the meadow lived a little girl named Anna. Anna had curly brown hair and bright blue eyes. She loved to play outside in the fresh air.

Anna saw Fluffy through her bedroom window. The sheep looked lonely. Anna's kind heart felt sorry for Fluffy.

"Mama, can I play with the sheep?" Anna asked. Her voice was hopeful and sweet.

"Yes, but be very gentle," Mama said with a smile. "Sheep can be shy at first."

Anna ran outside. The grass felt cool under her bare feet. She walked slowly toward Fluffy. Her steps were quiet and careful.

Fluffy saw Anna coming. The sheep felt nervous. What if this girl was mean? What if she was too loud?

"Hello, little sheep," Anna said in a whisper. "I'm Anna. Do you want to be friends?"

Fluffy's ears perked up. Anna's voice sounded kind and gentle. Maybe this girl was nice after all.

Anna sat down in the grass. She did not chase Fluffy. She just waited patiently. The meadow smelled like flowers and fresh air.

Fluffy took one small step toward Anna. Then another step. Anna smiled but stayed very still.

Soon Fluffy was close enough to sniff Anna's hand. It smelled like soap and cookies. Fluffy's pink nose tickled Anna's palm.

"You're so soft!" Anna giggled quietly. She gently petted Fluffy's woolly head. It felt like touching a cloud.

Fluffy's tail wiggled with happiness. This girl was very nice indeed!

"Want to play tag?" Anna asked. She stood up slowly so she would not scare Fluffy.

Anna ran a few steps, then stopped. Fluffy understood the game right away. She bounced after Anna on her four little legs.

They played tag around the big oak tree. Anna was "it" first. She chased Fluffy in circles. Both friends laughed and giggled.

Then Fluffy was "it." She gently bumped Anna with her nose. Anna pretended to fall down in the soft grass. She laughed so hard her tummy hurt.

Next they played hide and seek. Anna hid behind the oak tree. Fluffy found her easily. Then Fluffy hid behind a big rock. Anna found her too.

"You're really good at hiding," Anna said. She gave Fluffy another gentle pet.

They rolled down the small hill together. The grass was soft and tickly. They got dizzy and laughed even more.

Anna picked yellow dandelions. She made a flower crown for Fluffy. The sheep felt like a princess with flowers on her head.

"You look beautiful," Anna said. Fluffy's eyes sparkled with joy.

As the sun began to set, both friends felt tired but happy. They lay in the warm grass together. Anna's hand rested on Fluffy's soft wool.

"Will you be my best friend?" Anna asked. Her voice was full of hope.

"Baa!" said Fluffy. That meant "yes" in sheep language.

From that day on, Anna and Fluffy played together every afternoon. They never felt lonely again. The meadow was full of their laughter and joy.

Anna's mama watched from the window. She smiled to see her daughter so happy. True friendship was a beautiful thing to see.

Fluffy had learned that being brave enough to trust someone new could lead to wonderful things. Anna had learned that being patient and gentle made the best friendships.

Together in their special meadow, they had found exactly what they both needed. A friend to share sunny days, fun games, and happy memories.

The meadow was no longer quiet. It was filled with the sounds of friendship and love.

The end.
