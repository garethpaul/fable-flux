# Story story_1332

Ben sat in the big sandbox. The warm sand felt soft between his fingers. He made a small hill with his red bucket. But Ben felt lonely today.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," Ben said quietly.

The sun shone bright on the sandbox. Birds chirped in the tall oak tree above. Ben scooped more sand into his bucket. He turned it over carefully. The sand castle fell down with a soft thump.

"Oh no," Ben sighed. "It's hard to build alone."

Near the edge of the sandbox sat a small doll. She had curly brown hair and a pretty blue dress. The doll's name was Rosie. She watched Ben play all by himself.

Rosie wanted to help Ben. She wanted to be his friend. But she was just a doll. Could she really play in the sandbox?

Ben picked up his yellow shovel. He tried to dig a moat around his castle. The sand kept falling back in. Ben's shoulders drooped down.

"This is too hard," Ben said sadly.

Just then, a gentle breeze blew through the yard. The wind picked up some sand. It swirled around Rosie the doll. Suddenly, Rosie felt different. She felt brave and ready to play.

Rosie stood up slowly. She brushed sand off her blue dress. Then she walked over to Ben. Her small feet made tiny prints in the sand.

"Hi Ben," Rosie said in a sweet voice. "Can I help you build?"

Ben's eyes grew wide with surprise. He looked at the talking doll. Then his face broke into a big smile.

"Yes please!" Ben said happily. "I would love some help."

Rosie sat down next to Ben. The sand felt warm and grainy under her legs. She picked up a small shell from the sandbox edge.

"This could be a door for your castle," Rosie suggested.

Ben clapped his hands together. "That's a great idea, Rosie!"

They worked together in the sunshine. Ben scooped sand with his red bucket. Rosie smoothed the walls with her tiny hands. The sand felt cool and damp between their fingers.

"Let's make windows too," Ben said excitedly.

Rosie found two smooth pebbles. She pressed them into the castle walls. The pebbles looked like perfect little windows.

Ben dug a moat around their castle. This time the sand stayed in place. Rosie helped pat it down firmly. They filled the moat with water from Ben's watering can.

"Our castle looks amazing!" Ben laughed with joy.

The castle stood tall and proud. It had smooth walls and a shell door. Two pebble windows sparkled in the sunlight. Clear water filled the moat all around it.

"We make a great team," Rosie said warmly.

Ben nodded his head up and down. "The best team ever!"

They built a road leading to the castle. They made a garden with flower petals. They even built a small bridge over the moat. Everything looked beautiful in the golden sunshine.

"Thank you for playing with me, Rosie," Ben said. "I was feeling so lonely before."

"Thank you for letting me help," Rosie replied. "Playing together is much more fun."

Ben and Rosie sat back to admire their work. The castle looked like something from a fairy tale. The afternoon sun made everything glow warmly.

"Will you be my friend, Rosie?" Ben asked hopefully.

"Of course!" Rosie said with a big smile. "Best friends forever!"

They shook hands gently. Sand stuck to their palms, but they didn't mind. They had found something much better than a perfect sand castle. They had found friendship.

From that day on, Ben and Rosie played together every afternoon. They built many more castles in the warm sandbox. But the best thing they built together was their special friendship.

Ben never felt lonely again. And Rosie was the happiest doll in the whole wide world.

The end.
