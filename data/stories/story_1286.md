# Story story_1286

**Rosie's Magical Bath Time**

Rosie the doll sat on Emma's bed. Her pink dress was wrinkled and dirty. Her yellow hair was messy and tangled.

"Time for your bath, Rosie!" said Emma. She picked up her favorite doll carefully. Emma carried Rosie to the bathroom.

The warm water filled the little pink tub. Bubbles floated on top like tiny clouds. The bathroom smelled like lavender soap.

But Rosie felt scared. She had never had a real bath before. What if the water was too hot? What if she got soap in her eyes?

"I don't want a bath," whispered Rosie. But Emma couldn't hear her tiny voice.

Emma gently placed Rosie in the warm water. "Don't worry," Emma said softly. "This will feel nice."

The water felt strange at first. It was warm like sunshine on Rosie's plastic skin. The bubbles tickled her arms and legs.

Suddenly, something amazing happened. The bubbles began to sparkle! They glowed like little stars in the water. Rosie's eyes grew wide with wonder.

"Oh my!" gasped Rosie. The magical bubbles danced around her. They made tiny rainbow colors in the light.

Emma used a soft washcloth to clean Rosie's face. The cloth felt gentle like a cloud. Emma was very careful around Rosie's button eyes.

"You're being so good," Emma said. She smiled at her doll. "See? Bath time isn't scary at all."

The sparkly bubbles made Rosie giggle. They popped with tiny musical sounds. Pop! Pop! Pop! Each bubble sang a different note.

Emma poured warm water over Rosie's hair. The water ran down like a gentle waterfall. Rosie's tangled hair became smooth and shiny.

"This feels wonderful!" thought Rosie. The fear in her heart melted away. The magical bubbles swirled around her like dancing fairies.

Emma used baby shampoo to wash Rosie's hair. It smelled like sweet flowers. The bubbles in her hair sparkled even brighter.

"Almost done," said Emma. She rinsed the shampoo away carefully. The water was still perfectly warm.

Rosie felt so clean and fresh. Her skin sparkled in the bathroom light. Her hair shone like golden sunshine.

Emma lifted Rosie from the magical water. She wrapped her in a fluffy towel. The towel was soft and warm from the dryer.

"There you go," said Emma. She dried Rosie gently. "Don't you feel better now?"

Rosie felt amazing! She was clean from head to toe. Her hair smelled like flowers. Her skin felt smooth and new.

Emma brushed Rosie's hair until it was perfect. Each strand gleamed in the light. Then Emma put on Rosie's favorite blue dress.

"You look beautiful," said Emma. She hugged Rosie tight. "I love taking care of you."

Rosie felt so happy and loved. The bath had been magical and fun. She wasn't scared anymore.

Emma carried Rosie back to the bedroom. She tucked her into the little doll bed. The clean sheets felt soft and cozy.

"Good night, Rosie," whispered Emma. She kissed the doll's forehead. "Sweet dreams."

As Emma turned off the light, Rosie smiled. She thought about the sparkling bubbles. She remembered the warm, gentle water.

"That was the best bath ever," thought Rosie. Her clean hair spread across the tiny pillow. Her fresh dress was smooth and neat.

Rosie closed her eyes and dreamed. She dreamed of dancing bubbles and rainbow colors. She dreamed of Emma's gentle hands and kind words.

When morning came, Rosie felt excited. She couldn't wait for another magical bath time. Maybe the bubbles would sparkle again!

Emma woke up and smiled at her doll. "Good morning, Rosie," she said. "You still look so clean and pretty."

Rosie felt proud and happy. Bath time wasn't scary at all. It was magical and wonderful. She looked forward to getting clean again tomorrow.

From that day on, Rosie loved bath time. She knew Emma would always be gentle and kind. And maybe, just maybe, the bubbles would dance again.

The end.
