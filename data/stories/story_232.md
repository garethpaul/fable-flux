# Sam's Big View

Sam sat in his bedroom closet, surrounded by colorful clothes and cozy blankets. The little closet was his favorite thinking spot. Today, Sam had a big problem to solve.

"I want to see what's happening," Sam said softly. He hugged his knees close. "But I'm too short!"

Earlier that morning, Sam had heard exciting sounds from the neighbor's backyard. *Splash! Giggle! Splash!* It sounded like the most wonderful fun ever. Sam had run to the tall wooden fence between the yards.

He jumped up high. *Boing!* His sneakers hit the ground. Nothing. He could only see the very tops of the fence posts.

Sam tried again. *Jump, jump, jump!* Still nothing but brown wooden fence.

"This is tricky," Sam whispered in his closet. But Sam was not a giving-up kind of kid.

Sam looked around his thinking closet. His eyes sparkled with an idea. "Maybe I need to be taller!"

First, Sam tried standing on his tippy-toes. He stretched his arms up high like a giraffe. *Stretch, stretch, stretch!* He wobbled back and forth. Still too short.

Next, Sam found his favorite storybook. The thick book felt heavy in his hands. He carried it outside to the fence. Sam stood on the book and reached up high.

"Almost!" Sam said with excitement. He could see tiny bits of blue. Was that water? The book wiggled under his feet. *Wobble, wobble.* Down Sam went with a gentle *thump*.

Sam's tummy felt a little sad. But then he remembered something important. "I can try again!"

Back in his closet, Sam spotted his wooden step stool. It was yellow and sturdy. "Perfect!" Sam cheered.

Sam dragged the step stool across the grass. *Scrape, scrape, scrape.* The stool felt rough under his hands. It smelled like sunshine and wood.

Carefully, Sam climbed onto the step stool. One foot, then the other. He felt wobbly but brave.

Sam reached up toward the fence top. His fingers touched the warm wooden edge. "I can do this!" he said proudly.

Slowly, Sam pulled himself up. His arms felt strong. His heart beat fast with excitement.

And then... *pop!* Sam's head appeared over the fence!

"Wow!" Sam gasped with wonder.

The neighbor's backyard was magical! Three children were playing in a bright blue kiddie pool. Water sparkled like diamonds in the sunshine. A friendly golden dog was running in happy circles. Colorful beach balls bounced everywhere.

"Hi there!" called a girl with curly hair. "I'm Emma! Want to come play?"

Sam's face broke into the biggest smile. "Yes, please! I'm Sam!"

Sam carefully climbed down from his step stool. He felt proud and tall inside, even though his body was still small.

"Mom!" Sam called as he ran toward the house. "Can I play with our new neighbors?"

Sam's mom smiled warmly. "Of course, sweetie. How did you meet them?"

"I found a way to see over the fence!" Sam explained happily. "It was hard, but I kept trying different ideas."

Mom gave Sam a gentle hug. "I'm proud of you for not giving up."

Later that afternoon, Sam splashed and laughed with his new friends. The cool water felt wonderful on his skin. The sun warmed his face.

That evening, Sam sat in his cozy closet again. But now it wasn't for solving problems. Now it was for feeling grateful.

"I learned something important today," Sam whispered to his stuffed bear. "When something seems too hard, I can try different ways. I can keep going until I find what works."

Sam hugged his bear tight. Tomorrow would bring new adventures. And Sam knew he was ready for anything.

Outside his window, Sam could hear his new friends playing. *Splash! Giggle! Splash!* Soon he would join them again.

Sam smiled sleepily. Being persistent felt wonderful. And making new friends felt even better.

The little closet felt extra cozy that night, full of dreams and possibilities.

The end.
