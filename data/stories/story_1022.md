# Story story_1022

**Freddy's Magical Lunch**

The sun was high in the sky. It was time for lunch at Lily Pad Pond. All the little frogs hopped to the big rock where they ate together.

"Lunch time!" called Mama Frog.

But Freddy the frog sat on his favorite lily pad. He did not want to come. His tummy felt funny. Not hungry funny. Worried funny.

"I don't like lunch," Freddy said softly.

The other frogs were already eating. They crunched on crispy water bugs. They slurped sweet nectar from flowers. Everything smelled so good.

But Freddy stayed on his lily pad. He watched the water ripple around him. A butterfly landed nearby. It had sparkly blue wings.

"Why aren't you eating?" asked the butterfly. Her voice sounded like tiny bells.

"Lunch is scary," Freddy whispered. "What if I don't like the food? What if I spill? What if the other frogs laugh?"

The butterfly smiled. "I have an idea. Close your eyes."

Freddy closed his big green eyes tight. The butterfly fluttered her magical wings. Sparkly dust floated down like glitter rain.

"Now open them," she said.

Freddy opened his eyes. Everything looked different! The water bugs glowed like tiny stars. The flower nectar shimmered like liquid rainbows. Even the lily pads sparkled green and gold.

"Wow!" Freddy gasped.

"Food is more fun when you see the magic," said the butterfly. "Try one small bite."

Freddy hopped closer to the lunch rock. The magical sparkles made everything beautiful. He picked up one glowing water bug. It smelled like fresh rain.

He took a tiny bite. Crunch! It tasted like sunshine and morning dew. Sweet and crispy and wonderful.

"This is good!" Freddy said, surprised.

The other frogs smiled at him. "We saved you the best flower," said his friend Lily. "It tastes like honey and clouds."

Freddy sipped the nectar. It was cool and sweet. It tickled his tongue in the best way. The magical sparkles made it taste even better.

"Try the pond berries too," said his friend Splash. "They're purple and juicy."

Freddy popped a berry in his mouth. It burst with flavor like a tiny firework. Purple juice dripped down his chin. He giggled.

All the frogs laughed together. Happy laughing, not mean laughing. Freddy felt warm inside.

"I spilled!" Freddy said, pointing at his purple chin.

"Me too!" said Lily. She had flower petals stuck to her nose.

"And me!" said Splash. His face was covered in berry juice.

They all looked at each other and laughed even more. Lunch was turning into the best time ever.

The butterfly watched from a nearby flower. Her sparkly magic was working perfectly. She fluttered her wings and more glitter fell.

Freddy tried everything on the lunch rock. Crunchy seeds that tasted like nuts. Sweet grass that was soft and chewy. Cool water that tasted like spring.

His tummy felt happy and full. Not worried anymore. Just content and satisfied.

"Thank you," Freddy whispered to the butterfly.

"The magic was always there," she said. "You just needed to see it."

As the sun began to set, the frogs finished their lunch. They cleaned up together, putting scraps in the compost pile for the fish.

Freddy's lily pad felt different now. Not like a hiding place. Just like home.

"What's for lunch tomorrow?" Freddy asked Mama Frog.

"Flower soup and cricket cookies," she said.

"Yum!" said Freddy. "I can't wait!"

That night, Freddy dreamed of sparkling food and friendly faces. He dreamed of butterfly magic and purple berry juice. Most of all, he dreamed of tomorrow's lunch.

When morning came, Freddy was the first frog awake. He couldn't wait to see what magical flavors lunch would bring. The butterfly was right. Food was always magical. He just needed to look for the sparkles.

And from that day on, lunch time was Freddy's favorite time of day.

The end.
