# Story story_1143

**Nutkin and the Friendly Puzzle**

Nutkin the squirrel sat by the big window. The nursery was quiet and still. All the other toys were sleeping. Nutkin's fluffy tail drooped down sadly.

"I wish I had someone to play with," Nutkin whispered. He looked at his acorn collection. Playing alone wasn't very fun anymore.

The morning sun made golden stripes on the carpet. Nutkin could smell the sweet flowers outside. But even the pretty day couldn't cheer him up.

On the low shelf sat a colorful jigsaw puzzle. The puzzle had been watching Nutkin for days. The puzzle's pieces showed a beautiful rainbow and fluffy white clouds.

"Hello there," said the puzzle softly. "You look lonely."

Nutkin looked up with surprise. His dark eyes grew wide. "Did you just talk to me?"

"Yes! My name is Patches," said the puzzle. "I have one hundred pieces. Would you like to be friends?"

Nutkin's whiskers twitched with excitement. "Really? You want to play with me?"

"Of course!" Patches said warmly. "I've been hoping we could play together."

Nutkin scampered over to the shelf. He touched Patches' smooth cardboard edge. "But how do we play? You're a puzzle and I'm a squirrel."

"We can figure it out together," Patches said. "Maybe you could help put my pieces together?"

Nutkin had never tried a puzzle before. His tiny paws picked up a blue piece. "This one looks like sky," he said.

"Perfect!" Patches cheered. "Put it in my corner."

Nutkin carefully placed the piece. It fit! "It worked!" he squeaked happily.

"Now try the green piece," Patches suggested. "That's part of my grass."

Nutkin's paws were small but gentle. He found where the green piece belonged. Click! Another perfect fit.

"This is fun!" Nutkin chattered excitedly. His tail swished back and forth.

They worked together all morning. Nutkin would pick up pieces. Patches would tell him where they might go. Sometimes Nutkin got it wrong. But Patches was always patient and kind.

"Try turning that piece around," Patches would say gently.

The nursery filled with their happy laughter. Other toys began to wake up. They watched Nutkin and Patches playing together.

"Look at that teamwork!" said the teddy bear.

"They make a great pair," agreed the wooden blocks.

By lunchtime, they had finished half the puzzle. Nutkin could see the beautiful rainbow taking shape. The colors were bright red, orange, and yellow.

"You're a wonderful helper," Patches said. "I've never had so much fun!"

Nutkin's chest felt warm and happy. "You're my first real friend here!"

They continued working after lunch. Nutkin learned to look for straight edges. He found pieces that matched colors. His confidence grew with each success.

"Almost done!" Patches said as evening came.

Nutkin placed the very last piece. It was a white cloud piece. The completed picture was beautiful and bright.

"We did it together!" Nutkin cheered. He did a little happy dance.

"We make an amazing team," Patches said proudly.

From that day on, Nutkin and Patches were best friends. Every morning, Nutkin would scramble to the shelf. They would work on new puzzles together.

Sometimes they did easy puzzles with big pieces. Other times they tried harder ones. But they always helped each other.

The other toys loved watching them play. The nursery became a happier place. Laughter and friendship filled every corner.

Nutkin learned that friendship makes everything better. Even puzzles were more fun with a friend. Patches learned that helping others brought great joy.

Every evening, they would admire their finished puzzle. The pictures always looked more beautiful when they made them together.

"Thank you for being my friend," Nutkin would say.

"Thank you for making me complete," Patches would reply.

And in the cozy nursery, two unlikely friends had found something wonderful. They had found each other.

The end.
