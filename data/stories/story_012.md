# Gigi and Grandma Rose

Gigi the giraffe stood by the swing set. Her long neck drooped down sadly. The red swings moved gently in the breeze. But Gigi wasn't swinging at all.

"I wish I had someone to play with," Gigi sighed. Her spots seemed duller when she felt lonely. The playground was so quiet today.

Just then, Grandma Rose walked by slowly. She had silver hair and kind, twinkling eyes. Her purple sweater was soft and cozy. She carried a small wicker basket.

"Hello there, sweet giraffe," Grandma Rose said gently. "You look a little sad today."

Gigi's ears perked up. "I am sad. I want to swing, but it's no fun alone."

Grandma Rose smiled warmly. "May I join you? I love swings too!"

Gigi looked surprised. "Really? But you're a grandma!"

"Grandmas love to play too," Rose laughed. Her laugh sounded like tiny silver bells.

Gigi helped Grandma Rose onto the blue swing. Rose's feet barely touched the ground. Gigi sat on the red swing next to her.

"Ready?" asked Gigi excitedly.

"Ready!" said Grandma Rose.

They began to swing together. Back and forth. Up and down. The wind tickled their faces.

"This is wonderful!" called Grandma Rose. Her silver hair flew behind her like ribbons.

"Much better than swinging alone!" Gigi agreed happily.

After swinging, they sat on the soft grass. Grandma Rose opened her wicker basket. Inside were colorful yarn balls and knitting needles.

"I'm making scarves for winter," she explained. "Would you like to help?"

Gigi's eyes grew wide. "I don't know how to knit."

"I can teach you," Rose said kindly. "Your long neck would be perfect for modeling scarves!"

Gigi giggled. She held one end of the purple yarn. Grandma Rose showed her how to wrap it gently. The yarn felt soft and warm in Gigi's hooves.

"You're a natural!" praised Grandma Rose.

Working together felt wonderful. Gigi held the yarn steady. Rose's needles clicked cheerfully. They made a beautiful purple scarf together.

"What else do you like to do?" asked Gigi curiously.

"I love picking apples," said Rose. "But the best ones are too high for me."

Gigi's long neck stretched up proudly. "I can reach the highest branches!"

"Perfect teamwork!" exclaimed Grandma Rose.

They walked to the apple tree together. Gigi stretched her neck way up high. She picked the reddest, shiniest apples. Rose held the basket below carefully.

"These are the most beautiful apples ever!" Rose said gratefully.

They sat under the tree sharing sweet, crunchy apples. The juice dripped down their chins. They laughed together happily.

"I was so lonely this morning," Gigi admitted.

"Me too," said Rose gently. "My grandchildren live far away."

"But now we have each other!" Gigi said brightly.

"Yes, we do," Rose agreed. Her eyes sparkled with joy.

As the sun began to set, they made plans. Tomorrow they would swing again. Rose would teach Gigi more knitting. Gigi would help pick more apples.

"Thank you for being my friend," said Gigi warmly.

"Thank you for sharing your day with me," Rose replied. She hugged Gigi's long neck gently.

Gigi walked Rose home slowly. The evening air was cool and peaceful. Fireflies began to twinkle like tiny stars.

"See you tomorrow, dear friend," called Rose from her porch.

"See you tomorrow, Grandma Rose!" Gigi called back happily.

Gigi skipped home with a huge smile. Her spots seemed brighter now. Her heart felt warm and full.

She had learned something wonderful today. Playing alone was okay sometimes. But playing with a friend was magical. Helping each other made everything better.

Two friends, working together, could do amazing things. Like reaching high apples and making beautiful scarves. Like turning a lonely day into the best day ever.

Gigi fell asleep dreaming of purple yarn and red apples. And most of all, dreaming of her new best friend, Grandma Rose.

Tomorrow would bring more adventures together.

The end.
