# Story story_946

**The Dirty Blocks**

Benny the red block loved playing in the sandbox. Every day, he rolled and tumbled with his friends. There was Sally the yellow block and Max the blue block. They built tall towers together. They made pretend castles in the sand.

But today was different. Today was bath day.

"Time to get clean!" called Mrs. Chen, the playground helper. She held a big bucket of soapy water. Bubbles floated in the sunshine. The water sparkled like tiny diamonds.

"No way!" said Benny. He was covered in sticky sand. Bits of leaves stuck to his corners. "I don't want a bath!"

Sally shook her sandy sides. "Baths are scary!" she said. "What if the soap stings?"

Max rolled behind the slide. "What if we get too slippery?" he worried.

The three blocks huddled together. They watched other toys getting washed. The rubber balls came out shiny and bright. The jump ropes looked fresh and new.

"We need a plan," whispered Benny.

**First Try**

"Let's hide!" said Sally. She spotted a pile of autumn leaves. The blocks rolled as fast as they could. They dove under the crunchy orange and red leaves.

"Perfect!" said Max. "No one will find us here!"

But the leaves tickled their edges. Benny started to giggle. Then Sally began to laugh. Soon all three blocks were shaking with giggles.

Crunch, crunch, crunch! The leaves moved everywhere.

"I found you!" smiled Mrs. Chen. She gently picked up the blocks. "Nice try, little ones."

The blocks felt disappointed. Their first plan didn't work.

**Second Try**

"Wait," said Benny. "I have an idea." He looked at his friends' worried faces. "What if we help each other?"

"How?" asked Sally.

"We can hold hands," said Benny. "We'll be brave together."

Max's blue paint was chipped and faded. "But what if the water is too cold?"

"Mrs. Chen is kind," said Sally. "She won't hurt us."

The blocks rolled slowly toward the bucket. The water smelled like fresh flowers. Tiny rainbows danced in the bubbles.

"We can do this," said Benny. He reached out his corner to Sally. Sally grabbed Max's edge. Together, they made a block chain.

"Ready?" asked Mrs. Chen. Her voice was gentle and warm.

"Ready!" said the blocks together.

**Splash!**

The warm water felt wonderful! It wasn't scary at all. The soap was soft and gentle. It smelled like sunshine and daisies.

"This feels amazing!" laughed Benny. The sand washed away from his red sides.

Sally's yellow color became bright again. "I can see myself shine!" she said.

Max discovered his blue was deeper than ever. "I look like the ocean!" he said proudly.

Mrs. Chen used a soft brush. She cleaned every corner carefully. She hummed a happy song while she worked.

"You're all so beautiful!" she said.

**Clean and Happy**

Soon the blocks were sparkling clean. They felt light and fresh. Their colors were bright and cheerful.

"Thank you, Mrs. Chen!" said Benny.

"We're sorry we ran away," said Sally.

"Next time we'll be braver," promised Max.

The blocks rolled back to the sandbox. Other toys admired their shiny surfaces. The swings creaked happily in the breeze. Birds chirped overhead.

"You know what?" said Benny. "Bath time wasn't scary."

"It was actually fun!" agreed Sally.

"And we did it together," said Max. "That made us brave."

The three friends built the most beautiful castle ever. Their clean sides stuck together perfectly. They worked until the stars came out.

As the playground grew quiet, the blocks snuggled close.

"Tomorrow is another bath day," whispered Sally.

"I can't wait!" said Benny.

"Me too!" said Max.

And they all dreamed of warm, bubbly water and gentle brushes. They learned that trying new things together makes everything better. Sometimes what seems scary turns out to be wonderful.

The end.

The end.
