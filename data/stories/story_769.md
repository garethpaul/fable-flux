# Story story_769

**Milo and the Big Storm**

Milo the monkey loved playing in Sunny Park. He swung from tree to tree every day. The green leaves tickled his face. The warm sun made him happy.

One morning, Milo woke up feeling worried. Dark clouds filled the sky. The wind blew hard through the trees. "Oh no!" said Milo. "A big storm is coming!"

Milo looked around the park. He saw his friends playing. Bella the bunny was hopping near the flowers. Sam the squirrel was gathering acorns. They didn't know about the storm yet.

"I must warn everyone!" Milo said. He swung down from his tree. His furry hands felt cold. The wind made his tail blow around.

Milo ran to Bella first. "Bella! Bella!" he called. "A storm is coming! We need to hide!"

But Bella couldn't hear him. The wind was too loud. It whooshed through the trees. Milo jumped up and down. He waved his arms. Still, Bella kept hopping away.

Next, Milo tried to reach Sam. He climbed up Sam's oak tree. "Sam!" he shouted. "Listen to me!"

The wind blew harder. Sam's ears couldn't hear Milo's voice. The leaves rustled so loudly. Sam just kept looking for more acorns.

Milo felt scared and sad. How could he help his friends? The storm clouds grew darker. They looked like big gray blankets in the sky.

Then Milo had an idea. He remembered the old bell near the playground. The park keeper rang it when it was time to go home.

Milo swung through the trees quickly. His feet grabbed each branch tight. The wind pushed him around. But he kept going.

He reached the tall pole with the bell. Milo grabbed the rope with both hands. He pulled as hard as he could. CLANG! CLANG! CLANG!

The loud bell rang across the park. Bella stopped hopping. Sam looked up from his acorns. All the animals turned to listen.

"Storm coming!" Milo called out. "Follow me to safety!"

Bella hopped over quickly. Her soft ears were flat against her head. "Thank you, Milo!" she said. "I was scared of the wind!"

Sam scurried down his tree. His bushy tail twitched with worry. "Where should we go?" he asked.

Milo pointed to the big gazebo. It had a strong roof and walls. "There!" he said. "We'll be safe and dry!"

All the animals ran to the gazebo together. They made it just in time. The rain started to fall. It pattered on the roof like tiny drums.

Inside the gazebo, everyone felt cozy. Milo shared his bananas with his friends. Bella found some carrots in her pouch. Sam had plenty of acorns for everyone.

"You saved us, Milo!" said Bella. Her pink nose wiggled happily.

"How did you know about the storm?" asked Sam. His dark eyes sparkled with wonder.

"I felt the cold wind," said Milo. "I saw the dark clouds. I listened to my worried feelings."

The friends watched the rain fall outside. Lightning flashed far away. Thunder rumbled softly. But they felt safe together.

"I'm glad you didn't give up," said Bella. "When calling didn't work, you found another way."

"The bell was a great idea!" said Sam. "You kept trying until it worked."

After an hour, the storm passed. Sunshine peeked through the clouds. The air smelled fresh and clean. Puddles sparkled like mirrors on the ground.

The friends walked outside together. Everything looked bright and new. The flowers had water drops like tiny jewels. The grass was greener than before.

"Thank you for taking care of us," said Bella. She gave Milo a gentle hug.

"Friends help friends," said Milo. His heart felt warm and happy. "When we work together, we can solve any problem."

From that day on, Milo became the park's weather watcher. He always looked out for his friends. And whenever storms came, all the animals knew they could count on Milo to keep them safe.

The three friends played together every sunny day after that. They remembered that caring for others and never giving up made their friendship even stronger.

The end.
