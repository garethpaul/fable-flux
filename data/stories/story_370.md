# Ellie's Big Search

Ellie the elephant loved her sunny yellow kitchen. She had big gray ears that flapped when she walked. Her trunk swayed back and forth happily. This morning, something was very wrong.

"Oh no!" Ellie cried. "Where is my pretty doll?"

Ellie's doll had curly red hair. She wore a blue dress with tiny flowers. Ellie took her doll everywhere. Now she was gone!

Ellie's trunk drooped sadly. Her big brown eyes filled with tears. "I must find my doll," she whispered.

First, Ellie looked under the kitchen table. She got down on her knees. Her trunk felt around carefully. She found a red crayon. She found a cookie crumb. But no pretty doll.

"Not here," Ellie said with a sigh.

Next, Ellie opened all the cupboards. The doors made creaky sounds. She moved the cereal boxes. She looked behind the soup cans. Her trunk searched every corner.

"Still not here," Ellie mumbled.

Then Ellie checked the refrigerator. The cold air made her shiver. She moved the milk jug. She looked behind the apples. The bright light hurt her eyes.

"Where could she be?" Ellie wondered.

Ellie sat down on the floor. Her trunk curled up sadly. "Maybe I should give up," she thought.

But then Ellie remembered something. Mama always said, "Never give up, little one. Keep trying!"

Ellie stood up tall. Her ears flapped with new hope. "I won't give up!" she declared.

This time, Ellie thought carefully. "Where did I play yesterday?" she asked herself.

Ellie remembered washing dishes. She loved to splash in the soapy water. Her doll liked to watch her clean.

Ellie hurried to the sink. She looked all around it. Nothing there.

Then she opened the cabinet underneath. It was dark and musty inside. Her trunk felt around slowly.

Something soft touched her trunk tip!

"Could it be?" Ellie whispered excitedly.

She pulled gently. Out came her pretty doll! The red curls were a bit messy. The blue dress was wrinkled. But she was perfect!

"My doll!" Ellie trumpeted loudly.

She hugged her doll tight. The soft fabric felt wonderful. Ellie spun around the kitchen. Her feet made happy thumping sounds.

"I found you! I found you!" she sang.

Ellie remembered now. Yesterday, she had been playing house. She put her doll to "sleep" in the cabinet. Then she forgot all about it!

"You must have been scared," Ellie told her doll. "But I never stopped looking for you."

Ellie smoothed the doll's red curls. She straightened the tiny blue dress. "There," she said. "All better."

The kitchen felt bright and cheerful again. Sunlight danced through the windows. Birds chirped happy songs outside.

Ellie made her doll a little bed. She used a soft dish towel. She placed it on the counter where she could see it.

"Now I'll always know where you are," Ellie said.

Just then, Mama elephant walked in. Her trunk touched Ellie's head gently.

"You look happy, sweetie," Mama said.

"I lost my doll," Ellie explained. "But I didn't give up. I kept looking and looking. Then I found her!"

Mama's eyes sparkled with pride. "That's my smart girl. You were very persistent."

"What does persistent mean?" Ellie asked.

"It means you keep trying," Mama said. "Even when things are hard. You never gave up."

Ellie felt proud and strong. Her chest puffed out happily. "I was persistent!" she announced.

That evening, Ellie played carefully. She always put her doll in the same safe spot. The pretty doll with curly red hair never got lost again.

And whenever Ellie faced a hard problem, she remembered her big search. She would think, "I can be persistent. I can keep trying!"

Ellie learned that day that good things happen when you don't give up. Sometimes the answer is hiding right under your trunk!

The end.
