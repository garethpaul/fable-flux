# Story story_1167

**Duck and Emma**

Duck sat by the toy box. She felt very lonely. The nursery was big and bright. Colorful toys filled every corner. But Duck had no one to play with.

Duck's yellow feathers looked dull today. Her orange beak pointed down. She picked up a red block. She put it down again. Playing alone was no fun.

"I wish I had a friend," Duck whispered softly.

Emma walked into the nursery. She had curly brown hair and kind eyes. Emma saw Duck sitting alone. Duck looked so sad.

Emma wanted to help. She took small steps toward Duck. Her shoes made soft sounds on the carpet.

"Hi there," Emma said gently. "I'm Emma. What's your name?"

Duck looked up. Her black eyes sparkled a little. "I'm Duck," she said quietly.

"Would you like to play together?" Emma asked. She sat down next to Duck. The carpet felt soft and warm.

Duck's heart felt lighter. Someone wanted to play with her! "Yes, please," Duck said with a small smile.

Emma picked up the red block. "Let's build something," she said. Duck nodded and grabbed a blue block.

They stacked blocks high into the air. Red, blue, yellow, green. The tower grew taller and taller. Duck felt happy watching it grow.

"Look how high it is!" Emma laughed. Her voice was like music to Duck.

The tower wobbled. It swayed left and right. Then it crashed down! Blocks scattered everywhere. Both friends giggled together.

"That was fun!" Duck said. Her feathers looked brighter now.

Emma had another idea. "Let's play dress-up!" she said. She pointed to a box of costumes.

Duck flapped her wings with joy. They ran to the dress-up box together. Emma found a sparkly crown. Duck chose a purple cape.

"You look like a princess!" Duck told Emma. Emma's crown caught the sunlight from the window.

"You look like a superhero!" Emma said. Duck's cape fluttered as she spun around.

They marched around the nursery. Emma waved like a royal princess. Duck pretended to fly like a brave superhero. They felt so proud and happy.

Next, they found the art table. Crayons and paper waited for them. Emma picked up a pink crayon. Duck chose orange.

"Let's draw pictures of us," Emma suggested. They colored side by side. The crayons made scratchy sounds on paper.

Emma drew herself with her crown. Duck drew herself with her cape. Then Emma added Duck to her picture. Duck added Emma to hers.

"We're best friends in my picture," Emma said. She held up her drawing proudly.

"In mine too!" Duck said. She showed Emma her colorful picture.

Their drawings had big smiles and bright colors. The friends looked so happy together on paper.

Story time came next. Mrs. Rose, their teacher, gathered everyone. Emma and Duck sat together on the soft reading rug.

Mrs. Rose read about friendship and kindness. Emma and Duck listened carefully. They learned that friends share and care for each other.

"That's just like us," Emma whispered to Duck. Duck nodded and smiled warmly.

After story time, they played with puzzles. Duck was good at finding corner pieces. Emma was great at matching colors. Together, they finished a big butterfly puzzle.

"We make a perfect team," Duck said happily. Her lonely feeling was completely gone.

Emma agreed. "Best friends forever," she said. She gave Duck a gentle hug.

Duck felt so grateful. This morning she was sad and alone. Now she had a wonderful friend. Emma felt happy too. She loved making Duck smile.

The nursery felt different now. It was full of laughter and friendship. The toys seemed more colorful. The sunshine looked brighter.

When playtime ended, Duck and Emma promised to play again tomorrow. They couldn't wait for another fun day together.

Duck learned that friendship makes everything better. Emma discovered that kindness brings joy. Together, they made the nursery a happier place for everyone.

Their friendship would grow stronger every day. And they would never feel lonely again.

The end.
