# Story story_1177

**Benny's Big Bath Problem**

Benny Bunny loved his swing set. He swung high into the blue sky every day. Up and down, back and forth he went. The warm sun kissed his fluffy gray fur.

Today was extra special. Benny had been swinging since morning. He dug holes in the dirt with his paws. He rolled in the soft grass. He splashed in muddy puddles after yesterday's rain.

"Wheee!" Benny laughed as he swung higher.

But soon, Mama Bunny called from the burrow. "Bath time, Benny! Come inside now!"

Benny's ears drooped. His nose twitched with worry. He did NOT want a bath. The water felt cold and scary. The soap made his eyes sting.

"I don't want to!" Benny called back. "I want to keep swinging!"

He gripped the swing chains tighter. His little paws wrapped around the metal links. Maybe if he held on very tight, he wouldn't have to go inside.

Mama Bunny hopped outside. She saw her muddy little bunny. His gray fur was brown with dirt. His white tail had grass stains. His pink nose was smudged with soil.

"Oh my!" Mama said gently. "You're very dirty, sweet pea."

"I'm not dirty," Benny fibbed. "I'm just... colorful!"

Mama smiled. She knew Benny was scared. "Come along now. The bath is ready."

Benny shook his head fast. His long ears flapped. "No, no, no! I'll stay here forever!"

He swung back and forth, holding tight. But his paws were getting tired. The metal felt slippery. His grip started to loosen.

"Uh oh," Benny whispered.

His first solution wasn't working. He couldn't hold on much longer.

Mama Bunny sat on the grass nearby. "Tell me why you don't like baths," she said softly.

Benny slowed his swing. "The water is too cold. It makes me shiver. And soap gets in my eyes. It hurts!"

"I see," Mama nodded. "Those are real problems. Should we solve them together?"

Benny stopped swinging. His ears perked up. "We can fix bath problems?"

"Of course!" Mama smiled. "Let's try something new."

They hopped inside together. Mama showed Benny the bathtub. But this time, something was different.

"Feel the water," Mama said.

Benny dipped one paw in. His eyes grew wide. "It's warm! Like sunshine!"

"I added extra warm water," Mama explained. "Just for you."

Then she showed him a special washcloth. It was soft and yellow like a baby duck.

"This will keep soap away from your eyes," she said. "We'll wash your face very gently."

Benny touched the fluffy cloth. It felt like a cloud.

"And look!" Mama pointed to the water. Tiny bubbles floated everywhere. They smelled like strawberries. Some bubbles had rainbow colors.

"Pretty!" Benny gasped.

He stepped into the warm water. It felt like a cozy hug. The bubbles tickled his tummy. He giggled and scooped them up.

Mama washed him carefully with the soft cloth. She kept soap away from his eyes. She sang his favorite song about carrots and clover.

"This is fun!" Benny laughed. He splashed gently in the warm water.

When bath time ended, Benny felt amazing. His fur was fluffy and clean. He smelled like sweet strawberries. His skin felt soft and smooth.

"Thank you, Mama," he said, giving her a big hug. "You fixed all my bath problems!"

"We fixed them together," Mama said. "You told me what was wrong. Then we found better ways."

That night, Benny couldn't wait for tomorrow's swing time. And when he got dirty again, he knew something wonderful. Bath time could be warm and gentle and fun.

"Problems can be solved," he whispered to his stuffed carrot. "You just need to try new ways."

Benny fell asleep with a happy smile. Tomorrow would bring more swinging, more playing, and maybe even more bubble baths. And that sounded perfectly wonderful to one clean little bunny.

The end.
