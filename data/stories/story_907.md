# Story story_907

**Molly Mouse and the Lost Doll**

Molly Mouse loved playing in the big sandbox. The warm sand felt soft between her tiny paws. She always brought her favorite doll named Rosie.

Rosie had a pink dress and yellow hair. Her smile was painted bright red. Molly loved Rosie very much.

One sunny morning, Molly hopped into the sandbox. She placed Rosie carefully on a small sand hill. Then Molly began building a castle.

She scooped sand with her little paws. The sand was cool and smooth. Molly made tall towers and deep moats. She worked for a long time.

"There!" said Molly. "My castle is done."

She turned to show Rosie the beautiful castle. But Rosie was gone!

"Oh no!" cried Molly. "Where is Rosie?"

Molly looked all around the sandbox. She saw only sand and her castle. Her whiskers twitched with worry.

"Rosie must be buried in the sand," thought Molly.

Molly started digging with her paws. She dug in one corner of the sandbox. The sand flew everywhere. It stuck to her gray fur.

Dig, dig, dig went her little paws. But she found nothing.

She tried another corner. Dig, dig, dig. Still no Rosie.

Molly's paws were getting tired. Sand covered her nose. She felt very sad.

"This isn't working," said Molly. She sat down and thought hard.

Then Molly had a new idea. "Maybe I should look more carefully."

She stood up and brushed the sand off her fur. This time, she would search slowly.

Molly walked around the sandbox edge. She looked at every bump in the sand. She used her nose to sniff for Rosie's smell.

Near the big oak tree, Molly saw something pink. It was just a tiny bit of pink sticking up from the sand.

"That looks like Rosie's dress!" said Molly.

Very gently, Molly brushed away the sand. First she saw more pink fabric. Then she saw yellow hair. Finally, she saw Rosie's red smile.

"Rosie!" shouted Molly happily. "I found you!"

Molly carefully lifted her doll from the sand. She hugged Rosie tight against her soft belly.

"You must have rolled down the hill," said Molly to Rosie. "The sand covered you up."

Molly brushed all the sand off Rosie's dress. She smoothed down the yellow hair. Soon Rosie looked perfect again.

"I was so worried about you," whispered Molly. "I thought you were lost forever."

Molly learned something important that day. When digging everywhere didn't work, she tried looking carefully instead.

"Sometimes the best way to solve a problem is to slow down and think," said Molly.

She placed Rosie safely in her pocket. Then Molly built a new sand castle. This one was even better than the first.

She made windows in the towers. She drew pictures in the sand with her claw. She even made a garden with stick flowers.

"Look, Rosie," said Molly, taking her doll out of her pocket. "Our new castle has everything."

The sun felt warm on Molly's back. A gentle breeze blew through her whiskers. She could smell the sweet flowers growing nearby.

Molly and Rosie played in the sandbox until the sun began to set. The sky turned orange and pink.

"Time to go home," said Molly.

She picked up Rosie and gave her another hug. "I'll never lose you again," she promised.

As they walked home, Molly felt proud. She had solved a big problem all by herself. When the first way didn't work, she tried a different way.

"Tomorrow we'll play in the sandbox again," said Molly to Rosie. "But this time, I'll keep you safe in my pocket when I build castles."

Molly skipped all the way home. Her tail swished happily behind her. She couldn't wait to tell her mama about finding Rosie.

Sometimes problems seem very big. But if you don't give up and try new ways, you can solve them. That's exactly what Molly Mouse learned in the sandbox that day.

The end.
