# Dog's Missing Frisbee

Sunny the golden retriever loved playing fetch in his big, grassy backyard more than anything else in the whole world. His favorite toy was a bright blue frisbee that flew through the air like a magical flying disc. Every morning after breakfast, Sunny would bring the frisbee to Papa and wag his fluffy tail, which was his way of saying, "Please throw my frisbee so I can run and catch it!"

The backyard was perfect for playing fetch. It had soft green grass that felt wonderful under Sunny's paws, tall oak trees that provided shady spots for resting, and a wooden fence that kept the frisbee from flying too far away. Sunny knew every inch of that yard and could catch his frisbee no matter where Papa threw it.

One sunny Saturday morning, Sunny woke up feeling extra excited and energetic. His tail was wagging so hard it made his whole body wiggle with joy. "Today is going to be the best day ever for playing fetch!" he thought happily. He bounded outside and looked around for his beloved blue frisbee.

But something was wrong. Sunny looked in all the usual places where he kept his toys, but the frisbee was nowhere to be found. He checked under the big oak tree where he sometimes left it after playing. He looked near the back porch where Papa usually sat to watch him play. He even checked by the garden gate where sometimes toys got forgotten. But there was no blue frisbee anywhere.

"Woof! Where is my frisbee?" barked Sunny, feeling worried and confused. "I can't play fetch without it! Yesterday it was right here by the tree, and now it's completely gone!" He ran around the yard in circles, sniffing and searching, but still couldn't find his precious toy.

Sunny sat down in the middle of the yard and thought hard about where his frisbee could have gone. "Maybe the wind blew it somewhere," he wondered. "Or maybe it rolled under something where I can't see it." He was determined not to give up because that frisbee was too important to lose forever.

First, Sunny decided to search more carefully under all the bushes and plants around the yard. He got down low and crawled under the big lilac bush, pushing his nose through the leaves and branches. There were some interesting smells and a few old tennis balls, but no blue frisbee.

"That didn't work," panted Sunny, backing out of the bush with leaves stuck in his golden fur. "But I won't give up! There has to be somewhere else to look."

Next, Sunny remembered that sometimes toys bounced over the fence into the neighbor's yard when Papa threw them really hard. He stood up on his hind legs and put his front paws on the wooden fence, stretching his neck as high as he could to peer over into the next yard.

Sure enough, there in Mrs. Johnson's flower garden was something blue! "Woof! I found it!" barked Sunny excitedly. But then he realized he had a new problem. The frisbee was on the other side of the fence, and Sunny couldn't climb over or dig under the fence to get it.

"Oh no," whined Sunny. "I can see my frisbee, but I can't reach it! What am I going to do now?" He sat down again and thought about this new challenge. He needed help, but Papa was inside making breakfast and didn't know about the missing frisbee yet.

Then Sunny had a brilliant idea. He remembered that Mrs. Johnson was always very kind to him and often gave him treats when she saw him in the yard. Maybe she would help him get his frisbee back if he asked her nicely!

Sunny ran to the part of the fence that was closest to Mrs. Johnson's back door and began barking in his most polite, friendly voice. "Woof! Woof! Mrs. Johnson, could you please help me?" he called. He wagged his tail and did his best sitting-pretty pose to show that he was being good and not just making noise.

After a few minutes, Mrs. Johnson came outside to see what Sunny was trying to tell her. She was a gentle older lady with silver hair and kind eyes who loved all the animals in the neighborhood.

"Hello, Sunny!" she called over the fence. "What's all the barking about this morning? Are you trying to tell me something important?"

Sunny barked again and looked toward where his frisbee was lying in her flower garden. He hoped she would understand what he needed.

Mrs. Johnson followed Sunny's gaze and spotted the bright blue frisbee among her colorful flowers. "Oh my! Is this your frisbee, sweetheart? It must have flown over the fence yesterday evening. Let me get it for you right away!"

She carefully picked up the frisbee and walked to the gate between their yards. "Here you go, sweet boy," she said, handing the frisbee to Sunny. "Next time your toy comes to visit my garden, just bark like that and I'll bring it right back to you."

Sunny was so happy he could hardly contain his excitement! He took his precious frisbee gently in his mouth and ran in three joyful circles around the yard. Then he brought the frisbee to Papa, who had come outside to see what all the commotion was about.

"Well look at that!" laughed Papa. "Sunny solved his own problem and got his frisbee back! What a smart dog you are!" Papa threw the frisbee high into the air, and Sunny leaped up to catch it perfectly.

From that day on, Sunny felt very proud and confident about solving problems. He learned that when something seems impossible, there's usually a solution if you think carefully and ask for help when you need it.

Now Sunny feels happy and smart every day, and he knows he can handle any challenge that comes his way.

The end.
