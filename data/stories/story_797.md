# Story story_797

Max's pajamas felt soft and warm. The blue ones with tiny rockets. Mama called from downstairs.

"Max! Time for bed!"

Max frowned. He was building the best block tower ever. Red blocks on bottom. Yellow blocks on top. It reached his knee already.

"Five more minutes?" Max called back.

"Now, sweetie," Mama said gently.

Max's shoulders drooped. Bedtime meant no more playing. No more fun. Just lying in the dark.

He trudged upstairs slowly. Each step felt heavy. His room looked different at night. Shadows danced on the walls. The moon peeked through his window.

Mama helped him brush his teeth. The toothpaste tasted like strawberries. She combed his messy brown hair.

"I don't want to sleep," Max whispered.

"Why not, little one?" Mama asked.

"Sleep is boring. Nothing happens."

Mama smiled mysteriously. "Are you sure about that?"

She tucked him under his favorite blanket. The one with stars all over it. It smelled like lavender from the wash.

"Close your eyes," Mama said softly. "Listen carefully."

Max squeezed his eyes shut. At first, he heard nothing. Then... tiny sounds filled the air.

Tick-tock went the clock. Whoosh went the wind outside. His heart beat like a gentle drum.

"Do you hear that?" Mama whispered.

"What is it?" Max asked.

"The night sounds. They're magic."

Suddenly, Max's star blanket began to glow. Soft, golden light filled his room. The stars seemed to twinkle and dance.

"Wow!" Max gasped.

One star floated up from his blanket. It circled around his head like a tiny firefly. More stars joined the first one.

"They want to show you something," Mama said.

The stars swirled together. They formed pictures in the air. A rocket ship zoomed past. A friendly dragon waved hello. A rainbow bridge stretched across his ceiling.

Max giggled with delight. "They're beautiful!"

"This happens every night," Mama explained. "But only when you're ready for sleep."

The stars settled back onto his blanket. They pulsed gently like sleepy heartbeats. Max felt drowsy and peaceful.

"Will they come back tomorrow?" he asked.

"Every single night," Mama promised. "They're waiting for you."

Max's eyelids grew heavy. The magical feeling wrapped around him like a warm hug. Sleep didn't seem scary anymore.

"Goodnight, stars," he whispered.

The stars twinkled once more. Then they rested quietly on his blanket.

Mama kissed his forehead. "Sweet dreams, my brave boy."

Max drifted off to sleep. In his dreams, he rode the rocket ship. He played with the friendly dragon. He walked across the rainbow bridge.

When morning came, Max woke up smiling. Sunlight streamed through his window. Birds chirped cheerfully outside.

"Mama!" he called excitedly.

She appeared in his doorway. "Good morning, sleepyhead."

"The stars were amazing!" Max said. "We went on adventures together!"

"I'm so glad," Mama said, hugging him tight.

Max bounced out of bed. He felt rested and happy. His dreams had been full of wonder.

At breakfast, he told Daddy about his magical night. About the glowing stars and fantastic journeys.

"That sounds incredible," Daddy said.

All day, Max played happily. He built with blocks. He drew pictures of dragons. He colored rocket ships.

But something was different now. Max felt excited about something special.

When evening came, he didn't frown. Instead, he smiled widely.

"Is it bedtime yet?" he asked hopefully.

Mama looked surprised. "Soon, sweetie."

Max put on his rocket pajamas eagerly. He brushed his teeth without being asked. He climbed into bed with anticipation.

"Ready for the stars?" Mama asked.

"Yes!" Max said, pulling up his blanket.

He closed his eyes and listened. The magical sounds began again. His blanket started to glow softly.

Tonight, the stars showed him a castle in the clouds. Tomorrow night would bring new adventures.

Max realized something wonderful. Bedtime wasn't the end of fun. It was the beginning of magic.

He couldn't wait to see what dreams would come.

The end.
