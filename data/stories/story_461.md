# Anna's Magical Playtime

Anna sat on her bedroom floor. Her toys were scattered everywhere. Dolls here, blocks there, crayons rolled under the bed.

"Anna, it's playtime!" called Mama from downstairs.

Anna sighed a big sigh. She looked around her messy room. Nothing seemed fun today. The dolls looked boring. The blocks seemed too hard to stack. Even her favorite stuffed elephant looked sleepy.

"I don't know what to play," Anna whispered to herself.

She picked up a red crayon. It felt cold in her hand. She put it down again. She touched her puzzle box. The pieces rattled inside like tiny pebbles.

Anna flopped onto her soft rug. The blue carpet felt fuzzy against her cheek. She closed her eyes and listened. The house was quiet except for Mama humming downstairs.

Suddenly, something tickled her nose. Anna opened her eyes. A golden sunbeam danced across her face. It came through her window and landed right on her art supplies.

The sunbeam made her crayons sparkle. Red looked like strawberries. Yellow glowed like sunshine. Purple shimmered like grape juice.

"Ooh!" Anna sat up quickly.

She grabbed the sparkly red crayon. It felt warm now, like holding a tiny piece of summer. Anna pressed it to her paper. The crayon glided smoothly, making a perfect circle.

"A sun!" Anna giggled.

The yellow crayon called to her next. She drew bright rays shooting out everywhere. The purple crayon wanted to make clouds. The green crayon danced across the paper, creating grass that looked soft enough to touch.

Anna's picture grew bigger and bigger. She added a house with a red door. She drew herself with curly hair and a big smile. She even drew Mama with her favorite blue dress.

"This is the most beautiful picture ever!" Anna announced to her room.

But the magic wasn't finished yet. Anna noticed her blocks gleaming in the sunbeam too. They looked like colorful jewels waiting to become something special.

Anna stacked them carefully. One blue block, then a red one. The tower grew taller and taller. It looked like a castle reaching for the clouds.

"A princess castle!" Anna clapped her hands.

She placed her favorite doll at the top. The doll became a brave princess watching over the kingdom. Anna's stuffed elephant became the royal pet, guarding the castle gates.

Soon Anna was crawling around her room, making the elephant trumpet loudly. "PAWOO! PAWOO!" she called out, protecting the magical kingdom.

Her dolls came alive in her imagination. They had tea parties in the castle courtyard. They went on adventures through the crayon forests Anna had drawn.

The room filled with Anna's laughter and happy chatter. She talked to each toy like an old friend. The elephant told jokes. The princess sang songs. Even the blocks seemed to hum with joy.

"Anna! How's playtime going?" Mama called upstairs.

"WONDERFUL!" Anna shouted back. "I'm having the best time ever!"

Time flew by like a butterfly. Before Anna knew it, the golden sunbeam had moved across her room. But the magic stayed in every corner.

Anna looked around at her beautiful creations. Her artwork covered the floor like a colorful carpet. Her block castle stood tall and proud. Her toys were arranged in a perfect little village.

"Tomorrow," Anna said to her elephant friend, "we'll have even more adventures!"

She carefully placed her crayons back in their box. Each one seemed to wink at her, promising more magical pictures. She patted her block castle goodnight. She tucked her dolls into their tiny beds.

Anna skipped downstairs to find Mama. Her heart felt light and happy, like a balloon floating in a sunny sky.

"Mama, playtime was amazing today!" Anna hugged Mama tight.

"What made it so special?" Mama asked, stroking Anna's hair.

Anna thought for a moment. "Everything felt magical when I really looked at it."

That night, as Anna drifted off to sleep, she smiled thinking about tomorrow's playtime. Her room was full of possibilities waiting to come alive again.

Dreams of colorful adventures danced in her head until morning came.

The end.
