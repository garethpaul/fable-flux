# Story story_962

**The Breakfast Puzzle**

Emma woke up to sunshine streaming through her window. She stretched her arms high above her head. "Time for breakfast!" called Mama from the kitchen.

Emma loved breakfast time. She loved the smell of toast. She loved the sound of cereal crunching. But today felt different.

On the kitchen table sat a big wooden puzzle. It had pieces scattered everywhere. The box showed a picture of a farm with red barns and happy animals.

"Good morning, sweetie," said Mama. "I thought we could do this puzzle while we eat."

Emma's tummy rumbled loudly. She wanted her pancakes right now. The puzzle looked hard and confusing. There were so many tiny pieces!

"I don't want to do puzzles," Emma said. "I just want breakfast."

Mama smiled and poured orange juice into Emma's special cup. "How about we try for just five minutes? Sometimes puzzles are more fun than they look."

Emma picked up one puzzle piece. It was bright yellow like the sun. She turned it around and around. It didn't fit anywhere she tried.

"This is too hard," Emma sighed. She put the piece down with a little thump.

Mama sat beside her with warm pancakes. "Let's eat first. Maybe the puzzle will seem easier after we have some food."

Emma took a big bite of fluffy pancake. The syrup was sweet and sticky. She felt better with food in her tummy.

Then something magical happened. The yellow puzzle piece began to glow softly. Emma blinked her eyes. Was she dreaming?

"Did you see that?" Emma whispered to Mama.

"See what, honey?" Mama asked, cutting her own pancakes.

Emma picked up the glowing piece again. This time, it seemed to pull toward one spot on the puzzle. Like a magnet! She placed it down and it fit perfectly.

"I did it!" Emma cheered. "It worked!"

Another piece started to shimmer. This one was bright red. Emma followed where it wanted to go. Click! It snapped into place next to the yellow piece.

"You're doing great!" said Mama. "Look, you're making the sun and the barn roof."

Emma forgot all about being reluctant. The puzzle was talking to her! Each piece showed her exactly where it belonged. Green pieces danced toward the grass. Blue pieces floated toward the sky.

Emma ate bites of pancake between placing pieces. The syrup made her fingers a little sticky. But that helped her hold the pieces better!

"The animals want to come home," Emma said, placing a brown cow piece. "They're telling me where they live."

Mama watched with wonder as Emma worked. "You have magic puzzle fingers!"

Soon Emma could see the whole farm taking shape. There was a big red barn. Happy cows stood in green fields. A yellow sun smiled down from blue sky.

"Just a few more pieces," Emma said excitedly. She felt like a puzzle detective solving a mystery.

The last piece was a tiny white cloud. It floated right into place with a satisfying click. The puzzle was complete!

"We did it!" Emma shouted, jumping up and down. "We made a whole farm!"

The finished puzzle looked beautiful on the table. All the pieces fit together perfectly. Emma felt proud and happy inside.

"That was the best breakfast ever," Emma said, giving Mama a big hug.

Mama hugged her back tight. "You were so patient and clever. Even when it seemed hard at first."

Emma looked at her empty plate. She had eaten all her pancakes without even noticing. The puzzle had made breakfast time special and fun.

"Can we do another puzzle tomorrow?" Emma asked hopefully.

"Of course," said Mama. "What kind would you like?"

Emma thought carefully. "Maybe one with ocean animals! Or a rainbow! Or a castle!"

That night, Emma dreamed about magical puzzle pieces. They danced and glowed in her dreams. She couldn't wait for tomorrow's breakfast.

The next morning would bring new puzzles and new adventures. Emma smiled as she fell asleep, already excited for breakfast time.

The end.
