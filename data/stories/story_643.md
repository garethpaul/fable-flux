# Story story_643

**Ben and the Big Bath Day**

Ben lived in a cozy house on a green hill. The house had red shutters and yellow flowers by the door. Ben loved playing outside all day long.

Today, Ben had been very busy. He dug in the dirt with his toy truck. He climbed the old oak tree in the yard. He rolled down the grassy hill three times. Now his hands were muddy. His knees were grass-stained. His hair had leaves in it.

"Bath time, Ben!" called Mama from inside.

Ben's eyes grew wide. He did not want a bath. The water felt too cold. The soap made his eyes sting. He much preferred being dirty and free.

"I don't want a bath!" Ben shouted back.

He ran behind the big oak tree. Maybe Mama wouldn't find him there. Ben crouched down low. He made himself as small as a little mouse.

"Ben, where are you?" Mama called sweetly.

Ben stayed very quiet. A ladybug crawled on his dirty finger. A butterfly landed on his messy hair. Being dirty felt nice and natural.

But soon, Ben's tummy started rumbling. He was getting hungry for dinner. His hiding spot felt lonely too. He missed Mama's warm hugs.

"There you are!" Mama said, finding him behind the tree. "Why don't you want your bath, little one?"

"The water is too cold," Ben said sadly. "And soap hurts my eyes."

Mama smiled and took his muddy hand. "Let's try something different today."

They walked to the bathroom together. Mama turned on the water. She tested it with her elbow.

"Feel this," she said.

Ben touched the water carefully. It was still too cold for him. He shook his head and stepped back.

"Hmm," said Mama thoughtfully. "What if we make it warmer?"

She added more hot water. Steam began rising from the tub. The bathroom smelled like lavender soap. Mama had added Ben's favorite bubble bath.

"Try again," Mama encouraged.

Ben dipped his toe in the water. It felt just right now. Warm like a cozy blanket. The bubbles tickled his foot and made him giggle.

"What about the soap in my eyes?" Ben asked.

Mama showed him a special cup. "We'll pour water gently. You can close your eyes tight. Like this."

She demonstrated how to tip his head back. Ben practiced closing his eyes really tight. He scrunched up his whole face.

"I'll count to three," Mama said. "Then the soap will be all gone."

Ben felt brave now. He climbed into the warm, bubbly water. It felt wonderful on his tired legs. The bubbles made a soft, fizzy sound. They smelled like flowers in springtime.

Mama washed his hair very gently. Ben kept his eyes closed tight. She counted slowly: "One... two... three!" Then she poured the warm water over his head.

No soap got in his eyes at all! Ben opened them and smiled big.

"That wasn't scary!" he said happily.

Ben played with his rubber duck in the bubbles. He made bubble beards on his chin. He pretended to be a sea captain sailing in foam.

When bath time was over, Ben felt clean and fresh. His skin was soft and smooth. His hair smelled like flowers. Mama wrapped him in a fluffy towel that felt like a warm hug.

"I solved the bath problem!" Ben said proudly.

"You sure did," Mama agreed. "Sometimes we need to try new ways."

That night at dinner, Ben told Papa all about his bath adventure. He explained how the water needed to be just right. He showed Papa how to close eyes tight for hair washing.

"Tomorrow I want another bath!" Ben announced.

Mama and Papa laughed together. Ben had learned that problems could be solved. Sometimes you just needed to try different ways. And asking for help made everything easier.

From that day on, bath time became Ben's favorite time. He loved the warm water and sweet-smelling bubbles. But most of all, he loved figuring things out with Mama's help.

The little house on the hill was filled with the sound of happy splashing every evening.

The end.
