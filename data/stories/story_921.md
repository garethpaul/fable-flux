# Story story_921

Zoe sat on the soft green hill. The sun felt warm on her face. She picked yellow flowers and made a small pile. But something felt missing.

"I wish I had a friend to play with," Zoe said quietly.

The hill was so quiet. Only the wind made gentle whooshing sounds through the tall grass. Zoe's pile of flowers looked pretty, but playing alone wasn't very fun.

Behind a big oak tree, a fluffy brown bear watched Zoe. The bear had kind dark eyes and a friendly smile. He wanted to play too, but he felt shy.

"Maybe she won't like me," the bear thought. "I'm much bigger than her."

The bear took one small step forward. Then another step. His big paws made soft sounds on the grass.

Zoe heard something behind her. She turned around slowly. Her eyes grew wide when she saw the bear.

"Oh!" Zoe said. "Hello there!"

The bear stopped walking. His ears drooped down. "Hi," he said in a gentle voice. "I'm sorry if I scared you."

"You didn't scare me," Zoe smiled. "You look very nice. What's your name?"

"I'm Bear," he said, feeling a little braver. "I saw you playing by yourself. Can I help you pick flowers?"

Zoe's face lit up like sunshine. "Yes! I would love that!"

Bear walked over carefully. His big paws were very gentle as he picked bright purple flowers. Zoe picked more yellow ones and some white daisies too.

"These smell so sweet," Zoe said, sniffing a purple flower.

"They do!" Bear agreed. "And they feel soft like my fur."

Soon they had a huge pile of colorful flowers. The sweet smell filled the air around them.

"What should we do now?" Bear asked.

Zoe thought for a moment. "Let's make flower crowns!"

Bear had never made a flower crown before. "I don't know how," he said sadly.

"That's okay," Zoe said kindly. "I can teach you. We can learn together."

Zoe showed Bear how to twist the flower stems together. Bear's big paws made it tricky at first. Some flowers fell apart.

"Try again," Zoe encouraged him. "You're doing great!"

Bear tried once more. This time, the flowers stayed together. "I did it!" he said happily.

They made crowns for both of them. Zoe's crown had yellow and white flowers. Bear's crown had purple and pink ones.

"You look beautiful," Zoe told Bear.

"So do you," Bear said, feeling proud.

They rolled down the grassy hill together. The soft grass tickled their arms and legs. They laughed as they tumbled down.

At the bottom, they lay on their backs. Fluffy white clouds drifted across the blue sky above them.

"That cloud looks like a butterfly," Zoe pointed up.

"And that one looks like a big sandwich," Bear said.

They giggled together as they watched the clouds change shapes.

"Bear," Zoe said, "I'm so happy you came to play with me."

"Me too," Bear said. "I was watching you from behind the tree. I wanted to play, but I felt nervous."

"Why were you nervous?" Zoe asked.

"I thought you might not want to be friends with a bear," he said quietly.

Zoe sat up and looked at Bear with kind eyes. "I think bears make the very best friends. You're gentle and fun and sweet."

Bear's heart felt warm and happy. "Really?"

"Really," Zoe said. "Want to be best friends?"

"Yes!" Bear said, giving Zoe a gentle hug.

They spent the rest of the afternoon playing on the hill. They picked more flowers, chased butterflies, and told funny stories.

As the sun began to set, painting the sky orange and pink, Zoe and Bear sat together on top of the hill.

"This was the best day ever," Zoe said.

"Every day will be the best day now," Bear said, "because we're friends."

They hugged goodbye and promised to meet on the hill again tomorrow. Zoe skipped home with her flower crown, and Bear walked home with his.

Neither of them would ever feel lonely on the hill again.

The end.
