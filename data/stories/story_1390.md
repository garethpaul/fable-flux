# Story story_1390

**The Lonely Tiger in the Garden**

Tommy Tiger lived in a big backyard. The grass was soft and green. Tall trees gave cool shade. Pretty flowers smelled sweet.

But Tommy felt sad. He had no one to play with.

Every morning, Tommy walked around his yard. His orange and black stripes looked beautiful in the sunshine. His whiskers twitched in the warm breeze. But he was all alone.

"I wish I had a friend," Tommy said softly.

He watched birds fly high above. They chirped happy songs to each other. Bees buzzed from flower to flower. Even the ants marched together in long lines.

Everyone had friends except Tommy.

One sunny day, Tommy had an idea. "I will make myself look different! Then maybe someone will want to play with me."

Tommy rolled in the dirt. Brown mud covered his bright stripes. He looked in the pond water. Now he looked like a big brown dog.

"Perfect!" Tommy said. "Dogs have lots of friends."

Tommy walked to the fence. Next door, a little girl played with her puppy. The puppy was small and fluffy. It had a wagging tail.

"Woof!" Tommy called out. "Want to play?"

The little girl looked over. Her eyes grew very wide. The puppy hid behind her legs.

"You are too big!" the girl said. "And you sound scary!"

Tommy felt even sadder. His first plan did not work. The mud made him feel itchy too.

Tommy went to the pond. He washed off all the dirt. His beautiful stripes came back. The cool water felt good on his fur.

"Maybe I should try something else," Tommy thought.

Tommy looked around his yard again. He saw his favorite rubber ball. It was red and bouncy. He saw his soft rope toy. He saw his water bowl that made fun splashing sounds.

Then Tommy had a better idea.

"I will share my toys!" Tommy said. "Sharing is nice. Maybe that will help me make friends."

Tommy gathered all his toys. He put them near the fence. Then he sat down and waited.

Soon, three neighborhood cats came walking by. They saw the colorful toys through the fence.

"What pretty toys!" said the first cat.

"Can we play with them?" asked the second cat.

"Of course!" Tommy said with a big smile. "I have enough for everyone!"

The cats squeezed through the fence. Tommy rolled the red ball to them. They batted it with their paws. Everyone giggled when it bounced high.

Next, they played tug-of-war with the rope. Tommy was gentle with his new friends. He let them win sometimes.

Then they splashed in the water bowl. The cool drops felt wonderful on their whiskers.

"This is so much fun!" said the third cat.

"Tommy, you are very kind," said the first cat. "You share your toys with us."

"And you play so nicely," added the second cat.

Tommy felt warm and happy inside. His heart felt full of joy.

Soon, more animals came to play. Two rabbits hopped over. A friendly squirrel climbed down from a tree. Even some colorful birds joined the fun.

Tommy's backyard became full of laughter and friendship. The animals played together every day.

"I learned something important," Tommy told his new friends. "I do not need to change who I am. Being kind and sharing is what makes good friends."

The animals all agreed. They loved Tommy just the way he was.

From that day on, Tommy's backyard was never quiet. Friends came to play every morning. They played until the sun went down.

Tommy learned that friendship comes from being yourself. When you are kind and share with others, you will find wonderful friends.

Now Tommy Tiger was never lonely again. His backyard was full of love, laughter, and the very best kind of friendship.

And every night, Tommy fell asleep with a big, happy smile on his fuzzy face.

The end.
