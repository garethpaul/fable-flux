# Story story_917

**Ellie's Magic Lunch**

The big clock said twelve. Lunch time! Mama Elephant called from the kitchen. "Ellie, come eat your lunch!"

Ellie the baby elephant sat under her favorite tree. She did not want lunch. Her tummy felt funny. The green leaves looked boring. The crunchy bark seemed too hard.

"I don't want to eat," Ellie said softly. She wrapped her trunk around her leg. Her ears drooped down like wilted flowers.

Mama walked over slowly. Her big feet made soft sounds on the grass. "What's wrong, little one?" she asked gently.

"Lunch is not fun," Ellie whispered. "The food tastes the same every day. I want something different."

Mama's eyes twinkled like stars. She had an idea! "Close your eyes, Ellie. I have a surprise."

Ellie squeezed her eyes shut tight. She heard Mama's footsteps going away. Then she heard rustling sounds. Something smelled sweet and fresh.

"Open your eyes now," Mama said happily.

Ellie opened her eyes wide. Wow! The lunch looked magical! Mama had made a rainbow on the ground. Red apples sparkled in the sun. Orange carrots stood up like tiny trees. Yellow bananas curved like happy smiles.

"A rainbow lunch!" Ellie gasped. Her trunk wiggled with excitement.

"Try the red first," Mama suggested with a warm smile.

Ellie picked up a shiny apple. Crunch! The juice was sweet and cold. It tasted like sunshine and rain together. "Mmm! This is yummy!"

Next came an orange carrot. It was crunchy and fresh. The taste was earthy and sweet. Ellie's ears flapped with joy.

The yellow banana was soft and creamy. It melted on her tongue like a cloud. "This tastes like happiness!" Ellie giggled.

"Now try the green," Mama said, pointing to fresh lettuce leaves.

The green leaves were crisp and cool. They tasted like morning dew. Ellie felt the crunch between her teeth. Her tummy started feeling better.

Purple grapes came next. They popped in her mouth! Sweet juice ran down her chin. Ellie laughed out loud. The sound echoed through the trees.

Last were the blue berries. They were tiny but bursting with flavor. Sweet and a little tart. They made Ellie's tongue tingle.

"I ate a whole rainbow!" Ellie said proudly. Her trunk swayed back and forth. She felt full and happy.

Mama hugged her with her long trunk. "Food can be an adventure," she said warmly. "Every color has a different taste. Every bite tells a story."

Ellie looked at her empty plates. All the colors were gone. But her tummy felt wonderful. Warm and satisfied and full of good things.

"Can we make shapes tomorrow?" Ellie asked hopefully. Her eyes were bright and curious now.

"What kind of shapes?" Mama asked with interest.

"Maybe star sandwiches? Or triangle fruits? Or circle cookies?" Ellie's voice got more excited with each idea.

Mama laughed like tinkling bells. "We can try all of those! Lunch can be different every day."

Ellie clapped her feet together. "And maybe we can make animal shapes too! Like elephant crackers!"

"That sounds perfect," Mama agreed. She gathered the empty plates. "You are a very creative little elephant."

The sun felt warm on Ellie's back. A gentle breeze tickled her ears. She felt sleepy and content. Her tummy was happy and full.

"Thank you for the rainbow lunch, Mama," Ellie said with a big yawn.

"Thank you for trying new things," Mama replied softly.

Ellie curled up under her favorite tree. She closed her eyes and smiled. Tomorrow's lunch would be another adventure. Maybe they would make flower sandwiches. Or butterfly fruits. Or even rocket ship vegetables!

The possibilities seemed endless. Lunch wasn't boring anymore. It was magical and fun and full of surprises.

Ellie drifted off to sleep. She dreamed of colorful foods dancing in the sky. Her trunk twitched happily as she slept.

Tomorrow couldn't come fast enough.

The end.
