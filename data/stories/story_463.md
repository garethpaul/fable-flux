# Grandpa and the Missing Red Ball

Grandpa Joe loved visiting the nursery. The bright yellow walls were covered with happy pictures. Soft toys sat on every shelf. Today, he came to help little Emma.

Emma sat on the blue carpet. Her eyes were full of tears. "Grandpa," she sniffled. "I lost my favorite red ball. It's gone!"

Grandpa Joe's kind eyes twinkled. He sat down beside Emma. His knees made a little creaking sound. "Tell me about your special ball, sweetie."

"It's bright red like a cherry," Emma said. "It bounces really high. And it makes a soft *thump* sound. I love it so much!"

Grandpa Joe patted Emma's hand gently. "We'll find it together. Let's be ball detectives!"

First, they looked under the wooden toy box. Grandpa Joe got on his hands and knees. "Hmm," he said, peeking underneath. "I see dust bunnies. But no red ball."

Emma giggled a tiny bit. Dust bunnies were silly.

Next, they checked behind the rocking horse. The horse had a soft brown mane. Grandpa Joe moved it carefully. "Nope, not here either," he said.

Emma's bottom lip started to wobble again. "Maybe it's really gone forever."

"Never give up!" Grandpa Joe said cheerfully. "Good detectives keep looking. Where did you play with it last?"

Emma pointed to the corner. "Over there, by the bookshelf."

They walked to the tall bookshelf together. Grandpa Joe looked up high. Emma looked down low. Behind the books, something caught Grandpa's eye.

"Wait a minute," he said. His voice got excited. "Emma, look here!"

Between two thick picture books was a small gap. Something red peeked through. But it was stuck tight.

"My ball!" Emma shouted. She jumped up and down. "You found it, Grandpa!"

But the ball wouldn't come out. Grandpa Joe tried pulling gently. The ball stayed stuck. He tried pushing from the other side. Still stuck.

"What can we do?" Emma asked. Her voice sounded worried again.

Grandpa Joe scratched his gray head. "Let me think. We need something long and thin."

Emma ran to the art table. She brought back a wooden ruler. "Will this work?"

"Perfect!" Grandpa Joe smiled proudly. "You're such a smart helper!"

Very carefully, Grandpa Joe slid the ruler behind the ball. He pushed it slowly. The ball wiggled. It moved a little bit.

"It's working!" Emma clapped her hands.

Push, wiggle, push. Finally, the bright red ball popped out. It rolled across the floor with a happy *bounce, bounce, bounce*.

Emma scooped up her ball. She hugged it tight against her chest. "Thank you, Grandpa Joe! You're the best ball detective ever!"

Grandpa Joe's cheeks turned pink with happiness. "We did it together, Emma. You helped find the ruler. You remembered where you played last."

Emma bounced her ball gently. *Thump, thump, thump.* The familiar sound made her smile big.

"I was scared it was gone forever," Emma said. "But we didn't give up."

"That's right," Grandpa Joe said. He sat in the comfy reading chair. "When something seems impossible, we try different ways. We keep looking. We ask for help."

Emma climbed onto Grandpa's lap. The chair rocked back and forth slowly. "Will you play ball with me now?"

"Of course!" Grandpa Joe laughed. His laugh sounded like gentle thunder. "But let's play in the middle of the room. Away from the bookshelf!"

Emma giggled. She rolled the ball gently to Grandpa. He rolled it back to her. The red ball sparkled in the sunny nursery.

"I love you, Grandpa Joe," Emma said. "And I love my red ball. And I love that we never gave up!"

"I love you too, little detective," Grandpa Joe said. "Remember, there's always a way to solve a problem. Sometimes it just takes time and trying different ideas."

The red ball bounced happily between them. Outside, birds sang cheerful songs. Inside the cozy nursery, Grandpa and Emma played together, feeling proud and happy.

The end.
