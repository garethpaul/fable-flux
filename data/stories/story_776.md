# Story story_776

**Tiger's Dinner Adventure**

It was dinner time at the jungle house. Mama Tiger called from the kitchen. "Tiger, come eat your dinner!"

Tiger was playing with his toy blocks. He did not want to stop. "Five more minutes, Mama!" he called back.

"Dinner is getting cold," said Mama Tiger. "Come now, please."

Tiger walked slowly to the kitchen. His feet dragged on the floor. He sat down at his little table. On his plate were green beans, rice, and fish. Tiger made a sad face.

"I don't like green beans," Tiger said. He pushed them around with his fork. "They taste yucky."

Mama Tiger sat down next to him. "What if we make dinner more fun?" she asked with a smile.

Tiger looked up. "How?"

"Let's pretend your food is magical," said Mama Tiger. She pointed to the green beans. "These are tiny green trees from the Enchanted Forest."

Tiger picked up one green bean. He looked at it closely. It did look like a small tree. "What about the rice?" he asked.

"Those are tiny white pearls from the Ocean Kingdom," said Mama Tiger. "And the fish is from the Crystal Lake."

Tiger's eyes grew wide. This sounded exciting! He picked up a grain of rice. It was small and smooth like a pearl.

"I am the brave Tiger King," said Tiger. "I must eat the magical food to grow strong!"

He took a big bite of green beans. They were crunchy and fresh. "These magic trees taste good!" he said with surprise.

Next, Tiger ate some rice pearls. They were soft and warm. "The pearls give me power!" he roared softly.

Mama Tiger clapped her hands. "Look how strong you are getting!"

Tiger flexed his little arms. He felt bigger already. Then he tried the fish from Crystal Lake. It was flaky and mild. "This makes me swim fast like a fish!" he said.

"Tell me about your adventure, Tiger King," said Mama Tiger.

Tiger stood up on his chair. "I swam through the Crystal Lake. I climbed the magic trees. I found treasure pearls in the deep ocean!"

He sat back down and took another bite. The food tasted much better now. Tiger ate his green beans one by one. Each one was a magic tree. He ate his rice pearl by pearl. He finished all his fish from the special lake.

"I ate all the magical food!" said Tiger proudly. His plate was clean and shiny.

"You did such a good job," said Mama Tiger. She gave him a big hug. "How do you feel?"

Tiger patted his full tummy. "I feel strong and happy," he said. "My tummy feels warm and good."

"Magic food helps you grow big and healthy," said Mama Tiger. "It gives you energy to play and learn."

Tiger helped Mama Tiger clear the table. He carried his plate to the sink. "Mama, what magical food will we have tomorrow?" he asked.

"That's a surprise," said Mama Tiger with a wink. "Maybe we'll have golden corn from the Sun Castle. Or purple grapes from the Rainbow Mountain."

Tiger jumped up and down. "I can't wait! Can we play the magic food game again?"

"Of course we can," said Mama Tiger. "Every dinner can be an adventure."

Tiger brushed his teeth and put on his pajamas. As Mama Tiger tucked him into bed, he thought about tomorrow's dinner. Maybe there would be orange carrots from the Fire Desert. Or yellow cheese from the Moon Palace.

"Thank you for making dinner fun, Mama," said Tiger sleepily.

"Sweet dreams, my little Tiger King," said Mama Tiger. She kissed his forehead.

Tiger closed his eyes and smiled. His tummy felt happy and full. Tomorrow he would go on another magical dinner adventure. He couldn't wait to see what enchanted foods he would discover.

The jungle house grew quiet and peaceful. Tiger dreamed of magical forests and crystal lakes, where every bite was a new adventure.

The end.
